to obviate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑb.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to remove a difficulty or prevent something from happening, especially by taking effective action
to prevent or remove (especially a problem)

Examples

Careful planning can obviate the need for last-minute changes.
The new medical treatment obviates the need for surgery in most cases.
Installing smoke detectors obviates many fire-related risks in the home.

Synset Examples

Let's avoid a confrontation
head off a confrontation
avert a strike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening
  • Synonym: to avert - to prevent something bad from happening

Etymology

From Latin obviare (to oppose, hinder), from ob- (against) + via (way).

2. VERB

/ ˈɑb.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to remove a need or difficulty; to prevent something by making it unnecessary
remove or prevent a need or difficulty

Examples

The new security system obviates the need for a night guard.
Regular maintenance can obviate many mechanical problems before they start.
The use of technology has obviated several manual processes in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - remove or get rid of something
  • Synonym: to prevent - stop something from happening

Etymology

From Latin 'obviare' (to act contrary to, hinder), from 'ob-' (against) + 'via' (way).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02635337-v
Synset POS: v
