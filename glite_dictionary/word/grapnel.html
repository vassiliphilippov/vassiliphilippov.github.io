grapnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræpnəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrapnəl / (BrE)
a small anchor with several flukes, used for anchoring small boats or for securing lines to objects
a small anchor with several flukes

Examples

The fisherman dropped the grapnel overboard to secure the boat near the shore.
We used a small grapnel to anchor our rowboat for a picnic on the lake.
He retrieved the lost equipment from the riverbed using a grapnel attached to a sturdy rope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor - a device used to moor a vessel to the sea bottom

Etymology

From Old French 'grapel', diminutive of 'grape', meaning 'hook', from the shape resembling a hook or claw.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræpnəl / (AmE), / ˈɡræpnəl / (BrE)
a small anchor with several hooks, used for grasping or holding things, often thrown attached to a rope
small anchor with multiple hooks

Examples

The sailors used a grapnel to secure the boat to the dock.
A grapnel was thrown over the wall so the climbers could scale it.
The divers retrieved the lost equipment using a long-handled grapnel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grappling hook - a device with hooks used for grabbing or anchoring

Etymology

From Old French *grapinel*, diminutive of *grapon* (grappling hook), from *grape* (hook, claw).
