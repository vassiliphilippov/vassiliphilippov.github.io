nexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.səs / (BrE)
A group or series of things that are linked or connected; a central or connecting point.
a central or connecting link or series

Examples

The Internet acts as a nexus for global communication and information sharing.
A complex nexus of roads intersects in the city center, making navigation challenging.
The novel explores the nexus between personal ambition and societal expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - something that joins or links together
  • Synonym: link - a relationship or connection between things

Etymology

From Latin 'nexus' (a tying or connection), from 'nectere' (to tie, bind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛksəs / (AmE), / ˈnɛksəs / (BrE)
a connection or series of connections linking two or more things; a means by which things are connected
a connection or link between things

Examples

The city serves as a nexus for trade between East and West.
His family background provided a nexus of cultural influences in his life.
The internet is now the main nexus through which information is shared globally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: link - a relationship or connection between things
  • Synonym: connection - a relationship in which a person or thing is linked or associated with something else
  • Antonym: disconnect - the state of being unconnected

Etymology

From Latin 'nexus', meaning 'a binding together', from 'nectere' (to tie, bind).
