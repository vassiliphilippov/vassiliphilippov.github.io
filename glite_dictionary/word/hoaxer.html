hoaxer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊksər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊksə / (BrE)
a person who tricks people by making them believe something false, usually as a practical joke or to deceive
person who perpetrates a hoax

Examples

The notorious hoaxer convinced everyone that the statue had come to life.
After the prank went viral, the hoaxer revealed his true identity on television.
Online hoaxers often spread misinformation for amusement or attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickster - someone who deceives or plays tricks
  • Synonym: prankster - a person who plays practical jokes
  • Antonym: victim - a person who is deceived or tricked

Etymology

From 'hoax' (a deception or trick), possibly an alteration of 'hocus' (to fool, cheat), 18th century English.
