piles

1. NOUN

/ paɪlz / (AmE), / paɪlz / (BrE)
a large number or quantity of something, especially things that are stacked or heaped
large number or quantity

Examples

After cleaning the attic, we found piles of old clothes and photographs.
She has piles of homework to finish before the weekend.
Books were stacked in piles all over the floor.

2. NOUN

/ paɪlz / (AmE), / paɪlz / (BrE)
Swollen and inflamed veins in and around the anus and lower rectum, causing discomfort and bleeding; also known as hemorrhoids.
swollen anal veins; hemorrhoids
medical informal

Examples

Many people experience piles at some point in their lives, especially as they age.
He visited a doctor due to the discomfort caused by his piles.
A high-fiber diet can help prevent piles from developing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemorrhoids - swollen veins in the rectal area; same as piles
