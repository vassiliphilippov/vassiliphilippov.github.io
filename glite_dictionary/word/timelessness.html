timelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmləsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of not being affected by the passage of time or not belonging to any specific period.
the quality of being unaffected by time

Examples

The timelessness of classical art keeps it relevant for every new generation.
Many admire the timelessness of her style, which never seems dated or out of place.
The story's timelessness allows readers of all ages to relate to its themes.

Synset Examples

The following column explores the timelessness of nursing theory by highlighting Florence Nightingale's and Rosemarie Parse's theoretical underpinnings related to understanding the person's perspective on health and quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternity - infinite or unending time
  • Antonym: transience - the state of lasting only for a short time

Etymology

From 'timeless' (not affected by time) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmlɪsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of existing outside the limits of time, often associated with eternity or the afterlife in some religions
quality of being outside time; eternity

Examples

Many myths describe the afterlife as a realm of timelessness beyond human understanding.
The painting's beauty evokes a sense of timelessness that seems to suspend the viewer in eternity.
Some philosophers argue that true spiritual peace is found in a state of timelessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternity - infinite or unending time; state of timeless existence
  • Antonym: transience - the state of lasting only for a short time; impermanence

Etymology

From 'timeless' + '-ness'; 'timeless' from 'time' (Old English tīma) + '-less'.
