Wiccan

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪkən / (AmE), / ˈwɪkən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Wicca, a modern pagan religious movement
relating to Wicca

Examples

She follows Wiccan traditions and celebrates the seasonal festivals.
The store sells many Wiccan books and ritual items.
His Wiccan beliefs influence his daily practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pagan - relating to a religion other than the main world religions, especially a pre-Christian or nature-based faith

Etymology

From Wicca (modern pagan religion) + -an (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03142177-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkən / (AmE), / ˈwɪkən / (BrE)
a person who follows or practices Wicca, a modern pagan religious movement
follower of Wicca religion

Examples

As a proud Wiccan, she celebrates the solstices with her community.
The local library hosted a talk on modern paganism by a Wiccan priestess.
Many Wiccans honor the cycles of nature in their rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch - a practitioner of magic, especially in Wiccan or pagan traditions

Etymology

From Wicca (the religion), itself from Old English wicca (sorcerer, wizard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10799781-n
Synset POS: n
