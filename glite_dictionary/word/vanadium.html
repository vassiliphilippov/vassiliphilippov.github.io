vanadium

1. NOUN

/ vəˈneɪdiəm / (AmE), / vəˈneɪdiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white, toxic metallic element (symbol V) used in steel alloys, found in several minerals such as carnotite and vanadinite
a metallic chemical element (V)

Examples

The addition of vanadium improves the strength and durability of steel.
Geologists discovered rich deposits of vanadium in the region's mineral veins.
Exposure to vanadium compounds can be hazardous to human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titanium - a strong, low-density, silvery metal used in alloys and medical implants
  • Synonym: iron - a hard, strong, silvery-gray metal often alloyed with vanadium in steel

Etymology

From New Latin Vanadium, named after Vanadis, a Scandinavian goddess.
