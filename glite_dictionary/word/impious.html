impious

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmpiəs / (AmE), / ˈɪmpɪəs / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect or reverence for God or religion; lacking piety.
lacking respect for religion or God

Examples

Her impious remarks offended the deeply religious audience.
The play was banned for its impious treatment of sacred rituals.
He was accused of being impious and disrespectful toward established traditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pious - showing reverence and devotion to religion

Etymology

From Latin 'impius' meaning 'not pious, irreverent'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.pi.əs / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pɪ.əs / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect, especially towards someone or something considered sacred or deserving of reverence
not showing due respect

Examples

The novel was banned for being considered impious by religious authorities.
His impious laughter shocked the elders during the solemn ceremony.
Some viewed her refusal to observe the tradition as impious behavior.

Synset Examples

impious toward one's parents
an undutiful son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreverent - showing lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously
  • Antonym: pious - having or showing a dutiful spirit of reverence for God or an earnest wish to fulfill religious obligations

Etymology

From Latin impius (not pious), from in- (not) + pius (dutiful, pious).
