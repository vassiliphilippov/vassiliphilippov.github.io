shipbuilder

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌbɪldɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌbɪldə / (BrE)
a person or company engaged in the construction and repair of ships
person or business that makes ships

Examples

The shipbuilder has launched several new vessels this year.
As a respected shipbuilder, the company employs hundreds of skilled workers.
The family has been in the shipbuilder business for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipyard - a place where ships are built and repaired

Etymology

From 'ship' + 'builder', denoting someone who builds ships.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌbɪldɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌbɪldə / (BrE)
a person or carpenter who constructs and launches ships, especially wooden vessels
person who builds ships

Examples

The shipbuilder inspected the hull before the vessel was launched.
Many generations of his family had worked as shipbuilders in the coastal town.
A skilled shipbuilder is essential for constructing sturdy wooden boats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipwright - a person skilled in the construction and repair of ships

Etymology

From 'ship' + 'builder'.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌbɪldər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌbɪldə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is designing and constructing ships
person who builds ships

Examples

The experienced shipbuilder supervised the construction of the new vessel.
As a third-generation shipbuilder, he takes great pride in his family's legacy.
The city grew prosperous thanks to its talented shipbuilders and busy docks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipwright - a person skilled in making and repairing ships

Etymology

From 'ship' + 'builder'; 'builder' from Old English 'byldan' (to construct).
