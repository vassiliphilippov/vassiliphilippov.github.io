greenback

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnbak / (BrE)
A U.S. dollar bill, especially a paper note; more generally, any piece of paper money.
U.S. dollar bill or paper money

Examples

He found an old greenback tucked away in his grandfather's wallet.
Tourists often exchange their currency for greenbacks when visiting the United States.
She paid the vendor with a crisp greenback from her purse.

Synset Examples

he peeled off five one-thousand-zloty notes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank
  • Synonym: bill - a piece of paper money (especially in the U.S.)

Etymology

From the green-colored back of early United States paper currency issued from the 1860s.
