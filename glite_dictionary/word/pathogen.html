pathogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæθəˌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈpæθədʒən / (BrE)
an organism or agent, such as a virus or bacterium, that causes disease in its host
disease-causing organism or agent
medical

Examples

Washing your hands regularly helps prevent the spread of pathogens that cause illness.
Some pathogens have developed resistance to antibiotics, making treatment more difficult.
Vaccines can protect people from dangerous pathogens such as the influenza virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: germ - a microorganism, especially one that causes disease
  • Synonym: virus - a microscopic infectious agent that replicates only inside the living cells of an organism
  • Antonym: host - an organism that harbors a parasite or pathogen

Etymology

From Greek 'pathos' (suffering, disease) and '-genēs' (producing).
