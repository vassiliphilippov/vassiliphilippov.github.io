entrepreneurial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːntrəprəˈnɜriəl / (AmE), / ˌɒntrəprəˈnɜːriəl / (BrE)
having the qualities or mindset of a person who takes risks and initiates ventures to make a profit, especially in business
having qualities of an entrepreneur

Examples

She has an entrepreneurial spirit and is always looking for new business opportunities.
The company's entrepreneurial approach has led to rapid growth in new markets.
Many employers value workers with an entrepreneurial mindset who can innovate and solve problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprising - showing initiative and willingness to undertake new projects

Etymology

From French entrepreneur (one who undertakes a business) + English adjectival suffix -ial.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888530-s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːntrəprəˈnɜːriəl / (AmE), / ˌɒntrəprəˈnɜːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an entrepreneur or entrepreneurship, especially in taking risks or initiating business ventures.
relating to an entrepreneur or entrepreneurship

Examples

The company is looking for candidates with strong entrepreneurial skills and a passion for innovation.
She took an entrepreneurial approach to solving the community’s problems, launching several new projects.
His entrepreneurial spirit helped the startup grow rapidly during its first year.

Synset Examples

entrepreneurial risks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprising - showing initiative and willingness to take risks

Etymology

From French 'entrepreneur' (one who undertakes) + English '-ial' (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03075787-a
Synset POS: a
