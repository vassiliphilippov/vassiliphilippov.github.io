mazurka

1. NOUN

/ məˈzɝkə / (AmE), / məˈzɜːkə / (BrE)
A lively Polish dance in triple time, or the music composed for such a dance.
lively Polish dance or its music

Examples

The composer wrote a beautiful mazurka that captivated audiences across Europe.
Chopin was renowned for his expressive mazurkas for piano.
At the festival, dancers performed an energetic mazurka in traditional costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polka - a lively dance of Bohemian origin in duple time

Etymology

From Polish 'mazurek', diminutive of 'Mazur', referring to a person from Mazovia, a region of Poland.

2. NOUN

/ məˈzɜrkə / (AmE), / məˈzɜːkə / (BrE)
A lively Polish folk dance in triple time, typically with strong accents on the second or third beat.
lively Polish folk dance

Examples

The pianist performed a beautiful mazurka by Chopin during the recital.
She learned the steps to the traditional mazurka at her local dance club.
The lively rhythm of the mazurka filled the ballroom with energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polonaise - a slow, stately Polish dance in triple time

Etymology

From Polish 'mazurka', feminine of 'mazurek', a dance from Mazovia.
