initially

1. ADV

/ ɪˈnɪʃəli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃəli / (BrE)
at the beginning; at first
at the beginning

Examples

Initially, the company struggled to attract customers, but later it became very successful.
We had planned to leave initially at 8 a.m., but the weather delayed us.
Initially, I found the new job overwhelming, but I soon adjusted.

Synset Examples

at first he didn't notice anything strange

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finally - at the end; after a long time
  • Degree Higher: subsequently - after something has happened; later

Etymology

From 'initial' (from Latin 'initium' meaning 'beginning') + '-ly'.
