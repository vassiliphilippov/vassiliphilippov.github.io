Pernis apivorus

1. NOUN

/ pɜrnɪs eɪpɪˈvɔrəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːnɪs ˌeɪpɪˈvɔːrəs / (BrE)
A species of Old World hawk, also known as the European honey buzzard, which feeds on bee larvae, small rodents, and reptiles.
a species of Old World hawk

Examples

The Pernis apivorus is often mistaken for the common buzzard due to its similar appearance.
Researchers tracked the migration routes of Pernis apivorus across Europe and Africa.
The diet of Pernis apivorus primarily consists of wasp larvae and other insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European honey buzzard - another name for Pernis apivorus, an Old World hawk

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pernis' is the genus of honey buzzards, 'apivorus' means 'bee-eating' (from 'apis' bee + 'vorus' eating).
