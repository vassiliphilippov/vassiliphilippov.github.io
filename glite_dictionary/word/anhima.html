Anhima

1. NOUN

/ ænˈhaɪmə / (AmE), / ænˈhiːmə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Anhimidae, comprising the horned screamers, a type of large South American bird.
genus of horned screamers (birds)

Examples

The bird observed in the wetlands was identified as a species of Anhima.
Researchers studied the vocalizations of Anhima during the breeding season.
The Anhima is well known for the distinctive horn on its head.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Anhimidae - the bird family to which Anhima belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'anhimos' (without reins), alluding to the bird's wild nature.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːˈniːmə / (AmE), / ɑːˈniːmə / (BrE)
A genus of large, South American birds known as horned screamers, serving as the type genus of the family Anhimidae.
genus of South American horned screamers

Examples

The Anhima is notable for its loud calls that can be heard across great distances in South American wetlands.
Researchers have observed that the unique horn-like projection on the Anhima distinguishes it from other waterfowl.
Within the bird family Anhimidae, Anhima is recognized as the type genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Anhimidae - the family comprising screamers, including Anhima and related genera

Etymology

From New Latin Anhima, based on the Portuguese or Tupi name for these birds.
