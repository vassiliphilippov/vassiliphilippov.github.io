sublimely

1. ADV

/ səˈblaɪmli / (AmE), / səˈblaɪmli / (BrE)
in a manner that is of such excellence, grandeur, or beauty as to inspire great admiration or awe; to an extraordinary degree
to an extraordinary or excellent degree

Examples

She handled the difficult situation sublimely, remaining calm and composed throughout.
The pianist performed the piece sublimely, leaving the entire audience in awe.
He remained sublimely oblivious to the chaos happening around him.

Synset Examples

he was supremely confident
He seemed irritated at Edouard's questions but, apart from that, sublimely unconcerned
she remained sublimely oblivious to the possible havoc she might have caused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supremely - to the highest degree; extremely

Etymology

From 'sublime', from Latin 'sublimis' (uplifted, lofty) + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ səˈblaɪmli / (AmE), / səbˈlaɪmli / (BrE)
in a way that is of such excellence, grandeur, or beauty as to inspire great admiration or awe; in a lofty and exalted manner
in an exalted or magnificent way

Examples

The pianist played the sonata sublimely, captivating everyone in the audience.
She handled the difficult situation sublimely, remaining calm and dignified throughout.
The mountain landscape was sublimely beautiful at sunrise.

Synset Examples

it is an enormous, fruit-packed wine, smelling sublimely of ripe blackcurrants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificently - in a splendid, impressive, or exquisite manner
  • Antonym: modestly - in an unassuming or moderate manner

Etymology

From Latin 'sublimis' (lofty, exalted), via 'sublime'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00472106-r
Synset POS: r
