meagerness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiɡərnəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very small in amount, quantity, or extent; lack of richness, fullness, or adequacy
quality of being scant or insufficient

Examples

The meagerness of the meal left everyone still feeling hungry.
Despite the meagerness of their resources, they managed to finish the project.
She was disappointed by the meagerness of the applause after her performance.

Synset Examples

an exiguity of cloth that would only allow of miniature capes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meagerness - the quality of being meager
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something; plentifulness

Etymology

From 'meager' (from Old French 'maigre', from Latin 'macer' meaning 'thin').
