Numidinae

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmɪdəˌni / (AmE), / njuːˈmɪdɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of African and Madagascan birds in the family Numididae, including guinea fowl and their relatives.
subfamily of guinea fowl and related birds

Examples

The Numidinae are best known for their spotted plumage and noisy calls in the wild.
Several species within the Numidinae are important for local agriculture in Africa.
Researchers studied the behavior of Numidinae in both Madagascar and mainland Africa.

Etymology

From New Latin Numidinae, from Numida (type genus) + -inae (zoological subfamily suffix).

2. NOUN

/ nuːˈmɪdəniː / (AmE), / njuːˈmɪdəniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds that includes the guinea fowl and closely related species native to Africa and Madagascar.
subfamily of guinea fowl and related birds

Examples

The Numidinae are known for their distinctive spotted plumage and loud calls.
Researchers in Madagascar study the habitat preferences of several species within the Numidinae.
All domesticated guinea fowl belong to the subfamily Numidinae.

Etymology

From New Latin *Numida* (type genus of guinea fowls) + -inae (a zoological subfamily suffix).
