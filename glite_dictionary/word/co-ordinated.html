co-ordinated

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈɔrdɪneɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of colors, designs, objects, etc.) intentionally matched in style, color, or arrangement to create a pleasing combination.
intentionally matched in style or color

Examples

The designer chose co-ordinated cushions and curtains for a harmonious look.
She wore a co-ordinated outfit with matching shoes and bag.
The office decor features co-ordinated colors to create a welcoming atmosphere.

Synset Examples

curtains and walls were color coordinated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinated - matched or arranged to be harmonious
  • Antonym: clashing - not matching or harmonizing in style or color

Etymology

From 'co-' (together) + 'ordinated', from Latin 'ordinatus', meaning arranged.

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
operating together efficiently as a unit; organized so that all parts work well together.
working together efficiently as a unit

Examples

The co-ordinated efforts of the rescue teams saved many lives.
They launched a co-ordinated marketing campaign across all platforms.
A co-ordinated response is essential during an emergency.

Synset Examples

a unified utility system
a coordinated program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinated - working together efficiently as a unit
  • Synonym: unified - made into a single unit or entity
  • Antonym: disorganized - not properly arranged or lacking order

Etymology

Formed from 'co-' (together) and 'ordinated', from Latin 'ordinare' (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331181-s
Synset POS: s
