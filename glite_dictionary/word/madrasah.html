madrasah

1. NOUN

/ mæˈdrɑː.sə / (AmE), / məˈdrɑː.sə / (BrE)
A Muslim school or college, particularly in Bangladesh, Pakistan, and other South Asian countries, often focusing on religious instruction in Islam.
Muslim religious school or college

Examples

The government implemented new regulations for madrasah curricula across the country.
Many students enroll in a madrasah to study both religious and secular subjects.
The madrasah was constructed with the help of donations from the local community.

Synset Examples

the Pakistan government decided to close down madrasas that provided military training for their students
many madrasas in Bangladesh are supported with money from Saudi Arabia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school - an institution for educating children or students

Etymology

From Arabic *madrasah* (school; place of study), from the root *darasa* (to study).
