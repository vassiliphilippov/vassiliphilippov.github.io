recluse

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkluːs / (AmE), / rɪˈkluːs / (BrE)
a person who lives alone and avoids other people; someone who withdraws from society
person who lives in solitude

Examples

After losing his family, the author became a recluse and rarely spoke to anyone.
The recluse lived on the edge of town, surrounded by woods and wildlife.
She admired the peaceful lifestyle of a recluse, far from the noise of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in seclusion, often for religious reasons

Etymology

From Latin reclusus (shut up, secluded), past participle of recludere (to shut up).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈkluːs / (AmE), / rɪˈkluːs / (BrE)
Withdrawn from society and seeking solitude; living apart from others.
living in solitude; withdrawn from society

Examples

After losing his job, he became increasingly recluse and rarely left his apartment.
The artist led a recluse existence in a cottage in the woods.
Her recluse lifestyle made it difficult for her to maintain close friendships.

Synset Examples

lived an unsocial reclusive life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude, often for religious reasons
  • Antonym: sociable - willing to engage with others; enjoying being with people

Etymology

From Latin 'reclusus', past participle of 'recludere' (to shut up, seclude).
