pilotage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlədɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlətɪdʒ / (BrE)
the profession or activity of piloting ships or aircraft, especially the guiding of ships through dangerous waters or harbors
profession of piloting ships or aircraft

Examples

He specialized in pilotage along the rocky coastline, guiding vessels to safety.
The maritime authority granted him a license for pilotage in the busy harbor.
With years of experience in pilotage, she became an expert in navigating complex waterways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piloting - the act of navigating or operating a vehicle, especially aircraft

Etymology

From French 'pilotage', from 'pilote' (pilot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlətɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlətɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of navigating or guiding ships or aircraft from one place to another, especially by a trained pilot
navigation or guidance of ships or planes

Examples

The harbor requires ships to use local pilotage for safe entry and departure.
Advances in technology have made pilotage easier for modern airline pilots.
Without proper pilotage, the vessel risked running aground in foggy conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigation - the process of planning and directing the course of a vessel or aircraft

Etymology

From French *pilotage*, from *piloter* (to pilot), from Italian *pilota* (pilot).
