aromatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌærəˈmæɾɪk / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
having a strong, pleasant, and often distinctive smell
having a pleasant, distinctive smell

Examples

The garden was filled with aromatic herbs that scented the air.
She lit an aromatic candle to freshen up the living room.
Many spices are prized for their aromatic qualities in cooking.

Synset Examples

the pine woods were more redolent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Antonym: odorless - having no smell

Etymology

From Latin 'aromaticus', from Greek 'arōmatikos', from 'arōma' (spice, sweet herb).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(Chemistry) Containing or relating to one or more benzene rings; having the structure or properties characteristic of benzene or its derivatives.
containing benzene rings (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The laboratory synthesized an aromatic hydrocarbon as part of the experiment.
Many dyes are derived from aromatic compounds and are used in industry.
Benzene is one of the most basic aromatic substances found in organic chemistry.

Synset Examples

an aromatic organic compound

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aliphatic - (Chemistry) not aromatic; compounds not containing benzene rings

Etymology

From Greek *arōmatikos* (spicy, fragrant); in chemistry, from the early association of pleasant smells with such compounds, though the term now refers to ring structure.
