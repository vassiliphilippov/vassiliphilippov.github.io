Marcus Cocceius Nerva

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrkəs kəˈsiːiəs ˈnɜrvə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs kɒˈsiːiəs ˈnɜːvə / (BrE)
(30–98 AD) Roman emperor who ruled from 96 to 98 AD, known for restoring some freedoms after Domitian and for adopting Trajan as his successor.
Roman emperor (96–98 AD)

Examples

Marcus Cocceius Nerva became emperor after the assassination of Domitian in 96 AD.
Historians credit Marcus Cocceius Nerva with stabilizing Rome during a turbulent period.
The succession of Trajan was ensured by Marcus Cocceius Nerva's decision to adopt him as his heir.
