Spiraea prunifolia

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈriːə pruːnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / spaɪˈriːə pruːnɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of ornamental shrub with an abundance of small white flowers in spring, commonly known as bridal wreath spirea.
ornamental shrub with small white spring flowers

Examples

In early spring, the Spiraea prunifolia bursts into a profusion of delicate white blossoms.
Gardeners prize Spiraea prunifolia for its graceful, arching branches and showy spring flowers.
The Spiraea prunifolia hedge created a lovely border along the walkway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridal wreath - a common name for Spiraea prunifolia, an ornamental shrub

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Spiraea and the species epithet 'prunifolia' meaning 'plum-leaved' in Latin.
