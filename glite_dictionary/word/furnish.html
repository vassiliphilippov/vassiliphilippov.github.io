to furnish

1. VERB

/ ˈfɜrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to provide a room or building with furniture and fittings
provide with furniture

Examples

They decided to furnish their new apartment with vintage pieces.
It took them weeks to furnish the entire office after moving in.
The cottage is fully furnished and ready for visitors.

Synset Examples

We furnished the house in the Biedermeyer style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to supply with necessary items for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: unfurnish - to remove furniture from a place

Etymology

From Old French 'fournir', from Vulgar Latin '*fornīre' (to supply, provide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02341479-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɜrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to provide a room, building, or person with the things that are needed or useful, such as furniture, equipment, or supplies
provide or supply with necessary items

Examples

The landlord promised to furnish the apartment with new appliances before we moved in.
Hospitals must furnish adequate medical supplies to all their departments.
Many libraries furnish free internet access to the public.

Synset Examples

We provided the room with an electrical heater
The conference center offers a health spa
The hotel offers private meeting rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provide - to supply or make available something needed
  • Synonym: supply - to make available something that is needed

Etymology

From Old French 'fournir' (to furnish, provide), from Vulgar Latin *fornīre.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02332196-v
Synset POS: v
