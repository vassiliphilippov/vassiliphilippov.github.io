Negroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːɡ.rɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈniː.ɡrɔɪd / (BrE)
(now offensive, archaic) A term formerly used to refer to people of African ancestry, especially in classifications of human races; no longer used and considered pejorative.
offensive, archaic term for people of African ancestry
offensive archaic

Examples

The term Negroid was once used in early anthropological texts but is now avoided due to its offensive nature.
Old racial classifications such as Negroid are considered scientifically invalid and inappropriate today.
He found a 19th-century journal that referred to certain features as distinctly Negroid, reflecting outdated views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Negro - (now offensive) a term for a person of Black African descent
  • Antonym: Caucasian - (now outdated) term for people of European ancestry
  • Antonym: Mongoloid - (now outdated and offensive) term for people of Asian ancestry

Etymology

Formed from 'Negro' with the suffix '-oid', meaning 'resembling or like'.
