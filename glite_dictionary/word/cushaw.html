cushaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuː.ʃɔː / (AmE), / ˈkʊʃ.ɔː / (BrE)
A type of squash plant bearing large, usually ovoid or globular fruits with striped, warty rinds, commonly green, white, and grey.
type of squash plant and its fruit

Examples

The farmer harvested a dozen cushaw from his garden this fall.
She made a delicious pie using freshly picked cushaw.
The grocery store had both pumpkins and cushaw on display for autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - any of various fruits of plants in the gourd family, cultivated as vegetables

Etymology

From Louisiana French 'couchon', from Tamazight *kusku* (a kind of squash), or possibly Spanish 'calabaza' influence.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈʃɔ / (AmE), / kəˈʃɔː / (BrE)
A type of squash, usually globular or oval in shape, with a striped grey and green, sometimes warty, rind.
type of striped squash

Examples

The farmer displayed several large cushaws at the harvest festival.
She used a cushaw to make a rich and creamy soup for dinner.
Cushaws are often grown for their sweet flesh and colorful rinds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - a vegetable of the gourd family with edible flesh

Etymology

From Louisiana French 'couchon', from Spanish 'calabaza' (squash).
