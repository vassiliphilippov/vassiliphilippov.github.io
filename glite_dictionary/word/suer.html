suer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːə / (BrE)
a person who brings a case to court, especially one who sues for redress or the recovery of a right
person who initiates a lawsuit
legal

Examples

The suer provided extensive evidence to support her claim in court.
After months of negotiation, the suer finally agreed to a settlement.
In civil cases, the suer must prove the respondent's liability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
  • Antonym: defendant - the person being accused or sued in a court of law

Etymology

From 'sue' + '-er', formed from Old French *suer* (to sue), from Latin *sequi* (to follow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːə / (BrE)
a man who courts a woman with the aim of marriage; a suitor
man who courts a woman
archaic

Examples

The wealthy suer brought many gifts to impress the lady's family.
Stories of a brave suer risking everything for love are common in folklore.
Each suer tried to outdo the others at the royal banquet.

Synset Examples

a suer for the hand of the princess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitor - a man who pursues a romantic interest toward a woman, especially with intent to marry

Etymology

From Middle English 'suer' (one who sues or petitions), from Anglo-French 'suer', from Old French 'sueer' (to sue, court).
