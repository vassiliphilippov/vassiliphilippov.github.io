prednisone

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛd.nɪˌsoʊn / (AmE), / ˈprɛd.nɪˌsəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic corticosteroid drug used to reduce inflammation and suppress the immune system, commonly prescribed for conditions such as arthritis, allergies, and autoimmune diseases
synthetic corticosteroid drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed prednisone to help manage my severe asthma symptoms.
She began taking prednisone after her diagnosis of rheumatoid arthritis.
Because prednisone is an immunosuppressant, he was instructed to avoid contact with people who were ill.

Etymology

From prednisone (USAN), modelled on cortisone, with elements from prednisolone.
