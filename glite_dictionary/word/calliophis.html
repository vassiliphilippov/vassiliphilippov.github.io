Calliophis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæliˈɒfɪs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, highly venomous elapid snakes native to Asia, commonly known as Asian coral snakes.
genus of Asian coral snakes

Examples

The Calliophis genus includes some of the most brightly colored snakes in Asia.
Calliophis species are highly venomous and must be handled with extreme caution by herpetologists.
Researchers discovered a new species of Calliophis in the forests of India.

Etymology

From Greek kallios (beautiful) + ophis (snake).

2. NOUN

/ kæliˈɑfɪs / (AmE), / kæliˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
a genus of venomous Asian coral snakes found in South and Southeast Asia, known for their bright coloration
genus of Asian coral snakes

Examples

Some species in the genus Calliophis are among the most vividly colored snakes in Asia.
Researchers recently discovered a new member of Calliophis in the forests of India.
The venom of snakes from the genus Calliophis can be highly potent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - any of several venomous snakes with bright bands, including those in the genus Calliophis

Etymology

From Greek *kallos* (beauty) and *ophis* (snake), referring to the appearance of these snakes.
