dropkick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑpˌkɪk / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌkɪk / (BrE)
A kick, especially in football or rugby, made by dropping the ball and kicking it as it rebounds from the ground.
football/rugby kick dropped and kicked as it touches ground

Examples

The player attempted a dropkick for a field goal in the final seconds of the game.
Mastering the dropkick is crucial for rugby players aiming for accuracy and distance.
His successful dropkick tied the score before halftime.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punt - A kick in which the ball is dropped and kicked before it touches the ground

Etymology

From 'drop' + 'kick', describing the action performed.

2. VERB

/ ˈdrɑpˌkɪk / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌkɪk / (BrE)
to score points by dropping the ball and kicking it as it rebounds off the ground, especially after a touchdown in rugby or American football
score with a dropkick after a touchdown

Examples

He attempted to dropkick the ball for an extra point after the touchdown.
Players must have precise timing and skill to dropkick successfully in a rugby game.
She watched her teammate dropkick the ball through the posts, adding to their score.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punt - to kick the ball after dropping it from the hands before it touches the ground

Etymology

From 'drop' + 'kick'; term used in rugby and football contexts since the late 19th century.
