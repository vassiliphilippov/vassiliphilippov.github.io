well-behaved

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛl bəˈheɪvd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl bɪˈheɪvd / (BrE)
behaving in a polite or socially acceptable way; showing good manners, especially as expected by adults or society
behaving in a proper or polite way

Examples

The teacher praised the well-behaved students for their attentiveness during the assembly.
Their dog is remarkably well-behaved and never barks at strangers.
We were relieved that our children were well-behaved throughout the long family dinner.

Synset Examples

a well-behaved child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: badly behaved - behaving in an improper or rude way
  • Synonym: polite - having or showing good manners
