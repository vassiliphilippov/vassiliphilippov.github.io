Citroncirus webberi

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtrɑːnˈsaɪrəs ˈwɛbɚi / (AmE), / ˌsɪtrɒnˈsaɪrəs ˈwɛbəri / (BrE)
A hybrid citrus fruit, more aromatic and acidic than oranges, resulting from the cross between certain species of citrus plants.
a citrus hybrid fruit species

Examples

Some growers prefer Citroncirus webberi for its resistance to certain citrus diseases.
The flavor of Citroncirus webberi makes it a sought-after ingredient in gourmet recipes.
Researchers are studying Citroncirus webberi to improve citrus crop varieties.

Etymology

From Latinized genus Citroncirus (from 'citron' and 'Citrus') and the species name honours botanist Webber.
