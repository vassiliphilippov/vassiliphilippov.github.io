neodymium

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːoʊˈdɪmiəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəˈdɪmiəm / (BrE)
A silvery-yellow metallic chemical element of the rare earth group, used especially in powerful magnets and lasers.
rare earth metallic element

Examples

The neodymium magnets in headphones produce high-quality sound due to their strength.
A neodymium laser is often used in eye surgery.
The manufacturer uses neodymium to improve the performance of electric car motors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerium - a chemical element, rare earth metal related to neodymium
  • Synonym: praseodymium - a chemical element closely associated with neodymium

Etymology

From neo- (new) + Latin *Didymium*, a former name for a mixture containing neodymium, from Greek *didymos* (twin).
