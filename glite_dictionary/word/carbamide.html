carbamide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.bəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.maɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed from ammonia and carbon dioxide, found as the main solid component of urine, and used in fertilizers, animal feed, and plastics
chemical compound in urine; fertilizer component
medical

Examples

Carbamide is commonly used in fertilizers to provide plants with a source of nitrogen.
The doctor explained that carbamide is a natural waste product filtered by the kidneys.
Many plastics are manufactured using compounds derived from carbamide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urea - another name for carbamide

Etymology

From French 'carbamide', from 'carbone' (carbon) and 'amide' (compound derived from ammonia).
