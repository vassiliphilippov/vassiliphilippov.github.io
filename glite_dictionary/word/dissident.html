dissident

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (BrE)
a person who opposes official policy, especially that of an authoritarian state or organization
person opposed to official policy

Examples

The government imprisoned several prominent dissidents who had spoken out against its policies.
She became known as a leading dissident in the movement for democratic reform.
Many dissidents fled the country to escape persecution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to accepted behaviour or established practices

Etymology

From Latin 'dissidens', present participle of 'dissidere' (to disagree).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (BrE)
disagreeing with or rejecting accepted beliefs or standards, especially in politics or religion
disagreeing with accepted beliefs

Examples

The dissident views expressed by the scientist challenged the prevailing theories.
She published several dissident articles that questioned government policy.
The organization welcomes dissident voices and encourages debate among its members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - supporting or following accepted beliefs or standards

Etymology

From Latin dissidens, dissidentis (disagreeing), from dis- (apart) + sedere (to sit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01695927-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (BrE)
Disagreeing, especially with the official policy or the majority view.
disagreeing with a majority or authority

Examples

Several dissident voices challenged the council's decision during the meeting.
The government suppressed dissident journalists who disagreed with its policies.
Within the party, he was known for his dissident stance on key issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to accepted behaviour or established practices
  • Synonym: dissent - to disagree or refuse to conform to an official or majority view

Etymology

From Latin dissidens, dissident-, present participle of dissidēre (to disagree).

WordNet ID: oewn-00076345-s
Synset POS: s
