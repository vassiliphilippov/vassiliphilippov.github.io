inaudibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɔdəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɔːdɪbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be heard; so quietly that it is not audible.
so quietly as to be inaudible

Examples

She whispered so inaudibly that no one could make out her words.
The actor mouthed the lines inaudibly during the rehearsal.
He laughed inaudibly, but his eyes sparkled with amusement.

Synset Examples

she spoke inaudibly low

Related Terms

  • Antonym: audibly - in a manner that can be heard

Etymology

From 'inaudible' + '-ly'; 'inaudible' from Latin 'inaudibilis' (unable to be heard).
