Rosh Chodesh

1. NOUN

/ roʊʃ xoʊˈdeɪʃ / (AmE), / rəʊʃ xəʊˈdeɪʃ / (BrE)
(in Judaism) The beginning of each month in the Jewish calendar, observed with special prayers and rituals.
start of a Jewish calendar month

Examples

Many Jewish families gather for a festive meal on Rosh Chodesh to mark the new month.
Special prayers are recited during Rosh Chodesh in synagogues around the world.
The celebration of Rosh Chodesh often includes singing, learning, and community gatherings.

Etymology

From Hebrew רֹאשׁ חוֹדֶשׁ (rōsh ḥōḏeš), meaning 'head of the month.'
