autacoidal

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˌtæˈkɔɪdəl / (AmE), / ɔːˌtæˈkɔɪdəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an autacoid (a substance, such as a hormone, produced locally in tissues and having a physiological effect).
relating to an autacoid
medical

Examples

Recent studies focus on the autacoidal effects of prostaglandins in various organs.
The compound exhibited several autacoidal functions during inflammation.
Scientists are investigating autacoidal mechanisms involved in the regulation of blood flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autacoid - a locally acting, biologically active substance

Etymology

Derived from 'autacoid', itself from Greek 'autos' (self) and 'akos' (remedy), plus the suffix '-al' meaning 'pertaining to'.
