Descurainia pinnata

1. NOUN

/ dɛs.kjʊˈreɪ.ni.ə pɪˈneɪ.tə / (AmE), / dɛs.kjʊˈreɪ.ni.ə pɪˈneɪ.tə / (BrE)
A North American herb with bitter-tasting, pinnately divided leaves resembling those of tansy.
a North American herb with bitter leaves

Examples

Botanists collected samples of Descurainia pinnata from the arid slopes for study.
The bitter leaves of Descurainia pinnata are often compared to those of tansy.
Hikers can encounter Descurainia pinnata in many dry regions across North America.

Etymology

From genus name Descurainia, named after French botanist François Descurain, and specific epithet pinnata from Latin 'pinnatus' meaning 'feathered' or 'pinnate'.
