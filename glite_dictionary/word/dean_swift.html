Dean Swift

1. PROPN

/ din swɪft / (AmE), / diːn swɪft / (BrE)
Jonathan Swift (1667–1745), English satirist known for works such as 'Gulliver's Travels.'
English satirist and author (1667–1745)

Examples

Many of Dean Swift's satirical essays are still studied today for their wit and insight.

Few writers match the legacy of Dean Swift in the realm of political satire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jonathan Swift - The full name of Dean Swift, English satirist (1667–1745)

Etymology

'Dean' refers to his position as Dean of St. Patrick’s Cathedral, Dublin; Swift is his surname.
