monstera

1. NOUN

/ mɒnˈstɛrə / (AmE), / mɒnˈstɪərə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the genus Monstera, often grown as a houseplant for its large, perforated leaves.
tropical climbing plant, houseplant

Examples

She placed a monstera in the corner to add a touch of green to her living room.
The large, distinctive leaves of the monstera make it a favorite among houseplant enthusiasts.
My monstera has started to develop new splits in its leaves this spring.

Etymology

From Latin 'monstrum' meaning 'monster' or 'marvel', referring to the plant's unusual appearance.

2. NOUN

/ mɑnˈstɛrə / (AmE), / mɒnˈstɪərə / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with a cylindrical shape, similar to a pinecone, known for its pineapple-banana flavor; also refers to the plant producing it.
tropical fruit resembling a pinecone

Examples

The monstera fruit is prized for its sweet, pineapple-banana flavor when fully ripe.
She displayed a ripe monstera beside the more familiar tropical fruits at the farmers' market.
Before eating a monstera, you must ensure it is completely ripe to avoid irritation.

Etymology

From modern Latin Monstera, genus name, from Latin *monstrum* (monster, marvel), referencing the plant's unusual leaves.
