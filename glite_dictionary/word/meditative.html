meditative

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛdəˌteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪtətɪv / (BrE)
thinking deeply or seriously about something, often in a calm or absorbed way.
deeply thoughtful; absorbed in thought

Examples

She sat in a meditative silence, lost in her thoughts.
After dinner, his mood became meditative as he stared into the fire.
The painting captured the meditative expression of the monk perfectly.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the ‘Byronic hero’ - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemplative - thinking deeply or in a focused way

Etymology

From Latin 'meditativus', from 'meditari' meaning 'to think over'.
