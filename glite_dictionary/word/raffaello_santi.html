Raffaello Santi

1. PROPN

/ rəˌfɑːˌɛloʊ ˈsanti / (AmE), / raˌfaːˌɛloʊ ˈsanti / (BrE)
(1483–1520) An Italian painter and architect of the High Renaissance, known for his perfectly balanced compositions and influential frescoes.
Italian High Renaissance painter and architect

Examples

The frescoes by Raffaello Santi in the Vatican are renowned for their grace and clarity.
Many art historians regard Raffaello Santi as a master of spatial harmony.
The exhibition features several original sketches by Raffaello Santi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Raphael - Italian painter and architect of the High Renaissance (alternate name)

Etymology

Italian name, also known as Raphael in English, from Latinised form Raphael Sanctius.
