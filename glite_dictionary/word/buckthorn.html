buckthorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Bumelia or related genera, typically having spiny branches and berries
a spiny shrub or small tree with berries

Examples

The gardener warned us that buckthorn bushes could quickly spread across the fence line.
Some species of buckthorn are used in landscaping for their dense growth and spiny branches.
In late summer, the buckthorn produces small, berry-like fruits that attract birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea buckthorn - thorny shrub with orange berries (genus Hippophae)

Etymology

From Middle English 'buctor' or 'boketorne', from 'buck' (male deer) + 'thorn' (prickly shrub), possibly due to the thorny nature of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhamnus, often bearing berries, some species of which are used as a source of yellow dyes or pigments.
a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhamnus

Examples

The buckthorn in the hedge produced brilliant yellow berries in late summer.
Over time, buckthorn has spread invasively through many North American forests.
Dyers once used the fruit of the buckthorn to create natural dyes for fabrics.

Etymology

From Middle English 'boketorn', from 'buck' (male deer) + 'thorn', possibly referring to the thorny nature of the plant and its attractiveness to deer.
