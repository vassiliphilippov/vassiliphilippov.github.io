Astrantia

1. NOUN

/ əˈstræn.ʃə / (AmE), / asˈtrantɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian herbs in the family Apiaceae, known for aromatic roots, palmate leaves, and showy flowers.
genus of Eurasian herbaceous plants

Examples

Several varieties of Astrantia are popular in cottage gardens for their unusual, star-like flowers.
The roots of Astrantia have a distinctive aroma and have been used in traditional medicine.
Botanists have discovered new species of Astrantia growing in alpine regions.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'astron' (star), referring to the star-shaped flowers.
