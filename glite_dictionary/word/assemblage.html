assemblage

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛm.blɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsem.blɪdʒ / (BrE)
a collection or system of components assembled together for a particular purpose
collection of assembled components

Examples

The art installation featured an assemblage of recycled materials.
Engineers designed an assemblage of sensors and cameras to monitor the environment.
The museum displayed an assemblage of rare scientific instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people or things gathered together for a purpose

Etymology

From Old French 'assemblage', from 'assembler' (to assemble).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɛmblɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsɛmblɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of people gathered together in one place
group of people gathered together

Examples

The curator explained the significance of each assemblage in the exhibition hall.
An assemblage of protesters gathered outside the courthouse to demand justice.
A remarkable assemblage of scientists attended the international conference on climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered for a common purpose, especially a formal meeting

From Middle French assemblage (gathering, assembly), from assembler (to assemble).

4. NOUN

/ əˈsɛm.blɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsɛm.blɪdʒ / (BrE)
a collection or gathering of people or things considered as a complete group
a group of things or people collected together

Examples

The art exhibit featured an impressive assemblage of sculptures from around the world.
An assemblage of tools was found at the archaeological site, providing insights into ancient crafts.
The committee is an assemblage of experts from various scientific fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of things gathered together
  • Synonym: aggregation - a group or mass formed by combining several elements

Etymology

From Old French 'assembler', from Latin 'assimulare' (to bring together).
