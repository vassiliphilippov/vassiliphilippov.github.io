lipomatosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪpoʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which excess fatty tissue develops in the form of multiple lipomas throughout the body.
condition marked by multiple fatty tumors
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with lipomatosis after several fatty lumps appeared on her arms and back.
Extensive lipomatosis can lead to discomfort and may require surgical intervention.
Familial lipomatosis tends to manifest in adulthood and is often inherited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lipoma - a benign tumor made of fatty tissue

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'lipoma' (fatty tumor) and the suffix '-osis' (indicating a condition).
