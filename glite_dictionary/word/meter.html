meter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
The accent or rhythmic structure in a metrical foot of verse, forming the basis of a poem's rhythm.
accent or rhythm in verse foot

Examples

The poet carefully arranged each foot to maintain a consistent meter throughout the stanza.
Classical verse often relies heavily on meter to create musical effects in the poem.
She studied the differences between iambic and trochaic meter in her literature class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythm - The pattern of sounds in poetry or music
  • Synonym: beat - A rhythmic unit in poetry or music

Etymology

From Greek 'metron' (measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
the basic unit of length in the International System of Units, equal to approximately 39.37 inches or 1.094 yards
unit of length (SI)

Examples

The 100-meter race is one of the highlights of the Olympic Games.
The table is two meters long and one meter wide.
He measured the room and found it was exactly five meters in length.

6. NOUN

/ ˈmitər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
The rhythmic structure in poetry or music as determined by the division into parts of equal duration.
rhythmic structure in verse or music

Examples

The poem's meter gives it a musical quality that captivates readers.
Classical composers paid close attention to the meter of their works.
Learning to scan a line is essential for understanding its meter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythm - a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound
  • Synonym: cadence - the rhythmic flow of a sequence of sounds or words

Etymology

From Greek 'metron' (measure) via Latin 'metrum'.
