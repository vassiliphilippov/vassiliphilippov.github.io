Mormonism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːr.məˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.məˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
the religious doctrines, beliefs, and practices of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and related groups, based chiefly on the Book of Mormon
religious beliefs of the Mormon Church

Examples

She studied Mormonism as part of a comparative religions course at university.
The influence of Mormonism is especially strong in Utah and surrounding states.
His family converted to Mormonism when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Christianity - the religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Mormon (the Book of Mormon) + -ism (denoting practice or system).
