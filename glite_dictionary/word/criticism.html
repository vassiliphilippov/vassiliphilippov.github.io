criticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of expressing disapproval by pointing out faults or shortcomings
expression of disapproval by pointing out faults

Examples

Her report was met with harsh criticism from her colleagues.
The new film has attracted a lot of criticism for its lack of originality.
He accepted the criticism and promised to improve his performance.

Synset Examples

the senator received severe criticism from his opponent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • Synonym: disapproval - expression of unfavorable opinion

Etymology

From Greek kritikē (the art of judgment) via Latin and French.

WordNet ID: oewn-06723485-n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪt̬.ɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪt.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the act of carefully analyzing and making a judgment about the merits and faults of something
serious examination and judgment

Examples

Her manager offered helpful criticism of her presentation.
Art students are taught how to give and receive criticism.
Constructive criticism can help people improve their skills.

Synset Examples

constructive criticism is always appreciated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an evaluation or assessment of something
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Greek *kritikos* (able to discern), via Latin *criticus* and French *critique*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05741963-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪt̬ɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
a written or spoken evaluation of a literary, artistic, or other creative work, especially one that analyzes and judges its merits and faults
evaluation of a creative work

Examples

Her criticism of the new novel was published in the literary magazine.
The play received glowing criticism from several well-known reviewers.
He specialized in art criticism and wrote many essays on modern painters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an article or report that gives an opinion about a book, play, film, etc.

Etymology

From Greek kritikē (the art of judgment), via Latin and French.

Synset POS: n
