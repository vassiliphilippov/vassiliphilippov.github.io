corrections

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛk.ʃənz / (AmE), / kəˈrɛk.ʃənz / (BrE)
the branch of government responsible for overseeing the punishment, treatment, and supervision of people convicted of crimes
government body managing convicted offenders

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in corrections after studying criminal justice.
The state corrections department announced new rehabilitation programs for inmates.
Budget cuts have affected staffing levels in corrections across the country.

Synset Examples

for a career in corrections turn to the web site of the New Jersey Department of Corrections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penitentiary - a prison for people convicted of serious crimes
  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime

Etymology

From correction + -s, referring to the correction (rehabilitation) of offenders.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkʃənz / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkʃənz / (BrE)
the system responsible for supervising and rehabilitating offenders through imprisonment, probation, parole, and related measures
system for supervising and rehabilitating offenders

Examples

He is pursuing a career in corrections, working to help rehabilitate former inmates.
The department of corrections announced new policies on parole and probation.
Effective corrections programs focus as much on rehabilitation as on punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penology - the study of prison management and criminal rehabilitation
  • Synonym: criminal justice system - the system of law enforcement, courts, and corrections

Etymology

From 'correction' meaning punishment or discipline; modern usage arose in criminology in the 20th century.
