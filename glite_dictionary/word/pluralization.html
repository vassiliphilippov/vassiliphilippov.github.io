pluralization

1. NOUN

/ plʊr.ə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / plʊr.ə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making a word, especially a noun, plural by changing its form according to grammar rules
process of making plural forms

Examples

The pluralization of English nouns usually involves adding '-s' or '-es' to the end of the word.
Irregular forms can make pluralization challenging for language learners.
This grammar lesson focuses on the pluralization of nouns ending in 'y' or 'f'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plural - the form of a word that refers to more than one person or thing
  • Antonym: singularization - the act of making singular or attributing singularity to

Etymology

From 'pluralize' + '-ation', based on Latin 'pluralis' (plural) and '-atio' (action or process).
