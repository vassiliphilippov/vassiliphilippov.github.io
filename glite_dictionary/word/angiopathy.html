angiopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiˈɑːpəθi / (AmE), / ˌandʒiˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
any disease affecting the blood vessels or lymphatic vessels
disease of blood or lymph vessels
medical

Examples

Diabetic angiopathy can cause significant damage to both small and large blood vessels.
The neurologist explained that cerebral angiopathy was responsible for the patient's symptoms.
Researchers are developing new treatments to slow the progression of angiopathy in individuals with hypertension.

Etymology

From Greek 'angeion' (vessel) + 'pathy' (disease or suffering).
