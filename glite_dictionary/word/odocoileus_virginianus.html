Odocoileus virginianus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəˌsɔɪˈliːəs ˌvɜːrdʒɪˈneɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˌsɔɪˈliːəs ˌvɜːdʒɪˈneɪnəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the white-tailed deer, a common North American deer species with a tail that has a white underside
scientific name for white-tailed deer

Examples

The Odocoileus virginianus is widely distributed throughout North America from Canada to Central America.
Wildlife biologists often study Odocoileus virginianus populations to monitor ecosystem health.
The distinctive white underside of the tail makes Odocoileus virginianus easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed deer - common North American deer known for its white tail underside

Etymology

From Latin: Odocoileus from Greek ὀδούς (odous, tooth) + κοῖλος (koilos, hollow); virginianus, Latin for 'of Virginia'.
