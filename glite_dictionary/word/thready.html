thready

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛdi / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdi / (BrE)
(of a substance) forming thin, viscous, or glutinous threads when stretched or pulled.
forming glutinous or viscous threads

Examples

The mucus appeared thready when examined under the microscope.
The egg white became thready as it was slowly lifted by the fork.
In some infections, sputum may have a thready consistency, making it sticky and difficult to expel.

Etymology

From thread + -y, meaning 'having the characteristics of a thread'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛdi / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdi / (BrE)
thin in diameter or resembling a thread; fine, slender, or filamentous.
thin or thread-like

Examples

The old curtain hung in thready strips from the window frame.
She examined the thready roots of the plant under the microscope.
The nurse noticed the patient's pulse was weak and thready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threadlike - resembling or like a thread
  • Degree Lower: thin - having little thickness or depth

Etymology

From 'thread' + '-y', indicating resemblance to thread.
