Jacques Cartier

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑk karˈtjeɪ / (AmE), / ʒak kaʁˈtje / (BrE)
(1491–1557) A French explorer who navigated the St. Lawrence River and claimed the surrounding territory for France.
French explorer of the St. Lawrence River

Examples

Jacques Cartier made three voyages to Canada in the 16th century.
The St. Lawrence River was first explored by Jacques Cartier in 1534.
Many places in Canada bear the name of Jacques Cartier to honor his exploration.
