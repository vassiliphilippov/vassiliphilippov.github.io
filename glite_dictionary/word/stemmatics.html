stemmatics

1. NOUN

/ stɛmˈmætɪks / (AmE), / stɛmˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the study of the relationships between surviving manuscripts of a text in order to reconstruct its original form and transmission history
study of manuscript relationships and text reconstruction

Examples

Scholars used stemmatics to trace the lineage of the medieval manuscripts.
A sound understanding of stemmatics is crucial for editing ancient texts.
The field of stemmatics often overlaps with linguistic and historical research.

Synset Examples

stemmatology also plays an important role in musicology
transcription errors are of decisive importance in stemmatics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textual criticism - scholarly study of manuscripts to determine the original form of a text

Etymology

From Greek 'stemma' (wreath, pedigree) + English '-ics'.
