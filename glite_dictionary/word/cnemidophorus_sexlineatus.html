Cnemidophorus sexlineatus

1. NOUN

/ nɛmɪˌdɑːfəˈrəs sɛksˌlɪniˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / nɛmɪˌdɒfəˈrəs sɛksˌlɪniˈɑːtəs / (BrE)
A very swift lizard species, commonly known as the six-lined racerunner, found in eastern and central United States.
species of swift lizard in the US

Examples

The Cnemidophorus sexlineatus can be seen darting across open fields in summer.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of Cnemidophorus sexlineatus in the southeastern United States.
The six-lined racerunner, or Cnemidophorus sexlineatus, is known for its remarkable speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: six-lined racerunner - common name for Cnemidophorus sexlineatus

Etymology

From Greek 'knemid' (greave, leg armor) and 'phoros' (bearing), Latin 'sex' (six) and 'lineatus' (lined).
