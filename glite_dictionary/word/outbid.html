to outbid

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈbɪd / (AmE), / aʊtˈbɪd / (BrE)
to offer to pay a higher price than someone else, especially at an auction
bid higher than another; surpass in offering

Examples

At the auction, she outbid everyone else for the rare painting.
If another buyer tries to outbid you, will you increase your offer?
They outbid their competitors and secured the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outoffer - to offer more than someone else
  • Antonym: to underbid - to bid lower than someone else

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'to surpass') + 'bid'.

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈbɪd / (AmE), / aʊtˈbɪd / (BrE)
to offer more money than someone else in order to buy something, especially at an auction, or to bid higher than another player in a game.
offer more than another bidder

Examples

She managed to outbid all the other buyers at the auction.
If you want the painting, you'll need to outbid your competitors.
He was disappointed when someone outbid him at the last second.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underbid - to bid less than another bidder

Etymology

From the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'to exceed') + 'bid'.
