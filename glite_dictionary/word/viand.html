viand

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪənd / (AmE), / ˈvaɪənd / (BrE)
a choice or delicious dish of food, often used to refer to an item of fine or delicately prepared food
delicacy or choice food dish

Examples

The chef prepared a special viand for the evening guests.
Among the many viands on the table, the truffle risotto was the most sought after.
At the banquet, every viand was exquisitely presented and carefully seasoned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicacy - a rare or luxurious food; a delicacy

Etymology

From Old French 'viande' (food), from Latin 'vivenda' (things for living), from 'vivere' (to live).
