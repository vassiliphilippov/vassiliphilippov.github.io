Rosaceae

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈseɪˌʃiˌi / (AmE), / rəʊˈseɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including roses, apples, cherries, and many others, typically having alternate leaves and five-petaled flowers with numerous stamens
family of flowering plants (roses, apples, etc.)

Examples

The Rosaceae family includes popular fruit trees such as apples and cherries.
Botanists study the characteristics that distinguish Rosaceae from other plant families.
Many ornamental garden plants, including roses, belong to the Rosaceae.

Etymology

From Latin 'rosaceae', from 'rosa' (rose).
