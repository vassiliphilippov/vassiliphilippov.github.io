parishioner

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪʃənər / (AmE), / pəˈrɪʃənə / (BrE)
a member of a parish, especially one who attends or belongs to a particular church
member of a parish

Examples

Every Sunday, the parishioner would bring flowers to decorate the church altar.
The priest thanked each parishioner for their generous support during the fundraiser.
As a long-time parishioner, she volunteered to help with community events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parish - a local church community or its district

Etymology

From Old French 'paroissien', from 'paroisse' (parish) and ultimately from Late Latin 'parochia'.
