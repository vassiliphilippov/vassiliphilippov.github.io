Aythya valisineria

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪθiə ˌvælɪsɪˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˈaɪθiə ˌvælɪsɪˈnɪəriə / (BrE)
A North American wild duck, also known as the canvasback, prized for hunting and as a game bird.
species of North American wild duck

Examples

The Aythya valisineria migrates across North America and is highly sought after by birdwatchers.
Hunting regulations have helped protect the population of Aythya valisineria during the winter season.
Unlike the mallard, the Aythya valisineria prefers to feed on wild celery in wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canvasback - another name for Aythya valisineria, a species of North American wild duck
  • Degree Lower: mallard - a common wild duck, Anas platyrhynchos

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the duck genus 'Aythya' and species name 'valisineria', after the wild celery 'Vallisneria', an important food for this duck.
