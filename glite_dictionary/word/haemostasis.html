haemostasis

1. NOUN

/ hiːmoʊˈsteɪsɪs / (AmE), / hɪˌmɒsˈteɪsɪs / (BrE)
The stopping of bleeding or the flow of blood, especially by a surgical procedure or natural process.
stopping of bleeding
medical british

Examples

The surgeon achieved haemostasis before closing the incision.
Prompt haemostasis is essential during major operations to prevent excessive blood loss.
Various agents may be used to promote haemostasis in difficult surgical cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemostasis - The stopping of bleeding or the flow of blood (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + stasis (standing still).
