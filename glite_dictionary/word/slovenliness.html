slovenliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslʌvənlinəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌvənlinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being habitually untidy, dirty, or careless in appearance or habits
habitual untidiness or dirtiness

Examples

His slovenliness was evident from the messy state of his bedroom.
She was criticized for her slovenliness at work, especially for leaving papers scattered everywhere.
Despite his intelligence, his slovenliness made a poor impression during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neatness - the quality of being tidy and orderly

Etymology

From sloven + -liness; 'sloven' likely from Middle French 'slovain', meaning a careless person.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslʌvənlinəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌvənlinəs / (BrE)
The quality of being untidy, careless, or lacking order or cleanliness.
untidiness; lack of care or order

Examples

His slovenliness was evident from the clutter scattered across his desk.
The manager could not tolerate slovenliness in the workplace and insisted on strict cleanliness.
Her habitual slovenliness eventually led to an intervention from her concerned friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neatness - the quality of being tidy and orderly
  • Synonym: disorderliness - the quality of lacking order or organization

Etymology

From sloven + -liness, with 'sloven' from Middle French *sloven* (a careless person).
