Tacitus

1. PROPN

/ ˈtæsɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈtæsɪtəs / (BrE)
A Roman historian (56–120 AD) known for his influential works on the history and governance of the Roman Empire.
Roman historian, author of influential works

Examples

Many consider Tacitus one of the greatest Roman historians for his incisive analysis of imperial Rome.
The works of Tacitus offer valuable insights into the politics of the early Roman Empire.
Historians often reference Tacitus when studying the reigns of emperors like Tiberius and Nero.

Etymology

From Latin Tacitus, his Roman family name, meaning 'silent'.
