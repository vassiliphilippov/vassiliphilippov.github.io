modelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒd(ə)lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of representing something, especially on a smaller scale or in a simplified form, often for study or demonstration
representation, often on a smaller scale

Examples

The architect created a modelling of the new building for the presentation.
Accurate modelling helps scientists predict the effects of climate change.
Students practiced modelling molecules to understand their structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a small copy or representation of something
  • Synonym: simulation - the imitation of a situation or process

Etymology

From 'model' + '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːd(ə)lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒd(ə)lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of creating a preliminary sculpture in wax or clay, often to serve as a model for a final work
making preliminary sculptures for finished works

Examples

The artist spent hours on the modelling of the figure before beginning the marble carving.
In classical sculpture, modelling in clay was a crucial step prior to casting in bronze.
Her skills in modelling allowed her to experiment with different poses before completing the final piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpture - the art of making two- or three-dimensional representative or abstract forms

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a sculpture or artwork created by shaping a pliable material such as clay or wax
sculpture made by shaping material

Examples

The artist's latest exhibition features modelling in clay to capture delicate details.
Ancient civilizations often used modelling techniques to create religious figures and idols.
Her passion for modelling led her to sculpt impressive busts out of wax.

Etymology

From model + -ing, referring to the act or result of forming a model.
