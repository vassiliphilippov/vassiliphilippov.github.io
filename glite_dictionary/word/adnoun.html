adnoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈædˌnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈad.naʊn / (BrE)
A word, usually an adjective, that is used as a noun; for example, 'the meek' in 'the meek shall inherit the earth'.
an adjective used as a noun

Examples

In the phrase 'the rich and the poor,' both 'rich' and 'poor' are functioning as adnouns.
The term adnoun is rarely used outside of grammar discussions.
When an adjective stands alone to represent people, it is called an adnoun.

Synset Examples

‘meek’ in ‘blessed are the meek’ is an adnoun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantive - a noun or word functioning as a noun, especially in older grammar

Etymology

From Latin ad- (to) + noun, modeled after 'adjective'.
