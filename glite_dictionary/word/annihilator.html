annihilator

1. NOUN

/ əˈnaɪ.əˌleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əˈnaɪ.ɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person or thing that completely destroys something or brings it to nothing
one who totally destroys something

Examples

The new weapon was feared as the ultimate annihilator on the battlefield.
In the story, the villain unleashed an ancient annihilator upon the city.
Scientists worried that the device could become an accidental annihilator if not handled carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyer - a person or thing that destroys something

Etymology

From Latin 'annihilare' (to reduce to nothing) + '-ator' (agent noun suffix).
