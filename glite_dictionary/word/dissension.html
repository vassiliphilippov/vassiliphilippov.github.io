dissension

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
disagreement or strong difference of opinion, especially leading to conflict within a group or organization
disagreement; conflict within a group

Examples

There was growing dissension within the committee over the proposed changes.
The coach resigned after months of dissension among the players.
Efforts to resolve the dissension between management and staff ultimately failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony
  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord within a group

Etymology

From Latin 'dissensio', from 'dissentire' (to disagree).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
Disagreement or discord among people who are expected to work together.
disagreement among group members

Examples

There was growing dissension within the committee over the new policy.
Long-standing dissension among the team members led to poor performance.
Efforts to resolve the dissension between the two departments were unsuccessful.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accord among people or parties
  • Synonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony; conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'dissensio', from 'dissentire' (to disagree).
