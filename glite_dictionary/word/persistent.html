persistent

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(of a feeling, memory, or impression) continually recurring to the mind; difficult to forget or ignore.
continually recurring to the mind

Examples

The persistent memories of her childhood made it hard to move on.
Every night, a persistent tune echoed in his mind long after the concert ended.
She was troubled by a persistent sense of déjà vu whenever she entered the house.

Synset Examples

haunting memories
the cathedral organ and the distant voices have a haunting beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunting - continually recurring to the mind or difficult to forget

Etymology

From Latin persistens, present participle of persistere (to continue steadfastly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01044226-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(of leaves, gills, or similar parts) remaining attached to a plant or animal beyond the usual time of shedding.
not shed, remains attached (botanical/zoological)

Examples

In some evergreen species, persistent leaves help retain nutrients throughout the year.
The flower's persistent calyx is still visible long after the petals have fallen off.
Many mushrooms have persistent gills that remain after the fruit body matures.

Synset Examples

persistent leaves remain attached past maturity
the persistent gills of fishes

Etymology

From Latin 'persistens', present participle of 'persistere' (to persist, to stand firm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01762851-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ pərˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action in spite of difficulty or opposition; stubbornly unyielding
stubbornly unyielding; refusing to give up

Examples

She remained persistent despite repeated failures and criticism from her peers.
His persistent demands eventually wore down the negotiators and forced a compromise.
Only the most persistent students succeeded in completing the challenging experiment.

Synset Examples

dogged persistence
dour determination
the most vocal and pertinacious of all the critics
a mind not gifted to discover truth but tenacious to hold it
men tenacious of opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenacious - holding fast; not easily discouraged or stopped
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • Antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; easily influenced

Etymology

From Latin persistens, persistentis, present participle of persistere (to continue steadfastly).

/ pərˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
continuing firmly or obstinately over a prolonged period; never-ceasing or recurring constantly.
never-ceasing; continuing firmly

Examples

The persistent noise from the construction kept everyone awake all night.
Despite setbacks, her persistent efforts eventually paid off.
A persistent cough can indicate an underlying health issue.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unremitting - never relaxing or slackening; persistent

Etymology
