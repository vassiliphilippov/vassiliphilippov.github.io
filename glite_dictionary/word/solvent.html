solvent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlvənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒlvənt / (BrE)
a liquid that is able to dissolve other substances, forming a solution
liquid that dissolves other substances

Examples

Water is a common solvent used in chemistry laboratories.
The paint solvent quickly dissolved the stubborn stain on the brush.
Acetone is a powerful solvent often used to clean laboratory equipment.

Synset Examples

the solvent does not change its state in forming a solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solute - the substance dissolved in a solvent to form a solution
  • Synonym: solution - a mixture formed when a solute is dissolved in a solvent

Etymology

From Latin solvō, solvere (to loosen, dissolve).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːlvənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒlvənt / (BrE)
(of a person, business, or organization) having enough money to pay all debts and meet financial obligations.
able to pay debts

Examples

The company remained solvent despite the economic downturn.
It is important for individuals to stay solvent and avoid excessive debt.
After restructuring its loans, the business became solvent again.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts

Etymology

From Latin 'solvent-', present participle of 'solvere' (to loosen, pay).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
