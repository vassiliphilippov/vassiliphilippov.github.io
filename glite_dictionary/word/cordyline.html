Cordyline

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪlaɪn / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees and shrubs native to Asia and the Pacific, often grown as ornamental plants, noted for their ability to regrow from trunk fragments.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Cordyline in her garden survived the harsh winter and produced new shoots in spring.
Many gardeners choose Cordyline for its colorful foliage and easy propagation.
After the storm, they planted fragments of the Cordyline trunk to grow new plants.

Etymology

From the Greek *kordyle* (club), referring to the thickened rootstock in some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪliːn / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs native to Asia and the Pacific, known for their ability to regrow from trunk fragments.
genus of Asiatic and Pacific trees or shrubs

Examples

Gardeners appreciate Cordyline for its colorful foliage and easy propagation.
The Cordyline australis is often called the cabbage tree in New Zealand.
Fragments of a Cordyline trunk can regrow into new plants under the right conditions.

Etymology

From Greek 'kordyle' (club), referring to the club-shaped roots of some species.
