Necturus

1. NOUN

/ nɛkˈtʊr.əs / (AmE), / nɛkˈtjʊər.əs / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic salamanders in the family Proteidae, commonly known as mudpuppies, native to North America.
genus of North American aquatic salamanders

Examples

The Necturus is often studied for its unique regenerative abilities.
Researchers found several Necturus specimens in the river during their fieldwork.
Unlike many other amphibians, Necturus retains its external gills throughout its life.

Etymology

From Greek nektēr (swimmer) + oura (tail), referring to its swimming tail.
