Alismataceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪs.məˈteɪ.siː / (AmE), / əˌlɪs.məˈtiː.siː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic or marsh plants, including water plantains, typically found in fresh water or wet environments
family of aquatic or marsh plants

Examples

The Alismataceae family includes many common water plants found in wetlands.
Scientists conducted a study on local species belonging to the Alismataceae in the river delta.
Several members of the Alismataceae are cultivated as ornamental pond plants.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on genus Alisma and the suffix -aceae used in plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ əˌlɪs.məˈteɪ.si.i / (AmE), / əˌlɪs.məˈtæ.siː / (BrE)
a family of perennial or annual aquatic or marsh plants, including the water-plantains, found in both temperate and tropical regions
family of aquatic or marsh plants

Examples

Members of the Alismataceae can often be found in shallow lakes and wetlands.
The Alismataceae family includes many species commonly known as water-plantains.
Botanists study the Alismataceae to understand adaptations to aquatic environments.

Etymology

From the genus name Alisma, from Greek 'alisma' (water-plantain), with the botanical Latin suffix '-aceae' indicating a plant family.
