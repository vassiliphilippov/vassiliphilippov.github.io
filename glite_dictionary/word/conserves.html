conserves

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝvz / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvz / (BrE)
A type of preserve made by cooking whole or large pieces of fruit with sugar.
fruit preserve made with sugar

Examples

Strawberry conserves make a delicious addition to toast or scones.
She gifted several jars of homemade conserves to her neighbors during the holidays.
Apricot conserves pair well with cheese on a charcuterie board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - sweet spread made from fruit and sugar
  • Synonym: marmalade - jelly-like preserve made with citrus fruit

Etymology

From French 'conserver', from Latin 'conservare' (to preserve, conserve).
