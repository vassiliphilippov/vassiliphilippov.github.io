Hesperiphona vespertina

1. NOUN

/ hɛsˌpɛrɪˈfoʊnə vəˌspɜːrˈtiːnə / (AmE), / hɛsˌpɛrɪˈfəʊnə vɛˌspɜːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A North American grosbeak, commonly known as the evening grosbeak, characterized by its thick bill and yellow, black, and white plumage.
North American grosbeak (evening grosbeak)

Examples

The brilliant yellow and black plumage of the Hesperiphona vespertina makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Birdwatchers reported a large flock of Hesperiphona vespertina visiting their feeders this winter.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Hesperiphona vespertina across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening grosbeak - The common name for Hesperiphona vespertina

Etymology

From Greek 'hesperos' (evening) + 'phōnē' (sound/voice), reflecting its name 'evening grosbeak'.
