to pant-hoot

1. VERB

/ ˈpæntˌhuːt / (AmE), / ˈpæntˌhuːt / (BrE)
(of certain primates) to communicate by making a vocalization consisting of a series of hoots combined with rapid panting sounds.
primate vocalization: hoot and pant sequence

Examples

The chimpanzees began to pant-hoot loudly, filling the jungle with their calls.
Researchers recorded the silverback gorilla as it pant-hooted at sunrise.
Young primates often pant-hoot during play as a form of communication.

Etymology

Formed from 'pant' + 'hoot', describing the sound made.
