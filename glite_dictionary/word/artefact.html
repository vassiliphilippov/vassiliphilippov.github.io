artefact

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑr.t̬ə.fækt / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.fækt / (BrE)
An object made by a human being, typically one of historical or cultural interest.
human-made object, often historical

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient artefact that was unearthed during the excavation.
Each artefact in the collection has its own unique story.
Researchers carefully cleaned the fragile artefact for further analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artifact - alternative spelling, especially American

Etymology

From Latin 'arte factum', meaning 'something made with skill'.
