trigger-happy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ ˌhæpi / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə ˌhæpi / (BrE)
ready or eager to use violence or force, especially by shooting quickly without thinking; inclined to act rashly or aggressively.
eager or ready to use violence or act rashly

Examples

The soldier was accused of being trigger-happy during patrols in the tense border region.
Some politicians fear trigger-happy police responses could escalate the situation unnecessarily.
His trigger-happy attitude in meetings made teamwork challenging for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

fierce loyalty
in a tearing rage
vehement dislike
violent passions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsive - acting or done without forethought
  • Antonym: restrained - controlled, not impulsive or excessive

Etymology

Formed in the mid-20th century from "trigger" (the part of a gun that is pulled to fire) and "happy".

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ ˌhæpi / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə ˌhæpi / (BrE)
tending to use firearms or resort to violence irresponsibly or with excessive eagerness; also, inclined to act aggressively or rashly without due consideration.
eager to use firearms or act aggressively

Examples

The general warned his troops not to become trigger-happy during the tense standoff.
Some critics say the police department has a trigger-happy reputation.
He grew concerned about his trigger-happy neighbor after hearing frequent gunshots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - without care for consequences or danger
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid danger or risk

Etymology

Originating in the US in the 1940s, from 'trigger' (of a gun) + 'happy' (inclined toward).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
