Monarda fistulosa

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɑːrdə fɪˈstjuːloʊsə / (AmE), / məˈnɑːdə fɪˈstjʊlɒsə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to North America, also known as wild bergamot, with aromatic leaves and purplish flowers.
North American perennial herb (wild bergamot)

Examples

The garden was filled with Monarda fistulosa, attracting bees and butterflies in the summer.
Herbal tea made from Monarda fistulosa is prized for its minty flavor and calming properties.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Monarda fistulosa growing in prairie habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild bergamot - another common name for Monarda fistulosa

Etymology

From the genus name 'Monarda', honoring Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes, and Latin 'fistulosa' meaning 'tube-shaped'.
