Entomophthorales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˌmɑːfˈθɔːrəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˌɛntəmɒfˈθɔːrəliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi within the class Zygomycetes, characterized by their parasitic nature, especially on insects and other arthropods
order of parasitic fungi

Examples

Many species in the Entomophthorales are known for infecting and killing insects.
Biologists have studied the Entomophthorales for their role in controlling pest populations naturally.
Certain members of Entomophthorales are used as biological control agents against fly infestations.

Etymology

From Greek 'entomon' (insect) + 'phthora' (destruction) + Latin plural '-ales' (denoting orders).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˌmɑfθəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌɛntəmɒfθəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi within the class Zygomycetes, coextensive with the family Entomophthoraceae, known primarily as insect pathogens
order of fungi, insect pathogens

Examples

Many species in the Entomophthorales are known for infecting insects and other arthropods.
Scientists study members of the Entomophthorales to develop biological pest control methods.
The distinctive life cycle of Entomophthorales sets them apart from other fungal orders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Entomophthoraceae - family of fungi corresponding to Entomophthorales

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'entomo-' (insect) + Greek 'phthorá' (destruction), referring to their association with insects.
