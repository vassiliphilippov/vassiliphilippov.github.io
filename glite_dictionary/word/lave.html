to lave

1. VERB

/ leɪv / (AmE), / leɪv / (BrE)
to wash or cleanse (oneself or something), especially with water or other liquid
to wash or cleanse with water
archaic

Examples

She laved her hands in the cool stream after the long walk.
The old poet wrote of lovers who lave their bodies in scented baths.
He laved his face with fresh water to wake himself up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean with water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'lavare' (to wash), via Old French and Middle English.

/ leɪv / (AmE), / leɪv / (BrE)
(literary) to wash or bathe something, especially with a gentle or flowing motion; to flow over or against.
to wash or flow against (literary)

Examples

Cool water laved her tired feet after the long walk.
The small stream laves the base of the old stone bridge.
