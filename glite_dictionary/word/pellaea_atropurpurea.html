Pellaea atropurpurea

1. NOUN

/ pəˈliː.ə ˌæ.trə.pɚˈpɜːr.i.ə / (AmE), / pəˈliː.ə ˌæ.trə.pɜːˈpjʊə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A species of small fern native to North America, typically growing on cliffs or walls, with dark, glossy leaf stems and shallowly creeping roots.
a North American fern species with dark glossy stems

Examples

The rare Pellaea atropurpurea thrives on rocky cliffs where few other plants can survive.
Botanists discovered a colony of Pellaea atropurpurea growing along the old stone wall.
I admired the dark, shiny stems of the Pellaea atropurpurea during our hike in the canyon.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pellaea' (genus name) and 'atropurpurea' (meaning 'dark purple').
