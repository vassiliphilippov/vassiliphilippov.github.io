dimmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmər / (AmE), / ˈdɪmə / (BrE)
a device that adjusts the brightness of an electric light by varying the current passing through it
device to adjust light brightness

Examples

We installed a dimmer in the living room to control the lighting.
She turned the dimmer down to create a cozier atmosphere during dinner.
Modern LED bulbs are often compatible with a standard dimmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rheostat - an electrical instrument used to vary resistance

Etymology

From 'dim' (to make less bright) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
