oleomargarine

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊliˌoʊˈmɑrdʒərən / (AmE), / ˌəʊliəˈmɑːdʒəˌriːn / (BrE)
A spread made chiefly from vegetable oils, used as a substitute for butter; margarine.
vegetable oil butter substitute

Examples

My grandmother always preferred oleomargarine to butter for baking.
Decades ago, laws required restaurants to label dishes made with oleomargarine.
Some people say oleomargarine has a different taste than regular margarine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margarine - A spread made from vegetable oils as a substitute for butter

Etymology

From Latin 'oleum' (oil) + Greek 'margaron' (pearl, used for 'margarine').
