trichion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪkiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkɪɒn / (BrE)
the point on the forehead where the hairline meets its midpoint, used as a reference in anatomy
anatomical point at the hairline midpoint on the forehead
medical

Examples

The surgeon marked the trichion as a reference before making the incision.
Researchers measured the distance from the trichion to various facial landmarks.
The trichion can shift position in patients with receding hairlines.

Etymology

From Greek 'trix' (hair).
