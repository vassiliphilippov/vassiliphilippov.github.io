quango

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkwæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
an organization that is funded by the government but operates independently, often used for public services or regulatory purposes.
semi-independent government-funded organization

Examples

The government's decision to cut funding has left several quangos struggling to operate.
Critics argue that too many quangos lead to a lack of accountability in public spending.
She was recently appointed to the board of a major quango overseeing environmental regulation.

Etymology

Acronym from 'quasi-autonomous non-governmental organization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːŋˌgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkwæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
An organization that is financed by the government but operates independently and is not a direct part of government administration.
organization funded by government but independent
british

Examples

The government announced new guidelines for all quangos operating in the health sector.
Several quangos will be merged to reduce administrative costs next year.
Debates about funding often involve discussions of quango efficiency and accountability.

Synset Examples

The new policy will affect the funding of several quangos.

Etymology

Acronym from 'quasi-autonomous non-governmental organization'.
