Fagus sylvatica purpurea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə pɜrˈpjʊriə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə pɜːˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of the European beech tree distinguished by its glossy purple or copper-colored leaves.
beech variety with purple leaves

Examples

The garden was dominated by a majestic Fagus sylvatica purpurea with striking purple foliage.
Botanists admire the unique coloration of Fagus sylvatica purpurea in spring and summer.
Many parks feature at least one mature Fagus sylvatica purpurea as a centerpiece tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European beech - species of beech tree native to Europe (Fagus sylvatica)

Etymology

From Latin: Fagus (beech), sylvatica (of the woods), purpurea (purple).
