absolutisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.səˌluː.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˌluː.tɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something absolute, or treating something as absolute
the act of making absolute
british

Examples

The absolutisation of moral values can lead to intolerance.
In academic debates, absolutisation of particular viewpoints often hinders constructive dialogue.
The philosopher warned against the absolutisation of personal beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutization - the act or process of making something absolute (American spelling)

Etymology

From absolute + -isation (British spelling of -ization), meaning 'to render absolute'.
