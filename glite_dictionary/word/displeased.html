displeased

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈplizd / (AmE), / dɪsˈpliːzd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing annoyance or dissatisfaction; not pleased or satisfied.
feeling annoyance or dissatisfaction

Examples

She looked displeased when she heard the news.
The manager was clearly displeased with the team's performance.
His displeased expression made everyone in the room uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleased - feeling or showing satisfaction or pleasure
  • Synonym: annoyed - slightly angry or irritated
  • Antonym: content - feeling happy or satisfied

Etymology

From the verb 'displease', which is from Old French 'desplaire', from Latin 'displacere' (to displease).
