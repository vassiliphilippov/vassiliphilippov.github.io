Pineus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪniːəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪniːəs / (BrE)
A genus of aphids in the family Adelgidae, known to feed on pine trees.
genus of aphids feeding on pines

Examples

The species in the genus Pineus can cause significant damage to commercial pine forests.
Entomologists discovered a new Pineus colony on the young pine shoots.
Infestations of Pineus are managed through careful monitoring and occasionally, by biological controls.

Etymology

From Latin 'pinus' (pine), referring to the pine trees these insects inhabit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪniəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪniːəs / (BrE)
A genus of small aphid-like insects in the family Adelgidae, commonly found on pine trees.
a genus of insects in Adelgidae found on pines

Examples

Infestation by Pineus can be harmful to commercial pine plantations.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Pineus in northern forests.
The Pineus insects were observed causing needle loss on several pine trees.

Etymology

From Latin 'pinus' (pine), referring to the host plants of the genus.
