Umbelliferae

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˌbɛlɪˈfɛriː / (AmE), / ʌmˌbɛlɪˈfɪəriː / (BrE)
a former botanical family name for plants having flowers arranged in umbels, including parsley, carrot, anise, caraway, celery, and dill
former plant family name including parsley and carrot

Examples

Carrots and parsley are both members of the Umbelliferae family.
Many herbs in the Umbelliferae have aromatic seeds used in cooking.
Botanists now prefer to call the Umbelliferae family Apiaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apiaceae - current botanical family name for umbelliferous plants

Etymology

From Latin 'umbella' (a little shadow, parasol) and '-ferae' (bearing).
