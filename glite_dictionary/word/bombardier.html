bombardier

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːm.bɚˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌbɒm.bəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a noncommissioned officer of equivalent rank to corporal in the British artillery
British artillery rank; noncommissioned officer
british

Examples

He was promoted to bombardier after displaying exceptional leadership in the field.
The bombardier was responsible for ensuring the artillery unit was ready to fire.
As a bombardier, she took great pride in her regiment's traditions.

Etymology

From French *bombardier* (artillerist), from *bombarde* (cannon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːmbɑːrˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌbɒmbəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a member of a bomber aircraft crew responsible for aiming and releasing bombs during a mission
member of bomber crew who releases bombs

Examples

The bombardier carefully aligned the bombsight with the target before releasing the payload.
During World War II, the bombardier played a crucial role in strategic bombing missions.
As a bombardier, he trained for months to master accuracy and precision in high-stress situations.

Etymology

From French 'bombardier', from 'bombarde' (a type of cannon).
