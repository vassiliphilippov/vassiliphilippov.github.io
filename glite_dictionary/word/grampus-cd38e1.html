grampus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈɡræm.pəs / (BrE)
a large predatory toothed whale, typically black and white with a prominent dorsal fin, found in cold seas; commonly refers to the orca or sometimes the Risso's dolphin
predatory toothed whale (orca or Risso's dolphin)

Examples

We spotted a grampus swimming near the ice floes in the Arctic.
The grampus is known for its striking black-and-white coloration and large dorsal fin.
Marine biologists study the hunting behaviors of the grampus in cold ocean waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orca - largest species of dolphin, also known as killer whale

Etymology

From Latin 'grampus', possibly from 'grandis piscis' meaning 'great fish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmpəs / (AmE), / ˈɡræmpəs / (BrE)
A large, slaty-grey, blunt-nosed dolphin (Risso's dolphin), commonly found in northern seas.
a type of blunt-nosed dolphin

Examples

The grampus surfaced near the boat before quickly disappearing beneath the waves.
Scientists observed a pod of grampus feeding off the rocky northern coast.
A grampus is distinguished by its rounded head and patchy grey coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - a marine mammal known for its intelligence and playful behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'grampus', possibly influenced by Medieval Latin 'grandis piscis' (great fish).
