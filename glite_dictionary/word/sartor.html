sartor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑr.tər / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.tə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is making and altering garments, especially a tailor
person who makes or alters garments
archaic

Examples

The renowned sartor spent years perfecting his craft in the city's busy garment district.
She brought her torn dress to the local sartor for expert repair.
In medieval times, a sartor was regarded as an essential member of the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailor - a person whose job is making or altering clothes

Etymology

From Latin 'sartor' meaning 'tailor'.
