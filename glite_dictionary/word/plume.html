plume

1. NOUN

/ plum / (AmE), / pljuːm / (BrE)
A light, waterproof, and often decorative feather, forming part of the external covering of a bird.
a feather or cluster of feathers

Examples

The peacock's magnificent plume caught everyone's attention at the zoo.
She wore a hat decorated with a bright red plume from an exotic bird.
Several smaller birds preened their plumes in the morning sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feather - the flat appendage forming the plumage of birds

Etymology

From Latin 'pluma' (soft feather or down).

2. NOUN

/ plum / (AmE), / pluːm / (BrE)
a long, thin shape or mass that spreads out in the air like a feather, such as smoke, steam, or grass heads
a feather-like shape or mass (e.g. smoke, grass)

Examples

A thick plume of smoke rose from the burning building.
The grass waved in the wind, its golden plumes glowing in the sunlight.
A plume of steam drifted above the hot spring, visible for miles.

Synset Examples

a plume of smoke
grass with large plumes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feather - a light, soft growth on the body of birds

Etymology

From Latin pluma (small soft feather).
