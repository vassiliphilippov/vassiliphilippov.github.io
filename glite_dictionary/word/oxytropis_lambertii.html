Oxytropis lambertii

1. NOUN

/ ɑk.siˌtɹoʊ.pɪs læmˈbɜːr.taɪ / (AmE), / ɒk.sɪˌtrəʊ.pɪs læmˈbɜː.tiː / (BrE)
A tufted locoweed native to the southwestern United States, having purple, pink, or white flowers.
a plant species of locoweed

Examples

Cattle sometimes avoid grazing on Oxytropis lambertii due to its toxic properties.
Wildflower enthusiasts can spot Oxytropis lambertii blooming on rocky slopes in spring.
Scientists are studying Oxytropis lambertii to understand its effects on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locoweed - any of several poisonous plants of the genus Astragalus or Oxytropis

Etymology

Genus name from Greek oxys (sharp) + tropis (keel); species named after English botanist Aylmer Bourke Lambert.
