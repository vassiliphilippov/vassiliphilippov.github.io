obelion

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈbiːliən / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːlɪən / (BrE)
the craniometric point on the sagittal suture of the skull near the lambdoid suture
a craniometric point on the skull
medical

Examples

The obelion serves as an important landmark in studies of human skull anatomy.
Researchers measured the distance from the obelion to other cranial points.
In forensic anthropology, the obelion may provide clues about population differences.

Etymology

From Greek 'obelos' (spit; a pointed pillar), used for anatomical points marked with an obelus (a typographic symbol).
