phantom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.təm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm / (BrE)
an image, figure, or presence that appears to exist but has no physical reality, often experienced as a ghost or illusion
an illusory image or ghost

Examples

Many sailors believe they saw a phantom drifting over the foggy sea.
He chased the phantom of success, never finding real satisfaction.
The old mansion is said to be haunted by a phantom that appears at midnight.

Synset Examples

a ghostly apparition at midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparition - a ghost or ghostlike image of a person
  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, imagined as appearing to living people

Etymology

From Greek 'phantasma' (apparition, image), via Latin 'phantasma'.

WordNet ID: oewn-05906160-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæn.təm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm / (BrE)
existing only in the mind or as an illusion; something that seems real but has no physical presence
illusory; lacking physical reality

Examples

She often experienced phantom pains in her missing leg after the accident.
A phantom itch made him scratch at a spot where his arm used to be.
Scientists studied the phenomenon of phantom sounds reported after severe hearing loss.

Synset Examples

seemed to hear faint phantom bells
the amputee's illusion of a phantom limb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusory - not real and based on illusion
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact

Etymology

From Old French fantosme, from Latin phantasma (ghost, apparition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01946019-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.təm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm / (BrE)
a ghost or spirit; a figure that appears to be real but has no physical substance
ghostly, insubstantial figure

Examples

The old mansion was said to be haunted by a phantom in a flowing white dress.
He glimpsed a phantom in the fog, but it vanished as he approached.
Stories of the phantom frightened local villagers for generations.

Synset Examples

we were unprepared for the apparition that confronted us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - a spirit of a dead person, often appearing to the living
  • Synonym: apparition - a ghostly figure or supernatural appearance
  • Antonym: reality - actual existence or substance, the opposite of illusion or ghostliness

Etymology

From Greek 'phantasma' (apparition, ghost), via Latin 'phantasma' and Old French 'fantosme'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09570240-n
Synset POS: n
