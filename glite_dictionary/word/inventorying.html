inventorying

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛntɔriɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛntəriɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making a detailed, itemized list of merchandise or goods on hand
listing of merchandise or supplies

Examples

The company is inventorying its warehouse to prepare for the annual audit.
Careful inventorying can prevent costly mistakes in supply management.
During the end-of-year inventorying, several discrepancies were discovered.

Synset Examples

an inventory may be necessary to see if anything is missing
they held an inventory every month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stocktaking - the act of counting and listing all the goods in stock
  • Synonym: auditing - the process of examining financial records or inventories

Etymology

From the verb 'inventory', which comes from Middle English, from Old French 'inventorie', from Medieval Latin 'inventarium' (a list of what is found).
