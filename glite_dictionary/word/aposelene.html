aposelene

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpoʊˈsɛlɪni / (AmE), / ˌapəʊˈsɛlɪni / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of an object around the moon at which it is farthest from the moon's center
lunar orbit's farthest point

Examples

At aposelene, the spacecraft was more than 3,000 kilometers above the lunar surface.
The satellite transmits data each time it passes aposelene in its orbit.
Engineers carefully calculated the time to fire thrusters at aposelene to adjust the orbit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: periselene - the point in an orbit around the moon closest to the moon

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (away from) and 'Selene' (the moon goddess). Modeled on similar astronomical terms.
