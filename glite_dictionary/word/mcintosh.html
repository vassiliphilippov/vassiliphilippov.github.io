McIntosh

1. NOUN

/ məkˈɪn.tɑʃ / (AmE), / məkˈɪn.tɒʃ / (BrE)
A variety of early-ripening apple with white flesh and red skin, popular in the northeastern United States; often eaten raw or used for applesauce.
variety of apple from the northeastern US

Examples

She prefers a McIntosh apple for its tart flavor and crisp texture.
The orchard grows several varieties, but their McIntosh apples are always in high demand.
I bought a bag of fresh McIntoshes at the farmers' market yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with red or green skin and a crisp white flesh

Etymology

Named after John McIntosh, the Canadian farmer who discovered the variety in the early 19th century.
