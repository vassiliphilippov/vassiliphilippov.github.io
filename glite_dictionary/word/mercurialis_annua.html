Mercurialis annua

1. NOUN

/ mɜːrˌkjʊəriˈælɪs ˈænjuə / (AmE), / məˌkjuːərɪˈælɪs ˈanjʊə / (BrE)
A Eurasian and North African annual herb, sometimes considered a weed, formerly used as a purgative, diuretic, or antisyphilitic.
annual herb, sometimes weed; formerly medicinal

Examples

Mercurialis annua grows rapidly in disturbed soil and can become an invasive weed in gardens.
Historically, herbalists dried Mercurialis annua to prepare remedies for various ailments.
You can easily identify Mercurialis annua by its small greenish flowers and opposite leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Mercurialis' (after Latin Mercurius, the god Mercury) and 'annua', feminine of Latin annuus (yearly).
