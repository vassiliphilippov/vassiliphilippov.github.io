Anserinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.səˈraɪ.ni / (AmE), / ˌæn.səˈriː.niː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds in some classifications, comprising the swans and sometimes related waterfowl such as geese.
subfamily of swans (taxonomy)

Examples

The subfamily Anserinae has been used in some classifications to group all swans.
Recent taxonomic revisions have debated whether geese should be included in the Anserinae.
The fossil record provides evidence for the evolutionary history of the Anserinae lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swans - large, graceful water birds with long necks

Etymology

From Latin anser, meaning 'goose', with the scientific suffix -inae for subfamilies.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.səˈraɪ.ni / (AmE), / ˌæn.səˈriː.niː / (BrE)
a subfamily of waterfowl including swans and sometimes geese, used in some classifications.
subfamily of birds including swans

Examples

The mute swan is classified within the Anserinae according to some ornithological studies.
Taxonomists debate whether geese should also be included in the Anserinae subfamily.
Recent research has led to revisions in the definition of Anserinae, especially concerning swans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Anatidae - the family including ducks, geese, and swans
  • Synonym: swan - large waterbird in the Anserinae subfamily

Etymology

From Latin 'anser' (goose) with the taxonomic suffix '-inae' used for subfamilies.
