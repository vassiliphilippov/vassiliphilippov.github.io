pailful

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪl.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪl.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a pail.
amount filling a pail

Examples

She carried a pailful of water from the well to the house.
It took three pailfuls of sand to fill the sandbox.
A single pailful is enough to wash the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bucketful - the quantity contained in a bucket

Etymology

From 'pail' (a bucket) + '-ful' (denoting as much as can be held).
