abrocome

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbrəˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈab.rə.kəʊm / (BrE)
a ratlike rodent of the Andes with soft fur and large ears, belonging to the genus Abrocoma
ratlike Andean rodent genus

Examples

The abrocome lives in rocky crevices along the slopes of the Andes mountains.
Scientists studied the diet of the abrocome to learn more about its ecological role.
The soft fur and large ears make the abrocome easily distinguishable from other rodents.

Etymology

From New Latin Abrocoma, from Greek abros (delicate) + kome (hair), referring to its soft fur.
