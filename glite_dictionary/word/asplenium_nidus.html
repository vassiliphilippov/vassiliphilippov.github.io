Asplenium nidus

1. NOUN

/ æˈspliːniəm ˈnaɪdəs / (AmE), / æˈspliːniəm ˈniːdʌs / (BrE)
A species of epiphytic fern found in the tropical Old World and Australia, often forming tufted nests in the crevices of trees.
species of tropical epiphytic fern

Examples

The Asplenium nidus is prized by gardeners for its ability to thrive as a houseplant.
Birds sometimes nest in the broad leaves of the Asplenium nidus high up in the rainforest.
In botanical gardens, the Asplenium nidus is often grown attached to tree trunks to mimic its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Asplenium,' and species epithet 'nidus' meaning 'nest', referring to its nest-like shape.
