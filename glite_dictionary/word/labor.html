Labor

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the United States federal department responsible for promoting the working conditions, welfare, and rights of wage earners; created in 1913
US federal department of labor
american

Examples

The Labor Department announced new regulations to protect workplace safety.
Many policies from Labor have improved wage standards for American workers.
She began her career with the US Department of Labor in Washington, D.C.
