vulgarism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlɡərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an offensive, indecent, or coarse word or expression, especially one considered inappropriate in polite conversation
offensive or indecent expression

Examples

The teacher explained that using a vulgarism in class is unacceptable.
He was reprimanded for shouting a vulgarism during the meeting.
Writers often avoid any vulgarism to appeal to a wider audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profanity - language that is offensive or rude, especially relating to religion
  • Antonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word substituted for one considered harsh or offensive

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' (common), via French 'vulgarisme' or directly from English 'vulgar' + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlɡəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡərɪzəm / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking taste, refinement, or good manners; crude or coarse behavior or expression.
crudeness; lack of taste or refinement

Examples

Critics accused the play of vulgarism that overshadowed its artistic merits.
His speech was marred by a certain vulgarism that offended the audience.
The room’s decor bordered on vulgarism, with its gaudy colors and excessive ornaments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - The state or quality of being vulgar; lack of refinement

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' (common, ordinary) + English '-ism'.
