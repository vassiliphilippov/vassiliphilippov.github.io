Rhynchoelaps australis

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋkoʊˈilæps ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / rɪŋkəʊˈiːlæps ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
A small, venomous but harmless snake native to Australia, marked with distinctive black and white bands on a red body.
Australian red, black, and white banded snake

Examples

The Rhynchoelaps australis is often mistaken for more dangerous snakes because of its vibrant coloring.
Although Rhynchoelaps australis is venomous, its bite is not dangerous to humans.
Wildlife enthusiasts observed a Rhynchoelaps australis slithering through the underbrush during their visit.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhynchos' (snout) and 'elaps' (a kind of snake); 'australis' is Latin for 'southern'.
