timeserver

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌsɜrvər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌsɜːvə / (BrE)
a person who conforms to prevailing attitudes or opinions for their own personal advantage, rather than from conviction
someone who changes views for personal gain

Examples

The politician was accused of being a timeserver who shifted his stance with every public opinion poll.
In the corporate world, a timeserver may change allegiances to stay in favor with new management.
Many regarded him as a timeserver, always aligning himself with whatever party was in power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities regardless of principles

Etymology

From English 'time' + 'server', meaning one who serves the times.
