complication

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something more complex or difficult
the process of making something more complex

Examples

The complication of the procedure made it difficult for everyone to understand.
Adding new features led to further complication of the project.
The complication of her instructions confused the students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being complex

Etymology

From Latin 'complicatio' (a folding together), from 'complicare' (to fold together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a disease or disorder that develops while treating or as a result of another existing disease
secondary disease arising during another illness
medical

Examples

Bed sores are a common complication in cases of paralysis.
Pneumonia can be a serious complication of influenza, especially in elderly patients.
Physicians closely monitor patients for any complication that may arise post-surgery.
