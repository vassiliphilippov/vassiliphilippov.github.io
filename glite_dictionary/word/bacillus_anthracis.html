Bacillus anthracis

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪl.əs ænˈθreɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / bəˈsɪl.əs ænˈθreɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
a species of rod-shaped, spore-forming bacterium that causes anthrax in humans and animals
bacterium causing anthrax
medical

Examples

Bacillus anthracis can form spores that survive for decades in harsh environments.
Scientists developed rapid tests to detect Bacillus anthracis in suspicious powders.
Outbreaks of anthrax in livestock are traced to contamination with Bacillus anthracis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthrax - a serious infectious disease caused by Bacillus anthracis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Bacillus' (rod-shaped bacterium) and 'anthracis' (of anthrax, from Greek 'ánthrax', coal).
