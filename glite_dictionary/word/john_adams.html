John Adams

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈædəmz / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈædəmz / (BrE)
The 2nd President of the United States, serving from 1797 to 1801, and one of the Founding Fathers.
2nd U.S. President (1797–1801), Founding Father

Examples

John Adams was instrumental in drafting the Declaration of Independence.
Many historians regard John Adams as a key advocate for American independence.
The correspondence between John Adams and Thomas Jefferson is studied for its historical insight.
