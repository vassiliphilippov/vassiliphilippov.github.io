rehearing

1. NOUN

/ riˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈhɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
an instance of hearing a case or matter again, especially before a court or official body after a decision has been made
hearing a case again, especially in court

Examples

The court granted a rehearing after new evidence was discovered.
Following the controversial decision, the defense requested a rehearing.
The judge denied the motion for a rehearing on the grounds that no legal error was present.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hearing - an official meeting at which evidence and arguments are presented, especially in a court

Etymology

From re- (again) + hearing, following legal usage.
