to acknowledge

1. VERB

/ əkˈnɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to express gratitude or thanks for something; to show that you recognize or appreciate something
express thanks or gratitude

Examples

I would like to acknowledge your generous support of our project.
She always acknowledged the help she received from her colleagues in her speeches.
In the letter, we acknowledge your recent donation with sincere gratitude.

Synset Examples

We must acknowledge the kindness she showed towards us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thank - to express gratitude to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'acknowledgier', from earlier 'acnow' (acknowledge) + 'ledge' (knowledge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00893988-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to inform someone that something sent, such as a letter, document, or payment, has been received
confirm receipt of something

Examples

We greatly appreciate when clients acknowledge the receipt of invoices via email.
Please acknowledge this message as soon as you receive it.
The company quickly acknowledged delivery of the returned products.

Synset Examples

The program committee acknowledged the submission of the authors of the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confirm - to make sure that something is true or definite, sometimes after receiving it

Etymology

From Old French 'acknowledge' (reconnaître), from a- (to) + knowledge.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00894219-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to show that you have noticed someone or recognize the importance or existence of something
express recognition or awareness

Examples

Whenever she walked into the office, she made sure to acknowledge everyone with a smile.
He failed to acknowledge my presence, making me feel invisible at the meeting.
Please acknowledge receipt of this letter at your earliest convenience.

Synset Examples

He never acknowledges his colleagues when they run into him in the hallway
She acknowledged his compliment with a smile
it is important to acknowledge the work of others in one's own writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recognize - to identify or show awareness of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'acknowledier', influenced by Middle English 'knowlechen' (to admit, recognize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01060995-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əkˈnɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to admit or accept that something is true or exists, often reluctantly or after hesitation
admit or accept as true or real

Examples

After a long silence, he finally acknowledged his mistake to the team.
The company acknowledged that its data security procedures needed improvement.
She acknowledged the possibility that she could be wrong.

Synset Examples

He admitted his errors
She acknowledged that she might have forgotten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to confess to be true or to be the case
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Old French 'acnoissier', altered by association with knowledge, from Latin 'agnoscere' (to recognize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00818972-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ əkˈnɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to accept or admit that someone has the status, authority, or power that they claim
accept someone's status or authority

Examples

After a long dispute, the king was finally acknowledged as the rightful ruler by all the nobles.
It took years before the scientific community acknowledged her as an authority in the field.
The new government refused to acknowledge the religious leaders’ legitimacy.

Synset Examples

The Crown Prince was acknowledged as the true heir to the throne
We do not recognize your gods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recognize - to accept someone or something as having a particular status or being true

Etymology

From Old French *acknowledgier*, from Anglo-Norman *acknowlegier*, based on the obsolete verb 'knowledge'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00594278-v
Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ əkˈnɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to accept (a document, agreement, etc.) as legally binding and valid
accept as legally valid
legal

Examples

The notary must acknowledge the signature for the contract to be enforceable.
Before filing the document, the court required all parties to acknowledge its contents.
He refused to acknowledge the will as legally binding until all heirs were present.

Synset Examples

acknowledge the deed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to validate - to confirm something as officially acceptable or legitimate

Etymology

From Old French 'acognoiss-', stem of 'acognoistre', from Latin 'agnoscere' (to recognize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00742454-v
Synset POS: v
