goof-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːf ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈɡuːf ˌɒf / (BrE)
a person who avoids work or responsibility by wasting time or being lazy
person who avoids work; lazy person
informal american

Examples

My boss gets frustrated when she catches a goof-off hanging around the break room all day.
Jake has a reputation as a goof-off who never finishes his projects on time.
No one wanted to partner with the obvious goof-off during the group assignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or effort
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who compulsively works hard and long hours

Etymology

From American English 'goof' (to fool around) + 'off' (to be idle), first recorded in the 20th century.
