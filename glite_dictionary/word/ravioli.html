ravioli

1. NOUN

/ ˌræviˈoʊli / (AmE), / ˌræviˈəʊli / (BrE)
Small cases of pasta dough, usually square or circular, filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables and typically served with a sauce.
filled pasta cases

Examples

She ordered homemade ravioli stuffed with spinach and ricotta for dinner.
I learned how to make ravioli from scratch in my Italian cooking class.
The restaurant serves their signature mushroom ravioli in a creamy sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortellini - small ring-shaped filled pasta

Etymology

From Italian 'ravioli', plural of 'raviolo', of uncertain origin.
