laconism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlæk.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
a style of speaking or writing marked by brief, concise, or terse expression
concise or terse expression

Examples

His speech impressed everyone with its laconism and clarity.
The novel is notable for the laconism of its dialogue.
She admired his laconism, preferring fewer words over elaborate explanations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brevity - concise and exact use of words in writing or speech
  • Antonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed

Etymology

From Greek Laconismos, from Lakonizein 'to imitate the Spartans' (noted for brevity of speech), from Laconia, the district around Sparta.
