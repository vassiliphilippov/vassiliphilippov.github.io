astigmia

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪɡmiə / (AmE), / əˈstɪɡmiə / (BrE)
A defect in an optical system in which light rays from a single point fail to converge at a single focal point, causing blurred or distorted images.
optical defect affecting image clarity
medical

Examples

The optician explained that my blurry vision was caused by astigmia.
Lenses are designed to correct astigmia in optical instruments.
Due to astigmia, the telescope produced slightly distorted images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astigmatism - imperfection in the curvature of an optical system causing distorted images

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + stigma (point, mark).

2. NOUN

/ əˈstɪɡ.mi.ə / (AmE), / əˈstɪɡ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A defect in vision caused by an irregular curvature of the cornea or lens, resulting in distorted or blurred images.
impaired vision due to irregular cornea
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that my blurry vision was a result of astigmia.
Corrective lenses can help people who suffer from astigmia.
Although astigmia is common, many people are unaware they have it until they get an eye exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astigmatism - impaired eyesight due to irregular curvature of the eye's cornea or lens

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + stigma (mark, point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14577182-n
Synset POS: n
