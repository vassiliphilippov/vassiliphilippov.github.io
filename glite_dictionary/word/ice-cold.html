ice-cold

1. ADJ

/ aɪs ˈkoʊld / (AmE), / aɪs ˈkəʊld / (BrE)
Extremely cold; at or close to the temperature of ice.
extremely cold; near ice temperature

Examples

The lemonade was served ice-cold on a hot summer day.
She washed her face with ice-cold water to wake herself up.
The ice-cold breeze cut through his jacket as he walked outside.

Synset Examples

He served the drinks ice-cold to beat the heat.
The ice-cold water shocked him when he jumped in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freezing - very cold; at or below the temperature at which water freezes
  • Degree Lower: chilly - uncomfortably or unpleasantly cold
  • Antonym: lukewarm - moderately warm; tepid
  • Antonym: hot - having a high temperature
