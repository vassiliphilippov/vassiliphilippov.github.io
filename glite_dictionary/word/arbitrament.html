arbitrament

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbɪtrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrəmənt / (BrE)
the act of making a decision or authoritative judgment, especially by an arbiter
authoritative judgment by an arbiter
legal

Examples

The contract dispute was finally settled by arbitrament after months of negotiation failed.
Both parties agreed to abide by the arbitrament of the independent panel.
A fair arbitrament requires both sides to present their cases impartially.

Synset Examples

they submitted their disagreement to arbitration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitration - the process of resolving a dispute by an impartial third party

Etymology

From Latin *arbitramentum* (decision, judgment), from *arbitrari* (to judge).
