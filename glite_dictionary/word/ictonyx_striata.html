Ictonyx striata

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈtoʊnɪks straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɒnɪks straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of African mustelid, commonly known as the striped polecat, noted for its ferret-like size and often tamed.
species of African striped polecat

Examples

The Ictonyx striata is easily recognized by its bold black and white stripes.
Farmers sometimes keep Ictonyx striata to help control rodent populations.
Unlike skunks, the Ictonyx striata uses a powerful odor primarily for defense against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped polecat - common English name for Ictonyx striata, an African mustelid
  • Synonym: muishond - regional name for an African member of the weasel family, such as Ictonyx striata

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'ikton' (a kind of weasel) and Latin 'striata' (striped).
