redshank

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌʃæŋk / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌʃæŋk / (BrE)
a common wading bird of the sandpiper family, found in Eurasia, with long red legs and a straight bill
a wading bird with red legs

Examples

A redshank was seen feeding by the edge of the marsh this morning.
During migration, flocks of redshank often gather along the coastal mudflats.
The distinctive cry of the redshank echoed across the wetlands.

Etymology

From 'red' (the color) and 'shank' (leg), referring to the bird's distinctive red legs.
