stammerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæm.ər.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæm.ər.ə / (BrE)
a person who has a speech impediment characterized by involuntary pauses and repetitions while speaking
person who stammers in speech

Examples

As a child, he was a shy stammerer who avoided speaking in public.
The support group helps every stammerer build confidence in communication.
Many stammerers develop coping strategies to manage their speech difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stutterer - a person who speaks with involuntary repetition or prolongation of sounds

Etymology

From 'stammer' (Middle English stameren) + '-er', forming an agent noun.
