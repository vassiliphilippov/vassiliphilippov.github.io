gravitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the force by which all objects with mass attract one another, especially the force that causes objects to fall to the ground
the force of attraction between masses

Examples

Gravitation causes objects to fall toward the Earth when dropped.
The laws of gravitation help scientists understand the motion of planets.
Without gravitation, life on Earth as we know it would not be possible.

Synset Examples

the more remote the body, the less the gravity
the gravitation between two bodies is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them
gravitation cannot be held responsible for people falling in love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the force that attracts a body towards the center of the earth

Etymology

From Latin gravitas (weight, heaviness) via French gravitation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The movement of an object or fluid downward due to the force of gravity.
downward movement by gravity

Examples

The water flowed into the lower fields by gravitation alone.
Many early irrigation systems relied solely on gravitation for distributing water.
In some devices, the separation process occurs through gravitation rather than mechanical means.

Synset Examples

irrigation by gravitation rather than by pumps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - The force that attracts objects towards the center of the earth or other physical bodies

Etymology

From Latin gravitatio (a leaning, inclination), from gravitas (weight).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡrævəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a movement or tendency toward something attractive or desirable
movement toward an attraction (figurative)

Examples

There is a natural gravitation toward urban centers among young professionals.
The gravitation of artists to cosmopolitan cities is a well-known phenomenon.
Over time, his gravitation toward minimalist design became increasingly evident in his work.

Synset Examples

the gravitation of the middle class to the suburbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - the action or power of evoking interest, pleasure, or liking for someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'gravitatio' (a leaning or tendency towards), from 'gravitas' (weight).
