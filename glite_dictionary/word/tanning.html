2. NOUN

/ ˈtænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtanɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which skin becomes darker in color after exposure to ultraviolet light, especially sunlight
darkening of skin by ultraviolet light

Examples

She spent the afternoon at the beach, enjoying the tanning process.
Prolonged tanning without protection can increase the risk of skin cancer.
They warned about the dangers of artificial tanning in salons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sunburn - reddening of the skin caused by overexposure to sunlight
  • Antonym: bleaching - the process of making skin lighter in color

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'tan,' which comes from Old French 'tanner' (to convert hide into leather), later extended to mean darken the skin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtanɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of converting raw animal hides into leather by treating them with tannins or other chemicals
process of making leather from hides

Examples

The tanning of hides was a vital part of the town's economy.
Modern tanning methods often use synthetic chemicals instead of traditional plant materials.
He learned the ancient art of tanning from his grandfather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherworking - craft or industry of making things from leather

Etymology

From Old English 'tannian', from Medieval Latin 'tannare', from 'tannum' (oak bark used in tanning).
