oxbow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒks.bəʊ / (BrE)
A U-shaped bend or curve in the course of a river or stream, often forming an oxbow lake if cut off from the main flow.
U-shaped bend in a river

Examples

The river formed a broad oxbow after centuries of slow erosion.
Flooding can sometimes create a new channel, leaving the old oxbow cut off as a lake.
From the aerial view, the oxbow was clearly visible in the winding river valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meander - a winding curve or bend of a river

Etymology

Originally referred to the U-shaped collar of a yoke for oxen, from 'ox' + 'bow'. The geographic meaning is by analogy to this shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑksˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒks.bəʊ / (BrE)
A U-shaped wooden frame, the ends of which are attached to a yoke and the loop fits around the neck of an ox to enable it to pull a load.
U-shaped frame fitted around ox's neck

Examples

The farmer placed the oxbow around the animal's neck before attaching it to the plow.
One oxbow broke under pressure, making the yoke unusable until it was repaired.
Traditional yokes and oxbows are still used in some rural communities around the world.

Etymology

From Middle English ox (an ox) + bow (a curved piece of wood), referring to its shape and function.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑksˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒksˌbəʊ / (BrE)
a piece of land contained within the loop of a river bend that forms a curved or U-shaped meander
land inside a river's curved bend

Examples

The newly formed oxbow provided rich soil for farmers to cultivate crops.
Hiking trails often loop around the oxbow created by the slow-moving river.
Wildlife flourishes in the oxbow, where water collects after each flood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meander - a winding curve or bend in a river

Etymology

From the shape's resemblance to an oxbow, which is a U-shaped collar placed around an ox's neck, from Middle English ox (ox) + bow (bend, arch).
