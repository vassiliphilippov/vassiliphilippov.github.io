pumpkinseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌmpkɪnˌsid / (AmE), / ˈpʌmpkɪnˌsiːd / (BrE)
A small, brightly colored North American freshwater sunfish (Lepomis gibbosus), often found in lakes and streams.
small North American sunfish

Examples

The children caught a pumpkinseed while fishing in the shallow part of the lake.
Colorful pumpkinseed are common in ponds across much of North America.
He recognized the fish as a pumpkinseed by its bright orange markings and rounded shape.

Etymology

Named for its flattened, rounded body shape resembling a pumpkin seed.
