Threskiornithidae

1. NOUN

/ θrɛskiɔːrˈnɪθədiː / (AmE), / θrɛskiːɔːˈnɪθɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large wading birds including ibises and spoonbills, characterized by long legs and distinctive bills
family of wading birds: ibises and spoonbills

Examples

The family Threskiornithidae includes both the ibises and the spoonbills.
Researchers observed several unique feeding habits among members of the Threskiornithidae.
Modern classification places spoonbills within the bird family Threskiornithidae.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from the type genus Threskiornis and Greek 'ornis' (bird).

a family of large wading birds including the ibises and spoonbills, characterized by long legs and distinctive bills
ornithological family: ibises and spoonbills

Examples

The family Threskiornithidae includes both ibises and spoonbills found across the world.
Many species in the Threskiornithidae have adapted to wetland habitats.
Ornithologists are studying the evolutionary history of birds within the Threskiornithidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibis - type of wading bird in the Threskiornithidae family
  • Synonym: spoonbill - type of wading bird in the Threskiornithidae family distinguished by a spoon-shaped bill

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek threskios (religious, sacred) and ornis (bird), used in scientific classification.
