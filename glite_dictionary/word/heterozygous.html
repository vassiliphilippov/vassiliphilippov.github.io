heterozygous

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛt̬.ɚ.zaɪˈɡoʊs / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.ər.əʊˈzaɪ.ɡəs / (BrE)
(of an organism or gene) having two different alleles at a particular gene locus on homologous chromosomes.
having two different alleles (genetics)
medical

Examples

The child was found to be heterozygous for the trait, inheriting a dominant and a recessive allele.
A person who is heterozygous at a gene locus may not display symptoms of a recessive disease.
Scientists studied the effects of being heterozygous for a particular gene on susceptibility to malaria.

Synset Examples

heterozygous for eye color

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homozygous - having identical alleles at corresponding chromosomal loci

Etymology

From Greek 'hetero-' (different) + 'zygous' (from 'zygon', meaning yoke, pair).
