infomercial

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfoʊˈmɜːrʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəʊˈmɜːʃəl / (BrE)
a television program that promotes a product or service in the style of a documentary or informative broadcast
documentary-style TV advertisement

Examples

Late at night, an infomercial about kitchen gadgets caught my attention.
Many companies use an infomercial to demonstrate the benefits of their product.
I once bought a vacuum cleaner after watching an infomercial on television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertisement - a notice or announcement promoting a product, service, or event
  • Synonym: commercial - a television or radio advertisement

Etymology

Blend of 'information' and 'commercial'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfoʊˈmɜrʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəʊˈmɜːʃl / (BrE)
a television program that promotes a product or service in an informative, documentary-style format, often of extended length
long-form television advertisement

Examples

The kitchen gadget sold out quickly after its infomercial aired on late-night TV.
She was surprised by how persuasive the infomercial made the exercise machine seem.
Many viewers change the channel when an infomercial comes on during their favorite show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertisement - a public notice promoting products, services, or events
  • Synonym: commercial - a paid advertisement broadcast on television or radio

Etymology

Blend of information and commercial, first used in the 1980s.
