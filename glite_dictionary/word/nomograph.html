nomograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊməˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈnɒməˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a diagram or graphical calculating device used to represent mathematical relationships between three or more variables
graphical calculating device; mathematical diagram

Examples

The engineer used a nomograph to quickly estimate the required pipe diameter for the system.
Medical professionals sometimes rely on a nomograph for drug dosing calculations.
By aligning the scales on the nomograph, users can solve complex equations without lengthy computation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomogram - a chart representing mathematical relationships for calculation

Etymology

From Greek 'nomos' (law) and '-graph' (writing); first used in early 20th century for graphical calculating aids.
