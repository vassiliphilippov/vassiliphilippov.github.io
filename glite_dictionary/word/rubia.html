Rubia

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbiə / (AmE), / ˈruːbɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae, including Old World herbs and subshrubs cultivated for medicinal uses and dyes from their roots.
genus of plants in Rubiaceae family

Examples

Rubia species have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
The roots of Rubia plants are processed to produce red dye.
Botanists often study Rubia when researching natural sources of colorants.

Etymology

From Latin 'rubia', meaning madder (the type species).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːbiə / (AmE), / ˈruːbɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs and subshrubs of the family Rubiaceae, grown for their medicinal properties and for dyes extracted from their roots.
genus of dye- and medicine-producing plants

Examples

The roots of Rubia plants have been used for centuries to produce natural dyes.
Several species in the Rubia genus are valued for their medicinal properties.
Botanists debated the classification of Rubia after discovering new species in Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *rubia* (madder), referring to the plant known for its red dye.
