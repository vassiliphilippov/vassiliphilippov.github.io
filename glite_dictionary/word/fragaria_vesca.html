Fragaria vesca

1. NOUN

/ frəˈɡɛəriə ˈveskə / (AmE), / frəˈɡɛːriə ˈvɛskə / (BrE)
The botanical name for the woodland strawberry, a small wild strawberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia and North America, known for its edible red fruit.
woodland strawberry plant

Examples

The fruit of Fragaria vesca is prized for its intense flavor and aroma.
Botanists have studied the wild distribution of Fragaria vesca across different European habitats.
Gardeners often cultivate Fragaria vesca for its small but sweet berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland strawberry - A synonym for Fragaria vesca, the wild strawberry plant.

Etymology

From Latin: 'fragaria' meaning 'strawberry', and 'vesca' meaning 'thin' or 'delicate'.
