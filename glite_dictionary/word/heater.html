heater

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːtər / (AmE), / ˈhiːtə / (BrE)
(informal, US) In baseball, a pitch thrown at high speed; a fastball.
baseball: a fast, high-velocity pitch
informal american

Examples

The pitcher fired a heater past the batter for a strike.
He tried to hit the heater but was a split-second too late.
The rookie has a reputation for his powerful heater on the mound.

Synset Examples

he swung late on the fastball
he showed batters nothing but smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastball - a baseball pitch thrown at high speed

2. NOUN

/ ˈhiːtər / (AmE), / ˈhiːtə / (BrE)
a device for supplying heat or warmth, especially to a room or for heating water
device for providing heat

Examples

The heater broke last night, so the room was freezing this morning.
Please turn on the heater before you shower to warm up the bathroom.
They bought a portable heater to keep the office warm during winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiator - a device, typically metal, that emits heat into a room
  • Antonym: air conditioner - device that cools and sometimes heats air

Etymology

From 'heat' + '-er', denoting an agent noun; attested since the late 19th century.
