elastosis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlæˈstoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlæˈstəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a condition marked by the degeneration and loss of elasticity in connective tissues, especially skin, often associated with aging or sun exposure
degeneration of elastic tissue, esp. in skin
medical

Examples

Chronic sun exposure can lead to elastosis, causing the skin to lose its youthful appearance.
The pathologist identified marked elastosis in the dermal layers of the biopsy.
Doctors often see elastosis in elderly patients with significant photoaging.

Etymology

From Greek 'elastos' (flexible, ductile) + '-osis' (condition).
