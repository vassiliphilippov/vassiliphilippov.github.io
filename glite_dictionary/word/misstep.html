misstep

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌstɛp / (BrE)
an unintentional mistake or error, especially one that causes embarrassment
an unintentional mistake or blunder

Examples

His careless misstep during the interview cost him the job offer.
A single misstep can ruin an otherwise strong reputation.
The company tried to recover quickly after its financial misstep.

Synset Examples

he recited the whole poem without a single trip
he arranged his robes to avoid a trip-up later
confusion caused his unfortunate misstep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'step', originally referring to a wrong physical step, later used metaphorically.
