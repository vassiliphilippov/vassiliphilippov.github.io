to genuflect

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛnjʊˌflɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnjʊˌflɛkt / (BrE)
to bend one or both knees as a sign of respect or worship, especially in a religious context; to show an exaggerated or servile deference
bend knee as sign of respect

Examples

Before entering the chapel, she would always genuflect in front of the altar.
Some politicians are willing to genuflect to powerful interest groups to gain support.
He genuflected hurriedly, hoping not to draw attention to his unfamiliarity with the ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kneel - to rest on one's knees, especially as a sign of respect or submission

Etymology

From Latin 'genuflectere', from 'genu' (knee) + 'flectere' (to bend).

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛn.juˌflɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ju.flekt / (BrE)
To bend one or both knees as a sign of reverence or worship, especially in a church or before a religious figure or image.
bend the knee as an act of reverence or worship

Examples

During the ceremony, the congregation was asked to genuflect before the altar.
He paused to genuflect at the entrance of the chapel out of respect.
Many Catholics genuflect upon entering or leaving a pew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kneel - to be in or assume a position in which the body is supported by a knee or the knees

Etymology

From Latin 'genu' (knee) + 'flectere' (to bend).
