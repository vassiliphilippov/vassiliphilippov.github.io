Rivulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈrɪvjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of small freshwater fish found mainly in the Americas, known as killifish
a genus of killifish (fish genus)

Examples

Many species in the Rivulus genus inhabit shallow, slow-moving streams in Central and South America.
The Rivulus are popular among aquarium enthusiasts due to their bright colors and hardy nature.
Researchers study Rivulus to understand adaptation in variable aquatic environments.

Etymology

From Latin *rivulus* (small stream or brook), referring to the fish's natural habitat.
