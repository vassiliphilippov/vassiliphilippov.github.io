rivulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪv.jʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈrɪv.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A small tropical freshwater fish of the genus Kryptolebias (formerly Rivulus), often found in streams in the Americas and commonly kept in aquariums; many species are hermaphroditic.
a small tropical freshwater fish

Examples

The aquarium enthusiast added a rivulus to his collection for its unique reproductive behavior.
Researchers studied the adaptation of rivulus in seasonal ponds of South America.
Several species of rivulus are known for their ability to survive in low-oxygen environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killifish - a small, brightly colored freshwater fish often kept in aquariums; Rivulus is a type of killifish

Etymology

From Latin 'rivulus' meaning 'small stream' or 'brook'; reflects the fish's typical habitat.
