beaten

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtn̩ / (BrE)
(of a metal) formed or made thin by hammering.
made thin by hammering

Examples

The artisan created a beautiful necklace from beaten gold.
Traditional Japanese tea bowls are often made from beaten copper.
The museum displayed a collection of beaten silver dishes.

Synset Examples

beaten gold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammered - shaped or formed by hammering, especially of metal

2. ADJ

/ ˈbiːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtn̩ / (BrE)
(of a path, track, or surface) made smooth or bare by repeated use or treading.
worn smooth by repeated use

Examples

Hikers followed the beaten trail through the forest for miles.
She preferred to explore places off the beaten path.
The beaten ground around the playground showed where children played every day.

Synset Examples

did not stray from the beaten path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-trodden - path or surface frequently walked on
