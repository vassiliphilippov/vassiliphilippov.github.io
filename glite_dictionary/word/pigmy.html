pigmy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmi / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmi / (BrE)
a member of any of various peoples, especially in Africa and Asia, whose average adult height is less than five feet
a very short-statured person from certain ethnic groups

Examples

The forest-dwelling pigmy tribes have unique cultural traditions.
Anthropologists studied the way of life among the pigmy peoples of Central Africa.
Legends about pigmy people appear in several ancient cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy - member of ethnic groups of very short stature (more common spelling)

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'pygmy', from Latin 'pygmaeus', from Greek 'pygmaios' (of the length of a pygmē, a measure of length from the elbow to the knuckles).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmi / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmi / (BrE)
a person, animal, or thing that is significantly smaller than what is typical for its kind
an unusually small person, animal, or thing

Examples

The bonsai tree is a pigmy compared to other trees in the forest.
He was considered a pigmy among basketball players due to his height.
The museum features a preserved skeleton of a prehistoric pigmy elephant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: giant - a person or thing that is very large

Etymology

From Latin 'Pygmaeus', from Greek 'Pygmaios', meaning 'of a cubit high'.
