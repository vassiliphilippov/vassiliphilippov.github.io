Martian

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃən / (BrE)
an imaginary or fictional being said to inhabit the planet Mars
imaginary inhabitant of Mars

Examples

Many science fiction stories feature Martians as green-skinned extraterrestrials.
The children pretended to be Martians exploring Earth for the first time.
In old movies, Martians often arrive in flying saucers and cause panic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alien - a being from another world or planet

Etymology

From Mars, the planet, with the suffix -ian (denoting association).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to the planet Mars or its (especially fictional) inhabitants.
relating to Mars or its inhabitants

Examples

The rover was designed to withstand the harsh Martian climate.
She imagined what life would be like in a Martian colony.
Scientists are studying possible Martian microbes found in meteorites.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - of or relating to the Earth or its inhabitants
  • Synonym: Venusian - of or relating to the planet Venus or its inhabitants

Etymology

From Mars (the planet) + -ian (denoting belonging or relating to).
