unregistered

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈrɛdʒɪstərd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈrɛdʒɪstəd / (BrE)
Not officially entered on a list or record; not registered.
not registered or officially recorded

Examples

The company faced penalties for employing unregistered workers.
She bought an unregistered vehicle from a private seller.
Some pets are unregistered, making it hard to track their owners.

Synset Examples

an unregistered citizen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: registered - officially entered on a list or record
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01965469-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈrɛdʒɪstərd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈrɛdʒɪstəd / (BrE)
(of an animal, product, or entity) not officially recorded, listed, or certified by an authorized organization or authority.
not officially recorded or certified

Examples

The farm decided to buy only unregistered dairy cattle to reduce costs.
Some antique firearms remain unregistered due to changes in legislation.
It is illegal to drive an unregistered vehicle on public roads.

Synset Examples

unregistered dairy cattle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: registered - officially recorded or certified by an authority

Etymology

From un- (not) + registered, itself from register (to record or enroll), from Latin 'regesta' (things recorded).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01965921-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɛdʒɪstɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛdʒɪstəd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, vessel, or similar item) not officially recorded or licensed with the appropriate authorities.
not officially registered or licensed

Examples

Coast guard officials warned about the dangers of operating an unregistered boat.
The police seized several unregistered firearms during the operation.
He was fined for driving an unregistered vehicle on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: registered - officially recorded or licensed by authorities

Etymology

un- (not) + registered, from Latin registrare (to record).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00790557-s
