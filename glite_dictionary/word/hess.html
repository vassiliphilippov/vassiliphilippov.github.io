Hess

1. PROPN

/ hɛs / (AmE), / hɛs / (BrE)
(1894–1987) A prominent Nazi official who flew solo to Scotland in 1941 to attempt negotiations for peace with Great Britain, later imprisoned for life.
Nazi leader who attempted peace mission to Britain

Examples

During World War II, Hess surprised the world by flying alone to Scotland on a secret mission.
The trial of Hess at Nuremberg resulted in a life sentence for war crimes.
Many historians debate the true motivations behind Hess's flight to Britain in 1941.
