Polygonia comma

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪɡoʊniə ˈkɑmə / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪɡəʊnɪə ˈkɒmə / (BrE)
A North American anglewing butterfly with orange-brown wings and a small, comma-shaped mark on the underside of each hind wing.
a type of North American butterfly

Examples

The Polygonia comma can be identified by the white comma-shaped mark on its hind wing.
Gardeners often spot the Polygonia comma resting on tree bark in early spring.
A Polygonia comma fluttered past the window, its wings catching the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comma butterfly - common name for Polygonia c-album and related species

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining Greek 'polys' (many) and 'gonia' (angle) with Latin 'comma' (comma), referring to the wing shape and mark.
