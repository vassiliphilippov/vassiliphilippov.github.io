sleeve

1. NOUN

/ sliv / (AmE), / sliːv / (BrE)
a protective or enclosing covering or case into which an object fits, such as one for a record, cable, or pipe
protective case for an object

Examples

Please put the DVD back in its sleeve after you watch it.
Each vinyl record comes in its own protective sleeve to prevent scratches.
The cable was fitted with a rubber sleeve for extra insulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that covers or protects another object

Etymology

From Old English *slēfe*, related to Middle Dutch *slieve* (sleeve).

2. NOUN

/ sliːv / (AmE), / sliːv / (BrE)
the part of a garment that covers all or part of the arm, usually sewn at the armhole
part of a garment covering the arm

Examples

He rolled up his sleeve before washing his hands.
The dress has long, flowing sleeves that reach her wrists.
Her jacket's right sleeve was torn during the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuff - the end part of a sleeve, where it covers the wrist

Etymology

From Old English *slēfe*, related to Proto-Germanic *slēbō* (covering).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04243483-n
Synset POS: n
