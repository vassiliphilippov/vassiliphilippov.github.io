bigness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡ.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being large in size, extent, or importance.
the quality of being large

Examples

The bigness of the building made it an unmistakable landmark in the city.
She was intimidated by the bigness of the challenge ahead.
We were awed by the bigness of the mountains all around us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: largeness - the quality of being large
  • Antonym: smallness - the quality of being small

Etymology

From 'big' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being big.
