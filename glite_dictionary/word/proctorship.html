proctorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑk.tɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈprɒk.tə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of a proctor, especially in an academic institution
position of a proctor

Examples

She was recently appointed to the proctorship at the university, where she oversees examinations.
After several years of teaching, he accepted the proctorship and began supervising the testing process.
The responsibilities of the proctorship include maintaining academic integrity during assessments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proctor - a person who supervises students during exams; an official; chiefly in academic settings

Etymology

From 'proctor' + '-ship', denoting position or office.
