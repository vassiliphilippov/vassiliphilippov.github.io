vigorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪɡərəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡərəs / (BrE)
strong, healthy, and full of energy, physically or mentally
strong and energetic

Examples

After a vigorous workout, she felt refreshed and ready for the day.
His vigorous defense of the proposal convinced the committee to approve it.
The garden flourished under the vigorous sunlight.

Synset Examples

a vigorous old man who spent half of his day on horseback

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energetic - having a lot of energy and enthusiasm
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: frail - physically weak and delicate

Etymology

From Latin vigorosus, from vigor (liveliness, force).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪɡərəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡərəs / (BrE)
Done with force, energy, or strength; full of energy and enthusiasm.
full of force or energy

Examples

He is a vigorous supporter of environmental causes and participates in every protest.
After a vigorous workout, she felt both tired and exhilarated.
The debate became quite vigorous as both sides argued their points passionately.

Synset Examples

a vigorous hiker
gave her skirt a vigorous shake
a vigorous campaign
a vigorous foreign policy
vigorous opposition to the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: weak - lacking strength or energy

Etymology

From Latin 'vigorem' (force, health, energy), from 'vigere' (to be lively).
