shaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪki / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪki / (BrE)
trembling or quivering slightly, often because of fear, weakness, or cold
trembling or unsteady

Examples

Her shaky hands made it hard to pour the tea.
After the marathon, he stood on shaky legs, barely able to keep his balance.
The little dog's shaky body gave away how frightened it was.

Synset Examples

trembling hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trembling - shaking involuntarily, typically from anxiety, excitement, or frailty
  • Antonym: steady - firm, not shaking or changing

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪki / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪki / (BrE)
not firm or secure; likely to fail or experience problems
not secure; likely to fail

Examples

After the scandal, the company's future looked shaky at best.
They repaired their shaky alliance to present a united front.
His finances have been shaky since he lost his job.

Synset Examples

a shaky marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - not stable; likely to change or fall apart
  • Synonym: insecure - not securely fixed; not stable
  • Antonym: solid - stable and secure; not likely to fall or fail

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪki / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪki / (BrE)
prone to shaking, trembling, or lacking stability, especially due to weakness, poor construction, or defect.
unstable or liable to shake

Examples

After the accident, she felt a bit shaky on her feet.
The old bridge looked shaky, swaying slightly as the wind blew.
He gave a shaky laugh, betraying his nervousness.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobbly - liable to move unsteadily or lose balance
  • Synonym: rickety - poorly made and likely to collapse
  • Antonym: stable - firmly fixed and not likely to give way
