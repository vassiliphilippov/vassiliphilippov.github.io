Galahad

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡæləhæd / (AmE), / ˈɡaləhæd / (BrE)
(in Arthurian legend) the most virtuous knight of the Round Table, famous for his purity and his attainment of the Holy Grail.
virtuous knight of Arthurian legend

Examples

Many consider Galahad the embodiment of knightly virtue in the Arthurian legends.
According to the tales, only Galahad was pure enough to see the Holy Grail.
Some modern writers use Galahad as a symbol of moral perfection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lancelot - a prominent knight of the Round Table, Galahad's father
  • Synonym: Sir Percival - a knight of Arthurian legend associated with the Holy Grail quest

Etymology

From Old French Galaad, from Medieval Latin Galahadus, a name used in Arthurian legend.
