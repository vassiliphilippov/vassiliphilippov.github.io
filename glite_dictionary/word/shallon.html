shallon

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃælən / (AmE), / ˈʃælən / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, with edible dark purple berries
evergreen shrub with edible berries

Examples

The dense undergrowth was made up of shallon and ferns.
Foraging hikers sometimes snack on ripe shallon berries during late summer.
Native peoples traditionally used shallon leaves for medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From Chinook Jargon, ultimately from Salishan languages.
