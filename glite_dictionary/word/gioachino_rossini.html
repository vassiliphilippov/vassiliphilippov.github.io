Gioachino Rossini

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊ.əˈkiːnoʊ roʊˈsiːni / (AmE), / dʒoʊ.aˈkiːnəʊ rɒˈsiːni / (BrE)
(1792–1868) An Italian composer renowned for his operas, especially 'The Barber of Seville' and 'William Tell'.
Italian composer, famous for operas

Examples

Gioachino Rossini composed some of the most celebrated operas of the nineteenth century.
The overture from Gioachino Rossini's 'William Tell' is instantly recognizable.
As a music student, she admired the innovative style of Gioachino Rossini.
