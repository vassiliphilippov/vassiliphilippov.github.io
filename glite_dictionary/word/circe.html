Circe

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɜːrsi / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsi / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a sorceress who detained Odysseus on her island and transformed his men into swine.
Greek mythological sorceress

Examples

In Homer's Odyssey, Circe transforms Odysseus's crew into swine on her enchanted island.
The myth of Circe has inspired many works of art and literature.
Students discussed the symbolism of Circe in their classical studies class.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κίρκη (Kírkē), a mythological character.
