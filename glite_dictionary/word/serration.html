serration

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of having a notched or saw-toothed edge or outline
the state of being serrated

Examples

The serration on the knife blade made it easier to cut through bread.
You can see the serration of the mountain range against the evening sky.
The insect's wing edge has a clear serration that helps it camouflage among leaves.

Synset Examples

the serrations of a city skyline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - an indentation or cut at the edge of something
  • Antonym: smoothness - the state of being even or without roughness or projections

Etymology

From Latin 'serra' (saw) + '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a single notch or tooth in a series of notches along an edge or surface, such as on a blade or a leaf
a single notch or tooth on an edge

Examples

The broken serration made the knife less effective at cutting bread.
Each serration on the saw blade contributes to its smooth action.
The leaf's edge had a delicate serration missing, likely from being eaten by an insect.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tooth - a projection or point along an edge, as in gear teeth

3. NOUN

/ səˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A row of notches or tooth-like projections, typically found on an edge or surface, such as a knife blade or the jaws of a tool.
a row of notches or teeth

Examples

The chef preferred a bread knife with pronounced serrations for easier slicing.
The eagle's beak showed clear serrations along its edges.
With its serrations, the saw cut efficiently through the thick branch.

Synset Examples

the pliers had serrations to improve the grip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - a small cut or indentation on an edge or surface
  • Antonym: smooth edge - an edge without notches or projections

Etymology

From Latin 'serra' meaning 'saw', referring to a saw-toothed form.
