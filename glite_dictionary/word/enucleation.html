enucleation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnuːkliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnjuːklɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of an organ or tumor from the body without cutting into it
surgical removal of an organ/tumor
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended enucleation to treat the patient's eye tumor.
After successful enucleation of the mass, the patient showed remarkable improvement.
Ocular enucleation is sometimes necessary when vision cannot be restored.

Synset Examples

the enucleation of the tumor

Etymology

From Latin enucleatio (removal of the kernel), from enucleare 'to remove the kernel'.
