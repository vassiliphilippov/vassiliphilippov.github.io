handler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændlər / (AmE), / ˈhændlə / (BrE)
a person who trains, manages, or exhibits animals, especially for shows or competitions
person who trains or exhibits animals

Examples

The dog handler led her animal around the show ring with expert skill.
Each elephant in the circus was accompanied by its own handler.
After months of work, the handler finally got the falcon to respond to commands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trainer - a person who teaches animals or people to perform certain skills

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændlər / (AmE), / ˈhændlə / (BrE)
a person who manages, directs, or is responsible for someone or something, especially in politics, entertainment, or security.
person who manages or controls

Examples

The senator's handler arranged all of his interviews during the campaign.
Her celebrity handler made sure the schedule ran smoothly.
Security handlers coordinated the movement of VIPs at the event.

Synset Examples

the senator's campaign handlers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person who manages people or activities
  • Synonym: agent - person who acts on behalf of someone else

Etymology

From handle + -er, dating from the 15th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhændlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhændlə / (BrE)
(in sports) a person responsible for the training, coaching, or management of an athlete or team.
trainer or coach in sports

Examples

The boxer's handler helped him prepare for the upcoming championship fight.
Her handler made sure the team stayed motivated throughout the season.
The athlete spoke highly of his handler, crediting him with her improvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - person who trains and instructs athletes or a sports team
  • Synonym: trainer - someone who prepares athletes for competition
