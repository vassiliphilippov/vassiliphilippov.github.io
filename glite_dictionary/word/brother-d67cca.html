brother

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a boy or man who has the same parents as another person
male sibling

Examples

My brother taught me how to ride a bike when I was young.
The two brothers started their own business together.
She called her brother to wish him a happy birthday.

Synset Examples

my brother still lives with our parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sister - a girl or woman who has the same parents as another person

Etymology

From Old English brōþor, from Proto-Germanic *brothar, from Proto-Indo-European *bhrāter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
A close male friend who shares a strong bond or accompanies others in their activities, often used as a term of endearment or camaraderie among men.
close male friend; companion
informal

Examples

He considers Mark his brother, even though they are not related by blood.
What's up, brother? Ready for the big game tonight?
They stood by each other like true brothers through thick and thin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-86559942-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
Used as a term of address for a male member of the same group, organization, or movement, especially among those with a close or ideological bond.
term for fellow member of a group or movement

Examples

At the rally, he greeted each activist as brother and welcomed everyone warmly.
Union leaders often refer to each other as brother in solidarity speeches.
During the meeting, she addressed the new members as brother and sister to emphasize unity.

Synset Examples

Greetings, comrade!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comrade - companion or fellow member of an organization or movement
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðər / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a male member of a fraternity, religious order, or other group, regarded as a close associate or comrade
male member of a group or fraternity

Examples

All the brothers in the fraternity welcomed the new members warmly.
He considered everyone in the monastery to be his brother.
The union brothers stood in solidarity during the strike.

Synset Examples

none of his brothers would betray him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sister - female member of a group or fraternity
  • Synonym: comrade - a companion who shares one's activities or is a fellow member of an organization
