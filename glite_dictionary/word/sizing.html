sizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
a glutinous substance applied to fill pores in surfaces or to stiffen fabrics, especially in textiles and papermaking
glutinous material for filling or stiffening

Examples

The cloth was treated with sizing to give it a crisp texture.
Papermakers use sizing to improve the sheet’s resistance to water and ink.
Carpenters sometimes apply sizing before painting wooden surfaces to seal the grain.

Synset Examples

size gives body to a fabric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: size - glutinous substance used as an adhesive or filler

Etymology

Derived from 'size' in the sense of gelatinous preparation, from Old French 'sise' (glue).
