Lutra canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtrə kəˌnædɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈluːtrə kəˌnædɛnsɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the North American river otter, a sociable semi-aquatic mammal found along streams and lakes in North America.
North American river otter (scientific name)

Examples

The Lutra canadensis is known for its playful behavior and graceful swimming abilities.
Researchers tracked the movements of Lutra canadensis populations along the river.
Environmental changes have affected the habitat of Lutra canadensis in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otter - A semiaquatic fish-eating mammal of the weasel family

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lutra' meaning otter, and 'canadensis' indicating Canadian origin.
