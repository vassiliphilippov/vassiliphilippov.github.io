supersession

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pərˈseʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈseʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of replacing someone or something with another considered superior; the process of being replaced.
the act of replacing by a superior alternative

Examples

The law underwent supersession when a new policy was introduced by the government.
After the supersession of the old CEO, the company adopted new strategies.
Many traditions face supersession by modern practices as societies evolve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - the act of replacing one thing with another
  • Synonym: to supersede - to take the place of; to replace as superior

Etymology

From Latin 'supersedēre' (to sit above, to supersede) via Late Latin 'supersessio'.
