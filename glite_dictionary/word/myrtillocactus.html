Myrtillocactus

1. NOUN

/ mɜrˌtɪloʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / mɜːˌtɪl.əʊˈkaktəs / (BrE)
A small genus of tree-like cacti native to Mexico and Central America, known for their blue-green stems and edible fruit.
genus of tree-like cacti

Examples

The Myrtillocactus is popular among collectors because of its striking appearance and edible berries.
Many gardens in arid regions feature Myrtillocactus due to its drought tolerance.
Researchers are studying Myrtillocactus for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'myrtillus' (Latin for 'whortleberry') and 'cactus'.
