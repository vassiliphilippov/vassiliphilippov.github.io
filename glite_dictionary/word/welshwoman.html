Welshwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlʃˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈwɛlʃˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is a native or inhabitant of Wales.
female native or inhabitant of Wales

Examples

A proud Welshwoman, she often spoke about her homeland's traditions.
The first Welshwoman to win the award inspired many in her community.
My grandmother was a Welshwoman who moved to London in her twenties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Welshman - a man who is a native or resident of Wales

Etymology

Formed from 'Welsh' + 'woman', patterning after 'Englishwoman', attested from the 17th century.
