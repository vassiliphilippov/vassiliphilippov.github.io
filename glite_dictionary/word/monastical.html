monastical

1. ADJ

/ məˈnæstɪkəl / (AmE), / məˈnæstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the way of life in a monastery, especially a communal life separated from the world under religious vows
relating to monastic communal religious life
archaic

Examples

The monks adhered to a strict monastical discipline, shunning all contact with the outside world.
She found the monastical silence of the abbey both peaceful and unnerving.
Their community was organized along monastical lines, with shared property and daily prayers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastic - relating to monasteries or monks/nuns

Etymology

From Medieval Latin monasticus, from Greek monastikos; patterned after 'monastic'.
