to adjust

1. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to determine the amount to be paid on an insurance claim
determine insurance claim amount

Examples

The insurance company will adjust your claim after reviewing the damage report.
Once the inspector submits his findings, the insurer can adjust the claim amount accordingly.
It took several weeks for the company to adjust all the claims after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle - to resolve or agree on the payment of a claim or debt

2. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to arrange or position something so that it is aligned, straight, or parallel with something else
arrange to be straight or parallel

Examples

He stopped to adjust the chairs so that they were perfectly in line.
Please adjust the blinds to make them parallel with the window frame.
She carefully adjusted the pictures on the wall until they all lined up evenly.

Synset Examples

align the car with the curb
align the sheets of paper on the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to place or arrange things in a straight line

Etymology

From Old French ajuster, from Vulgar Latin *ad(i)uxtare, from Latin ad- (to) + juxta (near).

3. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to change or regulate something so that it is more accurate, effective, or conforms to a standard
change to achieve accuracy or conformity

Examples

Could you adjust the volume so everyone can hear clearly?
She adjusted her chair to fit under the desk.
The technician needs to adjust the settings to ensure accurate measurements.

Synset Examples

Adjust the clock, please
correct the alignment of the front wheels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modify - to change or alter something, usually slightly
  • Synonym: to calibrate - to adjust or mark a measuring device for accuracy
  • Antonym: misadjust - to adjust incorrectly

Etymology

From Old French 'ajuster', from Late Latin 'adjuxtare', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'juxta' (near).

4. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to change or adapt oneself to new or different conditions
adapt to new conditions

Examples

After moving to a new city, it took her months to adjust to the different climate.
Employees often find it difficult to adjust when a company changes its management style.
It is important that children adjust quickly to being back in school after the holidays.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to change in order to suit new conditions
  • Synonym: to acclimate - to become accustomed to a new environment
  • Antonym: to resist - to refuse to accept or adjust to change

Etymology

From Old French *ajuster*, from Late Latin *ad(i)ustare* (to make right, to fit).

5. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to change something or oneself so that it becomes suitable for a new situation, requirement, or condition; to adapt
adapt or make suitable for

Examples

After moving to a new country, it took her a while to adjust to the culture.
You may need to adjust your expectations when starting a new job.
His eyes needed time to adjust to the dimly lit room.

Synset Examples

Adjust your eyes to the darkness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to change in order to fit a new situation

Etymology

From Old French *ajuster*, based on Latin *ad* (to) + *iustus* (right).
