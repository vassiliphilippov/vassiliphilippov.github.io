beatnik

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtˌnɪk / (AmE), / ˈbiːtnɪk / (BrE)
A young person in the 1950s and early 1960s belonging to the Beat Generation, typically characterized by nonconformist attitudes and unconventional dress and behavior.
nonconformist of the Beat Generation

Examples

He adopted the look of a beatnik, complete with a beret and dark turtleneck.
In the 1950s, coffeehouses were filled with beatniks reading poetry and discussing philosophy.
The film portrays a group of beatniks living on the fringes of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bohemian - a person with artistic or unconventional social habits
  • Degree Lower: hipster - a person who follows the latest trends and fashions, especially those regarded as being outside the cultural mainstream

Etymology

Formed from 'beat' (from 'Beat Generation') and the Russian suffix '-nik', popularized in the 1950s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbit.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈbiːt.nɪk / (BrE)
a member of a young bohemian subculture in the United States during the 1950s and early 1960s, characterized by a rejection of conventional values, distinctive dress, a love of jazz music, and a tendency toward communal living and nonconformity
member of a 1950s-60s bohemian youth subculture

Examples

He dressed like a beatnik, favoring dark turtlenecks and berets.
In the 1950s, many beatniks gathered in coffeehouses to listen to jazz and discuss poetry.
The novel captures the attitude and lifestyle of the beatnik generation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippie - member of a 1960s-70s countercultural movement
  • Synonym: bohemian - person leading an unconventional lifestyle, especially involving artistic pursuits

Etymology

Coined in the late 1950s from 'beat' (as in 'beat generation') and the Russian suffix -nik (as in 'Sputnik').
