West African

1. ADJ

/ wɛst ˈæfrɪkən / (AmE), / wɛst ˈafrɪkən / (BrE)
of or relating to the countries, cultures, or people of West Africa.
relating to West Africa

Examples

She specializes in West African art and history.
The market had a wonderful selection of West African spices.
Many West African languages are spoken in that region.

2. NOUN

/ wɛst ˈæfrɪkən / (AmE), / west ˈæfrɪkən / (BrE)
a group of languages spoken in the far western region of West Africa, including languages such as Yoruba and Wolof.
languages of the far western West Africa region

Examples

Many West African languages are tonal and have complex grammatical systems.
She studied the phonetic features of several West African languages during her fieldwork.
The museum's new exhibit explores the diversity of West African linguistic heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Niger–Congo - major language family to which most West African languages belong
