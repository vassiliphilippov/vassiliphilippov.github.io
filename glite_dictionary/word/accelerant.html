accelerant

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛlɚˌænt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.ər.ənt / (BrE)
a substance, usually a chemical, that increases the speed at which a fire spreads or intensifies
substance that speeds up fire

Examples

The fire marshal detected traces of accelerant at the crime scene.
It is illegal to use an accelerant to start a bonfire in this area.
Gasoline is a common accelerant in arson cases.

Synset Examples

The investigation revealed that an accelerant was used in the arson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any change

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten).
