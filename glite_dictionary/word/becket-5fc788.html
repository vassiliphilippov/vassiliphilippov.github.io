becket

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(nautical) A short rope or line with an eye at one end and a knot at the other, used to secure loose items on a ship.
nautical line for securing items

Examples

The sailor quickly fastened the bucket with a becket to keep it from rolling away.
Each hammock was suspended from a becket attached to the ship’s beams.
He looped a becket through the oar to prevent it from getting lost overboard.

Etymology

Possibly from Old French *becquet* (a little beak or hook), referring to the hook-like appearance.
