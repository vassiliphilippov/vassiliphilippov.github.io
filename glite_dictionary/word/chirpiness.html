chirpiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɜrpinəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːpinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being cheerful, lively, and upbeat
cheerful, lively quality

Examples

Her chirpiness in the morning always makes the office feel brighter.
He greeted everyone with such chirpiness that it was impossible not to smile.
Despite the rainy weather, her chirpiness remained undiminished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloominess - the quality of being sad or depressed

Etymology

Formed from 'chirpy' + suffix '-ness.' 'Chirpy' is derived from 'chirp,' imitating the sound of small birds.
