to relieve

1. VERB

/ rɪˈliv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to grant someone relief or exemption from a rule or requirement
grant exemption from a rule or requirement

Examples

The principal decided to relieve her from gym class because of her injury.
He was relieved from jury duty due to a family emergency.
They relieve employees from certain regulations during public holidays.

Synset Examples

She exempted me from the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exempt - to free someone from an obligation or rule
  • Antonym: subject - to make someone liable to a rule or requirement

Etymology

From Old French 'reliever', from Latin 'relevare' (to raise, lighten, relieve).
