aeriform

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrɪfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈeərɪfɔːm / (BrE)
Having the form or nature of air; light, insubstantial, or intangible.
having the nature of air; insubstantial

Examples

The ghost appeared as an aeriform figure, drifting silently through the room.
Her aeriform presence in his memory haunted him with a sense of loss.
Artists often strive to capture the aeriform quality of dreams in their paintings.

Synset Examples

figures light and aeriform come unlooked for and melt away
aerial fancies
an airy apparition
physical rather than ethereal forms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light; otherworldly
  • Synonym: airy - light; delicate; resembling air
  • Antonym: substantial - real, having substance; solid

Etymology

From Latin 'aer' (air) + '-form' (having the form of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛrɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈɪə.rɪ.fɔːm / (BrE)
Having the form or characteristics of air; resembling air; gaseous or vaporous.
having the form or qualities of air

Examples

Under intense heat, the solid quickly became aeriform and invisible to the naked eye.
Certain perfumes release aeriform particles into the atmosphere.
The magician filled the stage with an aeriform mist that hovered above the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaseous - having the form of gas; not solid or liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'aer' (air) + '-form' (having the form of).
