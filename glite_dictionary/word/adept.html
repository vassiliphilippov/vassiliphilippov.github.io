adept

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɛpt / (AmE), / əˈdɛpt / (BrE)
a person who is highly skilled or proficient in a particular activity or field
highly skilled person

Examples

She is an adept at solving complex puzzles that stump others.
The journalist interviewed an adept in artificial intelligence for the article.
As an adept in the kitchen, he can prepare gourmet meals with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to a field or activity, lacking experience

Etymology

From Latin adeptus (one who has attained), from adipisci (to attain, achieve).

2. ADJ

/ əˈdɛpt / (AmE), / əˈdɛpt / (BrE)
very skilled or proficient at something; having a natural ability or expertise.
very skilled or proficient

Examples

Sarah is adept at solving complex mathematical problems quickly and accurately.
He became adept in the use of digital editing software after just a few months of practice.
Our new chef is particularly adept at preparing fresh seafood dishes.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts

a good mechanic

the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having skill or expertise
  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing something
  • Antonym: inept - having or showing no skill

Etymology

From Latin 'adeptus', past participle of 'adipisci' (to attain).
