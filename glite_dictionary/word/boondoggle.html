boondoggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːnˌdɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbuːnˌdɒɡl / (BrE)
work or activity that is wasteful or pointless but gives the appearance of having value, often done to look busy
pointless or wasteful activity

Examples

That committee meeting turned out to be a complete boondoggle with no real decisions made.
Many citizens were outraged to learn their tax money had funded a government boondoggle.
He spent weeks on a boondoggle that his manager eventually canceled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make-work - work that serves little purpose other than to keep someone busy

Etymology

Coined in 1920s United States; popularized during the New Deal era, origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ ˈbunˌdɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbuːnˌdɒɡl / (BrE)
to do work or engage in an activity that is pointless, wasteful, or of little or no value, often to give the appearance of useful activity
do pointless or wasteful work
american informal

Examples

The consultant was accused of trying to boondoggle instead of finding real solutions.
Instead of improving the process, the committee spent months boondoggling on unnecessary details.
We can't afford to boondoggle when there are real problems to solve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to waste time - spend time doing unnecessary things
  • Synonym: busywork - work that keeps someone busy but has little practical value

Etymology

Originally American slang; origin uncertain, possibly from scouts’ lanyards called 'boondoggles' (1930s).
