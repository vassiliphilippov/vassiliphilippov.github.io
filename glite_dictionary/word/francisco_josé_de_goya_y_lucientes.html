Francisco José de Goya y Lucientes

1. PROPN

/ frænˌsɪskoʊ hoʊˈseɪ ðeɪ ˈɡɔɪ.ə i luːˈθjentɛs / (AmE), / frænˌsɪskəʊ həʊˈseɪ deɪ ˈɡɔɪ.ə i lʊˈθjɛntɛs / (BrE)
(1746–1828) Spanish painter renowned for his portraits and satirical works, considered a leading figure of Spanish art.
Spanish painter, known for portraits and satires

Examples

The works of Francisco José de Goya y Lucientes are exhibited in major museums around the world.
Many art historians regard Francisco José de Goya y Lucientes as the last of the Old Masters and the first of the moderns.
The haunting etchings of Francisco José de Goya y Lucientes continue to inspire contemporary artists.
