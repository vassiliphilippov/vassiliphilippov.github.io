Loasa

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈɑːsə / (AmE), / ləʊˈɑːsə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American prickly herbs or subshrubs in the family Loasaceae, known for their stinging hairs.
genus of prickly tropical American herbs or subshrubs

Examples

The Loasa genus is noted for its distinctive stinging hairs.
Several species within Loasa are cultivated as ornamental plants in gardens.
Botanists have discovered a new species belonging to the Loasa genus in South America.

Etymology

From Latin *Loasa*, the botanical name assigned by Carl Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ loʊˈɑːsə / (AmE), / ləʊˈɑːsə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial South American plants with stinging hairs and showy white, yellow, or reddish-orange flowers.
genus of South American stinging plants

Examples

The garden featured a rare Loasa with striking orange blooms.
Some species of Loasa can irritate the skin due to their stinging hairs.
Botanists discovered several new Loasa varieties in the Andes.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name, derived from a South American (Peruvian) vernacular name for the plant.
