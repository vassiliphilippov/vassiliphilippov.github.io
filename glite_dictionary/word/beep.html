beep

1. NOUN

/ biːp / (AmE), / biːp / (BrE)
a short, high-pitched electronic sound, often used as a signal or warning
a short high-pitched electronic signal

Examples

The microwave lets out a beep when your food is ready.
I heard a beep from my phone, signaling a new message.
Every time you press a button, the machine emits a loud beep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - a continuous low, humming sound

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07391682-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ bip / (AmE), / biːp / (BrE)
to call, summon, or alert someone by sending a signal from a beeper or pager
call or alert by pager

Examples

If you need me during the meeting, just beep me.
Doctors were often beeped in the middle of the night for emergencies.
He beeped his assistant when he arrived at the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: page - to contact someone via a pager or public announcement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00793034-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ biːp / (AmE), / biːp / (BrE)
to make a short, loud, high-pitched sound, like those produced by horns or electronic devices
make a short, high-pitched sound

Examples

The alarm clock beeped every morning at six o'clock, waking him up on time.
She accidentally pressed a button, and the microwave suddenly beeped loudly.
As I waited at the crosswalk, a car behind me beeped impatiently.

Synset Examples

The horns of the taxis blared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blare - make a loud, harsh sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself; first recorded in the 1920s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02187621-v
Synset POS: v
