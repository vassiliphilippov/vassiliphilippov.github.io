mastectomy

1. NOUN

/ mæsˈtɛktəmi / (AmE), / mæsˈtɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove a breast, typically to treat or prevent breast cancer
surgical removal of a breast
medical

Examples

After her diagnosis, she decided to undergo a mastectomy as part of the treatment plan.
Some women choose a preventive mastectomy if they are at high risk of breast cancer.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of a mastectomy before the operation.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastos' (breast) and 'ektomē' (excision, removal).
