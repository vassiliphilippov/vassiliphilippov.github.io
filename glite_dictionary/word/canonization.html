canonization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkanənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic and Eastern Orthodox Churches) the official act of declaring a deceased person a saint.
proclamation of sainthood in Christian churches

Examples

The process of canonization requires thorough investigation into the candidate’s life and miracles.
Mother Teresa’s canonization ceremony was attended by thousands of people from around the world.
After decades of devotion, the church announced his canonization as a saint.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beatification - the recognition of a deceased person's entrance into heaven and capacity to intercede on behalf of individuals who pray in their name

Etymology

From Medieval Latin canonizatio, from canonizare (to canonize), from Latin canon (rule).
