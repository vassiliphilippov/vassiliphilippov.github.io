allmouth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈɔːlˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
A fish with a large mouth and a wormlike filament attached, used to lure prey.
fish with large mouth and filament for luring prey

Examples

The deep-sea researcher was thrilled to discover an allmouth hidden among the rocks.
An allmouth uses its filament to mimic the movement of small worms and attract unsuspecting prey.
Fishermen rarely catch an allmouth because it lives in such deep and inaccessible waters.
