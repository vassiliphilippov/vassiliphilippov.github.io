Rubus cuneifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs kjuːˌniːɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs kjuːˌniːɪˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
A stiff, shrubby species of blackberry native to the eastern United States, from Connecticut to Florida.
shrubby blackberry of eastern US

Examples

The dense thickets of Rubus cuneifolius provide shelter for many small animals.
In late summer, hikers can find ripe berries on Rubus cuneifolius along forest edges.
Botanists studied the leaf shape of Rubus cuneifolius to distinguish it from related species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - a dark edible fruit and the plant that bears it

Etymology

From Latin: Rubus (bramble, blackberry), cuneifolius (wedge-shaped leaves).
