notable

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbəl / (BrE)
worthy of notice or attention; remarkable or significant
worthy of notice or attention

Examples

Her dedication to the project was notable and inspired everyone around her.
There has been a notable improvement in the city's air quality this year.
The scientist received an award for his notable contribution to medical research.

Synset Examples

a noteworthy fact is that her students rarely complain
a noteworthy advance in cancer research
a remarkable achievement
a notable increase in the crime rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noteworthy - worthy of notice or attention
  • Synonym: remarkable - worthy of being noticed or extraordinary
  • Antonym: ordinary - not special or distinctive

Etymology

From Old French notable, from Latin notabilis (that can be noted), from notare (to mark, note).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbl̩ / (BrE)
a person who is prominent or distinguished, especially someone admired or respected by others
prominent or distinguished person

Examples

Several notables from the art world attended the gala.
The event was packed with local notables, including politicians and business leaders.
He was recognized as one of the leading notables of his generation.

Synset Examples

he was host to a large gathering of luminaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment or sport
  • Synonym: luminary - a person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere

Etymology

From Old French notable, from Latin notabilis (worthy of note).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10295935-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbl / (BrE)
well known and respected; deserving attention or notice
well known and respected

Examples

She became a notable figure in the movement for climate action.
Several notable scientists attended the international conference.
The museum's collection includes some notable pieces of modern art.

Synset Examples

a famous actor
a celebrated musician
a famed scientist
an illustrious judge
a notable historian
a renowned painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - known about by many people
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Old French 'notable' and Latin 'notabilis' (worthy of note).
