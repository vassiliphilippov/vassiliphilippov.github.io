counterplan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌplæn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌplæn / (BrE)
a secret plan or scheme designed to oppose or undermine another plan or plot
plan made to subvert another plan

Examples

The rebels devised a counterplan to expose the enemy's intentions.
When the board learned of the hostile takeover, they quickly formulated a counterplan.
His counterplan was so effective that it completely foiled the con artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan to achieve an aim, especially one that is illegal or underhand
  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or devious plan; a plot

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'plan'.
