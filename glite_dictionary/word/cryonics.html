cryonics

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈɑnɪks / (AmE), / kraɪˈɒnɪks / (BrE)
the practice of preserving a seriously ill or recently deceased person by freezing the body at very low temperatures, hoping to revive them in the future when medical advancements allow
the freezing of humans for potential future revival

Examples

Some people have chosen cryonics in the hope of being revived in the distant future.
The ethics of cryonics are widely debated among scientists and philosophers.
After his death, his family arranged for his body to undergo cryonics.

Synset Examples

cryonics is more science fiction than serious science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryogenics - the branch of physics related to the production and effects of very low temperatures

Etymology

From 'cryo-' (cold, from Greek 'kruos') + 'onics' (patterned after 'electronics').
