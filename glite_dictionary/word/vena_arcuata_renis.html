vena arcuata renis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ɑrˈkjuː.eɪ.tə ˈriː.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ɑːˈkjuː.eɪ.tə ˈriː.nɪs / (BrE)
a vein in the kidney that receives blood from the interlobular veins
kidney vein receiving blood from interlobular veins
medical

Examples

The vena arcuata renis plays a key role in draining blood from the cortex of the kidney.
Blood from the interlobular veins flows into the vena arcuata renis before leaving the kidney.
Researchers studied the function of the vena arcuata renis in various renal disorders.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' meaning 'vein', 'arcuata' meaning 'arched', and 'renis' meaning 'of the kidney'.
