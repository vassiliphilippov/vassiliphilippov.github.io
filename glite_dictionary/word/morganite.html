morganite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːr.ɡəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.ɡəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a pink variety of beryl used as a gemstone
a pink gemstone variety of beryl

Examples

The pendant featured a beautiful morganite surrounded by tiny diamonds.
She chose a pale pink morganite ring for her engagement.
The jeweler explained that morganite is prized for its delicate color.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beryl - a mineral consisting of a silicate of beryllium and aluminum, sometimes used as a gemstone

Etymology

Named after the American financier J. P. Morgan (1837–1913), a collector of gemstones.
