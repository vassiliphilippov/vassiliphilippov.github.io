excited

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing happiness, eagerness, or enthusiasm; in a state of heightened emotion or arousal
in a state of eagerness or enthusiasm

Examples

She was so excited about her birthday party that she couldn't sleep.
The children grew excited as the parade approached.
He sounded excited when he called to share the news.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling excitement, anger, or agitation
  • Synonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'excitare' (to call out, rouse), via Old French 'exciter'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a molecule or atom) being in a state of increased energy and reactivity, typically after absorbing energy.
having increased energy (chemistry, physics)
medical

Examples

When the atom is in an excited state, it can release energy as light.
The molecule became excited after absorbing a photon.
In the experiment, scientists observed the behavior of excited electrons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ground state - the lowest energy state of a molecule or atom
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01935325-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
experiencing strong or heightened emotions, especially in an excessive or agitated way
showing strong or excessive emotion

Examples

The children grew excited as the parade approached the street.
After hearing the news, she became so excited she couldn't sit still.
He was visibly excited and unable to focus on his work all morning.

Synset Examples

he would become emotional over nothing at all
she was worked up about all the noise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
  • Synonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From Latin ex-citāre (to set in motion, arouse), via Old French exciter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086315-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
showing or feeling very strong and often uncontrolled excitement or emotion.
showing strong, uncontrolled excitement

Examples

She was so excited that she could hardly sit still during the announcement.
The children were excited about the trip to the amusement park and barely slept the night before.
His excited reaction to the good news made everyone in the room smile.

Synset Examples

a crowd of delirious baseball fans
something frantic in their gaiety
a mad whirl of pleasure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing strong emotion; peaceful and untroubled
  • Degree Higher: overjoyed - extremely happy and excited
  • Degree Lower: interested - showing curiosity, but less intense than 'excited'

Etymology

From the verb 'excite', from Latin excitare (to call out, arouse).
