bountifulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊntɪf(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ˈbaʊntɪf(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being abundant or generous; great plenty
abundance; generosity

Examples

The bountifulness of the harvest surprised even the most optimistic farmers.
We admired the bountifulness of her table, which was laden with delicious food.
Nature's bountifulness provides opportunities for all living creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

From 'bountiful' + '-ness', with 'bountiful' from Old French *bontif* (good, kindly), from Latin *bonitas* (goodness).
