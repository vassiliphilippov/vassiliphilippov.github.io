hypnotic

1. ADJ

/ hɪpˈnɑtɪk / (AmE), / hɪpˈnɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or producing hypnosis; having the quality of inducing hypnosis or a trance-like state
relating to hypnosis

Examples

The therapist used a hypnotic technique to help the patient recall memories.
Her voice was so hypnotic that everyone in the room listened in silence.
Researchers studied the hypnotic effects of certain repetitive sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesmeric - of or relating to mesmerism or inducing a trance-like state

Etymology

From Greek 'hypnos' (sleep), via Latin 'hypnoticus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02755975-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ hɪpˈnɑːtɪk / (AmE), / hɪpˈnɒtɪk / (BrE)
A drug that induces sleep; a medication used to promote or maintain sleep.
sleep-inducing drug
medical

Examples

After surgery, the doctor prescribed a hypnotic to help him rest.
Long-term use of hypnotics can lead to dependence and other health issues.
Some herbal teas are believed to have mild hypnotic effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a drug tending to calm or relax

Etymology

From Greek hypnōtikos (tending to produce sleep), from hypnos (sleep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04268044-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ hɪpˈnɑtɪk / (AmE), / hɪpˈnɒtɪk / (BrE)
having an effect that attracts and holds your attention as if by a spell; fascinating or captivating.
fascinating; holding attention as if by a spell

Examples

The speaker's hypnotic voice held the audience in rapt attention throughout the lecture.
She watched the hypnotic movement of the waves and felt herself relax.
The film's hypnotic visuals made it impossible to look away.

Synset Examples

read the bedtime story in a hypnotic voice
she had a warm mesmeric charm
the sheer force of his presence was mesmerizing
a spellbinding description of life in ancient Rome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesmerizing - captivating; holding attention as if by magic
  • Synonym: spellbinding - so interesting or exciting that it holds your attention completely

Etymology

From French 'hypnotique', from Greek 'hypnos' (sleep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00169450-s
Synset POS: s
