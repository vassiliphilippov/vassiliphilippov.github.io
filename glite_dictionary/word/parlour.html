parlour

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrlər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə / (BrE)
a room in a private house or establishment where people can sit, talk, and relax
sitting room for conversation or relaxation

Examples

Guests gathered in the parlour for tea and quiet conversation.
The old mansion’s parlour was elegantly furnished with antique chairs.
In Victorian times, the parlour was reserved for receiving important visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: living room - a room in a house for general family use

Etymology

From Old French 'parlur' (speech), from 'parler' (to speak), later referring to a room for conversation in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlə / (BrE)
A room in an inn or club where visitors are received and entertained.
reception room for visitors

Examples

After checking in at the inn, the guests were shown into the parlour to wait for their rooms.
Members often gather in the parlour of the club for afternoon tea and conversation.
The old hotel still maintains a cozy parlour where visitors can relax by the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lounge - a room for sitting and relaxing in a hotel or club
  • Synonym: drawing room - a room in a house for entertaining guests

Etymology

From Old French *parler* (to speak), via Medieval Latin *parlatorium* (talking room).
