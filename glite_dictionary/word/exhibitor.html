exhibitor

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɪbɪtər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a person or organization that presents or organizes an exhibit for others to see, especially at a show or exhibition
person or group presenting an exhibit

Examples

The exhibitor set up his booth early to attract more visitors during the trade show.
Each exhibitor was given a unique space to display their products at the convention center.
As an exhibitor, she prepared detailed information about her artwork to engage with the audience.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exhibit - object or item displayed for public viewing

Etymology

From Latin 'exhibere' (to hold out, display), via Medieval Latin 'exhibitor' (one who displays).
