weatherworn

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðərˌwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌwɔːn / (BrE)
Worn or eroded in appearance due to prolonged exposure to the weather.
worn by the weather

Examples

The old barn’s weatherworn boards gave it a charming rustic look.
He admired the weatherworn statue standing in the garden.
Her weatherworn face told stories of years spent working outdoors.

Synset Examples

a house of weathered shingles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathered - worn or changed in appearance due to weather exposure

Etymology

Formed from 'weather' and 'worn,' literally meaning worn by weather.
