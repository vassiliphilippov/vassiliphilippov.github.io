dissentious

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛn(t)ʃəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛn(t)ʃəs / (BrE)
Inclined to dissent or cause disagreement; characterized by expressing opposition, especially to the majority opinion.
inclined to dissent; argumentative
archaic

Examples

Her dissentious remarks stirred up disagreement among the council members.
The assembly was disrupted by a dissentious group who opposed every proposal.
He became known for his dissentious attitude, always challenging the majority view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissenting - expressing disagreement or refusal to conform with the majority or authority
  • Antonym: conformist - accepting and following established customs or rules

Etymology

From Latin dissentio (to disagree) + English suffix -ous.
