tick-weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtɪkˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Coreopsis, native to North and South America, with bright, daisylike yellow flowers.
plant with yellow daisylike flowers (Coreopsis)

Examples

Clusters of tick-weed brought a splash of yellow to the prairie landscape.
Gardeners often choose tick-weed for its long-lasting, vibrant blooms.
Several species of tick-weed attract butterflies and provide nectar throughout the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coreopsis - a genus of flowering plants commonly known as tickseeds

Etymology

Refers to the resemblance of the plant's seeds to ticks; 'weed' denotes a wild plant.
