amphotericin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.foʊˈtɛr.ɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌam.fəˈtɛr.ɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic and antifungal agent used to treat serious fungal and yeast infections, especially in immunocompromised patients
antibiotic and antifungal drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amphotericin to treat the patient's severe fungal infection.
Researchers are studying the side effects of amphotericin in immunocompromised individuals.
Long-term use of amphotericin can cause kidney toxicity in some cases.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'amphoteric' referring to its chemical nature and '-in'.
