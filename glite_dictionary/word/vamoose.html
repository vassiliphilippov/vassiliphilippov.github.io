to vamoose

1. VERB

/ vəˈmus / (AmE), / vəˈmuːs / (BrE)
to leave or depart suddenly, especially in a hurry
leave suddenly; go away quickly
informal

Examples

We'd better vamoose before the police arrive.
As soon as the argument started to get heated, she chose to vamoose.
He vamoosed out the back door to avoid answering difficult questions.

Synset Examples

She persuaded him to decamp
skip town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decamp - to leave a place suddenly and secretly
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary

Etymology

From American Spanish 'vamos' meaning 'let's go', from Spanish 'ir' (to go).
