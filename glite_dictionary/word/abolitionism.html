abolitionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈlɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌabəˈlɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine or movement that advocates for the abolition of slavery
belief advocating abolition of slavery

Examples

The rise of abolitionism in the 19th century played a crucial role in ending slavery in many countries.
Many prominent leaders were influenced by abolitionism when they fought for civil rights.
Her research focuses on the development of abolitionism in the United States before the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abolitionist - a person who supports or works for abolitionism

Etymology

From 'abolition' + '-ism', modeled after French 'abolitionnisme'.
