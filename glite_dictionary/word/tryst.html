tryst

1. NOUN

/ trɪst / (AmE), / trɪst / (BrE)
a secret meeting, especially between lovers
secret meeting between lovers

Examples

They arranged a secret tryst at the old chapel just before midnight.
Rumors of a romantic tryst spread quickly through the small town.
Their tryst was interrupted when someone unexpectedly entered the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendezvous - a meeting at an agreed time and place, often secret

Etymology

From Old French *tristre* (appointed station), from Latin *tristis* (sad), relating to standing firm or waiting.

2. NOUN

/ traɪst / (AmE), / trɪst / (BrE)
a secret meeting, especially between lovers
secret meeting between lovers

Examples

They arranged a tryst in the quiet corner of the park.
The novel centers around the lovers' tryst at midnight beneath the old oak tree.
Their secret tryst was discovered by a passerby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendezvous - a meeting at an agreed time and place, often secret
  • Synonym: assignation - a secret appointment, especially between lovers

Etymology

From Old French 'triste' (appointed station in hunting), later associated with romantic meetings.
