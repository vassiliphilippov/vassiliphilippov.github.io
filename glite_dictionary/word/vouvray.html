Vouvray

1. NOUN

/ vuːˈvreɪ / (AmE), / vuːˈvreɪ / (BrE)
A dry white French wine, either still or sparkling, made in the Loire valley.
French white wine from the Loire valley

Examples

We ordered a chilled bottle of Vouvray to accompany our seafood dinner.
The subtle flavors of Vouvray pair beautifully with goat cheese.
He prefers sparkling Vouvray over Champagne for special occasions.

Etymology

From French Vouvray, the name of the region in the Loire valley where the wine is produced.
