Leipoa

1. NOUN

/ liˈpoʊ.ə / (AmE), / liˈpəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large Australian birds in the family Megapodiidae, known for including the malleefowl.
genus of Australian birds (malleefowl)

Examples

The Leipoa genus is represented by a single living species, the malleefowl.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Leipoa in the Australian outback.
The Leipoa is notable for its unique method of incubating eggs in mounds of vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleefowl - large, ground-dwelling Australian bird that builds mounds to incubate its eggs

Etymology

From Greek 'leipos' (to be left), used as genus name in scientific taxonomy.
