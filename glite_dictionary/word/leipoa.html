leipoa

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈpoʊə / (AmE), / laɪˈpəʊə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian mound-building birds, notably the malleefowl, which incubates eggs in sandy mounds using natural heat.
Australian mound-building bird genus

Examples

The leipoa carefully tends to its nesting mound, ensuring optimal incubation for its eggs.
Researchers have studied the unique breeding habits of the leipoa in the Australian outback.
Unlike many birds, the leipoa relies on the heat from decomposing vegetation to hatch its young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleefowl - An Australian bird (Leipoa ocellata) that builds large nesting mounds.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Leipoa, genus name, from Greek leipoō (to leave off), referring to its incubation method.
