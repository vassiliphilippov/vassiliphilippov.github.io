to symphonize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmfəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmfəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
(of musical instruments or voices) to play or sound together in harmony.
to sound together in harmony

Examples

The choir and orchestra symphonize beautifully during the grand finale.
Their voices symphonized in perfect harmony, filling the hall with music.
As the instruments symphonize, the audience is swept away by the layered melodies.

Etymology

From symphony + -ize, based on Greek 'symphōnia' (concord, harmony).
