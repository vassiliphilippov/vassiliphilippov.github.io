airgun

1. NOUN

/ ˈerˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈeəˌɡʌn / (BrE)
A gun that fires pellets or other small projectiles using compressed air or gas instead of explosive gunpowder.
gun using compressed air to shoot projectiles

Examples

He practiced shooting targets in the backyard with his airgun.
The museum displayed a collection of antique airguns dating back to the 19th century.
An airgun is often used for pest control and competitive shooting sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air rifle - a rifle that uses compressed air to fire projectiles
  • Antonym: firearm - a weapon that uses gunpowder to fire projectiles

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'gun', referring to use of compressed air.
