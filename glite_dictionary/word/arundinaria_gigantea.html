Arundinaria gigantea

1. NOUN

/ əˌrʌndəˈnɛriə ʤaɪˈgæn.ti.ə / (AmE), / əˌrʌndɪˈnɛːriə dʒɪˈgæn.ti.ə / (BrE)
A tall species of bamboo grass native to the southern United States, often growing in dense thickets; commonly known as giant cane.
tall bamboo grass of southern U.S.

Examples

The wetlands were filled with dense stands of Arundinaria gigantea reaching over ten feet in height.
Early settlers used Arundinaria gigantea for making baskets and fishing poles.
Many animal species depend on Arundinaria gigantea thickets for shelter and food.

Etymology

From Latin 'arundo' (reed) and 'gigantea' (giant), indicating a large reed-like plant.
