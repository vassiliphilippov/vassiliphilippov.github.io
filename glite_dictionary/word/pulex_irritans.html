Pulex irritans

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːlɛks ɪˈrɪtænz / (AmE), / ˈpjuːlɛks ɪˈrɪtænz / (BrE)
A species of flea commonly known as the human flea, which primarily infests humans and domestic animals.
human flea species
medical

Examples

Pulex irritans is responsible for many cases of flea bites in households worldwide.
Researchers found a high concentration of Pulex irritans in urban pet populations.
The bites from Pulex irritans can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small wingless jumping insect that feeds on blood

Etymology

From Latin: 'pulex' meaning 'flea' and 'irritans' meaning 'irritating'.
