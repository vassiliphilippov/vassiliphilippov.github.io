diorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəraɪt / (BrE)
a coarse-grained igneous rock composed mainly of plagioclase feldspar and one or more mafic minerals, typically found in intrusive geological formations
a coarse-grained igneous rock

Examples

The ancient statue was carved from diorite, giving it a distinctive gray appearance.
Geologists discovered layers of diorite beneath the mountain range during the survey.
The museum displayed several diorite tools from early civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granite - a hard, granular igneous rock similar to diorite but with more quartz
  • Synonym: gabbro - a coarse-grained mafic igneous rock, darker and denser than diorite

Etymology

From French diorite, based on Greek diorizein (to distinguish).
