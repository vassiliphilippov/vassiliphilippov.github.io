lyrically

1. ADV

/ ˈlɪrɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is expressive, poetic, or musical; with emotional or imaginative quality.
in a poetic or expressive manner

Examples

She spoke lyrically about her childhood memories during the interview.
The violinist played the solo lyrically, filling the room with emotion.
The novel describes nature lyrically, painting vivid pictures in the reader's mind.

Synset Examples

she danced the part of the Black Swan very lyrically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetically - in a poetic manner
  • Antonym: prosaically - in an unpoetic or matter-of-fact manner

Etymology

From 'lyrical' + '-ly'. 'Lyrical' from Greek 'lyrikos', relating to the lyre.
