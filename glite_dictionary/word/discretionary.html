discretionary

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɛʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛʃənəri / (BrE)
(especially of funds or income) available to be used at one's own judgment or choice; not earmarked for a particular purpose.
available for use as needed; not assigned

Examples

Employees may use their discretionary funds to attend professional development workshops of their choice.
After paying all the essentials, she set aside some discretionary income for entertainment and travel.
The manager has discretionary authority to allocate resources where they are most needed.

Synset Examples

discretionary funds
discretionary income

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mandatory - required by rules or authority, not optional

Etymology

From Latin 'discretio' (separation, distinction) via medieval Latin 'discretionarius'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɛʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛʃənəri / (BrE)
(of an action, power, or amount of money) available for use at the discretion of the user; subject to one's own judgment and choice.
subject to one's own judgment; optional

Examples

The manager has discretionary authority to approve travel expenses.
Employees are given a discretionary bonus based on performance.
The grant provides discretionary funds for research projects.

Synset Examples

The commission has discretionary power to award extra funds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mandatory - required by rules or laws; not optional

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'discretionarius', from 'discretio' meaning 'discernment; discretion'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00723263-s
Synset POS: s
