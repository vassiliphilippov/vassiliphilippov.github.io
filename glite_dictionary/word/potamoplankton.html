potamoplankton

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæmoʊˈplæŋktɑn / (AmE), / pəʊˌtaməʊˈplæŋktən / (BrE)
Plankton that inhabit rivers, as distinguished from those in lakes or oceans.
plankton found in rivers
medical

Examples

Scientists collect samples of potamoplankton to study the health of river ecosystems.
Changes in water flow can greatly impact the diversity of potamoplankton.
Unlike marine plankton, potamoplankton are specially adapted to freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plankton - microscopic organisms drifting in water
  • Degree Lower: limnoplankton - plankton that inhabit lakes

Etymology

From Greek potamos (river) + plankton (drifting).
