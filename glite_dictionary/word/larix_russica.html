Larix russica

1. PROPN

/ ˈlærɪks ˈruːsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈlarɪks ˈrʊsɪkə / (BrE)
A medium-sized larch tree native to northeastern Russia and Siberia, having a narrow conic crown and soft, narrow, bright-green leaves; cultivated for ornamental or silvicultural purposes.
species of larch tree from northeastern Russia

Examples

The Larix russica is favored in some arboretums for its striking appearance.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Larix russica in Siberian forests.
Gardeners choose Larix russica for its soft, bright-green foliage and narrow crown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - any tree of the genus Larix, including various species of deciduous conifer

Etymology

From Latin 'Larix' (larch) and 'russica' (Russian).
