Heteromeles

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əˈrɑməliːz / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈmiːliːz / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs, with one species (toyon), sometimes classified within the genus Photinia.
plant genus containing toyon

Examples

The Heteromeles bush, known commonly as toyon, grows wild across California hills.
Gardeners often choose Heteromeles for its hardy nature and bright red berries.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Heteromeles should be grouped with the genus Photinia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toyon - evergreen shrub native to California, also known as California holly

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) and mēlon (apple), referring to the plant's fruit.

2. NOUN

/ hɛt̬.əˈrɑː.mə.liz / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.ər.əˈmiːliːz / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs containing a single species, toyon, native to western North America; sometimes classified under the genus Photinia.
a genus of evergreen shrubs (toyon)

Examples

The Heteromeles genus is best known for its only species, the toyon shrub native to California.
Botanists sometimes classify Heteromeles as part of the genus Photinia, though this is debated.
The bright red berries of Heteromeles attract many species of birds during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Photinia - a genus of flowering plants closely related to Heteromeles
  • Synonym: toyon - the only species in the genus Heteromeles

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + mele (apple, fruit), referring to the distinct fruit of the plant.
