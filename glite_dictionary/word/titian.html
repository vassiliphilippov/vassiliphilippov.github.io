Titian

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
(c. 1490–1576) An Italian Renaissance painter, regarded as one of the leading figures of the Venetian school.
Italian Renaissance painter (Venetian school)

Examples

The rich colors in Titian's paintings influenced generations of artists.
Many art historians consider Titian a master of portraiture and use of color.
During our trip to Venice, we admired several works by Titian in the Doge's Palace.

Etymology

From Italian Tiziano Vecellio, Latinized as Titianus.
