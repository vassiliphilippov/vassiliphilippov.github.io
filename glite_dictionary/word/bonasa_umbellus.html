Bonasa umbellus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈneɪsə ʌmˈbɛləs / (AmE), / bəˈnɑːsə ʌmˈbɛləs / (BrE)
a North American grouse with a mottled brown plumage, commonly hunted as game in eastern United States and Canada; also known as the ruffed grouse
North American game bird (ruffed grouse)

Examples

The Bonasa umbellus is a favorite target of bird hunters in the Appalachian forests.
Many wildlife reserves monitor the population of Bonasa umbellus to ensure healthy ecosystems.
I spotted a Bonasa umbellus drumming in the underbrush during my morning hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruffed grouse - common name for Bonasa umbellus, a North American game bird

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Bonasa' and species epithet 'umbellus', meaning 'a little shade' or 'umbrella', referring to its ruff.
