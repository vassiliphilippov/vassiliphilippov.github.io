bisexuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪˌsɛkʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˌsɛkʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
the condition of showing characteristics of both male and female sexes, especially in biological or physical traits
showing characteristics of both sexes

Examples

Some plants exhibit bisexuality and produce both pollen and ovules.
The scientist explained bisexuality in certain fish species as an adaptation to environmental conditions.
Researchers studied how bisexuality affects reproductive success in various animal populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermaphroditism - the condition of having both male and female reproductive organs

Etymology

From Latin bis (twice, double) + sexus (sex) + -ality (quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪˌsɛkʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˌsɛkʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being sexually attracted to both men and women.
sexual attraction to both sexes

Examples

Many people are still learning about bisexuality and what it means in society.
His openness about his bisexuality has helped others feel more comfortable with their identity.
Discussions around bisexuality have become more common in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterosexuality - sexual attraction to people of the opposite sex
  • Antonym: homosexuality - sexual attraction to people of the same sex

Etymology

From 'bisexual' (from Latin 'bi-' meaning 'two' + 'sexual') + '-ity'.
