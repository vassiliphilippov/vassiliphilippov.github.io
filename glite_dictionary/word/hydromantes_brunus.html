Hydromantes brunus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.drəˈmæntiːz ˈbruːnəs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.drəˈmantɛs ˈbruːnəs / (BrE)
A species of salamander native to California, inhabiting cliff crevices and rocky slopes (taluses); also known as the limestone salamander.
a species of salamander

Examples

Biologists recently discovered a population of Hydromantes brunus living on a remote limestone cliff.
The Hydromantes brunus is threatened by habitat loss in its limited range in California.
Unlike other salamanders, Hydromantes brunus prefers to hide in rocky taluses and cliff crevices.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Hydromantes' meaning 'water prophet' and 'brunus' meaning 'brown'.
