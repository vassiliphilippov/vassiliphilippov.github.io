affricate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.rɪ.kət / (AmE), / ˈæf.rɪ.kət / (BrE)
A speech sound consisting of a stop followed immediately by a fricative, both articulated at the same point, as in 'ch' in 'chair' and 'j' in 'joy'.
a consonant sound combining a stop and a fricative

Examples

The English sound affricate, as in 'church', combines a stop and a fricative.
Children often have difficulty pronouncing the affricate in words like 'judge'.
Phonetic analysis revealed that the language features a rare retroflex affricate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fricative - a consonant produced by forcing air through a narrow channel
  • Synonym: plosive - a consonant sound produced by stopping airflow entirely then releasing it

Etymology

From Latin affricatus, past participle of affricare (to rub against).
