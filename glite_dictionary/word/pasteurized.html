pasteurized

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæstʃəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstʃəraɪzd / (BrE)
(of a food or drink, especially milk) having been heated to a specific temperature to destroy harmful microorganisms and prevent spoilage or fermentation.
heated to destroy germs, as in milk or juice

Examples

You should store pasteurized milk in the refrigerator to prevent it from spoiling.
Drinks made with pasteurized juice are considered safer for young children.
The label states that the cheese is made from pasteurized cow’s milk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpasteurized - not having been subjected to pasteurization
  • Degree Higher: sterilized - made free from all living microorganisms

Etymology

From Pasteur, after Louis Pasteur (French chemist).
