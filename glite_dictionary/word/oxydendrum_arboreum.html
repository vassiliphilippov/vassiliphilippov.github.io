Oxydendrum arboreum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdɛndrəm ɑrˈbɔriəm / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛndrəm ɑːˈbɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its deeply fissured bark, clusters of small fragrant white flowers, and sour-tasting leaves.
a North American deciduous tree (sourwood)

Examples

The Oxydendrum arboreum adds a burst of white flowers to the forest in midsummer.
Many beekeepers prize Oxydendrum arboreum for producing high-quality honey.
Hikers recognized the Oxydendrum arboreum by its distinctive sour leaves and deeply furrowed bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourwood - common name for Oxydendrum arboreum

Etymology

From Greek 'oxys' (sour) + 'dendron' (tree), referring to the tree's sour-tasting leaves.
