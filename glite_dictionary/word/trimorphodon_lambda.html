Trimorphodon lambda

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌmɔrfəˌdɑn ˈlæmbdə / (AmE), / traɪˌmɔːfəˌdɒn ˈlæmbdə / (BrE)
A species of colubrid snake, known as the Sonoran lyre snake, found in desert regions of southwestern North America.
species of desert dweller lyre snake

Examples

The Trimorphodon lambda is well adapted to the rocky deserts of Arizona and Sonora.
Researchers observed that Trimorphodon lambda hunts primarily at night.
Wildlife guides describe the Trimorphodon lambda as a nonvenomous species with a distinct V-shaped mark on its head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyre snake - a group of rear-fanged colubrid snakes found in the Americas

Etymology

From New Latin: genus 'Trimorphodon' (meaning 'three-shaped tooth') + Greek letter 'lambda,' referring to a head marking.
