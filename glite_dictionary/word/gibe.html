gibe

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
an insulting or mocking remark intended to hurt or provoke someone
insulting or mocking remark

Examples

She ignored his cutting gibe and continued speaking calmly.
The politician was quick to fire back at the gibes from his opponents.
Every meeting seems to end with a gibe from someone in the audience.

Synset Examples

his parting shot was ‘drop dead’
she threw shafts of sarcasm
she takes a dig at me every chance she gets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taunt - an insulting or mocking remark intended to provoke
  • Synonym: jeer - a rude or mocking remark, often made loudly
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

Possibly from Old French *giber* (to handle roughly), origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
to make mocking or jeering remarks; to laugh at with contempt and derision
to mock or jeer at someone

Examples

The students began to gibe at their classmate's mistakes during the presentation.
He couldn't help but gibe at his rival's failed attempts on stage.
Spectators gibed mercilessly at the losing team as they left the field.

Synset Examples

The crowd jeered at the speaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - make rude and mocking remarks
  • Synonym: taunt - make insulting or mocking remarks

Etymology

From Old French *giber* (to handle roughly, shake, mock).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00851872-v
Synset POS: v
