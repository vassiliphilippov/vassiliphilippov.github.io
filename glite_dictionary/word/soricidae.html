Soricidae

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɒˈrɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insectivorous mammals known as shrews, characterized by their pointed snouts and tiny eyes.
the shrew family of mammals

Examples

The Soricidae are among the smallest mammals found across the world.
Many members of the Soricidae family have venomous saliva, uncommon among mammals.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of various Soricidae species in temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - small mammal with a pointed snout; member of Soricidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Soricidae, based on Latin sorix (shrew).
