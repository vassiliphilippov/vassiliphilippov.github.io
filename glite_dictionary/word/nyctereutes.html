Nyctereutes

1. NOUN

/ nɪk.tɪˈruː.tiːz / (AmE), / nɪk.təˈruː.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small, dog-like mammals known as raccoon dogs, native to East Asia.
genus of raccoon dogs

Examples

The genus Nyctereutes includes the well-known raccoon dog of East Asia.
Scientists are studying how Nyctereutes species adapt to changing environments.
Unlike true foxes, members of Nyctereutes have unique facial markings and behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raccoon dog - A wild animal of East Asia that resembles a raccoon and belongs to the genus Nyctereutes

Etymology

From Greek nux, nuktos (night) and erēmos (wanderer), referring to the animal's nocturnal habits.

2. NOUN

/ nɪkˌtɛr.iˈuː.tiz / (AmE), / nɪkˌtɛr.iˈjuː.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small canids known as raccoon dogs, native to East Asia and eastern Europe.
genus of raccoon dogs

Examples

The genus Nyctereutes includes the well-known raccoon dog found in Asia.
Members of Nyctereutes are unique among canids for their omnivorous diet and hibernation behavior.
Recent studies have shown that Nyctereutes has expanded its range into eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raccoon dog - A canid species (Nyctereutes procyonoides) in the genus Nyctereutes

Etymology

From Greek nykt-, nyx (night) + erētēs (wanderer), referring to nocturnal habits.
