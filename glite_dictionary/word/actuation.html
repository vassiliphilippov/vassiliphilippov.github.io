actuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.tjuˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action of causing something to operate or move, especially by means of a mechanism or device
the act of causing movement or operation

Examples

The actuation of the engine requires precise timing and control.
Modern robotics relies on the rapid actuation of various mechanical parts.
Valve actuation can be controlled electronically in advanced car engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activation - the process of making something active or operative

Etymology

From Latin actuatio (a putting into action), from actuare (to set in motion); related to 'actuate'.
