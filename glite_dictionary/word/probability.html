probability

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːbəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌprɒbəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the extent to which something is likely to happen, often expressed as a numerical ratio or percentage
likelihood of something happening

Examples

The probability of rain today is much higher than it was yesterday.
In statistics, we often calculate the probability of certain outcomes.
There's a high probability that the train will be late again.

Synset Examples

the probability that an unbiased coin will fall with the head up is 0.5
if that phone call is for me, chances are it's my wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possibility - something that may happen or be the case
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is certain to happen or be the case
  • Synonym: chance - the likelihood of something happening

Etymology

From Latin probabilitas, from probabilis (provable, credible).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌprɒbəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being likely to happen; something that is likely to occur
likelihood of something happening

Examples

There is a strong probability of rain tomorrow.
After three failures, the probability of success seemed low.
It's only a matter of probability that this will occur again.

Synset Examples

for a while mutiny seemed a probability
going by past experience, there was a high probability that the visitors were lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likelihood - the state of being likely or probable
  • Degree Lower: possibility - something that may happen or be the case
  • Degree Higher: certainty - something that is sure to happen
  • Antonym: improbability - the state of being unlikely to occur

Etymology

From Latin probabilitas (likelihood), from probabilis (provable, likely).
