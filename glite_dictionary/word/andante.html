andante

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈdɑnteɪ / (AmE), / ænˈdɑːnteɪ / (BrE)
(in music) a moderately slow tempo, typically considered to be at a walking pace.
moderately slow musical tempo

Examples

The piece begins with an andante section that sets a calm, reflective mood.
The composer marked the movement to be played andante, creating a gentle, flowing feel.
After the fast introduction, the music settles into an andante that feels like a peaceful stroll.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adagio - a slow tempo in music, slower than andante
  • Degree Higher: moderato - a moderate tempo in music, faster than andante

Etymology

From Italian 'andante', present participle of 'andare' meaning 'to go'.

4. NOUN

/ ænˈdɑnteɪ / (AmE), / anˈdanteɪ / (BrE)
a piece of music, or a section of a larger work, performed at a moderately slow tempo
a musical piece played moderately slow

Examples

The pianist played the andante with remarkable sensitivity and poise.
In the second movement, the composer marked the music as andante to convey a calm mood.
Several of Beethoven's symphonies include an andante section characterized by gentle melodies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adagio - a musical passage played slowly
  • Degree Higher: allegro - a musical passage played at a fast tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'andante' (walking), present participle of 'andare' (to go).
