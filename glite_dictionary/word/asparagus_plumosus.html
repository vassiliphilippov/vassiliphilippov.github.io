Asparagus plumosus

1. NOUN

/ əˈspærəɡəs pluːˈmoʊsəs / (AmE), / əˈspærəɡəs pluːˈməʊsəs / (BrE)
a species of evergreen climbing plant with feathery foliage, native to South Africa and often used in floral arrangements
species of climbing plant with feathery foliage

Examples

The florist arranged delicate sprigs of Asparagus plumosus among the roses for added texture.
Asparagus plumosus is often mistaken for a true fern due to its light, feathery appearance.
Gardeners value Asparagus plumosus for its graceful, trailing stems and easy maintenance indoors.

Etymology

From the genus name Asparagus (Latin, from Greek asparagos) and the Latin 'plumosus', meaning 'feathery'.
