yawl

1. NOUN

/ jɔl / (AmE), / jɔːl / (BrE)
A sailing boat with two masts, the smaller mizzen mast located behind the rudderpost.
two-masted sailing boat

Examples

The yawl sailed gracefully across the bay in the early morning light.
He restored an old wooden yawl and entered it in the regatta.
Their family spent summers cruising the coastline in a sturdy yawl.

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *jol* (boat).

2. NOUN

/ jɔl / (AmE), / jɔːl / (BrE)
a ship's small boat, usually rowed by four or six oars
ship's small rowboat

Examples

The sailors lowered the yawl into the water to reach the shore.
A crew of six manned the yawl, rowing steadily towards the beach.
We used the yawl to transport supplies from the ship to the island.

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *jol* (small boat).
