purr

1. NOUN

/ pɜr / (AmE), / pɜː / (BrE)
a low, continuous, vibrating sound made by a cat when it is content
the sound a contented cat makes

Examples

The kitten curled up on my lap and began to purr softly.
The familiar purr of the cat filled the quiet living room.
Her happiness was as obvious as the purr of a contented pet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct, continuous sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07405245-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ pɝː / (AmE), / pɜː / (BrE)
(of a cat) to make a low continuous vibrating sound in the throat to show contentment, or to make a similar sound to express pleasure.
make a low vibrating sound (as a cat)

Examples

The kitten began to purr softly as soon as she stroked its back.
Whenever he sits by the fire, his old cat likes to purr in his lap.
I could hear the engine purr quietly, reminding me of a contented cat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: growl - make a low guttural sound (as a warning or anger)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01054808-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɜr / (AmE), / pɜː / (BrE)
(of a machine or vehicle) to make a soft, continuous, vibrating sound, similar to a cat's purr.
make a soft, continuous, vibrating sound

Examples

The new electric car's engine purrs quietly as it accelerates down the road.
She sat listening to the old refrigerator purring in the kitchen corner.
As we drove through the night, the motorcycle purred beneath us, steady and smooth.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whir - to make a low, continuous, regular sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02193033-v
Synset POS: v
