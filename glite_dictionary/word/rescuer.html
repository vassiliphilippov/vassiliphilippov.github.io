rescuer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛskjuɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɛskjuːə / (BrE)
a person who saves someone or something from danger or harm
person who saves from danger

Examples

The rescuer pulled the dog out of the freezing river just in time.
She always dreamed of becoming a rescuer and helping people in emergencies.
Firefighters acted as rescuers during the dramatic mountain rescue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savior - a person who saves someone or something
  • Synonym: protector - a person who protects or defends someone

Etymology

From 'rescue' + '-er,' indicating an agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛskjuːər / (AmE), / ˈrɛskjuːə / (BrE)
a person who saves someone from harm or danger
person who saves from danger

Examples

The firefighter was hailed as a rescuer after pulling the child from the burning house.
Without a rescuer arriving in time, the hikers could have been lost in the mountains.
The stranded sailor waited anxiously for a rescuer to spot his signal flare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savior - a person who saves someone or something from danger
  • Antonym: captor - a person who takes or holds someone captive

Etymology

From the verb 'rescue', ultimately from Old French 'rescourre', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'excutere' (to shake off).
