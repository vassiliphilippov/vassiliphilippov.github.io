Pliny

1. PROPN

/ ˈplɪni / (AmE), / ˈplɪni / (BrE)
(proper noun) A Roman author and naturalist (23–79 AD), known for his encyclopedic work 'Natural History' and for dying during the eruption of Mount Vesuvius.
Roman author and naturalist (23–79 AD)

Examples

Many facts about the ancient world are drawn from Pliny's writings.
Pliny died while witnessing the catastrophic eruption of Mount Vesuvius.
Historians often reference Pliny when studying early Roman natural history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Pliny the Younger - Nephew of Pliny the Elder, Roman writer and lawyer

Etymology

Latin Plinius, Roman family name.
