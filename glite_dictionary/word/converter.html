converter

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝː.tɚ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜː.tə / (BrE)
a device that changes something from one form, substance, or state into another
device for changing form or state

Examples

The car's catalytic converter needs to be replaced due to emission problems.
She bought a power converter to use her laptop while traveling in Europe.
A digital-to-analog converter allows old speakers to connect to modern devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformer - an electrical device for changing voltage

Etymology

From Latin 'convertere', meaning 'to turn around, transform'.
