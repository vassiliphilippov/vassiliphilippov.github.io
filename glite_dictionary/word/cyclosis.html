cyclosis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkloʊsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈkləʊsɪs / (BrE)
the streaming movement of cytoplasm within a living cell, enabling the distribution of materials
circulation of cytoplasm in a cell
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the cyclosis in the plant cell was clearly visible.
Some scientists study cyclosis to understand how nutrients are transported inside cells.
Disruption of cyclosis can affect the health and function of a cell.

Etymology

From Greek "kyklosis" (circular motion), from "kyklos" (circle).
