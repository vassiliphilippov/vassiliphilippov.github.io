sintered

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪntɚd / (AmE), / ˈsɪntəd / (BrE)
formed into a solid mass from powder or small particles by the application of heat and usually pressure, without melting the material to the point of liquefaction.
formed by heat and pressure (not melted)

Examples

The brake pads are made from sintered metal for greater durability.
Sintered ceramics are commonly used in advanced engineering applications.
The filter consists of sintered bronze particles fused together to allow air to pass through.

Etymology

From 'sinter', which comes from German 'Sinter' (slag or cinder), related to the process of binding by heat.
