Mastigomycotina

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪgoʊˌmaɪkəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪgəʊˌmʌɪkəʊˈtʌɪnə / (BrE)
a former subclass of fungi characterized by motile spores and gametes, sometimes grouped with Phycomycetes in older classifications
former subclass of motile-spored fungi

Examples

Recent studies have led to the reclassification of Mastigomycotina into other fungal groups.
The Mastigomycotina were distinguished by their ability to produce motile zoospores.
Mycology courses often discuss how Mastigomycotina fit into traditional classification systems.

Etymology

From Greek mastix (whip) + mykes (fungus), referring to the flagellated spores.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪɡoʊmaɪkəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪɡəʊˌmaɪkəʊˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a former subdivision of fungi characterized by motile spores and gametes, sometimes grouped with Zygomycota in older classifications
former fungal subdivision with motile spores

Examples

The fungi classified under Mastigomycotina have motile spores, distinguishing them from other groups.
Many mycologists no longer use the term Mastigomycotina in modern fungal taxonomy.
Studies of aquatic environments often mention Mastigomycotina for their role in decomposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phycomycetes - group of simple fungi, often including Mastigomycotina
  • Synonym: Zygomycota - a division of fungi with which Mastigomycotina is sometimes grouped

Etymology

From Greek mastigo- (whip) + mycota (fungi), referring to flagellated spores.
