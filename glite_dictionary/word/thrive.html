to thrive

1. VERB

/ θraɪv / (AmE), / θraɪv / (BrE)
to grow, develop, or be successful; to prosper or flourish, especially in favorable conditions
to prosper or flourish

Examples

Small businesses can thrive when given the right support and opportunities.
Many plants thrive in warm, sunny environments.
His company began to thrive after he introduced new management strategies.

Synset Examples

The new student is thriving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prosper - to be successful, especially financially; to flourish
  • Synonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed; to be unsuccessful

Etymology

From Old Norse 'þrífask' (to thrive, grasp).

2. VERB

/ θraɪv / (AmE), / θraɪv / (BrE)
to grow, develop, or be successful with vigor and health; to prosper
grow vigorously; prosper

Examples

Many native plants thrive in the wet climate of the Pacific Northwest.
During the economic boom, small businesses thrive and expand rapidly.
Her confidence seemed to thrive as she overcame each new challenge.

Synset Examples

The deer population in this town is thriving
business is booming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prosper - to succeed, especially financially; to flourish
  • Synonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way

Etymology

From Old Norse 'þrífa' (to thrive, grasp, get hold of).
