carinate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛrəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrɪneɪt / (BrE)
having a ridge or shaped like a keel; resembling the keel of a ship, especially used to describe anatomical or botanical structures
having a ridge like a keel

Examples

The leaf margins are carinate, forming a distinct ridge along the edge.
Some bird species are distinguished by having a carinate sternum.
The orchid's carinate sepal helps identify its species.

Synset Examples

a carinate sepal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keeled - having a ridge or projecting line like a keel

Etymology

From Latin 'carinatus', from 'carina' meaning 'keel (of a ship)'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkær.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkær.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
A bird having a keeled breastbone, allowing attachment of flight muscles; commonly refers to birds in the subclass Carinatae.
bird with a keeled breastbone

Examples

The majority of living birds are classified as carinates due to their keeled breastbones.
Unlike ratites, carinates possess a breastbone that supports powerful flight muscles.
The fossil record shows a clear distinction between carinates and flightless birds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ratite - flightless bird lacking a keeled breastbone

Etymology

From Latin 'carinatus', meaning 'provided with a keel'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01520607-n
Synset POS: n
