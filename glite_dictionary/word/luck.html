luck

1. NOUN

/ lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
success or good things happening by chance and not as a result of one's own actions or planning.
chance leading to good outcome

Examples

Winning the lottery was pure luck for her, as she had never expected to win.
Sometimes, finding the right job is more about luck than skill.
He attributed his success to hard work, but a little luck certainly helped.

Synset Examples

it was my good luck to be there
they say luck is a lady
it was as if fortune guided his hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as a force affecting people's lives
  • Antonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate condition or event

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *luc* (fortune, happiness).

2. NOUN

/ lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
the force that seems to cause good or bad things to happen to people in a way that cannot be controlled or explained
chance occurrence; fortune

Examples

Winning the lottery was a stroke of pure luck for the Johnson family.
Some people believe you make your own luck through hard work and determination.
He blamed his failure on bad luck, not on a lack of effort.

Synset Examples

bad luck caused his downfall
we ran into each other by pure chance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chance - the occurrence of events without apparent cause, random fate
  • Synonym: fortune - luck, especially good luck
  • Antonym: misfortune - bad luck or an unlucky event

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *luc* (happiness, good fortune), related to German *Glück* (fortune).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
a person's overall circumstances or condition in life, including everything that happens to them; fate or fortune
overall circumstances or fate in life

Examples

He considered himself a victim of bad luck, though he worked hard all his life.
Some people seem to be born with good luck and never face major hardships.
The luck of the Irish is often mentioned when someone overcomes great odds.

Synset Examples

whatever my fortune may be
deserved a better fate
has a happy lot
the luck of the Irish
a victim of circumstances
success that was her portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external, arbitrary force affecting human affairs

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *luc* (happiness, good fortune).
