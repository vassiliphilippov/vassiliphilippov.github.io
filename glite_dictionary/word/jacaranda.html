jacaranda

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒækəˈrændə / (AmE), / ˌdʒækəˈrændə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to South America, especially Brazil, known for its heavy, hard, dark-colored wood streaked with black, often used in furniture and construction.
Brazilian timber tree with dark wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from jacaranda, giving it a rich, dark appearance.
Many Brazilian homes feature floors made of durable jacaranda wood.
The export of jacaranda timber is an important industry in Brazil.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jacarandá', from Tupi 'yakaranda' (a tree species).
