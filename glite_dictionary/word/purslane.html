purslane

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːs.leɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɜːs.leɪn / (BrE)
a small annual plant (Portulaca oleracea) with thick, fleshy leaves, commonly used as a potherb or in salads and sometimes considered a weed
a succulent leafy plant, often eaten or considered a weed

Examples

We harvested some purslane from the garden for our salad.
Many gardeners consider purslane a troublesome weed, but it is edible and nutritious.
The market offered fresh bundles of purslane alongside more common herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portulaca - genus of flowering plants to which purslane belongs

Etymology

From Old French *pourcelaine*, from Medieval Latin *porcil(l)ana* (a kind of plant, purslane).
