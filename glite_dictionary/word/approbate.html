to approbate

1. VERB

/ ˈæprəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæprəˌbeɪt / (BrE)
to approve or sanction officially, especially in a formal or legal context
officially approve or sanction
legal

Examples

The board must approbate the proposal before any changes can be implemented.
The treaty was not approbated by the legislative assembly until the following year.
Before issuing the license, the committee will need to approbate the application.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to officially agree to or accept as satisfactory
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to approve or accept

Etymology

From Latin 'approbat-', past participle stem of 'approbare' (to approve).

2. VERB

/ ˈæp.roʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæp.rəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
to accept or approve (especially documents) as valid or satisfactory, often in a formal or legal context
formally accept or approve (documents)
legal

Examples

The court must approbate all agreements before they become binding.
The notary approbated the deeds after verifying their authenticity.
Before proceeding, the council will approbate the submitted documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to officially accept or agree to
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept

Etymology

From Latin *approbatus*, past participle of *approbare* (to approve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00688934-v
Synset POS: v
