Chloroxylon swietenia

1. NOUN

/ klɔːroʊˈzaɪlɒn swiːˈtiːniə / (AmE), / klɔːrəʊˈzaɪlən swiːˈtiːnɪə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to South and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard, lustrous, yellowish wood used in fine woodworking.
East Indian hardwood tree (satinwood)

Examples

The lustrous wood of Chloroxylon swietenia is highly prized in cabinet making.
Forests in southern India are home to Chloroxylon swietenia, commonly known as East Indian satinwood.
Furniture crafted from Chloroxylon swietenia displays a rich yellow hue and fine texture.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name from Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'xylon' (wood); species name honors Swedish botanist Gerard van Swieten.
