guile

1. NOUN

/ ɡaɪl / (AmE), / ɡaɪl / (BrE)
clever or cunning intelligence used to deceive or trick people.
cunning or deceitful intelligence

Examples

Relying on his guile, the con artist managed to trick several people out of their money.
She handled the negotiations with remarkable guile and subtlety.
Despite all his guile, he could not convince her to invest in the scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; free of deceit
  • Synonym: craft - skill, especially in a cunning or deceitful way

Etymology

From Old French 'guile' (deceit), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ɡaɪl / (AmE), / ɡaɪl / (BrE)
clever and dishonest behavior used to deceive or trick people
cunning or sly deception

Examples

The politician achieved his goals through guile and charm.
With remarkable guile, she convinced everyone the plan was her own.
He managed to escape the trap using nothing but his guile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful
  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit

Etymology

From Old French 'guile' (deceit), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ɡaɪl / (AmE), / ɡaɪl / (BrE)
the use of clever and usually dishonest methods to achieve something; cunning or deceitfulness
cunning or slyness

Examples

He used his guile to outsmart the competition and win the contract.
The spy survived on a mixture of guile and luck.
She approached the negotiations with both charm and guile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Antonym: honesty - quality of being truthful or sincere

Etymology

From Old French *guile*, probably of Germanic origin.
