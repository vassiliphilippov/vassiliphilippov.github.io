abstemiousness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstiːmiəsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstiːmiəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of avoiding excessive eating and drinking; self-restraint in consumption of food and alcohol.
moderation in eating and drinking

Examples

Despite the lavish buffet, his abstemiousness kept him from overindulging.
Her abstemiousness at parties was well known among her friends.
He admired her abstemiousness in both diet and drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - avoidance of excess or extremes
  • Antonym: gluttony - excessive eating or drinking

Etymology

From Latin abstemius (sober, restrained).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈstiːmiəsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstiːmiəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of restraining oneself from indulging in excess, especially with food or drink
self-restraint from excess

Examples

Many admired her abstemiousness during the lavish banquet.
His abstemiousness extended to all aspects of his daily routine, not just his diet.
The monk's abstemiousness was evident in his simple meals and modest living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes, especially in behavior or expression
  • Antonym: indulgence - the act of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasure, especially more than is good for one

Etymology

From Latin 'abstemius' (sober, moderate), via 'abstemious' + '-ness'.
