to alkalise

1. VERB

/ ˈæl.kə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make or become more alkaline (less acidic), especially by increasing the pH value
make or become more alkaline

Examples

Adding baking soda will alkalise the water, increasing its pH.
The liquid must be alkalised before use in this experiment.
Some diets claim to alkalise your body, though the scientific evidence is limited.

Synset Examples

the solution alkalized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acidify - to make or become more acidic

Etymology

From 'alkali' + '-ise' (British spelling of '-ize'); ultimately from Arabic 'al-qaly' (ashes of saltwort), via Medieval Latin and French.
