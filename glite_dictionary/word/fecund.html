fecund

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːkənd, ˈfɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈfiːkənd, ˈfɛkənd / (BrE)
(of people, animals, plants, or land) able to produce many offspring, seeds, or fruit; highly fertile or productive.
able to produce many offspring or plants

Examples

The fecund soil in the valley produced a rich harvest year after year.
That author has a fecund imagination, constantly coming up with new story ideas.
The fecund rabbit population quickly overwhelmed the small island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertile - (of soil, land, or people) able to produce abundant vegetation or offspring
  • Antonym: barren - (of land, plants, or women) too poor to produce much or any vegetation or offspring

Etymology

From Latin fecundus (fruitful, fertile).

Examples
