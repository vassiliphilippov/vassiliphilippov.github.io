to overproduce

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚprəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to produce more of something than is needed or can be sold or used
produce too much of something

Examples

The factory tends to overproduce when demand forecasts are inaccurate.
Farmers often overproduce crops, leading to surplus and lower prices.
Countries that overproduce certain goods may face economic challenges from market saturation.

Synset Examples

The country overproduces cars

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underproduce - to produce less of something than is needed

Etymology

Prefix 'over-' (excessively) + 'produce'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərprəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to produce more of something than is needed or wanted
produce more than necessary

Examples

To avoid financial losses, the company must not overproduce its products.
If farmers overproduce crops, the market can become saturated and prices may fall.
Some industries are criticized because they overproduce goods that often go to waste.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underproduce - to produce less than what is needed or wanted

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excess)+ 'produce' (to bring forth or manufacture), first recorded in the late 19th century.
