Agrimonia procera

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.rɪˈmoʊ.ni.ə proʊˈsɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.rɪˈməʊ.ni.ə prəʊˈsɪə.rə / (BrE)
A fragrant European perennial herb, typically found at the edges of woodlands on moist soil; also known as fragrant agrimony.
a European fragrant perennial herb

Examples

Agrimonia procera can be found growing in the damp woodland areas of central Europe.
Botanists often study Agrimonia procera for its distinctive fragrant leaves and yellow flowers.
The conservation group is working to preserve habitats for Agrimonia procera and other native plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agrimony - a genus of perennial herbs of the rose family

Etymology

From Latin 'agrimonia' (a kind of plant), 'procera' meaning 'tall, high'.
