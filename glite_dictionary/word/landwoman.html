landwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlændˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who lives and works on the land, especially in farming or agriculture
female agricultural worker

Examples

During the war, many a landwoman played a vital role in keeping the farms running.
As a proud landwoman, she rose before dawn each day to tend the fields.
The exhibition features photographs of landwomen harvesting crops in the 1940s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landman - a person who lives and works on land, especially in farming or rural occupations
  • Synonym: landgirl - young woman working in agriculture, especially in Britain during WWII

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + 'woman', after 'landman' and in parallel to forms like 'landgirl'.
