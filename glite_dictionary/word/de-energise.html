to de-energise

1. VERB

/ diˈɛnərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / diˈɛnəˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to remove or cut off the supply of energy or power, especially to electrical equipment
cut off power or energy supply

Examples

The technician must de-energise the equipment before performing repairs.
To ensure safety, always de-energise the circuit before inspection.
The power plant was temporarily de-energised during the maintenance procedure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to energise - to supply with energy or power

Etymology

From the prefix de- (removal of) + energise, meaning to supply with energy.
