thigh

1. NOUN

/ θaɪ / (AmE), / θaɪ / (BrE)
the upper part of the leg of a bird, especially as served as food
upper part of a bird's leg (food)

Examples

She ordered a grilled chicken thigh with vegetables for lunch.
The recipe calls for two turkey thighs and a selection of herbs.
He prefers the flavor of the thigh over the breast in roast chicken.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drumstick - the lower joint of a chicken or fowl's leg (food)

2. NOUN

/ θaɪ / (AmE), / θaɪ / (BrE)
the part of the leg between the hip and the knee
upper part of the leg

Examples

She bruised her thigh when she bumped into the table.
The thigh muscles are important for running and jumping.
A doctor examined the patient's injured thigh after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: calf - the back part of the lower leg below the knee
  • Degree Higher: hip - the joint connecting the thigh with the trunk

Etymology

Old English 'þēoh', related to Dutch 'dij' and German 'Dach' (thigh).
