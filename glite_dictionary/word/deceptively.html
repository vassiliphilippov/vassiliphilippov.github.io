deceptively

1. ADV

/ dɪˈsɛptɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛptɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that gives a false impression or misleads; so as to make something seem different from what it really is.
in a misleading or deceptive way

Examples

The cake looked deceptively simple to bake, but it was actually quite difficult.
He spoke deceptively calmly, masking his true frustration.
The path was deceptively straightforward, but it led us in the wrong direction.

Synset Examples

the exam looked deceptively easy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misleadingly - in a way that causes someone to have a wrong idea or impression

Etymology

Formed from 'deceptive' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'decipere' (to deceive).
