hag

1. NOUN

/ hæɡ / (AmE), / hæɡ / (BrE)
an eel-like marine animal with a round mouth surrounded by tentacles, feeding on dead or trapped fish by boring into their bodies
an eel-like marine scavenger fish

Examples

The hag feeds by boring into the bodies of dead fish lying on the ocean floor.
Researchers observed a hag using its tentacles to locate food in the dark sea.
The hag is known for secreting slime when threatened by predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - an eel-like jawless fish often called a hag

2. NOUN

/ hæɡ / (AmE), / hæɡ / (BrE)
An old woman who is ugly or regarded as evil, often featured in folklore or myth.
ugly or evil old woman

Examples

The fairy tale described the old woman as a hag living deep in the forest.
She was mistaken for a hag because of her ragged clothes and hunched back.
Local legends spoke of a hag who cursed anyone who dared to cross her path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch - a woman thought to have magical powers, especially evil ones
  • Synonym: crone - an old woman who is thin and ugly
  • Antonym: beauty - a beautiful woman, esp. one admired for her looks

Etymology

From Middle English *hagge*, possibly from Old English *hægtesse* (witch, fury).
