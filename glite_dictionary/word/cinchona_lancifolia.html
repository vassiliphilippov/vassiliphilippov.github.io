Cinchona lancifolia

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈkoʊnə ˌlænsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə ˌlansɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tree native to Colombia, known as a source of Cartagena bark, which is a type of cinchona bark used for medicinal purposes.
Colombian tree; source of Cartagena bark

Examples

The bark of Cinchona lancifolia was once highly prized for its medicinal properties.
Researchers visited Colombia to study the distribution of Cinchona lancifolia in its native habitat.
Cartagena bark harvested from Cinchona lancifolia played a major role in the development of early treatments for malaria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinchona - genus of trees producing medicinal bark

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Cinchona' (named for the Countess of Chinchón) and Latin 'lancifolia' (lance-shaped leaves).
