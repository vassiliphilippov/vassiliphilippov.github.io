oxycephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksiˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A congenital deformity in which the top of the skull is abnormally pointed or cone-shaped.
congenital skull abnormality (cone-shaped)
medical

Examples

The surgeon discussed treatment options for the infant diagnosed with oxycephaly.
Oxycephaly often results in increased pressure within the skull and requires medical attention.
Early detection of oxycephaly can improve outcomes for affected children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craniosynostosis - a condition in which the skull bones fuse prematurely, leading to deformities like oxycephaly

Etymology

From Greek oxy- (sharp) + kephalē (head).
