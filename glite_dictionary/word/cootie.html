cootie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːti / (AmE), / ˈkuːti / (BrE)
a parasitic louse, especially one that infests the body of humans
a parasitic louse found on humans

Examples

After the camping trip, Tom discovered a cootie in his sleeping bag.
During the war, soldiers often complained about cooties in the trenches.
Children used to accuse each other of having cooties on the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small, wingless parasitic insect

Etymology

Probably from Malay 'kutu' (louse); first used by British soldiers in World War I.
