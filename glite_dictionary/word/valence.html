valence

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪləns / (AmE), / ˈveɪl(ə)ns / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The combining capacity of an atom or radical, measured by the number of hydrogen atoms (or equivalents) it can combine with or displace.
combining power of an atom in chemistry
medical

Examples

Carbon usually has a valence of four in its compounds.
Knowing the valence of an element helps predict how it will react with others.
The valence of oxygen is two, as it typically forms two chemical bonds.

Etymology

From Latin 'valentia' (strength, capacity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈveɪləns / (AmE), / ˈveɪləns / (BrE)
(biology) The capacity of a biological substance, such as an antigen, to unite, react, or interact with another substance or cell.
capacity to react or unite (biology)
medical

Examples

Certain antibodies demonstrate high valence when binding to multiple antigens.
The valence of a virus influences its ability to infect host cells.
Researchers measured the valence of the new compound to determine its reactivity with enzymes.

Etymology

From Latin valentia (power, capacity), from valere (to be strong).
