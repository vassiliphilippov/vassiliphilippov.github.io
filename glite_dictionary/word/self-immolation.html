self-immolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ɪˈmoʊleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪˈməʊleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately setting oneself on fire, usually as a form of protest or suicide
suicide by setting oneself on fire

Examples

The monk's self-immolation drew international attention to the cause.
Reports of self-immolation as protest have occurred throughout history.
Many were shocked by the student's act of self-immolation in the city square.

Synset Examples

She spoke about the tragedy of self-immolation in her speech.
His self-immolation was a protest against injustice.
The act of self-immolation shocked the entire community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally killing oneself
  • Synonym: martyrdom - the suffering or death of a person for their beliefs

Etymology

From 'self-' + 'immolation', from Latin immolare (sacrifice).
