gavel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡævəl / (AmE), / ˈɡævəl / (BrE)
A small wooden mallet used by a person presiding over a meeting, court, or assembly to call for attention or signal decisions.
small mallet used by a judge or presiding officer

Examples

The judge struck the gavel to bring the courtroom to order.
At the meeting, the chairperson raised her gavel and called for silence.
When he announced the decision, he brought down his gavel with a sharp rap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallet - a hammer with a large head, used for striking without damaging the surface

Etymology

Probably of unknown or Old English origin; possibly related to Medieval Latin 'gabalum' (fork, staff).
