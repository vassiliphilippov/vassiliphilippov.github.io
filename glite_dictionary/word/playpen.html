playpen

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌpɛn / (AmE), / ˈpleɪpɛn / (BrE)
a portable enclosure in which babies or young children can safely play
portable enclosure for babies

Examples

She placed her daughter in the playpen while she prepared lunch.
The twins spent most afternoons safely inside their playpen with toys and books.
After cleaning up, he folded the playpen and stored it in the closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crib - a small bed with high sides for a baby or young child
  • Synonym: cot - a small bed with high barred sides for a baby

Etymology

From 'play' + 'pen', referring to a small enclosed area for children to play.
