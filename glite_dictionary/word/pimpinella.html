Pimpinella

1. NOUN

/ pɪm.pɪˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / pɪm.pɪˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
a genus of aromatic plants in the parsley family, including anise and other species used for their flavor or medicinal properties
a plant genus including anise

Examples

Several species within Pimpinella are cultivated for their aromatic seeds and medicinal uses.
The culinary herb anise belongs to the genus Pimpinella.
Botanists identified the wild specimen as part of the Pimpinella genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anise - an aromatic plant used for its seeds in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Latin *pimpinella*, a diminutive of *pipa* (pip, seed).
