guess

1. NOUN

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
An opinion or answer formed without sufficient information or with only partial evidence.
an opinion based on little evidence

Examples

She took a wild guess at the answer, hoping she was right.
My guess is that they'll arrive late due to the weather.
If I had to make a guess, I'd say he's around fifty years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - an approximate calculation or assessment
  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or known

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'gessen', perhaps of Scandinavian origin.

Examples

3. VERB

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
to form an opinion, estimate, or prediction without sufficient information to be certain, often by intuition
form an opinion without certain knowledge

Examples

I can only guess what might happen next year in the market.
She guessed that he would be late, and she was right.
Are you guessing that the answer is true, or do you know for sure?

Synset Examples

I am guessing that the price of real estate will rise again
I cannot pretend to say that you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppose - to assume something is true without proof
  • Antonym: to know - to be aware of something with certainty

Etymology

From Old Norse *geta* (to get, to guess).
