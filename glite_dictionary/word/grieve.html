to grieve

1. VERB

/ ɡriv / (AmE), / ɡriːv / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel great sadness or sorrow
cause great sadness

Examples

His absence deeply grieved his family during the holidays.
It would grieve her to know how upset you are.
The sudden changes in policy grieved employees across the company.

Synset Examples

his behavior grieves his mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sadden - to make someone sad
  • Antonym: console - to comfort someone who is sad or grieving

Etymology

From Old French *grever* (to burden, afflict), from Latin *gravare* (to make heavy).

WordNet ID: oewn-01801593-v

2. VERB

/ ɡriv / (AmE), / ɡriːv / (BrE)
to feel intense sorrow, especially because of a loss
feel intense sorrow

Examples

Many people grieve for months after losing a loved one.
She needed time alone to grieve after the accident.
Even years later, he still grieved the loss of his childhood home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mourn - to feel or show deep sorrow for someone's death
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology

From Old French *gref* (grief, sorrow) and Latin *gravare* (to burden).

Source: WordNet
