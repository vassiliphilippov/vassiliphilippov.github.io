scattering

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of spreading people or things over a wide area or causing them to separate and go in different directions
the act of spreading or dispersing

Examples

There was a scattering of people across the empty field.
A sudden scattering of birds took place when the dog ran through the park.
After the announcement, there was a scattering of applause from the small crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersion - the process of distributing things or people over a wide area

2. NOUN

/ ˈskæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of spreading or dispersing things or people over an area; the process of being scattered.
act of dispersing or spreading

Examples

The scattering of seeds by the wind allows plants to spread across the field.
There was a scattering of applause after the speaker finished his remarks.
The sudden scattering of birds startled everyone in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersal - the act of dispersing or spreading things over a wide area
  • Antonym: gathering - the act of collecting things or people together

Etymology

From the verb 'scatter,' which originated from Middle English 'scateren,' probably from Old Norse 'skatra' meaning 'to strew.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈskæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A small number of things spread irregularly or at random over an area or among other things.
a small number spread out randomly

Examples

A scattering of applause came from the back of the room.
There was only a scattering of houses along the remote country road.
He noticed a scattering of freckles across her nose.

Synset Examples

the first scatterings of green
a sprinkling of grey at his temples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinkling - small number of things scattered or distributed
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From 'scatter' (verb) + '-ing' (noun suffix), originally from Old Norse 'skatr'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈskæt̬ɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskætərɪŋ / (BrE)
a light shower of rain or snow that falls in some areas but not others nearby
localized light shower

Examples

There was a scattering of rain across the city this afternoon.
We had only a scattering of snowflakes before the sun came out again.
Tomorrow's forecast predicts a scattering of brief showers in the region.

Etymology

From scatter + -ing, meaning the action or result of scattering, applied figuratively to precipitation.

5. NOUN

/ ˈskæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process in which particles or waves are deflected in various directions as a result of collisions with other particles or obstacles
deflection of particles or waves by collisions

Examples

The scattering of light by the atmosphere causes the sky to appear blue.
Physicists studied the scattering of neutrons as they passed through the crystal.
Rayleigh scattering explains why sunsets are red and skies are blue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflection - return of light, heat, or sound from a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'scatter' (Middle English 'scateren', of uncertain origin).
