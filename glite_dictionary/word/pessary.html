pessary

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛsəri / (AmE), / ˈpɛsəri / (BrE)
A flexible, dome-shaped contraceptive device, usually made of rubber or plastic, that is filled with spermicide and fitted over the cervix to prevent pregnancy.
contraceptive device for the cervix
medical

Examples

The doctor explained how to insert the pessary correctly for effective contraception.
She chose a pessary because it offered a non-hormonal method of birth control.
Before using a pessary, it's important to apply spermicide as directed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphragm - a flexible cup-shaped contraceptive device placed over the cervix

Etymology

From Latin 'pessarium', from Greek 'pessos' (an oval stone for games; later, a medicated tampon).
