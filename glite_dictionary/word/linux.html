Linux

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪnəks / (AmE), / ˈlɪnəks / (BrE)
an open-source version of the UNIX operating system used on a wide variety of computers
open-source operating system

Examples

Many popular websites run on servers powered by Linux.
He decided to install Linux on his old laptop instead of buying new software.
The Linux community provides extensive online support for users of all levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: UNIX - a multiuser operating system developed in the 1970s

Etymology

Named for Linus Torvalds, its creator, and UNIX, the operating system it is based on.
