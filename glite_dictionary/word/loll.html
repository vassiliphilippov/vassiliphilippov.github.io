to loll

1. VERB

/ lɑl / (AmE), / lɒl / (BrE)
to sit, lie, or stand in a lazy, relaxed way, often with little energy or purpose
to lounge or be lazy

Examples

After lunch, the children lolled on the grass, enjoying the warm sunshine.
He likes to loll on the sofa and watch television after work.
Cats often loll in the sun for hours at a time.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lounge - to sit or lie in a relaxed way
  • Synonym: to laze - to spend time being lazy; to relax and do nothing

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'lollen', meaning to lounge or be idle.

2. VERB

/ lɑl / (AmE), / lɒl / (BrE)
to hang, lean, or droop loosely or in a relaxed manner
hang or droop loosely

Examples

The dog's tongue lolled out of its mouth in the heat.
Long branches lolled over the edge of the old stone wall.
She sat back in the chair, her arms lolling lazily at her sides.

Synset Examples

His tongue lolled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to droop - to bend or hang downwards limply
  • Synonym: to dangle - to hang or swing loosely

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin; recorded since the 14th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02723576-v
Synset POS: v
