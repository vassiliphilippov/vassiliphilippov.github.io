Cabombaceae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌbɒmˈbeɪsiː / (AmE), / kəˌbɒmˈbeɪsiː / (BrE)
A small family of aquatic flowering plants, sometimes classified as water lilies, including the genera Cabomba and Brasenia.
family of aquatic flowering plants

Examples

The Cabombaceae are often found in freshwater ponds and slow-moving rivers.
Researchers studied the distribution of Cabombaceae across different continents.
Botanists debate whether Cabombaceae should be considered part of the water lily family.

Etymology

From the genus name Cabomba + family suffix -aceae, following botanical Latin naming conventions.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌbɑːmˈbeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / kəˌbɒmˈbeɪsiː / (BrE)
a small family of aquatic flowering plants comprising the genera Cabomba and Brasenia, sometimes classified as an independent family of water lilies
family of aquatic flowering plants (Cabomba, Brasenia)

Examples

The Cabombaceae are found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats across the world.
Scientists have debated whether Cabombaceae should stand as an independent family distinct from the water lilies.
Both Cabomba and Brasenia, members of the Cabombaceae, play important roles in aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - aquatic plant of the family Nymphaeaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Cabomba and the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
