Otariidae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌtɛəriˈaɪdiˌi / (AmE), / əʊˌtɛəriˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine mammals comprising the eared seals, including sea lions and fur seals
family of eared seals (sea lions, fur seals)

Examples

The Otariidae family includes both sea lions and fur seals.
Researchers studied the behavior of various Otariidae species along the coastline.
Unlike true seals, members of the Otariidae have external ear flaps and are more agile on land.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pinnipedia - the group containing true seals, eared seals, and walruses

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'otarion' (diminutive of 'ous', ear) + '-idae' (family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˌtɛəriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / əʊˌtɛəriˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic mammals known as eared seals, including sea lions and fur seals.
family of eared seals

Examples

The Otariidae family includes both sea lions and fur seals.
Scientists studied several species within the Otariidae to learn about their behavior.
Unlike the true seals, animals in the Otariidae have visible external ear flaps.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Phocidae - family of true seals, lacking external ear flaps
  • Degree Higher: Pinniped - suborder of fin-footed marine mammals, includes seals, walruses, sea lions

Etymology

From New Latin 'Otariidae', based on Greek 'otarion' meaning 'little ear'.
