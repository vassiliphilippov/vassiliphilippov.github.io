Synapsida

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnæpsɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˈnapsɪdə / (BrE)
a major group of extinct reptiles from the Permian to Jurassic periods, regarded as ancestors of mammals
a group of extinct reptiles ancestral to mammals

Examples

Paleontologists have found fossils of Synapsida in many parts of the world.
The evolution of mammals can be traced back to early Synapsida.
Many textbooks illustrate the transition from Synapsida to true mammals during the Jurassic period.

Etymology

From Greek synapsis (connection, junction) + -ida (taxonomic suffix).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈnæpsɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˈnapsɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct subclass of reptiles from the Permian to Jurassic periods, considered to be ancestors of mammals
extinct reptiles ancestral to mammals

Examples

The Synapsida include many species that share characteristics with early mammals.
Paleontologists study fossils of the Synapsida to understand the evolution of mammalian features.
Most Synapsida disappeared by the end of the Permian period, but their descendants survived as mammals.

Etymology

From Greek 'synapsis' (a joining together) with New Latin scientific suffix '-ida'.
