to foreshorten

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / fɔːˈʃɔːtn / (BrE)
to reduce something in length, duration, or scope while keeping its essential parts
reduce in scope, keep essentials

Examples

The author decided to foreshorten the novel by removing secondary storylines.
Due to time constraints, the conference had to foreshorten several scheduled presentations.
The committee voted to foreshorten the meeting but address all key issues.

Synset Examples

The manuscript must be shortened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shorten - make something shorter or briefer
  • Antonym: to lengthen - make something longer or more extensive

Etymology

From Italian 'forzare' (to force) and 'corto' (short), via the artistic sense of 'shortening' perspective.

2. VERB

/ ˌfɔrˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈʃɔːtn̩ / (BrE)
to represent an object or figure in a picture or drawing so that it appears to recede sharply into the background, by shortening lines in proportion to the viewer’s perspective
depict in perspective to show depth

Examples

The artist skillfully foreshortened the figure's arm to make it look as if it was reaching out of the canvas.
In this drawing class, students learned how to foreshorten objects to create a sense of depth.
The horse appears to leap forward because its body is dramatically foreshortened in the painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspective - the art technique of representing three-dimensional objects on a two-dimensional surface

Etymology

From fore- (before) + shorten, in the sense of making something appear shorter in advance or in perspective.
