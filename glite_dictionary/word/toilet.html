toilet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlət / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪt / (BrE)
(informal) A state or situation in which something has been ruined, lost, or wasted, especially regarding money, effort, or reputation.
state of ruin or failure (informal)
informal

Examples

After the scandal, his reputation was in the toilet and he struggled to recover.
All my savings went down the toilet after that bad investment.
The company's profits are in the toilet this quarter due to declining sales.

Synset Examples

his career was in the gutter
all that work went down the sewer
pensions are in the toilet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - complete loss, destruction, or downfall
  • Synonym: gutter - a low or ruined state (informal)

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlət / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪt / (BrE)
A large bowl connected to a plumbing system, used for the disposal of human waste by defecation and urination.
fixture for disposing of human waste

Examples

Could you tell me where the nearest toilet is, please?
The toilet in this restaurant is very clean and modern.
He had to repair the toilet because it was leaking water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavatory - a room or fixture for washing or elimination of waste
  • Synonym: bathroom - a room containing a toilet and possibly a bath or shower

Etymology

From French 'toilette', meaning dressing room; later specialized in English to refer to the fixture.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlət / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪt / (BrE)
A room or small building containing one or more toilets, often with a washbasin, used for relieving oneself and for hygiene.
room with toilet facilities

Examples

The nearest toilet is down the hall, next to the kitchen.
Please make sure the toilet is clean before guests arrive.
They installed a new toilet in the office building last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restroom - a public room containing toilets and washbasins
  • Synonym: bathroom - a room containing a bath or shower and usually a toilet
  • Synonym: lavatory - a room or compartment with a toilet and washbasin

Etymology

From French 'toilette' (act of washing, dressing), diminutive of 'toile' (cloth, wrapper).
