coated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtɪd / (BrE)
covered with a thin layer of a substance, often for protection or decoration; frequently used in combination.
covered with a layer of something

Examples

The chocolate coated strawberries looked delicious at the dessert table.
She wore a coated jacket that protected her from the rain.
Many medications come in coated tablets to make swallowing easier.

Synset Examples

coated paper has a smooth polished coating especially suitable for halftone printing
sugar-coated pills

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncoated - not covered with a layer or film
  • Synonym: covered - having something on top as a covering

Etymology

From the verb 'coat' + -ed (past participle suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtɪd / (BrE)
wearing, provided with, or covered by a coat or layer of something.
covered or layered with a coat

Examples

The tablets are coated to make them easier to swallow.
He wore a thick, fur-coated parka in the snowstorm.
These fries are coated with a spicy seasoning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncoated - not provided with a coat or covering
  • Synonym: jacketed - having or provided with a jacket or protective outer covering
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00457438-s
Synset POS: s
