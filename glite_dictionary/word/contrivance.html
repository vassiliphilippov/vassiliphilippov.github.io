contrivance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtraɪvəns / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvəns / (BrE)
A scheme or plan, especially one that is cleverly or deceitfully devised to deceive or evade.
deceitful or clever scheme

Examples

Her entire story turned out to be an elaborate contrivance to hide the truth.
The witness's contrivance was uncovered after a week-long investigation.
Many critics saw the plot twist as a mere contrivance to surprise the audience.

Synset Examples

his testimony was just a contrivance to throw us off the track

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or underhanded plan; a plot
  • Synonym: ruse - a cunning plan or trick to deceive someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or sincere, opposite of deceit

Etymology

From Latin 'contrivare', meaning to bring about or devise.

6. NOUN

/ kənˈtraɪvəns / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvəns / (BrE)
the act of cleverly planning, inventing, or devising something; a scheme or invention resulting from ingenuity or resourcefulness
the act of devising or inventing

Examples

The contrivance of an elaborate security system impressed the committee.
Through the contrivance of careful planning, the mission succeeded against all odds.
Her contrivance of a solution to the problem was both creative and practical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invention - the act of inventing something
  • Synonym: creation - the act of bringing something into existence

Etymology

From Old French *contriver* (to devise, invent), from Late Latin *contropare* (to compare), later influenced by Latin *con-* (together) + *tropus* (song, manner, trope).
