hemophilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈfɪlɪə / (BrE)
A congenital disorder in which the blood does not clot properly, causing excessive bleeding, and typically affecting males; usually inherited from the mother.
inherited bleeding disorder
medical

Examples

His family has a history of hemophilia, so he receives regular medical check-ups.
Because hemophilia impairs blood clotting, even small injuries can be dangerous for patients.
Many people first learn about hemophilia through genetic counseling before having children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleeding disorder - a general term for conditions where blood does not clot normally

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'philia' (love, tendency).
