hardship

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdʃɪp / (BrE)
severe suffering or difficulty caused by misfortune, poverty, or adversity.
severe suffering or difficulty

Examples

Many families experienced great hardship during the long winter months.
Despite the hardships they faced, the community remained united and hopeful.
She remembers her childhood as a time marked by hardship and perseverance.

Synset Examples

debt-ridden farmers struggling with adversity
a life of hardship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adversity - difficult or unpleasant situation, often lasting for a period of time
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from suffering

Etymology

From Middle English hardeschipe, formed from 'hard' + the suffix '-ship'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdʃɪp / (BrE)
severe suffering or difficulty, often caused by a lack of something necessary.
severe suffering or difficulty

Examples

After the factory closed, many families faced unexpected hardship in the months that followed.
Despite the hardship of living in a foreign country, she remained optimistic and determined to succeed.
The community came together to support those experiencing financial hardship during the crisis.

Synset Examples

I cannot think it a hardship that more indulgence is allowed to men than to women
the many hardships of frontier life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privation - lack of basic necessities or comforts of life
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical or material ease

Etymology

From hard + -ship, first recorded in Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdʃɪp / (BrE)
a condition or situation that is difficult to endure; severe suffering or deprivation
difficulty or suffering

Examples

After losing his job, he experienced great hardship while trying to make ends meet.
Many people face financial hardship during a recession.
She overcame every hardship through determination and hard work.

Synset Examples

the asperity of northern winters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - the state of something being hard to do or endure
  • Antonym: ease - the state of being easy or free from difficulty

Etymology

From Middle English hard + -ship, patterned after similar Old English formations.
