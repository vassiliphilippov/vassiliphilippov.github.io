undividable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈvaɪdəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈvaɪdəbl̩ / (BrE)
not able to be divided into equal parts without leaving a remainder; indivisible.
not able to be divided equally

Examples

The number fifteen is undividable by four without leaving a remainder.
Some consider the soul to be undividable, incapable of being separated into parts.
In this puzzle, you must find all values that are undividable by three.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indivisible - unable to be divided into equal parts

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'dividable' (able to be divided), with roots in Latin 'dividere' (to divide).
