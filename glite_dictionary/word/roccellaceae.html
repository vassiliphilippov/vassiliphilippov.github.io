Roccellaceae

1. NOUN

/ roʊˌsɛləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / rɒˌsɛlɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the division Lichenes, most of which form lichens with green algae; some species grow on other lichens.
family of lichen-forming fungi

Examples

The Roccellaceae are commonly found in coastal regions where suitable algae are present.
Some species within the Roccellaceae family have been used historically to produce dyes.
Researchers discovered a new lichen in the genus Roccella, a member of the Roccellaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Roccellia, based on Italian botanist Luigi Roccella.

2. NOUN

A family of fungi in the division Lichenes, most of which form lichens with green algae, although some species grow on other lichens.
family of lichen-forming fungi
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Roccellaceae in coastal regions.
Many members of the Roccellaceae family are known for producing colorful dyes.
The ecological role of Roccellaceae includes symbiosis with green algae in forming lichens.

Etymology

From the genus name Roccellia, after Roccellina, and '-aceae', a standard botanical family suffix.
