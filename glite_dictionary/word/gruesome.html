gruesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡruːsəm / (AmE), / ˈɡruːsəm / (BrE)
Causing shock or horror; extremely unpleasant, especially because of involving death or injury.
causing horror or disgust

Examples

The police arrived at the scene of a gruesome crime that shocked the neighborhood.
She couldn't get the image of the gruesome accident out of her mind.
The novel is filled with gruesome details that may disturb sensitive readers.

Synset Examples

ghastly wounds
the grim aftermath of the bombing
the grim task of burying the victims
a grisly murder
gruesome evidence of human sacrifice
macabre tales of war and plague in the Middle ages
macabre tortures conceived by madmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghastly - Causing great horror or fear; frightful or macabre
  • Synonym: grisly - Causing horror or disgust
  • Synonym: macabre - Disturbing because concerned with or causing a fear of death

Etymology

Probably from Scots *grue* (to shudder) + *-some*.
