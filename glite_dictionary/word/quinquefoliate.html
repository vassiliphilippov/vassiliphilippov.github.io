quinquefoliate

1. ADJ

/ kwɪŋkwɪˈfoʊliət / (AmE), / kwɪŋkwɪˈfəʊliət / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having five leaflets.
having five leaflets (botany)

Examples

The rose species has quinquefoliate leaves, each consisting of five distinct leaflets.
Researchers identified the plant by its quinquefoliate leaf pattern.
In spring, the tree produces new quinquefoliate growth along its branches.

Etymology

From Latin 'quinque' (five) + 'folium' (leaf).
