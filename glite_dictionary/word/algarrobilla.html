algarrobilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɡəroʊˈbiːjə / (AmE), / ˌalɡəˌrɒˈbiːjə / (BrE)
The pod of certain South American mesquite trees, used especially in tanning and dyeing.
mesquite pod used in tanning/dyeing

Examples

The tannins extracted from algarrobilla make it valuable in the leather industry.
Local dyers use algarrobilla pods to create rich, earthy colors.
Researchers studied the chemical properties of algarrobilla for natural dye production.

Etymology

From Spanish 'algarrobilla' (diminutive of 'algarroba', carob pod).
