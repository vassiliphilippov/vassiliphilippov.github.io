lisinopril

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈsɪnoʊprɪl / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪnəʊprɪl / (BrE)
a drug used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure, belonging to the group of ACE inhibitors
ACE inhibitor drug for blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed lisinopril to help control her hypertension.
Many patients take lisinopril daily as part of their heart failure treatment regimen.
After his heart attack, his cardiologist added lisinopril to his list of medications.
