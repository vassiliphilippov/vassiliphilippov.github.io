durra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊr.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʊr.ə / (BrE)
A type of sorghum grown especially in dry regions of Asia and North Africa; the grain of this plant.
a type of sorghum grain

Examples

Durra is a staple crop in many arid regions of North Africa.
The villagers harvested the durra to make traditional flatbread.
Farmers prefer durra because it tolerates drought better than other grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grasses cultivated for grain and fodder

Etymology

From Arabic 'durrāʿ', a name for this variety of sorghum.
