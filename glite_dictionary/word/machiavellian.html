Machiavellian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.i.əˈvɛl.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌmak.i.əˈvɛl.i.ən / (BrE)
A person who adopts the political principles or schemes of Niccolò Machiavelli, especially the use of cunning and deceit in political or personal affairs.
follower of Machiavelli's principles

Examples

He was considered a Machiavellian by his colleagues, always plotting behind the scenes for his own benefit.
Some historians argue that Queen Elizabeth I had a Machiavellian advisor at her side.
In the corporate world, calling someone a Machiavellian is rarely a compliment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schemer - someone who forms secret plans, especially to deceive others
  • Antonym: idealistic - characterized by idealism; opposite of cunning, pragmatic thinking

Etymology

From the name of Niccolò Machiavelli (1469–1527), Italian political philosopher.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmæk.i.əˈvɛl.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌmak.i.əˈvɛl.i.ən / (BrE)
Using clever tricks and a lack of morality to achieve one's goals, especially in politics; relating to the ideas of Machiavelli.
cunning or scheming (esp. in politics)

Examples

Many historians describe his leadership style as Machiavellian and highly manipulative.
In the novel, the protagonist uses Machiavellian tactics to eliminate his rivals.
Her Machiavellian maneuvering in the workplace helped her rise to power quickly.

Synset Examples

Machiavellian thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheming - Involved in making secret and often devious plans
  • Antonym: honest - Free of deceit and untruthfulness; sincere

Etymology

From the name of Niccolò Machiavelli (1469–1527), Italian political philosopher.
