solemnisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːləm.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɒl.əm.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the formal ceremony or public performance of a religious or legal rite, especially a marriage, with all appropriate rituals
formal ceremony, especially for marriage

Examples

The solemnisation of their marriage was held in the cathedral.
Certain countries require a public solemnisation for a marriage to be legally recognized.
The priest oversaw the solemnisation of the religious ceremony with great reverence.

Synset Examples

the celebration of marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebration - a special social event, such as a ceremony, held to mark a particular occasion

Etymology

From Latin 'solemnis' (customary, celebrated at a fixed date), via French 'solennisation'.
