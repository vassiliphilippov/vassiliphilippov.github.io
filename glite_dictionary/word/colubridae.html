Colubridae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːbrɪdi / (AmE), / kəˈluːbrɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of mainly nonvenomous snakes, comprising about two-thirds of all living snake species.
a family of mainly nonvenomous snakes

Examples

Most common garden snakes in North America belong to the Colubridae family.
The Colubridae family includes a wide variety of snake species worldwide.
Unlike vipers, members of Colubridae are usually harmless to humans.

Etymology

From Latin 'coluber' (snake, serpent), with modern scientific usage.
