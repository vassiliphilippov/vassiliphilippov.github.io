Lamarckism

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɑrkɪzəm / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːkɪzəm / (BrE)
The theory that organisms can pass on characteristics acquired during their lifetime to their offspring.
theory that acquired traits are inherited

Examples

Although modern genetics disproved Lamarckism, it once greatly influenced evolutionary thought.
Some early naturalists believed in Lamarckism to explain how giraffes acquired their long necks.
The concept of Lamarckism is often referenced when discussing the history of evolutionary biology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Darwinism - the theory that species evolve through natural selection
  • Synonym: inheritance of acquired characteristics - the principle that features gained during life can be inherited by offspring

Etymology

Named after French naturalist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck (1744–1829).
