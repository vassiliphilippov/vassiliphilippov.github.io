parable

1. NOUN

/ ˈpær.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.bl̩ / (BrE)
a simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson, especially as told by Jesus in the Bible
a story illustrating a moral or spiritual lesson

Examples

Jesus often taught his followers in parables to help them understand his message.
The parable of the Good Samaritan is one of the most famous biblical stories.
In his sermon, the priest explained the deeper meaning behind a well-known parable.

Synset Examples

the parable of the prodigal son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fable - a short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral

Etymology

From Greek parabole (comparison, analogy), via Latin parabola.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpærəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpærəbəl / (BrE)
A simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson, often featuring human or animal characters.
simple story illustrating a moral lesson

Examples

The pastor used a parable to explain the importance of forgiveness to the congregation.
Aesop's parable involving the tortoise and the hare is known by almost everyone.
Many religious texts include a parable to teach fundamental moral principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fable - a short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral
  • Synonym: allegory - a story, poem, or picture that can be interpreted to reveal a hidden meaning, typically a moral or political one

Etymology

From Latin 'parabola' (comparison, parable), from Greek 'parabolē' (comparison, analogy).
