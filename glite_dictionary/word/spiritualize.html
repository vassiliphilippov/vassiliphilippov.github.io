to spiritualize

1. VERB

/ ˈspɪr.ɪ.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɪr.ɪ.tjuː.əˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to give a spiritual meaning or interpretation to something; to interpret or regard in a spiritual or religious sense
interpret spiritually; assign religious meaning

Examples

Some theologians spiritualize the miracle stories in the Bible.
She tends to spiritualize everyday experiences, finding deeper meaning in simple events.
The teacher urged students not to spiritualize historical facts when studying ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to literalize - to interpret or represent literally

Etymology

From Medieval Latin spiritualizare, from Latin spiritualis (spiritual).

WordNet ID: oewn-00625619-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to purify a person or thing from worldly or corrupting influences, often by infusing a spiritual quality or outlook
to purify from worldly influences

Examples

The teachings of the monk helped to spiritualize his followers and free them from material desires.
Many retreatants seek to spiritualize their lives by spending time in meditation and prayer.
The artist tried to spiritualize his work, removing any reference to material objects or everyday concerns.

Synset Examples

During his stay at the ashram he was spiritualized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to materialize - to become physical or worldly, the opposite of spiritualize

Etymology

From Medieval Latin spiritualizare, from Latin spiritus (spirit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00477244-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more spiritual, pure, or idealistic, often by giving it a higher or moral significance
make more spiritual or idealistic

Examples

She tried to spiritualize her daily routines by focusing on their deeper significance.
Some philosophers spiritualize nature, seeing it as an expression of the divine.
The novel spiritualizes hardship, interpreting suffering as a path to personal growth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to materialize - to make something concrete or physical, the opposite of spiritualize
  • Synonym: to idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than reality

Etymology

From Medieval Latin spiritualizare, from Latin spiritualis (spiritual) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00581589-v
Synset POS: v
