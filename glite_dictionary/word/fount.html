fount

1. NOUN

/ faʊnt / (AmE), / faʊnt / (BrE)
a particular size and style of a typeface within a type family, traditionally referring to all the characters in that style and size
type size and style in printing

Examples

The printer requested another fount of 12-point italic to complete the job.
Each fount was carefully organized in the typesetter's drawer.
The museum displays a rare fount used in 19th-century publications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - a set of printed or digital characters in a particular size and style

Etymology

From Old French 'font' (fountain, source), later used in English printing for a complete set of printed type.

2. NOUN

/ faʊnt / (AmE), / faʊnt / (BrE)
a plumbing fixture or device that provides a continuous flow of water, often used for drinking or ornamental purposes
plumbing fixture supplying water
archaic

Examples

The village center was marked by an old stone fount where travelers could drink.
She filled her cup from the cool water fount beside the garden path.
A marble fount stood in the courtyard, providing fresh water to the residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fountain - an artificial structure from which water flows

Etymology

From Old French font, from Latin fons, fontis (spring, fountain).
