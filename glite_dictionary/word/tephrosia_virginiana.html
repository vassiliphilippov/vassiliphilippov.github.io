Tephrosia virginiana

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈfroʊzi.ə vɚˌdʒɪnˈiːnə / (AmE), / tɛˈfrəʊzi.ə vɜːdʒɪˈniːnə / (BrE)
a perennial subshrub native to eastern North America, with downy leaves and yellowish-rose flowers, used as a source of rotenone
a North American perennial subshrub

Examples

The prairie was dotted with clusters of Tephrosia virginiana in full bloom.
Botanists study Tephrosia virginiana for its natural production of rotenone.
Gardeners value Tephrosia virginiana for its attractive flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Etymology

Tephrosia from Greek 'tephros' (ashen), referring to the leaf color; species name 'virginiana' refers to Virginia.
