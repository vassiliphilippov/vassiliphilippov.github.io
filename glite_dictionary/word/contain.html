to contain

1. VERB

/ kənˈteɪn / (AmE), / kənˈteɪn / (BrE)
to have or hold something within; to include as a part or component
include as part or component

Examples

The box contains all the necessary tools for the job.
This book contains several chapters on ancient history.
The salad contained nuts, so she couldn't eat it.

Synset Examples

A totally new idea is comprised in this paper
The record contains many old songs from the 1930's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to include - to have something as a part of a whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to not include something as a part or component

Etymology

From Latin continēre, from com- (together) + tenēre (to hold).
