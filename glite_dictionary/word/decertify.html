to decertify

1. VERB

/ diˈsɜːrtɪfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈsɜːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to officially withdraw approval or certification, making something no longer recognized or accepted by an authority
to revoke official approval

Examples

The state board voted to decertify the laboratory due to repeated safety violations.
If the union fails to meet the requirements, it may be decertified by the government.
The school was decertified after it lost its accreditation.

Synset Examples

Carter derecognized Taiwan in 1979 after the U.S. recognized the People's Republic of China

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to certify - to officially recognize or approve

Etymology

From Latin prefix de- (removal) + certify (to attest as certain).
