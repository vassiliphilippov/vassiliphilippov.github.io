Copernicus

1. PROPN

/ koʊˈpɜrnɪkəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈpɜːnɪkəs / (BrE)
a large and prominent lunar crater on the Moon, located in the eastern part of the Oceanus Procellarum.
prominent crater on the Moon

Examples

Astronomers often study the Copernicus crater to learn about the Moon's geological history.
Through my telescope, I could clearly see Copernicus shining on the lunar surface.
Many lunar maps highlight Copernicus as one of the most remarkable features of the Moon.

Etymology

Named after Nicolaus Copernicus (1473–1543), Polish astronomer.

2. PROPN

/ koʊˈpɝː.nɪ.kəs / (AmE), / kəˈpɜː.nɪ.kəs / (BrE)
(1473–1543) A Polish astronomer who developed the heliocentric model of the solar system, placing the sun at its center.
Polish astronomer; heliocentric solar system model

Examples

Copernicus revolutionized astronomy by proposing the heliocentric model of the solar system.
Many scientific institutions are named after Copernicus in honor of his contributions to astronomy.
Before Copernicus, most people believed the Earth was at the center of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Galileo - Italian astronomer and physicist who supported heliocentrism
  • Antonym: Ptolemy - Ancient Greek astronomer who developed the geocentric model

Etymology

Latinized form of Polish surname Kopernik.
