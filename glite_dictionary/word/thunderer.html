Thunderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndərər / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərə / (BrE)
an epithet for Jupiter (or Zeus) in classical mythology, referring to his power as the bringer of thunder
epithet for Jupiter or Zeus as thunder-bringer

Examples

In Homeric hymns, Zeus is often called the Thunderer for wielding the thunderbolt.
The ancient Romans revered Jupiter as the mighty Thunderer.
Poets have long referred to the Thunderer when describing storms and divine power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zeus - the chief god in ancient Greek mythology, counterpart to Jupiter

Etymology

From thunder + -er (agent noun); used in English translations of Latin and Greek epithets for Jupiter/Zeus.
