heptad

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛp.tæd / (AmE), / ˈhɛp.tæd / (BrE)
A group or set of seven; the number seven.
group or set of seven

Examples

The scientist isolated a heptad of protein molecules for further study.
Many ancient cultures ascribed special significance to a heptad in rituals and mythology.
The book is structured in a heptad of chapters, each exploring a different theme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septet - a group of seven people or things, especially in music
  • Degree Higher: octad - a group or set of eight
  • Degree Lower: hexad - a group or set of six

Etymology

From Greek 'heptas' (group of seven), from 'hepta' (seven).
