elusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒən / (BrE)
The act of escaping or avoiding something, especially by cleverness or cunning.
act of skillful avoidance or escape

Examples

The spy's elusion of the guards was nothing short of brilliant.
Thanks to her quick thinking, her elusion from the crowd went unnoticed.
Despite the search team's efforts, the suspect's elusion lasted for several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasion - the act of escaping or avoiding by cleverness or deceit

Etymology

From Latin eludere (to escape by playing).
