panocha

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnɑːtʃə / (AmE), / pəˈnɒtʃə / (BrE)
A type of fudge made with brown sugar, butter, milk, and nuts, popular in southwestern United States cuisine.
a kind of fudge with brown sugar and nuts

Examples

My grandmother made delicious panocha every Christmas using a family recipe.
He brought a tray of homemade panocha to the office party, and everyone loved it.
The bakery sells panocha alongside other traditional Southwestern sweets.

Etymology

From Spanish 'panocha', meaning a type of sugar or ear of corn, adopted into southwestern U.S. English.
