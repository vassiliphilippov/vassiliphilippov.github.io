Asimina triloba

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪmɪnə traɪˈloʊbə / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪnə trɪˈləʊbə / (BrE)
A small tree native to the eastern United States, known for its large oblong leaves and edible, fleshy fruit commonly called pawpaw.
species of North American pawpaw tree

Examples

The Asimina triloba produces greenish-brown fruits that are sweet and custard-like when ripe.
Several wild patches of Asimina triloba can be found along the riverbank, attracting local wildlife.
Gardeners interested in native fruit trees often plant the Asimina triloba for its unique flavor and low maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - the edible fruit or tree Asimina triloba

Etymology

From genus name Asimina (of Native American origin) and Latin triloba (three-lobed), referring to the leaf shape.
