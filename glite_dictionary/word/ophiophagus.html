Ophiophagus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌfaɪəˈfæɡəs / (AmE), / əʊˌfɪəˈfæɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes found in Asia, represented solely by the king cobra (Ophiophagus hannah).
genus of snakes (king cobra)

Examples

The scientific name Ophiophagus highlights the king cobra’s habit of eating other snakes.
Researchers studied the behavior of Ophiophagus in its natural habitat in Southeast Asia.
Unlike other cobras, Ophiophagus constructs elaborate nests for its eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king cobra - the world's longest venomous snake, sole species in Ophiophagus

Etymology

From Greek ophio- (snake) + phagos (eating), referring to the snake's diet of other snakes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiˈæfəɡəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊfiˈæfəɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of large venomous snakes in the family Elapidae, consisting solely of the king cobra.
genus of snakes (king cobra)

Examples

The Ophiophagus genus includes only the king cobra, one of the world's most dangerous snakes.
Unlike other snake genera, Ophiophagus is monotypic, containing just a single species.
Researchers examined the venom composition unique to Ophiophagus in their study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king cobra - the largest venomous snake, sole species of Ophiophagus

Etymology

From Greek 'ophio-' meaning 'snake' and '-phagus' meaning 'eating.'
