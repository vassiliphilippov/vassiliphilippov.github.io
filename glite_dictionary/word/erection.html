erection

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a structure that has been built or put up
a built structure

Examples

The new library is an impressive erection at the center of the campus.
That concrete erection has dominated the skyline for years.
Plans for the erection of a bridge were approved by the city council.

Etymology

From Latin erectio (a setting up, erection) via Old French erection.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the state of a penis when it is stiff and enlarged, typically as a result of sexual arousal
an enlarged, stiff penis (sexual context)

Examples

He felt embarrassed when he had an erection during class.
Morning erections are a common physiological phenomenon in men.
The medication can help men achieve an erection sufficient for sexual activity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flaccid - soft and limp, not erect (of the penis)

Etymology

From Latin erectio (a raising up), from erigere (to set up, erect).

the act of building or putting up
