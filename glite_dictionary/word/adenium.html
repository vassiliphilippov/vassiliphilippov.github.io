Adenium

1. NOUN

/ əˈdiːniəm / (AmE), / əˈdiːniəm / (BrE)
a genus of succulent shrubs or small trees native to tropical Africa and Arabia, known for their thick stems and showy flowers
a genus of succulent shrubs or trees

Examples

The Adenium is popular with gardeners for its striking pink and white flowers.
She keeps an Adenium on her windowsill because it thrives in bright sunlight.
Adeniums require well-drained soil and minimal watering to avoid root rot.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the plant’s Arabic name, likely referencing 'Aden' in Yemen.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdiːniəm / (AmE), / əˈdiːniəm / (BrE)
A genus of succulent shrubs or small trees native to tropical Africa and Arabia, known for thick stems and attractive flowers, especially the desert rose.
genus of succulent shrubs or trees

Examples

The Adenium is often called the desert rose because of its striking flowers and swollen stem.
Botanists have classified several varieties under the genus Adenium.
Gardeners appreciate Adenium for its drought tolerance and decorative appeal.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Arabic 'aden', a region in Yemen, where some species are native.
