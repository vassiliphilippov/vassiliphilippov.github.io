unionised

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not converted into ions; remaining in a neutral or non-ionic form.
not converted into ions

Examples

At lower pH levels, the drug remains largely unionised and can diffuse across cell membranes.
The solubility of unionised ammonia is higher in water than its ionised form.
Researchers measured the unionised fraction of the compound in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ionised - converted into ions; has undergone ionization

Etymology

Formed from 'union' + '-ise' + '-ed', by analogy with 'ionised'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a person, group, or workplace) having membership in or being formed into a labor union.
belonging to a labor union
british

Examples

Most of the factory staff are unionised and participate in collective bargaining.
The unionised sector of the workforce has greater job security than non-unionised workers.
Several unionised teachers joined the strike to protest working conditions.

Synset Examples

organized labor
unionized workers
a unionized shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unionized - being a member of or formed into a labor union (US spelling)

Etymology

From 'union' (association formed by workers) + '-ised', the British spelling of the suffix meaning made or become.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02483951-s
Synset POS: s
