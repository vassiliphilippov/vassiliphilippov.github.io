rave-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪvˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈreɪvˌʌp / (BrE)
a lively and noisy party or gathering, often involving music and dancing
wild party or gathering

Examples

They threw a wild rave-up to celebrate the end of finals.
Last night's rave-up at the club went on until dawn.
The band's fans organized a spontaneous rave-up after the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪv ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈreɪv ʌp / (BrE)
A large and usually overnight dance party where electronic music is played and illegal drugs are often used.
large overnight dance party with electronic music
informal

Examples

The warehouse was packed last night for the rave-up that lasted until dawn.
She met her partner at a legendary rave-up in Berlin five years ago.
After attending his first rave-up, he became fascinated by electronic music culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rave - an energetic dance party with electronic music

Etymology

From 'rave' (late 20th-century term for an energetic party) + 'up' as an intensifying particle.
