Erethizon dorsatum

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɛθɪˌzɑn dɔrˈsætəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛθɪˌzɒn dɔːˈsætəm / (BrE)
the North American porcupine, a large rodent found in northeastern North America, known for its barbed quills and tendency to gnaw on wood for salt and grease
North American porcupine species

Examples

We encountered an Erethizon dorsatum near the edge of the forest while hiking.
The Erethizon dorsatum is well known for its sharp, barbed quills.
Biologists tracked several Erethizon dorsatum to study their feeding habits in winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupine - large rodent with spiny quills for defense

Etymology

From Greek 'erethizō' (to irritate) and Latin 'dorsatum' (provided with a back), referring to the animal's irritating quills and back.
