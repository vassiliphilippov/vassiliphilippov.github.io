papist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɪst / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpɪst / (BrE)
(offensive) A Roman Catholic, especially one who is an ardent supporter of the pope and papal authority, often used derogatorily.
offensive term for Roman Catholics
offensive archaic

Examples

The term papist was often used derisively in 17th-century England.
He was accused of being a papist because of his loyalty to Rome.
Some anti-Catholic literature of the era is filled with the word papist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church
  • Antonym: Protestant - a member of any branch of Christianity rejecting papal authority

Etymology

From French papiste, from pape (pope), ultimately from Latin papa (pope).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪpɪst / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpɪst / (BrE)
of or relating to the Roman Catholic Church, especially in a manner that supports its doctrines or practices (often used disparagingly or in historical contexts)
supporting or relating to Roman Catholicism
archaic offensive

Examples

He was accused of holding papist sympathies during the Reformation.
The old laws discriminated against those with papist beliefs in England.
Her family was considered dangerously papist by their neighbors.

Synset Examples

the Roman Catholic Church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catholic - relating to the Roman Catholic Church
  • Antonym: protestant - relating to Protestant Christianity; opposite of Roman Catholic

Etymology

From Latin papa (pope), via French papiste.
