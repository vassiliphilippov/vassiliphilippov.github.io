plank

1. VERB

/ plæŋk / (AmE), / plæŋk / (BrE)
to cover or lay with planks, especially wood boards placed side by side
cover or lay with planks

Examples

The old pier was planked with sturdy oak boards to ensure safety.
Workers quickly planked the muddy street to make passage easier for carts.
The backyard path was planked to give it a rustic look.

Synset Examples

The streets were planked

2. NOUN

/ plæŋk / (AmE), / plæŋk / (BrE)
a specific policy or principle that is part of the official platform of a political party or organization
policy element in a political platform

Examples

Healthcare reform was a central plank of the candidate's election platform.
The party added an environmental plank to appeal to younger voters.
Tax reduction remains a major plank of their policy agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platform - the declared policies or aims of a political party

Etymology

Originally from Old Norse *planki* (board, slab), later used figuratively in politics to mean a single policy among many supporting a political 'platform'.

3. NOUN

/ plæŋk / (AmE), / plæŋk / (BrE)
a long, flat, and sturdy piece of sawn timber, typically wider than a board, used in building and construction
long, flat piece of sawn timber

Examples

He used a plank to replace the broken step on the porch.
Several planks were stacked in the workshop, ready for use in the new deck.
The gymnast balanced delicately on a narrow plank during her routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: board - a thin, flat piece of wood or other material

Etymology

From Old Norse 'planki', related to Latin 'planca' (board, plank).
