clench

1. NOUN

/ klɛntʃ / (AmE), / klɛntʃ / (BrE)
A small slip noose formed by seizing, used especially in ropework or nautical contexts.
small slip noose knot (nautical)

Examples

The sailor tied a clench to secure the end of the line to the post.
With a sturdy clench, the rope held fast despite the strong current.
Knowing how to make a proper clench is essential for any seafarer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - interlacement of rope for fastening or securing

2. VERB

/ klɛntʃ / (AmE), / klɛntʃ / (BrE)
to close or squeeze together tightly, especially with the hands, teeth, or muscles, often as a reaction to anger or determination
squeeze or close tightly

Examples

She tried to clench her fists to hide the trembling.
He clenched his jaw angrily during the argument.
You could see her hands clenching in frustration as she waited.

Synset Examples

clench one's jaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to grasp or hold firmly
  • Antonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or tight

Etymology

From Old English 'clencan', of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ klɛntʃ / (AmE), / klɛntʃ / (BrE)
to hold or grasp something tightly, especially with the hands or teeth
hold tightly; grasp firmly

Examples

He clenched his fists when he heard the bad news.
She clenched the steering wheel tightly as the car skidded on the ice.
During the tense moment, he found himself clenching his jaw involuntarily.

Synset Examples

clench a steering wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to hold something firmly
  • Antonym: release - to let go of something held

Etymology

From Middle English clenchen, from Old English clencan (to hold fast, secure).
