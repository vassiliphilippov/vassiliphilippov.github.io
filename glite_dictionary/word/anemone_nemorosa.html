Anemone nemorosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni nɛməˈroʊsə / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni nɛməˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
A European woodland plant with solitary white flowers, commonly found in deciduous forests; also known as wood anemone.
European woodland plant with white flowers

Examples

In early spring, the forest floor was carpeted with Anemone nemorosa in full bloom.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Anemone nemorosa found in European woodlands.
We spotted several clumps of Anemone nemorosa while walking through the ancient grove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood anemone - Common English name for Anemone nemorosa

Etymology

From Greek anemōnē (anemone, windflower) and Latin nemorosa (of groves, wooded).
