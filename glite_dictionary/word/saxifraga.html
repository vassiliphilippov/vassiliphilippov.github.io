Saxifraga

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksəˈfreɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfrɑːɡə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly perennial herbaceous plants, commonly known as saxifrages, found mainly in Arctic and cool regions of the Northern Hemisphere
genus of perennial plants (saxifrages)

Examples

Many species of Saxifraga thrive in rocky alpine gardens.
The botanist identified the small white flower as a Saxifraga.
Several varieties of Saxifraga are grown for their ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - common name for any plant of the genus Saxifraga

Etymology

From Latin 'saxifraga', meaning 'stone-breaker', referring to the plant's ability to grow in rocky places.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæksəˈfreɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə / (BrE)
a genus of mostly perennial herbaceous plants, commonly known as saxifrages, found mainly in Arctic and cool regions of the Northern Hemisphere
genus of saxifrage plants

Examples

Many alpine gardeners specialize in growing Saxifraga for their intricate flowers and tough nature.
The genus Saxifraga includes species that thrive in rocky, mountainous environments.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Saxifraga genus in the Himalayas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - any plant of the genus Saxifraga

Etymology

From Latin *saxifraga*, meaning 'stone-breaker', referring to the plant's habit of growing in rocky areas.
