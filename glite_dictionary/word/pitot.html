Pitot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəʊ / (BrE)
a measuring instrument consisting of a right-angled tube with an open end directed against the flow of a fluid, used to determine the fluid's velocity
instrument for measuring fluid velocity

Examples

The airplane's Pitot tube must be kept clear of obstructions to measure airspeed accurately.
Engineers inserted a Pitot tube into the pipe to determine the water flow rate.
The failure of the Pitot system can seriously affect the safety of an aircraft.

Etymology

Named after Henri Pitot (1695–1771), French engineer who invented it.
