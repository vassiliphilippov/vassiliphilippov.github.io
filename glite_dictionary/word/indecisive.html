indecisive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
not clearly defined or having no distinct limits; vague or ambiguous.
not clearly defined or fixed

Examples

The peace treaty was delayed by indecisive borders between the two nations.
The outcome of the match was still indecisive until the very last minute.
The argument centered on indecisive definitions in the company's policy.

Synset Examples

indecisive boundaries running through mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed, defined, or understood
  • Antonym: definite - clearly defined or determined

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + decisive from Latin decisivus (determining, deciding).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00705563-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
not settling an issue or unable to make decisions quickly or firmly.
not settling a decision or issue

Examples

The meeting was long and indecisive, leaving everyone frustrated.
His indecisive answer failed to resolve the dispute.
Voters grew tired of the government's indecisive response to the crisis.

Synset Examples

a long and indecisive war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisive - able to make decisions quickly and with firmness

Etymology

From in- (not) + decisive, from Latin dēcīdere (to decide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00688760-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈsaɪ.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈsaɪ.sɪv / (BrE)
not able to make decisions quickly or confidently; lacking firmness or resolve.
not able to make decisions firmly

Examples

The team's progress stalled because their leader was so indecisive.
She is often indecisive when choosing where to eat.
His indecisive nature makes planning anything a real challenge.

Synset Examples

an indecisive manager brought the enterprise to a standstill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisive - having the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively
  • Synonym: hesitant - unsure or slow in acting or speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'decisivus' (decisive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00689358-a
Synset POS: a
