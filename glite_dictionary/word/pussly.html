pussly

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌsli / (AmE), / ˈpʌsli / (BrE)
a plant with bright yellow flowers and succulent, mildly acidic leaves, often eaten raw or cooked, especially in Indian, Greek, and Middle Eastern cuisine; also known as purslane.
edible plant, also called purslane

Examples

They picked fresh pussly from the garden for their salad.
Some Southern cooks add pussly to soups for extra flavor and nutrition.
Wild pussly grows abundantly in the fields during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purslane - another name for the same plant, Portulaca oleracea

Etymology

Dialectal variant of 'purslane'.
