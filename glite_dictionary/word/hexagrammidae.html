1. NOUN

/ hɛk.səˈɡræm.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / hɛk.səˈɡræm.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)

Examples

The Hexagrammidae are native to the cold waters of the North Pacific Ocean.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛksəˈɡræmɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəˈɡræmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes known as greenlings, found in the North Pacific Ocean, characterized by elongated bodies and small scales.
family of marine fishes (greenlings)

Examples

The Hexagrammidae are commonly found in rocky coastal areas of the North Pacific.
Scientists have studied the reproductive behavior of several species within the Hexagrammidae family.
Members of Hexagrammidae are important to both commercial fisheries and recreational anglers.

Etymology

From New Latin Hexagrammus (type genus) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).
