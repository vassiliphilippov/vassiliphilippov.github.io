Calvinist

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæl.və.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vɪ.nɪst / (BrE)
relating to Calvinism or its followers
relating to Calvinism

Examples

The church follows a strictly Calvinist interpretation of Christian doctrine.
Many early American settlers held Calvinist beliefs about predestination.
His Calvinist upbringing greatly influenced his worldview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinism - the branch of Protestantism based on the teachings of John Calvin

Etymology

From the name of John Calvin (1509–1564), French theologian and reformer, plus -ist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.vɪ.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vɪ.nɪst / (BrE)
A person who follows or adheres to the religious teachings and doctrines of John Calvin, especially in relation to Protestant theology.
follower of John Calvin's theology

Examples

As a Calvinist, he believed strongly in the doctrine of predestination.
The Calvinist church in the city held services every Sunday.
She studied the history of Calvinist thought during her theology course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Puritan - a member of a group of English Protestants influenced by Calvinism
  • Antonym: Lutheran - an adherent of Martin Luther's theological doctrines

Etymology

From John Calvin (1509–1564), French Protestant theologian, plus the suffix -ist.

Synset POS: n
