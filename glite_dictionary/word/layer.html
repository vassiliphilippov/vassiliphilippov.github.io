layer

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ə / (BrE)
a thin sheet or stratum of material, especially one composed of a single thickness of cells or elements
thin sheet or stratum, often of cells

Examples

The outer layer of the skin is called the epidermis.
Each layer of the cake was separated by a thin spread of cream.
A single layer of cells protects the inner tissue of the leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratum - a single level of material or tissue in a layered system

Etymology

From Middle English 'leir', from Old English 'leġer' (bed, layer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪə / (BrE)
a sheet, level, or stratum of material, typically one of several, lying over or under another
sheet or level lying over or under another

Examples

A thick layer of snow covered the ground after the storm.
The cake has a layer of cream between each sponge.
Pollution has created a dark layer in the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratum - a layer or level of material, usually in geology or sociology
  • Synonym: coating - a thin layer covering a surface
  • Antonym: core - the central or innermost part, often contrasted with outer layers

Etymology

From Middle English *leier*, from Old English *leger* (bed, camp, lair).
