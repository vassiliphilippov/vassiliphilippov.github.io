immoveable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmuːvəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmuːvəbəl / (BrE)
not able or intended to be moved; firmly fixed or motionless.
not able to be moved

Examples

The old fortress was built on immoveable foundations of solid rock.
Her stance on the matter was immoveable, despite numerous arguments.
They erected a monument intended to be immoveable for generations to come.

Synset Examples

the immovable hills

Related Terms

  • Antonym: movable - able to be moved; not fixed in one place
  • Synonym: fixed - firmly attached and not easily moved

Etymology

From Latin immovibilis, from in- (not) + movēre (to move).
