unmannered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmænɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmænəd / (BrE)
not affected by artificiality or pretense; natural and straightforward in behavior or appearance.
natural; unaffected by artificiality

Examples

She admired his unmannered way of interacting with both patients and staff.
The actor's unmannered performance brought a sense of realism to the film.
Despite his status, his speech was warm and unmannered.

Synset Examples

the doctor's quiet unmannered entry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: affected - artificial or pretentious in manner or behavior
  • Synonym: natural - innate, genuine; not artificial in manner

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'mannered' (having manners or artificiality).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmænərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmænəd / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) lacking good manners; socially incorrect or rude.
lacking good manners

Examples

His unmannered remarks offended many people at the dinner table.
The guests were shocked by the unmannered behavior of their host.
She found his unmannered tone quite disrespectful.

Synset Examples

resentment flared at such an unmannered intrusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-mannered - rude or impolite; having bad manners
  • Antonym: polite - showing good manners and respect for others
Source: WordNet
