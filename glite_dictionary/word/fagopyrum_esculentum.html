Fagopyrum esculentum

1. NOUN

/ fəˌɡoʊˈpaɪrəm ˌɛskjəˈlɛntəm / (AmE), / fəˌɡəʊˈpɪərəm ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəm / (BrE)
An annual Asian plant with clusters of small pinkish-white flowers and edible triangular seeds that are used whole or ground into flour; commonly known as buckwheat.
species of buckwheat plant

Examples

Fagopyrum esculentum is widely cultivated for its nutritious seeds, commonly referred to as buckwheat.
The flour made from Fagopyrum esculentum seeds is popular in gluten-free diets.
Many traditional Asian dishes use the seeds of Fagopyrum esculentum as a staple ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckwheat - common name for Fagopyrum esculentum

Etymology

From scientific Latin: Fagopyrum from Latin 'fagus' (beech) and Greek 'pyros' (wheat), esculentum meaning 'edible' in Latin.
