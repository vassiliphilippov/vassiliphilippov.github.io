Priacanthidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪəˈkænθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpraɪəˈkanθɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small carnivorous percoid fishes found worldwide in tropical seas, commonly called bigeyes.
family of tropical carnivorous fishes (bigeyes)

Examples

The Priacanthidae are known for their large eyes, adapted to low-light environments in tropical seas.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Priacanthidae family near the coral reef last summer.
Many aquarists are fascinated by the vibrant colors and nocturnal habits of Priacanthidae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'prios' (saw) and 'akantha' (thorn, spine), referring to features of the fish.
