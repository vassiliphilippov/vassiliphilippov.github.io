Hyracoidea

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌrækoʊˈiːdiə / (AmE), / haɪˌrækɔɪˈiːdɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of small, herbivorous mammals known as hyraxes, including both living and extinct species
order of mammals (hyraxes)
medical

Examples

The order Hyracoidea includes several species of small, thickset mammals called hyraxes.
Modern representatives of Hyracoidea are found primarily in Africa and the Middle East.
Fossils from the Hyracoidea order reveal a much greater past diversity than is seen today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small, herbivorous mammal native to Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hyrax' (shrewmouse, hyrax) + -oidea (resembling, order).
