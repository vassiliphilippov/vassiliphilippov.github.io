Desmodium tortuosum

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdiəm tɔrˈtjuːəsəm / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdiəm tɔːˈtjʊəsəm / (BrE)
A tropical leguminous plant, also known as beggarweed, cultivated in the southern United States and the West Indies for forage and as a soil improver.
forage plant used to improve soil

Examples

Farmers often plant Desmodium tortuosum to enrich the soil with nitrogen and provide grazing for cattle.
Because Desmodium tortuosum grows well in poor soils, it's popular in subtropical and tropical agriculture.
Researchers study Desmodium tortuosum for its benefits in sustainable farming systems and natural weed control.

Etymology

From genus Desmodium (Greek 'desmos', meaning bond, and 'desmódion', meaning little bundle) and Latin 'tortuosum', meaning twisted or winding.
