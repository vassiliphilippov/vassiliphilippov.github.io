heroin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛroʊɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəʊɪn / (BrE)
A powerful and highly addictive narcotic drug made from morphine, often used illegally and associated with a high risk of dependence.
highly addictive narcotic drug

Examples

The police seized several bags of heroin during the raid.
He struggled with heroin addiction for many years before seeking help.
Reports indicate an increase in heroin overdoses in the area this year.

Etymology

From German 'Heroin', coined from 'heroisch' (heroic) for supposed heroic effects.
