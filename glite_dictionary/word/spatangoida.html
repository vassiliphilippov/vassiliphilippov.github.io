Spatangoida

1. NOUN

/ spəˌtæŋˈɡɔɪdə / (AmE), / spəˌtæŋˈɡɔɪdə / (BrE)
an order of sea urchins characterized by a heart-shaped body and often found buried in sand or mud
order of heart-shaped sea urchins

Examples

Fossils of Spatangoida provide important clues about ancient marine environments.
The Spatangoida burrow into the seabed to feed on organic material.
Researchers studied several species of Spatangoida collected off the coast of Australia.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek spatangos (a type of sea urchin) + -oida (like, resembling).

2. NOUN

/ spəˌtæŋɡɔɪˈdə / (AmE), / spəˌtæŋɡɔɪˈdə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of heart-shaped sea urchins found in marine environments, characterized by their bilateral symmetry and burrowing lifestyle
heart-shaped sea urchin order

Examples

Marine biologists discovered several Spatangoida specimens while exploring deep-sea sediment.
The Spatangoida are known for their unique heart-shaped bodies and specialized burrowing behavior.
Fossil records show that Spatangoida have existed since the Cretaceous period.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Spatangoida, based on genus Spatangus and the suffix -oida (resembling).
