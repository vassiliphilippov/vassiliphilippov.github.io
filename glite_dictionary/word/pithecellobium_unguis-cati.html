Pithecellobium unguis-cati

1. NOUN

/ pɪθəˌsɛloʊˈbiəm ˌʌŋɡwɪsˈkaɪti / (AmE), / pɪθɛlˌsiːliəm ˌʌŋɡwɪsˈkaɪti / (BrE)
an erect shrub from the West Indies and Florida, having few or no spines, white to yellow racemes of flowers, and curved pointed pods with shiny black seeds
a shrub native to West Indies and Florida

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare Pithecellobium unguis-cati near the tropical exhibit.
Many birds are attracted to the shiny seeds of the Pithecellobium unguis-cati.
Researchers documented the flowering period of Pithecellobium unguis-cati in southern Florida.

Etymology

From Greek 'pithēkos' (ape) and Latin 'unguis-cati' (cat's claw), referring to the shape of the pods.
