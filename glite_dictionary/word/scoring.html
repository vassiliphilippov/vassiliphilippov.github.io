scoring

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɔː.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of evaluating performance by assigning a grade or numerical score
evaluation by grading or assigning scores

Examples

The teacher spent all weekend scoring the final exams for her students.
Accurate scoring is essential in any competitive sport or standardized test.
He was criticized for his inconsistent scoring of the essays.

Synset Examples

what he disliked about teaching was all the grading he had to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grading - the act of assigning grades to evaluate performance

Etymology

From the verb 'score', from Old Norse 'skor' (notch, tally), originally referring to marks cut as a record.
