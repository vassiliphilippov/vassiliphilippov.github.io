Cinchona ledgeriana

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈkoʊnə ˌlɛdʒəriˈænə / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə ˌlɛdʒəriˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of Peruvian shrub or small tree with large, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers, cultivated for its bark, which has medicinal uses.
species of medicinal tree or shrub

Examples

The bark of the Cinchona ledgeriana tree is harvested for use in traditional medicine.
Researchers studied Cinchona ledgeriana to better understand its quinine content.
Many plantations in South America grow Cinchona ledgeriana for the pharmaceutical industry.

Etymology

From the genus name Cinchona, named after the Countess of Chinchón, and the species named after Charles Ledger, a 19th-century British trader.
