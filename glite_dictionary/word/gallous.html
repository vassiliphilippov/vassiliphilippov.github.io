gallous

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləs / (AmE), / ˈɡaləs / (BrE)
An archaic term for a structure, typically with two upright posts and a crossbeam, used for hanging people as a form of execution.
archaic term for gallows (execution structure)
archaic

Examples

The prisoner was marched to the gallous at dawn, watched by a silent crowd.
Stories of criminals meeting their fate at the gallous were common in the old town.
He feared nothing, not even the gallous erected in the square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallows - structure for hanging people as punishment

Etymology

Variant of 'gallows', from Old English *galga* (gallows).
