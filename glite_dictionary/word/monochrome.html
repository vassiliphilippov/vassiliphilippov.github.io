monochrome

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑnəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌkrəʊm / (BrE)
having or appearing to have only one color; made up of shades of a single color
using one color or shades of one color

Examples

The designer chose a monochrome palette for the living room, using only shades of blue.
She wore a striking monochrome outfit from head to toe.
The monochrome photograph gave the city a nostalgic and timeless feel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polychrome - having many colors or being decorated in several colors

Etymology

From Greek 'monokhrōmos', from 'monos' (single) + 'khrōma' (color).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkrəʊm / (BrE)
A photograph, slide, or picture in black and white or varying tones of only one color.
black-and-white or single-color photograph

Examples

The old family album is full of stunning monochromes from the early 1900s.
He prefers shooting in monochrome to capture the mood of the city streets.
A simple monochrome can sometimes convey more emotion than a color photograph.

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + khrōma (color).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02849637-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkrəʊm / (BrE)
A painting or drawing executed in varying tones of a single color.
artwork using one color with shades

Examples

The artist created a striking monochrome using only shades of blue.
In the gallery, a large monochrome dominated the central wall.
He prefers to work in monochrome rather than using a full spectrum of colors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polychrome - a painting or object in multiple colors

Etymology

From French 'monochrome', from Greek 'monokhrōmos' (of one color), from 'monos' (single) + 'khrōma' (color).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
