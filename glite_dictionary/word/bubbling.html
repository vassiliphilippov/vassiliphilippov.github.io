bubbling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌblɪŋ / (BrE)
full of lively excitement, enthusiasm, or high spirits; effervescent.
full of excitement or enthusiasm

Examples

Her bubbling personality quickly made her the life of the party.
He gave a bubbling account of his exciting adventures abroad.
The bubbling energy of the children filled the room with laughter.

Synset Examples

his fertile effervescent mind
scintillating personality
a row of sparkly cheerleaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effervescent - full of lively energy and enthusiasm
  • Synonym: sparkling - attractively lively and animated (of a personality)
  • Antonym: dull - lacking energy or excitement

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌb.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
emitting or filled with bubbles, typically as a result of carbonation or fermentation
emitting or filled with bubbles

Examples

The waiter poured the bubbling champagne into elegant flutes.
Freshly brewed beer often has a bubbling head of foam on the surface.
A bubbling fountain brightened the center of the courtyard.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothy - full of or covered with a mass of small bubbles
  • Synonym: effervescent - giving off bubbles; fizzy
