outline

1. VERB

/ ˈaʊtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.laɪn / (BrE)
to trace or mark the outer edge or shape of something
trace or mark the outer edge of

Examples

The artist carefully outlined the figure before filling it in with color.
She used a pencil to outline the shapes on the canvas.
Shadows from the streetlight outlined the branches against the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - to draw a rough or unfinished version of something
  • Synonym: contour - to mark the outline of something

Etymology

From out- + line, originally meaning to mark the outer line.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01693274-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.laɪn / (BrE)
a general description or plan giving the main ideas of something but not the details
general plan or summary

Examples

Could you prepare an outline of the project before the meeting?
The author presented an outline of her new book to the publisher.
We started with a rough outline before writing the full report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: draft - a preliminary version of a written work or plan
  • Antonym: detail - an individual feature, fact, or item

Etymology

From out- + line, meaning to mark out the lines or shape.
