laurelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔr.əld / (AmE), / ˈlɒr.əld / (BrE)
Crowned with or as if with laurel, symbolizing victory or honor.
adorned with laurel for victory or honor

Examples

The laurelled poet was praised for his remarkable achievements.
She stood beneath the arch, laurelled and smiling, as the crowd cheered her victory.
He entered the hall, laurelled for his triumph in the national competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowned - having received a crown as a mark of honor
  • Antonym: unhonored - not honored or recognized for achievement

Etymology

From 'laurel' (a type of plant used as a symbol of victory) + -ed suffix.
