unobvious

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːbviəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒbviəs / (BrE)
not easily noticed or understood; not immediately apparent or obvious
not immediately apparent

Examples

The solution to the puzzle was so unobvious that most people missed it completely.
Sometimes, the cause of a technical problem is unobvious and requires careful investigation.
Their connection was unobvious at first, but it made sense after some explanation.

Synset Examples

in mathematical science connections are exhibited which … are extremely unobvious

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'obvious,' from Latin 'obvius' (in the way).
