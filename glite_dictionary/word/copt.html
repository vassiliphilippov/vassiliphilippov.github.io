Copt

1. NOUN

/ kɑpt / (AmE), / kɒpt / (BrE)
a member of an ethnoreligious group native to Egypt, descended from the ancient Egyptians; often associated with the Coptic Christian Church
an Egyptian of ancient descent, often Christian

Examples

The Copts of Egypt have preserved many ancient traditions and customs.
During the festival, Copt families gather to celebrate together.
The church was built by the local Copt community in the 4th century.

Etymology

From Arabic 'qubṭ', from Coptic 'Kyptios', from Greek 'Aigyptios' (Egyptian).

2. NOUN

/ kɑpt / (AmE), / kɒpt / (BrE)
a member of the Coptic Christian Church, especially in Egypt
a member of the Coptic Church

Examples

Many Copts still speak the Coptic language during religious services.
The Copt community celebrates Christmas on January 7th.
A prominent Copt was elected to the Egyptian parliament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coptic - relating to the Coptic Church or its members

Etymology

From Arabic 'qubṭ', from Coptic 'kubti', from Greek 'Aigyptos' (Egyptian).
