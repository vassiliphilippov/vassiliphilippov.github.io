lipstick

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪp.stɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.stɪk / (BrE)
to apply lipstick to (someone or oneself)
apply lipstick to

Examples

She quickly lipsticked her lips before leaving the house.
He watched her as she carefully lipsticked her mouth in the mirror.
After dinner, she stepped away to lipstick again before the photos.

Synset Examples

She lipsticked her mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apply - to put or spread (something) on a surface

Etymology

From the noun 'lipstick' (a cosmetic for coloring the lips). The verb form derived from nominal usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00041148-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪp.stɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.stɪk / (BrE)
A colored cosmetic applied to the lips to enhance or change their color.
colored cosmetic for lips

Examples

She reapplied her lipstick before stepping out of the car.
The store carries every shade of lipstick you can imagine.
He noticed a faint mark of red lipstick on the cup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lip gloss - cosmetic for making lips shine
  • Synonym: lip balm - substance applied to lips to prevent dryness

Etymology

Formed from 'lip' + 'stick', referring to its original form as a stick for coloring lips.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03681809-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈlɪp.stɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.stɪk / (BrE)
to apply or mark with lipstick, especially by coloring or tracing a shape or area
to apply or mark with lipstick

Examples

The artist lipsticked a bright red heart on the canvas as a statement piece.
She lipsticked her signature onto the bathroom mirror before leaving.
During the play, the actors lipsticked large circles on their cheeks for a comic effect.

Synset Examples

The clown had lipsticked circles on his cheeks
