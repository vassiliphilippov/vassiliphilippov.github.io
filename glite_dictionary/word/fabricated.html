fabricated

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
invented or made up, especially to deceive or as a product of imagination rather than reality.
invented; made up (not real)

Examples

The defendant gave a fabricated account of his whereabouts to the police.
She realized that his fabricated story did not explain the missing money.
Many urban legends are entirely fabricated but still widely believed.

Synset Examples

a fabricated excuse for his absence
a fancied wrong
a fictional character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fictitious - not real or true, being imaginary or fabricated
  • Antonym: genuine - actually existing; real; not false or imitation

Etymology

From Latin 'fabricatus', past participle of 'fabricari' (to fashion, build).
