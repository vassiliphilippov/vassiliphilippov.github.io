canonic

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnɑnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪk / (BrE)
Following established or officially recognized rules or standards.
following orthodox or recognized rules

Examples

The museum only displays canonic works of art that define each movement.
Her analysis adheres strictly to the canonic interpretation of the novel.
The choir performed several canonic pieces from the Baroque period.

Synset Examples

the drinking of cocktails was as canonical a rite as the mixing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canonical - conforming to orthodox or recognized rules

Etymology

From Latin 'canonicus', meaning 'according to rule'.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈnɑnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪk / (BrE)
included in or relating to a recognized canon, especially a book accepted as part of the Bible
included in a religious canon, especially biblical

Examples

The Council deemed only certain texts to be canonic and worthy of inclusion in the Bible.
Scholars debate whether the Gospel of Thomas should be considered canonic.
Not all early Christian writings are recognized as canonic by every denomination.

Synset Examples

a canonical book of the Christian New Testament

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canonical - being part of a recognized canon, especially religious texts

Etymology

From Late Latin canonicus (canonical), from Greek kanōn (rule, standard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03013161-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ kəˈnɑːnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or required by canon law, especially within the context of the Christian Church.
relating to canon law

Examples

The priest explained the canonic requirements for a valid marriage.
Such a decree is not considered canonic by many theologians.
He studied the canonic regulations affecting the clergy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canonical - relating to canon law or established rules

Etymology

From Late Latin canonicus (according to rule), from Latin canon (rule, standard).

4. ADJ

/ kəˈnɑnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a form or expression) reduced to the simplest and most significant form possible without loss of generality.
simplest significant form (technical)

Examples

Every matrix can be transformed into its canonic form for easier analysis.
The canonic representation eliminates unnecessary complexity in the proof.
Linguists study canonic syllable patterns across different languages.

Synset Examples

a basic story line
a canonical syllable pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canonical - following or relating to an accepted rule or standard

Etymology

From Late Latin canonicus, via Latin canon (rule, standard).
