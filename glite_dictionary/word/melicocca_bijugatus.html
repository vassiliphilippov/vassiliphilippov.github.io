Melicocca bijugatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛl.ɪˈkɑː.kə baɪˈjuːɡətəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.ɪˈkɒk.ə baɪˈjuːɡətəs / (BrE)
a tropical American tree bearing small edible fruits with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy, translucent pulp; also called Spanish lime or guinep
tropical tree with edible green fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Melicocca bijugatus is popular as a snack in many Caribbean countries.
Local markets often sell bunches of Melicocca bijugatus when the fruit is in season.
Children climbed the tree to pick the ripest Melicocca bijugatus from its branches.

Etymology

From the scientific Latin name, with 'Melicocca' from Greek 'meli' (honey) and 'kokkos' (berry), referring to the sweet fruit.
