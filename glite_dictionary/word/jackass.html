jackass

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌæs / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌɑːs / (BrE)
a male donkey
male donkey

Examples

The farmer led the old jackass back into the stable for the night.
A jackass is often used to sire working mules.
We watched a jackass braying loudly in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donkey - a domesticated hoofed mammal of the horse family with long ears

Etymology

From jack (male) + ass (donkey); 'jack' is a slang term for a male animal.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02392595-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌæs / (AmE), / ˈdʒakˌas / (BrE)
a man who behaves in a very stupid or foolish way; an incompetent or senseless person
a foolish or stupid man
slang

Examples

Don't be such a jackass and listen to what I'm telling you.
He acted like a complete jackass at the meeting, embarrassing everyone.
Sometimes, his jokes go too far and make him sound like a jackass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • Synonym: idiot - a person of low intelligence or senseless behavior

Etymology

From 'jack' (a generic male name) and 'ass' (donkey, used as an insult for stupidity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10177319-n
Synset POS: n
