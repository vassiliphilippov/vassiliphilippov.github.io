Fritillaria micrantha

1. NOUN

/ frɪtɪˈlɛəriə maɪˈkrænθə / (AmE), / frɪtɪˈlɛəriə mɪˈkrænθə / (BrE)
A species of fritillary native to California, having brownish-purple or greenish bell-shaped flowers.
a Californian fritillary plant species

Examples

The hillsides were dotted with the rare Fritillaria micrantha during the spring bloom.
Botanists are working to protect Fritillaria micrantha from habitat loss in California.
Photographers travel from afar to capture images of the bell-shaped flowers of Fritillaria micrantha.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fritillary - a plant of the genus Fritillaria

Etymology

From Latin; 'Fritillaria' is a genus of flowering plants, and 'micrantha' means 'small-flowered' from Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'anthos' (flower).
