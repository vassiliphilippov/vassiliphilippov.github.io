to enucleate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnuː.kli.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuː.klɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to remove the nucleus from (a cell)
remove the nucleus from a cell
medical

Examples

Using a micropipette, the scientist carefully enucleated the egg cell.
Researchers can enucleate cells to study how they function without their nuclei.
The technician enucleated several cells as part of the genetic experiment.

Etymology

From Latin enucleatus (to remove the kernel), from e- (out of) + nucleus (kernel).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnuːkliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːklɪeɪt / (BrE)
to remove (a tumor, organ, or growth) from its surrounding covering or sac, especially during surgery
remove from a covering, esp. surgically
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to enucleate the tumor rather than remove the entire organ.
After examining the patient's eye, the medical team opted to enucleate it due to severe trauma.
Using delicate instruments, they carefully enucleated the cyst from the tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excise - to remove by cutting, especially surgically

Etymology

From Latin enucleatus, from e- (out) + nucleus (kernel).
