Poephila

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈɛfɪlə / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛfɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of grassfinches native to Australia, belonging to the family Estrildidae
genus of Australian grassfinches

Examples

The Poephila are known for their colorful plumage and lively social behavior.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Poephila in northern Australia.
Many bird enthusiasts keep Poephila in aviaries because of their vibrant appearance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek 'poe-' (making) + 'philos' (loving), referring to the birds' nesting behavior.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈɛfɪlə / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of small Australian grassfinches, including species such as the zebra finch and double-barred finch.
genus of Australian grassfinches

Examples

The Poephila genus includes several colorful species popular among bird enthusiasts.
Zebra finches, a member of the Poephila genus, are often kept as pets due to their lively nature.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Poephila in their native Australian habitats.

Etymology

From Greek poephilos (fond of grass).
