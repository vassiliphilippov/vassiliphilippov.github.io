Botrychium multifidum

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtrɪkiəm ˌmʌltɪˈfaɪdəm / (AmE), / bəʊˈtrɪkɪəm ˌmʌltɪˈfɪdəm / (BrE)
A species of European fern with leathery, sparsely hairy fronds, commonly known as leather grapefern.
a species of European fern

Examples

The Botrychium multifidum is often found in moist meadows and woodlands throughout northern Europe.
Several conservationists are working to protect the natural habitats of Botrychium multifidum due to its rarity.
The leathery texture of Botrychium multifidum's fronds makes it easily distinguishable from other ferns.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Botrychium from Greek 'botrys' (bunch of grapes), referencing the cluster-like sporangia; multifidum meaning 'many-divided' in Latin.
