I Thessalonians

1. PROPN

/ ˈfaɪrst ˌθɛsəˈloʊniənz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌθɛsəˈləʊniənz / (BrE)
the first book of the New Testament letters to the Thessalonians, attributed to Saint Paul
a New Testament epistle by Saint Paul

Examples

Many scholars believe that I Thessalonians was Paul's earliest letter to a Christian community.
During the Bible study, we read several passages from I Thessalonians.
The message of hope in I Thessalonians has inspired many believers throughout the centuries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: II Thessalonians - the second New Testament letter to the Thessalonians, also attributed to Saint Paul

Etymology

From Latin ‘Thessalonicenses’, referring to the people of Thessalonica, an ancient city in Macedonia.
