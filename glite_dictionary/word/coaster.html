coaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstɚ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstə / (BrE)
A small mat or plate placed under a glass, cup, or bottle to protect the surface of a table from moisture or heat.
small mat or plate for protecting tables

Examples

She placed a coaster under her cold drink to stop water rings from forming on the table.
Please remember to use a coaster before setting your mug down on my desk.
The restaurant provided colorful coasters for every glass on the table.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: placemat - a larger mat for a plate or setting at a table

Etymology

From 'coast' with the agent suffix '-er', originally referring to a ship that sails along a coast. The tableware meaning emerged in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstə / (BrE)
a person who lives in a coastal area
person living by the coast

Examples

As a coaster, she had grown up with the sound of waves always nearby.
Many coasters depend on fishing as their main source of income.
The hurricane greatly affected the lives of local coasters.
