solemness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːləmnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being solemn; dignified seriousness
dignified seriousness; solemn quality

Examples

There was a certain solemness in the ceremony that moved everyone present.
The judge's solemness commanded respect in the courtroom.
His voice carried the solemness of someone delivering grave news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solemnity - the state or quality of being solemn
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness; lightness of manner

Etymology

From solemn + -ness, modelled after 'solemnity' from Latin 'sollemnitas'.
