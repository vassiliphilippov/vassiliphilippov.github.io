chiefly

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃifli / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːfli / (BrE)
mainly; for the most part; primarily.
mainly; for the most part

Examples

The festival is chiefly celebrated in rural areas.
Their research focuses chiefly on environmental changes.
His interests are chiefly in literature and history.

Synset Examples

he is mainly interested in butterflies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainly - for the most part, primarily
  • Synonym: primarily - mainly; for the most part
  • Antonym: entirely - wholly; completely

Etymology

From chief + -ly, with 'chief' from Old French 'chef' (leader, head) and ultimately Latin 'caput' (head).
