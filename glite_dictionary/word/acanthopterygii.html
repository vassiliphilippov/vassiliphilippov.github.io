Acanthopterygii

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəpˌtɛˈrɪdʒiaɪ / (AmE), / əˌkanθɒptəˈrɪdʒɪaɪ / (BrE)
A large group of teleost fishes characterized by fins with sharp, bony spines.
group of spiny-finned teleost fishes

Examples

The order Acanthopterygii includes many familiar fish species found in both marine and freshwater habitats.
Ichthyologists study the unique fin structure that distinguishes Acanthopterygii from other teleost fishes.
The spiny fins of Acanthopterygii provide them with greater maneuverability and defense against predators.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + pteryx (fin, wing).
