Nepeta

1. NOUN

/ nəˈpiːtə / (AmE), / nɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic plants in the mint family, commonly known as catmint, often grown for their attractive flowers and scent.
genus of aromatic plants (catmint)

Examples

Many gardeners plant Nepeta for its blue flowers and fragrant foliage.
Cats are often attracted to Nepeta, commonly known as catmint.
The border of the garden was lined with a variety of Nepeta species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catnip - A species (Nepeta cataria) of the genus Nepeta known for its effect on cats

Etymology

From Latin *Nepeta*, the name of the ancient Italian town Nepi, where the plant was prevalent.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈpiːtə / (AmE), / nɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mint family, known as catmint or catnip, often grown for their aromatic leaves.
genus of aromatic mint plants (catmint/catnip)

Examples

The garden's border was lined with Nepeta to attract bees and butterflies.
Many cat owners plant Nepeta in their yards because cats love it so much.
Botanists studied several species of Nepeta for their distinct aromatic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catmint - another name for plants of the genus Nepeta, especially as garden plants
  • Synonym: catnip - common name for Nepeta cataria, a species in the genus Nepeta

Etymology

From Latin Nepeta, an ancient name for this herb.
