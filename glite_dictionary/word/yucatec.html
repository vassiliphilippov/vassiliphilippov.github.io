Yucatec

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːkəˈtɛk / (AmE), / ˌjuːkəˈtɛk / (BrE)
the Mayan language spoken by the Yucatec people, primarily in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico
a Mayan language of the Yucatán region

Examples

Many ancient inscriptions were written in Yucatec by the Maya civilization.
She is fluent in both Spanish and Yucatec, allowing her to translate historical documents.
The dictionary contains thousands of words from Yucatec and their English equivalents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan - relating to the family of languages spoken by the Maya people

Etymology

From Spanish 'yucateco', relating to the Yucatán region.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.kəˈtɛk / (AmE), / ˌjuː.kəˈtɛk / (BrE)
a member of the Mayan people native to the Yucatán peninsula in Mexico
member of Mayan people from Yucatán

Examples

Many Yucatec still speak their native language alongside Spanish.
The Yucatec are known for their traditional crafts and vibrant festivals.
Anthropologists have studied the customs of the Yucatec extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan - member of the indigenous peoples of Mesoamerica

Etymology

From Yucatán, the region in Mexico, plus the Spanish '-ec' suffix; originally from the Maya language.

Source: WordNet
