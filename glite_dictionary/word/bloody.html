bloody

1. VERB

/ ˈblʌdi / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi / (BrE)
to cover with blood, especially as a result of injury or violence
to cover with blood

Examples

He bloodied his knuckles during the fight.
A sharp rock bloodied her knee as she fell.
The hunter accidentally bloodied his hands while dressing the deer.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - to mark or discolor something, especially with a liquid

2. ADJ

/ ˈblədi / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Used to emphasize a statement or to express annoyance, anger, or surprise.
informal intensifier (esp. British)
informal british

Examples

That was a bloody brilliant performance from the whole team.
It's bloody cold out here, isn't it?
I can't find my keys—this is bloody annoying!

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree; not very

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; used as an intensifier in British English since the 17th century.

3. ADV

/ ˈblʌdi / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi / (BrE)
(used before adjectives or adverbs) extremely; used to add emphasis, often to express anger or annoyance
emphatic intensifier, especially in British English
informal british

Examples

It's bloody cold outside tonight, so grab a coat before leaving.
She was bloody furious when she heard the news.
That was a bloody brilliant performance in last night's match.

Synset Examples

you are bloody right
Why are you so all-fired aggressive?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - used for emphasis, meaning 'to a high degree'
  • Synonym: damn - used for emphasis, as an intensifier (informal, sometimes offensive)

Etymology

Originally from Old English *blōdig* (blood-stained); use as intensifier dates from 17th century Britain.

4. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdi / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi / (BrE)
covered with, stained by, or accompanied by blood; involving or resulting in bloodshed.
covered with or involving blood

Examples

He walked in with a bloody nose after the game.
The towel became bloody as she wiped her cut.
It was a bloody battle, leaving injuries on both sides.

Synset Examples

a bloody nose
your scarf is all bloody
the effects will be violent and probably bloody
a bloody fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gory - involving or showing a lot of bloodshed
  • Antonym: bloodless - without blood or not involving bloodshed

Etymology

From Old English 'blōdig', from 'blōd' (blood) + '-ig' (having the qualities of).
