Amorpha canescens

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɔːrfə kəˈnɛsənz / (AmE), / əˈmɔːfə kəˈnɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A shrub native to sandy woodlands and stream banks of the western United States, recognized by its grayish pinnate leaves and dull racemose flowers, sometimes associated with the presence of lead ore.
shrub native to western US woodlands

Examples

Many prairie restoration projects make use of Amorpha canescens for its hardiness and soil-improving properties.
Botanists observed Amorpha canescens thriving along the sandy banks after last season's flood.
Some miners once believed that finding Amorpha canescens in an area signaled the presence of lead ore underground.

Etymology

From Latin; Amorpha (genus name) from Greek 'amorphos' (shapeless), canescens (becoming gray) from Latin.
