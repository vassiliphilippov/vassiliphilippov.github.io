heading

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
A horizontal or nearly horizontal passageway dug in a mine.
horizontal passage in a mine

Examples

The miners extended the heading to access a new section of the coal seam.
After months of excavation, the heading was finally completed.
Proper ventilation is essential when working in a heading underground.

Synset Examples

they dug a drift parallel with the vein

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - a horizontal passage in a mine

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the direction in which something, especially a vehicle or vessel, is moving or facing
direction of movement

Examples

The ship changed its heading to avoid the storm.
After consulting the map, we realized our heading was slightly off.
Pilots must constantly monitor their heading during a flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: course - the intended direction of travel, especially of a ship or aircraft
  • Synonym: bearing - the direction or path along which something moves or lies, especially in navigation

Etymology

From 'head' + '-ing', originally referring to the point or direction towards which one heads.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
a line of text at the top of a page or section that introduces or summarizes what follows
line of text introducing a section

Examples

Each chapter begins with a bold heading to capture the reader's attention.
The newspaper article had a misleading heading that caused confusion.
Please check that every page in your report has the correct heading.

Synset Examples

the heading seemed to have little to do with the text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the name of a book, composition, or other artistic work
  • Degree Lower: subheading - a secondary heading indicating a subsection

Etymology

From the verb 'head' + -ing, referring to what is placed at the head or top.

Source: WordNet
