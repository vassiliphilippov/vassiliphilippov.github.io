Urosaurus

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊsɔːrəs / (AmE), / jʊˈrəʊsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small lizards in the family Iguanidae, commonly known as tree and brush lizards, native to North and Central America.
genus of tree and brush lizards

Examples

The Urosaurus is often found basking on tree branches in arid regions.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Urosaurus genus.
Because of its distinctive blue markings, the Urosaurus is easy to identify for herpetologists.

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (tail) and 'sauros' (lizard), referring to the distinctive tails of the lizards in this genus.
