devilishly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈdɛvɪlɪʃli / (BrE)
In a manner that is playfully wicked, mischievous, or extremely; to a great degree.
in a playfully wicked or extreme manner

Examples

The children were devilishly clever in planning their secret surprise.
He grinned devilishly before revealing the punchline of his joke.
This puzzle is devilishly difficult to solve, even for experts.

Synset Examples

the socialists are further handicapped if they believe that capitalists are not only wicked but also devilishly clever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievously - in a way that shows playful mischief or teasing

Etymology

From 'devilish' + '-ly', patterned after 'devil'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈdɛvɪlɪʃli / (BrE)
To an extreme or excessive degree; very.
to a great or excessive degree

Examples

The cake was devilishly rich and sweet, making it hard to stop at one piece.
He was devilishly clever at solving puzzles that baffled everyone else.
The exam turned out to be devilishly difficult despite all her preparation.

Synset Examples

she was madly in love
deadly dull
deadly earnest
deucedly clever
insanely jealous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a degree exceeding normal limits
  • Synonym: madly - extremely or excessively

Etymology

From 'devilish' + '-ly', with reference to the extreme or mischievous nature associated with the devil.

3. ADV

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈdɛvɪlɪʃli / (BrE)
In a wicked, mischievous, or extremely clever manner, as if like a devil.
in a wicked or mischievous manner

Examples

She smiled devilishly as she revealed her clever plan.
He laughed devilishly when the prank succeeded beyond his expectations.
The chef created a devilishly rich chocolate cake for dessert.

Synset Examples

his writing could be diabolically satiric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolically - in a devilish or fiendish manner

Etymology

From 'devilish' + '-ly', formed from Middle English 'develisch' (devil-like).
