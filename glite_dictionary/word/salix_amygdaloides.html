Salix amygdaloides

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.lɪks əˌmɪɡ.dəˈlɔɪ.diːz / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.lɪks amɪɡˌdalɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A species of willow native to western North America, with leaves resembling those of peach or almond trees.
a willow species with almond-like leaves

Examples

The Salix amygdaloides is commonly found along riverbanks in the western United States.
Leaves of the Salix amygdaloides resemble those of almond trees, giving the species its name.
Botanists often study Salix amygdaloides for its adaptability to varied environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - any tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: Salix (willow) and amygdaloides (almond-like), referring to leaf shape.
