to underperform

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚpɚˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌʌndəpəˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to perform less well or with less success than expected or required
perform less well than expected

Examples

Several students underperformed on the final exam despite extensive preparation.
The company’s shares underperformed the broader market this quarter.
If the new system continues to underperform, we will have to consider replacing it.

Synset Examples

John consistently underachieves, although he is very able
My stocks underperformed last year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to outperform - to perform better than others or better than expected

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (not enough) and 'perform' (to carry out an action).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02568500-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərpərˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌʌndəpəˈfɔːm / (BrE)
(of a play, musical work, or similar) to be performed less frequently than might be expected or deserved.
to be performed too rarely

Examples

Many of Shakespeare's comedies are underperformed compared to his tragedies.
This modern opera has been underperformed despite its critical acclaim.
Some classical pieces remain underperformed in concert halls around the world.

Synset Examples

Her plays are underperformed, although they are very good

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'under-' to 'perform', meaning 'less than' or 'not enough'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01719216-v
Synset POS: v
