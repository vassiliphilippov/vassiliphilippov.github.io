angioscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈændʒiˌoʊskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈændʒɪəskəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument, similar to a microscope, used for viewing the inside of blood vessels, especially capillaries.
instrument for viewing blood vessels
medical

Examples

The surgeon used an angioscope to examine the patient's capillaries during the procedure.
Recent advances in angioscope technology have made vascular imaging more precise.
An angioscope provides direct visualization of blood vessels, aiding doctors in diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for examining the interior of a bodily organ or cavity

Etymology

From Greek 'angeion' (vessel) + '-scope' (instrument for viewing).
