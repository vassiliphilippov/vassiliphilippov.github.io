to air-slake

1. VERB

/ ˈɛrˌsleɪk / (AmE), / ˈeəˌsleɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially lime) to alter or decompose by exposure to air, often resulting in conversion partly to a carbonate.
to decompose by exposure to air (esp. for lime)

Examples

If you air-slake quicklime, it turns into a powdery substance.
The process to air-slake lime must be carefully controlled to avoid excessive reaction.
Chemists often air-slake samples before further testing them.

Synset Examples

air-slake lime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slake - to satisfy or to react, especially by combining with water or air

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'slake', where 'slake' means to become less active or to undergo a chemical change, especially by reaction with water or air.
