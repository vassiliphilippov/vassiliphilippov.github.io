Corozo

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈɾoʊ.zoʊ / (AmE), / kɒˈɾəʊ.zəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American palm trees belonging to the monocotyledonous family Arecaceae, known for their hard seeds and fibrous fruits.
genus of tropical American palm trees

Examples

The Corozo palm is often cultivated for its decorative nuts.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Corozo genus in South America.
The hard fruits of the Corozo are sometimes used to make buttons and jewelry.

Etymology

From Spanish 'corozo', used in Latin binomials for palms of this genus.
