corozo

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊzoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊzəʊ / (BrE)
A tropical American palm tree of the genus Attalea, or its small, hard-shelled nut, often used for making buttons or as food.
a tropical palm or its nut

Examples

The artisan carved a beautiful button from corozo for the jacket.
In some countries, corozo nuts are eaten as a traditional snack.
The dense forests in Colombia are home to many corozo palms.

Etymology

From Spanish 'corozo', referring to the nut of certain palms.
