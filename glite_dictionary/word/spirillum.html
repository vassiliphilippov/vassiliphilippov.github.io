Spirillum

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrɪləm / (AmE), / spaɪˈrɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of large, spiral-shaped, rigid, gram-negative bacteria, including species such as Spirillum winogradskyi and Spirillum volutans
a genus of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Spirillum species are commonly found in stagnant freshwater environments.
Researchers observed the spiral-shaped cells of Spirillum under the microscope.
The presence of Spirillum can indicate certain environmental conditions in water samples.

Etymology

From Latin 'spirillum', diminutive of 'spira' (coil, spiral), itself from Greek 'speira'.
