Gaillardia pulchella

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈlɑrdiə pʊlˈkɛlə / (AmE), / ɡaɪˈlɑːdiə pʊlˈkɛlə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to central North America, known for its showy yellow flower heads with scarlet or purple centers, commonly called Indian blanket or firewheel.
a flowering plant with bright yellow and red blooms

Examples

The wildflowers included dozens of Gaillardia pulchella blooming along the roadside.
Gardeners value Gaillardia pulchella for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers in summer.
Gaillardia pulchella thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian blanket - common name for Gaillardia pulchella
  • Synonym: firewheel - another common name for Gaillardia pulchella

Etymology

From Latin, Gaillardia named after French botanist Gaillard de Charentonneau; pulchella means 'pretty' or 'beautiful' in Latin.
