Gnetophytina

1. NOUN

/ ˌgniː.t̬oʊ.faɪˈtaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌniː.təʊ.faɪˈtiː.nə / (BrE)
a subdivision of gymnospermous flowering plants, considered by some as a link between conifers and angiosperms; sometimes classified as a class (Gnetopsida) or a subdivision (Gnetophytina or Gnetophyta)
a group of gymnospermous plants related to conifers

Examples

Gnetophytina are considered an important evolutionary link between conifers and angiosperms.
Scientists are debating the exact classification of Gnetophytina within the plant kingdom.
Some botanists include Ephedra and Welwitschia in the subclass Gnetophytina.

Etymology

From genus name Gnetum + Ancient Greek phytón (plant), used in taxonomic nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɛt̬oʊfaɪˈtiːnə / (AmE), / ˌniːtəʊfaɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a subdivision of gymnospermous plants considered by some systems as linking conifers and angiosperms; includes plants in the group Gnetophyta or Gnetopsida
a plant subdivision linking conifers and angiosperms

Examples

The Gnetophytina are considered unique among gymnosperms for their vessel elements, which are typically found in angiosperms.
Some botanists classify Gnetophytina as a separate class, while others place them as a subdivision.
Recent genetic studies have provided new insights into the evolutionary relationships within Gnetophytina.

Etymology

From genus name Gnetum + Greek phuton (plant), with taxonomic suffix -ina.
