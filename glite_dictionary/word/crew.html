crew

1. NOUN

/ kruː / (AmE), / kruː / (BrE)
a team of people who row a racing boat, especially in a sport or competition
team of rowers in a racing shell

Examples

She joined the university crew in her first year and quickly excelled at rowing.
The crew practiced every morning on the river to prepare for the regatta.
At the finish line, the victorious crew celebrated their hard-earned win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowing team - a team competing in the sport of rowing

2. VERB

/ kruː / (AmE), / kruː / (BrE)
to serve as a crew member on a vehicle such as a ship, aircraft, or spacecraft.
serve as a crew member

Examples

Several volunteers agreed to crew the rescue boat during the storm.
She has crewed on research vessels in the Arctic for many years.
We spent the summer crewing on a luxury yacht in the Mediterranean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - to work as personnel or provide workers for a place

3. NOUN

/ kruː / (AmE), / kruː / (BrE)
a group of people who work together, especially for manual or practical tasks, such as on a ship, aircraft, or construction site
group of people working together

Examples

The construction crew finished building the bridge ahead of schedule.
A crew of electricians arrived to fix the power lines after the storm.
The film crew worked long hours to capture every scene perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: team - a group of people working together toward a common goal

Etymology

From Middle English 'crewe', from Old French 'creue' (increase, swelling), from 'croistre' (to grow), from Latin 'crescere'.

5. NOUN

/ kru / (AmE), / kru / (BrE)
the group of people who operate and work aboard a vehicle such as a ship, aircraft, or train
people operating a ship, aircraft, etc.

Examples

The airplane crew handled the emergency calmly and efficiently.
After dinner, the ship's crew gathered on deck for a meeting.
The train crew rotated shifts during the long overnight journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - the employees of an organization or business
  • Antonym: passenger - a person who travels in a vehicle but is not part of the crew

Etymology

From Middle French *creue* (increase, reinforcement), from Old French *creistre* (to grow).
