selfish

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛlfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfɪʃ / (BrE)
thinking only of yourself and not caring about the needs or feelings of other people
concerned only with oneself

Examples

It was selfish of him to eat all the cake without offering any to his friends.
She admitted that her decision had been selfish, but she insisted it was necessary.
Children can sometimes act in selfish ways without realizing the impact on others.

Synset Examples

Selfish men were … trying to make capital for themselves out of the sacred cause of civil rights
The merely egoistic satisfactions of fame are easily nullified
However, because of their strong-minded nature, they are often perceived as difficult and egoistical individuals.
I will never forgive her for her egocentric and shameful conduct towards Gilliatt
What I can't believe is that people are so stupid and self-centered that they think they HAVE to comment on things they don't like.
We're asked to see Rachel as this spoilt, self-centred woman but the rest of them are just as bad, if not worse.
As the crying baby, we express purely the self-serving thoughts that arise in our minds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unselfish - willing to put others' needs ahead of one's own; not selfish
  • Synonym: egotistical - excessively self-centered or conceited
  • Synonym: self-centered - preoccupied with oneself and one's own affairs

Etymology

From self + -ish, first recorded in English in the 17th century.
