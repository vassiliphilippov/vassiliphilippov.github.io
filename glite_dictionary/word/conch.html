conch

1. NOUN

/ kɑntʃ / (AmE), / kɒntʃ / (BrE)
A tropical marine mollusk with a brightly colored spiral shell and large outer lip, especially of the genus Strombus.
tropical marine mollusk with spiral shell

Examples

She picked up a beautiful conch while walking along the beach in the Bahamas.
Fishermen dive for conch to sell at the local seafood market.
The spiral shape of a conch shell makes it a popular souvenir for tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelk - a marine snail with a spiral shell, related to the conch

Etymology

From Greek 'konkhe' (shell) via Latin 'concha'.
