Omphalotus illudens

1. NOUN

/ ɑmˈfælətəs ɪˈludənz / (AmE), / ɒmˈfælətəs ɪˈluːdənz / (BrE)
A large poisonous mushroom with orange caps, narrow clustered stalks, and luminescent gills, commonly known as the Jack-o'-lantern mushroom.
poisonous orange mushroom with glowing gills

Examples

The Omphalotus illudens is often mistaken for edible chanterelles due to its bright orange color.
At night, the gills of the Omphalotus illudens can appear to glow in the dark woods.
Scientists warn that Omphalotus illudens is highly toxic and should never be consumed.

Etymology

From Greek 'omphalos' (navel) and Latin 'illudens' (mocking, deceiving).
