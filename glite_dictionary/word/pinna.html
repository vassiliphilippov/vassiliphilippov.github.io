pinna

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnə / (AmE), / ˈpɪnə / (BrE)
A primary segment or leaflet of a compound, typically pinnately divided leaf.
segment of a compound leaf
medical

Examples

Each pinna of the fern leaf is finely serrated along the edge.
The botanist examined the shape of each pinna to identify the species.
Some ferns have more than one pair of pinnae branching from the main stem.

Etymology

From Latin 'pinna' meaning feather or wing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnə / (AmE), / ˈpɪnə / (BrE)
the external, visible, cartilaginous part of the ear, projecting from the side of the head
outer visible part of the ear
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the child's pinna for any signs of injury.
A piercing in the pinna can sometimes lead to infection if not cared for properly.
The pinna helps direct sound waves into the ear canal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auricle - alternative anatomical term for the outer ear

Etymology

From Latin 'pinna' (feather, wing, fin, or projecting part).
