Urocyon

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈsaɪən / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈsaɪən / (BrE)
A genus of small, agile foxes native to North and Central America, including the common grey fox.
genus of grey foxes

Examples

The genus Urocyon includes the familiar gray fox found across much of North America.
Researchers studied the distribution and habitat preferences of Urocyon populations in Mexico.
Unlike other canids, Urocyon species are adept at climbing trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small to medium-sized, omnivorous mammal of the family Canidae

Etymology

From Greek oura (tail) + kyon (dog), referring to the tailed dog.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈsaɪən / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈsaɪən / (BrE)
A genus of small wild canids, including the grey fox and its relatives, native to the Americas.
genus of grey foxes

Examples

The Urocyon genus includes both the common grey fox and the island fox.
Scientists studied the genetic differences within Urocyon species to learn about their evolution.
Urocyon populations are distributed widely across North and Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey fox - a small wild fox found in North and Central America, member of genus Urocyon

Etymology

From Greek oura (tail) + kyon (dog), referring to the bushy tail of these foxes.
