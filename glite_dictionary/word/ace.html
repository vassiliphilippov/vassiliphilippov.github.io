ACE

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ si ˈi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ si ˈi / (BrE)
a proteolytic enzyme that converts angiotensin I into angiotensin II, playing a crucial role in blood pressure regulation
enzyme controlling blood pressure
medical

Examples

Doctors may prescribe ACE inhibitors to treat high blood pressure.
The level of ACE in the blood can be important for diagnosing certain conditions.
Research on ACE has led to new treatments for heart failure.

Etymology

Acronym from angiotensin-converting enzyme.
