Polybotria cervina

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈbɑtriə sərˈvaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɒtrɪə səˈviːnə / (BrE)
A species of tropical American terrestrial fern with leathery, lance-shaped fronds; sometimes classified in the genus Polybotrya.
a tropical American species of fern

Examples

The dense fronds of Polybotria cervina provide shelter for insects in the tropical rainforest.
Botanists discovered a new colony of Polybotria cervina growing along the riverbank.
Gardeners appreciate Polybotria cervina for its attractive leathery leaves in shaded landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Polybotria' (sometimes 'Polybotrya'), species epithet 'cervina', meaning 'deer-like'.
