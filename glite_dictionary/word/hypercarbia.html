hypercarbia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈkɑːrbiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkɑːbɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by an abnormally high level of carbon dioxide in the blood
high carbon dioxide level in blood (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hypercarbia after analysis revealed elevated blood carbon dioxide levels.
Prolonged hypercarbia can lead to respiratory acidosis and other serious complications if not treated.
Ventilation was adjusted in response to rising hypercarbia during the surgical procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypercapnia - abnormally high carbon dioxide in the blood
  • Antonym: hypocarbia - abnormally low carbon dioxide in the blood

Etymology

From hyper- (excessive) + carbia (relating to carbon dioxide, from Latin 'carbo' meaning coal or carbon).
