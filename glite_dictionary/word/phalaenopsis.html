Phalaenopsis

1. NOUN

/ fæliˈnɑpsɪs / (AmE), / fælɪˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of ornamental epiphytic orchids native to Asia and Australia, often grown for their striking, long-lasting flowers
a genus of Asian and Australian orchids

Examples

The Phalaenopsis orchid is often called the 'moth orchid' because of its wing-shaped flowers.
Many gardeners prefer Phalaenopsis for their homes due to their elegant appearance and ease of care.
Several new hybrids of Phalaenopsis have been introduced to the market this year.

Etymology

From Greek 'phalaina' (moth) and '-opsis' (appearance), referring to the moth-like shape of the flowers.
