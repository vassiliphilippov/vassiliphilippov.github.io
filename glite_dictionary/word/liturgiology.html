liturgiology

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌtɝːdʒiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / lɪˌtɜːdʒiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scholarly study of liturgies, especially their history, forms, and functions
study of liturgies

Examples

She pursued a doctorate in liturgiology, focusing on the evolution of Christian worship practices.
His book on liturgiology examines the differences between Eastern and Western rites.
Few universities offer courses in liturgiology, making it a niche academic field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liturgy - a form or formulary according to which public religious worship is conducted

Etymology

From liturgy (from Greek leitourgia, public service) + -logy (study of).
