jailbreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlˌbreɪk / (BrE)
an escape from jail, especially one involving force or careful planning
an escape from jail

Examples

The guards were caught off guard by the daring jailbreak that took place at midnight.
Authorities are still investigating how the jailbreak was orchestrated by the inmates.
A movie was made about the most famous jailbreak in the state's history.

Synset Examples

the breakout was carefully planned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakout - an escape from prison

Etymology

From 'jail' + 'break', referring to an act of breaking out of jail.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00060809-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlˌbreɪk / (BrE)
the process of removing security restrictions imposed by the manufacturer on an electronic device, typically to allow the installation of unauthorized software
removal of device security restrictions

Examples

Many users attempt to jailbreak their phones to access additional features.
Performing a jailbreak may void your device's warranty.
After the jailbreak, he installed apps that were previously unavailable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rooting - gaining root access to a device, especially Android phones

Etymology

Modelled on the literal sense 'breaking out of jail'; adapted to tech slang in the early 2000s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90019261-n
Synset POS: n
