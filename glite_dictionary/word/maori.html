Maori

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊri / (AmE), / ˈmaːri / (BrE)
the Polynesian language spoken by the indigenous Maori people of New Zealand
Polynesian language of New Zealand

Examples

She is learning to speak Maori at school as part of her studies.
Several official documents in New Zealand are translated into Maori.
The Maori language has seen a revival in recent decades.

Etymology

From Maori Māori, meaning 'ordinary' or 'native'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊri / (AmE), / ˈmaʊəri / (BrE)
A member of the indigenous Polynesian people of New Zealand, known for their unique language and culture.
indigenous people of New Zealand

Examples

Many Maori traditions are still practiced throughout New Zealand today.
The Maori are known for their rich mythology and distinctive art forms.
A group of Maori performed a haka to welcome the visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polynesian - A member of any of the indigenous peoples of Polynesia
  • Antonym: Pākehā - A non-Maori New Zealander, usually of European descent

Etymology

From Maori *Māori*, meaning 'normal' or 'ordinary' (people), used by the people to distinguish themselves from others.
