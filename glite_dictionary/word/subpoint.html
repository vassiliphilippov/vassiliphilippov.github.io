subpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbpɔɪnt / (BrE)
A point or argument that is part of a larger argument or main point.
minor point within a larger argument

Examples

In her presentation, she explained each subpoint supporting the main argument.
Please list several subpoints under every major heading in your outline.
The debate judge asked for clarification on a subpoint raised during the discussion.

Synset Examples

The subpoint is that slogans aren't going to help — as I have written here, and in all three of my books for parents, education from schools, faith based communities, and parents will.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: point - an idea, argument, or item in a discussion or outline

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (meaning 'under' or 'secondary') + 'point'.
