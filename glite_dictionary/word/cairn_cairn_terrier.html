cairn (Cairn Terrier)

1. NOUN

/ kɛrn / (AmE), / kɛːn / (BrE)
a small, rough-haired breed of terrier originating from Scotland, known for its alert and lively nature
Scottish small rough-haired terrier breed

Examples

The cairn is known for its spirited personality and rough coat.
Many families choose a cairn as a pet due to its friendly temperament.
At the dog show, the cairn impressed the judges with its agility and charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of small, energetic dog breed originally for hunting

Etymology

Named after cairns (piles of stones), as the breed was used to hunt among them in the Scottish Highlands.
