trauma

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːmə, ˈtraʊmə / (AmE), / ˈtrɔːmə, ˈtraʊmə / (BrE)
A deeply distressing or disturbing experience that causes emotional or psychological injury and may have lasting effects.
emotional or psychological injury

Examples

The accident left him with lasting trauma that required years of therapy.
She spoke about the trauma of losing her home in the fire.
Childhood trauma can affect a person's mental health for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting experience or disturbance

Etymology

From Greek *trauma* (wound).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɔmə, ˈtraʊmə / (AmE), / ˈtrɔːmə, ˈtraʊmə / (BrE)
A serious physical injury or wound caused by an external force like violence, accident, or fracture.
serious bodily injury
medical

Examples

The victim suffered severe trauma to his legs after the car crash.
Medical staff are trained to respond quickly to cases of blunt force trauma.
Emergency surgery was required to treat internal trauma caused by the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injury - harm or damage to the body
  • Synonym: wound - an injury to body tissue, typically one in which the skin is cut or broken

Etymology

From Greek trauma (wound).
