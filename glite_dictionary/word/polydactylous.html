polydactylous

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈdæktələs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
(of a person or other vertebrate) having more than the usual number of fingers or toes.
having extra fingers or toes
medical

Examples

The newborn kitten was found to be polydactylous, possessing extra toes on its front paws.
Some genetic conditions in humans can result in a polydactylous appearance of hands or feet.
Early studies described fossiled reptiles that were markedly polydactylous compared to modern species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polydactyly - the condition of having more than the usual number of fingers or toes
  • Antonym: syndactylous - (of fingers or toes) joined together

Etymology

From Greek polydaktulos, from poly- (many) + daktylos (finger, toe).
