Moirae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪˌraɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪriː / (BrE)
the three Greek goddesses who controlled fate and destiny, also known as the Fates
the three Greek goddesses of fate

Examples

According to myth, the Moirae determined the length of every person's life.
Artists often depicted the Moirae as three women spinning the thread of fate.
The ancient Greeks believed that even Zeus was subject to the will of the Moirae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fates - the group of goddesses who control human destiny in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Parcae - the three Roman goddesses of fate
  • Synonym: Norns - the Norse mythological beings who rule destiny

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μοῖραι (Moîrai), meaning 'the apportioners.'
