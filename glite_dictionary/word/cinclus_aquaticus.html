Cinclus aquaticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.kləs əˈkwæt.ɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.klʊs əˈkwæt.ɪ.kəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European dipper, a bird species known for its aquatic habits and found near mountain streams in Europe.
scientific name for the European dipper bird

Examples

The Cinclus aquaticus is often seen diving into fast-flowing streams in the Alps.
Researchers observed a pair of Cinclus aquaticus building their nest under a stone bridge.
Unlike most songbirds, the Cinclus aquaticus is adapted to hunt underwater insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dipper - common name for birds of the family Cinclidae
  • Synonym: white-throated dipper - common name for Cinclus aquaticus

Etymology

From Latin: 'cinclus' meaning 'dipper (bird)' and 'aquaticus' meaning 'aquatic' or 'of water'.
