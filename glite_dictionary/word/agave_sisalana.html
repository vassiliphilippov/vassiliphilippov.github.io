Agave sisalana

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːveɪ sɪˈsɑːlənə / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːvi sɪˈsælənə / (BrE)
A Mexican or West Indian plant with large, fleshy leaves that yield a stiff fiber used for making rope and other products.
plant yielding stiff fiber for rope

Examples

Agave sisalana is widely cultivated in tropical regions for its valuable sisal fiber.
The stiff leaves of Agave sisalana are processed to produce rope and twine.
Farmers in Mexico and Africa have large plantations of Agave sisalana to support the fiber industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sisal - strong fiber obtained from Agave sisalana

Etymology

From genus name Agave (Greek 'agavos', noble), plus species name 'sisalana', after Sisal, a port in Yucatán, Mexico, from which the fiber was exported.
