reincarnate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈɪnkɑːrneɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪnkɑːneɪt / (BrE)
to cause something, especially an idea, product, or form, to appear or exist again in a new or different form
cause to appear in a new form

Examples

The designer managed to reincarnate the brand with a modern look.
Classic songs are often reincarnated as fresh covers by new artists.
The company hopes to reincarnate its classic toy line for today's children.

Synset Examples

the old product was reincarnated to appeal to a younger market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring something back into use or popularity

Etymology

From Latin 'reincarnare' (to make flesh again): 're-' (again) + 'carn-' (flesh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00167460-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈɪnkɑrˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪnkɑːˌneɪt / (BrE)
(of a soul or spirit) to be born in another body after death.
to be born again in another body

Examples

Many followers of Eastern religions believe that people can reincarnate after death.
According to some legends, the wise sage was destined to reincarnate as a great leader.
She hoped that her beloved pet would reincarnate and find its way back to her.

Synset Examples

Hindus believe that we transmigrate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transmigrate - to pass into another body after death
  • Synonym: reincarnation - the rebirth of a soul in a new body

Etymology

From Latin 'reincarnare', from 're-' (again) + 'incarnare' (to make flesh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02704568-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ˌriˈɪnkɑrˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪŋ.kɑː.neɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or soul) having been born again in a new body after death.
born again in a new body

Examples

Many people believe the Dalai Lama is a reincarnate spiritual leader continuing his legacy across generations.
In the novel, the main character discovers she is a reincarnate soul with memories of her past life.
They searched for the reincarnate child who would one day lead their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reborn - having been born again, especially spiritually or in a new body

Etymology

From re- (again) + incarnate (from Latin incarnatus, 'made flesh').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00633568-s
Synset POS: s
