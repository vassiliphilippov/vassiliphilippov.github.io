joke

1. NOUN

/ dʒoʊk / (AmE), / dʒəʊk / (BrE)
a person or thing regarded as foolish, insignificant, or not to be taken seriously
something not to be taken seriously

Examples

Many considered his proposal for the new law a joke rather than a viable plan.
After his disastrous performance, the whole team was treated like a joke in the league.
The so-called security measures are a joke and won't stop anyone determined.

Synset Examples

I regarded his campaign for mayor as a joke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - quality of being serious or solemn

Etymology

From Latin iocus (jest, joke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05180970-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ doʊk / (AmE), / dəʊk / (BrE)
to say things intended to make people laugh; to speak humorously or playfully
say something to make others laugh

Examples

He likes to joke with his friends to lighten the mood.
When the meeting grew tense, she tried to joke but nobody laughed.
Please tell me you’re only joking about moving abroad!

Synset Examples

He often jokes even when he appears serious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jest - to speak or act in a joking or playful way; to make fun
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00855315-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dʒoʊk / (AmE), / dʒəʊk / (BrE)
to act or speak in a way intended to amuse or tease someone
act or speak to amuse or tease

Examples

He loves to joke with his colleagues to lighten the mood.
She was clearly joking when she said she'd move to the moon.
Please don't take it seriously—I was only joking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jest - to say or do something for amusement or as a joke
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful way
  • Antonym: mock - to make fun of someone, often unkindly

Etymology

From Latin 'jocus' (jest, joke) via Old French 'juker' and English 'joke'.

Source: WordNet

a humorous story or remark told to make people laugh
something said or done to make people laugh

Examples

He can't tell when someone is making a joke or being serious.
The comedian's jokes were the highlight of the evening.

5. NOUN

/ doʊk / (AmE), / dʒəʊk / (BrE)
an action or behavior that is ludicrous or grotesque, performed for fun and amusement
ludicrous act for amusement

Examples

The outrageous joke left everyone in the room shocked but laughing.
Her idea of a joke was to swap all the office chairs around overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a mischievous act or trick played for fun

Etymology

From Latin 'jocus' (jest, fun, or joke) via Old French 'joc'.
