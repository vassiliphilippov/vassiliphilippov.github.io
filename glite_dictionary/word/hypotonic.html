hypotonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪ.pəˈtɑn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈtɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of living tissue or muscle) having less than normal tone or tension.
lacking normal muscle or tissue tension
medical

Examples

The patient's muscles were hypotonic after the surgery, making movement difficult.
Babies with Down syndrome may have hypotonic muscle tone at birth.
During the examination, the doctor noted that the limbs felt unusually hypotonic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertonic - having abnormally high muscle or tissue tension
  • Synonym: flaccid - soft and hanging loosely or limply, especially of tissue

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + tonos (tension) + -ic.

2. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊˈtɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəˈtɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a solution) having a lower osmotic pressure than another solution, usually referring to lower solute concentration compared to another fluid.
having lower osmotic pressure
medical

Examples

When placed in a hypotonic solution, red blood cells can swell and burst due to water intake.
A hypotonic fluid is often used to treat dehydration when cells need to absorb more water.
The laboratory compared the effects of hypotonic and hypertonic solutions on plant cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertonic - (of a solution) having a higher osmotic pressure than another solution
  • Antonym: isotonic - (of a solution) having the same osmotic pressure as another solution

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'tonos' (tension).
