technocracy

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɑkrəsi / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A system of government in which decision-makers are selected on the basis of their expertise in technical and scientific fields.
government by technical experts

Examples

Some believe that a technocracy would be more efficient than a traditional political system.
Critics argue that a technocracy may ignore the social needs of the population.
The proposal to create a technocracy sparked intense debate among lawmakers.

Synset Examples

technocracy was described as that society in which those who govern justify themselves by appeal to technical experts who justify themselves by appeal to scientific forms of knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meritocracy - government by people selected on the basis of ability
  • Antonym: bureaucracy - government by departments of state officials

Etymology

From Greek 'tekhnē' (skill, art, craft) + 'kratos' (power, rule).
