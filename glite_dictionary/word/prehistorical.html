prehistorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːhɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːhɪsˈtɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
Belonging to or existing in the time before written or recorded history.
from the time before recorded history

Examples

The fossils were discovered in a prehistorical cave system deep underground.
Archaeologists study prehistorical artifacts to understand early human life.
She was fascinated by the animals that roamed the earth during prehistorical times.

Synset Examples

prehistoric settlements
prehistoric peoples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prehistoric - belonging to or existing in times before recorded history

Etymology

From pre- (before) + historical, patterned after 'prehistoric'.
