scouter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈskaʊtə / (BrE)
an adult member or leader of the Boy Scouts movement who helps to organize and supervise activities
adult member of the Boy Scouts movement

Examples

He became a scouter after his son joined the Boy Scouts.
The scouter organized an outdoor camping trip for the troop.
Many scouters volunteer their time to mentor young scouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scout - a member of the Boy Scouts movement, typically a child or adolescent

Etymology

Formed from 'Scout' with the agentive suffix '-er', denoting a person who participates in or is connected with scouting.
