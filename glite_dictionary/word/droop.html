to droop

1. VERB

/ druːp / (AmE), / druːp / (BrE)
To bend or hang down limply, often as a result of weakness, exhaustion, or lack of support.
hang or bend downward limply

Examples

The flowers began to droop after several days without water.
By the end of the long meeting, her eyelids started to droop with fatigue.
The old flag drooped sadly from the pole in the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilt - to become limp through heat, loss of water, or lack of energy
  • Synonym: sag - to sink, bend, or hang down under weight or pressure
  • Antonym: to perk up - to become more lively or cheerful

Etymology

From Middle English 'druppen' or 'drupen', likely of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse 'drúpa' (to hang the head).

3. VERB

/ druːp / (AmE), / druːp / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or a plant) to bend or hang down limply, often as a result of tiredness or lack of support.
bend or hang down limply

Examples

By the end of the day, his shoulders began to droop with exhaustion.
The sunflowers droop when they don't get enough water.
As the heat intensified, the dog's ears started to droop noticeably.

Synset Examples

The flowers wilted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilt - to become limp through heat, loss of water, or disease (plants)
  • Synonym: flop - to fall, hang, or move in a loose or ungainly way
  • Antonym: to perk up - to become more lively or upright after being droopy
