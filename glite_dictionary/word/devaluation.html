devaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an official reduction in the value of a country's currency relative to foreign currencies
official reduction of currency value

Examples

The government announced an unexpected devaluation of the national currency last night.
After the devaluation, imported goods became much more expensive for local consumers.
The country experienced inflation following the devaluation of its currency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revaluation - an official increase in the value of a country's currency
  • Synonym: depreciation - a decrease in the value of something over time (not always official)

Etymology

From de- (down) + valuation, patterned after 'devalue'.

2. NOUN

/ diˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌvæljuːˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of reducing the value or worth of something, especially the official lowering of a country's currency value relative to others
reduction in value or worth

Examples

The sudden devaluation of the currency caused panic in the financial markets.
Many feared a devaluation would undermine investor confidence.
After the announcement of the devaluation, imported goods became more expensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - a reduction in the value of an asset over time
  • Antonym: appreciation - an increase in value or worth

Etymology

From de- (down) + value + -ation; modelled after French dévaluation.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
