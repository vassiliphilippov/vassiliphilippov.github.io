battery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætəri / (AmE), / ˈbatəri / (BrE)
The act of deliberately making physical contact with another person in a violent or offensive way, especially as a criminal offense.
unlawful physical assault involving contact
legal

Examples

The defendant was found guilty of battery after striking the victim.
In criminal law, battery requires actual physical contact with another person.
She pressed charges for battery following the altercation at the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - a threat or attempt to harm another person physically

Etymology

From Old French *baterie*, from *batre* 'to beat', from Latin *battuere* 'to beat'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ər.i / (BrE)
(Baseball) The pair of pitcher and catcher considered as a unit or team.
baseball pitcher-catcher pair

Examples

The team's battery worked together flawlessly all season.
A strong battery is crucial for a winning baseball team.
Both the pitcher and the catcher are considered part of the battery.

Etymology

From military use denoting a unit of artillery, extended in 19th-century baseball slang.

6. NOUN

/ ˈbætəri / (AmE), / ˈbæ.tər.i / (BrE)
A device consisting of one or more electrochemical cells that produce electricity, used to power electrical devices.
electrical energy storage device

Examples

The remote control stopped working because the battery was dead.
She replaced the old battery in her phone with a new one.
Electric cars use a large battery pack to store energy for driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a single electrochemical unit producing electricity

Etymology

From French 'batterie' (action of battering or a set of artillery), adopted in the 18th century for electrical cells.
