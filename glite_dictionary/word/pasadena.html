Pasadena

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæsəˈdiːnə / (AmE), / ˌpæsəˈdiːnə / (BrE)
A city in southwestern California, located to the east of Los Angeles, known for the annual Rose Parade and as the home of the California Institute of Technology.
a city in southern California, USA

Examples

The Rose Parade in Pasadena attracts thousands of visitors every year.
She moved to Pasadena to study engineering at Caltech.
Many movies have been filmed on the tree-lined streets of Pasadena.

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'valley' or possibly named after Pasadena, Mississippi; adopted in the late 19th century.
