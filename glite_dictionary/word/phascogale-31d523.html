Phascogale

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæskəˈɡeɪli / (AmE), / ˌfæskəˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A genus of small marsupials known as pouched mice native to Australia, characterized by a bushy tail and insectivorous habits.
a genus of Australian pouched mice

Examples

The Phascogale is known for its energetic climbing and bushy tail.
Researchers identified a new species within the Phascogale genus in Western Australia.
Many conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitat of the Phascogale.

Etymology

From Greek phaskolos (pouch) + gale (weasel).
