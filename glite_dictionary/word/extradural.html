extradural

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strəˈdʊr.əl / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈdjʊə.rəl / (BrE)
Situated on or outside the dura mater (the outer membrane covering the brain and spinal cord).
outside the dura mater (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed an extradural procedure to stop the bleeding near the spinal cord.
Extradural hematomas require prompt diagnosis and treatment to prevent complications.
Bleeding in the extradural space can cause pressure on the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidural - on or outside the dura mater; relating to anesthesia administered outside the dura mater

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'dura mater' (the tough mother, membrane covering the brain and spinal cord).
