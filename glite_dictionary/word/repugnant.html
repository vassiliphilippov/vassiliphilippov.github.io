repugnant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpʌɡ.nənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌɡ.nənt / (BrE)
extremely distasteful, unacceptable, or offensive to the mind or moral sense.
offensive or morally unacceptable

Examples

Many people considered the practice of slavery to be repugnant and inhumane.
She found his unethical business dealings repugnant and refused to work with him.
The documentary exposed repugnant acts that shocked and angered the public.

Synset Examples

an abhorrent deed
the obscene massacre at Wounded Knee
morally repugnant customs
repulsive behavior
the most repulsive character in recent novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abhorrent - inspiring disgust and loathing; repugnant
  • Synonym: repulsive - arousing intense distaste or disgust

Etymology

From Latin repugnant-, present participle of repugnare 'to oppose'
