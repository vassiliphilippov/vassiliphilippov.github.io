superciliousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈsɪl.i.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsɪl.i.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of behaving or looking as though one thinks one is superior to others, typically with a patronizing attitude
arrogant or patronizing attitude

Examples

Her superciliousness made it difficult for her to connect with her colleagues.
There was an obvious superciliousness in his tone when he addressed the audience.
Critics accused the author of superciliousness due to her dismissive remarks about popular culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - offensive display of superiority or self-importance
  • Antonym: humility - modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin 'superciliosus' (haughty), from 'supercilium' (eyebrow, pride).
