Xenorhyncus asiaticus

1. NOUN

/ ziˌnɔɹɪŋˈkʊs æziˈætɪkəs / (AmE), / zɛnəˌrɪŋkəs æziˈatɪkəs / (BrE)
a large mostly white stork native to Australia and parts of Asia, commonly known as the Black-necked Stork.
Australian black-necked stork species

Examples

The Xenorhyncus asiaticus is often seen wading through wetlands in northern Australia.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Xenorhyncus asiaticus in Kakadu National Park.
Because of habitat loss, the population of Xenorhyncus asiaticus is closely monitored by conservationists.

Etymology

From Greek *xenos* (stranger) + *rhynchos* (beak), and Latin *asiaticus* (Asian), referring to the bird's appearance and distribution.
