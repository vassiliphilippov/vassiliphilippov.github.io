phrenology

1. NOUN

/ frəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / frɪˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the shape and bumps of the skull, once believed to reveal a person's character and mental abilities, now regarded as unscientific
study of skull shape to assess character (obsolete)

Examples

In the nineteenth century, phrenology was widely accepted despite lacking scientific evidence.
Many museums now display devices used in phrenology as historical curiosities.
She read a book about phrenology and its influence on Victorian society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craniology - the scientific study of skulls

Etymology

From Greek 'phren' (mind) + 'logos' (study).
