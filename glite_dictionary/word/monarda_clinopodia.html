Monarda clinopodia

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɑːrdə klɪnəˈpoʊdiə / (AmE), / məˈnɑːdə klɪnəˈpəʊdɪə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to North America, with aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-pink flowers, commonly found from New York to Illinois and in the mountains of Alaska.
a North American aromatic perennial herb

Examples

The woodland garden featured a patch of Monarda clinopodia blooming beside the path.
Botanists collected samples of Monarda clinopodia for their study on native aromatic plants.
I recognized Monarda clinopodia from its distinct yellowish-pink floral clusters and fragrant leaves.

Etymology

From the genus name Monarda (after Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes) and species clinopodia (from Greek, meaning 'bedfoot', referring to the shape of the leaves).
