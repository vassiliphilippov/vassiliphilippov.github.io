milliliter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌlitər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌliːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter, commonly used to measure small amounts of liquid.
one thousandth of a liter (volume unit)

Examples

The doctor prescribed 5 milliliters of medicine to be taken daily.
Please add 200 milliliters of water to the mixture.
Each serving contains just 50 milliliters of juice.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: liter - metric unit of capacity, equal to 1,000 milliliters

Etymology

From French 'millilitre', from Latin 'mille' (thousand) + 'litre' (liter).
