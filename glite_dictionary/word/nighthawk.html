nighthawk

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt.hɑk / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.hɔːk / (BrE)
A mainly nocturnal North American bird of the nightjar family that feeds on insects in flight.
nocturnal North American bird (nightjar family)

Examples

A nighthawk swooped down to catch insects at dusk.
We could hear the distinctive call of a nighthawk as the sun set.
The nighthawks returned to the open fields every summer evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - nocturnal bird with a similar lifestyle and taxonomy

Etymology

Formed from 'night' and 'hawk', referring to its nocturnal habits and swift flight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌhɔk / (AmE), / ˈnaɪthɔːk / (BrE)
a person who prefers to be active or awake late at night
person active late at night

Examples

My roommate is a total nighthawk and rarely goes to bed before 2 a.m.
As a devoted nighthawk, he finds early morning meetings unbearable.
The city's cafes are filled with nighthawks working or socializing long after midnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night owl - a person who habitually stays up late at night

Etymology

Figurative use of 'nighthawk', originally the name of a nocturnal bird.
