ennobling

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnoʊblɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊblɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something morally or socially better; inspiring or elevating in character or purpose.
inspiring moral or social elevation

Examples

Her selfless acts had an ennobling influence on everyone around her.
Reading about historic heroes can have an ennobling effect on young minds.
He found the work challenging but deeply ennobling in purpose.

Synset Examples

an exalting eulogy
ennobling thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exalting - raising in rank, power, or character; elevating
  • Antonym: degrading - causing a loss of respect or standing; lowering in dignity

Etymology

From the verb 'ennoble,' from Old French 'ennoblir', from Latin 'nobilis' (noble).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈnoʊblɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈnəʊblɪŋ / (BrE)
giving or conferring a sense of dignity, honor, or moral elevation; making someone or something nobler in character or quality
giving dignity or moral elevation

Examples

The ennobling influence of art can inspire people to achieve greatness.
She found the experience of helping others deeply ennobling.
His speech had an ennobling effect on everyone who heard it.

Synset Examples

the dignifying effect of his presence
the ennobling influence of cultural surroundings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uplifting - making someone feel better or more hopeful
  • Antonym: degrading - causing a loss of respect or dignity

Etymology

From the verb "ennoble" (from Old French ennoblir, from Latin nobilis meaning noble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01592833-s
Synset POS: s
