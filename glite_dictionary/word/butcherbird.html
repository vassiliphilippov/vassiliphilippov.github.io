butcherbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃɚˌbɜrd / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəbɜːd / (BrE)
a shrike, a type of predatory songbird known for impaling its prey on thorns or barbed wire
a shrike that impales its prey

Examples

The butcherbird is notorious for catching insects and impaling them on sharp twigs.
We spotted a butcherbird perched on a fence post with a grasshopper in its beak.
Butcherbirds use their hooked bills to hunt small animals and store them for later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a type of predatory songbird known for impaling prey

Etymology

Named for its practice of hanging prey, similar to a butcher's meat hooks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃɚˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəbɜːd / (BrE)
A large carnivorous Australian songbird known for its habit of impaling prey on thorns, similar to a shrike.
Australian carnivorous bird; impales prey

Examples

The butcherbird is known for its eerie song and its habit of impaling insects on thorns.
We saw a butcherbird catch a lizard and secure it on a sharp branch.
Several butcherbirds have built their nests in the eucalyptus trees along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a passerine bird known for impaling prey on thorns

Etymology

Named for its habit, from 'butcher' (one who slaughters animals) + 'bird'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01585850-n
Synset POS: n
