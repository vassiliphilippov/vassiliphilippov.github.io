Scleranthus

1. NOUN

/ sklɪˈrænθəs / (AmE), / sklɪˈrænθəs / (BrE)
a small genus of low-growing, often weedy annual plants native to the Old World, commonly known as knawel
genus of weedy prostrate annuals (knawel)

Examples

The botanist discovered a patch of Scleranthus growing along the old stone wall.
Gardeners often struggle to remove Scleranthus due to its resilient, weedy nature.
Scleranthus species are sometimes used in rock gardens for their mat-forming habit.

Etymology

From Greek *sklērós* (hard) and *ánthos* (flower), referring to the plant's tough flowers.

2. NOUN

/ skləˈrænθəs / (AmE), / sklɛˈranθəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, weedy, prostrate annual plants, commonly known as knawel, native to the Old World.
a genus of small annual plants (knawel)

Examples

The sandy field was covered in patches of Scleranthus during early summer.
Botanists collected specimens of Scleranthus for their research on weedy annuals.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Scleranthus for common grass due to its fine leaves.

Etymology

From Greek *sklēros* (hard) + *anthos* (flower).
