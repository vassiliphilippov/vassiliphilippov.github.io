innumerous

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnuːmɚəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːmərəs / (BrE)
too many to be counted; countless; innumerable
too many to be counted
archaic

Examples

The innumerous stars filled the night sky, creating a dazzling spectacle.
She spoke of innumerous challenges faced throughout her long career.
He spent innumerous hours perfecting his craft, never losing patience.

Synset Examples

countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innumerable - too many to be counted; countless
  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: myriad - countless or extremely great in number

Etymology

From Latin innumerosus (countless), from in- (not) + numerus (number).
