unveiling

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈveɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of formally introducing or presenting something new to the public
formal introduction or presentation of something new

Examples

The company held an unveiling of its latest smartphone to attract media attention.
Everyone applauded during the unveiling of the artist's new sculpture in the park.
The unveiling of the government's new policy was broadcast live on television.

Synset Examples

they looked forward to the debut of their new product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - the action of starting or introducing something new
  • Synonym: debut - a person's first appearance or the first presentation of something
  • Antonym: concealment - the action of hiding or keeping something secret

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'veil', meaning 'to cover', implying removal of a covering.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈveɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a ceremony or occasion at which something, especially a work of art or a new product, is shown or introduced to the public for the first time
ceremony or event to reveal something for the first time

Examples

The artist was nervous before the unveiling of her latest sculpture.
Hundreds gathered at the park for the unveiling of the commemorative plaque.
The company held an online event for the unveiling of its newest smartphone.

Synset Examples

he attended the unveiling of the statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make known or make visible, especially something hidden

Etymology

From un- (not) + veiling (from veil), meaning to remove a veil from.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06904389-n
Synset POS: n
