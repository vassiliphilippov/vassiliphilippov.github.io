subaltern

1. ADJ

/ səˈbɔl.tɚn / (AmE), / sʌbˈɔːl.tən / (BrE)
(of a person or group) lower in rank or status; subordinate.
of lower rank or status; subordinate

Examples

In many colonial societies, subaltern groups were deprived of formal power and authority.
The committee considered him a subaltern officer, unqualified for command.
Movements led by subaltern voices are now gaining international attention.

Synset Examples

a lowly corporal
petty officialdom

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior - higher in rank or status; having greater authority
  • Synonym: subordinate - lower in rank or position; secondary

Etymology

From Latin 'subalternus', from 'sub-' (below) + 'alternus' (alternate, following).

2. NOUN

/ səˈbɔl.tɚn / (AmE), / sʌbˈɑːl.tən / (BrE)
a British army officer ranking below captain, especially in the infantry and cavalry
junior British army officer below captain
british

Examples

He served as a subaltern in the British Army during the First World War.
The young subaltern followed his captain's orders with precision and discipline.
Many famous generals began their careers as humble subalterns in the military.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lieutenant - an army officer immediately junior to a captain

Etymology

From Latin 'subalternus' (subordinate), from 'sub-' (under) + 'alternus' (every other).
