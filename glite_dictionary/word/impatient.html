impatient

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
easily annoyed or provoked by delay, opposition, or having to wait; having little patience.
easily annoyed by waiting or delay

Examples

After waiting in line for an hour, she became increasingly impatient with the slow service.
Children can be especially impatient when they have to sit still for too long.
He grew impatient of the constant interruptions during his presentation.

Synset Examples

impatient with the slower students
impatient of criticism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patient - able to wait calmly for something or endure difficulties without complaint
  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered

Etymology

From Latin 'impatientem', from 'in-' (not) + 'patientem' (patient).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') feeling or showing strong eagerness to do something; unable to wait to begin.
full of eagerness to start

Examples

The children grew impatient to open their presents as the morning progressed.
She was impatient to share her good news with her friends.
Everyone was impatient to see the results of the competition.

Synset Examples

impatient to begin
raring to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something
  • Antonym: reluctant - unwilling and hesitant; not eager

Etymology

From Latin 'impatientem' (not enduring, intolerant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00815105-s
Synset POS: s
