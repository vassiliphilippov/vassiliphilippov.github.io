sunchoke

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌtʃoʊk / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌtʃəʊk / (BrE)
the edible tuber of a variety of sunflower, consumed raw or cooked
edible sunflower tuber

Examples

He roasted the sunchoke slices with olive oil and herbs for a crisp side dish.
The chef added thinly shaved sunchoke to the salad for extra crunch.
Farmers markets often offer fresh sunchokes in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem artichoke - another name for the sunchoke tuber

Etymology

Blend of 'sunflower' and 'artichoke'.
