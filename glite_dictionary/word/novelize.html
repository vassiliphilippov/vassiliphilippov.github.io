to novelize

1. VERB

/ ˈnɑːvɪˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɒvɪˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt (an event, story, film, etc.) into the form or style of a novel
adapt into a novel

Examples

The filmmaker decided to novelize his hit movie for a different audience.
She plans to novelize the popular TV series, adding new subplots and character development.
After years of research, the historian finally novelized the dramatic events for a broader readership.

Synset Examples

The author novelized the historical event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to change something to suit a different purpose

Etymology

From 'novel' + '-ize', patterned after similar English formations.
