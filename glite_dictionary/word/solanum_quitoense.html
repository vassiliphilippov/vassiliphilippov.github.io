Solanum quitoense

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlɑːnəm ˌkiːtoʊˈɛns.eɪ / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ˌkɪtəʊˈɛnsiː / (BrE)
A small perennial shrub native to the uplands of South America, cultivated for its edible, bright orange fruits that resemble tomatoes or oranges.
South American shrub with edible orange fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Solanum quitoense is prized for its refreshing, citrus-like flavor.
Farmers in Colombia cultivate Solanum quitoense for both local consumption and export.
Researchers are studying Solanum quitoense for its potential health benefits and unique taste.

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Solanum' (nightshade); species 'quitoense' refers to Quito, Ecuador.
