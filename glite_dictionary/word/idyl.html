idyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɪ.dɪl / (BrE)
a short poem or piece of prose that describes rural or pastoral life, typically idealized or romantic in tone
short poem about rural life

Examples

The poet composed an idyl about a tranquil afternoon in the countryside.
Her favorite idyl captures the peacefulness of rural villages.
Many 19th-century writers were inspired to write an idyl celebrating nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idyll - a short poem descriptive of rural or pastoral life

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'idyll', from Latin 'idyllium', from Greek 'eidyllion' (short poem).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈɪdɪl / (BrE)
a short musical composition that evokes the peacefulness or charm of rural life
pastoral musical composition

Examples

The composer's latest idyl beautifully captured the gentle rhythms of country life.
She played a brief idyl on the piano, evoking images of rolling meadows and grazing sheep.
Many of his works are inspired by nature, but this idyl stands out for its tranquil melody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - musical composition evoking rural scenes
  • Degree Higher: symphony - large-scale musical composition, often for orchestra

Etymology

From Greek eidullion (short poem), via Latin and French, originally referring to short pastoral poems.
