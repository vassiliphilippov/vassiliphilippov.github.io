to indicate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.dɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to be a sign or symptom of something; to show that something is likely to be true
be a sign or evidence of

Examples

The presence of smoke may indicate a fire nearby.
A fever can indicate an underlying infection.
Poor sales figures usually indicate problems with marketing or product quality.

Synset Examples

These symptoms indicate a serious illness
Her behavior points to a severe neurosis
The economic indicators signal that the euro is undervalued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to be a sign or warning of something
  • Synonym: to denote - to be a sign of; to indicate

Etymology

From Latin indicare (to point out, show), from in- (in, towards) + dicare (to proclaim).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00923271-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to show, suggest, or serve as a sign of something; to be evidence of

Examples

Examples
