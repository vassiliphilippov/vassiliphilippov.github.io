ethylene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθ.əˌlin / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.ɪliːn / (BrE)
a flammable, colorless gaseous hydrocarbon (C2H4) used in the manufacture of many chemicals and sometimes as an anesthetic
a colorless flammable gas (C2H4) used industrially

Examples

Industrial plants use ethylene to produce plastics and antifreeze.
Ripening fruit naturally releases ethylene into the air.
Safety protocols are strict when handling ethylene due to its flammability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethene - alternative chemical name for ethylene

Etymology

From French éthylène, from 'ethyl' + '-ene'.
