allice

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈæl.ɪs / (BrE)
another name for the European shad, a species of fish found in European rivers and coastal waters
the European shad fish

Examples

The fisherman was pleased to catch a large allice on the riverbank.
Allice migrate upstream each year to spawn in freshwater rivers.
Early spring is the best time to observe schools of allice in coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shad - a species of fish in the herring family, including the European shad

Etymology

From French 'alose', via Middle English 'allis', referring to the shad fish.
