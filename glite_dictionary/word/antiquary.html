antiquary

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪˌkwɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.kwəri / (BrE)
a person who studies, collects, or deals in objects or relics from the past, especially ancient artifacts or manuscripts
expert or collector of antiquities

Examples

The famous antiquary spent decades cataloguing ancient coins from Roman excavations.
She became a respected antiquary known for her collection of medieval manuscripts.
At the conference, every antiquary brought fascinating artifacts to display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeologist - a person who studies human history through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From Latin antiquarius (pertaining to antiquity), from antiquus (ancient).
