bookie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊki / (AmE), / ˈbʊki / (BrE)
a person who accepts and pays out bets, especially on sporting events such as horse races
person who accepts and pays out bets
informal

Examples

I placed a bet with my bookie on the outcome of the football match.
After the horse won, the bookie paid out my winnings in cash.
The police arrested a bookie for running an illegal betting operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookmaker - a person who takes bets, especially on sporting events

Etymology

Shortened form of 'bookmaker,' first recorded in late 19th century.
