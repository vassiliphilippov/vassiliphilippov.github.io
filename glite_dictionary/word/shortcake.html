shortcake

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtkeɪk / (BrE)
A sweet biscuit or cake, usually made with baking powder or soda, served with sweetened fruit and whipped cream.
sweet biscuit cake served with fruit and cream

Examples

Strawberry shortcake is a classic summer dessert in the United States.
She topped the warm shortcake with fresh berries and whipped cream.
At the bakery, they sell individual shortcakes loaded with peaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biscuit - a small baked unleavened cake, typically crisp, can refer to 'cookie' in American English or 'scone' in British English

Etymology

From 'short' in baking, meaning crumbly or rich in fat, and 'cake.'
