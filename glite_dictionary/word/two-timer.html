two-timer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːˌtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
a person who deceives a lover or spouse by having a sexual relationship with someone else at the same time
person who cheats on their partner
informal

Examples

Everyone warned her that he was a two-timer, but she didn't listen.
After discovering he was a two-timer, she ended the relationship immediately.
Being called a two-timer hurt his reputation among their friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheater - a person who cheats, especially in relationships
  • Antonym: faithful - loyal to a spouse or lover

Etymology

Formed from 'two-time' (to be unfaithful), with '-er' denoting the person.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuː ˌtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈtuː ˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
a person who is unfaithful in a romantic relationship, especially by having a secret relationship with someone else at the same time
person who is unfaithful in a relationship

Examples

After finding out he was a two-timer, she ended the relationship immediately.
No one wants to date a two-timer who can't be trusted.
Rumors spread quickly when someone is caught being a two-timer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheater - a person who cheats, especially in a relationship
  • Antonym: faithful - being loyal and true to a partner

Etymology

From the phrase 'two-time', meaning to be unfaithful to a partner.
