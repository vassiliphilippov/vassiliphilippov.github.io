recruit

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkrut / (AmE), / rɪˈkruːt / (BrE)
a person who has recently joined an organization, especially the armed forces
new member, especially in the military

Examples

The army is training the new recruits who joined last month.
As a recruit, she was eager to prove herself to her superiors.
Many companies hold orientation sessions for their latest recruits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veteran - someone with long experience in a particular area, especially military service
  • Synonym: trainee - someone undergoing training for a particular job or profession

Etymology

From French *recrue* (new growth, recruit), from *recruire* (to reinforce), from Latin *recrescere* (to grow again).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈkrut / (AmE), / rɪˈkruːt / (BrE)
a person who has recently joined the armed forces and is still in training or at an early stage of service
a newly enlisted soldier

Examples

The new recruit felt nervous on his first day of basic training.
Each recruit is assigned a mentor to help them adjust to military life.
The commander addressed the assembled recruits before their first march.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veteran - a person who has served long in the armed forces
  • Synonym: soldier - a member of the armed forces

Etymology

From French *recrue* (fresh supply of soldiers), from Old French *recroistre* (to grow again), from Latin *recrescere* (to regrow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10531965-n
Synset POS: n

/ rɪˈkrut / (AmE), / rɪˈkruːt / (BrE)

Examples

Examples

Examples
