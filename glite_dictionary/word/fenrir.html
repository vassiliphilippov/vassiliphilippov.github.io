Fenrir

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛnˌrɪr / (AmE), / ˈfɛnˌrɪə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) a monstrous wolf, the offspring of Loki, who is prophesied to kill Odin during Ragnarök.
giant wolf in Norse mythology

Examples

The legend says that Fenrir will break free at the time of Ragnarök.
In Norse myths, Fenrir is the wolf destined to slay Odin.
Many artists have depicted Fenrir as a fearsome and powerful beast.

Etymology

From Old Norse Fenrir, a mythological proper name.
