to inflect

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈflɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkt / (BrE)
to change the pitch or tone of one's voice during speech
vary the pitch of voice

Examples

She tried to inflect her voice to sound more enthusiastic during the presentation.
When reading stories to children, he would inflect his speech to make the characters come alive.
Actors must learn how to inflect their lines to convey different emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modulate - to adjust or vary the pitch, tone, or volume of the voice

Etymology

From Latin inflectere (to bend, change).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈflɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkt / (BrE)
(of a word) to change its form according to grammatical rules, such as for tense, case, or number.
change the form of a word grammatically

Examples

When you inflect a verb in Spanish, you must match the subject.
In English, nouns rarely inflect for gender.
Some languages inflect almost every word depending on its grammatical function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjugate - to inflect a verb, especially to indicate tense, person, or number
  • Synonym: decline - to inflect a noun, pronoun, or adjective, especially for case, number, or gender

Etymology

From Latin *inflectere* (to bend in, to change).
