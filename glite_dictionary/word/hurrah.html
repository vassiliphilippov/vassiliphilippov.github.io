hurrah

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɑ / (AmE), / hʊˈrɑː / (BrE)
a loud shout of joy or approval, typically given to celebrate a victory or success
a cheer of joy or approval

Examples

The crowd erupted in a loud hurrah as the team scored the winning goal.
Let's give the departing captain a big hurrah to show our appreciation.
The announcement of the new policy was met with a chorus of hurrah from the audience.

Synset Examples

let's give the team a big hurrah

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hooray - a shout of joy or approval, variant spelling
  • Antonym: boo - a shout to express disapproval or contempt

Etymology

From earlier hurra, perhaps of imitative origin or from German 'hurra'.

WordNet ID: oewn-06705718-n

2. VERB

/ həˈrɑ / (AmE), / həˈrɑː / (BrE)
to shout ‘hurrah!’ as an expression of joy, approval, or encouragement
shout ‘hurrah!’ in celebration

Examples

The crowd began to hurrah as the winning team walked onto the field.
Everyone hurrahed when the results were announced.
They hurrahed the speaker at the end of his inspiring address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout for joy or encouragement

Etymology

From earlier interjection ‘hurrah’, of imitative origin, possibly related to German ‘hurra’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00915935-v
Synset POS: v
