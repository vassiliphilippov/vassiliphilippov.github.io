contemptuousness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛmptʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of showing or expressing scorn or disdain for someone or something
quality of being contemptuous

Examples

His contemptuousness towards the committee became obvious during the heated discussion.
She could not hide her contemptuousness when addressing those she considered inferior.
The manager's contemptuousness made it difficult for employees to respect him.

Synset Examples

every subordinate sensed his contemptuousness and hated him in return

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless or deserving scorn
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From 'contemptuous' + '-ness'; 'contemptuous' from Latin 'contemptus' (scorn).
