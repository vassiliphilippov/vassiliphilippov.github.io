matron

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trən / (BrE)
a woman in charge of nursing staff in a hospital or other medical institution
female head nurse in a hospital
british medical

Examples

After years of experience, she was promoted to matron at the city hospital.
The matron supervised the nursing staff with discipline and care.
In British hospitals, the matron is responsible for maintaining high standards of cleanliness and patient comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head nurse - the chief nurse in a hospital or medical facility

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother), via Old French 'matrone' (married woman or matron).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trən / (AmE), / ˈmæ.trən / (BrE)
a married woman, especially one who is middle-aged or older, and is perceived as dignified or staid
dignified, often middle-aged married woman

Examples

The school hired a strict matron to oversee the boarding students.
At the dinner party, the matron was respected by everyone for her wisdom and poise.
The community regarded her as a model matron due to her charitable work and dignified manner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spinster - an unmarried woman, typically older

Etymology

From Latin 'matrona' (married woman), from 'mater' (mother).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪtrən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtrən / (BrE)
a female officer in charge of women prisoners or wards in an institution such as a prison or hospital
female officer in prison or institution

Examples

The matron inspected the women's cells each morning.
As matron of the prison, she was responsible for ensuring discipline among the inmates.
The new matron introduced several reforms to improve the living conditions for female prisoners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wardress - a woman who is in charge of prisoners in a prison

Etymology

From Latin matrona (married woman, wife), from mater (mother).
