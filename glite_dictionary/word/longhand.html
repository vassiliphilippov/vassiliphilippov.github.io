longhand

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌhænd / (BrE)
(of writing) written by hand in ordinary letters and words, rather than in shorthand or typed form.
ordinary handwritten form (not shorthand)

Examples

The contract was entirely written out in longhand.
She prefers to write her first drafts in longhand before typing them up.
His longhand notes were much easier to read than her shorthand ones.

Synset Examples

longhand writing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shorthand - method of rapid writing using abbreviations and symbols

Etymology

Formed from long + hand, indicating extended or full, handwritten form.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02293473-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌhænd / (BrE)
ordinary handwriting in which each letter is written out fully and is cursively connected, as opposed to typing or shorthand.
ordinary cursive handwriting

Examples

Some students prefer taking notes in longhand rather than on a laptop.
He wrote the entire report in longhand before typing it up.
Her grandmother's letters, written in elegant longhand, are treasured family keepsakes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shorthand - a method of rapid writing using abbreviations or symbols

Etymology

Formed from 'long' and 'hand', contrasting with 'shorthand'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06361681-n
Synset POS: n
