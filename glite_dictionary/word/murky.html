murky

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝːki / (AmE), / ˈmɜːki / (BrE)
(of a liquid) dark or cloudy, especially because of suspended dirt, sediment, or other particles.
cloudy or dark (liquid, with sediment)

Examples

The murky water in the pond made it impossible to see the fish.
After stirring the sediment, the wine became murky and unappetizing.
We couldn't drink from the river because the water was too murky.

Synset Examples

a cloudy liquid
muddy coffee
murky waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudy - (of a liquid) not clear; containing small particles that make it hard to see through
  • Antonym: clear - (of a liquid) transparent, with nothing suspended in it

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *mirke* (darkness), of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse *myrkr* (dark).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɝːki / (AmE), / ˈmɜːki / (BrE)
dark or dim, especially due to lack of light or presence of fog, smoke, or shadows; gloomy and difficult to see through
dark or gloomy; difficult to see through

Examples

The murky water made it impossible to see the bottom of the pond.
They wandered through the murky alleyways looking for the hidden café.
As night fell, the forest became increasingly murky and mysterious.

Synset Examples

a murky dungeon
murky rooms lit by smoke-blackened lamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially in a depressing way
  • Antonym: bright - full of light; shining strongly

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *mirke*, related to Old Norse *myrkr* (darkness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277555-s
Synset POS: s
