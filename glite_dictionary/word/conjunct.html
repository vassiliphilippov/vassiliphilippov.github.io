conjunct

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
(formal) closely joined or associated; connected together in close union.
closely joined or associated

Examples

The proposal called for conjunct actions from both departments to ensure success.
Their research revealed several conjunct factors contributing to the phenomenon.
Conjunct influences can often shape a person's decisions more than individual ones.

Synset Examples

conjunct influences
conjunct ideas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disjunct - not connected or joined; separated

Etymology

From Latin conjunctus, past participle of conjungere (to join together).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
(of melodic movement) moving by small, regular steps, especially by intervals of a second.
melodic movement by step (music)

Examples

The melody moves conjunctly, creating a sense of smoothness and continuity.
A conjunct scale ascends without any large jumps between the notes.
Many folk tunes feature conjunct melodic lines that are easy to sing.

Synset Examples

conjunct motion of an ascending scale

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disjunct - (of melodic movement) moving by larger intervals, especially leaps

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctus', past participle of 'conjungere' (to join together).

joint; combined; cooperative
