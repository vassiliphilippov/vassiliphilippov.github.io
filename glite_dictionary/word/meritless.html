meritless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛrɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪtlɪs / (BrE)
Having no merit, value, or worth; not deserving praise or approval.
having no merit or value

Examples

The judge dismissed the lawsuit because it was entirely meritless.
Her criticism of the project was considered meritless by other team members.
Many experts agreed that the accusations were meritless and unsupported by any real evidence.

Synset Examples

a sorry horse
a sorry excuse
a lazy no-count, good-for-nothing goldbrick
the car was a no-good piece of junk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - Having no value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - Having great value or worth

Etymology

From 'merit' + '-less', meaning 'without merit'.
