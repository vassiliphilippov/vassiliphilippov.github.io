cavalierly

1. ADV

/ ˌkævəˈlɪrli / (AmE), / ˌkævəˈlɪəli / (BrE)
in a proud, dismissive, or disdainful manner; showing a lack of proper concern or consideration
in a proud or dismissive manner

Examples

He dismissed their concerns cavalierly, as if they didn't matter at all.
The manager cavalierly ignored all safety protocols in his rush to complete the project.
She cavalierly brushed off criticism, confident in her own judgment.

Synset Examples

he treated his staff cavalierly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogantly - in a manner showing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a manner showing respect or deference

Etymology

From "cavalier" (a gallant or disdainful man), with -ly adverbial suffix; ultimately from Italian "cavaliere."
