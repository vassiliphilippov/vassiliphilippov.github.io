salicylate

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪleɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of salicylic acid, found in or used as medication in several drugs
a salt or ester of salicylic acid
medical

Examples

Many pain relievers contain salicylate as an active ingredient.
Some people are allergic to salicylates and need alternative medications.
The doctor explained that salicylate toxicity can occur with overdoses of aspirin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salicylic acid - a crystalline acid used in the synthesis of aspirin and other drugs

Etymology

From salicylic (acid), from Latin *salix* (willow tree) + -ate (chemical suffix).
