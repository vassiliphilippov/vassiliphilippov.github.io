sewage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsjuːɪdʒ / (BrE)
waste matter such as urine and feces, carried away from homes and factories through sewers or drains to be disposed of or treated
waste matter carried in sewers or drains

Examples

Raw sewage was discharged directly into the river, polluting the water supply.
The city invested in a modern sewage treatment plant to protect public health.
After the heavy rains, the storm drains overflowed and sewage backed up onto the streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wastewater - water that carries waste substances away
  • Synonym: effluent - liquid waste, especially chemicals or sewage, that is released into water

Etymology

From Old French 'essuage' (drainage), from 'essuier' (to wipe).
