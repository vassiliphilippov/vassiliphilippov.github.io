official

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
having the authority, approval, or recognition of a governing body, organization, or person in power
having authorized or sanctioned status

Examples

She waited for official permission before starting the construction project.
All official documents must be signed by the manager.
They issued an official statement regarding the incident.

Synset Examples

official permission
an official representative

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unofficial - not authorized or not formally recognized

Etymology

From Old French officiel, from Latin officialis (of or belonging to duty, service, or office), from officium (office, duty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01636580-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
(of information or a result) confirmed or authorized by a person or body with authority; having formal verification.
formally confirmed or authorized

Examples

The official results will be announced at noon.
Once the contract is signed, it becomes an official agreement.
The school’s official policy prohibits the use of cell phones during class.

Synset Examples

the election returns are now official

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unofficial - not confirmed or authorized by an authority

Etymology

From Latin officialis (of or belonging to duty, service, or office), from officium (duty, service, office).

3. ADJ

/ əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
relating to an office, position, or duty within an organization or institution, especially in a formal or established capacity.
relating to an office or position

Examples

Only those with official privileges are allowed to access the restricted area.
Your official responsibilities include managing project documentation and scheduling.
He wore his official badge to indicate his status within the company.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Latin 'officium' (service, duty, office).

4. NOUN

/ əˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃl / (BrE)
a person who holds a position of authority in an organization or government, especially one who is responsible for enforcing rules or laws
person holding an office or position of authority

Examples

An immigration official checked our documents at the border.
The official spoke to the press after the meeting.
Local officials are responsible for organizing the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functionary - a person who has a specific official position in an organization, especially in government
  • Degree Lower: employee - a person employed for wages or salary, especially in a non-executive role

Etymology

From Latin officium (service, duty, office) via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10392072-n
Synset POS: n

a person who is responsible for enforcing the rules in a game or sport
sports/game rules enforcer

Examples

A heated argument broke out, so the official stepped in to mediate between the players.
Tennis matches require at least one official to ensure fair play and resolve disputes.
