wicopy

1. NOUN

/ waɪˈkoʊpi / (AmE), / wɪˈkəʊpi / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, recognized for its tough, flexible branches, pliable bark, and small yellow flowers.
North American shrub with flexible branches

Examples

The bark of the wicopy is traditionally used for making baskets and cords.
In spring, the wicopy produces clusters of delicate yellow flowers along its branches.
Many gardeners admire the wicopy for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to different soils.
