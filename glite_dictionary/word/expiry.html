expiry

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspaɪəri / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaɪəri / (BrE)
the end of a period of validity for a contract, agreement, or official document.
end of validity period

Examples

Check the expiry date on your passport before booking your trip.
The expiry of the lease means we must find a new office soon.
His insurance policy is up for expiry next month.

Synset Examples

the expiry of his driver's license

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiration - the ending of the period of validity of something

Etymology

From Middle French *expirer* (to end), from Latin *exspirare* (to breathe out, come to an end).
