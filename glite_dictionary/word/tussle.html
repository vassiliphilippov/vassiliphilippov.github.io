tussle

1. VERB

/ ˈtʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌsəl / (BrE)
to fight or struggle roughly or vigorously, especially at close quarters
to fight or struggle roughly

Examples

The two boys began to tussle over the remote control in the living room.
During the match, the players tussled for possession of the ball near the sideline.
Police broke up a group of protesters who were tussling with security guards outside the building.

Synset Examples

the drunken men started to scuffle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scuffle - to engage in a short, confused fight or struggle at close quarters
  • Synonym: wrestle - to struggle or grapple with someone physically

Etymology

Apparently from earlier tusle, possibly frequentative of British dialect tuss (to pull roughly), origin uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌsəl / (BrE)
a short, vigorous struggle or fight, typically involving disorder or confusion
a brief, disorderly fight or struggle

Examples

The two players got into a tussle over the ball during the game.
After a brief tussle, the security guards managed to separate the crowd.
There was a noisy tussle outside the pub late last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scuffle - a short, confused fight or struggle at close quarters
  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy, rough fight in a crowd
  • Synonym: fight - an act of fighting with physical force

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from dialectal English 'tuss' (to pull roughly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01174931-n
Synset POS: n
