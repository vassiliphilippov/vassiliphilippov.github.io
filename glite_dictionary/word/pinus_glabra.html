Pinus glabra

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈɡleɪ.brə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈɡlæb.rə / (BrE)
A species of large two-needled pine tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its light, soft wood.
a southeastern US pine tree species

Examples

The forests in this region are dominated by Pinus glabra and sweetgum trees.
Researchers studied the growth rates of Pinus glabra under different soil conditions.
The soft wood of Pinus glabra is often used for pulpwood and timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - any tree of the genus Pinus, coniferous evergreens with needle-shaped leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' meaning pine, 'glabra' meaning smooth, referring to the bark or leaves.
