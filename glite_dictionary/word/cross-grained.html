cross-grained

1. ADJ

/ krɔs ˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / krɒs ˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
(of timber) having fibers that run irregularly rather than parallel to each other, making the wood harder to work.
wood with irregular fiber direction

Examples

The carpenter found the cross-grained oak difficult to plane smoothly.
Furniture made from cross-grained wood tends to warp more easily.
The cross-grained nature of the timber made it unsuitable for fine cabinetry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight-grained - (of timber) having fibers running parallel to each other

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' (across) + 'grained' (having a particular fiber pattern).

2. ADJ

/ krɔsˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / krɒsˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person) stubbornly contrary or difficult to deal with; inclined to oppose or disagree; self-willed.
stubbornly contrary; self-willed

Examples

Her cross-grained attitude made collaborations especially challenging for the team.
He was known for his cross-grained manner, rarely agreeing with anyone's plans.
Even in childhood, her cross-grained tendencies were clear whenever she stubbornly resisted advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - tending to oppose or disagree; stubborn
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion

Etymology

From 'cross-' (contrary) + 'grained', originally referring to wood with grain running across the main direction, later used figuratively of people.
