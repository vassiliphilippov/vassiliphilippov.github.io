oligonucleotide

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪɡoʊˈnuː.kli.əˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ɒˌlɪɡəˈnjuː.kli.ə.taɪd / (BrE)
A short nucleic acid molecule, typically made up of a small number of nucleotide units, used especially in genetic testing and research.
short chain of nucleotides
medical

Examples

Researchers used a synthetic oligonucleotide to detect the presence of the virus in blood samples.
The oligonucleotide sequence binds specifically to the target DNA region during the experiment.
She ordered several different oligonucleotides for her genetic analysis project.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polynucleotide - a long chain of nucleotide units

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + nucleotide.
