Anas rubripes

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəs ˈruːbraɪpiːz / (AmE), / ˈænəs ˈruːbrɪpiːz / (BrE)
A species of dusky duck native to the northeastern United States and Canada, commonly known as the American black duck.
species of duck (American black duck)

Examples

The Anas rubripes is often mistaken for a female mallard due to its similar appearance.
Researchers recorded the migration patterns of Anas rubripes across North America.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot several Anas rubripes in the wetland reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American black duck - Common name for Anas rubripes

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Anas' meaning 'duck', and species name 'rubripes' meaning 'red-footed'.
