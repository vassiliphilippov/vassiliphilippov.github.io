to neutralise

1. VERB

/ ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to get rid of someone who may be a threat, especially by killing them, often used in military or espionage contexts
eliminate a threat by killing (esp. military/espionage)

Examples

The agent was given orders to neutralise anyone attempting to sabotage the mission.
Several high-profile targets were neutralised during the covert operation.
Intelligence reports suggest that the group plans to neutralise the opposition leadership.

Synset Examples

The mafia liquidated the informer
the double agent was neutralized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquidate - to kill someone, especially for political reasons
  • Synonym: to assassinate - to murder an important person for political or religious reasons

Etymology

From French 'neutraliser', from Latin 'neutralis' (of neither side).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01329983-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnutʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a substance chemically neutral, especially by adding an acid to a base or vice versa
make chemically neutral

Examples

The chemist needed to neutralise the acidic solution before disposing of it.
You can neutralise a base by slowly adding an acid to it.
They used baking soda to neutralise the spilled vinegar on the floor.

Synset Examples

She neutralized the solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to neutralize - American spelling of 'neutralise'; make something chemically neutral

Etymology

From French *neutraliser*, from *neutre* (neutral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00470835-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnutʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something ineffective by counterbalancing its effect
make ineffective by counteracting

Examples

The new law will neutralise the impact of rising prices on consumers.
Vitamins can neutralise the harmful effects of some toxins.
He tried to neutralise her criticism by presenting solid evidence.

Synset Examples

Her optimism neutralizes his gloom
This action will negate the effect of my efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to counteract - to act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralise it
  • Antonym: to intensify - to make something stronger or more severe, the opposite of neutralise

Etymology

From French 'neutraliser', from 'neutre' (neutral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00471015-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈnuːtʃrəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to render a force or country incapable of military action
render militarily ineffective

Examples

The mission was to neutralise enemy air defenses before the main assault.
Special forces moved quickly to neutralise the threat without causing civilian casualties.
By striking strategic targets, the army was able to neutralise the opposition's military capabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disarm - to take weapons away from or render powerless
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make stronger, especially in a military sense

Etymology

From French 'neutraliser', from 'neutre' (neutral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01120349-v
Synset POS: v
