Henry VIII

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnɹi ðə ˈeɪtθ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnɹi ði ˈeɪtθ / (BrE)
(1491–1547) King of England from 1509 to 1547, known for his break with the Catholic Church and his role in the English Reformation.
King of England, led English Reformation

Examples

Henry VIII is famous for having had six wives and for initiating the English Reformation.
Many people visit Hampton Court Palace to learn about the reign of Henry VIII.
The establishment of the Church of England was a result of Henry VIII's dispute with the Pope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Elizabeth I - Queen of England, daughter of Henry VIII
  • Degree Lower: Henry VII - Father of Henry VIII, King of England

Etymology

From the personal name Henry, the eighth king of England with this name.
