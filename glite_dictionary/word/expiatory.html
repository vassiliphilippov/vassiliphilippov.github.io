expiatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛkspiəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈɛkspɪət(ə)ri / (BrE)
offered or done as an act of atonement or to make amends for wrongdoing or sin
serving to atone or make amends

Examples

The ceremony was held as an expiatory act to seek forgiveness for past transgressions.
An expiatory sacrifice was offered to appease the gods according to ancient tradition.
The monument was built as an expiatory gesture to remember those who suffered injustice.

Synset Examples

expiatory (or propitiatory) sacrifice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propitiatory - intended to appease or placate, especially a deity

Etymology

From Latin expiatorius, from expiare (to atone, make amends).
