tulipwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪpˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪpˌwʊd / (BrE)
The light, easily worked wood of a tulip tree, used in the making of furniture and for veneer.
wood of a tulip tree, used in furniture-making

Examples

The cabinet was beautifully crafted from tulipwood and stained a rich mahogany color.
Many antique desks were made using tulipwood because of its smooth grain and pale shade.
They chose tulipwood for the project due to its ease of carving and affordability.

Etymology

From 'tulip' (the tree's flower resemblance) + 'wood'. Name reflects the source tree, Liriodendron tulipifera.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪpˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪpˌwʊd / (BrE)
the variegated or distinctively striped ornamental wood obtained from various species of tulipwood trees, often used for fine woodworking
decorative wood from tulipwood trees

Examples

They crafted the jewelry box from fine tulipwood, highlighting its natural stripes.
That antique desk features drawers with elegant tulipwood inlays.
Furniture makers prize tulipwood for its colorful and decorative appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - a richly colored wood from tropical trees, used for furniture

Etymology

Named for its source: the tulipwood tree, so called for its tulip-shaped flowers.
