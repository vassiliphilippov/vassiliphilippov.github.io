dichromia

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkroʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / daɪˈkrəʊ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A deficiency of color vision in which a person can distinguish only two primary colors and requires only two wavelengths to match any hue, as opposed to the three needed for normal vision.
deficiency of color vision (two-color perception)
medical

Examples

People with dichromia can match all colors using only two wavelengths of light.
Diagnosing dichromia often involves testing the patient's ability to distinguish between certain hues.
Researchers study the genetic causes of dichromia to better understand color vision disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability or decreased ability to see color or perceive color differences

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + khrōma (color).
