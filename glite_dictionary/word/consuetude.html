consuetude

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnswɪˌtud, ˈkɑnsjuˌtud / (AmE), / ˈkɒnswɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
A custom or practice that, by being long established, has acquired the force of law.
custom with legal force
archaic legal

Examples

Ancient consuetude dictated the rights and responsibilities of the village council.
The court recognized consuetude as having the same force as written law in this case.
He studied legal consuetude to understand how unwritten norms shape the justice system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'consuetudo' (custom, habit).
