to retrogress

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛs / (BrE)
to return to an earlier or worse state or condition; to deteriorate or move backward
return to a worse or earlier state

Examples

After significant improvements in the company, poor management caused it to retrogress to its former inefficiency.
Without regular practice, language skills can retrogress over time.
The negotiations seemed promising, but later they began to retrogress rather than progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regress - to return to a former or less developed state
  • Antonym: progress - to move forward or develop toward a better state

Etymology

From Latin 'retrogressus', past participle of 'retrogradi' (to go backward); from 'retro' (back) + 'gradi' (to step).

2. VERB

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛs / (BrE)
to return to a worse or less developed state, often by reverting to bad habits or behaviors
to return to a worse state or bad habits

Examples

After months of improvement, he began to retrogress into old destructive habits.
Society must guard against the tendency to retrogress when faced with setbacks.
Despite initial success, the program started to retrogress due to lack of proper oversight.

Synset Examples

Those who recidivate are often minor criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regress - to return to a former or less developed state; similar meaning
  • Antonym: progress - to move forward or towards a better state; opposite meaning

Etymology

From Latin retrogressus, past participle of retrogradi (to go backwards), from retro (back) + gradi (to step, walk).
