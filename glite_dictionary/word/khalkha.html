Khalkha

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlkə / (AmE), / ˈkælkə / (BrE)
the dialect of Mongolian that is the official language of Mongolia, spoken chiefly by the Khalkha people.
dominant dialect of Mongolian language

Examples

Many people in Mongolia speak Khalkha as their native language.
The grammar of Khalkha Mongolian is considered the standard in language education.
Researchers recorded interviews with speakers of Khalkha to study linguistic variations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongolian - the official language of Mongolia, a group of Mongolic languages

Etymology

From Mongolian Халха (Khalqa), referring to the largest subgroup of the Mongol people.

2. NOUN

/ kɑlˈxɑ / (AmE), / kɑːlˈxɑː / (BrE)
a member of the Mongol people who live mainly in the central and eastern regions of Outer Mongolia
member of a Mongol ethnic group in Outer Mongolia

Examples

The Khalkha make up the largest subgroup of Mongol people in Mongolia.
Traditionally, the Khalkha have played a central role in the history of Outer Mongolia.
Many Khalkha still maintain a nomadic lifestyle on the steppes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongol - a member of the Mongolic peoples inhabiting Central Asia

Etymology

From Mongolian Халх (Khalh), the name of the group.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09757184-n
Synset POS: n
