archegonium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkɪˈgoʊniəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈgəʊnɪəm / (BrE)
a female reproductive organ in mosses, ferns, and most gymnosperms, consisting of a single egg cell
female sex organ in mosses and ferns
medical

Examples

Each archegonium contains a single egg cell ready for fertilization.
The archegonium can be found at the tip of the female gametophyte in mosses.
In ferns, the archegonium develops on the lower surface of the prothallus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antheridium - male reproductive organ in ferns, mosses, and algae

Etymology

From Greek arkhēgonios (originating), from arkhē (beginning) + gonos (offspring)
