solvate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːl.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.veɪt / (BrE)
a compound formed when solvent molecules combine with molecules or ions of a solute through solvation
compound formed by solvation
medical

Examples

A copper solvate was isolated during the reaction.
The stability of the solvate depends on the solvent used.
When heated, the solvate released its solvent molecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves a solute
  • Synonym: solution - a homogeneous mixture of two or more substances

Etymology

From 'solvent' and the suffix '-ate', patterned on names of chemical compounds.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɑlˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.veɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to undergo solvation, or to cause something to combine with solvent molecules to form a solvate.
to undergo or cause solvation
medical

Examples

Certain salts will solvate rapidly when mixed with water.
The chemist solvated the compound before further analysis.
If the compound cannot solvate easily, its reaction rate decreases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvation - the process of surrounding solute molecules with solvent molecules

Etymology

From 'solvation', modelled after 'hydrate' and similar chemical terms.

3. VERB

/ ˈsɒlˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.veɪt / (BrE)
(of a solvent) to surround or interact with molecules or ions of a solute, resulting in solvation.
to cause to undergo solvation
medical

Examples

Chemists can solvate ions by introducing a suitable solvent to the system.
The reagent is designed to solvate lithium ions efficiently during the reaction.
When water is added, it will solvate the salt and facilitate its dissolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to mix with a liquid and become part of the liquid
  • Antonym: precipitate - to cause (a substance) to be deposited in solid form from a solution

Etymology

From 'solvation,' modeled after 'hydrate.'
