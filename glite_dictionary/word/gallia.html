Gallia

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡæliə / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪə / (BrE)
the ancient region of western Europe that covered present-day northern Italy, France, Belgium, and parts of Germany and the Netherlands
ancient region in western Europe

Examples

Julius Caesar famously led campaigns in Gallia during the Gallic Wars.
Many tribes inhabited Gallia before the Roman conquest.
The legacy of Roman rule in Gallia can still be seen in modern France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaul - the English name for the ancient region of Gallia

Etymology

From Latin 'Gallia' meaning 'Gaul'; used by Romans to denote the region.
