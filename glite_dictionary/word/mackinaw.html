mackinaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmækɪˌnɔː / (BrE)
A heavy woolen cloth that is thickly napped and felted, often featuring a plaid pattern, used especially for coats and blankets.
thick, plaid woolen cloth

Examples

He wore a red-and-black mackinaw to keep warm in the snowy woods.
The thick mackinaw blanket was perfect for cold winter nights at the cabin.
She spread her grandfather's old mackinaw coat over the sled.

Etymology

Named after the Mackinac region (variant spelling Mackinaw) of Michigan, United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmækɪˌnɔː / (BrE)
A short, thick, woolen coat, typically with a plaid pattern, originally worn in North America for warmth.
short woolen plaid coat

Examples

He wore his favorite mackinaw during the winter hunting trip.
The vintage store had a red-and-black plaid mackinaw hanging by the door.
A heavy mackinaw is perfect for keeping you warm in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - an outer garment with sleeves, worn outdoors

Etymology

From Mackinac, a strait in Michigan, USA, where such coats were first made.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmakɪnɔː / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed boat, especially one used on the upper Great Lakes, designed for use in shallow waters
flat-bottomed Great Lakes boat

Examples

Fishermen loaded supplies onto the mackinaw before heading out onto Lake Superior.
The old mackinaw was perfectly suited for navigating the shallow waters near the shore.
Local historians restored a 19th-century mackinaw for the maritime museum display.

Etymology

Named after Mackinac Island, Michigan, influenced by Ojibwa term from the region.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmakɪnɔː / (BrE)
a heavy, thick plaid blanket, especially one formerly used in the northwestern United States and Canada
thick plaid blanket (US/Canada)

Examples

He unfolded his mackinaw and wrapped it tightly around his shoulders to keep out the cold.
The mackinaw was a prized possession among trappers of the early American Northwest.
She bought a vintage mackinaw at the antique fair for her cabin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blanket - a large piece of thick cloth used for warmth as a bed covering

Etymology

From Mackinac, a place name in Michigan, US; associated with trading blankets in the region.
