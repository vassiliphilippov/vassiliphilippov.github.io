choragic

1. ADJ

/ kɔːˈrædʒɪk / (AmE), / kəˈrædʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a choragus, especially in reference to ancient Greek festivals and monuments.
relating to a choragus (ancient Greek sponsor or monument)

Examples

The choragic monument stood at the end of the street, its columns finely decorated.
Scholars studied the choragic inscriptions to learn more about ancient Greek festivals.
The choragic function was vital in supporting public performances in Athens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choragus - the sponsor or leader of a chorus in ancient Greece

Etymology

From choragus (Greek khorēgos, leader of a chorus) + -ic.
