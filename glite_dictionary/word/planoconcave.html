planoconcave

1. ADJ

/ ˌplænoʊˈkɑnveɪv / (AmE), / ˌplænəʊˈkænveɪv / (BrE)
(of a lens or surface) having one side that is flat and the other side that is concave.
flat on one side, concave on the other

Examples

The planoconcave lens was perfect for spreading out the beam of light.
A planoconcave mirror is often used in optical experiments to control the direction of light.
He specialized in manufacturing planoconcave glass filters for laboratory use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: planoconvex - flat on one side and convex on the other

Etymology

From Latin 'planus' (flat) and English 'concave'.
