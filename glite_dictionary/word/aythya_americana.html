Aythya americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪθi.ə æˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈaɪθɪ.ə æˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nə / (BrE)
A North American diving duck with a grey-and-black body and a reddish-brown head, commonly known as the redhead.
North American diving duck (redhead)

Examples

The Aythya americana is often seen in freshwater lakes across North America during migration.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a male Aythya americana with its distinctive reddish-brown head.
Habitat loss has significantly affected the populations of Aythya americana in recent decades.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus name Aythya and species epithet americana ('of America').
