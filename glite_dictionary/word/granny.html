granny

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræni / (AmE), / ˈɡræni / (BrE)
an old woman, especially one's grandmother; used as a familiar or affectionate term
old woman; affectionate term for grandmother

Examples

My granny loves to bake cookies for the whole family.
When I was a child, my granny used to tell me bedtime stories.
Many people admire their granny for her wisdom and kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandmother - mother of one's father or mother

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'grandmother', altered by childlike speech.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn.i / (AmE), / ˈɡran.i / (BrE)
A reef knot tied incorrectly, resulting in an insecure knot.
an incorrectly tied reef knot

Examples

After the demonstration, he realized he had made a granny instead of a proper reef knot.
The rope slipped out of the granny, causing the bundle to fall apart.
Sailors are taught to avoid tying a granny because it is not secure.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡræni / (AmE), / ˈɡræni / (BrE)
An informal term for the mother of one's father or mother; grandmother.
informal term for grandmother
informal

Examples

My granny taught me how to bake apple pie when I was a child.
We visited my granny every summer in the countryside.
When I was sick, my granny made me her special chicken soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandmother - the mother of one's father or mother

Etymology

Diminutive of "grandmother," from "grand" + "-ny" (affectionate diminutive suffix).
