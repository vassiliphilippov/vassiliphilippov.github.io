castration

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / kæsˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of the testicles of a male animal or person, typically to render the animal sterile
removal of male testicles (neutering)
medical

Examples

The veterinary surgeon explained the procedure of castration to the farmer.
Cattle are often subjected to castration to improve their temperament.
After his injury, the horse required castration for medical reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutering - the removal of reproductive organs to render an animal sterile

Etymology

From Latin 'castratio', from 'castrare' meaning 'to cut off, to castrate'.

2. NOUN

/ kæsˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / kæsˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of the testes or ovaries, often to inhibit hormone secretion or prevent reproduction
removal of testes or ovaries
medical

Examples

After a diagnosis of advanced prostate cancer, the doctor recommended castration as a treatment option.
Veterinarians often perform castration to control the pet population.
Chemical castration can serve as an alternative to surgical procedures in certain medical cases.

Synset Examples

bilateral castration results in sterilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterilization - procedure to make an organism unable to reproduce

Etymology

From Latin 'castratio', from 'castrare' meaning 'to castrate'.

3. NOUN

/ kæsˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / kæsˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing or deleting objectionable or offensive parts from a literary work
removal of offensive parts from a text

Examples

Some critics argue that the castration of the novel robbed it of its original impact.
The play underwent significant castration before it was deemed acceptable for school performance.
Many authors oppose the castration of their works for political or moral reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowdlerization - removal of material considered improper from a text

Etymology

From Latin 'castratio', from 'castrare' (to cut off).
