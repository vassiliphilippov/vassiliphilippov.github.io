weigh-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈweɪ ɪn / (BrE)
an official event at which a competitor’s body weight is measured before a competition, especially to check it meets the required limits
official pre-competition weight measurement

Examples

The boxers arrived early at the weigh-in to prepare for their match.
He failed to make weight at the official weigh-in and was disqualified.
Fans gathered to watch the competitors at the tournament weigh-in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weigh in - to have one's weight measured officially, typically before a contest

Etymology

From the verb 'weigh in', meaning to have one's weight measured, with the noun form emerging in the 20th century in sports contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈweɪ ɪn / (BrE)
an event, especially in sports, where the weights of participants are officially measured, often before a competition
official event for measuring weight

Examples

Before the fight, each boxer must attend a weigh-in to ensure they meet the weight requirements.
The weigh-in will take place at the gym the day before the race.
Spectators gathered to watch the tense weigh-in between the rival competitors.

Synset Examples

The scales didn't balance out after the last weigh-in.
