gharry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæri / (AmE), / ˈɡæri / (BrE)
A horse-drawn carriage, especially one used as a mode of transport in India and some other Asian countries.
horse-drawn carriage (India)

Examples

The tourists enjoyed a leisurely ride through the old city in a beautifully decorated gharry.
In colonial times, the gharry was a common sight on the streets of Calcutta.
He hailed a gharry to take him from the station to his hotel.

Etymology

From Hindi 'ghāṛī', meaning 'cart' or 'carriage'.
