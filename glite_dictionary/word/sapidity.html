sapidity

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / səˈpɪdɪti / (BrE)
the quality of having a pleasant, strong, or distinctive flavour; tastiness
pleasant or distinctive taste

Examples

The sapidity of the broth delighted all who tasted it at the dinner table.
She praised the dish for its remarkable sapidity and perfect seasoning.
After weeks of bland meals, he craved something with real sapidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavor - the distinctive taste of a food or drink
  • Antonym: insipidity - lack of flavor or taste

Etymology

From Latin sapiditas, from sapidus (tasty).

2. NOUN

/ sæˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / səˈpɪdɪti / (BrE)
the quality of having a pleasant or agreeable flavor; tastiness
pleasant flavor or tastiness

Examples

The sapidity of the homemade soup impressed every guest at the dinner table.
Wine connoisseurs often debate the sapidity of different grape varieties.
A dash of herbs gave unexpected sapidity to the otherwise simple dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savoriness - pleasant, appetizing taste or smell
  • Antonym: insipidity - lacking flavor or zest

Etymology

From Latin 'sapiditas' (taste, flavor), from 'sapidus' (tasty).
