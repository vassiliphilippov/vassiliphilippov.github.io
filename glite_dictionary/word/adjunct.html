adjunct

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (BrE)
a person who is an assistant or subordinate to someone else, often in a supporting or auxiliary role
assistant or subordinate person

Examples

She worked as an adjunct to the director, handling administrative duties.
The professor was assisted by an adjunct during the research project.
In some companies, an adjunct may have a part-time or temporary position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone in their job
  • Antonym: superior - a person higher in rank or position

Etymology

From Latin adjunctus, past participle of adjungere (to join to).

2. ADJ

/ ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈadʒ.ʌŋkt / (BrE)
(of a person or position) subordinate or supplementary to another; not having a permanent or primary status.
subordinate or supplementary (person or role)

Examples

Dr. Smith is an adjunct professor who teaches part-time at the university.
The research was conducted by an adjunct member of the staff.
She works as an adjunct advisor to the main board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps or supports another
  • Antonym: principal - primary or main; a person or thing with highest authority

Etymology

From Latin 'adjunctus', past participle of 'adjungere' (to join to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00796805-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (BrE)
a thing added to something else as a supplementary rather than an essential part
supplementary addition; not essential

Examples

The professor served as an adjunct to the department, teaching only a few courses each semester.
Her meditation practice is an adjunct to the medical treatment she receives for anxiety.
Many herbs are used as an adjunct to traditional recipes to enhance flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - an additional item that is not essential
  • Antonym: integral - essential to completeness

Etymology

From Latin 'adjunctus', meaning 'joined to', past participle of 'adjungere'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (BrE)
(Grammar) A word, phrase, or clause added to a sentence to give extra information but which is not essential to its structure.
optional grammatical element in a sentence

Examples

In the sentence 'She ran quickly,' the word quickly is an adjunct that describes how she ran.
Temporal adjuncts like 'yesterday' or 'this morning' can specify when an action took place.
The phrase 'with a smile' acts as an adjunct in 'He greeted me with a smile.'

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complement - a word or phrase needed to complete the meaning of a sentence

Etymology

From Latin adjunctus, past participle of adjungere (to join to).
