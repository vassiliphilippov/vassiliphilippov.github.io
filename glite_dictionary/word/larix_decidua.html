Larix decidua

1. NOUN

/ ləˈrɪks dɪˈsɪdʒuə / (AmE), / ləˈrɪks dɪˈsɪdjʊə / (BrE)
A species of tall European larch tree with a slender conical crown, flat needle-like leaves, and hairy cone scales.
species of European larch tree

Examples

The Larix decidua is notable for its golden needles in autumn before they fall.
Foresters in Europe often plant Larix decidua to improve soil and timber quality.
Unlike most conifers, Larix decidua sheds its needles during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - any coniferous tree of the genus Larix

Etymology

From Latin: 'Larix' meaning larch, and 'decidua' meaning 'falling off' (referring to its deciduous needles).
