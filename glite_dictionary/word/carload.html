carload

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːˌləʊd / (BrE)
an amount of cargo that can be carried by a freight car, boat, or similar large container
amount of cargo held by a freight car

Examples

The factory shipped one carload of steel to the construction site every week.
They received a carload of fresh produce early in the morning.
The donation drive collected nearly a carload of winter coats for families in need.

Synset Examples

he imported wine by the boatload

Etymology

Formed from 'car' and 'load,' denoting the load a railway or freight car can carry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːˌləʊd / (BrE)
the number of people or amount of things that fills a car
number that fills a car

Examples

A whole carload of people showed up at the party.
She brought a carload of friends to the concert.
They waited for a carload of classmates before leaving for the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truckload - the quantity that fills a truck
  • Synonym: busload - the quantity that fills a bus
