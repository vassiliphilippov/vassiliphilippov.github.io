Orycteropus

1. PROPN

/ ɔːˌrɪktəˈroʊpəs / (AmE), / ɔːˌrɪktəˈrəʊpəs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals comprising the aardvark, native to Africa, and the sole genus in the family Orycteropodidae.
genus of mammals including the aardvark

Examples

The only living species in the genus Orycteropus is the aardvark.
Scientists have classified the aardvark as Orycteropus afer in taxonomic literature.
Fossil remains of extinct Orycteropus species have been discovered in several parts of Africa.

Etymology

From Greek oryktēr (digger) + pous (foot), referring to its digging habits.
