Marlowe

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːləʊ / (BrE)
A tough, cynical fictional detective created by Raymond Chandler, known as one of the early American hardboiled detective heroes.
fictional detective by Raymond Chandler

Examples

The character of Marlowe has become synonymous with the hardboiled detective genre.
Many film adaptations have portrayed Marlowe as a world-weary yet principled investigator.
Critics often praise Marlowe for his sharp wit and moral complexity.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.ləʊ / (BrE)
Christopher Marlowe (1564–1593), an English poet and playwright known for introducing blank verse to English drama.
English poet and playwright (1564–1593)

Examples

Many scholars believe that Marlowe's plays influenced Shakespeare's early work.
The death of Marlowe at such a young age remains a subject of speculation.
I have always enjoyed reading the poetry of Marlowe and his bold dramatic works.
