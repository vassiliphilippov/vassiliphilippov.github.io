Arum maculatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrəm mæˈkjʊlətəm / (AmE), / ˈɑːrəm məˈkjuːlətəm / (BrE)
A common European woodland plant with arrow-shaped leaves, a hood-like greenish spathe, and a short purple spadix, also known as lords-and-ladies.
species of European arum plant

Examples

Arum maculatum is known for its distinctive hooded flower that appears in early spring.
The berries of Arum maculatum are poisonous and should not be eaten.
Botanists often find Arum maculatum growing in the shaded understory of European woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lords-and-ladies - common name for Arum maculatum
  • Synonym: cuckoo-pint - alternative common name for Arum maculatum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Arum' (arum) and 'maculatum' (spotted).
