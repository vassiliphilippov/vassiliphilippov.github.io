gyral

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒaɪrəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəl / (BrE)
relating to or associated with a gyrus or convolution of the brain
of a brain gyrus or convolution
medical

Examples

Cortical development is often measured by changes in gyral patterns observed through brain imaging.
The researchers noted a significant increase in gyral thickness among the study participants.
A gyral abnormality may indicate an underlying neurological disorder.

Synset Examples

the gyral sulcus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sulcal - relating to a sulcus (a groove or furrow, especially on the brain surface)

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'gyrus', meaning 'circle' or 'coil', from Greek 'gyros'.
