Malva neglecta

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæl.və nɛɡˈlɛk.tə / (AmE), / ˈmæl.və nɪˈɡlɛk.tə / (BrE)
A species of mallow, an annual plant native to Europe and Asia, with clusters of pink or white flowers, now widespread in North America.
a species of mallow plant

Examples

Malva neglecta is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
Gardeners sometimes remove Malva neglecta because it is considered a weed in cultivated areas.
The clusters of pink or white flowers help to easily identify Malva neglecta in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - any plant of the genus Malva, to which Malva neglecta belongs

Etymology

From Latin: 'malva' meaning mallow and 'neglecta' meaning neglected or overlooked.
