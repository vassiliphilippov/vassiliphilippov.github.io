polyploid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑliˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌplɔɪd / (BrE)
(genetics) an organism or cell having more than two complete sets of chromosomes.
organism/cell with extra chromosome sets
medical

Examples

Many cultivated plants, such as wheat, are naturally polyploid and possess genetic advantages as a result.
Scientists created a polyploid strain of fish to study the effects of extra chromosomes on growth rates.
Because of its polyploid nature, this species is unusually resistant to certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: diploid - organism or cell with two sets of chromosomes
  • Degree Lower: haploid - organism or cell with one set of chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'poly-' (many) + '-ploid' (from 'ploos', meaning 'fold').

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑliˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having more than two complete sets of chromosomes.
having multiple sets of chromosomes

Examples

Many cultivated plants, such as wheat, are polyploid and have numerous chromosome sets.
Researchers discovered a polyploid strain of fish that shows enhanced tolerance to environmental stress.
Some cancers involve cells becoming polyploid, which is linked to genetic instability.

Synset Examples

a polyploid cell
a polyploid species

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: diploid - (of a cell or organism) having two complete sets of chromosomes
  • Degree Lower: haploid - (of a cell or organism) having a single set of chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'ploos' (fold).
