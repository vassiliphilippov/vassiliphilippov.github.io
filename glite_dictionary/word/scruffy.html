scruffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrʌfi / (AmE), / ˈskrʌfi / (BrE)
shabby and untidy in appearance or dress.
shabby and untidy

Examples

He looked scruffy after two weeks of camping in the mountains.
The children’s scruffy clothes suggested they had been playing outside all afternoon.
She always made sure her dog was clean and not scruffy before visiting guests.

Synset Examples

a surge of ragged scruffy children
he was soiled and seedy and fragrant with gin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Antonym: tidy - neat and well arranged
  • Synonym: unkempt - having an untidy or disheveled appearance
