tibialis posterior

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈbiː.ə.lɪs poʊˈstɪr.i.ɚ / (AmE), / tɪˈbɪə.lɪs pɒˈstɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a deep muscle in the lower leg that assists in foot movement and support of the arch
deep muscle in the lower leg
medical

Examples

The tibialis posterior plays a crucial role in supporting the arch of the foot.
Injuries to the tibialis posterior can cause pain and instability in the ankle.
Physical therapy may help strengthen a weakened tibialis posterior muscle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tibialis anterior - a muscle located at the front of the shin

Etymology

From Latin 'tibialis' (of the tibia) + 'posterior' (behind, at the back).
