Myrtaceae

1. NOUN

/ mərˈteɪsi.i / (AmE), / mɜːˈteɪsi.i / (BrE)
a family of trees and shrubs, including myrtle and eucalyptus, that typically yield a fragrant oil
plant family including myrtle and eucalyptus

Examples

Many species in the Myrtaceae family are cultivated for their ornamental and aromatic qualities.
Eucalyptus, a well-known member of the Myrtaceae, is prized for its distinctive scent.
Botanists study the Myrtaceae because of their ecological and economic importance.

Etymology

From modern Latin Myrtaceae, from Myrtus (genus name for myrtle) + -aceae (plant family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ mɜrˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / mɜːˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of trees and shrubs, including myrtle and eucalyptus, that yield fragrant oil
plant family including myrtle and eucalyptus

Examples

Many species in Myrtaceae are valued for their aromatic oils and medicinal properties.
Eucalyptus trees, part of the Myrtaceae family, are commonly found in Australia.
The Myrtaceae family also includes guava, clove, and feijoa species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrtle - an evergreen shrub of the genus Myrtus in Myrtaceae
  • Synonym: eucalyptus - a genus of trees in the Myrtaceae family

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek myrtos (myrtle).
