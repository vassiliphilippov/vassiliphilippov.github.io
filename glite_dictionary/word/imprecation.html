imprecation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmprəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmprɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a spoken curse or the act of invoking evil upon someone
spoken curse; act of invoking evil

Examples

The accused muttered an imprecation as he was led away by the guards.
Ancient texts warned that uttering an imprecation could bring ruin to one's enemies.
The angry woman hurled an imprecation at the man who had wronged her.

Synset Examples

he suffered the imprecations of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm or punishment
  • Antonym: blessing - a prayer or utterance asking for divine favor and protection

Etymology

From Latin 'imprecātiō' (invocation of evil), from 'imprecari' (to invoke).
