metallic

1. NOUN

/ məˈtælɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
A fabric made of yarn that contains metal, either partially or entirely, often used for decorative or special-occasion clothing.
fabric made with metal yarn

Examples

The designer showcased a dress made entirely of shimmering metallic.
Some evening gowns feature panels of metallic for extra sparkle and texture.
Vintage costumes sometimes use metallic to add a dramatic effect on stage.

2. ADJ

/ məˈtælɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
containing, made of, or resembling metal; having the characteristics of metal, such as a shiny surface or a harsh sound
containing, made of, or resembling metal

Examples

The painting had a metallic sheen that caught the light beautifully.
Coins are often made from a metallic alloy for durability.
His voice had a strange, metallic quality that was instantly recognizable.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metal - a solid substance that is typically hard, shiny, and conducts heat and electricity

Etymology

From French *métallique*, from Latin *metallicus*, from Greek *metallikos* (pertaining to metal).

/ məˈtælɪk / (AmE), / məˈtælɪk / (BrE)
Having the characteristics of metal, such as being made of, containing, or resembling metal, especially in luster, sound, or color.
having characteristics of metal

Examples

The paint has a metallic sheen that sparkles in the sunlight.
I could hear a strange metallic sound coming from the engine.
These rocks have a metallic luster, making them look almost like silver.

Synset Examples

a metallic compound
metallic luster
the strange metallic note of the meadow lark, suggesting the clash of vibrant blades

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metal - a solid material that is typically hard, shiny, and has good electrical and thermal conductivity

Etymology

From Late Latin metallicus, from Latin metallum (metal).

/ məˈtælɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)

yarn made with metal fibers
