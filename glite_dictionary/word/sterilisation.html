sterilisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɛrɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making an organism unable to reproduce, typically through medical procedures or treatments that result in infertility or barrenness
making an organism infertile

Examples

The veterinary clinic performs sterilisation on stray animals to control the population.
Her doctor discussed different methods of sterilisation as a permanent form of contraception.
Mandatory sterilisation policies have been controversial throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castration - removal of the testicles or ovaries; a form of sterilisation
  • Antonym: fertility - ability to reproduce; opposite of being infertile

Etymology

From French 'stérilisation', from Latin 'sterilis' (barren, unfruitful).

2. NOUN

/ ˌstɛrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɛrɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something free from all living bacteria and other microorganisms, usually by using heat or chemicals
process of eliminating all microorganisms
medical british

Examples

The sterilisation of surgical instruments is crucial before any operation.
Proper sterilisation ensures that laboratory equipment is free from contamination.
They use high-pressure steam for the sterilisation of glassware in the research facility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterilization - the process of eliminating all microorganisms (American spelling)
  • Degree Lower: disinfection - the process of reducing harmful microorganisms to a safe level, but not necessarily all

Etymology

From Latin sterilis (unfruitful) + English -ation, influenced by French stérilisation.
