Harlem

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑrləm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːləm / (BrE)
A district in the northern section of Manhattan, New York City, known for its rich African-American cultural history.
district of Manhattan, NYC

Examples

Many artists and musicians of the Jazz Age lived in Harlem during the 1920s.
Tourists often visit Harlem to experience its vibrant culture and historic landmarks.
After moving to New York, she rented an apartment in Harlem near the famous Apollo Theater.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Haarlem', after the city of Haarlem in the Netherlands.
