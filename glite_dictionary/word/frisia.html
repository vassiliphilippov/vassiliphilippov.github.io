Frisia

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɪʒə / (AmE), / ˈfrɪzɪə / (BrE)
an ancient coastal region of northwestern Europe, including the Frisian Islands, historically inhabited by the Frisians.
ancient region of northwestern Europe

Examples

Historians often study the migration patterns of ancient peoples in Frisia and surrounding areas.
The coastal climate of Frisia influenced the lives of its early inhabitants.
Much of modern-day Friesland in the Netherlands lies within the territory once known as Frisia.

Etymology

From Latin Frisia, related to the territory inhabited by the Frisii people.
