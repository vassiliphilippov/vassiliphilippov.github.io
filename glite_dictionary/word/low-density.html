low-density

1. ADJ

/ ˌloʊˈdɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˌləʊˈdɛn.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
having a relatively low density or specific gravity compared to the norm or to other substances or materials
having a low density or specific gravity

Examples

The engineer recommended using low-density foam for better insulation.
Helium is a low-density gas compared to air, which is why balloons filled with it float.
They built new homes in a low-density residential area to reduce crowding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-density - having a high density or specific gravity
