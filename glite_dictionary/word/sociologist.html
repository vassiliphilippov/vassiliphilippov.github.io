sociologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊsiˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies human society, social behavior, and institutions as a scientific discipline
expert in the study of society

Examples

The sociologist conducted research on patterns of urban migration.
As a sociologist, she focuses on family dynamics within modern societies.
Several sociologists have contributed to our understanding of class and inequality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social scientist - a scientist who studies society and social relationships

Etymology

From French sociologie (sociology) + -ist (one who practices)
