operational

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌɒp.ərˈeɪ.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
In use or functioning; currently active and able to be used.
working; in use; functioning

Examples

The hospital will not be operational until next week due to construction work.
After the storm, only a few power lines remained operational.
The team ensured all equipment was operational before the event began.

Synset Examples

de facto apartheid is still operational even in the ‘new’ African nations
bus service is in operation during the emergency
the company had several operating divisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functioning - working properly; operating as intended
  • Antonym: inactive - not active or not working

Etymology

From operate + -ional, dating from the early 19th century.

/ ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or connected with the practical operation or functioning of a process, system, or activity
relating to practical functioning or activities

Examples

We need to address the operational aspects of the new delivery system.
All crew members have taken their operational positions and are ready for launch.

Synset Examples

operational difficulties
they assumed their operational positions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - having a special activity, purpose, or task; relating to the way in which something works or operates

Etymology

relating to military operations

4. ADJ

/ ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənl / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənəl / (BrE)
(of a machine, system, or service) in working order; fit or ready for use or operation.
in working order; ready for use

Examples

The hospital's backup generator became operational just minutes after the power outage began.
All equipment must be fully operational before the factory can reopen.
Although the computer is old, it remains operational and useful for basic tasks.

Synset Examples

the toaster was still functional even after being dropped
the lawnmower is a bit rusty but still usable
an operational aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - in working order; able to operate; serving a purpose
  • Synonym: usable - able to be used; fit for use
  • Antonym: broken - not working; out of order

Etymology

From operation + -al; operation from Latin 'operatio' (work, effort).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02131634-s
Synset POS: s
