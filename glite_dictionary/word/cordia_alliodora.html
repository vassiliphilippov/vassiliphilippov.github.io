Cordia alliodora

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːr.di.ə ˌæl.i.oʊˈdɔːr.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.di.ə ˌæl.i.əʊˈdɔː.rə / (BrE)
A large tropical American tree of the genus Cordia, grown for its abundant creamy white flowers and valuable timber.
tropical American timber and ornamental tree

Examples

The wood of the Cordia alliodora is highly valued for furniture making.
In tropical regions, Cordia alliodora is often planted along roadsides for shade and decoration.
The creamy white flowers of the Cordia alliodora attract many pollinators during the blooming season.

Etymology

Cordia: named after German botanist Valerius Cordus. Alliodora: from Latin, meaning 'garlic-scented', referring to the odor of the wood.
