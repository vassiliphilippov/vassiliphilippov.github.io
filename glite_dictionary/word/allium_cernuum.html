Allium cernuum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm sərˈnuːəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm səːˈnjuːəm / (BrE)
A species of wild onion native to North America, with drooping clusters of white to rose-colored flowers.
species of wild onion native to North America

Examples

The distinctive drooping flowers of the Allium cernuum attract many pollinators in the summer.
Gardeners often plant Allium cernuum for its delicate beauty and resilience in native gardens.
The chef used the wild leaves of Allium cernuum to add a mild onion flavor to the dish.

Etymology

From Latin: 'allium' meaning 'garlic/onion'; 'cernuum' meaning 'nodding' or 'drooping'.
