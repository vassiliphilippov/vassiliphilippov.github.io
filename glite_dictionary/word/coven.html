coven

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvən / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ən / (BrE)
A group or assembly of witches who meet regularly, traditionally believed to number thirteen.
group of witches, often thirteen

Examples

The full moon was said to be the preferred gathering time for the local coven.
She wrote a novel about a mysterious coven living deep in the forest.
Rumors spread that a coven of witches conducted rituals in the abandoned house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convent - a community of religious people, especially nuns

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'covent', variant of 'convent', from Latin 'conventus' (assembly, meeting).
