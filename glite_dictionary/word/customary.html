customary

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌstəˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəməri / (BrE)
usual or typical of a particular person, place, or society; commonly practiced, used, or observed.
usual; commonly practiced

Examples

It is customary to shake hands when you meet someone for the first time.
She gave her customary polite smile before starting the meeting.
The restaurant will close on Sundays, as is customary in this region.

Synset Examples

his accustomed thoroughness
took his customary morning walk
his habitual comment
with her wonted candor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - done regularly or repeatedly; usual
  • Antonym: unusual - not common or ordinary; rare

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'customarius', from Latin 'consuetudinarius' (customary).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌstəˌmeri / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəməri / (BrE)
according to the usual customs or practices; typical or traditional for a particular situation
usual or traditional according to custom

Examples

It is customary for guests to bring a gift to the party.
He made his customary morning visit to the coffee shop.
A customary silence fell over the room before the big announcement.

Synset Examples

sealed the deal with the customary handshake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - done as a habit or regularly
  • Antonym: unusual - not usual, rare or uncommon

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'customarius' (pertaining to custom), from Latin 'consuetudo' (custom).
