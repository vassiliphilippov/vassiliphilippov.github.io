to fibrillate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪbrəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪbrɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a muscle, especially the heart) to make fine, rapid, irregular twitching movements.
make fine, irregular, rapid muscle movements
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the patient's heart could fibrillate unexpectedly during the operation.
When certain drugs are administered incorrectly, muscles may begin to fibrillate instead of contracting normally.
Doctors acted quickly when they saw the ventricles of his heart fibrillate on the monitor.

Synset Examples

His heart fibrillated and he died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiver - to shake with a slight rapid motion
  • Synonym: contract - (of muscles) to become shorter or tighter again and again

Etymology

From Latin 'fibrilla' (a small fiber), with influence from medical terminology.
