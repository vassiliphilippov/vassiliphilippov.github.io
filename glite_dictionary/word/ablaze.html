ablaze

1. ADJ

/ əˈbleɪz / (AmE), / əˈbleɪz / (BrE)
shining with bright color or light, resembling the appearance of flame
brightly colored or shining like flame

Examples

The hillside was ablaze with the vibrant colors of wildflowers in the spring.
By sunset, the sky was ablaze with streaks of orange and red.
During the parade, the entire street seemed ablaze with festive lights and decorations.

Synset Examples

maple trees ablaze in autumn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aflame - shining with the light or color of fire
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00403798-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ əˈbleɪz / (AmE), / əˈbleɪz / (BrE)
Glowing or illuminated with bright, often reddish light as if by fire.
glowing as if lit by flames

Examples

The sky was ablaze with the colors of the setting sun.
After the fireworks began, the city square was ablaze with light and excitement.
Her cheeks were ablaze after running in the cold wind.

Synset Examples

streets ablaze with lighted Christmas trees
the inflamed clouds at sunset
reddened faces around the campfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aflame - burning or glowing with flames
  • Antonym: dark - lacking light or brightness

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'a-' (on, in, into) + 'blaze' (flame).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00271362-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈbleɪz / (AmE), / əˈbleɪz / (BrE)
filled with intense excitement or enthusiasm; showing strong feelings, especially of excitement or desire
filled with excitement or enthusiasm

Examples

Her eyes were ablaze with anticipation as she waited for the announcement.
The crowd was ablaze with excitement during the closing moments of the game.
His face became ablaze when he heard the news of his promotion.

Synset Examples

his face all ablaze with excitement
he was aflame with desire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aflame - filled with excitement or desire
  • Antonym: calm - not showing excitement or emotion

Etymology

From a- (intensive prefix) + blaze (bright flame or display).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ əˈbleɪz / (AmE), / əˈbleɪz / (BrE)
burning fiercely with fire or filled with bright light; illuminated as if on fire
burning or brightly lit

Examples

The old barn was ablaze by the time the fire trucks arrived.
The city's skyline was ablaze with lights during the festival.
Her eyes were ablaze with excitement at the news.

Synset Examples

forests set ablaze (or afire) by lightning
even the car's tires were aflame
a night aflare with fireworks
candles alight on the tables
houses on fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aflame - burning or on fire
  • Synonym: alight - lit up; illuminated

Etymology

From a- (intensive prefix) + blaze (fire, flame); first recorded in the 18th century.
