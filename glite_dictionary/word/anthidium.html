Anthidium

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪdiəm / (AmE), / ænˈθɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of bees in the family Megachilidae, commonly known as potter bees, noted for their habit of constructing nests from plant fibers or resins.
a genus of potter bees

Examples

The Anthidium species are often called wool carder bees because of their nesting behavior.
Researchers discovered a new Anthidium near the Mediterranean coast.
Several gardens have attracted Anthidium due to their abundance of flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Megachilidae - a family of bees that includes leafcutter and mason bees
  • Degree Higher: bee - an insect that collects nectar and pollen, known for living in colonies

Etymology

From Greek anthos (flower) + idion (diminutive suffix), referring to their association with flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪdiəm / (AmE), / ænˈθɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of bees in the family Megachilidae, commonly known as potter bees, recognized for their habit of constructing woolly nests.
a genus of potter bees

Examples

The Anthidium genus includes bees known for their wool-collecting behavior.
Researchers discovered a new species of Anthidium in the Andean region.
Unlike honeybees, Anthidium bees build their nests from plant fibers and wool.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Megachilidae - family of solitary bees including leafcutter and mason bees
  • Synonym: potter bee - common name for bees in the genus Anthidium

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) + 'idion' (diminutive suffix), referencing their association with flowers.
