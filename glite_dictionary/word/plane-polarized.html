plane-polarized

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪnˌpɑːləˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌpɒləˌraɪzd / (BrE)
(of a wave, especially light) vibrating in a single plane.
vibrating in a single plane (of a wave)

Examples

A plane-polarized wave allows scientists to analyze properties of light more easily.
The experiment required a beam of plane-polarized light to obtain accurate results.
Under certain conditions, sunlight can become plane-polarized by reflection off water surfaces.

Synset Examples

plane-polarized light

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpolarized - (of light or other radiation) having vibrations in multiple planes
  • Degree Lower: circularly polarized - (of a wave) vibrating in a circular motion

Etymology

From 'plane' (a flat surface) + 'polarized', referring to orientation of vibration.
