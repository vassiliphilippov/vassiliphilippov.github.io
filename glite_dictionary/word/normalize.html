to normalize

1. VERB

/ ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something normal or restore it to a standard condition, typically by making it conform to a norm or regular pattern
make or restore normal; conform to a standard

Examples

Governments often try to normalize diplomatic relations after a period of conflict.
It took several hours for his blood pressure to normalize after the surgery.
Efforts are underway to normalize data entry procedures across all departments.

Synset Examples

normalize relations with China
normalize the temperature
normalize the spelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - make consistent with a standard or norm
  • Antonym: to destabilize - to make unstable or less normal

Etymology

From French *normaliser*, from Latin *normalis* (made according to a carpenter’s square) + English suffix *-ize*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469149-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to become normal or return to a normal state after being different or disrupted
become or return to normal

Examples

After a turbulent year, the economy began to normalize once confidence was restored.
Doctors hope the patient's blood pressure will normalize after treatment.
Relations between the two countries took time to normalize following the conflict.

Synset Examples

Let us hope that relations with this country will normalize soon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stabilize - to make or become steady or stable
  • Antonym: to destabilize - to make unstable or less normal

Etymology

Formed from 'normal' and the suffix '-ize', meaning to make or become normal.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469496-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a material, such as metal or glass, attain a desired consistency, texture, or hardness by gradually heating and then cooling it
heat-treat to desired hardness or texture

Examples

To ensure the steel frame is strong and flexible, manufacturers normalize the metal before use.
Modern production lines often normalize glass to remove internal stresses caused during shaping.
Engineers recommend that you normalize the alloy if you want uniformity in texture and hardness.

Synset Examples

temper glass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temper - to improve the hardness or elasticity of a material by heating and cooling
