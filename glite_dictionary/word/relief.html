relief

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlif / (AmE), / rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
(law) redress or remedy provided by a court, such as compensation or protection from harm.
court-ordered redress or remedy
legal

Examples

The plaintiff sought relief in the form of an injunction against the company.
After months of litigation, the court finally granted relief to the victims.
The judge considered what type of relief would be appropriate for the circumstances.

Synset Examples

was the relief supposed to be protection from future harm or compensation for past injury?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remedy - legal means for enforcing a right or redressing a wrong

10. NOUN

/ rɪˈlif / (AmE), / rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
a sculptural technique in which figures or designs are carved on a flat surface to project outward from the background
sculpture with raised shapes on a surface

Examples

The ancient temple walls were decorated with elaborate relief depicting historic battles.
Artists often use relief to add depth and interest to flat surfaces.
Many coins feature a relief of a famous person's profile.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bas-relief - a sculpture in which figures are only slightly raised from the background
  • Degree Higher: high relief - a sculptural form with figures deeply carved to stand out far from the background
  • Antonym: intaglio - a design engraved into a surface below the main surface level (opposite of relief)

Etymology

From Old French 'relief', from Latin 'relevare' (to raise or lift up).
