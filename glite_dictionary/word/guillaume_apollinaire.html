Guillaume Apollinaire

1. PROPN

/ ɡiːˈjoʊm ˌæpəˈlɪnɛr / (AmE), / ɡiːˈjəʊm ˌapɒlɪˈnɛə / (BrE)
(1880–1918) A French poet noted as a precursor of surrealism, known for his innovative poetry and influence on 20th-century literature.
French poet and precursor of surrealism

Examples

Guillaume Apollinaire is often credited with coining the term 'surrealism' in literature.
The poetry of Guillaume Apollinaire had a significant impact on modern French literature.
Many art historians study Guillaume Apollinaire for his relationship with Picasso and the Cubists.
