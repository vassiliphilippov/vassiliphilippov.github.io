1. ADJ

/ ˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˈaktɪv / (BrE)
(of the sun) having increased numbers of sunspots, solar flares, and radio emissions, indicating a period of heightened solar activity.

Examples

Astronomers observed that the sun was particularly active this year, with numerous flares disrupting communications.
During an active phase of the solar cycle, auroras can be seen farther from the poles.
Scientists monitor the sun for signs of an active period to predict space weather events.

Etymology

From Latin activus, from actus (a doing, act).

4. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv / (BrE)
The grammatical voice in which the subject of a verb performs the action or causes the event described by the verb.
grammatical voice where subject acts

Examples

Teachers often encourage students to use the active in their writing to make sentences clearer.
In the active, the subject typically comes before the verb, as in 'She wrote the letter.'
The active is generally preferred in scientific writing for its directness.

Synset Examples

‘The boy threw the ball’ uses the active voice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passive - the grammatical voice where the subject receives the action

Etymology
