tulip

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A plant with a large, brightly colored, cup-shaped flower, grown from a bulb and widely cultivated as an ornamental garden or cut flower.
a brightly colored spring-flowering plant

Examples

The tulip blooms in a variety of vibrant colors each spring.
She planted a row of red tulips along the garden path.
A single yellow tulip stood out among the purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daffodil - a spring-flowering plant with yellow or white petals and a central trumpet-shaped corona

Etymology

From French *tulipe*, from Turkish *tülbent* (turban), due to the flower's turban-like shape.
