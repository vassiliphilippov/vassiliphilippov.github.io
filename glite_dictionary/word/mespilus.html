Mespilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛspɪləs / (AmE), / ˈmɛspɪləs / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees in the rose family, notably including the medlar.
genus of small trees/shrubs (medlars)

Examples

The genus Mespilus is best known for the medlar fruit, which has been cultivated since Roman times.
Botanists have reclassified some species formerly included in Mespilus to related genera.
The Mespilus tree produces unique fruits that ripen in late autumn.

Etymology

From Latin *Mespilus*, from Greek *mespilon* (medlar).
