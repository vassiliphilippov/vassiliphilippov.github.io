Lathyrus niger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæθ.ə.rəs ˈnaɪ.dʒər / (AmE), / ˈlæθ.ɪ.rəs ˈnaɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
A perennial plant species native to Europe and North Africa, notable for its foliage that turns black when dried.
species of perennial plant

Examples

Lathyrus niger is often found growing in woodland clearings across Europe.
The dried leaves of Lathyrus niger turn a distinctive black color.
Botanists frequently study Lathyrus niger for its unusual pigmentation.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lathyrus' (genus of vetchlings), 'niger' meaning 'black'.
