Stygian

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɪdʒiən / (AmE), / ˈstɪdʒiən / (BrE)
Extremely dark, gloomy, or forbidding; relating to the River Styx or the underworld in Greek mythology.
dark and gloomy, hellish

Examples

She wandered through the Stygian gloom of the ancient forest, barely able to see a step ahead.
The power outage plunged the neighborhood into complete Stygian darkness.
His poetry often evokes Stygian images of despair and desolation.

Synset Examples

Hence loathed Melancholy … / In Stygian cave forlorn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infernal - of or relating to hell
  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing
  • Antonym: heavenly - of or relating to heaven, blissful

Etymology

From Latin Stygianus, from Greek Styx, the river of the underworld in Greek mythology.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɪdʒiən / (AmE), / ˈstɪdʒɪən / (BrE)
extremely dark, gloomy, or forbidding, especially in a way suggestive of the river Styx in Greek mythology.
extremely dark or gloomy; hellish

Examples

The abandoned house was plunged into a Stygian darkness as the sun set behind the hills.
A thick, Stygian mist crept across the graveyard, making everything seem eerie and forbidding.
He described the underground tunnels as a Stygian maze that no light could penetrate.

Synset Examples

in the depths of an Acheronian forest
upon those roseate lips a Stygian hue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Synonym: infernal - relating to or characteristic of hell or the underworld
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin Stygianus, from Greek Stygios, from Styx, the river of the underworld.
