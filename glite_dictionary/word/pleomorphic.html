pleomorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpliː.oʊˈmɔːr.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌpliː.əʊˈmɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism, cell, or structure) occurring in or exhibiting various distinct forms; characterized by pleomorphism.
having or showing various forms
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the tumor cells appeared pleomorphic in shape and size.
Some bacteria are pleomorphic, making them harder to identify using standard techniques.
The pathologist noted the pleomorphic nature of the samples, indicating a high-grade malignancy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monomorphic - having only one form

Etymology

From Greek pleo- (more) + -morph (form) + -ic (adjective suffix).
