St. Bride

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt braɪd / (AmE), / seɪnt braɪd / (BrE)
An Irish abbess and one of the patron saints of Ireland, also known as Saint Brigid of Kildare (c. 453–523).
Irish patron saint and abbess
british

Examples

The feast of St. Bride is celebrated on February 1st in Ireland.
Many churches in Scotland and England are dedicated to St. Bride.
Legends say that St. Bride was renowned for her generosity and miracles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Brigid - Irish patron saint, abbess, founder of Kildare

Etymology

St. Bride is an Anglicized form of 'Brigid', from Old Irish 'Brigit'.
