Enlil

1. PROPN

/ ɛnˈlɪl / (AmE), / ɛnˈlɪl / (BrE)
In Sumerian mythology, the god of the air and king of the gods.
Sumerian god of air and chief deity

Examples

In ancient texts, Enlil is often regarded as the supreme deity of the Sumerian pantheon.
Priests prayed to Enlil for favorable winds and good harvests.
The temple of Enlil in Nippur was a major religious center in ancient Mesopotamia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anu - sky god and father of the gods in Sumerian mythology
  • Synonym: Enki - god of water, knowledge, mischief, crafts, and creation in Sumerian mythology

Etymology

From Sumerian Enlil, possibly meaning 'Lord Wind'.
