Roccus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˈrɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Serranidae, which includes the common marine striped bass; formerly used in scientific classification.
genus of fish including the striped bass

Examples

The biologist studied several specimens of Roccus collected along the Atlantic coast.
Historically, the striped bass was classified under the genus Roccus before being reclassified.
Some older ichthyology texts refer to the fish as Roccus rather than Morone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Morone - genus now including species formerly classified as Roccus
