sardonyx

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdɑnɪks / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɒnɪks / (BrE)
a variety of onyx gemstone with parallel layers of sard (a reddish-brown mineral) and a differently colored mineral, typically white or black
striped variety of onyx gemstone

Examples

The museum displayed a rare sardonyx cameo from the Roman period.
She wore a ring set with an elegant sardonyx that caught the light beautifully.
Carved sardonyx was highly prized by ancient artists for making intaglios and cameos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onyx - a type of chalcedony with parallel bands of color

Etymology

From Latin *sardonyx*, from Greek *sardonux*, meaning a variety of onyx with reddish layers.
