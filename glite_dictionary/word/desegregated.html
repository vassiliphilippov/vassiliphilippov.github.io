desegregated

1. ADJ

/ ˌdiː.sɛˈɡreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌdiː.sɪˈɡreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a place, institution, or system) having had racial or other forms of segregation ended; open to all groups equally.
having segregation ended

Examples

After years of legal battles, the school became desegregated in the late 1960s.
Many communities are still striving for truly desegregated neighborhoods.
The company prides itself on maintaining a desegregated and inclusive workplace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: segregated - kept apart or separated, especially by race or class

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + segregate, from Latin segregatus (set apart).
