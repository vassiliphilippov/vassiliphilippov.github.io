low-set

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ sɛt / (AmE), / ˈləʊ sɛt / (BrE)
having a short height or positioned lower than usual, often short and thick in form; used to describe parts of the body, animals, or objects that are comparatively squat or near the ground.
short or positioned low; squat in form

Examples

The old cottage had a low-set door that forced visitors to bend as they entered.
Her low-set ears are characteristic of the breed.
Dachshunds are known for their long bodies and low-set frames.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - short and thick in form or low to the ground
  • Synonym: stumpy - short and thick or lacking length
  • Antonym: tall - having greater than average height
