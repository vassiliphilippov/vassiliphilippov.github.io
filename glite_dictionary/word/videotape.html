videotape

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊˌteɪp / (BrE)
a magnetic tape used to record visual images and sound, typically for use in video recording and playback devices
magnetic tape for video recording

Examples

My parents still have a videotape of their wedding from the 1980s.
The old videotape was damaged and couldn't be played anymore.
He transferred the footage from videotape to a digital format.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassette - a small case containing magnetic tape for use in a tape recorder
  • Antonym: DVD - digital optical disc for storing videos and audio

Etymology

Formed from 'video' (from Latin 'videre', to see) and 'tape'. Coined in the mid-20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪdioʊˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊˌteɪp / (BrE)
to record (a television program, event, etc.) on videotape
to record on videotape

Examples

We videotaped the entire wedding ceremony for family members who couldn't attend.
She videotapes every soccer game to analyze her performance afterward.
The teacher videotaped the experiment so the students could watch it again later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to preserve (sound or images) for later use

Etymology

From 'video' + 'tape', referring to recording images and sound onto magnetic tape.

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊˌteɪp / (BrE)
A video recording stored on magnetic tape, used especially before digital recording became widespread.
video recording on magnetic tape

Examples

We found an old videotape of my parents' wedding in the attic.
The documentary was recorded and distributed on videotape in the 1980s.
She transferred the content from videotape to digital format for preservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassette - a small, flat case containing magnetic tape for audio or video recording
  • Antonym: DVD - a digital video disc, used for storing video recordings

Etymology

Formed from 'video' (from Latin 'videre', to see) + 'tape' (referring to magnetic recording tape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04541815-n
Synset POS: n
