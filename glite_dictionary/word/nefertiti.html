Nefertiti

1. PROPN

/ ˌnɛfərˈtiːti / (AmE), / ˌnef.əˈtiː.ti / (BrE)
A queen of ancient Egypt and the wife of Pharaoh Akhenaten, renowned for her beauty and influence in the 14th century BC.
Egyptian queen, wife of Akhenaten

Examples

The famous bust of Nefertiti is displayed in a Berlin museum.
Historians study Nefertiti's influence on Egyptian art and religion.
Many tourists are fascinated by stories of Nefertiti and her mysterious disappearance.

Etymology

From Egyptian Nfr.t-jy.tj meaning 'the beautiful one has come'.
