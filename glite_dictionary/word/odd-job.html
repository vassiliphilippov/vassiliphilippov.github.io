odd-job

1. ADJ

/ ɑd dʒɑb / (AmE), / ɒd dʒɒb / (BrE)
(of work) not regular, skilled, or permanent; involving various casual or unskilled tasks.
not regular, skilled, or permanent work

Examples

He searched for odd-job work during the summer holidays.
Many people rely on odd-job opportunities when regular employment is scarce.
She took an odd-job position to help pay the bills.

Synset Examples

found only odd-job employment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casual - not regular or permanent, especially in employment
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent
  • Antonym: full-time - working the standard number of hours; opposite of occasional or irregular

Etymology

From the combination of "odd" (various, occasional) and "job" (task, work), originally referring to miscellaneous tasks.
