join

1. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to connect or fasten two or more things together, or to become connected
connect or link things

Examples

Please join the two wires by twisting them together.
The carpenter will join the wooden boards with glue.
Their hands joined as they walked across the street.

Synset Examples

join these two parts so that they fit together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to mix or bring together
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart

Etymology

From Latin 'jungere' (to yoke, join), via Old French 'joindre'.
