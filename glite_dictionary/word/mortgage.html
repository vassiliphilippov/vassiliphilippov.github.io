mortgage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
A legal agreement by which a bank or creditor lends money at interest in exchange for taking the title of the debtor's property, with the condition that the transfer of title becomes void upon the payment of the debt.
legal agreement for property loan security

Examples

After years of saving, they finally paid off their mortgage on the house.
The bank foreclosed on the property because he defaulted on his mortgage.
A fixed-rate mortgage provides predictable monthly payments for homeowners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loan - money that is borrowed and expected to be paid back with interest

Etymology

From Old French *morgage*, from 'mort' (dead) + 'gage' (pledge), meaning 'dead pledge'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɔr.gɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.ɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give property as security for a loan, so that the lender may take ownership if the loan is not repaid
give property as loan security

Examples

She decided to mortgage her house to pay for her children's college education.
The company had to mortgage several properties to raise the necessary capital.
He mortgaged his farm to secure a loan from the bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawn - to deposit an object as security for money lent

Etymology

From Old French mortgage (literally 'dead pledge'), from mort (dead) + gage (pledge).

Synset POS: v
