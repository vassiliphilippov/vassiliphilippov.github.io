stature

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstætʃə / (BrE)
The height of a person or animal when standing upright.
height of a standing person or animal

Examples

At six feet tall, his stature made him stand out in the crowd.
Giraffes are known for their impressive stature among animals of the African savanna.
The scientist measured the stature of each patient during the health survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: height - the measurement from base to top or head to foot

Etymology

From Latin 'statura' (height, appearance), from 'statuere' (to set up, establish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃər / (AmE), / ˈstatʃə / (BrE)
a high level of respect, importance, or reputation that someone has, often as a result of achievement or development
level of respect or reputation

Examples

She achieved international stature as a scientist after her groundbreaking research.
The company has gained stature over the past decade through innovation and leadership.
Despite his young age, his stature in the academic community is remarkable.

Synset Examples

a man of great stature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reputation - the beliefs or opinions that are generally held about someone or something
  • Synonym: prestige - respect and admiration felt for someone based on their achievements or quality
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From Latin 'statura' (height, size, stature), from 'stare' (to stand).
