Toulouse-Lautrec

1. PROPN

/ tuˌluːs loʊˈtrɛk / (AmE), / tuːˌluːs ləʊˈtrɛk / (BrE)
Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec (1864–1901), a French painter and printmaker best known for his vivid depictions of Parisian nightlife, especially the cafes and music halls of Montmartre.
French painter of Montmartre scenes

Examples

The works of Toulouse-Lautrec often capture the vibrant atmosphere of Parisian nightlife.
Many art lovers admire the unique style of Toulouse-Lautrec.
A famous poster by Toulouse-Lautrec hangs in the museum's gallery.

Etymology

From the full name Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec, a French family name.
