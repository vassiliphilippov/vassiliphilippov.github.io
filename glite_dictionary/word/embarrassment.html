embarrassment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛrəsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (BrE)
a state of financial difficulty or inadequacy; the condition of being short of money or resources
state of financial difficulty

Examples

The company faced an acute embarrassment when it could not meet its payroll obligations.
After his investments failed, Michael was left in a state of embarrassment.
To avoid financial embarrassment, she took out a modest loan from the bank.

Synset Examples

he is currently suffering financial embarrassments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - a situation that makes achieving something hard
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being successful or flourishing financially

Etymology

From French *embarras* (obstacle, hindrance), from Italian *imbarrazzo*, from *imbarrare* (to bar, block).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛr.əsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (BrE)
an extreme excess of something, especially one that causes difficulty in choosing; overabundance.
overabundance; extreme excess

Examples

There is an embarrassment of choices on the restaurant's menu, making it hard to decide what to order.
The team enjoys an embarrassment of talented players this season.
After the fundraiser, the charity was left with an embarrassment of donated goods.

Synset Examples

an embarrassment of riches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛrəsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (BrE)
an event or situation that causes someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed
event causing embarrassment

Examples

The failed presentation was a major embarrassment for the company.
He tried to laugh off the embarrassment of forgetting her name at the event.
Winning the award was a delight, but tripping on stage was an unexpected embarrassment.

Synset Examples

the outcome of the vote was an embarrassment for the liberals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliation - a feeling or event that makes someone feel very ashamed or foolish
  • Antonym: pride - a feeling or event that makes someone feel proud, opposite of embarrassment

Etymology

From French *embarras*, from Italian *imbarazzo* (obstacle, difficulty), from *imbarazzare* (to embarrass).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07320169-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness caused especially when one's inadequacy or guilt is exposed.
feeling of shame or awkwardness

Examples

He felt a surge of embarrassment when his mistake was pointed out in front of the whole class.
Her face turned red with embarrassment after she realized her shirt was inside out.
Despite the embarrassment of forgetting his lines, he continued his speech gracefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shame - a painful feeling of humiliation or distress caused by consciousness of wrong or foolish behavior
  • Antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements

Etymology

From French *embarras* (obstacle, hindrance), from Italian *imbarazzo*.
