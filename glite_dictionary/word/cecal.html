cecal

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːkəl / (AmE), / ˈsiːkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a cecum, the pouch at the beginning of the large intestine.
relating to the cecum (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted that the patient had an inflamed cecal appendix.
A cecal volvulus can cause bowel obstruction and requires prompt treatment.
Histological studies revealed cecal tissue showing signs of early infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cecum - a pouch forming the beginning of the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin caecum (blind, cecum), referring to the blind pouch at the start of the large intestine.
