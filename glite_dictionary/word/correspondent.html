correspondent

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrəˈspɑndənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈspɒndənt / (BrE)
a person who communicates with another by exchanging letters, especially regularly
someone who writes letters to another

Examples

As a young man, he was an avid correspondent, exchanging letters with friends across the world.
She became a lifelong correspondent of her favorite author after writing him a fan letter.
Each correspondent in the pen pal program writes at least one letter every month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen pal - a person with whom one exchanges letters regularly, especially as a hobby

Etymology

From Latin 'correspondere' (to correspond), via French 'correspondant'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌspɑn.dənt / (AmE), / kɒˌrɒs.pɒn.dənt / (BrE)
a journalist who is employed to report news for newspapers, magazines, radio, or television, often from a particular place or on a particular subject
journalist reporting for media organizations

Examples

The BBC's foreign correspondent filed a report from the war zone.
She became the chief political correspondent for the national newspaper last year.
A sports correspondent covered the Olympic Games live from Tokyo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reporter - a person who reports news stories; journalist
  • Antonym: editor - a person who is in charge of and determines the final content of a publication

Etymology

From Latin 'correspondentem', present participle of 'correspondere' (to correspond).
