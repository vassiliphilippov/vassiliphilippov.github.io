Sanguinaria canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæŋɡwɪˈnɛəriə kəˈnædɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsaŋɡwɪˈnɛːrɪə kəˈnadɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial woodland plant native to North America, having a red root and sap, a solitary lobed leaf, and a white flower in early spring; its rootstock has been used as a stimulant and expectorant.
North American woodland plant with red sap and white flowers

Examples

In the spring, patches of Sanguinaria canadensis can be seen blooming on the forest floor.
Traditional medicines often made use of Sanguinaria canadensis for its expectorant properties.
The white petals of Sanguinaria canadensis appear briefly before the trees fully leaf out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodroot - common name for Sanguinaria canadensis

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'sanguinarius' (of blood), referring to the red sap; 'canadensis' means 'of Canada'.
