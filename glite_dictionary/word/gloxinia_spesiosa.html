Gloxinia spesiosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑkˈsɪniə spɛˈsiːoʊsə / (AmE), / ɡlɒkˈsɪniə spɛˈsiːəʊsə / (BrE)
A South American herbaceous plant cultivated as a houseplant for its large, attractive leaves and colorful bell-shaped flowers.
ornamental South American houseplant

Examples

The vibrant purple flowers of the Gloxinia spesiosa made it the centerpiece of the living room.
Many gardeners prize Gloxinia spesiosa for its velvety leaves and striking bell-shaped blossoms.
My grandmother grew a Gloxinia spesiosa on her windowsill, and it bloomed every spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sinningia speciosa - the current botanical name for Gloxinia spesiosa

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Gloxinia' after Benjamin Peter Gloxin, an 18th-century German botanist; 'spesiosa' from Latin meaning 'showy'.
