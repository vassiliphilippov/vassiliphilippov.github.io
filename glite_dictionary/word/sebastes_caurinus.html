Sebastes caurinus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈbæstiz kɔːˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈbæstiz kɔːˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of rockfish found in the Pacific coastal waters of North America, commonly known as the copper rockfish.
species of Pacific rockfish

Examples

The Sebastes caurinus is commonly known as the copper rockfish.
Marine biologists study Sebastes caurinus to monitor the health of coastal ecosystems.
Anglers along the Pacific coast often hope to catch a Sebastes caurinus during their trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockfish - a general term for fish in the genus Sebastes

Etymology

Sebastes from Greek 'sebastos' (august); species name likely from Latinized form of a surname or descriptor.
