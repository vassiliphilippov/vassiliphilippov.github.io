isotope

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.səˌtoʊp / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sə.təʊp / (BrE)
One of two or more forms of a chemical element that have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons in their nuclei.
atom with same atomic number but different neutrons

Examples

Carbon-14 is a radioactive isotope commonly used in archaeological dating.
Scientists can trace the movement of nutrients using stable isotopes.
Uranium occurs in several isotopes with varying atomic masses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclide - a species of atom defined by its number of protons and neutrons

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + topos (place), referring to occupying the same place in the periodic table.
