chile

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːleɪ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
A very hot, finely tapering pepper known for its special pungency, used in cooking.
very hot pepper used in cooking

Examples

He added sliced chile to the salsa for extra heat.
This stew wouldn't be the same without a pinch of fresh chile.
Be careful when handling raw chile peppers, as their oils can irritate your skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - hot pepper used in cooking
  • Degree Lower: jalapeño - a medium-sized chili pepper
  • Degree Higher: habanero - a very hot variety of chili pepper

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'chile', from Nahuatl 'chīlli' (chili pepper).
