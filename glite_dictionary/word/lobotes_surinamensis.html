Lobotes surinamensis

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈboʊtiːz ˌsʊrɪnəˈmɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ləʊˈbəʊtiːz ˌsʊrɪnəˈmɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of percomorph fish, known as the tripletail, found in warm seas from Cape Cod to northern South America.
a species of tripletail fish

Examples

The Lobotes surinamensis is a prized catch among coastal anglers for its strong fight and delicious meat.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of the Lobotes surinamensis along the Gulf Coast.
A large Lobotes surinamensis drifted near the surface, resembling a floating leaf to avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tripletail - the common name for Lobotes surinamensis, a fish with three rounded fins

Etymology

From genus name Lobotes and specific epithet surinamensis, meaning 'from Suriname' in Latinized form.
