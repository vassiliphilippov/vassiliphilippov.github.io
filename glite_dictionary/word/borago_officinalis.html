Borago officinalis

1. NOUN

/ bəˈreɪgoʊ ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / bəˈreɪgəʊ ˌɒfɪˈsɪnælɪs / (BrE)
a hairy, blue-flowered annual herb native to Europe, used in herbal medicine and as a salad green or cooked vegetable
herb used in medicine and salads

Examples

Borago officinalis is often added to salads for its cucumber-like flavor.
Herbalists sometimes recommend Borago officinalis as a remedy for inflammation.
Gardeners grow Borago officinalis both for its edible leaves and its attractive blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borage - the common name for Borago officinalis, a European herb used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus 'Borago' (borage) and the Latin 'officinalis' (of the workshop, medicinal).
