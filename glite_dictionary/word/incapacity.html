incapacity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
the lack of ability, especially intellectual power, to do something or make decisions
lack of intellectual ability

Examples

The judge questioned the defendant's incapacity to understand the proceedings.
His mental incapacity prevented him from making important decisions on his own.
Social support is vital for people struggling with incapacity due to cognitive decline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inability - lack of ability or means to do something
  • Antonym: competence - ability to do something successfully or efficiently

Etymology

From Latin 'incapacitas', from 'in-' (not) + 'capacitas' (capacity).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to do something, especially due to a lack of physical or mental ability, power, or qualification
inability; lack of capacity

Examples

After the accident, his incapacity to walk unaided became apparent.
Her incapacity for making quick decisions affected the entire team.
The court declared his mental incapacity prevented him from managing his affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inability - the fact of being unable to do something
  • Antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incapacitas (incapability).
