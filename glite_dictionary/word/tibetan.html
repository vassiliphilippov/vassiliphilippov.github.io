Tibetan

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈbɛtən / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛtən / (BrE)
the language of the Tibetan people, spoken chiefly in Tibet and nearby regions
the language spoken in Tibet

Examples

She is learning to speak Tibetan fluently.
The ancient scriptures were written in classical Tibetan.
Many schools in Lhasa teach both Chinese and Tibetan languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibetan - a person from Tibet

Etymology

From Tibet + -an, indicating origin or relation.

2. NOUN

/ tɪˈbɛtən / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛtən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Tibet
person from Tibet

Examples

Many Tibetans have preserved their cultural traditions despite outside influences.
The government has launched new policies to help Tibetans in rural areas.
She studied the lifestyle of Tibetans living in the northern plateau.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibetan - the language spoken in Tibet

Etymology

From Tibet + -an, indicating origin or relation to Tibet.

/ tɪˈbɛt̬ən / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛtən / (BrE)
of or relating to Tibet, its people, or their language
of Tibet, its people, or language

Examples

The Tibetan landscape is famous for its high mountains and unique culture.
She wore a traditional Tibetan dress to the festival.
Researchers are studying Tibetan language and literature at the university.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
