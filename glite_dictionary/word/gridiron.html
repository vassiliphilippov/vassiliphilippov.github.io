gridiron

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪdˌaɪrn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪdˌaɪən / (BrE)
A frame of parallel metal bars used for grilling fish or meat over a fire.
cooking utensil for grilling food

Examples

He placed the steaks on the gridiron and began to cook them over the open flame.
After cleaning the gridiron, she used it to grill fresh fish for dinner.
The campfire meal required a sturdy gridiron to hold the heavy pieces of meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a device or utensil for cooking food over direct heat

Etymology

From Old French *gredil* (a gridiron) and *fer* (iron), ultimately from Latin *craticula* (small grid or grating).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪdˌaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪdˌaɪən / (BrE)
a field marked with regularly spaced parallel lines, on which American football is played
football playing field
american

Examples

Fans crowded the stadium to watch their team take the gridiron on a chilly autumn night.
The championship game was fought on the college's home gridiron.
After years away, he felt thrilled to step onto the gridiron again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: football field - the area where football is played

Etymology

Originally referred to a grid-like metal cooking grate; later applied to the lined appearance of football fields.
