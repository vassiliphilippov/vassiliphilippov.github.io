shagged

1. ADJ

/ ʃæɡd / (AmE), / ʃæɡd / (BrE)
having a rough, uneven surface or covered with long, hanging shags or tufts, typically giving a shaggy appearance
rough or shaggy in texture/surface

Examples

The old coat was shagged and rough to the touch after years of use.
They chose a shagged carpet for the living room, which felt soft underfoot.
The hikers rested against the shagged bark of an ancient pine tree.

Synset Examples

junipers with shagged trunks
shaggy rugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaggy - having long, thick, or unkempt hair or fibers

Etymology

From "shag," meaning a thick, coarse mass of hair or fiber; -ed form implies 'having shags'.
