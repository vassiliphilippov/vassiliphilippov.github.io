gipsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪp.si / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪp.si / (BrE)
(dated, sometimes offensive) A person who lives an unconventional, nomadic lifestyle, similar to the stereotyped behavior traditionally attributed to the Romani people.
person with a nomadic, unconventional lifestyle
archaic offensive

Examples

Although she was not a gipsy, her constant travels and disregard for convention made people think otherwise.
He led the life of a gipsy, moving from town to town without settling down.
In the novel, the protagonist is described as a gipsy because of her free spirit and nomadic ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bohemian - a person with artistic or unconventional habits
  • Synonym: nomad - a person who moves from place to place; wanderer

Etymology

Altered spelling of 'gypsy', derived from 'Egyptian', an old mistaken belief about the origin of the Romani people.
