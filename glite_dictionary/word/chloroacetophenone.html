chloroacetophenone

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːroʊˌæsɪtəfəˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəʊˌæsɪtəfəˈnəʊn / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as a tear gas, less potent than CS gas but with longer-lasting effects
a type of tear gas
medical

Examples

Police used chloroacetophenone during the riot to disperse the crowd.
Exposure to chloroacetophenone can cause intense eye irritation and tearing.
Compared to CS gas, chloroacetophenone acts more slowly but remains effective for a longer duration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear gas - a chemical substance that causes tearing and severe eye irritation
  • Degree Higher: CS gas - a more potent type of tear gas used for riot control

Etymology

From 'chloro-' (containing chlorine) + 'acetophenone' (an organic compound).
