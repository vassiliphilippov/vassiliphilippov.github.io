aetiologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːtiˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌiːtɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A medical specialist who studies the causes or origins of diseases.
disease cause specialist
medical

Examples

The aetiologist examined environmental factors that could have contributed to the outbreak.
Her research as an aetiologist has advanced the understanding of rare infectious diseases.
Hospitals often consult an aetiologist when a new illness emerges without a clear cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiologist - a specialist in the etiology of diseases (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'aitia' (cause) + 'logos' (study) via Late Latin 'aetiologia'.
