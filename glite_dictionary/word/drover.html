drover

1. NOUN

/ ˈdroʊvər / (AmE), / ˈdrəʊvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to move herds of cattle, sheep, or other livestock from place to place
person who drives livestock herds

Examples

The drover led his cattle across the dusty plain to the market.
During the nineteenth century, a skilled drover was essential for long journeys with large flocks.
After months on the road, the tired drover finally returned home with his herd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herdsman - a person who tends a herd, especially of cattle or sheep

Etymology

From drive + -er, influenced by the verb 'to drive'.
