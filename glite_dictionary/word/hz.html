Hz

1. NOUN

/ hɝts / (AmE), / hɜːts / (BrE)
the standard unit of frequency, equal to one cycle per second
frequency unit: one cycle per second

Examples

The sound had a frequency of 440 Hz, which is the standard pitch for tuning musical instruments.
This display refreshes at 60 Hz, providing smoother motion for videos.
Human hearing typically ranges from 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hertz - the SI unit of frequency, equal to one cycle per second

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'hertz', named after German physicist Heinrich Rudolf Hertz.
