unserviceable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɝː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
not able to be used or no longer fit for use
not able to be used

Examples

The old machinery was declared unserviceable after years of neglect.
Because of the damage, the road was completely unserviceable for several days.
Most aircraft are taken out of use once they become unserviceable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serviceable - fit for use; able to be used

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + serviceable (fit for use).

WordNet ID: oewn-02507643-s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɝː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
not usable or fit for service; not capable of functioning properly or being used as intended.
not fit for use or service

Examples

The old printer became unserviceable after years of heavy use.
Due to severe damage, the road was declared unserviceable until repairs could be made.
The mechanic informed us that the aircraft was unserviceable and could not be flown safely.

Synset Examples

unserviceable equipment may be replaced

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serviceable - capable of being used or of giving service
  • Synonym: usable - able to be used

Etymology

From un- (not) + serviceable, modeled after serviceable (fit for use).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02132035-a
Synset POS: a
