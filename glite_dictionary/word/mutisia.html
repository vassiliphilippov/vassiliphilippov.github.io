Mutisia

1. NOUN

/ muːˈtiːziə / (AmE), / mjuːˈtɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of South American shrubs or climbing plants with large flower heads and feathery pappuses, in the family Asteraceae.
genus of South American flowering plants

Examples

Several species of Mutisia are valued for their attractive flowers.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Mutisia in the Andes.
The feathery seeds of Mutisia aid in wind dispersal.

Etymology

Named after Spanish botanist José Mutis.

2. NOUN

/ muˈtiːziə / (AmE), / mjuːˈtɪziə / (BrE)
A genus of South American climbing or trailing plants in the daisy family, often grown for their showy flowers.
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

Several species of Mutisia are popular in gardens due to their attractive orange and pink flowers.
The botanist discovered a rare Mutisia plant while exploring the Andes.
Climbing over the fence, the vibrant Mutisia added color to the entire yard.

Etymology

Named after José Celestino Mutis, a Spanish botanist.
