Olea

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.li.ə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen trees and shrubs, including the olive, that have oily, one-seeded fruits.
genus of evergreen trees, incl. olive

Examples

The genus Olea includes the familiar olive tree, prized for its fruit and oil.
Botanists have identified several species in the Olea genus across Europe and Africa.
The leaves of some Olea species are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive - a small, oval fruit and its tree, Olea europaea

Etymology

From Latin *Olea* (olive tree), from Ancient Greek *elaía* (olive).
