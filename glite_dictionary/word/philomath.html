philomath

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləˌmæθ / (AmE), / ˈfɪləʊˌmæθ / (BrE)
A person who loves learning and studying; a lover of knowledge.
a lover of learning

Examples

As a true philomath, she spends her evenings reading about science and history.
The club attracted several philomaths eager to discuss new ideas.
His reputation as a philomath was known throughout the academic community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholar - a person who studies a subject in great detail
  • Antonym: ignoramus - a person who lacks knowledge or awareness in general

Etymology

From Greek 'philos' (loving) + 'mathēma' (learning).
