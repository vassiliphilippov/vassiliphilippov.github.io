IRS

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ ˌɑr ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌɑː ˈɛs / (BrE)
The government agency in the United States responsible for collecting federal taxes and enforcing tax laws.
U.S. federal tax collection agency
american

Examples

The IRS requires businesses to report all sources of income for tax purposes.
After filing his taxes, he received a letter from the IRS requesting additional information.
Many people fear an unexpected audit from the IRS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Internal Revenue Service - Full name of the IRS, U.S. tax agency

Etymology

Acronym for Internal Revenue Service, established 1918.
