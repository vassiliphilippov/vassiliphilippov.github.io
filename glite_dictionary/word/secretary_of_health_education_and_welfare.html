Secretary of Health, Education, and Welfare

1. NOUN

/ sɛk.rəˌtɛɹ.i əv hɛlθ ˌɛd.jʊˈkeɪ.ʃən ənd ˈwɛl.fɛɹ / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.rəˈtɛə.ri əv hɛlθ ˌed.jʊˈkeɪ.ʃən ənd ˈwel.feə / (BrE)
the head of the former United States Department of Health, Education, and Welfare, an executive department that existed from 1953 to 1979
former US government cabinet post (1953–1979)
american

Examples

The Secretary of Health, Education, and Welfare played a crucial role in shaping public policy during the 1960s.
Patricia Harris was appointed the last Secretary of Health, Education, and Welfare before the department was split.
Many reforms within healthcare and education originated under various Secretaries of Health, Education, and Welfare.
