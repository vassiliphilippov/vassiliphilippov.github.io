Cervus nippon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrvəs nɪˈpɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəs nɪˈpɒn / (BrE)
a species of small deer native to East Asia, especially Japan, with slightly forked antlers and a spotted coat
species of small spotted deer from East Asia

Examples

The Cervus nippon population in Japan has increased due to conservation efforts.
Researchers studied the behavior of Cervus nippon in their natural forest habitat.
The spotted coat of the Cervus nippon distinguishes it from other deer species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sika deer - common name for Cervus nippon, a small East Asian deer

Etymology

From Latin "Cervus" (deer) and Japanese "nippon" (Japan).
