frailty

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪlti / (AmE), / ˈfreɪlti / (BrE)
weakness in character or morals; a tendency to yield to temptation or lapse in judgment.
moral weakness; lack of moral strength

Examples

He prayed for strength to resist the frailty of temptation.
Human frailty makes us all vulnerable to mistakes.
Her confession revealed a moment of moral frailty in her otherwise upright life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards; moral excellence
  • Synonym: weakness - lack of strength or firmness, especially of will or character

Etymology

From Middle English frailete, from Old French fraileté, from Latin fragilitas, from fragilis (fragile).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪlti / (AmE), / ˈfreɪlti / (BrE)
The condition of being physically weak, especially because of old age or illness.
physical weakness, especially from old age

Examples

Her increasing frailty made it difficult for her to live alone.
The frailty of his health required special care and attention.
Old age often brings both wisdom and frailty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - the state of lacking physical strength or energy
  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong

Etymology

From Old French 'fraileté', from Latin 'fragilitas' (fragility).
