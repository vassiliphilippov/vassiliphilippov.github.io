Ornithogalum

1. PROPN

/ ɔrˌnɪθəˈɡɑːləm / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˈɡɑːləm / (BrE)
a genus of bulbous perennial plants in the asparagus family, commonly known as Star-of-Bethlehem, native to Eurasia and South Africa
genus of plants; Star-of-Bethlehem

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the delicate beauty of Ornithogalum in spring flower beds.
The bulbous plants known as Ornithogalum can be invasive if left unchecked in some regions.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Ornithogalum during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Star-of-Bethlehem - common name for Ornithogalum species

Etymology

From Greek 'ornis' (bird) and 'gala' (milk), referring to the appearance of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˌnɪθəˈɡeɪləm / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˈɡeɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial bulbous plants in the asparagus family, commonly known as Star-of-Bethlehem, with white or sometimes yellow flowers.
genus of bulbous plants (Star-of-Bethlehem)

Examples

Several species of Ornithogalum are popular ornamental plants in gardens for their star-shaped flowers.
The bulbs of Ornithogalum require well-drained soil and full sunlight to thrive.
Botanists debated whether Ornithogalum should be classified in the Hyacinthaceae or Asparagaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Star-of-Bethlehem - common name for plants in the genus Ornithogalum

Etymology

From Greek 'ornis' (bird) + 'gala' (milk), referring to the flower's color.
