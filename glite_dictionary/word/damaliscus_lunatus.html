Damaliscus lunatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæməˈlɪskəs luˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌdaməˈlɪskəs luːˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
a large African antelope, noted for its speed and distinctive markings, native to savannas and grasslands of southern and eastern Africa
a large, swift African antelope

Examples

The Damaliscus lunatus is renowned for being one of the fastest hoofed animals in the world.
Researchers observed a small herd of Damaliscus lunatus grazing in the savanna at dawn.
Wildlife documentaries often feature the striking appearance of the Damaliscus lunatus as it runs across the plains.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the former genus Damalis (from Greek damalis, 'heifer') and Latin lunatus 'crescent-shaped'.
