aversion

1. NOUN

/ əˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / əˈvɜːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act of turning oneself or one's gaze away from something.
the act of turning away
archaic

Examples

She showed her discomfort by a sudden aversion of her head.
His aversion from the unpleasant scene was plainly visible.
During the tense conversation, an aversion of her eyes signaled her unwillingness to engage.

Synset Examples

averting her gaze meant that she was angry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avert - to turn away (one's eyes or thoughts)

Etymology

From Latin aversio, from avertere (to turn away).

2. NOUN

/ əˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / əˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of dislike or opposition towards something or someone
strong dislike or opposition

Examples

She has an aversion to spicy food and always chooses the mildest dishes.
His strong aversion to risk kept him from investing in the stock market.
Many people have an aversion to public speaking, finding it stressful and intimidating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dislike - a feeling of not liking or approving of someone or something
  • Antonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another or others

Etymology

From Latin 'aversio' (a turning away), from 'avertere' (to turn away).
