Bufo canorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːfoʊ kəˈnoʊrəs / (AmE), / ˈbjuːfəʊ kəˈnɔːrəs / (BrE)
A species of toad, commonly known as the Yosemite toad, found in high meadows and forest edges of the Sierra Nevada mountains.
Yosemite toad species

Examples

The Bufo canorus is known for its distinctive mating calls in the springtime.
Biologists monitor populations of Bufo canorus to assess the health of Sierra Nevada wetlands.
Unlike other amphibians, Bufo canorus is adapted to the cold, high-altitude meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yosemite toad - Common name for Bufo canorus, a toad species of the Sierra Nevada

Etymology

From Latin: 'Bufo' meaning toad, 'canorus' meaning melodious or tuneful.
