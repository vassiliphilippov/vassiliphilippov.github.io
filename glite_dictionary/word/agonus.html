Agonus

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡoʊ.nəs / (AmE), / əˈɡəʊ.nəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Agonidae, comprising certain species of marine fish known as poachers.
genus of marine fish (Agonidae)

Examples

The genus Agonus includes several species of bottom-dwelling marine fish found in cold waters.
Scientists studying the Agonidae family often begin with an overview of Agonus.
A new species within the genus Agonus was recently discovered by marine biologists.

Etymology

From Greek agōnos, related to contest or struggle, adopted as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
