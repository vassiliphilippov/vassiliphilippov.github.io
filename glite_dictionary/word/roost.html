roost

1. NOUN

/ rust / (AmE), / ruːst / (BrE)
a place, such as a perch or support, where birds, especially domestic fowl, rest or sleep
a perch or place where birds rest or sleep

Examples

The chickens returned to their roost at sunset.
We built a new roost for the hens in the barn.
Birds often seek a high roost for safety from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a horizontal bar or branch on which a bird rests

Etymology

From Middle English 'roost', from Old English 'hrōst' (pole, perch for domestic fowl).

2. VERB

/ ɹust / (AmE), / ɹuːst / (BrE)
(of a bird) to settle or rest in a place, typically on a branch or similar perch, for the purpose of sleeping or resting.
(of a bird) settle or rest

Examples

At dusk, hundreds of crows roost in the old oak tree behind the house.
Small sparrows often roost together on telephone wires during cold nights.
We watched the flock of starlings roost on the barn roof as the sun set.

Synset Examples

The birds perched high in the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - to rest or sit on something high or narrow, like a branch

Etymology

From Middle English 'rooste' (a perch for birds), from Old English 'hrōst'.

3. VERB

/ ɹust / (AmE), / ɹuːst / (BrE)
(of a bird) to settle or rest in a place to sleep; (of a person or thing, often figurative) to come to rest or stay in a place.
to settle or rest in a place to sleep

Examples

At dusk, hundreds of birds roost in the tall trees near the lake.
During the storm, the chickens roosted safely inside their coop.
Every evening, bats roost together in the old barn rafters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - to rest or settle on something high or narrow

Etymology

From Middle English roste, related to Old English hrōst (perch for fowls).

4. NOUN

/ ɹuːst / (AmE), / ɹuːst / (BrE)
a shelter or place where birds perch, especially to sleep
place where birds perch or sleep

Examples

At dusk, the chickens returned to their roost for the night.
We built a small roost in the backyard for the pigeons.
The old barn's beams served as a perfect roost for owls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a place where a bird rests or sits

Etymology

From Middle English 'roost' or 'roust', from Old English 'hrōst' (perch or roof beam).
