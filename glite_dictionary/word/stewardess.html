stewardess

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːərdɪs / (AmE), / ˈstjuːədɪs / (BrE)
a woman employed on an aircraft to look after passengers and provide service on board; now generally replaced by 'flight attendant'
female flight attendant (dated)
archaic

Examples

The stewardess welcomed everyone aboard the plane with a warm smile.
In the 1960s, becoming a stewardess was considered a glamorous career for women.
My grandmother worked as a stewardess for an international airline before retiring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight attendant - a person who serves passengers on an aircraft

Etymology

Feminine form of 'steward,' modeled after words ending in '-ess' to indicate female gender.
