chanterelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃæn.təˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˌʃæn.təˈrel / (BrE)
a widely distributed edible mushroom, golden-yellow in color, with a smooth cap and a pleasant aroma, highly valued in cooking
golden-yellow edible mushroom

Examples

The chef sautéed fresh chanterelle mushrooms with garlic and herbs for a flavorful side dish.
We spent the morning foraging for chanterelles in the damp forest after a night of rain.
The golden color and delicate aroma of chanterelle mushrooms make them a prized ingredient in gourmet cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus
  • Synonym: morel - an edible wild mushroom with a honeycomb appearance

Etymology

French, from Latin 'cantharellus', diminutive of 'cantharus' (drinking vessel, cup, referring to the shape).
