distorted

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɔrtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːtɪd / (BrE)
changed from the original or intended meaning, often making it false or misleading.
altered from the true or intended meaning

Examples

The article presented a distorted view of what really happened.
Rumors often spread quickly, becoming more distorted with each retelling.
She was frustrated by the distorted interpretation of her words in the press.

Synset Examples

many of the facts seemed twisted out of any semblance to reality
a perverted translation of the poem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twisted - changed so that it is no longer true or original
  • Synonym: perverted - turned away from what is right or correct meaning
  • Antonym: accurate - correct and unaltered representation
  • Antonym: faithful - representing the original meaning exactly

Etymology

From Latin 'distortus', past participle of 'distorquere' (to twist apart).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɔrtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːtɪd / (BrE)
So badly formed or twisted out of shape that it appears ugly or unnatural.
badly out of shape; ugly

Examples

The sculpture had a distorted face, giving it a haunting appearance.
His vision became distorted after staring at the bright lights for too long.
Photographs taken through the cracked lens looked distorted and surreal.

Synset Examples

deformed thalidomide babies
his poor distorted limbs
an ill-shapen vase
a limp caused by a malformed foot
misshapen old fingers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misshapen - Badly or wrongly shaped
  • Synonym: deformed - Having a shape that is not normal, especially in a way that is considered unattractive or ugly

Etymology

Past participle of 'distort', from Latin 'distortus' (twisted apart).
