Convolvulus arvensis

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvɑlvjʊləs ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kənˌvɒlvjʊləs ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A weakly climbing perennial plant with white or pink flowers, native to Europe and considered an invasive weed in many areas.
a perennial weed; field bindweed

Examples

Convolvulus arvensis can quickly overrun fields and gardens due to its persistent growth.
Farmers struggle to control Convolvulus arvensis because of its extensive root system.
Known as field bindweed, Convolvulus arvensis is recognized as a noxious weed in many regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field bindweed - common name for Convolvulus arvensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'convolvulus' meaning 'bindweed' and 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field'.
