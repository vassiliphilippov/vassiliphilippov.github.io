savour

1. VERB

/ ˈseɪvər / (AmE), / ˈseɪvə / (BrE)
to taste or enjoy something slowly in order to fully appreciate its flavour or quality
taste or enjoy something fully

Examples

She paused to savour the taste of freshly baked bread.
He likes to savour every spoonful of soup slowly.
Take your time and savour the moment.

Synset Examples

savor the soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enjoy - to take delight or pleasure in something
  • Synonym: relish - to enjoy greatly; to take pleasure in

Etymology

From Old French savourer, from Latin sapere (to taste, to know).

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvər / (AmE), / ˈseɪvə / (BrE)
a distinctive taste or flavor, especially a pleasant or appetizing one
distinctive flavor or taste
british

Examples

The chef created a sauce with a rich savour that lingered on the palate.
She enjoyed the unique savour of the aged cheese in her sandwich.
A pinch of herbs added a new savour to the simple stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavor - the distinctive taste of a food or drink
  • Antonym: blandness - lack of strong taste or flavor

Etymology

From Old French 'savour', from Latin 'sapor' (taste), from 'sapere' (to taste).
