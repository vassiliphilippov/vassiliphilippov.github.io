raper

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈreɪpə / (BrE)
a person who forces another to have sexual intercourse without consent; a rapist
person who commits rape
offensive

Examples

The judge sentenced the raper to twenty years in prison for his crime.
She bravely testified against the raper in court.
The police are searching for the raper who attacked several women in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapist - a person who commits rape

Etymology

From the verb 'rape' + the agent noun suffix '-er'.
