ratter

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɾɚ / (AmE), / ˈratə / (BrE)
a dog, especially a breed of terrier, developed or used for catching rats.
a dog bred to catch rats

Examples

The farmer keeps a ratter on the property to control the rodent population.
Her family's ratter is incredibly quick and agile when hunting in the barn.
Many terrier breeds were originally bred as ratters in rural England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a breed of small, active dog originally bred for hunting vermin

Etymology

From rat + -er, indicating something that catches rats.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrætər / (AmE), / ˈrætə / (BrE)
a person who betrays or deserts their cause, party, religion, or friends; a traitor or turncoat
a person who betrays or deserts a cause or friend

Examples

He was branded a ratter after abandoning his longtime political allies.
No one trusted her anymore, suspecting she was a ratter in their midst.
During the conflict, becoming a ratter meant losing the respect of your peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something, such as a friend, cause, or principle
  • Synonym: turncoat - a person who switches to the opposing side or party

Etymology

From 'rat' (verb) meaning to betray or desert a party or cause, itself deriving from the noun 'rat' (the animal), with the sense of someone who deserts a sinking ship.
