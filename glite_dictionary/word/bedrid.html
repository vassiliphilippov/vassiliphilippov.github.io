bedrid

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛdˌrɪd / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌrɪd / (BrE)
Confined to bed because of illness or infirmity, especially over a long period.
confined to bed by illness
archaic

Examples

She cared for her bedrid grandmother until the end of her days.
After the accident, he became bedrid and required constant assistance.
The old journals tell the story of a bedrid noblewoman isolated in her chambers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedbound - unable to leave one's bed, typically due to illness or disability
  • Antonym: ambulatory - able to walk; not bedridden

Etymology

From Middle English 'bedrid', from Old English 'bedrida' ('bed-rider').
