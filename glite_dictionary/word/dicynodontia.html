Dicynodontia

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌsaɪnəˈdɒntiə / (AmE), / daɪˌsɪnəˈdɒntɪə / (BrE)
an extinct order of herbivorous therapsid reptiles, characterized by two tusks and a beaked jaw, that lived during the Permian and Triassic periods.
an extinct order of tusked therapsids

Examples

Fossils of Dicynodontia have been discovered on every continent, including Antarctica.
Paleontologists believe that Dicynodontia were among the most successful herbivores of the Permian period.
The extinction of Dicynodontia marked a significant change in prehistoric ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapsid - member of an extinct group of mammal-like reptiles

Etymology

From Greek di- (two), kynos (dog), and odont (tooth), referring to their two canine-like tusks.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌsaɪnəˈdɒntiə / (AmE), / daɪˌsaɪnɒnˈtɪə / (BrE)
a division of extinct herbivorous therapsid reptiles with two tusks, prominent in the late Permian and Triassic periods.
herbivorous therapsid reptile group (fossil)

Examples

Fossils of Dicynodontia have been found throughout the southern continents, showing their global distribution.
Many Dicynodontia species were well adapted to a herbivorous diet in arid environments.
The extinction of Dicynodontia marked the end of a major therapsid lineage in prehistoric times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapsid - a member of a group of synapsids including mammals and their ancestors

Etymology

From Greek di- (two), kynos (dog), and odontos (tooth), referring to their two canine-like tusks.
