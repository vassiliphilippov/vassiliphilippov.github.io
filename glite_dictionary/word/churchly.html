churchly

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɜrtʃli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃli / (BrE)
resembling, suggestive of, or appropriate to a church, especially in appearance, atmosphere, or character
resembling or appropriate to a church

Examples

The old cathedral had a churchly atmosphere that inspired awe.
She wore robes with a distinctly churchly elegance for the ceremony.
A churchly hush fell over the hall as the service began.

Synset Examples

churchlike silence
the pure fragrance of churchly incense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecclesiastical - relating to the Christian Church or its clergy

Etymology

From 'church' + '-ly', forming adjectives related to church things.
