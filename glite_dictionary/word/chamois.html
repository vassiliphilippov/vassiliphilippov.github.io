chamois

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmi / (AmE), / ˈʃæmi / (BrE)
a soft, pliable leather made originally from the skin of the chamois antelope, but now also made from sheepskin, used especially for cleaning and polishing
soft leather used for cleaning and polishing

Examples

He used a chamois to dry off the car after washing it.
The soft texture of chamois leather makes it ideal for cleaning delicate surfaces.
She polished her shoes with a piece of chamois for a brilliant shine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suede - a type of soft leather with a napped finish

Etymology

From French 'chamois', from Late Latin 'camox' (chamois, wild goat).

2. NOUN

/ ʃæˈmwɑː / (AmE), / ˈʃæmwɑː / (BrE)
A small agile mountain antelope of Eurasia with short hooked horns, found in the Alps and other mountain ranges.
mountain-dwelling antelope of Eurasia

Examples

The herd of chamois gracefully navigated the rocky cliffs.
Sightings of chamois are common in the European Alps during the early morning.
A single chamois stood motionless, blending into the rocky mountain landscape.

Etymology

From French 'chamois', from Late Latin 'camox', referring to a type of wild goat.
