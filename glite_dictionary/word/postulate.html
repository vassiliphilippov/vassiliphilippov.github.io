postulate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or assume the existence, fact, or truth of something as a basis for reasoning, discussion, or belief
assume or suggest as a basis

Examples

The scientist chose to postulate the existence of a new particle to explain the data.
We often postulate certain rules when developing mathematical theories.
Their hypothesis postulates a link between stress and heart disease.

Synset Examples

He posited three basic laws of nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assume - to accept something as true without proof
  • Synonym: to suppose - to think or believe something is true or possible
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true or does not exist

Etymology

From Latin postulat-, past participle of postulare (to ask, demand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00718663-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
A proposition that is assumed to be true without proof and is used as a basis for reasoning, argument, or calculation.
proposition assumed true for reasoning

Examples

The entire theory is built upon a single core postulate accepted without proof.
Euclid's fifth postulate is central to the development of classical geometry.
Before proceeding, the mathematician stated an important postulate that underpins the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiom - a statement or principle that is accepted as true without proof
  • Degree Lower: hypothesis - a supposition made as a basis for reasoning

Etymology

From Latin postulat- ‘asked, demanded’, from postulare ‘to ask’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06766013-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpɑːstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or assume the existence, fact, or truth of something as a basis for reasoning or argument; to assert or maintain as a fact.
to assert or assume as true

Examples

The scientist postulated that life could exist under extreme conditions.
Several philosophers postulate the existence of a higher reality.
She postulated that the results were due to an unknown factor.

Synset Examples

He contended that Communism had no future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true or does not exist

Etymology

From Latin postulat-, past participle of postulare (to ask, demand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00758943-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈpɑːstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to require or assume something as a necessary condition or prerequisite
require as necessary

Examples

The proposed policy changes postulate a significant increase in government funding.
International law sometimes postulates the cooperation of all member states.
Implementing this theory postulates a high level of technical expertise.

Synset Examples

It takes nerve to do what she did
success usually requires hard work
This job asks a lot of patience and skill
This position demands a lot of personal sacrifice
This dinner calls for a spectacular dessert
This intervention does not postulate a patient's consent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to require - to need for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: demand - to ask for as necessary or obligatory

Etymology

From Latin postulat-, past participle of postulare (to demand, request).
