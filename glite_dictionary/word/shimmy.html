shimmy

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
to shake or wobble the body or part of the body quickly from side to side, especially as a dance move
move or shake body in a dance

Examples

The dancers began to shimmy across the stage in perfect unison.
She loves to shimmy when her favorite song plays at parties.
During the performance, he shimmied with so much energy that the crowd cheered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly up and down or from side to side

Etymology

Probably from imitative origin, echoing the shaking movement of the dance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01759233-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
a lively dance, typically performed to ragtime or jazz music, involving vigorous shaking of the shoulders and hips
vigorous ragtime/jazz dance with shaking movements

Examples

The Charleston and the shimmy were popular dances during the Roaring Twenties.
She learned to shimmy at a jazz club downtown.
The band played upbeat ragtime tunes, encouraging everyone to try the shimmy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly up and down or from side to side with rapid movements

Etymology

Probably imitative of the shaking movement involved in the dance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00534572-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
to shake or tremble with a quick or slight movement
to tremble or shake

Examples

The car began to shimmy as it moved over the rough road.
She could feel her hands shimmy with nervous energy before the speech.
The old washing machine would shimmy across the floor during the spin cycle.

Synset Examples

His voice wobbled with restrained emotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety or excitement

Etymology

Originated in early 20th century American English, possibly from the dance of the same name.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01875539-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
an abnormal shaking or wobbling movement, especially in the front wheels or steering of a motor vehicle
abnormal wobble in a vehicle's front wheels

Examples

The old truck developed a pronounced shimmy whenever it hit a pothole.
After replacing the tires, the shimmy in the steering wheel disappeared.
If you feel a shimmy while driving, it's best to have your alignment checked.

Synset Examples

he could feel the shimmy in the steering wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobble - an unsteady, shaking movement

Etymology

Originally from the name of a dance characterized by fast shaking movements, possibly from the English dialect noun 'shim' (to shake or tremble), later adopted in automotive use.

Source: WordNet

sleeveless undergarment for women
