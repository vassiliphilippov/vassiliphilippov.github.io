bluebird

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A fruit-eating songbird with mostly brilliant blue plumage, found in the East Indies.
fruit-eating blue songbird of the East Indies

Examples

The striking color of the bluebird makes it easy to spot among the jungle foliage.
Researchers have studied the diet of the bluebird in the East Indies for decades.
A flock of bluebirds flew over the canopy, their bright plumage flashing in the sunlight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A small North American songbird with blue or blue and red plumage, belonging to the thrush family.
North American blue songbird

Examples

A brightly colored bluebird perched on the fence, singing a cheerful tune.
Many birdwatchers visited the park in hopes of spotting an Eastern bluebird.
The bluebird's vivid plumage stood out against the brown branches.
