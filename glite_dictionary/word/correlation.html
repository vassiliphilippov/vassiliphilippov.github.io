correlation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure that indicates the extent to which two or more variables fluctuate together, usually expressed as a value between -1 and 1
statistical relationship between variables

Examples

The study showed a strong correlation between hours studied and exam scores.
Researchers found little correlation between diet and the risk of the disease.
A negative correlation was observed between age and physical activity levels.

Synset Examples

what is the correlation between those two variables?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: association - a connection or relationship between things
  • Antonym: causation - the action of causing something; not the same as correlation

Etymology

From Latin ‘correlatio’, from ‘co-’ (together) + ‘relatio’ (relation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things, especially a statistical relationship in which changes in one variable are associated with changes in another
mutual or statistical relationship between things

Examples

The study found a strong correlation between exercise and overall health.
There is little correlation between the color of a car and its speed.
Analysts observed a negative correlation between unemployment and consumer spending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - a relationship in which a person or thing is linked or associated with something else
  • Antonym: causation - the action of causing something; the relationship between cause and effect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'correlatio', from Latin 'com-' (together) + 'relatio' (bringing back, reference).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things, where one thing affects or is associated with another
mutual relationship or connection

Examples

There is a strong correlation between hours studied and exam results.
Scientists found a positive correlation between exercise and improved mental health.
The data showed no significant correlation between diet and the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - a relationship in which things are linked or associated
  • Synonym: association - a connection or relationship between things or ideas
  • Antonym: causation - the action of causing something; the relationship between cause and effect

Etymology

From Latin correlatio, from com- (together) + relatio (reference, relation).
