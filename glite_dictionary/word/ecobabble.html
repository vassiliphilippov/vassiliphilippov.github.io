ecobabble

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːkoʊˌbæbəl / (AmE), / ˈiːkəʊˌbæbəl / (BrE)
language using ecological or environmental jargon, often to give an impression of concern for the environment without genuine understanding
empty ecological jargon

Examples

The company's report was filled with ecobabble that sounded impressive but meant very little.
Politicians often resort to ecobabble to appeal to environmentally conscious voters.
She criticized the advertisement for its excessive use of ecobabble instead of presenting real solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - confused or meaningless speech

Etymology

Blend of 'ecology' and 'babble'.
