extroversive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strəˈvɜr.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈvɜː.sɪv / (BrE)
Directed outward; showing interest in others or being concerned with external reality.
outward-directed; interested in others

Examples

Her extroversive personality made her popular at social gatherings.
An extroversive child tends to seek out friends and new experiences.
The team valued his extroversive approach to problem-solving, which brought fresh ideas from outside sources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introversive - directed inward; focused on internal thoughts or feelings
  • Synonym: extroverted - outgoing and socially confident

Etymology

From 'extrovert' + '-ive', influenced by Latin 'extra' (outside) and 'vertere' (to turn).
