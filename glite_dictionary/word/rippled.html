rippled

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪpəld / (AmE), / ˈrɪpəld / (BrE)
having small waves or undulations, often as caused by wind or water movement.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪpəld / (AmE), / ˈrɪpəld / (BrE)
Having small waves or undulating wrinkles on the surface; marked by a series of gentle curves or ridges.
having small waves or wrinkles

Examples

The lake's surface was rippled by a gentle breeze.
She admired the rippled texture of the sand along the shore.
His reflection appeared distorted in the rippled glass window.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from perceptible projections, lumps, or indentations
  • Synonym: wavy - having a series of curves; undulating

Etymology

From 'ripple' (verb/noun), which may be imitative in origin.
