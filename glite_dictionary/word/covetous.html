covetous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌvətəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪtəs / (BrE)
having a strong desire to possess something, especially something that belongs to someone else
having a strong desire for others' possessions

Examples

His covetous gaze lingered on the expensive car in the driveway.
They accused him of being covetous of his neighbor's good fortune.
The covetous look in her eyes made everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

he was never covetous before he met her
jealous of his success and covetous of his possessions
envious of their art collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envious - feeling or showing envy
  • Synonym: jealous - feeling or showing jealousy

Etymology

From Old French *covetous*, from covetier 'to desire', from Latin *cupiō* 'I desire'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌv.ə.təs / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
having a strong, often excessive desire to possess something, especially the property or wealth of others
having a strong desire for others' possessions

Examples

He looked at his neighbor's new car with a covetous expression.
Her covetous ambitions led her to betray her friends.
Business rivals cast covetous eyes on the lucrative contract.

Synset Examples

they are avaricious and will do anything for money
casting covetous eyes on his neighbor's fields
a grasping old miser
grasping commercialism
greedy for money and power
grew richer and greedier
prehensile employers stingy with raises for their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greedy - having an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or food
  • Synonym: avaricious - having an extreme desire for wealth; greedy
  • Antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected

Etymology

From Old French covetous, from covetier (to desire), from Latin cupiditas (desire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00030046-s
Synset POS: s
