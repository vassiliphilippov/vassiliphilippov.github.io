Vivaldi

1. PROPN

/ viˈvɑl.di / (AmE), / vɪˈvæl.di / (BrE)
(1678–1741) An Italian Baroque composer and violinist, famous for his concertos, especially 'The Four Seasons'.
Italian Baroque composer and violinist

Examples

Vivaldi composed hundreds of concertos during his lifetime, many of which are still performed today.
The orchestra played a beautiful rendition of Vivaldi's 'Spring' from The Four Seasons.
Many music students study the works of Vivaldi as part of their education.
