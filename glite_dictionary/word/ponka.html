Ponka

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɒŋkə / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋkə / (BrE)
the Dhegiha dialect spoken by the Ponca people, a Native American tribe
Dhegiha dialect of the Ponca people

Examples

Linguists have studied the structure of Ponka to understand its place among Siouan languages.
Many elders in the community are fluent speakers of Ponka.
The tribe offers Ponka language classes to preserve their cultural heritage.

Etymology

From Ponca, the name of the Native American tribe associated with the language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑŋkə / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋkə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American Siouan people originally from the Missouri river valley in northeastern Nebraska.
member of a Native American Siouan people

Examples

The Ponka have a rich tradition of storytelling passed down through generations.
Several Ponka leaders played important roles in the history of Nebraska.
Many Ponka artists contribute to preserving their cultural heritage today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - relating to a family of North American Indian peoples
