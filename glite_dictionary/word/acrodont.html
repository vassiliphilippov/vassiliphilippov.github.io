acrodont

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.rəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / ˈæk.rə.dɒnt / (BrE)
An animal whose teeth are attached to the crest of the jawbone without sockets.
animal with teeth attached to jaw crest
medical

Examples

Some lizards are classified as acrodonts because their teeth are fused to the top of the jawbone.
The acrodont condition differs from pleurodont and thecodont types of tooth attachment.
Paleontologists identified the fossil as an acrodont based on the structure of the jaw and teeth.

Etymology

From Greek akron (summit) + odont- (tooth).
