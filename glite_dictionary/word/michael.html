Michael

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkəl / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the archangel regarded as the protector of Israel and leader of the heavenly hosts.
archangel in the Bible

Examples

In many paintings, Michael is depicted defeating Satan.
The Book of Daniel refers to Michael as the great prince who protects the people.
Many Christians pray to Michael for protection from evil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gabriel - another principal archangel in the Bible

Etymology

From Hebrew *Mīkhā'ēl* meaning 'Who is like God?'.
