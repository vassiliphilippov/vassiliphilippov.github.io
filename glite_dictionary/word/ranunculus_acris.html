Ranunculus acris

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs ˈækrɪs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjələs ˈakrɪs / (BrE)
a perennial European buttercup with bright yellow spring flowers, widely naturalized in North America and elsewhere
a species of yellow-flowered European buttercup

Examples

The field was dotted with Ranunculus acris, adding a splash of yellow to the green grass.
Gardeners often try to control the spread of Ranunculus acris in meadows and lawns.
In spring, Ranunculus acris blooms profusely, creating a beautiful but sometimes invasive display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - any plant of the genus Ranunculus, typically with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ranunculus' (diminutive of 'rana', frog) and 'acris' (sharp, pungent).
