thumbscrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌskru / (AmE), / ˈθʌmˌskruː / (BrE)
a screw designed to be turned easily with the thumb and fingers, without the need for tools
screw turned by hand

Examples

Tightening the thumbscrew on the camera tripod secured it in place.
He replaced the rusty thumbscrew with a new one for easier adjustments.
The panel can be removed quickly by loosening the thumbscrew at the back.
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌskru / (AmE), / ˈθʌmˌskruː / (BrE)
a device used as an instrument of torture, designed to crush the thumb by tightening a screw
instrument of torture for crushing thumbs

Examples

The prisoner was threatened with the thumbscrew if he refused to answer.
Historical accounts describe the use of the thumbscrew during interrogations.
The museum displayed an original thumbscrew among its collection of ancient torture devices.
