technician

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a person whose job involves special training or skill in a specific technical process or activity
person trained in a technical skill

Examples

The hospital hired a new technician to maintain the medical equipment.
Every technician on the team specializes in a different type of software installation.
As a laboratory technician, she ensures that all tests are performed accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineer - person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or public works

Etymology

From French 'technicien', based on Greek 'tekhnē' (art, craft).

2. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is highly skilled in a particular intellectual or artistic technique
person skilled in a technique

Examples

She is a technician at the piano, impressing audiences with her flawless performances.
The laboratory hired a technician known for his skill in delicate procedures.
Although he is a great artist, critics say he is more of a technician than a visionary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person who has a comprehensive and authoritative knowledge of or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: amateur - a person who engages in a pursuit, especially a sport, on an unpaid basis or without professional skill

Etymology

From French *technicien*, from Greek *technē* (art, skill) + -ian.

WordNet ID: oewn-10716004-n
Synset POS: n
