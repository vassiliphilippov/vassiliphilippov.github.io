Hypoxidaceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɑksɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒksɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, sometimes treated as part of the Amaryllidaceae, including herbaceous species found in warm and tropical regions
family of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The Hypoxidaceae are recognized for their yellow, star-shaped flowers.
Recent studies have clarified the classification of the Hypoxidaceae within monocots.
Botanists often debate whether Hypoxidaceae should be included in Amaryllidaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amaryllidaceae - a plant family sometimes including Hypoxidaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from the genus name Hypoxis and the taxonomic suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɑksɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒksɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of perennial flowering plants, typically including small herbaceous species with grass-like leaves, sometimes treated as part of the Amaryllidaceae
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The Hypoxidaceae are known for their star-shaped yellow flowers found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Some botanists classify Hypoxidaceae within the larger Amaryllidaceae family.
Members of the Hypoxidaceae often thrive in grasslands and forest clearings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amaryllidaceae - a family of flowering plants to which Hypoxidaceae is sometimes assigned

Etymology

From genus name Hypoxis + Latin -aceae (a family suffix).
