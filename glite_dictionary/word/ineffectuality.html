ineffectuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɪˌfɛk.tʃuˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˌfek.tjuˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being incapable of producing the desired effect; lack of effectiveness
quality of being ineffective

Examples

His ineffectuality as a leader became apparent during the crisis.
The project failed due to the ineffectuality of its planning and execution.
Her ineffectuality in negotiations frustrated her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effectiveness - the degree to which something is successful in producing a desired result
  • Synonym: ineffectiveness - the condition of not achieving intended results

Etymology

From in- (not) + effectual (from Latin effectualis, effective) + -ity.
