loaner

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnər / (AmE), / ˈləʊnə / (BrE)
a person or organization that lends money or extends credit, typically as part of a business transaction
person or entity that lends money

Examples

The loaner agreed to give us additional time to repay the debt.
Banks act as a loaner when providing mortgages to customers.
If you can't find a traditional loaner, some online platforms offer peer-to-peer lending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lender - a person or organization that gives out loans
  • Antonym: borrower - a person or organization that receives something on loan

Etymology

From 'loan' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnər / (AmE), / ˈləʊnə / (BrE)
a car that is lent temporarily as a replacement when one's own vehicle is being repaired
temporary replacement car

Examples

My mechanic gave me a loaner while they fixed my transmission.
The dealership provided a loaner so I could get to work while my car was in the shop.
She drove a loaner for two days after her accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement car - a car provided as a substitute when another is out of service

Etymology

From 'loan' + '-er', denoting something or someone that loans or is loaned.

Synset POS: n
