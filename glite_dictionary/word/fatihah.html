Fatihah

1. NOUN

/ fæˈtiː.hɑː / (AmE), / fæˈtiː.hə / (BrE)
The first sura (chapter) of the Quran, regarded as the central prayer in Islam and recited during all daily prayers and special occasions.
first chapter of the Quran; Islamic prayer

Examples

Many Muslims recite the Fatihah as part of their daily prayers.
The imam began the service by reciting the Fatihah.
She taught her children to memorize and understand the Fatihah from a young age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: al-Fatiha - alternative spelling of the same sura, sometimes with definite article

Etymology

From Arabic fātiḥa (opening), from fataḥa (to open).
