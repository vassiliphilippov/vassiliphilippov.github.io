articulatio spheroidea

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃioʊ sfɛrˌɔɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃiəʊ sfɪərˌɔɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
A type of freely moving joint where the spherical head of one bone fits into a rounded cavity of another bone, allowing movement in multiple directions; a ball-and-socket joint.
anatomical term for ball-and-socket joint
medical

Examples

The shoulder is an example of an articulatio spheroidea, permitting rotational movement.
In anatomy lectures, the hip joint is often described as an articulatio spheroidea.
Damage to the articulatio spheroidea can severely restrict a person's range of motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ball-and-socket joint - a joint allowing movement in all directions, like the hip or shoulder

Etymology

From Latin 'articulatio' (joint) and 'spheroidea' (spherical).
