tragical

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrædʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪkəl / (BrE)
very sad or distressing, especially involving grief, death, or destruction; archaic form of 'tragic'.
very sad or involving tragedy (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The play ended with a tragical scene that moved the audience to tears.
Her life took a tragical turn after the accident.
He spoke in a tragical tone, as if mourning a great loss.

Synset Examples

a tragic face
a tragic plight
a tragic accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tragic - causing or characterized by extreme distress or sorrow

Etymology

From Middle English tragedical, from Latin tragicus (tragic) + -al.
