redeeming

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
serving to save someone from sin, error, or evil; making someone or something better or more acceptable
saving from sin or error

Examples

The novel explores the redeeming power of love and forgiveness.
His charity work was seen as a redeeming quality in his otherwise troubled life.
She searched for some redeeming feature in his behavior.

Synset Examples

saving faith
redemptive (or redeeming) love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redemptive - serving to redeem or save from error or evil

Etymology

From Latin redimere (to buy back, redeem), via Old French redimer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01134233-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈdiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
serving to compensate for faults or defects; making something bad or unpleasant seem better or more acceptable
compensating for a fault or defect

Examples

The only redeeming feature of the movie was the stunning cinematography.
Despite his rudeness, his generosity was a redeeming quality.
The weather was terrible, but the hotel’s excellent service was a redeeming aspect of our trip.

Synset Examples

the redeeming feature of the plan is its simplicity
his saving grace was his sense of humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saving - serving to compensate for a fault or defect

Etymology

From the verb 'redeem', ultimately from Latin 'redimere' (to buy back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01128372-s
Synset POS: s
