to debilitate

1. VERB

/ dəˈbɪləˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something physically weak or less effective
make physically or mentally weak

Examples

The disease can debilitate even the healthiest individuals.
Years of hard labor began to debilitate his body.
The long drought will debilitate the local agriculture if it continues.

Synset Examples

Life in the camp drained him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make or become less strong
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Latin debilitare (to weaken), from debilis (weak, feeble).
