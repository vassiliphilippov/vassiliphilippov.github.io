hydrophobic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
having an abnormal or extreme fear of water
abnormally afraid of water
medical

Examples

Some children with autism can be hydrophobic and refuse to participate in swimming lessons.
Because of her hydrophobic tendencies, she becomes anxious even near small puddles.
The patient was diagnosed as hydrophobic following a traumatic water-related incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquaphobic - having a fear of water

Etymology

From Greek hydor (water) + phobos (fear).

WordNet ID: oewn-00080688-s

2. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
tending to repel or fail to mix with water; not absorbing or dissolving in water
repelling or not absorbing water

Examples

The duck's feathers are naturally hydrophobic, keeping it dry in the water.
Some fabrics are treated to become hydrophobic so they resist stains and moisture.
This chemical coating is highly hydrophobic and prevents water from adhering to the surface.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hydrophilic - tending to attract and mix with water
  • Synonym: water-repellent - resistant to absorbing water

Etymology

From Greek hydrophobos, from hydro- (water) + phobos (fear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00493794-a
Synset POS: a
