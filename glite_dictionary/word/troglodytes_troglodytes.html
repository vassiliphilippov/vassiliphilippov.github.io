Troglodytes troglodytes

1. NOUN

/ ˌtroʊɡləˌdaɪtiːz ˌtroʊɡləˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌtrɒɡləˌdaɪtiːz ˌtrɒɡləˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A species of small wren native to the Northern Hemisphere, especially found in coniferous forests; also known as the Eurasian wren.
small Eurasian wren (bird species)

Examples

The Troglodytes troglodytes is often heard before it is seen, thanks to its loud, trilling song.
In spring, birdwatchers gather to observe the Troglodytes troglodytes as it builds its tiny nests.
Despite its small size, the Troglodytes troglodytes is known for its bold and lively behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - a small bird with a short upright tail

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name from Greek 'trōglodutēs', meaning cave-dweller.
