batten

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætən / (AmE), / ˈbæt(ə)n / (BrE)
a soft filling material made of rolls or sheets of cotton wool or synthetic fiber, used as stuffing, especially in upholstery or bedding
soft stuffing material for upholstery or bedding

Examples

The chair was padded with a thick batten for added comfort.
She replaced the old batten in the quilt to make it warmer.
Upholsterers commonly use synthetic batten as stuffing for cushions.

2. VERB

/ ˈbætən / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ən / (BrE)
to furnish or cover with battens, especially to reinforce or secure something such as hatches or doors
to furnish or cover with battens

Examples

The crew worked quickly to batten down the hatches before the storm hit.
Carpenters will batten the roof to secure the new insulation panels.
It's important to batten windows and doors when preparing for a hurricane.

Synset Examples

batten ships

Etymology

From Middle English *baton*, from Old French *baston* (stick), from Late Latin *bastum* (stick, staff).

3. VERB

/ ˈbætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbætn̩ / (BrE)
to secure or fasten with battens, especially by fixing boards or strips over something to hold it in place or make it weatherproof
secure or fasten with battens

Examples

After hearing the storm warning, the crew hurried to batten down the hatches.
You must batten the shutters before the hurricane arrives.
The carpenters will batten the loose panels so they won't move in the wind.

Synset Examples

batten down a ship's hatches

Etymology

From Middle English 'baton', from Old French, diminutive of 'batre' (to beat), ultimately from Latin 'battuere'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbætn̩ / (BrE)
a long, narrow strip of wood, metal, or other material, fixed to something to strengthen it, hold it in place, or as a fastening.
narrow strip for holding or strengthening

Examples

The roofer secured each tile with a wooden batten to keep them in place.
A batten was attached along the edge of the canvas to provide extra support.
They nailed a metal batten to the wall before installing the insulation sheets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lath - a thin flat strip of wood used especially in construction

Etymology

From Old French 'baton' (stick, staff), diminutive of 'bat' (cudgel), from Late Latin 'bastum'.
