mechanics

1. NOUN

/ məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
the technical or practical details and procedures involved in doing or understanding how something works, especially in a particular field or activity
technical details or processes involved in something

Examples

The author explains the mechanics of creating believable dialogue in fiction writing.
Many students struggle with the mechanics of solving complex math problems.
Before you can play the piece well, you need to master the mechanics of the piano technique.

Synset Examples

a mechanism of social control
mechanisms of communication
the mechanics of prose style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technique - a particular way of doing something, especially one involving skill or expertise
  • Synonym: procedure - an established or official way of doing something

Etymology

From mechanic (relating to machines or mechanisms), patterned after similar uses in French méchanique.

2. NOUN

/ məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the motion of objects and the forces acting upon them
branch of physics studying motion and forces

Examples

He is studying mechanics as part of his physics degree.
Classical mechanics provides the foundation for much of modern physics.
The principles of mechanics explain how planets move in the solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamics - branch of mechanics dealing with forces and motion
  • Synonym: statics - branch of mechanics studying forces without motion

Etymology

From Greek mēkhanikē (science of machines), from mēkhanē (machine).
