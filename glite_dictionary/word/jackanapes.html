jackanapes

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækəˌneɪps / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.ə.neɪps / (BrE)
a person who is impudent, mischievously bold, or presumptuously cheeky; someone of little importance behaving with unwarranted self-confidence
cheeky, presumptuous person
archaic

Examples

The young jackanapes talked back to his elders at every opportunity.
Nobody paid attention to the office jackanapes who always made cheeky remarks during meetings.
She dismissed him as nothing more than a jackanapes full of empty bravado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imp - a mischievous child or little devil
  • Synonym: scamp - a person, especially a child, who is mischievous in a likable or amusing way
  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person, especially a child or young man

Etymology

From Middle English Jack Napis, originally a nickname for a mischievous or impertinent person, possibly from 'Jack of Naples'.
