Strongylodon

1. NOUN

/ strɒŋˈɡaɪləˌdɒn / (AmE), / strɒŋˈɡɪlədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs or vines native to Polynesia or Southeast Asia, known for their striking flowers, especially the jade vine.
genus of tropical shrubs or vines

Examples

The Strongylodon macrobotrys, or jade vine, is famous for its vivid turquoise flowers.
Botanists discovered a new species of Strongylodon in the Philippine rainforest.
Many gardeners cultivate Strongylodon for its unique ornamental value.

Etymology

From Greek 'strongylos' (round) + 'odōn' (tooth), referring to features of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ strɒnˈdʒɪlədɒn / (AmE), / strɒŋˈɡɪlədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Polynesian or southeastern Asian shrubs or vines, including the jade vine, known for their ornamental flowers.
genus of tropical shrubs or vines

Examples

The famous jade vine, a member of the genus Strongylodon, blossoms with striking turquoise flowers.
Botanists discovered a new species within Strongylodon in a remote region of the Philippines.
Cultivation of Strongylodon plants requires a warm, humid environment similar to their native habitats.

Etymology

From Greek *strongylos* (round) and *odōn* (tooth), referring to the round-toothed shape of floral parts.
