endive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnˌdaɪv, ˈɛndɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛndɪv, ɑ̃ˈdiːv / (BrE)
A leafy vegetable of the daisy family, with crisp, irregularly frilled leaves, often used in salads.
leafy vegetable with frilled leaves

Examples

She added chopped endive to the salad for a slightly bitter flavor.
The market had beautiful heads of curly endive alongside other greens.
Baked dishes can also feature endive as a flavorful, tender ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escarole - a broad-leaved variety of endive

Etymology

From Old French 'endive', from Medieval Latin 'endivia', from Greek 'entubion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.daɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛndaɪv, ˈɑːndɪv / (BrE)
A leafy plant of the daisy family, grown for its bitter-tasting leaves which are used in salads; varieties may have curly or broad flat leaves.
salad plant with bitter leaves

Examples

The chef added fresh endive to the salad for a slightly bitter flavor.
I bought a bunch of endive at the farmers' market to use in tonight’s dinner.
Curly-leaf endive is often used to give salads an attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - similar leafy vegetable in the same family, also used in salads

Etymology

From Old French 'endive', from Medieval Latin 'endivia', from Greek 'endifion'.
