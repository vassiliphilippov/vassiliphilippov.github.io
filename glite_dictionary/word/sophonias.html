Sophonias

1. PROPN

/ soʊfəˈnaɪəs / (AmE), / sɒfəˈnaɪəs / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet of the late 7th century BC, also known as Zephaniah.
Hebrew minor prophet (7th century BC)

Examples

The book of Sophonias contains messages of judgment and hope for the people of Judah.
Biblical scholars often refer to Sophonias when discussing prophetic literature of the Old Testament.
In the liturgy, readings from Sophonias are included during Advent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zephaniah - Alternative name for Sophonias, Hebrew minor prophet

Etymology

From Latin 'Sophonias', from Hebrew 'Ṣǝfanyāh' meaning 'Yahweh has hidden'.

2. PROPN

/ soʊfəˈnaɪ.əs / (AmE), / sɒfəˈnaɪ.əs / (BrE)
another name for the biblical Book of Zephaniah in the Old Testament, recording prophecies of judgment against Judah.
biblical book; Book of Zephaniah

Examples

The book of Sophonias contains warnings about the coming Day of the Lord.
Many biblical scholars have studied the prophecies found in Sophonias.
In some versions of the Bible, Sophonias is used instead of Zephaniah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zephaniah - another name for the Book of Sophonias or its author, a prophet in the Old Testament

Etymology

From Latin *Sophonias*, from Greek *Sophonias* (Σοφονίας), a transliteration of Hebrew *Tsephanyāh* (Zephaniah).
