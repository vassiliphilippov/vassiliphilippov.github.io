seismology

1. NOUN

/ saɪzˈmɑlədʒi / (AmE), / saɪzˈmɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of earthquakes and the movement of elastic waves through the Earth or other planetary bodies.
study of earthquakes and seismic waves

Examples

She studied seismology at university to better understand earthquake hazards.
Advances in seismology have led to improved methods of detecting earthquakes.
Seismology helps scientists predict volcanic eruptions by monitoring underground movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geology - the science that deals with the Earth's physical structure and substance

Etymology

From Greek 'seismos' (earthquake) + '-logy' (study of).
