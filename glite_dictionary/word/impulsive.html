impulsive

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
Acting or done without careful thought or planning, usually as a result of sudden desire.
acting without forethought

Examples

She made an impulsive decision to quit her job and travel the world.
His impulsive buying habits often led to regret after shopping trips.
An impulsive remark during the meeting caught everyone by surprise.

Synset Examples

letting him borrow her car was an impulsive act that she immediately regretted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spontaneous - performed without premeditation or external stimulus
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Medieval Latin impulsivus, from Latin impulsus (impulse).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
(of actions or behavior) acting or done suddenly and without careful thought, often as a result of emotion or instinct rather than external stimulus.
acting without careful thought

Examples

It was an impulsive decision to buy the tickets at the last minute.
She gave him an impulsive hug when she heard the good news.
His impulsive actions sometimes get him into trouble.

Synset Examples

an impulsive gesture of affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spontaneous - acting on impulse without premeditation
  • Antonym: deliberate - done intentionally or after careful thought

Etymology

From Latin impulsivus (having the force of driving or pushing onward), from impellere (to drive, urge).

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
Acting or done without forethought, guided by sudden desire or whim rather than by reason or necessity.
done without forethought; driven by impulse

Examples

She made an impulsive decision to quit her job and travel the world.
Buying that expensive dress was quite impulsive of you.
His impulsive behavior often gets him into trouble.

Synset Examples

a capricious refusal

the victim of whimsical persecutions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capricious - given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior
  • Synonym: whimsical - playfully quaint or fanciful, especially in an appealing and amusing way
  • Antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration or attention

Etymology

From Medieval Latin impulsivus, from Latin impulsus (impulse, push).

4. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
acting or done suddenly without careful thought or planning, often because of an emotional urge.
acting without thought; spontaneous

Examples

She made an impulsive decision to quit her job and travel the world.
Buying the expensive car was an impulsive act she later regretted.
He can be impulsive at times, acting from emotion rather than reason.

Synset Examples

a hotheaded decision
liable to such impulsive acts as hugging strangers
an impetuous display of spending and gambling
madcap escapade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impetuous - acting quickly and without thought; rash
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally; careful and unhurried

Etymology

From Latin impulsivus (having the power to impel), from impulsus, past participle of impellere (to push against, drive).
