Myliobatidae

1. NOUN

/ maɪliˈoʊbəˌtɪdi / (AmE), / mɪlɪəʊˈbatɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large cartilaginous fishes known as eagle rays, characterized by winglike pectoral fins and a long tail.
family of eagle rays (fish)
medical

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new genus within the Myliobatidae family off the coast of Brazil.
The Myliobatidae are easily recognized by their distinctive wing-shaped pectoral fins.
Members of Myliobatidae are popular attractions in public aquariums due to their graceful swimming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagle ray - A type of cartilaginous fish with winglike fins

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek mylios (millstone) + batēs (going), referring to their shape and movement.

2. NOUN

/ maɪliːoʊˈbeɪtɪdiː / (AmE), / mɪlɪəʊˈbætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large rays known as eagle rays, characterized by their long wing-like fins and belonging to the class Chondrichthyes.
family of eagle rays (fish)
medical

Examples

The Myliobatidae are widely distributed in warm seas around the world.
Scientists have studied the migratory patterns of various species within the Myliobatidae family.
The unique shape of Myliobatidae makes them graceful swimmers in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagle ray - a type of ray fish with large winglike fins, member of Myliobatidae

Etymology

From New Latin Myliobatidae, based on the type genus Myliobatis (from Greek myle 'mill' + bato 'ray').
