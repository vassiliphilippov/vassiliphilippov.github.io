Gladstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlædˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡlæd.stən / (BrE)
a large, sturdy travelling bag with flexible sides and a rigid frame, typically made of leather
a large, rigid-sided travel bag

Examples

He packed all his clothes into a brown Gladstone for the weekend trip.
The old Gladstone in the attic belonged to my grandfather.
She preferred using a Gladstone because of its durability and classic design.

Etymology

Named after William Ewart Gladstone (1809–1898), British Prime Minister, as it was supposedly designed for him.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡlædˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡlæd.stəʊn / (BrE)
William Ewart Gladstone (1809–1898), a British Liberal statesman who served as Prime Minister four times and was known for his influential reforms.
British Liberal Prime Minister (1809–1898)

Examples

Gladstone was instrumental in enacting significant political reforms during the Victorian era.
The rivalry between Gladstone and Disraeli shaped British politics in the 19th century.
Many historians regard Gladstone as one of the most influential British Prime Ministers.
