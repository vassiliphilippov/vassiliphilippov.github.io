Potomac

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtoʊmɪk / (AmE), / pəˈtəʊmɪk / (BrE)
A term sometimes used to refer to the United States federal government or Washington, D.C., especially in political contexts.
term for U.S. federal government or Washington, D.C.

Examples

Many laws are made in Potomac, shaping the future of the entire country.
The debates raging in Potomac often seem distant from daily life elsewhere.
He left the Midwest for a career in Potomac politics.

Etymology

From the Potomac River, which runs through Washington, D.C.

2. PROPN

/ pəˈtoʊmək / (AmE), / pəˈtəʊmək / (BrE)
a major river in the east central United States, rising in the Appalachian Mountains of West Virginia and flowing east into the Chesapeake Bay, forming part of the boundary between Maryland and Virginia.
major river in the eastern United States

Examples

Many historical events have taken place along the banks of the Potomac.
The city of Washington, D.C., is located beside the Potomac River.
Fishermen enjoy catching bass in the Potomac during the summer months.

Etymology

From Algonquian, perhaps from a word meaning 'river of swans'.
