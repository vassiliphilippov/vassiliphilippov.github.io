crest

1. NOUN

/ krɛst / (AmE), / krɛst / (BrE)
(heraldry) a distinctive device or emblem originally used on a helmet in medieval times, often forming part of a coat of arms.
heraldic device on a helmet

Examples

The knight's helmet was adorned with a silver crest shaped like a lion.
Many noble families have a unique crest as part of their heraldry.
The crest atop his armor made him easily identifiable on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat of arms - heraldic design on a shield or escutcheon

Etymology

From Latin crista (tuft, plume, crest).

2. NOUN

/ krɛst / (AmE), / krɛst / (BrE)
a tuft or ridge of feathers, fur, or skin on the head of a bird or other animal, often used for display or identification
tuft or ridge on an animal's head

Examples

The cockatoo raised its crest when it became excited.
Certain lizards have a colorful crest running along their heads and necks.
The robin's small crest gave it a distinctive look among other birds.

Etymology

From Old French 'creste', from Latin 'crista' (tuft, plume).
