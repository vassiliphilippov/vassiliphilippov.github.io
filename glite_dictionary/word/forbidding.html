forbidding

1. ADJ

/ fərˈbɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
unfriendly or threatening in appearance; suggesting danger or unpleasantness
threatening or unfriendly in appearance

Examples

The castle's forbidding walls rose sharply against the darkening sky.
A forbidding silence fell over the abandoned street as night approached.
His forbidding expression made everyone hesitate before speaking.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds
his tone became menacing
ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menacing - threatening or suggesting danger
  • Synonym: ominous - suggesting that something bad will happen
  • Antonym: welcoming - friendly or inviting in appearance
  • Antonym: inviting - pleasant and attractive

Etymology

Present participle of 'forbid', from Old English *forbiddan* (to prohibit, refuse).

2. ADJ

/ fərˈbɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
unfriendly or threatening in appearance; making others feel unwilling to approach
threatening or unwelcoming in appearance

Examples

The castle's forbidding walls made visitors hesitant to approach.
Her forbidding glare silenced the noisy classroom instantly.
A forbidding mountain range loomed darkly against the evening sky.

Synset Examples

a dour, self-sacrificing life
a forbidding scowl
a grim man loving duty more than humanity
undoubtedly the grimmest part of him was his iron claw

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inviting - pleasant and attractive, encouraging approach
  • Synonym: daunting - seeming difficult to deal with, intimidating

Etymology

Present participle of 'forbid', from Old English 'forbēodan' (to prohibit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01808137-s
Synset POS: s
