Viminaria juncea

1. NOUN

/ vɪməˈnɛəriə dʒuːnˈsiːə / (AmE), / vɪmɪˈnɑːrɪə dʒuːnˈsiːə / (BrE)
An Australian leafless shrub of the pea family, resembling broom and bearing small yellow flowers; commonly called the native broom.
Australian shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The Viminaria juncea adds a splash of yellow to the Australian bushland in spring.
Gardeners often plant Viminaria juncea for its distinctive shape and drought resistance.
In its natural habitat, Viminaria juncea thrives along the banks of waterways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a shrub with long, thin, leafless branches and yellow flowers, often of the genus Cytisus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Viminaria' referring to 'osier' or 'willow', and species name 'juncea' meaning 'rush-like, reed-like'.
