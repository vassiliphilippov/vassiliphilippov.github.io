tabun

1. NOUN

/ təˈbun / (AmE), / təˈbuːn / (BrE)
a highly toxic nerve agent, first synthesized in 1936, used as a chemical weapon; a colorless to brownish liquid that is combustible and soluble in organic solvents.
toxic nerve agent used in chemical warfare

Examples

The army stored canisters of tabun in a secure facility for decades.
Exposure to tabun can result in serious harm or even death within minutes.
Scientists studied the effects of tabun to develop better protective equipment for soldiers.

Etymology

From German Tabun, the name given to the chemical by German chemists.
