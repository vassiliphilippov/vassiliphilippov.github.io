Viola

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
a large genus of flowering herbs, including violets and pansies, native to temperate regions
genus of flowering herbs (violets, pansies)

Examples

The Viola genus includes both wild violets and garden pansies.
Many species in the Viola are prized for their colorful spring flowers.
Botanists have identified over 500 species within the Viola genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple or white flowers, member of the Viola genus

Etymology

From Latin *viola* (violet), used as the Linnaean genus name.
