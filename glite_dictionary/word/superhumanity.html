superhumanity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərhjuˈmænɪti / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəhjuːˈmænɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being beyond what is possible for a human being.
state of being beyond human capability

Examples

The novel explores the potential for superhumanity through the use of advanced technology.
She spoke of superhumanity as a philosophical ideal that humanity might one day approach.
Some thinkers believe that striving for superhumanity can inspire people to achieve extraordinary things.

Synset Examples

I cannot account for Shakspeare's low estimate of his own writings, except from the sublimity, the superhumanity, of his genius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superhuman - having powers or abilities greater than those of humans
  • Antonym: inhumanity - lack of human qualities; cruelty or barbarity

Etymology

Formed from 'superhuman' + '-ity', patterned after Latin roots.
