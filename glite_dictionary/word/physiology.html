physiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfɪziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of biology that deals with the normal functions and processes of living organisms and their parts
biological study of functions in organisms
medical

Examples

She specializes in cardiovascular physiology and researches how the heart adapts to stress.
A solid understanding of physiology is essential for anyone pursuing a career in medicine.
The course covers both human anatomy and physiology in great detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the study of the structure of living organisms
  • Antonym: pathology - the study of disease processes in organisms

Etymology

From Ancient Greek phusiologia (study of nature), from phusis (nature) + -logia (study).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfɪziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the functions and vital processes of living organisms or their parts.
study of how living organisms function

Examples

After studying physiology, she became fascinated by how the heart and lungs work together.
The changes in plant physiology during drought conditions are a key topic in botany.
His research in animal physiology led to important discoveries about metabolic rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the scientific study of the structure of living organisms
  • Antonym: pathology - the scientific study of diseases and their effects

Etymology

From Greek phusiologia (natural science), from phusis (nature) + -logia (study of).
