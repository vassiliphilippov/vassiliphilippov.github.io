Kwan-yin

1. PROPN

/ kwɑːnˈjin / (AmE), / kwɑːnˈjiːn / (BrE)
In Chinese Buddhism, the female Bodhisattva known as the Goddess of Mercy, revered as an embodiment of compassion and identified with Avalokiteśvara.
Chinese Buddhist Goddess of Mercy

Examples

Many devotees pray to Kwan-yin for compassion and guidance in their daily lives.
A statue of Kwan-yin stands at the entrance of the temple, welcoming visitors.
Legends describe Kwan-yin as responding to those in need with boundless mercy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Avalokiteśvara - the Bodhisattva of compassion in Buddhist tradition
  • Synonym: Kwannon - Japanese form of Kwan-yin in Buddhism

Etymology

From Chinese 觀音 (*Guānyīn*), meaning 'Perceiving the Sounds (or Cries) of the World'.
