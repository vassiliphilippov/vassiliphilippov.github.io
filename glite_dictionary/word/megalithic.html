megalithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛɡəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
relating to large stone monuments or the people who built them, especially in prehistoric times
relating to large stone monuments

Examples

The ancient megalithic structures of Stonehenge attract thousands of visitors each year.
Archaeologists studied the megalithic tombs scattered across the countryside.
Some megalithic monuments were aligned with the movements of the sun.

Synset Examples

megalithic monuments like Stonehenge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megalith - a large stone that forms a prehistoric monument or part of one

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) and 'lithos' (stone).
