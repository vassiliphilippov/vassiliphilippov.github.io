to recommence

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəˈmɛns / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈmɛns / (BrE)
to begin or start again after an interruption or pause
to begin again after a pause

Examples

After a brief delay, the concert will recommence at 8 p.m.
Work on the project will recommence once the funding is approved.
She paused to answer a call, then recommenced her lecture without missing a beat.

Synset Examples

we recommenced his reading after a short nap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resume - to begin again after stopping

Etymology

From Old French recommencer, from Latin re- (again) + cominitiare (to begin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00347191-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəˈmens / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈmens / (BrE)
to begin or cause to begin again; to start anew, especially after a pause or interruption
to start again; begin anew

Examples

After the storm passed, workers recommenced repairs on the damaged bridge.
Talks between the two nations recommenced once the ceasefire was agreed upon.
She recommenced her studies after taking a year off for travel.

Synset Examples

The enemy recommenced hostilities after a few days of quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resume - to begin again after a pause; to continue
  • Antonym: to cease - to stop or come to an end

Etymology

From French recommencer, from re- (again) + commencer (to begin)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350070-v
Synset POS: v
