godlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌlaɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities or nature of a god; resembling or befitting a god in power, beauty, or excellence.
having the nature of a god

Examples

The painting depicted a godlike figure surrounded by light.
Her godlike abilities on the piano left the audience in awe.
The leader was admired for his godlike wisdom and compassion.

Synset Examples

the custom of killing the divine king upon any serious failure of his … powers
the divine will
the divine capacity for love
'Tis wise to learn; 'tis God-like to create

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - relating to a god or godlike
  • Antonym: mortal - subject to death; lacking godlike qualities

Etymology

From Old English godlic, from god (god) + -lic (like).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒd.laɪk / (BrE)
having qualities or power appropriate to a god; resembling or befitting a god in greatness, beauty, or power.
having qualities like a god

Examples

The athlete's godlike performance left everyone in awe.
Her godlike beauty captivated everyone she met.
Some view the ancient pharaohs as having godlike status.

Synset Examples

the divine strength of Achilles
a man of godlike sagacity
man must play God for he has acquired certain godlike powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - of or like a god; godly
  • Antonym: mortal - subject to death; human (contrasted with 'godlike')

Etymology

Formed from 'god' + 'like', meaning 'like a god'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01264199-s
Synset POS: s
