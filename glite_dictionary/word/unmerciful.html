unmerciful

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɜr.sɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɜː.sɪ.fəl / (BrE)
having or showing no mercy; very harsh or cruel
showing no mercy; very harsh

Examples

The dictator was known for his unmerciful treatment of prisoners.
She gave him an unmerciful rebuke that left him stunned.
The coach's unmerciful criticism discouraged many players.

Synset Examples

the merciless enemy
a merciless critic
gave him a merciless beating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciless - having or showing no mercy
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion

Etymology

from un- (not) + merciful (from Latin misericors, merciful)
