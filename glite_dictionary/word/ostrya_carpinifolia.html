Ostrya carpinifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstriə ˌkɑrpɪnəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈɒstriə ˌkɑːpɪnɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
a medium-sized species of hop hornbeam native to southern Europe and Asia Minor, known for its hard wood and catkin-like fruit clusters
a southern European hop hornbeam tree

Examples

The leaves of Ostrya carpinifolia resemble those of the common hornbeam.
Many parks in southern Europe feature Ostrya carpinifolia as an ornamental tree.
Wood from Ostrya carpinifolia is valued for its strength and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop hornbeam - a tree of the genus Ostrya, with birch-like leaves and hop-like fruit clusters

Etymology

From genus name Ostrya, derived from Greek ostrua (hop hornbeam), and species name carpinifolia from Latin, meaning 'having leaves like Carpinus (hornbeam)'.
