Senecio jacobaea

1. NOUN

/ səˈniːʃioʊ ˌdʒækəˈbiːə / (AmE), / sɪˈniːsiəʊ ˌdʒækəˈbiːə / (BrE)
A widespread European plant with yellow daisylike flowers, known as ragwort; sometimes considered an obnoxious weed and toxic to cattle if eaten in large amounts.
European plant; toxic weed called ragwort

Examples

Senecio jacobaea is often found growing in pastures, where its bright yellow flowers are easily spotted.
Farmers are advised to remove Senecio jacobaea from grazing areas due to its toxicity to livestock.
The rapid spread of Senecio jacobaea has made it a troublesome weed in many European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragwort - common name for Senecio jacobaea, a European weed with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Senecio', the genus name, meaning 'old man', and 'jacobaea', a species name referring to St. James (Jacobus in Latin).
