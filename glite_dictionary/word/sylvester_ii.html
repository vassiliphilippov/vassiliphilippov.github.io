Sylvester II

1. PROPN

/ sɪlˈvɛstər ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɛstə ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(c. 945–1003) Pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 999 to 1003, renowned for his scholarship and promotion of learning in Europe.
Pope from 999 to 1003, noted scholar

Examples

In medieval history, Sylvester II is remembered for his support of science and education.
The papacy of Sylvester II marked a significant period of intellectual revival in Europe.
Historians regard Sylvester II as one of the most learned men of his time.
