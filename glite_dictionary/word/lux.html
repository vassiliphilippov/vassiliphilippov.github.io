lux

1. NOUN

/ lʌks / (AmE), / lʌks / (BrE)
the SI unit of illumination, equal to one lumen per square meter
unit of illumination

Examples

The laboratory requires lighting levels of at least 500 lux for accurate work.
A well-lit office typically has an illumination of around 300 lux.
He measured the brightness with a sensor that displayed the value in lux.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumen - the SI unit of luminous flux

Etymology

From Latin 'lux' (light)
