mounting

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a frame or structure on which something is supported, fixed, or displayed, especially for exhibition or use
supporting frame or structure

Examples

The painting was placed in an elegant mounting before being hung in the gallery.
The jewel’s delicate mounting highlighted the brilliance of the diamond.
A proper mounting is essential to safely display rare coins in a collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frame - a rigid structure that surrounds or encloses something
  • Synonym: setting - the mounting of a gem in jewelry

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'mount', from Old French 'monter' (to mount, climb) and Latin 'montare', rooted in 'mons' (mountain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
an event or process in which something rises to a higher level, such as altitude, temperature, or intensity
process of rising or increasing

Examples

With the mounting of public pressure, the government agreed to reconsider its decision.
The mounting of temperature inside the greenhouse worried the farmers.
There was a noticeable mounting of excitement as the concert drew closer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: diminishing - becoming less in size or importance
