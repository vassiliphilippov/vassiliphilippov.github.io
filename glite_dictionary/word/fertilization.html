fertilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɜrtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an egg or ovule joins with a sperm or pollen to begin the development of a new individual in animals or plants
union of gametes to begin development
medical

Examples

The fertilization of the ovum by the sperm marks the beginning of pregnancy.
In flowering plants, fertilization occurs when pollen reaches the ovule.
Scientists observed the process of fertilization under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conception - the process of becoming pregnant involving fertilization

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɝːtɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making soil or land more fertile by applying fertilizer or manure
making soil more fertile with fertilizer

Examples

Proper fertilization of the soil can significantly increase crop yields.
Modern farming relies heavily on chemical fertilization for intensive agriculture.
Regular fertilization ensures that the garden remains lush and productive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manuring - the act of applying manure to land to improve fertility
  • Degree Higher: fertility - the quality of being fertile; ability to produce abundant vegetation or crops

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action or process).
