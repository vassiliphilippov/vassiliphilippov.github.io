Trental

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntəl / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used to treat claudication by improving the flexibility of red blood cells, enhancing blood flow to the legs and feet.
drug for treating claudication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Trental to help improve circulation in her legs.
Patients with peripheral artery disease may be given Trental as part of their treatment.
It is important to take Trental exactly as directed to see its full benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentoxifylline - the generic name for Trental, a drug treating poor blood flow
