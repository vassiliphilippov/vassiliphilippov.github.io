Psoralea esculenta

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈreɪli ə ɛˈskjuːlɛntə / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɑːlɪ ə ɛˈskjuːlɛntə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to central North America, noted for its densely hairy appearance and edible tuberous roots.
North American plant with edible tuberous root

Examples

Indigenous peoples traditionally harvested Psoralea esculenta for its nutritious roots.
Botanists conducted a study on the distribution of Psoralea esculenta across the Great Plains.
The edible tubers of Psoralea esculenta were once a valuable food source in central North America.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Psoralea' (the genus, from Greek psōraleos, 'scabby', referring to glandular spots on the plant) and 'esculenta' (Latin for 'edible').
