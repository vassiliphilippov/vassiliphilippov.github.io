Carthamus tinctorius

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrˈθæməs tɪŋˈktɔriəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːˈθæməs tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
a thistlelike Eurasian plant with red or orange flower heads, cultivated for its seeds that produce valuable oil
a plant grown for its oil and flower heads

Examples

The oil extracted from Carthamus tinctorius is considered one of the healthiest vegetable oils.
Farmers in arid regions often cultivate Carthamus tinctorius for its drought resistance and commercial value.
Botanists have studied Carthamus tinctorius for its medicinal properties and use in food coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safflower - the common name for Carthamus tinctorius, used for its oil and as a dye plant

Etymology

From Latin Carthamus (the plant's genus), and Latin tinctorius, meaning 'used for dyeing.'
