globose

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡloʊˌboʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊˌbəʊs / (BrE)
having the shape of a globe; spherical or nearly spherical
spherical or globe-shaped

Examples

The fruit of the plant is globose and bright red when ripe.
Under the microscope, the cells appeared globose and symmetrical.
The artist sculpted a globose figure from a block of marble.

Synset Examples

a spherical object
nearly orbicular in shape
little globular houses like mud-wasp nests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spherical - having the form of a sphere; perfectly round
  • Synonym: globular - shaped like a globe or sphere

Etymology

From Latin globosus (spherical), from globus (ball, sphere).
