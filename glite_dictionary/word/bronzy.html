bronzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɑnzi / (AmE), / ˈbrɒnzi / (BrE)
having the color or appearance of bronze, typically a metallic brown or gold tint
having the color of bronze

Examples

The statue had a bronzy sheen that caught the afternoon sunlight.
She wore a dress of bronzy silk that shimmered as she walked.
The leaves turned a bronzy hue in the late autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronze - a yellowish-brown alloy of copper and tin, or its color

Etymology

Formed from 'bronze' + '-y' (adjective forming suffix).
