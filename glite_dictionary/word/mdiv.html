MDiv

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˈdɪv / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈdɪv / (BrE)
A master's degree in divinity, typically awarded to those studying for Christian ministry or theology.
master's degree in divinity/theology

Examples

After completing his MDiv, he became a pastor at a local church.
Many seminaries require an MDiv for ordination into ministry.
She is pursuing an MDiv to deepen her understanding of theology.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Master of Divinity'.
