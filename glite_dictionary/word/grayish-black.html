grayish-black

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪ.ɪʃ blæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.ɪʃ blæk / (BrE)
Having a color that is primarily black with a tinge or shade of grey.
black tinged with grey

Examples

The old photograph had a distinct grayish-black tone to it.
Her dress was a deep grayish-black that shimmered in the light.
The sky turned grayish-black as the storm approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal - a dark grayish-black color, like charred wood
  • Degree Lower: jet-black - completely black, without any gray
  • Degree Higher: gray - a neutral color between black and white
