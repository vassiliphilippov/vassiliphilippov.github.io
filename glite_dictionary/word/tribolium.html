Tribolium

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈboʊliəm / (AmE), / trɪˈbəʊliəm / (BrE)
A genus of small beetles, known as flour beetles, which infest and feed on stored grain products.
genus of flour beetles

Examples

Tribolium species are often found in grain storage facilities around the world.
The red flour beetle, a member of the genus Tribolium, is a common pest in flour mills.
Scientists frequently use Tribolium as a model organism in genetic and evolutionary research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flour beetle - a small beetle that infests flour and stored grain products

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'tribolos' (a kind of plant, literally 'three-pointed'), used in taxonomy.
