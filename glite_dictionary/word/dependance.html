dependance

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
The state of being abnormally tolerant to and reliant on a habit-forming substance, especially drugs or alcohol; addiction.
addiction to a substance
medical

Examples

His dependance on painkillers became a serious problem after the accident.
Treatment programs are available for those struggling with alcohol dependance.
The doctor warned about the risk of developing a dependance on sleeping pills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addiction - the condition of being addicted to a substance or activity
  • Antonym: independence - freedom from being controlled or reliant on something

Etymology

From Old French 'dependance', from Latin 'dependere' (to hang down, depend).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else
state of reliance or being controlled by another

Examples

Her financial dependance on her parents made it difficult for her to move out.
The company’s dependance on imported materials increased its vulnerability to supply chain disruptions.
He struggled to break free from his emotional dependance on others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependence - the usual spelling; state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else
  • Antonym: independence - the state of not being subject to control by others

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'dependence', from Latin *dependere* (to hang down).
