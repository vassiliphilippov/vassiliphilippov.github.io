packman

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæk.mən / (AmE), / ˈpak.mən / (BrE)
a man who travels around selling goods, especially on the streets or at fairs; a peddler
travelling male peddler or street seller
archaic

Examples

The old packman trudged from village to village with his wares strapped to his back.
In the market square, a packman was selling colorful ribbons and trinkets.
Tales of the wandering packman fascinated children in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peddler - a person who travels about selling goods
  • Synonym: street vendor - someone who sells goods on the street

Etymology

From 'pack' (bundle of goods) + 'man'; historically used for itinerant sellers.
