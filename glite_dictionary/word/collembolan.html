collembolan

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛmbəˌlɑn / (AmE), / kɒˈlɛmbələn / (BrE)
A minute, wingless, primitive insect of the order Collembola, noted for its special abdominal appendage enabling it to jump; commonly found in soil or on surfaces with organic material.
minute jumping soil insect (springtail)

Examples

A collembolan can leap many times its body length thanks to its springing organ.
Farmers appreciate the role of collembolans in decomposing organic matter in the soil.
Scientists discovered a new species of collembolan living on the surface of Antarctic snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springtail - small jumping soil insect of the order Collembola

Etymology

From scientific Latin Collembola, from Greek kolla (glue) + embolos (wedge, peg), referring to the insect's springing organ.
