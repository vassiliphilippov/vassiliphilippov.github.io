flock

1. NOUN

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
a group of sheep or goats kept together
group of sheep or goats

Examples

The shepherd led his flock across the green meadow.
A flock of goats was grazing near the riverbank.
We watched as the entire flock moved toward the shelter for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - a group of cattle or other large animals

Etymology

From Old English flocc (group, troop), possibly of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
(of people, animals, etc.) to gather or move together in a large group.
gather or move in a large group

Examples

Tourists flock to the city every spring to see the cherry blossoms.
Young people flocked to the concert, eager to see their favorite band perform.
The protesters were flocking outside the government building by early morning.

3. VERB

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to move together in a large group, often towards something of interest or importance.
move in a group

Examples

Fans began to flock to the stadium hours before the match started.
In spring, birds flock north to their breeding grounds.
Shoppers flocked to the new store as soon as it opened.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together in a group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or move apart in different directions

Etymology

From Old English 'flocc' (a crowd or group of people or animals).

6. NOUN

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
a large group of people or animals moving or gathered together in an orderly way
large, orderly group of people or animals

Examples

A flock of tourists gathered near the entrance to the museum.
Every morning, a flock of students waits for the school bus at the corner.
The shepherd guided his flock through the narrow pass.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarm - a large group of insects, especially flying ones, moving together
