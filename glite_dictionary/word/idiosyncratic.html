idiosyncratic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdi.oʊsɪnˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdi.əʊsɪnˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular individual; highly distinctive or peculiar to one person or thing
peculiar or distinctive to an individual

Examples

Her idiosyncratic way of organizing her desk puzzled her coworkers.
The director is famous for his idiosyncratic approach to storytelling in films.
Their idiosyncratic sense of humor made them stand out in the group.

Synset Examples

we all have our own idiosyncratic gestures
Michelangelo's highly idiosyncratic style of painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinctive - having a quality or characteristic that makes something noticeable or different
  • Antonym: typical - having the usual qualities of a particular group or thing

Etymology

From Greek idiosynkrasia (a peculiar temperament), from idios (one's own) and synkrasis (mixture).
