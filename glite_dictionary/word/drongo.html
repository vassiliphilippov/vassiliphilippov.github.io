drongo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑŋgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdrɒŋgəʊ / (BrE)
a foolish or incompetent person, especially in Australian English
foolish or incompetent person (Australian, informal)
informal

Examples

I can't believe he locked his keys in the car again—what a drongo!
Don't be such a drongo—read the instructions first!
She called him a drongo after he tripped over his own shoelaces.

Synset Examples

He’s such a drongo for forgetting the meeting again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgement

Etymology

Originally the name of a bird (genus Dicrurus), later transferred in Australian slang to mean 'simpleton' or 'fool'.
