Antony

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.tə.ni / (AmE), / ˈæn.tə.ni / (BrE)
(c. 83–30 BC) A Roman general and statesman, ally of Julius Caesar, lover of Cleopatra, and opponent of Octavian in the final war of the Roman Republic.
Roman general, ally of Caesar, lover of Cleopatra

Examples

Antony formed a political alliance with Cleopatra, queen of Egypt.
The defeat of Antony and Cleopatra at Actium marked the end of the Roman Republic.
Shakespeare's play dramatizes the relationship between Antony and Cleopatra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cleopatra - Queen of Egypt, lover of Antony and Julius Caesar
  • Synonym: Octavian - Later known as Augustus, first Roman Emperor and opponent of Antony

Etymology

From Latin Antonius, a Roman family name.
