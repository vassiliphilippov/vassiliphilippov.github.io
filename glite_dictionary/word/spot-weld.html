spot-weld

1. NOUN

/ spɑt wɛld / (AmE), / spɒt wɛld / (BrE)
each of the individual welds made by welding at a particular spot, typically with spot welding equipment
individual weld made at a particular point

Examples

The car's frame is held together with hundreds of spot-welds for extra strength.
Inspect each spot-weld carefully to ensure there are no weak points in the structure.
A faulty spot-weld can compromise the durability of the metal joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - a joint formed by welding two metal pieces together

2. VERB

/ ˈspɑt ˌwɛld / (AmE), / ˈspɒt ˌwɛld / (BrE)
to join metal parts together by making small, circular welds at several points using heat and pressure
join metals with small welds

Examples

The technician will spot-weld the sheets together for extra strength.
These metal brackets are spot-welded to the frame at several points.
Many modern car bodies are spot-welded during the manufacturing process.

Synset Examples

These pipes are only spotwelded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - to join pieces of metal by heating and pressing them together
