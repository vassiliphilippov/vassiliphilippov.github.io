diabetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiz / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
A metabolic disease in which the body has abnormally high levels of blood glucose, often resulting in frequent urination and persistent thirst.
disease causing high blood sugar
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with diabetes at the age of forty after noticing frequent thirst and weight loss.
Managing diabetes requires careful monitoring of diet and blood sugar levels.
Type 2 diabetes has become increasingly common in many developed countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabetes mellitus - The most common form of diabetes, characterized by high blood glucose due to insulin problems
  • Degree Lower: insulin - A hormone important in the regulation of blood glucose
  • Synonym: hyperglycemia - High blood glucose, a key feature of diabetes
  • Antonym: hypoglycemia - Low blood glucose, the opposite condition to diabetes's main symptom

Etymology

From Greek 'diabētēs', meaning 'a passer-through, siphon', from 'diabainein' (to pass through).
