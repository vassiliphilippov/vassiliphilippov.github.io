otherworldliness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌðərˈwɜrldli.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌðəˈwɜːldlinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being concerned with spiritual, mystical, or supernatural matters rather than worldly affairs
quality of spiritual or mystical focus

Examples

The painting's otherworldliness lent the gallery an air of mystery and serenity.
Her otherworldliness set her apart from her peers who were preoccupied with everyday concerns.
Many poets seek otherworldliness in their work, drawing inspiration from dreams and visions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirituality - the quality of being concerned with the human spirit or soul as opposed to material things
  • Antonym: materialism - focus on material things rather than spiritual values

Etymology

Formed from 'otherworldly' + '-ness', meaning pertaining to another world (the spiritual or supernatural).
