Annona glabra

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊnə ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnə ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to tropical America, bearing edible fruit and mainly used as grafting stock
a tropical American evergreen tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Annona glabra is sometimes called pond apple and is eaten in some regions.
Horticulturists often use Annona glabra as rootstock for grafting other Annona species.
Annona glabra thrives in swampy areas and contributes to the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Annona' is the genus name, 'glabra' meaning 'smooth' in Latin.
