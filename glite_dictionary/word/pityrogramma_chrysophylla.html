Pityrogramma chrysophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪti.oʊˈɡræmə ˌkrɪsoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtiəʊˈɡræmə ˌkrɪsəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A fern species found in the West Indies and South America, known for its fronds that have bright golden-yellow undersides.
fern with golden-yellow undersides, West Indies/South America

Examples

The striking fronds of Pityrogramma chrysophylla add a splash of color to tropical gardens.
Many botanists study Pityrogramma chrysophylla for its unique golden-yellow leaf undersides.
In its native habitat, Pityrogramma chrysophylla thrives in moist, shaded areas.
