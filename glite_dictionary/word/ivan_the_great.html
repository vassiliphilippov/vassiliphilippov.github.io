Ivan the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪvən ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈaɪvan ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
Grand Duke of Muscovy (1440–1505) whose victories over the Tartars helped to unify Russia.
Russian Grand Duke, unifier of Russia

Examples

The rule of Ivan the Great marked the beginning of Russia's emergence as a powerful state.
Historians credit Ivan the Great with laying the foundations for a centralized Russian government.
Under Ivan the Great, Moscow finally broke free from Tartar domination.
