mercy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)
Leniency and compassion shown toward an offender by someone with authority, especially in a legal context.
leniency or compassion in justice

Examples

The judge showed mercy by giving her a lighter sentence than expected.
He appealed to the governor for mercy following his conviction.
The victim's family asked the court to grant mercy to the offender.

Synset Examples

he threw himself on the mercy of the court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clemency - leniency granted by a person in authority, especially in sentencing

Etymology

From Old French 'merci' (pity, thanks), from Latin 'merces' (reward, pity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, forgiving, or compassionate toward others, especially when they are at one's mercy
kindness or forgiveness toward others

Examples

The judge showed mercy to the young defendant, giving him a second chance.
Her mercy was evident when she forgave him for his mistakes.
In times of war, mercy is often in short supply.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain or suffering

Etymology

From Old French *merci* (reward, pity), from Latin *mercedem* (reward, wages, favor).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)
Kindness or compassion shown toward someone who is suffering or in distress, especially when it involves alleviating their hardship.
compassion or kindness toward the distressed

Examples

The organization extended mercy to the refugees by providing shelter and food.
She showed great mercy toward the injured animal, nursing it back to health.
After the disaster, neighbors acted out of mercy to help those who had lost everything.

Synset Examples

distributing food and clothing to the flood victims was an act of mercy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain and suffering

Etymology

From Old French *merci*, from Latin *mercedem* (reward, pity, thanks).

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)

feeling of compassion or forgiveness

Examples

Her mercy towards the injured bird was evident as she tended to its wounds.

  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference or pleasure in causing pain or suffering
