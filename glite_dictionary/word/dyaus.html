Dyaus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdjɑː.uːs / (AmE), / ˈdjɑː.uːs / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the god of the sky and one of the earliest Vedic deities, regarded as the personification of the sky.
Hindu god of the sky

Examples

In the Rigveda, Dyaus is often mentioned as the father of other gods.
Scholars compare Dyaus with the Greek god Zeus due to similarities in their attributes.
The myths surrounding Dyaus reflect ancient people's reverence for the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zeus - Greek god of the sky, king of the gods
  • Synonym: Jupiter - Roman god of the sky and thunder, king of the gods

Etymology

From Sanskrit dyauḥ (sky, heaven), related to Proto-Indo-European *dyew- (to shine).
