demo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɛm.əʊ / (BrE)
a demonstration, especially a visual presentation or display showing how something works
visual presentation of how something works

Examples

The software company gave a demo of their new app to potential investors.
Before you buy the product, you can watch a short demo online.
Many musicians release a demo to showcase their songs before recording a full album.

Synset Examples

the lecture was accompanied by dramatic demonstrations
the lecturer shot off a pistol as a demonstration of the startle response

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstration - an act or instance of showing how something works

Etymology

Shortened form of 'demonstration,' first recorded in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06892230-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɛməʊ / (BrE)
to give a demonstration of a product, skill, or process to an audience, especially to show how it works
demonstrate something to an audience

Examples

The company will demo its latest smartphone at the conference next week.
Can you demo that new software feature for the team?
She demoed her invention to potential investors, hoping to impress them.

Synset Examples

She shows her dogs frequently
We will demo the new software in Washington

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demonstrate - to show something clearly by giving proof or evidence

Etymology

Shortened form of 'demonstrate' or 'demonstration'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02153218-v
Synset POS: v
