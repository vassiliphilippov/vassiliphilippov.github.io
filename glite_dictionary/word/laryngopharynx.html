laryngopharynx

1. NOUN

/ ləˌrɪŋɡoʊˈfærɪŋks / (AmE), / ləˌrɪŋɡəʊˈfarɪŋks / (BrE)
the lower section of the pharynx, located behind the larynx, extending from the hyoid bone to the esophagus
lower part of the pharynx
medical

Examples

Food passes through the laryngopharynx before entering the esophagus.
During a throat infection, the laryngopharynx may become inflamed and sore.
The doctor examined the patient's laryngopharynx for signs of obstruction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pharynx - the membrane-lined cavity behind the nose and mouth, connecting them to the esophagus

Etymology

From laryngo- (relating to the larynx) + pharynx, both from Greek roots.
