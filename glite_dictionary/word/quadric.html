quadric

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drɪk / (AmE), / ˈkwɒ.drɪk / (BrE)
A curve or surface whose equation, in Cartesian coordinates, is of the second degree.
curve or surface defined by a second-degree equation

Examples

An ellipse is a special case of a quadric curve in two dimensions.
The equation of a quadric surface in three dimensions can describe a sphere, ellipsoid, or hyperboloid.
Researchers analyzed the intersection of two quadric surfaces to study their geometric properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conic - a curve defined by a second-degree equation in two variables

Etymology

From Latin 'quadr-
