unappeasable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈpizəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈpiːzəbəl / (BrE)
(of a feeling, demand, or person) impossible to calm, satisfy, or placate.
impossible to appease or satisfy

Examples

She faced her opponent's unappeasable anger with remarkable composure.
The dictator's ambitions seemed unappeasable, always demanding more territory and power.
Despite their efforts, the child's unappeasable curiosity could not be satisfied.

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implacable - unable to be placated
  • Antonym: appeasable - able to be appeased

Etymology

From un- (not) + appease + -able.
