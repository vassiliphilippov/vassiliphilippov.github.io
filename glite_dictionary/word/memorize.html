to memorize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛm.əˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmem.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to learn something so that you can remember it exactly; to commit to memory
to learn and remember exactly

Examples

She managed to memorize the entire poem in just one day.
Actors must memorize hundreds of lines for each performance.
We were required to memorize the periodic table for our chemistry class.

Synset Examples

Have you memorized your lines for the play yet?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to forget - to be unable to remember information
  • Synonym: to learn - to gain knowledge or skill by studying or experience

Etymology

From French 'mémoriser', from Latin 'memor' (mindful, remembering).
