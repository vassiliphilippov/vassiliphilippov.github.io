catechu

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætɪˌtʃu / (AmE), / ˈkætɪˌtjuː / (BrE)
an East Indian tree (Acacia catechu) known for its extract used in dyes, tanning, and medicine
an East Indian tree used for dye and tanning

Examples

The bark of the catechu tree is valued for its medicinal properties.
They harvested catechu to use as a natural dye for fabric.
A plantation of catechu trees stretched along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutch - extract from Acacia catechu used in tanning and dyeing

Etymology

From Malay 'kachu', through Portuguese 'catechu'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætɪˌtʃu / (AmE), / ˈkatɪˌtʃu / (BrE)
A brownish resinous extract obtained from the heartwood of the Acacia catechu tree, used for dyeing, tanning, preserving nets and sails, and formerly in medicine.
brownish extract from Acacia catechu tree

Examples

The fisherman treated his nets with catechu to protect them from rotting in the water.
Traditional textiles in India are sometimes dyed using catechu to produce a rich brown color.
In the past, catechu was prescribed by herbalists for its supposed medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Malay ketchu or katechu, from Sanskrit khadira (acacia tree).
