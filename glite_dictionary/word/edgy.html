edgy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒi / (BrE)
nervous or tense; easily irritated or unsettled
nervous or tense

Examples

She always gets edgy before giving a big presentation.
After hours of waiting, the crowd became increasingly edgy.
His edgy mood made everyone around him uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; anxious
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety

Etymology

From edge + -y, originally referring to being on edge.
