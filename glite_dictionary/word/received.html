received

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsivd / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvd / (BrE)
(of a belief, idea, or practice) generally accepted as true or authoritative, especially by most people in a society or group.
widely accepted as true or worthy

Examples

Her opinions often challenge the received wisdom of the academic community.
This tradition is a received part of local culture, passed down for generations.
Many received ideas about nutrition have recently been called into question.

Synset Examples

a received moral idea
Received political wisdom says not; surveys show otherwise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accepted - widely recognized or approved by most people
  • Synonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with general agreement, use, or practice
  • Antonym: unorthodox - contrary to what is usual, traditional, or accepted

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈsiːvd / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvd / (BrE)
(of language) considered correct and standard by educated native speakers, especially in terms of pronunciation or usage.
conforming to the standard usage of educated speakers

Examples

Many people aim to speak with received pronunciation in formal contexts.
Her essay reflected the received standards of academic English.
He criticized the received usage, preferring more colloquial expressions.

Synset Examples

standard (American) English
received standard English is sometimes called the King's English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - widely accepted and used as the norm
  • Antonym: nonstandard - not conforming to the established or accepted usage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02305339-a
Synset POS: a
