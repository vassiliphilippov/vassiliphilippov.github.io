backstretch

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstɹɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækstɹɛtʃ / (BrE)
the straight section on the far side of a racecourse or track, opposite the finish line
straight section of a racetrack

Examples

The runners accelerated down the backstretch to gain an advantage before the final turn.
Spectators lined the fence along the backstretch to watch the horses pick up speed.
During the backstretch, athletes often position themselves strategically for the final sprint.

Synset Examples

She sprinted her hardest on the backstretch of the race.
The horse sped up on the backstretch of the track.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homestretch - the straight final stretch leading to the finish line on a racetrack

Etymology

From 'back' (the rear or reverse side) + 'stretch' (a continuous area of land or water).
