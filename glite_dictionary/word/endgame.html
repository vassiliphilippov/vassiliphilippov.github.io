endgame

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛndɡeɪm / (BrE)
The final stage of a chess game, after most of the pieces have been removed from the board.
final stage of a chess game

Examples

In the endgame, precise calculation is crucial to secure a win.
The endgame started when only the kings and a few pawns remained on the board.
Mastering the endgame is essential for anyone hoping to improve at chess.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opening - the initial stage of a chess game
  • Antonym: middlegame - the middle phase of a chess game

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛndˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛndˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
the final stage of a chess game when most pieces have been removed from the board, often requiring precise play to secure a win or draw
final stage of a chess game

Examples

After trading queens, the match entered the endgame where each move became critical.
Mastering the endgame is essential for any serious chess player.
Many players overlook how important the endgame can be in securing a draw or a win.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opening - the initial phase of a chess game, where players develop their pieces
  • Antonym: middlegame - the middle phase of a chess game between the opening and the endgame

Etymology

From 'end' + 'game'; first used to describe chess, now also more generally applied.
