parkland

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkˌlænd / (BrE)
an extensive area of natural or semi-natural land, often preserved as public property for recreation or conservation
public natural area or green space

Examples

Much of the city is surrounded by protected parkland and forests.
The new highway will be routed to avoid the historic parkland.
Volunteers gathered to clean up the trash in the community parkland.

Synset Examples

there are laws that protect the wildlife in this park

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wilderness - a natural area of land left wild and uninhabited
  • Degree Lower: park - a public area of land for recreation, often landscaped

Etymology

From 'park' (enclosed preserve) + 'land'.
