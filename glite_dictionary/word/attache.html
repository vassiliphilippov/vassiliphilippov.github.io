attache

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˌætæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
a shallow, rectangular briefcase typically used for carrying documents
a shallow, rectangular briefcase

Examples

He placed the contracts carefully inside his attache before leaving for the meeting.
The attache slid neatly under her arm as she hurried into the office.
She bought a new leather attache for her job interviews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefcase - a flat, rectangular container for carrying documents and papers

Etymology

From French 'attaché', meaning 'attached', initially referring to a person attached to an embassy; later also used for the case such people often carried.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæt̬əˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˌætæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
a person assigned to the staff of an embassy or consulate as a specialist in a particular subject, such as military, cultural, or economic affairs
specialist at an embassy or consulate

Examples

The French embassy appointed a new attache to handle economic affairs.
As a military attache, he often attended international defense meetings.
Her role as a cultural attache involved organizing art exhibitions abroad.

Etymology

From French attaché (attached), past participle of attacher (to attach).
