chigoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A small tropical flea (Tunga penetrans) whose fertilized female burrows under the skin of humans and other animals, causing irritation and sometimes infection.
small tropical flea that burrows under skin
medical

Examples

The farmer discovered a chigoe had burrowed into his toe after walking barefoot.
Infestation by the chigoe can lead to serious skin infections if not treated promptly.
Travelers to tropical areas are advised to take precautions against chigoe fleas.

Etymology

From French 'chique', probably of West African origin; also related to Spanish 'chigoe'.
