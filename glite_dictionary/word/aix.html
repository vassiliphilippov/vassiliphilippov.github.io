Aix

1. NOUN

/ eɪks / (AmE), / eɪks / (BrE)
A genus of ducks including the wood duck and the mandarin duck, known for their brightly coloured males.
genus of ducks: wood duck, mandarin duck

Examples

Many ornithologists study species in the genus Aix, particularly the striking mandarin and wood ducks.
The wood duck, whose scientific classification is Aix sponsa, is native to North America.
The two living members of the genus Aix are renowned for their colourful plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood duck - a species of duck native to North America, member of the genus Aix
  • Synonym: mandarin duck - a species of duck native to East Asia, member of the genus Aix

Etymology

From Ancient Greek αἴξ (aix, 'water bird' or 'goat').

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.ɪks / (BrE)
A genus of ducks that includes the wood duck and mandarin duck.
genus of ducks (wood duck, mandarin duck)

Examples

The beautiful plumage of the Aix species fascinates many bird watchers.
Scientists are researching migration patterns of the Aix genus.
Both the wood duck and the mandarin duck belong to the Aix genus.

Etymology

From Greek aix (goat), a name applied to waterfowl in ancient zoological texts.
