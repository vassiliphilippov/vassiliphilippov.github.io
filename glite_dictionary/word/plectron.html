plectron

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛk.trən / (AmE), / ˈplɛk.trən / (BrE)
a small thin tool, often of metal, plastic, or ivory, used to pluck the strings of a musical instrument
tool for plucking stringed instruments

Examples

The musician used a plectron to produce bright, clear notes from the lute.
In ancient times, performers often carved a plectron from ivory or bone.
Her antique harp was accompanied by a decorative silver plectron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plectrum - a small flat tool for plucking or strumming stringed instruments

Etymology

From Greek *plēktron* (something to strike with, a plectrum).
