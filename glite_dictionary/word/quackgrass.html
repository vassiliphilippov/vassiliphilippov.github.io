quackgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwækˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkwakˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial European grass (Elymus repens) that spreads quickly through creeping rhizomes and is considered a troublesome weed, especially in North America.
a persistent weedy grass species

Examples

Quackgrass often invades gardens and lawns, making it difficult to remove completely.
Farmers consider quackgrass a major nuisance due to its aggressive growth and resilience.
The field was overrun with quackgrass, outcompeting most other plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couch grass - alternative name for quackgrass
