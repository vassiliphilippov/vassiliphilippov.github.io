co-op

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.ɑp / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.ɒp / (BrE)
a business or organization owned and operated by a group of people for their mutual benefit, often involving the production or distribution of goods and services
jointly owned business organization

Examples

The local farmers formed a co-op to sell their produce directly to consumers.
She shops at the neighborhood co-op because it offers organic products.
The members of the co-op share profits equally among themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperative - a business owned and operated for the benefit of those using its services

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'cooperative', from Late Latin 'cooperari' (to work together).
