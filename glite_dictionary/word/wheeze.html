wheeze

1. NOUN

/ wiːz / (AmE), / wiːz / (BrE)
A sound made while breathing that is husky or whistling, typically caused by some obstruction of the air passages.
a whistling or husky breathing sound

Examples

The patient’s wheeze was clearly audible during the examination.
A persistent wheeze is often a symptom of asthma in children.
I could hear the faint wheeze of his breath as he slept.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattle - a harsh, rattling breathing sound, often linked with respiratory distress

Etymology

Imitative; from earlier 'wheese' and related to the verb 'wheeze'.

3. VERB

/ wiz / (AmE), / wiːz / (BrE)
to breathe with difficulty, producing a whistling or rattling sound in the chest
breathe with a whistling sound

Examples

After running up the stairs, he began to wheeze from the effort.
The old man would often wheeze during the night due to his asthma.
Her chest tightened, and she started to wheeze in the cold air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pant - breathe quickly and with difficulty
  • Synonym: gasp - draw in breath sharply

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *hwæsan* (to breathe heavily), imitative in origin.
