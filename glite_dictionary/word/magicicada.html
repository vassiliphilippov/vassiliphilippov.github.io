Magicicada

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædʒɪsɪˈkeɪdə / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪsɪˈkeɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of cicadas found in North America, known for their long life cycles of 13 or 17 years spent underground before emerging in large numbers.
genus of periodical cicadas

Examples

The Magicicada emerges from the ground every seventeen years in some regions.
Researchers studied the mating calls of the Magicicada during its mass emergence.
Many people travel to see the remarkable event when Magicicada broods cover the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodical cicada - another term for Magicicada, cicadas with periodic emergence

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'magicus' (magical) and 'Cicada' (cicada).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmædʒɪsɪˈkeɪdə / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪsɪˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
a genus of North American cicadas known for their periodic emergence every thirteen or seventeen years, commonly called 'seventeen-year locusts'
a genus of periodical North American cicadas

Examples

Millions of Magicicada emerged across the eastern United States in a spectacular natural event.
The Magicicada species are famous for their unique life cycles of either thirteen or seventeen years.
Scientists are studying how Magicicada synchronize their long emergence intervals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - insect with large eyes and transparent wings, known for loud song

Etymology

From Modern Latin, constructed from magicus (magical) + cicada (cicada) due to their striking periodic emergence.
