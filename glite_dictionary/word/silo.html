silo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləʊ / (BrE)
A military installation consisting of an underground structure used for storing and launching ballistic missiles.
underground missile storage and launch facility

Examples

The government revealed the location of a new silo designed for intercontinental missiles.
Each silo is protected by reinforced concrete to withstand attacks.
Satellite images showed several silos hidden beneath the missile base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile silo - underground facility for storing and firing missiles
  • Antonym: launch pad - surface platform for launching rockets or missiles

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪləʊ / (BrE)
A tall, cylindrical structure, typically made of concrete or metal, used for storing silage or grain on a farm.
cylindrical tower for storing silage or grain

Examples

The farmer filled the silo with freshly harvested corn for the winter.
Modern farms often have more than one silo to store different types of feed.
After the harvest, the workers inspected the silo to ensure it was properly sealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granary - a storehouse for threshed grain

Etymology

From Spanish 'silo' (pit for holding grain), from Latin 'sirus', of Greek origin.
