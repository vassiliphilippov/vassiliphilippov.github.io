multiplex

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltɪˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪplɛks / (BrE)
A system or device that transmits two or more signals over a common channel.
system transmitting multiple signals over one channel

Examples

A multiplex allows television providers to broadcast several channels over a single frequency.
The engineers installed a new multiplex to manage the building’s data transmissions.
Without a multiplex, each signal would require its own dedicated line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiplexer - a device that combines multiple signals for transmission over a single medium

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplex' (having many folds or parts), from 'multi-' (many) + 'plex' (fold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌl.tiˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈmʌl.tɪ.pleks / (BrE)
a cinema complex with several separate auditoriums within one building, allowing multiple films to be shown simultaneously
cinema with multiple auditoriums

Examples

We decided to watch the new action movie at the multiplex downtown.
The multiplex offers a wide selection of films every night of the week.
It's convenient having a multiplex near my house, so I can always find something to watch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinema - a theater where films are shown

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplex' (having many folds), used in modern English since the late 20th century for cinema complexes.
