hydrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən / (BrE)
a chemical element that is a colorless, odorless, highly flammable gas, the lightest and most abundant element in the universe, with the symbol H
a colorless, flammable chemical element (H)

Examples

Water is made up of hydrogen and oxygen atoms bonded together.
The balloon rose quickly once it was filled with hydrogen gas.
Scientists are exploring ways to use hydrogen as a clean energy source.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: helium - a chemical element, the second lightest gas
  • Synonym: oxygen - a common gaseous element essential for respiration

Etymology

From French hydrogène, from Greek hydōr (water) + genes (forming, producing).
