doggerel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔːɡərəl / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡərəl / (BrE)
comic verse composed in irregular or loose rhythm, often regarded as trivial or crude
comic verse of irregular measure

Examples

His poem was dismissed as mere doggerel by the critics.
She recited some doggerel that made everyone laugh.
The children amused themselves by composing doggerel about their teachers.

Synset Examples

he had heard some silly doggerel that kept running through his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verse - writing arranged with a metrical rhythm, typically having rhyme
  • Degree Higher: poetry - literary work in which special intensity is given to the expression of feelings and ideas by the use of distinctive style and rhythm

Etymology

From Middle English dogerel, originally an adjective meaning 'of low quality', probably from dog.
