commensurate

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɛn.sə.rət / (AmE), / kəˈmɛn.sjər.ət / (BrE)
equal in size, amount, or degree; corresponding or proportionate to something else.
corresponding in size or degree

Examples

The responsibilities of the new role are commensurate with the pay offered.
Her skills are commensurate with the demands of the position.
The fines imposed must be commensurate with the severity of the offense.

Synset Examples

pay should be commensurate with the time worked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionate - having the correct relationship in size or amount
  • Antonym: disproportionate - not in correct relation or balance

Etymology

From Latin 'commensuratus', past participle of 'commensurare' (to measure together), from 'com-' (together) + 'mensurare' (to measure).
