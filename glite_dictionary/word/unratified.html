unratified

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrætɪfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrætɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a treaty, agreement, amendment, etc.) not formally approved or given legal force; not ratified.
not formally approved or ratified

Examples

The treaty remained unratified for several years due to political disagreements.
The unratified constitution amendment continues to be a topic of debate.
Because the contract was unratified, it had no binding legal effect.

Synset Examples

the unratified Equal Right Amendment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ratified - formally approved or confirmed

Etymology

From un- (not) + ratified, from Latin ratificare (to confirm).
