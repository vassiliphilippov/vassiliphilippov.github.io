vena bulbi vestibuli

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˈbʊl.baɪ ˌvɛs.tɪˈbjuː.laɪ / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˈbʊl.bɪ ˌvɛs.tɪˈbjuː.laɪ / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the vestibular bulb, located in the female genitalia.
vein of the vestibular bulb
medical

Examples

The vena bulbi vestibuli is an important vessel in the vascular anatomy of the external female genitalia.
During pelvic surgery, identifying the vena bulbi vestibuli helps prevent unnecessary bleeding.
In medical textbooks, diagrams often label the vena bulbi vestibuli to show venous drainage in the vulvar region.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' meaning 'vein', 'bulbi' meaning 'bulb', and 'vestibuli' meaning 'of the vestibule'.
