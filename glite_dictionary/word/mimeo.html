mimeo

1. VERB

/ ˈmiːmiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmiːmiəʊ / (BrE)
to print copies of a document using a mimeograph machine
make printed copies using a mimeograph
informal

Examples

She mimeoed the meeting notes for everyone to read.
Before photocopiers were common, teachers would often mimeo handouts for their classes.
They spent the afternoon mimeoing flyers for the event.

Synset Examples

She mimeographed the syllabus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimeograph - to make printed copies using a mimeograph machine

Etymology

Shortened from 'mimeograph'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01699152-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪmioʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪmiəʊ / (BrE)
a machine for making copies of documents by forcing ink through a stencil; also, a copy made using this machine
rotary stencil-based duplicator

Examples

She distributed the meeting notes on a mimeo to everyone in the office.
The classroom was filled with the distinct smell of fresh mimeo pages.
Before the rise of photocopiers, teachers often relied on a mimeo for handouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimeograph - a duplicating machine that makes copies using a stencil

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'mimeograph', from Greek mimeomai (to imitate) + -graph (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03772994-n
Synset POS: n
