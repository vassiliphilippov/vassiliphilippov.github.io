phonophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊnəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊnəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
A morbid or irrational fear of sounds, including one's own voice.
morbid fear of sounds
medical

Examples

Her phonophobia made it difficult to attend social gatherings where loud noises were common.
The patient was diagnosed with phonophobia after complaining of anxiety triggered by ordinary household sounds.
Because of his phonophobia, even the sound of his own footsteps caused him distress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acousticophobia - fear of sound or noise
  • Degree Lower: misophonia - strong dislike or hatred of specific sounds

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) + -phobia (fear).
