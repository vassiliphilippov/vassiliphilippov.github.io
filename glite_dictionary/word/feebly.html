feebly

1. ADV

/ ˈfiːbli / (AmE), / ˈfiːbli / (BrE)
In a weak or ineffective manner; lacking strength, force, or effectiveness.
in a weak or ineffective manner

Examples

He tried to explain his actions, but spoke so feebly that no one was convinced.
The candle burned feebly in the dark room, barely illuminating its surroundings.
She smiled feebly and nodded, lacking the energy to respond with words.

Synset Examples

reform, in fact, is, rather feebly, on the win

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakly - in a weak manner
  • Antonym: strongly - in a strong or forceful manner

Etymology

From feeble (from Old French 'feble', from Latin 'flebilis', meaning 'lamentable') + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈfiːbli / (AmE), / ˈfiːbli / (BrE)
In a weak or faint manner; lacking strength or force.
in a weak or faint manner

Examples

The wind blew feebly through the cracks in the window frame.
She smiled feebly, too exhausted to respond with words.
The candle flickered feebly in the dark, barely illuminating the room.

Synset Examples

the lighthouse, flashing feebly against the sleet-blurred, rocky backdrop of the coast of north west Norway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakly - in a weak manner

Etymology

From 'feeble' + '-ly'; 'feeble' from Old French 'feble', from Latin 'flebilis' (lamentable).
