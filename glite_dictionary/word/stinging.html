stinging

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
a sharp, sudden pain or sensation similar to being stung
sharp, sudden pain or sensation

Examples

He was surprised by the stinging of the icy wind against his face.
She felt a sudden stinging after touching the jellyfish.
The stinging from the paper cut lasted for several minutes.

Synset Examples

the sting of death
he felt the stinging of nettles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - a sharp pain caused by a stinger or similar object
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech or remarks) harsh, hurtful, or critical in tone or character; intended to wound or criticize sharply.
harsh or hurtful in tone

Examples

The politician responded with a stinging rebuke to his critics.
Her stinging sarcasm left everyone in the room silent.
He regretted his stinging words as soon as he saw her reaction.

Synset Examples

cutting remarks
edged satire
a stinging comment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - (of a remark) intending to wound or offend
  • Synonym: caustic - (of language) able to hurt or criticize severely
  • Antonym: gentle - showing a kind, mild, or tender quality

Etymology

From the verb 'sting', meaning to wound with a sharp point, used figuratively for speech since the 17th century.
