scabies

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪbiz / (AmE), / ˈskeɪbiːz / (BrE)
A contagious skin disease caused by the itch mite, characterized by intense itching and skin irritation.
contagious skin disease (mite infestation)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with scabies after noticing red, itchy rashes on her arms.
Outbreaks of scabies are common in crowded living conditions such as dormitories.
If you suspect you have scabies, seek medical treatment to prevent spreading it to others.

Synset Examples

he has a bad case of the itch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: itch - a persistent skin irritation marked by the desire to scratch; often a symptom of scabies

Etymology

From Latin *scabere* (to scratch).
