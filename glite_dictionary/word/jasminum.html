Jasminum

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæzmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈjazmɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and woody climbers, including the jasmines, found mainly in tropical and temperate regions of the Old World.
genus of shrubs (jasmine)

Examples

The genus Jasminum includes about 200 species distributed across Asia, Africa, and Europe.
Botanists are studying Jasminum to develop more fragrant varieties for ornamental gardens.
Jasminum sambac is especially prized in perfumery for its intense scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jasmine - a plant of the genus Jasminum, often valued for its fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'jasminum', from Arabic 'yāsamīn' (jasmine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæzmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒazmɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and woody climbers, chiefly native to tropical and temperate regions of the Old World, that includes species commonly known as jasmine.
botanical genus of jasmine plants

Examples

The botanical name for true jasmine is Jasminum.
Several species of Jasminum are cultivated for their fragrant flowers.
Jasminum sambac is widely used in perfumes and teas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jasmine - common name for species within the genus Jasminum

Etymology

From Latin 'jasminum', from Arabic 'yas(a)min' (jasmine).
