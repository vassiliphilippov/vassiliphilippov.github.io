existentialist

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛɡ.zɪˈstɛn.ʃə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɛɡ.zɪˈstɛn.ʃə.lɪst / (BrE)
relating to or involving existentialism, a philosophical theory emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice
relating to existentialism

Examples

The novel's protagonist is faced with an existentialist dilemma about the meaning of life.
Her thesis explores the existentialist influences on modern art.
Many 20th-century poets adopted an existentialist approach in their writings.

Synset Examples

existentialist movement
existentialist philosophy
the existentialist character of his ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existential - relating to existence, especially human existence

Etymology

From existentialism, which is derived from French existentialisme, based on Latin existentia (existence).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛɡˌzɪˈstɛn(t)ʃələst / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈstɛnʃəlɪst / (BrE)
a person, especially a philosopher, who emphasizes individual freedom and responsibility in an incomprehensible or indifferent universe
adherent of existentialism; philosopher about human existence

Examples

Jean-Paul Sartre is widely regarded as a leading existentialist of the twentieth century.
As an existentialist, she believes that individuals are responsible for finding their own meaning in life.
Many existentialist writers explore themes of alienation and freedom in their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existentialism - the philosophical theory or approach emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice

Etymology

From existential + -ist, after French existentialiste (20th century).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10091345-n
Synset POS: n
