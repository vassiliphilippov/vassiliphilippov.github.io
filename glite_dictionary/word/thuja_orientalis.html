Thuja orientalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈθuːdʒə ɔːr.i.ˈɛn.təl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈθjuːdʒə ɒr.i.ˈɛn.tə.lɪs / (BrE)
an evergreen Asiatic shrub or small tree, widely planted as an ornamental in gardens, sometimes classified in its own genus.
an Asiatic shrub or small tree, ornamental plant

Examples

Many gardeners choose to plant Thuja orientalis for its evergreen foliage and compact growth.
The Thuja orientalis in the front yard has grown over ten feet tall in just a few years.
Horticulturists sometimes classify Thuja orientalis in a separate genus, Platycladus.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from the genus name 'Thuja' and Latin 'orientalis' meaning 'eastern'.
