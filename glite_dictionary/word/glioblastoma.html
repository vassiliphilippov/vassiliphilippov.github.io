glioblastoma

1. NOUN

/ ɡliːoʊblæˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / ɡliːəʊblɑːˈstəʊmə / (BrE)
a fast-growing and aggressive malignant tumor of the brain, typically arising from glial cells, and nearly always fatal
malignant brain tumor
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a glioblastoma after experiencing persistent headaches and confusion.
Researchers are working to develop more effective treatments for glioblastoma, given its poor prognosis.
A glioblastoma is known for its rapid growth and resistance to conventional therapies.

Etymology

From Greek 'glia' (glue) + 'blastoma' (tumor arising from precursor cells).
