fishworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A worm, especially an earthworm, used as bait by anglers; often surfaces when the ground is wet or cool.
worm used as fish bait

Examples

After the rain, fishworms covered the sidewalk, making it easy for anglers to gather bait.
He dug through the garden soil, hoping to find a fishworm for his afternoon fishing trip.
Many fishermen believe that using a live fishworm increases their chances of catching big fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a common terrestrial worm that lives in soil; often used as bait

Etymology

From 'fish' + 'worm', referring to worms used for fishing bait.
