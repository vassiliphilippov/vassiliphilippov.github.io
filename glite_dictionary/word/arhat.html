Arhat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrˌhɑt / (AmE), / ˈɑːhæt / (BrE)
In Buddhism, a person who has attained nirvana and is freed from the cycle of rebirth.
person in Buddhism who has attained nirvana

Examples

After years of meditation and discipline, he was revered as an Arhat by his followers.
The teachings describe how a true Arhat is free from all attachments and desires.
Several ancient Buddhist texts recount the lives of prominent Arhats and their spiritual journeys.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bodhisattva - one who aspires to attain Buddhahood for the sake of all beings
  • Synonym: nirvana - the final goal of Buddhism, liberation from rebirth and suffering

Etymology

From Sanskrit arhat (worthy, venerable), from the root arh (to be worthy).
