Umbellularia

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌmbəˌluːˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌʌmbjʊˌlʊˈleəriə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic evergreen trees native to the Pacific coast of North America, including the California bay laurel.
genus of aromatic Pacific coast evergreen trees

Examples

The leaves of the Umbellularia tree are often used as a culinary herb in California.
Umbellularia is well known for its strong, spicy fragrance when its leaves are crushed.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Umbellularia in its native Pacific coastal environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California bay laurel - An aromatic evergreen tree native to western North America, species of the Umbellularia genus

Etymology

From Latin 'umbella' (umbrella) + '-aria', referring to the umbrella-like arrangement of the flower clusters.
