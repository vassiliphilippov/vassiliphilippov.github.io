to masticate

1. VERB

/ ˈmæstəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to chew (food) by biting and grinding it with the teeth, especially in a deliberate or thorough manner
chew food with the teeth

Examples

Cows spend a large part of the day masticating their food in the pasture.
Your dentist recommends that you thoroughly masticate each mouthful before swallowing.
He nervously masticated his sandwich while waiting for the interview.

Synset Examples

He jawed his bubble gum
Chew your food and don't swallow it!
The cows were masticating the grass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew - to crush food with the teeth before swallowing
  • Antonym: swallow - to cause or allow something to pass down the throat

Etymology

From Latin 'masticare' (to chew), from Greek 'mastikhan'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmæstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
To grind, knead, or soften a material, especially by mechanical means; used in processing substances such as rubber.
grind or knead a material

Examples

Modern machines masticate rubber to prepare it for manufacturing.
Before molding, the compound must be thoroughly masticated.
The technician monitored the process while the material was masticating in the mixer.

Synset Examples

masticate rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knead - to work a substance, such as dough or rubber, with the hands or a machine to make it smooth or flexible
  • Synonym: grind - to break something into small pieces or powder by pressing or rubbing it

Etymology

From Latin masticare (to chew), from Greek mastikhan (to gnash the teeth).
