rancher

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈræn.tʃə / (BrE)
a person who owns or manages a ranch, especially one raising cattle or sheep
person who owns or manages a ranch

Examples

The rancher inspected his cattle every morning before sunrise.
As a young rancher, she learned how to ride horses and manage the land.
Several ranchers attended the meeting to discuss water rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - person who owns or manages a farm

Etymology

From American English 'ranch' + '-er', denoting one who operates a ranch.
