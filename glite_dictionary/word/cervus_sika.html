Cervus sika

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvəs ˈsiːkə / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəs ˈsiːkə / (BrE)
A small deer native to Japan, known for its slightly forked antlers and spotted coat; also called sika deer.
small Japanese deer species

Examples

The Cervus sika can often be seen grazing in the forests of Japan.
Unlike native species, Cervus sika have a distinctive spotted coat throughout adulthood.
Conservationists are concerned about the impact of introduced Cervus sika populations on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sika deer - common name for Cervus sika

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Cervus' (deer) and Japanese 'shika' (deer).
