prolonge

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈlɒndʒ / (AmE), / prəˈlɒndʒ / (BrE)
A rope with a hook, used for towing a gun carriage, especially in artillery operations.
rope for towing a gun carriage

Examples

The artillery crew attached the prolonge to the gun carriage to help move it across the field.
After the battle, the prolonge was coiled neatly and stored with the rest of the equipment.
Without a sturdy prolonge, it would have been difficult to reposition the heavy cannons quickly.

Etymology

From French 'prolonge', meaning prolongation, referring to its use in extending the movement of artillery pieces.
