pragmatic

1. ADJ

/ præɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Dealing with problems or situations in a practical way rather than by using theory or abstract principles.
practical; focused on results

Examples

She offered a pragmatic solution to the complicated problem, focusing on what could actually be done.
His pragmatic attitude helped the team overcome many unexpected obstacles.
Instead of dreaming of ideal outcomes, the manager remained pragmatic about what was possible within the budget.

Synset Examples

a matter-of-fact (or pragmatic) approach to the problem
a matter-of-fact account of the trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice

Etymology

From Greek pragmatikos (relating to fact or deed), via Latin pragmaticus.

2. ADJ

/ præɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a way that is based on practical considerations rather than theoretical ones
practical and realistic; not theoretical

Examples

His pragmatic approach to problem-solving helped the team find an effective solution quickly.
Rather than discussing theories all day, she preferred to take a pragmatic stance and get things done.
They admired the mayor's pragmatic leadership during the crisis.

Synset Examples

a hardheaded appraisal of our position
a hard-nosed labor leader
completely practical in his approach to business
not ideology but pragmatic politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theories
  • Antonym: idealistic - guided more by ideals or theory than by practical considerations

Etymology

From Greek 'pragmatikos' (relating to fact or deed), via Late Latin 'pragmaticus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01947268-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ prægˈmætɪk / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the theory of pragmatism, a philosophical approach that evaluates theories or beliefs in terms of the success of their practical application.
relating to pragmatism (philosophy)

Examples

The pragmatic approach to philosophy emphasizes the practical consequences of ideas.
Her research centers on pragmatic principles rather than abstract theory.
Many debates in education policy now include a pragmatic perspective rooted in real-world outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatism - philosophical theory that truth is measured by practical effects

Etymology

From Greek pragmatikos (practical), from pragma (deed, act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02793891-a
Synset POS: a
