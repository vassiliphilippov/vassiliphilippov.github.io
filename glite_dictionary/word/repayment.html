repayment

1. NOUN

/ riˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the act of paying back money that was borrowed or received earlier
act of paying money back

Examples

The repayment of the loan will begin next month.
He struggled to keep up with his monthly repayment obligations.
Early repayment may result in a penalty fee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the act of paying money
  • Synonym: loan - money that is borrowed and expected to be repaid

Etymology

From the verb 'repay' (from re- + pay) plus the suffix -ment.

2. NOUN

/ riˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the act of paying back money that has been borrowed or owed
the act of paying back money owed

Examples

The monthly repayment on the loan is due every 15th of the month.
She made a final repayment and cleared her debt entirely.
Early repayment of the mortgage can sometimes lead to penalties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the action or process of paying someone or something
  • Antonym: debt - a sum of money that is owed or due

Etymology

Formed as 're-' (again) + 'payment' (from pay + -ment).
