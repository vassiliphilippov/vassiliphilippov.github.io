Casey Jones

1. PROPN

/ ˈkeɪsi ˈdʒoʊnz / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsi ˈdʒəʊnz / (BrE)
(1863–1900) An American railroad engineer famous for his heroic death while trying to prevent a train collision, commemorated in a popular ballad.
famous American railroad engineer (1863–1900)
american

Examples

Many American folk songs tell the story of Casey Jones and his fateful ride.
Children learned about Casey Jones in school while studying railroad history.
The legend of Casey Jones continues to inspire railroad workers to this day.
