liquescent

1. ADJ

/ lɪˈkwɛsənt / (AmE), / lɪˈkwɛsənt / (BrE)
becoming liquid; in the process of turning from solid into liquid.
becoming liquid

Examples

The ice was liquescent as the temperature rose above freezing.
A liquescent substance may be unstable at room temperature.
Certain metals become liquescent under intense heat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume

Etymology

From Latin liquescens, present participle of liquescere (to become liquid).
