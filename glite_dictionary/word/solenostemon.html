Solenostemon

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌlɪnoʊˈstiːmən / (AmE), / səʊˌlɛnɒˈstiːmən / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby, often succulent herbs native to tropical Africa and Asia, including some plants previously classified under the genus Coleus.
genus of tropical shrubby herbs

Examples

The brightly colored leaves of Solenostemon are popular in ornamental gardening.
Recent studies have reclassified some species from Coleus to the genus Solenostemon.
Solenostemon thrives in well-drained soil with moderate humidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coleus - genus of flowering plants, sometimes merged with Solenostemon

Etymology

From Greek 'solen' (tube) + 'stemon' (stamen), referring to tubular stamens.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊləˈnɒstəmɒn / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈnɒstɪmɒn / (BrE)
a genus of shrubby, often succulent herbs native to tropical Africa and Asia, including some species formerly classified under Coleus
genus of tropical African and Asian herbs

Examples

Many gardeners grow Solenostemon for its vibrant foliage.
The Solenostemon species thrive in warm, humid climates.
Several varieties of Solenostemon were once classified as Coleus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coleus - a former genus now often included in Solenostemon or Plectranthus, known for ornamental foliage

Etymology

From Greek *solen* (pipe) + *stēmon* (thread), referring to the form of the stamens.
