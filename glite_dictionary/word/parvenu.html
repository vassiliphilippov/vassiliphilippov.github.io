parvenu

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.vəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.və.njuː / (BrE)
a person who has suddenly gained wealth or influence but has not yet been accepted by others of that social class
person newly risen to wealth or status

Examples

The self-made millionaire was treated as a parvenu by the old aristocratic families.
Despite his financial success, he was often dismissed as merely a parvenu among the established elite.
Many socialites considered her a parvenu who did not understand their customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upstart - a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or power

Etymology

From French 'parvenu' (past participle of 'parvenir', meaning 'to arrive, to reach').

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑɹ.vəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.və.njuː / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a person who has recently acquired wealth or social status but is not yet accepted by the social class to which they aspire.
of or relating to a newly rich person

Examples

The family's parvenu taste was evident in their lavish, yet tasteless, home decor.
High society often looks down on parvenu manners as lacking refinement and tradition.
Many critics dismissed the parvenu elegance of their parties as mere show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nouveau riche - A person who has recently become rich, especially one flaunting new wealth

Etymology

From French parvenu, past participle of parvenir (to arrive).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɑɹ.vəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.və.njuː / (BrE)
Having or showing the characteristics of someone who has recently gained wealth or social position but lacks the manners or sophistication expected in that new status.
newly rich or socially elevated, lacking refinement

Examples

His parvenu manners made him the subject of ridicule among the old aristocracy.
The parvenu family tried to impress their neighbors with lavish parties but failed to fit in.
Despite her wealth, she remained a parvenu in the eyes of high society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nouveau riche - a person newly rich, especially one lacking good taste

Etymology

From French 'parvenu', from past participle of 'parvenir' meaning 'to arrive' or 'attain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01856790-s
Synset POS: s
