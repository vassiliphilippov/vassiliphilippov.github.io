flagrant

1. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪɡrənt / (AmE), / ˈfleɪɡrənt / (BrE)
(of an action considered wrong or immoral) conspicuously and outrageously bad; shockingly noticeable or evident.
conspicuously and outrageously bad

Examples

The referee ignored a flagrant foul just minutes before the game ended.
It was a flagrant abuse of company policy to share confidential information.
She was dismissed for a flagrant disregard of safety regulations.

Synset Examples

a crying shame
an egregious lie
flagrant violation of human rights
a glaring error
gross ineptitude
gross injustice
rank treachery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egregious - outstandingly bad; shocking
  • Synonym: glaring - highly and obviously bad
  • Degree Lower: minor - not very serious or significant (opposite on the severity spectrum)

Etymology

From Latin flagrans, flagrant- (burning, blazing), present participle of flagrāre (to blaze).
