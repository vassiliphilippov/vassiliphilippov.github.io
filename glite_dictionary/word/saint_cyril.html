Saint Cyril

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈsɪrɪl / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈsɪrɪl / (BrE)
A Greek missionary (826–869) who, along with his brother Methodius, is credited with inventing the Cyrillic alphabet and spreading Christianity among the Slavic peoples.
Greek missionary, creator of Cyrillic alphabet

Examples

The invention of the Cyrillic script by Saint Cyril greatly influenced the culture of Eastern Europe.
Many Slavic countries honor Saint Cyril for bringing them the written word and Christianity.
During the festival, students performed a play about the lives of Saint Cyril and his brother Methodius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Methodius - Brother of Saint Cyril and fellow missionary to the Slavs
  • Synonym: Cyrillic - Alphabet attributed to Saint Cyril and Saint Methodius

Etymology

Saint's name from Greek Kyrillos, from kyrios meaning 'lord'.
