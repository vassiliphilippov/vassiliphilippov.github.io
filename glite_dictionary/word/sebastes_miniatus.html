Sebastes miniatus

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈbæstiːz mɪˈniːətəs / (AmE), / sɪˈbæstiːz mɪˈniːətəs / (BrE)
A species of rockfish found along the Pacific coast of North America, valued as a commercial food fish.
species of rockfish; Pacific coast food fish

Examples

Commercial fisheries heavily rely on the Sebastes miniatus catch along the California coast.
Many anglers seek Sebastes miniatus for its distinctive red coloration and firm flesh.
The population status of Sebastes miniatus is closely monitored to prevent overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermilion rockfish - Common name for Sebastes miniatus, the red-colored Pacific rockfish

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sebastes' (a genus name) and 'miniatus' (vermilion-colored).
