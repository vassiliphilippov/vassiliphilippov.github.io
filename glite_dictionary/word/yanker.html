yanker

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈjaŋkə / (BrE)
a person who gives a strong, sudden pull to something
someone who pulls something sharply

Examples

The mechanic acted as the yanker who freed the stuck cable with a single motion.
A skilled yanker is sometimes needed to open old, stubborn doors.
Children often take turns being the yanker in a tug-of-war game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yanker - someone from the northern United States (slang, chiefly British or Southern US)

Etymology

From the verb 'yank' (to pull suddenly), plus agentive suffix '-er'.
