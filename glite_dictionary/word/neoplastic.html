neoplastic

1. ADJ

/ niː.oʊˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / niː.əʊˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a neoplasm (an abnormal growth of tissue, especially as a characteristic of cancer)
relating to abnormal tissue growth (neoplasm)
medical

Examples

The biopsy showed that the mass contained neoplastic cells consistent with malignancy.
Doctors are researching new treatments for neoplastic diseases such as cancer.
Certain viruses are known to induce neoplastic changes in animal tissues.

Synset Examples

neoplastic cells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neoplasm - an abnormal mass of tissue as a result of excessive cell division

Etymology

From neo- (new) and Greek plasis (formation); see neoplasm.
