sweetie

1. NOUN

/ ˈswiːti / (AmE), / ˈswiːti / (BrE)
A person who is loved by someone, often used as a term of endearment.
person who is loved; term of affection
informal

Examples

She called her granddaughter sweetie as she tucked her into bed.
Don't worry, sweetie, everything will be all right.
He bought his sweetie a bunch of flowers for her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person who is dearly loved
  • Synonym: honey - affectionate term for a loved person

Etymology

From 'sweet' + diminutive suffix '-ie', used as a term of endearment since the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswidi / (AmE), / ˈswiːti / (BrE)
A person, especially a child or romantic partner, regarded with affection; a term of endearment.
term of endearment for a loved one

Examples

Goodnight, sweetie, sleep well.
She called her granddaughter sweetie and gave her a hug.
Don't worry, sweetie, everything will be fine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - A person dearly loved; a term of endearment
  • Synonym: honey - Term of endearment for a loved person

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'sweet', meaning beloved or dear.
