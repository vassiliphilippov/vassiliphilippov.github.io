onwards

1. ADV

/ ˈɑːnwərdz / (AmE), / ˈɒnwədz / (BrE)
In a forward direction; continuing forward or toward a later time or place.
in a forward direction

Examples

The road wound onwards through the dense forest, disappearing into the mist.
From that point onwards, their journey became much more challenging.
He decided to focus on his studies onwards, determined to achieve better results.

Synset Examples

go ahead
the train moved ahead slowly
the boat lurched ahead
moved onward into the forest
they went slowly forward in the mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - in the direction that one is facing or traveling
  • Antonym: backwards - in the opposite direction; toward the rear

Etymology

From Old English *onweard* (toward), from 'on' + '-ward' (directional suffix).
