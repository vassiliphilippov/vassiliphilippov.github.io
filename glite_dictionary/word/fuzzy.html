fuzzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌzi / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi / (BrE)
indistinct or hazy in outline; not clear or sharp
indistinct or blurry in outline

Examples

The photograph was fuzzy and it was difficult to recognize the faces.
Through the mist, the mountains looked fuzzy and unreal.
Her memory of the incident became increasingly fuzzy over time.

Synset Examples

a landscape of blurred outlines
the trees were just blurry shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurry - lacking clear definition; not sharp
  • Antonym: sharp - clear and distinct in outline

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌzi / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi / (BrE)
(of ideas or information) confused and not clear; not expressed or thought out clearly.
not clear or confused in ideas

Examples

She has only a fuzzy memory of what happened that day.
His explanation of the plan was too fuzzy to follow.
I'm still feeling fuzzy about the details of the project.

Synset Examples

a vague and fuzzy idea of the world of finance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed, known, described, or decided
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; not confused
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00467576-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfʌzi / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi / (BrE)
covered with fine, light, and soft hairs or fibers
covered with fine soft hairs or fibers

Examples

The kitten's fuzzy fur made it look like a little ball of fluff.
After shaving, his cheeks were no longer fuzzy to the touch.
The leaves of the plant feel a bit fuzzy when you brush your fingers over them.

Synset Examples

his head fuzzed like a dandelion gone to seed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairy - covered with hair
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from hair, roughness, or projections

Etymology

Probably from 'fuzz' (fluffy mass of hair or fiber) + -y.
