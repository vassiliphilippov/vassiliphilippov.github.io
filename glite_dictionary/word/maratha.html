Maratha

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːtə / (AmE), / məˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
a member of a people of western India, primarily living in the state of Maharashtra
member of a people from western India

Examples

The Maratha empire played a significant role in Indian history during the 17th and 18th centuries.
Many Maratha traditions are still celebrated in Maharashtra today.
As a Maratha, he was proud of his community's rich heritage.

Etymology

From Marathi 'Marāṭhā', designating the ethnic group.
