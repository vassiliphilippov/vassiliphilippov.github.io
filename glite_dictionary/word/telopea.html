Telopea

1. NOUN

/ təˈloʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / təˈləʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large shrubs native to Australia, known for their striking, brightly colored flower heads, commonly called waratahs.
genus of Australian shrubs; waratahs

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of the Telopea are a symbol of New South Wales.
Gardeners often plant Telopea for its dramatic blooms and attractive foliage.
Several species of Telopea are cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waratah - common name for the Telopea flower

Etymology

From Greek *telopos* meaning 'seen from afar', referring to the showy flowers.

2. NOUN

/ təˈloʊpiə / (AmE), / təˈləʊpiə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs in the family Proteaceae, known for their large, showy red flowers, native to Australia and commonly known as waratahs.
genus of Australian shrubs (waratahs)

Examples

The bright red flowers of the Telopea are a symbol of New South Wales.
Botanists studied several species within the Telopea genus to understand their evolution.
Gardeners often plant Telopea for its spectacular blossoms and native appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waratah - the common name for plants of the genus Telopea

Etymology

From Greek 'telopos', meaning 'seen from afar'; referring to the conspicuous flowers.
