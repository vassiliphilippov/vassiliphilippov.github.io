neutron

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.trɑːn / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.trɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle with no electric charge and a mass nearly equal to that of a proton, present in all atomic nuclei except those of ordinary hydrogen
an uncharged subatomic particle

Examples

The neutron is one of the three main subatomic particles that make up an atom.
During nuclear fission, a neutron collides with the nucleus, causing it to split.
Ordinary hydrogen atoms lack a neutron in their nuclei.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: proton - a positively charged subatomic particle in atomic nuclei
  • Degree Lower: electron - a negatively charged subatomic particle orbiting atomic nuclei

Etymology

From Greek 'neutros' (neutral), referencing its lack of electric charge, formed on analogy with 'electron' and 'proton'.
