sedentary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛdn̩ˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɛd.ən.tər.i / (BrE)
(of a person, job, or lifestyle) involving much sitting and little physical activity.
characterized by much sitting and little activity

Examples

A sedentary lifestyle can increase the risk of chronic diseases.
Many office workers have sedentary jobs that require long hours at a desk.
After his injury, he became more sedentary and spent most days indoors.

Synset Examples

forced by illness to lead a sedentary life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaging or ready to engage in physically energetic pursuits
  • Synonym: inactive - not engaging in much physical activity

Etymology

From Latin sedentarius, from sedere (to sit).
