somewhat

1. ADV

/ ˈsʌmˌwʌt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmwɒt / (BrE)
to a moderate degree or extent; rather; slightly
to a moderate degree or extent

Examples

The instructions were somewhat unclear, so I had to ask for help.
The weather was somewhat better today than yesterday, but it was still chilly.
She looked somewhat surprised when she heard the news.

Synset Examples

pretty big
pretty bad
jolly decent of him
the shoes are priced reasonably
he is fairly clever with computers

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree or extent
  • Synonym: rather - to a certain or significant degree
  • Degree Higher: very - to a high degree or extent

Etymology

From Middle English 'sumwhat', from Old English 'sum' (some) + 'hwæt' (what).

2. ADV

/ ˈsʌmˌwʌt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmwɒt / (BrE)
to a small degree; slightly or moderately
to a small degree or extent

Examples

The film was somewhat disappointing, but the performances were excellent.
She looked somewhat confused by the instructions.
His mood seemed somewhat improved after the walk.

Synset Examples

his arguments were somewhat self-contradictory
the children argued because one slice of cake was slightly larger than the other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slightly - to a small degree; a little
  • Degree Higher: moderately - to an average or limited extent

Etymology

From Middle English 'somwhat', 'sumwhat', literally 'some' + 'what'.
