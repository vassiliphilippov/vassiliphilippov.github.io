chuckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkəl / (BrE)
a soft, partly suppressed laugh
a soft, quiet laugh

Examples

He let out a quiet chuckle as he read the comic.
A soft chuckle escaped her lips during the meeting.
We could hear his chuckle from across the room.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: giggle - a light, silly laugh
  • Degree Higher: laugh - the act of laughing aloud
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07142271-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌk.əl / (BrE)
to laugh quietly, especially in a restrained or suppressed way
to laugh quietly or softly

Examples

She couldn't help but chuckle at his silly joke.
He chuckled softly to himself while reading the funny story.
The children chuckling quietly in the corner made the teacher smile.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: giggle - to laugh with repeated short, high sounds
  • Degree Higher: laugh - to make spontaneous sounds and movements of the face and body expressing amusement
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, first attested in English in the late 16th century.
