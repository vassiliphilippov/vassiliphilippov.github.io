untied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪd / (BrE)
(of something that was tied) not fastened, bound, or secured; not tied.
not tied or fastened

Examples

Her shoelaces were untied, so she stopped to fix them.
The package arrived untied, with the string dangling loosely.
He noticed his necktie was untied before the important meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tied - fastened or bound with a tie
  • Synonym: loose - not firmly or tightly fixed in place; detached or easily moved

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪd / (BrE)
(of laces, knots, or similar things) not fastened or secured; not tied.
not fastened; not tied

Examples

Her shoes were untied, so she stopped to fix them before running across the street.
The boy laughed as his untied balloon floated away into the sky.
If your laces are untied, you might trip and fall.

Synset Examples

teenagers slopping around in unlaced sneakers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tied - fastened or secured with a knot or string

Etymology

Formed by prefixing un- (not) to 'tied'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00255481-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪd / (BrE)
not held by shackles, chains, or other bindings; released from physical restraint.
not bound; released from restraints

Examples

The prisoner remained untied but closely watched by the guards.
Once untied, she stretched her arms to restore circulation to her wrists.
The horse was untied and allowed to wander freely in the field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tied - held or fastened with a cord, rope, etc.; bound

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not', and 'tied' (from Old English 'tīegian', to tie).

Source: WordNet
