quintipara

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɪpərə / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɪpərə / (BrE)
(in obstetrics) a woman who has given birth to a viable infant in each of five separate pregnancies.
woman who has given birth five times (medical)
medical

Examples

The medical team carefully monitored the progress of the quintipara during her labor and delivery.
As a quintipara, she had plenty of advice for new mothers based on her experiences.
The study compared complications among primiparas and quintiparas in the maternity ward.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: primipara - woman who has given birth once
  • Degree Lower: multipara - woman who has given birth more than once
  • Degree Lower: secundipara - woman who has given birth twice

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) + 'para' (a woman who has given birth).
