bon ton

1. NOUN

/ bɔ̃ ˈtɑn / (AmE), / bɒ̃ ˈtɒ̃ / (BrE)
The fashionable elite, especially as a social group distinguished by refined manners and style.
the fashionable elite; high society

Examples

The party was attended by members of the bon ton from all over the city.
She longed for an invitation into the world of the bon ton.
In Regency England, the bon ton set the standards for fashion and etiquette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high society - the upper echelons of social class, especially the fashionable elite
  • Antonym: commoners - people without rank or title; those not belonging to the elite

Etymology

From French 'bon ton' (good tone or taste), referring to fashionable manners.
