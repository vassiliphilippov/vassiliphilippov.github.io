looseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈluːs.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of allowing movement or having more space than necessary between parts
state of allowing movement or space between parts

Examples

There was an uncomfortable looseness in the hinge, causing the door to rattle.
The mechanic noted some looseness in the steering mechanism.
This design flaw leads to looseness where the two parts join together.

Synset Examples

there was too much play in the steering wheel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightness - the state of being tight or having little or no space for movement

Etymology

From 'loose' + '-ness'; 'loose' from Old Norse 'lauss'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈluːsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being loose; the condition of being able to move freely because something is not securely fastened or attached.
the quality of being loose or movable

Examples

The looseness of the bolt allowed the wheel to wobble while driving.
He noticed the looseness in the hinges and decided to tighten the screws.
Careful inspection revealed the looseness of the joint, making it unsafe to use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightness - the quality of being tight or tightly fastened

Etymology

From 'loose' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state of being loose.
