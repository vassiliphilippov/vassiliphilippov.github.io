commination

1. NOUN

/ kɑməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a solemn threat or declaration of divine punishment or vengeance, especially in a religious context
threat of divine punishment

Examples

The priest delivered a commination against those who broke their vows.
In medieval times, commination was often used to warn parishioners about sin.
Reading the ancient text, we encountered a fierce commination directed at wrongdoers.

Etymology

From Latin comminatio (threat), from comminari 'to threaten'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a service or prayer in the Anglican Church, especially read on Ash Wednesday, warning of God's anger against sinners and pronouncing curses upon unrepentant people
Anglican prayer or service proclaiming God's anger at sinners

Examples

The commination service is traditionally read in Anglican churches on Ash Wednesday.
During the commination, the congregation hears solemn warnings against sin and unrepentance.
In his sermon, the priest explained the historical purpose of the commination in the liturgical year.

Etymology

From Latin comminatio (threatening, menacing), related to comminari (to threaten).
