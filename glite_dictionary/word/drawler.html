drawler

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who speaks in a slow, drawn-out manner
person who speaks with a drawl

Examples

The southern drawler recounted his stories with a relaxed charm.
You could tell he was a drawler by the way he elongated every word.
Among the group, the drawler stood out for his leisurely pace of speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawl - to speak with slow, drawn-out vowels

Etymology

From 'drawl' (to speak slowly), formed with agent suffix '-er'.
