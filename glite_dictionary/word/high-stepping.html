high-stepping

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪ ˌstɛpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌstɛpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) moving with steps that are lifted high off the ground; having a conspicuously high gait.
moving with high steps

Examples

The parade featured a high-stepping marching band that impressed the crowd.
The high-stepping horse caught everyone's attention in the arena.
She walked across the wet grass in a high-stepping manner to avoid getting her shoes muddy.

Synset Examples

his high-stepped stride
a high-stepping horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strutting - walking with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant gait
