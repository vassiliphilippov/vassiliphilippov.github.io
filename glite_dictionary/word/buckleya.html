buckleya

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkliə / (AmE), / ˈbʌkliə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic shrubs native to the eastern United States, characterized by opposite leaves, small greenish flowers, and dull green, olivelike fruits.
genus of parasitic shrubs in eastern U.S.

Examples

The rare buckleya can be found growing in the understory of Appalachian forests.
Botanists studied the parasitic relationship that buckleya forms with nearby trees.
During their field survey, they documented several mature buckleya specimens near the riverbank.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Botsford Buckley, American botanist (1809–1884).
