scowl

1. NOUN

/ skaʊl / (AmE), / skaʊl / (BrE)
an angry or disapproving expression on a person's face
angry or disapproving facial expression

Examples

She gave him a fierce scowl before turning away.
The teacher's scowl silenced the noisy students instantly.
With a deep scowl, he showed his disapproval of the plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frown - a facial expression of displeasure or concentration

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'scoulen' or 'scowlen', of unknown origin.

2. VERB

/ skaʊl / (AmE), / skaʊl / (BrE)
to frown in an angry or displeased way
frown with anger or displeasure

Examples

The teacher began to scowl when the students ignored her instructions.
She couldn't help but scowl at the disappointing news.
He scowled in frustration as the car refused to start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frown - to furrow one's brow, typically in displeasure or concentration
  • Antonym: smile - to make a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'skule' (to scowl, look sullen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00033845-v
Synset POS: v
