Turdinae

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrdɪˌni / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdɪniː / (BrE)
an alternative subfamily classification comprising the thrushes, used in some ornithological systems
a subfamily of thrushes (birds)

Examples

Many ornithologists classify robins within the subfamily Turdinae.
Recent research has led to the reorganization of the Turdinae within the thrush family.
Several species of forest thrushes are traditionally placed in the Turdinae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turdidae - the family containing the thrushes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Turdus (genus of thrushes) + -inae (taxonomic suffix for subfamilies).
