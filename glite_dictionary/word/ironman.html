ironman

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌmæn / (BrE)
a person, typically a man, noted for exceptional physical strength or endurance
man of great endurance or strength

Examples

He was considered an ironman after completing the marathon in below-freezing temperatures.
The grueling triathlon attracts only the most dedicated ironmen from around the world.
Years of training transformed him from an average athlete into a true ironman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: athlete - a person who is proficient in sports and physical exercise
  • Antonym: weakling - a person lacking physical strength

Etymology

Formed from 'iron' (suggesting strength) + 'man'. Popularized in athletic contexts.
