ammine

1. NOUN

/ əˈmiːn / (AmE), / əˈmiːn / (BrE)
A complex compound in which ammonia molecules are coordinated to a central metal ion.
complex with ammonia coordinated to a metal ion
medical

Examples

The chemist synthesized a cobalt ammine to study its magnetic properties.
Many ammines are important in the field of coordination chemistry.
Platinum ammine complexes are used in some cancer treatments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amine - an organic compound derived from ammonia by replacement of one or more hydrogen atoms by organic groups

Etymology

From ammonia + -ine, indicating a compound derived from ammonia.
