Allium canadense

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm kəˈneɪdənsi / (AmE), / ˈæliəm kəˈnædens / (BrE)
A species of wild onion native to North America, known for its edible bulb and grasslike leaves.
wild onion species native to North America

Examples

The bulbs of Allium canadense are often used in traditional Native American cuisine.
Allium canadense can be found growing in fields and along roadsides throughout the Midwest.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Allium canadense for its delicate pinkish-white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild onion - common name for Allium canadense, an edible North American plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'allium' meaning 'garlic', and 'canadense' referring to Canada.
