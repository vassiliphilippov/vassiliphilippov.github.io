ammoniac

1. ADJ

/ əˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / əˈmɒniˌak / (BrE)
Relating to, containing, or resembling ammonia.
pertaining to or containing ammonia

Examples

The ammoniac odor in the lab indicated a possible spill.
This fertilizer contains ammoniac compounds essential for plant growth.
She detected a faint, ammoniac smell coming from the cleaning solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonia - a colorless pungent gas composed of nitrogen and hydrogen

Etymology

From Latin 'ammoniacus', from Greek 'ammōniakos', referring to salt obtained near the temple of Ammon in Libya.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / əˈmɒnɪak / (BrE)
an aromatic gum resin obtained from the ammoniac plant, formerly used in medicine and perfumery
aromatic gum resin from the ammoniac plant

Examples

The apothecary stored blocks of ammoniac for making plasters and ointments.
Ancient physicians valued ammoniac for its expectorant properties.
A faint, musky smell rose from the basket of ammoniac at the herbal market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum resin - a plant exudate combining gums and resins, used in medicine and industry

Etymology

From Latin ammoniacum, from Greek ammoniakon (approximate resin of Ammonia), from Ammon, an Egyptian god near whose temple the plant grew.
