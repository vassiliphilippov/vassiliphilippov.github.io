Serratia marcescens

1. NOUN

/ səˌreɪtiə mɑrˈsɛsənz / (AmE), / sɛˌreɪtɪə mɑːˈsɛsɛnz / (BrE)
a species of bacterium found in water, soil, food, and insects, known to cause infections in humans and sometimes used in scientific testing
a bacterium causing human infections
medical

Examples

Serratia marcescens was identified as the cause of the hospital's recent outbreak.
The red pigment produced by Serratia marcescens is visible in contaminated bathroom tiles.
Researchers are studying antibiotic resistance in Serratia marcescens strains found in water sources.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Serratia' named after Italian physicist Serafino Serrati; 'marcescens' from Latin meaning 'decaying'.
