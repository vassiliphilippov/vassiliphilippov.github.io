aspergill

1. NOUN

/ əsˈpɜːrdʒɪl / (AmE), / əsˈpɜːdʒɪl / (BrE)
A short-handled device with a perforated globe containing a sponge, used for sprinkling holy water in religious ceremonies.
holy water sprinkling device

Examples

The priest dipped the aspergill into the bucket before sprinkling holy water over the congregation.
During the ritual, she watched as the bishop used the aspergill to bless the altar.
An ornate aspergill from the 18th century is displayed in the cathedral's treasury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspergillum - the more common full form for the holy water sprinkler

Etymology

From Latin 'aspergillum', diminutive of 'aspergere' (to sprinkle).
