malversation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.vərˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmal.vəˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
corrupt or dishonest behavior by a person in public office, especially the misuse of funds
misconduct or corruption in public office
legal

Examples

The politician was investigated for malversation after irregularities were found in the city’s accounts.
Several officials were charged with malversation for embezzling public funds.
Public trust erodes quickly when cases of malversation come to light.

Etymology

From French 'malversation' (misbehavior), from 'malverser' (to misbehave), from Latin 'malversari' (to behave badly).
