cox

1. NOUN

/ kɑks / (AmE), / kɒks / (BrE)
the person who steers and directs a racing boat or a ship's boat, especially in rowing
person who steers a racing boat

Examples

The cox shouted encouragement as the crew pulled harder.
Without a skilled cox, the team struggled to maintain their rhythm.
During the regatta, the cox navigated the tightest turns perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coxswain - the person in charge of a boat, especially its steering

Etymology

Short for 'coxswain', originally from Middle English 'cok' (cockboat) + 'swain' (boy, servant).

2. VERB

/ kɑks / (AmE), / kɒks / (BrE)
to act as the coxswain (the person who steers and directs a racing boat) in a boat race
steer a racing boat as coxswain

Examples

She was asked to cox the varsity eight during the regatta.
I have never coxed such a challenging course before.
He enjoys rowing but prefers to cox for the novice team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coxswain - the person who steers and directs a racing boat

Etymology

Shortened form of 'coxswain', originally from Middle English 'cok(swain)' meaning 'boat servant'.
