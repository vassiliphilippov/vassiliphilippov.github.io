rima respiratoria

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːmə ˌrɛspɪrəˈtɔriə / (AmE), / ˈriːmə ˌrɛspɪrəˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
the anatomical opening between the false vocal folds in the larynx
opening between the false vocal folds
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the rima respiratoria for any signs of obstruction.
Swelling of the false vocal cords can narrow the rima respiratoria and affect airflow.
The anatomy lecture covered the rima respiratoria and its role in respiratory function.

Etymology

From Latin 'rima' (cleft or opening) and 'respiratoria' (relating to breathing).
