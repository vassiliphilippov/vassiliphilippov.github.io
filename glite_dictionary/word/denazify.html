to denazify

1. VERB

/ diˈnɑːsɪfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈnɑːzɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove Nazi ideology, sympathies, or influence from (a person, group, or institution), especially after World War II
remove Nazi ideology or influence

Examples

After the war, the Allied forces set out to denazify German institutions and society.
Many individuals found themselves excluded from public office until they could be denazified.
Efforts to denazify education included rewriting textbooks and dismissing teachers with Nazi affiliations.

Synset Examples

Germany was denazified after WW II
The highest judges were never denazified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decommunize - remove communist ideology or influence
  • Antonym: to nazify - imbue with Nazi ideology or make supportive of Nazis
  • Synonym: purge - remove people regarded as undesirable from an organization or place

Etymology

Formed from prefix de- (removal) + Nazi + -fy (make, cause to be).
