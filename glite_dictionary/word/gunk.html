gunk

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌŋk / (AmE), / ɡʌŋk / (BrE)
any thick, sticky, or viscous substance, especially something dirty or unpleasant
thick or sticky dirty substance

Examples

After fixing his bike, his hands were covered in gunk.
She used a special cleaner to remove the gunk from the kitchen sink.
Rainwater mixed with dirt and formed a layer of gunk on the driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goo - a sticky or viscous substance
  • Synonym: muck - dirt, rubbish, or waste matter
  • Synonym: slime - a thick, slippery liquid substance

Etymology

Origin unknown; attested in American English from early 20th century.
