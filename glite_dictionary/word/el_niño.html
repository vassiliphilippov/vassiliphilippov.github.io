El Niño

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ˈniːnjoʊ / (AmE), / ɛl ˈniːnjəʊ / (BrE)
a periodic warm ocean current that flows along the equator, typically originating near the date line and extending southward along the coast of Ecuador and northern Peru, often around Christmas, causing significant changes in weather patterns
periodic warm ocean current affecting global weather

Examples

Scientists warn that El Niño could bring heavy rainfall and flooding to the region this year.
During El Niño events, some areas experience severe drought while others get unusually intense storms.
Farmers are often concerned about how El Niño will impact their crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: La Niña - the counterpart of El Niño with cooler ocean temperatures and opposite climate effects

Etymology

From Spanish 'El Niño' meaning 'the (Christ) Child', referring to its frequent appearance around Christmas.
