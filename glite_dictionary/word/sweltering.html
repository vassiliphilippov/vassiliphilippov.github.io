sweltering

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɛl.tɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɛl.tər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Uncomfortably hot and humid; oppressively warm, often causing sweating and discomfort.
extremely hot and humid

Examples

After a few minutes in the sweltering kitchen, she was dripping with sweat.
We tried to find some shade on that sweltering summer afternoon.
The stadium was packed with fans despite the sweltering heat.

Synset Examples

a sweltering room
sweltering athletes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorching - extremely hot (literal or figurative sense)
  • Antonym: chilly - uncomfortably or unpleasantly cold
  • Degree Lower: hot - having a high temperature

Etymology

Present participle of 'swelter,' from Middle English sweltre (to faint from heat), related to Old English 'sweltan' (to die, perish).
