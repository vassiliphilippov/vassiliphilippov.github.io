premonitory

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈmɑːnɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / prɪˈmɒnɪˌtəri / (BrE)
serving as a warning or sign of something, especially something unpleasant or dangerous that may happen in the future
serving as a warning sign

Examples

The patient reported premonitory symptoms before the onset of the migraine.
Dark clouds and sudden silence were considered premonitory signs of an approaching storm.
She ignored the premonitory feeling that something bad was about to happen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - something that indicates a possible danger or problem
  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen

Etymology

From Latin præmonitorius, from præmonere (to warn in advance).
