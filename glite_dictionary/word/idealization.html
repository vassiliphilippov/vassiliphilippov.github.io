idealization

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of representing or regarding something as perfect or better than it really is
portrayal as perfect or ideal

Examples

The idealization of childhood in literature often overlooks the difficulties that children actually face.
Many artists engage in the idealization of beauty in their work.
She criticized the idealization of historical figures, arguing it ignores their flaws.

Synset Examples

the idealization of rural life was very misleading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticization - the act of making something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
  • Antonym: realism - the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From late Latin *idealizatio*, based on modern Latin *idealizare* ('to make ideal') and English '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
An idea or concept imagined or portrayed as perfect or better than reality; something that exists only as an idea.
concept of something perfect or ideal

Examples

Her description of the city was an idealization, ignoring its many practical problems.
The hero in the story is simply an idealization of what people wish to be.
Philosophers often discuss the idealization of justice rather than its imperfect real-world forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideal - a person or thing regarded as perfect

Etymology

From the verb 'idealize,' from French idéaliser, from 'ideal.'

3. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) A defense mechanism in which a person splits an ambivalent object into two representations — one wholly good and one wholly bad.
psychological defense mechanism of splitting into good/bad
medical

Examples

In therapy, his pattern of idealization and devaluation became a focus of discussion.
The patient’s idealization of her parents made it difficult for her to acknowledge their faults.
Frequent idealization and subsequent disappointment often characterize borderline personality disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splitting - A psychological defense mechanism involving division of an object or person into all good or all bad, often seen in certain personality disorders.

Etymology

From 'idealize' + '-ation'; 'idealize' from French 'idéaliser', from 'idéal'.
