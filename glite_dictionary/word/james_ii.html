James II

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪmz səˈkənd / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪmz ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland from 1685 to 1688, the last Stuart to hold the English throne, deposed in the Glorious Revolution.
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1685–1688)

Examples

James II was deposed in the Glorious Revolution of 1688.
The reign of James II marked a turbulent period in British history.
Many nobles turned against James II because of his policies and religion.
