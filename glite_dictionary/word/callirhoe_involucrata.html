Callirhoe involucrata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈroʊ.i ɪnˌvɑːl.jəˈreɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈrəʊ.i ɪnˌvɒl.jʊˈreɪ.tə / (BrE)
A hairy perennial plant native to central United States, noted for its round deeply lobed leaves and loose clusters of large crimson-purple or cherry-red flowers.
a plant native to central US with red-purple flowers

Examples

The Callirhoe involucrata bloomed beautifully in the botanic garden this spring.
Gardeners admire Callirhoe involucrata for its striking cherry-red flowers and ground-covering habit.
Many native plant enthusiasts choose Callirhoe involucrata to attract pollinators to their landscapes.

Etymology

From Greek mythology, Callirhoe was a nymph; 'involucrata' refers to the plant's involucrate (whorled) bracts.
