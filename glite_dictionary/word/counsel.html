counsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
a lawyer who represents and advises someone in a court of law
lawyer in court
legal

Examples

The defense counsel presented a compelling argument to the jury.
She hired experienced counsel to represent her during the trial.
The judge addressed both counsel before announcing the verdict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters
  • Synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in higher courts
  • Synonym: solicitor - a lawyer who gives legal advice and prepares legal documents

Etymology

From Old French 'counseil', from Latin 'consilium' (advice, council).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
advice or guidance given to someone, especially regarding a decision or future course of action.
advice or guidance

Examples

She always seeks her father's counsel in difficult times.
Without wise counsel, he might have made a poor decision.
The committee offered their counsel on the proposed changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advice - recommendation about what someone should do

Etymology

From Latin consilium (deliberation, advice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06663446-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
to give advice to someone, especially on personal or legal matters
give advice to someone

Examples

The principal often counsels students facing disciplinary issues.
She counseled him to be patient during difficult times.
Professionals are trained to counsel people dealing with grief.

Synset Examples

The teacher counsels troubled students
The lawyer counselled me when I was accused of tax fraud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advise - to give advice or a recommendation to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'conseiller', from Latin 'consiliare' (to advise).

Synset POS: v
