chambray

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæm.breɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃɒm.breɪ / (BrE)
A lightweight fabric, typically cotton, woven with a colored warp and a white weft, giving it a slightly mottled appearance.
lightweight cotton fabric with colored warp and white weft

Examples

She wore a crisp chambray shirt with rolled-up sleeves.
The designer used chambray fabric for a casual and comfortable summer dress.
Light blue chambray has become popular for both shirts and dresses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denim - a sturdy cotton twill fabric, typically blue, used for jeans

Etymology

From French 'chambray', an alteration of 'Cambrai', the name of a town in northern France known for fabric production.
