haemorrhagic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiː.məˈrædʒ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving hemorrhage (the escape of blood from a ruptured blood vessel)
relating to bleeding or hemorrhage
medical british

Examples

The patient developed a haemorrhagic stroke following the surgery.
Doctors monitored her closely for any signs of haemorrhagic complications.
A haemorrhagic fever can be life-threatening without prompt treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemorrhagic - relating to hemorrhage; US spelling

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and '-rrhagic' from 'rhein' (to flow).
