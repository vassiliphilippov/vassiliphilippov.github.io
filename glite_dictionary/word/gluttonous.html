gluttonous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlʌt̬.ən.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌt.ən.əs / (BrE)
excessively fond of eating or drinking; having an insatiable appetite, especially for food or drink
excessively fond of eating or drinking

Examples

The gluttonous child ate three desserts before anyone else finished their main course.
His gluttonous habits led to frequent visits to the doctor.
After a gluttonous holiday feast, she felt uncomfortably full.

Synset Examples

over-fed women and their gluttonous husbands
a gluttonous debauch
a gluttonous appetite for food and praise and pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greedy - having an intense or selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food
  • Antonym: abstemious - not allowing oneself much food or drink; self-disciplined in eating or drinking

Etymology

From Latin gluttōnōsus, from gluttō (glutton).
