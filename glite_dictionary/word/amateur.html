amateur

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.ə.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈæm.ə.tə / (BrE)
An athlete who does not receive payment for participation in a sport; one who competes without professional status.
unpaid athlete

Examples

She competed as an amateur before turning professional two years later.
Many amateur athletes train just as hard as professionals, despite not earning a salary.
The Olympics were once reserved for amateur competitors only.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: professional - an athlete who is paid for participating in sports

Etymology

From French 'amateur' (lover, enthusiast), from Latin 'amator' (lover), from 'amare' (to love).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈæmətə, ˈamətjʊə / (BrE)
a person who engages in a pursuit, especially a sport or a study, for pleasure rather than as a profession.
non-professional participant

Examples

As an amateur photographer, she enjoys taking pictures in her free time.
The chess tournament was open to both amateur and professional players.
He cooks like an amateur but with great enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: professional - someone who does an activity as a paid occupation

Etymology

From French 'amateur' (lover of), from Latin 'amator' (lover), from 'amare' (to love).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈæmətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈæmətə, ˈæmətjʊə / (BrE)
not having professional skill or expertise; done by someone who is not a professional
lacking professional skill or expertise

Examples

The repairs to the car looked very amateur and unsafe.
His amateur attempt at fixing the sink only made things worse.
The website had an amateur design that lacked polish.

Synset Examples

an unskilled painting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: professional - having or showing professional skill or expertise

  • Synonym: unskilled - not having special skill or training

Etymology

From French amateur (lover of), from Latin amator (lover) from amare (to love).
