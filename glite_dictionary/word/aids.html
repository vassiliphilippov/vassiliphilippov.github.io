AIDS

1. NOUN

/ eɪdz / (AmE), / eɪdz / (BrE)
a serious disease caused by the HIV virus that weakens the immune system, making the body vulnerable to infections and certain cancers
HIV-related immune system disease
medical

Examples

The spread of AIDS in the 1980s raised major public health concerns around the world.
She works for an organization that supports people living with AIDS.
Advances in medication have improved the life expectancy of individuals diagnosed with AIDS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: HIV - virus that causes AIDS

Etymology

Acronym from 'Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome'.
