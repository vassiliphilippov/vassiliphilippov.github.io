Muskhogean

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskəˈɡiːən / (AmE), / ˌmʌskəˈɡiːən / (BrE)
a family of Native American languages spoken in the southeastern United States, including Creek, Choctaw, and Chickasaw
family of Native American languages (southeastern US)

Examples

Linguists have extensively studied Muskhogean languages and their historical relationships.
The Muskhogean language family includes widely known tongues like Creek and Choctaw.
Documentation of Muskhogean languages is vital for preserving indigenous cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean - alternative spelling of Muskhogean, referring to the same language family

Etymology

From Creek Muscogee, the name of one of the languages in the family.

2. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkoʊɡiən / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊɡɪən / (BrE)
a member of any of the Indigenous peoples formerly living in the southeastern United States who spoke Muskhogean languages
member of southeastern US Indigenous peoples

Examples

Many anthropologists have studied the burial practices of the Muskhogean peoples.
The Muskhogean were known for their agricultural skills and complex societies.
Artifacts attributed to ancient Muskhogean tribes have been discovered in Alabama and Georgia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - member of a Muskhogean people of the southeastern US
  • Synonym: Muscogee - another name for Creek; Muskhogean people and language

Etymology

From Creek *maskóki*, the self-designation of one of the Muskhogean peoples.
