Oscan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑskən / (AmE), / ˈɒskən / (BrE)
an extinct Italic language once spoken in ancient southern Italy by tribes such as the Samnites, Aurunci, and Sidicini
extinct Italic language of ancient Italy

Examples

The Oscan language was once widely spoken throughout southern Italy.
Many ancient inscriptions were found written in Oscan by archaeologists.
Few people today can read or understand Oscan scripts carved on those ancient stones.

Etymology

From Latin Oscus, referring to the Oscan people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːskən / (AmE), / ˈɒskən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people of southern Italy who spoke the Oscan language, especially in the region of Campania
ancient people of southern Italy

Examples

Archaeologists discovered pottery believed to have belonged to an Oscan family in Campania.
The Oscan resisted Roman rule fiercely during the early Republic.
Many artifacts uncovered in the region were inscribed with the Oscan language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Samnite - a member of an ancient Italic people closely related to the Oscans
  • Antonym: Etruscan - a member of another ancient people of Italy, but distinct from the Oscans

Etymology

From Latin Oscus, Oscan, referring to the ancient people of Italy and their language.
