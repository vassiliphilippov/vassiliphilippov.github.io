gospel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːspəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl / (BrE)
a principle or idea that is accepted as absolutely true
unquestionable truth or principle

Examples

He treated every word his mentor said as gospel and never questioned it.
Don't take her advice as gospel; it's just one opinion among many.
For years, the textbook's claims were considered gospel in the academic community.

Synset Examples

his word was gospel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • Antonym: myth - a widely held but false belief or idea

Etymology

From Old English *gōdspel* (good news), originally referring to the Christian message, later extended metaphorically.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑspəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl / (BrE)
the teachings regarded as authoritatively set forth and widely accepted within a particular religious group; especially, the body of doctrine forming the core beliefs of a religion.
the accepted teachings or doctrine of a religion

Examples

Members of the faith follow the gospel as their guiding principles in daily life.
He spent years studying the gospel promoted by the early church fathers.
Every major religion has its own version of gospel that unites its followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group
  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true

Etymology

Old English 'gōdspel' (good news), from 'gōd' (good) + 'spel' (news, story), referring originally to the Christian Gospels.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑspəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl / (BrE)
a type of folk music originating with Black slaves in the United States, typically performed a cappella and characterized by call and response vocals, which has strongly influenced genres such as soul and popular music.
African American folk music genre

Examples

The church choir performed a stirring gospel that moved everyone in the congregation.
Many modern artists cite gospel as a major influence on their music.
She grew up singing gospel in her hometown choir before becoming a soul singer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - a religious song of a kind associated with Black Christians of the southern U.S.
  • Degree Higher: soul - a genre of popular music influenced by gospel and rhythm and blues

Etymology

The musical sense derives from 'gospel' meaning 'good news' or 'Christian teachings', as songs often expressed religious beliefs.
