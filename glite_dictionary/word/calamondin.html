calamondin

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈmɑndɪn / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈmɒndɪn / (BrE)
a small, round citrus fruit with sour, orange-like flesh, used especially in Southeast Asian cuisine
small sour citrus fruit

Examples

He squeezed a fresh calamondin over his grilled fish to add a burst of tangy flavor.
The orchard was full of ripe calamondins, their bright orange skins shining in the sun.
Many Filipino desserts feature the juice of the calamondin as a key ingredient.

Synset Examples

Calamondin is usually used in its not-so-ripe stage as sour seasoning for many Southeast Asian food such as the shomai.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamansi - another name for calamondin, used especially in the Philippines
  • Synonym: kumquat - a small, orange-like citrus fruit

Etymology

From Tagalog 'kalamunding', possibly blended with Spanish diminutive '-in' forms; first recorded in English in the early 20th century.
