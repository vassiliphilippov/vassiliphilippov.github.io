chou

1. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃu / (BrE)
A cabbage, especially as referred to in French cuisine.
a type of cabbage (French term)

Examples

The chef used fresh chou in her traditional French soup.
In the market, she bought a large green chou for tonight's dinner.
Classic dishes like potée require several varieties of chou.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a vegetable with large green leaves and a dense head

Etymology

From French 'chou' meaning cabbage.

2. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃu / (BrE)
a small round pastry filled with cream or custard, made from choux pastry
cream-filled pastry

Examples

She ordered a chocolate chou for dessert at the French bakery.
The chou was light and airy, with a smooth vanilla filling.
At the patisserie, rows of choux tempted passersby with their powdered sugar tops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: éclair - a long pastry made from choux dough, filled with cream and topped with icing

Etymology

From French 'chou' (cabbage), used as a term for small pastries resembling the shape of a cabbage.
