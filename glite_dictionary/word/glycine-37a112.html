glycine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsiːn / (BrE)
a simple amino acid found in many proteins, important in the metabolism of living organisms
an amino acid in proteins
medical

Examples

Glycine plays a crucial role in the synthesis of proteins and other important compounds in the body.
Scientists found that the sweet taste of glycine distinguishes it from many other amino acids.
The structure of glycine makes it the simplest among all amino acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alanine - a common amino acid found in proteins

Etymology

From Greek 'glykys' (sweet), referring to its sweet taste.
