apposable

1. ADJ

/ əˈpoʊzəbl̩ / (AmE), / əˈpəʊzəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a body part or object) capable of being placed or positioned opposite to something else, especially so as to touch or interact with it.
capable of being placed opposite

Examples

Unlike most mammals, humans have an apposable thumb that enables precise gripping.
In some primates, the big toe is apposable and aids in climbing.
Researchers discussed whether the new fossil's digit was truly apposable or merely flexible.

Synset Examples

the thumb is opposable to the forefinger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposable - (of a digit) able to be placed opposite the other fingers

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (to) + 'ponere' (to place).
