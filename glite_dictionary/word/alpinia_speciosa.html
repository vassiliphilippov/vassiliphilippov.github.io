Alpinia speciosa

1. PROPN

/ ælˈpɪniə spəˈsioʊsə / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə spɛˈsiːəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of ginger plant cultivated for its shiny oblong leaves and arching clusters of white flowers with pink and yellow markings, commonly known as shell ginger.
species of ginger plant; shell ginger

Examples

The garden's Alpinia speciosa bloomed beautifully this spring, filling the air with a delicate fragrance.
Landscapers often choose Alpinia speciosa for its attractive foliage and decorative flowers.
After moving to a warmer climate, she planted Alpinia speciosa along her patio for a tropical look.

Etymology

From the genus name Alpinia (after Italian botanist Prospero Alpini) and Latin speciosa meaning 'showy, beautiful'.
