anomy

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəmi / (AmE), / ˈanəmi / (BrE)
A state of society characterized by the absence or breakdown of social and moral standards, often resulting in social instability or alienation.
breakdown of social norms; normlessness

Examples

Periods of political upheaval often lead to anomy within a society.
Sociologists study the effects of anomy when traditional values break down.
After the war, the nation faced years of anomy and uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomie - lack of the usual social or ethical standards in an individual or group

Etymology

From Greek 'anomia' (lawlessness), via French 'anomie'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌmi / (AmE), / ˈænəmi / (BrE)
A personal state of isolation and anxiety resulting from a lack of social control or regulation, often leading to a breakdown of social norms.
state of isolation from lack of social regulation

Examples

After the collapse of the old regime, many citizens experienced a profound sense of anomy.
Periods of rapid social change can foster feelings of anomy among individuals.
Sociologists study how anomy affects community relationships and mental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomie - Social instability caused by the breakdown of standards and values

Etymology

From French 'anomie', from Greek 'anomia' (lawlessness), from 'a-' (without) + 'nomos' (law).
