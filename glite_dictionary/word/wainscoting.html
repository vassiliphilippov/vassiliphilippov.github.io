wainscoting

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪnskətɪŋ, ˈweɪnskoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪnskətɪŋ, ˈweɪnskəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
Wooden panels or boards applied to the lower part of interior walls as a decorative or protective feature.
wooden panels for walls

Examples

The dining room's wainscoting added charm to the otherwise plain walls.
He restored the original wainscoting during the house renovation to preserve its historic feel.
White wainscoting contrasted elegantly with the blue paint above it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - decorative wooden panels fixed to walls or ceilings

Etymology

From Middle Low German 'wagenschot' (wall-board), via earlier forms in Dutch and German.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪnˌskoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪnskətɪŋ / (BrE)
wooden paneling that lines the lower part of the walls of a room
wooden paneling on lower walls

Examples

The dining room features elegant wainscoting that complements its historic charm.
We decided to add wainscoting to the hallway to make it feel more sophisticated.
Cleaning the wainscoting regularly helps maintain its polished appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - decorative wooden panels on interior walls

Etymology

From 'wainscot' (wooden panel), from Middle Low German 'wagenschot', meaning 'wall-board'.
