forefront

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌfrʌnt / (BrE)
the leading or most prominent position; the part at the very front or closest to the viewer
leading or frontmost position

Examples

She found herself at the forefront of the campaign for climate change action.
The company has always been at the forefront of technological innovation.
As the parade began, the band marched proudly in the forefront.

Synset Examples

he was in the forefront
he was at the head of the column

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanguard - the leading part of a movement or field; the foremost position
  • Antonym: rear - the back part of something, opposite of the forefront

Etymology

From fore- (before) and front, modeled after analogous formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌfrʌnt / (BrE)
the leading or most important position or place in a particular activity, field, or movement
leading or most important position

Examples

The company has always been at the forefront of technological innovation.
Her research placed her at the forefront of climate science worldwide.
Education reform is once again at the forefront of political debate.

Synset Examples

the Cotswolds were once at the forefront of woollen manufacturing in England
the idea of motion was always to the forefront of his mind and central to his philosophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanguard - the foremost part of an army or movement; a group leading the way in new developments
  • Antonym: rear - the back part or position, especially used in contrast to forefront

Etymology

From Middle English forefraunt, from fore- (before) + front.
