decapitated

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkæpɪˌteɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈkapɪteɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having had the head cut off, especially as a result of execution or violence.
having had the head cut off

Examples

The statue stood in the square, its decapitated form evoking a sense of loss.
Medieval executioners sometimes displayed the decapitated heads of traitors as a warning to others.
The decapitated flowers drooped sadly in the vase after the storm.

Synset Examples

the beheaded prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beheaded - having had the head cut off, typically as a form of execution

Etymology

From Latin 'decapitatus', past participle of 'decapitare' (to behead), from 'de-' (off) + 'caput' (head).
