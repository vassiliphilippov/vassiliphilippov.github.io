to shmoose

1. VERB

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
to talk informally or casually in a friendly way, often with the intention of gaining favor or networking
chat or converse in a friendly way
informal

Examples

He spent the evening trying to shmoose with the conference organizers.
After the meeting, she liked to shmoose with her colleagues in the hallway.
They were shmoosing over coffee when I walked into the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schmooze - to chat in a friendly and informal manner, often to gain favor

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmuesn* (to chat), related to German *schmusen* (to cuddle, whisper).
