Samia

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large silkworm moths native to Asia, known for their importance in silk production.
genus of silkworm moths

Examples

The Samia genus includes some of the world's largest moths.
Many communities in Asia rear Samia moths for silk production.
Scientists study Samia to learn more about silkworm evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saturniid - any moth of the family Saturniidae, to which Samia belongs
  • Synonym: silkworm moth - any moth species used in silk production

Etymology

From Latinized form of a Greek female name, used in taxonomy for genus names.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large silkworm moths, including species such as the ailanthus silkmoth, found mainly in Asia.
genus of silkworm moths

Examples

The caterpillars of Samia species are used in some regions for silk production.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of Samia to improve breeding programs.
The Samia genus includes several impressive and colorful moths.

Etymology

From New Latin, assigned in scientific nomenclature.
