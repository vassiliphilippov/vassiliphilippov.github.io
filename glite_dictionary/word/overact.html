to overact

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈækt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈækt / (BrE)
to exaggerate one's acting in a performance, often to an unnatural or excessive degree
exaggerate one's acting

Examples

The young actor tended to overact in emotional scenes, making his performance seem unnatural.
Critics agreed that she would have been more convincing if she hadn't overacted so much.
It's important not to overact in comedies, even though broad gestures are often expected.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underact - to act with less emotion or energy than required
  • Synonym: ham - to overact or perform with exaggerated style

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'act'.
