Calliandra

1. NOUN

/ kæliˈændrə / (AmE), / kæliˈændrə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical shrubs and small trees with pinnate leaves, native to parts of the Americas, India, and West Africa
a genus of tropical and subtropical shrubs and trees

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Calliandra attract hummingbirds and butterflies to the garden.
Several species within the Calliandra genus are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
Researchers are studying Calliandra for its potential benefits in sustainable agriculture.

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + anēr, andros (man), referring to the beautiful stamens of the flowers.
