azoimide

1. NOUN

/ ˌæzoʊˈɪmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌazəʊˈɪmaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, and poisonous liquid that is foul-smelling and explosive, used mainly in scientific research
a volatile and explosive chemical compound

Examples

Azoimide must be handled with extreme care due to its explosive properties.
The laboratory stored azoimide in tightly sealed containers in a ventilated area.
Researchers studied the reactivity of azoimide under controlled conditions.

Etymology

From 'azo-' (relating to nitrogen compounds) and '-imide' (a chemical suffix denoting derivatives of imides), reflecting its composition.
