Makaira marlina

1. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə mɑːrˈliːnə / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə mɑːˈliːnə / (BrE)
A large species of marlin found in the Pacific Ocean, known for its size, which can reach up to 1000 pounds.
large Pacific marlin species

Examples

The Makaira marlina is a prized catch among sport fishermen due to its impressive size.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of Makaira marlina to better understand ocean ecosystems.
Photographers captured stunning images of a Makaira marlina leaping from the water off the coast of Hawaii.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue marlin - a large, blue marlin species found in the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific oceans

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'makaira' meaning a kind of sword, and 'marlina,' referring to marlin.
