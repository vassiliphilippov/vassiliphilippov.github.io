Bombyx

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmbɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɒmbɪks / (BrE)
a genus of moths in the family Bombycidae, including the Chinese silkworm moth
genus of moths, including silkworm

Examples

The Bombyx genus is best known for the domesticated silkworm, Bombyx mori.
Scientists study the life cycle of Bombyx to improve silk production.
Many silk factories rely on the eggs of Bombyx for their raw materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkworm - larva of Bombyx mori, source of silk

Etymology

From Latin *bombyx* (silkworm), from Greek *bombux* (silkworm, silk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmbɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɒmbɪks / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Bombycidae, including the Chinese silkworm moth, which is used in silk production.
genus of silkworm moths

Examples

The Bombyx genus is well known for producing the silk used in textiles worldwide.
Scientists are studying Bombyx moths to improve silk quality and production.
The most famous member of the Bombyx genus is the domestic silkworm moth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silkworm - the caterpillar of the silkworm moth, used in silk production

Etymology

From Latin Bombyx, from Ancient Greek bómbux (silkworm, silk).
