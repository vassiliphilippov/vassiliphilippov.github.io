toponomy

1. NOUN

/ təˈpɒnəmi / (AmE), / təˈpɒnəmi / (BrE)
the naming of regions in anatomy; the terminology used to describe the areas of a body or organ
regional anatomical naming
medical

Examples

The toponomy of the human liver helps surgeons identify specific regions for procedures.
Medical students must be familiar with the toponomy of the brain to understand neurological disorders.
Recent advances in toponomy have standardized the naming of anatomical segments across different disciplines.

Etymology

From Greek topos (place) + -nomy (arrangement, law), by analogy with toponymy (naming of places).
