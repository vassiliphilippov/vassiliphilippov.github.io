snore

1. NOUN

/ snɔr / (AmE), / snɔː / (BrE)
a loud, harsh sound made while breathing during sleep
the sound made when snoring

Examples

The loud snore from the next room kept me awake all night.
She let out a tiny snore as she drifted into a deep sleep.
His snore echoed through the quiet house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snoring - the act of making a snoring noise while asleep

Etymology

From Old English 'snora', related to 'snoren' (to snore) in Middle Dutch.

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ snɔr / (AmE), / snɔː / (BrE)
to breathe noisily through the nose or mouth while asleep
breathe noisily while sleeping

Examples

Most people snore at least occasionally when they're very tired.
I woke up when I heard my brother snoring in the next room.
She snored so loudly that it kept her husband awake all night.

Synset Examples

she complained that her husband snores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - to rest with eyes closed and mind and body inactive

Etymology

From Middle English snoren, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00017024-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ snɔr / (AmE), / snɔː / (BrE)
a rattling or grunting noise produced when someone breathes while asleep, usually caused by vibration of the soft palate
the noise made when snoring

Examples

The loud snore from the next room kept me awake all night.
He was embarrassed when his snore startled everyone during the quiet meeting.
A gentle snore could be heard as the child napped on the sofa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snoring - the act or process of making a snore or snoring sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07409078-n
Synset POS: n
