to anele

1. VERB

/ əˈniːl / (AmE), / əˈniːl / (BrE)
to anoint with oil or ointment, especially as part of a religious ceremony
to anoint with oil, esp. in religion
archaic

Examples

The priest proceeded to anele the foreheads of the congregation during the ceremony.
According to tradition, one must anele the sick as a form of blessing.
In the ancient ritual, they would anele the king before his coronation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anoint - to smear or rub with oil, often as a religious rite

Etymology

From Old French 'aneler', from Late Latin 'inungere' (to anoint), based on Latin 'ungere' (to smear).
