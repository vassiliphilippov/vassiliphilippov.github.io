dereliction

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛrəˈlɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrəˈlɪkʃən / (BrE)
Deliberate or conscious failure to fulfill one's duties or obligations; willful negligence.
willful negligence; neglect of duty

Examples

The captain was charged with dereliction of duty after abandoning his post.
His repeated dereliction led to a decline in the team's performance.
The committee cited dereliction as the cause of the safety violations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort

Etymology

From Latin derelictio (abandonment), from derelinquere (to abandon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛrəˈlɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrɪˈlɪkʃən / (BrE)
a neglectful or careless attitude; a tendency to disregard one's duties or responsibilities
neglect of duty or responsibility

Examples

His repeated dereliction of duty eventually led to his dismissal from the company.
The officer was accused of dereliction after ignoring the safety regulations.
The team's failure was mostly due to dereliction on the part of its leaders.

Synset Examples

he inherited his delinquency from his father
his derelictions were not really intended as crimes
his adolescent protest consisted of willful neglect of all his responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - failure to care for something or someone properly

Etymology

From Latin derelictio (abandonment), from derelinquere (to abandon).
