Phragmites communis

1. NOUN

/ fræɡˈmaɪtiːz kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / fræɡˈmaɪtiːz kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
A tall perennial reed with broad leaves and large, feathery flower clusters, found widely in moist areas of North America and used for making mats, screens, and arrow shafts.
tall reed plant with feathery plumes

Examples

The wetlands were dominated by dense stands of Phragmites communis along the shoreline.
Conservationists are studying the effects of Phragmites communis on local ecosystems.
Traditional arrow shafts were often made from the strong stems of Phragmites communis.

Etymology

From Greek 'phragmites' (growing in hedges) and Latin 'communis' (common).
