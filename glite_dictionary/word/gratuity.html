gratuity

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈtuːəti / (AmE), / ɡrəˈtjuːəti / (BrE)
a small amount of money given to someone for providing a service, in addition to the basic payment
a tip for services rendered

Examples

The waiter was pleased with the generous gratuity left by the guests.
When paying the bill, she added a 15% gratuity for excellent service.
Some restaurants include a gratuity in the total bill for large parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a small sum of money given to someone for their service

Etymology

From Latin gratuitus (given freely), via French gratuité.

2. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈtuːəti / (AmE), / ɡrəˈtjuːəti / (BrE)
a sum of money given as a reward or token of appreciation for services rendered, without any legal obligation
gift of money for services

Examples

She received a gratuity from the company for her outstanding service.
The waiter smiled when the customers left a generous gratuity on the table.
Upon retirement, he was given a substantial gratuity as a token of appreciation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a small sum of money given to someone for a service, often in hospitality

Etymology

From Latin 'gratuitus' (given freely, spontaneous) via French 'gratuité'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13289043-n
Synset POS: n
