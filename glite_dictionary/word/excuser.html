excuser

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskjuːzər / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzə / (BrE)
a person who pardons, forgives, or excuses a fault or offense
person who pardons or forgives

Examples

She is known as an excuser rather than someone who holds grudges.
The meeting's excuser overlooked minor mistakes to keep morale high.
He gained a reputation as an excuser in the community, always showing leniency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgiver - a person who forgives someone

Etymology

From the verb 'excuse' + agent suffix '-er'.
