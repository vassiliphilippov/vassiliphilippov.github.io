microseism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌsaɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌsaɪzəm / (BrE)
A very slight or low-intensity vibration of the ground, often too small to be felt by humans, usually caused by natural phenomena such as ocean waves rather than earthquakes.
very slight ground vibration
medical

Examples

The seismologist detected a microseism triggered by distant ocean waves.
Sensitive instruments recorded a series of microseisms throughout the night.
Unlike an earthquake, a microseism is generally not felt by people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthquake - a sudden violent shaking of the ground

Etymology

From micro- (small) + Greek seismos (earthquake).
