Platypoecilus

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈpiːsɪləs / (AmE), / ˌplatɪˈpiːsɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fishes in the family Poeciliidae, commonly known as platys.
a genus of freshwater fish (platys)

Examples

The aquarium featured several vibrant Platypoecilus swimming among the plants.
Researchers are studying the mating behaviors of Platypoecilus in controlled environments.
Platypoecilus are popular among hobbyists for their colorful appearance and peaceful nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platy - a small tropical freshwater fish, popular in aquariums

Etymology

From Greek platys (broad) and poikilos (varied, spotted). Used in scientific Latin.
