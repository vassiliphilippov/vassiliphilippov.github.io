Phyllocladus asplenifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈkleɪdəs æˌsplɛnɪˈfoʊli.əs / (AmE), / ˌfɪləkˈlædəs æˌsplɛnɪˈfəʊli.əs / (BrE)
a medium-tall species of celery pine, native to Tasmania, with flattened, leaflike branchlets
Tasmanian celery pine species (tree)

Examples

The Phyllocladus asplenifolius is an evergreen tree found only in Tasmania's highland forests.
Botanists studied the unique leaflike branchlets of Phyllocladus asplenifolius to understand its adaptation to the climate.
Wood from Phyllocladus asplenifolius is valued for its durability and fine grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery pine - common name for several species in the genus Phyllocladus

Etymology

From Greek *phyllon* (leaf) + *klados* (branch) and Neo-Latin, with *asplenifolius* meaning 'with leaves like Asplenium' (a fern genus).
