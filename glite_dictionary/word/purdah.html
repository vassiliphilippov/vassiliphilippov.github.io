purdah

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrdə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdə / (BrE)
a curtain or screen used in some South Asian cultures, especially in India, to separate women from men or from strangers, or the practice of secluding women from public observation
curtain or practice secluding women (South Asia)

Examples

The royal family maintained purdah in their household for generations.
Behind the purdah, the women could observe the ceremonies without being seen.
The practice of purdah is less common in urban areas now.

Etymology

From Hindi and Urdu 'parda', from Persian 'parda' meaning 'curtain'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrdə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdə / (BrE)
The traditional practice among some Hindu and Muslim communities of keeping women secluded from the sight of men or strangers, often by means of physical segregation and special clothing.
system of secluding women (Hindu/Muslim)

Examples

Many women in the village still observe purdah, rarely appearing in public without a veil.
The novel explores the theme of purdah and its effect on women's lives within the family.
After her marriage, she entered purdah and participated less in community events.

Etymology

From Hindi *parda*, from Persian *parda* (curtain, veil).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.dɑ / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.dɑː / (BrE)
the practice of keeping women separate from men or the outside world, especially by means of physical seclusion or the wearing of concealing clothing, or more generally a state of social or institutional isolation
seclusion or social isolation (esp. of women)

Examples

She spent most of her adult life in purdah, rarely leaving her home.
After the scandal, the leader entered a kind of political purdah, avoiding all public appearances.
The tradition of purdah is still observed in some regions to maintain privacy and modesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from people

Etymology

From Hindi and Urdu *parda* (curtain, veil), from Persian *pardeh* (curtain).
