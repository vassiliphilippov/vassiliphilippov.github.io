rustication

1. NOUN

/ ˌrʌstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of sending someone away from a particular place, especially as a punishment, to live in the countryside; banishment to rural areas
banishment to the countryside

Examples

The rebellious student faced rustication for repeatedly violating college rules.
His rustication was seen as both a punishment and an opportunity for reflection.
After the scandal, the official went into rustication in his family's rural estate.

Etymology

From Latin 'rusticatio' (living in the country), from 'rusticus' (of the country).

4. NOUN

/ ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of decorating masonry or brickwork by giving the surface a rough, textured, or projecting appearance, often with deep grooves or recessed joints
rough-textured masonry finish

Examples

The rustication of the stone façade gave the building a rugged, classical appearance.
Many Renaissance palaces feature rustication on their ground floors to suggest strength and solidity.
The architect chose rustication for the entrance to highlight contrast with the smoother upper levels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ashlar - smooth-surfaced, finely dressed masonry

Etymology

From Latin 'rusticatio' (living in the country), via rustic ('rural, coarse') + -ation; used in architecture from the 17th century.

5. NOUN

/ ˌrʌstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
Suspension of a student from a university or college for a specific period as a disciplinary measure.
temporary suspension from university

Examples

The university threatened rustication if the protests continued.
After his involvement in the prank, he faced a term of rustication.
The college handbook details the process for appealing a rustication decision.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: expulsion - permanent dismissal from a school or university

Etymology

From Latin 'rusticari' (to live in the country), originally meaning to live in the country, later used in academic context for banishment from university residence.
