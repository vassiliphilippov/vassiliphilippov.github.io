Gutierrezia sarothrae

1. NOUN

/ ɡuːˌtɪəriˈɛziə səˈrɒθriː / (AmE), / ɡuːˌtɪəriˈɛziə səˈrɒθriː / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub native to the southwestern United States, characterized by narrow, linear leaves on many slender branches and numerous tiny yellow flower heads.
a sticky subshrub of the southwestern US

Examples

Gutierrezia sarothrae is a common sight in arid rangelands of the American Southwest.
Ranchers often try to control Gutierrezia sarothrae because it can be toxic to livestock in large quantities.
The tiny yellow flowers of Gutierrezia sarothrae bloom profusely in late summer.

Etymology

Genus Gutierrezia named after Spanish botanist Pedro Gutierrez; species epithet from Greek sarothra (broom).
