to inflame

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfleɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm / (BrE)
to cause to start burning; to set on fire
set on fire; cause to burn

Examples

The protesters threatened to inflame the government building if their demands were not met.
The match could easily inflame the dry grass, so be careful.
A single spark was enough to inflame the pile of old newspapers.

Synset Examples

The setting sun kindled the sky with oranges and reds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put out a fire or light

Etymology

From Latin inflammare (to set on fire), from in- (in) + flamma (flame).

WordNet ID: oewn-02767330-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfleɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm / (BrE)
(of an injury, substance, or action) to cause (a body part or tissue) to become red, swollen, and painful due to inflammation.
cause inflammation in a body part
medical

Examples

Repetitive motion can inflame the tendons in your wrist.
Allergic reactions may inflame the lining of the nose, causing discomfort.
Poor posture over time can inflame the muscles in your back.

Synset Examples

The repetitive motion inflamed her joint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - cause slight inflammation or discomfort in body tissue

Etymology

From Latin inflammare (to set on fire), from in- (in, upon) + flamma (flame).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈfleɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm / (BrE)
to make feelings or emotions stronger, especially negative ones such as anger or hatred
make emotions or passions more intense

Examples

The politician's speech was designed to inflame public opinion against the new law.
Insensitive remarks can inflame tensions between different communities.
Her angry words only served to inflame the situation further.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agitate - to stir up feeling or concern; to upset
  • Antonym: calm - to make less angry or excited; to soothe

Etymology

From Latin 'inflammare', from 'in-' (into) + 'flammare' (to flame).

/ ɪnˈfleɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm / (BrE)

to become red, swollen, and sore

Examples

After catching a cold, his throat would often inflame and feel sore.
