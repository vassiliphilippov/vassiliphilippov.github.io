plural

1. ADJ

/ ˈplʊr.əl / (AmE), / ˈplʊə.rəl / (BrE)
(of a word or set) consisting of more than one person or thing; relating to or expressing more than one.
more than one; multiple

Examples

The word 'dogs' is a plural noun because it refers to more than one dog.
A plural society includes many different cultural groups living together.
She used the plural form of the verb to match the subject.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: singular - relating to or denoting a single person or thing

Etymology

From Latin pluralis (of or relating to more than one).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʊrəl / (AmE), / ˈplʊərəl / (BrE)
The form of a word, especially a noun or pronoun, that refers to more than one person or thing.
grammatical term; more-than-one form

Examples

The plural of 'child' is 'children'.
In English, most nouns form the plural by adding '-s' or '-es'.
You should use the plural when talking about more than one object.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: singular - the grammatical form denoting one person or thing

Etymology

From Latin 'pluralis', from 'plus' (more).

3. ADJ

/ ˈplʊr.əl / (AmE), / ˈplʊə.rəl / (BrE)
(Grammar) Denoting or relating to a form of a word used to refer to more than one person or thing.
grammatical category for more than one

Examples

In English, most nouns form the plural by adding -s or -es.
The word 'children' is the plural of 'child'.
Use the plural form when talking about more than one object.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: singular - the form of a word referring to one person or thing

Etymology

From Latin pluralis (relating to more than one), from plus, plur- (more).
