fetoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːtoʊˈproʊtiːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːtəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
Any of several proteins usually found in the fetus, and sometimes present in adults with certain cancers, serving as antigens.
protein antigen found in fetuses or some cancers
medical

Examples

The level of fetoprotein in her blood was monitored during pregnancy.
A high fetoprotein reading can indicate certain types of cancer or liver disease.
Researchers are studying fetoprotein as a possible biomarker for tumor detection.

Etymology

From 'feto-', referring to fetus, and 'protein'.
