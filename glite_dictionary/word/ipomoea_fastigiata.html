Ipomoea fastigiata

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə fæˌstɪdʒ.iˈeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə fəˌstɪdʒ.iˈɑː.tə / (BrE)
A tropical American prostrate or climbing herbaceous perennial plant with a large starchy root; sometimes regarded as a source of the sweet potato.
tropical American perennial plant with starchy root

Examples

The large root of the Ipomoea fastigiata is often used as a food source in some regions.
Some botanists believe that Ipomoea fastigiata could be an ancestor of the modern sweet potato.
Farmers cultivate Ipomoea fastigiata for its enormous, starchy tubers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet potato - a tropical vine cultivated for its edible roots

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'ips' (worm) and 'homoios' (resembling), referring to the twining habit; species name from Latin 'fastigiatus' (pointed, peaked).
