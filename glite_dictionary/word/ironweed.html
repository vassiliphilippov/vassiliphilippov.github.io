ironweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwid / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall plant of the genus Vernonia, native mostly to North America, with clusters of purple or rose flowers that turn rusty-colored.
a tall North American plant with purplish flowers

Examples

Wild ironweed adds a splash of purple to the summer meadows.
She planted ironweed in her garden to attract butterflies.
The roadside was lined with blooming ironweed and goldenrod.
