carton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.tən / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.tən / (BrE)
the quantity of something that a single carton can hold; a container's standard amount
the amount held by a carton

Examples

She bought a carton of orange juice from the store.
The refrigerator was full of cartons of milk and eggs.
He drank an entire carton of chocolate milk after the game.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtən / (BrE)
a box made from cardboard or similar material, typically with flaps that open at the top, used for packaging and storing goods
a cardboard box for packaging

Examples

He packed the books into a large carton for the move.
We recycle every carton after unpacking the groceries.
A milk carton was left open on the kitchen counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - a container with flat sides and a lid

Etymology

From French 'carton' (cardboard; large piece of paper), from Italian 'cartone' (pasteboard), from 'carta' (paper).
