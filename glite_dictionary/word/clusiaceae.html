Clusiaceae

1. NOUN

/ kluːsiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kluːsiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines that produce oils, resins, and sometimes usable timber
family of tropical trees, shrubs, and vines

Examples

The Clusiaceae family includes plants such as the mangosteen and the autograph tree.
Several members of the Clusiaceae produce valuable resins used in varnishes and medicines.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Clusiaceae in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From the genus Clusia, named after Charles de l'Écluse (Clusius), a Flemish botanist.

2. NOUN

/ kluːsiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / kluːsɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines that produce oils, resins, and usable timber, widely distributed in tropical regions
family of tropical trees, shrubs, and vines

Examples

Many species in the Clusiaceae family are prized for their decorative flowers and foliage.
The Clusiaceae includes important timber trees as well as ornamental plants.
Botanists have studied the resins produced by members of the Clusiaceae for their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From genus name Clusia + standard botanical family suffix -aceae, from Latin.
