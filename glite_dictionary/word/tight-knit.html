tight-knit

1. ADJ

/ taɪt nɪt / (AmE), / taɪt nɪt / (BrE)
(of a group of people or an organization) closely connected and united, with strong relationships and little division.
closely connected or united group

Examples

The company prides itself on having a tight-knit staff that supports one another.
She grew up in a tight-knit community where everyone knew each other.
A tight-knit family can overcome many challenges together.

Synset Examples

a tight-knit organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close-knit - closely bound together or united group

Etymology

Formed from 'tight' (close) and 'knit' (fastened together) to describe close unity.
