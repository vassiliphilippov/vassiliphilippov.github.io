arteria facialis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə fəˈseɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə fəˈseɪlɪs / (BrE)
the artery that arises from the external carotid artery and supplies blood to the structures of the neck and face
facial artery from external carotid
medical

Examples

The arteria facialis is a major blood vessel supplying the face and lower jaw.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid injuring the arteria facialis.
Symptoms of trauma in the arteria facialis can include swelling or abnormal bleeding in the face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial artery - the artery in the face that supplies blood to facial structures

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) and 'facialis' (of the face).
