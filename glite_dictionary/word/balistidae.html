Balistidae

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstəˌdiː / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstəˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical marine fishes known as triggerfishes, distinguished by their deep, laterally compressed bodies and tough skin.
family of triggerfishes

Examples

The Balistidae are commonly found around coral reefs in tropical waters.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Balistidae family off the coast of Indonesia.
Members of the Balistidae have unique dorsal fin mechanisms for defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggerfish - Any fish of the family Balistidae, known for their tough skin and trigger-like dorsal spines

Etymology

From Greek *balistos* (easily thrown), referring to the fish's sudden movements, with the taxonomic suffix *-idae*.
