Vipera

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈpɪr.ə / (AmE), / vɪˈpɪə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes in the Viperidae family, including species such as the common viper found in Europe and parts of Asia.
genus of venomous snakes

Examples

The European adder, or Vipera berus, is widespread across northern Europe.
Many species in the Vipera genus are known for their potent venom.
Researchers studied the habitat of Vipera snakes to understand changes in their populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - any venomous snake of the Viperidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'vipera' meaning 'viper, adder.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪpərə / (AmE), / ˈvɪpərə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Viperidae, comprising true vipers found in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
genus of true vipers
medical

Examples

The genus Vipera includes several species found throughout Europe and Asia.
Researchers identified a new member of the Vipera genus during the field study.
Venom composition varies widely among different Vipera species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - a venomous snake of the family Viperidae

Etymology

From Latin 'vipera', meaning viper or snake.
