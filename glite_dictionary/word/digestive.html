digestive

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
relating to or having the power to cause or promote the process of digestion
relating to digestion

Examples

The doctor explained how digestive enzymes break down food in the stomach.
He was prescribed medicine to help with his digestive problems.
The digestive system includes the stomach, intestines, and other organs.

Synset Examples

digestive juices
a digestive enzyme
digestive ferment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indigestible - impossible or difficult to digest

Etymology

From Latin 'digestivus', from 'digestus' (to divide, dissolve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03071790-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / daɪˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
A substance that helps promote the process of digestion in the body.
substance aiding digestion
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a digestive to help relieve her stomach discomfort.
Enzymes can serve as natural digestives in treating certain digestive disorders.
Some herbal teas are believed to act as mild digestives after a heavy meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digestant - a substance aiding digestion, especially as a medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'digestivus', from 'digestus' (digested).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14858884-n
Synset POS: n
