Franciscan

1. ADJ

/ frænˈsɪskən / (AmE), / frænˈsɪskən / (BrE)
of or relating to Saint Francis of Assisi or the religious order founded by him.
relating to St Francis of Assisi or his order

Examples

The Franciscan tradition emphasizes humility and care for the poor.
She visited a Franciscan monastery during her trip to Italy.
The church's architecture reflects Franciscan simplicity and devotion.

Synset Examples

Franciscan monks

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Franciscanus', from 'Franciscus' (Francis).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03078086-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ frænˈsɪskən / (AmE), / frænˈsɪskən / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic religious order founded by St. Francis of Assisi, known for vows of poverty and wearing a distinctive habit.
member of the Franciscan religious order

Examples

The Franciscan led morning prayers in the small village chapel.
During the festival, the Franciscan wore a simple grey robe and sandals.
Many people sought advice from the wise old Franciscan who lived at the monastery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friar - a member of any of certain religious orders of men, especially in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Franciscanus', from Franciscus (Francis of Assisi).
