Lomatia

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈmeɪtiə / (AmE), / ləʊˈmeɪtɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of low-growing evergreen shrubs and trees native to Chile and Australia, some species of which are used for dyes.
genus of evergreen shrubs and trees

Examples

The Lomatia genus includes several species found in Australia and South America.
Botanists studied the dye-producing potential of certain Lomatia species.
The evergreen shrubs of Lomatia add variety to temperate gardens.

Etymology

From Greek *lōmatia* (fringe), due to the fringed appearance of some flowers.
