platen

1. NOUN

/ ˈplætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈplætən / (BrE)
the flat plate in a printing press or similar machine that presses paper or other material against a surface to create an impression
flat plate in a press or printer

Examples

The technician carefully adjusted the platen before starting the printing process.
An old letterpress uses a platen to press paper onto the inked type.
The rubber covering on the platen needed replacement after years of use.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: roller - cylindrical tool or part used for pressing, spreading, or forming materials

Etymology

From French 'platin', from Middle Dutch 'platen' (flat plate) related to 'plaat' (plate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈplætn̩ / (BrE)
the roller in a typewriter or similar machine against which paper rests and keys strike to produce an impression
roller in a typewriter for striking keys

Examples

She replaced the worn-out platen in her old typewriter to get clearer prints.
Carefully roll the paper around the platen before you start typing.
The platen must be cleaned regularly to prevent smudges on the page.

Etymology

From French 'platin', from Dutch 'plaat' (plate).

3. NOUN

/ ˈplætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈplæt.ən / (BrE)
a flat plate or surface in a machine, especially one that holds or supports an item being worked on, such as the work table of a machine tool
flat supporting plate in machinery

Examples

The engineer adjusted the platen to ensure the metal sheet was properly aligned.
The printing press uses a heavy iron platen to press paper against the type.
Before starting the task, they cleaned the platen of the machine thoroughly.

Etymology

From French *platine*, from *plat* meaning 'flat', ultimately from Latin *plattus* (flat).
