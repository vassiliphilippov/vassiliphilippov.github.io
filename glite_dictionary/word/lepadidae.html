Lepadidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpædəˌdiː / (AmE), / lɛˈpadɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine crustaceans commonly known as goose barnacles, characterized by long stalks and found attached to floating objects in the sea.
family of goose barnacles (marine crustaceans)

Examples

The Lepadidae family includes the goose barnacles often found on driftwood.
Some ships return to port with their hulls covered in Lepadidae.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Lepadidae on ocean debris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose barnacle - a common name for certain members of the Lepadidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Lepadidae, from Greek lepados (limpet).
