luncher

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈlʌntʃə / (BrE)
a person who is eating lunch, or habitually eats lunch in a particular place or way
someone eating lunch

Examples

The rooftop terrace was crowded with enthusiastic lunchers enjoying the midday sunshine.
Regular lunchers at the café benefit from a special discount card.
As the clock struck noon, dozens of lunchers began to fill the office cafeteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diner - a person eating dinner or, more generally, a person eating at a restaurant

Etymology

From 'lunch' + '-er', patterning after other agent nouns.
