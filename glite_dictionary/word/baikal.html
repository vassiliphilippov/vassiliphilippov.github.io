Baikal

1. PROPN

/ baɪˈkɑl / (AmE), / baɪˈkɑːl / (BrE)
the largest freshwater lake in Asia and Europe, and the deepest lake in the world, located in southern Siberia, Russia
largest and deepest lake in Asia/Europe (southern Siberia, Russia)

Examples

Lake Baikal contains about 20% of the world's unfrozen surface freshwater.
Scientists study Baikal to learn about unique freshwater ecosystems.
Tourists flock to Baikal in winter to see its famous ice formations.

Etymology

From Russian Байкал (Baykal), of uncertain origin, possibly from Mongolian.

2. PROPN

/ baɪˈkɑl / (AmE), / baɪˈkɑːl / (BrE)
a large lake in southern Siberia, Russia, renowned as the world's deepest and the largest by volume of freshwater
deepest and largest freshwater lake in Asia

Examples

Tourists travel from around the world to visit Baikal and experience its unique natural beauty.
Researchers study Baikal for its diverse and ancient ecosystem.
Winter transforms Baikal into a stunning landscape of ice and snow.

Etymology

From Russian Байка́л (Baykál), possibly from Turkic bai (rich) + kül (lake).
