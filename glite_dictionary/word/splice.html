splice

1. NOUN

/ splaɪs / (AmE), / splaɪs / (BrE)
a place where two pieces of something, such as rope, film, or tape, have been joined together
a junction where things have been joined

Examples

The technician noticed a weak splice in the cable and repaired it immediately.
An imperfect splice caused the film to break during the projection.
After examining the rope, he found the splice was holding up well under tension.

Synset Examples

the break was due to an imperfect splice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - a point at which two parts are joined

Etymology

From Dutch *splissen* meaning 'to splice ropes'.
