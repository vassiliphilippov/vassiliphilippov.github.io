paramagnet

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈmæɡ.nət / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈmaɡ.nət / (BrE)
a material or device that becomes magnetized temporarily in the presence of an external magnetic field, with magnetization proportional to the field strength
magnetizable material in external field

Examples

Oxygen is a classic example of a paramagnet due to its unpaired electrons.
A paramagnet only exhibits magnetization when an external magnetic field is present.
The scientist demonstrated how the paramagnet reacted to changing magnetic field strengths.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ferromagnet - material with permanent magnetization, much stronger magnetic effects

Etymology

From 'para-' (beside, abnormal) and 'magnet'.
