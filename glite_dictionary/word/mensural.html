mensural

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛnʒərəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛnsjʊrəl / (BrE)
(of music) having notes of fixed rhythmic value, especially as in medieval and Renaissance notation.
having notes of fixed rhythmic value (music)

Examples

The choir specializes in performing mensural music from the Renaissance period.
Understanding mensural notation is essential for studying early European music.
Scholars analyzed the mensural structure of the medieval motet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measured - having a regular, clearly defined rhythm

Etymology

From Medieval Latin mensuralis, from mensura (measure).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛnʒərəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛnsjʊrəl / (BrE)
relating to measure, especially to musical notation that indicates precise rhythm or duration of notes.
relating to measure or musical notation

Examples

The choir studied mensural notation to perform Renaissance music accurately.
In mensural music, each note has a specific time value.
Medieval theorists developed complex systems for mensural rhythm.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin mensuralis, from Latin mensura (measure).
