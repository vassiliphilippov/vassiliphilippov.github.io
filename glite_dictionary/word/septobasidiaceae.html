Septobasidiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛp.toʊ.bəˌsaɪ.diˈeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / ˌsɛp.təʊ.bəˌsɪ.diˈeɪ.siˌi / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the subdivision Basidiomycota, known for parasitizing plants, especially in tropical regions.
a family of fungi (Basidiomycota)

Examples

Members of the Septobasidiaceae are commonly found on the bark of tropical trees.
Research has shown that Septobasidiaceae fungi can cause disease in economically important crops.
The unique life cycle of the Septobasidiaceae has attracted interest from mycologists worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Septobasidium' (a genus of fungi) and the standard suffix '-aceae' used for plant and fungal families.
