Erethizon

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛθɪˌzɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛθɪˌzɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large New World porcupines, including the North American porcupine.
genus of New World porcupines

Examples

Erethizon is the only genus of New World porcupines found in North America.
Researchers have studied Erethizon to understand the evolution of quills in rodents.
The best-known member of the genus Erethizon is the North American porcupine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupine - a large rodent with spines or quills on the body and tail

Etymology

From Greek erethizon, meaning to irritate or provoke, relating to the quills.
