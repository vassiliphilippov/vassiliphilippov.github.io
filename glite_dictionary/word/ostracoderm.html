ostracoderm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstrəkəˌdɝm / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəkəˌdɜːm / (BrE)
an extinct group of jawless, fish-like vertebrates with heavily armored bodies, found in the Paleozoic Era.
extinct armored jawless fish

Examples

Fossils of the ostracoderm reveal the presence of bony armor over most of its body.
Scientists believe that the extinction of the ostracoderm marked a major turning point in vertebrate evolution.
The museum's exhibit includes a reconstructed model of an ancient ostracoderm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agnathan - jawless fish

Etymology

From Greek ostrakon (shell) + derma (skin), referring to their armored 'shell-like' bodies.
