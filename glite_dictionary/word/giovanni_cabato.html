Giovanni Cabato

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊˌvɑːni kɑːˈbɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / dʒəʊˌvɑːni kəˈbɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
The Italian explorer (c. 1450–1498) who led the English expedition in 1497 that discovered the mainland of North America, exploring the coast from Nova Scotia to Newfoundland.
Italian explorer who discovered mainland North America

Examples

Many historians regard Giovanni Cabato as the first European to land on mainland North America since the Vikings.
In Italy, Giovanni Cabato is celebrated as a national hero and pioneer.
The achievements of Giovanni Cabato paved the way for further English exploration of the New World.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Cabot - Alternative English name of Giovanni Cabato, the Italian explorer

Etymology

Italian form of the name John Cabot.
