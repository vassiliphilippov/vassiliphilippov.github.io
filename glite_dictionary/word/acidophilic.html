acidophilic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæsɪdəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌæsɪdəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism, especially certain bacteria) able to grow well in an acidic environment.
thriving in acidic environments

Examples

Acidophilic bacteria play an important role in the breakdown of mineral ores in mining operations.
Some acidophilic fungi can even survive at a pH level as low as 2.
Researchers are studying acidophilic microbes found in volcanic hot springs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidophil - an organism that thrives in an acidic environment
  • Antonym: acidophobic - unable to tolerate acidic environments

Etymology

From acid + Greek -philos (loving).
