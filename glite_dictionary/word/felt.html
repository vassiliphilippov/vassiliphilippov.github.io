felt

1. NOUN

/ fɛlt / (AmE), / fɛlt / (BrE)
A fabric made by compressing and matting animal fibers, often wool, together without weaving.
fabric of matted animal fibers

Examples

The hat was made of soft felt and fit perfectly on his head.
She bought a roll of green felt to use for her craft project.
Billiard tables are often covered with a special type of felt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabric - cloth, typically produced by weaving or knitting textile fibers

Etymology

From Middle English felt, from Old English felt, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch vilt and German Filz.

to cover or line with felt, especially in manufacturing or crafts
cover or line with felt

3. VERB

/ fɛlt / (AmE), / fɛlt / (BrE)
to change in texture and become matted and dense like felt, often as a result of heat, moisture, or friction
become matted and felt-like in texture

Examples

If you wash this sweater in hot water, it will felt and shrink.
After several years, the old carpet had felted in the corners.
Some animals' fur can felt if not brushed regularly.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From the noun 'felt' (a matted textile), originally from Old High German 'filz' and Latin 'filtrum' (felt).
