projecting

1. ADJ

/ prəˈdʒɛktɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪŋ / (BrE)
extending out above or beyond a surface or boundary; jutting out
extending out; jutting

Examples

A projecting ledge made the cliff dangerous to climb.
The bird built its nest on a projecting branch high above the ground.
His projecting teeth gave him a distinctive smile.

Synset Examples

the jutting limb of a tree
massive projected buttresses
his protruding ribs
a pile of boards sticking over the end of his truck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jutting - extending sharply outwards; sticking out
  • Antonym: receding - moving back or away from a point; not sticking out

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'project', from Latin 'proicere' (to throw forth).
