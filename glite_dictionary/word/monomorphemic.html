monomorphemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑnəˌmɔrˈfimɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˌmɔːˈfiːmɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or linguistic form) consisting of only one morpheme and thus not divisible into smaller meaningful parts.
consisting of one morpheme

Examples

The word monomorphemic refers to forms that cannot be split into smaller meaningful units.
'Dog' is a monomorphemic word, whereas 'dogs' is not.
Linguists analyze whether a term is monomorphemic or made up of multiple morphemes.

Synset Examples

‘raise’ is monomorphemic but ‘rays’ is not

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polymorphemic - consisting of more than one morpheme

Etymology

From mono- (one) + morpheme (smallest unit of meaning) + -ic (adjectival suffix).
