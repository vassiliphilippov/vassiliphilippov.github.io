scab

1. NOUN

/ skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
a hard, dry covering that forms over a healing wound or sore on the skin
crust forming over a healing wound

Examples

After falling off his bike, Tim developed a scab on his knee that itched for days.
Try not to pick at the scab so your cut can heal properly.
The doctor explained that a scab protects the underlying skin as it recovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crust - a hard outer layer or covering, especially over something moist or soft

Etymology

From Middle English *scabbe*, from Old Norse *skabb* (scab, itch), related to Old English *sceabb*.

2. VERB

/ skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
(of a wound or skin) to form a scab as part of the healing process.
to form a scab (wound healing)

Examples

After a few days, the cut began to scab and looked less red.
It is important not to pick at wounds as they scab.
Her scraped knees eventually scabbed over and stopped bleeding.

Synset Examples

the wounds will eventually scab

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to heal - to become healthy again; to return to a normal state after injury

3. VERB

/ skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
(of a person) to work or accept employment in place of a striking worker, especially during a labor dispute.
work while others are on strike

Examples

Anyone who decides to scab during the strike will face strong criticism from the union.
She refused to scab even though she needed the money desperately.
Several workers crossed the picket line to scab for higher pay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strikebreaker - a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02418069-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
a person who works or provides workers during a strike, especially as a replacement for striking workers; considered derogatory by many
person working during a strike (derogatory)

Examples

He lost the support of his colleagues after being labeled a scab during the factory strike.
Union members shouted at the scab who crossed the picket line this morning.
The company hired several scabs to keep production going while the employees were on strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strikebreaker - a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'scabbe', originally meaning a skin disease, later figuratively extended to describe strikebreakers.
