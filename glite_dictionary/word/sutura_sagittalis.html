sutura sagittalis

1. NOUN

/ suːˈtʊrə səˈdʒɪtəlɪs / (AmE), / suːˈtjʊərə səˈdʒɪtəlɪs / (BrE)
the fibrous joint uniting the two parietal bones of the skull along the midline
fibrous joint between parietal bones of the skull
medical

Examples

The sutura sagittalis is crucial for the symmetrical development of the human skull.
The surgeon carefully examined the sutura sagittalis for any signs of premature closure.
An X-ray helped to reveal a fracture close to the sutura sagittalis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagittal suture - the anatomical term in English for this cranial suture

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'sagittal suture'.
