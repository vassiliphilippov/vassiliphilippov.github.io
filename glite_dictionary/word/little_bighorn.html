Little Bighorn

1. PROPN

/ ˌlɪt̬l ˈbaɪˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˌlɪtl ˈbaɪˌhɔːn / (BrE)
A famous 1876 battle near the Little Bighorn River in Montana between United States cavalry led by General Custer and a coalition of Native American tribes, resulting in Custer's defeat and death.
1876 battle between Custer's forces and Native American tribes

Examples

Historians still debate the tactics used at the Little Bighorn.
The defeat at Little Bighorn profoundly affected U.S. military policy in the West.
Many tourists visit the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Little Bighorn - Synonymous historical event - the full name of the battle
  • Degree Higher: Wounded Knee - A later massacre of Native Americans by U.S. troops in South Dakota, 1890

Etymology

Named after Little Bighorn River in Montana, USA.

2. PROPN

/ ˌlɪt̬l ˈbaɪˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˌlɪtl ˈbaɪˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a river in northern Wyoming and southern Montana, United States, known as the site of the Battle of Little Bighorn in 1876
river in Wyoming and Montana, site of historic battle
american

Examples

The Battle of the Little Bighorn is one of the most famous events in American history.
Custer's troops camped along the Little Bighorn before the battle began.
The Little Bighorn flows through both Wyoming and Montana, offering scenic views along its banks.

Etymology

From the Bighorn River, which was named for the bighorn sheep native to the area; 'Little' distinguishes it as a tributary.
