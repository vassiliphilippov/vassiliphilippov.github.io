Dactylopteridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪlˌɒp.təˈrɪd.iː / (AmE), / ˌdaktɪlˌɒptəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fish, known as flying gurnards, characterized by their large pectoral fins that resemble wings
family of fish known as flying gurnards

Examples

Researchers observed that Dactylopteridae use their large pectoral fins to scare away predators.
The colorful fins of the Dactylopteridae are often mistaken for wings when they glide near the ocean floor.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of different Dactylopteridae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying gurnard - a species of fish in the family Dactylopteridae

Etymology

From Greek dactylos (finger) + pteron (wing), referring to the finger-like projections of their fins.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdæktɪloʊˌpɜrtəˈraɪdi / (AmE), / ˌdaktɪləʊˌpɜːtɪˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical marine fishes known as flying gurnards, characterized by their large pectoral fins which they spread like wings.
family of flying gurnards (fish)

Examples

The Dactylopteridae are remarkable for their broad, wing-like fins that they fan out when startled.
Researchers studied several species within the Dactylopteridae family to understand their unique gliding behavior.
The aquarium featured a display dedicated to Dactylopteridae, highlighting the flying gurnard's ability to 'walk' along the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying gurnard - a tropical fish known for its wing-like pectoral fins

Etymology

From Modern Latin Dactylopteridae, from Greek daktylos (finger) + pteron (wing), referring to the ray-like fins.
