punic

1. ADJ

/ ˈpjuː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.nɪk / (BrE)
(of behaviour or character) treacherous or faithless, as stereotypically attributed to the Carthaginians by the Romans; perfidious.
treacherous or deceitful (historical)
archaic

Examples

The general accused the diplomat of making a punic promise that was soon broken.
Historians often refer to the Romans' view of Carthaginians as punic and untrustworthy.
Their punic conduct during the negotiations became a cautionary tale for generations.

Synset Examples

Punic faith
the perfidious Judas
the fiercest and most treacherous of foes
treacherous intrigues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfidious - deliberately faithless; treacherous
  • Synonym: treacherous - guilty of or involving betrayal or deception
  • Antonym: faithful - loyal, remaining true to a commitment or belief

Etymology

From Latin Punicus (Carthaginian), associated by Romans with treachery in reference to Carthaginian diplomacy.
