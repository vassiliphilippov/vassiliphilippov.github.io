Punic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.nɪk / (BrE)
the Phoenician dialect spoken in ancient Carthage
Phoenician dialect of Carthage

Examples

Scholars have discovered several inscriptions in Punic on ancient tablets.
The Punic language became extinct after the fall of Carthage.
He specializes in the study of Punic and other ancient Semitic languages.

Etymology

From Latin Punicus, from Poenus (Carthaginian), of Phoenician origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpjuː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.nɪk / (BrE)
relating to ancient Carthage, its people, or their language
of ancient Carthage or its people

Examples

The Punic Wars were a series of conflicts between Rome and Carthage.
She studied the Punic language as part of her research on ancient Mediterranean cultures.
Artifacts from the Punic period were unearthed during the latest excavation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carthaginian - of or relating to Carthage or its people
  • Degree Lower: Phoenician - relating to the ancient Semitic civilization closely connected with Carthage

Etymology

From Latin 'Punicus', from Poenus 'Carthaginian', ultimately from Phoenician 'Pōnnim'.
