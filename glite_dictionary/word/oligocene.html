Oligocene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɪɡəˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəˈsiːn / (BrE)
the geological epoch lasting from about 34 to 23 million years ago, marked by the development of many modern mammals and significant climatic changes
geological epoch 34–23 million years ago

Examples

The fossils from the Oligocene provide insight into the evolution of early mammals.
During the Oligocene, Earth's climate underwent significant cooling and drying.
Many important plant and animal groups diversified in the Oligocene epoch.

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + kainos (new), meaning 'few new (species)'.
