Hemerocallis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmərəˈkælɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmərəˈkælɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs, commonly known as day lilies, native to East Asia and widely cultivated for their showy flowers on long, leafless stems
genus of perennial day lilies

Examples

The garden was filled with vibrant Hemerocallis that bloomed in succession throughout the summer.
She specializes in hybridizing Hemerocallis to achieve unique colors and shapes.
Many gardeners appreciate Hemerocallis for their low maintenance and reliable flowering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: day lily - common name for plants of the genus Hemerocallis

Etymology

From Greek hēmera (day) + kallos (beauty), referring to the short-lived nature of the flowers.
