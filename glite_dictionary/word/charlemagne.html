Charlemagne

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɑrləˌmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːləmɛn / (BrE)
(742–814) The king of the Franks and Holy Roman Emperor, who united much of western and central Europe during the early Middle Ages.
Frankish king and Holy Roman Emperor (742–814)

Examples

Charlemagne was crowned Emperor by Pope Leo III on Christmas Day in 800.
Many European institutions trace their roots back to the reign of Charlemagne.
The expansion of the Frankish state under Charlemagne greatly shaped the history of Europe.

Etymology

From French 'Charlemagne', from Medieval Latin 'Carolus Magnus' meaning 'Charles the Great'.
