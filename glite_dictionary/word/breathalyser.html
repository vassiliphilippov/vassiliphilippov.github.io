breathalyser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛθəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθələˌzə / (BrE)
a device used to measure the alcohol content in a person's breath, often used by police to check for drunk driving
device measuring alcohol in breath

Examples

The police officer asked the driver to blow into the breathalyser during the roadside check.
After the party, he was relieved that the breathalyser showed he was under the legal limit.
New technology has made the breathalyser more accurate and easier to use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breathalyzer - device measuring alcohol in breath (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'breath' + 'analyser', indicating a device that analyses breath.
