to lexicalize

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛksɪkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or coin (a concept) into a word, or to adopt a new word into the vocabulary of a language
make a concept into a word; add word to a language

Examples

It can take years for slang terms to lexicalize and appear in dictionaries.
The word 'email' was quickly lexicalized as technology advanced.
Some cultural ideas are hard to lexicalize in another language.

Synset Examples

The concept expressed by German ‘Gemuetlichkeit’ is not lexicalized in English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin - to invent a new word or phrase

Etymology

From 'lexical' (relating to words) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).
