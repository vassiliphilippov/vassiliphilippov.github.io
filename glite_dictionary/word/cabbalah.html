Cabbalah

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
A system of Jewish mystical and esoteric thought, developed by rabbis between the 7th and 18th centuries, based on interpretations of the Hebrew scriptures.
Jewish mystical philosophy

Examples

Many scholars study the Cabbalah to understand the mystical aspects of Judaism.
He became interested in the Cabbalah after reading ancient Jewish texts.
The teachings of the Cabbalah have influenced modern spiritual movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - Alternative spelling of Cabbalah, referring to the same mystical tradition

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cabbala, from Hebrew qabbālāh meaning 'received tradition'.
