vole

1. NOUN

/ voʊl / (AmE), / vəʊl / (BrE)
a small, mouselike rodent with a stout body, short tail, and small ears, typically found in fields or meadows
small rodent found in fields or meadows

Examples

As we walked through the grassy meadow, a vole darted out from under a rock.
The barn owl feeds mainly on small mammals, such as voles and mice.
Researchers studied the vole population to monitor the health of the local ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - a small rodent with a long tail
  • Degree Higher: rat - a larger rodent related to mice and voles

Etymology

From Norwegian 'vollmus', from 'voll' (field) + 'mus' (mouse).
