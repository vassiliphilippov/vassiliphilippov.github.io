polygynous

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪdʒənəs / (AmE), / pəˈlɪdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
having more than one wife at the same time; relating to or characterized by polygyny.
having more than one wife

Examples

Certain societies are traditionally polygynous, allowing men to have multiple wives.
The polygynous structure of the community influenced its social dynamics.
In some animal species, males lead polygynous groups during mating season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monogamous - having only one mate, wife, or husband at a time
  • Synonym: polygamous - having more than one mate, wife, or husband at a time

Etymology

From Greek polygynēs, from poly- (many) + gynē (woman, wife).
