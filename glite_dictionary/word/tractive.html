tractive

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / ˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or exerting traction; serving to pull or draw something
exerting traction or pulling force

Examples

The tractive effort of the locomotive allowed it to haul heavy freight trains over steep hills.
Modern tractors are designed to maximize their tractive power in difficult field conditions.
Increasing the tractive capability of the tires improved the vehicle's performance on muddy roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tractive force - the force used to pull or draw an object along; often in mechanics or engineering

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'tractivus', from Latin 'tractus' (a drawing or pulling).
