carter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to drive a cart, especially for transporting goods
person who drives a cart

Examples

The carter led his horse-drawn wagon through the busy market streets.
In the 19th century, a carter was essential for transporting goods across town.
The old carter knew every back road and shortcut in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driver - person who drives a vehicle

Etymology

From Middle English 'carte' (cart) + '-er' (agent noun suffix), meaning one who drives a cart.
