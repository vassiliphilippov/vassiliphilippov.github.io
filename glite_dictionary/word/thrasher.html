thrasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈθræʃər / (AmE), / ˈθræʃə / (BrE)
a large shark of warm seas, known for its long, whiplike tail used to herd small fish when hunting
large shark with a whiplike tail

Examples

The thrasher is easily recognized by its exceptionally long tail fin.
Fishermen on the coast often spot the thrasher hunting in shallow waters.
A thrasher uses its tail to stun schools of fish before feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher shark - another name for a thrasher shark, referring to the same species

2. NOUN

/ ˈθræʃər / (AmE), / ˈθræʃə / (BrE)
a farm machine used to separate seeds or grain from the husks and straw
machine for separating grain from husks

Examples

The old thrasher stood rusting in the corner of the barn after decades of use.
The invention of the thrasher revolutionized grain farming in the nineteenth century.
Farmers would gather to operate the thrasher during the busy harvest season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher - another spelling, often with same meaning as farm machine
  • Degree Higher: combine harvester - machine that combines reaping, threshing, and cleaning grain crops

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'thresh,' meaning to separate grain, from Old English 'þrescan.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈθræʃər / (AmE), / ˈθræʃə / (BrE)
A thrush-like American songbird belonging to the family Mimidae, noted for its ability to mimic the songs of other birds.
American songbird able to mimic birdsongs

Examples

The brown thrasher is known for its remarkable ability to mimic a variety of birdsongs.
During spring, we often hear a thrasher singing from the top of the hedge.
Many birdwatchers are thrilled to spot a thrasher in their backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockingbird - an American songbird that also mimics other birdsongs
