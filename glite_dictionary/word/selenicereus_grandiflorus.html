Selenicereus grandiflorus

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌliːnɪˌsɪəriəs ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrəs / (AmE), / sɛˌliːnɪˌsɪəriˈuːs ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A tropical American climbing cactus known for its large, showy, night-blooming flowers and triangular branches, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
climbing cactus with night-blooming flowers

Examples

The Selenicereus grandiflorus is prized for its spectacular blossoms that open only at night.
Many gardeners cultivate Selenicereus grandiflorus to enjoy its rare and fragrant midnight flowers.
The yellow, red-streaked fruit of Selenicereus grandiflorus add a splash of color to tropical gardens.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Selenicereus* (from Greek Selene, goddess of the moon, and cereus, Latin for candle) and species *grandiflorus* (from Latin, meaning 'large-flowered').
