rectory

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəri / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəri / (BrE)
an official residence provided by a church for its parson, vicar, or rector
official clergy residence provided by a church

Examples

The new rectory was built next to the village church.
After moving into the rectory, the family invited parishioners for tea.
The old rectory has been converted into a community center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicarage - residence provided by a church for a vicar
  • Synonym: parsonage - clergyman's house provided by a church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin rectoria, from rector (ruler, governor, rector).
