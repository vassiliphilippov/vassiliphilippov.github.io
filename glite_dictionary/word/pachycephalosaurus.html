Pachycephalosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækɪˌsɛfələˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˌsɛfələˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of herbivorous, dome-headed dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, known for their thick skulls.
dome-headed dinosaur genus

Examples

The fossilized skull of a Pachycephalosaurus displays an extraordinarily thick dome.
Scientists believe Pachycephalosaurus may have used their heads for combat or display.
A Pachycephalosaurus skeleton was recently discovered in Montana.

Etymology

From Greek *pakhys* (thick) + *kephalē* (head) + *sauros* (lizard).
