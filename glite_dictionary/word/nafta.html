NAFTA

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæf.tə / (AmE), / ˈnæf.tə / (BrE)
the North American Free Trade Agreement, an accord for free trade among the United States, Canada, and Mexico, which took effect in 1994
North American Free Trade Agreement (1994)

Examples

The NAFTA agreement significantly increased trade between the United States, Canada, and Mexico.
Many workers debated the impact of NAFTA on manufacturing jobs in their local communities.
After renegotiations, NAFTA was replaced by the United States–Mexico–Canada Agreement (USMCA) in 2020.

Etymology

Acronym for North American Free Trade Agreement.
