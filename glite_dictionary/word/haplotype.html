haplotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæp.loʊ.taɪp / (AmE), / ˈhæp.lə.taɪp / (BrE)
(Genetics) a group of genes or a combination of alleles inherited together from a single parent, located close together on the same chromosome.
group of genes inherited together
medical

Examples

Scientists used DNA sequencing to identify the haplotype shared by the affected family members.
Each haplotype can help trace genetic inheritance and disease risk.
The population study revealed a unique haplotype common among native groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genotype - the genetic constitution of an individual organism

Etymology

From Greek 'haplo-' (single, simple) + 'type'.
