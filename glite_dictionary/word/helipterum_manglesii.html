Helipterum manglesii

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪˈptɪrəm ˌmæŋˈɡliː.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈptɪərəm ˌmæŋˈɡliː.aɪ / (BrE)
An Australian annual everlasting plant with light pink, nodding flower heads, sometimes classified in the genus Helipterum.
Australian annual everlasting plant

Examples

During her visit to Western Australia, she photographed the delicate Helipterum manglesii in bloom.
Gardeners prize Helipterum manglesii for its persistent flowers and soft pink color.
The conservation group launched a program to protect wild populations of Helipterum manglesii.

Etymology

The species epithet ‘manglesii’ honors James Mangles, an English botanist.
