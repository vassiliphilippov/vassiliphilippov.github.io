muttering

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌt̬ərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtərɪŋ / (BrE)
a low, continuous, and indistinct sound, often produced by quietly moving the lips without clear or articulate speech
low, indistinct continuous sound

Examples

We could hear a low muttering coming from the next room, but couldn't make out the words.
His constant muttering during the meeting distracted everyone around him.
In the darkness, the only sound was the muttering of the old man by the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct, continuous sound
  • Synonym: whisper - to speak very softly using the breath but not the vocal cords

Etymology

From the verb 'mutter', probably imitative in origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌt̬ɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtəɹɪŋ / (BrE)
a quiet or indistinct complaint or remark, typically made in a low voice and often expressing discontent
low, indistinct complaint or remark

Examples

His constant muttering about the new policy annoyed his coworkers.
The teacher ignored the muttering coming from the back of the classroom.
There was a low muttering among the audience as the speaker took the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - a complaint spoken in a low voice; to complain quietly
  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct sound or whisper, often expressing dissatisfaction

Etymology

From the verb 'mutter' + -ing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07225752-n
Synset POS: n
