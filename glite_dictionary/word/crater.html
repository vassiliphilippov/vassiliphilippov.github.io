Crater

1. PROPN

/ ˈkreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near Hydra and Corvus
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Crater can be seen best during late spring in the Southern Hemisphere.
Ancient astronomers named Crater after a cup due to its shape in the sky.
Crater is not as bright as neighboring constellations like Hydra and Corvus.

Etymology

From Latin 'crater', meaning 'cup', reflecting the constellation's shape.
