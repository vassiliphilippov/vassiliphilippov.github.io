crater

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.tə / (BrE)
A large, bowl-shaped hole in the ground or on a planet's surface, typically caused by a meteorite or bomb impact.
bowl-shaped depression from impact

Examples

The crater left by the meteorite was over a kilometer wide.
After the explosion, a huge crater appeared in the middle of the street.
Scientists studied the crater to learn more about the asteroid impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caldera - a large volcanic crater, typically formed by collapse after an eruption

Etymology

From Latin 'crater' (mixing bowl), from Greek 'kratēr'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
a bowl-shaped depression at the summit of a volcano, formed by volcanic activity
bowl-shaped volcanic depression

Examples

The lava slowly filled the crater at the peak of the volcano.
Scientists studied the crater to understand the eruption's effects.
From the airplane, the volcanic crater was clearly visible.

Etymology

From Latin crater, from Ancient Greek kratēr (mixing bowl).
