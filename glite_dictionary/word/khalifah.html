khalifah

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈliːfə / (AmE), / kæˈliːfə / (BrE)
the civil and religious leader of a Muslim state, regarded as the representative of Allah on earth; a spiritual and political successor to the Prophet Muhammad
Islamic civil and religious leader

Examples

The appointment of a new khalifah was a significant event in the Muslim community.
Debates over who should become khalifah have shaped Islamic history.
Some scholars wrote extensively about the ideal qualities of a khalifah.

Synset Examples

many radical Muslims believe a Khalifah will unite all Islamic lands and people and subjugate the rest of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliph - chief Muslim civil and religious ruler, regarded as the successor to Muhammad

Etymology

From Arabic خليفة‎ (khalīfa), meaning 'successor' or 'steward'.
