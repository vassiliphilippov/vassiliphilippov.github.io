Roman

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən / (BrE)
a person who lived in ancient Rome or the Roman Empire
inhabitant of ancient Rome or its empire

Examples

Julius Caesar was a famous Roman known for his military leadership.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a Roman villa in the countryside.
The Roman colonies spread their culture throughout Europe and North Africa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Romanian - a native or inhabitant of modern Romania

Etymology

From Latin Romanus, meaning 'of Rome'.

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən / (BrE)
(of type) having upright characters, characteristic of the modern style directly based on those used in ancient Roman inscriptions.
upright style of printed type

Examples

She prefers books printed in Roman type rather than italic.
The document should be formatted using a standard Roman font for readability.
Traditional newspapers often use Roman style for their main text.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: italic - a type style with slanted letters usually used for emphasis

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (of Rome), referencing the style used in ancient Rome.

WordNet ID: oewn-02933399-a
Synset POS: a
