roman

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən / (BrE)
a typeface based on the upright lettering style used in ancient Roman inscriptions, featuring serifs and regular proportions
upright typeface with serifs based on Roman inscriptions

Examples

The book was set entirely in roman type for clarity.
Traditional newspapers often use roman faces for their main text.
This font family includes both roman and italic styles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: italic - a slanted typeface style often contrasted with roman

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' meaning 'Roman' or 'of Rome'.
