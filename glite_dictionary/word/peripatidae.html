Peripatidae

1. NOUN

/ pɛrɪˈpætɪˌdi / (AmE), / pɛrɪˈpætɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of velvet worms in the phylum Onychophora, characterized by soft, elongated bodies and numerous leg-like appendages.
a family of velvet worms (Onychophora)

Examples

Members of the Peripatidae are found mainly in tropical regions.
Scientists study the Peripatidae to understand early evolutionary adaptations in invertebrates.
The Peripatidae differ from related families by their reproductive and anatomical features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onychophora - a phylum of soft-bodied, many-legged invertebrates known as velvet worms

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Peripatus and the family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ pɛrɪˈpætɪdiː / (AmE), / pɛrɪˈpatɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of onychophoran invertebrates, commonly known as velvet worms, found mainly in tropical regions.
family of velvet worms (Onychophora)

Examples

The Peripatidae are distinguished from other velvet worm families by their reproductive anatomy.
Biologists discovered a new species of Peripatidae in the South American rainforest.
Members of the Peripatidae family bear live young, unlike some related families.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Peripatus + family suffix -idae.
