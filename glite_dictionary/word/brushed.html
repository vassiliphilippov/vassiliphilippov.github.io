brushed

1. ADJ

/ brʌʃt / (AmE), / brʌʃt / (BrE)
(of fabric) having a soft, raised surface created by brushing the fibers to make them smooth or fuzzy.
fabric with softened, raised surface

Examples

She bought a jacket made of brushed cotton for extra warmth.
The blanket's brushed finish makes it soft to the touch.
Brushed fabrics are often chosen for winter clothing because of their cozy feel.

Synset Examples

a dress of brushed cotton
a fleecy lining
napped fabrics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napped - (of fabric) having a soft, raised surface, typically achieved by brushing

Etymology

From the verb 'brush', referring to the use of a brush to raise the nap of fabric.

3. ADJ

/ brʌʃt / (AmE), / brʌʃt / (BrE)
(of hair or clothing) having been groomed or made neat with a brush.
groomed with a brush (hair/clothes)

Examples

His suit looked immaculate, with every part carefully brushed before the meeting.
The brushed coat on the dog made it appear soft and shiny.

Related Terms
