Pavo cristatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.voʊ krɪˈsteɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.vəʊ krɪsˈtɑː.təs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Indian peafowl, a large, colorful bird native to the Indian subcontinent, known for the male's spectacular tail feathers.
scientific name of the Indian peafowl

Examples

The Pavo cristatus is renowned for the spectacular display of its tail feathers during courtship.
You can spot the Pavo cristatus in many gardens and parks throughout India.
Unlike other birds, the male Pavo cristatus has iridescent blue and green plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacock - male Indian peafowl, known for its colorful tail
  • Synonym: peafowl - any bird of the genus Pavo, especially the Indian and green peafowl

Etymology

From Latin: pavo (peacock) + cristatus (crested, tufted).
