broad-leaved

1. ADJ

/ brɔd livd / (AmE), / brɔːd liːvd / (BrE)
(of plants) having relatively wide, flat leaves rather than needlelike or scalelike leaves.
having wide, flat leaves (not needles)

Examples

The broad-leaved trees in the park provided plenty of shade throughout the summer.
Many garden plants, such as hydrangeas and hostas, are broad-leaved species.
The forest is a mixture of pine and broad-leaved deciduous trees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrow-leaved - having relatively narrow leaves
