call-back

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlˌbæk / (BrE)
the act of asking an employee to return to work after a period of layoff
recall of an employee after a layoff

Examples

After being laid off in January, she received a call-back from her employer in March.
The company issued a call-back to fifty workers following the upturn in business.
Union members awaited news of a possible call-back after the seasonal downturn ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - the official order to return to work
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01220703-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlˌbæk / (BrE)
an act of telephoning someone again in response to an earlier call or message
a return telephone call

Examples

I'll give you a call-back as soon as I have more information.
The company promised a call-back within 24 hours.
I missed his call but he left a message asking for a call-back later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return call - an act of responding to a telephone call by calling back
