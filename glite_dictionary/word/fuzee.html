fuzee

1. NOUN

/ fjuˈzi / (AmE), / fjuˈzi / (BrE)
a type of friction match with a large head designed to stay alight in the wind
large-headed match that stays lit in wind
archaic

Examples

The sailor struck a fuzee to light his pipe in the blustery weather.
She carried a box of fuzees in her coat, just in case the wind picked up.
Lighting a fuzee, he managed to read the note despite the strong breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusee - a friction match or railway signal flare

Etymology

Variant of 'fusee', from French 'fusée' meaning 'spindle', influenced by 'fuse' (an igniting device).

a colored flare signal for vehicles

Examples

Railway workers placed a fuzee on the tracks to signal the oncoming train to stop.
After the collision, emergency responders deployed a red fuzee to mark the danger zone.

3. NOUN

/ fuˈzi / (AmE), / fjuːˈziː / (BrE)
an igniter used to initiate the burning of a propellant, such as a fuse or device in ammunition or fireworks
igniter for starting combustion in propellants

Examples

The artillery shell was equipped with a fuzee to ensure a controlled explosion.
Pyrotechnicians handled the fuzee carefully to safely ignite the fireworks display.
Each signal flare contains a special fuzee that initiates the propellant's burn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a device for igniting an explosive charge

Etymology

Variant of 'fuse', from French 'fusée' (spindle, rocket), from Latin 'fusus' (spindle).
