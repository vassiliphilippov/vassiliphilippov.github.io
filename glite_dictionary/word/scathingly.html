scathingly

1. ADV

/ ˈskeɪðɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈskeɪθɪŋli / (BrE)
In a harshly critical or severely disapproving manner.
in a harshly critical way

Examples

The editor wrote a scathingly negative review of the new novel.
She replied scathingly to his unwelcome comment, leaving him speechless.
Critics spoke scathingly about the government's handling of the crisis.

Synset Examples

she criticized him scathingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scathing - harshly critical; severely critical or scornful
  • Antonym: mildly - in a gentle or not harsh manner

Etymology

From 'scathing' (harshly critical) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
