isolationist

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that their country should not be involved in the affairs of other countries
someone who supports political isolationism

Examples

The senator was a known isolationist who opposed foreign intervention.
During the debate, several isolationists argued against joining the alliance.
Historically, isolationist policies have shaped the country's foreign affairs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interventionist - a person who favors intervening in the affairs of other countries

Etymology

From 'isolation' + '-ist', dating from early 20th century political discourse.

2. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (BrE)
favoring or relating to the policy of remaining apart from the affairs or interests of other groups, especially the political affairs of other countries.
related to isolationism; non-interventionist

Examples

The government adopted an isolationist stance during the early years of the conflict.
Her isolationist views influenced her opposition to foreign alliances.
Many politicians with isolationist beliefs argued against entering the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: non-interventionist - favoring not becoming involved in other countries' affairs
  • Antonym: internationalist - favoring cooperation among nations

Etymology

From 'isolation' (Latin 'insula' meaning island) plus '-ist'.
