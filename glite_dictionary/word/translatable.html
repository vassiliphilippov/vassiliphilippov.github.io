translatable

1. ADJ

/ trænzˈleɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / trænsˈleɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be expressed, converted, or rendered into another language, form, or style.
able to be translated or converted

Examples

The novel is highly translatable because its themes are universal.
Scientific terms are not always easily translatable into everyday language.
Many jokes in one culture are barely translatable to another.

Synset Examples

substances readily translatable to the American home table
his books are eminently translatable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untranslatable - not able to be translated or rendered into another language or form

Etymology

From Latin translatus (carried over, transferred) + English -able.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02498770-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ trænzˈleɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / trænsˈleɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be changed or transformed into another form or substance, especially in a way that resembles an alchemical or substantial conversion.
able to be transformed into another form

Examples

The plan is only useful if the ideas are translatable into practical solutions.
Some chemical elements are not translatable into other substances by ordinary means.
His vision was translatable into tangible results through hard work and collaboration.

Synset Examples

is lead really transmutable into gold?
ideas translatable into reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convertible - able to be changed into a different form or use
  • Antonym: immutable - unable to be changed; unchangeable

Etymology

From Late Latin translatabilis, from transferre (to carry over, transform).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00349639-s
Synset POS: s
