chalice

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃælɪs / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪs / (BrE)
a large, often ornate cup with a stem and base, used for drinking wine, especially during the Christian Eucharist or other religious ceremonies
ceremonial cup, especially for Eucharist

Examples

During the ceremony, the priest lifted the chalice and offered a blessing to the congregation.
The museum displayed an ancient silver chalice from the medieval period.
Each participant took a sip of wine from the same chalice during the ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goblet - a drinking glass with a foot and a stem

Etymology

From Latin calix (cup, goblet), via Old French calice.
