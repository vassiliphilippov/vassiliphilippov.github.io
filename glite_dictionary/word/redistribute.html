to redistribute

1. VERB

/ ˌridɪˈstrɪbjut / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈstrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to distribute something again or differently, especially to achieve greater fairness or effectiveness
distribute again or differently

Examples

The organization decided to redistribute its resources to better serve rural communities.
After the merger, management must redistribute staff across new departments.
To address inequality, the government plans to redistribute wealth through revised tax policies.

Synset Examples

redistribute the troops more strategically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or spread something to a number of people

Etymology

From re- (again) + distribute, from Latin distribuere (to divide, assign).
