biophysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈfɪz.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈfɪz.ɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of biological processes and systems using the methods and principles of physics
study of biological systems using physics

Examples

She decided to pursue a PhD in biophysics to bridge her interests in biology and physics.
Recent advances in biophysics have improved our understanding of cellular structures.
His research in biophysics focuses on the physical properties of biomolecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biochemistry - the branch of science concerned with the chemical processes within living organisms
  • Degree Lower: biology - the scientific study of life and living organisms
  • Degree Lower: physics - the branch of science concerned with the nature and properties of matter and energy

Etymology

From bio- (life) + physics, first used in the mid-19th century.
