glochidium

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊˈkɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈkɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A small barbed bristle or spine, especially one found in tufts on the surface of certain cacti.
barbed bristle or spine on cacti

Examples

Some species of cacti are covered with tiny glochidia that can cause skin irritation.
Be careful when handling prickly pears as their glochidia are easily detached and hard to remove.
The biologist used tweezers to study the structure of a single glochidium under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek glochidion (small point), diminutive of glochis (projecting point).
