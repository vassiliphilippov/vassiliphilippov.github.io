artlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːtləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being natural, simple, and innocent, without deception or pretense
innocent simplicity; lack of deceit

Examples

Her artlessness made her instantly likable to everyone she met.
The child's artlessness was clear in his honest answers.
Despite her artlessness, she quickly adapted to the complexities of city life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilelessness - the quality of being without cunning or deceit
  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being innocent or lacking in experience
  • Antonym: cunning - skill in achieving goals through deceit or evasion

Etymology

From 'artless' + '-ness'; 'artless' from Old French 'art' (skill) + '-less' (without).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrt.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːt.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being simple, natural, and free from cunning or deceit; innocence or sincerity
innocence; lack of cunning or deceit

Examples

Her artlessness made her instantly likable to everyone she met.
There was a charming artlessness in his way of speaking that put people at ease.
The artlessness of the painting gives it an honest and refreshing appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilelessness - the quality of being without cunning or slyness
  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being free from guilt or corruption
  • Antonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit

Etymology

From 'artless' + '-ness', 'artless' from Middle English, meaning 'without art or cunning'.
