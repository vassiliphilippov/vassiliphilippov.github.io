rue

1. NOUN

/ ruː / (AmE), / ruː / (BrE)
a strong-scented European perennial herb (Ruta graveolens) with bitter-tasting gray-green leaves, sometimes causing skin irritation
strong-scented European herb with bitter leaves

Examples

She grows rue in her herb garden for its distinctive aroma.
Some people use rue leaves in traditional remedies despite their bitterness.
Contact with fresh rue can cause a rash similar to poison ivy.

Etymology

From Old French *rue*, from Latin *ruta* (rue plant), from Greek *rhyte*.

2. NOUN

/ ruː / (AmE), / ruː / (BrE)
a strong-smelling Mediterranean shrub (Ruta graveolens) with bitter, blue-green leaves sometimes used in flavoring, but which can cause skin irritation.
a Mediterranean shrub with bitter leaves

Examples

She used a tiny amount of rue to flavor the stew, warning that too much could be harmful.
The gardener wore gloves when handling rue because its leaves can irritate the skin.
Historically, rue was believed to protect against evil spirits and was grown in many herb gardens.

Etymology

From Old French 'rue', from Latin 'ruta', from Greek 'rhutē' (plant name).

3. NOUN

/ ruː / (AmE), / ruː / (BrE)
a feeling of regret or sorrow, especially for something wrong done or a disappointment
regret or sorrow for a wrong or loss

Examples

He was filled with rue after realizing the consequences of his actions.
To her lasting rue, she missed her only chance to see the play.
The bitter rue in his voice was evident as he recounted the story.

Synset Examples

he drank to drown his sorrows
he wrote a note expressing his regret
to his rue, the error cost him the game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment over something that has happened or been done
  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Old French *rue*, from Latin *ruga* meaning 'wrinkle, crease, regret'.

5. VERB

/ ruː / (AmE), / ruː / (BrE)
to feel regret or remorse for something; to be sorry about an action or event
to regret or feel sorry for

Examples

He will surely rue the day he made that decision.
Many people rue their mistakes long after they are made.
She rued not taking the opportunity when it was offered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - to feel sad, repentant, or disappointed over something that has happened
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology
