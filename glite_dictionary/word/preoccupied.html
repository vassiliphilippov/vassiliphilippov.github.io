preoccupied

1. ADJ

/ priˈɑk.juˌpaɪd / (AmE), / priˈɒk.juˌpaɪd / (BrE)
thinking or worrying about something so much that you do not pay attention to other things or people
lost in thought or worry about something

Examples

She was so preoccupied with her work that she forgot to eat lunch.
His preoccupied expression made it clear he was thinking about something important.
While driving, try not to be preoccupied by your phone or other distractions.

Synset Examples

became more and more haunted by the stupid riddle
was absolutely obsessed with the girl
got no help from his wife who was preoccupied with the children
he was taken up in worry for the old woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsessed - continually and compulsively focused on something
  • Synonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because of worries or thoughts
  • Antonym: carefree - free from worries or focused concerns

Etymology

From Latin praoccupatus, past participle of praoccupare (to seize beforehand).

2. ADJ

/ priˈɑkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / priˈɒkjəpaɪd / (BrE)
Thinking deeply about something so that you are not aware of other things around you.
deeply absorbed in thought

Examples

She was so preoccupied with her work that she didn't hear the phone ring.
His preoccupied expression made it clear he was thinking about something important.
Walking down the street, I was too preoccupied to notice the rain starting.

Synset Examples

as distant and bemused as a professor listening to the prattling of his freshman class
lost in thought
a preoccupied frown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because one's mind is preoccupied
  • Synonym: engrossed - completely absorbed by something
  • Antonym: unconcerned - not worried or not showing interest

Etymology

From Latin praeoccupare (to seize beforehand).

Synset POS: s
