frond

1. NOUN

/ frɑnd / (AmE), / frɒnd / (BrE)
a large, divided leaf, especially of a fern, palm, or cycad
large divided leaf of a fern or palm

Examples

The delicate frond of the fern unfurled slowly in the morning light.
Palm fronds swayed gently in the tropical breeze above our heads.
She admired the intricate patterns on the frond as she examined the plant up close.

Etymology

From Latin frons, frondis (foliage, leaf).
