Acrocomia vinifera

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈkoʊmiə vaɪˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈkəʊmɪə vɪˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
A tropical American palm tree with edible nuts and valuable fibrous material.
tropical American palm tree

Examples

The Acrocomia vinifera palm is cultivated for its nutritious nuts in several regions of Central America.
Researchers are studying the fiber from Acrocomia vinifera for sustainable textile production.
Farmers appreciate Acrocomia vinifera because it provides both food and valuable materials.

Etymology

From the genus name Acrocomia, from Greek 'akros' (highest) + 'kome' (hair), and 'vinifera' from Latin 'vinum' (wine) + '-fer' (bearing).
