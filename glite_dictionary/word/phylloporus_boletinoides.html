Phylloporus boletinoides

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌloʊpərəs boʊˌlɛtəˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒpərəs bəʊˌlɛtəˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a broadly convex brown cap and pores that extend part way down the stalk.
a species of brown-capped fungus

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Phylloporus boletinoides after the heavy rains.
Mushroom enthusiasts are often keen to identify Phylloporus boletinoides due to its distinctive pores.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Phylloporus boletinoides in different woodland regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'poros' (pore), and Latinized form of 'boletus' (mushroom).
