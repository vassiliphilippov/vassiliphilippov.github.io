equalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːkwəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making things equal or uniform; the process of ensuring evenness or balance.
the act of making equal or uniform
british

Examples

The equalisation of salaries across different departments was a major company initiative.
Sound engineers use equalisation to balance the various audio frequencies in a recording.
Recent tax reforms aim at the equalisation of economic opportunities for all citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equalization - the act of making equal or uniform (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'aequare' (make equal), via 'equalisation' in French and 'equalize' in English.
