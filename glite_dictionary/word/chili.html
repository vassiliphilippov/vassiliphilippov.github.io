chili

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
A small, very hot pepper with a pointed shape, used especially to add spiciness to food.
a hot, spicy pepper

Examples

The recipe calls for one red chili to add heat to the dish.
She accidentally touched her eyes after chopping a chili and it stung for hours.
Farmers in Mexico grow dozens of varieties of chili every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - another term for a spicy pepper
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - a mild, sweet pepper

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chīlli* via Spanish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
A spicy stew made chiefly of ground beef and chili peppers or chili powder, often with tomatoes and kidney beans.
spicy stew with beef and beans
american

Examples

She made a big pot of chili for the family dinner.
The cook-off winner had the spiciest chili I've ever tasted.
His homemade chili is famous for its rich flavor and tender beef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilli con carne - Spicy stew of beef, chili peppers, and often beans, also known as chili
  • Synonym: stew - Dish of ingredients simmered in liquid, similar type of dish

Etymology

American English, short for 'chili con carne', from Spanish 'chile' (pepper) + 'con carne' (with meat).
