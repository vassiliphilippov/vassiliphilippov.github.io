unease

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈiz / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːz / (BrE)
a feeling of discomfort, anxiety, or mild physical or mental distress
feeling of discomfort or anxiety

Examples

There was a sense of unease in the room as they waited for the news.
After the argument, a lingering unease settled between them.
Traveling alone at night always fills her with unease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discomfort - a mild physical or mental uneasiness
  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calm - absence of nervousness or tension

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'ease'.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈiz / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːz / (BrE)
A feeling or quality of being uncomfortable, anxious, or not relaxed.
discomfort or anxiety

Examples

There was a sense of unease among the audience as the speaker hesitated.
His constant fidgeting was a sign of unease during the interview.
Despite her smile, a feeling of unease lingered in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discomfort - a feeling of slight pain or uneasiness
  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: ease - a state of being comfortable or relaxed

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'ease' (comfort, relaxation).
