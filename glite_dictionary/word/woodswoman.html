woodswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈwʊdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who lives in or is knowledgeable about the woods, especially one skilled in outdoor activities
woman who lives in the woods

Examples

She lived as a true woodswoman, relying on her skills to survive in the forest.
The book tells the story of a woodswoman who built her own cabin by the lake.
As a seasoned woodswoman, she led groups on wilderness expeditions each summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodsman - a man who lives in or is skilled at life in the woods

Etymology

Formed from 'woods' + 'woman', modeled after 'woodsman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈwʊdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who works with wood, especially by making things from it or living and working in the woods
woman skilled in working with wood

Examples

She became a skilled woodswoman after years of crafting furniture by hand.
As a woodswoman, she enjoyed the solitude of working in her forest cabin.
The woodswoman demonstrated traditional woodworking techniques at the fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodsman - a man who works with wood or lives in the woods
  • Synonym: carpenter - a person who makes or repairs wooden objects

Etymology

Formed from 'woods' + 'woman', modeled after 'woodsman'.

WordNet ID: oewn-82232966-n
Synset POS: n
