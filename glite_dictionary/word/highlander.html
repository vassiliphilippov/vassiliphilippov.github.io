Highlander

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌlændər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪləndə / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of the Highlands of Scotland.
native of the Scottish Highlands

Examples

The Highlander wore traditional tartan clothing at the festival.
Many Highlanders are proud of their Gaelic heritage and customs.
She learned about the history and culture of the Highlander communities in Scotland.

Etymology

From 'Highland' + '-er', denoting a person from the Highlands.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.læn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.læn.də / (BrE)
a soldier who serves in a regiment from the Scottish Highlands
soldier from a Scottish Highland regiment

Examples

The famous Black Watch regiment included many formidable Highlanders in its ranks.
Dressed in their traditional kilts, the Highlanders marched proudly through the town square.
During the ceremony, a Highlander played the bagpipes to honor fallen comrades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowlander - a person from the Lowlands of Scotland

Etymology

From 'Highland', referring to the mountainous region of Scotland, combined with the suffix '-er' indicating a person from or associated with a place.
