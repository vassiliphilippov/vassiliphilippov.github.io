Prunus americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
a species of wild plum tree native to eastern and central North America, bearing red-orange fruit with yellow flesh
wild plum tree of North America

Examples

The Prunus americana produces tart, edible plums that are used in making jams and jellies.
Several Prunus americana trees grow wild along the riverbank in Minnesota.
Botanists studied the Prunus americana to understand its adaptability to different soil types.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Prunus' meaning 'plum tree' and 'americana' denoting American origin.
