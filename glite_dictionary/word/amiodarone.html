amiodarone

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmiˈoʊdəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˌamiˈəʊdərəʊn / (BrE)
a powerful antiarrhythmic drug used to treat serious irregular heart rhythms, usually prescribed when other treatments have failed
antiarrhythmic heart drug
medical

Examples

After several failed treatments, the doctor decided to prescribe amiodarone to manage the patient's arrhythmia.
Because amiodarone can have serious side effects, its use is closely monitored by specialists.
The cardiologist explained that amiodarone is reserved for cases where other drugs have not been effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cordarone - trade name for amiodarone

Etymology

From am- (relating to the amine group) + iod- (iodine) + -arone (as in related compounds).
