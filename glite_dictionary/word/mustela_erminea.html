Mustela erminea

1. NOUN

/ məsˈtɛlə ɜrˈmiːniə / (AmE), / mʌˈstɛlə ɜːˈmiːnɪə / (BrE)
A small mustelid mammal of the Northern Hemisphere, also known as the stoat or ermine, which develops a white coat in winter.
stoat or ermine, a small mustelid mammal

Examples

In winter, the Mustela erminea turns completely white except for the black tip of its tail.
The Mustela erminea is prized for its soft, white winter fur known as ermine.
Scientists studied the hunting behavior of Mustela erminea in Alaska's forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ermine - alternative common name for Mustela erminea, especially in its white coat

Etymology

Modern Latin scientific name; 'mustela' means 'weasel' in Latin.
