Cattleya citrina

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætliə sɪˈtraɪnə / (AmE), / ˌkatlɪə sɪˈtrɪnə / (BrE)
A species of Mexican epiphytic orchid with gray-green leaves and partially opened yellow flowers, sometimes classified under the genus Cattleya.
Mexican epiphytic orchid species

Examples

The fragrant yellow blossoms of the Cattleya citrina make it a prized specimen for orchid collectors.
My greenhouse is home to a beautiful Cattleya citrina in full bloom.
Botanists debate whether Cattleya citrina should remain in the genus Cattleya or be placed elsewhere.

Etymology

Cattleya: after William Cattley, English horticulturist; citrina: from Latin 'citrinus' (lemon-colored).
