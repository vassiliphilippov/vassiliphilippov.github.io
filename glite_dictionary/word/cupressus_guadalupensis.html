Cupressus guadalupensis

1. PROPN

/ kuːˈprɛsəs ˌɡwɑːdəluˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs ˌɡwædəluːˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of cypress tree native to Guadalupe Island, cultivated for its bluish foliage
a cypress tree species native to Guadalupe Island

Examples

The Cupressus guadalupensis is prized for its attractive bluish leaves in ornamental gardens.
Researchers studied the Cupressus guadalupensis population on Guadalupe Island to understand its adaptation to harsh conditions.
Gardeners often choose Cupressus guadalupensis for landscaping projects due to its wide-spreading habit.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cupressus' (cypress) + 'guadalupensis' (of Guadalupe Island, Mexico).
