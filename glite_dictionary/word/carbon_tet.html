carbon tet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən tɛt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən tɛt / (BrE)
a colorless, nonflammable toxic liquid formerly used as a solvent and cleaning agent, now rarely used due to health risks
toxic liquid solvent once used in cleaning

Examples

The laboratory stopped using carbon tet due to its hazardous effects on health.
In the past, carbon tet was a common solvent for cleaning machinery.
Fire extinguishers once contained carbon tet, but it's now considered too dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon tetrachloride - the chemical compound CCl4, a colorless, nonflammable liquid

Etymology

Short for 'carbon tetrachloride,' from Latin carbo (coal) and Greek tetra (four) + chloride.
