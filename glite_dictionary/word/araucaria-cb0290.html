Araucaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈkɛriə / (AmE), / ˌarɔːˈkɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the family Araucariaceae, native to the southern hemisphere and including species such as the monkey puzzle tree.
genus of evergreen coniferous trees

Examples

The Araucaria genus includes the iconic monkey puzzle tree found in South America.
Many ancient forests once contained various species of Araucaria.
Botanists studied the distribution of Araucaria across different continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkey puzzle tree - a species of Araucaria, native to Chile and Argentina

Etymology

From Spanish *araucaria*, after the Araucanian (Mapuche) people of Chile, where some species are native.
