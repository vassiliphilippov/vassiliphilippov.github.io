leaflet

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːflət / (AmE), / ˈliːflɪt / (BrE)
a single segment or division of a compound leaf in plants; not an entire leaf but part of one that shares a stalk with others
one part of a compound leaf (botany)

Examples

Each leaflet on the fern is intricately shaped and contributes to the overall beauty of the plant.
The scientist explained that a compound leaf consists of several leaflets attached to a single central stem.
When identifying the tree, look closely at how many leaflets make up each compound leaf.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: leaf - the flat, typically green structure of a plant; the whole, not the part

Etymology

From leaf + diminutive suffix -let, indicating something smaller or a part of a whole.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːflət / (AmE), / ˈliːflɪt / (BrE)
a small printed sheet, usually folded, used for giving information or advertising, often having a paper cover
small informational booklet or pamphlet

Examples

The charity distributed thousands of leaflets to raise awareness of the campaign.
Please read the leaflet before taking any medication.
A leaflet was included in the mail to advertise the new restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pamphlet - a small booklet or brochure containing information or arguments

Etymology

From 'leaf' + diminutive suffix '-let', indicating a small leaf or page.

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːflət / (AmE), / ˈliːflət / (BrE)
a thin segment or flap of a heart valve that helps control the flow of blood through the heart
flap of a heart valve
medical

Examples

A damaged leaflet can interfere with the proper functioning of the mitral valve.
Surgeons repaired the torn leaflet to restore normal blood flow in the patient.
Calcification may cause a heart valve leaflet to become stiff and less flexible.

Etymology

From leaf + -let (diminutive suffix), originally referring to a small leaf; later extended to anatomical structures resembling small leaves.

Source: WordNet
