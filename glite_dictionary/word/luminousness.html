luminousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.mə.nəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈluː.mɪ.nəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of emitting or reflecting light; brightness or radiance
quality of emitting or reflecting light

Examples

The luminousness of the painting made it stand out in the dimly lit gallery.
Scientists studied the luminousness of different stars to understand their properties.
Her dress had a subtle luminousness that caught the eye under evening lights.

Synset Examples

its luminosity is measured relative to that of our sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminosity - the quality of being luminous; brightness, especially as an objectively measured physical property
  • Synonym: brightness - the quality or state of giving out or reflecting light
  • Antonym: darkness - the absence or lack of light

Etymology

From Latin 'lūminōsus' (full of light) + English '-ness'.
