Stentor

1. PROPN

/ ˈstɛntɔr / (AmE), / ˈstɛntɔː / (BrE)
a mythical Greek warrior famed for his unusually loud voice, who died after losing a shouting contest with Hermes
mythical Greek warrior with a loud voice

Examples

As loud as Stentor, his voice echoed through the hall.
The general addressed the troops with a Stentor-like bellow.
She was described as having the power of Stentor when giving speeches.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Στέντωρ (Sténtōr), a herald in the Trojan War in Greek mythology.
