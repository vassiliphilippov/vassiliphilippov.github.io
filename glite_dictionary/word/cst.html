CST

1. NOUN

/ si ɛs ti / (AmE), / si ɛs ti / (BrE)
abbreviation for Central Standard Time, the time zone observed in the central region of the United States, which is six hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC−6).
Central Standard Time (US time zone)

Examples

The meeting is scheduled for 3 p.m. CST, so please adjust your calendars accordingly.
Flights departing from Chicago usually list times in CST during the winter months.
When it's noon in CST, it's already 1 p.m. in the Eastern Time zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Central Time - time observed in the central region of the US (includes both CST and CDT)
  • Degree Higher: EST - Eastern Standard Time, one time zone east of CST (UTC−5)
  • Degree Lower: MST - Mountain Standard Time, one time zone west of CST (UTC−7)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Central Standard Time.'
