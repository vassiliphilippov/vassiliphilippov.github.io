Pluteus magnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpluːti.əs ˈmæɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpluːti.əs ˈmæɡ.nəs / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with black, coarsely wrinkled caps, typically found growing on piles of hardwood sawdust.
species of edible mushroom

Examples

After the rain, several Pluteus magnus appeared on the pile of old sawdust in the garden.
Some foragers seek out Pluteus magnus because of its edible qualities and distinctive wrinkled cap.
The field guide included photographs and descriptions to help identify Pluteus magnus in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pluteus - A genus of fungi commonly known as shield mushrooms

Etymology

From Latin 'Pluteus' (the genus name) and 'magnus' meaning 'large' or 'great'.
