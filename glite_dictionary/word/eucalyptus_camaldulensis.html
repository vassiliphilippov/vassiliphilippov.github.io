Eucalyptus camaldulensis

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkɑːlɪptəs kəˌmældəˈlɛnsɪs / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs kəˌmældəˈlɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of red gum tree native to Australia, chiefly growing along rivers, known for its somewhat crooked shape and durable reddish timber used in heavy construction.
Australian river red gum tree; source of reddish timber

Examples

The Eucalyptus camaldulensis is commonly found along riverbanks throughout Australia.
The reddish timber of the Eucalyptus camaldulensis is valued for its strength and durability.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Eucalyptus camaldulensis in different soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: river red gum - common name for Eucalyptus camaldulensis

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Eucalyptus + species name camaldulensis, after the Camalduli monastery gardens in Italy where the type was grown.
