Vicia

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃiə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the legume family (Fabaceae), including annual or perennial and often climbing herbs, commonly known as vetches.
genus of vetches (flowering plants)

Examples

The species within Vicia are important as fodder crops for livestock.
Botanists are studying the genetic diversity in Vicia to improve agricultural yields.
Some varieties of Vicia can fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a common name for plants of the genus Vicia

Etymology

From Latin 'vicia', meaning 'vetch'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃi.ə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the legume family, commonly known as vetches, found worldwide as annual or perennial, often climbing herbs.
genus of vetches (herbaceous plants)

Examples

Several species in the Vicia genus are widely grown as cover crops to replenish soil nutrients.
Farmers often plant Vicia for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
The flowers of most Vicia species are small and usually purple or white.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a common name for plants of the genus Vicia

Etymology

From Latin *vicia* (vetch, a type of legume).
