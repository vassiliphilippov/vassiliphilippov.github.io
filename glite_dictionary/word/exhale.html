to exhale

1. VERB

/ ɛksˈheɪl / (AmE), / ɛksˈheɪl / (BrE)
to breathe out air from the lungs through the mouth or nose
to breathe out

Examples

She took a deep breath and slowly exhaled through her mouth.
Remember to exhale as you lower your arms during the exercise.
After holding his breath, he finally exhaled and felt relaxed.

Synset Examples

Exhale when you lift the weight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inhale - to breathe in air through the mouth or nose

Etymology

From Latin exhalare, from ex- (out) + halare (to breathe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00004227-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɛkˈheɪl / (AmE), / ɛksˈheɪl / (BrE)
to breathe out air or emit a substance, such as smoke, vapor, or an odor, from the lungs or a source
breathe out; emit air or vapor

Examples

She took a deep breath and slowly exhaled, trying to calm her nerves.
The factory chimneys exhale smoke into the air every morning.
After running up the stairs, he exhaled heavily, catching his breath.

Synset Examples

The chimney exhales a thick smoke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inhale - to breathe in air or other substances into the lungs
  • Synonym: to emit - to send out or give off, especially gas or heat

Etymology

From Latin exhalare (to breathe out), from ex- (out) + halare (to breathe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00004605-v
Synset POS: v
