omniscience

1. NOUN

/ ɑmˈnɪʃəns / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪsɪəns / (BrE)
the state of having complete or unlimited knowledge; knowing everything
the state of knowing everything

Examples

Many religions attribute omniscience to their gods or deities.
The author writes with a tone of omniscience, revealing every character's thoughts.
His claim to omniscience seemed arrogant and unbelievable among his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omniscient - having complete or unlimited knowledge
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Latin 'omnis' (all) and 'scientia' (knowledge).
