erratic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrædɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈratɪk / (BrE)
not even or regular in movement; having no fixed course or pattern.
not regular; having no fixed course

Examples

The car's erratic movement on the road made the police pull it over.
Her attendance at work has been erratic lately, causing concern among her colleagues.
Meteorologists warned of erratic weather patterns throughout the summer.

Synset Examples

an erratic comet
his life followed a wandering course
a planetary vagabond

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regular - happening at fixed times or places, not erratic

Etymology

From Latin 'erraticus' (wandering), from 'errare' (to stray, wander).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈrædɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈratɪk / (BrE)
liable to sudden, unpredictable changes; not consistent or regular
sudden or unpredictable change

Examples

Her erratic driving made the passengers nervous during the storm.
The director is known for his erratic decisions, changing his mind without warning.
Stock prices often show erratic movements during times of uncertainty.

Synset Examples

erratic behavior
fickle weather
mercurial twists of temperament
a quicksilver character, cool and willful at one moment, utterly fragile the next

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fickle - liable to sudden unpredictable change, especially regarding affections or attachments
  • Antonym: consistent - always behaving or happening in the same way

Etymology

From Latin 'erraticus' (wandering), from 'errare' (to wander, err).

3. ADJ

/ əˈrætɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈratɪk / (BrE)
Likely to change or behave in an unpredictable or inconsistent way.
unpredictable or inconsistent

Examples

The team's erratic performance this season made it hard for fans to stay hopeful.
Sudden, erratic movements of the stock market worried investors.
Her erratic schedule made it difficult for her friends to make plans.

Synset Examples

erratic winds are the bane of a sailor
a temperamental motor: sometimes it would start and sometimes it wouldn't
that beautiful but temperamental instrument the flute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpredictable - impossible to know in advance; not behaving in a regular way
  • Antonym: consistent - always behaving in the same way; opposite of erratic

Etymology

From Latin erraticus (wandering), from errare (to stray, wander).
