jut

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌt / (AmE), / dʒʌt / (BrE)
an outward projection or the act of extending out from a surface
outward projection; act of sticking out

Examples

The rocky jut above the sea formed a perfect place to stand and admire the view.
From the wall, a small wooden jut held a faded lantern.
The architect designed a dramatic jut in the building’s façade to create visual interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - something that extends out from a surface
  • Antonym: recession - the act of receding or moving back

Etymology

From Middle English 'jutten', possibly from Old French 'juter' (to push, butt).

2. VERB

/ dʒʌt / (AmE), / dʒʌt / (BrE)
(of a part of something) to extend out, project, or stick out beyond the main line or surface.
extend out or project

Examples

The balcony seemed to jut over the street below.
Sharp rocks jut from the edge of the mountain path.
A pair of branches jutted out from the old tree trunk.

Synset Examples

His sharp nose jutted out
A single rock sticks out from the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: project - to extend outward beyond a surface or line
  • Antonym: to recede - to move back or withdraw

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'jowten' (to jolt, thrust).

Synset POS: v
