monosaccharose

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˈsækəˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈsækəˌrəʊs / (BrE)
a simple sugar that cannot be broken down into simpler sugars, representing the most basic form of carbohydrate
simple sugar; basic carbohydrate unit

Examples

Glucose is a common example of a monosaccharose found in nature.
The basic unit of many polysaccharides is a monosaccharose molecule.
Scientists study monosaccharose structures to understand carbohydrate metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monosaccharide - a sugar molecule consisting of a single saccharide unit; simplest carbohydrate

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + French 'saccharose' (sugar).
