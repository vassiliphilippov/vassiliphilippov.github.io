quantum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.təm / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm / (BrE)
a discrete or specific amount of something, especially used to refer to the smallest possible unit in physics or by analogy in other contexts
a discrete quantity or amount

Examples

Each quantum of energy released by the atom is absorbed by the detector.
The court awarded a quantum of damages to compensate for the losses.
Scientists studied the quanta of light produced in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amount - a quantity of something
  • Synonym: portion - a part or share of a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'quantum' (how much, as much as), neuter of 'quantus' (how great).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.təm / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm / (BrE)
the smallest discrete quantity of a physical property that a system can possess, as defined in quantum theory
smallest unit in quantum theory

Examples

In physics, energy is often transferred in discrete quantum units known as photons.
The quantum of action in quantum mechanics is represented by Planck’s constant.
Scientists discovered that light behaves as both a wave and a collection of quantum particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quanta - plural form of quantum

Etymology

From Latin quantum (how much, amount), neuter of quantus (how great).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
