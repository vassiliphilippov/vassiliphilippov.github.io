resuscitator

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsʌsɪˌteɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈsʌsɪteɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to assist or restore breathing by forcing air or oxygen into the lungs of a person who is not breathing or is breathing inadequately
device for restoring breathing
medical

Examples

The paramedics quickly used a resuscitator to help the unconscious man breathe.
Every ambulance is equipped with a portable resuscitator for emergencies.
Doctors checked the resuscitator to ensure it was functioning properly before the surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilator - machine that supports or replaces breathing

Etymology

From Latin 'resuscitare' (to revive) + '-or' (agent suffix).
