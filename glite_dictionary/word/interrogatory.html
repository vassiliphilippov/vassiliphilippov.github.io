interrogatory

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tər.i / (BrE)
Relating to questioning or having the characteristics of an interrogation.
relating to questioning or interrogation

Examples

The detective adopted an interrogatory tone during the interview.
Her interrogatory look made him feel uncomfortable about his alibi.
The lawyer's interrogatory methods helped uncover the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: questioning - the action of asking questions

Etymology

From Latin 'interrogatorius', from 'interrogare' meaning 'to ask'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tər.i / (BrE)
a formal set of written questions submitted to a party in a legal case to be answered under oath
legal written questions for a party
legal

Examples

The defendant's attorney prepared a detailed interrogatory to gather more information about the case.
Each party was required to respond to the interrogatories within thirty days as part of the discovery process.
Failure to answer an interrogatory can result in penalties or sanctions from the court.

Etymology

From Late Latin interrogatorius (of questioning), from Latin interrogare (to ask, question).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07208618-n
Synset POS: n
