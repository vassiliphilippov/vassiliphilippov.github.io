masochistically

1. ADV

/ ˌmæsəˈkɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmasəˈkɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that derives pleasure from pain, suffering, or humiliation, or by seeking such experiences for oneself.
in a masochistic manner

Examples

He masochistically accepted every difficult assignment the manager gave him.
Some athletes push their bodies masochistically beyond healthy limits.
She masochistically read all the negative comments about her work.

Synset Examples

masochistically he insisted on an even greater workload

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masochistic - deriving pleasure from one's own pain or humiliation
  • Antonym: sadistically - in a manner that derives pleasure from inflicting pain on others

Etymology

From 'masochistic' (from the name of Leopold von Sacher-Masoch, 19th-century Austrian novelist) plus '-ally', adverbial suffix.
