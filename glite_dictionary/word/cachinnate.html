to cachinnate

1. VERB

/ ˈkæk.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæk.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to laugh loudly and in an unrestrained way
laugh loudly and unrestrainedly

Examples

At the comedian's joke, the audience began to cachinnate uncontrollably.
He tends to cachinnate at even the slightest bit of humor, startling those around him.
She cachinnated so loudly that people in the next room could hear her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously
  • Degree Lower: giggle - to laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way

Etymology

From Latin cachinnatus, past participle of cachinnare (to laugh loudly).
