exabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛksəbʌɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,024 petabytes or 2^60 bytes, commonly used to measure data storage capacity
unit of digital information (2^60 bytes)

Examples

The company now handles more than one exabyte of data each month.
Storing an exabyte of information would require hundreds of thousands of high-capacity hard drives.
Researchers estimate that the world's total digital data surpassed an exabyte several years ago.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,024 terabytes or 2^50 bytes
  • Degree Higher: zettabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,024 exabytes or 2^70 bytes

Etymology

From 'exa-', a metric prefix for 10^18, and 'byte', a unit of digital information.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.sə.baɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛks.ə.baɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,000 petabytes, or 10^18 bytes.
unit of digital information (10^18 bytes)

Examples

The new data center can store several exabytes of information.
Internet traffic is expected to reach over one exabyte per day by next year.
An exabyte is an unimaginably large amount of digital storage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petabyte - a unit of information equal to 10^15 bytes
  • Degree Higher: zettabyte - a unit of information equal to 10^21 bytes

Etymology

From 'exa-' (denoting a factor of 10^18, after Greek 'hexa' meaning six) + 'byte'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13652498-n
Synset POS: n
