encircled

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɜrkəld / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːkəld / (BrE)
Surrounded or closed in on all sides; confined within a boundary or by something surrounding.
surrounded on all sides

Examples

The army was encircled by enemy forces and cut off from supplies.
A small cluster of houses lay encircled by dense forest.
She felt completely encircled by the demands of her new job.

Synset Examples

a camp surrounded by enemies
the encircled pioneers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrounded - Completely enclosed or encircled by something or someone

Etymology

From the verb 'encircle', based on 'en-' (to make) + 'circle'.
