to cocainise

1. VERB

/ koʊˈkeɪnaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈkeɪnaɪz / (BrE)
To administer cocaine as an anesthetic, especially in medical contexts.
use cocaine as an anesthetic
medical british

Examples

In the late nineteenth century, doctors would cocainise areas of the body before surgery.
The surgeon cocainised the patient's nasal passages to numb the area before the procedure.
Medical texts from the era describe how physicians routinely cocainised wounds for pain management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anesthetize - to administer an anesthetic

Etymology

Formed by combining 'cocaine' and the suffix '-ise', modeled after other verbs like 'anesthetise'.
