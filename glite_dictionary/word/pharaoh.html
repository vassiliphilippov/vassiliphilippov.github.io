pharaoh

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfeə.rəʊ / (BrE)
the title of the ancient rulers of Egypt, considered to be both political and religious leaders
ancient Egyptian king

Examples

The ancient pharaoh was believed to be a living god by the Egyptians.
Archaeologists have discovered the tomb of a little-known pharaoh in the Valley of the Kings.
Cleopatra was the last active pharaoh of ancient Egypt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king - male sovereign ruler of a nation or territory
  • Antonym: queen - female sovereign ruler or wife of a king

Etymology

From ancient Greek 'pharaō', from Hebrew 'par‘ōh', from Egyptian 'pr-ꜥꜣ' (great house).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɛə.rəʊ / (BrE)
The title of the monarchs of ancient Egypt, considered to be both political and religious leaders.
ancient Egyptian king

Examples

The mummy of the famous pharaoh Tutankhamun was discovered in 1922.
During his reign, the pharaoh commissioned the construction of many grand temples.
Stories about the power and wealth of Egypt's pharaohs have fascinated historians for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king - male monarch of a major territorial unit
  • Antonym: queen - female monarch of a major territorial unit

Etymology

From Egyptian 'pr-ʿo' (great house), via Greek 'pharaō' and Hebrew 'par‘ōh'.
