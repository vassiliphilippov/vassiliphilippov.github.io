chock-full

1. ADJ

/ tʃɑk ˈfʊl / (AmE), / tʃɒk ˈfʊl / (BrE)
Completely full; packed to capacity.
completely full or packed

Examples

The display case was chock-full of colorful pastries and cakes.
Her suitcase was chock-full after she packed all her new clothes.
During the sale, the store became chock-full of eager shoppers.

Synset Examples

chowder chockablock with pieces of fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full - Containing as much as possible; having no empty space.
  • Antonym: empty - Containing nothing; not filled.

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English chokkeful, with 'chock' meaning 'block, lump'.
