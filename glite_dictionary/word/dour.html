dour

1. ADJ

/ daʊər / (AmE), / dʊə, daʊə / (BrE)
Severe, unfriendly, or stern in manner or appearance; harshly uninviting.
stern or unfriendly in manner or appearance

Examples

The manager's dour demeanor intimidated everyone at the meeting.
Her dour expression softened when she saw the puppy.
The castle looked especially dour under the stormy gray clouds.

Synset Examples

a dour, self-sacrificing life
a forbidding scowl
a grim man loving duty more than humanity
undoubtedly the grimmest part of him was his iron claw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stern - serious and unrelenting in attitude or character
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Scottish and northern English, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Middle English 'dour' (hard),

2. ADJ

/ daʊr / (AmE), / ˈdʊə ˈdaʊə / (BrE)
Showing a stern and persistent determination; stubbornly unyielding in attitude or action.
sternly determined; unyielding

Examples

Her dour refusal to negotiate surprised everyone in the room.
Despite countless setbacks, his dour commitment to the cause never wavered.
The dour judge was unmoved by their emotional pleas for mercy.

Synset Examples

dogged persistence
dour determination
the most vocal and pertinacious of all the critics
a mind not gifted to discover truth but tenacious to hold it
men tenacious of opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenacious - holding firmly to something; persistent
  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's attitude or position
  • Antonym: yielding - inclined to give in; not stubborn or firm

Etymology

From Latin durus (hard, harsh) via Old French dur (hard).

3. ADJ

/ daʊr / (AmE), / daʊə / (BrE)
stern and unsmiling; showing a gloomy or sullen mood, especially in a persistent or unfriendly way.
gloomy and unfriendly; stern

Examples

His dour expression made everyone in the room feel uncomfortable.
Despite the celebration, she remained dour and withdrawn.
The coach was known for his dour demeanor, rarely smiling even after a win.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: morose - sullen and ill-tempered
  • Synonym: sullen - bad-tempered and sulky; gloomy

Etymology

From Scottish and Northern English, from Middle English 'dour', from Latin 'durus' (hard, harsh).
