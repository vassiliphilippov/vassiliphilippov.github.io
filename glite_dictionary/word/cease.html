to cease

1. VERB

/ sis / (AmE), / siːs / (BrE)
to bring or come to an end; to stop something from continuing
bring or come to an end

Examples

We were asked to cease all activity immediately during the inspection.
Once the hostilities ceased, peace was finally restored to the region.
If the noise does not cease soon, I will file a complaint.

Synset Examples

Quit teasing your little brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to bring to an end or halt; to discontinue
  • Antonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process; to keep going

Etymology

From Old French 'cesser', from Latin 'cessare' (to delay, to stop).

2. VERB

/ sis / (AmE), / siːs / (BrE)
to come to an end; to stop happening or existing, either in time, space, or extent
to come to an end; stop

Examples

The rain finally ceased after two hours of thunder and lightning.
All communication between the two companies ceased abruptly last year.
At midnight, the music will cease so the neighbors are not disturbed.

Synset Examples

the bronchioles terminate in a capillary bed
Your rights stop where you infringe upon the rights of other
My property ends by the bushes
The symphony ends in a pianissimo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to bring or come to an end; to halt
  • Antonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process; to last

Etymology

From Old French *cesser*, from Latin *cessare* (to delay, to cease), frequentative of *cedere* (to go, yield).

Synset POS: v
