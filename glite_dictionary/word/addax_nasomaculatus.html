Addax nasomaculatus

1. PROPN

/ ˈæd.æks ˌneɪ.soʊ.mæˈkjuː.lə.təs / (AmE), / ˈæd.aks ˌneɪ.səʊ.mak.juːˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
a large, light-colored antelope native to the desert regions of northern Africa, known for its long, slightly spiraled horns
large desert antelope of northern Africa

Examples

The Addax nasomaculatus is critically endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.
Researchers have observed the distinctive horns of Addax nasomaculatus in the Sahara desert.
Conservation efforts focus on reintroducing Addax nasomaculatus into protected wildlife reserves.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Addax (from Arabic 'addax', a wild animal) and species name 'nasomaculatus', meaning 'spotted nose'.
