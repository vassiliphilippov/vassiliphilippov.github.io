desolately

1. ADV

/ ˈdɛsələtli / (AmE), / ˈdɛsələtli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows great loneliness or abandonment; in a way that is grief-stricken and comfortless.
in a lonely or abandoned manner

Examples

She gazed desolately out the window at the empty street below.
After his friends left, he wandered desolately through the park, feeling utterly alone.
The house stood desolately on the windswept moor, its windows dark and empty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopelessly - in a way that shows no hope
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy and optimistic manner

Etymology

From 'desolate' + '-ly'; 'desolate' from Latin 'desolatus' meaning 'left alone, abandoned'.
