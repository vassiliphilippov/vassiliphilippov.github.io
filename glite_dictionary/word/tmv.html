TMV

1. NOUN

/ ti ɛm vi / (AmE), / tiː em viː / (BrE)
abbreviation for tobacco mosaic virus, a widely studied plant virus that infects tobacco and other plants, notable as the first virus ever discovered.
tobacco mosaic virus (plant virus)
medical

Examples

Scientists have used TMV to study viral infection in plants for over a century.
The symptoms of TMV include mottled leaves and reduced crop yields.
Because TMV was the first virus discovered, it is a key part of virology history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco mosaic virus - virus that infects tobacco and other plants, causing mosaic-like discoloration

Etymology

Abbreviation of tobacco mosaic virus.
