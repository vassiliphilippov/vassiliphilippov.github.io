precise

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈsaɪs / (AmE), / prɪˈsaɪs / (BrE)
(of ideas, images, expressions, etc.) exact and accurate; strictly correct or true in every detail.
exact and strictly accurate

Examples

The scientist presented a precise measurement of the chemical's mass.
Her description of the crime scene was remarkably precise and detailed.
The architect made a precise drawing to guide the construction team.

Synset Examples

a precise image
a precise measurement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exact - completely correct in every detail
  • Synonym: accurate - correct and without any mistakes
  • Antonym: imprecise - not exact, not strictly accurate

Etymology

From Latin 'precisus', meaning 'cut off, concise'.

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈsaɪs / (AmE), / prɪˈsaɪs / (BrE)
exact and accurate in every detail; clearly defined or delimited.
exact; accurate; clearly defined

Examples

She gave a precise description of the suspect to the police.
It is important to use precise measurements when baking a cake.
He arrived at the precise time we agreed upon.

Synset Examples

a precise mind
specified a precise amount
arrived at the precise moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exact - completely correct and without any mistakes
  • Synonym: accurate - correct and without error
  • Antonym: imprecise - not exact or accurate
  • Antonym: approximate - close to the exact, but not exact

Etymology

From Latin 'precīsus' (cut off, strict, exact), from 'praecīdere' (to cut off).
