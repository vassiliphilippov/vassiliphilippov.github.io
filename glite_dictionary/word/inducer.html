inducer

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdusər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a person who tries to persuade or lead someone to take a particular action or adopt a particular opinion
someone who persuades or leads others

Examples

As the chief inducer of the plan, she convinced the others to participate.
The inducer presented compelling arguments to change public opinion.
He acted as an inducer when he urged his friends to take up the challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuader - a person who convinces others to do or believe something

Etymology

From Latin inducere (to lead in, bring in), via Old French inducer and Middle English induce + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdusər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that initiates or increases the expression of specific genes, especially in a biological system
agent activating specific genes
medical

Examples

The presence of an inducer in the medium caused the bacteria to produce the required enzyme.
Scientists added a chemical inducer to trigger gene expression in the experimental cells.
Some drugs act as an inducer of detoxifying enzymes in the liver.

Etymology

From Latin inducere (to lead in).
