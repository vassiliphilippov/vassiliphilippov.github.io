marjoram

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒərəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒərəm / (BrE)
An aromatic Eurasian perennial herb of the mint family, with small white or purple flowers, used as a culinary seasoning.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh marjoram over the roasted vegetables to enhance their flavor.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of dried marjoram in the sauce.
A pot of marjoram thrives on the sunny windowsill in her kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - a related aromatic herb also known as wild marjoram

Etymology

From Old French majorane, from Medieval Latin maiorana.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒərəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒərəm / (BrE)
An aromatic herb of the mint family, with pungent leaves used as seasoning in cooking, especially with meats and in soups, stews, and omelets.
aromatic herb used as seasoning

Examples

I sprinkled fresh marjoram leaves over the roasted chicken for extra flavor.
Many Italian recipes call for a pinch of dried marjoram to enhance the taste.
Herb gardeners often grow marjoram alongside basil and thyme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - herb closely related to marjoram, often used as seasoning

Etymology

From Old French 'majorane', from medieval Latin 'majorana'.
