sinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkə / (BrE)
(US, informal, historical) A small ring-shaped fried cake, similar to a doughnut.
ring-shaped fried cake; doughnut
informal american archaic

Examples

He ordered coffee and a sinker at the old-fashioned diner.
Back in the day, my grandfather used to enjoy a fresh sinker with his morning tea.
The bakery is famous for its homemade sinkers that are crisp on the outside and soft inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doughnut - a small fried cake of sweetened dough, typically ring-shaped

Etymology

Probably from 'sink' + -er, alluding to the heavy or dense nature of the cake.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkə / (BrE)
a weight used to make fishing lines or nets sink under water
weight for sinking fishing lines or nets

Examples

The fisherman attached a sinker to his line to ensure the bait reached the bottom of the lake.
Without a sinker, the fishing net would drift near the surface and miss the deeper fish.
She needed a heavier sinker because the current was strong and kept pulling the line away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: float - a buoyant device attached to fishing lines or nets to keep them afloat

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkə / (BrE)
A type of baseball pitch that drops sharply downward as it approaches home plate.
baseball pitch with sharp downward movement

Examples

The pitcher threw a sinker that fooled the batter completely.
His sinker is known for its late, sharp drop near the plate.
She struck out the last batter with a perfect sinker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastball - a pitch thrown at high speed with little curve
  • Degree Higher: curveball - a pitch that curves while approaching the batter

Etymology

From 'sink' + '-er', referring to the ball's downward motion.
