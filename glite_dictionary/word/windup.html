windup

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnd.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwʌɪnd.ʌp / (BrE)
a concluding part or action, especially at the end of an event, activity, or process
conclusion or final action

Examples

The meeting's windup included a summary of the main points and next steps.
At the windup of the ceremony, everyone applauded the organizers' efforts.
Her speech served as a perfect windup to the evening's festivities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusion - the ending or final part of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00211959-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪndˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌʌp / (BrE)
(of a toy or other device) operated by turning a key or similar mechanism to wind a spring inside.
operated by winding a mechanism

Examples

He gave his nephew a windup robot for his birthday.
The old-fashioned windup alarm clock rang loudly every morning.
One of her favorite childhood toys was a little windup duck that waddled across the floor.

Synset Examples

windup toys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clockwork - operated by a spring-driven mechanism

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'wind up', referring to turning a key or knob to operate a mechanism.
