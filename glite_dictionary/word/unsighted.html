unsighted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Unable to see; lacking the sense of sight.
unable to see; blind

Examples

The school offers special programs for unsighted children.
He devoted his career to developing tools for the unsighted.
An unsighted person may require alternative reading methods, such as Braille.

Synset Examples

a person is blind to the extent that he must devise alternative techniques to do efficiently those things he would do with sight if he had normal vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind - unable to see; lacking vision

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'sighted' (able to see).
