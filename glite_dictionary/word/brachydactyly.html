brachydactyly

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˈdæktəli / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈdæktɪli / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the fingers and toes are abnormally short due to unusually short bones.
abnormal shortness of fingers and toes
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with brachydactyly after noticing her unusually short fingers.
Brachydactyly can be inherited and often runs in families.
Although brachydactyly is rare, it is recognized as a distinct genetic condition.

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short) and 'daktylos' (finger).
