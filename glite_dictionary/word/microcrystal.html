microcrystal

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈkrɪs.təl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈkrɪs.təl / (BrE)
A crystal that is so small it can only be seen under a microscope.
crystal of microscopic size

Examples

Under the electron microscope, scientists observed several microcrystals embedded in the thin film.
The formation of microcrystals can affect the properties of certain pharmaceuticals.
To analyze the mineral sample, researchers isolated single microcrystals for X-ray diffraction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crystal - a solid material whose atoms are arranged in an ordered pattern

Etymology

From micro- (small) + crystal, indicating a small crystal.
