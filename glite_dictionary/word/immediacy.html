immediacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmidiəsi / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiəsi / (BrE)
a direct, intuitive awareness or experience of something without mediation or delay
direct intuitive awareness

Examples

The painting's vivid colors and brushstrokes give a sense of immediacy to the viewer.
In meditation, practitioners strive for the immediacy of presence, experiencing each moment as it comes.
Her poetry conveys the immediacy of emotion, making readers feel as if they are living the events themselves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mediation - the process of intervening or acting as a mediator

Etymology

From immediate + -cy, first recorded in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmidiəsi / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being direct and without an intervening or mediating agency
directness without mediation

Examples

The immediacy of live broadcasts brings events straight to viewers as they happen.
She valued the immediacy of email communication over traditional mail.
The artist's work is praised for its raw immediacy and emotional impact.

Synset Examples

the immediacy of television coverage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directness - the quality of being direct in manner or action

Etymology

From immediate + -cy, after Latin 'immediatus' (without anything between).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈmidiəsi / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiəsi / (BrE)
the quality of happening or being done without delay; quickness or directness of action or response
quick or direct occurrence or action

Examples

The immediacy of her reply showed she had been waiting for the call.
Social media adds a sense of immediacy to news reporting.
The immediacy of the emergency required everyone to act fast.

Synset Examples

the immediacy of their response
the instancy of modern communication

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promptness - the quality of acting quickly; synonym
  • Antonym: delay - a period of waiting before an action; antonym

Etymology

From immediate +‎ -cy; ultimately from Latin 'immediatus' (without anything between).
