wen

1. NOUN

/ wɛn / (AmE), / wɛn / (BrE)
a common cyst on the skin filled with fatty matter, usually resulting from a blocked sebaceous gland
sebaceous cyst on the skin
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the lump on her scalp as a wen and recommended minor surgery.
After months of ignoring it, his wen became swollen and started to ache.
A wen is usually harmless but can become infected if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyst - an abnormal, typically noncancerous, closed sac within the body
  • Degree Higher: boil - a painful, pus-filled bump under the skin caused by infected hair follicles

Etymology

From Old English 'wæn', meaning a swelling or tumor.
