quantic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.tɪk / (BrE)
a homogeneous polynomial involving two or more variables, each term having the same total degree
homogeneous polynomial in multiple variables

Examples

The professor demonstrated how to factor a complex quantic during the lecture.
Invariant theory often deals with the properties of a quantic under various transformations.
She spent hours calculating the roots of the third-degree quantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polynomial - an expression consisting of variables and coefficients involving only addition, subtraction, multiplication, and non-negative integer exponents

Etymology

From Latin 'quantus' (how much), modeled after 'quadratic' and similar mathematical terms.
