red-coated

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛd ˌkoʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌkəʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of a British soldier during the American Revolutionary War) wearing a red military coat; referring to British troops of that era.
wearing a red military coat (historical term)

Examples

The red-coated soldiers marched steadily across the battlefield.
Patriots devised tactics to surprise the red-coated British army in the woods.
In history books, red-coated troops are often portrayed in orderly lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redcoat - British soldier, especially during the American Revolutionary War, known for wearing a red coat
