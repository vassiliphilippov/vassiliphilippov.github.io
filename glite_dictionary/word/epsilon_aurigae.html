Epsilon Aurigae

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛp.sɪ.lɒn ɔːˈraɪ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛp.sɪ.lɒn ɔːˈrɪ.dʒi / (BrE)
a bright, variable star in the constellation Auriga, notable for its unusual eclipsing binary system
a bright variable star in Auriga

Examples

Epsilon Aurigae is famous among astronomers for the mysterious dimming in its light curve every 27 years.
Some researchers believe that the companion star to Epsilon Aurigae is obscured by a huge disk of dust.
Epsilon Aurigae is easily located with a telescope within the constellation Auriga.

Etymology

From Greek letter 'epsilon' (Ε), used to designate the fifth-brightest star in a constellation, and Auriga, a constellation name meaning 'charioteer' in Latin.
