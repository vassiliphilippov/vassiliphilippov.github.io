kishke

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪʃ.kə / (AmE), / ˈkɪʃ.kə / (BrE)
A traditional Jewish dish consisting of roasted intestines of fowl or beef, stuffed with a seasoned filling of matzo meal, suet, and spices.
stuffed intestines in Jewish cuisine

Examples

At the deli, I ordered a plate of kishke along with my corned beef sandwich.
Her grandmother taught her how to make homemade kishke for special occasions.
The Shabbat dinner featured traditional foods like kishke and chicken soup.

Etymology

From Yiddish 'kishke', meaning 'gut' or 'intestine', ultimately from Slavic roots.
