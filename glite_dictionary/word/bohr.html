Bohr

1. PROPN

/ bɔr / (AmE), / bɔː / (BrE)
Niels Bohr (1885–1962), a Danish physicist known for his pioneering contributions to the understanding of atomic structure and quantum theory.
Danish atomic physicist (1885–1962)

Examples

Bohr's model of the atom was a major breakthrough in physics.
Many scientific concepts are named after Bohr, including the Bohr radius.
Bohr received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1922.
