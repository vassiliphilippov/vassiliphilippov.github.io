arcus zygomaticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkəs ˌzaɪɡəˈmætɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːkəs ˌzaɪɡəˈmætɪkəs / (BrE)
the arch-shaped bony structure in the skull formed by the cheekbone (zygomatic bone) joining with the temporal bone
arch of bone in the cheek (skull anatomy)
medical

Examples

A fracture of the arcus zygomaticus can affect facial symmetry and jaw movement.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the arcus zygomaticus during the procedure.
In mammals, the arcus zygomaticus provides an important attachment point for the masseter muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zygomatic arch - the bony arch of the cheek formed by the zygomatic and temporal bones

Etymology

From Latin arcus (arch) + zygomaticus (relating to the yoke or cheekbone).
