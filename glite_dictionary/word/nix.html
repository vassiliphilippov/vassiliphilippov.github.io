to nix

1. VERB

/ nɪks / (AmE), / nɪks / (BrE)
to reject, refuse permission for, or put an end to (something)
reject or veto (informal verb)
informal

Examples

The manager decided to nix the proposed changes to the schedule.
They tried to get pizza for dinner, but their parents nixed the idea quickly.
If you don't like the plan, just nix it and suggest something else.

Synset Examples

I forbid you to call me late at night
Mother vetoed the trip to the chocolate store
Dad nixed our plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veto - to refuse to allow or approve something
  • Antonym: to approve - to accept or allow something officially

Etymology

From German dialect 'nix', meaning 'nothing', from Latin 'nihil'.

2. VERB

/ nɪks / (AmE), / nɪks / (BrE)
to reject or put an end to something; to prohibit or refuse.
reject or refuse
informal american

Examples

The manager decided to nix the proposed changes after hearing concerns from the staff.
She planned to nix any negative comments during the meeting to keep the discussion positive.
They voted to nix the idea before it even reached the approval stage.

Synset Examples

She planned to nix any negative comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • Antonym: to approve - to officially agree to or accept something

Etymology

Originally American English slang, from German 'nichts' meaning 'nothing'.
