delay

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
a period of time by which something is late or postponed; an interval in which action or progress is held up
period of waiting before something happens

Examples

The flight was cancelled after a two-hour delay.
Traffic accidents often cause long delays during rush hour.
We apologize for the delay in processing your application.

Synset Examples

instant replay caused too long a delay
he ordered a hold in the action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the act of delaying or putting off to a later time
  • Antonym: advance - action occurring sooner than scheduled; opposite of delay

Etymology

From Old French 'delaier', from Vulgar Latin *dilatare (to delay), related to Latin 'differre' (to defer).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
a period of waiting or inactivity that causes something to happen later than planned or expected
a period when something is postponed

Examples

The train was late due to a technical delay on the tracks.
There was a long delay before the meeting started because of the missing documents.
We apologize for the delay in responding to your request.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the action of delaying something to a later time
  • Antonym: promptness - taking place or being done without delay

Etymology

From Old French *delai*, from *delayer* (to postpone).
