Myricales

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪrɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌmɪrɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants, formerly including the family Myricaceae (bayberry family) and related families
order of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The Myricales have historically included various families such as Myricaceae and Juglandaceae.
Modern botanical classification rarely uses Myricales as an order, preferring other groupings.
She specialized in the ecology of Myricales during her graduate studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myricaceae - the bayberry family of flowering plants

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Myrica (a genus within Myricaceae) + -ales (denoting botanical orders).
