convincing

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɪnsɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈvɪnsɪŋ / (BrE)
able to make someone believe that something is true or real; persuasive.
persuasive; credible

Examples

Her convincing argument changed the minds of several people on the committee.
The special effects in the movie were so convincing that the audience forgot they were watching fiction.
He wore a convincing disguise that fooled even his closest friends.

Synset Examples

a convincing story
a convincing manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuasive - able to persuade someone to believe or do something
  • Antonym: unconvincing - not able to persuade or induce belief

Etymology

From the verb 'convince', which comes from Latin 'convincere' (to overcome, convince).
