excision

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
the act of removing part of a written text, especially as an editorial decision to shorten or alter it
removal of part of a text

Examples

The excision of several paragraphs improved the article's clarity.
After an excision, the report was much more concise and direct.
The author disagreed with the excision of key scenes from her manuscript.

Synset Examples

an editor's deletions frequently upset young authors
both parties agreed on the excision of the proposed clause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deletion - the removal of text in editing
  • Antonym: insertion - the addition of text in editing

Etymology

From Latin excisio (a cutting out), from excidere (to cut out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of cutting something out or removing it, especially by surgery or as a form of elimination
the act of cutting out or removal

Examples

The surgeon performed an excision of the tumor during the operation.
Early excision of damaged tissue can help prevent infection.
The law called for the excision of outdated statutes from the legal code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away
  • Antonym: insertion - the act of putting something in

Etymology

From Latin 'excidere' (to cut out), from 'ex-' (out) + 'caedere' (to cut).

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of formally expelling a member from a church, depriving them of membership and religious privileges
formal expulsion from church membership

Examples

After repeated warnings, the church resorted to excision to uphold its doctrines.
The process of excision was carried out with great solemnity in front of the congregation.
Historically, excision was used as a disciplinary measure against heretics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excommunication - official exclusion from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church

Etymology

From Latin excisio (a cutting out, removal), from excidere (to cut out).

4. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a body part, organ, or tissue
surgical removal of tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed an excision of the tumor to prevent its spread.
Scar tissue formed at the site of the excision after the minor operation.
Complete excision of the lesion was recommended by the dermatologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resection - the surgical removal of part of an organ or structure
  • Synonym: extraction - the act of removing something, especially by force or surgery
  • Antonym: incision - a surgical cut made in skin or flesh

Etymology

From Latin excisio meaning 'a cutting out', from excidere (to cut out).
