endemic

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, or custom) native to or restricted to a certain country or area.
native or restricted to a place

Examples

The koala is an endemic species to Australia and cannot be found in the wild elsewhere.
Certain diseases are endemic to tropical regions due to climate conditions.
Some folk traditions remain endemic to remote mountain villages despite modern influences.

Synset Examples

the autochthonal fauna of Australia includes the kangaroo
autochthonous rocks and people and folktales
endemic folkways
the Ainu are indigenous to the northernmost islands of Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigenous - originating or occurring naturally in a particular place
  • Antonym: exotic - not native; introduced from another place

Etymology

From Greek endēmos (native, dwelling in a place), from en- (in) + dēmos (people, country).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɛnˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) regularly found among particular people or in a certain area.
consistently present in a particular region (disease, condition)

Examples

Malaria was once endemic in many parts of southern Europe.
Poverty is endemic in some rural areas of the country, affecting generations of families.
Corruption remains endemic within certain sectors of the government.

Synset Examples

diseases endemic to the tropics
endemic malaria
food shortages and starvation are endemic in certain parts of the world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time
  • Antonym: pandemic - an epidemic occurring over a wide geographic area and affecting a large portion of the population

Etymology

From Greek endēmos, from en- (in) + dēmos (people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01430630-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
A disease or condition regularly found among particular people or in a certain area.
disease regularly present in an area
medical

Examples

Malaria remains an endemic in certain regions of Africa, affecting millions every year.
The yellow fever endemic has shaped public health policies in the affected country.
After several decades, the disease is now considered an endemic rather than an emerging threat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epidemic - A widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time.
  • Degree Higher: pandemic - An outbreak of a disease prevalent over a whole country or the world.

Etymology

From French endémique, from New Latin endēmicus, from Greek endēmos (native, in the population).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

(of a plant, animal, or disease) native or restricted to a particular region or environment.
native or confined to a region

Examples

The disease is endemic in certain tropical regions, making eradication challenging.
That particular plant is endemic to the coastal forests of California.

  • Antonym: exotic - introduced from another region; not native

Examples

Scientists are working to protect the rare endemic from extinction due to habitat loss.
Each rainforest has its own unique endemic that has adapted to its environment.

Related Terms
