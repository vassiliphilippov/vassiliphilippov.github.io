mod

1. ADJ

/ mɑd / (AmE), / mɒd / (BrE)
relating to a recently developed or fashionable style, especially in design, music, or culture; modern or contemporary in appearance or attitude.
relating to modern or fashionable style

Examples

Her apartment is decorated in a mod style, with bright colors and sleek furniture.
He wore a sharp mod suit to the party, standing out from everyone else.
Many young people in the 1960s identified with mod fashion and music.

Synset Examples

their offices are in a modern skyscraper
tables in modernistic designs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past
  • Antonym: old-fashioned - not modern; out of date in style or design

Etymology

Shortening of 'modern', popularized in the 1960s to refer to a subculture in Britain.

2. NOUN

/ mɑd / (AmE), / mɒd / (BrE)
(in the 1960s, Britain) a young person, especially a teenager, who belonged to a subculture characterized by stylish clothing, a love of modern jazz or pop music, and opposition to rockers.
youth subculture member in 1960s Britain
british

Examples

In the 1960s, being a mod meant dressing sharply and riding a scooter.
He still collects memorabilia from his days as a mod in London.
The mods and rockers often clashed at seaside resorts during bank holidays.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rocker - a member of a rival 1960s British youth subculture associated with motorcycles and leather jackets

Etymology

Shortening of 'modernist', originally referring to followers of modern jazz.
