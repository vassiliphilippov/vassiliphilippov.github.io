President

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
the elected head of state and chief executive of the United States government
head of the US government

Examples

During the State of the Union, the President addressed Congress and the nation.
The President signed the bill into law this afternoon.
Every four years, Americans elect their next President in a nationwide vote.

Synset Examples

the President likes to jog every morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commander-in-chief - the person who exercises supreme command and control over an armed force or military branch
  • Antonym: prime minister - the head of an elected government, usually in countries other than the US

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + sedere (to sit), originally meaning one who presides
