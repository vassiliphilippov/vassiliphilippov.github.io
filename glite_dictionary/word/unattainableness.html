unattainableness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.əˈteɪ.nə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈteɪ.nə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to achieve or reach.
impossibility of being attained

Examples

The unattainableness of peace seemed apparent during the height of the conflict.
For many, perfection has an air of unattainableness that makes it both alluring and frustrating.
The story explores the unattainableness of lost youth and innocence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unattainability - the state of being impossible to obtain or achieve
  • Antonym: attainableness - the quality of being able to be achieved or reached

Etymology

From un- (not) + attainable + -ness (forming nouns of quality or state).
