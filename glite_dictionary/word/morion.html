morion

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔriən / (AmE), / ˈmɒrɪən / (BrE)
a metal helmet with a crest or comb, worn by common soldiers in the 16th and early 17th centuries, especially in Europe.
16th-century metal helmet

Examples

The museum displayed a morion once worn by Spanish conquistadors.
In the reenactment, each soldier donned a morion to complete his costume.
The morion provided some protection against sword blows and arrows.

Etymology

From Spanish 'morrión' (helmet), ultimately from 'morra' (head).
