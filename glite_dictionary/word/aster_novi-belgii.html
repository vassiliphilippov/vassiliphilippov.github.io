Aster novi-belgii

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər ˌnoʊvaɪˈbɛlɡiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈastə ˌnəʊvɪˈbɛldʒii / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant of the aster family with small, autumn-blooming purple, pink, or white flowers, widely naturalized in Europe.
a North American perennial aster species

Examples

The Aster novi-belgii adds vibrant color to gardens in late autumn.
Many gardeners appreciate Aster novi-belgii for its long flowering season and resilience.
You can find Aster novi-belgii thriving along roadsides across Europe and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York aster - Common name for Aster novi-belgii

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Aster' and specific epithet 'novi-belgii' meaning 'of New Belgium' (an old name for New York).
