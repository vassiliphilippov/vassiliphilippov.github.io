punctum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌŋktəm / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktəm / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A point or small, distinct area, especially as a minute spot on a structure or organ.
anatomical point or small area
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist examined the lacrimal punctum for signs of blockage.
Each taste bud contains a tiny punctum sensitive to specific flavors.
During dissection, several blood vessels were visible at the punctum of insertion.

Etymology

From Latin 'punctum' meaning 'point, dot, spot'.
