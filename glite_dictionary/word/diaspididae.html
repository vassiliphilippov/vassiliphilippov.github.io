Diaspididae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæspɪˈdɪdi / (AmE), / dɪˌaspɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insects known as armored scale insects, which are pests of many plants due to their hard protective covering.
family of armored scale insects

Examples

Several agricultural crops are vulnerable to infestations by Diaspididae.
The Diaspididae are known for the hard armor that protects their bodies.
Researchers are working on methods to control Diaspididae populations in orchards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored scale - any of various small insects of the family Diaspididae with a hard waxy covering

Etymology

From New Latin Diaspididae, from the genus Diaspis.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌæspɪˈdiːdiː / (AmE), / daɪˌæspɪˈdiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, plant-feeding insects known as armored scales, characterized by hard, protective coverings.
family of armored scale insects

Examples

Many fruit trees are damaged by Diaspididae infestations each year.
Entomologists study Diaspididae to monitor pest populations in orchards.
The protective covering of Diaspididae makes them difficult to control with pesticides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored scale - any small insect of the family Diaspididae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on type genus Diaspis.
