to align

1. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to place or arrange (components or parts) into correct or proper position in relation to each other
arrange parts correctly

Examples

The mechanic will align the wheels to ensure the car drives straight.
Make sure you align the holes before inserting the screws.
He carefully aligned the picture frames on the wall.

Synset Examples

align the wheels of my car
ordinate similar parts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to alter or move something slightly in order to achieve the desired fit, appearance, or result
  • Antonym: to misalign - to place out of correct alignment or position

Etymology

From French 'aligner', from 'à ligne' (in line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466966-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to arrange or place something in a straight line or in correct relative positions
arrange in a straight line or correct position

Examples

Please align the chairs along the wall for the meeting.
He carefully aligned the picture frames so they would be at the same height.
The technician spent several minutes aligning the machine's components for optimal performance.

Synset Examples

align the car with the curb
align the sheets of paper on the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to straighten - to make or become straight
  • Antonym: to misalign - to place out of line or not correctly positioned

Etymology

From French *aligner* (to line up), from Latin *linea* (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00465234-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to arrange or position something so that it is in the correct or appropriate relationship with something else; to bring into agreement or alignment
arrange in line or bring into agreement

Examples

The team worked together to align the panels on the roof.
She tried to align her goals with the company's mission.
Before printing, make sure the text is properly aligned on the page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to alter or move something slightly in order to achieve the desired fit, appearance, or result

Etymology

From French 'aligner', from 'à' (to) + 'ligne' (line), from Latin 'linea' (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02664945-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to support or associate oneself with a particular group, cause, or way of thinking
associate or support a group/idea

Examples

She chose to align herself with the environmental movement for the upcoming election.
Many young voters align with progressive values on social issues.
After much debate, he finally decided to align with his colleagues on the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side - to take the side of a group or person in an argument
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist a group, cause, or idea

Etymology

From French 'aligner', from 'à' (to) + 'ligne' (line).
