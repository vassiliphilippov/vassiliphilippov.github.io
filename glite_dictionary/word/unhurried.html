unhurried

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɜr.id / (AmE), / ʌnˈhʌr.ɪd / (BrE)
calm and relaxed, not worried about being delayed or rushed
not rushed or agitated

Examples

She spoke in an unhurried tone, making everyone feel at ease.
Despite the tight schedule, his movements remained unhurried and deliberate.
The unhurried pace of village life attracted her to stay longer.

Synset Examples

was unhurried with the small children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaxed - calm and free from tension or anxiety
  • Antonym: rushed - feeling or acting with urgency or speed

Etymology

From un- (not) + hurried (past participle of 'hurry').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɜr.id / (AmE), / ʌnˈhʌr.id / (BrE)
relaxed and not rushed; not done in a hurry or with haste.
not rushed, relaxed

Examples

He spoke in an unhurried manner, carefully considering each word before he answered.
The unhurried pace of life in the countryside appeals to many city dwellers.
Their unhurried walk along the beach allowed them to enjoy the scenery.

Synset Examples

people strolling about in an unhurried way
an unhurried walk
spoke in a calm and unhurried voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisurely - acting or done without haste
  • Antonym: rushed - done hastily or with excessive speed

Etymology

from prefix un- (not) + hurried (past participle of hurry)
