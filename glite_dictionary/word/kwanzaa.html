Kwanzaa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.zə / (AmE), / ˈkwæn.zə / (BrE)
An annual festival celebrating African-American culture and heritage, observed from December 26 to January 1.
African-American cultural festival (Dec 26–Jan 1)

Examples

Many families light candles each night during Kwanzaa to honor its principles.
The community center held a Kwanzaa celebration with music, food, and storytelling.
She explained the meaning of Kwanzaa to her classmates during the holiday season.

Etymology

From the Swahili word 'kwanza' meaning 'first.'
