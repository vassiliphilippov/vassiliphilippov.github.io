legionnaire

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːdʒəˈnɛr / (AmE), / ˌliːdʒəˈneə / (BrE)
a soldier who is a member of a legion, especially the French Foreign Legion
member of a legion (especially French Foreign Legion)

Examples

He served as a legionnaire in the French Foreign Legion for five years.
The movie tells the story of a brave legionnaire posted in North Africa.
As a former legionnaire, he shared many stories about his military adventures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army

Etymology

From French légionnaire (member of a legion), from Latin legio (legion).
