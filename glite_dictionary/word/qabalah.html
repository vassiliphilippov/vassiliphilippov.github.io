qabalah

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
An esoteric or occult tradition resembling the Kabbalah, often involving mystical interpretations and traditionally kept secret.
esoteric tradition similar to Kabbalah

Examples

Some scholars study the qabalah to gain insight into mystical traditions beyond mainstream religion.
The secretive nature of the qabalah has intrigued occultists for centuries.
He attended a lecture on the qabalah to understand its influence on modern spirituality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kabbalah - A system of Jewish mysticism and esoteric teachings

Etymology

Variant transliteration of Hebrew קַבָּלָה (Kabbalah), meaning 'reception' or 'tradition'.
