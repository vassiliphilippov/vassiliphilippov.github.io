allantois

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæntoʊɪs / (AmE), / əˈlæntəʊɪs / (BrE)
a vascular fetal membrane formed from the hindgut in the embryos of reptiles, birds, and mammals, lying below the chorion and involved in gas exchange and waste removal
fetal membrane in vertebrate embryos
medical

Examples

The allantois plays a crucial role in the removal of waste products during embryonic development.
In mammals, the allantois helps form part of the umbilical cord.
Scientists observed abnormalities in the allantois that affected the embryo's growth.

Etymology

From Greek allas, allant- (sausage) + -ous, referring to the sausage-shaped structure.
