Phalaenoptilus nuttallii

1. PROPN

/ fəˌliːnɒpˈtɪləs nʌˈtæliː / (AmE), / fəˌliːnɒpˈtɪlɪəs nʌˈtalɪaɪ / (BrE)
A species of nightjar, commonly known as the common poorwill, found in western North America.
species of nightjar; common poorwill

Examples

The Phalaenoptilus nuttallii is famous for its ability to enter prolonged periods of torpor during cold weather.
Researchers observed a Phalaenoptilus nuttallii camouflaged among the desert rocks at dusk.
Unlike many other nocturnal birds, the Phalaenoptilus nuttallii can survive in extremely arid habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common poorwill - the common name for Phalaenoptilus nuttallii, a nightjar of western North America

Etymology

From Greek phalaina (moth) + ptilon (feathered/wing) and dedicated to Thomas Nuttall, English botanist and zoologist.
