linkman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋkˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkmən / (BrE)
(historical) A person whose job was to carry a torch and guide pedestrians through dark streets, especially before the widespread introduction of street lighting.
historical torchbearer for pedestrians
archaic

Examples

In eighteenth-century London, a linkman would guide citizens safely along unlit roads for a small fee.
The wealthy often hired a linkman to light their way home from evening events.
A linkman stood by the theatre entrance, his torch flickering in the chilly night air.

Etymology

From 'link', an old word for a torch, and 'man'.
