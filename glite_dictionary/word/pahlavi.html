Pahlavi

1. NOUN

/ pɑːˈlɑːvi / (AmE), / pɑːˈlɑːvi / (BrE)
the Middle Iranian language used chiefly in Zoroastrian literature from the 3rd to the 10th centuries CE
Middle Iranian language of Zoroastrian texts

Examples

Scholars study Pahlavi texts to better understand early Zoroastrian beliefs.
He translated an ancient Pahlavi manuscript into modern Persian.
The sacred fires are mentioned frequently in Pahlavi writings from the Sasanian era.

Etymology

From Persian پهلوی (Pahlavi), referring to Parthia (ancient region of Iran) and its language.

2. NOUN

/ pɑːˈlɑːvi / (AmE), / pɑːˈlɑːvi / (BrE)
the script, derived from the Aramaic alphabet, that was used to write Middle Persian (Pahlavi) language.
script used for the Pahlavi language

Examples

Scholars have studied ancient manuscripts written in Pahlavi to understand the Sassanian era.
The Pahlavi script played a crucial role in the development of Middle Persian literature.
Deciphering Pahlavi inscriptions requires specialized knowledge of both the script and the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aramaic alphabet - writing system from which Pahlavi script was derived
  • Synonym: Middle Persian - the language written using the Pahlavi script

Etymology

From Persian پهلوی (Pahlavi), referring both to a historical region and to the Middle Persian language and script.
