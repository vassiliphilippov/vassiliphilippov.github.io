asclepiad

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiˌæd / (AmE), / əˈskliːpɪad / (BrE)
Any plant of the family Asclepiadaceae, known for their milky sap and often showy flowers, including milkweeds.
plant of the Asclepiadaceae family

Examples

The garden features several rare asclepiad species among its collection of succulents.
Some members of the asclepiad family are used in traditional medicine for their unique properties.
Milkweed is perhaps the most familiar North American asclepiad due to its importance to monarch butterflies.

Etymology

From Latin Asclepiad-, Asclepiās, from Asclepius, a Greek god of medicine.
