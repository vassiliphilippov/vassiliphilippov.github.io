Limnodromus scolopaceus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪmnəˈdroʊməs ˌskoʊləˈpeɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌlɪmnəˈdrəʊməs ˌskɒləˈpeɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of North American wading bird, known as the long-billed dowitcher, characterized by its relatively long bill and reddish breast during breeding season.
long-billed dowitcher species of bird

Examples

The Limnodromus scolopaceus can be identified by its long bill and reddish underparts during the breeding season.
Birdwatchers spotted several Limnodromus scolopaceus near the marshlands in spring.
The migration patterns of Limnodromus scolopaceus cover vast distances across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowitcher - a group of wading birds including Limnodromus scolopaceus

Etymology

From Greek *limnē* (marsh), *dromos* (runner), and Latinized form of *scolopax* (snipe, woodcock), referring to the bird's wading habitat and shape.
