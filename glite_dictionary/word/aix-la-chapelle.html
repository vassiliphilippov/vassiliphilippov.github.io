Aix-la-Chapelle

1. PROPN

/ ɛks lɑ ʃəˈpɛl / (AmE), / ɛks la ʃaˈpɛl / (BrE)
a city in western Germany near the Dutch and Belgian borders, historically known as the northern capital of Charlemagne
city in western Germany, Charlemagne's northern capital

Examples

The cathedral in Aix-la-Chapelle is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Many tourists visit Aix-la-Chapelle to learn about its connection to Charlemagne.
The peace treaty was negotiated at Aix-la-Chapelle in the eighteenth century.

Etymology

From French 'Aix-la-Chapelle', meaning 'Aix at the chapel', the French name for the German city Aachen.
