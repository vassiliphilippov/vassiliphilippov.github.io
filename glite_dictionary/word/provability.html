provability

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.vəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.vəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be demonstrated or logically proved
the ability to be proved or demonstrated

Examples

The provability of the theory is still a subject of intense debate among logicians.
Mathematical logic is concerned with the provability of formal statements.
We questioned the provability of his claims without concrete evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provable - able to be proved or demonstrated
  • Antonym: unprovability - the quality of not being able to be proved

Etymology

From 'provable' + '-ity', based on Latin roots: 'probare' (to prove) and '-itas' (quality, state).
