Oestrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstrəs / (AmE), / ˈiːstrəs / (BrE)
A genus of botflies, especially known as sheep botflies, whose larvae are internal parasites of sheep.
genus of sheep botflies

Examples

The larvae of Oestrus can cause significant health issues in lambs.
Veterinarians study the life cycle of Oestrus to help prevent infections in sheep flocks.
Oestrus is best known for the species Oestrus ovis, the common sheep botfly.

Etymology

From Greek oistros meaning 'gadfly' or 'frenzy'.
