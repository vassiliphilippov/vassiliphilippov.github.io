oestrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstrəs / (AmE), / ˈiːstrəs / (BrE)
A recurring period of sexual receptiveness and fertility in many female nonhuman mammals, during which they exhibit heightened sexual arousal and are capable of conceiving.
period of fertility in female mammals

Examples

The female dog was in oestrus and attracted several males from the neighborhood.
Many breeders monitor livestock carefully to determine when they enter oestrus for optimal mating.
During oestrus, the cat's behavior changes noticeably, becoming more affectionate and vocal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrus - American English spelling of 'oestrus'; same biological phenomenon

Etymology

From Greek oistros (gadfly, frenzy).
