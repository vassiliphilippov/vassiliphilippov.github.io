tyrannicide

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈræn.əˌsaɪd / (AmE), / tɪˈran.ɪ.sʌɪd / (BrE)
The act of killing a tyrant or the person who kills a tyrant.
killing of a tyrant

Examples

The history book described the tyrannicide of Julius Caesar in great detail.
Debate continues over whether tyrannicide can ever be morally justified.
He was hailed as a tyrannicide after the dictator's death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassination - the act of murdering a prominent person for political reasons

Etymology

From Latin tyrannicida ('killer of a tyrant'), from Greek tyrannos ('tyrant') and Latin -cida ('killer').
