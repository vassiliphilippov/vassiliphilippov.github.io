Halictidae

1. NOUN

/ həˈlɪktɪˌdi / (AmE), / həˈlɪktɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, often metallic-colored bees; many species are important solitary or social pollinators in agriculture and wild ecosystems.
family of small bees, key pollinators
medical

Examples

Many species in the Halictidae family are essential for pollinating crops around the world.
Researchers have discovered several new Halictidae species in tropical forests.
Unlike honeybees, some Halictidae species show diverse social behavior, from solitary to eusocial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - insect belonging to the superfamily Apoidea

Etymology

From New Latin Halictidae, based on the genus Halictus.

2. NOUN

/ hæˌlɪk.tɪˈdiː / (AmE), / hæˌlɪk.tɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, usually solitary bees, many of which are important agricultural pollinators
family of small solitary bees

Examples

The Halictidae are often called sweat bees due to their attraction to perspiration.
Many crops depend on the Halictidae for effective pollination in the absence of honey bees.
Recent studies show a decline in several Halictidae species due to pesticide use.

Etymology

From New Latin Halictidae, based on Halictus (type genus) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).
