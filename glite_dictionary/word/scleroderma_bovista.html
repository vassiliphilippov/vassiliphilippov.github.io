Scleroderma bovista

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˌdɜrmə boʊˈvɪstə / (AmE), / sklɪərəˌdɜːmə bəʊˈvɪstə / (BrE)
A species of earthball fungus with a firm, dry, smooth outer layer when young, developing cracks as it matures; it is pale orange-yellow when young and turns reddish brown at maturity.
a species of earthball fungus
medical

Examples

Scleroderma bovista is commonly found in sandy soils during the autumn months.
The outer skin of Scleroderma bovista cracks as the fungus matures, revealing the spore mass inside.
Mycologists warn that Scleroderma bovista should not be consumed due to potential toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthball - a type of fungus with a tough outer layer, related to puffballs

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'derma' (skin), and Latin 'bovista' (puffball).
