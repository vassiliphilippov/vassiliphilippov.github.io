cacodylic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkækəˈdɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈdɪlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or derived from cacodyl or its compounds, especially in chemical contexts
relating to cacodyl in chemistry
medical

Examples

The researchers studied the toxic effects of cacodylic acid on plant cells.
Handling cacodylic compounds requires great caution due to their arsenic content.
He analyzed the environmental impact of cacodylic substances in herbicides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacodyl - a poisonous, volatile compound of arsenic

Etymology

From cacodyl, itself from Greek kakōdēs (stinking) + -yl, with the chemical suffix -ic.
