coffin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.fɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒf.ɪn / (BrE)
A long, narrow box, typically made of wood, in which a dead body is buried or cremated.
box for a dead body

Examples

The family gathered around the coffin during the funeral service.
After the ceremony, the coffin was lowered into the ground.
The ornate coffin was covered with a velvet cloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casket - a box for burying a dead body, especially in North American English

Etymology

From Old French cofin (basket), from Latin cophinus (basket).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑf.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒf.ɪn / (BrE)
to place (a dead body) into a coffin for burial or cremation
place in a coffin

Examples

After the ceremony, they coffined the remains with great care.
The family watched as the undertakers coffined her gently.
All the deceased were coffined before the service began.

Synset Examples

her body was coffined

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entomb - to place in a tomb; to bury
