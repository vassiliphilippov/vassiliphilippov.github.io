Le Douanier Rousseau

1. PROPN

/ lə dwanˈjeɪ ruːˈsoʊ / (AmE), / lə dwanˈjeɪ ruːˈsəʊ / (BrE)
The nickname of Henri Rousseau (1844–1910), a French painter known for his distinctive primitive style.
French primitive painter, 1844–1910

Examples

A major exhibition of Le Douanier Rousseau's works attracted thousands of visitors to the museum.
Many art historians have studied the unique, dreamlike quality in Le Douanier Rousseau's jungle scenes.
The naive style of Le Douanier Rousseau was initially ridiculed but is now highly celebrated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Henri Rousseau - French painter, full name of Le Douanier Rousseau

Etymology

French, literally 'the customs officer Rousseau', from his former job as a toll collector.
