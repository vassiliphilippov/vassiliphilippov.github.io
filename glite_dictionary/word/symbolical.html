symbolical

1. ADJ

/ sɪmˈbɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
serving as a symbol; representing something abstract through a visible sign or object
serving as a symbol; representing the abstract

Examples

The dove is symbolical of peace in many cultures.
The statue's raised arm is symbolical of hope for the future.
Wearing white at weddings is symbolical of purity and new beginnings.

Synset Examples

a crown is emblematic of royalty
the spinning wheel was as symbolic of colonical Massachusetts as the codfish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic - serving as a symbol; representing something abstract

Etymology

From Medieval Latin symbolicus, from Greek symbolikos.

2. ADJ

/ sɪmˈbɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or using symbols; proceeding by means of symbols.
using or relating to symbols

Examples

Her writing takes a highly symbolical approach to exploring cultural traditions.
The ceremony was rich in symbolical meaning, with each gesture carefully chosen.
Symbolical representations are often used in religious art to convey spiritual concepts.

Synset Examples

symbolic logic
symbolic operations
symbolic thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic - relating to or using symbols

Etymology

From symbol + -ical, patterned after symbolic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02817730-a
Synset POS: a
