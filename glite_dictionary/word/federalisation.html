federalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of being brought under the control of a federal government.
state under federal control

Examples

The federalisation of the railway system unified many regional networks under one authority.
Some states resisted federalisation due to fears of losing local autonomy.
Debates about federalisation often arise during constitutional reforms.

Synset Examples

the federalization of postal service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centralisation - the act of bringing activities under a single, central authority
  • Antonym: decentralisation - the transfer of authority from central to local government

Etymology

From 'federal' + '-isation', modeled after French 'fédéralisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of bringing an organization, country, or region under federal control or adopting a federal system of government.
the process of becoming federal

Examples

The federalisation of the country's police forces sparked widespread debate among political leaders.
Several provinces resisted federalisation, fearing a loss of local autonomy and influence.
During the late twentieth century, the federalisation of certain public services became a controversial issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federalization - the act of forming or becoming a federation; US spelling

Etymology

From 'federal' + '-isation', modeled after Latin roots 'foedus' (treaty, agreement).
