rouge

1. VERB

/ ruːʒ / (AmE), / ruːʒ / (BrE)
to redden (a part of the face) by applying rouge or similar cosmetic
apply red cosmetic to the face

Examples

Every morning, she carefully rouges her cheeks before going to work.
The actress rouged her lips and cheeks for the role.
He noticed that she had rouged slightly to look more lively for the photograph.

Synset Examples

she rouged her cheeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blush - to become red in the face, or a cosmetic applied to color the cheeks

Etymology

From French 'rouge' meaning 'red.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00041285-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ruːʒ / (AmE), / ruːʒ / (BrE)
a red or pink powder or cream used as a cosmetic to give colour to the cheeks
red or pink cheek makeup

Examples

She dabbed a little rouge on her cheeks before leaving the house.
The actress wore bright red rouge for her stage performance.
My grandmother always kept a compact of rouge in her purse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blusher - a cosmetic used to give a warm color to the cheeks

Etymology

From French 'rouge' meaning 'red'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04119846-n
Synset POS: n
