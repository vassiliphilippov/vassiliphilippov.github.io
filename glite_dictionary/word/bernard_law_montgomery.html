Bernard Law Montgomery

1. PROPN

/ bɚˌnɑrd lɔ mɑnˈtɡʌməri / (AmE), / bəˈnɑːd lɔː mɒnˈtɡʌməri / (BrE)
(1887–1976) British field marshal who led Allied forces to important victories in North Africa and Normandy during World War II.
British WWII general and field marshal

Examples

Bernard Law Montgomery played a key role in planning the D-Day invasion during World War II.
Many historians regard Bernard Law Montgomery as one of Britain’s greatest military leaders.
Bernard Law Montgomery defeated Rommel at the Battle of El Alamein, turning the tide in North Africa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Erwin Rommel - German field marshal, opponent of Montgomery in North Africa
