signed

1. ADJ

/ saɪnd / (AmE), / saɪnd / (BrE)
bearing a handwritten signature; marked with a person's signature.
having a handwritten signature

Examples

I received a signed copy of the author’s latest novel.
Please ensure that all forms are signed before submitting them to the office.
The contract is not legally binding until it is signed by both parties.

Synset Examples

a signed letter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsigned - not having a handwritten signature

Etymology

Past participle of 'sign', from Latin 'signare' (to mark, to sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02168293-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ saɪnd / (AmE), / saɪnd / (BrE)
using or expressed in sign language, especially by or for people who are deaf.
expressed using sign language

Examples

The lecture was signed for deaf audience members to follow along.
She prefers to use signed communication in group settings.
A signed performance is available for every theater production this season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoken - expressed using speech; not signed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500206-s
Synset POS: s
