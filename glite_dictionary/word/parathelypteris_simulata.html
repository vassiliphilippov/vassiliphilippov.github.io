Parathelypteris simulata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌθɛlɪpˈtɛrɪs ˌsɪmjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpærəˌθɛlɪpˈtɛrɪs ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a delicate, feathery shield fern native to the eastern United States, sometimes classified in the genus Thelypteris
species of delicate shield fern

Examples

Parathelypteris simulata thrives in moist woods and wetlands across the eastern United States.
Botanists have reclassified Parathelypteris simulata several times due to changes in fern taxonomy.
The delicate fronds of Parathelypteris simulata make it a distinctive presence in shaded forest understories.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Parathelypteris' from Greek 'para' (beside) and 'Thelypteris' (a fern genus); 'simulata' from Latin, meaning 'imitated' or 'resembling'.
