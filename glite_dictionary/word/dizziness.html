dizziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪz.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪz.i.nəs / (BrE)
A feeling of unsteadiness or that you are about to fall, often accompanied by lightheadedness or spinning sensations.
feeling unsteady or about to fall

Examples

She complained of dizziness after standing up too quickly.
The heat made him experience sudden dizziness during the marathon.
If you feel dizziness while driving, it's best to pull over immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertigo - a sensation of spinning or loss of balance
  • Synonym: faintness - a feeling of being close to losing consciousness

Etymology

From 'dizzy' + '-ness,' with 'dizzy' from Old English *dysig* (foolish, stupid, later 'giddy, foolish').
