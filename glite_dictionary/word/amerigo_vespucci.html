Amerigo Vespucci

1. PROPN

/ əˌmɛrɪɡoʊ vɛsˈputʃi / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪɡəʊ vɛsˈpʊtʃi / (BrE)
(1454–1512) An Italian navigator and explorer of the coast of South America, after whom the continents of America are named.
Italian explorer; America’s namesake

Examples

Amerigo Vespucci was one of the first Europeans to suggest that the newly discovered lands were not part of Asia.
Many historians credit Amerigo Vespucci with recognizing South America as a separate continent.
The continents of North and South America are named in honor of Amerigo Vespucci.

Etymology

Italian form of the given name Amerigo, from Medieval Latin Americus; surname Vespucci.
