multiprogramming

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈproʊɡræmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈprəʊɡræmɪŋ / (BrE)
the simultaneous execution of two or more computer programs by a single computer system
running several programs at once on a computer

Examples

Early mainframes used multiprogramming to maximize the use of CPU resources.
With multiprogramming, several users could share the same computer system effectively.
Multiprogramming led to improved efficiency in batch processing environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multitasking - execution of multiple tasks or processes simultaneously by a computer

Etymology

Formed from 'multi-' meaning 'many' and 'programming', referring to the operation of multiple programs.
