bald-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔld ˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɔːld ˌfeɪst / (BrE)
(of an animal) having white markings on the face, especially as a distinct patch or blaze.
having white markings on the face (animal)

Examples

The horse stood out with its striking bald-faced appearance.
That rancher breeds bald-faced Hereford cattle known for their distinctive facial markings.
We admired the elegant, bald-faced mare grazing in the field.

Etymology

From 'bald' (white, marked by a lack of color or hair) and 'face.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔld ˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɔːld ˌfeɪst / (BrE)
(of an action, statement, or person) shamelessly open and blatant; undisguised or brazen.
shamelessly open; blatant

Examples

He told a bald-faced lie to avoid getting into trouble.
Her bald-faced arrogance shocked everyone at the meeting.
It was a bald-faced admission of guilt in front of the entire audience.

Etymology

From 'bald' (lacking hair) + 'faced'; originally suggesting a face without concealment.
