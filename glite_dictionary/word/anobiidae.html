Anobiidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌnoʊˈbaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / aˌnəʊˈbɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles commonly known as deathwatch beetles, which are wood-boring insects that can cause damage to wooden structures and furniture.
family of wood-boring beetles
medical

Examples

While inspecting the old church, we found evidence of Anobiidae infestation in the wooden beams.
The furniture had to be replaced due to severe damage from Anobiidae.
Experts were called in to treat the house after signs of Anobiidae activity were detected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deathwatch beetle - a wood-boring beetle from the family Anobiidae; notorious for producing a ticking sound

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Anobium.

2. NOUN

/ əˌnoʊ.biˈɪd.iː / (AmE), / əˌnəʊ.bɪˈɪd.iː / (BrE)
A family of beetles commonly known as deathwatch beetles, characterized by wood-boring larvae that can damage wooden structures.
a family of wood-boring beetles

Examples

The larvae of the Anobiidae are notorious for damaging wooden furniture and beams.
Many museums take precautions to prevent infestations of Anobiidae in their collections.
A pest control expert identified the insects as members of the Anobiidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deathwatch beetle - a common member of the Anobiidae family; known for making tapping sounds in wood

Etymology

From New Latin Anobiidae, based on the type genus Anobium.
