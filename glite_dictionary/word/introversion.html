introversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the condition or process of being folded inward or sheathed, especially in a biological or anatomical context
the state of being folded inward

Examples

The introversion of the membrane can be observed under the microscope.
Some marine worms protect themselves through introversion of their body parts.
A defect may arise if introversion does not occur properly during embryonic development.

Etymology

From Latin 'introvertere' (to turn inward): 'intro-' (inward) + 'vertere' (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntroʊˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəʊˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the process of a layer folding inward to form a pocket on a surface, especially in a biological context.
inward folding of a layer (biology)
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, introversion leads to the formation of important internal structures.
A surgeon observed introversion of the membrane during the procedure.
The phenomenon of introversion is essential for understanding certain biological processes.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the personality trait of being more focused on one's own inner thoughts and feelings than on external events; a tendency toward being reserved or withdrawn
preference for inner thoughts over social interaction

Examples

Introversion can make social gatherings exhausting for some people.
Many creative individuals attribute their success to their introversion and rich inner life.
The psychologist discussed the benefits and drawbacks of introversion in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extroversion - outgoing, socially oriented personality trait
  • Synonym: introvert - a person characterized by introversion

Etymology

From Latin 'introvertere' meaning 'to turn inward'.
