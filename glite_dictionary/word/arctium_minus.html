Arctium minus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrk.ti.əm ˈmaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːk.ti.əm ˈmaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a species of burdock native to Europe and North Africa, commonly found in a wide range of soils except very acid ones
a widespread species of burdock

Examples

Arctium minus thrives in fields and roadsides throughout much of Europe.
The broad leaves of Arctium minus make it easy to identify among other wild plants.
Many herbalists use Arctium minus for traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdock - a large-leaved plant with prickly heads, to which 'Arctium minus' belongs

Etymology

From Latin: Arctium (genus name), and minus (meaning 'lesser').
