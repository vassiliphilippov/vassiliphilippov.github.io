Cynoscion regalis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌnɒsioʊn rəˈɡælɪs / (AmE), / saɪˌnɒsiɒn rɪˈɡælɪs / (BrE)
a food and game fish found in North American coastal waters, also known as weakfish, notable for a tender mouth from which fishing hooks can easily tear out
North American coastal food and game fish (weakfish)

Examples

Anglers along the Atlantic coast often pursue the Cynoscion regalis during its spring migration.
The delicate mouth of the Cynoscion regalis makes it challenging to land without careful technique.
Commercial fisheries value Cynoscion regalis both for its taste and its popularity as game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakfish - the common name for Cynoscion regalis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kyon' (dog) and Latin 'scion' (fish), with 'regalis' meaning 'royal' in Latin.
