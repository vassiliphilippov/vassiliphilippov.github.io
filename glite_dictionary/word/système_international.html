Système International

1. NOUN

/ sisˈtɛm ˌɪntərnæˈsjɒnəl / (AmE), / sisˈtɛm ˌɪntəˈnæsjɒnəl / (BrE)
the international system of metric units used in science; its fundamental quantities are the meter, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, and candela
the international metric system of units

Examples

All scientific measurements are reported in the Système International to maintain consistency.
The laboratory converted their results from imperial units to the Système International.
Universities worldwide teach the Système International as part of their physics curricula.

Synset Examples

Today the United States is the only country in the world not totally committed to the Systeme International d'Unites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: International System of Units - the official English term for Système International
  • Antonym: Imperial system - the system of weights and measures formerly used in Britain

Etymology

French, meaning 'International System' (official name for the International System of Units, abbreviated SI).
