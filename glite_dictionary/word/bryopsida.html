Bryopsida

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / braɪˈɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
a class of bryophytes, known as true mosses, having leafy gametophytes rather than thalloid ones, and including several orders such as Bryales and Sphagnales
class of true mosses (plants)

Examples

Scientists study Bryopsida to better understand the evolution of land plants.
The forest floor was thickly carpeted with Bryopsida species.
Members of Bryopsida are important in many ecosystems due to their moisture-retaining properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - small green non-vascular plant in division Bryophyta

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek bryon (moss) + -psida (used in plant class names).
