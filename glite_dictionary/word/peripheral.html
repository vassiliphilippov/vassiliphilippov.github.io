peripheral

1. ADJ

/ pəˈrɪfəɹəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəl / (BrE)
relating to, situated on, or forming the outer edge or boundary of something; outer or marginal.
on or near an outer edge

Examples

Many small towns are located in peripheral areas far from the city center.
Wildlife is more abundant in the peripheral zones of the forest.
The company's offices are mostly based in peripheral suburbs.

Synset Examples

Russia's peripheral provinces
peripheral suburbs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central - at or near the center

Etymology

From Greek *peripheria* (circumference), via Late Latin *peripheria* and Medieval Latin *periphericus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00332344-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪfɚəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəl / (BrE)
a device, such as a keyboard, printer, or external drive, that is connected to a computer and works with it but is not an essential part of the central processing unit
external computer device

Examples

Our office recently upgraded several peripherals, including new printers and external hard drives.
A keyboard is considered a basic peripheral for any desktop computer setup.
Installing the correct drivers is crucial for your peripheral devices to function properly.

Synset Examples

disk drives and printers are important peripherals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - an extra piece of equipment that can be added for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole, especially in technology

Etymology

From Greek *peripheria* (circumference, outer surface), via French and late Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03922839-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ pəˈrɪfərəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəl / (BrE)
Not as important as the main subject; relating to or situated on the edge or periphery of something.
not central; minor or secondary

Examples

His comments were interesting but largely peripheral to the main topic.
The committee discussed several peripheral issues before tackling the core problem.
While technology is important, it remains peripheral to the main objectives of the project.

Synset Examples

a peripheral interest
energy is far from a peripheral issue in the economy
peripheral issues

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central - of primary importance; focused on the main point

Etymology

From Greek *peripheria* (circumference), via Latin *peripheria*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01863683-s
Synset POS: s
