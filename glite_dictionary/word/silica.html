silica

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkə / (BrE)
a hard, unreactive, colorless or white compound (silicon dioxide, SiO₂) that occurs naturally as quartz and in various living organisms, commonly used in making glass, ceramics, and other materials
silicon dioxide; mineral used in glass and ceramics

Examples

The sand on this beach contains a high proportion of silica from weathered rocks.
Manufacturers use silica to make glass, ceramics, and some types of electronics.
Workers should wear masks to avoid inhaling silica dust in construction areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard, white or colorless mineral consisting of silicon dioxide, present in many rocks

Etymology

From Latin 'silex' (flint, hard stone).
