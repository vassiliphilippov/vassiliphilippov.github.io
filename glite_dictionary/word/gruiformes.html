Gruiformes

1. NOUN

/ ɡruː.ɪˈfɔːr.miːz / (AmE), / ɡruː.ɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
a large order of marsh-dwelling birds with long legs, necks, and bills, including cranes, rails, and bustards
order of marsh-dwelling birds (cranes, rails, bustards)

Examples

The Gruiformes are found in marshes and wetlands around the world.
Cranes and rails both belong to the order Gruiformes.
Scientists study the migration of Gruiformes to understand wetland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin 'grus' (crane) + 'formis' (shaped, formed).
