Vespasian

1. PROPN

/ vɛˈspeɪʒ(i)ən / (AmE), / vɛˈspeɪzɪən / (BrE)
Roman emperor (9–79 AD), founder of the Flavian dynasty, who consolidated Roman rule, reformed the army, improved financial stability, and initiated construction of the Colosseum.
Roman emperor, founder of Flavian dynasty

Examples

The Roman emperor Vespasian began construction of the Colosseum in Rome.
Vespasian is known for bringing stability to the Roman Empire after a period of chaos.
Historians credit Vespasian with vital military and economic reforms during his reign.

Etymology

From Latin Vespasianus, the emperor's cognomen.

2. PROPN

/ vɛˈspeɪʒən / (AmE), / vɛˈspeɪzɪən / (BrE)
(AD 9–79) Roman emperor, founder of the Flavian dynasty, known for consolidating Roman rule in Germany and Britain, reforming the army, bringing prosperity, and initiating the construction of the Colosseum.
Roman emperor, founder of the Flavian dynasty

Examples

Vespasian became emperor after a period of civil war and restored stability to the Roman Empire.
The construction of the Colosseum was started under the rule of Vespasian.
Many historians praise Vespasian for his effective reforms and pragmatic leadership.

Etymology

From Latin Vespasianus, the family name of the emperor.
