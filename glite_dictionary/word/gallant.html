gallant

1. ADJ

/ ɡəˈlænt / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.ənt / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a man) lively and spirited; displaying courage or energy in a bold or showy way.
lively and spirited; dashing

Examples

The gallant young officer led his troops bravely into battle.
She admired his gallant attitude at the party, always energetic and full of charm.
With a gallant leap, the performer stole the spotlight and delighted the crowd.

Synset Examples

a dashing hero

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dashing - lively, spirited, and stylish
  • Antonym: cowardly - lacking courage or spirit; opposite of gallant

Etymology

From Old French 'galant' (courteous, brave), present participle of 'galer' (to make merry).

3. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlænt / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.ənt / (BrE)
a man who pays particular attention to his dress and appearance, often in a refined or showy manner
fashion-conscious man
archaic

Examples

In the ballroom, every gallant vied for the admiration of the ladies with his elegant attire.
The young gallant adjusted his cravat before stepping outside.
Among the city’s fashionable elite, a gallant was often recognized by his impeccable taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man excessively concerned with clothes and appearance
  • Synonym: fop - a man overly concerned with his clothes and appearance

Etymology

From Old French *galant* (courteous, fashionable man), present participle of *galer* (to make merry).
