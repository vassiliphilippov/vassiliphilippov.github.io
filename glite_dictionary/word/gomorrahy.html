Gomorrahy

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈmɔːrəhi / (AmE), / ɡəˈmɒrəhi / (BrE)
A state of extreme moral corruption or chaos, suggestive of the biblical city of Gomorrah.
a state of moral corruption or chaos

Historians often describe the final years of the empire as a time of Gomorrahy and decadence.
Some critics saw the novel as a commentary on the Gomorrahy of modern urban life.

Synset Examples

The Gomorrahy of the situation was too much to bear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonic - relating to or resembling Babylon, often in reference to decadence or moral decline

Etymology

Formed from 'Gomorrah', the biblical city destroyed for its wickedness, with the suffix '-y' indicating a state or quality.
