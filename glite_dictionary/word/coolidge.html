Coolidge

1. PROPN

/ ˈkuːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkuːlɪdʒ / (BrE)
Calvin Coolidge (1872–1933), the 30th President of the United States, who succeeded Warren G. Harding in 1923 and served until 1929.
30th U.S. President (1872–1933)

Examples

President Coolidge was known for his quiet demeanor and strong support of business.
Coolidge took office after President Harding died suddenly in 1923.
During Coolidge's presidency, the United States experienced significant economic growth.
