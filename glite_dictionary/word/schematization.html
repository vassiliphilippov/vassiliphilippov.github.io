schematization

1. NOUN

/ ˌskiːmətɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌskiːmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of representing or arranging something according to a scheme, plan, or formula, often simplifying complex information
arrangement by scheme or formula

Examples

The schematization of the data allowed the researchers to identify important trends more easily.
His argument suffered from excessive schematization, losing sight of important nuances in the process.
Artistic schematization often emphasizes form over content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplification - the act of making something simpler

Etymology

From Greek 'schēmatizein' (to form or shape according to a scheme) + English suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ skiː.mə.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / skiː.mət.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing something in a simplified or diagrammatic form, such as a chart or outline
the act of making a schematic representation

Examples

The schematization of the process made it much easier for the engineers to understand each step.
Her research focuses on the schematization of complex information for educational purposes.
Effective schematization can turn overwhelming data into easily digestible visual formats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagramming - the act of drawing a diagram to represent information
  • Synonym: representation - the action of depicting or standing for something

Etymology

From Greek 'schēmatizein' (to give form), through Late Latin 'schematizare' and French 'schématisation'.
