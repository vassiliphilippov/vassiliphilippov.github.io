oversight

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəsaɪt / (BrE)
the act of overseeing or supervising the performance or operation of a person, group, or process
supervision or management

Examples

The new project will require close oversight to ensure it meets all safety standards.
The committee was established to provide government oversight of financial institutions.
Without proper oversight, mistakes and inefficiencies are likely to occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervision - the act of supervising; close observation and direction
  • Synonym: management - the act or process of controlling or dealing with something

Etymology

From oversee + -ight, after insight, meaning the act of overseeing.

WordNet ID: oewn-01137850-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəsaɪt / (BrE)
an unintentional error or omission due to carelessness or neglect
mistake caused by inattention

Examples

Due to an unfortunate oversight, the invitation was never sent to her.
He apologized for the oversight and promised to correct the error immediately.
The missing page was simply an oversight by the printer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong or incorrect
  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Synonym: error - a mistake

Etymology

From over- + sight; originally meaning 'supervision', later also 'an omission through carelessness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00075344-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəsaɪt / (BrE)
an unintentional failure to notice or do something; a mistake made because something was overlooked
an unintentional omission or mistake

Examples

The missing signature on the document was simply an oversight on my part.
They apologized for the oversight and quickly corrected the invoice.
Due to an oversight, she forgot to include the attachments in the email.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake, especially one that is unintentional
  • Synonym: mistake - an incorrect action or assumption

Etymology

From oversee + -ight, meaning the result of failing to oversee.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05714762-n
Synset POS: n
