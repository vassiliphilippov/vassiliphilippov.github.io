noonday

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːnˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈnuːndeɪ / (BrE)
the middle of the day; the time around noon
the middle of the day

Examples

The heat at noonday was too intense to work outside.
We rested under the tree during noonday to avoid the sun.
The shadows were shortest at noonday, marking the peak of sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noon - 12 o'clock in the day; midday

Etymology

From 'noon' + 'day', meaning the part of the day around noon.
