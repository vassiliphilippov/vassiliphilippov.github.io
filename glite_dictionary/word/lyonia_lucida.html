Lyonia lucida

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈoʊni.ə ˈluːsɪdə / (AmE), / laɪˈəʊnɪə ˈluːsɪdə / (BrE)
A showy evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, with glossy leaves, angled branches, and clusters of pink to reddish flowers resembling an umbel.
evergreen shrub of southeastern US with pink flowers

Examples

The garden's new section features Lyonia lucida, prized for its shiny leaves and vibrant flowers.
Botanists collected samples of Lyonia lucida from several wetlands in Florida.
Lyonia lucida thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found along the edges of swamps.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'lyon', personal name; species epithet from Latin 'lucidus' meaning 'bright' or 'shining', referring to the shiny leaves.
