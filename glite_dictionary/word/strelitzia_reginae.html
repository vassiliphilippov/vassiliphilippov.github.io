Strelitzia reginae

1. NOUN

/ strəˈlɪtsiə rɪˈdʒaɪniː / (AmE), / strɛˈlɪtsiə rɪˈdʒiːniː / (BrE)
an ornamental plant native to South Africa, with striking orange and blue flowers that resemble the shape of a bird; also known as the bird of paradise.
ornamental tropical plant; bird of paradise

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a magnificent Strelitzia reginae in full bloom.
Botanists prize the Strelitzia reginae for its vibrant, bird-like flowers.
We planted a young Strelitzia reginae near the patio for a tropical look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird of paradise - common name for Strelitzia reginae, referring to its flower's appearance

Etymology

Named after Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, wife of King George III.
