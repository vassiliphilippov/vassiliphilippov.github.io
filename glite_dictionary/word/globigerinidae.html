Globigerinidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊˌbɪdʒəˈrɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒbɪdʒəˈrɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of single-celled marine protozoa with calcareous shells, found mostly as plankton.
family of marine protozoa with shells

Examples

The fossilized shells of Globigerinidae contribute to the formation of deep-sea sediments.
Scientists classified several new species under the family Globigerinidae.
Planktonic foraminifera like Globigerinidae are important indicators in paleoclimatic studies.

Etymology

From New Latin Globigerina (a genus name) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡloʊbɪdʒəˈrɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɡləʊbɪdʒəˈrɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of single-celled marine organisms (foraminiferans) characterized by calcareous, chambered shells.
family of marine foraminiferans

Examples

The Globigerinidae are widespread in marine environments and are important in the study of paleoclimates.
Fossils of Globigerinidae play a crucial role in biostratigraphy and geological dating.
Researchers analyzed the shells of Globigerinidae to understand ancient ocean conditions.

Etymology

From the genus name Globigerina, from Latin 'globus' (sphere) + 'gerere' (to bear), with suffix '-idae' used for animal family names.
