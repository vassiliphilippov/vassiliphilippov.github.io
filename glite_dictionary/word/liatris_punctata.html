Liatris punctata

1. NOUN

/ liˈætrɪs pʌŋkˈteɪtə / (AmE), / laɪˈætrɪs pʌŋkˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant with narrow stems tipped by dense spikes of rose-lavender flowers, native to central North America.
a North American perennial herb with lavender flower spikes

Examples

Botanists studied the drought resistance of Liatris punctata in prairie ecosystems.
Gardeners appreciate Liatris punctata for its resilience and striking flower spikes.
Pollinators such as bees and butterflies are attracted to Liatris punctata throughout the summer.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *liatris*; species name from Latin *punctata* meaning 'spotted'.
