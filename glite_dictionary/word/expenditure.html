expenditure

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛndɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛndɪtjə / (BrE)
the total amount of money that is spent, especially by a government, organization, or individual
money spent; amount spent

Examples

The company's annual expenditure on advertising has increased significantly.
Government expenditure on healthcare is a major part of the national budget.
Careful tracking of your household expenditure helps with financial planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expense - an amount of money spent; a cost
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially for work or through investments

Etymology

From Latin 'expendere' (to pay out), via French 'dépense' and English 'expend'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛndɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛndɪtʃə / (BrE)
the act of using up or consuming something, especially money, time, or energy
the act of using up resources

Examples

The company's annual expenditure on research and development has increased this year.
Any unnecessary expenditure should be avoided if we want to save money.
There was a significant expenditure of energy during the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumption - the act of consuming or using something up

Etymology

From Latin expendere (to weigh out, pay out) via Medieval Latin expenditūra (payment).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛndɪtʃər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛndɪtʃə / (BrE)
the act of spending money on goods or services; an amount of money spent
the act of spending money

Examples

Government expenditure on healthcare has increased significantly in recent years.
We need to keep track of every expenditure to stay within our budget for the project.
The company reduced its expenditure to improve overall profitability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expense - a cost or amount of money spent
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis

Etymology

From Latin 'expenditura', from 'expendere' meaning 'to pay out'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
