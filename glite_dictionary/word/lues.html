lues

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.iːz / (AmE), / ˈluː.iːz / (BrE)
an old term for syphilis, a chronic sexually transmitted disease caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum
archaic term for syphilis
archaic medical

Examples

The physician diagnosed the patient with lues after a series of laboratory tests.
Many 19th-century writers referred to syphilis as lues in their medical texts.
Cases of congenital lues were unfortunately common before the advent of antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted disease caused by Treponema pallidum

Etymology

From Latin 'lues' meaning 'plague' or 'pestilence'.
