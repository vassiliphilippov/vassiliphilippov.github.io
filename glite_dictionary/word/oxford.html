Oxford

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑksfərd / (AmE), / ˈɒksfəd / (BrE)
a prestigious university located in Oxford, England, renowned for its academic excellence and long history
university in Oxford, England

Examples

She received her doctorate from Oxford after years of intensive research.
Getting accepted into Oxford is a dream for many ambitious students worldwide.
The traditions and architecture of Oxford attract thousands of tourists each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cambridge - another prestigious university in England

Etymology

Named after the city of Oxford, where the university is located.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɑːk.sfərd / (AmE), / ˈɒk.sfəd / (BrE)
A city in southern England, northwest of London, known for being the home of Oxford University.
city in England, home of Oxford University

Examples

I spent a summer in Oxford studying English literature.
Oxford is famous for its prestigious university and beautiful architecture.
Many international students dream of attending Oxford University.

Etymology

From Old English 'Oxenaforda', meaning 'ford of the oxen'.
