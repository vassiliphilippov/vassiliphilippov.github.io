febrifuge

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛbrəˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ˈfiːbrɪˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that reduces or eliminates fever.
medicine that reduces fever
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a febrifuge to help lower the patient's high temperature.
Some herbal remedies act as a natural febrifuge during a mild fever.
After taking the febrifuge, her fever gradually subsided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipyretic - a drug or substance that reduces fever

Etymology

From Latin 'febris' (fever) + '-fuge' (from 'fugare', to drive away).
