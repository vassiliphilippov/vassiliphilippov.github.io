to recriminate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkrɪməˌneɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkrɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make a counter-accusation against someone; to accuse in return
make a counter-accusation

Examples

She refused to recriminate, choosing instead to end the argument peacefully.
As soon as he was blamed, he began to recriminate against his colleague.
During the heated debate, both parties continued to recriminate, exchanging accusations with growing intensity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accuse - to charge someone with an offense or crime

Etymology

From Latin 'recriminari' (to make a countercharge), from 're-' (back) + 'criminari' (to accuse).
