Otoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people who traditionally lived in the valleys of the Platte and Missouri rivers in Nebraska.
a member of a Native American people of Nebraska

Examples

The Otoe lived along the banks of the Missouri River for centuries.
Many Otoe traditions continue to be practiced today.
An Otoe leader signed the treaty with the United States government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages including the Otoe language
  • Synonym: Omaha - a neighboring Native American people in Nebraska

Etymology

From native name Wadóhti (Otoe self-designation).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
the dialect of the Chiwere language spoken by the Otoe people, a Native American tribe
dialect of the Chiwere language

Examples

The elders worked hard to preserve the Otoe language for future generations.
Researchers have compiled a dictionary of Otoe to document its unique vocabulary.
She is fluent in both English and Otoe, thanks to her grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chiwere - a Siouan language spoken by the Iowa, Otoe, and Missouria peoples

Etymology

From the name of the Otoe people, a Native American tribe of the Siouan language family.
