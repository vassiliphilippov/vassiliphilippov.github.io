Amorpha californica

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɔɹfə ˌkæləˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / əˈmɔːfə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of erect to spreading hairy shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, bearing racemes of red to indigo flowers.
a shrub native to Pacific US with red to indigo flowers

Examples

The Amorpha californica is known for its striking indigo blossoms along the western coast.
Researchers documented several Amorpha californica shrubs thriving in the hillside nature reserve.
Gardeners appreciate Amorpha californica for its resilience and attractive floral displays.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Amorpha' and species indicating origin, 'californica' (from California).
