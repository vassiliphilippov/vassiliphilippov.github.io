tempestuousness

1. NOUN

/ tɛmˈpɛstʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / tɛmˈpɛstjuːəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being full of strong, turbulent, or conflicting emotion or resembling a violent storm.
state of being stormy or turbulent

Examples

The tempestuousness of the debate left many participants emotionally drained.
You could feel the tempestuousness of the ocean in the roaring wind and crashing waves.
Her poems often capture the tempestuousness of adolescent emotions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being calm and free from disturbance
  • Synonym: turbulence - violent or unsteady movement; conflict or disorder

Etymology

From tempestuous + -ness; 'tempestuous' from Old French 'tempestueux', from Latin 'tempestuosus' (stormy).

2. NOUN

/ tɛmˈpɛstʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / tɛmˈpɛstjuːəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being full of violent or turbulent change; emotional or situational agitation.
state of turbulence or upheaval

Examples

There was a period of great tempestuousness in the country after the revolution.
The tempestuousness of their relationship made it difficult for either of them to find peace.
His writing reflects the tempestuousness of his emotions during those years.

Synset Examples

the political ferment produced new leadership
social unrest

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion
  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of confusion or disorder

Etymology

From 'tempestuous' (violent storm), ultimately from Latin 'tempestas' (storm, weather, season).
