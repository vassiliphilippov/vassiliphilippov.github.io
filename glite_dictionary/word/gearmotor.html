gearmotor

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪrˌmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪəˌməʊtə / (BrE)
an electric motor combined with a gearbox for reducing speed and increasing torque
motor with built-in speed-reducing gears

Examples

The conveyor belt was powered by a gearmotor for smooth and controlled movement.
Our robotics project used a compact gearmotor to optimize efficiency and torque.
They replaced the old drive system with a more reliable gearmotor.

Etymology

Formed from 'gear' and 'motor', referring to a motor integrated with gearing.
