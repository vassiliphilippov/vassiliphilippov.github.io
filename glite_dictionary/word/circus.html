Circus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɜːrkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of hawks known as harriers, medium-sized birds of prey found in various parts of the world.
genus of harriers (hawks)

Examples

The Circus genus includes several species of harriers found across different continents.
Naturalists observed a male Circus flying low over the marshlands in search of prey.
The wingspan of birds in the Circus genus allows them to glide effortlessly above open fields.

Etymology

From Latin *circus*, meaning 'circle', possibly referencing the harriers' circling flight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the hawk family, comprising the harriers, which are slender, long-winged birds of prey.
a genus of birds (harriers)

Examples

Among the birds of prey, the genus Circus is known for its graceful flight patterns.
Researchers observed several species belonging to the Circus genus in the wetlands.
The marsh harrier, a member of the Circus genus, has distinctive hunting techniques.

Etymology

From Latin *circus* (circle, ring), used in taxonomy for its circling flight behavior.
