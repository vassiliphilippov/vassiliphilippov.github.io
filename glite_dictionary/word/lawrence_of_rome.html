Lawrence of Rome

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɔː.rəns əv roʊm / (AmE), / ˈlɒr.əns əv rəʊm / (BrE)
a Christian martyr of the 3rd century who, according to tradition, was executed in Rome for refusing to surrender church treasures, presenting the poor instead, and was martyred by being roasted on a gridiron.
early Christian martyr in Rome

Examples

Many churches are dedicated to Lawrence of Rome in honor of his sacrifice.
The story of Lawrence of Rome is celebrated each year on August 10th.
Medieval artists often depicted Lawrence of Rome holding a gridiron, symbolizing his martyrdom.
