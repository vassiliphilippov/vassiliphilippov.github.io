crossed

1. ADJ

/ krɔst / (AmE), / krɒst / (BrE)
placed or arranged so that one part passes over or across another; lying or fitted crosswise.
placed crosswise; intersecting

Examples

She sat at her desk with her arms crossed, waiting for the meeting to begin.
A pair of crossed swords decorated the family crest above the fireplace.
He answered her question with his fingers crossed behind his back.

Synset Examples

spoken with a straight face but crossed fingers
crossed forks
seated with arms crossed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncrossed - not crossed; arranged in a parallel or open position

2. ADJ

/ krɔst / (AmE), / krɒst / (BrE)
(of a cheque) marked with two parallel lines across it to indicate that it may only be deposited directly into a bank account and not cashed.
(of a cheque) marked for deposit only
british

Examples

The company issued a crossed cheque to ensure security for the payment.
Always write your recipient’s name on a crossed cheque to prevent misuse.
She deposited the crossed cheque directly into her bank account.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncrossed - (of a cheque) not marked with lines and thus payable over the counter
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
