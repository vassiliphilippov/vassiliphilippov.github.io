derrick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪk / (BrE)
A tall framework erected over an oil well that supports the equipment used for drilling and handling drill pipes.
framework over an oil well for drilling

Examples

The derrick towered above the desert landscape as workers prepared for another day of drilling.
Engineers inspected the derrick to ensure it was safe before starting the operation.
A new derrick was assembled on site to support deeper exploration of the oil field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil rig - a large structure with equipment for drilling and extracting oil

Etymology

Named after Thomas Derrick, an English executioner who invented a type of gallows.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪk / (BrE)
A type of crane with a movable boom that is used to lift or move heavy weights, especially on ships or at construction sites.
type of crane with a movable boom

Examples

The workers used a derrick to lift the steel beams into place.
A large derrick towered over the oil field.
The ship was equipped with its own derrick for loading and unloading cargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane - a machine for lifting and moving heavy objects

Etymology

Named after Thomas Derrick, an English executioner in the 17th century, whose name became associated with gallows and later with lifting devices.
