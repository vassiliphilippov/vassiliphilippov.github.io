Atriplex

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtrɪplɛks / (AmE), / ˈeɪtrɪplɛks / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the amaranth family, known as orach or saltbush, found especially in saline soils and arid regions.
genus of plants (saltbush/orach)

Examples

The Atriplex species are often used to reclaim salty or degraded lands.
Many birds and mammals feed on the seeds of Atriplex plants in the wild.
Some ancient cultures cultivated Atriplex as a leafy vegetable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orach - a common name for plants in the Atriplex genus
  • Synonym: saltbush - another common name for plants in the genus Atriplex

Etymology

From Latin *Atriplex*, the ancient name for orach.
