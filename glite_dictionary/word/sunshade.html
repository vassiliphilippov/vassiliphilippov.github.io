sunshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌnʃeɪd / (BrE)
A device, often made of canvas, used as a canopy to provide shelter from the sun or rain.
canopy for shelter from sun or rain

Examples

We sat under the sunshade to escape the midday heat.
He carried a large sunshade to the beach for the family picnic.
The garden table came with a built-in sunshade to protect diners from the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasol - light umbrella used to give shade from the sun

Etymology

From 'sun' + 'shade', first recorded in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌnʃeɪd / (BrE)
A portable device, usually collapsible, held by hand or mounted to provide shade from the sun.
handheld or portable shade from sun

Examples

She opened her sunshade as she walked along the sunny beach.
The children sat under a colorful sunshade to escape the midday heat.
He bought a small sunshade to protect himself while watching the parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasol - a light umbrella used as sun protection

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'shade,' referring to a device that blocks sunlight.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03895568-n
Synset POS: n
