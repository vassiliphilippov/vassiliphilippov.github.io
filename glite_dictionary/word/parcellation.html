parcellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɑːsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into parcels, especially land or areas
division into parcels or parts

Examples

Recent parcellation of farmland has reduced the size of individual plots significantly.
The parcellation of brain regions is important in neuroscience research.
Over time, the parcellation of the estate made it difficult to manage as a single unit.

Synset Examples

the increasing parcellation of land with every generation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the action of separating something into parts
  • Synonym: partition - the action of dividing or being divided into parts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin parcella (parcel) + -ation (forming nouns of action or process).
