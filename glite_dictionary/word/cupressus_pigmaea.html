Cupressus pigmaea

1. PROPN

/ kuˈprɛsəs pɪgˈmiːə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs pɪgˈmiːə / (BrE)
A rare, small cypress tree native to northern California, sometimes regarded as the same species as the Gowen cypress.
rare cypress species from northern California

Examples

The Cupressus pigmaea is found primarily along the northern California coast.
Botanists debate whether Cupressus pigmaea is distinct from the Gowen cypress.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the rare habitats of Cupressus pigmaea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gowen cypress - a species of cypress tree sometimes confused with Cupressus pigmaea

Etymology

From genus name Latin *Cupressus* (cypress); 'pigmaea' from Latin *pygmaeus* (dwarf, small).
