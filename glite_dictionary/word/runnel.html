runnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌnəl / (BrE)
a small, narrow stream or channel of water
a small stream or channel

Examples

Tiny fish darted through the runnel that wound its way across the meadow.
After the storm, a runnel formed at the base of the hill, trickling down to the pond.
She followed the runnel through the woods, enjoying the sound of flowing water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook - a small stream
  • Degree Higher: river - a large natural stream of water

Etymology

From Middle English runel, from Old English rynel, related to 'run.'
