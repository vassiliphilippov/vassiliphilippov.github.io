Rhus ovata

1. NOUN

/ ruːs oʊˈveɪtə / (AmE), / ruːs əʊˈvɑːtə / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, with reddish-yellow flower spikes and glandular hairy fruits, commonly known as sugar bush.
evergreen shrub (sugar bush) of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Rhus ovata grows abundantly in the chaparral regions of California.
Hikers admire the bright flower clusters of Rhus ovata in spring.
Botanists study the glandular fruit of Rhus ovata for its unique adaptations.

Etymology

From Latin 'Rhus' (sumac) + 'ovata' (egg-shaped), referring to the plant's leaves.
