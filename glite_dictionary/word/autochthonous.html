autochthonous

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑːkθənəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkθənəs / (BrE)
(of rocks, deposits, etc.) Found in the place where they and their constituent materials were originally formed, not transported from elsewhere.
formed in original place; not transported

Examples

The autochthonous limestone in this region reveals much about its ancient geography.
Researchers identified autochthonous sediments that had not been moved by water or wind.
The site contains both autochthonous and transported rock layers, indicating varied geological processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allochthonous - (of rocks, deposits, etc.) Formed elsewhere and transported to their current location

Etymology

From Greek 'autokhthōn' (native, sprung from the land itself), from 'autos' (self) + 'khthōn' (earth).

2. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑkθənəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkθənəs / (BrE)
(chiefly technical) originating in the place where it is found; native to a particular area or environment.
native to a specific place; indigenous

Examples

Many scientists study the autochthonous plants that have evolved unique adaptations to their local environments.
The museum's collection includes artifacts created by autochthonous peoples of the region.
Geologists found that the rock formation was autochthonous, having formed in its current location rather than being transported.

Synset Examples

the autochthonal fauna of Australia includes the kangaroo
autochthonous rocks and people and folktales
endemic folkways
the Ainu are indigenous to the northernmost islands of Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigenous - originating or occurring naturally in a particular place; native
  • Synonym: endemic - restricted or peculiar to a locality or region; native

Etymology

From Greek 'autokhthōn' (native, sprung from the land itself), from 'autos' (self) + 'khthōn' (earth).
