aerie

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
a house, room, or other habitation situated at a high altitude, often on a mountain or high building
high-altitude dwelling

Examples

The billionaire's aerie overlooks the city from the top of a skyscraper.
Their mountain aerie could only be reached by a narrow winding path.
She called her penthouse apartment her personal aerie above the bustling streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nest - a structure or place made or chosen by a bird for laying eggs

Etymology

From Latin *area* (level ground, threshing floor), later associated with nests of birds of prey.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
A large nest of a bird of prey, such as an eagle or hawk, typically built high in a tree or on a cliff.
nest of a bird of prey

Examples

Through her binoculars, she spotted a golden eagle’s aerie perched high atop the rocky cliff.
The abandoned aerie was nestled in the tallest pine at the edge of the forest.
Researchers carefully climbed to the aerie to observe the behavior of the young hawks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyrie - alternative spelling of 'aerie', nest of a bird of prey

Etymology

From Latin *area* (a level space or threshing floor), later used for 'bird's nest' in Medieval Latin.
