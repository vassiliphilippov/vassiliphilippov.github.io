Nauclea

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˈkliːə / (AmE), / nɔːˈkliːə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen tropical shrubs or trees with smooth, leathery leaves, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.
genus of evergreen tropical shrubs or trees

Examples

The botanist discovered a new species within the genus Nauclea in the rainforest.
Leaves of the Nauclea tree are used in traditional medicine in Southeast Asia.
Researchers collected bark samples from several mature Nauclea trees for chemical analysis.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'naus' (ship), referring to the boat-shaped capsule of some species.
