quincentennial

1. ADJ

/ kwɪnˌsɛnˈtɛniəl / (AmE), / kwɪnˌsɛnˈtɛnɪəl / (BrE)
relating to a 500th anniversary or its celebration.
of or relating to a 500th anniversary

Examples

The city hosted a quincentennial festival marking 500 years since its founding.
The quincentennial celebration drew visitors from all over the world.
Special coins were minted for the university’s quincentennial in 2048.

Synset Examples

the quincentennial celebration of the founding of the city

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centennial - relating to a 100th anniversary
  • Degree Lower: bicentennial - relating to a 200th anniversary
  • Degree Higher: millennial - relating to a 1000th anniversary

Etymology

From Latin 'quinque' (five) + 'centum' (hundred) + English '-ennial' (recurring every specified number of years).

2. NOUN

/ kwɪnˌsɛnˈtɛniəl / (AmE), / kwɪnˌsɛnˈtɛnɪəl / (BrE)
The 500th anniversary of an event or the celebration of such an anniversary.
500th anniversary or its celebration

Examples

The city hosted a grand parade in honor of its quincentennial this year.
Scholars gathered to commemorate the quincentennial of the explorer's voyage.
Many books were published to celebrate the quincentennial of the university's founding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bicentennial - the 200th anniversary or its celebration
  • Degree Lower: centennial - the 100th anniversary or its celebration

Etymology

From Latin 'quinque' (five) and 'centum' (hundred), modeled after 'centennial'.

Synset POS: n
