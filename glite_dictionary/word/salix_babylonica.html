Salix babylonica

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks ˌbæbəˈloʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ˌbabɪˈləʊnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of willow tree with long, drooping branches and slender leaves, commonly known as the weeping willow, native to China and widely grown as an ornamental tree.
species of willow tree (weeping willow)

Examples

The Salix babylonica in their garden provided ample shade during the summer months.
Artists often depict Salix babylonica trees in tranquil park landscapes and along riverbanks.
Botanists studied the growth pattern of Salix babylonica to improve ornamental horticulture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeping willow - common name for Salix babylonica

Etymology

From Latin: Salix (willow) + babylonica (of Babylon), named from a mistaken belief that it originated in Babylon.
