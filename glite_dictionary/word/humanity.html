humanity

1. NOUN

/ hjuˈmænəti / (AmE), / hjuːˈmænɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, compassionate, and considerate towards others
the quality of being humane

Examples

The doctor treated the wounded with great humanity during the crisis.
Showing humanity to strangers can help make the world a better place.
Her humanity shone through in every generous act she performed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference to suffering or causing pain

Etymology

From Latin humanitas (human nature, kindness, courtesy), from humanus (human).

2. NOUN

/ hjuˈmænəti / (AmE), / hjuːˈmænɪti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being human
the quality of being human

Examples

Philosophers have long debated what defines our humanity.
War sometimes makes people question the limits of humanity.
Artists try to capture the essence of humanity in their work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhumanity - lack of human qualities such as compassion or mercy

Etymology

From Latin humanitas (human nature, kindness, refinement), from humanus (human).

3. NOUN

/ hjuˈmænəti / (AmE), / hjuːˈmænəti / (BrE)
all human beings collectively; the human race.
the human race; all people

Examples

Humanity faces many challenges, including climate change and inequality.
The discovery was a triumph for all of humanity, not just a single nation.
She believed that science should benefit humanity as a whole.

Synset Examples

all the world loves a lover
she always used ‘humankind’ because ‘mankind’ seemed to slight the women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mankind - all human beings collectively
  • Synonym: humankind - all the people of the world, considered as a group

Etymology

From Latin humanitas (human nature, mankind), from humanus (human).
