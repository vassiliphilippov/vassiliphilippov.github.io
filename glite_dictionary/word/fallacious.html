fallacious

1. ADJ

/ fəˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / fəˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
intended to deceive or based on a mistaken belief; misleading or deceptive.
deceptive or misleading

Examples

The argument was rejected by the jury as it was clearly fallacious in nature.
Many consumers have been misled by fallacious advertisements promising unreal results.
Relying on fallacious data can lead to poor decision making in science.

Synset Examples

deceitful advertising
fallacious testimony
smooth, shining, and deceitful as thin ice
a fraudulent scheme to escape paying taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - deliberately misleading or dishonest
  • Antonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth

Etymology

From Latin fallaciosus (deceitful), from fallacia (deceit).

2. ADJ

/ fəˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / fəˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
based on a mistaken belief or unsound reasoning; containing a logical error
based on a fallacy or mistaken belief

Examples

The lawyer quickly pointed out the fallacious logic in the witness’s statement.
Relying on fallacious evidence can lead to incorrect conclusions.
His argument was dismissed because it was clearly fallacious.

Synset Examples

fallacious reasoning
an unsound argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erroneous - incorrect; containing an error
  • Antonym: sound - based on valid reasoning or reliable evidence

Etymology

From Latin fallacia (deceit, trick), from fallax (deceptive).

3. ADJ

/ fəˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / fəˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
based on a mistaken belief or unsound reasoning; misleading or deceptive.
based on mistaken belief; misleading

Examples

Some scientists dismissed the idea as a fallacious assumption without real evidence.
His argument was criticized for its fallacious reasoning and lack of facts.
Don't be led astray by fallacious claims in advertising.

Synset Examples

fallacious hope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misleading - giving the wrong idea or impression
  • Synonym: deceptive - tending to mislead or give a false impression
  • Antonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth

Etymology

From Latin fallaciosus (deceptive), from fallacia (deceit), from fallere (to deceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00635924-s
Synset POS: s
