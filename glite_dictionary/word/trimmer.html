trimmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to thin out and trim trees and shrubs, keeping them well-shaped and healthy.
worker who trims trees and shrubs

Examples

The trimmer carefully shaped the hedges along the walkway.
We hired a trimmer to tidy up the overgrown shrubs in our garden.
During spring, the trimmer works long hours maintaining the trees at the park.

Synset Examples

untouched by the pruner's axe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pruner - a person whose job is to cut back and shape plants, especially trees and shrubs

Etymology

From the verb 'trim', meaning to make neat by cutting.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmə / (BrE)
a machine used for cutting or shaping timber or wood precisely, especially by trimming excess material
machine for trimming timber or wood

Examples

The carpenter used a trimmer to smooth the edges of the wooden boards.
Modern lumber mills rely on automatic trimmers for efficient processing of timber.
After sawing the plank, he ran it through the trimmer to achieve a perfect length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saw - a hand tool or machine used for cutting wood or other material

Etymology
