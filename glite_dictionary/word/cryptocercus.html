Cryptocercus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈsɜrkəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of wood-dwelling cockroaches found mainly in temperate forests of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of wood-dwelling cockroaches

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Cryptocercus in the Appalachian Mountains.
Unlike most cockroaches, Cryptocercus relies on wood for both food and habitat.
The life cycle of Cryptocercus is unusually long compared to other cockroach genera.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'kerkos' (tail), referring to its concealed tail structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈsɜrkəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of wood-eating cockroaches found in temperate forests, closely related to termites.
genus of wood-eating cockroaches

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Cryptocercus in the Appalachian Mountains.
Cryptocercus cockroaches play an important role in decomposing decaying wood in forests.
Scientists often compare Cryptocercus to termites because of their similar symbiotic gut bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - an insect of the order Blattodea

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'kerkos' (tail), referring to features of their anatomy.
