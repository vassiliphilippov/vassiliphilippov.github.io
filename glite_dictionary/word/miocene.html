Miocene

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.əˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.əʊˌsiːn / (BrE)
the geological epoch lasting from about 23 to 5 million years ago, characterized by the development of most modern mammal families and the expansion of grasslands
geological epoch, 23–5 million years ago

Examples

Fossil records from the Miocene provide evidence of the early evolution of many mammal species.
Many grassland ecosystems first appeared during the Miocene epoch.
Geologists have identified volcanic activity dating back to the middle Miocene.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pliocene - the geological epoch following the Miocene
  • Degree Lower: Oligocene - the geological epoch preceding the Miocene

Etymology

From Greek 'meion' (less) and 'kainos' (new), meaning 'less recent.'
