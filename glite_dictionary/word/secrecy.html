secrecy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkrəsi / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrəsi / (BrE)
the state of being kept hidden or private; the action or habit of keeping things confidential
state of being hidden or confidential

Examples

The negotiations were conducted in complete secrecy to avoid public scrutiny.
He valued his privacy and maintained secrecy about his personal life.
The organization's work is shrouded in secrecy, making it difficult to obtain information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidentiality - the quality of being private or secret
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of not hiding secrets, being transparent

Etymology

From Old French 'secrécie', from Medieval Latin 'secretia', from Latin 'secretus' (hidden).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.krə.si / (AmE), / ˈsiː.krə.si / (BrE)
The state or condition of keeping something hidden or not disclosed; the quality of being secret.
the quality of being secret

Examples

The negotiations were conducted in complete secrecy to avoid media attention.
He admired her ability to maintain secrecy even under pressure.
Companies often operate with a certain level of secrecy about new products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidentiality - the state of keeping something private or secret
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being open and transparent

Etymology

From Latin secretus (set apart, hidden).
