Sabbatarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌsæbəˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌsæbəˈteəriən / (BrE)
Relating to the Sabbath and its observance, especially in a strict or particular manner.
relating to the Sabbath observance

Examples

Some Sabbatarian churches forbid all forms of work on Sundays.
Her Sabbatarian beliefs made her reluctant to travel on the Sabbath.
The movement advocated Sabbatarian laws to protect Sunday as a day of rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sabbath - the day of religious observance and abstinence from work

Etymology

From 'Sabbath' (from Hebrew 'shabbāth', day of rest) + '-arian' (forming nouns and adjectives).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæbəˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌsæbəˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who observes Saturday as the Sabbath, especially for religious reasons, as practiced in Judaism and some Christian denominations
one who observes Saturday as the Sabbath

Examples

As a Sabbatarian, he refrained from working on Saturdays.
Many early Christians were Sabbatarians who observed the seventh day as holy.
Her strict Sabbatarian beliefs shaped her family's weekly routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sabbath - the day of religious observance and abstinence from work, observed differently in Judaism and Christianity

Etymology

From Sabbath, with the suffix -arian (one who practices or believes in), coined in the 17th century.
