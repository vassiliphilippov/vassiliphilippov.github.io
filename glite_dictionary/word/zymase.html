zymase

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.meɪs / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.meɪz / (BrE)
a complex of enzymes found in yeast and other organisms that together catalyze the process of glycolysis
enzyme complex for glycolysis
medical

Examples

Yeast cells contain zymase, which is essential for converting glucose into alcohol during fermentation.
Scientists studied the role of zymase in the breakdown of sugars within the cell.
The discovery of zymase helped advance our understanding of cellular respiration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - biological molecule that accelerates chemical reactions

Etymology

From German Zymase, coined in the late 19th century from Greek zymē (leaven, yeast) + -ase (enzyme suffix).
