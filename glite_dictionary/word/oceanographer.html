oceanographer

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊʃəˈnɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌəʊʃəˈnɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the physical and biological aspects of the oceans
scientist specializing in oceans

Examples

The oceanographer spent months at sea collecting data on marine currents.
Many oceanographers collaborate internationally to study the effects of climate change on the oceans.
As an oceanographer, she specialized in examining the chemistry of deep-sea water samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine biologist - a scientist who studies life in the oceans

Etymology

From 'ocean' + 'grapher', patterned after 'oceanography'.
