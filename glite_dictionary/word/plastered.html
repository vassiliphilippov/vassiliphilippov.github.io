plastered

1. ADJ

/ ˈplæstərd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstəd / (BrE)
(of walls) covered with a smooth layer of plaster, typically in preparation for painting or decoration.
walls coated with plaster

Examples

The living room had freshly plastered walls, ready for painting.
After renovation, the once rough stone walls were beautifully plastered.
A plastered surface provides a solid base for wallpaper.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplastered - (of walls) not covered with plaster
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈplæstɚd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstəd / (BrE)
Very drunk; heavily intoxicated by alcohol.
very drunk; intoxicated
informal slang

Examples

He was completely plastered after the party last night.
I don't remember anything from that night because I was so plastered.
They got plastered celebrating their team's big win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasted - very drunk or intoxicated
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol

Etymology

Originally from the past participle of 'plaster'; slang sense for 'drunk' dates from the late 1800s, suggesting being 'plastered' to the wall.

3. ADJ

/ ˈplæstɚd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstəd / (BrE)
(of hair) made smooth and flat by applying a sticky or glossy substance, such as pomade or gel.
hair smoothed down with substance

Examples

His dark hair was plastered back with gel for the party.
After walking in the rain, her hair became plastered to her forehead.
He preferred the classic look, with every strand plastered into place.

Synset Examples

black hair plastered with pomade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slicked - hair made smooth and flat, usually with gel or oil

Etymology

From 'plaster', in the sense of spreading or smoothing a substance over a surface.
