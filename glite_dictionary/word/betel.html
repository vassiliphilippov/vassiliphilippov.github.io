betel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈbiːtəl / (BrE)
an Asian climbing plant (Piper betle) whose leaves are chewed with betel nut by people in Southeast Asia
Asian plant chewed with betel nut

Examples

In many traditional ceremonies, betel leaves play an important symbolic role.
She offered me a betel leaf wrapped around a piece of areca nut.
The use of betel is widespread across parts of India and Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - the seed of the betel palm, chewed with betel leaves

Etymology

From Portuguese 'betel', from Malayalam 'vettila' (betel leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈbiːtəl / (BrE)
the leaf of a tropical vine (Piper betle) of Southeast Asia, commonly chewed with areca nut and other ingredients for its stimulant effect
leaf of a tropical vine chewed for stimulation

Examples

Chewing betel leaves is a common tradition in many parts of Southeast Asia.
A small parcel of betel and areca nut was offered to each guest at the ceremony.
The market stall sold fresh betel leaves alongside spices and dried fruits.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'betel', from Malayalam 'vettila' (betal leaf).
