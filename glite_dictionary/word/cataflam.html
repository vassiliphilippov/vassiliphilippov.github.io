Cataflam

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌflæm / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌflam / (BrE)
A trademarked nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (diclofenac potassium) used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation.
a brand of pain-relief medication (NSAID)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Cataflam to help with my back pain after the injury.
If you experience stomach upset while taking Cataflam, inform your healthcare provider immediately.
Some patients find Cataflam more effective than other painkillers for managing arthritis symptoms.

Related Terms
