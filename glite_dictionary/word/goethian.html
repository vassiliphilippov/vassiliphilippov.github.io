Goethian

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡoʊ.θi.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.θi.ən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe or his works
relating to Goethe or his work

Examples

Her analysis provided a distinctly Goethian perspective on the transformation of nature.
The artist's style was influenced by Goethian ideals of beauty and harmony.
Many scholars still debate the relevance of Goethian philosophy in modern literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Goethean - relating to Goethe; variant spelling

Etymology

From the name of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749–1832), German writer, poet, and philosopher, with the adjectival suffix -ian.
