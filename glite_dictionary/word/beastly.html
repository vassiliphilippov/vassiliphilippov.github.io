2. ADJ

/ ˈbiːstli / (AmE), / ˈbiːstli / (BrE)
Resembling a beast; lacking human sensibility or showing cruel or coarse qualities.
cruel or brutal; like a beast

Examples

The prisoners were subjected to beastly treatment throughout their captivity.
He displayed a beastly disregard for the feelings of others, acting with shocking cruelty.
Her beastly behavior at the party shocked everyone present.

Synset Examples

beastly desires
a bestial nature
brute force
a dull and brutish man
bestial treatment of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bestial - lacking human sensibility; relating to a beast
  • Synonym: brutish - resembling a brute; cruel or coarse
  • Antonym: humane - showing compassion or benevolence

Etymology

Formed from 'beast' with suffix '-ly', recorded in English since the 14th century.

/ ˈbiːstli / (AmE), / ˈbiːstli / (BrE)
(informal) very unpleasant or disagreeable.
very unpleasant; disagreeable
informal

Examples

She found the food in the cafeteria beastly and refused to eat it.
What a beastly day—it's been raining nonstop since dawn.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of enjoyment; nice

Etymology
