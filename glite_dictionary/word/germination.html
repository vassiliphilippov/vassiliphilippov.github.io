germination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɝː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a seed, spore, or other reproductive body begins to grow and sprout into a new organism
beginning of seed or spore growth

Examples

The germination of the seeds took place within a week after planting.
Adequate moisture and warmth are crucial for successful germination.
Scientists study the factors that influence germination rates in different plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprouting - the act of beginning to grow or bud

Etymology

From Latin 'germinationem' (sprouting, budding), from 'germinare' (to sprout).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɜrməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɜːməˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of something beginning or developing; the origin or earliest stage of a development, often used figuratively
beginning or origin of development

Examples

Her interest in science found its germination in childhood experiments in the kitchen.
The germination of new ideas often comes from unexpected conversations.
The germination of the movement can be traced back to a small group of activists.

Synset Examples

the germination of their discontent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning - the point at which something starts
  • Synonym: origin - the point where something begins, arises, or is derived

Etymology

From Latin 'germinatio', from 'germinare' (to sprout).
