Nolina microcarpa

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈliːnə ˌmaɪkroʊˈkɑːrpə / (AmE), / nəʊˈliːnə ˌmɪkrəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A stemless plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with tufts of grasslike leaves and an erect panicle of small creamy white flowers.
a plant native to southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Nolina microcarpa thrives in arid regions of Arizona and New Mexico.
Gardeners admire the unique appearance of Nolina microcarpa in xeriscape landscapes.
The creamy white flowers of Nolina microcarpa bloom in late spring.

Etymology

From the genus name Nolina and Latin microcarpa, meaning 'small-fruited'.
