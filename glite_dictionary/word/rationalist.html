rationalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who emphasizes reason and observable facts, and tends to reject metaphysical speculation or supernatural explanations
person valuing reason over metaphysics

Examples

As a proud rationalist, she refuses to accept claims without objective evidence.
The debate between the rationalist and his religious counterpart was spirited and thought-provoking.
Many scientists consider themselves rationalists who seek explanations based solely on observable data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empiricist - one who relies on experience or observation alone; often contrasted with rationalists

Etymology

From rational + -ist, originally from Latin rationalis (reasonable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəˌlɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of rationalism, especially the philosophical doctrine that reason is the chief source of knowledge
relating to rationalism (philosophy)

Examples

The university offers a course in rationalist thought and its influence on modern science.
His arguments were deeply rooted in rationalist principles, emphasizing logic over emotion.
This book provides an overview of rationalist approaches to philosophical questions.

Synset Examples

rationalist philosophy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empiricist - relating to empiricism, the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience

Etymology

From 'rational' + '-ist', influenced by French 'rationaliste'.
