Juncaceae

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋˈkeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / dʒʌŋˈkeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants commonly known as rushes, consisting of tufted herbs that resemble grasses.
family of rushes; grass-like plants

Examples

Members of the Juncaceae family are often found in wetlands and marshy areas.
The botanist identified the plant as belonging to the Juncaceae rather than the true grasses.
Unlike sedges or grasses, the Juncaceae have rounded stems and lack obvious leaf blades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a grass-like plant of the family Juncaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Juncus and the standard botanical suffix -aceae (family).
