catalexis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈlɛksɪs / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɛksɪs / (BrE)
The omission of a syllable from the final foot of a line of verse, especially in metrical poetry.
omission of final syllable in verse

Examples

The poem employs catalexis to create a subtle sense of unease at the end of each stanza.
In classical poetry, catalexis can affect the overall rhythm and musicality of a verse.
Many English hymns make deliberate use of catalexis in their metrical patterns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acatalectic - having the full number of syllables in the final foot

Etymology

From Greek katalēxis (a leaving off), from kata- (down, completely) + leipein (to leave).
