inconsideration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of failing to consider the feelings or needs of others; lack of consideration
lack of consideration for others

Examples

Her inconsideration of her neighbors made her unpopular in the building.
He apologized for his inconsideration during the meeting, admitting he should have listened more carefully.
The manager’s inconsideration for staff wellbeing led to high turnover.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consideration - thoughtfulness toward others; careful thought

Etymology

From in- (not) + consideration, modeled after 'considerate'.
