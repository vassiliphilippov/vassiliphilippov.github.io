divisional

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɪʒənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒənəl / (BrE)
serving to divide or relating to a division or separation, especially by marking a boundary or distinction.
serving to divide or mark a division

Examples

A fence acts as a divisional barrier between the two properties.
The divisional line on the map marks the border of the district.
An argument arose over the exact location of the divisional boundary.

Synset Examples

the divisional line between two states

Etymology

From 'division' + '-al', formed in English.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɪʒənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒənəl / (BrE)
relating to a military division or a specific unit within a larger organization, especially in the armed forces.
pertaining to a military division

Examples

The divisional commander addressed his troops before the operation began.
She was assigned to the divisional headquarters after receiving her promotion.
The divisional artillery played a crucial role in the success of the campaign.

Synset Examples

divisional artillery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - a large military unit or formation

Etymology

From division + -al.

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɪʒənl / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒənl / (BrE)
relating to a coin or currency that represents a fraction of the basic monetary unit; fractional.
denoting fractional currency

Examples

The U.S. Treasury once issued divisional notes during periods of coin shortage.
Collectors often seek out rare divisional coins from the nineteenth century.
British currency formerly included several divisional pieces like the farthing.

Synset Examples

American divisional (fractional) coins include the dime and the nickel
fractional currency is currency in denominations less than the basic monetary unit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fractional - relating to a part or fraction of the whole, especially of a monetary unit
