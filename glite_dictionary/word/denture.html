denture

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈdɛntʃə / (BrE)
A removable dental appliance that artificially replaces missing teeth and adjacent tissues.
artificial replacement for missing teeth
medical

Examples

She removed her denture and placed it in a glass of water before going to bed.
Proper cleaning of a denture is important to maintain oral hygiene.
After losing several teeth, he was fitted with a partial denture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false teeth - artificial teeth worn to replace missing natural teeth

Etymology

From French 'denture', from Latin 'dens' (tooth).
