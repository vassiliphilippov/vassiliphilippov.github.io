Adiantum capillus-veneris

1. NOUN

/ ˌædiˈæntəm kəˌpɪləs ˈvɛnərɪs / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈæntəm kəˌpɪləs ˈvɛnərɪs / (BrE)
a delicate species of maidenhair fern with slender, shiny black leaf stalks, found in many parts of the world
a species of delicate maidenhair fern

Examples

The shady corner of the greenhouse was filled with Adiantum capillus-veneris thriving in the moist air.
She identified the delicate black stems as belonging to Adiantum capillus-veneris during her botany fieldwork.
Gardeners often grow Adiantum capillus-veneris indoors as an ornamental houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - A type of fern with delicate, divided fronds and black stalks

Etymology

From Latin: Adiantum (the genus name, from Greek 'adiantos', meaning unwettable) and capillus-veneris ('hair of Venus').
