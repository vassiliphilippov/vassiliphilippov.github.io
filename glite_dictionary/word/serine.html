serine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriːn / (BrE)
a sweet-tasting crystalline amino acid important in the synthesis of proteins and found in many biological processes
an amino acid found in proteins
medical

Examples

Serine is crucial for the function of many enzymes in the body.
The laboratory identified an unusually high amount of serine in the sample.
Dietary proteins contain serine among other amino acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - organic compound that combines to form proteins

Etymology

From Latin 'sericum' (silk), because it was first obtained from silk protein.
