repudiation

1. NOUN

/ rəˌpjuːdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌpjuːdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of rejecting, disowning, or declaring something as invalid or unaccepted
the act of rejecting or disowning

Examples

The company's repudiation of the earlier agreement caused a major dispute.
His public repudiation of the accusations helped clear his name.
The government's repudiation of debt obligations worried international investors.

Synset Examples

Congressional repudiation of the treaty that the President had negotiated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept, use, or believe something
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of agreeing with or approving something

Etymology

From Latin repudiare (to cast off, reject), from repudium (divorce, rejection).

exposure of falseness or pretension

Examples

3. NOUN

/ rɪˌpjuːdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌpjuːdɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the refusal to acknowledge, pay, or honor a debt or contract, especially by public authorities
refusal to honor a debt or contract
legal

Examples

The government's repudiation of its loan guarantees triggered a financial crisis.
Their contract was terminated following the city's repudiation of its obligations.
The court ruled that the repudiation of the agreement was unlawful.

Synset Examples

the repudiation of the debt by the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: default - failure to fulfill a financial obligation
  • Antonym: honor - to fulfil or respect an agreement or contract

Etymology

From Latin repudiare (to cast off, reject).
