kindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnd.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate; the act or tendency to be kind and forgiving
quality of being kind

Examples

Her kindness to strangers is truly remarkable.
A simple act of kindness can brighten someone's day.
He was remembered for his kindness and generosity throughout his life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cruelty - the quality of being cruel
  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others' suffering

Etymology

From Old English 'cyndnes', from 'cynd' meaning 'kind' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnd.nɪs / (BrE)
an act of being friendly, generous, or considerate toward someone
a kind or generous act

Examples

Her unexpected kindness to the new student made him feel welcome.
A small kindness can brighten someone’s entire day.
The elderly man was moved by the kindness shown to him by strangers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodwill - friendly or helpful feeling, attitude, or act
  • Antonym: cruelty - a cruel or harsh act

Etymology

From Old English 'kyndnes' (nation, kind, nature), later 'kindness' meaning courtesy, from 'kind' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnd.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate toward others.
the quality of being kind

Examples

Her kindness to strangers made her well loved in the community.
A simple act of kindness can brighten someone's entire day.
He will never forget the kindness shown to him during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cruelty - the quality of being cruel
  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others

Etymology

From Middle English kindnesse, from 'kind' (nature, courtesy) + '-ness'.
