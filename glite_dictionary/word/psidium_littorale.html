Psidium littorale

1. NOUN

/ siˈdiːəm lɪˈtɔrəli / (AmE), / sɪˈdɪəm lɪˈtɒrəli / (BrE)
A small tropical shrub or tree of the myrtle family, native to South America, that bears small yellowish fruit known as the strawberry guava or Cattley guava.
tropical shrub/tree bearing strawberry guava fruit

Examples

The Psidium littorale in my garden produces sweet, fragrant fruit every summer.
Botanists are studying Psidium littorale for its resistance to certain plant diseases.
Local markets often sell the yellow fruit of Psidium littorale as a delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawberry guava - common name for Psidium littorale and its fruit
  • Degree Higher: guava - tropical fruit, general term for species in the Psidium genus

Etymology

From Latin 'Psidium' (the guava genus) and 'littorale' (of the shore).
