swampy

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɑmpi / (AmE), / ˈswɒmpi / (BrE)
(of land or soil) soft, wet, and often difficult to walk on, like a swamp.
soft, wet, swamp-like (of land)

Examples

The field became swampy after several days of heavy rain.
We slogged through the swampy area, our boots sinking into the wet ground.
The trail was too swampy to follow, so we had to find another route.

Synset Examples

the ground was boggy under foot
a marshy coastline
miry roads
wet mucky lowland
muddy barnyard
quaggy terrain
the sloughy edge of the pond
swampy bayous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marshy - (of land) resembling a marsh; wet and soft
  • Synonym: boggy - (of ground) wet and spongy; like a bog
  • Antonym: dry - (of land) not wet or moist

Etymology

From "swamp" + "-y", meaning "resembling a swamp".
