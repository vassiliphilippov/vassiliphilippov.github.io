triple-spacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl ˌspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəl ˌspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of typing or formatting text with two blank lines between each line of writing.
text formatting with two blank lines

Examples

To comply with the professor's request, every essay must use triple-spacing throughout the document.
Older style manuals sometimes required triple-spacing instead of double-spacing for clarity.
He adjusted the formatting to triple-spacing to make space for handwritten comments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double-spacing - typing that leaves one line blank between lines of typing
