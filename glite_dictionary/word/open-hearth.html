open-hearth

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊ.pənˈhɑrθ / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.pənˈhɑːθ / (BrE)
relating to or produced by the open-hearth process, a method used in steelmaking involving a reverberatory furnace
relating to the open-hearth process

Examples

The plant specializes in producing open-hearth steel for industrial customers.
Many old bridges were constructed using open-hearth iron due to its strength.
With the decline of open-hearth furnaces, newer steelmaking techniques became dominant.

Synset Examples

open-hearth steel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Bessemer - relating to the Bessemer process of steelmaking
  • Antonym: basic oxygen - relating to the basic oxygen process of steelmaking

Etymology

From 'open' + 'hearth'; refers to the open-hearth furnace used in steel production, a method developed in the 19th century.
