to replant

1. VERB

/ riˈplænt / (AmE), / riˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to plant (something) again or in a new place, especially after removal or disturbance
to plant again or anew

Examples

After the storm, farmers had to replant many of their crops to ensure a good harvest.
The gardener decided to replant the flowers in a sunnier spot for better growth.
It is important to replant trees after logging to maintain a healthy forest ecosystem.

Synset Examples

They replanted the land
He replanted the seedlings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - to put (a seed, bulb, or plant) in the ground so that it can grow

Etymology

From re- (again) + plant, first recorded in late Middle English.
