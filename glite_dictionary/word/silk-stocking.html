silk-stocking

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlk ˌstɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlk ˌstɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
associated with the wealthy, privileged, or elite, especially in terms of class or influence
associated with the wealthy elite

Examples

The city council is controlled by a group of silk-stocking politicians.
Her silk-stocking upbringing influenced her attitude toward money.
The charity moved away from its silk-stocking image to appeal to a broader community.

Synset Examples

The silk-stocking luxury brand opened a new boutique downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocratic - belonging to or typical of the aristocracy; noble or high-born
  • Synonym: blue-blooded - having noble or aristocratic ancestry
  • Antonym: working-class - relating to people whose social or economic status depends on their physical work

Etymology

Originally from the literal use referring to the fine silk stockings worn by upper-class people.
