to aggroup

1. VERB

/ əˈgruːp / (AmE), / əˈɡruːp / (BrE)
to bring together or form into a group; to cluster or assemble
to form into a group
archaic

Examples

The students aggrouped themselves by interests for the project.
The trees naturally aggroup in clusters along the riverbank.
In her painting, the figures are aggrouped around the central fountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group - to arrange or form into a group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or break up a group

Etymology

From French 'agrouper', from 'a-' (to) + 'grouper' (to group).
