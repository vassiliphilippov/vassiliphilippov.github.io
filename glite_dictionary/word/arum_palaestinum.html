Arum palaestinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛərəm ˌpæl.aɪˈstiː.nəm / (AmE), / ˈɑː.rəm ˌpæl.iˈstiː.nəm / (BrE)
A perennial ornamental plant native to the Middle East, cultivated for its dark purple spathe.
ornamental Middle Eastern plant

Examples

The Arum palaestinum is often grown in gardens for its striking dark purple flowers.
Researchers are studying medicinal uses of Arum palaestinum in traditional Middle Eastern remedies.
The unique appearance of Arum palaestinum makes it a rare but desirable addition to botanical collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black calla - another common name for Arum palaestinum

Etymology

From Latin: 'arum' (a genus of flowering plants) and 'palaestinum' (Palestine; referring to its geographic origin).
