Coventry

1. PROPN

/ ˈkʌvəntri / (AmE), / ˈkʌvəntri / (BrE)
A city in central England known for its historical significance, industrial heritage, and as the home of Lady Godiva; heavily bombed during World War II.
city in central England

Examples

During the Second World War, Coventry was heavily damaged by aerial bombardment.
The legend of Lady Godiva is still associated with the city of Coventry.
Many automotive companies originated in Coventry during the industrial revolution.

Etymology

From Old English *Cofantreo*, early name for the settlement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvəntri / (AmE), / ˈkɒvəntri / (BrE)
a state of being deliberately excluded or ostracized by general agreement within a group or society
state of being ostracized
archaic

Examples

He was sent to Coventry by his colleagues for breaking the team's trust.
After the scandal, the politician found himself in social Coventry, ignored by former allies.
Her constant gossiping eventually led to her quiet Coventry within the workplace.

Synset Examples

the association should get rid of its elderly members — not by euthanasia, of course, but by Coventry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostracism - exclusion from a group or society

Etymology

From the English city of Coventry; origin possibly from the perceived hostility of its residents during the English Civil War, leading to the expression 'send to Coventry'.
