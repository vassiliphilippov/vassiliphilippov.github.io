Polybotrya cervina

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈbɑːtriə sərˈvaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɒtrɪə səˈviːnə / (BrE)
a tropical American terrestrial fern with leathery, lance-shaped fronds, sometimes classified within the genus Polybotrya
a species of tropical American fern

Examples

The lush growth of Polybotrya cervina was a highlight of the rainforest exhibit.
Botanists documented the presence of Polybotrya cervina in several protected reserves.
Collectors admire Polybotrya cervina for its striking, leathery fronds.

Etymology

Polybotrya from Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'botrys' (cluster), referring to the frond structure; 'cervina' from Latin for 'of a deer', referencing the shape or texture.
