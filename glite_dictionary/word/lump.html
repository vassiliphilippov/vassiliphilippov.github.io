lump

1. NOUN

/ lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
a large piece of a substance that does not have a definite or regular shape
a large shapeless piece

Examples

She dropped a lump of sugar into her tea.
The miner found a lump of gold in the riverbed.
We bought a big lump of cheese for the picnic.

Synset Examples

a hunk of bread
a lump of coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chunk - a thick, solid piece cut or broken off something; synonym of 'lump' in this sense
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09330206-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
a compact mass of a substance, especially one without a definite or regular shape
a compact mass of something

Examples

He found a lump of clay on the playground.
Add a lump of sugar to your tea, if you like it sweet.
There was a mysterious lump in her mashed potatoes.

Synset Examples

a ball of mud caught him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clump - a compact group or mass, especially of something soft or wet
  • Synonym: mass - a body of matter with no definite shape
  • Synonym: blob - a drop or small lump of a thick liquid or viscous substance

Etymology

From Middle English lumpe (small mass), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07977630-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
to put or group things together indiscriminately as if they are alike, especially without considering differences
group together indiscriminately

Examples

It's unfair to lump all teenagers together when every individual is different.
The report tends to lump minor offenses with serious crimes.
Some critics lump modern art with abstract art, though they are not the same.

Synset Examples

lump together all the applicants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not put together
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
an awkward or stupid person, especially someone who is clumsy or slow-witted
awkward or stupid person
informal

Examples

Stop being such a lump and help me with these boxes.
He felt like a lump when everyone else solved the puzzle so quickly.
Don’t just sit there like a lump—do something!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clod - a dull or stupid person
  • Antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intelligence

5. NOUN

/ lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
a swelling or abnormal protuberance on the body, usually felt under the skin
an abnormal swelling or protuberance

Examples

She found a small lump in her neck and decided to see a doctor.
The lump on his shoulder turned out to be harmless after medical tests.
After bumping his head, a painful lump appeared above his eyebrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bump - a swelling or raised area, often minor
  • Degree Higher: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue
  • Degree Lower: nodule - a small rounded lump or swelling
Source: WordNet

6. VERB

/ lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
to group or put things together without distinguishing their individual characteristics; to treat as a single unit
put things together as a group

Examples

The report tends to lump all minor offenses together with serious crimes.
Critics argue that the data should not be lumped into a single category.
People often lump these issues together, but they are actually quite different.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group - to put or place into groups; to arrange things together based on shared qualities
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; to divide into groups or categories
