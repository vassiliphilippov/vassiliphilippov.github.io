hammer-headed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæmər ˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈhamə ˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Unreasonably stubborn or obstinate in attitude or behavior.
stubborn; obstinate

Examples

Even when everyone disagreed, he remained hammer-headed about his point of view.
Her hammer-headed refusal to listen frustrated the entire team.
He can be so hammer-headed that no amount of reasoning will change his mind.

Synset Examples

She called him hammer-headed for his stubbornness in discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion
  • Synonym: stubborn - refusing to change one's attitude
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas

Etymology

Formed from 'hammer' + 'headed', by analogy to other similar compounds like 'pigheaded'.
