preamble

1. NOUN

/ priˈæm.bəl / (AmE), / priˈæm.bəl / (BrE)
an introductory statement, especially to a statute or constitution, that explains its purpose and underlying philosophy
introductory statement to a law or constitution

Examples

The US Constitution begins with a famous preamble that outlines its purposes.
Every new statute in that country includes a preamble explaining the background.
The committee argued over the wording of the preamble for several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the opening section that leads into a document or speech

Etymology

From Latin 'preambulum' (a walking before), from 'prae-' (before) + 'ambulare' (to walk).

2. VERB

/ ˈpriˌæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌæmbəl / (BrE)
to make a preliminary introduction, especially to a formal statement or document
introduce formally or preliminarily

Examples

The chairman chose to preamble the document with a short statement about its significance.
She decided to preamble her remarks with a quote from the Constitution.
They often preamble official reports with background information for clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introduce - to bring something into use or operation for the first time

Etymology

From Latin 'preambulare' (to walk before), via 'preambulum'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00903907-v
Synset POS: v
