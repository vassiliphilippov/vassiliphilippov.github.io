tiresome

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪərsəm / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəsəm / (BrE)
Causing boredom, annoyance, or mental fatigue; uninteresting in a way that makes one feel weary.
causing boredom or annoyance

Examples

His constant complaining became extremely tiresome after a while.
The meeting was so tiresome that several people struggled to stay awake.
I find housework especially tiresome when I'm already tired from work.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; tedious or dull
  • Antonym: stimulating - encouraging interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Middle English tiresom (tedious), formed from 'tire' (to exhaust) + '-some'.
