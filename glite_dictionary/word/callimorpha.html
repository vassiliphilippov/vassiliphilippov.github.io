Callimorpha

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈmɔrfə / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Erebidae, commonly known as cinnabar moths.
a genus of moths (cinnabar moths)

Examples

The scientist collected several Callimorpha specimens to study their mating habits.
Callimorpha are easily recognized by their striking red and black wing markings.
Many gardeners welcome Callimorpha because their caterpillars help control ragwort weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnabar moth - a brightly colored red and black moth of the genus Callimorpha

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + morphē (form), referring to the moths' attractive appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.ɪˈmɔːr.fə / (AmE), / ˌkæl.ɪˈmɔː.fə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored moths known as cinnabar moths, belonging to the family Erebidae.
genus of cinnabar moths

Examples

The Callimorpha genus is well known for its brightly colored cinnabar moths.
Researchers studied the life cycle of several Callimorpha species in their natural habitat.
Many gardeners recognize Callimorpha for its striking appearance and role in controlling ragwort populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnabar moth - A brightly colored moth of the species Callimorpha jacobaeae

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'morphē' (form, shape).
