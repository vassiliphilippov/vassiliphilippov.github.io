geezer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡizər / (AmE), / ˈɡiːzə / (BrE)
an old man, especially one who is rather eccentric or unusual
an eccentric or old man
informal

Examples

Everyone at the pub knows the old geezer who tells wild stories.
Some people think he’s just an eccentric geezer, but he’s actually very wise.
A group of young men helped the confused geezer cross the busy street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: codger - an old man, especially one who is eccentric
  • Synonym: fogey - an old-fashioned or eccentric person, especially an old man

Etymology

Probably from English dialectal 'geyser', meaning 'odd person', possibly from 'geyser' (Icelandic hot spring) via sense of explosiveness or unpredictability.
