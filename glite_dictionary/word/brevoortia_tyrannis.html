Brevoortia tyrannis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɛv.uˈɔr.ti.ə taɪˈræn.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrɛv.uˈɔː.ti.ə taɪˈran.ɪs / (BrE)
A North American marine fish, commonly known as the Atlantic menhaden, used for fish meal, oil, and fertilizer.
Atlantic menhaden fish species

Examples

Large schools of Brevoortia tyrannis are often seen along the Atlantic coast during summer.
The fishing industry relies on Brevoortia tyrannis for high-quality fish oil and meal.
Scientists are studying Brevoortia tyrannis to understand its role in coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menhaden - a common name for Brevoortia tyrannis and related species used for fish meal and oil

Etymology

From the genus name Brevoortia, named after J. Carson Brevoort, and species name tyrannis, from Latin meaning 'of a tyrant'.
