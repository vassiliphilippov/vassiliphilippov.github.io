means

1. NOUN

/ minz / (AmE), / miːnz / (BrE)
a method, instrument, or resource used to achieve a result or accomplish an end
method or resource for achieving something

Examples

Education is often considered a means to personal and professional development.
The Internet has become a powerful means of communication worldwide.
They found an effective means to reduce energy consumption in the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a particular procedure for accomplishing or approaching something
  • Antonym: end - the result or outcome that is desired or sought

Etymology

From Old English 'mænan' (to mean, intend), influenced by Old French 'moien' (means, middle).

2. NOUN

/ minz / (AmE), / miːnz / (BrE)
wealth or financial resources; the money or capital that someone possesses, especially in significant amount.
wealth; substantial financial resources

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally accumulated the means to travel the world.
He comes from a family of considerable means and influence.
Philanthropists often use their means to support charitable causes.

Synset Examples

he is a man of means

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - abundance of valuable possessions or money
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Middle English 'menes', plural of 'mean' (that by which something is accomplished), from Old French 'moyen', from Latin 'medianus'.

3. NOUN

/ minz / (AmE), / miːnz / (BrE)
a method, instrument, or process by which something is accomplished or achieved
method or way of achieving something

Examples

Education is often considered a means to a better life.
The company found a new means of increasing productivity without hiring more staff.
For many, public transport is the only means of commuting to work.

Synset Examples

a means of control
an example is the best agency of instruction
the true way to success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a way of doing something, especially a systematic one
  • Synonym: way - a means or manner of doing something
  • Antonym: end - the final result or objective; often an antonym in the phrase 'means to an end'

Etymology

From Old French *meien* (means, intermediary), from Latin *medianus* (of the middle).
