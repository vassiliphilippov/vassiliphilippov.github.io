Abies fraseri

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ˈfreɪ.zəɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.biː.iːz ˈfreɪ.zər.i / (BrE)
A small, fast-growing but short-lived species of fir native to the southern Appalachian Mountains, similar to balsam fir but with very short leaves.
a species of fir tree from the southern Appalachians

Examples

The Abies fraseri is often used as a Christmas tree in the United States.
Researchers are studying the decline of Abies fraseri populations in the wild.
The needles of Abies fraseri are notably shorter than those of related fir species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam fir - a North American fir tree closely related to Abies fraseri

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Abies' meaning fir, species named for botanist John Fraser.
