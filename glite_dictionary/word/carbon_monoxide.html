carbon monoxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrbən məˈnɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən məˈnɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless, and highly poisonous gas produced by the incomplete combustion of carbon-containing materials
poisonous gas from burning carbon

Examples

A faulty heater can give off dangerous amounts of carbon monoxide.
Long-term exposure to carbon monoxide can cause serious health problems.
They installed a detector to warn of any carbon monoxide leaks in the house.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: carbon dioxide - a colorless, odorless gas produced by complete combustion of carbon

Etymology

From Latin 'carbo' (charcoal) and 'monoxide' meaning one oxygen atom.
