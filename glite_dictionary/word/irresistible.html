irresistible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪrɪˈzɪstəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈzɪstəbl̩ / (BrE)
so attractive, appealing, or powerful that it cannot be resisted
impossible to resist

Examples

The freshly baked bread looked absolutely irresistible to everyone in the kitchen.
He found her charm irresistible, despite his attempts to stay focused.
The puppy's pleading eyes were simply irresistible to the children.

Synset Examples

irresistible (or resistless) impulses
what happens when an irresistible force meets an immovable object?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistible - able to be resisted
  • Synonym: compelling - having a powerful and irresistible effect

Etymology

From Latin 'irresistibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'resistere' (to resist).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪrəˈzɪstəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈzɪstəbl̩ / (BrE)
so attractive, appealing, or compelling that it is impossible to resist or refuse
impossible to resist due to attraction

Examples

Her irresistible charm made her the center of attention at every gathering.
The dessert was so irresistible that he had to have a second helping.
He found the offer simply irresistible, despite his initial hesitation.

Synset Examples

irresistible beauty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistible - able to be resisted or refused
  • Synonym: compelling - so forceful or attractive as to demand attention

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + resistere (to resist) via French irrésistible.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00169813-s
Synset POS: s
