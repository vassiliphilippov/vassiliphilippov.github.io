urbanisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɝː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɜː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process by which towns and cities grow as more people begin living and working in central areas, leading to an increased proportion of the population living in urban environments.
the growth of cities and urban life

Examples

The rapid urbanisation of developing countries has strained infrastructure and public services.
As urbanisation increases, so does the demand for affordable housing in big cities.
Environmental issues often arise as a consequence of extensive urbanisation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanization - alternative American spelling of 'urbanisation'
  • Antonym: ruralisation - the process of making an area more rural or less urban

Etymology

From Latin 'urbanus' (of a city) + English suffix '-isation', via French 'urbanisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɝː.bə.nɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɜː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition of an area or population becoming more urban, especially through the growth of cities and towns
process of becoming more urban

Examples

The rapid urbanisation of the region has led to significant changes in local lifestyles.
Countries experiencing urbanisation often face challenges such as overcrowding and pollution.
Government policies can influence both the rate and the pattern of urbanisation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanization - alternate spelling; especially common in American English

Etymology

Formed from 'urban' + '-isation', ultimately from Latin 'urbanus' (of a city).
