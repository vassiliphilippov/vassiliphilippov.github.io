to reciprocate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈsɪprəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkeɪt / (BrE)
to respond to an action, feeling, or gesture with a similar one; to give, feel, or do in return
respond in kind; give or feel in return

Examples

She felt gratitude and tried to reciprocate his kindness with a thoughtful gift.
When he smiled at her, she was happy to reciprocate with a warm smile of her own.
If you help your colleagues, they are more likely to reciprocate when you need assistance.

Synset Examples

We always invite the neighbors and they never reciprocate!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to give back or do something in response
  • Synonym: to repay - to give back something in compensation or thanks

Etymology

From Latin reciprocatus, past participle of reciprocare (to move back and forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02377417-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈsɪprəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkeɪt / (BrE)
(of a part or device) to move alternately back and forth in a straight line; to cause such motion.
move or cause to move back and forth

Examples

The piston in the engine will reciprocate hundreds of times a minute.
A crankshaft is used to reciprocate the saw blade.
The mechanism allows the lever to reciprocate smoothly within its housing.

Synset Examples

the engine reciprocates the propeller

Etymology

From Latin reciprocatus, past participle of reciprocare (to move back and forth).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
