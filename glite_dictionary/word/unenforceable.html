unenforceable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˌɪnˈfɔrsəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnɪnˈfɔːsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a law, contract, or rule) not able to be enforced or made effective by legal or other means.
not able to be enforced

Examples

The new regulation was declared unenforceable by the court.
Many critics argue that the policy is unenforceable in practice.
Because of its vague language, the contract proved to be unenforceable.

Synset Examples

an unenforceable law
unenforceable reforms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enforceable - able to be enforced or imposed by law or authority

Etymology

From un- (not) + enforceable, from Old French enforcier (to force).
