Gynura

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈnjʊrə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Old World herbs in the daisy family, known as velvet plants, often grown for their ornamental foliage.
genus of tropical velvet plants

Examples

The Gynura genus includes species like Gynura aurantiaca, prized for their purple-tinged leaves.
Gardeners often cultivate Gynura as ornamental houseplants due to their attractive foliage.
Some varieties of Gynura are used in traditional herbal medicine in Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'gyne' (woman) and 'oura' (tail), referring to aspects of the plant's appearance.
