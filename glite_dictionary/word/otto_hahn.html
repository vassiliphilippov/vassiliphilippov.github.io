Otto Hahn

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtoʊ hɑn / (AmE), / ˈɒtəʊ hɑːn / (BrE)
(1879–1968) A German chemist famous for co-discovering nuclear fission, sharing this work with Lise Meitner.
German chemist, co-discoverer of nuclear fission

Examples

Otto Hahn received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on nuclear fission.
Many historians credit Otto Hahn and Lise Meitner with making one of the greatest scientific breakthroughs of the 20th century.
The laboratory in Berlin where Otto Hahn worked became famous for its groundbreaking research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lise Meitner - Austrian-Swedish physicist who co-discovered nuclear fission
