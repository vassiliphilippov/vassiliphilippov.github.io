North Carolina

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ kəˈraɪlɪnə / (AmE), / nɔːθ kəˈraɪlɪnə / (BrE)
a state in the southeastern United States, one of the original thirteen colonies, known for its Atlantic beaches and the Blue Ridge Mountains
state in southeastern United States

Examples

She moved to North Carolina to start a new job in Raleigh.
The Blue Ridge Parkway is a popular scenic road in North Carolina.
Basketball is a beloved sport in North Carolina, especially at the college level.

Etymology

Named after King Charles I of England; from Latin 'Carolus' meaning 'Charles'.

2. PROPN

/ nɔrθ kəˈraɪlɪnə / (AmE), / nɔːθ kəˈraɪlɪnə / (BrE)
one of the original British colonies in North America that later became a U.S. state, historically with capitals including Bath, Edenton, Brunswick Town, and New Bern
historical British colony; now a U.S. state

Examples

In the 18th century, North Carolina was known for its tobacco plantations and growing port cities.
Many historical documents refer to North Carolina as having several capitals before Raleigh.
After the Revolution, North Carolina became the twelfth state to join the Union.

Etymology

Named after King Charles I of England; 'Carolus' is Latin for Charles.
