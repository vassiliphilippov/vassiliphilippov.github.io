salary

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæləri / (AmE), / ˈsæləri / (BrE)
A fixed regular payment, typically paid monthly or biweekly, made by an employer to an employee, especially a professional or office worker.
fixed regular payment for employment

Examples

She negotiated a higher salary before accepting the job offer.
His annual salary allows him to comfortably support his family.
Due to budget cuts, the company froze all salary increases this year.

Synset Examples

wages were paid by cash
he wasted his pay on drink
they saved a quarter of all their earnings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wage - payment for work, usually calculated by the hour, day, or week
  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments
  • Degree Higher: bonus - an extra payment in addition to the regular salary or wage
  • Synonym: stipend - a fixed regular sum paid as a salary or allowance

Etymology

From Latin 'salarium' (originally a payment made for salt), via Old French 'salaire'.
