reinsurance

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˈʃʊə.rəns / (BrE)
insurance purchased by an insurance company from another company in order to share or mitigate risk for certain policies or portfolios.
insurance for insurance companies to share risk

Examples

Reinsurance can help insurance companies cover exceptionally large or risky policies.
The company sought reinsurance to minimize its exposure to hurricane-related claims.
Without reinsurance, a single disaster could bankrupt a small insurer.

Synset Examples

reinsurance enables a client to get coverage that would be too great for any one company to assume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance - arrangement providing compensation for specified loss or damage

Etymology

From re- + insurance; first recorded in the mid-19th century.
