Scomber scombrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɒmbər ˈskɒmbrəs / (AmE), / ˈskɒmbə ˈskɒmbrəs / (BrE)
A species of mackerel found in the northern Atlantic and Mediterranean, valued as a food fish; its body is greenish-blue with dark bars and few or no scales.
a species of Atlantic mackerel

Examples

Fishermen in the North Sea often catch Scomber scombrus during the summer months.
The distinct markings of Scomber scombrus help scientists identify it among other mackerel species.
Grilled Scomber scombrus is a popular dish in many Mediterranean countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a species of edible fish in the Scombridae family

Etymology

From Latin, where 'scomber' means 'mackerel'.
