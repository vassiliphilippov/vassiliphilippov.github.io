efficacious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛfəˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɛfɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
having the power to produce the intended effect or result, especially in a striking or effective manner
having the power to produce the intended effect

Examples

The new drug proved to be highly efficacious in treating the disease.
Only the most efficacious remedies were used by the doctor.
Her efficacious management style led to significant improvements in the company's performance.

Synset Examples

an air-cooled motor was more effective than a witch's broomstick for rapid long-distance transportation
effective teaching methods
effective steps toward peace
made an effective entrance
his complaint proved to be effectual in bringing action
an efficacious law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effective - successful in producing the intended result
  • Antonym: ineffective - not producing the desired effect

Etymology

From Latin efficax, efficacis (effectual, powerful).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛfəˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɛfɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having the power to produce the desired effect; effective.
effective in producing a desired result

Examples

This treatment has proven to be efficacious in reducing symptoms of the disease.
The committee implemented an efficacious plan to improve workplace productivity.
Her speech was more efficacious than expected, convincing many skeptics.

Synset Examples

written propaganda is less efficacious than the habits and prejudices … of the readers
the medicine is efficacious in stopping a cough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effective - Successful in producing a desired or intended result
  • Antonym: ineffective - Not producing any significant or intended effect

Etymology

From Latin 'efficax, efficacis' (powerful, effective), from 'efficere' (to accomplish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00842395-a
Synset POS: a
