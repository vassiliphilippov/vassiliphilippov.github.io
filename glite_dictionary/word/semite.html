Semite

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsiːmaɪt / (BrE)
of or relating to the Semites, a group of peoples originating in the Middle East and North Africa, or their languages and cultures
relating to Semites or their culture

Examples

The ancient Akkadian language is classified as a Semite language.
Artifacts with Semite origins have been found across the region.
Many Semite traditions are still practiced today in various forms.

Synset Examples

Semite peoples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Semitic - relating to Semites, especially their languages

Etymology

From Latin 'Semita', from Greek 'Sēm' (Shem), son of Noah, considered ancestor of Semitic peoples in biblical tradition.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03127361-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsiːmaɪt / (BrE)
A member of any of the peoples who speak or spoke Semitic languages, especially of the Middle East and northern Africa.
member of Semitic-speaking peoples

Examples

The ancient Assyrians were considered Semites due to their language and culture.
Many Semites migrated across the Middle East thousands of years ago.
The word 'Semite' refers not only to Jews but also to Arabs and other peoples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Semitic - relating to a family of languages including Hebrew and Arabic

Etymology

From French 'sémite', from Modern Latin 'Semita', from Semitic 'Shem', name of a biblical figure.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09660898-n
Synset POS: n
