Limousin

1. PROPN

/ ˌliːmuˈzæ̃ / (AmE), / ˌliːmuˈzæ̃ / (BrE)
a region in central France, located to the west of the Auvergne mountains, known for its rural landscape and historical significance
region of central France

Examples

The rolling hills of Limousin are famous for their lush green pastures and traditional stone houses.
She spent her summer holiday exploring the quiet villages of Limousin.
Many consider Limousin to be one of the best places in France for authentic countryside experiences.
