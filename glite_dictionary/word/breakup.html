breakup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkʌp / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkʌp / (BrE)
the act or process of separating into parts or pieces; a division or disintegration.
separation into parts; disintegration

Examples

The breakup of the ice on the river marked the start of spring.
After years of tension, the breakup of the company was inevitable.
Scientists studied the breakup of the asteroid as it entered Earth's atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart
  • Synonym: disintegration - the process of coming to pieces
  • Antonym: union - the action of joining together

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkʌp / (BrE)
the act or process of ending a relationship or association, especially between romantic partners or groups
ending of a relationship

Examples

After their breakup, they found it difficult to stay friends.
The breakup of the alliance led to increased tensions in the region.
She struggled to move on after her recent breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart
  • Synonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage
  • Antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'break up', meaning to separate or dissolve, used as a noun since the early 20th century.
