frankness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋknəs / (AmE), / ˈfræŋknəs / (BrE)
The quality of being open, honest, and direct in speech or expression; straightforwardness.
openness and straightforwardness

Examples

His frankness made the meeting more productive because everyone knew where they stood.
She admired his frankness, even when his opinions were difficult to hear.
Sometimes frankness can be mistaken for rudeness if not delivered carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest; frankness
  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; truthfulness
  • Antonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues; opposite of blunt frankness

Etymology

From Old French 'franche', from Latin 'Francus' (Frankish, free).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋknəs / (AmE), / ˈfraŋknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being open, honest, and direct in speech or attitude, often without hesitation or reserve
openness and honesty in speech or attitude

Examples

Her frankness during the meeting surprised everyone and led to an open discussion.
I appreciated his frankness when he explained the situation so honestly.
Sometimes, too much frankness can be mistaken for rudeness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest; frankness
  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; truthfulness
  • Antonym: evasion - the act of avoiding giving a direct answer or opinion

Etymology

From Old French *franc* (free, open, sincere), from Medieval Latin *Francus*.
