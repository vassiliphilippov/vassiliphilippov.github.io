groats

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊts / (AmE), / ɡrəʊts / (BrE)
Hulled and crushed grains of cereals, such as oats, wheat, or barley, often used as food.
hulled and crushed cereal grains

Examples

The recipe calls for a cup of groats to create a hearty porridge.
Steel-cut oats are a type of groats that retain much of their nutritional value.
After harvesting, the barley was processed into groats for use in soups and stews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain - small hard seeds of food plants such as wheat, rice, or oats

Etymology

From Middle English *grot*, of Old English origin; related to Dutch *groot* (‘coarse’).
