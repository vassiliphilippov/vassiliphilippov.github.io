lunchroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌntʃˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈlʌntʃˌruːm / (BrE)
a room or small restaurant, especially in a school or workplace, where lunch is served or can be purchased
room or restaurant for serving lunch

Examples

All the students gathered in the lunchroom for their midday meal.
Our office lunchroom was recently renovated with more seating and new appliances.
She forgot her wallet and couldn't buy anything in the lunchroom today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - a restaurant in which customers serve themselves or are served at a counter and take food to tables

Etymology

From 'lunch' + 'room', first used in the late 19th century in American English.
