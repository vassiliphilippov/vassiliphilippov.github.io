quickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of moving or proceeding rapidly; speed
speed; rapid movement or response

Examples

Her quickness on the tennis court impressed all the spectators.
The quickness of his response showed that he was paying attention.
Good drivers react with quickness in dangerous situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
  • Antonym: slowness - the quality of being slow or lacking speed

Etymology

From 'quick' + '-ness', Old English 'cwic' (alive, lively).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to think and respond rapidly, especially showing intelligence or mental agility
ability to think and respond rapidly

Examples

Her quickness of mind impressed everyone during the debate.
The comedian's quickness allows him to make witty remarks on the spot.
Interviewers value quickness in candidates who must solve problems in real time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - nimbleness of mind or body
  • Antonym: slowness - the quality of being slow to think, move, or respond

Etymology

From Middle English quiknesse, from Old English cwicnes (liveliness, vitality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɪknəs / (BrE)
the ability to perform a skill easily and efficiently, often with speed and agility
skillful or agile ability, ease of performance

Examples

Her quickness on the piano earned her first place in the competition.
You could see the quickness of his mind as he solved the puzzle in seconds.
The team's victory was due in part to the goalkeeper's quickness and sharp reflexes.

Synset Examples

his quick adeptness was a product of good design
he was famous for his facility as an archer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adeptness - high level of skill or proficiency
  • Antonym: slowness - lack of speed or agility in action

Etymology

From Middle English 'quiknesse', from Old English 'cwicness' (liveliness).
