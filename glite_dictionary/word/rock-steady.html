rock-steady

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑk ˌstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌstɛdi / (BrE)
consistently reliable or dependable, especially in behavior or performance
consistently reliable or dependable

Examples

She is a rock-steady presence in times of crisis.
His rock-steady approach reassured the team.
Despite the challenges, her performance remained rock-steady throughout the year.

Synset Examples

dependable in one's habits
a steady-going family man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be trusted or depended on

Etymology

Formed from 'rock' (suggesting firmness or stability) and 'steady.'
