absolvitory

1. ADJ

/ əbˈzɑːlvəˌtɔri / (AmE), / əbˈzɒlvɪtəri / (BrE)
providing absolution; serving to absolve or forgive someone of guilt or wrongdoing
providing absolution

Examples

The priest pronounced the absolvitory words, granting forgiveness to the penitent.
In certain legal systems, an absolvitory verdict means the accused is fully cleared of all charges.
The ritual ended with an absolvitory blessing given to all participants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutory - serving to absolve or clear from blame or guilt

Etymology

From Medieval Latin absolutorius, from absolvere (to absolve).
