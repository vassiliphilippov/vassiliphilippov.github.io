caespitose

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛspɪˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈsɛspɪtəʊs / (BrE)
(of plants) growing in small, dense clumps or tufts.
growing in dense clumps (plants)

Examples

The grass species is caespitose, forming dense tufts across the hillside.
Botanists identified the caespitose growth pattern in the new alpine plant.
Many ornamental grasses are valued for their neat, caespitose habit in garden borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufted - (of plants) growing in tufts or clumps
  • Antonym: solitary - (of plants) growing singly, not in groups or tufts

Etymology

From Latin caespitosus (full of turf, turfy).
