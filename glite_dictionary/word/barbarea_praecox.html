Barbarea praecox

1. PROPN

/ bɑrˈbɛəriə preɪˈkɑks / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɛəriə priːˈkɒks / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to southwestern Europe and cultivated in Florida, belonging to the genus Barbarea.
species of flowering plant

Examples

Barbarea praecox is often found in the wild in southwestern Europe.
Gardeners in Florida sometimes cultivate Barbarea praecox for its attractive yellow flowers.
The leaves of Barbarea praecox resemble those of other plants in the mustard family.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Barbarea' (the genus, named after St. Barbara) and 'praecox' meaning 'early' or 'precocious'.
