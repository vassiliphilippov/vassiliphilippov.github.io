hermeneutic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɜːrməˈnuːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɜːməˈnjuːtɪk / (BrE)
relating to interpretation, especially of texts or symbolic systems; interpretive or explanatory.
interpretive; explanatory

Examples

The scholar applied a hermeneutic approach to analyze the ancient manuscript.
Her argument relied on a hermeneutic framework to interpret the symbolism in the artwork.
Modern philosophers often debate the role of hermeneutic methods in understanding historical texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretive - serving to explain or provide an interpretation
  • Synonym: explanatory - intended to explain or make clear

Etymology

From Greek hermēneutikos (interpretive), from hermēneuein (to interpret).
