wondrous

1. ADV

/ ˈwʌndrəs / (AmE), / ˈwʌndrəs / (BrE)
Excellently or remarkably; used as an intensifier, especially in literary or poetic contexts.
remarkably; excellently (intensifier)

Examples

The children sang wondrous sweetly in the choir.
She played the piano with a wondrous skill that captivated everyone.
The garden bloomed wondrous bright in the morning sunlight.

Synset Examples

her voice is superbly disciplined
the colors changed wondrously slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonderfully - in a wonderful or excellent manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'wonderous', from 'wonder' + '-ous'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʌndrəs / (AmE), / ˈwʌndrəs / (BrE)
extraordinarily good, impressive, or remarkable; inspiring wonder
extraordinarily good or impressive

Examples

The wondrous colors of the sunset left everyone speechless.
She told a wondrous story that captured the imagination of every listener.
The museum displayed a wondrous collection of ancient artifacts.

Synset Examples

a fantastic trip to the Orient
the film was fantastic!
a howling success
a marvelous collection of rare books
had a rattling conversation about politics
a tremendous achievement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marvelous - causing wonder; extremely good or impressive
  • Synonym: amazing - causing great surprise or wonder
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual; normal

Etymology

From Middle English 'wondrous', formed from 'wonder' + -ous.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
