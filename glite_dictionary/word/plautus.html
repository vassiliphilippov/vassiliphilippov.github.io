Plautus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɔːtəs / (AmE), / ˈplɔːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Alcidae, including the extinct great auk.
genus of seabirds (great auk)

Examples

The extinct great auk belonged to the genus Plautus.
Scientists studied fossil remains from Plautus to learn about ancient seabirds.
The family Alcidae includes several genera, but Plautus is notable for its extinct species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great auk - a large, extinct, flightless seabird formerly belonging to the genus Plautus

Etymology

From New Latin, named in taxonomic classification, possibly after the Roman playwright Plautus as a naming convention.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɔːtəs / (AmE), / ˈplɔːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Alcidae, known as auks, found in Arctic regions.
genus of seabirds (auks)

Examples

The thick-billed murre belongs to the genus Plautus.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of the Plautus seabirds in the North Atlantic.
A new fossil discovery suggests the ancient range of Plautus was much wider than today.

Etymology

From modern Latin, adopted as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
