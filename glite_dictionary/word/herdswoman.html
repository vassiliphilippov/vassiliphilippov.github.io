herdswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːdzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman whose job is to look after and drive a herd of animals, especially cattle or sheep.
female herder of animals

Examples

The herdswoman guided the cattle across the open pasture at sunrise.
As a herdswoman, she spent most of her days caring for the sheep.
The villagers respected the experienced herdswoman for her skill with the animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herdsman - a man who looks after a herd of animals
  • Synonym: shepherd - a person who tends and herds sheep

Etymology

Formed from 'herd' + 'woman', modeled after 'herdsman'.
