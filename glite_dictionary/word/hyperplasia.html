hyperplasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pɚˈpleɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈpleɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
an abnormal increase in the number of cells in a tissue or organ, often resulting in enlargement
abnormal increase in cell numbers
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed hyperplasia of the thyroid gland.
Chronic irritation can sometimes cause tissue hyperplasia.
Endometrial hyperplasia is a risk factor for developing certain cancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypertrophy - increase in the size of an organ or tissue due to enlargement of its cells
  • Antonym: atrophy - decrease in size or wasting away of a body part or tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over) + 'plasis' (formation).
