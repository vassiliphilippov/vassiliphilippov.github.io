isosceles

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈsɑs.əˌliz / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒs.ɪ.liːz / (BrE)
(of a triangle) having two sides of equal length.
triangle with two equal sides

Examples

The base angles of an isosceles triangle are always equal.
If two sides of a triangle are equal, it is called an isosceles triangle.
Her favorite geometric shape to draw is the isosceles triangle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scalene - (of a triangle) having all sides of different lengths
  • Antonym: equilateral - (of a triangle) having all sides of equal length

Etymology

From Greek isoskelēs, from isos (equal) + skelos (leg).
