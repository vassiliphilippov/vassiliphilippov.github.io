statement

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
(music) The presentation or introduction of a musical theme or melody, especially in the opening section of a composition.
presentation of a musical theme (music)

Examples

The composer's bold statement of the theme sets the tone for the whole symphony.
After the initial statement, the orchestra repeated the melody in a different key.
The pianist's clear statement of the motif helped the audience follow the development section.

Synset Examples

the initial statement of the sonata

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of expressing something in speech or writing, especially in a clear or definite way
the act of expressing something

Examples

She made a powerful statement about the need for change in her country.
Every witness is required to give a statement to the police.
His resignation letter included a clear statement of his reasons for leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a statement declaring something is true
  • Antonym: question - a sentence worded to elicit information

Etymology

From Old French *statement*, from *statir* (to state).

6. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
a clear expression of something in speech or writing, especially a formal account of facts or opinions
a formal spoken or written declaration

Examples

The police asked him to make a formal statement about what he saw.
Her statement was clear and left no room for misunderstanding.
The company issued a statement denying all the allegations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Antonym: question - an expression seeking information rather than stating facts

Etymology

From the verb 'state' + -ment, influenced by Old French 'estat', from Latin 'status' (standing, position).

7. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
a clear or definite expression of something in speech or writing, especially one presented as a fact or assertion to support an argument or claim
an assertion offered as evidence or fact

Examples

She presented a convincing statement to support her case.
Each statement in his report was carefully verified for accuracy.
The lawyer demanded that every statement made in court be backed by evidence.

Synset Examples

it was a strong argument that his hypothesis was true

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Synonym: claim - an assertion that something is true, often without providing evidence or proof
  • Antonym: denial - a statement refuting or rejecting a claim

Etymology

From English 'state' + '-ment', influenced by Latin 'statum' (to set forth).
