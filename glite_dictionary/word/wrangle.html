wrangle

1. VERB

/ ˈræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
to herd and care for livestock, especially horses or cattle, typically on a ranch
herd and care for livestock
american

Examples

He was hired to wrangle the horses at the ranch.
Every morning, the cowboys wrangle the cattle before sunrise.
She spent her summers wrangling mustangs in the Nevada desert.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A loud or intense argument or dispute, especially one involving prolonged negotiation or bargaining.
intense argument or dispute

Examples

The meeting ended in a bitter wrangle over funding allocations.
After hours of wrangle, the two parties finally reached a compromise.
There was a lengthy wrangle between the buyers and sellers at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - a heated argument or disagreement
  • Synonym: dispute - a disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or arrangement reached in an argument

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Dutch or Low German *wrangelen* (to struggle).

3. VERB

/ ˈræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to quarrel or argue noisily, angrily, or disruptively, often for a prolonged period
argue or quarrel noisily

Examples

The two neighbors often wrangle over the boundary between their properties.
They wrangled for hours about which movie to watch.
Despite their efforts to stay calm, they began wrangling loudly in the middle of the meeting.

Synset Examples

The bar keeper threw them out, but they continued to wrangle on down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to express opposite opinions in a conversation
  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion; not to argue

Etymology

From Middle English 'wranglen', frequentative of 'wrangen' (to dispute), probably from Old Norse 'vrangr' (wrong, awry).

to manage or control (people, especially children or groups) with some effort or difficulty

Examples
