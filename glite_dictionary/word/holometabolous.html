holometabolous

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊləˈmɛtəˌboʊləs / (AmE), / ˌhɒləˈmɛtəbələs / (BrE)
(of an insect) undergoing complete metamorphosis, passing through egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages.
insect undergoing complete metamorphosis
medical

Examples

Many species of butterflies are holometabolous insects, experiencing a pupal stage before adulthood.
Beetles, flies, and bees are all holometabolous, going through distinct larval and adult stages.
The holometabolous life cycle allows for specialization of larval and adult forms in insects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hemimetabolous - (of an insect) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole, entire) + 'metabole' (change).
