Chimakum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪməkəm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪməkəm / (BrE)
A member of a Salishan indigenous people formerly living in northwestern Washington State, USA.
member of a Salishan people of Washington

Examples

The Chimakum once inhabited the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State.
Anthropologists have studied the culture and traditions of the Chimakum.
Only a few descendants of the Chimakum remain today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salishan - relating to the Salishan group of peoples or languages

Etymology

From the Salishan self-designation, possibly via Chinook Jargon.
