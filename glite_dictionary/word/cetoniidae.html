Cetoniidae

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtoʊniˌaɪdi / (AmE), / siːˌtəʊnɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles, commonly known as flower beetles and goliath beetles, often found on flowers and fruit.
family of flower beetles

Examples

The Cetoniidae are often found feeding on nectar and pollen in gardens.
Some species of Cetoniidae display striking metallic colors on their bodies.
Goliath beetles, a part of the Cetoniidae family, are among the largest insects in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scarabaeidae - family of beetles commonly called scarab beetles

Etymology

From New Latin Cetoniidae, based on Cetonia (a beetle genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ sɛˌtoʊniˈɪdiː / (AmE), / sɛˌtɒniˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A subfamily of beetles known as flower beetles and goliath beetles, typically brightly colored and often found on flowers.
subfamily of beetles: flower beetles, goliath beetles

Examples

The Cetoniidae are often seen feeding on the nectar and pollen of garden flowers.
Several species within the Cetoniidae are prized by collectors for their bright metallic colors.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Cetoniidae in the rainforest last year.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cetoniidae', from the genus name 'Cetonia', referring to a type of beetle.
