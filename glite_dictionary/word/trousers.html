trousers

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraʊzɚz / (AmE), / ˈtraʊzəz / (BrE)
an outer garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles, with a separate part for each leg, usually worn on the lower half of the body
an outer garment for the lower body, with parts for each leg
british

Examples

He bought a new pair of trousers for the interview.
She accidentally spilled coffee on her favorite trousers.
School uniforms often require students to wear trousers instead of skirts.

Synset Examples

he had a sharp crease in his trousers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pants - outer garment for the lower body with a part for each leg (American English)

Etymology

From Old French 'tro(u)ses', plural of 'tros' (bundle, truss), relating to medieval clothing.
