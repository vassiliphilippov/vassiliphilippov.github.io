cartouch

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈtuʃ / (AmE), / kɑːˈtuːʃ / (BrE)
A cartridge, especially one with a paper casing used for firearms.
cartridge with paper casing

Examples

The antique rifle used a cartouch instead of a modern metal cartridge.
Collectors highly value firearms that still have original cartouches in good condition.
He loaded a paper cartouch into the musket before firing the demonstration shot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartridge - a container holding a charge for a firearm or device

Etymology

From French 'cartouche', from Italian 'cartoccio' (paper roll).
