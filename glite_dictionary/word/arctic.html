arctic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrktɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk / (BrE)
A waterproof overshoe designed to protect shoes from water or snow.
waterproof protective overshoe

Examples

He slipped his arctics over his boots before heading out into the blizzard.
During wet winters, farmers often wore arctics to keep their feet dry in the fields.
She removed her muddy arctics at the door to avoid tracking water through the house.

Etymology

From the brand name 'Arctic', originally trademarked for a type of overshoe, inspired by the Arctic region's harsh climate.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːk.tɪk / (BrE)
Extremely cold; resembling the conditions of the Arctic region.
extremely cold

Examples

After stepping outside on that arctic morning, her breath instantly turned to mist in the frigid air.
The wind blowing off the lake made the city feel arctic in the middle of January.
They huddled by the fire, escaping the arctic chill that crept through the old cabin.

Synset Examples

an arctic climate
a frigid day
gelid waters of the North Atlantic
glacial winds
icy hands
polar weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frigid - very cold
  • Synonym: glacial - extremely cold
  • Antonym: hot - having a high temperature; the opposite of extremely cold

Etymology

From Arctic, relating to the Arctic region, from Greek arktikos (of the bear, northern), referring to the constellation Ursa Major.
