Arctic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk / (BrE)
the region of the Earth surrounding the North Pole, characterized by extreme cold and polar conditions, lying north of the Arctic Circle
region around the North Pole

Examples

Polar bears are native to the Arctic and depend on sea ice for hunting.
Scientists are studying the effects of climate change in the Arctic region.
The beauty of the northern lights attracts many tourists to the Arctic each year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Antarctic - the region around the South Pole, opposite of the Arctic

Etymology

From Greek 'arktikos' (northern, of the Bear, i.e. Ursa Major), from 'arktos' (bear).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrktɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk / (BrE)
relating to the region around the North Pole or its climate, people, or conditions
relating to the North Pole region

Examples

Scientists study Arctic ecosystems to understand the impact of climate change.
The Arctic landscape is covered with snow and ice for most of the year.
She wore a heavy coat when sailing in Arctic waters.

Synset Examples

the Arctic summer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Antarctic - relating to the region around the South Pole

Etymology

From Greek arktikos (of the Bear, northern), from arktos (bear), referring to the northern constellation Ursa Major.
