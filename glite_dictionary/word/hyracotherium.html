Hyracotherium

1. PROPN

/ haɪˌrækəˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / hʌɪˌrakəˈθɪərɪəm / (BrE)
an extinct genus of small hoofed mammals, formerly known as Eohippus, considered the earliest ancestor of modern horses
extinct early horse ancestor

Examples

The fossil remains of Hyracotherium have been discovered in both Europe and North America.
Scientists consider Hyracotherium to be one of the earliest ancestors of modern horses.
The small size and multi-toed feet of Hyracotherium distinguish it from present-day horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eohippus - another name for Hyracotherium, early ancestor of horses

Etymology

From Greek 'hyrax' (shrew, mouse) and 'therion' (beast), referring to its shrew-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌræsəˈθɪəriəm / (AmE), / haɪˌræsəˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
an extinct genus of small, early horses that lived during the Eocene epoch, formerly called Eohippus
extinct genus of early horses

Examples

Fossils of Hyracotherium have provided important insights into the evolution of modern horses.
The small size of Hyracotherium made it well-suited to life in dense forests.
Scientists previously referred to Hyracotherium as Eohippus, meaning 'dawn horse'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eohippus - earlier name for Hyracotherium, small extinct horse of the Eocene

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'hyraks' (shrew/mouse) and 'therion' (beast).
