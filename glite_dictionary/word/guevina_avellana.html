Guevina avellana

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈviː.nə ə.vɛˈjɑː.lə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈviː.nə ə.vɛˈjæ.lə / (BrE)
A Chilean shrub that produces coral-red fruit containing an edible seed similar to a hazelnut.
Chilean shrub with edible hazelnut-like seed

Examples

The seeds of the Guevina avellana are harvested and eaten much like hazelnuts.
Botanists discovered a healthy stand of Guevina avellana near the southern forests of Chile.
The bright coral-red fruit of the Guevina avellana makes it popular in ornamental gardens.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Guevina' and species name 'avellana', the latter from Spanish for 'hazelnut'.
