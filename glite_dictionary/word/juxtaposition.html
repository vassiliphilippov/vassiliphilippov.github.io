juxtaposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of placing things close together or side by side, especially for comparison or contrast
placing things side by side for comparison

Examples

The juxtaposition of modern furniture with vintage decor created a striking effect.
Her photography often explores the juxtaposition of city life and nature.
Critics admired the juxtaposition of bright colors in the artist's latest painting.

Synset Examples

it is the result of the juxtaposition of contrasting colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Latin juxta (next to) + French position (position)

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or instance of placing two or more things side by side, especially to compare or contrast them
placing things side by side for comparison or contrast

Examples

The juxtaposition of old and new buildings creates a unique cityscape.
Her artwork relies on the juxtaposition of bright colors and dark shadows.
The documentary uses the juxtaposition of different cultures to highlight their similarities and differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Latin 'juxta' (next to) + French 'position' (position).
