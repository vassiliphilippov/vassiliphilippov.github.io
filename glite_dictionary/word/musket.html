musket

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌskɪt / (BrE)
A muzzle-loading shoulder firearm with a long barrel, used by infantry soldiers from the 16th to the 19th centuries.
early long-barreled infantry firearm

Examples

The soldier loaded his musket and aimed carefully at the advancing enemy.
During the reenactment, volunteers demonstrated how a musket was fired in battle.
The museum displayed a collection of 18th-century muskets and other historical weapons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rifle - a shoulder firearm with a rifled barrel
  • Degree Lower: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand

Etymology

From Middle French *mousquet*, from Italian *moschetto* (sparrow hawk, musket).
