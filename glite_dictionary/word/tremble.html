tremble

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɛm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛm.bəl / (BrE)
to shake slightly and involuntarily, especially as a result of anxiety, excitement, or weakness
to shake or quiver involuntarily

Examples

She began to tremble with fear as she heard the strange noise outside.
His voice trembled when he tried to explain what had happened.
I could see her hands trembling as she held the cup.

Synset Examples

His hands were trembling when he signed the document

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiver - to shake with slight, rapid motion
  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly up and down or side to side
  • Antonym: steady - firm and not moving or shaking

Etymology

From Old French 'trembler', from Latin 'tremulare' (to tremble), from 'tremulus', from 'tremere' (to shake)

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmbl / (BrE)
A sudden shaking or quivering movement caused by cold, fear, or excitement.
involuntary shaking movement

Examples

A slight tremble passed through her hands as she gripped the cup.
He noticed a tremble in his voice when he spoke about the accident.
The sudden cold breeze made me feel a tremble run down my spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shudder - an involuntary shiver or tremble, often from fear or cold
  • Synonym: shake - to move or cause to move back and forth with quick movements

Etymology

From Old French *trembler*, from Latin *tremulus* (trembling), from *tremere* (to tremble).
