Kennedya

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛdiə / (AmE), / kəˈnɛdiə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian woody vines that bear showy red or purplish flowers.
genus of Australian flowering vines

Examples

Several species of Kennedya are cultivated for their attractive, brightly colored flowers.
The Kennedya vines thrived in the Australian botanical garden, adding a splash of red among the greenery.
Botanists documented a new type of Kennedya growing wild in Western Australia.

Etymology

Named after Lewis Kennedy, an English horticulturist.

genus of Australian woody vines

Examples

The Kennedya genus is prized by gardeners for its vibrant, pea-like flowers.
Several species within Kennedya are cultivated ornamentally in Australia and beyond.

Etymology
