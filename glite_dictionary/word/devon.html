Devon

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛv.ən / (AmE), / ˈdɛv.ən / (BrE)
A county in southwestern England, known for its coastline, rural landscapes, and historic towns.
county in southwestern England

Examples

She spent her summer holidays exploring the beaches and countryside of Devon.
The cream tea, a traditional snack, is especially popular in Devon.
Many picturesque villages in Devon attract tourists throughout the year.

Etymology

Named after the Dumnonii, a Celtic tribe formerly inhabiting the area.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛv.ən / (AmE), / ˈdɛv.ən / (BrE)
a breed of red cattle from Devon, England, valued for both milk and meat production
breed of red English cattle

Examples

The farmer raised a herd of Devon cattle known for their rich milk and quality beef.
Several prize-winning Devon cows were displayed at the agricultural fair this year.
The distinctive reddish-brown coat makes the Devon easily recognizable among other cattle breeds.

Etymology

Named after the county of Devon in southwest England, where the breed originated.
