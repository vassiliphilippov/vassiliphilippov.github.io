explanation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspləˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a statement or account that makes something clear or helps someone understand how or why something happens
a statement that makes something clear

Examples

Her explanation helped everyone understand the new policy.
The scientist gave a detailed explanation of the experiment's results.
He demanded an explanation for his sudden dismissal from work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer or easier to understand
  • Antonym: obfuscation - the act of making something obscure, unclear, or unintelligible

Etymology

From Latin explanatio (an explanation), from explanare (to make plain).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspləˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a statement, fact, or account that makes something clear or easy to understand
making something clear or understandable

Examples

She gave a detailed explanation of the procedure to the new employees.
The teacher's explanation helped me finally understand the concept.
Without a clear explanation, the instructions were confusing to everyone.

Synset Examples

the explanation was very simple
I expected a brief account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarification - the act of making something less confused and more comprehensible
  • Antonym: obfuscation - the act of making something less clear or harder to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'explanatio', from 'explanare' (to make plain).
