to seclude

1. VERB

/ sɪˈklud / (AmE), / sɪˈkluːd / (BrE)
to keep someone or oneself away from other people; to isolate
to keep away from others

Examples

The author decided to seclude himself in a mountain cabin while working on his novel.
After her surgery, she chose to seclude herself from visitors until she felt better.
Monks often seclude themselves from society to focus on meditation and prayer.

Synset Examples

He sequestered himself in his study to write a book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to set apart or keep separate from others
  • Synonym: to sequester - to remove or withdraw into solitude or retirement
  • Antonym: to expose - to make visible or accessible to others; not keep secluded

Etymology

From Latin secludere (to shut off), from se- (apart) + claudere (to shut).
