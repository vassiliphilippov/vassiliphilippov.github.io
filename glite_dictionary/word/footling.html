footling

1. ADJ

/ ˈfuːtlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtlɪŋ / (BrE)
of little value or importance; trivial or insignificant
trivial; insignificant
informal british

Examples

She dismissed his concerns as merely footling and refused to discuss them further.
Why waste time arguing over such a footling issue when there are bigger problems to solve?
His footling excuses did nothing to change her mind.

Synset Examples

a fiddling sum of money
a footling gesture
our worries are lilliputian compared with those of countries that are at war
a little (or small) matter
a dispute over niggling details
limited to petty enterprises
piffling efforts
giving a police officer a free meal may be against the law, but it seems to be a picayune infraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: important - of great value or significance

Etymology

From the verb 'footle' (to dither or trifle), probably of dialect origin; recorded in English since the mid-19th century.
