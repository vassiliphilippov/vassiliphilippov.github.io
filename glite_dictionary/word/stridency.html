stridency

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪ.dən.si / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.dən.si / (BrE)
the quality of having a loud, harsh, and high-pitched sound
loud, harsh, high-pitched quality

Examples

The stridency of her voice made it difficult to concentrate on the lyrics.
Critics noted the stridency in the political debate, which grew louder as the night progressed.
The alarm's stridency jolted everyone awake in the early morning hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshness - the quality of being unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses
  • Antonym: softness - the quality of being gentle or mild, especially in sound

Etymology

From strident + -cy, modeled on leniency, potency, etc. Strident from Latin strident-, stridens (present participle of stridere, to creak).
