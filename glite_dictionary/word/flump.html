to flump

1. VERB

/ flʌmp / (AmE), / flʌmp / (BrE)
to drop or set (something or oneself) down heavily or with a dull sound
drop or set something down heavily

Examples

After a long day, she flumped onto the sofa with a sigh.
He flumped his backpack on the floor and headed to the kitchen.
The cat flumped down beside the heater, seeking warmth.

Synset Examples

He planked the money on the table
He planked himself into the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plonk - to put or set something down heavily
  • Synonym: plunk - to set something down abruptly and noisily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503211-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ flʌmp / (AmE), / flʌmp / (BrE)
to fall, drop, or collapse heavily and with a dull sound
fall or drop heavily
informal

Examples

After running all day, he flumped onto the sofa and sighed with relief.
The cat flumped down beside the heater and immediately fell asleep.
She flumped her heavy backpack on the floor when she got home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plop - to fall or drop with a soft, slightly audible sound
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01982354-v
Synset POS: v
