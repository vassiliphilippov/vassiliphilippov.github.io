shattering

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of breaking something into small pieces, especially with force or suddenness
the act of breaking into pieces

Examples

The shattering of the vase startled everyone in the room.
Years of pressure eventually led to the shattering of the glacier into many pieces.
The sudden shattering of glass echoed through the empty hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breaking - the act of causing something to separate into pieces
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00377633-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a noise, impact, or event) extremely loud, violent, or forceful, as if capable of breaking something.
extremely loud or violent (noise/event)

Examples

The shattering blast from the explosion echoed through the city streets.
A shattering crash resounded as the tree fell across the roof.
The shattering roar of the crowd could be heard from several blocks away.

Synset Examples

shattering rain striking the windowpanes
the shattering tones of the enormous carillon
the shattering peal of artillery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deafening - so loud as to cause temporary hearing loss
  • Synonym: thunderous - making a sound like thunder; very loud
