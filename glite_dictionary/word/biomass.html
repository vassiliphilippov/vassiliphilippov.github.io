biomass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊ.mæs / (BrE)
the total amount of living matter, such as plants and animals, in a given area or volume at a particular time
total mass of living matter in an area

Examples

The wetland supports a large biomass of insects, birds, and amphibians.
Researchers measured the forest's biomass to assess its carbon storage capacity.
Changes in temperature can affect the biomass of phytoplankton in the ocean.

Etymology

From bio- (life) + mass, first used in ecology in the 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊ.mæs / (BrE)
organic matter, especially plant materials and animal waste, used as a source of fuel or energy.
organic matter used as fuel

Examples

The power plant generates electricity by burning biomass such as wood chips and agricultural waste.
Many researchers are developing new methods to convert biomass into renewable energy.
Farmers can sell their crop residues as biomass for use in green energy projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biofuel - fuel produced from organic matter
  • Antonym: fossil fuel - fuel such as coal or oil formed from ancient organic remains

Etymology

From bio- (life) + mass, first coined in scientific contexts in the mid-20th century.
