palmately-lobed

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːm.eɪt.li loʊbd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːm.ət.li ləʊbd / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other organ) having lobes that spread out from a single central point, like the fingers of a hand.
having lobes radiating from a central point

Examples

The maple tree's palmately-lobed leaves are easily recognized in the autumn.
Many houseplants, such as begonias, display palmately-lobed foliage.
Botanists often use the term palmately-lobed to describe certain leaf shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmate - having parts or lobes radiating from a central point, like a hand

Etymology

From Latin 'palmatus' (shaped like a hand) + 'lobed.'
