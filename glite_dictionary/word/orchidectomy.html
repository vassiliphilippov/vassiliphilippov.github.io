orchidectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrkɪˈdɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌɔːkɪˈdɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which one or both testicles are removed
removal of one or both testicles
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with cancer, he had to undergo an orchidectomy as part of his treatment.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of an orchidectomy to the patient and his family.
An orchidectomy may be performed to treat certain testicular disorders or as part of gender-affirming surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castration - removal of the testicles or ovaries

Etymology

From Greek orkhis (testicle) + -ectomy (removal, from Greek ektomē, 'cutting out').
