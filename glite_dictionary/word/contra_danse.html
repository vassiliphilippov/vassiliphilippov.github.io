contra danse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.trə dæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə dɑːns / (BrE)
A type of folk dance in which couples are arranged in sets or face one another in lines, typically moving through a sequence of figures.
folk dance with lines of couples

Examples

The group spent the evening learning a lively contra danse from the instructor.
At the festival, dancers formed two long lines for a traditional contra danse.
Many communities host regular contra danse events open to all ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country dance - a similar folk dance with couples arranged in lines or circles

Etymology

From French 'contredanse', itself an alteration of English 'country dance'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑntrə dæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrə dans / (BrE)
to perform a contradance, a type of folk dance in which couples form two facing lines or a square and follow a sequence of steps
to perform a type of folk dance

Examples

Every Saturday night, locals gather in the barn to contra danse together.
She learned how to contra danse during her summer in Vermont.
At the festival, dozens of couples contra dansed joyfully until midnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradance - to take part in a contradance; to perform the folk dance

Etymology

From French 'contredanse', itself from English 'country dance'.
