infinitive

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪnɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪnɪtɪv / (BrE)
the basic form of a verb, usually occurring with or without the word 'to' and not inflected for tense, person, or number
basic verb form (e.g. 'to go')

Examples

In English, the infinitive form of the verb is often preceded by the word 'to.'
Please use the infinitive rather than the past tense in this sentence.
'To run,' 'to see,' and 'to be' are examples of the infinitive in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participle - a verbal form used as an adjective or to form verb tenses
  • Synonym: gerund - the -ing form of a verb used as a noun

Etymology

From Late Latin *infinitivus* (unlimited), from Latin *infinitus* (infinite, unbounded).
