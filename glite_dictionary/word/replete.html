replete

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈplit / (AmE), / rɪˈpliːt / (BrE)
having eaten or drunk so much that no more can be consumed; fully satisfied, especially with food or drink.
full after eating or drinking

Examples

After the five-course meal, she felt completely replete and could not eat another bite.
He leaned back in his chair, replete with good food and wine.
Everyone left the banquet replete, happy and satisfied.

Synset Examples

a full stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full - having eaten enough so that no more can be consumed

Etymology

From Latin repletus, past participle of replere (to fill again).

WordNet ID: oewn-02308182-s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈplit / (AmE), / rɪˈpliːt / (BrE)
filled or well-supplied with something; having a large amount of something (usually used as 'replete with').
filled or well-supplied with

Examples

The garden was replete with vibrant flowers in every color imaginable.
His essay was replete with insightful observations and clever arguments.
The museum is replete with rare artifacts from ancient civilizations.

Synset Examples

imbued with the spirit of the Reformation
words instinct with love
it is replete with misery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full - containing as much as possible; filled to capacity
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied

Etymology

From Latin repletus (filled up), past participle of replēre, from re- (again) + plēre (to fill).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01089130-s
Synset POS: s
