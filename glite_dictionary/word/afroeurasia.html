Afroeurasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊjʊˈreɪʒə / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊjʊˈreɪzɪə / (BrE)
a large landmass comprising Africa, Europe, and Asia considered as a single continent or region, especially in historical or geographical contexts
Africa, Europe, and Asia as one landmass

Examples

Many early trade routes spanned Afroeurasia, linking distant civilizations together.
The spread of crops and technologies across Afroeurasia significantly shaped world history.
Historians often refer to Afroeurasia as the Old World in contrast to the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurasia - Europe and Asia considered together as one continent

Etymology

Blend of 'Africa' and 'Eurasia' (Europe + Asia).
