thyroprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪroʊˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌθʌɪrəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a preparation made by iodinating protein, used as a thyroid hormone substitute with an action similar to thyroxine
iodinated protein with thyroid-like action
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed thyroprotein as a supplement for patients with thyroid deficiencies.
Early treatments for hypothyroidism included preparations like thyroprotein.
Recent studies compare thyroprotein with pure thyroxine for medical effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thyroxine - a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that increases metabolic rate

Etymology

From 'thyro-' (relating to the thyroid gland) + 'protein'.
