Salix caprea

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks kəˈpriːə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks kəˈpriːə / (BrE)
a species of willow native to Europe and western Asia, noted for its broad leaves and large, fluffy catkins; commonly called goat willow or pussy willow
species of willow with broad leaves and large catkins

Examples

The Salix caprea produces soft, silvery catkins in early spring.
Gardeners planted Salix caprea along the riverbank to prevent erosion.
Many people recognize Salix caprea by its distinctive broad leaves and fuzzy flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goat willow - a common name for Salix caprea
  • Degree Higher: willow - tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning 'willow', and 'caprea' meaning 'of a goat', reflecting the common name 'goat willow'.
