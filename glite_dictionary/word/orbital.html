orbital

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔr.bɪ.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈɔː.bɪ.təl / (BrE)
relating to or involving an orbit, especially the path of a celestial body or object around another.
relating to an orbit or path around something

Examples

The satellite maintains a stable orbital path around the Earth.
Scientists measured the planet's orbital velocity with precision instruments.
During the orbital revolution, the spacecraft collected valuable data.

Synset Examples

orbital revolution
orbital velocity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orbital - a type of atomic electron configuration

Etymology

From Latin 'orbita' (track, course), referring to a path or route.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrbɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪtəl / (BrE)
relating to the eye socket (orbit) in anatomy or medicine.
relating to the eye socket
medical

Examples

The patient suffered an orbital fracture in the accident.
Swelling around the orbital area can indicate trauma to the face.
A CT scan showed a mass in the orbital cavity.

Synset Examples

orbital scale
orbital arch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocular - relating to the eye

Etymology

From Medieval Latin orbitalis, from Latin orbita (track, course, orbit).
