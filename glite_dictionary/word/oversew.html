to oversew

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsəʊ / (BrE)
to sew two edges together using close stitches that pass over both edges, typically to prevent fraying or to join them neatly
sew edges with overcasting stitches

Examples

To finish the seam, the tailor will oversew the edges to keep them from fraying.
She carefully oversewed the hem to ensure a neat finish.
When repairing socks, it's common to oversew the hole for extra durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcast - to sew with loosely spaced, over-the-edge stitches

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (above, across) + 'sew', reflecting the action of stitching over the edges.
