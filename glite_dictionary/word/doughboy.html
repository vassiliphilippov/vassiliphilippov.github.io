doughboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a small rounded lump of dough that is deep-fried and served hot as bread
deep-fried bread lump

Examples

She ordered a fresh doughboy dusted with sugar at the fair.
The aroma of a hot doughboy filled the kitchen as it was taken from the fryer.
At breakfast, locals often enjoy a doughboy with jam or honey.

Synset Examples

the doughboy was a predecessor of the doughnut

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: doughnut - a small, sweet fried cake of dough, typically ring-shaped

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
an American infantryman, especially one who served in World War I
American infantryman in World War I

Examples

Many young doughboys crossed the Atlantic to fight in the trenches of France.
The memorial honored the sacrifice of every American doughboy who served in World War I.
My great-grandfather was a doughboy and kept a diary from his time overseas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: GI - an enlisted American soldier, especially in World War II
  • Antonym: Tommy - a British soldier, especially one from World War I or II

Etymology

Origin uncertain; popularized during World War I to refer to American soldiers.
