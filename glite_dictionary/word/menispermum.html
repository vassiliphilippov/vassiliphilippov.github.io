Menispermum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnɪˈspɜrməm / (AmE), / ˌmɛnɪˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A genus of climbing woody vines in the family Menispermaceae, commonly known as moonseed, found in North America and Asia.
genus of climbing vines (moonseed)

Examples

The genus Menispermum is well known for its distinctive moon-shaped seeds.
Native Americans traditionally used the roots of Menispermum for medicinal purposes.
Menispermum species are often found climbing along riverbanks and forest edges.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek 'mēn' (moon) + 'sperma' (seed), referring to the shape of the seed.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnɪˈspɝːməm / (AmE), / ˌmɛnɪˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A genus of climbing herbs, commonly known as moonseeds, native to North America and eastern Asia.
genus of climbing herbs (moonseeds)

Examples

The Menispermum vines are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
North American Menispermum species are sometimes called 'Canadian moonseed'.
Botanists study Menispermum to understand its medicinal and toxic properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'mēn' (moon) and 'sperma' (seed), referring to the crescent-shaped seeds.
