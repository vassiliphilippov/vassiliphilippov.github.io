Shah Pahlavi

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑː pɑːˈlɑːvi / (AmE), / ʃɑː pæˈlɑːvi / (BrE)
the Shah of Iran from 1941 until he was deposed in 1979, known formally as Mohammad Reza Pahlavi (1919–1980)
last Shah of Iran (1919–1980)

Examples

Shah Pahlavi introduced a series of modernization reforms in Iran during his reign.
After the Islamic Revolution, Shah Pahlavi went into exile and never returned to Iran.
Many historical analyses focus on the foreign policy of Shah Pahlavi before 1979.

Etymology

From Persian 'shāh' (king) and 'Pahlavi', the ruling dynasty's name.
