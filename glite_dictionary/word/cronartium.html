Cronartium

1. NOUN

/ krəˈnɑrtiəm / (AmE), / krəˈnɑːtiəm / (BrE)
A genus of rust fungi characterized by aecia produced in raised or swollen sori and teliospores formed in waxy columns.
a genus of rust fungi

Examples

Scientists studied the spread of Cronartium to better understand its impact on pine forests.
Cronartium species are known to cause significant diseases in commercial timber.
Identifying Cronartium infections early can help prevent large-scale damage to trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'kronos' (time) + Latin 'artium' (art), possibly referencing patterns or structures.

2. NOUN

/ kroʊˈnɑːrtiəm / (AmE), / krəˈnɑːtɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of rust fungi that produce aecia in raised or swollen sori and bear teliospores in waxy columns, some of which cause economic damage to pine trees.
genus of rust fungi affecting pines

Examples

The fungus Cronartium ribicola is responsible for white pine blister rust.
Researchers studied various species within the genus Cronartium for their effects on forestry.
Control measures are implemented to prevent the spread of Cronartium-related diseases in pine forests.

Etymology

From Greek 'kronos' (time) and 'artos' (bread), via botanical Latin.
