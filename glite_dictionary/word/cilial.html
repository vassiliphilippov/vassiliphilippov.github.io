cilial

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪliəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving cilia, the microscopic hair-like structures projecting from the surface of a cell.
relating to cilia on cells
medical

Examples

The cilial movement in the respiratory tract helps remove mucus and debris.
Researchers studied the effects of toxins on cilial function in human cells.
A defect in cilial structure can cause serious genetic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ciliary - relating to or resembling cilia

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' (eyelash, small hair).
