culdoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.dəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.dəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A specialized tubular instrument (endoscope) used to visually examine a woman's pelvic organs through the vaginal wall.
instrument for examining pelvic organs
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a culdoscope to visualize the patient's pelvic cavity.
A culdoscope allows for minimally invasive examination of the pelvic organs.
After the procedure, the culdoscope was carefully sterilized for future use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for examining the interior of a bodily organ or cavity

Etymology

From Latin 'culdus' (pouch, from 'cul-de-sac') + 'scope' (from Greek 'skopein', to look at).
