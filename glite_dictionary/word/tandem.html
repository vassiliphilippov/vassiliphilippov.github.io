tandem

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.dəm / (AmE), / ˈtæn.dəm / (BrE)
a bicycle designed for two riders, with two sets of pedals and two seats arranged one behind the other
bicycle for two riders

Examples

They rode a tandem along the country road, enjoying the morning breeze.
Riding a tandem requires good coordination between both cyclists.
We rented a tandem for the weekend and explored the city together.

Etymology

From Latin 'tandem' (at length), later used in English for carriages drawn in a single line, then for bicycles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtændəm / (AmE), / ˈtændəm / (BrE)
an arrangement in which two or more objects or people are placed one behind the other
objects or people in a row, one behind another

Examples

The two cyclists rode the steep hill on a tandem to save energy.
The dogs were harnessed in tandem for the sledding race.
Our office desks are arranged in tandem to maximize floor space.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: side by side - arrangement where items are placed next to each other

Etymology

From Latin 'tandem' (at length, finally), adopted for the vehicle arrangement in the 18th century.

3. ADV

/ ˈtændəm / (AmE), / ˈtændəm / (BrE)
(adverb) In a line, one behind the other; together or in sequence.
one behind the other; sequentially

Examples

The two cyclists rode tandem down the narrow country lane.
The trucks moved tandem along the highway during the convoy.
Horses were harnessed tandem to pull the carriage through the field.

Synset Examples

ride tandem on a bicycle built for two
riding horses down the path in tandem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: side by side - positioned next to each other, not in sequence

Etymology

From Latin 'tandem' meaning 'at length, at last', adopted into English in the 18th century for sequential arrangements.
