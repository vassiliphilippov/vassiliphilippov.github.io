to unsnarl

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈsnɑrl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsnɑːl / (BrE)
to free or disentangle something from a tangle or confusion
free from entanglement or tangle

Examples

It took me an hour to unsnarl the knots in my shoelaces.
The technician managed to unsnarl the wires behind the computer desk.
She calmly began to unsnarl the dog's tangled leash.

Synset Examples

Can you disentangle the cord?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to entangle - to twist together in a confusing mass
  • Synonym: to disentangle - to free from a tangle or complication

Etymology

From un- (reversal prefix) + snarl (to tangle).
