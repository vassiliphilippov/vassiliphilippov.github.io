Levant

1. NOUN

/ ləˈvænt / (AmE), / lɪˈvɑːnt / (BrE)
A heavy, high-quality morocco leather, often used for bookbinding.
type of bookbinding leather

Examples

The cover of the rare book was bound in dark brown Levant.
Traditional bookbinders still value Levant for its rich texture and durability.
He selected a fine Levant to restore the antique volume.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: morocco - a fine flexible leather made from goatskin, used especially for bookbinding

Etymology

From French 'levant', referring to the eastern Mediterranean, as such leather was originally imported from that region.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈvænt / (AmE), / lɪˈvɑːnt / (BrE)
the historical term for the countries of the eastern Mediterranean, especially present-day Lebanon, Syria, and Israel
eastern Mediterranean region (historical)

Examples

Traders from the Levant played an important role in Mediterranean commerce during the Renaissance.
The cuisine of the Levant is renowned for its rich flavors and use of fresh ingredients.
During the Ottoman Empire, the Levant was administered as several distinct provinces.

Etymology

From French 'levant' (rising, east), from Latin 'levare' (to rise), referring to the rising sun in the east.
