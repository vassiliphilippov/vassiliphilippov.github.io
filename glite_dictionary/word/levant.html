to levant

1. VERB

/ lɪˈvænt / (AmE), / lɪˈvɑːnt / (BrE)
to leave secretly, especially to avoid paying a debt
run off to avoid paying a debt
british

Examples

The innkeeper was furious when he realized the guest had levanted without settling his bill.
He planned to levant in the middle of the night to escape his creditors.
When the debts became too much, she simply levanted and disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest

Etymology

From French 'lever l’ancre' (weigh anchor).
