unwieldiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwiːl.dɪ.nɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːl.dɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to carry, move, or manage because of size, shape, or weight
difficulty in being carried or managed due to bulk or shape

Examples

The unwieldiness of the old wardrobe made it nearly impossible to get through the doorway.
Because of its unwieldiness, the large package required two people to carry it safely.
The design of the sculpture was beautiful, but its unwieldiness limited where it could be displayed.

Synset Examples

the movers cursed the unwieldiness of the big piano

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsiness - awkwardness in movement or handling
  • Antonym: manageability - quality of being easy to manage or handle

Etymology

From 'unwieldy' + '-ness', where 'unwieldy' is from Old English 'welan' (to handle).

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwildi.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːldi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to control, manage, or handle, typically because of complexity, size, or awkwardness
difficulty in being managed or controlled

Examples

The unwieldiness of the new regulations made them hard to implement effectively.
Their argument centered on the unwieldiness of the current tax code.
He criticized the unwieldiness of the committee's structure as a barrier to progress.

Synset Examples

avoiding the unwieldiness of formal legal processes
the onset of unwieldiness and bureaucracy in large organizations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manageability - the quality of being easy to control or deal with
  • Synonym: unwieldy - difficult to handle because of size or complexity

Etymology

From 'unwieldy' + '-ness'; 'unwieldy' from Middle English 'unweldy', meaning not easily handled.
