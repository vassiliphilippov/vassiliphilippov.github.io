zettabit

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛtəˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛtəbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,000 exabits or 10^21 bits, used especially in computing and data storage.
unit equal to 10^21 bits

Examples

The new data center has a theoretical capacity of over one zettabit of storage.
Internet traffic is projected to reach multiple zettabits per year by the end of the decade.
Transferring a zettabit of data would require enormous bandwidth and advanced infrastructure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exabit - a unit of information equal to 10^18 bits
  • Degree Higher: yottabit - a unit of information equal to 10^24 bits

Etymology

From prefix 'zetta-' (denoting a factor of 10^21, from Italian 'sette' meaning seven, seventh in a series of metric prefixes) + 'bit'.
