acting

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈæktɪŋ / (BrE)
the art or occupation of performing in plays, movies, or television as a character
performing a role in drama

Examples

She has been studying acting at the academy for two years.
His acting in that film was widely praised by critics.
Many people dream of making a career out of acting on stage or screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a play, concert, or other form of entertainment
  • Antonym: directing - the act of supervising the performances of actors and the staging of a play or film

Etymology

Present participle of 'act', from Latin 'agere' (to do, perform).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈæktɪŋ / (BrE)
serving temporarily in place of another, especially as a substitute in a position or office
serving temporarily as substitute

Examples

She was appointed acting manager until a permanent replacement could be found.
During the CEO's absence, the vice president served as acting director.
The acting chairman led the meeting with confidence.

Synset Examples

the acting president

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'act', used as an adjective.
