Hyalospongiae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌæloʊˈspʌndʒiˌi / (AmE), / haɪˌaləʊˈspʌndʒɪˌiː / (BrE)
A class of sponges characterized by skeletons composed of siliceous spicules with six rays, and choanocytes confined to finger-shaped chambers.
class of sponges with six-rayed siliceous spicules

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species within the Hyalospongiae during the deep-sea expedition.
The unique skeletal structure of Hyalospongiae helps differentiate them from other sponge classes.
Textbooks often illustrate the six-rayed spicules that are typical of the Hyalospongiae.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) + 'spongia' (sponge).

2. NOUN

/ haɪ.ə.ləˈspɒn.dʒi.i / (AmE), / haɪ.ə.ləˈspɒn.dʒiː / (BrE)
a class of sponges characterized by siliceous spicules with six rays and choanocytes restricted to finger-shaped chambers
class of sponges with six-rayed siliceous spicules

Examples

Scientists studied the unique skeletal structure of Hyalospongiae found in deep ocean habitats.
The siliceous spicules of Hyalospongiae are often referred to as glass-like needles.
Classification within Hyalospongiae is based on the arrangement of their six-rayed spicules.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) + 'spongia' (sponge), referring to their glass-like spicules.
