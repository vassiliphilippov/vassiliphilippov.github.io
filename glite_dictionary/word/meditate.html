to meditate

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛdɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪteɪt / (BrE)
to focus one's mind for a period of time, often for spiritual or relaxation purposes; to contemplate deeply or thoughtfully
focus mind; reflect deeply

Examples

Every morning, she likes to meditate before starting her day.
He sat quietly in the garden and meditated on the meaning of life.
Some people meditate to manage stress and improve their concentration.

Synset Examples

He is meditating in his study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contemplate - to think deeply about something
  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully

Etymology

From Latin meditari (to contemplate, reflect)

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛdəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪteɪt / (BrE)
to think deeply or focus one's mind on a particular subject, usually for a period of time and in a calm, reflective way
think deeply or reflect

Examples

Each morning, she likes to meditate on her goals for the day.
After receiving the news, he sat quietly to meditate on its implications.
Philosophers often meditate deeply on questions of existence and meaning.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contemplate - to think about something deeply and at length
  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: to speculate - to form a theory or conjecture about a subject without firm evidence

Etymology

From Latin 'meditatus,' past participle of 'meditari' (to contemplate, reflect).
