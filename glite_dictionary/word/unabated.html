unabated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈbeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈbeɪtɪd / (BrE)
continuing at full strength or intensity, without any reduction
not reduced in intensity or force

Examples

Despite the late hour, their enthusiasm remained unabated throughout the event.
The storm raged with unabated fury for several hours.
Her interest in classical music continued unabated over the years.

Synset Examples

the winds are unabated
the popularity of his books among young people continued unabated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceaseless - never stopping or pausing; unending
  • Antonym: diminished - reduced or made smaller in degree

Etymology

From un- (not) + abated (past participle of 'abate', from Old French 'abatre' meaning 'to beat down').
