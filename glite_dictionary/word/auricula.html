auricula

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered primrose native to the Alps, often cultivated for its ornamental blossoms
a yellow-flowered Alpine primrose

Examples

She bought a beautiful auricula from the florist to plant in her rock garden.
The auricula thrives best in cool, well-drained alpine soil.
Gardeners often show rare varieties of auricula at horticultural exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a flowering plant of the genus Primula

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula', diminutive of 'auris' (ear), referring to the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələ / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
a small, ear-shaped pouch projecting from the upper front part of each atrium of the heart
ear-shaped pouch of the heart
medical

Examples

A blood clot can sometimes form in the left auricula of the heart.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully examined the patient's right auricula.
The auriculae play a role in increasing the capacity of the atria.

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' (little ear), diminutive of 'auris' (ear).
