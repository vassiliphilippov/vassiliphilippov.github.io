Georges Henri Lemaître

1. PROPN

/ ʒɔːʒ ɑnˈʁi ləˈmɛtʁə / (AmE), / ʒɔːʒ ɑnˈʁi ləˈmɛtʁə / (BrE)
(1894–1966) A Belgian priest, astronomer, and cosmologist who proposed the theory of the expanding universe and is regarded as the originator of what became known as the Big Bang theory.
Belgian cosmologist, proposed Big Bang theory

Examples

Georges Henri Lemaître was one of the first scientists to suggest that the universe is expanding.
The Big Bang theory owes much to the groundbreaking work of Georges Henri Lemaître.
Many astronomers regard Georges Henri Lemaître as a pioneer in cosmology.
