prototherian

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈθɪriən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈθɪəriən / (BrE)
a member of a group of primitive egg-laying mammals, including the platypus and echidnas, native to Australia, Tasmania, and New Guinea
primitive egg-laying mammal

Examples

The platypus is a well-known example of a prototherian found in Australia.
Scientists study prototherian fossils to learn about mammalian evolution.
Unlike most mammals, prototherians lay eggs rather than giving birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal, such as platypus or echidna

Etymology

From Greek protos (first) + therion (beast).
