vaunt

1. NOUN

/ vɑnt / (AmE), / vɔːnt / (BrE)
extravagant self-praise or boasting
extravagant self-praise

Examples

The leader's endless vaunt irritated those who preferred modesty.
Her vaunt of her achievements soon wore thin with her colleagues.
In his speech, there was not a trace of vaunt or arrogance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - an act of talking with excessive pride
  • Antonym: humility - modest view of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French 'vanter' (to praise), from Latin 'vanitare' (to boast), from 'vanus' (vain, empty).

2. VERB

/ vɔnt / (AmE), / vɔːnt / (BrE)
to boast about or praise something, especially excessively or ostentatiously; to show off
to boast or show off

Examples

The company did not hesitate to vaunt its latest achievements in the press release.
He tends to vaunt his academic credentials at every opportunity.
Their vaunting of past victories irritated many in the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin 'vanitare', frequentative of 'vanus' (vain, empty).
