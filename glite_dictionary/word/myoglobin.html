myoglobin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
a red protein found in muscle tissue that receives oxygen from hemoglobin and stores it for use by the muscles
oxygen-storing protein in muscles
medical

Examples

The athlete's muscle tissue contains a high concentration of myoglobin.
Damage to muscle cells can cause myoglobin to enter the bloodstream, which may harm the kidneys.
Scientists study myoglobin to better understand how muscles store and use oxygen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoglobin - the protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from the lungs to the tissues

Etymology

From German Myoglobin, from Greek mys 'muscle' and Latin globin (protein family).
