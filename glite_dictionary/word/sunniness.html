sunniness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌni.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being filled with sunlight or bright with sunshine.
brightness from sunshine

Examples

I love the sunniness of this room every morning.
The sunniness of the weather lifted everyone's spirits at the picnic.
Her garden is known for its sunniness even in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightness - The quality of being bright or filled with light
  • Antonym: gloominess - The quality of being dark, cloudy, or lacking sunshine

Etymology

From "sunny" + "-ness" forming the noun indicating state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌn.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being cheerful and bringing brightness or positivity
brightness; cheerful quality

Examples

Her sunniness made everyone in the office feel welcome and uplifted.
Despite the gray weather, his sunniness brightened the entire classroom.
I admire her sunniness even in difficult situations.

Synset Examples

flowers added a note of cheerfulness to the drab room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloominess - the quality of being dark, depressing, or lacking cheer

Etymology

From 'sunny' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being sunny (bright or cheerful).
