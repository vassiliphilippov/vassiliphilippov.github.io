shinleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪnˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈʃɪnˌliːf / (BrE)
A North American evergreen plant with small pinkish bell-shaped flowers and oblong leaves, historically used for treating shin wounds.
North American evergreen plant

Examples

The forest floor was covered with shinleaf, their delicate flowers standing out against the green foliage.
In the past, people used shinleaf to treat wounds on their legs and shins.
Botanists found a rare species of shinleaf thriving in the national park.

Etymology

From 'shin' (leg) + 'leaf', referring to former medicinal uses of the leaves for treating shin injuries.
