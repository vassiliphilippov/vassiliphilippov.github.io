lash

1. NOUN

/ læʃ / (AmE), / læʃ / (BrE)
any of the short, curved hairs that grow along the edge of the eyelid; an eyelash
eyelid hair; eyelash

Examples

She brushed mascara onto her lashes before leaving for the party.
A single lash fell onto her cheek as she made a wish.
His dark lashes framed his bright blue eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelash - a hair growing on the edge of the eyelid

2. NOUN

/ læʃ / (AmE), / læʃ / (BrE)
the flexible leather strip that forms the end of a whip, used for striking
the leather strip of a whip

Examples

The cowboy cracked the lash of his whip to get the cattle moving.
Years of use had worn the lash on his old riding crop thin.
The leather lash left a sharp mark on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - an instrument for striking, typically consisting of a handle and a flexible lash

Etymology

From Middle English lasche (a blow), probably of Scandinavian origin (compare Danish laske).
