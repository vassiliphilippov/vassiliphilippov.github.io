Oedogoniales

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːdəˌɡoʊniˈæliːz / (AmE), / ˌiːdəˌɡəʊniˈɑːliːz / (BrE)
an order of simple or branched filamentous freshwater green algae
an order of freshwater green algae

Examples

The Oedogoniales are commonly found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats around the world.
Scientists studied the reproductive structures of Oedogoniales under the microscope.
Members of Oedogoniales play an important role in aquatic ecosystems as primary producers.

Etymology

From genus name Oedogonium + New Latin suffix -ales (denoting orders)
