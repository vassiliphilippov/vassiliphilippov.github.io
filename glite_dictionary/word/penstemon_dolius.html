Penstemon dolius

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.stəˌmɑn ˈdoʊ.li.əs / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.stɪmən ˈdəʊ.li.əs / (BrE)
A low-growing plant native to Nevada and Utah, with light blue and violet flowers in short clusters near the tips of its stems.
species of flowering plant, Nevada-Utah

Examples

The wildflowers included Penstemon dolius, adding a splash of violet to the desert landscape.
Botanists documented several mature Penstemon dolius plants during their spring survey.
Hikers in Utah stopped to admire the clusters of Penstemon dolius along the trail.

Etymology

From genus name Penstemon, from Greek 'pente' (five) + 'stēmōn' (stamen), and Latin species epithet 'dolius'.
