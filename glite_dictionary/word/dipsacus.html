Dipsacus

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˈseɪkəs / (AmE), / dɪpˈseɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall, prickly perennial herbs known as teasels, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.
genus of teasel plants

Examples

The prickly heads of Dipsacus make them easily recognizable in the wild.
Several species in the genus Dipsacus have been used in traditional medicine.
Dipsacus plants often grow in hedgerows and damp grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - a prickly plant in the genus Dipsacus, used for textile carding

Etymology

From Greek dipsakos (thirsty), referring to the plant's leaves that collect water.

2. NOUN

/ dɪpˈseɪkəs / (AmE), / dɪpˈsɑːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of prickly herbs known as teasels, native to Eurasia and Africa, and notable for their spiny flower heads.
genus of prickly teasel plants

Examples

Botanists studied several species within the Dipsacus genus in their survey of invasive plants.
The dried flower heads of Dipsacus are often used in traditional crafts.
Members of the Dipsacus genus can be recognized by their spiny stems and distinctive inflorescences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - common name for plants in the genus Dipsacus

Etymology

From Greek dipsakos (thirsty), referring to the cup formed by the leaves that can collect rainwater.
