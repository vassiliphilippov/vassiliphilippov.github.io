prolixness

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈlɪksnəs / (AmE), / prəˈlɪksnəs / (BrE)
The quality of using too many words, making speech or writing tediously lengthy and verbose.
tedious wordiness or verbosity

Examples

The prolixness of his lecture made it difficult to stay focused.
Editors often cut prolixness to improve an article’s clarity and impact.
Her novel was criticized for its excessive prolixness and meandering plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbosity - the fact or quality of using more words than needed
  • Antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point

Etymology

From Latin 'prolixus' (stretched out, extended).
