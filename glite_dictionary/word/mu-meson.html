mu-meson

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuˌmɛsɒn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːˌmiːsɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle with a negative charge and a half-life of about 2 microseconds, which decays into an electron, a neutrino, and an antineutrino
a negatively charged subatomic particle
medical

Examples

The discovery of the mu-meson contributed significantly to our understanding of particle physics.
A mu-meson is unstable and decays quickly after being produced in cosmic rays.
Scientists initially classified the mu-meson as a type of meson before realizing its distinct properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muon - an elementary particle similar to an electron, with negative charge and greater mass

Etymology

From Greek letter 'mu' (μ) + 'meson' (middle particle, from Greek 'mesos').
