gravure

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
an intaglio print made using the gravure printing process, in which an image is etched onto a printing plate
intaglio print made by gravure process

Examples

The artist's latest gravure was displayed in the gallery alongside her oil paintings.
Collectors prize early twentieth-century gravure prints for their rich detail and tonal range.
The catalog features several pages reproduced by gravure, giving the images a distinct depth and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intaglio - a printing process in which the image is incised into a surface

Etymology

From French 'gravure' (engraving), from 'graver' (to engrave).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
A printing technique in which an image is engraved onto a surface, and the engraved areas hold the ink; also refers to a print made by this process.
intaglio printing method

Examples

The magazine's elegant images were produced using gravure, resulting in rich and detailed prints.
He studied gravure to better understand advanced printing processes for art reproduction.
Modern packaging often employs gravure for high-quality color printing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intaglio - a printing process in which the image is incised into a surface

Etymology

From French 'gravure' (engraving), from 'graver' (to engrave), from Old French.

4. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
a printing process in which an image is etched or engraved onto a plate, which is then inked and wiped so that only the ink in the recessed areas creates the print
printing process using an etched plate

Examples

Many magazines are produced using the gravure printing process for high-quality images.
The gravure method allows for sharper detail and more vivid color reproduction.
Advancements in gravure technology have improved the speed and efficiency of large-scale printing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intaglio - a printing process where the image is incised into a surface
  • Antonym: relief printing - a printing process in which the printing surface is raised above the nonprinting area

Etymology

From French 'gravure' (engraving), from 'graver' (to engrave).
