keratoscope

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræt̬.ə.skoʊp / (AmE), / kəˈræt.ə.skəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to examine the cornea, especially to detect irregularities in its front surface
medical instrument for examining the cornea
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist used a keratoscope to assess the patient's corneal surface after the injury.
A keratoscope can reveal subtle distortions in the cornea that might affect vision.
Modern keratoscopes are often combined with digital imaging for more detailed analysis.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn, referring to the cornea) + 'skopein' (to look or examine).
