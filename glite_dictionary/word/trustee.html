trustee

1. NOUN

/ trʌsˈti / (AmE), / trʌsˈtiː / (BrE)
A person who is a member of a board responsible for managing the affairs or assets of an organization, such as a charity, trust, or educational institution.
member of a governing board

Examples

She was elected as a trustee of the university, helping to oversee its finances and policies.
The trustees met monthly to discuss the management of the foundation's endowment.
As a trustee, he has a legal responsibility to act in the organization's best interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - a member of the board managing a company or organization

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'trusté', past participle of 'truster', based on 'trust'.

2. NOUN

/ trʌsˈti / (AmE), / trʌsˈtiː / (BrE)
a person or organization that holds and manages assets or property for the benefit of another, under the terms of a trust
person managing assets for another
legal

Examples

The court appointed her as a trustee of the estate to oversee its distribution.
As trustee of the scholarship fund, he ensures the money is used properly.
The charity's finances are managed by a board of trustees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beneficiary - person who benefits from a trust or will

Etymology

From 'trust' + '-ee', modeled after similar legal nouns like 'lessee', from Anglo-French and Old French sources.
