doctrinal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑktrənl / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.trɪ.nəl / (BrE)
concerned with, relating to, or based on a particular set of principles or doctrines, especially in religion, law, or policy.
relating to doctrine or principles

Examples

The council debated several doctrinal issues during the meeting.
Some scholars are less interested in doctrinal differences and more focused on practical outcomes.
The church split was largely due to doctrinal disagreements.

Synset Examples

quibbling over doctrinal minutiae

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church, political party, or other group

Etymology

From Late Latin doctrīnālis, from doctrina (‘teaching, doctrine’).
