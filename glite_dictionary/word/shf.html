SHF

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs.eɪtʃˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌɛs.eɪtʃˈɛf / (BrE)
super high frequency; a range of radio frequencies from 3 to 30 gigahertz, used for radar, satellite communication, and microwave transmission.
super high frequency range (3–30 GHz)

Examples

The SHF band is widely used in microwave communication systems.
Satellite signals are often transmitted in the SHF range due to its high data capacity.
Engineers must account for signal loss when working within the SHF spectrum.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'super high frequency'.
