femtometre

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛmtoʊˌmitər / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.təˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one quadrillionth (10⁻¹⁵) of a metre, used especially in nuclear and particle physics
one quadrillionth of a metre (10⁻¹⁵ m)

Examples

The diameter of a proton is roughly one femtometre.
Physicists use the femtometre to measure atomic nuclei.
One femtometre is an extremely small distance, only useful at the subatomic level.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: attometre - a metric unit of length equal to 10⁻¹⁸ metre
  • Degree Higher: picometre - a metric unit of length equal to 10⁻¹² metre

Etymology

From Swedish 'femto-' (from 'femton', meaning fifteen) and 'metre', indicating a factor of 10 to the power of minus fifteen.
