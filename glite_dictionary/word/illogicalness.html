illogicalness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɑdʒɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒdʒɪkəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being illogical; lacking clear or valid reasoning.
lack of logical reasoning

Examples

The illogicalness of his argument made it difficult to follow.
Critics pointed out the illogicalness in the proposed solution.
She was frustrated by the illogicalness of the policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illogicality - the quality of being illogical
  • Antonym: logic - correct reasoning

Etymology

From 'illogical' + '-ness', by analogy to other nouns formed from adjectives.
