photo-offset

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈɔfsɛt / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈɒfset / (BrE)
a method of offset printing that uses photomechanical plates to reproduce images or text
printing using photomechanical plates

Examples

The magazine was produced using photo-offset printing for higher image quality.
Advancements in photo-offset made mass publication of books much easier.
The company invested in new photo-offset equipment to modernize its printing processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offset printing - a general printing technique where ink is transferred from a plate to paper via a rubber blanket

Etymology

Formed from 'photo-' (from Greek 'phōt-', meaning 'light') and 'offset' (in printing, a method where ink is transferred from a plate to a rubber blanket then to paper).
