flathead

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈflæthɛd / (BrE)
A bottom-dwelling fish with a flattened head, large eyes, and a broad, duck-like snout, found in the Indo-Pacific region.
a flat-headed bottom-dwelling fish

Examples

The fisherman was delighted to catch a large flathead in the estuary.
Many anglers use soft plastic lures to attract flathead along sandy riverbeds.
Freshly caught flathead is considered a delicacy in some coastal regions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌhɛd / (BrE)
a marine food fish of the Indo-Pacific region, with a flattened head and body, resembling gurnards
Indo-Pacific food fish with a flattened head

Examples

Fishermen caught several flathead near the coral reefs this morning.
The chef grilled a fresh flathead and served it with lemon and herbs.
Flathead is a popular choice in many Southeast Asian seafood dishes.
