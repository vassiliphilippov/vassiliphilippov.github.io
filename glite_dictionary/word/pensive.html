pensive

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Thinking deeply or seriously, often with a sad or reflective mood.
deeply thoughtful, often sad

Examples

She gazed out the window with a pensive look on her face, lost in thought.
After hearing the news, he sat in pensive silence for a long time.
The pensive expression in the portrait made viewers wonder what was on her mind.

Synset Examples

the sensitive and wistful response of a poet to the gentler phases of beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtful - absorbed in or involving thought
  • Synonym: wistful - having or showing a feeling of vague or regretful longing
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Old French 'pensif', from Latin 'pensare' (to weigh, consider).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Engaged in or involving deep or serious thought, often with a hint of sadness.
deeply thoughtful, with a hint of sadness

Examples

She sat in a pensive mood, gazing quietly through the window at the falling rain.
His pensive expression suggested he was lost in deep thought about the future.
After hearing the news, Maria grew pensive and withdrew into her thoughts for the rest of the evening.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the ‘Byronic hero’ - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtful - absorbed in or showing deep thought
  • Synonym: reflective - characterized by deep thought

Etymology

From Old French *pensif* (thoughtful), from Latin *pensare* (to ponder, consider).
