carp

1. NOUN

/ kɑrp / (AmE), / kɑːp / (BrE)
a freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae, widely kept for food or ornamental purposes.
freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae

Examples

A large carp swam lazily near the surface of the pond.
Many people keep ornamental carp, such as koi, in their garden ponds.
The fisherman was thrilled to catch a ten-pound carp in the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldfish - a small red or orange ornamental carp
  • Degree Lower: minnow - a small freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae

Etymology

From Old French *carpe*, from Late Latin *carpa*, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ kɑɹp / (AmE), / kɑːp / (BrE)
The flesh of a large freshwater fish, often farmed for food, that can be baked or braised.
the flesh of a freshwater fish used as food

Examples

The chef prepared the carp with a delicate herb sauce for the evening special.
Grilled carp is a traditional dish in many Eastern European countries.
Some people prefer to bake their carp with vegetables and spices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish of the genus Cyprinus

Etymology

From Latin 'carpa,' from Old High German 'karpho.'

3. VERB

/ kɑrp / (AmE), / kɑːp / (BrE)
to complain or find fault continually about trivial matters; to raise petty or unnecessary objections
to raise trivial complaints or objections

Examples

It's easy to carp about small errors, but it doesn't help solve the problem.
She tends to carp at everything her coworkers do, no matter how minor the issue.
Rather than carping about the meal, you should thank them for cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something

Etymology

From Old Norse *karpa* (to boast, brag; to wrangle, quarrel).
