tick-tack-toe

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪk tæk ˈtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtɪk tæk ˈtəʊ / (BrE)
A game for two players who take turns marking spaces in a grid with crosses and circles, aiming to form a row of three of their own marks.
simple two-player grid game

Examples

Whenever it rained, we would play tick-tack-toe on paper to pass the time.
The children quickly drew a tick-tack-toe board on the sidewalk with chalk.
He challenged his sister to a game of tick-tack-toe after dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noughts and crosses - British name for tick-tack-toe game

Etymology

From the words 'tick' and 'tack', possibly imitative, and 'toe', a variant of the word 'tow'. Related to the alternative name 'tic-tac-toe'.
