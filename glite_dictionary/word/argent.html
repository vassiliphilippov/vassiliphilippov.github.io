argent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
having the color or appearance of silver; silvery or lustrous grey, especially as used in heraldry or descriptive writing.
silvery; silver-colored

Examples

The knight's shield was emblazoned with an argent lion on a field of blue.
Her hair turned an argent shade as she grew older.
The sky glowed with an argent light just before dawn.

Synset Examples

silvery hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silvery - having a shiny grey or white appearance like silver

Etymology

From Old French 'argent' (silver), from Latin 'argentum' (silver).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
The silver or white color used as a metal tincture in heraldry, symbolizing silver.
the silver color in heraldry

Examples

The family crest features a lion rampant on a field of argent with blue accents.
In heraldry, argent is often used to represent peace and sincerity.
His shield bore three black crosses on argent, denoting purity and innocence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: or - the gold color (tincture) in heraldry
  • Antonym: gules - the red color (tincture) in heraldry

Etymology

From Old French *argent* (silver), from Latin *argentum* (silver).
