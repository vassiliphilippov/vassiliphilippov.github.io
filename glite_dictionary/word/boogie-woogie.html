boogie-woogie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːɡi ˌwʊɡi / (AmE), / ˌbuːɡi ˈwʊɡi / (BrE)
a style of blues music, typically played on the piano, characterized by a strong, repetitive bass figure and improvised melodies
blues-based piano music style

Examples

The pianist played a lively boogie-woogie that got everyone on their feet.
He spent hours practicing boogie-woogie rhythms on his old upright piano.
Many early rock musicians were influenced by boogie-woogie styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blues - a genre of music characterized by melancholy melodies and themes

Etymology

Reduplication of 'boogie' (a type of dance or music), originating in African-American communities in the early 20th century.
