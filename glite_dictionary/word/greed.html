greed

1. NOUN

/ ɡrid / (AmE), / ɡriːd / (BrE)
an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food, beyond what is needed or deserved
intense selfish desire, especially for wealth or power

Examples

His greed for money led him to make unethical decisions.
The company was accused of greed after laying off workers to increase profits.
Stories often portray greed as the downfall of otherwise successful people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give or share; lack of selfishness

Etymology

From Old English grēd (appetite, hunger); related to Dutch gretig and German gierig.

2. NOUN

/ ɡrid / (AmE), / ɡriːd / (BrE)
an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food; excessive acquisitiveness
intense, selfish desire for wealth or more

Examples

His greed for money led him to make unethical decisions at work.
The story warns of how greed can destroy friendships and families.
Corporate greed has been blamed for many recent scandals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and giving freely

Etymology

From Old English grǣdig (greedy, covetous); related to Old High German grātīg.
