D-layer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiˌleɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈdiːˌleɪ.ə / (BrE)
the lowest region of the ionosphere, located about 35 to 50 miles (56 to 80 km) above the Earth's surface, which reflects low-frequency radio waves
lowest region of the ionosphere

Examples

During the day, the D-layer absorbs low-frequency radio waves, reducing their transmission range.
Scientists monitor fluctuations in the D-layer to predict changes in radio communication quality.
The thickness of the D-layer varies depending on solar activity and time of day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: E-layer - region of the ionosphere above the D-layer, reflecting higher-frequency radio waves
  • Synonym: ionosphere - the part of Earth's upper atmosphere ionized by solar radiation

Etymology

Named after the convention of labelling ionospheric layers with letters; 'D' denotes its position as the lowest recognized layer.
