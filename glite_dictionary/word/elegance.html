elegance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪɡəns / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡəns / (BrE)
the quality of being simple, ingenious, and effective, especially in the solution of a problem in science or mathematics
simplicity and ingenious effectiveness

Examples

The elegance of her mathematical proof impressed the entire faculty.
He admired the elegance of the code, which solved the problem in just a few lines.
The scientist was praised for the elegance of his experiment's design.

Synset Examples

the simplicity and elegance of his invention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple

Etymology

From Latin elegantia (taste, refinement).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪɡəns / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡəns / (BrE)
the quality of being graceful and stylish in appearance or manner; refined beauty and good taste
refined grace or stylishness

Examples

Her elegance was evident in the way she carried herself at the event.
The room was decorated with a simplicity that added to its elegance.
He admired the elegance of her writing style.

Synset Examples

she conveys an aura of elegance and gentility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracefulness - the quality of moving in a smooth, attractive way
  • Antonym: coarseness - lack of refinement or good taste

Etymology

From French 'élégance', from Latin 'elegantia' (refinement, tasteful choice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04819517-n
Synset POS: n
