subterraneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌbtəˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / ˌsʌbtəˈreɪnɪəs / (BrE)
existing, situated, or operating beneath the surface of the earth.
beneath the earth's surface

Examples

The miners discovered a subterraneous tunnel that led to an unknown chamber.
Much of the city's water supply is carried through subterraneous pipes.
Scientists investigate subterraneous ecosystems to understand life beneath the surface.

Synset Examples

subterranean passages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subterranean - existing or situated below the surface of the earth

Etymology

From Latin 'subterraneus', from 'sub' (under) + 'terra' (earth).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsʌbtəˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / ˌsʌbtəˈreɪnɪəs / (BrE)
(formal) hidden or concealed; not openly shown or acknowledged.
hidden; not openly revealed

Examples

Some politicians act out of subterraneous motives not disclosed to the public.
Her outward kindness masked a subterraneous resentment.
Investigators searched for a subterraneous reason behind the sudden resignation.

Synset Examples

subterranean motives for murder
looked too closely for an ulterior purpose in all knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulterior - existing beyond what is openly revealed; hidden
  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin subterraneus (underground), from sub (under) + terra (earth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01712186-s
Synset POS: s
