Abelmoschus esculentus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəlˈmɑskəs ˌɛskjəˈlɛntəs / (AmE), / ˌabɛlˈmɒskəs ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for okra, a tall annual plant cultivated for its edible green pods used in cooking, especially in soups and stews
okra plant; edible green pods

Examples

Abelmoschus esculentus is widely cultivated in warm regions for its nutritious pods.
Researchers studied the benefits of Abelmoschus esculentus in controlling blood sugar levels.
The garden features several rows of Abelmoschus esculentus alongside other tropical vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okra - common name for Abelmoschus esculentus, the edible green pod

Etymology

From Latinized New Latin; genus Abelmoschus from Arabic *abū al-musk* (father of musk), and species *esculentus* meaning 'edible' in Latin.
