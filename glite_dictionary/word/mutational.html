mutational

1. ADJ

/ mjuˈteɪʃənəl / (AmE), / mjuːˈteɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by mutation, especially a change in the structure of genes or chromosomes
relating to or caused by mutation
medical

Examples

Scientists observed a mutational change in the plant's DNA.
The disease is associated with several mutational hotspots in the genome.
Understanding the mutational processes helps researchers discover the origins of genetic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutation - a change in the genetic material of an organism

Etymology

From 'mutation' + '-al'; 'mutation' from Latin 'mutatio' (a changing)
