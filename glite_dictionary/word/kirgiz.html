Kirgiz

1. NOUN

/ kɪrˈɡiz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group traditionally inhabiting Kyrgyzstan and neighboring regions of Central Asia and Siberia
a member of a Central Asian ethnic group

Examples

Many Kirgiz still practice traditional nomadic herding in the highlands.
The Kirgiz are known for their distinctive equestrian games and yurt-dwelling culture.
A group of Kirgiz migrated across the mountains in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - a member of the same Turkic ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian 'киргиз' (kirgiz), itself from Turkic roots.

2. NOUN

/ kɪrˈɡiz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken chiefly in Kyrgyzstan and neighboring areas
Turkic language of Kyrgyzstan

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Kirgiz.
The film was subtitled in English, Russian, and Kirgiz.
Linguists have noted the unique vowel harmony in Kirgiz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - alternative spelling of the same language and its people

Etymology

From Russian Киргиз (Kirgiz), ultimately from Turkic roots.

3. PROPN

/ kɪrˈɡiz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of Kyrgyzstan, a landlocked country in Central Asia, formerly part of the Soviet Union
variant spelling of Kyrgyzstan (country in Central Asia)

Examples

Many travelers are fascinated by the mountainous landscapes of Kirgiz.
After gaining independence in 1991, Kirgiz began to develop its own national identity.
The border between Kirgiz and China is marked by rugged terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyzstan - a landlocked republic in central Asia; the standard English form for this country

Etymology

From Russian 'Киргиз' (Kirgiz), relating to the Kyrgyz people and nation.
