Rubus fruticosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs fruːˈtɪkoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs fruːˈtɪkəsəs / (BrE)
the European blackberry; a bramble plant bearing sweet, edible black or dark purple berries, and any of several related varieties
the European blackberry plant

Examples

Wild Rubus fruticosus is commonly found along hedgerows throughout Europe.
The sweet berries of Rubus fruticosus are popular in jams and desserts.
Gardeners appreciate Rubus fruticosus for its vigorous growth and abundant fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - an edible dark purple or black berry produced by various species of bramble

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rubus' meaning bramble and 'fruticosus' meaning shrubby/bushy.
