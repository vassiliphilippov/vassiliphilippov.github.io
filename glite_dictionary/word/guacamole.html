guacamole

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡwɑːkəˈmoʊli / (AmE), / ˌɡwækəˈməʊli / (BrE)
A thick, creamy dip made from mashed avocados, often mixed with chopped onions, tomatoes, lime juice, and seasonings.
avocado-based dip

Examples

She served homemade guacamole with a bowl of tortilla chips at the party.
I ordered tacos with a side of guacamole and salsa.
Fresh guacamole adds flavor and creaminess to any Mexican dish.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'guacamole', from Nahuatl 'ahuacamolli' (avocado sauce).
