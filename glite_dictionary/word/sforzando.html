sforzando

1. NOUN

/ sfɔrˈzɑndoʊ / (AmE), / sfɔːˈtsandəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) an instruction to play a note or chord with a sudden, strong emphasis.
sudden musical emphasis

Examples

The composer marked the passage with a sforzando to highlight its dramatic character.
Make sure you give the sforzando the strong attack it requires in measure sixteen.
The sudden sforzando startled the audience during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: forte - (music) loud; played or sung loudly
  • Degree Higher: fortissimo - (music) very loud; played or sung very loudly

Etymology

From Italian 'sforzando', present participle of 'sforzare' (to force).

2. NOUN

/ sfɔrˈzɑndoʊ / (AmE), / sfɔːˈtsandəʊ / (BrE)
A musical instruction indicating that a note or chord should be played with a sudden, strong emphasis.
sudden musical accent

Examples

The composer marked the chord with a sforzando to give it extra impact.
That measure features a striking sforzando that surprises the audience.
You should play the note with a clear sforzando as indicated in the sheet music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accent - a mark or instruction showing emphasis on a note in music

Etymology

From Italian sforzando, present participle of sforzare (to force), from Latin fortis (strong).
