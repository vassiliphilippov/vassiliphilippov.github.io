Heteroptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəˈrɒptərə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
A suborder of insects within the order Hemiptera, commonly known as true bugs, characterized by having forewings with both membranous and hardened portions.
suborder of true bugs (insects)

Examples

Members of the Heteroptera suborder include stink bugs, water striders, and bed bugs.
The forewings of Heteroptera are partly hardened and partly membranous, a key identifying feature.
Scientists studied the distribution of Heteroptera in freshwater habitats across the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hemiptera - the order of insects including true bugs and related species

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + pteron (wing), referring to the structure of their wings.
