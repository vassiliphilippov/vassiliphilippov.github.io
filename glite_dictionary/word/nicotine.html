nicotine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkətiːn / (AmE), / ˈnɪkətiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous, addictive alkaloid present in tobacco plants and used in medicine and as an insecticide
addictive compound in tobacco

Examples

The addictive properties of nicotine make quitting smoking difficult for many people.
Exposure to large amounts of nicotine can be toxic and even fatal in some cases.
Some products claim to help reduce nicotine cravings during smoking cessation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caffeine - stimulant alkaloid found in coffee and tea
  • Synonym: tobacco - plant containing nicotine, used for smoking and chewing

Etymology

From French nicotine, named after Jean Nicot, who introduced tobacco to France.
