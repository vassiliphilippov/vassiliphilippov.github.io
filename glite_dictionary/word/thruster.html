thruster

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrʌstər / (AmE), / ˈθrʌstə / (BrE)
A small rocket engine used to provide thrust for maneuvering a spacecraft.
spacecraft maneuvering engine

Examples

During docking, the spacecraft fired its thruster to adjust its position.
Astronauts use handheld thrusters during spacewalks to move around safely.
The malfunctioning thruster caused the satellite to drift off its intended orbit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine - machine with moving parts that converts power into motion
  • Synonym: rocket - device propelled by exhaust, used for propulsion in space

Etymology

From 'thrust' (to push with force) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrʌstər / (AmE), / ˈθrʌstə / (BrE)
a person who pushes themselves forward aggressively or intrudes where they are not wanted
an intrusive or self-assertive person

Examples

Everyone found the new manager to be something of a thruster, always seeking attention at meetings.
The board disliked having a thruster among them who pushed ahead without considering others' opinions.
In politics, it's not uncommon for a thruster to climb the ranks through sheer assertiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intruder - a person who enters or becomes involved in a place or situation where they are unwelcome
  • Synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities with little regard for principle or consequences
  • Antonym: reticent - reluctant to speak or act; reserved or restrained

Etymology

From 'thrust' (to push with force) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
