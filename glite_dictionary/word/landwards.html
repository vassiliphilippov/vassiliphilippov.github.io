landwards

1. ADV

/ ˈlændwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈlændwədz / (BrE)
toward the land or inland, away from the sea
toward the land; inland direction

Examples

We walked landwards from the shore to escape the rising tide.
The wind was blowing landwards, bringing with it salty air from the ocean.
Migratory birds returned landwards at the end of summer.

Synset Examples

landward, miles of rough grass marshes melt into low uplands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seawards - toward the sea; in the direction of the sea

Etymology

From 'land' + suffix '-wards' (directional), akin to 'seawards'.
