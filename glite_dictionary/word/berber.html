Berber

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrbər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbə / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous people of northern Africa, especially Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, and Libya.
member of an indigenous North African people

Examples

Many Berber communities maintain distinct languages and traditions in Morocco.
Ancient rock art in the Sahara was created by early Berber peoples.
He learned several words in the Berber language during his travels.

Etymology

From Arabic *barbar*, likely imitative of perceived speech or referring to non-Greek, non-Roman peoples.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːbər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbə / (BrE)
a group of closely related languages and dialects historically spoken across North Africa, especially in Morocco and Algeria
group of North African languages

Examples

Linguists have studied the Berber languages to trace the history of North Africa.
He is fluent in both Berber and Arabic, reflecting the region's cultural diversity.
Many traditional tales are still told in Berber across remote mountain villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afro-Asiatic - a large language family to which Berber languages belong
  • Antonym: Arabic - the dominant language replacing Berber in much of North Africa

Etymology

From Arabic barbar, referring to the Berber people and their languages.
