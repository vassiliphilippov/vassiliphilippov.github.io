Icteria

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of American songbirds in the family Icteriidae, known as New World chats, including the yellow-breasted chat.
genus of American songbirds (New World chats)

Examples

The Icteria genus is noted for its only member, the yellow-breasted chat.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot Icteria species during their migration season.
Scientists debated the classification of Icteria for years before assigning it to its own family.

Etymology

From Greek ikteros (jaundice), likely referring to the yellow color of the bird's breast.

2. NOUN

/ aɪkˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds commonly known as the yellow-breasted chats, belonging to the family Icteriidae.
genus of North American songbirds (yellow-breasted chats)

Examples

The yellow-breasted chat is the best-known member of the genus Icteria.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare migration of Icteria in the region.
Taxonomists have debated whether Icteria should be placed in a distinct bird family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek ikteros (jaundice-yellow), relating to the yellow color of the type species.
