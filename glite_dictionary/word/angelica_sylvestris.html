Angelica sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪkə sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə sɪlˈviːstrɪs / (BrE)
a species of European herb with divided leaves and umbels of small white flowers, found in woods and damp meadows
a species of European herbaceous plant

Examples

Angelica sylvestris thrives in damp meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
Botanists noted the spread of Angelica sylvestris on Cape Breton Island.
The white umbels of Angelica sylvestris attract many pollinators during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelica - genus of plants including Angelica sylvestris

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Angelica' from medieval Latin, possibly referring to angelic healing properties; 'sylvestris' from Latin for 'of the woods.'
