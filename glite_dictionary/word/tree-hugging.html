tree-hugging

1. ADJ

/ ˈtriˌhʌɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtriːˌhʌɡɪŋ / (BrE)
showing strong support for protecting the natural environment, often through activism or advocacy; sometimes used informally or disparagingly
strong support for environmental protection
informal

Examples

Many people considered his tree-hugging attitude too radical for mainstream politics.
The company adopted tree-hugging policies to reduce its environmental impact.
Some critics dismiss tree-hugging activism as unrealistic, but it has inspired important change.

Synset Examples

His tree-hugging friends organized a cleanup event.
They held a tree-hugging festival to raise awareness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmentalist - a person who is concerned with or advocates for the protection of the environment
  • Antonym: anti-environmentalist - a person opposed to environmental protection measures

Etymology

Formed from 'tree' and 'hugging', after the image of people literally hugging trees to protect them.
