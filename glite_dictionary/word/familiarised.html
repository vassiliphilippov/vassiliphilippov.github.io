familiarised

1. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliəraɪzd / (BrE)
having become comfortable and well-acquainted with a place, situation, or system
becoming comfortable in a situation or place
british

Examples

After a few weeks, the new employees felt familiarised with the company's procedures.
It took him some time to become familiarised with the local customs.
Sarah quickly familiarised herself with the office layout during her first day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accustomed - being used to something
  • Antonym: unfamiliar - not known or recognized

Etymology

From British spelling of 'familiarize', from Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, intimate).
