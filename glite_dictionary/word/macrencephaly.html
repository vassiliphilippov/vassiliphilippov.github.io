macrencephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.rɛnˈsɛf.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌmak.rɛnˈsɛf.ə.li / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an abnormally large braincase.
abnormally large braincase (medical)
medical

Examples

Infants diagnosed with macrencephaly often require specialized neurological care.
The presence of macrencephaly can be detected through imaging techniques such as MRI.
Researchers are still studying the genetic causes of macrencephaly in newborns.

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + enkephalos (brain).
