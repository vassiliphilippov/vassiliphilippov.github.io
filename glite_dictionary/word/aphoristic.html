aphoristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæfəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌæfəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
written or expressed in the form of brief, wise, or concise statements (aphorisms or maxims)
expressed in brief, wise statements

Examples

Her aphoristic style made the essay both memorable and thought-provoking.
Many of his speeches are aphoristic, filled with short, powerful statements.
The book is known for its aphoristic observations about human nature.

Synset Examples

axiomatic wisdom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succinct - briefly and clearly expressed, especially in writing or speech
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy, the opposite of concise

Etymology

From aphorism + -ic.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæfərˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌæfəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
expressed in a concise, witty, and memorable way, like an aphorism or maxim
terse and witty; like an aphorism

Examples

Her aphoristic remarks always left the audience thinking.
The essay is known for its aphoristic style and memorable expressions.
He preferred aphoristic wisdom to lengthy explanations.

Synset Examples

much given to apothegmatic instruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxim - a short, pithy statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct
  • Synonym: proverbial - relating to or resembling a proverb; commonly referred to
  • Antonym: verbose - using or expressed in more words than are needed

Etymology

From 'aphorism' (concise statement of a principle), itself from Greek 'aphorismos' (a definition, concise statement).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00549171-s
Synset POS: s
