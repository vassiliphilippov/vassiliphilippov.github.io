to writhe

1. VERB

/ raɪð / (AmE), / raɪð / (BrE)
to twist or move one's body with continual squirming or contorted motion, usually as a result of pain or discomfort
twist and squirm in discomfort or pain

Examples

The injured player began to writhe on the ground in obvious pain.
She tried not to writhe as the dentist pulled her tooth.
He writhed with embarrassment when his mistake was revealed in front of the class.

Synset Examples

The prisoner writhed in discomfort
The child tried to wriggle free from his aunt's embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirm - to wiggle or wriggle the body as if to escape
  • Synonym: twist - to turn or bend the body or something in a specified way

Etymology

From Old English 'wrīthan' (to twist, bend)
