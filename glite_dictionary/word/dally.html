to dally

1. VERB

/ ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈdæli / (BrE)
to waste time or delay, especially by being slow or indecisive
waste time; be slow or indecisive

Examples

The students were told not to dally in the hallway after the bell rang.
If you dally too long, you'll miss your train.
She tends to dally over her coffee every morning, making her late.

Synset Examples

Get busy — don't dally!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary, often wasting time
  • Antonym: to hasten - to move or act quickly, opposite of delay

Etymology

From Old French *dallier* (to waste time, chat, amuse oneself).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02015537-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈdæli / (BrE)
to act or move slowly or without serious intention, especially by playing with someone's feelings or delaying action
act or move without seriousness

Examples

He tended to dally with his assignments, leaving them unfinished until the last minute.
She warned him not to dally with her emotions if he wasn't serious.
The guests dallied in the garden, unconcerned about the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

Play about with a young girl's affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifle - to treat someone or something without seriousness or respect

Etymology

From Old French 'dalier' (to chat, waste time), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02442312-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈdæli / (BrE)
to consider or deal with something in a casual or unserious way; to toy with an idea or possibility without commitment
to consider or treat casually

Examples

She tends to dally with different career options without making a decision.
Don't dally over the idea if you have no intention of following through.
He would often dally with plans for travel, never committing to any.

Synset Examples

He is trifling with her
She plays with the thought of moving to Tasmania

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifle - to treat someone or something without seriousness or respect
  • Synonym: toy - to consider something lightly or playfully, not seriously

Etymology

From Old French dalier (to chat, amuse oneself), of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00713761-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈdæli / (BrE)
to talk or behave flirtatiously without serious intentions
flirt without serious intentions

Examples

He tends to dally with his colleagues at office parties, but never takes it further.
She accused him of trying to dally with her feelings instead of being sincere.
If you dally with everyone you meet, people may question your intentions.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - behave as though attracted to someone without serious intent
  • Antonym: court - try to win the affection of someone in a serious way

Etymology

From Old French 'dalier' (to amuse oneself, chat), origin uncertain.

Source: WordNet
