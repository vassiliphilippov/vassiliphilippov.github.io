rootstalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtˌstɔk / (AmE), / ˈruːtˌstɔːk / (BrE)
a horizontal underground stem of a plant with shoots above and roots below that serves as a means of vegetative reproduction.
horizontal underground plant stem

Examples

Many irises spread by sending out a rootstalk beneath the soil surface.
The gardener divided the plant at its rootstalk to propagate new shoots.
Ginger is harvested from its thick, aromatic rootstalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhizome - a horizontal, usually underground stem that often sends out roots and shoots

Etymology

Formed from 'root' and 'stalk,' describing its function and form.
