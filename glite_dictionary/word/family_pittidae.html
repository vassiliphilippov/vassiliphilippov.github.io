family Pittidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ˈpɪtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˈpɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, ground-dwelling birds known as pittas, found mainly in tropical Asia, Africa, and Australia.
family of birds: pittas

Examples

The family Pittidae includes some of the most colorful songbirds in the world.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of the family Pittidae in Southeast Asia.
Many members of the family Pittidae are threatened by deforestation and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitta - a brightly colored, ground-dwelling bird of the family Pittidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Pittidae, based on the type genus Pitta.
