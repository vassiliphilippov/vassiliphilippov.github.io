chirrup

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪrəp / (BrE)
a series of short, sharp, high-pitched sounds, especially those made by small birds or insects
a series of short, high-pitched sounds

Examples

The chirrup of sparrows filled the quiet morning air.
We listened to the gentle chirrup of crickets at dusk.
A sudden chirrup from the hedge made her pause in her walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp, high-pitched sound, especially from a bird or insect

Etymology

Imitative origin; related to 'chirp' and influenced by sound repetition.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪrəp / (BrE)
(of a bird or insect) to make repeated short, high-pitched sounds; to chirp or twitter.
make repeated short, high-pitched sounds

Examples

Early in the morning, the sparrows chirrup outside my window.
She listened as the crickets chirruped softly in the grass.
The children giggled as they tried to chirrup like the little birds in the garden.

Synset Examples

the birds were chirping in the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - to make short, sharp, high-pitched sounds (especially a bird or insect)
  • Synonym: twitter - to make a series of light, high-pitched sounds (esp. birds)
Source: WordNet
