pliantness

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈplaɪəntnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being pliant; flexibility or adaptability.
flexibility; adaptability

Examples

The pliantness of the material makes it ideal for crafting intricate shapes.
Her pliantness in negotiations helped the team reach a compromise quickly.
Over time, yoga increases both strength and pliantness in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppleness - the quality of being easily bent or flexed
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and inflexible

Etymology

From 'pliant' (from Old French, from plier 'to bend') + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪ.ənt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.ənt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily adaptable in mind or character; flexibility or responsiveness to change
adaptability of mind or character

Examples

Her pliantness allowed her to adapt quickly to new challenges at work.
Some leaders mistake pliantness for weakness, but it often requires great strength.
The pliantness of the committee made it easy to implement reforms.

Synset Examples

he was valued for his reliability and pliability
he increased the leanness and suppleness of the organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking; willingness to change
  • Antonym: rigidity - inability or unwillingness to change or be adapted

Etymology

From Middle English 'pliant' (from Old French), ultimately from Latin 'plicare' (to fold).
