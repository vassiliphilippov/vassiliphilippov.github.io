incarnadine

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɑrnəˌdin / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑːnədiːn / (BrE)
to make or color something flesh-colored, especially with a pinkish or reddish hue
color or make flesh-colored

Examples

A warm light seemed to incarnadine the clouds at sunset.
The artist incarnadined the cherub's cheeks to give them a lifelike glow.
Her memories of those days incarnadined her thoughts with nostalgia.

Etymology

From Italian incarnadino, from Latin incarnatus (made flesh), referring to the color of flesh.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kɑːr.nəˈdiːn / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑː.nə.diːn / (BrE)
of a deep red or crimson color.
deep red or crimson color

Examples

The roses in the garden bloomed in an incarnadine hue that caught everyone's attention.
Her lips were painted a glossy incarnadine, making her smile even more striking.
He stared at the incarnadine streaks that marked the evening sky.

Synset Examples

She wore a dress that was a stunning incarnadine shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a rich deep red color inclining to purple
  • Synonym: scarlet - bright red color with a slight orange tinge
  • Synonym: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet pigment

Etymology

From Latin 'incarnatinus', from 'incarnare' meaning 'to make flesh'; originally referring to the color of flesh.
