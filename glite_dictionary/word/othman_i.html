Othman I

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑθˌmɑn ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈɒθˌmæn ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
the founder of the Ottoman Empire and its ruling dynasty, who established his rule over most of Asia Minor in the late 13th and early 14th centuries
founder of the Ottoman Empire

Examples

Othman I is credited with founding the Ottoman Empire, which grew to rule much of the Middle East and Europe.
Many historians consider Othman I's leadership style instrumental in uniting the Turkish tribes.
The reign of Othman I marked the beginning of centuries of Ottoman dominance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ottoman Empire - empire founded by Othman I, spanning Southeast Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa

Etymology

From Turkish Osman (from Arabic ʿUthmān), romanized as Othman in older sources.
