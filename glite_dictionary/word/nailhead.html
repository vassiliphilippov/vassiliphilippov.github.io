nailhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈneɪlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a small knob or decorative feature resembling the head of a nail, used especially as an ornament in furniture or architecture
decorative knob like a nail's head

Examples

The antique chair was trimmed with brass nailheads around the seat cushion.
They chose a sofa accented with nailhead trim for a classic look.
The designer pointed out the elegant nailhead details on the cabinet doors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stud - a small decorative knob typically used in furniture or fashion

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈneɪlˌhɛd / (BrE)
the flat, enlarged end of a nail opposite the point, designed to be struck by a hammer
flat end of a nail (hardware)

Examples

The hammer accidentally slipped off the nailhead and dented the wood.
She painted each nailhead with a coat of black enamel for a decorative touch.
Make sure you strike the nailhead squarely to drive the nail in straight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - a small metal spike with a sharp point and a flat head
