facilitation

1. NOUN

/ fəˌsɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˌsɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
In neurophysiology, a phenomenon in which two or more neural impulses, each insufficient alone to trigger a response, combine to produce an action potential.
neural impulses combining to trigger action
medical

Examples

The facilitation of nerve impulses can lead to more efficient signal transmission in neural pathways.
Researchers studied the facilitation that occurs when subthreshold stimuli combine in the nervous system.
Synaptic facilitation is crucial for understanding how information is processed in the brain.

Etymology

From Latin facilitatio, from facilitare (to make easy).

2. NOUN

/ fəˌsɪləˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˌsɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making a process or task easier or helping it to progress more smoothly
the act of making something easier

Examples

The facilitation of the workshop was handled by an experienced trainer.
Effective facilitation can improve communication and decision-making in meetings.
Technological advances have led to the facilitation of remote learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistance - help or support; the action of assisting
  • Antonym: obstruction - the act of blocking or hindering progress

Etymology

From Latin facilitas (easiness, readiness) via French facilitation.
