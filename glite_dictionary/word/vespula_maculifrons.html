Vespula maculifrons

1. NOUN

/ vɛsˈpjuːlə ˌmækjʊˈlɪfrɒnz / (AmE), / vɛsˈpjuːlə ˌmækjʊˈlɪfrɒnz / (BrE)
A small species of yellow-marked social wasp, commonly found nesting in the ground in North America.
species of yellow-marked ground wasp

Examples

The Vespula maculifrons is often seen buzzing around picnic areas searching for food.
Researchers studied the nesting behavior of Vespula maculifrons in suburban gardens.
Unlike honeybees, Vespula maculifrons typically build their colonies underground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowjacket - common name for several species of wasps in the genus Vespula

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vespula' (a genus of wasps) and 'maculifrons' meaning 'spotted forehead'.
