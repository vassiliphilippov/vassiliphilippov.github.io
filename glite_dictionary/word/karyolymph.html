karyolymph

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæri.oʊ.lɪmf / (AmE), / ˈkæri.əʊ.lɪmf / (BrE)
the clear, fluid substance found inside the cell nucleus, in which structures like the nucleolus and chromatin are suspended
clear fluid inside a cell nucleus
medical

Examples

The karyolymph provides a medium for the movement of materials within the nucleus.
Under the microscope, the scientist observed details suspended in the karyolymph.
Changes in the composition of karyolymph can affect nuclear processes.

Etymology

From Greek 'karyo-' (nut, nucleus) + 'lymph' (clear fluid).
