peevishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːvɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːvɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easily irritated or annoyed
irritable or easily annoyed state

Examples

His peevishness made it difficult for others to enjoy his company at the meeting.
She apologized for her peevishness after realizing she had snapped at her friends.
The manager's peevishness grew as the stressful day dragged on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: good-naturedness - the quality of having a pleasant and easy-going disposition

Etymology

From 'peevish' + '-ness,' where 'peevish' dates to late Middle English of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːvɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːvɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily irritated or showing annoyance or impatience
irritability; easily annoyed state

Examples

His peevishness made working with him quite difficult during stressful days.
Despite her peevishness, her friends remained patient and understanding.
He later apologized for his peevishness after realizing he had snapped at everyone.

Synset Examples

his temper was well known to all his employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed
  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being calm and not easily annoyed

Etymology

From 'peevish' + '-ness'; 'peevish' likely from late Middle English, of unknown origin.
