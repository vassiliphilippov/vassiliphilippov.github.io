plangent

1. ADJ

/ ˈplæn.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈplæn.dʒənt / (BrE)
(of a sound) loud, deep, and resonant, often with a mournful tone.
loud and resonant (of sound)

Examples

The plangent bells echoed through the quiet village at dusk.
Her voice had a plangent quality that lingered in the memory.
A plangent note of sorrow rang out in the funeral dirge.

Synset Examples

plangent bells
the plangent minority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonant - deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • Synonym: sonorous - (of a sound) imposingly deep and full
  • Antonym: muffled - (of a sound) not loud because obstructed in some way

Etymology

From Latin *plangere* (to strike, to lament).
