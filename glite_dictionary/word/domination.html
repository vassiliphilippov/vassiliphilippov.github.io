domination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the exercise of power or control over others, especially in a social or political context
exercise of power or control

Examples

The company's domination of the market raised concerns among competitors.
Throughout history, many societies have struggled against the domination of oppressive rulers.
Psychological domination can be just as damaging as physical control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under domination or control
  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or exercise of authority
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants

Etymology

From Latin 'dominatio' (rule, sovereignty), from 'dominari' (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the exercise of power or influence over others; the state of having control or authority to defeat or master
power to control or defeat

Examples

The company's domination of the market has made it difficult for new businesses to compete.
For centuries, the empire maintained its domination over vast territories across Europe.
Their domination in the championship was clear after they won every single match.

Synset Examples

mastery of the seas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastery - complete control or superiority over something or someone
  • Antonym: submission - the act of accepting the authority or control of someone else

Etymology

From Latin 'dominatio' (rule, sovereignty), from 'dominari' (to rule, dominate).
