mentality

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / mɛnˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the capacity for intelligent thought; intelligence or mental ability
mental ability; intelligence

Examples

Her sharp mentality helped her solve the complex puzzle in minutes.
The test is designed to measure the mentality of students in different age groups.
Despite his young age, his mentality rivals that of experienced professionals.

Synset Examples

he's got plenty of brains but no common sense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • Degree Higher: brilliance - exceptional talent or intelligence
  • Degree Lower: dullness - lack of intelligence or mental ability

Etymology

From French 'mentalité', from Late Latin 'mentalitas' (‘state of mind’).

2. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / menˈtalɪti / (BrE)
A particular way of thinking; a habitual or characteristic mental attitude that determines how a person interprets and responds to situations.
way of thinking; mental attitude

Examples

Her competitive mentality helped her to succeed in a demanding career.
To change the team's fortunes, we need to shift their mentality from defeatist to optimistic.
He approached every challenge with a scientific mentality, analyzing problems before acting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mindset - a person's way of thinking and their opinions
  • Synonym: attitude - a settled way of thinking or feeling about something

Etymology

From French mentalité, from Latin mentalis (of the mind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06205574-n
Synset POS: n
