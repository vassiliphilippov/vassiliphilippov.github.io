Australopithecus boisei

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔstrələˌpɪθəkəs ˈbɔɪzaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒstrələˌpɪθəkəs ˈbɔɪzaɪ / (BrE)
an extinct species of large-toothed early hominid that lived in eastern Africa about 1 to 2 million years ago
extinct East African hominid species

Examples

Fossil evidence of Australopithecus boisei has been discovered in Tanzania and Ethiopia.
The powerful jaws of Australopithecus boisei suggest a diet of tough vegetation.
Scientists debate how Australopithecus boisei fits into the evolutionary tree of early humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus robustus - another robust early hominid species from southern Africa
  • Degree Lower: Australopithecus - genus of extinct hominids from Africa
  • Degree Higher: Homo habilis - an early human species that appeared after Australopithecus

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'Australopithecus' (southern ape) and the name of Charles Boise, a benefactor of research.
