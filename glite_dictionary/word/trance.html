trance

1. NOUN

/ træns / (AmE), / trɑːns / (BrE)
A style of electronic dance music featuring a fast tempo, repetitive melodic phrases, and a hypnotic or dreamy effect.
genre of electronic dance music

Examples

She loves listening to trance when studying because it helps her concentrate.
The DJ played a set filled with uplifting trance tracks at the festival.
Many clubs feature special nights dedicated exclusively to trance music.

Synset Examples

Classic trance employs a 4/4 time signature, a tempo of 125 to 150 BPM, and 32 beat phrases and is somewhat faster than house music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: techno - genre of electronic dance music with a repetitive beat and synthesized sounds
  • Synonym: house - a genre of electronic dance music with a repetitive 4/4 beat

2. NOUN

/ træns / (AmE), / trɑːns / (BrE)
A state of mind in which a person is not fully conscious and may not be aware of what is happening around them, often as if under a spell or induced by hypnosis.
semi-conscious spellbound state

Examples

During the ceremony, the priest fell into a deep trance and spoke in strange tongues.
The magician claimed he could put audience members into a trance with just a few words.
Lost in thought, she moved about the room as if in a trance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - an artificially induced state of consciousness, similar to a trance
  • Synonym: ecstasy - an intense state of emotion or trance-like absorption

Etymology

From Old French 'transe' (fear, passage), from Latin 'transīre' (to cross, go across).

3. NOUN

/ træns / (AmE), / trɑːns / (BrE)
a state of mind in which a person is not fully conscious and cannot control their actions, often resembling deep sleep or being hypnotized
semiconscious state or hypnotic condition

Examples

The shaman entered a deep trance during the ritual.
The music put her into a trance that lasted for hours.
He seemed to be in a trance, staring blankly at the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - a trance-like state of focused attention and heightened suggestibility
  • Degree Higher: coma - a state of deep unconsciousness

Etymology

From Old French 'transir' (to pass away, die, be numb with fear) and Latin 'transīre' (to cross, pass over).
