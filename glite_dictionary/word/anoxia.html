anoxia

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑksiə / (AmE), / əˈnɒksiə / (BrE)
a complete lack of oxygen in the body tissues, blood, or inspired air, often leading to serious health consequences
complete lack of oxygen
medical

Examples

The patient developed anoxia after being deprived of oxygen for several minutes.
Brain cells can be permanently damaged by prolonged anoxia.
Doctors are trained to recognize the signs of anoxia in emergency situations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypoxia - a condition in which there is a deficiency of oxygen in the tissues

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'oxys' (sharp, oxygen).
