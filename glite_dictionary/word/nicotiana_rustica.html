Nicotiana rustica

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌkoʊʃiˈænə ˈrʌstɪkə / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊtɪˈɑːnə ˈrʌstɪkə / (BrE)
a species of tobacco plant native to South America and Mexico, often used for its high nicotine content
South American/Mexican tobacco plant species

Examples

Indigenous peoples traditionally grew Nicotiana rustica for shamanic rituals and healing ceremonies.
The leaves of Nicotiana rustica contain much higher nicotine levels than ordinary tobacco.
Museum displays showcased dried Nicotiana rustica to illustrate its cultural importance in Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicotiana tabacum - the main cultivated species of tobacco plant

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Nicotiana' after Jean Nicot, French ambassador who introduced tobacco to France; 'rustica' meaning 'rural' or 'wild'.
