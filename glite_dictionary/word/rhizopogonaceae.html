Rhizopogonaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪzoʊˌpoʊɡəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌraɪzəʊˌpəʊɡəˈneɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Hymenogastrales, characterized by round, subterranean fruiting bodies (sporophores).
family of fungi with underground fruiting bodies

Examples

Members of the Rhizopogonaceae play important ecological roles in forest soils.
Truffles produced by Rhizopogonaceae are vital to many small mammals' diets.
Scientists are studying the symbiotic relationships formed by Rhizopogonaceae with tree roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a member of the kingdom Fungi; includes yeasts, molds, mushrooms

Etymology

From New Latin Rhizopogon (type genus) + -aceae (standard suffix for plant and fungal families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪzoʊˌpoʊɡəˈneɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌraɪzəʊˌpəʊɡəˈneɪsi.i / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Hymenogastrales, characterized by round subterranean sporophores.
a family of fungi with underground fruiting bodies

Examples

The Rhizopogonaceae are known for their symbiotic relationships with certain tree roots.
Mycology students studied specimens from the Rhizopogonaceae family during their lab session.
Some truffle-like fungi in the Rhizopogonaceae are important for forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a member of the kingdom Fungi

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'rhiza' (root) and 'pogon' (beard), named for the genus Rhizopogon.
