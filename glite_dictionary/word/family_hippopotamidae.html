family Hippopotamidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˌpɑtəˈmeɪdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˌpɒtəˈmiːdaɪ / (BrE)
the taxonomic family comprising hippopotamuses, large semiaquatic mammals found in Africa
the scientific family of hippopotamuses

Examples

The family Hippopotamidae consists of two living species: the common hippopotamus and the pygmy hippopotamus.
Unlike many other mammals, members of the family Hippopotamidae spend much of their lives in water.
Research has shown that the family Hippopotamidae shared a common ancestor with whales millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippopotamus - a large, thick-skinned, semiaquatic African mammal

Etymology

From modern Latin Hippopotamidae, from Hippopotamus (the type genus) + -idae (a family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
