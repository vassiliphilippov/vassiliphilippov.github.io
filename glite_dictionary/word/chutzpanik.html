chutzpanik

1. NOUN

/ ˈxʊtspɑˌnɪk / (AmE), / ˈxʊtspɑˌnɪk / (BrE)
A person who displays extreme audacity or nerve; someone noted for their bold or brazen behavior.
person displaying extreme audacity

Examples

Everyone knew Max was a chutzpanik who never hesitated to speak his mind.
It takes a real chutzpanik to ask for a raise on the first day.
The chutzpanik in the meeting shocked everyone with her blunt comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chutzpa - shameless audacity; impudence

Etymology

From Yiddish 'chutzpanik', from 'chutzpa' (audacity, nerve) + '-nik' (person suffix).
