dirigible

1. ADJ

/ dəˈrɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪdʒɪbəl / (BrE)
Able to be steered or directed; capable of being controlled in direction.
capable of being steered or directed

Examples

The new model is fully dirigible, allowing remote adjustments to its course.
Unlike a balloon, a dirigible vehicle can be actively maneuvered in the air.
The concept of a dirigible underwater drone is being explored by marine engineers.

Etymology

From French *dirigeable*, from Latin *dirigere* (to direct).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈrɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪdʒɪbəl / (BrE)
a steerable airship or lighter-than-air aircraft that can be propelled and controlled in flight
steerable airship

Examples

The dirigible floated gracefully above the city during the festival.
In the early twentieth century, travel by dirigible was considered a luxury.
The engineer explained the mechanics behind steering a dirigible through high winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airship - a powered, steerable aircraft that is lighter than air

Etymology

From French 'dirigible' (capable of being directed), from Latin 'dirigere' (to direct).
