to concuss

1. VERB

/ kənˈkʌs / (AmE), / kənˈkʌs / (BrE)
to shake or jolt violently, especially as a result of a blow or explosion
to shake violently due to impact

Examples

The blast was so powerful it concussed the windows of nearby buildings.
An explosion concussed the ground beneath our feet, sending dust into the air.
The crash concussed the car's occupants, leaving them dazed and disoriented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with force

Etymology

From Latin concussus, past participle of concutere (to shake violently).

2. VERB

/ kənˈkʌs / (AmE), / kənˈkʌs / (BrE)
to injure the brain by a blow or shock, typically causing a temporary loss of function or consciousness
to injure the brain by a blow

Examples

The accident concussed him so badly he couldn't remember the event.
Boxers sometimes get concussed during matches if they're hit in the head.
The player was concussed and had to leave the field immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concussion - a temporary unconsciousness or confusion caused by a blow to the head

Etymology

From Latin 'concussus', past participle of 'concutere' meaning 'to shake violently'.
