stultification

1. NOUN

/ ˌstʌltɪfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstʌltɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something seem futile or useless, especially as a result of routine or rigid restrictions
making something seem futile or useless

Examples

The rigid policies led to the stultification of creative thinking in the company.
Excessive bureaucracy can result in the stultification of even the most enthusiastic employees.
Many teachers fear the stultification of young minds through repetitive and uninspired curricula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futility - the quality of being pointless or useless
  • Antonym: efficacy - the ability to produce a desired result

Etymology

From Latin 'stultificare' (to make foolish) + English suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstʌltɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstʌltɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making someone or something appear foolish, absurd, or inconsistent; the process or result of rendering something ineffective or pointless, often through ridicule or derision
the act of making something seem foolish or pointless

Examples

The constant stultification of innovative ideas in the committee discouraged creative thinking.
He feared the stultification of his efforts by critics who misunderstood his goals.
The play became subject to stultification in the media, who dismissed it as incoherent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derision - contemptuous ridicule or mockery

Etymology

From Latin 'stultificare' (to make foolish), from 'stultus' (foolish).
