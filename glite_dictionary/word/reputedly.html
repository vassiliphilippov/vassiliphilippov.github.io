reputedly

1. ADV

/ rɪˈpjuːtɪdli / (AmE), / rɪˈpjuːtɪdli / (BrE)
According to what is generally believed or reported, though not necessarily proven; by reputation.
according to general belief; by reputation

Examples

The house is reputedly haunted, attracting many curious visitors every year.
He was reputedly the richest man in town, though few had seen any proof.
That restaurant is reputedly the oldest in the city, dating back to the 18th century.

Synset Examples

fish with reputedly poisonous flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegedly - according to what is claimed but not proven

Etymology

From "reputed" + "-ly"; "reputed" from Latin reputare (to think over).
