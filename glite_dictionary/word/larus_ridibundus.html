Larus ridibundus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛə.rəs ˌrɪd.ɪˈbʌn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.rəs ˌrɪd.ɪˈbʌn.dəs / (BrE)
A small European gull with a dark head in breeding plumage, commonly known as the black-headed gull.
a species of European black-headed gull

Examples

During spring, the Larus ridibundus can be seen nesting in large colonies across European wetlands.
A flock of Larus ridibundus soared above the river, their distinctive calls echoing in the early morning.
The Larus ridibundus is easily recognized by its white body and chocolate-brown head during breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-headed gull - common English name for Larus ridibundus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Larus' meaning 'gull', 'ridibundus' meaning 'laughing'.
