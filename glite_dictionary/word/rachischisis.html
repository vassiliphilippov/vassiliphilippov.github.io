rachischisis

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪkaɪˈskɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌrakɪˈskɪsɪs / (BrE)
a congenital defect in which one or more vertebrae are malformed, sometimes resulting in exposed spinal cord tissue
congenital vertebral defect
medical

Examples

The prenatal scan revealed a case of rachischisis in the developing fetus.
If left untreated, rachischisis can sometimes lead to neurological complications.
Diagnosing rachischisis early can improve outcomes for infants with spinal defects.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhakhis' (spine) and 'schisis' (fissure, split).
