North America

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / nɔːθ əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
the continent comprising Canada, the United States, Mexico, and often Greenland and the countries of Central America and the Caribbean, considered collectively
continent: US, Canada, Mexico, etc.

Examples

The wildlife of North America is incredibly diverse, ranging from polar bears in Canada to jaguars in Mexico.
Economic ties between the countries of North America are strengthened by agreements like NAFTA.
Many migratory birds travel thousands of miles across North America each year.

Etymology

Named for Italian explorer Amerigo Vespucci and its northern location relative to South America.
