tumultuous

1. ADJ

/ tuːˈmʌltʃuəs / (AmE), / tjuːˈmʌltjʊəs / (BrE)
full of confusion, disorder, or uncertainty; involving a lot of loud, excited activity, or strong feelings.
full of disorder or chaos

Examples

The city experienced a tumultuous period following the sudden resignation of its mayor.
His tumultuous relationship with his parents shaped his outlook on life.
There were cheers and applause as the singer took the stage to a tumultuous welcome.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; tranquil

Etymology

From Latin tumultuosus (full of confusion or uproar), from tumultus (uproar).
