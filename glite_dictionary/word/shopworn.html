shopworn

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑːpˌwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpˌwɔːn / (BrE)
(of an idea, phrase, etc.) no longer interesting or effective because it has been used too often; overfamiliar through overuse.
stale from overuse

Examples

His speech was filled with shopworn clichés that failed to impress the audience.
The movie's plot felt shopworn, as if it had been seen a hundred times before.
Critics dismissed the novel for its reliance on shopworn themes and predictable twists.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackneyed - lacking in freshness or originality due to overuse
  • Synonym: trite - (of a remark or idea) lacking originality or freshness
  • Antonym: original - new and not derived from something else; not repeated too often

Etymology

Originally referring to goods made less valuable by being displayed in a shop; later extended metaphorically to ideas and expressions.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑpˌwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpˌwɔːn / (BrE)
(of goods) worn, faded, or slightly damaged from being displayed or handled in a shop, but not used.
worn or faded from store display

Examples

The bookstore offered several shopworn copies at a reduced price.
Although the jacket was shopworn, it was still in wearable condition.
Customers can sometimes find shopworn items marked down during clearance sales.

Synset Examples

shopworn merchandise at half price

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new - not previously used or worn
  • Synonym: used - having been used before

Etymology

From 'shop' + 'worn'; patterned after colloquial use since the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593905-s
Synset POS: s
