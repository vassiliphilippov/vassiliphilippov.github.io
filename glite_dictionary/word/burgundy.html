Burgundy

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɝː.ɡən.di / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.ɡənd.i / (BrE)
a former province in eastern France, noted for its fine wines
former French province famous for its wines

Examples

On their trip to France, they toured several vineyards in Burgundy.
The wines produced in Burgundy are renowned throughout the world.
She has always wanted to visit the historic castles scattered across Burgundy.

Etymology

From Old French Bourgogne, from Latin Burgundia, land of the Burgundians.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːɡəndi / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəndi / (BrE)
A red table wine produced in the Burgundy region of France, or any similar wine made elsewhere.
red wine from Burgundy, France

Examples

He ordered a bottle of Burgundy to go with the steak.
Many collectors value rare vintages of Burgundy above all other wines.
The restaurant specializes in wines, particularly classic French Burgundy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bordeaux - red wine from the Bordeaux region of France

Etymology

Named after the Burgundy region in eastern France.
