burgundy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːɡəndi / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəndi / (BrE)
a dark purplish-red or blackish-red color, named after the wine of the Burgundy region in France
dark purplish-red color

Examples

She wore a stunning dress in burgundy to the gala.
The living room walls were painted a rich shade of burgundy.
His tie added a touch of burgundy to his otherwise plain suit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maroon - a dark brownish-red color
  • Degree Higher: crimson - a deep red color with a hint of blue
  • Degree Lower: red - the color at the end of the visible spectrum, primary color

Etymology

Named after the Burgundy region in France, famous for its red wines.
