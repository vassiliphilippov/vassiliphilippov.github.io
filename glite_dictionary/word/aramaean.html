Aramaean

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈmiːən / (AmE), / ˌarəˈmiːən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Semitic people inhabiting Aram (modern-day Syria and surrounding regions) and parts of Mesopotamia from the 11th to the 8th century BC.
member of an ancient Semitic people of Aram

Examples

The ancient Aramaeans played a significant role in the history of the Middle East.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts believed to belong to the Aramaean civilization.
Several Old Testament passages mention interactions with the Aramaeans of Syria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aramaic - the Semitic language historically spoken by the Aramaeans

Etymology

From Latin Aramaeus, from Greek Aramaios, from Aram (ancient region and people in Syria).

2. ADJ

/ ˌærəˈmiːən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to Aram, an ancient region in the Near East, its inhabitants, their culture, or their language.
of Aram, its people, culture, or language

Examples

The Aramaean tribes played a significant role in the region's early history.
He studied ancient Aramaean inscriptions discovered in northern Syria.
Many Aramaean cultural practices influenced neighboring civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aramaic - the Semitic language of the Aramaeans

Etymology

From Latin Aramaeus, from Ancient Greek Aramaios, from Aram, a region mentioned in the Bible.
