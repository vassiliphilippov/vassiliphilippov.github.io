haler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlə / (BrE)
A monetary unit of Slovakia, equal to one hundredth of a koruna.
Slovak monetary unit, 1/100 koruna

Examples

She found an old coin worth fifty halers in her grandfather’s collection.
Prices in Slovakia used to be listed in korunas and halers before the euro was adopted.
The vending machine only accepted coins of ten halers or higher.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: koruna - the basic monetary unit of Slovakia before the adoption of the euro

Etymology

From Slovak 'halier', from Hungarian 'fillér', ultimately from German 'Heller' (a small coin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈheɪlə / (BrE)
a former monetary unit of the Czech Republic, equal to one hundredth of a koruna.
former Czech monetary subunit

Examples

The price was listed as ten halers in the old catalogue.
After the introduction of the euro, the haler ceased to be used in Czech currency.
Collectors value coins denominated in halers for their historical significance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: koruna - the basic monetary unit of the Czech Republic

Etymology

From Czech 'haléř', ultimately from German 'Heller', a small medieval coin.
