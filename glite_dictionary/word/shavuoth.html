Shavuoth

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑːˈvuːət / (AmE), / ʃəˈvuːət / (BrE)
A Jewish holy day celebrated on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating Moses receiving the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai.
Jewish holiday marking the giving of the Ten Commandments

Examples

Every year, the family gathers to celebrate Shavuoth with traditional foods and prayers.
Shavuoth marks the occasion when Moses received the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai.
Many synagogues hold all-night study sessions in honor of Shavuoth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentecost - Christian holy day corresponding to the Jewish Shavuoth, fifty days after Easter

Etymology

From Hebrew שָׁבוּעוֹת (shavu'ot, "weeks"), marking seven weeks after Passover.
