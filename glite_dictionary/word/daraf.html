daraf

1. NOUN

/ ˈdær.æf / (AmE), / ˈdar.af / (BrE)
a unit of elastance in electrical engineering, equal to the reciprocal of a farad
unit of electrical elastance

Examples

The elastance of the component was measured in darafs by the engineer.
One daraf is the reciprocal of one farad, representing the measure of elastance.
Although rarely used, the daraf appears in certain older electrical engineering texts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: farad - unit of electrical capacitance

Etymology

Formed by reversing the spelling of 'farad', after Michael Faraday.
