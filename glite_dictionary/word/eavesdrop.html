to eavesdrop

1. VERB

/ ˈivzˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈiːvzdrɒp / (BrE)
to secretly listen to other people's conversations without their knowledge or permission
listen secretly to conversation

Examples

The manager suspected someone might eavesdrop on their private meeting.
It's rude to eavesdrop on conversations that don't concern you.
She accidentally eavesdropped while walking past the open window.

Synset Examples

the jealous man was eavesdropping on his wife's conversations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overhear - hear something unintentionally or secretly

Etymology

From the noun 'eavesdrop' (the ground where water drips from eaves); from the idea of standing under the eaves to listen secretly.
