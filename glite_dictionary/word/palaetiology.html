palaetiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæliˌiːtiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪˌiːtɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the causes of past events, especially in geology or natural history
study of causes of past events

Examples

Geologists use palaetiology to understand how mountain ranges formed millions of years ago.
Through palaetiology, scientists reconstruct the history of Earth's climate changes.
The professor lectured on palaetiology as a crucial method for interpreting fossil evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiology - the study of causes or origins, especially of diseases

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + aitia (cause) + -logy (study).
