isomerization

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.sə.mə.rəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.sə.mə.rɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a compound is converted into an isomer with a different arrangement of atoms but the same molecular formula
conversion of a compound into an isomer
medical

Examples

Isomerization of hydrocarbons is a common process in the petroleum industry to improve fuel quality.
The reaction vessel was designed to optimize the isomerization of glucose to fructose.
Scientists studied the isomerization under various temperature and pressure conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomer - a compound with the same molecular formula as another but a different arrangement of atoms

Etymology

From isomer + -ization, modeled after French isomérisation.
