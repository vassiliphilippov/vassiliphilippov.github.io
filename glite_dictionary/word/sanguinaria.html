Sanguinaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæŋɡwɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌsaŋɡwɪˈneəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the poppy family, consisting of a single species, bloodroot, known for its red sap.
genus of plants including bloodroot
medical

Examples

The medicinal properties of Sanguinaria have been studied for their potential applications in herbal remedies.
Botanists collected samples of Sanguinaria from the woodland to examine its unique red sap.
Traditional uses of Sanguinaria include treatment for respiratory issues and topical applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodroot - a perennial plant with reddish sap, Sanguinaria canadensis

Etymology

From Latin 'sanguinarius' (of blood), referring to the plant's red sap.
