Bibos

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪbɒs / (AmE), / ˈbiːbɒs / (BrE)
A genus of wild oxen found in South and Southeast Asia, including species such as the gaur and banteng.
genus of wild oxen

Examples

The gaur, a member of the genus Bibos, is the largest species of wild cattle.
Scientists are studying the habitat loss affecting wild Bibos populations in Asia.
Some conservation programs aim to protect endangered species within the Bibos genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bos - genus that includes domestic cattle and some wild oxen

Etymology

From Latin 'bibos', used in zoological classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪbɒs / (AmE), / ˈbiːbɒs / (BrE)
A former genus of large wild oxen native to Asia, now generally included within the genus Bos.
former genus of wild Asian oxen

Examples

The gaur was once classified under the genus Bibos.
Taxonomists now prefer to include Bibos species in the genus Bos.
The taxonomy of Bibos has been debated among zoologists for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bos - genus of cattle including domestic cattle and some wild oxen

Etymology

From Greek 'bous' (ox) via modern Latin, used in scientific taxonomy.
