average

1. VERB

/ ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
to calculate the average value of a set of numbers
calculate the average value

Examples

The company averaged the monthly sales data to determine the yearly growth.
If you average all your test scores, you'll see you are doing quite well.
Scientists averaged the temperatures recorded across the region to estimate the overall climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calculate - to determine something mathematically
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00641660-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈævɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
an amount, standard, or level regarded as usual or typical within a group; the norm
usual or typical amount or level

Examples

The temperature this spring was close to the average for the region.
Her grades last semester were just above average.
This player is taller than the team average.

Synset Examples

he is about average in height
the snowfall this month is below average

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - the sum of values divided by their number; mathematical average
  • Synonym: median - the middle value in a set of numbers
  • Antonym: extreme - very unusual or exceptional compared to what is normal or typical

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'averagium' (a duty on goods), later extended to the mean value.

5. NOUN

/ ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a number expressing the central or typical value in a set of data, calculated by dividing the total of all values by their number; the mean
the mean or typical value in data

Examples

The class average on the exam was higher than expected.
To find the average, add up all the scores and divide by the number of students.
The city's temperature average in July is 28°C.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - the sum of values divided by their number; the arithmetic mean
  • Antonym: median - the value separating the higher half from the lower half of a data set
  • Antonym: mode - the value that appears most frequently in a data set

Etymology

From Old French *avarage* (damage sustained at sea), later generalized to mean 'mean value', from Medieval Latin *avaragium*.
