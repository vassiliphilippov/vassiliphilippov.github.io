isophone

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.səˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sə.fəʊn / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A line on a map marking the boundary of an area where a particular feature of pronunciation occurs.
pronunciation boundary in linguistics

Examples

Linguists traced the isophone that separates regions with distinct vowel pronunciations.
The isophone indicates where the local accent begins to differ markedly from neighboring dialects.
A new isophone was drawn after changes in pronunciation were discovered in the region.

Synset Examples

To the north of the isophone, speakers have a tendency to pronounce triphthongs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isogloss - a geographic boundary marking a difference in linguistic features

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) + 'phōnē' (sound, voice).
