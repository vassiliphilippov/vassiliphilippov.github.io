palmyra

1. NOUN

/ pælmˈaɪrə / (AmE), / pælˈmaɪərə / (BrE)
A tall fan palm found in Africa, India, and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard wood, edible sap used for palm wine and sugar, and leaves used for thatching and weaving.
a tropical fan palm tree

Examples

The palmyra grows abundantly in southern India, providing food, shelter, and materials for rural communities.
Local villagers use palmyra leaves to weave baskets and mats.
Sweet sap from the palmyra palm is collected to make traditional palm wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - any tree of the family Palmae, typically tropical and with large leaves

Etymology

From Tamil 'paṟam' via Portuguese 'palmeira'.
