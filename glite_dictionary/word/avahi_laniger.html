Avahi laniger

1. NOUN

/ əˈvɑː.hi ləˈnɪ.dʒər / (AmE), / əˈvɑː.hi læˈnɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
a species of nocturnal lemur native to Madagascar, known for its thick grey-brown fur and long tail
species of nocturnal lemur

Examples

Researchers observed the Avahi laniger in its natural forest habitat in Madagascar.
The thick fur of the Avahi laniger helps it stay warm during cool nights.
Unlike other lemurs, the Avahi laniger is highly adapted to a nocturnal lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a primate native to Madagascar, with a long tail and large eyes

Etymology

From the genus name Avahi and Latin laniger (wool-bearing).
