misleading

1. ADJ

/ mɪsˈlidɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪsˈliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
Giving the wrong idea or impression, making someone believe something that is not true.
giving a false impression or idea

Examples

The advertisement was found to be misleading and was banned by regulators.
Some statistics can be misleading if they are taken out of context.
His explanation was misleading, causing many to misunderstand the situation.

Synset Examples

the deceptive calm in the eye of the storm
deliberately deceptive packaging
a misleading similarity
statistics can be presented in ways that are misleading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceptive - giving an appearance or impression different from the true one
  • Antonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'leading' (directing, guiding).
