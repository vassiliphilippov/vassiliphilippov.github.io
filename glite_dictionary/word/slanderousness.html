slanderousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslændərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈslɑːndərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of making false and damaging statements about someone; the state of being slanderous.
quality of being slanderous

Examples

His slanderousness cost him many friends and ruined his reputation in the community.
The slanderousness of the article led to a lawsuit for defamation.
We were shocked by the slanderousness of the rumors spreading around the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: defamation - the action of damaging the good reputation of someone

Etymology

From 'slanderous' + '-ness', where 'slanderous' derives from Old French 'esclandre' meaning scandal.
