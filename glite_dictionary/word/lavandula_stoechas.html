Lavandula stoechas

1. NOUN

/ ləˌvændjələ ˈstikiəs / (AmE), / ləˌvændjʊlə ˈstiːkəs / (BrE)
A shrubby, greyish species of lavender native to southwestern Europe, usually bearing reddish-purple flowers.
a species of lavender with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

Lavandula stoechas is commonly grown in Mediterranean gardens for its vibrant, aromatic flowers.
Gardeners recommend pruning Lavandula stoechas regularly to maintain its compact shape.
The scent of Lavandula stoechas is often used in herbal sachets and potpourri.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - a genus of aromatic evergreen shrubs (Lavandula)

Etymology

From the genus name 'Lavandula' (Latin for lavender) and species 'stoechas', after the Stoecades Islands in the Mediterranean.
