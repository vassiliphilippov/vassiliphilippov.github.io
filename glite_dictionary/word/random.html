random

1. ADJ

/ ˈrændəm / (AmE), / ˈrændəm / (BrE)
made, done, or happening without method or conscious decision; lacking a definite plan, order, or purpose and often governed by chance
without pattern or specific purpose; by chance

Examples

The winning ticket was drawn at random from the box.
His answers seemed completely random and unrelated to the topic.
There was a random scattering of books on the table.

Synset Examples

a random choice
bombs fell at random
random movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrary - based on random choice or personal whim
  • Antonym: systematic - done according to a fixed plan or system

Etymology

From Middle French *random*, from Old French *randir* (to run fast).
