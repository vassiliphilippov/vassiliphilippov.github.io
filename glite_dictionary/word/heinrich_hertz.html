Heinrich Hertz

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪnˌrɪk ˈhɜrts / (AmE), / ˈhaɪnʁɪç ˈhɛəts / (BrE)
(1857–1894) German physicist who was the first to produce and detect electromagnetic waves artificially, confirming the theories of James Clerk Maxwell.
German physicist; pioneer in electromagnetic waves

Examples

The contributions of Heinrich Hertz revolutionized the field of physics and wireless communication.
In his experiments, Heinrich Hertz succeeded in generating and detecting electromagnetic waves.
Many scientific terms and units are named after Heinrich Hertz in honor of his groundbreaking work.
