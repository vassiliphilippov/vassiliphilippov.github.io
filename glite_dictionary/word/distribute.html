to distribute

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstrɪbjut / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
(of a mathematical operation) to be distributive, such that the operation applied to a set of terms is equal to the operation applied individually and then aggregated according to another operation, as in a(b + c) = ab + ac.
to be mathematically distributive

Examples

In arithmetic, multiplication distributes over addition, making equations easier to solve.
When the function distributes over the sum, you can expand the expression accordingly.
In Boolean algebra, 'and' distributes over 'or' in logical expressions.
