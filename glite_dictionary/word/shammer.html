shammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmər / (AmE), / ˈʃæmə / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be ill or incapacitated to avoid work or duty
someone feigning illness to shirk duty

Examples

The manager quickly spotted the shammer who kept calling in sick every Monday.
HR introduced stricter policies to discourage any shammer from exploiting sick leave.
It's unfair to genuine employees when a shammer abuses the company's trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malingerer - a person who feigns illness to escape duty or work

Etymology

From 'sham' (to pretend, to fake) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmər / (AmE), / ˈʃæmə / (BrE)
a person who pretends or makes deceitful claims; an impostor or deceiver
person who deceives or pretends

Examples

Everyone thought he was a war hero, but he turned out to be a shammer.
The company was exposed as a shammer making false promises to investors.
Detectives are trained to spot a shammer among genuine witnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impostor - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
  • Synonym: faker - a person who pretends or deceives; not genuine
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who is truthful and does not deceive

Etymology

From 'sham' (to pretend or feign) + -er (agent noun suffix).
