thermograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːməˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument that uses infrared imaging to detect and record temperature variations on the surface of the body
infrared device for mapping body temperature
medical

Examples

The doctor used a thermograph to assess temperature differences in the patient's limbs.
A thermograph can help detect abnormal heat patterns that may indicate underlying health issues.
Advancements in thermograph technology have improved non-invasive diagnostic methods in medicine.

Etymology

From Greek 'thermo-' (heat) + '-graph' (writing, recording).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːməˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument that automatically records temperature changes over time on a graph
temperature-recording instrument

Examples

The laboratory used a thermograph to monitor temperature fluctuations during the experiment.
The thermograph produced a continuous record of the patient's body temperature throughout the night.
Engineers installed a thermograph in the greenhouse to track daily temperature changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermometer - an instrument for measuring temperature

Etymology

From Greek thermos (hot) + -graph (writing, recording).
