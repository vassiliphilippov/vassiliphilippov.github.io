deamination

1. NOUN

/ diˌæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of an amino group from an amino acid or other compound, typically as part of metabolic processes
removal of amino group from a molecule
medical

Examples

The process of deamination occurs in the liver as part of amino acid metabolism.
Excess dietary protein can lead to increased deamination in the body.
Enzymes such as deaminases are responsible for catalyzing deamination reactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amination - addition of an amino group to a molecule

Etymology

From de- (removal) + amine + -ation; first used in 19th-century scientific language.
