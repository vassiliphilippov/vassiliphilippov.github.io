mink

1. NOUN

/ mɪŋk / (AmE), / mɪŋk / (BrE)
the soft, luxurious fur of a mink, often used to make expensive coats and other clothing
luxurious fur from a mink

Examples

She wore a beautiful mink around her shoulders at the gala.
The store window displayed a luxurious mink coat that caught everyone's eye.
Vintage mink is still popular among collectors and fashion enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ermine - the white fur of a stoat, used for trimming clothes
  • Synonym: sable - the dark brown fur from a marten, used in luxury garments

Etymology

From Swedish *mink* or Danish *mink*, of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ mɪŋk / (AmE), / mɪŋk / (BrE)
A fur coat made from the soft, lustrous fur of a mink.
fur coat made from mink

Examples

She arrived at the gala in a luxurious mink that caught everyone's attention.
During winter, her grandmother always wore a vintage mink to formal events.
The actress donated her favorite mink to a charity auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur coat - a coat made from animal fur

Etymology

From Scandinavian origins; related to Swedish 'mink' and Norwegian 'mink'.

3. NOUN

/ mɪŋk / (AmE), / mɪŋk / (BrE)
A small, slender-bodied semiaquatic mammal with partially webbed feet, valued for its soft, dense fur.
fur-bearing semiaquatic mammal

Examples

The mink swam gracefully along the riverbank in search of food.
Her coat was made of luxurious mink fur, which attracted many compliments.
Wild minks are difficult to spot due to their elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ermine - a small mustelid mammal valued for its white winter fur
  • Synonym: otter - a semiaquatic mammal with dense fur and webbed feet

Etymology

From Swedish *mink*, possibly of Finnish origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
