aerodynamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the properties or study of the motion of air and how it interacts with solid objects, especially those with a shape designed to reduce resistance to motion through air.
relating to movement through air

Examples

The designer created an aerodynamic shape to make the car go faster.
Cyclists often wear aerodynamic helmets to reduce wind resistance.
Birds have naturally aerodynamic bodies that help them glide effortlessly through the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streamlined - designed to reduce resistance to air or water
  • Antonym: drag - the resistance of air against forward movement

Etymology

From 'aero-' (air) and 'dynamic', relating to force and motion.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
(of a shape or design) having a form that reduces air resistance and allows smoother movement through air or other fluids.
minimizing air or fluid resistance

Examples

Modern cars are designed to be as aerodynamic as possible to improve fuel efficiency.
The new bicycle helmet features an aerodynamic shape for better speed.
Birds have naturally aerodynamic bodies that help them glide through the air.

Synset Examples

a streamlined convertible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streamlined - designed with a smooth shape to reduce resistance to air or water
  • Antonym: drag - the resistance force experienced by an object moving through a fluid

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'dynamis' (power).

Synset POS: s
