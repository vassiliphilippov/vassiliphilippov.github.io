Marcus Tullius Cicero

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrkəs ˈtʌlius ˈsɪsəˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ˈtʌliəs ˈsɪsərəʊ / (BrE)
(106–43 BC) Roman statesman, orator, and writer, renowned for his skill in Latin prose and influence on European thought.
Roman statesman and orator (106–43 BC)

Examples

Marcus Tullius Cicero was one of Rome's greatest orators and philosophers.
Many of Marcus Tullius Cicero's speeches survive and are still studied today.
The influence of Marcus Tullius Cicero can be seen in the works of later European writers.

Etymology

Latin name: Marcus (personal name), Tullius (family name), Cicero (cognomen).
