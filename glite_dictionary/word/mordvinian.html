Mordvinian

1. NOUN

/ mɔrdˈvɪniən / (AmE), / mɔːdˈvɪniən / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Mordvinian people, consisting of the Erzya and Moksha varieties
Finnic language of the Mordvinian people

Examples

Many linguists specialize in the study of Mordvinian and its dialects.
She grew up speaking Mordvinian at home with her family.
The Mordvinian language is related to other Finno-Ugric languages such as Finnish and Estonian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - relating to the group of Finno-Ugric languages

Etymology

From Mordvin, denoting the ethnic group, plus English suffix -ian.

2. NOUN

/ mɔrdˈvɪniən / (AmE), / mɔːdˈvɪniən / (BrE)
a member of the Mordvin people, an agricultural ethnic group living in the central Volga provinces of European Russia
a member of an ethnic group in central Russia

Examples

The Mordvinian people have preserved many of their ancient traditions and customs.
As a Mordvinian, she spoke one of the Finno-Ugric languages native to the Volga region.
Several Mordvinian villages are known for their colorful folk festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mordvin - another term for a member of the Mordvinian ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian 'Мордвин' (Mordvin), the self-designation of the people.
