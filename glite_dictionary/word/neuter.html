neuter

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuːtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtə / (BrE)
(of an organism or animal) having no, nonfunctional, or imperfectly developed sex organs.
lacking functional sex organs (biology)

Examples

In some ant species, the worker caste is neuter and cannot reproduce.
Certain flowers are classified as neuter because they lack both male and female organs.
The neuter animals in the colony serve different functions than the reproductively active individuals.

Etymology

From Latin neuter meaning 'neither one nor the other.'

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈnuːtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtə / (BrE)
(of grammatical gender) belonging to a category that is neither masculine nor feminine.
of neither masculine nor feminine grammatical gender

Examples

In German, the word 'Mädchen' is classified as neuter rather than feminine.
The pronoun 'it' is considered a neuter form in English.
Some languages lack a neuter gender and use only masculine and feminine.

Synset Examples

‘it’ is the third-person singular neuter pronoun

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculine - of the grammatical gender typically associated with males
  • Antonym: feminine - of the grammatical gender typically associated with females

Etymology

From Latin neuter (neither of two, neither), from ne- (not) + uter (either of two).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01489634-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtə / (BrE)
a grammatical gender denoting words that are neither masculine nor feminine, usually referring to inanimate objects or abstract concepts
grammatical gender: neither masculine nor feminine

Examples

In many languages, nouns are classified as masculine, feminine, or neuter.
The German word for 'girl' is actually of neuter gender.
Some pronouns, like 'it,' belong to the neuter gender.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculine - grammatical gender referring to male or male-like nouns
  • Antonym: feminine - grammatical gender referring to female or female-like nouns

Etymology

From Latin 'neuter' (neither), from 'ne-' (not) + 'uter' (either, of two).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06340306-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈnuːtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtə / (BrE)
to remove the reproductive organs of an animal, especially the ovaries or testicles, to prevent it from reproducing
remove reproductive organs (of animal)

Examples

The veterinarian will neuter your dog to prevent unwanted litters.
Many animal shelters require that all adopted cats be neutered before going to new homes.
We decided to neuter our rabbit last month for health reasons.

Synset Examples

Is your cat spayed?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spay - to remove the ovaries of a female animal
  • Synonym: castrate - to remove the testicles of a male animal

Etymology

From Latin 'neuter' meaning 'of neither sex'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
