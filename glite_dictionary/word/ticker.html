ticker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪkə / (BrE)
a device that automatically prints stock prices and other financial information on a continuous strip of paper, formerly used in stock exchanges
stock price printing device

Examples

Stock brokers would often gather around the ticker to get the latest market updates.
The old ticker in the museum still prints mock trades for visitors.
In the early 20th century, every major financial office had a ticker transmitting stock quotations.

Etymology

From the verb 'tick', referring to the ticking sound made by the device.

informal

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkər / (AmE), / ˈtɪkə / (BrE)
a small portable watch or timepiece, especially a wristwatch or pocket watch
small portable watch or timepiece

Examples

He reached into his pocket, pulled out his ticker, and checked the time.
Grandpa shows me the old ticker he used to carry everywhere.
The ticker chimed softly every hour, marking the passing of the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - a small portable timepiece worn typically on the wrist
  • Degree Higher: clock - an instrument for measuring and indicating time, usually larger and not portable

Etymology

From the verb 'tick' (referencing the sound made by a clock or watch); 'ticker' was first used in the late 19th century.
