Polygonatum commutatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈɡoʊnətəm ˌkɒməˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈɡəʊnətəm ˌkɒmjʊˈteɪtəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with smooth leaves and drooping, tubular greenish flowers, commonly known as Smooth Solomon's Seal.
North American perennial herb (Smooth Solomon's Seal)

Examples

Botanists discovered a patch of Polygonatum commutatum growing along the forest path.
The delicate, drooping flowers of Polygonatum commutatum appear in late spring.
Gardeners appreciate Polygonatum commutatum for its graceful arching stems and shade tolerance.

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Polygonatum* (from Greek 'many knees', referring to the jointed rhizomes) and Latin 'commutatum' meaning 'changed' or 'exchanged.'
