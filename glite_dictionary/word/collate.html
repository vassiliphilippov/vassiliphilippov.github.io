to collate

1. VERB

/ kəˈleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈleɪt / (BrE)
to collect and arrange items, especially documents or pieces of information, in the correct order
assemble items in proper sequence

Examples

Please collate the reports by date before submitting them to the manager.
The assistant was asked to collate the handouts for the meeting in the correct order.
After printing, make sure to collate the pages to avoid any confusion.

Synset Examples

collate the papers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort - put things in a particular order or category
  • Antonym: to disarrange - to put out of order

Etymology

From Latin 'collatus', past participle of 'conferre' (to bring together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01388413-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kəˈleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈleɪt / (BrE)
to examine and compare texts or information in order to identify similarities and differences, especially for accuracy or consistency
compare texts or information critically

Examples

The researcher spent weeks to collate the ancient manuscripts and highlight discrepancies between copies.
We need to collate these two documents to ensure there are no inconsistencies before publication.
He was asked to collate the original versions of the play to produce an authoritative edition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compare - to examine the differences and similarities between things

Etymology

From Latin collātus, past participle of conferre (to bring together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00663631-v
Synset POS: v
