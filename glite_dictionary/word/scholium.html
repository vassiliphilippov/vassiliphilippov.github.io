scholium

1. NOUN

/ skoʊliəm / (AmE), / skəʊliəm / (BrE)
a marginal note or explanatory comment written by a commentator, especially on classical or ancient literature
marginal note by a classical commentator

Examples

The manuscript contained a scholium in the margin, clarifying a difficult passage.
A famous scholium helped modern scholars interpret the original Greek text.
He studied the scholium to gain deeper insight into the philosopher's intent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annotation - a note of explanation or comment added to a text

Etymology

From Greek *skholion* (comment, note) via Latin *scholium*.
