pearmain

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrˌmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛəˌmeɪn / (BrE)
Any of several varieties of apple with red skin, often sweet and crisp.
a variety of apple with red skin

Examples

The orchard was famous for its old pearmain trees, which produced apples every autumn.
She brought a basket of pearmain apples to the farmers' market.
A slice of pearmain pairs well with sharp cheddar cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, which typically has thin red or green skin

Etymology

From Middle French 'parmain', from Old French 'permain', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'Parma' (the Italian city).
