Ranunculus lingua

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs ˈlɪŋɡwə / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs ˈlɪŋɡwə / (BrE)
A species of semiaquatic European crowfoot (buttercup) with spear-shaped leaves, commonly found in wetlands.
a semiaquatic European buttercup species

Examples

The wetlands were filled with Ranunculus lingua, their spear-shaped leaves glistening in the sun.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Ranunculus lingua in various European marshes.
Gardeners admire Ranunculus lingua for its striking foliage and adaptability to moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - any plant of the genus Ranunculus, with bright yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ranunculus' meaning 'little frog' (referring to wet habitats), 'lingua' meaning 'tongue' (referring to the leaf shape).
