Viverricula

1. NOUN

/ vɪˌvɛrɪˈkjuːlə / (AmE), / vɪˌvɛrɪˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender carnivorous mammals in the family Viverridae, including the small Indian civet.
genus of small carnivorous mammals (viverrids)

Examples

The Viverricula genus includes the well-known small Indian civet.
Scientists have conducted extensive studies on the distribution of Viverricula species across Asia.
Unlike some larger viverrids, members of Viverricula are primarily nocturnal and solitary.

Etymology

From Latin 'viverricula', diminutive of 'viverra' (ferret).

2. NOUN

/ vɪˌvɛrˈrɪkjələ / (AmE), / vɪˌvɛrˈrɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the family Viverridae, including the small Indian civet.
genus of small carnivorous mammals (civets)

Examples

The Viverricula genus is best known for the small Indian civet.
Recent studies on Viverricula have helped clarify its evolutionary relationships within Viverridae.
Only one species, Viverricula indica, is currently recognized in the Viverricula genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Viverridae - the biological family of civets and related mammals

Etymology

From Latin 'viverra' (ferret) with a diminutive suffix in Neo-Latin taxonomy.
