topaz

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌpæz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.pæz / (BrE)
a hard, transparent mineral, usually yellow, brown, or clear, used as a gemstone
precious gemstone mineral

Examples

She wore a necklace set with a sparkling topaz pendant.
The jeweler admired the brilliant cut of the yellow topaz.
A rare blue topaz was on display in the museum's mineral collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gemstone - a precious or semi-precious stone, especially when cut and polished or engraved

Etymology

From Latin topazus, from Greek topazos (a type of precious stone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌpæz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.pæz / (BrE)
A yellow variety of quartz used as a gemstone.
yellow quartz gemstone

Examples

She wore a beautiful ring set with a yellow topaz.
The necklace was adorned with large topaz stones that sparkled in the sunlight.
Mineralogists classify citrine as a type of yellow quartz, but true topaz is more rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow quartz - a variety of quartz with a yellow coloration

Etymology

From Old French 'topace', from Latin 'topazus', from Greek 'topazos', name of a Red Sea island.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌpæz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.pæz / (BrE)
A light brown or yellowish-brown color resembling that of the topaz gemstone.
light brown color

Examples

She wore a scarf in a rich topaz that matched her earrings perfectly.
The sunset cast a warm topaz glow across the fields.
Interior designers recommend using topaz accents to introduce warmth into modern rooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amber - a yellowish-orange color similar to fossil resin
  • Degree Higher: golden - bright yellowish color like that of gold

Etymology

From Old French *topace*, from Latin *topazus*, from Greek *topazos* (a precious stone).
