hirsutism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrsuˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / həˈsjuːtɪzəm / (BrE)
(Medical) Excessive growth of hair in women in areas where hair is normally minimal or absent, typically due to hormonal imbalance.
excessive hairiness (esp. in women)
medical

Examples

Women with polycystic ovary syndrome often experience hirsutism as one of the symptoms.
Treatment for hirsutism may include hormonal therapy to reduce excess hair growth.
The patient was concerned about the sudden onset of hirsutism and sought medical advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypertrichosis - excessive hair growth over the entire body

Etymology

From Latin hirsutus (hairy) + -ism.
