Wolffiella

1. NOUN

/ wʌlˈfiːələ / (AmE), / wɒlˈfiːələ / (BrE)
A genus of tiny, rootless aquatic herbs with flat fronds floating on or beneath the water surface, native to America and Africa.
genus of tiny rootless aquatic herbs

Examples

Wolffiella grows in calm freshwater habitats across North and South America.
Researchers studied the floating fronds of Wolffiella for their unique reproductive patterns.
The aquarium contained several Wolffiella plants, distinguishable by their flat, rootless bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wolffia - genus of minute rootless aquatic plants closely related to Wolffiella

Etymology

From the genus name, formed from the surname Wolff, honoring German botanist Johann Friedrich Wolff, with the diminutive suffix -ella.

2. NOUN

/ wʌlˈfiː.ələ / (AmE), / wɒlˈfiː.ɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of minute rootless aquatic herbs, with flat floating or submerged fronds and 1–2 flowers per frond, native to America and Africa
a genus of tiny rootless aquatic plants

Examples

The tiny Wolffiella plants thrive in calm freshwater ponds.
Scientists study the growth patterns of Wolffiella to better understand aquatic ecosystems.
Because Wolffiella lacks roots, it absorbs nutrients directly from the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wolffia - a closely related genus of tiny aquatic flowering plants

Etymology

Formed from the genus name 'Wolffia' with the Latin diminutive suffix '-ella', indicating a smaller form.
