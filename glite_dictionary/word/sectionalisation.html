sectionalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkʃənəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of dividing something into sections or parts, often by creating boundaries or partitions
division into sections or parts

Examples

The new office layout will require the sectionalisation of the main hall into smaller workspaces.
Urban planners discussed the sectionalisation of the park to accommodate different recreational activities.
Excessive sectionalisation of teams can hinder communication across departments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the act of separating into parts
  • Synonym: partition - the act of dividing or separating; something that divides

Etymology

From 'sectional' + '-isation', based on Latin 'sectio' (cutting) and English '-isation' (process).
