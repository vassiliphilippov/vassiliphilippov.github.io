migraine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪˌɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡreɪn / (BrE)
A recurrent, severe headache, often affecting one side of the head and sometimes accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light or sound.
severe recurring headache condition
medical

Examples

She stayed home from work because her migraine made it impossible to concentrate.
Bright lights and loud noises often trigger a migraine for him.
After hours of suffering, her migraine finally subsided with medication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: headache - pain in the head

Etymology

From French 'migraine', from Late Latin 'hemicrania' (pain in one side of the head), from Greek 'hēmikrania'.
