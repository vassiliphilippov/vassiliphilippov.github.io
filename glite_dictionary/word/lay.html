lay

1. NOUN

/ leɪ / (AmE), / leɪ / (BrE)
a narrative poem, often of medieval origin, typically intended to be sung or recited
narrative poem, often medieval

Examples

The minstrel performed a lay recounting ancient tales of valor.
Medieval poets often wrote a lay to honor their patrons.
She studied the famous Breton lays in her medieval literature class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballad - a narrative poem or song, often telling a folk story

Etymology

From Old French 'lai', meaning a short lyrical or narrative poem.
