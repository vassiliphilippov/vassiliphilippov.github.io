ingenue

1. NOUN

/ ˌænʒəˈnu / (AmE), / ˈænʒeɪnjuː / (BrE)
An actress who specializes in playing the role of an artless and innocent young woman, typically in a play or film.
innocent young woman role (acting)

Examples

The director cast her as the ingenue in his latest romantic drama.
Though she began her career as an ingenue, she soon moved on to more complex roles.
Critics praised the subtlety of her performance as the innocent ingenue caught in a web of intrigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • Antonym: sophisticate - a person with much worldly experience and knowledge

Etymology

From French 'ingénue' (feminine of ingénu, meaning naive), from Latin 'ingenuus' (freeborn, candid).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænʒəˈnu / (AmE), / ˈænʒeɪnjuː / (BrE)
an innocent, unsophisticated young woman, especially as a character or role in a play or film
innocent young female role in drama

Examples

She was cast as the ingenue in the new Broadway production.
The film's plot centers around an ingenue who arrives in the city seeking her fortune.
Many actresses begin their careers playing the ingenue before transitioning to more complex roles.

Etymology

From French ingénue (feminine of ingénu), meaning 'ingenuous, innocent'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈænʒəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈænʒəˌnjuː / (BrE)
an innocent and naive young woman, especially as portrayed on stage or in literature
innocent young woman (esp. in drama/literature)

Examples

The actress was often cast as the ingenue in romantic comedies.
Her wide-eyed expression perfectly suited the role of the innocent ingenue in the play.
Throughout the novel, the ingenue learns to navigate the complexities of adult life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgement; unsophisticated
  • Antonym: sophisticate - a person who is worldly-wise and not naive

Etymology

From French ingénue (feminine of ingénu, meaning 'ingenuous')
