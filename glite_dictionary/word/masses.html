masses

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæsɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæsɪz / (BrE)
the ordinary people in society, especially as distinct from those with power or influence
the common people

Examples

The politician promised to fight for the rights of the masses, not just the wealthy few.
Popular culture often reflects the tastes and preferences of the masses.
The new movement aimed to uplift the masses through education and reform.

Synset Examples

separate the warriors from the mass
power to the people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elite - a select group that is superior in terms of ability or qualities to the rest of a group or society

Etymology

From Latin massa (a lump, mass), through French masse; pluralized in English to refer to large groups of people.
