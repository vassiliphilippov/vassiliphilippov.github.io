strikingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of attracting attention by being unusual, noticeable, or impressive
the quality of being striking or noticeable

Examples

The strikingness of the painting made it the centerpiece of the exhibition.
Her outfit had a certain strikingness that drew everyone's attention.
What impressed me most was the strikingness of the mountain landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noticeability - the quality of being easily seen or noticed
  • Antonym: unremarkableness - the quality of not being striking or remarkable

Etymology

From 'striking' (from the verb 'strike') + -ness, forming a noun meaning the quality of being striking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very noticeable or attracting attention; salience
quality of being very noticeable

Examples

The strikingness of her red dress made her stand out in the crowd.
Scientists noted the strikingness of the results in the latest experiment.
He admired the strikingness of the mountain scenery during his hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salience - the quality of being particularly noticeable or important
  • Synonym: prominence - the state of being important, famous, or easily seen
  • Antonym: insignificance - the state of being unimportant or hardly noticeable

Etymology

From the adjective 'striking' + suffix '-ness' forming nouns denoting state or quality.
