Stenochlaena

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛnoʊˈkleɪnə / (AmE), / ˌstiːnɒˈkleɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical ferns, including some climbing epiphytes and bog-dwelling terrestrial species, native to Africa, Asia, and Australasia.
genus of large tropical ferns

Examples

The Stenochlaena genus is commonly found in tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists studied the climbing ability of Stenochlaena on tall trees in the region.
Some indigenous communities use Stenochlaena leaves as a vegetable and in traditional medicine.

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'chlaena' (cloak), referring to a feature of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstɛnoʊˈkleɪnə / (AmE), / ˌstɛnəʊˈkleɪnə / (BrE)
a genus of large tropical ferns, some of which are epiphytic climbers and some terrestrial bog ferns, native to Africa, Asia, and Australasia
a genus of tropical ferns

Examples

Stenochlaena is widespread in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Some species in the Stenochlaena genus climb high into the forest canopy.
Researchers discovered a new Stenochlaena species in the wetlands of northern Australia.

Etymology

From Greek *stenos* (narrow) + *chlaena* (cloak or covering), referring to features of the plant.
