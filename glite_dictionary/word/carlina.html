Carlina

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈliːnə / (AmE), / kɑːˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean thistles in the daisy family, characterized by spiny leaves and large, often straw-colored, flower heads.
genus of Mediterranean thistles

Examples

The rare Carlina is recognized for its unique, silvery flower head found in Mediterranean regions.
Botanists collected several Carlina specimens for their study on Mediterranean flora.
The fields were dotted with the spiny leaves and straw flowers of the Carlina genus.

Etymology

From New Latin, probably derived from the Italian 'Carlina' or Medieval Latin for 'thistle', possibly from the root name 'Charles' (Latin Carolus) as a dedication.

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈliːnə / (AmE), / kɑːˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean thistles in the family Asteraceae, known for their spiny leaves and flower heads.
genus of Mediterranean thistles

Examples

The Carlina genus comprises several species of thistles found throughout the Mediterranean region.
Some species in the Carlina genus are valued for their medicinal properties.
Researchers studied the pollination of Carlina flowers in southern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'Carlina', likely named after Emperor Charles V.
