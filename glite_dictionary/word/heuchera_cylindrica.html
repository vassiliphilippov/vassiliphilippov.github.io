Heuchera cylindrica

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːkərə sɪˈlɪndrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈhjuːkərə sɪˈlɪndrɪkə / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant native to western North America, with leathery heart-shaped leaves at the base of long stalks and greenish-white flowers arranged along the upper part of the stem.
a perennial plant of western North America

Examples

Several gardeners planted Heuchera cylindrica to add texture and color to the shady borders.
The Heuchera cylindrica blooms in late spring, displaying its delicate greenish-white flowers.
Researchers studied how Heuchera cylindrica adapts to different soil conditions in the Pacific Northwest.

Etymology

Genus name from Johann Heinrich von Heucher, German botanist; species epithet from Latin 'cylindricus', meaning cylindrical.
