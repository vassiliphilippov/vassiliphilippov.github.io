assignment

1. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈsaɪnmənt / (BrE)
a task or duty that someone is given to perform, especially in the armed forces or by an authority
task or duty given to someone

Examples

The soldier accepted the assignment despite knowing it would be dangerous.
Her latest assignment required her to travel overseas for six months.
After completing his assignment, he reported back to his commanding officer.

Synset Examples

hazardous assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: task - a piece of work to be done
  • Synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a responsibility
  • Synonym: mission - an important assignment carried out for a specific purpose

Etymology

From Latin assignare (to allocate, mark out), via Old French assignement (assignment, appointment).

/ əˈsaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈsaɪnmənt / (BrE)

a legal document or instrument by which a right, claim, interest, or property is transferred from one person to another

Examples
