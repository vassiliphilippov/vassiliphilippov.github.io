edda

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdə / (AmE), / ˈɛdə / (BrE)
a tropical starchy tuberous root that is cooked and eaten as food, especially in Caribbean and African cuisine
tropical starchy tuberous root

Examples

The stew contained pieces of edda, adding a rich, satisfying texture to the dish.
Farmers in the region grow edda as a staple food crop alongside yams and sweet potatoes.
She boiled the edda before mashing it and serving it with spiced vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical plant grown for its edible starchy root
