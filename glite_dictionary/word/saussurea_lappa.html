Saussurea lappa

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈsʊriːə ˈlæpə / (AmE), / sɔːˈsjʊəriə ˈlæpə / (BrE)
An annual herb native to the eastern Himalayas, especially Kashmir, with purple florets and a fragrant root used in perfumery and for preserving furs.
Himalayan herb with fragrant root

Examples

The essential oil extracted from Saussurea lappa is highly valued in the perfume industry.
Traditional medicine in Kashmir often includes preparations made from Saussurea lappa roots.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Saussurea lappa in its native Himalayan habitat.

Etymology

Genus name from M.-D. Saussure, Swiss naturalist; species from Hindi 'lappa'.
