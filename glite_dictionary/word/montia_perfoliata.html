Montia perfoliata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑntiə pɜrˌfoʊliˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈmɒntiə pɜːˌfəʊliˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A succulent herb native to western North America, sometimes cultivated for use as a salad green or pot herb; also known as miner's lettuce.
succulent herb, also called miner's lettuce

Examples

Montia perfoliata is a popular choice in spring salads due to its mild flavor.
Wild patches of Montia perfoliata can be found along the coast during the rainy season.
Chefs often use Montia perfoliata as a garnish for its crisp texture and freshness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miner's lettuce - Alternative common name for Montia perfoliata

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Montia' (after Giuseppe Monti, Italian botanist) and 'perfoliata' (Latin for 'through the leaf').
