saline

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪliːn / (BrE)
a solution of salt (sodium chloride) in distilled water, commonly used in medicine to match the salt concentration of body fluids
salt solution used in medicine
medical

Examples

The nurse prepared a fresh bag of saline for the patient’s IV drip.
After cleaning the wound with saline, the doctor applied a bandage.
Physiological saline is often used to rinse contact lenses safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saline solution - a solution of salt in water; another term for saline

Etymology

From Latin salina (salt pit), from sal (salt).

2. ADJ

/ ˈseɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪliːn / (BrE)
containing or consisting of salt; salty
containing salt; salty

Examples

The doctor prescribed a saline solution to clean the wound.
The water in the lake is highly saline due to mineral deposits.
Scientists studied the effects of saline soils on crop growth.

Synset Examples

a saline substance
salty tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salty - tasting of or containing salt

Etymology

From Latin salinus (of salt), from sal (salt).
