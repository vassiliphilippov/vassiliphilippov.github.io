Apis

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pɪs / (BrE)
A genus of bees comprising the honeybees, especially the common honeybee (Apis mellifera).
genus of honeybees

Examples

The genus Apis includes several species important for honey production.
Scientists study the behavior of Apis to understand pollination.
The European Apis mellifera is widely kept by beekeepers around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeybee - the common bee that produces honey

Etymology

From Latin 'apis' meaning 'bee'.
