Casanova de Seingalt

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑː.səˈnoʊ.və də seɪnˈɡælt / (AmE), / ˌkæ.səˈnəʊ.və də seɪnˈɡælt / (BrE)
An Italian adventurer and writer (1725–1798), famous for his autobiographical accounts of his romantic and sexual exploits.
Italian adventurer and memoirist (1725–1798)

Examples

Many biographies have been written about Casanova de Seingalt and his extraordinary life.
The original memoirs of Casanova de Seingalt offer a vivid portrait of 18th-century Europe.
Historians often reference Casanova de Seingalt when discussing libertine culture.

Etymology

From the Italian name Giacomo Girolamo Casanova de Seingalt.
