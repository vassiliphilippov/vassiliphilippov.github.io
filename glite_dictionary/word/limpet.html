limpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmpət / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpɪt / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a low, conical shell that attaches tightly to rocks or other hard surfaces
marine mollusk with conical shell

Examples

The rocky shoreline was covered with limpets clinging to the stones.
At low tide, children searched for crabs and limpets among the tidal pools.
A limpet uses its strong foot to attach itself firmly to rocks in the intertidal zone.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin limpetta, diminutive of Latin lampetra (a kind of shellfish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪm.pɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪm.pɪt / (BrE)
a marine mollusc with a low, conical shell that firmly attaches itself to rocks in coastal areas
marine mollusc with conical shell

Examples

The rocky shore was covered with limpets and barnacles.
During low tide, children searched for limpets attached to the exposed boulders.
Some birds, like oystercatchers, feed on limpet meat by prising them off rocks.

Etymology

From Middle English *lempet*, from Old French *limpite*, from Medieval Latin *lempeda*.
