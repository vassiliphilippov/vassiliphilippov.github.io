attendance

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndəns / (AmE), / əˈtɛndəns / (BrE)
The act of being present at a place, such as at a meeting, event, or class.
the act of being present at an event

Examples

Her attendance at every class was noted by the teacher.
Employee attendance at the meeting was mandatory.
The concert had a record attendance of over 10,000 people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absence - the state of being away or not present

Etymology

From Old French 'attandance', from 'attendre' (to attend), from Latin 'attendere' (to stretch toward).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndəns / (AmE), / əˈtɛndəns / (BrE)
the act or state of being present at a place, or the frequency with which someone is present, especially at work or school
rate or act of being present

Examples

Regular attendance is required to pass the course.
His poor attendance at work has been noticed by his supervisor.
School attendance dropped during the flu outbreak.

Synset Examples

a student's attendance is an important factor in her grade

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absence - the state of being away or not present

Etymology

From Old French 'attendance', from 'attendre' (to attend), from Latin 'attendere' (to give heed to).

3. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndəns / (AmE), / əˈtɛndəns / (BrE)
the number of people present at an event, meeting, or place
number of people present

Examples

The concert's attendance exceeded expectations, with more than 5,000 people present.
School attendance dropped noticeably during the winter months.
A record attendance was reported for the final match of the tournament.

Synset Examples

attendance was up by 50 per cent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnout - the number of people attending or taking part in an event

Etymology

From Old French 'attendre' (to attend, wait for) and Medieval Latin 'attendentia' (attention, waiting upon).
