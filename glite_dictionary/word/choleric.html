choleric

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑlərɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒlərɪk / (BrE)
easily angered; showing or tending to show quick irritability or anger.
easily angered; irritable

Examples

The choleric coach shouted at the referee after a questionable call.
Despite his choleric nature, his friends valued his honesty and loyalty.
As a boss, he could be choleric when faced with repeated mistakes.

Synset Examples

a hotheaded commander

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irascible - easily made angry
  • Antonym: calm - not easily angered or stirred up

Etymology

From Greek *kholērikos*, relating to bile, via Latin *cholericus*; in medieval medicine, a 'choleric' temperament was thought to result from an excess of yellow bile.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːlərĭk / (AmE), / ˈkɒlərɪk / (BrE)
Easily angered; bad-tempered or irritable.
easily angered; irritable

Examples

Despite his choleric nature, he was respected by his peers for his honesty.
The manager's choleric outbursts made the office atmosphere tense.
People with a choleric temperament can lose their patience quickly.

Synset Examples

men of the choleric type take to kicking and smashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: calm - not easily angered or excited

Etymology

From Latin *cholericus* (pertaining to choler, bile), from Greek *kholē* (bile), reflecting ancient beliefs about temperament.

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːlərɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒlərɪk / (BrE)
Easily angered; showing or tending to show anger or irritability.
easily angered; irritable

Examples

He became increasingly choleric as the discussion continued.
The choleric tone in her voice made everyone uncomfortable.
Facing traffic every morning put him in a choleric mood for hours.

Synset Examples

a choleric outburst
an irascible response

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irascible - easily angered or prone to anger
  • Antonym: placid - not easily upset or excited; calm

Etymology

From Latin 'cholericus', from 'cholera' (bile), associating temperaments with bodily humours.
