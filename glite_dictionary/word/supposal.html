supposal

1. NOUN

/ səˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
the act or process of supposing or assuming something; a supposition
the act of supposing or assuming

Examples

The theory is based on the supposal that markets behave rationally.
His argument falls apart if the initial supposal is false.
Philosophers debated the consequences of this supposal for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supposition - an idea that is assumed or taken for granted

Etymology

From 'suppose' + '-al', patterned after Latin derivatives.

2. NOUN

/ səˈpoʊzəl / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzəl / (BrE)
an idea or hypothesis that is assumed to be true for the sake of argument or reasoning
an assumed hypothesis or idea

Examples

Let us examine your supposal and see if it holds up to scrutiny.
The entire argument is based on the supposal that humans are inherently social.
Her supposal about the cause of the incident proved to be incorrect.

Synset Examples

any society is built upon certain assumptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true without proof
  • Synonym: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence

Etymology

From suppose + -al, modeled on analogous formations in Latin and English.

Synset POS: n
