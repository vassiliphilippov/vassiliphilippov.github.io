insatiably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈseɪʃəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪʃəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be satisfied; to an insatiable degree.
in a manner that cannot be satisfied

Examples

He insatiably pursued knowledge, always seeking more answers to his questions.
The crowd cheered insatiably for their favorite band during the entire concert.
She scrolled insatiably through social media, never feeling content with what she saw.

Synset Examples

she was insatiably hungry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insatiable - impossible to satisfy
  • Antonym: satiably - in a way that can be satisfied

Etymology

From Latin 'insatiabilis' (unsatisfiable) with English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈseɪʃəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪʃəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be satisfied; with relentless or unquenchable desire.
in an unsatisfiable manner

Examples

She pursued her goals insatiably, never feeling content with her achievements.
The public's appetite for celebrity gossip seemed to grow insatiably with each passing year.
He read insatiably, devouring book after book late into the night.

Synset Examples

he clawed insatiably at the traditional precepts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insatiable - incapable of being satisfied
  • Antonym: satiably - in a manner that can be satisfied

Etymology

From 'insatiable' + '-ly', with 'insatiable' from Latin 'insatiabilis'.
