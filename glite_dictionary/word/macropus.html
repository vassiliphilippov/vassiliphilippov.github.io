Macropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.rə.pəs / (AmE), / ˈmæk.rə.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials in the family Macropodidae, including typical kangaroos and wallabies native to Australia and nearby islands.
genus of kangaroos and wallabies

Examples

The red kangaroo, classified in the genus Macropus, is the largest marsupial in the world.
Several species within Macropus inhabit the woodlands and grasslands of Australia.
Scientists have debated the evolutionary history of the Macropus genus based on fossil evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo - large marsupial native to Australia
  • Synonym: wallaby - small to medium-sized marsupial of Australia

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + pous (foot), referring to the large hind feet of kangaroos and wallabies.
