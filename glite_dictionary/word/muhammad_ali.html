Muhammad Ali

1. PROPN

/ məˌhɑməd ɑːˈli / (AmE), / məˌhɑːməd ɑːˈliː / (BrE)
(1942–2016) United States professional boxer, world heavyweight champion three times, known for his charisma and activism.
famous American heavyweight boxer

Examples

Muhammad Ali was known as much for his quick wit and activism as for his success in the ring.
Many consider Muhammad Ali to be one of the greatest athletes of the twentieth century.
The legacy of Muhammad Ali extends beyond boxing, inspiring people worldwide.

2. PROPN

/ məˌhɑːməd ɑːˈliː / (AmE), / ˌmʊhəməd ɑːˈliː / (BrE)
(1769–1849) Albanian-born soldier and ruler who became the Ottoman viceroy of Egypt, effectively founding modern Egypt by asserting autonomy from the Ottoman Empire and implementing major reforms.
Ottoman viceroy and founder of modern Egypt

Examples

Muhammad Ali introduced sweeping reforms that shaped Egypt's economy and military in the nineteenth century.
Historians regard Muhammad Ali as the founder of modern Egypt due to his extensive modernization efforts.
The dynasty established by Muhammad Ali ruled Egypt until the mid-twentieth century.

Etymology

From Arabic Muḥammad ʿAlī; Arabic personal names.
