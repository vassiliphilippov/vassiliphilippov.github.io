glamor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæmər / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmə / (BrE)
alluring beauty or charm, often with an element of sophistication or sex appeal
alluring beauty or charm

Examples

She was drawn to the glamor of Hollywood and dreamt of becoming a movie star.
The party was filled with glamor and excitement, dazzling all who attended.
Despite the apparent glamor of his job, it involved long hours and little sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glamour - variant British spelling with the same meaning
  • Antonym: plainness - lack of embellishment or attraction

Etymology

From Scots 'glamer' or 'gramarye', meaning a spell or enchantment.
