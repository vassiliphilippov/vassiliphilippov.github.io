Thermopylae

1. PROPN

/ θərˈmɑːpəˌli / (AmE), / θəˈmɒpɪliː / (BrE)
the site of a famous battle in 480 BC where a small Greek force led by King Leonidas resisted a much larger Persian army
ancient battle site in Greece

Examples

The Battle of Thermopylae has become a symbol of courage against overwhelming odds.
Many tourists visit Thermopylae to see the monument honoring King Leonidas and his 300 Spartans.
Historians still debate the exact number of soldiers present at Thermopylae.

Etymology

From Greek Thermopylai, meaning 'hot gates', referring to the hot springs in the area.
