rug-cutting

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡ ˌkʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡ ˌkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
dancing energetically, especially with skill and enthusiasm.
energetic or skillful dancing
informal

Examples

The couple amazed everyone with their rug-cutting at the wedding reception.
Old swing music often brings out some serious rug-cutting from the crowd.
He was famous for his spontaneous rug-cutting whenever the band played his favorite song.

Synset Examples

The dancers showcased impressive rug-cutting skills at the competition.
After a few drinks, he started rug-cutting on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut a rug - to dance energetically; to dance well

Etymology

From the idiom 'cut a rug', American English slang from early 20th century, referring to energetic dancing on carpeted floors.
