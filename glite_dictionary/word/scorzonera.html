scorzonera

1. NOUN

/ skɔːrzoʊˈnɛrə / (AmE), / skɔːzəˈnɪərə / (BrE)
A perennial European herb of the genus Scorzonera, with narrow leaves, solitary yellow flower heads, and long edible black roots.
edible-rooted European herb

Examples

The roots of scorzonera are often cooked and served like salsify.
She planted scorzonera in her vegetable garden for its edible black roots.
The chef used scorzonera to add a unique flavor to the vegetable dish.

Etymology

From Italian 'scorzonera', probably from 'scorza' (bark, rind).

2. NOUN

/ skɔrˌzoʊˈnɛrə / (AmE), / skɔːˌzəʊˈnɪərə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the daisy family, cultivated for its edible long black root, also known as black salsify.
edible black-rooted plant; black salsify

Examples

The chef used scorzonera in the soup for its earthy flavor and unique texture.
Farmers harvest scorzonera in late autumn when its roots are at their best.
Roasted scorzonera pairs well with other root vegetables in winter dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsify - an edible root vegetable with a white or black root

Etymology

From Italian 'scorzonera', probably from 'scorzone' (adder, large snake), referring to the black skin of the root.
