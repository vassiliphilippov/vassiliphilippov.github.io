blastocoel

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstəˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈblastəˌsiːl / (BrE)
the fluid-filled cavity inside a blastula, formed during the early stages of embryonic development
cavity inside a blastula
medical

Examples

The blastocoel expands as the embryo continues to develop in the early stages.
Scientists examined the role of ions in forming the blastocoel during embryogenesis.
A healthy blastocoel is essential for the proper development of the blastula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastula - an early stage of embryo development, a hollow sphere of cells

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ, sprout) + 'koilos' (hollow, cavity).
