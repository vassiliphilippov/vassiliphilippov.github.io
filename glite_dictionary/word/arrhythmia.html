arrhythmia

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪð.mi.ə / (AmE), / əˈrɪð.mi.ə / (BrE)
a condition in which the heart beats with an irregular or abnormal rhythm
irregular heartbeat condition
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with arrhythmia after noticing irregular patterns in his heartbeat.
Many types of arrhythmia can be harmless, but some may require medical attention.
She wears a heart monitor to detect any signs of arrhythmia during exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpitation - noticeably rapid, strong, or irregular heartbeat
  • Degree Higher: tachycardia - abnormally fast heart rate
  • Degree Lower: bradycardia - abnormally slow heart rate

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'rhythmos' (rhythm).
