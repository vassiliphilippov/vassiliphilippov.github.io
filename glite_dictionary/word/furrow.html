furrow

1. VERB

/ ˈfɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːrəʊ / (BrE)
to make a long, narrow groove or trench in a surface, especially in the ground
make a groove or trench

Examples

The farmer used a plow to furrow the field before planting seeds.
Heavy rain can furrow the hillside, creating small channels in the soil.
Earthworms naturally furrow the ground as they move through it.

Synset Examples

furrow soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groove - to cut a long, narrow channel or depression in a surface

Etymology

From Old English furh (a trench, furrow), related to Old High German furuh.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːrəʊ / (BrE)
to make lines, wrinkles, or creases in something, especially on the skin or face
make creased or wrinkled

Examples

She furrowed her brow in concentration as she read the instructions.
A deep frown furrowed his forehead when he heard the news.
The old man furrowed his brow, recalling a distant memory.

Synset Examples

furrow one's brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - to make a small line or fold in a surface, especially the skin

Etymology

From Old English 'furh' (trench, furrow), related to plowing lines in a field.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01280348-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfʌrəʊ / (BrE)
a long, narrow trench made in the ground by a plow, especially for planting seeds or for irrigation
long narrow trench in ground, esp. by plow

Examples

The farmer guided his horse-drawn plow carefully to make a straight furrow in the soil.
After the rain, water collected in the deep furrows left in the field.
Seeds are typically planted in a furrow to protect them from wind and birds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ridge - a raised strip of soil between furrows

Etymology

From Old English 'furh', related to Old High German 'furuh' (furrow) and Latin 'porca' (ridge between furrows).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03411876-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈfɜːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfʌrəʊ / (BrE)
a slight depression or fold in a smooth surface, especially one caused by movement or pressure
a slight fold or groove in a surface

Examples

She noticed a deep furrow between his brows as he concentrated on the puzzle.
Ironing the shirt removed every furrow and left the fabric perfectly smooth.
Rainwater collected in the shallow furrows that had formed over time on the old statue.
