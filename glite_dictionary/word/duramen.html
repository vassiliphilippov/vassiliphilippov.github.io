duramen

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊrəmən / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəmən / (BrE)
the hard, dense, inner part of the wood in a tree, usually darker and older than the surrounding sapwood
the hard inner wood of a tree

Examples

The craftsman selected pieces of duramen for their strength and durability.
Unlike sapwood, the duramen is more resistant to decay and pests.
The color difference between the duramen and the sapwood was clearly visible in the cross-section of the log.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heartwood - the dense inner part of a tree trunk, yielding the hardest timber
  • Antonym: sapwood - the softer, younger outer wood in a tree, lighter and less dense than heartwood

Etymology

From Latin 'duramen' meaning 'hardness', from 'durare' (to harden).
