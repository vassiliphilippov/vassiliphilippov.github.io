syndicalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndɪkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪndɪkəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a political movement or theory advocating the control of industry and government by labor unions, typically by direct action such as strikes or sabotage
control of industry by labor unions

Examples

Syndicalism gained popularity among workers in the early twentieth century.
Many advocates of syndicalism believed in organizing general strikes to achieve their aims.
He wrote a book explaining the principles of syndicalism and its role in labor movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchism - belief in abolishing all government; self-managed societies

Etymology

From French syndicalisme, from syndicat (trade union).
