to horripilate

1. VERB

/ hɔːˈrɪpɪleɪt / (AmE), / hɒˈrɪpɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or stimulus) to cause the hair to stand on end and produce goosebumps, typically as a reaction to fear or awe.
to cause hair to stand on end; produce goosebumps

Examples

The chilling story made the audience horripilate as they listened in the dark.
Thunder crashing overhead can horripilate even the bravest campers.
Her eerie whispering voice horripilated everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

Hitchcock movies horripilate me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosebumps - small bumps on the skin caused by cold or fear

Etymology

From Latin horripilare, from horrere (to bristle, shudder) + pilus (hair).

2. VERB

/ hɔˈrɪpɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / hɒˈrɪpɪˌleɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to have one's hair stand on end, especially as a result of fear, cold, or excitement.
to have one's hair stand on end

Examples

Whenever he watched horror movies alone, he would horripilate uncontrollably.
The chilly wind made her skin horripilate during the morning walk.
Many animals horripilate when threatened as a form of defense.

Synset Examples

I horripilate when I see violence on television

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosebumps - a state in which the skin becomes bumpy and hair stands on end from emotion or cold

Etymology

From Latin horripilare, from horrere (to bristle, shudder) + pilus (hair).
