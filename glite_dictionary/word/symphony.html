symphony

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.fə.ni / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.fə.ni / (BrE)
a long, elaborate musical composition, typically for a full orchestra, usually consisting of several movements
orchestral musical composition

Examples

The orchestra performed Beethoven's Fifth symphony to a packed concert hall.
She has composed her first symphony, which will premiere this autumn.
Each movement of the symphony has its own distinct character and mood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concerto - a musical work for solo instrument(s) and orchestra

Etymology

From Greek 'symphōnia' (concord of sound, harmony), via Latin 'symphonia'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.fə.ni / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.fə.ni / (BrE)
A large orchestra, typically consisting of strings, woodwinds, brass, and percussion, capable of performing symphonic music.
large orchestra

Examples

The symphony played a magnificent rendition of Beethoven's Fifth last night.
She auditions every year for the city symphony, hoping to join as a violinist.
The Vienna symphony is renowned for its rich sound and talented musicians.

Synset Examples

we heard the Vienna symphony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestra - a large group of musicians who play together on various instruments

Etymology

From Latin 'symphonia', from Greek 'symphōnia' meaning 'concord or harmony of sound'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
