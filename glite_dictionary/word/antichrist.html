Antichrist

1. NOUN

/ ˈæntiˌkraɪst / (AmE), / ˈæntɪˌkraɪst / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the great adversary of Christ who is prophesied to appear before the end of the world, opposing Christ and deceiving many until ultimately defeated.
the adversary of Christ in Christian prophecy

Examples

Many believers fear the rise of the Antichrist as a sign of the end times.
Throughout history, some have speculated about the identity of the Antichrist.
The concept of the Antichrist has influenced countless works of literature and art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Messiah - the savior or liberator in Jewish and Christian beliefs

Etymology

From Greek 'antichristos' (opponent of the Messiah), from 'anti-' (against) + 'Christos' (Christ).
