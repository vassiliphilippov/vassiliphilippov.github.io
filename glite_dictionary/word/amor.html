Amor

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.mɔr / (AmE), / ˈɑː.mɔː / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of love, equivalent to the Greek Eros.
Roman god of love

Examples

In ancient texts, Amor is often depicted as a youthful figure with wings and arrows.
The poet wrote about Amor inspiring the hearts of lovers.
Statues of Amor adorned many Roman gardens as symbols of affection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eros - Greek god of love, equivalent to Amor
  • Synonym: Cupid - Roman god of desire and affection, often identified with Amor

Etymology

From Latin *amor* meaning 'love.'
