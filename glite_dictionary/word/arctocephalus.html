Arctocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ɑrktoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ɑːktəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
a genus of fur seals containing most species of fur seal, found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere
genus of fur seals

Examples

The genus Arctocephalus includes several species of fur seals found in the southern oceans.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Arctocephalus pups along the coastline.
Members of Arctocephalus have been heavily hunted for their pelts in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur seal - a marine mammal with external ears and thick fur, found in cold regions

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + kephalē (head), referring to the bear-like head shape.

2. NOUN

/ ɑrktoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ɑːktəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A genus of fur seals comprising several southern hemisphere species.
a genus of fur seals

Examples

Scientists observed a group of Arctocephalus on the rocky shores of the island.
The Arctocephalus genus includes several species commonly known as fur seals.
Conservation efforts have helped increase Arctocephalus populations in some regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'arktos' (bear) + 'kephalē' (head), referring to the bear-like head of these seals.
