to trivialize

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪviəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem less important, serious, or significant than it really is
make seem less important or serious

Examples

We should not trivialize the challenges faced by students from disadvantaged backgrounds.
His comments seemed to trivialize the impact of climate change on local communities.
The film was criticized for trivializing a very serious topic.

Synset Examples

Don't trivialize the seriousness of the issue!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to minimize - reduce the importance or seriousness of something
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - represent something as being larger, greater, or more important than it really is

Etymology

From 'trivial' + '-ize' (suffix forming verbs).
