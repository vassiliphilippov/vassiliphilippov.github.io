snakefly

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
a predatory insect found mainly in western North America, distinguished by its long, necklike prothorax
predatory insect with long necklike prothorax

Examples

Researchers discovered a rare snakefly species in the forest last summer.
The snakefly uses its elongated neck to attack smaller insects.
Some gardeners welcome the presence of snakeflies because they help control pests.

Etymology

From 'snake' + 'fly', named for its long neck resembling that of a snake.
