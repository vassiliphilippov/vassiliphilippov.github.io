Buteo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of broad-winged, soaring hawks found mainly in the Old and New Worlds
genus of broad-winged soaring hawks

Examples

Many North American hawks, such as the red-tailed hawk, belong to the Buteo genus.
The Buteo species are easily recognized by their broad wings and robust bodies.
During migration, large flocks of Buteo can be observed soaring over open fields.

Etymology

From Latin *buteo* meaning 'buzzard' or 'hawk'.
