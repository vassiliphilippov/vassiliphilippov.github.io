Prenanthes purpurea

1. NOUN

/ priˈnænθiːz pɜːrˈpjʊriə / (AmE), / priˈnænθiːz pɜːˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to central and southern Europe, with purple flower heads.
European herb with purple florets

Examples

Prenanthes purpurea grows in shady woodland areas across southern Europe.
The striking purple florets of Prenanthes purpurea make it easy to spot in the wild.
Botanists collected samples of Prenanthes purpurea to study its distribution and variations.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Prenanthes' and species 'purpurea' (purple), referring to the flower color.
