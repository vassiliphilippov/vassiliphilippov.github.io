cyanamid

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænəmɪd / (AmE), / saɪˈænəmɪd / (BrE)
A weak, soluble dibasic acid that is the parent acid of cyanamide salts.
weak dibasic acid in chemistry
medical

Examples

In the experiment, cyanamid was used as a precursor for synthesizing new compounds.
The solubility of cyanamid makes it useful in various industrial applications.
Researchers studied the properties of cyanamid in aqueous solutions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cyanamide - A chemical compound derived from cyanamid, used in fertilizers

Etymology

From 'cyanamide' with 'cyan-' (blue, cyanogen) and '-amide' (chemical suffix from Greek 'amide').
