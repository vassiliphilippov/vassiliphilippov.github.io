proximity

1. NOUN

/ prɑːkˈsɪməti / (AmE), / prɒkˈsɪməti / (BrE)
the state of being near in space or distance; nearness to a person or thing.
nearness; closeness in space

Examples

The restaurant’s proximity to the beach makes it a popular choice for tourists.
Please remain within close proximity of the emergency exits at all times.
The proximity of the train tracks means we often hear trains passing by.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: distance - the amount of space between two points
  • Synonym: vicinity - the area near or surrounding a particular place

Etymology

From Latin proximus (nearest, next).

nearness in space, time, or relationship

Wildlife often avoids areas with high human proximity.

  • Synonym: closeness - the state of being close or near

3. NOUN

/ prɑkˈsɪməti / (AmE), / prɒkˈsɪməti / (BrE)
In psychology, the principle that objects or events that are close to each other in space or time are perceived as a group or unit.
Gestalt principle; perception of closeness as grouping

Examples

According to the principle of proximity, we tend to see the dots grouped together as a single object.
Gestalt psychologists explained how proximity influences our perception of visual patterns.
The proximity of the lines in the diagram makes them appear related.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - Gestalt principle: elements that are similar tend to be grouped together
  • Synonym: continuity - Gestalt principle: elements arranged in a line or curve are perceived as a group
  • Synonym: closure - Gestalt principle: the mind completes incomplete figures to form familiar shapes

Etymology

From Latin proximus (nearest, next).
