Oxyuranus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɒksiˈjʊrənəs / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈjʊərənəs / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous snakes native to Australia and New Guinea, commonly known as taipans.
genus of highly venomous snakes (taipans)

Examples

The coastal Oxyuranus is considered one of the world's most dangerous snakes.
Scientists studied the behavior of Oxyuranus in its natural habitat.
Medical professionals are trained to treat bites from Oxyuranus species, particularly the inland taipan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taipan - a highly venomous snake native to Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Greek oxys (sharp) + oura (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ɑːk.siˈjuː.rə.nəs / (AmE), / ɒk.sɪˈjʊə.rə.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous snakes native to Australia and New Guinea, commonly known as taipans.
genus of venomous snakes (taipans)

Examples

The Oxyuranus genus includes some of the most dangerous snakes in the world.
Researchers observed the behavior of several Oxyuranus specimens in their natural habitat.
A bite from a snake of the Oxyuranus genus requires immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taipan - a highly venomous snake native to Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Greek 'oxys' (sharp) and 'ouranos' (sky or tail), referring to features of the snakes.
