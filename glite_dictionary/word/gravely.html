gravely

1. ADV

/ ˈɡreɪvli / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvli / (BrE)
In a serious and solemn manner.
in a serious or solemn way

Examples

He looked at her gravely and nodded in agreement.
The judge spoke gravely about the consequences of the crime.
She gravely explained the importance of following the rules.

Synset Examples

he walked soberly toward the altar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solemnly - in a serious and dignified way
  • Antonym: lightly - without seriousness or gravity

Etymology

From grave (serious) + -ly (adverb suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈɡreɪvli / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvli / (BrE)
To a very serious or severe degree; in a manner that suggests something is grave or critical.
to a serious or severe degree

Examples

The doctor said her condition was gravely concerning and required immediate surgery.
He looked gravely at the committee before delivering the bad news.
Several villagers were gravely injured in the accident.

Synset Examples

fingers so badly frozen they had to be amputated
badly injured
a severely impaired heart
is gravely ill
was seriously ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriously - to a serious degree or extent
  • Antonym: lightly - not in a serious or severe manner

Etymology

From grave (serious) + -ly (adverb suffix).
