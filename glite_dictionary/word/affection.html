affection

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈfɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
A gentle feeling of fondness, liking, or love towards someone or something.
a feeling of fondness or love

Examples

She showed her affection for her grandmother with a warm embrace.
His affection towards animals was obvious whenever he volunteered at the shelter.
They exchanged letters to maintain their affection despite the distance.

Synset Examples

he had trouble expressing the affection he felt
the child won everyone's heart
the warmness of his welcome made us feel right at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fondness - a feeling of affection or liking for someone or something
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'affectio' (disposition, affection), from 'afficere' (to influence).
