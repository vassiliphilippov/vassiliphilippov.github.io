ASALA

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɑːlə / (AmE), / əˈsɑːlə / (BrE)
an Armenian militant Marxist-Leninist organization, founded in 1975, known for acts of political violence to pressure Turkey to acknowledge the Armenian genocide, pay reparations, and give territory to Armenia.
Armenian militant group from 1975

Examples

The ASALA bombing at Orly Airport in Paris drew international condemnation.
Authorities linked the series of attacks in the 1980s to ASALA.
Discussions about Armenian political history often mention ASALA's activities.

Synset Examples

ASALA bombing at Orly Airport in Paris in 1983 killed 8 and wounded 55 people

Etymology

Acronym of Armenian Secret Army for the Liberation of Armenia.
