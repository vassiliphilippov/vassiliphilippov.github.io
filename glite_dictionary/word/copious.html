copious

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊpiəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpiəs / (BrE)
Large in quantity or number; abundant; supplying or yielding a plentiful amount.
abundant; in large quantity

Examples

She took copious notes during the lecture to make sure she didn't miss any details.
After the storm, copious amounts of rainwater flooded the streets and gardens.
The author received copious praise for his thorough and detailed research.

Synset Examples

had ample food for the party
copious provisions
food is plentiful
a plenteous grape harvest
a rich supply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundant - existing or occurring in large amounts
  • Antonym: scarce - not enough in quantity; hard to find

Etymology

From Latin 'copiosus' meaning 'plentiful', from 'copia' (plenty).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊ.pi.əs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.pi.əs / (BrE)
existing or produced in large quantities; abundant, especially in writing or speech.
in large quantities; abundant

Examples

She took copious notes during the lecture to make sure she didn't miss anything important.
After a week of copious rainfall, the rivers began to overflow.
The author received copious feedback from her editors before publishing the novel.

Synset Examples

she took copious notes
a subject of voluminous legislation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundant - present in large quantities
  • Synonym: plentiful - existing in great quantity
  • Antonym: scarce - in short supply or not enough

Etymology

From Latin 'copiosus' (plentiful, abundant), from 'copia' (plenty).
