unevenness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈivənnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvnnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not level, equal, or balanced; lack of uniformity or regularity
lack of balance or uniformity

Examples

The unevenness of the pavement made walking difficult for pedestrians.
Critics noted the unevenness in the quality of the performances throughout the season.
She was frustrated by the unevenness of her progress in learning the language.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evenness - the quality of being level, equal, or uniform

Etymology

From 'uneven' (not even) + '-ness', forming a noun to indicate a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈivənnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvənnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being uneven or lacking uniformity; irregularity or lack of smoothness or equality in surface, distribution, or consistency
the state of being uneven; irregularity

Examples

The unevenness of the terrain made it difficult for hikers to walk safely.
There was a noticeable unevenness in the paint coverage on the wall.
Critics pointed out the unevenness in the book's pacing and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irregularity - the state or quality of being not regular or uniform
  • Antonym: smoothness - quality of being even or free from roughness

Etymology

Formed from 'uneven' (from Middle English, based on 'even') + '-ness', a noun-forming suffix.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
