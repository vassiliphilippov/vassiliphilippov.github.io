eyeball

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌbɔːl / (BrE)
the round part of the eye of a vertebrate, contained within the eye socket
the round part of the eye

Examples

The doctor examined his eyeball for any signs of infection.
A speck of dust got into her eyeball, making it water.
Some animals have much larger eyeballs relative to their head size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye - the organ of sight in animals and humans

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to look at or examine closely or with interest, often in a direct or noticeable way
look at closely or directly
informal

Examples

She eyeballed the new student as he walked into the classroom.
Before buying the house, they eyeballed every inch of it.
The coach eyeballed the players during warm-ups to see who was ready to start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - to look at someone or something fixedly
  • Antonym: glance - to look quickly or briefly

Etymology

From 'eyeball' (noun), referring to the ball-shaped organ of sight.
