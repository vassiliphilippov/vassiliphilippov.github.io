viricide

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪərɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
A substance or agent that inactivates or destroys viruses.
substance that kills viruses
medical

Examples

The new laboratory tested the effectiveness of each viricide against different strains of influenza.
After cleaning the room, a viricide was sprayed on all surfaces to ensure complete virus elimination.
Healthcare workers must use a certified viricide to prevent viral transmission in hospitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfectant - a chemical liquid that destroys bacteria
  • Synonym: bactericide - an agent that destroys bacteria
  • Synonym: fungicide - a substance that destroys fungi

Etymology

From Latin 'virus' (poison) + '-cide' (killer, from Latin 'caedere' to kill).
