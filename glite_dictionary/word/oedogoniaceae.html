Oedogoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ iˌdoʊɡoʊniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / iˌiːdəʊˌɡəʊnɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of filamentous green algae mainly found in freshwater, characterized by long, unbranched filaments and large oogonia
family of filamentous freshwater green algae
medical

Examples

Several species within the Oedogoniaceae are important for freshwater ecosystem studies.
Scientists examined the reproductive structures unique to the Oedogoniaceae family.
The pond's algae population included representatives from the Oedogoniaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Oedogonium + Latin suffix -aceae used for botanical family names.

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːdəˌɡoʊniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌiːdəˌɡəʊniˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of filamentous green algae found mostly in freshwater habitats, characterized by cylindrical cells and unique reproductive features
family of filamentous green algae
medical

Examples

Members of the Oedogoniaceae are commonly found in freshwater ponds and streams.
Scientists often study the Oedogoniaceae to understand algal growth and reproduction.
Unique cell division patterns distinguish the Oedogoniaceae from other green algal families.

Etymology

From the type genus Oedogonium, from Greek 'oidos' (swelling) and 'gonos' (seed, offspring), with Latin scientific suffix -aceae for families.
