hot-mouthed

1. ADJ

/ hɑt ˈmaʊθt / (AmE), / hɒt ˈmaʊθt / (BrE)
Quick to speak passionately or angrily; apt to express emotions forcefully in speech.
quick to speak angrily or passionately

Examples

The hot-mouthed politician often got into heated arguments during interviews.
Her hot-mouthed remarks left a strong impression on the panel.
Though usually calm, he can be surprisingly hot-mouthed when provoked.

Synset Examples

The hot-mouthed journalist stirred up controversy.
He's known for being hot-mouthed during debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quick-tempered - easily angered or excited; having a quick temper
  • Antonym: even-tempered - not easily angered or upset; calm and composed
