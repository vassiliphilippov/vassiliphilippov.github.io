Menshevik

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnʃəˌvɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɛnʃəˌvɪk / (BrE)
a member of the moderate socialist group in Russia that opposed the Bolsheviks and supported gradual reform prior to and during the Russian Revolution
Russian moderate socialist, opposed Bolsheviks

Examples

As a committed Menshevik, he advocated for reforms rather than violent revolution.
Many Mensheviks fled Russia after the Bolsheviks took control in 1917.
The Menshevik leaders argued for a democratic, parliamentary approach to socialism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Bolshevik - a member of the radical socialist group that seized power in Russia in 1917

Etymology

From Russian Меншевик (Menʹshevik), from меньший (menʹshiy, 'minority'), opposed to Bolshevik ('majority').
