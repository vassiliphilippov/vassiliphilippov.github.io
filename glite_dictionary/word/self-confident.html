self-confident

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈkɑnfɪdənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈkɒnfɪdənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing assurance in one's own abilities or worth
assured in one's own abilities

Examples

Despite the pressure, Rachel remained self-confident throughout the interview process.
A self-confident leader can inspire trust and motivate a team.
He spoke self-confidently in front of a large audience for the first time.

Synset Examples

hardly more than a boy but firm-knit and self-confident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - sure of oneself and one's abilities
  • Antonym: insecure - lacking confidence or assurance

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to oneself) + 'confident', from Latin 'confidere' (to have full trust).
