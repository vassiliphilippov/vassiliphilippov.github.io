goliard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊliˌɑrd / (AmE), / ˈɡɒliˌɑːd / (BrE)
A wandering scholar in medieval Europe, known for composing satirical Latin verses and leading a hedonistic and riotous lifestyle.
medieval wandering satirical scholar

Examples

The goliard traveled from town to town, entertaining crowds with clever and subversive Latin songs.
Many poems written by a goliard mocked the hypocrisy of religious leaders during the Middle Ages.
His rowdy lifestyle and biting satire earned him a reputation as a modern goliard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troubadour - a medieval lyric poet composing and singing in Provençal

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'goliardus', possibly from the name Goliath, used satirically.
