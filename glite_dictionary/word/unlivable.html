unlivable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɪvəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪvəbl / (BrE)
not suitable or safe to live in; unfit for habitation.
not fit to live in

Examples

The abandoned building is completely unlivable due to mold and structural damage.
Extreme heat made the apartment unlivable during the summer months.
They were forced to leave their home after flooding rendered it unlivable.

Synset Examples

unlivable substandard housing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhospitable - (of a place) harsh and difficult to live in
  • Antonym: habitable - (of a place) fit to live in

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'livable' (suitable for living).
