to laze

1. VERB

/ leɪz / (AmE), / leɪz / (BrE)
to spend time relaxing and being idle, doing very little or nothing
relax idly; be lazy

Examples

On Sundays, I like to laze around the house with a good book.
They spent the afternoon lazing by the pool, soaking up the sun.
He often lazes in bed until midday on weekends.

Synset Examples

The old man sat and stagnated on his porch
He slugged in bed all morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lounge - to sit or lie in a relaxed way; to spend time in a lazy, relaxed manner
  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing; to be inactive
  • Antonym: work - to exert effort or be active; the opposite of being idle

Etymology

Back-formation from 'lazy', first recorded in the 19th century.
