Victorian

1. ADJ

/ vɪkˈtɔriən / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Queen Victoria of Great Britain, her reign (1837–1901), or the culture, morals, and styles of that period.
relating to Queen Victoria or her era

Examples

The museum has an extensive collection of Victorian paintings and furniture.
Her grandmother’s house was decorated in a distinctly Victorian style.
Some people view Victorian values as overly strict and conservative today.

Synset Examples

Victorian morals

Etymology

From Queen Victoria (1819–1901), who reigned in Britain from 1837 to 1901.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03039307-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ vɪkˈtɔriən / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
Typical of, or relating to the moral standards, attitudes, or behaviour associated with the period of Queen Victoria's reign (1837–1901), especially characterized by strictness and propriety.
relating to the morals/manner of Victoria's reign

Examples

Her parents hold very Victorian views about relationships and modesty.
The novel satirizes the Victorian obsession with propriety and appearances.
Some consider these strict dress codes too Victorian for modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puritanical - Having strict moral or religious beliefs, especially about pleasure and sex

Etymology

From Queen Victoria (1819–1901), monarch of the United Kingdom 1837–1901.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01541854-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ vɪkˈtɔɹ.i.ən / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔː.ri.ən / (BrE)
a person who lived during the reign of Queen Victoria, especially in Britain (1837–1901)
person from Queen Victoria's reign

Examples

Many Victorians witnessed rapid technological changes during their lifetimes.
The novel features a family of Victorians adjusting to the industrial revolution.
Some Victorians held very strict views on social etiquette.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Edwardian - person who lived during the reign of Edward VII

Etymology

From Queen Victoria (1819–1901), who reigned over the United Kingdom from 1837 to 1901.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10772729-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ vɪkˈtɔriən / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
Having very strict moral standards or attitudes, especially regarding sex and social behavior; exaggeratedly proper or prudish.
exaggeratedly proper or prudish

Examples

His Victorian views on marriage seem outdated in today's society.
She grew up in a Victorian household where even dancing was discouraged.
The school's Victorian rules prohibit any displays of affection.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straitlaced - having very strict moral standards
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing great freedom of behavior

Etymology

From the reign of Queen Victoria (1837–1901), noted for its strict social codes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01886245-s
Synset POS: s
