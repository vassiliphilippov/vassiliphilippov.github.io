Max Muller

1. PROPN

/ mæks ˈmʊlər / (AmE), / mæks ˈmʊlə / (BrE)
(1823–1900) A British philologist and Orientalist, born in Germany, renowned for his pioneering work in the study of Sanskrit and comparative religion.
British philologist and Sanskrit scholar

Examples

Max Muller contributed significantly to the Western understanding of Eastern religions through his translations and commentaries.
As a professor at Oxford, Max Muller advanced the academic study of language and myth.
Many modern scholars of Sanskrit cite Max Muller as a foundational figure in their field.
