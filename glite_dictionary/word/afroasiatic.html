Afroasiatic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊˌeɪʒiˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊˌeɪʃɪˈatɪk / (BrE)
relating to a large family of languages spoken in parts of Africa and Western Asia, including Arabic, Hebrew, Amharic, and Hausa
family of related languages in Africa and Asia

Examples

The Afroasiatic language family includes Arabic, Hebrew, and Somali among many others.
Scholars have studied the origins of Afroasiatic languages for decades.
Hausa is classified as an Afroasiatic language widely spoken in West Africa.

Etymology

Blend of 'Africa' and 'Asia' plus '-atic', indicating connection to both continents.
