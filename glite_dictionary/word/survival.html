survival

1. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
the process by which organisms that are best adapted to their environment continue to live and reproduce, often used in the context of evolution
continuing to exist by adaptation

Examples

The theory of evolution explains why survival depends on adaptation.
Natural survival favors the strongest and most flexible species.
Her research focuses on the survival of endangered animals in changing habitats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extinction - the process in which a species ceases to exist

Etymology

From French 'survivre' (to survive), from Latin 'supervivere' (to live beyond).

2. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪ.vəl / (AmE), / səˈvaɪ.vəl / (BrE)
the state or fact of continuing to live or exist, especially in spite of difficult conditions
continuing to live or exist

Examples

Her courage and determination were crucial to her survival in the wilderness.
The doctors were amazed by the patient's survival after such a serious accident.
Access to clean water is essential for the survival of every community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: death - the end of life; the cessation of existence
  • Synonym: existence - the state of being or existing

Etymology

From French 'survivre' (to survive), from Latin 'supervivere' (to live beyond).

3. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
A person, thing, or practice that continues to exist from an earlier time.
something that continues to exist

Examples

This custom is a survival from pagan times.
Fossils are often described as survivals of ancient species.
Many idioms in language are survivals of old traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a part or quantity that is left after the greater part has been used, removed, or destroyed

Etymology

From survive + -al, based on the earlier verb survive.
