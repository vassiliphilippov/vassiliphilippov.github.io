myeloid

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪ.əˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ɪ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or resembling bone marrow; involving cells derived from bone marrow, especially in medical contexts.
relating to bone marrow
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a myeloid lineage in the patient's blood sample.
Certain types of leukemia affect myeloid cells rather than lymphoid cells.
A biopsy can determine if the abnormal growth is of myeloid origin.

Etymology

From Greek myelos (marrow) + -oid (like, resembling).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəlɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or derived from bone marrow or bone marrow cells, especially in reference to certain blood cells or diseases.
relating to bone marrow or its cells
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient had a myeloid form of leukemia.
Certain myeloid cells play a key role in the immune system.
Researchers study myeloid differentiation to understand blood cell development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lymphoid - pertaining to lymph or lymphatic tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'myelos' (marrow) and '-oid' (like, of the nature of).
