misogamy

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈsɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / mɪˈsɒɡəmi / (BrE)
Hatred or strong dislike of marriage or the institution of marriage.
hatred or dislike of marriage

Examples

His outspoken misogamy made him a controversial figure among his married friends.
The novel explores a character's deep-rooted misogamy and its effect on relationships.
She argued that misogamy is often misunderstood as simple disinterest in romance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misogamist - a person who hates or avoids marriage
  • Degree Lower: misogyny - hatred of women

Etymology

From Greek 'misos' (hatred) + 'gamos' (marriage).
