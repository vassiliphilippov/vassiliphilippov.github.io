classically

1. ADV

/ ˈklæsɪkli / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkli / (BrE)
In the style or manner characteristic of ancient Greek and Roman culture.
in the style of ancient Greece and Rome

Examples

The building is classically designed, featuring columns and marble facades.
She posed for a portrait classically draped, reminiscent of ancient statues.
This sculpture is classically inspired, echoing the art of ancient Rome.

Synset Examples

this exercise develops a classically shaped body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditionally - in a traditional manner

Etymology

From 'classical', ultimately from Latin 'classicus', meaning 'of the highest class'.
