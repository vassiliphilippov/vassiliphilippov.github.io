to ancylose

1. VERB

/ ˈæn.sɪ.loʊz / (AmE), / ˈæn.sɪ.ləʊz / (BrE)
to surgically produce ankylosis (the stiffening or immobility of a joint by fusion of the bones)
produce ankylosis surgically
medical

Examples

The doctor opted to ancylose the joint to relieve the patient's chronic pain.
If natural fusion fails, surgeons may decide to ancylose the affected area.
Advances in orthopedic surgery have made it safer to ancylose problem joints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankylosis - the stiffening or immobility of a joint due to fusion of bones

Etymology

From French 'ancyloser', from Greek 'ankylos' (crooked, stiffened).

2. VERB

/ ˈæŋkɪˌloʊz / (AmE), / ˈaŋkɪləʊz / (BrE)
(of a joint) to become stiff or fused due to ankylosis.
to become stiff or fused (of a joint)
medical

Examples

If untreated, the patient's knee may ancylose over time.
Chronic inflammation caused his fingers to ancylose, making movement difficult.
Doctors warned that immobilizing the joint could cause it to ancylose.

Synset Examples

joints ankylose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankylosis - stiffening or fusion of a joint

Etymology

From French 'ancyloser', from Greek ankylos (bent, crooked). Related to 'ankylosis'.
