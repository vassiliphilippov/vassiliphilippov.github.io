sectarian

1. NOUN

/ sɛkˈtɛriən / (AmE), / sɛkˈteəriən / (BrE)
A member of a religious or political sect, especially one who is strongly devoted to its beliefs.
member of a sect

Examples

The group was composed mainly of sectarians who refused to accept mainstream doctrine.
Many sectarians gathered at the rally to promote their beliefs.
Historically, sectarian leaders often engaged in disputes with other religious groups.

Synset Examples

most sectarians are intolerant of the views of any other sect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partisan - a committed member of a party, faction, or cause
  • Antonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs at odds with the established religious doctrine

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sectarius', from 'secta' (sect).

2. ADJ

/ sɛkˈtɛriən / (AmE), / sɛkˈtɛːrɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a sect, especially a religious or political group with particular beliefs that distinguish it from others.
relating to a sect or sects

Examples

The country has experienced decades of sectarian conflict between rival groups.
Laws were introduced to reduce sectarian discrimination in education and employment.
He was criticized for making sectarian remarks during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

sectarian differences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: religious - relating to religion
  • Antonym: nonsectarian - not associated with any sect or group

Etymology

From sect + -arian, first attested in the early 19th century.
