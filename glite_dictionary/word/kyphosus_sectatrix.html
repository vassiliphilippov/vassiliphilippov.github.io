Kyphosus sectatrix

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈfoʊsəs sɛkˈtreɪtɪks / (AmE), / kaɪˈfəʊsəs sɛkˈtrætrɪks / (BrE)
a species of food and game fish found around Bermuda and Florida, often known to follow ships
a food and game fish species near Bermuda and Florida

Examples

The Kyphosus sectatrix often swims near boats in tropical Atlantic waters.
Anglers prize the Kyphosus sectatrix for its fighting spirit and good taste.
Marine biologists observed a large school of Kyphosus sectatrix following the ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudderfish - another name for Kyphosus sectatrix

Etymology

From Latin; genus Kyphosus (meaning 'hump-backed') and species sectatrix.
