Leuwenhoek

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːvənˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪvənˌhʊk / (BrE)
Antonie van Leuwenhoek (1632–1723), Dutch pioneer microscopist known for first describing microbes, spermatozoa, and blood corpuscles.
Dutch pioneer microscopist, first described microbes

Examples

The discoveries of Leuwenhoek revolutionized the study of biology and microscopy.
Researchers still marvel at the simple microscopes crafted by Leuwenhoek in the seventeenth century.
Many textbooks credit Leuwenhoek as the father of microbiology.

Etymology

From the Dutch surname 'van Leeuwenhoek'.
