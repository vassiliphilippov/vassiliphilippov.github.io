Schizanthus

1. NOUN

/ skɪˈzænθəs / (AmE), / skɪˈzanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of Chilean herbs in the nightshade family, known for their colorful, orchid-like flowers.
genus of Chilean herbs with showy flowers

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Schizanthus for its brilliant and exotic-looking blooms.
The Schizanthus genus is native to Chile but popular in ornamental gardens worldwide.
Some species of Schizanthus are commonly called 'poor man's orchid' due to their striking resemblance to orchids.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (to split) + 'anthos' (flower), referring to the flower's divided appearance.
