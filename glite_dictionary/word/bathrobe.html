bathrobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθˌroʊb / (AmE), / ˈbɑːθˌrəʊb / (BrE)
A loose-fitting robe, usually made of absorbent material, worn after bathing or swimming.
robe worn after a bath or swim

Examples

She wrapped herself in a fluffy bathrobe after her shower.
He came to breakfast still wearing his bathrobe.
Please hang your bathrobe on the hook when you're finished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing gown - a long, loose robe worn in the house, especially in the morning or evening

Etymology

Formed from 'bath' and 'robe', indicating its use after bathing.
