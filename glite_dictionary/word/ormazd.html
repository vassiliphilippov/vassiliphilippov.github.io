Ormazd

1. PROPN

/ ɔrˈmɑzd / (AmE), / ɔːˈmɑːzd / (BrE)
the supreme deity of Zoroastrianism, regarded as the source of all light and goodness
supreme god of Zoroastrianism

Examples

Many ancient texts praise Ormazd as the creator of the world and the source of all goodness.
Zoroastrians pray to Ormazd for guidance and protection against evil forces.
The struggle between Ormazd and Ahriman is central to Zoroastrian religious beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ahura Mazda - supreme god of Zoroastrianism, another name for Ormazd
  • Antonym: Ahriman - evil spirit and adversary of Ormazd in Zoroastrianism

Etymology

From Middle Persian 'Ohrmazd', from Avestan 'Ahura Mazda' (Wise Lord).
