Panicum virgatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪkəm vɝːˈɡætəm / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkəm vɜːˈɡætəm / (BrE)
A tall grass native to North America, commonly used for hay and as a bioenergy crop; also known as switchgrass.
tall North American grass; switchgrass

Examples

Panicum virgatum is widely cultivated for its use as forage and in soil conservation projects.
Many biofuel researchers are studying Panicum virgatum as a renewable energy source.
Farmers often grow Panicum virgatum in areas prone to erosion due to its deep root system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switchgrass - common name for Panicum virgatum, a tall North American grass

Etymology

From Latin: Genus name 'Panicum' (a type of grass) and species name 'virgatum' meaning 'twiggy' or 'having twigs'.
