to apperceive

1. VERB

/ ˌæp.ɚˈsiːv / (AmE), / ˌæp.əˈsiːv / (BrE)
To perceive something while relating it to previous experiences or understanding.
perceive with reference to past experience

Examples

Through repeated encounters, people tend to apperceive new situations in familiar ways.
Children often apperceive unfamiliar events based on their own personal history.
He tried to apperceive the novel experience by drawing parallels to his past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perceive - to become aware of something through the senses or intellect

Etymology

From Latin ad- (to) + percipere (to perceive); influenced by German 'apperzipieren'.
