James Bowie

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈbuːi / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈbəʊi / (BrE)
(1796–1836) An American frontiersman and soldier, known for his role in the Texas Revolution and as a commander at the Alamo.
American frontiersman, Alamo commander

Examples

James Bowie became famous for his bravery during the Battle of the Alamo.
Many historians regard James Bowie as a symbol of Texas independence.
A popular type of hunting knife is named after James Bowie.
