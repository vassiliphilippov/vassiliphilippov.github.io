Acanthus

1. NOUN

/ əˈkænθəs / (AmE), / əˈkanθəs / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants with spiny leaves and tall flower spikes, commonly known as bear's breeches, native to the Mediterranean region
a plant genus known as bear's breeches

Examples

The large, decorative leaves of the Acanthus are often depicted in classical architecture.
Several gardeners plant Acanthus for its striking flower spikes and bold foliage.
We found wild Acanthus growing along the rocky Mediterranean hillsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear's breeches - common name for plants of the genus Acanthus

Etymology

From Greek akanthos (thorny plant), referring to the plant's spiny leaves.
