incorporated

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔːrpəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːpəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
formed or united into a single whole, especially as a legally recognized organization or entity
formed into a single whole; united

Examples

The company became incorporated in 2010, allowing it to expand internationally.
Several villages were incorporated into a single municipality to improve governance.
The amendments were incorporated into the official document after much debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: disjointed - lacking a coherent or united form

Etymology

From Latin 'incorporatus', past participle of 'incorporare' (to embody, make into a body).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔːrpəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːpəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a company, town, or organization) formed into a legal corporation with official status and rights under the law.
formed as a legal corporation
legal

Examples

The business became incorporated in 2001, giving it more legal protection.
Every incorporated city must adhere to state regulations regarding governance.
Their firm is now an incorporated entity, separate from its individual owners.

Synset Examples

a special agency set up in corporate form
an incorporated town

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unincorporated - not formed into a legal corporation

Etymology

From Latin 'incorporare' (to embody), via late Middle English and legal usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01674975-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔrpəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːpəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
included in or made part of something larger; introduced as a component or element of a whole
included as part of a whole

Examples

Many of the suggestions from employees were incorporated into the final policy.
The new data was quickly incorporated into the report for accuracy.
Her unique style became incorporated in the overall design of the building.

Synset Examples

the ideas incorporated in his revised manuscript

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated - combined into a whole; unified
  • Antonym: excluded - left out; not included as part of a whole

Etymology

From Latin incorporatus, past participle of incorporare, meaning 'to form into a body'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331556-s
Synset POS: s
