attended

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
(of a performance) accompanied by instrumental or vocal music; with musical accompaniment.
with musical accompaniment

Examples

The song was beautifully attended by a string quartet.
Her solo was attended with soft piano music in the background.
The ceremony featured readings attended by a live choir.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaccompanied - not having musical accompaniment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02259797-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈtɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or person) having a caretaker, aide, or watcher present to provide supervision or service.
having a caretaker or watcher present

Examples

The facility is always attended by a nurse for the safety of the residents.
You must park your car in the attended lot during events.
Children should not be left attended by inexperienced supervisors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unattended - without anyone present to supervise or watch over

Etymology

From the past participle of 'attend', from Middle English 'attenden', from Anglo-Norman 'atendre'.
