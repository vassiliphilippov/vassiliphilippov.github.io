Ceratostomataceae

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætəˌstoʊməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛrətəˌstəʊməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by carbonous perithecia with long necks
a family of fungi

Examples

The Ceratostomataceae are studied for their unique reproductive structures in mycology.
Members of the Ceratostomataceae can be identified by the presence of long-necked perithecia.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Ceratostomataceae family in the rainforest.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Ceratostoma + the standard taxonomic suffix -aceae.
