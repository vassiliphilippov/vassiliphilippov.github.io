armed

1. ADJ

/ ɑrmd / (AmE), / ɑːmd / (BrE)
(of plants or animals) having bristles, thorns, or other defensive structures.
having bristles or thorns (biology)

Examples

The cactus is armed with sharp spines to protect itself from herbivores.
Many insects are armed with bristles that deter predators.
Some tropical plants are heavily armed to prevent animals from eating their leaves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unarmed - lacking bristles, thorns, or other natural defenses

Etymology

From Latin 'armare' (to arm), meaning equipped or provided with arms or implements.

/ ɑrmd / (AmE), / ɑːmd / (BrE)
having one or more arms or limbs, often used in combination to specify the number or character of the arms
having arms or limbs (often in combination)

Examples

The artist painted a mythical eight-armed creature in vibrant colors.

3. ADJ

/ ɑrmd / (AmE), / ɑːmd / (BrE)
equipped with or carrying weapons; having arms for attack or defense
carrying weapons

Examples

The armed guards patrolled the building throughout the night.
Many countries have both armed and unarmed police officers.
During the protest, several individuals were reportedly armed with sticks and bats.

Synset Examples

armed robbery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unarmed - not carrying weapons

Etymology

From Old French 'armer' (to arm), from Latin 'armare' (to arm).
