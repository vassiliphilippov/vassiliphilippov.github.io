habitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the natural home or environment where an animal or plant lives
natural home of an animal or plant

Examples

The polar bear's habitation is threatened by the melting Arctic ice.
Researchers studied the habitation of rare orchids in the mountain forests.
Changes to the river have affected the fish's natural habitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitat - the natural environment of an organism

Etymology

From Latin 'habitatio', from 'habitare' (to inhabit).

/ ˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of living in a particular place or occupying it as a home
the act of living in a place

Examples

The cave showed signs of ancient human habitation.
Beavers' lodges are a clear evidence of animal habitation along the riverbanks.
