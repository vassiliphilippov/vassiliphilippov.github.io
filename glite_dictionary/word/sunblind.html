sunblind

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
a screen or canopy, typically made of fabric or other material, used to protect people or things from direct sunlight
a screen or canopy for sun protection

Examples

The large sunblind on the patio made outdoor dining much more comfortable.
She lowered the sunblind to keep the room cool during the afternoon heat.
Most cars in the region are equipped with a sunblind to block harsh sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awning - a sheet of canvas or other material extending over a doorway, window, or deck, to provide shelter

Etymology

From 'sun' + 'blind', referring to a device that blocks sunlight.
