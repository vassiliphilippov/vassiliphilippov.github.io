embroilment

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbrɔɪlmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪlmənt / (BrE)
a complicated and confusing situation, often involving conflict or entanglement, especially in interpersonal or political affairs
a complicated, confusing situation

Examples

The sudden resignation led to a political embroilment that lasted several months.
Their friendship ended in an embroilment over money and trust issues.
The region has a history of embroilment in territorial disputes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entanglement - the state of being involved in difficulties or complicated circumstances

Etymology

From French 'embrouiller' (to tangle), from 'en-' (in) + 'brouiller' (to jumble).
