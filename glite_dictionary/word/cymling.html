cymling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmlɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of squash plant that produces flattened, round fruit with a scalloped edge, usually greenish white in color.
type of scalloped squash

Examples

She harvested several ripe cymlings from her garden in late summer.
The chef roasted the cymling and served it as a side dish.
Many gardeners enjoy growing cymlings for their unique shape and delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pattypan squash - a variety of summer squash with a similar shape and appearance

Etymology

From obsolete French *cymeline*, diminutive of *cyme* (a squash).
