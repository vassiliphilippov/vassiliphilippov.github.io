Ostrea gigas

1. NOUN

/ ɑsˈtriːə ˈɡaɪɡəs / (AmE), / ɒsˈtriːə ˈɡɪɡəs / (BrE)
a large oyster species native to Japan and introduced to other regions, including the Pacific coast of the United States
a species of large oyster

Examples

The Ostrea gigas has become widely cultivated in aquaculture due to its rapid growth.
Scientists are studying the impact of Ostrea gigas on local ecosystems in the Pacific Northwest.
Many seafood restaurants now serve Ostrea gigas as a delicacy, often labeling it as 'Pacific oyster.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific oyster - common name for Ostrea gigas, also known as the Japanese oyster

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Ostrea* (oyster) and species name *gigas* (giant).
