indubitably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈd(j)uːbɪtəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːbɪtəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be doubted; unquestionably.
without doubt; unquestionably

Examples

It is indubitably true that exercise improves mental health.
The actor's performance was indubitably impressive, drawing praise from all critics.
Her honesty was indubitably clear to everyone who met her.

Synset Examples

it was immediately and indubitably apparent that I had interrupted a scene of lovers
his guilt was established beyond a shadow of a doubt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undoubtedly - without doubt; certainly
  • Antonym: doubtfully - in a manner that can be doubted; uncertainly

Etymology

From Latin indubitabilis (not to be doubted), from in- (not) + dubitabilis (doubtful).
