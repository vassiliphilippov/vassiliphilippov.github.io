hussy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌsi / (AmE), / ˈhʌsi / (BrE)
A woman who is considered sexually promiscuous or flirtatious, often used as an insult or in a disparaging way.
sexually promiscuous woman (disparaging)

Examples

The townspeople gossiped about the new girl, calling her a hussy behind her back.
She was unfairly labeled a hussy just because she enjoyed flirting with men.
In old novels, a woman with too much independence was often dismissed as a hussy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slut - a woman regarded as sexually promiscuous
  • Antonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity

Etymology

From earlier English 'houswife' (housewife), later used disparagingly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌsi / (AmE), / ˈhʌsi / (BrE)
an insulting term for a woman who is considered to be immoral, especially one who has many sexual partners or is an adulterer
insult for an immoral or adulterous woman
offensive archaic

Examples

The townspeople gossiped about the new neighbor, calling her a hussy behind her back.
He accused her of being a hussy after learning about her affair.
The novel describes the protagonist as a misunderstood hussy shunned by polite society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slut - a woman who has many casual sexual partners (offensive slang)
  • Synonym: adulteress - a woman who commits adultery

Etymology

From Middle English 'houswif' (housewife), later used as a term of contempt for women.
