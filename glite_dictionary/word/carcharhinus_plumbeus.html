Carcharhinus plumbeus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrkəˈraɪnəs ˈplʌmbiəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːkəˈraɪnəs ˈplʌmbiːəs / (BrE)
A species of large, gray shark commonly found along the coasts of the middle Atlantic states of the US, known as the sandbar shark.
a species of gray shark (sandbar shark)

Examples

The Carcharhinus plumbeus is frequently spotted in shallow coastal waters during summer months.
Fishermen sometimes encounter Carcharhinus plumbeus while casting off the Atlantic seaboard.
Biologists tagged several Carcharhinus plumbeus to study their migratory patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandbar shark - Common name for Carcharhinus plumbeus

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Carcharhinus' (from Greek karcharos ‘sharp’ + rhinos ‘nose’); species name 'plumbeus' is Latin for ‘lead-colored’.
