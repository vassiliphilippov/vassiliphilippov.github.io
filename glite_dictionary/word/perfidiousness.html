perfidiousness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɪdiəsnəs / (AmE), / pəˈfɪdiəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being disloyal or treacherous; betrayal of trust or faith.
betrayal; treacherousness

Examples

The perfidiousness of the advisor shocked the entire royal court.
He could not forgive her perfidiousness after she revealed his secret to his enemies.
History is replete with tales of political perfidiousness that changed the fate of nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treachery - betrayal of trust; treason
  • Antonym: loyalty - faithfulness or allegiance to a person or cause

Etymology

From Latin perfidia (treachery) + -ous + -ness.
