any

1. DET

/ ˈɛni / (AmE), / ˈɛni / (BrE)
used to refer to one, some, or every member of a group of people or things, without specificity.
referring to one, some, or all, not specific

Examples

You may borrow any book you like from the shelf.
Is there any coffee left in the pot?
If any problems arise, let me know immediately.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: some - referring to an unspecified amount or number of things or people
  • Antonym: every - referring to all the members of a group considered individually

Etymology

From Old English 'ænig', meaning 'any, anyone'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛni / (AmE), / ˈɛni / (BrE)
(used with comparatives or negatives) to any degree or extent.
to any degree or extent (adv.)

Examples

The situation isn't any better than it was yesterday.
She doesn't feel any happier despite the news.
His response wasn't any different from before.

Synset Examples

it isn't any better

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at all - in any way or of any kind

Etymology

From Old English 'ænig' (any, anyone), from 'ān' (one).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024868-r
Synset POS: r
