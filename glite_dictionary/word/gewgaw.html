gewgaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡyuːˌɡɔ / (AmE), / ˈɡjuːˌɡɔː / (BrE)
a small, cheap, showy ornament or piece of jewelry, often considered gaudy or of little value
cheap, showy ornament or jewelry

Examples

She decorated her desk with every kind of gewgaw she could find at the market.
The necklace looked pretty, but it was just another gewgaw bought from a tourist shop.
His jacket was covered in gewgaws and flashy pins, attracting lots of attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament or item of jewelry that is of little value
  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration

Etymology

Imitative, probably from earlier forms like 'gewgaw' (Middle English 'gew-gaw').
