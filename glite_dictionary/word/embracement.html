embracement

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbreɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbreɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of clasping someone in the arms, typically as a greeting or to show affection
the act of embracing or hugging

Examples

Their tearful embracement at the airport touched everyone watching.
The two old friends greeted each other with a warm embracement.
In times of sorrow, a simple embracement can offer great comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - the act of holding someone closely in one's arms

Etymology

From Old French *embracer*, from Latin *bracchium* (arm), with English noun-forming suffix -ment.
