palaver

1. VERB

/ pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
To talk or have a long discussion, especially one that is elaborate or intended to persuade.
talk at length, especially to persuade

Examples

The two leaders palavered for hours before reaching a compromise.
They palavered endlessly about the best approach to the project.
We palavered with the locals to gain their trust and understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly, informal way
  • Synonym: debate - to discuss opposing viewpoints
  • Antonym: to be quiet - to not speak

Etymology

From Portuguese 'palavra' (word, speech), from Latin 'parabola' (comparison, parable).

WordNet ID: oewn-00765305-v

2. VERB

/ pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and continuously about unimportant matters, often in a way that is seen as pointless or idle
talk at length about trivial matters

Examples

They spent hours palavering about nothing in particular during the meeting.
She tends to palaver when she's nervous, filling the silence with pointless chatter.
As the sun set, the villagers continued palavering by the fireside, ignoring the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly about trivial things
  • Synonym: prattle - talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

From Portuguese 'palavra' (word, speech), ultimately from Latin 'parabola' (comparison, speech).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038676-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
loud, confused, or elaborate talk that has little real meaning or value; empty chatter or fuss
empty or pointless talk

Examples

The meeting was full of palaver that didn't achieve anything productive.
All we got from the spokesperson was a lot of palaver and no real answers.
Instead of action, the debate consisted mainly of political palaver.

Synset Examples

mere rhetoric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetoric - language intended to persuade or impress, often regarded as lacking in sincerity or meaningful content
  • Synonym: babble - foolish or meaningless talk
  • Antonym: sense - sound practical judgment; logical meaning

Etymology

From Portuguese 'palavra' (word, speech), from Latin 'parabola' (speech, comparison).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
flattering or insincere talk intended to persuade or influence someone
flattery meant to persuade

Examples

The salesman tried to win her trust with charming palaver about how wonderful the product was.
I'm not fooled by all your palaver; just tell me the truth.
His endless palaver grew tiring, as everyone could see through his insincere compliments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive praise intended to influence someone
  • Synonym: cajolery - persuasion by flattery or gentle urging
  • Antonym: sincerity - honesty and genuineness in expression

Etymology

From Portuguese 'palavra' (word, speech), from Latin 'parabola' (speech, discourse).

5. VERB

/ pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
to influence or persuade someone by gentle urging, caressing, or flattering; to cajole or coax
persuade by gentle urging or flattery

Examples

He managed to palaver his co-workers into staying late to help him finish the project.
She palavered her parents with compliments until they agreed to let her go to the concert.
They spent hours palavering the stubborn client, hoping he would accept their proposal.

Synset Examples

He palavered her into going along

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cajole - to persuade by flattery or gentle pleading
  • Synonym: coax - to persuade gently, often using flattery

Etymology

From Portuguese *palavra* (word, talk), from Latin *parabola* (speech, discourse).
