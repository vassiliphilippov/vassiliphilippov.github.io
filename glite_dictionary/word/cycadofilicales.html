Cycadofilicales

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˌdoʊˌfɪlɪˈkeɪlz / (AmE), / ˌsɪkədəʊˌfɪlɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of fossil gymnospermous trees or climbing plants of the Devonian period, commonly known as seed ferns.
extinct order of fossil seed ferns

Examples

The discovery of Cycadofilicales fossils provided insight into the evolution of seed plants.
Paleobotanists studied Cycadofilicales to understand their relationship to modern ferns and cycads.
During the Devonian period, Cycadofilicales were prominent in the Earth's prehistoric forests.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'kykas' (cycad) and 'phullon' (leaf), referencing their foliage resemblance to cycads.
