majagua

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɑːɡwə / (AmE), / məˈhɑːɡwə / (BrE)
An erect forest tree of Cuba and Jamaica, known for its hairy leaves, orange-yellow or orange-red flowers, and moderately dense timber used in cabinetwork and gunstocks.
Caribbean tree valued for its timber

Examples

The sturdy wood of the majagua is often used for crafting fine furniture in Cuba.
Bright orange blossoms covered the majagua lining the narrow Jamaican path.
Carpenters prize the timber of majagua for making durable gunstocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - genus of tropical and subtropical plants with large flowers, related to majagua

Etymology

From Spanish 'majagua,' likely of Taíno origin, applied in various regions to hibiscus trees.

2. NOUN

/ məˈhɑːɡwə / (AmE), / məˈhɑːɡwə / (BrE)
A shrubby tropical tree, often found along coasts, valued for its light, tough wood and fibrous bark, used in making cordage and caulking, and also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
tropical shrubby tree used for wood and fiber

Examples

The craftsmen used majagua wood to build sturdy canoe outriggers.
Baskets woven from majagua fibers are both strong and flexible.
In many coastal gardens, the majagua tree is planted for its lush foliage and yellow flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hibiscus tiliaceus - scientific name for majagua tree

Etymology

From Spanish 'majagua', originally from the Taíno language.
