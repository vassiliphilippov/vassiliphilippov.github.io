gritstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪtˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪt.stəʊn / (BrE)
a hard, coarse-grained sandstone consisting mainly of quartz or silica grains, commonly found in geological formations
a type of coarse-grained sandstone

Examples

The Peak District is famous for its towering gritstone edges and cliffs.
Geologists collected samples of gritstone from the northern hillside for analysis.
The old cottage was built entirely from local gritstone blocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock composed mainly of sand-sized mineral particles

Etymology

From 'grit' (coarse sand) + 'stone', referring to the rock's texture.
