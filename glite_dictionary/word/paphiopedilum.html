Paphiopedilum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæfi.oʊˈpɛdɪləm / (AmE), / ˌpæfi.əˈpɛdɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of mainly terrestrial orchids native to southeastern Asia and the Pacific, valued for their showy, slipper-shaped flowers
genus of Asian slipper orchids

Examples

The Paphiopedilum thrives in humid, shaded environments similar to its native rainforest habitat.
Orchid enthusiasts often collect rare Paphiopedilum hybrids for their unique slipper-shaped blooms.
We attended an exhibit featuring several award-winning Paphiopedilum specimens from Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypripedium - a genus of North American and Eurasian slipper orchids

Etymology

From Greek 'Paphos' (a city in Cyprus, sacred to Aphrodite) and 'pedilon' (sandal), referring to the slipper-like flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæfiˈoʊpɪdɪləm / (AmE), / ˌpæfiəʊˈpɪdɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of mainly terrestrial orchids, native to southeastern Asia and the Pacific, noted for their distinctive slipper-shaped flowers and frequently cultivated as ornamentals.
genus of slipper orchids

Examples

Many orchid enthusiasts specialize in growing Paphiopedilum species for their unique floral structures.
The Paphiopedilum genus includes both naturally occurring and hybrid orchids popular among collectors.
During the exhibition, several rare Paphiopedilum varieties attracted attention with their slipper-shaped blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypripedium - another genus of slipper orchids sometimes including Paphiopedilum

Etymology

From Greek Paphos (a city in Cyprus, sacred to Aphrodite) and Greek pedilon (slipper), referring to the flower's shape.
