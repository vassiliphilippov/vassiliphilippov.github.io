engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
The branch of science and technology concerned with designing, building, and using engines, machines, and structures by applying scientific and mathematical knowledge to practical problems.
the application of science to practical problems

Examples

She decided to major in engineering because she loved solving technical problems.
Modern engineering has revolutionized transportation and communication.
There are many branches of engineering, including civil, mechanical, and electrical.

Synset Examples

he had trouble deciding which branch of engineering to study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technology - the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'ingeniāria' (contrivance, clever invention), from Latin 'ingenium' (cleverness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the practical application of scientific and mathematical knowledge to design, build, and maintain structures, machines, and systems
application of science to design and construction

Examples

She decided to study engineering at university because she loved solving technical problems.
The new bridge is an impressive feat of engineering that took years to complete.
Advances in engineering have made modern transportation faster and safer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technology - the practical use of scientific knowledge, especially in industry
  • Degree Lower: science - systematic study of the structure and behaviour of the physical and natural world

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'ingeniaria', meaning 'contrivance' or 'devising', from 'ingenium' (inborn talent, cleverness).
