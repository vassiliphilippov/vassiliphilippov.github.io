heartily

1. ADV

/ ˈhɑrt̬ɪli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtɪli / (BrE)
In a cheerful, sincere, or enthusiastic manner; with enthusiasm or wholeheartedness.
in a cheerful or enthusiastic way

Examples

He laughed heartily at the joke, unable to stop his amusement.
The guests ate heartily after the long hike through the mountains.
She heartily agreed with the proposal and offered her help.

Synset Examples

‘Yes,’ the children chorused heartily
We welcomed her warmly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordially - in a warm and friendly manner
  • Synonym: warmly - in a friendly or enthusiastic manner

Etymology

From 'hearty' + '-ly', 'hearty' from Middle English 'herti', from 'heart'.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɑːr.t̬ə.li / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.tɪ.li / (BrE)
with great enthusiasm, energy, or sincerity; in a wholehearted way
with enthusiasm or wholeheartedness

Examples

She laughed heartily at the comedian's jokes, tears streaming down her face.
The crowd heartily applauded the performance after the final act.
He heartily agreed with the proposal and offered his full support.

Synset Examples

the boy threw himself heartily into his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastically - in a very energetic and eager way
  • Antonym: halfheartedly - without much enthusiasm or energy

Etymology

From Middle English 'hertely', from Old English 'heortlīċ' (cordial, sincere), from 'heorte' (heart).
