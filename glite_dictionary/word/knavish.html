knavish

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪvɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈneɪvɪʃ / (BrE)
skilled at deception or dishonesty; cunning or tricky
deceitful, cunning, or tricky

Examples

The knavish merchant tricked his customers with false promises.
Her knavish smile made it clear she was up to something clever.
In the story, a knavish fox outwitted all the other animals.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skilled at achieving one's aims by deceit
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English 'knave' (dishonest man) + '-ish' (having the qualities of).
