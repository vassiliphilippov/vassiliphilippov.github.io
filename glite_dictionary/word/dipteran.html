dipteran

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪptərən / (AmE), / ˈdɪptərən / (BrE)
an insect of the order Diptera, typically having a single pair of functional wings and specialized mouthparts for sucking, lapping, or piercing
insect of the Diptera order

Examples

Scientists collected several dipteran specimens from the marsh for further study.
Mosquitoes and houseflies are well-known examples of dipteran insects.
The anatomy of a dipteran is adapted for rapid flight and diverse feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - common name for insects of the order Diptera

Etymology

From Greek 'dipteros' meaning 'two-winged'.
