suttee

1. NOUN

/ sʌˈti / (AmE), / ˈsʌtiː / (BrE)
(historical) The former practice among some Hindus of a widow cremating herself on her husband's funeral pyre.
Hindu widow self-immolation (historical)
archaic

Examples

The British colonial authorities eventually outlawed suttee in the nineteenth century.
Many historical accounts describe the tragic ritual of suttee in India.
The practice of suttee has been widely condemned and is no longer legal.

Etymology

From Hindi 'satī', from Sanskrit 'sati', meaning 'faithful wife'.
