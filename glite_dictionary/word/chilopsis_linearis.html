Chilopsis linearis

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈlɑpsɪs laɪˈnɛərɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈlɒpsɪs lɪˈnɪərɪs / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with willow-like leaves, showy purple flowers, and long seed pods.
evergreen shrub/tree with purple flowers (desert willow)

Examples

The Chilopsis linearis thrives in arid landscapes where other trees might struggle.
Botanists admire Chilopsis linearis for its striking purple trumpet-shaped flowers each spring.
We planted a Chilopsis linearis in our yard to attract hummingbirds and add color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert willow - common name for Chilopsis linearis

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the flower's shape; 'linearis' is Latin for 'linear', describing the leaves.
