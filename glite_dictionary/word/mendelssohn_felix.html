Mendelssohn, Felix

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛn.dəl.sɔn ˈfiː.lɪks / (AmE), / ˈmɛndl̩sɔn ˈfiːlɪks / (BrE)
(1809–1847) German composer of the Romantic era, noted for orchestral, choral, and chamber works.
German Romantic composer (1809–1847)

Examples

The music of Mendelssohn, Felix is celebrated for its melodic elegance and emotional clarity.
Many choirs regularly perform the choral works of Mendelssohn, Felix.
Felix Mendelssohn was also a talented conductor and pianist.

Etymology

German surname, widely known by full name Felix Mendelssohn(-Bartholdy).
