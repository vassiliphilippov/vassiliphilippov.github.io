kok-saghyz

1. NOUN

/ koʊkˈsɑːgiz / (AmE), / kɒkˈsægiːz / (BrE)
A perennial dandelion (Taraxacum kok-saghyz), native to Kazakhstan, grown for its fleshy roots which are rich in rubber.
Kazakh dandelion with rubber-rich roots

Examples

During wartime, kok-saghyz was cultivated as an alternative source of natural rubber.
Scientists are studying kok-saghyz as a potential sustainable crop for rubber production.
The roots of kok-saghyz contain significant amounts of latex.

Etymology

From Kazakh 'көксағыз' (koksaghyz), meaning 'blue rubber'.
