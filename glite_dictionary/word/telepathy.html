telepathy

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛpəθi / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛpəθi / (BrE)
The supposed communication of thoughts or ideas from one person to another by means other than the known senses.
direct mind-to-mind communication

Examples

Many scientists remain skeptical about the existence of telepathy due to the lack of empirical evidence.
The twins claimed that their connection was so strong it sometimes felt like telepathy.
In science fiction stories, telepathy is often portrayed as a common superpower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clairvoyance - the supposed ability to perceive things beyond normal sensory contact
  • Synonym: mind reading - the ability to know what someone is thinking

Etymology

From Greek tele- (far) + -patheia (feeling, perception).
