Bartholomeu Diaz

1. PROPN

/ bɑrˌθoʊloʊˈmeɪ ˈdiːəz / (AmE), / bɑːˌθɒləˈmeɪ ˈdiːæz / (BrE)
(c. 1450–1500) A Portuguese explorer who was the first European to sail around the Cape of Good Hope in 1488, opening the sea route from Europe to Asia.
Portuguese explorer who rounded Cape of Good Hope

Examples

Bartholomeu Diaz was the first European to sail around the southern tip of Africa.
The voyage of Bartholomeu Diaz marked a turning point in maritime exploration.
Many historians credit Bartholomeu Diaz with helping to establish the sea route to Asia.
