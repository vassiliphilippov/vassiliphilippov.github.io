tiara

1. NOUN

/ tiˈɛr.ə / (AmE), / tiˈɑː.rə / (BrE)
A jeweled ornamental band worn on the front of a woman's hair, typically on formal occasions.
jeweled women's headdress for formal occasions

Examples

The bride wore a sparkling tiara that complemented her wedding gown beautifully.
At the gala, the actress stunned the crowd with a diamond-studded tiara.
Her daughter loves to play dress-up and always chooses the pink tiara first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - a circular ornamental headdress worn as a symbol of authority or rank
  • Synonym: diadem - a jeweled crown or headband worn as a symbol of sovereignty

Etymology

From Latin *tiara*, from Ancient Greek *τιάρα* (tiára), a royal headdress.
