outage

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount of a substance, such as liquid or gas, lost during storage or transportation
amount lost in storage or transit

Examples

The outage of crude oil during the long shipment was higher than expected.
Regular checks help minimize outage in the whiskey barrels during aging.
Companies measure the outage of a liquid to manage inventory accurately.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊ.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaʊ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
A period when a service, such as electricity or computer systems, is temporarily unavailable or stopped.
temporary interruption of service

Examples

The company apologized to customers for the unexpected outage that lasted several hours.
A severe storm caused a power outage across the whole city last night.
Due to maintenance, the website will experience a brief outage this weekend.

Synset Examples

there will be a network outage from 8 to 10 a.m.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackout - a period when all electrical power is lost
  • Synonym: interruption - a break in continuity or service

Etymology

From 'out' + '-age', modeled after words like shortage.
