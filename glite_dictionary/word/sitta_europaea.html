Sitta europaea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtə jʊˈroʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌsɪtə jʊəˈrəʊpiːə / (BrE)
a species of nuthatch, commonly known as the Eurasian nuthatch, found across Europe and parts of Asia
species of nuthatch bird

Examples

The Sitta europaea is easily recognized by its blue-grey upperparts and white face.
Birdwatchers in the UK often look for Sitta europaea in mature woodlands.
Unlike many other birds, Sitta europaea can climb down trees headfirst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuthatch - a small songbird of the family Sittidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name *Sitta* with the species epithet from Latin *europaea* (European).
