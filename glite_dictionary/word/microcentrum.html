Microcentrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈsɛn.trəm / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈsɛn.trəm / (BrE)
A genus of katydids characterized by their leaflike appearance, found mostly in the Americas.
a genus of leaflike katydids

Examples

The Microcentrum is easily recognized by its green, leaf-shaped wings.
Several species within the genus Microcentrum are distributed throughout North and South America.
Entomologists collected a specimen of Microcentrum for further study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katydid - insect related to the cricket, known for leaflike appearance and sound production

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'kentron' (spur, point).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈsɛntrəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəʊˈsɛntrəm / (BrE)
A genus of katydids known for their broad, leaf-like wings, commonly found in the Americas.
a genus of katydids

Examples

The Microcentrum are often mistaken for leaves due to their shape and coloration.
Scientists studied the mating calls of several Microcentrum species in the rainforest.
A brightly colored Microcentrum landed on the garden fence, blending in with the foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katydid - a type of insect belonging to the Tettigoniidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'kentron' (point, center).
