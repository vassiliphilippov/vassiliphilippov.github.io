apomict

1. NOUN

/ ˈæp.ə.mɪkt / (AmE), / ˈap.ə.mɪkt / (BrE)
A plant or organism that reproduces by apomixis, bypassing the typical sexual reproductive process.
plant reproducing by apomixis
medical

Examples

Some grasses are apomicts, allowing them to reproduce without pollination.
The botanist studied several apomicts to better understand their genetic uniformity.
Because it is an apomict, this plant produces seeds asexually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apomixis - reproduction without fertilization, especially in plants

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (from, away from) + 'miktos' (mixed).
