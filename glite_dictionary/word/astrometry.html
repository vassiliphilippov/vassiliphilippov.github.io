astrometry

1. NOUN

/ əˈstrɑːmɪtri / (AmE), / əˈstrɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the branch of astronomy concerned with measuring the positions and motions of celestial bodies
measurement of position and motion of celestial bodies

Examples

Modern observatories rely on astrometry to track the movement of distant stars and planets.
Advancements in astrometry have improved our understanding of the galaxy's structure.
Precision in astrometry is essential for accurate mapping of celestial coordinates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomy - the scientific study of celestial bodies, space, and the universe as a whole

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'metron' (measure).
