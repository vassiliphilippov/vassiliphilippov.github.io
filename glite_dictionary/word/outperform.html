to outperform

1. VERB

/ ˌaʊt.pɚˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌaʊt.pəˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to do better than someone or something; to achieve superior results compared to others
perform better than others

Examples

This new phone consistently outperforms its competitors in battery life.
Despite his young age, the swimmer outperformed all the other finalists at the event.
Our sales team is on track to outperform last year's record-breaking results.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to do or be better than; to exceed
  • Synonym: to excel - to be exceptionally good at or proficient in an activity or subject
  • Antonym: to underperform - to perform less well than expected or less well than others

Etymology

From prefix 'out-' (beyond) + 'perform'.
