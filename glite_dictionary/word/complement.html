complement

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmpləmənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
a group of enzymes in blood serum that work together as part of the immune response by enhancing the body's ability to fight infection
a group of immune-response enzymes in serum
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a low complement level could indicate an immune system problem.
The complement system helps defend the body against bacteria and viruses.
Blood tests measured the patient's complement levels to assess her immune function.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmpləmənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
a thing that completes or brings to perfection; something that enhances or improves another thing when added to it
something that completes or enhances

Examples

The crisp salad made a delightful complement to the rich main course.
Her red shoes provided a perfect complement to her elegant dress.
A good sauce can serve as a complement to any meat dish.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplement - something added to make up for a deficiency or to enhance

Etymology
