slimness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractively thin or slender
quality of being attractively thin

Examples

Her slimness was accentuated by the elegant dress she wore for the party.
The model was admired for her natural slimness and graceful posture.
Despite her slimness, she was surprisingly strong and athletic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinness - the state of being thin
  • Antonym: plumpness - the state of being plump or slightly overweight
  • Antonym: fatness - the state of being fat

Etymology

From 'slim' (of Dutch origin, meaning 'slender, thin') + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the condition of being only just sufficient, or involving a very small difference or margin, especially in competitions or elections
small margin; slight difference

Examples

The slimness of the victory left the candidates anxious until the very last vote was counted.
She was surprised by the slimness of the difference between the two teams' final scores.
Despite the slimness of the majority, the law was passed in parliament.

Synset Examples

the president was not humbled by his narrow margin of victory
the landslide he had in the electoral college obscured the narrowness of a victory based on just 43% of the popular vote

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrowness - the state of being limited in width or extent; also, small margin
  • Antonym: breadth - wide extent or margin

Etymology

From 'slim' (narrow, slight) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).
