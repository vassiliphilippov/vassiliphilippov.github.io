neatness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnitnəs / (AmE), / ˈniːtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being tidy, orderly, and clean in appearance or arrangement
quality of being tidy and orderly

Examples

Her neatness impressed everyone in the office.
A sense of neatness made his room a pleasant place to work.
He was praised for the neatness of his handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: messiness - the quality or state of being messy or untidy

Etymology

From neat + -ness, where 'neat' comes from Old French 'nete', from Latin 'nitidus' (shining, elegant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnit.nəs / (AmE), / ˈniːt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being tidy, orderly, and free from clutter
quality of being tidy and orderly

Examples

She takes great pride in the neatness of her home and office.
His neatness was evident from the way all his clothes were folded perfectly.
The teacher praised the student's neatness in presenting her assignments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orderliness - the state of being orderly or well-arranged
  • Antonym: messiness - the state of being untidy or disorganized

Etymology

From 'neat' + '-ness', from Middle English 'net' (clean, tidy), from Old French 'net' (clean).
