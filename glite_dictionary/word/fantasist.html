fantasist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.sɪst / (AmE), / ˈfæntəsɪst / (BrE)
a person who creates or indulges in fantasies, often imagining things that are unlikely or untrue.
person who creates or indulges in fantasies

Examples

As a child, he was a fantasist who spent hours inventing imaginary worlds.
The novelist's vivid imagination marks her as a true fantasist.
Critics accused the witness of being a fantasist after her unbelievable story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamer - a person who dreams or has impractical ideas
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts things as they are and deals with them practically

Etymology

From 'fantasy' + '-ist', patterned after similar agent nouns.
