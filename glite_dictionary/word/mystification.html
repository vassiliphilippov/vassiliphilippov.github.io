mystification

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪstəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
something that is intended to confuse or bewilder people; an act or result that makes something unclear or puzzling.
something meant to confuse or bewilder

Examples

The magician's trick was a deliberate mystification meant to leave the audience in awe.
His explanation was pure mystification, making the question even more confusing than before.
The instructions were written with such mystification that few people could actually follow them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - the state of being bewildered or unclear
  • Antonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer or easier to understand

Etymology

From French mystification, from mystifier (to mystify).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪstəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of confusion or bewilderment caused by not understanding something.
state of confusion from not understanding

Examples

There was obvious mystification on her face when she read the instructions.
His explanation only added to the general mystification in the room.
The students stared in mystification at the complex equation written on the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a lack of understanding; state of being unclear or bewildered
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From French 'mystification', from 'mystifier' (to mystify).

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɪstəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something difficult or impossible to understand, often causing confusion or bewilderment
the act of making something confusing or mysterious

Examples

The company’s financial report was an exercise in mystification, leaving investors more confused than informed.
Some magicians rely on mystification to keep the secrets behind their tricks.
Her explanation was so full of jargon that it felt like deliberate mystification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of being bewildered or unclear in one’s mind
  • Antonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer or easier to understand

Etymology

From French mystification, from mystifier (to mystify) + -ation.
