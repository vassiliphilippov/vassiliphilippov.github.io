needlecraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəlˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈniːdl̩ˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
the activity or art of making things by hand with a needle, especially sewing, embroidery, or similar work
decorative or functional work done with a needle

Examples

She enjoys needlecraft and often spends her evenings embroidering pillows.
Many traditional needlecraft skills have been passed down through generations.
The exhibition features the finest examples of Victorian needlecraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlework - the activity of sewing, embroidery, or other work done with a needle

Etymology

Formed from 'needle' + 'craft', denoting craftwork using a needle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈniː.dəlˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈniː.dəl.krɑːft / (BrE)
An object or work produced using needle and thread, such as embroidery, knitting, or other textile arts.
object created with needle and thread

Examples

Her latest needlecraft is a beautifully embroidered tablecloth.
The artisan displayed various needlecrafts at the local fair.
Each needlecraft piece reflected hours of patient stitching and creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handicraft - an activity or object created by hand, often decorative

Etymology

From 'needle' (a small slender tool for sewing) and 'craft' (an activity involving skill).

Synset POS: n
