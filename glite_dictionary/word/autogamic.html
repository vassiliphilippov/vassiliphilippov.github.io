autogamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔtəˈɡæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈɡamɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) characterized by or capable of autogamy; able to self-fertilize.
pertaining to self-fertilization
medical

Examples

Some species of grass are naturally autogamic, relying on self-pollination to reproduce.
The plant's autogamic nature allows it to thrive even in isolated environments.
Researchers studied the autogamic mechanisms that ensure self-fertilization in certain flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autogamous - capable of or relating to self-fertilization
  • Antonym: allogamic - characterized by cross-fertilization

Etymology

From Greek auto- (self) + gamos (marriage), via autogamy.
