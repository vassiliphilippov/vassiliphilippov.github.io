rendition

1. NOUN

/ rɛnˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / rɛnˈdɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an explanation or interpretation of something that is not immediately obvious
explanation or interpretation

Examples

The professor's rendition of the poem clarified its hidden symbolism for the students.
Each scholar provided a different rendition of the ancient text's meaning.
His rendition of the contract clause made the terms easier to understand.

Synset Examples

the edict was subject to many interpretations
he annoyed us with his interpreting of parables
often imitations are extended to provide a more accurate rendition of the child's intended meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining the meaning of something
  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear

Etymology

From Latin 'redditionem' (a giving back, surrender); influenced by 'render'.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɛnˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / rɛnˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a performance or interpretation of a musical composition, dramatic role, poem, or other work of art
performance or interpretation of a work

Examples

The orchestra gave a stunning rendition of Beethoven's Ninth Symphony last night.
Her rendition of the classic play impressed the critics.
We listened to a beautiful jazz rendition of the national anthem.

Synset Examples

they heard a live rendition of three pieces by Schubert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a piece of music, a play, etc.
  • Synonym: interpretation - the particular way in which someone performs a piece of music, role, etc.

Etymology

From Latin 'rendere' (to give back, return) via Middle English 'rendren' and French 'rendre'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06906604-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɛnˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / rɛnˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular way of performing, singing, or interpreting something, especially a piece of music or literature
interpretation or performance of a work

Examples

Her rendition of the national anthem impressed the entire audience.
The actor's unique rendition brought new meaning to the classic play.
Critics praised the pianist's emotional rendition of Chopin's nocturnes.

Synset Examples

her rendition of Milton's verse was extraordinarily moving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or performing something in a particular way
  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a piece of music, play, etc.

Etymology

From Latin 'reddere' (to render, return) via Old French 'rendre' and Middle English 'renden'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101363-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ rɛnˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / rɛnˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of handing over a person, especially a prisoner, to another country where a crime was committed
the act of handing over a prisoner to another country
legal

Examples

The government approved the rendition of the suspect to the country seeking his extradition.
Critics argue that rendition can sometimes bypass legal protections for the accused.
International laws regulate the rendition of prisoners to ensure fair treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extradition - the act of sending someone accused of a crime back to the country where the crime was committed

Etymology

From Latin 'redditionem' (a giving back), via Old French 'rendition'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00422316-n
Synset POS: n
