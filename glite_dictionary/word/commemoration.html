commemoration

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɛməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a ceremony or act held to honor the memory of someone or something
ceremony to honor someone's or something's memory

Examples

The town holds an annual commemoration for those who lost their lives in the war.
A special commemoration was organized to honor the hospital's founding.
Veterans attended the commemoration ceremony at the national monument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorial - something established to remind people of a person or event

Etymology

From Latin commemoratio (reminding, remembering), from commemorare (to recall, call to mind).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɛməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an official act or ceremony held to honor and recognize meritorious service, achievement, or memory of a person or event
official recognition or remembrance, usually by ceremony

Examples

The town held a commemoration for the firefighters who risked their lives in the rescue.
At the annual commemoration, awards were presented to honor outstanding community service.
A bronze plaque was unveiled as a commemoration of his decades of public service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebration - the action of celebrating an event or occasion
  • Synonym: memorial - something, especially a ceremony, to honor and remember someone or something

Etymology

From Latin commemoratio (remembrance, calling to mind), from commemorare (to remember together).
