telepathist

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛpəθɪst / (AmE), / tɛˈlɛpəθɪst / (BrE)
a person who claims to have the ability to communicate thoughts directly from one mind to another without using the usual senses
person claiming telepathic ability

Examples

The main character in the novel is a gifted telepathist who can sense danger before anyone else.
Some scientists doubt the existence of true telepathists and demand more evidence.
During the experiment, the self-proclaimed telepathist attempted to transmit his thoughts to the volunteer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepath - a person who can read others' thoughts; synonym for telepathist

Etymology

From 'telepathy' (from Greek tele- ‘distant’ + -pathy ‘feeling’) + -ist (denoting a person).

2. NOUN

/ təˈlɛpəθɪst / (AmE), / tɛˈlɛpəθɪst / (BrE)
A magician or performer who claims to read thoughts, often by using subtle signals from an accomplice rather than actual telepathy.
magician claiming to read minds by trickery

Examples

The telepathist astounded the audience by correctly naming the card chosen by a volunteer.
It was later revealed that the telepathist used subtle hand signals from an accomplice.
Despite his convincing performance, the telepathist admitted that it was all a clever illusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentalist - A performer who claims to have highly developed mental powers

Etymology

Formed from 'telepathy' and the suffix '-ist.' 'Telepathy' from Greek 'tēle' (far off) + 'patheia' (feeling).
