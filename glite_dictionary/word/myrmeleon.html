Myrmeleon

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˈmiːliːˌɒn / (AmE), / mɪˈmɛliːən / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Myrmeleontidae, comprising various species of antlions distinguished by their slender bodies and net-veined wings.
genus of antlions (Myrmeleontidae)

Examples

The genus Myrmeleon includes some of the best-known species of antlions.
Researchers observed several Myrmeleon larvae constructing pit traps in the sandy soil.
The distinctive wing patterns of Myrmeleon adults help to differentiate them from related genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antlion - insect known for its predatory larva, member of Myrmeleontidae

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmēx' (ant) and 'leōn' (lion), referring to the insect's predatory larva.

2. NOUN

/ mɪrˈmiːliːɒn / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊliːɒn / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the antlion family (Myrmeleontidae), known for their predatory larvae that build pitfall traps for ants and other prey.
a genus of antlion insects

Examples

The Myrmeleon larvae are famous for constructing conical traps to capture ants.
Entomologists discovered a new species within the Myrmeleon genus in South America.
Adult Myrmeleon insects resemble delicate dragonflies with long, slender bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antlion - Insect known for its predatory larvae that trap ants

Etymology

From Greek myrmēx (ant) + leōn (lion), referring to the predatory behavior of the larvae.
