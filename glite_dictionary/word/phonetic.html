phonetic

1. ADJ

/ fəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the sounds of human speech, or to the study of these sounds
relating to speech sounds

Examples

She is studying phonetic transcription in her linguistics class.
The dictionary includes phonetic symbols for pronunciation guidance.
A phonetic alphabet represents the sounds of spoken language.

Synset Examples

phonetic transcription

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonological - relating to the systematic organization of sounds in languages

Etymology

From Greek phōnētikos (vocal, relating to sound), from phōnē (sound, voice).

2. ADJ

/ fəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the sounds of speech, especially as studied scientifically or represented in writing.
relating to speech sounds

Examples

The linguist conducted a phonetic analysis of regional accents.
Students practiced writing words in the phonetic alphabet to improve pronunciation.
He is interested in the phonetic differences between English and Spanish.

Synset Examples

phonetic analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonological - relating to the systematic organization of sounds in languages
  • Antonym: orthographic - relating to conventional spelling systems

Etymology

From Greek *phōnētikos* (vocal), from *phōnē* (sound, voice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02868815-a
Synset POS: a
