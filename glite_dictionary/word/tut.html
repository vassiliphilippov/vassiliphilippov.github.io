to tut

1. VERB

/ tʌt / (AmE), / tʌt / (BrE)
to express disapproval, impatience, or annoyance by making a short clicking sound with the tongue
make a disapproving clicking sound

Examples

She tutted at the mess on the kitchen table, clearly displeased.
The teacher tutted when the class was noisy.
He walked past the broken vase and couldn't help but tut in annoyance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tsk - to make a similar sound of disapproval with the tongue
  • Synonym: cluck - to make a short clicking sound with the tongue, often to express annoyance

Etymology

Imitative of the clicking sound made to show disapproval; first recorded in English in the 16th century.
