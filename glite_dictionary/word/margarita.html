margarita

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrɡəˈriːtə / (AmE), / ˌmɑːɡəˈriːtə / (BrE)
a cocktail consisting of tequila, triple sec, and lime or lemon juice, typically served in a salt-rimmed glass
cocktail with tequila and lime

Examples

She ordered a margarita with extra salt on the rim.
The bar is famous for its frozen margarita.
Would you like a classic margarita or something different?

Etymology

Spanish, meaning 'daisy', used for the drink since the 20th century.
