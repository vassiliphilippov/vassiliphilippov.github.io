acentric

1. ADJ

/ eɪˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of a chromosome or chromosome fragment) lacking a centromere.
lacking a centromere (genetics)
medical

Examples

Recent research focused on the behavior of acentric fragments during cell division.
An acentric chromosome cannot attach to the spindle and is often lost during mitosis.
The scientist observed an acentric piece of DNA under the microscope.

Synset Examples

an acentric chromosome fragment

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'kentron' (center).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03151778-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ eɪˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
not centered or lacking a center
not centered; having no center

Examples

In genetics, an acentric chromosome fragment is one that lacks a centromere.
The design was deliberately acentric, drawing attention away from a central focus.
Because the figure was acentric, it seemed to float irregularly on the page.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centric - centered; having or situated at a center

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'kentron' (center).
