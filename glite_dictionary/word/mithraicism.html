Mithraicism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪθˈreɪ.ɪ.sɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪθˈreɪ.ɪ.sɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
An ancient Persian religion centered on the god Mithras, especially popular among the Romans during the first three centuries A.D.
ancient Persian religion involving Mithras

Examples

Scholars have debated the influence of Mithraicism on early Christianity.
The mysterious rituals of Mithraicism were often conducted in underground temples called Mithraea.
Interest in Mithraicism has grown among historians studying ancient Persian and Roman cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mithraism - the worship of Mithras; practice and beliefs associated with Mithraicism

Etymology

From the name of the ancient Persian god Mithras + -icism (patterned on words like Buddhism).
