civilisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a society or place reaches an advanced stage of social and cultural development and organization.
process of advanced social development

Examples

The rise of Athens marked a new phase in the civilisation of ancient Greece.
Some historians argue that trade was crucial to the civilisation of early societies.
Education is often considered a fundamental part of societal civilisation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civilization - Alternate (American) spelling for the same concept

Etymology

From French civilisation, from Latin civilis (relating to citizens).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A society considered in terms of its culture, development, and historical period, especially one regarded as advanced or distinct.
particular society at a given time/place

Examples

The museum has a fascinating exhibit on the ancient Egyptian civilisation.
Archaeologists continue to discover artifacts from the lost civilisation of the Indus Valley.
Each civilisation has contributed unique achievements to human history.

4. NOUN

/ ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A human society that has reached an advanced stage of social development and organization, often characterized by complex institutions, culture, and technology.
advanced human society and culture

Examples

Many historians study the rise and fall of ancient civilisations like Egypt, Greece, and Rome.
Some argue that the Internet is transforming global civilisation as radically as the printing press did.
The museum's exhibit explores the origins of civilisation in Mesopotamia.

Synset Examples

the people slowly progressed from barbarism to civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culture - the ideas, customs, and social behaviour of a society
  • Antonym: barbarism - absence of culture and civilization

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to citizens) via French 'civilisation'.
