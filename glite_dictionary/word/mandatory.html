mandatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.dəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dəˌtɔː.ri / (BrE)
A territory formerly controlled by Turkey or Germany that was placed under the administration of another power by international mandate after World War I, until considered capable of self-government.
territory under international mandate after WWI

Examples

After World War I, Palestine became a mandatory under British administration.
Several former German colonies in Africa were designated as mandatories by the League of Nations.
The transition from mandatory status to independence was a lengthy process for some territories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandate - the authority or commission given to administer such a territory

Etymology

From French 'mandataire' (agent, trustee), from Latin 'mandatum' (commission, mandate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmæn.dəˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈmæn.də.təri / (BrE)
Required by law or rules; compulsory.
required by law or rule

Examples

Wearing seat belts is mandatory in most countries for all passengers.
Attendance at the meeting is mandatory for all staff members.
Submission of the application form is mandatory before the deadline.

Synset Examples

in most schools physical education is compulsory
attendance is mandatory
required reading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsory - required by law or a rule; obligatory
  • Antonym: optional - not required; left to one's choice

Etymology

From Medieval Latin mandatorius, from Latin mandatum (command, order).
