spuriousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈspjʊriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈspjʊəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being false, fake, or not genuine.
the state of being fake or not genuine

Examples

The spuriousness of the document was quickly uncovered by the expert.
She was troubled by the spuriousness of his compliment, sensing it was not sincere.
The report questioned the spuriousness of the newly discovered evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falseness - the quality of being untrue or insincere
  • Antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real

Etymology

From 'spurious' (of Latin origin: spurius, meaning false or illegitimate) + '-ness'.
