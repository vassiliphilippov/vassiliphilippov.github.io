Cyrtomium

1. NOUN

/ sɝˈtoʊmiəm / (AmE), / sɜːˈtəʊmiəm / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical Asiatic ferns, often grown in greenhouses and known for their decorative, leathery fronds.
genus of tropical Asiatic ferns

Examples

Cyrtomium species such as the holly fern are valued for their hardy foliage in shaded gardens.
She chose to decorate the conservatory with several Cyrtomium ferns for a tropical touch.
Botanists debated whether Cyrtomium should be classified in the Dryopteridaceae or Polypodiaceae family.

Etymology

From Greek kyrtos (curved, arched) + -ium, New Latin suffix used in botanical names.
