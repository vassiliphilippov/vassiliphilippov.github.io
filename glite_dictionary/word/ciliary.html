ciliary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪliˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəri / (BrE)
relating to the ciliary body or associated structures of the eye, such as the ciliary muscle or ciliary processes.
of the ciliary body of the eye
medical

Examples

The ciliary muscle controls the shape of the eye's lens during focusing.
In glaucoma, damage to the ciliary body can affect intraocular pressure.
The doctor examined the ciliary processes under the microscope.

Etymology

From Latin cilium (eyelash).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪliˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪəri / (BrE)
relating to the eyelashes or to structures resembling fine hairs, especially in anatomy and biology.
of the eyelashes or hair-like structures

Examples

The doctor examined the patient for signs of ciliary inflammation around the eye.
Certain eye medications may cause ciliary muscle spasms, resulting in discomfort.
The ciliary body in the eye is essential for changing the lens shape during focusing.

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' (eyelid, eyelash) + '-ary'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪliˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəri / (BrE)
relating to cilia, the microscopic hair-like structures projecting from the surface of a cell
relating to cell cilia
medical

Examples

The movement of the ciliary structures helps propel the cell through liquid environments.
Some respiratory illnesses can damage the ciliary cells lining the airways.
Scientists observed the synchronized beating of ciliary hairs under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cilia - microscopic hair-like structures on cells

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' (eyelid, eyelash), referring to the hair-like appearance.
