Gonorhynchidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊ.nəˌrɪn.kɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ɡɒ.nəˌrɪŋ.kɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes in the order Gonorynchiformes, coextensive with the genus Gonorhynchus.
family of fishes (Gonorynchus genus)
medical

Examples

The family Gonorhynchidae comprises several primitive ray-finned fishes.
Researchers study the distribution and characteristics of Gonorhynchidae in marine environments.
Members of the Gonorhynchidae family are known for their unique anatomical features.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Gonorhynchus + -idae (denoting a zoological family).
