benzofuran

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnzoʊˈfjʊræn / (AmE), / ˌbɛnzəˈfjʊəræn / (BrE)
A colorless oily organic compound obtained from coal tar, used in the manufacture of synthetic resins and other chemicals.
a colorless oily organic compound

Examples

The laboratory ordered a new shipment of benzofuran for their polymer experiments.
Some synthetic resins are produced using benzofuran as a raw material.
Because benzofuran has industrial importance, safety precautions must be followed during handling.

Etymology

From 'benzo-' (referring to the benzene ring) and 'furan' (a heterocyclic compound).
