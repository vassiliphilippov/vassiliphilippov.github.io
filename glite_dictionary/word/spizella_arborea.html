Spizella arborea

1. NOUN

/ spɪˈzɛlə ɑrˈbɔriə / (AmE), / spɪˈzɛlə ɑːˈbɔːriə / (BrE)
A small North American finch, known as the American tree sparrow, commonly seen in winter in the northern United States.
species of North American finch (American tree sparrow)

Examples

Every winter, flocks of Spizella arborea can be spotted feeding on seeds in snowy fields.
Birdwatchers were excited to see a rare sighting of Spizella arborea during their morning walk.
The Spizella arborea migrates from its breeding grounds in Canada to the northern United States each winter.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Spizella' (a diminutive of spiza, 'finch') and 'arborea' (meaning 'of the trees').
