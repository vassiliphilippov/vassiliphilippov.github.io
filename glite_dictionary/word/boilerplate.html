boilerplate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlərˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ.lə.pleɪt / (BrE)
thick, strong steel plate used in the manufacture of boilers and similar heavy equipment
thick iron plate for making boilers

Examples

Early steam engines required boilerplate sturdy enough to withstand high pressure.
The factory specialized in rolling boilerplate for shipbuilding and heavy machinery.
They inspected the boilerplate for any signs of corrosion before assembly.

Etymology

From 'boiler' + 'plate', referring to plate iron used to make boilers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlərˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪləˌpleɪt / (BrE)
standardized language or passages used repeatedly in documents such as contracts, legal notices, or news articles.
standardized wording in documents

Examples

The contract was filled with so much boilerplate that it was hard to find the important information.
Journalists often rely on boilerplate paragraphs when writing routine news stories.
Much of the document is just legal boilerplate and can be skimmed over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: template - a preset format used as a starting point for a particular application
  • Antonym: custom text - text written specifically for a document without reuse

Etymology

Originally referred to metal plates used for making boilers; later used for set text in printing.
