Gallinago

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, known commonly as snipes, characterized by long, slender bills and cryptic feather patterns.
genus of wading birds (snipes)

Examples

The marshes were home to several species in the genus Gallinago.
Researchers observed the distinctive foraging behavior of Gallinago during their study.
The Gallinago are known for their excellent camouflage and elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a bird in the genus Gallinago, known for its long bill and marshy habitat

Etymology

From Latin 'Gallinago' (snipe), related to 'gallina' (hen).

2. NOUN

/ ɡælɪˈneɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡalɪˈneɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the snipe family, characterized by long bills and typically found in wetlands and marshy areas.
genus of snipes (birds)

Examples

The Gallinago genus includes several species of snipes found across the globe.
Researchers observed the migratory patterns of Gallinago in the wetland reserve.
The long, straight bill is a distinctive feature of birds in the Gallinago genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long bill, found in wetlands

Etymology

From Latin 'gallinago', meaning 'snipe', related to 'gallina' (hen).
