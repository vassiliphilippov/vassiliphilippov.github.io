Anas acuta

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.əs əˈkjuː.tə / (AmE), / ˈæn.əs əˈkjuː.tə / (BrE)
A long-necked species of duck, also known as the pintail, found in various parts of the world and distinguished by its elongated central tail feathers.
species of duck; pintail

Examples

The northern Anas acuta is a familiar sight in wetlands during migration season.
Birdwatchers observed a flock of Anas acuta feeding along the riverbank.
Male Anas acuta are easily recognized by their elegant pointed tail feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pintail - a type of duck with a pointed tail, often called 'northern pintail'

Etymology

From Latin: 'anas' meaning 'duck' and 'acuta' meaning 'pointed', referring to its pointed tail.
