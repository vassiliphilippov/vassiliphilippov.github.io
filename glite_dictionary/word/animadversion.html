animadversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪˌmædˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌænɪmadˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
strong criticism or disapproval, especially when expressed publicly or formally.
harsh criticism or disapproval

Examples

The mayor's decision provoked considerable animadversion from the local press.
Her speech was free from any animadversion toward her opponents.
Public figures often face animadversion for even minor mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something based on perceived faults or mistakes
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'animadversio', from 'animadvertere' (to notice, censure).
