nonaddictive

1. ADJ

/ nɑn.əˈdɪk.tɪv / (AmE), / nɒn.əˈdɪk.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance or activity) not causing or characterized by addiction.
not causing addiction

Examples

Doctors recommend nonaddictive medications for patients with a history of substance abuse.
Unlike many painkillers, this treatment is completely nonaddictive.
Researchers are developing nonaddictive alternatives to traditional opioids for chronic pain management.

Synset Examples

for years tobacco companies have contended that tobacco is nonaddictive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: addictive - causing or likely to cause addiction

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'addictive'.
