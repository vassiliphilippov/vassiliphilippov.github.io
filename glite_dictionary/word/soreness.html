soreness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.nəs / (BrE)
a painful or tender feeling in a part of the body, especially when touched
painful or tender bodily feeling

Examples

After the long run, I felt significant soreness in my legs the next day.
He complained of soreness in his throat whenever he swallowed.
The injection site can sometimes cause mild soreness or redness for a few hours.

Synset Examples

the best results are generally obtained by inserting the needle into the point of maximum tenderness
after taking a cold, rawness of the larynx and trachea come on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenderness - the quality of being easily hurt or sensitive to touch
  • Synonym: rawness - the condition of being raw or abraded, especially skin

Etymology

From sore + -ness, with 'sore' from Old English 'sār' (wound, pain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of emotional pain, distress, or hurt caused by disappointment, insult, or loss.
emotional pain or distress

Examples

She couldn't hide the soreness she felt after being excluded from the group.
His voice was filled with soreness as he spoke about the lost opportunity.
There was a lingering soreness between the friends after their argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical or emotional suffering or discomfort
  • Synonym: hurt - emotional pain or distress
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical or emotional ease

Etymology

From sore + -ness, with sense extension to emotional pain.
