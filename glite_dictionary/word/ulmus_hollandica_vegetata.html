Ulmus hollandica vegetata

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs hɒˈlændɪkə vɛdʒɪˈteɪtə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs hɒˈlændɪkə vɛdʒɪˈteɪtə / (BrE)
an erect, vigorous hybrid ornamental elm tree, cultivated for its form and foliage
hybrid ornamental elm tree

Examples

The manor house was surrounded by Ulmus hollandica vegetata, providing shade and elegance to the gardens.
Arborists recommend Ulmus hollandica vegetata for city parks because of its resilience and fast growth.
Botanists collected samples from a mature Ulmus hollandica vegetata to study its disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of deciduous tree known for its serrated leaves and hardwood

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ulmus' (elm), 'hollandica' (of Holland), 'vegetata' (vigorous or lively).
