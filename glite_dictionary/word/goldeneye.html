goldeneye

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldənˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldənˌaɪ / (BrE)
A green lacewing of the family Chrysopidae, known for its golden-colored eyes and delicate wings.
a green lacewing insect with golden eyes

Examples

The goldeneye is often seen in gardens where it preys on aphids.
At dusk, a goldeneye settled on the windowsill, its iridescent eyes glinting in the light.
Farmers value the goldeneye for its role in controlling pest populations naturally.

Etymology

From 'golden' (of gold color) + 'eye', referring to the insect's eye color.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldənˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldənˌaɪ / (BrE)
a large-headed, fast-flying diving duck found in Arctic regions, noted for its golden-yellow eyes
Arctic diving duck with golden eyes

Examples

A flock of goldeneye landed briefly on the frozen lake before taking off again.
The distinctive golden eyes make the goldeneye easy to identify among other ducks.
We spotted several female goldeneyes swimming near the northern shore during our trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

From the golden-yellow coloring of the duck's eyes.
