millimicron

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪˈmaɪkrɑn / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈmaɪkrɒn / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter, now more commonly called a nanometer
one billionth of a meter (nanometer)

Examples

The virus measured only 120 millimicrons in diameter.
Before 'nanometer' became standard, scientists often used the term millimicron.
Under the microscope, the particles were just a few millimicrons wide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanometer - a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter

Etymology

From milli- (one thousandth) + micron (micrometer); now superseded by nanometer.
