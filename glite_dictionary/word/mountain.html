mountain

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn / (BrE)
A very large natural elevation of the earth's surface rising abruptly from the surrounding level; higher and steeper than a hill.
large natural elevation of land

Examples

After hours of hiking, they finally reached the summit of the mountain.
Snow covered the peaks of every mountain visible from the village.
She dreamed of climbing a famous mountain one day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high or steep as a mountain

Etymology

From Old French 'montaigne', from Latin 'montanea', from 'mons' (mountain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən / (BrE)
A very large amount or number of something, especially when it seems overwhelming or difficult to manage, often used metaphorically and followed by 'of'.
very large amount or number (metaphoric)

Examples

He left a mountain of paperwork on my desk before leaving.
There is a mountain of evidence against the defendant in this case.
After the party, we had a mountain of dishes to wash.

Synset Examples

a batch of letters

a lot of money

see the rest of the winners in our huge passel of photos
it must have cost plenty
a slew of journalists
a wad of money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heap - a large pile or mass of something
  • Synonym: pile - a large quantity of something stacked together
  • Antonym: handful - a small quantity or amount

Etymology

From Old French 'montaine', from Latin 'montana', feminine of 'montanus' (relating to a mountain), from 'mons, mont-' (mountain).
