backsword

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌsɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbækˌsɔːd / (BrE)
A sword with a single cutting edge and a flat back, typically used in fencing or dueling.
sword with one cutting edge

Examples

The knight drew his backsword and prepared to defend himself against the attackers.
Traditional English fencing often made use of the backsword due to its sturdy design.
Collectors of antique weapons prize the backsword for its historical significance and unique shape.

Etymology

From 'back' + 'sword', indicating the flat back edge of the blade.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌsɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbækˌsɔːd / (BrE)
A stick used in place of a sword for fencing practice, especially in historical contexts.
stick used for fencing practice

Examples

The students practiced their moves with a backsword before handling real blades.
During training, a backsword was used to prevent injury.
He collected antique fencing equipment, including an old backsword.

Etymology

From 'back' + 'sword', originally referring to a type of sword with a flat back, later applied to the stick used for practice.
