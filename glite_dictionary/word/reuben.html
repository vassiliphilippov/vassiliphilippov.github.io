Reuben

1. PROPN

/ ˈruːbən / (AmE), / ˈruːbən / (BrE)
(In the Old Testament) the eldest son of Jacob and the forefather of one of the twelve tribes of Israel.
eldest son of Jacob in the Old Testament

Examples

Reuben was the firstborn son of Jacob and Leah in the Hebrew Bible.
According to tradition, the tribe of Reuben settled east of the Jordan River.
In many biblical stories, Reuben is portrayed as a protector of his younger brother Joseph.

Etymology

From Hebrew Reʾuven, meaning 'behold, a son'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːbən / (AmE), / ˈruːbən / (BrE)
a hot sandwich made with corned beef or pastrami, Swiss cheese, and sauerkraut, typically served grilled on rye bread
a type of grilled sandwich with corned beef, cheese, and sauerkraut

Examples

I ordered a Reuben at the deli, and it came with a side of pickles.
Her favorite lunch is a classic Reuben with extra sauerkraut.
The café serves a vegetarian version of the Reuben using grilled mushrooms instead of meat.

Etymology

Probably named after Reuben Kulakofsky, an Omaha grocer involved in its creation.
