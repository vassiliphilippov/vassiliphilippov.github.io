originality

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪdʒəˈnæləti / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being novel or unusual, not copied from something else; creativity in thought or expression
quality of being new or creative

Examples

Her paintings show remarkable originality and imagination.
The originality of his ideas impressed the judges at the competition.
Critics praised the film for its originality and fresh perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual
  • Antonym: derivativeness - the quality of being derived from something else, lacking originality

Etymology

From French 'originalité', from 'original' (originating, first) + '-ité' (-ity).

2. NOUN

/ əˌrɪdʒəˈnæləɾi / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the ability to think or produce ideas in a new and independent way; the quality of being original
the quality of being original

Examples

Her originality impressed everyone on the panel of judges.
The book stands out for its remarkable originality and depth of insight.
His originality allows him to view problems from entirely new perspectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creativity - the use of imagination or original ideas
  • Antonym: conventionality - adherence to accepted standards or conventions

Etymology

From French originalité, from original.
