damnable

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæm.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdæm.nə.bəl / (BrE)
Deserving to be condemned, detested, or strongly criticized.
deserving condemnation or detestation

Examples

Her damnable actions caused outrage among her peers.
He was frustrated by the damnable noise from the construction site.
Many considered the villain's lies a damnable offense.

Synset Examples

her damnable pride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detestable - deserving intense dislike or hatred
  • Antonym: commendable - deserving praise or approval

Etymology

From Late Latin damnabilis (worthy of condemnation), from Latin damnare (to condemn).
