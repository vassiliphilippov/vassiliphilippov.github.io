logician

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈdʒɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊˈdʒɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who studies or is skilled in logic, especially formal logic
expert in logic

Examples

The renowned logician presented his new proof at the academic conference.
As a logician, she often analyzed arguments for hidden fallacies.
Many logicians have contributed to the field of computer science through their research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged in the study of philosophy
  • Synonym: mathematician - an expert in or student of mathematics

Etymology

From French 'logicien', from Medieval Latin 'logica' (logic) + '-ian'.
