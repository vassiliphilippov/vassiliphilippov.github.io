curlicue

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.lɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.lɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
a decorative spiral or curved line, especially one formed by a series of concentric or looped curves
a decorative spiral or curved line

Examples

She signed her name with a little curlicue at the end of each letter.
The ironwork on the gate was adorned with elegant curlicues.
A curlicue of smoke rose from the chimney and drifted into the clear sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - a curve that winds around a central point and gradually moves away from or toward it
  • Synonym: scrollwork - decorative work featuring spirals and curves, often in architecture or metalwork

Etymology

Perhaps a fanciful formation, possibly from 'curl' + 'cue' (as in ‘queue’ or tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrliˌkju / (AmE), / ˈkɜːlɪkjuː / (BrE)
a decorative twist or curl, especially a short, twisting line or flourish made in writing or design
decorative twisting line or flourish

Examples

She decorated the border of the invitation with golden curlicues.
The old letter was adorned with elegant curlicues on every capital letter.
The artist added a few curlicues to give the drawing a whimsical feel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scroll - an ornamental design with spirals or curves

Etymology

Imitative; from 'curl' and 'cue' (tail), reflecting the twisted form.
