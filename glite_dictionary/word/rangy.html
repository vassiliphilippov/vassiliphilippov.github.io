rangy

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒi / (BrE)
spacious or allowing plenty of room for movement; providing ample space for ranging.
allowing ample room for ranging

Examples

The rangy pasture allowed the cattle to graze freely all day.
We searched for rangy fields where the dogs could run off-leash.
He admired the rangy layout of the park, perfect for long walks.

Etymology

From English 'range' + '-y', forming adjectives from nouns.

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒi / (BrE)
(of an animal) adapted to wandering or roaming; suited to wide-ranging movement over large areas.
adapted to wandering or roaming

Examples

The rangy cattle grazed freely across the vast prairie, hardly stopping in one place for long.
Mustangs are known for their rangy build and ability to cover great distances in search of food.
She adopted a rangy dog that loved nothing more than exploring the countryside.

Etymology

From 'range' + -y, meaning characterized by ranging or roaming.

3. ADJ

/ ˈreɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒi / (BrE)
(of a person) tall and thin, with long, slender limbs.
tall and thin, with long limbs

Examples

He was a rangy basketball player with an impressive wingspan.
The rangy model walked elegantly down the runway.
A group of rangy teenagers were shooting hoops at the park.

Synset Examples

a gangling teenager
a lanky kid transformed almost overnight into a handsome young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanky - tall and thin, often with long limbs
  • Synonym: gangly - awkwardly tall and thin, loosely built
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built, the opposite of tall and thin
