Sheetrock

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːtˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃiːtˌrɒk / (BrE)
A brand of plasterboard made of gypsum used especially for interior walls and ceilings; often used generally to mean drywall.
brand of plasterboard (drywall)
american

Examples

The contractor recommended using Sheetrock for the interior walls because it is easy to install.
After the leak, they had to replace the damaged Sheetrock in the living room ceiling.
He learned how to patch Sheetrock when he renovated his old house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drywall - common name for gypsum plasterboard used in walls and ceilings
  • Synonym: plasterboard - panel made of gypsum plaster pressed between two thick sheets of paper

Etymology

Trademark, coined from 'sheet' + 'rock', referring to sheets of gypsum rock.
