Ginkgo biloba

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪŋkoʊ bɪˈloʊbə / (AmE), / ˈɡɪŋkəʊ bɪˈləʊbə / (BrE)
A deciduous Chinese tree with distinctive fan-shaped leaves and yellow seeds, often grown as an ornamental plant in cities.
deciduous Chinese tree with fan-shaped leaves

Examples

The Ginkgo biloba in our park turns a striking yellow every autumn.
Many city planners choose Ginkgo biloba for street trees because of their resistance to pollution.
She collected some Ginkgo biloba leaves to use in her botanical artwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair tree - Another common name for Ginkgo biloba

Etymology

From Japanese ginkyō (銀杏, 'silver apricot') and New Latin biloba ('two-lobed', referring to the leaf shape).
