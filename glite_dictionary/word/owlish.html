owlish

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊ.lɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈaʊ.lɪʃ / (BrE)
Resembling an owl, especially in being solemn or wise in appearance.
resembling an owl; solemn or wise-looking

Examples

He looked at her with an owlish expression, his glasses perched low on his nose.
The professor's owlish demeanor made students listen attentively to his every word.
Her owlish gaze gave the impression that she was pondering something deeply important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with large eyes and a flat face

Etymology

From 'owl' + '-ish', meaning 'like an owl'.
