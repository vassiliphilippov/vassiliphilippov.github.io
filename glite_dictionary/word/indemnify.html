to indemnify

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to secure someone against possible loss, damage, or legal responsibility, especially by providing compensation or protection
protect against financial loss or liability
legal

Examples

The insurance policy will indemnify the homeowners against damage caused by fire.
Employers are required to indemnify their workers for injuries sustained on the job.
The company agreed to indemnify its directors against any legal costs incurred in the lawsuit.

Synset Examples

This plan indemnifies workers against wages lost through illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compensate - to give someone something, usually money, to make up for loss or damage
  • Synonym: to insure - to provide compensation in case of loss, damage, or liability, especially through an insurance policy
  • Antonym: to expose - to leave someone unprotected from potential loss or harm

Etymology

From Latin 'indemnis' (unhurt, undamaged) + '-fy' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɛmnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to compensate someone for harm, loss, or damage; to pay or promise to pay for losses incurred
compensate for loss or damage

Examples

The insurance policy will indemnify the homeowner against any future losses.
The company agreed to indemnify its employees for expenses incurred during business trips.
After the lawsuit, the firm was required to indemnify the customers for damages caused by its product.

Synset Examples

One can never fully repair the suffering and losses of the Jews in the Third Reich
She was compensated for the loss of her arm in the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compensate - to give someone something, often money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury
  • Synonym: to reimburse - to pay back money to someone that they have spent or lost
  • Antonym: to penalize - to subject someone to a penalty or punishment

Etymology

From Latin *indemnis* (unhurt, undamaged) + *-fy* (to make).
