Avena

1. NOUN

/ əˈviːnə / (AmE), / əˈviːnə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses that includes oats, often cultivated for their edible seeds used as food and fodder.
genus of grasses (oats)

Examples

Many breakfast cereals are made from Avena species cultivated around the world.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of Avena plants in various climates.
The genus Avena includes several important crops besides the common oat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oat - edible grain of the Avena genus, used as food and animal feed

Etymology

From Latin *avēna* (oats, wild oats).
