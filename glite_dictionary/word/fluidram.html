fluidram

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪdˌræm / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪdˌræm / (BrE)
a British imperial unit of capacity, liquid or dry, equal to 60 minims or approximately 3.55 milliliters
British imperial unit of capacity (3.55 ml)
british

Examples

The prescription specified one fluidram of the medication to be taken daily.
A fluidram is no longer commonly used, but it sometimes appears in old medical recipes.
One fluidram is equivalent to approximately 3.55 milliliters in volume.

Etymology

From 'fluid' + 'dram', with 'dram' from Greek *drakhmē* (a unit of weight) via Latin and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪˌdræm / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪˌdræm / (BrE)
a unit of capacity in the apothecary system equal to one eighth of a fluid ounce, used chiefly in pharmacy and medicine
apothecary unit of volume (1/8 fluid ounce)
archaic medical

Examples

The prescription called for one fluidram of the medicine to be administered daily.
Historically, the fluidram was used in preparing liquid medications in pharmacies.
One fluidram is equal to approximately 3.55 milliliters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fluidounce - an apothecary unit of volume equal to 1/16 pint
  • Degree Lower: minim - a small unit of fluid measure in the apothecary system equal to 1/60 of a fluidram

Etymology

From 'fluid' + 'dram', with 'dram' from Greek 'drakhmē' (a unit of weight).
