sulphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of sulphuric acid, containing the anion SO₄²⁻
salt or ester of sulphuric acid

Examples

The water in this area contains high levels of sulphate ions.
Glauber's salt is a well-known example of a sulphate compound.
Adding sulphate fertilizers can help improve soil quality in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfate - alternative spelling (primarily American English) of 'sulphate'

Etymology

From French *sulfate*, based on Latin *sulphur* (sulfur).
