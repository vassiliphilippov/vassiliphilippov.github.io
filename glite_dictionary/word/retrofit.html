retrofit

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌfɪt / (BrE)
to provide a machine, building, or system with new parts or equipment that were not available or used when it was first built
add new parts to something old

Examples

The company decided to retrofit its old factories with energy-efficient lighting.
After the earthquake, many buildings were retrofitted to meet new safety standards.
They plan to retrofit their fleet of buses to run on electric power.

Synset Examples

The car companies retrofitted all the old models with new carburetors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upgrade - to raise something to a higher standard
  • Synonym: to modernize - to make something more modern

Etymology

From retro- (backward) + fit, coined mid-20th century in US engineering contexts.
