porcupinefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrk.jəˌpaɪn.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.kjə.paɪn.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish found in warm seas worldwide, able to inflate its body and raise sharp spines as a defense mechanism
a spiny fish that inflates its body

Examples

The porcupinefish puffed up and displayed its sharp spines when threatened.
Divers often spot the porcupinefish hiding among coral reefs in tropical oceans.
When alarmed, a porcupinefish can inflate its body to appear much larger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pufferfish - a fish that can inflate its body as a defense

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a porcupine due to the spiny appearance when inflated.
