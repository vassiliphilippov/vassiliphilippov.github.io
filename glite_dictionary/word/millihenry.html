millihenry

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪliˈhɛnri / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈhɛnri / (BrE)
A unit of electrical inductance equal to one thousandth of a henry.
unit of inductance (1/1000 henry)

Examples

The inductor has a value of 10 millihenry for this circuit.
She measured the coil's inductance to be just 2 millihenry.
Engineers often use millihenry as a convenient unit when working with small inductors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: henry - the SI unit of electrical inductance

Etymology

From milli- (meaning 'thousandth') and henry (the SI unit of inductance, named after Joseph Henry).
