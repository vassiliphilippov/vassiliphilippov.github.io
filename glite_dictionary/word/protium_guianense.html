Protium guianense

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊti.əm ɡiˈænɛnsi / (AmE), / ˈprəʊti.əm ɡɪˈanɛnseɪ / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to South America, belonging to the frankincense family (Burseraceae), known for its resinous wood.
tropical South American tree

Examples

The resin from Protium guianense is valued for its fragrance in traditional ceremonies.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Protium guianense in the Amazon rainforest.
The wood of Protium guianense is sometimes used in local construction.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Protium' and species 'guianense', referring to the Guianas region of South America.
