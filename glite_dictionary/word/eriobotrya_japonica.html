Eriobotrya japonica

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈbɒtri.ə dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˌɛri.əˈbɒtrɪ.ə dʒəˈpəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to China and Japan, cultivated for its fuzzy yellow fruit with a large stone, commonly known as the loquat
evergreen tree bearing the loquat fruit

Examples

The Eriobotrya japonica thrives in warm climates and is valued for its delicious fruit.
Gardeners often plant Eriobotrya japonica for its attractive foliage and edible yellow fruit.
The fruit of Eriobotrya japonica, known as loquat, is used in jams and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loquat - common name for the fruit and tree Eriobotrya japonica

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'botrys' (cluster, grape), referencing the fruit's appearance, and specific epithet from Latin for 'Japanese.'
