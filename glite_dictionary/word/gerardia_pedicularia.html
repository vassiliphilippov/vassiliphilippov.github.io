Gerardia pedicularia

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɹdiə pɛdɪˌkjulˈɛəriə / (AmE), / dʒəˈrɑːdɪə pɛdɪˌkjʊlˈɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A multi-stemmed annual plant native to North America with solitary, dark golden-yellow flowers resembling those of the foxglove; sometimes classified in the genus Gerardia.
North American annual with yellow foxglove-like flowers

Examples

Gerardia pedicularia blooms in late summer, adding a splash of golden yellow to woodland clearings.
Many botanists are interested in the pollinators attracted to Gerardia pedicularia due to its distinctive flowers.
Some conservationists work to protect Gerardia pedicularia, which is threatened in certain parts of its native range.

Etymology

Genus name from French botanist John Gerard; species name from Latin 'pedicularis' (relating to lice), an old belief about the plant's effect on livestock.
