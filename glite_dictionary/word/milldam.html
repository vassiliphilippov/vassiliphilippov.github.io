milldam

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪl.dæm / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.dam / (BrE)
A dam built to create a millpond that supplies water power to a water mill.
dam for supplying a mill with water power

Examples

The old milldam was rebuilt to restore power to the historic water mill.
Flooding washed away part of the milldam, threatening the structure upstream.
They picnicked by the milldam, watching the water spill into the millrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water

Etymology

From 'mill' (a device for grinding) + 'dam' (a barrier to hold back water).
