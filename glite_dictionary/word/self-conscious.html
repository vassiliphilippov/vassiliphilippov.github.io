self-conscious

1. ADJ

/ sɛlfˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / sɛlfˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
excessively and uncomfortably aware of yourself, especially how you look or act
uncomfortably aware of oneself

Examples

She felt self-conscious wearing the bright red dress to the party.
Many people are self-conscious about speaking in public.
He became self-conscious when he realized everyone was watching him.

Synset Examples

self-conscious teenagers
wondered if she could ever be untidy without feeling self-conscious about it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing assurance, especially in oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'conscious', dating from the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
nervous or uncomfortable because you are worried about what people think about you, or highly aware of yourself and your actions
uncomfortably aware of oneself or one's actions

Examples

She felt self-conscious about her new hairstyle at the party.
He became self-conscious when everyone in the room stared at him.
Teenagers are often self-conscious about their appearance.

Synset Examples

self-conscious awareness
self-conscious about their roles as guardians of the social values

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • Synonym: embarrassed - feeling awkward or ashamed, often due to self-awareness

Etymology

From 'self' + 'conscious', first recorded in the 18th century.

3. ADJ

/ sɛlfˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / sɛlfˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
nervous or uncomfortable because you are aware of being observed or judged by others; excessively aware of oneself
uncomfortably aware of oneself

Examples

He felt self-conscious about his accent when speaking in public.
Mary became self-conscious after noticing everyone was watching her.
Adolescents are often self-conscious about their appearance.

Synset Examples

Being a misopogon makes him a bit self-conscious.
The pansyish remarks made him feel self-conscious at the party.
She felt self-conscious about her herpie until she learned more.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty about oneself; self-assured
  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'conscious', meaning aware of oneself.
