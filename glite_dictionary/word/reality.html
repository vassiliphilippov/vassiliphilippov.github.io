reality

1. NOUN

/ riˈæləti / (AmE), / riˈæləti / (BrE)
the state of things as they actually exist, rather than as imagined or wished.
the state of things as they actually exist

Examples

After the excitement wore off, she was forced to accept the reality of the situation.
Many people find it hard to separate dreams from reality.
The reality is that not everyone can win.

Synset Examples

businessmen have to face harsh realities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illusion - a false idea or belief; something that is not real
  • Synonym: actuality - the state of being actual or real; real existence

Etymology

From Medieval Latin realitas, from Latin realis (real).

2. NOUN

/ riˈæləti / (AmE), / riˈalɪti / (BrE)
the way things appear or seem to a person or group, shaped by their own experiences or perspective
personal or subjective perception of the world

Examples

For some people, religious beliefs are a central part of their reality.
Growing up in the city gave her a very different reality than someone raised in a small village.

3. NOUN

/ riˈæləti / (AmE), / riˈalɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of having actual existence or being real, as opposed to being imaginary or illusionary.
actual existence; state of being real

Examples

Some people choose to escape reality by watching movies or reading books.
The reality of the situation became clear once the facts were revealed.
Children often have trouble distinguishing between reality and imagination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fantasy - the faculty or activity of imagining things, especially that which is impossible or improbable
  • Antonym: illusion - a false idea or belief; something that is not real
  • Synonym: actuality - the state of existing in fact; reality

Etymology

From Late Latin realitas (the quality of being real), from Latin realis (real).
