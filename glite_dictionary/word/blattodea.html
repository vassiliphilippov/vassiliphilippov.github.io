Blattodea

1. NOUN

/ blæˈtoʊdiə / (AmE), / bləˈtəʊdiə / (BrE)
An order of insects that includes cockroaches and, in some classifications, termites.
order of insects including cockroaches

Examples

Scientists recently reclassified termites as part of the Blattodea order.
The museum's entomology exhibit featured several preserved specimens from the Blattodea.
Research on Blattodea helps us understand the evolution of social behavior in insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a common household insect, member of Blattodea

Etymology

From Latin 'blatta' (an insect, especially a cockroach) and the taxonomic suffix '-odea'.

2. NOUN

/ blæˈtoʊdiə / (AmE), / blætəˈdiːə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder (sometimes considered an order) of insects that includes cockroaches.
taxonomic group including cockroaches

Examples

Scientists have identified nearly 4,600 species within Blattodea worldwide.
Blattodea exhibits a wide range of adaptations to diverse environments.
Taxonomists sometimes debate whether Blattodea should be considered a suborder or an order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a common insect in the order/suborder Blattodea

Etymology

From New Latin, after Latin 'blatta' (cockroach) and Greek 'eidos' (form).
