sheltered

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɛltərd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛltəd / (BrE)
protected from danger, difficulties, or bad weather; having shelter or cover
protected from danger or bad weather

Examples

We anchored the boat in a sheltered bay to avoid the storm.
The children played in the sheltered courtyard during the heavy rain.
They enjoyed a picnic in a sheltered spot away from the wind.

Synset Examples

a sheltered harbor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exposed - not protected from danger or the elements

Etymology

From Middle English 'sheltren' (to take shelter) and 'shelter', likely from Old English 'scield' (shield).
