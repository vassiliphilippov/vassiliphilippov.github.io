disability

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
A physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities, making it difficult to perform certain tasks.
physical or mental impairment

Examples

Many buildings have ramps to ensure that people with a disability have access.
She was born with a hearing disability, but she communicates fluently using sign language.
After the accident, his disability made it difficult for him to continue his job as a construction worker.

Synset Examples

reading disability
hearing impairment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impairment - a loss of function or ability
  • Antonym: ability - the capacity to do something; talent or skill

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + ability (from Latin habilitas ‘ability’).
