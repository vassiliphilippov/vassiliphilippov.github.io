Cypripedium calceolus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌprɪpiːdiəm ˌkælsiˈoʊləs / (AmE), / sɪˌprɪpiːdiəm ˌkælsiˈəʊləs / (BrE)
A species of orchid with maroon to purple-brown flowers and a distinctive yellow lip, found in Europe, North America, and Japan; commonly known as the lady's slipper orchid.
a species of lady's slipper orchid

Examples

The rare Cypripedium calceolus is a protected species throughout much of Europe.
During our hike, we were thrilled to spot a blooming Cypripedium calceolus in the forest.
Botanists monitor populations of Cypripedium calceolus due to its endangered status in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's slipper - any orchid of the genus Cypripedium or related genera with a slipper-shaped lip

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cypripedium' from 'Cypris' (Venus) + 'pedilon' (slipper); 'calceolus' means 'little shoe' in Latin, referring to the shape of the lip.
