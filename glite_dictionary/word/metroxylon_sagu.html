Metroxylon sagu

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtroʊˈzaɪlɒn ˈsɑːɡuː / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrəʊˈzaɪlən ˈsɑːɡuː / (BrE)
a Malaysian palm whose trunk pith yields sago, a starch used as a food thickener and in fabric production, native from Malaya to Fiji
Malaysian palm yielding sago starch

Examples

The Metroxylon sagu is cultivated extensively for its starchy trunk, which provides a staple food for many communities.
Traditional methods for harvesting sago from the Metroxylon sagu have been passed down through generations.
The widespread distribution of Metroxylon sagu spans from Malaysia to islands in the western Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sago palm - a palm tree from which sago starch is obtained

Etymology

From the genus name Metroxylon (Greek metra 'womb' + xylon 'wood') and Malay sagu ('sago palm'); scientific botanical nomenclature.
