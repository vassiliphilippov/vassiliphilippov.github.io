quill-driver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪl ˌdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkwɪl ˌdraɪvə / (BrE)
(historical, sometimes humorous) A person whose occupation is writing, especially in a routine or clerical way; a writer or scribe. Rarely, a tool used for writing, such as a pen.
person who writes; sometimes, a writing tool
archaic

Examples

The company hired another quill-driver to handle the mountain of paperwork.
In Victorian novels, a quill-driver often appears as a minor clerical character.
She prided herself on not being a mere quill-driver, but a true author.

Synset Examples

The artist's quill-driver was a cherished tool.
The quill-driver made the writing process much smoother.
She prefers a quill-driver for its precision and control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribe - a person who copies out documents or writes
  • Synonym: author - a writer of books, articles, or documents
  • Synonym: writer - a person who writes (books, articles, etc.)

Etymology

From 'quill' (a feather used as a pen) + 'driver' (one who operates), originally referring to clerks using quill pens.
