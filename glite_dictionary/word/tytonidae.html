Tytonidae

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈtɑːnɪdiː / (AmE), / tɪˈtɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of owls comprising only the barn owls, distinguished by their heart-shaped faces and cosmopolitan distribution.
family of barn owls (taxonomy)

Examples

The Tytonidae are known for their distinct heart-shaped faces and nocturnal habits.
Unlike other owl families, Tytonidae includes only the barn owls.
Researchers study the adaptation of Tytonidae to different environments around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'tyto' (owl) and the family suffix '-idae'.
