colloquial

1. ADJ

/ kəˈloʊkwiəl / (AmE), / kəˈləʊkwiəl / (BrE)
(of language) used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary.
informal conversational language

Examples

In academic writing, avoid using colloquial expressions such as 'gonna' or 'wanna'.
Her speech was filled with colloquial phrases that made it feel warm and relatable.
Many dictionaries clearly mark which terms are colloquial and which are formal.

Synset Examples

wrote her letters in a colloquial style
the broken syntax and casual enunciation of conversational English

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formal - language that is very correct and suitable for official or important occasions
  • Synonym: conversational - appropriate to an informal spoken exchange

Etymology

From Latin 'colloquium' (conversation), from 'col-' (together) + 'loqui' (to speak).
