varlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːr.lət / (AmE), / ˈvɑː.lɪt / (BrE)
In medieval times, a youth who served as a knight's attendant as the first stage of training for knighthood.
knight's attendant in training (medieval)
archaic

Examples

In his youth, the prince worked as a varlet to learn the art of chivalry.
The varlet cleaned his master's armor carefully before every battle.
Stories of a faithful varlet often appear in medieval literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: page - a boy serving as a knight's attendant in training for knighthood

Etymology

From Old French *vaslet* (servant, young man), which also gave rise to the English word 'valet'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑrlət / (AmE), / ˈvɑːlɪt / (BrE)
an untrustworthy or dishonest person; a rogue or scoundrel (now archaic)
dishonest person; scoundrel (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The knight scorned the varlet for his treacherous behavior.
In the play, the scheming varlet tricks the hero with lies and flattery.
Once trusted, he revealed himself as nothing more than a varlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person

Etymology

From Old French 'vaslet' (squire, young man), diminutive of 'vassal.'
