feeble

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːbəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːbl / (BrE)
lacking physical strength, energy, or effectiveness; weak or inadequate.
weak; lacking strength or effectiveness

Examples

The patient spoke in a feeble whisper, barely audible to the nurse.
His feeble attempt to lift the box only made his friends chuckle.
After running for hours, her legs felt feeble and shaky.

Synset Examples

damning with faint praise
faint resistance
feeble efforts
a feeble voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking the power to perform physically demanding tasks
  • Antonym: strong - having power and energy, physically or figuratively

Etymology

From Old French 'feble', from Latin 'flebilis' (lamentable), from 'flere' (to weep).
