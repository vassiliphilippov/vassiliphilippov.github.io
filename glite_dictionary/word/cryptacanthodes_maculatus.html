Cryptacanthodes maculatus

1. NOUN

/ krɪp.təˌkænˈθoʊ.diːz ˌmæk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (AmE), / krɪp.təˌkænˈθəʊ.diːz ˌmak.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
A species of eellike bottom-dwelling fish found in the Atlantic Ocean, known for its large, almost vertical mouth.
Atlantic eellike bottom fish

Examples

The Cryptacanthodes maculatus is rarely seen due to its preference for muddy Atlantic seabeds.
Researchers observed a Cryptacanthodes maculatus using its large mouth to capture small invertebrates.
Fishermen occasionally catch Cryptacanthodes maculatus when trawling near the ocean floor.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'akantha' (thorn); 'maculatus' is Latin for 'spotted'.
