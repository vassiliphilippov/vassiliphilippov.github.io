thirteen

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɜrˈtin / (AmE), / ˌθɜːˈtiːn / (BrE)
being one more than twelve; the number 13.
number: one more than twelve

Examples

He finished thirteen chapters in a single night.
There were thirteen people at the dinner table.
She received thirteen birthday cards from her friends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twelve - the number 12
  • Degree Higher: fourteen - the number 14

Etymology

From Old English thrēotīene, from thrēo (three) + tīene (ten).

2. NUM

/ ˌθɜrˈtin / (AmE), / ˌθɜːˈtiːn / (BrE)
the number that is equal to twelve plus one; 13.
the number 13

Examples

She celebrated her thirteenth birthday with a big party.
There are thirteen cards in each suit of a standard deck.
Only thirteen students signed up for the course this year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twelve - the number 12
  • Degree Higher: fourteen - the number 14

Etymology

From Old English thrītīene, a compound of three and ten.
