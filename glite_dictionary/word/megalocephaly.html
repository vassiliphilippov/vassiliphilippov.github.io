megalocephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələ(ʊ)ˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the head is abnormally large, without increased intracranial pressure and with symmetrical overgrowth.
abnormally large head (medical)
medical

Examples

The genetic disorder was associated with megalocephaly and delayed development.
Doctors confirmed that the infant's megalocephaly was not due to hydrocephalus.
Research is ongoing to understand the causes of megalocephaly in some children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macrocephaly - the condition of having an abnormally large head
  • Antonym: microcephaly - the condition of having an abnormally small head

Etymology

From Greek 'megalo-' (large) + 'kephalē' (head).
