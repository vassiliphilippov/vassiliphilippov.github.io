talented

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæləntɪd / (AmE), / ˈtæl.ən.tɪd / (BrE)
Having a natural ability to do something well; endowed with talent.
having natural ability or talent

Examples

She is a talented musician who started playing piano at the age of four.
Many talented young artists displayed their work at the gallery.
The talented chef quickly became known for her creative dishes.

Synset Examples

a gifted writer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gifted - having exceptional talent or natural ability
  • Antonym: untalented - lacking natural ability or talent

Etymology

From 'talent' (Middle English, from Old French, from Latin 'talentum'), with the adjective suffix '-ed.'
