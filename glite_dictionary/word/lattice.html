lattice

1. NOUN

/ ˈlætɪs / (AmE), / ˈlætɪs / (BrE)
an arrangement of points, particles, or objects in a regular, repeating pattern in two or three dimensions, often seen in mathematics, physics, or crystallography
regular repeating pattern of points or objects

Examples

In solid-state physics, a crystal can be described as atoms arranged in a lattice.
The researchers analyzed the lattice structure of the new material to determine its properties.
Many mathematical problems involve studying the points of a lattice in two or three dimensions.

Etymology

From Old French 'lattis', from 'latte' (lath), ultimately from Proto-Germanic.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlætɪs / (AmE), / ˈlætɪs / (BrE)
a small opening with a gridded or crisscrossed framework, often used in doors or partitions, through which business or communication is conducted
small gridded opening for transactions

Examples

She spoke to the clerk through the lattice in the wooden door.
The teller slid the documents through a small lattice for customers' privacy and security.
A wrought iron lattice separated the visitors from the officials during business hours.

Etymology

From Old French *latis* (lathwork, trellis), from *lath* (a thin strip of wood).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪs / (BrE)
A structure made from strips of wood, metal, or other materials crossing each other in a regular, often decorative pattern.
crisscrossed framework of strips

Examples

The garden was surrounded by a beautiful lattice covered in climbing roses.
She chose a pie with a lattice crust, which looked both decorative and delicious.
The sunlight filtered through the lattice, creating patterns on the patio floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grid - network of evenly spaced horizontal and vertical lines
  • Synonym: mesh - material made of a network of wire or thread

Etymology

From Old French *lattis*, from *latte* (lath, slat).
