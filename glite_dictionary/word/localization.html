localization

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining the exact position or location of something
determining the location of something

Examples

Accurate localization of the earthquake epicenter is crucial for emergency response teams.
The doctor explained that the localization of the tumor would help plan the surgery.
GPS technology allows for the localization of delivery vehicles in real time.

Synset Examples

he got a good fix on the target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identification - the process of recognizing or establishing what something is

Etymology

From French localisation, from localiser (to localize) + -ation; ultimately from Latin locus (place).

2. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle that specific functions are associated with particular, limited regions of an organ, especially in the brain or body
assignment of functions to specific body parts
medical

Examples

The localization of speech production in Broca’s area is a classic finding in neuroscience.
Modern imaging techniques have provided strong evidence supporting the localization of sensory functions in the brain.
Early theories about localization of cognitive abilities led to the identification of specialized brain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lateralization - the localization of function to one side of the brain or body
  • Antonym: diffusion - the opposite principle, with no specific functional regions

Etymology

From French 'localisation', from 'localiser' (to localize), ultimately from Latin 'locus' (place).
