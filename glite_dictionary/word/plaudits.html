plaudits

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɔːdɪts / (AmE), / ˈplɔːdɪts / (BrE)
enthusiastic praise or approval, often expressed publicly
enthusiastic praise or approval

Examples

The director received plaudits from critics for his innovative approach to the play.
Her performance earned her the plaudits of both the audience and the judges.
After the announcement, he enjoyed the plaudits of his supporters and colleagues.

Synset Examples

the book met with modest acclaim
he acknowledged the plaudits of the crowd
they gave him more eclat than he really deserved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaim - public praise or enthusiastic approval
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or finding fault

Etymology

From Latin 'plaudite' (imperative of 'plaudere', meaning 'to clap').
