conjointly

1. ADV

/ kənˈdʒɔɪntli / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɔɪntli / (BrE)
together with one or more other people, things, or actions; in combination; jointly.
together; in combination

Examples

The partners signed the contract conjointly to demonstrate mutual agreement.
These two issues must be addressed conjointly rather than separately.
The award will be received conjointly by both authors at the ceremony.

Synset Examples

our salaries collectively couldn't pay for the damage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jointly - together with others; sharing responsibility or activity

Etymology

From late Middle English, from Old French 'conjoint', from Latin 'conjunctus', past participle of 'conjungere' (to join together).
