maneuverability

1. NOUN

/ məˌnuːvərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / məˌnjuːvərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to move or be directed easily and effectively
ability to be maneuvered easily

Examples

One advantage of the new sports car is its exceptional maneuverability on narrow roads.
The maneuverability of the drone allows it to perform complex aerial stunts.
Safety regulations often require boats to demonstrate adequate maneuverability before being licensed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - the ability to move quickly and easily
  • Antonym: clumsiness - the quality of being awkward in movement

Etymology

From maneuver + -ability, meaning the ability to be maneuvered.
