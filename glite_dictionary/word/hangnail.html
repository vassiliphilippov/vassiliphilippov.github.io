hangnail

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌneɪl / (BrE)
A small, thin piece of torn skin at the edge or base of a fingernail or toenail, often causing pain and easily becoming infected.
small torn piece of skin by a nail

Examples

Be careful not to pull that hangnail, or it might become infected.
After gardening without gloves, she noticed a painful hangnail on her thumb.
Applying moisturizer regularly helps prevent hangnails in the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuticle - the layer of skin at the base of a fingernail or toenail

Etymology

From Middle English 'agnail', altered by folk etymology; from Old English 'angnægl' (painful spike of skin).
