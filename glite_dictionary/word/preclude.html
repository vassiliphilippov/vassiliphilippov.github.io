to preclude

1. VERB

/ prɪˈklud / (AmE), / prɪˈkluːd / (BrE)
to prevent something from happening or make it impossible, especially by taking action in advance
prevent; make impossible in advance

Examples

The heavy rain will preclude us from playing tennis today.
His criminal record may preclude him from getting certain jobs.
Careful planning can preclude many problems before they arise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening
  • Antonym: to allow - to permit something to happen

Etymology

From Latin praecludere (to shut off, hinder), from prae- (before) + claudere (to close).

2. VERB

/ prɪˈklud / (AmE), / prɪˈkluːd / (BrE)
to prevent something from happening or make it impossible; to rule out
to make impossible; prevent

Examples

His lack of experience may preclude him from getting the job.
Heavy rain can preclude outdoor activities for several days.
The agreement does not preclude future negotiations between the two countries.

Synset Examples

My sense of tact forbids an honest answer
Your role in the projects precludes your involvement in the competitive project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening
  • Antonym: to allow - to permit something to happen

Etymology

From Latin praeccludere, from prae- ‘before’ + claudere ‘to shut’.
