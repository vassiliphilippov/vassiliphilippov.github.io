Bertholletia excelsa

1. NOUN

/ bɝˌθɑːˈleɪti.ə ɪkˈsɛlsə / (AmE), / bɜːˌθɒlˈliːtɪə ɪkˈsɛlsə / (BrE)
A tall South American tree that produces the Brazil nut.
tree producing Brazil nuts

Examples

The Bertholletia excelsa grows in the Amazon rainforest and can reach heights of 50 meters.
Researchers are studying the ecological importance of Bertholletia excelsa in South America.
The nuts harvested from Bertholletia excelsa are widely exported around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazil nut tree - another term for Bertholletia excelsa

Etymology

From New Latin, named after French chemist Claude Louis Berthollet.
