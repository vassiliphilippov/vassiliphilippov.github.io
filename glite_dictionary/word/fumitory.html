fumitory

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuː.məˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈfjuː.mɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
A delicate European herb with greyish leaves and spikes of purplish flowers, formerly used medicinally.
European herb with purplish flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with fumitory, adding a hint of purple to the landscape.
Historically, herbalists valued fumitory for its supposed medicinal properties.
She picked a sprig of fumitory and pressed it between the pages of her book.

Etymology

From Latin *fumus terrae* (smoke of the earth), via Old French *fumeterre*.
