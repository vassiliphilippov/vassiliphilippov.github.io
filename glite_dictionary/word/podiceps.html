Podiceps

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.dɪ.sɛps / (AmE), / ˈpɒd.ɪ.sɛps / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic diving birds in the family Podicipedidae, known as grebes.
a genus of aquatic birds (grebes)

Examples

Several species in the genus Podiceps can be found across Europe and North America.
The great crested grebe, a member of Podiceps, is known for its elaborate courtship display.
Ornithologists studied the plumage differences among Podiceps species in different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - an aquatic diving bird in the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From Latin 'podiceps' (podicis, 'rump' + pes, 'foot'), referring to the position of the legs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊdɪˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˈpɒdɪˌsɛps / (BrE)
A genus of birds that includes the typical grebes, found in the family Podicipedidae.
genus of typical grebes (birds)

Examples

The genus Podiceps comprises several species of grebes commonly found in freshwater habitats.
One well-known member of Podiceps is the great crested grebe.
Ornithologists study the breeding behaviors of birds within the Podiceps genus across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - a diving waterbird with lobed toes, in the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From Latin podiceps, meaning 'rump-footed' (from 'podici-' meaning 'rump' and '-pes' meaning 'foot'), referring to the rear position of the legs.
