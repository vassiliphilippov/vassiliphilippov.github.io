Phalaris arundinacea

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlɛərɪs ˌɑːrʌn.dɪˈneɪsiə / (AmE), / fəˌlɑːrɪs əˌrʌn.dɪˈniːsiə / (BrE)
A tall perennial grass with broad leaves, typically growing in wetlands and along ditches in Europe and North America.
wetland grass species

Examples

Phalaris arundinacea can spread quickly and dominate wetland habitats if not managed.
Many gardeners planted Phalaris arundinacea along the edge of their water features for erosion control.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Phalaris arundinacea in marsh restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed canary grass - Common name for Phalaris arundinacea

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Phalaris' from Greek phalaros (having a patch of white), and species name 'arundinacea' from Latin arundo (reed).
