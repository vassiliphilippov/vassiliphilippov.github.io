fierceness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪrsnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being intense, wild, or violent in character or action
quality of being fierce or intense

Examples

The fierceness of the wind knocked down several trees during the storm.
She admired the fierceness with which he defended his beliefs.
The fierceness of the competition pushed everyone to perform at their best.

Synset Examples

the storm's violence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - the use of physical force intended to hurt or damage
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being kind, calm, or soft

Etymology

From Middle English fer, from Old French fers, from Latin ferus (wild, untamed), plus the suffix -ness.
