overwhelming

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚˈwɛlmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlmɪŋ / (BrE)
very great in amount or intensity; so powerful that it is difficult to resist or cope with
very great in amount or intensity

Examples

The support from the community was overwhelming, leaving her speechless.
He felt an overwhelming sense of relief when the exam was over.
The overwhelming noise in the stadium made it hard to concentrate.

Synset Examples

politics is his consuming passion
overwhelming joy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intense - having strong or extreme force
  • Antonym: mild - not strong in effect or degree

Etymology

From the verb 'overwhelm', which comes from Middle English 'overwhelmen', from 'over-' + 'whelmen' (to overturn, cover up), of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɛlmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlmɪŋ / (BrE)
very great in amount or force, making it difficult to resist or handle; extremely strong or intense.
very strong or intense; difficult to resist

Examples

The evidence against him was overwhelming, leaving little doubt about his guilt.
She felt an overwhelming sense of relief when the exam was finally over.
The response to their charity appeal was overwhelming, far exceeding their expectations.

Synset Examples

an overpowering need for solitude
the temptation to despair may become overwhelming
an overwhelming majority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irresistible - too attractive or strong to be resisted
  • Antonym: minor - not important or strong; the opposite of overwhelming in degree
  • Synonym: powerful - having or capable of exerting great force

Etymology

From the verb 'overwhelm', formed from 'over-' and Middle English 'whelmen' (to turn upside down, engulf).
