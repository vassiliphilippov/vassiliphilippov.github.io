put-up

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʊt ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpʊt ˌʌp / (BrE)
(of a plan or action) secretly arranged or contrived for a particular purpose, often to deceive.
secretly arranged or contrived

Examples

The whole situation was a put-up affair from the beginning.
She suspected the contest was just a put-up to promote the sponsor.
Everyone realized later that the bet was a put-up designed to trick him.

Synset Examples

it was a put-up job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrived - deliberately created rather than arising naturally
