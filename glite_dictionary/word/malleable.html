malleable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæliəbl / (AmE), / ˈmalɪəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or their character) easily influenced or changed; adaptable.
easily influenced or changed

Examples

Children are often malleable and can easily absorb new ideas from their environment.
Her opinions are quite malleable, changing depending on who she's talking to.
The media can play a powerful role in shaping the malleable minds of the public.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intractable - not easily managed or influenced
  • Synonym: pliable - easily bent or influenced; flexible

Etymology

From Medieval Latin malleabilis, from Latin malleare (to hammer), from malleus (hammer).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmæliəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmalɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be easily shaped, bent, or hammered without breaking; easily influenced or changed.
easily shaped or influenced

Examples

Gold is a highly malleable metal, allowing it to be shaped into thin sheets.
Her opinions are still malleable and easily influenced by those around her.
The clay was so malleable that the children could sculpt it into any shape they wanted.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ductile - easily drawn out into a thin wire; pliable
  • Synonym: pliable - easily bent, flexible
  • Antonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible

Etymology

From Latin malleabilis (able to be hammered), from malleare (to hammer) and malleus (hammer).
