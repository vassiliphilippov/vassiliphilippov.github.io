Sonchus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɒŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as sow thistles, characterized by yellow flowers and spiny leaves.
genus of plants, sow thistles

Examples

The Sonchus species are often considered weeds in gardens due to their rapid growth.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several Sonchus varieties.
Many insects feed on the leaves of Sonchus plants in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow thistle - common name for Sonchus genus plants

Etymology

From Greek sonkhos (soft plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑn.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as sow thistles, with milky sap and often prickly leaves.
genus of flowering plants (sow thistles)

Examples

Many species within the Sonchus genus are considered common weeds in gardens and agricultural fields.
The leaves of Sonchus plants have a distinctive milky sap when broken.
Botanists have identified over 50 species belonging to the Sonchus genus worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow thistle - any plant of the genus Sonchus, with milky juice and prickly leaves

Etymology

From Greek sonchos (σονχος), the ancient name for sow thistle.
