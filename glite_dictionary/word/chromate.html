chromate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of chromic acid, typically containing the anion CrO₄²⁻
salt or ester of chromic acid

Examples

Potassium chromate is commonly used as a laboratory reagent.
The factory was fined for improper disposal of chromate waste.
Exposure to chromate compounds can pose serious health risks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dichromate - a salt containing the anion Cr₂O₇²⁻

Etymology

From chromium + -ate, denoting a salt or ester.
