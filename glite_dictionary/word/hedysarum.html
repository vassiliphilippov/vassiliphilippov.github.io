Hedysarum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛdɪˈsɛərəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːdɪˈsɑːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the pea family, native to northern temperate regions.
genus of herbaceous plants

Examples

The roots of Hedysarum are sometimes used in traditional medicine in parts of Asia.
Several species within the Hedysarum genus are known for their attractive pink or purple flowers.
Many botanists study Hedysarum because of its importance in grassland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin Hedysarum, derived from Ancient Greek hēdýsaron, a kind of leguminous plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛdɪˈsɛərəm / (AmE), / ˌhɛdɪˈsɛːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants native to north temperate regions, known for their compound leaves and often colorful flowers.
genus of temperate-region herbs

Examples

Several species within the Hedysarum genus are cultivated for their attractive flowers.
Botanists have studied the Hedysarum plants to understand their adaptation to cold climates.
The seeds of Hedysarum are an important food source for some animals in the wild.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēdysaron', an ancient name for a type of clover.
