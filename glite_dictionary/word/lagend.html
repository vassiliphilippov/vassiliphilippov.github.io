lagend

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdʒənd / (AmE), / ˈlændʒənd / (BrE)
goods or wreckage lying on the sea bed, attached to a buoy so that it can be recovered
sea bed goods attached to a buoy

Examples

Divers discovered lagend near the old shipwreck, marked by a weathered buoy.
Salvage crews used specialized equipment to recover the lagend from the ocean floor.
The ship's captain reported the lagend location to authorities for later retrieval.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flotsam - wreckage of a ship or its cargo floating on the sea
  • Synonym: jetsam - goods cast overboard and washed ashore
  • Synonym: derelict - abandoned ship or cargo at sea

Etymology

From legal French 'lagan' (goods lying at the bottom of the sea), related to 'jettison'.
