soupçon

1. NOUN

/ supˈsɒn / (AmE), / suːpˈsɒ̃ / (BrE)
A very small quantity of something; a slight trace or hint.
a very small amount; a hint

Examples

The recipe calls for a soupçon of nutmeg to enhance the flavor.
A soupçon of humor can make even serious conversations more enjoyable.
There was a soupçon of doubt in her voice when she agreed.

Synset Examples

this dish could use a touch of garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion; a very small amount
  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount of something

Etymology

From French 'soupçon' meaning 'suspicion, trace', from Latin 'suspectio'.
