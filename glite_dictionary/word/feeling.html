feeling

1. NOUN

/ ˈfilɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an intuitive or instinctive understanding of something, especially a particular subject or art
intuitive understanding of something

Examples

She has a natural feeling for languages and picks up new words easily.
His strong feeling for painting is evident in his work.
You need to develop a feeling for rhythm if you want to play the drums well.

Synset Examples

he had a great feeling for music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something immediately, without the need for conscious reasoning
  • Synonym: instinct - a natural tendency or ability to act or feel a certain way, often without conscious thought

Etymology

From Old English *fēlan* (to feel) + *-ing* suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the physical sensation experienced when something touches the skin, especially as perceived through the sense of touch
sensation from touching or being touched

Examples

She ran her fingers over the fabric, enjoying the soft feeling.
The rough feeling of the wall made it easy to find in the dark.
He disliked the sticky feeling left by the lotion on his hands.

Synset Examples

she likes the touch of silk on her skin
the surface had a greasy feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - the sense that allows perception through contact with the skin

Etymology

From feel + -ing; feel from Old English *fēlan* (to touch, perceive).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an emotional state or reaction; the experience of emotion or affect
an emotional state or reaction

Examples

She couldn’t hide the feeling of excitement that rushed over her.
He had a strange feeling that something was wrong.
The movie stirred deep feelings in everyone who watched it.

Synset Examples

she had a feeling of euphoria
he had terrible feelings of guilt
I disliked him and the feeling was mutual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotion - a strong feeling, such as joy, anger, or fear
  • Degree Lower: sensation - a physical feeling or perception resulting from something that happens to or comes into contact with the body
  • Degree Higher: passion - a very strong emotion, especially of love or anger

Etymology

From Old English *fēlan* (to touch, perceive), influenced by the verb 'feel'.
