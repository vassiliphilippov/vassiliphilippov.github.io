Gerrhonotus

1. NOUN

/ dʒerˈhəʊnətəs / (AmE), / dʒəˈhɒnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of anguid lizards commonly known as alligator lizards, native to North and Central America.
genus of alligator lizards

Examples

The Gerrhonotus lizards are often called alligator lizards because of their appearance.
Researchers have discovered a new species within the Gerrhonotus genus in Central America.
Unlike other lizards, Gerrhonotus is known for its armored, flexible body.

Etymology

From Greek gerrhon (wickerwork, shield) + notos (back), referring to the lizard's armored appearance.
