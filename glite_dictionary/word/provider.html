provider

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
a person or company that supplies a particular service or commodity
person or company supplying a service or commodity

Examples

The internet provider resolved the service outage within a few hours.
We recently switched to a new electricity provider for better rates.
Finding a reliable childcare provider is essential for many working parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplier - a person or organization that provides something needed such as a product or service

Etymology

From 'provide' (from Latin 'providere', to foresee, provide) + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
a person who supports others by supplying the necessities of life, such as food, shelter, or income
person who supplies necessities or support

Examples

After losing his job, Mark struggled with the pressure of being the family's sole provider.
Traditionally, the father was seen as the main provider in many households.
In single-parent families, one person must often act as both caretaker and provider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadwinner - main person who earns money to support a family

Etymology

From provide + -er, from Latin providere (to foresee, provide) + English agent suffix -er.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10505934-n
Synset POS: n
