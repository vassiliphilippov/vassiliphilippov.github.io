Mahabharata

1. PROPN

/ məˌhɑːbəˈrɑːtə / (AmE), / məˌhɑːbəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A sacred Sanskrit epic poem of ancient India, recounting the struggle between two groups of royal cousins and forming an important part of Hindu tradition.
ancient Indian epic poem

Examples

The Mahabharata is considered one of the longest epic poems in the world.
Many moral and philosophical lessons are found within the Mahabharata.
She learned about Indian mythology by reading parts of the Mahabharata in her literature class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ramayana - another major ancient Indian epic poem

Etymology

From Sanskrit महाभारतम् (Mahābhāratam), meaning 'the great story of the Bharata dynasty'.
