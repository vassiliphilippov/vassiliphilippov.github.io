blockage

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈblɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
an obstruction that prevents the normal flow of something, especially in a pipe, tube, or passage
an obstruction in a pipe or passage

Examples

The plumber discovered a blockage in the kitchen sink that needed urgent attention.
A stubborn blockage in the pipe caused the water to back up into the bathroom.
Doctors identified a blockage in the patient's artery during the examination.

Synset Examples

we had to call a plumber to clear out the blockage in the drainpipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or hinders passage
  • Antonym: clearance - the removal of an obstruction

Etymology

From block + -age, formed in English in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈblɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
The act or process of blocking or being blocked; an obstruction that prevents movement or flow.
the act or process of blocking

Examples

The pipe had a severe blockage that had to be cleared by a plumber.
Doctors discovered a blockage in her artery that required immediate treatment.
Heavy snow caused a blockage on the main highway for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or prevents passage
  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement through something

Etymology

From block + -age, formed in English in the 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈblɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a condition in which a passage, tube, or pipe is blocked or obstructed, preventing movement through it
obstruction of a passage or tube

Examples

Doctors removed the blockage in her artery to restore normal blood flow.
There was a blockage in the sink, causing the water to overflow.
The accident on the highway created a blockage that delayed traffic for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or closes up a passage; an obstacle
  • Antonym: clearance - the removal of an obstruction to allow free passage

Etymology

Formed from 'block' + '-age' (suffix forming nouns).
