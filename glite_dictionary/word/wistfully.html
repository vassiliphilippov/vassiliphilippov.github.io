wistfully

1. ADV

/ ˈwɪstfəli / (AmE), / ˈwɪstfəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a feeling of longing or regretful yearning.
with a sense of longing or regret

Examples

She gazed wistfully out the window, remembering her childhood home.
He smiled wistfully when he spoke of his days at university.
The old man looked wistfully at the group of children playing in the park.

Synset Examples

his sister would have looked beautiful in that dress, he thought wistfully, just like an angel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wistful - showing a feeling of vague longing or regret

Etymology

From 'wistful' + '-ly'; 'wistful' from obsolete 'wist' (intent, intent look).
