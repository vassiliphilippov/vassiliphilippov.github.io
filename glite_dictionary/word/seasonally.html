seasonally

1. ADV

/ ˈsiːzənəli / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəli / (BrE)
In a way that depends on, or varies with, the season of the year.
in relation to the season

Examples

Tourist numbers in the town fluctuate seasonally, peaking during the summer months.
The farm hires extra workers seasonally to help with the harvest.
Energy consumption tends to increase seasonally during the colder winter months.

Synset Examples

prices are seasonally adjusted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annually - occurring or done once every year

Etymology

From 'season' + adverbial suffix '-ally'.
