suggestively

1. ADV

/ səgˈdʒɛstɪvli / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛstɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that implies something without stating it directly, often with a sexual or provocative undertone.
in a manner that implies or hints at something

Examples

She leaned closer and smiled suggestively, making her intentions clear.
He winked suggestively after telling the joke.
She spoke suggestively, leaving no doubt about what she meant.

Synset Examples

she smiled suggestively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocatively - in a way that provokes or arouses, often sexual interest
  • Antonym: innocently - in a way lacking any sexual implication

Etymology

From 'suggestive' + '-ly'. 'Suggestive' from Latin 'suggestus', past participle of 'suggere' (to bring up, suggest).
