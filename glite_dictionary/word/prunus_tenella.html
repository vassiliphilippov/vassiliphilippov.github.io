Prunus tenella

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs tɪˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs tɪˈniː.lə / (BrE)
A small Asiatic shrub of the rose family, cultivated for its attractive rosy red flowers; also known as dwarf Russian almond.
Asiatic shrub with rosy red flowers

Examples

Prunus tenella is a popular choice for ornamental gardens due to its beautiful springtime blossoms.
Gardeners often plant Prunus tenella along borders to add a splash of rosy red color.
The delicate flowers of Prunus tenella attract many pollinators in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwarf Russian almond - Common name for Prunus tenella
  • Degree Lower: shrub - A small to medium-sized woody plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' (plum tree) and 'tenella' (delicate, tender).
