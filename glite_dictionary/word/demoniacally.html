demoniacally

1. ADV

/ dɪˌmoʊniˈækəli / (AmE), / dɪˌməʊnɪˈækəli / (BrE)
In a wild, frenzied, or violently agitated manner, as if possessed by a demon or evil spirit.
in a wild, frenzied manner

Examples

She screamed demoniacally as the thunder crashed overhead.
The composer pounded the piano keys demoniacally during the climactic passage.
His eyes glinted demoniacally in the flickering candlelight, sending shivers down my spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonically - in a manner characteristic of a demon

Etymology

Formed from 'demoniac' (relating to a demon or evil spirit) plus the adverbial suffix '-ally'.
