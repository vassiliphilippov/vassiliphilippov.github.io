abstracted

1. ADJ

/ əbˈstræktɪd / (AmE), / əbˈstraktɪd / (BrE)
thinking deeply or about something else so that you do not notice what is happening around you; lost in thought.
lost in thought, preoccupied

Examples

She gave an abstracted smile, clearly thinking about something else entirely.
He walked through the park in an abstracted mood, hardly noticing anyone around him.
During the meeting, her abstracted expression suggested her mind was elsewhere.

Synset Examples

an absent stare
an absentminded professor
the scatty glancing quality of a hyperactive but unfocused intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because of other thoughts
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention
  • Synonym: absentminded - frequently forgetting things because you are thinking of something else

Etymology

From Latin 'abstractus' (drawn away), past participle of 'abstrahere'.
