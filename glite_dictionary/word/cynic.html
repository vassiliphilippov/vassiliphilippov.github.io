Cynic

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪk / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Greek philosophical school that believed virtue is the only true good and that self-control is the essence of virtue
an ancient Greek philosopher of the Cynic school

Examples

Diogenes was perhaps the most famous Cynic in ancient Greece.
The Cynic philosophers rejected material wealth in favor of simplicity and virtue.
She wrote her dissertation on the teachings of the Cynic school.

Etymology

From Greek *Kynikos* (dog-like), referring to the philosophical school founded by Antisthenes, a pupil of Socrates.
