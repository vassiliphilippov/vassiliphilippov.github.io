Dalbergia cearensis

1. NOUN

/ dalˈbɜr.dʒi.ə si.əˈrɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / dalˈbɜː.dʒi.ə si.əˈrɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A small Brazilian tree valued for its fine, purplish cabinet wood, also known as kingwood.
Brazilian tree yielding valuable cabinet wood

Examples

The rare Dalbergia cearensis is highly prized for its deep purple kingwood used in fine furniture.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of Dalbergia cearensis due to illegal logging.
Many antique cabinets feature inlays of Dalbergia cearensis because of its rich color and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingwood - a dense, purplish wood obtained from Dalbergia cearensis

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus Dalbergia (after Swedish botanist Nils Dalberg) + specific epithet from Ceará, a state in Brazil.
