Macoun

1. NOUN

/ məˈkun / (AmE), / məˈkaʊn / (BrE)
a variety of juicy, late-ripening apple similar to the McIntosh, popular for eating fresh.
a variety of juicy, late-ripening apple

Examples

I bought a bag of Macoun apples at the farmers' market this weekend.
The Macoun is prized for its sweet, juicy flavor and crisp texture.
Many bakers prefer Macoun apples for making pies in the fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: McIntosh - a popular variety of apple, crisp and tart

Etymology

Named after W. T. Macoun (1869–1933), a Canadian horticulturist.
