stopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈstɒpə / (BrE)
a remark during a conversation to which there is no polite or appropriate reply
conversation-ending remark

Examples

His last comment was a real stopper that left everyone in awkward silence.
She unintentionally delivered a stopper when she revealed the surprise too early.
The joke fell flat and turned into a stopper for the entire meeting.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpər / (AmE), / ˈstɒpə / (BrE)
(bridge) a high card that can win a trick in a particular suit, preventing opponents from taking further tricks in that suit.
high card in bridge that stops a suit

Examples

I need a stopper in clubs before I can safely bid no trump.
She played her stopper in hearts, allowing the partnership to win the trick.
Without a stopper in spades, our contract is vulnerable to attack in that suit.

Synset Examples

if my partner has a spade stopper I can bid no trump

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocker - a card that prevents opponents from running a suit (card games)

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɑː.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɒp.ə / (BrE)
An event or performance in a show that is so extraordinary or impressive that it prompts such an enthusiastic response from the audience that the performance must pause.
striking show event causing a pause

Examples

Her solo dance was a real stopper, earning a standing ovation from the crowd.
The comedian's joke turned out to be a stopper, halting the show with laughter.
That song was such a stopper that the band had to wait for applause to die down before continuing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showstopper - A performance or act that draws such applause that the show is temporarily stopped
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06905397-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈstɑpɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpə / (BrE)
to close or seal (a container) with a stopper or something serving as a stopper
to seal with a stopper

Examples

He stoppered the decanter and set it back on the shelf.
After pouring the perfume, she carefully stoppered the bottle.
They stoppered the test tubes to avoid contamination.

Synset Examples

She stoppered the wine bottle
The mothers stoppered their babies' mouths with pacifiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to close tightly or secure as to prevent leakage or entry

Etymology

From the noun 'stopper', derived from stop + -er.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpər / (AmE), / ˈstɒpə / (BrE)
an object used to block or seal a hole, especially in a bottle or container, to prevent liquid or gas from escaping
object that tightly blocks a hole

Examples

The bottle had a cork stopper to keep the wine fresh.
She pulled out the stopper and poured herself a glass of water.
Make sure the stopper fits tightly to prevent any leaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plug - an object used to close off a hole or opening, especially for preventing passage of fluid
  • Synonym: cap - a cover to close the top of a container

Etymology

From stop + -er, meaning 'one who or that which stops.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
