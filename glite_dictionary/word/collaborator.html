collaborator

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪtəɹ / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who works jointly with others, especially in an intellectual or creative activity
person who works jointly with others

Examples

She became a trusted collaborator on the research project, contributing valuable ideas and expertise.
The two artists have been frequent collaborators on exhibitions over the years.
He sought a collaborator to help write the script for the documentary.

Synset Examples

the musician and the librettist were collaborators
sexual partners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - a person who takes part in an undertaking with another

Etymology

From Latin collaborare (to work together), from com- (together) + laborare (to work).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlæb.əˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlæb.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who works with an enemy occupying force, especially during a war, often regarded as a traitor to their own country
someone aiding an enemy force

Examples

During the occupation, she was accused of being a collaborator who passed information to the enemy.
After the war, many alleged collaborators faced public trials for their actions.
He strongly denied being a collaborator and insisted that he helped the resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays their country or friends
  • Antonym: patriot - a person who vigorously supports their country and is prepared to defend it

Etymology

From Latin collaborare (to work together), later acquiring a negative sense in World War II.

3. NOUN

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who helps others, especially in secret or in a questionable or criminal way, such as assisting in a plot
person who assists in a plot

Examples

The police arrested the main suspect and his collaborator after uncovering their plan.
Rumors spread that there was a collaborator within the organization leaking information.
The spy trusted his collaborator to provide him with the secret documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • Synonym: conspirator - someone involved in a conspiracy
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who opposes or is against a plan or plot

Etymology

From Latin collaborare (to work together), via late 19th century English.
