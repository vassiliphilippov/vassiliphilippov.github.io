hoummos

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːməs / (AmE), / ˈhʊm.ɒs / (BrE)
A thick, creamy spread made from mashed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, commonly eaten as a dip or spread, especially in Middle Eastern cuisine.
spread made with chickpeas and tahini

Examples

We served fresh vegetables with hoummos as a healthy appetizer.
She bought some pita bread to dip in the hoummos for lunch.
Many Middle Eastern restaurants offer homemade hoummos on their menus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hummus - alternative spelling; spread made from chickpeas and tahini

Etymology

From Arabic ḥummuṣ (chickpeas).
