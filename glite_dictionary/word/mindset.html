mindset

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪndˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndˌsɛt / (BrE)
a habitual or characteristic way of thinking that influences how a person interprets and responds to situations
way of thinking or attitude

Examples

Her positive mindset helped her overcome many challenges at work.
Traveling abroad changed his mindset about different cultures.
The company aims to foster an innovative mindset among its employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attitude - a settled way of thinking or feeling about something
  • Synonym: outlook - a person's general attitude toward life
  • Antonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience

Etymology

From 'mind' + 'set'; first used in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪndˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndˌsɛt / (BrE)
a habitual or characteristic way of thinking that influences how you interpret and respond to situations
way of thinking; mental attitude

Examples

Her positive mindset helped her overcome many obstacles in life.
Teachers play a crucial role in shaping a student's mindset toward learning.
Changing your mindset can lead to greater success and happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attitude - a settled way of thinking or feeling about something
  • Synonym: outlook - a person's general attitude or way of thinking about life

Etymology

Formed from 'mind' + 'set', first used in mid-20th century.
