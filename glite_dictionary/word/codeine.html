codeine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌdin / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdiːn / (BrE)
An alkaloid derived from opium, used as a medicine to relieve pain and suppress coughing.
pain-relieving and cough-suppressing drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed codeine to manage her persistent cough.
Some people can experience drowsiness after taking codeine for pain relief.
Medications containing codeine are regulated in many countries due to potential misuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphine - a powerful opiate pain-relieving drug
  • Antonym: paracetamol - a non-opioid pain reliever and fever reducer

Etymology

From French 'codéine', from Greek 'kōdeia' (poppy head).
