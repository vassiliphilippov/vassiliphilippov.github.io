Prosopis pubescens

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊpɪs pjuˈbɛsənz / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpɪs pjuːˈbɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, known for its spirally twisted seed pods; also called screwbean mesquite.
shrub/small tree with twisted pods, aka screwbean mesquite

Examples

The Prosopis pubescens thrives in arid regions where water is scarce.
Local wildlife often feed on the seeds produced by the Prosopis pubescens.
Researchers are studying how Prosopis pubescens adapts to drought conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screwbean mesquite - common name for Prosopis pubescens

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Prosopis' and species name 'pubescens' meaning 'hairy' in Latin.
