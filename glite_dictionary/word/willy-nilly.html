willy-nilly

1. ADV

/ ˌwɪliˈnɪli / (AmE), / ˌwɪliˈnɪli / (BrE)
In a random, haphazard, or disorderly way; without order or planning.
in a random or disorderly manner

Examples

Papers were spread willy-nilly across the desk after the wind blew in from the open window.
Tourists left empty bottles and wrappers willy-nilly on the beach, making it look untidy.
After the announcement, the crowd dispersed willy-nilly in all directions, with no sense of order.

Synset Examples

the houses were randomly scattered
bullets were fired into the crowd at random

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - in a way that happens without order or plan
  • Synonym: haphazardly - in a manner lacking any obvious principle of organization
  • Antonym: systematically - done according to a fixed plan or system

Etymology

Possibly from reduplication of 'willing' and 'nilling' (wanting and not wanting), meaning 'whether one wants to or not'; later extended to random or disorderly behavior.
