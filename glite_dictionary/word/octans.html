Octans

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑk.tænz / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tænz / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the southern sky that contains the south celestial pole
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The constellation Octans is visible only in the far southern latitudes.
Navigators use Octans to help locate the south celestial pole.
Unlike Orion, Octans is not easily recognized by its stars.

Etymology

From Latin 'octans' meaning 'the eighth part of a circle' or 'octant', reflecting its resemblance to an octant.
