Salix lucida

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks luˈsiːdə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ljuːˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A species of North American willow shrub distinguished by its shiny, lance-shaped leaves; commonly known as the shining willow.
a North American willow shrub

Examples

The riverbank was lined with Salix lucida, their glossy leaves shimmering in the sunlight.
Botanists studied the habitat requirements of Salix lucida in wetland environments.
Gardeners often cultivate Salix lucida for its attractive, shiny foliage and resilience.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning 'willow', and 'lucida' meaning 'shining' or 'bright'.
