tabby

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæbi / (AmE), / ˈtabi / (BrE)
A female cat.
female cat

Examples

My grandmother's old tabby has been a loyal companion for years.
The shelter currently has several tabbies looking for new homes.
Although the tabby was shy at first, she soon warmed up to her new family.

Etymology

From French *tabis* (a type of silk taffeta), which later referred to striped cloth resembling the patterns on tabby cats.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæbi / (AmE), / ˈtæbi / (BrE)
having a coat of fur marked with streaks, spots, or patches of color, especially grey or brown, used especially of cats
furry pattern marked with stripes or spots (esp. for cats)

Examples

The rescue shelter had a beautiful tabby kitten with striking green eyes.
Her favorite childhood pet was a tabby cat that loved to sleep in the sun.
We adopted a tabby from the shelter, and his patterned fur makes him stand out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped - having stripes or bands of color

Etymology

From French 'tabis', meaning a rich watered silk, and later used to describe the striped appearance, then applied to cats' coats.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01792057-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæbi / (AmE), / ˈtæbi / (BrE)
a cat, especially a female, with a grey or tawny coat marked with stripes, spots, or swirling patterns, often mottled with black
a cat with a striped or mottled coat

Examples

Her old tabby curls up on the sunny windowsill every afternoon.
The shelter has several tabby kittens available for adoption.
I used to have a tabby that loved to chase butterflies in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - a domesticated feline animal

Etymology

Probably from French *tabis* (striped silk taffeta), referring to the coat's appearance.

Source: WordNet
