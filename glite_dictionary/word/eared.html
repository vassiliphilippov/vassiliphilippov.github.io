eared

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪrd / (AmE), / ˈɪəd / (BrE)
(of an object, especially a book or its pages) worn or shabby from overuse, or having corners turned down.
worn or with turned-down corners (esp. of pages)

Examples

The old novel had eared pages from being read so many times.
She pulled a eared envelope from her bag, its corners obviously bent.
The librarian discarded eared and tattered books from the collection.

Synset Examples

a somewhat dog-eared duke … a bit run down
an old book with dog-eared pages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog-eared - (of a page) having the corners turned down, worn
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪrd / (AmE), / ɪəd / (BrE)
having ears or appendages resembling ears, or having ears of a specified kind (usually used in combination)
having ears or ear-like appendages

Examples

The eared seal is distinguished from the true seal by its visible external ears.
Many children enjoy drawing eared rabbits and other animals in their notebooks.
The explorer spotted a white-eared opossum during his expedition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: earless - lacking ears or ear-like appendages

Etymology

Formed by combining 'ear' with the suffix '-ed' meaning 'having'.
