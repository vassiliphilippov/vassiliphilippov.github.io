barely

1. ADV

/ ˈbɛrli / (AmE), / ˈbɛːli / (BrE)
Almost not; only just.
almost not; only just

Examples

We could barely see the road through the heavy fog.
She had barely enough money to pay her rent this month.
He barely passed the exam, but he was relieved nonetheless.

Synset Examples

he hardly ever goes fishing
he was scarce sixteen years old
they scarcely ever used the emergency generator
I can hardly hear what she is saying
she barely seemed to notice him
we were so far back in the theater we could barely read the subtitles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardly - almost not; barely
  • Synonym: scarcely - only just; almost not

Etymology

From Old French 'bare', from Latin 'barus' (bare, naked), plus adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈbɛrli / (AmE), / ˈbɛəli / (BrE)
only a very short time before a particular moment; by the smallest possible margin in time
only a moment before; almost not

Examples

She barely arrived at the station before the train left.
I had barely finished my sentence when the phone rang.
We barely knew him before he moved away.

Synset Examples

we hardly knew them
had scarcely rung the bell when the door flew open
would have scarce arrived before she would have found some excuse to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarcely - only just; almost not
  • Synonym: hardly - almost not or only a short time before

Etymology

From Old French 'bare', from Latin 'barus', meaning 'bare', plus the adverbial suffix -ly.

3. ADV

/ ˈbɛrli / (AmE), / ˈbɛəli / (BrE)
only just; by a very small margin; almost not.
only just, by a small margin

Examples

She barely had enough time to finish her assignment before the deadline.
The patient was barely conscious after the operation.
He barely touched his dinner because he felt unwell.

Synset Examples

I only just caught the bus
he finished the marathon in just under 3 hours
it was barely 5 a.m.
the network has barely 5 percent of viewers
the batter just missed being hit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarcely - only just; almost not
  • Synonym: hardly - almost not; with difficulty

Etymology

From Old French *bare* (bare) + -ly, or from bare + -ly, meaning 'without covering, just'.

Source: WordNet
