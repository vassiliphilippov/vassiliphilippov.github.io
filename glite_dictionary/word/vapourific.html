vapourific

1. ADJ

/ ˌveɪ.pjəˈrɪf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌveɪ.pjʊəˈrɪf.ɪk / (BrE)
resembling or having the characteristics of vapor; vaporous
resembling or characteristic of vapor

Examples

The early morning haze gave the landscape a vapourific appearance.
Her dreams were filled with vapourific images that faded upon waking.
A vapourific mist settled on the fields after the rain.

Synset Examples

vaporous clouds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaporous - having the characteristics of vapor; indistinct or insubstantial

Etymology

From 'vapour' + Latin suffix '-ificus' (making, producing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌveɪpərˈɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌveɪpjʊˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of being converted into vapor by heat or volatilized.
capable of being volatilized

Examples

The chemical was found to be highly vapourific when exposed to heat.
Engineers tested the vapourific properties of the substance under various temperatures.
This solvent is vapourific and should be used in a well-ventilated area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volatile - easily evaporated at normal temperatures

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam) + '-ificus' (making), meaning 'making vapor'.
