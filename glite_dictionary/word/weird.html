Weird

1. NOUN

/ wɪrd / (AmE), / wɪəd / (BrE)
Fate personified; in old legend and literature, any one of the three Fates or Weird Sisters.
one of the Fates; a Weird Sister
archaic

Examples

In Macbeth, each Weird predicts events that will shape the king’s future.
The ancient tale spoke of a Weird guiding the hero’s path.
Many regarded her as a modern Weird, able to foretell destiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fate - The force or power that determines events; often personified in mythology.
  • Synonym: Weird Sister - One of the three witches in Macbeth; a personification of fate.

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrd' (fate, destiny); famously used in Shakespeare as 'Weird Sisters'.
