Ursus ursinus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɜːrsəs ɜːrˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈɜːsəs ɜːˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of coarse-haired, long-snouted bear native to south-central Asia, commonly known as the sloth bear.
species of bear native to south-central Asia

Examples

The Ursus ursinus is distinguishable by its shaggy black coat and white chest patch.
Wildlife researchers observed a group of Ursus ursinus in an Indian forest.
The diet of Ursus ursinus primarily consists of insects, especially termites and ants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth bear - common name for Ursus ursinus, a bear native to South Asia

Etymology

From Latin: 'ursus' meaning 'bear' and 'ursinus' meaning 'bear-like'.
