Passer domesticus

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæsər doʊˈmɛstɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈpasə dəʊˈmɛstɪkəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the house sparrow, a small, hardy brown-and-grey bird native to Europe and now found worldwide
house sparrow species (scientific name)

Examples

The Passer domesticus is one of the most widely distributed birds in urban areas.
Scientists have observed a decline in Passer domesticus populations in recent decades.
The distinctive chirping of the Passer domesticus is a familiar sound in many cities worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house sparrow - common small brown and grey bird found near habitation

Etymology

From Latin: 'passer' meaning 'sparrow' and 'domesticus' meaning 'domestic' or 'of the house'.
