flatfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈflætˌfʊt / (BrE)
a foot with a fallen arch, causing the sole to be abnormally flat and spread out
a foot with a fallen arch
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with flatfoot after examining the shape of his feet.
Children with flatfoot may experience pain or fatigue after walking long distances.
Special insoles can help people manage the discomfort caused by flatfoot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallen arch - the condition of the arch of the foot collapsing

2. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈflætˌfʊt / (BrE)
an informal term for a police officer, especially one who patrols on foot
informal term for a police officer
informal american

Examples

The flatfoot paced the sidewalk, keeping a watchful eye on the neighborhood.
A flatfoot questioned witnesses at the scene of the incident.
Despite his reputation as a tough flatfoot, he was kind to the local children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cop - informal term for a police officer
  • Synonym: policeman - a male member of a police force

Etymology

Originally American slang; possibly from the idea of policemen walking or standing on their feet for long hours.
