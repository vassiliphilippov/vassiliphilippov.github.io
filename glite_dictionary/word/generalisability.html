generalisability

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛn.ə.rə.lə.zaɪ.ləˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / dʒɛn.ə.rə.laɪ.zəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being applicable or relevant to a broad range of situations, groups, or cases.
applicability to many cases or situations

Examples

The generalisability of this research is limited due to a small sample size.
Researchers questioned the generalisability of the study's outcomes to other populations.
Improving participant diversity can enhance the generalisability of scientific findings.

Synset Examples

Although useful, such samples are not representative of the scientific workforce as a whole and thus have limited generalizability
This study is limited in its power and generalizability.
When altering specific methodological or participant characteristics of a previous study, they serve to test generalizability of the earlier findings under different conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalizability - the US spelling of generalisability

Etymology

From general + -ise/-ize + -ability; relating to the ability to generalise.
