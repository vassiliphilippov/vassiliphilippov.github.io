Scutigera coleoptrata

1. NOUN

/ skuːˈtɪdʒərə ˌkoʊliːˈɒptrətə / (AmE), / skuːˈtɪdʒərə ˌkəʊliːˈɒptrətə / (BrE)
a species of long-legged centipede commonly found in damp indoor places such as cellars and bathrooms
a species of long-legged centipede

Examples

The Scutigera coleoptrata darted across the bathroom floor and disappeared behind the sink.
Many people mistake Scutigera coleoptrata for a harmful pest, but it actually preys on other insects.
Biologists collected several specimens of Scutigera coleoptrata during their survey of basement habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house centipede - common name for Scutigera coleoptrata, a type of centipede found indoors

Etymology

From Latin: 'scutigera' meaning 'shield-bearer' and 'coleoptrata' referring to resemblance to beetles (Coleoptera).
