Phyllitis scolopendrium

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈlaɪtɪs ˌskoʊləˈpɛndrɪəm / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪtɪs ˌskɒləˈpɛndrɪəm / (BrE)
A Eurasian fern with simple, lance-shaped fronds, commonly known as hart's-tongue fern.
species of Eurasian fern; hart's-tongue fern

Examples

The shaded garden thrives with a cluster of Phyllitis scolopendrium near the old stone wall.
Botanists identified the unique fronds of Phyllitis scolopendrium during their field study in the woodland area.
In damp, limestone-rich soil, Phyllitis scolopendrium can spread and create a lush green carpet.

Etymology

From Greek *phyllon* (leaf) and Latin *scolopendra* (centipede), referring to the shape of the frond.
