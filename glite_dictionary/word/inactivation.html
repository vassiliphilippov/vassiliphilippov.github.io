inactivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌæk.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.æk.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of disbanding a military unit, typically through the transfer or discharge of its members
disbanding of a military unit

Examples

The inactivation of the regiment was announced at the ceremony last Friday.
After decades of service, the squadron faced inactivation due to military restructuring.
During inactivation, personnel were reassigned to other units or discharged from service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disbandment - the act of breaking up an organized group, especially a military unit

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + Late Latin activatio (making active).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something inactive or rendering it unable to function
process of making inactive

Examples

The inactivation of the virus was achieved by heating the sample.
Enzyme inactivation can occur at high temperatures during processing.
They studied the inactivation mechanism of the gene under various laboratory conditions.

Synset Examples

the gene inactivation system
thermal inactivation of serum samples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: activation - the process of making something active or operative

Etymology

From inactivate + -ion, modeled on activation.

Synset POS: n
