blandishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈblændɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈblændɪʃmənt / (BrE)
A flattering or pleasing statement or action used to gently persuade someone to do something.
flattering persuasion; gentle flattery

Examples

She ignored his blandishment and stayed firm in her decision.
No amount of blandishments could convince him to change his mind.
The salesman used blandishment to try to make the customer buy the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise, especially to gain advantage
  • Antonym: threat - a statement of intention to cause harm if demands are not met

Etymology

From French 'blandissement', from 'blandir' (to flatter), from Latin 'blandus' (smooth, flattering).

2. NOUN

/ ˈblændɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈblændɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a flattering action or speech intended to persuade someone to do something
flattering speech or action to persuade

Examples

No amount of blandishment could persuade her to change her mind.
He saw through the blandishment of the salesman and refused to buy the car.
With gentle blandishment, she convinced him to try the new restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise
  • Synonym: coaxing - gentle persuasion or urging
  • Antonym: threat - a statement of intention to inflict harm, typically used as coercion

Etymology

From French *blandissement*, from *blandir* 'to flatter'; related to Latin *blandiri* 'to caress, flatter'.
