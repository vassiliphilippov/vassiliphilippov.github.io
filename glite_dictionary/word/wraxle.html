to wraxle

1. VERB

/ ˈræk.səl / (AmE), / ˈræk.səl / (BrE)
to engage in playful wrestling or grappling, especially in a friendly or lighthearted manner
playfully wrestle or grapple
informal

Examples

After dinner, the kids loved to wraxle on the living room carpet.
She would often wraxle with her siblings before bedtime, laughing and shouting.
They started to wraxle in the backyard, pretending to be professional wrestlers.

Synset Examples

He decided to wraxle with his friend for fun.

Related Terms

Etymology

Dialectal variant of 'wrestle,' likely influenced by regional pronunciation.
