David Ben-Gurion

1. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪvɪd bɛn ɡʊˈriːən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪd bɛn ɡʊˈrɪən / (BrE)
(1886–1973) Israeli statesman, a leading Zionist and the primary national founder and first prime minister of Israel.
Israeli statesman; first prime minister of Israel

Examples

David Ben-Gurion played a central role in the founding of the State of Israel.
Israel's first prime minister, David Ben-Gurion, is remembered for his leadership during a critical time.
The policies established by David Ben-Gurion shaped the modern Israeli government.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪvɪd ˌbɛn ɡʊˈriːən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪd ˌbɛn ɡʊəˈrɪən / (BrE)
(1886–1973) Israeli statesman and Zionist leader who became the primary national founder and the first Prime Minister of Israel.
Israeli statesman and first Prime Minister

Examples

David Ben-Gurion declared the independence of Israel in 1948, becoming its first Prime Minister.
As a leader, David Ben-Gurion played a crucial role in shaping the early policies of the new state.
Many historians credit David Ben-Gurion with uniting diverse Jewish communities in Israel after World War II.

Etymology

From Hebrew *David Ben-Guryon*, meaning 'David, son of Gurion'.
