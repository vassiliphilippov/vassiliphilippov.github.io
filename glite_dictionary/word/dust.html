dust

1. VERB

/ dʌst / (AmE), / dʌst / (BrE)
to rub a powdery substance over a surface, especially to blur or soften the outlines of a shape
rub powder to blur outlines

Examples

The artist carefully dusted the charcoal portrait to soften the features.
By gently dusting the pastel drawing, she created a misty effect around the edges.
If you dust too much, the image may lose its definition entirely.

Synset Examples

The artist dusted the charcoal drawing down to a faint image

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dʌst / (AmE), / dʌst / (BrE)
fine, dry particles of matter, such as dirt or tiny fragments of rock, often found as a thin layer covering surfaces
fine dry particles; powdery matter

Examples

A thin layer of dust covered the shelves after weeks of neglect.
The sunlight streaming through the window revealed floating dust particles.
She wiped the dust from her desk before starting work.

Synset Examples

astronomers say that the empty space between planets actually contains measurable amounts of dust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt - earth or soil, or anything unclean
  • Synonym: powder - a dry substance composed of fine particles

Etymology

Old English *dust* (fine powder, ashes).
