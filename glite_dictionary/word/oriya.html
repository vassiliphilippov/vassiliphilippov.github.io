Oriya

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈriːə / (AmE), / ɔːˈriːə / (BrE)
a member of a people native to Odisha (formerly Orissa) and neighboring regions in eastern India
member of an ethnic group in eastern India

Examples

Many Oriya have migrated to cities in search of work.
During the festival, Oriya traditions are celebrated with dance and music.
She interviewed several Oriya about their cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odia - the language spoken by the Oriya people

Etymology

From Oriya (Odia), the Indo-Aryan language spoken by this group, derived from the name of the Odisha region.

2. NOUN

/ əˈriːjə / (AmE), / əˈrɪjə / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in the Indian state of Odisha, and the state's official language
Indo-Aryan language of Odisha, India

Examples

Oriya is spoken by millions of people in eastern India.
She learned to read and write in Oriya during her childhood.
Many ancient texts from Odisha are written in classical Oriya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odia - the modern standard name for the same language

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Odra', the ancient name of the region now called Odisha.
