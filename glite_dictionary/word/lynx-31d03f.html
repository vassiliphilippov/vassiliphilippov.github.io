lynx

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋks / (AmE), / lɪŋks / (BrE)
A wildcat of the genus Lynx, characterized by a short tail, tufted ears, and valued for its thick fur.
wildcat with tufted ears, short tail

Examples

The lynx is known for its keen vision and silent hunting abilities.
A lynx was spotted in the snowy forests near the mountain village.
Fur from the lynx was highly prized in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobcat - a North American species of lynx

Etymology

From Latin 'lynx', from Greek 'lunx', referring to the animal.
