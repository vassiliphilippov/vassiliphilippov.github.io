bicentenary

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪsɛnˈtɛnəri / (AmE), / ˌbaɪsɛnˈtiːnəri / (BrE)
relating to a period of two hundred years or marking its completion.
relating to two hundred years

Examples

The museum held a bicentenary exhibition to celebrate its 200th year.
The bicentenary celebration attracted thousands of visitors from around the world.
Several cities marked the nation's bicentenary with fireworks and special events.

Synset Examples

bicentennial celebration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicentennial - relating to or marking the two hundredth anniversary of an event

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'centenary' (hundred years).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.sɛnˈtiː.nɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.sɛnˈtiː.nəri / (BrE)
The 200th anniversary of a significant event or the celebration marking this occasion.
200th anniversary or its celebration

Examples

The museum held a special exhibition to commemorate the bicentenary of Charles Darwin's birth.
Next year marks the bicentenary of the university, and a series of events are planned.
The town celebrated its bicentenary with a parade and fireworks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centenary - the 100th anniversary or its celebration
  • Degree Higher: tercentenary - the 300th anniversary or its celebration

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'centenarius' (of a hundred), meaning two hundreds.
