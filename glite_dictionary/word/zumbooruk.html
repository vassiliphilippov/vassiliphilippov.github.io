zumbooruk

1. NOUN

/ zʊmˈbuːrɪk / (AmE), / zʊmˈbʊərʊk / (BrE)
A small cannon mounted on a camel, used as mobile artillery by Arab, Afghan, Persian, and Indian armies in the early modern era.
small camel-mounted cannon

Examples

The Mughal army deployed a zumbooruk unit to support their advance across the desert.
Historians describe how zumbooruks provided both mobility and firepower in 18th-century battles.
A line of camels carrying zumbooruks was a distinctive sight on the battlefield.

Etymology

From Persian *zambūrak*, diminutive of *zambūr* (hornet), referring to the buzzing sound or the weapon's small size.
