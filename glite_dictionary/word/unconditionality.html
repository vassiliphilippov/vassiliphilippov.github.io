unconditionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.kənˌdɪʃ.əˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˌdɪʃ.əˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being without conditions, limitations, or reservations.
state of having no conditions or limits

Examples

Despite the unconditionality of her support, he still doubted her loyalty.
The terms of the agreement emphasized unconditionality, leaving no room for negotiation.
Religious love is often described as having a quality of unconditionality.

Synset Examples

Given the difficulty of gradualism in highly polarized polities, the better path is to qualify the unconditionality of the grant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditionality - the quality or state of being subject to conditions

Etymology

From un- (not) + conditional + -ity, meaning 'state or quality.'
