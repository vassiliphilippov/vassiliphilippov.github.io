Chelicerata

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪsəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / kɪˌlɪsəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
a large group of arthropods that includes spiders, scorpions, ticks, and horseshoe crabs, characterized by having chelicerae (claw-like mouthparts)
group of arthropods: spiders, scorpions, horseshoe crabs

Examples

Scientists classify spiders and scorpions as members of the Chelicerata group.
Horseshoe crabs are one of the oldest living representatives of the Chelicerata.
The distinguishing feature of Chelicerata is the presence of specialized mouthparts called chelicerae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'khēlē' (claw) and Latin '-cera' (horn).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪsəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / kɛˌlɪsəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A large group (superclass) of arthropods that includes spiders, scorpions, mites, and horseshoe crabs, characterized by having chelicerae (mouthparts) and lacking antennae.
arthropod group including spiders and scorpions
medical

Examples

Many arachnologists study Chelicerata to understand the evolution of spiders and their relatives.
Horseshoe crabs, although not true crabs, are classified within the Chelicerata.
The Chelicerata are distinguished by their unique mouthparts called chelicerae.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'chele' (claw) + 'keras' (horn).
