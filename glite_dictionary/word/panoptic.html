panoptic

1. ADJ

/ pænˈɑptɪk / (AmE), / pænˈɒptɪk / (BrE)
including everything visible in a single view or scene; comprehensive in scope or coverage
comprehensive; covering all that can be seen

Examples

The gallery provided a panoptic view of the city’s art history.
A panoptic survey of the forest revealed previously undocumented species.
The scientist used a panoptic lens to capture the entire specimen in one image.

Synset Examples

a panoptic aerial photograph of the missile base
a panoptic stain used in microscopy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - covering all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • Antonym: partial - existing only in part; incomplete

Etymology

From Greek panoptēs (all-seeing): pan- (all) + optos (seen).

2. ADJ

/ pænˈɑptɪk / (AmE), / pænˈɒptɪk / (BrE)
encompassing or viewing everything in a comprehensive or broad way; all-seeing.
comprehensive in scope; all-seeing

Examples

The report offered a panoptic overview of climate change and its effects on society.
Her panoptic vision allowed her to notice even the smallest details in the project.
The museum's new exhibit presents a panoptic history of the city from its founding to the present.

Synset Examples

an invention with broad applications
a panoptic study of Soviet nationality
granted him wide powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something
  • Antonym: narrow - limited in extent, amount, or scope

Etymology
