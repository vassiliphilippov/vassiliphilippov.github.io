to hop-skip

1. VERB

/ hɑp skɪp / (AmE), / hɒp skɪp / (BrE)
to jump lightly in a manner similar to hopping and skipping alternately
jump lightly in a hopping-skipping manner

Examples

The children hop-skipped across the playground, laughing gleefully.
She hop-skipped over the puddles to avoid getting her shoes wet.
He hop-skipped down the garden path on a sunny afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip - to move along lightly, stepping from one foot to the other with a hop or bounce
