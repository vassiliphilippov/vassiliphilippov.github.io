Psephurus gladis

1. NOUN

/ siːˈfʊərəs ˈɡleɪdɪs / (AmE), / siːˈfiːfərəs ˈɡleɪdɪs / (BrE)
A large, extinct species of freshwater fish from China's major rivers, closely related to the paddlefish.
extinct Chinese paddlefish species

Examples

The Psephurus gladis was once one of the largest freshwater fishes in the world.
Scientists mourned the extinction of Psephurus gladis due to habitat loss and overfishing.
Museum exhibits include a preserved specimen of Psephurus gladis to educate visitors about biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - large freshwater fish with a long, flat snout, native to North America and China

Etymology

From Greek psephos (pebble) and Latinized genus name, with the species epithet from Latin.
