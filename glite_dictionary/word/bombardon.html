bombardon

1. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbɑrdən / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːdən / (BrE)
a large wind instrument of the shawm family, serving as its bass member
large bass shawm instrument

Examples

The orchestra included a bombardon to provide a deep, resonant bass line.
Early music ensembles sometimes feature the bombardon alongside other period instruments.
He demonstrated the unique, powerful sound of the bombardon during the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shawm - a double-reed woodwind instrument popular in the Renaissance and Baroque periods

Etymology

From Italian 'bombardone', an augmentative of 'bombarda' (bombard, a type of cannon), referencing its size and sound.

2. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbɑrdən / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːdən / (BrE)
a large, low-pitched brass instrument, similar to a tuba, that coils over the shoulder of the player
large, coiled brass instrument (like a tuba)

Examples

The musician slung the bombardon over his shoulder before joining the marching band.
In the brass section, the deep notes of the bombardon were clearly heard.
He learned to play the bombardon for the festival parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuba - a large brass wind instrument with a deep sound

Etymology

From Italian 'bombardone', augmentative of 'bombarda' (bombard, type of bass instrument).
