to snarf

1. VERB

/ snɑrf / (AmE), / snɑːf / (BrE)
to take or steal something quickly, often without permission
steal or take something quickly
informal

Examples

Someone had snarfed my sandwich from the office fridge again.
He quickly snarfed her pen when she left the room.
Teenagers sometimes snarf snacks from the kitchen when their parents aren’t looking.
