immortelle

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmɔrˈtɛl / (AmE), / ɪˌmɔːˈtɛl / (BrE)
A plant with flowers that retain their shape and color when dried, particularly species commonly known as everlasting flowers.
everlasting flower; retains color when dried

Examples

The immortelle was chosen for the bouquet because its flowers keep their color for months.
Gardeners in southern Europe often grow the immortelle for its decorative dried blooms.
A wreath made of immortelle adorned the memorial, symbolizing remembrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everlasting - a plant with flowers that remain attractive when dried

Etymology

From French 'immortelle', from Latin 'immortalis' (immortal), referring to the long-lasting blooms.
