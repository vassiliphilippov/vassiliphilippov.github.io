duffel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌfəl / (BrE)
a large cylindrical bag made of sturdy cloth, used for carrying personal belongings, often when traveling or camping
large cylindrical cloth bag

Examples

He packed all his clothes and gear into a sturdy duffel for the camping trip.
The soldier slung his duffel over his shoulder as he boarded the bus.
After the gym, she carried her duffel back to her car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duffel bag - large cylindrical bag used for carrying belongings

Etymology

From the town of Duffel in Belgium, where the thick cloth used to make such bags originated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
A coarse, heavy woolen fabric, typically used for coats and blankets.
coarse woolen fabric

Examples

The coat was made from thick duffel to withstand the cold winters.
Early explorers favored duffel for its warmth and durability.
The blanket, woven from grey duffel, covered the old wooden bed.

Etymology

From Dutch 'duffel', after Duffel, a town in Belgium where the cloth was originally made.
