yottabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑtəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɒtəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,000 zettabytes or 10²⁴ bytes, used to measure digital data.
unit of digital information (10²⁴ bytes)

Examples

Some experts predict that global data will surpass one yottabyte in the next decade.
A yottabyte is such a massive quantity of storage that it can hold the data of millions of data centers.
No current computer systems are capable of managing even a single yottabyte of information.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zettabyte - A unit of information equal to 10²¹ bytes

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'zetta-' (for 10²¹) and the International System of Units, from 'yotta-' (representing 10²⁴) and 'byte'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑtəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɒtəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 zettabytes or 2^80 bytes (approximately 1 septillion bytes).
unit of digital information (2^80 bytes)

Examples

It is estimated that the entire internet contains several yottabytes of data.
Storing a yottabyte would require millions of high-capacity hard drives.
Scientists predict humanity will generate a yottabyte of digital information in the near future.

Etymology

Formed from the International System of Units (SI) prefix 'yotta-' (denoting 10^24), and 'byte'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13653625-n
Synset POS: n
