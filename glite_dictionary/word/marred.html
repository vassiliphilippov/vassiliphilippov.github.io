marred

1. ADJ

/ mɑrd / (AmE), / mɑːd / (BrE)
damaged or spoiled, especially by a mark or injury that makes something less attractive or perfect.
damaged; spoiled; blemished by marks

Examples

The wooden floor was marred by scratches from moving furniture.
Her reputation was marred by unfounded rumors in the press.
The view was marred by the construction site next door.

Synset Examples

the scarred piano bench
walls marred by graffiti

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarred - marked or damaged by scars; bearing the marks of injury
  • Antonym: unblemished - without any mark or flaw; not damaged

Etymology

Past participle of 'mar', from Old English 'merran' or 'mierran' (to hinder, spoil).
