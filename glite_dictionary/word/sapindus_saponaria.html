Sapindus saponaria

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɪndəs ˌsæpəˈnɛriə / (AmE), / səˈpɪndəs ˌsæpəˈnɑːrɪə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to tropical America, with pulpy fruit containing saponin, historically used as soap by Native Americans
tropical American soapberry tree

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used the fruit of the Sapindus saponaria tree as a natural soap.
The Sapindus saponaria thrives in warm, tropical climates across Central and South America.
Researchers are studying the saponin content in Sapindus saponaria for potential industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapberry - tree bearing fruit containing saponin, used as soap substitute

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Sapindus' (from 'sapo' meaning soap), and species name 'saponaria' meaning 'of or relating to soap'.
