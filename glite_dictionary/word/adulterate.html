adulterate

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʌltəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈdʌltəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) made impure or inferior by adding extraneous or improper ingredients.
made impure by adding other substances

Examples

The chef refused to use adulterate spices in his signature dish.
Some herbal supplements are adulterate and contain hidden synthetic chemicals.
Regulations are in place to prevent adulterate milk from reaching consumers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure - not mixed with anything else; free of impurities

Etymology

From Latin adulteratus, past participle of adulterare (to corrupt, to debase).

2. VERB

/ əˈdʌltəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈdʌltəreɪt / (BrE)
to make something impure or weaker by adding inferior or foreign substances, especially to food, drink, or chemicals
make impure by adding inferior substances

Examples

The company was fined for attempting to adulterate the olive oil with cheaper vegetable oils.
Unscrupulous vendors sometimes adulterate spices to increase their profit margin.
You should always check the label to ensure the honey has not been adulterated with added sugars.

Synset Examples

adulterate liquor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contaminate - to make something impure or harmful by adding unwanted substances
  • Antonym: to purify - to remove impurities from something

Etymology

From Latin adulterare (to corrupt, falsify).
