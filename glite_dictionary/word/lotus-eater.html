lotus-eater

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtəs ˌitər / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs ˌiːtə / (BrE)
a person who lives in dreamy indifference to the busy world, often avoiding responsibilities or strenuous activity
a person living in dreamy indolence

Examples

After retiring, he became something of a lotus-eater, spending his days in leisure and contemplation.
Some critics accused the novelist of being a lotus-eater, disconnected from the practical concerns of life.
The island resort attracts lotus-eaters seeking escape from the demands of modern society.

Synset Examples

in the Odyssey, Homer tells of lotus-eaters who live in dreamy indolence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who avoids work or spends time lazily
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who compulsively works hard and long hours
  • Synonym: dreamer - a person who is impractical or given to daydreaming

Etymology

From the Lotus-eaters in Homer's Odyssey, who lived in indolent contentment after eating the lotus plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.t̬əs ˌiː.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs ˌiː.tə / (BrE)
a person who lives in pleasure and ease, avoiding responsibility and reality, often in a dreamy or detached manner
person living in dreamy ease or idleness

Examples

Her friends called her a lotus-eater because she traveled the world without any plans or worries.
The novel's protagonist is a lotus-eater who wants nothing more than to bask in the sun all day.
Critics accused the artist of being a lotus-eater, living an existence detached from everyday struggles.

Synset Examples

She enjoyed the carefree life of a lotus-eater, ignoring her deadlines.
In the world of business, being a lotus-eater can lead to missed opportunities.
He was criticized for being a lotus-eater, avoiding responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who avoids work or activity
  • Synonym: sybarite - a person devoted to luxury and pleasure
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who compulsively works too much

Etymology

From Greek mythology: the Lotus-eaters (Λωτοφάγοι, Lotus-eaters) were people in the Odyssey who ate lotus plants causing forgetfulness and dreamy idleness.
