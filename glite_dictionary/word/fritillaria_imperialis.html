Fritillaria imperialis

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪlɪˈɛriəs ɪmˌpɪriˈɑ:lɪs / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪlɪˈɛːrɪəs ɪmˌpɪərɪˈɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant with whorls of leaves and bright orange-red, bell-shaped flowers at the top of a tall stem, commonly grown as an ornamental.
species of Eurasian flowering plant

Examples

The gardener planted Fritillaria imperialis along the pathway for its spectacular spring display.
Large clusters of Fritillaria imperialis create a striking effect in formal gardens.
I admired the orange-red bells of Fritillaria imperialis swaying gently in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown imperial - common name for Fritillaria imperialis, a flowering plant with crown-like flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'fritillaria' (dice box, referencing the shape of the flower) and 'imperialis' (imperial, majestic), named for its grand appearance.
