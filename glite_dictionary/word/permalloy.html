Permalloy

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈmælˌɔɪ / (AmE), / pəˈmælɔɪ / (BrE)
an alloy consisting of about 80% nickel and 20% iron, easily magnetized and demagnetized, used especially in electrical and electronic devices
nickel-iron magnetic alloy

Examples

The transformer core was made of Permalloy to enhance its magnetic performance.
Researchers are investigating the magnetic properties of thin Permalloy films for use in sensors.
Thanks to its composition, Permalloy is widely used in telecommunications equipment.

Etymology

Blend of 'permeability' and 'alloy', coined in the early 20th century.
