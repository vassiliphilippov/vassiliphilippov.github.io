Halakah

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈlɑːkə / (AmE), / hɑːˈlɑːkə / (BrE)
The collective body of Jewish law, including the legal parts of the Talmud and the interpretation of the laws in the Hebrew Scriptures.
Jewish religious law and its interpretation

Examples

Many aspects of daily Jewish life are governed by the Halakah as interpreted by rabbis.
He spent years studying the Halakah to better understand Jewish legal traditions.
Debates over the modern application of Halakah often appear in religious journals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Halacha - Alternate spelling for the body of Jewish law

Etymology

From Hebrew *halakhah* (the way, the law), from *halakh* (to walk).
