maguey

1. NOUN

/ məˈɡeɪ / (AmE), / məˈɡeɪ / (BrE)
A Mexican agave plant, especially one used to make pulque and mescal.
Mexican agave plant used for pulque and mescal

Examples

The traditional drink pulque is made from the fermented sap of the maguey plant.
Farmers harvest maguey for producing both paper and alcoholic beverages.
In many Mexican regions, maguey leaves are used for wrapping food for cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agave - Succulent plant found in hot countries, genus of maguey

Etymology

From Spanish 'maguey', from Taíno or Arawak origin.

2. NOUN

/ məˈɡeɪ / (AmE), / məˈɡeɪ / (BrE)
A Philippine plant (Agave cantala) that produces a hard fiber used to make coarse twine and rope.
Philippine plant yielding fiber for rope

Examples

The maguey plant is cultivated for its strong, durable fiber used in making rope and nets.
Workers harvested maguey leaves early in the morning to avoid the midday heat.
In some rural areas, maguey twine remains an important material for traditional crafts.

Etymology

From Spanish 'maguey', from Taíno.
