eddo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛdəʊ / (BrE)
the edible starchy tuberous root of certain taro plants, especially cultivated in tropical regions as a food crop
a starchy root of taro plants used as food

Examples

She used eddo in the stew as a substitute for potatoes.
The market sells fresh eddoes alongside other tropical vegetables.
After boiling the eddo, it was mashed and served as a side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical plant grown for its edible starchy corm

Etymology

From Portuguese 'eddo', probably from a West African language.
