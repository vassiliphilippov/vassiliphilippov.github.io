Menopon gallinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˌpɒn ɡəˈlaɪniː / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəˌpɒn ɡəˈlɪniː / (BrE)
a species of louse that parasitizes poultry, feeding on their feathers and skin
louse species parasitic on poultry
medical

Examples

The veterinarian discovered an infestation of Menopon gallinae in the chicken coop.
Proper hygiene can help prevent the spread of Menopon gallinae among poultry.
Farmers are advised to monitor their flocks for signs of Menopon gallinae to maintain bird health.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Menopon' + Latin 'gallinae' (of hens/chickens).
