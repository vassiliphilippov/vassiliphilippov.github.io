biodefence

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
measures and strategies designed to protect people and environments from attacks involving biological agents or diseases, especially in the context of warfare or terrorism
measures against biological attacks

Examples

The government is increasing funding for biodefence research to prepare for potential biological threats.
Many hospitals follow strict biodefence protocols in case of an outbreak of a dangerous pathogen.
Developing new vaccines is an important part of modern biodefence strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological warfare - the use of biological toxins or infectious agents as weapons
  • Synonym: biosecurity - preventative measures to protect against biological threats

Etymology

From bio- (life) + defence, modelled after 'biological defense'.
