Thomas Paine

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs peɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs peɪn / (BrE)
(1737–1809) English-born American political activist, philosopher, and writer who supported the American Revolution and the French Revolution.
American Revolutionary leader and writer

Examples

Thomas Paine wrote 'Common Sense,' which inspired many colonists to support independence.
Many historians credit Thomas Paine with influencing public opinion during the American Revolution.
The ideas of Thomas Paine had a profound impact on democratic movements across the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pamphleteer - a writer or publisher of pamphlets, especially for political causes
