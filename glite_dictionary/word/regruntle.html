to regruntle

1. VERB

/ riˈɡrʌn.tl̩ / (AmE), / riˈɡrʌn.tl / (BrE)
to make someone who is disgruntled satisfied or content again
make someone content again
informal

Examples

After receiving an apology and a refund, the company managed to regruntle the frustrated customer.
Managers tried to regruntle their employees with a team-building retreat.
It often takes more than a simple gesture to regruntle someone who feels wronged.

Synset Examples

Efforts to regruntle customers have shown positive results.
She found ways to regruntle her team after the layoffs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disgruntled - annoyed, dissatisfied, or discontented

Etymology

Formed humorously on the analogy of 'disgruntled', from prefix 're-' meaning 'again' + 'gruntle'.
