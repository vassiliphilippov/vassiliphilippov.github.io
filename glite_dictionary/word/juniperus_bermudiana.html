Juniperus bermudiana

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːˈnɪpərəs bərˌmjuːdiˈænə / (AmE), / dʒuːˈnɪpərəs bəˌmjuːdiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a species of juniper native to Bermuda, also known as Bermuda cedar, with a dense pyramidal shape, often grown as an ornamental tree
species of juniper native to Bermuda

Examples

In Bermuda, the Juniperus bermudiana is prized both for its beauty and its historical importance.
The Juniperus bermudiana was once dominant in Bermuda's landscape before disease reduced its numbers sharply.
Many gardens feature Juniperus bermudiana as a windbreak due to its dense, pyramidal foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda cedar - another common name for Juniperus bermudiana
  • Degree Higher: juniper - evergreen shrub or tree, genus Juniperus, to which this species belongs

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Juniperus' (juniper) and species 'bermudiana' (of Bermuda).
