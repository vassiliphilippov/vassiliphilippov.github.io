forlorn

1. ADJ

/ fərˈlɔrn / (AmE), / fəˈlɔːn / (BrE)
feeling or showing hopelessness and sadness; unlikely to succeed or be fulfilled.
hopeless and sad; unlikely to succeed

Examples

She gave a forlorn glance at the empty street, hoping he might return.
After the hurricane, stray animals wandered in a forlorn search for shelter.
His forlorn efforts to fix the old car ended in disappointment.

Synset Examples

the last forlorn attempt
a forlorn cause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despondent - feeling or showing deep hopelessness or dejection
  • Antonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring optimism or hope for success

Etymology

From Old English forloren, past participle of forlēosan (to lose).
