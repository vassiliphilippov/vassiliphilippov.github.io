to marinate

1. VERB

/ ˈmærɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmærɪneɪt / (BrE)
to soak food in a seasoned liquid, often before cooking, to add flavor or make it more tender
soak food in a flavored liquid before cooking

Examples

You should marinate the chicken overnight for the best flavor.
Before grilling, she likes to marinate the vegetables in olive oil and herbs.
The chef marinated the fish to make it more tender and tasty.

Synset Examples

marinade herring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickle - to preserve food in vinegar or brine

Etymology

From Spanish 'marinar', from 'marinado' (pickled in brine).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæɹɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmærɪneɪt / (BrE)
to soak food in a seasoned liquid, often to add flavor or tenderize it before cooking
soak food in flavored liquid

Examples

You should marinate the chicken in lemon juice for a few hours.
She likes to marinate tofu before adding it to stir-fry dishes.
Before grilling, they always marinate the meat to make it more flavorful.

Synset Examples

You should marinate the steak overnight for best flavor.
I like to marinate vegetables before grilling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to leave something in liquid so that it becomes wet or soft

Etymology

From Spanish 'marinar', meaning 'to pickle in brine'.
