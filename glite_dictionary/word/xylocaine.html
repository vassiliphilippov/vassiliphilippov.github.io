Xylocaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.loʊˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ləˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A proprietary (trade) name for lidocaine, a local anesthetic used for numbing skin and mucous membranes.
trade name for a local anesthetic (lidocaine)
medical

Examples

The dentist applied Xylocaine gel to numb the area before the injection.
Xylocaine is often used to treat minor burns and skin irritations.
If you are allergic to Xylocaine, inform your healthcare provider before any procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lidocaine - a local anesthetic commonly used to numb tissue in a specific area
