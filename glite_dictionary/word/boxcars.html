boxcars

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksˌkɑrz / (AmE), / ˈbɒksˌkɑːz / (BrE)
(in dice games) a throw of two sixes; double six.
a throw of two sixes in dice

Examples

He cheered loudly when he rolled boxcars in the final round.
Rolling boxcars is considered both lucky and unlucky, depending on the game.
Every gambler hopes for boxcars when the stakes are high.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: snake eyes - a throw of two ones in dice
  • Synonym: double six - a throw of two sixes in dice

Etymology

From the resemblance between the six spots on a die and the rectangular shape of railroad boxcars.
