conserved

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɝvd / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvd / (BrE)
protected from harm, loss, or destruction, especially by careful management or preservation
protected; preserved from harm or loss

Examples

Many rare animal species are now conserved in protected reserves.
Much of the region’s original forest has been carefully conserved for future generations.
Water needs to be conserved in order to prevent shortages during dry seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserved - kept safe from harm or decay

Etymology

From the past participle of 'conserve,' from Latin 'conservare' (to preserve).
