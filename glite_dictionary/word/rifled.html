rifled

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪfəld / (AmE), / ˈraɪfəld / (BrE)
(of a firearm) having spiral grooves cut inside the barrel to make a bullet spin as it is fired, increasing accuracy.
having spiral grooves in a gun barrel

Examples

The hunter chose a rifled barrel for his shotgun to improve its accuracy.
All modern military rifles have rifled barrels to stabilize bullets during flight.
The museum displayed an antique rifled musket from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothbore - (of a firearm) having a barrel without spiral grooves

Etymology

From the verb 'rifle', meaning to cut spiral grooves inside a gun barrel.
