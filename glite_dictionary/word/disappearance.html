disappearance

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪərəns / (BrE)
the act or fact of someone or something ceasing to be visible or going out of sight
the act of becoming invisible or lost from view

Examples

The sudden disappearance of the sun behind the clouds changed the atmosphere completely.
Police are investigating the disappearance of several valuable paintings from the gallery.
Her disappearance from the crowded party went unnoticed for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appearance - the act of becoming visible

Etymology

From French 'disparaître' (to disappear), from Latin 'dis-' (away) + 'parere' (to appear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪərəns / (BrE)
the process of gradually ceasing to be visible
gradual vanishing or becoming invisible

Examples

The disappearance of the sun behind the clouds signaled the start of the storm.
She watched the magician's disappearance from the stage with amazement.
The gradual disappearance of the mountain in the distance was caused by dense fog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanishing - the process of disappearing or becoming invisible

Etymology

From disappear + -ance, based on French disparition.

Her fame ended almost as quickly as its disappearance from public interest.

4. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪərəns / (BrE)
the act of leaving secretly or without explanation, so that one's whereabouts become unknown
the act of leaving without explanation

Examples

The sudden disappearance of the famous artist left everyone puzzled.
There was great concern after the disappearance of the funds from the bank account.
Her unexplained disappearance sparked a massive search across the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanishing - the act of suddenly going out of sight or existence
  • Antonym: appearance - the act of becoming visible or present

Etymology

From dis- (opposite of) + appearance, from Latin apparere (to appear).
