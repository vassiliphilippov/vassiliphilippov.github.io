Zulu

1. NOUN

/ ˈzuːluː / (AmE), / ˈzuːluː / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu-speaking people of eastern South Africa, primarily living in KwaZulu-Natal
member of the Zulu people of South Africa

Examples

A famous Zulu warrior led the charge against the British during the Battle of Isandlwana.
Many Zulu live in KwaZulu-Natal but also work in major cities across South Africa.
The Zulu are known for their rich culture, unique dances, and traditional beadwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zulu - the Bantu language spoken by the Zulu people

Etymology

From Zulu, the self-designation of the people, meaning ‘heaven’ in the Zulu language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzuːluː / (AmE), / ˈzuːluː / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu ethnic group living mainly in eastern South Africa, particularly KwaZulu-Natal
Bantu ethnic group of South Africa

Examples

The Zulu people have a rich cultural heritage and are known for their vibrant traditional ceremonies.
Historically, the Zulu kingdom played a major role in shaping the region’s history.
She is proud of her Zulu ancestry and speaks isiZulu fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xhosa - neighboring Bantu ethnic group in South Africa
  • Synonym: Swazi - Bantu ethnic group in southern Africa

Etymology

From Zulu (the name of the founder of the Zulu nation), from isiZulu (the language of the Zulu people).

3. NOUN

/ ˈzuːluː / (AmE), / ˈzuːluː / (BrE)
a Bantu language of considerable literary importance, spoken mainly in southeastern Africa, particularly in South Africa
Bantu language spoken in southeastern Africa

Examples

Many people in South Africa speak Zulu as their first language.
She is studying Zulu literature at university.
The translation was provided in both English and Zulu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xhosa - a closely related Bantu language spoken in South Africa

Etymology

From Zulu, the name of the people and their language; originally from the Zulu word 'iZulu' meaning 'heaven' or 'sky'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07007452-n
Synset POS: n
