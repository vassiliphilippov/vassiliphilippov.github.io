despondency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɑndənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒndənsi / (BrE)
a state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage
a feeling of hopelessness or dejection

Examples

He sank into a deep despondency after losing his job.
She struggled to overcome the despondency that followed the end of her relationship.
A sense of despondency spread throughout the team after their defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dejection - a sad and depressed state; low spirits
  • Synonym: hopelessness - a feeling or state of despair; lack of hope
  • Antonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future
  • Synonym: gloom - a state of depression or despondency

Etymology

From Latin despondentia, from despondēre (to give up, lose hope).
