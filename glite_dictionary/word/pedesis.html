pedesis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdiːsɪs / (AmE), / pɛˈdiːsɪs / (BrE)
the random motion of small particles suspended in a fluid, caused by collisions with molecules of the fluid
random motion of particles in a fluid

Examples

The phenomenon of pedesis can be observed under a microscope when watching pollen grains in water.
Scientists studied pedesis to understand how microscopic particles interact with molecules in a liquid.
The explanation of pedesis played a key role in the development of the atomic theory of matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brownian motion - the random motion of particles suspended in a fluid, a synonym for pedesis

Etymology

From Greek pēdēsis (a leaping, a jumping about).
