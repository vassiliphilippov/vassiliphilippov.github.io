hypercatalectic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpərˌkætəˈlɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkætəˈlɛktɪk / (BrE)
(of a line of verse) having one or more extra syllables at the end of a metrically complete verse or metrical foot.
having extra syllables in verse

Examples

The poem's last line is hypercatalectic, containing an extra weak syllable beyond the regular meter.
Scholars noted that the stanza included a hypercatalectic line, altering the poem's rhythm.
A hypercatalectic ending can create a sense of extension or emphasis in traditional verse forms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catalectic - (of a verse line) lacking a syllable at the end

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over, beyond) + catalectic (incomplete line of verse).

2. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪ.pərˌkæt.əˈlɛk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˌkæt.əˈlɛk.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a poetic line) having one or more extra syllables beyond the normal length, especially at the end of the last metrical foot.
having extra syllables at end of poetic line

Examples

The first line of the poem is hypercatalectic, ending with an unexpected extra syllable.
Scholars debated whether the verse was hypercatalectic or simply a variant in pronunciation.
A hypercatalectic ending often adds a subtle irregularity to metrical poetry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catalectic - a line of poetry missing one or more syllables at the end of the last metrical foot

Etymology

From hyper- (over, beyond) and catalectic (from Greek katalēktikos, incomplete at the end).
