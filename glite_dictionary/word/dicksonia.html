Dicksonia

1. NOUN

/ dɪkˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / dɪkˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tree ferns native to temperate regions of Australasia, characterized by divided fronds and usually marginal sori; sometimes classified in the family Cyatheaceae.
genus of tree ferns from Australasia

Examples

The Dicksonia in our garden have grown remarkably tall over the years.
A Dicksonia is ideal for creating a lush, subtropical effect in shaded landscapes.
During the hike, we spotted several mature Dicksonias along the forest trail.

Etymology

Named after James Dickson (1738–1822), a Scottish botanist.

2. NOUN

/ dɪkˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / dɪkˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of tree ferns native to temperate regions of Australasia, characterized by bipinnatifid or tripinnatifid fronds and usually marginal sori.
genus of Australasian tree ferns

Examples

Several species of Dicksonia are popular in ornamental gardens for their striking fronds.
The Dicksonia tree fern can grow to over five meters tall in its native habitat.
Botanists debated whether Dicksonia should be classified in its own family or within Cyatheaceae.

Etymology

Named after James Dickson (1738–1822), a Scottish botanist.
