draftee

1. NOUN

/ dræfˈti / (AmE), / drɑːfˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who has been selected for compulsory military service by a draft
person conscripted for military service

Examples

During the war, every draftee underwent rigorous training before deployment.
As a draftee, he felt nervous about leaving home to join the army.
Many young men feared being called as a draftee when conscription was announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscript - a person compulsorily enlisted for military service
  • Antonym: volunteer - a person who freely offers to enlist in military service

Etymology

Formed from "draft" + "-ee" (one who is the object of an action), early 20th century US military context.
