Anoplophora glabripennis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˌplɒfəˈrɑː ˌɡlæb.rəˈpɛn.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˌplɒf.əˈrɪs ˌɡlæb.rɪˈpɛn.ɪs / (BrE)
a large black-and-white beetle native to Asia, known as the Asian longhorned beetle, that infests and damages hardwood trees, particularly in North America and Europe
Asian longhorned beetle, tree pest

Examples

The Anoplophora glabripennis is responsible for the destruction of thousands of hardwood trees in the United States.
Researchers are developing new methods to control the spread of Anoplophora glabripennis in urban forests.
Infested wood should be inspected for the characteristic holes made by Anoplophora glabripennis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asian longhorned beetle - common name for Anoplophora glabripennis

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Anoplophora' meaning 'unarmed bearing' and 'glabripennis' meaning 'smooth-winged', both from Latin roots.
