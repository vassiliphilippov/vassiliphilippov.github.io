fetidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a strong, unpleasant, and offensive smell.
state of having a strong bad smell

Examples

The fetidness of the stagnant pond drove everyone indoors.
His boots carried the fetidness of the swamp into the house.
Despite the flower arrangements, the room's fetidness was impossible to ignore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malodor - an unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

From Latin 'fetidus' (stinking) + English '-ness'.
