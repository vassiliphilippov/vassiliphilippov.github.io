spur

1. NOUN

/ spɝː / (AmE), / spɜː / (BrE)
A tubular projection at the base of the corolla of some flowers, often containing nectar.
tubular projection at flower base

Examples

The columbine's unique spur holds nectar that attracts hummingbirds.
Some orchids have a long spur extending from the back of their flowers.
The violet is distinguished by a short, curved spur at the base of its petals.

2. VERB

/ spɝː / (AmE), / spɜː / (BrE)
to urge a horse forward or increase its speed by using spurs
urge a horse with spurs

Examples

The jockey spurred his horse to take the lead in the final stretch.
She spurred the mare gently, urging it up the hillside.
As soon as he spurred his stallion, it broke into a gallop.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
