bridecake

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdˌkeɪk / (BrE)
a rich, decorated cake with two or more tiers, traditionally served at a wedding reception
decorated layered wedding cake

Examples

The bridecake was elegantly decorated with white roses and pearls.
Guests eagerly gathered around the table to admire the towering bridecake.
She ordered a chocolate bridecake for her wedding instead of the usual vanilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding cake - a cake eaten at a wedding reception

Etymology

From 'bride' and 'cake', referring to the tradition of serving a special cake at a wedding.
