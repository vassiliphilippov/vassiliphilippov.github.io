burnoose

1. NOUN

/ bɚˈnus / (AmE), / bɜːˈnuːs / (BrE)
a long, hooded cloak made from a single piece of wool, traditionally worn by Arabs and Moors in North Africa
long, hooded North African cloak

Examples

The old man wrapped his burnoose tightly around him to shield himself from the desert wind.
She admired the intricate embroidery on the burnoose displayed in the museum's North African collection.
As night fell, he pulled his burnoose over his head to keep warm in the chilly air.

Etymology

From Arabic 'burnus', via French 'burnous'.
