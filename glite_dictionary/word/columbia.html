Columbia

1. PROPN

/ kəˈlʌmbiə / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbiə / (BrE)
a major river in northwestern North America, flowing from southwestern Canada through Washington, forming part of the border with Oregon before emptying into the Pacific Ocean
major river in northwest North America

Examples

The Columbia is well known for its powerful salmon runs that attract fishermen each spring.
Hydroelectric dams along the Columbia have transformed the region's economy and landscape.
We drove alongside the Columbia and marveled at its sheer size and beauty.

Etymology

Named after Christopher Columbus; name used historically for various places in North America.
