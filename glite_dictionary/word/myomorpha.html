Myomorpha

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːmɔːrfə / (AmE), / maɪˈɒməfə / (BrE)
A major suborder of rodents including true rats, mice, and their relatives.
suborder of rodents: rats, mice, etc.

Examples

The suborder Myomorpha includes a wide variety of mice, rats, and gerbils.
Scientists often study Myomorpha to understand rodent evolution and classification.
Members of Myomorpha are commonly found across all continents except Antarctica.

Etymology

From Greek myo- (mouse) + -morpha (form, shape).
