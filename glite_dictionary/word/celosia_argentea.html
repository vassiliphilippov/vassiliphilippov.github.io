Celosia argentea

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈloʊsi.ə ɑːrˈdʒɛntiə / (AmE), / sɪˈləʊsi.ə ɑːˈdʒɛntiə / (BrE)
A weedy annual plant, also known as silver cockscomb, characterized by tall spikes of silver-white flowers.
a plant with silver-white flower spikes

Examples

In late summer, the Celosia argentea filled the garden with brilliant, silvery spires.
Some farmers regard Celosia argentea as a weed because it grows abundantly in disturbed soils.
The florist arranged Celosia argentea alongside other exotic flowers for a striking bouquet.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Celosia', from Greek 'kelos' (burned), referring to the flame-like flower heads; 'argentea' from Latin 'argentum' (silver), referencing the flower color.
