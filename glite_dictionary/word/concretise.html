to concretise

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.krəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.krɪˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an idea, plan, etc.) specific, definite, or real; to become specific or clear
make something specific or definite
british

Examples

The author tried to concretise her abstract theories in the final chapter.
His proposal helped concretise our vague ideas into an action plan.
It took several discussions before the vision began to concretise in their minds.

Synset Examples

the idea concretized in her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to concretize - make something specific or definite (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to generalize - make something more general, less specific

Etymology

From concrete + -ise, following the pattern of 'concretize' (chiefly US).
