significant

1. ADJ

/ sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (BrE)
important, notable, or having a particular meaning or effect; sufficiently great or important to be worthy of attention.
important; notable; meaningful

Examples

The company made a significant investment in renewable energy technologies this year.
There was no significant difference between the two groups in the study.
Winning the award was a significant milestone in her career.

Synset Examples

a significant change in tax laws
a significant change in the Constitution
a significant contribution
significant details
statistically significant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: important - having great significance or value
  • Antonym: insignificant - not important or noteworthy

Etymology

From Latin significans, present participle of significare (to signify).

5. ADJ

/ sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a result, difference, or effect) too great or correlated to be due to chance alone; indicating a real or systematic relationship, especially in statistics.
statistically meaningful; not due to chance

Examples

The results of the experiment were significant at the 0.05 level, indicating a strong effect.
There was no significant change in the patients' blood pressure after treatment.
A significant correlation was found between exercise frequency and improved mood.

Synset Examples

the interaction effect is significant at the .01 level
no significant difference was found

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insignificant - not large or important enough to be considered statistically meaningful

Etymology

From Latin significans, significare (to signify, indicate).
