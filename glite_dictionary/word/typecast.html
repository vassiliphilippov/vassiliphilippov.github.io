to typecast

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪpˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to identify or regard someone or something as belonging to a particular type or group, often based on superficial characteristics
to classify as belonging to a type

Examples

It is unfair to typecast children based on their family backgrounds.
He was often typecast as the villain because of his appearance.
Some critics typecast the novel as just another detective story, ignoring its deeper themes.

Synset Examples

Such people can practically be typed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereotype - to have an oversimplified and fixed idea about a group or type

Etymology

From 'type' + 'cast', originally used in theater for assigning actors to roles based on type.

2. VERB

/ ˈtaɪpˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpkɑːst / (BrE)
to always give an actor the same type of character to play, or to always consider someone as having only a particular skill or quality
to assign the same type of role repeatedly

Examples

He was typecast as a villain after his breakout role in the movie.
Many actors fear being typecast and missing out on a variety of roles.
The director decided not to typecast her, offering her a part unlike any she had played before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereotype - to unfairly categorize someone based on a perceived type

Etymology

From 'type' (kind or class) + 'cast' (to assign a role in a play or film).
