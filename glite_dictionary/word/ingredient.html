ingredient

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (BrE)
a substance that is part of a mixture, especially in food or chemical compounds
component of a mixture or compound

Examples

Flour is the main ingredient in this bread recipe.
Each ingredient in the new shampoo was carefully chosen for its benefits.
You should check the ingredient list to ensure it does not contain allergens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole
  • Synonym: additive - a substance added to something in small quantities, typically to improve or preserve it
  • Antonym: whole - something that is complete in itself; contrasting with a part or ingredient

Etymology

From Latin 'ingredior' (to enter), via Medieval Latin 'ingredientem' (entering), as in entering into a mixture.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (BrE)
a food item that is one of the components used to make a dish or recipe
food component of a recipe

Examples

The chef carefully measured each ingredient before adding it to the bowl.
Flour is a key ingredient in most types of bread.
Always check the label to see what ingredients are in the snack.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additive - a substance added to something to improve or preserve it

Etymology

From Latin 'ingredī' (to enter), via Medieval Latin 'ingredientia' (that which enters in), referring to what enters into a mixture.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (BrE)
a component or element that is part of a mixture, situation, or abstract whole
component or element of something

Examples

A sense of humor is an important ingredient of a happy relationship.
Dedication was a key ingredient in her remarkable success.
Innovation is often an essential ingredient in breakthrough discoveries.

Synset Examples

the grammatical elements of a sentence
a key factor in her success
humor: an effective ingredient of a speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole
  • Synonym: constituent - an essential part or element of something
  • Synonym: element - a basic or essential part of something

Etymology

From Latin 'ingredior' (to enter, go into), via Medieval Latin 'ingredientem' (present participle) meaning entrant.
