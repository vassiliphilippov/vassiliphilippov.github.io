to seeping

1. VERB

/ ˈsipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
flowing or leaking out slowly, especially through small openings or porous material
leaking or flowing out slowly

Examples

Rainwater is seeping through the cracks in the basement walls.
An eerie mist was seeping into the valley at dawn.
Oil was seeping from the damaged pipe, creating a dark stain on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material or small holes

Etymology

Present participle of 'seep', from Middle English 'sipen' (to soak, percolate).
