Squalus suckleyi

1. NOUN

/ ˈskweɪləs sʌkˈlaɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈskwaləs sʌkˈliː.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of dogfish shark found along the Pacific coast of North America.
Pacific coast dogfish shark species

Examples

Researchers spotted several Squalus suckleyi near the coast during the marine survey.
The population of Squalus suckleyi has remained stable compared to other shark species in the region.
Fishermen often encounter Squalus suckleyi while trawling off the Pacific Northwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogfish - a small shark of the family Squalidae

Etymology

From Latin; Squalus meaning 'shark' and named after George Suckley, American naturalist.
