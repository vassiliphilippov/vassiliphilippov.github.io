Taraxacum ruderalia

1. NOUN

/ təˈræksəkəm ruːdəˈreɪliə / (AmE), / təˈraksəkəm ruːdəˈreɪlɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant species of the dandelion genus, widely naturalized as a weed in North America, used in salads and to make wine.
species of dandelion plant

Examples

Some people cultivate Taraxacum ruderalia for its edible leaves and roots.
The presence of Taraxacum ruderalia in lawns is considered a nuisance by many gardeners.
Researchers study Taraxacum ruderalia for its potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - common name for plants in Taraxacum genus

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Taraxacum and specific epithet ruderalia (of waste ground).
