parasitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌparəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by parasites.
caused by or relating to parasites
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed a parasitic infection after examining the patient's symptoms.
Some plants, like mistletoe, live a parasitic lifestyle by taking nutrients from their hosts.
The research team studied the complex parasitic relationship between tapeworms and their animal hosts.

Synset Examples

parasitic infection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitical - alternative form meaning 'relating to or caused by parasites'
  • Synonym: parasitism - the condition of living as a parasite
  • Antonym: host - the organism a parasite lives on or in

Etymology

From Greek 'parasitos' (one who eats at another's table), via Latin 'parasiticus'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of plants, animals, or people) having the nature or habits of a parasite; living by taking advantage of or deriving benefit from others without giving anything in return.
living off another; like a parasite

Examples

The parasitic plant drew nutrients from its host tree, eventually weakening it.
Some critics argue that inherited wealth can lead to a parasitic elite class.
The parasitic relationship between certain fungi and trees is fascinating to scientists.

Synset Examples

a wealthy class parasitic upon the labor of the masses
parasitic vines that strangle the trees
bloodsucking blackmailer
his indolent leechlike existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitical - variant form, also meaning living like a parasite
  • Antonym: self-sufficient - able to maintain oneself without relying on others

Etymology

From Greek *parasitos* (person eating at another's table).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00730167-s
Synset POS: s
