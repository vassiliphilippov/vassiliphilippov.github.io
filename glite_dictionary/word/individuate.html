to individuate

1. VERB

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to give distinctive or individual character to something; to make distinct from others
to make distinct or individual

Examples

The artist sought to individuate each sculpture by adding unique details.
Education should help individuate students by nurturing their personal strengths.
The therapist worked to individuate her clients' identities apart from family expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differentiate - to recognize or create differences between things

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'individuatus', past participle of 'individuare' (to make individual), from Latin 'individuus' (indivisible).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjueɪt / (BrE)
to give individual character or form to; to distinguish something as an individual entity
give individual character or form to

Examples

Psychological theories often seek to individuate each person’s experience within a broader framework.
The sculptor aimed to individuate each statue by giving it a unique expression.
Careful observation helps teachers individuate the needs of every student in a diverse classroom.

Synset Examples

Language that individuates his memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differentiate - to recognize or show the differences between things
  • Synonym: to identify - to recognize someone or something as being a particular person or thing

Etymology

From medieval Latin individuatus, past participle of individuare (to make individual), from Latin individuus (indivisible, individual).
