subjacent

1. ADJ

/ səbˈdʒeɪsənt / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒeɪsənt / (BrE)
situated directly below or underlying something else, especially in a physical or spatial sense.
lying or situated below

Examples

Low hills and their subjacent valleys form the landscape of this region.
A protective layer covers the subjacent rock formation from erosion.
Engineers studied the subjacent structures before starting the construction project.

Synset Examples

hills and subjacent valleys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underlying - situated beneath something else

Etymology

From Latin subjacentem, present participle of subjacēre (to lie under).
