bather

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪðər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːðə / (BrE)
a person who swims or bathes in a body of water, especially for recreation
person who swims or bathes in water

Examples

In the early morning, a solitary bather entered the cold lake for a swim.
The beach was crowded with bathers enjoying the summer sun and waves.
A lifeguard keeps a watchful eye on every bather in the pool.

Synset Examples

he is not a good swimmer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimmer - a person who swims

Etymology

From 'bathe' + '-er', denoting a person who bathes or swims.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪðər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪðə / (BrE)
a person who is bathing, especially in a bath or in water such as a pool or the sea
person bathing or swimming

Examples

The public pool was crowded with bathers on a hot summer afternoon.
A sign warned bathers not to swim beyond the marked area.
Early morning bathers enjoyed the calm waters of the lake.

Synset Examples

the bathers enjoyed the cool water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimmer - person who swims, especially for sport or recreation

Etymology

From bath + -er, denoting someone who bathes.
