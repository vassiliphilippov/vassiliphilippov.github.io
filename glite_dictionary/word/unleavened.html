unleavened

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɛvənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɛvənd / (BrE)
(of bread, cakes, etc.) made without any leavening agent such as yeast or baking powder.
made without leavening agent

Examples

Matzos are an example of unleavened bread used in Jewish traditions.
Some diets require eating only unleavened foods for a certain period.
The bakery offers both unleavened and traditional loaves for customers to choose from.

Synset Examples

unleavened bread is often simply flour mixed with water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leavened - made with leavening agent such as yeast or baking powder

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) + leavened (containing leaven).
