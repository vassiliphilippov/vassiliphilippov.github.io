seamanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.mən.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsiː.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill, techniques, or practice of navigating, managing, and operating a ship or boat
skill in sailing or managing ships

Examples

His seamanship saved the crew during the violent storm at sea.
She was admired for her excellent seamanship and calm decision-making under pressure.
Years of training and experience contributed to his remarkable seamanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigation - the act or science of plotting and directing the course of ships
  • Synonym: pilotage - the skill or act of guiding ships through difficult waters

Etymology

From seaman + -ship, modelled after words denoting skill or craft.
