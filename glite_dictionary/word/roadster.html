roadster

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd.stər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd.stə / (BrE)
a small, lightweight, horse-drawn carriage designed for use by one person and pulled by a single horse
small one-horse carriage
archaic

Examples

The wealthy gentleman arrived at the party in his elegant roadster.
She cherished the afternoons spent riding through the countryside in a roadster.
A single horse was harnessed to the roadster waiting outside the inn.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd.stə / (BrE)
an open car with two seats, often with an additional folding seat behind, typically designed for driving pleasure rather than practicality.
open two-seater automobile

Examples

He restored a vintage roadster from the 1930s and takes it to classic car shows.
The roadster provided little protection from the rain, but its style was unmatched.
She always dreamed of driving down the coast in a red roadster with the top down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convertible - a car with a folding or detachable roof
  • Antonym: coupe - a car with a fixed roof and two doors

Etymology

From 'road' + '-ster', originally used in the 19th century for horses suited to travel on roads, later applied to cars.
