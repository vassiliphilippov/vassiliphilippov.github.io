scandalmonger

1. NOUN

/ ˈskændlˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈskændlˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who delights in spreading malicious gossip or scandal about others
person who spreads gossip or scandal

Examples

The scandalmonger couldn't resist telling everyone about the rumor she had overheard.
Newspapers accused of being scandalmongers often prioritize sensational stories over factual reporting.
He resented being labeled a scandalmonger just for sharing what he'd heard in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - a person who habitually reveals personal information about others
  • Synonym: tale-bearer - a person who maliciously spreads secrets or rumors
  • Antonym: truth-teller - someone who tells the truth and does not spread rumors

Etymology

From 'scandal' (disgraceful event or gossip) + 'monger' (dealer or trader), ultimately from Old English 'mangere'.
