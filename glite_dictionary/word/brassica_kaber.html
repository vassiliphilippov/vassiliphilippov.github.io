Brassica kaber

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈkeɪbər / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈkeɪbə / (BrE)
a weedy Eurasian plant of the mustard family, often a pest in grain fields
a weedy plant species, pest in grain fields

Examples

Brassica kaber can quickly overtake fields and reduce crop yields.
Farmers often struggle to control the spread of Brassica kaber in their grain fields.
The bright yellow flowers of Brassica kaber make it easy to spot among other weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mustard - common name for Brassica kaber, a weedy Eurasian plant

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Brassica' for cabbages, species 'kaber,' scientific naming convention.
