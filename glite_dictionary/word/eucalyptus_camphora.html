Eucalyptus camphora

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkælɪptəs kæmˈfɔrə / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs kæmˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A medium-sized species of swamp gum native to New South Wales and Victoria, Australia, known for its smooth bark and camphor-scented leaves.
species of Australian swamp gum tree

Examples

The leaves of Eucalyptus camphora emit a distinctive camphor scent when crushed.
Botanists conducted a survey of Eucalyptus camphora populations in southern New South Wales.
Eucalyptus camphora thrives in wetland areas and helps stabilize the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp gum - a common name for several species of Eucalyptus found in wet areas

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'eukalyptos' (well-covered), referring to the bud cap; species name from Latin 'camphora' (camphor), due to the leaf scent.
