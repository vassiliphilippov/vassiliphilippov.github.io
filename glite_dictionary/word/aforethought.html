aforethought

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɔrˌθɔt / (AmE), / əˈfɔːˌθɔːt / (BrE)
(formal) planned or intended beforehand, especially used in legal contexts to indicate something done with prior intention.
planned or intended beforehand

Examples

The jury had to decide if the killing was committed with malice aforethought.
The contract was drawn up with aforethought to protect both parties' interests.
His actions were not impulsive; they were deliberate and carefully aforethought.

Synset Examples

with malice aforethought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premeditated - planned or thought out in advance
  • Antonym: spontaneous - done without planning or premeditation

Etymology

From Middle English āforþought, from afore (before) + thought.
