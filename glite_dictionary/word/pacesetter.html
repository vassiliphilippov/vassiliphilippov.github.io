pacesetter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsˌsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌsɛtə / (BrE)
a horse that is used to establish the speed for a race, often to encourage a fast pace for others
horse used to set pace in racing

Examples

The trainer used a pacesetter to ensure a fast early tempo in the race.
Without a strong pacesetter, the other horses started off slowly.
The pacesetter tired quickly after leading the pack for half the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacemaker - a horse or person that sets the pace in a race

Etymology

From 'pace' (speed) + 'setter' (one who sets).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsˌsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌsɛtə / (BrE)
An individual or thing that serves as an example or standard for others to follow, especially by being the most progressive or successful in a particular area.
leading example; standard-setter

Examples

The company's innovative design made it a true pacesetter within the industry.
Her research has become a pacesetter for others in the scientific community to follow.
The new smartphone is considered a pacesetter in mobile technology.

Synset Examples

the new policy will be a pacesetter in community relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trailblazer - a person who is the first to do something; pioneer
  • Synonym: leader - a person or thing that leads; foremost in any field
  • Antonym: follower - a person or thing that comes after another

Etymology

Formed from 'pace' (meaning speed or rate) and 'setter' (one who sets or establishes), first attested early 20th century.
