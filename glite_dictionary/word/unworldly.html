unworldly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɜrldli / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːldli / (BrE)
lacking practical experience or sophistication; not knowledgeable about the ways of the world.
lacking worldly experience or sophistication

Examples

She was seen as too unworldly to thrive in the competitive corporate environment.
His unworldly honesty often left him vulnerable to those with less scruples.
Despite her unworldly nature, people admired her sincerity and kindness.

Synset Examples

either too unsophisticated or too honest to promise more than he could deliver
this helplessly unworldly woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From un- (not) + worldly (experienced in the ways of the world).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02280838-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɜrldli / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːldli / (BrE)
not concerned with material or practical matters; not influenced by the pursuits and concerns of the everyday world
not concerned with material or practical matters

Examples

Although he is brilliant, his unworldly nature makes it hard for him to navigate corporate politics.
She had an unworldly charm that set her apart from the rest of her classmates.
His unworldly attitude meant he rarely cared about money or influence.

Synset Examples

was unworldly and did not greatly miss worldly rewards

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worldly - concerned with material values or ordinary life

Etymology

From un- (not) + worldly (concerned with the world), combining prefix and adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02588673-a
Synset POS: a
