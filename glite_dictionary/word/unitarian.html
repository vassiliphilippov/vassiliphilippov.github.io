Unitarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Unitarianism or its followers, who believe in the oneness of God rather than the Trinity
relating to Unitarianism or its followers

Examples

The Unitarian church welcomed people of all backgrounds to its Sunday services.
Her beliefs reflected a Unitarian approach, emphasizing reason and individual conscience.
Several towns in New England have historic Unitarian congregations dating back to the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Trinitarian - relating to the Christian doctrine of the Trinity

Etymology

From Latin 'unitarius' (relating to unity), from 'unitas' (oneness) + English suffix '-ian'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02967542-a

2. NOUN

/ juːˈtɛriən / (AmE), / juːˈtɪəriən / (BrE)
a person who belongs to or supports Unitarianism, especially as a member of the Unitarian church
adherent of Unitarianism

Examples

The Unitarian community met every Sunday for worship and discussion.
She was raised as a Unitarian and values openness to different beliefs.
Many Unitarians are involved in social justice and interfaith work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Unitarianism - the doctrine emphasizing the oneness of God and rejecting the Trinity

Etymology

From Latin 'unitarius' (relating to unity), referring to the Christian doctrine of the unity of God.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10758327-n
Synset POS: n
