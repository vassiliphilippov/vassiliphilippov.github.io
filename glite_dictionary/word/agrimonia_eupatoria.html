Agrimonia eupatoria

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.rəˈmoʊ.ni.ə ˌjuː.pəˈtɔːr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌaɡ.rɪˈməʊ.ni.ə ˌjuː.pəˈtɔː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A species of upright perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, found in dry, grassy habitats; commonly known as common agrimony.
a species of perennial herb (common agrimony)

Examples

Agrimonia eupatoria is often found growing along hedgerows and dry roadsides across Europe.
Herbalists have long used extracts from Agrimonia eupatoria in traditional remedies.
The delicate yellow flowers of Agrimonia eupatoria bloom from early summer through autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agrimony - the common name for plants of the genus Agrimonia

Etymology

From Latin 'agrimonia,' derived from Greek 'argemōnē' (a kind of poppy), and 'eupatoria,' from King Mithridates Eupator.
