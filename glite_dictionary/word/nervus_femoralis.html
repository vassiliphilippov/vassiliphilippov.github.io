nervus femoralis

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəs fɛməˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs fɛməˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
one of a pair of nerves that arise from the lumbar spinal nerves and supply the muscles and skin of the front part of the thigh; also known as the femoral nerve
major nerve of the front part of the thigh
medical

Examples

Injury to the nervus femoralis can lead to difficulty in extending the knee.
The nervus femoralis is vital for sensation in the anterior thigh region.
Surgeons must carefully identify the nervus femoralis during hip replacement procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: femoral nerve - the common English name for nervus femoralis

Etymology

From Latin: 'nervus' meaning 'nerve' and 'femoralis' meaning 'of the thigh' (from 'femur', thigh).
