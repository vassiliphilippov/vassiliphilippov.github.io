petrissage

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtrɪˈsɑʒ / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrɪˈsɑːʒ / (BrE)
A massage technique in which the skin and underlying muscles are gently lifted, kneaded, and squeezed.
massage technique of kneading and squeezing
medical

Examples

The therapist used petrissage on my shoulders to relieve muscle tension.
During the massage, petrissage was applied to improve circulation.
She prefers petrissage over other massage techniques for her sore back.

Etymology

From French 'pétrir' (to knead), related to Latin 'petrire'.
