path

1. NOUN

/ pæθ / (AmE), / pɑːθ / (BrE)
the course or direction along which something or someone moves
route or course of movement

Examples

The tornado cut an unpredictable path across the countryside, destroying everything in its way.
The spacecraft followed a precise path through the solar system to reach Mars.
The river has carved a winding path through the mountains over thousands of years.

Synset Examples

the hurricane demolished houses in its path
the track of an animal
the course of the river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: route - a way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination
  • Synonym: track - a course or path along which something moves
  • Antonym: destination - the place to which someone or something is going

Etymology

From Old English pæth (track, way), related to Dutch pad, German Pfad.

2. NOUN

/ pæθ / (AmE), / pɑːθ / (BrE)
a way or track, especially one made or designed for a particular use, such as for walking, cycling, or vehicles
way or track made for use

Examples

We followed the narrow path through the woods until we reached the river.
A new cycling path was built alongside the road for commuters.
The garden has a winding path made of stone slabs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a path or rough road, often for vehicles
  • Synonym: trail - a path or track, especially one through countryside or wilderness

Etymology

From Old English *pæth* (path, road), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03905025-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ pæθ / (AmE), / pɑːθ / (BrE)
a way or track laid down for walking or made by continual treading; an established route for travel or access
a track or route for travel or walking

Examples

The path through the forest was covered in leaves after the storm.
She followed the narrow path down to the river's edge.
A winding path leads visitors from the gate to the old mansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: route - a way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination
  • Synonym: trail - a path or track laid out for walking, riding, or driving
  • Degree Higher: road - a wide way leading from one place to another, especially one with a specially prepared surface

Etymology

From Old English pæth (way, track).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ pæθ / (AmE), / pɑːθ / (BrE)
a course of action or way of behaving that a person chooses to follow
course of conduct or chosen direction

Examples

She chose the path of honesty, even though it was more difficult.
Many young people struggle to find the right path in life.
He decided to change his career path after realizing his passion for teaching.

Synset Examples

the path of virtue
we went our separate ways
our paths in life led us apart
genius usually follows a revolutionary path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: course - a direction or route taken or to be taken
  • Synonym: way - a method, style, or manner of doing something

Etymology

From Old English *pæth* (track, way, or road), influenced by Old High German *pfad* and Old Norse *patr*.
