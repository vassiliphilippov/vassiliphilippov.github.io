nebbish

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛb.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɛb.ɪʃ / (BrE)
a person who is timid, weak, or ineffectual, often viewed with mild pity or contempt
timid or ineffectual person

Examples

Everyone treated the nebbish at the office as if he were invisible.
The play’s main character is a nebbish who struggles to assert himself in a crowded city.
Despite being dismissed as a nebbish, he finally found the courage to speak up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milquetoast - a person who is timid or submissive
  • Synonym: loser - a person who is habitually unsuccessful or inept

Etymology

From Yiddish 'nebekh' (poor thing, unfortunate one).
