Piroplasma

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈplæzmə / (AmE), / ˌpɪərəˈplazmə / (BrE)
A genus of protozoan parasites in the family Babesiidae, which infect the blood of various mammals.
genus of blood parasites (Babesiidae)
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered the presence of Piroplasma in several wild animals.
The disease is caused by a blood parasite from the genus Piroplasma.
Microscopic examination confirmed an infection by Piroplasma in the canine patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babesia - genus of protozoan parasites in the family Babesiidae

Etymology

From Greek 'peira' (an attempt, trial) and 'plasma' (something formed or molded), originally relating to its appearance under the microscope.
