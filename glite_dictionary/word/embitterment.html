embitterment

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɪtərmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɪtəmənt / (BrE)
A feeling or state of being filled with bitterness or resentment.
state of being embittered

Examples

His long years of disappointment led to a deep embitterment that affected all his relationships.
Political injustice fueled the embitterment of the local population.
Despite her embitterment over the betrayal, she tried to move forward with her life.

Synset Examples

the embitterment that resulted from the loss of his job never left him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterness - sharpness of feeling; resentment or cynicism
  • Synonym: resentment - bitter indignation at having been treated unfairly
  • Antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction

Etymology

From 'embitter' + '-ment', ultimately based on Old French 'amertume' meaning bitterness.
