mainland

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnlənd / (BrE)
the principal landmass of a country or continent, as distinct from its islands or peninsulas
the main landmass of a country or continent

Examples

Many tourists visit the Hawaiian Islands as well as the mainland United States.
After spending months on the remote island, she was eager to return to the mainland.
Ferries run regularly between the mainland and the nearby archipelago.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: island - a piece of land surrounded by water

Etymology

From main (principal) + land; attested since the 16th century.
