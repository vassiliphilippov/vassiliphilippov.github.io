Rh

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɑː eɪtʃ / (BrE)
a chemical element, a hard silvery-white metal of the platinum group, found in platinum ores and used in alloys and as a catalyst
chemical symbol for rhodium, a metallic element

Examples

The catalytic converter contains trace amounts of Rh to reduce emissions.
Platinum and Rh are often found together in nature.
Researchers studied alloys made from Rh and platinum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhodium - a hard silvery-white metallic element

Etymology

From Greek 'rhodon' (rose), referring to the rose color of some of its salts.

2. NOUN

/ ɑr eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɑː ɪtʃ / (BrE)
a protein found on the surface of red blood cells in most people, used to determine blood group compatibility, especially in transfusions; also called the Rh factor.
a blood group antigen important in transfusions
medical

Examples

All donors are screened for Rh compatibility before giving blood.
The baby's Rh status was tested immediately after birth.
Being Rh-negative can complicate pregnancy if the fetus is Rh-positive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rh factor - the same antigen, often referred to as 'Rh factor'
  • Degree Higher: ABO blood group - another major blood group system

Etymology

Abbreviation of Rhesus, from the Rhesus monkey, in which the antigen was first discovered.
