footfault

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌfɔlt / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌfɔːlt / (BrE)
(in tennis) a fault that occurs when the server fails to keep both feet behind the baseline during the serve.
fault in tennis when feet cross baseline

Examples

The umpire called a footfault after she stepped on the baseline during her serve.
He lost the point because of a footfault in the final game.
Repeated footfaults can disrupt a player's rhythm in a tennis match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fault - an incorrect action in tennis resulting in loss of point or serve

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' + 'fault', referencing a fault made with the feet in tennis.
