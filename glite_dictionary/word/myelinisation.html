myelinisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˌlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.ɪ.lɪ.nɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a myelin sheath forms around a nerve fiber.
formation of myelin sheath around nerve fiber
medical british

Examples

The rapid myelinisation of nerve fibers is critical for brain development in infants.
Delayed myelinisation can affect the speed of nerve signal transmission.
Researchers are studying disorders linked to abnormal myelinisation in the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myelination - formation of a myelin sheath around a nerve fiber (US spelling)

Etymology

From myelin + -isation, based on Greek 'myelos' (marrow) and medical suffix '-isation'.
