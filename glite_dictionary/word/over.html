over

1. ADV

/ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
To or at a place on the other side of an intervening space.
across to the other side

Examples

She asked me to come over after work for dinner.
Look over there—do you see the rainbow?
The children ran over to their friends on the other side of the park.

Synset Examples

come over and see us some time
over there

2. ADV

/ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something happens during the whole period of a stated time.
throughout a period of time

Examples

They worked over the summer to finish the project.
She completed the assignment over the weekend.
Attendance increased steadily over the last five years.

Synset Examples

stay over the weekend

Etymology

From Old English 'ofer' (above, across, beyond).

5. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
In cricket, a set of six consecutive balls bowled by a single bowler from one end of the pitch to the batsman.
six balls bowled in cricket

Examples

The team scored fifteen runs in the last over of the match.
After bowling three overs, the player was replaced by another bowler.
The umpire signaled the end of the over with a wave of his hand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ball - a single delivery of the cricket ball to the batsman
