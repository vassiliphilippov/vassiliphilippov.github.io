voiced

1. ADJ

/ vɔɪst / (AmE), / vɔɪst / (BrE)
expressed or communicated in spoken or written words; stated aloud or made known verbally.
expressed in words or speech

Examples

Her concerns were voiced during the meeting, ensuring everyone understood her position.
It is important that every idea is voiced in a group discussion.
He never voiced his disappointment, but it was clear from his reaction.

Synset Examples

frequently uttered sentiments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressed - made known in words or by actions
  • Antonym: unspoken - not expressed in words or aloud

Etymology

From voice + -ed, indicating having been voiced (expressed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02291757-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ vɔɪst / (AmE), / vɔɪst / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords.
with vocal cord vibration (phonetics)

Examples

The English 'z' is a voiced consonant, while 's' is voiceless.
Some children find it difficult to distinguish between voiced and voiceless sounds.
In the word 'bag', all three sounds are voiced.

Synset Examples

voiced consonants such as ‘b’ and ‘g’ and ‘z’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voiceless - (of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02293817-a
Synset POS: a
