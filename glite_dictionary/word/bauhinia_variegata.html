Bauhinia variegata

1. NOUN

/ baʊˈhɪniə ˌvɛəriˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / bɔːˈhɪniə ˌveərɪˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
A small tree native to India and Southeast Asia, known for its orchid-like flowers and hard, dark wood.
species of small flowering tree

Examples

The Bauhinia variegata is widely admired for its striking, orchid-like blossoms in shades of pink and white.
In many gardens across Southeast Asia, Bauhinia variegata adds a splash of color in spring.
Local craftsmen use the dense wood of Bauhinia variegata for making durable tools and furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid tree - another common name for Bauhinia variegata

Etymology

From genus name Bauhinia (after Swiss botanists Jean and Gaspard Bauhin) and species Latin 'variegata' (variegated).
