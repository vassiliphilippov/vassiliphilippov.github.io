mutability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being capable of change or alteration.
the ability to change

Examples

The mutability of fashion means trends can fade quickly.
Scientists are fascinated by the mutability of viruses over time.
Her poetry often explores the mutability of human emotions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immutability - the quality of being unable to change

Etymology

From Latin 'mutabilis' (changeable) via Old French 'mutabilite'.
