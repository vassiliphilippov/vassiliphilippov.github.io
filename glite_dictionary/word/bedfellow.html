bedfellow

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌfɛləʊ / (BrE)
a person or thing closely connected with another, often temporarily or unexpectedly, for a particular purpose
temporary associate or partner

Examples

In politics, unusual alliances can make bedfellows of rivals.
Environmental groups and oil companies became unlikely bedfellows during the negotiations.
The crisis created strange bedfellows in the business world.

Synset Examples

politics makes strange bedfellows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - a person who takes part in an activity with another

Etymology

Derived from 'bed' + 'fellow,' originally meaning someone who shares a bed, later used figuratively.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌfɛləʊ / (BrE)
a person who shares a bed with another
person who shares a bed

Examples

The two travelers became unexpected bedfellows due to the crowded inn.
He never imagined his roommate would become his bedfellow during the expedition.
In old stories, kings and advisors often ended up as bedfellows to save space.

Etymology

From 'bed' + 'fellow', meaning a companion in a bed.
