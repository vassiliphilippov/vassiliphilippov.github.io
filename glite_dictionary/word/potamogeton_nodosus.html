Potamogeton nodosus

1. NOUN

/ pəˌtæməˈdʒitɒn ˈnoʊdəsəs / (AmE), / pəˌtaməˈdʒiːtɒn ˈnəʊdəsəs / (BrE)
A species of pondweed with floating leaves, found in the northern United States and Europe.
aquatic plant species with floating leaves

Examples

Potamogeton nodosus often appears in calm, shallow lakes across North America.
The floating leaves of Potamogeton nodosus provide habitat for aquatic insects and fish.
Researchers studied the spread of Potamogeton nodosus in European river systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pondweed - any aquatic plant of the genus Potamogeton

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Potamogeton from Greek potamos (river) + geiton (neighbor), and species name nodosus from Latin nodosus (knotty).
