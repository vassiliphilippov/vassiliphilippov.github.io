bolshy

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊlʃi / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃi / (BrE)
Deliberately combative, uncooperative, or defiant, often in a challenging or rebellious way.
deliberately combative or defiant
informal british

Examples

He's always so bolshy in meetings, never willing to go along with the group.
Her bolshy attitude made negotiations very difficult.
The team manager warned the new player not to get too bolshy during training sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - openly resisting or challenging authority
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to obey or cooperate

Etymology

From 'Bolshevik', used informally as an abbreviation and generalized term for someone disruptive or dissenting.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɒlʃi / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃi / (BrE)
Deliberately difficult, uncooperative, or defiant, especially in a way that challenges authority; obstreperous.
defiantly uncooperative; obstreperous
informal british

Examples

The team became bolshy when asked to work overtime for the third night in a row.
Don't get bolshy with your manager just because you disagree with her.
As a teenager, he was notorious for his bolshy attitude towards authority figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstreperous - noisy and difficult to control; defiant
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to obey or cooperate

Etymology

Alteration of 'Bolshevik', used informally in British English for rebelliousness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02335307-s
Synset POS: s
