Zalophus lobatus

1. NOUN

/ zəˈlɑː.fəs loʊˈbeɪ.təs / (AmE), / zəˈləʊ.fəs ləʊˈbɑː.təs / (BrE)
a species of sea lion found in Australia
Australian sea lion species

Examples

The Zalophus lobatus is native to the southern coasts of Australia.
Marine biologists conducted a study on Zalophus lobatus populations in their natural habitat.
Unlike other sea lions, Zalophus lobatus has distinct physical features adapted to Australian waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lion - large marine mammal of the family Otariidae

Etymology

From Greek 'zalophos' (crested) and Latin 'lobatus' (having lobes), following scientific binomial naming conventions.
