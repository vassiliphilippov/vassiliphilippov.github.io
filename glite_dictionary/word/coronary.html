coronary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ə.nər.i / (BrE)
A blockage of blood flow in a coronary artery, often leading to a heart attack.
heart attack caused by artery blockage
medical

Examples

After his coronary, he made significant changes to his lifestyle.
She was rushed to the hospital when the doctors suspected a coronary.
Family history of coronaries can increase a person's risk for heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart attack - a sudden and sometimes fatal occurrence of coronary thrombosis

Etymology

From Latin 'coronarius' (of a crown), referring to the crown-like arteries encircling the heart.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔːrəˌneri / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ə.nər.i / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting the arteries that surround and supply the heart, or resembling a crown in position or arrangement.
relating to the arteries of the heart
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the coronary arteries for any signs of blockage.
After his heart attack, he underwent a coronary bypass procedure.
Plaque buildup in the coronary vessels can seriously affect heart function.

Synset Examples

coronary arteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiac - relating to the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'coronarius', from 'corona' meaning 'crown', referring to the crown-like arrangement of the vessels around the heart.
