abiotrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.baɪˈɑː.trə.fi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.baɪˈɒ.trə.fi / (BrE)
(medical) The degeneration of cells or tissues due to loss of vitality, not caused by injury.
degeneration of tissues due to loss of vitality
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from abiotrophy, leading to progressive muscle weakness.
Certain neurodegenerative diseases are classified as forms of abiotrophy.
Recent studies investigate the genetic basis of abiotrophy in animal models.

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + bios (life) + trophē (nourishment).
