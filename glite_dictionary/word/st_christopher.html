St. Christopher

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfə / (BrE)
A Christian martyr and the patron saint of travellers, believed to have lived in the 3rd century.
Christian patron saint of travellers

Examples

Many people carry a St. Christopher medal for protection when they travel.
The church dedicated to St. Christopher is a popular pilgrimage site.
Legend says that St. Christopher carried a child across a dangerous river.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfə / (BrE)
the largest island of the nation Saint Kitts and Nevis in the West Indies, also known as Saint Kitts.
island in Saint Kitts and Nevis

Examples

The beautiful beaches of St. Christopher attract visitors from all over the world.
Sugar cane was once a key crop on St. Christopher.
Many cruise ships make a stop at St. Christopher during their Caribbean routes.

Etymology

Named after Saint Christopher, an early Christian martyr.
