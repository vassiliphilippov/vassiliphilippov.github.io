lading

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
goods or cargo that are being transported, especially by ship, truck, or train
cargo; goods being transported

Examples

The lading was carefully inspected before unloading at the port.
Each truck carried a different lading of raw materials to the factory.
All necessary documents for the lading must be presented to customs officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cargo - goods carried by a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle
  • Synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft

Etymology

From Old English 'hladan' (to load), via Middle English 'lading' (loading).
