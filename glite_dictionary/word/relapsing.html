relapsing

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlæpsɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈlapsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of returning to a previous, often undesirable, state, especially after a period of improvement.
return to a previous bad state
medical

Examples

After months of sobriety, his relapsing set back his progress significantly.
The doctor discussed the possible causes of relapsing in patients with chronic illnesses.
Support groups try to reduce the risk of relapsing among members with addictive behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - a return to a worse condition after partial recovery

Etymology

From the verb 'relapse,' from Latin 'relapsus,' past participle of 'relabi' (to slip back).
