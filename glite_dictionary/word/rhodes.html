Rhodes

1. PROPN

/ roʊdz / (AmE), / rəʊdz / (BrE)
a Greek island in the southeast Aegean Sea, near the Turkish coast, known for its historical significance and as the site of the ancient Colossus of Rhodes
Greek island in the Aegean Sea

Examples

The ancient city on Rhodes attracts thousands of tourists each year.
She dreamed of sailing to Rhodes to explore its historic ruins and sunny beaches.
The Colossus of Rhodes was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

2. PROPN

/ roʊdz / (AmE), / rəʊdz / (BrE)
(Cecil John Rhodes) (1853–1902), British colonial administrator and financier in South Africa, known for his role in mining, imperial expansion, and for founding the Rhodes Scholarship.
British colonial statesman and founder of the Rhodes Scholarship
british

Examples

Rhodes founded the De Beers diamond company in South Africa.
The Rhodes Scholarship continues to attract students from around the world.
Many historians debate the legacy of Rhodes as both a visionary and a controversial imperialist.
