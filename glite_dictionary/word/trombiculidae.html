Trombiculidae

1. NOUN

/ trɒmˌbɪkjʊˈlaɪdiː / (AmE), / trɒmˌbɪkjʊˈliːdɪ / (BrE)
A family of mites whose larvae are parasitic and can cause skin irritation in humans and animals, commonly known as chiggers or harvest mites.
family of parasitic mites (chiggers)
medical

Examples

After hiking through tall grass, she discovered itchy red welts caused by Trombiculidae larvae.
The doctor identified the rash as a reaction to bites from Trombiculidae.
Many campers use insect repellent to avoid Trombiculidae infestations in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chigger - the parasitic larval form of Trombiculidae mites

Etymology

From New Latin Trombiculidae, based on the type genus 'Trombicula'.

2. NOUN

/ trɑmˌbɪkjəˈlaɪdiː / (AmE), / trɒmˌbɪkjʊˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mites whose larvae are known as chiggers or harvest mites, often causing itching or dermatitis in humans and animals
a family of mites including chiggers
medical

Examples

The larvae of the Trombiculidae family are commonly referred to as chiggers.
Many cases of itching and skin irritation are caused by mites in the Trombiculidae family.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Trombiculidae during their field study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chigger - the larval form of certain mites in the Trombiculidae family, known for causing skin irritation
  • Synonym: mite - a very small arachnid related to ticks, includes Trombiculidae

Etymology

From New Latin Trombiculidae, formed from the genus Trombicula + -idae (suffix for zoological family names).
