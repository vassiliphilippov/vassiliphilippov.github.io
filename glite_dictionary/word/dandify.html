to dandify

1. VERB

/ ˈdæn.dɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈdæn.dɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(usually passive) To make (oneself or someone) look or behave like a dandy; to dress or adorn in an unusually fashionable or showy manner.
make or dress like a dandy

Examples

He tried to dandify himself for the party, wearing a silk cravat and polished shoes.
Their leader was dandified in a bright waistcoat and fancy cane, standing out from the crowd.
She enjoyed dandifying her little brother for the family photo, adding a bow tie and combing his hair neatly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes
  • Antonym: undress - to remove one's clothes

Etymology

Formed from "dandy" (a man unduly concerned with fashion) + "-ify" (verb-forming suffix meaning 'to make').
