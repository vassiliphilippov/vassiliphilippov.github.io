to altercate

1. VERB

/ ˈɔltərˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːltəkeɪt / (BrE)
to argue or dispute angrily or vehemently, especially in a formal or prolonged way
argue vehemently or dispute sharply

Examples

The two politicians began to altercate in front of the cameras over the proposed tax reform.
Neighbors often altercate about where the property line actually lies.
She refused to altercate with customers, preferring instead to resolve complaints calmly.

Synset Examples

We quarreled over the question as to who discovered America
These two fellows are always scrapping over something

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to express opposite views in a conversation or debate
  • Synonym: quarrel - to have a heated argument or disagreement
  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion or to consent

Etymology

From Latin altercari (to wrangle, dispute), from alter (other).
