glib

1. ADJ

/ ɡlɪb / (AmE), / ɡlɪb / (BrE)
(of words or a person speaking) fluent but insincere and shallow; lacking intellectual depth.
fluent but insincere or superficial

Examples

Her glib remarks failed to impress the interview panel.
Politicians sometimes give glib answers to difficult questions.
He offered a glib apology that seemed rehearsed rather than heartfelt.

Synset Examples

glib generalizations
a glib response to a complex question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or complete; lacking depth
  • Antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration or deep thought

Etymology

From German glibberig (slippery, smooth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01880815-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɡlɪb / (AmE), / ɡlɪb / (BrE)
(of words or a person speaking) fluent and persuasive but insincere or superficial.
fluent but insincere or superficial

Examples

He made a glib apology, but I doubted he really meant it.
Politicians are often criticized for their glib answers during debates.
Her glib remarks did not convince anyone in the meeting.

Synset Examples

glib promises
a slick commercial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slick - persuasive but insincere or superficial
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine; free from pretense or deceit

Etymology

From German glibberig (slippery, smooth), related to Middle Low German glibberich.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01804305-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɡlɪb / (AmE), / ɡlɪb / (BrE)
(of words or a speaker) fluent and persuasive, but often insincere or superficial.
fluent but insincere (speech)

Examples

The politician gave a glib response to the tough question, avoiding the real issues.
His glib charm made him popular, but many doubted his sincerity.
She was wary of his glib promises and chose not to trust him.

Synset Examples

a glib tongue
a smooth-tongued hypocrite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth-talking - skilled at speaking easily and confidently, especially in a way that is not sincere
  • Antonym: sincere - free from pretense or deceit in expression; genuine

Etymology

From German glibberig (slippery).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01775250-s
Synset POS: s
