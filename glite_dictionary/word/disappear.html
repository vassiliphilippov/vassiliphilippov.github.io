to disappear

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpɪə / (BrE)
to become impossible to see or to notice; to cease to be visible or apparent
to become invisible or unnoticeable

Examples

The magician made the rabbit disappear from the hat.
As the fog rolled in, the mountains slowly disappeared from view.
Her smile would disappear whenever she thought about the accident.

Synset Examples

The effect vanished when day broke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: to appear - to become visible or noticeable

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart, away) + apparēre (to appear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02160976-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪə / (BrE)
to cease to exist, especially in a way that is sudden or cannot be easily explained
cease to exist or be visible

Examples

Entire species can disappear due to environmental changes.
When the fog lifted, the ship had disappeared from view.
Old traditions sometimes slowly disappear as society changes.

Synset Examples

An entire civilization vanished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: to appear - to become visible or to come into existence

Etymology

From French disparaître, from Latin dis- (apart) + parēre (to appear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00428350-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪə / (BrE)
(of a feeling, quality, or condition) to become less intense and gradually fade away until no longer present.
to fade away gradually (figurative)

Examples

Over time, her anger towards him began to disappear.
As the fog of doubt started to disappear, he felt more confident in his decision.
The excitement of the new job didn't disappear even after several months.

Synset Examples

her resistance melted under his charm
her hopes evaporated after years of waiting for her fiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissipate - to disappear or cause to disappear, especially gradually
  • Synonym: to vanish - to cease to be visible or to exist, especially quickly

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + apparēre (to appear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00224354-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪə / (BrE)
to stop being visible or to go somewhere so that nobody knows where you are; to vanish suddenly or without explanation
stop being visible; vanish suddenly

Examples

The cat seemed to disappear into the shadows as night fell.
He watched the ship slowly disappear over the horizon.
My keys always disappear when I need them the most.

Synset Examples

He disappeared without a trace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: to appear - to become visible or come into sight

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'parere' (to appear).
