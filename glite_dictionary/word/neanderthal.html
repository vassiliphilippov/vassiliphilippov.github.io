Neanderthal

1. NOUN

/ niˈæn.dərˌtɑl / (AmE), / niˈændətɑːl / (BrE)
an extinct species or subspecies of robust humans that lived in Europe and western Asia during the Middle Paleolithic period
extinct human species of the Middle Paleolithic

Examples

The skull of a Neanderthal was discovered in a cave in Germany.
Some scientists believe that Neanderthals and modern humans interbred.
Archaeologists study Neanderthal tools to learn about their way of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Homo sapiens - the modern human species

Etymology

Named after the Neander Valley in Germany, where the first fossils were discovered.

2. ADJ

/ niˈæn.dərˌtɑl, niˈæn.dərˌθɔl / (AmE), / niˈɒn.dəˌtɑːl, niˈæn.dəˌθɑːl / (BrE)
relating to, belonging to, or resembling the extinct human species Neanderthal man; primitive or uncivilized (often capitalized).
relating to Neanderthal man or primitive

Examples

The museum displayed a Neanderthal skull alongside other ancient human fossils.
Some scientists are researching Neanderthal DNA in the modern human genome.
His Neanderthal attitude toward change frustrated his colleagues.

Synset Examples

Neanderthal skull

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past

Etymology

From Neanderthal (valley in Germany where remains were discovered), from German 'Neanderthal' ('Neander valley').
