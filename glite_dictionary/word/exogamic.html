exogamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛksəˈɡæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛksəˈɡamɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) suitable for fertilization by another not closely related individual; promoting cross-fertilization.
relating to cross-fertilization in plants

Examples

Some plant species have evolved exogamic flowers to promote genetic diversity.
The exogamic tendency in these wildflowers ensures pollen comes from distant populations.
Researchers studied the exogamic mechanisms that prevent self-fertilization in the species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endogamic - relating to fertilization by a closely related individual

Etymology

From Greek exō (outside) + gamos (marriage).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.səˈɡæm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səˈɡæm.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by the custom of marrying only outside a specific clan, tribe, or social group.
relating to marrying outside a social group

Examples

The tribe followed an exogamic tradition, requiring its members to marry outside their own group.
Among some cultures, exogamic marriages are essential for forming alliances with neighboring communities.
Their exogamic customs helped prevent inbreeding and encouraged social cohesion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endogamic - relating to the custom of marrying within a specific group

Etymology

From Greek exō (outside) + gamos (marriage).

WordNet ID: oewn-00874563-a
Synset POS: a
