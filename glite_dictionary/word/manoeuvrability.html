manoeuvrability

1. NOUN

/ məˌnuːvərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / məˌnjuːvərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to change position or direction easily and effectively.
ability to be maneuvered easily

Examples

The manoeuvrability of the new aircraft impressed test pilots during the demonstration.
Compact cars are often valued for their manoeuvrability in heavy city traffic.
The yacht's excellent manoeuvrability allowed it to avoid hitting the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuverability - alternative (American) spelling: ability to maneuver easily

Etymology

From 'manoeuvre' (from French 'manœuvre', meaning to handle or manage) + '-ability'.
