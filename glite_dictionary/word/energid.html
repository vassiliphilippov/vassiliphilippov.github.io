energid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnərˌɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌɡɪd / (BrE)
a biological unit composed of a nucleus and the surrounding cytoplasm with which it interacts, considered as a single functional entity
biological unit: nucleus and interacting cytoplasm

Examples

In certain algae, each energid plays a role in cellular organization.
The division of an energid may result in two functionally distinct units sharing the same cytoplasm.
Researchers studied the behavior of every energid within the multinucleate cell.

Etymology

From German 'Energid', introduced in 1892 by biologist Richard Altmann, from 'Energie' (energy) + '-id'.
