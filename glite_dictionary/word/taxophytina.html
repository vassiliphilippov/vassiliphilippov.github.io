Taxophytina

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæksəˌfaɪˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌtaksəˌfʌɪˈtʌɪnə / (BrE)
a subdivision in some systems of plant classification, comprising the yews; in various classifications, considered either a class (Taxopsida) or a subdivision of Gymnospermophyta
a botanical subdivision including yews
medical

Examples

The yews are classified under Taxophytina in certain botanical systems.
Some older botany texts refer to Taxophytina as a distinct subdivision of Gymnosperms.
Researchers debated the placement of Taxophytina within the broader classification of gymnosperm plants.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Taxus' (yew) and Greek 'phyton' (plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtæksəˌfaɪˈtiːnə / (AmE), / ˌtaksəˌfɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a taxonomic subdivision used in some classifications to group the yews, sometimes treated as a class (Taxopsida) and considered one of the five subdivisions of Gymnospermophyta
taxonomic subdivision for yews

Examples

The classification of yews within Taxophytina remains a subject of botanical debate.
Some older systems assign yews to the subdivision Taxophytina rather than the class Taxopsida.
Botanists sometimes refer to Taxophytina when discussing gymnosperm diversity.

Etymology

From Greek 'taxis' (arrangement) and 'phyton' (plant), used in taxonomy to name plant subdivisions.
