stranglehold

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋɡəlˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡəlˌhəʊld / (BrE)
A wrestling hold in which the arms are pressed against the opponent's windpipe, restricting their breathing.
wrestling hold that restricts breathing

Examples

The wrestler applied a stranglehold to his opponent, forcing him to surrender.
She broke free from the stranglehold just as the referee counted to three.
His use of the stranglehold was both impressive and intimidating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokehold - a wrestling hold involving pressure on the neck

Etymology

From 'strangle' (Middle English, from Old French 'estrangler', from Latin 'strangulare') + 'hold'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋ.ɡəlˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈstræŋ.ɡəlˌhəʊld / (BrE)
complete and overwhelming control over a person, organization, or situation, preventing change or freedom
complete control; unbreakable grip

Examples

Many believe the company has a stranglehold on the entire market.
The new regulations broke the union's stranglehold over the industry.
For years, corruption maintained a stranglehold on political reform efforts.

Synset Examples

corporations have a stranglehold on the media
the president applied a chokehold to labor disputes that inconvenienced the public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokehold - a firm hold or control, often used metaphorically for dominance
  • Synonym: grip - a firm or controlling grasp, literal or figurative

Etymology

From strangle + hold, originally referring to a tight grip on the neck.
