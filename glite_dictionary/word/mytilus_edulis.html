Mytilus edulis

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɪləs ˈɛd.jʊ.lɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈtɪl.əs ˈɛd.juː.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of edible mussel with a dark blue or black shell, commonly found attached to rocks in coastal waters.
species of edible mussel

Examples

The rocky shores were covered with clusters of Mytilus edulis.
Marine biologists are studying how pollution affects Mytilus edulis populations.
Many seafood dishes feature Mytilus edulis as a main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a type of bivalve mollusc with a brown or black shell, found in both salt and fresh water

Etymology

From Latin: 'Mytilus' meaning mussel and 'edulis' meaning edible.
