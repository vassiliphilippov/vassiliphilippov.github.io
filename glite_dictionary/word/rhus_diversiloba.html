Rhus diversiloba

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs daɪˌvɜrsɪˈloʊbə / (AmE), / ɹʌs dɪˌvɜːsɪˈləʊbə / (BrE)
A poisonous shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, whose contact causes skin irritation or rash; commonly known as Pacific poison oak.
poisonous shrub of Pacific North America
medical

Examples

The hiker developed a severe rash after brushing against Rhus diversiloba along the trail.
Botanists warn that Rhus diversiloba is highly irritant and should be identified accurately in the wild.
Contact with Rhus diversiloba can cause an allergic skin reaction in many people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison oak - Common name for Rhus diversiloba, a poisonous shrub causing rash

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rhus' (sumac) + 'diversiloba' (with diverse lobes, referring to the leaves).
