to shirk

1. VERB

/ ʃɝːk / (AmE), / ʃɜːk / (BrE)
to avoid or neglect (a duty or responsibility), often by pretending to be ill or busy
avoid or neglect a duty or responsibility

Examples

He was known to shirk his household chores whenever possible.
Workers who shirk their responsibilities may face disciplinary action.
She tried to shirk her homework by pretending to be ill.

Synset Examples

The derelict soldier shirked his duties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid or escape from, especially by trickery or cleverness
  • Synonym: neglect - fail to care for or attend to something
  • Antonym: to fulfil - carry out (a duty or role) as required

Etymology

Perhaps from German 'Schurke' (scoundrel).

2. VERB

/ ʃɝrk / (AmE), / ʃɜːk / (BrE)
to avoid doing something you should do, especially because it is difficult or unpleasant
avoid responsibility or duty

Examples

He was accused of trying to shirk his military service.
Some employees tend to shirk their responsibilities when no one is watching.
You can't just shirk your chores and expect someone else to do them.

Synset Examples

She shirks her duties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • Synonym: dodge - to avoid something, especially in a clever or dishonest way
  • Antonym: to fulfill - to do or carry out a duty or responsibility

Etymology

Probably from German *schurke* (scoundrel) or related Low German roots.
