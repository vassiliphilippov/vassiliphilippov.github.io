callithump

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪˌθʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkælɪˌθʌmp / (BrE)
A noisy mock serenade performed by a crowd, often using pans and kettles, especially to celebrate a wedding.
mock noisy serenade, often at weddings

Examples

The newlyweds awoke to a raucous callithump outside their window.
Villagers gathered at night, banging pots in a traditional callithump for the bride and groom.
The sound of the callithump echoed down the street, making everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charivari - a noisy mock serenade for newlyweds, especially in rural communities

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps a fanciful or onomatopoeic coinage in 19th-century American English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌθʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkælɪˌθʌmp / (BrE)
a noisy and boisterous parade, often with music, shouting, and homemade instruments
noisy, boisterous parade

Examples

The small town held a lively callithump to celebrate the end of summer.
Children dressed in costumes joined the callithump, banging pots and pans as they marched down the street.
Spectators laughed and cheered as the noisy callithump made its way through the square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, often celebratory

Etymology

Probably a fanciful coinage, possibly altered from 'Gallithumpians', meaning noisy disturbers, recorded in England in the 19th century.
