to overcook

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkʊk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkʊk / (BrE)
to cook food for too long, making it spoiled, dry, or less tasty
cook for too long

Examples

If you overcook the pasta, it will become mushy and lose its texture.
She tends to overcook chicken, making it dry and tough.
The recipe warns not to overcook the sauce or it will lose its flavor.

Synset Examples

The vegetables were completely overcooked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercook - to cook insufficiently, leaving food not fully done

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' meaning 'excessively' and 'cook'.
