impingement

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
the action or effect of one thing having a strong impact or influence on another, often in a way that is unwanted or intrusive
strong impact or influence, often unwanted

Examples

Some artists worry about the impingement of commercial interests on their creative freedom.
He objected to the government's impingement on personal privacy rights.
The impingement of noise from the nearby construction site made it difficult to concentrate.

Synset Examples

they resented the impingement of American values on European culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - the power to have an effect on someone or something
  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of entering or affecting without invitation or permission

Etymology

From Latin 'impingere' (to thrust upon), via the verb 'impinge' + '-ment'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a sharp collision or forceful contact resulting from striking against something
a forceful collision or contact

Examples

Repeated impingement of the branches can cause damage to the tree bark over time.
The impingement of the ball against the window startled everyone in the room.
Turbine blades are designed to withstand the impingement of high-velocity gas streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impact - the action of one object coming forcibly into contact with another

Etymology

From Latin 'impingere' (to strike against).

Synset POS: n
