alterable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltərəbl̩ / (BrE)
Capable of being changed or modified.
able to be changed

Examples

The contract terms are alterable only under special circumstances.
Many aspects of this schedule remain alterable at the manager's discretion.
The judge ruled that the sentence was alterable based on new evidence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immutable - not able to be changed
  • Synonym: mutable - liable to change

Etymology

From Latin 'alterare' (to change), from 'alter' (other).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔltərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːltərəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being changed or modified in some way
able to be changed or modified

Examples

Some company policies are alterable depending on new regulations.
The contract is alterable before it is signed by both parties.
Her dress was easily alterable to fit a smaller size.

Synset Examples

alterable clothing
alterable conditions of employment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unalterable - not able to be changed
  • Synonym: mutable - liable to change; able to be altered

Etymology

From Latin alterāre (to change), from alter (other).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350397-a
Synset POS: a
