Hydrus

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪdrəs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəs / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near the South Celestial Pole
constellation near South Celestial Pole

Examples

The constellation Hydrus is only visible from the Southern Hemisphere.
Early astronomers identified Hydrus because of its proximity to the South Celestial Pole.
Hydrus is often mistaken for Hydra, but the two are different constellations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

From Latin Hydrus, meaning 'male water snake', modeled on Greek hudros (water snake).
