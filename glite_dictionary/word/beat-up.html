beat-up

1. ADJ

/ ˈbit ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbiːt ˌʌp / (BrE)
(of an object) damaged or worn out from rough treatment or long use.
damaged or worn from use

Examples

He drove an old beat-up truck across the country.
We sat on a beat-up sofa that had seen better days.
The book's cover was completely beat-up after years of use.

Synset Examples

a battered old car
the beaten-up old Ford

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battered - damaged by repeated blows or hard use
  • Antonym: pristine - in perfect condition; not damaged or worn
