cashew

1. NOUN

/ kəˈʃu / (AmE), / kəˈʃuː / (BrE)
A tropical American evergreen tree (Anacardium occidentale) that produces kidney-shaped edible nuts, which must be roasted before eating.
tropical tree producing edible nuts

Examples

A single cashew tree can produce hundreds of nuts each season.
The cashew thrives in tropical climates with sandy soil and plenty of sunlight.
Farmers roast the cashew seeds before selling them as snacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashew nut - the edible nut from the cashew tree

Etymology

From Portuguese 'caju', from Tupian 'acajú'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈʃu / (AmE), / kəˈʃuː / (BrE)
A kidney-shaped, edible nut, typically eaten roasted, that comes from a tropical tree native to South America.
edible kidney-shaped nut

Examples

She sprinkled chopped cashew pieces over her salad for extra crunch.
The recipe calls for roasted cashew nuts as a topping.
A single cashew contains beneficial fats and essential minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond - edible nut of the almond tree
  • Synonym: peanut - edible legume often called a nut

Etymology

From Portuguese 'caju', from the Tupi 'acajú' (nut).
