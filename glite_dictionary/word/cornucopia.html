cornucopia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrnjuˈkoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌkɔːnjuˈkəʊpiə / (BrE)
an abundant supply of good things; a state or source of great plenty
an abundance; great supply

Examples

The festival offered a cornucopia of foods from around the world.
There is a cornucopia of information available online for new learners.
Her backyard was a cornucopia of wildflowers in the spring.

Synset Examples

the profusion of detail
the idiomatic richness of English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply; shortage

Etymology

From Latin 'cornu copiae' meaning 'horn of plenty'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrn.jəˈkoʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.njuːˈkəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
A decorative container shaped like a goat's horn, overflowing with fruits, flowers, and grains, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
horn-shaped symbol of abundance

Examples

The Thanksgiving table was decorated with a cornucopia overflowing with fresh fruit and vegetables.
The cornucopia has long been used as a symbol of prosperity and abundance in art.
She placed a ceramic cornucopia on the mantelpiece as part of the autumn decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horn of plenty - another term for cornucopia, horn-shaped symbol of abundance

Etymology

From Latin 'cornu copiae' meaning 'horn of plenty'.
