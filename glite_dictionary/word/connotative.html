connotative

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑː.nə.teɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ə.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or expression) having the power to imply or suggest an additional meaning beyond its explicit definition.
implying additional meaning

Examples

The word 'home' is often used in a connotative sense to evoke warmth and comfort.
Poets frequently choose language for its connotative meanings as well as its literal ones.
Legal language strives to be precise and non-connotative to avoid misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denotative - indicating the explicit or primary meaning of a word, as opposed to implied meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'connotare' (to mark in addition), via Medieval Latin 'connotativus'.
