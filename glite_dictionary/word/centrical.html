centrical

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
having or situated at or near the center; central.
situated at or near the center

Examples

The city has a centrical location, making it ideal for transportation links.
This centrical point serves as the hub of the entire network.
They chose a centrical venue so all guests would have easy access.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central - located at or forming the center; of great importance

Etymology

From Latin centrum (center) + -ical (forming adjectives).
