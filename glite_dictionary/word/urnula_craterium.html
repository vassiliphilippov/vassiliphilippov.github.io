Urnula craterium

1. NOUN

/ ərˈnuːlə kreɪˈtɪəriəm / (AmE), / ɜːˈnjuːlə kreɪˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A species of fungus with an urn-shaped fruiting body and a nearly black interior.
urn-shaped fungus species

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with the unique Urnula craterium fungi after the spring rains.
Mycologists collected specimens of Urnula craterium for their morphological studies.
The nearly black interior of Urnula craterium makes it easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin 'urnula' (small urn) and 'craterium', referencing the shape of its fruiting body.
