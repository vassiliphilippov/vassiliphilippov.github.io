platitudinous

1. ADJ

/ ˌplætɪˈtuːdənəs / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈtjuːdɪnəs / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) dull, unoriginal, and full of trite statements while seeming to be meaningful or significant.
full of dull, trite statements

Examples

His speech was so platitudinous that the audience quickly lost interest.
The book offers only platitudinous advice instead of genuine insight.
She found his remarks platitudinous and uninspiring.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness
  • Synonym: clichéd - full of clichés; overused and unoriginal
  • Antonym: original - new and inventive; not imitative

Etymology

From 'platitude' (borrowed from French) plus the suffix '-inous'.
