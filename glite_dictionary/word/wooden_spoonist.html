wooden spoonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdən ˈspuːnɪst / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.ən ˈspuː.nɪst / (BrE)
(informal) A person who finishes last in a competition, especially as a humorous or light-hearted designation.
person finishing last in a contest (humorous)
informal british

Examples

He was dubbed the wooden spoonist after finishing last in the annual chess tournament.
Although they were the wooden spoonist this season, the team kept their spirits high.
The student proudly accepted the wooden spoonist award at the university's fun sports day.

Synset Examples

As a wooden spoonist, she excels at rustic recipes.
The title of wooden spoonist was humorously given to him.
He's considered a wooden spoonist in the cooking contest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - person who finishes first in a competition
  • Synonym: loser - person who loses or finishes behind others in a competition

Etymology

From the tradition of awarding a 'wooden spoon' to the last-place finisher in British academic or sporting contexts.
