thanatophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌθænətoʊˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌθænətəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
An intense and irrational fear of death or dying.
irrational fear of death
medical

Examples

She developed thanatophobia after witnessing a serious accident as a child.
People with thanatophobia may experience anxiety at the thought of mortality.
Therapy can help individuals manage their thanatophobia and improve quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necrophobia - fear of dead things or corpses

Etymology

From Greek 'thanatos' (death) + 'phobia' (fear).
