Diapsida

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæpsɪdə / (AmE), / daɪˈapsɪdə / (BrE)
a major group of reptiles characterized by having two temporal openings in the skull, formerly used to include all living reptiles except turtles; now superseded in classification by the subclasses Lepidosauria and Archosauria
major reptile group (zoology)

Examples

Many paleontologists classify dinosaurs as members of the Diapsida based on skull structure.
The group Diapsida is distinguished by two openings in each side of the skull.
Modern classification often splits former Diapsida into Lepidosauria and Archosauria.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Lepidosauria - a subclass including lizards, snakes, and tuataras
  • Degree Lower: Archosauria - a subclass including crocodiles, birds, and dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'apsis' (arch), referring to the two temporal openings in the skull.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈæpsədə / (AmE), / daɪˈapsɪdə / (BrE)
a former subclass of reptiles comprising all living reptiles except turtles, now generally divided into Lepidosauria and Archosauria
former reptile subclass (excludes turtles)

Examples

The Diapsida were once thought to include all living reptiles except turtles.
Modern classifications have replaced Diapsida with more specific groupings like Lepidosauria and Archosauria.
The skull structure of Diapsida is distinguished by two temporal openings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Lepidosauria - subclass including lizards, snakes, and tuataras
  • Degree Lower: Archosauria - subclass including crocodiles, alligators, and birds

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'apsis' (arch), referring to the two temporal openings in the skull.
