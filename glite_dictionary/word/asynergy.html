asynergy

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪsɪˈnɜrdʒi / (AmE), / ˌeɪsɪˈnɜːdʒi / (BrE)
absence of coordination between organs or body parts that normally function together harmoniously, especially used in medical contexts
lack of coordination between body parts
medical

Examples

The patient's heart exhibited asynergy following the myocardial infarction.
Neurologists noted asynergy in the patient's limb movements after the stroke.
Cardiac asynergy can result in impaired blood pumping efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyskinesia - impairment of voluntary movements
  • Antonym: synergy - cooperation of two or more organs or substances to produce a combined effect

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'synergos' (working together).
