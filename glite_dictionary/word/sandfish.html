sandfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsændˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
Either of two small silvery, scaleless fishes found in the northern Pacific that burrow into sand.
small burrowing fish of the northern Pacific

Examples

The sandfish quickly disappeared beneath the wet shoreline as the tide came in.
Fishermen in Alaska sometimes catch sandfish while digging for clams.
The silvery skin of the sandfish helps it blend in with its sandy habitat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsand.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish found in sandy areas of the western Pacific and Indian oceans, having an angular snout adapted for burrowing into sand
burrowing fish of sandy ocean areas

Examples

The sandfish burrows quickly into the sand to escape predators.
Marine biologists observed a sandfish off the coast of Indonesia.
The unique snout of the sandfish helps it dig efficiently through sandy ocean floors.
