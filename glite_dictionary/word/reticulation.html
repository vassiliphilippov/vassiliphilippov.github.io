reticulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛt̬ɪˈkjuːleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːleɪʃən / (BrE)
an arrangement or pattern in the form of a net or network
net-like arrangement or pattern

Examples

The intricate reticulation of veins in the leaf was clearly visible.
The reticulation in the photographic film resulted in a web-like pattern across the image.
The architect admired the reticulation of the ceiling's decorative beams.

Synset Examples

the reticulation of a leaf
the reticulation of a photographic emulsion

Etymology

From Latin reticulatio, from reticulum (little net).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Photography) The process or result of forming a network of cracks or wrinkles in the emulsion layer of photographic film, usually caused by improper temperature control during development.
network of cracks in photo emulsion

Examples

Improper temperature changes during film development can cause reticulation in your negatives.
The photographer was disappointed to discover reticulation in his prints after accidentally using hot water in the rinse.
Many vintage photographs show visible reticulation due to early chemical processing errors.

Etymology

From Latin reticulatus (netted), from reticulum (small net).
