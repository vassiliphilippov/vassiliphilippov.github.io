hundredweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌndrɪdˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrɪdˌweɪt / (BrE)
a unit of weight equal to 100 kilograms in the metric system
unit of weight, 100 kilograms

Examples

The shipment was listed at three hundredweight per package.
In many countries, farmers buy seeds by the hundredweight.
The regulations specify limits in hundredweight instead of kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centner - metric unit of mass equal to 100 kilograms

Etymology

From hundred + weight, referring to a quantity of one hundred units of weight.

Examples

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌndrədˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrədˌweɪt / (BrE)
A British unit of weight equal to 112 pounds (approximately 50.8 kilograms).
British unit of weight (112 pounds)
british

Examples

The sack of coal weighed one hundredweight and was difficult to lift.
Farmers often traded grain by the hundredweight in the UK.
He ordered five hundredweights of potatoes for the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintal - metric unit of weight equal to 100 kilograms

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'weight of one hundred,' based on hundred plus weight; the British hundredweight is 112 pounds due to historical trade practices.
