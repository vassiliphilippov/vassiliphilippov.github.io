achondritic

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪˌkɑnˈdrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌkɒnˈdrɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an achondrite (a type of stony meteorite that does not contain chondrules).
relating to achondrites (stony meteorites)
medical

Examples

The researchers analyzed the achondritic composition of the meteorite fragment.
Unlike chondritic meteorites, achondritic ones lack rounded chondrules in their structure.
Her thesis focused on the origin and classification of achondritic meteorites found in Antarctica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achondrite - a stony meteorite without chondrules

Etymology

From 'achondrite' (a meteorite without chondrules), with '-ic' forming an adjective.

2. ADJ

/ ˌeɪˌkɒnˈdrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌkɒnˈdrɪtɪk / (BrE)
Lacking a granular structure, especially referring to meteorites without chondrules.
lacking granular structure (esp. meteorites)

Examples

The meteorite was classified as achondritic due to its lack of chondrules.
Scientists studied the achondritic material to understand planetary formation.
Unlike chondritic meteorites, achondritic stones have a more uniform texture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chondritic - having a granular structure or containing chondrules

Etymology

From a- (not) + chondrite (type of stony meteorite) + -ic (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00367016-a
Synset POS: a
