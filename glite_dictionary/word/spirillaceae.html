Spirillaceae

1. NOUN

/ spɪˌrɪləˈsiːiː / (AmE), / spɪˌrɪləˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of rigid, spirally curved, elongated bacteria.
family of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

The Spirillaceae family includes several bacteria noted for their distinctive spiral shape.
Scientists studied Spirillaceae under the microscope to better understand their unique movement.
Most members of the Spirillaceae are found in aquatic environments rich in organic matter.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Spirillum' (a genus of spiral bacteria) + '-aceae' (a standard family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ spaɪˌrɪləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / spaɪˌrɪləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of rigid, spirally curved, elongated bacteria in the class Betaproteobacteria.
family of spiral-shaped bacteria

Examples

The Spirillaceae family includes bacteria with a distinctive spiral shape.
Scientists study Spirillaceae to understand their role in aquatic ecosystems.
Many Spirillaceae bacteria are found in stagnant water and can be observed under a microscope.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Spirillum' (a genus of spiral bacteria) + '-aceae' (a standard suffix for bacterial family names).
