to unsheathe

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈʃið / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃiːð / (BrE)
to draw or remove (a weapon, such as a sword or knife) from its sheath or scabbard
draw a weapon from its sheath

Examples

The knight unsheathed his sword as he prepared for battle.
She unsheathed the letter opener to open the envelope with dramatic flair.
He unsheathes his dagger only in times of true danger.

Synset Examples

the knight unsheathed his sword

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sheathe - to put a weapon or blade into its sheath

Etymology

From un- (not) + sheath, influenced by the idea of reversing the action of sheathing.
