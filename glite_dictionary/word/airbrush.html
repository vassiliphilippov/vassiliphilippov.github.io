airbrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈeəˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
A small device that sprays paint, ink, or dye using compressed air, used especially in art and design.
device for spraying paint with compressed air

Examples

The artist used an airbrush to create smooth gradients on the canvas.
For detailed model painting, an airbrush offers greater precision than a traditional brush.
Many makeup artists rely on an airbrush for flawless foundation application.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray gun - tool for spraying liquids, usually larger than an airbrush

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'brush' to describe a painting tool that uses air pressure.

2. VERB

/ ˈɛrˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈeəˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
to paint or spray a surface using an airbrush, a device that uses compressed air to apply paint evenly
to paint or spray with an airbrush

Examples

The artist decided to airbrush a mural on the gallery wall for a smooth finish.
After the repairs, the mechanic airbrushed the fender to match the car’s original color.
She spent hours airbrushing the portrait to remove any visible blemishes.

Synset Examples

The old car looks like new now that we airbrushed it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray-paint - to paint with spray paint (aerosol can)

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'brush'; airbrush tool patented in 1876.
