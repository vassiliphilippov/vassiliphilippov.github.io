hostler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑslɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒslə / (BrE)
a person employed to take care of horses in a stable, especially one who tends to horses at an inn or stable
person who tends horses in a stable

Examples

The hostler checked each horse’s saddle and feed before the riders arrived.
At the old inn, the hostler greeted travelers and readied their teams for the night.
She admired how quickly the hostler calmed the nervous stallion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stablehand - person who works in a stable taking care of horses
  • Synonym: groom - person employed to take care of horses

Etymology

From Middle English *hosteler*, from Anglo-French *hostiler* (innkeeper, stable keeper), from Late Latin *hostilarius* (monk responsible for guests), from Latin *hospes* (guest, host).
