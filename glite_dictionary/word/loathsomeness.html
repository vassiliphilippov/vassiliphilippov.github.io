loathsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊð.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊð.səm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant or disgusting to the senses or emotions
quality of being extremely unpleasant or disgusting

Examples

The loathsomeness of the abandoned building made it difficult for anyone to enter.
She recoiled at the loathsomeness of the smell coming from the garbage heap.
He spoke with such loathsomeness in his voice that everyone in the room was offended.

Synset Examples

the vileness of his language surprised us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vileness - the quality of being morally despicable or physically repulsive
  • Antonym: attractiveness - the quality of being appealing or pleasing

Etymology

From 'loathsome' + '-ness'; 'loathsome' from Middle English, from Old English lāðsum (hateful, odious).
