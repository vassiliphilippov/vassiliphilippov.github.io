unitarism

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.nə.tɑːˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.təˌrɪz.əm / (BrE)
The political principle or policy supporting the unification of a political entity by centralizing authority, often reducing the autonomy of constituent regions.
political centralization favoring unity

Examples

Many national leaders advocated unitarism to ensure stronger governmental control over the provinces.
The debate between federalism and unitarism has shaped the country's constitution.
Critics argue that unitarism can undermine local identities and governance.

Synset Examples

The simultaneous process of pluralisation, unitarisation and centralisation was one of the most significant results of pillarisation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federalism - Political system promoting autonomy of regions within a nation
  • Synonym: centralization - The concentration of control and authority in a central organization

Etymology

From Latin 'unitas' (unity) and English suffix '-ism' (doctrine or system).
