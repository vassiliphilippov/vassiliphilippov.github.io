backstroker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstrəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who swims the backstroke, especially as a competitor in swimming
person who swims the backstroke

Examples

She was a talented backstroker in her high school swim team.
The Olympic gold medalist is regarded as the best backstroker of his generation.
As a backstroker, he trains extensively to perfect his underwater phase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backstroke - a swimming stroke performed on the back

Etymology

From 'backstroke' + '-er', denoting a person who performs the action.
