hybridizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪbrəˌdaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪbrɪdʌɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of crossing different species or varieties of animals or plants to produce hybrids
the act of creating animal or plant hybrids
medical

Examples

The scientist spent years hybridizing various plant species to create a drought-resistant crop.
Hybridizing rare orchids often results in new and unique flower colors.
Advances in hybridizing have led to the development of disease-resistant livestock breeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbreeding - the process of producing offspring by mating different breeds, varieties, or species

Etymology

From "hybridize" + -ing; "hybrid" from Latin *hybrida* (offspring of a tame sow and wild boar).
