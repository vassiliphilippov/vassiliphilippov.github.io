Manteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæntiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmæntiˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as mantises, characterized by their elongated bodies and grasping forelegs.
family of mantises (insects)

Examples

The Manteidae include many well-known species of praying mantises found worldwide.
Biologists often study the mating behaviors unique to insects in the Manteidae family.
Fossils reveal that the Manteidae have existed for millions of years, adapting to various environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - an insect of the order Mantodea, known for its upright posture and grasping forelegs

Etymology

From Modern Latin Manteidae, based on the type genus Mantis, from Greek mantis (seer, prophet) due to their prayer-like stance.
