Israelite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪz.rəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪz.rə.laɪt / (BrE)
a member, native, or inhabitant of the ancient kingdom of Israel or a descendant of the Hebrew people of ancient Israel
member or native of ancient Israel

Examples

The ancient Israelite civilization played a key role in the history of the region.
Archaeological discoveries provide insight into Israelite daily life and culture.
According to tradition, the prophet Samuel was an Israelite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew - a member of the ancient people of Israel and Palestine

Etymology

From Late Latin Israelita, from Greek Israēlitēs, from Hebrew Yisrāʾēl (Israel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪzrəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪz.rə.laɪt / (BrE)
a member of the ancient Hebrew nation, especially in the period from the Exodus to the Babylonian Exile; also, a person descended from Jacob or associated with the Jewish people by heritage or conversion.
member of the ancient Hebrew nation

Examples

Many ancient Israelites are believed to have lived in Canaan during biblical times.
She studied the customs of the Israelites as part of her research into Middle Eastern history.
According to tradition, Jacob had twelve sons, and their descendants became known as the Israelites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jew - member of the people and cultural community historically descended from the Israelites

Etymology

From Latin 'Isrāēlīta', from Greek 'Israēlitēs', from Hebrew 'Yisraʾēl' (Israel).
