cutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut or carve stone, especially in building or sculpture
person who cuts or carves stone

Examples

The skilled cutter carefully shaped the marble for the new monument.
She apprenticed as a cutter at the local quarry, learning the craft from experienced artisans.
Each stone block bore the mark of the cutter who had shaped it by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonemason - a person who builds or works with stone
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtə / (BrE)
a small sailing vessel with a single mast set further back than the mast of a sloop, typically rigged with a fore-and-aft mainsail and two or more headsails
type of sailing boat with one mast

Examples

The cutter quickly maneuvered through the narrow channel with ease.
Our team restored a nineteenth-century cutter for the maritime museum.
A cutter is preferred for coastal cruising because of its sail configuration.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sloop - a single-masted sailing boat with the mast forward of the center

Etymology

From Middle English 'cutter', referring to something that cuts; in the nautical sense, from the vessel's ability to 'cut' through the water.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtə / (BrE)
a small, fast boat, especially one used for carrying messages or passengers between a ship and the shore
small boat for ship-to-shore transport

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to lower the cutter for a trip ashore.
Supplies were transferred from the ship to the port using the cutter.
A cutter was dispatched to deliver the message from the navy vessel.

Etymology

From the verb 'cut', referring to the boat's speed and ability to cut through water.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut materials, especially cloth, for making garments or other products
person who cuts materials for manufacturing

Examples

The cutter at the factory ensures each piece of fabric is trimmed to the correct shape.
As an experienced cutter, she can quickly turn raw fabric into perfectly sized garments.
The company is hiring a skilled cutter for its bespoke tailoring department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailor - a person who makes, alters, or repairs clothes

Etymology

From Middle English 'cutten', meaning to cut, plus agent suffix '-er'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈkʌtə / (BrE)
a tool or implement designed for cutting something
tool for cutting

Examples

You'll need a sharp cutter to open the packaging safely.
She bought a new glass cutter for her stained-glass project.
The pastry cutter makes it easy to shape the dough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - a tool with a sharp blade used for cutting
  • Synonym: scissors - a cutting tool with two blades that move past each other

Etymology

From Middle English 'cutter', agent noun from 'cut'.
