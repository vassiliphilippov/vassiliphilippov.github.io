Ulmus parvifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs pɑːrˈvaɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs pɑːˈvɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
A small, fast-growing tree native to East Asia, commonly known as the Chinese elm, often grown for shelterbelts and hedges.
species of elm tree from East Asia

Examples

The landscaping plan features several Ulmus parvifolia along the driveway to provide shade and beauty.
Gardeners often choose Ulmus parvifolia for its resistance to Dutch elm disease.
In the park, the mature Ulmus parvifolia created a green canopy over the walking paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese elm - common name for Ulmus parvifolia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ulmus' (elm) and 'parvifolia' (small-leaved).
