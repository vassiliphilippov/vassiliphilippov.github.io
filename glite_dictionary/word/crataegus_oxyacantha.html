Crataegus oxyacantha

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiɡəs ˌɑksiəˈkænθə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌɒksiəˈkanθə / (BrE)
a species of evergreen hawthorn native to southeastern Europe, known for its small white flowers and red berries
evergreen hawthorn species of southeastern Europe

Examples

The gardens featured several mature Crataegus oxyacantha trees in full bloom.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Crataegus oxyacantha extracts.
Botanists identified the shrub as Crataegus oxyacantha due to its distinctive leaves and red berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - any shrub or tree of the genus Crataegus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Crataegus' (hawthorn) from Greek 'krataigos', and 'oxyacantha', from Greek 'oxys' (sharp) + 'akantha' (thorn).
