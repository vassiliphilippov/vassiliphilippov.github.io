bugbane

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡˌbeɪn / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the genus Cimicifuga, having white or cream flowers in long spikes and reputed to repel insects
perennial plant of the Cimicifuga genus

Examples

The gardener planted bugbane along the shady border to help deter mosquitoes.
In midsummer, the delicate plumes of bugbane add a graceful touch to woodland gardens.
Herbalists sometimes recommend bugbane for its traditional use in repelling insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cimicifuga - genus of perennial plants known as bugbanes

Etymology

From 'bug' + 'bane', meaning 'killer of bugs' or 'something that repels bugs'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡˌbeɪn / (BrE)
A North American plant with large leaves and racemes of yellowish-green flowers, known for yielding a toxic alkaloid used medicinally.
North American medicinal plant

Examples

The herbalist recommended bugbane as a traditional remedy for certain ailments.
Bugbane thrives in shaded woodland areas and attracts many gardeners for its distinctive appearance.
Researchers are studying compounds extracted from bugbane for potential pharmaceutical use.

Etymology

From 'bug' + 'bane', meaning a plant that repels or is harmful to bugs.
