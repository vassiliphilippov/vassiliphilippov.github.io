Medicago

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkɑːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the pea family, commonly known as medicks, including important forage crops such as alfalfa
genus of clover-like flowering plants

Examples

Medicago species like alfalfa are widely cultivated as forage for livestock.
Several wild Medicago plants can be found growing in Mediterranean regions.
The agriculturist studied the nitrogen-fixing abilities of various Medicago species.

Etymology

From Latin 'medica', from Greek 'medike', referring to 'Medican grass', originally introduced from Media (region in Iran).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkɑgoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkɑːgəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of herbs in the pea family (Fabaceae), commonly known as medicks, that resemble clover and include plants such as alfalfa
a genus of clover-like herbs

Examples

Medicago species such as alfalfa are important forage crops worldwide.
The leaves of Medicago closely resemble those of clover.
Researchers are studying Medicago for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Etymology

From Latin *Medicago*, referring to alfalfa, ultimately from Greek *Medike* (Median, of Media), where the plant was believed to originate.
