surveyor

1. NOUN

/ sərˈveɪ.ər / (AmE), / səˈveɪ.ə / (BrE)
an engineer or professional who measures and determines the boundaries, features, and elevations of land or structures
person who measures land boundaries and features

Examples

The surveyor carefully mapped out the property boundaries before construction began.
A team of surveyors was hired to determine the elevation of the new road site.
The landowner consulted a surveyor to resolve a dispute with his neighbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineer - person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or structures

Etymology

From Old French *surveeur*, from *surveoir* (to oversee), from Latin *supervidere* (to oversee).

2. NOUN

/ sərˈveɪər / (AmE), / səˈveɪə / (BrE)
a person who conducts a statistical survey, typically gathering and analyzing data from a sample of people
person who conducts a statistical survey

Examples

The company hired a surveyor to collect responses for their annual customer satisfaction study.
Each surveyor visited fifty households to ask about voting intentions.
A team of surveyors analyzed the collected data for emerging trends in the population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interviewer - a person who asks questions during a survey or interview

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'surveior', from 'surveier' (to oversee) + '-or' (agent noun).
