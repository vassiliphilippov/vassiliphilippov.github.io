forgery

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒəri / (BrE)
a copy of an object, document, or work of art that is made or altered with the intent to deceive by representing it as the genuine original
fake copy made to deceive

Examples

The art dealer was arrested for selling a forgery instead of a genuine painting.
Experts examined the signature and quickly determined it was a forgery.
That document is an obvious forgery and cannot be trusted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfeit - an imitation made to deceive; especially of money or documents
  • Antonym: authentic - genuine; not a copy or imitation

Etymology

From Old French *forgerie* ('fabrication, false document'), from *forger* ('to forge').

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrdʒɚi / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒəri / (BrE)
The crime of making a false document, signature, or imitation of an object with the intent to deceive or defraud.
criminal falsification of documents or objects
legal

Examples

The art dealer was arrested for selling a forgery of a famous painting.
The bank detected a forgery on one of the checks submitted for payment.
Forgery
forgery is a serious offense that can result in imprisonment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfeiting - the crime of making an exact imitation of something, usually money, with the intent to deceive
  • Antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real

Etymology

From Old French *forgerie* (fabrication, invention), from *forger* (to forge).
