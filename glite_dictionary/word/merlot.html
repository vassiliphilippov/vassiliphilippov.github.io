Merlot

1. NOUN

/ mɚˈloʊ / (AmE), / mɛəˈləʊ / (BrE)
a dry red wine made from a grape variety originally from Bordeaux, now grown worldwide, especially in Bordeaux and California
type of dry red wine

Examples

She ordered a glass of Merlot to go with her steak.
The vineyard is known for producing award-winning Merlot each year.
Do you prefer Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon with your dinner?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cabernet Sauvignon - another major red wine grape and wine
  • Antonym: Chardonnay - popular white wine grape and wine

Etymology

French, from the name of the grape, itself a diminutive of 'merle' (blackbird).

2. NOUN

/ mɚˈloʊ / (AmE), / mɛəˈloʊ / (BrE)
A variety of black grape originally from Bordeaux, widely used to make red wine; also, a red wine made from this grape.
a type of black grape or its red wine

Examples

He ordered a glass of Merlot to go with his steak.
The Merlot grapes produced a wine with a rich, smooth flavor.
Many vineyards in California now grow Merlot as their primary varietal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cabernet Sauvignon - another major red wine grape variety
  • Antonym: Chardonnay - a major white wine grape variety

Etymology

French, from the name 'merle' (blackbird), said to be fond of the grapes or for the grape's dark color.
