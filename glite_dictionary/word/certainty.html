certainty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrtnti / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtnti / (BrE)
something that is certain or sure to happen; a thing that is certain.
something certain to happen

Examples

The team's promotion is now almost a certainty after their latest win.
There can be no certainty about the outcome of the negotiations.
Her skill and dedication made her success a virtual certainty.

Synset Examples

his victory is a certainty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the state of being completely confident or having no doubt
  • Antonym: uncertainty - something that is not known or sure; doubt

Etymology

From Old French 'certeinte' (certainty), from Latin 'certus' (sure, certain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.tn̩.ti / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪn.ti / (BrE)
the state of being completely confident or sure about something, without any doubt
the state of being sure or certain

Examples

After hours of research, she spoke with an air of certainty.
There is no certainty that the plan will succeed, but it has a good chance.
His certainty about the outcome comforted the entire team.

Synset Examples

his certainty reassured the others

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not certain
  • Synonym: confidence - a feeling or belief that one can rely on something; firm trust

Etymology

From Old French *certeinte*, from Latin *certus* (certain).
