botulinum toxin A

1. NOUN

/ ˌboʊtʃəˈlaɪnəm ˈtɑksɪn eɪ / (AmE), / ˌbɒtjʊˈlɪnəm ˈtɒksɪn eɪ / (BrE)
a neurotoxin used in medicine and cosmetic surgery to treat muscle disorders and reduce wrinkles; commonly known as Botox
medical neurotoxin used as Botox
medical

Examples

The doctor injected botulinum toxin A into the patient's forehead to reduce frown lines.
Treatment with botulinum toxin A can help relieve chronic muscle spasms in some neurological disorders.
Cosmetic procedures using botulinum toxin A have become increasingly common in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Botox - brand name of botulinum toxin A used in cosmetic and medical treatments

Etymology

From 'botulinum', the bacterium producing the toxin (Clostridium botulinum), and 'toxin'.
