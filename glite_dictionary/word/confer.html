to confer

1. VERB

/ kənˈfɝː / (AmE), / kənˈfɜː / (BrE)
to discuss something with others or consult, especially in order to make a decision
discuss or consult with others

Examples

The committee will confer again before announcing its decision.
Before making changes, I prefer to confer with my colleagues.
The doctors conferred privately to discuss the best course of treatment.

Synset Examples

We conferred about a plan of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consult - to seek advice or discuss with someone before making a decision
  • Synonym: deliberate - to discuss or consider carefully

Etymology

From Latin conferre, meaning 'to bring together'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00878196-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈfɝː / (AmE), / kənˈfəː / (BrE)
to officially give or grant (a degree, honor, right, or title) to someone
to grant (a degree, honor, etc.) officially

Examples

The committee decided to confer the award upon Dr. Lee for her outstanding research.
Each year, the college confers honorary degrees to distinguished guests.
After much deliberation, the board conferred full membership on the new partners.

Synset Examples

The university conferred a degree on its most famous former student, who never graduated
bestow an honor on someone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bestow - to give (an honor or right) formally
  • Synonym: award - to give (something such as a prize or a degree) officially to someone
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired

Etymology

From Latin conferre (to bring together, to grant), from com- (together) + ferre (to bring).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02268154-v
Synset POS: v
