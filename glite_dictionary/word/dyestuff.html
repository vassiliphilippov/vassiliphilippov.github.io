dyestuff

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌstʌf / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.stʌf / (BrE)
a soluble substance used for coloring or staining materials, such as fabrics or hair
soluble substance for coloring materials

Examples

Many industries rely on synthetic dyestuffs to achieve vibrant colors in their products.
The development of new dyestuff technologies has made eco-friendly textiles possible.
Before the invention of chemical dyestuffs, natural dyes extracted from plants and insects were commonly used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - a substance used to add color to materials

Etymology

Formed from "dye" + "stuff" (substance), mid-19th century.
