palatinate

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlæt.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / pəˈlæt.ɪ.nət / (BrE)
A territory under the jurisdiction of a count palatine; a region with special privileges ruled by a noble granted royal authority.
territory ruled by a count palatine

Examples

The Holy Roman Empire contained several prominent palatinates during the Middle Ages.
The palatinate enjoyed certain privileges and autonomy not granted to other regions.
Many families in the palatinate owed allegiance directly to the count palatine.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin palatinatus (the office or territory of a palatine), from palatinus (palatine official).
