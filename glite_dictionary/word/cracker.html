cracker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrækər / (AmE), / ˈkrækə / (BrE)
A firework consisting of a small explosive charge and fuse in a heavy paper casing, producing a loud noise when set off.
small explosive firework

Examples

The children were excited to light a cracker during the holiday festival.
Lighting a cracker in the street is illegal in some cities due to safety concerns.
She bought a box of colorful crackers for the New Year's celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firecracker - a type of firework with a small explosive charge

/ ˈkrækɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrækə / (BrE)
a person who gains unauthorized access to computer systems, often with malicious intent

Some experts distinguish between a hacker and a cracker based on their intentions.
After the cracker defaced the website, administrators quickly restored the original content.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkrækər / (AmE), / ˈkrækə / (BrE)
(offensive, chiefly US) A disparaging term for a poor White person, especially from the southern United States.
offensive US term for poor White Southerner
offensive american

Examples

He grew up in rural Georgia where the term cracker was often used for poor White farmers.
She found the label cracker both offensive and outdated when referring to her family.
The word cracker has a complicated and controversial history in the American South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hillbilly - a disparaging term for a poor White person from a rural area, especially the Appalachians or Ozarks
  • Synonym: redneck - a derogatory term for a White working-class person, especially from the southern United States

Etymology

Originally from the sense of 'one who boasts' or 'loud talker' from Middle English crack, later used in reference to poor rural Whites in the American South.
