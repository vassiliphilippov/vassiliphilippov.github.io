meliorism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːliərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmiːlɪərɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that the world can be improved by human effort.
belief that the world can be improved

Examples

Her philosophy of meliorism inspired her lifelong commitment to social reform.
Advocates of meliorism believe that progress is always possible.
The novel's underlying theme is a subtle endorsement of meliorism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimism - the belief that the world tends towards the worst possible outcome
  • Synonym: optimism - belief that good ultimately predominates over evil in the world

Etymology

From Latin 'melior' (better), plus '-ism'.
