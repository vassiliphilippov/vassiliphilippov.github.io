cheesecloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃizˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːzˌklɒθ / (BrE)
A coarse, loosely woven cotton fabric, often used in cooking, cleaning, and originally for wrapping cheeses.
coarse cotton cloth for cooking or wrapping

Examples

She strained the soup through a cheesecloth to remove all the solids.
The recipe suggests wrapping the herbs in cheesecloth before adding them to the pot.
He bought a new roll of cheesecloth for making homemade cheese.

Etymology

From 'cheese' + 'cloth', referring to its original use in cheese-making.
