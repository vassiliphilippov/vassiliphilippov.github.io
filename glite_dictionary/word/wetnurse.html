wetnurse

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛtˌnɝs / (AmE), / ˈwɛtˌnɜːs / (BrE)
A woman who is hired to breastfeed and care for another person's child.
woman hired to breastfeed another's child

Examples

In royal courts, a wetnurse was often employed for the newborn princes.
The family hired a wetnurse to care for the infant after the mother fell ill.
Historical records describe the life of a wetnurse in 18th-century England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nursemaid - a woman employed to care for children

Etymology

From 'wet' + 'nurse', indicating nourishment provided by breastfeeding.
