frustrating

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing annoyance or discouragement by making progress difficult or hindering success.
causing annoyance or discouragement

Examples

Dealing with slow internet can be frustrating when you're trying to get work done.
It was frustrating to watch the team come so close to winning and then lose at the last minute.
She finds it frustrating that her efforts are not recognized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoying - causing irritation or mild anger
  • Antonym: satisfying - giving pleasure or fulfillment

Etymology

From the verb 'frustrate,' which comes from Latin frustrare (to deceive, disappoint).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing annoyance or disappointment because something prevents the realization or attainment of a desired outcome
causing annoyance by blocking desires

Examples

It is frustrating when plans are suddenly changed without warning.
The repeated errors were frustrating for the entire team.
Waiting in a long queue can be incredibly frustrating at the end of a busy day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoying - causing irritation or bother
  • Antonym: satisfying - giving fulfillment or pleasure

Etymology

From Latin frustrare (to deceive, disappoint) via frustrate + -ing.
