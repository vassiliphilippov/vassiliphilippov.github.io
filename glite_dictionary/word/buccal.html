buccal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.əl / (BrE)
relating to the mouth or the region of the mouth
of the mouth or mouth region
medical

Examples

The dentist pointed out a small cavity on the buccal surface of her molar.
Swab the inside of your cheek to collect a buccal cell sample for DNA testing.
The medication is administered through the buccal route, meaning it is absorbed in the mouth.

Synset Examples

the oral cavity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oral - relating to the mouth

Etymology

From Latin bucca (cheek).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.əl / (BrE)
relating to or located in the cheek, or toward the cheek, especially in anatomy and dentistry.
relating to the cheek
medical

Examples

A dental hygienist may examine the buccal surfaces of your teeth for signs of plaque.
Topical medications can sometimes be administered via the buccal cavity.
A buccal swab is commonly used to collect DNA samples from the inside of the cheek.

Synset Examples

the buccal aspect of the gum

Etymology

From Latin 'bucca' meaning 'cheek'.
