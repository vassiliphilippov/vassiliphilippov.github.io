officious

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɪʃəs / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
too eager to offer advice or help, especially when it is unwelcome or meddlesome
meddling and intrusive

Examples

The officious security guard kept stopping people to check their bags for no reason.
She resented her officious colleague for constantly offering unwanted advice at work.
Despite his good intentions, the officious neighbor annoyed everyone by always prying into their business.

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meddlesome - fond of interfering in other people's affairs
  • Synonym: interfering - tending to involve oneself in other people's affairs without invitation

Etymology

From Latin officiosus (obliging, dutiful), from officium (service, duty).
