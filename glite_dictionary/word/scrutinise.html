to scrutinise

1. VERB

/ ˈskruːtəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskruːtənaɪz / (BrE)
to examine or inspect something closely and thoroughly
examine or inspect closely
british

Examples

The detective scrutinised every detail of the crime scene for clues.
Investors carefully scrutinise financial statements before making decisions.
Her supervisor scrutinised her work to ensure everything was in order.

Synset Examples

he scrutinized his likeness in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrutinize - the US spelling of 'scrutinise'
  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something closely in order to learn more about it
  • Antonym: to ignore - to intentionally not pay attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'scrutinium' (a searching), from 'scrutari' (to search).

2. VERB

/ ˈskruːtəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskruːtənaɪz / (BrE)
to examine something very carefully in order to discover information or ensure accuracy
examine closely and carefully
british

Examples

The manager scrutinised each application before making her decision.
The committee will scrutinise the proposal for any potential issues.
He scrutinised the document to ensure there were no mistakes.

Synset Examples

audit accounts and tax returns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrutinize - alternate spelling (US English)
  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something closely
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or examine

Etymology

From Latin 'scrutinium' (a searching) via French 'scrutiniser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00698828-v
Synset POS: v
