squealing

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwilɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
producing or characterized by a high-pitched, piercing sound, like that of a mouse or a rusty hinge
making a high-pitched sound

Examples

The squealing brakes of the bus made everyone wince.
We could hear the squealing children playing in the yard.
The squealing hinges on the old door needed some oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeaky - making a short, high-pitched sound
  • Antonym: silent - not making any sound

Etymology

Present participle of 'squeal', from Middle English 'squele', related to Swedish 'skvala' (to squall).
