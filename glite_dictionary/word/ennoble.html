to ennoble

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
to give someone a noble title or rank; to make someone a member of the nobility
make someone a noble or give a title

Examples

The king decided to ennoble his most loyal advisor for outstanding service.
Several artists were ennobled after making significant contributions to the empire.
The queen plans to ennoble distinguished citizens during the annual ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elevate - to raise to a higher rank or position
  • Antonym: to degrade - to lower in dignity or rank

Etymology

From Old French ennoblir, from Latin nobilis (noble).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
to confer dignity, honor, or a noble rank upon someone; to make someone or something noble in quality or character
give dignity or noble rank

Examples

The king decided to ennoble the general for his bravery in battle.
Acts of kindness can ennoble even the humblest person.
The ceremony will ennoble her with the title of Dame.

Synset Examples

He was dignified with a title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dignify - to confer honor or dignity upon someone
  • Antonym: to debase - to reduce in quality or value; to degrade

Etymology

From Old French 'ennoblir', from Latin 'nobilis' (noble).

Synset POS: v
