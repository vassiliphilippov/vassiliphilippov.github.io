Iris tingitana

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs tɪn.dʒɪˈtɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪs tɪŋ.gɪˈtɑː.nə / (BrE)
a species of bulbous Spanish iris distinguished by its blue flowers, native to North Africa and cultivated as an ornamental plant
a species of blue-flowered Spanish iris

Examples

The Iris tingitana blooms in early spring with striking blue petals.
Gardeners often choose Iris tingitana for its vivid color and elegance.
You can find Iris tingitana growing naturally in parts of Morocco and Algeria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin; genus 'Iris' + specific epithet 'tingitana' referring to Tangier, Morocco.
