underdevelopment

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˌdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
in photography, inadequate processing of film resulting in images with insufficient contrast
insufficient film processing; low contrast

Examples

The negative suffered from underdevelopment, making the image appear washed out and lacking detail.
Improper timing during processing often leads to underdevelopment in photographic film.
Photographers must avoid underdevelopment to ensure proper contrast in their prints.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overdevelopment - excessive processing of film resulting in too much contrast

Etymology

From 'under-' meaning 'insufficiently' + 'development' (from develop, originally from French 'développer').

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndədɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
The condition of not having developed sufficiently, especially in economic, social, or physical aspects.
insufficient or incomplete development

Examples

The persistent underdevelopment of rural regions hampers national economic progress.
Health issues can result from the underdevelopment of vital organs during childhood.
Many experts focus on the causes of underdevelopment in sub-Saharan Africa.

Synset Examples

much poverty can be traced to the underdevelopment of industry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: development - process of growing or becoming more advanced

Etymology

From under- (not enough) + development.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
