Isaac

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪzək / (AmE), / ˈaɪzək / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the second patriarch, son of Abraham and Sarah, offered by Abraham as a sacrifice and father of Jacob and Esau.
son of Abraham and Sarah in the Bible

Examples

Isaac is a major figure in the Book of Genesis, known for his faith and obedience.
The story of Abraham's sacrifice of Isaac is central to several religious traditions.
Many people name their children Isaac in honor of the biblical patriarch.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yitzḥāq, meaning 'he will laugh' or 'he laughs'.
