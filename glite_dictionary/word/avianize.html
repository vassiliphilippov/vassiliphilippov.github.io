to avianize

1. VERB

/ ˈeɪviəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈeɪvɪənaɪz / (BrE)
to modify microorganisms through repeated cultivation in the developing chick embryo, typically to adapt them for study or vaccine production.
modify microorganisms in chick embryo
medical

Examples

Scientists often avianize viruses before preparing vaccines for poultry.
The influenza strain was avianized to facilitate further research in the lab.
Researchers are currently avianizing the pathogen by culturing it in chick embryos.

Etymology

From 'avian' (relating to birds) + suffix '-ize' (to make or become).
