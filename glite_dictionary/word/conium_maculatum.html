Conium maculatum

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈnaɪəm ˌmækjʊˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / kəʊˈnʌɪ.əm ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A highly poisonous Eurasian and African biennial herb, commonly known as hemlock, with fernlike leaves and clusters of small white flowers, often growing in damp areas.
poisonous plant; hemlock

Examples

Children should be taught to recognize Conium maculatum because all parts of the plant are dangerously toxic.
The death of Socrates was caused by a concoction containing Conium maculatum.
Botanists warn that Conium maculatum can easily be mistaken for harmless wild parsley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - common name for Conium maculatum, a poisonous plant

Etymology

From Latin 'conium' (hemlock) and 'maculatum' (spotted), referring to its markings.
