nominal

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a thing) named or designated for a particular person; bearing the name of a specific person.
named for a specific person

Examples

The company issued nominal shares to its founders.
Certain accounts must be held in a nominal form to identify the holder.
A nominal roll contains the names of all eligible participants.

Synset Examples

nominative shares of stock

Etymology

From Latin nominalis (pertaining to a name), from nomen (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01103503-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɑː.mə.nəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to a noun or to a word group that functions as a noun, especially in grammar or linguistics.
relating to nouns or noun phrases (grammar)

Examples

In the sentence 'Swimming is fun,' 'swimming' is a nominal word.
A nominal group may contain determiners and adjectives as well as the noun itself.
Linguists often analyze the role of nominal phrases in sentence structure.

Synset Examples

nominal phrase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noun - a word used to identify any of a class of people, places, or things

Etymology

From Latin 'nomen' (name), via Late Latin 'nominalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02779730-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɑː.mə.nəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of names; existing or listed in name only, rather than in fact or reality.
relating to names or naming, in name only

Examples

The candidates appeared on the nominal list but had no real influence in the decision-making process.
All taxable individuals were included in the government's nominal rolls.
Despite being the nominal leader, she held little actual power in the organization.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Latin nominalis (pertaining to names), from nomen (name).

(of a sum or amount) very small; far below the real value or cost; existing in name only or as a mere formality.

Examples

5. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a title, role, or status) existing in name only; not having the full power or authority normally associated with it.
existing in name only

Examples

Although she was the nominal manager, her assistant made most of the decisions.
He holds the nominal position of chairman, but has little influence.
The king remained the nominal head of state after the country became a republic.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titular - holding a title without real power or authority
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or having actual authority

Etymology

From Latin nominalis (pertaining to a name), from nomen (name).

Source: WordNet

6. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a value or amount) stated without adjustment for changes in price or inflation; not accounting for the effect of inflation.
unadjusted for inflation (economics)

Examples

The country's nominal GDP grew by 5% last year, but real growth was lower due to inflation.
Banks often advertise nominal interest rates, which do not reflect the effects of inflation.
When comparing salaries over time, it's important to distinguish between nominal and real values.

Synset Examples

the nominal GDP
nominal interest rates

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real - adjusted for inflation; expressing value in constant prices

Etymology

From Latin nominam (name) via Late Latin nominalis, meaning 'pertaining to a name'; in economics, the sense refers to value 'in name only.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01946401-a
Synset POS: a

7. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A word or phrase that functions as a noun within a sentence, often serving as the subject or object of a verb.
word or phrase acting as a noun

Examples

In the sentence 'The old man left,' the nominal is 'the old man.'
Some nominals can be very complex, including long descriptive phrases.
Linguists often analyze the structure of nominals when studying syntax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noun - a word that is the name of a person, place, thing, or idea

Etymology

From Latin nominalis (relating to a name or noun), from nomen (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06327473-n
Synset POS: n
