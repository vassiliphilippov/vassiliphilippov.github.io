Blattella

1. NOUN

/ bləˈtɛlə / (AmE), / bləˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small cockroaches found worldwide, including some household species.
genus of small cockroaches

Examples

The German cockroach, a member of the genus Blattella, is commonly found in kitchens.
Scientists studied several Blattella species to better understand pest control methods.
Blattella populations can increase rapidly in warm, humid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - an insect of the order Blattodea, known for its hardiness and flat, oval body

Etymology

From Latin 'blatta' (a cockroach) + diminutive '-ella'.

2. NOUN

/ bləˈtɛlə / (AmE), / bləˈtiːlə / (BrE)
A genus of small cockroaches, including several common household pest species.
genus of small cockroaches

Examples

The most well-known species in the genus Blattella is the German cockroach.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of several Blattella species in urban environments.
Pest control experts often encounter infestations of Blattella in residential buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - an insect of the order Blattodea, commonly found as household pests

Etymology

From New Latin, diminutive form of Latin 'blatta' (cockroach).
