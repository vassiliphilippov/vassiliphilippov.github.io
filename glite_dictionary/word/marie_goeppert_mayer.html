Marie Goeppert Mayer

1. PROPN

/ məˈri ˈɡoʊpərt ˈmaɪ.ər / (AmE), / məˈriː ˈɡɛpət ˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
(1906–1972) United States physicist, born in Germany, known for her pioneering research on the structure of the atom and Nobel laureate.
German-born American physicist, Nobel laureate

Examples

The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Marie Goeppert Mayer in 1963 for her work on nuclear shell structure.
Many young women in science look up to Marie Goeppert Mayer as a pioneering role model.
Born in Germany, Marie Goeppert Mayer made significant contributions to theoretical physics in the United States.
