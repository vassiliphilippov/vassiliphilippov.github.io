actuating

1. ADJ

/ ˈæktʃuˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaktjʊˌeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing motion, action, or change; having the ability to trigger or set something in motion.
causing motion, action, or change

Examples

The actuating mechanism inside the device caused the gears to turn.
Many modern vehicles have an actuating system that responds to driver input.
The engineer tested the actuating force required to move the valve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activating - making active or operative
  • Synonym: triggering - initiating a process or response
  • Antonym: inhibiting - hindering or preventing an action

Etymology

From Latin 'actuatus', past participle of 'actuare' (to set in motion), from 'actus' (act).
