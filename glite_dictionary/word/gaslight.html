gaslight

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːslaɪt / (BrE)
Light produced by the burning of illuminating gas, especially as used for lighting streets and buildings before the widespread adoption of electricity.
light from burning illuminating gas

Examples

In Victorian London, the streets were lit by gaslight after dusk.
The flickering gaslight cast eerie shadows on the walls of the old theatre.
Before electricity, many homes relied on gaslight for evening illumination.

Etymology

From 'gas' and 'light', originally referring to the method of lighting using gas first developed in the early 19th century.
