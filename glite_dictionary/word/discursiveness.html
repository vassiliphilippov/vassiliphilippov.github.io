discursiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskɝː.sɪv.nəs / (AmE), / dɪˈskɜː.sɪv.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of talking or writing in a rambling or disgressive manner, or of proceeding by reasoned argument.
rambling or reasoned style; discursiveness

Examples

The discursiveness of his novel makes it both challenging and rewarding to read.
Her essays are marked by a certain discursiveness that allows for digressions into different subjects.
While some critics dislike the discursiveness of the argument, others find it thoroughly engaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discursive - moving from topic to topic; rambling or reasoned

Etymology

From discursive + -ness, formed from Latin discursus (running about) via French.
