NAD

1. NOUN

/ ɛn eɪ di / (AmE), / ɛn eɪ di / (BrE)
a coenzyme found in living cells, derived from nicotinic acid, and involved in redox reactions during metabolism
coenzyme involved in metabolism
medical

Examples

The presence of NAD is essential for cellular respiration.
Scientists studied how NAD levels affect metabolic processes in mammals.
A deficiency in NAD can lead to various health problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: FAD - a coenzyme that acts as a hydrogen acceptor in dehydrogenation reactions

Etymology

Abbreviation of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide.
