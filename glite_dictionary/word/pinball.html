pinball

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a game typically played on a sloping board where players propel a ball to hit pins or targets, aiming to score points
arcade game with balls and targets

Examples

The old arcade still has a pinball machine from the 1970s.
He spent hours playing pinball with his friends after school.
Winning at pinball requires quick reflexes and good timing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcade game - a coin-operated entertainment machine

Etymology

Formed from 'pin' and 'ball', describing the game's main components.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a game, usually played on a sloping board, in which the player uses a plunger to propel a ball and scores points by hitting various targets, pins, or pockets with the ball as it bounces around
a game with a ball bouncing among pins on a board

Examples

He spent hours playing pinball at the local arcade after school.
The loud noises from the pinball machine attracted a curious crowd.
Many collectors enjoy restoring vintage pinball games to their original condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcade game - a game often found in amusement arcades, including video and mechanical games

Etymology

From 'pin' + 'ball', referring to the pins and rolling ball in the original versions of the game.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
