cerement

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəmənt / (BrE)
A waxed cloth or shroud used to wrap a dead body for burial; a burial garment.
shroud or burial garment
archaic

Examples

The ancient tomb held the remains tightly wrapped in cerement for centuries.
Legends say the haunting figure still drapes itself in its old cerements.
Removing the cerement revealed the perfectly preserved corpse beneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shroud - cloth used to wrap a corpse for burial

Etymology

From Old French cirement from Medieval Latin ceramentum (‘waxed cloth’ from Latin cera ‘wax’).
