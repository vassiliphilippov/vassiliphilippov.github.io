Tebet

1. NOUN

/ teɪˈbɛt / (AmE), / teˈbet / (BrE)
The fourth month of the civil year and the tenth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in December and January.
Jewish calendar month (December–January)

Examples

Hanukkah sometimes extends into Tebet on the Jewish calendar.
The fast of the Tenth of Tebet is observed as a day of mourning.
According to tradition, Queen Esther was taken to the king's palace in the month of Tebet.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Tevet*, name of a month in the Jewish calendar.
