Aceraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈreɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈreɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of trees and shrubs in the order Sapindales, including the maples
family of trees and shrubs (maples)

Examples

The Aceraceae family is well known for its beautiful maple trees.
Many botanists now include the Aceraceae within the Sapindaceae family.
The leaves of trees in the Aceraceae are typically palmate and lobed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a tree or shrub of the genus Acer, known for lobed leaves and winged fruit

Etymology

From Latin *Acer* (maple) + -aceae, a standard botanical suffix for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈreɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a botanical family of trees and shrubs, chiefly including the maples, belonging to the order Sapindales
maple family of trees and shrubs

Examples

The Aceraceae are well known for their distinctive leaf shapes, especially among maples.
Many species in the Aceraceae family are valued for their ornamental foliage in gardens.
Canada's national symbol, the maple, belongs to the Aceraceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a tree or shrub with lobed leaves and winged fruits, genus Acer, member of Aceraceae

Etymology

From Latin 'Acer', the genus name for maple, plus the botanical suffix '-aceae'.
