dreck

1. NOUN

/ drɛk / (AmE), / drɛk / (BrE)
shoddy, inferior, or worthless merchandise or material
inferior or worthless goods

Examples

Most of the souvenirs in that shop are just dreck and not worth buying.
He regretted spending so much money on what turned out to be dreck.
The critic dismissed the movie as commercial dreck aimed at undiscerning audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk - goods of little value; trash
  • Synonym: trash - things that are of low quality or worthless
  • Antonym: treasure - something of great value or worth

Etymology

From Yiddish *drek* (dirt, trash), from Middle High German *drec* (dirt, excrement).
