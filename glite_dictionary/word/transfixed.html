transfixed

1. ADJ

/ trænzˈfɪkst / (AmE), / trænsˈfɪkst / (BrE)
so fascinated, shocked, or absorbed that you are unable to move or look away; held motionless with awe, fear, or amazement
unable to move; held by fascination or shock

Examples

She stood transfixed by the beauty of the sunrise, unable to look away.
The children were transfixed as the magician pulled a rabbit from the hat.
He watched the unfolding scene, transfixed with horror and disbelief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spellbound - captivated as if by magic
  • Synonym: mesmerized - having your attention fully absorbed

Etymology

Past participle of 'transfix', from Latin 'transfixus' (pierced through).
