derp

1. ADJ

/ dɝːp / (AmE), / dəːp / (BrE)
appearing or behaving in a silly or stupid way, especially in an endearingly cute or amusing manner
silly or stupid, often cutely
informal slang

Examples

He made a derp face when he tried to take the selfie.
The puppy looked kind of derp with its tongue hanging out.
Sometimes my brother acts totally derp just to make us laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dopey - foolish or silly in a gentle way

Etymology

Originally an internet slang term popularized in the late 1990s, possibly imitative in origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90003181-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ dɝːp / (AmE), / dɜːp / (BrE)
A person who is silly or acts in a foolish or stupid manner, often in an amusing or cute way.
silly or foolish person (slang)
slang

Examples

Whenever he forgets his keys for the third time, we just call him a derp.
She made a silly face in the photo and looked like a total derp.
Don't be such a derp—read the instructions before you start!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid or foolish person
  • Antonym: genius - a person with outstanding intelligence or skill

Etymology

Originated as an exclamation in internet culture and memes, first popularized in the 1990s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90003182-n
Synset POS: n

3. INTJ

/ dɜrp / (AmE), / dəːp / (BrE)
An exclamation used to comment on a foolish, silly, or endearingly clumsy action or mistake, often in a lighthearted way.
exclamation for foolish or cute mistakes
slang informal

Examples

When he tripped over his shoelaces, he laughed and said, derp!
She sent the wrong email to her boss and texted her friend, 'derp, I messed up.'
Every time my cat falls off the couch, I just say, 'derp.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duh - exclamation expressing obviousness or foolishness

Etymology

Originated as internet slang, popularized by animated TV shows in the late 1990s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90003183-r
Synset POS: r
