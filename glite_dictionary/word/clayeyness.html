/ ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of resembling clay, especially in thickness, consistency, or texture.

The wine's flavor was influenced by the clayeyness of the vineyard's terrain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being composed of or containing clay, especially in relation to soil.
quality of being clayey

Examples

The clayeyness of the soil makes it difficult for water to drain properly after heavy rain.
Gardeners must consider the clayeyness of their land before planting certain types of flowers.
Excessive clayeyness can lead to soil compaction and poor root growth in crops.

Synset Examples

During periods of excess moisture, grazing needs to be restricted in many places because of the clayiness of the surface soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - fine-grained natural soil material
  • Antonym: sandiness - quality of containing sand

Etymology

From 'clayey' (containing or resembling clay) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a quality or state).
