to accuse

1. VERB

/ əˈkjuz / (AmE), / əˈkjuːz / (BrE)
to say that someone has done something wrong or illegal; to charge someone with a fault or crime
to charge someone with wrongdoing

Examples

She was quick to accuse her colleague of stealing credit for the idea.
The police accused him of breaking into the store last night.
Before you accuse someone, make sure you have all the facts.

Synset Examples

he charged the director with indifference

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charge - to formally accuse someone of a crime
  • Antonym: to exonerate - to officially declare someone not guilty

Etymology

From Latin accusare (to call to account, accuse), from ad- (to) + causa (reason, case, lawsuit).

2. VERB

/ əˈkjuz / (AmE), / əˈkjuːz / (BrE)
to say that someone has done something wrong or illegal, often officially
to charge someone with wrongdoing

Examples

She decided to accuse her coworker of stealing her lunch.
Before you accuse someone, make sure you have evidence.
The police accused him of breaking into the house last night.

Synset Examples

The neighbors accused the man of spousal abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - to hold responsible for a fault or wrong
  • Antonym: to defend - to support someone against an accusation

Etymology

From Latin accusare (to call to account, indict), from ad- (toward) + causa (reason, lawsuit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00844668-v
Synset POS: v
