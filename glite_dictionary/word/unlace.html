to unlace

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈleɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈleɪs / (BrE)
to undo or loosen the laces or fastenings of something
undo the laces or fastenings

Examples

She quickly unlaced her boots as soon as she got home.
He unlaced the corset carefully to avoid damaging the fabric.
As he sat by the fire, he began unlacing his shoes to relax.

Synset Examples

They untied the prisoner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lace - to fasten or tighten with a cord or string
  • Synonym: to untie - to undo or release something that is tied

Etymology

From un- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse action') + lace, Middle English from Old French 'laz', from Latin 'laqueus' (noose, snare).
