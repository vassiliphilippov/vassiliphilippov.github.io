to filiate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪliːeɪt / (BrE)
to establish or formally recognize the paternity of (a child), especially by legal means
establish the paternity of a child
legal

Examples

The court decided to filiate the child after a lengthy hearing.
If no man comes forward to filiate the child, the state will intervene.
Lawyers filed a petition to filiate the minor based on new DNA evidence.

Synset Examples

The court filiated the child born out of wedlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legitimate - to make (a child) legitimate, especially by subsequent marriage of the parents
  • Synonym: to adopt - to take legally as one's own child

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'filiatus', past participle of 'filiare' (to make someone a son or daughter), from Latin 'filius' (son).
