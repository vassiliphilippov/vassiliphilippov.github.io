melodious

1. ADJ

/ məˈloʊdiəs / (AmE), / mɪˈləʊdiəs / (BrE)
pleasant to listen to because of a sweet, musical sound; having a pleasing melody.
having a pleasant, musical sound

Examples

The melodious sound of her singing filled the entire room with warmth.
A melodious breeze rustled gently through the trees on that quiet evening.
He played a melodious tune on the piano that captivated the audience.

Synset Examples

the melodious song of a meadowlark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuneful - having a pleasant or melodious sound
  • Antonym: discordant - harsh or jarring in sound; lacking harmony

Etymology

From Late Latin melodiosus, from Greek melōidía (singing, chanting).

2. ADJ

/ məˈloʊdiəs / (AmE), / mɪˈləʊdiəs / (BrE)
having a pleasant and musical sound; tuneful.
pleasant and musical in sound

Examples

The melodious sound of the violin filled the concert hall.
She woke to the melodious singing of birds outside her window.
His voice was so melodious that everyone stopped to listen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuneful - having a pleasant tune or melody
  • Synonym: harmonious - having components that are musically pleasing together
  • Antonym: discordant - having sounds that clash or lack harmony

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'melodiosus', from Greek 'melōidía' (singing, song).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01505874-a
Synset POS: a
