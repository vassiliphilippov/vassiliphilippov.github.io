troublous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌb.ləs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌb.ləs / (BrE)
full of trouble or distress; troubled; causing difficulty or unrest
full of trouble; troubled
archaic

Examples

The country endured many troublous years during the civil war.
In those troublous times, hope was hard to find.
Poets of the era wrote of troublous days and uncertain futures.

Synset Examples

these are troublous times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troubled - feeling or characterized by distress or worry
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance, calm or tranquil

Etymology

Formed from 'trouble' + '-ous', following the pattern of English adjectives ending in '-ous'.
