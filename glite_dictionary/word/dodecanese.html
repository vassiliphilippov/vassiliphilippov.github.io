Dodecanese

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊdəkəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌdəʊdəkəˈniːz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the southeastern Aegean Sea, belonging to Greece, known for their historical significance and tourism
Greek island group in the Aegean Sea

Examples

Many tourists visit the Dodecanese each summer to enjoy its beaches and ancient sites.
Rhodes is the largest island in the Dodecanese archipelago.
The Dodecanese were under Italian control before becoming part of Greece in 1947.

Etymology

From Greek *Dodekanēsos*, meaning 'twelve islands' (*dōdeka* 'twelve' + *nēsos* 'island').
