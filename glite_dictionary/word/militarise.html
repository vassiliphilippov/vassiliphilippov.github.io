to militarise

1. VERB

/ mɪˈlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to adapt or equip something for military use, or to make something more military in character
make military in use or character
british

Examples

The government decided to militarise the border area in response to rising tensions.
Plans to militarise several key industries were met with public concern.
After the coup, the new regime began to militarise the police force.

Synset Examples

militarize the Civil Service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demilitarise - to remove military influence or presence
  • Synonym: to militarize - to make military in use or character (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'militariser', based on 'militaire' (military), and ultimately from Latin 'militaris'.

2. VERB

/ mɪˈlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
To give a country, organization, or region a military character or capability, especially by building up armed forces.
give military character or arms

Examples

The government decided to militarise the border region in response to rising tensions.
After the coup, the leadership began to militarise the police force.
The treaty was intended to prevent any attempt to militarise the disputed territory.

Synset Examples

militarize Germany again after the war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demilitarise - remove military forces or character from a country or region
  • Synonym: arm - to equip with weapons

Etymology

From French *militariser*, based on *militaire* (military).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01101280-v
Synset POS: v
