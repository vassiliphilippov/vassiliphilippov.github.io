subpopulation

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌpɑpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A distinct group within a larger population, often with shared characteristics.
distinct group within a larger population

Examples

The researchers focused on a subpopulation of birds living in coastal regions.
Understanding the genetics of a subpopulation can help identify risk factors for disease.
Each subpopulation displayed different migration patterns throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: population - all the inhabitants of a particular area or group

Etymology

From sub- (Latin, 'under' or 'part of') + population.
