perfectibility

1. NOUN

/ pərˌfɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / pəˌfɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the capability of becoming perfect; the quality or condition of being capable of perfection.
capability of becoming perfect

Examples

Many philosophers have debated the perfectibility of human nature throughout history.
He devoted his life to the pursuit of moral perfectibility.
Some religious traditions teach the eventual perfectibility of the soul.

Synset Examples

he believes in the ultimate perfectibility of man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfection - the state of being imperfect; a flaw or defect

Etymology

From French *perfectibilité*, from Latin *perfectus* (perfect) + *-ibility* (ability, capability).
