gossamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːsəmər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsəmə / (BrE)
A fine, filmy, and delicate fabric with a very light and sheer texture.
delicate, fine gauze fabric

Examples

She draped a gossamer scarf over her shoulders for the evening gala.
The dress was made from layers of pale blue gossamer that floated as she walked.
Sunlight filtered through the gossamer curtains, filling the room with a gentle glow.

Etymology

From Middle English 'gossomer', originally meaning 'goose summer', referring to fine filmy cobwebs seen in autumn.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑsəmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsəmə / (BrE)
extremely light, thin, and delicate; having a fine, filmy texture like a cobweb
delicate and light; filmy

Examples

The gossamer fabric floated in the breeze, almost invisible against the sunlight.
Her voice had a gossamer quality, light and barely audible.
A gossamer layer of mist drifted across the quiet lake at dawn.

Synset Examples

this smallest and most ethereal of birds
gossamer shading through his playing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light, seeming too perfect for this world

Etymology

From Middle English 'gossomer', originally referring to a cobweb, likely from 'gos' (goose) and 'somer' (summer), perhaps from when geese are seen and cobwebs are common.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːsəmər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsəmə / (BrE)
fine, filmy threads spun by spiders, especially those seen floating in air or spread over grass or bushes
fine spider silk threads

Examples

A delicate sheet of gossamer floated through the morning air, glistening in the sunlight.
The garden was covered with gossamer after the fog lifted, making everything sparkle.
Children chased after the gossamer threads drifting across the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobweb - a web spun by a spider, especially when old and dusty

Etymology

Middle English, probably from 'goose' + 'summer', possibly first referring to cobwebs seen in late autumn.

4. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑːsəmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsəmə / (BrE)
(of fabric, material, etc.) extremely light, thin, and almost transparent; delicate and insubstantial.
light, thin, and almost transparent

Examples

The bride wore a gossamer veil that floated softly behind her.
Morning dew sparkled on the gossamer threads of a spider's web.
She admired the gossamer fabric of her new scarf in the sunlight.

Synset Examples

a hat with a diaphanous veil
filmy wings of a moth
gauzy clouds of dandelion down
gossamer cobwebs
sheer silk stockings
transparent chiffon
vaporous silks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphanous - (of fabric) light, delicate, and translucent
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to see through; not transparent

Etymology

From Middle English 'gossomer' (fine, filmy substance, especially cobwebs), probably from 'gos' (goose) + 'somer' (summer), perhaps from sightings of cobwebs during warm weather.
