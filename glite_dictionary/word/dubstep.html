dubstep

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.stɛp / (BrE)
A genre of electronic dance music featuring dark, syncopated rhythms, heavy basslines, and a tempo around 140 beats per minute.
genre of electronic dance music

Examples

He started producing dubstep tracks in his bedroom studio.
The club was packed with fans dancing to heavy dubstep beats.
Several mainstream artists have incorporated dubstep elements into their music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drum and bass - fast-paced genre of electronic music with prominent bass and breakbeats
  • Degree Lower: garage - a genre of electronic dance music that influenced dubstep
  • Degree Higher: trap - genre of electronic music sharing characteristics with dubstep but often higher energy

Etymology

Blend of 'dub' (a genre of music) and 'step' (from 2-step garage, a music style).
