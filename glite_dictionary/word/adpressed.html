adpressed

1. ADJ

/ ædˈprɛst / (AmE), / ədˈprɛst / (BrE)
pressed closely to or flat against something, especially a surface or another structure
pressed closely to a surface

Examples

The adpressed leaves conserve moisture by minimizing exposure to air.
Many insects have adpressed wings while at rest, making them less noticeable to predators.
The moss's adpressed stems allowed it to cling tightly to the rock face.

Synset Examples

adpressed hairs along the plant's stem
igneous rocks … closely appressed by this force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appressed - (of a structure) pressed closely against another part or surface

Etymology

From Latin adpressus, past participle of adprimere (to press toward).
