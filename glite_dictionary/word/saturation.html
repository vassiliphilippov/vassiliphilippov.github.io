saturation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæ.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsætʃ.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of soaking or filling something completely with a liquid or substance so that no more can be absorbed.
the act of thoroughly soaking or filling

Examples

The fabric reached full saturation after being submerged in dye for several hours.
The saturation of the soil with water caused flooding in the basement.
They measured the saturation of the air to determine humidity levels.

Synset Examples

the impregnation of wood with preservative
the saturation of cotton with ether

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impregnation - the process of soaking or infusing a substance

Etymology

From Latin saturatio(n-), from saturare ‘fill, saturate’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsatʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the state in which a system or substance cannot absorb or respond to any more of something due to having reached its maximum capacity
maximum absorption or response state

Examples

The amplifier reached saturation and no further increase in input changed the output.
Humidity climbed to saturation, making air feel heavy and dense.
After a certain point, the market achieved saturation and growth stagnated.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supersaturation - the state of being more highly concentrated than normally possible under given temperature and pressure
  • Antonym: desaturation - the process of removing saturation or reducing it

Etymology

From Latin saturatio(n-), from saturare 'fill, satisfy.'

3. NOUN

/ ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsatʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of thoroughly soaking something with a liquid until no more can be absorbed
soaking something completely with liquid

Examples

After an hour of heavy rain, the soil reached full saturation and began to flood.
The saturation of the towel made it drip with water when picked up.
Proper saturation of the sponge is necessary before cleaning the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaking - the act of making extremely wet or saturated with liquid
  • Antonym: drying - the process of removing moisture from something

Etymology

From Latin saturatio (a filling, satiety), from saturare (to fill, to saturate).

4. NOUN

/ ˌsæʧəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsætʃʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The intensity of a color, defined by its freedom from dilution with white and the vividness of its hue.
intensity or purity of a color

Examples

The vivid saturation of the artist's paintings drew the viewer's attention immediately.
Adjust the saturation on your monitor to make the colors appear more vibrant.
As the sun set, the saturation of the sky's colors began to deepen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chroma - the purity or intensity of a color
  • Degree Lower: hue - the attribute of color perceived as red, blue, etc.
  • Degree Higher: brightness - perceived luminance or lightness of a color

Etymology

From Medieval Latin saturatio(n-), from Latin saturare 'fill, sate'.
