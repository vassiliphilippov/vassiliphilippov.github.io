intangibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtændʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtændʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being intangible or not able to be touched or grasped physically.
the quality of being intangible

Examples

The intangibility of her emotions made them difficult to express in words.
Investors sometimes struggle with the intangibility of intellectual property compared to physical assets.
The artist sought to capture the intangibility of dreams on canvas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tangibility - the quality of being tangible or able to be touched

Etymology

From French *intangibilité*, from Latin *in-* (not) + *tangibilis* (that may be touched).
