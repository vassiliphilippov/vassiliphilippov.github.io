Phasmidia

1. NOUN

/ fæzˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / fazˈmɪdɪə / (BrE)
a subgroup of nematode worms in some classifications, characterized by the presence of phasmids and specific sensory structures
subgroup of nematode worms
medical

Examples

Phasmidia are distinguished from other nematodes by their unique sensory organs called phasmids.
Many parasitic nematodes that infect plants and animals belong to the Phasmidia subgroup.
The presence of lateral cervical papillae helps scientists identify members of the Phasmidia during microscopic examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Aphasmidia - subgroup of nematodes lacking phasmids
  • Synonym: Nematoda - phylum of roundworms

Etymology

From Greek phasma (apparition) + -idia (diminutive), referring to small, ghost-like sensory organs.

2. NOUN

/ fæzˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / fazˈmɪdɪə / (BrE)
a group of nematode worms characterized by phasmids, typically papillose sensory organs, and simple lateral sensory depressions at the anterior end, used in some classification systems
subgroup of nematode worms (Nematoda)
medical

Examples

Phasmidia are distinguished from other nematodes by the presence of specialized sensory organs called phasmids.
Many parasitic roundworms that affect humans belong to the Phasmidia subgroup.
Recent taxonomy debates question whether Phasmidia should be considered a distinct class in nematode classification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Aphasmidia - an alternate subgroup of nematode worms lacking phasmids

Etymology

From Greek 'phásma' (apparition, phantom) and '-idia' (diminutive plural ending), referring to small, subtle features.
