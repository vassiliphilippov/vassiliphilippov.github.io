hotel

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈtɛl / (AmE), / həʊˈtɛl / (BrE)
a building where people pay to stay and are usually provided with lodging, meals, and other services, especially for travelers or visitors
building offering paid lodging and services

Examples

We stayed at a beautiful hotel by the beach during our vacation.
The city center hotel offers complimentary breakfast and free Wi-Fi to all guests.
After a long day of driving, they checked into a small hotel just off the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motel - a roadside hotel designed primarily for motorists
  • Synonym: inn - an establishment for travelers offering lodging and typically food and drink
  • Synonym: hostel - budget accommodation with shared rooms, often for travelers

Etymology

From French *hôtel*, originally meaning a townhouse or mansion.
