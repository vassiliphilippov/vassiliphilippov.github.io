depredation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
(usually plural) an act of attacking or plundering; a destructive action, especially causing damage or loss.
destructive or plundering action

Examples

The abandoned village bore the unmistakable signs of depredation by marauding bands.
Centuries of depredation have left the ancient forest a shadow of its former self.
The local museum preserves relics that survived the depredations of war.

Synset Examples

the ravages of time
the depredations of age and disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravage - an act of violent destruction
  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage

Etymology

From Late Latin depraedatio (pillaging), from Latin depraedari (to plunder).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
an act of attacking, plundering, or laying waste, especially by robbing or ravaging
an act of plundering or ravaging

Examples

The village suffered repeated depredations by invading armies during the war.
Environmentalists are concerned about the depredation of forests by illegal loggers.
For centuries, coastal towns guarded against the depredations of pirates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - the act of stealing goods, typically using force
  • Synonym: pillage - the act of looting or violently robbing
  • Synonym: ravage - to cause severe and extensive damage or destruction to

Etymology

From Latin depraedatio (plundering), from depraedari (to plunder).
