resonant

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛzənənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzənənt / (BrE)
making you think of or remember a particular feeling, idea, or image; evocative.
evocative; suggestive; bringing to mind

Examples

Her words were resonant of her childhood in Italy, painting vivid pictures in our minds.
The film's soundtrack is resonant of classic jazz, transporting the audience to another era.
He spoke in a tone resonant with nostalgia, reminding everyone of summers long past.

Synset Examples

cannot forbear to close on this redolent literary note
a campaign redolent of machine politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evocative - bringing strong images, memories, or feelings to mind

Etymology

From Latin resonant-, 'echoing', from resonare 'to resound'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛzənənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzənənt / (BrE)
(of sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring; characterized by resonance.
deep, ringing sound; echoing

Examples

The speaker's resonant voice filled the auditorium with ease.
Her laughter was bright and resonant, echoing down the hallway.
A resonant tone is often preferred in classical singing.

Synset Examples

a resonant voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echoing - (of sound) repeating or reverberating
  • Antonym: dull - (of sound) lacking resonance, flat or muffled

Etymology

From Latin resonare (to resound), via Latin resonant- (echoing).
