marbles

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrbəlz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəlz / (BrE)
a children's game in which small glass or stone balls are rolled or shot to hit other balls
children's game with small balls

Examples

When I was a child, my friends and I would spend hours playing marbles in the schoolyard.
He lost all his favorite marbles in a single game to his older brother.
The rules of marbles vary from one region to another, but the game is popular worldwide.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəlz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəlz / (BrE)
a person's mental faculties; the ability to think clearly and rationally
mental faculties; rational capacity

Examples

After the accident, he was relieved to find he still had all his marbles.
She feared her grandfather was losing his marbles as he started forgetting simple things.
You have to keep your marbles when faced with such a stressful situation.

Synset Examples

he used his wits to get ahead
I was scared out of my wits
he still had all his marbles and was in full possession of a lively mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wits - mental faculties; ability to think clearly
  • Synonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind
  • Antonym: insanity - the state of being seriously mentally ill; madness

Etymology

Figurative use derived from the game 'marbles', suggesting the idea of 'having all one's marbles' as having one's faculties intact.
