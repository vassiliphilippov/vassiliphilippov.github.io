shuttlecock

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌt̬əlˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtəlkɒk / (BrE)
A light, cone-shaped object with a rounded end, typically made of cork or rubber and fitted with feathers or synthetic materials, that is struck back and forth in badminton and similar games.
badminton object with feathers

Examples

She hit the shuttlecock over the net with a swift flick of her wrist.
After losing the shuttlecock in the bushes, the game had to pause until they found it.
Professional players often prefer feather shuttlecocks to those made of synthetic materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birdie - another term for shuttlecock, mainly North American

Etymology

From 'shuttle' (as in weaving shuttle) + 'cock' (as in a male bird, referring to the feathered appearance).

2. VERB

/ ˈʃʌt̬lˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtlˌkɒk / (BrE)
to send or toss to and fro rapidly between people or places, especially in a repetitive or back-and-forth manner, like a shuttlecock is hit in badminton.
toss back and forth repeatedly

Examples

The proposal was shuttlecocked between different departments for months before a decision was made.
They shuttlecocked the blame, each refusing to accept responsibility for the error.
During the meeting, ideas were shuttlecocking back and forth across the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandy - to pass or exchange (words) rapidly and back and forth

Etymology

Derived from 'shuttlecock', the name of the badminton object, itself from 'shuttle' (a device for weaving) and 'cock' (from its resemblance to a bird).
