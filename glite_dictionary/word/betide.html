to betide

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtaɪd / (AmE), / bɪˈtaɪd / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) to happen to; to befall.
happen to; befall (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

Whatever may betide, she vowed to remain steadfast in her beliefs.
Woe betide anyone who tries to deceive the old king.
He wondered what would betide his dreams in the coming years.

Synset Examples

He promised that no harm would befall her
What has become of my children?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befall - (of something bad) happen to someone

Etymology

From Middle English betiden, from be- (prefix) + tiding (happening).
