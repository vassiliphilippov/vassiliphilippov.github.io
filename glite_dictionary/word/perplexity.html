perplexity

1. NOUN

/ pərˈplɛksəti / (AmE), / pəˈplɛksəti / (BrE)
A state of being confused or puzzled, especially because something is complex or difficult to understand.
confusion from complexity or difficulty

Examples

She stared at the complex puzzle in perplexity, unsure of where to begin.
His sudden change of plans filled his friends with perplexity.
There was perplexity on her face as she tried to follow the professor's explanation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of not being certain or clear
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin perplexitas (confusion, intricacy), from perplexus (entangled, confused).
