discredited

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkrɛdɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having lost respect, trust, or credibility, especially as a result of disapproval or being proven wrong.
having lost credibility or respect

Examples

After the scandal, the once-revered scientist became a discredited figure in the academic community.
The party distanced itself from the discredited leader to rebuild its reputation.
Many of the old theories have been discredited by new scientific evidence.

Synset Examples

a discredited politician
her damaged reputation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respected - regarded with admiration or esteem
  • Synonym: disgraced - having fallen from favor or lost respect due to a shameful act

Etymology

From the verb 'discredit' (from Latin 'dis-' expressing reversal + 'creditum' a loan, trust), meaning to damage the reputation of.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkrɛdɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪtɪd / (BrE)
having lost respect or credibility; regarded with shame or suspicion.
having lost respect or credibility

Examples

The scientist's reputation was permanently discredited after the falsified data came to light.
Once a respected leader, he is now a discredited figure in the community.
Their theory was discredited when new evidence emerged contradicting their claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgraced - having lost honor or respect, often publicly
  • Antonym: honored - respected and celebrated

Etymology

Past participle of 'discredit', from Latin 'dis-' (not) + 'creditum' (a loan, belief).
