raver

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.vər / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.və / (BrE)
a person who attends and participates in a rave, typically characterized by energetic dancing and electronic music
person attending a rave party

Examples

The club was packed with ravers dancing under flashing lights.
She's been a raver since her teenage years, attending parties across Europe.
Dozens of raver outfits were on display at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rave - a large dance party featuring electronic music

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪvər / (AmE), / ˈreɪvə / (BrE)
a person who speaks loudly or violently, often in an uncontrolled or angry manner
someone who speaks loudly or violently

Examples

The neighbors complained that the raver next door kept shouting all night.
When he lost his temper, he turned into a real raver, impossible to reason with.
Despite his reputation as a raver, he was calm and thoughtful in private.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranter - a person who speaks or complains loudly and angrily

Etymology

From 'rave' (to speak wildly), with agent suffix '-er'.
