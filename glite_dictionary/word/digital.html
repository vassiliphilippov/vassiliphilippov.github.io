digital

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl / (BrE)
relating to or performed with the fingers, especially in a medical context.
relating to the fingers
medical

Examples

During the physical, the doctor performed a digital examination to check for abnormalities.
A digital rectal exam is a common procedure used to assess prostate health.
The physician explained that a digital test involves using the fingers rather than instruments.

Synset Examples

digital examination

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger, toe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02723832-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl / (BrE)
(of a device or display) showing information using numbers rather than by a pointer or a continuous scale.
showing information using numbers

Examples

The digital clock on the wall showed the exact time in large red numbers.
She bought a new digital thermometer to get more accurate temperature readings.
A digital speedometer displays the speed as clear numbers instead of a moving needle.

Synset Examples

digital clock
digital readout

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analogue - describing a device or display using a continuous scale rather than discrete numbers

Etymology

From Latin digitus (finger, toe); modern sense via use in technology to denote discrete values.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02723968-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl / (BrE)
(of circuits, devices, or technology) representing information using discrete digits, especially binary numbers, rather than a continuous range.
using digits to represent data

Examples

Many modern cameras use digital sensors to capture and store images.
A digital thermometer displays the exact temperature in numbers on its screen.
Compared to analog watches, digital watches show the time with numbers instead of hands.

Synset Examples

digital computer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analog - representing data by a continuously variable physical quantity

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger, toe); later referring to numbers represented by digits.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00111512-a
Synset POS: a
