hole-and-corner

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊl ən ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / ˌhəʊl ən ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
(of an activity) conducted secretly or in a furtive manner, often for illicit or dubious purposes.
secretive or furtive (of activity)
archaic british

Examples

The group held their meetings in a hole-and-corner manner to avoid the authorities.
He was tired of his boss's hole-and-corner deals and lack of transparency.
The hole-and-corner nature of the negotiations made the employees suspicious.

Synset Examples

clandestine intelligence operations

a secret agent
secret sales of arms
surreptitious mobilization of troops
an undercover investigation
underground resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretly, especially for an illicit purpose
  • Synonym: secret - done, made, or conducted without the knowledge of others
  • Antonym: open - not concealed or secret; obvious

Etymology

From the idea of something being hidden away in a hole or a corner, implying secrecy or concealment.
