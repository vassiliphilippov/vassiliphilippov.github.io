answerability

1. NOUN

/ ˌænsərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɑːnsərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state of being responsible or accountable to someone, or for some activity or outcome
state of being responsible or accountable

Examples

The board emphasized the answerability of its members for any financial mismanagement.
Government officials must recognize their answerability to the public.
With greater power comes increased answerability for one's decisions and actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountability - the fact or condition of being accountable; responsibility
  • Synonym: responsibility - the state or fact of having a duty to deal with something or of having control over someone

Etymology

From 'answerable' + '-ity', with 'answerable' from Middle English.
