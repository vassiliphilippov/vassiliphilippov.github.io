Lutra

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtrə / (AmE), / ˈluːtrə / (BrE)
A genus of otters in the subfamily Lutrinae, including the Eurasian otter and related species.
genus of otters in Lutrinae

Examples

The Eurasian otter belongs to the genus Lutra and is found across Europe and Asia.
Researchers studied population trends of Lutra in different river systems.
The distribution of Lutra species has been affected by habitat loss and pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otter - a semiaquatic mammal of the weasel family, related to Lutra

Etymology

From Latin 'lutra' meaning 'otter'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːtrə / (AmE), / ˈluːtrə / (BrE)
A genus of otters in the subfamily Lutrinae, comprising several species found in Europe, Asia, and parts of Africa.
a genus of otters

Examples

The European otter belongs to the genus Lutra and is widespread across freshwater habitats.
Several members of the genus Lutra are known for their excellent swimming abilities.
Research indicates that Lutra species play an important role in aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otter - a semiaquatic mammal of the family Mustelidae
  • Degree Higher: Mustelidae - the family of mammals that includes otters, weasels, and badgers

Etymology

From Latin *lutra* (otter).
