arduousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdʒuəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːdjuːəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of requiring great effort or endurance; extreme difficulty or laboriousness.
quality of being extremely difficult or requiring great effort

Examples

He was unprepared for the arduousness of climbing the mountain in such harsh weather.
Many underestimate the arduousness of training for a marathon.
The arduousness of her daily commute eventually led her to move closer to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - the state of being hard to do or overcome
  • Antonym: ease - the quality of being easy or effortless

Etymology

From arduous + -ness; ultimately from Latin arduus (steep, difficult).
