decree

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkri / (AmE), / dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
to order or decide something officially, especially by legal or authoritative command
order or decide officially

Examples

The government decreed that all businesses must close by 8 p.m.
The court may decree the dissolution of the partnership if necessary.
The King decreed a national day of celebration.

Synset Examples

The King only can decree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - to command or instruct officially
  • Synonym: to proclaim - to announce officially or publicly

Etymology

From Latin 'decretum' (something decided), from 'decernere' (to decide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01029517-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈkri / (AmE), / dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
to order, decide, or declare something officially, especially by the authority of a ruler or government
order or decide officially with authority

Examples

The king decreed that a festival would be held every spring.
The government decreed new regulations to protect the environment.
After a lengthy debate, the committee decreed that the new policy would take effect immediately.

Synset Examples

The King decreed that all firstborn males should be killed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - to give an authoritative direction or instruction
  • Synonym: to proclaim - to announce officially or publicly

Etymology

From Old French *decre*, from Latin *decretum* ('something decided'), from *decernere* ('to decide').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00717773-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈkri / (AmE), / dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
an official order issued by a legal authority, especially a court; a legally binding command or decision recorded by a judge
official court order or command
legal

Examples

The court issued a decree prohibiting further construction on the land.
After the divorce, the judge signed the final decree settling custody and property issues.
Under the new decree, all businesses must comply with stricter safety regulations.

Synset Examples

a friend in New Mexico said that the order caused no trouble out there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Synonym: ruling - an official decision by a judge or court

Etymology

From Old French 'decre', from Latin 'decretum' (something decided), from 'decernere' (to decide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06551761-n
Synset POS: n
