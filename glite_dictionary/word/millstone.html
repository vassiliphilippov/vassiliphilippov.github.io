millstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.stəʊn / (BrE)
A heavy responsibility or burden that is difficult to carry or deal with.
a heavy burden or responsibility

Examples

The failing business became a millstone around his neck.
His debt was a millstone that he struggled to escape.
She viewed the old house as a financial millstone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or responsibility

Etymology

From Old English 'mylenstān' (millstone), from Latin 'molīna' (mill) + 'stān' (stone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɪlstəʊn / (BrE)
A heavy burden or responsibility, especially one that causes difficulty or prevents progress.
a heavy burden or hindrance

Examples

For years, the debt was a millstone that kept the company from growing.
His criminal record proved to be a millstone around his neck in the job market.
Trying to maintain that old house has become a real millstone for the family.

Synset Examples

she was an albatross around his neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albatross - a psychological burden that feels like a curse; something that impedes progress
  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load, especially one that is difficult to bear
  • Antonym: boon - a helpful or beneficial thing

Etymology

From Old English 'mylnstān', from 'mylen' (mill) + 'stān' (stone).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɪlstəʊn / (BrE)
one of a pair of large, heavy, flat stones used for grinding grain in a mill by rotating against one another
large grinding stone for grain

Examples

The old flour mill still has its original millstone on display for visitors.
Over centuries, the millstone has symbolized hard labor and sustenance.
The millstone ground the wheat into fine flour for baking bread.

Etymology

From Middle English milne-ston, from Old English mylenstān (mill-stone), from Latin molīna (mill) + stān (stone).
