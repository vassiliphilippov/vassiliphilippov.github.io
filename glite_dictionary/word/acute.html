acute

1. ADJ

/ əˈkjut / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) characterized by a rapid onset and a short but severe course.
having rapid, severe onset (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that acute infections require immediate attention and can worsen quickly if untreated.
Her symptoms were so sudden and intense that the condition was classified as acute.
Unlike chronic pain, acute pain appears abruptly and is usually more severe at first.

Synset Examples

acute appendicitis
the acute phase of the illness
acute patients

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronic - (of a disease) persisting for a long time or constantly recurring

Etymology

From Latin acutus (sharp, pointed), past participle of acuere (to sharpen).

6. NOUN

/ əˈkjut / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt / (BrE)
A mark ( ́ ) placed above a vowel to indicate a particular pronunciation, such as stress or a specific vowel quality.
a diacritical mark ( ́ ) over vowels

Examples

In Spanish, the word 'José' is spelled with an acute accent over the 'e'.
French uses the acute to show that the letter 'e' should be pronounced 'ay'.
Type the letter with an acute by holding down the appropriate key on your keyboard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grave - a mark ( ̀ ) placed over a vowel indicating pronunciation or stress

Etymology
