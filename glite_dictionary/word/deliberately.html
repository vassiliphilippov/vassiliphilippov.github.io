deliberately

1. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪbɚətli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərətli / (BrE)
In a careful and unhurried way; with intention and thought.
in a careful or intentional manner

Examples

She spoke deliberately, choosing her words with care.
The painter moved deliberately as he worked on the canvas.
He walked deliberately to avoid making any noise.

Synset Examples

she was working deliberately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentionally - done on purpose
  • Antonym: accidentally - not done on purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'deliberatus', past participle of 'deliberare' (to consider carefully).

2. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪb.ɚ.ət.li / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪb.ər.ət.li / (BrE)
In a way that is done on purpose and not by accident; intentionally.
intentionally; on purpose

Examples

She deliberately avoided answering the question when asked about her plans.
The artist deliberately chose bright colors to make the painting stand out.
He deliberately left the door open so the cat could come inside.

Synset Examples

he used that word intentionally
I did this by choice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentionally - in an intentional manner, on purpose
  • Antonym: accidentally - by chance; not on purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'deliberatus', past participle of 'deliberare' (to weigh, consider).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00062868-r
Synset POS: r
