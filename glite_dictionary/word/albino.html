albino

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbaɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ælˈbiːnəʊ / (BrE)
An animal or person having a congenital absence of pigment in the skin, hair, and eyes, resulting in white skin and hair and usually pink eyes.
animal or person with congenital lack of pigment

Examples

The zookeeper pointed out the rare albino peacock to the group of visitors.
Scientists have studied the genetics behind why some animals are born albino.
They spotted an albino deer roaming the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albinism - the condition of having no pigment in the skin, hair, and eyes

Etymology

From Latin 'albinus' (whitish), from 'albus' (white).

3. NOUN

/ ælˈbaɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ælˈbiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A person or animal born with a congenital absence of pigment in the skin, hair, and eyes, resulting in a very pale appearance.
person or animal with congenital absence of pigment

Examples

The albino sat quietly in the shade to protect his sensitive skin from the sun.
Some animals, like white rabbits, are naturally albino and have red eyes.
Doctors recommend that an albino use sunscreen and protective clothing when outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albinism - congenital condition marked by lack of pigment in skin, hair, and eyes

Etymology

From Portuguese or Spanish 'albino', from Latin 'albus' (white).
