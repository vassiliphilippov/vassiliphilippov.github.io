organdy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡəndi / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡəndi / (BrE)
a fine, sheer, and stiff muslin fabric, typically used for women's dresses and curtains
sheer, stiff muslin fabric

Examples

The bridal gown was made of delicate organdy that shimmered in the sunlight.
She wore a white organdy dress to the summer party, looking both elegant and fresh.
Vintage blouses often feature ruffles and trims of fine organdy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muslin - a lightweight cotton cloth in plain weave

Etymology

From French 'organdi', of unknown origin, possibly from a place name.
