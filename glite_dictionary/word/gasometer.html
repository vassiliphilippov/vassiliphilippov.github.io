gasometer

1. NOUN

/ ɡæˈsɑːmətər / (AmE), / ɡəˈsɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a large, airtight container, often spherical or cylindrical, used for storing gas to be used as fuel
large container for storing gas

Examples

The old gasometer near the river has become a city landmark.
Engineers inspected the gasometer regularly to ensure there were no leaks.
A massive gasometer dominates the skyline in that industrial area.

Etymology

From French 'gazomètre', from 'gaz' (gas) + '-mètre' (meter/measurer).

2. NOUN

/ ɡæˈsɑːmətər / (AmE), / ɡəˈsɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the amount of gas passing through a pipe
instrument for measuring gas flow

Examples

The engineer used a gasometer to monitor the flow rate in the pipeline.
Regular maintenance of the gasometer ensures accurate gas measurements.
A faulty gasometer can lead to incorrect billing for gas usage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas meter - device for measuring the quantity of gas consumed

Etymology

From gas + -meter, possibly influenced by French 'gazomètre.'
