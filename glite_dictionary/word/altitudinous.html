altitudinous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæl.tɪˈtuː.də.nəs / (AmE), / ˌæl.tɪˈtjuː.dɪ.nəs / (BrE)
very high or lofty; rising to a great height
very high or lofty

Examples

The hikers marveled at the altitudinous cliffs that surrounded the valley.
Her altitudinous ambitions led her to pursue a career in aerospace engineering.
The altitudinous peaks of the Himalayas are often shrouded in clouds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - of great height; elevated
  • Synonym: towering - extremely tall, especially in comparison with the surroundings
  • Antonym: low - not far above the ground; having little height

Etymology

Formed from 'altitude' (height) with the adjectival suffix '-inous'.
