cerecloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪrˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.klɒθ / (BrE)
A waterproof waxed cloth, especially one used in the past as a shroud for covering dead bodies.
waterproof waxed cloth (historical shroud)
archaic

Examples

The body was wrapped in cerecloth to prevent moisture from seeping in.
Historians discovered a cerecloth still intact within the ancient tomb.
She examined the texture of the old cerecloth found in the museum's collection.

Etymology

From Latin 'cera' (wax) + English 'cloth'.
