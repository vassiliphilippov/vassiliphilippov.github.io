textbook

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛkstˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɛkst.bʊk / (BrE)
very typical; serving as a perfect or classic example of something, as described in a textbook.
very typical or classic example

Examples

Her conduct in the emergency was a textbook example of professionalism under pressure.
The researchers observed a textbook case of the disease in the patient.
He responded with a textbook answer that covered all the key points.

Synset Examples

a casebook schizophrenic
a textbook example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - serving as a perfect example of its kind

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɛkst.bʊk / (BrE)
A book used as a standard source of information on a particular subject for study in schools or colleges.
book for study in schools or colleges

Examples

She spent the afternoon reading her biology textbook for the upcoming exam.
The teacher asked everyone to bring their math textbook to class.
His economics textbook is filled with helpful diagrams and charts.

Synset Examples

his economics textbook is in its tenth edition
the professor wrote the text that he assigned students to buy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual - a handbook or reference book giving instructions
  • Synonym: guidebook - a book of information about a subject, especially for tourists or learners

Etymology

From 'text' (main body of a printed work) + 'book'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstbʊk / (BrE)
a book used as a standard work for the study of a particular subject in schools or colleges
book for study in schools or colleges

Examples

She opened her textbook to review the chapter before the exam.
Most students bought the required textbook at the campus bookstore.
The professor assigned a new textbook for the upcoming chemistry course.

Synset Examples

his economics textbook is in its tenth edition
the professor wrote the text that he assigned students to buy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual - a book giving instructions, especially for operating something
  • Synonym: reference book - a book intended to be consulted for information on specific matters
  • Antonym: novel - a fictitious prose narrative of considerable length

Etymology

From 'text' (from Latin textus, 'woven, tissue, discourse') + 'book'.
