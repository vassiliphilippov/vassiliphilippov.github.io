cytopathogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪtoʊˌpæθəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˌpæθəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
causing or relating to pathological changes in cells, especially as a result of infection or disease.
causing disease-related changes in cells
medical

Examples

The cytopathogenic effects of the virus were evident under the microscope.
Researchers are studying how cytopathogenic factors contribute to tissue degeneration.
A cytopathogenic strain was identified as the cause of the rapid cell destruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytotoxic - toxic to cells; causing cell damage or death

Etymology

From Greek kytos (cell) + pathogenic (causing disease).
