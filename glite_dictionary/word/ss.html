SS

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛs ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˈɛs / (BrE)
an elite paramilitary organization under Adolf Hitler and the Nazi Party, responsible for many of the crimes against humanity during World War II, including running the concentration camps
Nazi paramilitary organization; World War II

Examples

The atrocities committed by the SS during the Holocaust are widely documented.
Many members of the SS were tried for war crimes after the Second World War.
The SS played a key role in enforcing Nazi policies throughout Germany and occupied territories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gestapo - the official secret police of Nazi Germany

Etymology

Abbreviation of German 'Schutzstaffel' meaning 'protection squadron'.

2. NOUN

/ ɛs ɛs / (AmE), / ɛs ɛs / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the United States Secret Service, the federal agency responsible for protecting the president, vice president, their families, and certain foreign dignitaries, as well as for investigating counterfeiting and related crimes.
United States Secret Service (agency)
american

Examples

The SS was called in after a credible threat to the president was reported.
Agents from the SS coordinated security for the visiting diplomat.
Counterfeiting cases are investigated by the SS in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Secret Service - the United States federal agency that protects the president and investigates counterfeiting

Synset POS: n
