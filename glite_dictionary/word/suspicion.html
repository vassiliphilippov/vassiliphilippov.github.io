suspicion

1. NOUN

/ səˈspɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃən / (BrE)
the tendency to suspect or mistrust others; a state or quality of being suspicious
quality of being suspicious

Examples

Her constant suspicion made it hard for her to form close relationships.
A marriage can be ruined by mutual suspicion and lack of trust.
The manager's suspicion caused him to double-check every employee's work.

Synset Examples

his suspiciousness destroyed his marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence
  • Antonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or truth of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'suspicio' (act of looking up to, mistrust).

2. NOUN

/ səˈspɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling or belief that someone may be guilty of dishonesty or wrongdoing, without definite proof.
doubt about someone's honesty or actions

Examples

His constant late arrivals raised suspicion among his coworkers.
There was a widespread suspicion that the votes had been tampered with.
Despite her alibi, the police still had suspicion about her involvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence in someone or something
  • Antonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or truth of someone

Etymology

From Old French 'suspicion', from Latin 'suspicio' (mistrust, suspicion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05706756-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ səˈspɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being suspected or thought likely to have done something wrong or illegal
the state of being suspected

Examples

After the incident, he fell under suspicion and faced many questions from the authorities.
There was no concrete evidence, but a cloud of suspicion hung over her for months.
His behavior aroused suspicion among his colleagues, who began to watch him closely.

Synset Examples

he tried to shield me from suspicion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being completely confident or having no doubt

Etymology

From Latin suspicio (mistrust), from suspicere (to look up at, mistrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14006324-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ səˈspɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a feeling or belief that something may be true or exist, often without sure proof
impression something may be true

Examples

She had a strong suspicion that her keys were lost in the garden.
My suspicion is that he forgot to send the email.
There was a growing suspicion that the company would announce bad news.

Synset Examples

he had an intuition that something had gone wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something immediately, without conscious reasoning
  • Antonym: certainty - firm conviction that something is the case

Etymology

From Latin suspicio (looking up to, mistrust), from suspicere (to look up at).
