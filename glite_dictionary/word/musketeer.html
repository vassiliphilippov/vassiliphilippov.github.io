musketeer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌmʌskɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
A soldier, especially in the past, who was armed with a musket.
historical soldier armed with a musket

Examples

The royal guard was composed of elite musketeers ready to defend the king.
During the 17th century, every European army deployed musketeers on the battlefield.
He dressed as a musketeer for the historical reenactment parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot

Etymology

From French 'mousquetaire', from 'mousquet' (musket).
