mural

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjʊrəl / (AmE), / ˈmjʊə.rəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring on a wall or walls
relating to walls

Examples

The artist specializes in mural decorations for public buildings.
They installed mural tiles to give the kitchen a Mediterranean feel.
A mural plaque commemorates the event on the community center's wall.

Synset Examples

mural painting

Etymology

From Latin muralis (of a wall), from murus (wall).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjʊr.əl / (AmE), / ˈmjʊə.rəl / (BrE)
A painting or other artwork applied directly to a wall or ceiling surface as a decoration.
a wall painting or artwork

Examples

The city commissioned a new mural for the community center wall.
Tourists stopped to admire the colorful mural depicting local history.
Students painted a mural in the school hallway to brighten the space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fresco - a technique of mural painting on wet plaster

Etymology

From Latin 'muralis' (of a wall), from 'murus' (wall).
