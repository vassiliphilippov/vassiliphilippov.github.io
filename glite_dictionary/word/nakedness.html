nakedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being without clothing or lacking any covering
the state of being without clothing

Examples

The artist sought to capture the beauty of nakedness in her paintings.
In many cultures, nakedness is associated with vulnerability and innocence.
He felt a sense of nakedness without the comfort of his old uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudity - the state of being naked, especially in art or public

Etymology

From Old English 'nacodnes', from 'nacod' (naked) + '-nes' (ness, state).

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
an appearance or state of being empty, bleak, or desolate, especially in a landscape or environment
bleak or exposed state or atmosphere

Examples

Standing on the hill, she was struck by the utter nakedness of the winter landscape.
Photographers are often drawn to the nakedness of abandoned buildings.
The nakedness of the desert after the fire was haunting.

Synset Examples

the nakedness of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleakness - a state or quality of being cold, empty, or miserable
  • Synonym: barrenness - state of being bare or lacking vegetation or life

Etymology

From Middle English nakednes, from Old English nacodnes.
