tarragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtærəɡən / (AmE), / ˈtarəɡən / (BrE)
An aromatic perennial herb of the daisy family, native to southeastern Europe and Asia, often used for flavoring food.
aromatic culinary herb

Examples

She added fresh tarragon to the vinaigrette for a distinctive flavor.
The chicken was seasoned with tarragon and lemon juice.
You can grow tarragon in your herb garden if the climate is mild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estragon - another term for tarragon, especially in culinary contexts

Etymology

From Old French 'targon', from Medieval Latin 'tarchon', from Arabic 'ṭarḵūn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtærəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈtærəɡən / (BrE)
A fragrant herb with narrow leaves, used fresh or preserved in vinegar as a seasoning in cooking.
herb used as seasoning

Examples

She added a handful of tarragon to the sauce for extra flavor.
The chef recommends using fresh tarragon leaves in this chicken recipe.
Pickled tarragon gives the vinaigrette a distinctive taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - a plant used for flavoring food or for medicinal purposes

Etymology

From Old French targon, via Medieval Latin tarchon, ultimately from Arabic ṭarxūn.
