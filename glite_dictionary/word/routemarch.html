routemarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtˌmɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈruːtmɑːtʃ / (BrE)
A long march undertaken by troops as a form of training or exercise, typically over a set route or distance.
military training march

Examples

The soldiers prepared for a strenuous routemarch early in the morning.
A ten-mile routemarch is part of their basic training regimen.
They completed the routemarch despite the cold and rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - an organized walk by soldiers or a group

Etymology

Formed from 'route' (a set path) and 'march' (walk as military exercise).
