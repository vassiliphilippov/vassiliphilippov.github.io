William Schwenk Gilbert

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ʃwɛŋk ˈgɪlbɚt / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ʃwɛŋk ˈɡɪlbət / (BrE)
(1836–1911) English librettist and dramatist, best known for his collaboration with Sir Arthur Sullivan on a famous series of comic operettas.
English librettist and dramatist, collaborator with Sullivan

Examples

William Schwenk Gilbert wrote the libretti for some of the most beloved comic operettas in English theatre.
Fans of musical theatre often study the works of William Schwenk Gilbert and Arthur Sullivan.
The satirical wit of William Schwenk Gilbert remains influential in modern stage productions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arthur Sullivan - English composer who collaborated with Gilbert on comic operettas
