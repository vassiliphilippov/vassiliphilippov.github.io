to decarbonize

1. VERB

/ diˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈkɑːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to remove carbon deposits from (an engine or similar device)
remove carbon from an engine

Examples

The mechanic had to decarbonize the engine to restore its performance.
It's important to decarbonize your lawn mower regularly for optimal functioning.
After years of use, the old car needed to be decarbonized to remove built-up deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to remove dirt, marks, or unwanted substances

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (removal) and 'carbon' (element name), plus '-ize'.
