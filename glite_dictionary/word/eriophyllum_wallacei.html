Eriophyllum wallacei

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈfɪliəm ˈwɒləsi / (AmE), / ˌɛri.əʊˈfɪliəm ˈwɒləsi / (BrE)
A small, gray, woolly annual plant with tufted leaves and small golden-yellow flower heads, native to the southwestern United States.
species of small woolly-flowered desert plant

Examples

In the Mojave Desert, the Eriophyllum wallacei blooms with bright yellow flowers each spring.
Many botanists admire the dense woolly leaves of Eriophyllum wallacei for their unique adaptation to arid environments.
Photographers often seek out Eriophyllum wallacei during wildflower season for its striking golden blossoms.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'phyllon' (leaf); species named for botanist William Allen Wallace.
