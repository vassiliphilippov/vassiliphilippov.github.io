Zygophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˌɡɑfɪˈleɪsi.i / (AmE), / zaɪˌɡɒfɪˈliːsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, including small trees, shrubs, and herbs typically found in warm, arid, and saline regions, often resinous and sometimes poisonous, such as genera Zygophyllum, Tribulus, Guaiacum, and Larrea.
a plant family of arid and saline regions

Examples

The Zygophyllaceae family includes plants that thrive in dry desert environments.
Several species in the Zygophyllaceae family are known for their medicinal properties.
Larrea tridentata, a member of Zygophyllaceae, is commonly known as the creosote bush.

Etymology

From modern Latin Zygophyllaceae, based on the genus Zygophyllum, from Greek zygon (yoke) + phyllon (leaf).
