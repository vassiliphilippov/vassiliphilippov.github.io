placable

1. ADJ

/ ˈplækəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplækəb(ə)l / (BrE)
Easily calmed or pacified; willing to forgive or be appeased.
easily calmed or appeased

Examples

The placable child stopped crying as soon as he was given his favorite toy.
Despite the harsh words, her placable nature allowed her to forgive quickly.
Unlike his implacable rival, John was known for his placable temperament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: implacable - unable to be placated or appeased; relentless

Etymology

From Latin placabilis (easily appeased), from placare (to calm or appease).
