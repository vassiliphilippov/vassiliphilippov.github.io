committee

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A group of people appointed or elected to perform a specific function or to consider and report on a particular matter.
group appointed for a specific task

Examples

The school formed a committee to plan the annual fundraising event.
She serves on the safety committee at her workplace.
A special committee was created to investigate the incident.

Synset Examples

a committee is a group that keeps minutes and loses hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panel - a small group of people brought together to discuss or judge something
  • Synonym: board - a group of persons having managerial or advisory duties

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Middle French 'comité' (person entrusted), from Latin 'committere' (to entrust).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A group of people appointed or self-organized to discuss, manage, or promote a particular issue or activity.
group formed to manage or promote something

Examples

The school formed a committee to organize the annual science fair.
Each committee meets monthly to review progress and set new goals.
He volunteered to serve on the fundraising committee for the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: board - a group of people officially responsible for a particular organization
  • Synonym: panel - a group of people gathered for a special purpose, e.g., judging or discussion
  • Synonym: commission - an official group given a particular task or responsibility

Etymology

From early modern English, based on Latin 'committere' (to entrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08342616-n
Synset POS: n
