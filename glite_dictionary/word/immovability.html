immovability

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊvəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to move or change position
the state of not being movable

Examples

The immovability of the ancient statue amazed the archaeologists.
Due to the immovability of the safe, it could not be stolen from the premises.
She admired his immovability in the face of criticism and pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mobility - the ability to move or be moved easily

Etymology

From Latin 'immovibilis' (not movable) + English '-ity'.
