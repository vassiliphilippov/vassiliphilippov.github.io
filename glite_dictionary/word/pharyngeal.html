pharyngeal

1. ADJ

/ færɪnˈdʒiəl / (AmE), / fɑːrɪnˈdʒiːəl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated in the pharynx (the part of the throat behind the mouth and nasal cavity).
relating to the pharynx (throat)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a pharyngeal infection during the examination.
Certain languages contain unique pharyngeal consonants not found in English.
Swelling in the pharyngeal area can make swallowing difficult.

Synset Examples

pharyngeal fricatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laryngeal - relating to the larynx (voice box)

Etymology

From Greek pharyng-, meaning 'pharynx', + -eal (relating to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03008607-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ fəˈrɪndʒiəl / (AmE), / fəˈrɪndʒɪəl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with the root of the tongue against the pharynx (back of the throat).
consonant produced in the pharynx

Examples

Arabic contains several pharyngeal consonants not found in English.
The sound represented by 'ʕ' in the IPA is a voiced pharyngeal.
Students practiced articulating a pharyngeal during their phonetics class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: uvular - a consonant produced with the back of the tongue against or near the uvula

Etymology

From Greek pharynx (throat) + -eal (pertaining to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07134884-n
Synset POS: n
