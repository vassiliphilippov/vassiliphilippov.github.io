jerboa

1. NOUN

/ dʒɚˈboʊ.ə / (AmE), / dʒəˈbәʊ.ə / (BrE)
A small, mouselike rodent with long hind legs for jumping, native to arid regions of North Africa and Asia.
desert-dwelling jumping rodent

Examples

The Sahara jerboa uses its powerful hind legs to leap away from predators.
Scientists observed the nocturnal behavior of the jerboa in its natural desert habitat.
A jerboa can cover surprising distances in a single bound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - North American desert rodent that hops in a similar way

Etymology

From Arabic 'jarbū‘'.
