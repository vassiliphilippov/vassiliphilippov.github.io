predisposed

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriː.dɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
likely or liable to be affected or influenced by a particular condition or factor, especially in terms of health or behavior.
susceptible or inclined to something

Examples

People with a family history of diabetes may be predisposed to the condition.
His genetic makeup means he is predisposed to high blood pressure.
Stressful environments can leave children predisposed to develop anxiety disorders.

Synset Examples

because of conditions in the mine, miners are predisposed to lung disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
  • Antonym: immune - not susceptible or resistant to something, especially disease

Etymology

From Latin 'praedisponere' (to arrange beforehand), via French 'prédisposer'.
