to formalise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔːrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something formal or official, often by following established rules or procedures
make formal or official
british

Examples

The organization decided to formalise its partnership with a written agreement.
She wanted to formalise the training process to ensure consistency.
They formalised their engagement at a small family ceremony.

Synset Examples

We formalized the appointment and gave him a title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to formalize - make formal or official (US spelling)

Etymology

From French *formaliser*, from Latin *formalis* (formal).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔr.məˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something official or legally valid by following an established procedure or set of rules
make legally or officially valid
british

Examples

The company decided to formalise its partnership by signing a contract.
After months of negotiation, they finally formalised the agreement in court.
We need to formalise these procedures to ensure consistency across all departments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to formalize - to make something official or legally valid (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'formaliser', from Latin 'formalis' (formal).
