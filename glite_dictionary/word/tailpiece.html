tailpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌpis / (AmE), / ˈteɪl.piːs / (BrE)
an appendage attached to the end of something to extend its length
an attachment extending the end of something

Examples

The old ship's mast needed a tailpiece to make it tall enough for the new sail.
The mechanic attached a tailpiece to lengthen the exhaust pipe.
A wooden tailpiece was fixed to the beam to improve its reach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extension - something that is added to another thing to lengthen it; an extending part

Etymology

From 'tail' + 'piece', indicating an added part at the end.
