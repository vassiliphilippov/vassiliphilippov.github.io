to prink

1. VERB

/ prɪŋk / (AmE), / prɪŋk / (BrE)
to dress or arrange oneself in a careful and fussily elegant manner
dress up in a fussily elegant way

Examples

She always takes time to prink in front of the mirror before leaving the house.
He prinked for hours before the dinner party, choosing just the right tie and jacket.
You could see her prinking with her scarf, adjusting every fold until it was perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - put on formal or elaborate clothing
  • Synonym: to preen - devote effort to making oneself look attractive and tidy

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'prinken' (to adorn oneself, to strut), related to 'pronk' (finery).

2. VERB

/ prɪŋk / (AmE), / prɪŋk / (BrE)
to dress or adorn oneself with care or in a showy manner, especially to look attractive
dress up or adorn oneself

Examples

She spent hours prinking in front of the mirror before the wedding.
He likes to prink himself up for special occasions.
The girls prinked before the dance, hoping to impress everyone.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes
  • Synonym: to spruce up - to make oneself neat or smart in appearance
  • Antonym: to dress down - to wear more casual or simple clothing

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'prinken' (to adorn), or related to 'prink', meaning a smart or prim appearance in English dialects.
