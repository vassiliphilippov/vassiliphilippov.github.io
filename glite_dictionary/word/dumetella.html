Dumetella

1. NOUN

/ duːməˈtɛlə / (AmE), / djuːmɪˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds in the family Mimidae, known as catbirds, especially the gray catbird (Dumetella carolinensis).
genus of catbirds

Examples

The gray catbird is the only species in the genus Dumetella.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a pair of Dumetella nesting in the garden.
Research on Dumetella has contributed to our understanding of mimicry in songbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catbird - a North American songbird in the genus Dumetella

Etymology

From Latin 'dumetum' (thicket) + diminutive suffix '-ella', referring to its preference for thicket habitats.

2. NOUN

/ duːməˈtɛlə / (AmE), / djuːmɪˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of North American catbirds in the family Mimidae, best known for the gray catbird (Dumetella carolinensis).
genus of North American catbirds

Examples

The Dumetella genus includes only a single species, the gray catbird.
Birdwatchers observed a Dumetella singing in the dense shrubs along the riverbank.
The scientific name for the gray catbird is Dumetella carolinensis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catbird - a bird of the family Mimidae, known for its catlike call

Etymology

From Latin 'dumetum' (thicket), referring to its habitat.
