Centrosema virginianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəˈsiːmə vərˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəʊˈsiːmə vɜːˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (BrE)
A large-flowered, weakly twining or prostrate vine native to eastern North America, sometimes cultivated for its purple and white flowers.
a North American vine with purple and white flowers

Examples

The delicate blossoms of Centrosema virginianum are a highlight of many native plant gardens.
Botanists study Centrosema virginianum for its interesting twining growth habit.
In spring, Centrosema virginianum covers the ground with a carpet of purple and white flowers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Centrosema from Greek kentron (spur) + sêma (sign); species epithet virginianum for Virginia, USA.
