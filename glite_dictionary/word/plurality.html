plurality

1. NOUN

/ plʊˈrælɪti / (AmE), / plʊəˈralɪti / (BrE)
the state of being plural, especially in grammar; the condition of having or referring to more than one person or thing
state of being plural (grammar)

Examples

In English, plurality is typically shown by adding 's' to the end of a noun.
Some languages indicate plurality with word endings, while others use separate words entirely.
Understanding plurality helps learners master correct subject-verb agreement in writing and speech.

Synset Examples

to mark plurality, one language may add an extra syllable to the word whereas another may simply change the vowel in the existing final syllable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plural - form indicating more than one in grammar

Etymology

From Medieval Latin pluralitas, from Latin pluralis (plural).

2. NOUN

/ plʊˈræl.ə.ti / (AmE), / plʊəˈral.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a large number or variety of people or things, not specified or counted precisely.
large number or variety; multitude

Examples

There was a plurality of opinions expressed at the forum.
The city offers a plurality of cultural events throughout the year.
A plurality of approaches can lead to innovative solutions.

Synset Examples

a battalion of ants
a multitude of TV antennas
a plurality of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things
  • Synonym: variety - a number of different types or things

Etymology

From Latin pluralitas (multiplicity), from pluralis (plural).

3. NOUN

/ pləˈræl.ə.ti / (AmE), / plʊəˈral.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(in elections with more than two options) the number of votes received by the candidate or party with the most votes, when it is less than half of the total votes cast.
largest number of votes (less than majority)

Examples

The candidate won with a plurality of votes, not an absolute majority.
In a three-way race, she secured a plurality, finishing ahead of her two opponents.
No party achieved a majority, but the Conservatives claimed a plurality in Parliament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: majority - the greater number or part; more than half of the total

Etymology

From Latin pluralitas (number consisting of more than one).
