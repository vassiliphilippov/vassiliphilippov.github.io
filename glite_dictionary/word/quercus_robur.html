Quercus robur

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs roʊˈbɜr / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈrəʊbər / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous European oak tree with smooth, lobed leaves, valued for its hard, light-colored wood; also known as the English oak.
European oak tree species; English oak

Examples

The majestic Quercus robur dominates many ancient English woodlands.
Carpenters often use timber from Quercus robur for making durable furniture.
Scientists studied the leaf shape of Quercus robur to understand its adaptability to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English oak - Common name for Quercus robur, a prominent European oak species

Etymology

From Latin *Quercus* (oak) and *robur* (hardwood, strength).
