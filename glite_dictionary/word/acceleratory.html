acceleratory

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛl.əˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.ər.ə.tər.i / (BrE)
Causing or tending to increase speed or rate of movement.
tending to increase speed

Examples

The medication had an acceleratory effect on the patient's heart rate.
Recent changes in technology have had an acceleratory influence on economic growth.
The coach implemented new drills to provide an acceleratory boost to the team's performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deceleratory - tending to decrease velocity
  • Synonym: accelerative - serving to accelerate; increasing speed

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten) + '-ory' (having the effect of).
