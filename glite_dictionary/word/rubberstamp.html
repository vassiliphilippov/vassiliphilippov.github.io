to rubberstamp

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌbərˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈrʌbəˌstæmp / (BrE)
to mark a document with a rubber stamp, usually as a form of official approval or confirmation
mark officially with a rubber stamp

Examples

The manager must rubberstamp each invoice before payment is processed.
Every proposal was rubberstamped without any real discussion.
She spent the morning rubberstamping attendance forms for her supervisor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to officially accept or agree to something

Etymology

From 'rubber stamp', referring to a stamp made of rubber used for imprinting marks or signatures on documents.

2. VERB

/ ˈrʌbərˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈrʌbəˌstæmp / (BrE)
to approve or endorse automatically, without proper consideration or scrutiny
automatically approve (without scrutiny)

Examples

The board tends to rubberstamp the CEO's recommendations without discussion.
Congress cannot simply rubberstamp every proposal brought before it.
Critics accused the committee of rubberstamping decisions made by higher authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - to give support or approval to something officially
  • Antonym: veto - to reject a decision or proposal officially

Etymology

From the noun 'rubber stamp,' referring to a stamp used for quick, mechanical approval.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00676436-v
Synset POS: v
