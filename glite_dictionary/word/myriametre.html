myriametre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪriəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmɪriəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to 10,000 meters (10 kilometers); now rarely used.
metric unit of length, 10,000 meters

Examples

The distance between the two cities was measured as five myriametres.
Although rarely used today, the myriametre was once part of the metric system.
The scientist recorded the length as 2 myriametres, equivalent to 20 kilometers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kilometre - A metric unit of length equal to 1,000 meters

Etymology

From French 'myriamètre', from Greek 'myrios' (countless, ten thousand) + 'mètre' (meter).
