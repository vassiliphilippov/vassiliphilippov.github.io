Chabad

1. NOUN

/ xəˈbɑd / (AmE), / xəˈbɑːd / (BrE)
a Jewish Hasidic movement known for outreach, hospitality, and emphasis on religious study and community engagement
Jewish Hasidic movement

Examples

The Chabad center welcomed both locals and tourists for the holiday celebration.
Many universities have a Chabad house that provides meals and classes for Jewish students.
Thanks to Chabad, travelers often have a place to celebrate religious festivals abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidism - a Jewish religious movement founded in 18th-century Eastern Europe

Etymology

From Hebrew חב״ד (Chabad), acronym of חָכְמָה (wisdom), בִּינָה (understanding), דַּעַת (knowledge).

2. NOUN

/ xəˈbad / (AmE), / xəˈbad / (BrE)
a Hasidic movement within Orthodox Judaism known for outreach and education, particularly associated with the Lubavitch community
Hasidic Jewish movement (Lubavitch)

Examples

Many Jewish communities around the world are supported by the outreach efforts of Chabad.
He attended a Chabad house while studying at university, where he learned more about Judaism.
The teachings of Chabad emphasize joy and the importance of helping others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidism - a Jewish religious movement emphasizing mysticism and piety

Etymology

From Hebrew חב״ד (Chabad), acronym of חכמה, בינה, דעת (Chokhmah, Binah, Da'at – 'wisdom, understanding, knowledge').
