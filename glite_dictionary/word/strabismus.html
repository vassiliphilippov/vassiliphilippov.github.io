strabismus

1. NOUN

/ strəˈbɪzməs / (AmE), / strəˈbɪzməs / (BrE)
A condition in which one or both eyes are not properly aligned and point in different directions.
abnormal eye alignment
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with strabismus after noticing her eyes did not move together.
Children with strabismus often require corrective glasses or even surgery.
Early detection of strabismus can help prevent long-term vision problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squint - a condition where the eyes do not properly align with each other

Etymology

From Late Latin strabismus (squinting), from Greek strabismos.
