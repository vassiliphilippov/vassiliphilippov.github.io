snicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪkə / (BrE)
a quiet, partly suppressed laugh, especially expressing scorn or disrespect
a suppressed, disrespectful laugh

Examples

The teacher heard a snicker from the back of the classroom when he made a mistake.
She tried to ignore the snicker that followed her awkward comment.
A stifled snicker broke the silence as the speaker tripped over his words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snigger - a partly suppressed laugh, especially one expressing disrespect; chiefly British equivalent
  • Degree Lower: giggle - a light, silly laugh, usually out of amusement
  • Degree Higher: laugh - to produce laughter or the sound of laughter (general term)

2. VERB

/ ˈsnɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪkə / (BrE)
to laugh quietly in a partly suppressed way, especially at something secretive or disrespectful
to laugh quietly or slyly

Examples

The students snickered when the teacher made a silly mistake on the board.
He tried not to snicker during the serious meeting, but the joke was too funny.
She snickered quietly at her friend's outrageous outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - to laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way
  • Synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly or inwardly
  • Synonym: snigger - to laugh in a half-suppressed, typically scornful way

Etymology

Perhaps imitative; related to 'snigger', possibly of Scandinavian origin.
