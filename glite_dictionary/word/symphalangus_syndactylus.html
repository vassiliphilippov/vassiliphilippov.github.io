Symphalangus syndactylus

1. NOUN

/ sɪm.fəˈlæŋ.ɡəs sɪnˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (AmE), / sɪm.fəˈlæŋ.ɡəs sɪnˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the siamang, a large black gibbon native to Sumatra, distinguished by partially webbed second and third toes
a large gibbon species (siamang) of Sumatra

Examples

The Symphalangus syndactylus is renowned for its loud, resonant calls heard throughout the Sumatran forests.
Scientists observed the Symphalangus syndactylus using its webbed toes to deftly grasp tree branches.
Conservationists have focused efforts on protecting the habitat of Symphalangus syndactylus from deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siamang - a large black-furred gibbon native to Sumatra and the Malay Peninsula

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Symphalangus' from Greek 'sym' (together) + 'phalangos' (finger/toe), 'syndactylus' from Greek 'syn' (together) + 'daktylos' (finger/toe).
