Ichneumonidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌnuːˈmɒnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɪkˌnjuːˈmɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a large family of parasitic wasps, commonly known as ichneumon flies, whose larvae feed on or in other insects
family of parasitic wasps (ichneumon flies)

Examples

The larvae of Ichneumonidae develop inside the bodies of other insects, eventually killing their hosts.
Many gardeners welcome Ichneumonidae because these wasps help control insect pests naturally.
Scientists have described over 24,000 species within the Ichneumonidae family worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichneumon fly - a parasitic wasp of the family Ichneumonidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ikhneumōn' (tracker) for the type genus Ichneumon.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌnuːməˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪkˌnjuːməˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of parasitic wasps, known as ichneumon wasps or ichneumon flies, many of which lay their eggs in or on the larvae of other insects.
family of parasitic wasps

Examples

Members of the Ichneumonidae play an important role in controlling pest populations.
Many scientists study the Ichneumonidae because of their diverse behaviors and life cycles.
The orchard was home to several species within the Ichneumonidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichneumon wasp - a type of wasp from the family Ichneumonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Ichneumonidae, based on Greek ikhneúmōn (tracker).
