video

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
the broadcasting or recording of visual images of stationary or moving objects, especially for entertainment or information
recording or transmission of visual images

Examples

He prefers to watch documentaries on video rather than live television.
The conference was broadcast live via video to viewers around the world.
She appeared in a video that demonstrated the new product features.

Synset Examples

she is a star of screen and video
Television is a medium because it is neither rare nor well done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: television - transmission of moving images via electronic signals

Etymology

From Latin 'videre' (to see), influenced by the introduction of television and video technology in the 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
the part of a television broadcast that consists of moving visual images, as distinct from the accompanying sound
visual component of a TV broadcast

Examples

The video feed was interrupted during the storm, but we could still hear the audio clearly.
Poor video quality can make it difficult to follow what’s happening on screen.
They adjusted the video settings to fix the brightness on the television.

Synset Examples

they could still receive the sound but the picture was gone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: audio - the part of a broadcast that consists of sound

Etymology

Shortened form of 'videotape' and related to Latin 'videre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06288456-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
A recording of moving visual images, often with accompanying sound, especially of a movie, television program, or other event.
recording of visual images and sound

Examples

I watched a video of the concert last night.
Can you send me the video of the birthday celebration?
She posted a funny video on social media this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a movie; a story or event recorded by a camera
  • Antonym: audio - sound, especially as recorded or transmitted

Etymology

Shortened from 'videotape', from Latin 'videre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04541422-n
Synset POS: n
