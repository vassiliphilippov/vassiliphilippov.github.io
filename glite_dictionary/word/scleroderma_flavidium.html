Scleroderma flavidium

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˈdɜrmə fləˈvɪdiəm / (AmE), / sklɪərəˈdɜːmə fləˈvɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a species of earthball fungus with a smooth upper surface, initially buried in sand, whose fruiting body opens at the top into segments resembling rays.
species of earthball fungus

Examples

The rare Scleroderma flavidium was discovered growing beneath a layer of sand in the nature reserve.
When mature, the fruiting body of Scleroderma flavidium splits open in segments that resemble rays.
Mycologists studied the Scleroderma flavidium to better understand its unique reproduction process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthball - a type of round fungus with a thick skin, related to puffballs

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Scleroderma (from Greek 'skleros' meaning hard and 'derma' meaning skin) and species epithet 'flavidium', from Latin 'flavidus' meaning yellowish.
