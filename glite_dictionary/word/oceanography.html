oceanography

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊʃəˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌəʊʃəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the physical and biological characteristics of the oceans.
study of oceans' physical and biological aspects

Examples

She decided to study oceanography because of her fascination with marine life.
Recent advances in oceanography have improved our understanding of climate change impacts on the oceans.
The university offers a degree program in oceanography for aspiring scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine biology - the branch of biology dealing with organisms living in the sea

Etymology

From ocean + -graphy, modeled after earlier geography.
