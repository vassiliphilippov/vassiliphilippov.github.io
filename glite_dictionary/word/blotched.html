blotched

1. ADJ

/ blɑtʃt / (AmE), / blɒtʃt / (BrE)
marked with irregularly shaped spots or blotches, often of a different color than the background
marked with irregular spots

Examples

The blotched surface of the wall needed to be repainted to hide the stains.
Her hands were blotched with ink after writing all afternoon.
The snake had a blotched pattern running down its back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - marked with spots
  • Synonym: mottled - marked with spots or smears of color
  • Antonym: plain - not marked with any spots or blots

Etymology

From 'blotch' (a spot or stain) + '-ed' suffix forming adjectives.
