disconcertment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskənˈsɜrtmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈsɜːtmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of anxious embarrassment or confusion caused by something unexpected or unsettling.
anxious embarrassment or confusion

Examples

His sudden question caused a visible disconcertment among the council members.
Despite her initial disconcertment, she quickly regained her calm and answered with confidence.
There was a sense of disconcertment in the air after the unexpected announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness
  • Antonym: composure - calmness or self-possession, especially in difficult situations

Etymology

From disconcert + -ment, modeled on French 'déconcerter'.
