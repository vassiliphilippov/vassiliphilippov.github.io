chromatographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkroʊˌmætəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊˌmətəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving chromatography, a technique for separating mixtures into their components
relating to chromatography
medical

Examples

The sample was analyzed using chromatographic techniques to identify its components.
Recent advances in chromatographic methods have greatly improved the accuracy of chemical analysis.
Proper calibration of the chromatographic equipment is essential for reliable results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatography - the process of separating mixtures in a laboratory

Etymology

From 'chromatography', which comes from Greek 'chroma' (color) + '-graphy' (writing), relating to the original technique's use in separating colored substances.
