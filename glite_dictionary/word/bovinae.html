Bovinae

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈvaɪniː / (AmE), / bəʊˈviːniː / (BrE)
A biological subfamily that includes domestic cattle, buffalo, and sometimes related animals such as kudu.
biological group including cattle and buffalo

Examples

The Bovinae subfamily includes both domestic cattle and wild buffalo.
Many large grazing mammals, such as bison and yaks, are classified under Bovinae.
Bovinae are important to human societies for their roles in agriculture and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bos - genus including domestic cattle and related animals

Etymology

From Latin 'bovinus' (of or relating to cattle).

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈvaɪni / (AmE), / bəʊˈviːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of mammals that includes cattle, buffalo, and sometimes antelopes such as kudu; used in zoological classification, though not always technically defined.
subfamily including cattle and buffalo

Examples

The Bovinae subfamily encompasses both domesticated cattle and wild buffalo species.
Scientists are debating whether kudu should be classified within the Bovinae.
The evolutionary history of Bovinae reveals diversification across several continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bos - genus including cattle and related species
  • Synonym: bovine - relating to cattle or cows

Etymology

From Latin 'bos' (ox, cow), with scientific suffix '-inae' for animal subfamilies.
