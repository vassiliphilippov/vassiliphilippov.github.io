Malva moschata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæl.və ˈmɑː.skə.tə / (AmE), / ˈmæl.və ˈmɒs.kə.tə / (BrE)
a perennial plant with musky-scented foliage and white or pink flowers, native to Europe and Asia and known as musk mallow.
a species of musky-scented flowering plant

Examples

The Malva moschata in my garden blooms with delicate pink petals every summer.
Botanists studied the musky aroma emitted by the Malva moschata leaves.
Wild Malva moschata can sometimes be found growing along country roadsides.

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Malva' (mallow) + species 'moschata' (musky), referring to its scent.
