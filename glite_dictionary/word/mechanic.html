mechanic

1. ADJ

/ məˈkænɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪk / (BrE)
(literary) Resembling the action or characteristics of a machine; mechanical.
resembling a machine; mechanical

Examples

His mechanic movements lacked any hint of emotion or spontaneity.
The soldiers advanced in a mechanic fashion, unwavering and precise.
She carried out her daily routine in a mechanic manner, unaffected by her surroundings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanical - relating to or resembling machinery

2. NOUN

/ məˈkænɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪk / (BrE)
A skilled worker who repairs or maintains machinery or engines, especially in vehicles or industrial settings.
person skilled in repairing machinery

Examples

The car wouldn't start, so I called a mechanic to take a look at the engine.
As a mechanic, she specializes in working on large industrial machines.
Every airplane is checked thoroughly by a mechanic before takeoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technician - a person skilled in the technical details of a subject or occupation

Etymology

From Old French *mécanique*, from Latin *mechanicus*, from Greek *mēkhanikos* (engineer, ingenious).

3. NOUN

/ məˈkænɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈkanɪk / (BrE)
a person whose job is to repair and maintain cars and other machinery
a person who repairs cars or machinery

Examples

The mechanic fixed my car's engine within a few hours.
If there's a strange noise coming from your car, you should take it to a mechanic.
As a skilled mechanic, she can diagnose almost any automotive problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technician - a person skilled in the technical details of a subject or occupation

Etymology

From French *mécanique*, from Latin *mechanicus*, from Greek *mēkhanikos* (inventive, ingenious).
