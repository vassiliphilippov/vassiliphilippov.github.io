peahen

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌhɛn / (AmE), / ˈpiːhɛn / (BrE)
the female of the peafowl species, especially of the species Pavo cristatus or Pavo muticus, distinguished from the peacock.
female peafowl

Examples

The peahen built her nest in the tall grass near the edge of the garden.
Unlike the colorful peacock, a peahen has more subdued brown and gray feathers.
At the zoo, we saw a proud peahen carefully watching over her chicks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peacock - the male of the peafowl species, known for its colorful plumage
  • Synonym: peafowl - the species collectively, including both male and female birds

Etymology

Formed from 'pea-' (as in 'peafowl') + 'hen' (female bird), dating to the 17th century.
