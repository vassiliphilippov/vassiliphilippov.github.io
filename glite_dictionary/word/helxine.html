Helxine

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈksaɪni / (AmE), / hɛlˈksaɪni / (BrE)
A genus of dwarf, creeping evergreen herbs forming mats, especially known for species such as soleirolia (mind-your-own-business).
genus of small mat-forming evergreen herbs

Examples

Helxine prefers well-drained soil and does well in shaded garden areas.
Gardeners often use Helxine as ground cover in terrariums and rock gardens.
The lush green carpet formed by Helxine adds texture to any landscape.

Etymology

From Greek helxine, a classical plant name.

2. NOUN

/ hɛlˈsaɪni / (AmE), / hɛlˈsaɪni / (BrE)
A genus of small, trailing, mat-forming evergreen herbs, typically comprising a single species.
genus of mat-forming evergreen herb

Examples

The ground was covered in a thick carpet of Helxine.
Gardeners often use Helxine as an attractive ground cover in shaded spots.
During the tour, the botanist pointed out the tiny leaves of Helxine growing between the stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soleirolia - a synonym genus often used for Helxine in modern classification

Etymology

From Greek helxine, a kind of plant, possibly curly or twisted in growth.
