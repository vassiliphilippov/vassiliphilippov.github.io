Parnassia

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈnæsiə / (AmE), / pɑːˈnæsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of bog herbs native to Arctic and northern temperate regions, known for their star-shaped flowers and wetland habitat.
genus of bog herbs in temperate and Arctic regions

Examples

The wetland was filled with Parnassia blooming among the moss and sedges.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Parnassia genus in northern Alaska.
Gardeners admire the delicate white flowers of Parnassia in water gardens and bogs.

Etymology

From Latin 'Parnassia', named after Mount Parnassus in Greece, where the plant was first described.
