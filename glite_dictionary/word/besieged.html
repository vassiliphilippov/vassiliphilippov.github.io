besieged

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈsidʒd / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒd / (BrE)
Surrounded by armed or hostile forces, often under attack or threat.
surrounded by hostile forces

Examples

The besieged city held out bravely against the enemy army for weeks.
Aid convoys struggled to reach the besieged region due to constant shelling.
The besieged villagers sent out a plea for help as supplies dwindled.

Synset Examples

the besieged town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encircled - closely surrounded on all sides
  • Synonym: attacked - subjected to armed aggression
  • Antonym: defended - protected from attack

Etymology

From Middle English 'besegen', from Old French 'sege' (seat, siege).
