basically

1. ADV

/ ˈbeɪ.sɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.sɪ.kəli / (BrE)
used to emphasize the most important or fundamental aspect of something; in essence or fundamentally.
in essence; fundamentally

Examples

Basically, there are two main reasons for the company's success.
His explanation was basically the same as yours, just with more detail.
It was basically a misunderstanding that led to the whole argument.

Synset Examples

He is basically dishonest
the argument was essentially a technical one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essentially - used to emphasize the basic, central, or fundamental aspect
  • Synonym: fundamentally - in central or primary respects

Etymology

From base + -ic + -ally, modeled on similar adverbs such as 'practically'.
