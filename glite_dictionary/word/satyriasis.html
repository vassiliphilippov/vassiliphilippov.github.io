satyriasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætɪˈraɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsatɪˈraɪəsɪs / (BrE)
Abnormally intense or excessive sexual desire in men, regarded as a medical condition.
excessively strong male sexual desire (medical)
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist diagnosed the patient with satyriasis after several assessments.
His struggle with satyriasis affected his relationships and daily life.
Medical literature describes satyriasis as the male counterpart to nymphomania.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nymphomania - abnormally intense sexual desire in women

Etymology

From Greek 'satyriasos', from 'satyros' (satyr), mythical beings associated with lust.
