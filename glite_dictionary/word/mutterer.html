mutterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌt̬ɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtərə / (BrE)
a person who speaks in a low and indistinct voice, often so as to be difficult to hear or understand
person who speaks indistinctly or softly

Examples

He was known as a mutterer, rarely saying anything clearly enough to be understood.
The mutterer in the back of the room annoyed everyone during the meeting.
She became a mutterer when she was upset, mumbling complaints under her breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutter - to speak in a low, indistinct voice

Etymology

From the verb 'mutter' + agent noun suffix '-er'.
