meekness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiknəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːknəs / (BrE)
the quality of being patient, gentle, and willing to accept criticism or hardship without complaint
the quality of being patient and gentle

Examples

Her meekness was evident when she accepted the criticism without protest.
Despite his meekness, he stood firm in his beliefs when it mattered most.
The teacher admired the student's meekness and patience during the difficult discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogance - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities

Etymology

From Old Norse *mjúkr* (soft), influenced by Middle English *meke* (gentle, humble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmik.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːk.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being quiet, gentle, and unwilling to argue or express strong opinions
quality of being gentle or submissive

Examples

She accepted the criticism with admirable meekness, never once raising her voice.
His meekness was mistaken for weakness by his more aggressive colleagues.
The leader's meekness and patience earned her the respect of the entire team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogance - an attitude of superiority and self-importance

Etymology

From Old Norse *mjúkr* (soft, gentle) via Middle English *meke* (meek).
