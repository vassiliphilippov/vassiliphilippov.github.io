rust

1. NOUN

/ rʌst / (AmE), / rʌst / (BrE)
A plant disease characterized by reddish-brown spots or streaks on leaves and stems, caused by various fungi.
plant disease causing brown leaf spots

Examples

The wheat crop was devastated by a severe outbreak of rust this year.
Farmers regularly inspect their fields for signs of rust on the leaves.
Certain varieties of beans are more resistant to rust than others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mildew - a plant disease caused by fungi resulting in a whitish coating on the leaves
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rʌst / (AmE), / rʌst / (BrE)
a reddish-brown flaky coating formed on iron or steel by oxidation, especially in the presence of moisture
reddish-brown coating on iron or steel

Examples

The old bicycle was covered in rust after being left outside for months.
You should paint the metal to protect it from rust.
A reddish layer of rust had formed on the abandoned tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrosion - gradual destruction of materials (usually metals) by chemical reaction

Etymology

From Old English 'rūst', related to Old High German 'rost' (rust), from Proto-Germanic 'rusta' (redness).

5. NOUN

/ rʌst / (AmE), / rʌst / (BrE)

Examples

Farmers use resistant plant varieties to minimize rust infections.
Scientists study different types of rust to improve agricultural yields.

Related Terms
