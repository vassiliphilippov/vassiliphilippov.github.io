kibbutznik

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈbʊtsnɪk / (AmE), / kɪˈbʊtsnɪk / (BrE)
a member of a kibbutz, an Israeli collective community traditionally based on agriculture
member of a kibbutz (Israeli collective community)

Examples

After living as a kibbutznik for ten years, she decided to move to Tel Aviv.
The young kibbutznik enjoyed working in the fields every morning.
Many kibbutzniks have played important roles in Israel's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kibbutz - an Israeli collective community

Etymology

From Hebrew קִבּוּצְנִיק (kibbutznik), from kibbutz (communal settlement) + -nik (Yiddish/Russian suffix denoting a person associated with).
