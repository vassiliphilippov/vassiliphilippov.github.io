caulking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
A waterproof material used as a filler and sealant in building to make joints watertight.
waterproof filler and sealant

Examples

Before painting, make sure to apply caulking to all the window frames to prevent leaks.
The builder checked the caulking around the bathtub to ensure it was still intact.
Over time, old caulking can crack and should be replaced to keep water out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - a material used to block the passage of fluids through surfaces or joints

Etymology

From the verb 'caulk', from Middle English 'cauken', from Old Northern French 'cauquer' (to press down), ultimately from Latin 'calcāre' (to tread).
