Campanula glomerata

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.jʊ.lə ˌɡloʊ.məˈrɑː.tə / (AmE), / kæmˈpæn.jʊ.lə ˌɡlɒ.məˈrɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of bellflower native to Europe and temperate Asia, known for its dense clusters of violet-blue to white flowers.
a species of bellflower

Examples

The Campanula glomerata is a favorite among gardeners for its striking violet-blue blooms.
We planted Campanula glomerata along the garden border for a vibrant summer display.
Botanists admire the way Campanula glomerata forms dense clusters of flowers on a single stem.

Etymology

From Latin campanula (little bell) and glomerata (clustered).
