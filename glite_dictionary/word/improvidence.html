improvidence

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɑvədəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒvɪdəns / (BrE)
lack of careful planning or foresight, especially in the management of money or resources.
lack of foresight or thrift

Examples

His financial ruin was largely the result of his improvidence throughout his career.
The government blamed the famine on years of administrative improvidence.
It was her improvidence that led to the family’s mounting debts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prudence - careful, wise management of resources
  • Antonym: thrift - using resources wisely and not wastefully
  • Synonym: prodigality - reckless extravagance or wastefulness in spending

Etymology

From Latin improvidentia (lack of foresight), from in- (not) + providentia (foresight).
