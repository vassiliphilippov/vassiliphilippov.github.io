midday

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.deɪ / (BrE)
The middle of the day; the time around noon.
middle of the day (noon)

Examples

We met for lunch at midday in the city square.
By midday, the sun was directly overhead and the park was full of people.
She usually takes a walk every day around midday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noon - 12 o'clock in the day; midday

Etymology

From Middle English 'mydday', from Old English 'middæg', meaning 'middle day'.
