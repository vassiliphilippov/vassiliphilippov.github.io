ohmage

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.mɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.mɪdʒ / (BrE)
the electrical resistance of a conductor expressed in ohms
electrical resistance in ohms

Examples

The ohmage of the wire was measured to ensure it met safety standards.
Due to its high ohmage, the component limited the flow of electricity.
Selecting a lightbulb with the correct ohmage prevents electrical surges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance - opposition to current flow in an electrical circuit

Etymology

From 'ohm' (unit of electrical resistance) + '-age' (forming nouns indicating result or condition).
