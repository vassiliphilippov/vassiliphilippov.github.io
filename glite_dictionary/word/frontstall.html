frontstall

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌnt.stɔl / (AmE), / ˈfrʌnt.stɔːl / (BrE)
A piece of medieval plate armor designed to protect a horse's head in battle.
armor for a horse's head

Examples

The knight's horse wore a heavy frontstall into battle for protection.
Museum exhibits often display an ornate frontstall alongside other pieces of horse armor.
Blacksmiths in the Middle Ages crafted each frontstall to fit the horse perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanfron - a piece of armor to protect a horse's head
