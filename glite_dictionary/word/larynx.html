larynx

1. NOUN

/ ˈlærɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈlarɪŋks / (BrE)
The hollow muscular organ forming an air passage to the lungs and containing the vocal cords in humans and other mammals; also known as the voice box.
voice box; organ with vocal cords
medical

Examples

The doctor examined her larynx to determine the cause of her hoarseness.
Damage to the larynx can affect a person's ability to speak.
He developed an infection in his larynx after catching a severe cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voice box - informal term for the larynx

Etymology

From Greek 'larunx' meaning 'upper windpipe'.
