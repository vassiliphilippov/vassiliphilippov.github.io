Parkia javanica

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrki.ə dʒɑːˈvænɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪə dʒɑːˈvɑːnɪkə / (BrE)
a tall evergreen rainforest tree native to Southeast Asia, with wide-spreading crown and yellow-white flowers, often grown as an ornamental in parks and large gardens
a Southeast Asian ornamental rainforest tree

Examples

The botanical garden features a magnificent Parkia javanica in its tropical section.
Parkia javanica is prized for its wide crown and attractive yellow-white blossoms.
Many parks in Southeast Asia plant Parkia javanica to provide shade and beauty.

Etymology

Genus name 'Parkia' honors Scottish botanist Mungo Park; specific epithet 'javanica' refers to Java, Indonesia.
