linage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the number of lines in a piece of printed material, used especially in publishing and journalism to measure or charge for space used
number of lines in printed material

Examples

The advertisement is priced according to its linage rather than its size.
Increasing the linage of your article may result in higher publication costs.
Editors requested a reduction in linage to save space in the newspaper.

Etymology

From line + -age, modeled after similar formations in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
A rate of payment for written material, calculated according to the number of lines submitted.
payment rate per line of writing

Examples

The newspaper pays its freelance writers by linage instead of a flat fee.
Payment was calculated strictly on linage, so she wrote as concisely as possible.
Some magazines have replaced linage-based compensation with fixed salaries.

Etymology

From 'line' + '-age', indicating a charge related to lines of text.
