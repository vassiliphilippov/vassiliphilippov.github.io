unrest

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈrɛst / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛst / (BrE)
a state of restlessness or agitation, especially caused by worry or dissatisfaction
restless agitation or discontent

Examples

There was a deep sense of unrest in the community after the announcement.
His constant unrest kept him awake through the night, worrying about the future.
Periods of political unrest often coincide with widespread public dissatisfaction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - a state of mental or emotional tranquility
  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance or confusion

Etymology

From un- (not) + rest (rest), first recorded in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ənˈrɛst / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛst / (BrE)
A state in which people are dissatisfied or agitated, often leading to disturbance, disorder, or attempts to bring about change.
state of agitation or disturbance

Examples

The city experienced widespread unrest following the sudden government announcement.
Labor unrest often leads to strikes and disruptions in production.
After weeks of political unrest, the situation finally began to stabilize.

Synset Examples

the political ferment produced new leadership
social unrest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - interruption of a state of peace or quiet
  • Antonym: peace - a state of calm or absence of conflict

Etymology

Formed by prefix 'un-' (not) + 'rest', meaning 'not at rest'.
