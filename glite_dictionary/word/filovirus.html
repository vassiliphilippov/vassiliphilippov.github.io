filovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪloʊˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪləʊˌvaɪrəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of viruses belonging to the Filoviridae family, which includes pathogens such as the Ebola and Marburg viruses known for causing severe hemorrhagic fevers in humans and primates.
family of viruses including Ebola and Marburg
medical

Examples

The outbreak was traced back to a filovirus that spread quickly among villagers.
Researchers are studying how the filovirus infects host cells to improve treatments.
The Marburg virus is a type of filovirus known for causing severe disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ebola virus - a filovirus that causes severe hemorrhagic fever
  • Synonym: Marburg virus - a filovirus causing severe hemorrhagic fever

Etymology

From Latin 'filum' (thread) + 'virus', referring to the filamentous shape of the virus.
