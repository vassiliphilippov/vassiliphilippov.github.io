Wesleyan

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛzliən / (AmE), / ˈwɛzlɪən / (BrE)
A follower or supporter of Wesleyanism, a Protestant Christian movement based on the teachings of John Wesley.
follower of Wesleyanism

Examples

His grandfather was a dedicated Wesleyan who rarely missed church services.
In the 19th century, many Wesleyans were active in social reform movements.
She was raised as a Wesleyan and cherished the denomination's emphasis on holiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodist - a member of a Protestant denomination founded on the teachings of John Wesley

Etymology

From the name of John Wesley (1703–1791), founder of the Methodist movement, plus the suffix -an indicating affiliation.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɛz.li.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɛz.lɪ.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the branch of Protestant Christianity based on the teachings of John Wesley, especially Methodism.
relating to John Wesley's Protestant movement

Examples

The Wesleyan tradition places great emphasis on personal holiness and social justice.
Many churches in the area follow Wesleyan doctrine and practices.
She studied the development of Wesleyan theology in nineteenth-century America.

Synset Examples

Methodist theology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodist - Relating to or characteristic of Methodism or its adherents

Etymology

From the name John Wesley (1703–1791), founder of Methodism, plus the suffix -an.
