graining

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a texture or pattern resembling the grain of wood, often produced artificially to imitate wood surfaces
a wood-like texture or pattern

Examples

The painter used a special technique to give the door a realistic graining.
The wallpaper had a subtle graining that mimicked oak wood.
She admired the intricate graining of the antique table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood grain - the natural pattern or texture in wood

Etymology

From 'grain' (the texture of wood) + -ing (noun-forming suffix).
