Rhinoceros antiquitatis

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌnɑsəˌroʊs ænˌtɪkwɪˈteɪtɪs / (AmE), / raɪˌnɒsərəʊs ænˌtɪkwɪˈteɪtɪs / (BrE)
an extinct species of thick-haired rhinoceros that lived in the Arctic regions during the Pleistocene epoch
extinct woolly rhinoceros species

Examples

Fossils of Rhinoceros antiquitatis have been found across Europe and northern Asia.
The thick fur of Rhinoceros antiquitatis helped it survive the harsh Ice Age climate.
Scientists study Rhinoceros antiquitatis to understand Pleistocene megafauna and their extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly rhinoceros - the common name for Rhinoceros antiquitatis

Etymology

From Latin: 'rhinoceros' (rhinoceros) and 'antiquitatis' (of antiquity, ancient).
