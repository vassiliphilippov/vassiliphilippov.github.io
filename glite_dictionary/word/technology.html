technology

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study and application of knowledge to practical tasks, especially in industry and engineering
application of scientific knowledge; technical discipline

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in technology after graduating from university.
The rapid development of technology has transformed the way we live and work.
Students can choose to specialize in a particular area of technology such as biotechnology or information systems.

Synset Examples

he had trouble deciding which branch of engineering to study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering - the application of science and mathematics to develop solutions for technical problems

Etymology

From Greek tekhnologia (‘systematic treatment’), from tekhnē (‘art, skill’) + -logia (‘study of’).

2. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes, especially in industry; the use of tools, machines, or techniques to control the environment or solve problems
application of scientific knowledge for practical use

Examples

Modern medicine owes much of its progress to advances in technology.
The rapid development of digital technology has changed the way we communicate.
Farmers are adopting new technology to increase crop yields.

Synset Examples

the mastery of fire was a huge advance in human technology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering - the branch of science and technology concerned with the design, building, and use of engines, machines, and structures

Etymology

From Greek 'techne' (art, craft) + 'logia' (study).

3. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes, especially in industry; machinery and equipment developed from engineering or other applied sciences.
machinery and equipment from applied sciences

Examples

Advancements in technology have transformed the way we communicate with each other.
This company invests heavily in the latest technology to stay ahead in the market.
Some people worry that rapid technology adoption may lead to job losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering - the application of scientific and mathematical principles to practical ends
  • Degree Lower: science - the study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world

Etymology

From Greek 'tekhnologia' (systematic treatment of an art or craft), from 'tekhnē' (art, skill) and '-logia' (study).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03707142-n
Synset POS: n
