dollop

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːləp / (AmE), / ˈdɒləp / (BrE)
a small amount of a soft substance, especially one put or served on something
a small amount of a soft substance (esp. food)

Examples

She added a dollop of cream to her coffee before taking a sip.
A generous dollop of jam made the scone even tastier.
The soup was served with a dollop of sour cream on top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonful - an amount held by a spoon
  • Synonym: dab - a small amount, especially of something soft
  • Synonym: smear - an amount smeared on a surface

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from a dialectal English word meaning 'a lump or glob.'
