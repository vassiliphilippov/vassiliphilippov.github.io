indecipherable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪfərəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪfəɹəbl / (BrE)
impossible to read or understand; not able to be deciphered or interpreted.
impossible to understand or read

Examples

The writing on the old document was indecipherable due to water damage.
His mumbling was so low and fast that it became indecipherable to the entire audience.
She received an indecipherable note scribbled hastily on a scrap of paper.

Synset Examples

an indecipherable message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegible - impossible to read, especially due to poor handwriting
  • Antonym: decipherable - able to be deciphered or understood

Etymology

From in- (not) + decipherable, based on the French 'déchiffrer' (to decipher).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪfərəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪf(ə)rəbl̩ / (BrE)
impossible or extremely difficult to read, understand, or interpret.
impossible to understand or read

Examples

The directions he left behind were so indecipherable that no one could follow them.
Her signature was indecipherable on the form, making it hard to verify her identity.
Historians struggled to decode the indecipherable manuscript found in the old library.

Synset Examples

indecipherable handwriting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegible - impossible to read because of poor handwriting or fading
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand, see, or read

Etymology

From in- (not) + decipher + -able; decipher from French décipherer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01408549-s
Synset POS: s
