client

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈklaɪənt / (BrE)
a person or organization that pays for and receives goods or services from a professional or company
person receiving goods or services

Examples

The law firm has many important clients in the banking industry.
As a financial advisor, she meets with new clients every week.
The salon offers special discounts to regular clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services, especially from shops
  • Antonym: provider - a person or organization that supplies goods or services

Etymology

From Latin 'cliens', 'clientis' (follower, dependent, client).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.ənt / (BrE)
a person or organization that seeks and receives professional advice or services, especially from a lawyer
person seeking professional advice, esp. a lawyer's

Examples

The client met with her attorney to discuss the case.
Every client is entitled to confidential legal advice.
The law firm represents several high-profile clients in corporate matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services from a business

Etymology

From Latin 'cliens, clientis' meaning dependent, follower.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈklaɪənt / (BrE)
A computer or program on a network that accesses services provided by a server.
networked computer or program accessing a server

Examples

The software allows the client to request data from the central database.
Each client on the network requires a unique IP address.
The company upgraded all client machines to improve performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: server - a computer or program that provides resources to a client
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03832647-n
Synset POS: n
