saquinavir

1. NOUN

/ səˈkwɪnəˌvɪr / (AmE), / səˈkwɪnəvɪə / (BrE)
A protease inhibitor drug used in the treatment of HIV infection, often under the trade name Invirase.
HIV medication (protease inhibitor)
medical

Examples

Doctors may prescribe saquinavir in combination with other antiretroviral drugs to manage HIV infection.
Adherence to a saquinavir regimen is crucial for its effectiveness against the virus.
Some patients report mild side effects after starting saquinavir therapy.
