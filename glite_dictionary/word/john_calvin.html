John Calvin

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈkælvɪn / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈkælvɪn / (BrE)
(1509–1564) A French-born Swiss theologian whose teachings, especially on predestination and justification by faith, greatly influenced the development of Presbyterianism and the Protestant Reformation.
French-born Swiss theologian and Reformation leader

Examples

Many historians regard John Calvin as a central figure in the Reformation movement.
The doctrines of John Calvin have shaped many Protestant churches around the world.
Students learned about John Calvin and his ideas in their religious history class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Martin Luther - German theologian and a key figure in the Protestant Reformation
