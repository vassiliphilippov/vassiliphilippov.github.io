Sabaoth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæbiˌɔθ, ˈseɪbaʊˌɒθ / (AmE), / ˈsabɪˌɒθ, səˈbeɪɒθ / (BrE)
(in the Bible) Hosts or armies, especially as a title for God meaning 'the Lord of hosts.'
biblical term for hosts or armies

Examples

The hymn praises the Sabaoth, acknowledging God's power over all armies.
In church, the congregation sang about the Lord of Sabaoth.
Biblical references often describe God as the ruler of Sabaoth.

Synset Examples

Lord of Sabaoth

Etymology

From Hebrew ṣəḇāʾōṯ (hosts, armies), plural of ṣābāʾ (host, army).
