pizzicato

1. ADV

/ ˌpɪtsiˈkɑtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɪtsɪˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
in a style of playing stringed instruments by plucking the strings rather than using the bow
played by plucking the strings

Examples

The violinist played the next passage pizzicato, creating a bright and percussive effect.
Please perform this section pizzicato as indicated in the score.
The composer marked the strings to switch from arco to pizzicato for a contrasting sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arco - played with the bow instead of plucked

Etymology

From Italian pizzicato, past participle of pizzicare (to pluck).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɪtsɪˈkɑtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɪtsɪˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(of instruments in the violin family) played by plucking the strings with the finger rather than using the bow.
played by plucking strings (music)

Examples

The passage is marked pizzicato, so the violins should pluck the strings.
She played the entire movement pizzicato for a delicate, percussive sound.
The conductor asked the orchestra to perform the section pizzicato instead of with the bow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arco - (music) played with the bow, as opposed to plucked

Etymology

From Italian pizzicato, past participle of pizzicare (to pluck).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00949134-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtsɪˈkɑtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɪtsɪˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A note or passage played by plucking the strings of a violin or other stringed instrument with the fingers, rather than using the bow.
plucking of stringed instruments

Examples

The violinist played the delicate section pizzicato, creating a light and playful sound.
In the orchestra, the cellos performed a rapid pizzicato passage during the second movement.
The composer indicated that the entire line should be played pizzicato for a unique effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arco - playing stringed instruments with a bow, not plucking

Etymology

From Italian 'pizzicato', past participle of 'pizzicare' (to pluck).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07037891-n
Synset POS: n
