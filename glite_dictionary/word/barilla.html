barilla

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / bəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
a plant, especially of the genus Salsola, formerly burned to produce soda ash or calcium carbonate, especially in North Africa and Spain
plant burned to produce soda ash/calcium carbonate

Examples

Historically, barilla was harvested and burned to obtain valuable soda ash.
The process of extracting calcium carbonate from barilla was vital to ancient glassmaking.
In 19th-century Algeria, fields were cultivated specifically for growing barilla.

Etymology

From Spanish 'barilla', referring to the saltwort plant, used for making soda ash.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / bəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A bushy plant of Old World salt marshes and sea beaches, with prickly leaves, formerly burned to produce crude soda ash for use in glass and soap making.
Old World plant yielding soda ash

Examples

The villagers collected barilla from the salt marshes to use in soap production.
In the eighteenth century, barilla was an important source of soda ash in Europe.
Dried barilla plants were burned to extract chemicals for glassmaking.

Etymology

From Spanish 'barrilla', referring to the soda-yielding plant.
