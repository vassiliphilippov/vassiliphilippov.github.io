Elops

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlɑps / (AmE), / ˈiːlɒps / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Elopidae, commonly known as tenpounders.
genus of fish; tenpounder

Examples

The genus Elops includes several species commonly called tenpounders.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Elops genus in tropical waters.
Fishermen often encounter Elops while casting their nets near the coast.

Etymology

From Greek elops (a kind of fish).
