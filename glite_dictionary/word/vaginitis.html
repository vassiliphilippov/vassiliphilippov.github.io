vaginitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌvædʒɪˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌvædʒɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the vagina, often caused by infection
inflammation of the vagina
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with vaginitis after experiencing discomfort and unusual discharge.
Certain antibiotics may disrupt the balance of vaginal flora, leading to vaginitis.
Proper hygiene can help prevent recurrent episodes of vaginitis.

Etymology

From Latin 'vagina' (sheath, vagina) + '-itis' (indicating inflammation).
