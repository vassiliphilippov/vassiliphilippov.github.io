frijol

1. NOUN

/ friˈhoʊl / (AmE), / friˈhɒl / (BrE)
A common bean plant, especially one with large red kidney-shaped beans, grown for its seeds and used in Latin American cuisine.
a bean plant used in Latin American cooking

Examples

The soup was made with black frijol simmered for hours with spices.
She grew frijol in her garden to use in traditional Mexican dishes.
Dishes like refried frijol are staples in many Latin American households.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - the edible seed of plants of the legume family

Etymology

From Spanish 'frijol' (bean), from Latin 'phaseolus' (bean).
