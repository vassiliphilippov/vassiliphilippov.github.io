myrmidon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.mɪˌdɑːn / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.mɪ.dɒn / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) one of the members of a legendary Thessalian people who accompanied Achilles in the siege of Troy; often used figuratively for a loyal follower, especially one who carries out orders without question.
warrior follower of Achilles in Greek myth

Examples

In Homer’s Iliad, the myrmidons are known for their unwavering loyalty to Achilles.
The ruthless commander was surrounded by his band of myrmidons, ready to carry out his every order.
She compared the manager's assistants to myrmidons, blindly following his every instruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follower - a person who supports and is guided by another

Etymology

From Greek Myrmidōn, Myrmidones, legendary people said to be created from ants (myrmex in Greek).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrmɪˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɜːmɪdɒn / (BrE)
a person who executes orders unquestioningly or pitilessly, especially one who carries out the commands of a powerful person without hesitation
one who unquestioningly follows orders

Examples

The dictator surrounded himself with myrmidons who enforced his every command.
He refused to become another myrmidon blindly following the company’s questionable orders.
Stories of ruthless myrmidons carrying out their leader's will spread fear among the people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minion - a follower or underling of a powerful person
  • Synonym: henchman - a faithful follower or political supporter, especially one prepared to engage in crime or dishonest practices

Etymology

From Greek Myrmidones, legendary people of Thessaly who followed Achilles in the Trojan War.
