larkspur

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːrkˌspɝː / (AmE), / ˈlɑːkˌspɜː / (BrE)
a flowering plant of the genus Delphinium, cultivated for its tall spikes of brightly colored flowers
a type of flowering Delphinium plant

Examples

The larkspur in her garden bloomed in shades of blue and purple during the summer.
He picked a bouquet of larkspur for the wedding ceremony.
Many gardeners choose larkspur for its vibrant flowers and tall, graceful stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delphinium - a plant of the genus Delphinium, with tall spikes of flowers

Etymology

From 'lark' (the bird) and 'spur', referencing the flower's spur-shaped part resembling a lark's claw.
