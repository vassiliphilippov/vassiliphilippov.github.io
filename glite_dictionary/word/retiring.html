retiring

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
shy and reluctant to draw attention to oneself; preferring to be quiet or reserved
shy; not drawing attention

Examples

Although Emma was talented, her retiring nature made her avoid the spotlight.
He has always been a retiring type who prefers to listen rather than talk.
The retiring student sat quietly at the back of the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid about meeting or speaking to people
  • Antonym: outgoing - sociable and enthusiastic about meeting new people

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈtaɪɚɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
Shy and reserved; tending to avoid drawing attention to oneself.
shy and reserved

Examples

Despite her accomplishments, she remained a retiring presence at the office events.
He is a retiring man who rarely speaks in public.
The new student was polite but rather retiring in classroom discussions.

Synset Examples

unassuming to a fault, skeptical about the value of his work
a shy retiring girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unassuming - not pretentious or arrogant; modest
  • Synonym: diffident - lacking self-confidence; modest or shy

Etymology

From English 'retire' (to withdraw) + -ing, indicating a tendency to withdraw from company.

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) about to leave or having recently left a job, position, or office, especially as a result of reaching the usual age for leaving that position.
leaving a position or office

Examples

The retiring president delivered a farewell speech at the company's annual meeting.
They hosted a party in honor of their retiring colleague.
A retiring director will be succeeded by a new appointee next month.

Synset Examples

a retiring member of the board

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incumbent - currently holding a specified office or position
  • Synonym: former - having previously held a certain office or position

Etymology

From the verb 'retire', from French 'retirer' (to withdraw), from re- (again) + tirer (to draw).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
