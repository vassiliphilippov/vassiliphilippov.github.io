freedman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdmən / (AmE), / ˈfriːdmən / (BrE)
A man who has been released from slavery.
a man freed from slavery

Examples

After the Civil War, many freedmen sought to build new lives for themselves in the South.
The law granted full citizenship rights to every former freedman.
A community of freedmen established their own schools and churches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slave - a person who is legally owned by someone else and forced to work for them
  • Synonym: freedwoman - a woman who has been released from slavery

Etymology

Old English 'frēoman' (free man); from 'free' + 'man'.
