egocentrism

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːɡoʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
The state of regarding oneself and one's own interests as the most important or central, often to the exclusion or neglect of others' perspectives or needs.
self-centeredness; focus on oneself

Examples

The child's egocentrism made it hard for her to understand her friend's feelings.
In psychological development, egocentrism often declines as empathy grows.
His decisions were often driven by egocentrism rather than consideration for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfishness - concern exclusively with one's own advantage
  • Antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

From ego ('I' in Latin) + centrism, modeled on French égocentrisme.
