ransomware

1. NOUN

/ ˈrænsəmˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm.weə / (BrE)
malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or data until a sum of money is paid.
malicious software demanding payment

Examples

The hospital's computer network was infected with ransomware, locking staff out of patient records.
A new variant of ransomware is targeting businesses through phishing emails.
After the ransomware attack, the company had to decide whether to pay the hackers or rebuild their systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malware - software intended to damage or disable computers or systems

Etymology

Blend of 'ransom' and 'software'.
