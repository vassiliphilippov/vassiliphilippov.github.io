Oncorhynchus tshawytscha

1. NOUN

/ ɑŋkoʊˈraɪŋkəs tʃəˈwɪtʃə / (AmE), / ɒŋkəˈrɪŋkəs tʃəˈwɪtʃə / (BrE)
a large Pacific salmon, also known as king salmon or Chinook salmon, valued as food; adults die after spawning
species of large Pacific salmon

Examples

The Oncorhynchus tshawytscha is prized by anglers for its impressive size and strength.
After spawning, the Oncorhynchus tshawytscha die, completing their remarkable life cycle.
Fisheries in Alaska heavily depend on the annual migration of Oncorhynchus tshawytscha for commercial harvests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinook salmon - another name for Oncorhynchus tshawytscha, especially in North America
  • Degree Higher: salmon - a general term for fish of the family Salmonidae, including Oncorhynchus species

Etymology

Genus and species names from Greek and Russian origins; 'Oncorhynchus' from Greek 'onkos' (hook) + 'rhynchos' (snout); 'tshawytscha' from a Russian adaptation of the indigenous Kamchatkan name.
