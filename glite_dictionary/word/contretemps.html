contretemps

1. NOUN

/ kɑːntrəˌtɑ̃ː / (AmE), / kɒ̃trəˌtɒ̃ / (BrE)
an unexpected and awkward event, argument, or minor dispute, often causing embarrassment
awkward or unexpected incident

Examples

The meeting was delayed due to a minor contretemps between two members of the board.
Their vacation was nearly ruined by a contretemps with the hotel staff.
After a brief contretemps on the soccer field, the match continued as normal.

Synset Examples

he tried to smooth over his contretemps with the policeman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incident - an event or occurrence, especially one that is unusual or unpleasant
  • Degree Higher: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument

Etymology

From French contretemps (literally 'against time'), meaning 'unexpected event'.
