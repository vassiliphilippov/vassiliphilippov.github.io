tolerant

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑːlɚənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒlərənt / (BrE)
able to withstand adverse environmental conditions or physiological stress without harm
able to endure adverse conditions

Examples

The new crop variety is especially tolerant of drought conditions.
Some bacteria are highly tolerant of acidic environments.
This type of grass is tolerant to heavy foot traffic and poor soil.

Synset Examples

the plant is tolerant of saltwater
these fish are quite tolerant as long as extremes of pH are avoided
the new hybrid is more resistant to drought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistant - able to withstand a specific adverse effect or substance

Etymology

From Latin tolerans, present participle of tolerare (to bear, endure).

WordNet ID: oewn-02444201-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɑːlərənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒlərənt / (BrE)
willing to accept or allow opinions, behaviors, or people that are different from one's own; showing broad-mindedness.
accepting or broad-minded

Examples

Her parents were surprisingly tolerant of her unconventional lifestyle.
A tolerant society values freedom of expression for everyone.
He tried to remain tolerant when listening to opposing viewpoints.

Synset Examples

a broad political stance
generous and broad sympathies
a liberal newspaper
tolerant of his opponent's opinions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intolerant - unwilling to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own
  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced

Etymology

From Latin 'tolerans', present participle of 'tolerare' (to bear, endure).
