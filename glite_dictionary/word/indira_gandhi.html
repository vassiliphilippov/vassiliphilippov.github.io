Indira Gandhi

1. PROPN

/ ɪnˈdɪrə ˈɡændi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪərə ˈɡandi / (BrE)
(1917–1984) Indian politician and daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru who served as Prime Minister of India from 1966 to 1977 and again from 1980 to 1984.
Indian Prime Minister, daughter of Nehru

Examples

Indira Gandhi was the first and, to date, the only female Prime Minister of India.
The emergency declared by Indira Gandhi in 1975 remains a significant event in India's history.
Many political scientists have studied the leadership style of Indira Gandhi.

2. PROPN

/ ɪnˈdɪrə ɡɑnˈdi / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪərə ˈɡandi / (BrE)
(1917–1984) Indian stateswoman; daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru, who served as prime minister of India from 1966 to 1977 and from 1980 until her death in 1984.
prime minister of India, 1966–77, 1980–84

Examples

Indira Gandhi was the first and, to date, only female prime minister of India.
Many historians study the economic reforms initiated during Indira Gandhi's tenure.
The assassination of Indira Gandhi in 1984 was a turning point in Indian history.
