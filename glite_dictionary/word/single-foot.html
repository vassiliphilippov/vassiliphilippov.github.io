single-foot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəlˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡlˌfʊt / (BrE)
A rapid gait of a horse in which each foot strikes the ground separately, making four distinct beats.
horse's rapid four-beat gait

Examples

The Tennessee Walking Horse is known for its smooth single-foot gait.
Some trainers specialize in teaching horses to perform the single-foot.
During the competition, the horse impressed everyone with its fast single-foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rack - a rapid, even, four-beat, lateral gait of a horse

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋɡəlˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡlˌfʊt / (BrE)
(of a horse) to move at a rack, a smooth, four-beat gait faster than a walk.
to move at a rack (horse gait)

Examples

The mare loves to single-foot along the country lane, making for a smooth ride.
During the parade, the show horse single-footed gracefully past the judges' stand.
He trained his Tennessee Walking Horse to single-foot for competitions.

Synset Examples

the horses single-footed

Etymology

From 'single' + 'foot', referring to each foot striking the ground separately in this gait.
