Dibranchia

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / daɪˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
a major group of cephalopods that includes all living forms except the family Nautilidae, comprising octopuses and squids
group of cephalopods excluding nautiluses

Examples

Modern squids and octopuses are classified under the subclass Dibranchia.
Unlike nautiluses, all members of Dibranchia possess two gills.
Cephalopods in the Dibranchia group are known for their advanced nervous systems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cephalopod - marine mollusk group including squids, octopuses, and cuttlefish
  • Antonym: Nautilidae - family of cephalopods containing nautiluses, excluded from Dibranchia

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'di-' (two) + 'branchia' (gills), referring to their two gills.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / daɪˈbræŋkɪə / (BrE)
A subclass of cephalopods comprising all living species except the family Nautilidae, including octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.
subclass of cephalopods (except nautiluses)
medical

Examples

The Dibranchia contain most of the well-known cephalopods, such as octopuses and squids.
Many marine biologists study the locomotion of Dibranchia in order to understand their unique adaptations.
Unlike the nautiluses, members of Dibranchia possess only two gills.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Nautilidae - a family of cephalopods including the nautiluses, excluded from Dibranchia

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' meaning 'two' and 'branchia' meaning 'gills', referring to the two gills possessed by members.
