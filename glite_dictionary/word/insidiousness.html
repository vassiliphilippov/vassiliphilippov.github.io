insidiousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪdi.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪd.i.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being subtly harmful or gradually causing harm, especially in a way that is not immediately obvious
subtle or gradual harmfulness

Examples

The insidiousness of the disease made it difficult to detect in its early stages.
She underestimated the insidiousness of the new policy's impact on small businesses.
Doctors warned of the insidiousness of symptoms that develop without clear warning signs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being dangerous

Etymology

From Latin insidiosus (deceitful, cunning), from insidiae (ambush, plot).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪdiəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪdiəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being subtly harmful or intended to entrap or deceive
quality of being subtly harmful or entrapping

Examples

The insidiousness of the plan made it difficult to detect until it was too late.
Many were alarmed by the insidiousness with which the disease spread through the community.
She underestimated the insidiousness of his schemes and was caught off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviousness - the quality of using clever and indirect methods to achieve something
  • Antonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest

Etymology

From Latin insidiosus (deceitful), from insidiae (ambush).
