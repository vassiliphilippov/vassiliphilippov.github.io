whinny

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪni / (AmE), / ˈwɪni / (BrE)
the characteristic high-pitched sound made by a horse, similar to a gentle neigh
the sound made by a horse

Examples

We heard the whinny of a horse coming from the stable at dawn.
The children's laughter mingled with the whinny of the pony as they played nearby.
A sudden whinny broke the evening silence on the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - the loud, characteristic cry of a horse

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound itself.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪni / (AmE), / ˈwɪni / (BrE)
(of a horse) to make a gentle, high-pitched neighing sound.
(of a horse) make a gentle neighing sound

Examples

The mare began to whinny softly as she saw her foal approach.
Horses often whinny when they are excited or want attention.
As he entered the stable, a few horses whinnied in greeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - to make the loud, characteristic cry of a horse

Etymology

From Middle English 'whinien', likely of imitative origin.
