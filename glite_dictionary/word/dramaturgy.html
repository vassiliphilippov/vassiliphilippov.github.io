dramaturgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræməˌtɝrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːmətɜːdʒi / (BrE)
the art or technique of writing and producing plays, including the theory and practice of dramatic composition
art of writing and producing plays

Examples

She studied dramaturgy at university to better understand the structure of plays.
The festival offers workshops in dramaturgy and stage direction for aspiring playwrights.
His keen interest in dramaturgy led him to write several original scripts for the local theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playwriting - the activity or occupation of writing plays
  • Synonym: theatre - the art or practice of producing and acting in plays

Etymology

From French dramaturgie, from Greek dramatourgia (drama + work).
