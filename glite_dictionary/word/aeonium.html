Aeonium

1. NOUN

/ iˈoʊniəm / (AmE), / iːˈəʊniəm / (BrE)
a genus of succulent plants in the stonecrop family, native to the Canary Islands and North Africa, often grown as ornamental houseplants
a genus of succulent plants

Examples

Many gardeners enjoy growing Aeonium for its striking rosettes and drought tolerance.
The Aeonium in her collection had vibrant green leaves tipped with red.
Some species of Aeonium thrive outdoors in mild climates but must be protected from frost.

Etymology

From Greek aiōnios (ageless, everlasting), referring to the plant's leathery leaves.

2. NOUN

/ iˈoʊ.ni.əm / (AmE), / iːˈəʊ.ni.əm / (BrE)
a genus of succulent plants in the family Crassulaceae, native to the Canary Islands and nearby regions, known for their rosettes of fleshy leaves
genus of succulent plants in Crassulaceae

Examples

The Aeonium she planted thrived in the sunny, dry corner of the garden.
Several varieties within the Aeonium genus display strikingly colorful foliage.
Botanists often study Aeonium to understand how succulents adapt to arid environments.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek aiōnion (‘everlasting’), referring to the plant's hardiness.
