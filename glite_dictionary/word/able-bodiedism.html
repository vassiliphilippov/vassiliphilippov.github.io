able-bodiedism

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.bəlˈbɑː.diˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.bəlˈbɒd.iˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
Discrimination or prejudice in favor of able-bodied people, often resulting in unequal treatment of people with disabilities.
discrimination favoring able-bodied people

Examples

Many activists argue that able-bodiedism is deeply embedded in our social structures.
The policy was criticized for perpetuating able-bodiedism by ignoring the needs of people with disabilities.
Her research focuses on combating able-bodiedism in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ableism - discrimination against people with disabilities

Etymology

From able-bodied + -ism, modeled after terms like racism and sexism.
