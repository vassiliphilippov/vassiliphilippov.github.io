kiam chye

1. NOUN

/ kiˈɑm tʃaɪ / (AmE), / kiˈam tʃaɪ / (BrE)
a type of salted and preserved mustard green or leafy vegetable, used especially in Southeast Asian Chinese cuisine
preserved salted mustard green vegetable

Examples

The chef added kiam chye to the soup for an extra tangy taste.
At the hawker stall, I ordered rice with braised pork and kiam chye.
My grandmother often uses kiam chye when making fish head soup.

Synset Examples

Kiam chye adds a unique flavor to dishes.
We enjoyed a delicious kiam chye for lunch.
They served kiam chye as a side dish at dinner.

Etymology

From Hokkien, literally 'salted vegetable' (kiam meaning 'salty', chye meaning 'vegetable').
