Bromus arvensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.məs ɑːrˈvɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.məs ɑːˈvɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
An annual grass species native to Europe and temperate Asia, often found as a weed in cultivated ground.
annual grass species (Europe/Asia)

Examples

The Bromus arvensis infested large areas of the wheat field, reducing crop yield.
Farmers often struggle to control Bromus arvensis because of its rapid spread and adaptability.
Botanists identified the grassy weed as Bromus arvensis during their survey of invasive plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field brome - common name for Bromus arvensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'bromus' (a kind of oat or brome grass) + 'arvensis' (of the field).
