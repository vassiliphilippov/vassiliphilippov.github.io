half-time

1. ADV

/ ˈhæf taɪm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf taɪm / (BrE)
For less than the standard number of hours, especially relating to employment.
working less than standard hours

Examples

Since becoming a parent, she now works half-time instead of full-time.
The company offers several half-time positions for students.
He switched to half-time hours after his surgery to focus on recovery.

Synset Examples

he works part-time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: part-time - for less than the standard number of hours
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00254209-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæfˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌtaɪm / (BrE)
involving or working for only half the usual period or duration for an activity, especially as a formal arrangement
involving half the usual duration

Examples

She switched to a half-time contract after having her second child.
He was offered a half-time teaching position at the college.
After the injury, she trained on a half-time basis for several months.

Synset Examples

he had two years of half-time training

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'time', denoting partial duration.
