diluvian

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈluːviən / (AmE), / dɪˈluːvɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or connected with a flood, especially a great or biblical deluge.
of or relating to a flood or deluge

Examples

The ancient myths often reference a diluvian catastrophe that reshaped the world.
Geologists have discovered evidence of a diluvian event in this region millions of years ago.
The painting depicted a diluvian scene, with water engulfing the entire landscape.

Etymology

From Latin 'diluvium' (flood, deluge) + English suffix '-an'.
