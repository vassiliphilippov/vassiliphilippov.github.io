slashing

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Striking, forceful, or energetic, especially in a way that cuts sharply or powerfully.
forceful or sharply cutting

Examples

The critic wrote a slashing review of the new play, sparing no one.
He delivered a slashing attack with his rapier during the fencing match.
Her slashing wit made her popular on the comedy circuit.

Synset Examples

his slashing demon-ridden cadenza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - forceful or sharply reducing or wounding

Etymology

Present participle of 'slash', from Middle French esclachier (to break, smash).
