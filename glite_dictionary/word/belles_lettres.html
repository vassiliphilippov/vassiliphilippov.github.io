belles lettres

1. NOUN

/ bɛl ˈlɛtərz / (AmE), / bɛl ˈlɛtəz / (BrE)
literary works, especially essays, poetry, and criticism, that are appreciated for their aesthetic qualities rather than for their informative or practical content.
literary works valued for style and beauty

Examples

He is a devoted student of belles lettres and reads poetry every evening.
The anthology showcases works of belles lettres from the nineteenth century.
She aspires to contribute to the field of belles lettres with her essays and stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literature - written works considered as art, especially novels, plays, and poetry

Etymology

French, literally 'beautiful letters', referring to fine writing.
