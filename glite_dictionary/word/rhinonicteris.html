Rhinonicteris

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌnoʊ.nɪkˈtɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / raɪˌnɒn.ɪkˈtɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of orange horseshoe bats native to Australia, known for their distinctive nose-leaf structure.
genus of orange horseshoe bats

Examples

The Rhinonicteris is the only genus of its kind found in Australia.
Researchers have studied Rhinonicteris to understand bat echolocation abilities.
The caves were inhabited by several colonies of Rhinonicteris bats.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhinos' (nose) + 'nykteris' (bat).

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪnoʊnɪkˈtɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌraɪnənɪkˈtɛrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of bats known as orange horseshoe bats, native to northern Australia.
genus of orange horseshoe bats

Examples

Scientists have studied the Rhinonicteris population to assess the impact of mining in the region.
The Rhinonicteris is distinguished by its orange fur and unique horseshoe-shaped nose.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting habitats critical to the survival of Rhinonicteris bats.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhinos' (nose) + 'niktēros' (of the night), referring to the bat's nose structure and nocturnal habits.
