Shishapangma

1. PROPN

/ ʃiːʃəˈpɑːŋmə / (AmE), / ʃiːʃəˈpæŋmə / (BrE)
a mountain in the Himalayas located in Tibet, reaching a height of 26,290 feet (8,027 meters)
mountain in Tibet, Himalayas

Examples

Climbers from around the world travel to Tibet to ascend Shishapangma, one of the world’s highest peaks.
After months of preparation, the expedition finally reached the summit of Shishapangma.
Shishapangma is considered the easiest of the fourteen 8,000-meter peaks to climb.

Etymology

From Tibetan ཤི་ཤ་པོང་མ་ (Shi sha spong ma), meaning 'crest above the grassy plains'.
