profession

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an open and straightforward declaration of a belief, opinion, or feeling, whether genuine or false
open declaration of belief or opinion

Examples

Her profession of loyalty was met with skepticism by the board.
Despite his profession of innocence, the evidence pointed to his guilt.
He made a public profession of faith during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

a profession of disagreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avowal - an open statement or declaration
  • Antonym: denial - a statement asserting that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin professio (public declaration).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a formal declaration or public affirmation of religious faith or belief
formal declaration of faith

Examples

Her profession of Christianity was met with both admiration and skepticism in her community.
The ceremony included the profession of faith by all new members.
He made a public profession of his beliefs before the congregation.

Synset Examples

a profession of Christianity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - an admission or acknowledgment of faith, or of guilt

Etymology

From Latin professio (public declaration), from profiteri (declare publicly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06697785-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
the people who collectively practice a particular learned occupation, especially as a group distinguished by training and qualifications
group practicing a learned occupation

Examples

After the new regulations were announced, the entire profession had to adjust its practices.
She is highly respected within the legal profession for her expertise and integrity.
The teaching profession has faced many challenges over the last decade.

Synset Examples

the news spread rapidly through the medical profession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupation - job or profession; employment
  • Degree Lower: trade - a skilled job, typically one requiring manual skills
  • Synonym: vocation - a strong feeling of suitability for a particular career or occupation

Etymology

From Latin 'professio' (public declaration), from 'profiteri' (to declare publicly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08129062-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
an occupation that requires specialized education, training, or skill, especially in the arts or sciences
occupation needing special skill or education

Examples

Medicine is considered a noble profession that has a great impact on society.
She entered the teaching profession after earning her degree in education.
Law is a demanding profession that requires years of study and training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupation - a person's usual or principal work or business, especially as a means of earning a living
  • Degree Lower: trade - a skilled job, typically one requiring manual skills and special training

Etymology

From Latin 'professio' (public declaration, occupation), from 'profiteri' (to profess).
