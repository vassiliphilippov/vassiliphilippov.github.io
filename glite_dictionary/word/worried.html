worried

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʌrid / (AmE), / ˈwʌrɪd / (BrE)
anxious or troubled about possible problems or dangers
anxious; feeling concern

Examples

She looked worried when she heard the news about the storm.
Parents often feel worried when their children are late coming home.
I’ve been worried about my exam results all week.

Synset Examples

apprehensive about her job
not used to a city and worried about small things
felt apprehensive about the consequences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not feeling worried or anxious
  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease

Etymology

Past participle of 'worry', from Old English 'wyrgan' (to strangle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɜːr.id / (AmE), / ˈwʌr.id / (BrE)
feeling or showing anxiety or concern about something possible or actual
anxious or concerned

Examples

She looked worried when she heard the news about the accident.
His mother gave him a worried glance as he left for the interview.
I have been worried about the deadline all week.

Synset Examples

too upset to say anything
spent many disquieted moments
distressed about her son's leaving home
lapsed into disturbed sleep
worried parents
a worried frown
one last worried check of the sleeping children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'worry' with the suffix '-ed'. 'Worry' ultimately comes from Old English 'wyrgan' (to strangle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
