archipelago

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrtʃəˈpɛlɪˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃɪˈpelɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group or chain of islands clustered together in a sea or large body of water
group or chain of islands

Examples

Indonesia is the world's largest archipelago, consisting of more than 17,000 islands.
The Aegean Sea is famous for its beautiful archipelago dotted with small, scenic islands.
We spent our vacation exploring each island in the Hawaiian archipelago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: island - a piece of land surrounded by water

Etymology

From Italian 'archipelago', originally referring to the Aegean Sea, from Greek 'arkhipelagos' (arkhi- 'chief' + pelagos 'sea').
