persecution

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɝː.sɪˈkjuː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.sɪˈkjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
Hostile or cruel treatment of someone, especially because of their race, religion, or beliefs.
hostile treatment due to race, religion, etc.

Examples

Many fled their country to escape persecution for their religious beliefs.
Throughout history, the threat of persecution has forced minorities to hide their identities.
The organization supports victims of political persecution worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment
  • Synonym: discrimination - unjust treatment on grounds such as race, age, or sex
  • Antonym: tolerance - acceptance of different views or practices

Etymology

From Latin persecutio (pursuit, following after), from persequi (to pursue).
