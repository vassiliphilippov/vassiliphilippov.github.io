Romaic

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪɪk / (BrE)
the modern vernacular form of the Greek language, especially as distinguished from ancient Greek
modern Greek vernacular

Examples

Many scholars study Romaic to better understand the evolution of the Greek language.
The novel is written in Romaic rather than classical Greek.
He speaks Romaic fluently, having lived in Athens for several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modern Greek - the modern form of the Greek language
  • Antonym: ancient Greek - the ancient form of the Greek language

Etymology

From Modern Greek 'Romaïkós', meaning 'Roman' or 'Byzantine Greek', referring to the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) heritage.

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈmeɪɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪɪk / (BrE)
relating to modern Greece, its people, or its language, especially the modern Greek language as opposed to ancient Greek.
relating to modern Greece or its language

Examples

The poet wrote in Romaic rather than classical Greek to reach a wider audience.
Scholars of Romaic literature study the evolution of the Greek language.
He immersed himself in Romaic culture during his time in Athens.

Etymology

From Modern Greek 'Romaïkós', from 'Romaios' (Roman), referring to Greeks as Romans of the Byzantine Empire.
