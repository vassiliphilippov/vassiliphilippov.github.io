Arminian

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmɪniən / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪniən / (BrE)
A person who adheres to Arminianism, a Christian theological movement emphasizing free will in salvation.
a follower of Arminianism

Examples

John identified as an Arminian and often discussed the emphasis on free will in his church.
The Arminians held a conference to debate theological differences with other groups.
She studied the writings of early Arminian theologians for her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Calvinist - a follower of Calvinism, which contrasts with Arminianism

Etymology

From Jacobus Arminius, a 16th-century Dutch theologian, plus the English suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈmɪniən / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪniən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Arminianism, a Christian theological doctrine emphasizing free will in salvation.
relating to Arminianism (Christian theology)

Examples

The Arminian perspective on predestination differs from the Calvinist view.
Her theological writings reflect a strong Arminian influence.
Many Methodist churches hold to Arminian doctrines regarding salvation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Calvinist - Relating to Calvinism, a contrasting theological doctrine

Etymology

From Jacobus Arminius (Dutch theologian, 1560–1609), whose views Arminianism is based on.
