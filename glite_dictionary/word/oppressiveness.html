oppressiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈprɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈprɛsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being oppressive, especially in a way that causes discomfort or a sense of unjust restriction
quality of being oppressive

Examples

The oppressiveness of the humid summer air made it difficult to concentrate.
Many people fled the country to escape the oppressiveness of the regime.
There was a feeling of oppressiveness in the office after the announcement of new, strict policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from oppression

Etymology

From 'oppressive' + '-ness', where 'oppressive' comes from Latin 'opprimere' (to press against).

2. NOUN

/ əˈprɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈprɛsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being harsh, burdensome, or stifling; causing discomfort or distress by severe or unjust conditions.
quality of being oppressive

Examples

The oppressiveness of the summer heat made it hard to work outside for long.
She spoke about the oppressiveness of the strict rules at her old school.
Many protested against the oppressiveness of the government's policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from oppression

Etymology

From oppress + -ive + -ness, ultimately from Latin opprimere (to press against, overwhelm).
