Algonkin

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkɪn / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkɪn / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Algonkian people, a Native American group of the Ottawa River region in Canada.
Algonquian language of the Algonkian people

Examples

The Algonkin language is taught in some Canadian schools to preserve cultural heritage.
Linguists have documented many traditional stories in Algonkin.
She grew up speaking both English and Algonkin at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquin - another term for the Algonkin language or people
  • Degree Higher: Algonquian - the family of languages that includes Algonkin and related languages

Etymology

From the French 'Algonquin', originally referring to the people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkɪn / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkɪn / (BrE)
a member of a North American Indigenous people living in the Ottawa River valley of Ontario and Quebec.
member of an Indigenous group in Canada

Examples

The Algonkin traditionally lived along the Ottawa River in what is now Canada.
Many Algonkin continue to preserve their language and customs.
An Algonkin elder shared stories passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquin - member of a North American Indigenous group in eastern Canada

Etymology

Variant of 'Algonquin', from French 'Algonquin', probably from Maliseet-Passamaquoddy 'elakómkwik' (they are our relatives/kinsmen).
