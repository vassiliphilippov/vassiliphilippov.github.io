scalene

1. ADJ

/ skeɪˈliːn / (AmE), / skeɪˈliːn / (BrE)
(of a triangle) having all three sides of different lengths.
triangle with three unequal sides

Examples

The mathematician drew a scalene triangle on the board to illustrate his point.
Unlike an equilateral triangle, a scalene triangle has no equal sides or angles.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the area of a scalene triangle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isosceles - triangle with two sides of equal length
  • Antonym: equilateral - triangle with all sides of equal length

Etymology

From Latin *scalenus*, from Greek *skalēnos* (uneven, unequal).

2. ADJ

/ skeɪˈliːn / (AmE), / skeɪˈliːn / (BrE)
Relating to any of the scalene muscles, which are a group of three pairs of muscles in the lateral neck.
relating to the neck scalene muscles
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the scalene nerves during the procedure.
Pain in the neck can sometimes originate from a scalene muscle strain.
Physical therapists often stretch the scalene region to relieve tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scalene muscle - any of three muscles on each side of the neck

Etymology

From Latin 'scalenus', from Greek 'skalenos' meaning uneven or oblique.

Synset POS: a
