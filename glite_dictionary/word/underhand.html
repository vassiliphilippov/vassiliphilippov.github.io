underhand

1. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəhænd / (BrE)
in a manner with the hand swung below shoulder level, typically when throwing or striking
with the hand below shoulder level

Examples

He served the ball underhand to surprise his opponent.
The coach suggested trying an underhand throw for more accuracy.
Softballs are often pitched underhand in amateur leagues.

Synset Examples

throwing a ball underarm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overhand - with the hand swung above shoulder level, especially in throwing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00488998-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌndərˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌhænd / (BrE)
acting or done in a secret or dishonest way; intended to deceive
deceptive or secretive

Examples

They won the contract through underhand dealings that eventually came to light.
Her underhand tactics at work earned her few friends among colleagues.
The politician denied using any underhand methods during the election campaign.

Synset Examples

achieved success in business only by underhand methods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceptive - intended to mislead or trick
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02327024-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəhænd / (BrE)
(of a throw, stroke, or pitch) made with the hand brought forward and up from below shoulder level.
with hand moving upward from below shoulder

Examples

The pitcher delivered an underhand throw to surprise the batter.
In badminton, an underhand serve is often used for greater control.
She used an underhand motion to toss the ball to her teammate.

Synset Examples

an underhand pitch
an underhand stroke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overhand - (of a throw or movement) made with the hand brought forward from above shoulder level
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02480006-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADV

/ ˌʌndɚˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈhænd / (BrE)
in a sly or secret manner, especially to achieve something dishonest or unfair
secretly and dishonestly

Examples

She acted underhand to get the promotion, going behind her colleague's back.
The company won the contract underhand, using insider information.
Politicians sometimes make underhand deals to secure their positions.

Synset Examples

Mean revenge, committed underhand
oldline aristocratic diplomats underhandedly undermined the attempt … to align Germany with the Western democracies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covertly - in a way that is not openly acknowledged or displayed
  • Antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret or hidden

Etymology

From 'underhand', originally referring to a position below the hand; later figuratively used for indirect dealings.
