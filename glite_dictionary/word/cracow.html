Cracow

1. PROPN

/ ˈkraˌkaʊ / (AmE), / ˈkræk.aʊ / (BrE)
an industrial city in southern Poland, situated on the Vistula River; also known as Kraków
a city in southern Poland

Examples

Many tourists visit Cracow to see its famous medieval old town and historic castle.
Cracow has become a bustling cultural center with numerous art galleries and museums.
After moving to Cracow, she quickly fell in love with the city's vibrant atmosphere.

Related Terms

Etymology
