psychometry

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑːmətri / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒmətri / (BrE)
The branch of psychology concerned with the measurement of mental functions and behaviors, especially the measurement of intelligence, aptitude, and personality traits.
psychological measurement science
medical

Examples

Many graduate students in psychology must study psychometry to analyze test data effectively.
Modern advances in psychometry have improved the reliability of personality assessments.
The field of psychometry plays a vital role in educational and clinical testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychometrics - the science of measuring mental capacities and processes

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) + 'metron' (measure).
