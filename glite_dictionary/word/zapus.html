Zapus

1. PROPN

/ ˈzeɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈzæpəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American jumping mice, which are small rodents characterized by long tails and powerful hind legs.
genus of North American jumping mice

Examples

The Zapus genus includes several species of jumping mice found throughout North America.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of Zapus in the wetlands region.
Zapus is distinguished by its exceptionally long hind legs and tail.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Zapus, the genus name given in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzeɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈzæpəs / (BrE)
A genus of small North American jumping mice, the type genus of the family Zapodidae.
genus of North American jumping mice

Examples

The woodland jumping mouse belongs to the Zapus genus, which is found throughout North America.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Zapus genus living in alpine meadows.
The Zapus genus is known for its long hind legs and ability to leap great distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zapus hudsonius - species of jumping mouse in the genus Zapus

Etymology

From New Latin Zapus, genus name coined in zoological taxonomy.
