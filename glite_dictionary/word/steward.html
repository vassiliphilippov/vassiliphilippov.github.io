steward

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːərd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːəd / (BrE)
a union member elected to represent fellow workers in dealings with management, especially concerning workplace rights and conditions
union representative elected by workers

Examples

She became the union steward and helped negotiate better working conditions for her colleagues.
If you have a problem at work, talk to your steward for advice and support.
The steward attended every meeting between employees and management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representative - a person chosen or appointed to act or speak for others

Etymology

From Old English *stiweard*, meaning 'house guardian.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːərd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːəd / (BrE)
a ship's officer responsible for provisions and managing dining arrangements on board
ship officer in charge of catering

Examples

The steward ensured that all passengers had their meals served promptly during the voyage.
We asked the steward about food allergies before booking our cruise dinner.
The ship's steward managed the inventory of provisions for the entire crew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purser - an officer on a ship responsible for handling financial accounts and supplies
  • Antonym: captain - the person in command of a ship

Etymology

From Old English 'stiward', meaning household guardian.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstuːɚd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːəd / (BrE)
a man employed to look after the needs of passengers on an aircraft; a male flight attendant
male attendant on an airplane

Examples

The steward offered me a blanket and a drink during the flight.
After the plane landed, the steward thanked everyone for flying with them.
The steward demonstrated the safety procedures before takeoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight attendant - someone who looks after passengers on an aircraft

Etymology

From Old English *stiweard* (guardian of a house or hall); later extended to service roles.

4. NOUN

/ ˈstuːərd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːəd / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for managing another's property, finances, or other affairs
person managing property or affairs for someone else

Examples

As the estate's steward, she managed the finances and daily operations for the absent landowner.
He acted as a careful steward of his client's investments, ensuring steady growth over the years.
The board appointed a new steward to oversee the foundation's assets and charitable activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person who directs or manages operations
  • Synonym: caretaker - person employed to look after a property
  • Antonym: owner - person who possesses something, opposite of someone managing for another

Etymology

From Old English *stiweard*, meaning 'house guardian,' from *stig* (hall, pen) + *weard* (warden, keeper).

5. NOUN

/ ˈstuːərd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːəd / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for the management of buildings, grounds, or animals, especially for an organization or estate
person managing property or animals

Examples

The steward is responsible for maintaining the old mansion's gardens and stables.
As a steward of the estate, he ensured the animals were cared for properly.
The company hired a steward to oversee the upkeep of the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - person who takes care of a building or property

Etymology

From Old English *stiweard*, meaning 'house guardian', from *stig* (hall, household) + *weard* (ward, guard).
