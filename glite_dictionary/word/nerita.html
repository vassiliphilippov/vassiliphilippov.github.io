Nerita

1. NOUN

/ nəˈraɪtə / (AmE), / nɪəˈraɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of small, marine gastropod mollusks in the family Neritidae, commonly found in tropical and subtropical seas.
genus of marine snails

Examples

Several species within the Nerita genus can be found on rocky tropical shorelines.
The shell of Nerita is recognized by its rounded shape and distinctive coloration.
Marine biologists often study Nerita snails to learn more about tidal adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Neritidae - family of small sea snails known as nerites

Etymology

From Latin 'Nerita', derived from the Greek mythological figure Nereis (sea nymph).
