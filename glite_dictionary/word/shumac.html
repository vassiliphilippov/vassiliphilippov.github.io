shumac

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈʃuːmæk / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus, usually referring to non-poisonous species, known for its compound leaves and clusters of red berries.
non-poisonous Rhus shrub or small tree

Examples

The dried berries of the shumac are sometimes used as a spice in cooking.
Several shumac bushes grew along the edge of the field, adding a splash of color in autumn.
Gardeners often plant shumac for its ornamental foliage and vibrant red fruit clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus, often spelled 'sumac'

Etymology

Variant of 'sumac', from Old French sumac, from Arabic summāq.
