hereditarianism

1. NOUN

/ həˌrɛdəˈtɛr.i.ə.nɪzəm / (AmE), / hɪˌrɛd.ɪˈteə.ri.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
the doctrine that heredity plays a more significant role than environment in determining intellectual and personal development
belief in heredity's primacy over environment

Examples

Some psychologists have long debated the influence of hereditarianism on human intelligence.
The idea of hereditarianism has faced criticism from those who emphasize the power of environment.
Historical arguments for hereditarianism often reflected social and political beliefs of their time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: environmentalism - the belief that environment is more important than heredity in determining development

Etymology

From heredity + -arian + -ism; patterned after the term heredity.
