chaffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃæfi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːfi / (BrE)
abounding in, covered with, or resembling chaff; having the texture or appearance of chaff.
covered with or resembling chaff

Examples

The wheat had a chaffy outer layer that needed to be removed.
Many grass species produce chaffy husks that protect their seeds.
The chaffy residue made cleaning the barn a dusty chore.

Etymology

From chaff + -y (adjective suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02701166-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃæfi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːfi / (BrE)
of little value or importance; worthless
worthless; of little value

Examples

His ideas were dismissed as chaffy nonsense by the rest of the group.
The market was full of chaffy trinkets that no one really wanted to buy.
Critics agreed the novel's plot was chaffy and lacked real substance.

Synset Examples

an empty chaffy book by a foolish chaffy fellow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no real value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - worth a lot of money or important

Etymology

From 'chaff' (the husks of grains, considered worthless) + '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02512484-s
Synset POS: s
