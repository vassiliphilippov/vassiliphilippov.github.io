to construe

1. VERB

/ kənˈstru / (AmE), / kənˈstruː / (BrE)
to interpret or explain the meaning of words, actions, or events; to assign a meaning to something
interpret or assign meaning to something

Examples

The judge will construe the contract in accordance with the law.
Her words could easily be construed as an insult if taken out of context.
He tends to construe every comment as a personal attack.

Synset Examples

What message do you see in this letter?
How do you interpret his behavior?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpret - to explain the meaning of something

Etymology

From Latin *construere* (to build up, arrange).
