Carphophis

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈfoʊfɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈfəʊfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, burrowing snakes native to North America, commonly known as thunder snakes.
genus of North American burrowing snakes

Examples

The Carphophis is often found beneath logs or in loose soil in the eastern United States.
Unlike many larger snakes, the Carphophis prefers underground habitats and feeds on earthworms.
Farmers sometimes encounter the Carphophis while tilling their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunder snake - the common name for snakes in the genus Carphophis

Etymology

From Greek karphos (dry stalk) + ophis (snake).
