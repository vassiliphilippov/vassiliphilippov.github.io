cornmeal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌmil / (AmE), / ˈkɔːn.miːl / (BrE)
meal made by coarsely grinding dried maize (corn), used in cooking and baking.
coarsely ground dried corn used in cooking

Examples

The recipe called for a cup of cornmeal to give the bread its texture.
She dusted the baking sheet with cornmeal before placing the dough on it.
You can use cornmeal to make cornbread, muffins, or coating for fried foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polenta - a dish made from boiled cornmeal
  • Degree Lower: maize flour - finely ground corn, smoother than cornmeal

Etymology

From 'corn' (maize) + 'meal' (ground grain), referring to ground maize.
