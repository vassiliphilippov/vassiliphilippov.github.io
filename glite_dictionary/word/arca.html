Arca

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkə / (AmE), / ˈɑːkə / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve mollusks that includes ark shells and blood clams, belonging to the family Arcidae.
genus of ark shells and blood clams

Examples

The genus Arca is characterized by its robust, box-like shells.
Researchers collected several species from the Arca genus for their marine biology study.
Fossils of Arca have been discovered in rock formations dating back millions of years.

Etymology

From Latin arca (a chest, box), referring to the shell's resemblance to a chest.
