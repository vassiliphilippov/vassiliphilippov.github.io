sower

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who sows seeds, especially by scattering them on the ground
person who sows seeds

Examples

The sower walked across the field, scattering seeds with careful sweeps of his arm.
In the old painting, a sower is depicted at dawn, beginning the day's work.
Each sower played a crucial role in ensuring the village would have a good harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planter - a person or machine that plants seeds
  • Antonym: harvester - a person who gathers crops

Etymology

From Middle English 'sowere', from 'sow' + '-er'.
