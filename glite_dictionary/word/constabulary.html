constabulary

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstæbjələri / (AmE), / kənˈstæbjʊləri / (BrE)
the police force of a particular district, city, or country, especially in Britain or in historical contexts
police force, especially in Britain
british

Examples

The local constabulary launched an investigation into the break-in last night.
She worked for the county constabulary for over a decade before retiring.
The town relied on its small constabulary to keep the peace during busy holidays.

Synset Examples

the law came looking for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police - the civil force of a national or local government

Etymology

From late Latin *constabularius* (officer in charge of a stable), later used for certain officers of state and magistrates.
