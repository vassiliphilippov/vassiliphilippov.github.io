saw

1. NOUN

/ sɔ / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
a hand tool or power tool with a thin blade used for cutting wood or other materials
tool for cutting wood or materials

Examples

He used a saw to cut through the thick wooden beam.
The carpenter kept his electric saw in the workshop for big jobs.
Always wear safety goggles when operating a power saw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handsaw - a saw operated by hand, not powered by electricity
  • Degree Higher: chainsaw - a motorized saw with a chain set with teeth

Etymology

Old English *sagu*, from Proto-Germanic *sagō*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ sɔ / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
a hand tool with a toothed blade used for cutting wood or other materials
hand tool for cutting

Examples

He used a saw to cut the wooden planks for his new bookshelf.
The teeth of the saw became dull after hours of use.
She bought a new saw at the hardware store for her carpentry projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handsaw - a type of saw operated by hand, not powered

Etymology

Old English *sagu*, from Proto-Germanic *sagō*; related to Old Norse *sǫg*, German *Säge*.

3. NOUN

/ sɔ / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
a short, memorable saying or maxim that expresses a widely accepted truth or piece of advice
a proverb or maxim

Examples

There's an old saw that says, 'A stitch in time saves nine.'
My grandmother was fond of quoting the saw 'An apple a day keeps the doctor away.'
The teacher reminded us of the saw that hard work pays off in the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proverb - a short, commonly known saying that expresses a general truth
  • Synonym: maxim - a short, pithy statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct

Etymology

From Old English 'sagu' (speech, saying), related to 'say'.

4. VERB

/ sɔ / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to cut or shape (wood, metal, etc.) using a saw
cut using a saw

Examples

Every weekend, he spends an hour sawing logs for the fireplace.
The carpenter skillfully sawed through the thick wooden beam.
Please saw the board in half before bringing it inside.

Synset Examples

saw wood for the fireplace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to divide something into pieces with a sharp instrument

Etymology

From Old English 'sagu', meaning a cutting tool; verb use dates to Middle English.
