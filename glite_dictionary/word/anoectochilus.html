Anoectochilus

1. NOUN

/ əˌniːk.təˈkaɪ.ləs / (AmE), / əˌniːk.təˈkɪl.əs / (BrE)
A genus of delicate Asiatic orchids with attractive foliage, sometimes called jewel orchids.
genus of delicate Asiatic orchids

Examples

The lush green leaves of Anoectochilus make it a prized houseplant for orchid enthusiasts.
Several species of Anoectochilus are native to Southeast Asia's humid forests.
The delicate flowers of Anoectochilus often attract collectors seeking unique orchids.

Etymology

From Greek 'anoiktos' (open) and 'cheilos' (lip), referring to the orchid's open-lipped flowers.

2. NOUN

/ əˌniktəˈkaɪləs / (AmE), / əˌniːktəˈkʌɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of delicate Asiatic orchids known for their attractive veined leaves and small flowers.
a genus of Asiatic orchids

Examples

Many collectors prize Anoectochilus for its striking, glittering leaf patterns.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Anoectochilus genus in a remote forest in Laos.
The delicate beauty of Anoectochilus makes it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - any plant of the family Orchidaceae, to which Anoectochilus belongs

Etymology

From Greek anoiktos (open) and cheilos (lip), referring to the orchid's open-lipped flower.
