snailflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈsneɪlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a perennial tropical American climbing plant with showy yellow and purple flowers whose corolla forms a coil like a snail shell; sometimes classified in the genus Phaseolus
tropical vine with coiled, showy flowers

Examples

The snailflower climbed the trellis, displaying its spiral-shaped blossoms all summer long.
Gardeners admire the snailflower for both its vibrant colors and unusual coiled petals.
A single snailflower can quickly cover a fence with its lush, twisting vines.

Etymology

Formed from 'snail' and 'flower', referring to the coiled shape of its flower resembling a snail shell.
