dodge

1. NOUN

/ dɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɒdʒ / (BrE)
a quick movement made to avoid someone or something
quick evasive movement

Examples

He made a swift dodge to avoid the ball.
With a sudden dodge, she slipped past the crowd.
The boxer's quick dodge saved him from being hit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidestep - a step taken to one side, especially to avoid something

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from 16th-century English thieves' slang.

Source: WordNet
