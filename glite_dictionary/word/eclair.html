eclair

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈklɛr / (AmE), / eɪˈklɛə / (BrE)
A light, oblong pastry filled with cream and typically topped with chocolate icing.
cream-filled oblong pastry

Examples

She bought a chocolate eclair from the bakery for her afternoon snack.
The cafe is famous for its delicious cream-filled eclairs.
He carefully piped vanilla custard into each eclair before adding the chocolate glaze.

Etymology

From French 'éclair', meaning 'flash of lightning', possibly referring to the pastry's glossy appearance or the speed at which it is eaten.
