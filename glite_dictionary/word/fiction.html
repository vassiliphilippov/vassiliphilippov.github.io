fiction

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
something that is invented or imagined and not true; a false or made-up story or statement.
an invented or false statement

Examples

The politician’s explanation was dismissed as pure fiction by the press.
She accused her brother of spreading fiction about her to their parents.
The alibi he gave turned out to be complete fiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
  • Antonym: truth - a true or actual account, as opposed to something invented

Etymology

From Latin 'fictio' (the act of shaping, a feigning).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
prose writing that describes imaginary events and people, not necessarily based on fact
literature based on imagination; not factual

Examples

Many readers escape into fiction to experience lives and places they've never known.
Historical fiction blends real events with imaginary characters and plots.
Her love of science fiction inspired her to become a writer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonfiction - prose writing that is based on facts and real events
  • Synonym: novel - a long fictional narrative work
  • Degree Higher: fantasy - fiction with magical or supernatural elements

Etymology

From Latin fictio (the act of shaping, a feigning), from fingere (to form, to shape).
