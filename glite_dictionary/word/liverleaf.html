liverleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvərˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəˌliːf / (BrE)
a small perennial plant of the genus Hepatica, with three-lobed leaves and white or pinkish flowers blooming in early spring, found in moist, mossy woods of northern temperate regions
a woodland plant, Hepatica, with three-lobed leaves and spring flowers

Examples

The liverleaf is one of the first wildflowers to bloom in spring woodland.
She spotted a patch of liverleaf nestled among the mossy rocks near the stream.
Botanists admire the liverleaf for its delicate, three-lobed foliage and pastel flowers.

Etymology

Named for the leaf shape's supposed resemblance to a liver, from 'liver' + 'leaf'.
