yesterday

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛstɚˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈjɛstədeɪ / (BrE)
the recent past; a time that has just gone by
the recent past

Examples

Many of the problems we face today have their roots in yesterday.
She often dreams about the lost opportunities of yesterday.
Historians study the events of yesterday to understand our present.

Synset Examples

yesterday's solutions are not good enough
we shared many yesterdays

Etymology

Old English giestran dæg (yesterday), from Germanic roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15287949-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˈjɛstɚˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈjɛstədeɪ / (BrE)
on the day before today; during the day preceding today
on the day before today

Examples

I called her yesterday but she didn't answer the phone.
We finished the project yesterday after working late into the night.
Yesterday, it rained all afternoon, so we stayed indoors.

Synset Examples

yesterday the weather was beautiful

Related Terms

  • Antonym: today - on or during the present day
  • Antonym: tomorrow - on the day after today

Etymology

From Old English 'giestran dæg' (yesterday).

3. NOUN

/ ˈjɛstərˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈjestədeɪ / (BrE)
the day before today
the day before today

Examples

I read about the announcement in yesterday's newspaper.
He only arrived yesterday, but he's already adapting well to the new job.
We finished the project yesterday after working late into the night.

Synset Examples

it was in yesterday's newspapers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: today - the present day; the day when an event occurs
  • Antonym: tomorrow - the day after today

Etymology

Old English 'giestrandæg', from 'gystran' (yesterday) + 'dæg' (day).

4. ADV

/ ˈjɛstərˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈjɛstədeɪ / (BrE)
Used to refer to a time in the recent past; only a short time ago.
recently; in the recent past

Examples

He talked about his trip as if it had happened just yesterday.
The technology they used was cutting-edge only yesterday, but it's already outdated now.
People seem to forget the scandal that broke yesterday, as if it never happened.

Synset Examples

I was not born yesterday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recently - not long ago; in the recent past

Etymology

Old English geostran dæg (yesterday's day); 'yester-' meaning 'of yesterday' and 'day'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
