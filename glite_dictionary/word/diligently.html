diligently

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪlədʒəntli / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒəntli / (BrE)
in a way that shows careful and persistent effort or work
with careful and persistent effort

Examples

She completed her research diligently, reviewing every source thoroughly before drawing conclusions.
He diligently practiced the piano every day to prepare for the concert.
The team members worked diligently to meet the project’s tight deadline.

Synset Examples

we may diligently observe the Lord's supper on the first day of the week, diligently preach the gospel, or minister to the saint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - in a way that is attentive to detail and caution
  • Antonym: lazily - in a way that shows unwillingness to work or use energy

Etymology

From Latin diligenter, from diligens (diligent).
