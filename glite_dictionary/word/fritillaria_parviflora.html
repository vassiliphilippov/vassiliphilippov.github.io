Fritillaria parviflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrɪtəˈlɛriə ˌpɑːrvɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / ˌfrɪtɪˈlɑːriə ˌpɑːvɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of California herb with brownish-purple or greenish bell-shaped flowers, belonging to the lily family.
California herb with bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The rare Fritillaria parviflora blooms in spring along California's grassy hillsides.
Botanists study Fritillaria parviflora for its unique bell-shaped flowers.
Hikers are delighted to spot Fritillaria parviflora during wildflower season.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Fritillaria' referring to the genus and 'parviflora' meaning 'small-flowered'.
