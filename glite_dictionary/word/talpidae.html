Talpidae

1. NOUN

/ tælˈpɪdiː / (AmE), / talˈpɪdɪi / (BrE)
A family of small, burrowing mammals known as moles, characterized by their cylindrical bodies, velvety fur, and spade-like forelimbs.
family of burrowing mammals (moles)

Examples

Many members of Talpidae are found in Europe and North America.
The Talpidae family includes several species adapted for underground life.
Researchers study Talpidae to understand specialized digging adaptations in mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole - small, burrowing mammal with velvety fur

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Talpidae*, from Latin *talpa* (mole) + taxonomic suffix *-idae* (denoting families in zoology).
