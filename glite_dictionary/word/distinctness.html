distinctness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈtɪŋkt.nəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɪŋkt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clearly different or separate from others; the state of being distinct
quality of being different or separate

Examples

The distinctness of her voice made her easy to recognize in a crowd.
Scientists study the distinctness between species to understand evolution.
The distinctness of each flavor is what makes this dish special.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difference - the state of being different or unlike
  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike

Etymology

From Latin 'distinctus', past participle of 'distinguere' (to separate, distinguish), with suffix '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋktnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being sharply defined, clear, or easily distinguishable
clarity or sharpness

Examples

The distinctness of her speech made it easy for everyone to understand.
Through the fog, the distinctness of the mountain's outline gradually faded away.
Good lenses enhance the distinctness of the image on the screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in particular clarity of sound, vision, or thought
  • Antonym: vagueness - the quality of being unclear or not precisely defined

Etymology

From Latin 'distinctus' (separated, distinguished), via Middle English and French.
