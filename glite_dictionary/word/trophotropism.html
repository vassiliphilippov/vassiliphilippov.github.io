trophotropism

1. NOUN

/ ˌtroʊfəˈtroʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtrɒfəˈtrəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
a physiological response in which an organism or part of an organism orients or adjusts itself towards a source of food.
an organism's orienting response to food
medical

Examples

Certain protozoa exhibit trophotropism by moving towards nutrients in their environment.
The plant's roots display trophotropism as they grow in the direction of decomposing organic matter.
Research into trophotropism has revealed complex behavioral patterns in simple organisms.

Etymology

From Greek trophē (nourishment) and tropism (turning).
