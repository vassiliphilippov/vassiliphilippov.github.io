Aeolia

1. PROPN

/ iˈoʊliə / (AmE), / iˈəʊliə / (BrE)
an ancient coastal region of northwestern Asia Minor, including Lesbos, where the Aeolians established cities around 1100 BC
ancient region in northwestern Asia Minor

Examples

The ancient poets often mentioned Aeolia as a homeland of the legendary Aeolian Greeks.
Archaeologists have discovered ruins of several ancient cities in Aeolia.
Lesbos, once part of Aeolia, played a significant role in Greek history.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰολία (Aiolía), named after the Aeolians, a Greek tribe.
