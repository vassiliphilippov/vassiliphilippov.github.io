helmet

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪt / (BrE)
A hard or padded protective covering worn on the head to protect it from injury, typically used in combat, sports, or hazardous activities.
protective head covering

Examples

The motorcyclist always wore a helmet for safety on busy roads.
Ancient soldiers often decorated their helmets with symbols and plumes.
You must put on your helmet before entering the construction area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard hat - protective helmet worn by workers

Etymology

From Old French 'helmet', diminutive of 'helme', from Frankish or Germanic origins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛl.mɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛl.mɪt / (BrE)
A hard or padded protective covering worn on the head to guard against injuries, especially in activities such as riding, biking, construction, or sports.
protective head covering

Examples

All construction workers are required to wear a helmet for safety on site.
The cyclist adjusted his helmet before starting the race.
In medieval times, a knight’s helmet was often decorated with a family crest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard hat - a protective helmet worn on construction sites
  • Antonym: cap - a soft, close-fitting head covering

Etymology

From Old French 'helmet' (diminutive of 'helme', meaning helmet), from Frankish or Germanic origins.

Synset POS: n
