panegyrist

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænəˈdʒɪrɪst / (AmE), / ˌpanɪˈdʒɪərɪst / (BrE)
a person who delivers eulogies or panegyrics, especially in public or formal settings
one who delivers eulogies or panegyrics

Examples

As the chief panegyrist at the ceremony, she spoke eloquently about the mayor's achievements.
The renowned poet was often called upon to serve as a panegyrist at official events.
He gained a reputation as a skilled panegyrist whose praises could move any audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eulogist - a person who gives a eulogy
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproving judgments

Etymology

From Greek *panēgyris* (public assembly) via French *panégyriste* and Latin *panegyrista*.
