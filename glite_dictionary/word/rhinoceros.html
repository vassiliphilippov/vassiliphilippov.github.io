rhinoceros

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈnɑsɚəs / (AmE), / raɪˈnɒsərəs / (BrE)
a large, heavy plant-eating mammal of Asia and Africa, with very thick skin and one or two horns on its nose
large horned mammal of Africa and Asia

Examples

The rhinoceros is endangered due to poaching for its horn.
We saw a massive rhinoceros grazing near the river on our safari.
The thick skin of a rhinoceros protects it from most predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhino - informal, shortened term for rhinoceros

Etymology

From Greek 'rhinokerōs' (from 'rhino-' meaning nose + 'keras' meaning horn) via Latin.
