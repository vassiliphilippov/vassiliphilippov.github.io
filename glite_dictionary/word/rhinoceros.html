Rhinoceros

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈnɑːsərəs / (AmE), / raɪˈnɒsərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, herbivorous, thick-skinned mammals in the family Rhinocerotidae, characterized by one or two horns on the snout; includes species such as the Indian and Javan rhinoceroses.
a genus of large horned mammals

Examples

The Rhinoceros genus includes species that are native to both Asia and Africa.
Scientists are studying the differences between members of the Rhinoceros genus and other rhinos.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered species within the Rhinoceros genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinocerotid - member of the family Rhinocerotidae

Etymology

From Greek 'rhis' (nose) + 'keras' (horn), referring to the animal's horned nose.
