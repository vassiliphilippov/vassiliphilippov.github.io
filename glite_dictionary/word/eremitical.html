eremitical

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛr.əˈmɪ.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈmɪt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a hermit or to lives of seclusion and ascetic solitude.
relating to hermit-like solitude

Examples

She chose to live an eremitical life, far removed from the distractions of society.
The monastery fostered an eremitical spirit among its monks, encouraging long periods of solitude.
His eremitical tendencies grew stronger as he sought peace in the wilderness.

Synset Examples

the eremitic element in the life of a religious colony
his hermitic existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermitic - relating to the life of a hermit
  • Antonym: communal - shared by all members of a group; antonym of solitary or hermit-like

Etymology

From Late Latin eremiticus (of a hermit), from Greek erēmitēs (hermit).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛrəˈmɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɛrəˈmɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, or characteristic of a hermit or hermitic life; resembling or befitting an eremite.
relating to hermits or hermitic life

Examples

She adopted an eremitical lifestyle, spending years in solitude and meditation.
The monastery followed eremitical traditions, emphasizing silence and separation from society.
His eremitical habits made it hard for friends to keep in touch with him.

Synset Examples

eremitic austerities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermitic - relating to or characteristic of a hermit

Etymology

From Late Latin 'eremīticus', from Greek 'erēmitikos', relating to desert or solitary life.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02697687-a
Synset POS: a
