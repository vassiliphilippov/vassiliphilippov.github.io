attempt

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛmpt / (AmE), / əˈtɛmpt / (BrE)
an act of trying to harm or kill someone, especially in the phrase 'an attempt on someone's life'
an act of trying to harm or kill

Examples

The police prevented an attempt on the ambassador's life last night.
He survived an attempt on his life and increased his security.
There was another attempt on the king's life during the parade.

Synset Examples

attacks on women increased last year
they made an attempt on his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place

Etymology

From Old French tenter (to try), from Latin attemptare (to try, attack).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00775311-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ əˈtɛmpt / (AmE), / əˈtɛmpt / (BrE)
to start to do something, especially something difficult or new
try to begin something (esp. difficult)

Examples

She decided to attempt a solo climb of the mountain despite the risks.
The team will attempt to break the world record this year.
I wouldn't attempt such a complex recipe without some help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to make an effort to do something
  • Synonym: to undertake - to begin to do or take responsibility for something
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired result
  • Antonym: to fail - to not achieve the desired result

Etymology

From Old French *atempter*, from Latin *attemptare* or *attentare* (to try, attempt, attack).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01654835-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ əˈtɛmpt / (AmE), / əˈtɛmpt / (BrE)
an act of trying to achieve or accomplish something, typically one that is earnest or determined
act of trying to achieve something

Examples

She made a final attempt to explain her side of the story.
His first attempt at baking bread was surprisingly successful.
Despite several attempts, the team could not unlock the door.

Synset Examples

made an effort to cover all the reading material
wished him luck in his endeavor
she gave it a good try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Synonym: endeavor - a serious and determined effort
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in an attempt

Etymology

From Old French 'atempter', from Latin 'attemptare' (to try, to test).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ əˈtɛmpt / (AmE), / əˈtɛmpt / (BrE)
to make an effort to do or achieve something, especially something difficult
try to do something

Examples

She decided to attempt the challenging hike despite the bad weather forecast.
The company will attempt to launch its new product next month.
He attempted to lift the heavy box, but it was too much for him.

Synset Examples

He tried to shake off his fears
The infant had essayed a few wobbly steps
The police attempted to stop the thief
He sought to improve himself
She always seeks to do good in the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to make an effort to do or accomplish something
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal

Etymology

From Latin 'attemptare' (to try, to urge), from 'ad-' (to) + 'temptare' (to try, to test).
