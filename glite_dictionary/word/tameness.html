tameness

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈteɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being tame, especially as a result of being domesticated
the quality of being tame or domesticated

Examples

The tameness of the fox surprised the biologists who studied its behavior.
Centuries of selective breeding have contributed to the tameness of domestic dogs.
Some people admire the tameness of zoo animals, while others prefer to see wildlife in its natural state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wildness - the quality of being wild or untamed
  • Synonym: docility - the quality of being easily managed or taught

Etymology

From tame + -ness, formed in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈteɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dull, unexciting, or lacking in sophistication or originality
dullness; lack of excitement or originality

Examples

The tameness of the speech made it forgettable for most listeners.
Critics complained about the tameness of the film, expecting something more bold and original.
Despite the big budget, there was a certain tameness in the musical that left audiences underwhelmed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidity - the quality of lacking flavor or interest
  • Antonym: vitality - energy, liveliness, or excitement

Etymology

From tame + -ness, forming a noun of quality.
