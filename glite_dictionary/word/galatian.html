Galatian

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Galatia, an ancient region in central Asia Minor, especially one descended from the Gauls who settled there in the 3rd century BC
a native or inhabitant of ancient Galatia

Examples

The ancient Galatians played an important role in the history of Asia Minor.
Paul's Epistle to the Galatians is part of the New Testament.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts from Galatian settlements dating back to the Hellenistic period.

Etymology

From Latin Galatianus, from Galatia, the name of the ancient region.
