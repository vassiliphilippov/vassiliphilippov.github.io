organophosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrɡənoʊˈfɑsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənəʊˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a compound, especially used as an insecticide, that disrupts the nervous system of insects by inhibiting certain enzymes
type of insecticidal compound
medical

Examples

Many agricultural crops are treated with organophosphate insecticides to protect them from pests.
Exposure to organophosphates can be dangerous to human health if not handled correctly.
The use of organophosphate pesticides has declined due to concerns about environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbamate - another type of insecticide affecting the nervous system

Etymology

From 'organo-' (organic chemical compounds) and 'phosphate' (a salt or ester of phosphoric acid).
