velvety

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛlvəti / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvəti / (BrE)
smooth and soft in appearance, sound, touch, or taste, like velvet
smooth and soft, like velvet

Examples

The chocolate mousse had a velvety texture that melted in my mouth.
She spoke in a velvety voice that was soothing to everyone who listened.
The petals of the flower felt velvety beneath my fingertips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky - having a smooth, fine texture or quality, like silk
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface or quality

Etymology

From 'velvet' + '-y' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛl.və.ti / (AmE), / ˈvɛl.vɪ.ti / (BrE)
smooth and soft to the touch or appearance, like velvet
smooth and soft like velvet

Examples

She loved the velvety texture of the rose petals.
The chocolate mousse had a velvety smoothness that melted in your mouth.
His velvety voice made the song sound especially soothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface

Etymology

From 'velvet' + '-y', meaning 'like velvet'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01158709-s
