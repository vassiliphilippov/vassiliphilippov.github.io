Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkoʊlaɪ ˈrɪmski kɔːrˈsɑːkɒf / (AmE), / nɪˈkɒlaɪ ˈrɪmski kɔːˈsɑːkəf / (BrE)
(1844–1908) A Russian composer noted for operas and orchestral works, often drawing on folk music themes.
Russian composer (1844–1908)

Examples

The vibrant orchestration in Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov's 'Scheherazade' dazzled audiences around the world.
Many students study Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov to understand Russian classical music traditions.
The museum featured a special exhibit on Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov and his contributions to music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pyotr Tchaikovsky - Russian composer famous for ballets and symphonies (1840–1893)
  • Synonym: Modest Mussorgsky - Russian composer known for works inspired by folk themes (1839–1881)
