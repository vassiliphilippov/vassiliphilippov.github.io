perry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɛri / (BrE)
A fermented alcoholic beverage made from the juice of pears, often sparkling and similar to cider.
pear-based alcoholic drink

Examples

They enjoyed a cold glass of perry on the patio during the summer evening.
English orchards are famous for producing high-quality perry and cider.
The menu featured several local perries alongside traditional ales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cider - an alcoholic drink made from fermented apple juice

Etymology

From Old English 'perrie', from Latin 'pirum' (pear).
