credibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being believable or trustworthy.
quality of being credible

Examples

The credibleness of his testimony was questioned by the jury.
A news source loses its credibleness when it spreads misinformation.
She worked hard to build the credibleness of her organization in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credibility - quality of being trusted and believed in

Etymology

From Latin 'credibilis' (worthy of belief) + English suffix '-ness'.
