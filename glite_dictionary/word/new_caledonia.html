New Caledonia

1. PROPN

/ nu ˌkælɪˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / njuː ˌkælɪˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
a large island and French overseas territory in the southwest Pacific Ocean, east of Australia and north of New Zealand
French territory island in the southwest Pacific

Examples

The capital of New Caledonia is Nouméa, a vibrant port city.
Many tourists visit New Caledonia for its impressive coral reefs and beaches.
New Caledonia is known for its unique blend of French and Melanesian culture.

Etymology

Named by Captain James Cook, who thought the island resembled Scotland (Latin 'Caledonia') in appearance.
