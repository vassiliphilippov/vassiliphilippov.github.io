inconstancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑnstənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒnstənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being likely to change frequently, especially in one's feelings, opinions, or loyalty
changeability; tendency to change often

Examples

His inconstancy in relationships made it difficult for anyone to trust him.
The inconstancy of the weather in April can be frustrating for gardeners.
She was criticized for her political inconstancy during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fickleness - the quality of being likely to change frequently, especially regarding affections or loyalties
  • Antonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or unwavering

Etymology

From Latin inconstantia, from in- (not) + constantia (steadiness).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑnstənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒnstənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being unreliable, changeable, or unfaithful, especially in allegiance or affection
unreliability; lack of constancy or faithfulness

Examples

Her inconstancy in friendships made it difficult for anyone to trust her for long.
The politician's inconstancy on key issues frustrated his supporters.
He was hurt by the inconstancy of her affection, which seemed to change with the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fickleness - the quality of changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties
  • Antonym: constancy - the quality of being faithful and dependable

Etymology

From Latin inconstantia, from in- (not) + constantia (steadfastness).
