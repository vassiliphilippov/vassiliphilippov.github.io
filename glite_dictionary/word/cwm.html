cwm

1. NOUN

/ kuːm / (AmE), / kuːm / (BrE)
A steep-walled, semicircular basin in a mountain, often formed by glacial erosion and sometimes containing a lake.
steep mountain basin; glacial cirque

Examples

The climbers set up camp in a sheltered cwm beneath the summit.
A small lake had formed in the glacial cwm over thousands of years.
We admired the wildflowers dotting the grassy floor of the cwm as we hiked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cirque - a bowl-shaped, steep-walled mountain basin formed by glaciation

Etymology

From Welsh 'cwm' (valley).
