Freemason

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌmeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌmeɪsən / (BrE)
a member of an international fraternal order (Freemasonry) with secret rituals and a tradition of mutual assistance
member of a secret fraternal order

Examples

He discovered that his grandfather had been a prominent Freemason in the 1920s.
The Freemason attended a meeting at the lodge every month.
Some believe that being a Freemason opens doors to powerful networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freemasonry - the system and institutions of the Freemasons

Etymology

Originally from 'free' + 'mason', referring to skilled stonemasons free to travel to work; later applied to a fraternal order dating from the 17th century.
