Flindersia

1. NOUN

/ flɪnˈdɜrziə / (AmE), / flɪnˈdɜːziə / (BrE)
A small genus of Australian timber trees in the family Rutaceae, noted for their valuable wood.
genus of Australian timber trees

Examples

Several species within the Flindersia genus are prized for their durable hardwood.
The rainforest reserve is home to multiple Flindersia trees essential to local biodiversity.
Carpenters value Flindersia timber for making furniture and flooring.

Etymology

Named after Matthew Flinders, English navigator and cartographer.
