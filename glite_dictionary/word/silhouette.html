silhouette

1. VERB

/ ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (BrE)
to represent or depict as a dark shape or outline against a lighter background
depict as dark outline against light

Examples

The tree was silhouetted against the evening sky.
Photographers often silhouette their subjects to create dramatic effects.
A bird was silhouetted in the window as the sun set behind it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - show the outer shape of something

Etymology

From French 'silhouette', named after Étienne de Silhouette, 18th-century French financier.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪləˈwɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪluˈɛt / (BrE)
the dark shape or outline of someone or something visible against a lighter background, especially in dim light
dark outline or shape against a light background

Examples

The silhouette of the mountain was visible against the sunrise.
She recognized him only by his silhouette in the doorway.
The artist drew a silhouette of a dancing couple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - a line or shape that shows the edge of an object

Etymology

From French 'silhouette', after Étienne de Silhouette (18th-century French finance minister), whose name became associated with outline profiles.

3. VERB

/ ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (BrE)
to project or represent the dark outline or shape of something against a lighter background
project as an outline against a background

Examples

The tree was silhouetted against the orange glow of the sunset.
A lone figure was silhouetted by the headlights as he crossed the road.
Lightning briefly silhouetted the mountains on the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - draw or depict the outer edge or shape of something

Etymology

From French 'silhouette', named after Étienne de Silhouette (1709–1767), a French finance minister, whose name became associated with outline portraiture.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02142756-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪl.uˈet / (BrE)
a drawing or image of the outline of an object, filled in with a uniform color, typically black
outline drawing filled in with one color

Examples

The artist created a dramatic silhouette of the mountain against the sunset.
She traced her friend's silhouette onto the wall for an art project.
A bird's silhouette could be seen perched on the power line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - a line or shape that shows the outer edge of something

Etymology

From French 'silhouette', named after Étienne de Silhouette (1709–1767), a French finance minister known for austerity, as such portraits were inexpensive.
