supply

1. VERB

/ səˈplaɪ / (AmE), / səˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to say or add something as a further remark; to state in addition
add or state as a further remark

Examples

"It's already late," he supplied quietly, ending the discussion.
She looked confused, so he quickly supplied the missing details.
"That won't be necessary," she supplied with a gentle smile.

Synset Examples

‘It doesn't matter,’ he supplied

2. NOUN

/ səˈplaɪ / (AmE), / səˈplaɪ / (BrE)
the act of providing goods or services that are available to people, particularly for sale
provision of goods or services

Examples

The supply of fresh vegetables has increased this year due to better harvests.
When supply exceeds demand, prices often fall.
They struggled to maintain a steady supply of medical equipment during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demand - the desire for particular goods or services
  • Synonym: provision - the action of providing or supplying something for use

Etymology

From Old French 'souplir', based on Latin 'supplementum' (filling up).
