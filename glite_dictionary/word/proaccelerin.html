proaccelerin

1. NOUN

/ proʊ.əkˈsɛl ər ɪn / (AmE), / prəʊ.ækˈsɛl ər ɪn / (BrE)
a coagulation factor in blood, also known as Factor V, important for the blood-clotting process
a blood coagulation factor (Factor V)
medical

Examples

A deficiency in proaccelerin can lead to excessive bleeding after injury.
The laboratory measured her proaccelerin levels to assess her blood's clotting ability.
Researchers are studying how mutations in proaccelerin affect the risk of thrombosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor V - another name for proaccelerin, a blood coagulation factor

Etymology

From Latin 'pro-' (before) and 'accelero' (to hasten), reflecting its role in accelerating clotting.
