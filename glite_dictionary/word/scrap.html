scrap

1. NOUN

/ skræp / (AmE), / skræp / (BrE)
a small piece of something that remains after the rest has been used or removed
small leftover piece

Examples

She wrote her phone number on a scrap of paper and handed it to me.
There wasn't a scrap of food left after the picnic.
He used every scrap of material to finish the project.

Synset Examples

she jotted it on a scrap of paper
there was not a scrap left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or detached
  • Synonym: shred - a thin strip or small amount of something cut or torn off
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04157733-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ skræp / (AmE), / skræp / (BrE)
to dispose of or discard something because it is no longer useful or wanted
dispose of something as useless or old

Examples

We decided to scrap the old washing machine and buy a new one.
The government plans to scrap outdated regulations.
After several failed attempts, the engineers finally scrapped the whole project.

Synset Examples

trash these old chairs
junk an old car
scrap your old computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer useful or wanted
  • Synonym: junk - to throw away something as worthless
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'scrappe' (small piece), from Old Norse 'skrap' (scraps).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
