holograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɒləˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A handwritten book or document, especially one written entirely by the person whose signature it bears.
handwritten document or book

Examples

The will was a holograph that the deceased had written and signed himself.
She donated her father's holograph manuscript to the university library.
Legal disputes often arise over the authenticity of a holograph document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autograph - a manuscript or document written by its author

Etymology

From Greek holographos, from holos (whole) + graphein (to write).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɒləˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a photographic record containing information for reproducing a three-dimensional image by holography
photographic record for holography

Examples

The scientist carefully developed the holograph before reconstructing the 3D image.
A holograph captures both the intensity and the phase of the reflected light.
After exposure, the holograph was illuminated with laser light to reveal the subject in three dimensions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hologram - a three-dimensional image formed by the interference of light beams from a laser or other coherent light source

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + -graph (something written or recorded).
