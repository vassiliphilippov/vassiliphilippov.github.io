Dirca

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɜːrkə / (AmE), / ˈdɜːkə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs native to North America, commonly known as leatherwood, with pliable, tough branches and small yellow flowers.
genus of North American leatherwood shrubs

Examples

The pliable branches of Dirca make it easily distinguishable in the wild.
Botanists studied the distribution of Dirca across eastern North America.
The bark of Dirca has traditionally been used for making cords due to its toughness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherwood - common name for shrubs of the genus Dirca

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Dirca, named after Dirce, a spring in Thebes; used in taxonomic classification.
