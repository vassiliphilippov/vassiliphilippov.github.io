grownup

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊnʌp / (BrE)
an adult; a fully developed person who is no longer a child
adult; fully developed person

Examples

Only one grownup was present at the children's party.
Sometimes, being a grownup means making difficult decisions.
He never felt like a true grownup even after he turned thirty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adult - a fully developed person who is legally of age
  • Antonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty

Etymology

From 'grown' (past participle of 'grow') + 'up', originally forming the phrase 'grown up' before becoming a single word.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡroʊn.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊn.ʌp / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) fully developed; adult.
fully developed; adult (of animal/person)

Examples

The lion is now a grownup male and leads his own pride.
After several years with the family, the puppy is finally a grownup dog.
Caterpillars turn into grownup butterflies after their transformation.

Synset Examples

an adult animal
a grown woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adult - (of an animal or person) fully grown or mature

Etymology

From 'grown' (past participle of 'grow') + 'up', indicating something that has completed growth.
