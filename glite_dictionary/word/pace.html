pace

1. NOUN

/ peɪs / (AmE), / peɪs / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 3 feet (91.44 centimeters), originally based on the average length of a stride
unit of length, 3 feet, stride

Examples

The old Roman road was measured in paces rather than in modern meters.
He calculated the distance by counting his paces from the gate to the river.
A single pace equals exactly 3 feet in traditional English units.

Etymology

From Latin passus (step, stride), used as a unit of length in Roman measurement.

/ peɪs / (AmE), / peɪs / (BrE)

Examples
