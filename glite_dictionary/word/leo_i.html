Leo I

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːoʊ wʌn / (AmE), / ˈliːəʊ wʌn / (BrE)
(c. 400–461) Pope from 440 to 461, also known as Leo the Great, who expanded papal authority and famously persuaded Attila the Hun not to attack Rome.
Pope from 440–461, 'Leo the Great'

Examples

Leo I is remembered for negotiating with Attila the Hun and preventing the sack of Rome.
Many historians regard Leo I as one of the most influential early popes.
The feast day of Leo I is celebrated on November 10th in the Catholic Church.

Etymology

From Latin 'Leo', meaning 'lion'; used as papal name.
