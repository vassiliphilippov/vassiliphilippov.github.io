Voltairianism

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˌtɛr.iˈæn.ɪzəm / (AmE), / vɒlˌtɛər.iˈæn.ɪzəm / (BrE)
The set of beliefs or practices characterized by skepticism, deistic religious views, opposition to intolerance, and criticism of bigotry, as associated with Voltaire.
beliefs and practices of Voltaire

Examples

The spirit of Voltairianism inspired reforms against religious intolerance in the 18th century.
Her essay embraced Voltairianism, emphasizing rational thought and freedom of expression.
Some critics argue that Voltairianism remains relevant in debates on secularism and free speech.

Synset Examples

Declarations of pan-Voltairianism will prove no panacea, but rather simply capture an aspirational bonhomie reflected in ‘unity’ marches that, however impressive, conceal seeds of future violent explosions given a still elusive and unfinished accounting of the broader equities at play.

Etymology

From the name Voltaire (French Enlightenment philosopher) + -ianism.
