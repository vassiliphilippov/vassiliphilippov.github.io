newcomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuˌkʌmər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌkʌmə / (BrE)
a person who has recently arrived in a place or joined a group
recent arrival; new member

Examples

As a newcomer to the company, Maria quickly adapted to her team.
It's never easy being a newcomer in a close-knit community.
The city offers special programs to help newcomers adjust to life here.

Synset Examples

he's a newcomer to Boston

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginner - a person new to a subject or activity
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a place or activity

Etymology

From 'new' + 'comer', recorded since Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuˌkʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌkʌmə / (BrE)
a person who has recently arrived in a place, joined a group, or started to participate in an activity
person who is new to a place or group

Examples

As a newcomer to the city, she was eager to make new friends.
At first, the newcomer felt nervous joining the weekly book club.
The company organizes an orientation session for every newcomer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veteran - a person with experience in a particular activity or field
  • Synonym: beginner - a person who has just started learning or doing something

Etymology

Derived from 'new' + 'comer', meaning someone who has newly come or arrived.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10375148-n
Synset POS: n
