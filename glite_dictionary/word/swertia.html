Swertia

1. PROPN

/ swɜːrtiə / (AmE), / swɪəˈtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants found in mountainous regions of North America, Eurasia, and Africa, known for their bitter compounds and sometimes used in traditional medicine.
genus of mountain herbs

Examples

Several species of Swertia are valued in traditional medicine for their bitter principles.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Swertia in the Himalayan region.
The striking flowers of Swertia make it a favorite among alpine plant collectors.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Swertia,' named after the 18th-century Dutch botanist Emanuel Swert.

2. NOUN

/ swɜrʃə / (AmE), / swɜːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs native to mountainous regions of North America, Eurasia, and Africa, within the gentian family.
genus of mountain herbs (plant)

Examples

Several species in the Swertia genus are valued for their medicinal uses.
Botanists discovered a new population of Swertia in the Alps.
The beautiful blue flowers of Swertia often stand out in their mountain habitats.

Etymology

From the Latinized form of a plant name, ultimately from Swedish botanist Emanuel Sweertz (1557–1612).
