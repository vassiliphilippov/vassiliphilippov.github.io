injustice

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
an act or instance of unfair treatment or a violation of someone's rights; an unjust act
an unjust act or instance

Examples

Many people protested against the injustice they had suffered.
The lawyer fought tirelessly to correct the injustice done to her client.
Each injustice that goes unpunished undermines trust in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfairness - lack of justice or fairness
  • Antonym: justice - fairness; the quality of being just

Etymology

From Latin 'iniustitia', from 'in-' (not) + 'iustitia' (justice).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
the practice or state of being unjust; lack of fairness or justice
lack of fairness; unjust conduct

Examples

Many people protested against the injustice they witnessed in the legal system.
She dedicated her life to fighting social injustice around the world.
He could not tolerate even the slightest injustice against his friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justice - just or fair behavior; the opposite of injustice
  • Synonym: inequity - lack of fairness or equality
  • Synonym: unfairness - the quality of being unfair

Etymology

From Old French injustice, from Latin injustitia (wrongfulness, injustice), from in- (not) + justitia (justice).
