FNMA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf ˌɛn ˌɛm ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛf ˌɛn ˌɛm ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A federally chartered corporation in the United States that purchases and guarantees mortgages, also known as Fannie Mae.
U.S. corporation that buys mortgages

Examples

The FNMA plays a crucial role in the U.S. housing finance system.
Many mortgages are bought and securitized by the FNMA.
Investors closely monitor the financial performance of FNMA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fannie Mae - Informal name for the Federal National Mortgage Association

Etymology

Acronym for Federal National Mortgage Association (founded 1938, U.S.).
