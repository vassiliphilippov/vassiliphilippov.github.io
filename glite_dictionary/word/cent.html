cent

1. NOUN

/ sɛnt / (AmE), / sɛnt / (BrE)
a coin or unit of currency worth one-hundredth of the basic unit in many monetary systems, such as the US dollar or the euro
coin or unit worth one-hundredth of a base currency

Examples

She handed the cashier a cent to pay for the candy.
In the United States, a cent is often called a penny.
Not a cent was left in his wallet after the shopping spree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penny - a coin worth one cent in the US and Canadian currency

Etymology

From Latin 'centum' meaning 'hundred'.

2. NOUN

/ sɛnt / (AmE), / sɛnt / (BrE)
a unit of money that is equal to one hundredth of the basic unit in the currencies of many countries, such as the US dollar or the euro.
one hundredth of a basic currency unit

Examples

He handed the cashier a fifty-cent coin for his purchase.
A cent is worth one hundredth of a US dollar.
Many countries use the cent as a subdivision of their main currency unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penny - a coin worth one cent in US and some other currencies

Etymology

From Latin centum (hundred), via French cent (hundred).
