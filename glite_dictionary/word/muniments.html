muniments

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.nə.mənts / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.nɪ.mənts / (BrE)
documents, such as deeds, proving ownership or title, especially to land or property
documents evidencing title to property

Examples

The old chest was filled with muniments that traced the estate's history back centuries.
Lawyers reviewed the muniments to establish clear title to the ancestral lands.
Without the original muniments, it was difficult to prove ownership of the property.

Etymology

From Latin 'munimentum' (defence, protection), from 'munire' (to fortify).
