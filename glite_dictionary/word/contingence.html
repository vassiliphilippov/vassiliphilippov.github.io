contingence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪndʒəns / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒəns / (BrE)
A possible future event or occurrence; something that may happen by chance.
possible event or occurrence

Examples

The plan was created with every contingence in mind, ensuring no surprises would arise.
She explained that such a contingence was unlikely but not impossible.
In legal contracts, a contingence clause prepares for unexpected circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contingency - a possible event that may or may not occur

Etymology

From Latin 'contingentia', from 'contingere' (to touch, befall).
