botulismotoxin

1. NOUN

/ boʊˌtjuːlɪzmoʊˈtɒksɪn / (AmE), / bəʊˌtjuːlɪzməʊˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a highly potent toxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum, responsible for causing botulism and sometimes used in medical or bioweapon contexts
toxin causing botulism
medical

Examples

Botulismotoxin is considered one of the most poisonous biological substances known to science.
The laboratory studied how botulismotoxin affects nerve function in mammals.
Accidental exposure to botulismotoxin can lead to severe and sometimes fatal illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botulinum toxin - the neurotoxin produced by Clostridium botulinum, also causes botulism

Etymology

From 'botulism' + 'toxin', referring to the toxin causing the disease botulism.
