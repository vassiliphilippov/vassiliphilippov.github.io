disinclination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪn.klɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪn.klɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A lack of willingness or desire to do something; a reluctance.
unwillingness; reluctance

Examples

Despite her disinclination to join the group, she eventually agreed to participate.
He felt a strong disinclination to confront his friend about the issue.
There was a noticeable disinclination among employees to work overtime during the holidays.

Synset Examples

a reluctance to commit himself
his hesitancy revealed his basic indisposition
after some hesitation he agreed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctance - unwillingness or resistance to do something
  • Antonym: inclination - a tendency or willingness to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'inclination' (leaning, tendency).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪŋklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A lack of willingness or enthusiasm to do something; a feeling of reluctance or aversion.
lack of willingness; reluctance

Examples

She spoke with a clear disinclination to discuss her private life in detail.
Despite his disinclination for exercise, he agreed to join the hiking trip.
There was a noticeable disinclination among the staff to work overtime.

Synset Examples

his disinclination for modesty is well known

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctance - Unwillingness or hesitation to do something
  • Antonym: inclination - A tendency or preference toward something

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + inclinationem (a leaning), through French désincliner.
