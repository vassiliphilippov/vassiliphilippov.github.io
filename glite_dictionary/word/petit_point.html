petit point

1. NOUN

/ pəˈti pwæ̃ / (AmE), / pəˈti pwɛ̃ / (BrE)
a type of fine needlepoint embroidery made with small diagonal stitches, typically used to create detailed decorative designs
a fine needlepoint stitch

Examples

Her grandmother taught her to do petit point on delicate silk canvases.
The purse was embroidered in exquisite petit point, showcasing tiny, precise stitches.
Victorian chairs often feature upholstery decorated with petit point needlework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlepoint - embroidery stitched on canvas with yarn or thread

Etymology

From French 'petit point', literally 'small stitch'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈti pwæ̃ / (AmE), / pəˈti pwæ̃ / (BrE)
a type of needlepoint embroidery made with very small, close stitches, often used for detailed designs
needlepoint with small, fine stitches

Examples

The antique chair was upholstered with beautiful petit point showing detailed floral patterns.
She learned the art of petit point from her grandmother, who cherished fine embroidery.
The museum's textile collection includes several 18th-century petit point samplers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlepoint - embroidery worked on canvas with tapestry stitches
  • Degree Lower: gros point - needlepoint embroidery with large stitches

Etymology

From French 'petit point' (small stitch, fine point).
