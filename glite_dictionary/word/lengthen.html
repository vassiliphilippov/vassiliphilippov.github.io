to lengthen

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛŋθən / (AmE), / ˈleŋθən / (BrE)
to make something longer; to increase the length of something
make longer; increase length

Examples

The tailor offered to lengthen the pants for a better fit.
During winter, the nights gradually lengthen and the days shorten.
If you lengthen the straps, the bag will hang lower.

Synset Examples

Lengthen this skirt, please

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to shorten - to make something shorter; to reduce the length of something

Etymology

Formed from 'length' (from Old English 'lengðu', length) + '-en' (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00318501-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈlɛŋθən / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθən / (BrE)
to become or make longer in distance or duration
become or make longer

Examples

The days gradually lengthen as spring turns into summer.
He asked the tailor to lengthen his trousers by two inches.
Her speech seemed to lengthen with each new topic she introduced.

Synset Examples

In Spring, the days lengthen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to shorten - to become or make shorter

Etymology

From length + -en, where -en forms verbs meaning 'to become' or 'to make'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00317432-v
Synset POS: v
