sooth

1. NOUN

/ suːθ / (AmE), / suːθ / (BrE)
truth; reality (now archaic, chiefly literary or poetic use)
archaic: truth or reality
archaic

Examples

In sooth, the king's words held more weight than gold.
She spoke in sooth, never hiding her true feelings from her friends.
The knight answered, 'In sooth, I have seen stranger things.'

Synset Examples

in sooth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

Old English sōth (truth); related to Old Norse sanna (to affirm, to prove), Gothic sunja (truth).
