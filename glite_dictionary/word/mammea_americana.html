Mammea americana

1. NOUN

/ mæˈmiː əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / mæˈmiː əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree with edible fruit that has a leathery rind, commonly known as mammee apple.
tropical American fruit tree

Examples

The fruit of the Mammea americana is prized for its sweet, aromatic flesh.
In some Caribbean countries, people use Mammea americana in jams and desserts.
The Mammea americana tree thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, where 'Mammea' is a genus name, and 'americana' refers to its American origin.
