brooklime

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʊkˌlaɪm / (AmE), / ˈbrʊkˌlaɪm / (BrE)
A low-growing European plant (Veronica beccabunga) with succulent stems and blue flowers, sometimes found in North America.
European aquatic plant with blue flowers

Examples

The brooklime thrives along the edges of slow-moving streams and ponds.
A patch of brooklime added a splash of blue to the marshy bank.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate brooklime for its attractive flowers and creeping habit.

Etymology

From 'brook' (a small stream) and 'lime' (an old term for plants of the Veronica genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrʊkˌlaɪm / (AmE), / ˈbrʊkˌlaɪm / (BrE)
a low-spreading plant found in wet places, with fleshy leaves and small blue or violet flowers, native to parts of Europe, western North America, and northeastern Asia
spreading wetland plant with blue or violet flowers

Examples

We spotted a patch of brooklime growing beside the stream during our hike.
The delicate flowers of brooklime add a touch of color to many wetlands in spring.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate brooklime as a ground cover in damp, shady spots.

Etymology

From Middle English 'brook' (a small stream) + 'lime', referring to moist habitat.
