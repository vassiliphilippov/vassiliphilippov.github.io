Ptilocercus

1. PROPN

/ taɪloʊˈsɜrkəs / (AmE), / tɪləˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small nocturnal mammals known as pentails, found in Southeast Asia.
genus of small nocturnal mammals (pentails)

Examples

Ptilocercus is notable for its unique feather-like tail, which is rare among mammals.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Ptilocercus in the dense forests of Borneo.
The nocturnal habits of Ptilocercus make it a challenge to observe in the wild.

Etymology

From Greek *ptilon* (feather) + *kerkos* (tail), referring to the animal's feather-like tail.
