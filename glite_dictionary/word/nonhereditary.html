nonhereditary

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.həˈrɛdəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.həˈrɛd.ɪ.təri / (BrE)
(of a trait, disease, or condition) not passed on from parents to offspring by inheritance.
not passed on by inheritance

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the disease was nonhereditary, so it would not be passed to her children.
Many cancers are nonhereditary and occur due to environmental or lifestyle factors.
The researchers studied both hereditary and nonhereditary cases to understand the causes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hereditary - (of a trait or condition) able to be passed from parent to offspring by inheritance

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'hereditary' (from Latin hereditarius, from hereditas 'inheritance').
