Thelephoraceae

1. NOUN

/ θɛlɪfəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / θɛlɪfəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by leathery or membranous fruiting bodies (sporophores)
family of fungi with leathery or membranous fruiting bodies
medical

Examples

Several members of the Thelephoraceae family are found in temperate forests.
Researchers are studying the ecological roles of Thelephoraceae in nutrient cycling.
The leathery texture of Thelephoraceae fruiting bodies makes them distinct from other fungi.

Etymology

From the genus name Thelephora, from Greek 'thelephoros' (bearing nipples), and the taxonomic suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ θɛlɪˌfɔrəˈsiː / (AmE), / θɛlɪˌfɒrəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by leathery or membranous fruiting bodies (sporophores)
family of fungi with leathery or membranous fruiting bodies

Examples

Many members of the Thelephoraceae produce tough fruiting bodies that can persist for months in the forest.
The Thelephoraceae play an important role in decomposing organic matter in woodland ecosystems.
Researchers identified a new species belonging to the Thelephoraceae family during the mycological survey.

Etymology

From the genus Thelephora (Greek thele, nipple + phora, bearing) + Latin family suffix -aceae.
