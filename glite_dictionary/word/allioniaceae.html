Allioniaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌliːniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / əˌliːniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Caryophyllales, recognized in some botanical classification systems.
family of flowering plants (Caryophyllales)
medical

Examples

The Allioniaceae are distinguished by certain unique floral structures among the Caryophyllales.
Several rare species belong to the Allioniaceae, attracting interest from botanists worldwide.
Recent genetic studies have led to debates over the proper classification of Allioniaceae.

Etymology

Named for Italian botanist Carlo Allioni (1728–1804), with the standard botanical suffix -aceae.
