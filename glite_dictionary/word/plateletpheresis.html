plateletpheresis

1. NOUN

/ pleɪt.lət.fəˈriː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌplætlət.fəˈriː.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which platelets are separated from whole blood and the remaining components are returned to the donor
medical procedure to extract platelets from blood
medical

Examples

The hospital conducts plateletpheresis to collect platelets for patients with blood disorders.
After undergoing plateletpheresis, the donor felt perfectly fine and resumed normal activities.
Modern blood banks use automated machines for efficient plateletpheresis.

Etymology

From 'platelet' + Greek 'aphairesis' (a removal or taking away), used in medical terminology.
