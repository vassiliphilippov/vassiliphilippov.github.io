buffalo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌf.əˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌf.ə.ləʊ / (BrE)
a large, shaggy-haired, brown wild animal native to the plains of North America, also known as the bison
large North American wild bison

Examples

We saw a herd of buffalo grazing on the open plains.
The buffalo nearly became extinct in the nineteenth century due to overhunting.
A buffalo uses its massive head to push snow aside in the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - large wild animal also called American buffalo

Etymology

From Portuguese or Spanish 'búfalo', from Latin 'bubalus', from Greek 'boubalos' (wild ox).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
meat from an American bison, used as food.
meat from an American bison

Examples

The restaurant serves buffalo burgers that are leaner than regular beef burgers.
Some people prefer the taste of buffalo to that of traditional beef.
Farmers raise buffalo for their nutrient-rich meat and hides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - large North American mammal also known as buffalo
  • Degree Lower: beef - meat from cattle
  • Synonym: venison - meat from a deer

Etymology

From Italian 'bufalo', from Latin 'bubalus', from Greek 'boubalos' (buffalo, antelope).

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
to intimidate or overawe someone, especially by a display of force or aggressive behavior
to intimidate or overawe

Examples

The manager tried to buffalo his employees into working overtime.
Don't let them buffalo you with technical jargon you don't understand.
She buffaloed her way past the guards with an air of authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, making them lose confidence
  • Synonym: bully - to frighten or hurt a weaker person; to use one's strength or power to harm or intimidate

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'buffalo', possibly alluding to the intimidating behavior of the animal.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
a large wild animal of the cattle family with horns and a heavy body, found in parts of Africa and Asia, such as the water buffalo and Cape buffalo.
large wild ox-like animal of Africa and Asia

Examples

A herd of buffalo grazed near the riverbank at sunrise.
The water buffalo is often used as a draft animal in Southeast Asia.
Unlike the American bison, the African Cape buffalo is much more aggressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - a large, shaggy-haired wild ox, native to North America and Europe

Etymology

From Portuguese 'búfalo', from Latin 'bufalus', from Greek 'boubalos' (buffalo, antelope).
