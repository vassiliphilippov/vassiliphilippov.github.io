Macrotis

1. PROPN

/ məˈkroʊtɪs / (AmE), / məˈkrəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, burrowing marsupials known as bilbies, native to Australia.
genus of marsupials (bilbies)

Examples

The genus Macrotis includes the greater bilby, a marsupial native to arid regions of Australia.
Scientists are studying the habitat requirements of Macrotis populations in the wild.
Due to habitat loss, Macrotis species have become increasingly rare in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilby - common name for species in the genus Macrotis

Etymology

From Greek makrós (long) + ous, ot- (ear), referring to their large ears.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.rəˈtaɪs / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəʊˈtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials in the family Peramelidae, commonly known as bilbies, native to Australia.
genus of marsupial mammals (bilbies)

Examples

The Macrotis genus includes the iconic greater bilby of Australia.
Conservation efforts for Macrotis are ongoing due to habitat loss.
Researchers studied the nocturnal habits of Macrotis in the arid regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilby - common name for species in the genus Macrotis

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) and otis (ear), referencing the animal's large ears.
