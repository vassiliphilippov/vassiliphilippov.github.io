metascience

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
the scientific study of science, including its methods, practices, and reproducibility
study of science itself

Examples

The recent surge in metascience aims to improve research practices across all disciplines.
She published a groundbreaking study in metascience that analyzed peer review processes.
Funding for metascience has increased as concerns about reproducibility have grown.

Synset Examples

The driving force behind this battery of calls, commentaries, infrastructure, and metascience is a perceived crisis in the state of replication research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scientometrics - the quantitative study of science, communication in science, and science policy

Etymology

From meta- (beyond, about) + science, modelled after similar constructs like 'metaphysics'.
