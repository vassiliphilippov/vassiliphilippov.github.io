supernatural

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈnætʃ.rəl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈnætʃ.rəl / (BrE)
the realm of forces, events, or beings that are believed to exist beyond or outside the laws of nature
forces or phenomena beyond nature

Examples

Many horror movies feature the supernatural as a central theme.
She has always been fascinated by stories involving the supernatural.
The locals believed the old mansion was touched by the supernatural.

Synset Examples

She doesn't believe in the supernatural

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranormal - phenomena beyond the scope of normal scientific understanding

Etymology

From Medieval Latin supernaturalis, from Latin super- (above) + natura (nature).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈnætʃ.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈnætʃ.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a phenomenon, event, or being) not able to be explained by natural laws or science; attributed to a force beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature.
beyond natural laws; unexplainable by science

Examples

Many people believe that supernatural forces are responsible for unexplained events.
The story is filled with supernatural creatures and mysterious powers.
She claimed to have experienced a supernatural vision that changed her life.

Synset Examples

supernatural forces and occurrences and beings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranormal - denoting phenomena beyond normal scientific explanation
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humankind

Etymology

From Medieval Latin supernaturalis, from Latin super- (above) + natura (nature).
