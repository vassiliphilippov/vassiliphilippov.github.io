scrivener

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪvənər / (AmE), / ˈskrɪvənə / (BrE)
a person employed to write out documents or manuscripts by hand, especially before printing was common
person who copies documents by hand
archaic

Examples

The wealthy merchant hired a scrivener to prepare all his business contracts.
In medieval times, a scrivener was essential for keeping legal and financial records.
The story revolves around a young scrivener who works in a bustling city office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribe - a person who copies out documents, especially in the past

Etymology

From Middle English scriveyn, from Anglo-French escrivein, from Latin scriba (scribe, clerk).
