medlar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of southern Africa that bears edible fruit.
tree with edible fruit, native to southern Africa

Examples

The medlar tree produces unusual fruit that are enjoyed in some African cuisines.
They harvested ripe medlars from the small grove near the river.
The garden features a variety of fruit trees, including a rare medlar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loquat - evergreen Asian tree with edible yellow fruit, sometimes confused with medlar

Etymology

From Old French 'medle', from Latin 'mespilum', referring to the fruit and tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə / (BrE)
a South African globular fruit with brown, leathery skin and pithy flesh that has a sweet-acid taste
South African fruit with leathery skin

Examples

The ripe medlar is often eaten fresh, straight from the tree.
At the market, he bought a basket of medlars to make jam.
The leathery skin of a medlar protects its sweet and tangy pulp.

Etymology

From Latin *mespilum*, from Greek *mespilon*, originally referring to a similar fruit.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of Eurasia, cultivated for its edible fruit that resembles a crab apple.
Eurasian tree, fruit like a crab apple

Examples

The old orchard still had a single medlar tree bearing fruit in autumn.
She made a delicious jelly from ripe medlars gathered in October.
Early botanists often wrote about the distinctive taste of the medlar fruit.

Etymology

From Old French medler, from Latin mespilum (medlar tree).

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɛd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə / (BrE)
A small brownish fruit that resembles a crabapple and is often used in preserves, or the tree that bears this fruit.
fruit similar to a crabapple, used in preserves

Examples

The ripe medlar has a unique, tangy flavor ideal for making jellies and preserves.
After the first frost, we gathered medlars from the old orchard for dessert.
She planted a medlar tree in her garden for its unusual blossoms and fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabapple - small sour apple-like fruit

Etymology

From Old French 'mesle', from Latin 'mespilum' (medlar tree).
