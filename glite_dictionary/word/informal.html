informal

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɔrml̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːml̩ / (BrE)
relaxed or casual, not following strict rules or formal procedures; suitable for ordinary, everyday situations.
casual, not formal

Examples

The dinner was informal, so everyone wore jeans and T-shirts.
Her informal tone helped put the audience at ease.
They arranged an informal meeting to discuss the project.

Synset Examples

conservative people unaccustomed to informal dress
an informal free-and-easy manner
an informal gathering of friends

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formal - strict, following established rules or conventions

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'formalis' (formal).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɔrməl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːməl / (BrE)
(of language, style, or behavior) relaxed or unofficial; not following established or formal conventions.
not formal; relaxed style or language

Examples

Her email was written in a very informal style, using everyday language.
The meeting was informal, so there was no need for suits or ties.
He prefers informal conversations to official meetings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formal - following established rules, conventions, or ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'informalis' (without form; formless).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01049410-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɔrməl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːməl / (BrE)
not officially recognized, organized, or controlled; relaxed in structure or formality
not officially recognized or controlled

Examples

The two companies reached an informal agreement over lunch.
Their informal group meets every Saturday at the park.
She prefers to keep her meetings informal and open to discussion.

Synset Examples

an informal agreement
a loose organization of the local farmers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formal - following established conventions or officially recognized rules

Etymology

From Latin 'informalis' (formless, shapeless), from 'in-' (not) + 'formalis' (formal).

Synset POS: s
