fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a state of intense nervous excitement or agitation
state of nervous excitement

Examples

She waited in a fever of anticipation for the phone to ring.
The city was in a fever as election results began to pour in.
His discovery sent the team into a fever of activity.

Synset Examples

in a fever of resentment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

From Latin febris (fever).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An abnormally high body temperature, often a sign of illness or infection.
abnormally high body temperature

Examples

She developed a high fever and chills overnight.
Taking medication helped reduce his fever.
A fever can be a sign that your body is fighting an infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrexia - medical term for fever
  • Antonym: normal temperature - average healthy body temperature

Etymology

From Latin 'febris' (fever).
