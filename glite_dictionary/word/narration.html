narration

1. NOUN

/ nəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / nəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of telling a story or describing a series of events, especially in writing, drama, or media
the act of telling a story or describing events

Examples

The documentary used a voice-over narration to explain each historical event.
Her vivid narration brought the story to life for all the listeners.
Without clear narration, the movie was difficult to follow.

Synset Examples

his narrative was interesting
Disney's stories entertain adults as well as children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrative - a spoken or written account of connected events; a story
  • Synonym: description - a spoken or written account that gives details about something

Etymology

From Latin narratio (a relating, recounting).

2. NOUN

/ nəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / nəˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(in rhetoric) the part of a speech following the introduction, in which the facts of the case are presented.
rhetorical section presenting facts

Examples

In classical rhetoric, the narration lays out the background necessary for the argument.
Following the introduction, the speaker moved smoothly into the narration of the case.
The lawyer's narration was clear and concise, detailing all relevant facts.

Etymology

From Latin narratio (a relating or recounting).

3. NOUN

/ nəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / nəˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of telling a story or giving an account of events.
the act of telling a story

Examples

The author's narration kept the audience engaged throughout the entire book.
Her clear and concise narration made the instructions easy to follow.
The film was accompanied by a voice-over narration explaining the history of the city.

Synset Examples

his narration was hesitant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrative - a spoken or written account of connected events
  • Synonym: description - a spoken or written representation or account of a person, object, or event

Etymology

From Latin narratio (a relating, narrative), from narrare (to relate, tell).
