Scorpius

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɔːr.pi.əs / (AmE), / ˈskɔː.pi.əs / (BrE)
a large constellation of the zodiac, situated between Libra and Sagittarius, representing a scorpion
constellation between Libra and Sagittarius

Examples

The constellation Scorpius is easily identified by its bright red star, Antares.
Many cultures have stories about Scorpius and its place in the night sky.
Scorpius is most visible in the southern hemisphere during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scorpio - astrological sign corresponding to Scorpius

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'the scorpion', from Latin 'scorpius'.
