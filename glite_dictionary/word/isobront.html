isobront

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsoʊˌbrɒnt / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌbrɒnt / (BrE)
A line on a map connecting locations where a particular phase of thunderstorm activity occurred at the same time.
line showing simultaneous thunderstorm activity

Examples

Meteorologists studied the isobront to analyze the spread of the thunderstorm across the region.
The isobront on the weather map indicated where the lightning strikes occurred at the same moment.
Each isobront helps forecasters track the movement of thunderstorm cells in real time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isotherm - a line on a map connecting points of equal temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'brontē' (thunder).
