to unclog

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈklɑɡ / (AmE), / ʌnˈklɒɡ / (BrE)
to remove or cause to remove an obstruction from something so that it becomes clear or free-flowing
remove an obstruction; make clear

Examples

We tried everything to unclog the kitchen sink, but nothing worked until we called a plumber.
The powerful drain cleaner quickly unclogged the bathroom pipes.
Pouring hot water down the gutter helped unclog it after the heavy rain.

Synset Examples

The chemical that we poured down the drain unclogged it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clog - to block something so that movement is difficult or impossible
  • Synonym: clear - to remove something that is blocking; to make free of obstruction

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (meaning not) and 'clog' (to block).
