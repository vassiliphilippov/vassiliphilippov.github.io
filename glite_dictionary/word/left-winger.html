left-winger

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛftˈwɪŋər / (AmE), / ˌlɛftˈwɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who supports or belongs to the political left, typically advocating progressive or socialist policies
supporter of left-wing politics

Examples

Many left-wingers supported the new environmental legislation.
He has been called a left-winger because of his views on healthcare and education reform.
The debate included both left-wingers and right-wingers voicing their opinions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-winger - a person with right-leaning or conservative political views
  • Synonym: progressive - a person who advocates for reform and progressive policies

Etymology

Formed from 'left-wing' (referring to the progressive side of the political spectrum) + '-er' (denoting a person)
