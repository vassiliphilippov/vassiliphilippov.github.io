baklava

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːk.ləˈvɑː / (AmE), / ˌbæk.ləˈvɑː / (BrE)
a rich Middle Eastern dessert made of thin layers of flaky pastry filled with nuts and sweetened with honey or syrup
Middle Eastern pastry dessert with nuts and honey

Examples

My favorite dessert at the Mediterranean restaurant is the baklava.
She prepared a tray of homemade baklava for the family gathering.
The bakery offers several types of baklava filled with pistachios or walnuts.

Etymology

From Turkish 'baklava', itself of unknown origin but possibly from Arabic or Mongolian roots.
