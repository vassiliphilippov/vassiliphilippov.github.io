caribe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrib.eɪ / (AmE), / kəˈriː.beɪ / (BrE)
A small, fiercely carnivorous freshwater fish from South America, known for attacking and consuming living animals.
South American carnivorous freshwater fish

Examples

Fishermen avoid swimming where the caribe are known to be abundant.
The river is home to several dangerous species, including the infamous caribe.
Locals warned us about the sharp-toothed caribe that inhabit these waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piranha - A small South American freshwater fish known for its sharp teeth and aggression

Etymology

From Spanish 'caribe', referring to the fish, possibly linked to the Carib people.
