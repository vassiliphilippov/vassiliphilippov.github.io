Zapotecan

1. NOUN

/ ˌzæpəˈtikən / (AmE), / ˌzapəˈtiːkən / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous people of southern Mexico whose civilization flourished between AD 300 and 900.
a member of an ancient Mexican people

Examples

Many Zapotecan artifacts have been found in the Oaxaca region of Mexico.
The Zapotecan civilization is known for its impressive archaeological sites.
Some Zapotecan descendants still preserve their ancient customs and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zapotec - a member of an indigenous people of Oaxaca, Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish 'Zapoteca', referring to the people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˌzæpəˈtikən / (AmE), / ˌzapəˈtiːkən / (BrE)
the language of the Zapotec people, or a member of the group of languages spoken by the Zapotec in Oaxaca, Mexico.
language of the Zapotec people

Examples

Many linguists study Zapotecan to understand the history of Mesoamerican languages.
The Zapotecan languages are known for their complex tone systems.
She is fluent in both Spanish and Zapotecan, allowing her to translate between the two.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zapotec - an indigenous people of southern Mexico; also their individual language

Etymology

From Zapotec, the name of the indigenous people of Oaxaca, Mexico, with the suffix -an to form the language name.
