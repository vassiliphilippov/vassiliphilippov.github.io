width

1. NOUN

/ wɪdθ / (AmE), / wɪdθ / (BrE)
The measurement or extent of something from side to side; the distance across an object.
distance across; side-to-side extent

Examples

The painter measured the width of the doorway to ensure the frame would fit.
This table comes in different lengths and widths to suit your room size.
The width of the river made it impossible to swim across.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadth - the distance or measurement from side to side of something
  • Antonym: length - the measurement or extent of something from end to end
  • Antonym: narrowness - the quality of being narrow, opposite of width

Etymology

From Old English 'wīdthu', related to 'wide'.
