Giuseppe Mazzini

1. PROPN

/ dʒuˈzɛpeɪ mɑˈtsiːni / (AmE), / dʒuˈzɛpeɪ mætˈsiːni / (BrE)
(1805–1872) An Italian nationalist and political activist whose writings and actions were instrumental in the movement for a unified and independent Italy.
Italian nationalist and unification leader

Examples

Giuseppe Mazzini was a key figure in the campaign for Italian unification during the 19th century.
The political philosophy of Giuseppe Mazzini continues to influence nationalist movements worldwide.
Many historians credit Giuseppe Mazzini with inspiring generations of Italians to pursue independence.

Etymology

Italian name, from Giuseppe (Joseph) and the surname Mazzini.
