satanic

1. ADJ

/ səˈtænɪk / (AmE), / səˈtanɪk / (BrE)
Extremely evil, cruel, or wicked; characteristic of or resembling Satan or hell.
extremely evil or cruel

Examples

The dictator was known for his satanic cruelty toward his enemies.
Her laughter had a strangely satanic quality in the darkness.
The novel describes a satanic plot to destroy the world.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - Extremely evil or cruel; devilish
  • Antonym: angelic - Exceptionally kind and innocent; resembling an angel

Etymology

From Medieval Latin satanicus, from Greek Satanas (Satan).
