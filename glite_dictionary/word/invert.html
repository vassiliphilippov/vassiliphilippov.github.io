to invert

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɜrt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to change the order of notes in a chord or melody by moving the lowest note to another position, as in music; to perform an inversion
change order of notes in music

Examples

In this variation, the composer decided to invert the melody to create a fresh sound.
The harmony becomes more complex when you invert the chords in each measure.
To add interest, the music teacher asked the students to invert the theme in their composition.

Synset Examples

here the theme is inverted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to turn something the opposite way or order

Etymology

From Latin invertĕre, meaning 'to turn upside down'.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɝt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to turn something upside down or inside out, or to reverse the normal order or position of
turn upside down or inside out

Examples

Before serving the cake, invert the pan to release it onto a plate.
He accidentally inverted the image while editing the photo.
To see the hidden message, you must invert the colors on the screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to turn something the opposite way or change to the opposite direction
  • Synonym: flip - to turn over or cause to turn over with a sudden quick movement
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position; not inverted

Etymology

From Latin 'invertĕre' (to turn upside down), from 'in-' (in, upon) + 'vertere' (to turn).
