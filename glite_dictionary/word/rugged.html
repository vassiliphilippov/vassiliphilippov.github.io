rugged

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪd / (BrE)
very difficult or demanding; severely testing stamina or determination
very difficult or demanding

Examples

The hikers faced a rugged journey through dense forests and steep mountains.
Only a few applicants made it through the rugged selection process for the elite unit.
The team braced themselves for the rugged challenges of surviving in the harsh desert.

Synset Examples

a rugged competitive examination
the rugged conditions of frontier life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - difficult to do or endure

Etymology

From Middle English *rugged* (rough, shaggy), probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪd / (BrE)
Strongly built or sturdily constructed, able to withstand rough use or difficult conditions.
strongly built; sturdy; durable

Examples

This rugged backpack is designed for hikers who face harsh weather and tough terrain.
The rugged construction of the bridge allows it to endure extreme conditions year after year.
They needed rugged equipment to handle the demanding work on the construction site.

Synset Examples

with a house full of boys you have to have rugged furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturdy - strongly and solidly built
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From Middle English 'rugged' (rough, shaggy), probably from Old Norse 'rugga' (to move unsteadily).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00710810-a

3. ADJ

/ ˈrʌgɪd / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪd / (BrE)
(of land or terrain) having a rough and uneven surface; rocky or broken.
rough, uneven (land or terrain)

Examples

The hikers had difficulty crossing the rugged mountain pass after the storm.
We camped on a rugged stretch of coastline, surrounded by cliffs and boulders.
The landscape became increasingly rugged as we approached the highlands.

Synset Examples

broken terrain
rugged ground

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from perceptible projections, lumps, or indentations

Etymology

From Middle English 'rugged', meaning 'rough', of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian dialect 'rugga' (to make rough).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
