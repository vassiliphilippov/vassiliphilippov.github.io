turbulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜrbjələnt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊlənt / (BrE)
characterized by confusion, disorder, or unrest; marked by disturbance or turbulence
marked by disorder or unrest

Examples

The country went through a turbulent period following the revolution.
She had a turbulent relationship with her parents during her teenage years.
Economic conditions have been particularly turbulent this year, causing uncertainty for many businesses.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - free from disturbance or turmoil
  • Synonym: tumultuous - involving a lot of noise, confusion, or violent change

Etymology

From Latin 'turbulentus' (full of commotion).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɜrbjələnt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊlənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) moving in a violently irregular or agitated way; characterized by disturbance or turbulence.
agitated or disturbed (of liquid/gas)

Examples

The boat struggled to stay afloat in the turbulent waters of the river.
During the storm, the usually still lake became noticeably turbulent.
Strong winds created turbulent currents that made swimming dangerous.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not agitated; free from disturbance (of liquid/gas)

Etymology

From Latin 'turbulentus', from 'turba' meaning 'crowd, disturbance'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00088282-s
Synset POS: s
