Schizopetalon walkeri

1. NOUN

/ skiˌzoʊˈpɛtəlɑn ˈwɔːlkəri / (AmE), / skɪˌzɒˈpɛtælən ˈwɔːlkəri / (BrE)
a delicate South American annual plant with deeply divided leaves and racemes of almond-scented purple-white fringed flowers
a South American annual plant with fragrant purple-white flowers

Examples

Schizopetalon walkeri is prized in gardens for its delicate and fragrant blossoms.
Many horticulturists recommend Schizopetalon walkeri for rockeries and borders due to its compact growth habit.
The racemes of Schizopetalon walkeri add a charming touch to spring floral arrangements.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'schizo' (split) + 'petalon' (petal); species named after John Walker, a botanist.
