pepperiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpepəri.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a sharp, hot, or spicy flavor resembling that of pepper
hot or spicy flavor

Examples

The pepperiness of the dish made my eyes water, but I enjoyed every bite.
She detected a pleasant pepperiness in the sauce that lingered on her tongue.
White wine often pairs well with dishes that have a subtle pepperiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiciness - the quality of being spicy or piquant
  • Antonym: blandness - the quality of lacking strong flavor or spice
