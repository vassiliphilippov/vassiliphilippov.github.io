flaming

1. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal) Used as an intensifier to express annoyance or emphasis, especially before adjectives and nouns.
informal intensifier, expresses emphasis or annoyance
informal british

Examples

It was a flaming disaster from start to finish.
Why don't you turn that flaming music down?
You flaming idiot, look what you've done!

Synset Examples

what a bally (or blinking) nuisance
a bloody fool
a crashing bore
you flaming idiot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - informal intensifier used especially in British English
  • Synonym: blinking - euphemistic intensifier in British English

2. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
showing intense passion or strong emotion, often in a sudden or uncontrolled way
intensely passionate or quick-tempered

Examples

She was known for her flaming temper, which flared up at the slightest provocation.
The author's flaming passion for social justice can be felt on every page of the book.
During the debate, his flaming arguments left the audience stunned.

Synset Examples

a fiery temper
flaming passions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiery - showing strong emotion, passionate or quick-tempered
