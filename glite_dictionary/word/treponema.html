treponema

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɛpəˈniːmə / (AmE), / ˌtrɛpəˈniːmə / (BrE)
a genus of spiral-shaped bacteria, some species of which cause diseases such as syphilis and yaws in humans
a genus of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Treponema pallidum is the bacterium responsible for causing syphilis in humans.
The diagnosis revealed an infection with Treponema, which required immediate treatment.
Recent research has focused on how Treponema species interact with human immune systems.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Treponema, from Greek trepein (to turn) + nēma (thread).
