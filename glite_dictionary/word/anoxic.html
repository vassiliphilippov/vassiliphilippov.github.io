anoxic

1. ADJ

/ æˈnɑksɪk / (AmE), / əˈnɒksɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by a severe deficiency or absence of oxygen, especially in tissues or an environment.
lacking or severely deficient in oxygen
medical

Examples

Fish cannot survive in anoxic water because there is not enough oxygen.
The tissue damage was caused by anoxic conditions during surgery.
Researchers observed that the sediment layer was completely anoxic after the flood event.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypoxic - having less than normal oxygen levels
  • Antonym: normoxic - having normal levels of oxygen

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'oxys' (sharp, here meaning oxygen) + '-ic'.
