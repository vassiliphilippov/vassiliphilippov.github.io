whispering

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (BrE)
making a low, continuous, indistinct sound, resembling a whisper; often used of wind, water, or voices.
low, continuous, indistinct sound

Examples

We listened to the whispering wind as it moved through the trees at dusk.
The whispering waves made the shore seem peaceful all night long.
A whispering stream ran behind the cottage, barely louder than a breath.

Synset Examples

like murmuring waves
susurrant voices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmuring - making a soft, low, continuous sound
  • Degree Lower: hushed - softened to a quiet or low volume
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01458817-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪspɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of speaking very softly without vibrating the vocal cords, so only the breath is heard.
speaking very softly or murmuring

Examples

I heard a whispering behind the door, but I couldn't make out the words.
The whispering of the wind through the trees was calming.
There was a constant whispering in the library as students studied together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisper - Speak very softly using one's breath rather than one's vocal cords

Etymology

From the verb 'whisper', which originated from Middle English 'whisperen', probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07145049-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (BrE)
a soft, gentle sound like that of silk or leaves moving in the wind
soft, gentle rustling sound

Examples

The gentle whispering of the autumn leaves lulled her to sleep.
I listened to the whispering of silk as she moved across the room.
The forest was filled with the whispering of the breeze through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustle - a soft, muffled crackling sound like dry leaves or paper
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07407383-n
Synset POS: n
