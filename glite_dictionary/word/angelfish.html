angelfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪndʒəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈeɪndʒəlfɪʃ / (BrE)
a deep-bodied, disk-shaped marine fish of warmer western Atlantic coastal waters, often kept in aquariums or used as food
a disk-shaped marine fish of the Atlantic

Examples

The vibrant colors of the angelfish make it a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts.
Local fishermen often catch angelfish along the Caribbean coast.
The chef prepared a delicious meal using freshly caught angelfish.

3. NOUN

/ ˈeɪndʒəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈeɪndʒəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of shark with a broad, flat body and winglike pectoral fins, belonging to the family Squatinidae, also known as angel sharks.
a type of flat-bodied shark

Examples

The angelfish glided over the sandy ocean floor, almost hidden by its flat body.
Divers spotted a rare angelfish near the edge of the reef yesterday.
Unlike most sharks, an angelfish lies flat on the seabed, waiting to ambush its prey.
