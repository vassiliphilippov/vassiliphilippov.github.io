saccharide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæk.əˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsak.ə.rʌɪd / (BrE)
any of a class of carbohydrates, including sugars and starches, that serve as structural components in living cells and as a source of energy
type of carbohydrate (sugar, starch)
medical

Examples

Glucose is a simple saccharide that plays an important role in cellular respiration.
Complex saccharides like starch are broken down into sugars the body can use for energy.
Scientists study how different saccharides function in the structure of plant cell walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbohydrate - group of organic compounds including sugars, starch, and cellulose
  • Degree Lower: monosaccharide - a simple sugar containing one saccharide unit
  • Degree Higher: polysaccharide - a carbohydrate whose molecules consist of many saccharide units

Etymology

From Greek sakcharon (sugar).
