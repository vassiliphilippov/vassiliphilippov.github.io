topgallant

1. NOUN

/ tɑpˈɡælənt / (AmE), / tɒpˈɡæl(ə)nt / (BrE)
A sail set on the yard of a topgallant mast, above the topsail, on a square-rigged ship.
a sail above a ship's topsail

Examples

The crew hurried to lower the topgallant as the storm intensified.
During the race, they set both the topsail and the topgallant to catch more wind.
An experienced sailor knows when to reef the topgallant for safety.

Etymology

From 'top' + 'gallant', relating to the uppermost part of a ship's rigging. 'Gallant' here means 'fine', 'splendid'.

2. NOUN

/ tɑpˈɡæl.ənt / (AmE), / tɒpˈɡæl.ənt / (BrE)
the section of a mast fixed above the topmast on a square-rigged ship
mast section above the topmast

Examples

The crew climbed the rigging to secure the topgallant before the approaching storm.
After the mainmast, the topgallant rises further, supporting additional sails.
Damage to the topgallant required immediate repairs to maintain the ship’s speed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: topmast - the mast above the lower mast and below the topgallant on a ship

Etymology

From 'top' (mast section) + 'gallant', originally referring to sails set on this mast, attested from the 16th century.
