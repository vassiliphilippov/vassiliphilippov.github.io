celestial

1. ADJ

/ səˈlɛstʃəl / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl / (BrE)
relating to heaven or the divine; of or pertaining to the sky or heavenly realms, especially as inhabited by deities or spirits.
relating to heaven or the divine

Examples

Ancient myths often featured celestial beings who guided or protected humans.
The cathedral's ceiling was painted with celestial scenes depicting angels and stars.
Many people find comfort in the belief that celestial forces watch over them.

Synset Examples

celestial beings
heavenly hosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavenly - of or relating to heaven; divine
  • Antonym: earthly - of or relating to the earth; worldly, not spiritual or heavenly

Etymology

From Latin caelestis (heavenly), from caelum (sky, heaven).

relating to heaven, the divine, or the spiritual rather than the earthly or physical

Examples

The sky at dawn had a celestial beauty that took my breath away.
Poets often write about celestial harmony and the music of the spheres.
