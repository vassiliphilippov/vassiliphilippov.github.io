extremist

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɹimɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːmɪst / (BrE)
a person who holds extreme political, religious, or social views, especially one who advocates radical action
person with extreme views

Examples

The group was led by an extremist who called for radical change.
Many people fear the influence of religious extremists in politics.
He distanced himself from the extremist views expressed by some members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderate - a person holding moderate views
  • Synonym: radical - a person who supports thorough or complete political or social reform

Etymology

From Latin extremus (the utmost, farthest).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstriːmɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːmɪst / (BrE)
(of opinions or actions) markedly far from what is considered usual or moderate; advocating or representing extreme views or measures.
far beyond normal; extreme in views or actions

Examples

Some groups with extremist beliefs often use disruptive tactics to gain attention.
His extremist stance on environmental policy alienated more moderate supporters.
The internet has made it easier to spread extremist views worldwide.

Synset Examples

extremist political views
radical opinions on education
an ultra conservative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - advocating thorough or complete political or social reform; extreme in positions
  • Antonym: moderate - holding opinions or views that are not extreme; avoiding excesses

Etymology

From 'extreme' + '-ist' (suffix indicating a person who practices or is concerned with something).
