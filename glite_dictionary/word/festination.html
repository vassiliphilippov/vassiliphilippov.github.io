festination

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛstəˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an involuntary shortening of stride and quickening of gait, often seen in neurological diseases such as Parkinson's disease
involuntary quickening of gait in disease
medical

Examples

The patient's festination made it increasingly difficult for him to walk normally.
Doctors observed festination as a symptom during the neurological examination.
Managing festination is an important aspect of treating patients with Parkinson's disease.

Etymology

From Latin 'festinare' (to hasten).
