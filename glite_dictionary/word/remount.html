remount

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
a fresh horse, especially one provided to replace a horse killed or injured in battle
fresh horse, esp. for replacement in battle

Examples

The cavalry unit waited anxiously for a remount after losing several horses in combat.
He chose a sturdy remount from the fresh batch delivered to the regiment.
Without a reliable remount, the messenger could not continue his urgent journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - a thing or person that takes the place of another

Etymology

From re- (again) + mount (to get on a horse), ultimately from Old French monter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02380801-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ riˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / rɪˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to provide with fresh horses, especially to replace those that are tired or unfit for service
provide with fresh horses

Examples

The cavalry officer needed to remount his troops after a long day's march.
Supplies arrived to remount the exhausted regiment before the next advance.
After the battle, they worked quickly to remount the entire unit with strong, fresh horses.

Synset Examples

remount a regiment

Etymology

From re- (again) + mount (from Old French monter, to mount).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01186728-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / rɪˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to mount or put back into position again, especially after being disassembled or removed
mount or put back again

Examples

After repairing the device, he carefully remounted the cover.
She had to remount the horse after falling during the lesson.
The engineer will remount the panel once the inspection is complete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mount - to put in position or assemble for use

Etymology

From re- (again) + mount, from Old French monter, from Latin montare, from mons (mount, hill).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01346054-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ riˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to get on a horse, bicycle, or other vehicle again after being dismounted or after having got off; to mount again.
mount again, especially a horse or vehicle

Examples

After a brief rest, she remounted her bicycle and continued along the trail.
The knight quickly remounted after being thrown from his horse in the joust.
He remounted the motorcycle, determined to finish the race despite the setback.

Synset Examples

he remounted his horse

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dismount - to get off a horse, bicycle, or other vehicle

Etymology

From re- (again) + mount, from Old French monter (to go up), from Latin montare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01927583-v
Synset POS: v
