beheading

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of killing someone by cutting off their head.
killing by decapitation

Examples

The public beheading shocked the entire nation.
During the Middle Ages, beheading was a common method of execution.
The film depicted the beheading of the historical figure in graphic detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decapitation - the act of cutting off the head of a person or animal

Etymology

Formed from 'behead' (from Old English 'beheafdian') + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of executing someone by cutting off their head
execution by decapitation

Examples

The king ordered the beheading of the captured rebel leader.
Historical records describe the beheading as a common form of capital punishment.
News of the beheading shocked people around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decapitation - the action of cutting off the head of a person or animal

Etymology

From the verb 'behead' (from Middle English, prefix 'be-' + 'head').
