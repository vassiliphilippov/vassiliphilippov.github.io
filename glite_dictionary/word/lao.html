Lao

1. NOUN

/ laʊ / (AmE), / laʊ / (BrE)
The Tai language spoken by the majority people of Laos and also in parts of Thailand.
the Tai language of Laos and Thailand

Examples

Many people in Laos speak Lao as their mother tongue.
She is studying Lao at university to better understand Southeast Asian cultures.
The translation was available in English, Thai, and Lao.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thai - the Siamese language spoken in Thailand

Etymology

From Lao (ພາສາລາວ, phasa Lao), the name for the people and language of Laos.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06948371-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ laʊ / (AmE), / laʊ / (BrE)
a member of a Buddhist people living mainly in the Mekong River region of Laos and Thailand, who speak the Lao language; related to the Thai people.
member of a Southeast Asian ethnic group

Examples

Many Lao have migrated to Thailand in recent decades.
The Lao are known for their rich Buddhist traditions and vibrant festivals.
She is studying the culture and language of the Lao people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thai - a member of the dominant ethnic group of Thailand

Etymology

From Lao (ລາວ), the self-designation of the people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09739190-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ laʊ / (AmE), / laʊ / (BrE)
relating to Laos, its people, language, or culture
of Laos or its people

Examples

The Lao people have a rich tradition of weaving beautiful textiles.
He learned several Lao customs during his stay in Vientiane.
Traditional Lao music is often played at local festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Laotian - of or relating to Laos, its people, or culture (less commonly used as adjective)

Etymology

From Lao, the name of the dominant ethnic group and language in Laos.

Source: WordNet
