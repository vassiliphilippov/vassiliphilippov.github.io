Planococcus

1. NOUN

/ ˌplænəˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌplanəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small insects in the family Pseudococcidae, known as mealybugs, some of which are pests of agricultural crops.
genus of mealybugs (insect)

Examples

Planococcus species are commonly found on citrus trees, causing damage to the fruit and leaves.
The life cycle of Planococcus includes both crawlers and adults, which are important to recognize for pest management.
Researchers identified the infestation as coming from Planococcus, a genus notorious for targeting tropical crops.

Etymology

From Greek 'planos' (wandering) and 'kokkos' (berry).

2. NOUN

/ plænəˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / plænəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of scale insects in the family Pseudococcidae, some of which are agricultural pests.
genus of scale insects

Examples

The citrus mealybug, a member of the Planococcus genus, is a significant pest of citrus crops.
Researchers are studying ways to control Planococcus populations in greenhouses.
Several species of Planococcus can infest ornamental plants and cause damage.

Etymology

From Greek 'plano-' (wandering) + 'coccus' (berry), referring to the insect's appearance or movement.
