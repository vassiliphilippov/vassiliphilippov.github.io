Kyzyl Kum

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɪzəl ˈkuːm / (AmE), / ˈkɪzəl ˈkʊm / (BrE)
A large desert in Uzbekistan, located southeast of the Aral Sea, known for its arid landscape and unique geology.
desert in Uzbekistan

Examples

The Kyzyl Kum stretches across central Uzbekistan and is famous for its harsh climate.
Many expeditions have explored the Kyzyl Kum in search of ancient fossils.
Nomads have traversed the Kyzyl Kum for centuries, adapting to its extreme environment.

Etymology

From Turkic languages, meaning 'Red Sand'.
