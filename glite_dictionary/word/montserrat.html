Montserrat

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːnsəˈræt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnsəˈræt / (BrE)
A volcanic island in the Caribbean, part of the West Indies, known for its mountainous landscape and active volcano.
volcanic island in the Caribbean

Examples

The volcano on Montserrat erupted in the 1990s, forcing many residents to evacuate.
Tourists are drawn to Montserrat for its lush hills and black sand beaches.
After the eruption, much of Montserrat's capital, Plymouth, was abandoned.

Etymology

From Spanish Montserrat, meaning 'serrated mountain', originally referring to a mountain in Catalonia, Spain.
