Caloscypha fulgens

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈsaɪ.fə fʊlˈdʒɛnz / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈsaɪ.fə fʊlˈdʒɛnz / (BrE)
a species of cup fungus found in early spring, recognized by its yellow to orange-yellow inner surface
a yellow-orange spring cup fungus species

Examples

The distinctive yellow cup of the Caloscypha fulgens stands out against the forest floor in early spring.
Mycology students often seek out Caloscypha fulgens as one of the season's first fungi.
Photographers admire the striking color of Caloscypha fulgens whenever it emerges after the snow melts.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'scypha' (cup); species name 'fulgens' from Latin, meaning 'shining' or 'bright'.
