harsh

1. ADJ

/ hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
Unpleasantly severe, strict, or unyielding, often causing discomfort or difficulty.
severely unpleasant or strict

Examples

She had to accept the harsh reality of her situation.
The critics delivered a harsh review of the performance.
He faced harsh consequences for breaking the rules.

Synset Examples

the harsh facts of court delays
an abrasive character

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gentle - mild and not harsh

/ hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
(of actions, conditions, or treatments) severe, strict, or unkind.
severe or strict

Examples
