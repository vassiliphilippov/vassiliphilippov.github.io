proletariat

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.lɪˈtɛr.i.ət / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈteə.ri.ət / (BrE)
the social class of people who do manual labor or work for wages, especially those who do not own capital or property
working class; wage laborers

Examples

The rise of the proletariat shaped much of nineteenth-century European history.
Many factory owners exploited the proletariat during the Industrial Revolution.
The novel depicts the daily struggles faced by the urban proletariat.

Synset Examples

there is a shortage of skilled labor in this field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work

Etymology

From Latin 'proletarius' (of the lowest class), via French 'prolétariat'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.ləˈtɛr.i.ət / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈtɛə.ri.ət / (BrE)
the class of workers, especially industrial wage earners, who do not own property and must sell their labor to survive, particularly as used in Marxist theory
working class in society

Examples

The proletariat played a central role in the revolution.
Marx believed that the proletariat would eventually rise against the bourgeoisie.
As factory jobs grew, the proletariat became a larger part of society.

Synset Examples

The proletariat united for better working conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bourgeoisie - the middle/upper class, especially as the capitalist class in Marxist theory
  • Synonym: working class - people who work for wages, especially in manual or industrial jobs

Etymology

From French *prolétariat*, from Latin *proletarius* (citizen of the lowest class).
