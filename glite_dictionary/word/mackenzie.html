Mackenzie

1. PROPN

/ məˈkɛnzi / (AmE), / məˈkɛnzi / (BrE)
A major river in northwestern Canada that flows northwest into the Beaufort Sea.
Canadian river flowing to Beaufort Sea

Examples

The Mackenzie is Canada's longest river, running over 1,700 kilometers.
Many communities along the Mackenzie depend on it for transportation and fishing.
Researchers studied climate change effects in the Mackenzie River basin.

Etymology

Named after Alexander Mackenzie, a Scottish explorer.
