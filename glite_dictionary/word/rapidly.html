rapidly

1. ADV

/ ˈræp.ɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈræp.ɪd.li / (BrE)
In a quick or fast manner; with great speed.
quickly; with great speed

Examples

The company is expanding rapidly into international markets.
Temperatures dropped rapidly after sunset.
She learned how to use the software rapidly and became an expert in no time.

Synset Examples

he works quickly
John got ready in short order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quickly - at a fast speed
  • Antonym: slowly - at a slow speed

Etymology

From Latin 'rapidus' (swift, fleet) + English adverbial '-ly'.
