handsome

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæn.səm / (AmE), / ˈhan.səm / (BrE)
attractive and pleasant in appearance, especially in a way that is considered traditionally masculine or in accordance with ideals of proportion and form
attractive in appearance (esp. for men)

Examples

He was a handsome young man, tall and well-built.
She gave a handsome smile as she accepted the award.
The room was decorated in a handsome style, with elegant furnishings and high ceilings.

Synset Examples

a fine-looking woman
a good-looking man
better-looking than her sister
very pretty but not so extraordinarily handsome
our southern women are well-favored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good-looking - attractive in appearance
  • Antonym: ugly - unpleasant or unattractive in appearance
  • Synonym: beautiful - very pleasing to look at

Etymology

From Old English 'handsum' (easy to handle, ready at hand), later meaning 'good-looking'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæn.səm / (AmE), / ˈhæn.səm / (BrE)
(of an amount or gesture) given or provided generously or on a large scale.
given generously; large in amount

Examples

Her grandfather gives her a handsome allowance every month.
The charity received a handsome donation from a local business owner.
They were given a handsome reward for returning the lost dog.

Synset Examples

a munificent gift
her fond and openhanded grandfather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary
  • Antonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality; scanty
