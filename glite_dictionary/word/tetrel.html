tetrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtrəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəl / (BrE)
any of the chemical elements in group 14 of the periodic table, including carbon, silicon, germanium, tin, and lead
element of carbon group (periodic table)

Examples

Silicon is a tetrel widely used in the electronics industry.
Chemists study the properties of each tetrel to understand the group trends.
Lead, the heaviest tetrel, has very different properties from carbon.

Synset Examples

Carbon, the lightest carbon group element, sublimates at 3825 °C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon group - group 14 elements in the periodic table
  • Degree Higher: pnictogen - any element of group 15 in the periodic table
  • Degree Higher: chalcogen - any element of group 16 in the periodic table

Etymology

From Latin 'tetra-' (four), referencing group 14 elements in the periodic table.
