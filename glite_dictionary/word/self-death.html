self-death

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf dɛθ / (AmE), / sɛlf dɛθ / (BrE)
the act or concept of ending one's own life, especially as a metaphor for personal transformation or self-exploration
concept of ending one's own life, often metaphorical

Examples

The novel's protagonist undergoes a symbolic self-death, emerging with a new sense of identity.
Therapists sometimes discuss self-death when exploring radical changes in self-concept.
Artists have used the theme of self-death to represent the shedding of old habits or beliefs.

Synset Examples

She wrote about self-death as a metaphor for personal transformation.
The concept of self-death can be liberating for some.
Exploring self-death often leads to a deeper understanding of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally causing one's own death

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'death'; following the pattern of self-related compounds.
