hollo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
a very loud cry or shout, similar to the call or bellow of an animal
a loud cry or shout

Examples

He let out a hollo that echoed across the field.
The distant hollo warned the workers of approaching danger.
With a triumphant hollo, she signaled victory to her team.

Synset Examples

his bellow filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellow - to shout in a deep, loud voice

Etymology

Perhaps a variant of 'halloo,' from Middle English, imitative of a loud shout.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07136069-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
to utter a sudden, loud cry, especially to attract attention
utter a sudden loud cry

Examples

The shepherd holloed across the field to call his dog back.
She holloed for help when she saw smoke rising from the barn.
He holloed so loudly that everyone turned to look at him.

Synset Examples

she cried with pain when the doctor inserted the needle
I yelled to her from the window but she couldn't hear me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - utter a loud call or cry
  • Synonym: yell - shout loudly, for pain, excitement, or attention
  • Antonym: whisper - speak very softly using breath instead of vocal cords

Etymology

Variant of 'holler', influenced by similar calls such as 'halloo' or 'hello'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00915018-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈhɑː.loʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.əʊ / (BrE)
to shout or call out, especially to attract attention or as a greeting; to cry 'hollo'.
shout or call out
archaic

Examples

He holloed to his friend across the field, waving his arms enthusiastically.
Suddenly, a voice holloed from the woods, startling the group of hikers.
They could hear the children holloing as they ran down the hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halloo - to shout or cry out to attract attention

Etymology

Related to 'halloo', from Middle English 'halow', used to call attention.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00915748-v
Synset POS: v
