potomania

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊtəˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌpɒtəˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
A pathological and persistent craving for excessive consumption of alcoholic beverages.
pathological craving for alcohol
medical

Examples

Some rare psychiatric disorders, such as potomania, involve bizarre patterns of alcohol consumption.
The patient's potomania was so severe that medical intervention was necessary to prevent physical harm.
Doctors are researching the causes and treatment options for potomania in chronic alcoholics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dipsomania - An uncontrollable craving for alcohol; alcoholism

Etymology

From Latin "potare" (to drink) and Greek "mania" (madness, frenzy).
