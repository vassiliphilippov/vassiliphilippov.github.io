papaya

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpaɪə / (AmE), / pəˈpaɪə / (BrE)
A large, oval-shaped tropical fruit with sweet, yellowish flesh, typically orange when ripe.
large tropical fruit with yellowish flesh

Examples

She sliced a ripe papaya and served it with breakfast.
The market was selling fresh papayas at a reasonable price.
Green papaya is often used in Southeast Asian salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - another name for papaya, especially in some English varieties

Etymology

From Spanish 'papaya', originating from Taíno or Arawakan languages.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈpaɪə / (AmE), / pəˈpɑːjə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree or shrub with large deeply lobed leaves, grown for its large oblong yellow or orange fruit with sweet flesh and black seeds.
tropical fruit-bearing tree or its fruit

Examples

We sliced a ripe papaya for breakfast and enjoyed its sweet, juicy flavor.
The papaya tree in our backyard produces fruit all year round.
Adding papaya to smoothies gives them a tropical taste and vibrant color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - another name for papaya (the tropical fruit)

Etymology

From Spanish 'papaya', probably of Cariban origin.
