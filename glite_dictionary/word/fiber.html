fiber

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a slender, threadlike substance, especially one that can be spun into yarn or used to make fabric
threadlike substance for yarn or fabric

Examples

Cotton fiber is widely used to make textiles and clothing.
The quality of silk fiber contributes to the smoothness of luxury fabrics.
Scientists are developing new synthetic fiber for stronger and lighter materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filament - a slender threadlike object or fiber, often found in plants or textiles

Etymology

From Latin fibra (fiber, filament).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.bə / (BrE)
A leatherlike material made by compressing layers of paper or cloth, used as an inexpensive substitute for leather or wood.
compressed paper/cloth material

Examples

The suitcase was made of sturdy fiber instead of real leather.
Some early phonograph records were produced from compressed fiber.
He repaired the chair using pieces of fiber where wood was missing.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
any of several elongated, threadlike cells such as a muscle fiber or a nerve fiber in living tissue
elongated threadlike cell (e.g. muscle, nerve)
medical

Examples

Damage to a muscle can cause the fibers to tear, resulting in pain and weakness.
Nerve fibers transmit signals quickly throughout the body to coordinate movement.
Under the microscope, each fiber appeared as a long, thin strand within the tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filament - a slender threadlike object or structure, especially in biology

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (a fiber, filament).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
Plant material that cannot be digested but helps move food through the digestive system by adding bulk.
indigestible plant material in food

Examples

Foods high in fiber can help prevent constipation and maintain healthy digestion.
Nutritionists recommend eating plenty of vegetables to boost your daily fiber intake.
Whole grains are a good source of dietary fiber and promote a feeling of fullness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughage - coarse indigestible plant material; dietary fiber

5. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
the qualities of moral strength, integrity, and resolve that influence a person’s ethical actions and reactions
moral strength or integrity

Examples

It takes a great deal of fiber to stand up for what is right under pressure.
The school aims to develop the intellectual and moral fiber of its students.
Leaders are expected to show strong fiber when making difficult decisions.

Synset Examples

education has for its object the formation of character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: character - moral or ethical qualities that distinguish an individual
  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • Antonym: weakness - the lack of moral strength or resolve

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (a fiber, thread, or filament), later figuratively used for strength of character.
