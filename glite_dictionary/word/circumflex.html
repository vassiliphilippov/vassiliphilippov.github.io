circumflex

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːkəmˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌflɛks / (BrE)
a diacritical mark (^) placed above a letter, especially a vowel, in some languages to indicate a special pronunciation or quality
diacritical mark (^) above a letter

Examples

The French word "forêt" contains a circumflex over the letter 'e'.
In Portuguese, a circumflex can indicate a stressed vowel.
Type the circumflex by pressing the control key and the caret symbol together.

Etymology

From Latin 'circumflexus', past participle of 'circumflectere' (to bend around), from 'circum-' (around) + 'flectere' (to bend).
