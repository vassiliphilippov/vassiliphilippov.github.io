refrigeration

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of deliberately lowering the body's temperature for therapeutic purposes, especially in medical treatment
lowering body temperature for therapy
medical

Examples

After the accident, the doctors used refrigeration to reduce swelling and limit brain damage.
Therapeutic refrigeration is sometimes applied to patients following cardiac arrest.
Rapid refrigeration of the body can help slow down certain metabolic processes during emergency care.

Synset Examples

refrigeration by immersing the patient's body in a cold bath

Etymology

From Latin 'refrigerare' (to make cold) via French 'réfrigération'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of cooling or freezing something, especially food, to preserve it
process of cooling for preservation

Examples

Modern supermarkets rely on refrigeration to keep perishable foods fresh for longer periods.
After the power outage, failure of refrigeration led to spoilage of the meat and dairy products.
Advances in refrigeration have made it possible to safely store vaccines at low temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freezing - the process of making something very cold so that it becomes solid
  • Antonym: heating - the process of making something hot or warmer

Etymology

From Latin 'refrigeratio' (cooling), from 'refrigerare' (to make cool).
