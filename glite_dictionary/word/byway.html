byway

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌweɪ / (BrE)
A minor road or track, especially one that is not often used and runs through the countryside.
minor or little-used side road

Examples

They prefer to cycle along the quiet byways rather than on busy highways.
Many picturesque villages can be found down narrow country byways.
The author explores the forgotten byways of English history in his book.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highway - a main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities

Etymology

From Middle English bywei, from by- ('side') + way ('road').
