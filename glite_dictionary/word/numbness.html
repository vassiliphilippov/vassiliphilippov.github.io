numbness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌmnəs / (AmE), / ˈnʌmnəs / (BrE)
a loss or reduction of physical sensation in a part of the body, often caused by nerve damage, cold, or pressure
loss or reduction of physical sensation

Examples

After sitting in the same position for hours, she experienced numbness in her legs.
A common symptom of carpal tunnel syndrome is numbness in the fingers and hand.
Cold weather can cause temporary numbness in your toes and fingers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tingling - a prickling sensation usually felt in the skin
  • Degree Higher: anesthesia - complete loss of feeling, especially as induced medically

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʌmnəs / (AmE), / ˈnʌmnəs / (BrE)
a lack of enthusiasm or interest in things generally, often resulting in emotional indifference or apathy
lack of enthusiasm or interest

Examples

After months of bad news, he felt a deep numbness toward everything around him.
Her numbness to the team's progress worried her coach.
Prolonged stress can lead to a sense of emotional numbness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of feeling or emotion; indifference
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
