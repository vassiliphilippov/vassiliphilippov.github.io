Plasmodium vivax

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmoʊdiəm ˈvaɪˌvæks / (AmE), / plazˈməʊdɪəm ˈvɪˌvæks / (BrE)
a species of parasitic protozoan of the genus Plasmodium that causes a type of malaria in humans, characterized by recurring fevers
species causing recurring malaria in humans
medical

Examples

Plasmodium vivax is responsible for causing recurring fevers in many malaria patients worldwide.
Researchers are working to develop a vaccine that targets Plasmodium vivax infection.
Unlike Plasmodium falciparum, Plasmodium vivax can remain dormant in the liver for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plasmodium falciparum - species of Plasmodium causing the most severe form of malaria

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Plasmodium' (the genus name) and Latin 'vivax' meaning 'tenacious of life'.
