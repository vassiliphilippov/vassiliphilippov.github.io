carpenteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrpənˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpənˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A California evergreen shrub (Carpenteria californica) with glossy opposite leaves and terminal clusters of fragrant white flowers.
California evergreen shrub with white flowers

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful carpenteria in full bloom.
Native to California, carpenteria thrives in well-drained soils and sunny locations.
Botanists admire the fragrant white flowers of carpenteria during spring and early summer.

Etymology

Named after Dr. William Marbury Carpenter, an American botanist.
