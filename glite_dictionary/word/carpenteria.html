Carpenteria

1. NOUN

/ kɑːr.pənˈtɪəri.ə / (AmE), / kɑː.pənˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the shrub family Hydrangeaceae, consisting of a single species native to California, sometimes placed in the Saxifragaceae family.
genus of flowering shrub native to California

Examples

The rare Carpenteria grows naturally only in a limited area of California.
Botanists debated whether Carpenteria should be classified in the Saxifragaceae family.
Gardeners value Carpenteria for its white, fragrant blossoms and evergreen foliage.

Etymology

Named after Dr. William Marbury Carpenter, an American botanist.
