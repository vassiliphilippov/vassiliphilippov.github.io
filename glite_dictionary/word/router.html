router

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtər / (AmE), / ˈraʊtə / (BrE)
a power tool with a rotating shaped cutter used in carpentry and woodworking for cutting grooves and decorative edges in wood
woodworking power tool for cutting grooves

Examples

The carpenter used a router to create precise grooves along the edges of the tabletop.
I bought a new router for my workshop to help with detailed woodwork.
Proper use of a router can add elegant designs to cabinet doors.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊtər / (AmE), / ˈruːtə / (BrE)
a device that forwards data packets between computer networks, typically directing traffic within or between local area networks and the internet
device forwarding data in networks

Examples

Our home network uses a router to connect all computers to the internet.
The technician installed a new wireless router to improve Wi-Fi coverage.
A malfunctioning router can cause many connectivity problems across a network.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - device connecting devices within a network
  • Synonym: hub - a basic device for connecting multiple network devices
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04121938-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈraʊtər / (AmE), / ˈraʊtə / (BrE)
a worker responsible for directing shipments or materials for distribution and delivery
worker who directs shipments for delivery

Examples

The router makes sure all packages reach the correct destinations efficiently.
After the morning rush, the router reviewed the day's delivery schedules.
A skilled router can improve the entire warehouse's productivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatcher - a person who sends out vehicles according to a schedule

Etymology

From route + -er, denoting someone who routes goods or materials.
