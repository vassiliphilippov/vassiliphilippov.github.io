dolorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊlɚəs / (AmE), / ˈdɒlərəs / (BrE)
feeling or expressing great sorrow or distress; mournful
feeling or expressing sorrow

Examples

The dolorous music filled the cathedral, moving the congregation to tears.
Her dolorous expression revealed the depth of her grief after the loss.
He spoke in a dolorous tone, telling stories of hardship and heartbreak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournful - feeling, expressing, or inducing sadness, regret, or grief
  • Synonym: sorrowful - full of sorrow; feeling or showing grief
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin 'dolorosus', from 'dolor' meaning 'pain' or 'grief'.
