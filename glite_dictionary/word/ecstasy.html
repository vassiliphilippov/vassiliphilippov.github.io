ecstasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.stə.si / (AmE), / ˈɛk.stə.si / (BrE)
A state of intense joy or overwhelming emotion in which a person seems to be carried beyond self-control.
intense joy; overwhelming emotion

Examples

As the final note faded, she was left in a state of pure ecstasy.
He spoke with such passion that his listeners hung on every word in ecstasy.
The team's victory sent fans into ecstasy as they flooded the streets to celebrate.

Synset Examples

listening to sweet music in a perfect rapture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapture - a feeling of intense pleasure or joy
  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or satisfaction
  • Antonym: despair - complete loss or absence of hope

Etymology

From Greek ekstasis (standing outside oneself, trance), via Latin and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.stə.si / (AmE), / ˈek.stə.si / (BrE)
a state of overwhelming happiness or joyful excitement
overwhelming happiness or bliss

Examples

After hearing the good news, she was in a state of pure ecstasy.
Winning the championship filled the entire team with ecstasy.
His face glowed with ecstasy as he listened to the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bliss - perfect happiness; great joy
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering or unhappiness

Etymology

From Greek ekstasis (to stand outside oneself), via Latin ecstasis.
