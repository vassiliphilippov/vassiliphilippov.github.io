onomatopoetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːnəˌmætəpoʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˌmætəʊpəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or expression) relating to or characterized by onomatopoeia; imitative of natural sounds.
relating to onomatopoeia (sound imitation)

Examples

The word "buzz" is onomatopoetic because it imitates the sound it describes.
Children often enjoy using onomatopoetic words like "meow" and "clang" in their stories.
The poet employed onomatopoetic expressions to make the verses more vivid and engaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onomatopoeic - relating to or imitative of onomatopoeia (alternative form)

Etymology

From Greek onomatopoiia (making of a name or word), via Late Latin onomatopoeia.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːnəˌmætəpoʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˌmætəʊpəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a word) formed by imitating or suggesting the sound made by the thing it describes.
imitating natural sounds (of words)

Examples

Many animal sounds in English are described using onomatopoetic words, such as 'buzz' or 'meow'.
Her poem was rich in onomatopoetic terms that brought the jungle to life.
The word 'sizzle' is onomatopoetic because it sounds like what it describes.

Synset Examples

onomatopoeic words are imitative of noises
it was independently developed in more than one place as an onomatopoetic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onomatopoeic - (of a word) imitative of the sound it denotes; variant form

Etymology

From Greek onomatopoiētikos, from onomatopoiia (onomatopoeia, the making of a name or word).

Synset POS: a
