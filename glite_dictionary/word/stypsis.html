stypsis

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪpsɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɪpsɪs / (BrE)
the ability to contract or cause contraction of body tissues to check bleeding or restrict the flow of fluids
contraction of tissues to stop bleeding
medical

Examples

The stypsis of certain herbs makes them valuable for treating minor cuts and wounds.
Some medical compounds are prized for their stypsis in stopping nosebleeds.
Wine with high stypsis can cause a puckering sensation due to tissue contraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astringency - the characteristic of causing contraction of body tissues

Etymology

From Greek *stypsis* (astringency, contraction), from *styphein* (to contract).
