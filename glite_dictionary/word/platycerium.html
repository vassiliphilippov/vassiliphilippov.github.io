Platycerium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpleɪtɪˈsɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical ferns, often epiphytic, native to the Old World and commonly known as staghorn ferns.
genus of epiphytic tropical ferns

Examples

The Platycerium is prized by gardeners for its impressive, antler-shaped fronds.
We mounted a Platycerium on a wooden board to grow as a houseplant.
Some species of Platycerium can thrive indoors with the right humidity and light.

Etymology

From Greek 'platy-' (broad, flat) and 'keras' (horn), referring to the shape of the fronds.

2. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of mostly epiphytic ferns native to tropical regions of the Old World, commonly known as staghorn ferns.
a genus of epiphytic ferns (staghorn ferns)

Examples

The Platycerium is a striking addition to any greenhouse collection.
Many Platycerium species grow on trees in their native habitats.
We mounted a Platycerium on a wooden board to display its unique fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn fern - a common name for ferns of the genus Platycerium

Etymology

From Greek 'platy-' meaning 'broad' and 'keras' (kerat-) meaning 'horn', referring to the broad, horn-shaped fronds.
