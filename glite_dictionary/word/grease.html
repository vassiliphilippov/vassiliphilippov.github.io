grease

1. NOUN

/ ɡris / (AmE), / ɡriːs / (BrE)
A thick, oily or fatty substance, especially one used to lubricate machinery or parts.
a thick oily lubricant

Examples

You should apply grease to the gears to keep them running smoothly.
The mechanic wiped his hands, stained with grease, on a rag.
Heavy grease is needed to lubricate the moving parts of this engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil - a viscous liquid used for lubrication or fuel
  • Synonym: lubricant - a substance used to reduce friction between surfaces

Etymology

From Old French 'gres', from Latin 'crassus' (fat, thick).

2. VERB

/ ɡris / (AmE), / ɡriːs / (BrE)
to apply grease to something, especially to lubricate moving parts so they work smoothly
apply grease to lubricate

Examples

Remember to grease the hinges, or the door will squeak.
The mechanic greased all the parts before reassembling the engine.
You should grease the bike chain regularly to keep it running smoothly.

Synset Examples

grease the wheels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lubricate - to apply a substance to make something slippery or smooth; reduce friction

Etymology

From Old French *gresse* (fat, grease), from Latin *crassus* (thick, fat).
