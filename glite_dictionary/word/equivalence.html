equivalence

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɪvələns / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvələns / (BrE)
the state of being equal or interchangeable, especially in value, meaning, or significance
state of being equal or interchangeable

Examples

The judge found no equivalence between the two cases, as the circumstances differed greatly.
In mathematics, equivalence is used to show that two statements can be substituted for one another.
Many people debate the equivalence of different qualifications when applying for jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • Antonym: inequality - the state of not being equal

Etymology

From Medieval Latin equivalentia, from Latin aequivalēns (being equal in value).

the state or condition of being equal in value, function, meaning, or effect to something else

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɪvələns / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvələns / (BrE)
the state of being equal or comparable in value, function, meaning, or effect
the quality of being equal or comparable

Examples

There is a strong equivalence between the two theories proposed by the scientists.
His apology was seen as the moral equivalence of an admission of guilt.
In mathematics, equivalence refers to the relationship between two entities that are comparable in a specific way.

Synset Examples

no comparison between the two books
beyond compare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status or rights
  • Antonym: inequality - the state of not being equal, especially in status or rights

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *aequivalentia* (equality in value), from Latin *aequi-* (equal) + *valere* (to be worth).
