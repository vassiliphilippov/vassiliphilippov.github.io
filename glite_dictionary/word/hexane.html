hexane

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛk.seɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛk.seɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid hydrocarbon obtained from petroleum, used mainly as a solvent and in chemical synthesis
a colorless liquid hydrocarbon solvent

Examples

The laboratory technician used hexane to extract the oil from the seeds.
Because hexane is highly flammable, proper safety measures must be followed during its use.
Industrial cleaners sometimes contain hexane as a powerful solvent for removing grease.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alkane - any saturated hydrocarbon; a class of compounds containing only single bonds
  • Degree Lower: pentane - an alkane hydrocarbon with five carbon atoms
  • Degree Higher: heptane - an alkane hydrocarbon with seven carbon atoms

Etymology

From Greek 'hex' (six), referring to its six carbon atoms, combined with '-ane' (a suffix for saturated hydrocarbons).
