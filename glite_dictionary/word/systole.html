systole

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəˌli / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəli / (BrE)
the phase of the heartbeat when the heart's chambers contract and pump blood out into the arteries
phase when heart contracts to pump blood
medical

Examples

During systole, the ventricles contract, pushing blood into the main arteries.
The doctor listened to the patient's heartbeat, noting the intervals of systole and diastole.
High blood pressure can strain the heart during systole and increase risk of disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diastole - the phase of the heartbeat when the heart relaxes and fills with blood

Etymology

From Greek systolē (a drawing together), from syn- (together) + stellein (to place).
