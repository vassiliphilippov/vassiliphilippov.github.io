sesquipedalian

1. NOUN

/ sɛsˌkwɪpɪˈdeɪliən / (AmE), / sɛsˌkwɪpɪˈdeɪlɪən / (BrE)
A very long word; a word that is unusually lengthy or polysyllabic.
very long word

Examples

He is famous for using at least one sesquipedalian in every speech.
The dictionary includes many strange sesquipedalians that most people have never heard of.
The students laughed at the professor's favorite sesquipedalian example.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polysyllable - a word consisting of more than one syllable

Etymology

From Latin 'sesquipedalis' (a foot and a half long), from 'sesqui-' (one and a half) and 'pes' (foot).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛs.kwə.pɪˈdeɪ.li.ən / (AmE), / ˌsɛs.kwɪ.pɪˈdeɪ.li.ən / (BrE)
(of words) long and ponderous; having many syllables, often in a way that is considered overly complex or pretentious.
long or ponderous (of words)

Examples

The professor's lecture was filled with sesquipedalian terminology that confused the students.
Writers are sometimes criticized for using sesquipedalian words when simpler ones would suffice.
Her essay was so sesquipedalian that it was difficult for the average reader to understand.

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɛs.kwɪ.pɪˈdeɪ.li.ən / (AmE), / ˌsɛs.kwɪ.pəˈdeɪ.li.ən / (BrE)
(of a person, speech, or style) tending to use long words; given to the overuse of long or complicated words.
given to using long words

Examples

The professor's sesquipedalian lectures often left his students bewildered.
Her sesquipedalian writing style made the article difficult to understand for the average reader.
He has a reputation as a sesquipedalian orator, delighting in complicated vocabulary.

Synset Examples

sesquipedalian orators
this sesquipedalian way of saying one has no money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbose - using more words than necessary; wordy
  • Antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words; brief

Etymology

From Latin sesquipedalis ('a foot and a half long'), from sesqui- ('one and a half') + pes, pedis ('foot'). Popularized in English by Horace's phrase 'sesquipedalia verba' (words a foot and a half long).
