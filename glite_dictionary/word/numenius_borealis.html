Numenius borealis

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmiːni.əs bɔːˈreɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / njuːˈmiːni.əs bɒˈrɪə.lɪs / (BrE)
a species of curlew native to North America, commonly known as the Eskimo curlew, now considered critically endangered or possibly extinct
species of North American curlew (Eskimo curlew)

Examples

The Numenius borealis was once abundant across the central plains of North America.
Researchers have extensively surveyed traditional breeding grounds for signs of Numenius borealis.
Historical records suggest that hunters significantly reduced Numenius borealis populations in the late 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eskimo curlew - common name for Numenius borealis, a rare North American curlew
  • Degree Higher: Numenius americanus - long-billed curlew, another North American species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Numenius' (the curlew genus) and 'borealis' meaning 'northern'.
