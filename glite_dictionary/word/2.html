1. NUM

/ tuː / (AmE), / tuː / (BrE)
the cardinal number that is the sum of one and one; the numeral representing this number
the number two

Examples

She has 2 cats and a dog at home.
Please pick 2 books from the shelf.
Only 2 seats are left for the concert.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: one - the cardinal number before two
  • Degree Higher: three - the cardinal number after two

Etymology

From Old English 'twa', from Proto-Germanic 'twai', related to Latin 'duo', Greek 'duo'.
