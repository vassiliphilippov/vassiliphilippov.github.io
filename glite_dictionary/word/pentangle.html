pentangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnˌtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛntæŋɡl / (BrE)
a five-pointed star formed by drawing five straight lines between the vertices of a pentagon, often enclosing another pentagon
five-pointed star-shaped figure

Examples

The medieval knight wore a shield decorated with a pentangle to symbolize truth and integrity.
She drew a perfect pentangle in her geometry notebook, connecting each vertex of the pentagon.
A pentangle appears frequently in various mystical traditions and heraldic emblems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentagram - a five-pointed star drawn with five straight strokes

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'penta-' (Greek for 'five') + 'angle' (from Latin 'angulus')
