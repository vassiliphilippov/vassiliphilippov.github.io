neoconservative

1. NOUN

/ niː.oʊ.kənˈsɜːr.və.tɪv / (AmE), / niː.əʊ.kənˈsɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
A conservative, especially in politics, who supports neoconservatism, typically advocating assertive promotion of democracy and US national interests abroad.
supporter of neoconservatism

Examples

He often refers to himself as a neoconservative when discussing foreign policy.
Many neoconservatives supported the invasion, seeing it as a means to promote democracy abroad.
The influence of neoconservative ideas has been debated in recent political circles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds to traditional values and attitudes; especially in politics
  • Antonym: progressive - a person advocating reform and new ideas, typically in politics

Etymology

Formed from 'neo-' (new) and 'conservative'.
