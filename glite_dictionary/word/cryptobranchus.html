Cryptobranchus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈbræŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈbraŋkəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Cryptobranchidae, comprising large aquatic salamanders known as giant salamanders, found in eastern North America.
genus of large aquatic salamanders

Examples

The hellbender, a member of the genus Cryptobranchus, is the largest salamander in North America.
Scientists studied several Cryptobranchus specimens to understand their adaptation to aquatic environments.
Cryptobranchus species are known for their wrinkled skin and preference for clean, fast-moving streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cryptobranchidae - family of large aquatic salamanders including Cryptobranchus

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'branchia' (gills).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈbræŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈbraŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large aquatic salamanders in the family Cryptobranchidae, commonly known as the hellbenders.
genus of large aquatic salamanders

Examples

The Cryptobranchus genus includes the hellbender, a large salamander found in North American rivers.
Biologists studied the habitat of Cryptobranchus to better understand its declining populations.
Members of the Cryptobranchus genus require clean, oxygen-rich streams for survival.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cryptobranchidae - the family of aquatic salamanders including Cryptobranchus

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'branchos' (gill), referring to concealed gill structures.
