Marasmius

1. NOUN

/ məˈræz.mi.əs / (AmE), / məˈraz.mɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly small mushrooms with white spores, often characterized by their ability to revive after drying.
genus of small white-spored mushrooms

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with clusters of Marasmius mushrooms after the rain.
Some species within the genus Marasmius are capable of reviving after being dried.
Mycologists collected several Marasmius specimens for their spore analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - the fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus
  • Synonym: fungus - a group of unicellular or multicellular organisms, such as mushrooms, molds, and yeasts

Etymology

From Greek marasmos (withering), referring to the mushrooms' ability to revive after drying.

2. NOUN

/ məˈræz.mi.əs / (AmE), / məˈraz.mi.əs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly small mushrooms with white spores, belonging to the family Marasmiaceae.
genus of small, white-spored mushrooms

Examples

The Marasmius genus contains several mushrooms known for their ability to revive after drying out.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Marasmius genus growing in tropical forests.
Many edible forest mushrooms belong to Marasmius, though proper identification is essential.

Etymology

From Greek 'marasmos' (decay), referring to the mushrooms' ability to dry out and revive.
