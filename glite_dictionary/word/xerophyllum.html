Xerophyllum

1. NOUN

/ zɛr.əˈfɪl.əm / (AmE), / zɛr.əˈfɪl.əm / (BrE)
A small genus of North American perennial herbs with grasslike basal leaves, including species such as squaw grass, sometimes classified in the family Melanthiaceae.
genus of North American perennial herbs

Examples

Botanists discovered a rare Xerophyllum species growing in the alpine meadows of Montana.
The flower spikes of Xerophyllum are particularly striking during late spring and early summer.
Native Americans historically used the leaves of Xerophyllum for weaving baskets and mats.

Etymology

From Greek xēros (dry) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the plant's dry-adapted leaves.

2. NOUN

/ zɛrəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / zɛərəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A small genus of North American perennial herbs with grasslike basal leaves, known commonly as squaw grass, sometimes placed in the family Melanthiaceae.
genus of North American herbs

Examples

Xerophyllum is commonly found in the mountainous regions of North America.
The leaves of Xerophyllum species resemble grass blades and are often mistaken for grasses.
Botanists debated whether Xerophyllum should be placed in the Melanthiaceae family.

Etymology

From Greek xēros (dry) + phyllon (leaf).
