to misjudge

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / mɪsˈdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
to form an incorrect opinion or conclusion about someone or something
to judge incorrectly

Examples

It's easy to misjudge someone's abilities based on first impressions.
The driver misjudged the distance to the curb and scratched the car.
We often misjudge situations when we lack complete information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underestimate - to estimate something as less than the actual value; judge insufficiently
  • Synonym: overestimate - to estimate something as greater than the actual value; judge excessively

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + judge, after Middle English mesjuggen.
