sleekness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːknəs / (AmE), / ˈsliːknəs / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth, polished, and well-groomed in appearance or style
polished or well-groomed quality

Examples

Her hairstyle added to the overall sleekness of her professional look.
The sleekness of the new car impressed everyone at the showroom.
He carried himself with the sleekness of a well-dressed executive.

Synset Examples

the sleekness of his appearance reminded me of his financial successes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish
  • Antonym: shabbiness - the quality of being untidy or in poor condition

Etymology

From 'sleek' + '-ness', with 'sleek' from Middle English 'slek', of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslik.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsliːk.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth and glossy, especially to the touch, like silk fabric
smooth, glossy quality or feel

Examples

The sleekness of the silk scarf was immediately noticeable to anyone who touched it.
Her hair had a brilliant sleekness that made it look like it belonged in a shampoo commercial.
The sleekness of the new phone's surface made it pleasant to hold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth, even, or without roughness
  • Antonym: roughness - the quality of being rough, uneven, or coarse in texture

Etymology

From 'sleek' + '-ness', dating to Middle English; 'sleek' from Middle Dutch 'sliken' (to smooth).
