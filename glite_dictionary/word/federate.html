federate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛdəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdəreɪt / (BrE)
to unite into a federation or league, especially of states or organizations, under a central authority
to unite in a federation

Examples

Several small nations chose to federate for mutual security and economic benefit.
After years of negotiation, the provinces finally federated into a single country.
The leaders hope to federate their clubs and create a stronger unified organization.

Synset Examples

The country was federated after the civil war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confederate - to join together in a confederacy or alliance

Etymology

From Latin 'foederatus' (joined by treaty), from 'foedus' (treaty, league).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00369847-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛdəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdəreɪt / (BrE)
to join together to form a single group or organization with a central authority, especially for a common purpose
to unite into a federation

Examples

After decades of conflict, the small states decided to federate and form a stronger political union.
The member organizations federated in order to better represent their shared interests.
Several colonies federated under a single constitution to become a new nation.

Synset Examples

The republics federated to become the Soviet Union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to join together or combine into one
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not united

Etymology

From Latin 'foederatus', past participle of 'foederare' (to ally), from 'foedus' (league, treaty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00370095-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɛd ər ɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛd ər ət / (BrE)
(of states or organizations) united under a central government while retaining certain individual powers.
united under a central government

Examples

Several federate states agreed to form a new country.
The concept of a federate government balances local autonomy with central authority.
Germany is a federate structure, composed of several federal states known as Länder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federal - relating to a system of government in which several states form a unity but remain independent in internal affairs

Etymology

From Latin 'foederatus' (joined by treaty), from 'foedus' (treaty, league).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
