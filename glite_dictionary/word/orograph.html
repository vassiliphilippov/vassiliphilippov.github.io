orograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A machine used for making topographical maps.
machine for making topographical maps

Examples

The cartographer demonstrated how the orograph could simplify the mapping of mountainous regions.
Advances in technology have largely replaced the orograph with digital mapping tools.
The museum displayed a vintage orograph used by early surveyors.

Etymology

From Greek 'oros' (mountain) + '-graph' (instrument for recording).
