Hirudo

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈruːdoʊ / (AmE), / hɪˈruːdəʊ / (BrE)
the genus of leeches that includes the medicinal leech, serving as the type genus of the family Hirudinidae
genus of leeches (type of Hirudinidae)

Examples

The medicinal leech, classified under Hirudo, has been used in therapy for centuries.
Several species within the genus Hirudo are important for medical research.
Scientists studied the behavior of Hirudo in freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leeches - parasitic or carnivorous, aquatic or terrestrial annelid worms

Etymology

From Latin 'hirudo', meaning 'leech'.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈruːdoʊ / (AmE), / hɪˈruːdəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of leeches, the type genus of the family Hirudinidae, including the medicinal leech.
genus of leeches

Examples

The medicinal leech belongs to the genus Hirudo.
Research on Hirudo has contributed greatly to our understanding of anticoagulation.
Several species within the genus Hirudo are used in modern medicine.

Etymology

From Latin *hīrūdō* (leech).
