irrationally

1. ADV

/ ɪˈræʃənəli / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃ(ə)nəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not based on reason, logic, or clear thinking.
in an unreasonable or illogical manner

Examples

She irrationally feared that something terrible would happen every time she left the house.
The manager irrationally blamed the entire team for the mistake.
He irrationally refused to accept any help, even though he clearly needed it.

Synset Examples

they acted irrationally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rationally - in a logical or reasoned manner

Etymology

From 'irrational' + '-ly'; 'irrational' from Latin 'irrationalis' (not rational).
