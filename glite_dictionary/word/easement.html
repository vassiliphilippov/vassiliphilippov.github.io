easement

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːzmənt / (AmE), / ˈiːzmənt / (BrE)
(law) The legal right to use another person's land for a specific purpose, such as a right of way.
legal right to use another's land
legal

Examples

The neighbor granted us an easement so we can access the main road through their property.
A dispute arose after the city tried to remove a historic easement for a pedestrian walkway.
When buying land, always check if any easements are attached to the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right of way - the legal right to pass through land owned by another

Etymology

From Old French *aisement* (comfort, convenience), from *aiser* (to ease).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːzmənt / (AmE), / ˈiːzmənt / (BrE)
The act of making something unpleasant, such as pain or discomfort, less severe.
the alleviation of pain or discomfort

Examples

Meditation provided some easement from his persistent anxiety.
She sought easement of her pain through gentle stretching and rest.
The new medication promised easement of her chronic symptoms.

Synset Examples

he asked the nurse for relief from the constant pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - a feeling or act of reducing pain or discomfort
  • Antonym: aggravation - the act of making something worse, especially pain or an unpleasant feeling

Etymology

From Old French 'aisement' (comfort, convenience), from 'aiser' (to ease).
