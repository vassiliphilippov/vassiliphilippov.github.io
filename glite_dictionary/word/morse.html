Morse

1. NOUN

/ mɔrs / (AmE), / mɔːs / (BrE)
a system of representing letters and numbers using sequences of dots and dashes, used in telegraphy and signaling
system of code using dots and dashes

Examples

She learned how to send messages in Morse during her time in the Scouts.
Rescue workers used Morse to communicate when the radio failed.
The distress signal SOS is famous in Morse as three short, three long, and three short signals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Morse code - a telegraph code using dots and dashes to represent letters and numbers

Etymology

Named after Samuel Morse (1791–1872), American inventor.
