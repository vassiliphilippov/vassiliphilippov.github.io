Juncus articulatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkəs ˌɑːrtɪˈkjuːlətəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəs ˌɑːtɪˈkjuːlətəs / (BrE)
A species of rush native to wet areas, commonly known as the jointed rush, found in Australia and other regions.
species of rush (jointed rush)

Examples

The wet meadows were dotted with Juncus articulatus, thriving in the soft soil.
Botanists often study Juncus articulatus for its unique jointed stems.
Juncus articulatus can be found alongside streams and marshes in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Latin: 'juncus' meaning rush and 'articulatus' meaning jointed.
