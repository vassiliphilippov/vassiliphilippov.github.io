cupping

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌpɪŋ / (BrE)
a form of therapy in which heated or vacuum-sealed cups are applied to the skin to create suction, sometimes drawing blood to the surface
therapy using suction cups on skin
medical

Examples

Many athletes use cupping to help relieve muscle pain and aid recovery.
Traditional Chinese medicine often employs cupping as part of its healing practices.
The marks left by cupping are usually temporary but can be quite noticeable.

Etymology

From 'cup' + '-ing'; use for medical/suction therapy dates to at least the 17th century.
