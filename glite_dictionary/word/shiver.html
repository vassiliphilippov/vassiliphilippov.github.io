shiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪvər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
an involuntary trembling or shaking movement of the body, typically caused by cold, fear, or excitement
involuntary trembling or shaking

Examples

She felt a sudden shiver run down her spine as the door creaked open.
A cold shiver passed through the crowd when the announcement was made.
He tried to hide his shiver of excitement as he opened the envelope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremor - an involuntary quivering or shaking movement
  • Synonym: tremble - a slight involuntary shaking movement
  • Antonym: calm - a state free from motion or disturbance

Etymology

From Middle English 'shiveren' (to shiver, tremble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
a slight trembling or tingling sensation, especially caused by fear, excitement, or cold; an almost pleasurable sensation of fright
slight trembling sensation, often from fear or excitement

Examples

A sudden shiver ran down my spine when I heard the strange noise.
She felt a shiver of excitement as the curtain rose.
The story sent a pleasant shiver through the audience.

Synset Examples

a frisson of surprise shot through him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frisson - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or fear; a thrill

Etymology

From Middle English 'shiveren' (to break, shatter), related to Middle Dutch 'schiveren' (to split), root sense of trembling or breaking up.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07535351-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈʃɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
to shake slightly and uncontrollably, often because of cold, fear, or excitement
tremble from cold, fear, or excitement

Examples

She began to shiver uncontrollably in the icy wind.
The child would shiver at the sound of thunder during storms.
I shivered with excitement before going on stage for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety, excitement, or frailty
  • Synonym: shake - to move up and down or from side to side with small, quick movements

Etymology

From Middle English *shiveren* (to break, shatter), related to Middle Dutch *schivere* (splinter), influenced by the sense of trembling.

4. VERB

/ ˈʃɪvər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
to shake slightly and uncontrollably, typically because of cold, fear, or excitement
shake slightly from cold or fear

Examples

He began to shiver as the cold wind blew through the open window.
She tried to hide her fear, but she could not help but shiver.
The puppy would shiver every time it was left alone outside.

Synset Examples

The children are shivering — turn on the heat!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety, excitement, or frailty
  • Synonym: quiver - to tremble or shake with a slight rapid motion
  • Antonym: freeze - become hardened into ice or be very cold (opposite effect; not shaking but being still from cold)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *shiveren* or *shiveren*, related to Old English *sceofelian* (to tremble).
