gamut

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmət / (AmE), / ˈɡæmət / (BrE)
the complete range or scope of something
complete range or scope

Examples

The festival runs the gamut from folk music to classical performances.
Her emotions during the movie ran the gamut from joy to sorrow.
The new restaurant offers a gamut of dishes from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrum - a broad range of related qualities, ideas, or activities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'gamma ut', the lowest note of the medieval musical scale, later used to mean the entire scale.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.ət / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.ət / (BrE)
the complete scale of musical notes; the entire range of musical sounds
entire musical note range

Examples

The singer's voice covered the entire gamut of notes from the lowest to the highest.
Music students must learn to play scales that encompass the full gamut of octaves.
The piano was tuned to ensure that every note in the gamut was perfectly in pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: range - the extent between limits, especially of musical notes
  • Synonym: scale - an ordered sequence of musical notes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'gamma ut', referring to the lowest note in the medieval musical scale (gamma) and the syllable 'ut'.
