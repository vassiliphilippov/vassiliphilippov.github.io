to portray

1. VERB

/ pɔːrˈtreɪ / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to represent or play a particular role or character in a performance or artistic work
play or represent a character

Examples

She was chosen to portray the lead character in the new drama series.
In last night's play, he portrayed a famous historical figure.
Actors must deeply understand the person they portray to deliver an authentic performance.

Synset Examples

She impersonates Madonna
The actor portrays an elderly, lonely man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impersonate - to pretend to be another person as an entertainer or for fraud
  • Synonym: to depict - to represent or show in art or literature

Etymology

From Old French *portraire* (to depict), from Latin *portrahere* (to draw, to drag out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01727567-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɔːrˈtreɪ / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ / (BrE)
To describe or represent someone or something in words; to depict in speech or writing.
describe or represent in words

Examples

The biography portrays the politician as a complex and misunderstood figure.
Reviewers praised how the novel portrays rural life with honesty and compassion.
The documentary portrays the struggles of immigrants trying to build a new life.

Synset Examples

The book portrays the actor as a selfish person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depict - to represent or describe in words or pictures

Etymology

From Old French *portraire* (to portray), from Latin *portrahere* (to draw, drag forth), from *pro-* (forth) + *trahere* (to draw).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00990922-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɔˈtreɪ / (AmE), / pɔˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to represent or depict someone or something in a work of art such as a painting, drawing, or sculpture
represent in art

Examples

The artist chose to portray the queen as both regal and approachable.
Many ancient sculptures portray gods and heroes from mythology.
This mural portrays the struggles and hopes of the local community.

Synset Examples

The father is portrayed as a good-looking man in this painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depict - to represent by drawing, painting, or other art form
  • Synonym: to represent - to show or illustrate someone or something through art or imagery

Etymology

From Middle French *pourtraire* (to depict), from Latin *protractus*, past participle of *protrahere* (to draw forth, prolong).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

to create a picture or likeness of someone, especially by drawing, painting, or photographing
create a picture or likeness of someone

Examples

She chose to portray her sister in a soft, natural light.
Throughout his career, the photographer portrayed many famous authors.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depict - to represent or show someone or something in a picture or story

From Anglo-Norman *portraier* (to depict), from Latin *protrahere* (to draw forth, bring out).
