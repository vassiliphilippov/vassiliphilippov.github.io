chit

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪt / (AmE), / tʃɪt / (BrE)
a dismissive or mildly contemptuous term for a young girl, especially one regarded as immature or disrespectful
contemptuous term for an immature girl
archaic

Examples

She dismissed the newcomer as just a chit who had no experience in real business.
In the old novel, Lady Carter called her a rude chit for interrupting the conversation.
The headmistress could not believe such a mere chit would challenge her authority.

Synset Examples

she was incensed that this chit of a girl should dare to make a fool of her in front of the class
she's a saucy chit

Etymology

Probably from Hindi *chiṭṭi* (note, letter), later used pejoratively in English.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪt / (AmE), / tʃɪt / (BrE)
a small note or printed slip, especially one recording a sum owed, as a bill in a restaurant
restaurant bill; small written slip

Examples

She handed the chit to the waiter when she was ready to pay.
The manager asked for the chit after finishing his meal.
Could you please bring me the chit so I can settle the bill?

Synset Examples

he asked the waiter for the check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check - a written order directing a bank to pay money; in American English, also used for 'bill' in a restaurant
  • Synonym: bill - a printed or written statement of the money owed for goods or services

Etymology

From Hindi 'chiṭṭhi' (letter, note), ultimately from Sanskrit 'ciṭṭa' (written note).
