mycosterol

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkoʊˈstɛrɑl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkəʊˈstɛrɒl / (BrE)
a type of sterol compound found in fungi
sterol found in fungi
medical

Examples

Researchers isolated mycosterol from several samples of edible mushrooms.
The presence of mycosterol helps distinguish fungal cells from animal and plant cells.
Dietary supplements may contain purified mycosterol extracted from fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ergosterol - a predominant sterol in many fungi

Etymology

From Greek 'mykes' (fungus) and 'sterol' (a type of steroid alcohol).
