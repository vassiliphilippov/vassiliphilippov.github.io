reject

1. VERB

/ rɪˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to refuse to allow someone entrance, membership, or acceptance in a group or organization
refuse entrance or membership

Examples

The club decided to reject his application for membership due to lack of experience.
Several universities rejected her because she did not meet the entrance requirements.
Applicants without proper identification will be rejected at the door.

Synset Examples

They turned away hundreds of fans
Black people were often rejected by country clubs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to accept - to consent to allow someone into a group or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'reiectus', past participle of 'reicere' (to throw back, reject).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02508565-v
