hire

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
a person who has recently been employed by a company or organization
a newly employed person

Examples

The company introduced all the hires at the team meeting.
Our training program is designed for new hires to adapt quickly.
Three recent hires have backgrounds in computer science.

Synset Examples

the new hires need special training

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruit - a person who has recently joined an organization
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10189653-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈhaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.ə / (BrE)
to employ someone for a job or position, usually for pay
to employ someone for work

Examples

The company plans to hire more engineers next year to expand its development team.
She was hired as the new marketing coordinator after a series of interviews.
They are currently hiring seasonal workers for the busy holiday period.

Synset Examples

They hired two new secretaries in the department
How many people has she employed?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employ - to give work to someone and pay them for it
  • Antonym: fire - to dismiss someone from employment

Etymology

From Old English hyran (to hire, pay wages), related to Old High German hūren.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02414542-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to obtain the temporary use of goods or services by paying for them under a lease or rental agreement
obtain use by paying rent

Examples

We decided to hire a car for our holiday in Spain.
You can hire bicycles from the shop next to the station.
It is common to hire extra equipment for big events rather than buying it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to pay for the use of something (especially property or equipment)

Etymology

From Old English hyran (to hire, lease) via Proto-Germanic *hūzōną.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02466042-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to pay someone to work for you or to use something for a period of time under a contract
employ or rent for a period

Examples

We decided to hire a car for our trip around the island.
The company needs to hire more engineers this year.
She was hired as a consultant for six months.

Synset Examples

We took an apartment on a quiet street
Let's rent a car
Shall we take a guide in Rome?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - pay to use something for a period of time
  • Synonym: employ - give someone a paid job
  • Antonym: fire - dismiss from employment

Etymology

From Old English *hȳrian* (to hire, pay for), related to Old Norse *hӯra* (rent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02213319-v
Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ haɪɚ / (AmE), / haɪə / (BrE)
the act of paying to use something or employ someone for a period of time
the act of hiring something or someone
british

Examples

We arranged a hire car for our trip to Scotland.
The company charges a daily fee for equipment hire.
She found temporary work through an agency that specializes in short-term hire.

Synset Examples

he signed up for a week's car hire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rental - the act of renting something

Etymology

From Old English hȳrian (to hire, employ).
