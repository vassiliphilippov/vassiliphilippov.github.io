enlightened

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlaɪtnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtnd / (BrE)
having or showing a full understanding of a situation or problem; showing insight or awareness.
showing understanding or insight

Examples

The new policy reflects a more enlightened approach to education.
She appreciated working for such an enlightened employer.
An enlightened leader listens to the concerns of the people.

Synset Examples

an educated guess
an enlightened electorate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Synonym: informed - having knowledge of a subject

Etymology

From Enlighten + -ed, ultimately from Old English 'inlīhtan' (to illumine, instruct).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlaɪtənd / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪt(ə)nd / (BrE)
having or showing a rational, informed, and spiritual outlook; possessing knowledge and insight, especially of a spiritual nature.
having knowledge or spiritual insight

Examples

After many years of study, she felt truly enlightened by the teachings of her faith.
The country adopted more enlightened policies to improve education and equality.
He spoke with an enlightened perspective on the nature of consciousness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Synonym: informed - having or showing knowledge

Etymology

From Middle English 'enlighten', from en- (make) + lighten (to give light).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00887249-a
Synset POS: a
