biogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈdʒɛn.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈdʒɛn.ɪs / (BrE)
the production of a chemical compound by a living organism
production by a living organism

Examples

The biogenesis of hormones is a complex process involving many enzymes.
Researchers study the biogenesis of lipids to understand cell membrane formation.
A disruption in mitochondrial biogenesis can lead to various diseases.

Etymology

From Greek bio- (life) + genesis (origin or creation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈdʒɛn.ɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈdʒen.ɪsɪs / (BrE)
the production of living organisms from other living organisms, rather than from nonliving matter
origin of life from existing life

Examples

Pasteur's experiments provided strong evidence for biogenesis over spontaneous generation.
The concept of biogenesis is central to modern theories of evolution and reproduction.
Many textbooks discuss the principle of biogenesis when explaining the origin of microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abiogenesis - the origin of living matter from nonliving matter

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
