accountable

1. ADJ

/ əˈkaʊn.tə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈkaʊn.tə.bəl / (BrE)
responsible for one's actions and required to explain or justify them, especially to an authority
responsible and required to justify actions

Examples

All elected officials are accountable to the public for their decisions.
As project manager, she is accountable for meeting all deadlines.
Employees are accountable for any equipment they sign out.

Synset Examples

governments must be accountable to someone beside themselves
fully accountable for what they did
the court held the parents answerable for their minor child's acts of vandalism
he was answerable to no one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: answerable - required to explain or justify actions to someone
  • Synonym: responsible - having a duty to deal with something or being in charge of someone
  • Antonym: unaccountable - not required or not able to explain or justify actions

Etymology

From Old French 'acontable', from 'aconter' (to account), influenced by 'account'.
