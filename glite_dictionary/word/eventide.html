eventide

1. NOUN

/ ˈivənˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈiːvənˌtaɪd / (BrE)
the period of the evening when daylight fades and night begins; the latter part of the day
the latter part of the day, evening
archaic

Examples

At eventide, the sky glowed with a golden light as the sun set.
The old church bells rang out softly in the quiet eventide.
She often took solitary walks through the fields at eventide, enjoying the peace.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed the evening light across the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening - the period of time at the end of the day, usually from about 6 p.m. to bedtime
  • Degree Higher: dusk - the time when the sun has set and it is getting dark, just before night

Etymology

From Old English æfentīd (evening time), from æfen (evening) + tīd (time).
