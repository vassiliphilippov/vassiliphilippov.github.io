Vitis rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtɪs roʊˌtʌndəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈviːtɪs rəʊˌtʌndɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of grape native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as muscadine, and the origin of many cultivated grape varieties.
species of grape native to southeastern U.S.

Examples

The southeastern United States is home to Vitis rotundifolia, an important grape species in viticulture.
Many wine producers in the region grow Vitis rotundifolia for its sweet, hearty fruit.
Scientists have studied Vitis rotundifolia for its resistance to common grapevine diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscadine - common name for Vitis rotundifolia grape

Etymology

From Latin: 'vitis' meaning 'vine' and 'rotundifolia' meaning 'round-leaved'.
