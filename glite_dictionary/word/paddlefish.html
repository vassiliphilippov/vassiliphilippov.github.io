paddlefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpæd(ə)lˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish of North America, especially the Mississippi valley, with a long paddle-shaped snout, belonging to the family Polyodontidae.
primitive freshwater fish with a paddle-shaped snout

Examples

The paddlefish is notable for its unusually long, flat snout resembling a paddle.
Paddlefish populations have declined due to overfishing and habitat loss in the Mississippi River.
Scientists study the paddlefish to learn about the evolution of ancient freshwater fish.

Etymology

From 'paddle' (due to its snout shape) + 'fish'.
