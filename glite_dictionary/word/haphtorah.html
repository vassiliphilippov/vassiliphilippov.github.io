Haphtorah

1. NOUN

/ hɑfˈtoʊrə / (AmE), / hɑːfˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
A short reading from the books of the Prophets, recited in Jewish synagogue services on the Sabbath and certain festivals, following the Torah reading.
reading from Prophets after Torah in synagogue

Examples

After the Torah was read, the rabbi chanted the Haphtorah portion for the congregation.
The Haphtorah reading varies according to the week and specific Jewish holiday.
She was honored with the responsibility of reciting the Haphtorah at her bat mitzvah.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'haftarah' (conclusion, dismissal), from 'hifdir' (he dismissed, concluded).
