Monotropa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈtroʊpə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈtrəʊpə / (BrE)
A genus of leafless, fleshy, saprophytic plants, sometimes classified in the family Pyrolaceae.
genus of leafless saprophytic plants

Examples

The ghostly white Monotropa is often found in dark forest understories.
Scientists studied the unique saprophytic lifestyle of Monotropa during their research.
Unlike most plants, Monotropa lacks chlorophyll and relies on fungi for nutrients.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'tropos' (turn), referring to the plant's single-flowered stems.
