ferrocerium

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛroʊˈsɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊˈsɪəriəm / (BrE)
a pyrophoric alloy of iron and cerium, commonly used to produce sparks in lighter flints
a spark-producing iron-cerium alloy

Examples

Modern lighter flints are usually made from ferrocerium because of its ability to easily produce sparks.
The survival kit included a ferrocerium rod for starting fires in an emergency.
When you strike ferrocerium with a hard surface, it emits hot sparks ideal for ignition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flint - a hard stone that produces sparks when struck; synthetic types are used in lighters

Etymology

From Latin 'ferrum' (iron) and 'cerium' (the element cerium), referring to its composition.
