to appoint

1. VERB

/ əˈpɔɪnt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to furnish or equip (a room or building) with the necessary or desired items, especially in a particular style or manner
furnish or equip (a building or room)

Examples

The hotel was elegantly appointed with modern amenities and comfortable furnishings.
Their new apartment was beautifully appointed with stylish furniture and artwork.
A well-appointed office can make a strong impression on clients.

Synset Examples

a beautifully appointed house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to furnish - to provide (a room or building) with furniture or equipment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02346366-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈpɔɪnt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to assign a person to a role, position, or responsibility officially
assign officially to a position

Examples

The board will appoint a new director next month.
She was appointed to lead the research committee.
A panel of experts is appointed annually to review the applications.

Synset Examples

nominate a committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nominate - to formally propose someone for a role or honor
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a position

Etymology

From Old French *apointer* (to arrange), from Late Latin *ad* (to) + *punctum* (point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02401347-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈpɔɪnt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to officially choose someone for a job, duty, or responsibility
assign to a position or duty

Examples

The board will appoint a new director next month.
She was appointed as the project leader due to her expertise.
It is the president's role to appoint ambassadors to other countries.

Synset Examples

He was appointed deputy manager
She was charged with supervising the creation of a concordance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a particular job or responsibility
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job or duty

Etymology

From Old French 'apointier' (arrange, settle), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'punctum' (point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02481345-v
Synset POS: v
