thrombolysis

1. NOUN

/ θrɑːmˈbɑːləsɪs / (AmE), / θrɒmˈbɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the dissolution or breaking down of blood clots, especially by medical treatment
medical breakdown of blood clots
medical

Examples

The patient underwent thrombolysis to dissolve the blockage in his artery.
Early thrombolysis can significantly improve outcomes after a stroke.
Doctors discussed the risks and benefits of thrombolysis with the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrinolysis - the enzymatic breakdown of the fibrin in blood clots

Etymology

From Greek thrombos (clot) + lysis (loosening, dissolution).
