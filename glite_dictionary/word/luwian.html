Luwian

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈluː.i.ən / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language closely related to Lycian, once spoken in ancient Turkey
extinct Anatolian language

Examples

Archaeologists discovered inscriptions in Luwian at several ancient Anatolian sites.
The Luwian language was written in both cuneiform and hieroglyphic scripts.
Scholars study Luwian to learn more about the history of the Hittite Empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycian - an extinct Anatolian language related to Luwian

Etymology

Named after the Luwians, an ancient Anatolian people; related to Hittite and Lycian languages.
