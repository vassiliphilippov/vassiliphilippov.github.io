Sparganium

1. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈɡeɪniəm / (AmE), / spɑːˈɡeɪniəm / (BrE)
A genus of marsh or aquatic herbs, commonly known as bur-reeds, found in temperate regions.
genus of marsh or aquatic plants

Examples

Sparganium thrives in shallow ponds and slow-moving streams across much of the northern hemisphere.
Wetland restoration projects often include the planting of Sparganium to improve ecosystem health.
The distinctive spherical seed heads of Sparganium make it easy to identify among other aquatic plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'sparganium', from Greek 'sparganion', a plant name.

2. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈɡeɪniəm / (AmE), / spɑːˈɡæniəm / (BrE)
A genus of marsh or aquatic herbs found in temperate regions, and the only genus in the family Sparganiaceae.
genus of temperate marsh herbs

Examples

Several species of Sparganium can be found growing along the edges of ponds and streams.
Researchers studied the distribution of Sparganium in various wetland habitats.
Sparganium plants are easily recognized by their elongated leaves and spherical flower clusters.

Etymology

From Greek *sparganion* (a swaddling band), possibly referring to the ribbon-like leaves of the plants.
