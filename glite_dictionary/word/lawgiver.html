lawgiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːˌɡɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːɡɪvə / (BrE)
a person who formulates or enacts laws, especially one regarded as the originator of a legal code or system
person who makes laws

Examples

Moses is often considered a great lawgiver in religious and historical texts.
The lawgiver introduced sweeping changes to the country's legal framework.
As a respected lawgiver, she helped establish fairer rules for her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws

Etymology

From law + giver, modeled on Latin legis lator ('law bringer').
