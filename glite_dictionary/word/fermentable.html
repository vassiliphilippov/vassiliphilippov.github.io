fermentable

1. ADJ

/ fərˈmɛntəbl̩ / (AmE), / fəˈmɛntəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of undergoing fermentation; able to be fermented.
able to be fermented

Examples

Most types of sugar are fermentable and can be used in brewing beer.
The researchers tested which fibers were fermentable by gut bacteria.
Only fermentable material is suitable for producing bioethanol.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonfermentable - not capable of being fermented

Etymology

From Latin 'fermentare' (to leaven, ferment) + '-able' (capable of).
