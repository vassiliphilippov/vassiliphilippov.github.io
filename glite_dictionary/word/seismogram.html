seismogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪzməˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzməɡræm / (BrE)
A graphical record produced by a seismograph that shows the intensity and duration of ground movements such as earthquakes.
graphical earthquake record

Examples

The seismogram revealed a sharp spike corresponding to the main shock of the earthquake.
Researchers analyzed the seismogram to estimate the magnitude and epicenter of the tremor.
A clear seismogram is essential for understanding the characteristics of seismic events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seismograph - an instrument that measures and records earth tremors

Etymology

From Greek seismos (earthquake) + -gram (something written or recorded).
