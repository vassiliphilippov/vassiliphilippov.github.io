uncultivable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌltɪˌveɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪvəbl / (BrE)
(of land) not suitable for cultivation or farming.
not able to be cultivated (land)

Examples

Government initiatives have failed to make the uncultivable soil productive for farmers.
Much of the desert region is considered uncultivable due to lack of water.
He purchased a large tract of rocky, uncultivable land outside the city.

Synset Examples

thickets of indigenous trees … on uncultivable land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cultivable - suitable for cultivation or farming

Etymology

From un- (not) + cultivable, from cultivate.
