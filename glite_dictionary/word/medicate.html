to medicate

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to treat a person or condition with medicine, especially as part of medical care
treat with medicine

Examples

The doctor decided to medicate the patient to reduce the fever.
Many people try to medicate themselves for minor ailments without consulting a doctor.
If symptoms persist, your veterinarian may medicate your pet accordingly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat - to give medical care to a person or condition

Etymology

From Latin medicatus, past participle of medicare (to heal, give medicine).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to impregnate or treat with a medicinal substance
treat with a medicinal substance
medical

Examples

The nurse will medicate the bandages before applying them to the patient's wound.
Some soaps are medicated to help treat acne and skin conditions.
These drops are specially medicated to relieve eye irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to medicate - to treat with medicine (give medicine to someone)
  • Synonym: dose - to administer a measured amount of medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'medicatus', past participle of 'medicari' (to heal, give medicine).
