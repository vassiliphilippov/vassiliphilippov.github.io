ersatz

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜrˌzɑts / (AmE), / ˈɜːzæts / (BrE)
(of a product) made or used as a substitute, typically of inferior quality to the original.
artificial and inferior substitute

Examples

During the war, people drank ersatz coffee made from roasted barley.
His apology sounded so ersatz that no one believed he was sincere.
Many travelers were disappointed by the ersatz charm of the reconstructed village.

Synset Examples

ersatz coffee
substitute coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic, not artificial or inferior

Etymology

From German 'Ersatz', meaning 'substitute' or 'replacement'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜr.sɑts / (AmE), / ˈɛː.zats / (BrE)
an artificial or inferior substitute or imitation
inferior substitute or imitation

Examples

Many considered the instant coffee to be an ersatz for the real thing.
During the war, people had to settle for ersatz goods due to shortages.
He was never satisfied with ersatz leather shoes, preferring genuine leather instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or serving in place of another
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic; actually being what it is claimed to be

Etymology

From German 'ersatz' meaning 'substitute' or 'replacement'.
