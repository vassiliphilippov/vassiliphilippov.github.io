halfback

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌbak / (BrE)
In football, the running back who plays in the offensive halfback position, typically responsible for carrying the ball on running plays.
offensive running back position in football

Examples

The team's halfback rushed for more than 100 yards in the game.
As a halfback, he must be quick and able to read the defense well.
The coach praised the halfback for his agility and vision on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: running back - player who primarily runs with the football
  • Degree Lower: fullback - offensive football player who lines up in the backfield, typically behind the quarterback

Etymology

Formed from 'half' and 'back', referring to the position halfway to the backfield in early football formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌbæk / (BrE)
(in American football) a player occupying a backfield position, typically smaller and faster than a fullback, often responsible for running with the ball.
American football backfield position
american

Examples

The team's halfback sprinted down the sideline to score the winning touchdown.
Our coach decided to start a rookie as halfback for the first game of the season.
His small stature and speed made him an ideal halfback in college football.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fullback - a backfield position in football, usually larger and more defensive
  • Synonym: running back - a generic term for backfield players who run with the ball

Etymology

From 'half' and 'back,' indicating the original placement halfway back from the line of scrimmage.
