Gabonese

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæbəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌɡabəˈniːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Gabon
person from Gabon

Examples

The Gabonese are proud of their rich cultural heritage.
A group of Gabonese students visited the capital on a school trip.
Many Gabonese have a deep connection with their natural environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gabon - a country on the west coast of Central Africa

Etymology

From Gabon (country in Central Africa) + -ese (denoting nationality).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɡæbəˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌɡabəˈniːz / (BrE)
relating to Gabon or its people
of Gabon or its people

Examples

The museum showcased several Gabonese artifacts from the 19th century.
She enjoys reading literature by Gabonese authors.
Our school welcomed a group of Gabonese exchange students this semester.

Synset Examples

Gabonese hills
Gabonese writers

Etymology

Formed from Gabon (country in West Africa) + -ese (denoting origin or relation).
