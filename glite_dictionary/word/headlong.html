headlong

1. ADV

/ ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlɒŋ / (BrE)
with the head leading or first, especially when moving quickly or without control
with the head first

Examples

The diver plunged headlong into the pool, barely making a splash.
She ran headlong down the stairs, eager to greet her friend at the door.
The cyclist fell headlong over the handlebars after hitting a rock.

Synset Examples

the runner slid headlong into third base

Etymology

From Middle English 'hedlong', from Old English 'heafodlong', meaning 'with the head forward'.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.lɒŋ / (BrE)
With great speed or force; in a rush, without pausing or planning.
at great speed; recklessly

Examples

The car sped headlong down the empty street, barely missing the corner.
She rushed headlong into the room without knocking.
They dove headlong into the icy water, eager to win the race.

Synset Examples

burst headlong through the gate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessly - without regard for danger or consequences
  • Antonym: cautiously - in a careful and well-considered manner

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English heafodlong (with the head first).
