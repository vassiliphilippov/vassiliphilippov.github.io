cercarial

1. ADJ

/ sərˈkɛəriəl / (AmE), / səˈkɑːrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a cercaria, the larval stage in the life cycle of certain parasitic flatworms.
relating to cercaria (larval flatworm)
medical

Examples

The patient's skin rash was caused by cercarial penetration during swimming.
Researchers observed cercarial movement under the microscope to study infection pathways.
Cases of cercarial dermatitis are common in regions with infested freshwater lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cercaria - the free-swimming larval stage of a parasitic flatworm

Etymology

From 'cercaria' (New Latin, from Greek 'kerkos' meaning 'tail') + '-al'.
