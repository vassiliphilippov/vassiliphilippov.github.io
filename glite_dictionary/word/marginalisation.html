marginalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrdʒənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdʒɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person or group becomes or is made to feel less important or excluded from the dominant social group or activity
process of being excluded or made less important
british

Examples

Activists are raising awareness about the marginalisation of minority communities in urban areas.
Educational reform aims to combat the marginalisation of certain subjects in the curriculum.
Economic shifts can lead to the marginalisation of traditional industries and their workers.

Synset Examples

the marginalization of the underclass
the marginalization of literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from taking part in something
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining or being combined into a whole, especially in society

Etymology

From 'marginalise' (British spelling), from 'margin' + '-al' + '-ise'; ultimately from Latin 'margo' (edge, border).
