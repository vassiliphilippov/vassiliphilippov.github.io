orderliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə.li.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being arranged or organized in a neat, regular, or proper way
state of being orderly or well-arranged

Examples

The orderliness of her office impressed all the visitors.
Maintaining orderliness in the classroom helps students focus.
His attention to orderliness is evident in every aspect of his work.

Synset Examples

he put his desk in order
the machine is now in working order

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disorderliness - lack of regular or proper arrangement; a messy state

Etymology

From 'orderly' + '-ness', with 'orderly' from Latin 'ordo' (row, series).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdərliˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəliːnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being orderly and organized; an appreciation for method and system
quality of being orderly or organized

Examples

Her orderliness impressed her colleagues, as every document was perfectly filed and labeled.
A sense of orderliness in the workplace can reduce stress and increase productivity.
Children can benefit from routines that instill orderliness in their daily activities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disorderliness - the quality of being messy or lacking arrangement
  • Synonym: neatness - the quality of being neat, tidy, and clean

Etymology

From 'orderly' + '-ness', with 'orderly' deriving from Middle English 'ordren' (to arrange) and Latin 'ordo' (order).
