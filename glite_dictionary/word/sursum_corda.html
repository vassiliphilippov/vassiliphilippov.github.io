sursum corda

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrsəm ˈkɔrdə / (AmE), / ˌsɜːsəm ˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
(in Christian liturgy) a Latin phrase meaning ‘lift up your hearts’, used as an invitation to the congregation at the beginning of the Eucharistic prayer.
liturgical call: lift up your hearts

Examples

The priest began the Eucharistic prayer with the words sursum corda.
During Mass, the congregation responded after the sursum corda was proclaimed.
The phrase sursum corda reminds worshippers to focus their hearts on spiritual matters.

Etymology

Latin, literally ‘lift up (your) hearts’.
