collectivization

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɛktɪvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌlɛktɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the organization of a society or economy in which property and means of production are owned collectively, typically by the state or community, rather than by individuals
system of organizing collective ownership

Examples

The rapid collectivization of agriculture fundamentally changed rural life in the Soviet Union.
Government policies promoted the collectivization of industry and farmland in the 20th century.
Debate continues over the long-term effects of collectivization on both the economy and social structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual in it
  • Antonym: individualization - the process of making individual or distinct, or organizing based on individuals rather than groups

Etymology

From 'collective' + '-ization', modeled after Russian kollektivizatsiya.
