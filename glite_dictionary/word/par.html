par

1. NOUN

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
(in golf) the number of strokes a skilled golfer is expected to take to complete a hole or course.
standard number of golf strokes

Examples

He finished the round two strokes under par, which was quite an achievement.
This hole is a tough par 4, especially on windy days.
She made par on the last three holes to win the tournament.

Synset Examples

a par-5 hole
par for this course is 72

Etymology

From Latin par (equal). Adopted into golf terminology in the late 19th century.

2. VERB

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
(in golf) to achieve a score on a hole that is equal to the par for that hole.
score par in golf

Examples

She managed to par each of the last three holes to finish the round.
If you par this hole, you'll keep your overall score even.
He parred the ninth after a brilliant approach shot.

Etymology

From Latin par (equal), adopted into golf terminology in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01086742-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
the state of being equal to or comparable with another, especially in value, status, or quality
state of equality or equivalence

Examples

Her performance was on a par with the best in the competition.
The company's safety standards are on par with international regulations.
This year's profits are on a par with those from last year.

Synset Examples

on a par with the best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal - having the same value or amount
  • Antonym: superior - of higher quality or rank
  • Antonym: inferior - of lower quality or rank

Etymology

From Latin 'par' (equal).
