relieved

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈlivd / (AmE), / rɪˈliːvd / (BrE)
(of pain or sorrow) made less severe or easier to bear; experiencing a reduction in discomfort or distress.
made less painful or distressing

Examples

After taking the medicine, I felt much more relieved from the headache.
She was relieved when the pain in her knee finally subsided.
The family's sorrow was somewhat relieved by the support from friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soothed - (of pain or emotion) calmed or eased
  • Synonym: alleviated - (of suffering or a problem) made less severe
  • Antonym: intensified - (of pain, feeling, etc.) made stronger or more severe

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'relieve,' from Old French 'relever', from Latin 'relevare' (to raise, lighten).
