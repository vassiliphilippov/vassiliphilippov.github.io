actiniarian

1. NOUN

/ ækˌtɪniˈɛriən / (AmE), / ækˌtɪnɪˈɛəriən / (BrE)
any sea anemone or an animal related to sea anemones, typically belonging to the order Actiniaria
sea anemone or related animal

Examples

Some actiniarians form symbiotic relationships with clownfish in coral reefs.
The researcher observed several species of actiniarian along the rocky shoreline.
Unlike corals, actiniarians do not produce calcium carbonate skeletons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - a marine animal of the order Actiniaria, with a columnar body and tentacles

Etymology

From modern Latin Actiniaria (order of sea anemones), from Greek aktis, aktin- (ray, beam).
