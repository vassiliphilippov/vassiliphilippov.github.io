nonrestrictive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.rɪˈstrɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈstrɪktɪv / (BrE)
(of a clause, phrase, or modifier) not restricting or limiting the meaning of the word it modifies, but simply adding extra information.
not limiting; adds extra information (linguistics)

Examples

In the sentence 'My car, which is red, runs well,' the nonrestrictive clause gives extra information.
The teacher explained the difference between nonrestrictive and restrictive relative clauses to the class.
A comma is used to set off a nonrestrictive modifier from the rest of the sentence.

Synset Examples

the nonrestrictive clause in ‘I always buy his books, which have influenced me greatly’ refers to his books generally and adds an additional fact about them

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restrictive - (of a clause or modifier) limiting the meaning of the word it modifies

Etymology

From non- (not) + restrictive, meaning not serving to restrict.
