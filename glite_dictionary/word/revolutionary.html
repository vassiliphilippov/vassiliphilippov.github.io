revolutionary

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
involving or supporting a complete and dramatic change, especially in politics or society; advocating or engaged in revolution
advocating or involved in revolution

Examples

The revolutionary group called for the overthrow of the government.
His revolutionary ideas inspired thousands to join the movement.
During the uprising, revolutionary forces gained control of the capital.

Synset Examples

revolutionary pamphlets
a revolutionary junta

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - favoring extreme change or reform, especially in politics
  • Antonym: reactionary - opposing political or social progress or reform

Etymology

From French révolutionnaire, from révolution (revolution).

The declaration marked a revolutionary moment in the nation's history.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - favoring thorough or complete change; extreme
  • Antonym: conservative - holding to traditional attitudes; resistant to change, especially abrupt change

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈljuːʃənəri / (BrE)
a person who actively supports or participates in a political or social revolution
supporter of revolution

Examples

Che Guevara is often celebrated as a revolutionary who fought for social change in Latin America.
The young revolutionary distributed pamphlets calling for an end to the monarchy.
Many famous revolutionaries have inspired people to challenge injustice throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: reformer - a person who advocates gradual changes to improve a system
  • Synonym: insurgent - a person who rises in revolt against authority, especially one not recognized as a lawful combatant
  • Antonym: counterrevolutionary - a person who opposes a revolution or revolutionary government

Etymology

From French révolutionnaire, from révolution (revolution) + -aire (-ary).

Synset POS: n
