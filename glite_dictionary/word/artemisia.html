artemisia

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmiː.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmiː.zi.ə / (BrE)
any of a genus of aromatic shrubs or herbs, such as wormwood and sagebrush, with green or greyish foliage
aromatic plant of genus Artemisia

Examples

The herbal tea was made from dried artemisia leaves known for their medicinal properties.
Deserts in North America are often covered with sagebrush, a type of artemisia.
Botanists studied several species of artemisia growing in the mountain meadows.

Etymology

From Latin Artemisia, from Greek Artemisía, named after the Greek goddess Artemis or possibly Queen Artemisia.
