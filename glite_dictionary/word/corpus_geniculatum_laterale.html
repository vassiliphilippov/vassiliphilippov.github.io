corpus geniculatum laterale

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrpəs dʒəˌnɪkjəˈleɪtəm ləˈtɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpəs dʒɪˌnɪkjʊˈleɪtəm læˈtɪərəli / (BrE)
a structure in the thalamus of the brain that acts as a relay station for visual information from the retina to the occipital lobe of the cerebral cortex
neural processing station for vision in the brain
medical

Examples

The corpus geniculatum laterale plays a crucial role in visual signal processing.
Scientists studied the structure and function of the corpus geniculatum laterale in primates.
Damage to the corpus geniculatum laterale can result in visual field deficits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lateral geniculate body - another name for corpus geniculatum laterale

Etymology

From Latin: 'corpus' (body), 'geniculatum' (bent like a knee), 'laterale' (lateral, at the side).
