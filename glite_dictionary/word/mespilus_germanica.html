Mespilus germanica

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛspɪləs dʒɚˈmænɪkə / (AmE), / ˈmɛspɪləs dʒɜːˈmanɪkə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Eurasia, cultivated for its edible fruit which resembles crab apples; also known as the medlar.
eurasian fruit tree; the medlar

Examples

The Mespilus germanica tree produces unusual fruit that ripen in late autumn.
Gardeners value Mespilus germanica for both its attractive blossoms and edible medlars.
Historically, Mespilus germanica was cultivated in Europe for its fruit and ornamental qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medlar - the edible fruit of Mespilus germanica or the tree itself

Etymology

From Latin *Mespilus* (medlar) and *germanica* (German/related to Germany).
