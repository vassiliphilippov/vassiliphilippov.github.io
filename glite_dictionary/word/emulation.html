emulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the effort or ambition to match or surpass another, usually by imitation
effort to match or surpass another

Examples

Her success was born of emulation rather than envy.
He pursued his goals with a spirit of emulation, always seeking to surpass his peers.
The fierce emulation between the two artists led to groundbreaking work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something
  • Synonym: rivalry - competition between people or groups

Etymology

From Latin 'aemulatio', from 'aemulari' (to rival).

Examples

His emulation was clear in every attempt to surpass his colleagues.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Computing) The technique by which one computer system imitates or replicates the functions of another, enabling the user to run software or use features originally designed for a different system.
computer system imitation technique

Examples

The gaming console supports emulation of classic arcade systems, letting users play older titles.
With software emulation, a Windows program can run on a Mac computer without modification.
Developers rely on emulation to test apps on different operating systems before release.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simulation - the imitation of a situation or process, often for training or testing

Etymology

From Latin emulatio (rivalry, imitation), from emulari (to strive to equal, emulate).
