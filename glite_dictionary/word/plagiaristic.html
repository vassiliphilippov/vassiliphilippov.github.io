plagiaristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpleɪdʒiəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpleɪdʒɪəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
copied from someone else's work and presented as if it were your own; relating to plagiarism
relating to or involving plagiarism

Examples

The essay was filled with plagiaristic passages that matched published articles verbatim.
Her plagiaristic tendencies eventually led to disciplinary action by the university.
Critics accused the artist of producing plagiaristic work lacking original ideas.

Synset Examples

used plagiarized data in his thesis
a work dotted with plagiarized phrases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarized - copied from someone else and passed off as original

Etymology

From 'plagiarism' + '-istic' (forming adjectives meaning ‘of or relating to’).
