bummer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌmər / (AmE), / ˈbʌmə / (BrE)
An experience that causes disappointment, frustration, or irritation.
disappointing or frustrating experience
informal american

Examples

Missing the train was a real bummer for everyone.
It's a bummer that the concert was cancelled due to rain.
Sorry to hear about your lost keys—that's such a bummer.

Synset Examples

having to stand in line so long was a real bummer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letdown - a disappointment or something unsatisfactory
  • Antonym: blessing - a thing conducive to happiness or welfare

Etymology

Originally US slang (mid-20th century), from 'bum' (vagrant) with the suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌmə / (BrE)
A bad experience or unpleasant reaction resulting from the use of a hallucinogenic drug.
bad drug reaction (slang)
slang

Examples

He described his acid trip as a total bummer that left him anxious and paranoid for hours.
I'm glad I never had a bummer while experimenting in college.
Some users fear taking LSD because they might experience a bummer instead of the euphoria they expect.
