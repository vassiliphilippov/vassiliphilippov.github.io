Samuel F. B. Morse

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæmjəl ɛf bi ˈmɔrs / (AmE), / ˈsæmjʊəl ɛf bi ˈmɔːs / (BrE)
(1791–1872) American portrait painter and inventor who patented the telegraph and developed the Morse code.
American inventor of the telegraph and Morse code

Examples

Samuel F. B. Morse revolutionized communication with his invention of the telegraph.
Many museums feature paintings by Samuel F. B. Morse, who was also an accomplished artist.
The development of Morse code by Samuel F. B. Morse had a profound impact on global communication.
