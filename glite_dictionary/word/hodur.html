Hodur

1. PROPN

/ ˈhoʊ.dʊr / (AmE), / ˈhɒd.ʊə / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, a blind god who was misled by Loki into killing his brother Balder by throwing a shaft of mistletoe.
blind Norse god who killed his brother Balder

Examples

According to legend, Hodur was tricked by Loki into killing Balder.
Many stories in Norse mythology feature the tragic fate of Hodur.
Loki's deception led Hodur to throw a mistletoe dart at his brother.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Balder - the brother of Hodur, slain in Norse mythology
  • Synonym: Loki - the trickster god who deceived Hodur

Etymology

From Old Norse Höðr, meaning 'warrior' or 'battle.'
