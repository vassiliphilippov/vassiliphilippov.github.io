Cibotium barometz

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈboʊtiəm bəˈrɒmɛts / (AmE), / sɪˈbəʊtiəm ˈbærəmɛts / (BrE)
An Asiatic tree fern with dense, matted hairs that are sometimes used as a styptic.
species of Asiatic tree fern

Examples

The dense matted hair from the Cibotium barometz was traditionally used in folk medicine.
Botanists studied the unique properties of Cibotium barometz in its natural habitat in Southeast Asia.
Some herbal remedies include fibers harvested from the Cibotium barometz fern.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Cibotium and species name barometz, possibly from Russian or Mongolian folk legends.
