Philippians

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪpiənz / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪpiənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, being an epistle from Saint Paul to the church at Philippi in Macedonia
New Testament epistle of Paul to the Philippians

Examples

Many Christians find inspiration in the teachings of Philippians.
The pastor preached from Philippians during the Sunday service.
She cited a verse from Philippians to comfort her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephesians - New Testament epistle of Paul to the Ephesians
  • Synonym: Colossians - New Testament epistle of Paul to the Colossians
  • Synonym: Galatians - New Testament epistle of Paul to the Galatians

Etymology

From the Greek 'Philippēsioi', inhabitants of Philippi, from 'Philippi', the city addressed in the letter.
