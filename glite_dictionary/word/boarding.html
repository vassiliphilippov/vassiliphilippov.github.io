boarding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A structure or barrier made from boards, especially used for enclosing, covering, or protecting an area.
structure or barrier made of boards

Examples

The construction site was surrounded by boarding to keep out unauthorized visitors.
Temporary boarding was set up to protect the storefront windows during renovations.
They removed the old boarding from the shed before repainting it.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of passengers and crew getting onto a ship, aircraft, or vehicle
the act of getting onto a ship or aircraft

Examples

Boarding will begin at Gate 10 in approximately ten minutes.
The cruise ship completed boarding well before its scheduled departure.
Please have your passport and boarding pass ready at the gate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disembarkation - the act of leaving a ship, aircraft, or vehicle
  • Synonym: embarkation - the act of going onto a ship or aircraft (especially at the start of a journey)

Etymology

From the verb 'board', meaning to get onto a vehicle, from Old French 'aborder' (to board a ship).
