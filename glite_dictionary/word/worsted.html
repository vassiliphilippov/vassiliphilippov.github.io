worsted

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːstɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊstɪd / (BrE)
A smooth woolen fabric with a hard textured surface and no nap, woven from long-staple worsted yarns.
smooth, hard woolen fabric

Examples

He wore a finely tailored worsted suit to the meeting.
The fabric store carries a wide selection of worsted materials in various colors.
Traditional men's suits are often made of worsted for their durability and smooth texture.

Synset Examples

he wore a worsted suit

Etymology

From Worstead, a village in Norfolk, England, historically known for manufacturing this fabric.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrstɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊstɪd / (BrE)
a tightly twisted yarn made from long-staple wool fibers, used for weaving and knitting
strong, smooth woolen yarn

Examples

She bought a skein of worsted to knit a warm winter scarf.
The tailor recommended using worsted for a smooth, professional finish.
Fine worsted fabrics are often used to make suits and uniforms.

Etymology

From Worstead, a village in Norfolk, England, historically known for its woolen industry.
