dishonorable

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɑnərəbəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbəl / (BrE)
Lacking honor or integrity; deserving shame or disgrace.
lacking honor or integrity

Examples

His dishonorable actions led to his resignation.
It would be dishonorable to break your promise.
She accused him of making a dishonorable suggestion.

Synset Examples

dishonorable in thought and deed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honorable - deserving of respect or honor
  • Synonym: shameful - deserving of shame; disgraceful

Etymology

From Old French deshonorable, from Latin honorabilis (worthy of honor) with the prefix dis- (expressing negation).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɑːnərəbəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbəl / (BrE)
deceptive, fraudulent, or lacking in honesty and moral principles; deserving of shame or disgrace.
deceptive; lacking honour or morality

Examples

Many considered his actions to be dishonorable and unworthy of his position.
Cheating on the exam was a dishonorable choice that disappointed his teachers.
Her dishonorable behavior cost her the trust of her friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honorable - deserving of honor; having moral integrity
  • Synonym: deceitful - behaving in a dishonest or misleading way
  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; intending to mislead or cheat

Etymology

From Latin 'dishonorabilis', from 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'honorabilis' (honourable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01225555-a
Synset POS: a
