barmy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈbɑːmi / (BrE)
silly, foolish, or slightly crazy; not acting in a sensible way, especially in an amusing or harmless manner
silly or crazy (informal)
informal british

Examples

He came up with another one of his barmy ideas that made everyone laugh.
Some people thought her plan was barmy, but she proved them wrong.
It sounds barmy to try swimming in the sea during winter, but he enjoys it.

Synset Examples

it used to drive my husband balmy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or extremely foolish
  • Antonym: sensible - acting with good sense or judgment

Etymology

Originally from 'barm', meaning the froth on fermenting liquor, suggesting lightness or lack of substance.
