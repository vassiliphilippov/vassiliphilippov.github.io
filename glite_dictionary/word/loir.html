loir

1. NOUN

/ lɔɪr / (AmE), / lɔɪə / (BrE)
A large European dormouse, especially the edible dormouse (Glis glis), known for its bushy tail and hibernating habits.
large European dormouse

Examples

The loir is found throughout much of southern and central Europe.
While hiking in France, we caught a glimpse of a rare loir nesting in a hollow oak tree.
Loirs hibernate for months during the winter, emerging in the spring to forage for fruit and nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormouse - small nocturnal mouse-like rodent

Etymology

From French 'loir', referring to the dormouse Glis glis.
