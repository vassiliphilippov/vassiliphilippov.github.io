Cinchona officinalis

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈkoʊnə ˌɒfɪsəˈnaɪlɪs / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə ˌɒfɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
A South American tree or shrub with large glossy leaves and cymes of fragrant flowers, cultivated for its medicinal bark which is the source of quinine.
South American tree, source of quinine

Examples

The bark of Cinchona officinalis is famous for containing quinine, an early treatment for malaria.
Many botanical gardens cultivate Cinchona officinalis due to its historical medicinal use.
The extraction of quinine from Cinchona officinalis was a crucial breakthrough in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinine - A bitter compound derived from cinchona bark, used to treat malaria

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Cinchona' as the genus (named after the Countess of Chinchón), and 'officinalis' meaning 'used in medicine'.
