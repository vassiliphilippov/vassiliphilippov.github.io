slaphappy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæpˌhæpi / (AmE), / ˈslæpˌhæpi / (BrE)
carefree and cheerfully irresponsible; behaving in a light-hearted or reckless way, often without regard for consequences
cheerfully irresponsible or carefree
informal

Examples

He grew increasingly slaphappy during his summer travels, ignoring all his work responsibilities.
The group’s slaphappy attitude made them popular at parties but unreliable at work.
Her slaphappy spending habits soon got her into debt.

Synset Examples

carefree with his money
freewheeling urban youths
had a harum-scarum youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from worries or responsibilities
  • Degree Higher: reckless - showing a lack of care or caution

Etymology

Originally used in boxing slang (1930s), from 'slap' + 'happy', referring to a state of dazed euphoria, later generalized.

2. ADJ

/ ˈslæpˌhæpi / (AmE), / ˈslæpˌhæpi / (BrE)
Dazed or mildly disoriented, as from repeated blows or extreme exhaustion; also, behaving in a silly or reckless manner.
dazed or silly, often from blows or exhaustion

Examples

After twelve hours on night shift, Mark felt completely slaphappy and could hardly string a sentence together.
The boxer became slaphappy after several hard rounds in the ring.
By the end of finals week, the students were so exhausted they were downright slaphappy.

Synset Examples

knocked silly by the impact
slaphappy with exhaustion
punch-drunk with love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch-drunk - dazed or confused from repeated blows, especially in boxing
  • Synonym: silly - having or showing a lack of common sense or judgment; foolish

Etymology

Formed from 'slap' (strike with an open hand) and 'happy', originally from boxing slang of the 1930s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00438938-s
Synset POS: s
