Latona

1. PROPN

/ ləˈtoʊnə / (AmE), / ləˈtəʊnə / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the mother of Apollo and Artemis (Diana), and partner of Jupiter (the Roman equivalent of Zeus); equivalent to Leto in Greek mythology.
mythological mother of Apollo and Artemis

Examples

According to legend, Latona wandered in search of a place to give birth to her children.
Temples dedicated to Latona can be found throughout ancient Roman territories.
The story of Latona and her children was a popular subject in Renaissance art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leto - Mother of Apollo and Artemis in Greek mythology; equivalent to Latona in Roman mythology

Etymology

From Latin Latona, name of a Roman goddess, counterpart of Greek Leto.
