appointee

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɔɪnˈti / (AmE), / əˌpɔɪnˈtiː / (BrE)
A person who has been officially appointed to a job or position.
person appointed to a position

Examples

The new appointee will start her job in June after completing the training program.
As the government's most recent appointee, he will oversee the project.
The company announced its latest appointee to the board of directors yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominee - a person who is proposed or formally entered for a position or office
  • Antonym: incumbent - a person currently holding an office or position

Etymology

From 'appoint' + '-ee', a suffix forming nouns meaning the person to whom an action is done, from French '-é', from Latin '-atus'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌpɔɪnˈti / (AmE), / əˌpɔɪnˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who has been officially appointed to a position or office
person officially appointed to a position

Examples

The new appointee will begin her term as ambassador next month.
Each appointee is required to complete a background check before assuming office.
As a political appointee, he was often in the public eye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position
  • Synonym: nominee - a person who is proposed or formally entered as a candidate

Etymology

From 'appoint' + '-ee', a suffix denoting someone who is the recipient of an action.
