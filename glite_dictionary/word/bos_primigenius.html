Bos primigenius

1. NOUN

/ boʊs praɪˌmɪdʒiˈniːəs / (AmE), / bɒs prɪˌmɪdʒɪˈniːəs / (BrE)
The large, recently extinct wild ox of Europe, also known as the aurochs, considered one of the ancestors of domestic cattle.
extinct European wild ox; ancestor of cattle

Examples

Fossil remains of the Bos primigenius have been found throughout Europe and Asia.
The Bos primigenius played a significant role in the domestication of modern cattle.
Ancient cave paintings often depict the powerful horns of the Bos primigenius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aurochs - another name for Bos primigenius, the extinct wild ox

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'primeval ox.'
