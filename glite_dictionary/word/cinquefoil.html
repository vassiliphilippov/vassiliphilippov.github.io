cinquefoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkfɔɪl / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Potentilla, typically having compound leaves and five-petaled flowers, common in temperate regions.
plant with five-petaled flowers, genus Potentilla

Examples

The garden was bordered by a row of blooming cinquefoil in early summer.
Medieval herbalists believed cinquefoil could be used to treat various ailments.
A bright yellow cinquefoil peeked out among the wild grasses along the trail.

Etymology

From Old French cinquefoil (five-leaved), from Latin quinque (five) + folium (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
an ornamental carving or design consisting of five arcs or lobes arranged in a circle, often found in Gothic architecture
ornamental design with five arcs

Examples

The cathedral's window features a beautiful cinquefoil at its apex.
Restorers carefully cleaned the stone cinquefoil above the entrance arch.
You can find a cinquefoil motif repeated throughout the Gothic chapel's tracery.

Etymology

From Old French 'cincfoille', from Latin 'quinque' (five) + 'folium' (leaf).
