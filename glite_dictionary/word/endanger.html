to endanger

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdeɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdeɪndʒə / (BrE)
to put someone or something at risk of harm or danger
put at risk; threaten with harm

Examples

Industrial waste can endanger the surrounding wildlife if not properly managed.
Smoking in bed could endanger everyone in the house.
Conservationists work hard to protect species that endanger of extinction.

Synset Examples

The pollution is endangering the crops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to threaten - to state one's intention to harm or put in danger

Etymology

From Old French 'endanger', from 'en-' (in) + 'danger' (danger).

WordNet ID: oewn-02702962-v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdeɪndʒər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdeɪndʒə / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a situation where there is a risk of harm, loss, or danger
put at risk or in danger

Examples

Smoking near gas can endanger everyone in the building.
The new highway could endanger several rare animal species in the forest.
You must not endanger your health by working too many hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jeopardize - to put at risk or in danger
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or danger

Etymology

From Old French 'endangerer', from en- (make) + danger (danger).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038191-v
Synset POS: v
