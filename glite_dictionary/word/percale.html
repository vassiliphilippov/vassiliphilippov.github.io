percale

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkeɪl / (AmE), / pəˈkɑːl / (BrE)
a fine, closely woven cotton fabric, often used for sheets and clothing
fine closely woven cotton fabric

Examples

The hotel room was outfitted with crisp percale sheets that felt cool to the touch.
She prefers percale pillowcases because of their smooth texture and durability.
After years of use, the percale fabric still maintained its strength and soft feel.

Etymology

From French 'percale', possibly from Persian 'pargāla' (rag).
