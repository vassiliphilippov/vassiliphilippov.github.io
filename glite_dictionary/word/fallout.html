fallout

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.aʊt / (BrE)
an adverse or unintended consequence of an event, especially one causing difficulties or problems
adverse consequence or side effect

Examples

The fallout from the company's bankruptcy affected thousands of employees and investors.
Politicians debated how to manage the fallout of the controversial policy change.
There was significant economic fallout after the rapid rise in oil prices.

Synset Examples

a strategy to contain the fallout from the accounting scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: repercussion - an unintended consequence, often unwelcome, of an event or action
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something

Etymology

Originally referring to radioactive particles falling after a nuclear explosion; extended to mean unintended consequences from the late 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlaʊt / (BrE)
radioactive particles that are carried into the atmosphere after a nuclear explosion and settle back to the ground
radioactive particles after a nuclear explosion

Examples

After the nuclear test, concerns grew about the fallout affecting nearby communities.
Residents were advised to stay indoors to avoid exposure to radioactive fallout.
The long-term environmental impact of nuclear fallout remains a topic of research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation - energy as electromagnetic waves or as moving subatomic particles
