pilferer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlfərər / (AmE), / ˈpɪlfərə / (BrE)
a person who steals small items or amounts, typically without using violence
someone who steals small items

Examples

The pilferer managed to sneak a wallet from the unattended bag.
Security caught a pilferer in the act of stealing candy from the shop.
Even a careful pilferer leaves clues behind for a diligent investigator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals, especially secretly or without open force
  • Antonym: robber - a person who takes property unlawfully, often using force or threat

Etymology

From Old French *pelfrer* (to plunder), related to 'pilfer'.
