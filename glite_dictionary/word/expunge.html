to expunge

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspʌndʒ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌndʒ / (BrE)
to remove or erase completely, especially something unwanted or objectionable
remove or erase completely

Examples

The judge ordered the clerk to expunge the statement from the official record.
She tried to expunge all traces of her old social media accounts.
He hoped to expunge his past mistakes from his memory.

Synset Examples

Please strike this remark from the record
scratch that remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - remove written or recorded marks
  • Synonym: to delete - remove or obliterate text, data, or record
  • Antonym: to retain - continue to have or keep something

Etymology

From Latin expungere (to prick out, erase), from ex- (out) + pungere (to prick).
