Pseudechis

1. NOUN

/ sjuːˈdiːkɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdiːkɪs / (BrE)
a genus of large, venomous blacksnakes native to Australia and New Guinea, known for their dark coloration and potent venom
genus of Australian venomous blacksnakes

Examples

The Pseudechis genus includes some of the largest venomous snakes in Australia.
Herpetologists have documented the defensive behavior of Pseudechis in the wild.
Bites from Pseudechis species can be dangerous and require prompt medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black snake - a common name for several species in the genus Pseudechis

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) and echis (viper), referring to resemblance to true vipers.

2. NOUN

/ suːˈdiːkɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdiːkɪs / (BrE)
a genus of venomous Australian blacksnakes in the family Elapidae, including several species such as the red-bellied black snake and the mulga snake.
genus of Australian blacksnakes

Examples

Several species in the genus Pseudechis are among the most venomous snakes in Australia.
The red-bellied black snake, a member of Pseudechis, is commonly found near water.
Researchers studied the ecology of Pseudechis in arid regions of northern Australia.

Etymology

From Greek pseudes (false) and echis (viper), referring to snakes resembling but not being true vipers.
