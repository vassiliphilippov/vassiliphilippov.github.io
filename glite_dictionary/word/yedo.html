Yedo

1. PROPN

/ ˈjeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈjiːdəʊ / (BrE)
an old name for Tokyo, the capital and largest city of Japan, especially used before the mid-19th century
former name for Tokyo, Japan

Examples

During the Edo period, Yedo grew rapidly in both population and influence.
Many Westerners visited Yedo in the nineteenth century and wrote about its culture.
The city's name was changed from Yedo to Tokyo in 1868.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tokyo - the capital city of Japan

Etymology

From Japanese 江戸 (Edo), meaning 'bay door' or 'estuary'.
