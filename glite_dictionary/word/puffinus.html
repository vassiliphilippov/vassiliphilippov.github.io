Puffinus

1. NOUN

/ pʌˈfaɪnəs / (AmE), / pʌˈfɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of seabirds in the family Procellariidae, commonly known as shearwaters, characterized by long wings and oceanic habits
genus of seabirds (shearwaters)

Examples

The genus Puffinus includes several species of medium-sized shearwaters found across the world's oceans.
Researchers observed large flocks of Puffinus during the annual migration period.
Detailed studies on Puffinus behavior have provided insights into the life of pelagic seabirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shearwater - a long-winged seabird in the family Procellariidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Latin puffinus (manx shearwater).

2. NOUN

/ pʌˈfaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈpʌfɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds known as shearwaters, characterized by long wings and oceanic habits.
genus of shearwater seabirds

Examples

The Puffinus genus includes several species of medium-sized seabirds commonly called shearwaters.
Researchers observed a large flock of Puffinus flying low over the waves at dawn.
Many Puffinus species undertake long migratory journeys across oceans each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shearwater - a long-winged seabird of the genus Puffinus or similar genera

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly an alteration of 'puffin' (an unrelated bird) or from Medieval Latin.
