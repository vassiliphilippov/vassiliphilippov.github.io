thoroughbred

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝː.oʊˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈθʌr.əʊˌbrɛd / (BrE)
A person of noble birth or upbringing; someone considered to be refined and of high social class.
person of noble or refined breeding

Examples

As a thoroughbred, she was accustomed to the manners of high society.
He behaved like a true thoroughbred throughout the entire ordeal.
Despite his wealth, he never quite felt like a genuine thoroughbred among the aristocrats.

Etymology

Originally from horse breeding, indicating purity of race; later applied to people as metaphor.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɜroʊˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈθʌrəʊˌbrɛd / (BrE)
A horse of a breed developed in England by crossing Arabian stallions with English mares, especially known for racing and endurance.
breed of racehorse

Examples

The thoroughbred galloped past the finish line with astonishing speed.
She has been training thoroughbreds for national competitions for over a decade.
Owning a champion thoroughbred is a dream for many horse racing enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racehorse - a horse bred or trained for racing

Etymology

From thorough + bred, referring to being bred thoroughly or purebred.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02385883-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈθɝː.oʊˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈθʌr.əʊˌbrɛd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having a recorded pedigree demonstrating pure breeding.
purebred with recorded pedigree

Examples

The kennel only accepts thoroughbred dogs for its breeding program.
To enter the competition, your horse must be a certified thoroughbred.
She prides herself on owning several thoroughbred cats, each with a detailed lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purebred - bred from parents of the same breed or variety
  • Antonym: mongrel - an animal resulting from the crossing of different breeds

Etymology

From 'thorough' + 'bred,' originally applied to horses with proven ancestry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01910144-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːroʊˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈθʌr.ə.bred / (BrE)
An animal, especially a horse, of pure breed with a recorded pedigree.
a purebred animal, especially a horse

Examples

The Kentucky Derby features only thoroughbred horses from around the world.
He paid a high price for a thoroughbred with an impressive pedigree.
The farm specializes in breeding thoroughbred racehorses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purebred - an animal bred from parents of the same breed

Etymology

From 'thorough' meaning complete, and 'bred' meaning produced by breeding; referring to animals bred from pure lineage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01326240-n
Synset POS: n
