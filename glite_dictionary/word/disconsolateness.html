disconsolateness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɑnsələtnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɒnsələtnɪs / (BrE)
A state of being extremely sad and unable to be comforted; deep hopelessness or dejection.
state of extreme sadness or dejection

Examples

A sense of disconsolateness filled the room after the team lost the championship game.
Despite her friends' efforts, his disconsolateness persisted for weeks after the breakup.
He stared out the window in utter disconsolateness, unable to find comfort in anything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope
  • Synonym: dejection - a sad and depressed state; low spirits
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From the adjective 'disconsolate', from Late Latin 'disconsolatus' (inconsolable), from Latin 'dis-' (not) + 'consolari' (to console).
