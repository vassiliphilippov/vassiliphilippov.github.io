Euphorbia villosa

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbi.ə vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A species of perennial, herbaceous plant in the spurge family, native to Europe, known for its hairy leaves and stems.
hairy-leaved European spurge plant species

Examples

Euphorbia villosa thrives in woodland edges throughout central Europe.
Botanists admire Euphorbia villosa for its distinctively hairy foliage.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Euphorbia villosa in its natural habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *euphorbos* (a physician); species name from Latin *villosa* (hairy).
