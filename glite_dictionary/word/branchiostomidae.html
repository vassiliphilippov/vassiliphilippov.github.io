Branchiostomidae

1. NOUN

/ bræn.kiˌɑː.stəˈmɪ.daɪ / (AmE), / bræŋ.kɪ.ˌɒs.təˈmɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of small, elongated, fish-like marine animals known as lancelets, belonging to the subphylum Cephalochordata.
family of lancelets (marine animals)
medical

Examples

Members of the Branchiostomidae family are considered important for studies on vertebrate evolution.
The anatomy of Branchiostomidae helps illuminate the transition from invertebrates to vertebrates.
Researchers collected specimens from several species within the Branchiostomidae for genetic analysis.

Etymology

From the genus name Branchiostoma (Greek branchia 'gills' + stoma 'mouth') plus the zoological family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ brænkiˌɑːˈstɑːməˌdi / (AmE), / bræŋkɪˌɒstəˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, elongated marine animals known as lancelets, comprising most species of amphioxus.
family of lancelets (marine animals)
medical

Examples

Members of the Branchiostomidae are often studied to understand early vertebrate evolution.
The Branchiostomidae family includes several species of lancelets found in shallow seas worldwide.
In marine biology classes, students dissect specimens from the Branchiostomidae family to examine their anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lancelet - small, elongated marine invertebrate; also called amphioxus

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Branchiostoma ('gill mouth') and Greek roots.
