surmounter

1. NOUN

/ sɚˈmaʊntɚ / (AmE), / səˈmaʊntə / (BrE)
a person who overcomes difficulties or prevails over obstacles, often establishing control or dominance by force or persuasion
person who overcomes obstacles or difficulties

Examples

As a true surmounter, she faced every challenge with determination and grace.
Throughout history, many leaders have proven themselves to be surmounters of adversity.
He prided himself on being a surmounter, always finding a way to prevail over obstacles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcomer - a person who succeeds in dealing with or gaining control of a problem or difficulty
  • Antonym: to succumb - to fail to resist pressure, temptation, or some other negative force

Etymology

From the verb 'surmount,' which derives from Old French 'surmonter,' from Latin 'super' (over) + 'montare' (to mount, climb).
