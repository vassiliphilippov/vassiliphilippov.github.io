disorganisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state in which order and systematic arrangement are disrupted, leading to disorder and confusion.
disturbance of order; disorder

Examples

The sudden resignation of the manager led to disorganisation in the office.
After the storm, widespread disorganisation delayed the relief efforts.
The team's disorganisation was evident in their chaotic performance.

Synset Examples

the disorganization of the enemy troops by a flank attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - lack of order or organization
  • Antonym: organization - the arrangement of parts for a specific purpose

Etymology

From French désorganisation, from dés- (dis-) + organisation (organization).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The condition of being disordered or lacking organization; a state where an orderly system has been disrupted.
state of being disordered or unorganized
british

Examples

The sudden resignation of the manager led to complete disorganisation within the team.
Years of poor planning resulted in the disorganisation of the festival.
She was frustrated by the disorganisation she encountered at her new workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - a state of confusion or lack of order
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is in its correct or appropriate place

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + organisation, a variant of organization; chiefly British spelling.
