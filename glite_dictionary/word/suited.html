suited

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtɪd / (BrE)
wearing clothes that are supplied or planned for a specific purpose or situation
furnished with specific clothing

Examples

The new recruits were suited in their camouflage uniforms for the training exercise.
Everyone at the gala was suited in elegant attire, ready for the evening's festivities.
Before entering the laboratory, all visitors must be suited in protective gear.

Synset Examples

recruits suited in green

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothed - wearing clothes; covered with clothing
  • Synonym: outfitted - provided with a set of clothes, equipment, or gear
  • Antonym: bare - without clothing; uncovered

2. ADJ

/ ˈsuː.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈsuː.tɪd / (BrE)
Adapted or appropriate for a particular purpose, situation, or person.
adapted or appropriate for a use

Examples

This job is suited to someone with excellent communication skills.
She wore a dress suited for the formal dinner.
The movie isn't suited for young children because of its violent scenes.

Synset Examples

a tractor suitable (or fit) for heavy duty
not an appropriate (or fit) time for flippancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitable - right or appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation
  • Antonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From the verb 'suit', from Anglo-French 'siuter', 'suiter' (to follow, pursue), influenced by Old French 'suite'.
