Medinilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen shrubs with fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers, native to the Old World and grown as ornamentals.
tropical ornamental shrub genus

Examples

The gardener proudly displayed a new Medinilla plant in the greenhouse.
Many collectors appreciate the striking blooms of Medinilla species.
She watered her Medinilla each week to keep its leaves healthy and glossy.

Etymology

Named after José de Medinilla, Spanish governor of the Mariana Islands in the early 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmiː.dəˈnɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌmɛd.ɪˈnɪl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen shrubs native to the Old World, noted for their fleshy leaves and large panicles of pink or white flowers, often cultivated as ornamentals.
genus of tropical ornamental evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Medinilla is prized for its striking pink flower clusters and glossy foliage.
Botanical gardens often feature the Medinilla as an exotic ornamental plant.
Her new greenhouse is home to several rare Medinilla species from Southeast Asia.

Etymology

Named after J. de Medinilla, a Spanish governor of the Mariana Islands in the early 19th century.
