to individualise

1. VERB

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or recognize as an individual; to give distinctive character to something or someone
make or treat as individual
british

Examples

Teachers are encouraged to individualise instruction to meet the needs of different students.
Each trophy was carefully individualised with the recipient's name engraved on it.
The therapist tried to individualise her approach for every patient.

Synset Examples

The sounds were individualized by sharpness and tone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to individualize - to make or treat as individual (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to generalize - to make general or common

Etymology

From individual + -ise, modelled on French individualiser.

2. VERB

/ ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something personal or tailored to the needs of an individual
make personal or tailored
british

Examples

The school tried to individualise its curriculum for each student.
Therapists often individualise treatment plans to suit their clients' needs.
We aim to individualise our services so that every client feels valued.

Synset Examples

personalized service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to individualize - make something personal or more suited to one person; US spelling

Etymology

From individual + -ise, modelled after the American -ize ending.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00388736-v
Synset POS: v
