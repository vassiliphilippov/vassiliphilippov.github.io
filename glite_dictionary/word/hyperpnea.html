hyperpnea

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɜrˌpniə / (AmE), / hʌɪˈpniːə / (BrE)
a condition of breathing that is deeper and more rapid than normal, typically occurring after exercise or as a result of certain medical conditions
abnormally deep and rapid breathing
medical

Examples

After running the marathon, she experienced hyperpnea as her body tried to recover.
The doctor noted hyperpnea during the patient's fever, indicating a possible underlying infection.
Hyperpnea can sometimes be mistaken for anxiety-induced rapid breathing in clinical settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tachypnea - abnormally rapid breathing
  • Antonym: bradypnea - abnormally slow breathing
  • Antonym: eupnea - normal, unlabored breathing

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over) + pnoia (breath), from pnein (to breathe).
