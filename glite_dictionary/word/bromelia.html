Bromelia

1. NOUN

/ broʊˈmiːliə / (AmE), / brəʊˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American plants in the family Bromeliaceae, known for their deeply cleft calyx.
tropical American plant genus

Examples

The garden features several Bromelia species known for their striking foliage.
Researchers discovered a new Bromelia in the rainforests of Brazil.
Many Bromelia plants thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromeliad - any plant of the family Bromeliaceae

Etymology

From Latin *Bromelia*, named after Olof Bromelius, a Swedish botanist (1639–1705).
