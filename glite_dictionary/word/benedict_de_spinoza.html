Benedict de Spinoza

1. PROPN

/ bəˈnɛdɪkt də spɪˈnoʊzə / (AmE), / bəˈnɛdɪkt də spɪˈnəʊzə / (BrE)
(1632–1677) A Dutch philosopher known for advocating a pantheistic system and for his influential works in rationalist philosophy.
Dutch philosopher, pantheism advocate

Examples

Many scholars regard Benedict de Spinoza as a pioneer in rationalist philosophy.
The writings of Benedict de Spinoza have greatly influenced modern thinkers.
In his lifetime, Benedict de Spinoza was both admired and criticized for his radical ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spinozism - the philosophy of Spinoza
  • Degree Lower: René Descartes - French rationalist philosopher whose work influenced Spinoza
