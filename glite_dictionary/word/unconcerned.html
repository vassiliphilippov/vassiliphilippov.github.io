unconcerned

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈsɝːnd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
not involved with or interested in something; not worried or affected by something.
not involved or worried

Examples

He seemed unconcerned about the risks involved in his decision.
Many people remained unconcerned with the local politics.
She answered the question with an unconcerned shrug.

Synset Examples

readers unconcerned with style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or concern
  • Antonym: concerned - worried or interested; affected by something

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'concerned'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈsɝnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
not worried or anxious; showing a lack of interest or concern about something
not worried or showing no interest

Examples

She appeared unconcerned by the news of the approaching storm.
He strolled into the meeting unconcerned about being late.
Despite the chaos around him, James remained unconcerned and calm.

Synset Examples

the average American … is unconcerned that his or her plight is the result of a complex of personal and economic and governmental actions … beyond the normal citizen's comprehension and control
blithely unconcerned about his friend's plight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness; the opposite of unconcerned

Etymology

From un- (not) + concerned (past participle of concern).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈsɝnd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
not worried, anxious, or interested; showing a lack of concern or interest.
not worried or interested

Examples

She appeared unconcerned by the sudden change in plans and carried on working.
He remained unconcerned about the upcoming exam while everyone else was nervous.
Despite the chaos around him, the dog looked completely unconcerned and continued sleeping.

Synset Examples

the prisoner seems entirely unconcerned as to the outcome of the examination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: anxious - worried or concerned about something

Etymology

From un- (not) + concerned (from late Middle English, from concern, ultimately from Latin concernere).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02469327-s
Synset POS: s
