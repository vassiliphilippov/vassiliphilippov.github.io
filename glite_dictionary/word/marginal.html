marginal

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
located at or forming the border or edge of something.
at or near an edge or border

Examples

A narrow marginal path runs along the riverbank.
Only a marginal area of grass separated the forest from the fields.
The developer wants to build on the marginal land around the lake.

Synset Examples

the marginal strip of beach

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central - located at or forming the center; opposite of marginal location

Etymology

From Latin 'marginalis', from 'margo' meaning 'edge'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00332787-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a business or activity) producing just enough income to cover production costs, with little or no profit.
barely profitable; just covering costs

Examples

Many marginal industries shut down during the economic downturn.
Farmers operating marginal businesses often struggle to survive market changes.
The company remained marginal until demand for its products increased.

Synset Examples

marginal industries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profitable - making a profit; financially beneficial
  • Synonym: unprofitable - not yielding profit or financial gain

Etymology

From Latin 'margo' (edge, border), via medieval Latin 'marginalis' (on the margin or border).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01878454-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
Of questionable, limited, or minimal quality; barely acceptable or sufficient.
of minimal or questionable quality

Examples

Her performance in the class was considered marginal by the teacher.
The research produced only marginal results, raising doubts about its validity.
Because his skills were marginal, he struggled to keep up with the team.

Synset Examples

borderline grades
marginal writing ability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good
  • Antonym: excellent - of the highest quality

Etymology

From Latin 'margo' (edge, border) + English '-al' (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01499606-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
(of results, differences, or amounts) very small or barely sufficient; close to the limit of what is acceptable or possible.
barely sufficient; close to the limit

Examples

The team's marginal lead was enough to secure them a place in the finals.
He passed the exam with only a marginal difference above the minimum requirement.
The benefits of the new policy are marginal at best, according to some analysts.

Synset Examples

a bare majority
a marginal victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligible - so small or unimportant as to be not worth considering; insignificant
  • Antonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth

Etymology

From Latin 'marginalis', from 'margo' meaning 'edge, border'.
