Dentaria bulbifera

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɛəri.ə ˌbʌl.bɪˈfɪr.ə / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɛːrɪə ˌbʌlbɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A European species of bittercress with a knotted white rootstock, also known as bulbous toothwort.
a European bittercress plant species

Examples

The Dentaria bulbifera blooms in woodland areas during spring.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare specimen of Dentaria bulbifera in the forest.
The knotted rootstock of Dentaria bulbifera distinguishes it from other bittercress species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittercress - a group of plants in the genus Cardamine, closely related to Dentaria

Etymology

From Latin: Dentaria, from 'dens' (tooth), and bulbifera, from 'bulbus' (bulb) + 'ferre' (to bear).
