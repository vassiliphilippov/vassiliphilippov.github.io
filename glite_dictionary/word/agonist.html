agonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡ.ə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈæɡ.ə.nɪst / (BrE)
a substance, such as a drug, that binds to a receptor in a cell and triggers a physiological response
drug or substance that activates a receptor
medical

Examples

The new drug acts as an agonist by stimulating the same receptors as the natural hormone.
Opioid agonists are used to treat pain by activating opioid receptors in the brain.
Researchers compared the effects of the agonist to those of an antagonist in the experimental group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antagonist - a substance that interferes with or inhibits the physiological action of another

Etymology

From Greek agonistēs (contestant, champion), from agon (contest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ˈaɡənɪst / (BrE)
a person who is involved in a contest, conflict, or struggle, especially as a principal figure
person in a contest or struggle

Examples

The hero, as the main agonist, struggled valiantly against overwhelming odds.
In Greek drama, the agonist faces moral and physical challenges.
Each agonist in the tournament brought unique skills and fierce determination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antagonist - a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone; an adversary

Etymology

From Greek agonistēs (contestant), from agōn (contest or struggle).

3. NOUN

/ ˈæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ˈæɡənɪst / (BrE)
a muscle that contracts to produce a specific movement while another muscle relaxes
a muscle causing movement
medical

Examples

The biceps acts as the agonist during elbow flexion, while the triceps relaxes.
When you lift a glass, your forearm muscles serve as the agonist for the movement.
During a squat, the quadriceps function as the agonist muscles responsible for straightening the knee.

Synset Examples

when bending the elbow the biceps are the agonist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antagonist - a muscle that opposes the action of an agonist

Etymology

From Greek agōnistēs (combatant), from agōn (contest).
