desperation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of being driven by a lack of hope, often leading to reckless or extreme actions
reckless state from hopelessness

Examples

In his desperation, he took risks he would normally avoid.
The team's desperation was clear as they made a final attempt to win.
Sometimes desperation leads people to make unwise decisions.

Synset Examples

it was a policy of desperation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hope - a feeling of expectation and desire for a certain thing to happen
  • Synonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope

Etymology

From Latin desperatio (despair), from desperare (to lose hope).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of having lost all hope, leading someone to act rashly or without regard for consequences
extreme hopelessness; reckless despair

Examples

In his desperation, he agreed to anything that might solve his problems.
There was a look of desperation in her eyes as she reached out for help.
Driven by desperation, the team made a last-minute attempt to win the game.

Synset Examples

in the depths of despair
they were rescued from despair at the last minute
courage born of desperation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despair - complete loss of hope
  • Antonym: hope - a feeling of expectation and desire for something to happen

Etymology

From Latin desperatio (hopelessness), from desperare (to despair).
