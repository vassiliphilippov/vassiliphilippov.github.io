phalangist

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlæŋɡɪst / (AmE), / fəˈlæŋɡɪst / (BrE)
a member of the Falange, the Spanish Fascist political party founded by General Franco
member of the Spanish Falange party

Examples

The phalangist movement gained influence during the Spanish Civil War.
Several phalangists held key positions in Franco's government.
As a staunch phalangist, he supported strict nationalist policies.

Etymology

From Spanish 'falangista', from 'Falange' (the party's name), ultimately from Latin 'phalanx' (line of battle).
