conjecturally

1. ADV

/ kənˈdʒɛktʃɚəli / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃərəli / (BrE)
In a way that is based on conjecture or supposition rather than certain knowledge.
based on conjecture or supposition

Examples

His theory was conjecturally supported by the limited data available at the time.
The age of the ruins was conjecturally estimated by several archaeologists.
They arrived, conjecturally, at a solution to the puzzle before all the facts were known.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculatively - in a way based on guesswork or hypothesis rather than firm evidence

Etymology

From 'conjectural' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'conjectura' (interpretation, guess).
