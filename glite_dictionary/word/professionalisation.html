professionalisation

1. NOUN

/ prəˌfɛʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌfɛʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an activity or occupation becomes regarded as a profession and is carried out for pay or as a main livelihood, often with standards and qualifications.
process of making an activity a profession

Examples

The professionalisation of teaching has changed how teachers are trained and evaluated.
Many sports have undergone rapid professionalisation in the last century.
The professionalisation of journalism led to the development of ethical standards and training programs.

Synset Examples

the professionalization of American sports
the professionalization of warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: professionalization - alternative spelling, American English

Etymology

From 'professional' + '-isation'; based on French 'professionnalisation'.
