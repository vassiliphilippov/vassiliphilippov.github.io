containership

1. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪnɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnəˌʃɪp / (BrE)
A large cargo ship designed to transport standardized shipping containers.
cargo ship for containers

Examples

The new containership can carry up to 20,000 containers at once.
Ports around the world are being upgraded to handle larger containerships.
A containership was delayed due to rough seas in the Pacific Ocean.

Synset Examples

the weight of the documentation of all the consignments on board a contemporary container ship can exceed 90 pounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: container ship - a cargo ship designed to carry containers

Etymology

From 'container' + 'ship'; related to the rise of standardized container transport in global shipping (mid-20th century).
