Percidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.sɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.sɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of active freshwater fishes, including true perches and pike perches, found mainly in the Northern Hemisphere
family of freshwater fishes (perches, pike perches)

Examples

The family Percidae includes both perch and pike-perch species found in North American rivers.
Researchers study the Percidae for their importance in freshwater ecology.
Many anglers target fish from the Percidae family due to their fighting spirit and taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a common freshwater fish, member of the Percidae family
  • Synonym: pike-perch - a type of fish closely related to perches, also in Percidae

Etymology

From New Latin Percidae, based on Latin perca (perch) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
