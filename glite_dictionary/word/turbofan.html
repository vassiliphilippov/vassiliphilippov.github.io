turbofan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːboʊˌfæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌfæn / (BrE)
A type of jet engine that uses a fan to generate additional thrust, commonly used in modern aircraft.
type of jet engine for aircraft

Examples

The new commercial airliner is powered by highly efficient turbofan engines.
Technicians inspected the turbofan for signs of wear before the next flight.
Modern turbofans are much quieter and more fuel-efficient than older turbojet engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet engine - an engine that generates thrust by expelling jet of gas

Etymology

From 'turbo-' (referring to turbine) + 'fan'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːboʊˌfæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌfæn / (BrE)
a type of jet engine in which a turbine-driven fan provides additional air to the burner, increasing thrust and efficiency
type of jet engine with fan-driven thrust

Examples

The new airliner is powered by advanced turbofan engines for better fuel efficiency.
Engineers inspected the turbofan after the aircraft landed safely.
Most modern commercial jets use a turbofan rather than a turbojet for quieter operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbojet - a jet engine variant where all air passes through the combustion chamber
  • Antonym: propeller - a mechanical device with blades that rotates to propel an aircraft

Etymology

From 'turbo-' (relating to turbines) + 'fan'.
