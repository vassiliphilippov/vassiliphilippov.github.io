petitionary

1. ADJ

/ pəˈtɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / pɪˈtɪʃənəri / (BrE)
of or relating to a petition; expressing or characterized by a request, especially a formal one.
expressing or relating to a petition

Examples

The lawyer made a petitionary appeal to the court on behalf of her client.
His tone was petitionary, as if he sincerely hoped for mercy.
Several petitionary letters were submitted to the city council before the vote.

Synset Examples

the petitionary procedure had a quality of indecisiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petitional - relating to or expressing a petition

Etymology

From Latin petitionarius (pertaining to a petition), from petitio (petition, request).
