Genipa americana

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈniːpə ˌæməˈrɪkənə / (AmE), / dʒɛˈniːpə ˌaməˈrɪkənə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to the West Indies and northern South America, known for its orange-sized edible fruit.
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Genipa americana is often used to make jams and beverages in South America.
Many gardens in the Caribbean feature the Genipa americana for its attractive flowers and edible fruit.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of extracts from the Genipa americana tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genipap - the edible fruit of the Genipa americana tree

Etymology

From Latin Genipa (genip tree) + americana (of America), scientific name assigned in taxonomy.
