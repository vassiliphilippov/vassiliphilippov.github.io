Edo

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛdəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a West African ethnic group living mainly in the tropical forest region of southern Nigeria
member of a Nigerian ethnic group

Examples

Many Edo have preserved their traditional customs despite modern influences.
The art and craftsmanship of the Edo are renowned throughout Nigeria.
She learned about the history of the Edo people during her trip to Benin City.

2. PROPN

/ ˈeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːdəʊ / (BrE)
the former name of Tokyo, which served as the capital and largest city of Japan, and was the country's economic and cultural center during the Tokugawa period (1603–1868)
historic name for Tokyo, Japan

Examples

During the Tokugawa period, Edo grew into one of the largest cities in the world.
Many historic neighborhoods in modern Tokyo were originally part of Edo.
The shogunate established its government in Edo in the early 17th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tokyo - the present-day capital city of Japan

Etymology

From Japanese 江戸 (Edo), meaning 'bay entrance'.
