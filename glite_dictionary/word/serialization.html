serialization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of publishing a work in successive parts or installments, often in a newspaper, magazine, or other periodical.
publication in successive installments

Examples

The serialization of the novel helped build suspense among its readership each week.
Charles Dickens was famous for the serialization of his works in popular magazines.
The story first appeared as a serialization before being published as a complete book.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: serialization - the process of converting data into a sequence for storage or transmission (computing)

Etymology

From serialize + -ation; 'serialize' from French 'série' (series).
