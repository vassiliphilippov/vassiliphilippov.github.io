Scabiosa atropurpurea

1. NOUN

/ skæˈbioʊsə ˌætroʊˌpɜrˈpjʊriə / (AmE), / skəˈbɪəʊsə ˌatrəʊˌpɜːˈpjʊərɪə / (BrE)
An Old World annual plant with fragrant purple to deep crimson flower heads, commonly known as the pincushion flower and naturalized in the United States.
flowering plant with purple to crimson heads

Examples

The garden's border was brightened by clusters of Scabiosa atropurpurea in full bloom.
I planted Scabiosa atropurpurea for its rich, long-lasting purple flowers.
Florists often use Scabiosa atropurpurea in arrangements because of its unique color and fragrance.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Scabiosa; species name from Latin 'atro-' (dark) and 'purpurea' (purple).
