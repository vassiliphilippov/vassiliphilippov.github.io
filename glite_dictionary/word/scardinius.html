Scardinius

1. NOUN

/ skɑrˈdɪniəs / (AmE), / skɑːˈdɪnɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly referred to as rudds, found in Europe and western Asia.
a genus of freshwater fish (rudds)

Examples

The genus Scardinius is well known among freshwater anglers across Europe.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of several Scardinius species in different river basins.
Fossil records indicate that Scardinius has been present in European waters since ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudd - a common European and Asian freshwater fish (Scardinius erythrophthalmus)

Etymology

From New Latin Scardinius, the scientific genus name.
