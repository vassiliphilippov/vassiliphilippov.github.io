cochlea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.kli.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.li.ə / (BrE)
the spiral-shaped cavity in the inner ear that contains the organ of Corti, responsible for converting sound vibrations into nerve impulses.
spiral cavity in the inner ear
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the cochlea to diagnose the patient's hearing loss.
Sound waves are transmitted through the ear canal and eventually reach the cochlea.
Damage to the cochlea can result in permanent hearing impairment.

Etymology

From Latin cochlea (snail, spiral shell), from Greek *kokhlias* (snail, spiral shell).
