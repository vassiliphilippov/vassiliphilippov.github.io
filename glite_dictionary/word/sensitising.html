sensitising

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.səˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone or something to become more sensitive to physical or emotional stimuli.
making more sensitive to stimuli

Examples

The sensitising effect of the drug made patients more aware of pain.
Exposure to certain allergens has a sensitising impact on young children.
The training had a sensitising influence, making staff more responsive to cultural differences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desensitising - making less sensitive to stimuli

Etymology

From 'sensitise' + '-ing'; 'sensitise' from French 'sensibiliser', based on 'sensitive'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of making an organism sensitive to a substance, especially by repeated injections of a serum, so that it reacts to it upon subsequent exposure.
making sensitive to a serum via injections
medical

Examples

The sensitising of laboratory animals is an essential step in some allergy experiments.
Repeated sensitising injections were given before testing the immune response.
The process of sensitising can help researchers study allergic reactions in controlled settings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desensitising - the process of making less sensitive, especially to an allergen or stimulus

Etymology

From the verb 'sensitise', from sense 'to make sensitive', based on 'sensitive'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00830831-n
Synset POS: n
