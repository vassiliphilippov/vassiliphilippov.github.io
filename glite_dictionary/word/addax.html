addax

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.æks / (AmE), / ˈæd.aks / (BrE)
a large antelope (Addax nasomaculatus) of northern Africa, with long, spiraled horns and adapted to desert environments
large desert antelope of northern Africa

Examples

The addax is critically endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.
While traveling in the Sahara, we spotted a herd of addaxes near an oasis.
The zoo's new exhibit educates visitors about the unique adaptations of the addax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running deerlike grazing animal of Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'addax', of North African origin.
