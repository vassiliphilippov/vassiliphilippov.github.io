Addax

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.æks / (AmE), / ˈæd.æks / (BrE)
A genus of large antelopes native to the northern African deserts, known for their twisted horns and light-colored coats.
genus of African desert antelopes

Examples

The Addax is well adapted to the harsh desert environment.
Scientists are concerned about the declining population of Addax in the wild.
A herd of Addax can survive for long periods without water.

Etymology

From Latin Addax, an antelope mentioned by Pliny, likely from a North African source.
