Cimabue

1. PROPN

/ ˌtʃiməˈbuːeɪ / (AmE), / ˌtʃiːməˈbuːeɪ / (BrE)
(c. 1240–1302) Italian painter of the Florentine school who was a precursor of the Renaissance, noted for moving away from the Byzantine style towards greater naturalism.
Italian painter, precursor of the Renaissance

Examples

Cimabue is considered one of the most important painters in the transition from medieval to Renaissance art.
The influence of Cimabue can be seen in the works of his pupil Giotto.
Many historians credit Cimabue with breaking away from rigid Byzantine artistic conventions.

Etymology

Italian, originally a nickname meaning 'bull-head', real name Cenni di Pepo.
