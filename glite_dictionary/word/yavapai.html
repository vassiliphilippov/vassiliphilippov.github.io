Yavapai

1. NOUN

/ ˌjævəˈpaɪ / (AmE), / ˌjævəˈpaɪ / (BrE)
the Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Yavapai people of Arizona
Yuman language of the Yavapai people

Examples

The linguist devoted years to studying the Yavapai language and its oral traditions.
Efforts are underway to preserve and revitalize Yavapai among younger generations of the tribe.
There are only a few fluent speakers of Yavapai remaining today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Yuman - a group of languages spoken by several Native American peoples of the southwestern US and northern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Yavapai people, an indigenous group in Arizona.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjævəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈjævəˌpaɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people traditionally living in central Arizona.
member of a Native American group in Arizona

Examples

She is proud to be a Yavapai and celebrates her heritage every year.
The Yavapai have a rich tradition of basket weaving and storytelling.
Many Yavapai continue to live in central Arizona, maintaining their cultural practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apache - a member of another Native American group in the southwestern US
  • Synonym: Navajo - a member of a Native American people of the southwestern US

Etymology

From the Yavapai language, meaning 'people of the sun.'
