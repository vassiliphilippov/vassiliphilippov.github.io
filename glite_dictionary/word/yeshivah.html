yeshivah

1. NOUN

/ jəˈʃiːvə / (AmE), / jəˈʃiːvə / (BrE)
an academy for advanced study of Jewish texts, especially the Talmud
Jewish religious academy

Examples

After finishing high school, he enrolled in a yeshivah to deepen his understanding of Jewish law.
Many rabbis received their training at a well-known yeshivah in Jerusalem.
The students spent hours each day studying the Talmud in the yeshivah.

Etymology

From Hebrew *yeshivah* (sitting; session; assembly), referring to a place of study.
