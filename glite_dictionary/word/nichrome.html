nichrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪkˌroʊm / (AmE), / ˈnɪkrəʊm / (BrE)
an alloy of nickel and chromium, known for its high electrical resistance and ability to withstand high temperatures, used especially in heating elements
nickel-chromium alloy used in heating elements

Examples

The heating coil in the toaster is made of nichrome wire.
Nichrome is often used in laboratory equipment requiring precise temperature control.
To repair the electric heater, he replaced the old nichrome filament.

Etymology

Blend of nickel and chromium, referring to its main components.
