retrospectively

1. ADV

/ ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪvli / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that considers or relates to past events; looking back on or dealing with something after it has happened.
in a way relating to the past

Examples

Looking at the financial crisis retrospectively, it's clear that warning signs were ignored.
The policy was applied retrospectively, affecting decisions made years earlier.
She realized retrospectively that her childhood experiences shaped her career choices.

Synset Examples

retrospectively, he seems like a great artist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retrospective - looking back on or dealing with past events (adjective)

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectus' (looked back), from 'retro-' (back) + 'specere' (to look) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
