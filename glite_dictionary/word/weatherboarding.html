weatherboarding

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðɚˌbɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəbɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a long, thin board with one edge thicker than the other, used for exterior siding by overlapping each board over the one below
overlapping exterior siding board

Examples

The old cottage was covered in weatherboarding to protect it from the rain.
We decided to repaint the weatherboarding to freshen up the house's appearance.
Some houses in coastal areas still feature traditional wooden weatherboarding on their façades.
