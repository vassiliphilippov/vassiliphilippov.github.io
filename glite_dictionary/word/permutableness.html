permutableness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmjuːtəblnəs / (AmE), / pəˈmjuːtəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being capable of being permuted or having its sequence changed.
ability to be permuted or rearranged

Examples

The permutableness of the seating arrangement allowed for various group combinations.
In mathematics, permutableness is a key factor when studying algorithms that sort data.
The puzzle's difficulty lies in its permutableness, as every tile can be moved into a different position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permutability - quality of being able to be changed or permuted

Etymology

From Latin 'permutare' (to interchange) + English suffix '-ble' (able) + '-ness'.
