Uria aalge

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊəriə ˈælɡi / (AmE), / ˈjʊəriə ˈælɡi / (BrE)
a species of seabird in the auk family, commonly called the common murre, found in northern oceans
species of seabird (common murre)

Examples

The Uria aalge nests in large colonies along rocky cliffs in the North Atlantic.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the Uria aalge during winter.
A sudden decline in fish stock threatens the survival of Uria aalge populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murre - a common name for birds of the genus Uria, including Uria aalge

Etymology

From the genus name 'Uria' (probably from Greek ouria, a seabird) and the Latin species name 'aalge'.
