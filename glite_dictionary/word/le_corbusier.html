Le Corbusier

1. PROPN

/ lə kɔːrˈbuːzi.eɪ / (AmE), / lə kɔːˈbjuːzɪeɪ / (BrE)
(1887–1965) A renowned Swiss-French architect, designer, and pioneer of modern architecture, known for his influential theories and buildings worldwide.
pioneering Swiss-French modernist architect

Examples

Many modern buildings were influenced by Le Corbusier's principles of design.
The urban plan was inspired by the ideas set forth by Le Corbusier.
Architecture students often study the works of Le Corbusier for their innovative use of concrete.

Etymology

Pseudonym of Charles-Édouard Jeanneret; 'Le Corbusier' adapted from his maternal grandfather’s name, Lecorbésier.
