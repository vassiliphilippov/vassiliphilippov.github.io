rigamarole

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡəməˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡəmərəʊl / (BrE)
a long, complicated, and confusing process or procedure, often regarded as unnecessary or tedious
complicated, tedious procedure

Examples

It took hours to get through the rigamarole of applying for a simple permit.
She was frustrated by the endless rigamarole required to file a complaint.
We had to endure the usual bureaucratic rigamarole before we could see the doctor.

Synset Examples

all that academic rigmarole was a waste of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigmarole - a long, complicated, and confusing procedure (alternative spelling)

Etymology

Variant of 'rigmarole', from a corruption of 'ragman roll', a twelfth-century legal document.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡəməˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡəmərəʊl / (BrE)
a long, complicated, and confusing procedure or set of statements, often regarded as pointless or meaningless
long, pointless, or confusing procedure or talk

Examples

Dealing with the insurance company involved a lot of rigamarole that left me frustrated.
He grew tired of the endless rigamarole required just to submit a simple request.
Let's skip all the rigamarole and get straight to the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palaver - prolonged and tedious fuss or discussion
  • Synonym: bureaucracy - excessively complicated administrative procedure

Etymology

Alteration of 'rigmarole', which may be a contraction of 'ragman roll', a list or petition in Middle English.
