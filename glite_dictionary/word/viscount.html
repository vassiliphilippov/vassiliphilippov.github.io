viscount

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪkaʊnt / (BrE)
A British nobleman ranking below an earl and above a baron.
British noble title below earl
british

Examples

The viscount hosted a grand reception at his country estate.
After his father's death, he inherited the title of viscount.
A viscount sits in the House of Lords, representing the peerage beneath the rank of earl.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: baron - a British nobleman of the lowest hereditary rank
  • Degree Higher: earl - a British nobleman ranking above a viscount and below a marquess

Etymology

From Old French *visconte*, from Medieval Latin *vicecomes* (deputy count).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪkaʊnt / (BrE)
A nobleman ranking below an earl or count and above a baron, used as a title in Britain and some other countries.
noble title below earl/count

Examples

The viscount inherited his father's estate after the funeral.
As a viscount, he held considerable influence in local government.
The dinner guest was introduced as Lord Smith, the newly appointed viscount.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: baron - a lower-ranking nobleman or lord
  • Degree Higher: earl - a nobleman ranking above a viscount in Britain
  • Degree Higher: count - a nobleman ranking similarly to an earl, above a viscount

Etymology

From Old French 'visconte', from Medieval Latin 'vicecomes', meaning 'deputy count'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10775816-n
Synset POS: n
