to phonate

1. VERB

/ ˈfoʊ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.neɪt / (BrE)
to produce speech sounds by vibrating the vocal cords
produce speech sounds with vocal cords

Examples

Some birds are unable to phonate because they lack vocal folds.
After the surgery, the patient struggled to phonate clearly.
Humans phonate by bringing their vocal cords together and creating vibration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to speak or express clearly
  • Synonym: to vocalize - to produce sound with the voice
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or without vibrating the vocal cords

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (voice) + -ate.
