brae

1. NOUN

/ breɪ / (AmE), / breɪ / (BrE)
A slope or hillside, especially in Scotland or northern England.
slope or hillside (Scottish usage)

Examples

The cottage stood on a grassy brae overlooking the loch.
Children ran down the brae laughing and shouting.
Wildflowers grow in abundance along the brae in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high or craggy as a mountain

Etymology

From Scottish, of Old Norse origin; related to Old Norse 'brá' (eyelid, edge), referring to a slope.
