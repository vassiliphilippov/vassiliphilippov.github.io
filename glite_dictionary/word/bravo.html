bravo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
A shout of approval or praise, especially from an audience at the end of a performance.
cry of approval or praise

Examples

The audience erupted in bravo after the singer finished the final note.
A loud bravo was heard from the back row as the curtain fell.
She smiled as the hall echoed with cheers and bravo for her performance.

Etymology

From Italian 'bravo' (brave, excellent).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
a person, especially a hired killer, who murders by a surprise attack
hired assassin or murderer

Examples

The prince was ambushed by a bravo hired by his political enemies.
In Renaissance Italy, a powerful noble might employ a bravo to eliminate his rivals.
The detective suspected that the murder was the work of a skilled bravo.

Synset Examples

his assassins were hunted down like animals
assassinators of kings and emperors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin - a person who murders an important person for political or religious reasons

Etymology

From Italian 'bravo' (bold, daring; hired assassin), from Latin 'bravus' (wild, savage).

3. VERB

/ ˈbrɑːvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
to applaud by shouting 'bravo' or 'brava' as an expression of approval or admiration, typically at a performance
to applaud by shouting 'bravo'

Examples

At the end of the singer’s performance, the audience bravoed enthusiastically.
They bravoed the actors throughout the curtain call to show their appreciation.
As the orchestra finished, a few fans bravoed from the front row.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout with joy or in support
  • Antonym: boo - to shout disapproval

Etymology

From Italian 'bravo' (well done), used as an exclamation of approval.
