metameric

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism) having the body divided into a series of similar segments or metameres, as in earthworms or lobsters.
having body divided into segments
medical

Examples

Earthworms are classic examples of metameric animals, with their bodies divided into ring-like segments.
Lobsters exhibit a metameric structure, where each segment performs specialized functions.
The scientist explained how the metameric arrangement benefits mobility and flexibility in annelids.

Etymology

From Greek 'meta-' (after, beyond) + 'meros' (part), referring to segmentation.
