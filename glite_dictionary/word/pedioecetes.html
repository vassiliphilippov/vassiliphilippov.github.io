Pedioecetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdi.oʊˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪ.əʊˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of North American grouse, including the sharp-tailed grouse.
a genus of North American grouse

Examples

Researchers observed several Pedioecetes in the grasslands during their spring mating displays.
The Pedioecetes are known for their unique tail feathers and elaborate dances.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect habitats critical to the survival of Pedioecetes species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-tailed grouse - a species of grouse native to North America, formerly classified in this genus

Etymology

From Greek 'pedion' (plain) + 'oiketēs' (inhabitant), referring to its habitat.

genus of sharp-tailed grouse

Examples
