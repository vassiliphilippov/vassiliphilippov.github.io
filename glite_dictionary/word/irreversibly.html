irreversibly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪrəˈvɜrsəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈvɜːsəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be undone or changed back to a previous state.
in a way that cannot be reversed

Examples

The disease had damaged his nerves irreversibly, leaving permanent effects.
Due to climate change, some species have been irreversibly lost from the region.
The contract was irreversibly signed, making further negotiations impossible.

Synset Examples

this old tradition is irreversibly disappearing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reversibly - in a way that can be undone or returned to a previous state

Etymology

From 'irreversible' + '-ly'; 'irreversible' from Latin 'irreversibilis' (not able to be turned back).
