to despise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈspaɪz / (BrE)
to feel a strong dislike or lack of respect for someone or something; to regard with contempt or scorn
feel strong dislike or contempt

Examples

She openly despised his arrogant attitude at the meeting.
Many people despise cruelty towards animals and take action against it.
He despised dishonesty in all its forms, refusing to tolerate it in his business.

Synset Examples

He despises the people he has to work for
The professor scorns the students who don't catch on immediately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorn - to feel or express contempt or derision for someone or something
  • Synonym: disdain - to regard or treat with haughty contempt; to look down on
  • Antonym: to admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Latin despicere meaning 'to look down on'.
