calque

1. NOUN

/ kælk / (AmE), / kælk / (BrE)
A word or expression introduced into a language by translating it literally from another language.
a literal translation from another language

Examples

The English word skyscraper is a calque of the French term "gratte-ciel".
Linguists often study how a calque can reflect cultural influence between languages.
The term "flea market" is widely considered to be a calque from the French "marché aux puces".

Synset Examples

‘superman’ is a calque for the German ‘Ubermensch’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loanword - a word borrowed from another language, often unchanged
  • Synonym: translation - rendering from one language to another, not necessarily literal

Etymology

From French 'calque' (copy, tracing), from Italian 'calco'.
