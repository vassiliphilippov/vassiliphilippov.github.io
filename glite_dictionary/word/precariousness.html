precariousness

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈkɛriəsnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the state of being dangerously unstable, insecure, or uncertain
state of being unstable or insecure

Examples

The precariousness of the cliff edge made hiking there particularly risky.
Investors were concerned about the precariousness of the financial market during the crisis.
The refugees lived in constant precariousness, never feeling truly safe or secure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the state of being likely to change or fail; lack of stability
  • Antonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat

Etymology

From Latin 'precarius' (obtained by entreaty, depending on favor).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈkɛriəsnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəsnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being uncertain, unstable, or dependent on chance
state of being unstable or uncertain

Examples

Investors were worried about the precariousness of the market after the sudden drop.
She spoke openly about the precariousness of her family's financial situation.
The precariousness of their position forced them to be extremely cautious.

Synset Examples

the uncertainty of the outcome
the precariousness of his income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not known
  • Antonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat

Etymology

From Latin 'precarius' (obtained by entreaty, uncertain).
