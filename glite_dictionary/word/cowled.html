cowled

1. ADJ

/ kaʊld / (AmE), / kaʊld / (BrE)
having the head covered or enclosed in a cowl or hood
with head covered by a cowl or hood

Examples

The cowled figure moved silently through the monastery's stone corridors.
Beneath his cowled robe, the monk's face was barely visible in the candlelight.
The procession was led by a cowled priest carrying a golden censer.

Synset Examples

a cowled monk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hooded - having a hood or having the head covered by a hood

Etymology

From 'cowl' (a monk's hood or hooded garment), itself from Old English 'cule', from Latin 'cucullus' (hood, cowl).
