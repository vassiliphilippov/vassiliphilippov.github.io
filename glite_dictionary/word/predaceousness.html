predaceousness

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈdeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of preying upon and feeding on other live animals.
quality of preying on live animals

Examples

The predaceousness of certain birds ensures the balance of local ecosystems.
Her research focused on the predaceousness displayed by hawks in their natural habitat.
Some insects exhibit remarkable predaceousness during their larval stage.

Synset Examples

The rapacity of carnivorous animals has been considered by some writers to have had a considerable effect on the distribution and even on the extinction of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predacity - the quality of being predatory, fondness for preying on others

Etymology

From 'predaceous' + '-ness'; 'predaceous' from Latin praeda (prey).
