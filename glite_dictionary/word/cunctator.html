cunctator

1. NOUN

/ kʌŋkˈteɪtər / (AmE), / kʌŋkˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who habitually delays actions or decisions; one who procrastinates
someone who delays or postpones tasks

Examples

The project was delayed yet again by the team's habitual cunctator.
Many brilliant ideas are lost to the cunctator who never acts on them.
In history, the title of cunctator was both a compliment and a criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastinator - a person who delays doing things until later

Etymology

From Latin *cūnctātor* (delayer), notably used as a title for the Roman general Fabius Maximus (‘Fabius the Delayer’).
