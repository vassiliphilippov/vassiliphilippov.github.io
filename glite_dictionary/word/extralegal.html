extralegal

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strəˈliː.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈliː.ɡəl / (BrE)
not regulated, authorized, or sanctioned by law; falling outside the boundaries of official legal authority
not authorized by law
legal

Examples

The organization engaged in extralegal activities that were not covered by any official statutes.
Seeking justice, the villagers took extralegal measures when the authorities failed to intervene.
The dispute was settled by an extralegal tribunal, which lacked formal government approval.

Synset Examples

there were only extralegal recourses for their grievances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegal - forbidden by law or statute

Etymology

From Latin extra- (outside) + legalis (legal, pertaining to the law).
