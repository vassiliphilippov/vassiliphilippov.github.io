foundation

1. NOUN

/ faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
basic education or instruction that provides the essential knowledge needed in a particular subject or field.
basic instruction in a subject

Examples

Before you specialize, you need a solid foundation in the general principles.
Her foundation in chemistry helped her succeed in advanced courses.
He built his career on a strong foundation of statistics and logic.

Synset Examples

he lacks the foundation necessary for advanced study
a good grounding in mathematics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grounding - basic instruction or preparation in a subject
  • Synonym: background - basic knowledge or training in a subject

Etymology

From Latin 'fundatio', from 'fundare' (to lay a base for).

5. NOUN

/ faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the lowest part of a building or other structure, typically below ground level, that supports it and distributes its weight
lowest structural support of a building

Examples

The foundation of the old church was made of stone and still stands today.
Before building the house, the workers dug and poured a strong concrete foundation.
Cracks in the foundation can lead to serious structural problems over time.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: base - the bottom support of anything; that on which a thing stands or rests
  • Synonym: foot - the lower part or bottom of something, especially of a structure
  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or structure above its foundation

Etymology

From Latin 'fundatio', from 'fundare' (to lay a base), from 'fundus' (bottom).
