Davallia canariensis

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvæliə kəˌnɛriˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / dəˈvæliə kəˌnæriˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of fern native to the Canary Islands and Madeira, known for its finely divided fronds and hairy rhizomes
fern species native to Canary Islands and Madeira

Examples

The Davallia canariensis thrives in humid, shaded environments similar to its native Canary Islands.
Botanists study Davallia canariensis for its distinctive woolly rhizomes, which help anchor it to rocks and trees.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Davallia canariensis as an ornamental plant due to its attractive fronds.

Etymology

Genus name from French botanist Edmond Davall; species epithet from Latin, referring to the Canary Islands.
