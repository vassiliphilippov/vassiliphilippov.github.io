anicteric

1. ADJ

/ ˌænɪkˈtɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌanɪkˈtɛrɪk / (BrE)
(of a medical condition) not accompanied by jaundice; without yellowing of the skin or eyes.
not accompanied by jaundice
medical

Examples

The patient presented with anicteric hepatitis, showing no visible signs of jaundice.
Early stages of the disease may remain anicteric and thus harder to detect.
Most cases in the study were anicteric, with patients appearing otherwise healthy.

Synset Examples

anicteric hepatitis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jaundiced - showing yellowing of the skin or eyes due to jaundice

Etymology

From Greek an- (not) + ikteros (jaundice).

2. ADJ

/ ænˈɪktərɪk / (AmE), / ænˈɪktərɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or condition) not affected by jaundice; showing no yellow discoloration of the skin or eyes.
not affected by jaundice
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that the patient was anicteric during the examination.
Despite her liver issues, she remained anicteric throughout her hospital stay.
Laboratory results confirmed an anicteric presentation in the majority of cases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: icteric - affected by jaundice; showing yellow coloration of the skin or eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'icteric,' from 'ikteros' (jaundice).
