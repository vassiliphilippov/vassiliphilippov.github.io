Pereskia aculeata

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛskiə əkjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / pəˈrɛskiə əkjuːˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of West Indian woody climbing plant with spiny stems and clusters of fragrant white flowers, followed by small yellow to orange fruits.
a spiny, climbing plant with white flowers and small fruits

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare Pereskia aculeata climbing along the trellis.
Many gardeners are attracted by the fragrant flowers of Pereskia aculeata.
The small orange fruits of Pereskia aculeata are a food source for several bird species.

Etymology

From genus name Pereskia, honoring Nicolas-Claude Fabri de Peiresc (1580–1637), and Latin aculeata meaning 'prickly'.
