Avada Kedavra

1. NOUN

/ əˌvɑːdə kəˈdɑːvɹə / (AmE), / əˌvɑːdə kɪˈdɑːvɹə / (BrE)
A fictional spell from the Harry Potter series that causes instant death to the target.
killing curse in Harry Potter

Examples

With a flash of green light, the wizard shouted Avada Kedavra, ending the duel instantly.
Few dared utter Avada Kedavra aloud, knowing its deadly power in the wizarding world.
Bellatrix Lestrange used the curse Avada Kedavra during the final battle at Hogwarts.

Synset Examples

In the duel, he cast 'Avada Kedavra' without hesitation.

Etymology

Invented by J.K. Rowling, possibly inspired by the Aramaic phrase 'avra kehdabra' (I will create as I speak).
