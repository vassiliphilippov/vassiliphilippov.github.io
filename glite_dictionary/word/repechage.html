repechage

1. NOUN

/ rəˌpɛˈʃɑʒ / (AmE), / rəˌpɛˈʃɑːʒ / (BrE)
A race or contest, especially in rowing or other sports, in which competitors who failed to qualify in initial rounds have another chance to compete for a place in the final.
second-chance race in sports

Examples

After losing in the heat, she qualified for the final through the repechage.
The repechage offered athletes a second chance to advance to the medal round.
Several rowers entered the repechage after missing automatic qualification in their first race.

Etymology

From French 'repêchage' (fishing out again, second chance).
