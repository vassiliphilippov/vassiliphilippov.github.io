customhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstəmˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəmhaʊs / (BrE)
a government building where customs duties are collected and ships are cleared for entry or departure
building for customs duties and ship clearance

Examples

The ship's captain reported to the customhouse upon arrival to complete the necessary paperwork.
Historic port cities often feature an old customhouse as a prominent building near the docks.
Before unloading their cargo, merchants had to obtain clearance from the local customhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customs office - an office where customs duties are managed

Etymology

From 'custom', referring to duties on imports/exports, and 'house'.
