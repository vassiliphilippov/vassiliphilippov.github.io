coy

1. ADJ

/ kɔɪ / (AmE), / kɔɪ / (BrE)
pretending to be shy or modest, often in a playful or flirtatious way
playfully shy or modest

Examples

She gave him a coy smile before turning away.
His coy responses made everyone at the table laugh.
The actress was known for her coy demeanor during interviews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bashful - reluctant to draw attention to oneself; shy
  • Antonym: brazen - bold and without shame

Etymology

From Middle English *coy*, from Old French *coi* (quiet, still), from Latin *quietus* (quiet).

2. ADJ

/ kɔɪ / (AmE), / kɔɪ / (BrE)
deliberately behaving in a shy or modest way, often to seem attractive or to avoid giving a direct answer or encouragement
deliberately shy or modest

Examples

She gave him a coy smile and quickly looked away.
He acted coy about his achievements, never bragging even when asked.
The kitten was coy at first, but soon warmed up to her new owner.

Synset Examples

like a wild young colt, very inquisitive but very coy and not to be easily cajoled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid with other people
  • Antonym: bold - showing courage; not shy or modest

Etymology

From Middle English 'coy', from Old French 'coi', from Latin 'quietus' (quiet).

3. ADJ

/ kɔɪ / (AmE), / kɔɪ / (BrE)
deliberately evasive or reluctant to give a definite answer, often in a playful or irritating way.
deliberately evasive or reluctant

Examples

The company spokesperson remained coy about the details of the new product launch.
She gave a coy smile, refusing to answer the reporter's question directly.
He was coy when asked if he would run for president again.

Synset Examples

a politician coy about his intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elusive - difficult to grasp or define clearly
  • Antonym: forthcoming - willing to give information or express opinions

Etymology

From Middle English *coy*, from Old French *coi* (quiet, still), from Latin *quietus* (quiet).
