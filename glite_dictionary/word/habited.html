habited

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæbɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) wearing a habit, especially a religious garment.
wearing a religious habit

Examples

The monks were habited in plain brown robes during the ceremony.
She sketched a group of habited nuns walking through the old city.
All habited brothers gathered in the chapel for morning prayers.

Synset Examples

the habited men of the monastery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothed - wearing clothes
  • Antonym: unclothed - without clothes; not wearing clothing

Etymology

From Old French habiter (to dwell, wear), from Latin habitare (to have, to hold, to inhabit, to dwell), related to 'habit' as attire.
