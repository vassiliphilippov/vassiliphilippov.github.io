hepatotoxin

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈpætoʊˌtɑksɪn / (AmE), / hɪˈpætəʊˌtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A substance that is toxic to the liver.
substance toxic to the liver
medical

Examples

Exposure to even small amounts of the hepatotoxin can lead to severe liver damage.
Researchers are studying herbal compounds to determine if any act as a hepatotoxin.
Aflatoxin is a well-known hepatotoxin produced by certain molds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotoxin - a substance toxic to the nervous system
  • Antonym: hepatoprotective - serving to protect the liver

Etymology

From Greek 'hēpar' (liver) and 'toxin' (poison).
