bevatron

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛvəˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈbiːvəˌtrɒn / (BrE)
a type of cyclotron designed to accelerate protons to energies of several billion electron volts.
high-energy particle accelerator

Examples

The bevatron at Berkeley was vital in advancing research on subatomic particles.
Scientists used the bevatron to discover the antiproton in the 1950s.
Construction of the bevatron marked a significant milestone in the history of particle physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclotron - circular particle accelerator

Etymology

From 'bev' (billion electron volts) + '–atron' (as in 'cyclotron').
