circumspection

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈspɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈspɛkʃən / (BrE)
The quality of being careful to consider all circumstances and possible consequences; prudence or caution.
careful consideration; caution

Examples

He acted with great circumspection when handling the sensitive negotiations.
Her circumspection prevented her from making any impulsive decisions.
Due to his circumspection, he gained a reputation for sound judgment among his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent; cautiousness
  • Antonym: recklessness - the quality of being reckless; lack of caution

Etymology

From Latin 'circumspectio' (a looking around), from 'circumspicere' (to look around).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˈspɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈspek.ʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being careful to consider all circumstances and possible consequences before acting or speaking, especially to avoid risk or embarrassment
cautious prudence; carefulness in actions or speech

Examples

She handled the sensitive situation with great circumspection, ensuring no one was offended.
His circumspection in business dealings earned him a reputation for reliability and wisdom.
Approaching the delicate topic required tact and circumspection from the entire team.

Synset Examples

the servants showed great tact and discretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - careful and sensible judgment that avoids unnecessary risks
  • Antonym: recklessness - lack of caution or concern for consequences

Etymology

From Latin 'circumspicere' (to look around), via Old French 'circumspection'.
