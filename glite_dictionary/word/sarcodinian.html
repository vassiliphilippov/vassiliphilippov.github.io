sarcodinian

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkoʊdɪniən / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊdɪniən / (BrE)
A protozoan belonging to a group characterized by movement and feeding by means of pseudopods.
type of protozoan with pseudopods

Examples

The sarcodinian uses its flexible pseudopods to engulf food particles in its environment.
Scientists observed several sarcodinians under the microscope, watching their cytoplasm flow into new shapes.
Unlike many other protozoa, a sarcodinian moves by extending portions of its cell body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeba - single-celled organism that moves and feeds using pseudopodia

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'podion' (little foot), referencing 'pseudopod' (false foot).
