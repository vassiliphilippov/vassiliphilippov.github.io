Onopordon acanthium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑnəˈpɔrdən əˈkænθiəm / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈpɔːdən əˈkanθɪəm / (BrE)
A biennial Eurasian thistle with white hairy leaves and pale purple flowers, also known as cotton thistle, and naturalized in North America.
a Eurasian thistle; cotton thistle

Examples

The fields were full of Onopordon acanthium, its spiky leaves glinting in the sun.
Gardeners often remove Onopordon acanthium to prevent it from spreading too aggressively.
He photographed a bee perched on a blooming Onopordon acanthium flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton thistle - common name for Onopordon acanthium

Etymology

From Greek 'onos' (donkey) and 'pordon' (fart), referencing the donkey's fondness for thistles; 'acanthium' from Greek 'akanthos' (thorn).
