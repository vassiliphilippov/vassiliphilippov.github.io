detonation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing an explosive to explode suddenly and violently
the act of exploding an explosive

Examples

The detonation of the bomb could be heard for miles around.
Firefighters arrived shortly after the detonation to assess the damage.
Improper handling can lead to accidental detonation of explosives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent release of energy causing a loud noise and destruction

Etymology

From Latin detonare (to thunder down) via French détonation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a violent and sudden release of energy resulting from a chemical or nuclear reaction, often producing an explosion
violent release of energy; explosion

Examples

The engineers studied the detonation caused by the fuel-air mixture in the lab.
There was a massive detonation when the explosives were triggered.
The authorities are investigating the source of the detonation heard last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a sudden and violent burst of energy

Etymology

From French *détonation*, from Latin *detonare* (to thunder down).

Synset POS: n
