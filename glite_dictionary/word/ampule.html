ampule

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.pjul / (AmE), / ˈæm.pjuːl / (BrE)
A small sealed vial that contains a liquid, especially a sterile solution for injection.
sealed vial for liquid medicine
medical

Examples

The nurse drew the vaccine from the ampule using a sterile syringe.
Each ampule is strictly checked for contamination before use in the hospital.
She carefully snapped open the ampule to administer the medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vial - a small container used to hold liquid medicines

Etymology

From French *ampoule*, from Latin *ampulla* (small bottle).
