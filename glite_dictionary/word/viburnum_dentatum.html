Viburnum dentatum

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɝːnəm dɛnˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / vaɪˈbɜːnəm dɛnˈteɪtəm / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, bearing blue-black berries and tough, flexible wood once used for making arrows.
deciduous North American shrub with blue-black berries

Examples

The park ranger pointed out a Viburnum dentatum growing beside the trail.
Blue-black berries from Viburnum dentatum attract a variety of birds in late summer.
Early settlers valued Viburnum dentatum for its strong, pliant wood.

Etymology

From Latin 'viburnum' (a type of shrub) and 'dentatum' (toothed), referring to the toothed edges of the leaves.
