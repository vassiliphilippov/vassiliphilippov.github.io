Assumption

1. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the taking up into heaven of the body and soul of the Virgin Mary at the end of her earthly life.
Virgin Mary's ascent into heaven (Christianity)

Examples

The Feast of the Assumption is celebrated by Catholics every August 15th.
Many famous works of art depict the Assumption of the Virgin Mary.
Pilgrims gathered to honor the Assumption at the cathedral.

Etymology

From Latin assumptio, meaning 'a taking up.'

2. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the celebration of the Virgin Mary being taken up into heaven at the end of her earthly life.
Virgin Mary's ascent into heaven (Catholic feast)

Examples

Catholics around the world celebrate the Assumption on August 15 each year.
The church was decorated with flowers in honor of the Assumption.
Many believers attended mass to commemorate the Assumption of the Virgin Mary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dormition - the Eastern Orthodox celebration of Mary's death and assumption into heaven

Etymology

From Latin assumptio, from assumere meaning 'to take up'.
