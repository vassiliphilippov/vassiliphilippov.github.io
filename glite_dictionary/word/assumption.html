assumption

1. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking possession of or control over something, especially an office, role, or responsibility
act of taking control or possession

Examples

His assumption of the presidency was marked by a lavish ceremony.
The country's rapid assumption of new responsibilities surprised many observers.
Upon her assumption of the leadership role, significant changes were implemented.

Synset Examples

his assumption of office coincided with the trouble in Cuba
the Nazi assumption of power in 1934
he acquired all the company's assets for ten million dollars and the assumption of the company's debts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accession - the act of attaining or assuming power or a position
  • Antonym: abdication - the act of giving up a position of power or responsibility

Etymology

From Latin assumptio (a taking up, adoption), from assumere (to take up).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking something for granted or accepting something as true without proof
something accepted as true without proof

Examples

Her entire argument was based on a false assumption.
The experiment began with the assumption that temperature would remain constant.
It's a dangerous assumption to think everyone shares your beliefs.

Synset Examples

your assumption that I would agree was unwarranted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presumption - something that is assumed to be true, often without proof
  • Synonym: supposition - an uncertain belief or idea assumed to be true
  • Antonym: fact - something that can be proven to be true

Etymology

From Latin assumptio (a taking, undertaking), from assumere (to take up, adopt).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
something that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof
something accepted without proof

Examples

The argument is based on the assumption that all people desire success.
We need to challenge the assumptions behind this policy.
Her theory depended on a faulty assumption about human nature.

Synset Examples

any society is built upon certain assumptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presumption - something believed to be true without proof
  • Synonym: premise - a statement or idea assumed as the basis for reasoning
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Latin assumptio (a taking up, adoption), from assumere (to take up, adopt).

4. NOUN

/ əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
a thing that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof, often forming the basis for reasoning or argument
something accepted as true without proof

Examples

The entire plan was made on the assumption that funding would be available.
If our assumption is incorrect, the results could be misleading.
She questioned the basic assumption underlying his argument.

Synset Examples

on the assumption that he has been injured, we can infer that he will not play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presumption - an idea that is taken to be true based on probability
  • Degree Higher: conclusion - a judgment or decision reached by reasoning

Etymology

From Latin assumptio (a taking up), from assumere (to take up).
