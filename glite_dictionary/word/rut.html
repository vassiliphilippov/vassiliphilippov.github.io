rut

1. NOUN

/ rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
A period of sexual excitement and reproductive activity in some male mammals, especially deer, typically occurring seasonally.
mammals' mating season

Examples

During the autumn rut, male deer compete fiercely for mates.
You can observe increased aggression in elks during the rut.
The park is closed during the rut to avoid disturbing the animals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: estrus - period of sexual receptivity in female mammals

Etymology

From Latin 'rugire' (to roar), originally referring to the bellowing of deer during the mating season.

4. NOUN

/ rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
a long, narrow groove or track in the ground, especially one made by the repeated passage of wheels
groove or track made by wheels

Examples

The heavy truck left a deep rut in the muddy road after the rain.
Bicyclists must watch out for every rut on the trail to avoid accidents.
After years of use, the old lane was marked by ruts that filled with water during storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furrow - a long, narrow trench made in the ground by a plow, especially for planting seeds or irrigation
  • Synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface

Etymology

From Middle French route (road, track), from Latin rupta (broken ground).

5. VERB

/ rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
(of male mammals) to be in a state of sexual excitement, especially during the breeding season.
male mammal: be in a state of sexual excitement

Examples

During mating season, stags rut violently, competing for access to females.
Wild boars will often rut for weeks on end, scarcely eating as they seek to mate.
Naturalists observed that the elk began to rut in late September.

Etymology

From Latin 'rugire' (to roar), later adapted in zoological context for breeding behavior.
