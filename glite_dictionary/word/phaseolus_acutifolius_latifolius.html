Phaseolus acutifolius latifolius

1. NOUN

/ fəˌziːoʊləs əˌkjuːtɪˈfoʊliəs ˌlætɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / fəˌziːələs əˌkjuːtɪˈfəʊliəs ˌlætɪˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
A variety of tepary bean, a twining plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its roundish beans that can be white, yellow, brown, or black.
variety of tepary bean plant

Examples

The Phaseolus acutifolius latifolius thrives in the arid climate of the southwestern deserts.
Farmers are interested in cultivating Phaseolus acutifolius latifolius for its drought-resistant properties.
The beans of Phaseolus acutifolius latifolius vary in color from white to black.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tepary bean - a bean plant native to the arid southwestern U.S. and Mexico

Etymology

From Latin: Phaseolus (bean genus), acutifolius ('sharp-leaved'), and latifolius ('broad-leaved').
