backpack

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈbæk.pæk / (BrE)
a bag with shoulder straps carried on the back, used for carrying belongings
bag carried on the back

Examples

She packed her books and lunch into her backpack before leaving for school.
Hikers should always carry a first aid kit in their backpack.
My backpack was so heavy that my shoulders started to ache during the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rucksack - a bag carried on the back with shoulder straps

Etymology

From 'back' + 'pack', originally from hiking and mountaineering jargon (1910s).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈbæk.pæk / (BrE)
a bag with shoulder straps, carried on the back, used to transport clothes, books, or other items
bag carried on the back

Examples

She carried her textbooks and lunch in a sturdy backpack.
After the hike, my shoulders ached from wearing a heavy backpack all day.
He bought a new backpack for his trip to Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knapsack - a bag carried on the back, similar to a backpack
  • Synonym: rucksack - a bag carried on the back, chiefly British term for backpack

Etymology

Back- + pack, first used in the early 20th century in the United States.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02772753-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbækˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈbækˌpak / (BrE)
to hike or travel, especially in the countryside, while carrying one's belongings in a backpack
hike or travel carrying a backpack

Examples

She plans to backpack across Europe after graduation.
They spent two weeks backpacking in the Grand Canyon.
He backpacked through the Andes last summer.

Synset Examples

Every summer they are backpacking in the Rockies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hike - to walk a long distance, especially across country
