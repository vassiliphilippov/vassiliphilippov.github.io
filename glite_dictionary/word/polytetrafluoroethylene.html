polytetrafluoroethylene

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˌtɛtrəˌflʊəroʊˈɛθəliːn / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌtɛtrəˌflʊərəʊˈɛθəliːn / (BrE)
A synthetic polymer used for non-stick coatings on cookware and in industrial applications where materials need to resist sticking and high temperatures.
synthetic non-stick polymer
medical

Examples

The frying pan's surface is coated with polytetrafluoroethylene to prevent food from sticking.
Many laboratories use polytetrafluoroethylene tubing because of its chemical resistance.
Engineers selected polytetrafluoroethylene for parts exposed to extreme heat and friction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teflon - trade name for polytetrafluoroethylene, used for non-stick coatings

Etymology

From poly- (many) + tetrafluoro- (containing four fluorine atoms) + ethylene (an unsaturated hydrocarbon).
