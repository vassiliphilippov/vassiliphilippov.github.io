arrowroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈærəʊˌruːt / (BrE)
a starch obtained from the root of the arrowroot plant, used as a thickening agent in cooking
edible starch from arrowroot plant

Examples

She used arrowroot to thicken the fruit sauce for the dessert.
Many gluten-free recipes call for arrowroot as a substitute for wheat flour.
The chef recommended arrowroot because it produces a clear, glossy sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starch - a carbohydrate used as a thickener in cooking

Etymology

From Spanish *arorut*, probably from an Arawak word.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈarəʊˌruːt / (BrE)
A tropical plant (Maranta arundinacea) with white flowers, native to the West Indies, whose root yields a starchy substance used in cooking.
tropical plant yielding edible starch

Examples

The arrowroot thrives in warm, moist climates with well-drained soil.
Farmers harvested arrowroot to produce the highly valued starch.
She showed us how to identify an arrowroot plant by its distinctive white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowroot starch - fine, starchy powder derived from the root of the arrowroot plant

Etymology

From the arrowroot plant (Maranta arundinacea), so named because the plant's roots were traditionally used to treat wounds from arrows.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛroʊˌrut / (AmE), / ˈærəʊˌruːt / (BrE)
a tropical plant whose edible root is used to produce a fine, easily digestible starch, often used in cooking and baking
edible plant yielding starchy root

Examples

The chef thickened the sauce with arrowroot for a glossy finish.
Arrowroot is a popular gluten-free alternative to wheat flour in baking.
She bought a packet of arrowroot to use in homemade puddings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starch - a substance used in cooking, extracted from plants like arrowroot or corn

Etymology

From arrow + root, originally referring to the plant's use in treating arrow wounds; sense extended to the plant itself.
