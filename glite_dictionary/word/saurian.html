saurian

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔriən / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪən / (BrE)
Any reptile of the suborder Sauria, especially lizards, and formerly including crocodiles and dinosaurs.
reptile of suborder Sauria, such as a lizard

Examples

The museum featured a spectacular fossil of a prehistoric saurian.
Some scientists believe that modern birds evolved from a group of small, feathered saurians.
In Victorian times, the term saurian was also used to describe dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile typically with four legs and external ear openings

Etymology

From modern Latin Sauria, from Greek saura (lizard).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɔriən / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of lizards or lizard-like reptiles
of or relating to lizards or lizard-like reptiles

Examples

The museum showcased several saurian fossils from the Jurassic period.
Unlike mammals, saurian species lay eggs and have scaly skin.
The saurian structure of the creature's limbs suggested it was a reptile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a type of reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, and movable eyelids

Etymology

From Greek 'sauros' (lizard) with Latin adjectival ending '-ian'.
