Strix nebulosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪks nɛbjuˈloʊsə / (AmE), / ˈstrɪks nɛbjʊˈləʊsə / (BrE)
a large owl with a distinctive dish-shaped face, found in northern North America and western Eurasia, also known as the great grey owl.
large northern owl; the great grey owl

Examples

The Strix nebulosa is renowned for its large facial disk and silent flight.
Wildlife photographers travel north to catch a glimpse of the elusive Strix nebulosa.
Unlike many other owls, the Strix nebulosa hunts mainly during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great grey owl - common name for Strix nebulosa, a large owl of the Northern Hemisphere

Etymology

From Latin: 'strix' (owl) and 'nebulosa' (cloudy, misty).
