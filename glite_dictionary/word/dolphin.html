dolphin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlfɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪn / (BrE)
a small, intelligent marine mammal with a streamlined body and a beaklike snout, belonging to the toothed whale family and larger than a porpoise
a small toothed whale with a beaklike snout

Examples

The aquarium has a new exhibit featuring a trained dolphin performing tricks for the audience.
A group of dolphins swam alongside our boat as we crossed the bay.
Scientists have found that the dolphin is one of the most intelligent marine animals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: porpoise - a similar marine mammal with a shorter snout

Etymology

From Greek 'delphin', via Latin 'delphinus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlfɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪn / (BrE)
A large, slender food and game fish (Coryphaena hippurus) found in warm seas, especially around Hawaii, known for its brightly colored body and also called mahi-mahi or dorado.
a large food and game fish (mahi-mahi)

Examples

Fishermen in Hawaii often target dolphin for both sport and food.
The chef prepared a delicious grilled dolphin with tropical salsa.
Unlike the mammal, the dolphin fish is best known for its dazzling colors and speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahi-mahi - another name for the dolphin fish
  • Synonym: dorado - another name for the dolphin fish
  • Antonym: dolphin - aquatic mammal related to whales
