safe

1. NOUN

/ seɪf / (AmE), / seɪf / (BrE)
a ventilated or refrigerated cupboard used for storing food to protect it from pests
cupboard for securing food from pests

Examples

The old farmhouse still had a safe in the kitchen for storing butter and cheese.
She placed the bread in the safe to keep flies away.
In warmer months, the safe was essential for preserving perishable foods.

2. NOUN

/ seɪf / (AmE), / seɪf / (BrE)
A strong container, typically made of metal, used for keeping money, documents, or other valuables secure against theft or damage.
strongbox for valuables

Examples

The jewelry was locked in the safe overnight for protection.
We keep important documents in a fireproof safe at home.
After the robbery, police found the safe empty and its door open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - a secure room or compartment for storing valuables
  • Degree Lower: locker - a small secured compartment for storing personal items

Etymology

From Old French 'sauf', from Latin 'salvus' (safe, unharmed).
