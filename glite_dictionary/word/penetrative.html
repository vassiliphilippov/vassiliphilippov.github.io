penetrative

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪ.trə.tɪv / (BrE)
having the power to enter, pierce, or affect deeply; able to penetrate.
able to enter or pierce

Examples

The scientist wore gloves to avoid absorbing the penetrative chemicals through her skin.
He shivered in the penetrative wind that cut through his coat.
The detective noticed a penetrative gaze from the suspect across the room.

Synset Examples

a toxic penetrative spray applied to the surface

a penetrating odor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penetrating - having the power to enter or pierce deeply
  • Antonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface; not deep or penetrating

Etymology

From Latin penetrare (to enter, pierce) + -ive (forming adjectives).

having or showing an ability to recognize or understand things quickly and deeply; highly perceptive or insightful
having keen insight or perception

Examples

Her penetrative analysis of the problem revealed details everyone else missed.
The journalist is known for his penetrative questions during interviews.
With a penetrative mind, she quickly understood the subtle differences between the two proposals.

Synset Examples

penetrating insight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull - lacking keenness of perception or intellect
