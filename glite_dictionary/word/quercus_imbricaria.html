Quercus imbricaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ɪmˌbrɪˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ɪmˌbrɪˈkɛəriə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to eastern and central United States, known as the shingle oak, with shiny laurel-like leaves and wood used for shingles.
species of oak tree (shingle oak)

Examples

The Quercus imbricaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils across the Midwest.
Many settlers used wood from Quercus imbricaria to make durable shingles for their homes.
Quercus imbricaria is easily distinguished by its glossy, unlobed leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shingle oak - common name for Quercus imbricaria

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'imbricaria' meaning 'overlapping like tiles,' referring to the shape of the leaves.
