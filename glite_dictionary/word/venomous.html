venomous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛnəməs / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəməs / (BrE)
(of an animal or its bite or sting) capable of injecting venom by means of a bite or sting, and thus capable of causing harm or death.
producing and capable of injecting venom

Examples

The bite of a venomous snake requires immediate medical attention.
She recognized the scorpion as a venomous species native to the desert.
Some spiders are harmless, while others are dangerously venomous.

Synset Examples

venomous snakes
a virulent insect bite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisonous - containing or producing poison; harmful if ingested or absorbed

Etymology

From Latin 'venenosus', from 'venenum' (poison).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛnəməs / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəməs / (BrE)
full of deep hatred or intended to harm someone; showing or expressing strong ill will or malice
filled with hatred; deliberately harmful

Examples

Her venomous remarks left everyone in the room feeling hurt and insulted.
He delivered a venomous attack on his critics during the press conference.
The online comments about the politician were unusually venomous and personal.

Synset Examples

poisonous hate
venomous criticism
vicious gossip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm
  • Synonym: malevolent - showing a wish to do evil to others
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly

Etymology

From Latin 'venenosus' (full of poison), figuratively extended to mean malicious or spiteful.
