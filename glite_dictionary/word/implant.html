implant

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to fix or set securely or deeply, especially into a body or other surface
set something securely or deeply

Examples

The surgeon will implant a pacemaker beneath the patient's skin.
Technicians carefully implanted sensors into the concrete foundation for monitoring.
The archaeologist implanted a tracking device in the sculpture for security purposes.

Synset Examples

He planted a knee in the back of his opponent
The dentist implanted a tooth in the gum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embed - to fix something firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass

Etymology

From Latin 'implantare', from 'in-' (into) + 'plantare' (to plant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01531609-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
(of a fertilized egg) to become attached to and embedded in the lining of the uterus.
(of embryo) attach in uterus
medical

Examples

When the fertilized egg successfully implants, pregnancy can proceed normally.
The embryo implanted itself into the uterine wall during the early stage of development.
Doctors monitored how the transferred embryo implanted without intervention.

Synset Examples

The egg fertilized in vitro implanted in the uterus of the birth mother with no further complications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embed - to become fixed firmly in a surrounding substance

Etymology

From Latin 'implantare', meaning to plant in.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01293333-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to establish an idea, belief, or attitude deeply and firmly in someone's mind
instill an idea or belief deeply

Examples

Her teachers tried to implant a love of reading in all their students.
The experience implanted a deep sense of responsibility in him.
Propaganda can implant harmful beliefs in vulnerable minds.

Synset Examples

Plant a thought in the students' minds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instill - to gradually but firmly establish in someone's mind
  • Antonym: to eradicate - to remove or destroy completely from the mind

Etymology

From Latin 'implantare', from 'in-' (into) + 'plantare' (to plant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00736813-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈɪm.plænt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
A device or tissue that is inserted permanently into the body surgically, especially as a medical prosthesis.
permanently inserted medical device
medical

Examples

The surgeon inserted a dental implant to replace the missing tooth.
After the accident, she required a knee implant to regain mobility.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits of having a cochlear implant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthesis - an artificial device that replaces a missing body part

Etymology

From Latin 'implantare', from 'in-' (into) + 'plantare' (to plant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03568890-n
Synset POS: n
