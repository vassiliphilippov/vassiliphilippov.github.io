Sasquatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæskwɑːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsæskwɒtʃ / (BrE)
a large, hairy, humanlike creature said to inhabit remote forests of the northwestern United States and Canada; also called Bigfoot.
legendary hairy creature of North America

Examples

Stories about the elusive Sasquatch have fascinated people for decades.
Some hikers claim to have seen a Sasquatch in the remote forest.
The legend of the Sasquatch is a popular subject in North American folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bigfoot - another name for Sasquatch, legendary creature in North American folklore

Etymology

From Halkomelem (Salishan language) sásq’ets, a local tribal word for the creature.
