Little Mo Connolly

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪt̬.əl ˈmoʊ kəˈnɑː.li / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl ˈməʊ kəˈnɒl.i / (BrE)
(1934–1969) United States tennis player, the first woman to win the United States, British, French, and Australian championships in the same year (1953).
American tennis champion (1934–1969)

Examples

Little Mo Connolly made history by winning all four major tennis championships in 1953.
Tennis fans remember Little Mo Connolly for her remarkable achievements at such a young age.
Inspiring generations of athletes, Little Mo Connolly remains a legendary figure in women's sports.
