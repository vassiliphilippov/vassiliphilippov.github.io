December

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛmbɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmbə / (BrE)
The twelfth and last month of the year, following November and preceding January.
twelfth month of the year

Examples

The holidays in December are always a busy time for families around the world.
We often get the first snowfall of the season in early December.
She plans to graduate in December and start her new job in January.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: January - the first month of the year
  • Degree Lower: November - the eleventh month of the year

Etymology

From Latin 'December' (tenth month), from 'decem' meaning ten, as the Roman calendar began in March.
