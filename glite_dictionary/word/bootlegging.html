bootlegging

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making, distributing, or selling alcoholic liquor illegally
illegal production or sale of alcohol

Examples

During the 1920s, bootlegging was a common way to circumvent Prohibition laws in the United States.
Police raided a warehouse that was being used for bootlegging operations.
He was arrested for bootlegging whiskey across state lines.

Synset Examples

the Prohibition amendment made bootlegging profitable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggling - the act of illegally importing or exporting goods

Etymology

From 'bootleg' (to carry liquor in a boot), used in the US during Prohibition era.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making, selling, or distributing goods illegally or without authorization
illegal making or selling of goods

Examples

Authorities cracked down on bootlegging in the local market, confiscating hundreds of counterfeit DVDs.
During Prohibition, bootlegging of alcohol became a lucrative but dangerous business.
The artist suffered financially due to widespread bootlegging of his music online.

Synset Examples

the bootlegging of videotapes is common in Asia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggling - the act of illegally importing or exporting goods
  • Synonym: piracy - the unauthorized use or reproduction of another's work

Etymology

From 'bootleg', meaning smuggling goods in the legs of boots; originally referred to illegal alcohol; now used more broadly.
