puncher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃə / (BrE)
a tool for making holes or indentations in material such as paper, leather, or metal
tool for making holes or indentations

Examples

The mechanic grabbed a puncher to create small holes in the sheet metal.
She used a puncher to put holes in the stack of documents.
Leatherworkers often rely on a sturdy puncher to do precise work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch - a tool for making holes by driving through material

Etymology

From 'punch' (tool for making holes) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃə / (BrE)
a person who delivers punches, especially in boxing or fighting
person who delivers punches

Examples

He is known as a strong puncher in the boxing world.
The young fighter proved himself to be an aggressive puncher early in his career.
Most professional coaches can spot a talented puncher from their very first match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxer - someone who fights as a sport, especially professionally

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃə / (BrE)
a person, especially a hired hand, who tends cattle and performs duties on horseback, particularly on a ranch in the western United States
cowboy; cattle hand on horseback

Examples

The experienced puncher guided the herd across the open plains.
He worked as a puncher on a Texas ranch for several years.
Stories about the adventures of a puncher often capture the spirit of the Old West.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a person who tends cattle, especially on horseback, in North America

Etymology

Derived from the phrase 'cow-puncher,' used in the American West for cattle handlers.
