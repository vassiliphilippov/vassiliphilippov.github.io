Acorus

1. PROPN

/ əˈkoʊrəs / (AmE), / əˈkɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial wetland plants commonly known as sweet flags, sometimes placed in the family Acoraceae.
genus of sweet flag wetland plants

Examples

The wetlands were dominated by stands of Acorus, their sword-like leaves waving in the breeze.
Botanists debated whether Acorus should be classified in its own family, Acoraceae.
Medicinal uses of Acorus have been documented in traditional herbal practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet flag - a common name for plants of the genus Acorus

Etymology

From Latin *acorus*, from Ancient Greek *ἄκορος* (ákoros), a plant name.
