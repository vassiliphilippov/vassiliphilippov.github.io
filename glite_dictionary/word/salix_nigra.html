Salix nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks ˈnaɪɡrə / (BrE)
A species of willow native to North America, also known as black willow, with dark bark and narrow leaves, commonly found near streams and lakes.
species of willow tree (black willow)

Examples

The Salix nigra is often planted along riverbanks to help control erosion.
Bark from the Salix nigra has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
Many birds build their nests in the branches of the Salix nigra near the water's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black willow - common name for Salix nigra, species of willow tree

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Salix' meaning 'willow' and 'nigra' meaning 'black'.
