dextral

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛk.strəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛk.strəl / (BrE)
preferring to use the right hand, foot, or eye for tasks; relating to right-sided dominance
right-sided dominance (hand, foot, or eye)

Examples

Most people are dextral, using their right hand for writing and most other tasks.
The study compared dextral and sinistral participants in a series of coordination tests.
Her dextral preference was clear when she kicked the ball with her right foot every time.

Synset Examples

dextral individuals exhibit dominance of the right hand and eye

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sinistral - preferring to use the left hand, foot, or eye; left-sided dominance

Etymology

From Latin 'dexter' (right, on the right side) + '-al' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkstrəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛkstrəl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or situated on the right side; right-handed or turning to the right, especially used in biology and anatomy.
on or relating to the right side

Examples

The snail's dextral shell curves to the right as you follow it from the apex.
Geologists can determine fault motion by identifying whether the movement is sinistral or dextral.
The surgeon made an incision on the patient's dextral side.

Synset Examples

a dextral gastropod shell with the apex upward has its opening on the right when facing the observer
a dextral flatfish lies with the right eye uppermost

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sinistral - of or on the left side

Etymology

From Latin 'dexter' (right, on the right side) + '-al'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00746491-a
Synset POS: a
