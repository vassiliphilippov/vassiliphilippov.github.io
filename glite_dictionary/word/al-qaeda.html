Al-Qaeda

1. PROPN

/ ælˈkaɪdə / (AmE), / ælˈkaɪdə / (BrE)
an international militant Islamist organization founded in 1988, known for its opposition to the United States and other governments, and responsible for numerous terrorist attacks worldwide
international Islamist terrorist organization

Examples

The United States government has taken extensive measures to combat Al-Qaeda and its affiliates.
Several countries have been affected by terrorist attacks orchestrated by Al-Qaeda.
Intelligence agencies continue to monitor the activities and communications of Al-Qaeda around the world.

Etymology

From Arabic القاعدة (al-qāʿida), meaning 'the base'.
