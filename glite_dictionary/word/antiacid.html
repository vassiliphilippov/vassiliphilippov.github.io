antiacid

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a substance that counteracts or neutralizes acidity, especially in the stomach
substance that counteracts acidity
medical

Examples

Some people take an antiacid after eating spicy foods to soothe their stomach.
The doctor recommended an antiacid for her occasional heartburn.
Remember to check with your pharmacist before combining any medication with an antiacid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antacid - a substance that neutralizes stomach acidity

Etymology

From anti- (against) + acid.
