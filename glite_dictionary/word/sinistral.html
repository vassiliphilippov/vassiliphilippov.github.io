sinistral

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnɪstrəl / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪstrəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated on the left side; left-handed or left-sided, especially in scientific contexts such as zoology and anatomy.
on or relating to the left side

Examples

The sinistral coiling of the shell makes this snail species easily identifiable.
A sinistral flatfish has both eyes on its left side.
Researchers noted the sinistral dominance in the arrangement of the specimen's body.

Synset Examples

a sinistral gastropod shell with the apex upward has its opening on the left when facing the observer
a sinistral flatfish lies with the left eye uppermost

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dextral - relating to or on the right side

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' meaning 'left.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪn.ə.strəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.ɪ.strəl / (BrE)
(of a person) preferring to use the left hand, foot, or eye; left-handed or left-sided.
left-handed or left-sided

Examples

Among surgeons, being sinistral can sometimes require adapting tools designed for right-handed use.
The artist is uniquely sinistral, painting with her left hand and favoring her left eye.
Research suggests that sinistral children may face different challenges in learning to write.

Synset Examples

sinistral individuals exhibit dominance of the left hand and eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left-handed - using the left hand more skillfully than the right
  • Antonym: dextral - preferring to use right hand, foot, or eye; right-sided

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' meaning 'left'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
