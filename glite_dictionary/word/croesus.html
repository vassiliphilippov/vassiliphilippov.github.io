Croesus

1. PROPN

/ ˈkriːsəs / (AmE), / ˈkriːsəs / (BrE)
the last king of Lydia, renowned for his wealth, who reigned until 546 BC
last king of Lydia, famed for vast wealth

Examples

In ancient times, Croesus was admired across the world for his legendary riches.
Historians often mention Croesus as a symbol of immense wealth and luxury.
The downfall of Croesus marked the end of the Lydian kingdom.

Etymology

From Greek Kroisos (Κροῖσος), Latinized as Croesus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkriːsəs / (AmE), / ˈkriːsəs / (BrE)
a very rich man; a person of great wealth
a very wealthy man

Examples

Since he sold his company, people say he's a Croesus who can afford anything.
She dreamed of marrying a Croesus and living in luxury for the rest of her life.
You don't have to be a Croesus to enjoy the finer things in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tycoon - a wealthy, powerful businessperson
  • Antonym: pauper - a very poor person

Etymology

From Croesus, the famously wealthy king of Lydia in the 6th century BC.
