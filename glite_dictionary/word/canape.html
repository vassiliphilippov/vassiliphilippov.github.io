canape

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænəˌpeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkanəpeɪ / (BrE)
A small, decorative piece of bread or toast with a savory topping, served as an appetizer.
savory appetizer on bread or toast

Examples

The waiter passed a tray of smoked salmon canapes at the reception.
She prepared several different canapes for the party guests to enjoy.
A single canape can be both beautiful and delicious, perfect for a fancy event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hors d'oeuvre - a small dish served before the main part of a meal

Etymology

From French 'canapé', meaning 'sofa', alluding to the toppings sitting on the bread.
