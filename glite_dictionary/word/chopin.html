Chopin

1. PROPN

/ ʃoʊˈpæn, ˈʃoʊpæn / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpæn, ʃoʊˈpæn / (BrE)
(1810–1849) A Polish-born French composer and pianist, regarded as one of the leading musicians of the Romantic period.
Polish-French Romantic composer and pianist

Examples

Many pianists admire the lyrical quality of Chopin's nocturnes.
The concert featured several pieces by Chopin, delighting classical music lovers.
As a young musician, she was inspired by Chopin's expressive piano works.

Etymology

From the surname 'Chopin,' of French origin.
