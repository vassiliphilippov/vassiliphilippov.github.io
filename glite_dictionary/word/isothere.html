isothere

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌθɪr / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌθɪə / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting points that have the same mean summer temperature.
line connecting equal mean summer temperatures

Examples

The isothere clearly marks the regions sharing the same average summer temperature on the climatological map.
Meteorologists used an isothere to study heat distribution across the continent during the summer.
When comparing temperature data, tracing the isothere helps visualize patterns of seasonal warmth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isotherm - a line on a map connecting points of equal temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) + 'thermē' (heat).
