Macadamia integrifolia

1. NOUN

/ məˌkædəˈmiːə ˌɪntəɡrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / məˌkædəˈmiːə ˌɪntɪɡrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia, valued for its creamy-white flowers and edible nuts.
Australian tree with edible nuts

Examples

The orchard specializes in growing Macadamia integrifolia, renowned for its high-quality nuts.
Botanists studied the flowering patterns of Macadamia integrifolia in its native Australian habitat.
Several farms in Queensland cultivate Macadamia integrifolia commercially for global export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia - the tree or its edible nut

Etymology

Genus name 'Macadamia' honors John Macadam, a Scottish-born Australian chemist. Species name from Latin 'integrifolia' (whole-leaved).
