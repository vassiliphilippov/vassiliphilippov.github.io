minivan

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪniˌvan / (BrE)
a small, box-shaped passenger van with removable seats, often used as a family car
small passenger van for families

Examples

They decided to buy a minivan to accommodate their large family and all the luggage.
The minivan has sliding doors, making it easy for children to get in and out.
We rented a minivan for the road trip because it offered plenty of space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: van - a medium- to large-sized motor vehicle used for transporting goods or people

Etymology

Formed from 'mini-' (small) and 'van', first used in the US in the late 20th century.
