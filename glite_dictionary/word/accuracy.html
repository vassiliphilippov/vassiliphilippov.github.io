accuracy

1. NOUN

/ ˈækjɚəsi / (AmE), / ˈækjʊrəsi / (BrE)
The quality or state of being correct or precise, especially how close something is to the true or accepted value.
state of being correct or precise

Examples

The accuracy of the laboratory results was verified several times.
Modern GPS devices offer remarkable accuracy in tracking your location.
His statement was questioned because the accuracy of his data could not be confirmed.

Synset Examples

he was beginning to doubt the accuracy of his compass
the lawyer questioned the truth of my account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precision - the quality of being exact, often with fine detail
  • Antonym: inaccuracy - the state of being incorrect or not precise

Etymology

From Latin accuratia, from accuratus (carefully done).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæk.jɚ.ə.si / (AmE), / ˈæk.jʊ.rə.si / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The number of significant figures with which a value is expressed, indicating the degree of exactness of a number.
degree of exactness in a number

Examples

The accuracy of a measurement improves with more significant figures.
Scientific calculations require accuracy to minimize errors.
A calculator can display results with greater accuracy than manual computation.

Synset Examples

the atomic clock enabled scientists to measure time with much greater accuracy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precision - exactness, degree of detail in measurement

Etymology

From Latin 'accuratia' (care, exactness), from 'accuratus'.
