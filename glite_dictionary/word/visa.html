visa

1. VERB

/ ˈviːzə / (AmE), / ˈviːzə / (BrE)
to mark or endorse a passport with an official visa, permitting entry into or exit from a country
endorse a passport with a visa

Examples

The consulate refused to visa his passport without additional documentation.
You must have your passport visaed before traveling to that country.
The official carefully visaed each passport before handing it back to the traveler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - to mark a document officially

Etymology

From Latin 'charta visa' (paper that has been seen), later adopted into French as 'visa.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈviːsə / (AmE), / ˈviːzə / (BrE)
an official mark or endorsement placed in a passport, allowing the holder to enter, leave, or stay for a specified period in a particular country
endorsement in passport for entry to a country

Examples

She needed a visa to study abroad for a year in France.
The embassy denied his visa application due to incomplete documents.
Many tourists apply for a visa before traveling to the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - an official document giving someone authorization to do something
  • Synonym: passport - an official government document allowing travel abroad and certifying identity and citizenship

Etymology

From Latin 'charta visa' (paper that has been seen), via French 'visa' (stamp).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06700628-n
Synset POS: n
