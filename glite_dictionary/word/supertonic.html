supertonic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(Music) The second degree or note of a diatonic scale, situated one step above the tonic.
second note of a diatonic scale

Examples

In the key of C major, D is the supertonic note.
The melody moves from the tonic to the supertonic before resolving.
A common chord progression uses the supertonic to add tension.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tonic - the first note of a diatonic scale
  • Degree Higher: mediant - the third note of a diatonic scale

Etymology

From Latin 'super-' meaning 'above' and 'tonic', referring to its position above the tonic in the scale.
