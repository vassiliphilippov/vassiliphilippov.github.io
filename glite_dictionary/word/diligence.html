diligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
careful and persistent effort or work
persistent, careful effort

Examples

Without diligence, it is difficult to achieve long-term goals.
The project was a success thanks to her persistence and diligence.
Employers value diligence just as much as talent or skill.

Synset Examples

it is a job requiring serious application

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industriousness - quality of being hard-working and persistent
  • Antonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'diligentia' (care, attentiveness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
careful and persistent effort or work; the quality of being diligent.
persistent effort or work

Examples

Her diligence in completing every assignment impressed all of her teachers.
The team's diligence paid off when they finished the project ahead of schedule.
He rose quickly through the company due to his diligence and commitment.

Synset Examples

his diligence won him quick promotions
frugality and industry are still regarded as virtues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industry - persistent application to work or duty
  • Antonym: laziness - the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy

Etymology

From Latin 'diligentia' (attentiveness, carefulness), from 'diligere' (to value highly, take delight in).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
careful and persistent effort or attention to a task or duty
careful, persistent effort

Examples

Her diligence in completing the project ahead of schedule impressed her supervisors.
Teachers appreciate students who approach their work with diligence and focus.
Through sheer diligence, he was able to master the complex material.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or care when doing something
  • Synonym: industry - habitual diligence, especially in work

Etymology

From Latin 'diligentia' (care, attentiveness), from 'diligere' (to esteem, to love).
