reverence

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvərəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərəns / (BrE)
A gesture or act, such as a bow or curtsy, showing deep respect or honor for someone or something.
an act of showing deep respect

Examples

The knight performed a deep reverence before the queen.
As a sign of reverence, the children bowed their heads in silence.
He greeted the elderly priest with a humble reverence at the church door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - a feeling or expression of esteem or honor
  • Antonym: disrespect - lack of respect or courtesy

Etymology

From Latin reverentia (awe, respect), from revereri (stand in awe of).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvərəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərəns / (BrE)
A feeling or attitude of deep respect, veneration, or awe.
deep respect or veneration

Examples

The students listened in reverence as the survivor shared her story.
Many cultures show reverence for their ancestors through special rituals.
His voice was full of reverence when he spoke about his mentor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
  • Antonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless or beneath consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'reverentia' (awe, respect), from 'revereri' (to stand in awe of).

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛvərəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərəns / (BrE)
to regard with deep respect or awe; to treat as sacred or exalted
regard with deep respect or awe

Examples

Many people reverence their ancestors and uphold family traditions.
The students reverenced their teacher for his wisdom and kindness.
She reverences the ancient texts as the foundation of her beliefs.

  • Antonym: to despise - to feel strong dislike or lack of respect
