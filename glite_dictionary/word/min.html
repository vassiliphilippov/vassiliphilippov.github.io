Min

1. PROPN

/ mɪn / (AmE), / mɪn / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian god associated with procreation, fertility, and reproduction.
Egyptian god of fertility

Examples

Min was worshipped as the god of fertility in ancient Egypt.
Statues of Min were often carried in processions celebrating the harvest.
The cult of Min was especially prominent in the city of Akhmim.

2. NOUN

/ min / (AmE), / mɪn / (BrE)
any of several Sinitic languages and dialects spoken primarily in Fujian province and parts of southeast China, known collectively as Min Chinese.
a group of Chinese languages of Fujian province

Examples

Many emigrants from Fujian speak Min dialects at home.
Linguists classify Hokkien as a variety of Min Chinese.
She is fluent in both Mandarin and Min.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mandarin - the most widely spoken group of Chinese languages

Etymology

From Chinese (閩, Mǐn), the name of Fujian province and its main river.
