acerate

1. ADJ

/ ˈæsəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈæsəˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of leaves or other objects) narrow, long, and pointed in shape, like a needle; needle-shaped.
needle-shaped (esp. leaves)

Examples

The pine tree's acerate leaves allow it to reduce water loss in dry environments.
Some desert plants have acerate foliage that helps them survive harsh conditions.
Botanists use the term acerate to describe narrow, pointed features in certain plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acicular - needle-shaped, especially in reference to crystals or leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'aceratus', from 'acies' (sharpness; point).
