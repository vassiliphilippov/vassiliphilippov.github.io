rails

1. NOUN

/ reɪlz / (AmE), / reɪlz / (BrE)
parallel bars of steel that form the tracks along which trains and other railway vehicles travel
parallel bars forming a railway track

Examples

The train glided smoothly along the rails as it sped toward the next station.
Workers inspected the rails for damage after the storm had passed.
Snow had accumulated on the rails, leading to a delay in service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - the pair of rails on which trains run
  • Synonym: roadbed - the foundation or bed supporting railroad tracks

Etymology

Plural of 'rail', from Old French *reille* (iron bar), from Latin *regula* (rule, straight bar).
