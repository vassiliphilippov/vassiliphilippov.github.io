detente

1. NOUN

/ deɪˈtɑnt / (AmE), / deɪˈtɒ̃t / (BrE)
the easing of hostility or strained relations, especially between countries
easing of tense relations, especially between nations

Examples

The signing of the treaty ushered in a period of detente between the two superpowers.
Leaders agreed on economic cooperation as a step toward detente.
The policy of detente helped reduce the risk of nuclear conflict during the Cold War.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hostility - state of being hostile; unfriendly or antagonistic relations

Etymology

From French détente (loosening, relaxation), from Old French destendre (to loosen), from Latin dis- (apart) + tendere (to stretch).
