miniver

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪvər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪvə / (BrE)
a kind of trimming made from white or light grey fur, often used on ceremonial or royal robes
white or grey fur used on ceremonial robes

Examples

The king's robe was trimmed with miniver to symbolize his royal status.
In medieval portraits, nobles are often depicted in garments edged with miniver.
The historian explained that miniver was considered a luxury fur in the Middle Ages.

Etymology

From Old French *menu vair* (small vair), vair being a type of fur.
