details

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːˌteɪlz / (AmE), / ˈdiːteɪlz / (BrE)
information about specific facts or circumstances, often of a private, confidential, or precise nature.
specific facts, often confidential

Examples

The journalist refused to publish the details of the classified report.
He promised to share the details of the agreement after the announcement.
Only those with clearance are allowed to know the details of the investigation.

Synset Examples

after the trial he gave us the real details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: information - facts or knowledge provided or learned

Etymology

From French 'détail' (itemized piece), from 'détailler' (to cut in pieces).
