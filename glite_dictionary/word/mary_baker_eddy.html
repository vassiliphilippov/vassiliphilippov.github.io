Mary Baker Eddy

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ˈbeɪkər ˈɛdi / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ˈbeɪkə ˈɛdi / (BrE)
(1821–1910) The American religious leader who founded the Church of Christ, Scientist, known as Christian Science, in 1866.
founder of Christian Science

Examples

Mary Baker Eddy established the Church of Christ, Scientist, in Boston in 1879.
Her writings, especially by Mary Baker Eddy, laid the groundwork for Christian Science teachings.
Many people have studied the life of Mary Baker Eddy to understand the origins of her faith.
