Swahili

1. NOUN

/ swɑːˈhiːli / (AmE), / swɑːˈhiːli / (BrE)
a Bantu language that is the official language of Kenya and Tanzania and widely used as a lingua franca in East and Central Africa
a Bantu language spoken in East Africa

Examples

She is fluent in Swahili after living in Tanzania for several years.
Students can choose to study Swahili as a foreign language at the university.
Many East African countries use Swahili as a common means of communication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bantu - a group of Niger-Congo languages spoken in central and southern Africa

Etymology

From Arabic 'Sawāḥil', plural of 'sāḥil' (coast), referring to the coastal people of East Africa.
