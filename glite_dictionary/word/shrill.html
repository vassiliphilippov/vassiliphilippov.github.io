shrill

1. ADJ

/ ʃrɪl / (AmE), / ʃrɪl / (BrE)
(of a sound) having a high-pitched and piercing quality; sharp and intense in tone.
high-pitched, piercing (sound)

Examples

The teacher's shrill voice easily rose above the noise in the classroom.
A shrill whistle signaled the end of the game.
Her laughter became almost shrill with excitement.

Synset Examples

a shrill whistle
a shrill gaiety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piercing - having a sharp or intense quality, especially a sound
  • Antonym: mellow - (of a sound) soft, rich, and pleasant

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrille', probably of Germanic origin; related to Swedish 'skråla' (to bawl).

2. VERB

/ ʃrɪl / (AmE), / ʃrɪl / (BrE)
to utter or make a high-pitched, piercing sound or cry.
make a high-pitched, piercing cry

Examples

The child suddenly shrilled in fright when she saw the dog.
A whistle shrilled across the field, signaling the end of the match.
She shrilled his name, desperate to get his attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - to make a loud, high-pitched cry
  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly

Etymology

From Middle English shril, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00916373-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ʃrɪl / (AmE), / ʃrɪl / (BrE)
(of a sound or manner) being sharply insistent, forceful, or piercing in making itself heard; often unpleasantly loud or critical.
sharply insistent or piercingly forceful

Examples

The critics made a shrill call for the company's immediate resignation.
His shrill accusations lost him many supporters among the public.
There was a shrill urgency in her voice during the debate.

Synset Examples

strident demands
shrill criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strident - loud and harsh; grating
  • Antonym: gentle - moderate in effect or force; not harsh or forceful

Etymology

From Middle English *shril* (piercing), probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00717282-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ʃrɪl / (AmE), / ʃrɪl / (BrE)
(of a color) excessively bright and gaudy, unpleasantly vivid or garish.
(of color) overly bright, gaudy

Examples

The artist avoided using shrill colors in the portrait to maintain a calm mood.
Some fashion designers prefer shrill shades to create bold, attention-grabbing looks.
A shrill turquoise dominated the painting, making it difficult to look at for long.

Synset Examples

a shrill turquoise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garish - unpleasantly bright or showy (especially of color)
  • Antonym: subdued - (of color) not bright or harsh, muted
Source: WordNet
