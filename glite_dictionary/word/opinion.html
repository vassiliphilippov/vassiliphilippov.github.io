opinion

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
a formal written statement by a judge or a court explaining the reasons for a legal decision
legal document explaining a judge's reasoning
legal

Examples

The Supreme Court issued its opinion on the case yesterday.
Judge Walker wrote a dissenting opinion challenging the majority's ruling.
The lawyer carefully studied the court's opinion before preparing his response.

Synset Examples

opinions are usually written by a single judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - the official decision given by a court on a legal matter

Etymology

From Latin opinionem (belief, notion).

3. NOUN

/ əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
A belief or sentiment held by most people; the collective view or prevailing sentiment of a community or group.
collective belief or prevailing sentiment

Examples

Public opinion can have a strong influence on government decisions.
The survey was conducted to measure opinion on environmental issues.
His statement went against the prevailing opinion in the community.

Synset Examples

he asked for a poll of public opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: view - a belief or way of thinking about something
  • Synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion
  • Antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true (the opposite of opinion)

Etymology

From Old French 'opinion', from Latin 'opinione', from 'opinari' (to think, believe).
