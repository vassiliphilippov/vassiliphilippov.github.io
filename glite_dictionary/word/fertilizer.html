fertilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance added to soil to improve its fertility and promote plant growth
substance added to soil to aid plant growth

Examples

The farmer applied fertilizer to the fields to boost the harvest.
Organic fertilizer is preferred by many gardeners because it is environmentally friendly.
Too much fertilizer can harm plants rather than help them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manure - animal dung used to fertilize land

Etymology

From French 'fertiliser' (to fertilize) and '-er' (agent noun suffix).
