loaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
containing or carrying as much or as many as possible; filled with a great quantity.
filled with a great quantity

Examples

The table was loaded with delicious cakes and pastries.
She handed me a loaded shopping bag that was too heavy to lift easily.
After the move, the truck was loaded with boxes from floor to ceiling.

Synset Examples

a tray loaded with dishes
table laden with food
‘ladened’ is not current usage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laden - heavily loaded or weighed down

Etymology

From the verb 'load', from Old English 'ladan' (to load, lead).

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of statements or questions) phrased in a way that suggests a particular answer or causes someone to be influenced or misled.
phrased to mislead or influence

Examples

The lawyer objected to the loaded question during the trial.
Political debates are often filled with loaded statements and arguments.
Try to avoid asking loaded questions in the survey.

Synset Examples

a loaded question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leading - (of a question) intended to prompt a particular answer
  • Antonym: neutral - (of a question or statement) free from bias or influence
