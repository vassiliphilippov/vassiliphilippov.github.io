tuckahoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌk.əˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʌk.ə.həʊ / (BrE)
a perennial herb of the eastern United States with arrowhead-shaped leaves, an elongate pointed spathe, and green berries
a perennial herb of eastern US

Examples

The wetland was filled with tuckahoe growing along the creek banks.
Native Americans once used tuckahoe as a source of food in this region.
She photographed the distinctive arrow-shaped leaves of the tuckahoe for her field guide.

Etymology

From Powhatan (Algonquian language), originally referring to certain edible roots used by Native Americans.
