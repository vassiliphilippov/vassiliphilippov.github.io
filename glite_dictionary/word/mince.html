mince

1. NOUN

/ mɪns / (AmE), / mɪns / (BrE)
Food, especially meat, that has been cut or ground into very small pieces.
finely chopped or ground food, esp. meat

Examples

The chef added a generous portion of mince to the sauce.
We bought some beef mince for tonight's dinner.
Vegetarian mince can be used instead of ground meat in many recipes.

Synset Examples

a mince of mushrooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground meat - meat that has been ground or minced
  • Antonym: chunk - a thick, solid piece of something

Etymology

From Old French *mincier* (to chop finely), from Vulgar Latin *minutiare*, from Latin *minutia* (smallness, fineness).

2. VERB

/ mɪns / (AmE), / mɪns / (BrE)
to walk with short, dainty steps, often in an affected or delicate manner
walk with short, dainty steps

Examples

She minced down the street in her new heels, careful not to scuff them.
The actor minced across the stage to exaggerate the character's nervousness.
He minced through the puddles, trying not to get his shoes wet.

3. VERB

/ mɪns / (AmE), / mɪns / (BrE)
to cut food, especially meat or vegetables, into very small pieces, often using a special tool or machine
cut into very small pieces

Examples

You need to mince the onions before adding them to the pan.
She carefully minced the fresh herbs for the sauce.
After mincing the garlic, mix it into the dressing.

Synset Examples

mince the garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chop - to cut into pieces, usually with repeated sharp blows
  • Synonym: dice - to cut food into small cubes

Etymology

From Old French 'mincier', from Vulgar Latin *minutiare (to cut into small pieces).
