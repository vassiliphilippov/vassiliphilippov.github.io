Chrysosplenium americanum

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsoʊˈspliːniəm əˌmɛrɪˈkænəm / (AmE), / krɪsɒˈspliːniəm əˌmɛrɪˈkanəm / (BrE)
A species of aquatic herb with yellowish flowers, native to central and western United States.
species of aquatic herb native to US

Examples

The wetlands were home to Chrysosplenium americanum, which added a splash of yellow to the spring landscape.
Botanists documented several clusters of Chrysosplenium americanum along the riverbanks.
Because Chrysosplenium americanum prefers moist soil, it thrives in shallow streams and marshy areas.

Etymology

From Latin genus Chrysosplenium (from Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'splēn' (spleen), referring to the leaf color and former medicinal use) and species epithet 'americanum', meaning 'of America'.
