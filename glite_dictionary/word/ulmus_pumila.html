Ulmus pumila

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs pjuːˈmaɪlə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs pjuːˈmɪlə / (BrE)
a fast-growing shrubby elm tree native to Asia and naturalized in the United States, often used for shelter or ornament
a shrubby Asian elm tree species

Examples

The city planted several Ulmus pumila along the new boulevard.
Gardeners appreciate Ulmus pumila for its rapid growth and resilience in harsh climates.
The leaves of Ulmus pumila are smaller than those of most native elms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siberian elm - common name for Ulmus pumila
  • Degree Higher: elm - tree of the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Ulmus' means 'elm' and 'pumila' means 'dwarf' or 'small'.
