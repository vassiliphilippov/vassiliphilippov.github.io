skepful

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛp.fəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛp.fʊl / (BrE)
The amount that a skep can hold, typically referring to a full load of a skep (such as straw, grain, or bees).
amount a skep can hold

Examples

The farmer brought in a skepful of grain from the field.
They filled each basket with a skepful of honeycomb during the harvest.
She carried a skepful of straw into the barn on her shoulder.

Etymology

Formed from 'skep' (a basket, usually for bees) + '-ful' (denoting 'as much as will fill').
