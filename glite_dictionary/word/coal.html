coal

1. VERB

/ koʊl / (AmE), / kəʊl / (BrE)
to supply (a vehicle, engine, or vessel) with coal as fuel.
to supply with coal

Examples

The steamship stopped at the port to coal before continuing its journey.
Workers coaled the locomotive early in the morning for its daily run.
During the voyage, the ship was coaled several times at different stations.

2. NOUN

/ koʊl / (AmE), / kəʊl / (BrE)
a piece of wood or coal left from a fire that glows or smolders with heat
a glowing piece from a fire

Examples

The campfire had died down, leaving only a single glowing coal in the ashes.
He picked up a hot coal with a pair of tongs to light his pipe.
A spark from a burning coal set the old newspaper alight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ember - a small piece of burning or glowing coal or wood in a dying fire

Etymology

From Old English 'col' (charcoal, coal), related to German 'Kohle'.

Examples

4. NOUN

/ koʊl / (AmE), / kəʊl / (BrE)
a hard, black or brownish-black sedimentary rock that burns and is used as a fuel, formed from fossilized plant material
a black rock used as fuel

Examples

The power plant burns coal to generate electricity for the city.
Workers loaded tons of coal onto freight trains for delivery.
Concerns over pollution have led to a decline in coal mining in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charcoal - a black, porous form of carbon made from burning wood, used as fuel
  • Degree Lower: peat - an accumulation of partially decayed vegetation matter, sometimes used as fuel
  • Degree Higher: anthracite - a hard, high-grade form of coal with high carbon content

Etymology

From Old English *col* (charcoal, coal), related to Old High German *koli*.
