Laozi

1. PROPN

/ laʊˈdzɪ / (AmE), / laʊˈtsɪ / (BrE)
A Chinese philosopher of the 6th century BC, regarded as the founder of Taoism and the reputed author of the Tao Te Ching.
founder of Taoism, Chinese philosopher

Examples

Many modern thinkers draw inspiration from Laozi's teachings in the Tao Te Ching.
Some scholars debate whether Laozi was a historical individual or a legendary figure.
The philosophy of Laozi emphasizes harmony with the natural order of the universe.

Etymology

From Mandarin 老子 (Lǎozǐ), meaning 'Old Master'.
