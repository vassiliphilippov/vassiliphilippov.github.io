anarchist

1. NOUN

/ ˈænərkɪst / (AmE), / ˈanəkɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes in or tries to bring about a society without government or laws; an advocate of anarchism.
person who advocates anarchism

Examples

The anarchist rejected the authority of the state and campaigned for a society based on voluntary cooperation.
During the protest, several known anarchists distributed pamphlets advocating for the abolition of government.
Her grandfather was an outspoken anarchist who was imprisoned for his beliefs in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - person advocating or engaged in political revolution
  • Antonym: authoritarian - person favoring strict obedience to authority

Etymology

From French 'anarchiste', from Greek 'anarkhos' (without a chief, ruler).
