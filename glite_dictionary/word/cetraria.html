Cetraria

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtreɪriə / (AmE), / sɛˈtrɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of foliose lichens found chiefly in northern latitudes.
genus of foliose lichens

Examples

Cetraria is commonly found in arctic and alpine regions around the world.
Scientists studied the chemical compounds present in Cetraria species for medicinal uses.
The foliose thallus of Cetraria makes it easily distinguishable from other lichens.

Etymology

From New Latin *Cetraria*, from Latin *cetra* (a small shield), referring to the lichen's shape.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈtreɪriə / (AmE), / sɛˈtrɑːriə / (BrE)
A genus of foliose lichens found chiefly in northern latitudes.
genus of northern foliose lichens

Examples

The Cetraria species can be found growing abundantly on Arctic tundra landscapes.
Botanists studied the unique properties of Cetraria in their research on northern lichens.
Some Cetraria lichens have been used in traditional remedies in Scandinavian countries.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek kētrárion (a kind of plant or lichen).
