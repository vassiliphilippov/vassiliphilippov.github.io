asphodel

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsfəˌdɛl / (AmE), / ˈæsfədɛl / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the Mediterranean region, especially of the genera Asphodeline and Asphodelus, with narrow leaves and clusters of white, pink, or yellow flowers
Mediterranean plant with narrow leaves and clusters of flowers

Examples

The ancient Greeks often planted asphodel around graves as a symbol of the afterlife.
A row of asphodels lined the path, their pale flowers glowing in the evening light.
In spring, the meadows are dotted with blooming asphodel, adding beauty to the landscape.

Etymology

From Greek asphodelos (a type of plant), via Latin asphodelus.
