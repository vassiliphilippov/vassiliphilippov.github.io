to tribulate

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to oppress or trouble greatly; to afflict with suffering or distress
to oppress or afflict greatly

Examples

Throughout history, many regimes have sought to tribulate those who opposed them.
The villagers were tribulated by years of drought and famine.
She felt increasingly tribulated by the relentless pressures of her demanding job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to afflict - to cause pain or suffering to
  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Latin 'tribulare' (to press, oppress, afflict).
