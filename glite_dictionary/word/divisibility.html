divisibility

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌvɪzɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be divided into parts or sections, especially without remainder or damage
quality of being divisible

Examples

The divisibility of the cake made it easy to share among all the children.
Mathematicians often discuss the divisibility of numbers when exploring number theory.
Legal experts questioned the divisibility of the estate among the heirs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indivisibility - the quality of not being able to be divided
  • Synonym: divisible - able to be divided; capable of division

Etymology

From Medieval Latin divisibilitas, from Latin divisibilis ‘divisible’.
