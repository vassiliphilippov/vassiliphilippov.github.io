splattering

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsplæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a noise produced by something spattering or sputtering suddenly and explosively
explosive spattering noise

Examples

The splattering of rain against the window kept him awake all night.
An abrupt splattering echoed from the kitchen as the oil hit the hot pan.
We heard a loud splattering from the pipes when the heating turned on.

Synset Examples

he heard a spatter of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - a short burst of noise or liquid striking a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'splatter,' possibly imitative in origin, referring to the sound of liquid striking a surface.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplæt̬ərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsplætərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of splashing or scattering liquid or semi-liquid substance onto a surface, often resulting in spots or drops
the act of splashing a liquid on a surface

Examples

The splattering of paint on the floor made the artist's studio look lively.
She heard the splattering of rain against the window as the storm intensified.
Cooking bacon without a lid led to a messy splattering of grease on the stove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splashing - the act of causing liquid to hit or cover something in drops

Etymology

Derives from the verb 'splatter', which is imitative in origin, resembling the sound made by a splash.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00719000-n
Synset POS: n
