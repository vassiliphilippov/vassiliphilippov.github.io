animator

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌmeɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈænɪˌmeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who creates animated cartoons or sequences by making drawings, models, or computer images appear to move
person who creates animated cartoons or sequences

Examples

The animator spent months bringing the film's characters to life using computer graphics.
After studying art, she became a lead animator at a major studio.
Each animator contributed their unique style to the animated short.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartoonist - artist who draws cartoons

Etymology

From Latin animare (to give life, to animate), via French and Italian animateur/animatore.

2. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌmeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈanɪmeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who gives energy, enthusiasm, or spirit to others; one who enlivens a group or activity
person who inspires or energizes others

Examples

Her presence at the meeting was that of an animator, bringing everyone together with energy and purpose.
As the animator of the group, he made sure every event was lively and fun.
The teacher acted as an animator, encouraging students to participate and share their ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspirer - a person who inspires or motivates others
  • Synonym: energizer - a person who provides energy and enthusiasm to others
  • Antonym: demotivator - a person who causes others to lose motivation or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin animātor (one who animates), from animāre (to give life, to animate).
