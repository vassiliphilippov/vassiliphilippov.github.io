metrification

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adopting the metric system of measurement in place of imperial or other systems
adoption of the metric system

Examples

The government's metrification of road signs was completed over several years.
Opponents of metrification argued in favor of keeping traditional units of measurement.
Businesses faced challenges during the metrification process but eventually adapted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metrication - the process of adopting the metric system

Etymology

From metric + -fication, modeled after Latin-derived process nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of composing text in metrical form, or the arrangement of a composition's metrical structure
composition in metrical form

Examples

The poet devoted hours to the metrification of his latest piece, ensuring each line followed a precise rhythm.
Some scholars argue that metrification can transform even simple prose into a compelling work of art.
Her study focused on the metrification of classical epics across different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: versification - the art or practice of composing verses

Etymology

From 'metric' + '-fication', patterned after similar formations (e.g., versification).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00933115-n
Synset POS: n
