Cyprus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
An island country in the eastern Mediterranean Sea, known for its long history and divided governance.
island country in the eastern Mediterranean

Examples

Many tourists are drawn to Cyprus for its sunny beaches and ancient ruins.
The peace process in Cyprus remains a topic of international interest.
She spent her summer volunteering in a small village in Cyprus.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κύπρος (Kúpros), the name for the island.
