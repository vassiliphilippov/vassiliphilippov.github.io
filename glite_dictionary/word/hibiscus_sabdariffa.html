Hibiscus sabdariffa

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs sæb.dəˈrɪfə / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs sab.daˈrɪfə / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the mallow family, cultivated for its fleshy, edible calyces used in jellies and beverages, and for its strong bast fiber.
plant with edible calyces and fiber

Examples

Many people in West Africa brew a bright red tea from the Hibiscus sabdariffa plant.
The calyces of Hibiscus sabdariffa are often used to make jams, sauces, and jelly.
Farmers grow Hibiscus sabdariffa primarily for its edible parts and natural fiber.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Hibiscus and the species name sabdariffa, of unknown origin.
