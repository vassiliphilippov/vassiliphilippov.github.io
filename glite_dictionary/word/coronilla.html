coronilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the pea family, typically with purple, pink, or yellow flowers arranged in heads or umbels.
genus of flowering plants in the pea family

Examples

The coronilla adds a splash of yellow to the early spring garden.
Botanists have classified several species under the genus coronilla.
I planted coronilla along the pathway for its delicate, fragrant flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'coronilla', diminutive of 'corona' (crown), referring to the crown-like flower heads.
