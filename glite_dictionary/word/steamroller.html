steamroller

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmˌroʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊlə / (BrE)
a vehicle equipped with heavy, wide, smooth rollers, used to compact and level roads, pavements, or surfaces during construction
vehicle for compacting road surfaces

Examples

The old steamroller rumbled down the street, flattening the newly laid asphalt.
During the parade, a vintage steamroller drew curious glances from the crowd.
The construction crew used a steamroller to smooth the surface of the parking lot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: road roller - vehicle with rollers for compacting roads, not necessarily steam-powered

Etymology

From 'steam' + 'roller', originally denoting a road roller powered by steam.
