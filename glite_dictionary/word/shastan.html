Shastan

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæstən / (AmE), / ˈʃæstən / (BrE)
A group of closely related Native American languages of the Hokan family, formerly spoken in northern California and southern Oregon.
group of Native American languages (Hokan family)

Examples

Linguists have studied the Shastan languages to understand their connection to the Hokan family.
Few speakers of Shastan languages remain today, making preservation efforts critical.
The museum exhibited artifacts from tribes who spoke various Shastan languages.

Etymology

From 'Shasta', the name of an indigenous people and region in California, with -an suffix indicating relatedness.
