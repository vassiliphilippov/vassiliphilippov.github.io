closeout

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊz.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkləʊz.aʊt / (BrE)
a sale in which goods are sold at reduced prices because the business is closing or wants to dispose of remaining stock
sale to dispose of remaining stock

Examples

The store is having a big closeout on all electronics this weekend.
He bought the furniture at a closeout and saved a lot of money.
After announcing their closure, the shop held a final closeout to sell off remaining inventory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearance sale - a sale to clear out old stock, often at reduced prices
