voyeuristic

1. ADJ

/ vɔɪ.jɚˈɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / vɔɪˈjʊə.rɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
having or showing an excessive interest in observing the private or intimate affairs of others, especially in a sexual context.
relating to voyeurism or voyeurs

Examples

The film was criticized for its voyeuristic depiction of the characters' private lives.
He took a voyeuristic pleasure in reading other people's personal emails.
Her voyeuristic tendencies made others uncomfortable at the party.

Synset Examples

his voyeuristic pleasures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prying - curious about other people's affairs
  • Antonym: uninquisitive - not inclined to inquire about others' business

Etymology

From French 'voyeur' (one who sees), from 'voir' (to see), plus English '-istic'.
