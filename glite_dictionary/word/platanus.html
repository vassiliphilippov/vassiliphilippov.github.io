Platanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplætənəs / (AmE), / ˈplætanəs / (BrE)
a genus of large deciduous trees with broad leaves, including the London plane and American sycamore
genus of plane trees

Examples

The Platanus genus includes some of the most recognizable trees in city landscapes.
Botanists studied the leaves of several Platanus species found along the riverbank.
The Platanus, or plane tree, is valued for its resistance to urban pollution.

Etymology

From Latin *platanus*, from Ancient Greek *plátanos* (plane tree).
