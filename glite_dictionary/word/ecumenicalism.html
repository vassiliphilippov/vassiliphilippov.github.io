ecumenicalism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkjuməˈnɪkəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌkjuːmɪˈnɪkəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Christianity) the belief in or advocacy of promoting cooperation and better understanding among different Christian denominations, aiming for universal Christian unity.
belief in Christian denominational unity

Examples

The church embraced ecumenicalism to foster unity with neighboring Christian communities.
Many modern theologians see ecumenicalism as essential for bridging denominational divides.
Debates about ecumenicalism have shaped major religious conferences over the last century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenism - the idea or principle of promoting unity among the world's Christian churches

Etymology

From ecumenical, ultimately from Greek oikoumenikos (of the (inhabited) world).
