yip

1. VERB

/ jɪp / (AmE), / jɪp / (BrE)
to bark or cry in a short, high-pitched tone, especially like a small dog or puppy
to bark in a high-pitched tone

Examples

The puppies began to yip with excitement when their owner entered the room.
Coyotes sometimes yip at night, filling the air with their high calls.
I could hear my neighbor's little dog yipping at the mail carrier.

Synset Examples

the puppies yelped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yelp - to give a sharp, high-pitched cry or bark
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01050043-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ jɪp / (AmE), / jɪp / (BrE)
a sharp, high-pitched cry or bark, especially made by a dog or similar animal
sharp, high-pitched animal cry

Examples

The puppy let out a sudden yip when it was startled by the noise.
We heard the yip of a fox echoing through the forest at night.
Each excited yip from the dogs made the children laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yelp - a short, sharp cry, especially of pain or alarm
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07415152-n
Synset POS: n
