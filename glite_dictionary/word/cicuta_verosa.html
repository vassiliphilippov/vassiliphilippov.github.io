Cicuta verosa

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjuːtə vəˈroʊsə / (AmE), / sɪˈkjuːtə vɛˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
A tall, erect, highly poisonous perennial herb native to Eurasia, commonly found in marshy areas.
poisonous Eurasian marsh plant

Examples

The wetland edges were lined with Cicuta verosa, posing a danger to livestock.
Botanists warn that Cicuta verosa is among the most toxic native plants in the region.
Always avoid ingesting any part of Cicuta verosa due to its lethal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hemlock - a common name for Cicuta species, extremely poisonous plants

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Cicuta' (water hemlock) and Latin 'verosa'.
