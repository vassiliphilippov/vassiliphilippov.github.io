gangliocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋɡli.oʊ.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡaŋ.ɡli.əʊ.saɪt / (BrE)
a nerve cell whose cell body is located outside the central nervous system, especially in a ganglion
nerve cell outside CNS
medical

Examples

Loss of gangliocytes can lead to certain neurodegenerative diseases.
Researchers are studying how gangliocytes contribute to synaptic transmission outside the brain.
Damage to retinal gangliocytes is associated with the progression of glaucoma.

Synset Examples

damage to ganglion cells in the retina may play a role in the development of glaucoma

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuron - a nerve cell that transmits nerve impulses

Etymology

From Greek 'ganglion' (knot, nerve center) + '-cyte' (cell).
