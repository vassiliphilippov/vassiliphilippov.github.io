Gobio

1. NOUN

/ ˈgoʊ.bi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈgəʊ.bi.əʊ / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly known as true gudgeons, native to Eurasia.
genus of small freshwater fish (true gudgeons)

Examples

The genus Gobio consists of several species commonly found in European rivers.
Ichthyologists have discovered a new species within the Gobio genus.
Freshwater aquariums sometimes include Gobio species due to their small size and peaceful nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gudgeon - a small freshwater fish often used as bait

Etymology

From Latin 'gobio', meaning gudgeon (a small fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.bi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.bi.əʊ / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish known as true gudgeons, found in rivers and lakes in Europe and Asia.
genus of small freshwater fish (true gudgeons)

Examples

The Gobio species are common in European rivers.
Biologists have classified several new subspecies within the genus Gobio.
The true gudgeons of the genus Gobio are often studied for their diverse habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gudgeon - a small edible freshwater fish of the carp family, related to Gobio

Etymology

From Latin *gobius* (gudgeon), which is from Greek *kōbios* (small fish).
