Panorpidae

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnɔːr.pɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pəˈnɔː.pɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, usually slender insects in the order Mecoptera, commonly known as scorpionflies.
family of insects called scorpionflies

Examples

Scientists collected several specimens of Panorpidae from the forest for genetic analysis.
The elongated snout is a distinguishing feature of the Panorpidae family.
Many Panorpidae are found in cool, moist environments around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpionfly - insect of the family Panorpidae with an elongated face and tail resembling a scorpion's

Etymology

From New Latin Panorpidae, from Panorpa (type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ pænˈɔːrpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / panˈɔːpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Mecoptera, commonly known as scorpionflies, characterized by their elongated faces and membranous wings.
family of insects (scorpionflies)

Examples

The Panorpidae are known for the male's tail, which resembles a scorpion's stinger but is harmless.
Many members of the Panorpidae family inhabit moist woodlands throughout the northern hemisphere.
Scientists recently described a new species within the Panorpidae from a remote region of China.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on type genus Panorpa + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
