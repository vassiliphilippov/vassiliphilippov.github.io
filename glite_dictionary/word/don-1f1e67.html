don

1. NOUN

/ dɑn / (AmE), / dɒn / (BrE)
a university teacher, especially at Oxford or Cambridge; a fellow or tutor at a college or university
university teacher, esp. at Oxford or Cambridge
british

Examples

The don delivered a fascinating lecture on medieval history.
She consulted her don about her dissertation topic.
At Oxford, every student is assigned a don to oversee their academic progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fellow - member of a college with teaching duties, especially at a university
  • Synonym: professor - senior academic teaching or researching at a university

Etymology

From Italian *don* (title), from Latin *dominus* (lord, master).
