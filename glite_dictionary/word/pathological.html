pathological

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study, causes, or effects of diseases (pathology) or the methods and practice of pathology
relating to pathology or the study of disease
medical

Examples

The pathological laboratory analyzed tissue samples for signs of disease.
She received a pathological diagnosis after her biopsy was examined by specialists.
Many advances in medicine have come from pathological research into the causes of cancer.

Synset Examples

pathological laboratory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinical - relating to the observation and treatment of actual patients

Etymology

From Greek pathologikos (pertaining to disease), from pathos (suffering) + -logia (study).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
involving, caused by, or characteristic of disease; relating to pathology
relating to disease or pathology
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed that the tissue had pathological changes consistent with cancer.
Researchers studied the pathological mechanisms behind the progression of Alzheimer's disease.
Untreated high blood pressure can lead to pathological damage in the arteries.

Synset Examples

diseased tonsils
a morbid growth
pathologic tissue
pathological bodily processes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morbid - relating to or caused by disease
  • Antonym: healthy - in good health, not diseased or pathological

Etymology

From Greek pathologikos, from pathos (suffering, disease) + -logia (study).

3. ADJ

/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) exhibiting or caused by a strong and often irrational compulsion or obsession, especially relating to a mental disturbance.
caused by mental disturbance or irrational compulsion

Examples

His obsession with cleanliness reached a pathological level, making daily life difficult.
She was known as a pathological liar, unable to tell the truth.
The company's pathological need for control eventually drove away its most creative employees.

Synset Examples

a pathological liar
a pathological urge to succeed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsive - acting on an irresistible urge, especially against one's conscious wishes
  • Antonym: normal - free from mental disturbance or irrational compulsion

Etymology

From Greek pathologikos (treating of diseases), from pathos (suffering, disease) + -logia (study).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01589899-s
Synset POS: s
