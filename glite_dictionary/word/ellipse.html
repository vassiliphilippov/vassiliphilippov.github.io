ellipse

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪps / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪps / (BrE)
a regular oval-shaped closed curve, especially one produced by the intersection of a cone with a plane at an angle to its base
oval-shaped closed curve

Examples

The planets in our solar system travel around the Sun in nearly ellipse orbits.
She drew an ellipse with a compass and a loop of string.
The gardener designed the flower bed in the shape of an ellipse.

Synset Examples

the sums of the distances from the foci to any point on an ellipse is constant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval - an egg-shaped or elliptical curve
  • Degree Lower: circle - a round plane figure whose boundary consists of points equidistant from the center

Etymology

From Greek elleipsis (a falling short, deficiency), referring to the shape's appearance compared to a circle.
