stenograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈstiːnəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a written character or symbol used in shorthand writing systems
shorthand character or symbol

Examples

She quickly jotted down the speech using a complex stenograph.
Every stenograph in her notebook stands for a full word or phrase.
Learning each unique stenograph takes time and practice for aspiring court reporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorthand - a method of rapid writing using symbols

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'graphō' (write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06854337-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈstɛnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈstiːnəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to write or record information in shorthand using stenography
write in shorthand

Examples

During the meeting, she stenographed every word for the official record.
Some court reporters stenograph testimony in real time to ensure accuracy.
He learned how to stenograph speeches for his job as a secretary.

Synset Examples

The students were able to stenograph and record the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transcribe - to make a written copy of spoken material

Etymology

From Greek *stenos* (narrow) and *graphō* (to write), via 'stenography'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01695401-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɛnoʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈstɛnəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a machine used for typewriting shorthand characters, especially in court reporting and transcription
shorthand typewriting machine

Examples

The court reporter operated the stenograph with remarkable speed during the trial.
Modern versions of the stenograph have computerized components for easier transcription.
He trained for months to become proficient at using the stenograph professionally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenotype - a machine used to type stenographic symbols quickly

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow, close) + '-graph' (writing instrument), reflecting its use in shorthand writing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04321770-n
Synset POS: n
