leveling

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvəɫɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of completely destroying a building so that it is flat with the ground
complete destruction of a building

Examples

The leveling of the old factory made way for a new park in the neighborhood.
After the earthquake, the leveling of several buildings was necessary for safety reasons.
The town council approved the leveling of the abandoned warehouse last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolition - the act of deliberately destroying a building

Etymology

From the verb 'level', originally from Old French 'leveler' (to make level).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛv(ə)lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making things equal or uniform in standard, amount, or level
making things equal or uniform

Examples

The new policy is designed for the leveling of opportunities among all students.
Some critics argue that excessive leveling can discourage individual achievement.
Leveling efforts aim to reduce income inequality in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equalization - the process of making things equal
  • Synonym: standardization - the process of making things conform to a standard

Etymology

From the verb 'level' (to make even), with -ing forming the noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00045411-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛv(ə)lɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making the ground or surface smooth, horizontal, or gently sloping
making a surface level or smooth

Examples

The construction crew began leveling the field before laying the foundation.
Proper leveling is essential to prevent water accumulation around the building.
Landscaping requires careful leveling to ensure good drainage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grading - the process of making a surface level or having a desired slope

Etymology

From the verb 'level', which comes from Old French 'leveler', from 'niveau' meaning 'level'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00914407-n
Synset POS: n
