Asplenium bradleyi

1. NOUN

/ æˈspliːniəm ˈbrædliːaɪ / (AmE), / æˈspliːniəm ˈbrædliːaɪ / (BrE)
A species of spleenwort fern found in the eastern and southern United States, known for growing on rocky cliffs and ledges.
species of spleenwort fern in the US

Examples

The rare Asplenium bradleyi can be found clinging to limestone cliffs in Appalachia.
Botanists recorded a new population of Asplenium bradleyi in the southern United States.
Hikers often overlook Asplenium bradleyi due to its small, dark-green fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spleenwort - any fern of the genus Asplenium

Etymology

Named after American botanist Frank Howe Bradley. Genus from Greek *asplēnion* (spleenwort).
