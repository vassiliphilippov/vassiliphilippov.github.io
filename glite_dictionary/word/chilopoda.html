Chilopoda

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈlɑpədə / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊpədə / (BrE)
A class of arthropods comprising centipedes, characterized by elongated bodies with numerous segments, each bearing one pair of legs.
class of arthropods: centipedes
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Chilopoda class in the tropical rainforest.
The Chilopoda are easily distinguished from millipedes by their single pair of legs per segment.
Biology students studied the anatomy of Chilopoda to understand arthropod diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centipede - a predatory arthropod with many body segments and legs

Etymology

From modern Latin Chilopoda, from Greek kheilos (lip) and pous, pod- (foot).

a class of arthropods comprising the centipedes, characterized by bodies with many segments, each bearing a single pair of legs

Examples

Chilopoda includes all true centipedes commonly found in soil and leaf litter.
Biology students studied the anatomy of the Chilopoda during their arthropod classification unit.
Unlike millipedes, members of Chilopoda have one pair of legs per body segment.

Related Terms

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek kheilos (lip) + poda (feet).
