patristic

1. ADJ

/ pəˈtrɪstɪk / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the writings or teachings of the early Christian church fathers
relating to early church fathers or their writings

Examples

She specialized in patristic theology for her graduate studies.
The patristic period is crucial for understanding early Christian doctrine.
Many patristic texts have recently been translated into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patristical - relating to the church fathers; variant of patristic

Etymology

From Greek 'patēr' (father) via Late Latin 'patristicus' (of the fathers).
