Erodium cicutarium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊdiəm sɪkjuːˈtɛəriəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdiəm sɪkjuːˈtɛːrɪəm / (BrE)
A small European weed with reddish, sprawling stems, fernlike leaves, reddish-lavender flowers, and upright slender fruits, naturalized in North America and often used as forage.
a weed with reddish stems and lavender flowers

Examples

Erodium cicutarium is commonly found growing along roadsides and in open fields across California.
Ranchers sometimes use Erodium cicutarium as a forage plant for their livestock.
The delicate, fern-like leaves and upright fruits make Erodium cicutarium easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From genus name Erodium (from Greek erōdios, heron, for beak shape) and cicutarium from Latin cicuta, hemlock.
