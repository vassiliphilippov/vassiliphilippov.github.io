debilitated

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈbɪlɪˌteɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪd / (BrE)
Weak and lacking in strength, energy, or vitality, especially as a result of illness or injury.
lacking strength or energy

Examples

After months in hospital, she felt debilitated and struggled to stand.
The infection left him so debilitated that he could barely walk across the room.
The elderly dog appeared thin and debilitated, needing special care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking strength or power
  • Antonym: vigorous - strong and full of energy

Etymology

From Latin 'debilitare' (to weaken), from 'debilis' (weak).
