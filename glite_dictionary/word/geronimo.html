Geronimo

1. PROPN

/ dʒəˈrɑːnɪˌmoʊ / (AmE), / dʒəˈrɒnɪməʊ / (BrE)
(1829–1909) A prominent Apache leader who led resistance against U.S. and Mexican forces and opposed the confinement of his people to reservations.
famous Apache leader (1829–1909)

Examples

Many history books recount the bravery and tactics of Geronimo in defending his people.
After years of pursuit, Geronimo finally surrendered to U.S. forces in 1886.
Some legends still surround the daring escapes made by Geronimo from military captivity.

Etymology

From the Spanish form of his given name, possibly derived from Jerome.
