unpersuaded

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.pərˈsweɪ.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.pəˈsweɪ.dɪd / (BrE)
not having been convinced or influenced to change one’s opinion or belief.
not convinced or influenced

Examples

She remained unpersuaded by his arguments and decided not to support the proposal.
Despite the evidence presented, the jury was still unpersuaded.
He listened attentively but was ultimately unpersuaded by their reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persuaded - convinced or caused to believe or do something by reasoning or argument
  • Synonym: unconvinced - not certain about something; not persuaded

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' plus 'persuaded', from Latin 'persuadere' (to convince).
