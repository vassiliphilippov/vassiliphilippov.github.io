pleat

1. VERB

/ plit / (AmE), / pliːt / (BrE)
to fold fabric or other material into pleats; to form into even, narrow folds, often for decorative or functional effect
to fold fabric into pleats

Examples

She decided to pleat the skirt to give it a more elegant look.
Before attaching the curtain, you should pleat the fabric evenly.
The tailor will pleat the dress by hand for a custom fit.

Synset Examples

ruffle the curtain fabric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to draw fabric together and sew it so that it forms small folds

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *plet*, from Old French *pliet*, past participle of *plier* (to fold), from Latin *plicare* (to fold).

2. NOUN

/ plit / (AmE), / pliːt / (BrE)
A fold formed by doubling fabric back upon itself and securing it by pressing or stitching, used to give shape and style to clothing and textiles.
fold in fabric for style or shape

Examples

She ironed a perfect pleat into the skirt before wearing it to the party.
The dress featured narrow pleats that added movement and volume to the fabric.
A tailor can add a pleat to trousers for extra comfort and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - a bend or crease in something, especially fabric or paper

Etymology

Probably from French *plisser* (to pleat, to fold), from Old French *plessier*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03972239-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ plit / (AmE), / pliːt / (BrE)
to fold (fabric or material) into pleats
fold into pleats

Examples

She decided to pleat the skirt to give it more volume.
The tailor will pleat the dress fabric as requested.
If you pleat the curtains, they will hang more elegantly.

Synset Examples

Pleat the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to draw fabric together in small folds or puckers

Etymology

From Middle English *pleten*, related to 'pleat' (noun), from Old French *plait* (fold).
