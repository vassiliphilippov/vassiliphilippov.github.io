archespore

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃəˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tʃɪ.spɔː / (BrE)
(Biology) A primitive cell or group of cells from which a mother cell develops, especially in the formation of spores or reproductive cells.
primitive cell from which mother cell develops
medical

Examples

During reproduction, the archespore gives rise to the mother cell that will eventually form the spore.
Botanists examined the archespore to better understand the origins of cellular differentiation.
The development of the archespore is crucial in the life cycle of many plants and fungi.

Etymology

From Greek arkhē (beginning) + sporos (seed).
