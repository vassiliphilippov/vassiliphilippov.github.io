Croatian

1. ADJ

/ kroʊˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / krəʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Croatia, its people, or its language
relating to Croatia or its people/language

Examples

The restaurant serves delicious Croatian cuisine influenced by the Adriatic coast.
She studied the unique traditions found in Croatian villages for her research.
Many Croatian artists have gained international recognition for their work.

Synset Examples

Croatian villages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Croatia - a country in southeastern Europe
  • Synonym: Croat - a person from Croatia or of Croatian descent

Etymology

From Croatia, the country name, ultimately from Medieval Latin Croātia.

2. NOUN

/ kroʊˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / krəʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is a member of the Slavic ethnic group native to Croatia
a person from Croatia

Examples

The Croatian greeted us warmly when we arrived in Zagreb.
She learned about the traditions of the Croatian people during her stay.
Many famous athletes are proud to be Croatian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Croat - a person from Croatia; a member of the Croatian people

Etymology

From Croatian Hrvatski, the self-designation of the people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09770587-n
Synset POS: n
