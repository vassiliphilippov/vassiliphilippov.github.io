Pseudowintera colorata

1. NOUN

/ suːdoʊˈwɪntərə kəˈlɔːrətə / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˈwɪntərə kəˈlɒrətə / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, with striking red and yellow-blotched leaves and small black fruits; also known as horopito or pepper tree.
New Zealand evergreen shrub with blotched leaves

Examples

The leaves of Pseudowintera colorata add a splash of color to native gardens.
Indigenous peoples have used Pseudowintera colorata for its medicinal properties.
The striking appearance of Pseudowintera colorata makes it a popular ornamental plant.

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + Wintera (a related genus), and Latin colorata (colored).
