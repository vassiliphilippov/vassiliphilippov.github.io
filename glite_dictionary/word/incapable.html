incapable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (BrE)
not able to experience, contain, or allow a particular thing; not susceptible to or not permitting something, usually used with 'of'.
unable or not permitting something (with 'of')

Examples

The problem was considered incapable of solution by the experts.
This device is incapable of operating at such low temperatures.
Some species are incapable of surviving in cold climates.

Synset Examples

incapable of solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capable - having the ability or qualities necessary for a particular thing

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + capable (from Latin capabilis, able to take in).

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (BrE)
not having the temperament or ability to do something; unable to do a particular thing (used with 'of').
not having ability or temperament to do something

Examples

She was incapable of telling a lie, no matter the situation.
Many people are incapable of understanding abstract art.
He felt incapable of expressing his emotions clearly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capable - having ability or qualities necessary to do something

Etymology

From Late Latin 'incapabilis', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'capabilis' (capable).

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (BrE)
Not having the ability, power, or skill to do something; lacking the capacity for a particular action or quality.
lacking the ability or capacity

Examples

He felt completely incapable of solving the complicated puzzle.
Some plants are incapable of surviving in cold climates.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

4. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (BrE)
not having the ability or qualities necessary to do something; not meeting requirements or demands.
not able to do something

Examples

She felt incapable of dealing with the stress at work.
The patient was incapable of walking after the surgery.
He admitted he was incapable of understanding advanced mathematics.

Synset Examples

unequal to the demands put upon him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capable - having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do or achieve a specified thing

Etymology

From late Latin *incapabilis* ('not able to contain or receive'), from Latin in- ('not') + capabilis ('capable').
