conga

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə / (BrE)
A Latin American dance in which people form a single-file line, moving forward with three steps and a kick, often to rhythmic music.
Latin American dance in a line

Examples

During the celebration, everyone joined the conga and snaked through the crowded hall.
The energetic rhythm of the music made it impossible not to dance the conga.
At the party, a long line formed as guests began a spontaneous conga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: samba - a lively Brazilian dance of African origin

Etymology

From Spanish 'conga', referring to the dance and music style, ultimately from Cuba.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə / (BrE)
A style of lively music composed to accompany the conga dance, characterized by a syncopated rhythm and Afro-Cuban influences.
lively dance music (Afro-Cuban rhythm)

Examples

The band played a lively conga that got everyone on their feet.
She prefers listening to salsa and conga at Latin music festivals.
The rhythmic beat of the conga filled the room with energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a genre of Cuban dance music

Etymology

From Spanish 'conga', from Cuba, likely from an African language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07069335-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkɑŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə / (BrE)
to dance the conga, a Latin American dance in which people form a line and follow each other around the room while performing simple steps
to dance the conga

Examples

At the wedding, everyone congaed happily around the dance floor.
We conga in a line every New Year's Eve to celebrate the holiday.
She was congaing with her friends at the party last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conga line - group procession formed when dancing the conga

Etymology

From Spanish 'conga', referring to the dance and accompanying music from Cuba.
