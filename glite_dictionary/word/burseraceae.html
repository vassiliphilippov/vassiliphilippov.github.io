Burseraceae

1. NOUN

/ bɜr.səˈreɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / bɜː.səˈreɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of resinous or aromatic shrubs and trees, chiefly found in tropical regions, including the frankincense and myrrh trees
a family of tropical resinous trees and shrubs

Examples

The aromatic trees of the Burseraceae family are valued for their fragrant resins.
Frankincense and myrrh come from different genera within Burseraceae.
Botanists have identified several hundred species in the Burseraceae family worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Bursera + -aceae (a suffix used in botanical family names).

2. NOUN

/ bɜrsəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bɜːsəˈreɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of resinous or aromatic shrubs and trees, chiefly found in tropical regions.
family of resinous tropical trees/shrubs

Examples

The genus Boswellia, which produces frankincense, belongs to the Burseraceae family.
Many species in the Burseraceae are valued for their aromatic resins.
Members of Burseraceae are often found in tropical forests.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus Bursera, named for Joachim Burser, a German botanist.
