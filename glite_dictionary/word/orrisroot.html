orrisroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪsˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪsˌruːt / (BrE)
the fragrant root of certain iris species, especially the Florentine iris, used in perfumes and medicines
fragrant iris root used in perfumes

Examples

The main ingredient in the perfume was orrisroot, giving it a delicate floral scent.
Some traditional herbal remedies include powdered orrisroot to ease respiratory problems.
Cosmetic companies often value orrisroot for its ability to fix scents in perfumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant of the genus Iris, whose root produces orrisroot

Etymology

From Middle English 'orris', alteration of 'iris', from Latin 'iris' (rainbow, iris plant).
