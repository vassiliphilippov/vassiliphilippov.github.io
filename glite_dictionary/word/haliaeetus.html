Haliaeetus

1. NOUN

/ hæliˈiːtəs / (AmE), / hæliˈiːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of large birds of prey in the family Accipitridae, commonly known as sea eagles.
genus of sea eagles

Examples

The genus Haliaeetus includes the well-known bald eagle of North America.
Researchers studied several Haliaeetus specimens to learn more about their feeding habits.
Many species in the Haliaeetus genus inhabit coastal areas and feed primarily on fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea eagle - a large eagle typically found near water, feeding mainly on fish

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek haliaetos ('sea eagle'), from hals ('sea') + aetos ('eagle').
