grammatical

1. ADJ

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmatɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to grammar or to the rules of language structure.
relating to grammar or language rules

Examples

All her essays are grammatical and well-structured.
The teacher explained the grammatical differences between the two sentences.
Is this sentence grammatical, or should we rephrase it?

Synset Examples

the grammatic structure of a sentence
grammatical rules
grammatical gender

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ungrammatical - not following the rules of grammar

Etymology

From Latin grammaticus (of grammar), from Greek grammatikos.

2. ADJ

/ ɡrəˈmæt̬ɪkəl / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmætɪkəl / (BrE)
conforming to the rules of grammar or standard usage accepted by speakers or writers of a language
following grammar rules

Examples

Her essay was clear and grammatical throughout.
Make sure your questions are grammatical before submitting them.
The teacher praised his grammatical English in class.

Synset Examples

spoke in grammatical sentences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ungrammatical - not following the rules of grammar
  • Synonym: syntactic - relating to the arrangement of words and phrases within sentences

Etymology

From Latin grammaticalis, from Greek grammatikos (relating to letters or grammar).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01149515-a
Synset POS: a
