hitherto

1. ADV

/ ˌhɪðərˈtu / (AmE), / ˌhɪðəˈtuː / (BrE)
up to this time; until now
until now; up to this point

Examples

The painting had lain undiscovered hitherto, hidden behind a false wall.
Hitherto, she had avoided discussing her past.
Scientists have found a cure for a disease that was hitherto thought incurable.

Synset Examples

So far he hasn't called
the sun isn't up yet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: so far - up to the present moment
  • Synonym: yet - up to now; until the present
  • Antonym: formerly - in the past; previously

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *hider to* meaning 'to here, to this place/time'.
