to illustrate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪləˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪləstreɪt / (BrE)
to explain or clarify something by giving an example, drawing, or other means
clarify by giving an example

Examples

Let me illustrate this concept with a simple example from daily life.
The teacher used a diagram to illustrate how the water cycle works.
His story illustrates the importance of perseverance in achieving success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exemplify - to be a typical example of
  • Synonym: to explain - make something clear to someone

Etymology

From Latin illustrāre (to make light, enlighten)

2. VERB

/ ˈɪləˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪləstreɪt / (BrE)
to explain or decorate something by using pictures, diagrams, or other visual elements
depict with a visual illustration

Examples

The book is beautifully illustrated with detailed drawings of each animal.
She used diagrams to illustrate her point during the presentation.
The artist was commissioned to illustrate a series of children's stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depict - to show, represent, or describe something in a picture or words

Etymology

From Latin illustrāre, meaning 'to make bright, make clear.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01691296-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɪl.əˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪl.ə.streɪt / (BrE)
to provide (a book, article, etc.) with pictures, drawings, or other illustrations to explain or decorate the text
to provide with pictures or drawings

Examples

The artist was hired to illustrate the new children's book with colorful images.
She plans to illustrate her essay with several historical photographs.
The science textbook was illustrated by a well-known botanical artist.

Synset Examples

illustrate a book with drawings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to add something to make more attractive
  • Synonym: illustration - a picture or drawing used to explain or decorate

Etymology

From Latin illustrare (to light up, make clear), from in- (upon) + lustrare (to illuminate).
