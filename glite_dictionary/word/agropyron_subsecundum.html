Agropyron subsecundum

1. NOUN

/ əˌgroʊˈpaɪrɑn ˌsʌbsɪˈkʌndəm / (AmE), / əˌgrəʊˈpɪərɒn ˌsʌbsɪˈkʌndəm / (BrE)
A species of wheatgrass distinguished by its straight terminal awns on the flowering glumes.
a species of wheatgrass

Examples

Agropyron subsecundum is commonly found in prairies and open woodlands across North America.
The straight awns of Agropyron subsecundum help distinguish it from other wheatgrass species.
Researchers studied the adaptability of Agropyron subsecundum to different soil conditions.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Agropyron subsecundum*: 'Agropyron' from Greek 'agros' (field) + 'pyros' (wheat); 'subsecundum' meaning 'almost one-sided' in Latin.
