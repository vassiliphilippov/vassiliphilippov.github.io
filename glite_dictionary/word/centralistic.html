centralistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛntrəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Favoring or advocating the concentration of control or authority in a single central administration or organization.
favoring centralization of control

Examples

The government adopted a centralistic approach to managing the country's education system.
Many citizens resisted the centralistic reforms, preferring local autonomy.
Her policies were criticized for being too centralistic and not respecting regional diversity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralized - characterized by the distribution of authority away from a central location

Etymology

Formed from 'central' + '-istic', following the pattern of adjectives signifying characteristic or advocacy.
