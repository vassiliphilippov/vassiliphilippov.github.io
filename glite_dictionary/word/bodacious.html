bodacious

1. ADJ

/ boʊˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈdeɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
(informal, US) Audacious, bold, or remarkable; sometimes also used to describe someone incorrigible, especially in their behavior.
bold, audacious, or incorrigible (informal US)
informal american

Examples

The bodacious prankster was always in trouble for his wild antics at school.
Her bodacious charm made her the center of attention wherever she went.
Even after being scolded, he remained a bodacious rule-breaker.

Synset Examples

a bodacious gossip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audacious - showing a willingness to take bold risks; daring
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

Blend of 'bold' and 'audacious', originating in US English early 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ boʊˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
very impressive, remarkable, or audacious; bold in manner or appearance, often in an unconventional or uninhibited way.
impressive or boldly audacious
informal american

Examples

She pulled off a bodacious stunt at the skate park, impressing everyone who watched.
His bodacious personality made him the life of every party.
The actor wore a bodacious outfit to the awards show that turned heads all night.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audacious - showing a willingness to take bold risks; daring
  • Synonym: brazen - bold and without shame
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

Probably blend of 'bold' and 'audacious'; first used in American English in the mid-19th century.
