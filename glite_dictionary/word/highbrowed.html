highbrowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌbraʊd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbraʊd / (BrE)
having or showing highly cultured, intellectual, or educated tastes and interests; sometimes used with a hint of pretension
highly cultured or intellectual

Examples

The gallery attracted a highbrowed audience who were keen to discuss abstract art.
Sarah's highbrowed taste in literature impressed her professors.
Some considered his remarks a little too highbrowed for the casual book club.

Synset Examples

highbrow events such as the ballet or opera
a highbrowed literary critic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highbrow - having intellectual or cultured tastes
  • Antonym: lowbrowed - having or showing less cultured or intellectual tastes

Etymology

From the phrase 'highbrow', influenced by the notion that a high forehead signified intelligence (late 19th century).
