to cicatrize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪkəˌtraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.ə.trʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a wound or injury) to form a scar as part of the healing process.
form a scar after an injury

Examples

After surgery, the incision will cicatrize over time if properly cared for.
The doctor explained that the wound should cicatrize naturally without complications.
Her injury began to cicatrize within a few days, leaving only a faint mark.

Synset Examples

the skin will cicatrize and it will heal soon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin or within body tissue after healing of a wound or sore

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cicatrizare, from Latin cicatrix (scar).
