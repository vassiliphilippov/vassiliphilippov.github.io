flapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæpər / (AmE), / ˈflæpə / (BrE)
a young woman in the 1920s who rejected conventional behavior and dress, often characterized by energetic freedom, short skirts, and bobbed hair
young, unconventional 1920s woman

Examples

The novel's main character is a flapper who defies traditional roles for women.
Photographs from the 1920s often show flappers dancing at jazz clubs in their distinctive outfits.
She admired the flappers for their independence and bold sense of style.

Etymology

Originally British slang for a lively young woman, popularized in the 1920s in America to describe a new generation of women.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæpər / (AmE), / ˈflæpə / (BrE)
a young woman in the 1920s who rejected conventional standards of behavior and dressed in a bold, stylish way
1920s unconventional young woman

Examples

Many flappers challenged social norms by wearing short dresses and cutting their hair into bobs.
The novelist captured the spirit of the flapper era in her writing.
She admired her grandmother's stories about being a flapper during the Roaring Twenties.

Synset Examples

The flapper danced gracefully at the jazz club.

Etymology

Probably from 'flap' (move quickly up and down), imitative of the energetic style associated with these women.
