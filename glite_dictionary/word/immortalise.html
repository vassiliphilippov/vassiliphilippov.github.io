to immortalise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɔrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something famous or remembered forever, especially by creating a lasting memorial or record
make remembered or famous forever
british

Examples

The monument was erected to immortalise the achievements of the city's founder.
His heroic deeds were immortalised in song and story for generations to come.
Many artists hope their work will immortalise their names long after they are gone.

Synset Examples

This sculpture commemorates the victims of the concentration camps
We memorialized the Dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something with a ceremony or object
  • Synonym: to memorialize - to do or create something that keeps a person or event in people's memory

Etymology

From Latin 'immortalis' (immortal) + '-ise' (to make).

british

With her book, she hoped to immortalise her family's incredible story.
