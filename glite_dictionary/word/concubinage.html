concubinage

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkjuːbɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / kɒnˈkjuːbɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
The state or practice of living together as a couple without being legally married, often with reference to historical or legal contexts.
cohabitation without legal marriage

Examples

In ancient societies, concubinage was a recognized institution distinct from formal marriage.
The novel explores themes of love, concubinage, and societal expectations.
He faced criticism for maintaining a relationship resembling concubinage instead of marrying her.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marriage - the legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners
  • Synonym: cohabitation - the state of living together and having a sexual relationship without being married

Etymology

From French 'concubinage', from Latin 'concubinatus' (cohabitation).
