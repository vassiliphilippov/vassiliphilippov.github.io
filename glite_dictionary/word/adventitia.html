adventitia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.vənˈtɪʃ.ə / (AmE), / ˌad.vɛnˈtɪʃ.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
the outermost layer of connective tissue that surrounds an organ, vessel, or other body structure.
outer connective tissue layer of organs/vessels
medical

Examples

The adventitia provides structural support to the blood vessel and connects it to surrounding tissues.
Inflammation of the adventitia can lead to significant vascular complications.
The surgeon carefully dissected down to the adventitia of the esophagus during the procedure.

Etymology

From Latin 'adventicius' (coming from outside, foreign).
