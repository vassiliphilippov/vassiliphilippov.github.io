motivity

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈtɪvɪti / (AmE), / məʊˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the power or ability to move or produce motion.
capacity for movement

Examples

Increasing the motivity of the fluid allows the machine to work more efficiently.
Some philosophers debate whether life and motivity are inseparable.
Muscle tissue is essential to the motivity of most animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobility - the ability to move or be moved freely
  • Antonym: immobility - the state of being unable to move

Etymology

From Latin 'motivus' (moving), from 'motus' (motion).
