symbolization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of using symbols to represent ideas, concepts, or objects
use of symbols to represent meaning

Examples

The symbolization of love as a heart shape is common in many cultures.
Modern art often relies heavily on symbolization to convey deeper meanings.
Religious rituals are rich with the symbolization of complex beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representation - the act of representing or depicting something

Etymology

From symbol + -ization, after Medieval Latin symbolizatio.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing ideas or qualities by symbols or giving symbolic meaning to something
representation by symbols; giving symbolic meaning

Examples

The symbolization of peace by a white dove is widely recognized.
Modern art often relies on symbolization rather than realistic depiction.
He studied the symbolization of dreams in psychoanalytic theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to symbolize - to represent something by means of a symbol

Etymology

From symbol + -ization, modeled after French 'symbolisation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of a visible sign, object, or image to represent something else, often an idea or quality that is not directly observable
representation by a visible symbol

Examples

The dove is often used as a symbolization of peace in art and literature.
In religious ceremonies, water can serve as a symbolization of purification and renewal.
The red ribbon is a worldwide symbolization of support for people living with HIV/AIDS.

Synset Examples

the eagle is a symbol of the United States

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - a thing that represents or stands for something else
  • Synonym: representation - the action of representing something

Etymology

From Greek symbolon (token, sign) via Late Latin and French; root 'symbol-' meaning 'mark, token'.
