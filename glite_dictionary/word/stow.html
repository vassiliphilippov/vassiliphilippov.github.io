to stow

1. VERB

/ stoʊ / (AmE), / stəʊ / (BrE)
to fill or pack something tightly, especially so as to make efficient use of space
pack tightly; fill efficiently

Examples

She carefully stowed her luggage in the overhead compartment before taking her seat.
The sailors stowed supplies below deck for the long journey.
Please stow all loose items in the bins before we depart.

Synset Examples

stow the cart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - to fill a container tightly with objects
  • Antonym: to unpack - to remove things from a container or space, opposite of packing

Etymology

From Middle English stowen, related to Old English stowian (to place, arrange).
