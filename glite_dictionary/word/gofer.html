gofer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊfər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
a person employed to run errands or perform minor tasks, especially in an office or organization
person who runs errands

Examples

During his internship, Jake often acted as the office gofer and fetched coffee for everyone.
She started as a gofer before working her way up to a managerial position.
The production team relied on their gofer to handle all the small but crucial tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: errand boy - a person, usually a junior employee, who runs errands

Etymology

From 'go for', referring to someone who 'goes for' things.
