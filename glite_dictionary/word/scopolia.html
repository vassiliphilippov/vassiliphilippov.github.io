Scopolia

1. PROPN

/ skoʊˈpoʊliə / (AmE), / skəˈpəʊliə / (BrE)
A genus of European perennial herbs known for producing medicinal alkaloids.
genus of herbs producing medicinal alkaloids

Examples

Scopolia is often studied for its valuable alkaloids used in pharmaceuticals.
Botanists have discovered new species within the Scopolia genus in Eastern Europe.
Extracts from Scopolia roots have a long history of medicinal use in folk remedies.

Etymology

Named after Giovanni Antonio Scopoli, Italian naturalist (1723–1788).

2. NOUN

/ skoʊˈpoʊliə / (AmE), / skɒˈpəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of European perennial herbs known for yielding medicinal alkaloids, including scopolamine, used in pharmacology.
genus of European alkaloid-producing herbs

Examples

Scopolia species are often studied for their pharmacologically active compounds.
Researchers identified several new alkaloids from the roots of Scopolia.
In herbal medicine, extracts from Scopolia have traditional uses.

Etymology

Named after the Italian naturalist Giovanni Antonio Scopoli (1723–1788).
