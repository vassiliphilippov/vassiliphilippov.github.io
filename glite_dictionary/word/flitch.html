flitch

1. NOUN

/ flɪtʃ / (AmE), / flɪtʃ / (BrE)
a steak or side of fish, usually cut from a halibut, typically prepared for cooking
a cut of fish, usually halibut

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious flitch of halibut for dinner.
At the market, she bought a fresh flitch to grill at home.
A flitch of fish can be seasoned with herbs and baked whole for a simple meal.

2. NOUN

/ flɪtʃ / (AmE), / flɪtʃ / (BrE)
a side of bacon, especially the salted and cured abdominal wall of a pig
side of bacon (salted, cured pork)

Examples

The butcher displayed a large flitch of bacon in the shop window.
Traditionally, a flitch could be stored for months as a source of meat during winter.
They won a flitch of bacon in the village competition.

Etymology

Old English *flicce* (side of bacon).
