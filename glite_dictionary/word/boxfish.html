boxfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɒksˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small tropical fish with a body and head encased in bony plates, found mainly in warm ocean waters
small tropical fish with bony plates

Examples

The brightly colored boxfish glided slowly over the coral reef.
Scientists studied the unique armor of the boxfish to develop stronger materials.
A boxfish can maneuver around obstacles with surprising agility despite its rigid body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trunkfish - a similar type of fish closely related to the boxfish
  • Synonym: cowfish - a related tropical fish also encased in bony plates, often with horn-like projections
