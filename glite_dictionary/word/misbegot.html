misbegot

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪs.bɪˈɡɑt / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.bɪˈɡɒt / (BrE)
archaic: born out of wedlock; illegitimate.
archaic; illegitimate (born out of wedlock)
archaic

Examples

The king refused to recognize his misbegot children as heirs to the throne.
In the old novel, the protagonist struggled with the shame of being misbegot.
The estate was divided among the legitimate and misbegot offspring of the nobleman.

Synset Examples

the dominions of both rulers passed away to their spurious or doubtful offspring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimate - born to parents not lawfully married
  • Antonym: legitimate - born to parents who are legally married

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (badly, wrongly) + obsolete past participle of 'beget'.
