anabolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈbɑ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈbɒ.lɪk / (BrE)
relating to or promoting the synthesis of complex molecules in living organisms, especially from simpler ones, as part of metabolism.
relating to metabolic synthesis
medical

Examples

Athletes sometimes take anabolic steroids to increase muscle mass, despite the health risks.
The anabolic process builds complex molecules from simpler ones within the body.
Insulin is considered an anabolic hormone because it stimulates the synthesis of proteins and fats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catabolic - relating to the breakdown phase of metabolism

Etymology

From Greek 'anabolē' (ascent, from 'ana-' up + 'ballein' to throw).

medical

Examples
