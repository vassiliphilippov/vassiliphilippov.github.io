incompetently

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətəntli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of ability or skill; in an incompetent manner.
in an incompetent manner

Examples

The documents were incompetently prepared, causing confusion among the team.
She handled the machine incompetently, resulting in a minor accident.
If the repairs are incompetently done, you risk further damage to your car.

Synset Examples

he did the job rather incompetently

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competently - in a competent manner
  • Synonym: ineptly - in an inept manner; without skill

Etymology

From 'incompetent' + '-ly', following standard adverb formation from adjectives.

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətəntli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows lack of ability, skill, or competence.
in an unskilled or incapable way

Examples

He tried to fix the sink, but did it incompetently and made things worse.
The report was incompetently handled, resulting in several errors.
The students complained that their test results were incompetently graded.

Synset Examples

he did the job rather incompetently

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competently - in a competent manner

Etymology

From 'incompetent' + '-ly'. 'Incompetent' from Latin *incompetens* (not suitable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00186886-r
Synset POS: r
