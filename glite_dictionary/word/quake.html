quake

1. NOUN

/ kweɪk / (AmE), / kweɪk / (BrE)
An instance of shaking and vibration at the surface of the earth caused by underground movement such as an earthquake or volcanic activity.
earth tremor; earthquake

Examples

The city was struck by a powerful quake that damaged several buildings.
Scientists recorded a small quake near the coast late last night.
After the quake, residents gathered in open spaces, fearing aftershocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthquake - a sudden and violent shaking of the ground

Etymology

From Middle English ‘quaken’ (to tremble), from Old English ‘cwacian’.

2. VERB

/ kweɪk / (AmE), / kweɪk / (BrE)
to shake or tremble with rapid, slight, and often violent movements, usually caused by fear, cold, or an external force
shake or tremble rapidly

Examples

The ground began to quake beneath their feet as the earthquake started.
She tried to speak, but her voice quaked with fear.
His hands were quaking in the freezing cold.

Synset Examples

His nostrils palpitated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety or excitement
  • Synonym: shudder - shake or tremble convulsively, typically as a result of fear or revulsion
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become firm, fixed, or stable; not shaking or moving

Etymology

From Middle English 'quaken', from Old English 'cwacian' (to tremble).

3. VERB

/ kweɪk / (AmE), / kweɪk / (BrE)
(of the earth or a structure) to shake or tremble, especially as a result of seismic activity.
to shake or tremble (seismic events)

Examples

The ground began to quake after the powerful explosion.
Villagers watched in fear as the earth started to quake beneath their feet.
During the night, the city quaked due to the unexpected earthquake.

Synset Examples

The earth was quaking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety or excitement
  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements

Etymology

From Old English *cwacian* (to shake).
