monied

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌnid / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪd / (BrE)
Having a great deal of money; wealthy.
wealthy; possessing much money

Examples

His family is one of the oldest and most monied in the city.
The new legislation was designed to limit the influence of monied interests.
She moved in exclusive circles, full of monied and influential people.

Synset Examples

moneyed interests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable or normal

Etymology

From money + -ed, modeled after the earlier 'moneyed'.
