hone

1. NOUN

/ hoʊn / (AmE), / həʊn / (BrE)
a fine-grained whetstone used for sharpening razors and other cutting tools
fine sharpening stone

Examples

He carefully ran the blade across the hone to restore its edge.
The barber kept his hone on the counter for sharpening his straight razor.
A fine hone is essential for achieving a perfectly sharp knife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whetstone - a stone used for sharpening cutting tools

Etymology

From Old English *hān* (stone); related to Old Norse *hein* (whetstone).

2. NOUN

/ hoʊn / (AmE), / həʊn / (BrE)
a tool with fine abrasive surfaces, often machine-driven, used to produce a smooth finish on the inside of cylinders or similar surfaces
tool for smoothing cylinders

Examples

To ensure a perfect seal, the mechanic used a hone to finish the cylinder bore.
The technician checked the hone for wear before starting the engine rebuild.
This hone is specially designed for automotive applications and produces a remarkably smooth surface.

Etymology

From Old English *hān* (stone, rock), later extended to the tool using a stone or abrasive.

Examples

It takes practice to hone a blade to perfect sharpness.
