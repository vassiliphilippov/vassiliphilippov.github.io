aficionado

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɪʃiəˈnɑdoʊ / (AmE), / əˌfɪʃiəˈnɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A person who is very knowledgeable and enthusiastic about a particular subject or activity, especially music or performing arts.
passionate enthusiast or devotee

Examples

As a jazz aficionado, he attends every major festival in the city.
The room was filled with opera aficionados eagerly awaiting the star's performance.
She became a true aficionado of classical music after hearing Mozart live.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - a person who has a strong interest in or admiration for a particular person or thing
  • Synonym: enthusiast - someone who is very interested in a particular activity or subject

Etymology

From Spanish 'aficionado' (devotee, fan), from 'aficionar' (to inspire affection).

2. NOUN

/ əˌfɪʃəˈnɑdoʊ / (AmE), / əˌfɪʃɪəˈnɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A person who is very passionate and knowledgeable about a particular activity, subject, or pastime, especially bullfighting.
enthusiastic and knowledgeable fan

Examples

She is an aficionado of Spanish bullfighting, traveling to Madrid every year to see the events.
The festival attracts every aficionado of traditional dance from around the world.
As a jazz aficionado, he owns an impressive collection of rare recordings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiast - A person who is very interested in a particular subject or activity
  • Synonym: fan - A person who has a strong interest in or admiration for something

Etymology

From Spanish 'aficionado' (fond of), from 'afición' (fondness).
