hip

1. NOUN

/ hɪp / (AmE), / hɪp / (BrE)
either side of the body below the waist and above the thigh
area below waist and above thigh

Examples

She placed her hand on her hip as she spoke.
After the fall, he complained of pain in his left hip.
Dancers often sway their hips to the rhythm of the music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thigh - the part of the leg between the hip and the knee

2. NOUN

/ hɪp / (AmE), / hɪp / (BrE)
the rounded fruit of a rose plant, typically red or orange, which contains seeds and is often used in preserves and herbal remedies
the fruit of a rose plant

Examples

Rose hips are often used to make herbal teas and syrups.
The bright red hip of the wild rose stands out in the autumn hedgerow.
Birds are attracted to the hips of the rose bush during winter.

Etymology

From Old English 'heope', meaning the fruit of the wild rose.

4. NOUN

/ hɪp / (AmE), / hɪp / (BrE)
the structure in the lower part of the trunk in humans and other vertebrates, forming the joint connecting the legs with the torso
joint connecting leg to pelvis

Examples

He injured his hip while playing football and needed physical therapy.
The doctor examined her hip to check for signs of arthritis.
After the accident, she had to undergo hip replacement surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvis - the large, bony structure at the base of the spine to which the limbs are attached
  • Antonym: shoulder - the joint connecting the arm to the torso, opposite part of the body

Etymology

Old English *hype* (hip), related to Dutch *heup* and German *Hüfte*.
