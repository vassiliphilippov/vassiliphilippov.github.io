parlous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑrləs / (AmE), / ˈpɑːləs / (BrE)
full of danger or uncertainty; precarious or risky.
fraught with danger or risk

Examples

The hikers faced a parlous climb up the icy mountain trail.
Due to the parlous state of the economy, many families struggled to make ends meet.
They made a parlous decision to continue sailing despite the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

a parlous journey on stormy seas
a perilous voyage across the Atlantic in a small boat
the precarious life of an undersea diver
dangerous surgery followed by a touch-and-go recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perilous - full of danger or risk
  • Antonym: safe - free from danger or risk

Etymology

From Middle English 'parlous', a variant of 'perilous', from Old French 'perilous', from Latin 'periculosus' (dangerous).
