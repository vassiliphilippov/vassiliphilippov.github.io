to misbelieve

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsbɪˈliv / (AmE), / ˌmɪsbɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to hold a false or mistaken belief, especially one that is unorthodox or incorrect
hold a false or mistaken belief

Examples

It is dangerous to misbelieve persistent rumors without verifying the facts.
Many in the community began to misbelieve the official reports due to widespread misinformation.
She realized too late that she had misbelieved what her friends had told her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disbelieve - to not accept as true
  • Antonym: to believe - to accept as true

Etymology

From Old French *mescroire* (to disbelieve) and Middle English *misbelieven*.
