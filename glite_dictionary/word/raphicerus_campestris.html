Raphicerus campestris

1. NOUN

/ ˌræfɪˈsɛrəs kæmˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˌræfɪˈsɪərəs kəmˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A small antelope of southeastern Africa, commonly called the steenbok, known for its slender build and reddish-brown coat.
small African plains antelope (steenbok)

Examples

The Raphicerus campestris is often seen grazing alone in the grasslands of southeastern Africa.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Raphicerus campestris to better understand their habitat preferences.
Unlike larger antelopes, the Raphicerus campestris relies on camouflage and agility to evade predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steenbok - another common name for Raphicerus campestris
  • Degree Lower: antelope - fast-running deer-like grazing mammal of Africa and Asia

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining genus 'Raphicerus' and species 'campestris' (from Latin 'campester', meaning 'of the field').
