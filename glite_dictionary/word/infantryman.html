infantryman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fənˌtrimən / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fən.tri.mən / (BrE)
a soldier who fights on foot, typically with small arms, as part of an infantry unit
foot soldier; infantry unit member

Examples

The infantryman marched steadily with his unit through the dense forest.
As an experienced infantryman, he knew how to handle high-pressure combat situations.
The medal was awarded to the brave infantryman who protected his comrades under fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army

Etymology

From infantry + man. Ultimately from Italian 'infanteria' (infantry), from 'infante' (foot soldier, originally 'child').
