noncellular

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsɛljələr / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛljələ / (BrE)
not consisting of, made up of, or divided into cells; lacking a cellular structure.
not made up of cells

Examples

Viruses are considered noncellular organisms because they lack cellular structure.
Many types of synthetic materials are completely noncellular in nature.
Some ancient organisms were noncellular, existing before the evolution of true cells.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'cellular' from Latin 'cellula' (small room or chamber).
