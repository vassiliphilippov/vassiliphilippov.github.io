Metasequoia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˌsiˈkwoɪə / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˌsiˈkwɔɪə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous coniferous trees, including both living and fossil forms, with one extant species, the dawn redwood, native to China
genus of deciduous conifers; includes dawn redwood

Examples

The Metasequoia is famed for its beautiful, feathery foliage that turns bronze in autumn.
Many fossilized remains of Metasequoia have been found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Botanists were amazed to discover a living Metasequoia in China during the 1940s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn redwood - the only living species of Metasequoia, native to China
  • Synonym: Sequoia - a related genus of coniferous trees, including the coast redwood and giant sequoia

Etymology

From Greek 'meta-' (meaning 'after' or 'beyond') and 'Sequoia', referring to its resemblance to the Sequoia genus.
