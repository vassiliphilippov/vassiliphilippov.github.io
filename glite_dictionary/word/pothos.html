pothos

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊˌθɑs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.θɒs / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen climbing plant widely grown as a houseplant for its attractive variegated leaves
tropical climbing houseplant with variegated leaves

Examples

I placed a pothos on my bookshelf because it thrives in low light.
The pothos has heart-shaped leaves that make it a popular decorative plant.
She learned to propagate her pothos by cutting the stems and placing them in water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊˌθoʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɒθɒs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical climbing plants, especially Scindapsus aureus, often grown as a houseplant for its attractive, trailing foliage.
tropical climbing plant, often houseplant

Examples

The pothos in my office thrives with very little sunlight.
She decorated her living room with several hanging baskets of pothos.
Many people choose pothos because it is easy to care for indoors.

Etymology

From the genus name Scindapsus in botanical Latin.
