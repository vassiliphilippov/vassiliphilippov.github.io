tufa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːfə / (AmE), / ˈtjuːfə / (BrE)
A soft, porous rock composed of calcium carbonate, formed by precipitation from spring or groundwater rich in lime.
soft porous calcium carbonate rock

Examples

The ancient baths were carved directly into the region's abundant tufa rock.
Geologists examined the tufa deposits surrounding the mineral-rich spring.
The city walls were built with large blocks of tufa due to its ease of quarrying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travertine - a form of limestone deposited by mineral springs, especially hot springs

Etymology

From Italian 'tufa', from Latin 'tofus' or 'tophus' (a kind of porous stone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːfə / (AmE), / ˈtjuːfə / (BrE)
a porous, soft volcanic rock formed by the compaction of volcanic ash, often found around hot springs and volcanic areas
porous volcanic rock of compacted ash

Examples

A layer of tufa covers the ancient ruins near the volcano.
The cave walls are made of soft, easily carved tufa.
Geologists studied the deposits of tufa formed by the hot spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuff - a similar type of rock formed from volcanic ash

Etymology

From Italian 'tufo', from Latin 'tophus' (a kind of porous stone).
