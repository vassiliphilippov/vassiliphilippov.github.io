hoarseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔr.snəs / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.snɪs / (BrE)
A rough, harsh, or weak quality in the voice, often caused by irritation or illness of the throat.
harsh or roughness in the voice

Examples

After shouting at the game, she noticed a persistent hoarseness in her voice.
The singer canceled his performance due to sudden hoarseness.
Allergy season often brings on temporary hoarseness for many people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husky - having a rough, deep, or harsh-sounding voice
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, particularly in voice

Etymology

From Middle English 'hors' (hoarse) + '-ness'.
