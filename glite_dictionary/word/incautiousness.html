incautiousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɔʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being careful or attentive to potential danger or risk
lack of carefulness or caution

Examples

His incautiousness behind the wheel led to several near-accidents.
The manager criticized the team for their incautiousness when handling sensitive information.
Because of her incautiousness, she found herself in avoidable trouble.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cautiousness - the quality of being careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • Synonym: recklessness - the quality of acting without thinking about the consequences or dangers

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + cautious + -ness (noun-forming suffix).
