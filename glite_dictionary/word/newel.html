newel

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːəl / (BrE)
the central supporting pillar of a circular staircase, or the main post at the foot or top of a staircase
central pillar of a circular staircase

Examples

The winding staircase wrapped elegantly around the newel.
She placed her hand on the newel as she descended the spiral stairs.
The ornate newel was carved from a single piece of mahogany.

Etymology

From Old French 'nouel', from Latin 'nodellus', diminutive of 'nodus' (knot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːəl / (BrE)
the central supporting post at the top or bottom of a staircase, especially one supporting a handrail
central post supporting a stair handrail

Examples

She gripped the newel tightly as she descended the stairs.
The ornate newel at the foot of the staircase was beautifully carved.
During renovations, they replaced the old wooden newel with a modern metal one.

Etymology

From Old French *nuel*, from Latin *nodellus*, diminutive of *nodus* (knot).
