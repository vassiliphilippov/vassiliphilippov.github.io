Thermopsis macrophylla

1. NOUN

/ θɜːrˈmɒp.sɪs ˌmæk.rəˈfɪl.ə / (AmE), / θəːˈmɒp.sɪs ˌmak.rəˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
A bushy herb native to the western United States, with large leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers.
western US bushy herb with yellow flowers

Examples

Thermopsis macrophylla can often be found in open woodlands and meadows of California.
Botanists are studying the pollination habits of Thermopsis macrophylla to better understand its ecological role.
Gardeners appreciate Thermopsis macrophylla for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thermopsis - genus of North American and Asian herbs with pea-like yellow flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' (hot) and 'opsis' (appearance); 'macrophylla' from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
