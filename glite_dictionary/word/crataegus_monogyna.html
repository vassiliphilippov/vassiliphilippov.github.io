Crataegus monogyna

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiɡəs ˌmɑnəˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌmɒnəˈdʒɪnə / (BrE)
A species of hawthorn native to Europe, characterized by deeply lobed leaves and bright red fruit, often cultivated as hedges.
species of European hawthorn

Examples

The Crataegus monogyna in the garden produces a profusion of red fruit each autumn.
Many old English hedgerows are composed of Crataegus monogyna for its dense, thorny branches.
Biologists noted the spread of Crataegus monogyna as an introduced species in several North American states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a genus of shrubs with thorny branches and small, bright fruit

Etymology

From Latin: Crataegus (the genus name, meaning 'hawthorn') and monogyna (from Greek monos 'single' + gyne 'female', referring to the single style in the flower).
