hematohiston

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmətoʊˈhɪstən / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtɒhɪstən / (BrE)
A colorless protein obtained by removing heme from hemoglobin, involved in the oxygen-carrying function of red blood cells.
protein from hemoglobin (heme removed)
medical

Examples

Researchers isolated hematohiston by removing the heme group from hemoglobin in the lab.
The structure of hematohiston provides insight into how hemoglobin functions after losing its heme component.
Medical students studied the properties of hematohiston to better understand oxygen transport in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoglobin - the main oxygen-carrying protein in red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + histos (tissue), with scientific -in suffix.
