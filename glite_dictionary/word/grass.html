2. NOUN

/ ɡræs / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a common plant with narrow green leaves, often covering the ground in fields, lawns, and pastures, and used as food for grazing animals or cut and dried as hay
narrow-leaved green ground plant

Examples

The children played on the grass in the park all afternoon.
Cows were grazing quietly on the lush green grass of the pasture.
After mowing, the scent of fresh grass filled the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawn - area of mown grass in a garden or park

Etymology

Old English græs (herb, grass); related to Dutch gras and German Gras.
