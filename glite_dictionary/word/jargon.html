jargon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
specialized language used by a particular group, profession, or activity, often difficult for outsiders to understand
specialized language of a group

Examples

The technical jargon used by doctors can be confusing to patients.
Computer experts often communicate with jargon that outsiders don't understand.
Legal documents are notorious for being filled with complex jargon.

Synset Examples

they don't speak our lingo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slang - very informal language, often spoken within a particular group

Etymology

From Old French *jargoun* (chatter, gibberish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑr.ɡən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑː.ɡən / (BrE)
special words or expressions used by a particular profession or group that are difficult for others to understand
specialized technical language

Examples

Medical jargon can be confusing for patients who are not familiar with the terms.
The report was filled with technical jargon that made it hard to read.
He tried to avoid using too much legal jargon when explaining the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slang - very informal language, often specific to a group
  • Synonym: terminology - the set of technical words or expressions used in a particular subject
  • Antonym: plain language - language that is clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Old French 'jargon' (chatter, gibberish), possibly of imitative origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
a colorless, pale yellow, or smoky variety of the mineral zircon, sometimes used as a gem.
a colorless or pale form of zircon (gemstone)

Examples

The jeweler showed me a clear jargon stone set in a silver ring.
Collectors often seek rare colors of jargon for their mineral displays.
That necklace features a pale yellow jargon amidst other gemstones.

Etymology

From French *jargon*, applied to zircon in the 18th century; possibly from Italian *giargone*, itself from Persian *zargun* (gold-colored).
