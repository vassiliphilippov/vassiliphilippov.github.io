loot

1. NOUN

/ luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
(informal) Money, especially when gained or spent casually or illegally.
money (informal)
informal

Examples

He managed to make a lot of loot from the new business venture.
She was saving her loot for a trip to Europe.
After a night out, my wallet was empty of loot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of bills and coins
  • Synonym: dough - (slang) money

Etymology

Originally from Hindi *lūṭ*, meaning 'to plunder.'

WordNet ID: oewn-13406389-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
goods or money stolen, especially during a war or riot; things taken illegally or by force
stolen goods or money

Examples

The thieves divided the loot among themselves after the robbery.
Police recovered all of the stolen loot hidden in an abandoned house.
During the riot, shops were broken into and loot was carried away in broad daylight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booty - valuable stolen goods, especially those taken in war
  • Synonym: spoils - goods taken forcibly from a person or place, often in war
  • Degree Higher: treasure - valuables or riches, often hidden or accumulated

Etymology

From Hindi lūṭ (plunder, booty), from Sanskrit luṇṭh (to rob).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13283562-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
to steal goods from a place, especially during a war, riot, or disaster
steal goods, especially during chaos

Examples

Several people tried to loot the abandoned warehouses after the storm hit the city.
The army threatened to arrest anyone attempting to loot shops during the unrest.
Thieves broke into the museum to loot valuable artifacts amid the confusion.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, especially during war or conflict
  • Synonym: pillage - to rob a place using violence, especially in wartime

Etymology

From Hindi lūṭ (plunder, booty), via Urdu, ultimately from Sanskrit luṇṭhati (he plunders).

4. VERB

/ luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
to take or use something illegally or without permission, especially in relation to intellectual property or ideas
take or use illegally (esp. ideas)

Examples

The professor accused the student of trying to loot ideas from published research.
Many artists believe their work has been looted by others without credit.
He was notorious for looting jokes from classic comedians.

Synset Examples

This writer plundered from famous authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plagiarize - to use someone else's work or ideas as your own without permission
  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you

Etymology

From Hindi *lūṭ* (plundering), via Urdu and earlier Sanskrit *luṇṭh-*.

5. NOUN

/ luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
goods or treasures that have been stolen, especially during war or by thieves.
stolen goods or treasures

Examples

The police recovered the stolen loot hidden in an abandoned warehouse.
After the heist, the thieves divided the loot among themselves.
The museum displayed ancient loot seized from shipwrecks.

Synset Examples

The pirates were thrilled with the loot they gathered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - stolen goods taken by force, especially in war
  • Synonym: booty - valuable things taken by thieves or in war
  • Synonym: spoils - goods or benefits taken by force, especially in war

Etymology

From Hindi *lūṭ* (plunder), from Sanskrit *lōṭ* (he steals).
