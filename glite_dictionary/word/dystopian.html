dystopian

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈtoʊpiən / (AmE), / dɪsˈtəʊpiən / (BrE)
As bad as can be; relating to or characteristic of a society marked by great suffering, oppression, or misery.
characterized by great misery or oppression

Examples

Many critics see the novel as a warning about a possible dystopian future.
The dystopian setting of the film left audiences feeling deeply unsettled.
His paintings often depict dystopian cityscapes full of decay and despair.

Synset Examples

AIDS is one of the dystopian harbingers of the global villages

Related Terms

  • Antonym: utopian - characterized by ideal perfection or an imagined perfect society

Etymology

From dystopia (from Greek dys- 'bad' + topos 'place') + -an.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstoʊpiən / (AmE), / dɪsˈtəʊpɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an imagined society where there is great suffering, injustice, or oppression, often in the future.
relating to a grim or oppressive society

Examples

The novel depicts a dystopian future where personal freedoms are severely restricted.
Many science fiction films explore dystopian societies marked by surveillance and control.
Her latest painting has a distinctly dystopian atmosphere, filled with dark, looming buildings and empty streets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: utopian - relating to an ideal or perfect society

Etymology

From dystopia, coined in the 19th century as opposite to utopia; from Greek 'dys-' (bad) + 'topos' (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03031589-a
Synset POS: a
