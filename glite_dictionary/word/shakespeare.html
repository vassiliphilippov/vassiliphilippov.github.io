Shakespeare

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃeɪkspɪər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkspɪə / (BrE)
William Shakespeare (1564–1616), an English poet, playwright, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language.
English poet and playwright (1564–1616)

Examples

Many consider Shakespeare the greatest playwright in the English language.
The students performed a scene from one of Shakespeare's comedies at the festival.
Reading Shakespeare can be challenging, but his works are still studied worldwide.

Etymology

From the English surname Shakespeare, borne by William Shakespeare.
