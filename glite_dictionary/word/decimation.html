decimation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsɪˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛsɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of destroying or killing a large proportion of a population or group
destroying or killing a large part of a group

Examples

The decimation of the local wildlife was evident after the forest fire.
Waves of disease led to the decimation of the city's population in the 14th century.
Some fear that climate change could cause the decimation of coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage
  • Synonym: annihilation - complete destruction of something
  • Degree Lower: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less

Etymology

From Latin decimatio (removal of a tenth), originally a Roman military punishment.
