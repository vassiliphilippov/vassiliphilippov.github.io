kedgeree

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈkɛdʒəri / (BrE)
A dish consisting of rice mixed with cooked flaked fish, hard-boiled eggs, and sometimes parsley, originating from Anglo-Indian cuisine.
dish of rice, fish, and eggs
british

Examples

She served kedgeree for breakfast during the family gathering.
Traditional kedgeree often includes smoked haddock, rice, and hard-boiled eggs.
His favorite brunch dish is kedgeree garnished with fresh parsley and lemon.

Etymology

From Hindi 'khichri' (a dish of rice and lentils), influenced by Anglo-Indian cuisine.
