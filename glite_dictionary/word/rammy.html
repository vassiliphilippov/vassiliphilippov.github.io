rammy

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmi / (AmE), / ˈræmi / (BrE)
a noisy argument, fight, or quarrel, especially in a loud or disorderly manner; chiefly Scottish and Northern English.
a noisy argument or quarrel
informal british

Examples

The children got into a rammy over who would get the last piece of cake.
There was a bit of a rammy outside the pub last night.
We heard a loud rammy coming from the next room.

Synset Examples

We had a rammy over who would control the playlist.
The kids had a rammy in the backyard during the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: row - a noisy argument or quarrel
  • Synonym: brawl - a rough or noisy fight or quarrel
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; quiet and tranquility
