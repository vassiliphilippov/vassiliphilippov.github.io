hermetically

1. ADV

/ hɚˈmɛtɪkli / (AmE), / hɜːˈmɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is completely airtight; so that no air can enter or escape.
in an airtight manner

Examples

The laboratory containers must be hermetically sealed to prevent contamination.
Cheese is often stored hermetically to extend its shelf life.
After cleaning, the instruments were hermetically packaged for transport.

Synset Examples

this bag is hermetically sealed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airtight - completely sealed so that no air can get in or out

Etymology

From 'hermetic,' which is from Medieval Latin 'hermeticus', relating to Hermes Trismegistus, who was associated with alchemy and airtight seals.
