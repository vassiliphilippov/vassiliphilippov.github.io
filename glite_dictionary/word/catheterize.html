to catheterize

1. VERB

/ ˈkæθəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæθətəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to insert a catheter into a body part, especially to allow the passage or drainage of fluids
insert a catheter into a body part
medical

Examples

The nurse had to catheterize the patient before the surgery.
Doctors sometimes need to catheterize the bladder in cases of urinary retention.
She was taught how to catheterize patients safely during her clinical training.

Synset Examples

catheterize the patient's bladder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catheter - a thin tube inserted into the body to remove or deliver fluids

Etymology

From 'catheter' + '-ize', patterned after French 'cathétériser'.
