ceramic

1. ADJ

/ səˈræmɪk / (AmE), / səˈræmɪk / (BrE)
made of, relating to, or consisting of ceramic material such as pottery or porcelain
made of ceramic material

Examples

The collection includes a rare ceramic vase from the eighteenth century.
We installed elegant ceramic tiles in the kitchen.
The company specializes in ceramic products for industrial applications.

Synset Examples

a ceramic dish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcelain - a white vitrified translucent ceramic; synonym in some contexts
  • Antonym: glass - non-ceramic hard material; antonym in material science contexts

Etymology

From Greek keramikos (of pottery), from keramos (potter's clay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03026954-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ səˈræmɪk / (AmE), / səˈræmɪk / (BrE)
an object made from a nonmetallic mineral that has been fired at a high temperature, such as pottery, tiles, or figurines
object made from fired clay or similar material

Examples

The museum's exhibit featured an ancient ceramic bowl decorated with intricate patterns.
She collects ceramics from around the world, including plates, vases, and tiles.
The artist shaped the wet clay before firing it to create a unique ceramic sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pottery - vessels or objects made of baked clay
  • Synonym: porcelain - a fine, white, vitrified ceramic material

Etymology

From Greek keramikos (of pottery), from keramos (potter's clay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03000998-n
Synset POS: n
