zoomastigote

1. NOUN

/ zuːˈmæstəˌɡoʊt / (AmE), / zuːˈmæstɪˌɡəʊt / (BrE)
A flagellate protozoan that lacks photosynthesis and other plant-like traits, typically living as a free-living or parasitic organism.
flagellate protozoan without plant traits

Examples

Under the microscope, the scientist identified the organism as a zoomastigote.
Some zoomastigotes are known to cause diseases in humans and animals.
Unlike algae, a zoomastigote does not perform photosynthesis and is more animal-like in its behavior.

Etymology

From Greek 'zōon' (animal) + 'mastix' (whip) + '-ote', referring to whip-like flagella.
