to overemphasise

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vərˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (BrE)
to place excessive emphasis on something; to give something more importance or attention than is appropriate
put too much emphasis on
british

Examples

Teachers sometimes overemphasise the importance of test results.
It is easy to overemphasise minor issues while overlooking bigger problems.
The report tends to overemphasise the risks involved in the project.

Synset Examples

I cannot overemphasize the importance of this book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overemphasize - place special or excessive emphasis on (American spelling)
  • Antonym: underemphasise - give too little emphasis to

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' meaning 'excessively' and 'emphasise'.
