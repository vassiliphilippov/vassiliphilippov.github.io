Antheraea

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪriə / (AmE), / ænˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of large moths whose larvae produce high-quality silk, including the tussah and muga silkworms
genus of silk-producing moths

Examples

The larvae of Antheraea are cultivated for the production of tussah silk.
Researchers study Antheraea to improve silk quality and yield.
In India, the Antheraea genus includes the muga silkworm, valued for its golden silk.

Etymology

From modern Latin Antheraea, genus name, possibly from Greek 'antheros' (flowerlike), alluding to the attractive appearance of the moths.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪriə / (AmE), / ænˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of large moths whose larvae produce high-quality silk, including important species such as the tasar silkworm.
genus of silk-producing moths

Examples

The larvae of Antheraea are cultivated for their valuable silk.
Scientists are researching Antheraea species to improve natural fibre production.
Wild Antheraea populations are found throughout parts of Asia and India.

Etymology

From modern Latin Antheraea, the genus name given by Jacob Hübner, likely related to 'anther' (flower part) due to appearance.
