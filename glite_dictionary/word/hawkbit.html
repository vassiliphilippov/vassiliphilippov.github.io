hawkbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑkˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkˌbɪt / (BrE)
A common wildflower of the genus Leontodon, found in temperate Europe and the Mediterranean region, with yellow dandelion-like flowers.
wildflower of the genus Leontodon

Examples

We saw a field dotted with bright yellow hawkbit flowers growing among the grass.
The hawkbit can be identified by its jagged leaves and sunny blossoms.
Gardeners often mistake hawkbit for dandelion due to its similar appearance.

Etymology

Formed from 'hawk' + 'bit' (possibly from a folk belief that hawks ate the plant).
