desipramine

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsɪˈpræmin / (AmE), / ˌdɛsɪˈpræmiːn / (BrE)
A tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat depression and sometimes attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and other conditions.
a tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed desipramine to help manage his depressive symptoms.
Some patients respond well to desipramine after other antidepressants fail.
High doses of desipramine may cause side effects such as dry mouth and drowsiness.

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: combining 'des-' (removal of a group) and 'imipramine' (a related tricyclic compound).
