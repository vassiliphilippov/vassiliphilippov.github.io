tasse

1. NOUN

/ tæs / (AmE), / tæs / (BrE)
one of a pair of pieces of armor that hang from the lower edge of the fauld to protect the upper thighs
piece of armor protecting the thighs

Examples

The knight's tasses protected his upper thighs during battle.
Museum displays often include a full suit of armor with faulds and tasses.
After his tasse was damaged, he had it repaired by the village blacksmith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauld - horizontal armor plate worn around the hips or waist area

Etymology

From French tasse, meaning 'cup', relating to the curved shape of the plates.
