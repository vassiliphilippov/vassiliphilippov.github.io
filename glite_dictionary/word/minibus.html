minibus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniˌbʌs / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪbʌs / (BrE)
a small passenger bus designed to carry about 4 to 10 people, smaller than a full-sized bus.
small passenger bus (4–10 seats)

Examples

The school decided to buy a minibus for transporting students to sports events.
Our tour group traveled comfortably in a minibus through the city streets.
A minibus stopped outside the hotel to pick up the guests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bus - a large vehicle for carrying passengers by road

Etymology

From English "mini-" (small) and "bus."
