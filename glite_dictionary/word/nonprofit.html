nonprofit

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈprɑːfɪt / (AmE), / nɒnˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
Not intended to make a profit; not commercially motivated, especially in relation to organizations or activities.
not commercially motivated; not for profit

Examples

The hospital operates as a nonprofit institution focused on patient care rather than generating income.
Many nonprofit organizations rely on donations and volunteers to support their missions.
She works for a nonprofit theater company that offers free performances to the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: for-profit - operating with the intention to make a profit

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'profit' (financial gain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00486668-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ nɑnˈprɑfɪt / (AmE), / nɒnˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
An organization that operates for purposes other than making a profit, typically focusing on charitable, educational, or social causes.
organization not for profit-making

Examples

The museum is a nonprofit that relies on donations to operate.
She decided to start a nonprofit to help support local artists.
Many nonprofits depend on volunteers to deliver their programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - an organization that helps those in need, often nonprofit
  • Antonym: business - an organization intended to make a profit

Etymology

From the prefix 'non-' (not) + 'profit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01139918-n
Synset POS: n
