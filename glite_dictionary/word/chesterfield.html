chesterfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛstərˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛstəˌfiːld / (BrE)
a formal, tailored overcoat, typically featuring a velvet collar
a formal overcoat with a velvet collar

Examples

He wore a sleek chesterfield to the evening gala.
She inherited her grandfather’s vintage chesterfield from the 1920s.
A chesterfield is considered a classic choice for winter formal wear.

Etymology

Named after the Earl of Chesterfield, an English nobleman associated with this style.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛstɚˌfild / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛstəfiːld / (BrE)
a large, overstuffed sofa with upright arms and a back of the same height, often upholstered in leather and with deep buttoning
large, upholstered sofa with upright arms

Examples

They decided to buy a beautiful leather chesterfield for their living room.
She lounged comfortably on the chesterfield while reading a book.
The antique chesterfield was the centerpiece of the drawing room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a long upholstered seat typically with a back and arms
  • Synonym: couch - a piece of furniture for sitting or reclining, similar to a sofa

Etymology

Named after the Earl of Chesterfield, an 18th-century English nobleman traditionally credited with introducing the design.
