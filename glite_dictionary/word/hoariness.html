hoariness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːr.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.rɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being grey or white with age; great age.
state of being grey or aged

Examples

The hoariness of his beard gave him a dignified, wise appearance.
Botanists remarked on the hoariness of the leaf surfaces.
There was a certain hoariness to the tradition, handed down through countless generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grizzliness - the state of being grizzled or grey-haired
  • Antonym: youth - the state of being young

Etymology

From Middle English 'hor' (grey, old), ultimately of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɔːrɪnɪs / (BrE)
a silvery-white color, especially as seen in hair or on plants due to age or frost
silvery-white color, especially of hair or frost

Examples

The old man's beard had a distinct hoariness that made him appear wise.
In autumn, early morning hoariness covered the meadow grass with a silvery sheen.
The hoariness of the sage leaves gave them a frosted appearance even in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grayness - the quality or state of being gray in color
  • Degree Higher: whiteness - the quality or state of being white

Etymology

From Middle English hore (gray, white with age), from Old English hār (gray, venerable).
