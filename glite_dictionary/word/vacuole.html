vacuole

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juː.əʊl / (BrE)
a small cavity within the cytoplasm of a cell, surrounded by a membrane and typically containing fluid or other materials
small cavity in a cell containing fluid

Examples

The plant cell's central vacuole stores water and maintains cell structure.
Scientists observed several vacuoles containing nutrients in the amoeba's cytoplasm.
During osmosis, water collects in the vacuole of the cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesicle - a small fluid-filled sac within a cell

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuolum', diminutive of 'vacuus' (empty).
