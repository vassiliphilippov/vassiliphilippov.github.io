Hanukkah

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.nə.kə / (AmE), / ˈhæn.ʊ.kə / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish holiday commemorating the rededication of the Temple in Jerusalem in 165 BC, celebrated with the lighting of the menorah.
Jewish festival of lights

Examples

Many Jewish families celebrate Hanukkah by lighting candles and exchanging small gifts each night.
The story of Hanukkah commemorates the victory of the Maccabees and the miracle of the oil.
During Hanukkah, traditional foods like latkes and sufganiyot are commonly eaten.

Etymology

From Hebrew חֲנוּכָּה‎ (ḥanukkáh), meaning 'dedication'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːnəkə / (AmE), / ˈhanəkə / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish holiday commemorating the rededication of the Temple in Jerusalem in 165 BC, celebrated with the lighting of the menorah and traditional foods.
Jewish festival of lights

Examples

Every year, my family lights the menorah to celebrate Hanukkah.
During Hanukkah, children often play dreidel and eat potato latkes.
The traditions of Hanukkah include lighting candles and singing special songs.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːnəkə / (AmE), / ˈhænəkə / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish festival commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC, celebrated by the lighting of candles on each night.
Jewish festival of rededication of the Temple

Examples

Every year, families gather to celebrate Hanukkah by lighting the menorah.
Children play with dreidels and eat traditional foods during Hanukkah.
The story of Hanukkah is an important part of Jewish history and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Festival of Lights - Another name for Hanukkah, emphasizing the candle-lighting tradition

Etymology

From Hebrew חֲנֻכָּה‎ (ḥănukkāh, ‘dedication’).
