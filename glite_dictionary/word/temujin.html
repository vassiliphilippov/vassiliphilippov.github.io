Temujin

1. PROPN

/ təˈmuːdʒɪn / (AmE), / tɛˈmuːdʒɪn / (BrE)
The birth name of Genghis Khan, the Mongolian emperor who founded the Mongol Empire, stretching from the Black Sea to the Pacific Ocean (1162–1227).
birth name of Genghis Khan, Mongolian emperor

Examples

Few people know that Temujin was the original name of the conqueror later known as Genghis Khan.
Historians have studied the rise of Temujin from a tribal leader to the ruler of a vast empire.
The story of Temujin is a testament to the power of ambition and leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Genghis Khan - the title assumed by Temujin as emperor of the Mongol Empire

Etymology

From Mongolian Темүжин (Temüjin), his given name at birth.
