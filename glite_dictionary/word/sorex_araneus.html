Sorex araneus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrɛks əˈræniəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɛks əˈræniəs / (BrE)
A small mammal of the shrew family, known as the common shrew, found in Europe and northern Asia.
species of common shrew

Examples

The Sorex araneus is often found in hedgerows and grasslands across Europe.
Researchers studied the diet of Sorex araneus in its natural habitat.
Unlike mice, the Sorex araneus has a distinctly elongated snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a small insectivorous mammal with a long pointed snout

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Sorex' (shrew) and species 'araneus' (spider-like).
