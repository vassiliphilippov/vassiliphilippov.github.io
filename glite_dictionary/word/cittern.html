cittern

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtɚn / (AmE), / ˈsɪtən / (BrE)
a 16th-century musical instrument resembling a guitar, with a pear-shaped soundbox and wire strings, popular in Renaissance Europe
Renaissance stringed musical instrument

Examples

The musician played a cittern at the Renaissance fair, delighting the audience with its bright sound.
In the museum's collection, the cittern stands next to other early stringed instruments.
The composer wrote a piece especially for the cittern and lute, evoking a sense of the 16th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guitar - a modern stringed instrument with a flat body and fretted neck
  • Synonym: mandolin - a stringed musical instrument resembling a lute

Etymology

From Old French 'cistre', from Latin 'cithara', from Ancient Greek 'kithara' (a kind of lyre).
