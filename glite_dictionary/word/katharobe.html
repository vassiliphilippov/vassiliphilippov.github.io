katharobe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθæroʊb / (AmE), / kəˈθærəʊb / (BrE)
a microorganism that lives in an oxygen-rich environment lacking organic matter
organism thriving in oxygenated, organic-free environments
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of katharobe thriving in highly purified water systems.
Unlike most bacteria, a katharobe adapts to environments with no available organic nutrients.
The presence of a katharobe suggests extremely clean water with sufficient oxygen.

Etymology

From Greek 'katharos' (pure) and '-bion' (life), indicating purity of environment.
