debt

1. NOUN

/ dɛt / (AmE), / dɛt / (BrE)
A sum of money, goods, or services that is owed by one person or organization to another.
money or goods owed to someone

Examples

After college, she had a large amount of debt to repay.
His company is struggling because of mounting debts.
They worked hard to pay off their debt before buying a new car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liability - something for which someone is responsible, often a financial obligation
  • Antonym: credit - an arrangement to receive goods or services before payment, or money that is owed to you

Etymology

From Latin debitum (something owed), via Old French dette.

2. NOUN

/ dɛt / (AmE), / dɛt / (BrE)
A sum of money that is owed or the state of owing money or something else to another person or organization.
the state of owing money

Examples

After losing his job, John found himself deep in debt.
The company finally managed to pay off all its debt.
She avoided buying things on credit to stay out of debt.

Synset Examples

he is badly in debt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: credit - an amount of money available to be borrowed or the state of having funds available
  • Synonym: liability - a thing for which someone is responsible, especially a financial obligation

Etymology

From Old French 'dette', from Latin 'debitum' (something owed).

3. NOUN

/ dɛt / (AmE), / dɛt / (BrE)
a sum of money or an obligation that is owed or due to another person or entity
money or obligation owed

Examples

He is working overtime to pay off his debt before graduating from college.
The company collapsed under the weight of massive debt.
They settled the debt after months of negotiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loan - money that is borrowed and expected to be paid back
  • Synonym: credit - the ability to obtain goods or services before payment
  • Antonym: asset - something owned that has value

Etymology

From Old French dette, from Latin debitum (something owed), from debere (to owe).
