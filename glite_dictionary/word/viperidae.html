Viperidae

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈpɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / vɪˈpɛərɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of venomous snakes found chiefly in Europe, Asia, and Africa, known as Old World vipers
family of venomous Old World vipers

Examples

Members of the Viperidae are characterized by their long, hinged fangs.
The forested regions of Africa are home to several species within the Viperidae family.
Researchers discovered a new species of Viperidae in the mountains of Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - any snake of the viper family, Viperidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'vipera' (viper) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ vaɪˈpɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / vɪˈpɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of venomous snakes commonly known as vipers, native to the Old World, characterized by long, hinged fangs capable of deep envenomation
family of venomous Old World vipers
medical

Examples

The Viperidae are known for their long, hinged fangs used to inject venom into their prey.
Many members of Viperidae can be found in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Scientists study the evolution and diversity of Viperidae to understand venom mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - any snake of the family Viperidae

Etymology

From New Latin Viperidae, from Latin vipera (viper) + -idae (family suffix)
