exhilaration

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzɪləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɪləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of lively happiness and excitement.
feeling of lively joy and excitement

Examples

She felt a surge of exhilaration as the roller coaster raced down the tracks.
The team's victory was met with cheers and exhilaration from the fans.
Hiking in the mountains always fills him with a sense of exhilaration.

Synset Examples

he could hardly conceal his excitement when she agreed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Degree Higher: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness or joyful excitement
  • Degree Lower: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction

Etymology

From Latin exhilaratio (cheerfulness), from exhilarare (to cheer up).
