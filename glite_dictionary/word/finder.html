finder

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndə / (BrE)
an optical device used to help a user locate or aim at an object of interest, especially in cameras or telescopes
optical device for locating targets

Examples

The photographer adjusted the finder to frame the perfect shot.
Through the telescope's finder, she quickly spotted the planet.
He replaced the broken finder on his old camera with a modern one.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03345668-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndə / (BrE)
a person who discovers or is the first to notice something
person who discovers something

Examples

The finder of the lost manuscript received a generous reward.
As the finder of the ancient fossil, she was honored by the museum.
The law sometimes grants special rights to the finder of valuable lost property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discoverer - person who discovers something
  • Antonym: loser - person who loses something contrasted with one who finds it

Etymology

From Old English findan (to find) + -er (agent noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndə / (BrE)
a person who discovers something after searching for it
person who discovers something after searching

Examples

The finder of the lost wallet returned it to the owner.
As a treasure finder, she spent years searching for rare coins along the beach.
Being the finder of the missing key, he was praised by everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discoverer - person who discovers something previously unknown
  • Antonym: loser - person who has lost something

Etymology

From Old English 'findan' (to find) + -er (agent suffix).
