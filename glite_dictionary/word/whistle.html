whistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
an act of signalling by producing a high-pitched sound with the mouth or by blowing a device called a whistle
act of signalling by whistling or whistle blow

Examples

The referee's whistle brought the first half of the match to an end.
We waited for the train whistle before crossing the tracks.
At noon, the factory whistle sounded, and all the workers headed to lunch.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an action or sound that gives information or instructions

Etymology

Old English *hwistlian*, from Proto-Germanic origins.
