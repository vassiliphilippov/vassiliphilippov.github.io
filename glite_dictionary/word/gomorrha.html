Gomorrha

1. PROPN

/ goʊˈmɔr.ə / (AmE), / gəˈmɒr.ə / (BrE)
A city mentioned in the Old Testament, destroyed by God due to the wickedness of its inhabitants, often referenced alongside Sodom.
biblical city destroyed for wickedness

Examples

The destruction of Gomorrha is often cited as a warning against moral decay.
In his sermon, the preacher spoke of Gomorrha as a symbol of divine punishment.
Scholars continue to debate the historical location of Gomorrha near the Dead Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sodom - Another biblical city destroyed for wickedness

Etymology

From Hebrew עֲמֹרָה (*ʿĂmōrāh*), via Greek Γόμορρα (*Gómorra*), used in the Latin Bible.
