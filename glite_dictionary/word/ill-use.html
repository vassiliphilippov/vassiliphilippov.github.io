to ill-use

1. VERB

/ ɪlˈjuːz / (AmE), / ɪlˈjuːz / (BrE)
to treat someone or something in a harsh, unfair, or cruel way; to abuse or mistreat.
treat badly or unfairly

Examples

The manager would often ill-use his staff to achieve his own goals.
It is wrong to ill-use animals, regardless of the situation.
She felt hurt after being ill-used by her closest friends.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to treat (a person or animal) with cruelty or violence, especially regularly or repeatedly
  • Synonym: to mistreat - to treat (a person or animal) badly, cruelly, or unfairly
  • Antonym: treat well - to act towards someone in a kind, fair, or pleasant way

Etymology

From 'ill' (badly) + 'use' (employ, treat), formed in English.
