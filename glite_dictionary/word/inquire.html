to inquire

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to ask for information about something; to seek to know or learn about something through questioning
ask for information

Examples

She called the office to inquire about the status of her application.
Whenever I travel, my parents always inquire if I've arrived safely.
The journalist inquired whether the rumors were true during the press conference.

Synset Examples

He wondered who had built this beautiful church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ask - to request information, usually in the form of a question

Etymology

From Latin inquirere, from in- (into) + quaerere (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00731363-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to ask someone for information; to address a question to someone and seek an answer
to ask about something

Examples

She called to inquire about the status of her application.
If you inquire at the reception desk, they can give you more information.
I often inquire whether my students need extra help with their assignments.

Synset Examples

Ask your teacher about trigonometry
The children asked me about their dead grandmother
I inquired about their special today
He had to ask directions several times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ask - to put a question to someone
  • Synonym: question - to ask questions or interrogate

Etymology

From Latin 'inquirere' from 'in-' (into) + 'quaerere' (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00899241-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to formally ask for information or investigate something, especially as part of an official process
to seek information; to investigate

Examples

The committee decided to inquire into the misuse of funds within the organization.
Detectives were called in to inquire about the sudden disappearance.
Journalists began to inquire further after noticing several inconsistencies in the report.

Synset Examples

The district attorney's office investigated reports of possible irregularities
inquire into the disappearance of the rich old lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry to discover facts
  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or investigate closely

Etymology

From Latin inquirere, from in- (into) + quaerere (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00787624-v
Synset POS: v
