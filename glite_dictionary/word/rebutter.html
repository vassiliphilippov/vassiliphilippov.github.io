rebutter

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈbʌtər / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌtə / (BrE)
a person who refutes or disproves an argument by presenting contrary evidence or reasoning, especially in a debate
person who refutes arguments in debate

Examples

The team's rebutter skillfully addressed every point raised by the opposition.
As a seasoned rebutter, she quickly dismantled the flawed reasoning in the proposal.
During the debate, the role of rebutter requires careful listening and sharp critical thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rebut - to refute or contradict an argument
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who defends or backs an argument

Etymology

From 'rebut', based on the pattern of agent nouns with '-er'; 'rebut' from Old French 'rebouter' (to thrust back).

2. NOUN

/ ɹɪˈbʌtɚ / (AmE), / ɹɪˈbʌtə / (BrE)
(law) A legal pleading made by the defendant in reply to a plaintiff's surrejoinder.
legal pleading by defendant in reply
legal

Examples

The defendant's lawyer filed a rebutter after reviewing the plaintiff's surrejoinder.
In historical English law, the sequence of pleadings included the rejoinder and the subsequent rebutter.
The judge allowed the submission of a rebutter to address new points raised by the plaintiff.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surrejoinder - a pleading by the plaintiff in answer to the defendant's rejoinder
  • Synonym: rejoinder - a pleading made by a defendant in response to the plaintiff's replication

Etymology

From Middle French rebouter (to drive back), from re- (again) + bouter (to push).
