Zannichellia palustris

1. NOUN

/ zænɪˈkiːliə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / zænɪˈkiːliə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with thread-like leaves, commonly found in still or slow-moving fresh or brackish waters; often used to oxygenate cool water ponds and aquaria
species of aquatic plant (horned pondweed)

Examples

Zannichellia palustris is often added to aquariums to help increase oxygen levels.
Ecologists found abundant Zannichellia palustris in the marsh's shallow waters.
Because Zannichellia palustris thrives in brackish water, it's a common sight in estuarine habitats.

Etymology

From the genus name Zannichellia, named after Italian botanist Giovanni Zannichelli, and Latin palustris meaning ‘of the marsh’.
