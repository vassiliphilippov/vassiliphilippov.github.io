untried

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtraɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtraɪd / (BrE)
not yet proved, attempted, or subjected to testing or experience
not yet proved or tested

Examples

The team used an untried strategy to surprise their opponents.
He was nervous about taking on such an untried role in the company.
The medicine remains untried on children and needs further research.

Synset Examples

an untested drug
untested theory
an untried procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untested - not tested or examined

Etymology

From un- (not) + tried (past participle of try).

WordNet ID: oewn-01647536-s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtraɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtraɪd / (BrE)
not experienced, used, or tested before; not having been subjected to a particular trial or challenge.
not tested or experienced

Examples

He was an untried rookie, nervous before his first game.
The judge refused to risk the company's reputation on untried technology.
Her ideas, though untried, offered a fresh perspective on the project.

Synset Examples

unseasoned artillery volunteers
still untested in battle
an illustrator untried in mural painting
a young hand at plowing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untested - not subjected to testing or trials
  • Antonym: seasoned - having a lot of experience of doing something

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'tried' (past participle of 'try').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941001-s
Synset POS: s
