braced

1. ADJ

/ breɪst / (AmE), / breɪst / (BrE)
supported or held in position by braces or buttresses to prevent movement or collapse.
held up by braces or supports

Examples

The old bridge was braced with steel beams to improve its stability.
After the earthquake, the walls were braced to prevent further damage.
The tent was braced against strong winds using additional poles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supported - kept from falling or giving way; held up
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02359688-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ breɪst / (AmE), / breɪst / (BrE)
(of a person or body part) tensed or positioned so as to be ready for confrontation or danger.
tensed or prepared for danger

Examples

He stood to attention, his shoulders braced for the approaching storm.
With her legs braced against the wind, she refused to move.
The soldiers waited, braced for the commander’s next order.

Synset Examples

he stood to attention with his shoulders braced

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or strain

Synset POS: s
