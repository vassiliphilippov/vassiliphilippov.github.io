sprawling

1. ADJ

/ ˈsprɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
spreading out over a large area in an irregular or untidy way
spread out irregularly

Examples

The city has become a sprawling metropolis with neighborhoods stretching to the horizon.
His sprawling signature was difficult to decipher on the official documents.
The sprawling branches of the old oak tree provided ample shade in the park.

Synset Examples

sprawling handwriting
straggling branches
straggly hair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compact - closely and neatly packed together

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'sprawl', from Middle English 'sprawlen'.
