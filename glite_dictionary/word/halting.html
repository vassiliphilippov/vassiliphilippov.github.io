halting

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɔltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltɪŋ / (BrE)
(archaic) Lame; disabled in the feet or legs, so as to have difficulty walking.
lame; disabled in the feet or legs (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the ancient tale, the halting knight struggled to keep up with his companions.
She aided the halting traveler who could scarcely cross the cobblestone street.
Old records describe many soldiers returning from battle halting and forever changed.

Synset Examples

a crippled soldier
a game leg

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lame - unable to walk properly because of injury or disability of the leg or foot

Etymology

From Middle English 'halte', from Old English 'healtan' (to limp).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɔltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech or movement) slow and hesitant, often because of uncertainty or emotional strain.
slow or hesitant due to emotion or uncertainty

Examples

She spoke in a halting voice, struggling to contain her emotions.
His halting explanation made it clear that he was unprepared for the question.
In her halting speech, she tried to apologize for her mistake.

Synset Examples

uttered a few halting words of sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitant - slow to act or speak because of uncertainty
  • Antonym: fluent - able to express oneself easily and smoothly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00291650-s
Synset POS: s
