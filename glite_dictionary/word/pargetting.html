pargetting

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrdʒɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdʒɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
plaster, often decoratively applied, used to coat the outside walls of buildings or to line chimneys.
decorative external plasterwork

Examples

The Tudor house was noted for its elaborate pargetting on the exterior walls.
Artisans restored the crumbling pargetting using traditional techniques and materials.
Fireplaces built centuries ago often feature pargetting in their chimneys for improved insulation.

Etymology

From Middle English 'parget', from Old French 'pargeter' (to coat with plaster).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrdʒɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdʒɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Decorative plasterwork applied to the outside walls of buildings, often with patterns or relief designs.
decorative external plasterwork

Examples

The Tudor house is famous for its intricate pargetting on the front façade.
After restoration, the original pargetting was revealed beneath layers of paint.
Many historic buildings in East Anglia display decorative pargetting on their walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasterwork - work or decoration made from plaster

Etymology

From Middle English 'parget', from Old French 'parjeter' (to throw about, cover).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrɡətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːɡɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
ornamental plasterwork applied to walls or ceilings, especially found in historical buildings
decorative plasterwork on buildings

Examples

The Tudor house was famous for its elaborate pargetting above the entrance.
Restoration of the building preserved much of the original pargetting on its facade.
Traditional pargetting can still be seen on several old cottages in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stucco - fine plaster used for coating wall surfaces or molding into architectural decorations

Etymology

From Old French 'pargeter' (to coat with plaster).
