confidential

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.fɪˈden.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˈden.ʃəl / (BrE)
intended to be kept secret or private; not to be disclosed to others without permission.
intended to be kept secret or private

Examples

All financial records are strictly confidential and cannot be shared without consent.
He trusted me with confidential information about the upcoming merger.
Please mark these documents as confidential before sending them to the legal department.

Synset Examples

this arrangement must be kept confidential
their secret communications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - not meant to be known or seen by others
  • Antonym: public - openly accessible or known to everyone

Etymology

From Latin confidens (trusting), from confidere (to trust).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnfɪˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of documents or information) classified at a level above 'restricted' and below 'secret', and accessible only to authorized persons.
classified level for documents; above restricted, below secret

Examples

Only those with proper clearance can access confidential government reports.
The email contained confidential information not meant for the public.
Unauthorized disclosure of confidential files may result in prosecution.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secret - the next higher level of official classification for documents
  • Degree Lower: restricted - the next lower level of official classification for documents

Etymology

From Latin confidere (to trust) + English -ial.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00417215-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.fɪˈden.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˈden.ʃəl / (BrE)
expressed or given in trust or with the expectation of secrecy; intended to be kept private or intimate
intended to be private or secret

Examples

She told me in a confidential whisper so nobody else could hear.
His confidential manner made her trust him immediately.
The letter was written in a confidential tone, hinting at a close friendship.

Synset Examples

a confidential approach
in confidential tone of voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private - intended for or restricted to the use of a particular person, group, or class
  • Antonym: public - open to or shared by all the people in a society or community

Etymology

From Latin 'confidentialis', from 'confidentia' (confidence, trust).

4. ADJ

(of a person or their job) entrusted with private or secret information from others; able to be trusted with confidential matters.
entrusted with private information

Examples

As a confidential secretary, she handled many sensitive documents every day.
The company relied on him as a confidential advisor to the board.
All confidential employees signed a non-disclosure agreement before starting their roles.

Synset Examples

a confidential secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private - not intended for or shared with others; secret
  • Antonym: public - open to all or shared with others; not secret

Etymology

From Latin 'confidentialis', from 'confidentia' (trust, confidence).
