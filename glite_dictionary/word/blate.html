blate

1. ADJ

/ bleɪt / (AmE), / bleɪt / (BrE)
(chiefly Scottish) shy, bashful, or disposed to avoid attracting attention.
shy or bashful (Scottish)
archaic

Examples

She grew blate in the company of strangers, speaking only when spoken to.
His blate manner made him seem distant at social gatherings.
Don't be so blate; everyone here is friendly and welcoming.

Synset Examples

they considered themselves a tough outfit and weren't bashful about letting anybody know it
‘blate’ is a Scottish term for bashful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bashful - reluctant to draw attention to oneself; shy
  • Synonym: timid - lacking in courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

From Scots, possibly related to Old Norse *blēttr* (timid).
