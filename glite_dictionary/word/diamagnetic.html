diamagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.ə.mæɡˈnɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ə.mæɡˈnɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting diamagnetism; (of a substance) slightly repelled by a magnetic field.
repelled by a magnetic field

Examples

Bismuth is a classic example of a diamagnetic material.
Because the compound is diamagnetic, it is slightly repelled by the external magnetic field.
Water is actually diamagnetic, which is why it is pushed away by a strong magnet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paramagnetic - (of a substance) weakly attracted by a magnet and forming internal, induced magnetic fields
  • Antonym: ferromagnetic - (of a substance) strongly attracted by magnets and capable of being permanently magnetized

Etymology

From dia- (Greek, meaning 'through, across') + magnetic, reflecting opposition to magnetic influence.
