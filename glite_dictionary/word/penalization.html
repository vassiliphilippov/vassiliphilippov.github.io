penalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpiːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of subjecting someone to a penalty as punishment for an offense or rule violation
the act of punishing or imposing a penalty

Examples

The penalization of players for minor infractions is strictly enforced in this league.
New tax laws resulted in the penalization of small businesses.
He argued that the penalization was too harsh for a first-time offender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punishment - the act of penalizing or punishing someone for an offense
  • Antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement; opposite of punishment

Etymology

From penal + -ization, based on Latin 'poena' (punishment).
