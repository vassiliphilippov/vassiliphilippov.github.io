verge

1. NOUN

/ vɜrdʒ / (AmE), / vɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a ceremonial or emblematic staff carried as a symbol of office or authority
ceremonial staff or emblem of office

Examples

The dean carried the verge at the front of the procession.
In medieval ceremonies, the verge symbolized the authority of the official.
A silver verge lay beside the judge's seat as a sign of office.

Etymology

From Latin 'virga' meaning 'rod, stick, wand'.

/ vɝdʒ / (AmE), / vɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a region or point at which something begins or ends; the edge or margin, especially marking a boundary

Examples
