Cornwallis

1. PROPN

/ kɔrnˈwɑlɪs / (AmE), / kɔːnˈwɒlɪs / (BrE)
Charles Cornwallis (1738–1805), a British military commander and colonial administrator, best known for his defeat at Yorktown during the American War of Independence.
British commander in the American War of Independence

Examples

General Cornwallis surrendered to American and French forces at Yorktown in 1781.
The defeat of Cornwallis marked a turning point in the War of Independence.
Many history books detail the military strategies of Cornwallis during the late eighteenth century.

Etymology

From the surname Cornwallis, of Norman origin.
