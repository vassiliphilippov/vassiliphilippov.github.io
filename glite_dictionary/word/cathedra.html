cathedra

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθiːdrə / (AmE), / kəˈθiːdrə / (BrE)
the official chair or throne of a bishop in a church, symbolizing episcopal authority
bishop's official throne or chair in a church

Examples

The bishop took his place on the cathedra during the service.
The ornate cathedra in the cathedral was crafted in the 15th century.
The ceremony required the bishop to be seated on the cathedra before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throne - a ceremonial chair for a monarch or bishop

Etymology

From Latin *cathedra* (chair), itself from Greek *kathedra* (seat).
