sufferance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈsʌf.ər.əns / (BrE)
the ability or willingness to tolerate or endure something, especially something unpleasant or difficult
tolerance; willingness to endure

Examples

His remarkable sufferance of criticism earned him respect from many colleagues.
The policy was only maintained by the sufferance of the board members.
Without much sufferance for rude behavior, she quickly left the room.

Synset Examples

all people should practice toleration and live together in peace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerance - willingness to accept feelings, habits, or beliefs that are different from your own
  • Antonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own

Etymology

From Old French 'sufferance', from Latin 'sufferentia' (endurance), from 'sufferre' (to endure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfɚəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌfərəns / (BrE)
the patient endurance of pain, difficulty, or distress
patient endurance of pain or distress

Examples

Despite years of hardship, her sufferance inspired everyone around her.
He bore the pain in silence, a testament to his great sufferance.
Long sufferance of injustice led to a desire for change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endurance - the ability to withstand hardship or adversity
  • Synonym: tolerance - the capacity to endure continued subjection to something

Etymology

From Old French *sufferance*, from Latin *sufferentia* (endurance), from *sufferre* (to bear, endure).
