Rangifer arcticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋɪfər ɑːrˈktɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈraŋɪfə ɑːkˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
A subspecies of caribou native to the tundra regions of northern Canada; sometimes considered part of the species Rangifer tarandus.
Canadian tundra caribou subspecies

Examples

The migration patterns of Rangifer arcticus are crucial to the ecosystem of northern Canada.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the habitat of Rangifer arcticus.
Indigenous communities have traditionally relied on Rangifer arcticus for food and clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caribou - large North American reindeer
  • Synonym: Rangifer tarandus - the species name for reindeer/caribou

Etymology

Modern Latin; from genus name 'Rangifer' (reindeer) and Latin 'arcticus' (northern, arctic).
