spicule

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪk.juːl / (AmE), / ˈspɪk.juːl / (BrE)
a small, slender, pointed structure that forms part of the internal skeleton of some marine and freshwater invertebrates, such as sponges and corals
small skeletal structure in invertebrates

Examples

The skeleton of many sponges is made up of tiny spicules of silica or calcium carbonate.
Under the microscope, each spicule can be seen as a needle-like structure within the tissue.
The scientist collected coral samples to analyze the composition of their spicules.

Etymology

From Latin spicula (a small spike), diminutive of spica (spike, ear of grain).
