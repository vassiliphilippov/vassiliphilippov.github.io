monoatomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːn.oʊ.əˈtɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əʊ.əˈtɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or element) consisting of single atoms rather than molecules or groups of atoms.
consisting of single atoms
medical

Examples

Helium is a monoatomic gas under standard conditions.
Some noble gases in their natural state exist as monoatomic elements.
A monoatomic molecule consists of only one atom, as opposed to diatomic or polyatomic molecules.

Synset Examples

helium and argon are monatomic gases

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diatomic - consisting of two atoms per molecule
  • Antonym: polyatomic - consisting of more than two atoms per molecule

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'atomos' (indivisible).
