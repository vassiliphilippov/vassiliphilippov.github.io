crib

1. NOUN

/ krɪb / (AmE), / krɪb / (BrE)
a bin or granary used for storing grain, especially on a farm
bin or granary for storing grain

Examples

The old wooden crib behind the barn still holds several tons of corn.
Farmers checked the crib each week to ensure the grain was dry and safe from rodents.
The wheat was transferred from the field directly to the crib for storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granary - a storehouse for threshed grain

Etymology

From Old English 'cribb' (manger, stall), of Germanic origin.
