indigestibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dəˈdʒɛs.tə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈdʒɛs.tɪ.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult or impossible to digest
state of being difficult to digest

Examples

The indigestibleness of the meal made him feel uncomfortable for hours afterward.
Some wild plants are avoided by animals due to the indigestibleness of their leaves.
Critics noted the indigestibleness of the book's dense and complex prose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigestibility - the quality of being indigestible
  • Antonym: digestibility - the quality of being able to be digested

Etymology

Formed from 'indigestible' + '-ness'.
