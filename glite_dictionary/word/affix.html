affix

1. VERB

/ əˈfɪks / (AmE), / əˈfɪks / (BrE)
to attach or add something to the end or surface of something else
attach or add

Examples

Please affix a recent photograph to your application form.
The company seal must be affixed at the bottom of every official document.
She carefully affixed the address label to the package before shipping it.

Synset Examples

He appended a glossary to his novel where he used an invented language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to append - to add something to the end of a written document or text
  • Antonym: to detach - to remove or separate something from something else

Etymology

From Latin affixus, past participle of affigere (to attach to).

2. VERB

/ əˈfɪks / (AmE), / əˈfɪks / (BrE)
to attach or become attached to a stem word in grammar, usually as a prefix or suffix
attach to a word stem (grammar)

Examples

In this language, prefixes regularly affix to the verb stem to alter the meaning.
The negative marker is affixed at the end of the base word.
Suffixes can be affixed to indicate tense or number.

Synset Examples

grammatical morphemes affix to the stem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attach - to fasten or join one thing to another

Etymology

From Latin affixare, from ad- (to) + figere (to fasten).

3. NOUN

/ əˈfɪks / (AmE), / əˈfɪks / (BrE)
a morpheme or element added to a word, such as a prefix or suffix, to alter its meaning or function
linguistic element added to a word

Examples

The word 'unhappy' contains the prefix un- as an affix.
Suffixes like '-ed' and '-ing' are common types of affix in English.
When you add an affix to a root word, you can create a new meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prefix - an affix placed at the beginning of a word
  • Synonym: suffix - an affix placed at the end of a word
  • Antonym: root - the base form of a word to which affixes are added

Etymology

From Latin affixus (fastened to), past participle of affigere (to fasten to).

Source: WordNet
