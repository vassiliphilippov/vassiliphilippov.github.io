disparate

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪspərət / (AmE), / ˈdɪspərət / (BrE)
essentially different in kind; not able to be compared
fundamentally different; distinct

Examples

The survey compared disparate groups from across the country.
Her interests are so disparate that it's hard to find common ground.
The museum displays disparate works ranging from classical sculptures to modern installations.

Synset Examples

such disparate attractions as grand opera and game fishing
disparate ideas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance in appearance or nature; alike

Etymology

From Latin 'disparatus', past participle of 'disparare', from 'dis-' (apart) + 'parare' (prepare).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪspərət, dɪˈspærət / (AmE), / ˈdɪspərət, dɪˈspærət / (BrE)
essentially different in kind; not able to be compared
markedly different or distinct

Examples

The committee was composed of disparate individuals from various professional backgrounds.
Her art collection features disparate styles, ranging from traditional landscapes to abstract sculptures.
Scientists often collaborate across disparate fields to solve complex problems.

Synset Examples

a disparate aggregate of creeds and songs and prayers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: different - not the same as another or each other; unlike
  • Antonym: similar - resembling without being identical

Etymology

From Latin 'disparatus', past participle of 'disparare', meaning 'to separate.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01203019-s
Synset POS: s
