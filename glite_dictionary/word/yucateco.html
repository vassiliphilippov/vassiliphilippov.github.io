Yucateco

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːkəˈteɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌjuːkəˈteɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a Mayan language spoken by the Yucatec people, primarily in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico.
a Mayan language of the Yucatec people

Examples

Many indigenous communities still speak Yucateco as their first language.
She is learning to translate ancient texts from Yucateco into Spanish and English.
The documentary explored the preservation of Yucateco among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yucatec - the Yucatec language or people of the Yucatán Peninsula
  • Degree Higher: Mayan - relating to the languages of the Maya people

Etymology

From Spanish Yucateco, meaning 'from Yucatán.'

2. NOUN

/ juː.kəˈteɪ.koʊ / (AmE), / juː.kəˈteɪ.kəʊ / (BrE)
A member of the Mayan people native to the Yucatan peninsula in Mexico.
member of the Mayan people of Yucatan

Examples

The Yucateco people have preserved many of their ancient traditions.
A prominent Yucateco artisan showed us how to weave traditional textiles.
Researchers have worked closely with Yucateco communities to study Mayan language and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maya - member of the indigenous people of southern Mexico and Central America

Etymology

From Spanish 'yucateco', meaning 'of or from Yucatán'.
