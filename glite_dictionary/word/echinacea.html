Echinacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkɪˈneɪʃə / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪˈneɪsiə / (BrE)
a genus of coarse perennial herbs native to North America, often used in herbal medicine for their immune-boosting properties
genus of North American herbs

Examples

Many people take Echinacea supplements to support their immune system during cold season.
The garden was filled with purple Echinacea thriving in the summer sun.
Researchers are still studying the effectiveness of Echinacea in preventing illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coneflower - common name for plants of the genus Echinacea, known for their cone-shaped flower heads

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek ekhinos (hedgehog, sea urchin), referring to the spiny central disk of the flower.
