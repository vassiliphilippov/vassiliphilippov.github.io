unpermissive

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.pɚˈmɪs.ɪv / (AmE), / ʌn.pəˈmɪs.ɪv / (BrE)
not inclined to allow permission; strict or severe in enforcing rules or discipline
not granting permission; strict

Examples

The school's unpermissive policies made it difficult for students to request time off.
Her unpermissive attitude led to a very disciplined household.
Managers who are too unpermissive may lower employee morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict - enforcing rules rigidly
  • Antonym: lenient - showing tolerance or mercy

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'permissive'.
