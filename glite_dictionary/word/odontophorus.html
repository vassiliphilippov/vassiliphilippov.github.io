Odontophorus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌdɑːntəˈfɔrəs / (AmE), / əʊˌdɒntəˈfɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of Central and South American crested partridges that resemble quails, often classified in their own family.
genus of crested partridges in America

Examples

The genus Odontophorus includes several species of wood quails found in Central and South America.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Odontophorus in the cloud forests.
The distinctive crest of Odontophorus birds makes them easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From Greek odous, odont- (tooth) + phoros (bearing), referring to structures on their beak.
