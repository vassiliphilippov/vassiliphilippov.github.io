waxen

1. ADJ

/ ˈwæksən / (AmE), / ˈwæksən / (BrE)
made of or resembling wax; having the appearance or texture of wax
made of or resembling wax

Examples

The museum displayed a waxen figure of the famous queen.
Her waxen complexion made her seem strangely lifeless in the candlelight.
The artist created a waxen sculpture that looked almost real.

Synset Examples

waxen candles
careful, the floor is waxy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxy - having a wax-like appearance or texture

Etymology

From Old English 'weaxen' (made of wax), from 'wæx' (wax).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwæksən / (AmE), / ˈwæksən / (BrE)
having a pale or unnatural complexion resembling the color or texture of wax
having a pale, wax-like appearance

Examples

After the long illness, her waxen face alarmed her visitors.
The child's waxen pallor suggested he might faint at any moment.
His waxen cheeks glistened in the candlelight, making him appear almost lifeless.

Synset Examples

the poor face with the same awful waxen pallor
the soldier turned his waxlike features toward him
a thin face with a waxy paleness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxy - resembling or containing wax; often refers to a wax-like appearance, especially paleness
  • Synonym: sallow - of an unhealthy, yellow or pale brown color (often used to describe complexion)

Etymology

From Old English 'weaxen', meaning 'made of wax' (from 'wax').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00407572-s
Synset POS: s
