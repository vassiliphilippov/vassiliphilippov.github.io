Gibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,073,741,824 (2^30) bits, chiefly used in computing and digital storage
unit of digital information (2^30 bits)

Examples

The file size was measured at 3 Gibits to ensure accurate data transfer.
Network speeds are sometimes expressed in Gibit per second for high-capacity systems.
A 4 Gibit chip holds twice as much data as a 2 Gibit chip.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Mibit - a unit of information equal to 2^20 (1,048,576) bits
  • Synonym: Gbit - one billion (1,000,000,000) bits, a gigabit

Etymology

Blend of 'giga-' (meaning billion) and 'bit', modeled after units defined by the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC).
