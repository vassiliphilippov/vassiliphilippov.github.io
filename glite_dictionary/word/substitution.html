substitution

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of replacing one thing or person with another
the act of replacing something or someone

Examples

The team made a substitution in the second half of the match.
Vegetarian dishes are offered as a substitution for meat options.
His sudden substitution surprised everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - the act of replacing one thing with another
  • Antonym: removal - the act of taking something away

Etymology

From Latin substitutio (a placing under or replacing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of replacing one person or thing with another
the act of replacing one thing with another

Examples

The coach made a substitution at halftime to strengthen the team's defense.
Vegetarian recipes often use tofu as a substitution for meat.
The substitution of plastic for glass bottles has reduced shipping costs.

Synset Examples

the replacement of lost blood by a transfusion of donor blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - the act of replacing something or someone
  • Synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another

Etymology

From Latin substitutio (a putting in place of another), from substituere (to put in place of).
