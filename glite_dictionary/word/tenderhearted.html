tenderhearted

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛndərˈhɑrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛndəˈhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
Showing sympathy and concern for others; easily moved by another's distress or suffering.
sympathetic and compassionate

Examples

Her tenderhearted response brought comfort to everyone in the room.
Many people see him as tenderhearted because he always helps those in need.
The tenderhearted nurse stayed late to care for her patient.

Synset Examples

a noble tenderhearted creature who sympathizes with all the human race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassionate - feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Antonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling toward others

Etymology

Formed from 'tender' (gentle, sensitive) + 'hearted' (having a specified kind of heart or temperament).

2. ADJ

/ ˌtɛndɚˈhɑrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛndəˈhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
Easily moved by compassion or affection; having a gentle and sympathetic nature.
compassionate and gentle

Examples

Her tenderhearted response to the stray kitten showed her true character.
He is so tenderhearted that sad movies often bring him to tears.
Despite his gruff appearance, the coach is incredibly tenderhearted towards his players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassionate - feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Antonym: hardhearted - lacking in sympathy or compassion; unfeeling

Etymology

From 'tender' (gentle, soft) + 'hearted' (having a particular type of heart or temperament).
