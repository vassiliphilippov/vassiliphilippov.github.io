unconditionally

1. ADV

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nəl.i / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ən.əl.i / (BrE)
in a way that is not limited by or dependent on any conditions; absolutely, without reservations
without any conditions or limitations

Examples

She promised to support him unconditionally through every challenge.
The children were loved unconditionally by their parents.
He unconditionally surrendered to the police after days on the run.

Synset Examples

he accepted the offer unconditionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditionally - only if particular things happen or are agreed to

2. ADV

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nəl.i / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ən.əl.i / (BrE)
in a way that is not limited by any conditions; absolutely or without reservation
without any conditions or restrictions

Examples

She loves her children unconditionally, no matter what they do.
He agreed unconditionally to the terms of the contract.
The charity accepts all donations unconditionally, regardless of amount.

Synset Examples

he flatly denied the charges

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditionally - with limitations or requirements; not absolute

Etymology

From 'unconditional' + '-ly', modeled on 'condition' from Latin 'condicio' (agreement, arrangement).
