to weaken

1. VERB

/ ˈwiːkən / (AmE), / ˈwiːkən / (BrE)
to make something less strong, powerful, or effective
make less strong or effective

Examples

The medicine will weaken the effects of the virus in your body.
Continued stress can weaken your immune system over time.
They built barriers to weaken the impact of the incoming waves.

Synset Examples

soften a shock
break a fall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make something stronger or more effective
  • Synonym: to diminish - to decrease in strength, size, or importance

Etymology

From Old English wācian, meaning 'to become weak'.
