self-reproach

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf rɪˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / sɛlf rɪˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
the act of blaming or criticizing oneself, especially for a fault or mistake
blaming oneself for a fault or mistake

Examples

She was filled with self-reproach after realizing her mistake.
His self-reproach was evident in the way he avoided meeting our eyes.
Despite her friends' reassurances, a deep self-reproach lingered for days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - a deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: guilt - a feeling of having done wrong or failed in an obligation

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + 'reproach' (Old French reproche; from reprochier, to blame).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.rɪˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌself.rɪˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A feeling of deep regret or guilt about one's own actions or behavior, especially after doing something wrong.
deep regret or shame about one's own actions

Examples

After the argument, she was overwhelmed with self-reproach for her harsh words.
His constant self-reproach made it difficult for him to move past his mistake.
Unable to forgive himself, he lived in a state of self-reproach for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilt - a feeling of having committed a wrong or failed in an obligation
  • Synonym: remorse - a deep sense of regret for a wrong committed

Etymology

Formed from 'self' and 'reproach', the latter from Old French 'reproche' (blame).
