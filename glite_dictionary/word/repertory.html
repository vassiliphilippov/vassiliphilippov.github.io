repertory

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛpərˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrɛpətəri / (BrE)
a collection of plays, dances, or musical works that a performer or group can perform and regularly presents as part of their schedule
collection of works performed by an artist or group

Examples

The theater company added a new play to its repertory this season.
Her repertory includes classical piano pieces and contemporary compositions.
The ballet troupe performs different works from their repertory each week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repertoire - all the works a performer is prepared to present

Etymology

From Latin 'repertorium' meaning 'inventory, list' via late Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛp.ərˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / ˈrep.ə.tri / (BrE)
a theatrical company that regularly presents plays from a set repertoire; also, a system of presenting such plays.
theatrical company presenting a repertoire of plays

Examples

Our local theater operates as a repertory, staging different plays throughout the season.
Joining the repertory gave her the chance to perform in many classic dramas and comedies.
The repertory enjoys a loyal audience, thanks to its rotating schedule of productions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repertoire - a stock of plays, dances, or pieces that a company or a performer knows or is prepared to perform

Etymology

From Late Latin 'repertorium' (inventory, list), from Latin 'reperire' (to find).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛpərˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrɛpətəri / (BrE)
A place or container where a stock of things is kept; a storehouse or repository.
storehouse; place where things are stored

Examples

The ancient library served as a repertory of valuable manuscripts and scrolls.
Researchers accessed the repertory of geological samples kept in the university vaults.
A museum can act as a repertory for artifacts from lost civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repository - a place or container where things are stored

Etymology

From Late Latin *repertorium* (inventory, list), from Latin *repertus* (found).

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɛpərˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrɛpətəri / (BrE)
the complete range of skills, aptitudes, or devices available to a person or used in a particular field or occupation
complete range of skills or devices

Examples

As a chef, her repertory includes both classic and modern French techniques.
The comedian’s repertory of voices and accents amazed the audience.
Advances in technology have greatly expanded the repertory of tools available to scientists.
