decomposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(Chemistry) the process of separating a substance into two or more simpler substances, which may differ from each other and from the original.
separation of a substance into simpler parts (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate produces calcium oxide and carbon dioxide.
The decomposition of water into hydrogen and oxygen is an important chemical process.
Rapid decomposition can be observed when hydrogen peroxide is exposed to light and heat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthesis - combining two or more substances to form a new one (chemistry)
  • Synonym: analysis - breaking down a compound into its components (chemistry)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin decompositio(n-), from Latin de- (down) + compositio (putting together).

The process of decay and breaking down of organic matter, typically by bacteria or fungi.
biological decay process

Examples

Heat and moisture can accelerate the decomposition of compost in the garden.
During decomposition, bacteria and fungi break down dead material into simpler substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition

From Late Latin decompositio (separation into parts).

4. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking down a vector field or mathematical entity into simpler or distinct components for analysis
breaking down a mathematical entity into components

Examples

The Helmholtz decomposition allows any vector field to be written as a sum of an irrotational and a solenoidal part.
Fourier decomposition is used to analyze periodic functions in terms of sines and cosines.
In fluid dynamics, decomposition of the velocity field helps to understand the flow's underlying structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of something complex
  • Antonym: composition - the act of putting parts together to form a whole

Etymology

From Latin decompositio, from de- (down, apart) + compositio (putting together).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putrefaction - the process of decay or rotting in a body or other organic matter
