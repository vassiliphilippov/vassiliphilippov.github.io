Geoffroea decorticans

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑːfri.ə dɪˌkɔːrˈteɪkᵻnz / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒfri.ə dɪˌkɔːˈteɪkɪnz / (BrE)
A thorny shrub or small tree native to central Argentina, bearing small orange or yellow flowers and edible berries.
thorny Argentinian shrub or tree with edible berries

Examples

The fruit of the Geoffroea decorticans is enjoyed as a traditional snack in some Argentinian communities.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of Geoffroea decorticans in arid regions.
Local farmers cultivate Geoffroea decorticans for its edible berries and hardy wood.

Etymology

From Latinized form of botanists' surnames (Geoffroy) and Latin 'decorticans', meaning 'removing the bark'.
