mugwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡˌwəːt / (BrE)
a common aromatic plant of the genus Artemisia, especially Artemisia vulgaris, used in herbal medicine and sometimes in cooking
an aromatic plant of the genus Artemisia

Examples

Many herbalists use mugwort to promote relaxation and vivid dreams.
We found mugwort growing wild along the riverbank.
Some traditional dishes are flavored with dried mugwort leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - a related plant of the genus Artemisia, especially Artemisia absinthium

Etymology

From Old English *mucgwyrt*, from *mucg* (“mugwort”) + *wyrt* (“plant, root, herb”).
