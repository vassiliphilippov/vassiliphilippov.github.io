snick

1. NOUN

/ snɪk / (AmE), / snɪk / (BrE)
A small, sharp cut or notch.
a small, sharp cut

Examples

The razor left a snick in the edge of the paper.
She noticed a snick on her finger where the knife had slipped.
There was a tiny snick in the old wooden table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nick - a small cut or notch

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin.

2. NOUN

/ snɪk / (AmE), / snɪk / (BrE)
a slight, glancing contact with the ball off the edge of the cricket bat, especially as detected in cricket
slight contact with cricket ball by bat edge

Examples

The umpire gave him out after hearing a faint snick as the ball passed the bat.
There was a clear snick, but the batsman stood his ground.
The wicketkeeper heard the snick and immediately appealed for a catch.

4. VERB

/ snɪk / (AmE), / snɪk / (BrE)
(in cricket) to hit the ball with a glancing blow from the edge of the bat, often resulting in a catch behind the wicket.
hit with the edge of the bat (cricket)

Examples

The batsman managed to snick the ball just past the wicketkeeper's gloves.
He snicked the delivery, resulting in a sharp catch at slip.
She tried not to snick the ball, but it brushed the edge of her bat.
