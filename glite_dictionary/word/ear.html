ear

1. NOUN

/ ɪr / (AmE), / ɪə / (BrE)
attention or willingness to listen; consideration of what someone says or thinks
attention to what is said

Examples

The senator has the president's ear on this issue.
After years of hard work, she finally gained her boss's ear.
He tried to get her ear to discuss the proposal.

Synset Examples

he tried to get her ear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attention - noticing or focusing on someone or something

2. NOUN

/ ɪr / (AmE), / ɪə / (BrE)
the externally visible part of the outer ear, consisting mainly of cartilage and skin, used for hearing and balance
the visible outer part of the ear

Examples

She tucked her hair behind her ear so she could hear better.
The doctor examined my left ear for signs of infection.
A tiny earring dangled from each ear as she walked into the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auricle - the external part of the ear

Etymology

Old English *ēare*; related to German *Ohr* and Dutch *oor*.

3. NOUN

/ ɪr / (AmE), / ɪə / (BrE)
The part of a cereal plant, such as wheat or corn, that contains the flowers and later the grains; a fruiting spike.
fruiting spike of cereal plant

Examples

The farmer inspected each ear of corn to check if it was ripe for harvesting.
Heavy rain damaged the ears of wheat in the field.
An ear of barley was lodged between two stones in the old wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spike - a long, narrow cluster of flowers or seeds along a central stem

Etymology

From Old English *ēar* (ear of grain), related to Old High German *āhra*.

4. NOUN

/ ɪr / (AmE), / ɪə / (BrE)
the organ on the sides of the head that is responsible for hearing and balance in people and animals
organ for hearing and balance

Examples

She cupped her hand to her ear to hear the faint sound more clearly.
The doctor examined the patient's right ear for signs of infection.
Dogs have a much stronger sense of hearing thanks to their sensitive ears.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eye - the organ of sight
  • Antonym: nose - the organ of smell
  • Synonym: auditory organ - another term for ear, especially in anatomical context

Etymology

Old English *ēare*, from Proto-Germanic *auzōn*

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ɪr / (AmE), / ɪə / (BrE)
the ability to perceive or appreciate sounds, especially in music, speech, or language
ability to perceive or appreciate sounds

Examples

She has an ear for languages and picks up accents easily.
A musician needs a good ear to distinguish subtle changes in notes.
Over time, his ear for jazz improved and he could identify complex rhythms.

Synset Examples

he had a keen ear
a good ear for pitch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - a natural ability to do something well
  • Antonym: tone-deafness - inability to perceive differences in musical pitch
  • Synonym: musicality - sensitivity to, knowledge of, or talent for music
