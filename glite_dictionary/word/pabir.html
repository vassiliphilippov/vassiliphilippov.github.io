Pabir

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːbɪr / (AmE), / ˈpæbɪə / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken south of Lake Chad, mainly in Borno State and parts of Adamawa State, Nigeria
Chadic language of Nigeria

Examples

The Pabir language is still spoken by thousands of people in northeastern Nigeria.
Researchers are documenting stories in Pabir to help preserve the language for future generations.
He learned to greet the local chiefs in Pabir during his visit to Borno State.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chadic - relating to the branch of the Afro-Asiatic languages that includes Hausa and other languages spoken in the Lake Chad region
