Urban VIII

1. PROPN

/ ˌɜrbən ðə ˈeɪtθ / (AmE), / ˌɜːbən ði ˈeɪtθ / (BrE)
(1568–1644) Italian pope from 1623 to 1644, known for sanctioning the condemnation of Galileo and later freeing him.
Italian pope (1623–1644) who condemned Galileo

Examples

Urban VIII reigned during a period of intense scientific and religious conflict in Europe.
Galileo faced condemnation for his views under Urban VIII's papacy.
Many baroque constructions in Rome were commissioned by Urban VIII.

Etymology

From Latin 'Urbanus' (urban, city-dweller), used as a papal regnal name.
