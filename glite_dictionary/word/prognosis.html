prognosis

1. NOUN

/ prɑgˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / prɒgˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A prediction of the probable course and outcome of a situation, especially the likely development of a disease.
prediction of future outcome

Examples

The doctor gave her a positive prognosis after reviewing the test results.
Economists offered their prognosis for the post-pandemic recovery.
The weather service issued a prognosis predicting continued heavy rain over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diagnosis - identification of the nature of an illness or problem

Etymology

From Greek 'prognōsis' (foreknowledge), from 'pro-' (before) + 'gnōsis' (knowledge).

2. NOUN

/ prɑːɡˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / prɒɡˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
An opinion, based on medical experience, of the likely course and outcome of a disease or condition.
medical prediction of disease outcome
medical

Examples

The doctor gave her a hopeful prognosis after reviewing the test results.
According to the prognosis, he is expected to make a full recovery within six months.
Early treatment can often improve the prognosis for many types of cancer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diagnosis - identification of the nature of a disease

Etymology

From Greek prognōsis, from pro- (before) + gnōsis (knowing).
