Artsakh

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈtsɑk / (AmE), / ɑːˈtsɑːk / (BrE)
the Armenian name for the region known as Nagorno-Karabakh, a disputed, unrecognized de facto republic in the South Caucasus
disputed region in the South Caucasus

Examples

The conflict in Artsakh has attracted international attention for decades.
Many Armenians consider Artsakh to be an integral part of their historical homeland.
Talks about the future status of Artsakh continue between Armenia and Azerbaijan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nagorno-Karabakh - disputed region in the South Caucasus; Russian/Azerbaijani name

Etymology

From Armenian Արցախ (Artsakh), the historical name for the region.
