Calapuya

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈpuːjə / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈpuːjə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people traditionally inhabiting the Willamette Basin in Oregon, United States
a Native American people of Oregon

Examples

Many Calapuya were relocated to reservations in the 19th century.
The Calapuya cultivated the land and gathered camas as a staple food.
A descendant of the Calapuya people shared stories at the local museum.
