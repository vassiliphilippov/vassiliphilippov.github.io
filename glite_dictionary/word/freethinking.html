freethinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːˌθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the doctrine or practice of forming opinions and making decisions based on reason and evidence, especially in matters of religion or morality, rather than accepting authority or tradition
forming opinions based on reason, rejecting authority

Examples

She encouraged freethinking among her students, urging them to question assumptions and think independently.
The movement for freethinking gained momentum during the Enlightenment, challenging traditional authorities.
His commitment to freethinking often put him at odds with the more dogmatic members of his community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dogmatism - tendency to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true, without consideration of evidence

Etymology

From 'free' + 'thinking', popularized in the 18th century to denote those who rejected accepted religious authority.
