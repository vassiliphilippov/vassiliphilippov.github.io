impressible

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsɪbəl / (BrE)
easily influenced or affected by impressions or experiences; susceptible to being impressed.
easily influenced or affected

Examples

Children can be especially impressible during their early teenage years.
The jury was criticized for being too impressible and swayed by emotion rather than facts.
Her impressible mind absorbed every new idea she encountered.

Synset Examples

an impressionable youngster
an impressionable age
a waxy mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressionable - easily influenced or affected, especially emotionally

Etymology

From French 'impressible', from Latin 'imprimere' (to impress) + '-ible' (able to be).
