sailfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large predatory saltwater fish distinguished by its long, pointed bill and a tall, sail-like dorsal fin, prized for its speed and as a game fish.
large saltwater fish with a sail-like dorsal fin

Examples

Anglers from around the world travel to Florida to catch the elusive sailfish.
The sailfish is known for reaching speeds of up to 68 miles per hour in the water.
Grilled sailfish is considered a delicacy in some coastal countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - a large saltwater fish with a spear-like upper jaw, related to the sailfish
  • Synonym: swordfish - a large predatory fish with a long, flat bill

Etymology

Named for its sail-like dorsal fin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large marine fish with a long, slender upper jaw and a tall dorsal fin resembling a sail, found in warm seas and prized as a game fish
large marine fish with sail-like dorsal fin

Examples

The sailfish is known for its incredible speed and agility in the water.
Anglers come from around the world hoping to catch a sailfish off the coast of Florida.
With its striking dorsal fin raised, the sailfish cut through the waves gracefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - large, fast-swimming predatory fish related to the sailfish

Etymology

From 'sail' (referring to its prominent dorsal fin) + 'fish'.
