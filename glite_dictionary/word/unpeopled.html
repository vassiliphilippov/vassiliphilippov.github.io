unpeopled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpipəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈpiːpəld / (BrE)
(of a place) having no people living there; not inhabited.
not inhabited; with no people

Examples

The remote island has remained unpeopled for centuries due to its harsh climate.
After the disaster, the town was eerily unpeopled and silent.
They explored miles of unpeopled desert under the scorching sun.

Synset Examples

vast unpopulated plains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uninhabited - (of a place) not lived in by people
  • Antonym: inhabited - (of a place) lived in by people

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'peopled' (inhabited by people).
