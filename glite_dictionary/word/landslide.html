landslide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌslaɪd / (BrE)
an election in which one candidate or party wins by a very large majority
overwhelming victory in an election

Examples

The party secured a landslide in the general election, winning nearly every seat.
She was elected president in a landslide, receiving over 70% of the vote.
Political analysts predicted a landslide after seeing the polling results.

Synset Examples

Roosevelt defeated Hoover in a landslide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowout - victory by a large margin
  • Antonym: narrow victory - a win by a small margin

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌslaɪd / (BrE)
a mass of earth, rock, and debris rapidly moving down a slope or cliff
mass of earth/rock sliding down a slope

Examples

A landslide destroyed several homes at the base of the mountain.
Heavy rains increased the risk of a landslide in the valley.
The highway was closed due to a massive landslide blocking both lanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avalanche - a mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside
  • Synonym: mudslide - a mass of mud sliding down a hill or slope
