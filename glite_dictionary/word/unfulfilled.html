unfulfilled

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.fʊlˈfɪld / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fʊlˈfɪld / (BrE)
(of a person) not having achieved their potential or not satisfied because their abilities or desires have not been realized.
not having achieved potential; not satisfied

Examples

She felt increasingly unfulfilled in her job, despite her many responsibilities.
Many artists remain unfulfilled when they are unable to share their work with a wider audience.
After years in a routine position, he realized he was living an unfulfilled life.

Synset Examples

unfulfilled and uneasy men
unrealized dreams and ambitions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrealized - not achieved or brought to fruition
  • Antonym: fulfilled - having achieved one's potential or desires

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fulfilled' from Old French 'fulfil', influenced by fill and full.
