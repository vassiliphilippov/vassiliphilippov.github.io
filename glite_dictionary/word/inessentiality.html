inessentiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌɛsənʃiˈælɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˌsɛnʃɪˈalɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being essential; lack of fundamental importance
lack of essential importance

Examples

The inessentiality of certain details allowed the report to remain concise.
He questioned the inessentiality of the extra paperwork imposed by the committee.
Some critics highlight the inessentiality of decorative features in modern architecture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: essentiality - the quality of being essential or absolutely necessary

Etymology

From in- (not) + essential + -ity, modeled on Latin elements.
