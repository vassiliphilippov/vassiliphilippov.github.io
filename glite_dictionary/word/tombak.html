tombak

1. NOUN

/ tɒmˈbɑːk / (AmE), / tɒmˈbæk / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and zinc (and sometimes arsenic), used to imitate gold in inexpensive jewelry and for gilding
copper-zinc alloy for cheap gold imitation

Examples

The imitation gold chain was made of tombak, making it affordable but less durable.
Many antique picture frames from the region are gilded using tombak instead of real gold.
Tarnishing is common with tombak jewelry due to its copper content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a common alloy of copper and zinc, similar to tombak

Etymology

From Malay *tambaga* (copper), via Dutch *tombak*.
