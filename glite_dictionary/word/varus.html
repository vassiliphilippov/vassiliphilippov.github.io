varus

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛrəs / (AmE), / ˈvɑːrəs / (BrE)
A deformity in which a part of a limb is turned inward toward the body's midline to an abnormal degree.
inward turning limb deformity (medical)
medical

Examples

The X-ray confirmed a varus deformity in the patient's right knee.
Treatment of varus alignment often involves corrective surgery.
Infants may sometimes be born with a varus foot that requires intervention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: valgus - a deformity where a limb is turned outward

Etymology

From Latin 'varus' (knock-kneed, bent inward).
