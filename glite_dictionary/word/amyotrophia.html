amyotrophia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmi.oʊˈtroʊfi.ə / (AmE), / ˌæmi.əʊˈtrəʊfi.ə / (BrE)
Progressive wasting or degeneration of muscle tissue.
wasting of muscle tissues
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with amyotrophia after experiencing a gradual loss of muscle strength.
Certain neurological disorders can result in severe amyotrophia over time.
Physical therapy aims to slow the progression of amyotrophia in affected individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular atrophy - wasting away of muscle tissue
  • Antonym: hypertrophy - enlargement of an organ or tissue from the increase in size of its cells

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without), 'myo-' (muscle), and 'trophē' (nourishment).
