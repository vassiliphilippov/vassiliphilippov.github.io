to restrict

1. VERB

/ rɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to put a limit on something, or to control the amount, extent, or range of it
place a limit on; control amount or range

Examples

Due to safety concerns, the school decided to restrict access to the laboratory after hours.
Some diets restrict the number of calories you can consume each day.
The government may restrict the sale of certain medications to protect public health.

Synset Examples

restrict the use of this parking lot
limit the time you can spend with your friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - to set boundaries on something; similar meaning to 'restrict'
  • Antonym: permit - to allow something, opposite of restrict

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictus', past participle of 'restringere' (to bind fast).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00234091-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to limit the use, possession, or access to something, often by law or regulation
limit or control (by law or rule)

Examples

Access to the laboratory is restricted to authorized personnel.
The government decided to restrict the use of certain pesticides for safety reasons.
The park authority restricts vehicle entry during nesting season to protect wildlife.

Synset Examples

this substance is controlled
that area is restricted to security personnel only

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - to set a limit on; to restrict
  • Antonym: permit - to allow or authorize
  • Antonym: to prohibit - to formally forbid by law or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'restringere' meaning 'to hold back, bind fast'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02514936-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to limit the size, amount, or range of something; to place limits or controls on what someone can do or what can happen
limit or place controls on

Examples

The school has decided to restrict access to certain websites during class hours.
Doctors may restrict your diet if you have certain health conditions.
To improve safety, the company will restrict the number of visitors allowed each day.

Synset Examples

curtail drinking in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - to set a boundary or maximum
  • Antonym: permit - to allow, to give permission

Etymology

From Latin 'restringere', meaning 'to bind fast, check'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00237442-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ rɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to prevent information or something from being circulated, shared, or disclosed to others
prevent (information) from being shared

Examples

The company decided to restrict access to sensitive financial information.
Government regulations restrict the release of personal data to protect individual privacy.
During the investigation, the police restricted the circulation of details about the case.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to allow - to permit information to be shared or disclosed
  • Synonym: limit - to confine within boundaries; to restrict in general sense

Etymology

From Latin 'restringere', meaning to bind or draw back.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02455890-v
Synset POS: v
