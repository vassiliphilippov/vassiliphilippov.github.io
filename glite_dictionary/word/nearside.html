nearside

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪrˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɪə.saɪd / (BrE)
the side of a vehicle that is closest to the kerb, especially in countries where vehicles drive on the left
side of a vehicle nearest the kerb
british

Examples

The taxi stopped on the nearside of the street to let passengers out.
Check your nearside mirror before turning left at the junction.
The damage was mostly to the nearside door of the car.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offside - the side of a vehicle farthest from the kerb

Etymology

Formed from 'near' + 'side'; in British English, refers to the passenger side.
