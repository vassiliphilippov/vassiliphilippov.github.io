monogyny

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑdʒəni / (AmE), / məˈnɒdʒɪni / (BrE)
the practice or state of having only one wife at a time
having only one wife at a time

Examples

Some traditional societies practice monogyny, while others allow polygynous marriages.
Monogyny is common among many bird species, where a male pairs with only one female.
The legal system in that country enforces monogyny and prohibits multiple wives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monogamy - the practice of being married to one person at a time
  • Antonym: polygyny - the practice of having more than one wife at a time
  • Antonym: polyandry - the practice of having more than one husband at a time

Etymology

From Greek monos (single, alone) + gynē (woman, wife).
