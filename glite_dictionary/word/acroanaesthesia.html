acroanaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəʊˌænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˌanɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
Loss of sensation or numbness in the extremities, such as the hands or feet, usually due to nerve dysfunction.
loss of sensation in extremities
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from acroanaesthesia in both hands after exposure to cold temperatures.
Doctors diagnosed acroanaesthesia when the patient reported numbness in her toes and fingers.
Acroanaesthesia can be a symptom of certain peripheral nerve disorders.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (extremity) + 'anaesthesia' (loss of sensation).
