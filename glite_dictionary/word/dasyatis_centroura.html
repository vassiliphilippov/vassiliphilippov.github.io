Dasyatis centroura

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsiˈeɪtɪs sɛnˈtrʊərə / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈaɪtɪs sɛnˈtrʊərə / (BrE)
A large species of stingray found in the western Atlantic Ocean, especially from Cape Cod to Cape Hatteras.
a large Atlantic stingray species

Examples

The Dasyatis centroura can grow to impressive sizes, making it one of the most notable rays along the Atlantic coast.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of Dasyatis centroura to better understand its habitat needs.
Fishermen occasionally encounter Dasyatis centroura while working off the shores of the Carolinas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingray - A type of cartilaginous fish with a flattened body and long, slender tail, often with a venomous spine.

Etymology

From the genus name Dasyatis (Greek dasys, 'hairy' + atis, possibly 'ray') and the species name centroura (Greek kentron, 'sting' + oura, 'tail').
