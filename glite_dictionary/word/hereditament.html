hereditament

1. NOUN

/ həˌrɛdəˈteɪmənt / (AmE), / hɛˌrɛdɪˈteɪmənt / (BrE)
any property, whether real, personal, or mixed, that can be inherited by law.
property that can be inherited
legal

Examples

The deceased's estate included several hereditaments passed down to his heirs.
Only hereditaments mentioned in the will are subject to inheritance tax.
Land and buildings are considered real hereditaments under property law.

Etymology

From Latin hereditamentum, from hereditare (to inherit).
