allelomorph

1. NOUN

/ əˈliːləˌmɔɹf / (AmE), / əˈliːləmɔːf / (BrE)
(Genetics) an alternative form of a gene that can occupy the same position (locus) on a chromosome, influencing the same trait; now usually called an allele.
alternative form of a gene (allele)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new allelomorph responsible for flower color variation.
The concept of the allelomorph is crucial to understanding Mendelian genetics.
Although 'allele' is commonly used today, textbooks from the early 20th century often use allelomorph.

Synset Examples

some alleles are dominant over others

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allele - alternative form of a gene at a specific locus; preferred modern term

Etymology

From Greek allēlo- (of each other, reciprocal) + -morph (form).
