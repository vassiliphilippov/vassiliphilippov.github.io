Ipomoea pes-caprae

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ˌpɛs.kəˈpreɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ɪə ˌpɛs.kæˈpraɪ / (BrE)
a sprawling perennial plant of coastal sand dunes, native from Florida to Texas, commonly known as beach morning glory or goat's foot.
creeping coastal plant; beach morning glory

Examples

The leaves of Ipomoea pes-caprae are shaped like goat’s feet and help stabilize sandy shores.
Conservationists planted Ipomoea pes-caprae along the dunes to prevent erosion.
Ipomoea pes-caprae is often seen blooming with purple flowers on coastal beaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beach morning glory - common name for Ipomoea pes-caprae

Etymology

From Latin: Ipomoea (genus name), Latin 'pes-caprae' meaning 'goat's foot' (referring to the leaf shape).
