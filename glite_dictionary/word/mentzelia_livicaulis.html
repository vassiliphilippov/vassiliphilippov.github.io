Mentzelia livicaulis

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtsɛliə ˌlaɪvɪˈkɔlɪs / (AmE), / mɛnˈtsɛliə ˌlɪvɪˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A biennial plant native to the southwestern United States, with white stems, toothed leaves, and large pale yellow flowers that open in the early morning.
biennial plant of the southwestern U.S.

Examples

The Mentzelia livicaulis displays its striking yellow flowers at sunrise in the desert.
Gardeners cherish Mentzelia livicaulis for its resilience and decorative value in dry landscapes.
Researchers have noted the unique petal structure in Mentzelia livicaulis compared to related species.

Etymology

From the genus name Mentzelia, named after German botanist Christian Mentzel, and the species epithet livicaulis from Latin roots meaning 'smooth stem'.
