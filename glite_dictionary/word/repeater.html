repeater

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpitər / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a firearm capable of firing multiple rounds in succession without needing to be reloaded after each shot
firearm that fires several rounds without reloading

Examples

The old rifle collection included a classic repeater from the nineteenth century.
Hunters often prefer using a repeater because it allows for quick follow-up shots.
The introduction of the repeater revolutionized firearm technology in the 1800s.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpiːtər / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a person who is repeatedly arrested for criminal behavior, especially for committing the same offense multiple times
person repeatedly committing the same crime

Examples

The new law aims to impose harsher penalties on repeaters to reduce crime.
As a known repeater, he was under close surveillance by the police.
The judge showed little leniency for repeaters who committed the same offense again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recidivist - a person who relapses into criminal behavior

Etymology

From 'repeat' + '-er', meaning one who repeats an action.

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈpitər / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
an electronic device that receives a signal and retransmits it at a higher power, used to extend the range of communication systems
device that retransmits and amplifies signals

Examples

The radio station installed a repeater to improve coverage in remote areas.
A repeater is essential for extending Wi-Fi reach in large buildings.
The technician checked the repeater to ensure the signal was being transmitted correctly.

Synset Examples

repeaters can be used in computer networks to extend cabling distances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplifier - an electronic device for increasing the strength of signals

Etymology

From the verb 'repeat' + '-er', meaning something that repeats.
