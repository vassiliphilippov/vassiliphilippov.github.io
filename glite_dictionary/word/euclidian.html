Euclidian

1. ADJ

/ juːˈklɪdiən / (AmE), / juːˈklɪdɪən / (BrE)
Relating to the geometry based on the axioms and principles set out by Euclid, especially as distinct from non-Euclidean geometries.
relating to Euclid's geometry

Examples

The rules of Euclidian geometry do not apply on the surface of a sphere.
Many classical architectures are based on Euclidian principles of space and proportion.
In physics, spacetime is often modeled as a Euclidian space for simplicity.

Synset Examples

Euclidian geometry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-Euclidean - relating to geometries that are not based on Euclid's postulates

Etymology

From Euclid, the ancient Greek mathematician, with the suffix '-ian' denoting relation or pertaining to.
