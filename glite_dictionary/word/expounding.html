expounding

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
a detailed explanation or systematic interpretation of a particular subject, often in written form
detailed explanation or interpretation

Examples

Her expounding of the theory clarified many complex ideas for the students.
The professor's expounding on the subject lasted nearly an hour.
He published an expounding of classical philosophy that influenced many later scholars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining the meaning of something

Etymology

From the verb 'expound', which comes from Latin 'expoundere' (to explain, set forth).
