ladle

1. VERB

/ ˈleɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdəl / (BrE)
to serve or remove a liquid or other substance using a ladle or in a similar manner
serve or remove with a ladle

Examples

She carefully ladled the soup into each bowl.
The volunteer ladled hot stew to the waiting guests.
He ladled water from the well to fill the bucket.

Synset Examples

ladle the water out of the bowl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - to pick up and move something with a scoop

Etymology

From Middle English 'ladel', from Old English 'hlædel' (ladle), from 'hladan' (to load).

2. VERB

/ ˈleɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdəl / (BrE)
to serve or transfer (a liquid or soft food) using a ladle or similar deep spoon
serve/transfer liquid with a ladle

Examples

She carefully ladled the hot stew into each guest's bowl.
He ladled sauce over the pasta before serving it at the table.
The chef ladled soup into cups for the waiting customers.

Synset Examples

ladle soup into the bowl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - to lift or take up with a scoop
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01581842-v

3. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdəl / (BrE)
a large, deep spoon with a long handle, used for serving liquids such as soup or stew
deep spoon with a long handle for serving liquids

Examples

She used a ladle to serve the hot soup into the bowls.
The chef dipped the ladle into the pot of stew to check the thickness.
A silver ladle was placed next to the punch bowl at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoon - a utensil consisting of a small shallow bowl and a handle, used for stirring, serving, and eating food

Etymology

From Old English hladan (to load) via Middle English ladel; related to 'load'.
