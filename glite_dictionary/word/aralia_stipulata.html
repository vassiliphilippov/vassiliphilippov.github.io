Aralia stipulata

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪ.li.ə ˌstɪ.pjəˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.lɪ.ə ˌstɪ.pjʊˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
A species of aralia native to China, similar to the American angelica tree but less prickly.
Chinese aralia species, less prickly than angelica tree

Examples

The Aralia stipulata grows well in shaded gardens and requires little maintenance.
Compared to its American relative, Aralia stipulata has fewer and softer prickles on its stems.
Botanists discovered several specimens of Aralia stipulata thriving in eastern China.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Aralia spinosa - American angelica tree, a similar but more prickly species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus Aralia and Latin stipulata (‘with stipules’).
