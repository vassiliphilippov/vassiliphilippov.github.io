hypovolaemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpoʊvəˈliːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊvəˈliːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by a decreased volume of circulating blood in the body.
relating to decreased blood volume
medical

Examples

The patient became hypovolaemic after losing a significant amount of blood during surgery.
Rapid fluid replacement is required in cases of hypovolaemic shock.
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of hypovolaemic complications following the accident.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypervolaemic - relating to an increased blood volume
  • Synonym: hypovolemia - a condition of decreased blood volume

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under, less) and volume, with the suffix -aemic (relating to blood).
