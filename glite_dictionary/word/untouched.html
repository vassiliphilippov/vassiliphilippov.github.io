untouched

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (BrE)
Not affected, changed, or used; not having come in contact with something.
not affected or changed; not in contact

Examples

The top of the mountain remained untouched by climbers for decades.
Please leave the evidence untouched until the police arrive.
After the storm, the garden appeared strangely untouched by the wind and rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled
  • Synonym: unspoiled - not changed or damaged; still pure
  • Antonym: affected - influenced or changed by something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02454622-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (BrE)
not used, drunk, or consumed; left in its original state or still full.
not used or still full

Examples

She left her untouched glass of wine on the table all night.
The plate of food remained untouched during the entire meeting.
After hours, the celebratory cake was still untouched in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

an untouched cocktail in her hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled
  • Antonym: used - having already been consumed or handled

Etymology

From un- (not) + touched, past participle of touch.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01090028-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (BrE)
not affected or influenced emotionally; not moved or disturbed in feeling.
not emotionally affected or moved

Examples

Despite the tragic news, she remained untouched by emotion and continued working.
His calm expression showed he was untouched by the harsh criticism.
Many people were moved by the movie, but I was surprisingly untouched.

Synset Examples

always appeared completely unmoved and imperturbable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmoved - not affected by emotion; not influenced or upset
  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy
  • Antonym: touched - emotionally affected or moved

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (BrE)
not influenced, affected, or changed by something; remaining in its original state
not influenced or changed

Examples

The remote village remained untouched by modern technology until the late 1990s.
Her opinion was untouched by the criticism she received from colleagues.
Much of the ancient forest is still untouched by human activity.

Synset Examples

stewed in its petty provincialism untouched by the brisk debates that stirred the old world
unswayed by personal considerations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaffected - not influenced or changed by something
  • Antonym: altered - changed from its original state

Etymology

from un- (not) + touched (from Old French toucher, from Vulgar Latin *toccare, to strike)
