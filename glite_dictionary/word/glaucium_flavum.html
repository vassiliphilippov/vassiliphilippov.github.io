Glaucium flavum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡloʊsiəm ˈfleɪvəm / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːsiəm ˈfleɪvəm / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered Eurasian plant with glaucous leaves, commonly found along sandy shores and naturalized in parts of eastern North America
yellow-flowered Eurasian coastal plant

Examples

The Glaucium flavum is easily recognizable by its distinctive yellow flowers.
Researchers found Glaucium flavum thriving along the sandy banks of the coast.
Gardeners value Glaucium flavum for its ornamental appearance in seaside gardens.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Glaucium, species flavum meaning 'yellow'.
