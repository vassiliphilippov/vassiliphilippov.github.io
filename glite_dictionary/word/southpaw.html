southpaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊθˌpɔ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθˌpɔː / (BrE)
a person who is left-handed, especially in sports such as baseball or boxing
a left-handed person (esp. in sports)

Examples

The team's new southpaw dominated the game with his fast pitches.
As a southpaw, she had an advantage against her right-handed opponent.
Many boxers struggle when facing a skilled southpaw.

Synset Examples

their pitcher was a southpaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left-hander - a person who uses the left hand more skillfully
  • Antonym: right-hander - a person who uses the right hand more skillfully

Etymology

Originated in American English, possibly from the orientation of baseball fields in the 19th century, with left-handed pitchers facing south.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊθˌpɔ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊθˌpɔː / (BrE)
A baseball pitcher who throws with the left hand; more generally, a left-handed person, especially in sports contexts.
a left-handed baseball pitcher

Examples

The team signed a veteran southpaw to strengthen their pitching lineup.
Being a southpaw often gives pitchers an advantage against right-handed batters.
The young southpaw delivered a fastball that stunned the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left-hander - a person who uses the left hand more skillfully than the right
  • Antonym: right-hander - a person who uses the right hand more skillfully than the left

Etymology

Originated in 19th-century American baseball, possibly from the fact that left-handed pitchers faced south in some ballparks.
