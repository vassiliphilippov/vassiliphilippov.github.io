Davallia mariesii

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvæliə ˌmɑːriˈesi.aɪ / (AmE), / dəˈvæliə ˌmær.iˈiː.saɪ / (BrE)
A species of feathery fern native to tropical Asia and Malaysia, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
species of feathery tropical Asian fern

Examples

The delicate fronds of Davallia mariesii make it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
Botanists often study Davallia mariesii to understand fern adaptation in tropical climates.
She added a pot of Davallia mariesii to her greenhouse for its unique, feathery foliage.

Etymology

Genus name from French botanist Edmond Davall; species named after Charles Maries, English plant collector.
