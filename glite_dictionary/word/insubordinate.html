insubordinate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsəˈbɔrdnət / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈbɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
refusing to obey orders or show respect for someone in authority; disobedient
refusing to obey authority

Examples

The soldier was punished for his insubordinate behavior during training.
Her insubordinate attitude quickly got her into trouble with management.
Teachers considered him insubordinate because he routinely disregarded the school's rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedient - willing to do what one is told by someone in authority
  • Synonym: defiant - openly resisting or challenging authority

Etymology

From Latin 'insubordinatus', meaning 'not subject to order'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsəˈbɔrdnət / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈbɔːdnət / (BrE)
refusing to obey orders or show respect to someone in authority
not obeying authority

Examples

The manager warned the insubordinate employee about his repeated refusal to follow instructions.
Her insubordinate attitude eventually led to disciplinary action at work.
Teachers struggled to manage the insubordinate students during the assembly.

Synset Examples

a history of insubordinate behavior
insubordinate boys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disobedient - not obeying rules or authority
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to do what one is told by those in authority

Etymology

From Latin 'subordinare' (to subordinate) with prefix 'in-' meaning 'not'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02338328-a
Synset POS: a
