Salvelinus alpinus

1. NOUN

/ sælˈviːlɪnəs ælˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / salˈviːlɪnəs alˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
A species of small trout found in northern waters, known as Arctic char, with landlocked populations in Quebec and northern New England.
species of trout; Arctic char

Examples

The Salvelinus alpinus thrives in cold, freshwater lakes across the Arctic region.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Salvelinus alpinus in Northern Canada.
Fisheries in Quebec are known for their abundant Salvelinus alpinus populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic char - A common name for Salvelinus alpinus, a species of trout found in Arctic and subarctic waters.

Etymology

From Latin: Salvelinus (a genus of char), alpinus ('of the Alps').
