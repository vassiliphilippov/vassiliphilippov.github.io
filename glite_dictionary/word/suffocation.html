suffocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃn / (BrE)
the act of killing someone by depriving them of oxygen; death or serious harm caused by preventing normal breathing
killing by depriving of oxygen

Examples

The cause of death was determined to be suffocation due to lack of air.
Accidental suffocation can occur if a baby sleeps face-down on a soft surface.
He narrowly escaped suffocation when smoke filled the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphyxiation - death or unconsciousness caused by lack of oxygen

Etymology

From Latin suffocatio (a choking), from suffocare (to choke, stifle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of being deprived of oxygen, causing difficulty in breathing and potentially leading to unconsciousness or death
deprivation of oxygen; difficulty breathing

Examples

Several deaths occurred due to suffocation after the fire filled the room with smoke.
The feeling of suffocation overwhelmed him in the crowded, windowless elevator.
Prompt treatment prevented suffocation when the child accidentally inhaled a small toy.

Synset Examples

asphyxiation is sometimes used as a form of torture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphyxiation - the process of being deprived of oxygen, leading to unconsciousness or death

Etymology

From Latin suffocatio, from suffocare (to choke, to stifle).
