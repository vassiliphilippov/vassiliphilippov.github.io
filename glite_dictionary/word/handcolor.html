to handcolor

1. VERB

/ ˈhændˌkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌkʌlə / (BrE)
to add color to (a photograph, drawing, or similar object) by hand rather than by mechanical or digital means
add color to something by hand

Examples

My grandmother used to handcolor black-and-white photos to make them more lifelike.
Some artists prefer to handcolor their prints for a unique touch.
The gallery features several handcolored vintage postcards from the early 1900s.

Synset Examples

Some old photographs are handcolored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - to give something color, especially with paint, dye, etc.
  • Synonym: paint - to cover a surface with paint or color by applying it by hand

Etymology

Formed from 'hand' and 'color' to indicate coloring done manually.
