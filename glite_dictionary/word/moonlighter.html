moonlighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌlaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who has a second job, usually at night or in addition to regular employment.
person with a second job

Examples

Many teachers act as moonlighters to supplement their income during the summer months.
He’s a moonlighter who works the night shift at a convenience store after his day job.
The company discovered several moonlighters among their staff during an audit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonlight - to work at a second job, often at night, in addition to regular employment

Etymology

From the verb 'moonlight' (to work a second job at night), from 'moon' + 'light.'
