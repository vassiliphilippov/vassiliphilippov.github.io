Coccidia

1. NOUN

/ kɑkˈsɪdiə / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɪdiə / (BrE)
A subclass of microscopic, spore-forming, single-celled parasites that live inside the cells of animals, belonging to the apicomplexan class Conoidasida.
parasitic protozoan subclass
medical

Examples

Many species in the Coccidia subclass infect the intestines of birds and mammals.
Veterinarians frequently encounter Coccidia when diagnosing young animals with diarrhea.
The life cycle of Coccidia involves both asexual and sexual reproduction within the host.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek kokkos (grain, berry) referring to its shape.
