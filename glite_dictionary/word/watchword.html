watchword

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːtʃ.wɝːd / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.wɜːd / (BrE)
a word or phrase used as a slogan to express a guiding principle or rally support for a cause
slogan or rallying phrase for a cause

Examples

The campaign's watchword was 'equality for all,' inspiring many supporters to join.
‘Progress’ became the watchword for the new government initiative.
Throughout the movement, freedom remained their constant watchword.

Synset Examples

a cry to arms
our watchword will be ‘democracy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a memorable phrase used in advertising or promotion

Etymology

From Middle English 'wacche word' (watch word), originally a password for guards.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07167460-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃ.wɝd / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.wɜːd / (BrE)
a secret word or phrase used to gain admission or indicate membership in a group; a password or code word
secret password or code word

Examples

The guard demanded the watchword before letting anyone enter the building.
Each night, a new watchword was distributed to the soldiers for security.
Without the correct watchword, the door would remain locked.

Synset Examples

he forgot the password

Related Terms

  • Synonym: password - a secret word or phrase used to gain access to something

Etymology

From Middle English wacche word, originally meaning a word given to a sentry to allow passage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06686933-n
Synset POS: n
