conquistador

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkwɪstəˌdɔr / (AmE), / kɒnˈkwɪstədɔː / (BrE)
a Spanish conqueror, especially one of the explorers who conquered Mexico and Peru in the 16th century
Spanish conqueror in the 16th century

Examples

Many conquistadors sought fame and riches in the New World.
Hernán Cortés was a famous conquistador who conquered the Aztec Empire.
The legacy of the conquistadors is still debated among historians today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explorer - a person who investigates unknown regions
  • Synonym: invader - a person who enters a place by force in order to conquer it

Etymology

From Spanish 'conquistador', from 'conquistar' (to conquer), from Latin 'conquaerere' (to seek, procure).
