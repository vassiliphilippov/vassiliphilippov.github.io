Liguria

1. PROPN

/ lɪˈɡjʊəriə / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjʊəriə / (BrE)
a region of northwestern Italy bordering the Ligurian Sea, known for its picturesque coastline and the city of Genoa
region of northwestern Italy

Examples

Many tourists visit Liguria for its stunning coastal villages and cuisine.
The city of Genoa, located in Liguria, is an important Italian port.
Hiking along the trails of Liguria offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean.

Etymology

From Latin Liguria, from Ligures, the ancient people of the region.
