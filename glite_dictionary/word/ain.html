ain

1. ADJ

/ eɪn / (AmE), / eɪn / (BrE)
(chiefly Scottish) own; belonging to or associated with oneself or the specified person, especially used after a possessive.
Scottish form of 'own'
british

Examples

He did it with his ain hands despite the harsh winter.
That's ma ain house at the end o' the lane.
She preferred to use her ain recipe for the shortbread.

Synset Examples

for your own use
do your own thing
she makes her own clothes
‘ain’ is Scottish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: own - belonging to oneself or itself

Etymology

Scots form of 'own', from Old English 'āgen' (own, possessed).
