beakless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːkləs / (AmE), / ˈbiːkləs / (BrE)
having no beak or bill.
lacking a beak or bill

Examples

The fossil revealed a beakless ancestor of today's birds.
Some species of salamanders are beakless, unlike their frog relatives.
The puppet's beakless face made it look unusually friendly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beaked - having a beak

Etymology

From 'beak' + '-less', an Old English suffix meaning 'without'.
