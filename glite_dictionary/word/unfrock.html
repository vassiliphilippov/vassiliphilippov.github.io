to unfrock

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈfrɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈfrɒk / (BrE)
to remove a church official from their position or deprive them of clerical status or authority
remove clerical office or authority

Examples

The bishop was unfrocked after allegations of misconduct came to light.
It is rare for the church to unfrock a priest, but exceptions do occur.
A formal decision to unfrock him was announced after a lengthy investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defrock - to remove a member of the clergy from their office

Etymology

From un- (reversing) + frock (a clerical gown or habit).
