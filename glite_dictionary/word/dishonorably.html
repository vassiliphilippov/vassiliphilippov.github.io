dishonorably

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈɑːnərəbli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks honor or integrity; in an unethical or shameful way.
in a disreputable or unethical manner

Examples

He was criticized for behaving dishonorably during the negotiations.
The official was removed from office after acting dishonorably toward his colleagues.
Many believe that betraying a friend is to act dishonorably.

Synset Examples

he acted dishonorably

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honorably - in a way that shows honor and integrity

Etymology

Formed from dishonorable + -ly.

2. ADV

/ dɪsˈɑnərəbli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that brings shame or disgrace; without honor.
in a shameful or disgraceful manner

Examples

He was dishonorably discharged from the army after the investigation.
The company dishonorably avoided paying its debts, ruining its reputation.
She felt dishonorably treated by her former colleagues after the incident.

Synset Examples

he was dishonorably discharged

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honorably - in a way that brings honor or credit

Etymology

From 'dishonorable' + '-ly', based on Latin 'honor' (honour).

3. ADV

/ dɪsˈɑnərəbli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbli / (BrE)
In a manner lacking honor or integrity; in a way that brings shame or disgrace.
in a dishonorable manner

Examples

He was discharged dishonorably from the army for misconduct.
The company treated its former employees dishonorably during the restructuring process.
They acted dishonorably to secure the contract, ignoring ethical standards.

Synset Examples

his grades were disgracefully low

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honorably - in a manner showing honor or integrity

Etymology

From 'dishonorable' + '-ly'. 'Dishonorable' from Old French deshonorer, from Latin dishonor (loss of honor).
