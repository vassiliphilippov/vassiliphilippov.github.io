Erigeron speciosus

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɪdʒəˌrɒn spɪˈsioʊsəs / (AmE), / ɛˈrɪdʒərɒn spɪˈsiːəsəs / (BrE)
A plant of the daisy family native to the northwestern United States, having branching stems with showy solitary flower heads bearing many narrow pink, lavender, or white rays.
showy North American daisy-like plant

Examples

The wild Erigeron speciosus brightened the mountain slope with its purple blooms.
Gardeners admire Erigeron speciosus for its long-lasting, vivid flower heads.
In late spring, Erigeron speciosus attracts a variety of bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy fleabane - common name for Erigeron speciosus, a daisy-like flower

Etymology

From Latin *Erigeron* (a genus of flowering plants) and Latin *speciosus* (showy, beautiful).
