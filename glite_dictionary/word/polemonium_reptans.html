Polemonium reptans

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌliːmoʊˈniːəm ˈrɛptænz / (AmE), / pəʊˌliːməˈniːəm ˈrɛptænz / (BrE)
A perennial herb of the eastern United States, with clusters of blue flowers, also known as Jacob's ladder.
a North American perennial herb (Jacob's ladder)

Examples

The woodland garden features Polemonium reptans blooming in early spring among the ferns.
Hikers often spot Polemonium reptans along shaded forest trails in its native range.
Botanists study Polemonium reptans because of its attractive, ladder-like leaves and delicate blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacob's ladder - common name for Polemonium reptans, a perennial herb with blue flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Polemonium' from Greek polemōnion (a kind of plant) and 'reptans' from Latin meaning 'creeping'.
