William Ewart Gladstone

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ˈjuːərt ˈɡlædˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈjuːət ˈɡlæd.stən / (BrE)
(1809–1898) a liberal British statesman who served as Prime Minister four times and was a prominent political leader in 19th-century Britain.
British Prime Minister (1809–1898)
british

Examples

William Ewart Gladstone is remembered for his significant reforms in British politics and society.
The leadership of William Ewart Gladstone shaped much of Victorian-era government policy.
Historians often compare the policies of William Ewart Gladstone and his rival Benjamin Disraeli.
