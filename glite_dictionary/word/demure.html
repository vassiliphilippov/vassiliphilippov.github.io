demure

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmjʊr / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊə / (BrE)
(especially of a woman or her behavior) affectedly modest or shy, often in a playful or provocative way.
affectedly modest or shy

Examples

She gave him a demure smile that hinted at mischief.
Wearing a demure dress, she flirted quietly from across the room.
His comments made her laugh, but she quickly resumed a demure expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modest - having or showing a moderate or humble estimate of one's merits
  • Antonym: bold - showing willingness to take risks; confident and courageous

Etymology

From Old French *demoure* (quiet, reserved), from Latin *demorari* (to linger).
