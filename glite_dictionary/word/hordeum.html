Hordeum

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrdiəm / (AmE), / ˈhɔːdɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of grasses in the family Poaceae, including barley and related species, found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and South America
genus of grasses including barley

Examples

Hordeum species are cultivated in many regions for their economic value.
Barley, one of the most common cereals, belongs to the genus Hordeum.
Some wild Hordeum varieties are used in agricultural research for developing new crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a cereal plant of the species Hordeum vulgare, cultivated for food and brewing

Etymology

From Latin hordeum (barley), referring to the genus name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔr.di.əm / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.di.əm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses including barley, found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and South America.
genus of grasses; includes barley

Examples

Several species in the genus Hordeum are cultivated as crops for food and animal feed.
Scientists discovered a wild variety of Hordeum in South America with resistance to drought.
The agricultural study focused on the genetic diversity within Hordeum populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a widely cultivated cereal grass; the main species in the Hordeum genus

Etymology

From Latin *hordeum* (barley).
