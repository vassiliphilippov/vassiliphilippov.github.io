fado

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A style of Portuguese folk music characterized by mournful tunes and lyrics, often about longing, love, or loss.
Portuguese folk music genre

Examples

The singer performed a haunting fado that moved the audience to tears.
Lisbon is famous for its traditional fado houses where musicians sing about love and sorrow.
She grew up listening to her grandmother's collection of classic fado recordings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk song - a song that originates in traditional popular culture

Etymology

From Portuguese 'fado', meaning 'fate', from Latin 'fatum'.
