winceyette

1. NOUN

/ wɪnˈsiɛt / (AmE), / wɪnˈsɪət / (BrE)
A soft cotton fabric with a nap on both sides, resembling flannelette and used especially for making nightwear and bed linen.
soft cotton fabric with nap on both sides

Examples

She bought a pair of winceyette pajamas to stay warm during the winter.
The children's beds were covered with winceyette sheets for extra comfort.
Many prefer winceyette for its softness and warmth in nightclothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flannelette - a lightweight, soft, woven cotton fabric with a brushed surface

Etymology

From 'wincey' (a type of fabric) + the diminutive suffix '-ette'.
