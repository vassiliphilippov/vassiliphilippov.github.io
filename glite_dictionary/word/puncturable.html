puncturable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌŋktʃərəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktʃərəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be punctured or pierced by a sharp object
capable of being punctured

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the membrane was puncturable during the procedure.
Because the packaging is puncturable, care is needed when handling sharp objects nearby.
Some materials are specifically designed to be non-puncturable for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impermeable - not allowing fluid to pass through; not penetrable
  • Synonym: pierceable - capable of being pierced

Etymology

From Latin 'punctura' (a prick) + '-able' (able to be).
