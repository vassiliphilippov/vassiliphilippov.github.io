Otididae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈtɪdəˌdiː / (AmE), / əʊˈtɪdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The scientific family of large terrestrial birds known as bustards, found in the Old World.
family of bustard birds

Examples

The family Otididae includes some of the largest flying birds in the world.
Most species in the Otididae family inhabit grasslands and open plains across Africa and Eurasia.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of several Otididae species in southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large terrestrial bird of the family Otididae

Etymology

From New Latin Otididae, from Otis (a genus of bustards) + -idae (denoting a zoological family).
