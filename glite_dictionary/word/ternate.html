ternate

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜːr.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nət / (BrE)
(of a leaf or plant part) divided into or arranged in groups of three.
divided or grouped in threes (esp. leaves)

Examples

The clover plant is easily recognized by its ternate leaves.
Many woodland flowers display ternate leaf arrangements along their stems.
The botanist noted that the leaf was ternate, not simple or pinnate.

Etymology

From Latin 'ternātus', meaning 'three each', from 'terni' (three each).
