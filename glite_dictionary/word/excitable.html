excitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or cell) capable of responding readily to stimuli; easily excited.
easily stimulated or excited

Examples

The laboratory mice are highly excitable when exposed to loud noises.
Some nerve cells are more excitable than others due to their membrane properties.
Children can become excitable after eating too much sugar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not easily excited or aroused

Etymology

From Latin 'excitare' (to arouse), plus English suffix '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtəbl / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtəbl / (BrE)
easily excited; quick to respond with strong emotion or enthusiasm.
easily excited

Examples

The puppy was so excitable that it jumped up at every sound.
Some children are naturally more excitable than others in busy environments.
His excitable nature makes him a lively companion at parties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not easily excited or disturbed
  • Antonym: unflappable - not easily upset or excited

Etymology

From Latin excitabilis (capable of being excited), from excitare (to excite).
