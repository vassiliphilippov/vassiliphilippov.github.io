Erica vagans

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪkə veɪˈɡænz / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪkə vəˈɡænz / (BrE)
a bushy evergreen shrub native to Cornwall and southwestern Europe, with pink to white flowers, also grown as an ornamental plant
a species of heath with pink or white flowers

Examples

Erica vagans is often used in landscaping for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
The moors of Cornwall are covered in wild Erica vagans during the summer months.
Gardeners prize Erica vagans for its resistance to poor soil conditions.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Erica' meaning 'heath' and 'vagans' meaning 'wandering'.
