dioxide

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical compound in which each molecule consists of two atoms of oxygen combined with another element.
compound with two oxygen atoms

Examples

Carbon dioxide is one of the main greenhouse gases responsible for climate change.
Sulfur dioxide emissions can cause acid rain and environmental damage.
The experiment involved measuring the amount of nitrogen dioxide in urban air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxide - a compound of oxygen with another element

Etymology

From di- (two) + oxide, indicating two oxygen atoms.
