to fixate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪk.seɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.seɪt / (BrE)
to make something fixed, stable, or stationary; to fasten or secure in place
make fixed or stable

Examples

The technician carefully fixated the microscope slide onto the stage to prevent movement.
She used glue to fixate the decorative trim to the vase.
The surgeon must fixate the bone fragments to ensure proper healing.

Synset Examples

let's fix the picture to the frame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fix - to fasten or attach firmly

Etymology

From Latin 'fixare' (to fix), from 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00487934-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪkˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.seɪt / (BrE)
to become obsessively or excessively attached to a person or thing, often in a neurotic way
become obsessively attached

Examples

She tends to fixate on her mistakes rather than her achievements.
After the breakup, he seemed to fixate on his ex-girlfriend's social media.
It is unhealthy to fixate on minor problems and ignore the bigger picture.

Synset Examples

He fixates on his mother, even at the age of 40

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obsess - to be preoccupied with or constantly think about something
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate oneself from emotional connection

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02544031-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfɪk.seɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.seɪt / (BrE)
to become intensely focused on something or someone; to direct one's attention or emotions exclusively toward a particular object.
focus intensely on something

Examples

She tends to fixate on minor details when she's nervous.
It's unhealthy to fixate on your mistakes instead of learning from them.
He fixated on the ticking clock, unable to concentrate on anything else.

Synset Examples

Her eyes fixated on a point on the horizon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obsess - to preoccupy the mind excessively

Etymology

From Latin 'fixare' (to fix), from 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00487639-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈfɪkˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪkseɪt / (BrE)
to focus one's attention on something or someone exclusively and obsessively
focus obsessively on something

Examples

The press tends to fixate on the private lives of celebrities.
Try not to fixate on your mistakes and focus on your progress instead.
He fixated on the smallest details, missing the bigger picture.

Synset Examples

The media are fixating on Princess Diana's death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obsess - to preoccupy the mind excessively with something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fix), via French 'fixer' (to fix).
