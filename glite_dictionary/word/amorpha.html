Amorpha

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɔr.fə / (AmE), / əˈmɔː.fə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), characterized by odd-pinnate leaves and dense spikes of purplish flowers.
genus of plants with purplish flowers

Examples

Several species of Amorpha are prized for their attractive purple flower spikes in gardens.
The botanist documented a new variety of Amorpha growing along the riverbank.
Many North American prairies are home to native Amorpha plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'amorphos' (shapeless, without form), referring to the irregular flowers.
