selsyn

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.sɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.sɪn / (BrE)
A system composed of a generator and a motor interconnected so that the motor replicates the generator's position; used to transmit angular position signals by electrical means.
synchronous signal transmission system

Examples

The flight simulator uses a selsyn to synchronize the instrument panel with the control inputs.
Engineers replaced the old selsyn in the radar tracking system for more accurate positioning.
The technician explained how the selsyn ensures both devices move in perfect unison.

Etymology

Portmanteau of 'self-synchronizing'.
