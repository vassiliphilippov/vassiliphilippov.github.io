scornful

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɔːrnfəl / (AmE), / ˈskɔːnf(ə)l / (BrE)
showing or expressing strong contempt or disdain for someone or something
showing contempt or disdain

Examples

She gave him a scornful look as he tried to explain himself.
The critic wrote a scornful review of the poorly produced play.
His tone was scornful when discussing the proposal, making his disapproval clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuous - showing contempt; scornful
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or esteem

Etymology

From 'scorn' + '-ful', Middle English, from Old French 'escarn'.
