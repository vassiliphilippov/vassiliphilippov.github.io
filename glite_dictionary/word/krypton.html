krypton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪp.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪp.tɒn / (BrE)
A colorless, odorless, inert gaseous element found in trace amounts in the Earth's atmosphere, used in lighting and photography.
a colorless inert gaseous element

Examples

The scientist filled the tube with krypton to produce a bright white light.
Krypton is one of the noble gases found in the periodic table.
Trace amounts of krypton are present in the Earth's atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xenon - a colorless, dense, inert gas in the noble gas group
  • Synonym: neon - a colorless, inert noble gas used in neon signs

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden), due to its discovery as a rare component in air.
