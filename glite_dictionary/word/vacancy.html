vacancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪkənsi / (AmE), / ˈveɪkənsi / (BrE)
A state of being unoccupied, available, or not filled, especially for positions, rooms, or roles.
state of being unoccupied or available

Examples

We have a vacancy for a software engineer at our company.
The hotel had no vacancy during the busy holiday season.
After the manager retired, a vacancy was created in the department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening - an available position or opportunity
  • Synonym: position - a job or role that is filled or vacant
  • Antonym: occupancy - the state of being occupied

Etymology

From Latin 'vacantia' (being empty), from 'vacans' (empty, unoccupied).

2. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.kən.si / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.kən.si / (BrE)
An empty area or space; a place that is unoccupied or devoid of contents.
an empty space or area

Examples

The explorers discovered a vast vacancy where the building once stood.
Peering through the telescope, she was amazed by the endless vacancy of space.
After the crowd dispersed, the stadium echoed with a strange vacancy.

Synset Examples

the huge desert voids
the emptiness of outer space
without their support he'll be ruling in a vacuum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - completely empty space
  • Synonym: gap - an opening or empty space in something
  • Antonym: fullness - the state of containing as much as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'vacantia' (emptiness), from 'vacans' (being empty).
