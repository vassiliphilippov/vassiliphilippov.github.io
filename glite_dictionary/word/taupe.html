taupe

1. ADJ

/ toʊp / (AmE), / təʊp / (BrE)
of a dusky brownish-gray color, often used to describe fabrics, paint, or other materials.
dusky brownish-gray color

Examples

She chose a taupe dress for the wedding because it complemented her skin tone.
The living room was painted in a soft taupe that felt both cozy and modern.
I prefer taupe curtains because the color goes with almost any decor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - a color between black and white
  • Degree Lower: beige - a pale sandy yellow-brown color
  • Synonym: brown - a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and blue, and containing no blue or green

Etymology

From French 'taupe' meaning 'mole', referring to the color of the animal's fur.

2. NOUN

/ toʊp / (AmE), / təʊp / (BrE)
a dark grayish-brown color, especially as used in fabrics and interior decoration
greyish-brown color

Examples

She painted her living room walls a soothing shade of taupe.
The designer recommended taupe curtains to complement the neutral tones in the room.
He wore a taupe sweater that matched his dress pants perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown color

Etymology

From French 'taupe' (mole), originally referring to the color of a mole's fur.
