Reims

1. PROPN

/ ʁɛ̃s / (AmE), / ʁɛ̃s / (BrE)
a city in northeastern France, known for its historical cathedral, coronation site of most French kings, and the signing of the German surrender in 1945
city in northeastern France; site of royal coronations and WWII surrender

Examples

Many tourists visit Reims to see its famous Gothic cathedral.
The German surrender at the end of World War II was signed in Reims.
Champagne lovers often travel to Reims to explore the region's renowned wineries.

Etymology

From French 'Reims', from Latin 'Durocortorum', later 'Remi', the tribe inhabiting the area.
