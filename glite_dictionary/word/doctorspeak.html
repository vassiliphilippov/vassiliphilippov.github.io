doctorspeak

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktər.spiːk / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.tə.spiːk / (BrE)
language used by doctors or medical professionals that is full of technical or specialized terms, often difficult for outsiders to understand.
medical jargon or technical language
informal

Examples

The patient's family found the doctorspeak confusing and asked for a simpler explanation.
Sometimes doctorspeak can make a diagnosis sound more serious than it actually is.
Medical students often need time to get used to all the doctorspeak in their textbooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a particular profession
  • Degree Higher: gobbledygook - language that is meaningless or hard to understand; often used of official or technical writing

Etymology

Formed by combining 'doctor' and 'speak', following a pattern found in English (e.g., 'technospeak').
