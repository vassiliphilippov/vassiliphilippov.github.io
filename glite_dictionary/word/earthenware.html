earthenware

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθənˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ(ə)nˌweə / (BrE)
Pottery made of porous clay that is fired at a relatively low temperature and usually finished with a glaze.
pottery made from fired porous clay

Examples

The museum displayed ancient earthenware bowls from various cultures.
She prefers drinking tea from earthenware mugs because they retain heat well.
The artisan specializes in creating handmade earthenware pots and vases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stoneware - type of pottery fired at a higher temperature than earthenware
  • Degree Higher: porcelain - fine, high-fired, white, translucent ceramic
  • Synonym: pottery - ceramic ware in general

Etymology

From 'earthen' (made of earth) + 'ware' (manufactured goods).
