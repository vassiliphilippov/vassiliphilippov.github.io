mynah

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
A tropical Asian bird of the starling family, known for its ability to mimic human speech.
tropical Asian starling bird

Examples

A mynah bird can often be taught to imitate human words.
We saw a flock of mynahs perched on the telephone wires.
The mynah in the pet shop greeted customers with a cheerful hello.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - small to medium-sized passerine birds in the family Sturnidae

Etymology

From Hindi mainā, from Sanskrit madanā (a kind of bird).
