Bumelia

1. NOUN

/ buˈmiːliə / (AmE), / bjuːˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of American deciduous or evergreen shrubs and small trees with very hard wood and milky latex, formerly classified in the family Sapotaceae.
genus of American shrubs or small trees

Examples

Bumelia species are often found in dry, sandy soils throughout the southern United States.
The hard wood of Bumelia has historically been used for tool handles and fence posts.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Bumelia for its attractive foliage and resistance to drought.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'boumelia' (wild olive), referring to the resemblance of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ buːˈmiːliə / (AmE), / bjuːˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs and small trees native to America, characterized by very hard wood and milky latex.
genus of American shrubs and small trees

Examples

The botanist discovered a new species within the genus Bumelia during his expedition in Texas.
Many species of Bumelia produce small, white flowers and tough, durable wood.
Due to its hard wood, Bumelia has been traditionally used for tool handles in certain regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *boumelia*, the name for a wild pear tree.
