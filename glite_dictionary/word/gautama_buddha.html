Gautama Buddha

1. PROPN

/ gaʊˈtɑːmə ˈbʊdə / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːtəmə ˈbʊdə / (BrE)
the founder of Buddhism, who lived during the 6th to 5th centuries BC and is regarded as the Enlightened One
founder of Buddhism

Examples

Many people around the world follow the teachings of Gautama Buddha.
Scholars study the life of Gautama Buddha to understand the origins of Buddhism.
A large statue of Gautama Buddha stands at the entrance to the temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddha - a title for those who have attained enlightenment in Buddhism

Etymology

From Sanskrit Gautama (family name) + Buddha (enlightened one).
