Copernicia

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌpɜrˈniːʃə / (AmE), / kəʊˌpɜːˈnɪʃiə / (BrE)
A genus of slow-growing tropical fan palms native to the Americas, especially known for their waxy leaves.
genus of tropical fan palms

Examples

The Copernicia palms are renowned for producing carnauba wax.
A botanist studied the unique leaf structure of several Copernicia species.
Many Copernicia palms thrive in the wetlands of South America.

Etymology

Named after the astronomer Nicolaus Copernicus.
