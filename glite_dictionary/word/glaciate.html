to glaciate

1. VERB

/ ˈɡleɪ.si.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlæ.si.eɪt / (BrE)
to cover with ice, snow, or a glacier; to subject to glaciation
to cover with ice or a glacier

Examples

Rising temperatures could affect areas previously glaciated during the last Ice Age.
The mountain range became glaciated over thousands of years.
Scientists study landscapes that were glaciated to understand past climate changes.

Synset Examples

the entire area was glaciated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to turn into ice or become covered with ice

Etymology

From Latin glaciāre (to freeze), from glacies (ice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01211328-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɡleɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlaːsɪeɪt / (BrE)
(of a region) to become frozen and covered with glaciers, especially as a result of climatic changes.
become frozen and covered with glaciers

Examples

During the last Ice Age, large portions of North America began to glaciate.
Scientists believe that the mountain valley will eventually glaciate as temperatures drop further.
If the climate remains cold enough, the landscape could glaciate over the next few millennia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to become hardened into ice or turn to ice
  • Antonym: thaw - to become unfrozen or no longer covered by ice

Etymology

From Latin glaciat-, past participial stem of glaciare (to freeze), from glacies (ice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00375046-v
Synset POS: v
