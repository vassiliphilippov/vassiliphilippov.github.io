almoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.mən.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈæl.mən.ə / (BrE)
a person in a British hospital, traditionally responsible for looking after the social and material needs of patients, often by distributing charity or arranging welfare support
hospital official who assists patients' social needs (Britain; historical)
british archaic

Examples

The hospital almoner arranged transportation and financial assistance for the patient's family.
In the past, an almoner would help patients access charity funds for medical treatment.
Her aunt worked as an almoner during the 1940s, supporting countless recovering soldiers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social worker - a person whose job is to help people in need or at risk

Etymology

From Old French 'aumonier', from Medieval Latin 'almonarius' (distributor of alms), from Latin 'eleemosyna' (alms).
