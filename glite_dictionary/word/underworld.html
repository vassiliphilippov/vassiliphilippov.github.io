underworld

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌwɝld / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌwɜːld / (BrE)
The world of organized crime and people involved in criminal activities.
the criminal class or world of organized crime

Examples

The police are making efforts to infiltrate the underworld and gather information about illegal activities.
He was rumored to have strong connections in the underworld of the city.
Journalists often risk their lives investigating stories about the underworld.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underworld - the mythical abode of the dead
  • Synonym: criminal - a person who commits crimes; lawbreaker

Etymology

From under- + world; originally referred to the mythical land of the dead, later applied to criminal society.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌwɜrld / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌwɜːld / (BrE)
(in religion and mythology) the place where the souls of the dead go after death; the world of the dead.
the world of the dead in religion or myth

Examples

In Greek mythology, souls would journey to the underworld after death.
Some cultures believe the underworld is ruled by a powerful deity.
Legends tell of heroes who ventured into the underworld to rescue lost souls.

Synset Examples

No one goes to Hades with all his immense wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - the Greek underworld or god of the underworld
  • Synonym: afterlife - life after death; existence beyond death

Etymology

From Old English 'underweorold', meaning 'world beneath'.
