hyperbetalipoproteinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pɚˌbiː.tə.lɪp.oʊˌproʊ.tiː.niːˈmiː.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˌbiː.tə.lɪp.əʊˌprəʊ.tiː.niːˈmiː.ə / (BrE)
a genetic disorder characterized by abnormally high levels of beta-lipoproteins and cholesterol in the blood, often leading to early atherosclerosis
genetic disorder with high beta-lipoproteins and cholesterol
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with hyperbetalipoproteinemia after observing extremely high cholesterol levels.
People with hyperbetalipoproteinemia often require early intervention to prevent cardiovascular disease.
Genetic counseling was recommended for the family because of their history of hyperbetalipoproteinemia.

Etymology

From hyper- (excessive) + beta-lipoprotein (a type of blood protein) + -emia (condition of the blood), modeled after medical Latin.
