Sedum rosea

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdəm roʊˈziːə / (AmE), / ˈsiːdəm ˈrəʊziə / (BrE)
A Eurasian mountain plant with succulent, pink-tipped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers, also known as roseroot.
Eurasian mountain succulent plant

Examples

The Sedum rosea grows abundantly in alpine regions across Europe and Asia.
Gardeners admire Sedum rosea for its attractive pink-tipped foliage and bright flowers.
Traditional remedies sometimes use extracts from Sedum rosea roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roseroot - another common name for Sedum rosea

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Sedum' being the genus name, and 'rosea' meaning 'rosy' or 'pink'.
