atorvastatin

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɔr.vəˈstæt.ɪn / (AmE), / əˌtɔː.vəˈstæt.ɪn / (BrE)
a statin drug used to lower cholesterol and triglyceride levels in the blood, often prescribed to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease
cholesterol-lowering statin drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed atorvastatin to help manage her high cholesterol levels.
Patients taking atorvastatin should have their liver function monitored regularly.
Atorvastatin is commonly sold under the brand name Lipitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simvastatin - another statin drug used to lower cholesterol
  • Synonym: statin - category of drugs used to lower cholesterol levels

Etymology

From ator- (likely arbitrary prefix) + statin (class of cholesterol-lowering drugs, from -statin, used for inhibitors)
