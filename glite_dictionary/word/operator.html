operator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɒpəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
(mathematics) a symbol or function that represents a mathematical operation, such as addition, multiplication, or differentiation.
symbol or function representing a mathematical operation

Examples

In calculus, the differential operator is used to represent differentiation with respect to a variable.
The plus sign '+' is a simple arithmetic operator indicating addition.
Quantum mechanics makes frequent use of the Hamiltonian operator to describe a system's energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operation - a mathematical process such as addition or multiplication

Etymology

From Latin operari (to work), via Medieval Latin operator (performer, worker).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɒpəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who aggressively speculates and trades in stock or commodity markets, often to make short-term profits
speculator in financial markets

Examples

The financial news reported that a well-known operator made millions in a single week.
Some consider the operator a risk-taker, while others see them as reckless gamblers.
During volatile market periods, an operator can influence prices with large trades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculator - a person who engages in risky financial transactions
