bran

1. NOUN

/ bræn / (AmE), / bræn / (BrE)
the hard outer layers of cereal grains, separated from the flour after milling, often used as a source of dietary fiber
outer husks of cereal grains

Examples

Whole-wheat bread contains more bran than white bread, making it higher in fiber.
He added a spoonful of bran to his morning cereal for extra nutrition.
The mill separates the flour from the bran during processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: germ - the part of a cereal grain that contains the embryo
  • Degree Lower: endosperm - nutritive tissue within seeds of most flowering plants

Etymology

From Middle English 'bran', from Old French 'bran' (bran, refuse of flour).

2. NOUN

/ bræn / (AmE), / bræn / (BrE)
The outer layers of cereal grains, separated during milling and used as a food rich in fiber.
outer husks of cereal grains used as food

Examples

She sprinkled bran on her cereal to add extra fiber to her breakfast.
Whole wheat bread contains more bran than white bread, making it healthier.
The recipe calls for wheat bran to improve the texture and nutritional value of the muffins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: germ - the nutrient-rich core of a cereal grain
  • Degree Lower: endosperm - the starchy inner part of a cereal grain

Etymology

From Old French 'bran' (bran, mill-dust).
