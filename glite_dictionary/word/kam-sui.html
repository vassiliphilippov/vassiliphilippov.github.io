Kam-Sui

1. NOUN

/ kɑm suw.i / (AmE), / kæm suː.i / (BrE)
a group of closely related languages within the Kadai branch, spoken mainly in southern China and northern Southeast Asia
group of Kadai languages

Examples

Linguists have classified the Kam-Sui languages as a distinct subgroup of the Kra-Dai family.
The Kam-Sui languages include Kam, Sui, and several lesser-known tongues.
Fieldwork on Kam-Sui languages helps document endangered dialects in southern China.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Kra-Dai - language family to which Kam-Sui belongs

Etymology

From the names of two major languages in the group, Kam and Sui.
