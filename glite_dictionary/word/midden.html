midden

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdən / (AmE), / ˈmɪd(ə)n / (BrE)
A heap or deposit of dung, refuse, or other waste material, especially one found at an archaeological site.
refuse or dung heap; archaeological dump

Examples

Archaeologists uncovered a midden containing ancient pottery fragments and animal bones.
The pile behind the barn was a midden where farmers discarded manure and rubbish.
Shellfish remains in the coastal midden provided evidence of prehistoric diets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dump - a place where waste is deposited
  • Synonym: landfill - a site for the disposal of waste materials by burial

Etymology

From Middle English midding, from Old Norse mykdyngja (dung heap, manure pile).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.ən / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.ɪn / (BrE)
(archaeology) a mound or deposit containing domestic refuse such as shells, bones, and other remnants, especially marking the site of a prehistoric settlement.
archaeological refuse mound

Examples

The archaeologist uncovered a prehistoric midden filled with animal bones and broken pottery.
Shells and charcoal in the coastal middens offer clues about ancient diets and habits.
Excavations revealed that the site had several overlapping middens from different eras.

Etymology

From Middle English 'midding', from Old Norse 'mykdyngja' meaning dung heap.
