astounding

1. ADJ

/ əˈstaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈstaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
so surprising or impressive as to cause astonishment or shock
very surprising; astonishing

Examples

The magician's astounding tricks left the audience speechless.
Scientists made an astounding discovery that could change the future of medicine.
Her astounding memory allowed her to recall every detail from their conversation.

Synset Examples

such an enormous response was astonishing
an astounding achievement
the amount of money required was staggering
suffered a staggering defeat
the figure inside the boucle dress was stupefying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - very surprising; causing amazement
  • Synonym: staggering - deeply shocking; astonishing
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull

Etymology

From the verb 'astound' + -ing; 'astound' is from Middle English 'astoned' (amazed).

2. ADJ

/ əˈstaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈstaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
so surprising or remarkable as to cause astonishment or wonder; astonishing.
very surprising or amazing

Examples

Her performance in the play was simply astounding and left the entire audience speechless.
The astounding discovery changed scientists’ understanding of the solar system.
It’s astounding how much technology has advanced in just a few years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - causing a feeling of great surprise or amazement
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not surprising or unusual; ordinary

Etymology

Present participle of 'astound', from Latin 'extonare' (to thunder out).
