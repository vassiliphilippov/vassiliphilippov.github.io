butter

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌtər / (AmE), / ˈbʌtə / (BrE)
to spread butter on (a surface, especially bread or toast)
spread butter on food

Examples

She likes to butter her toast generously every morning.
Please butter the bread before making the sandwiches.
He buttered each slice while it was still warm.

Synset Examples

butter bread

Etymology

From Old English *butere*, from Latin *butyrum*, from Greek *bouturon* (cow cheese).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01270562-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtə / (BrE)
a pale yellow edible fatty substance made by churning cream or milk, used in cooking and as a spread
edible fat made from cream or milk

Examples

She spread butter on her toast before adding jam.
Melt the butter in a pan and sauté the onions until golden.
The chef uses fresh butter in all his pastry recipes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: margarine - a butter substitute made from vegetable oils

Etymology

From Old English 'butere', from Latin 'butyrum', from Greek 'bouturon' (cow cheese).
