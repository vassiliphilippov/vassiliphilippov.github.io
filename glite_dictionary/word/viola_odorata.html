Viola odorata

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˌoʊlə oʊˈdɔrətə / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələ əʊˈdɒrətə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to Europe and Asia, known for its fragrant purple or white flowers and often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
a fragrant species of violet

Examples

The sweet scent of Viola odorata filled the early spring garden.
Many gardeners plant Viola odorata for its attractive and fragrant blossoms.
Botanists have documented Viola odorata in woodlands across Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - A small plant with purple or white flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'viola' (violet) and 'odorata' (fragrant).
