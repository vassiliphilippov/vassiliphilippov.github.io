keratoconjunctivitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛr.ə.toʊ.kənˌdʒʌŋk.təˈvaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɛr.ət.ə.kənˌdʒʌŋk.tɪˈvaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of both the cornea and the conjunctiva of the eye, often caused by infection, allergy, or irritation.
inflammation of cornea and conjunctiva
medical

Examples

Keratoconjunctivitis is often seen in people with severe dry eyes or allergic reactions.
The doctor prescribed eye drops to treat her keratoconjunctivitis.
Dogs are also susceptible to keratoconjunctivitis, especially certain breeds.

Etymology

From Greek *kerato-* (horn, cornea) and Latin *conjunctiva* (joining together) plus *-itis* (inflammation).
