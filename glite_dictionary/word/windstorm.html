windstorm

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌstɔːm / (BrE)
a storm marked by very strong winds, often causing damage, but with little or no precipitation
storm with violent winds

Examples

The windstorm knocked down several trees in the neighborhood.
A severe windstorm disrupted travel plans across the region.
After the windstorm, debris was scattered throughout the city streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gale - a very strong wind
  • Degree Higher: hurricane - a violent tropical storm with strong winds
  • Degree Lower: breeze - a gentle wind
