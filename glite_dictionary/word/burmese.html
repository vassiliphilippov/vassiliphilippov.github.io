Burmese

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈmiz / (AmE), / bɜːˈmiːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Myanmar (formerly Burma), or a person of Burmese descent.
person from Myanmar (Burma)

Examples

Many Burmese have moved abroad in search of better opportunities.
The Burmese are known for their rich cultural traditions.
He met several Burmese while traveling through Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myanmar - the Southeast Asian country formerly known as Burma

Etymology

From Burma (former name of Myanmar) + -ese (denoting origin or relation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09715143-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ bɜrˈmiz / (AmE), / bɜːˈmiːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Myanmar (formerly Burma), its people, language, or culture.
relating to Myanmar or its people

Examples

She cooked a delicious Burmese curry for dinner.
The Burmese language has a unique script and tone system.
Many Burmese traditions celebrate the New Year with a water festival.

Synset Examples

the Burmese capital
Burmese tonal languages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myanmar - the official name of the country formerly known as Burma

Etymology

From Burma, the former name of Myanmar, with the English suffix -ese forming adjectives and demonyms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03063423-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ bɜrˈmiz / (AmE), / bɜːˈmiːz / (BrE)
the official language of Myanmar (formerly Burma), belonging to the Sino-Tibetan language family
official language of Myanmar (Burma)

Examples

She learned to speak Burmese after moving to Yangon for work.
The Burmese language uses its own unique script.
Translations were available in English, Burmese, and several regional dialects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myanmar - the country where Burmese is spoken

Etymology

From Burma, the former name of Myanmar, with the English suffix -ese denoting origin or language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06945212-n
Synset POS: n
