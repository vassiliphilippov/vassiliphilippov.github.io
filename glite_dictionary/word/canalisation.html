canalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkanəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of directing or managing something, especially information or communication, through specified channels
management through specified channels

Examples

The canalisation of customer feedback ensures that information reaches the right department efficiently.
Successful canalisation of internal communications can improve team coordination.
Without proper canalisation, important updates may be lost or overlooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channeling - the act of directing something through a particular route or medium
  • Antonym: dissemination - the act of spreading information widely

Etymology

From French 'canalisation', from 'canaliser' (to channel), from 'canal' (channel, canal).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnæl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌnal.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of constructing canals or converting an area so that it is served by canals, especially for irrigation or navigation
the construction or conversion to canals
british

Examples

The canalisation of the river transformed the region’s agricultural potential.
Government investment in canalisation aimed to enhance inland transportation in the 19th century.
The canalisation project was completed after five years of extensive engineering work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canalization - the construction or conversion to canals (US spelling)

Etymology

From French 'canalisation', from 'canaliser' (to canalize), from 'canal' (canal).
