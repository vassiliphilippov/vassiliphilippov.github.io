ministry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstri / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstri / (BrE)
A building where the administrative work of a government department is carried out.
government department building

Examples

The new ministry was inaugurated by the prime minister last week.
Security has been tightened around the ministry following recent protests.
All official documents are handled within the main ministry in the capital.

Etymology

From Latin ministerium (office, service, ministry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstri / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstri / (BrE)
The body of religious ministers collectively, particularly within certain Christian denominations such as the Presbyterian Church.
religious ministers collectively

Examples

The annual conference brings together the entire ministry for discussion and prayer.
The Presbyterian ministry was highly influential in the local community.
She respected the dedication and compassion shown by the ministry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergy - officials who conduct religious services, especially Christian priests and ministers
  • Antonym: laity - members of a religious community who are not clergy

Etymology

From Latin 'ministerium' (office, service, ministry), from 'minister' (servant).

WordNet ID: oewn-08130763-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnəstri / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstri / (BrE)
A government department headed by a minister, responsible for a particular area of public affairs.
government department headed by a minister

Examples

The foreign ministry issued a statement regarding the international summit.
She works at the ministry of education, focusing on school reform.
A new policy was introduced by the health ministry to improve public hospitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: department - a division of a large organization such as a government or university
  • Synonym: agency - a government office providing a specific service

Etymology

From Latin 'ministerium' (office, service) via Old French 'ministrei'.

Synset POS: n

/ ˈmɪnɪstri / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstri / (BrE)
the work or duties of a minister of religion, especially as a profession
work of a minister of religion

Examples

After years of preparation, she finally entered the ministry and began serving her congregation.
He felt a calling to devote his life to the ministry and spiritual guidance of others.
Many students at the seminary are studying for the ministry and hope to become ordained ministers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergy - people ordained for religious duties
  • Antonym: laity - ordinary people, not clergy
