colloquy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.lə.kwi / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ə.kwi / (BrE)
a formal conversation or discussion, especially on a serious topic
formal conversation or discussion

Examples

The legal team participated in a lengthy colloquy with the judge concerning the evidence.
During the conference, a private colloquy between the scientists clarified the main issues at stake.
The university symposium featured a colloquy on the ethical implications of artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversation - an informal talk between two or more people
  • Synonym: dialogue - a conversation between two or more people

Etymology

From Latin colloquium (conversation, conference) from col- (together) + loqui (to speak).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.lə.kwi / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ə.kwi / (BrE)
a formal conversation or serious discussion, especially on a specific subject
formal conversation or discussion

Examples

The annual conference included a fascinating colloquy between leading scientists.
Their colloquy on international relations lasted well into the night.
She was invited to participate in a colloquy on ethics and technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people
  • Synonym: debate - formal discussion on a particular topic
  • Antonym: monologue - long speech by one person

Etymology

From Latin *colloquium* (conversation, conference), from *col-* (together) + *loqui* (to speak).
