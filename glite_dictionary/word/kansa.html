Kansa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænzə / (AmE), / ˈkænzə / (BrE)
the Siouan language variety spoken by the Kansa people, closely related to other Dhegiha dialects.
Siouan language of the Kansa people

Examples

The Kansa language was historically spoken along the Kansas River.
Linguists have documented many words from Kansa and related Dhegiha dialects.
He is learning to speak Kansa in an effort to help revitalize the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Osage - the language of the Osage people, closely related Dhegiha Siouan dialect
  • Synonym: Kaw - the same language, also called 'Kaw' in reference to the Kansa tribe

Etymology

From the name of the Kansa people, a Native American tribe of the central United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.sə / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of the Siouan language family, originally living in the Kansas river valley in Kansas.
a member of a Native American people of Kansas

Examples

The Kansa once inhabited large areas along the Kansas River.
Descendants of the Kansa still live in parts of Oklahoma today.
The traditions of the Kansa people are preserved through tribal ceremonies and storytelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kaw - another name for the Kansa people

Etymology

From the self-designation of the people, commonly rendered as Kansa or Kaw.
