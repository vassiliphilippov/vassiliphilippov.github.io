chinquapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋ.kəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋ.kwəˌpɪn / (BrE)
A shrubby chestnut tree of the southeastern United States, bearing small edible nuts.
chestnut tree with edible nuts

Examples

The forest was full of chinquapin trees, their branches heavy with nuts in autumn.
Wildlife in the region depend on the chinquapin for food during the winter months.
She roasted a handful of chinquapin nuts over the campfire for a snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a tree producing large, edible nuts; related genus

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *chechinquamin* (tree bearing edible nuts).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (BrE)
A small North American tree or shrub of the beech family, closely related to the chestnut, with edible nuts and typically large leaves.
tree/shrub related to chestnut

Examples

The chinquapin produces small, sweet nuts that are a favorite of wildlife.
We planted a chinquapin in the backyard to provide shade and food for birds.
Several types of chinquapin grow naturally in the southern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinkapin - alternative spelling for the same tree/shrub
  • Synonym: chestnut - a large tree producing edible nuts; closely related species

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *chechinquamin* (edible nut).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (BrE)
A small edible nut from either of two small chestnut trees native to the southern United States, resembling a hazelnut.
small edible nut from southern U.S. chestnut trees

Examples

The squirrels gathered chinquapins from the forest floor to store for winter.
She roasted a handful of chinquapins over the campfire for a tasty snack.
During the hike, we stopped to collect ripe chinquapins beneath the old trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - edible brown nut from the chestnut tree
  • Synonym: hazelnut - edible nut of the hazel tree

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *chechinquamin*.
