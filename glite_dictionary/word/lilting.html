lilting

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪltɪŋ / (BrE)
having a pleasantly light and rhythmic quality, often applied to music, speech, or movement.
having a light, rhythmic quality

Examples

The singer's lilting voice enchanted the entire audience.
A lilting melody drifted from the open window, filling the street with music.
She recited the poem in a lilting tone that brought it to life.

Synset Examples

an easy lilting stride
the flute broke into a light lilting air
a swinging pace
a graceful swingy walk
a tripping singing measure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swinging - having a lively, rhythmic motion or quality
  • Synonym: tripping - having a light, quick, rhythmic character

Etymology

From the verb 'lilt', probably of Scandinavian origin.
