sialadenitis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌæl.ə.dəˈnaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / saɪˌæl.ə.dɪˈnaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the salivary glands, often caused by infection or blockage
inflammation of salivary glands
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with sialadenitis after noticing swelling near his jaw.
Antibiotics are often prescribed for bacterial sialadenitis to reduce inflammation and treat the infection.
Chronic sialadenitis can result in persistent pain and difficulty swallowing.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'sialon' (saliva), 'aden' (gland), and '-itis' (inflammation).
