arteria lienalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪr.i.ə liːˈæn.əlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪə.rɪə liːˈeɪ.nə.lɪs / (BrE)
the artery that branches from the celiac trunk and supplies blood to the spleen; also called the splenic artery
artery supplying blood to the spleen
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the arteria lienalis was carefully isolated and clamped.
Aneurysms of the arteria lienalis can be life-threatening and require prompt intervention.
The arteria lienalis supplies oxygen-rich blood directly to the spleen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splenic artery - main artery supplying the spleen

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'lienalis' (of the spleen, from 'lien' meaning spleen).
