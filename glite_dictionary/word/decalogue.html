Decalogue

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌlɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəlɒɡ / (BrE)
The Ten Commandments given to Moses in the Bible, regarded as moral laws.
the Ten Commandments; biblical laws

Examples

Many believe that the Decalogue provides a foundation for ethical behavior.
The laws in the Decalogue are central to both Jewish and Christian traditions.
He studied the Decalogue to understand its role in religious history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ten Commandments - the biblical commandments given to Moses

Etymology

From Greek dekalogos, from deka (ten) + logos (word).
