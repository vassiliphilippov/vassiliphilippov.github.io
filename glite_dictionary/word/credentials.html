credentials

1. NOUN

/ krəˈdɛnʃəlz / (AmE), / krɪˈdɛnʃəlz / (BrE)
documents or certificates that prove a person’s identity, qualifications, or status
documents proving identity or qualifications

Examples

Applicants must provide their credentials before being considered for the job.
He showed his press credentials at the security checkpoint.
Her academic credentials include degrees from two prestigious universities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate - an official document attesting a fact, qualification, or achievement
  • Synonym: diploma - a document certifying the successful completion of a course of study
  • Synonym: identification - official documents that prove who someone is

Etymology

From Medieval Latin credentialis (pertaining to credence), from Latin credentia (belief, trust).
