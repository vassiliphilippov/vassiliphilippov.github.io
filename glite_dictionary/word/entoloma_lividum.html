Entoloma lividum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.toʊˈloʊ.mə lɪˈvɪd.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛn.təˈləʊ.mə lɪˈvɪd.əm / (BrE)
a poisonous species of mushroom with a large cap, initially white or lead-colored, turning yellowish or tan when mature
a deadly poisonous mushroom species

Examples

The toxic Entoloma lividum is often mistaken for edible mushrooms by foragers.
Experts warn against consuming wild fungi that resemble Entoloma lividum.
Due to its deadly nature, Entoloma lividum should be carefully avoided when collecting mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - an umbrella-shaped fungus with a stem and cap
  • Synonym: poisonous - causing harm or death if consumed

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Entoloma' + Latin 'lividum' (lead-colored).
