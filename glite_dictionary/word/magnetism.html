magnetism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The physical phenomenon by which materials such as iron are attracted by a magnet, or by which electric currents produce magnetic fields.
the property of magnetic attraction or field

Examples

The earth's magnetism causes compasses to point north.
He studied magnetism in his physics class at university.
Some animals can sense magnetism and use it to navigate long distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electricity - the phenomenon associated with electric charge
  • Synonym: magnet - an object that attracts iron and produces a magnetic field

Etymology

From French 'magnétisme', from Latin 'magnes' (magnet, lodestone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nə.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The branch of science concerned with the properties and effects of magnets and magnetic fields.
science of magnetic phenomena

Examples

He devoted his career to the study of magnetism and its effects on electrical devices.
The field of magnetism saw rapid advances in the 19th century.
Many basic physics courses cover electricity and magnetism as fundamental topics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: electromagnetism - the study of the interaction of electric and magnetic fields

Etymology

From French 'magnétisme', from 'aimant' meaning magnet, ultimately from Greek 'magnētis (lithos)' meaning (stone) from Magnesia.
