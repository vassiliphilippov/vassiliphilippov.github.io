rigged

1. ADJ

/ rɪɡd / (AmE), / rɪɡd / (BrE)
(of a ship, boat, or similar vessel) fitted or equipped with the necessary rigging such as sails, ropes, and stays.
equipped with sails and rigging (of vessels)

Examples

The schooner was fully rigged and ready to set sail at dawn.
Each type of ship is classified by how it is rigged.
A newly rigged yacht always attracts attention at the marina.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrigged - not provided with sails or rigging

Etymology

Past participle of 'rig' (to equip with rigging), from early Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'rigga'.
