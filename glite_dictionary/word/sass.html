sass

1. NOUN

/ sæs / (AmE), / sæs / (BrE)
impudent or disrespectful speech; cheeky or insolent talk, especially in reply to someone in authority
impudent or insolent talk
informal american

Examples

Don’t give me any of your sass when I’m speaking to you.
She got in trouble at school for talking back and showing sass to her teacher.
His constant sass makes him both amusing and infuriating to his parents.

Synset Examples

don't give me any of your sass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheek - impudent talk or disrespect, especially to someone in authority
  • Antonym: respect - polite regard or consideration shown to someone

Etymology

Alteration of 'sauce' (slang for impertinent talk) in American English.

2. VERB

/ sæs / (AmE), / sæs / (BrE)
to answer or speak to someone in a rude or cheeky manner, especially to someone in authority
answer back rudely or cheekily
informal

Examples

Don't sass your mother when she's giving you instructions.
If you sass the coach again, you'll have to leave practice early.
The employee was reprimanded for sassing his supervisor during the meeting.

Synset Examples

don't sass me!
The teacher punished the students who were sassing all morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backtalk - impudent or disrespectful reply
  • Synonym: disrespect - to show a lack of respect
  • Antonym: to obey - to follow commands or rules without argument

Etymology

Alteration of 'sauce' in the sense of impudence; American English, 19th century.
