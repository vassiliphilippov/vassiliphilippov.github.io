megacardia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈkɑrdiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈkɑːdiə / (BrE)
An abnormal enlargement of the heart, usually diagnosed through medical imaging or examination.
enlarged heart (medical term)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with megacardia after reviewing the chest X-ray.
Untreated high blood pressure can sometimes lead to megacardia and other complications.
Regular check-ups are important for athletes to monitor for potential megacardia.

Synset Examples

mild cardiomegaly is common in athletes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiomegaly - enlargement of the heart (medical term)

Etymology

From Greek mega (large) + kardia (heart).
