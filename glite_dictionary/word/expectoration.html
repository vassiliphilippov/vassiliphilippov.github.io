expectoration

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛk.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛk.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of forcefully expelling saliva or other material from the mouth, especially as a result of coughing
forceful expulsion of saliva or mucus
medical

Examples

Patients with chronic bronchitis may experience frequent expectoration during coughing episodes.
The nurse examined the color and consistency of the patient's expectoration for diagnostic purposes.
Excessive expectoration can be a symptom of respiratory infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spitting - the act of ejecting saliva forcibly from the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'expectoratio', from 'ex' (out) + 'pectus' (chest).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛktəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛktəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of coughing up and spitting out material from the airways, especially mucus or phlegm
coughing up and spitting out material
medical

Examples

Chronic bronchitis often leads to frequent expectoration in patients.
The physician asked if there had been any blood in the expectoration.
Proper disposal of expectoration is important to prevent the spread of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough - to expel air from the lungs with a sudden sharp sound

Etymology

From Latin expectoratio (discharge from the chest), from expectorare (to expel from the chest).
