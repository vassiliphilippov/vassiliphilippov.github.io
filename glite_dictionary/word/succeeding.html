succeeding

1. ADJ

/ səkˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / səkˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an elected official) having been elected to a position but not yet serving; about to take office.
elected but not yet serving

Examples

The succeeding governor will address the nation after taking office.
As the succeeding chairperson, she will take over responsibilities next month.
The ceremony included both the current and succeeding mayors.

Synset Examples

our next president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incumbent - currently holding office

Etymology

From Latin 'succedere' (to come after).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01298098-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ səkˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / səkˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
coming after or following someone or something in order, time, or sequence
coming after or following

Examples

The succeeding chapters of the book build upon the foundation established in the introduction.
In succeeding years, the event grew in size and reputation.
Each succeeding generation faces its own unique challenges.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: preceding - coming before in order or time

Etymology

From the verb 'succeed', from Latin 'succedere' (come after).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00128168-a
Synset POS: a
