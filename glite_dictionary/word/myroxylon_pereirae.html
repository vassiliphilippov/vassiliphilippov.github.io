Myroxylon pereirae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌrɒksɪˌlɒn pəˈraɪri.i / (AmE), / mɪˌrɒksɪˌlɒn pəˈrɪəri.i / (BrE)
a tree native to South and Central America that yields an aromatic balsam used in perfumes and medicines
tree yielding aromatic balsam

Examples

Myroxylon pereirae is commonly used as a source for balsam in the perfume industry.
The aromatic resin of Myroxylon pereirae has been valued for centuries in traditional medicine.
When visiting Central America, we saw a grove of Myroxylon pereirae thriving in the warm climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam - an aromatic resinous substance obtained from certain trees and shrubs

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'myron' (sweet oil, balsam) and 'xylon' (wood); species name from Pereira, a botanist.
