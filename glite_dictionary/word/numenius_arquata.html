Numenius arquata

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈmiːniəs ɑːrˈkweɪtə / (AmE), / njuːˈmiːniəs ɑːˈkwɑːtə / (BrE)
A large, long-billed wading bird of Eurasia and North Africa, commonly known as the Eurasian curlew.
large Eurasian wading bird (curlew)

Examples

The Numenius arquata is known for its distinctive, curved bill and haunting call.
Many wetlands have become crucial habitats for the Numenius arquata during migration season.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the Numenius arquata from habitat loss and population decline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew - common name for several wading birds in the genus Numenius

Etymology

From Latin: Numenius, genus name; arquata, meaning 'bowed' or 'arched', referring to the bird's curved bill.
