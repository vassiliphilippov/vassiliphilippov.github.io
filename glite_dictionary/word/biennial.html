biennial

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɛniəl / (AmE), / baɪˈɛniəl / (BrE)
A plant that completes its life cycle in two years, typically flowering and producing fruit in the second year before dying.
plant living two years
medical

Examples

Foxglove is a classic example of a biennial because it flowers only in its second year.
Gardeners often plant biennials one year and enjoy their blooms the next spring.
Carrots are technically biennial plants, but they are usually harvested after one growing season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: annual - a plant that completes its life cycle in one year
  • Antonym: perennial - a plant that lives for more than two years

Etymology

From Latin biennis (of two years), from bi- (two) + annus (year).

3. ADJ

/ baɪˈɛniəl / (AmE), / baɪˈeniəl / (BrE)
(of a plant) having a life cycle that lasts two years, typically flowering and producing seeds in the second year.
lasting two years (especially plants)

Examples

Many wildflowers are biennial, growing leaves one year and flowering the next.
Carrots are typically biennial but are often harvested in their first year.
The gardener identified the biennial plants by their two-year life cycles.

Synset Examples

a biennial life cycle
parsnips and carrots are biennial plants often grown as annuals

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: annual - lasting one year, especially referring to plants
  • Degree Higher: perennial - lasting several years, especially referring to plants

Etymology

From Latin 'biennis' (of two years), from 'bi-' (two) + 'annus' (year).
