Pinctada

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋkˈtɑːdə / (AmE), / pɪŋkˈtɑːdə / (BrE)
a genus of marine bivalve mollusks known as pearl oysters, which produce pearls inside their shells
genus of pearl oysters

Examples

The Pinctada genus includes several species that are cultivated for their high-quality pearls.
Scientists discovered a new variety of Pinctada in the waters off the coast of Australia.
Pearl farmers monitor the health of their Pinctada oysters to ensure the best pearl production.

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed from Latin roots ‘pinctus’ (painted) and ‘ada’; used as a genus name in zoological nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ pɪŋkˈtɑːdə / (AmE), / pɪŋkˈteɪdə / (BrE)
a genus of pearl oysters, some species of which are cultivated for natural pearls
genus of pearl oysters

Examples

Many cultured pearls are produced by oysters of the genus Pinctada.
The Pinctada genus plays a significant role in the pearl industry across Asia and the Pacific.
Biologists study the shell structure of Pinctada to understand pearl formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearl oyster - a marine bivalve mollusc known for producing pearls

Etymology

From Latinized Greek, based on Greek 'pincta' (painted), referencing the shell's appearance.
