tapestry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæpəstri / (AmE), / ˈtæpɪstri / (BrE)
A piece of thick textile fabric with designs or pictures woven or embroidered into it, often used as a wall hanging or for furniture covering.
decorative woven wall hanging

Examples

The medieval castle displayed a magnificent tapestry depicting battles and royal life.
She admired the intricate patterns woven into the old tapestry hanging in the museum.
A beautiful tapestry covered the wall, adding warmth and color to the room.

Etymology

From Old French *tapisserie*, from *tapisser* (to cover with tapestry), from *tapis* (carpet), from Latin *tapes* (cloth, carpet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæp.ə.stri / (AmE), / ˈtæp.ɪ.stri / (BrE)
A heavy woven textile with decorative designs, often used for wall hangings, curtains, or upholstery.
decorative woven textile

Examples

The medieval castle was adorned with a beautiful tapestry depicting scenes of battle and royalty.
She chose a floral tapestry to use as upholstery for her vintage armchair.
A colorful tapestry hung on the wall, adding warmth and texture to the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet - a floor covering made from thick woven fabric
  • Synonym: curtain - a piece of material hung to cover a window or doorway

Etymology

From Old French *tapisserie*, based on *tapis* (carpet), from Latin *tapete* (carpet, coverlet).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæpəstri / (AmE), / ˈtæpɪstri / (BrE)
A complex or intricate combination of different things or elements, resembling the elaborate pattern of a woven tapestry.
complex or intricate combination of elements

Examples

The tapestry of European history is woven from countless cultural and political influences.
Her life formed a rich tapestry of experiences, joys, and hardships.
The city's neighborhoods together create a vibrant tapestry of traditions and languages.

Synset Examples

the tapestry of European history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web - a complicated network or arrangement
  • Synonym: fabric - the basic structure or framework of something complex

Etymology

From Old French 'tapisserie', from 'tapis' (carpet), from Latin 'tapete'.
