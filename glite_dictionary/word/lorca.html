Lorca

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɔrkə / (AmE), / ˈlɔːkə / (BrE)
Federico García Lorca (1898–1936), a prominent Spanish poet and dramatist, known for his influential works and tragic death during the Spanish Civil War.
Spanish poet and dramatist (1898–1936)

Examples

Many critics regard Lorca as one of the greatest Spanish poets of the 20th century.
The plays of Lorca often explore themes of passion and repression.
During my literature studies, I became fascinated by the tragic life of Lorca.

Etymology

From the surname of Federico García Lorca, influential Spanish literary figure.
