ragwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌwɔːt / (BrE)
A plant of the daisy family with clusters of yellow flowers, especially common in American species such as Senecio aureus.
a plant with yellow flowers (daisy family)

Examples

Yellow ragwort grew in thick clusters along the roadside.
The fields were dotted with patches of ragwort swaying in the summer breeze.
Farmers often remove ragwort because it can be toxic to livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundsel - a related plant of the genus Senecio

Etymology

Formed from 'rag' + 'wort' (an old word for plant or herb).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A widespread European wild plant with bright yellow, daisylike flowers, sometimes considered a weed and toxic to livestock if eaten in quantity.
yellow-flowered European weed, toxic to animals

Examples

We noticed a patch of ragwort growing along the roadside as we hiked through the countryside.
Farmers work hard to control ragwort because it can be harmful to grazing cattle and horses.
The bright yellow flowers of ragwort stand out in the fields during late summer.

Etymology

From 'rag' + 'wort', denoting a plant with ragged leaves; 'wort' from Old English 'wyrt' meaning 'plant'.
