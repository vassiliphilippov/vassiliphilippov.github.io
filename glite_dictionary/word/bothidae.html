Bothidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊθɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊθɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of flatfish, commonly known as lefteye flounders, found in marine environments worldwide.
family of flatfish (lefteye flounders)

Examples

The Bothidae family includes numerous species of lefteye flounders found in warm and temperate seas.
Scientists study the diversity within the Bothidae to understand evolutionary adaptations among flatfish.
Some members of Bothidae are commercially important for fisheries in various regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish found at the bottom of seas and rivers

Etymology

From New Latin Bothidae, based on Bothus (a genus of flatfish) and the taxonomic suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈθaɪdiː / (AmE), / bəʊˈθɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of flatfish, commonly known as lefteye flounders, in the order Heterosomata, characterized by both eyes on the left side of the body
a family of flatfish (lefteye flounders)

Examples

The Bothidae family includes many species of flatfish found in oceans worldwide.
Marine biologists often study Bothidae to understand adaptation in flatfish.
Most members of Bothidae are easily identified by having both eyes on the left side of their bodies.

Etymology

From the genus name Bothus, type genus of the family, and the suffix -idae used in zoological family names.
