exaltolide

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɔl.əˌlaɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːl.əˌlaɪd / (BrE)
a macrocyclic lactone with fifteen carbon atoms, noted for its musky scent and used in perfumery
macrocyclic lactone with a musky scent

Examples

Exaltolide is valued in the perfume industry for its natural musky aroma.
Scientists studied how exaltolide interacts with human scent receptors.
Many luxury fragrances contain a small amount of exaltolide to add depth to their scent.

Synset Examples

Women tend to detect exaltolide better than men, and also find its odor more intense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaltone - a synthetic musky fragrance compound

Etymology

Blend of 'exaltone' (a related compound) and '-olide' (a suffix for lactones).
