postmortem

1. ADJ

/ poʊstˈmɔrtəm / (AmE), / pəʊstˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
occurring or done after death, especially relating to an examination to determine the cause of death
occurring or done after death
medical

Examples

The pathologist conducted a postmortem examination to establish the cause of death.
Some changes in body tissues are only visible during a postmortem inspection.
Investigators relied on postmortem findings to clarify what happened.

Synset Examples

postmortem changes
a postmortem examination to determine cause of death
postmortal wounds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antemortem - done before death

Etymology

From Latin 'post mortem' meaning 'after death'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00122459-a

2. NOUN

/ poʊstˈmɔrtəm / (AmE), / pəʊstˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
a discussion or analysis of an event after it has happened, especially to examine what went right or wrong
discussion after an event to analyze it

Examples

After the project ended, the team held a postmortem to identify what worked and what didn't.
The coach led a detailed postmortem to analyze the reasons behind the team's defeat.
We always conduct a postmortem after major incidents to improve our response in the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an examination of something that has happened

Etymology

From Latin post mortem, meaning 'after death'.

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ poʊstˈmɔrtəm / (AmE), / pəʊstˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
Occurring or done after death; also, an analysis or discussion of an event after it has occurred.
after death; after-the-event analysis

Examples

The doctor performed a postmortem examination on the body to determine the cause of death.
There was a lengthy postmortem after the project failed to meet its goals.
The committee held a postmortem discussion to analyze the problems with last year's event.

Synset Examples

a postmortem examination to determine the cause of death
the postmortem discussion of the President's TV address

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopsy - a medical examination of a body after death to determine cause of death
  • Antonym: ante mortem - before death (opposite of postmortem)

Etymology

From Latin 'post mortem', meaning 'after death'.

4. NOUN

/ poʊstˈmɔrtəm / (AmE), / pəʊstˈmɔːtəm / (BrE)
An examination and dissection of a dead body, especially to determine the cause of death or the effects of disease; an autopsy.
examination of a dead body (autopsy)
medical

Examples

The coroner scheduled a postmortem to determine the cause of the sudden death.
A detailed postmortem revealed signs of poisoning that were not initially suspected.
After his passing, the family requested a postmortem for further clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopsy - an official examination of a dead body to find the cause of death

Etymology

From Latin post mortem, meaning 'after death.'

Synset POS: n
