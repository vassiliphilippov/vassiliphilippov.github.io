amniote

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmnioʊt / (AmE), / ˈamniəʊt / (BrE)
a member of the group of vertebrate animals whose embryos are enclosed within an amnion, including reptiles, birds, and mammals
vertebrate with an amnion (reptile, bird, or mammal)

Examples

The evolution of the amniote egg allowed vertebrates to reproduce on land without water.
Reptiles, birds, and mammals are all examples of amniotes.
The earliest known amniote fossils date back to the late Carboniferous period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reptile - cold-blooded vertebrate of the class Reptilia
  • Synonym: bird - warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate of the class Aves
  • Synonym: mammal - warm-blooded vertebrate animal of the class Mammalia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Amniota, from Greek amnion (membrane surrounding an embryo) + -ote (denoting member of a group).
