Brickellia

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈkɛliə / (AmE), / brɪˈkɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants found mainly in southwestern America, characterized by creamy flower heads and fruits enclosed in a bristly sheath.
genus of southwestern American herbs

Examples

The desert landscape was dotted with flowering Brickellia plants.
Scientists are studying Brickellia for its potential medicinal uses.
Brickellia thrives in arid environments throughout the southwestern United States.

Etymology

Named after John Brickell, an American naturalist.
