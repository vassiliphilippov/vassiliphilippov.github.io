Psidium guajava

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪdiəm ɡwɑːˈjɑːvə / (AmE), / ˌpsɪdiʊm ɡwʌɪˈɑːvə / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree, widely grown in warm regions for its sweet, yellow, globular fruit known as guava.
species of tree producing guava fruit

Examples

The Psidium guajava tree thrives in regions with warm climates and ample rainfall.
Many farmers cultivate Psidium guajava for its highly valued fruit, commonly known as guava.
Research has been conducted into the medicinal properties of Psidium guajava leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guava - the edible fruit of Psidium guajava

Etymology

From Latin, Psidium (the genus) + guajava, from Taíno 'guayaba' (guava fruit).
