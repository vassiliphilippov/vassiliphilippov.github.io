systematizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəməˌtaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəmətaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who arranges things into a systematic order or creates an organized framework for ideas, processes, or objects
person who puts things in systematic order

Examples

Marie Curie was a notable systematizer of scientific data in her field.
The success of the company was due in part to its founder, a natural systematizer.
A skilled systematizer can turn chaotic information into a clear, logical structure.

Synset Examples

Aristotle was a great orderer of ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges things or events

Etymology

From 'systematize' + '-er'; 'systematize' from Greek 'systēmat-', meaning 'organized whole'.
