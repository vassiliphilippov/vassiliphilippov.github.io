venae pancreatica

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnaɪ pæŋkriˈætɪkə / (AmE), / ˈviːnaɪ paŋkrɪˈatɪkə / (BrE)
Veins that drain blood from the pancreas and empty into the superior mesenteric and splenic veins.
veins draining the pancreas
medical

Examples

The venae pancreatica play a crucial role in transporting blood away from the pancreas.
Damage to the venae pancreatica can disrupt pancreatic function and cause complications.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully preserved the venae pancreatica to maintain proper blood flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancreatic vein - vein draining blood from the pancreas
  • Degree Higher: superior mesenteric vein - vein into which the venae pancreatica may empty

Etymology

From Latin 'venae' (veins) and 'pancreatica' (of the pancreas).
