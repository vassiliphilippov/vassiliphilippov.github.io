coordinative

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈɔrdn̩ˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or construction) serving to connect two words or phrases of the same type or grammatical construction, especially in grammar.
serving to link similar grammatical units

Examples

In English, coordinative conjunctions like 'and' or 'or' join two independent clauses.
The phrase 'bread and butter' uses a coordinative structure to connect two nouns.
She explained that 'but' is another commonly used coordinative word in grammar.

Synset Examples

‘and’ in ‘John and Mary’ or in ‘John walked and Mary rode’ is a coordinating conjunction; and so is ‘or’ in ‘will you go or stay?’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinating - serving to link grammatical elements of equal rank
  • Antonym: subordinative - serving to connect a subordinate grammatical element

Etymology

From Latin 'coordinare' (to arrange in order) + English suffix '-ive'.
