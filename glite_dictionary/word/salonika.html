Salonika

1. PROPN

/ səˈlɒnɪkə / (AmE), / səˈlɒnɪkə / (BrE)
a city and port in northeastern Greece, situated on an inlet of the Aegean Sea; the country's second largest city, also known as Thessaloniki.
port city in northeastern Greece

Examples

During the early 20th century, Salonika became an important center of commerce and culture.
We visited the vibrant markets and ancient ruins in Salonika last summer.
Many historical events during World War I took place in and around Salonika.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thessaloniki - the official Greek name for Salonika, the same city

Etymology

From Greek Σαλονίκη (Saloníki), an alternative form of Θεσσαλονίκη (Thessaloniki), the official Greek name.
