AAVE

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˌviːˈiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌviːˈiː / (BrE)
African American Vernacular English; a nonstandard variety of English spoken primarily by African Americans in the United States, with distinct linguistic features.
nonstandard variety of American English spoken by African Americans

Examples

Linguists have studied AAVE to better understand its grammar and usage.
Some aspects of AAVE are rooted in the language traditions of West Africa.
Many popular music genres feature elements of AAVE in their lyrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebonics - another term for African American Vernacular English

Etymology

Acronym for African American Vernacular English.
