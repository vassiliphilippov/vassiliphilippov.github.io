angioplasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈændʒioʊˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈandʒiəʊˌplasti / (BrE)
a medical procedure to repair or unblock a blood vessel, especially a coronary artery, by using a balloon or similar device
procedure to unblock or repair a blood vessel
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an angioplasty to open up the blocked artery.
After his angioplasty, he experienced significant relief from chest pain.
Advancements in angioplasty have reduced recovery times for many heart patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass surgery - surgical procedure to restore blood flow by diverting blood around blocked arteries

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + plassein (to form).
