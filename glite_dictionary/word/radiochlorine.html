radiochlorine

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈklɔːriːn / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈklɔːriːn / (BrE)
any radioactive isotope of chlorine, especially as used in scientific or medical contexts
radioactive chlorine isotope
medical

Examples

Scientists use radiochlorine tracers to study how chlorine moves in biological systems.
The decay rate of radiochlorine must be carefully monitored during experiments.
Exposure to radiochlorine requires strict safety protocols in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chlorine - a chemical element, symbol Cl, used especially in disinfectants and bleaches

Etymology

Formed from 'radio-' (referring to radioactivity) and 'chlorine'.
