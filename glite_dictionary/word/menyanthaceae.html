Menyanthaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.i.ænˈθeɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.i.anˈθiː.siː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous marsh plants in the order Gentianales, including the buckbean and water shamrock
family of marsh plants in Gentianales

Examples

The Menyanthaceae family includes several species adapted to wetland habitats.
Botanists have studied Menyanthaceae for their unique floral structures.
Members of the Menyanthaceae are often found in bogs and marshes around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus Menyanthes.

2. NOUN

/ mɛnˌjænθiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mɛnˌjænθiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous marsh plants in the order Gentianales, including aquatic and semi-aquatic species commonly found in temperate regions.
family of marsh plants (order Gentianales)

Examples

The Menyanthaceae family includes species that thrive in wetland habitats.
Botanists often study the Menyanthaceae for their unique adaptations to aquatic environments.
Members of the Menyanthaceae, such as buckbean, play an important role in marsh ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gentianales - an order of flowering plants including Gentianaceae, Rubiaceae, and related families

Etymology

From the type genus Menyanthes, of Greek origin, combined with the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
