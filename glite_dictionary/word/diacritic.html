diacritic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of an ability or element) serving to distinguish or differentiate between things.
capable of distinguishing

Examples

Her diacritic abilities helped her notice subtle differences in the paintings.
The report relied on diacritic features to separate the two species.
A diacritic approach is essential in identifying the underlying causes of the problem.

Synset Examples

students having superior diacritic powers
the diacritic elements in culture

Etymology

From Greek diakritikos (able to distinguish), from diakrinein (to separate, distinguish).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪ.əˈkrɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
A sign or mark added to a letter to alter its pronunciation or to distinguish it in some way.
mark added to a letter for pronunciation or distinction

Examples

Some languages use a diacritic to distinguish vowel sounds.
The letter 'é' has a diacritic that changes its pronunciation.
Adding the correct diacritic is important when spelling words in French.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accent - a mark above a letter showing pronunciation or stress

Etymology

From Greek diakritikos (distinguishing), from diakrinein (to distinguish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
