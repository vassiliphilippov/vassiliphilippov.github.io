microcephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈsɛf.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈsɛf.ə.li / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person has an abnormally small head and an underdeveloped brain, often leading to developmental issues.
abnormally small head (medical condition)
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed the infant with microcephaly during a routine examination.
Babies born with microcephaly may experience developmental delays and neurological problems.
The health campaign focused on raising awareness of microcephaly linked to certain infections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: macrocephaly - an abnormally large head

Etymology

From micro- (small) + Greek kephalē (head), referring to small head size.
