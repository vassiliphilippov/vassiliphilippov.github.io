Lygodium palmatum

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡoʊdiəm pælˈmeɪtəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡəʊdiəm pælˈmeɪtəm / (BrE)
A delicate climbing fern native to the eastern United States, with a twining stem, palmately-lobed sterile fronds, and forked fertile fronds.
climbing fern of eastern US

Examples

The shady slopes of the forest were covered in Lygodium palmatum, displaying their unique fronds.
Botanists studied the delicate structure of Lygodium palmatum in its natural habitat.
Many gardeners try to cultivate Lygodium palmatum for its ornamental foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American climbing fern - common name for Lygodium palmatum

Etymology

From genus name Lygodium (from Greek lygos, 'pliant twig') and Latin palmatum, 'palm-shaped.'
