Charleston

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlstən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlstən / (BrE)
A port city in southeastern South Carolina, United States, known for its historic architecture and significance in American history.
port city in South Carolina, USA

Examples

We spent our summer vacation exploring Charleston and its beautiful historic district.
After moving to Charleston, she quickly fell in love with its vibrant food scene.
Hurricane warnings were issued for Charleston due to the approaching storm.

Etymology

Named after King Charles II of England.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrlstən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlstən / (BrE)
a lively American ballroom dance in syncopated rhythm, popular in the 1920s
lively ballroom dance from the 1920s

Examples

They learned the Charleston for the 1920s-themed party.
She won the competition with an impressive Charleston routine.
The music began, and everyone joined in the lively Charleston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lindy hop - another energetic American dance style from the early 20th century

Etymology

Named after the city of Charleston, South Carolina, USA, where the dance became popular.

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑrlstən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlstən / (BrE)
to perform the Charleston, a lively dance popular in the 1920s characterized by swinging the arms and fast-paced movement of the legs and feet
to perform the Charleston dance

Examples

She loves to Charleston whenever she hears jazz music from the 1920s.
At the dance marathon, dozens of couples Charlestoned energetically across the ballroom.
He learned how to Charleston for his role in the stage production set in the Roaring Twenties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music

Etymology

Named after Charleston, South Carolina, where the dance originated.
