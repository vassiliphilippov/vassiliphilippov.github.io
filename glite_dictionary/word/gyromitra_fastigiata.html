Gyromitra fastigiata

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə fæˌstɪdʒiˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌɪrəˈmʌɪtrə fæˌstɪdʒɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of fungus in the genus Gyromitra, with a deep brownish-red cap and a white stalk, commonly known as a type of lorchel.
species of fungus with reddish-brown cap and white stalk

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Gyromitra fastigiata, their reddish caps contrasting with the fallen leaves.
Mycologists warn that Gyromitra fastigiata can be toxic if not properly prepared before consumption.
During our hike, we identified a patch of Gyromitra fastigiata growing near the old birch trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lorchel - another term for mushrooms in the genus Gyromitra

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Gyromitra' and species epithet 'fastigiata.'
