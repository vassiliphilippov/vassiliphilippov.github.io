Pyrrhocoridae

1. NOUN

/ pɪr.hoʊˈkɔː.rəˌdiː / (AmE), / pɪr.hɒˈkɒ.rɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored true bugs commonly known as firebugs, found especially in warm regions.
family of insects known as firebugs

Examples

The bright red and black coloration of Pyrrhocoridae serves as a warning to predators.
Scientists studied the behavior of several species in the Pyrrhocoridae family.
Many Pyrrhocoridae are often found congregating on the trunks of linden trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebug - a common name for insects of the Pyrrhocoridae family
  • Degree Higher: Hemiptera - the order of insects that includes true bugs such as Pyrrhocoridae

Etymology

From New Latin Pyrrhocoridae, based on Greek pyrros (flame-colored) + koris (bug).

A family of brightly colored insects known as firebugs, commonly found on plants and recognized by their striking red and black markings.
family of firebugs (insects)

Examples

Researchers studied the population dynamics of Pyrrhocoridae in urban gardens.
The bright red color of Pyrrhocoridae acts as a warning to predators.
Members of the Pyrrhocoridae family are often mistaken for other red and black insects.

From the genus name Pyrrhocoris, from Greek 'pyrros' (flame-colored) + 'koris' (bug).
