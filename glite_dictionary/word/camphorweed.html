camphorweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.fɚˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈkæm.fəˌwiːd / (BrE)
an aromatic plant of the western United States, especially of the genus Heterotheca, with camphor-scented leaves and yellow flowers
aromatic US plant with camphor scent

Examples

The fields were dotted with bright yellow camphorweed after the spring rain.
Ranchers often recognize camphorweed by its pungent aroma when crushed.
Hikers can spot camphorweed along trails throughout the southwestern desert.

Etymology

From 'camphor' (the fragrant substance) + 'weed' (plant regarded as undesirable or wild).
