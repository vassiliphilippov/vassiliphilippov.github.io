belletristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɛlɪˈtrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌbɛlɪˈtrɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to literature that is written and valued for its aesthetic qualities and artistic style rather than for informative or practical content.
concerned with literary style or aesthetics

Examples

Many critics dismissed the novel as belletristic rather than meaningful.
Her essays took a belletristic approach, prioritizing elegant style over argument.
Some journals focus on publishing belletristic writing that celebrates beauty in language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literary - concerned with literature or writing

Etymology

From French 'belles-lettres' (fine letters, literature), from 'belle' (beautiful) + 'lettre' (letter).
