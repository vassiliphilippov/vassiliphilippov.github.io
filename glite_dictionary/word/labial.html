labial

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the female labium.
relating to the female labium
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the labial region for any signs of irritation.
Certain infections can cause swelling in the labial area.
A labial cyst is a common condition treated by gynecologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labium - a lip-like structure, especially part of the female external genitalia

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip) with the suffix '-al'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with movement or closure of the lips.
consonant articulated with the lips

Examples

The English sound /p/ is a classic example of a labial.
Some African languages have more labials than English does.
The teacher pointed out the difference between a labial and a dental sound.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bilabial - a consonant articulated with both lips, such as 'p' or 'b'
  • Antonym: dental - a consonant articulated with the tongue against the teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip) + '-al'.

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (BrE)
relating to or situated at the lips of the mouth, especially in reference to sounds produced using the lips
relating to the lips or their use

Examples

The word 'pie' begins with a labial consonant.
Many African languages feature voiced labial sounds not found in English.
A labial wound made it hard for him to pronounce certain syllables.

Synset Examples

labial stops

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bilabial - (of a speech sound) made with both lips
  • Antonym: dental - (of a speech sound) made with the tongue against the teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip).
