Pagophila

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪɡəˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / ˌpaɡəʊˈfiːlə / (BrE)
a genus of gulls in the family Laridae, containing the ivory gull (Pagophila eburnea)
genus of gulls (ivory gull)

Examples

The Pagophila genus is known for its association with Arctic habitats.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of birds within the Pagophila genus.
Only the ivory gull belongs to the genus Pagophila.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory gull - a small Arctic gull (Pagophila eburnea), noted for its white plumage

Etymology

From Greek pagos (frost, ice) + philos (loving), referring to the bird's Arctic habitat.
