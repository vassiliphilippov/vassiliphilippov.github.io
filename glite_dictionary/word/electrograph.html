electrograph

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktroʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an apparatus used for transmitting images or pictures by electrical means
apparatus for electrical transmission of pictures

Examples

The news agency used an electrograph to transmit the photograph across the country.
Researchers experimented with the electrograph in the early days of image transmission technology.
Before the advent of modern fax machines, the electrograph enabled pictures to be sent over telegraph wires.

Etymology

From electro- (referring to electricity) + -graph (from Greek 'graphos', meaning 'writing' or 'instrument for recording').

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktroʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an electrical device used for etching materials by electrolytic means
device used for electrolytic etching

Examples

The artisan used an electrograph to etch the intricate design onto the metal plate.
Advancements in electrograph technology have improved the precision of decorative etching.
Safety precautions are essential when operating an electrograph due to the involvement of electrical currents.

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + -graph (instrument for recording or writing).
