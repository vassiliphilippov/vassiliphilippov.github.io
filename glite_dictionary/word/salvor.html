salvor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vər / (AmE), / ˈsæl.və / (BrE)
a person or company engaged in salvaging property, especially from shipwrecks or other disasters; particularly in maritime law, one who recovers cargo, ships, or other property at sea
person who salvages property, especially from shipwrecks
legal

Examples

The salvor was rewarded for retrieving the cargo from the sinking vessel.
A professional salvor must act promptly and efficiently to save property at risk at sea.
The court recognized the rights of the salvor after the difficult recovery operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone or something from danger
  • Antonym: looter - a person who takes goods unlawfully, often in chaotic situations

Etymology

From salvage + -or, modelled after French 'salveur' or Late Latin 'salvator' (saviour).
