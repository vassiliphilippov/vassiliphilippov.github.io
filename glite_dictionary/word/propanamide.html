propanamide

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpænəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / prəˈpænəmaɪd / (BrE)
A colourless, crystalline amide derived from propionic acid, with the chemical formula C2H5CONH2, used in organic synthesis.
amide of propionic acid (C2H5CONH2)
medical

Examples

Propanamide is an important intermediate in certain pharmaceutical syntheses.
Researchers studied the melting point of propanamide in the laboratory.
The conversion of propionic acid to propanamide involves an amination reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetamide - an amide derived from acetic acid

Etymology

From 'propanoic acid' + 'amide', indicating it is the amide form of propanoic acid.
