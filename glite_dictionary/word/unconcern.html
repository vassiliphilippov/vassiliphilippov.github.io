unconcern

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnkənˈsɜrn / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈsɜːn / (BrE)
a lack of worry or interest about something; the state of not caring
lack of concern or interest

Examples

Her apparent unconcern about the result surprised everyone.
He greeted the bad news with complete unconcern.
Despite the chaos around her, she maintained an air of unconcern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
  • Antonym: concern - a feeling of worry or interest

Etymology

Formed by prefixing un- (not) to concern; dates from the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.kənˈsɜrn / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈsɜːn / (BrE)
the state of not being worried or interested; a calm lack of concern about something
lack of worry or interest

Examples

Despite the chaos around him, he maintained an air of unconcern.
Her apparent unconcern about the looming deadline surprised her colleagues.
There was a distinct note of unconcern in his voice when he talked about the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or sympathy
  • Antonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease

Etymology

From un- (not) + concern (from French concerner, from Latin concernere 'sift together, mix up').
