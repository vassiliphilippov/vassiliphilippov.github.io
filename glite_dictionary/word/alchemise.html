to alchemise

1. VERB

/ ˈælkəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈalkɪmʌɪz / (BrE)
to change or transform something in a mysterious or magical way, especially by means similar to alchemy
transform in a magical or mysterious way

Examples

The artist was able to alchemise ordinary materials into stunning works of art.
Some writers strive to alchemise pain and hardship into beautiful poetry.
The chef seemed to alchemise simple ingredients into a delicious meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transmute - to change from one form, nature, substance, or state to another

Etymology

From alchemy, via Medieval Latin alchimia.
