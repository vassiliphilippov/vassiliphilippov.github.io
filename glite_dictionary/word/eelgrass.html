eelgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈilˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈiːlɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a submerged marine plant with long narrow leaves, found especially along North Atlantic coasts
submerged marine plant with long leaves

Examples

Large underwater meadows of eelgrass provide vital habitats for many marine creatures.
Scientists are studying how pollution affects the growth of eelgrass along the coast.
Young fish often hide among the leaves of eelgrass to escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seagrass - any of various grass-like marine plants found in shallow waters

2. NOUN

/ ˈilˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈiːlˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with long, ribbonlike leaves, found in the Old World and Australia, often forming underwater meadows
submerged aquatic plant with ribbonlike leaves

Examples

The restoration of eelgrass beds has been vital for improving water quality in the bay.
Many fish and invertebrates use eelgrass meadows as important nursery habitats.
Researchers carefully mapped the distribution of eelgrass along the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seagrass - a general term for grasslike marine plants

Etymology

Named for its long, grasslike leaves resembling eels.
