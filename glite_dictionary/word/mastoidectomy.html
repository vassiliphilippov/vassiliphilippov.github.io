mastoidectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɔɪˈdɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌmastoɪˈdɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of part or all of the mastoid process, often to treat infections or other ear conditions
removal of the mastoid process (surgery)
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a mastoidectomy to treat a severe middle ear infection.
Advances in mastoidectomy techniques have reduced the risk of complications.
After a mastoidectomy, regular follow-up appointments are crucial to monitor healing.

Etymology

From mastoid (relating to the mastoid process) + -ectomy (surgical removal), from Greek ektemnein (to cut out).
