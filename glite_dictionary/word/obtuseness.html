obtuseness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈtusnəs / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking a sharp edge or point; bluntness.
quality of lacking sharpness or point

Examples

The obtuseness of the knife made it difficult to slice the bread cleanly.
The obtuseness of the stone's edge suggests it was shaped by water over time.
She criticized the obtuseness of the pencil, asking for a sharper one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntness - the quality of being blunt or not sharp
  • Antonym: sharpness - the quality of having a sharp edge or point

Etymology

From Latin obtusus (blunt) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ əbˈtuːsnəs, ɑbˈtuːsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːsnəs, ɒbˈtjuːsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being slow to understand or perceive things; dullness of comprehension.
slowness to understand

Examples

His obtuseness made it difficult for his colleagues to explain the project to him.
She was frustrated by the obtuseness of the bureaucratic process.
The teacher addressed the student's obtuseness with patience and repeated explanations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dullness - lack of intelligence or quickness of mind
  • Antonym: sharpness - quickness of mind or understanding

Etymology

From Latin 'obtusus' (blunted, dull) + English '-ness.'
