anole

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊli / (AmE), / əˈnəʊli / (BrE)
a small, tree-dwelling lizard from tropical America, known for its ability to change skin color
a small color-changing tropical lizard

Examples

An anole can rapidly shift its skin color from brown to green depending on its mood and environment.
We spotted a bright green anole climbing up the tree in our backyard.
Many anoles are excellent at catching insects while darting among the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, movable eyelids, and rough, scaly or spiny skin

Etymology

From French anolis, from the Carib word anolí.
