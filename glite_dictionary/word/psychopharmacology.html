psychopharmacology

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌfɑrməˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌfɑːməkəˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the effects of drugs on the mind and behavior
study of mind-affecting drugs
medical

Examples

The field of psychopharmacology has led to major advances in the treatment of mental illnesses.
She is pursuing a PhD in psychopharmacology to better understand how medication can influence behavior.
Recent developments in psychopharmacology have given hope to people suffering from depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacology - the branch of medicine concerned with the uses, effects, and modes of action of drugs

Etymology

From Greek psykhe (mind) + pharmacology (study of drugs).
