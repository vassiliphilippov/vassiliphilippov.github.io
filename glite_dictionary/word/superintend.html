to superintend

1. VERB

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛnd / (AmE), / ˌsjuːpərɪnˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to be in charge of and direct the work or activities of others; to oversee and manage a process or group
to oversee or direct; manage

Examples

She was hired to superintend the construction of the new building.
The principal will superintend the final exams this year.
It is his job to superintend the budget and ensure everything runs smoothly.

Synset Examples

Who is overseeing this project?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oversee - to watch over and direct a process or activity
  • Synonym: to supervise - to direct and closely monitor the work of others

Etymology

From Latin superintendere, meaning 'to supervise,' from super- 'over' + intendere 'to direct.'
