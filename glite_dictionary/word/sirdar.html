sirdar

1. NOUN

/ sɪrˈdɑr / (AmE), / sɜːˈdɑː / (BrE)
a leader, chief, or person of authority, especially in India; historically used for a military or administrative leader.
chief or leader in India

Examples

The British appointed a sirdar to oversee the local administration.
As sirdar of the regiment, he commanded the troops with authority.
During the expedition, the sirdar coordinated all the sherpas and porters.

Etymology

From Persian 'sardār' (commander, leader), adopted into Hindi and Urdu.
