breather

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriðər / (AmE), / ˈbriːðə / (BrE)
a short period of rest or relief from activity
a short rest; respite

Examples

Let’s take a quick breather before we start the next task.
After running up the stairs, he paused for a breather.
We worked all morning without a breather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - a short period of rest or pause from work

2. NOUN

/ ˈbriðɚ / (AmE), / ˈbriːðə / (BrE)
a device with intake and exhaust pipes that allows a submarine to remain submerged for an extended period while taking in air
submarine air intake device

Examples

The submarine's breather enabled the crew to take in fresh air while remaining undetected underwater.
After several hours submerged, the engineers checked the breather for blockages.
Modern submarines rely on an efficient breather system to extend their underwater endurance.
