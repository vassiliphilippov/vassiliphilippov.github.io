lucrative

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːkrətɪv / (AmE), / ˈluːkrətɪv / (BrE)
producing a large amount of profit; financially rewarding
producing much profit

Examples

She received a lucrative job offer from a major tech company.
The partnership turned out to be a lucrative venture for both sides.
He left academia to pursue a more lucrative career in finance.

Synset Examples

a remunerative business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitable - producing a financial gain or profit
  • Antonym: unprofitable - not producing a financial gain

Etymology

From Latin lucrativus (profitable), from lucrum (profit, gain).
