tinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪniə / (AmE), / ˈtɪnɪə / (BrE)
A fungal infection of the skin or nails, usually appearing as circular, itchy patches.
fungal skin or nail infection
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the red, scaly rash on her arm as tinea.
Athletes are especially prone to developing tinea on their feet, known as athlete's foot.
Prompt treatment with antifungal cream can usually clear up most cases of tinea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringworm - A common name for tinea, marked by ring-shaped rashes

Etymology

From Latin *tinea* (a worm, ringworm).
