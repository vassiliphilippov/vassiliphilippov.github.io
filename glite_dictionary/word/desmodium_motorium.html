Desmodium motorium

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊ.di.əm moʊˈtɔr.i.əm / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊ.di.əm məʊˈtɔː.ri.əm / (BrE)
an erect tropical Asian shrub whose small lateral leaflets move visibly in response to sunlight, also known as the telegraph plant
an Asian shrub with moving leaflets

Examples

The Desmodium motorium is famous for its unique leaf movements that can be seen with the naked eye.
Scientists study Desmodium motorium to understand plant responses to light and environmental changes.
Visitors to the botanical garden were fascinated by the dancing leaves of the Desmodium motorium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegraph plant - common name for Desmodium motorium, referring to its rapidly moving leaflets

Etymology

From genus name Desmodium (from Greek 'desmos' meaning band or chain) and Latin 'motorium' (of motion), referring to the plant's leaf movements.
