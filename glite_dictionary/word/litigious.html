litigious

1. ADJ

/ lɪˈtɪdʒəs / (AmE), / lɪˈtɪdʒəs / (BrE)
having a tendency to engage in lawsuits or to be prone to dispute or argument.
prone to legal disputes

Examples

The company became increasingly litigious after losing several major contracts.
Her litigious nature often led to conflicts with her neighbors.
In recent years, society has grown more litigious, resulting in a surge of lawsuits.

Synset Examples

a style described as abrasive and contentious
a disputatious lawyer
a litigious and acrimonious spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contentious - likely to argue or cause disagreement
  • Synonym: argumentative - prone to argue or dispute

Etymology

From Latin 'litigiosus', from 'litigium' (dispute, lawsuit).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ lɪˈtɪdʒuəs / (AmE), / lɪˈtɪdʒuəs / (BrE)
Tending to engage in lawsuits or to be involved in litigation; relating to or concerned with legal proceedings.
prone to lawsuits; relating to litigation

Examples

The company became increasingly litigious over patent rights, filing multiple lawsuits against competitors.
His litigious nature made it difficult to settle disputes out of court.
In a litigious society, legal action is often seen as the first response to conflict.

Etymology

From Latin 'litigiosus' (fond of lawsuits), from 'litigium' (dispute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02870310-a
Synset POS: a
