thyreophoran

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌriːəˈfɔːrən / (AmE), / θaɪˌriːəˈfɔːrən / (BrE)
a member of a group of herbivorous, armored dinosaurs that includes stegosaurs and ankylosaurs
armored dinosaur (Stegosauria, Ankylosauria)

Examples

The discovery of a new thyreophoran species has shed light on dinosaur evolution.
Among the Jurassic fauna, thyreophorans were easily identified by their protective plates and spikes.
Scientists debate how effective the armor of a thyreophoran would have been against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stegosaur - a genus of quadrupedal, armored dinosaurs with distinctive back plates
  • Synonym: ankylosaur - a group of heavily armored dinosaurs with club-like tails

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreos' (shield) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to their distinctive body armor.
