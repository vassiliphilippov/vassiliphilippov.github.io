proportionateness

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɔrʃənətnəs / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənətnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of corresponding in degree, size, or amount; being proportionate
state of being proportionate

Examples

The proportionateness of the response was praised by observers worldwide.
There is a proportionateness to his designs that makes them aesthetically pleasing.
The law requires proportionateness in punishment to ensure fairness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disproportionateness - the quality of lacking proportion or correspondence in degree, size, or amount
  • Synonym: proportionality - the state of being proportional or having a constant ratio

Etymology

From 'proportionate' + '-ness'. 'Proportionate' from Latin 'proportionatus'.
