to protect

1. VERB

/ prəˈtɛkt / (AmE), / prəˈtɛkt / (BrE)
(of a government) to favor domestic industry by imposing tariffs or other barriers on foreign goods.
favor domestic industry by tariffs

Examples

Many countries protect their steel industries by imposing high tariffs on imports.
The government decided to protect local farmers from foreign competition through trade barriers.
Some economists argue that it is harmful to protect industries instead of promoting free trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield - to protect from harm or danger, sometimes used figuratively for economic protection
  • Antonym: to expose - to leave unprotected; the opposite of protecting industries

2. VERB

/ prəˈtɛkt / (AmE), / prəˈtɛkt / (BrE)
to keep someone or something safe from harm, danger, or damage
keep safe from harm or damage

Examples

Parents do their best to protect their children from harm.
Wearing sunscreen will protect your skin against UV rays.
Special covers are used to protect the seats from dirt and spills.

Synset Examples

Weatherbeater protects your roof from the rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defend - to guard against attack or harm
  • Antonym: harm - cause physical injury or damage to

Etymology

From Latin 'protectus', past participle of 'protegere' (to cover in front).
