corbelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrbəld / (AmE), / ˈkɔːbəld / (BrE)
Built or constructed with corbels; having a structure in which stones or bricks project out from the wall to support weight above.
constructed with corbels

Examples

The ancient stone doorway was corbelled to create a gentle arch.
Many medieval castles feature corbelled parapets for support and decoration.
A series of corbelled ledges projected from the face of the old tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corbel - a stone or brick jutting out from a wall to support a structure above

Etymology

From 'corbel', from Old French *corbel* (supporting bracket), from Latin *corvus* (raven, as the bracket's shape resembled a beak).
