Lactophrys quadricornis

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtɑːfrɪs kwɒdˌraɪˈkɔːrnɪs / (AmE), / lækˈtɒfrɪs kwɒdˌrɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A species of trunkfish, commonly known as the scrawled cowfish, characterized by hornlike spines over the eyes, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
species of trunkfish with hornlike spines

Examples

The Lactophrys quadricornis is easily recognized by the four hornlike projections above its eyes.
Divers often spot the distinctive Lactophrys quadricornis among coral reefs in the Caribbean.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Lactophrys quadricornis along the Florida coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawled cowfish - common name for Lactophrys quadricornis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Lactophrys' (a genus name from Greek 'gala, galaktos' meaning milk + 'ophrys' meaning eyebrow) and Latin 'quadricornis' meaning four-horned.
