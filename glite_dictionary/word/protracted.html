protracted

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈtræktɪd / (AmE), / prəˈtræktɪd / (BrE)
lasting for a long time or longer than expected; prolonged or drawn out, often to a tedious extent.
lasting unusually long time

Examples

The protracted negotiations between the two companies finally ended in an agreement.
Recovery from the illness was protracted, taking several months longer than anticipated.
There was a protracted silence after he asked the difficult question.

Synset Examples

a drawn-out argument
an extended discussion
a lengthy visit from her mother-in-law
a prolonged and bitter struggle
protracted negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolonged - lasting longer than usual or expected
  • Antonym: brief - lasting only a short time

Etymology

From Latin protractus, past participle of protrahere (to prolong, to draw forth).
