mothball

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑθˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to put something, especially equipment or a plan, into long-term storage or suspend its use, usually with the possibility of future activation
put into long-term storage

Examples

The company decided to mothball the factory until demand improved.
Several old ships were mothballed after the war and kept in reserve.
They are mothballing the project until they secure more funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to put or keep things in a particular place for future use
  • Synonym: to shelve - to postpone or suspend consideration or action on something

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'mothball', used to protect stored clothing from moths, giving rise to the verb.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑθˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a small ball, typically made of camphor or naphthalene, placed among stored clothing to repel moths and other insects
small ball to repel moths in clothing

Examples

I packed my sweaters with mothballs to protect them during the summer.
The closet smelled faintly of mothballs when I opened the door.
She prefers cedar chips to mothballs for keeping insects away from clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naphthalene - chemical compound commonly used in mothballs

Etymology

From 'moth' + 'ball', referring to its use against moths and its shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03794236-n
Synset POS: n
