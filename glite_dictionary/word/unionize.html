to unionize

1. VERB

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːniənaɪz / (BrE)
to organize workers into a labor union or become organized in this way
organize into a labor union

Examples

Many employees wanted to unionize after changes to their working conditions.
The company resisted attempts to unionize its workforce for several years.
After months of discussion, the teachers voted to unionize for better representation.

Synset Examples

We don't allow people to come into our plant and try to unionize the workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to organize - arrange into a structured whole, especially for a common purpose

Etymology

From 'union' + '-ize', meaning to make or become a union.

2. VERB

/ ˈjun.jəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to organize workers into a labor union or to join a labor union
form or join a labor union

Examples

After months of negotiation, the employees voted to unionize at last.
Many teachers are considering whether to unionize in response to the new regulations.
The company tried to discourage its workers from attempting to unionize.

Synset Examples

The auto workers decided to unionize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to organize - to form into a structured group, especially for a purpose

Etymology

From 'union' (Latin 'unio', oneness, unity) + '-ize', a verb-forming suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02439085-v
Synset POS: v
