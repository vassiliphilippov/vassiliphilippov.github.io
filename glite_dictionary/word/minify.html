to minify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪnəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something smaller in size or amount; to reduce or lessen.
make smaller; reduce

Examples

Web developers often minify JavaScript files to improve website loading times.
The company decided to minify its operations due to decreased demand.
By removing unnecessary code, you can minify the file size considerably.

Synset Examples

He decreased his staff

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in size or amount
  • Synonym: to reduce - to make smaller in size, amount, or number

Etymology

From Latin 'minificare', from 'minimus' (smallest) + '-fy' (to make).
