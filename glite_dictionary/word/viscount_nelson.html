Viscount Nelson

1. PROPN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊnt ˈnɛlsən / (AmE), / ˈvaɪkaʊnt ˈnɛlsən / (BrE)
A title given to Horatio Nelson, the English admiral who defeated Napoleon's French fleets and died at the Battle of Trafalgar.
English admiral, defeated French at Trafalgar

Examples

Viscount Nelson is celebrated for his victory at the Battle of Trafalgar in 1805.
The statue of Viscount Nelson stands atop Nelson's Column in Trafalgar Square, London.
Many naval strategies owe their origins to Viscount Nelson's leadership and tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nelson - reference to Admiral Lord Nelson, same individual

Etymology

'Viscount' is a British noble title from Old French *visconte*, from Medieval Latin *vicecomes* (deputy count); 'Nelson' is the family name.
