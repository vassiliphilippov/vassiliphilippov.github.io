to dismember

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈmɛmbər / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
to divide or cut up something, especially a body or organization, into parts; to separate into pieces, often violently or forcibly
to divide into parts, cut up

Examples

The rebels threatened to dismember the statue as a symbol of resistance.
After the company was acquired, its divisions were dismembered and sold off individually.
The ancient kingdom was gradually dismembered by invading forces.

Synset Examples

our department was dismembered when our funding dried up
The Empire was discerped after the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismantle - to take apart a structure or machine
  • Synonym: fragment - to break into smaller parts

Etymology

From Old French desmembrer, from Late Latin dismembrare (dis- 'apart' + membrum 'limb').

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈmɛmbɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈmembə / (BrE)
to cut or pull off the limbs of a person or animal; to divide a body into separate parts
cut off the limbs of a body

Examples

The tiger dismembered the tourist in a matter of minutes.
Forensic experts suspect that the victim was dismembered after death.
The butcher skillfully dismembered the animal for meat processing.

Synset Examples

the tiger dismembered the tourist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mutilate - to inflict serious damage on a body, especially by cutting or tearing
  • Synonym: butcher - to kill and cut up an animal or person, especially in a violent way

Etymology

From Old French 'desmembrier', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'membrum' (limb).
