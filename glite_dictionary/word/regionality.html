regionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːdʒəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌriːdʒəˈnalɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being characteristic of a particular region or district.
quality of being regional

Examples

The regionality of local dialects has fascinated linguists for decades.
Economic policies often fail to address the regionality of development challenges.
The festival aims to celebrate the regionality of its traditional cuisine.

Synset Examples

The party's regionality prevented it from winning a national election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionalism - Emphasis on regional characteristics or interests

Etymology

From 'regional' + suffix '-ity', after French 'régionalité'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.dʒəˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌriː.dʒəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being characteristic of or relating to a particular geographic region.
quality of being regional

Examples

The regionality of cuisine is evident in the variety of dishes found across different provinces.
Researchers are studying the regionality of air pollution to improve environmental policies.
The book explores the regionality of dialects throughout the country.

Synset Examples

In particular, the authors examine the link between the seasonality and regionality of the climate trends over the United States and the leading patterns of sea surface temperature (SST) variability, including a global warming (GW) pattern and a Pacific decadal variability (PDV) pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regional - relating to a particular geographic region

Etymology

Formed from 'region' + '-ality', after 'nationality'.
