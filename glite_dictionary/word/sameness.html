sameness

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being monotonous or lacking in variety, leading to dullness or routine
lack of variety; monotony

Examples

After several weeks, the sameness of the daily routine began to wear on the team.
She was frustrated by the sameness of meals served in the cafeteria.
Many people seek travel to escape the sameness of everyday life.

Synset Examples

he had never grown accustomed to the monotony of his work
he was sick of the humdrum of his fellow prisoners
he hated the sameness of the food the college served

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotony - lack of variety and interest; tedious repetition
  • Antonym: variety - the quality of being different or diverse

Etymology

From same + -ness, forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being the same.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being the same as something else; lack of variety or difference
the state of being the same; lack of difference

Examples

The sameness of their answers made it difficult to distinguish between the students.
Many people find comfort in the sameness of their daily routines.
Some critics complained about the sameness of the TV show's episodes.

Synset Examples

sameness of purpose kept them together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike
  • Antonym: difference - the state or quality of being unlike or dissimilar

Etymology

From same + -ness, an Old English suffix forming nouns denoting quality or state.
