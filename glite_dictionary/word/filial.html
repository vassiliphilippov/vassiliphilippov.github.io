filial

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪliəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪliəl / (BrE)
Relating to or designating generations following the parental generation, especially in biology and genetics.
of a generation after the parents

Examples

The genetic traits were studied across several filial generations.
Scientists refer to the first filial generation as F1 in Mendelian genetics.
Each subsequent filial generation exhibited new variations from the parents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parental - relating to the parent generation; in biology, referring to the original parents in a genetic cross

Etymology

From Latin 'filius' (son), 'filia' (daughter).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪliəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪliəl / (BrE)
relating to or suitable for a son or daughter; pertaining to the relationship between children and their parents.
relating to a son or daughter

Examples

She showed filial devotion by caring for her elderly parents every day.
The tradition emphasizes filial duty and respect towards one's elders.
His filial affection was clear in the way he supported his mother.

Synset Examples

filial respect

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parental - relating to a parent or parents

Etymology

From Latin 'filialis', from 'filius' (son) and 'filia' (daughter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01726916-a
Synset POS: a
