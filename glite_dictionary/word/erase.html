to erase

1. VERB

/ ɪˈreɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪs / (BrE)
to remove or delete information recorded digitally or magnetically, making it unrecoverable
remove digitally or magnetically recorded information

Examples

He accidentally erased all the photos from his camera's memory card.
Make sure to back up your work before you erase the hard drive.
The technician erased the confidential data from the computer before recycling it.

Synset Examples

Who erased the files from my hard disk?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delete - remove or eliminate data, usually from a digital medium
  • Antonym: to restore - to return deleted information to its original state

Etymology

From Latin eradere, meaning 'to scrape out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01001860-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈreɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪz / (BrE)
to remove something, especially writing or marks, by rubbing, wiping, or using a special tool
remove by rubbing or wiping

Examples

You should erase any incorrect answers before submitting your test.
She tried to erase the pencil marks from the page.
The technician will erase all data from the old computer before recycling it.

Synset Examples

Please erase the formula on the blackboard — it is wrong!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delete - to remove, especially digitally or from a written record
  • Synonym: remove - to take away or get rid of something
  • Antonym: to write - to create or put down marks or letters on a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'erasus', past participle of 'eradere' (to scrape out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01551500-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈreɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪs / (BrE)
to remove something completely from existence or from memory, especially so that no trace remains
remove completely from existence or memory

Examples

The regime tried to erase all evidence of the event from history books.
Painful memories can be difficult to erase completely from one's mind.
The eruption nearly erased the ancient city from the map.

Synset Examples

The Turks erased the Armenians in 1915

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe out - to destroy completely, remove all traces
  • Synonym: to obliterate - to destroy utterly, remove all trace
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something in existence or memory

Etymology

From Latin 'erasare', past participle of 'eradere' (to scrape out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00480322-v
Synset POS: v
