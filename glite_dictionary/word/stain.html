stain

1. VERB

/ steɪn / (AmE), / steɪn / (BrE)
to color or dye something with a liquid, often changing its surface color permanently
to color with a liquid dye or tint

Examples

They decided to stain the old wooden floor a deep cherry color.
You can stain the fabric to give it a unique appearance.
The craftsman carefully stained the glass for the church windows.

Synset Examples

Stain this table a beautiful walnut color
people knew how to stain glass a beautiful blue in the middle ages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - to color or stain with a dye
  • Antonym: bleach - to make something lighter or whiter in color

Etymology

From Old French *esteindre* (to dye, tinge), from Latin *tingere* (to dye, color).
