tablespoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪbəlˌspoʊn / (AmE), / ˈteɪbl̩ˌspəʊn / (BrE)
a large spoon, larger than a dessert spoon, used for serving food or for measuring an amount of an ingredient
large spoon for serving or measuring

Examples

She used a tablespoon to serve the soup into each bowl.
Add one tablespoon of sugar to the mixture and stir well.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of olive oil.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: teaspoon - a small spoon used for stirring tea or measuring small amounts
  • Degree Lower: dessert spoon - a spoon somewhat larger than a teaspoon, used for eating dessert

Etymology

table + spoon; originally referring to a spoon used at the dining table.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.bəlˌspoʊn / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.bəl.spuːn / (BrE)
an amount of substance equal to what a tablespoon can hold (approximately 15 milliliters); used as a unit of measurement in cooking and medicine
unit of measurement (about 15 ml)

Examples

The recipe calls for two tablespoons of olive oil.
She added a tablespoon of sugar to her tea.
Take one tablespoon of medicine after each meal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: teaspoon - a smaller unit of measure, about 5 ml
  • Degree Higher: cup - a larger unit of measure, usually about 240 ml
