individualism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dəˈvɪdʒ.u.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the quality of being individual; a belief in or emphasis on the importance of each person as distinct and independent from others
the quality of being individual

Examples

Her art celebrates individualism and the unique voice of each person.
The school encourages individualism rather than conformity.
He valued individualism and independence over group consensus.

Synset Examples

so absorbed by the movement that she lost all sense of individuality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individuality - the quality that makes one person or thing different from others

Etymology

From individual + -ism, modeled on French individualisme.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that government should not interfere in commercial or economic affairs, emphasizing personal and economic freedom.
belief in minimal government interference in commerce

Examples

Many economists argue that individualism leads to greater innovation and prosperity in the marketplace.
The political party's platform was strongly rooted in individualism and free enterprise.
Critics claim that unchecked individualism can increase social inequality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal governmental interference in economic affairs

Etymology

From French individualisme, from individu ('individual') + -isme ('-ism').

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that the individual is more important than the collective, emphasizing self-reliance and personal independence
belief in personal independence and self-reliance

Examples

She admired the country's emphasis on individualism over collective conformity.
The artist's strong sense of individualism was evident in every piece of her work.
Critics argue that extreme individualism can weaken social bonds and community values.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual in it

Etymology

From French *individualisme*, from individu (‘individual’) + -isme (‘-ism’).
