introvert

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrəˌvɜrt / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəˌvɜːt / (BrE)
a person whose personality is characterized by inward focus, preferring solitary activities and often feeling drained by social interaction
a person who prefers solitude to socializing

Examples

Although she is an introvert, Maria enjoys spending time with a few close friends.
As an introvert, he finds large parties overwhelming and prefers quiet evenings at home.
Many introverts excel in roles that require deep concentration and minimal social interaction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrovert - a person who is outgoing and energized by social interaction
  • Degree Higher: ambivert - a person whose personality has a balance of introvert and extrovert features

Etymology

From Latin *introvertere*, meaning 'to turn inward' (*intro-* 'inward' + *vertere* 'to turn').

2. VERB

/ ˈɪntrəˌvɜrt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.trə.vɜːt / (BrE)
(of an organ, structure, etc.) to fold or turn inward upon itself.
fold or turn inward (technical use)
medical

Examples

During surgery, the surgeon may need to introvert the edge of the wound for proper healing.
Certain sea creatures can introvert their feeding organs for protection.
In the experiment, the scientist observed the tissue introvert under specific conditions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invaginate - to turn or fold inward, especially in biological contexts
  • Antonym: to evert - to turn outward or inside out

Etymology

From Latin introvertere: intro- (inward) + vertere (to turn).

3. VERB

/ ˈɪntrəˌvɝt / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəˌvɜːt / (BrE)
to turn (one's thoughts or feelings) inward; to focus attention on one's own inner life or emotions rather than external events.
turn thoughts or feelings inward

Examples

After the argument, she introverted her emotions and spoke very little.
Some people tend to introvert their worries rather than express them openly.
He introverted his feelings, making it difficult for others to understand what he was going through.

Synset Examples

He introverted his feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to internalize - to make (attitudes or behavior) part of one's nature by learning
  • Antonym: extravert - to direct (thoughts or energy) outward rather than inward

Etymology

From Latin introvertere, from intro- (inward) + vertere (to turn).
