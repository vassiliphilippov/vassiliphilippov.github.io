tenderizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.dəˌraɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.dəˌraɪ.zə / (BrE)
a substance, typically an enzyme or chemical, applied to meat to make it softer and easier to chew
substance that softens meat

Examples

Add a little tenderizer to the steak before grilling to help break down the tough fibers.
Some chefs prefer to use natural tenderizer like papaya extract.
Be careful not to use too much tenderizer, or the meat may become mushy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meat tenderizer - a tool or substance used to soften meat
  • Synonym: marinade - a seasoned liquid used to soak food for flavor and tenderness

Etymology

From 'tender' (soft, easy to chew) + '-izer' (agent noun suffix).
