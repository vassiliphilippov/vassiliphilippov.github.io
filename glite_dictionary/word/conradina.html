Conradina

1. NOUN

/ kɑn.rəˈdiː.nə / (AmE), / kɒn.rəˈdiː.nə / (BrE)
a small genus of aromatic shrubs native to the southeastern United States, belonging to the mint family
genus of aromatic shrubs (southeastern US)

Examples

Botanists conducted a survey to locate populations of Conradina in the wild.
The leaves of Conradina release a distinctive, pleasant aroma when crushed.
Conradina species are an important part of the coastal ecosystem in parts of Florida.

Etymology

Named after the German botanist Solomon White Conrad.

2. NOUN

a small genus of aromatic shrubs native to the southeastern United States, in the mint family
a genus of aromatic shrubs (mint family)

Examples

Several species of Conradina are endemic to Florida's sandy scrublands.
The spicy scent of Conradina leaves is distinctive and pleasant.
Botanists are studying how Conradina adapts to fire-prone environments.

Etymology

Named after Solomon White Conrad (1779–1831), an American geologist and botanist.
