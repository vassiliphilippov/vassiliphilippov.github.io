Ledum palustre

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdəm pəˈlʌstri / (AmE), / ˈliːdəm pəˈlʌstri / (BrE)
A bog shrub native to northern and central Europe, eastern Siberia, Korea, and Japan, with aromatic leaves traditionally used to brew tea.
a bog shrub native to northern Eurasia

Examples

Ledum palustre grows abundantly in peat bogs across Scandinavia and Siberia.
The leaves of Ledum palustre have been used traditionally to brew herbal teas.
Scientists are studying the medicinal properties of Ledum palustre in northern Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Labrador tea - common name for Ledum palustre and related species

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'Ledum' (the genus name) and 'palustre' (of marshes).
