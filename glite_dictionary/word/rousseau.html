Rousseau

1. PROPN

/ ruːˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ruːˈsəʊ / (BrE)
(1712–1778) A French philosopher and writer whose ideas about the natural goodness of humans and social contract influenced the French Revolution.
French philosopher and writer (1712–1778)

Examples

Many Enlightenment thinkers, such as Rousseau, challenged traditional notions of government and society.
The concept of the social contract is most famously associated with Rousseau.
In her essay, she compared the views of Rousseau and Voltaire on education.

Etymology

French family name; Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712–1778) is the most notable bearer.
