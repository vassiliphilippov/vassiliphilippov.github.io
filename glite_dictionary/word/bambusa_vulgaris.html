Bambusa vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ bæmˈbuːsə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / bamˈbuːsə vʌlˈɡɛːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of bamboo with vigorous growth and thin-walled culms, usually striped green and yellow, widely cultivated in tropical regions.
species of highly vigorous bamboo

Examples

The tall clumps of Bambusa vulgaris provided much-needed shade in the garden.
Many landscapes in tropical countries feature Bambusa vulgaris for its ornamental value.
The rapid growth of Bambusa vulgaris makes it a popular choice for erosion control.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Bambusa' and species epithet 'vulgaris' meaning 'common'.
