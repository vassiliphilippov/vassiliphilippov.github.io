condescension

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːndəˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
an attitude or manner of behaving that shows you consider yourself more intelligent or important than someone else, often expressed by talking down to them
patronizing attitude or behavior

Examples

The manager's condescension was evident in the way he explained the simplest details to his team.
She responded to his condescension with a calm but firm correction.
Many people found the politician's condescension towards the audience insulting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patronizing - showing an apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From Late Latin 'condescensio', from Latin 'condescendere' (to stoop together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːndəˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
an attitude or behavior that shows you think you are more important or intelligent than other people, often by being patronizing
patronizing attitude or behavior

Examples

She addressed the staff with barely concealed condescension, making everyone feel uncomfortable.
His condescension was evident in the way he explained even simple ideas as if to children.
Carol resented the condescension in his voice whenever he offered to help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patronizing - speaking or behaving in a way that shows you think you are superior
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of not thinking you are better than others

Etymology

From Late Latin 'condescensio', from 'condescendere' (to stoop, yield politely).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.dɪˈsen.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈsen.ʃən / (BrE)
a manner or attitude of behaving as if one is superior to others, often by displaying kindness or courtesy toward those considered of lower status
patronizing attitude or behavior

Examples

There was a hint of condescension in his tone when he addressed the interns.
Her condescension made the staff feel uncomfortable rather than grateful.
Despite his best efforts, his condescension was obvious to everyone in the meeting.

Synset Examples

the queen's condescension was intended to make us feel comfortable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of being humble or having a modest opinion of one's own importance

Etymology

From Latin 'condescendere' (to stoop, yield politely) via Late Latin and Old French.
