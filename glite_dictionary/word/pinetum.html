pinetum

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈniːtəm / (AmE), / paɪˈniːtəm / (BrE)
an area, often a part of a botanical garden or arboretum, where pine trees and other conifers are grown for scientific or ornamental purposes
plantation of pine or conifer trees

Examples

The botanical garden features a beautiful pinetum with rare species of conifers.
Many researchers visit the pinetum each year to study the diverse pine trees.
The pinetum provides a tranquil setting for visitors to walk among towering pines.

Etymology

From Latin pinus (pine) + -etum (a place planted with), meaning a planting of pines.
