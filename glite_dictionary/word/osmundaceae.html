Osmundaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsmənˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌɒzmənˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of ferns widely distributed in temperate and tropical regions, known as the royal fern family.
a family of ferns (royal fern family)
medical

Examples

Several species within the Osmundaceae family are cultivated as ornamental plants.
The Osmundaceae are recognized for their large, feathery fronds and distinctive sporangia.
Paleobotanists study fossilized Osmundaceae to understand fern evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant of the class Polypodiopsida

Etymology

From the genus name Osmunda, plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae (from Latin, denoting 'family').

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑsmənˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɒzmʊnˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of ferns widely distributed in temperate and tropical regions, including the royal ferns
a family of widely distributed ferns

Examples

The Osmundaceae are recognized for their thick, fleshy roots and tall, elegant fronds.
Several species in the Osmundaceae family are prized by gardeners for their ornamental value.
Fossils show that members of the Osmundaceae have existed since the Triassic period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Osmunda - a genus of ferns within the family Osmundaceae

Etymology

From the type genus Osmunda, with standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
