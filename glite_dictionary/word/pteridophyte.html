pteridophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrɪdəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪdəʊˌfaɪt / (BrE)
A plant belonging to a group that has vascular tissue and reproduces by spores, including ferns and related species.
a vascular plant reproducing by spores
medical

Examples

Many pteridophytes thrive in moist, shady forests where their spores can easily spread.
Unlike flowering plants, pteridophytes do not produce seeds or flowers.
Botanists study pteridophyte fossils to learn about the evolution of vascular plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a common type of pteridophyte

Etymology

From Greek 'pteris' (fern) + 'phyton' (plant).
