inactivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ækˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ækˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of not doing anything; lack of activity or movement
lack of activity or movement

Examples

After months of inactivity, the machinery began to rust.
A period of inactivity can affect both physical and mental health.
The doctor advised her to avoid prolonged inactivity during her recovery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: activity - the condition of being active or doing something; movement or action

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'activity', from Latin 'activitas'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnækˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
a tendency or state of not being active; lack of movement, action, or activity
lack of activity or movement

Examples

After months of inactivity, she found it difficult to start exercising again.
Too much inactivity can lead to health problems like obesity.
Their inactivity during the crisis was widely criticized.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laziness - unwillingness to work or use energy
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active; doing something

Etymology

From Latin inactivitas (lack of action), from in- (not) + actus (act, action).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ækˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ækˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state of being inactive or not engaging in any physical or mental activity.
lack of activity; idleness

Examples

Long periods of inactivity can lead to health problems.
The job's repetitive nature often resulted in long stretches of inactivity.
After the holidays, she struggled to shake off her inactivity and get back to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idleness - the state of being idle; not working or active
  • Antonym: activity - the condition of being active or engaged

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'activity'.
