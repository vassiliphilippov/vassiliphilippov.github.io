argentite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdʒənˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːdʒəntaɪt / (BrE)
a dark, metallic mineral consisting of silver sulfide, Ag₂S, and serving as an important ore of silver
a silver ore mineral (silver sulfide)

Examples

The miners discovered a rich vein of argentite deep within the old silver mine.
Geologists identified the dark crystals as argentite based on their composition and metallic luster.
Large deposits of argentite were crucial to the town's rapid economic growth in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acanthite - a mineral form of silver sulfide (low-temperature form)

Etymology

From Latin 'argentum' (silver) + '-ite' (mineral suffix).
