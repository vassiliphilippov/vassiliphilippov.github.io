Tamiasciurus douglasi

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæmiəˈskaɪərəs duˈɡlɑːsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌtæmiəˈskaɪərəs duˈɡlæsiː / (BrE)
A species of squirrel native to the far western United States, commonly known as the Douglas squirrel.
species of squirrel (Douglas squirrel)

Examples

The Tamiasciurus douglasi is commonly found in coniferous forests along the Pacific coast.
Unlike the eastern gray squirrel, the Tamiasciurus douglasi is smaller and more vocal.
Researchers studied the food storage behavior of the Tamiasciurus douglasi during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas squirrel - Common name for Tamiasciurus douglasi, a squirrel species of the western U.S.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Tamiasciurus from Greek 'tamias' (steward) + 'sciurus' (squirrel); douglasi named after Scottish botanist David Douglas.
