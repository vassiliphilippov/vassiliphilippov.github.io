kilovolt-ampere

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪloʊvoʊlt ˈæmpɪr / (AmE), / ˌkɪləʊvɒlt ˈæmpɪə / (BrE)
a unit of apparent power in an electrical circuit equal to 1,000 volt-amperes (VA)
unit of 1,000 volt-amperes (electrical power)

Examples

The generator has a capacity of 500 kilovolt-amperes, making it suitable for large-scale industrial use.
Engineers measured the load in kilovolt-amperes to determine the required transformer size.
Most hospital backup systems are rated at several hundred kilovolt-amperes to ensure continuous power supply.

Etymology

From kilo- (thousand) + volt (after Italian physicist Alessandro Volta) + ampere (after French physicist André-Marie Ampère).
