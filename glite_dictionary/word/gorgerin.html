gorgerin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔrdʒərɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdʒərɪn / (BrE)
the molding or ring located at the top of a column, just below the capital
molding at the top of a column

Examples

The architect restored the damaged gorgerin to preserve the column's original design.
Experts carefully cleaned centuries-old dust from the gorgerin during the cathedral's renovation.
The ornate gorgerin distinguished the Corinthian columns from simpler styles.

Etymology

From French *gorgerin* (neck piece), from *gorge* (throat).
