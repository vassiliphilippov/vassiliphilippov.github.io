hematin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːmətɪn / (AmE), / ˈhiːmətɪn / (BrE)
a complex red pigment containing iron, formed from hemoglobin and involved in oxygen transport.
iron-containing red pigment from hemoglobin
medical

Examples

The levels of hematin can be measured in laboratory analyses of blood.
Some medical treatments rely on the synthesis of hematin to address certain disorders.
Researchers studied the binding properties of hematin with oxygen molecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heme - iron-containing component of hemoglobin
  • Degree Higher: hemoglobin - oxygen-carrying protein in red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'haimatinos' (of blood), from 'haima' (blood).
