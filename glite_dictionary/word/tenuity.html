tenuity

1. NOUN

/ təˈnuːəti / (AmE), / tɪˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being very thin or slender; slightness of thickness or diameter.
the thinness or slenderness of something

Examples

The tenuity of the thread made it nearly invisible against the fabric.
Engineers had to account for the tenuity of the metal wires in the experiment.
She admired the tenuity of the artist's brushstrokes on the delicate canvas.

Synset Examples

the tenuity of a hair
the thinness of a rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinness - the state of being thin in dimension
  • Antonym: thickness - the state of being thick in dimension

Etymology

From Latin 'tenuitas', from 'tenuis' meaning 'thin'.

2. NOUN

/ təˈnuː.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / tɪˈnjuː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very thin, fine, or rare; a condition of low density or slightness
the quality of being thin or rare

Examples

The tenuity of the upper atmosphere makes breathing impossible without an oxygen supply.
She admired the tenuity of the silk fabric, so delicate that it almost seemed transparent.
Due to the tenuity of the evidence, the case was dismissed for lack of substantial proof.

Synset Examples

the tenuity of the upper atmosphere

Related Terms

  • Antonym: density - the degree of compactness of a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'tenuitas', from 'tenuis' meaning 'thin' or 'slender'.

3. NOUN

/ təˈnuːəti / (AmE), / tɪˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being thin, slight, or lacking in intensity or substance
thinness; lack of substance or intensity

Examples

The tenuity of the fabric made it unsuitable for winter clothing.
She was struck by the tenuity of his arguments during the debate.
A shrill yet sweet tenuity of voice filled the silent chapel.

Synset Examples

a shrill yet sweet tenuity of voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinness - the state of being thin
  • Antonym: substance - physical material or solidness

Etymology

From Latin tenuis (thin) via Late Latin tenuitas (thinness, rarity).
