incorrupted

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (BrE)
Not spoiled, contaminated, or morally perverted; pure and untainted.
not corrupted; pure
archaic

Examples

The manuscript was preserved in an incorrupted state through the centuries.
She was admired for her incorrupted honesty and unblemished character.
They strove to maintain an incorrupted version of the ancient ritual.

Synset Examples

faultless logic
speaks impeccable French
timing and technique were immaculate
an immaculate record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncorrupted - not corrupted or spoiled
  • Antonym: corrupted - spoiled or morally perverted

Etymology

From Latin incorruptus, from in- (not) + corruptus (spoiled, depraved).
