elocutionary

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛləˈkjuːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌɛləˈkjuːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to the art or style of speaking in public, especially with clear pronunciation and expressive delivery
pertaining to elocution or public speaking

Examples

The judge praised her elocutionary skills during the competition.
He attended an elocutionary course to improve his public speaking.
Her elocutionary recital captivated the audience with clear pronunciation and dramatic gestures.

Synset Examples

elocutionary recitals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elocution - the art of clear and expressive speech, especially in public speaking

Etymology

From Latin *elocutio* (speaking out) + English suffix -ary.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛləˈkjuːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌɛl.əˈkjuː.ʃən.ər.i / (BrE)
(of a style of speaking) excessively formal, ornate, or embellished in delivery.
overly ornate style of speaking

Examples

Her elocutionary manner distracted from the content of her speech.
The actor delivered the monologue in a highly elocutionary fashion.
Critics described his presentation as elocutionary, lacking natural warmth.

Synset Examples

an elocutionary Oxonian delivery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetorical - relating to speech that is elaborate or persuasive

Etymology

From late Latin 'elocutionarius', from 'elocutio' meaning 'speaking out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00074543-s
Synset POS: s
