lychee

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.tʃi / (BrE)
A small, round fruit with a thin, rough shell, sweet translucent flesh, and a single large seed, native to China and often eaten fresh or dried.
a sweet, round fruit native to China

Examples

I love eating fresh lychee on hot summer days.
She added some sliced lychee to the tropical fruit salad.
You can find dried lychee in many Asian grocery stores.

Etymology

From Cantonese lai6 zi1, via Chinese 荔枝 (lìzhī).
