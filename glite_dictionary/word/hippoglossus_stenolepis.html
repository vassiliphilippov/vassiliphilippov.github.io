Hippoglossus stenolepis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈɡlɑsəs ˌstɛnoʊˈlɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs ˌstɛnəʊˈlɛpɪs / (BrE)
a large species of righteye flounder native to the northern Pacific Ocean, commonly known as the Pacific halibut.
species of Pacific halibut

Examples

The commercial fishing industry depends heavily on the Hippoglossus stenolepis for its high-quality meat.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Hippoglossus stenolepis in the Bering Sea.
The Hippoglossus stenolepis can grow to an impressive size and is popular among sport fishermen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific halibut - common name for Hippoglossus stenolepis

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'glossa' (tongue), and Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'lepis' (scale).
