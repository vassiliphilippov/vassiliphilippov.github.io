gloomful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡluːmfəl / (AmE), / ˈɡluːmfʊl / (BrE)
depressingly dark or producing a sense of gloom; gloomy.
depressingly dark; gloomy (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The old, gloomful mansion stood isolated at the edge of the woods.
A gloomful silence filled the abandoned church as dusk fell.
She wandered through the gloomful corridors, haunted by memories of happier days.

Synset Examples

the gloomy forest
the glooming interior of an old inn
‘gloomful’ is archaic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or dismal; producing a feeling of sadness or depression
  • Antonym: cheerful - full of cheer; bright and pleasant
