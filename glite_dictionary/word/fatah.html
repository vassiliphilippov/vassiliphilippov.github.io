Fatah

1. PROPN

/ fəˈtɑː / (AmE), / fəˈtɑː / (BrE)
a Palestinian political and military organization founded in 1958, aiming to establish a Palestinian state
Palestinian political and military organization

Examples

Fatah has played a central role in Palestinian politics since its founding in 1958.
Members of Fatah participated in peace negotiations during the 1990s.
The rivalry between Fatah and Hamas has shaped recent developments in the region.

Synset Examples

al-Fatah carried out numerous acts of international terrorism in western Europe and the Middle East in the 1970s

Etymology

From Arabic 'fatḥ', meaning 'conquest' or 'opening'.
