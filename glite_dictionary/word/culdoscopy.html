culdoscopy

1. NOUN

/ kʌlˈdɑːskəpi / (AmE), / kʌlˈdɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical examination of a woman's pelvic organs using an endoscope inserted through the vagina
endoscopic examination of female pelvic organs
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a culdoscopy to investigate unexplained pelvic pain.
A culdoscopy allows surgeons to directly view the uterus and surrounding tissues.
After the culdoscopy, the patient was advised to rest for a day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laparoscopy - endoscopic examination of the abdominal cavity

Etymology

From Latin 'culdus' (hollow, as in 'cul-de-sac') and Greek 'skopein' (to look, examine).
