James Dewey Watson

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈduː.i ˈwɑːt.sən / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdjuː.i ˈwɒt.sən / (BrE)
(born 1928) An American geneticist who, with Francis Crick, discovered the double-helix structure of DNA in 1953.
American geneticist; co-discoverer of DNA structure

Examples

James Dewey Watson was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1962.
Many biology textbooks credit James Dewey Watson and Francis Crick with the discovery of the DNA double helix.
Few scientists have influenced genetics as profoundly as James Dewey Watson.

Related Terms
