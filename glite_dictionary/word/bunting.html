bunting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌntɪŋ / (BrE)
A loosely woven fabric, usually made of cotton or wool, used for making flags, banners, and festive decorations.
fabric used for flags or decorations

Examples

Red, white, and blue bunting decorated the parade route for the national holiday.
The store used colorful bunting to attract customers to its grand opening.
After the match, the stadium was draped with team bunting in celebration.

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *bontyn* (to make up, to ornament).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Any of various small seed-eating songbirds of the family Emberizidae, found in Europe and North America.
small seed-eating songbird

Examples

We saw a bright yellow bunting perched on the fence near the field.
The bunting is known for its cheerful song during spring mornings.
Several buntings migrate south for the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparrow - small, plump seed-eating songbird, often brown or grey

Etymology

From Old French 'bontin' (a kind of bird), of uncertain origin.
