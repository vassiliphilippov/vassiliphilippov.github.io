wreath

1. NOUN

/ riːθ / (AmE), / riːθ / (BrE)
An arrangement of flowers, leaves, or stems in the shape of a ring, used as a decoration or symbol, especially at ceremonies or funerals.
circular floral or foliage arrangement

Examples

A beautiful wreath of roses hung on their front door during the holidays.
The family placed a wreath on the grave in memory of their loved one.
She made a homemade wreath of wildflowers to celebrate spring.

Etymology

From Old English *writha* (band, ring), related to *wrīthan* (to twist).
