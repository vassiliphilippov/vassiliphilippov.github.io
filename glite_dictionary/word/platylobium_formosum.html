Platylobium formosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpleɪtɪˌloʊbiəm fɔrˈmoʊsəm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈləʊbiəm fɔːˈməʊsəm / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to Australia and Tasmania, with almost heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow, pea-like flowers, followed by flat, winged seed pods
Australian evergreen shrub with yellow, pea-like flowers

Examples

The Platylobium formosum adds a splash of yellow color to native Australian gardens every spring.
Researchers studied the seed dispersal mechanisms of Platylobium formosum in Tasmanian forests.
Hikers often admire the Platylobium formosum for its distinctively shaped leaves and bright flowers.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name 'Platylobium' from Greek 'platy-' (broad) + 'lobos' (lobe), 'formosum' from Latin meaning 'beautiful' or 'well-formed'.
