to decalcify

1. VERB

/ diˈkælsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈkɑːlsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove or lose calcium or calcium compounds, especially from bones or teeth
remove or lose calcium
medical

Examples

The bones began to decalcify due to the disease.
Vinegar can cause shells to decalcify over time.
If teeth decalcify, they become more susceptible to cavities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to calcify - to become or make hard by the deposit of calcium salts

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (removal) + 'calx, calcis' (lime) + '-ify' (make or become).

2. VERB

/ diˈkælsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diːˈkælsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove calcium or lime from something, especially by a chemical process
remove calcium or lime

Examples

The technician used an acid to decalcify the water pipes before repairs could begin.
Scientists decalcify bone samples to prepare them for microscopic study.
Vinegar can help decalcify your kettle if mineral deposits accumulate over time.

Synset Examples

decalcify the rock

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to calcify - to become or cause to become inflexible or hardened, especially by deposition of calcium salts

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (removal) + 'calx' (lime) + '-ify' (to make or become).
