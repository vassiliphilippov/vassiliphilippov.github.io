whiff

1. NOUN

/ wɪf / (AmE), / wɪf / (BrE)
a brief, light gust or puff of air, smoke, or scent
a light gust or puff of air

Examples

A whiff of fresh air drifted through the open window.
He caught a whiff of smoke as he entered the room.
A sudden whiff of perfume made her look up.

Etymology

From Middle English *weffe* (gust of wind), probably imitative.

2. VERB

/ wɪf / (AmE), / wɪf / (BrE)
to smoke (a cigar, pipe, etc.) and exhale strongly
smoke and exhale strongly

Examples

He took a deep whiff from his pipe and blew smoke into the air.
She liked to whiff cigars on the porch after dinner.
The old man slowly whiffed his favorite tobacco, savoring every puff.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ wɪf / (AmE), / wɪf / (BrE)
(in baseball) to strike out by swinging and missing the pitch charged as the third strike.
strike out in baseball by missing the pitch

Examples

The batter whiffed on the final pitch and the game was over.
He has a tendency to whiff under pressure, striking out at key moments.
She whiffed three times in the game, missing every third pitch she faced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike out - (in baseball) to be put out by failing to hit three pitches

Etymology

Of imitative origin; sense extended in U.S. baseball slang from 'to strike at and miss'.

Source: WordNet

(Baseball) A strikeout in which the batter swings at and misses the ball for the third strike.

Examples

7. VERB

/ wɪf / (AmE), / wɪf / (BrE)
to move, drive, or carry something as if by a puff or gust of air
move or carry as by a puff of air

Examples

The sudden breeze whiffed the curtain aside, letting sunlight spill into the room.
A cool wind whiffed the scent of jasmine across the patio.
As she opened the window, the draft whiffed his papers off the desk.

Synset Examples

The gust of air whiffed away the clouds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow - to move something by the force of air

Etymology

From Middle English 'whiffen' (to move lightly and quickly), imitative of a light puff of air.
