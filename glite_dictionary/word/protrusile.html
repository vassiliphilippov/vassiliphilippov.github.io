protrusile

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈtruːsəl / (AmE), / prəˈtruːsaɪl / (BrE)
(of a body part) capable of being thrust forward or extended, especially used of anatomical parts such as the tongue or jaw.
able to be thrust forward (anatomy)

Examples

The frog uses its protrusile tongue to catch insects quickly.
Many fish have a protrusile jaw, which allows them to extend their mouth outward.
Some birds possess protrusile beaks that help them access food hidden in crevices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protractile - (of a part) able to be extended

Etymology

From Latin 'protrus-' (thrust forward) + English '-ile'.
