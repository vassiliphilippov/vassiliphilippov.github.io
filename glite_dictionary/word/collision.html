collision

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / kəˈlɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more ideas, interests, or goals are in strong disagreement or conflict with each other
conflict of ideas or interests

Examples

The meeting revealed a collision of values between the traditionalists and the progressives.
There was an inevitable collision of priorities during the budget discussions.
The collision of their aspirations led to a prolonged negotiation.

Synset Examples

a collision of interests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accord in opinion or feeling

Etymology

From Latin 'collisio', from 'collidere' (to strike together).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪʒən / (AmE), / kəˈlɪʒən / (BrE)
an accident in which two or more moving objects hit each other violently, often causing damage or injury
accident from violent impact

Examples

The highway was closed for hours after a fatal collision between two trucks.
Investigators are still determining what caused the collision at the busy intersection.
Emergency crews arrived quickly at the scene of the collision to assist the injured.

Synset Examples

three passengers were killed in the collision
the collision of the two ships resulted in a serious oil spill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - a violent accident in which a vehicle hits something

Etymology

From Latin 'collisionem' (a dashing together), from 'collidere' (to dash together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07316161-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪʒən / (AmE), / kəˈlɪʒən / (BrE)
a brief event in which two or more bodies come into contact, often resulting in a change of motion or direction, especially in physics.
brief impact between bodies

Examples

The scientist recorded the collision of the protons in the accelerator.
A collision between two school buses was reported, but fortunately no one was injured.
Careful calculations can predict the outcome of a collision between particles.

Synset Examples

the collision of the particles resulted in an exchange of energy and a change of direction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impact - a forceful contact or striking between bodies

Etymology

From Latin 'collisio', from 'collidere' meaning 'to strike together'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07317160-n
Synset POS: n
