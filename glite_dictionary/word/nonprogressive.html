nonprogressive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.prəˈɡrɛs.ɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.prəˈɡrɛs.ɪv / (BrE)
Old-fashioned and not making or characterized by progress; resistant to change or innovation.
resistant to change; old-fashioned

Examples

The company's nonprogressive policies made it difficult to attract younger employees.
He was criticized for his nonprogressive views on technology and education reform.
In a rapidly changing industry, a nonprogressive mindset can be a significant disadvantage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - favoring change or innovation; advancing

Etymology

From non- (not) + progressive, from Latin progressus (advance).
