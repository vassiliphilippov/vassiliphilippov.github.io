prickleback

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪk.əlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈprɪk.əl.bæk / (BrE)
a small, elongated fish found in shallow northern seas, characterized by a long dorsal fin consisting entirely of spines
small elongated fish with spiny dorsal fin

Examples

The tide pools are home to several prickleback species hiding among the rocks.
A marine biologist studied the distinctive spiny dorsal fin of the prickleback.
Fishermen in northern coastal waters often encounter pricklebacks while collecting bait.

Etymology

From 'prickle' (sharp point) + 'back', referring to the spiny dorsal fin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈprɪkəlˌbæk / (BrE)
A small spiny-backed fish, mostly scaleless, found in northern fresh and coastal waters, known for its elaborate courtship behavior.
a small spiny-backed fish

Examples

The prickleback is notable for its ability to survive in both fresh and saltwater environments.
Researchers observed the elaborate mating display of the male prickleback in the shallow stream.
Despite its small size, the prickleback is quite territorial and aggressive when threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickleback - a small spiny-finned fish, closely related group

Etymology

From 'prickle' (sharp spine or thorn) + 'back', referring to the spiny back of the fish.
