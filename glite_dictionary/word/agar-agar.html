agar-agar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑɡɑr ˈɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡɑː ˈɑːɡə / (BrE)
A gel-like substance obtained from seaweed, used as a culture medium in laboratories and as a gelling agent in foods.
gelatinous extract of seaweed, gelling agent

Examples

The scientist used agar-agar to prepare the petri dishes for bacterial cultures.
Vegetarian desserts often substitute gelatin with agar-agar for a similar texture.
After heating the mixture, she added agar-agar to make the fruit jelly set properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelatin - a colorless, flavorless food ingredient, derived from collagen

Etymology

From Malay 'agar-agar', referring to a type of seaweed.
