Sterculia foetida

1. NOUN

/ stɚˈkjuːliə ˈfiːtɪdə / (AmE), / stɜːˈkjuːliə ˈfiːtɪdə / (BrE)
a large tropical tree of the Old World with foul-smelling orange-red flowers and red pods containing oil-rich edible seeds
tropical tree with pungent flowers and edible seeds

Examples

The Sterculia foetida produces a strong odor when its orange-red blossoms bloom in the tropics.
Seeds from the Sterculia foetida are sometimes roasted and eaten as a local delicacy.
Botanists studied the Sterculia foetida for its unusual flowers and oil-rich seeds.

Etymology

From the genus name Sterculia, after Latin 'Sterculius', Roman god of manure, and Latin 'foetida', meaning 'foul-smelling'.
