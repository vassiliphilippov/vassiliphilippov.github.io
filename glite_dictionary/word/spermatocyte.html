spermatocyte

1. NOUN

/ spɚˈmæt̬.əˌsaɪt / (AmE), / spəˈmæt.ə.saɪt / (BrE)
a cell produced in the male testes that undergoes division to form four spermatids during sperm development
cell in testes forming sperm
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the scientist observed a large spermatocyte undergoing division.
A spermatocyte develops into four spermatids as part of the process of sperm formation.
Abnormalities in spermatocyte development can lead to male infertility.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: spermatid - an immature male sex cell formed from a spermatocyte
  • Degree Higher: spermatozoon - mature male reproductive cell

Etymology

From Greek 'sperma' (seed) + 'kytos' (cell).
