Poeciliidae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌsɪl.iˈɪ.diː / (AmE), / pəʊˌsɪl.ɪˈɪ.dɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of small freshwater fishes, commonly known as topminnows, including guppies and mollies, found mainly in the Americas.
family of small freshwater fishes

Examples

Many popular aquarium fish, such as guppies and mollies, belong to the Poeciliidae family.
Scientists observed that species within the Poeciliidae display a wide range of coloration.
The Poeciliidae are native to the Americas but have been introduced to various habitats worldwide.

Etymology

From modern Latin Poeciliidae, based on Greek poikilos (variegated), the type genus being Poecilia.

2. NOUN

/ piːˌsɪliˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / piːˌsɪlɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small freshwater fishes, commonly called topminnows, including guppies and mollies, found mainly in the Americas
family of small freshwater fishes (topminnows)

Examples

The guppy is one of the most popular members of the Poeciliidae family among aquarium enthusiasts.
Several species within Poeciliidae are known for their ability to give birth to live young rather than laying eggs.
Scientists study Poeciliidae to understand patterns of evolution and adaptation in freshwater fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topminnow - any small live-bearing fish of the family Poeciliidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Poecilia; Poecilia from Greek poikilos (variegated, spotted).
