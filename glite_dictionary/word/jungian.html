Jungian

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋiən / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋiən / (BrE)
A follower or advocate of the theories of Carl Jung, the Swiss psychiatrist and psychoanalyst.
follower of Jung's theories

Examples

As a practicing Jungian, she often interpreted dreams using archetypes and symbolism.
Many Jungians believe that the unconscious mind is the key to personal growth.
He became a Jungian after reading extensively about psychoanalysis in his twenties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freudian - a follower of the theories of Sigmund Freud

Etymology

Formed from the name of Carl Jung (1875–1961), Swiss psychiatrist, plus the suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʊŋiən / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊŋiən / (BrE)
Relating to the theories or ideas of the Swiss psychologist Carl Jung, especially his concepts of the collective unconscious and archetypes.
relating to Carl Jung's psychology

Examples

Many therapists use Jungian principles to explore their patients' dreams.
Her artwork is clearly influenced by Jungian archetypes and symbolism.
A Jungian analysis often involves interpreting myths and cultural stories.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Freudian - Relating to the theories of Sigmund Freud, especially psychoanalysis

Etymology

From the name of Carl Jung (1875–1961), Swiss psychoanalyst, with the suffix -ian indicating relation.

Synset POS: a
