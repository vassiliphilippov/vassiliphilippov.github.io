timbered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
furnished with or made of wood or timbers; having wooden beams or structures.
made of or furnished with wood

Examples

The cottage had thick timbered walls that added to its rustic charm.
Many old European houses have beautifully timbered façades.
The architect designed a timbered ceiling for the new library.

Synset Examples

timbered walls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooden - made of wood

Etymology

From the noun 'timber' (wood prepared for building) plus the adjectival suffix '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02438383-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
(of land or an area) covered with growing trees; wooded.
covered with trees; wooded

Examples

The valley was densely timbered with a variety of pine and oak trees.
Much of the property consists of timbered land that attracts wildlife.
They camped on a timbered hillside overlooking the river.

Synset Examples

thickly timbered ridges clothed with loblolly pine and holly
hills timbered up to their summits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooded - covered with trees; forested

Etymology

From 'timber' (wooded land) + '-ed,' meaning provided with or covered by.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02585144-s
Synset POS: s
