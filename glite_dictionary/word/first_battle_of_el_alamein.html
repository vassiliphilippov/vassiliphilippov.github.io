First Battle of El Alamein

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝst ˌbætəl əv ˌɛl ˌæləˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌbætəl əv ˌɛl ˌæləˈmeɪn / (BrE)
a major World War II battle in 1942 in which British forces under Montgomery defeated German troops led by Rommel near El Alamein, Egypt
World War II battle in Egypt, 1942

Examples

The First Battle of El Alamein halted the Axis advance into Egypt during the summer of 1942.
Historians often cite the First Battle of El Alamein as a turning point in the North African campaign.
Montgomery’s tactics at the First Battle of El Alamein led to a significant Allied victory over Rommel’s forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Battle of El Alamein - the subsequent battle at El Alamein, also an Allied victory
  • Synonym: Battle of Stalingrad - major Eastern Front battle in World War II
