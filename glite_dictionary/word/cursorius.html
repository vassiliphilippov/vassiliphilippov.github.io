Cursorius

1. NOUN

/ kɜrˈsɔriəs / (AmE), / kɜːˈsɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds known as coursers, found in arid regions of Africa and Asia.
genus of birds called coursers

Examples

The genus Cursorius includes several species that inhabit open semi-deserts and grasslands.
Ornithologists observed a member of the Cursorius genus during their expedition in North Africa.
Bird guides often list Cursorius under the family Glareolidae.

Etymology

From Latin 'cursor' meaning 'runner', in reference to the birds' running habits.

2. NOUN

/ kərˈsɔriəs / (AmE), / kɜːˈsɔːriəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Glareolidae, commonly known as coursers, found in Africa and Asia.
a genus of courser birds

Examples

The Somali Cursorius is notable for its distinctive long legs and curved bill.
Species within the genus Cursorius are well adapted to dry, open habitats.
The naturalist was excited to spot a rare member of the Cursorius genus in the savanna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courser - a fast-running bird of open country

Etymology

From Latin *cursor* (runner), referring to the birds' running habits.
