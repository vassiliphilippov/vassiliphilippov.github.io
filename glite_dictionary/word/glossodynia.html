glossodynia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɑːsəˈdɪniə / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒsəˈdɪnɪə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by pain or burning sensations in the tongue, often without an obvious medical cause.
pain or burning in the tongue
medical

Examples

The patient complained of persistent glossodynia despite normal test results.
Treatments for glossodynia often focus on relieving symptoms rather than curing the underlying condition.
Stress and anxiety can sometimes contribute to the development of glossodynia.

Etymology

From Greek 'glōssa' (tongue) and 'odynē' (pain).
