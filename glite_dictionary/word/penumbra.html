penumbra

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnʌmbrə / (AmE), / pɪˈnʌmbrə / (BrE)
the partially shaded outer region of a shadow, especially the area between complete shadow (umbra) and full light, as seen in eclipses or around objects blocking light
partially shaded region around a shadow

Examples

During a solar eclipse, the penumbra causes a partial darkening, while the umbra results in total darkness.
The planet's penumbra extended far into space, creating a gentle gradient of shadow.
Through the telescope, we could see the subtle penumbra at the edge of the moon's shadow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: umbra - the fully shaded inner region of a shadow

Etymology

From Latin 'paene' (almost) + 'umbra' (shadow).
