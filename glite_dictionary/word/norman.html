2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔr mən / (AmE), / ˈnɔː mən / (BrE)
a person from Normandy, a region in northern France; especially used to refer to people from this area in historical contexts
person from Normandy, France

Examples

The Norman conquest of England in 1066 changed the country's history forever.
She traced her ancestry back to a Norman nobleman who came over with William the Conqueror.
The traditions of the Norman people still influence the culture of Normandy today.

From Old French *Normant*, from Medieval Latin *Normannus* ('Northman'), referring to the Norsemen who settled in Normandy.
