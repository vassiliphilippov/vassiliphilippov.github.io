Dryas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of small, low-growing flowering plants in the rose family (Rosaceae), commonly known as mountain avens, found in arctic and alpine regions.
a genus of mountain avens plants

Examples

The Dryas thrives in harsh alpine climates where few other plants can survive.
Many botanists study Dryas to understand how arctic tundra ecosystems function.
The delicate white flowers of the Dryas species carpet the mountain slopes in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain avens - common name for plants in the genus Dryas

Etymology

From Greek Dryas, a nymph associated with the woods, from 'drys' meaning 'oak'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ.əs / (BrE)
a genus of small evergreen shrubs in the rose family, found in arctic and alpine regions, known commonly as mountain avens.
genus of mountain avens shrubs

Examples

The Arctic tundra is home to several species of Dryas, which thrive in cold conditions.
Botanists studied Dryas to understand how plants adapt to extreme climates.
The white flowers of Dryas blanket the mountain slopes in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain avens - common name for plants in the genus Dryas

Etymology

From Greek dryas, meaning 'oak nymph', referencing the leaf shape resemblance to oak.
