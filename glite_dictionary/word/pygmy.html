Pygmy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmi / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmi / (BrE)
a member of any of various ethnic groups whose average adult height is unusually short, traditionally defined as less than five feet (about 1.5 meters)
a member of a very short-statured ethnic group

Examples

Many anthropologists have studied the forest-dwelling Pygmy populations of Central Africa.
The Pygmy communities are known for their intimate knowledge of the rainforest.
Some research focuses on traditional music and dances among different Pygmy groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: giant - a person of exceptional height

Etymology

From Greek 'pygmaios', meaning 'diminutive' or 'of a cubit’s length'.
