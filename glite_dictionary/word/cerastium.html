Cerastium

1. NOUN

/ səˈræst.i.əm / (AmE), / səˈrɑːs.ti.əm / (BrE)
A genus of small flowering plants in the pink family, commonly known as mouse-eared chickweed, with hairy leaves and small white flowers.
genus of plants (mouse-eared chickweed)

Examples

Cerastium is commonly found in meadows and grassy areas across Europe and North America.
Gardeners sometimes remove Cerastium to prevent it from spreading among cultivated plants.
The small white flowers of Cerastium make it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse-eared chickweed - common name for plants in the genus Cerastium

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn), referring to the horn-shaped seed capsules.

2. NOUN

/ səˈræstiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈrastɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the pink family (Caryophyllaceae), commonly known as mouse-eared chickweed, with small white flowers and hairy leaves.
a genus of plants (mouse-eared chickweed)

Examples

The fields were covered with blooming Cerastium in early spring.
Botanists classified the plant as a member of the genus Cerastium.
We found several varieties of Cerastium growing along the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickweed - a common name for several small-leaved plants in the Caryophyllaceae family

Etymology

From Greek *kerastion*, diminutive of *keras* (horn), referring to the shape of the seed capsule.
