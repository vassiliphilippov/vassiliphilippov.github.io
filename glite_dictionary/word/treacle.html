treacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriːkəl / (AmE), / ˈtriːkəl / (BrE)
excessive sweetness or sentimentality in writing or music, often to the point of cloying
excessive sentimentality (writing/music)

Examples

Critics dismissed the film's ending as pure treacle that undermined its earlier realism.
Her new novel is free from the treacle that characterized her previous works.
The speech was full of treacle and felt insincere to most of the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive expression of feelings of tenderness or nostalgia
  • Synonym: claptrap - language or ideas that are pretentious or insincerely sentimental
  • Synonym: saccharine - excessively sweet or sentimental

Etymology

Originally from Old French *triacle*, from Latin *theriaca* (antidote against poison), historically a medicinal syrup, later metaphorically used for excessive sweetness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtriːkəl / (AmE), / ˈtriːkəl / (BrE)
a thick, sweet syrup made from refined sugar, especially from sugar cane; commonly known as golden syrup in its pale form
sweet syrup from cane or sugar refining
british

Examples

She poured some treacle over her porridge for a sweet breakfast treat.
Traditional British recipes often call for treacle or golden syrup as an ingredient.
The baker drizzled treacle onto the freshly baked scones to add extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molasses - a thick, dark syrup produced during sugar refining

Etymology

From Old French *triacle*, from Latin *theriaca* (antidote against poison).
