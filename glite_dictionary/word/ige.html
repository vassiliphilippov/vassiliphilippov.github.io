IgE

1. NOUN

/ aɪ dʒi i / (AmE), / aɪ dʒiː iː / (BrE)
a type of antibody found mainly in the skin and mucous membranes, involved in allergic reactions and defense against parasites
type of antibody, involved in allergies
medical

Examples

Elevated levels of IgE are often seen in patients with seasonal allergies.
Doctors may order an IgE test to help diagnose certain allergic conditions.
IgE antibodies play an important role in the body’s immune response to parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - any of a class of proteins functioning as antibodies

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'immunoglobulin E', where 'immunoglobulin' is from Latin 'immunis' (exempt) and 'globulin'.
