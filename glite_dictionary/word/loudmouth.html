loudmouth

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊdˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈlaʊdˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
a person who talks too much or too loudly, especially in an indiscreet or offensive way
person who talks too much or indiscreetly

Examples

Nobody wanted to sit near the loudmouth at the meeting because he kept interrupting everyone.
The interview went badly after a loudmouth in the audience started heckling the speaker.
She was embarrassed by her friend's reputation as a loudmouth who couldn't keep a secret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blabbermouth - a person who talks indiscreetly or reveals secrets
  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, often excessively
  • Antonym: tight-lipped - unwilling to talk or answer questions, especially about something secret

Etymology

From 'loud' + 'mouth'; attested in American English since the early 20th century.
