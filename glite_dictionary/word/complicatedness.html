complicatedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmpləˌkeɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪˌkeɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being complicated; the state of being made up of many interconnected or puzzling parts.
the state of being complicated

Examples

The complicatedness of the instructions left everyone confused.
She was frustrated by the complicatedness of the tax forms.
We tried to reduce the complicatedness of the new system to make it user-friendly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being complex or intricate
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or easy to understand

Etymology

From 'complicated' plus the suffix '-ness', forming a noun.
