Diervilla

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɜrvɪlə / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːvɪlə / (BrE)
A small genus of low, deciduous shrubs known as bush honeysuckles, native to North America.
genus of shrubs; bush honeysuckles

Examples

Several species of Diervilla are commonly found in the woodlands of eastern North America.
Gardeners appreciate Diervilla for its hardiness and colorful autumn foliage.
The Diervilla produces clusters of yellow tubular flowers in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush honeysuckle - any shrub of the genus Diervilla; low North American honeysuckles

Etymology

Named after French surgeon and botanist N. Dierville (1658–1730), who first described the plant.
