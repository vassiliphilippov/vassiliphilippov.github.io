monk

1. NOUN

/ mʌŋk / (AmE), / mʌŋk / (BrE)
A man who is a member of a religious community living under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience, typically devoted to prayer, contemplation, and work.
male member of a religious community

Examples

The monk rose early every morning to attend prayers in the chapel.
During his travels, he visited a Tibetan monastery and met a Buddhist monk.
The monk dedicated his life to studying ancient manuscripts in the abbey.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nun - a woman who is a member of a religious community

Etymology

From Late Latin 'monachus', from Ancient Greek 'monakhos' (solitary, monk).
