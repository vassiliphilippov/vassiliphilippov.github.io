Tanzanian

1. NOUN

/ tænˈzeɪniən / (AmE), / tænˈzeɪniən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Tanzania.
native or inhabitant of Tanzania

Examples

She met a Tanzanian while traveling in East Africa.
Many Tanzanians speak both Swahili and English.
As a proud Tanzanian, he celebrates national holidays with his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tanzania - country in East Africa

Etymology

From the name of the country Tanzania + English suffix -an denoting nationality.

2. ADJ

/ tænˈzeɪniən / (AmE), / tanˈzɑːnɪən / (BrE)
Relating to Tanzania or its people.
of or relating to Tanzania

Examples

She enjoyed tasting traditional Tanzanian food during her visit to Dar es Salaam.
The Tanzanian government announced new education reforms this year.
He met several Tanzanian artists at the cultural fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tanzania - East African country

Etymology

From Tanzania, the name of the East African country, plus the English adjectival suffix -an.
