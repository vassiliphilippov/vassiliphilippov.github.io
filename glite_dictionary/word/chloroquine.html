chloroquine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊ.rəˌkwiːn / (AmE), / ˈklɒr.ə.kwiːn / (BrE)
A synthetic drug used to prevent and treat malaria and certain other diseases such as amebic dysentery and systemic lupus erythematosus.
antimalarial drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed chloroquine to treat her malaria infection after her trip to Africa.
Some strains of malaria have developed resistance to chloroquine over time.
Researchers are studying the effects of chloroquine on autoimmune diseases like lupus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinine - a natural antimalarial drug from cinchona bark

Etymology

From chloro- (relating to chlorine) + quin(ine), reflecting its chemical structure.
