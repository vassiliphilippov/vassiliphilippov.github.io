diversion

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a military attack or maneuver intended to distract the enemy and draw defense away from the principal point of attack
military distraction maneuver

Examples

The general ordered a diversion on the eastern flank to confuse the enemy.
By launching a diversion, they successfully drew attention away from the real assault.
The success of the mission depended on the effectiveness of the initial diversion.

Etymology

From Latin 'diversio' (a turning aside), via Old French 'diversion'.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
an activity that entertains, amuses, or distracts people from their usual concerns
entertaining or distracting activity

Examples

Chess was his favorite diversion after a long day at work.
The community center offers many diversions for local youth.
For many people, reading serves as a pleasant diversion from daily stress.

Synset Examples

scuba diving is provided as a diversion for tourists
for recreation he wrote poetry and solved crossword puzzles
drug abuse is often regarded as a form of recreation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recreation - an activity done for enjoyment when one is not working
  • Synonym: pastime - an activity that someone does regularly for enjoyment rather than work
  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort done to achieve a purpose or result

Etymology

From Latin divertō (to turn aside, divert) via Old French diversion.

3. NOUN

/ dəˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
an act or instance of turning aside from a course, activity, or concern; a deviation or detour
an act of turning aside or deviating

Examples

The accident caused a diversion from the main road, adding an extra hour to our journey.
His sudden interest in gardening was merely a diversion from his professional responsibilities.
During the meeting, the discussion made a diversion into unrelated topics.

Synset Examples

a diversion from the main highway
a digression into irrelevant details
a deflection from his goal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • Synonym: deflection - the action of turning aside from a straight course
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on with the current course

Etymology

From Latin 'divertere' (to turn aside).
