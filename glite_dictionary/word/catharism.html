Catharism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkæθərɪzəm / (BrE)
A Christian movement in southern France during the 12th and 13th centuries, characterized by dualistic beliefs and later suppressed as heresy.
medieval Christian dualist movement

Examples

Many medieval historians have studied the impact of Catharism on the religious landscape of southern France.
The spread of Catharism alarmed the Catholic Church, leading to the Albigensian Crusade.
The dualistic beliefs of Catharism sharply contrasted with orthodox Christian doctrine.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Cathari (the Cathars), from Greek katharoi (the pure ones).
