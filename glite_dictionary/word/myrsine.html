Myrsine

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˈsaɪni / (AmE), / mɪˈsiːni / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs with aromatic foliage, native to Africa and parts of Asia and the Pacific, including New Zealand.
genus of evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

Myrsine species are often used as ornamental plants in tropical and subtropical gardens.
The fragrant leaves of Myrsine make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
Some Myrsine shrubs produce small, colorful berries that attract birds.

Etymology

From New Latin *Myrsine*, from Greek *myrsínē* (myrtle).
