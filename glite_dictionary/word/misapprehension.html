misapprehension

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən / (BrE)
a mistaken understanding or interpretation of something
mistaken understanding

Examples

There was a clear misapprehension about the terms of the agreement.
Julia acted under the misapprehension that the meeting was scheduled for Friday.
To avoid any misapprehension, the instructions were repeated at the start.

Synset Examples

he wasn't going to admit his mistake
make no mistake about his intentions
there must be some misunderstanding — I don't have a sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misunderstanding - incorrect interpretation or idea about something

Etymology

From Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'apprehendere' (to seize, understand).
