chastisement

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæstɪzˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːstɪzmənt / (BrE)
the act of criticizing or rebuking someone for a mistake or wrongdoing
a reprimand or rebuke

Examples

His harsh chastisement left the student feeling embarrassed in front of the class.
The manager’s public chastisement of her team was unexpected and awkward.
Parents should be careful that chastisement is fair and constructive, not overly harsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: compliment - an expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'chastisement', from 'chastiier' (to chastise), from Latin 'castigare' (to punish, correct).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæstaɪzmənt / (AmE), / ˈtʃæstɪzmənt / (BrE)
the act of scolding or criticizing someone severely, especially as a form of correction or punishment
severe scolding or criticism

Examples

The manager's harsh chastisement left the employee feeling embarrassed in front of her colleagues.
After breaking the vase, the child received a gentle chastisement from his mother rather than a severe punishment.
The teacher's public chastisement was meant to deter other students from cheating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scolding - angry or harsh reprimand
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'chastier' (to punish, correct), from Latin 'castigare' (to punish, chasten).
