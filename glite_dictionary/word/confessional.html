confessional

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃənl̩ / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a small enclosed booth or room in a church where a priest hears confessions from members of the congregation
booth for hearing confessions (church)

Examples

The church added a new confessional near the altar for parishioners.
He waited nervously outside the confessional before entering to speak with the priest.
The confessional was decorated with ornate wood carvings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - the act of admitting sins to a priest

Etymology

From Latin 'confessionalis', relating to confession.
