Vertebrata

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɝːtəˈbreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌvɜːtɪˈbreɪtə / (BrE)
A major group of animals comprising all those that have a backbone, including fishes, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals; a subphylum of the phylum Chordata.
animals with backbones (subphylum)

Examples

The subphylum Vertebrata includes fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals.
Biologists classify humans as members of the Vertebrata because we possess a backbone.
Unlike invertebrates, Vertebrata exhibit a high level of organization and complexity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invertebrate - an animal lacking a backbone

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'vertebratus' (jointed), from 'vertebra' (joint of the spine).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɜːrtəˈbreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌvɜːtɪˈbreɪtə / (BrE)
A major taxonomic subphylum comprising animals with a backbone or spinal column, including fishes, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals.
animals with a backbone (subphylum)

Examples

Members of the Vertebrata include a wide range of species, from tiny fish to large mammals.
The evolutionary history of the Vertebrata is a central topic in zoology.
Both birds and reptiles belong to the subphylum Vertebrata.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invertebrate - animal lacking a backbone
  • Degree Higher: chordate - animal belonging to the phylum Chordata

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebratus' (jointed), from 'vertebra' (joint, spinal bone).
