turnout

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrˌnaʊt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.naʊt / (BrE)
(in ballet) the outward rotation of a dancer's leg from the hip joint, enabling the feet and knees to face away from the body.
ballet: outward leg rotation

Examples

Proper turnout is essential for performing accurate ballet steps.
Her natural turnout impressed the instructors during the audition.
Developing a strong turnout requires both flexibility and training.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːn.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn.aʊt / (BrE)
a short section of railroad track connected to the main line, used for storing trains or allowing them to pass each other
railroad siding or passing track

Examples

The engineer directed the freight cars onto the turnout to clear the main line.
There was a turnout ahead where trains could pass each other safely.
Maintenance crews stored their equipment on a nearby turnout.

  • Synonym: siding - a short track at the side of a railroad, where trains can be stored or pass
