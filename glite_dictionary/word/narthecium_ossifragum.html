Narthecium ossifragum

1. NOUN

/ nɑːrˈθiːsiəm ˌɒsɪˈfreɪɡəm / (AmE), / nɑːˈθiːsɪəm ˌɒsɪˈfraɪɡəm / (BrE)
A small perennial herb found in wet habitats of western Europe, known as bog asphodel.
a wetland herb (bog asphodel) of western Europe

Examples

Narthecium ossifragum is commonly found in peat bogs throughout western Europe.
The bright yellow flowers of Narthecium ossifragum appear in summer across marshy ground.
Botanists studied the distribution of Narthecium ossifragum in the wetlands of northern Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bog asphodel - common name for Narthecium ossifragum

Etymology

From Greek narthēkion (small cane) and Latin ossifragum (bone-breaking, from ossum 'bone' + frangere 'to break').
