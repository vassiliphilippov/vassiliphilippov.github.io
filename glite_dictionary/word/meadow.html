meadow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ / (BrE)
a piece of land covered with grass, often used for hay or pasture, typically found in the countryside
grassy field, often for hay or pasture

Examples

In the spring, wildflowers bloom across the meadow, painting it with vibrant colors.
The cows were grazing peacefully in the lush meadow outside the village.
We enjoyed a picnic in a sunlit meadow by the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass and suitable for grazing animals
  • Synonym: field - an area of open land, especially one planted with crops or pasture

Etymology

From Old English 'mædwe' or 'mæd', related to Old High German 'matta' (meadow).
