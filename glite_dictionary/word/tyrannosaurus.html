Tyrannosaurus

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌrænəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / tɪˌrænəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large carnivorous dinosaurs that lived during the late Cretaceous period, known for including the species Tyrannosaurus rex.
genus of large carnivorous dinosaur

Examples

The bones of a Tyrannosaurus were discovered in North America.
Many children are fascinated by the fierce appearance of the Tyrannosaurus.
The Tyrannosaurus was likely one of the top predators of its era.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Tyrannosaurus rex - a species within the genus Tyrannosaurus, one of the largest known carnivorous dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek 'tyrannos' (tyrant) + 'sauros' (lizard).
