fizgig

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzˌɡɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈfɪzˌɡɪɡ / (BrE)
A type of firework that moves about while producing a hissing or fizzing sound.
type of firework that fizzes

Examples

The children squealed with delight as the fizgig shot across the yard, fizzing and sparkling.
He lit a fizgig and quickly backed away as it danced around in circles on the pavement.
During the festival, a colorful fizgig whizzed past, leaving a trail of smoke and light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firework - an object that explodes to produce colored lights and noise for entertainment

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzˌɡɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈfɪz.ɡɪɡ / (BrE)
a spear with a barbed point, attached to a shaft, used for catching fish
barbed fishing spear
archaic

Examples

The fisherman skillfully hurled his fizgig into the shallow stream.
Before the advent of modern rods, a fizgig was commonly used to spear fish at night.
Several old fizgigs were discovered at the archaeological site, indicating historical fishing practices.

Etymology

Probably from alteration of 'fish gig' (gig meaning a pronged spear).
