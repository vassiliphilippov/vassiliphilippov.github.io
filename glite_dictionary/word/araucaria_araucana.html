Araucaria araucana

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɔːˈkɛəriə əˌrɔːˈkɑːnə / (AmE), / əˌrɔːˈkɛəriə əˌrɔːˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
a large evergreen coniferous tree native to Chile and western Argentina, with distinctive spiral branches and edible seeds, commonly known as the monkey puzzle tree
large evergreen tree (monkey puzzle) native to Chile

Examples

The Araucaria araucana is often planted in gardens for its unique appearance.
Seeds from the Araucaria araucana are edible and have been used as food by indigenous peoples.
Many visitors are attracted by the ancient Araucaria araucana trees in the national park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkey puzzle tree - common name for Araucaria araucana

Etymology

From genus name Araucaria, from Latinization of Arauco, a region in Chile, combined with specific epithet araucana referring to its origin.
