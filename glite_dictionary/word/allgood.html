allgood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌɡʊd / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.ɡʊd / (BrE)
a leafy plant of the goosefoot family, native to Europe but naturalized in North America, often used as a potherb
European leafy plant used as a potherb

Examples

The leaves of allgood can be cooked and eaten like spinach.
Many wild-food enthusiasts collect allgood in spring for its tender shoots.
In some gardens, allgood is grown for its nutritious, edible leaves.
