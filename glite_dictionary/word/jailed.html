jailed

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪld / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪld / (BrE)
kept in jail; being in captivity or under restraint by legal authority.
kept in jail or captivity

Examples

The jailed activist spent years fighting for his release.
Many of the jailed protesters are awaiting trial.
The documentary highlights the lives of jailed journalists around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisoned - kept in prison; deprived of liberty
  • Antonym: free - not in captivity; at liberty

Etymology

From 'jail' (Middle English, from Old French 'jaiole', from Latin 'cavea'), with '-ed' forming the past participle and adjectival form.
