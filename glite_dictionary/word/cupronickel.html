cupronickel

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjuːproʊˈnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkjuːprəʊˈnɪkəl / (BrE)
an alloy consisting mainly of copper and nickel, typically used for coins, marine engineering, and other applications requiring corrosion resistance
copper-nickel alloy

Examples

The pipes on the ship were made from cupronickel due to its excellent resistance to seawater corrosion.
Many modern coins are manufactured from cupronickel instead of pure silver or copper.
Engineers selected cupronickel for the heat exchanger to ensure durability and reliability.

Etymology

From Latin 'cuprum' (copper) and 'nickel', indicating its elemental composition.
