rheumy

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːmi / (AmE), / ˈruːmi / (BrE)
(of a part of the body, especially the eyes) moist, watery, or affected with rheum; sometimes related to symptoms of arthritis or age.
watery or affected with rheum (e.g., eyes)

Examples

His rheumy eyes glistened in the candlelight as he spoke.
The dog's rheumy gaze made him look older than his years.
After a sleepless night, her rheumy eyes betrayed her exhaustion.

Synset Examples

my creaky old joints
rheumy with age and grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watery - containing or producing water or moisture

Etymology

From rheum + -y, related to Middle English 'rheumy', derived from Old French and ultimately Greek 'rheuma' (that which flows).

2. ADJ

/ ˈruːmi / (AmE), / ˈruːmi / (BrE)
(of air, eyes, etc.) damp, moist, or wet, often in an unpleasant or unhealthy way.
damp or moist, often unpleasantly so

Examples

The rheumy air clung to everything in the old basement, making it smell musty.
After a day in the rheumy fog, his clothes felt cold and damp.
Her eyes, rheumy with age, filled with tears as she spoke.

Synset Examples

the raw and rheumy damp of night air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moist - slightly wet; damp
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid

Etymology

From Middle English *reumy*, from Old French *reume* (head cold), from Latin *rheuma* (a flow, stream), from Greek *rheuma* (stream, flow).
