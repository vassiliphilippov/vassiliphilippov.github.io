shrubbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrʌbəri / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌbəri / (BrE)
an area in a garden or park where a number of shrubs are grown together; a group of shrubs.
area planted with shrubs

Examples

The old mansion was surrounded by an overgrown shrubbery that blocked the windows.
He spent the afternoon trimming the shrubbery at the edge of the garden path.
A family of birds had made their home in the dense shrubbery behind the fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a small bushy plant, smaller than a tree

Etymology

From shrub + -ery, with -ery indicating a place associated with a particular thing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrʌbəri / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌbəri / (BrE)
An area of a garden or park where a number of shrubs are planted, often for decorative purposes.
area planted with shrubs

Examples

We walked past the shrubbery at the front of the mansion.
The gardener trimmed the shrubbery to keep it neat and attractive.
Children were hiding in the shrubbery during their game of hide and seek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a woody plant smaller than a tree, usually with multiple stems

Etymology

From 'shrub' + '-ery', denoting a place containing shrubs. 'Shrub' likely from Middle English 'shrubbe'.
