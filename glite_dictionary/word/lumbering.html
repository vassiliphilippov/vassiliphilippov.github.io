lumbering

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbərɪŋ / (BrE)
the trade or business of cutting, preparing, or selling timber
timber cutting or timber trade

Examples

The lumbering industry has played a crucial role in the region's economy for decades.
His family has been involved in lumbering for several generations.
Due to new regulations, many lumbering operations have become more environmentally friendly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logging - the activity or business of felling trees and cutting and preparing the timber

Etymology

From 'lumber' (timber) + '-ing' forming a noun of action.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌmbərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbərɪŋ / (BrE)
moving in a slow, heavy, and awkward way, usually because of large size or weight.
moving heavily and clumsily

Examples

The lumbering elephant made its way slowly through the jungle.
She watched the lumbering truck struggle up the steep hill.
His lumbering footsteps echoed in the empty hallway.

Synset Examples

the heavy tread of tired troops
moved with a lumbering sag-bellied trot
ponderous prehistoric beasts
a ponderous yawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponderous - slow and clumsy because of great weight
