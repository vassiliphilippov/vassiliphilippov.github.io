diethylstilboestrol

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪˌɛθəlˌstɪlboʊˈɛstrɔl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪˌiːθɛlˌstɪlbəʊˈiːstrɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic and potent estrogen used in medicine and formerly as a growth promoter in livestock and poultry
synthetic estrogen used medically and in animal feed
medical

Examples

Doctors no longer prescribe diethylstilboestrol to pregnant women due to its health risks.
Researchers studied the effects of diethylstilboestrol exposure on reproductive health.
The use of diethylstilboestrol in animal feed was banned in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrogen - a group of steroid hormones that promote the development and maintenance of female characteristics

Etymology

From diethyl (two ethyl groups) + stilbene (chemical structure) + oestr- ('estrogen') + -ol (alcohol group).
