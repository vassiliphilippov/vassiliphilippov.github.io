monoecious

1. ADJ

/ məˈniːʃəs / (AmE), / məˈniːʃɪəs / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) having both male and female reproductive organs on the same individual.
having both male and female organs (botany/biology)

Examples

Many pine trees are monoecious, bearing both male and female cones on the same tree.
Scientists observed that the monoecious plant produced both pollen and ovules.
Unlike dioecious species, monoecious species can self-fertilize.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dioecious - having male and female reproductive organs on separate individuals

Etymology

From Greek monoeidēs (single, alone) and oikos (house), referencing both sexes housed together.
