Ailuropodidae

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌlurəˈpɑdəˌdiː / (AmE), / aɪˌluərəˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mammals that includes the giant panda, sometimes recognized as a separate family in certain classifications
family of mammals including giant panda

Examples

The Ailuropodidae are often recognized as a distinct family due to the unique characteristics of giant pandas.
Some taxonomists classify the giant panda in its own family, Ailuropodidae, instead of grouping it with bears.
Research on the evolutionary history of Ailuropodidae has provided insight into panda adaptations.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ailuros' (cat) + 'pous, pod-' (foot), with scientific suffix '-idae' (family).

2. NOUN

/ aɪˌlʊrəˈpɒdɪdi / (AmE), / aɪˌlʊərəˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising the giant panda and its extinct relatives, according to some biological classifications.
family of mammals including the giant panda

Examples

The giant panda belongs to the family Ailuropodidae according to some zoological classifications.
Researchers debate whether red pandas should also be included in Ailuropodidae or placed in a separate family.
Fossil discoveries have shed light on extinct members of the Ailuropodidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant panda - large bear-like mammal, native to China, known for black and white markings

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'ailouros' (cat) and 'pous, podos' (foot), with '-idae' denoting animal family.
