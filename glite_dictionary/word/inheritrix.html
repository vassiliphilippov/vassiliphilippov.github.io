inheritrix

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtrɪks / (BrE)
A female heir, especially one who inherits property or a title.
female heir
archaic legal

Examples

In the novel, Lady Arabella is revealed as the long-lost inheritrix of the estate.
As the only child, she became the sole inheritrix after her father passed away.
The law once used the word inheritrix to specify female heirs in formal documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heiress - a woman who inherits property or a title
  • Synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to inherit property or a title

Etymology

From Latin 'heredatrix', feminine form of 'heredator' (heir).
