Hemiascomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiː.mi.æs.kəˈmaɪ.siːts / (AmE), / ˌhɛ.mi.æs.kəˈmaɪ.siːts / (BrE)
A class of fungi characterized by the absence of ascocarps, including yeasts and certain plant parasites.
class of fungi lacking ascocarps
medical

Examples

Laboratory studies often use Hemiascomycetes to investigate cellular processes in yeasts.
Many species within the Hemiascomycetes class are important plant parasites.
Advances in genetics have improved our understanding of Hemiascomycetes diversity.

Etymology

From Greek hēmi- (half) + Ascomycetes (a class of fungi with asci).
