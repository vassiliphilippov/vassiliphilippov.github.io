to vesiculate

1. VERB

/ vəˈsɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / vɪˈsɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
To cause to become vesicular, or to develop air cells or small cavities.
to make vesicular or full of small cavities
medical

Examples

The treatment can vesiculate the tissue, creating small fluid-filled spaces.
Certain bacteria may vesiculate cell membranes during infection.
The researchers observed that heating could vesiculate the mineral samples, forming numerous air pockets.

Synset Examples

vesiculate an organ

Etymology

From Latin vesicula (small bladder or sac), diminutive of vesica (bladder).

2. VERB

/ vəˈsɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / vɪˈsɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of tissue or organs) to form or become filled with small cavities or air cells; to become vesicular.
form vesicles or air cells; become vesicular
medical

Examples

During certain infections, the lung tissue can vesiculate rapidly.
Under intense heat, the surface of the rock began to vesiculate and form tiny bubbles.
The cells vesiculated after exposure to the chemical reagent.

Synset Examples

The organs vesiculated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesicular - having the form or nature of vesicles (small cavities)

Etymology

From Latin vesicula (small bladder, vesicle), diminutive of vesica (bladder).
