proselyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒsɪˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a person who has recently converted to a faith or belief, especially a gentile who has converted to Judaism
recent convert, esp. to Judaism

Examples

The community welcomed the proselyte with open arms at the synagogue.
During ancient times, it was rare for a gentile proselyte to join the faith.
As a recent proselyte, she studied the religious texts with enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - a person who has undergone a change in religion, opinion, or belief

Etymology

From Greek prosēlutos (stranger, convert), from pros (towards) + ēluthen (he has come, from erchomai, to come).
