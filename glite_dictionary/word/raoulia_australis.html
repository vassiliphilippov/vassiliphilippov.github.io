Raoulia australis

1. NOUN

/ raʊˈuːliə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / raʊˈuːliə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a perennial, low-growing herbaceous plant with silvery, woolly foliage, found in New Zealand; commonly known as one of the 'vegetable sheep'.
a mat-forming herb with woolly leaves, native to New Zealand

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with patches of Raoulia australis, their woolly leaves gleaming in the sunlight.
Researchers studying alpine plants often encounter Raoulia australis while collecting samples in the mountains.
Gardeners appreciate Raoulia australis for its hardy nature and unique mat-forming growth habit.

Etymology

From genus name Raoulia, after Etienne Raoul, a French botanist; species name australis meaning 'southern' in Latin.
