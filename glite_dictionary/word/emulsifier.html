emulsifier

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmʌlsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ɪˈmʌlsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a substance that helps mix liquids that normally do not combine easily, such as oil and water, by promoting the formation of an emulsion
a substance that helps mix unblendable liquids

Examples

Lecithin is commonly added to chocolate as an emulsifier to keep the cocoa butter from separating.
Many salad dressings require an emulsifier so the oil and vinegar don't separate.
Some ice creams use egg yolk as a natural emulsifier to keep the texture smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emulsion - a mixture of liquids that are normally unblendable

Etymology

From emulsion + -ifier, meaning 'something that causes emulsification'.
