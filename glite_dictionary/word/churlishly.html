churlishly

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃɝːlɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːlɪʃli / (BrE)
In a rude, surly, or unfriendly manner.
in a rude, surly way

Examples

He spoke churlishly to the waiter when his order was delayed.
She turned away churlishly, refusing to acknowledge the apology.
The athlete responded churlishly to questions from the press after his defeat.

Synset Examples

the store owner treated his customers churlishly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudely - in an impolite or offensive manner
  • Antonym: courteously - in a polite and respectful manner

Etymology

From 'churlish' + '-ly'. 'Churlish' from Old English 'ceorl' (man of low rank; peasant).
