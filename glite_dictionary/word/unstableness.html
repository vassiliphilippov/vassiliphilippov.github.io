unstableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsteɪb(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪb(ə)lnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unstable; lack of firmness, security, or constancy.
the state of being unstable

Examples

The unstableness of the platform made it dangerous to stand on.
Her emotional unstableness worried her friends and family.
Political unstableness often leads to economic challenges in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the quality or state of being unstable
  • Antonym: stability - the quality or state of being stable

Etymology

From 'unstable' + '-ness', formed from Late Latin 'stabilis' (firm) with English prefix and suffix.
