Stenotaphrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛnoʊˈtæfrəm / (AmE), / ˌstɛnəʊˈtafɹəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical grasses, some species of which are cultivated as lawn grasses
genus of tropical lawn grasses

Examples

Stenotaphrum is often chosen for golf courses in warm climates due to its durability.
The most widely known member of Stenotaphrum is Stenotaphrum secundatum, commonly called St. Augustine grass.
Researchers are studying Stenotaphrum to improve drought resistance in lawns.

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'taphros' (trench or ditch), referring to the groove in the spikelets.
