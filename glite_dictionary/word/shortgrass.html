shortgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any of various types of grass that remain short and are adapted to tolerate dry conditions, especially those common on the plains east of the Rocky Mountains.
drought-tolerant, low-growing grass

Examples

The prairie ecosystem is dominated by shortgrass species adapted to arid climates.
Ranchers often rely on shortgrass to feed their cattle in dry regions.
Much of the western plains are covered by shortgrass rather than taller vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tallgrass - a type of grass that grows tall, often in wetter areas
