Machaeranthera tanacetifolia

1. NOUN

/ məˌkɛr.ənˈθɛr.ə ˌtæn.əˌsiː.tɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / məˌkɪə.rænˈθɪə.rə ˌtæn.əsɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
A wild aster with fernlike leaves and flower heads with very narrow, bright purple rays, found from Alberta to Texas and Mexico.
a wild aster with narrow purple rays

Examples

The field was dotted with the bright purple flowers of Machaeranthera tanacetifolia in late summer.
Researchers studied how Machaeranthera tanacetifolia adapted to the arid climate of the region.
Wildlife in the area depend on Machaeranthera tanacetifolia as a source of nectar.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'makhaira' (sword) and 'anthera' (anther), referencing botanical features, and Latin 'tanacetifolia' meaning 'with leaves like tansy'.
