satanophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪtənəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌseɪtənəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
An abnormal or extreme fear of Satan or the devil.
abnormal fear of Satan
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with satanophobia after he refused to enter a church for fear of encountering evil.
Her severe satanophobia made it difficult for her to watch movies with supernatural themes.
People with satanophobia may experience anxiety when exposed to religious imagery related to the devil.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of a specific object, activity, or situation

Etymology

From 'Satan' + Greek 'phobos' (fear).
