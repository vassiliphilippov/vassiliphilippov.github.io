blackbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a common European thrush (Turdus merula), the male of which has all-black plumage and a yellow bill
a common European thrush with black plumage

Examples

A male blackbird perched on the garden fence, singing at dawn.
We spotted several blackbirds foraging for worms on the lawn.
The song of the blackbird is especially melodious and easily recognized in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - a songbird from the family Turdidae

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'black' + 'bird'; used for black-plumaged birds, especially Turdus merula.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A North or Central American songbird of the family Icteridae, the male of which is black or mostly black.
North/Central American black songbird (Icteridae family)

Examples

A flock of blackbirds landed in the field, their dark feathers shining in the sunlight.
The male blackbird is easily recognized by its glossy black plumage and bright yellow eyes.
Several species of blackbirds can be found in woodlands and marshes across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grackle - a songbird of the family Icteridae with iridescent black plumage
  • Synonym: red-winged blackbird - a North American bird of the Icteridae family noted for the male's red and yellow shoulder patches

Etymology

From black + bird, describing the male's color.
