Reseda

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːdə / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs with racemose flowers, including mignonette and dyer's rocket.
genus of herbs, includes mignonette

Examples

The Reseda genus includes several species valued for their fragrance.
Gardeners often cultivate Reseda for its attractive racemose flowers.
Mignonette, a member of the Reseda genus, is popular in old-fashioned gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mignonette - a fragrant plant of the genus Reseda

Etymology

From Latin 'resedare' (to calm, to soothe), possibly referring to the medicinal uses of some species.
