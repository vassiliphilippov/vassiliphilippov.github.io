auctioneer

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔkʃəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌɔːkʃəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to conduct an auction, selling goods or property to the highest bidder
person who conducts an auction

Examples

The auctioneer raised his hammer and called out the next item for sale.
As the auctioneer spoke rapidly, the bidders signaled their offers.
The auctioneer sold the antique vase to the highest bidder in just a few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bidder - a person who offers to pay a certain amount at an auction
  • Synonym: auction - a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder

Etymology

From 'auction' + '-eer', modeled on words like 'engineer' and 'mountaineer'.

2. VERB

/ ˌɔkʃəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌɔːkʃəˈnɪə / (BrE)
to sell something by auction, typically as a professional auctioneer
to sell at an auction

Examples

The art dealer will auctioneer several rare paintings next month.
She has auctioneered historic memorabilia for more than a decade.
He was hired to auctioneer the company’s assets after it closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auction - a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
  • Synonym: sell - to exchange something for money

Etymology

From the noun 'auctioneer,' itself derived from 'auction' (from Latin 'auctio': a public sale) + '-eer' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02249555-v
Synset POS: v
