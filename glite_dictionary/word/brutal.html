brutal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbruːtəl / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) cruel and violent; able or willing to inflict pain or suffering.
cruel and violent

Examples

The dictator's brutal regime left thousands of people living in fear.
The movie was criticized for its brutal scenes of violence.
The police condemned the brutal attack on the innocent bystander.

Synset Examples

a barbarous crime
brutal beatings
cruel tortures
Stalin's roughshod treatment of the kulaks
a savage slap
vicious kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - willing to cause pain or suffering to others
  • Antonym: kind - friendly, generous, or considerate

Etymology

From Medieval Latin brutalis (of or belonging to animals), from Latin brutus (dull, stupid, insensitive).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbruːtəl / (AmE), / ˈbruːtl̩ / (BrE)
(of a remark or manner) unpleasantly direct and lacking in tact; harshly frank.
unpleasantly direct; harshly frank

Examples

Sometimes brutal feedback is necessary to help someone improve.
Her brutal honesty made the conversation uncomfortable but productive.
He gave a brutal assessment of the project's chances.

Synset Examples

he spoke with brutal honesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - uncompromisingly forthright; direct to the point of rudeness
  • Antonym: tactful - having or showing sensitivity in dealing with others or difficult issues

Etymology

From French 'brutal', from Medieval Latin 'brutalis' (like a beast).

3. ADJ

/ ˈbruːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəl / (BrE)
(of conditions or experiences) extremely harsh or severe; causing great discomfort or difficulty.
harsh or severe (of conditions)

Examples

The brutal heatwave lasted for weeks, making it dangerous to go outside at midday.
After a brutal winter, many roads were left damaged by the ice and snow.
Surviving the brutal training schedule required both physical and mental toughness.

Synset Examples

the brutal summer sun
a brutal winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or severe (of conditions)
  • Antonym: mild - gentle or not severe (of conditions)

Etymology

From Middle French brutal (relating to beasts), from Latin brutalis, from brutus (dull, unreasonable).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ˈbruːt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəl / (BrE)
Resembling a beast in cruelty or insensitivity; lacking human sensibility or compassion.
cruel, insensitive; like a beast

Examples

The dictator was known for his brutal methods of suppressing dissent.
Prisoners endured brutal treatment that shocked the international community.
His brutal honesty left many people feeling hurt and offended.

Synset Examples

beastly desires
a bestial nature
brute force
a dull and brutish man
bestial treatment of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bestial - of or like a beast; lacking reason or humanity
  • Synonym: beastly - cruel or unpleasant in a way that resembles a beast
  • Antonym: humane - showing compassion or kindness, especially toward others

Etymology

From Latin brutus (heavy, dull, unintelligent).
