pirana

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈrɑːnə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɑːnə / (BrE)
A small, voracious carnivorous freshwater fish of South America that is known for attacking and consuming living animals.
South American carnivorous freshwater fish

Examples

The locals warned us about the presence of pirana in the river before we went swimming.
A single pirana can strip its prey to the bone within minutes.
Researchers observed the hunting techniques of the pirana in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piranha - A small voracious carnivorous freshwater fish native to South America

Etymology

Variant of 'piranha', from Portuguese, originally from Tupi 'pira' (fish) + 'aña' (tooth).
