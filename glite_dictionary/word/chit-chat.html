chit-chat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪt ˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪt ˌtʃæt / (BrE)
casual and light informal conversation about unimportant matters
light informal conversation

Examples

The guests engaged in some chit-chat before dinner was served.
She enjoys a bit of chit-chat with her colleagues every morning.
The office was filled with the sound of friendly chit-chat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small talk - polite conversation about unimportant things

Etymology

Reduplicative formation from 'chit' (idle talk) and 'chat' (talk casually); first attested in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (BrE)
informal, light conversation about trivial matters, typically for social purposes
light informal conversation
informal

Examples

We had some pleasant chit-chat over coffee before the meeting started.
Their chit-chat filled the room with relaxed laughter.
He prefers to skip the chit-chat and get straight to business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversation - talk between two or more people; discussion

Etymology

Reduplication of "chat", imitating the sound of casual talk; first recorded in the 18th century.

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (BrE)
to talk socially in a light and informal way, often without exchanging important information
talk casually or socially

Examples

We sat in the garden and chit-chatted until the sun went down.
The colleagues like to chit-chat by the coffee machine during their breaks.
She was chit-chatting with her neighbor when I walked by.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Degree Higher: gossip - to talk about the personal lives of others, often spreading rumors

Etymology

Reduplication of 'chit' (short informal talk), probably imitative, early 18th century.
