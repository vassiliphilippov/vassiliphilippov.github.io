amphibious

1. ADJ

/ æmˈfɪbiəs / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbiəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of animals belonging to the class Amphibia, such as frogs, toads, and salamanders.
relating to animals of the class Amphibia

Examples

Frogs are amphibious creatures that can live both in water and on land.
The biology class studied the unique traits of amphibious animals.
Some reptiles and all amphibious animals lay their eggs in moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - living or growing on land
  • Antonym: aquatic - living or growing in water

Etymology

From Greek 'amphibios', meaning 'living a double life': 'amphi-' (both) + 'bios' (life).

2. ADJ

/ æmˈfɪbiəs / (AmE), / amˈfɪbɪəs / (BrE)
able to live or operate on both land and water.
able to live or function on land and in water

Examples

Some military forces use amphibious vehicles to cross rivers and beaches.
The frog is a classic example of an amphibious animal.
These new robots are fully amphibious, capable of moving on both land and water.

Synset Examples

amphibious vehicles
amphibious operations
amphibious troops
frogs are amphibious animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - living or operating only on land
  • Antonym: aquatic - living or operating only in water

Etymology

From Greek 'amphibios' (having a double life), from 'amphi-' (both) + 'bios' (life).
