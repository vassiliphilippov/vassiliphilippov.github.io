Tartary

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑr.tə.ˌri / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
a vast historical region in Europe and Asia, controlled by the Mongols in the 13th and 14th centuries
historical region of Eurasia, Mongol-controlled

Examples

Many European maps from the Middle Ages feature Tartary as a vast and mysterious land.
The Mongol Empire carved out Tartary after conquering much of central Asia.
Legends about lost cities in Tartary captivated travelers and scholars for centuries.

Synset Examples

under Genghis Khan Tartary extended as far east as the Pacific Ocean

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Tartaria, referring to lands inhabited by the Tartars (Tatars).
