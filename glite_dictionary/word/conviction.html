conviction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
a firmly held belief or opinion that does not require proof or evidence
firmly held belief

Examples

She spoke with such conviction that everyone in the room was convinced by her argument.
Despite the lack of evidence, his conviction never wavered.
Her conviction in the importance of honesty inspired the whole team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or strongly believed
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Latin 'convictionem' (a proving, refuting), from 'convincere' (to convince, overcome).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
(Law) a formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense, made by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law.
formal finding of guilt in a court
legal

Examples

His conviction for fraud resulted in a five-year prison sentence.
The lawyer appealed the conviction on the grounds of insufficient evidence.
A criminal conviction can have serious consequences for future employment.

Synset Examples

the conviction came as no surprise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they have been charged

Etymology

From Latin convictio (proof, conviction), from convincere (to overcome, demonstrate).
