Lycosidae

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkɑːsɪˌdi / (AmE), / lɪˈkɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of spiders commonly known as wolf spiders, characterized by their excellent eyesight and hunting behavior.
family of wolf spiders

Examples

Lycosidae are commonly found in grasslands and forests around the world.
Unlike many other spiders, members of Lycosidae actively hunt their prey instead of spinning webs.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Lycosidae family during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf spider - Any spider of the family Lycosidae, known for hunting prey without webs

Etymology

From Greek lykos (wolf), referring to the hunting habits of these spiders.
