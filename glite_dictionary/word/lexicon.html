lexicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛksɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkən / (BrE)
a reference book or database containing an alphabetical list of words of a language with their meanings, origins, and other relevant information
dictionary or word list

Examples

She consulted the lexicon to better understand the origins of rare words.
A comprehensive lexicon of medical terms can be invaluable to doctors and nurses.
The scholar compiled a lexicon of ancient Greek, listing thousands of entries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictionary - a book or online resource that lists words and their meanings

Etymology

From Greek leksikon (wordbook), from leksis (word, speech).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06430544-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛksɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkən / (BrE)
the complete set of words or vocabulary used by a person, language, or branch of knowledge
vocabulary of a person or language

Examples

A child's lexicon grows rapidly during the early years of language development.
Linguists study the mental lexicon to understand how people store and access words.
Her lexicon includes many technical terms from the field of biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocabulary - the set of words known and used by a person

Etymology

From Greek lexikon (book of words), from lexis (word or speech), from legein (to speak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05659087-n
Synset POS: n
