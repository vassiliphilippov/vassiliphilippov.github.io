Pipturus albidus

1. NOUN

/ pɪpˈtʊr.əs ælˈbaɪ.dəs / (AmE), / pɪpˈtjʊər.əs ælˈbɪd.əs / (BrE)
A Hawaiian tree of the genus Pipturus, with bark used to make tapa cloth.
Hawaiian tree used for tapa cloth

Examples

The bark of the Pipturus albidus has long been used in Hawaii to make traditional tapa cloth.
Many native Hawaiian gardens include the Pipturus albidus for its cultural significance and beautiful foliage.
Researchers are studying how the Pipturus albidus contributes to island ecosystems.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Pipturus' is the genus name, 'albidus' meaning 'whitish' in Latin.
