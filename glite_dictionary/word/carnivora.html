Carnivora

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈnɪvərə / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɪvərə / (BrE)
A large order of mammals that primarily eat meat, including cats, dogs, bears, and related animals.
an order of meat-eating mammals

Examples

Members of the Carnivora include both domestic and wild species such as dogs, cats, and bears.
Despite being called Carnivora, not all members are strictly meat-eaters, as some have omnivorous diets.
Tigers, lions, and foxes all belong to the biological order Carnivora.

Etymology

From Latin 'carnivorus' (flesh-eating).
