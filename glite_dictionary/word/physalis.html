Physalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.sə.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɪz.ə.lɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the nightshade family, whose species are commonly known as ground cherries and have small, edible fruits enclosed in a papery husk.
genus of plants; ground cherries

Examples

The garden was filled with Physalis plants, their fruits hanging in delicate lantern-like husks.
Many people enjoy the tart flavor of Physalis in jams and desserts.
The bright orange fruit of the Physalis stands out against the green leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - Edible fruit of the Physalis genus, enclosed in a papery husk

Etymology

From Greek 'physalis' (bladder), referring to the plant's papery husk.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.sə.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɪs.ə.lɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the nightshade family, known as ground cherries, with small, edible fruits enclosed in papery husks.
genus of ground cherries

Examples

The fruit of the Physalis is sweet and often used in desserts.
Many gardeners enjoy growing Physalis for its ornamental and edible qualities.
A variety of Physalis can be found in South America and other temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - a plant of the genus Physalis with edible fruit in a papery husk

Etymology

From Greek 'physalis' (bladder), referring to the inflated husk surrounding the fruit.
