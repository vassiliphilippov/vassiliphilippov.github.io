crematory

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈkriːmətri / (BrE)
A furnace used for the incineration of corpses to ashes.
furnace for burning corpses to ashes

Examples

The deceased was taken to the crematory for a private ceremony.
Many modern funeral homes have their own crematory on site.
After the service, the body was respectfully placed in the crematory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crematorium - a place where cremation is carried out, often including one or more furnaces
  • Antonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground

Etymology

From Latin 'cremare' (to burn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkriːməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈkriːməˌtəri / (BrE)
A building or facility where dead bodies are cremated.
place where bodies are cremated

Examples

The city's main crematory was built in the early twentieth century.
After the service, the body was taken to the crematory for cremation.
Many families choose a crematory that offers both ceremony and cremation services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crematorium - A furnace or building for incinerating corpses
  • Synonym: mortuary - A place where dead bodies are kept before burial or cremation
  • Antonym: cemetery - A place for burying the dead

Etymology

From Latin 'crematorium', meaning 'place for burning'.
