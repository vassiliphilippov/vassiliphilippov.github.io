satisfactory

1. ADJ

/ ˌsæt̬.ɪsˈfæk.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˌsæt.ɪsˈfæk.tər.i / (BrE)
good enough to meet a particular need or requirement, though not excellent; giving satisfaction.
good enough; meeting requirements

Examples

The inspector confirmed that the restaurant's cleanliness was satisfactory.
His performance on the test was satisfactory, but there is still room for improvement.
After reviewing the repairs, Julia found the results to be satisfactory.

Synset Examples

satisfactory living conditions
his grades were satisfactory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfying - giving pleasure or fulfillment
  • Antonym: unsatisfactory - not good enough; not meeting requirements

Etymology

From Latin satisfactorius (causing contentment), via Medieval Latin and Old French.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsæ.tɪsˈfæk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌsæt.ɪsˈfæk.tər.i / (BrE)
Good enough to meet a particular need or requirement; acceptable, though not outstanding.
meeting requirements; acceptable

Examples

The solution provided a satisfactory answer to the client's problem.
Her work performance this year has been satisfactory, but there is room for improvement.
Is the quality of this product satisfactory for your needs?

Synset Examples

the step makes a satisfactory seat
I would kill for a decent cup of coffee
a decent wage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptable - good enough for a purpose or requirement
  • Synonym: adequate - sufficient for a specific need; acceptable but not impressive
  • Antonym: unsatisfactory - not good enough; failing to meet requirements

Etymology

From Late Latin satisfactorius, from satisfacere (to satisfy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01128566-s
Synset POS: s
