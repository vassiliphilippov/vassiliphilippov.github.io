four-membered

1. ADJ

/ fɔr ˈmɛm.bɚd / (AmE), / fɔː ˈmɛm.bəd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having a ring structure composed of four atoms or groups.
having a ring with four members (chemistry)

Examples

Many organic compounds contain a four-membered ring, such as cyclobutane.
The four-membered structure of the molecule increases ring strain compared to larger rings.
Research has focused on the reactivity of four-membered heterocycles in synthetic chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: five-membered - (of a chemical compound) having a ring with five atoms or groups
  • Degree Lower: three-membered - (of a chemical compound) having a ring with three atoms or groups
