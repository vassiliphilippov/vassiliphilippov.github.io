heating

1. NOUN

/ ˈhitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the system or process used to provide warmth to a building or room
system for warming a building

Examples

The heating broke down last night, so the office was freezing in the morning.
Modern heating is much more energy-efficient than older systems.
They turned on the heating early to keep the house warm during the cold snap.

Synset Examples

the heating system wasn't working
they have radiant heating

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooling - the process or system for making a building cooler
  • Synonym: heating system - an apparatus or arrangement for warming a building

2. NOUN

/ ˈhiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming warm or causing something to become warm, especially the increase in temperature
increase in temperature; becoming warm

Examples

The heating of the room was slow after the windows had been opened.
Rapid heating can sometimes damage delicate materials.
The climate is changing, and global heating is a major concern.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooling - the process of becoming cooler; a falling temperature

Etymology

From the verb 'heat' + -ing suffix, forming a noun meaning 'the process of heating'.
