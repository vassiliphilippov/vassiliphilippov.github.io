headstrong

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdˌstrɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌstrɒŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) determined to do as one wants, often in spite of advice or warnings; willful or stubborn.
stubborn; willful person

Examples

She was so headstrong that she refused to listen to her teacher's advice.
His headstrong behavior often got him into trouble at work.
Despite everyone's warnings, the headstrong boy went ahead with his plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or attitude
  • Synonym: obstinate - refusing to change one's opinion despite attempts to persuade
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to obey or agree with others

Etymology

From head + strong, originally referring to someone led by their own will, attested since the 16th century.
