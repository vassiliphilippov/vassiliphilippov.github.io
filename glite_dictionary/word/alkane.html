alkane

1. NOUN

/ ˈælˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.keɪn / (BrE)
a saturated hydrocarbon consisting only of single bonds between carbon atoms, with the general formula CnH₂n₊₂
saturated hydrocarbon (CnH₂n₊₂)
medical

Examples

Methane, ethane, and propane are common alkanes found in natural gas.
The general formula for an alkane is CnH₂n₊₂, which distinguishes them from other hydrocarbons.
He explained that every alkane is a saturated compound with only single bonds between carbon atoms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkene - a hydrocarbon containing at least one double bond
  • Synonym: paraffin - another term for alkane, especially in British English

Etymology

From German Alkane, derived from 'alkyl' and '-ane', following chemical nomenclature conventions.
