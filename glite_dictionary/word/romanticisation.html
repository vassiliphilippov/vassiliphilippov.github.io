romanticisation

1. NOUN

/ roʊˌmæntɪsəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / rəˌmæntɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something seem more attractive, idealized, or sentimental than it really is
the act of idealizing or sentimentalizing

Examples

The romanticisation of rural life often ignores the struggles of farming communities.
Many critics argue that there is a dangerous romanticisation of violence in some films.
His memoir is criticized for its romanticisation of his troubled youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealization - the act of representing something as perfect or better than in reality

Etymology

From romanticise + -ation, influenced by French 'romantisation'.
