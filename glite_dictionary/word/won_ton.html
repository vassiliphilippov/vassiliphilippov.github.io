won ton

1. NOUN

/ wʌn tɑn / (AmE), / wɒn tɒn / (BrE)
A Chinese soup consisting of broth and dumplings filled with meat or vegetables.
Chinese soup with dumplings

Examples

She ordered a bowl of won ton to start her meal.
The restaurant is famous for its homemade won ton.
Would you like some won ton with your lunch?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumpling - A small mass of dough cooked by boiling or steaming, sometimes filled

Etymology

From Cantonese 雲吞 (wan4 tan1), meaning 'swallowing clouds,' referring to the dumpling's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ wʌn tɑn / (AmE), / wɒn tɒn / (BrE)
a Chinese dumpling filled with spiced minced pork or other ingredients, typically served in soup
Chinese dumpling, often in soup

Examples

I ordered a bowl of won ton soup for lunch at the new Chinese restaurant.
The chef taught us how to make homemade won ton wrappers from scratch.
She carefully folded the pork filling inside each won ton before boiling them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumpling - small mass of dough cooked by boiling or steaming, sometimes with a filling

Etymology

From Cantonese 粽 ‘wonton’, meaning 'swallowing a cloud', referring to the dumpling's appearance.
