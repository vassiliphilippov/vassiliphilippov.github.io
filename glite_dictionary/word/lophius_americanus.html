Lophius americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊfiəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒfɪəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of anglerfish found in the northwest Atlantic, having a large mouth and a wormlike filament to lure prey.
species of anglerfish (monkfish) in the Atlantic

Examples

The Lophius americanus is prized by chefs for its mild, firm flesh known as monkfish.
Fishermen along the New England coast often catch Lophius americanus in their nets.
The distinctive lure on the Lophius americanus helps it capture unsuspecting prey in dark waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - a fish with a luminescent lure used to attract prey
  • Synonym: monkfish - common name for several anglerfish species, especially Lophius americanus

Etymology

From genus name 'Lophius' (Greek lophos, 'crest') and Latin 'americanus' (American).
