mattress

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætrəs / (AmE), / ˈmætrəs / (BrE)
A large, thick pad filled with resilient material and often containing springs, used for sleeping on as part of a bed.
large pad for sleeping on

Examples

She bought a new mattress to help with her back pain.
The hotel room had a comfortable mattress on the king-sized bed.
After years of use, their old mattress had become lumpy and uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bed - a piece of furniture for sleep or rest

Etymology

From Arabic *maṭrah* (cushion, place where something is thrown), via Old French and Middle English.
