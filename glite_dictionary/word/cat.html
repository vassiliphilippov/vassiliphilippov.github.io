CAT

1. NOUN

/ si eɪ ti / (AmE), / siː eɪ tiː / (BrE)
a medical imaging method in which a computer reconstructs detailed cross-sectional images of the body from X-ray scans taken along a single axis; also known as a CT scan
medical imaging scan using X-rays and computers
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a CAT to get a clearer picture of the patient's head injury.
Advancements in CAT technology have greatly improved the accuracy of diagnoses.
A CAT can help detect tumors and other abnormalities inside the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CT scan - medical imaging method using computed tomography; same as CAT

Etymology

Acronym of 'computerized axial tomography.'
