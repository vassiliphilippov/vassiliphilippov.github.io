tribulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪb.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɪb.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a cause of great trouble, suffering, or distress
a great trouble or suffering

Examples

After years of tribulation, their family finally found peace.
The novel told the story of a woman's tribulations during the war.
He viewed every setback as a new tribulation to overcome.

Synset Examples

his mother-in-law's visits were a great trial for him
life is full of tribulations
a visitation of the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a test of one's patience, endurance, or faith
  • Synonym: ordeal - a painful or difficult experience
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling or event of great pleasure and happiness (opposite of suffering)

Etymology

From Latin tribulatio (distress, trouble), from tribulare (to oppress or afflict).
