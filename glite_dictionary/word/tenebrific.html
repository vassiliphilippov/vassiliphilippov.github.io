tenebrific

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛnəˈbrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛnɪˈbrɪfɪk / (BrE)
Dark and gloomy; producing darkness or obscurity.
dark and gloomy

Examples

The old, abandoned mansion had a tenebrific atmosphere that unsettled all who entered.
A tenebrific sky loomed overhead as the storm approached the town.
The artist was inspired by the tenebrific nature of the ancient forest, depicting it in deep shades of black and gray.

Synset Examples

a tenebrous cave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenebrous - dark, shadowy, or obscure
  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or badly lit; causing a feeling of depression

Etymology

From Latin 'tenebrae' (darkness) + '-fic' (making).
