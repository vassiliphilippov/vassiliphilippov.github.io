Krigia bulbosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪdʒiə bʌlˈboʊsə / (AmE), / ˈkɹɪdʒɪə bʌlˈbəʊsə / (BrE)
A small yellow-flowered herb resembling dandelions, native to central and southeastern regions of the United States.
yellow-flowered herb native to U.S.

Examples

The fields were dotted with blooming Krigia bulbosa during the early summer months.
Botanists surveyed the area and recorded several rare plants, including Krigia bulbosa.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Krigia bulbosa for dandelions because of its bright yellow flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Krigia, named after David Krieg, and Latin bulbosa meaning 'bulbous'.
