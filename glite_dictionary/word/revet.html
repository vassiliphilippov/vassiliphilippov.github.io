to revet

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɛt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛt / (BrE)
to face (an embankment, wall, etc.) with a layer of stone, concrete, or other supporting material to strengthen or retain it
to cover and support with protective facing

Examples

The engineers decided to revet the banks of the river to prevent erosion.
Workers revetted the old stone wall with concrete for additional stability.
It is necessary to revet the embankment before the heavy rain season starts.

Synset Examples

revet an embankment

Etymology

From French 'revêtir', from Latin 'revestire' (to clothe, cover).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɛt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛt / (BrE)
to face, support, or protect (an embankment, wall, etc.) with a revetment, usually to prevent erosion or collapse
to reinforce with a revetment

Examples

Engineers were hired to revet the coastline against further erosion.
They plan to revet the riverbank with large stones to prevent flooding.
The team revetted the dam to ensure its long-term stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reinforce - to strengthen or support an object or structure

Etymology

From French 'revêtir', from Latin 'revestire' (to clothe again, cover).
