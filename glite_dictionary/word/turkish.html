Turkish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪʃ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken chiefly in Turkey and parts of Cyprus, and by Turkish communities worldwide
the main language of Turkey

Examples

He studied Turkish for three years before traveling to Istanbul.
Many signs in Cyprus are written in both Greek and Turkish.
She speaks English, Turkish, and a little Arabic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Turkic - a family of closely related languages of Central Asia, including Turkish

Etymology

From Turkish 'Türkçe', from Türk (Turk) + the suffix '-çe' (language of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06939575-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.kɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.kɪʃ / (BrE)
the language of Turkey, belonging to the Turkic language family and spoken mainly by Turks in Turkey and surrounding regions
the main language of Turkey

Examples

He learned to speak Turkish fluently during his stay in Istanbul.
Many books are published each year in Turkish to support education.
The airline staff were able to help in both English and Turkish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Turkic - a family of related languages spoken across Central Asia and Eastern Europe

Etymology

From Turkey, via Medieval Latin 'Turcus' (Turk).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɝː.kɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.kɪʃ / (BrE)
Relating to Turkey, its people, or their language.
relating to Turkey or its people/language

Examples

I bought a beautiful Turkish rug during my trip to Istanbul.
She loves the rich flavors of Turkish cuisine, especially baklava and kebabs.
His parents speak Turkish at home, so he became fluent as a child.

Synset Examples

Turkish towels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkey - a country at the crossroads of Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Turkey + -ish, denoting relation to the country or its people.
