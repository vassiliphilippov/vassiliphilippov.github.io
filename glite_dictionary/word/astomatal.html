astomatal

1. ADJ

/ əˈstoʊmətl̩ / (AmE), / æˈstɒmətl̩ / (BrE)
(of a plant or plant part) lacking a stoma or stomata; having no natural openings for gas exchange.
lacking stomata (biology)

Examples

The astomatal surface of certain aquatic plants limits water loss through evaporation.
Some mosses are astomatal throughout their life cycle, relying on passive diffusion for gas exchange.
In an astomatal leaf, gas exchange occurs directly through the epidermal layer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stomatal - having or relating to stomata

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + stomatal (from stoma, mouth, opening).
