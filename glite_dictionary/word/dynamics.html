dynamics

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mechanics concerned with the forces and motions of bodies
branch of mechanics studying forces and motion

Examples

She is studying dynamics in her mechanical engineering course.
The principles of dynamics help engineers predict how objects will move under different forces.
Understanding dynamics is essential for analyzing the motion of vehicles and machinery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kinematics - the branch of mechanics concerned with the motion of objects without reference to forces

Etymology

From Greek dynamis (power, force), via French dynamique.
