Sceloporus undulatus

1. NOUN

/ skəˌlɑːpərəs ˌʌn.dʒəˈleɪ.təs / (AmE), / skɪˌlɒpərəs ˌʌn.djʊˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
A small, active lizard species native to the United States and extending north to British Columbia, commonly known as the eastern fence lizard.
a species of North American lizard

Examples

Researchers observed the Sceloporus undulatus basking on sunlit logs in the forest.
The Sceloporus undulatus can rapidly change color to regulate its body temperature.
Many herpetologists study the territorial behaviors of Sceloporus undulatus in their natural habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern fence lizard - common name for Sceloporus undulatus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sceloporus' (genus name) from Greek 'skelos' (leg) and 'poros' (pore), 'undulatus' (wavy), referring to the lizard's appearance.
