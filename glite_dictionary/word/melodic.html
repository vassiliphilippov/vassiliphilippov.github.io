melodic

1. ADJ

/ məˈlɑːdɪk / (AmE), / məˈlɒdɪk / (BrE)
having a pleasant or tuneful melody; relating to or characterized by melody.
having a pleasing melody

Examples

The piano piece she played was exceptionally melodic and enchanting.
He prefers melodic tunes over those that are loud or harsh.
Many listeners praised the band's melodic style for its emotional depth.

Synset Examples

the melodious song of a meadowlark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodious - having a sweet, pleasant, or musical sound
  • Antonym: tuneless - lacking a pleasing melody or musical quality

Etymology

From French mélodique, from Greek melōidikos (of singing or melody).

2. ADJ

/ məˈlɑːdɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈlɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing melody; having a pleasing tune or musical quality.
relating to melody; tuneful

Examples

The song's melodic line lingered in her mind long after it ended.
His playing has a distinctly melodic quality, making the piece enjoyable to listen to.
Many composers admired her melodic sense and ability to create memorable tunes.

Synset Examples

melodic harmony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuneful - having a pleasant or melodious sound
  • Synonym: melodious - pleasant-sounding, musical, or tuneful
  • Antonym: unmelodic - not having a melody or pleasing tune

Etymology

From Greek *melōidikos* via French *mélodique*, relating to melody.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02875350-a
Synset POS: a
