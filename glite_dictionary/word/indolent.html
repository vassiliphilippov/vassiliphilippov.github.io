indolent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.də.lənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dələnt / (BrE)
(of tumors or diseases) developing, progressing, or healing slowly and usually causing little or no pain.
slow-developing or healing (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor's report described the mass as an indolent tumor that required only careful observation.
Her grandmother suffered from an indolent ulcer that healed very slowly over the years.
Unlike aggressive cancers, some lymphomas are classified as indolent and may not need immediate treatment.

Synset Examples

an indolent ulcer
leprosy is an indolent infectious disease

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggressive - (of diseases or tumors) progressing rapidly and typically causing severe symptoms

Etymology

From Latin 'indolens', from 'in-' (not) + 'dolere' (to feel pain).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.də.lənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dələnt / (BrE)
habitually lazy or disinclined to exert oneself; avoiding activity or work
habitually lazy; avoiding exertion

Examples

Despite his indolent attitude, he somehow managed to finish his assignments on time.
The indolent cat slept for hours in the warm sunlight.
Her indolent approach to her studies worried her parents.

Synset Examples

faineant kings under whose rule the country languished
an indolent hanger-on
too lazy to wash the dishes
slothful employees
the unemployed are not necessarily work-shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy
  • Antonym: industrious - diligent and hard-working
  • Synonym: slothful - lazy, slow-moving, or inactive

Etymology

From Latin 'indolens', from 'in-' (not) + 'dolens' (feeling pain), originally meaning insensitive to pain.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
