to publish

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃ / (BrE)
to have one's work prepared and released for public sale, distribution, or readership, especially in print or digital form
issue work for public distribution

Examples

After years of research, she finally published her findings in a leading scientific journal.
The famous novelist published his latest book last month to critical acclaim.
Some musicians choose to publish their songs online instead of through traditional record labels.

Synset Examples

She published 25 books during her long career
Mozart didn't publish all of his works

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - make something available to the public
  • Antonym: to withhold - refuse to publish or make public

Etymology

From Latin publicare (to make public), from publicus (public).

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃ / (BrE)
to make information, a book, newspaper, or other material available to the public, especially by printing and distributing it
make information publicly available, usually by printing

Examples

The university plans to publish a new history textbook next year.
Authors often dream of seeing their novels published by a major company.
Every scientific journal carefully reviews articles before they publish them.

Synset Examples

The newspaper published the news of the royal couple's divorce
This news should not be printed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: print - to produce text or images on paper with a machine
  • Antonym: to suppress - to prevent information from being published or made public

Etymology

From Latin 'publicare' (to make public) via Old French 'publier'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01749530-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃ / (BrE)
to prepare and issue (a book, magazine, newspaper, or other material) for public distribution or sale
prepare and issue for public distribution

Examples

The company decided to publish a new edition of the textbook this year.
She hopes to publish her first novel before turning thirty.
Several scientific journals publish the results of groundbreaking research every month.

Synset Examples

publish a magazine or newspaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to make something available for general use or knowledge
  • Antonym: to suppress - to prevent something from being published or made public

Etymology

From Latin 'publicare' (to make public) via Old French 'publier'.
