dioxin

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑksɪn / (AmE), / daɪˈɒksɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of highly toxic chemical compounds, some of which occur as by-products in herbicides and other industrial processes
toxic chemical compound (by-product of herbicides)
medical

Examples

Recent studies have found dangerous levels of dioxin in the soil near the old chemical plant.
Exposure to dioxin has been linked to serious health issues, including cancer and birth defects.
Scientists are researching ways to neutralize dioxin contamination in water supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polychlorinated biphenyl - another group of toxic chemical compounds related to industrial contamination

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'oxys' (sharp, acid), referencing its chemical structure.
