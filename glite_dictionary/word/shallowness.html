shallowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having little physical depth.
the state of being shallow

Examples

Check the shallowness of the water before letting children swim there.
The shallowness near the shore makes it safe for beginners to practice snorkeling.
Divers should be careful of the shallowness at this end of the pool.

Synset Examples

take into account the shallowness at that end of the pool before you dive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depth - the distance from the top or surface to the bottom of something
  • Synonym: shallowness - lack of intellectual or emotional depth

Etymology

From 'shallow' + '-ness', an Old English formation indicating a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking depth, especially in knowledge, understanding, or feeling
lack of depth in knowledge or feeling

Examples

His shallowness became obvious during the serious discussion about politics.
She was frustrated by the shallowness of their conversation, which never moved beyond small talk.
Critics pointed out the shallowness of the film's characters and plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficiality - the quality of being superficial; lack of thoroughness or depth
  • Antonym: depth - profoundness; the quality of being deep in knowledge or feeling

Etymology

From 'shallow' + '-ness', after Middle English 'schalowe' (not deep).
