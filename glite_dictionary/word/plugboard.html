plugboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌɡˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈplʌɡˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a board used in early telephone exchanges or computers where connections between circuits are made using patch cords
board for connecting circuits with patch cords

Examples

The operator connected calls manually by inserting cords into the plugboard.
Early computers relied on a plugboard to route signals for calculations.
The plugboard allowed quick reconfiguration of the telephone exchange circuitry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switchboard - a panel used to connect telephone calls manually

Etymology

From 'plug' + 'board', describing a board with sockets for plugs.
