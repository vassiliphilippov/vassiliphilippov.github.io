Union

1. NOUN

/ ˈjun.jən / (AmE), / ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
the United States as a single nation, especially the northern states during the American Civil War period
the United States, especially the North in the Civil War

Examples

He had family members fighting on both sides of the Union and the Confederacy.
After the Civil War, the Union was preserved, but many scars remained.
Maryland was a border state whose loyalty to the Union was crucial during the conflict.

Synset Examples

he has visited every state in the Union
Lee hoped to detach Maryland from the Union
the North's superior resources turned the scale

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Confederacy - the southern states that seceded from the U.S. during the Civil War
  • Synonym: North - the northern states of the U.S., especially during the Civil War

Etymology

From Latin 'unio' (oneness, unity), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːniən / (AmE), / ˈjuːniən / (BrE)
of or relating to the northern states of the United States and those loyal to the federal government during the American Civil War (1861–65).
relating to the northern side in the US Civil War

Examples

Many Union soldiers marched south in the early years of the Civil War.
The Union army won a significant victory at Gettysburg.
His grandfather served as a Union officer during the conflict.

Synset Examples

Union soldiers
Federal forces
a Federal infantryman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Confederate - of or relating to the southern states that seceded from the US during the Civil War

Etymology

From Latin 'unio' (oneness, unity), referring to the preservation of the United States as a single nation during the Civil War.

Synset POS: s
