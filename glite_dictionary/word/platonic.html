platonic

1. ADJ

/ pləˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / pləˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a relationship or affection) intimate and affectionate but not sexual or romantic; free from physical desire.
relating to affection free from physical desire

Examples

Although they spent much time together, their friendship remained strictly platonic.
She values platonic relationships because they allow for deep connection without romance.
He insists that his feelings for her are platonic and not romantic.

Synset Examples

platonic love

Related Terms

  • Antonym: romantic - characterized by or relating to love involving sexual attraction

Etymology

From Plato, the Greek philosopher, whose writings discussed idealized, non-physical love.
