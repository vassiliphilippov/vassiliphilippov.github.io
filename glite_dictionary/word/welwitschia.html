welwitschia

1. NOUN

/ wɛlˈwɪtʃiə / (AmE), / wɛlˈwɪtʃɪə / (BrE)
a remarkable desert plant native to southwestern Africa, known for its thick trunk and only two long, persistent leaves; considered a living fossil
desert plant with two long leaves; living fossil

Examples

The welwitschia thrives in some of the harshest deserts of Namibia and Angola.
A single welwitschia can live for thousands of years, making it one of the oldest plants on Earth.
Scientists study the welwitschia to understand how plants adapt to extreme environments.

Etymology

Named after Friedrich Welwitsch, the Austrian botanist who discovered it.
