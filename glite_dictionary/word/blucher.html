blucher

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbluːtʃə / (BrE)
A type of high shoe or boot with laces running over the tongue, usually featuring two side flaps for the laces.
high shoe or boot with laces over the tongue

Examples

He wore polished bluchers to the formal dinner.
The blucher design offers more comfort and flexibility than an Oxford shoe.
Shop assistants recommended bluchers for their easy fit and classic look.

Etymology

Named after Prussian general Gebhard Leberecht von Blücher, who popularized this style for his troops.
