rad

1. NOUN

/ ræd / (AmE), / ræd / (BrE)
a unit of absorbed ionizing radiation equal to the absorption of 100 ergs per gram of material
unit of absorbed ionizing radiation

Examples

Scientists measured the exposure in rads after the nuclear accident.
The patient received a dose of 50 rads during the treatment.
Older research papers often report radiation in rad units rather than grays.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gray - SI unit of absorbed dose of ionizing radiation, equal to 100 rads

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'radiation absorbed dose'.

2. NOUN

/ ræd / (AmE), / ræd / (BrE)
a unit of plane angle equal to the angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc equal in length to the radius; short for radian, approximately 57.295 degrees
unit of plane angle (radian)

Examples

The angle was measured as two rad in the experiment.
Trigonometric functions are often evaluated in rad rather than degrees.
This calculator can display results in either degrees or rad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radian - the full form of 'rad' as a unit of angle
  • Antonym: degree - a unit for measuring angles, equal to 1/360 of a full rotation

Etymology

Shortened from 'radian', formed from 'radius' + 'an'.
