inauspiciously

1. ADV

/ ɪn.ɔˈspɪʃ.əs.li / (AmE), / ɪn.ɔːˈspɪʃ.əs.li / (BrE)
In a manner that suggests a lack of success or good fortune; in an unpromising or unfavorable way.
in a way that is unpromising or unlucky

Examples

The meeting began inauspiciously, with technical problems delaying the start.
Their vacation started inauspiciously, as their luggage was lost at the airport.
He inauspiciously tripped on the stage while accepting his award.

Synset Examples

he started his new job inauspiciously on Friday the 13th

Related Terms

  • Antonym: auspiciously - in a way that is promising or indicates good fortune

Etymology

From 'inauspicious' + '-ly'. Ultimately from Latin 'auspicium' (divination by birds).
