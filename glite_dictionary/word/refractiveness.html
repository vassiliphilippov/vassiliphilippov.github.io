refractiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfræktɪvnɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of a material that determines how much it bends or changes the direction of light passing through it, as measured by its index of refraction.
quality of bending light; index of refraction

Examples

Different materials have varying degrees of refractiveness that affect how light travels through them.
The refractiveness of water is responsible for the bending of a straw in a glass.
Scientists measure the refractiveness of substances to design better optical lenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refractivity - capacity of a substance to refract light

Etymology

From 'refractive' + '-ness', based on Latin 'refringere' (to break up).
