halyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæljɚd / (AmE), / ˈhæljəd / (BrE)
a rope used for raising or lowering a sail, flag, or yard on a ship or at a flagpole
rope for raising a sail or flag

Examples

The sailor pulled the halyard to hoist the mainsail.
He checked the halyard before raising the flag at dawn.
A frayed halyard can make it difficult to lower the sails quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - a general term for a rope used on a boat

Etymology

From Middle English 'halier', related to 'haul', from Old French 'haler'.
