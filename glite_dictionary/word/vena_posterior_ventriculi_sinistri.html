vena posterior ventriculi sinistri

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnə pɑːˈstɪəriər vɛnˈtrɪkjʊlaɪ sɪnˈɪstraɪ / (AmE), / ˈviːnə pɒˈstɪəriə vɛnˈtrɪkjʊlaɪ sɪnˈɪstriː / (BrE)
A vein that arises near the apex of the heart and empties into the coronary sinus, draining the posterior portion of the left ventricle.
vein draining the posterior left ventricle
medical

Examples

The vena posterior ventriculi sinistri plays an important role in cardiac venous drainage.
During cardiac surgery, careful identification of the vena posterior ventriculi sinistri is necessary to avoid complications.
Researchers studied the flow through the vena posterior ventriculi sinistri in patients with heart disease.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'posterior' (behind), 'ventriculi sinistri' (of the left ventricle).
