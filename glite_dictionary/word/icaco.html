icaco

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈkɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈkɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree (Chrysobalanus icaco) bearing edible fruit resembling a plum.
tropical American tree with edible fruit

Examples

The icaco is often cultivated for its sweet, plumlike fruit.
Several birds were seen perching in the icaco near the riverbank.
She collected icacos to make a traditional jelly.

Etymology

From Taíno, through Spanish 'icaco'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈkɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
A plum-shaped fruit, varying in color from whitish to almost black, native to tropical America, and often used for preserves.
plum-shaped tropical American fruit

Examples

The icaco is popular in the Caribbean for making sweet preserves and jams.
After tasting the ripe icaco, Maria described its flavor as mild and pleasantly tart.
Local markets sell fresh icacos alongside other tropical fruits during the summer months.

Etymology

From Taíno *ikako*, adopted via Spanish.
