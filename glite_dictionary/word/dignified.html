dignified

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪɡnəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪd / (BrE)
having or showing a calm, serious, and self-respecting manner that commands respect from others
having or showing dignity and self-respect

Examples

She remained dignified throughout the difficult proceedings, never losing her composure.
His dignified response to the criticism won him the respect of his colleagues.
The ambassador gave a dignified speech that impressed everyone present.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undignified - lacking in dignity or self-respect
  • Synonym: noble - having fine personal qualities; showing high moral principles

Etymology

From English "dignify" + "-ed"; ultimately from Latin "dignitas" (worthiness).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪd / (BrE)
having or showing a composed or serious manner that is worthy of respect; formal or stately in appearance or behavior
having a manner or appearance worthy of respect

Examples

The ambassador maintained a dignified silence throughout the heated debate.
She walked into the room with a dignified air that demanded attention.
Even in defeat, he remained calm and dignified.

Synset Examples

her dignified demeanor
the director of the school was a dignified white-haired gentleman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undignified - not dignified; lacking dignity or decorum
  • Synonym: stately - impressive in appearance or manner

Etymology

From Latin dignificatus, past participle of dignificare ‘to esteem, to honor’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00755046-a
Synset POS: a
