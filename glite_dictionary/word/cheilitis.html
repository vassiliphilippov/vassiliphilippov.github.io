cheilitis

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / kaɪˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the lips, often causing redness, cracking, and soreness of the skin
inflammation of the lips
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with cheilitis after developing cracked and inflamed lips.
Chronic cheilitis can result from frequent lip licking or exposure to harsh weather.
Treatment for cheilitis often involves moisturizing the lips and addressing any underlying causes.

Etymology

From Greek kheilos (lip) + -itis (inflammation).
