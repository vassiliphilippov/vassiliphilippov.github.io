Besseya alpina

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛsiə ælˈpaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈbɛsiə ælˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A small perennial plant with dense spikes of pale bluish-violet flowers, native to high, cold meadows from Wyoming and Utah to New Mexico.
a plant with pale bluish-violet flowers of high-altitude western US meadows

Examples

The rare Besseya alpina blooms each summer in the highest Colorado meadows.
Botanists have documented the distribution of Besseya alpina across several mountain states.
During our hike, we spotted clusters of Besseya alpina beside the melting snow.
