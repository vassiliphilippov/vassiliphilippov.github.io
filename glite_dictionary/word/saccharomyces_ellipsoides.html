Saccharomyces ellipsoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌsækəroʊˈmaɪsiːz ɛˌlɪpsoʊˈaɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌsakərəˈmʌɪsiːz ɛˌlɪpsəʊˈɪdiːz / (BrE)
a species of yeast used in wine fermentation
yeast species used in wine making

Examples

Saccharomyces ellipsoides is vital for initiating the alcoholic fermentation process in wine production.
Researchers isolated Saccharomyces ellipsoides from several vineyards to study fermentation efficiency.
Many winemakers select Saccharomyces ellipsoides to achieve specific flavor profiles in their wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - microscopic fungi used in fermentation

Etymology

From Greek 'sakcharon' (sugar) and 'mykēs' (fungus), and Latin 'ellipsoides' (ellipse-shaped).
