sackful

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæk.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈsæk.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a sack; as much as a sack will hold.
the amount a sack holds

Examples

He brought a sackful of potatoes from the farm.
After the harvest, they had sackfuls of apples stored in the barn.
A sackful of mail arrived at the office this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bagful - the amount contained in a bag

Etymology

Formed from 'sack' + '-ful' (denoting 'full of').

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæk.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈsæk.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that is enough to fill a sack, often used to refer to a large quantity of something.
amount filling a sack

Examples

They collected a sackful of walnuts from the orchard.
After months of saving, he carried a sackful of coins to the bank.
The children donated a sackful of toys to the charity.

Synset Examples

She brought a sackful of apples to the picnic.
We harvested a sackful of potatoes this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bagful - an amount that fills a bag
  • Synonym: bucketful - an amount that fills a bucket

Etymology

Formed from 'sack' + '-ful', following the pattern of container-based quantity nouns (e.g. handful, cupful).
