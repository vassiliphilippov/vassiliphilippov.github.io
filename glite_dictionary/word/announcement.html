announcement

1. NOUN

/ əˈnaʊnsmənt / (AmE), / əˈnaʊnsmənt / (BrE)
a formal public statement or notice provided to inform people of something
formal public statement or notice

Examples

The company issued an announcement regarding the new policy changes.
We awaited the official announcement of the election results all evening.
The principal's announcement about the upcoming holiday was met with cheers from the students.

Synset Examples

the government made an announcement about changes in the drug war
a declaration of independence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declaration - a formal or explicit statement or announcement

Etymology

From announce + -ment; from Old French annoncer, from Latin annuntiare (to announce).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnaʊnsmənt / (AmE), / əˈnaʊnsmənt / (BrE)
a public or formal statement that gives information about something that has happened or will happen
public or formal statement

Examples

The company made an announcement about the launch of its new product.
Please listen carefully to the announcement before boarding the train.
The wedding announcement was posted in the local newspaper.

Synset Examples

the announcement appeared in the local newspaper
the promulgation was written in English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declaration - a formal or explicit statement or announcement
  • Synonym: statement - a definite or clear expression in speech or writing
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or did not happen

Etymology

From announce + -ment; announce from Old French 'anoncer', from Latin 'annuntiare' (to report).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06759293-n
Synset POS: n
