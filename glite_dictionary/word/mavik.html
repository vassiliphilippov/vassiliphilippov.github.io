Mavik

1. NOUN

/ ˈmævɪk / (AmE), / ˈmævɪk / (BrE)
A trademarked drug (trandolapril), an ACE inhibitor used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure, or after a heart attack.
ACE inhibitor drug (trandolapril)
medical

Examples

After his heart attack, the doctor prescribed Mavik to help lower his blood pressure.
Many patients tolerate Mavik well with few side effects.
The cardiologist explained that Mavik works by relaxing blood vessels, making it easier for the heart to pump.
