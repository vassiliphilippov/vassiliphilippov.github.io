boustrophedon

1. NOUN

/ buːˌstrɑːfəˈdɑːn / (AmE), / buːˌstrɒfɪˈdiːɒn / (BrE)
an ancient writing system in which alternate lines are written in opposite directions, resembling the way an ox turns when ploughing a field
ancient writing with alternating direction

Examples

The ancient Greek tablets were inscribed in boustrophedon, making them challenging to read.
Some early Etruscan manuscripts use a boustrophedon format rather than writing every line left to right.
The term boustrophedon comes from the Greek words for 'ox' and 'to turn.'

Etymology

From Greek boustrophēdon, from bous (ox) + strophē (turn), referring to the ox’s turning when ploughing.

2. NOUN

/ buːˌstrɑːfrəˈdɑːn / (AmE), / buːˌstrɒfrɪˈdɒn / (BrE)
A method of writing in which lines alternate direction, going left to right and then right to left, as seen in some ancient inscriptions.
writing alternating left-to-right and right-to-left

Examples

The archaeologists discovered an inscription written in boustrophedon.
Some early Greek texts were recorded using the boustrophedon method.
Understanding boustrophedon scripts requires adjusting to the alternating line directions.

Synset Examples

The artist chose a boustrophedon layout for his poem.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek boustrophēdon, meaning 'turning like oxen in plowing'.
