consignment

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈsaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the official act of placing someone in confinement, such as committing them to prison or a mental institution
official act of confining a person
legal

Examples

The judge ordered the consignment of the defendant to the state psychiatric facility.
After the trial, his consignment to prison was carried out immediately.
There are strict procedures governing the consignment of individuals to mental hospitals.

Etymology

From consign + -ment; from Latin consignare (to seal, mark).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈsaɪnmənt / (BrE)
a batch of goods that are sent or delivered, especially by a large vehicle such as a truck or ship, typically for sale or storage
batch of goods sent or delivered

Examples

The truck arrived with a consignment of fresh produce from the farm.
This consignment required special handling because of its fragile contents.
They inspected the entire consignment before signing for delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipment - act of sending goods, or the goods sent

Etymology

From French 'consigner', from Latin 'consignare' (to seal, register).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02967841-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kənˈsaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈsaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the delivery of goods to an agent for sale, storage, or disposal, usually without transferring ownership until the goods are sold
delivery of goods for sale or disposal

Examples

The antiques arrived on consignment and will be sold next week.
The shop accepts clothes for consignment and pays the owner once they are sold.
She sent the artwork on consignment to a gallery in Paris hoping for a good price.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipment - a batch of goods sent to a place

Etymology

From Latin consignare (to seal, sign), via French consigner and English consign.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
