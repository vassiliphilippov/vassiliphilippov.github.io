actually

1. ADV

/ ˈæk.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈaktʃ.u.əli / (BrE)
used to emphasize what is really true, especially when it is surprising or contrasts with a previous statement or belief
in fact; really; in actual fact

Examples

I didn’t think I’d like the movie, but I actually enjoyed it a lot.
She said she was busy, but she was actually at the café with her friends.
Many people believe the rumor, but it’s not actually true.

Synset Examples

to be nominally but not actually independent
no one actually saw the shark
large meteorites actually come from the asteroid belt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: really - in actual fact; truly; genuinely
  • Antonym: apparently - as far as one knows or can see; seemingly

Etymology

From actual + -ly, ultimately from Latin 'actualis' (active, practical).

2. ADV

/ ˈæk.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.əli / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something is true, especially when it is surprising or the opposite of what was expected.
in fact, surprisingly true

Examples

I was surprised that he actually finished the entire marathon.
She actually apologized for her mistake, which we didn't expect.
We thought the shop was closed, but it was actually open all night.

Synset Examples

you may actually be doing the right thing by walking out
she actually spoke Latin
they thought they made the rules but in reality they were only puppets
people who seem stand-offish are in reality often simply nervous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: really - used to emphasize truth, similar to 'actually'
  • Antonym: apparently - used to suggest something appears to be true, possibly contrary to reality; near antonym in this sense

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'actual', from Late Latin 'actualis' (active, practical), from Latin 'actus' (act).

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈæk.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈaktʃʊəli / (BrE)
Used to add emphasis to a statement, often to introduce a contradiction or to express surprise or clarification.
used to add emphasis to a statement

Examples

Actually, I was planning to leave early this evening if that's all right.
He didn't apologize—as a matter of fact, he seemed actually rather pleased.
I don't mind helping at all, actually.

Synset Examples

actually, we all help clear up after a meal
actually, I haven't seen the film
I'm not all that surprised actually
she hasn't proved to be too satisfactory, actually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: really - to a degree or as an intensifier for truth or fact
  • Degree Higher: literally - used for emphasis, especially to state something is true in a strict sense

Etymology

From actual + -ly, modeled on Latin actualiter or Middle French actualement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00150802-r
Synset POS: r
