mastership

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəʃɪp / (BrE)
great skill or expertise in a particular activity, especially as shown by a master
great skill or expertise; mastery

Examples

Her mastership of the piano was evident in every note she played.
Years of dedicated practice led to his mastership in the art of calligraphy.
The chef’s mastership impressed the entire culinary community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastery - comprehensive knowledge or skill in a subject or accomplishment

Etymology

From Middle English 'maistirschip', from 'master' + '-ship'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, state, or authority of being a master, especially in a particular context such as an organization, vessel, or profession
the position of master

Examples

After years of dedication, he finally achieved mastership of the guild.
The captain's mastership of the ship was undisputed by the crew.
She assumed mastership of the estate upon her father's retirement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leadership - the position or function of a leader
  • Antonym: servitude - the state of being a servant or slave

Etymology

From master + -ship, Old English -scipe (state, condition, office).
