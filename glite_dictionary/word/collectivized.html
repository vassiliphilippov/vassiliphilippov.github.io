collectivized

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzd / (BrE)
Organized on the basis of ownership by the state or the people of the means of production, typically under a system of collective or state control.
organized under state or collective ownership

Examples

The farms were collectivized as part of the government's agrarian reform policy.
Many critics argue that collectivized industries can stifle innovation and efficiency.
In the 20th century, several countries adopted collectivized economic systems with varying success.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: private - individually or corporately owned, not by the state or people

Etymology

Derived from 'collectivize', based on 'collective' and the suffix '-ize', meaning to make or become collective.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪˌvaɪzd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzd / (BrE)
Subscribing to or characterized by the principle of collective ownership and control, especially under socialism.
relating to collective ownership

Examples

Many farms were collectivized during the Soviet era, changing the structure of rural society.
The collectivized approach to managing resources prioritizes shared ownership over private interests.
A collectivized economy can affect individual incentives and innovation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individualized - made individual; given distinctive character
  • Synonym: collective - done by people acting as a group

Etymology

From 'collectivize' + '-ed'; 'collectivize' from 'collective' (from French 'collectif', from Latin 'collectivus').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00299373-s
Synset POS: s
