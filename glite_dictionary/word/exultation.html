exultation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛɡˌzʌlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛɡˌzʌlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the expression of great joy or triumph, especially through joyful sounds or shouts
joyful expression or triumphant feeling

Examples

The stadium burst into exultation as the team scored the winning goal.
His cries of exultation echoed through the hall after hearing the good news.
The air was filled with laughter and exultation on New Year's Eve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jubilation - a feeling or expression of great happiness and triumph
  • Antonym: despair - complete loss or absence of hope

Etymology

From Latin exsultatio, from exsultare (to leap up, rejoice).

2. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzʌlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzʌlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of great or triumphant joy; elation or jubilation
intense joy or jubilation

Examples

She cried out in exultation when her team won the championship.
His face shone with exultation after he received the award.
The crowd erupted in exultation as the final goal was scored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jubilation - a feeling of great happiness and celebration
  • Antonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope

Etymology

From Latin exsultatio (a leaping for joy), from exsultāre (to leap up)
