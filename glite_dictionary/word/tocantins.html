Tocantins

1. PROPN

/ toʊˈkænˌtiːnz / (AmE), / təʊˈkænˌtiːnz / (BrE)
A river in eastern Brazil that flows generally northward into the Pará River.
river in eastern Brazil

Examples

The Tocantins River is an important waterway for transporting goods in eastern Brazil.
Researchers studied fish diversity in the lower Tocantins after the construction of a major dam.
Flooding along the Tocantins has impacted several riverside communities in recent years.

Etymology

From Portuguese Tocantins, possibly from a Tupi language meaning 'toucan's beak'.
