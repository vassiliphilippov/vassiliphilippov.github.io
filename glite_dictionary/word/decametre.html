decametre

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəmiːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to ten meters.
metric unit of length (10 meters)

Examples

The field measured exactly one decametre in width.
They spaced the trees every two decametres along the path.
One decametre is equivalent to ten meters in the metric system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: meter - the base unit of length in the metric system
  • Degree Higher: hectometre - a metric unit of length equal to one hundred meters

Etymology

From French 'décamètre,' from Greek 'deka' (ten) + 'metron' (measure).
