retrial

1. NOUN

/ riˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtraɪ.əl / (BrE)
a new trial of a case in the same court, typically ordered when the first trial was improper or unfair due to errors in procedure
a new trial ordered after an improper or unfair first trial
legal

Examples

After the judge declared a mistrial, the court scheduled a retrial for later in the year.
New evidence presented by the defense prompted the judge to grant a retrial.
The defendant's lawyers argued that a retrial was necessary due to jury misconduct in the original case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - the formal examination of evidence in court

Etymology

From re- (again) + trial.
