enophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.əˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈiː.nəʊ.faɪl / (BrE)
A person who enjoys and appreciates wine, especially as a connoisseur.
wine enthusiast; wine lover

Examples

As a true enophile, she traveled through France visiting famous vineyards.
He joined a local wine club to meet fellow enophiles and expand his collection.
Many enophiles keep detailed notes about every bottle they taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oenophile - Person with a fondness for wine (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'oinos' (wine) + '-phile' (lover of).
