senatorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnəˌtɔrˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɛnətəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or term of being a senator
the office or position of a senator

Examples

She won the senatorship after a closely contested election.
His senatorship lasted for two terms before he retired from politics.
The responsibilities of a senatorship include making and voting on national laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senator - a member of a senate

Etymology

From 'senator' + '-ship', denoting office or position.
