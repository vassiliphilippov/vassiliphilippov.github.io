Gruyere

1. NOUN

/ gruːˈjɛr / (AmE), / gruːˈjɛə / (BrE)
A firm Swiss cheese with a pale yellow color and small holes, known for its nutty flavor.
a type of Swiss cheese with small holes

Examples

She added grated Gruyere to the top of the French onion soup.
The quiche recipe calls for a generous amount of Gruyere cheese.
At the market, he found imported Gruyere for his cheese platter.

Etymology

From the French 'Gruyère', named after the town of Gruyères in Switzerland.
