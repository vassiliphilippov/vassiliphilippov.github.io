imperiousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being domineering, commanding, or arrogantly authoritative
the state of being domineering or overbearing

Examples

Her imperiousness made it hard for her colleagues to work with her.
He spoke with a tone of imperiousness that demanded obedience.
Many resented the manager's constant imperiousness in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - the quality of having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humility - modest view of one's own importance

Etymology

From Latin 'imperiosus' (commanding, mighty) via Old French 'impérieus' and English 'imperious.'
