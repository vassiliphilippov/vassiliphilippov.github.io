Palmales

1. NOUN

/ pɑːlˈmeɪliːz / (AmE), / pɑːlˈmɑːliːz / (BrE)
an order of monocotyledonous flowering plants comprising the palms; now usually transferred to the family Arecaceae (Palmae)
order of plants comprising the palms
medical

Examples

Scientists classify most types of palms within the order Palmales.
Recent research suggests that the traditional grouping Palmales is now generally considered synonymous with Arecaceae.
The Palmales are studied extensively for their unique adaptations to tropical environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arecaceae - the botanical family comprising the palms; also known as Palmae

Etymology

From Latin 'palma' (palm tree) + '-ales', a standard taxonomic suffix for plant orders.
