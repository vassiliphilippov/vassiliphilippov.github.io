maxilla

1. NOUN

/ mækˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / mækˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
the upper jawbone in vertebrates, which is typically fused to the cranium and holds the upper teeth
upper jawbone in vertebrates
medical

Examples

The dentist pointed out that the fracture extended through the maxilla and required surgery.
In many mammals, the maxilla houses the canine and incisor teeth.
The X-ray revealed a tumor growing near the left maxilla.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mandible - the lower jawbone

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla', meaning 'jaw'.
