fourpence

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːr.pəns / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.pəns / (BrE)
a former English silver coin equal to four pennies, used in Britain before decimalization
a former English coin worth four pennies
british archaic

Examples

The merchant handed him a fourpence in change after his purchase.
In Victorian England, a fourpence could buy a loaf of bread and some butter.
Collectors often seek out rare examples of the old fourpence for their coin collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groat - a former silver coin worth four old pence in Britain

Etymology

From 'four' + 'pence', referring to its value of four pennies.
