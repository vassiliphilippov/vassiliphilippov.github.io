Pellaea rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈliːə roʊˌtʌndɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / pɛˈliːə rəʊˌtʌndɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
a fern native to New Zealand and Australia, with trailing fronds bearing dark green, buttonlike leaflets
fern with buttonlike leaflets, native to New Zealand and Australia

Examples

The Pellaea rotundifolia is a popular choice for indoor gardens due to its attractive foliage.
Many gardeners appreciate Pellaea rotundifolia for its resilience and compact form.
The distinctive round leaves of Pellaea rotundifolia make it easy to identify among ferns.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pellaea' is the genus name, and 'rotundifolia' means 'round-leaved.'
