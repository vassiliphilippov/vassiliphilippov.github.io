Eresh-kigal

1. PROPN

/ ɛˈrɛʃ kɪˌɡɑl / (AmE), / ɛˈrɛʃ kɪˌɡɑːl / (BrE)
The goddess of the underworld and death in Mesopotamian mythology, consort of Nergal.
Mesopotamian goddess of the underworld

Examples

In ancient texts, Eresh-kigal is described as ruling the realm of the dead with stern authority.
Legends often recount the story of Eresh-kigal and her consort Nergal.
The myth of Inanna's descent into the underworld features Eresh-kigal as a central figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nergal - Mesopotamian god of the underworld and decay

Etymology

From Sumerian *Ereš-ki-gal* (Lady of the Great Earth/Underworld).
