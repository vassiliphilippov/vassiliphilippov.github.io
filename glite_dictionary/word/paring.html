paring

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeərɪŋ / (BrE)
a small piece cut off from the surface of a fruit or vegetable, especially the skin or peel; often used in the plural form
piece of fruit or vegetable cut off while peeling

Examples

She swept the potato parings into the compost bin after making dinner.
A pile of apple parings collected on the cutting board as he worked.
Always discard the orange parings before juicing the fruit.

Synset Examples

she could peel an apple with a single long paring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peel - the outer skin of a fruit or vegetable
  • Synonym: rind - the tough outer layer of certain fruits and vegetables

Etymology

From the verb 'pare,' from Old French 'parer' (to trim), from Latin 'parare' (to prepare).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeərɪŋ / (BrE)
a thin piece or fragment that is cut or shaved from something, especially wood, fruit, or vegetables
thin piece cut or shaved off

Examples

I swept the apple parings into the trash after peeling the fruit.
Wood parings covered the floor of the workshop after hours of carving.
She used a small knife to remove the potato parings before boiling them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaving - a thin slice removed from a surface with a knife or implement

Etymology

From Old French *parier* (to prepare, trim), from Latin *parāre* (to prepare).

Synset POS: n
