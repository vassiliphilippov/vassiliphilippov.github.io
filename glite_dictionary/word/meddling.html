meddling

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of interfering in something, especially in a secret or improper way
interference, usually improper

Examples

The politician was accused of meddling in the committee's decisions.
His constant meddling made the project much harder than it should have been.
There was evidence of meddling with the computer files before the audit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - the act of getting involved in a situation where you are not wanted
  • Degree Lower: involvement - the act of being involved or taking part in something, sometimes unwanted

Etymology

From the verb 'meddle', from Old French 'mesler' (to mix, meddle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) interfering in matters that do not concern them, especially in an intrusive or unwanted way.
interfering; intrusive

Examples

She found her neighbor's meddling attitude increasingly annoying over time.
The meddling uncle tried to organize everyone's affairs at the family reunion.
His meddling in the dispute only made things worse for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interfering - involving oneself in matters that do not concern one
  • Synonym: officious - assertive of authority in an annoyingly domineering way, especially with regard to trivial matters

Etymology

From Middle English medlen, from Old French mesler (to mix, meddle).
