to parti-color

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːrtiˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtiˌkʌlə / (BrE)
to color or decorate something with different colors, especially in a motley or variegated pattern
color with different colors; make motley

Examples

The artist chose to parti-color the walls, creating a vibrant and playful atmosphere in the room.
Nature seemed to parti-color the petals with splashes of red, yellow, and orange.
To celebrate the festival, villagers would parti-color their costumes in bold, contrasting hues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motley - to make variegated in color or pattern

Etymology

From Old French 'particoler', based on 'parti' (divided) + 'color' (colour).
