lenient

1. ADJ

/ ˈliniənt / (AmE), / ˈliːniənt / (BrE)
Not strict; allowing more freedom and less punishment than expected.
not strict or harsh

Examples

The judge was surprisingly lenient with the young offender, giving him a warning instead of jail time.
Her parents are quite lenient when it comes to curfew, rarely punishing her for coming home late.
Some schools have lenient policies regarding homework deadlines.

Synset Examples

an easy teacher
easy standards
lenient rules
an easy penalty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strict - demanding that rules are followed exactly, opposite of lenient
  • Synonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow opinions or behavior that one does not necessarily agree with

Etymology

From Latin *lenis* (mild, gentle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈliːniənt / (AmE), / ˈliːnɪənt / (BrE)
Not strict; inclined to show tolerance and mercy, especially in punishing or judging others.
tolerant or merciful, not strict

Examples

The teacher was lenient when grading the late assignments, accepting excuses from her students.
His lenient attitude towards minor mistakes made him popular with his employees.
Some people believe that parents today are too lenient with their children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strict - demanding total obedience or exactness, harsh or rigorous
  • Synonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness despite having power to punish

Etymology

From Latin 'lenient-', present participle of 'lenire' (to soften), from 'lenis' (soft, mild).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈliniənt / (AmE), / ˈliːniənt / (BrE)
not strict or harsh; showing tolerance and mercy, especially in enforcing rules or punishing wrongdoing
not strict; tolerant or merciful

Examples

The teacher was surprisingly lenient with students who handed in their assignments late.
Her parents were known for being lenient about curfew compared to other families.
The judge took a lenient approach, giving the defendant a second chance.

Synset Examples

indulgent parents risk spoiling their children
too soft on the children
they are soft on crime

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strict - demanding rigid adherence to rules; not lenient
  • Synonym: indulgent - having or indicating a tendency to be overly generous to or lenient with someone

Etymology

From Latin 'lenis' (mild, gentle) via French 'léniant'.
