tramontana

1. NOUN

/ trəˌmɒnˈtɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌtræ.mɒnˈtɑː.nə / (BrE)
a cold, dry wind that blows from the mountains into Italy and the western Mediterranean
cold mountain wind in Italy and western Mediterranean

Examples

In winter, the strong tramontana swept through the village, keeping everyone indoors.
Sailors on the western Mediterranean often watch for signs of the tramontana before setting out to sea.
The olive groves suffered some damage after the intense tramontana last week.

Etymology

From Italian 'tramontana', meaning 'north wind'; ultimately from Latin 'transmontanus' (beyond the mountains).
