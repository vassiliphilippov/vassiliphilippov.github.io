Pereskia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛskiə / (AmE), / pəˈrɛskiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubby trees and woody climbers with slender branches, broad flat leaves, and large clusters of flowers.
genus of tropical American plants with broad leaves

Examples

The Pereskia is unique among cacti for retaining its leafy appearance.
Botanists are fascinated by the climbing habit of several Pereskia species.
In tropical gardens, Pereskia can be grown for its attractive flowers and foliage.

Etymology

Named after French scientist Nicolas-Claude Fabri de Peiresc.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛskiə / (AmE), / pəˈrɛskɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubby trees and woody climbers, notable for their slender branches, broad flat leaves, and large clusters of flowers.
a genus of tropical American shrubs and climbers

Examples

The Pereskia genus is unusual among cacti for having broad leaves instead of spines.
Botanists discovered a new species within Pereskia in the rainforests of Brazil.
Several Pereskia plants were thriving in the botanical garden's cactus collection.

Etymology

Named after the French botanist Nicolas-Claude Fabri de Peiresc (1580–1637).
