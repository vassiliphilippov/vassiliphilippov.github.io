dolour

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.lər / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.lə / (BrE)
A feeling of deep sorrow or painful grief, especially as expressed in poetry or old literature.
deep sorrow; painful grief (poetic)
archaic

Examples

The ancient poet wrote of his dolour at the fall of his city.
She expressed her dolour in mournful verses that moved all who read them.
Even in happiness, a shadow of dolour lingered in his heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment
  • Synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Old French *dolour*, from Latin *dolor* (pain, grief).
