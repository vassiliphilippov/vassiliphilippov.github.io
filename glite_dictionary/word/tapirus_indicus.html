Tapirus indicus

1. NOUN

/ təˈpaɪɹəs ɪnˈdɪkəs / (AmE), / təˈpʌɪrəs ɪnˈdɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of tapir native to the forests of Malaysia, Sumatra, and southern Thailand, notable for its distinctive black-and-white coloring
Malayan tapir species

Examples

The Tapirus indicus is easily recognized by its striking black-and-white coloration.
Habitat loss poses a serious threat to the survival of Tapirus indicus in Southeast Asia.
Zoologists observed a family of Tapirus indicus feeding near the river bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - large, herbivorous mammal with a short, prehensile snout

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Tapirus' and the species epithet 'indicus' meaning 'of India' (referring to Southeast Asia).
