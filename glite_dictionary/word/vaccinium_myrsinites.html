Vaccinium myrsinites

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm mɪrˈsaɪnaɪtiːz / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm mɪəˈsaɪniːtiːz / (BrE)
A shrub native to the eastern United States, with shining evergreen leaves and bluish-black berries.
a North American evergreen shrub with bluish-black fruit

Examples

Vaccinium myrsinites grows abundantly in pine forests throughout Florida and the southeastern United States.
The bluish-black berries of Vaccinium myrsinites are popular with many bird species in the region.
Botanists studied the evergreen leaves of Vaccinium myrsinites to better understand its ecological role.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Vaccinium' is the genus name for blueberries, from Medieval Latin, and 'myrsinites' refers to resemblance to myrtle (Myrsinus).
