insufferably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsʌf.ɚ.əb.li / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌf.ər.əb.li / (BrE)
In an extremely unpleasant, arrogant, or intolerable manner.
in an extremely unpleasant or arrogant way

Examples

He behaved insufferably during the meeting, constantly interrupting everyone else.
She spoke insufferably about her achievements, making it difficult for others to join the conversation.
The heat was insufferably intense, forcing everyone to stay indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intolerably - in an unbearable or impossible-to-endure way

Etymology

From 'insufferable' + '-ly', originating from Latin 'insufferabilis'.

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈsʌfərəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌf(ə)rəbli / (BrE)
To a degree or in a manner that is impossible to endure; unbearably.
to an unbearable degree

Examples

The insufferably arrogant tone of his speech made everyone uncomfortable.
It grew insufferably humid during the afternoon, making it hard to concentrate.
She found the constant noise in the apartment insufferably irritating.

Synset Examples

it was insufferably hot in the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbearably - to an unbearable degree

Etymology

Formed from 'insufferable' + '-ly'; 'insufferable' from Latin 'insufferabilis'.

Source: WordNet

3. ADV

/ ɪnˈsʌf.ər.ə.bli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌf.ər.əbli / (BrE)
To an extreme and unbearable degree; in an insufferable manner.
to an unbearable or intolerable degree

Examples

The heat in the room became insufferably intense by midday.
She found his arrogance insufferably irritating during the entire meeting.
The movie was insufferably long and tedious, so we left before it ended.

Synset Examples

it was insufferably hot in the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intolerably - to an intolerable degree
  • Antonym: bearably - to a bearable degree

Etymology

From 'insufferable' + '-ly'; 'insufferable' from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'sufferre' (to endure).

Synset POS: r
