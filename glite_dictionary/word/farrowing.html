farrowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfarəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of a sow giving birth to a litter of pigs
the birth of piglets by a sow

Examples

The farmer made sure the barn was clean before the sow’s farrowing.
Proper care of piglets begins immediately after farrowing to ensure their survival.
The vet assisted with the difficult farrowing to help both the sow and her piglets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parturition - the act of giving birth (technical term)

Etymology

From 'farrow' (a young pig), of Old English origin.
