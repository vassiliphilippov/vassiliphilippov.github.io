Bankia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋ.ki.ə / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.kɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of large marine bivalve mollusks known as giant shipworms, which bore into wood submerged in seawater
a genus of giant shipworm bivalves

Examples

The Bankia species play a major role in the degradation of wooden ships and piers in saltwater environments.
Scientists were amazed by the size and wood-boring abilities of the Bankia found off the coast.
Museum exhibits often include preserved shells of Bankia to illustrate marine wood-boring organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - a marine bivalve mollusk that bores into wood, destroying ships and piers

Etymology

Modern Latin, named by John Edward Gray in 1842; origin of the name is unclear, but may honor a person named Bank.
