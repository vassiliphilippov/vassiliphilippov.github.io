Olympics

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɪmpɪks / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪks / (BrE)
An international sporting event held every four years in which athletes from many countries compete in a variety of sports, modeled after the ancient Greek Olympic Games.
international multi-sport event held every four years

Examples

Hosting the Olympics was a major event for the country, requiring years of preparation.
Thousands of athletes train for years to qualify for the Olympics.
The 2016 Olympics were held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - alternative term for the same global multi-sport event

Etymology

From Greek Olympia, the site of the ancient Olympic Games.
