assemblyman

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛmbliˌmæn / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbliˌman / (BrE)
a male member of a legislative assembly, especially in certain US states
male member of a legislative assembly

Examples

The assemblyman proposed new legislation to improve public education in his district.
After serving two terms, the assemblyman decided not to seek reelection.
Citizens met with their local assemblyman to discuss concerns about environmental issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assemblywoman - female member of a legislative assembly
  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws, especially as a member of a legislature

Etymology

From 'assembly' and 'man', following the pattern of occupational titles.
