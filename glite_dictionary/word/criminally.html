criminally

1. ADV

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəli / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəli / (BrE)
in a way that is outrageously, shamefully, or extremely bad, often used for emphasis rather than literal criminality
outrageously or shamefully (emphatic)

Examples

He was criminally careless with confidential information, putting the project at risk.
The apartment was criminally overpriced considering its poor condition and location.
It was criminally unfair to blame her for the team's mistakes.

Synset Examples

the garden was criminally neglected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamefully - in a manner worthy of shame or disgrace

Etymology

From 'criminal' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00293079-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəli / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves a violation of the law or is characteristic of a crime
in a criminal manner

Examples

The company was found to have acted criminally negligent in its safety procedures.
He was prosecuted for criminally misappropriating funds from the charity.
Altering the records was deemed criminally fraudulent by the court.

Synset Examples

the alterations in the document were ruled to be criminally fraudulent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegally - in a manner not allowed by law
  • Antonym: lawfully - in a manner permitted by law

Etymology

From 'criminal' + '-ly', with 'criminal' from Latin 'criminālis' (criminal, relating to crime).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292903-r
Synset POS: r
