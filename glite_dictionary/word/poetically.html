poetically

1. ADV

/ poʊˈɛtɪkli / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is characteristic of poetry, using beautiful or imaginative language.
in a poetic manner

Examples

She described the autumn landscape poetically, evoking vivid images with her words.
His speech was poetically crafted, full of rich metaphors and rhythm.
The novel opens with a scene that is poetically rendered, captivating the reader immediately.

Synset Examples

poetically expressed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosaically - in a way that lacks poetic beauty; in a matter-of-fact manner

Etymology

From 'poetical', ultimately derived from Greek 'poietikos' (pertaining to making or poetry).
