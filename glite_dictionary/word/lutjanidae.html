2. NOUN

/ luːtˈdʒænəˌdiː / (AmE), / luːtˈdʒænɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes known as snappers, typically characterized by a slender body, large mouth, and prominent canine teeth
family of marine fishes (snappers)

Examples

Researchers studied the migration patterns of several species within the Lutjanidae family.
Commercial fishing regulations often focus on protecting juvenile Lutjanidae to prevent overfishing.
The vibrant colors of Lutjanidae make them popular in marine aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a common name for any fish in the family Lutjanidae

Etymology

From New Latin Lutjanidae, from Lutjanus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
