decrementation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkrəmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkrɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the operation of subtracting one from a number or variable, especially in computing or mathematics.
subtracting one from a value (technical)

Examples

In programming, a decrementation operation is often used within loops to reduce a counter by one.
The software automatically performs a decrementation each time a resource is deleted.
Understanding both incrementation and decrementation is essential for basic algorithm design.

Synset Examples

The second parameter provides an encoding of either one of the incrementation or decrementation operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incrementation - the operation of adding one to a number or variable

Etymology

From Latin 'decrementum' (a decrease) + '-ation' (action or process).
