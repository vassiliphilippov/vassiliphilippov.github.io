Lichanura trivirgata

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈnʊr.ə traɪˈvɜːr.gə.tə / (AmE), / lɪ.kəˈnjuː.rə traɪˈvɜː.gə.tə / (BrE)
A species of small, nonvenomous boa found in the rocky deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, commonly known as the rosy boa.
species of small desert-dwelling boa

Examples

The Lichanura trivirgata can be found sheltering under rocks in the Sonoran Desert.
Researchers studied the behavior of Lichanura trivirgata in its natural habitat.
Many reptile enthusiasts keep Lichanura trivirgata as a pet due to its docile nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosy boa - common English name for Lichanura trivirgata

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Lichanura + species epithet trivirgata, meaning 'three-striped'.
