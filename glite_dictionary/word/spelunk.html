to spelunk

1. VERB

/ spəˈlʌŋk / (AmE), / spəˈlʌŋk / (BrE)
to explore natural caves as a hobby or for scientific purposes
explore caves

Examples

Every summer, they spelunk in the limestone caves just outside town.
Before you spelunk unfamiliar caverns, it's best to travel with an experienced guide.
She has spelunked some of the deepest cave systems in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cave - a large underground chamber, typically in a hillside or cliff
  • Synonym: spelunker - a person who explores caves

Etymology

Back-formation from 'spelunker', from Latin 'spelunca' (cave), via French and Late Latin.
