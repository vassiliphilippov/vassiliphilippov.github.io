Shoshone

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈʃoʊni / (AmE), / ʃəˈʃəʊni / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting the western United States, especially Idaho, Utah, and Nevada
member of a Native American people of the western US

Examples

Several Shoshone attended the cultural festival to share their traditions with visitors.
The Shoshone were instrumental in guiding early European explorers across the Rocky Mountains.
She is proud of her Shoshone heritage and often participates in tribal ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shoshoni - another spelling for the Shoshone people or their language

Etymology

From the Shoshone people's own name for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈʃoʊni / (AmE), / ʃəˈʃəʊni / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Shoshone people, native to the western United States
Uto-Aztecan language of the Shoshone people

Examples

Few people still speak Shoshone fluently in the United States today.
She is taking a course to learn Shoshone as part of her heritage.
Researchers are documenting the grammar of Shoshone to help preserve it for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uto-Aztecan - a family of Native American languages

Etymology

From Shoshone Shoshóni, a self-designated name for the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06927578-n
Synset POS: n
