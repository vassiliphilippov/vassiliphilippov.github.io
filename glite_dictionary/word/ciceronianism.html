Ciceronianism

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌsɛroʊˈniːɪzəm / (AmE), / sɪˌsɛrəʊˈniːɪzəm / (BrE)
the imitation of or adherence to the oratorical or literary style of Cicero, especially as practiced by certain writers during the Renaissance
imitation of Cicero's style

Examples

The scholar's essays were marked by a pronounced Ciceronianism that echoed the rhetoric of ancient Rome.
During the Renaissance, Ciceronianism became the ideal for many humanist writers seeking eloquence.
Some critics argue that excessive Ciceronianism can lead to artificial and overly ornate prose.

Synset Examples

Ciceronianism of the narrowest variety towards a more direct and natural form of expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicist - person who studies or imitates classical (especially ancient Greek and Roman) literature and art

Etymology

From Latin Ciceronianus (of Cicero) + English -ism.
