Eucalyptus gunnii

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkælɪptəs ˈɡʌniː / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs ˈɡʌniː / (BrE)
A species of small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Tasmania, commonly known as the cider gum; cultivated for ornamental use and valued for its aromatic leaves.
Tasmanian tree, also called cider gum

Examples

The Eucalyptus gunnii is prized in gardens for its striking blue-grey foliage.
Farmers in Tasmania have long tapped the Eucalyptus gunnii for its sweet sap.
Young Eucalyptus gunnii trees require protection from frost during their first winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cider gum - Common name for Eucalyptus gunnii, tree from Tasmania

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Ronald Campbell Gunn, Scottish botanist, explorer, and politician in Tasmania.
