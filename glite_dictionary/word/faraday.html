Faraday

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛrəˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfærəˌdeɪ / (BrE)
Michael Faraday (1791–1867), an English physicist and chemist known for his discovery of electromagnetic induction.
English physicist and chemist (1791–1867)

Examples

The discoveries of Faraday fundamentally changed the understanding of electricity and magnetism.
Many universities have laboratories named after Faraday in recognition of his contributions.
During the lecture, the professor discussed Faraday and his experiments with electromagnetic fields.
