spotting

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of causing spots or stains to appear on something
the act of making spots or stains

Examples

After the science experiment, there was significant spotting on the lab coat.
The old photographs showed spotting due to water damage over the years.
She noticed some spotting on the tablecloth after the dinner party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staining - the act of applying a stain or becoming stained
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of detecting or noticing something, especially by catching sight of it
the act of detecting or noticing

Examples

After the spotting of whales, tourists rushed to the side of the boat to take photos.
His keen sense of spotting rare birds made him a valued member of the group.
The security team's spotting of the suspicious package prevented a possible incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detection - the act of discovering or noticing something
  • Antonym: overlooking - failing to notice or see something
