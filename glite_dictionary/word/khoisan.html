Khoisan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪˌsɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪsæn / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken in southern Africa, characterized by the use of click consonants
southern African language family

Examples

Linguists have studied the unique click sounds found in the Khoisan languages.
The cultural heritage of the region is closely tied to the Khoisan people and their language family.
Many Khoisan languages are endangered due to declining numbers of native speakers.

Etymology

Formed from the names of two groups: Khoikhoi and San.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪˌsɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪsɑːn / (BrE)
A collective term for the indigenous peoples of southern Africa comprising the San (Bushmen) and the Khoikhoi (Hottentots), traditionally nomadic hunters and gatherers.
indigenous peoples of southern Africa

Examples

The Khoisan have a long history of living in the deserts and savannahs of southern Africa.
Many linguists study the unique clicking sounds in Khoisan languages.
Archaeologists have found ancient rock paintings created by the Khoisan.

Etymology

From Khoikhoi and San, the two main ethnic groups grouped under this term.
