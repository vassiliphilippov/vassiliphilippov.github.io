detailed

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːˌteɪld / (AmE), / ˈdiː.teɪld / (BrE)
developed or executed with great attention to small facts or particulars; including many details
including many small or precise details

Examples

She gave a detailed account of what happened during the meeting.
The police prepared a detailed report of the incident.
His drawing was so detailed that every tiny feature was visible.

Synset Examples

a detailed plan
the elaborate register of the inhabitants prevented tax evasion
the carefully elaborated theme

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - involving many careful details or complicated parts

Etymology

From 'detail' + '-ed', meaning 'having details'. 'Detail' from French 'détail' (a small item, detail), from 'détailler' (to cut in pieces).
