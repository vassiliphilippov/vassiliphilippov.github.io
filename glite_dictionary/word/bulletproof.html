bulletproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊl.ɪt.pruːf / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ɪt.pruːf / (BrE)
incapable of being disproved, refuted, or penetrated by criticism; without any flaws or loopholes
impossible to disprove or attack

Examples

Her lawyer presented a bulletproof argument in court that the prosecution could not challenge.
They needed a bulletproof contract to make sure all responsibilities were clear.
His bulletproof plan left no room for error or misunderstanding.

Synset Examples

an ironclad contract
a watertight alibi
a bulletproof argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watertight - impossible to contradict or find fault with; particularly in reference to arguments or alibis
  • Synonym: ironclad - unbreakable or impossible to contradict; especially laws, contracts, or agreements
  • Antonym: flawed - having faults or weaknesses

Etymology

From 'bullet' + 'proof', originally describing material resistant to bullets; figurative use attested from mid-20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʊlɪtˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪtˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make (something) resistant to bullets, especially by reinforcing it with special materials
make resistant to bullets

Examples

The president's team decided to bulletproof the limousine for added security.
It can be expensive to bulletproof your home, but some people think it's necessary.
Technicians worked overnight to bulletproof the vehicle before its next mission.

Synset Examples

bulletproof the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor - to protect or cover with armor

3. ADJ

/ ˈbʊlɪtˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪtˌpruːf / (BrE)
(of material or object) not penetrable by bullets; able to stop bullets from passing through.
impervious to bullets

Examples

The police officer wore a bulletproof vest during the raid.
They installed bulletproof glass in the reception area for added security.
The limousine was equipped with bulletproof windows to protect its passengers.

Synset Examples

bulletproof glass
bulletproof vest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor-plated - reinforced or covered with armor for protection

Etymology

Formed from 'bullet' + 'proof', following the pattern of 'proof' meaning protected from, as in 'waterproof'.
