Sonora semiannulata

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔɹə ˌsɛmiˌænjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / səˈnɔːrə ˌsɛmiˌanjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A small, brightly ringed, terrestrial snake of arid or semiarid regions of western North America, known as the Western Ground Snake.
species of Western Ground Snake

Examples

Sonora semiannulata is often found in sandy or rocky soils across the American Southwest.
Researchers observed that Sonora semiannulata displays a variety of color patterns, from red and black rings to solid brown.
Despite its vivid appearance, Sonora semiannulata is harmless and typically avoids human contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Western Ground Snake - Common name for Sonora semiannulata, a small terrestrial snake of western North America

Etymology

From Latin; 'Sonora' refers to the region (Sonoran Desert), 'semiannulata' means 'half-ringed', referring to its banded appearance.
