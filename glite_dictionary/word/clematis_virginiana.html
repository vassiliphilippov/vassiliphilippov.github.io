Clematis virginiana

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs vɜrˌdʒɪˈneɪniə / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs vɜːˌdʒɪˈneɪnɪə / (BrE)
A species of climbing vine native to eastern North America, known for its clusters of small creamy white flowers.
North American climbing vine with creamy white flowers

Examples

The old fence was covered by a thick mass of Clematis virginiana in full bloom.
Gardeners often plant Clematis virginiana for its attractive white flowers and vigorous growth.
In late summer, Clematis virginiana transforms the woodland edges with its fragrant panicles.

Etymology

Clematis from Greek klēmatis (climbing plant); virginiana refers to Virginia (US state), indicating origin.
