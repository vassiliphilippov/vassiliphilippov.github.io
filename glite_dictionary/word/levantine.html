Levantine

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɛˈvænˌtin / (AmE), / ˌlɛˈvænˌtiːn / (BrE)
relating to the Levant or its inhabitants, cultures, or languages
of or relating to the Levant region

Examples

The Levantine diet is known for its use of fresh vegetables and olive oil.
Many historic cities along the Levantine coast have been centers of trade for centuries.
The Levantine languages include various dialects spoken in the eastern Mediterranean.

Synset Examples

the Levantine coast

Etymology

From French 'levantin', from Levant (the eastern Mediterranean region).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03092290-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛvənˈtin / (AmE), / ˌlɛvənˈtiːn / (BrE)
(formerly) a native or inhabitant of the Levant, a geographical region in the eastern Mediterranean.
native or inhabitant of the Levant

Examples

Several prominent Levantines played important roles in 19th-century trade.
The family identified culturally as Levantines and spoke several Mediterranean languages.
During Ottoman times, European powers often sought alliances with local Levantines.

Etymology

From French Levantin, from Levant (the eastern Mediterranean) + -ine.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09740098-n
Synset POS: n
