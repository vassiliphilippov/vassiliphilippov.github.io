harem

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrəm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːrɛm / (BrE)
The part of a Muslim household reserved for wives, concubines, and female relatives.
part of a house for women (in Muslim households)

Examples

The palace harem was strictly off-limits to all men except the sultan.
Guides explained how the women lived secluded lives within the harem.
She wrote her thesis on the roles of women in the Ottoman harem.

Etymology

From Turkish harem, from Arabic ḥarīm (forbidden place, sanctuary).
