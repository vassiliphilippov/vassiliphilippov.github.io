Acer campestre

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər kæmˈpɛstri / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə kæmˈpɛstri / (BrE)
a shrubby Eurasian maple species, commonly known as the field maple, often used for hedges and landscaping
Eurasian maple tree species (field maple)

Examples

The Acer campestre is popular in Europe for creating natural hedges and windbreaks.
Gardeners often choose Acer campestre for its resilience and attractive autumn foliage.
We planted several Acer campestre along the driveway to provide shade and privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field maple - another name for Acer campestre

Etymology

From Latin: 'acer' (maple tree) and 'campestre' (of the field).
