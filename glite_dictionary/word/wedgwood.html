Wedgwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdʒ.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɛdʒ.wʊd / (BrE)
a type of fine pottery, typically with white classical decoration on a blue background, made by Josiah Wedgwood and his successors
type of fine pottery with white decoration

Examples

She cherished her grandmother's Wedgwood vase, which displayed delicate white figures against a pale blue background.
A set of Wedgwood plates was displayed in the museum's English ceramics collection.
Collectors often seek out rare pieces of Wedgwood to add to their antique pottery collections.

Etymology

Named after Josiah Wedgwood (1730–1795), English potter and founder of the pottery company.

2. PROPN

/ ˈwɛdʒ.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɛdʒ.wʊd / (BrE)
Josiah Wedgwood (1730–1795), an English potter renowned for founding the Wedgwood company and revolutionizing the pottery industry.
English potter and industrialist (1730–1795)

Examples

Wedgwood was a pioneer in the industrialization of pottery manufacturing.
Many fine porcelain pieces bear the Wedgwood mark, indicating their origin.
The influence of Wedgwood can still be seen in modern ceramic designs.

Etymology

Surname of Josiah Wedgwood, founder of the Wedgwood pottery company.
