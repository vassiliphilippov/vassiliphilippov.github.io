phrasal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfreɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a phrase, especially in grammar.
relating to a phrase; grammatical

Examples

The term phrasal is commonly used in linguistic textbooks to describe properties of phrases.
A phrasal verb, like 'look up', combines a verb with a preposition or adverb.
Many grammatical constructions have unique phrasal patterns in English.

Synset Examples

phrasal verb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrasal verb - a verb made up of a main verb with one or more particles

Etymology

From 'phrase' + '-al' (forming adjectives from nouns).
