Mozambican

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌzæmˈbiːkən / (AmE), / məʊˌzæmˈbiːkən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Mozambique
native/inhabitant of Mozambique

Examples

Many Mozambicans speak both Portuguese and local African languages at home.
Several Mozambicans participated in the international conference on sustainable development.
His parents are Mozambican, but he was born and raised in South Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mozambique - a country in southeast Africa

Etymology

From Mozambique, the country in southeast Africa, + -an (demonymic suffix).
