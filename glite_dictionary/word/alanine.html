alanine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌnin / (AmE), / ˈaləniːn / (BrE)
A crystalline amino acid found in many proteins, important for metabolism and protein synthesis.
an amino acid found in proteins
medical

Examples

Foods such as meat and beans are rich in alanine.
Scientists measured the alanine concentration in the blood samples.
Alanine plays an important role in energy production within muscle tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycine - a simple amino acid found in proteins
  • Synonym: serine - an amino acid found in many proteins, involved in metabolism

Etymology

From German 'Alanin', derived from 'Aldehyd' (aldehyde) referencing its chemical structure.
