Maya

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of Yucatán, Belize, and Guatemala, known for their advanced civilization, especially in architecture, astronomy, and writing, flourishing especially between AD 300 and 900
member of an ancient Mesoamerican civilization

Examples

The ancient Maya were known for their remarkable achievements in mathematics and astronomy.
Archaeologists have discovered new temples built by the Maya in the jungle.
Many descendants of the Maya still live in present-day Guatemala and speak their traditional languages.

Synset Examples

Mayans had a system of writing and an accurate calendar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan - relating to the Maya people or their language

Etymology

From Spanish 'maya', from Yucatec Maya 'máayáa', the name of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a group of related indigenous languages spoken by the Maya peoples of Mexico and Central America
family of indigenous Central American languages

Examples

The Maya languages are still spoken by millions of people in Central America.
Many linguists study the Maya family to understand ancient Mesoamerican cultures.
She is fluent in multiple Maya dialects, including Yucatec and Kʼicheʼ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yucatec - a specific Mayan language spoken in the Yucatán Peninsula
  • Synonym: Quiché - a Mayan language spoken in the highlands of Guatemala

Etymology

From Spanish maya, from a native Yucatec Maya term ('maaya' for the language and people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06931991-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of Mexico and Central America who speak Mayan languages and are native to regions including Yucatán, Guatemala, and Belize
indigenous people of Mexico and Central America

Examples

Many Maya continue to preserve their traditional customs and language in present-day Guatemala.
Archaeologists have studied the ruins left by the ancient Maya civilization for decades.
The modern Maya face challenges in maintaining their cultural heritage amidst globalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aztec - indigenous people of central Mexico with a distinct language and history
  • Synonym: Inca - indigenous people of South America, especially Peru, with a distinct language and empire

Etymology

From Spanish 'maya', from the name of their ancient civilization.

Synset POS: n
