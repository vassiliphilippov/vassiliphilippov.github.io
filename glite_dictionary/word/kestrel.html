kestrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛstrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛstrəl / (BrE)
a small falcon with long wings and tail, found chiefly in Eurasia and North America, often hovering while hunting for prey
small falcon species

Examples

We spotted a kestrel hovering above the meadow, looking for small animals to catch.
The kestrel is known for its ability to hover in mid-air while hunting.
Many birdwatchers are excited to see a kestrel during their hikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey with long pointed wings

Etymology

From Middle English castrel or kestrel, probably derived from Old French crecerelle (a kind of bird of prey).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛstrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛstrəl / (BrE)
a small Old World falcon, typically with a reddish-brown back and wings, that often hovers in the air while hunting
a small hovering falcon

Examples

A kestrel hovered motionless above the field, searching for its next meal.
We spotted a kestrel perched on the fence post early in the morning.
With sharp eyesight, the kestrel can spot even the smallest rodent from high above.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey with long pointed wings

Etymology

From Middle English *kastreel*, from Old French *crecerelle*, related to the bird's call.
