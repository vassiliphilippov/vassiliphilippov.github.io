phyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪli, ˈfaɪleɪ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪli, ˈfaɪleɪ / (BrE)
In ancient Athens, a division or tribe, especially any of the groups into which citizens were organized for political and social purposes.
ancient Athenian tribe or division

Examples

Each Athenian phyle was responsible for sending representatives to the city council.
After the reforms of Cleisthenes, the citizen body was organized into ten phylae.
Membership in a particular phyle influenced one's social and political life in Athens.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek φυλή (phulē, phylē) meaning 'tribe, clan.'
