Porzana

1. NOUN

/ pɔːrˈzeɪnə / (AmE), / pɔːˈzɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small waterbirds in the rail family, commonly known as spotted crakes.
genus of small waterbirds (spotted crakes)

Examples

The Porzana are often found in marshes across Europe and Asia.
Researchers observed several young Porzana feeding at the water's edge.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Porzana during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crake - any of several short-billed marsh birds of the rail family

Etymology

From New Latin, introduced by Louis Jean Pierre Vieillot in 1816, possibly from Venetian dialect.

2. NOUN

/ pɔrˈzeɪnə / (AmE), / pɔːˈzɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater birds in the rail family (Rallidae), commonly known as spotted crakes and their relatives.
a genus of small freshwater birds (rails)

Examples

The rare Porzana species are now the focus of conservation efforts due to habitat loss.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a Porzana near the edge of the marsh.
The Porzana genus includes several species with striking plumage and secretive habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crake - a small, secretive bird of the rail family
  • Degree Higher: Rallidae - the rail family of birds

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Porzana', the scientific genus name.
