passerine

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæsəˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈpasərɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of birds belonging to the order Passeriformes, especially perching birds such as sparrows and finches
relating to perching birds (Passeriformes)

Examples

Many birdwatchers are especially interested in studying passerine species due to their diversity.
The distinctive beak shape is a typical trait of passerine birds.
After migration, several passerine birds appeared in the hedgerows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscine - relating to songbirds, a subgroup of passerines

Etymology

From Latin 'passerinus', from 'passer' meaning 'sparrow'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsəˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈpasərɪn / (BrE)
a bird belonging to the largest order of birds (Passeriformes), typically small, perching, with feet adapted for grasping branches, including most songbirds
a perching bird in the order Passeriformes

Examples

Many common garden birds, such as sparrows and robins, are passerines.
Scientists study the migration patterns of passerines to understand climate change effects.
The newly hatched passerine chicks were completely dependent on their parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: songbird - a bird known for its melodic singing, typically a passerine

Etymology

From Latin passerinus, from passer (sparrow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01527000-n
Synset POS: n
