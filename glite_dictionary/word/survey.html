survey

1. VERB

/ sərˈveɪ / (AmE), / səˈveɪ / (BrE)
to examine or collect information about something, especially by asking people questions for statistical analysis
examine or collect data, usually statistically

Examples

The research team will survey 2,000 households to gather information about health habits.
Before launching the new product, the company decided to survey potential customers.
Every five years, the government surveys the population about housing conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poll - to collect opinions or votes by questioning people

Etymology

From Old French *sorveoir* (to look over), from Latin *supervidere* (to oversee).

2. VERB

/ sərˈveɪ / (AmE), / səˈveɪ / (BrE)
to formally inspect or review troops, especially during a ceremonial gathering or parade
formally inspect troops

Examples

The general will survey the troops before the ceremony begins.
After the commander surveyed the battalion, the parade officially started.
Dignitaries stood on the platform, surveying the soldiers as they marched past.

Etymology

From Anglo-French *surveier*, from Old French *surveoir*, from *sur-* (over) + *veoir* (to see), from Latin *vidēre* (to see).
