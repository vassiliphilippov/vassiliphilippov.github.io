illogical

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
not using clear or reasonable thinking; lacking sense or good reasoning.
not logical; unreasonable

Examples

It is illogical to assume results without sufficient evidence.
Her argument seemed illogical and difficult to follow.
The decision to cancel the event at the last minute was completely illogical.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logical - using valid reasoning or clear thinking
  • Synonym: irrational - not based on clear thought or reason

Etymology

From Latin 'illogicus', from 'in-' (not) + 'logicus' (logical).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
lacking clear or orderly continuity; not following a logical sequence or flow.
not logical; lacking continuity

Examples

The plot of the film became increasingly illogical and hard to follow.
Her explanation was so illogical that nobody could understand the sequence of events.
It would be illogical to organize the files without any clear system.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logical - following a clear, orderly sequence; reasoning in a rational manner
  • Synonym: confused - lacking clarity or order; difficult to follow

Etymology

From Latin 'illogicus', meaning 'not logical', from 'in-' (not) + 'logicus' (logical).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00467212-s
Synset POS: s
