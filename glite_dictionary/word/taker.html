taker

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkə / (BrE)
a person who accepts a bet or wager from another person
person who accepts a bet or wager

Examples

During the poker match, he acted as the taker for several side bets.
The casino often assigns a taker to keep track of wagers placed.
She looked for a taker willing to accept her risky bet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bettor - a person who places a bet or wager

Etymology

From the verb 'take' plus the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkə / (BrE)
a person who accepts or agrees to receive something that is offered
person who accepts an offer

Examples

There were no takers for the old bicycle, so it stayed in the garage.
She made an offer to sell her car, but she couldn't find a taker.
Is there any taker for this last slice of pizza?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptor - a person who accepts something
  • Antonym: refuser - a person who refuses something offered

Etymology

From take + -er, indicating one who takes or receives.

Synset POS: n
