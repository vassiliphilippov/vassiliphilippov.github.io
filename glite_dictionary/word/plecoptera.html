Plecoptera

1. NOUN

/ pliˈkɑp.tə.rə / (AmE), / plɪˈkɒp.tə.rə / (BrE)
An order of insects comprising the stoneflies, characterized by membranous wings and aquatic nymphs, commonly found near fresh water.
order of insects, stoneflies

Examples

Researchers collected several Plecoptera specimens from the riverbank for their aquatic insect survey.
The presence of Plecoptera larvae is often an indicator of good water quality in streams.
Many anglers study the life cycle of Plecoptera to select effective fishing flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonefly - a common name for insects of the order Plecoptera

Etymology

From Greek plekein (to braid) + pteron (wing), referring to the folded wings of these insects.
