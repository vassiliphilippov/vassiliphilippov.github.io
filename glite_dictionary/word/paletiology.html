paletiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæl.i.iˈɑː.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliːɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study or explanation of past events, especially by identifying their causes
study of causes of past events

Examples

Modern geology uses paletiology to understand how the landscape was formed.
The discussion focused on the importance of paletiology in reconstructing historical climate changes.
Through paletiology, scientists can determine the causes of ancient mass extinctions.

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + -logy (study of).
