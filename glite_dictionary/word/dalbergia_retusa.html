Dalbergia retusa

1. NOUN

/ ˌdælˈbɜrdʒiə rɛˈtusə / (AmE), / ˌdalˈbɜːdʒɪə rɛˈtjuːsə / (BrE)
a tropical South American tree valued for its hard, dense wood, often used in fine woodworking and musical instruments
valuable timber tree of tropical South America

Examples

The wood of the Dalbergia retusa is prized for its richness and durability.
Many luxury pens are crafted from the striking grain of Dalbergia retusa.
Strict regulations protect Dalbergia retusa due to concerns about illegal logging.

Etymology

Dalbergia named after Swedish botanist Nils Dalberg; retusa from Latin, meaning 'with a notched apex'.
