sanies

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.niːz / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.niːz / (BrE)
A thin, often foul-smelling fluid produced by certain types of inflamed or ulcerated tissue, especially in wounds.
fluid from inflamed tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted that sanies was draining from the infected wound.
A strong odor from sanies indicated a severe and persistent infection.
Proper cleaning is necessary to manage the accumulation of sanies near ulcerated tissue.

Etymology

From Latin 'sanies' (corrupt matter from a wound), related to 'sanus' (healthy).
