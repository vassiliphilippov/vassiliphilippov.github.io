Robin Goodfellow

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑbɪn ˈɡʊdˌfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɒbɪn ˈɡʊdˌfɛləʊ / (BrE)
a mischievous sprite or fairy in English folklore, also known as Puck, often causing playful trouble
mischievous sprite in English folklore

Examples

Many old stories describe Robin Goodfellow playing tricks on unwary travelers.
In Shakespeare's 'A Midsummer Night's Dream,' Puck is another name for Robin Goodfellow.
Children were warned that Robin Goodfellow might hide their shoes if they left them out at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Puck - a mischievous fairy or sprite in English folklore, especially in Shakespeare's 'A Midsummer Night's Dream'

Etymology

From Middle English folklore; 'Goodfellow' reflects the character's reputation as a playful, helpful, or tricky spirit.
