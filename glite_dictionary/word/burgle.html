to burgle

1. VERB

/ ˈbɜrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡl̩ / (BrE)
to enter a building illegally with the intention of stealing; to commit burglary
enter illegally to steal

Examples

The police believe someone tried to burgle the apartment last night.
Several houses in the area have been burgled this month.
He was caught in the act, attempting to burgle a jewelry store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burglarize - to commit burglary; American English equivalent
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From 'burglar', by back-formation (19th century, British English).
