seafarer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌfɛrər / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌfeərə / (BrE)
a person who works or travels on the sea, especially as a sailor
person working or traveling on the sea

Examples

As an experienced seafarer, he had sailed across every ocean in the world.
Many ancient seafarers relied on the stars to navigate their ships.
The life of a seafarer can be both adventurous and dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor - a person whose job is sailing or working on a ship
  • Antonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or sailing

Etymology

From Old English 'sæfaru' (sea journey), from 'sæ' (sea) + 'faru' (journey).
