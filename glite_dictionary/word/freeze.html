freeze

1. NOUN

/ friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
A period of weather cold enough to cause water and other liquids to freeze.
period of freezing weather

Examples

A sudden freeze last night damaged many of the crops.
The city warned residents to prepare for another hard freeze this weekend.
After the rain, a severe freeze turned the roads into ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frost - a thin layer of ice crystals formed on the ground or other surfaces during cold weather
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
