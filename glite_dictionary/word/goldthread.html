goldthread

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌθɹɛd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌθɹɛd / (BrE)
A low-growing North American woodland plant with trifoliate leaves, a yellowish rootstock, and white flowers (genus Coptis).
North American woodland plant

Examples

The herbalist collected goldthread roots for use in traditional medicine.
Delicate white flowers marked the patch of goldthread beneath the pine trees.
The bright yellow root of the goldthread stands out against the dark forest soil.

Etymology

Named for its yellow, threadlike roots; from 'gold' (the color) + 'thread'.
