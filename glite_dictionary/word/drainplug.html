drainplug

1. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪnˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
a removable plug used to seal the drain of a tub, basin, or similar container to hold water in
removable plug for a drain

Examples

The bathtub was full because the drainplug was tightly in place.
Remember to remove the drainplug before cleaning the sink.
He couldn't find the drainplug, so the water drained away immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper - a device used to close a hole or vent, especially in a container

Etymology

Formed from 'drain' and 'plug'.
