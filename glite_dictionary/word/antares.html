Antares

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtɛriz / (AmE), / ænˈtɑːriːz / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Scorpius, noted for its red color and prominence in the night sky
brightest star in Scorpius

Examples

Through the telescope, Antares appeared as a brilliant red point amid surrounding stars.
Antares is often called the 'heart of the scorpion' because of its position in the constellation.
Many cultures have myths and stories associated with Antares, reflecting its brightness and red hue.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀντάρης (Antárēs), meaning 'rival of Ares (Mars)', due to its reddish appearance.
