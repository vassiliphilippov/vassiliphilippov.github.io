bosh

1. NOUN

/ bɑʃ / (AmE), / bɒʃ / (BrE)
pretentious, foolish, or nonsensical talk or writing
nonsense; foolish talk

Examples

His explanation was dismissed as utter bosh by the committee.
Don't listen to that; it's just political bosh.
She called his claims complete bosh and refused to believe a word of them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are foolish or make no sense

Etymology

From Turkish 'boş' (empty, worthless), adopted into English in the 19th century.
