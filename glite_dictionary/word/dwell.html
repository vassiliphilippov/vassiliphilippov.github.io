to dwell

1. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
(of an idea, feeling, or quality) to exist or be present in a particular place, especially the mind or heart.
exist or reside within (esp. the mind or heart)

Examples

Doubts continued to dwell in her mind long after the event.
A deep sadness seemed to dwell within his heart.
Resentment can dwell inside us for years if left unaddressed.

Synset Examples

Strange notions inhabited her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reside - to live or be situated in a particular place
  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in or occupy a place

Etymology

Old English *dwellan* (‘lead astray, hinder’), later ‘remain, stay’ in Middle English.

2. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
(of a feeling, problem, or quality) to exist, be present, or have an origin in a particular place or situation.
to originate or exist in something

Examples

The real issue dwelled in the lack of communication between departments.
A sense of unease dwells in the community after the incident.
For many, hope dwells in the belief that change is possible.

Synset Examples

The problems dwell in the social injustices in this country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reside - to exist or be present in something permanently or for a long time

Etymology

From Old English ‘dwellan’ (to lead astray, hinder); later ‘linger’ or ‘remain’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02659476-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
to live in or occupy a place; to reside
live or reside in a place

Examples

Many ancient tribes used to dwell in these remote mountain valleys.
He prefers to dwell in urban areas rather than the countryside.
Some rare animals still dwell in the depths of this old forest.

Synset Examples

People lived in Africa millions of years ago
The people inhabited the islands that are now deserted
this kind of fish dwells near the bottom of the ocean
deer are populating the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in or occupy a place
  • Synonym: to reside - to live in a particular place
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place where one has dwelled

Etymology

From Old English 'dwellan' (to lead astray, hinder; later, to abide), with influence from Old Norse 'dvelja' (delay, linger; remain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02655932-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
to think or talk about something for a long time, especially with worry or sadness
think or talk at length about something (often worry)

Examples

Try not to dwell on your mistakes for too long.
She tends to dwell on negative thoughts when she's stressed.
Despite his success, he would often dwell upon past failures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ruminate - to think deeply about something

Etymology

Old English 'dwellan' (to lead astray, hinder); later also 'remain, stay'.

Source: WordNet
