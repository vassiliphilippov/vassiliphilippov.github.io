liable

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
likely to experience or suffer from something, especially something unpleasant or unwelcome.
likely to suffer or experience (unpleasant thing)

Examples

People living near the river are liable to flooding during the rainy season.
After so little sleep, you're liable to make mistakes at work.
He was liable to lose his temper if things didn’t go his way.

Synset Examples

he is apt to lose
she is liable to forget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prone - likely to suffer from or experience something, especially something unwelcome
  • Synonym: susceptible - easily influenced or harmed by something
  • Antonym: immune - protected or exempt, especially from something unpleasant

Etymology

From Old French *liable* (bound, obliged), from Latin *ligare* (to bind).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
legally responsible for something; subject to a legal obligation or penalty
legally responsible
legal

Examples

The company may be liable for damages if it fails to meet its contractual obligations.
As a parent, you could be held liable for your child's actions.
Owners are liable to pay property tax on all their real estate.

Synset Examples

men between the ages of 18 and 35 were liable for military service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsible - having a duty to deal with something or being accountable for something
  • Antonym: immune - not subject to a particular legal duty or penalty

Etymology

From Latin *ligābilis* (that can be bound), from *ligāre* (to bind), via Old French *liable*.
