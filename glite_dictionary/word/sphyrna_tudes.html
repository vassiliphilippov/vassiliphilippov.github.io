Sphyrna tudes

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪrnə ˈtuːdiːz / (AmE), / ˈsfɪənə ˈtjuːdiːz / (BrE)
A species of hammerhead shark, commonly known as the smalleye hammerhead, found in the western Atlantic Ocean and valued for its hide and vitamin-rich liver.
species of hammerhead shark (smalleye hammerhead)

Examples

The Sphyrna tudes is distinguished by its narrow, hammer-shaped head and golden eyes.
Fishermen in South America catch Sphyrna tudes for its valuable liver oil and tough hide.
Unlike other hammerheads, Sphyrna tudes prefers shallow coastal waters where it hunts small fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammerhead shark - a group of sharks with a distinctive hammer-shaped head

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sphyrna' meaning hammer (referring to the shape of the head), and 'tudes' is species-specific.
