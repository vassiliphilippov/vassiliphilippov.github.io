buteonine

1. ADJ

/ bjuːˈtiːəˌnaɪn / (AmE), / bjuːˈtiːəˌniːn / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a hawk of the genus Buteo.
of or like a Buteo hawk

Examples

The researcher identified the bird as a buteonine species due to its broad wings and soaring flight.
Many buteonine raptors can be observed migrating in large numbers during the autumn.
Her dissertation focuses on the hunting techniques of buteonine hawks found in North America.

Etymology

From New Latin Buteo (a genus of hawks) + English -ine (forming adjectives).

2. NOUN

/ bjuːˈtiːəˌnaɪn / (AmE), / bjuːˈtiːəˌnaɪn / (BrE)
Any hawk of the genus Buteo, characterized by broad wings and a soaring flight.
hawk of the genus Buteo

Examples

The red-tailed hawk is a common buteonine found throughout North America.
Researchers observed several buteonine species nesting in the canyon.
Unlike falcons, buteonine hawks typically soar with their wings held in a shallow 'V'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buteo - a genus of broad-winged, soaring hawks

Etymology

From New Latin 'Buteoninae', from the genus name 'Buteo'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01609950-n
Synset POS: n
