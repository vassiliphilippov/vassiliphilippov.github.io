ICBM

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ ˌsi ˌbiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌsiː ˌbiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a missile with the capability to travel from one continent to another, typically carrying nuclear warheads
intercontinental ballistic missile

Examples

The country successfully launched an ICBM during a military exercise last month.
Several nations have invested heavily in developing advanced ICBMs.
Tensions rose when reports surfaced of a new ICBM test.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: missile - an object that is forcibly propelled at a target

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'intercontinental ballistic missile.'
