virtuosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrtʃuˈɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌvɜːtjuˈɒsəti / (BrE)
exceptional technical skill, fluency, or style displayed by a virtuoso, especially in the arts or music
outstanding technical skill or artistry

Examples

The pianist's virtuosity was on full display during the demanding solo.
Critics praised the violinist's virtuosity and emotional intensity.
Her virtuosity in dance left the audience in awe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well; expertise
  • Antonym: clumsiness - lack of skill or dexterity

Etymology

From Italian 'virtù', meaning 'skill', itself from Latin 'virtus' (excellence, virtue).
