output

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpʊt / (BrE)
the signal, data, or information produced by an electronic system or device
signal or data produced by a device

Examples

The audio output from the computer was connected to the speakers.
You can adjust the video output settings for better image quality.
The technician measured the electrical output of the amplifier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: input - data or signal entering an electronic system

Etymology

From out + put, originally meaning 'thing put out' (1850s).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07279593-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpʊt / (BrE)
the amount of something produced by a person, machine, factory, or system, typically within a specific period
the amount produced; production

Examples

The factory increased its output of cars by 20% this year.
We need to measure the output of each department to assess performance.
Falling demand resulted in a reduction in energy output.

Synset Examples

production was up in the second quarter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: production - the process or result of making or manufacturing something
  • Antonym: input - resources or materials put into a process to achieve an output

Etymology

From out + put; earliest uses in early 19th century industrial contexts.
