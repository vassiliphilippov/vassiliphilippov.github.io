intussusception

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təs.səpˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tʌs.sɛpˈʃən / (BrE)
(biology) The increase in the surface area of a cell by incorporating new particles between existing ones in the cell wall.
cell surface growth by particle insertion
medical

Examples

The biologist studied how intussusception contributes to cell wall remodeling in plants.
Recent research focuses on the mechanisms of intussusception during bacterial growth.
The expansion of the cell was attributed to the process of intussusception rather than simple stretching.

Etymology

From Latin intus (within) + suscipere (to take up, receive).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tə.səpˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjʊ.səˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a part of a structure folds into itself, creating a pocket or cavity within its own surface
folding of a surface to form a pocket
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, intussusception helps form important body structures.
Some microorganisms move by intussusception, where their cell membrane folds inward.
In biological systems, intussusception can occur in tissues to increase surface area.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'intussusceptio', from 'intus' (within) + 'suscipere' (to take up).
