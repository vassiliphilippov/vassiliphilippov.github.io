Nineveh

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪnəvə / (AmE), / ˈnɪnɪvə / (BrE)
an ancient Assyrian city located on the eastern bank of the Tigris River opposite modern Mosul, in northern Iraq, which served as the capital of the Assyrian Empire
ancient Assyrian city; former empire capital

Examples

The ruins of Nineveh attract archaeologists from around the world every year.
Nineveh was once the largest city in the ancient world.
Many biblical stories reference the city of Nineveh and its importance in history.

Etymology

From Hebrew Nīnəweh, via Latin and Greek, originally from Akkadian Ninua.
