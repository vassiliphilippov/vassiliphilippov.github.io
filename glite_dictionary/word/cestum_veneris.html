Cestum veneris

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːstəm vəˈnɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈsiːstəm vɛˈnɪərɪs / (BrE)
A species of ctenophore, known for its ribbon-shaped, iridescent, gelatinous body, found in marine environments.
a ribbon-shaped marine ctenophore

Examples

The Cestum veneris glides gracefully through the ocean with its translucent, ribbon-like body.
Biologists observed a Cestum veneris emitting pulses of light as it moved.
The unique structure of Cestum veneris helps it navigate the open water.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'belt of Venus', referring to the goddess Venus and the ribbon-like shape of the animal.
