radome

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪˌdoʊm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdəʊm / (BrE)
A dome-shaped structure protecting a radar antenna, which is transparent to radio waves.
protective cover for a radar antenna

Examples

The fighter jet's radome houses sensitive electronics vital for navigation and targeting.
Engineers checked the integrity of the radome after the severe storm damaged several aircraft on the tarmac.
A radome allows radar signals to pass through while shielding the equipment from weather.

Etymology

Blend of 'radar' and 'dome', first used in the mid-20th century.
