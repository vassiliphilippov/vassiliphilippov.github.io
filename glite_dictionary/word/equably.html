equably

1. ADV

/ ˈɛkwəbli / (AmE), / ˈɛkwəbli / (BrE)
in an even or unvarying manner; without sudden changes or extremes.
in a calm or steady manner

Examples

Despite facing criticism, she responded equably and addressed each concern thoughtfully.
The team worked equably together, maintaining steady progress throughout the project.
He managed the stressful situation equably, without raising his voice or showing frustration.

Synset Examples

he is an equably cheerful fellow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equable - having an even, steady, or unvarying nature

Etymology

From 'equable' + '-ly', with 'equable' from Latin 'aequabilis' (even, equal).
