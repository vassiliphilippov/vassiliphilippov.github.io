to coapt

1. VERB

/ koʊˈæpt / (AmE), / kəʊˈæpt / (BrE)
to fit or join together tightly; to fasten, especially used in medical or anatomical contexts
to fit or fasten together tightly

Examples

During surgery, the surgeon must coapt the edges of the wound precisely to ensure proper healing.
The doctor coapted the fractured bone fragments using a special device.
To restore function, the tissue is coapted with fine sutures under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fasten - to attach or join securely

Etymology

From Latin 'coaptare' (to fit together), from 'co-' (together) + 'aptare' (to fit).
