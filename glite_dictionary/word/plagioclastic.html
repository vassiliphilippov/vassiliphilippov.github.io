plagioclastic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpleɪ.dʒioʊˈklæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌpleɪ.dʒiəʊˈklas.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of plagioclase, a group of feldspar minerals found in igneous rocks.
relating to plagioclase feldspar

Examples

The granite contains a significant amount of plagioclastic material.
Plagioclastic textures are often seen in volcanic rocks formed from rapidly cooled magma.
Scientists examined the plagioclastic composition to determine the rock's formation history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagioclase - a group of feldspar minerals in igneous rocks

Etymology

From 'plagioclase' (a mineral group) plus the suffix '-ic' meaning 'pertaining to'.
