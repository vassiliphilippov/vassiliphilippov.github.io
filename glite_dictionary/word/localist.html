localist

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports the interests and independence of a particular locality, especially in politics or policy
supporter of local interests

Examples

The new party attracted many localist supporters who opposed central government policies.
Her views as a localist often put her at odds with national leaders.
Several localist candidates won seats in the regional election.

Synset Examples

Other anti-establishment candidates elected in this district were localist camp's Cheng Chung-tai of Civic Passion
The rest of the candidates elected in this constituency were young and relatively new activists from the localist camp
Localist group Civic Passion's Wong Yeung-tat was defeated in this district, and resigned his leadership in Civic Passion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionalist - a person supporting the interests of a region over those of a broader area
  • Antonym: globalist - a person who advocates the operation or planning of economic and foreign policy on a global basis

Etymology

From 'local' + '-ist', meaning someone who advocates for a specific cause or group.
