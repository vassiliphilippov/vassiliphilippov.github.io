Solanum jasminoides

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌleɪnəm dʒæs.mɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / səˌleɪnəm dʒæs.mɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A copiously branched vine native to Brazil, with deciduous leaves and white flowers tinged with blue; commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
Brazilian vine with white and blue-tinged flowers

Examples

Solanum jasminoides is a popular choice for growing on trellises due to its attractive blooms.
Gardeners prize Solanum jasminoides for its resilience and ease of cultivation.
The delicate flowers of Solanum jasminoides appear in clusters throughout the summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'Solanum' (the nightshade genus) and 'jasminoides' meaning 'resembling jasmine'.
