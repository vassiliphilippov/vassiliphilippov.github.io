Madonna

1. PROPN

/ məˈdɑnə / (AmE), / məˈdɒnə / (BrE)
The stage name of an American pop singer, songwriter, and actress, prominent since the 1980s and known for her influence on popular culture.
American pop singer and cultural icon
american

Examples

Madonna revolutionized pop music with her distinctive style and bold performances.
Many consider Madonna a trailblazer for female artists in the music industry.
The documentary takes an in-depth look at Madonna's career and impact on culture.

2. PROPN

/ məˈdɑːnə / (AmE), / məˈdɒnə / (BrE)
A title for the Virgin Mary, the mother of Jesus, especially venerated in Christian traditions.
the Virgin Mary in Christianity

Examples

Many Renaissance paintings depict the Madonna holding the infant Jesus.
Catholics around the world pray to the Madonna for guidance and comfort.
The artist was inspired by the image of the Madonna in religious art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgin Mary - the mother of Jesus in Christian tradition

Etymology

From Italian 'Madonna', meaning 'my lady', used as a title of respect for the Virgin Mary.
