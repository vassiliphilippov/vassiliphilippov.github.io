Romani

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mə.ni / (BrE)
A member of an ethnic group with origins in northern India, now living mainly in Europe, North Africa, and North America, traditionally known for their itinerant lifestyle and distinctive culture.
member of the Romani ethnic group

Examples

There are large Romani communities in Romania, Bulgaria, and Spain.
The Romani people have a rich tradition of music and storytelling.
Many Romani migrated across Europe over the centuries, adapting to different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gypsy - another term for a member of the Romani people (now often considered offensive)

Etymology

From Romani *rom* (man, husband), the self-designation of the people.

/ roʊˈmɑːni / (AmE), / rəʊˈmɑːni / (BrE)
of or relating to the Romani people, their language, or their culture
relating to the Romani people or culture

Examples

The museum featured a display of Romani costumes from different regions of Europe.
She specializes in studying Romani music and its influence on European traditions.
Efforts have been made to preserve the Romani language among younger generations.

Synset Examples

Romany folk songs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romany - relating to the Romani people or their language or culture

Etymology

From Romani, the self-designation of the people, related to Sanskrit *ḍoma* (man of low caste).
