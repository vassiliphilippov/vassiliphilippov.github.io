absolutely

1. ADV

/ ˈæbsəˌluːtli / (AmE), / ˈabsəluːtli / (BrE)
completely and without any qualification; totally or fully.
completely; totally; without qualification
informal

Examples

She was absolutely certain that she had locked the door before leaving.
The food at that restaurant is absolutely delicious.
I'm absolutely exhausted after the long flight.

Synset Examples

an absolutely magnificent painting
a perfectly idiotic idea
you're perfectly right
utterly miserable
you can be dead sure of my innocence
was dead tired
dead right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utterly - completely; to an absolute degree
  • Antonym: partially - to some extent; incompletely

Etymology

From Latin absolutus (unrestricted, complete), via Middle English absolutely.

2. ADV

/ ˈæbsəˌluːtli / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːtli / (BrE)
in a way that is total and definite; without any doubt
totally; without doubt

Examples

The instructions must be followed absolutely, without any exceptions.
She was absolutely certain that she had locked the door before leaving.
That performance was absolutely incredible and left the audience speechless.

Synset Examples

we are absolutely opposed to the idea
he forced himself to lie absolutely still
iron is absolutely necessary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; to the full extent
  • Antonym: partially - in part; not completely

Etymology

From Latin 'absolutus' (completed, absolute), via Middle English and Old French.
