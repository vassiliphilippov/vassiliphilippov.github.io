barbaric

1. ADJ

/ bɑːrˈbærɪk / (AmE), / bɑːˈbærɪk / (BrE)
cruel, brutal, or lacking the standards and restraints of civilized society; primitive or uncivilized.
uncivilized; cruel or brutal

Examples

Many historians consider the public executions of the past to be barbaric.
The invading army was known for its barbaric treatment of prisoners.
Some traditions once common in ancient times seem barbaric by today's standards.

Synset Examples

barbarian invaders
barbaric practices
a savage people
fighting is crude and uncivilized especially if the weapons are efficient
wild tribes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civilized - polite and refined in manners or culture
  • Synonym: savage - fierce, violent, and uncontrolled; primitive
  • Synonym: brutal - savagely violent; cruel

Etymology

From Latin barbaricus (foreign, uncivilized), from Greek barbarikos (foreign, barbarous).

2. ADJ

/ bɑˈbærɪk / (AmE), / bɑːˈbærɪk / (BrE)
(of style or decoration) extravagantly bold, unrestrained, and crudely rich or elaborate; lacking refinement.
extravagantly bold or crudely rich, lacking refinement

Examples

The room was filled with a barbaric display of gold, velvet, and crystal.
His paintings were known for their barbaric colors and intense energy.
The architect's choice of barbaric ornamentation clashed with the delicate structure of the building.

Synset Examples

barbaric use of color or ornament

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately decorated
  • Antonym: refined - elegant and cultured in appearance, manner, or taste

Etymology

From Latin barbaricus, from Greek barbarikos (foreign, uncivilized).
