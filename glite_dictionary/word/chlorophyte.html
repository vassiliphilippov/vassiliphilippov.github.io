chlorophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːrəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
a type of green algae, usually found growing on wet rocks, damp wood, or in stagnant water
a green algae species

Examples

Many freshwater ponds have a layer of chlorophyte covering the surface.
Scientists study chlorophytes to understand the evolution of plant life.
The slippery green coating on the rocks was identified as a type of chlorophyte.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green alga - an alga belonging to the division Chlorophyta; commonly green in color

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) + 'phyton' (plant).
