Francis Bacon

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræn.sɪs ˈbeɪ.kən / (AmE), / ˈfræn.sɪs ˈbeɪ.kən / (BrE)
(1561–1626) English statesman and philosopher, regarded as a precursor of British empiricism and an advocate of inductive reasoning.
English philosopher and statesman (1561–1626)

Examples

Francis Bacon played a key role in developing the scientific method through his advocacy of empirical research.
Many historians credit Francis Bacon with laying the groundwork for modern scientific inquiry.
The writings of Francis Bacon continue to influence philosophy and science today.
