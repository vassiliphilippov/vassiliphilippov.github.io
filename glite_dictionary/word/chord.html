chord

1. VERB

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
to play chords on a stringed instrument, such as a guitar or piano
play chords on an instrument

Examples

She chorded a C major progression beautifully on her guitar.
After learning the basics, he practiced chording different songs every day.
If you chord lightly, the melody will stand out more clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strum - to play a stringed instrument by brushing the strings with the fingers or a pick

to bring into harmony or accord, especially in music or singing
bring into musical harmony

3. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
A straight line connecting two points on a curve, especially in geometry or mathematics.
straight line joining two points on a curve

Examples

In a circle, the longest chord passes through the center and is called the diameter.
The engineer measured the chord length to determine the distance between the two endpoints of the curve.
If two chords of a circle intersect, the products of their segments are equal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: arc - a part of the circumference of a circle or other curve

Etymology

From Latin 'chorda' (string, cord), from Greek 'khordē' (string, gut, cord).

4. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
a group of three or more musical notes played together, producing a harmonious sound
harmonious group of musical notes

Examples

The guitarist played a chord that filled the room with rich sound.
Learning how to form a basic chord is fundamental for any aspiring pianist.
The song begins with a simple chord progression that repeats throughout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes
  • Antonym: melody - a sequence of single notes that is musically satisfying

Etymology

From Greek 'khordē' (string, gut), via Latin 'chorda.'
