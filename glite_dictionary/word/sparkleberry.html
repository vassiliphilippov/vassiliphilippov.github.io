sparkleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːrkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, producing black inedible berries.
North American shrub with black berries

Examples

We found a wild sparkleberry growing near the edge of the forest.
The leaves of the sparkleberry are glossy and the fruit is small and black.
Although the sparkleberry bears berries, they are not suitable for eating.
