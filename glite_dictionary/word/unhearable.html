unhearable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɪrəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɪərəbl / (BrE)
impossible to hear; not able to be perceived by the ear.
impossible to hear

Examples

The dog's whistle was unhearable to humans but made the pets sit up instantly.
She whispered in an unhearable voice, so no one caught her secret.
The signal was so faint it was almost unhearable even with advanced equipment.

Synset Examples

an inaudible conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaudible - impossible to hear

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'hear' + suffix '-able'.
