jurisprudence

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʊrɪsˈpruːdəns / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˈpruːdəns / (BrE)
the theory or philosophy of law; the body of laws or legal principles set by authority
theory or philosophy of law
legal

Examples

Modern jurisprudence is constantly evolving to adapt to new societal challenges.
Many legal scholars have contributed to the field of jurisprudence with influential theories.
The country's jurisprudence reflects centuries of historical development and changing values.

Synset Examples

civilization presupposes respect for the law
the great problem for jurisprudence to allow freedom while enforcing order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law - the system of rules recognized by a particular country or community
  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body

Etymology

From Latin 'jurisprudentia', from 'juris' (of law) + 'prudentia' (knowledge).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʊrɪsˈprudns / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˈpruːd(ə)ns / (BrE)
the science or philosophy of law; the systematic study of the principles underlying legal systems and judicial decisions
the philosophy or study of law
legal

Examples

She decided to study jurisprudence at university to better understand the foundations of legal systems.
Modern jurisprudence explores the relationship between law, morality, and justice.
His lectures on jurisprudence are highly regarded by both students and legal scholars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law - the system of rules that a particular country or community recognizes as regulating the actions of its members

Etymology

From Latin 'jurisprudentia', from 'juris' (of law) + 'prudentia' (knowledge, skill).
