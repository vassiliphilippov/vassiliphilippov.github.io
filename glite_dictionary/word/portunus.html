Portunus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːrtjənəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtjʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of swimming crabs that is the type genus of the family Portunidae.
genus of swimming crabs

Examples

The blue crab, a species of the genus Portunus, is widely found in western Atlantic waters.
Scientists have identified several new species within the Portunus genus based on genetic studies.
Most Portunus crabs are characterized by their flattened back legs, which help them swim.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Portunidae - the family of swimming crabs to which Portunus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'Portunus', a sea god, later used in scientific naming.

a genus of swimming crabs that is the type genus of the family Portunidae, including several economically important species
genus of swimming crabs

Examples

The blue crab, an important food source, belongs to the genus Portunus.
Researchers studied several species within the Portunus genus to understand their evolutionary adaptations.
Fishermen often catch crabs from the Portunus group in coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Portunidae - the family of swimming crabs to which Portunus belongs

Etymology

From Latin Portūnus, Roman god of harbors and ports.
