trigonometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪɡəˈnɑːmətri / (AmE), / ˌtrɪɡəˈnɒmətri / (BrE)
The branch of mathematics dealing with the relationships between the angles and sides of triangles, especially using trigonometric functions.
mathematics of triangles and their angles

Examples

Many engineering problems require a solid understanding of trigonometry.
He struggled with trigonometry in high school but excelled at algebra.
The surveyor used trigonometry to calculate the height of the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometry - branch of mathematics concerned with the properties of shapes and space
  • Degree Higher: calculus - branch of mathematics dealing with rates of change and accumulation

Etymology

From Greek trigōnon (triangle) and metron (measure).
