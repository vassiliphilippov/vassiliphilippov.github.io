brimstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪmˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbrɪmstəʊn / (BrE)
an old name for sulfur, especially as used in literary or biblical contexts
old name for sulfur
archaic

Examples

The ancient texts described fire and brimstone raining down from the sky.
The smell of brimstone filled the air after the volcanic eruption.
He preached about hellfire and brimstone in his sermon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur - a yellow chemical element with a distinct odor

Etymology

From Old English *brynstān* (burning stone), referring to sulfur's flammability.
