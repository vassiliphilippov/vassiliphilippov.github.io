skin-dive

1. VERB

/ ˈskɪnˌdaɪv / (AmE), / ˈskɪnˌdaɪv / (BrE)
to swim or dive underwater using only a snorkel and no scuba equipment
swim underwater using a snorkel

Examples

They love to skin-dive along the coral reefs during their vacation.
She learned to skin-dive before attempting scuba diving.
A group of tourists decided to skin-dive in the shallow bay.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scuba dive - to swim underwater using a self-contained underwater breathing apparatus (scuba)

Etymology

From 'skin' + 'dive', popularized in the mid-20th century as interest in recreational diving grew.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnˌdaɪv / (AmE), / ˈskɪn.daɪv / (BrE)
the activity of swimming underwater without the use of any breathing equipment other than a snorkel
underwater swimming with minimal equipment

Examples

Many tourists love to skin-dive in the clear, tropical waters of the Caribbean.
He learned to skin-dive before trying more advanced scuba diving techniques.
On weekends, she often goes to the lake to skin-dive with her friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scuba diving - the activity of swimming underwater using special breathing equipment
  • Synonym: snorkeling - the practice of swimming on or through water while equipped with a diving mask and snorkel

Etymology

From 'skin' and 'dive', referring to diving with only one's skin exposed, without full diving gear.
