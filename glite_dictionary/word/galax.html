galax

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælæks / (AmE), / ˈɡalaks / (BrE)
A North American evergreen perennial herb (Galax urceolata) with clusters of tiny white flowers and glossy, rounded leaves that turn reddish or purplish in autumn.
evergreen perennial herb of North America

Examples

The forest floor was covered in clusters of galax leaves that had turned deep purple for the fall.
She planted galax to add texture to her shade garden.
Florists often use galax leaves to enhance the beauty of their arrangements.

Etymology

From Greek 'galaxias,' relating to milk, referring to the family name Galax, possibly for the white flowers.
