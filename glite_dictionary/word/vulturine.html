vulturine

1. ADJ

/ ˈvʌltʃəˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃəriːn / (BrE)
(of animals) living by preying on other animals, especially by hunting and catching living prey; predatory.
predatory; preying on others

Examples

The vulturine instincts of the creature made it a fearsome predator in its environment.
Scientists studied the vulturine habits of raptors in different regions.
His vulturine appetite for gossip was well known among his colleagues.

Synset Examples

a predatory bird
the rapacious wolf
raptorial birds
ravening wolves
a vulturine taste for offal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - living by preying on others
  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants only

Etymology

From Latin 'vulturinus', from 'vultur' (vulture).
