spinnable

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɪn.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be spun into fibers or thread, especially for use in textiles or manufacturing
capable of being spun into fibers

Examples

The wool is spinnable and can be turned into high-quality yarn.
Only spinnable polymers are used in this type of fiber production.
The researchers tested whether the material remained spinnable after chemical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinnability - the quality of being spinnable

Etymology

From 'spin' (to draw out and twist fibers into thread) + '-able' (capable of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɪn.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of information or a situation) capable of being presented or manipulated to convey a particular, often biased, interpretation.
able to be influenced by biased information

Examples

The senator's statement was highly spinnable, allowing journalists to interpret it in multiple ways.
Marketing teams are often looking for spinnable angles on disappointing results.
The report was written in a deliberately neutral tone to be less spinnable by the media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to present information in a way that creates a biased interpretation

Etymology

From the verb 'spin' (to present in a particular way), with the adjective-forming suffix '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02373402-s
Synset POS: s
