Hsian

1. PROPN

/ ʃjɑːn / (AmE), / ʃjɑːn / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of Xi'an, a city in central China, historically the capital of the ancient Chinese empire from 221 to 206 BC.
historical name for Xi'an, China

Examples

The ancient city of Hsian was once the starting point of the Silk Road.
Historians have found numerous artifacts in Hsian dating back over two thousand years.
Today, Hsian, now known as Xi'an, attracts tourists from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xi'an - city in central China, capital of several ancient Chinese dynasties

Etymology

Older English romanization of the Chinese name for Xi'an.
