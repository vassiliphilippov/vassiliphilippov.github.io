fundamentalness

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlnəs / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being fundamental; essential importance or basic nature.
quality of being fundamental

Examples

The fundamentalness of these moral principles cannot be overstated in a just society.
Scientists debated the fundamentalness of certain rules in determining the universe's behavior.
She questioned the fundamentalness of the issue in the context of organizational change.

Synset Examples

At a similar level of fundamentalness or ‘broadness’ is the question of whether to adopt strategies that are overt versus covert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentality - the quality of being fundamental

Etymology

From fundamental + -ness, forming a noun indicating a state or quality.
