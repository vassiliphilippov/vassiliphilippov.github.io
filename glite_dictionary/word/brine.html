brine

1. NOUN

/ braɪn / (AmE), / braɪn / (BrE)
a strong solution of salt and water, especially used to preserve or pickle food
strong saltwater for pickling or preserving

Examples

The cucumbers were soaked in brine to make them into pickles.
Seafood is often stored in brine to keep it fresh before cooking.
The chef prepared a brine for the turkey to enhance its flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickle - food preserved in brine or vinegar

Etymology

From Old English *brȳne* (salt water, brine).

2. NOUN

/ braɪn / (AmE), / braɪn / (BrE)
water that contains a high concentration of salt, often used for preserving or pickling food, or found naturally in the sea
water with a high salt content

Examples

The cucumbers were soaked in brine to make them into pickles.
After the storm, the air by the shore smelled strongly of brine.
Sea salt can be harvested from evaporated brine.

Synset Examples

the water in the ocean is all saltwater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltwater - water containing a significant concentration of salt

Etymology

From Old English *brȳne* (salt water, brine), related to Old Norse *brýni* (brine), and Middle Dutch *brīne*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15033414-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ braɪn / (AmE), / braɪn / (BrE)
to soak or preserve something in a solution of salt and water
soak or preserve in saltwater

Examples

Before roasting the turkey, they decided to brine it overnight for extra flavor.
She prefers to brine cucumbers before turning them into pickles.
Some chefs brine pork chops to keep them juicy during cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickle - to preserve or soak in a salty or acidic liquid

Etymology

From Middle English brine, from Old English brȳne (salt water).
