gracelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪs.lɪs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪs.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
An unpleasant lack of grace in manner, movement, form, or expression.
lack of grace or elegance

Examples

His gracelessness on the dance floor embarrassed his friends.
The gracelessness of her apology made it hard to forgive.
Despite the painting's beauty, a certain gracelessness in the composition caught the critic's eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - The quality of being awkward or lacking in grace
  • Antonym: elegance - Graceful and stylish appearance or manner

Etymology

From 'grace' + suffix '-less' + noun-forming suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪslɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪsləsnəs / (BrE)
lack of elegance, ease, or social grace, often resulting in awkward or unrefined behavior or movement.
lack of grace or elegance

Examples

Her gracelessness at the dance was obvious to everyone watching.
Despite his intelligence, his gracelessness in social situations often made him uncomfortable.
The actor’s gracelessness on stage contrasted with his charming off-stage persona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being awkward or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish

Etymology

From 'grace' + '-less' + '-ness'; 'grace' from Latin 'gratia' (favor, charm).
