stylopodium

1. NOUN

/ staɪləˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / staɪləˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
An enlargement at the base of the style in some plants, especially in the family Umbelliferae (Apiaceae).
enlargement at base of style in some plants
medical

Examples

The stylopodium is clearly visible at the base of the flower's style.
Several species in the Umbelliferae family exhibit a prominent stylopodium.
The botanist noted the unusual shape of the stylopodium during her research.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'stylos' (pillar) + 'podion', diminutive of 'pous' (foot).
