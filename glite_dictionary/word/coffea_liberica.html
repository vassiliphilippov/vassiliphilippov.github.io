Coffea liberica

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑfiə lɪˈberɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkɒfiə lɪˈbɛrɪkə / (BrE)
A small tree native to West Africa, grown for its large coffee beans which are used to produce a distinct type of coffee.
West African species of coffee plant

Examples

Farmers in Southeast Asia are increasingly cultivating Coffea liberica because of its resistance to certain diseases.
The beans of Coffea liberica are larger and have a unique flavor compared to those of Arabica and Robusta.
A botanical garden in Ghana features a rare specimen of Coffea liberica on display for visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffee - a beverage made from roasted coffee beans

Etymology

From Latin Coffea (the coffee genus) and liberica, referring to Liberia, a country in West Africa.
