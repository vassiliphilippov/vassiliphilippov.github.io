Junker

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈjʊŋkə / (BrE)
A member of the Prussian aristocracy, especially noted for their military traditions and influence.
Prussian aristocratic landowner, militarist

Examples

The rise of the Junker class significantly influenced Prussian politics and society.
As a Junker, he served as an officer in the Prussian army.
Many estates in East Prussia were controlled by Junker families for generations.

Etymology

From German 'Junker', originally meaning 'young lord' (junior nobleman).
