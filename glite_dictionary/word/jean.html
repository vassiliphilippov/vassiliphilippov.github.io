jean

1. NOUN

/ dʒin / (AmE), / dʒiːn / (BrE)
A strong, durable twill-weave cotton fabric, often used for making jeans and work clothes.
a strong twilled cotton fabric

Examples

The work overalls were made from heavy jean to withstand daily wear and tear.
Traditional jean fabric was commonly used in laborers' garments for its strength.
She prefers jackets made of soft jean because they're comfortable and hard-wearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denim - a sturdy cotton twill fabric, typically blue, used for jeans

Etymology

From Old French 'Jean', referring to Genoa (Italy), where the fabric was originally made.
