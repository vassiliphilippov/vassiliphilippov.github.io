Troglodytes

1. NOUN

/ troʊˈɡlɑdəˌtiːz / (AmE), / trɒˈɡlɒdɪˌtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the wren family, Troglodytidae.
genus of wren birds

Examples

The winter wren belongs to the genus Troglodytes, which is known for its complex song.
Researchers observed several Troglodytes species nesting in dense undergrowth along the river.
The Troglodytes genus includes some of the smallest songbirds found in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Troglodytidae - the family of birds including the wrens

Etymology

From Greek *trōglodutēs* ('cave-dweller'), referring to perceived nesting habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtroʊɡləˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌtrɒɡləˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the family Troglodytidae, commonly known as wrens.
genus of wrens (birds)

Examples

The Troglodytes genus includes the well-known Eurasian wren.
Researchers observed several species within the Troglodytes genus in the forest undergrowth.
Members of Troglodytes are noted for their energetic songs and small size.

Etymology

From Greek *trōglodutēs* (cave dweller), referring to the birds' tendency to nest in crevices.
