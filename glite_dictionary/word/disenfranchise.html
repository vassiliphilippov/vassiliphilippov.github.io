to disenfranchise

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
to deprive someone of the right to vote or other rights of citizenship
deprive of voting rights

Examples

The new law would disenfranchise thousands of eligible voters.
Groups have protested against efforts to disenfranchise certain communities.
Historically, racial minorities were disenfranchised through discriminatory policies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to enfranchise - to grant the right to vote
  • Synonym: to disfranchise - to deprive of a right, especially the right to vote; variant spelling

Etymology

From French 'franchise' (freedom, right), with prefix 'dis-' meaning removal.
