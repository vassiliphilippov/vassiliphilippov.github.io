Grand Marnier

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd mɑrˈnjeɪ / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnd mɑːˈnjeə / (BrE)
A French liqueur flavored with orange, often used in cocktails and desserts.
orange-flavored French liqueur

Examples

He added a splash of Grand Marnier to the soufflé batter for extra flavor.
She prefers her margaritas with Grand Marnier instead of triple sec.
The dessert was flambéed with Grand Marnier at the table.

Etymology

From French, named after its creator Louis-Alexandre Marnier Lapostolle in the late 19th century.
