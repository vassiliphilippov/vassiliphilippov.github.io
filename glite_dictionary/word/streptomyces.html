Streptomyces

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptoʊˈmaɪsiːz / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈmaɪsiːz / (BrE)
a genus of soil-dwelling, filamentous bacteria notable for producing many antibiotics
genus of antibiotic-producing soil bacteria
medical

Examples

Many antibiotics, such as streptomycin, are derived from Streptomyces bacteria found in soil.
The discovery of Streptomyces revolutionized the treatment of bacterial infections.
Researchers are exploring new compounds produced by Streptomyces for use in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Actinomyces - a genus of bacteria related to Streptomyces

Etymology

From Greek 'streptos' (twisted) + 'mykēs' (fungus), referring to their twisted, fungus-like filaments.
