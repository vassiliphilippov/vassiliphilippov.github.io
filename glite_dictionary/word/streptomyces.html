streptomyces

1. NOUN

/ strɛpˈtoʊmaɪˌsiːz / (AmE), / strɛpˈtɒmɪsiːz / (BrE)
a genus of aerobic bacteria, some species of which produce antibiotics, including streptomycin
genus of antibiotic-producing bacteria
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that streptomyces bacteria can produce a variety of antibiotics.
The soil sample contained several strains of streptomyces important for pharmaceutical research.
Streptomyces is a key genus studied in microbiology due to its antibiotic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteria - microscopic single-celled organisms

Etymology

From New Latin 'Streptomyces', from Greek 'streptos' (twisted) + 'mykēs' (fungus).
