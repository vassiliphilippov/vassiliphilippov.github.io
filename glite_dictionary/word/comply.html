to comply

1. VERB

/ kəmˈplaɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to act in accordance with a request, rule, or command; to obey or conform
act in accordance with rules, commands, or requests

Examples

All employees are expected to comply with the company's safety regulations.
The teacher warned that students who do not comply will receive a penalty.
After a brief hesitation, she decided to comply with his request.

Synset Examples

He complied with my instructions
You must comply or else!
Follow these simple rules
abide by the rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obey - to do what someone tells you to do or follow rules
  • Antonym: to disobey - to refuse or fail to obey rules or instructions

Etymology

From Latin 'complere' (to fill up, fulfil), later via Italian 'compiere'.
