disablement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈseɪbəlmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪblmənt / (BrE)
the condition of being unable to carry out normal activities due to physical or mental impairment
state of being unable due to impairment

Examples

The disablement caused by his injury prevented him from returning to work.
Many organizations advocate for the rights of those living with disablement.
Advancements in technology have made it easier for people with disablement to lead independent lives.

Synset Examples

reading disability
hearing impairment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disability - a physical or mental condition that limits movements, senses, or activities

Etymology

From 'disable' + '-ment', following the pattern of forming nouns indicating a state or condition.
