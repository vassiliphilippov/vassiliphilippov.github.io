Tyrannus vociferans

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈrænəs voʊˈsɪfərəns / (AmE), / tɪˈranəs vəʊˈsɪfərəns / (BrE)
A species of kingbird found in the southwestern United States, largely grey with a yellow abdomen.
species of kingbird, southwestern US

Examples

Many birdwatchers travel to Arizona to spot the distinctive Tyrannus vociferans during migration season.
The Tyrannus vociferans is easily identified by its grey plumage and yellow belly.
Biologists studied the nesting habits of Tyrannus vociferans in the canyon region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassin's kingbird - common name for Tyrannus vociferans

Etymology

From Latin: 'tyrannus' meaning 'tyrant' (referring to the bird's aggressive behavior), 'vociferans' meaning 'shouting' or 'clamorous'.
