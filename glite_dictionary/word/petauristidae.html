Petauristidae

1. NOUN

/ piˌtɔːrɪˈstɪdiː / (AmE), / pɛˌtɔːrɪˈstɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mammals comprising the Old World flying squirrels, found mainly in Asia and known for their gliding abilities
family of Old World flying squirrels

Examples

The Petauristidae are known for their ability to glide long distances between trees.
Scientists are studying the unique adaptations of the Petauristidae family.
Many species in Petauristidae are native to Southeast Asia's forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying squirrel - gliding squirrel belonging to the family Petauristidae (Old World) or Sciuridae (New World)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'petauristēs' (acrobat) and the taxonomic suffix '-idae' for families.
