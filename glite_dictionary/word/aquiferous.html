aquiferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌækwəˈfɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈfɛːrəs / (BrE)
relating to or containing an aquifer; able to carry or transmit water through porous rock or sediment
relating to an aquifer; water-carrying

Examples

The rocks in this region are aquiferous and supply water to the surrounding wells.
Scientists studied the aquiferous properties of the subterranean limestone to assess its groundwater potential.
An aquiferous layer can provide a reliable source of fresh water for the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquifer - an underground layer of water-bearing rock

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) + '-ferous' (bearing, carrying), from Latin 'ferre' (to carry).
