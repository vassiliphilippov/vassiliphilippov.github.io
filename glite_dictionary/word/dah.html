dah

1. NOUN

/ dɑ / (AmE), / dɑː / (BrE)
the longer of the two signals used in Morse code, typically representing a dash
long signal in Morse code

Examples

In Morse code, the letter O is transmitted by three dahs.
To send the SOS signal, you use three dits, three dahs, and three dits again.
He learned to distinguish between a dah and a dit when practicing Morse code.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dit - the shorter of the two signals in Morse code
