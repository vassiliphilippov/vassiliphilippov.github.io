incurable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their qualities) impossible to change or correct; unalterable in character or habit.
impossible to change (personality, habits)

Examples

Despite his many setbacks, he remained an incurable optimist throughout his life.
My grandfather is an incurable romantic who still writes love poems to my grandmother.
She's an incurable perfectionist, always striving to make everything just right.

Synset Examples

an incurable optimist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorrigible - impossible to correct or reform (of a person or their behavior)

Etymology

From Late Latin incurabilis (not able to be cured).

WordNet ID: oewn-00350861-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) impossible to cure; not able to be healed or remedied.
not able to be cured

Examples

Despite years of research, the illness remained incurable and devastating to many families.
Doctors told him his condition was incurable, but he continued to hope for a breakthrough.
She has shown remarkable strength in facing an incurable disorder with such optimism.

Synset Examples

an incurable disease
an incurable addiction to smoking

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curable - able to be cured or healed

Etymology

From Old French 'incurable', from Latin 'incurabilis' ('in-' not + 'curare' to care for).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00997914-a
Synset POS: a
