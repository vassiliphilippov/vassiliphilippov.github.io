Serenoa repens

1. NOUN

/ səˌriːnoʊ.ə rɪˈpɛnz / (AmE), / sɛˌriːnəʊ.ə rɪˈpɛnz / (BrE)
a small, hardy, clump-forming spiny palm native to the southern United States, commonly called saw palmetto
a small spiny palm of the southeastern US

Examples

The fruits of the Serenoa repens are often harvested for herbal supplements.
You can find Serenoa repens growing wild in the pine forests of Florida.
Botanists study Serenoa repens for its unique adaptation to sandy soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saw palmetto - common name for Serenoa repens, a spiny palm of the southeastern US

Etymology

From the genus name 'Serenoa,' honoring botanist Sereno Watson, and Latin 'repens' meaning 'creeping.'
