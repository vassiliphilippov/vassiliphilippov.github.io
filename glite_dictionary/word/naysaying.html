naysaying

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪˌseɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪˌseɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of saying no or expressing refusal or opposition.
saying no; expressing refusal

Examples

His constant naysaying made it difficult for the team to move forward with new ideas.
Despite some naysaying from the committee, the project was eventually approved.
We need constructive criticism, not just naysaying for the sake of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposition - resistance or dissent expressed in action or argument
  • Antonym: approval - the act of agreeing to or accepting something

Etymology

Derived from 'nay' (no) and 'saying' (from 'say'), first used in this sense in the 20th century.
