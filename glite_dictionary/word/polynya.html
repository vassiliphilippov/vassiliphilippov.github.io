polynya

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪn.jə / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪn.jə / (BrE)
a stretch of open water surrounded by sea ice, especially in Arctic or Antarctic regions
open water area amid sea ice

Examples

A large polynya formed near the coastal research station, allowing seals to surface for air.
Scientists observed that the polynya expanded rapidly during the unusually mild winter.
The ship became trapped in the ice, but a nearby polynya offered hope for escape.

Etymology

From Russian 'polyn'ya' (open water in ice).
