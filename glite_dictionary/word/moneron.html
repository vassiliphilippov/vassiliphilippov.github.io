moneron

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪər.ɒn / (AmE), / məˈnɪə.rɒn / (BrE)
A single-celled organism without a distinct nucleus, once classified in the kingdom Monera, that typically reproduces by asexual budding or fission and obtains food by absorption, photosynthesis, or chemosynthesis.
single-celled organism of former kingdom Monera

Examples

Scientists studied the moneron to better understand early life forms on Earth.
Unlike plants and animals, a moneron lacks a defined cell nucleus.
The classification of the moneron has changed with advances in microbiology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - a single-celled microorganism, usually classified within Monera
  • Synonym: prokaryote - an organism whose cell lacks a nucleus

Etymology

From Greek monērēs (single, solitary).
