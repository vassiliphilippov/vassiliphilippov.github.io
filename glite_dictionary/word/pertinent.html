pertinent

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.t̬ən.ənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
relevant or applicable to a particular matter; having a clear connection to the subject at hand.
relevant; related to the matter

Examples

His comments were particularly pertinent to the ongoing debate about climate change.
Only information that is pertinent to the investigation will be considered by the board.
Before you answer, make sure your response is pertinent to the main topic.

Synset Examples

a list of articles pertinent to the discussion
remarks that were to the point

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something
  • Synonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to the matter at hand

Etymology

From Latin pertinent-, present participle stem of pertinere (to pertain).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɜrtənənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtɪnənt / (BrE)
relevant or applicable to a particular matter; appropriate.
relevant; appropriate to the matter

Examples

She always asks pertinent questions during meetings that move the discussion forward.
The report only included pertinent details, making it concise and easy to read.
Please provide any pertinent information related to your medical history on the form.

Synset Examples

the successful copywriter is a master of apposite and evocative verbal images
an apt reply

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something
  • Synonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to the matter at hand

Etymology

From Latin 'pertinens', present participle of 'pertinere' (to pertain).
