gummosis

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɡʌˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A pathological condition in certain trees in which gum-like substances ooze from the bark, especially in citrus and stone-fruit trees.
disease causing gummy exudate in trees
medical

Examples

The peach tree showed signs of gummosis after the bark was injured during pruning.
Citrus growers monitor for gummosis as an indicator of disease in their orchards.
Excess moisture in the soil can increase the risk of gummosis in young trees.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'gum' plus '-osis' indicating a condition.

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɡʌˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease of citrus and some other trees in which gum is exuded from the bark, usually caused by a fungal infection.
disease causing gum exudation in trees
medical

Examples

The orange trees showed clear signs of gummosis after weeks of heavy rain.
Orchard managers must monitor for gummosis to prevent the spread of fungal infections.
Effective treatments can reduce the damage caused by gummosis in citrus groves.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'gum' + '-osis' (disease condition).
