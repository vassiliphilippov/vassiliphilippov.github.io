to linearize

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪniəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəraɪz / (BrE)
to make linear or to represent something in a linear form, especially in mathematics, engineering, or chemistry
make or express in linear form

Examples

The engineer used an algorithm to linearize the complex data set for analysis.
Researchers linearized the gene sequence to better understand its structure.
A catalyst can linearize certain polymers during the chemical process.

Synset Examples

a catalyst linearizes polyethylene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From 'linear' + '-ize', based on Latin 'linea' (line).
