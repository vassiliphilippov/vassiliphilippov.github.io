Pagophilus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪɡəˈfaɪləs / (AmE), / ˌpeɪɡəˈfaɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of earless seals, with the only member being the harp seal, found in the North Atlantic and Arctic Oceans.
genus of harp seal

Examples

The genus Pagophilus is represented by only one living species, the harp seal.
Researchers observed juvenile Pagophilus migrating south as the sea ice receded.
The adaptation of Pagophilus to icy habitats makes it unique among seals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harp seal - a species of earless seal in the genus Pagophilus

Etymology

From Greek pagos (ice) + philos (loving), referring to the animal's affinity for ice.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪɡoʊˈfaɪləs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪɡəʊˈfaɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of earless seals that includes the harp seal, native to the Arctic and North Atlantic Oceans.
genus of earless seals (harp seal)

Examples

The Pagophilus genus is known for its adaptation to life in icy waters.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Pagophilus in the North Atlantic.
The harp seal, classified as Pagophilus groenlandicus, is famous for its white pups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harp seal - a species of earless seal found in the northern Atlantic and Arctic Oceans

Etymology

From Greek 'pagos' (ice) and 'philos' (loving), referring to its icy habitat.
