Angora

1. NOUN

/ ænˈɡɔːrə / (AmE), / æŋˈɡɔːrə / (BrE)
a breed of domestic goat known for its long, silky hair, which produces true mohair.
breed of goat producing mohair

Examples

The Angora goat is prized for the high quality of its mohair.
Farmers raise Angora goats mainly in Turkey and South Africa.
She spun a luxurious scarf from the fleece of her Angora goat.

Etymology

From Angora, the former name of Ankara, the capital of Turkey, where the breed originated.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈɡɔrə / (AmE), / ænˈɡɔːrə / (BrE)
a breed of domestic rabbit characterized by long, silky white hair, often bred for its wool
breed of rabbit with long silky hair

Examples

The Angora is prized for the softness and quality of its wool.
At the fair, her Angora won first place in the rabbit competition.
She raises Angora rabbits to spin their luxurious fur into yarn.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ænˈɡɔːrə / (AmE), / æŋˈɡɔːrə / (BrE)
a breed of domestic cat with long silky hair, originally from Turkey, similar in appearance to the Persian cat
long-haired breed of cat

Examples

The Angora is prized for its luxurious white fur and bushy tail.
She adopted an Angora from the animal shelter last year.
Unlike the Persian, the Angora has a more slender build and longer legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Persian cat - a breed of long-haired cat with a short muzzle

Etymology

From Angora, former name of Ankara, the capital of Turkey, where the breed originated.
