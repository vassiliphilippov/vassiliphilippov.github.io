perceptuality

1. NOUN

/ pərˌsɛp.tʃuˈælə.ti / (AmE), / pəˌsɛp.tʃuˈal.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being related to perception, especially the organization and interpretation of sensory information.
quality of relating to perception

Examples

The philosopher discussed the perceptuality inherent in all sensory experiences.
Some psychologists examine how perceptuality shapes our understanding of reality.
In art analysis, the perceptuality of color and form is a key topic.

Synset Examples

If the lack of uniformity in relations is no objection to the perceptuality of visual, auditory and olfactory experiences, no objection should be raised if the relation in negative cognition is different from accepted kinds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses

Etymology

From perceptual + -ity; perceptual (relating to perception) from Latin 'perceptio' (perception) + English adjective suffix '-al'; '-ity' denotes quality or state.
