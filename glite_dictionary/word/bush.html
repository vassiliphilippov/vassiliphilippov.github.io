2. NOUN

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
an area of land covered with dense vegetation, especially stunted trees and shrubs, typical of many hot, dry regions
area of dense stunted vegetation

Examples

Many animals make their homes in the bush far from human settlements.
After hiking through the bush, we emerged scratched and covered in dust.
The explorers got lost in the dense bush and had to wait for rescue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: forest - large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Synonym: scrub - vegetation consisting mainly of small, stunted trees or bushes

Etymology

From Middle English 'busche', from Old French 'buis', from Latin 'buxus' (box tree).

3. NOUN

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
an area of wild or uncultivated land, especially one covered with shrubs or low trees and not settled or farmed, often used to refer to remote or wilderness regions; commonly used in Australia, Africa, and Canada
wild or uncultivated wilderness area

Examples

Many explorers have ventured deep into the bush in search of rare wildlife.
They spent the summer camping in the rugged bush of northern Canada.
Learning to survive in the bush can be challenging for the unprepared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilderness - an area that is not cultivated or settled
  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth

Etymology

From Old French 'busche' (firewood), likely of Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
to fit or provide a mechanical part with a bushing (a lining or sleeve used to reduce friction or wear)
fit with a bushing (engineering)

Examples

The mechanic had to bush the wheel to ensure it rotated smoothly.
After repeated use, the hole became worn and needed to be bushed.
Engineers often bush moving parts to extend the lifespan of the machinery.

5. NOUN

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
a woody plant that is smaller than a tree and typically has several stems arising at or near the ground
a low, multi-stemmed woody plant

Examples

A bush had grown rapidly outside their window, providing extra shade in the summer.
They planted a rose bush in the front yard last weekend.
Many small animals make their homes under a dense bush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a low, multi-stemmed woody plant similar to bush

Etymology

From Middle English *busch*, from Old French *buis*, possibly of Germanic origin.
