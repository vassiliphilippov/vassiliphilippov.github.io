fragmentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of breaking or being broken into small parts or fine particles.
splitting into small pieces or particles

Examples

The fragmentation of the rock was caused by years of weathering.
Modern warfare often results in the fragmentation of buildings and infrastructure.
The fragmentation of the material made it easier to analyze under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disintegration - the process of losing cohesion or strength and breaking apart

Etymology

From Latin 'fragmentum' (a fragment) with the suffix '-ation' forming nouns of action.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(computing) the process or condition where a file is broken into pieces and stored in multiple locations on a disk, which can degrade system performance.
breaking up of data on a disk

Examples

Disk fragmentation can cause your computer to run much slower than usual.
Many operating systems offer tools to reduce fragmentation and improve disk efficiency.
The IT technician explained how fragmentation affects file retrieval during system maintenance.

Synset Examples

fragmentation slows system performance because it takes extra time to locate and assemble the parts of the fragmented file

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defragmentation - the process of reorganizing fragmented data so that pieces of files are stored together

Etymology

From Latin fragmentum (fragment) + -ation, meaning the act or process of fragmenting.

3. NOUN

/ ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or effect of something, especially a bomb, breaking into small pieces or fragments that are scattered by force
the scattering of fragments, often by an explosion

Examples

The grenade caused fragmentation that injured several people nearby.
Modern body armor is designed to protect soldiers from fragmentation injuries.
The bomb's fragmentation extended hundreds of meters from the blast site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent burst, often with noise and force
  • Synonym: shrapnel - fragments of a bomb, shell, or other object thrown out by an explosion

Etymology

From Latin 'fragmentum' (a broken piece) and suffix '-ation' forming nouns.

4. NOUN

/ ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of breaking into separate parts, especially the decline or disintegration of established social norms or relationships
breakdown of social norms or unity

Examples

The fragmentation of communities has led to increased feelings of isolation among individuals.
Many scholars argue that modernization can accelerate the fragmentation of traditional social values.
Political polarization often contributes to the fragmentation of national identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disintegration - the process of losing cohesion or strength
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole

Etymology

From Latin fragmentum (a piece broken off), via French fragmentation.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
