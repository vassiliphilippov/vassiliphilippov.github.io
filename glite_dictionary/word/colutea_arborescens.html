Colutea arborescens

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈluːtiː əˌɹboʊˈɹɛsənz / (AmE), / kəˈluːtiː əˌbɒɹɪˈsɛnz / (BrE)
A European shrub with yellow flowers and inflated, bladder-like pods, often grown as an ornamental plant and wildlife food source.
European shrub with yellow flowers and bladdery pods

Examples

The Colutea arborescens in our garden attracts many butterflies with its bright yellow blooms.
Gardeners appreciate Colutea arborescens for its unique, bladder-like seed pods that add interest to borders.
Local wildlife benefits when Colutea arborescens is planted, as its flowers and pods provide food throughout the season.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Colutea' and species epithet 'arborescens' meaning 'becoming tree-like'.
