Plutonian

1. ADJ

/ pluˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / pluːˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
of or relating to Hades or the underworld; characteristic of the realm of the dead in Greek mythology.
relating to Hades or the underworld

Examples

The poet described the river Styx as a Plutonian stream that separated the living from the dead.
The catacombs had a Plutonian darkness that chilled everyone who entered.
His imagination conjured up Plutonian visions of a shadowy afterlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infernal - relating to or characteristic of hell or the underworld

Etymology

From Latin Plutonius (of Pluto), from Pluto (the ruler of the underworld in Roman mythology).
