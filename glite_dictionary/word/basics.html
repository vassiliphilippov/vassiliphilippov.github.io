basics

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪks / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪks / (BrE)
the essential facts or principles of a subject that must be learned or understood first
fundamental facts or principles

Examples

Before you start cooking complicated dishes, it's important to master the basics.
The course covers the basics of computer programming for beginners.
He explained the basics of photography to the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentals - basic facts or foundational principles
  • Synonym: essentials - the most important principles or aspects
  • Antonym: details - the small parts that make up a whole or complete picture

Etymology

From 'basic' (from Greek 'basis' meaning 'base, foundation') plus the plural -s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪks / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪks / (BrE)
the essential principles or fundamental facts that form the foundation of a subject or skill
essential principles; fundamental facts

Examples

Before learning advanced techniques, it's important to master the basics.
The course covers the basics of computer programming for beginners.
Let's review the basics one more time before the test.

Synset Examples

first you must learn the fundamentals
let's get down to basics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentals - essential or primary principles of a subject
  • Synonym: essentials - most important or necessary parts of something

Etymology

From basic, in plural use since the mid-20th century to mean fundamental principles.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
