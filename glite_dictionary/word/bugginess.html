bugginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of having many bugs or errors, especially in software or technology
state of having many bugs or errors

Examples

The bugginess of the new update frustrated many users.
Developers are working hard to reduce the bugginess of the application before launch.
People often complain about the bugginess of beta versions of software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buggy - containing many bugs or defects, especially in software

Etymology

From 'buggy' + '-ness', where 'bug' refers to software errors.
