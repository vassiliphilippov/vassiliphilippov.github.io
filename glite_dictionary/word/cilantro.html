cilantro

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɑntroʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈlæntrəʊ / (BrE)
an herb with fragrant, parsley-like leaves and seeds, used especially in cooking; also known as coriander in British English
aromatic herb used in cooking
american

Examples

We sprinkled fresh cilantro over the tacos before serving.
Some people think cilantro tastes soapy, while others love its fresh flavor.
She bought a bunch of cilantro to use in her homemade salsa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - a herb also called cilantro, especially in British English

Etymology

From Spanish 'cilantro', from Latin 'coriandrum' (coriander).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈlæntroʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈlæntrəʊ / (BrE)
the leaves of the coriander plant, used as an herb in cooking, especially in Mexican, Indian, and Southeast Asian cuisine
herb; coriander leaves used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled chopped cilantro on top of the soup for extra flavor.
Some people love the fresh taste of cilantro, while others find it soapy.
Fresh cilantro is a common ingredient in salsa and guacamole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - seed and plant whose leaves are called cilantro in American English

Etymology

Spanish cilantro, from Latin coriandrum (coriander).
