Rubia tinctorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbiə tɪŋkˈtɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈruːbɪə tɪŋkˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial herb with small yellow flowers and red roots, once widely cultivated as the main source of the red dye alizarin
a plant used for red dye (alizarin)

Examples

Rubia tinctorum was once widely cultivated in Europe for its red dye.
The roots of Rubia tinctorum have been used in textile dyeing for centuries.
Botanists study Rubia tinctorum to understand traditional sources of pigments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madder - the common name for Rubia tinctorum, a plant yielding red dye

Etymology

From Latin: 'rubia' meaning 'madder' and 'tinctorum' meaning 'of dyes'.
