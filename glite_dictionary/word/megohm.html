megohm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡoʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəʊm / (BrE)
a unit of electrical resistance equal to one million ohms
unit of electrical resistance (1,000,000 ohms)

Examples

Most household insulators have a resistance measured in megohms.
The technician measured the circuit's resistance and found it was over five megohms.
A megohm is commonly used as a benchmark when testing electrical insulation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ohm - unit of electrical resistance
  • Degree Higher: gigaohm - unit of electrical resistance equal to one billion ohms
  • Degree Lower: kilohm - unit of electrical resistance equal to one thousand ohms

Etymology

From 'mega-' meaning one million and 'ohm,' the SI unit of resistance.
