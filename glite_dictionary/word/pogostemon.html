Pogostemon

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈɡoʊstəˌmɒn / (AmE), / pəˈɡɒstɪmɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic shrubs and small trees whose leaves yield a fragrant oil, used especially in perfumery.
genus of fragrant Asiatic shrubs and trees

Examples

Pogostemon species are commonly cultivated for their aromatic leaves that produce essential oils.
The patchouli plant, known for its perfume, belongs to the genus Pogostemon.
Botanists discovered a new Pogostemon species in the wilds of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'pōgōstēs' (bearded) + 'stēmōn' (thread), referring to the hairy stamens of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈɡɑːstəˌmɒn / (AmE), / pəˈɡɒstəmɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic shrubs or trees whose leaves yield a fragrant oil, including patchouli and other aromatic plants.
genus of aromatic Asiatic shrubs or trees

Examples

The essential oil extracted from Pogostemon leaves is widely used in perfumes.
Pogostemon is best known for species such as patchouli, valued for their fragrance.
Botanists have studied various Pogostemon species for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Etymology

From Greek *pōgōn* (beard) + *stēmōn* (thread), referring to the bearded stamens of the flowers.
