Kach

1. NOUN

/ kɑx / (AmE), / kax / (BrE)
a Jewish militant political organization founded in the 1970s to combat antisemitism and promote restoration of a biblical state of Israel, later designated as a terrorist group by Israel and other countries
Jewish militant political organization (founded 1970s)

Examples

The Kach movement was banned by the Israeli government in 1994.
Members of Kach were known for their hardline views and controversial actions.
Several countries have designated Kach as a terrorist organization.

Etymology

From Hebrew כך (kach), meaning 'thus' or 'so'.
