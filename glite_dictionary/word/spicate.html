spicate

1. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈspaɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant or inflorescence) arranged in or resembling a spike.
arranged in spikes; spike-like

Examples

The plant's spicate flowers form a long, narrow cluster at the top of the stem.
Many grasses have a spicate inflorescence, where the flowers are arranged along a central axis.
The botanist noted the spicate arrangement of seeds on the specimen.

Synset Examples

spicate inflorescence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiky - having or resembling spikes

Etymology

From Latin spicatus (bearing ears of grain), from spica (spike, ear of grain).
