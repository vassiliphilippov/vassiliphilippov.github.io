nor'-nor'-east

1. ADV

/ nɔːr nɔːr ˈiːst / (AmE), / nɔː nɔː ˈiːst / (BrE)
to, toward, or in the north-northeast direction; in a direction between north and northeast.
toward north-northeast direction

Examples

The ship sailed nor'-nor'-east out of the harbor.
We should head nor'-nor'-east at sunrise to avoid the storm.
The trail bends nor'-nor'-east after the old oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north-northeast - the compass direction between north and northeast

Etymology

Contracted from 'north-north-east,' indicating a specific compass point.

2. NOUN

/ nɔr nɔr ˈist / (AmE), / nɔː nɔː ˈiːst / (BrE)
the compass point that is midway between north and northeast, corresponding to north-northeast (NNE) on the compass rose
compass point between north and northeast

Examples

The ship changed course to nor'-nor'-east to avoid the shallows.
On the old map, the island sits just off the coast at nor'-nor'-east.
The wind was blowing steadily from nor'-nor'-east all morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north-northeast - compass point halfway between north and northeast

Etymology

From a contraction of 'north-north-east', a traditional nautical compass point name.
