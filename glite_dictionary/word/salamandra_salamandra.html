Salamandra salamandra

1. NOUN

/ sæləˈmændrə sæləˈmændrə / (AmE), / sæləˈmændrə sæləˈmændrə / (BrE)
A European salamander species, commonly known as the fire salamander, characterized by its black body with yellow spots or streaks.
a European fire salamander species

Examples

The Salamandra salamandra is often found in deciduous forests across Europe.
Herpetologists are studying the distinct coloration patterns of the Salamandra salamandra.
Local folklore in some European regions features the Salamandra salamandra as a creature with magical powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire salamander - Common name for Salamandra salamandra
  • Degree Lower: salamander - Amphibian with lizard-like appearance in the family Salamandridae

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on the genre 'Salamandra', from Greek 'salamandra' (a kind of lizard or salamander).
