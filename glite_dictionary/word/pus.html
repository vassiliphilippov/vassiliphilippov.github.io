pus

1. NOUN

/ pʌs / (AmE), / pʌs / (BrE)
a thick yellowish or greenish fluid produced at sites of infection, consisting of dead white blood cells, bacteria, and tissue debris
thick fluid from infection
medical

Examples

The wound began to ooze pus, indicating an infection.
Doctors drained the pus to help the patient recover.
You should see a doctor if you notice pus around a cut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppuration - the process of forming pus

Etymology

From Latin 'pus' (matter from a sore).
