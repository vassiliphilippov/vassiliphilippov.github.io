decomposable

1. ADJ

/ ˌdiːkəmˈpoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈpəʊzəbl / (BrE)
able to be separated or broken down into parts or components; capable of being partitioned.
able to be separated into parts

Examples

The molecule is decomposable into simpler chemical compounds.
In mathematics, a set may be decomposable into several subsets with distinct properties.
Plastic is not easily decomposable in the environment, causing long-term pollution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indivisible - not able to be divided or separated

Etymology

From Latin 'decomponere' (to separate) + '-able'.
