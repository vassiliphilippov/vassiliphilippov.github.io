dolichocephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːlɪkoʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkə(ʊ)ˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
The condition of having a long, narrow head in proportion to its width.
long, narrow head shape (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that dolichocephaly can result from premature closure of certain skull sutures.
In anthropology, skull shapes such as dolichocephaly are used to classify populations.
Certain genetic syndromes may increase the likelihood of dolichocephaly in infants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brachycephaly - The condition of having a short, broad head

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + kephalē (head).
