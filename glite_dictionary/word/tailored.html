tailored

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪlərd / (AmE), / ˈteɪləd / (BrE)
(of clothing) made or fitted to the specific measurements or requirements of an individual; custom-made.
custom-made or fitted (clothing)

Examples

She prefers to wear tailored suits that fit her perfectly.
The designer specializes in tailored dresses for special occasions.
His tailored jacket gave him a sharp, professional appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-the-rack - (of clothing) sold in finished standard sizes, not custom-made

Etymology

From the verb 'tailor', meaning to make or adapt clothing, from Anglo-Norman French 'taillour' (cutter, tailor), from Old French 'tailler' (to cut).

2. ADJ

/ ˈteɪ.lɚd / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.ləd / (BrE)
(of clothing or design) having a simple, neat, and fitted style, often made to fit closely and precisely; characterized by clean lines and a sleek appearance.
simple, neat, and fitted in style or design

Examples

She wore a tailored dress that emphasized her elegant style.
The living room was decorated with tailored curtains and modern furniture.
He prefers tailored jackets over baggy ones for a sharper look.

Synset Examples

a neat tailored suit
tailored curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fitted - closely shaped to the body or to specific dimensions
  • Antonym: loose - not fitting closely or tightly

Etymology

From 'tailor,' which comes via Middle French 'taillour' from Latin 'taliare' (to cut).
