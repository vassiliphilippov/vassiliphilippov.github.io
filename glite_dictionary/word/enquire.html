to enquire

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to ask someone for information about something; to seek an answer by asking
to ask for information

Examples

I called the office to enquire about the status of my application.
You should enquire at the hotel desk if you need extra towels.
A student enquired whether the class would be held online this week.

Synset Examples

Ask your teacher about trigonometry
The children asked me about their dead grandmother
I inquired about their special today
He had to ask directions several times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inquire - to ask for information, usually more formal spelling
  • Synonym: to ask - to put a question to someone
  • Antonym: answer - to respond to a question

Etymology

From Old French enquerre, from Latin inquirere (to seek after, ask).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to ask for information or investigate; to seek to know about something
ask for information, investigate

Examples

She called the office to enquire about the status of her application.
Journalists enquired if the minister would be making a statement.
Before booking the tickets, he decided to enquire about any available discounts.

Synset Examples

He wondered who had built this beautiful church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inquire - ask for information; alternate spelling with identical meaning
  • Synonym: wonder - desire or be curious to know something
  • Synonym: to ask - request information from someone

Etymology

From Old French *enquerre*, from Latin *inquirere* (to seek, inquire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00731363-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to conduct an official investigation or to seek information about something
conduct an investigation; seek information

Examples

The committee will enquire into possible misconduct within the organization.
Reporters began to enquire about the cause of the sudden resignation.
The police were called to enquire into the theft of several valuable paintings.

Synset Examples

The district attorney's office investigated reports of possible irregularities
inquire into the disappearance of the rich old lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to carry out a systematic examination or inquiry
  • Synonym: to inquire - to ask for information or investigate

Etymology

From Old French enquerre, from Vulgar Latin *inquaerere, variant of Latin inquirere (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00787624-v
Synset POS: v
