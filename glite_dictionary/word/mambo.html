mambo

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈmam.bəʊ / (BrE)
to dance the mambo, a lively Latin American dance with syncopated rhythm
to dance the mambo

Examples

She loves to mambo whenever the band plays Latin music at the club.
They mamboed across the dance floor with impressive energy and style.
Last night, we spent hours mamboing at the salsa party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsa - a lively, energetic Latin American dance; also its verb sense
  • Synonym: tango - a ballroom dance of South American origin; also its verb sense

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish mambo, name of a dance and musical style.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈmambəʊ / (BrE)
A lively Latin American dance style, similar in rhythm to the rumba, often performed to the music of the same name.
Latin American dance style

Examples

They learned to mambo at a dance class in Havana.
The club featured a live band playing classic mambo rhythms all night.
Her favorite dance is the energetic mambo because of its lively steps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a Latin American dance with a similar rhythmic pattern

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish 'mambo', possibly of African origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00539705-n
Synset POS: n
