cooperation

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of working together with others towards a common goal or benefit.
working together for a common goal

Examples

The project succeeded thanks to the cooperation between the two companies.
International cooperation is essential to address climate change effectively.
The teacher praised the students' cooperation during the group activity.

Synset Examples

economic cooperation
they agreed on a policy of cooperation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaboration - the action of working with someone to produce something
  • Antonym: competition - rivalry between individuals or groups

Etymology

From Latin 'cooperatio', from 'cooperari' (to work together).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of working together with another person or group to achieve a common goal
the act of working together

Examples

The project succeeded thanks to the excellent cooperation between all departments.
International cooperation is crucial in addressing climate change.
Their cooperation on this issue made the process faster and smoother.

Synset Examples

their cooperation with us was essential for the success of our mission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaboration - the act of working together, especially in a creative or intellectual endeavor
  • Antonym: competition - the act of opposing or striving against others for the same goal

Etymology

From Latin cooperatio(n-), from cooperari ‘work together’.
