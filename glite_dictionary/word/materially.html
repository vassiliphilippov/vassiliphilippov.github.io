materially

1. ADV

/ məˈtɪr.i.ə.li / (AmE), / məˈtɪə.ri.ə.li / (BrE)
to a significant or important degree; substantially.
to a significant degree

Examples

The new policies have materially improved working conditions in the factory.
Her financial situation has changed materially over the past year.
If the facts change materially, the conclusion might be different.

Synset Examples

it aided him materially in winning the argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significantly - to a notable or important degree
  • Synonym: substantially - to a large or considerable degree
  • Antonym: insignificantly - to an unimportant or trivial degree

Etymology

From Latin 'materialis' (relating to matter) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ məˈtɪriəli / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəli / (BrE)
with regard to physical or practical aspects rather than the psychological or theoretical; in a substantial or significant way.
with respect to material or practical aspects

Examples

Their financial situation has materially improved this year compared to last.
The proposal would not materially affect current regulations.
She is psychologically similar to her sister, but they are materially different in their lifestyles.

Synset Examples

psychologically similar but materially different

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantially - to a great or significant degree; largely
  • Antonym: immaterially - in an immaterial or insignificant way

Etymology

From 'material' + -ly, meaning 'having to do with matter or substance', from Latin 'materialis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137680-r
Synset POS: r
