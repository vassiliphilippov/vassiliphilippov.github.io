to unionise

1. VERB

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːniənaɪz / (BrE)
to form or join a labor union, or cause workers to do so
form or join a labor union

Examples

Many employees voted to unionise after the company announced pay cuts.
The company resisted efforts by workers to unionise.
After years of discussion, the factory staff finally decided to unionise.

Synset Examples

The auto workers decided to unionize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unionize - alternative spelling (American English) of 'unionise'

Etymology

From 'union' plus the suffix '-ise', modeled after similar verbs. Spelling 'unionize' is used in American English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02439085-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːniənaɪz / (BrE)
to recruit workers for a labor union or organize workers into a union
organize into a labor union
british

Examples

The workers tried to unionise after management changed the work conditions.
Activists visited the factory in an attempt to unionise the staff.
She spent years helping employees unionise in various industries.

Synset Examples

We don't allow people to come into our plant and try to unionize the workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unionize - to organize or recruit for a labor union (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'union' + '-ise', modeled after the noun 'union'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
