Philipp Melanchthon

1. PROPN

/ fɪˈlɪp məˈlɑŋkθɑn / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪp mɛˈlaŋkθən / (BrE)
(1497–1560) German theologian and reformer, known as Martin Luther's successor and a key leader of the Reformation in Germany.
German Reformation leader (1497–1560)

Examples

Philipp Melanchthon was instrumental in shaping the educational reforms of the Reformation era.
The partnership between Luther and Philipp Melanchthon greatly influenced Protestant theology.
Many universities in Germany honor Philipp Melanchthon for his contributions to humanist scholarship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Martin Luther - German theologian and leader of the Protestant Reformation
