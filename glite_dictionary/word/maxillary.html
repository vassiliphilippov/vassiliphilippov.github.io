maxillary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæksəˌleri / (AmE), / makˈsɪləri / (BrE)
relating to the upper jaw or the maxilla, especially in vertebrates
relating to the upper jaw
medical

Examples

Swelling in the maxillary region often indicates a sinus infection.
The dentist explained the structure of the maxillary arch to the patient.
Orthodontic treatment sometimes requires maxillary expansion to correct bite issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mandibular - relating to the lower jaw
  • Synonym: maxilla - the upper jawbone

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' (jawbone, jaw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæksəˌlɛri / (AmE), / makˈsɪləri / (BrE)
the upper jaw in vertebrates, especially when it is fused to the cranium.
upper jawbone (especially when fused)
medical

Examples

The maxillary supports the upper teeth in most mammals.
In many fish, the maxillary is a prominent bone of the upper jaw.
The surgeon examined the patient’s maxillary for signs of trauma.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mandible - the lower jawbone in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'maxillaris', relating to the jawbone, from 'maxilla' (jawbone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05291631-n
Synset POS: n
