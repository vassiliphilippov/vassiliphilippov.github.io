dodo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊdəʊ / (BrE)
a large, flightless bird native to Mauritius, now extinct, related to pigeons
extinct flightless bird of Mauritius

Examples

The dodo became extinct in the late 17th century due to overhunting and introduced predators.
Scientists study the dodo to understand the impact of human activity on extinction.
The expression 'as dead as a dodo' is used to describe something that is completely extinct or obsolete.

Etymology

Probably from Portuguese 'doudo' (simpleton, fool), possibly via Dutch 'dodaars' (knot-arse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.dəʊ / (BrE)
An old-fashioned person, or someone whose style or ideas are no longer fashionable.
person with outdated style
informal

Examples

People called him a dodo because he still wore bell-bottom jeans.
If you don’t learn to use new technology, you’ll end up a dodo at work.
She was worried she might look like a dodo in her grandmother’s dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuddy-duddy - a person with old-fashioned ideas and style
  • Antonym: modern - relating to current or recent times, up-to-date

Etymology

From the extinct bird dodo (Raphus cucullatus), used figuratively for someone considered out of date or behind the times.
