Pavonia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈvoʊniə / (AmE), / pəˈvəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical hairy shrubs or herbs found in tropical and subtropical regions, especially in South America.
genus of tropical shrubs or herbs

Examples

Several species of Pavonia are cultivated for their attractive flowers in gardens.
The Pavonia genus was first described in the 18th century by European botanists.
Researchers discovered a new Pavonia species in the tropical forests of Brazil.

Etymology

Named after Spanish botanist José Antonio Pavón (1754–1844).
