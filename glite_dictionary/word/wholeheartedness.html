wholeheartedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊlˈhɑrtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊlˈhɑːtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being fully committed or completely enthusiastic about something
complete commitment or enthusiasm

Examples

She tackled the new project with wholeheartedness, inspiring everyone around her.
His wholeheartedness in volunteering was evident to everyone at the shelter.
The team's wholeheartedness was key to their success in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - strong excitement or eagerness
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

Formed from 'wholehearted' + '-ness', where 'wholehearted' means 'with one's whole heart'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊlˈhɑrtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊlˈhɑːtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being completely sincere, enthusiastic, or committed to something
complete sincerity or enthusiasm

Examples

She approached every project with remarkable wholeheartedness and dedication.
His wholeheartedness won the respect and admiration of his colleagues.
The team’s wholeheartedness was evident in their passionate performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: halfheartedness - lacking enthusiasm or commitment

Etymology

From 'wholehearted' + '-ness', meaning the state or quality of being wholehearted.
