remise

1. NOUN

/ rəˈmiːz / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːz / (BrE)
A small building for housing coaches, carriages, or other vehicles, typically used in historical contexts.
building for storing carriages or vehicles
archaic

Examples

The old estate still has a remise behind the stables where the carriages were once kept.
During the tour, we saw a beautifully restored remise full of vintage coaches.
The remise now serves as a garage for modern cars, but once housed elegant horse-drawn carriages.

Etymology

From French remise (coach-house, shed), from remettre (to put back).

3. NOUN

/ rəˈmiz / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːz / (BrE)
In fencing, a second thrust made on the same lunge, typically when an opponent fails to riposte.
fencing: second thrust on same lunge

Examples

After parrying his attack, she quickly executed a remise when he failed to defend himself.
The coach praised his perfect timing on the remise during the final bout.
A well-placed remise can catch an inattentive opponent off guard.

Etymology

From French remise (a putting back, remitting), from remettre (to put back, to send again).
