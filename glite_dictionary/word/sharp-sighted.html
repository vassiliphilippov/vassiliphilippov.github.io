sharp-sighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑrpˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having very keen or acute vision; able to see details clearly.
having very keen vision

Examples

The sharp-sighted hawk spotted a mouse in the field from high above.
Even at night, her sharp-sighted eyes could make out shapes in the darkness.
As a photographer, being sharp-sighted helps him notice details others might miss.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted as a cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keen-eyed - having very acute eyesight

Etymology

Formed from 'sharp' (keen) + 'sighted' (having sight).

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑrpˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
having an unusually keen perception or alertness; able to notice or understand things quickly and clearly
keenly perceptive or alert

Examples

The detective was sharp-sighted, instantly noticing small details others missed.
She proved to be incredibly sharp-sighted when it came to understanding people's motives.
His sharp-sighted analysis helped the company spot a flaw in the plan.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted into the faults of the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • Synonym: observant - quick to notice things
  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing an accurate and deep understanding

Etymology

Formed from 'sharp' (keen, acute) + 'sighted' (having sight), indicating keenness of mental or physical perception.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01749902-s
Synset POS: s
