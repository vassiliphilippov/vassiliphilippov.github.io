multicolor

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈkʌlə / (BrE)
having sections or patches of different, usually bright, colors.
having more than one color

Examples

The artist created a multicolor mural that brightened the entire room.
She wore a multicolor scarf that caught everyone’s attention.
Our living room rug features a bold multicolor pattern.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polychromatic - having multiple colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + color (color).
