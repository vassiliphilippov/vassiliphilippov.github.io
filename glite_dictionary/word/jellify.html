to jellify

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlɪˌfʌɪ / (BrE)
to become or make into a jelly-like substance
become or make into jelly

Examples

If you leave the mixture to cool, it will eventually jellify.
The biochemist observed the solution begin to jellify under specific conditions.
Some desserts require gelatin so the mixture can jellify in the refrigerator.

Synset Examples

The sauce jellified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gelatinize - to make or become gelatinous; to form a gel

Etymology

Formed from 'jelly' + '-ify' (to make or become).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to make or become like jelly; to convert into a jelly-like substance
make into or become jelly

Examples

When you add agar-agar, the solution will jellify within an hour.
High concentrations of sugar can jellify fruit preserves during cooking.
The mixture needs to jellify before it is ready to serve.

Synset Examples

jellify a liquid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid
  • Synonym: to gelatinize - to make into a gel or jelly-like state

Etymology

Formed from 'jelly' + '-ify' (a suffix meaning 'to make' or 'become').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
