screwball

1. NOUN

/ ˈskruˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈskruːbɔːl / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a strange, whimsical, or eccentric way
eccentric or whimsical person

Examples

Everyone at work thinks Jerry is a screwball, but he always makes us laugh.
She may seem like a screwball, but her creative ideas often lead to brilliant solutions.
The movie is full of screwball characters who get into hilarious situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentric - a person with unconventional or odd habits
  • Synonym: oddball - a strange or eccentric person

Etymology

From 'screw' (colloquially meaning 'to twist') + 'ball', originally a baseball term for a type of pitch.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskruˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈskruːbɔːl / (BrE)
a baseball pitch with reverse spin that causes the ball to curve toward the same side from which it was thrown
a baseball pitch with reverse spin

Examples

The pitcher surprised everyone by throwing a screwball in the final inning.
His signature screwball baffled hitters throughout the season.
She practiced her screwball for weeks before mastering the tricky pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curveball - a pitch that curves as it approaches the batter

3. ADJ

/ ˈskruˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈskruːbɔːl / (BrE)
foolish or completely unreasonable; lacking any sense or likelihood of success
foolish or totally unsound
informal american

Examples

The board rejected his screwball idea as soon as they heard the details.
She always comes up with screwball schemes that make everyone laugh.
Launching that product without market research was a completely screwball move.

Synset Examples

a crazy scheme
half-baked ideas
a screwball proposal without a prayer of working

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - foolish or lacking common sense
  • Synonym: half-baked - not fully thought through or planned; foolish
  • Antonym: sensible - reasonable; showing good sense

Etymology

Originally American English; from baseball, where a 'screwball' is a type of pitch with an unexpected curve.
