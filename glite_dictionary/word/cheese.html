cheese

1. NOUN

/ tʃiz / (AmE), / tʃiːz / (BrE)
A solid food made by pressing the curd of milk, often aged and flavored in various ways.
solid food from pressed curd of milk

Examples

She bought a block of sharp cheddar cheese from the market.
A platter of fruit and cheese was served before dinner.
He sprinkled grated cheese on top of the pasta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dairy - food products made from milk
  • Degree Lower: yogurt - a creamy food made by fermenting milk

Etymology

From Old English 'cēse', from Latin 'caseus' (cheese).

2. NOUN

/ tʃiz / (AmE), / tʃiːz / (BrE)
an Old World perennial plant (Malva species) with erect or spreading stems and clusters of rosy-purple flowers, introduced into the United States
a plant with rosy-purple flowers (Malva, or mallow)

Examples

The common cheese grew wild across the old meadow, its purple flowers bright among the grass.
Children would often play among the tall stems of cheese plants near the hedgerows.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate cheese as a hardy ornamental plant in American gardens.

3. VERB

/ tʃiz / (AmE), / tʃiːz / (BrE)
(imperative, slang, dated) Used to tell someone to stop what they are doing, or to leave quickly, especially to avoid trouble.
(dated slang) Stop it; get away
slang archaic

Examples

The boys heard the police siren and one of them shouted, "Cheese it!"
If you see the boss coming, just cheese it so she doesn't catch you.
Back in those days, crooks would cheese it whenever the law showed up.

Synset Examples

Cheese it!

Etymology

Uncertain; possibly from thieves' cant, late 19th century American slang.
