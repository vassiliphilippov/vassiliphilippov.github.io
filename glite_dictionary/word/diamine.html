diamine

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæmin / (AmE), / daɪˈæmiːn / (BrE)
Any organic compound containing two amino (–NH2) groups.
organic compound with two amino groups

Examples

Ethylenediamine is a simple diamine used in many industrial applications.
The synthesis produced a new aromatic diamine with interesting properties.
A diamine can react with dicarboxylic acids to form polyamides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amine - an organic compound derived from ammonia by replacement of one or more hydrogen atoms by organic groups

Etymology

From di- (two) + amine (derived from ammonia).
