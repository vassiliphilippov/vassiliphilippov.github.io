consolable

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsoʊl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊl.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be comforted or soothed after disappointment or distress
able to be comforted

Examples

After the kind words from her friends, she was finally consolable.
The child was upset at first, but became consolable after a warm hug.
Unlike her inconsolable brother, Emily was surprisingly consolable after the loss.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconsolable - unable to be comforted or soothed

Etymology

From Latin consolari (to console) with the suffix -able.
