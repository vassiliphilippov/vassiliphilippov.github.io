Faruk I

1. PROPN

/ fæˈruːk / (AmE), / fæˈruːk / (BrE)
King of Egypt from 1936 to 1952, deposed by a military coup; notable for his lavish lifestyle and subsequent exile.
King of Egypt (1936–1952), deposed in coup

Examples

Faruk I was forced into exile following the 1952 Egyptian Revolution.
Many historians critique the reign of Faruk I for its extravagance and political mismanagement.
After his abdication, Faruk I lived in Italy until his death in 1965.

Etymology

From Arabic فاروق (Fārūq), a personal name, combined with 'I' for regnal numeral.
