savoir-faire

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæv.wɑrˈfer / (AmE), / ˌsæv.wɑːˈfeə / (BrE)
the ability to act or speak appropriately in social situations; social skill and tact
social skill; ability to handle situations

Examples

She handled the difficult situation with great savoir-faire and professionalism.
His savoir-faire made him a favorite at diplomatic gatherings.
A little savoir-faire can go a long way when meeting new people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tact - skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or difficult issues
  • Antonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability, especially in social situations

Etymology

From French 'savoir-faire', literally 'know how to do'.
