animal

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəməl / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl / (BrE)
A living organism that feeds on organic matter, typically has specialized sense organs and nervous system, and is capable of voluntary movement.
a living organism capable of movement

Examples

The dog is an animal that has been domesticated for thousands of years.
Children are often fascinated by every kind of animal at the zoo.
The biologist studied how each animal in the ecosystem interacted with its environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plant - a living organism lacking the power of voluntary movement

Etymology

From Latin animal (a living being), from anima (breath, soul).

2. ADJ

/ ˈænɪməl / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl / (BrE)
relating to physical appetites and passions rather than intellect or reason; sensual or carnal
relating to physical appetites or passions

Examples

He tried to resist his animal instincts and act rationally.
There was an animal intensity in their rivalry that surprised everyone.
Food provided her with a purely animal pleasure that had little to do with nutrition.

Synset Examples

animal instincts
carnal knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnal - relating to physical, especially sexual, needs and activities
  • Synonym: sensual - relating to the gratification of the senses or physical pleasures
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the intellect or spirit rather than material or physical things

Etymology

From Latin animalis (having breath, living, animal), from anima (breath, soul).
