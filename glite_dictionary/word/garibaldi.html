garibaldi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡærɪˈbɑldi / (AmE), / ˌɡærɪˈbɑːldi / (BrE)
a loose, high-necked blouse with long sleeves, originally modeled after the red shirts worn by Garibaldi's soldiers
loose, long-sleeved blouse

Examples

She wore a bright red garibaldi with a simple skirt to the event.
The fashion of the garibaldi blouse spread rapidly throughout Europe in the 19th century.
My grandmother showed me an old photo of her mother in a garibaldi blouse.

Etymology

Named after Giuseppe Garibaldi, an Italian patriot whose followers wore similar red shirts.
