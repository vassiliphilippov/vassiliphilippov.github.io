Garibaldi

1. PROPN

/ ˌgɛrəˈbɑldi / (AmE), / ˌɡærɪˈbɑːldi / (BrE)
Giuseppe Garibaldi (1807–1882), an Italian patriot and military leader whose campaigns contributed to the unification of Italy.
Italian patriot and unification leader

Examples

Garibaldi is celebrated as a hero of Italian unification.
The legacy of Garibaldi continues to inspire movements for freedom.
During his campaigns, Garibaldi became a symbol of nationalism across Europe.

Etymology

From Italian Garibaldi, the surname of Giuseppe Garibaldi.
