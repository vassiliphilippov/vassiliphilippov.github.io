Down Easter

1. NOUN

/ daʊn ˈiːstər / (AmE), / daʊn ˈiːstə / (BrE)
a native or resident of Maine, especially from the eastern coastal region
a person from Maine (US)
american

Examples

The harbor was filled with fishing boats owned by Down Easters who had lived there for generations.
As a proud Down Easter, she shared stories about Maine's rugged coastline and traditions.
Many Down Easters gather each summer for the annual lobster festival in Rockland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mainer - a person from Maine

Etymology

From 'Down East,' a regional term referring to the coastal area of Maine, plus 'er' denoting an inhabitant.
