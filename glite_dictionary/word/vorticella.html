Vorticella

1. NOUN

/ vɔr.tɪˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌvɔː.tɪˈsel.ə / (BrE)
a genus of ciliated protozoans characterized by a goblet-shaped body and a retractile stalk
a genus of ciliated protozoans

Examples

Vorticella can often be found attached to submerged plants in freshwater ponds.
Scientists studied the contractile stalk mechanism of Vorticella under a microscope.
A colony of Vorticella was observed interacting with other protozoa in the laboratory.

Etymology

From Latin vorticella, diminutive of vortex (whirlpool), referring to the organism's cilia movement.
