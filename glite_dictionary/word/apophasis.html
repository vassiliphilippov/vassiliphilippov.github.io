apophasis

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑː.fə.sɪs / (AmE), / əˈpɒf.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which something is mentioned by stating that it will not be mentioned.
rhetorical mention by negation

Examples

The candidate used apophasis when she said, 'I won't even mention my opponent's criminal record.'
By employing apophasis, the writer drew attention to the very issue he claimed to ignore.
His subtle use of apophasis allowed him to criticize without being overtly accusatory.

Etymology

From Greek 'apophanai' (to say no, deny), from 'apo-' (off, away) + 'phanai' (to speak).
