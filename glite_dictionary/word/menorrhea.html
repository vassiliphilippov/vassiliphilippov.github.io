menorrhea

1. NOUN

/ mɛnəˈriːə / (AmE), / mɛnəˈriːə / (BrE)
the normal monthly flow of blood from the uterus during a woman's reproductive years; menstruation
normal monthly menstrual flow
medical

Examples

Some women experience discomfort during menorrhea each month.
Medical textbooks describe menorrhea as a normal physiological process.
Regular menorrhea is considered a sign of reproductive health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menstruation - the process of monthly uterine bleeding in women

Etymology

From Greek mēn (month) + rhoia (flow).
