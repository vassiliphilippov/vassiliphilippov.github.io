Hollands

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːləndz / (AmE), / ˈhɒləndz / (BrE)
a type of gin distilled in the Netherlands, typically maltier and heavier than London dry gin
Dutch gin (jenever)

Examples

He ordered a glass of Hollands at the bar, preferring its malty flavor to regular gin.
Traditional recipes for gin punch often call for Hollands instead of London dry gin.
Collectors sometimes search for vintage bottles of Hollands for their unique taste and historical value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gin - a clear alcoholic spirit distilled from grain or malt and flavored with juniper berries
  • Synonym: jenever - Dutch and Belgian juniper-flavored national spirit

Etymology

Refers to Holland (historical region, now commonly 'the Netherlands'), indicating its place of origin.
