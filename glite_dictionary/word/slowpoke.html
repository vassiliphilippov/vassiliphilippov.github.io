slowpoke

1. NOUN

/ ˈsloʊˌpoʊk / (AmE), / ˈsləʊˌpəʊk / (BrE)
a person who moves, acts, or responds more slowly than others; someone who is slow
a person who moves slowly
informal american

Examples

Hurry up, slowpoke, or we'll be late for the movie!
He called his brother a slowpoke for taking so long to get ready.
Sometimes I feel like a slowpoke compared to my energetic friends.

Synset Examples

in England they call a slowpoke a ‘slowcoach’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowcoach - a person who moves or acts slowly (chiefly British)

Etymology

American English, first recorded in the mid-19th century, probably modeled after "poke" meaning 'person' (now rare).
