Lugh

1. PROPN

/ luː / (AmE), / luː / (BrE)
an important god in ancient Celtic mythology, associated with arts, crafts, and skill
ancient Celtic god of skill and arts

Examples

Many ancient Irish legends feature Lugh as a central character and hero.
The festival of Lughnasadh is named in honor of the Celtic god Lugh.
Scholars believe that Lugh was worshipped throughout much of pre-Christian Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lugus - another name for Lugh in continental Celtic mythology

Etymology

From Old Irish Lug, cognate with Welsh Lleu; possibly connected to Proto-Indo-European *lewk- (light, brightness).
