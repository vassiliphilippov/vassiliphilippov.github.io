anapest

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌpɛst / (AmE), / ˈanəpɛst / (BrE)
A metrical unit in poetry consisting of two unstressed syllables followed by one stressed syllable.
metrical unit: unstressed-unstressed-stressed

Examples

The poet used an anapest to create a bouncing, rhythmic effect in the verse.
Each line of the limerick begins with an anapest to maintain its playful rhythm.
When you scan the poem, you can clearly identify the anapest pattern in each line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dactyl - metrical unit with stressed-unstressed-unstressed syllables

Etymology

From Latin 'anapaestus', from Greek 'anapaistos' meaning 'struck back'.
