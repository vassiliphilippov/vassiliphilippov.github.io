alcoholic

1. ADJ

/ ˌælkəˈhɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌalkəˈhɒlɪk / (BrE)
containing or relating to alcohol; characteristic of or caused by alcohol
containing or relating to alcohol

Examples

Some alcoholic drinks are stronger than others.
She prefers fruit juices over alcoholic beverages.
This restaurant does not serve any alcoholic products.

Synset Examples

alcoholic drinks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonalcoholic - not containing alcohol

Etymology

From alcohol + -ic, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01162103-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌæl.kəˈhɑ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈhɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
a person who habitually drinks excessive amounts of alcohol and is often unable to stop.
person addicted to alcohol

Examples

His family struggled for years to help their alcoholic relative overcome his addiction.
The support group was founded to assist alcoholics on their road to recovery.
She realized she had become an alcoholic when drinking interfered with her job and relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - person who is habitually drunk
  • Antonym: teetotaler - person who abstains completely from alcohol

Etymology

From alcohol +‎ -ic. 'Alcohol' from Arabic al-kuḥl.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09801608-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌæl.kəˈhɑ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈhɒ.lɪk / (BrE)
Addicted to or affected by the habitual consumption of alcohol.
addicted to alcohol

Examples

She struggled to care for her alcoholic father during his illness.
The organization provides support for alcoholic patients seeking recovery.
Several alcoholic employees requested flexible work hours for treatment.

Synset Examples

alcoholic expatriates in Paris

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not addicted to alcohol

Etymology

From alcohol + -ic, first used in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047463-s
Synset POS: s
