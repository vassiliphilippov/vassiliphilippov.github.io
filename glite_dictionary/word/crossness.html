crossness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs.nəs / (BrE)
A state of being irritable or easily annoyed; petulance.
irritability or petulance

Examples

Her crossness was obvious after hours of waiting in the queue.
He apologized for his crossness during the meeting and promised to be more patient.
The child's crossness faded once she had her favorite toy back.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From 'cross' (irritable) + -ness (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being irritable or ill-tempered; a tendency to be easily annoyed or angry.
tendency to be ill-tempered

Examples

His crossness this morning surprised everyone in the office.
A little sleep often helps to dispel a child's crossness.
She apologized later for her crossness during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed
  • Antonym: good nature - pleasant or cheerful disposition

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'cross' (meaning 'irritable' or 'angry') + the noun suffix '-ness'.
