mortar

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔrt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
to apply, join, or coat with mortar, especially in construction work
to apply or join with mortar

Examples

He carefully mortared the bricks to build a strong wall.
The workers will mortar the gaps between the stones before finishing the patio.
After mixing the cement, she began mortaring the tiles into place on the fireplace.

Synset Examples

mortar the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - to cover a surface with plaster

Etymology

From Old French *mortier*, from Latin *mortarium* (bowl for mixing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
a mixture, typically of cement, sand, and water, used to bond bricks or stones or to cover walls in building construction
building mixture used for bonding bricks or covering walls

Examples

The workers spread mortar between the bricks to strengthen the wall.
After mixing the mortar, the mason applied it evenly over the surface.
Cracks appeared in the old mortar holding the stones together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cement - the powdery substance used to make mortar and concrete
  • Degree Higher: concrete - building material composed of cement, sand, gravel, and water

Etymology

From Old French *mortier*, from Latin *mortarium* (bowl for mixing).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
a hard, bowl-shaped vessel in which substances are crushed or ground with a pestle, commonly used in cooking, pharmacy, or laboratory settings
bowl for grinding with a pestle

Examples

She ground the spices in a heavy mortar to release their flavor.
The pharmacist used a mortar and pestle to prepare the medication.
Every kitchen in the village had a mortar for crushing herbs and grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pestle - a tool used for crushing or grinding in a mortar

Etymology

From Old French 'mortier', from Latin 'mortarium' (bowl for pounding).

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
a short, smoothbore gun designed to fire shells at high angles, typically for delivering explosives over a short distance
short, high-angle artillery gun

Examples

The soldiers quickly assembled the mortar to provide cover fire for their advancing unit.
Heavy rain made it difficult to aim the mortar accurately during the battle.
Each team was equipped with a light mortar for launching explosive shells over enemy lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howitzer - a type of artillery piece with a shorter barrel, used for firing shells on trajectories between those of guns and mortars
  • Degree Higher: cannon - a large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels or fixed mounts

Etymology

From Old French *mortier*, from Latin *mortarium* (bowl for pounding), extended to the weapon from its resemblance to the bowl.
