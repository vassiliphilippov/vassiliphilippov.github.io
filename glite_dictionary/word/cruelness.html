cruelness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkruːl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being cruel; the inclination to cause pain or suffering to others
quality of being cruel

Examples

The cruelness of his actions shocked everyone who witnessed them.
Children should be protected from cruelness in all its forms.
There was a certain cruelness in the way she spoke to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - the quality of being cruel; causing pain or suffering

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix -ness to 'cruel', following standard English word formation.
