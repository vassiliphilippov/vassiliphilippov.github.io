Reseda luteola

1. PROPN

/ rəˈsidə luˈtiːələ / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːdə ljuːˈtiːələ / (BrE)
a European plant of the mignonette family, cultivated as a source of yellow dye and naturalized in North America; also known as weld
plant yielding yellow dye; weld

Examples

For centuries, Reseda luteola has been used to produce vibrant yellow dyes for textiles.
The botanist identified the wildflower as Reseda luteola growing on the hillside.
In traditional dyeing, leaves and stems of Reseda luteola are harvested in midsummer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - common name for Reseda luteola, a plant yielding yellow dye

Etymology

From Latin: 'Reseda' (the genus, meaning 'to calm' or 'to assuage'), 'luteola' (diminutive of 'luteus', meaning 'yellow').
