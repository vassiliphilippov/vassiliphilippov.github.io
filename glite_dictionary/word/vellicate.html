to vellicate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to touch or irritate (a part of the body) lightly, especially so as to cause a tingling or twitching sensation
touch or irritate lightly (body part)

Examples

If you vellicate the sole of his foot, he instinctively pulls away.
The doctor used a feather to vellicate the patient's skin and test nerve response.
Repeatedly vellicating the area behind the knee can cause uncontrollable giggles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tickle - to touch lightly so as to cause laughter or twitching

Etymology

From Latin vellicare (to pluck or twitch).

2. VERB

/ ˈvɛlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to irritate or stimulate as if by a nip, pinch, or minor tear; to twitch or cause slight irritation
to stimulate or irritate as if by pinching

Examples

Some fabrics can vellicate sensitive skin, causing mild discomfort.
Sudden cold air began to vellicate the back of his neck.
He winced as the dentist's tool seemed to vellicate his gum with each movement.

Synset Examples

smooth surfaces can vellicate the teeth
the pain is as if sharp points pinch your back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - to cause slight annoyance or discomfort
  • Synonym: to stimulate - to cause to be more active or alert, often by slight physical sensation

Etymology

From Latin 'vellicare' (to twitch, to pluck).

3. VERB

/ ˈvɛlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛlɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to twitch, stimulate, or excite with a slight or sudden sensation; to irritate slightly
to twitch or stimulate suddenly

Examples

The sudden cold breeze seemed to vellicate his skin as he walked outside.
She tried not to vellicate when the doctor tapped her knee during the examination.
A feather brushed against my arm, causing me to vellicate involuntarily.

Synset Examples

The sudden noise seemed to vellicate her senses.
The cat's scratch made me vellicate in surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twitch - to make a short, sudden jerking movement
  • Synonym: to stimulate - to spur or excite a response in something or someone
  • Antonym: calm - to make quiet or less excited

Etymology

From Latin vellicare (to pluck, twitch).
