Vladimir Ilyich Lenin

1. PROPN

/ ˈvlɑːdɪmɪr ɪˈlitʃ ˈlɛnɪn / (AmE), / ˈvlædɪmɪə ɪˈliːɪtʃ ˈlɛnɪn / (BrE)
(1870–1924) Russian revolutionary leader who founded the Bolshevik party and was the first head of the Soviet Union.
Russian revolutionary leader, first Soviet head

Examples

Vladimir Ilyich Lenin was instrumental in shaping the course of twentieth-century Russian history.
Many cities and landmarks in Russia were named after Vladimir Ilyich Lenin during the Soviet era.
Students studied the political theories developed by Vladimir Ilyich Lenin in their history class.
