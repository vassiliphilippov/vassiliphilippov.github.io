to fend

1. VERB

/ fɛnd / (AmE), / fɛnd / (BrE)
to look after and provide for oneself without help from others
manage without help

Examples

After losing his job, Mark had to fend for himself until he found new work.
The cubs are old enough to fend for themselves in the wild.
She learned how to fend on her own when she moved abroad for college.

Synset Examples

The youngsters had to fend for themselves after their parents died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provide - to make available what is needed; offer or supply
  • Antonym: to depend - to rely on help from others

Etymology

From Middle English fenden, from Old English fendian (to defend), of Germanic origin.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɛnd / (AmE), / fɛnd / (BrE)
to withstand or defend oneself against something, especially an attack or difficulty
withstand or defend against something

Examples

The old fortress managed to fend off multiple attacks from invaders.
You need to fend against criticism if you want to succeed as a politician.
The hikers had to fend for themselves during the severe storm.

Synset Examples

The trees resisted her
stand the test of time
The mountain climbers had to fend against the ice and snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something
  • Antonym: to succumb - to fail to resist pressure or an attack

Etymology

From Old English *fendan*, related to 'defend', via the Old French *defendre*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01118334-v
Synset POS: v
