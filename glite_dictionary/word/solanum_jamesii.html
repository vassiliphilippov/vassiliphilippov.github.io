Solanum jamesii

1. PROPN

/ soʊˈlænəm ˈdʒeɪmziː.aɪ / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ˈdʒeɪmziː.aɪ / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the southwestern United States and Mexico, with erect or spreading stems and small, pale brown to cream-colored tubers resembling potatoes.
species of perennial plant with potato-like tubers

Examples

Native peoples once cultivated Solanum jamesii for its edible tubers in the Southwest.
The Solanum jamesii plant thrives in dry, rocky soils of New Mexico and Arizona.
Several researchers study Solanum jamesii for its resilience to drought conditions.

Etymology

Genus from Latin 'Solanum' (nightshade); species named after botanist Edwin James.
