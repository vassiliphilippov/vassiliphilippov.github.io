Pteridium

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / təˈrɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Dennstaedtiaceae, commonly known as bracken ferns, distributed worldwide.
genus of bracken ferns

Examples

Pteridium is widely regarded as one of the most common genera of ferns worldwide.
Bracken, scientifically classified as Pteridium, often grows in open woodlands and fields.
Researchers found that Pteridium species can be invasive in certain ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracken - a large coarse fern, especially of the genus Pteridium

Etymology

From Greek pteridion, diminutive of pteris (fern).

2. NOUN

/ tɛˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / tɛˈrɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed ferns in the family Dennstaedtiaceae, including common bracken.
genus of ferns (bracken)

Examples

Pteridium is best known for the species bracken, found on every continent except Antarctica.
Some Pteridium species are considered invasive due to their hardy nature and rapid spread.
The study focused on the reproductive structures of Pteridium in temperate forests.

Etymology

From Greek pteridion, diminutive of pteris (fern).
