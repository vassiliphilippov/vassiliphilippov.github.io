Cronartium ribicola

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈnɑːrtiəm raɪˈbɪkələ / (AmE), / krəʊˈnɑːtɪəm rɪˈbɪkələ / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes white pine blister rust and requires plants of the genus Ribes as an alternate host
fungus causing white pine blister rust
medical

Examples

Cronartium ribicola has devastated many North American white pine forests over the last century.
Foresters monitor areas with abundant Ribes shrubs for signs of Cronartium ribicola infection.
The complex life cycle of Cronartium ribicola involves both white pine and currant plants as hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white pine blister rust - a disease of white pine trees caused by Cronartium ribicola

Etymology

From the genus name 'Cronartium' (Greek: Kronos, 'Cronus', a god) and species epithet 'ribicola' (Latin: 'Ribes' + 'cola', meaning 'dweller in Ribes').
