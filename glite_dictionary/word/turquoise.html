turquoise

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.kɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.kwɔɪz / (BrE)
a greenish-blue color, similar to that of the turquoise gemstone
a greenish-blue color

Examples

She wore a beautiful dress in turquoise that matched her eyes.
The waters of the lagoon shimmered a bright turquoise under the sun.
He chose turquoise for the walls to create a calming atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: teal - a dark greenish-blue color
  • Degree Higher: cyan - a greenish-blue color, lighter than turquoise

Etymology

From Old French 'turqueise', 'Turkish (stone)', referring to the stone imported from Turkey.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrˌk(w)oɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkwɔɪz / (BrE)
a blue to green mineral consisting of a hydrous phosphate of copper and aluminum, used as a gemstone
blue-green mineral used as a gemstone

Examples

The artist set a bright turquoise in the center of the silver ring.
Collectors prize high-quality turquoise for its vibrant color and rarity.
That necklace features polished beads of natural turquoise.

Synset Examples

blue turquoise is valued as a gemstone

Etymology

From Old French 'turqueise', meaning 'Turkish (stone)', named because it was originally imported from Turkey.
