offhanded

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔfˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhændɪd / (BrE)
showing a casual, careless, or inconsiderate attitude, sometimes without due thought or attention
casually thoughtless or inconsiderate

Examples

His offhanded remarks during the meeting upset several colleagues.
She gave an offhanded apology that seemed insincere.
The manager's offhanded manner left employees feeling unappreciated.

Synset Examples

an offhand manner
she treated most men with offhand contempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casual - done without much thought or effort
  • Antonym: considerate - thoughtful of the feelings of others

Etymology

From 'off hand', meaning without preparation or forethought, combined with the adjectival suffix '-ed'.
