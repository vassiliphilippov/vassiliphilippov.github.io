melamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛl.əˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.əˌmiːn / (BrE)
a white crystalline organic compound used especially in the manufacture of synthetic resins and plastics
an organic compound used to make resins and plastics
medical

Examples

The plates are made from durable melamine, making them ideal for outdoor use.
Researchers analyzed the composition of melamine found in contaminated milk powder.
Exposure to high levels of melamine can be harmful to human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urea - a colorless crystalline compound used in fertilizers and in chemical synthesis

Etymology

From German Melamin, from Melam (a chemical compound) + -in (chemical suffix).
