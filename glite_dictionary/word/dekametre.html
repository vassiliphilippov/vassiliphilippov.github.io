dekametre

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to ten metres
metric unit of length (10 metres)

Examples

The new sports field is exactly one dekametre wide.
A river's depth might sometimes be measured in dekametres instead of metres.
Each dekametre contains ten metres, making conversions straightforward in metric systems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: metre - the base unit of length in the metric system
  • Degree Higher: hectometre - a metric unit of length equal to one hundred metres

Etymology

From Greek 'deka' (ten) + 'metre'.
