Vombatidae

1. NOUN

/ vɑmˈbætɪdi / (AmE), / vɒmˈbætɪdi / (BrE)
A family of burrowing marsupials native to Australia, comprising the wombats.
family of marsupials including wombats

Examples

The Vombatidae family is endemic to Australia and includes all species of wombats.
Many zoologists specialize in studying the Vombatidae due to their unique burrowing behavior.
Fossil discoveries have shed new light on the evolutionary history of the Vombatidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wombat - Any of several burrowing Australian marsupials of the family Vombatidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Vombatidae, based on the genus Vombatus (wombat).

2. NOUN

/ vɒmˈbætəˌdiː / (AmE), / vɒmˈbætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marsupials comprising the wombats, native to Australia and Tasmania.
biological family of wombats

Examples

The Vombatidae family is distinguished by its members' sturdy bodies and burrowing habits.
All species within Vombatidae are native to Australia and Tasmania.
Scientists study Vombatidae to understand the evolutionary adaptations of marsupials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wombat - a burrowing Australian marsupial with a stout body

Etymology

From New Latin Vombatidae, based on the type genus Vombatus.
