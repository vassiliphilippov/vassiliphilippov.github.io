rubble

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.əl / (BrE)
broken pieces of stone, brick, or other materials left after a building or structure has been destroyed or damaged
broken pieces after destruction

Examples

The collapsed building left a huge pile of rubble in the street.
Teams searched through the rubble looking for survivors after the earthquake.
After the old house was demolished, all that remained was a mound of rubble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked or destroyed
  • Synonym: wreckage - the remains of something that has been badly damaged or destroyed

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *robil*, related to the Old French *rubble* or *roble* (broken stone).
