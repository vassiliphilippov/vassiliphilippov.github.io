Bosporus

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑspərəs / (AmE), / ˈbɒspərəs / (BrE)
A narrow strait in northwestern Turkey connecting the Mediterranean Sea with the Black Sea and dividing the European and Asian parts of Turkey.
strait between Europe and Asia in Turkey

Examples

Ships must pass through the Bosporus to travel from the Mediterranean to the Black Sea.
The city of Istanbul spans both sides of the Bosporus, linking Europe and Asia.
The Bosporus is one of the world's busiest waterways for international shipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dardanelles - the strait connecting the Aegean Sea to the Sea of Marmara in Turkey
  • Synonym: strait - a narrow passage of water connecting two seas or two other large areas of water

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Bosporos (Βόσπορος), meaning 'cattle passage' or 'ox ford'.
