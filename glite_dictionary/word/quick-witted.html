quick-witted

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪkˌwɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌkwɪkˈwɪtɪd / (BrE)
able to think and respond quickly and intelligently; mentally nimble and resourceful.
able to think and respond quickly

Examples

The quick-witted comedian had a snappy comeback for every heckler in the crowd.
Her quick-witted response prevented the meeting from turning awkward.
Police praised the quick-witted actions of a passerby who helped prevent an accident.

Synset Examples

quick-witted debater
saved an embarrassing situation with quick-witted tact

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow-witted - slow to understand or think; lacking mental agility
  • Synonym: sharp - mentally keen or quick-thinking

Etymology

Formed from 'quick' (meaning fast) and 'witted' (having wit), dating from the 17th century.
