anaesthetic

1. NOUN

/ ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug that causes temporary loss of bodily sensation, especially pain, used during surgical or medical procedures.
drug causing loss of sensation
medical british

Examples

The dentist gave me an anaesthetic before removing my wisdom tooth.
He was under general anaesthetic during the operation and felt nothing.
An injection of local anaesthetic will numb the area for the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - American spelling of 'anaesthetic'
  • Degree Lower: analgesic - drug that relieves pain without causing loss of sensation

Etymology

From Greek anaisthēsia (insensibility), from an- (without) + aisthēsis (sensation).

Examples

He stared at the wall with an anaesthetic detachment, oblivious to the chaos around him.

causing a loss of sensation or consciousness, especially to prevent pain during a medical procedure.
causing loss of sensation
medical british

Examples

The patient was given an anaesthetic injection to ensure she felt no pain.
The dentist explained that the anaesthetic might leave your mouth numb for a few hours.
