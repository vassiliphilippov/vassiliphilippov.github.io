Bryaceae

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / braɪˈeɪsi.iː / (BrE)
a family of acrocarpous mosses, including typical mosses often found on soil or rocks
a family of acrocarpous mosses

Examples

The Bryaceae family is widespread in temperate regions around the world.
Scientists discovered several new species of Bryaceae during the expedition.
Many gardeners encounter Bryaceae mosses growing on shaded stone walls.

Etymology

From the genus name Bryum, from Greek *bryon* (moss), plus the botanical family suffix -aceae.
