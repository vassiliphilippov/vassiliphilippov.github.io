Ophioglossaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiˌɑːˌɡlɒsəˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊfɪəˌɡlɒsəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns with fleshy, succulent fronds, found worldwide and classified in the order Ophioglossales
family of succulent ferns
medical

Examples

The Ophioglossaceae are known for their simple leaves and distinctive reproductive structures.
Many Ophioglossaceae species can be found in damp forests across different continents.
Scientists study Ophioglossaceae to better understand the evolution of vascular plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with fronds and spores

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to the shape of some species' fronds.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfi.oʊˌɡlɒsəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌəʊfi.əˌɡlɒsəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of succulent ferns, widely distributed worldwide, belonging to the order Ophioglossales
family of succulent ferns

Examples

Members of the Ophioglossaceae family are often found in moist, shaded habitats.
The Ophioglossaceae include ferns such as adder's-tongue and moonwort.
Botanists study the Ophioglossaceae to understand the evolution of primitive ferns.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophio-' (snake) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the snake-tongue-like appearance of some species.
