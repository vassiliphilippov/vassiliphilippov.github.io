Gesneriaceae

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛsnəˌriˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / dʒɛsnəˌrɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of tropical herbs, shrubs, or lianas found mainly in the tropics, including African violets and streptocarpus.
family of tropical herbs and shrubs

Examples

Many popular houseplants, like African violets, belong to the Gesneriaceae family.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Gesneriaceae in the rainforests of Borneo.
The Gesneriaceae are known for their diverse and vibrant flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Michel Gesner, Swiss naturalist, plus the botanical suffix '-aceae' (family).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɛsˌnɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / dʒɛsˌnɛəriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of tropical herbs, shrubs, or lianas, including African violets, typically found in warm regions.
family of tropical herbs and shrubs
medical

Examples

The Gesneriaceae family includes the popular houseplant known as the African violet.
Many Gesneriaceae species are prized for their vibrant flowers and ease of cultivation.
The unique floral structures of Gesneriaceae members are often studied by botanists.

Etymology

From the genus name Gesneria, named after Swiss naturalist Conrad Gessner.
