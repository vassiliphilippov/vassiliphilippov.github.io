inexplicitness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ɪkˈsplɪs.ətnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪkˈsplɪs.ɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being not explicit; lack of clarity or preciseness
lack of explicitness or clarity

Examples

The inexplicitness of his instructions led to confusion among the team members.
Legal documents should avoid inexplicitness to prevent misunderstandings.
The report was criticized for its inexplicitness regarding the main findings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicitness - the quality of being explicit or clearly expressed
  • Synonym: ambiguity - uncertainty or inexactness of meaning

Etymology

From 'inexplicit' (not explicit) + '-ness'.
