Tragopogon

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræɡəˈpoʊɡən / (AmE), / ˌtræɡəˈpɒɡən / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs with linear, entire leaves and yellow or purple flower heads, commonly known as salsify or goatsbeard.
genus of herbs; salsify, goatsbeard

Examples

Tragopogon is easily recognized by its long, grass-like leaves and striking flower heads.
Many species within the Tragopogon genus are cultivated for their edible roots.
The purple blooms of Tragopogon add vibrant color to meadows in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsify - edible root vegetable from plants of the genus Tragopogon
  • Synonym: goatsbeard - common name for species in the genus Tragopogon

Etymology

From Greek tragos (goat) + pogon (beard), referencing the plant's bristly seed head.

2. NOUN

/ træˈɡɑpəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / træˈɡɒpəɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs with narrow, entire leaves and yellow or purple flower heads, commonly known as salsify.
a genus of herbs with yellow or purple flowers

Examples

Many Tragopogon species are cultivated for their edible roots and shoots.
The field was filled with blooming Tragopogon plants, their purple flowers swaying in the breeze.
Scientists studied the hybridization patterns within the Tragopogon genus in Eastern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek *tragos* (goat) + *pogon* (beard), referring to the bristly pappus of the seed head.
