hard-core

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrd ˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌkɔː / (BrE)
having an unyielding or uncompromising attitude; stubbornly resistant to change or improvement, especially in negative behaviours or beliefs.
stubbornly resistant to change

Examples

The clinic specializes in treating hard-core addicts who have relapsed many times.
Only a hard-core group of protesters remained after the rain started.
He is a hard-core skeptic, never persuaded by new evidence.

Synset Examples

hard-core addicts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die-hard - someone who strongly resists change; a persistent person

Etymology

From 'hard' + 'core', originally referring to the most dedicated members of a group.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌkɔː / (BrE)
having a strong and unwavering commitment; intensely loyal or dedicated, especially as a supporter or member of a group or cause.
intensely loyal or committed

Examples

She has been a hard-core fan of the band since their very first concert.
Only the most hard-core supporters attended the rally in the pouring rain.
He is a hard-core member of the club, never missing a single meeting.

Synset Examples

his hard-core supporters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diehard - someone or something stubbornly resistant or intensely loyal

Etymology

Originally from slang use in the early 20th century, referring to the 'central or most active part' of a group, based on 'hard core' as the unyielding center of something.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00965214-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈhɑrd ˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌkɔː / (BrE)
(of entertainment, especially pornography) extremely explicit, leaving nothing to the imagination.
extremely explicit (esp. in entertainment)

Examples

The film was criticized for its hard-core scenes that left nothing to the imagination.
Some websites restrict access to hard-core material for legal reasons.
He argued that hard-core images should be clearly labeled to warn viewers.

hard-core pornography
