zenithal

1. ADJ

/ ˈzɛnəθəl / (AmE), / ˈziːnɪθəl / (BrE)
relating to or located at or near the zenith, especially the point in the sky directly above an observer
relating to the zenith; directly overhead

Examples

The photographer adjusted the camera to capture the zenithal sunlight streaming down at noon.
At the equator, the sun appears almost zenithal during the equinox.
The satellite's sensors measured the zenithal angle to determine its precise location.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zenith - the point in the sky directly above an observer

Etymology

From 'zenith' (from Arabic samt ar-ra's, 'path over the head') + '-al' (forming adjectives).
