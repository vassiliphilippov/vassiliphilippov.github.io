riposte

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpoʊst / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒst / (BrE)
A quick and clever reply to a remark or criticism.
quick, witty reply

Examples

Her clever riposte left everyone in the room laughing.
When challenged on the subject, he fired back a riposte that ended the debate.
The comedian is known for his quick riposte to hecklers in the audience.

Synset Examples

it brought a sharp rejoinder from the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejoinder - a sharp or witty reply
  • Synonym: retort - a quick, incisive reply to a remark

Etymology

From French 'riposte', from Italian 'risposta' (answer), past participle of 'rispondere' (to respond).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpoʊst / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒst / (BrE)
(in fencing) a quick counterattack made immediately after parrying an opponent's lunge.
fencing counterattack after a parry

Examples

She executed a flawless riposte after blocking his attack.
The fencer's quick riposte caught her opponent off guard.
After a perfect parry, he launched a swift riposte to score the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parry - to ward off or deflect an attack, especially in fencing

Etymology

From French 'riposte', from Italian 'risposta' (answer), from 'rispondere' (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01177315-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rɪˈpoʊst / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒst / (BrE)
to make a quick, sharp return in speech or action, originally meaning to respond immediately with a counterattack in fencing
make a quick counterattack or reply

Examples

She was quick to riposte when challenged about her decision.
During the fencing match, he expertly riposted after each attack.
He riposted with a clever remark that left the audience laughing.

Synset Examples

his opponent riposted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retort - to reply in a sharp or witty manner

Etymology

From French 'riposte', from Italian 'risposta' (answer, reply), past participle of 'rispondere' (to answer), from Latin 'respondēre'.

4. VERB

/ rɪˈpoʊst / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒst / (BrE)
to reply quickly and sharply, often as a clever or witty response to a criticism or comment
reply quickly and sharply

Examples

When challenged on her proposal, she riposted with a series of persuasive arguments.
He riposted immediately to the criticism, leaving his opponent speechless.
She ripostes with a clever joke whenever someone makes a sarcastic comment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retort - to reply in a sharp or witty manner
  • Synonym: to respond - to answer or reply to something

Etymology

From French 'riposte' (a quick reply), originally from Italian 'risposta' (answer)
