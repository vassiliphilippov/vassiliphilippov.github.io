isoantibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈæntɪˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈantɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an antibody produced by an individual that reacts against tissue or blood cells from another individual of the same species
antibody against tissue from same species
medical

Examples

The presence of an isoantibody can cause transfusion reactions if blood types are not matched carefully.
Doctors tested for isoantibodies before the organ transplant to avoid rejection.
Detection of an isoantibody is important in managing hemolytic disease of the newborn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloantibody - an antibody produced against antigens from the same species but with genetic differences

Etymology

From iso- (Greek isos, meaning 'equal' or 'same') + antibody.
