Agapanthus africanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡəˈpænθəs æˈfrɪkənəs / (AmE), / ˌæɡəˈpænθəs æˈfrɪkənəs / (BrE)
A species of African plant with bright green evergreen leaves and umbels of many usually deep violet-blue flowers.
African plant with violet-blue flowers

Examples

The Agapanthus africanus blooms beautifully in midsummer, adding vibrant color to the garden.
Gardeners recommend planting Agapanthus africanus in well-drained soil and full sun.
Many landscapers use Agapanthus africanus for its striking violet-blue flowers and easy maintenance.

Etymology

From Greek 'agapē' (love) and 'anthos' (flower); 'africanus' refers to Africa, its native region.
