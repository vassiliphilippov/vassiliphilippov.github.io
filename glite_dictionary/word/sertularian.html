sertularian

1. NOUN

/ sɜr.tjʊˈlɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / sɜː.tjʊˈlɛə.ri.ən / (BrE)
A colonial marine hydroid of the family Sertulariidae, forming feathery, branching stems that bear pairs of stalkless polyps.
colonial marine hydroid (hydrozoan)

Examples

The diver discovered a beautiful sertularian colony clinging to the underside of the rock.
Scientists studied the branching structure of the sertularian under the microscope.
The aquarium exhibit features several species of sertularian hydroids on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroid - a colonial or solitary organism of the class Hydrozoa

Etymology

From New Latin Sertularia (the genus), based on Latin 'sertula' (a little garland).
