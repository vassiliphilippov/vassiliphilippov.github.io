Silene vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈliːni vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to Arctic Europe, known for its large white flowers with an inflated calyx; commonly known as bladder campion.
a European perennial with white, bladder-shaped flowers

Examples

The Silene vulgaris thrives in well-drained soils across much of Europe.
Gardeners admire Silene vulgaris for its unique, balloon-like calyx.
While hiking in Scandinavia, we spotted clusters of Silene vulgaris along the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladder campion - common name for Silene vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Silene', species name 'vulgaris' meaning 'common'.
