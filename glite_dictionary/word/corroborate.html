to corroborate

1. VERB

/ kəˈrɑːbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈrɒbəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to provide evidence or information that supports a statement, theory, or finding
support with evidence

Examples

The witness was able to corroborate the defendant's alibi with new evidence.
Several studies corroborate the theory that regular exercise benefits mental health.
Her story was corroborated by photographs taken at the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confirm - to show or prove to be true or certain
  • Antonym: to contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement

Etymology

From Latin corroborare (to strengthen), from com- (together) + roborare (to strengthen).

3. VERB

/ kəˈrɑːbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈrɒbəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to provide evidence or information that supports a statement, theory, or finding; to confirm or strengthen with additional proof
to confirm or support with evidence

Examples

Several witnesses corroborated the suspect's alibi during the trial.
The findings were corroborated by independent researchers in a separate study.
Her account was corroborated by video evidence from the security cameras.

Synset Examples

his story confirmed my doubts
The evidence supports the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something
  • Antonym: to refute - to prove that something is wrong or false

Etymology

From Latin corroborare (to strengthen), from cor- (together) + roborare (to make strong).
