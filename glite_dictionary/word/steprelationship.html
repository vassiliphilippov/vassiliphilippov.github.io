steprelationship

1. NOUN

/ stɛpˌrɛˈleɪʃənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / stɛpˌrɪˈleɪʃənʃɪp / (BrE)
a family relationship created as a result of remarriage, such as between a stepparent and stepchild, rather than by blood or adoption
family link by remarriage

Examples

A good steprelationship can provide children with additional support and guidance.
Navigating the dynamics of a new steprelationship can be challenging for both parents and children.
Their steprelationship gradually grew stronger as they spent more time together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stepfamily - a family in which at least one parent has children from a previous relationship

Etymology

Formed by combining 'step-' (from Old English 'steop-', denoting relationship by remarriage) and 'relationship'.
