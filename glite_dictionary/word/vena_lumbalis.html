vena lumbalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə lʌmˈbeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌveɪnə lʌmˈbɑːlɪs / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain the posterior body wall and lumbar vertebral venous plexuses
lumbar vein in the lower back
medical

Examples

The vena lumbalis helps drain blood from the muscles of the lower back into the larger veins.
Damage to the vena lumbalis can lead to complications during spinal surgery.
The anatomy class focused on identifying the vena lumbalis and its connections to the venous plexus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumbar vein - a vein in the lumbar region draining the posterior body wall

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'lumbalis' (of the loins/lumbar region).
