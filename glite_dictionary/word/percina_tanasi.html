Percina tanasi

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈsaɪnə təˈneɪsi / (AmE), / pəˈsiːnə təˈnɑːsi / (BrE)
A small species of freshwater perch, known as the snail darter, found in the Tennessee River and notable for its snail-based diet.
a small freshwater fish of the Tennessee River

Examples

The endangered Percina tanasi became famous due to its impact on the construction of the Tellico Dam.
Researchers study Percina tanasi to monitor the health of river ecosystems in the southeastern United States.
Efforts to protect the habitat of Percina tanasi have raised awareness about freshwater biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snail darter - common name for Percina tanasi, a small freshwater fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin; Percina is a genus of perches, tanasi refers to the Tennessee River.
