bitters

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtɚz / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəz / (BrE)
a strong alcoholic liquor flavored with bitter herbs and roots, commonly used in cocktails or as a digestive aid
alcoholic liquor with bitter herbs

Examples

The bartender added a dash of bitters to the cocktail for extra flavor.
Traditional recipes often call for a few drops of bitters to enhance the taste.
Some people drink bitters after a meal to aid digestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aperitif - an alcoholic drink taken before a meal to stimulate the appetite

Etymology

From the word 'bitter' referring to the taste, with the plural -s indicating the beverage.
