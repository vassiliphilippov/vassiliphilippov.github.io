uncorrupted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (BrE)
Not influenced by corruption; not debased, pure or morally upright.
not corrupted or debased

Examples

Despite constant temptation, she remained uncorrupted throughout her long career.
The original manuscript has survived in an uncorrupted state for centuries.
They were praised for their uncorrupted sense of justice and integrity.

Synset Examples

though his associates were dishonest, he remained uncorrupted
uncorrupted values

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corrupt - dishonest or morally debased
  • Synonym: pure - not mixed or adulterated, morally clean

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not') + corrupted, from Latin 'corruptus' (spoiled, ruined).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (BrE)
not having decayed or decomposed; remaining in an original, unspoiled state
not decayed or decomposed

Examples

The ancient manuscript was found uncorrupted after centuries buried underground.
Despite years in the harsh environment, the metal statue remained uncorrupted.
Her faith in justice was uncorrupted by cynicism or disappointment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corrupted - decayed, decomposed, or morally tainted
  • Synonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled

Etymology

From un- (not) + corrupted, from Latin corruptus (spoiled, decayed).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈrʌp.tɪd / (BrE)
(of language) not debased in purity or excellence; remaining in its original or unaltered state.
not debased or altered (of language)

Examples

Scholars value texts that remain uncorrupted over centuries of copying.
He prefers to teach uncorrupted English to his students.
Many believe the original poems have not survived in their completely uncorrupted form.

Synset Examples

uncorrupted English
learn to speak pure English undefiled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pure - not mixed with anything else; free from contamination
  • Antonym: corrupted - debased or altered from its original form or purity
