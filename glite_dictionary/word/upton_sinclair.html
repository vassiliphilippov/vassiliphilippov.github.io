Upton Sinclair

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌptən ˈsɪŋklɛr / (AmE), / ˈʌptən ˈsɪŋklɪə / (BrE)
(1878–1968) An American writer and social reformer known for his novels exposing social injustice, especially 'The Jungle'.
American writer and social reformer

Examples

Upton Sinclair's novel 'The Jungle' led to major reforms in the American meatpacking industry.
Many people admire Upton Sinclair for his relentless pursuit of social justice.
As a prominent muckraker, Upton Sinclair influenced public opinion in the early twentieth century.
