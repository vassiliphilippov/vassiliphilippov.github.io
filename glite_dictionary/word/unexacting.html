unexacting

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪɡˈzæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪɡˈzæktɪŋ / (BrE)
not demanding; not requiring great effort, precision, or rigor.
not demanding or rigorous

Examples

She preferred jobs that were unexacting and stress-free.
The hike was unexacting, suitable even for beginners.
Our coach sets unexacting standards for the recreational team.

Synset Examples

relaxed and unexacting standards

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exacting - demanding a lot of effort, precision, or rigor

Etymology

From un- (not) + exacting (demanding effort or attention).
