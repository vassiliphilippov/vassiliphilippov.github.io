Nicaea

1. PROPN

/ naɪˈsiːə / (AmE), / nɪˈsiːə / (BrE)
an ancient city in northwestern Asia Minor, notable for the First Council of Nicaea in 325 AD where the Nicene Creed was established
ancient city in Asia Minor; site of First Council of Nicaea

Examples

The city of Nicaea played a pivotal role in early Christian history.
The First Council of Nicaea was convened by Emperor Constantine in 325 AD.
Many theological debates were resolved at Nicaea, shaping the doctrine of the early church.

Etymology

From Greek Νίκαια (Nikaia), named after the Greek goddess Nike, meaning 'victory'.

2. PROPN

/ naɪˈsiːə / (AmE), / naɪˈsiːə / (BrE)
the seventh ecumenical council held in 787 AD, which condemned iconoclasm and established regulations for the veneration of holy images in the Christian church
the seventh ecumenical council (787 AD)

Examples

The decisions made at Nicaea had a profound impact on the Christian doctrine regarding images.
Historians often study the acts of Nicaea to understand the church's stance on iconoclasm.
The Second Council of Nicaea is considered one of the most significant events in church history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Council of Nicaea - the seventh ecumenical council (787 AD)

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Νίκαια (Nikaia), the name of the city where the council was held.

3. PROPN

/ naɪˈsiː.ə / (AmE), / naɪˈsiː.ə / (BrE)
the first ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 325 AD in Nicaea, which formulated the Nicene Creed and condemned Arianism as heresy
first ecumenical Christian council (325 AD)

Examples

The Council of Nicaea marked a turning point in the development of Christian doctrine.
Debates at Nicaea led to the creation of the Nicene Creed.
Many historical discussions refer to the decisions made at Nicaea in 325 AD.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicene Creed - a statement of Christian faith formalized by the Council of Nicaea
  • Antonym: Arianism - a Christian heresy condemned at the Council of Nicaea

Etymology

From the name of the ancient city Nicaea (Greek Nikaia) in Asia Minor, where the council took place.
