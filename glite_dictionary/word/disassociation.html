disassociation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A psychological state in which a part of a person's experience becomes separated from the rest of their personality and functions independently.
psychological separation of mental processes

Examples

After the traumatic event, he experienced disassociation and felt detached from his surroundings.
Therapists often help patients understand the triggers for their disassociation.
Periods of disassociation can make it difficult to remember important personal information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissociation - process of separating or disconnecting from something, especially in psychology

Etymology

Formed from dis- (expressing reversal) and association.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being separated or disconnected in memory, imagination, or experience
state of being disconnected or separated

Examples

He spoke about his childhood with a sense of disassociation from his family's struggles.
The disassociation between the two events made it difficult to draw any connection.
She experienced a feeling of disassociation after the traumatic incident.

Synset Examples

I could not think of him in disassociation from his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissociation - the disconnection or separation of something from something else or the state of being disconnected

Etymology

Formed from dis- (apart) + association; influenced by 'dissociation.'
