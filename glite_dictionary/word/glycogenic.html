glycogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡlaɪ.kəˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡlʌɪ.kəˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving glycogen, especially in the formation or storage of glycogen in tissues.
relating to glycogen
medical

Examples

Insulin promotes glycogenic activity in liver cells.
A glycogenic diet can help athletes restore muscle energy.
Certain enzymes play a key role in glycogenic pathways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycogen - a polysaccharide stored in animals as a form of energy

Etymology

From Greek glykys (sweet) + -gen (producing) + -ic, relating to glycogen.
