Nellie Bly

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛli blaɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɛli blaɪ / (BrE)
(1867–1922) An American journalist known for her investigative reporting and for exposing abuses in mental institutions.
American investigative journalist (1867–1922)

Examples

Nellie Bly became famous for her daring undercover investigations and exposés.
The pioneering work of Nellie Bly helped improve conditions for mental health patients.
Many journalists cite Nellie Bly as an inspiration for investigative reporting.

Etymology

Born Elizabeth Jane Cochran; 'Nellie Bly' adopted from the Stephen Foster song.
