incontrovertibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɑːntrəˌvɝːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒntrəˌvɜːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being undeniable, indisputable, or not open to question.
quality of being undeniable or indisputable

Examples

The incontrovertibility of the evidence left little room for doubt in the jury's mind.
Historians debated the fact, but soon its incontrovertibility was accepted by all.
He relied on the incontrovertibility of scientific laws to make his argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incontrovertible - impossible to deny or dispute
  • Antonym: disputability - the quality of being open to question or debate

Etymology

Formed from 'incontrovertible' (from Latin 'in-' not + 'controvertibilis' disputable) plus the suffix '-ity.'
