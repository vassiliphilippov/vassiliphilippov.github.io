auspex

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔspɛks / (AmE), / ˈɔːspɛks / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) a religious official who interpreted omens, especially from the behavior of birds, to guide public decisions or policy.
Roman omen-interpreting priest

Examples

The Roman senate consulted an auspex before making important decisions.
As an auspex, he carefully observed the flight patterns of birds.
The role of the auspex was crucial in ancient ceremonial events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: augur - a religious official in ancient Rome who interpreted omens

Etymology

From Latin auspex, auspicis (observer of birds).
