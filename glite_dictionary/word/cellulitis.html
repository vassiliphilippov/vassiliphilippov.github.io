cellulitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛl.jəˈlaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛl.jʊˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A bacterial infection causing inflammation of the skin and underlying tissues, often accompanied by redness, swelling, warmth, and pain.
skin infection causing swelling and redness
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her leg pain as cellulitis and prescribed antibiotics.
If left untreated, cellulitis can spread to deeper tissues and become dangerous.
Red, swollen skin with a fever may be a sign of cellulitis.

Etymology

From Latin 'cellula' (small cell) + '-itis' (inflammation).
