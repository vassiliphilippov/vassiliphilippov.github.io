Takakkaw Falls

1. PROPN

/ təˈkɑːkəw fɔlz / (AmE), / təˈkækəw fɔːlz / (BrE)
a waterfall in southeastern British Columbia, Canada, recognized as the highest waterfall in the country at 1,250 feet (381 meters) tall
the highest waterfall in Canada

Examples

Many tourists visit Takakkaw Falls each summer to witness its spectacular height and beauty.
The mist from Takakkaw Falls can be felt from the nearby walking trails.
Photographers often choose Takakkaw Falls as a subject for stunning landscape shots.

Etymology

From Cree *takâkaw*, meaning 'it is magnificent'.
