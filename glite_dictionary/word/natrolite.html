natrolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæt.rəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈnatrəlʌɪt / (BrE)
a white or colorless mineral of the zeolite group consisting of a hydrous silicate of sodium and aluminum
a mineral of the zeolite group

Examples

The museum displays a large cluster of natrolite crystals obtained from India.
Geologists discovered veins of natrolite while surveying the volcanic rock formations.
A specimen of natrolite was used to demonstrate the properties of zeolite minerals in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeolite - a group of minerals having a microporous structure, often used as molecular sieves

Etymology

From German 'Natrolith', from Natron (sodium carbonate) + -lith (stone).
