ribaldry

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.əld.ri / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.əld.ri / (BrE)
rude or irreverent talk or behavior that is amusing or intended to be amusing, especially about sexual matters.
rude or irreverent humor

Examples

His stories were full of ribaldry that shocked some listeners.
The comedian's act was criticized for relying too heavily on ribaldry.
Medieval festivals were often occasions for much laughter and ribaldry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bawdiness - indecent or lewd humor or talk
  • Antonym: innocence - lack of vulgar or offensive qualities, especially in speech or humor

Etymology

From Old French *ribaldrie*, related to *ribaut* (wanton person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbəldri / (AmE), / ˈrɪbəldri / (BrE)
coarse, vulgar, or indecent language or behavior, especially that intended to be humorous
vulgar or indecent humor or behavior

Examples

His stand-up comedy routine was full of ribaldry that some found offensive.
The novel's dialogue contained a surprising amount of ribaldry for its time period.
Despite the ribaldry at the party, everyone seemed to be having a good time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innuendo - an indirect or subtle, usually derogatory implication in expression
  • Synonym: profanity - offensive or obscene language

Etymology

From Old French 'ribaldrie' (ribald talk or conduct).
