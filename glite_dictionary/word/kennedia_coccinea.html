Kennedia coccinea

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnidiə kɑkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / kɛˌnɪdiə kɒkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A woody vine native to Australia and Tasmania, with small leathery leaves and clusters of red flowers.
Australian vine with red flowers

Examples

The Kennedia coccinea is prized in Australian gardens for its striking red flowers and attractive foliage.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Kennedia coccinea along the coastal dunes of Western Australia.
She planted Kennedia coccinea to add vibrant color and texture to her native plant collection.

Etymology

Genus name 'Kennedia' commemorates John Kennedy, an English nurseryman; 'coccinea' from Latin meaning 'scarlet'.
