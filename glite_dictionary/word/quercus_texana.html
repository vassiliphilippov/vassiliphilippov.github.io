Quercus texana

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs tɛkˈsænə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs tɛkˈsænə / (BrE)
A small deciduous oak tree native to Texas and southern Oklahoma, with a trunk that branches near the base and spreading branches; also known as Nuttall oak.
species of oak tree native to Texas and Oklahoma

Examples

The Quercus texana provides valuable habitat for wildlife in southern Oklahoma forests.
We planted a Quercus texana in our yard to enjoy its beautiful autumn foliage.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Quercus texana across Texas woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nuttall oak - common name for Quercus texana

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning oak, and 'texana' referring to Texas, where the species is native.
