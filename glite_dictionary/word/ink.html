ink

/ ɪŋk / (AmE), / ɪŋk / (BrE)
to mark, cover, or stain (something) with ink

Examples

She inked the drawing carefully with a fine pen.
He accidentally inked his shirt pocket during the meeting.

Synset Examples

2. NOUN

/ ɪŋk / (AmE), / ɪŋk / (BrE)
a dark fluid ejected by cephalopods such as cuttlefish and octopuses to obscure predators or escape danger
dark fluid ejected by cephalopods

Examples

The octopus released a cloud of ink to escape from the predator.
Some species of squid shoot ink to distract their enemies in the water.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of cuttlefish ink for potential medical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sepia - a brownish-grey fluid ejected by cuttlefish, also used as a pigment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05411941-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪŋk / (AmE), / ɪŋk / (BrE)
a colored liquid used for writing, printing, or drawing
colored fluid for writing, printing, or drawing

Examples

He spilled a bottle of ink on the carpet while refilling his pen.
The artist prefers to use black ink for her detailed sketches.
Most printers require a special type of ink for high-quality color images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paint - a colored substance spread over a surface to leave a thin decorative or protective coating

Etymology

From Latin 'encaustum' (purple or red ink, esp. for official documents), from Greek 'enkauston' (burnt in), from 'enkaiein' (to burn in).

5. VERB

/ ɪŋk / (AmE), / ɪŋk / (BrE)
to sign a contract or agreement formally, especially by adding one's signature
sign a contract or agreement

Examples

After months of negotiation, the two companies finally inked the merger agreement.
The athlete inked a multi-year endorsement deal with a major sports brand.
Once both parties ink the document, the contract becomes legally binding.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - to write one's signature on (something) to indicate approval or agreement

Etymology

Based on the noun 'ink'; figurative extension meaning to sign using ink.
