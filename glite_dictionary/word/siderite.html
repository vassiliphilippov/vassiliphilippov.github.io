siderite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.dəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪd.əˌraɪt / (BrE)
a brownish mineral composed of iron(II) carbonate (FeCO₃), used as an iron ore
a brown iron carbonate mineral

Examples

The geologist identified the brown mineral as siderite during the survey.
Early mines in the region primarily extracted siderite for use in steel production.
Siderite often occurs with other iron minerals such as hematite and magnetite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron ore - a mineral from which iron is extracted

Etymology

From Greek sideros (iron) + -ite (mineral suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪdəˌraɪt / (BrE)
A meteorite composed chiefly of nickel and iron.
iron-nickel meteorite

Examples

The museum displayed a massive siderite that had been recovered from a desert.
Scientists analyzed the siderite for traces of rare elements found in meteorites.
A siderite is distinguished from stony meteorites by its high metal content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron meteorite - a meteorite made mainly of metallic iron or iron-nickel alloy

Etymology

From Greek 'sideros' (iron), via French 'sidérite'.
