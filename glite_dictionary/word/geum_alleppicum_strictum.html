Geum alleppicum strictum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːəm æˌlɛpɪkəm ˈstrɪktəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəm æˌlɛpɪkəm ˈstrɪktəm / (BrE)
A subspecies of yellow avens, an erect perennial subshrub with deep yellow flowers, native to Europe, Asia, and North America.
a subspecies of yellow avens plant

Examples

The garden features several Geum alleppicum strictum plants, whose bright yellow flowers attract pollinators.
Researchers documented the spread of Geum alleppicum strictum across northern woodlands.
Botanists collected specimens of Geum alleppicum strictum during their survey of the wetland area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Geum alleppicum - yellow avens, the broader species to which this subspecies belongs

Etymology

From Latin: Geum (the genus), alleppicum (of Aleppo region), strictum (straight, upright).
