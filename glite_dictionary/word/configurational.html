configurational

1. ADJ

/ kənˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the arrangement or structure of parts; characterized by or involving configuration
relating to configuration or arrangement

Examples

The architect discussed the configurational aspects of the new building's design.
Differences in configurational order can greatly affect the properties of these molecules.
The study explored the configurational changes in the crystal structure during the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: configurative - relating to forming or arranging into a configuration

Etymology

From 'configuration' + '-al', with root from Latin 'configurare' (to shape, form).
