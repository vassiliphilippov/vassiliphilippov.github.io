Milton Friedman

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪltən ˈfriːdmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪltən ˈfriːdmən / (BrE)
(1912–2006) A prominent American economist known for his advocacy of monetarism and opposition to government intervention in the economy.
American economist, monetarism advocate

Examples

Milton Friedman won the Nobel Prize in Economics for his work on consumption analysis and monetary history.
The policies advocated by Milton Friedman continue to influence economic debates around the world.
Many universities offer courses dedicated to the theories of Milton Friedman and his contributions to monetarism.
