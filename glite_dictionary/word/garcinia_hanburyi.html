Garcinia hanburyi

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑrˈsɪniə hænˈbɜri.aɪ / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈsɪniə hanˈbʊəri.aɪ / (BrE)
A low, spreading tree native to Indonesia that produces an orange to brown gum resin (gamboge), used as a yellow pigment when powdered.
tree yielding gamboge resin, used as pigment

Examples

The resin collected from Garcinia hanburyi is widely used to make the yellow pigment gamboge.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Garcinia hanburyi in its native Indonesian forests.
Traditional artists have valued the powder from Garcinia hanburyi as a natural source of vivid yellow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamboge - a yellow pigment obtained from the resin of Garcinia hanburyi

Etymology

Genus name Garcinia from botanist L. Garcin; species named after Daniel Hanbury, English botanist.
