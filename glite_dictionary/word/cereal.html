cereal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
a food made from processed grains, usually eaten with milk for breakfast
breakfast food made from grains

Examples

Every morning, she pours a bowl of cereal and adds fresh strawberries.
Some children prefer sweetened cereal over toast for breakfast.
The supermarket aisle is filled with different brands of cereal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porridge - hot breakfast dish made by boiling oats or other grains

Etymology

From Latin 'cerealis', from 'Ceres', the Roman goddess of agriculture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
a grass cultivated for its edible starchy grains, such as wheat, rice, or oats, which are used as food
grass grown for edible grains (e.g., wheat, rice)

Examples

Wheat and rice are two of the most important cereal crops grown worldwide.
Maize is a staple cereal in many parts of Africa and the Americas.
The museum displayed ancient tools used for harvesting cereal grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain - the seeds of cereal grasses used as food

Etymology

From Latin 'cerealis', from Ceres, Roman goddess of agriculture.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
A food made from the grains of cereal grasses, such as wheat, corn, oats, or rice, often eaten as breakfast food.
grain-based food, often for breakfast

Examples

He likes to start his day with a bowl of cereal and milk.
Many cereal brands are fortified with vitamins and minerals.
Children often prefer sweetened cereal for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain - the seeds of cereal grasses
  • Degree Lower: porridge - a soft food made by boiling grains in milk or water

Etymology

From Latin 'cerealis', from 'Ceres', Roman goddess of agriculture and grain.
