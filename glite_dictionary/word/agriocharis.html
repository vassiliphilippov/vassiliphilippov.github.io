Agriocharis

1. PROPN

/ ˌæɡri.oʊˈkɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɡriəʊˈkarɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the turkey family (Meleagrididae), including the ocellated turkey.
a bird genus including the ocellated turkey

Examples

The Agriocharis genus is known for its only living species, the ocellated turkey.
Researchers conducted a genetic study on birds within the Agriocharis genus.
Unlike the common turkey, Agriocharis is native to Central America.

Etymology

From Greek 'agrios' (wild) + 'charis' (grace/beauty), used in zoological taxonomy.
