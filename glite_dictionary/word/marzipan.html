marzipan

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrziˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːzɪpan / (BrE)
A sweet confection made from ground almonds, sugar, and egg whites, often molded into shapes or used as a cake covering.
a sweet almond paste confection

Examples

She decorated the cake with colorful marzipan fruits and flowers.
I love the rich, sweet taste of marzipan in holiday desserts.
The bakery sells handmade marzipan shaped like tiny animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond paste - a paste made from ground almonds used in baking and confections

Etymology

From German 'Marzipan', ultimately from Italian 'marzapane', of uncertain origin.
