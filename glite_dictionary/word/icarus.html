Icarus

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪkərəs / (AmE), / ˈɪkərəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the son of Daedalus who flew too close to the sun with wings made of feathers and wax, causing the wax to melt and him to fall into the sea and drown.
son of Daedalus in Greek myth; flew too close to the sun

Examples

The cautionary tale of Icarus is often cited as a warning against overambition.
Artists have depicted Icarus falling from the sky in countless paintings and sculptures.
He compared his reckless risk to the actions of Icarus in Greek mythology.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἴκαρος (Íkaros), the name of the mythological character.
