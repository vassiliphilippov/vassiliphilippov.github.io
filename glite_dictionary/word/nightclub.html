nightclub

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌklʌb / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌklʌb / (BrE)
a place open late at night offering entertainment, such as music or dancing, along with food and drink
a late-night entertainment venue

Examples

They went to a nightclub downtown to dance and listen to live music.
Many celebrities are often seen at the trendiest nightclubs in the city.
He started his career as a DJ at a popular nightclub.

Synset Examples

don't expect a good meal at a cabaret
the gossip columnist got his information by visiting nightclubs every night
he played the drums at a jazz club

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabaret - a restaurant or nightclub where entertainment is performed while patrons eat or drink
  • Synonym: bar - a place where alcoholic drinks are served

Etymology

Formed from 'night' + 'club'; in use since the early 20th century to describe a club open during night hours.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌklʌb / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌklʌb / (BrE)
A place open late at night that provides music, entertainment, dancing, and serves food and drink.
entertainment venue open late at night

Examples

He met his friends at the nightclub for an evening of music and dancing.
The city is famous for its vibrant nightclub scene and late-night parties.
She performed as a singer at a popular nightclub downtown.

Synset Examples

don't expect a good meal at a cabaret
the gossip columnist got his information by visiting nightclubs every night
he played the drums at a jazz club

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabaret - a restaurant or nightclub offering entertainment while customers eat or drink

Etymology

From 'night' + 'club', first used in the early 20th century to describe venues operating at night.
