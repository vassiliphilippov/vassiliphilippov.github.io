Ligustrum amurense

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡʌstrəm ˌæmjəˈrɛns / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm ˌamjʊˈrɛnsɪ / (BrE)
an eastern Asian shrub cultivated for its persistent foliage and often used in hedging; also known as Amur privet
eastern Asian shrub; Amur privet

Examples

The dense foliage of Ligustrum amurense makes it a popular choice for privacy hedges.
Gardeners often prefer Ligustrum amurense because it retains its leaves through winter.
Ligustrum amurense is valued for its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amur privet - common name for Ligustrum amurense, an Asian shrub used for hedges

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Ligustrum' and species epithet 'amurense', meaning 'of the Amur River' (in Asia).
