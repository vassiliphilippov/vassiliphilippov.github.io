double-dealing

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌdiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l ˌdiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
deceitful or dishonest behaviour, especially the practice of pretending to act one way while behaving differently for personal advantage
dishonest or deceitful behaviour

Examples

The investigation revealed his double-dealing and betrayal of his business partners.
Her double-dealing nature made it hard for anyone to trust her promises.
In politics, accusations of double-dealing can ruin a person's reputation.

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - behaving in a way intended to mislead or trick others
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlˈdiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈdiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
dishonest or deceitful behavior, especially by pretending to have one set of intentions while acting under another
deceitful or dishonest behavior

Examples

The company lost its reputation due to years of double-dealing and fraud.
She was tired of his double-dealing and demanded the truth.
In politics, accusations of double-dealing can ruin a career overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest

Etymology

From the verb 'deal double,' meaning to act deceitfully, first recorded in the 16th century.
