unjustifiably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˌdʒʌstəˈfaɪəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˌdʒʌstɪˈfaɪəbli / (BrE)
without any good reason or justification; in a way that cannot be defended or excused
without good reason or excuse

Examples

The manager was unjustifiably critical of the team's performance.
She felt that her work had been unjustifiably overlooked for promotion.
The company was accused of unjustifiably raising its prices.

Synset Examples

he is unjustifiably harsh on her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justifiably - with good reason or justification; in a manner that can be defended

Etymology

Formed from 'unjustifiable' (not able to be justified) + '-ly' (adverb suffix).
