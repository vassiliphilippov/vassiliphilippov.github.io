bruising

1. ADJ

/ ˈbruːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbruːzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing mental or emotional pain or distress; deeply hurtful or damaging
emotionally painful or damaging

Examples

Losing the championship was a bruising experience for the young athlete.
She went through a bruising divorce that left her emotionally drained.
The negotiations proved to be a bruising ordeal for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

a bruising experience
protected from the bruising facts of battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painful - causing or affected by physical or emotional pain
  • Synonym: traumatic - emotionally disturbing or distressing
  • Antonym: healing - having a positive, restorative emotional effect

2. ADJ

/ ˈbruːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbruːzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an action or event) harsh, forceful, and damaging, often causing emotional or psychological pain.
harsh; forcefully damaging (emotionally or psychologically)

Examples

The campaign turned into a bruising contest between the two candidates.
After a bruising meeting, the team members were left exhausted and frustrated.
She endured a bruising interview but remained calm throughout.

Synset Examples

protected from the bruising facts of battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painful - causing physical or emotional pain
  • Antonym: gentle - having a mild or kind effect; not harsh or severe

Etymology

Present participle of 'bruise'; extended metaphorically from causing physical bruises to intense, damaging encounters.
