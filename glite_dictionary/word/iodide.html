iodide

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪəˌdaɪd / (AmE), / ˈaɪədaɪd / (BrE)
a compound formed when iodine combines with another element, especially as a salt or ester of hydriodic acid
compound of iodine and another element

Examples

Potassium iodide is often used in medical treatments for thyroid conditions.
The solution turned brown when iodide ions reacted with chlorine.
Many table salts are fortified with sodium iodide to prevent iodine deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iodine - a chemical element with symbol I, used in medicine and photography

Etymology

From 'iodine' + '-ide', indicating a binary compound.
