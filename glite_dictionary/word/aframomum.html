Aframomum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæf.rəˈmoʊ.məm / (AmE), / ˌaf.rəˈməʊ.məm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African plants in the ginger family (Zingiberaceae), some of which produce edible seeds and fruits.
African genus of the ginger family

Examples

Several species of Aframomum are cultivated for their aromatic seeds.
The fruit of Aframomum is used in traditional African cuisine.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Aframomum plants.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the region 'Africa' plus Greek 'momum' (a type of plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfrəˈmoʊməm / (AmE), / ˌæfrəˈməʊməm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native to Africa, belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae), some species of which are used as spices or for medicinal purposes.
genus of African plants in the ginger family

Examples

The Aframomum genus includes several species used traditional African medicine.
Seeds from Aframomum plants are often ground into a flavorful spice.
Researchers are studying the potential health benefits of extracts from Aframomum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - a spice made from the root of Zingiber officinale, also in the ginger family

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek roots 'afra' (Africa) and 'momum' (spice plant).
