calabura

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈbʊrə / (AmE), / ˌkæləbˈjʊərə / (BrE)
A fast-growing tropical American evergreen tree with white flowers, edible red fruit, and bark yielding a silky fiber used in cordage.
tropical American tree with edible fruit

Examples

The calabura tree grows rapidly and produces small, sweet red fruits throughout the year.
Local villagers harvest the bark of calabura to make strong, silky cordage.
Children enjoy picking and eating the fresh calabura fruit in the summertime.

Etymology

From Spanish calabura, name for the tree in South America.
