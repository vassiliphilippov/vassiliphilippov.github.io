recorded

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɔrdɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdɪd / (BrE)
(of securities) having the owner's name entered in an official register.
of securities, entered in a register

Examples

Only recorded shareholders are entitled to receive dividends.
The transfer is not effective until the new owner's name is recorded on the register.
Unregistered shares cannot be traded as recorded securities on the exchange.

Synset Examples

recorded holders of a stock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: registered - (of securities) having ownership officially recorded
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01965322-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɔrdɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdɪd / (BrE)
(of sound, images, or information) set down or preserved in a permanent form, especially on film, tape, or digital medium, for reproduction.
preserved on film, tape, or digital medium

Examples

The band released their first recorded album in 1998.
Many interviews in the documentary were recorded weeks before it aired.
This lecture is recorded so that students who miss class can watch it later.

Synset Examples

recorded music

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live - performed or taking place at the time, not recorded
  • Antonym: unrecorded - not set down or preserved in a permanent form

Etymology

Past participle of 'record', from Old French 'recorder', from Latin 'recordari' (to remember).

Source: WordNet
