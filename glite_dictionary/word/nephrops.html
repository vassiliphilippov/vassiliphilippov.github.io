Nephrops

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛfrɒps / (AmE), / ˈnɛfrɒps / (BrE)
A genus of clawed lobsters in the family Nephropsidae, including the Norway lobster.
genus of clawed lobsters

Examples

The Nephrops genus includes several commercially important crustaceans.
Scientists conducted a survey of Nephrops populations in the North Atlantic.
The Norway lobster, classified as Nephrops norvegicus, is a popular seafood delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and pincers
  • Synonym: Nephrops norvegicus - species of Nephrops, commonly known as the Norway lobster or scampi

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney), describing the shape of certain anatomical features.
