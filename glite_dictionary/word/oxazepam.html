oxazepam

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈsæzɪˌpæm / (AmE), / ɒkˈsæzɪˌpam / (BrE)
a tranquilizing drug used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and symptoms of alcohol withdrawal
tranquilizing drug for anxiety and insomnia
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed oxazepam to help manage her anxiety symptoms.
Patients experiencing alcohol withdrawal are sometimes treated with oxazepam to reduce agitation.
Before bedtime, he took oxazepam as part of his routine for insomnia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diazepam - a related benzodiazepine tranquilizer used to treat anxiety
  • Synonym: lorazepam - a benzodiazepine drug used to treat anxiety disorders

Etymology

From 'oxa-' (indicating an oxygen atom in place of a carbon atom in the chemical structure) + 'azepam' (as in 'diazepam', from azine and -epam).
