nonadsorbent

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.ædˈsɔːr.bənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.ædˈzɔː.bənt / (BrE)
not able to adsorb or cause substances to accumulate on its surface
not able to adsorb

Examples

Glass is considered a nonadsorbent material and does not retain gases or liquids on its surface.
Make sure to use a nonadsorbent container for this chemical process to avoid contamination.
The powder was stored in a nonadsorbent bottle to prevent absorption of moisture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adsorbent - a material having capacity to adsorb substances on its surface

Etymology

From the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'adsorbent', from Latin 'ad-' (to) and 'sorbere' (to suck in).
