shovelboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəlˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈʃʌv(ə)lˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a game in which players use long sticks to push wooden disks onto a marked scoring area on a smooth surface
a disk-pushing game, also known as shuffleboard

Examples

The cruise ship offered shovelboard as a popular activity on deck.
My grandfather taught me how to play shovelboard when I was a child.
A set of shovelboard disks was found in the attic, alongside a vintage cue stick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffleboard - a game in which players push disks with cues onto a scoring area

Etymology

From 'shovel' + 'board', referring to shoving (pushing) disks on a board.
