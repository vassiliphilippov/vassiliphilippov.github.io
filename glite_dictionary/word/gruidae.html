Gruidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the family of large, long-legged, long-necked wading birds known as cranes
family of birds: cranes

Examples

The family Gruidae includes about fifteen species of cranes worldwide.
Researchers studied migratory patterns within the Gruidae to understand environmental impacts.
The elegant courtship dances of birds in the Gruidae are well known among avian biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane - a large, long-legged and long-necked bird in the family Gruidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Gruidae', based on Latin 'grus' (crane).
