enanthema

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnænˈθiːmə / (AmE), / ɪˌnanˈθiːmə / (BrE)
An eruption or rash occurring on a mucous membrane, especially inside the mouth, usually as a symptom of disease.
eruption on a mucous membrane
medical

Examples

The physician noted an enanthema on the patient's palate, suggesting a viral infection.
Some illnesses, such as measles, can present with both a skin rash and an enanthema.
The sudden appearance of an enanthema prompted further diagnostic tests.

Etymology

From Greek 'enanthema', meaning 'eruption within'.
