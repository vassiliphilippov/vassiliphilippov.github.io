nonetheless

1. ADV

/ ˌnʌn.ðəˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌnʌn.ðəˈlɛs / (BrE)
in spite of what has just been said or done; nevertheless
in spite of that; nevertheless

Examples

She knew it would be challenging, but she decided to try nonetheless.
The weather was terrible; nonetheless, the event continued as planned.
I was exhausted from work; nonetheless, I stayed up late to finish the project.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; anyway
  • Synonym: however - despite this; nevertheless
  • Synonym: still - despite what has just been said

Etymology

Formed from 'none the less', ultimately from Old English 'nān' (none) + 'þē lǣs' (the less).
