nervous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrvəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs / (BrE)
relating to the nerves or the nervous system of the body
relating to the nervous system
medical

Examples

Symptoms of a nervous disorder may include muscle weakness and loss of sensation.
The doctor explained that the disease attacks the nervous system.
Some toxins can severely damage nervous tissue and cause paralysis.

Synset Examples

nervous disease
neural disorder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neural - relating to nerves or the nervous system

Etymology

From Latin nervosus (sinewy, vigorous), from nervus (nerve).

/ ˈnɜrvəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs / (BrE)
Easily excited or agitated and difficult to control, especially due to fear or sensitivity to stimuli (often used of animals such as horses).

Examples

This breed is known to be rather nervous around loud noises and sudden movements.
If a horse becomes nervous during transport, it may injure itself or others.

Related Terms
