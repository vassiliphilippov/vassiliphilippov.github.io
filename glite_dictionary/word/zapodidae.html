Zapodidae

1. NOUN

/ zəˈpɒdɪˌdiː / (AmE), / zəˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small North American rodents known as jumping mice, characterized by their long hind legs and ability to leap great distances
family of jumping mice

Examples

Unlike other small rodents, members of the Zapodidae family can leap impressive distances to escape predators.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of Zapodidae throughout the northeastern United States.
The long hind legs of the Zapodidae enable these jumping mice to navigate tall grasses efficiently.

Etymology

From New Latin Zapodidae, from genus Zapus + -idae (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ zəˈpɑdəˌdi / (AmE), / zæˈpəʊdɪdi / (BrE)
a family of small North American and Asian rodents known as jumping mice, characterized by their long hind legs and tails, adapted for leaping.
family of jumping mice (rodents)

Examples

The Zapodidae are well adapted to living in grassy and woodland environments.
Scientists have studied Zapodidae for their unique leaping abilities.
Among North American rodents, Zapodidae stand out due to their unusually long tails and hind legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumping mouse - a rodent with long hind legs for leaping, of the family Zapodidae

Etymology

From New Latin Zapodidae, from genus name Zapus + -idae (family suffix).
