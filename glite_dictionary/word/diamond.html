diamond

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəmənd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd / (BrE)
the area of a baseball field enclosed by the three bases and home plate, shaped like a diamond
baseball field area between bases

Examples

During practice, the players gathered around the diamond to run drills.
The grounds crew worked hard to keep the diamond in top condition before the big game.
She stood on the pitcher's mound, at the very center of the diamond.

Examples

The jeweler showed us a rare blue diamond valued at millions of dollars.

Related Terms

6. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəmənd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd / (BrE)
A transparent, extremely hard, precious stone consisting of pure crystalline carbon, often valued as a gemstone.
precious stone of pure carbon

Examples

The jeweler placed the sparkling diamond into a velvet box.
She wore a necklace with a large diamond pendant to the gala.
Industrial drills sometimes use diamond tips to cut through hard materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a precious or semi-precious stone, typically one that has been cut and polished
  • Antonym: graphite - a soft black form of carbon, a less hard allotrope than diamond

Etymology

From Old French 'diamant', from Late Latin 'adamas, -antis' (diamond, the hardest iron, steel), from Greek 'adamas' (invincible, untamable).
