tachograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.əˌgræf / (AmE), / ˈtak.ə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A device that automatically records the speed and distance traveled by a motor vehicle, as well as the periods of driver activity and rest.
speed and activity recorder for vehicles

Examples

Many commercial trucks in Europe are fitted with a tachograph to ensure compliance with driving regulations.
When reviewing the tachograph data, the inspector found that the driver had exceeded his legal hours.
The mechanic checked the tachograph to verify the speed at which the bus had traveled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tachometer - an instrument for measuring speed, especially the rotational speed of an engine

Etymology

From Greek 'tachos' (speed) + '-graph' (writing, recording).
