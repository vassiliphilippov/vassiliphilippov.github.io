impressionistic

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˌprɛʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɛʃəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Impressionism, especially in art, music, or literature, emphasizing a subjective and sensory impression rather than objective reality or detail.
of or relating to Impressionism

Examples

The gallery displayed an impressionistic painting by Monet.
Her writing style is impressionistic, focusing more on feelings than on events.
Debussy is known for his impressionistic approach to music.

Synset Examples

impressionist music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressionist - an artist or musician associated with Impressionism

Etymology

From Impressionism, the 19th-century art movement, plus -istic, indicating relation or characteristic of.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˌprɛʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɛʃ.əˈnɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
based on subjective impressions rather than on objective facts or detailed analysis; giving a general sense or feeling rather than precise details
based on impressions, not facts

Examples

The critic wrote an impressionistic account of the play, focusing on mood rather than plot details.
Her impressionistic memories of early childhood are vivid but not always accurate.
The artist's impressionistic style captured the energy of the city rather than its precise architecture.

Synset Examples

a surprisingly impressionistic review bearing marks of hasty composition
she had impressionistic memories of her childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: factual - based on or restricted to facts

Etymology

From 'impressionism,' based on French 'impressionnisme' (art movement), plus English '-ic'.
