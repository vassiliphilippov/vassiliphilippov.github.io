Cyclamen hederifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkləmən ˌhɛdərɪfoʊˈliːəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləmən ˌhɛdərɪfəˈliːəm / (BrE)
A species of wild European cyclamen with pink or pale purple flowers and ivy-shaped leaves, commonly found in woodland areas.
wild European cyclamen species

Examples

The shady garden bed was filled with the delicate pink blossoms of Cyclamen hederifolium.
Many woodland areas in southern Europe are carpeted by Cyclamen hederifolium in autumn.
Gardeners appreciate Cyclamen hederifolium for its attractive foliage and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclamen - any plant of the genus Cyclamen

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Cyclamen' and specific epithet 'hederifolium' meaning 'ivy-leaved'.
