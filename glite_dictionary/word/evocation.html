evocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː.voʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌiː.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to mind or recreating something in the imagination, especially in art or literature.
imaginative re-creation

Examples

The novel is a brilliant evocation of post-war Paris.
Her paintings are an evocation of childhood memories and lost innocence.
The film’s evocation of the 1960s is both nostalgic and deeply moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjuring - the act of calling something to mind or into existence
  • Synonym: invocation - the act of calling upon something, often a deity, for inspiration or help

Etymology

From Latin evocatio (a calling forth), from evocare (to call forth).

2. NOUN

His speech was noted for its evocation of patriotism among the crowd.

3. NOUN

/ ˌiː.voʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌiː.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of calling forth supernatural forces or spirits, often by means of spells or incantations
summoning supernatural forces by spells

Examples

The priest performed an evocation to summon the ancient spirits of the forest.
Tales of witches often involve the evocation of demons or other supernatural beings.
During the ritual, the group chanted in unison in hopes of a successful evocation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjuration - the act of invoking spirits or supernatural agencies
  • Synonym: invocation - the act of calling upon a deity or spirit for aid or inspiration
  • Antonym: banishment - the act of sending away or dismissing a spirit or supernatural force

Etymology

From Latin evocatio (a calling forth), from evocare (to call out).
