leather

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðər / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
the skin of an animal, made smooth and flexible by removing the hair and tanning, used for making shoes, bags, clothes, etc.
animal skin treated for use as material

Examples

The jacket was made from high-quality leather and felt very soft to the touch.
He polished his leather boots before the meeting.
Many luxury handbags are crafted from genuine leather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - the skin of an animal, especially when tanned or dressed

Etymology

Old English lether, from Proto-Germanic *lethram (skin, hide).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɛðər / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
to whip or strike someone or something with a leather strap
to whip with a leather strap

Examples

The teacher would leather misbehaving students in the old days.
He threatened to leather the boy if he didn't confess.
They used to leather the horse to make it run faster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to beat repeatedly and violently
  • Synonym: to flog - to beat with a whip or stick as punishment
