virginal

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
a small, legless, rectangular keyboard instrument of the harpsichord family, popular in the 16th and 17th centuries, typically played by women
small rectangular keyboard instrument, historical

Examples

A collection of early music features pieces written for the virginal.
The museum displayed a beautifully decorated virginal from the Renaissance period.
She learned to play the virginal as part of her studies in historical music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpsichord - a keyboard instrument, precursor of the piano, in which strings are plucked

Etymology

From Latin 'virginalis' (of a maiden), likely referring to its association with young women.
