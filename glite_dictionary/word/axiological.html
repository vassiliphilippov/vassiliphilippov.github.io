axiological

1. ADJ

/ ˌæksiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌæksiəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy that studies values, such as ethics and aesthetics.
relating to the study of values

Examples

Her research takes an axiological approach to questions of morality and ethics.
The debate involved complex axiological considerations about what is truly valuable in human life.
Philosophers often address axiological issues when discussing the meaning of the good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiology - the study of values, especially in ethics and aesthetics

Etymology

From Greek axios (worthy) + -logical, after axiology (the study of values).
