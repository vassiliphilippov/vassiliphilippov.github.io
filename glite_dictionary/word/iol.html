IOL

1. NOUN

/ aɪ oʊ ɛl / (AmE), / aɪ əʊ ɛl / (BrE)
a clear artificial lens implanted in the eye, usually to replace a natural lens removed during cataract surgery
clear artificial lens implanted in the eye
medical

Examples

After her cataract surgery, an IOL was implanted to restore her vision.
The surgeon explained the benefits and risks of different types of IOLs to the patient.
Advances in IOL technology have improved the outcomes of cataract operations significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intraocular lens - artificial lens implanted in the eye; full form of IOL

Etymology

Acronym for intraocular lens.
