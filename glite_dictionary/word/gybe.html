to gybe

1. VERB

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
(of a sailing vessel or its sail) to shift suddenly from one side to the other when running before the wind, especially by turning the stern through the wind.
change course by shifting the sail across the wind

Examples

The skipper ordered the crew to gybe as we approached the mark.
If you gybe too quickly in strong wind, the boom can be dangerous.
We gybed several times while maneuvering through the narrow channel.

Synset Examples

The sail jibbed wildly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jibe - to change course by shifting the sail across the wind; alternative spelling

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'jibe', origin unknown, but first recorded in sailing contexts in the 17th century.
