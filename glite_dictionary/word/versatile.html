versatile

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɜrsətl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːsətaɪl / (BrE)
(of a part of a body, organ, or structure) able to move freely in all directions.
able to move freely in all directions

Examples

The bird's versatile toes help it grasp branches from different angles.
Orchids often have versatile anthers that can pivot as the wind blows.
The beetle's versatile antennae detect changes in its environment.

Synset Examples

an owl's versatile toe can move backward and forward
an insect's versatile antennae can move up and down or laterally
a versatile anther of a flower moves freely in the wind

Etymology

From Latin versatilis (turning easily, revolving), from versare (to turn around).
