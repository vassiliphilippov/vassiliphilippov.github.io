Mandean

1. ADJ

/ mænˈdiːən / (AmE), / manˈdiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Mandaean people, their language, or their culture
relating to Mandaeans or their culture

Examples

The Mandean religion has ancient roots in Mesopotamia.
He is researching Mandean manuscripts for his thesis.
Some Mandean ceremonies are still practiced today in southern Iraq.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mandaean - a member of the Mandaean people or relating to them

Etymology

From Mandaean, referring to an ethno-religious group in the Middle East.

WordNet ID: oewn-02769522-a

2. NOUN

/ mænˈdiːən / (AmE), / mænˈdiːən / (BrE)
a member of a small Gnostic religious sect, originating in the region of Jordan and surviving mainly in Iraq, which regards John the Baptist as the Messiah
member of a Gnostic sect believing John the Baptist was the Messiah

Examples

The Mandean community continues to practice its ancient rituals along the rivers of Iraq.
As a Mandean, he celebrates the baptism of John rather than traditional Christian festivals.
Scholars have preserved several sacred texts written in the Mandean language.

Etymology

From Mandaean, ultimately from the Mandaic word for 'knowledge' (mandā).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10309884-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ mænˈdiːən / (AmE), / mænˈdiːən / (BrE)
the dialect of Aramaic traditionally used by the Mandaeans in their religious writings and rituals
Aramaic dialect used by Mandaeans

Examples

Ancient religious texts were written in Mandean to ensure their authenticity.
Few people today are fluent in Mandean, making translation challenging.
Scholars gather annually to discuss the preservation of the Mandean language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aramaic - a family of languages historically spoken in the Near East

Etymology

From Mandaean, relating to the Mandaeans, an ethnoreligious group speaking this dialect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07001468-n
Synset POS: n
