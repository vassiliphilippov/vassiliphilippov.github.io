ponderosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːndəˈroʊsə / (AmE), / ˌpɒndəˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
a large pine tree (Pinus ponderosa) of western North America, known for its tall stature, dark green needles, and thick, scaly bark
a tall pine tree of western North America

Examples

Dense forests of ponderosa covered the hills outside the town.
The thick bark of the ponderosa helps protect it from forest fires.
Biologists measured the growth rings of an ancient ponderosa to study climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponderosa pine - another name for the ponderosa tree (Pinus ponderosa)

Etymology

From Latin ponderosus (heavy), referring to the weight of its wood.
