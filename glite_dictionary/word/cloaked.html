cloaked

1. ADJ

/ kloʊkt / (AmE), / kləʊkt / (BrE)
concealed or disguised so as to hide the true nature or intention, especially with the intent to mislead
disguised or concealed

Examples

The politician delivered a cloaked warning to his rivals, hinting at consequences without stating them directly.
Her true intentions were cloaked in layers of politeness and charm.
The project was cloaked in secrecy until the official announcement.

Synset Examples

masked threat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masked - concealed or hidden, often to mislead
  • Antonym: overt - open and not hidden

Etymology

From the noun 'cloak' (a type of cloak used to cover oneself), from Old French 'cloque', from Medieval Latin 'clocca' (bell, cloak).

2. ADJ

/ kloʊkt / (AmE), / kləʊkt / (BrE)
covered or concealed as if with a cloak; hidden or disguised
covered or hidden as if with a cloak

Examples

Mist cloaked the distant hills, making them barely visible.
The company's true intentions were cleverly cloaked behind vague statements.
She moved through the night, her presence cloaked in shadows.

Synset Examples

leaf-clothed trees
fog-cloaked meadows
a beam draped with cobwebs
cloud-wrapped peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veiled - partially hidden or concealed
  • Antonym: uncovered - not covered or hidden; exposed

Etymology

Past participle of 'cloak', from Old French 'cloque' (travelling cloak), from Medieval Latin 'clocca'.
