loyal

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
Firm and consistent in supporting someone or something, showing unwavering devotion or allegiance.
showing firm support or devotion

Examples

She is a loyal friend who always stands by my side in difficult times.
The loyal supporters cheered loudly despite the team's defeat.
Many employees remained loyal to the company during the financial crisis.

Synset Examples

a firm ally
loyal supporters
the true-hearted soldier … of Tippecanoe
fast friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast
  • Antonym: disloyal - not loyal; unfaithful

Etymology

From Old French *loial* (lawful, faithful), from Latin *legalis* (legal).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
showing firm and constant support or allegiance, especially to one's country, friends, or cause.
supportive and faithful, especially to your country

Examples

The soldiers remained loyal to their country even in difficult times.
She comes from a loyal family that always celebrates national holidays with pride.
Despite political turmoil, many citizens stayed loyal to their homeland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast
  • Synonym: patriotic - having or showing love of one's country
  • Antonym: disloyal - not loyal; unfaithful or treacherous

Etymology

From Old French 'loial', from Latin 'legalis' (legal, lawful).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
remaining firm in allegiance to a person, group, or cause; faithful and devoted.
faithful and devoted; steadfast in allegiance

Examples

She has always been a loyal friend, standing by me during tough times.
Many employees remained loyal to the company even during financial difficulties.
The king rewarded his loyal supporters with titles and land.

Synset Examples

loyal subjects
loyal friends stood by him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithful - remaining true and supportive to someone or something
  • Antonym: disloyal - not faithful or steadfast in allegiance
  • Synonym: devoted - very loving or loyal; keenly committed

Etymology

From Old French *loyal* (from Latin *legalis* meaning 'legal, according to the law').
