sufficient

1. ADJ

/ səˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / səˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
enough for a particular purpose; adequate to meet the needs or requirements of a situation, though not more than necessary
enough to meet a need

Examples

There was sufficient evidence to support his claim in court.
We have sufficient funds to complete the project this year.
Is there sufficient space in the car for all the luggage?

Synset Examples

sufficient food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insufficient - not enough to meet a need or requirement
  • Synonym: adequate - good enough for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'sufficiens', present participle of 'sufficere' (to meet the need of, to supply).
