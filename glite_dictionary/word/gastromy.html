gastromy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrəmi / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrəmi / (BrE)
A surgical incision into the stomach.
surgical incision into the stomach
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a gastromy to access the patient's stomach lining.
A gastromy may be required in cases of severe gastric obstruction.
Advancements in minimally invasive surgery have changed how a gastromy is performed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gastrectomy - surgical removal of part or all of the stomach

Etymology

From Greek gastēr (stomach) + -tomy (cutting, incision).
