to immerse

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɜrs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to become or make someone become completely involved in an activity or interest
to become fully involved in something

Examples

After moving to Paris, she immersed herself in French culture and language.
He tends to immerse himself in his work whenever he's facing personal challenges.
They encouraged students to immerse themselves fully in the research process.

Synset Examples

He immersed himself into his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take up all the attention or interest of someone
  • Synonym: to engross - to occupy someone’s full attention
  • Antonym: to distract - to prevent someone from giving full attention to something

Etymology

From Latin immersus, past participle of immergere (to dip into, plunge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00602438-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmɝs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to become completely involved in or absorbed by something; to submerge in a liquid or activity
cause to be fully involved in or covered by something

Examples

The teacher decided to immerse her class in a week-long experiment about marine life.
He chose to immerse himself in the culture by living with a local family.
To make proper tea, you should immerse the bag in boiling water for several minutes.

Synset Examples

The professor plunged his students into the study of the Italian text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submerge - to put or sink below the surface of water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin ‘immersus’, past participle of ‘immergere’ (to dip into).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00602773-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈmɜrs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to put or dip something completely into a liquid or another substance, especially so that it is covered
to dip or submerge in a liquid

Examples

She carefully immersed the vegetables in boiling water for a few minutes.
He likes to immerse himself in the pool after a long run.
The instructions say to immerse the filter in water before use.

Synset Examples

Immerse yourself in hot water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submerge - to go or put under the surface of water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'immersus', past participle of 'immergere' (to dip in, plunge).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪˈmɜrs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to enclose or envelop completely; to cover all sides thoroughly, as if surrounding or swallowing
enclose or envelop completely

Examples

The city was immersed in darkness after the power outage.
A thick fog immersed the entire valley, making it impossible to see.
The actor felt immersed in the role, surrounded by the elaborate set and costumes.

Synset Examples

The huge waves swallowed the small boat and it sank shortly thereafter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engulf - to surround or cover something completely
  • Synonym: to envelop - to wrap up, cover, or surround completely
  • Antonym: to expose - to leave something uncovered or unprotected

Etymology

From Latin immergere (to plunge in), from in- (in) + mergere (to dip).

Source: WordNet
