silver-tongued

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪlvərˈtʌŋd / (AmE), / ˌsɪlvəˈtʌŋd / (BrE)
able to express oneself in a fluent, eloquent, and persuasive manner.
eloquent and persuasive in speech

Examples

The silver-tongued lawyer convinced the jury with his compelling arguments.
She was known as a silver-tongued negotiator who could win over anyone.
His silver-tongued compliments quickly put everyone at ease during the meeting.

Synset Examples

able to dazzle with his facile tongue
silver speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
  • Synonym: glib - (often negative) fluent but insincere or shallow in speech

Etymology

From the association of 'silver' with value or beauty and 'tongue' representing speech, first recorded in the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɪlvərˈtʌŋd / (AmE), / ˌsɪlvəˈtʌŋd / (BrE)
having a persuasive and eloquent way with words; able to speak in a manner that charms or convinces others easily
persuasive or eloquent in speech

Examples

The silver-tongued lawyer convinced the jury of his client's innocence.
Many admired the writer for his silver-tongued storytelling and memorable speeches.
She relied on her silver-tongued charm to win over skeptical investors.

Synset Examples

The silver-tongued salesman sold me a car I didn't need.
A silver-tongued politician can easily sway the crowd.
Her silver-tongued remarks charmed the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
  • Synonym: persuasive - able to convince others easily
  • Antonym: blunt - uncompromisingly forthright; not subtle or persuasive

Etymology

From the metaphor of a tongue made of silver, symbolizing value and smoothness in speech. Used in English since the 16th century.
