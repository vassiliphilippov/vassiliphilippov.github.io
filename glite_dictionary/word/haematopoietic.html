haematopoietic

1. ADJ

/ hiˌmætəpoʊˈiːtɪk / (AmE), / hiˌmætəʊpɔɪˈiːtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the formation of blood or blood cells.
relating to blood cell formation
medical

Examples

The bone marrow is a haematopoietic tissue responsible for producing blood cells.
Certain diseases affect the haematopoietic system, leading to abnormal blood cell counts.
Doctors studied the haematopoietic effects of the new drug on patients with anemia.

Synset Examples

hemopoietic stem cells in bone marrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemopoietic - alternative spelling, relating to blood cell formation

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + poiein (to make, to form), via medical Latin.
