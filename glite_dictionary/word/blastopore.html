blastopore

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstəˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈblæstəpɔː / (BrE)
the opening that forms during early embryonic development, leading into the archenteron (the primitive gut) of an embryo
opening formed in early embryo development
medical

Examples

During gastrulation, the blastopore forms and gives rise to the digestive tract.
The fate of the blastopore can determine whether an animal is a protostome or deuterostome.
Scientists observed the early stages of embryo development, focusing on the formation of the blastopore.

Etymology

From Greek blasto- (bud, germ) + -pore (opening).
