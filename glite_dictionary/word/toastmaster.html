toastmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊstˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊstˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who introduces speakers and proposes toasts at a formal gathering or banquet
person who presides at a banquet

Examples

The toastmaster called for everyone's attention before introducing the guest of honor.
She volunteered to be the toastmaster at her best friend's wedding reception.
A skilled toastmaster can keep a banquet lively and engaging.

Etymology

From 'toast' (salutatory drink) + 'master'.
