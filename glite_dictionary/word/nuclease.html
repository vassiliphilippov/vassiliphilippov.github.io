nuclease

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.eɪs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.eɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of nucleic acids by cleaving the chains of nucleotides into smaller units
enzyme that breaks down nucleic acids
medical

Examples

Restriction nuclease enzymes are vital tools in genetic engineering.
The scientist used a nuclease to cut the DNA at specific sites.
Many viruses encode their own nuclease to help evade host defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a catalyst in biological reactions

Etymology

From nucleus (referring to nucleic acid) + -ase (enzyme suffix).
