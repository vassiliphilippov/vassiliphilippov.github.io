bacteremia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæk.təˈriː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌbæk.təˈriː.mi.ə / (BrE)
The presence of bacteria in the bloodstream, usually of a transient nature and often without symptoms.
presence of bacteria in the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with bacteremia after finding bacteria in her blood sample.
Early detection of bacteremia is crucial to prevent more severe infections like sepsis.
Routine dental procedures can sometimes cause transient bacteremia in healthy individuals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: septicemia - blood poisoning caused by bacteria and their toxins; a more serious and symptomatic progression

Etymology

From New Latin *bacterium* (bacterium) + Greek *haima* (blood).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbæktəˈriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌbæktɪəˈriːmɪə / (BrE)
the presence of bacteria in the bloodstream, often temporary or transient
presence of bacteria in the blood
medical

Examples

The patient developed bacteremia after his surgical procedure, requiring immediate antibiotic treatment.
Doctors routinely check blood cultures when they suspect bacteremia in critically ill patients.
Early detection of bacteremia is crucial in preventing the spread of infection throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septicemia - serious bloodstream infection causing symptoms

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'bacteria' and Greek 'haima' (blood).
