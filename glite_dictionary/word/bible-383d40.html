bible

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪbəl / (BrE)
a book considered to be the most authoritative or comprehensive source on a particular subject
authoritative book on a subject

Examples

For beginner chefs, this cookbook is considered the bible of French cuisine.
The style guide is the bible for writers working at the magazine.
Many regard Gray's Anatomy as the medical bible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handbook - a concise reference book providing specific information
  • Synonym: encyclopedia - a comprehensive reference work containing information on all branches of knowledge

Etymology

From Greek *biblion* (book), later used to refer to authoritative writings.
