mess

1. NOUN

/ mɛs / (AmE), / mɛs / (BrE)
A meal eaten by military or service personnel in a mess hall.
military meal in a mess hall

Examples

The soldiers gathered for mess at noon in the mess hall.
During evening mess, announcements were made to the company.
He missed mess because his patrol ran late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess hall - a hall or dining area where military personnel eat

Etymology

From Old French *mes* (portion of food), from Late Latin *missum* (a course at dinner, literally 'a sending').

Examples

The officers gathered in the mess after training for dinner.

Etymology

From Old French *mes* (dish, course of a meal), later extended in English to mean a place where such meals are served, especially for military personnel.
