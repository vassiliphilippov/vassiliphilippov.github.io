fructose

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌkˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈfrʌktəʊz / (BrE)
a simple sugar found naturally in honey and many ripe fruits, often used as a sweetener
a simple sugar in fruits and honey

Examples

Many soft drinks contain high levels of fructose as a sweetener.
Fructose occurs naturally in honey, apples, and other fruits.
The nutrition label indicates how much fructose the product contains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glucose - a simple sugar present in the blood and many foods
  • Synonym: sucrose - common table sugar, a compound of glucose and fructose

Etymology

From Latin 'fructus' (fruit) with the chemical suffix '-ose' indicating a sugar.
