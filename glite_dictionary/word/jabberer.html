jabberer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæbərər / (AmE), / ˈdʒæbərə / (BrE)
a person who talks rapidly and trivially, often making little sense
someone who talks rapidly and trivially

Examples

The meeting was dominated by a jabberer who wouldn't let anyone else speak.
She avoided sitting next to the office jabberer during lunch breaks.
No one could concentrate with a jabberer rambling on in the background.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot
  • Synonym: blabbermouth - someone who talks too much, especially indiscreetly
  • Antonym: silent - not speaking, quiet

Etymology

From 'jabber' + '-er', where 'jabber' comes from Middle English 'jabberen' (to chatter).
