surreptitiously

1. ADV

/ sɝː.rəpˈtɪʃ.əs.li / (AmE), / sʌr.əpˈtɪʃ.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that attempts to avoid notice or attention; secretly or stealthily.
secretly; stealthily

Examples

She glanced surreptitiously at the clock, hoping no one would notice her impatience.
He surreptitiously slipped the note into her bag before leaving the room.
They communicated surreptitiously during the meeting to avoid drawing attention.

Synset Examples

he was watching her surreptitiously as she waited in the hotel lobby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - without others knowing; in secret
  • Antonym: openly - in an honest and public way, not hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'surrepticius' (stolen, secret), from 'surripere' (to steal).
