rabbi

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈræbaɪ / (BrE)
a Jewish religious leader and teacher, especially one qualified to interpret Jewish law and tradition
Jewish religious leader or teacher

Examples

The rabbi led an insightful discussion on ancient traditions at the synagogue.
Many people consulted the rabbi for guidance during the holiday.
Their family invited the rabbi to dinner after the service.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'rabbī' (my master), via Greek and Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈræb.aɪ / (BrE)
A Jewish religious leader and teacher, especially one qualified to interpret and apply Jewish law.
Jewish religious leader and teacher

Examples

The rabbi delivered an inspiring sermon at the synagogue.
She asked the rabbi for advice about a religious question.
Many people turned to their rabbi for guidance during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - Christian religious leader or clergyman
  • Synonym: imam - Muslim religious leader

Etymology

From Hebrew *rabbī* (my master or teacher), via Greek and Latin.
