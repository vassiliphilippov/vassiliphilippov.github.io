arcade

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ɑːˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a covered passageway lined with shops or stalls on either side, often with arches along the sides or overhead
covered passageway with shops

Examples

We strolled through the brightly lit arcade, stopping to look at the shop displays.
The old city center features an elegant arcade with marble floors and glass ceilings.
Many people like to take shelter in the arcade during sudden rain showers while shopping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallery - a long, narrow passage, often with shops; similar architectural term

Etymology

From French 'arcade', from Italian 'arcata' (arch), from 'arco' (arch).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ɑːˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a covered passageway or structure with a series of arches supported by columns, often forming a gallery or walkway
series of arches supported by columns

Examples

The museum entrance was sheltered by a magnificent arcade of marble columns.
We strolled along the arcade, enjoying the shade provided by its arches.
The ancient city is known for its impressive Roman arcade flanking the central plaza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonnade - a row of columns supporting a roof, not necessarily with arches

Etymology

From French 'arcade', from Italian 'arcata', from 'arco' meaning 'arch', from Latin 'arcus'.
