equine

1. ADJ

/ ˈiːˌkwaɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːkwʌɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of horses, or animals in the family Equidae; resembling a horse.
relating to horses or horse family

Examples

The veterinarian specialized in treating equine diseases.
Her equine features gave her a distinctive appearance.
He has a passion for all things equine, especially show jumping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - a large domesticated mammal of the species Equus ferus caballus

Etymology

From Latin 'equīnus' (of a horse), from 'equus' (horse).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛkˌwaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛk.wʌɪn / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of horses, or resembling a horse.
relating to or resembling a horse

Examples

The artist painted the statue with distinctly equine features.
Her long face and high cheekbones gave her an almost equine appearance.
Veterinarians who specialize in equine medicine work exclusively with horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse-like - having characteristics similar to a horse

Etymology

From Latin 'equus' (horse), via French 'équin'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌkwaɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːkwʌɪn / (BrE)
a member of the horse family, including horses, donkeys, and zebras
animal of the horse family

Examples

The veterinarian specializes in treating equines such as horses and donkeys.
Zebras are wild equines native to Africa, known for their distinct stripes.
Proper care of equines requires knowledge of their unique dietary and behavioral needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - large domesticated animal used for riding and work
  • Synonym: donkey - domesticated hoofed mammal related to the horse
  • Synonym: zebra - African wild animal with black-and-white stripes, related to horses

Etymology

From Latin 'equus' (horse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02376801-n
Synset POS: n
