bonemeal

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌmiːl / (BrE)
a fertilizer made by grinding animal bones into a powder, used to enrich soil with nutrients, especially phosphorus and calcium
fertilizer made from ground bones

Examples

The gardener sprinkled bonemeal around the rose bushes to promote healthy growth.
Many organic farmers use bonemeal as a natural source of nutrients for their crops.
Check the label to see if the fertilizer contains bonemeal or other organic additives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to increase its fertility
