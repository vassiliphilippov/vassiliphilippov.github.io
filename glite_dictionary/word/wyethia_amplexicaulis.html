Wyethia amplexicaulis

1. NOUN

/ waɪˈiːθi.ə æmˌplɛksɪˈkɔːlɪs / (AmE), / waɪˈiːθi.ə æmˌplɛksɪˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the northwestern United States, with lance-shaped leaves and large deep yellow flowers on long stalks.
herbaceous plant with yellow flowers, native to northwestern US

Examples

During our hike, we spotted several Wyethia amplexicaulis blooming along the mountain trail.
The deep yellow flowers of Wyethia amplexicaulis brighten the meadows every spring in Idaho.
Botanists use the distinctive leaves to reliably identify Wyethia amplexicaulis in the wild.

Etymology

Genus name Wyethia honors American explorer Nathaniel Wyeth; species name from Latin 'amplexicaulis' meaning 'clasping the stem'.
