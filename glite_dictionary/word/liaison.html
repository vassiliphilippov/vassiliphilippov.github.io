liaison

1. NOUN

/ liˈeɪzɑn / (AmE), / liˈeɪzɒn / (BrE)
a person or means of communication that helps groups or organizations work together and exchange information
person or means of communication between groups

Examples

She was appointed as the liaison between the two departments to facilitate cooperation.
The embassy acted as a liaison for the international organizations during the crisis.
Their team maintains a liaison with local authorities to ensure smooth operations.

Synset Examples

he provided a liaison with the guerrillas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - person who acts as a go-between or point of communication
  • Antonym: disconnect - lack of communication or link between groups

Etymology

From French 'liaison', meaning 'a binding or linking', from 'lier' (to bind).

2. NOUN

/ liˈeɪzɑn / (AmE), / liˈeɪzɒn / (BrE)
a sexual relationship, often secret or illicit, between two people, especially when one or both are married to someone else
secret or illicit sexual relationship

Examples

Their liaison was the subject of gossip throughout the office.
He tried to keep the liaison with his neighbor hidden from his wife.
The novel details a passionate liaison between the two main characters.

Etymology

From French 'liaison' (a binding or connection), from 'lier' (to bind).
