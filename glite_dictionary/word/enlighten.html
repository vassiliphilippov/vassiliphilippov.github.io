to enlighten

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪtən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
(in religion) To provide someone with spiritual insight or knowledge.
give spiritual insight (religion)

Examples

Meditation is said to enlighten those who sincerely seek spiritual growth.
Many teachers aim to enlighten their students spiritually as well as intellectually.
The guru hoped to enlighten his followers by sharing profound wisdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to illuminate - to make clear or shed light upon, often intellectually or spiritually

Etymology

From Old French enluminer/enlighten, from Latin illuminare (to illuminate, enlighten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00928903-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪtən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtən / (BrE)
to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject or situation; to clarify or make something clear
make clear; give knowledge or understanding

Examples

The teacher tried to enlighten us about the complex theory during the lecture.
Could you enlighten me as to why this change was necessary?
After reading the book, she felt enlightened about the subject's history.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible
  • Antonym: to confuse - to make something unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Old French 'enluminer', from Latin 'illuminare' (to light up, make light).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00622730-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtn / (BrE)
to give someone greater knowledge and understanding about a subject or situation
give knowledge or understanding

Examples

Her lecture helped enlighten the audience about climate change.
If you have time, could you enlighten me on how this system works?
Reading widely can enlighten your perspective on many issues.

Synset Examples

Can you enlighten me — I don't understand this proposal?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information; to tell
  • Antonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression

Etymology

From Old French *enlighter*, from en- (make) + Latin *lux* (light).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00993417-v
Synset POS: v
