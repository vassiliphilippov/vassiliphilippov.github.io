softener

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔfənər / (AmE), / ˈsɒfənə / (BrE)
a substance that is added to make another material or substance less hard or softer
a substance that makes something less hard

Examples

Add a cup of softener to the wash cycle to keep your clothes feeling gentle.
The water softener reduces the mineral content in tap water.
Some hair products include a softener to prevent dryness and brittleness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardener - a substance added to make something harder

Etymology

From 'soften' + '-er', denoting an agent or thing that softens.
