moon-curser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌkɝːsər / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌkɜːsə / (BrE)
a person who smuggles goods by importing or exporting them without paying duties
person who smuggles goods, avoids duties

Examples

The authorities finally caught the notorious moon-curser as he tried to bring contraband across the border.
Stories of moon-cursers were common along the coastline during the age of sail.
The old inn was rumored to be a hideout for moon-cursers and their hidden treasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - a person who illegally transports goods to avoid customs or duties

Etymology

From 'moon' and 'curser', alluding to smugglers wishing for clouds to cover the moon's light while moving illicit goods.
