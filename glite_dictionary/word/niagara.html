Niagara

1. PROPN

/ naɪˈæɡərə / (AmE), / naɪˈaːɡərə / (BrE)
A famous large waterfall on the border between Canada and the United States, comprising the Horseshoe Falls and the American Falls.
famous waterfall on US-Canada border

Examples

Tourists from all over the world come to see Niagara each year.
The mist from Niagara could be seen from miles away.
They took a boat trip to get closer to Niagara and experience its power.

Etymology

From the Native American (Iroquoian) word, possibly meaning 'thunder of waters.'

2. PROPN

/ naɪˈæɡɚə / (AmE), / naɪˈaːɡərə / (BrE)
A river that flows from Lake Erie to Lake Ontario, forming part of the border between Ontario, Canada, and New York, United States.
river between Lake Erie and Lake Ontario

Examples

The Niagara is famous for its spectacular waterfalls.
Many tourists visit the Niagara region each year to see the falls.
The Niagara River is an important source of hydroelectric power for New York and Ontario.

Etymology

From the Iroquoian word *Onguiaahra*, meaning ‘thunder of waters’.
