Cerapteryx

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈræptərɪks / (AmE), / sɛˈræptərɪks / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Noctuidae, commonly known as antler moths.
a genus of moths (antler moths)

Examples

The Cerapteryx genus includes several species that are important in agricultural ecosystems.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of Cerapteryx moths across Europe.
I found a specimen belonging to Cerapteryx during my summer fieldwork.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn) and 'pteryx' (wing), referring to the moths' wing patterns.

2. NOUN

Examples

The Cerapteryx genus includes moths that are commonly found in grassy habitats across Europe.
Researchers documented several Cerapteryx species during the summer survey.
The antler moth, a member of the Cerapteryx genus, is known for the distinctive markings on its wings.

Etymology

From New Latin Cerapteryx, the scientific name assigned in taxonomy.
