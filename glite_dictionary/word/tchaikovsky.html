Tchaikovsky

1. PROPN

/ tʃaɪˈkɑːfski / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈkɒfski / (BrE)
Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky (1840–1893), an important Russian composer known for his expressive melodies and major works such as ballets, symphonies, and concertos.
Russian composer (1840–1893)

Examples

The music of Tchaikovsky is still widely performed around the world today.
Many dancers consider Tchaikovsky’s Swan Lake to be the ultimate ballet score.
She wrote her thesis on the influences of folk music in Tchaikovsky’s compositions.

Etymology

From Russian Чайкoвский (Chaykovskiy), the composer's surname.
