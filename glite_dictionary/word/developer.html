developer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɛləpɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpə / (BrE)
a chemical solution used to make a photographic image visible on film or paper during the development process
photographic chemical for developing film

Examples

The technician poured the developer into the tray and waited for the image to appear.
To process black-and-white photos, you need both developer and fixer chemicals.
The developer must be handled with care to avoid damaging the negatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixer - a chemical used after developer to stabilize a photographic image
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɛləpər / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpə / (BrE)
a person or company that buys land or buildings, makes improvements to them, and sells them for profit, especially for residential or commercial use
person/company developing real estate for profit

Examples

The developer plans to turn the old factory into luxury apartments.
A group of developers purchased the vacant land next to the river.
Many residents opposed the developer's proposal to build a new shopping center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: builder - a person who constructs buildings

Etymology

From develop + -er, first used in this real estate sense in the mid-20th century.
