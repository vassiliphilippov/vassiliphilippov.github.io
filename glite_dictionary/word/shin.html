shin

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪn / (BrE)
A cut of meat taken from the lower part of the leg of an animal, especially a cow or calf, often used for making stews or soups.
cut of meat from the lower leg

Examples

The recipe calls for two pounds of shin to add rich flavor to the stew.
The butcher recommended using shin because it becomes tender when slow-cooked.
She simmered the shin for hours to make a hearty broth.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪn / (BrE)
the larger and thicker of the two bones in the lower leg, situated between the knee and the ankle
the larger bone of the lower leg

Examples

He injured his shin during the soccer match and had to sit out for the rest of the game.
The doctor examined the X-ray and pointed out a small fracture in her shin.
She felt pain in her shin after accidentally bumping into the coffee table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tibia - the medical term for the shin bone

Etymology

Old English *scinu* (shin, fore part of the leg).

4. NOUN

/ ʃɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪn / (BrE)
the front part of the human leg between the knee and the ankle
front part of the lower leg

Examples

He accidentally kicked the table and bruised his shin.
The soccer ball struck her shin, making her wince in pain.
He wore pads to protect his shins while playing football.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calf - the back part of the lower leg between the knee and ankle

Etymology

Old English *scinu*, related to Old Norse *skina* (to shine, be visible), referring to the exposed part of the leg.

5. VERB

/ ʃɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪn / (BrE)
to climb up or down something awkwardly, using one's hands and shins for support
climb using hands and shins

Examples

He managed to shin up the tree to rescue the kitten.
We watched the children shinning over the garden wall despite the rain.
She shinned down the drainpipe to avoid being seen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scramble - to move or climb quickly and awkwardly using hands and feet

Etymology

From noun 'shin,' referring to the part of the leg, originally Middle English, possibly from Old English 'scinu' (shin, foreleg).
