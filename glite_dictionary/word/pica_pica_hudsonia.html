Pica pica hudsonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkə ˈpaɪkə hʌdˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈpiːkə ˈpiːkə hʌdˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A subspecies of magpie native to the Rocky Mountains and parts of western North America, distinguished by its black and white plumage and long tail.
North American magpie subspecies

Examples

The Pica pica hudsonia can often be seen scavenging in western North America.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the Pica pica hudsonia in the Rocky Mountains.
Unlike its Eurasian relatives, the Pica pica hudsonia displays unique social behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magpie - A bird of the crow family with long tail and black-and-white coloring

Etymology

Latin: 'Pica' meaning 'magpie'; 'hudsonia' indicates connection to Hudson Bay region.
