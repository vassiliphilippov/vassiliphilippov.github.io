tawdry

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔːdri / (AmE), / ˈtɔːdri / (BrE)
showy but cheap and of poor quality; made of inferior workmanship and materials
cheap and of poor quality

Examples

The market was full of tawdry jewelry that looked impressive at first glance but broke easily.
She regretted buying the tawdry dress as it started to unravel after one wash.
The film received criticism for its tawdry production values and uninspired performances.

Synset Examples

cheapjack moviemaking … that feeds on the low taste of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - brilliantly or excessively showy; tastelessly ornamented
  • Synonym: shoddy - badly made or of poor quality
  • Antonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner

Etymology

From Saint Audrey's lace, a type of cheap necklace sold at St Audrey's fair in England, later associated with gaudy and inferior ornaments.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɔdɹi / (AmE), / ˈtɔːdɹi / (BrE)
Showing off in a cheap or tasteless way; gaudy and lacking in good taste.
showy but cheap and tasteless

Examples

She wore a tawdry necklace that glittered but looked cheap up close.
The shop was filled with tawdry souvenirs that tourists snapped up eagerly.
He tried to impress everyone with his tawdry new jacket, but it only drew laughs.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - showy in a tasteless or vulgar way
  • Synonym: garish - too bright or showy; harsh or tasteless
  • Antonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner

Etymology

From St. Audrey's lace, a fair in England known for selling cheap showy lace; St. Audrey became corrupted to 'tawdry'.
