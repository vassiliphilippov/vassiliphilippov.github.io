Eichhornia spesiosa

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈhɔrniə spəˈsiːoʊsə / (AmE), / aɪkˈhɔːnɪə spɪˈsiːəʊsə / (BrE)
a tropical aquatic plant with floating leaves and spikes of large blue flowers, known for blocking waterways, especially in the southern United States
tropical floating aquatic plant

Examples

The spread of Eichhornia spesiosa has caused severe problems for local fisheries.
Boaters struggled to make their way through the thick mats of Eichhornia spesiosa on the river.
Efforts to control Eichhornia spesiosa have increased due to its impact on water flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hyacinth - common name for Eichhornia species, especially Eichhornia crassipes

Etymology

Genus name from German botanist Johann Eichhorn; species epithet from Latin 'speciosus' (showy, beautiful).
