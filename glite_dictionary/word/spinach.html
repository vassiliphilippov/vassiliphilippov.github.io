spinach

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
A plant with dark green leaves that are eaten as a vegetable, either cooked or raw in salads.
dark green leafy vegetable

Examples

She made a salad with fresh spinach, cherry tomatoes, and feta cheese.
When cooked, spinach shrinks to a much smaller volume than when raw.
Popeye is famous for gaining strength after eating cans of spinach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a leafy green vegetable, often used in salads
  • Synonym: kale - a type of green leafy vegetable similar to spinach

Etymology

From Old French espinache, from Arabic isbānākh, from Persian espenaj.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪtʃ / (BrE)
A leafy green vegetable with succulent edible dark green leaves, commonly used in salads and cooking.
a leafy green vegetable

Examples

She added fresh spinach to the salad for extra flavor.
Spinach is rich in vitamins and often served steamed or sautéed.
Popeye famously ate spinach to gain strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a leafy green vegetable often used in salads
  • Synonym: kale - a hardy cabbage with curled or ruffled leaves, used as a vegetable

Etymology

From Old French espinache, from Arabic isbānākh, from Persian esfenāj.
