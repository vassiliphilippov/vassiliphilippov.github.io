Leipoa ocellata

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈpoʊ.ə ɒˈsɛl.ə.tə / (AmE), / laɪˈpəʊ.ə ɒˈsɛl.ə.tə / (BrE)
A large Australian bird, commonly known as the malleefowl, that incubates its eggs in large sandy mounds.
Australian bird (malleefowl) that incubates eggs in sandy mounds

Examples

The Leipoa ocellata is famous for building enormous mounds to incubate its eggs.
Researchers studied the incubation habits of the Leipoa ocellata in the arid Australian bush.
The conservation status of Leipoa ocellata has become a concern due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleefowl - Australian ground-dwelling bird that builds mounds for nesting

Etymology

From Greek *leipo* (to leave) + Latin *ocellata* (spotted, from *ocellus*, little eye).
