amenity

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛnɪti / (AmE), / əˈmiːnɪti / (BrE)
A feature or facility that provides comfort, convenience, or pleasure.
feature providing comfort or convenience

Examples

The hotel offers a variety of amenities, including a swimming pool and a fitness center.
Residents appreciate the modern amenities available in the new apartment complex.
Free Wi-Fi and complimentary breakfast are popular amenities in most hotels.

Synset Examples

all the comforts of home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facility - something that is built or provided for a particular purpose
  • Degree Higher: luxury - something adding to pleasure or comfort but not absolutely necessary
  • Degree Lower: necessity - something essential or indispensable

Etymology

From Latin 'amoenitas' (pleasantness), from 'amoenus' (pleasant).

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛnəti / (AmE), / əˈmiːnɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant or agreeable, especially in environment or circumstances
pleasantness; agreeable quality

Examples

Her new apartment had an amenity that brought a sense of peace to her evenings.
The amenity of the lakeside cabin made their vacation truly relaxing.
We valued the amenity of quiet surroundings in choosing our hotel.

Synset Examples

a well trained staff saw to the agreeableness of our accommodations
he discovered the amenities of reading at an early age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasing or delightful
  • Antonym: disagreeableness - the quality of being unpleasant or not agreeable

Etymology

From Latin amoenitas (pleasantness), from amoenus (pleasant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04786292-n
Synset POS: n
