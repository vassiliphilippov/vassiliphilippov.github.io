mouton

1. NOUN

/ muˈtɑn / (AmE), / muːˈtɒ̃ / (BrE)
meat from a mature domestic sheep, especially as used in cooking or butchery.
meat from an adult sheep

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional stew using mouton instead of lamb for a richer flavor.
Many French recipes call for mouton when a deeper, gamier taste is desired.
At the butcher’s counter, you can request mouton cuts for certain ethnic dishes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lamb - meat from a young sheep
  • Synonym: mutton - meat from a mature sheep (variant spelling)

Etymology

From French 'mouton' (sheep), from Latin 'multo', 'multonem' (ram).
