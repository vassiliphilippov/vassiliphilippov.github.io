Shrovetide

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃroʊv.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃrəʊv.taɪd / (BrE)
The period of celebration and confession in the Christian calendar immediately preceding Lent, traditionally including the days from Sunday to Shrove Tuesday.
period before Lent in Christian calendar

Examples

Many people celebrate Shrovetide by eating pancakes on Shrove Tuesday.
During Shrovetide, it is customary in some countries to hold carnivals and festive parades.
Historically, Shrovetide involved confessing sins before the solemn season of Lent began.

Etymology

From 'shrive', meaning to confess, plus 'tide' (period), from Old English roots.
