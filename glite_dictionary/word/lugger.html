lugger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈlʌɡə / (BrE)
a small fishing boat equipped with one or more lugsails
small fishing boat with lugsails

Examples

The old fisherman repaired his lugger before setting out to sea.
Several luggers were anchored in the small harbor, ready for the morning catch.
They sailed their lugger along the coast every summer.

Etymology

From 'lug' (the type of sail) + '-er', used for naming boats.
