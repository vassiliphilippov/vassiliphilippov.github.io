deerberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪrˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdɪəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, branching blueberry shrub native to marshy areas of eastern North America, bearing greenish or yellowish berries that are typically unpalatable to humans but reportedly eaten by deer.
a blueberry shrub with yellowish berries eaten by deer

Examples

The deerberry thrives in sandy, acidic soils common to eastern U.S. wetlands.
Though the deerberry is rarely eaten by people, its berries are a food source for local wildlife.
We learned to recognize the pale yellow fruit of the deerberry while hiking through the preserve.

Etymology

Formed from 'deer' + 'berry,' referring to the belief that deer eat its fruit.
