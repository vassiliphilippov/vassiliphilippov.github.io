to falsify

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔl.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to prove something to be false; to show that something is not true
prove to be false

Examples

The investigator was able to falsify the suspect's alibi through evidence.
Scientists sometimes try to falsify hypotheses to test their validity.
The attorney aimed to falsify the witness's statement during cross-examination.

Synset Examples

Falsify a claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disprove - to show that something is not true or correct

Etymology

From Latin falsificare, from falsus (false) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00669860-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔl.sɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something false by altering it, especially in order to deceive; to tamper with information, documents, or evidence so as to mislead
to make false by altering or adding

Examples

The journalist was accused of trying to falsify the facts in his report.
Employees who falsify records may face criminal charges.
She warned that any attempt to falsify the evidence would be discovered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - to make a false copy of something with intent to deceive
  • Antonym: to authenticate - to prove that something is genuine and true

Etymology

From Latin falsificare, from falsus (false) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00837582-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈfɔl.sɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to deliberately change or misrepresent information, documents, or facts in order to deceive
deliberately alter to deceive

Examples

The accountant was charged because he had falsified several financial statements.
It is illegal to falsify medical records.
She was accused of falsifying data in her research paper.

5. VERB

/ ˈfɔl.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to change (information, documents, etc.) in order to make people believe something that is not true; to forge or misrepresent
to alter in order to deceive

Examples

He was accused of trying to falsify government records for personal gain.
The scientist lost her reputation after it was discovered she had falsified the data.
Anyone who attempts to falsify evidence in court can face serious legal consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - to make a fake copy of something, especially a document or signature
  • Synonym: to fabricate - to make up or invent (something false)
  • Antonym: to authenticate - to prove that something is genuine or true

Etymology

From Latin falsificare, from falsus (false) + facere (to make).
