Quiscalus quiscula

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪskələs ˈkwɪskjʊlə / (AmE), / ˈkwɪskjələs ˈkwɪskjʊlə / (BrE)
a species of grackle found in eastern North America, known for its glossy black plumage and iridescent sheen
species of North American grackle

Examples

Flocks of Quiscalus quiscula can often be seen foraging in agricultural fields.
The iridescent feathers of Quiscalus quiscula shimmer in the sunlight.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Quiscalus quiscula in both rural and urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common grackle - the common name for Quiscalus quiscula, a blackbird native to North America

Etymology

From Latin, the genus name 'Quiscalus' and species 'quiscula' used in scientific taxonomy.
