androgynous

1. ADJ

/ ænˈdrɑːdʒənəs / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
Having both male and female characteristics or qualities; not specifically masculine or feminine in appearance or style.
having both male and female characteristics

Examples

The model's androgynous look challenged traditional ideas about gender in fashion.
Many pop stars adopt an androgynous style that appeals to a wide audience.
Her performance was praised for its subtle, androgynous energy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculine - having male characteristics; associated with men
  • Antonym: feminine - having female characteristics; associated with women

Etymology

From Greek 'androgynos', from 'anēr' (man) + 'gynē' (woman).

2. ADJ

/ ænˈdrɑːdʒənəs / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting physical characteristics of both male and female, especially having both sexual organs but with a predominantly female appearance
having both male and female physical characteristics

Examples

The androgynous plant species was studied for its unique combination of reproductive organs.
Some reptiles can exhibit androgynous features, displaying both types of sex organs.
In rare medical cases, humans may be born with androgynous anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermaphroditic - having both male and female reproductive organs

Etymology

From Greek androgynos (male and female), from anēr (man) + gynē (woman).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
