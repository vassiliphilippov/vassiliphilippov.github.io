copperplate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpərˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpəˌpleɪt / (BrE)
a graceful style of handwriting characterized by fine, flowing strokes, originally based on writing used for engraving on copper plates
elegant handwriting style from engraving tradition

Examples

Her copperplate excited admiration from everyone who saw her homework.
The invitation was written in beautiful copperplate script.
He practiced copperplate until his penmanship was nearly perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calligraphy - decorative handwriting or handwritten lettering

Etymology

From the use of copper plates for engraved printing, where the handwriting style was imitated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑpɚˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpəˌpleɪt / (BrE)
A print produced from an engraved copper plate, especially in traditional printmaking and illustration.
a print from an engraved copper plate

Examples

The museum exhibited a rare copperplate of a 17th-century map.
She specializes in restoring antique copperplate prints for collectors.
Original copperplate engravings are highly valued by art historians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from an engraved surface

Etymology

From copper + plate, referring to the copper sheets used for engraving.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.pɚˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒp.əˌpleɪt / (BrE)
A smooth plate of copper that has been etched or engraved, used for printing images or text.
engraved copper printing plate

Examples

The museum displays a nineteenth-century copperplate used for printing old maps.
Restoring the fragile copperplate revealed intricate details of the artist's original work.
She studied the technique of making a copperplate during her printmaking course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etching - The process of using acid to engrave a design on a metal plate

Etymology

From 'copper' + 'plate'; refers to the use of copper as a material for engraved plates.
