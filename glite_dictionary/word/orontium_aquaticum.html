Orontium aquaticum

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɑːnʃiəm əˈkwætɪkəm / (AmE), / ɒˈrɒntɪəm əˈkwætɪkəm / (BrE)
an aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States, with blue-green leaves and a spadix resembling a club covered in tiny yellow flowers
aquatic plant of southeastern US

Examples

Orontium aquaticum is commonly found in shallow waters of ponds and streams in the southeastern United States.
The distinctive spadix of Orontium aquaticum sets it apart from other aquatic plants in its habitat.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Orontium aquaticum for its attractive blue-green foliage and yellow club-like flower spikes.

Etymology

From the genus name Orontium, of Latin and Greek origin, and the species name aquaticum, Latin for 'of water'.
