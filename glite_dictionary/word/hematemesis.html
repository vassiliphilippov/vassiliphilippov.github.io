hematemesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈtɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛməˈtiːməsɪs / (BrE)
the vomiting of blood, especially as a symptom of disease or injury
vomiting of blood
medical

Examples

The patient was rushed to the emergency room after an episode of hematemesis.
Chronic liver disease can sometimes lead to hematemesis due to ruptured blood vessels.
Doctors treated her for hematemesis following the severe bleeding.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + emesis (vomiting).
