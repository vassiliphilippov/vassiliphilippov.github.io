cusk

1. NOUN

/ kʌsk / (AmE), / kʌsk / (BrE)
the white, lean flesh of a North Atlantic fish (Brosme brosme), similar to cod, used as food
lean flesh of a North Atlantic fish

Examples

Fishermen brought crates of fresh cusk to the market early in the morning.
The restaurant's special tonight is grilled cusk served with lemon butter sauce.
In New England, cusk is sometimes used as an alternative to cod in chowders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - the flesh of a large marine fish often used as food

Probably from Danish 'torsk' meaning 'cod.'

2. NOUN

/ kʌsk / (AmE), / kʌsk / (BrE)
a large edible marine fish of northern coastal waters, related to the cod, with a long body and barbel on the chin (Brosme brosme)
large edible northern marine fish related to cod

Examples

Fishermen in the North Atlantic often catch cusk along with cod and haddock.
The restaurant featured a dish made with fresh cusk from local waters.
Scientists are monitoring cusk populations due to concerns about overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish with white flesh, important as food

Etymology

Possibly from Old Norse *kusk* (codfish) or from earlier Scandinavian roots.

3. NOUN

/ kʌsk / (AmE), / kʌsk / (BrE)
an elongate freshwater cod of northern Europe, Asia, and North America, having barbels around its mouth; also known as burbot
a type of freshwater codfish

Examples

The cusk is commonly found in deep, cold lakes and rivers in northern regions.
Fishermen prize the cusk for its unique flavor and texture.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of the cusk to understand population declines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burbot - another name for the freshwater codfish cusk
