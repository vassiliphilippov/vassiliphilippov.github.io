to cross-refer

1. VERB

/ ˌkrɔs rəˈfɜr / (AmE), / ˌkrɒs rɪˈfɜː / (BrE)
to refer from one entry or item to another within catalogues, books, or lists, often for clarification or further information
refer from one entry to another

Examples

The index cross-refers to all the relevant topics discussed in the main text.
See also section 2.4, which is cross-referred to in the glossary.
Some legal documents cross-refer to specific statutes or other clauses for clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refer - to direct someone to a different place or source for information

Etymology

Formed from 'cross-' and 'refer', indicating reference across entries.
