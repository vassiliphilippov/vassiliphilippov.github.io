impotency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪm.pə.tən.si / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pə.tən.si / (BrE)
the inability, especially in men, to achieve or maintain an erection or to engage in sexual intercourse
inability to engage in sexual intercourse
medical

Examples

After his diagnosis, he discussed his impotency with a doctor to explore treatment options.
Certain medications can sometimes lead to temporary impotency in men.
The novel addresses themes such as aging and impotency in a sensitive manner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impotence - lack of sexual power or ability in men
  • Antonym: virility - manly qualities, especially sexual potency or strength

Etymology

From Latin 'impotentia', from 'in-' (not) + 'potentia' (power).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpətnsi / (AmE), / ˈɪmpətənsi / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking strength, power, or effectiveness; weakness or feebleness.
lack of strength or power

Examples

The impotency of the leadership led to a swift collapse of the movement.
She was frustrated by her impotency to effect any real change in the organization.
Repeated defeats revealed the impotency of their strategy against a stronger opponent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - the state of having no power or control
  • Antonym: strength - the state of being strong

Etymology

From Latin impotentia (inability, weakness), from in- (not) + potentia (power).
