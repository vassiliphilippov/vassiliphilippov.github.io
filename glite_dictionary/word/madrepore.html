madrepore

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædrəˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈmædrəˌpɔː / (BrE)
A type of coral with a hard, calcareous skeleton, whose colonies contribute to the formation of reefs and islands.
a stony reef-building coral

Examples

The Great Barrier Reef contains a variety of madrepores that are vital to its ecosystem.
Scientists study madrepore skeletons to learn about ancient ocean conditions.
Many island formations are the result of massive madrepore colonies over centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral - marine invertebrate with a calcareous skeleton

Etymology

From French madrépore, likely from Italian madrepora, from madre (mother) + pora (pore), referring to the porous structure.
