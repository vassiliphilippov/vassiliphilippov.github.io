sard

1. NOUN

/ sɑrd / (AmE), / sɑːd / (BrE)
a deep orange-red variety of chalcedony, used as a semi-precious gemstone
deep orange-red type of chalcedony

Examples

The necklace was adorned with polished sard gemstones.
In ancient times, sard was highly valued for its rich color.
She collects different forms of quartz, including sard and agate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnelian - a reddish variety of chalcedony, similar to sard

Etymology

From Latin 'sardius', from Greek 'sardios', likely referring to the ancient region Sardis.
