guesstimate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛs.tɪ.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɛs.tɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
An estimate made without sufficient information, combining elements of guessing and estimating.
an estimate made by guessing
informal

Examples

The contractor gave a guesstimate of how long the repairs would take.
My guesstimate is that there were around fifty people at the party.
Without all the data, any figure we provide will be just a guesstimate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - a rough judgment or calculation
  • Synonym: guess - an opinion or answer based on little or no evidence

Etymology

Blend of 'guess' and 'estimate,' first recorded in the 1930s.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɛstɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɛstɪˌmeɪt / (BrE)
to make an estimate based on a mixture of guesswork and calculation, usually when precise information is unavailable
estimate roughly by guessing and calculating
informal american

Examples

Can you guesstimate how long the repairs will take?
We had to guesstimate the number of guests attending the event.
She quickly guesstimated the cost of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - to judge or calculate an approximate value

Etymology

Blend of 'guess' and 'estimate'; first recorded in the 1930s in American English.
