to admeasure

1. VERB

/ ædˈmɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ædˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
to determine and allocate the quantity of someone's share, especially of land or property
determine someone's share or portion
legal

Examples

The court will admeasure the compensation among the rightful heirs.
When distributing the estate, the executor must admeasure each beneficiary's portion accurately.
The law requires the government to admeasure land shares fairly during a partition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apportion - to divide and allocate proportionally

Etymology

From Latin admetiri (to measure out), from ad- (to) + metiri (to measure).
