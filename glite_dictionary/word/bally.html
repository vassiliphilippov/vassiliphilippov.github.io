bally

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæli / (AmE), / ˈbæli / (BrE)
(informal, British) Used to emphasize or intensify statements, especially to express annoyance or anger.
informal British intensifier
informal british

Examples

It's a bally shame we missed the last train.
That was a bally stupid thing to do!
He couldn't find his keys and got in a bally state about it.

Synset Examples

what a bally (or blinking) nuisance
a bloody fool
a crashing bore
you flaming idiot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - (informal) Used for emphasis or to express anger, mainly British English
  • Synonym: blinking - (British, informal) Mild intensifier

Etymology

Euphemistic alteration of 'bloody', British English, late 19th century.
