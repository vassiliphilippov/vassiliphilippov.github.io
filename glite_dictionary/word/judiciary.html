judiciary

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃiəri / (BrE)
the system of courts and judges who are responsible for administering justice in a country or state
system of courts and judges

Examples

The independence of the judiciary is vital for upholding the rule of law.
The reforms were intended to make the judiciary more transparent and accountable.
Many countries have a separate judiciary to protect the rights of citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judges - officials who preside over courts
  • Synonym: court - a place where justice is administered

Etymology

From Latin *judiciarius*, from *judicium* (judgment).

2. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃiəri / (BrE)
the system of courts that interprets and applies the law and makes up the judicial branch of government
system of law courts

Examples

The independence of the judiciary is vital to a functioning democracy.
A restructuring of the judiciary was proposed to improve efficiency.
The judiciary plays a crucial role in upholding the rule of law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judicial - relating to courts or judges

Etymology

From Latin 'judiciarius', from 'judicium' (judgment).
