kindjal

1. NOUN

/ kɪnˈdʒɑl / (AmE), / kɪnˈdʒɑːl / (BrE)
a short sword or dagger with a slightly curved blade, traditionally used in the Caucasus region
Caucasian short sword or dagger

Examples

The Russian officer displayed his kindjal with pride during the ceremony.
Museum visitors admired the ornate designs on the ancient kindjals in the display case.
He drew his kindjal swiftly, demonstrating its sharp, curved blade.

Synset Examples

Russian Cossacks usually carried kindjals as their signature weapon.

Etymology

From Russian 'кинжал' (kinzhal), of Turkic origin.
