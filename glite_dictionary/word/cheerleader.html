cheerleader

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪrˌliːdər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəˌliːdə / (BrE)
a person, usually a member of a team, who leads chants and cheers to encourage a crowd and support a sports team at an event
person who leads cheers at sporting events

Examples

She was the captain of the cheerleader squad in her high school.
During halftime, the cheerleader performed a pyramid stunt to excite the crowd.
Many athletes appreciate the energy brought by each cheerleader at games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who gives encouragement or approval to someone or something

Etymology

Formed from 'cheer' + 'leader', first used in early 20th century US sports culture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪrˌlidər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəˌliːdə / (BrE)
A person who enthusiastically and publicly supports a cause, idea, or group, often in a vocal or visible way.
enthusiastic public supporter

Examples

She has always been a prominent cheerleader for environmental reform.
The senator became a vocal cheerleader for the education bill.
Despite criticism, he acted as a cheerleader for the team's decision.

Synset Examples

he has become a cheerleader for therapeutic cloning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with and speaks against a particular cause or idea

Etymology

From 'cheer' + 'leader'; originally referring to someone who leads cheers at sporting events.
