hypnotiser

1. NOUN

/ hɪpˈnɑtaɪzər / (AmE), / hɪpˈnəʊtaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who induces hypnosis in others
a person who induces hypnosis

Examples

The hypnotiser asked the volunteer to focus on the swinging pendulum.
She became a skilled hypnotiser after years of studying the art of hypnosis.
Many people are skeptical about the powers of a hypnotiser in entertainment shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnotist - a person who puts people into a hypnotic state

Etymology

From hypnotise + -er, formed from French *hypnotiser* or English *hypnotize* plus agent suffix.
