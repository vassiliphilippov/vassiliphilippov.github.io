murmuration

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɝːmɚˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɜːməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a low, continuous, and indistinct sound, often made by people speaking softly and without clear articulation
low, indistinct continuous sound

Examples

The murmuration of voices filled the dimly lit library, making it hard to concentrate.
A gentle murmuration drifted from the hallway as the guests awaited the ceremony.
She listened to the murmuration outside her window, unsure whether it was the wind or distant conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, low, and indistinct sound

Etymology

From Latin 'murmuratio' (a murmuring), from 'murmurare' (to murmur).
