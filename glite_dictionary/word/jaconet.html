jaconet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækənɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒækənɪt / (BrE)
a lightweight cotton fabric with a smooth, slightly stiff finish, used in clothing, linings, and bandages
lightweight cotton fabric

Examples

The dress was made from pale blue jaconet that felt cool against her skin.
Nurses in the 19th century often used jaconet for making bandages.
He examined the fine quality of the jaconet before purchasing several yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muslin - a lightweight cotton cloth of plain weave
  • Synonym: percale - a closely woven fine cotton fabric

Etymology

From French *jaconas*, from Hindi *jākunāt*, named after the Indian town of Jagannath where such cloth was made.
