nonappointive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.əˈpɔɪn.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əˈpɔɪn.tɪv / (BrE)
(of an office or position) filled by popular election rather than by appointment.
filled by election, not appointment

Examples

The position of mayor became nonappointive after the new city charter was adopted.
Many prefer nonappointive roles because they believe elections are more democratic.
Some members of the board hold nonappointive offices, having been elected by the public.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appointive - filled by appointment rather than by election

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'appointive' (related to appointment).
