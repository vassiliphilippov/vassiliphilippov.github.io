manipulative

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
Skilful at influencing or controlling others to your own advantage, often in a clever or unfair way.
controlling or influencing others for personal advantage

Examples

She realized her friend could be very manipulative when she wanted something.
The company was accused of using manipulative marketing tactics to target children.
His manipulative behavior eventually alienated everyone around him.

Synset Examples

the early manipulative techniques of a three-year-old child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheming - having a secret or devious plan
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin manipulatus (handled), from manus (hand) and plere (to fill).
