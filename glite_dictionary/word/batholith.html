batholith

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθ.əˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈbæθ.ə.lɪθ / (BrE)
a very large mass of igneous rock that has solidified deep within the earth, typically exposed through erosion
large underground igneous rock mass

Examples

Geologists discovered that the mountain range was actually a single massive batholith.
The exposed batholith formed the backbone of the region's rugged landscape.
Over millions of years, erosion revealed the batholith buried deep within the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluton - a body of intrusive igneous rock

Etymology

From Greek 'bathos' (depth) and 'lithos' (stone), coined in geology in the 19th century.
