Leptotyphlopidae

1. NOUN

/ lɛp.toʊ.tɪˈfloʊ.pɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɛp.təʊ.tɪˈflɒ.pɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, slender snakes, commonly known as slender blind snakes or thread snakes, characterized by their burrowing habits and reduced eyesight
family of slender blind snakes

Examples

The Leptotyphlopidae are found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Leptotyphlopidae family.
Members of the Leptotyphlopidae are often mistaken for earthworms due to their slender appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'leptos' (slender) + 'typhlos' (blind) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛptoʊˌtaɪfloʊˈpɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌlɛptətiˌflɒpɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of slender, blind snakes, commonly known as thread snakes, found in warm regions worldwide.
family of slender blind snakes

Examples

The Leptotyphlopidae are commonly known as thread snakes due to their thin, worm-like appearance.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the Leptotyphlopidae family in South America.
Unlike other snakes, members of the Leptotyphlopidae spend most of their lives underground.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek leptos (slender) + typhlos (blind) + -idae (family suffix).
