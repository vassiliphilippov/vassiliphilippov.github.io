cinematography

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪnəməˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəməˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art or process of making motion pictures by capturing images on film or digital media
art of film photography

Examples

The cinematography in the film was praised for its creative use of lighting and color.
She studied cinematography at a prestigious art school in Los Angeles.
Many critics believe that strong cinematography can elevate even a simple story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filmmaking - the process of making a film or movie
  • Degree Lower: photography - the art or practice of taking and processing photographs

Etymology

From French cinématographie, itself from Greek kinēma (movement) and -graphy (writing).
