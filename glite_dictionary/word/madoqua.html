Madoqua

1. NOUN

/ məˈdoʊkwə / (AmE), / məˈdəʊkwə / (BrE)
A genus of small antelopes native to eastern and northeastern Africa, commonly known as dik-diks.
genus of small African antelopes

Examples

The Madoqua are well adapted to arid environments in eastern Africa.
Biologists studied the social behavior of Madoqua in the wild.
Several species within the Madoqua genus are known for their distinctive elongated snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dik-dik - a small antelope belonging to the genus Madoqua

Etymology

From scientific Latin Madoqua, named in 1836 by J.E. Gray.
