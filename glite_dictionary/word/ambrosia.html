ambrosia

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbroʊʒə / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziə / (BrE)
a mixture of nectar and pollen prepared by worker bees and fed to their larvae
substance fed to bee larvae

Examples

Worker bees produce ambrosia to nourish the developing larvae in the hive.
The composition of ambrosia may vary depending on the flowers available to the bees.
Beekeepers study ambrosia to understand the health of the bee colony.

Etymology

From Greek ambrosia, meaning 'food of the gods'.

3. NOUN

/ æmˈbroʊʒə / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziə / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) the food and drink of the gods, believed to make those who consumed it immortal.
food and drink of the gods in mythology

Examples

In the ancient myths, only the gods could taste ambrosia and enjoy eternal life.
The poet described the exotic fruit as a modern-day ambrosia.
Legends say that even a single drop of ambrosia could bless mortals with immortality.

Etymology

From Greek ambrosia (immortality), related to ambrotos (immortal).

4. NOUN

/ æmˈbroʊʒə / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly North American weedy plants whose pollen is a major cause of hay fever and asthma
plant genus causing hay fever

Examples

Many people suffer from allergies when ambrosia releases its pollen in late summer.
The invasive spread of ambrosia has become a serious environmental and health issue in Europe.
Researchers are developing new ways to control ambrosia to reduce hay fever cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragweed - common name for several plants of the genus Ambrosia

Etymology

From Latin ambrosia, from Greek ambrosia (food of the gods); applied to the plant genus in the 18th century.
