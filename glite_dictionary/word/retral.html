retral

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːtrəl / (AmE), / ˈriːtrəl / (BrE)
situated at, near, or toward the rear or back; posterior.
at or toward the rear; posterior

Examples

The turtle's shell curves upward near its retral edge.
Muscle movement is often described relative to retral or anterior directions.
The scientist noted a small bump in the retral region of the insect's body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anterior - situated toward the front
  • Synonym: posterior - further back in position; of or nearer the rear

Etymology

From Latin 'retralis', from 'retro-' (back, backward) and '-alis' (pertaining to).

2. ADJ

/ ˈriː.trəl / (AmE), / ˈriː.trəl / (BrE)
moving, directed, or tending in a backward direction or toward the rear
moving or directed backward

Examples

The retral movement of the machine was necessary to adjust its position.
Some fish display retral fins that aid in swimming backward.
The design featured both forward and retral facing sensors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anterior - situated at or toward the front

Etymology

From Latin retrālis, from retrō (backward, behind) + -ālis (suffix forming adjectives).
