orthoepist

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθoʊəpɪst / (AmE), / ɔːˈθəʊəpɪst / (BrE)
A person who studies or is an expert in orthoepy, especially one who seeks to reform language pronunciation or spelling to better reflect pronunciation.
expert in pronunciation reform

Examples

The 18th-century orthoepist proposed numerous spelling reforms to standardize English pronunciation.
As an orthoepist, she devoted her career to documenting regional variations in spoken English.
Many orthoepists of the time were also lexicographers concerned with dictionaries and pronunciation guides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthoepy - the correct or accepted pronunciation of words

Etymology

From orthoepy + -ist; orthoepy from Greek 'orthos' (correct) + 'epos' (speech).
