injudiciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dʒʊˈdɪʃ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dʒuːˈdɪʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking good judgment or discretion.
lack of good judgment

Examples

The manager's injudiciousness during the crisis led to disastrous results for the company.
His injudiciousness when handling sensitive information risked damaging important relationships.
Critics pointed out the injudiciousness of launching the new product without adequate market research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: judiciousness - the quality of having or showing good judgment

Etymology

From injudicious (from Latin in- 'not' + judicium 'judgment') + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndʒəˈdɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dʒuːˈdɪʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking good judgment or discretion; unwise conduct or decisions.
lack of good judgment

Examples

The manager's injudiciousness led to significant financial losses for the company.
He regretted his injudiciousness in trusting someone he barely knew.
Public criticism arose due to the government's repeated injudiciousness in policy decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprudence - lack of care for the consequences of an action
  • Antonym: judiciousness - the quality of having or showing good judgment

Etymology

From injudicious + -ness; 'injudicious' from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'judicium' (judgment).
