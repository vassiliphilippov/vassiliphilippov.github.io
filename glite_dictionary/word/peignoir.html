peignoir

1. NOUN

/ peɪnˈwɑr / (AmE), / peɪnˈwɑː / (BrE)
a woman's loose-fitting dressing gown, typically made of a light fabric and worn over nightwear
woman's loose dressing gown

Examples

She slipped into her soft silk peignoir before getting ready for bed.
The actress wore a pale blue peignoir in the early morning scene.
Her grandmother's vintage peignoir was trimmed with delicate lace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing gown - a long, loose robe worn inside the house, especially before dressing or after bathing

Etymology

From French 'peignoir', from 'peigner' (to comb the hair), ultimately from Latin 'pectināre'.
