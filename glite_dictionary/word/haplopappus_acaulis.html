Haplopappus acaulis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæpləˈpæpəs əˈkɔləs / (AmE), / ˌhapləˈpapəs əˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A dark green, erect herb native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada, with stiff leaves in dense tufts and yellow flower heads; sometimes placed in the genus Haplopappus.
herbaceous plant native to northwestern North America

Examples

Haplopappus acaulis grows in rocky soils and blooms in late summer.
Botanists recently reclassified Haplopappus acaulis based on new genetic evidence.
The stiff, tufted leaves of Haplopappus acaulis help it survive harsh mountain climates.

Etymology

From Greek haplo- (single, simple) + pappus (downy appendage of plant seeds); 'acaulis' from Greek meaning 'stemless'.
