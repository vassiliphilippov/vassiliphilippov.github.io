Sennenhunde

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛnɛnˌhʊndə / (AmE), / ˈzɛnənˌhʊndə / (BrE)
Any of four Swiss dog breeds originally used for herding cattle and as farm dogs in the Alps, including the Bernese Mountain Dog, Greater Swiss Mountain Dog, Appenzeller, and Entlebucher.
group of four Swiss mountain dog breeds

Examples

The Sennenhunde are prized for their loyalty and strength in the Swiss Alps.
Among the Sennenhunde, the Bernese Mountain Dog is especially popular as a family pet.
He devoted his research to documenting the history of the four Sennenhunde breeds.

Etymology

From German, literally 'Alpine herdsman's dog,' from 'Senn' (herdsman) + 'Hund' (dog).
