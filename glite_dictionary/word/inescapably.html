inescapably

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnəˈskeɪpəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈskeɪpəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be avoided or escaped; unavoidably or inevitably.
unavoidably; inevitably

Examples

We are inescapably bound by the laws of nature.
His actions led inescapably to his downfall.
Change comes inescapably with the passage of time.

Synset Examples

the situation slid inescapably toward disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - in such a way that it cannot be avoided; certainly to happen
  • Synonym: unavoidably - in a manner that cannot be avoided

Etymology

From 'inescapable' + '-ly', where 'inescapable' derives from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'escapare' (to escape).
