Noctua

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːk.tju.ə / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.tjuː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Noctuidae, whose larvae are commonly known as cutworms.
genus of moths (family Noctuidae)

Examples

The larvae of Noctua species are notorious agricultural pests known as cutworms.
Entomologists have identified a new species within the genus Noctua.
Many Noctua moths are active at night and attracted to artificial lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Noctuidae - a family of moths to which Noctua belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'noctua', meaning owl, referencing the nocturnal habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːk.tju.ə / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.tjʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Noctuidae, whose larvae are known as cutworms.
genus of moths (cutworms)

Examples

The caterpillars of Noctua species are infamous agricultural pests known as cutworms.
A new species of Noctua was discovered in the forests of Western Europe.
Studying the life cycle of Noctua helps scientists develop better pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Noctuidae - the family of moths to which Noctua belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'noctua' meaning 'night owl', used in taxonomy for this nocturnal genus.
