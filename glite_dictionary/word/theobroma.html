Theobroma

1. NOUN

/ θiˈoʊbɹəmə / (AmE), / θiˈɒbɹəmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the family Malvaceae, including the cacao plant which produces cocoa beans.
genus of tropical trees including cacao

Examples

Scientists studied the growth patterns of Theobroma species in different climates.
The seeds of Theobroma are the primary source of cocoa and chocolate around the world.
Botanists debated how many species belong to the genus Theobroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacao - the tropical tree whose seeds are used to make cocoa and chocolate

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + broma (food), meaning 'food of the gods'.
