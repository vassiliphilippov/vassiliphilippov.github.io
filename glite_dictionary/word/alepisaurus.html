Alepisaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪˈpaɪsərəs / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈpaɪsərəs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, scaleless, predatory deep-sea fishes known as lancetfishes, found in tropical and temperate oceans.
genus of predatory deep-sea fishes (lancetfishes)

Examples

The Alepisaurus is notable for its long, slender body and sharp teeth.
Researchers encountered a rare specimen of Alepisaurus during their deep-sea expedition.
Alepisaurus species are known for their voracious predatory habits in tropical ocean depths.

Etymology

From Greek 'alepis' (without scales) and 'sauros' (lizard).
