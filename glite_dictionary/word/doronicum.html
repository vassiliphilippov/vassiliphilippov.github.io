Doronicum

1. NOUN

/ dɔˈrɑː.nɪ.kəm / (AmE), / dɒˈrɒ.nɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian perennial herbs in the daisy family, known as leopard's bane, with yellow, daisy-like flowers.
genus of Eurasian perennial herbs (leopard's bane)

Examples

Several species of Doronicum are popular in gardens for their bright spring flowers.
The botanist identified the plant as a member of the Doronicum genus.
Doronicum grows best in cool, moist soils with partial sunlight.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Arabic doronigi, referring to a plant of this type.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈrɑːnɪkəm / (AmE), / dəˈrɒnɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian perennial plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as leopard's bane, with tuberous or rhizomatous roots and yellow flowers.
genus of Eurasian perennial herbs (leopard's bane)

Examples

Several species of Doronicum are popular in gardens for their bright yellow flowers.
The botanist identified the plant as a member of the Doronicum genus.
Most Doronicum varieties prefer moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.

Etymology

From Latin Doronicum, from Arabic *dorūnīg* (a similar plant).
