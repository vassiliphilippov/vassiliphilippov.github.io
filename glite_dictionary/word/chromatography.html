chromatography

1. NOUN

/ kɹoʊˌmætəˈɡɹɑfɪ / (AmE), / kɹəˌmætəˈɡɹɑːfi / (BrE)
a laboratory technique for separating mixtures into their individual components by passing them through a medium in which the components move at different rates
separation technique used in labs
medical

Examples

Scientists used chromatography to separate the pigments in the leaf extract.
The lab technician demonstrated how chromatography can identify unknown compounds in a sample.
Modern forensic analysis often relies on chromatography for accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distillation - process to separate components based on boiling points
  • Synonym: precipitation - chemical process causing solid to form from a solution

Etymology

From Greek 'khrōma' (color) + 'graphein' (to write), reflecting its original use to separate colored substances.
