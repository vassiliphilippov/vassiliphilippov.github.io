Narthecium americanum

1. NOUN

/ nɑːrˈθiːsiəm əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (AmE), / nɑːˈθiːsiəm əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the Nartheciaceae family, native to wetlands of the eastern United States, especially from New Jersey to South Carolina.
a flowering plant native to eastern U.S. wetlands

Examples

The Narthecium americanum is considered a rare and threatened species in several states.
Botanists conducted a thorough survey of wetlands to monitor the populations of Narthecium americanum.
Preserving habitats is essential for protecting plants like Narthecium americanum from further decline.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Narthecium + Latin americanum, referring to America.
