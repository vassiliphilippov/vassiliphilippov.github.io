althea

1. NOUN

/ ælˈθiːə / (AmE), / ælˈθiːə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Althaea, related to hollyhocks, typically with smaller flowers.
plant of the genus Althaea

Examples

The garden was full of althea bushes in full bloom.
She planted an althea near the porch because of its pretty pink flowers.
Some herbal teas use dried althea roots for their mild soothing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollyhock - a tall plant of the genus Alcea, similar to althea, with larger flowers

Etymology

From Latin *althaea*, from Greek *althaia* (mallow).
