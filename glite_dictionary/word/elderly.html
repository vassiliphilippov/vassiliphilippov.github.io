elderly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛldərli / (AmE), / ˈɛldəli / (BrE)
(of a person) old or advanced in years, typically used to describe someone who is past middle age.
old; advanced in years

Examples

Many elderly people prefer to live independently for as long as possible.
The city opened a new community center for its elderly residents.
After his elderly neighbor fell, he checked in on her every day.

Synset Examples

aged members of the society
elderly residents could remember the construction of the first skyscraper
senior citizen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aged - having lived a specified number of years; old
  • Antonym: young - having lived or existed for only a short time; not old

Etymology

From 'elder' (older person) + '-ly' suffix, used since the late Middle English period.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚli / (AmE), / ˈɛldəli / (BrE)
old people considered as a group; people who are advanced in years collectively.
old people collectively

Examples

The city has built new facilities for the elderly to improve their quality of life.
Special discounts are often available for the elderly at museums and theaters.
Volunteers delivered meals to the homes of the elderly during the winter.

Synset Examples

special arrangements were available for the aged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aged - old people collectively
  • Antonym: youth - young people collectively

Etymology

From 'elder' (older), with the suffix '-ly' forming an adjective; noun use dates to the 19th century.

Synset POS: n
