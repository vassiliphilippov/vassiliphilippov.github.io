Piscidia

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsɪdiə / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪdiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs or small trees producing indehiscent pods with black seeds; the roots and bark are used to make fish poisons.
genus of tropical shrubs or trees, fish poison plant

Examples

The bark of Piscidia has traditionally been used by indigenous people as a natural fish poison.
Botanists recently identified several new species within the Piscidia genus in Central America.
The Piscidia tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in some herbal traditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'piscis' (fish), reflecting its use to poison fish.
