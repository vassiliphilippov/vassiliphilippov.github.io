expat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌspæt / (AmE), / ˈɛkˌspat / (BrE)
a person who lives outside their native country, usually by choice
person living abroad

Examples

Many expats gather at local festivals to celebrate their home cultures.
After ten years as an expat in Japan, she speaks Japanese fluently.
The city has a large expat community from around the world.

Synset Examples

American expatriates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expatriate - a person who lives outside their native country; original, longer form

Etymology

Shortened form of 'expatriate', from Latin 'ex' (out of) + 'patria' (native country).
