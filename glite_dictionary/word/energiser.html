energiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚˌdʒaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒʌɪzə / (BrE)
A device that supplies electrical energy to an electrical circuit or system.
device supplying electrical energy

Examples

The electric fence was powered by a solar energiser to keep the livestock safe.
He tested the battery energiser to ensure it was supplying the correct voltage.
Most modern energisers include features for surge protection and efficient power usage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energizer - device that supplies electrical energy (US spelling)

Etymology

From 'energise' (to supply with energy) + '-er', influenced by 'energizer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚˌdʒaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒʌɪzə / (BrE)
a person who imparts energy, vitality, or enthusiasm to others
person who imparts energy or enthusiasm to others

Examples

She is a real energiser, always lifting the team's spirits during difficult times.
The coach acted as an energiser, motivating the players before every match.
Every group needs an energiser to maintain a positive and productive atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivator - a person who motivates others
  • Synonym: inspirer - one who inspires others

Etymology

From 'energise' + '-er', patterned after similar agent nouns.
