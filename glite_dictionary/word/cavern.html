cavern

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævərn / (AmE), / ˈkævən / (BrE)
a large dark enclosed space, often used metaphorically for anything vast and gloomy
large, dark enclosed space (literal or figurative)

Examples

When the lights went out, the room felt like a cavern filled with shadows.
Her loneliness echoed in the cavern of her empty apartment.
The abandoned warehouse was a cavern of darkness and dust.

Synset Examples

his eyes were dark caverns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cave - a natural underground space, typically larger than a grotto

Etymology

From Middle English caverne, from Latin caverna (cavity, cave).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkævərn / (AmE), / ˈkævən / (BrE)
a large cave or a large chamber within a cave, typically underground and spacious
large cave or cave chamber

Examples

They explored the dark cavern hidden beneath the mountains.
Stalactites hung from the ceiling of the vast cavern.
The guide led us through the winding tunnels into a massive cavern filled with crystals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cave - a natural underground space, typically smaller than a cavern

Etymology

From Latin 'caverna' (cave, hollow place), from 'cavus' (hollow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09262208-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkævərn / (AmE), / ˈkævən / (BrE)
to hollow out an area or space as if making a cavern
hollow out like a cavern

Examples

Over centuries, the river slowly caverned the limestone beneath the mountain.
Workers used specialized equipment to cavern the rock and create storage tunnels.
The artist imagined a world where great machines would cavern out entire landscapes for cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to dig out and remove earth

Etymology

From Latin 'caverna' (cave, cavern), via Old French.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
