Larix laricina

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛər.ɪks ləˈrɪs.ɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈlar.ɪks laˈrɪk.ɪ.nə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous coniferous tree of northern North America, also known as the tamarack or American larch, with a broad conical crown and rust-brown scaly bark.
medium-sized North American larch tree

Examples

The Larix laricina thrives in cold, boggy areas across northern Canada and Alaska.
Every autumn, the needles of Larix laricina turn golden yellow before they fall off.
In wetland restoration projects, Larix laricina is often planted to help stabilize the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarack - an alternative common name for Larix laricina, a North American larch tree
  • Synonym: larch - any tree belonging to the genus Larix, deciduous conifers

Etymology

From Latin 'Larix' (larch) and Neo-Latin 'laricina', from Latin 'larix' plus '-ina' (pertaining to); used in botanical nomenclature.
