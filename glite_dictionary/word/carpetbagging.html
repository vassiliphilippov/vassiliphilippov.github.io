carpetbagging

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːrpɪtˌbæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪtbæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of opportunistically seeking success, especially political office, in a place where one has no local connections or previous residency
opportunistically seeking office in a new locality

Examples

The senator accused his rival of carpetbagging when he moved to the state just before the election.
Many locals resented the carpetbagging businessman who arrived hoping to profit from the recent disaster.
Critics said her carpetbagging campaign showed little understanding of the community's true needs.

Synset Examples

a carpetbag stranger
a carpetbag politician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunism - the practice of taking advantage of opportunities with little regard to principles or consequences

Etymology

From 'carpetbagger,' originally referring to northerners who moved to the South after the American Civil War to seek political or financial advantage, named after the bags they carried.
