Reticulitermes

1. NOUN

/ rəˌtɪkjəˈlɪtərˌmiz / (AmE), / rɪˌtɪkjʊˈlɪtəˌmiːz / (BrE)
A genus of subterranean termites, some species of which are highly destructive to buildings and living trees.
genus of destructive subterranean termites

Examples

Reticulitermes species are common pests in many parts of the world, causing expensive structural damage.
Scientists studied the behavior of Reticulitermes to develop effective termite control methods.
Infestations by Reticulitermes can be difficult to detect until significant damage has occurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - an insect that feeds on wood and can damage structures

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulum' (small net) + Greek 'termes' (woodworm).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌtɪkjəˈlaɪtərˌmiːz / (AmE), / rɪˌtɪkjʊˈlaɪtəˌmiːz / (BrE)
a genus of termites whose species are highly destructive to wooden structures and living trees
a genus of destructive termites

Examples

Several species in the Reticulitermes genus are major pests in urban and rural environments.
The eastern subterranean termite, part of the Reticulitermes genus, can cause significant structural damage.
Researchers studied how Reticulitermes colonies adapt to changing soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social insect that feeds on wood
  • Degree Higher: Coptotermes - another genus of termites, also destructive to wood

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulus' (little net) + 'termes' (woodworm, termite), referring to the net-like pattern of some species’ wings.
