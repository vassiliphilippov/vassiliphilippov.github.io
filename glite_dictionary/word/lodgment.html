lodgment

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒmənt / (BrE)
the condition of being lodged or fixed in a particular place, even temporarily
state of being lodged or stuck

Examples

The sudden lodgment of the ball between the branches surprised everyone.
Doctors were concerned about the lodgment of the clot in the patient's artery.
The heavy rain led to the lodgment of debris in the drainage system.

Synset Examples

the lodgment of the balloon in the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixation - the act of being fixed in place

Etymology

From Old French *lojement* (a place to stay), from *loger* (to lodge).
