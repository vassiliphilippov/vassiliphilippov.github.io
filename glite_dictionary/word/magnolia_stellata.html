Magnolia stellata

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊliə stɛˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə stɛˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to Japan with fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom before the leaves, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
Japanese deciduous shrub with star-shaped white flowers

Examples

The Magnolia stellata in our garden is covered with beautiful white blossoms every spring.
Many landscapers recommend Magnolia stellata for its early flowering and compact size.
She planted a Magnolia stellata near the entrance to enjoy its fragrant blooms each year.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Magnolia' after French botanist Pierre Magnol, and 'stellata' meaning 'star-shaped' in Latin.
