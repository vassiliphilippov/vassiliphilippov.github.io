angel

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
A person of exceptional holiness, regarded as especially kind, pure, or virtuous.
person of great holiness or virtue

Examples

She was an angel to everyone she met, always helping those in need.
People called him an angel for his tireless charity work.
In the community, she is known as a real angel because of her generous spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saint - a person acknowledged as holy or virtuous

Etymology

From Old English engel, from Latin angelus, from Greek angelos (messenger).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪn.dʒəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪn.dʒəl / (BrE)
A spiritual being believed to act as a messenger or attendant of God, often depicted in religions such as Christianity, Judaism, and Islam.
spiritual being; attendant of God

Examples

According to the story, an angel appeared and delivered a message of hope to the shepherds.
She collects figurines of every kind of angel imaginable, from delicate cherubs to mighty guardians.
Many believe that each person has a guardian angel watching over them throughout their life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: archangel - an angel of high rank
  • Antonym: demon - an evil spirit; supernatural being opposed to angels

Etymology

From Old English engel, from Latin angelus, from Greek angelos (messenger).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
a person who provides financial backing for a business venture, especially a start-up
financial backer for a venture

Examples

The start-up was able to launch thanks to an angel who believed in its mission.
After months of searching, they finally found an angel willing to fund their project.
Many entrepreneurs rely on an angel for the initial capital needed to grow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backer - a person who gives financial support to a project or person
  • Synonym: angel investor - a person who invests personal funds in a start-up business

4. NOUN

/ ˈeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
a person who is very kind, helpful, or supportive to others
kind and helpful person

Examples

Thank you for bringing me dinner—you’re such an angel.
He was a real angel during my illness, taking care of everything.
Whenever anyone is in trouble, she acts like an angel and helps out.

Synset Examples

She’s my angel, always there when I need her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, especially financial help

From Late Latin angelus (messenger), from Greek angelos.
