rickey

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪki / (AmE), / ˈrɪki / (BrE)
a mixed drink consisting of sweetened lime juice and soda water, usually combined with liquor such as gin or bourbon
a cocktail with lime juice, soda, and liquor

Examples

She ordered a gin rickey to enjoy on the patio.
The bartender recommended a bourbon rickey on a hot afternoon.
A classic rickey combines soda water, lime juice, and your choice of liquor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocktail - an alcoholic mixed drink

Etymology

Named after Colonel Joe Rickey, a 19th-century American lobbyist reputedly associated with the drink's invention.
