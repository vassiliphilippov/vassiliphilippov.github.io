Sylvia communis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.vi.ə kəˈmjuː.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vɪ.ə kəˈmjuː.nɪs / (BrE)
A greyish-brown Old World warbler with a white throat and underparts, commonly known as the common whitethroat.
a species of Old World warbler (common whitethroat)

Examples

Many birdwatchers hope to spot the elusive Sylvia communis during migration season.
The Sylvia communis is known for its distinctive white throat and melodious song.
Conservationists are studying the breeding habits of Sylvia communis in various European habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common whitethroat - the common name for Sylvia communis, a species of Old World warbler

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sylvia' meaning woodland or forest, and 'communis' meaning common.
