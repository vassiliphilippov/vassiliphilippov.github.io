quickset

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪkˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of a hedge or plant) grown from cuttings or shoots set directly into the ground to take root and grow.
grown from cuttings planted directly in soil

Examples

They decided to plant a quickset hedge to mark the boundary of their field.
The quickset hawthorn provided excellent shelter for wildlife in the garden.
Farmers often use quickset methods when establishing new hedgerows.

Synset Examples

a quickset hawthorn hedge

Etymology

From 'quick' (alive) + 'set' (plant), referring to living shoots set to grow as a hedge.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌsɛt / (BrE)
a living cutting or shoot of a plant, especially hawthorn, set in the ground to form a hedge; also, a hedge made of such cuttings
living plant cuttings for hedges

Examples

The farmer used quickset hawthorn to establish a natural barrier along his property.
In the spring, gardeners planted quickset along the road to create a living hedge.
They replaced the old fence with a row of quickset to encourage wildlife.

Synset Examples

a quickset of a vine planted in a vineyard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - a piece of a plant cut off for rooting or propagation
  • Synonym: hedge - a row of bushes or small trees planted close together

Etymology

From quick (alive) + set (to plant in the ground); 'quick' in this sense means living or rooted.
