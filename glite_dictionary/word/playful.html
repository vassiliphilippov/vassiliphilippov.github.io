playful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈpleɪf(ə)l / (BrE)
full of fun and high spirits; fond of play or games; lively and lighthearted.
full of fun and high spirits

Examples

The kitten was especially playful this morning, chasing shadows across the room.
Despite his age, he maintained a playful attitude at family gatherings.
Her playful joke lightened the mood in the office.

Synset Examples

playful children just let loose from school

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serious - not playful or lively; solemn or earnest

Etymology

Formed from 'play' + '-ful', meaning 'full of play' or 'ready to play'.
