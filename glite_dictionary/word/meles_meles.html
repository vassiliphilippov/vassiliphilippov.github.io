Meles meles

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːliːz ˈmiːliːz / (AmE), / ˈmiːleɪz ˈmiːleɪz / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European badger, a species of badger native to Europe and parts of Asia.
the European badger (scientific name)

Examples

The Meles meles population is an important part of European woodland ecosystems.
Scientists conducted a study on the behavior of Meles meles in its natural habitat.
Unlike other badger species, Meles meles is known for forming large social groups called clans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a nocturnal burrowing mammal with a black-and-white striped face

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Meles' meaning 'badger'.
