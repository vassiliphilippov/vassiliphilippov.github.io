aiglet

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡlət / (AmE), / ˈiːɡlɪt / (BrE)
A metal or plastic sheath at the end of a shoelace or ribbon, used to prevent fraying and make lacing easier.
sheath at end of shoelace or ribbon

Examples

The aiglet on his shoelace broke off, making it hard to thread the lace.
Modern shoelaces usually have plastic aiglets instead of metal ones.
She replaced the ribbon's frayed aiglet with a new one for a neater appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aglet - alternative spelling for aiglet; sheath at end of shoelace

Etymology

From Old French 'aguillette' (small needle), diminutive of 'aguille' (needle), from Latin 'acucula'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡlət / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡlɪt / (BrE)
a decorative metal tag or tip used to fasten or adorn a cord or braid, often found on the shoulder of military uniforms
decorative tag or tip on a cord, often on uniforms

Examples

The general’s uniform was distinguished by golden aiglets on his shoulders.
The ornate aiglet completed the ceremonial appearance of the officer’s braid.
Historical reenactors often pay close attention to small details, such as period-correct aiglets.

Etymology

From Old French *aiguillette* (small needle), diminutive of *aiguille* (needle), from Latin *acus* (needle).
