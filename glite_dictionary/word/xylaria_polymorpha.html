Xylaria polymorpha

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɛəriə ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːrfə / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɑːriə ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
a species of fungus in the genus Xylaria, known for its elongated, finger-like fruiting bodies often called 'dead man's fingers'
a species of fungus with finger-like fruiting bodies

Examples

The distinctive black clusters of Xylaria polymorpha are sometimes mistaken for decaying fingers emerging from the soil.
Scientists discovered Xylaria polymorpha growing at the base of rotting tree stumps in the old forest.
In mycology class, we studied Xylaria polymorpha because of its unusual appearance and ecological role.

Etymology

From modern Latin: 'Xylaria' from Greek 'xylon' (wood), and 'polymorpha' from Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'morphē' (shape).
