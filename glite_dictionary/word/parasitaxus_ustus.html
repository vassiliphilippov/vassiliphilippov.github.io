Parasitaxus ustus

1. NOUN

/ pæɹəˌsaɪtæksəs ˈʌstəs / (AmE), / pæɹəˌsɪtæksəs ˈʌstəs / (BrE)
a rare and endangered parasitic conifer native to New Caledonia, known for being the only parasitic conifer species
rare parasitic conifer of New Caledonia

Examples

Parasitaxus ustus is unique among conifers for its parasitic lifestyle, attaching to the roots of other plants for nutrients.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining population of Parasitaxus ustus due to habitat loss in New Caledonia.
The discovery of Parasitaxus ustus challenged previous assumptions about the diversity of plant life in conifer families.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'parasitos' (parasitic) and 'taxus' (yew); species name 'ustus' is Latin for 'burnt'.
