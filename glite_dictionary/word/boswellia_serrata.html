Boswellia serrata

1. NOUN

/ bɑzˈwɛliə səˈreɪtə / (AmE), / bɒzˈwɛliə səˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
an East Indian tree that yields a resin used medicinally and as incense
East Indian tree yielding medicinal resin

Examples

The resin collected from Boswellia serrata is highly valued in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
Many people burn Boswellia serrata resin during religious ceremonies for its fragrant smoke.
Scientists are studying extracts from Boswellia serrata for their potential anti-inflammatory effects.

Etymology

Genus name Boswellia named after Scottish botanist John Boswell; species name serrata from Latin 'serratus', meaning 'saw-edged', referring to the leaves.
