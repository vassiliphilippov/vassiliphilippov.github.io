Pachysandra

1. NOUN

/ pækɪˈsændrə / (AmE), / pakɪˈsandrə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen perennial subshrubs or herbs, commonly grown as groundcover for shady areas.
genus of evergreen groundcover plants

Examples

Pachysandra is popular as a groundcover in shaded gardens due to its dense foliage.
Gardeners often plant Pachysandra under trees where grass cannot grow.
The thick, glossy leaves make Pachysandra a low-maintenance option for covering large areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxwood - woody evergreen shrubs, often used for hedges (genus Buxus)

Etymology

From modern Latin Pachysandra, from Greek 'pachys' (thick) and 'andros' (man), referring to the thick stamens.
