carancha

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɑːntʃə / (AmE), / kəˈran.tʃə / (BrE)
A South American bird of prey, specifically a type of caracara found in open areas and savannas.
South American caracara bird

Examples

The carancha soared above the pampas, searching for its next meal.
Many ranchers in Argentina consider the carancha both a scavenger and a symbol of the wild.
Photographers were excited to spot a carancha during their trip to the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caracara - a bird of prey in the family Falconidae

Etymology

From Spanish 'carancha', local South American name for the bird.
