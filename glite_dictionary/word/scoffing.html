scoffing

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɒfɪŋ / (BrE)
The action or expression of mocking or showing contempt, typically by making scornful remarks or gestures.
mocking or contemptuous expression

Examples

Her comment was met with open scoffing from the audience.
Despite the scoffing of his classmates, he continued to pursue his unusual hobbies.
The teacher ignored the scoffing and continued with her lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockery - teasing or contemptuous language or behavior directed at a particular person or thing
  • Synonym: derision - contemptuous ridicule or mockery
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From the verb 'scoff', which likely originates from Middle English; related to Middle Low German 'schōff' (mockery).
