dimout

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdɪm.aʊt / (BrE)
a partial reduction of lights in a city or area, especially to make it less visible to enemy aircraft during wartime
partial reduction of lights (wartime blackout)

Examples

During the war, the city enforced a dimout every evening to protect against air raids.
Strict dimout regulations were implemented, requiring businesses to shield their windows.
The dimout gave the streets an eerie, almost deserted appearance at night.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blackout - a complete turning off of all lights to prevent enemy detection

Etymology

Formed from "dim" + "out", popularized during World War II for air-raid safety measures.
