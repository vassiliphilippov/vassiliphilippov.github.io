ripely

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪpli / (AmE), / ˈraɪpli / (BrE)
In a way that shows maturity, full development, or readiness for use or enjoyment.
in a mature or developed manner

Examples

The peaches hung ripely on the branches, glowing in the afternoon sun.
Her ideas were ripely conceived, showing a depth of thought rare among her peers.
The cheese matured ripely, acquiring a rich and complex flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripe - fully matured or developed

Etymology

From 'ripe' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
