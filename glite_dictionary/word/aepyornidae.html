Aepyornidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːpiːˈɔrnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪˈɔːnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, extinct, flightless birds from Madagascar, commonly known as elephant birds.
family of extinct flightless birds from Madagascar

Examples

Fossils of the Aepyornidae suggest they were once the heaviest birds to have lived.
The extinction of the Aepyornidae is believed to have occurred less than a thousand years ago.
Research into the diet of Aepyornidae has shed light on Madagascar's ancient environment.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Ancient Greek aēpys (tall, lofty) and ornis (bird), plus the zoological family suffix -idae.
