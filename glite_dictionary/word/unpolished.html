unpolished

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpɑlɪʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
lacking refinement or sophistication in manners or social behavior; gauche or awkward in social situations.
lacking social refinement; awkward

Examples

Despite his intelligence, Mark's unpolished manner often made business meetings uncomfortable.
Her unpolished behavior at the formal dinner surprised everyone at the table.
He felt awkward and unpolished at his first networking event.

Synset Examples

too gauche to leave the room when the conversation became intimate
their excellent manners always made me feel gauche

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauche - lacking ease or grace; socially awkward
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having a great deal of worldly experience; refined

Etymology

From un- (not) + polished (made smooth, refined).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpɑlɪʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
not made smooth, perfect, or refined; lacking finish or sophistication.
not made smooth or refined

Examples

His unpolished speech revealed a lack of public speaking experience.
The table had an unpolished surface that felt rough to the touch.
She admired the artist's unpolished sketches for their raw honesty.

Synset Examples

dull unpolished shoes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polished - made smooth or refined; perfected

Etymology

Un- (prefix meaning 'not') + polished (from Latin 'politus', past participle of 'polire', meaning 'to polish').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01818131-a
Synset POS: a
