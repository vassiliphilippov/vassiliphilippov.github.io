Uygur

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡə / (BrE)
an alphabetic script, derived from Aramaic, formerly used to write the Uighur language
script used to write Uighur language

Examples

The ancient manuscripts were written in the Uygur script.
Linguists study the Uygur to understand Central Asian history.
The Uygur is an important part of Silk Road scholarly research.

Etymology

From the name of the Uighur people and language; script name referencing the ethnic group.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːˌɡʊr / (AmE), / ˈwiːˌɡʊə / (BrE)
The Turkic language spoken by the Uighur people, primarily in the Xinjiang region of northwestern China.
Turkic language of the Uighur people

Examples

Many students at the university have chosen to study Uygur as part of their linguistics program.
The Uygur language uses a script that is derived from the Arabic alphabet.
She translated the folk songs from Uygur into English for the cultural festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - Relating to the family of languages including Turkish, Uygur, and others

Etymology

From Uighur (ئۇيغۇر), the self-designation of the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06940263-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːɡʊr, ˈuːjɚ / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡʊə, ˈuːjɜː / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Xinjiang and surrounding regions of Central Asia, chiefly speaking the Uighur language
member of a Central Asian Turkic ethnic group

Examples

The Uygur are known for their rich traditions in dance and music.
Many Uygur live in the Xinjiang region of China.
She studied Uygur culture while traveling through Central Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uighur - alternative spelling for the same ethnic group

Etymology

From Turkic *Uyghur*, a self-designation of the people.
