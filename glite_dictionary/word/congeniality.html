congeniality

1. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒiniˈæləti / (AmE), / kənˌdʒiːniˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant, friendly, or agreeable; a congenial nature or disposition
pleasant and friendly quality

Examples

Her congeniality made her popular with both colleagues and clients.
The congeniality of the host helped put everyone at ease during the party.
Despite his talent, his lack of congeniality sometimes made teamwork difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiability - the quality of being friendly and pleasant
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'congenialis' (of the same kind, agreeable) + English '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒiniˈælədi / (AmE), / kənˌdʒiːniˈaləti / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant and compatible with others; friendliness and agreeableness in social interactions
pleasantness and compatibility between people

Examples

Her congeniality made her popular among all her colleagues.
The team thrived thanks to the remarkable congeniality among its members.
A sense of congeniality at the party made everyone feel at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly and kind
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendliness or opposition

Etymology

From Latin 'congenialis' (of the same kind, agreeable).
