Molva molva

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊl.və ˈmoʊl.və / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.və ˈmɒl.və / (BrE)
A species of large, elongated marine fish found in Greenland and northern European waters, often salted and dried as food; also known as the common ling.
a large northern European marine fish (common ling)

Examples

The fishermen caught several Molva molva off the coast of Norway.
Traditional dishes in Scandinavia often feature salted Molva molva.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Molva molva in northern European waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common ling - large marine food fish of the North Atlantic

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Molva' as the genus name; scientific classification for the common ling.
