subvocalizer

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈvoʊ.kəˌlaɪ.zər / (AmE), / sʌbˈvəʊ.kəˌlaɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who silently articulates speech without making audible sounds
someone who mouths words silently

Examples

Many beginning readers are subvocalizers, mouthing words silently as they read.
The teacher encouraged students to become faster readers by reducing their tendency to be subvocalizers.
Even experienced readers may act as subvocalizers when processing unfamiliar vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subvocalization - the process of silently articulating words

Etymology

From 'subvocalize' (to articulate words silently), from 'sub-' (under, below) + 'vocalize'
