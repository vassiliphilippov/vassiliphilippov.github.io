chaeta

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a stiff bristle or hair, typically made of chitin, found especially on annelid worms and some other invertebrates
stiff bristle on invertebrates

Examples

The earthworm's body is covered with tiny chaetae that help it move through soil.
Under the microscope, the segmented worm's chaeta was clearly visible.
Many marine polychaetes use their chaetae for both locomotion and defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seta - a bristle or hairlike structure on animals or plants

Etymology

From Greek *khaite* (long, flowing hair).
