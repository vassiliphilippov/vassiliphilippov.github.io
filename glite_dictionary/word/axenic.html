axenic

1. ADJ

/ eɪˈzɛnɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈzɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a culture of microorganisms) free from contamination by other living organisms; pure.
free from other organisms (microbiology)
medical

Examples

The laboratory maintained an axenic culture to ensure the results were not affected by contaminants.
Obtaining axenic samples is critical for accurate microbiological research.
The scientist confirmed that the sample was axenic before proceeding with the experiment.

Synset Examples

an axenic culture

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'xenos' (stranger, foreigner), indicating 'without strangers'.

2. ADJ

/ eɪˈzɛnɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈzɛnɪk / (BrE)
Free from living microorganisms except for those of a single type; especially used to describe environments or experimental animals maintained under sterile conditions.
free from other microorganisms (sterile)
medical

Examples

The laboratory maintained the mice in axenic conditions to prevent contamination.
Researchers often use axenic cultures to study the effects of a single microorganism.
Successful experiments require axenic techniques to ensure results are not influenced by unknown microbes.

Synset Examples

axenic conditions

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'xenos' (stranger, foreigner).
