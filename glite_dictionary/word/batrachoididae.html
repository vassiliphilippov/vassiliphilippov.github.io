Batrachoididae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌtrækɔɪˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / bəˌtrækɔɪˈdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes known as toadfishes, notable for their large heads and wide mouths, related to anglers and batfishes.
a family of fishes (toadfishes)

Examples

The Batrachoididae are commonly found in shallow coastal waters around the world.
Biologists study the Batrachoididae because of their ability to produce sounds.
Members of the Batrachoididae family are also known as toadfishes due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadfish - common name for members of Batrachoididae

Etymology

From Greek *batrachos* (frog) + *-oid* (resembling) + Latin *-idae* (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌtrækiˈoɪdɪdiː / (AmE), / bəˌtrækɪˈɔɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of fishes known as toadfishes, related to anglers and batfishes, characterized by a scaleless, flattened body and inhabiting coastal waters and estuaries
family of fishes (toadfishes)

Examples

The Batrachoididae family includes several species known for their croaking sounds.
Researchers are studying the unique adaptations of Batrachoididae in brackish environments.
Members of the Batrachoididae are often called toadfishes due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadfish - common name for fishes of the family Batrachoididae

Etymology

From Greek 'batrachos' (frog) + '-oid' (resembling) + Latin '-idae' (family suffix in zoological names).
