Conopodium

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɑːpədiəm / (AmE), / kəˈnɒpədiəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae (Umbelliferae), native to Europe and known for species such as Conopodium majus (pignut).
genus of flowering plants in Apiaceae

Examples

The tubers of Conopodium majus have been eaten as a wild food in some European countries.
Botanists have debated the classification of certain species within the Conopodium genus.
Conopodium is commonly found in hedgerows and grasslands throughout western Europe.

Etymology

From Greek 'kónops' (gnat, mosquito) and 'podion' (little foot), due to the shape of the fruit.

Several herbs in the Conopodium group grow wild across temperate Europe.

Etymology

From Greek konops (gnat, mosquito), referencing the appearance of the plant's parts.
