oxidiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːksɪˌdaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzə / (BrE)
A substance that causes another substance to lose electrons, especially by accepting electrons during a chemical reaction.
substance causing oxidation
british

Examples

The rocket uses liquid oxygen as its primary oxidiser to support combustion.
In chemical reactions, an oxidiser can often be a source of oxygen.
Some cleaning products contain a strong oxidiser that removes tough stains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxidizer - substance that oxidizes another; American English spelling
  • Antonym: reducer - substance that causes reduction by losing electrons

Etymology

From 'oxidize' + '-er', influenced by 'oxide'. Spelling follows British English.
