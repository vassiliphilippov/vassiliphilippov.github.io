prevalence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛvələns / (AmE), / ˈprɛvələns / (BrE)
The condition of being greater in number, amount, or frequency than others.
superiority in numbers or amount

Examples

The prevalence of the species in the area makes it a key part of the ecosystem.
Studies reveal a high prevalence of heart disease among people aged over 60.
There is a clear prevalence of evidence supporting the new theory.

Synset Examples

a preponderance of evidence against the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominance - the state of being dominant or having more power
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

From Latin praevalentia (‘superiority, prevalence’), from praevalēre (‘to prevail’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛvələns / (AmE), / ˈprɛvələns / (BrE)
The fact or condition of being widespread or common in a particular place or at a particular time.
the state of being widespread

Examples

The prevalence of smartphones has changed the way people communicate around the world.
Doctors are concerned about the rising prevalence of obesity among children.
Recent surveys indicate the prevalence of remote work has increased significantly since last year.

Synset Examples

he was surprised by the prevalence of optimism about the future

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rarity - the state of being rare or uncommon

Etymology

From Latin 'praevalentia' (superiority, strength), from 'praevalens' (having greater power).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɛvələns / (AmE), / ˈprɛvələns / (BrE)
(Epidemiology) The proportion of a population found to have a particular disease or condition at a specific time.
proportion of population with a condition
medical

Examples

The prevalence of diabetes has increased significantly over the last decade.
Researchers examined the prevalence of asthma in children living in urban areas.
Accurate data on HIV prevalence is essential for planning effective public health policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidence - the number of new cases of a disease in a population over a specific period

Etymology

From Latin 'praevalentia' (superiority, prevalence), from 'praevalēre' (to be stronger).
