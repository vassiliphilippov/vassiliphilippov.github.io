impressment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɛsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsmənt / (BrE)
the act of forcing people into government service, especially into the navy or armed forces, often without their consent
forcing into government service
legal

Examples

During the eighteenth century, the British navy relied heavily on impressment to fill its ranks.
The practice of impressment caused significant tension between Britain and the United States.
An old law permitted the impressment of able-bodied men into naval service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscription - compulsory enlistment for state service, typically into the armed forces

Etymology

From obsolete English 'impress' (to enforce service), from Latin 'imprimere' (to press into).
