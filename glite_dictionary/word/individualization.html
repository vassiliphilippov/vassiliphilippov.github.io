individualization

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdɪvɪdʒuələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɪvɪdjuːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of distinguishing or recognizing an individual as separate from a group or species
distinguishing an individual from a group

Examples

Modern teaching methods focus on the individualization of instruction for each student's needs.
DNA evidence allows for the individualization of suspects in forensic science.
The success of the treatment depends on the precise individualization of medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: differentiation - the action or process of distinguishing between things
  • Antonym: generalization - making something less specific or more general

Etymology

From 'individual' + '-ization'; related to Latin 'individuum' (indivisible thing).
