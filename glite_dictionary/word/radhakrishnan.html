Radhakrishnan

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɑːdəˈkrɪʃnən / (AmE), / ˌrɑːdəˈkrɪʃnən / (BrE)
Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan (1888–1975), Indian philosopher and statesman who played a prominent role in introducing Indian philosophy to the Western world.
Indian philosopher and statesman (1888–1975)

Examples

Radhakrishnan argued that philosophy should serve as a bridge between different cultures and religions.
Many universities honor Radhakrishnan for his contributions to both education and interfaith dialogue.
In 1962, Radhakrishnan became the President of India, furthering his work on peace and unity.

Etymology

From the personal name of Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, prominent Indian philosopher and former President of India.
