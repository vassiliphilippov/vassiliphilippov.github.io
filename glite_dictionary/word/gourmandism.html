gourmandism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊrˌmɑndɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡʊəməndɪzəm / (BrE)
the enjoyment and practice of eating and appreciating good food, often to excess
love of eating and fine food

Examples

His gourmandism was famous among friends, who knew his passion for decadent feasts and rare delicacies.
There is a fine line between culinary appreciation and gourmandism.
Critics claimed the royal court's gourmandism set a poor example for the nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating
  • Antonym: abstinence - the practice of restraining oneself from indulging, especially in food

Etymology

From French 'gourmandisme', from 'gourmand' (glutton, gourmet).
