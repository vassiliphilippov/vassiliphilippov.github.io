distasteful

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈteɪst.fəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪst.fəl / (BrE)
causing dislike or disgust; unpleasant or offensive to the senses or mind
causing dislike or disgust

Examples

I found her distasteful remarks hard to ignore during the meeting.
The menu included a distasteful dish that most guests avoided.
He considers lying to be a distasteful practice in any situation.

Synset Examples

a disgusting smell
distasteful language
a loathsome disease
the idea of eating meat is repellent to me
revolting food
a wicked stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offensive - causing someone to feel deeply hurt, upset, or angry
  • Synonym: repulsive - arousing intense distaste or disgust
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From 'distaste' + '-ful', modeled after 'tasteful' as its opposite.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈteɪstfəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪstfəl / (BrE)
having an unpleasant taste or smell; not pleasing to the senses of taste or smell.
unpleasant in taste or smell

Examples

The leftovers had become distasteful and had to be thrown away.
I found the medicine quite distasteful due to its bitter flavor.
Her expression showed that she considered the cheese's odor distasteful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpalatable - not pleasant to taste; unpleasant in flavor
  • Antonym: tasty - having a pleasant, distinct flavor

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + tasteful, meaning 'lacking taste'.
