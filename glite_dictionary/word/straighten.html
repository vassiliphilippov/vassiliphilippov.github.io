to straighten

1. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtn / (BrE)
to make or become straight or orderly; to remove bends, curves, or disorder
make or become straight

Examples

He paused to straighten the pile of papers on his desk.
The yoga instructor asked everyone to straighten their backs and relax their shoulders.
After the storm, we had to straighten the fence that was leaning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bend - to form a curve or angle; the opposite of straightening
  • Synonym: to align - to put things into a straight line or correct position

/ ˈstreɪtən / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtn / (BrE)

3. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən / (BrE)
to make (something, especially a place or things) tidy or put in order
make tidy or put in order

Examples

Before guests arrive, I need to straighten the living room.
He always straightens his desk before starting work each day.
Could you please straighten the books on that shelf?

Synset Examples

Tidy up your room!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tidy - to put things in order; to clean up
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disordered

Etymology

From Middle English 'streighten', from Old English 'streccan' (to stretch).

5. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən / (BrE)
to make or become straight; to put (something) into a straight or proper position
make or become straight

Examples

She took a moment to straighten the picture frame on the wall.
He paused to straighten his tie before entering the meeting room.
The dentist asked her to straighten her back during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bend - to make something curved or change its shape from straight to bent
  • Synonym: to align - to adjust into proper position or order, especially in a straight line

Etymology

From the adjective 'straight' + suffix '-en' (forming verbs).
