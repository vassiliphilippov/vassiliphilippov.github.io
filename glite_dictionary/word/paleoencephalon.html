paleoencephalon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊɛnˈsɛfəlˌɑn / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
the phylogenetically older parts of the brain, excluding the cerebral cortex, responsible for basic life functions and instinctive behaviors
ancient parts of the brain (excluding cortex)
medical

Examples

Damage to the paleoencephalon can affect basic survival instincts in mammals.
The paleoencephalon includes structures such as the brainstem and limbic system.
Researchers believe the paleoencephalon controls many autonomic functions essential for life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neocortex - the more recently evolved outer layer of the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'palaiós' (ancient) and 'enkephalos' (brain).
