Leyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪti / (AmE), / ˈleɪti / (BrE)
a decisive World War II battle in 1944 on Leyte Island, Philippines, marking the beginning of the recapture of the Philippines by Allied forces and the first use of kamikaze attacks by the Japanese
World War II battle on Leyte Island, Philippines

Examples

The Battle of Leyte was a turning point in the Pacific campaign during World War II.
Many historians consider Leyte one of the most significant Allied victories in the Philippines.
At Leyte, Japanese forces launched fierce resistance but were ultimately defeated by the invading American troops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Leyte Gulf - major naval battle connected to the Leyte landings in World War II
  • Degree Lower: kamikaze - Japanese suicide attack aircraft first used in the Battle of Leyte

Etymology

From the name of Leyte Island in the Philippines.
