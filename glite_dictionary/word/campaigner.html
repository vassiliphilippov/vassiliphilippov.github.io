campaigner

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpeɪnər / (AmE), / kæmˈpeɪnə / (BrE)
a politician who is seeking election to public office by conducting a campaign
person seeking election to office

Examples

The campaigner gave a passionate speech at the town hall meeting.
As a dedicated campaigner, she traveled extensively to meet with voters across the state.
The campaigner promised to address healthcare reform if elected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candidate - a person seeking to be elected to a position

Etymology

From campaign + -er, first used in the 19th century for those involved in organized political efforts.
