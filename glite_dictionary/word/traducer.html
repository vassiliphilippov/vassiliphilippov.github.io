traducer

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdusər / (AmE), / trəˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a person who speaks badly of someone, especially by telling lies, in order to damage their reputation
one who slanders or defames another

Examples

The politician's reputation was destroyed by a persistent traducer in the press.
Despite his critics and traducers, he maintained his good name.
She took legal action against her traducer for spreading false rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamer - a person who harms someone's reputation by false statements
  • Synonym: libeler - a person who publishes false statements that damage a person's reputation
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who backs or defends someone

Etymology

From Latin 'traducere' (to lead across; to transfer blame), via Old French and late Middle English.
