eparch

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌpɑrk / (AmE), / ˈiːˌpɑːk / (BrE)
the governor or prefect of an eparchy in ancient Greece or in the Byzantine Empire; a provincial administrator.
provincial governor in ancient Greece or Byzantium

Examples

The eparch was responsible for administering justice in the province.
During the Byzantine era, the eparch of Constantinople oversaw the city's markets.
An edict from the eparch was treated as law in the eparchy.

Etymology

From Greek eparchos (ἔπαρχος), from epi- (over) + archos (ruler).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːpɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɑːk / (BrE)
a bishop or metropolitan who is in charge of an eparchy in the Eastern Church
bishop in charge of an eparchy

Examples

The newly appointed eparch visited every parish in his eparchy during his first year.
Each eparch is responsible for overseeing the spiritual and administrative needs of his community.
The synod convened all the eparchs to discuss important matters affecting the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishop - senior member of the Christian clergy, usually in charge of a diocese
  • Synonym: metropolitan - a bishop of higher rank in charge of an ecclesiastical province

Etymology

From Greek eparchos (governor of a province), from epi- (upon) + archon (ruler).
