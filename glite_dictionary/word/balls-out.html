balls-out

1. ADJ

/ bɔlz aʊt / (AmE), / bɔːlz aʊt / (BrE)
Extremely bold, reckless, or energetic, showing maximum effort or intensity.
extremely bold or reckless; at full intensity
slang vulgar american

Examples

The team played balls-out to win the championship, risking everything.
She went balls-out on her final presentation and impressed the entire panel.
His balls-out attitude often got him into trouble but sometimes led to great success.

Synset Examples

His balls-out approach to the project impressed everyone.
They went balls-out on the marketing campaign.
The team was balls-out during the last quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all-out - using all available resources or effort; full-scale

Etymology

Originating as American slang, possibly from the 'balls out' position of engine governors (spherical weights) indicating maximum speed; later given a vulgar reinterpretation.
