Canticles

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæn.tɪ.kəlz / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɪ.kəlz / (BrE)
another name for the Song of Solomon, a book of the Old Testament consisting of a series of love poems traditionally attributed to Solomon.
biblical book; Song of Solomon

Examples

Many scholars study Canticles to interpret its poetic imagery and allegorical significance.
Some churches read passages from Canticles during wedding ceremonies to celebrate love and commitment.
In literary discussions, Canticles is often referenced as a prime example of biblical poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Song of Solomon - another name for Canticles, a book of love poems in the Old Testament
  • Synonym: Song of Songs - another name for Canticles, biblical book of love poetry

Etymology

From Latin 'Canticum' (song, chant), pluralized in English to refer to the biblical book.
