Centaurus

1. PROPN

/ sɛnˈtɔr.əs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔː.rəs / (BrE)
a prominent constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near the Southern Cross
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

Centaurus contains Alpha Centauri, one of the brightest stars in the night sky.
The ancient Greeks named the constellation Centaurus after the mythological centaur.
From Australia, Centaurus is clearly visible during autumn and winter evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crux - a nearby constellation also known as the Southern Cross

Etymology

From Latin 'Centaurus', meaning centaur, referencing the mythological creature.
