Aster ericoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər ɛrɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈæstə ɛrɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a North American perennial plant with many branches, heath-like leaves, and small, star-shaped white flowers; also known as heath aster.
a North American perennial with white star-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden was brightened by the blooming Aster ericoides every autumn.
Many pollinators are attracted to Aster ericoides because of its abundant white flowers.
He planted Aster ericoides along the path to create a natural, wildflower look.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Aster' (star) and Latin 'ericoides' (resembling heath), referring to its foliage.
