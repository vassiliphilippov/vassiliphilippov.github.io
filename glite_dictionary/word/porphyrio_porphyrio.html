Porphyrio porphyrio

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˈfɪri.oʊ pɔrˈfɪri.oʊ / (AmE), / pɔːˈfɪrɪəʊ pɔːˈfɪrɪəʊ / (BrE)
A species of large, brightly colored bird known as the purple gallinule, native to southern Europe and parts of Africa and Asia.
species of bird; purple gallinule

Examples

The Porphyrio porphyrio is recognized for its vivid plumage and striking red bill.
Birdwatchers in Spain often seek out the elusive Porphyrio porphyrio in wetland habitats.
Conservation efforts have helped the population of Porphyrio porphyrio recover in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple gallinule - Common name for Porphyrio porphyrio

Etymology

From Latin, where 'porphyrio' refers to a purple bird, from Greek 'porphyra' (purple).
