claustrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːstrəm / (AmE), / ˈklɔːstrəm / (BrE)
a thin layer of grey matter in the brain located next to the lenticular nucleus, involved in integrating information across different regions
thin layer of grey matter in the brain
medical

Examples

The claustrum has been studied for its potential role in consciousness and sensory integration.
Damage to the claustrum can affect communication between different parts of the brain.
Researchers are still trying to fully understand the functions of the claustrum in the central nervous system.

Etymology

From Latin 'claustrum' (a bolt, bar, enclosure).
