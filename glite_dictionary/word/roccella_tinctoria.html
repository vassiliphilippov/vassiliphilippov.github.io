Roccella tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈsɛlə tɪŋkˈtɔːriə / (AmE), / rɒˈsɛlə tɪŋkˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A species of lichen used as a source of the dyes archil and litmus.
lichen used for archil and litmus dyes

Examples

Some natural dyes, such as those derived from Roccella tinctoria, were highly prized in centuries past.
The purple color of archil dye was originally extracted from the lichen Roccella tinctoria.
Scientists isolated litmus from Roccella tinctoria to use in chemical testing.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Roccella,' species 'tinctoria' meaning 'used for dyeing.'
