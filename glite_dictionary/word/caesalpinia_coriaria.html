Caesalpinia coriaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə ˌkɔːriˈɑːriə / (AmE), / ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə ˌkɒrɪˈɑːriə / (BrE)
a small thornless tree or shrub native to tropical America, whose seed pods are used as a source of tannin
tree/shrub native to tropical America, source of tannin

Examples

The bark and pods of Caesalpinia coriaria are important in the leather tanning industry.
Botanists collected several specimens of Caesalpinia coriaria during their expedition.
The Caesalpinia coriaria tree thrives in the dry forests of Central America.

Etymology

Latin binomial; from the genus Caesalpinia, named after the Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino, and species from the Spanish word 'coriaria' for a similar plant.
