to evangelise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒə.laɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.laɪz / (BrE)
to try to spread the teachings of Christianity to others, especially by preaching or missionary work
promote or spread Christianity

Examples

The church sent volunteers to evangelise in remote villages.
Many television programs aim to evangelise to a broader audience.
She devoted her life to evangelising among the local population.

Synset Examples

The missionaries evangelized the Pacific Islanders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proselytize - to try to convert someone to a religion or belief

Etymology

From Greek euangelizein (to bring good news), via Late Latin evangelizare.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.laɪz / (BrE)
to preach the Christian gospel or try to convert someone to Christianity
preach the Christian gospel
british

Examples

The church sent missionaries to evangelise in remote villages.
She felt called to evangelise and share her faith with others.
Many use modern media to evangelise beyond traditional church walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proselytize - to try to convert someone to a faith or belief

Etymology

From Greek euangelizesthai (to bring good news), via Latin evangelizare.
