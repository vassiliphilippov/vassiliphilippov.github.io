hardtack

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌtæk / (BrE)
a very hard, dry, unsalted biscuit or bread, formerly used as a staple food on ships and in military rations
very hard dry biscuit or bread, ship's staple

Examples

During the long sea voyage, the sailors survived mainly on hardtack and salted meat.
Civil War soldiers complained about the taste and texture of hardtack in their rations.
The museum displayed a piece of nineteenth-century hardtack found in a shipwreck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biscuit - a small baked unleavened cake, typically crisp
  • Synonym: cracker - a thin, crisp, unsweetened baked product

Etymology

From 'hard' + 'tack', with 'tack' derived from nautical slang for food.
