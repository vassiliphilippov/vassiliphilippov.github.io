architrave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkɪˌtreɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtreɪv / (BrE)
a molded frame or band surrounding a doorway, window, or other opening, especially in classical architecture.
molding around a door or window

Examples

The ornate architrave around the entrance caught everyone's attention.
As the carpenter worked, he carefully installed the architrave above the window.
Many classical buildings feature a decorative architrave as part of their interior trim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lintel - a horizontal support across the top of a door or window

Etymology

From Italian 'architrave', from Latin 'architravus', from Greek 'arche' (chief) + Latin 'trabs' (beam).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃɪˌtreɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃɪtreɪv / (BrE)
the lowest part of an entablature in classical architecture, resting directly on the capitals of columns
lowest part of an entablature above columns

Examples

The decorative architrave supports the frieze and cornice above the ancient columns.
Each column bore an ornately carved architrave that highlighted the entrance to the hall.
Restorers carefully cleaned the architrave to reveal its original stonework details.

Etymology

From Italian 'architrave', from Latin 'archi-' (chief, principal) + 'trabs' (beam).
