earned

1. ADJ

/ ɝːnd / (AmE), / ɜːnd / (BrE)
gained or acquired as a result of one's own effort, action, or merit, rather than by chance or inheritance.
gained through effort or merit

Examples

She received an earned promotion after years of dedicated work.
The team celebrated their earned victory with great enthusiasm.
His earned reputation for fairness brought him many loyal clients.

Synset Examples

a well-earned reputation for honesty
earned income
an earned run in baseball

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unearned - not gained through effort or action; received without working for it

Etymology

Past participle of 'earn', from Old English *earnian* (to earn).
