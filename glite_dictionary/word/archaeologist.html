archaeologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.kiˈɑː.lə.dʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kiˈɒl.ə.dʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts and other physical remains
a person who studies ancient human history

Examples

The archaeologist carefully brushed away the dirt to reveal an ancient artifact.
After years of study, she became an expert archaeologist specializing in Roman ruins.
The archaeologist gave a fascinating lecture on prehistoric cave paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropologist - a person who studies humans, past and present
  • Synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaiologia' (study of ancient things), via French and Latin.
