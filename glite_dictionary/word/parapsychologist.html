parapsychologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæɹəsaɪˈkɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌpærəsaɪˈkɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies paranormal psychological phenomena such as telepathy, clairvoyance, and psychokinesis
someone who studies paranormal phenomena

Examples

The parapsychologist conducted experiments to test claims of telepathic communication.
Many parapsychologists gather at conferences to discuss their findings on extrasensory perception.
A noted parapsychologist wrote a book about the scientific investigation of hauntings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychologist - a person who studies or practices psychology

Etymology

Formed from 'para-' (beyond) and 'psychologist,' referring to the study of phenomena beyond traditional psychology.
