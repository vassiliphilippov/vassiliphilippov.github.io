receding

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsidɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a hairline, chin, etc.) gradually moving away from its original position, especially becoming higher or less prominent.
gradually moving back (e.g. hairline)

Examples

As he got older, his receding hairline became more noticeable.
The artist skillfully captured the man's receding chin in the portrait.
Worries about a receding hairline are common among men in their thirties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreating - gradually moving back or withdrawing
  • Antonym: advancing - moving forward, opposite of receding

Etymology

Present participle of 'recede', from Latin 'recedere' (to go back, withdraw).
