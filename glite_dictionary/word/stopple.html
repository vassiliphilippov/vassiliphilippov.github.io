stopple

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑpəl / (AmE), / ˈstɒpəl / (BrE)
an object used to close or block the opening of a container or hole tightly
object for tightly blocking an opening

Examples

He removed the stopple from the bottle and poured a glass of wine.
The sink was leaking until a rubber stopple was put in the drain.
She lost the stopple that sealed her antique decanter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper - an object used to seal a container or hole

Etymology

From Middle English 'stopel', from Middle Dutch 'stoppel' (stopper, bung).

2. VERB

/ ˈstɑːpəl / (AmE), / ˈstɒpəl / (BrE)
to close or seal something with or as if with a stopper
close with a stopper

Examples

He carefully stoppled the jug to preserve the scent of the wine.
The pharmacist stoppled the vial before handing it to the nurse.
To avoid spillage, make sure to stopple every bottle tightly after use.

Synset Examples

She stoppered the wine bottle
The mothers stoppered their babies' mouths with pacifiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper - to close or seal with a stopper

Etymology

From Middle English 'stoplen', from 'stopple' (stopper), related to 'stop'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01223200-v
Synset POS: v
