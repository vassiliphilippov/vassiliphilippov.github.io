carcajou

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrkəˌʒuː / (AmE), / ˌkɑːkəˈʒuː / (BrE)
A stocky, shaggy-coated, carnivorous mammal (the wolverine) found in northern North America.
North American carnivorous mammal (wolverine)

Examples

The trapper spotted a carcajou prowling near his camp in the morning light.
Legends often tell of the carcajou's cunning and strength in the northern wilderness.
Few animals can intimidate a carcajou when defending its territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolverine - another name for the carcajou, a large, fierce member of the weasel family

Etymology

From Canadian French "carcajou", likely from Algonquian languages (compare Innu-aimun "kuekuatsheu").
