abscission

1. NOUN

/ æbˈsɪʃən / (AmE), / æbˈsɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of cutting off or separating something, especially by natural or surgical means
the act of cutting off or separation

Examples

The abscission of the branch was necessary to save the tree from disease.
During surgery, abscission of the tumor was performed with great precision.
In autumn, leaf abscission is triggered by changes in daylight and temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or off

Etymology

From Latin abscissio (a cutting off), from abscindere (to cut off).

2. NOUN

/ æbˈsɪʃən / (AmE), / æbˈsɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the natural process by which a plant sheds its leaves, flowers, or fruit, usually after forming a scar at the detachment point
shedding of plant parts
medical

Examples

The abscission of leaves is a key part of a tree's annual cycle.
After pollination, abscission occurs to separate the mature fruit from the plant.
Researchers studied the chemical signals that trigger abscission in grapevines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shedding - the process of discarding something, especially parts of a plant or an animal's skin

Etymology

From Latin abscissio (a cutting off), from abscindere (to cut off).
