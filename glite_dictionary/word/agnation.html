agnation

1. NOUN

/ æɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / æɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the line of descent or kinship traced exclusively through the male side of the family
kinship traced through the male line

Examples

In some cultures, inheritance follows the principle of agnation, passing only through the male descendants.
The royal family's agnation determined who would next take the throne.
Anthropologists study both agnation and cognation to understand family structures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cognation - kinship traced through the female or both parental lines

Etymology

From Latin 'agnatio', from 'agnatus' (male relative).
