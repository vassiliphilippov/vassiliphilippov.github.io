Cycadophyta

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌkædəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / saɪˌkadəˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
A division or class of palmlike gymnospermous plants, including the surviving order Cycadales and various extinct orders.
division of palmlike gymnosperms

Examples

Fossils show that Cycadophyta were much more widespread during the Mesozoic era.
Many species within Cycadophyta have become extinct, with only the order Cycadales surviving today.
Botanists debate whether Cycadophyta constitutes a natural group or a collection of similar lineages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cycadales - an order of cycad plants

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Cycad-' (from cycas, a genus of cycads) + '-phyta' (from Greek 'phuton' meaning plant).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌkædəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / saɪˌkadəˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
a division of palmlike gymnosperm plants, including both the surviving cycads and several extinct orders; sometimes treated as a class or alternatively as a subdivision
division of palmlike gymnosperm plants

Examples

The fossil record indicates that Cycadophyta were much more diverse during the Mesozoic era.
Modern cycads are the only living members of the Cycadophyta.
Botanists debate whether Cycadophyta should be classified as a class or a division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a type of palmlike gymnosperm plant, part of Cycadophyta

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek kykas (cycad) and phyton (plant).
