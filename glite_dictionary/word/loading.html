loading

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
The ratio of the gross weight of an airplane to a factor that determines its lift, used in aeronautics to assess aircraft performance.
aeronautics: ratio of aircraft weight to lift factor

Examples

The aircraft's loading must be carefully calculated before every flight.
Engineers examined the loading to ensure the new design could support higher weights.
Improper loading can negatively affect the lift and performance of an airplane.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the weight or force that is supported or carried by something, especially a structure or vehicle
weight or force supported by something

Examples

The bridge collapsed under excessive loading during the storm.
Engineers must calculate structural loading before construction begins.
The truck's maximum loading capacity is clearly marked on its side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: load - amount of weight carried or supported; burden
  • Antonym: unloading - the process of removing a load or weight from something

Etymology

From the verb 'load' + '-ing', Middle English, from Old English 'lad'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of putting goods, cargo, or materials onto or into a vehicle, ship, or container
act of putting goods onto or into something

Examples

The loading of the cargo onto the truck was completed by noon.
Strict safety protocols are followed during loading at the port.
Due to the weather, the loading took much longer than usual.

Synset Examples

the loading took 2 hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unloading - the act or process of removing a load from a vehicle, ship, or container

Etymology

From the verb 'load' + -ing suffix, traceable to Old English 'hladan' (to load, heap up).
