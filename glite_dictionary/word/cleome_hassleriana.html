Cleome hassleriana

1. NOUN

/ kliˈoʊmi hɑːsləˈriːənə / (AmE), / kliːˈəʊmiː hasləˈriːənə / (BrE)
A plant native to South America, cultivated for its long-lasting spider-shaped flowers that range from white to pink-purple, commonly known as spider flower.
ornamental plant with spider-shaped flowers

Examples

Cleome hassleriana is a favorite among gardeners for its tall stems and showy blooms.
Many butterfly species are attracted to the nectar of Cleome hassleriana flowers.
The pink and white blossoms of Cleome hassleriana add color to summer borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider flower - common name for Cleome hassleriana

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name Cleome from Greek; species named after Swiss botanist Émile Hassler.
