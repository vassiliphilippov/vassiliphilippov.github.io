thinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who thinks carefully and deeply about things, especially when making decisions or forming opinions
person who thinks deeply

Examples

She is a thoughtful thinker who always considers every option before making a decision.
Great thinkers throughout history have shaped the way we understand the world.
He is known as a creative thinker in his field, constantly coming up with innovative solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy, especially as an academic discipline
  • Degree Lower: dreamer - a person who dreams or is idealistic, often with less emphasis on reason

Etymology

From Old English 'þencan' (to conceive in the mind, consider).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who is known for deep or intellectual thought, especially someone regarded as influential in their field
person known for intellectual thought

Examples

Albert Einstein is often celebrated as one of the greatest thinkers of the twentieth century.
Many thinkers have tried to answer fundamental questions about the nature of reality.
The Renaissance produced a remarkable number of influential thinkers across Europe.

Synset Examples

the great minds of the 17th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy, especially as an academic discipline
  • Synonym: intellectual - a person possessing a highly developed intellect
  • Antonym: doer - a person who actively does things, often contrasted with someone who just thinks
