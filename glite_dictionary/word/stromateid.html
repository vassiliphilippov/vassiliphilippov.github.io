stromateid

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈmeɪtiːd / (AmE), / strɒˈmeɪtɪɪd / (BrE)
a small marine fish with a short, compressed body and weak spines, belonging to the family Stromateidae
small marine fish of the Stromateidae family

Examples

The stromateid is often found in warm, shallow coastal waters around the world.
Ichthyologists studied the stromateid population to assess their breeding habits.
Several species of stromateids are commercially important as food fish.

Etymology

From New Latin Stromateidae, the scientific name for the fish family.
