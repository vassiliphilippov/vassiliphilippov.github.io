pectin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛktɪn / (BrE)
a water-soluble substance found in ripe fruits and some vegetables, used to thicken jams and jellies
fruit-derived thickening agent

Examples

The recipe requires a high level of pectin to set the jelly properly.
Apples have naturally high pectin content, making them ideal for homemade jams.
Commercial pectin is often added to fruit preserves to achieve the right consistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelatin - a protein used as a gelling agent, especially in desserts and candies

Etymology

From Greek pēktos (congealed, curdled) via French pectine.
