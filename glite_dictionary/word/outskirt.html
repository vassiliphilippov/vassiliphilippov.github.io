outskirt

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌskɝt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.skɜːt / (BrE)
an area at the edge of a city or town, far from its center
area at the edge of a city or town

Examples

New housing developments have appeared on the outskirts of town.
The airport is located on the outskirts, several miles from the city centre.
Wildlife is often seen roaming the outskirts during early mornings.

Synset Examples

they built a factory on the outskirts of the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suburb - an outlying residential area of a city
  • Antonym: center - the central part of a city or town

Etymology

Formed from 'out' + 'skirt', meaning the outer edge; 'skirt' from Old Norse 'skyrta' (shirt), figuratively meaning an edge or border.
