unspoken

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspoʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈspəʊkən / (BrE)
not expressed in words; not stated aloud or made explicit
not stated aloud or made explicit

Examples

There was an unspoken rule in the office that everyone arrived before nine o'clock.
They shared an unspoken understanding that things would soon change.
His decision was influenced by unspoken expectations from his family.

Synset Examples

the unexpressed terms of the agreement
things left unsaid
some kind of unspoken agreement
his action is clear but his reason remains unstated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implicit - implied though not plainly expressed
  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for doubt

Etymology

From un- (not) + spoken (past participle of speak); patterned after similar formations meaning 'not said'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspoʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈspəʊkən / (BrE)
not expressed in words; not said aloud but understood or felt.
not expressed in words; not spoken

Examples

There was an unspoken agreement between them that they would not discuss the topic again.
Her unspoken feelings for him were obvious to everyone in the room.
Much of their communication was unspoken, conveyed through glances and silence.

Synset Examples

a mute appeal
a silent curse
best grief is tongueless
the words stopped at her lips unsounded
unspoken grief
choking exasperation and wordless shame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsaid - not spoken or expressed in words
  • Antonym: spoken - expressed in words; verbalized

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'spoken' (past participle of 'speak').
