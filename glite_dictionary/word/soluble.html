soluble

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːljəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒljʊbəl / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to be dissolved in a liquid, especially water.
able to be dissolved in a liquid

Examples

Salt is soluble in water and dissolves quickly when stirred.
Sugar is highly soluble, which is why it disappears so easily in tea.
Not all vitamins are soluble in water; some require fat to be absorbed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insoluble - unable to be dissolved in a liquid

Etymology

From Latin solubilis (able to be loosened or dissolved), from solvere (to loosen).

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːljəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒljʊbəl / (BrE)
(of a problem or mystery) capable of being solved or explained.
able to be solved or explained

Examples

The committee believes the issue is soluble with more discussion and planning.
Many scientific mysteries are difficult but ultimately soluble.
She found the riddle surprisingly soluble once she had all the facts.

Synset Examples

the puzzle is soluble

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insoluble - impossible to solve or explain

Etymology

From Latin solubilis (that can be loosened or dissolved), from solvere (to loosen, solve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02273901-a
Synset POS: a
