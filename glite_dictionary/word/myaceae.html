Myaceae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / maɪˈeɪ.si.i / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks that includes certain types of clams.
family of clams (bivalve mollusks)

Examples

The Myaceae family includes several species of saltwater clams.
Researchers studied the distribution of Myaceae along the coast.
Shell collectors often seek rare specimens from the Myaceae family.

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Myaceae*, from Greek *mya* (a kind of shellfish or clam) + -aceae (standard suffix for family names in taxonomy).
