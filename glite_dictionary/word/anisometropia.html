anisometropia

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪˌsoʊməˈtroʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌænɪˌsəʊməˈtrəʊpɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which the two eyes have unequal refractive power, causing different focusing abilities
unequal refractive power in the eyes
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist diagnosed her with anisometropia after the eye exam.
Untreated anisometropia can lead to vision problems such as amblyopia in children.
Correcting anisometropia often requires different lens prescriptions for each eye.

Etymology

From Greek anisos (unequal) + metron (measure) + -opia (vision).
