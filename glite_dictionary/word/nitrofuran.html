nitrofuran

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪ.troʊˈfjʊr.æn / (AmE), / ˌnɪ.trəʊˈfjʊə.ræn / (BrE)
A chemical compound derived from furan, used as an antibacterial agent to inhibit bacterial growth.
antibacterial compound derived from furan
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a nitrofuran antibiotic to treat the patient's urinary tract infection.
Certain nitrofuran compounds are used in veterinary medicine for their broad-spectrum antibacterial activity.
The study showed that excessive use of nitrofuran in livestock can lead to drug residues in food products.

Etymology

From 'nitro-' (containing a nitro group) and 'furan' (a five-membered aromatic ring compound).
