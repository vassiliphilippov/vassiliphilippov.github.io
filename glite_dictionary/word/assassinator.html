assassinator

1. NOUN

/ əˈsæs.əˌneɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əˈsæs.ɪ.neɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who murders, especially one who kills a prominent figure by a sudden or secret attack, often for hire
one who commits assassination

Examples

The assassinator planned the attack for weeks before making his move.
Several assassinators throughout history have changed the course of nations.
The capture of the infamous assassinator was hailed as a major victory for security forces.

Synset Examples

his assassins were hunted down like animals
assassinators of kings and emperors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin - a person who murders an important figure, usually for political or religious reasons

Etymology

From 'assassin' with the suffix '-ator' forming an agent noun.
