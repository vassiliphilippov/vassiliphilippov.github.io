isoflurane

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈflʊːreɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈflʊəreɪn / (BrE)
a volatile liquid used as an inhalation anesthetic during surgery
inhalation anesthetic used in surgery
medical

Examples

The patient was given isoflurane to ensure a smooth and painless surgical procedure.
Veterinarians often use isoflurane for anesthesia in animal surgeries.
Proper ventilation is important when administering isoflurane in an operating room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sevoflurane - a similar inhalation anesthetic

Etymology

From iso- (denoting isomers) + fluor- (relating to fluorine) + -ane (organic chemistry suffix).
