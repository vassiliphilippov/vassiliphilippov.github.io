excrescence

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskrɛsəns / (AmE), / ɪkˈskrɛsəns / (BrE)
an abnormal outgrowth, projection, or development, especially from the surface of a body or object
abnormal outgrowth or projection

Examples

The doctor examined the unusual excrescence on the patient’s neck.
Over many years, mineral excrescences formed on the cave walls.
Artists sometimes highlight the excrescences of old trees in their paintings.

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protuberance - something that juts out from a surface
  • Synonym: bulge - a rounded swelling that distorts a flat surface
  • Antonym: depression - a sunken or recessed area on a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'excrescentia' (outgrowth), from 'excrescere' (to grow out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskrɛsəns / (AmE), / ɪkˈskrɛsəns / (BrE)
an abnormal outgrowth or enlargement of part of the body, especially one that is the result of disease or abnormal growth
abnormal bodily outgrowth
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a small excrescence from the patient's skin.
An excrescence near the vocal cords can sometimes affect a person's voice.
The dentist noticed a hard excrescence on the inside of her lower gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue resulting from excessive cell division
  • Synonym: growth - an abnormal increase in size or mass, especially on the body
  • Antonym: normality - the normal condition, absence of abnormal growth

Etymology

From Latin excrescentia (outgrowth), from excrescere (to grow out).
