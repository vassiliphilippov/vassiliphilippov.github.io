steep

1. ADJ

/ stip / (AmE), / stiːp / (BrE)
(of a slope or angle) rising or falling sharply; not gentle or gradual.
having a sharp slope or angle

Examples

The road became dangerously steep as it wound up the mountain.
A steep staircase can be difficult for the elderly to climb.
The hill behind the house is surprisingly steep and covered in wildflowers.

Synset Examples

note the steep incline
a steep roof sheds snow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gentle - (of a slope or incline) not steep or abrupt

Etymology

From Old English stēap (high, lofty, deep, prominent).

/ stip / (AmE), / stiːp / (BrE)

3. NOUN

/ stip / (AmE), / stiːp / (BrE)
A steep place, such as the side or slope of a hill or cliff.
a steep slope or place

Examples

The hikers climbed carefully up the steep before reaching the summit.
Goats grazed on the rocky steeps above the valley.
From our window, we could see the green steeps rising behind the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliff - a high, steep face of rock or earth
  • Synonym: slope - an area of ground that forms a natural or artificial incline
