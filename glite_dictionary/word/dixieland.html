Dixieland

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɪksiˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdɪksiˌlænd / (BrE)
the southern states of the United States, especially those that seceded to form the Confederacy during the Civil War (1861–1865)
the southern states of the former Confederacy

Examples

Many Civil War reenactments take place in Dixieland every year.
He grew up in Dixieland, surrounded by southern traditions and hospitality.
The music of Dixieland reflects its rich cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Confederacy - the group of southern states that seceded during the Civil War
  • Antonym: Union - the northern states during the American Civil War

Etymology

From Dixie, a nickname for the Southern United States, combined with land.
