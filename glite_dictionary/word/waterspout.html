waterspout

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtərˌspaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌspaʊt / (BrE)
a channel or pipe through which water is carried away, especially one that drains water from the gutter of a roof
channel for draining water from a roof

Examples

After the storm, debris clogged the waterspout and caused a leak in the roof.
He climbed a ladder to clear the leaves from the waterspout before winter arrived.
The old house had a decorative metal waterspout shaped like a dragon's head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downspout - a pipe for carrying rainwater from a gutter to the ground

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌspaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌspaʊt / (BrE)
a rotating column of air and water mist resembling a tornado, occurring over a body of water
tornado-like column over water

Examples

A massive waterspout formed off the coast, drawing the attention of local fishermen.
The ferry captain spotted a waterspout and quickly altered course to avoid it.
Photographs of the rare waterspout made headlines in the morning newspapers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tornado - a violent rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground
