Pouteria campechiana nervosa

1. NOUN

/ paʊˈtɪəriə kæmˈpiːkiːˌænə nərˈvoʊsə / (AmE), / paʊˈtɪəriə kæmˈpeɪkiːˌænə nɜːˈvəʊsə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Florida and the West Indies, known for producing edible fruit.
tropical tree producing edible fruit

Examples

The Pouteria campechiana nervosa tree produces yellow, egg-shaped fruits that are popular in tropical markets.
Botanists are researching the potential of Pouteria campechiana nervosa for future sustainable agriculture.
I tasted the fruit of the Pouteria campechiana nervosa during my trip to the West Indies.

Etymology

Pouteria from Portuguese 'pouteria' (plant genus), Campechiana referring to Campeche, Mexico, nervosa from Latin 'nervosus' (veined, sinewy).
