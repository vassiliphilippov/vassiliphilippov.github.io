dahlia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæliə, ˈdeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈdɑːliə, ˈdeɪliə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Dahlia, native to Mexico and Central America, grown for its tuberous roots and large, colorful, rayed flower heads
a flowering plant with showy blooms

Examples

She planted a row of colorful dahlias along the garden fence.
The bright pink dahlia stood out among the other flowers in the bouquet.
Many gardeners prize the dahlia for its striking variety of shapes and colors.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Anders Dahl (1751–1789).
