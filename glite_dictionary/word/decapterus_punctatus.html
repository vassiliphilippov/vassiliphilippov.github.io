Decapterus punctatus

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈkæptərəs pʌŋkˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / dɛˈkæptərəs pʌŋkˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A small fusiform fish of the western Atlantic, also known as the round scad.
a small western Atlantic fish (round scad)

Examples

Fishermen often catch Decapterus punctatus in the shallow waters off the Caribbean coast.
The diet of Decapterus punctatus includes plankton and small crustaceans.
Researchers have studied the migration patterns of Decapterus punctatus in the western Atlantic.

Etymology

From Latin; Decapterus from Greek 'deka' (ten) and 'pteron' (fin), punctatus from Latin meaning 'spotted.'
