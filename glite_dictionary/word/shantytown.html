shantytown

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæn.ti.taʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃæn.ti.taʊn / (BrE)
An area of a city where very poor people live in small, roughly built huts and shelters.
area with makeshift huts for the poor

Examples

Thousands of families were forced to live in the shantytown after losing their homes.
The outskirts of the city are dotted with a large shantytown built from scrap materials.
Local charities are working to improve conditions in the shantytown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a very poor and crowded area of a city

Etymology

From 'shanty' (a crude hut) + 'town'.
