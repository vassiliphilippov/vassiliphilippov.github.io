Swietenia

1. NOUN

/ swɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / swɪˈtiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Meliaceae, including the source of true mahogany.
genus of mahogany trees

Examples

The durable wood of Swietenia is highly valued for furniture making.
Several species in the Swietenia genus are now protected due to overharvesting.
Botanists have studied Swietenia to understand the ecological importance of mahogany trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - the wood of trees in the genus Swietenia

Etymology

Named after Gerard van Swieten, a Dutch-Austrian physician and botanist.
