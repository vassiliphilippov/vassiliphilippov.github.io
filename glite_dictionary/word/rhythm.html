rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
the basic unit of recurring beats or patterns in music that establishes the tempo and structure of a piece
the basic rhythmic unit in music

Examples

The drummer kept a steady rhythm throughout the entire song.
Classical compositions often feature a complex rhythm that guides the performers.
The dancers matched their steps perfectly to the rhythm of the music.

Synset Examples

the piece has a fast rhythm
the conductor set the beat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - a single pulse of sound in music

Etymology

From Greek *rhythmos* (measured flow or movement), via Latin and Old French.

/ ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
