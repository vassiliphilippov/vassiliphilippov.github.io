Phaethon

1. PROPN

/ ˈfeɪ.ə.θɑn / (AmE), / ˈfiː.ə.θən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the son of Helios who was killed when he lost control of the sun chariot and approached too close to Earth.
mythical son of Helios in Greek mythology

Examples

The tragic story of Phaethon explains why mortals cannot wield the power of the gods.
Many ancient poets wrote about Phaethon's disastrous attempt to drive the chariot of the sun.
A statue of Phaethon stands in the gardens, commemorating his myth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helios - the sun god in Greek mythology, father of Phaethon

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Φαέθων (Phaéthōn), meaning 'shining' or 'radiant'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfeɪ.əˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈfiː.ə.θən / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Phaethontidae, comprising the tropicbirds, which are seabirds found in tropical oceans.
genus of seabirds (tropicbirds)

Examples

The white-tailed Phaethon is commonly seen in tropical waters around islands.
Scientists observed several Phaethon nesting on the remote cliffs.
A Phaethon hovered gracefully above the waves, searching for its next meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropicbird - a seabird of the genus Phaethon

Etymology

From Latin 'Phaethon', from Greek 'Phaethōn', a mythological figure, used in taxonomy for the genus of tropicbirds.
