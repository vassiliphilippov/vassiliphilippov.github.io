botonnee

1. ADJ

/ boʊ.tənˈneɪ / (AmE), / bə.tɒnˈneɪ / (BrE)
(of a heraldic cross) having each arm end in a cluster of three buttons or knobs.
heraldic cross with three knobs per arm

Examples

The family's coat of arms displayed a cross botonnee at its center.
In medieval manuscripts, a cross botonnee often symbolizes faith and rebirth.
The design features a gold cross botonnee outlined in black.

Etymology

From French 'boutonnée', meaning 'buttoned'; from 'bouton' (button).
