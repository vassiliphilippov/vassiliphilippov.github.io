hilum

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪləm / (BrE)
the scar or mark on a seed that shows where it was attached to the ovary wall or funicle
mark on seed showing point of attachment

Examples

The hilum is often visible as a small spot on a bean seed.
When examining the seed under a microscope, the hilum can be clearly identified.
Botanists use the hilum to determine the seed's orientation during development.

Etymology

From Latin 'hilum', meaning a little thing or trifle; later used in botany and anatomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈhɪləm / (BrE)
A depression or fissure in an organ where blood vessels, nerves, or ducts enter or leave.
entry point in an organ for vessels or nerves
medical

Examples

The hilum of the kidney is where the renal artery, vein, and ureter pass through.
Lymph nodes have a hilum through which blood vessels and nerves enter and exit.
When examining the lung on a chest X-ray, radiologists look closely at the hilum for abnormalities.

Synset Examples

the hilus of the kidney

Etymology

From Latin *hilum* (a small thing, trifle).
