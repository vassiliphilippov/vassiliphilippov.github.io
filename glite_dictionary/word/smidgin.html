smidgin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɪdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈsmɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
A very small or barely noticeable amount of something.
very small amount
informal

Examples

Could I have just a smidgin of milk in my tea, please?
There isn't a smidgin of truth in his story.
She added a smidgin of pepper to the soup for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash - a small amount or quantity (especially of a liquid)
  • Synonym: trace - an extremely small amount
  • Antonym: heap - a large amount or pile

Etymology

Variant of 'smidgen,' perhaps from the Scottish word 'smitch' (a small amount or mark).
