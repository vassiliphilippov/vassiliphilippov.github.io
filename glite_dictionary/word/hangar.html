hangar

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋə / (BrE)
a large building at an airport used for housing and maintaining aircraft
large building for storing aircraft

Examples

The new jet was parked in the hangar overnight for safety.
Maintenance crews worked tirelessly inside the hangar to repair the damaged airplane.
Each hangar at the airport can accommodate several small planes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garage - a building for housing vehicles
  • Degree Higher: aircraft - a vehicle capable of flight

Etymology

From French 'hangar' meaning shed or shelter, possibly from Medieval Latin 'angarium', shelter for horses.
