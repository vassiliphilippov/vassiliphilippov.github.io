pulp

1. VERB

/ pʌlp / (AmE), / pʌlp / (BrE)
to crush or grind something into a soft, moist, shapeless mass
reduce to a soft, shapeless mass

Examples

The chef pulped the tomatoes to make the sauce smooth.
They pulp wood to manufacture paper products.
After blending, the fruit was completely pulped.

Synset Examples

pulp fruit
pulp wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mash - to reduce to a soft mass by crushing

Etymology

From Middle English *pulp*, from Latin *pulpa* (flesh, pulp).

2. NOUN

/ pʌlp / (AmE), / pʌlp / (BrE)
the soft tissue in the center of a tooth, containing nerves and blood vessels
soft inner tissue of a tooth
medical

Examples

A cavity that goes untreated can reach the pulp of the tooth and cause pain.
Dentists check the health of the pulp using a special test to see if the nerves are functioning.
Injuries can sometimes expose the dental pulp, increasing the risk of infection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dentine - calcified tissue beneath the tooth enamel and surrounding the pulp
