to customize

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌstəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəməˌaɪz / (BrE)
to modify or build something according to individual requirements or specifications
make or modify to personal requirements

Examples

You can customize your laptop by choosing the color and hardware options.
The company will customize this software to fit your business needs.
She customized her car with special wheels and a new paint job.

Synset Examples

I had this desk custom-made for me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to personalize - to make something suitable for a particular person

Etymology

From 'custom' (Latin 'consuetudo') + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01626526-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌstəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəməˌaɪz / (BrE)
to modify or build something according to individual requirements or preferences
modify to suit individual needs

Examples

She decided to customize her phone case with a colorful design.
Many companies offer options to customize your order online.
The chef can customize the meal to accommodate food allergies.

Synset Examples

customize a car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to personalize - to design or adapt something to meet a person's individual needs or preferences

Etymology

From 'custom' (tradition, made to order) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00388590-v
Synset POS: v
