arteria uterina

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪriə juːˈtɛrɪnə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriə juːˈtɛrɪnə / (BrE)
a branch of the internal iliac artery that supplies blood to the uterus and the upper part of the vagina
artery supplying uterus and vagina
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the arteria uterina was carefully preserved to maintain blood flow to the uterus.
A blockage in the arteria uterina can lead to complications in pregnancy.
The arteria uterina branches off from the internal iliac artery and is essential for uterine health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uterine artery - the main English synonym for arteria uterina

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'uterina' (of the uterus).
