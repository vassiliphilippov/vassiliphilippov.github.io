spectacles

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktəkəlz / (AmE), / ˈspɛktəklz / (BrE)
A pair of lenses set in a frame worn on the nose and ears to correct or aid defective eyesight; eyeglasses.
eyeglasses for vision correction

Examples

She reached for her spectacles before opening the morning newspaper.
After misplacing his spectacles, he found it difficult to read the sign.
Her new spectacles have thin, gold-colored frames that suit her face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glasses - eyeglasses; a device with lenses for correcting vision
  • Antonym: contact lenses - small lenses worn directly on the eye for vision correction

Etymology

From Latin 'spectaculum' (a show, spectacle), from 'spectare' (to watch).
