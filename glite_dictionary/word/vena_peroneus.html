vena peroneus

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə pəˈroʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə pəˈrəʊniəs / (BrE)
Either of the veins accompanying the peroneal arteries, running from the heel up the back of the leg to join the posterior tibial veins of the popliteal vein.
vein accompanying the peroneal artery in the leg
medical

Examples

In some patients, the vena peroneus can be clearly visualized on imaging studies of the lower leg.
The surgeon carefully preserved the vena peroneus during the operation to prevent excessive bleeding.
An obstruction in the vena peroneus can lead to swelling and complications in the lower limb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibular vein - another name for the vein accompanying the peroneal artery

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' meaning 'vein' and 'peroneus' relating to the fibula (perone).
