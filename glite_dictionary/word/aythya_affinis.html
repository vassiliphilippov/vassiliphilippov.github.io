Aythya affinis

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪθiə əˈfɪnɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪθɪə əˈfɪnɪs / (BrE)
A North American diving duck, known as the lesser scaup, with males distinguished by purplish heads.
North American diving duck species

Examples

During migration season, flocks of Aythya affinis can be seen on many North American lakes.
The male Aythya affinis is easily recognized by its glossy purplish head and bright yellow eyes.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Aythya affinis in prairie wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesser scaup - common name for Aythya affinis, a North American diving duck

Etymology

From Greek aithuia (a kind of sea bird) and Latin affinis (related).
