pilferage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlfərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlfərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or practice of stealing small amounts or minor items, especially repeatedly
theft of small items or amounts

Examples

The company lost thousands of dollars last year due to employee pilferage.
Retail stores often increase security to prevent pilferage by customers or staff.
Strict measures have been introduced to reduce pilferage in the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the act of stealing
  • Synonym: burglary - illegal entry to commit theft
  • Synonym: larceny - theft of personal property

Etymology

From pilfer + -age; 'pilfer' from Old French 'pelfrer', meaning 'to pillage'.
