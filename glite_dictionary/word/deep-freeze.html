deep-freeze

1. NOUN

/ diːp friːz / (AmE), / diːp friːz / (BrE)
an electric freezer for storing food at very low temperatures for extended periods
electric freezer for long-term food storage

Examples

We keep our surplus vegetables in the deep-freeze throughout the winter.
The restaurant has a large deep-freeze for storing meat and seafood.
After buying in bulk, she divided the food and put it in the deep-freeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freezer - a device for freezing and storing food

2. VERB

/ ˈdip ˌfriz / (AmE), / ˈdiːp ˌfriːz / (BrE)
to store or preserve (food or other items) at a very low temperature in a deep freezer
store at very low temperatures

Examples

After cooking the soup, she decided to deep-freeze several portions for later use.
Some laboratories deep-freeze specimens to preserve their integrity for future studies.
You can deep-freeze berries in season to enjoy them all year round.

Synset Examples

deep-freeze the food
