icon

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪkɒn / (BrE)
a religious painting, typically in oil on wood, especially one venerated in the Eastern Orthodox Church
religious painting venerated in Eastern Church

Examples

The museum's collection includes a rare Russian icon from the 15th century.
Pilgrims paused before the gilded icon to say a prayer.
She studied the intricate details painted on the wooden icon hanging in the chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idol - an image or representation of a god used as an object of worship

Etymology

From Greek eikōn (image, likeness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03564151-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪkɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪkɒn / (BrE)
a small graphic symbol on a computer screen that represents a program, function, file, or concept, used to trigger actions in a graphical user interface
graphic symbol representing a program or file

Examples

To open the application, double-click the icon on your desktop.
Each file type is represented by a unique icon in the folder window.
She created a shortcut icon for quick access to her favorite program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - a mark or character representing an idea, object or relationship

Etymology

From Greek eikōn (image, likeness), via Russian ikona (religious image), later extended in computer science.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07284621-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.kɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.kɒn / (BrE)
a visual representation of a person, object, scene, or idea, especially one produced on a surface
visual representation; image or symbol

Examples

The desktop icon represents the shortcut to the application.
Religious art often features a painted icon of a saint or angel.
The app logo has become a recognizable icon on smartphones worldwide.

Synset Examples

they showed us the pictures of their wedding
a movie is a series of images projected so rapidly that the eye integrates them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: image - a representation of the external form of a person or thing in art
  • Synonym: picture - a painting, drawing, or photograph
  • Synonym: symbol - a mark or character used as a conventional representation of something

Etymology

From Greek eikōn (image, likeness).

Source: WordNet
