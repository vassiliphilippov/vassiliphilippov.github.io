Empetraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.pəˈtreɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / ˌɛmpɪˈtreɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of heathlike shrubs found in cool regions, known for species such as crowberry.
family of heathlike shrubs (botany)

Examples

The Empetraceae family includes several species adapted to arctic and alpine habitats.
Botanists often study Empetraceae to understand plant survival in harsh climates.
Some members of the Empetraceae are valued for their berries and ornamental use.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Empetrum (the type genus) + -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛmpɪtəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɛmpɪtəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of heathlike shrubs, including crowberries, found in cool and temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and South America.
a family of heathlike shrubs

Examples

The Empetraceae are often found in cold, acidic soils in the far north.
Many species in the Empetraceae family produce small, edible berries.
Botanists debated whether new discoveries should be classified within the Empetraceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowberry - a plant belonging to Empetraceae, bearing small edible black berries

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Empetraceae, from Empetrum (a genus of crowberry plants) + -aceae (family suffix).
