Pedionomus torquatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdiəˈnoʊməs tɔrˈkwætəs / (AmE), / ˌpiːdiəˈnəʊməs tɔːˈkwɑːtəs / (BrE)
a small, ground-dwelling Australian bird resembling a button quail, sometimes classified as a wading bird but typically found on plains; also known as the plains-wanderer
small Australian plains bird (plains-wanderer)

Examples

The critically endangered Pedionomus torquatus is native to the grasslands of southeastern Australia.
Researchers are working to protect the habitat of Pedionomus torquatus to prevent its extinction.
Unlike many wading birds, Pedionomus torquatus spends most of its time on dry plains.

Etymology

From Greek 'pedion' (plain) and 'nomos' (dweller), and Latin 'torquatus' (collared).
