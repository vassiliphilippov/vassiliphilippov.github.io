centerline

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.təˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line that divides a plane figure or object into two equal or symmetrical parts, running through its center
dividing line through the center

Examples

The architect marked the centerline on the blueprint to ensure perfect symmetry.
Drivers must not cross the centerline painted on the road.
To draw a circle, first locate its centerline for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axis - a straight line about which a body or geometric object rotates or is symmetrical

Etymology

Formed from 'center' and 'line', first used in technical and engineering contexts.
