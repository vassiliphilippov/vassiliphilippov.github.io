Sangraal

1. NOUN

/ sænˈɡrɑl / (AmE), / sanˈɡrɑːl / (BrE)
(legendary) The cup or chalice said to have been used by Christ at the Last Supper, often identified with the Holy Grail in Arthurian legend.
legendary chalice of Christ at the Last Supper

Examples

The knights embarked on a quest to find the legendary Sangraal.
Many myths surround the true location of the Sangraal and its supposed powers.
In medieval literature, the Sangraal often symbolizes purity and divine grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Grail - the legendary cup used by Christ at the Last Supper, sought after in Arthurian legend

Etymology

From Old French *San Graal* ('Holy Grail'), from Latin *sanctum graalem*.
