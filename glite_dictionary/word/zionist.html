Zionist

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.ə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ə.nɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports Zionism, especially a Jewish supporter of the movement for the re-establishment and development of a Jewish nation in what is now Israel.
supporter of Zionism

Examples

He has always identified himself as a proud Zionist and supports the state of Israel.
The conference brought together Zionist leaders from around the world.
She studied the history of the Zionist movement for her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti-Zionist - a person opposed to Zionism

Etymology

From 'Zion', a historic name for Jerusalem, with the suffix '-ist' denoting an adherent or supporter.

Examples

She wrote a book detailing the history of the Zionist movement in the twentieth century.
Many early Zionist leaders were also noted intellectuals and activists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti-Zionist - opposed to Zionism or its ideals

Etymology

From Zionism, itself from Zion (a hill in Jerusalem symbolizing the Jewish homeland) + -ist.
