overlook

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌlʊk / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəlʊk / (BrE)
a high place, often by a road or trail, from which you can see a wide area below
a viewpoint or scenic high place

Examples

We stopped at the overlook to take in the breathtaking view of the valley below.
Tourists gathered at the overlook to watch the sunrise over the mountains.
There's a popular overlook just off the main highway that offers spectacular photo opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viewpoint - a place from which something can be viewed
  • Synonym: lookout - a place from which you can see clearly in all directions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08631679-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈlʊk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlʊk / (BrE)
(of a building, room, etc.) to have a view over or look onto a place.
to have a view over (a place)

Examples

Our hotel room overlooks the Pacific Ocean, offering stunning views at sunrise.
The balcony overlooks a peaceful garden filled with colorful flowers.
From the hill, you can overlook the entire village and the surrounding countryside.

Synset Examples

The house looks out on a tennis court
The apartment overlooks the Hudson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - be turned or oriented toward something
  • Antonym: to ignore - fail to notice or pay attention to

Etymology

From over- + look, originally meaning to look over or inspect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02727841-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈlʊk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlʊk / (BrE)
to supervise someone or something; to watch over and monitor progress or activity
to supervise or monitor

Examples

As manager, she must overlook the entire team to ensure productivity.
A teacher may overlook students during group projects to give guidance when needed.
He was hired to overlook the construction site and report on progress daily.

Synset Examples

I am overlooking her work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supervise - to oversee or watch a person or activity carefully
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or give proper attention to someone or something

Etymology

From over- (above) + look, originally meaning 'look over from above'.

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈlʊk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlʊk / (BrE)
to fail to notice, include, or do something; to miss unintentionally
fail to notice or include

Examples

I'm sorry I overlooked your name on the list.
It's easy to overlook small errors when proofreading a long report.
She overlooked several emails that needed a response.

Synset Examples

How could I miss that typo?
The workers on the conveyor belt miss one out of ten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss - fail to notice or do something
  • Antonym: notice - become aware of or observe

Etymology

From over- (meaning 'above') + look, originally meaning 'to look over' or 'to inspect'. The sense of 'fail to notice' developed later.
