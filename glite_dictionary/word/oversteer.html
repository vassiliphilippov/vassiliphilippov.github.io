oversteer

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌstɪr / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəstɪə / (BrE)
a situation in which a car turns more sharply than intended, causing the rear wheels to lose traction and the rear of the vehicle to swing outward
car turns more than intended, rear skids outward

Examples

The sports car suffered from oversteer when entering tight corners at high speed.
Drivers must learn how to correct oversteer to maintain control during sharp maneuvers.
Heavy rain increased the risk of oversteer on the winding mountain roads.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: understeer - when a car turns less than intended, the front wheels lose grip

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'steer' (to control the direction of a vehicle).
