tidy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdi / (BrE)
a small container for holding small objects, especially sewing materials or odds and ends
small receptacle for miscellaneous items

Examples

She kept her extra buttons and threads neatly organized in a decorative tidy.
The antique tidy on her dresser once belonged to her grandmother.
A porcelain tidy sat on the windowsill, storing pins and needles.

2. VERB

/ ˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdi / (BrE)
to put things or a place into a neat and orderly condition; to clean up or arrange neatly
to put things in order; clean up

Examples

Before guests arrive, I'll tidy the living room to make it more presentable.
Can you tidy your desk before leaving the office?
Every Saturday morning, we all tidy our bedrooms.

Synset Examples

Tidy up your room!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to remove dirt or make something free of mess
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make something untidy or disordered

4. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdi / (BrE)
having a neat and orderly appearance or characterized by cleanliness and organization
neat and organized; not messy

Examples

She always keeps her desk tidy and free of clutter.
After the children cleaned their room, it looked much more tidy.
He takes pride in being a tidy person, both at home and at work.

Synset Examples

a tidy person
a tidy house
a tidy mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neat - organized and tidy in appearance or arrangement
  • Antonym: messy - untidy or disorganized

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'tide' (time, season), later connoting timeliness and orderliness.
