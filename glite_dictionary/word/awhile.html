awhile

1. ADV

/ əˈwaɪl / (AmE), / əˈwaɪl / (BrE)
For a short period of time; briefly.
for a short time

Examples

She sat awhile on the porch, watching the sun set behind the hills.
Let's stay awhile and talk before heading home.
He paused awhile to consider the best way to respond.

Synset Examples

sit down and stay awhile
they settled awhile in Virginia before moving West
the baby was quiet for a while

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefly - for a short period; quickly
  • Synonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

From Middle English a while (for a while), later reanalyzed as a single word.
