well-heeled

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈhiːld / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈhiːld / (BrE)
having plenty of money; wealthy or comfortably well-off.
wealthy; comfortably well-off

Examples

The charity gala attracted many well-heeled donors from the city.
Although he appears humble, his family is actually quite well-heeled.
A well-heeled couple purchased the historic mansion last year.

Synset Examples

they were comfortable or even wealthy by some standards
easy living
a prosperous family
his family is well-situated financially
well-to-do members of the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
  • Synonym: affluent - having an abundance of wealth, property, or material goods
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable or normal

Etymology

Originated in the late 19th century; from 'heeled' (slang for having money) with reference to shoes as a symbol of wealth.
