wriggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡl / (BrE)
an act of twisting or squirming movements, typically in order to move or escape from something
twisting or squirming movement

Examples

The child gave a quick wriggle and slipped out of her mother's grasp.
With a wriggle of his hips, the puppy escaped from under the fence.
She tried to sit still, but an occasional wriggle betrayed her impatience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiggle - small movements from side to side or up and down

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'wriggle,' which is from Middle English 'wriglen', related to 'wricken' (to twist).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡəl / (BrE)
to twist and turn the body or part of it with small, quick movements, especially in an attempt to escape or get free
move by twisting and turning quickly

Examples

The baby began to wriggle when her mother tried to change her diaper.
He managed to wriggle out of the narrow gap in the fence.
She could feel the worm wriggle in her hand.

Synset Examples

The prisoner writhed in discomfort
The child tried to wriggle free from his aunt's embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirm - to wriggle or twist about, especially from discomfort or nervousness
  • Synonym: to writhe - to twist or contort the body, often in pain or struggle

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to 'wiggle'.
