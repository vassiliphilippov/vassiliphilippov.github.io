admonishing

1. ADJ

/ ædˈmɑnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ədˈmɒnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing reproof or criticism, especially intended to correct behavior
expressing reproof to correct

Examples

She gave her son an admonishing glance when he started to complain in public.
The teacher’s admonishing tone made the students fall silent.
With an admonishing shake of his head, he reminded them about the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproachful - expressing disapproval or disappointment
  • Antonym: praising - expressing approval or admiration

Etymology

From the verb 'admonish,' which comes from Latin 'admonere' (to warn, advise).
