peptization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛptəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛptɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of dispersing a precipitate into a colloidal solution by adding an electrolyte or peptizing agent
process of forming a colloidal solution
medical

Examples

The addition of sodium chloride facilitated the peptization of the iron(III) hydroxide precipitate.
Understanding peptization is crucial for producing stable colloidal solutions in industrial chemistry.
Through careful peptization, scientists can control the size and stability of colloidal particles.

Etymology

From 'peptone' (a protein derivative) + '-ization', indicating the process.
