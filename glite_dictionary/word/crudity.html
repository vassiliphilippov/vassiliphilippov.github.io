crudity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruˌdəti / (AmE), / ˈkruːdəti / (BrE)
rude or vulgar behavior that lacks tact or refinement; impoliteness or coarseness in manner
rudeness or lack of refinement

Examples

The dinner conversation was spoiled by his crudity and lack of good manners.
Some critics objected to the crudity of the humor in the play.
She was surprised by the crudity of his remarks at the formal event.

Synset Examples

the whole town was famous for its crudeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crudeness - the quality of being rude or lacking refinement
  • Antonym: refinement - the quality of being polite and cultured

Etymology

From Middle French 'crudité', from Latin 'cruditas' (rawness, rudeness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːdəti / (AmE), / ˈkruːdəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being raw, unrefined, or lacking sophistication
the state of being raw or unrefined

Examples

The crudity of the landscape impressed upon them the power of untouched nature.
Some critics complained about the crudity of the early sketches in the exhibit.
Despite its crudity, the film had an undeniable, wild energy that captivated audiences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refinement - the process of removing impurities or making more sophisticated
  • Synonym: rawness - the quality of being in a natural, unrefined state

Etymology

From Latin cruditas (rawness, undigestedness), from crudus (raw).
