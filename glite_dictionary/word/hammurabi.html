Hammurabi

1. PROPN

/ ˌhæmʊˈrɑbi / (AmE), / ˌhamʊˈrɑːbi / (BrE)
(c. 1810–1750 BC) The sixth king of the First Babylonian Dynasty, famous for codifying the laws of ancient Mesopotamia, known as the Code of Hammurabi.
Babylonian king who codified ancient laws

Examples

Hammurabi is best known for his comprehensive code of laws, one of the earliest in human history.
The reign of Hammurabi marked a golden era for Babylon.
Many historians study the Code of Hammurabi to understand ancient legal systems.

Etymology

From Akkadian Ḫammu-rāpi, meaning 'the kinsman is a healer.'
