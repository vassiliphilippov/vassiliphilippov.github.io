Lepiota procera

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpaɪ.ətə proʊˈsɪr.ə / (AmE), / lɛˈpɪ.ə.tə prəʊˈsɪə.rə / (BrE)
an edible mushroom with a long stalk, white flesh, and gills, usually found in open woodlands in autumn; also known as the parasol mushroom
an edible woodland mushroom species

Examples

Many foragers seek out Lepiota procera in the autumn because of its delicate flavor.
Be sure to correctly identify Lepiota procera before eating any wild mushrooms.
The tall cap of the Lepiota procera makes it easily distinguishable from other fungi.

Etymology

From New Latin Lepiota (genus name) and Latin procera meaning tall or slender.
