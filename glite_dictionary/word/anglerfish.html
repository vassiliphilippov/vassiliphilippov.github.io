anglerfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡləˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of fish with a large mouth and a wormlike growth on its head used for luring prey.
predatory fish with lure on head

Examples

The anglerfish uses its luminous lure to attract unsuspecting prey in the dark ocean depths.
A documentary showed how a female anglerfish devours food with her enormous mouth.
Scientists studied the anglerfish to understand bioluminescence in deep-sea creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkfish - a type of anglerfish found in the Atlantic

Etymology

From 'angler' (one who fishes with a rod and line) + 'fish', referring to the lure-like appendage.
