Polygonatum biflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈgɑːnətəm baɪˈflɔːrəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈɡeɪnətəm baɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb also known as Solomon's seal, with smooth leaves and drooping, tubular greenish flowers.
species of herbaceous plant (Solomon's seal)

Examples

Polygonatum biflorum is often found in shady woodlands across eastern North America.
Gardeners prize Polygonatum biflorum for its elegant, arching stems and subtle, drooping flowers.
Traditional medicine has used rhizomes of Polygonatum biflorum for a variety of remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solomon's seal - common name for Polygonatum biflorum, a perennial herb with arching stems

Etymology

From Greek 'polys' (many) + 'gonu' (knee) and Latin 'biflorus' (two-flowered).
