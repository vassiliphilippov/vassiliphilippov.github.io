Melba

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛlbə / (AmE), / ˈmɛlbə / (BrE)
(1861–1931) The stage name of Dame Nellie Melba, a renowned Australian operatic soprano celebrated internationally for her vocal talents and performances.
Australian operatic soprano (1861–1931)

Examples

As a tribute to Melba, the French chef Auguste Escoffier created the famous dessert Peach Melba.
Melba performed at major opera houses around the world, enchanting audiences with her voice.
There is a commemorative plaque in Melbourne honoring Dame Nellie Melba's contributions to music.

Etymology

Stage name chosen by Helen Porter Mitchell, derived from Melbourne, Australia.
