to repercuss

1. VERB

/ ˌriːpərˈkʌs / (AmE), / ˌriːpəˈkʌs / (BrE)
(of an action or event) to cause repercussions or to have an effect that spreads more widely, especially an unwanted or negative effect.
cause repercussions or effects

Examples

The company's decision to lay off workers may repercuss throughout the local economy.
Harsh words spoken in anger can repercuss in unexpected ways.
A small change in regulations can repercuss across the entire industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to repeat a sound by reflection or to repeat an idea
  • Synonym: rebound - to have an unintended effect or consequence, often back on the originator

Etymology

From Latin repercussus, past participle of repercutere, meaning 'to strike back' or 'to reflect'.
