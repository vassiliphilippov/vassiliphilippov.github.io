coneflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
any of several North American plants of the genus Rudbeckia, cultivated for their large, usually yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent central cones.
a daisy-like flower with a central cone

Examples

We planted several coneflowers to attract butterflies and bees to our garden.
The bright yellow petals of the coneflower stood out against the green foliage.
Native coneflower species are popular in wildflower meadows throughout North America.

Etymology

From 'cone' + 'flower', referring to the cone-shaped center of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊnˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊnˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A North American wildflower of the genus Ratibida, with cone-shaped central disks and radiating petals, often yellow or purple.
wildflower of the genus Ratibida

Examples

The prairie was dotted with blooming coneflowers in midsummer.
She planted purple coneflowers along the garden path to attract butterflies.
A field of yellow coneflower stretched toward the distant hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echinacea - a genus of coneflowers with medicinal uses

Etymology

Formed from 'cone' (for its central cone-shaped disk) and 'flower'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊnˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊnˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
any of several perennials of North America having rough leaves and large, showy flowers with drooping petals and a central cone-shaped disk, especially of the genera Echinacea or Rudbeckia.
North American perennial with a cone-shaped flower center

Examples

The garden was full of bright purple coneflowers swaying gently in the breeze.
Many people plant coneflowers to attract butterflies and bees to their yards.
She recognized the distinctive drooping petals of the coneflower immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Echinacea - a genus of coneflowers commonly used in herbal remedies

Etymology

So named for its distinctive cone-shaped central disk.
