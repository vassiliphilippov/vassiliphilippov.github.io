Spirochaetaceae

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˌroʊkiːtəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌspaɪərəʊkiːtəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of large, coarsely spiral-shaped bacteria found in fresh or salt water, or as commensals in the bodies of certain animals, including oysters.
family of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Members of the Spirochaetaceae family include bacteria capable of moving in a corkscrew motion.
Scientists found Spirochaetaceae in both saltwater and freshwater environments.
The Spirochaetaceae often live as harmless commensals in the bodies of marine animals.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'spirochaete' (a type of spiral bacterium) and the suffix '-aceae' (used in taxonomy for families).
