farce

1. NOUN

/ fɑrs / (AmE), / fɑːs / (BrE)
A comedy that uses exaggerated, improbable situations and broad satire to entertain.
type of comedy with exaggerated situations

Examples

The play was a farce full of mistaken identities and slapstick humor.
Many critics dismissed the trial as a complete farce lacking any seriousness.
He laughed at the farce unfolding on stage, unable to take the plot seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to expose or criticize

Etymology

From French *farce* (stuffing, joke), from Latin *farcire* (to stuff).

2. NOUN

/ fɑrs / (AmE), / fɑːs / (BrE)
A seasoned mixture of finely ground meat, vegetables, or other ingredients, used as a stuffing in cooking.
seasoned stuffing mixture (culinary)

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate farce of chicken and wild mushrooms for the ravioli.
A traditional French pâté often features a rich farce blended with herbs and spices.
The recipe calls for a farce to be piped inside the tender vegetables before baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffing - a mixture used to fill poultry or other foods before cooking

Etymology

From French 'farce' meaning 'stuffing', from Latin 'farcīre' (to stuff).
