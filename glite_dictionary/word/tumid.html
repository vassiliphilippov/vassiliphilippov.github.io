tumid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuː.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.mɪd / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) pompously or pretentiously inflated in style or language.
pompously inflated in style

Examples

The politician's tumid speech failed to impress the skeptical audience.
Critics mocked the author's tumid prose as overly elaborate and grandiloquent.
Her tumid language made the simple idea seem unnecessarily complicated.

Synset Examples

a man given to large talk
tumid political prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pompous - affectedly grand or self-important, especially in speech or writing
  • Synonym: bombastic - high-sounding but with little meaning; inflated
  • Antonym: plain - not elaborate or pretentious in style

Etymology

From Latin tumidus (swollen), from tumēre (to swell).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtuː.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.mɪd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body, especially sexual organs) swollen, stiff, and rigid, often as a result of fluid accumulation or arousal.
swollen or rigid (body, esp. sexual organs)

Examples

During the examination, the doctor noted that the tissue appeared unusually tumid and reddened.
The poet described the flower's tumid petals as if swollen with life.
Some medical textbooks refer to tumid organs when discussing certain physiological states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - Enlarged or expanded by fluid or pressure
  • Antonym: flaccid - Soft and hanging loosely or limply; not stiff or firm

Etymology

From Latin tumidus (swollen), from tumēre (to swell).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtuː.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.mɪd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) swollen or distended, especially abnormally due to fluids or gas.
swollen or distended

Examples

After the allergic reaction, her lips appeared tumid and painful.
The frog's tumid belly was a clear sign of infection.
Doctors examined the patient's tumid limbs for signs of fluid retention.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - part of the body enlarged by fluid retention or injury
  • Synonym: puffy - softly swollen or rounded in appearance
  • Antonym: flat - (of a part of the body) not swollen or distended

Etymology

From Latin tumidus (swollen), from tumēre (to swell).
