geothermally

1. ADV

/ ˌdʒiːoʊˈθɜrməli / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈθɜːməli / (BrE)
By means of or relating to heat derived from the interior of the Earth.
by means of Earth's internal heat

Examples

The entire spa is heated geothermally, making it warm even in winter.
Some greenhouses are powered geothermally to maintain stable temperatures for the plants.
Electricity was generated geothermally in this region for the first time last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geothermal - Relating to the heat produced inside the Earth

Etymology

From 'geothermal' (relating to Earth’s heat), itself from Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'therme' (heat) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
