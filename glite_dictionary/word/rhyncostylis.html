Rhyncostylis

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋˈkɑːstɪlɪs / (AmE), / rɪŋˈkɒstɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic orchids native to tropical Asia, known for their fragrant and ornamental flowers.
genus of Asian epiphytic orchids

Examples

Many collectors prize the Rhyncostylis for its spectacular blooms and sweet fragrance.
The Rhyncostylis requires warm temperatures and high humidity, mimicking its native habitat.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the Rhyncostylis genus during an expedition in Thailand.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhynchos' (beak) and 'stylos' (column, pillar), referring to the shape of the orchid's reproductive structure.
