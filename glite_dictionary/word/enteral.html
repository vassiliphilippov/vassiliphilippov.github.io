enteral

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛn.tə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə.rəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring within the intestines, especially via the alimentary canal rather than by injection
relating to the intestines or alimentary canal
medical

Examples

Some medications are administered through enteral routes, such as oral or feeding tube methods.
The patient required enteral nutrition due to difficulty swallowing.
Enteral feeding is often preferred when the gastrointestinal tract is still functional.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parenteral - administered or occurring elsewhere in the body than the digestive tract

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + '-al' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛn.tə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tər.əl / (BrE)
relating to or situated within the intestines, especially as a route of administration for medication or nutrition
relating to the intestines
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed enteral nutrition due to difficulty swallowing.
Doctors often prefer enteral feeding over intravenous methods when the digestive tract is functional.
Certain medications are administered through enteral routes for more effective absorption.

Synset Examples

intestinal disease

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parenteral - administered or occurring elsewhere in the body than the digestive tract
  • Synonym: intestinal - relating to the intestines

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) plus '-al'.
