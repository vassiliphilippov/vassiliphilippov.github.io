astigmatic

1. ADJ

/ əˌstɪɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / əˌstɪɡˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by astigmatism, a defect in the eye or a lens that causes distorted images due to a deviation from spherical curvature.
relating to astigmatism; distorted vision
medical

Examples

Her astigmatic eyes made it difficult to see clearly without corrective lenses.
The astigmatic lens caused the image to appear blurred at the edges.
An astigmatic patient often experiences headaches due to eye strain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astigmatism - a defect of the eye or lens causing distorted vision

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'stigma' (point, mark) + '-tic' (adjective suffix).
