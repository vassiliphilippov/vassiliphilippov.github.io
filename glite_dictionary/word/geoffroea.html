Geoffroea

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɒfriə / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒfriːə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs or small trees native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, belonging to the pea family.
genus of American shrubs or small trees

Examples

The Geoffroea trees are well adapted to arid environments in South America.
Researchers identified several species within the Geoffroea genus during their expedition.
Some indigenous communities use Geoffroea for its medicinal properties.

Etymology

Named after French botanist Étienne Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire.
