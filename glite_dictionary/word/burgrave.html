burgrave

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrˌɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡreɪv / (BrE)
the military governor of a German town, especially in the 12th and 13th centuries
military governor of a medieval German town

Examples

The powerful burgrave presided over the defense and administration of the fortified city.
During the Middle Ages, a burgrave wielded significant authority in certain German principalities.
The title of burgrave was often hereditary, passing from one noble family member to another.

Etymology

From German *Burggraf*, from *Burg* (castle, fortress) + *Graf* (count).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːˌɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˈbɜːˌɡreɪv / (BrE)
a nobleman who ruled a German castle and the surrounding territory, typically by hereditary right, especially in the Middle Ages
hereditary lord of a German castle

Examples

The burgrave commanded respect throughout the region for his leadership and justice.
During the feudal era, each burgrave was responsible for defending his castle and its lands.
History books often mention the influence of the burgrave in medieval German politics.

Etymology

From German 'Burg' (castle) + 'Graf' (count).
