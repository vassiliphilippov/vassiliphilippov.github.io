cervix

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.vɪks / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪks / (BrE)
the narrow, lower part of the uterus in women, forming a canal between the uterus and vagina
lower, necklike part of the uterus
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the cervix plays a crucial role during childbirth.
During a routine checkup, the nurse checked the patient's cervix for any abnormalities.
A Pap smear is a test used to detect changes in the cervix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uterus - the organ in female mammals where offspring develop

Etymology

From Latin 'cervix' meaning 'neck'.
