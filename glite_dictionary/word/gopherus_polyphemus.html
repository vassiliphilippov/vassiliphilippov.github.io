Gopherus polyphemus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊˈfɛrəs ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊˈfɛrəs ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs / (BrE)
A burrowing land tortoise native to the southeastern United States, known for creating extensive burrows and playing a key role in its ecosystem.
burrowing land tortoise of southeastern US

Examples

The Gopherus polyphemus is listed as a threatened species due to habitat loss.
Conservationists are working to protect the burrows made by Gopherus polyphemus in Florida pine forests.
The Gopherus polyphemus plays an important ecological role by providing shelter for many other animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher tortoise - Common name for Gopherus polyphemus

Etymology

From the genus name 'Gopherus' and species epithet 'polyphemus', both derived from Neo-Latin; 'Polyphemus' refers to the Greek mythological giant, alluding to the tortoise's large burrows.
