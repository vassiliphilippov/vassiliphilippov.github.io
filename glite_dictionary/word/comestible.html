comestible

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛstəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɛstɪbəl / (BrE)
An item of food; anything that can be eaten.
edible item; foodstuff

Examples

The picnic basket was filled with delicious comestibles, including sandwiches, fruit, and pastries.
At the market, vendors offered a wide variety of local comestibles to passing tourists.
Please help yourself to the comestibles on the table while we wait for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edible - fit to be eaten; safe or suitable for eating
  • Antonym: inedible - not fit to be eaten; not suitable for food

Etymology

From French 'comestible', from Latin 'comestibilis', meaning 'edible'.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmɛstəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈmɛstɪbəl / (BrE)
suitable to be eaten as food; edible.
suitable to be eaten; edible

Examples

Only comestible mushrooms should be collected in the wild.
Many plants are not comestible and can even be poisonous.
The inspector marked all comestible goods with a safety sticker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edible - fit or suitable to be eaten
  • Antonym: inedible - not suitable for human consumption

Etymology

From Latin 'comestibilis', from 'comedere' meaning 'to eat up'.
