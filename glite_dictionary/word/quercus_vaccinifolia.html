Quercus vaccinifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs vækˌsɪnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs vaksɪnɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a low, spreading shrub oak of the southwestern United States, with leaves resembling those of the huckleberry and small acorns
a low shrub oak of the southwestern US

Examples

Quercus vaccinifolia is commonly found in high mountain regions of California and Oregon.
The leaves of Quercus vaccinifolia closely resemble those of a huckleberry plant.
Botanists often study Quercus vaccinifolia for its adaptation to dry, rocky soils.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' (oak) and 'vaccinifolia' from 'Vaccinium' (huckleberry genus) + 'folia' (leaves).
