Spheniscidae

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sfɛˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the scientific family comprising all living species of penguins
penguin family (taxonomic)

Examples

The family Spheniscidae encompasses all modern penguin species, from the Emperor to the Little Blue Penguin.
Fossil records show that Spheniscidae has existed for millions of years in the Southern Hemisphere.
Researchers presented a new genetic study on the evolutionary history of Spheniscidae at the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - flightless seabird of the southern hemisphere

Etymology

From New Latin Spheniscidae, from Greek sphēnis (wedge, referring to penguins' shape).
