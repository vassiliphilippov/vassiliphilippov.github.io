Acrobates

1. NOUN

/ əˈkroʊ.bə.tiːz / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊ.bə.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small gliding marsupials, known as feathertail gliders, native to Australia.
genus of feathertail gliders

Examples

The Acrobates genus includes the tiny feathertail glider, which is famous for its remarkable ability to glide.
Researchers have documented several new behaviors in Acrobates populations in eastern Australia.
Unlike other marsupials, Acrobates possess a distinctive feather-shaped tail that aids in their movement.

Etymology

From Greek akrobatēs (a walker on tiptoe); adopted as a genus name in zoology.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkroʊˌbeɪtiːz / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊbəˌtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small marsupials in the Phalangeridae family comprising the feathertail glider.
genus of marsupials (feathertail glider)

Examples

The Acrobates genus is best known for its unique gliding ability.
Researchers observed several Acrobates in the eucalyptus forests.
The feathertail glider belongs to the genus Acrobates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathertail glider - a small gliding marsupial of the genus Acrobates

Etymology

From Greek akrobatēs (one who walks on tiptoe), referring to agility.
