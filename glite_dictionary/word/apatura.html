Apatura

1. NOUN

/ əˈpætʃərə / (AmE), / əˈpɑːtjʊrə / (BrE)
A genus of large butterflies native to the Old World, known for their distinctive iridescent wings.
a genus of large Old World butterflies

Examples

The Apatura genus includes the well-known purple emperor butterfly.
Entomologists study the habitat and behavior of Apatura species in Europe and Asia.
An Apatura was spotted near the riverbank, its wings shimmering in the sunlight.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Apatura', the formal genus name in taxonomy.
