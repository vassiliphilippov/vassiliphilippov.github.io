munch

1. NOUN

/ mʌntʃ / (AmE), / mʌntʃ / (BrE)
a large bite or mouthful of food taken while chewing noisily or enthusiastically
a large bite of food

Examples

She took a big munch of the crisp apple and smiled.
Between munches, he managed to explain the movie plot.
With each noisy munch, the cookies on the plate disappeared quickly.

Synset Examples

he tried to talk between munches on the sandwich
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00843054-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ mʌntʃ / (AmE), / mʌntʃ / (BrE)
To chew food with steady, audible grinding movements of the teeth, often making a crunching noise.
chew food with grinding, crunching sound

Examples

She munched on a crisp apple while walking through the park.
The children eagerly munched their popcorn during the movie.
He was munching carrots as he worked at his desk.

Synset Examples

The children crunched the celery sticks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch - to crush or chew with a noisy sound
