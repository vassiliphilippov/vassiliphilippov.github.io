atrium

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (BrE)
A central open area within a building, often with a glass roof and typically open to several floors and sometimes to the sky.
central open space in a building

Examples

A spacious atrium greeted visitors with natural light pouring in from above.
The hotel features an impressive glass-roofed atrium filled with tropical plants.
Many modern office buildings have an atrium at their center to provide a communal gathering space.

Etymology

From Latin atrium (central hall of a Roman house).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtriəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪtrɪəm / (BrE)
An anatomical chamber of the heart that receives blood entering the heart from the veins, or any chamber that is connected to other chambers or passageways.
chamber of the heart; connected chamber

Examples

Blood returning from the body flows into the right atrium of the heart.
The design of the building included a spacious atrium with a glass roof.
Oxygenated blood enters the left atrium before moving into the ventricle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ventricle - lower chamber of the heart that pumps blood out
  • Synonym: hall - large entrance room or lobby in a building

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (central room of a Roman house).
