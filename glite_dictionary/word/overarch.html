to overarch

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to form an arch over something; to span or cover with an arching shape
form an arch over

Examples

The ancient stone bridge overarches the narrow river below.
Tall trees overarch the pathway, creating a tunnel of green leaves overhead.
A glass dome overarches the atrium, filling the space with natural light.

Synset Examples

Big rocks overarch the stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - to curve or form an arch over

Etymology

From 'over' + 'arch', indicating to arch over something.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02039160-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to be central, dominant, or overarching, especially in relation to a subject or theme
to be central or dominant

Examples

A sense of mystery overarches the novel from start to finish.
The new policy overarches all departmental guidelines to ensure consistency.
The theme of forgiveness overarches every story in the collection.

Synset Examples

This scene overarches the entire first act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have control or power over; to be the most important feature

Etymology

From over- (above) + arch (as in arching over), extending the sense to figurative or central dominance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02650855-v
Synset POS: v
