to encroach

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkroʊt͡ʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to gradually intrude on or infringe upon the rights, property, or territory of others
gradually intrude or infringe

Examples

The new building project will encroach on the public park.
He felt that the company was starting to encroach upon his personal time.
The weeds quickly encroached into the garden, taking over the flower beds.

Synset Examples

This impinges on my rights as an individual
This matter entrenches on other domains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intrude - to put oneself deliberately into a place or situation where one is unwelcome
  • Synonym: to infringe - to act so as to limit or undermine (something); to encroach

Etymology

From Old French *encrochier* (to seize, fasten upon), from en- (in) + croc (hook).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkroʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to gradually advance beyond the usual or acceptable limits, often in a way that is unwanted or intrusive
intrude or go beyond normal limits

Examples

Developers must be careful not to encroach on protected wildlife habitats.
The city's suburbs continue to encroach upon farmland each year.
She felt her colleague was starting to encroach on her responsibilities at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intrude - to enter somewhere without permission or welcome
  • Synonym: to invade - to enter forcefully or as an unwelcome intruder
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to move back or away, the opposite of encroach

Etymology

From Old French *encrochier* (to seize, fasten upon), from en- (in) + croc (hook).
