Minoan

1. ADJ

/ mɪˈnoʊ.ən / (AmE), / mɪˈnəʊ.ən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Bronze Age civilization that flourished on Crete, known for its palaces, art, and advanced society
of the ancient Bronze Age culture of Crete

Examples

Archaeologists discovered beautiful Minoan frescoes while excavating the ruins of Knossos.
The Minoan civilization was noted for its advanced architecture and vibrant art.
Some experts believe the Minoan script known as Linear A has not yet been fully deciphered.

Synset Examples

the Minoan palace at Knossos

Etymology

From Minos, a legendary king of Crete in Greek mythology, plus the English suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈnoʊ.ən / (AmE), / mɪˈnəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a member of the Bronze Age civilization of Crete, flourishing from about 3000 to 1100 BC, known for its advanced art, architecture, and trade.
member of ancient Cretan Bronze Age civilization

Examples

Archaeologists discovered frescoes created by the Minoans during excavations on Crete.
The Minoan civilization was renowned for its palaces and vibrant artwork.
Many scholars believe trade played a crucial role in the daily lives of Minoans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mycenaean - a member of a later Bronze Age civilization in mainland Greece

Etymology

From Minos, legendary king of Crete, with the suffix -an.

Synset POS: n
