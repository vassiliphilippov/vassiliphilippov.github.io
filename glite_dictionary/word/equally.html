equally

1. ADV

/ ˈiːkwəli / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəli / (BrE)
in the same degree or amount; in a balanced, impartial, or fair way
to the same degree or amount

Examples

The property was divided equally among her children.
All students should be treated equally regardless of their background.
He distributed the snacks equally between his friends.

Synset Examples

a class evenly divided between girls and boys
they split their winnings equally
deal equally with rich and poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evenly - in an equal or uniform manner
  • Synonym: fairly - in a just or impartial manner
  • Antonym: unequally - in an unequal manner

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'equal' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈikwəli / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəli / (BrE)
to the same degree or level as something or someone else; in an equal manner
to the same degree; in an equal way

Examples

Both candidates are equally qualified for the position.
She divided the cake equally among the children.
The rules apply equally to all members of the club.

Synset Examples

they were equally beautiful
birds were singing and the child sang as sweetly
sang as sweetly as a nightingale
he is every bit as mean as she is

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarly - in a similar way; in a like manner
  • Antonym: differently - in a different way

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'equalité', from Latin 'aequalis' (equal).
