Moehringia mucosa

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɪriːnjə mjuːˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / məʊˈhɪərɪndʒə mjuːˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
A loosely matted plant with moss-like foliage and tiny star-shaped, four-petaled white flowers, native to mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.
moss-like European mountain plant with starry white flowers

Examples

Moehringia mucosa grows in shaded crevices among limestone rocks in the Alps.
Botanists were excited to discover a patch of Moehringia mucosa during their field survey.
The delicate white blooms of Moehringia mucosa stand out against its mossy, green foliage.

Etymology

Genus name from German botanist Paul Moehring; species name from Latin 'mucosus' meaning 'slimy', referring to texture.
