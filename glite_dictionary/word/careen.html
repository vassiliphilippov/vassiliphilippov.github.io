to careen

1. VERB

/ kəˈrin / (AmE), / kəˈriːn / (BrE)
to move swiftly and in an uncontrolled or unsteady way, often from side to side
move swiftly and unsteadily

Examples

The car began to careen dangerously down the icy road after hitting a patch of black ice.
He tried to steady himself, but ended up careening into the crowd at the busy station.
The toddler let go of the railing and careened across the living room, giggling as she went.

Synset Examples

The drunken man staggered into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: lurch - to make an abrupt, unsteady, uncontrolled movement

Etymology

From French cariner, earlier meaning 'to turn a ship on its side.'

2. VERB

/ kəˈrin / (AmE), / kəˈriːn / (BrE)
to move swiftly and in an uncontrolled way in a specific direction, often sideways or in an unsteady manner
move unsteadily or uncontrollably

Examples

The truck began to careen down the icy slope, narrowly missing the trees.
The bicycle careened across the road after hitting a pothole.
The children cheered as the roller coaster careened around the sharp curve.

Synset Examples

The ship careened out of control

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to change direction abruptly
  • Synonym: to veer - to change direction suddenly or turn aside

Etymology

From French *cariner* (to put a ship on the keel), from Italian *carena* (keel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01888967-v
Synset POS: v
