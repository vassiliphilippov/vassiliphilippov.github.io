bifurcation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪfərˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪfəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
either of the two branches produced when something divides into two parts or forks.
one of two branches at a fork

Examples

The river flows smoothly until it reaches a bifurcation and splits in two.
Each bifurcation in the tree's branches creates a complex, tangled structure.
During surgery, the doctor identified a bifurcation in the blood vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a part of a tree or other structure that grows out from the main stem
  • Synonym: fork - a place where something divides into two or more branches

Etymology

From Late Latin bifurcatio (a forking), from bifurcare (to divide into two forks).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪfərˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪfəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the point or place where something, such as a road, river, or structure, divides into two branches or parts.
point of division into two branches

Examples

The river forms a bifurcation just before it enters the valley.
A sudden bifurcation in the path left the hikers unsure which way to go.
In anatomy, the bifurcation of the trachea leads to the left and right bronchi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a part of something that splits off from the main structure

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bifurcatio, from Latin bifurcus (two-pronged).

division into two branches or parts

Examples

The bifurcation of the river created a small island in the middle.
In biology, leaf venation often shows a clear bifurcation pattern.
The debate led to a bifurcation within the group, with members taking opposing sides.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bifurcatio, from Latin bifurcus (two-pronged).
