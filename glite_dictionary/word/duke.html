duke

1. NOUN

/ duːk / (AmE), / djuːk / (BrE)
a male nobleman of the highest hereditary rank in the British peerage, ranking just below the monarch
British nobleman of the highest rank
british

Examples

The duke inherited the title after his father's passing.
As a duke, he owned several large estates across England.
The duke attended the royal wedding in a ceremonial uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchess - a woman holding the rank of duke in her own right or as the wife of a duke

Etymology

From Latin 'dux' (leader, commander), via Old French 'duc'.

2. NOUN

/ duːk / (AmE), / djuːk / (BrE)
a male noble of the highest hereditary rank, especially in some European countries, just below the monarch
male noble of highest rank (below monarch)

Examples

The duke hosted a grand ball at his country estate.
After the king's death, the duke was considered the most powerful man in the realm.
Historical novels often feature a duke as a central character.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duchess - the wife or widow of a duke, or a woman holding the rank of duke in her own right

Etymology

From Latin 'dux' (leader, commander) via Old French 'duc'.
