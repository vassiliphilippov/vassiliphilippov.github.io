Wagnerian

1. ADJ

/ wɑːɡˈnɪriən / (AmE), / wæɡˈnɪəriən / (BrE)
Relating to the German composer Richard Wagner or his music, especially as characterized by dramatic intensity, complexity, or grand scale.
relating to Richard Wagner or his music

Examples

Her performance had a distinctly Wagnerian quality, with powerful vocals and dramatic flair.
Some critics admire the Wagnerian complexity of his later symphonies.
The opera was staged with a Wagnerian grandeur that captivated the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wagner - Richard Wagner, German composer

Etymology

From the name Richard Wagner (1813–1883), German composer, plus suffix -ian.

2. NOUN

/ wɑːɡˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌvɑːɡˈnɪəriən / (BrE)
A follower of the theories or an admirer of the music of Richard Wagner.
admirer of Richard Wagner's music or theories

Examples

He considers himself a dedicated Wagnerian and attends every available performance of the operas.
As a true Wagnerian, she could discuss the intricacies of leitmotifs for hours.
Many Wagnerians travel to Bayreuth each year for the renowned festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wagnerism - The musical and philosophical movement inspired by Richard Wagner

Etymology

From Richard Wagner, 19th-century German composer, with English suffix -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10782148-n
Synset POS: n
