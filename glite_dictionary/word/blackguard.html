blackguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈblæɡɑːd / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a dishonorable or contemptible way; a scoundrel
dishonorable or contemptible person

Examples

He called his rival a blackguard in front of the entire company.
No blackguard would ever be trusted in this town again.
She despised him for being a liar and a blackguard.

Synset Examples

you dirty dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of good and courteous conduct

Etymology

From black (dark, dirty) + guard; originally referred to menial kitchen workers in great houses, later evolved to mean a scoundrel.

2. VERB

/ ˈblækˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈblæɡɑːd / (BrE)
to use foul or abusive language towards someone; to insult or revile
use abusive language towards
archaic

Examples

The driver began to blackguard the traffic warden for giving him a ticket.
In his fury, he blackguarded everyone who tried to help him.
The fans blackguarded the referee after his controversial decision.

Synset Examples

The actress abused the policeman who gave her a parking ticket
The angry mother shouted at the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to use insulting or offensive language towards someone
  • Synonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect or scorn

Etymology

Originally a noun meaning 'scoundrel' or 'villain,' later used as a verb meaning to revile like a blackguard.
