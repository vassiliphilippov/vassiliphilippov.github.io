Elaeocarpus grandis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌliːoʊˈkɑːrpəs ˈɡrændɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌliːəˈkɑːpəs ˈɡrændɪs / (BrE)
An Australian tree with hard white timber, glossy green leaves, white flowers, and one-seeded glossy blue fruit.
Australian tree with blue fruit

Examples

The lush rainforest was dominated by tall Elaeocarpus grandis trees reaching towards the sunlight.
Carpenters value the wood of Elaeocarpus grandis for its strength and pale color.
Elaeocarpus grandis produces distinctive blue fruits that attract many local birds.

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'Elaeocarpus' (from Greek 'elaia' meaning olive and 'karpos' meaning fruit) and Latin 'grandis' (large, great).
