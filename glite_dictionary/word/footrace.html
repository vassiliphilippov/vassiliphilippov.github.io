footrace

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.reɪs / (BrE)
a race in which competitors run on foot over a set distance
race run on foot

Examples

The annual charity footrace attracted hundreds of participants.
She trained all summer for the local 10-kilometer footrace.
Winning the footrace required both speed and endurance.

Synset Examples

she broke the record for the half-mile run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marathon - long-distance footrace of 42.195 km
  • Synonym: sprint - short-distance running race

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' and 'race', referring to a running competition.
