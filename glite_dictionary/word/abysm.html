abysm

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈbɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a deep or seemingly bottomless pit, gulf, or chasm, often used figuratively to describe something unfathomable or immeasurably profound
a deep chasm or gulf (often figurative)
archaic

Examples

The poem speaks of a dark abysm from which no soul returns.
She felt as if she were staring into the abysm of her own doubts.
Time seemed to swallow their memories into an abysm of oblivion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abyss - a deep or seemingly bottomless chasm

Etymology

From Middle English abisme or abysme, from Old French abisme, from Late Latin abyssimus (very deep abyss), from Latin abyssus (abyss).
