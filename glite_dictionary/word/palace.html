palace

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæləs / (AmE), / ˈpæləs / (BrE)
A large, impressive residence, especially the official home of a king, queen, or other high-ranking dignitary.
official residence of royalty or dignitaries

Examples

The royal family greeted the guests at the front of the palace.
Tourists flocked to see the ancient palace and its beautiful gardens.
She dreamed of living in a magnificent palace overlooking the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castle - A large fortified building or group of buildings; often serves as a residence for nobility.

Etymology

From Old French 'palais', from Latin 'palatium', the name of the Palatine Hill in Rome, where emperors lived.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæləs / (AmE), / ˈpæləs / (BrE)
the official governing group or administration of a monarchy; the royal court or its officials regarded as a political authority
the governing group of a monarchy

Examples

The palace announced a new public holiday in celebration of the king's birthday.
Following unrest, the palace called for calm among the citizens.
An official statement was released by the palace regarding the proposed reforms.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old French 'palais', from Latin 'palatium' (Palatine Hill, site of the imperial residence).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæləs / (AmE), / ˈpæləs / (BrE)
A large and impressive house, especially one that is stately or luxurious.
large, stately mansion

Examples

They converted the old estate into a luxurious palace for visiting dignitaries.
Every room in the massive palace was adorned with exquisite artwork and gold.
The billionaire's new home is more of a palace than a regular house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Antonym: cottage - a small, simple house

Etymology

From Old French 'palais', from Latin 'palatium' (the Palatine hill in Rome, site of imperial residences).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpæləs / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ɪs / (BrE)
a large and ornate exhibition hall, often used for public events or displays, especially in historical contexts
large ornate exhibition hall

Examples

The Crystal Palace in London was famous for hosting the Great Exhibition of 1851.
Several cities built a palace to showcase industrial and artistic achievements.
The exhibition was held in a grand palace designed specifically for international fairs.

Etymology

From Old French 'palais', from Latin 'palatium', originally referring to the Palatine Hill in Rome, where emperors lived.
