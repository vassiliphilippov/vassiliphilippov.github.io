cohesiveness

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈhiːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈhiːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being cohesive; the property of holding or sticking together as a unit
quality of being cohesive or unified

Examples

The cohesiveness of the team was evident in their seamless cooperation.
This glue is prized for its strong cohesiveness, making it ideal for difficult repairs.
Some desserts rely on the cohesiveness of gelatin to maintain their shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesiveness - the quality of sticking or adhering to objects
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the state or process of breaking into small parts

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈhiːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈhiːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being cohesive; the ability of elements to stick together or form a united whole
the quality of sticking together or forming a whole

Examples

The cohesiveness of the team made them unbeatable in the championship match.
A successful essay relies on the cohesiveness of its arguments and evidence.
Artists often experiment with different materials to achieve greater cohesiveness in their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohesion - the action or fact of forming a united whole
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the process or state of breaking into pieces

Etymology

From cohesive + -ness, cohesive from Latin cohaesus (to cleave together).
