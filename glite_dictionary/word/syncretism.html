syncretism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.krəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The combination or attempted combination of different religions, philosophies, or systems of belief into one.
blending of beliefs or philosophies

Examples

The region's unique culture is a result of syncretism between indigenous practices and European traditions.
Modern spirituality often involves a degree of syncretism, blending ideas from multiple religions.
Anthropologists study how syncretism shapes communities' beliefs and rituals.

Synset Examples

a syncretism of material and immaterial theories

Etymology

From Greek *synkretismos* (combination, union of communities), via Latin *syncretismus*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.krəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the merging of distinct inflected forms within a language, resulting in fewer inflectional distinctions
merging of different inflected forms

Examples

In some languages, syncretism occurs when different case endings merge into a single form.
The phenomenon of syncretism simplifies the grammatical structure by reducing the number of inflections used.
The study focused on the syncretism of subject and object pronouns in colloquial speech.

Etymology

From Greek synkrētismos (union of communities), via French syncrétisme.
