soya

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
A protein-rich food product made from soybeans, widely used as an alternative to animal protein in foods.
protein-rich food from soybeans

Examples

Many vegetarians use soya as a substitute for meat in their diets.
Soya milk is a popular alternative to dairy milk for people with lactose intolerance.
The farm produces large quantities of soya each year for export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - the bean from which soya products are derived

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu', meaning soy sauce, via earlier forms in Portuguese and Dutch.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.jə / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
A bushy annual plant native to Asia, widely grown for its nutritious seeds, used especially for food, oil production, and animal feed; also known as the soya bean.
the soya bean plant

Examples

The farmer planted rows of soya to harvest in the autumn.
Many vegetarian foods are made with soya as a main ingredient.
Soya crops improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - seed or plant of the soya bean; another common name for soya

Etymology

From Japanese *shōyu* (soy sauce), via early trade with East Asia.
