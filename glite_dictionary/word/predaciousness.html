predaciousness

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈdeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of living by preying on other animals for food.
quality of being predatory

Examples

The predaciousness of the hawk makes it a formidable hunter in the wild.
Some conservationists study the predaciousness of invasive species to understand their impact on ecosystems.
The eagle's predaciousness was evident as it swooped down to catch a rabbit.

Synset Examples

The rapacity of carnivorous animals has been considered by some writers to have had a considerable effect on the distribution and even on the extinction of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatoriness - the state or quality of being predatory
  • Synonym: rapacity - aggressive greed or grasping behavior, especially in animals

Etymology

From 'predacious' (meaning 'predatory'), itself from Latin 'praedari' (to plunder).
