stymie

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈstaɪmi / (BrE)
A situation or obstacle that prevents progress or makes it difficult to proceed.
an obstacle or hindrance

Examples

The new regulations created a significant stymie for the project's development.
Despite his best efforts, he couldn't find a way around the financial stymie.
For many startups, lack of funding remains a major stymie to innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or hinders progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction

Etymology

Originally a golf term, from Scottish 'stymie', meaning a situation where an opponent's ball blocks the hole.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈstaɪmi / (BrE)
In golf (historical), a situation in match play where an opponent's ball blocks the direct path between your ball and the hole.
golf: opponent's ball blocks shot

Examples

In early twentieth-century golf, a stymie often forced players to chip over another ball on the green.
The introduction of the stymie rule added a unique challenge to golf strategy.
Many golf purists reminisce about the days when the stymie was part of match play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes it difficult

Etymology

Originally Scots, possibly from 'stymie', meaning a person who cannot see well, later adapted in golf context.

Source: WordNet

to prevent or hinder the progress or accomplishment of something
hinder or prevent progress

Examples

Unexpected technical issues can often stymie even the most experienced engineers.

Related Terms
