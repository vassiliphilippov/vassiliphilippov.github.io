rhetorician

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtəˈrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtəˈrɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in or studies the art of rhetoric, especially someone who delivers speeches or orations
expert in rhetoric or public speaking

Examples

The accomplished rhetorician captivated the entire audience with his eloquent speech.
In ancient Greece, a rhetorician was highly respected in public affairs.
She aspired to become a rhetorician and practice the art of persuasive speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orator - a person who gives formal speeches
  • Synonym: speaker - someone who speaks or delivers speeches
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens, not speaks, at speeches or orations

Etymology

From Late Latin rhetoricianus, from rhetorica (rhetoric).
