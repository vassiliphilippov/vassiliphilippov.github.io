pinstripe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌstraɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌstraɪp / (BrE)
a fabric, usually of wool, with very thin, parallel stripes, often used for suits
fabric with thin parallel stripes

Examples

The businessman wore a dark blue suit made of pinstripe.
Her favorite coat is tailored from a classic pinstripe fabric.
Many traditional uniforms use pinstripe to add a touch of elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long, narrow band differing in color or texture from the surrounding area

Etymology

From 'pin' + 'stripe', referring to the narrow, pin-like appearance of the stripes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌstraɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌstraɪp / (BrE)
a very thin stripe, especially a white stripe on dark fabric, often used in suits or formal wear
very thin stripe on fabric

Examples

His tailored suit featured a subtle pinstripe pattern.
The designer chose navy blue fabric with white pinstripes for the collection.
A classic pinstripe suit is often associated with business professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long narrow band or strip, typically one of several

Etymology

From 'pin' (as in thin like a pin) + 'stripe'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌstraɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌstraɪp / (BrE)
a suit made from cloth with very thin, light, parallel stripes, typically worn in formal or business settings
a suit with thin vertical stripes

Examples

He arrived at the interview wearing a sharp pinstripe and a crisp white shirt.
The lawyer’s pinstripe exuded professionalism in the courtroom.
Classic pinstripes never go out of style in corporate fashion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suit - a set of outer clothes made of the same fabric and designed to be worn together

Etymology

From 'pin' (meaning very thin) and 'stripe'; originally referred to the fine lines in the fabric.
