Canella-alba

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɛl.əˈæl.bə / (AmE), / kəˌnɛl.əˈæl.bə / (BrE)
A large evergreen shrub or small tree with white aromatic bark, leathery leaves, and small purple to red flowers at the tips of its branches.
evergreen shrub or small tree with aromatic bark

Examples

The Canella-alba is prized for its aromatic white bark, often used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners cultivate Canella-alba for its attractive purple-red flowers and evergreen foliage.
Researchers discovered new insect-repellent compounds in the bark of Canella-alba.

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'canella' (small cane, cinnamon tree) and 'alba' (white).
