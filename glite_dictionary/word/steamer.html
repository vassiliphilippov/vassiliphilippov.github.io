steamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmər / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə / (BrE)
an edible clam with a thin oval shell, commonly found in coastal areas of the United States and Europe and often steamed for food
an edible clam with a thin oval shell

Examples

We ordered a bucket of steamers as an appetizer at the seaside restaurant.
The steamers were served with melted butter and lemon wedges.
Local fishermen harvest steamers along the coast every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft-shell clam - an edible clam with a thin shell, often called a steamer in the US

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə / (BrE)
a cooking vessel used for preparing food by exposing it to steam
cooking vessel for steaming food

Examples

I placed the vegetables in the steamer to keep their nutrients intact.
Our new bamboo steamer is perfect for making dumplings.
She cooked the fish in a steamer instead of frying it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steaming basket - a basket-like utensil placed in a pan for steaming food
  • Synonym: saucepan - a deep cooking pan; often used alongside a steamer insert

Etymology

From steam + -er, denoting something that uses or produces steam.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmər / (AmE), / ˈstiːmə / (BrE)
A ship powered by one or more steam engines, often used for transporting people or goods.
ship powered by steam engines

Examples

The old steamer sailed up the river carrying passengers to distant towns.
He collects models of historic steamers from the nineteenth century.
The company operated a fleet of steamers on the Great Lakes for many decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steamship - a ship powered by steam engines
  • Antonym: motor ship - a ship powered by internal combustion engines

Etymology

From 'steam' + '-er', indicating something that operates by or produces steam.
