unswerving

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈswɝvɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈswɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
not deviating from a straight course; going directly ahead without turning or veering aside
not deviating; straight or direct

Examples

The captain steered an unswerving course through the storm despite high winds.
She showed unswerving loyalty to her friends even in difficult times.
The river flowed in an unswerving line toward the sea, straight through the valley.

Synset Examples

some people see evolution as an undeviating upward march from simple organisms to the very complex
a straight and narrow tree-lined road unswerving across the lowlands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deviating - turning aside from a course or path
  • Synonym: straight - without bend, angle, or curve; direct

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'swerving' (turning aside, from Old English sweorfan 'to rub, wipe off, file away').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈswɜrvɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈswɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
not changing or becoming weaker; resolutely firm and dependable, especially in loyalty or commitment.
firm and unwavering; steadfast

Examples

Her unswerving loyalty to her friend earned her respect from everyone.
The team maintained unswerving dedication to their project goals despite many setbacks.
His belief in justice remained unswerving throughout years of adversity.

Synset Examples

a steadfast ally
a staunch defender of free speech
unswerving devotion
unswerving allegiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering, especially in loyalty or belief
  • Synonym: staunch - very loyal and committed in attitude
  • Antonym: wavering - becoming weaker; losing strength or determination

Etymology

From un- (not) + swerve (to change direction or deviate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00586061-s
Synset POS: s
