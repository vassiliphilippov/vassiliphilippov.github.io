Dolomites

1. PROPN

/ ˈdoʊ.ləˌmaɪts / (AmE), / ˈdɒl.əˌmaɪts / (BrE)
a mountain range in northeastern Italy that forms part of the Alps, noted for its distinctive pale-colored dolomitic limestone peaks
mountain range in northern Italy

Examples

The Dolomites attract thousands of hikers and climbers every year for their breathtaking scenery.
In winter, the Dolomites become a popular skiing destination for tourists from around the world.
Photographers often visit the Dolomites to capture the dramatic alpenglow on the mountain peaks.

Etymology

Named after the French geologist Déodat Gratet de Dolomieu, who studied the area's unique limestone (dolomite).
