sandgrouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌɡraʊs / (AmE), / ˈsændˌɡraʊs / (BrE)
a pigeon-like bird found in arid regions of the Old World, characterized by long pointed wings and tail, and having precocial downy young
arid-region bird with long wings and tail

Examples

We spotted a sandgrouse near the desert waterhole early in the morning.
The feathers of the sandgrouse provide excellent camouflage against sandy terrain.
Several species of sandgrouse are well adapted to surviving in harsh, arid climates.

Etymology

From 'sand' + 'grouse', indicating its habitat and resemblance to grouse.
