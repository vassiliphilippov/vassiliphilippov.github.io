footwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈfʊtwɜːk / (BrE)
the ability to handle a situation or problem skillfully and with flexibility, especially in avoiding difficulty or achieving a goal
skillful maneuvering or dealing

Examples

To succeed in politics, you need excellent footwork when dealing with rivals and controversy.
Her quick footwork in negotiations helped the company avoid a costly lawsuit.
Managing the different interests at the meeting required some agile footwork on his part.

Synset Examples

she needs some fancy footwork to cover all those lies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuvering - skillful or cunning movement or action
  • Antonym: blundering - making clumsy or careless mistakes

Etymology

Originally referring to the movement of the feet in sports or dance; later extended metaphorically to skillful maneuvering in other contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the way in which a person moves or positions their feet, especially in activities such as sports, dance, or martial arts
the way of moving or positioning the feet

Examples

Good footwork is essential for a successful tennis player.
The dancer's footwork was light and precise throughout the performance.
Boxers train hard to improve their footwork and agility in the ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stance - the way in which someone stands, especially when playing a sport
