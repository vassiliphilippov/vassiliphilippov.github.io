adamance

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.ə.məns / (AmE), / ˈad.ə.məns / (BrE)
firm determination or unyielding inflexibility, especially in maintaining an opinion or decision
firm, unyielding determination

Examples

Her adamance on the issue frustrated her colleagues, who wished she would compromise.
Despite strong opposition, his adamance did not waver throughout the negotiations.
The project's failure was partly due to the manager's adamance about following the original plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adamancy - the quality of being adamant
  • Antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or yield

Etymology

From 'adamant' (hard, unyielding) + '-ance' (forming nouns).
