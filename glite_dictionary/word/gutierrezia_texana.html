Gutierrezia texana

1. NOUN

/ ɡuˌtɪəriˈɛziə tɛkˈsɑːnə / (AmE), / ɡjuːˌtɪəriˈɛziə tɛkˈsɑːnə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to the southwestern United States, known for its rigid woody branches, sticky leaves, and yellow flowers.
annual plant with rigid branches and yellow flowers

Examples

Gutierrezia texana thrives in dry, sandy soils across Texas and New Mexico.
The sticky foliage of Gutierrezia texana makes it easy to identify among other wildflowers.
During summer, fields of Gutierrezia texana are dotted with small, vibrant yellow blossoms.

Etymology

From the genus name Gutierrezia, honoring Spanish botanist Pedro Gutiérrez, and the species epithet 'texana' referring to Texas.
