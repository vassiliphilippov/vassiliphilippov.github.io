wriggler

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡlər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡlə / (BrE)
the aquatic larval stage of a mosquito, noted for its wriggling movement in water.
mosquito larva

Examples

The pond was teeming with wrigglers after the rain last week.
Scientists collected several wrigglers for study under the microscope.
You can often spot wriggler activity near the surface of stagnant water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito larva - the larval stage of a mosquito
  • Degree Higher: mosquito - a type of small flying insect whose larvae are called wrigglers

Etymology

From the verb 'wriggle' meaning to move with twisting motions, plus the agent suffix '-er.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡlər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡlə / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who cannot keep still and is always moving or fidgeting
restless, fidgety person (esp. child)

Examples

The toddler is such a wriggler during storytime that it's hard to keep him in one place.
Despite our best efforts, the little wriggler wouldn't sit still for the family photo.
Teachers sometimes find it challenging to manage a classroom with more than one wriggler.

Synset Examples

the toddler was a real wiggler on plane trips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidget - a person who fidgets or cannot keep still
  • Synonym: wiggler - a person or thing that wiggles or moves restlessly

Etymology

From 'wriggle' (to twist or squirm), with the agent suffix '-er'.
