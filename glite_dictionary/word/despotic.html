despotic

1. ADJ

/ dɛˈspɑːtɪk / (AmE), / dɛˈspɒtɪk / (BrE)
ruling or having the qualities of a despot; using power in a cruel and oppressive way
tyrannical or oppressive; like a despot

Examples

The country suffered under a despotic regime for over three decades.
Many revolutions in history began as a response to despotic rulers abusing their power.
Her leadership style was criticized as despotic, stifling any form of dissent.

Synset Examples

moved from a feudal to a despotic order
his administration was arrogant and despotic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrannical - exercising power in a cruel or arbitrary way
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly, especially as a ruler

Etymology

From French despotique, from Greek despotikos (of or for a master); from despotēs (master, lord).

2. ADJ

/ dɛˈspɑtɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒtɪk / (BrE)
Exercising absolute power, especially in a cruel or oppressive way.
having absolute and oppressive power

Examples

The emperor ruled his country in a distinctly despotic manner, silencing all opposition.
Many citizens fled the despotic regime in search of freedom and safety.
History books often describe the king’s despotic rule as a time of great suffering for the people.

Synset Examples

an authoritarian regime
autocratic government
despotic rulers
a dictatorial rule that lasted for the duration of the war
a tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - exercising unlimited power; taking no account of others' wishes
  • Synonym: tyrannical - using power in a cruel or arbitrary way
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to democracy or ruled by the people

Etymology

From French despotique, from Greek despotikos (of or for a master).

3. ADJ

/ dɛˈspɑːtɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒtɪk / (BrE)
ruling or acting like a despot; exercising power in a cruel or oppressive way
characteristic of a despot; tyrannical

Examples

The king's despotic rule left his subjects in constant fear.
Many revolutions in history have been sparked by despotic governments.
She criticized the company's despotic management style during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrannical - exercising power in a cruel or arbitrary way
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly, especially used of rulers

Etymology

From French despotique, from Greek despotikos (of a master, authoritative), from despotēs (master).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02722028-a
Synset POS: a
