moneymaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌniˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who is successful at earning and accumulating wealth
someone who makes a lot of money

Examples

She was always a moneymaker and built a business empire from scratch.
Tech startups have proven to be real moneymakers in the last decade.
The company hired a new CEO known as a moneymaker to turn things around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earner - a person who earns money in general
  • Antonym: spender - a person who spends money rather than accumulates it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10348103-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌniˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a project, business, or activity that produces a regular supply of money or is a reliable source of profit
a source of steady income or profit

Examples

The new software quickly became a moneymaker for the company.
Tourism is the region’s biggest moneymaker, supporting thousands of jobs.
Publishing cookbooks has turned into a major moneymaker for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash cow - a business or product that provides a steady profit
  • Antonym: loss leader - a product sold at a loss to attract customers

Etymology

Formed from 'money' + 'maker', meaning something that makes money.
