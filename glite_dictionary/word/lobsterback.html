lobsterback

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑbstɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈlɒbstəbæk / (BrE)
A colloquial and usually derogatory term for a British soldier, especially during the American Revolution, referring to their red coats.
derogatory term for British soldier (historical)

Examples

Patriots often referred to British troops as lobsterbacks because of their bright red uniforms.
A group of lobsterbacks marched down the street, alerting the colonists to their presence.
During the skirmish, the young rebel hurled insults at the advancing lobsterbacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redcoat - British soldier, especially during the 18th and early 19th centuries

Etymology

Derived from the red color of British soldiers' coats, likened to the red back of a cooked lobster.
