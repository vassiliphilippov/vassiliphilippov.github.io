nymphomania

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪm.foʊˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌnɪm.fəˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (BrE)
a psychological condition characterized by an abnormally intense or excessive sexual desire in women
excessive sexual desire in women
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with nymphomania after several clinical assessments.
In literature, nymphomania is often portrayed as a source of both fascination and stigma.
The concept of nymphomania has evolved and is now understood in different cultural and psychological contexts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satyriasis - abnormally intense sexual desire in men

Etymology

From French *nymphomanie*, from Greek *nymphē* (bride, young woman) + *mania* (madness).
