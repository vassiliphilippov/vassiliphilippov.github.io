colloidally

1. ADV

/ kəˈlɔɪ.ə.dl̩.i / (AmE), / kəˈlɔɪ.ɪ.dl̩.i / (BrE)
In a manner relating to or resembling a colloid, especially in reference to the dispersion or movement of particles within another substance.
in a colloidal manner
medical

Examples

The medication was administered colloidally to improve absorption in the body.
The solution remained stable because the pigments were suspended colloidally.
In their experiment, the silver particles were colloidally dispersed throughout the solution.

Synset Examples

particles were colloidally dispersed in the medium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloidal - relating to or resembling a colloid

Etymology

From 'colloidal' + '-ly'; 'colloidal' from 'colloid' (from Greek 'kolla', meaning 'glue') + '-al'.
