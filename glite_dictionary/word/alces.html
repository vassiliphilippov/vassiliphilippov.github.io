Alces

1. NOUN

/ ˈælˌsiːz / (AmE), / ˈæl.siːz / (BrE)
A genus of large mammals comprising the moose and the Eurasian elk.
genus of moose and elk

Examples

The genus Alces includes the largest members of the deer family.
Researchers tracked migration patterns of Alces in northern forests.
The moose, classified as Alces alces, is native to both North America and Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moose - large deer species of North America and northern Eurasia
  • Synonym: elk - name for moose in British English or for a related species in North America

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name, from Latin 'alces' meaning elk or moose.
