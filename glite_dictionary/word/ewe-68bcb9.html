ewe

1. NOUN

/ ju / (AmE), / juː / (BrE)
A female sheep.
female sheep

Examples

The ewe gave birth to two healthy lambs in the early morning.
Farmers often separate the ewe from the rest of the flock before lambing season.
That ewe has the softest fleece in our herd.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ram - an adult male sheep
  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant animal kept for its wool or meat

Etymology

From Old English eowu, from Proto-Germanic *awi, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ówis (sheep).
