glow

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊ / (AmE), / ɡləʊ / (BrE)
the steady emission of light by an object, especially as its temperature increases
light emitted by a heated object

Examples

The iron rod began to emit a faint glow as it was heated in the forge.
The glow from the coals illuminated the small, dark room.
As the temperature rose, a visible glow appeared around the filament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incandescence - the emission of visible light by a body caused by its high temperature

Etymology

From Middle English 'glowen' (to glow), from Old English 'glowan' (to glow, shine).
