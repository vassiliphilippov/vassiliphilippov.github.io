internationalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The quality or principle of being international in scope or character, especially the advocacy of greater cooperation or understanding among nations.
the quality of being international in scope

Examples

The organization promotes internationalism through cultural exchange programs and global education initiatives.
Many scientists believe that internationalism is essential to addressing global problems like climate change and pandemics.
His commitment to internationalism sometimes put him at odds with more nationally focused politicians.

Synset Examples

he applauded the internationality of scientific terminology

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalism - advocacy of the interests of one’s own nation, often to the exclusion of others
  • Synonym: globalism - the operation or planning of economic and foreign policy on a global basis

Etymology

From 'international' + '-ism', coined in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that nations should work together because their shared interests outweigh their differences
belief in nations cooperating

Examples

Many leaders promoted internationalism after the devastation of the Second World War.
The policy of internationalism encouraged countries to resolve disputes through diplomacy rather than conflict.
Critics argue that excessive internationalism can undermine national sovereignty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalism - the doctrine emphasizing national interests and sovereignty over international cooperation
  • Synonym: cosmopolitanism - the idea that all humans belong to a single global community

Etymology

From international + -ism, first used in the mid-19th century.
