impermanent

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɜrmənənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːmənənt / (BrE)
lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent.
not lasting; temporary

Examples

The festival is an impermanent feature of the city landscape, lasting only a few days each year.
Many digital files are impermanent and can easily be lost if not backed up properly.
They built an impermanent shelter to protect themselves from the sudden rain.

Synset Examples

politics is an impermanent factor of life
impermanent palm cottages
a temporary arrangement
temporary housing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited time

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'permanens' (remaining through).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɜrmənənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːmənənt / (BrE)
not lasting or enduring; temporary or transient.
not lasting; temporary

Examples

Many cultures recognize that life is impermanent and constantly changing.
The exhibits were impermanent, staying only a few weeks before being replaced.
Her job was impermanent, so she continued to look for long-term opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short time; impermanent

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'permanens' (remaining).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01010709-s
Synset POS: s
