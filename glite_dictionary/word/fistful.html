fistful

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪstˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɪst.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount of something that can be held in a closed hand.
amount that fits in a closed hand

Examples

She grabbed a fistful of candies from the jar before anyone could see her.
The child offered me a fistful of wildflowers she had picked in the garden.
He scattered a fistful of rice onto the ground for the birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - the quantity that fills the hand

Etymology

Formed from "fist" (hand closed tightly) and the suffix "-ful" (as much as can be held).
