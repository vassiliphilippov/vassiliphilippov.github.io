Mark Anthony

1. PROPN

/ mɑrk ænˈθəni / (AmE), / mɑːk ænˈθəni / (BrE)
(c. 83–30 BC) Roman general and politician; ally of Julius Caesar and lover of Cleopatra, defeated by Octavian at the Battle of Actium.
Roman general and lover of Cleopatra

Examples

Mark Anthony played a crucial role in the events following Julius Caesar's assassination.
The relationship between Mark Anthony and Cleopatra is one of history's most famous romances.
After his defeat at Actium, Mark Anthony took his own life in Egypt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cleopatra - Egyptian queen and partner of Mark Anthony
  • Antonym: Octavian - Roman leader who defeated Mark Anthony
