Macaca radiata

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑːkə ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.tə / (AmE), / məˈkɑːkə ˌræd.iˈɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of macaque native to southern India, known for the distinctive bonnet-like tuft of hair on its head.
a macaque species native to southern India

Examples

The Macaca radiata is often found in urban areas of southern India, where it adapts easily to human presence.
Researchers studied the social behavior of the Macaca radiata living near temples and villages.
The bonnet-like hair tuft gives Macaca radiata its distinctive appearance among Indian primates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonnet macaque - Common name for Macaca radiata, a macaque species found in southern India

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Macaca' and species epithet 'radiata', meaning 'rayed' or 'with rays'.
