Palestinian

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæl.əˈstɪn.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌpæl.ɪˈstɪn.i.ən / (BrE)
connected with Palestine or its people
relating to Palestine or its people

Examples

The Palestinian territories have been at the center of international discussions for decades.
Many Palestinian artists have gained recognition worldwide.
She attended a seminar on Palestinian history and culture.

Synset Examples

Palestinian guerrillas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Israeli - relating to Israel or its people

Etymology

From Palestine + -ian, denoting relation or belonging.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02986160-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ pæləˈstɪniən / (AmE), / paləˈstɪniən / (BrE)
a person who is a descendant of the Arabs who inhabited Palestine, especially one identifying as a member of the Palestinian people or nationality.
a descendant of the Arabs of Palestine

Examples

The Palestinian community held a cultural festival in the city center.
As a Palestinian, he feels a strong connection to his ancestral homeland.
Many Palestinians have lived abroad for generations while maintaining their cultural identity.

Etymology

Formed from 'Palestine' + '-ian', denoting affiliation or origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09732753-n
Synset POS: n
