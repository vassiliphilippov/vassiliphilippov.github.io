hole-in-corner

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊl ɪn ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / ˌhəʊl ɪn ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
concerned with secretive, petty, or insignificant matters, or taking place in a hidden or obscure way
secretive or insignificant; obscure
british archaic

Examples

He led a hole-in-corner existence, rarely venturing out in public.
The scheme was a hole-in-corner affair, hidden from the main authorities.
Her involvement in local politics was limited to a few hole-in-corner meetings.

Synset Examples

a hole-and-corner life in some obscure community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially because illicit
  • Synonym: secretive - inclined to conceal feelings and intentions or not to disclose information
  • Antonym: open - not hidden or secret; frank

Etymology

Originally British English, attested since the 18th century; literally from the idea of something taking place in a corner or hidden hole.
