2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪʃəns / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃ(ə)ns / (BrE)
The ability to accept or tolerate delays, problems, or suffering without becoming annoyed or anxious.
tolerance of delay or difficulty

Examples

Dealing with young children requires a great deal of patience.
She waited with remarkable patience for her turn in the long queue.
His lack of patience often leads to frustration when things don't go smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impatience - lack of patience; intolerance of delay or difficulty
  • Synonym: endurance - the ability to sustain prolonged effort or suffering

Etymology

From Old French *pacience*, from Latin *patientia* (endurance, forbearance).
