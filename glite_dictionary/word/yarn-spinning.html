yarn-spinning

1. ADJ

/ jɑrn ˈspɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / jɑːn ˈspɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
Tending to tell long and elaborate stories, often involving incredible events.
inclined to tell tall or fanciful stories

Examples

Everyone gathered around to hear the yarn-spinning old sailor's latest tale of adventure.
His yarn-spinning nature kept the children entertained for hours with stories of pirates and treasure.
You'll never be bored with a yarn-spinning friend at your side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storytelling - the activity of telling or writing stories
  • Antonym: truthful - tending to tell the truth; honest

Etymology

From 'yarn' (a tale) and 'spin' (to tell), reflecting the act of storytelling as spinning a thread.
