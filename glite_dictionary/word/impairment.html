impairment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeəmənt / (BrE)
A reduction in strength, quality, or ability caused by damage or dysfunction.
reduction in strength or quality

Examples

Continuous noise exposure can lead to hearing impairment over time.
The report described the environmental impairment caused by industrial waste.
Regular maintenance prevents the impairment of engine performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - physical harm or injury that reduces value or usefulness
  • Antonym: improvement - an instance of making or becoming better

Etymology

From the verb 'impair', ultimately from Latin 'impedīre' (to hinder).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeəmənt / (BrE)
The condition of being unable to perform fully or normally, typically due to physical or mental unfitness.
loss or reduction of normal ability

Examples

The new law aims to protect people with visual impairment from discrimination.
He suffered a hearing impairment after the accident.
Cognitive impairment can affect memory, thinking, and judgment.

Synset Examples

hearing impairment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disability - a physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities

Etymology

From Latin 'impairare' meaning 'to make worse'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeəmənt / (BrE)
A loss or reduction in quality, strength, or function, often relating to a physical or mental ability.
loss or reduction in function or ability

Examples

Long-term exposure to loud noise can cause hearing impairment.
Visual impairment is common among elderly people.
Cognitive impairment affects a person’s ability to remember or reason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disability - a physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities
  • Synonym: damage - physical harm caused to something reducing its value or normal function
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better or making something better

Etymology

From Latin imparare (to make worse), via Old French empeirement.

4. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛr.mənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeə.mənt / (BrE)
a condition in which something is damaged, weakened, or made worse; a reduction in ability or function
damage or weakening; reduction in ability

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient suffered from a mild impairment of hearing after the accident.
Years of pollution led to the impairment of water quality in the river.
Early treatment can help prevent the impairment of cognitive abilities in older adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterioration - gradual worsening or decline in quality or value
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of making or becoming better

Etymology

From Latin 'impair', via Old French 'empairer' (to make worse).
