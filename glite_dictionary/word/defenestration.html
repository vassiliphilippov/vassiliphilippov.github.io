defenestration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfəˌnɛsˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfənɛsˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of throwing someone or something out of a window
the act of throwing out of a window

Examples

The infamous defenestration of Prague sparked a major religious war in Europe.
Accidental defenestration is a rare but dangerous household hazard.
The novel opens with the defenestration of a priceless vase during a heated argument.

Etymology

From Latin de- (down from) + fenestra (window).
