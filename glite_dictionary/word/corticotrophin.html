corticotrophin

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrtɪkəˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəˈtrəʊfɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to release corticosteroids
pituitary hormone stimulating the adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that low levels of corticotrophin can affect the body's response to stress.
Researchers studied the effects of synthetic corticotrophin on adrenal gland disorders.
A deficiency in corticotrophin may lead to reduced production of adrenal hormones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenocorticotropic hormone - hormone produced by the anterior pituitary that stimulates the adrenal cortex (more common synonym)

Etymology

From cortico- (relating to the cortex) + -trophin (Greek trophein, to nourish, to stimulate).
