cue

1. NOUN

/ kju / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
a hint or piece of evidence that helps to solve a problem or puzzle
hint or evidence aiding problem-solving

Examples

The detective searched the room for any cue that could identify the suspect.
Her puzzled expression was a cue that she hadn't understood the instructions.
Finding the missing document provided the final cue to solve the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clue - a piece of evidence leading toward the solution of a problem or mystery
  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
  • Antonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes problem-solving harder

Etymology

Probably from French 'queue' (tail), referring to a signal or prompt in theatrical contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06656526-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
to give a prompt or reminder to someone, especially an actor or speaker, by suggesting the next words when they have been forgotten or are not fully learned
to prompt someone with the next words (theatre, speech)

Examples

If you forget your lines during the play, the stage manager will cue you.
She stood just offstage, ready to cue the actors if they stumbled.
When he hesitated in the middle of his speech, his assistant quietly cued him with the next sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - to supply someone with a forgotten word or line during a performance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00879379-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
a long, tapering rod used to strike the cue ball in billiards, pool, or snooker
stick for striking ball in billiards or pool

Examples

He chalked his cue before taking the shot.
A good cue is essential for playing professional billiards.
She bought a new cue with a smooth, polished finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cane - a slender stick used as an aid in walking or as a weapon

Etymology

From French *queue* (tail), referring to the shape of the stick.

4. NOUN

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
A line or action that prompts another actor to begin their speech or action during a performance.
prompt for an actor's speech or action

Examples

As soon as he heard his cue, he stepped confidently onto the stage.
Missing her cue caused an awkward pause in the play.
The director told the actor to listen carefully for his cue before delivering his line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - an assist or reminder for an actor to act or speak

Etymology

Possibly from the Latin 'quando' (“when”), used in theater scripts as an abbreviation (q), or from the French 'queue' meaning 'tail, end.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07024325-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
A signal or stimulus that prompts an action or provides information about what to do next.
signal or stimulus prompting action

Examples

She waited for her cue to enter the stage during the play.
The director gave the actors a cue to start speaking their lines.
A sudden drop in temperature was the cue for the animals to migrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an action or sound that sends information or instructions
  • Synonym: prompt - an act of encouraging or reminding someone to do something
  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion

Etymology

Possibly from French 'queue' (tail), originally referring to the tail of a written note indicating actors' turns.

Source: WordNet
