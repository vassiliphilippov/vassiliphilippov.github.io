tubful

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌbˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʌb.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to what a tub can hold.
amount a tub will hold

Examples

The farmer poured a tubful of water into the trough for the animals.
She made a tubful of popcorn for the movie night with friends.
After the heavy rain, the children found a tubful of rainwater in the backyard.

Synset Examples

a tub of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bucketful - an amount that a bucket can hold
