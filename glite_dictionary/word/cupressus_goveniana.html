Cupressus goveniana

1. NOUN

/ kuˈprɛsəs ˌgoʊvɪˈnaɪənə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs ˌɡəʊvɪˈneɪənə / (BrE)
A small, sometimes shrubby cypress tree native to California, often cultivated as an ornamental plant. It includes forms such as pygmy cypress and Santa Cruz cypress in some classifications.
California cypress tree species

Examples

Cupressus goveniana grows naturally along the central coast of California.
The rare Cupressus goveniana is valued by horticulturists for its ornamental qualities.
Botanists are working to protect populations of Cupressus goveniana from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - a type of coniferous tree or shrub; genus Cupressus

Etymology

From Latin, genus name Cupressus (cypress) and specific epithet honoring H. N. Goven.
