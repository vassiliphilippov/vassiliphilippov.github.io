Drepanididae

1. NOUN

/ drɛpəˈnɪdəˌdi / (AmE), / drɛpəˈnɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, often brightly colored birds native to Hawaii, commonly known as Hawaiian honeycreepers
family of Hawaiian honeycreeper birds

Examples

Many species within the Drepanididae family exhibit strikingly different beak shapes adapted to specific food sources.
The extinction of several Drepanididae species is a significant concern for conservationists in Hawaii.
Scientists study Drepanididae to better understand how birds adapt to island environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeycreeper - a nectar-feeding songbird of the family Drepanididae or Thraupidae

Etymology

From New Latin Drepanididae, from Drepanis (a genus in the family) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ drɛpəˈnɪdəˌdi / (AmE), / drɛpəˈnɪdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds found in Hawaii, commonly known as Hawaiian honeycreepers, known for their adaptive radiation and diverse beak shapes.
family of Hawaiian honeycreepers

Examples

The Drepanididae are noted for their remarkable variety of beak shapes and feeding habits.
Several species within the Drepanididae have become extinct due to habitat loss and the introduction of predators.
Researchers travel to the Hawaiian Islands to study the unique evolution of the Drepanididae.

Etymology

From modern Latin Drepanididae, based on the genus Drepanis, from Greek drepanē (sickle), referring to the shape of the bill in some species.
