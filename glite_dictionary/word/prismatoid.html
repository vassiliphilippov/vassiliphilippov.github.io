prismatoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪz.mə.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈprɪz.mə.tɔɪd / (BrE)
A polyhedron whose vertices all lie in one or the other of two parallel planes, with the faces in those planes serving as its bases.
type of geometric polyhedron

Examples

The volume of a prismatoid can be calculated using a specific mathematical formula.
Any polyhedron with its vertices lying in two parallel planes is considered a prismatoid.
The architect used the concept of a prismatoid to design a unique roof structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polyhedron - a solid figure with many flat faces, typically more than six

Etymology

From prism + -oid, from Greek 'eidos' meaning form or shape.
