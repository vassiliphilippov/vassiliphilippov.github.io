Alternanthera philoxeroides

1. NOUN

/ ˌæltərˈnænθərə ˌfɪlɒksəˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌæltəˈnænθərə ˌfɪlɒksəˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a prolific South American aquatic weed with grasslike leaves and short spikes of white flowers, known for forming dense floating masses that can clog waterways
prolific South American aquatic weed

Examples

The spread of Alternanthera philoxeroides has caused serious problems in local river systems.
Farmers struggle to control Alternanthera philoxeroides because it grows so quickly and densely.
Efforts to remove Alternanthera philoxeroides from lakes require ongoing monitoring and management.
