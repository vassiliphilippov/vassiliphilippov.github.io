pixie

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.si / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.si / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen shrub with narrow overlapping leaves and early white star-shaped flowers, native to the pine barrens of New Jersey and the Carolinas.
creeping evergreen shrub of American pine barrens

Examples

The pixie is a characteristic sight in the pine barrens of New Jersey during spring.
Gardeners appreciate the hardy nature of the pixie and its delicate white flowers.
The pixie forms a dense mat, making it ideal for ground cover in sandy soils.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪksi / (AmE), / ˈpɪksi / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, magical creature with pointed ears, known for being playful and mischievous.
small, mischievous fairy in folklore

Examples

According to legend, a pixie led travelers astray in the misty woods.
She dressed up as a pixie for the costume party, complete with wings and a green dress.
Stories about pixies causing harmless trouble are common in English folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being with magical powers

Etymology

Possibly from Cornish *piskie* or Welsh *pwca*, referring to a supernatural being.
