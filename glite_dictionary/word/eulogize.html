to eulogize

1. VERB

/ ˈjuːləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːləˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to praise someone or something highly and formally, especially in a speech at a funeral
formally praise, especially at a funeral

Examples

During the ceremony, her colleagues eulogized her commitment to education and her kindness to students.
The mayor eulogized the city's founders during his annual address to honor their vision and dedication.
When he retired, several speakers eulogized his long career and many achievements in public service.

Synset Examples

The dead woman was eulogized at the funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of someone or something
  • Antonym: to criticize - indicate faults in a disapproving way

Etymology

From Greek eulogein (to praise), from eu (well) + logein (to speak).
