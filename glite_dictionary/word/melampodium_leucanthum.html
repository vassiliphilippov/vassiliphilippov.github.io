Melampodium leucanthum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləmˈpoʊdiəm luˈkænθəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛləmˈpəʊdiəm luˈkanθəm / (BrE)
A bushy subshrub native to southwestern North America, with flower heads resembling asters featuring broad white rays.
desert plant with white, aster-like flowers

Examples

The Melampodium leucanthum blooms throughout the summer in dry, open areas of Texas.
Gardeners admire Melampodium leucanthum for its drought resistance and delicate white flowers.
Researchers found that Melampodium leucanthum supports pollinators in Southwest desert ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackfoot daisy - common name for Melampodium leucanthum

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Melampodium (the genus name) and leucanthum from Greek leuko- (white) + anthos (flower).
