Triopidae

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɑːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / traɪˈɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small freshwater crustaceans within the order Notostraca, commonly known as tadpole shrimps.
family of tadpole shrimps (crustaceans)

Examples

The family Triopidae contains several species that live in temporary freshwater pools.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Triopidae in a remote desert pond.
Members of Triopidae are often called living fossils due to their ancient lineage.

Etymology

From the genus name Triops, from Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'ops' (eye), plus the standard zoological family suffix '-idae'.
