gynogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪnəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌgʌɪnəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
A form of reproduction in which the embryo develops from the female's egg cell without genetic contribution from the male, as the sperm fails to fuse with the egg nucleus and only maternal chromosomes are present.
reproduction using only maternal chromosomes
medical

Examples

Some fish species are capable of gynogenesis, producing offspring that inherit genes only from the mother.
Scientists studied the process of gynogenesis to understand the mechanisms of uniparental inheritance.
In gynogenesis, the egg develops without any genetic material from the sperm being incorporated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parthenogenesis - reproduction without fertilization of the egg, involving only one parent

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman, female) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
