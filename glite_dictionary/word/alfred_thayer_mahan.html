Alfred Thayer Mahan

1. PROPN

/ ˈælfrəd ˈθeɪər məˈhæn / (AmE), / ˈælfrɪd ˈθeɪə məˈhæn / (BrE)
(1840–1914) United States naval officer and historian, known for his influential works on naval strategy and the importance of sea power in global politics.
U.S. naval officer and historian (1840–1914)

Examples

Alfred Thayer Mahan's theories changed the way nations approached naval power in the 20th century.
Many military academies still study the writings of Alfred Thayer Mahan.
The influence of Alfred Thayer Mahan extended beyond the United States to other naval powers around the world.
