watcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person who keeps a devotional vigil by the bedside of a sick or dead person
person who keeps vigil by the sick or dead

Examples

The watcher sat silently through the night, praying beside the dying man.
Each family member took turns as a watcher during the long illness.
Tradition required a watcher to stay by the body until burial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigil - a period of keeping awake, especially to keep watch or pray

Etymology

From Middle English *waccher* (one who keeps watch), from Old English *wæccan* (to watch, be awake).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person who keeps guard or observes in order to protect or monitor a place, event, or person
guard or observer keeping watch

Examples

The night watcher walked silently along the fence, scanning for any sign of movement.
A watcher was posted at the entrance to alert the others of any intruders.
As the appointed watcher, she stayed awake while the rest of the group slept.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch over something or someone
  • Synonym: sentinel - a soldier or guard whose job is to stand and keep watch

Etymology

From Middle English *waccher*, from 'watch' + '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person who closely observes or keeps watch over something, especially as part of an activity or event
person who observes closely

Examples

The bird watcher quietly observed the rare species through his binoculars.
A storm watcher posted updates as the hurricane approached the coast.
Night watchers often report seeing shooting stars from the hilltop.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - a person who observes; synonym for watcher

Etymology

From Middle English *wacche* (watch), from Old English *wæccan*; the agent suffix -er denotes 'one who watches'.
