genus Lemmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɛm.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɛm.əs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents commonly known as lemmings, native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.
genus of small Arctic rodents (lemmings)

Examples

Scientists studying Arctic wildlife often focus on the genus Lemmus due to their population cycles.
The genus Lemmus includes several species of lemmings found in northern tundra ecosystems.
Recent research has revealed interesting migration patterns within the genus Lemmus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - small, furry Arctic rodent

Etymology

From Latin 'lemmus', used in scientific taxonomy for this genus of lemmings.
