four

1. NUM

/ fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
The number equivalent to the sum of three and one; 4.
the number 4

Examples

There are four seasons in a year: spring, summer, autumn, and winter.
She bought four apples at the market.
The clock struck four as they left the house.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three - the number 3
  • Degree Higher: five - the number 5

Etymology

Old English 'feower', from Proto-Germanic *fedwōr.

Examples

3. NOUN

/ fɔːr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
A playing card, domino, or die with four spots or pips on its face.
card or die showing four pips

Examples

He drew the four of spades from the deck and smiled.
She rolled a four on the die and moved her piece forward.
There are only two fours in a standard set of dominoes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three - a playing card, domino, or die showing three pips
  • Degree Higher: five - a playing card, domino, or die showing five pips

4. NUM

/ fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
The number 4; the cardinal number that is the sum of three and one.
the number 4

Examples

Our meeting is scheduled for four o'clock this afternoon.
She bought four apples from the market.
There are four seasons in a year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three - the cardinal number 3
  • Degree Higher: five - the cardinal number 5

Etymology

From Old English 'feower', from Proto-Germanic '*fedwōr', ultimately from Proto-Indo-European '*kʷetwóres'.
