thermalgesia

1. NOUN

/ θɜrˌmælˈɡiːʒə / (AmE), / θɜːˌmalˈɡiːziə / (BrE)
Pain that is caused by exposure to heat.
pain caused by heat
medical

Examples

Exposure to hot surfaces can trigger thermalgesia in sensitive individuals.
Some nerve disorders make patients particularly susceptible to thermalgesia.
Research into thermalgesia has improved our understanding of pain mechanisms.

Etymology

From Greek thermē (heat) + algesis (sense of pain).
