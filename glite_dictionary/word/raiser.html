raiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈreɪzə / (BrE)
a bridge partner who increases the partner's bid by raising it
bridge partner who increases a partner's bid

Examples

In her last hand, Alice was the raiser, supporting her partner's suit with a higher bid.
His role as the raiser showed their strong partnership at the bridge table.
Being the raiser can signal to your partner that you believe in their bid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bidder - person who makes a bid

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈreɪzə / (BrE)
a person who cultivates and grows crops, especially as a profession; a cultivator or grower
person who grows crops

Examples

The wheat raiser invested in new irrigation systems to improve his fields.
She became a successful fruit raiser in the region, known for her organic produce.
Many vegetable raisers attend workshops to learn sustainable farming methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm
  • Synonym: grower - a person who grows plants or crops
  • Synonym: harvester - a person who gathers crops at harvest

Etymology

From 'raise' (to grow or cultivate) + agent suffix '-er'.
