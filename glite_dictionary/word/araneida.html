Araneida

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈnaɪɪdə / (AmE), / ˌærəˈniːɪdə / (BrE)
an order of arachnids comprising the true spiders
order of true spiders (scientific)

Examples

Many biologists study the anatomy of Araneida to understand their web-spinning abilities.
The fossil record of Araneida dates back to the Carboniferous period.
Unlike scorpions, Araneida typically produce silk from specialized spinnerets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider - an arachnid of the order Araneae

Etymology

From Latin 'aranea' (spider) + -ida (taxonomic suffix).
