justifiable

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒʌstəˈfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be shown to be right or reasonable; defensible.
able to be justified or defended

Examples

The employee's actions were justifiable given the unusual circumstances.
There is no justifiable reason to ignore public safety concerns.
His anger seemed justifiable after hearing the disappointing news.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unjustifiable - unable to be shown to be right or reasonable; indefensible
  • Synonym: defensible - able to be defended or supported as right or reasonable

Etymology

From Old French 'justifiable', from Medieval Latin 'justificabilis', from Latin 'justificare' (to justify).
