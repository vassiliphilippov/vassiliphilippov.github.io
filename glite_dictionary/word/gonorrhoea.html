gonorrhoea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɑːnəˈriːə / (AmE), / ˌɡɒnəˈriːə / (BrE)
a common sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae, typically resulting in painful urination and discharge from the urethra or genitals
sexually transmitted infection caused by bacteria
medical british

Examples

If left untreated, gonorrhoea can cause serious reproductive health problems in both men and women.
The clinic provides confidential testing and advice for those who think they may have gonorrhoea.
She was relieved to learn that gonorrhoea can be effectively treated with antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonorrhea - American spelling of gonorrhoea
  • Synonym: syphilis - another common sexually transmitted infection

Etymology

From Greek gonos (seed, semen) and rhoia (flow), referring to the discharge symptom of the disease.
