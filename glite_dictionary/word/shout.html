shout

1. VERB

/ ʃaʊt / (AmE), / ʃaʊt / (BrE)
to speak or call out in a loud voice, often to be heard over distance or noise
speak loudly or call out

Examples

Please don't shout; I can hear you perfectly well.
The coach had to shout instructions over the noise of the crowd.
She shouted his name, hoping he would hear her across the field.

Synset Examples

My grandmother is hard of hearing — you'll have to shout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly, typically in pain, anger, or excitement
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

Etymology

From Middle English shuten, schuten, from Old English scēotan (to shoot, throw, also to utter loudly, proclaim).

2. NOUN

/ ʃaʊt / (AmE), / ʃaʊt / (BrE)
a loud cry or call, often made to express strong emotion, protest, or attract attention
a loud cry or call

Examples

The angry crowd let out a deafening shout as the announcement was made.
A sudden shout from the back of the hall caught everyone's attention.
The children responded to the invitation with a loud shout of joy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cry - a loud vocal sound to express emotion or attract attention
  • Antonym: whisper - a very quiet way of speaking

Etymology

From Middle English 'shouten', probably imitative in origin.
