Windsors

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnzərz / (AmE), / ˈwɪnzəz / (BrE)
Eyeglasses with round frames, often made of wire, popularized in the early 20th century and associated with celebrities like John Lennon.
round-framed eyeglasses style

Examples

He wore a pair of Windsors to give himself a retro look.
The optician recommended Windsors for people who prefer round frames.
Thanks to John Lennon, Windsors became iconic in the 1960s.

Synset Examples

Windsors are very chic this season.

Etymology

Named after the Duke of Windsor, with the style gaining popularity in the early 20th century.
