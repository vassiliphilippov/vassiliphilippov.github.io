Sturnidae

1. NOUN

/ stɜrˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / stɜːˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as the Old World starlings, native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.
family of Old World starlings (birds)

Examples

The family Sturnidae includes many species commonly known as starlings.
Researchers have studied the migratory patterns of Sturnidae across continents.
Many Sturnidae species display remarkable vocal mimicry abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a small to medium-sized passerine bird of the family Sturnidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Sturnus, from Latin sturnus (starling).
