haircut

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈheəˌkʌt / (BrE)
the act or process of cutting and shaping the hair on a person’s head
the act of cutting hair

Examples

She went to the salon for a haircut before her job interview.
His new haircut made him look much younger.
After months of quarantine, he finally got a haircut.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360648-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈheəˌkʌt / (BrE)
the style in which a person's hair is cut and arranged
hair style resulting from cutting

Examples

She showed the barber a picture of the haircut she wanted.
His new haircut made him look much younger.
The stylist recommended a short haircut for the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairstyle - a particular way of arranging the hair

Etymology

From hair + cut, indicating the cutting of hair into a particular style.
