to codify

1. VERB

/ ˈkoʊdɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to arrange laws, rules, or information into a systematic code or ordered set
organize laws or rules systematically

Examples

The committee worked to codify company policies into a single manual.
The new constitution will codify the rights of citizens.
Lawmakers sought to codify existing regulations to simplify enforcement.

Synset Examples

Hammurabi codified the laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to systematize - to arrange according to a system or plan
  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange or structure

Etymology

From Latin 'codex' (book of laws) + '-fy' (to make).
