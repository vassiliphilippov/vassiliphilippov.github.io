Quercus shumardii

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ʃuːˈmɑrdi / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ʃuːˈmɑːdiː / (BrE)
A large deciduous red oak native to the southern and eastern United States, characterized by large elliptical leaves with seven to nine lobes, large acorns, and medium hard, coarse-grained wood.
a species of red oak tree in the U.S.

Examples

The Quercus shumardii is valued for its strong wood and shade in urban landscapes.
Many parks in the southeastern United States have planted Quercus shumardii to support local wildlife.
The lobed leaves of Quercus shumardii turn a brilliant red in the fall.

Etymology

From Latin 'Quercus' (oak) and named after Benjamin F. Shumard, an American geologist.
