bean

/ bin / (AmE), / biːn / (BrE)
(informal) A person's head.
slang for the head
informal slang

Examples

He took a nasty blow to the bean during the game.
Use your bean and figure it out!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noggin - informal term for the head

2. NOUN

/ bin / (AmE), / biːn / (BrE)
an edible seed, typically from plants of the legume family, eaten as a vegetable or used as food
edible seed from a legume plant

Examples

The farmer harvested a large crop of beans this year.
She added black beans to the chili for extra flavor and protein.
We grew several varieties of bean in our vegetable garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - a small, round green seed eaten as a vegetable
  • Synonym: lentil - small, lens-shaped edible seed of a legume

Etymology

From Old English 'bēan', related to German 'Bohne' and Dutch 'boon'.

3. NOUN

/ bin / (AmE), / biːn / (BrE)
any of various plants of the legume family that are cultivated for their edible seeds and pods, or the seeds themselves
edible seed or plant of legume family

Examples

The farmer rotated his crops to include bean plants for better soil quality.
She added a handful of black beans to the salad for protein.
Many traditional dishes feature bean stew as a main course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - a small, round, green seed eaten as a vegetable, also from a leguminous plant
  • Synonym: lentil - a small, round, flat seed from a plant of the legume family, used as food

Etymology

From Old English 'bēan', of Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ bin / (AmE), / biːn / (BrE)
to hit someone on the head with a hard object, especially a pitched baseball
hit on the head (esp. with a baseball)
informal american

Examples

The pitcher accidentally beaned the batter in the fifth inning.
If you’re not careful, you might get beaned by a foul ball.
She beaned him with her notebook for telling another bad joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clout - to hit someone hard, especially on the head
  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcefully

Etymology

Slang extension of 'bean' as a colloquial term for 'head' (originating from late 19th-century American English).

5. NOUN

/ bin / (AmE), / biːn / (BrE)
the edible seed or pod of various plants in the legume family, or any seed resembling this shape
edible leguminous seed or pod

Examples

He added cooked beans to the salad for extra protein.
The farmer grew several types of beans in his fields.
Black beans are a staple ingredient in many Latin American dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - small, round, green seed of the pea plant
  • Degree Lower: grain - small, hard, dry seed used as food

Etymology

From Old English 'bēan', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'boon' and German 'Bohne'.
