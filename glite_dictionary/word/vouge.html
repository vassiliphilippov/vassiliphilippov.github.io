vouge

1. NOUN

/ voʊɡ / (AmE), / vəʊɡ / (BrE)
A polearm with a broad blade mounted on a long shaft, used by foot soldiers in the 14th century.
medieval pole weapon
archaic

Examples

The museum displayed a vouge among other medieval weapons.
Each foot soldier was armed with a vouge for use in battle.
The vouges had broad blades designed for both cutting and thrusting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poleaxe - a type of axe on a long handle used as a weapon
  • Synonym: halberd - a combined spear and battle-axe weapon from the 14th–16th centuries

Etymology

From Old French 'vouge', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'voice' meaning 'sword'.
