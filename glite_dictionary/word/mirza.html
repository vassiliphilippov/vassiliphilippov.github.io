mirza

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪr.zə / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.zə / (BrE)
a title of respect placed before the surname of an official, scholar, or other distinguished man, especially in Persian, South Asian, or Central Asian contexts
title of respect for officials or scholars

Examples

The famous historian was addressed as Mirza Ahmad by his students and colleagues.
In some families, the title mirza is still used out of respect for tradition.
He proudly introduced himself as Mirza Ali Baig during the ceremony.

Etymology

From Persian *mirzā*, a contraction of *amirzādeh* (son of a prince).
