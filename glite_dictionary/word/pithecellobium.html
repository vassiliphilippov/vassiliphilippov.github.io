Pithecellobium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪθəˈsɛlɪbiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɪθəˈsɛlɪbiəm / (BrE)
A genus of thorny shrubs and trees native to tropical and subtropical regions of America and Asia.
genus of tropical shrubs and trees

Examples

Several species of Pithecellobium are cultivated for their ornamental value in tropical gardens.
The thorny branches of Pithecellobium make it a useful natural barrier in landscaping.
Botanists recently discovered a new Pithecellobium species in the forests of South America.

Etymology

From Greek 'pithēkos' (ape) and 'lobos' (pod or lobe), referring to the shape of the seed pod.
