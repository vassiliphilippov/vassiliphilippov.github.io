adduct

1. VERB

/ əˈdʌkt / (AmE), / əˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to move a limb or other body part toward the central axis of the body or toward another body part
move a limb toward the body
medical

Examples

The physiotherapist showed me how to adduct my arm correctly during exercises.
If you adduct your legs too forcefully, you might feel strain in your inner thigh.
The bird can adduct its wings to rest them against its body.

Synset Examples

adduct the thigh muscle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to abduct - to move a limb away from the body's midline

Etymology

From Latin adducere (to bring toward).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæd.ʌkt / (AmE), / ˈæd.ʌkt / (BrE)
A compound formed when two or more molecules combine by an addition reaction, typically in chemistry.
compound formed by addition reaction
medical

Examples

The chemist isolated an adduct after reacting the two substances together.
Formation of an adduct can change the physical properties of a compound.
In this reaction, the ammonia acts as a ligand to form an adduct with boron trifluoride.

Etymology

From Latin adductus, past participle of adducere, meaning 'to bring to'.
