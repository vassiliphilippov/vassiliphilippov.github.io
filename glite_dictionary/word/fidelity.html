fidelity

1. NOUN

/ fəˈdɛl.ə.ti / (AmE), / fɪˈdɛl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The degree of accuracy with which a system, especially in electronics, reproduces sound or images from its input signal.
accuracy of sound or image reproduction

Examples

The new headphones offer outstanding fidelity in music playback.
Audio engineers strive for the highest possible fidelity when mastering tracks.
Low fidelity can result in noise and poor sound quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accuracy - the quality or state of being correct or precise
  • Antonym: distortion - loss or lack of fidelity; alteration of the original signal

Etymology

From Latin fidelitas (faithfulness), from fidelis (faithful), transferred to technical use indicating accuracy or faithfulness in reproduction.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈdɛlɪti / (AmE), / fɪˈdɛlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being loyal or faithful to someone or something; strict observance of promises or duties.
faithfulness; loyalty

Examples

Her fidelity to her friends was admired by everyone who knew her.
They doubted his fidelity after he betrayed their trust.
The lawyer questioned the fidelity of the witness's account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal
  • Antonym: infidelity - unfaithfulness, especially in marriage

Etymology

From Latin 'fidelitas' (faithfulness), from 'fidēlis' (faithful).
