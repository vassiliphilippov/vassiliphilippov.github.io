ammonium

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / əˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
a positively charged ion (NH4⁺) formed from ammonia, which behaves similarly to an alkali metal ion in many chemical reactions
NH4⁺ ion formed from ammonia
medical

Examples

The fertilizer contains ammonium nitrate to boost plant growth.
Rainfall can convert atmospheric ammonia into ammonium compounds in the soil.
Scientists measured the concentration of ammonium ions in the water sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonia - a pungent gas with the formula NH3

Etymology

From ammonia, patterned after Latin/Greek naming conventions for chemical ions.
