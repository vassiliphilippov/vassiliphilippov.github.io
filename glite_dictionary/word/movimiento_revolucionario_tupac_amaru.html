Movimiento Revolucionario Tupac Amaru

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈvimjentoʊ rɛvəˌluːsioʊˈnaːrioʊ ˈtuːpak ɑːˈmɑːruː / (AmE), / məʊˈviːmientoʊ rɛvəˌluːʃɪəˈnɑːriəʊ ˈtuːpak ɑːˈmæruː / (BrE)
a Marxist-Leninist guerrilla organization in Peru, founded in 1983, that sought to overthrow the Peruvian government and establish a Marxist regime; also known for its involvement in terrorist activities and connections with other militant groups in South America.
Peruvian Marxist-Leninist guerrilla group (1983–)

Examples

The Movimiento Revolucionario Tupac Amaru gained international attention after the Japanese embassy hostage crisis in Lima.
Reports suggest that the Movimiento Revolucionario Tupac Amaru maintained links with other insurgent groups in South America.
The Peruvian government launched a major campaign against the Movimiento Revolucionario Tupac Amaru in the late 1990s.

Etymology

Named after Túpac Amaru II, an 18th-century indigenous Peruvian revolutionary leader.
