five-petaled

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪv ˌpɛtəld / (AmE), / ˈfaɪv ˌpɛtəld / (BrE)
(of a flower) having five petals.
having five petals (of a flower)

Examples

The wild rose is easily recognized by its five-petaled blossoms.
Botanists often classify flowers based on whether they are five-petaled or have a different number of petals.
She admired the delicate, five-petaled white flower growing by the path.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: four-petaled - having four petals (of a flower)
  • Degree Higher: six-petaled - having six petals (of a flower)
