bluefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbluːfɪʃ / (BrE)
A bluish, warm-water marine fish (Pomatomus saltatrix), popular as food and game and known for following schools of small fish into shallow waters.
a bluish marine food and game fish

Examples

The fishermen caught several bluefish off the coast during the early morning.
Grilled bluefish is a local delicacy in many seaside towns.
A large bluefish swam near the surface, following a school of baitfish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The fatty bluish flesh of the bluefish, eaten as food.
fatty bluish fish flesh (food)

Examples

The chef grilled a fresh fillet of bluefish with lemon and herbs.
Many restaurants along the coast serve smoked bluefish as an appetizer.
I bought some bluefish at the market to prepare for dinner tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluefish - a marine fish with a blue or greenish back, found in Atlantic waters
