to decolour

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to cause something to lose its color or become lighter, especially by removing or fading the original color
make lighter or remove color
british

Examples

Several chemicals can decolour fabric when left to soak overnight.
Prolonged exposure to sunlight will decolour the curtains, making them look old.
He used a special solution to decolour the stained paper for preservation.

Synset Examples

The sun bleached the red shirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decolor - to remove color from something (US spelling)
  • Synonym: bleach - to make something white or lighter by removing color

Etymology

From Latin de- (removal) + color (color); British spelling of 'decolor'.
