Arrhenatherum elatius

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɛnəˈθɪərəm ɪˈleɪʃiəs / (AmE), / əˌrɛnəˈθɪərəm ɪˈleɪtɪəs / (BrE)
A coarse, perennial Eurasian grass resembling oat, commonly found on roadside verges, rough grassland, and hay meadows, and introduced to North America for forage purposes.
a tall perennial Eurasian grass species

Examples

Arrhenatherum elatius is often found growing in wildflower meadows managed for biodiversity.
Farmers sometimes plant Arrhenatherum elatius to provide forage for grazing animals.
You can easily identify Arrhenatherum elatius by its tall, loose flower heads and oat-like appearance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name combining Greek 'arrhen' (male) and 'ather' (beard or awn), with Latin 'elatius' (taller).
