Kobus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊbəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊbəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, antelopes found in sub-Saharan Africa, including waterbucks and their close relatives.
a genus of African antelopes (waterbucks)

Examples

The genus Kobus includes several antelope species adapted to wetlands.
Scientists observed a herd of Kobus grazing near the riverbank.
Within Africa, members of the Kobus genus are commonly found in marshy regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterbuck - a species of large antelope found near water in Africa

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek kōbos (a kind of African antelope).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊbəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊbəs / (BrE)
A genus of antelopes known as waterbucks, native to sub-Saharan Africa, recognized for their lyre-shaped horns and habitat near water.
genus of African waterbucks (antelopes)

Examples

The genus Kobus includes both the waterbuck and the lechwe.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Kobus genus in central Africa.
Members of Kobus are easily identified by their preference for wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterbuck - a large antelope found near water in Africa

Etymology

From Modern Latin Kobus, based on the Afrikaans word 'koevoet' (crowbar), referencing horn shape.
