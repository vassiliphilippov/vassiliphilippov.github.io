registered

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdʒɪstɚd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstəd / (BrE)
officially listed or recorded as having the necessary qualifications, especially to practice a profession or perform a specific function
officially listed as qualified

Examples

To work as a registered nurse in this country, you must pass specific exams.
She visited a registered dietitian for advice on her meal plans.
Only registered electricians are allowed to sign off on the safety of this building.

Synset Examples

a registered pharmacist
a registered hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licensed - having official permission to do something, especially professionally
  • Antonym: unregistered - not officially recorded or enlisted

Etymology

From Latin 'registrare', from 'regesta' (things recorded).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdʒ.ɪ.stɚd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒ.ɪ.stəd / (BrE)
officially listed or recorded by an authority or organization.
officially listed or recorded

Examples

The company only hires registered nurses to ensure a high standard of care.
He sent the documents by registered mail for extra security.
You must be a registered user to access premium features.

Synset Examples

record is made of ‘registered mail’ at each point on its route to assure safe delivery
registered bonds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unregistered - not officially listed or recorded

Etymology

From the verb 'register', from Old French 'register', from Medieval Latin 'registrare' (to enter in a register).

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdʒɪstɚd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstəd / (BrE)
(of an animal) officially recorded with or certified by a recognized breed association, especially in a stud book.
officially recorded animal (breed association)

Examples

The kennel club only accepts registered dogs for this competition.
She breeds registered Arabian horses on her farm.
A registered Hereford bull was sold at the auction yesterday.

Synset Examples

a registered Percheron

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purebred - an animal of pure breed, especially one registered with a breed association

Etymology

From 'register' + -ed, referring to animals recorded in official lists.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01965708-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdʒɪstɚd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstəd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or vessel) officially recorded with the appropriate authorities and furnished with documents specifying ownership and other details.
officially documented (of a vehicle or boat)

Examples

The yacht is not allowed to leave the harbor until it is properly registered.
Only registered boats are permitted to participate in the annual regatta.
He showed the coast guard the registered documentation for his vessel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unregistered - not officially recorded or documented with authorities

Etymology

From register + -ed, meaning 'having been entered into an official record.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00790179-s
Synset POS: s
