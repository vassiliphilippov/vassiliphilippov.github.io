skew-whiff

1. ADJ

/ skjuːˈwɪf / (AmE), / skjuːˈwɪf / (BrE)
not straight or level; twisted, askew, or turned to one side
not straight; askew
british

Examples

The picture on the wall was skew-whiff after the earthquake.
His glasses always sit skew-whiff on his nose, no matter how often he adjusts them.
She noticed the blinds were skew-whiff and straightened them before the guests arrived.

Synset Examples

a … youth with a gorgeous red necktie all awry
his wig was, as the British say, skew-whiff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position
  • Antonym: straight - in a straight or correct position

Etymology

Possibly from the Scots phrase 'skew-whiff' meaning askew, of uncertain origin.

2. ADV

/ skjuːˈwɪf / (AmE), / skjuːˈwɪf / (BrE)
Not straight or aligned; turned or twisted to one side.
not straight; lopsided or crooked
british informal

Examples

Her painting hung skew-whiff on the wall, making the room look messy.
His glasses always sit skew-whiff after he naps on the sofa.
The sign was skew-whiff after last night's storm.

Synset Examples

rugs lying askew
with his necktie twisted awry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position
  • Antonym: straight - in a direct or properly aligned position

Etymology

Originating in Britain, possibly from the Scottish 'skew' (to move sideways, to squint) and 'whiff' (gust of wind), with imitative or colloquial formation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00273600-r
Synset POS: r
