enginery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪ.nɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪ.nər.i / (BrE)
machines and equipment consisting of engines collectively; engine-powered machinery as a whole
machinery consisting of engines

Examples

The factory floor was crowded with all manner of enginery humming away.
He admired the complex enginery that powered the steamship across the ocean.
Early military enginery changed the course of many battles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machinery - machines collectively; the working parts of a machine

Etymology

From Latin *ingenium* (engine, device) via the English word 'engine'.
