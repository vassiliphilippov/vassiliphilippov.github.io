Lysiloma bahamensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪsɪˈloʊmə bəˈheɪmɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsɪˈləʊmə bəˈheɪmɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a tropical tree of the West Indies, Florida, and Mexico, resembling the tamarind and bearing long flat seed pods.
a tropical tree of the West Indies and Florida

Examples

The wood of the Lysiloma bahamensis is valued for making furniture in some Caribbean regions.
In southern Florida, the Lysiloma bahamensis grows wild along the coast.
Botanists studied the unique pods produced by Lysiloma bahamensis trees found in the Bahamas.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Lysiloma and the species epithet bahamensis (referring to the Bahamas).
