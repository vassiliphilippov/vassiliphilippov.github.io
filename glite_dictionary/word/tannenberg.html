Tannenberg

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.ən.bɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈtan.ən.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
a decisive battle of World War I in 1914, in which German forces defeated the Russian army near the town of Tannenberg in East Prussia.
battle of World War I (1914), German defeat of Russians

Examples

The Battle of Tannenberg marked a turning point early in World War I.
Many Russian soldiers were captured during the fighting at Tannenberg.
Historians often cite Tannenberg as a classic example of military strategy.

Etymology

Named after the town of Tannenberg (now Stębark, Poland), from German Tannen (firs) + Berg (mountain).
