Aetobatus narinari

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːtəˈbeɪtəs ˌnæ.rɪˈnɑːr.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌiːtəˈbeɪtəs ˌnæ.rɪˈnɑːr.i / (BrE)
A large species of ray with a back covered in white or yellow spots, widely found in warm seas; known as the spotted eagle ray.
species of spotted eagle ray

Examples

Divers were thrilled to spot an Aetobatus narinari gliding gracefully through the coral reef.
The Aetobatus narinari is easily identified by its distinctive pattern of white spots.
Conservationists are studying the migration patterns of Aetobatus narinari in the Caribbean Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted eagle ray - common name for Aetobatus narinari, a species of ray with spotted back

Etymology

From Latinized Neo-Latin names: 'Aetobatus' from Greek 'aetós' (eagle) and 'batis' (ray or flatfish); 'narinari' of unknown origin, likely an indigenous name.
