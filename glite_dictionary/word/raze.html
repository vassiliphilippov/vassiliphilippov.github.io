to raze

1. VERB

/ reɪz / (AmE), / reɪz / (BrE)
to completely destroy a building, town, or other structure so that nothing remains; to demolish to the ground
demolish to the ground

Examples

The old stadium will be razed to make way for a new shopping center.
Many historic buildings were razed during urban redevelopment in the 1960s.
The storm threatened to raze entire villages along the coast.

Synset Examples

The building was levelled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - to pull or knock down a building; destroy completely
  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something

Etymology

From Middle English *rasen*, from Old French *raser* (to scrape, erase), from Latin *rasare* (to scrape, shave).

3. VERB

/ reɪz / (AmE), / reɪz / (BrE)
to completely destroy or demolish a building, structure, or area so that nothing remains
destroy or level completely

Examples

The city decided to raze the old stadium to make space for a park.
Lightning struck the tower, threatening to raze it to the ground.
Developers planned to raze the block of houses despite protests from residents.

Synset Examples

She urged the council to raze the abandoned factory.
The fire will raze the forest if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demolish - to pull or knock down a building or structure
  • Antonym: build - to construct by putting parts or materials together

Etymology

From Middle English 'rasen', from Old French 'raser', from Latin 'rasare' (to scrape, shave closely), from 'radere' (to scrape).
