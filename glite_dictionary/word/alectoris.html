Alectoris

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktərɪs / (AmE), / əˈlɛktərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of partridges in the family Phasianidae, native to Eurasia and North Africa
a genus of partridges

Examples

The red-legged partridge belongs to the Alectoris genus.
Researchers observed several species of Alectoris in the mountainous region.
The distribution of Alectoris extends throughout southern Europe and parts of Asia.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'alektōr' meaning 'rooster, cock'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktərɪs / (AmE), / əˈlɛktərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of partridges found in Eurasia and North Africa, including species such as the chukar and red-legged partridges.
genus of Eurasian partridges

Examples

The Alectoris genus includes the well-known chukar and red-legged partridges.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Alectoris species across several European countries.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot several members of the Alectoris genus during their field trip.

Etymology

From Greek *alektoris* (a hen, partridge).
