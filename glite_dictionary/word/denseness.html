denseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛns.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛns.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a high amount of matter or units packed within a given space; density
the degree of compactness in space

Examples

The denseness of the forest made it hard to see very far.
Scientists measured the denseness of the metal to determine its composition.
The denseness of the crowd caused delays at the event entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: density - the degree of compactness of a substance

Etymology

From the adjective 'dense' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnsnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛnsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being slow to understand or having limited mental ability
mental slowness

Examples

Her denseness made it difficult for her to follow the complex instructions.
He was frustrated by the denseness of his classmates during the discussion.
Sometimes his denseness led to misunderstandings in group projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or common sense
  • Synonym: witlessness - lack of intelligence or wit
  • Antonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills

Etymology

From dense + -ness; 'dense' from Latin densus (thick, crowded).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛns.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛns.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being closely packed or crowded together.
quality of being crowded or compact

Examples

The denseness of the forest made it difficult to walk through.
I was surprised by the denseness of the crowd at the concert.
Urban planners must consider the denseness of residential areas when designing public parks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: density - the degree to which something is compacted or crowded together
  • Antonym: sparsity - the quality of being thinly dispersed or scattered

Etymology

From 'dense' + '-ness'; 'dense' from Latin 'densus' (thick, crowded).
