autochthon

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈtɑːkθɑn / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkθɒn / (BrE)
a member of the earliest known inhabitants of a region; an original or indigenous inhabitant.
original inhabitant of a region

Examples

The aboriginal tribes were considered the autochthon of the island.
Some myths describe kings as direct descendants of the land's autochthon.
Archaeologists study the culture of the autochthon populations in ancient Greece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigene - a person or thing native to a particular place
  • Antonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From Greek 'autokhthōn' (sprung from the land itself), from 'autos' (self) + 'khthōn' (earth).
