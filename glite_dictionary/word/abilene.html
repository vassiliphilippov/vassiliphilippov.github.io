2. PROPN

/ ˈæbɪˌliːn / (AmE), / ˈæbɪliːn / (BrE)
A town in central Kansas, United States, known as the boyhood home of President Dwight D. Eisenhower.
town in Kansas, US

Examples

Dwight D. Eisenhower spent much of his childhood in Abilene.
Many tourists visit Abilene to see the Eisenhower Presidential Library and Museum.
Abilene is located about ninety miles west of Topeka in central Kansas.

Etymology

Named after Abilene, Texas, which was itself named after a passage in the Bible meaning 'grassy plain.'
