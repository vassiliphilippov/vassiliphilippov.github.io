Dante Alighieri

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑnteɪ ˌælɪˈɡɛri / (AmE), / ˈdanteɪ ˌalɪˈɡɪəri / (BrE)
An Italian poet (1265–1321) renowned for writing the Divine Comedy, an epic poem describing a journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Paradise.
Italian poet; author of the Divine Comedy

Examples

Dante Alighieri is best known for his masterpiece, the Divine Comedy.
Many scholars consider Dante Alighieri the father of the Italian language.
Tourists in Florence often visit the house where Dante Alighieri was born.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Divine Comedy - Epic poem by Dante Alighieri about a journey through the afterlife

Etymology

Italian, from his full name Durante Alighieri.
