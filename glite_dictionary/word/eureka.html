Eureka

1. NOUN

/ juˈriːkə / (AmE), / juˈriːkə / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and nickel with high electrical resistance and a low temperature coefficient, used to make resistance wire
copper-nickel alloy for resistance wire

Examples

Eureka is often chosen for precision resistors due to its stable resistance over temperature.
The laboratory uses Eureka wire in the construction of heating coils.
Because Eureka resists corrosion, it's ideal for electrical applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constantan - an alloy of copper and nickel with similar properties
