dissyllable

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪˌsɪl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪˌsɪl.ə.bəl / (BrE)
A word consisting of two syllables.
word with two syllables

Examples

The word table is a common example of a dissyllable.
In poetry, dissyllable words can affect the meter of a line.
Students were asked to list as many dissyllables as they could find in the passage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trisyllable - a word with three syllables
  • Degree Lower: monosyllable - a word with one syllable

Etymology

From Greek disyllabos, from di- (two) + syllabē (syllable).
