XX

1. NUM

/ ˌɛks ˈɛks / (AmE), / ˌɛks ˈɛks / (BrE)
the Roman numeral for the number twenty (20).
Roman numeral for twenty

Examples

The ancient inscription was dated with the Roman numeral XX.
She marked her twentieth birthday by writing XX on the cake.
Page XX of the book contains the list of diagrams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twenty - the cardinal number 20

Etymology

From Latin numerals, where 'X' stands for ten; thus, 'XX' is ten plus ten.

2. NOUN

/ ɛks ɛks / (AmE), / ɛks ɛks / (BrE)
the normal combination of two X chromosomes present in the cells of typical female mammals, determining female sex; used chiefly in genetics and biology.
normal female sex chromosomes (genetics)
medical

Examples

Genetic testing revealed that she had an XX karyotype, confirming the typical female chromosome pattern.
In mammals, individuals with an XX chromosome pair usually develop as females.
The presence of two XX chromosomes is characteristic of most female humans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: XY - normal male complement of sex chromosomes (one X and one Y)

Etymology

Denotes the pairing of two X chromosomes, from the use of 'X' to label these sex chromosomes.
