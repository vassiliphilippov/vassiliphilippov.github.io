fascism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæˌʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfæʃɪzəm / (BrE)
A political system or ideology that supports a centralized, authoritarian government, and opposes democracy and liberalism, often emphasizing nationalism and suppressing opposition.
authoritarian political ideology

Examples

The rise of fascism in Europe led to significant political and social upheaval in the 20th century.
Many historians have studied how economic crises can fuel the spread of fascism.
His speeches were criticized for containing rhetoric reminiscent of fascism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: democracy - a political system in which government is by the people, typically through elected representatives
  • Synonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom

Etymology

From Italian *fascismo*, from *fascio* (bundle, group), symbolizing unity and authority.
