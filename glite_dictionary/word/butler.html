butler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.lə / (BrE)
the chief male servant in a large household, responsible for serving meals and managing the wine and dining rooms
chief household servant

Examples

The butler greeted guests at the door and directed them to the dining room.
Their family's butler has managed the wine cellar for over twenty years.
In the old mansion, the butler oversaw the other servants and arranged elegant dinners.

Etymology

From Old French 'bouteillier' (cupbearer, officer in charge of wine), from 'bouteille' (bottle).
