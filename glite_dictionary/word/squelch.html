squelch

1. VERB

/ skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to make a soft, sucking sound, often when walking on wet ground or when something is pressed into mud
make a soft, sucking sound

Examples

Her boots squelched as she walked across the muddy field.
The wet sponge squelched between his fingers as he cleaned the dish.
After the rain, every step on the trail squelched loudly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squelch - to suppress or silence (another sense of 'squelch')

2. NOUN

/ skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
a forceful or cutting remark that silences or humiliates someone
cutting, silencing remark

Examples

The room fell silent after his witty squelch shut down the argument.
She delivered a perfect squelch to the heckler during the debate.

Related Terms

/ skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
an electronic circuit in a receiver that automatically suppresses the output when the signal is weaker than background noise
an electronic noise-suppressing circuit

Examples

The radio's squelch feature eliminated the annoying static between transmissions.
Adjust the squelch control to prevent background noise from coming through the speakers.
Most modern walkie-talkies include an automatic squelch circuit to improve audio clarity.

4. VERB

/ skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to walk noisily through soft, wet ground, typically making a squelching sound
walk noisily through wet ground

Examples

We had to squelch across the muddy path to reach the cabin.
Her boots squelched as she made her way through the sodden field after the rain.
The children laughed as they squelched through the wet grass after the storm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to splosh - to move with splashing or squelching sounds, especially in wet conditions

Etymology

Examples

He squelched the mud in his hands, shaping it into a ball.

6. VERB

/ skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to suppress or crush something completely, especially opposition or unwanted ideas
suppress or crush completely

Examples

The government tried to squelch the protests before they could gain momentum.
Rumors spread quickly, but the company was able to squelch them with an official statement.
He attempted to squelch any criticism by threatening those who spoke out.

Synset Examples

squelch any sign of dissent
quench a rebellion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quash - to reject or suppress forcefully
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

Probably imitative of a squishing or crushing sound, later applied figuratively to suppressing actions.
