dewberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌbɛri / (BrE)
a trailing bramble plant of the rose family, especially of North America, bearing edible black or dark purple berries similar to blackberries
a trailing bramble with blackberry-like fruit

Examples

We found a patch of dewberry bushes near the riverbank.
The dewberry has a sweet, tangy flavor that is perfect for making jam.
She gathered a basket full of ripe dewberries on her morning walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - an edible soft fruit, black or dark purple, from a related bramble plant

Etymology

From Middle English dewbery, possibly so called because the fruit appears during the time when dew is heavy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈduˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌbəri / (BrE)
A small, dark edible fruit similar to a blackberry, produced by several species of trailing bramble bushes.
a blackberry-like fruit from trailing brambles

Examples

We picked wild dewberries along the edge of the woods.
The pie was filled with sweet dewberries gathered that morning.
In early summer, dewberries ripen on low, thorny bushes near the creek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - an edible soft fruit, black or very dark purple when ripe, from bramble bushes

Etymology

From 'dew' + 'berry', likely referring to the fruit's cool, moist habitats.
