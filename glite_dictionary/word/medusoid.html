medusoid

1. ADJ

/ məˈduː.sɔɪd / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuː.sɔɪd / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a medusa, especially a jellyfish.
resembling a medusa or jellyfish

Examples

The jellyfish exhibited a distinctly medusoid shape under the microscope.
Some corals have a medusoid larval stage during their life cycle.
The medusoid movement allows the animal to propel itself through water efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medusa - a free-swimming sexual form of a coelenterate such as a jellyfish

Etymology

From 'Medusa,' the mythological figure and zoological term, plus '-oid' (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ məˈduː.sɔɪd / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuː.sɔɪd / (BrE)
a free-swimming, umbrella-shaped sexual form in the life cycle of certain coelenterates, such as jellyfish, characterized by a gelatinous body and tentacles
free-swimming sexual form of some coelenterates
medical

Examples

The jellyfish spends a part of its life cycle in the medusoid stage.
Marine biologists collected samples of the medusoid forms found in coastal waters.
Unlike the polyp stage, the medusoid is free-swimming and able to reproduce sexually.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyp - attaching, non-swimming form in coelenterates

Etymology

From Medusa (a genus of jellyfish) + -oid (form or resemblance).
