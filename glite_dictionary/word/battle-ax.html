battle-ax

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl ˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌaks / (BrE)
a woman, especially a wife, who is forceful, domineering, and sharp-tongued, often in a way that is regarded as aggressive or intimidating
forceful, domineering woman (derogatory)
informal offensive

Examples

Everyone was afraid to contradict the battle-ax who ran the committee.
The old movie featured a classic battle-ax as the main antagonist.
He joked that his mother-in-law could be a real battle-ax when she was angry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon lady - a powerful and intimidating woman

Etymology

Derived from the literal sense of 'battle-axe', a large axe used in combat, used figuratively from early 20th century to describe a formidable woman.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbætl ˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbætl ˌæks / (BrE)
A large, broad-bladed ax designed for use as a weapon in combat.
large ax used as a weapon

Examples

The knight swung his battle-ax with great force at his opponent.
A Viking battle-ax was discovered during the archaeological dig.
She admired the ornate engravings on the ancient battle-ax displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadax - an ax with a wide blade, used for hewing timber or as a weapon

Etymology

From 'battle' (combat) + 'ax', referring to its use in warfare.
