Nucifraga

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈsɪfrəɡə / (AmE), / njuːˈsɪfrəɡə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the crow family known as nutcrackers, found in Eurasia and North America.
genus of nutcracker birds

Examples

The Eurasian Nucifraga is well known for storing pine seeds in the forest.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of Nucifraga populations in the alpine regions.
A member of the crow family, the Nucifraga specializes in cracking open nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutcracker - a bird of the genus Nucifraga

Etymology

From Latin *nux* (nut) and *frangere* (to break).

2. NOUN

/ nuːˈsɪfrəɡə / (AmE), / njuːˈsɪfrəɡə / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the crow family Corvidae, commonly known as nutcrackers, found in forests of Europe, Asia, and North America.
a genus of birds (nutcrackers) in Corvidae

Examples

The Nucifraga species are renowned for their ability to crack open tough pine cones.
Researchers observed a Nucifraga collecting seeds for the winter.
Members of the Nucifraga genus play an important role in their forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutcracker - a bird of the genus Nucifraga, known for cracking nuts

Etymology

From Latin 'nux' (nut) and 'frangere' (to break), referring to the bird's habit of cracking nuts.
