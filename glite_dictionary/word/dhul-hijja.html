Dhu'l-Hijja

1. NOUN

/ dʊl hɪˈdʒɑː / (AmE), / dʊl hɪˈdʒɑː / (BrE)
The twelfth month of the Islamic calendar, during which the Hajj pilgrimage takes place; has an extra day in leap years.
twelfth month of the Islamic calendar

Examples

Muslims from around the world travel to Mecca during Dhu'l-Hijja to perform the Hajj pilgrimage.
The festival of Eid al-Adha is celebrated on the tenth day of Dhu'l-Hijja.
In leap years, Dhu'l-Hijja contains an extra day, making it thirty days long.

Etymology

From Arabic ذُو الْحِجَّة‎ (dhū al-ḥijja), meaning 'possessor of the pilgrimage'.
