Gavialis gangeticus

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈvaɪəlɪs ɡænˈdʒɛtɪkəs / (AmE), / ɡəˈvaɪəlɪs ɡænˈdʒɛtɪkəs / (BrE)
A large fish-eating crocodilian native to India, characterized by its long, slender snout.
large Indian crocodilian with long snout

Examples

The endangered Gavialis gangeticus is now limited to a few rivers in northern India.
Researchers observed the hunting techniques of Gavialis gangeticus in its natural habitat.
The unique snout of Gavialis gangeticus helps it catch fish more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gharial - large fish-eating crocodilian of northern Indian rivers

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Gavialis' from the Hindi 'ghariyal' and 'gangeticus' meaning 'of the Ganges.'
