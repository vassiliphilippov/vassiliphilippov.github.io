to soft-soap

1. VERB

/ sɔft soʊp / (AmE), / sɒft səʊp / (BrE)
to try to persuade or influence someone by using insincere praise or flattery
persuade with insincere flattery

Examples

He tried to soft-soap the manager into giving him a raise.
You can't soft-soap me—I know when you're being insincere.
She spent all morning soft-soaping the guests with compliments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to butter up - to flatter someone excessively for personal gain
  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively or insincerely for personal gain
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or point out faults

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'soft soap' (liquid soap), extended in the 19th century to mean smoothing someone over with words.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɔft ˌsoʊp / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌsəʊp / (BrE)
to use flattering or insincerely kind talk to persuade or influence someone
flatter to persuade

Examples

He tried to soft-soap his boss with compliments before asking for a raise.
Don't think you can soft-soap me into changing my mind.
She soft-soaped the committee members with kind words before making her request.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely, especially to gain favor
  • Synonym: to butter up - to praise or flatter someone excessively to gain favor

Etymology

Originally from the figurative sense of 'soft soap', referring to smooth, ingratiating talk likened to the gentleness of soft soap (the literal product).

3. VERB

/ ˈsɔft ˌsoʊp / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌsəʊp / (BrE)
to flatter or try to persuade someone using insincere praise or smooth talk, often to achieve a personal advantage
flatter or persuade with insincere praise

Examples

He tried to soft-soap the manager in hopes of getting a raise.
Don't soft-soap me; I know you want a favor.
She soft-soaped the committee to approve her project.

Synset Examples

He used soft-soap to charm his way out of trouble.
His soft-soap tactics didn't convince anyone.
She always soft-soaps her clients to keep them happy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely
  • Synonym: to butter up - to praise or flatter someone excessively to gain favor

Etymology

Refers to the literal 'soft soap' (mild soap), used metaphorically since the 19th century for smoothing over or flattering.
