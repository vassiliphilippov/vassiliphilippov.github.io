allopatry

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑː.pə.tri / (AmE), / əˈlɒ.pə.tri / (BrE)
The occurrence of closely related organisms in separate, non-overlapping geographical areas, preventing interbreeding.
separate geographic distribution (biology)
medical

Examples

The concept of allopatry plays a crucial role in understanding how new species can form.
Two populations became isolated through allopatry, resulting in genetic divergence over time.
Allopatry reduces the possibility of gene flow between these animal groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympatry - occurrence of related species in the same geographic area

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) + 'patra' (fatherland).
