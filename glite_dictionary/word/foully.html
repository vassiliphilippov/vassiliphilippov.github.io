foully

1. ADV

/ ˈfaʊlli / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlli / (BrE)
In an unfair, shocking, or morally wrong manner.
in a morally wrong or unfair way

Examples

The referee admitted that the player had been foully treated during the match.
He was foully accused of a crime he did not commit.
The rebels were foully betrayed by someone they trusted.

Synset Examples

this internationally known writer was foully condemned by the Muslim fundamentalists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfairly - in an unjust or inequitable manner
  • Antonym: justly - in a fair or morally right way

Etymology

From Middle English "foulli," from "foul" + adverbial suffix "-ly."

2. ADV

/ ˈfaʊli / (AmE), / ˈfaʊli / (BrE)
In a wicked, immoral, or shameful manner.
in a wicked or shameful manner

Examples

The knight was foully betrayed by his closest friend.
Her reputation was foully tarnished by false accusations.
The company acted foully in hiding evidence from the authorities.

Synset Examples

two policemen were foully murdered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedly - in a wicked way
  • Synonym: shamefully - in a shameful way

Etymology

From 'foul' (Old English 'fūl', meaning 'dirty' or 'morally corrupt') + '-ly'.
