Chanukkah

1. NOUN

/ xɑːˈnʊkə / (AmE), / xæˈnʊkə / (BrE)
a Jewish festival lasting eight days, commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC, celebrated with the lighting of the menorah
Jewish festival of lights, eight days

Examples

My family lights the menorah every night during Chanukkah.
We exchange small gifts with friends and relatives for each of the eight days of Chanukkah.
The story of Chanukkah is taught in many religious school programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanukkah - alternative spelling of Chanukkah

Etymology

From Hebrew 'ḥanukkāh' meaning 'dedication'.
