Faeroese

1. NOUN

/ ˌfeɪəroʊˈiːz / (AmE), / ˌfeərəʊˈiːz / (BrE)
the Scandinavian language spoken in the Faroe Islands, closely related to Icelandic
the language of the Faroe Islands

Examples

Many people in the Faroe Islands speak Faeroese as their first language.
The grammar of Faeroese is complex and shares similarities with Icelandic.
She is studying Faeroese to better understand Faroese culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Icelandic - a North Germanic language spoken in Iceland
  • Degree Lower: Danish - a North Germanic language spoken in Denmark

Etymology

From Danish 'færøsk', relating to the Faroe Islands.
