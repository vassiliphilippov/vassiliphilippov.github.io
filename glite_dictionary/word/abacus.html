abacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbəkəs / (AmE), / ˈabəkəs / (BrE)
A frame with rods or grooves along which beads are slid by hand, used for performing arithmetic calculations, especially in ancient and traditional cultures.
manual calculating device with rods and beads

Examples

Children in some schools learn math by using an abacus to perform basic calculations.
Merchants in ancient China relied on the abacus for quick arithmetic.
She demonstrated how to add numbers using a wooden abacus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - an electronic or mechanical device for performing mathematical calculations

Etymology

From Latin 'abacus', from Greek 'abax' (slab, board, tile).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbəkəs / (AmE), / ˈabəkəs / (BrE)
A flat slab or tablet placed horizontally atop the capital of a column to support the architrave in classical architecture.
tablet on column capital in architecture

Examples

In classical temples, the abacus helps distribute the weight from the architrave to the capital below.
Many Corinthian columns feature an ornate abacus with intricate carvings above the capital.
The architect explained how the abacus plays a critical role in ancient Greek column design.

Etymology

From Latin *abacus* (counting board; slab), from Greek *abakos*, originally referring to a flat surface.
