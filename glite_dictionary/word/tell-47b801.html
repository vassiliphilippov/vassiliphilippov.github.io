to tell

1. VERB

/ tɛl / (AmE), / tɛl / (BrE)
to give instructions to or direct someone to do something with authority
to instruct or order someone

Examples

My manager told me to finish the report by Friday.
She told her son to clean his room before dinner.
The teacher told the class to open their textbooks.

Synset Examples

I said to him to go home
She ordered him to do the shopping
The mother told the child to get dressed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - to command someone to do something
  • Synonym: command - to give an authoritative order
  • Degree Lower: to ask - to request someone to do something (less forceful)

Etymology

From Old English 'tellan' (to count, enumerate, recount, tell), related to German 'zählen' (to count).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00748704-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɛl / (AmE), / tɛl / (BrE)
To inform someone about something with certainty or confidence.
inform confidently or with certainty

Examples

I tell you, there is no way she made that mistake.
Can you tell me where the nearest bank is?
He told everyone the news with great confidence.

Synset Examples

I tell you that man is a crook!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information to someone
  • Synonym: to notify - to formally or officially tell someone about something
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep from being known; to hide

Etymology

From Old English 'tellan' (to count, recount, relate), related to Old Norse 'telja', German 'zählen'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
