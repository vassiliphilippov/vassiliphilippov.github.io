Sporozoa

1. NOUN

/ spɔːrəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / spɔːrəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A former classification for a group of parasitic protozoans that are typically immobile, including organisms such as malaria parasites, plasmodia, and coccidia.
group of parasitic protozoans
medical

Examples

Sporozoa include important disease-causing organisms such as those responsible for malaria and toxoplasmosis.
Scientists once classified certain parasites as Sporozoa, but the group is now divided into several new phyla.
The life cycle of Sporozoa often involves multiple hosts and complex developmental stages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic organism of the kingdom Protista

Etymology

From Greek 'sporos' (seed) + 'zōion' (animal).

former class of parasitic protozoans
medical

Examples

The malaria parasite is an example of a Sporozoa species.
Researchers studied how Sporozoa infect their hosts at different life stages.
Some Sporozoa are responsible for serious diseases in both humans and animals.
