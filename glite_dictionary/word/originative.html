originative

1. ADJ

/ əˈrɪdʒəˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnətɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or power to create or originate something; creative.
having creative power or ability

Examples

Her originative ideas led the company to new heights in innovation.
The artist was praised for his originative approach to sculpture.
Scientific progress often depends on the work of originative thinkers.

Synset Examples

a creative imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative - having the ability to create or invent things
  • Antonym: imitative - copying or following another's example

Etymology

From Latin 'originatus', past participle of 'orīrī' (to rise, to originate).

2. ADJ

/ əˈrɪdʒɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnətɪv / (BrE)
having the ability to originate, create, or produce new ideas or things; containing the seeds of later development
creative; able to produce new things

Examples

Her originative approach to problem-solving inspired the whole team to think differently.
The professor is known for his originative theories that have shaped modern philosophy.
Those originative inventions laid the groundwork for much of today's technology.

Synset Examples

seminal ideas of one discipline can influence the growth of another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative - able to create new things or ideas
  • Antonym: unimaginative - lacking originality or creativity

Etymology

From Latin 'originativus', from 'origo' meaning 'beginning, source'.
