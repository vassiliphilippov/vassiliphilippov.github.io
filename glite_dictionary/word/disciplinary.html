disciplinary

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsəpləˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪplɪnəri / (BrE)
connected with the enforcement or maintenance of discipline, especially in behavior or organization.
relating to discipline or enforcement of rules

Examples

The school has strict disciplinary procedures for handling student misconduct.
Her disciplinary record was reviewed before she was considered for promotion.
Management took disciplinary action against several employees who broke company rules.

Synset Examples

disciplinary problems in the classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - intended as punishment

Etymology

From late Latin disciplinarius, from disciplina (instruction, knowledge, discipline).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03072860-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsəˌplɛˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪplɪn(ə)ri / (BrE)
relating to a particular branch or field of academic study
relating to an academic discipline

Examples

The research was published in a journal known for its disciplinary rigor.
Interdisciplinary programs often combine the strengths of two or more disciplinary approaches.
Each disciplinary field has its own methodologies and standards.

Synset Examples

economics in its modern disciplinary sense

Etymology

From Latin disciplina (instruction, knowledge, discipline) + -ary.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03072486-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.əˌplɪ.ner.i / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.ɪ.plɪ.nər.i / (BrE)
intended to enforce or promote discipline or order, especially by correcting or punishing misbehavior.
intended to promote discipline

Examples

The school implemented disciplinary actions to address repeated absenteeism.
He was given a disciplinary warning after violating company policies.
Strict disciplinary rules were enforced in the military academy.

Synset Examples

the teacher's action was corrective rather than instructional
disciplinal measures
the mother was stern and disciplinary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrective - intended to correct or remedy faults or errors

Etymology

From Latin disciplina (instruction, knowledge), with English suffix -ary.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01303596-s
Synset POS: s
