plainspoken

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪnˌspoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌspəʊkən / (BrE)
expressing oneself in a simple and direct way, without using complicated or elaborate language
using simple and direct language

Examples

The senator was known for his plainspoken responses, which appealed to many voters.
Her plainspoken advice made it easy for everyone to understand what needed to be done.
As a plainspoken leader, she earned respect for her honesty and clarity.

Synset Examples

a plainspoken country doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straightforward - honest, clear, and direct in speech or manner
  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing

Etymology

Formed from 'plain' (simple, clear) + 'spoken' (past participle of speak).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪnˌspoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌspəʊkən / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner of speech) speaking in a direct, honest, and straightforward way, without trying to hide what is meant.
direct and honest in speech

Examples

She is known for her plainspoken approach to difficult topics, never avoiding the hard questions.
His plainspoken manner sometimes surprises people who are used to more tactful conversation.
The senator gained respect for his plainspoken honesty during debates.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting
a blunt New England farmer
I gave them my candid opinion
forthright criticism
a forthright approach to the problem
tell me what you think — and you may just as well be frank
it is possible to be outspoken without being rude
plainspoken and to the point
a point-blank accusation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Synonym: blunt - straightforward in speech, even if not polite
  • Antonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly

Etymology

Formed from 'plain' (clear, straightforward) + 'spoken' (past participle of 'speak').
