Mande

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːn.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪ / (BrE)
a group of closely related African languages of the Niger-Congo family, spoken chiefly from Senegal to the Ivory Coast
West African language group

Examples

Linguists study the Mande languages to trace the history of West African peoples.
The Mande group includes several widely spoken languages in Mali and Guinea.
He specialized in comparative research on Mande phonology and grammar.

Etymology

From the name of the Mandé people of West Africa.
