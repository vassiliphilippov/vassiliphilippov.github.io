Shoshonian

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈʃoʊniən / (AmE), / ʃəʊˈʃəʊniən / (BrE)
A subfamily of Uto-Aztecan languages spoken mainly in the southwestern United States, including languages such as Shoshone, Comanche, and Hopi.
Uto-Aztecan language subfamily

Examples

Linguists have studied the Shoshonian languages to understand their relationship with other Native American tongues.
Comanche is one of the languages classified under the Shoshonian subfamily.
The Shoshonian group includes languages spoken across several western states in the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shoshone - a Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Shoshone people

Etymology

From Shoshone (a Native American people and their language) + -ian.
