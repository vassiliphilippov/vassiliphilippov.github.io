oscillatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.sə.ləˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ɒˈsɪl.ə.təri / (BrE)
characterized by or involving regular back-and-forth movement or vibrations
having regular vibrations or movements

Examples

The oscillatory motion of the pendulum continued for several minutes before stopping.
Scientists studied the oscillatory behavior of the electrical circuit.
Earthquakes often generate oscillatory ground movements that can damage buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibratory - involving, causing, or characterized by vibration
  • Antonym: steady - not changing or fluctuating, regular and unvarying

Etymology

From Latin oscillatio (a swinging or oscillating), from oscillare (to swing).
