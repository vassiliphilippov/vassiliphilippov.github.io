tarsier

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːr.si.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.sɪ.ə / (BrE)
a small nocturnal tree-dwelling primate native to Southeast Asia, especially Indonesia and the Philippines, with large eyes and long fingers, feeding only on insects and small animals
small nocturnal primate of Southeast Asia

Examples

The tarsier uses its huge eyes to hunt insects in the darkness of the rainforest.
A tarsier can turn its head almost 180 degrees to spot prey.
Many tourists visit the Philippines to catch a glimpse of the elusive tarsier.

Etymology

From French *tarsier*, from New Latin *Tarsius*, referring to the elongated tarsus bones in the animals' feet.
