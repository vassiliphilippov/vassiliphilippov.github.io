audile

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔː.daɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔː.daɪl / (BrE)
relating to the sense or process of hearing.
relating to hearing

Examples

The psychologist noted that the patient had a predominantly audile learning style.
An audile impression can be more powerful than a visual one for some people.
His audile memory allowed him to recall entire conversations word for word.

Synset Examples

auditory processing
an audile person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory - relating to hearing or the auditory sense

Etymology

From Latin audīre (to hear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdaɪl / (BrE)
a person whose mental imagery is predominantly auditory rather than visual or motor
person with auditory mental imagery
medical

Examples

As an audile, she often remembers information by repeating it to herself.
Teachers may notice that audiles learn best through listening and discussion.
He identified himself as an audile, finding it easier to imagine sounds than pictures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visual - a person whose mental imagery is primarily visual
  • Antonym: motor - a person whose mental imagery is primarily related to movement

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear), modeled on 'visual'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10185266-n
Synset POS: n
