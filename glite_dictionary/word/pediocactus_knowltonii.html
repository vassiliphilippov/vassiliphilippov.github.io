Pediocactus knowltonii

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdioʊˈkæktəs naʊlˈtoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪəʊˈkaktəs nəʊlˈtəʊni.aɪ / (BrE)
A small, clustering cactus native to the southwestern United States, recognized as a threatened species due to its rarity and limited distribution.
rare threatened cactus species of the US Southwest

Examples

The critically endangered Pediocactus knowltonii is found in only a few remote locations in New Mexico.
Conservationists are working hard to protect habitats suitable for Pediocactus knowltonii.
Botanists surveyed the area and found several healthy examples of Pediocactus knowltonii thriving among the rocks.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'pedion' (plain) + 'cactus'; species named after F. H. Knowlton, American paleobotanist.
