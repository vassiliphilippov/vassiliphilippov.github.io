moire

1. ADJ

/ mwɑːˈreɪ, mɔːˈreɪ / (AmE), / mwɑːˈreɪ, mɔːˈreɪ / (BrE)
(of silk or other fabric) having a wavelike or rippled appearance, typically produced by a special finishing process.
having a wavelike pattern (fabric)

Examples

She wore a moire gown that shimmered with elegant waves under the lights.
The designer chose moire silk for the curtains to add a luxurious texture to the room.
A moire ribbon was tied around the gift, giving it a delicate, wavy sheen.

Etymology

From French 'moiré', past participle of 'moirer' (to produce a watered effect).

2. NOUN

/ mwɑːr / (AmE), / ˈmwɑːreɪ / (BrE)
A silk fabric that has a rippled or wavy appearance, often produced by passing the material through engraved rollers.
silk fabric with a wavy pattern

Examples

The dress was made of shimmering moire that caught the light with every movement.
She selected a moire ribbon to add a touch of elegance to her hat.
Antique furniture is sometimes upholstered with richly colored moire fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taffeta - a crisp, smooth woven fabric, similar to moire

Etymology

From French 'moiré', from past participle of 'moirer' (to give a watered appearance to silk), from 'moire' (mohair).
