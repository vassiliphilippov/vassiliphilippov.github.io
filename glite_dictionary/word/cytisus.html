Cytisus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈtiːsəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtʃaɪsəs / (BrE)
A genus of stiff or spiny evergreen or deciduous shrubs, native to the Old World, commonly known as broom.
genus of shrubs (broom)

Examples

The gardener planted a row of Cytisus along the edge of the driveway.
Many species within the Cytisus genus are valued for their brilliant yellow spring blossoms.
Botanists often study Cytisus to understand the adaptation of shrubs to dry habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - any of various shrubs of the genus Cytisus or similar genera, with slender green branches and yellow flowers

Etymology

From Greek kytisos, the ancient name for a kind of leguminous shrub.
