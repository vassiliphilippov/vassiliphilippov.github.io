extroversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.strəˈvɝː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈvɜː.ʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being outgoing, socially confident, and oriented towards external activities and interaction with others
quality of being outgoing and sociable

Examples

Her extroversion helped her quickly make friends at the new school.
The job is ideal for someone with a high level of extroversion.
Psychologists often assess extroversion as part of personality tests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introversion - the quality of being shy and concerned with one's own thoughts
  • Synonym: extraversion - alternative spelling of extroversion, same meaning

Etymology

From Latin extra (outside) + versio (a turning), modelled on 'introversion'.
