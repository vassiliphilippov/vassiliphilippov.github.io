hock

1. VERB

/ hɑk / (AmE), / hɒk / (BrE)
to disable an animal, especially a horse, by cutting the tendons at the hock joint
disable by cutting the hock (animal)

Examples

The cruel poachers hocked the horses so they could not escape.
Farmers in the past sometimes hocked livestock as a punishment for straying.
Deliberately hocking a working animal is now illegal in many countries.

british

3. NOUN

/ hɑk / (AmE), / hɒk / (BrE)
the joint on the hind leg of a four-legged animal, especially a horse, that corresponds to the human ankle
joint on the hind leg of hoofed animals

Examples

The horse injured its hock while running in the field.
Tenderness in the hock can be a sign of joint problems in cattle.
The veterinarian examined the swollen hock for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankle - the joint connecting the foot with the leg in humans

Etymology

From Middle English *hoke*, related to Dutch *hock* and German *Hocke*, meaning 'bend of the knee.'
