vivid

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪd / (BrE)
producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind; evoking lifelike images
evoking lifelike images in the mind

Examples

The author’s vivid storytelling makes the characters come alive in your imagination.
She gave a vivid description of her journey through the rainforest.
His memory of the accident is still so vivid that it feels like it happened yesterday.

Synset Examples

pictorial poetry and prose
graphic accounts of battle
a lifelike portrait
a vivid description

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphic - giving a clear and explicit picture
  • Synonym: lifelike - very similar to the person or thing represented
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness, interest, or clarity

Etymology

From Latin 'vividus' (lively), from 'vivere' (to live).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪd / (BrE)
(of memories, descriptions, etc.) producing clear, powerful, and detailed images in the mind, often as if experienced directly.
clear and lifelike (memory, description)

Examples

She still has a vivid memory of her first day at school.
His novel is filled with vivid descriptions that bring the setting to life.
The dream was so vivid that it felt like reality.

Synset Examples

a vivid recollection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive or understand; not vague or confused
  • Antonym: dull - lacking vividness, detail, or impact (of memory or description)

Etymology

From Latin vividus (full of life, lively), from vivere (to live).

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪd / (BrE)
(of a color) intensely deep or bright; having the highest degree of saturation.
intensely deep or bright color

Examples

The painting is dominated by a vivid shade of red that instantly catches the eye.
She wore a vivid green dress that stood out among the crowd.
The artist chose vivid colors to express the intensity of the landscape.

Synset Examples

vivid green
intense blue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull - (of a color) not bright or vivid; lacking intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'vividus' meaning 'lively, vigorous'.
