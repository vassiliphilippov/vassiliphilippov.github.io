terminus a quo

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜrmɪnəs eɪ ˈkwoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtɜːmɪnəs ɑː ˈkwəʊ / (BrE)
the starting point or earliest limiting boundary, especially in legal or philosophical contexts.
starting point; earliest limiting boundary
legal

Examples

The statute lists 1900 as the terminus a quo for property ownership disputes.
Determining the terminus a quo of the contract is essential for legal clarity.
Historians debate the exact terminus a quo of the Roman Empire's decline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terminus ad quem - the ending point or latest limiting process

Etymology

Latin, literally 'the point from which' (terminus = boundary or end, a = from, quo = which).
