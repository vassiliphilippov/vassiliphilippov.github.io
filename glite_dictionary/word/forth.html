forth

1. ADV

/ fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
out from a place or source and into view or notice
out into view or notice

Examples

She called his name, and he stepped forth to answer.
The butterfly broke forth from its cocoon.
The witness came forth to reveal important information.

Synset Examples

came forth from the crowd
put my ideas forth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - toward the front or ahead

Etymology

From Old English forth (forward, onward).

3. ADV

/ fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
(formal) Forward in time, order, or degree; onward from a particular point.
forward in time or order

Examples

The tradition has continued from that year forth in our community.
From the sixth century forth, major changes transformed the region.
Let it be known from this day forth that the policy will change.

Synset Examples

from that time forth
from the sixth century onward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onward - Moving forward in time, proceeding further

Etymology

Old English 'forð' (forward, away), related to German 'fort'.
