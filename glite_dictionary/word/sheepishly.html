sheepishly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃiːpɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpɪʃli / (BrE)
In a shy, embarrassed, or self-conscious manner, typically as a result of having done something wrong or foolish.
in an embarrassed or self-conscious way

Examples

He sheepishly apologized for forgetting her birthday.
The student sheepishly admitted he hadn't done his homework.
After spilling his drink, Mark looked around sheepishly.

Synset Examples

sheepishly he handed her back the money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confidently - in a self-assured or bold manner

Etymology

From sheepish + -ly.
