workforce

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːk.fɔɹs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.fɔːs / (BrE)
All the people who are employed or available for work in a particular company, industry, or country.
people available for work

Examples

The company expanded its workforce to meet the growing demand for its products.
A skilled workforce is essential for maintaining high productivity.
The government is introducing new training programs to prepare the workforce for technological changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor force - all the workers available for work (synonym for workforce)

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝrkˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌfɔːs / (BrE)
All the people who are available for work or are employed in a particular country, industry, or organization.
people available for work

Examples

The company's workforce has grown rapidly over the past decade.
Automation is expected to reduce the need for a large workforce in manufacturing.
Women now make up over half of the national workforce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor force - the total number of people employed or seeking employment
  • Synonym: staff - the people who work for an organization

Etymology

Formed from "work" + "force" in the early 20th century.
