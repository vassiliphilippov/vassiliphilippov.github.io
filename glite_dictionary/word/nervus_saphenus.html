nervus saphenus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəs səˈfinəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs səˈfiːnəs / (BrE)
a branch of the femoral nerve that supplies the skin on the inner side of the leg and foot
branch of femoral nerve in the leg
medical

Examples

The nervus saphenus is vulnerable during knee surgery due to its superficial location.
Damage to the nervus saphenus may result in numbness along the inner side of the leg and foot.
Surgeons carefully identify the nervus saphenus to prevent inadvertent injury during procedures on the lower limb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saphenous nerve - the sensory branch of the femoral nerve supplying the inner leg and foot

Etymology

From Latin nervus (nerve) + saphenus (hidden; also the term used for the saphenous vein and nerve).
