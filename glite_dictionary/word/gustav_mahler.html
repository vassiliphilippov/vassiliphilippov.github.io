Gustav Mahler

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡʊs.tɑːv ˈmɑː.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʊs.tav ˈmɑː.lə / (BrE)
(1860–1911) Austrian composer and conductor, known for his symphonies and orchestral songs, influential in the transition from the Romantic to the modern era in classical music.
Austrian composer and conductor (1860–1911)

Examples

Many musicians consider Gustav Mahler's symphonies to be masterpieces of the orchestral repertoire.
The music of Gustav Mahler often explores profound and complex emotions.
Gustav Mahler worked as a conductor at leading opera houses across Europe.
