matchbush

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈmatʃˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
any of several North American plants in the genus Gutierrezia, having small yellow flower heads that resemble match heads
a plant of the genus Gutierrezia

Examples

The arid landscape was dotted with clusters of matchbush thriving under the harsh sun.
Many ranchers consider the matchbush a weed because it can spread quickly across rangeland.
With its tiny yellow flowers, the matchbush is a common sight in southwestern deserts during late summer.

Etymology

From 'match' (as in a stick used to start a fire) + 'bush', referring to the plant’s small, match-like flower heads.
