Podiceps nigricollis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.dɪˈsɛps nɪˈɡrɪk.əlɪs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊ.dɪˈsɛps nɪˈɡrɪk.ə.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of small grebe with yellow ear tufts and a black neck, found in Eurasia, southern Africa, and western United States; commonly known as the black-necked grebe.
species of grebe (black-necked grebe)

Examples

During migration, the Podiceps nigricollis gathers in large flocks on saline lakes.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Podiceps nigricollis in the wetlands of southern Africa.
The striking yellow ear tufts make the Podiceps nigricollis easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-necked grebe - common name for Podiceps nigricollis

Etymology

From Latin: Podiceps (grebe genus), nigricollis ('black necked').
