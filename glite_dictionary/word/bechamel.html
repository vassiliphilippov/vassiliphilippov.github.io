bechamel

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪʃəˈmɛl / (AmE), / ˌbɛʃ.əˈmɛl / (BrE)
A white sauce made by thickening milk with a mixture of butter and flour (roux), often used as a base in various dishes.
white sauce of butter, flour, and milk

Examples

He made a creamy bechamel to pour over the lasagna before baking.
You start the recipe by preparing a classic bechamel sauce.
The dish was finished with a rich layer of bechamel and grated cheese.

Etymology

From French béchamel, named after Louis de Béchameil, a French courtier.
