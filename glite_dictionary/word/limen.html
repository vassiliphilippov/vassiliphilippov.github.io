limen

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈlʌɪ.mɛn / (BrE)
the smallest amount of a stimulus that can be detected by the senses; a sensory threshold
minimum detectable stimulus level

Examples

In clinical trials, the limen of taste sensitivity is determined using solutions of varying concentrations.
The patient was unable to perceive sound below a certain limen, indicating hearing loss.
Scientists seek to measure the visual limen in order to understand how sensitive the eye is to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshold - the point at which a stimulus is strong enough to produce a response

Etymology

From Latin *limen* (threshold).
