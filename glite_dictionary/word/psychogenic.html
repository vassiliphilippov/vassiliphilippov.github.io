psychogenic

1. ADJ

/ saɪ.kəˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / saɪ.kəˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a symptom or illness) having a mental or emotional origin rather than a physical one.
originating from psychological causes
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that her symptoms were psychogenic rather than physical.
Researchers studied cases of psychogenic blindness among trauma survivors.
The patient's illness was determined to be psychogenic in nature.

Synset Examples

a psychogenic disorder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychosomatic - (of a condition) involving both mind and body, especially bodily symptoms caused by mental factors

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind, soul) and 'genic' from 'genes' (born of, produced by).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a disorder or symptom) having a psychological origin rather than a physical one.
originating from psychological causes
medical

Examples

Some forms of amnesia are considered psychogenic rather than resulting from brain injury.
The patient’s pain was believed to be psychogenic in origin, not the result of any physical disease.
Doctors distinguished between psychogenic and organic causes for her seizures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychosomatic - (of a physical illness or condition) caused or aggravated by a mental factor

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind, soul) + 'genic' (produced by).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02917372-a
Synset POS: a
