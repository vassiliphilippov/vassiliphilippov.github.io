to magnetize

1. VERB

/ ˈmæɡnɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) magnetic or to give magnetic properties to it
make magnetic; impart magnetism

Examples

The technician used a coil to magnetize the metal rod for the experiment.
Leaving a screwdriver near a magnet can magnetize it over time.
Scientists need to magnetize the material before it can be used in electronic devices.

Synset Examples

The strong magnet magnetized the iron shavings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demagnetize - to remove magnetic properties from (something)

Etymology

From French magnétiser, from magnétisme (magnetism) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00400694-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmaɡnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to attract or influence strongly, as if by a magnet
attract or influence strongly

Examples

Her performance managed to magnetize everyone in the room.
The charismatic leader could magnetize crowds wherever he went.
The story was so intriguing that it seemed to magnetize my attention.

Synset Examples

She magnetized the audience with her tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the attention of someone by being interesting or charming
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or force away

Etymology

From French 'magnétiser', from 'aimant' (magnet), ultimately from Greek 'magnēs' (Magnesian stone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00779567-v
Synset POS: v
