remarkably

1. ADV

/ rɪˈmɑrkəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːkəbli / (BrE)
to a striking or exceptional degree; in an unusual or noteworthy way
to a striking or exceptional degree

Examples

The weather this spring has been remarkably warm for the region.
She handled the difficult situation remarkably well, showing great composure.
Bamboo is remarkably strong yet incredibly lightweight.

Synset Examples

she was unusually tall
Notably missing from the network's fall line-up are any half-hour scripted comedies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notably - to a notable or significant degree
  • Synonym: exceptionally - to an exceptional or unusual degree
  • Antonym: ordinarily - in an ordinary or usual way

Etymology

From 'remarkable' + '-ly'. 'Remarkable' from French 'remarquable', from 'remarquer' (to remark).
