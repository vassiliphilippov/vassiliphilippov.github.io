terrapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.ə.pɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ə.pɪn / (BrE)
a small edible North American turtle with webbed feet, found in fresh or brackish water
an edible North American water turtle

Examples

The diamondback terrapin is native to the coastal marshes of the eastern United States.
Local fishermen often catch terrapins in the shallow rivers during the summer.
She read a book about protecting terrapin habitats from pollution and development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - a reptile with a bony or leathery shell and flippers or webbed toes

Etymology

Probably from Algonquian (Powhatan) *torope*, meaning 'a little turtle.'
