vertical

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɝːtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of business organization) relating to or involving all stages, from production to distribution, within a single company or structure.
covering all business stages, production to distribution

Examples

The company adopted a vertical strategy by acquiring suppliers and distributors.
Tech firms often expand through vertical integration to control every step of production.
Our vertical approach gives us more control over product quality and delivery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: horizontal - involving just one stage or level of business, not integration across stages
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02834979-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɝː.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to different levels within a hierarchy, such as social class or income group.
concerning hierarchical levels

Examples

The report highlights significant vertical differences in income among urban residents.
Sociologists study both vertical and horizontal mobility within communities.
Many policies aim to reduce vertical inequality between social classes.

Synset Examples

vertical social mobility

Related Terms

  • Antonym: horizontal - relating to the same level in a hierarchy; used in contrast with 'vertical' in social sciences

Etymology

From Latin verticalis, from vertex (highest point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01206814-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɝː.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Standing or pointing straight up, at a right angle to the horizon or base line.
upright; at right angles to the horizon

Examples

The lamp must be kept in a perfectly vertical position to work properly.
Rainwater runs down the vertical walls of the building during storms.
He drew a vertical line down the center of the page.

Synset Examples

a vertical camera angle
the monument consists of two vertical pillars supporting a horizontal slab
measure the perpendicular height

Related Terms

  • Antonym: horizontal - parallel to the horizon or ground; not vertical
  • Synonym: upright - in a vertical position; straight up
  • Synonym: perpendicular - at an angle of 90 degrees to a given line, surface, or ground

Etymology

From Late Latin verticalis (overhead, zenith), from Latin vertex (highest point).
