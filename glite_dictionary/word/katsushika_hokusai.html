Katsushika Hokusai

1. PROPN

/ kɑːtsuːˈʃiːkə hoʊˈkuːsaɪ / (AmE), / kat.suːˈʃiː.kə həʊˈkuː.saɪ / (BrE)
(1760–1849) A renowned Japanese painter and printmaker, best known for his woodblock print series 'Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji', which influenced Western art and the Impressionists.
famous Japanese painter and printmaker

Examples

The famous wave depicted by Katsushika Hokusai is recognized around the world.
Many impressionist painters were inspired by the works of Katsushika Hokusai.
The British Museum holds several prints by Katsushika Hokusai in its collection.
