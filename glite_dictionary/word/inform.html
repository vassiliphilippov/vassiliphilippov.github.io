to inform

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːm / (BrE)
(formal) To give character, substance, or distinctive quality to (something); to imbue with an essential quality.
give character or essence to

Examples

A belief in equality continues to inform their approach to social policy.
The artist's worldview informs every aspect of her work.
Classic French style informs the design of the entire building.

Synset Examples

The principles that inform modern teaching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imbue - to inspire or permeate with a feeling or quality

Etymology

From Latin informare (to give form or shape to, instruct), from in- (in) + forma (form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00523693-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to give someone information about something; to tell someone about a fact or situation
to give information to someone

Examples

The police informed the victim about the progress of the case.
Please inform me if there are any changes to the schedule.
She informed her manager of her decision to resign.

Synset Examples

I informed him of his rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to notify - to formally tell someone about something
  • Synonym: to advise - to recommend or inform someone about what to do
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin informare (to shape, instruct), from in- (into) + forma (form, shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00833312-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to secretly give information about someone, often to authorities, especially about wrongdoing or criminal activity
secretly give information about someone

Examples

After being caught, he decided to inform on his accomplices to avoid a harsher sentence.
She was afraid that someone might inform on her to the police.
Their neighbor informed on them, leading to their arrest.

Synset Examples

She had informed on her own parents for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to deliver someone to an enemy or authority by treachery
  • Synonym: snitch - to inform on someone in a secretive or disloyal way

Etymology

From Latin informare (to shape, instruct), via Old French informer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00834860-v
Synset POS: v
