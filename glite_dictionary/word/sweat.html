sweat

1. VERB

/ swɛt / (AmE), / swɛt / (BrE)
to excrete perspiration through the pores in the skin, especially as a result of heat, physical exertion, or stress
to excrete perspiration

Examples

He began to sweat heavily after running up the stairs.
Some people sweat more than others during exercise.
I always sweat when I'm nervous before a big presentation.

Synset Examples

Exercise makes one sweat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perspire - to excrete sweat through the pores of the skin

Etymology

From Old English 'swātan', related to 'swāt' (noun) meaning 'perspiration'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067316-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ swɛt / (AmE), / swɛt / (BrE)
A salty, clear fluid produced by sweat glands in the skin, usually released in response to heat, stress, or physical activity.
salty fluid from sweat glands

Examples

After the long run, sweat dripped down his face and neck.
The gym was so hot that her shirt was soaked with sweat.
Wiping the sweat from his brow, he took a break from working in the garden.

Synset Examples

sweat poured off his brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspiration - the process or result of sweating

Etymology

From Old English swāt (sweat), related to Old High German swāz and Old Norse sviti.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05413094-n
Synset POS: n
