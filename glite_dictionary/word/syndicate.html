syndicate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪndɪkət / (BrE)
a news agency that supplies articles, features, or photographs to multiple newspapers or media outlets for simultaneous publication
news agency syndicating content

Examples

The article was distributed by a syndicate to newspapers across the country.
Many small newspapers rely on a syndicate for national news stories and editorials.
She sold her column to a syndicate, ensuring its appearance in dozens of publications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agency - an organization that provides a particular service

Etymology

From Medieval Latin syndicatūs (office of a syndic), from French syndicat (council, syndicate).

WordNet ID: oewn-08372251-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪndɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to sell articles, television programs, or photos to several publications or broadcasting stations for simultaneous publication or broadcast
sell content to multiple media outlets

Examples

The columnist's weekly articles are syndicated to newspapers across the country.
The show was originally broadcast on one channel but was later syndicated to dozens of local stations.
He plans to syndicate his travel photography to online publications worldwide.

Etymology

From French syndicat (council, representative body), from Latin syndicus (representative of a corporation).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪndɪkət / (BrE)
an association of companies or organizations formed to carry out a specific business project or to promote a shared interest
business association of companies

Examples

A group of banks formed a syndicate to finance the new railway project.
The newspaper was distributed by a national syndicate of publishers.
The oil companies worked together as a syndicate to explore the region for new resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consortium - an association of companies for a common purpose

Etymology

From Medieval Latin syndicus (representative), from Greek syndikos (advocate).

6. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪndɪkət / (BrE)
an organization or group of people involved in illegal business activities, often associated with organized crime
criminal organization or group

Examples

The police finally dismantled the powerful syndicate that controlled drug trafficking in the city.
Several local gangs had joined together to form a criminal syndicate.
The government is cracking down on the activities of the notorious syndicate operating in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartel - a group of independent organizations working together, often illegally
  • Synonym: gang - an organized group of criminals
  • Synonym: trust - a group of organizations run as a single enterprise (in business), sometimes used for criminal groups

Etymology

From French 'syndicat', from Medieval Latin 'syndicatus', relating to an elected council or organization.
