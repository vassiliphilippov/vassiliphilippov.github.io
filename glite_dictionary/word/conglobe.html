to conglobe

1. VERB

/ kənˈɡloʊb / (AmE), / kənˈɡləʊb / (BrE)
to form into a rounded or globular shape; to gather or shape into a globe
form or become rounded like a globe

Examples

Under a microscope, the cells conglobe when exposed to the solution.
The liquid metal conglobed into tiny spheres as it cooled.
During the experiment, particles began to conglobe in the center of the dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conglomerate - to gather together into a mass or whole
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or spread in different directions

Etymology

From Latin 'conglobo', from 'con-' (together) + 'globus' (globe, sphere).
