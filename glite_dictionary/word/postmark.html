postmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌmɑːk / (BrE)
an official mark stamped on a letter or package by a post office, showing the date and place of mailing
official postal stamp showing date and place

Examples

The envelope still had the original postmark from Paris dated March 12.
Collectors often look for rare stamps and unique postmarks on old letters.
The postmark proved that the package was sent before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - a mark that defaces a stamp to prevent reuse

Etymology

From 'post' (mail) + 'mark' (sign, imprint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07285423-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpoʊstˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to stamp (a letter or package) with an official mark showing the date and place of mailing
stamp mail with official mailing date/address

Examples

The clerk postmarked the letter before sending it overseas.
All outgoing packages are now postmarked electronically at the facility.
Be sure to postmark your application envelope before tomorrow’s deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to mark a postage stamp to prevent reuse

Etymology

From 'post' (mail) + 'mark', referring to the official markings made by postal services.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01276668-v
Synset POS: v
