unsounded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊndɪd / (BrE)
not pronounced; (of a letter or sound) written or represented but not spoken aloud.
not pronounced; silent (of a letter or sound)

Examples

The 'k' in 'knight' is an unsounded letter.
Some French words contain unsounded consonants at the end.
In the word 'honest', the initial 'h' is unsounded.

Synset Examples

the silent ‘h’ at the beginning of ‘honor’
in French certain letters are often unsounded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silent - Not pronounced or voiced (of a letter or sound)

Etymology

From un- (not) + sounded (past participle of sound, meaning 'pronounce').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊndɪd / (BrE)
not having been measured or explored for depth, especially of waters or cavities; too deep to have been sounded or plumbed.
not measured for depth; unexplored

Examples

The divers avoided the unsounded region of the cave, fearing hidden dangers in its depths.
Ancient mariners spoke of unsounded waters where no one knew how deep the ocean lay.
He was fascinated by the unsounded mysteries beneath the earth's surface.

Synset Examples

the profound depths of the sea
the dark unfathomed caves of ocean
unplumbed depths of the sea
remote and unsounded caverns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unplumbed - not measured for depth or unexplored

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'sounded' (measured with a sounding line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00694418-s
Synset POS: s
