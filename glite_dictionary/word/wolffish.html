wolffish

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, elongated, scaleless oceanic fish with sharp teeth and a long dorsal fin that resembles a sail, found in northern Atlantic waters.
large elongate oceanic fish with sharp teeth

Examples

The fisherman was surprised to catch a wolffish in the cold Atlantic waters.
A wolffish can use its sharp teeth to break open clams and sea urchins.
Because of its unusual appearance, the wolffish is often a topic of interest among marine biologists.

Etymology

Named for its wolf-like teeth and predatory appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, fierce northern deep-sea fish with strong teeth and no pelvic fins, valued as food.
large deep-sea fish with strong teeth

Examples

The chef prepared grilled wolffish with lemon and herbs for dinner.
Marine biologists are studying the strong jaws of the wolffish.
The wolffish inhabits the cold, rocky waters of the North Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
