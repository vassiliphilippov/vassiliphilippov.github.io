to suffuse

1. VERB

/ səˈfjuz / (AmE), / səˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to gradually spread through or over, especially with a liquid, color, light, or feeling
to spread through or over

Examples

A warm glow suffused the room as the sun set behind the trees.
Her face was suffused with joy when she heard the good news.
Tears suffused his eyes, making everything look blurry for a moment.

Synset Examples

His whole frame suffused with a cold dew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pervade - to spread through every part of something
  • Synonym: to imbue - to fill or inspire deeply with a feeling or quality

Etymology

From Latin suffūsus, past participle of suffundere ‘to pour beneath’.

2. VERB

/ səˈfjuz / (AmE), / səˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(usually passive) to spread or flush throughout something, so as to fill or cover it with a color, feeling, or quality.
to spread through or over (something)

Examples

A gentle light suffused the room as the sun rose.
Her face was suffused with embarrassment when she realized her mistake.
A sense of calm suffused the audience during the soothing music.

Synset Examples

The sky was suffused with a warm pink color

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pervade - to be present and apparent throughout
  • Synonym: to infuse - to fill or pervade with a quality or feeling
  • Synonym: stain - to color or mark by permeating

Etymology

From Latin *suffūsus*, past participle of *suffundere* meaning 'to pour beneath or over'.

3. VERB

/ səˈfjuz / (AmE), / səˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(of a quality, emotion, color, or light) to gradually spread through or over something, so that it is completely filled or affected.
to spread through or over something

Examples

A gentle pink glow suffused the sky at dawn.
A sense of calm suffused the room as peaceful music played softly.
Warmth suffused his voice as he spoke to the children.

Synset Examples

Joy suffused her face when she heard the news.
Colors suffused the canvas, creating a vibrant painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pervade - to be present throughout; to permeate
  • Synonym: to imbue - to fill or inspire with a feeling or quality
  • Antonym: drain - to remove a quality or substance from something

Etymology

From Latin suffūsus, past participle of suffundere (‘to pour from below, overspread’), from sub- (‘under’) + fundere (‘to pour’).
