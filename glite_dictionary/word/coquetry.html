coquetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkɪtri / (AmE), / ˈkɒkɪtri / (BrE)
playful or flirtatious behavior designed to attract sexual attention
playful flirtatious behavior

Examples

Her coquetry at the party caught everyone's attention and sparked much gossip.
There was an air of coquetry in her comments that made him blush.
He was amused by her obvious coquetry but remained unfazed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirtation - behavior that suggests a playful sexual attraction

Etymology

From French 'coquetterie', from 'coquet' (flirt), ultimately from Middle French 'coq' (rooster), referring to strutting behavior.
