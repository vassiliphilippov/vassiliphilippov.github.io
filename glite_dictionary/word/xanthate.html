xanthate

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænˌθeɪt / (AmE), / ˈzanθeɪt / (BrE)
A chemical compound that is a salt or ester of xanthic acid, used especially in the mining industry for ore flotation.
salt or ester of xanthic acid (chemical)

Examples

The flotation process often relies on the addition of xanthate as a collector reagent.
Researchers studied the properties of several metal xanthate complexes in solution.
Handling xanthate requires caution due to its potential health hazards.

Etymology

From xanthic acid + -ate, following chemical nomenclature.
