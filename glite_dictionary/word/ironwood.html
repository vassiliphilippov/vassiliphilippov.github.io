ironwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwʊd / (BrE)
the very hard and dense wood of any of several trees, noted for its exceptional toughness
extremely hard wood from certain trees

Examples

The craftsman used ironwood to make tools that would last for decades.
Because of its weight and density, ironwood is often chosen for heavy-duty construction.
Finding a piece of genuine ironwood can be quite difficult in some regions.

Etymology

From 'iron' + 'wood', referring to the wood's great hardness and strength.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwʊd / (BrE)
A tree with extremely hard, heavy wood, often referring to several species including a handsome East Indian evergreen with fragrant white flowers used for perfume and railroad ties.
tree with very hard, heavy wood

Examples

The dense, durable timber of the ironwood is prized for construction and furniture making.
Gardeners planted ironwood trees along the avenue for their fragrant blooms and sturdy appearance.
The railway company chose ironwood for its sleepers because of its resistance to decay and wear.

Etymology

From iron + wood, referring to the exceptional hardness of the wood.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwʊd / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree of eastern North America (hop hornbeam) with very hard wood, commonly called 'ironwood.'
tree with very hard wood (hop hornbeam)

Examples

The dense wood of the ironwood makes it ideal for tool handles.
We found an old ironwood growing at the edge of the forest.
Because ironwood is so tough, it is often used for making durable furniture.

Etymology

From 'iron' + 'wood,' referring to the wood's hardness.

4. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌwʊd / (BrE)
A small, slow-growing deciduous tree of northern Iran with a low domed shape, valued for its very hard wood.
tree of northern Iran with very hard wood

Examples

The ironwood of northern Iran is admired for its resilience and strength.
Gardeners often plant ironwood trees for their unique domed canopy and slow growth.
Carpenters prize ironwood because its timber is exceptionally hard and durable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornbeam - A similar deciduous tree with hard wood, related to ironwood

Etymology

Named for the extreme hardness of its wood, reminiscent of iron.
