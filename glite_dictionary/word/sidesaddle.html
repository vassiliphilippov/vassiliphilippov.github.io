sidesaddle

1. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌsædəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌsædəl / (BrE)
in a manner of riding a horse with both legs on the same side of the animal, as is traditional for women in certain periods
riding a horse with legs on one side

Examples

She preferred to ride sidesaddle during the historical reenactment.
The queen made her entrance on horseback, sitting gracefully sidesaddle.
During the parade, the rider performed tricks while seated sidesaddle.

Synset Examples

she rode sidesaddle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: astride - riding with a leg on each side of a horse

Etymology

From side + saddle, meaning a saddle designed to be ridden with both legs on one side.

WordNet ID: oewn-00455466-r

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌsædəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌsæd.əl / (BrE)
a saddle designed for a rider, traditionally a woman, to sit with both legs on one side of the horse
saddle for side-seated riding

Examples

In the competition, she rode her horse using a sidesaddle to demonstrate traditional techniques.
The museum displayed an ornate Victorian sidesaddle made of leather and velvet.
Riding sidesaddle requires a different set of skills than riding astride.

Etymology

From 'side' + 'saddle', indicating the side-seated riding position.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04222220-n
Synset POS: n
