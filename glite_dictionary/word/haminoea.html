Haminoea

1. NOUN

/ həˈmɪnioʊə / (AmE), / həˈmɪniːə / (BrE)
A genus of marine bubble shell mollusks found chiefly along the Pacific coast of North America.
genus of marine bubble shells

Examples

Haminoea species can often be found in tide pools along the California coastline.
Researchers discovered a new population of Haminoea in a sheltered bay.
The fragile shell of Haminoea provides limited protection from predators.

Etymology

From New Latin Haminoea, the genus name assigned in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ hæˌmɪniˈoʊə / (AmE), / hæˌmɪniˈəʊə / (BrE)
A genus of small marine gastropod mollusks known as bubble shells, found along the Pacific coast of North America.
genus of marine bubble shells

Examples

The Haminoea are especially abundant in the sheltered bays of California.
Researchers collected several specimens of Haminoea during their marine survey.
The delicate shells of Haminoea can be found washed up on Pacific beaches.

Etymology

Modern Latin, formed from Greek 'haminos' (jar, jug) + '-oea', a zoological suffix.
