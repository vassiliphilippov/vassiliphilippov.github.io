jumper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌm.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.pə / (BrE)
a sleeveless, collarless dress or loose garment worn over a shirt, especially by children as a coverall
children's coverall garment

Examples

The toddler wore a cute jumper over her T-shirt when playing outside.
We dressed our son in a warm jumper before heading to the park.
She bought several colorful jumpers for her children to wear at preschool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overalls - loose-fitting protective garment worn over clothes

Etymology

Probably from the French 'jupe', via late Middle English 'jump' (short coat).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌm.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.pə / (BrE)
A small device used to make or break a temporary electrical connection, typically by bridging contacts on a circuit board.
temporary electrical connector

Examples

Be careful not to misplace the jumper when configuring the motherboard.
You can reset the device by moving the jumper to the next set of pins.
The technician used a jumper to temporarily bypass the faulty connection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shunt - an electrical device placed across a circuit to divert current

8. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpə / (BrE)
(in basketball) a shot taken while the player is jumping, usually releasing the ball at the highest point of the jump.
basketball shot released at jump's peak

Examples

Her signature move is the jumper from the top of the key.
He hit a last-second jumper to win the game for his team.
During practice, she worked on improving the accuracy of her jumper.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: layup - a shot made close to the basket by laying the ball up near the rim
  • Degree Higher: dunk - a shot in which the player jumps and scores by putting the ball directly through the basket
  • Antonym: free throw - an unopposed attempt to score from the free-throw line
