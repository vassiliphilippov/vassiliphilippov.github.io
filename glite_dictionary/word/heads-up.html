heads-up

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdzˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɛdzˌʌp / (BrE)
fully alert, attentive, and watchful; ready to react quickly to what is happening
fully alert and watchful

Examples

The team played a heads-up game and avoided costly errors.
You need to stay heads-up when working in a busy kitchen.
Her heads-up driving helped her avoid an accident on the road.

Synset Examples

played heads-up ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - fully aware and attentive, especially to danger or trouble
  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdzˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɛdzˌʌp / (BrE)
an advance warning or notice about something, especially so that preparations can be made
advance warning or notice
informal

Examples

Thanks for the heads-up about the schedule change; I was able to adjust my plans.
She gave me a quick heads-up that the meeting would start early.
If you hear anything important, could you give me a heads-up?

Synset Examples

he sent a heads-up to the District Attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - statement or signal that something needs caution
  • Synonym: alert - a signal that warns of danger

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdzˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɛdzˌʌp / (BrE)
an act of warning or giving advance notice about something, often informally
advance warning; notice
informal

Examples

I appreciate the heads-up about the road closure before I left for work.
She sent her team a quick heads-up that the client meeting was rescheduled.
Before you travel, here's a heads-up: the train lines might be delayed.

Synset Examples

Thanks for the heads-up about the schedule change.
Just a heads-up, the meeting starts at noon.
He gave me a heads-up about the upcoming deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - an act of alerting someone to danger or potential problems
  • Synonym: notification - an act or instance of notifying; notice

Etymology

From the phrase 'heads up!' used as a call to alert people to danger or attention, originally from sports and military contexts.
