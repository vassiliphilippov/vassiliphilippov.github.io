marlingspike

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrlɪŋˌspaɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɑːlɪŋˌspaɪk / (BrE)
a pointed metal tool used for separating the strands of rope or cable, especially when splicing
pointed tool for rope splicing

Examples

The sailor used a marlingspike to carefully separate the rope strands before splicing.
A marlingspike is an essential part of any good rigger's toolkit.
With a marlingspike, she untangled the tight knots in the thick cable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fid - a conical tool used for splicing rope, often made of wood or bone

Etymology

From Middle English "marlin" (to fasten) + "spike".
