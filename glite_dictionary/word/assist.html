assist

1. VERB

/ əˈsɪst / (AmE), / əˈsɪst / (BrE)
to serve or attend to someone, especially in a professional or supportive role
to serve or wait on someone

Examples

The nurse will assist you during your stay at the hospital.
Do you need someone to assist you with your shopping today?
A friendly clerk assisted the customers throughout their visit.

Synset Examples

May I serve you?
She attends the old lady in the wheelchair
Can you wait on our table, please?
Is a salesperson assisting you?
The minister served the King for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to perform duties or services for someone
  • Synonym: to attend - to give practical help or care to someone

Etymology

From Latin assistĕre, from ad- (to) + sistere (to stand).

WordNet ID: oewn-02546367-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈsɪst / (AmE), / əˈsɪst / (BrE)
to give help or support to someone; to make it easier for someone to do something
give help or support

Examples

Several volunteers assist the elderly residents at the community center.
The nurse will assist the doctor during the operation.
Can you assist me with setting up the new computer?

Synset Examples

Everyone helped out during the earthquake
Can you help me carry this table?
She never helps around the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to make it easier or possible for someone to do something by offering assistance
  • Antonym: hinder - to make it more difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'assistere', meaning 'to stand by or near, to help'.

5. NOUN

/ əˈsɪst / (AmE), / əˈsɪst / (BrE)
(in sports) a play in which a player passes or contributes the ball or puck to a teammate, helping them to score or achieve a successful play.
helping play in sports, leading to a score

Examples

The point guard recorded his tenth assist of the game in the third quarter.
Her crucial assist set up the winning goal for the team.
He leads the league in assists, showcasing his teamwork and skill.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: goal - a point scored in various sports
  • Synonym: pass - transferring the ball or puck to another player in sports

From Latin 'assistere' meaning 'to stand by'.
