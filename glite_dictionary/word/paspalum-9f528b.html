paspalum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæspələm / (AmE), / ˈpaspələm / (BrE)
A tall, tufted perennial grass native to tropical America, widely grown as pasture and forage grass in the southern United States.
tropical American grass used as pasture

Examples

Many cattle ranchers rely on paspalum as a nutritious forage grass for their herds.
The fields were covered in paspalum, providing lush grazing for livestock.
Researchers are studying ways to improve paspalum varieties to withstand drought.

Etymology

From Latin 'paspalum', derived from Ancient Greek 'πάσπαλος' (páspalos), meaning millet.
