fissure

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
A long, narrow opening or crack, especially in a rock or the earth.
a long, narrow crack or opening

Examples

A deep fissure had formed in the glacier after the recent thaw.
The earthquake caused a narrow fissure to appear across the road.
Water seeped into the fissure, gradually widening the crack in the rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a break or split in a surface
  • Synonym: crevice - a narrow opening, especially in a rock

Etymology

From Latin 'fissura' (a cleft or crack), from 'findere' (to split).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
A long, narrow opening or crack in a surface, especially in rock, earth, or a body part.
long narrow crack or opening

Examples

The earthquake left a deep fissure in the road, making it unsafe to drive on.
Tiny fissures formed between the rocks as water seeped in and froze overnight.
Doctors discovered a fissure in the patient's colon during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a line on the surface of something along which it has split without breaking apart

Etymology

From Latin fissura (a cleft, split), from findere (to split).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
(Anatomy) a long, narrow slit or groove that divides an organ, such as the brain or liver, into lobes or parts.
long narrow groove in an organ
medical

Examples

The central fissure separates the brain into left and right hemispheres.
A fissure in the liver divides it into distinct lobes.
During surgery, the doctor identified the fissure running along the surface of the lung.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulcus - A groove or furrow, especially in the brain, less deep than a fissure.

Etymology

From Latin fissura, from findere 'to split'.
