maledict

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæl.əˌdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈmal.ɪ.dɪkt / (BrE)
Cursed; under a curse or subject to a malediction.
under a curse
archaic

Examples

The haunted manor was said to be maledict by the village elders.
In their tales, a maledict prince wandered the forests, seeking redemption.
The artifact was believed to be maledict, bringing misfortune to all who possessed it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cursed - affected by a curse; under a curse
  • Antonym: blessed - consecrated, made holy, or enjoying happiness

Etymology

From Latin maledictus, past participle of maledicere (to speak ill, to curse).

2. VERB

/ ˌmæl.ɪˈdɪkt / (AmE), / ˌmal.ɪˈdɪkt / (BrE)
to wish harm upon; to utter a curse or invoke evil upon someone or something
to curse or wish harm upon
archaic

Examples

The malicious sorcerer maledicted his enemies before vanishing into the night.
In the tale, villagers believed that the old woman could maledict anyone who crossed her.
He warned not to maledict the innocent, for such wishes might rebound upon the speaker.

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to bless - to ask for or bestow divine favor upon
  • Synonym: curse - to call down harm or misfortune upon; to curse

Etymology

From Latin maledicere, from malus (bad) + dicere (to speak).
