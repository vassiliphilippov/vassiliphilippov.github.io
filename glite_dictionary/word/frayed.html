frayed

1. ADJ

/ freɪd / (AmE), / freɪd / (BrE)
(of cloth or other material) worn, unraveled, or tattered along the edges from use or age.
worn or tattered at the edges

Examples

The old jeans had frayed hems from years of use.
She noticed the frayed edges of her favorite blanket.
The book’s cover looked frayed after being carried everywhere.

Synset Examples

frayed cuffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worn - damaged or thin as a result of much use
  • Synonym: tattered - old and torn; in poor condition

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fray', from Middle English 'fraien', from Old French 'fraier' (to rub, wear down).
