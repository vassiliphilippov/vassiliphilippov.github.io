Liopelmidae

1. NOUN

/ li.oʊˈpɛl.mɪ.diː / (AmE), / li.əʊˈpɛl.mɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of primitive frogs native to New Zealand, characterized by ancient anatomical features and lacking certain modern frog adaptations
family of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

The Liopelmidae are among the most ancient frog families still living today.
Scientists have studied Liopelmidae to understand amphibian evolution in New Zealand.
Unlike most frogs, members of the Liopelmidae have remained largely unchanged for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with a short body and long hind legs

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Lio-' (possibly from Greek leios, 'smooth') and '-pelma' (Greek, 'sole of the foot'), with the family suffix '-idae' used in zoology.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪ.oʊˈpɛl.mɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌlaɪ.əʊˈpɛl.mɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of primitive frogs native to New Zealand, comprising a single genus (Leiopelma) distinguished by primitive anatomical features
family of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

The Liopelmidae are considered living fossils among amphibians due to their primitive characteristics.
Researchers observed that members of the Liopelmidae family have unique reproductive behaviors.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered Liopelmidae frogs of New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leiopelma - the single genus of frogs within the family Liopelmidae

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) and 'pelma' (sole of the foot), with standard zoological family suffix '-idae'.
