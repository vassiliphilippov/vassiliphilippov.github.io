Asarum canadense

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛɹəm kəˈnædəns / (AmE), / əˈsɑːrəm kəˈnædəns / (BrE)
A species of low-growing, deciduous perennial plant native to Canada and eastern and central United States, commonly known as wild ginger.
species of wild ginger plant

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used Asarum canadense as a medicinal plant and seasoning.
The spreading leaves of Asarum canadense provide excellent ground cover for shade gardens.
Botanists surveyed populations of Asarum canadense in the eastern United States for signs of habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild ginger - common name for Asarum canadense, a low-growing North American plant

Etymology

From the genus name Asarum, Latinized from Greek 'ásaron' (wild ginger), and the species epithet 'canadense', meaning 'of Canada'.
