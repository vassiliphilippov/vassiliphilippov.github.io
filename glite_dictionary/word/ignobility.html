ignobility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪɡˌnoʊˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪɡnəʊˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ignoble; baseness or dishonor
baseness or dishonor

Examples

Her actions were marked by ignobility rather than the courage we had hoped for.
The novel explores the ignobility hidden beneath the surface of polite society.
He could not forgive himself for the ignobility of his betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nobility - the quality of being noble in character, mind, birth, or rank

Etymology

From Latin ignobilitas, from ignobilis (unknown, base).
