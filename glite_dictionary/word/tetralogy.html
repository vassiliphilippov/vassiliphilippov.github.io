tetralogy

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈtrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / tɛˈtralədʒi / (BrE)
a group or series of four related literary or dramatic works, such as plays, operas, or novels
series of four related works

Examples

The playwright's tetralogy explores the rise and fall of an ancient kingdom.
Fans eagerly awaited the final installment of the epic tetralogy.
Wagner's 'Ring Cycle' is a famous tetralogy in the world of opera.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trilogy - series of three related works (plays, operas, or novels)

Etymology

From Greek tetralogia, from tetra- (four) + -logia (discourse).
