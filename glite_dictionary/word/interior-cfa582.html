interior

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Located within or suitable for use inside a building.
inside or suitable for indoors

Examples

The new furniture was chosen to match the interior colors of the house.
They specialize in interior design for modern apartments.
The museum features an interior courtyard filled with plants and natural light.

Synset Examples

an interior scene
interior decoration
an interior bathroom without windows

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exterior - situated on or suitable for outside a building

Etymology

From Latin *interior* (inner, interior, deeper within).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00956057-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
the inner or inside part, surface, or space of something; the area contained within the outer limits or surface
inner part or surface of something

Examples

The interior of the cathedral was breathtakingly beautiful with intricate stained glass windows.
After the renovation, the interior of the house felt much more spacious and light.
He wiped the interior of the car windshield to remove the condensation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exterior - the outer surface or outside part of something

Etymology

From Latin *interior* (comparative of *intra*, meaning ‘within’), meaning ‘inner’ or ‘further inside’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08605707-n
Synset POS: n

/ ɪnˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriə / (BrE)

7. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
relating to the domestic affairs or matters within a particular country, especially as opposed to foreign or external affairs.
relating to affairs within a country

Examples

The Department of the Interior oversees the nation's natural resources and public lands.
Recent interior policies have focused on economic development and infrastructure.
The president appointed a new interior minister to handle domestic affairs.

Synset Examples

the nation's internal politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domestic - relating to the internal affairs of a country
  • Antonym: foreign - involving another country; outside one's own country

Etymology

From Latin 'interior', comparative of 'inter' (between, among).
