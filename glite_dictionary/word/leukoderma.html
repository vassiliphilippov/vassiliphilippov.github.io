leukoderma

1. NOUN

/ luːkəˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌluː.kəˈdɜː.mə / (BrE)
A congenital skin condition marked by patches of skin lacking pigmentation.
skin condition with unpigmented patches
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with leukoderma after several white patches appeared on his arms.
Some people with leukoderma experience social challenges because of the visible nature of the condition.
Research continues into new treatments for leukoderma and its underlying causes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitiligo - A chronic skin condition causing depigmented patches

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white) and derma (skin).
