civics

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪv.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.ɪks / (BrE)
the study of the rights and duties of citizenship and the functioning of society and government
the study of citizenship and government

Examples

In high school, we studied civics to learn about how government works.
A good understanding of civics helps citizens participate in their communities.
Many educators believe civics should be required in all school curricula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political science - the academic study of government and politics

Etymology

From Latin 'civicus' (relating to a citizen).
