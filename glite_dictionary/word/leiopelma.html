Leiopelma

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.oʊˈpɛl.mə / (AmE), / laɪ.əʊˈpɛl.mə / (BrE)
A genus of primitive frogs native to New Zealand, comprising the sole genus in the family Leiopelmatidae.
genus of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Leiopelma in a remote forest in New Zealand.
The Leiopelma genus is notable for its ancient lineage and primitive characteristics.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of endangered Leiopelma frogs.

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) and 'pelma' (sole of the foot), referring to the frog's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ laɪ.oʊˈpɛl.mə / (AmE), / laɪ.əʊˈpɛl.mə / (BrE)
A genus of primitive frogs native to New Zealand and the sole genus in the family Leiopelmatidae.
genus of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

The Leiopelma genus includes several rare frog species found only in New Zealand.
Scientists study Leiopelma to learn more about ancient frog lineages.
Many species within Leiopelma are threatened due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek leios (smooth) + pelma (sole of the foot), referring to the smooth undersides of the frogs.
