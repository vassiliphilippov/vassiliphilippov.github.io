Digitaria ischaemum

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɪtəriə ɪsˈkiːəməm / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɪtəriə ɪsˈkiːəməm / (BrE)
a low-growing summer annual grass, commonly considered a weed, that spreads by seed and from rooting at the lowest joints of its stems
a species of crabgrass weed

Examples

Digitaria ischaemum thrives in compacted lawns where other grasses may struggle to grow.
Some gardeners use mulch to suppress the spread of Digitaria ischaemum in flower beds.
The golf course has implemented a special herbicide to control Digitaria ischaemum infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - a general term for grasses of genus Digitaria, including several weedy species

Etymology

Digitaria from Latin 'digitus' (finger), referring to the finger-like arrangement of spikelets; ischaemum from Greek 'ischaimos' (weak, feeble), likely describing the plant's habit.
