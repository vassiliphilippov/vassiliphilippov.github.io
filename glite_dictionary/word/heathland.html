heathland

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiθˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈhiːθ.lænd / (BrE)
An area of open, uncultivated land with poor, sandy soil and characteristic low shrub vegetation such as heather.
uncultivated land with low shrubs

Examples

The heathland was covered with purple heather in late summer.
Many rare bird species thrive in the heathland habitat.
Walking across the windy heathland, we enjoyed the wide-open views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moorland - open land with coarse grass and heather, especially in Britain
