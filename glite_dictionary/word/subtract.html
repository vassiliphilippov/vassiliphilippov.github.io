to subtract

1. VERB

/ səbˈtrækt / (AmE), / səbˈtrakt / (BrE)
to take one number or amount away from another; to perform the operation of subtraction
take away one amount from another

Examples

If you subtract ten from twenty, you get ten.
The teacher asked the students to subtract five from each number in the list.
The accountant will subtract expenses before calculating the final profit.

Synset Examples

subtract this amount from my paycheck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deduct - to take away an amount from a total, often in accounting or calculation
  • Antonym: to add - to combine numbers or amounts to get a total; the opposite operation of subtract

Etymology

From Latin 'subtrahere', from 'sub-' (from under) + 'trahere' (to pull).

2. VERB

/ səbˈtrækt / (AmE), / səbˈtrækt / (BrE)
to take a number or amount away from another number or amount
take away (a number or amount)

Examples

If you subtract five from ten, you get five.
The accountant will subtract taxes from your paycheck.
She quickly subtracted the smaller number from the larger one to find the difference.

Synset Examples

this prefix was subtracted when the word was borrowed from French

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deduct - to take away an amount from a total, especially money
  • Antonym: to add - to put numbers or amounts together to get a total

Etymology

From Latin 'subtrahere', from 'sub-' (from under) + 'trahere' (to pull).
