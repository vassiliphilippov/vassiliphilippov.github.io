rehabilitative

1. ADJ

/ riˌhæbɪˈleɪtɪv / (AmE), / riːˌhæbɪˈleɪtətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the process of helping someone return to health, normal life, or good condition after illness, injury, or imprisonment
helping restore health or normal life

Examples

The prison introduced several rehabilitative programs to help inmates reintegrate into society.
She underwent rehabilitative therapy after her knee surgery.
Many experts support a rehabilitative rather than punitive approach to drug addiction.

Synset Examples

from a penal to a rehabilitative philosophy
rehabilitative treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapeutic - relating to the treatment of illness or injury

Etymology

From 'rehabilitate' (from Medieval Latin rehabilitatus, past participle of rehabilitare 'to enable again') + '-ive'.

2. ADJ

/ riˌhæbɪˈleɪtɪv / (AmE), / riˌhæbɪˈleɪtətɪv / (BrE)
intended to help restore health or normal life to someone after illness, injury, or addiction; relating to rehabilitation.
restoring health or condition

Examples

The patient participated in rehabilitative exercises after the knee surgery.
A rehabilitative approach can help offenders reintegrate into society.
Physiotherapists often design rehabilitative programs tailored to individual needs.

Synset Examples

reconstructive surgery
rehabilitative exercises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health, strength, or well-being
  • Synonym: curative - able to cure disease or restore health

Etymology

From rehabilitate + -ive, after the Latin root habilitare (to enable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00588549-s
Synset POS: s
