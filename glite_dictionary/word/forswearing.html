forswearing

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈswɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈswɛərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of formally renouncing, giving up, or surrendering a right, possession, or privilege
act of formally giving up something

Examples

His forswearing of all claims to the estate was noted in the legal documents.
The forswearing of personal ambition is essential in some religious traditions.
After much thought, she made a public forswearing of her royal title.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renunciation - the formal rejection of something, typically a belief, claim, or course of action
  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of giving up something completely

Etymology

From Old English 'forswerian' (to renounce or deny on oath), with suffix '-ing' forming the noun.
