Estonian

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈstoʊniən / (AmE), / ɪˈstəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to Estonia, its people, or their culture
relating to Estonia or Estonians

Examples

The museum displayed several Estonian folk costumes from the 19th century.
She enjoys listening to Estonian music during her travels.
The Estonian government introduced a new digital initiative last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Estonian - the Finno-Ugric language spoken in Estonia

Etymology

From Estonia + -ian, indicating association or belonging.

2. NOUN

/ ɛˈstoʊniən / (AmE), / ɛˈstəʊniən / (BrE)
The official language of Estonia, belonging to the Baltic-Finnic group of the Uralic language family.
the official language of Estonia

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Estonian.
The novel was originally written in Estonian before being translated into English.
Many road signs in Tallinn are written in Estonian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish - language closely related to Estonian, spoken in Finland
  • Synonym: Hungarian - a Uralic language, related but more distantly than Finnish

Etymology

From Estonia, the country name, plus the English suffix -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06970636-n
Synset POS: n
