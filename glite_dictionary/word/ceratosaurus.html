ceratosaurus

1. NOUN

/ sɛrətoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / sɛrətəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized theropod dinosaurs from the Jurassic period, characterized by a short horn on the nose, grasping hands with sharp claws, and a carnivorous diet.
genus of horned Jurassic theropod dinosaurs

Examples

The remains of a ceratosaurus were discovered in the Morrison Formation of North America.
Paleontologists believe the ceratosaurus used its distinctive nasal horn for display or combat.
Unlike many other theropods, ceratosaurus had unusually long, blade-like teeth.

Etymology

From Greek keras, keratos (horn) + sauros (lizard).
