enema

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnəmə / (AmE), / ˈɛnəmə / (BrE)
the injection of a liquid into the rectum through the anus, typically to stimulate bowel evacuation or for diagnostic purposes
injection of liquid into rectum for medical reasons
medical

Examples

The nurse administered an enema before the procedure.
Doctors sometimes use an enema to relieve severe constipation.
A barium enema can help detect abnormalities in the colon.

Etymology

From Greek 'enienai' (to send or inject into), from 'en-' (in) + 'hienai' (to send).
