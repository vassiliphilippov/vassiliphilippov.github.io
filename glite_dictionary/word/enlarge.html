to enlarge

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈlɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to make or become larger; to increase the size, extent, or amount of something
make or become larger

Examples

They decided to enlarge their kitchen to create more space for family gatherings.
This photo can be enlarged without losing much detail.
The company plans to enlarge its product range next year.

Synset Examples

She enlarged the flower beds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expand - to become or make larger in size, number, or importance
  • Antonym: to reduce - to make or become smaller or less in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Old French enlarger, from Latin largus (large, abundant).
