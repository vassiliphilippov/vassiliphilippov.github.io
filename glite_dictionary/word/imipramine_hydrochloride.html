imipramine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪprəˌmin haɪˌdrɑːkləˌraɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪprəmiːn haɪˌdrɒklaɪd / (BrE)
A tricyclic antidepressant medication, often used to treat clinical depression; also known by trade names such as Imavate and Tofranil.
tricyclic antidepressant medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed imipramine hydrochloride to help manage her depression.
Patients taking imipramine hydrochloride should be monitored for possible side effects.
Imipramine hydrochloride is often marketed under the trade name Tofranil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imipramine - a tricyclic antidepressant used to treat depression and other conditions

Etymology

From 'imipramine' (the drug name) plus 'hydrochloride' indicating the hydrochloride salt form.
