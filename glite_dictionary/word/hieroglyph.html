hieroglyph

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪrəˌɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərəˌɡlɪf / (BrE)
a symbol used in a system of writing that uses pictures, especially as used in ancient Egypt
picture symbol in ancient Egyptian writing

Examples

The Rosetta Stone was crucial in deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs.
Each hieroglyph in the tomb told a part of the pharaoh's story.
Scholars study ancient hieroglyphs to learn more about Egyptian civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictogram - a symbol representing a word or phrase through illustration
  • Synonym: cuneiform - an ancient writing system using wedge-shaped marks, mainly of Mesopotamia

Etymology

From Greek hieroglyphikos, from hieros (sacred) + gluphein (to carve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪɚəˌɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərəˌɡlɪf / (BrE)
a written character or mark that is difficult to read, resembling ancient hieroglyphics in its illegibility
illegible or cryptic written character

Examples

Her doctor's handwriting is such a hieroglyph that no one can read the prescription.
The notes on the blackboard were reduced to a single hieroglyph after hours of scribbling.
I could barely make out the hieroglyph on the ancient map.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - hurried or careless writing that is difficult to read
  • Synonym: scribble - hastily or carelessly written text

Etymology

From Greek hieroglyphikos, meaning 'sacred carving', via French hiéroglyphe and Latin hieroglyphus.
