absolutism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səˌlu.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.luː.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A political system or practice in which one authority holds absolute power, often maintained through threat of punishment and violence.
absolute power or authority

Examples

The king's rule was marked by absolutism and intolerance of dissent.
Some historians argue that absolutism leads to abuses of power and civil unrest.
The revolution emerged as a response to decades of absolutism and repression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despotism - the exercise of absolute power, especially in a cruel and oppressive way
  • Synonym: tyranny - cruel and oppressive government or rule
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

From French 'absolutisme', from 'absolu' (absolute), from Latin 'absolūtus' (unrestricted, absolute).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səˌluː.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.luː.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
(in philosophy) the doctrine that reality is fundamentally absolute and not dependent on anything external, often asserting the existence of an absolute being or principle.
philosophical doctrine of absolute reality

Examples

Some philosophers argue that absolutism provides a necessary foundation for morality and ethics.
Hegel is known for his complex system of absolutism in philosophy.
The debate between absolutism and relativism remains central to metaphysical discussions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relativism - the doctrine that knowledge, truth, and morality exist in relation to culture or society and are not absolute

Etymology

From absolute + -ism, modeled on French 'absolutisme'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæbsəˌluːˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
A political system in which a single ruler holds absolute, unrestricted power, not limited by laws or opposition.
rule by an absolute dictator or monarch

Examples

Louis XIV is often associated with the concept of absolutism in European history.
Under absolutism, monarchs claimed divine right to rule without checks on their power.
The rise of absolutism in the seventeenth century changed the political landscape of many nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government in which power is vested in the people

Etymology

From French *absolutisme*, from Latin *absolūtus* (absolute).

4. NOUN

/ ˈæbsəˌluːtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈabsəluːtɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief or practice that a government or ruler should have complete and unrestricted power.
political belief in complete government power

Examples

Many monarchs in seventeenth-century Europe ruled with absolutism and refused to share power.
He criticized absolutism as a threat to individual freedom and good governance.
The rise of absolutism led to conflicts between kings and their subjects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarianism - a system of government that is centralized and dictatorial
  • Antonym: constitutionalism - belief in government limited by a constitution

Etymology

From French *absolutisme*, from Latin *absolutus* (absolute, unrestricted).
