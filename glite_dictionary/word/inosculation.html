inosculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪˌnɑːskyəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɒskyʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the natural or surgical joining of parts or branches of tubular structures, such as blood vessels or tree branches, so that they become continuous
joining of structures (medical/biological)
medical

Examples

The inosculation of blood vessels is crucial for successful tissue transplantation.
Botanists studied the inosculation between the two tree branches, where they fused over time.
Surgeons achieved inosculation of the graft with the patient's existing artery, restoring blood flow.

Etymology

From Latin 'inosculare' (to unite by kissing), from 'in-' (in) + 'osculare' (to kiss).
