butchery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəri / (BrE)
the business or trade of preparing and selling meat as a butcher
the trade of a butcher

Examples

Her family has run a butchery in the village for three generations.
He apprenticed in a butchery before opening his own shop.
Local farmers supply fresh meat to the butchery in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcher - person who prepares and sells meat

Etymology

From Old French 'boucherie', from 'boucher' (butcher).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəri / (BrE)
A building or place where animals are slaughtered and prepared for sale as meat.
building for slaughtering animals

Examples

The new regulations were implemented at every butchery in the region.
Health inspectors visited the butchery to ensure proper sanitation procedures were followed.
The town once had a butchery at the end of Main Street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughterhouse - a building where animals are slaughtered for food

Etymology

From Old French *boucherie* (slaughterhouse), from *boucher* (butcher).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02669573-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəri / (BrE)
the savage and excessive killing of people; massacre or brutal slaughter
savage mass killing; massacre

Examples

The documentary explored the butchery that took place during the civil war.
Many historians have condemned the event as an act of senseless butchery.
Survivors recalled scenes of unimaginable butchery in the abandoned village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - an indiscriminate and brutal slaughter of many people
  • Synonym: slaughter - the killing of many people in a cruel or violent way

Etymology

From Old French *boucherie* (slaughterhouse), from *boucher* (butcher).
