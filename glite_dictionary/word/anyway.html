anyway

1. ADV

/ ˈɛniˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛnɪweɪ / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that explains or supports a previous statement or adds information, often after an interruption or change of subject.
used to add or explain; change of subject

Examples

We missed the train, but anyway, we arrived in time for dinner.
I'm not sure how much it will cost; anyway, we need to fix the car.
She didn't want to go out, but anyway, her friends insisted.

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyhow - used similarly to introduce or emphasize a statement regardless of preceding circumstances
  • Synonym: in any case - used to indicate that something remains true regardless of other factors
  • Antonym: nevertheless - used to introduce a contrasting statement

2. ADV

/ ˈɛniˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈeniweɪ / (BrE)
in any way or manner; by whatever means possible.
by whatever means possible

Examples

If you can't fix the machine, try to finish the project anyway you can.
She wanted the job done anyway possible before the weekend.
They crossed the river anyway they could, even if it meant swimming.

Synset Examples

they came anyhow they could
get it done anyway you can

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somehow - by some means or in some way
  • Synonym: anyhow - in any way or manner; however
