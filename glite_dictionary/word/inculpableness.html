inculpableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌlpəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌlpəblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being innocent or free from blame
innocence; freedom from blame

Examples

His inculpableness was established after a thorough investigation cleared him of all charges.
The lawyer argued passionately for his client's inculpableness in the matter.
She maintained her inculpableness throughout the trial, insisting she had done nothing wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being free from guilt or moral wrong
  • Antonym: culpability - state of being responsible or at fault

Etymology

From Medieval Latin inculpabilis (not to be blamed), from Latin in- (not) + culpabilis (blameworthy).
