Alsatian

1. ADJ

/ ælˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ælˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Alsace or its inhabitants
relating to Alsace or its people

Examples

The Alsatian wine region is famous for its white varietals.
He was born to an Alsatian family with deep roots in the area.
The festival highlights traditional Alsatian cuisine and culture.

Etymology

From Alsace, a region in France, plus '-ian'.

2. NOUN

/ ælˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ælˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Alsace, a region in northeastern France
person from Alsace, France

Examples

As an Alsatian, he grew up speaking both French and German.
Many Alsatians are proud of their unique cultural heritage.
She met several friendly Alsatians during her trip to Strasbourg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frenchman - a native or inhabitant of France

Etymology

From Alsace (region of France) + -ian (denoting origin or citizenship).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09805227-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ælˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ælˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a large breed of dog, also known as the German Shepherd, often used in police work and as a guide for the blind
a large breed of shepherd dog
british

Examples

The police force uses an Alsatian to assist in their search operations.
Her grandfather has trained an Alsatian as a guide dog for the visually impaired.
Many people admire the loyalty and intelligence of an Alsatian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German Shepherd - a large, intelligent breed of working dog

Etymology

Named after Alsace, a region in France, because the breed was first introduced to Britain from that area; British English term for German Shepherd.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02109314-n
Synset POS: n
