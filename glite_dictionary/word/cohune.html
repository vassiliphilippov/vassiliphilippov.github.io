cohune

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈhuːn / (AmE), / kəʊˈhuːn / (BrE)
A tropical American palm tree (Attalea cohune) whose large nuts produce valuable oil and a type of vegetable ivory.
tropical American palm tree (Attalea cohune)

Examples

The cohune palm is valued for the oil extracted from its nuts.
Manufacturers use the hard seeds of the cohune in making buttons and carvings.
Extensive groves of cohune are found in the tropical regions of Central America.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cohune', likely from a Central American indigenous language.
