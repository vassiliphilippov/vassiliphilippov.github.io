bidirectional

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪdəˈrɛkʃənl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪdɪˈrɛkʃənl / (BrE)
Functioning, reactive, or allowing movement in two usually opposite directions.
working in two opposite directions

Examples

Fiber optic cables provide bidirectional communication between network devices.
The bidirectional flow of information makes the system more efficient.
A bidirectional motor can rotate both clockwise and counterclockwise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unidirectional - functioning or moving in only one direction

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'direction' + '-al'.
