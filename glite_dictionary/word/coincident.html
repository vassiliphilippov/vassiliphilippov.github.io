coincident

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈɪnsɪdənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdənt / (BrE)
occurring or existing at the same time; simultaneous
occurring at the same time

Examples

Several coincident power failures occurred across the city last night.
The coincident rise in both interest rates and unemployment surprised economists.
The two signals were so coincident that they were hard to distinguish.

Synset Examples

a series of coincident events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneous - occurring, existing, or done at the same time
  • Synonym: synchronous - happening at the same time; occurring simultaneously

Etymology

From Latin coincidere, from co- (together) + incidere (to fall upon).

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈɪnsɪdənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdənt / (BrE)
(of two or more objects) occupying exactly the same space or position at the same time; matching point for point.
occupying the same position; matching exactly

Examples

The two graphs are coincident along this segment.
When superimposed, the circles appear perfectly coincident.
After careful adjustment, the laser beams became coincident at the focal point.

Synset Examples

coincident circles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concurrent - occurring or existing simultaneously
  • Antonym: separate - not touching or occupying the same space

Etymology

From Latin 'coincident-', present participle of 'coincidere' (to coincide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00564022-s
Synset POS: s
