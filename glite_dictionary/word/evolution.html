evolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛvəˈluːʃən, ˌivəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːvəˈluːʃən, ˌɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a gradual process of change or development, especially into a more advanced or complex form
gradual development or change

Examples

The evolution of technology has greatly impacted the way we communicate.
Her artistic style showed remarkable evolution over the course of her career.
We discussed the evolution of social norms in modern society.

Synset Examples

the development of his ideas took many years
the evolution of Greek civilization
the slow development of her skill as a writer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: development - The process of growing or changing and becoming more advanced
  • Antonym: regression - Return to a former or less developed state

Etymology

From Latin evolutio(n-) (unrolling, opening), from evolvere (to unroll).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛvəˈluːʃən, ˌivəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːvəˈluːʃən, ˌɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The gradual development of living organisms over successive generations, resulting in changes in species over time through natural processes.
gradual development of species by natural processes

Examples

Darwin’s theory of evolution fundamentally changed our understanding of how species develop.
Modern genetics provides additional evidence supporting the process of evolution.
The evolution of birds from dinosaurs is well documented in the fossil record.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural selection - the process by which organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring
  • Antonym: extinction - the end or dying out of a species

Etymology

From Latin 'evolutio' (unrolling, opening), from 'evolvere' (to unroll).
