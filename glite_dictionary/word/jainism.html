Jainism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
An ancient Indian religion that emphasizes nonviolence, asceticism, and the pursuit of liberation from the cycle of rebirth.
Indian religion emphasizing nonviolence and asceticism

Examples

Many followers of Jainism practice strict nonviolence in their daily lives.
Jainism teaches that all living beings have souls and deserve compassion.
The principles of Jainism have influenced the culture and philosophy of India.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hinduism - major Indian religion from which Jainism diverged
  • Synonym: Buddhism - another religion originating as a reform movement in ancient India

Etymology

From Jain, relating to the followers of Jina (spiritual conqueror) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A religion, originating in India in the 6th century BC, that teaches the way to liberation and bliss is to live a life of harmlessness and renunciation; it emphasizes asceticism, non-violence, and the transmigration of souls, and does not accept a supreme creator deity.
ancient Indian religion emphasizing non-violence and asceticism

Examples

Jainism promotes the principle of non-violence towards all living beings as a central tenet.
Many people in western India practice Jainism and follow its strict vegetarian diet.
The teachings of Jainism have influenced other Indian religions and philosophies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hinduism - major Indian religion from which Jainism diverged
  • Synonym: Buddhism - another major Indian religion focusing on liberation and renunciation

Etymology

From Hindi *Jaina* (follower of Jina, a spiritual conqueror) + -ism.
