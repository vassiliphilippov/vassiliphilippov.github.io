mending

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
clothes or other textiles that need to be repaired by sewing or similar means
clothes that need repairing

Examples

I have a pile of mending to do this weekend.
She brought her mending to the living room and started sewing.
The basket was full of mending that had been waiting for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the act of fixing something that is broken or damaged

Etymology

From the verb 'mend', meaning to repair, with the -ing suffix forming a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of repairing something that is broken, damaged, or worn, especially clothes or fabric
act of repairing something, especially fabric

Examples

She sat quietly in the corner, working on her mending while the children played.
After the hike, my socks needed some serious mending.
He always keeps a basket of mending by his chair for rainy evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the action of restoring something to a good or sound condition

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'mend' with the suffix '-ing.'
