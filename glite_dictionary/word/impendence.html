impendence

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the state or condition of being imminent or about to happen
state of being imminent

Examples

The impendence of the storm forced the residents to take shelter.
There was a sense of impendence as the deadline approached.
Scientists warned of the impendence of a major earthquake in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imminence - the state of being about to occur

Etymology

From Latin 'imminere' (to overhang, threaten) via related English verb 'impend' and suffix '-ence'.
