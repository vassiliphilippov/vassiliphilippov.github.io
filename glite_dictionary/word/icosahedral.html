icosahedral

1. ADJ

/ aɪˌkɑːsəˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / aɪˌkɒsəˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
relating to or having the shape of an icosahedron (a polyhedron with twenty faces)
having the shape of an icosahedron

Examples

The virus has an icosahedral structure, making it stable and symmetrical.
Crystals with an icosahedral symmetry are rare in nature but found in some alloys.
Mathematicians studied the icosahedral pattern to understand its unique geometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icosahedron - polyhedron with twenty faces

Etymology

From icosahedron + -al; icosahedron from Greek eikosa (twenty) + hedra (seat, face).
