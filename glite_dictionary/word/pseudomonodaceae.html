Pseudomonodaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊməʊnəˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊmɒnəˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria, including several important plant and animal pathogens.
family of rod-shaped Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

Certain members of the Pseudomonodaceae family are linked to plant diseases such as bacterial blight.
The Pseudomonodaceae are studied extensively due to their industrial and medical importance.
Recent research focuses on the antibiotic resistance of Pseudomonodaceae strains found in hospitals.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'pseudo-' (false), 'monas' (single unit), and '-aceae' (a suffix for bacterial families).
