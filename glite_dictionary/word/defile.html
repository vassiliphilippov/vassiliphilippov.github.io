defile

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfaɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪl / (BrE)
a narrow passage or gorge, especially between mountains
narrow mountain pass or gorge

Examples

The hikers carefully made their way through the steep defile before reaching the valley beyond.
Enemy troops had concealed themselves above the narrow defile waiting to ambush the caravan.
A river ran through the defile, carving the passage deeper into the mountains over centuries.

Etymology

From French 'défilé' (narrow passage), from 'défiler' (to march past, to file off).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈfaɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪl / (BrE)
to make something dirty, polluted, or impure, especially in a way that shows disrespect or causes harm
to make dirty or impure

Examples

Industrial waste continued to defile the once pristine lake.
He was fined for defiling the public park with graffiti.
Tourists should respect the local culture and not defile sacred sites.

Synset Examples

The townspeople defiled the river by emptying raw sewage into it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pollute - to make air, water, or soil dirty or harmful
  • Synonym: to contaminate - to make something impure by adding something harmful or unwanted
  • Antonym: to purify - to remove harmful substances and make clean

Etymology

From Old French *defouler*, from Latin *defile-*, past participle of *defilare* (to trample down).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ dɪˈfaɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪl / (BrE)
to make something dirty, spoiled, or impure; to damage the purity, cleanliness, or reputation of someone or something, often in a figurative sense
make dirty or impure; sully

Examples

The vandals defiled the ancient temple with graffiti and litter.
His political rivals tried to defile his reputation with false accusations.
The water became defiled by industrial waste from the nearby factory.

Synset Examples

The silver was tarnished by the long exposure to the air
Her reputation was sullied after the affair with a married man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sully - to damage the purity or integrity of; to soil
  • Synonym: tarnish - to make or become less bright or less good; to diminish
  • Antonym: to purify - to make clean or pure

Etymology

From Old French *defouler* (to trample down), from Latin *diffulare* (to trample down).

4. VERB

/ dɪˈfaɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪl / (BrE)
to damage the purity, honor, or reputation of something; to sully or cast doubt upon
damage reputation or purity

Examples

False accusations can easily defile an innocent person's reputation.
He would never defile her good name with such baseless rumors.
A single careless remark might defile years of hard-earned trust.

Synset Examples

sully someone's reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sully - to damage the purity or integrity of; tarnish
  • Synonym: tarnish - to make or become less valuable or respected
  • Antonym: to purify - to make pure; remove contamination

Etymology

From Old French *defouler* (trample down), from Latin *de* (down) + *foliare* (to tread).
