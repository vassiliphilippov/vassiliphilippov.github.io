Rubus flagellaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs fləˈdʒɛləriːs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs flaˈɡɛlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of dewberry native to eastern North America, producing trailing brambles with edible blackberries.
dewberry plant of eastern North America

Examples

Rubus flagellaris can be found growing wild along roadsides and open fields in the eastern United States.
The berries produced by Rubus flagellaris are juicy and delicious when eaten fresh or used in desserts.
Gardeners often plant Rubus flagellaris to attract wildlife and enjoy its edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - a trailing bramble with edible berries, related to the blackberry
  • Synonym: blackberry - a prickly shrub of the genus Rubus with black edible fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'rubus' meaning bramble or blackberry, and 'flagellaris' meaning 'whip-like', referring to its trailing stems.
