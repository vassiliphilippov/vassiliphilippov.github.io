appointive

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪv / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪv / (BrE)
Relating to the act of appointing someone to a position rather than electing or hiring them.
relating to appointment to a position

Examples

He accepted an appointive post on the city council.
The committee has both elective and appointive members.
Her appointive power allowed her to choose the new director.

Synset Examples

appointive powers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elective - relating to positions filled by election rather than appointment

Etymology

From 'appoint' + '-ive', meaning tending or relating to appointment.

WordNet ID: oewn-02646445-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪn.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪn.tɪv / (BrE)
(of an office or position) filled by appointment rather than by election.
filled by appointment (not election)

Examples

The governor holds several appointive offices in addition to his elected position.
The committee reviewed all appointive posts to ensure fair representation.
Many government jobs are appointive rather than competitive or elective.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elective - (of an office) filled by election

Etymology

From 'appoint' + '-ive', indicating 'having the nature of' (from Latin -ivus).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00850001-a
Synset POS: a
