Hoenir

1. PROPN

/ ˈhoʊ.nɪr / (AmE), / ˈhəʊ.nɪə / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the Aesir gods, noted for his strong and handsome appearance but lacking in intelligence.
one of the Aesir gods in Norse mythology

Examples

In the ancient texts, Hoenir is often portrayed as indecisive and reliant on the wisdom of others.
According to Norse myths, Hoenir accompanied Odin and Loki on many journeys.
The characteristics of Hoenir reflect the complex nature of the gods in Viking legend.

Etymology

From Old Norse Hœnir, a god in Norse mythology.
