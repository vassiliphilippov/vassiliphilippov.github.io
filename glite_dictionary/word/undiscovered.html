undiscovered

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈskʌv.ɚd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈskʌv.əd / (BrE)
not yet found, known, or identified
not yet found or known

Examples

The explorer dreamed of finding an undiscovered island in the Pacific.
Many species of plants remain undiscovered deep in the rainforest.
Scientists believe there could be undiscovered elements in the universe.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discovered - having been found or identified

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪsˈkʌvərd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪsˈkʌvəd / (BrE)
not yet found, detected, or revealed; not discovered.
not discovered or found

Examples

Many species in the rainforest remain undiscovered by scientists.
The ancient tomb lay undiscovered for centuries beneath the sand.
She dreamed of traveling to undiscovered lands and exploring new cultures.

Synset Examples

with earth-based telescopes many stars remain undiscovered

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discovered - found or detected; the opposite of undiscovered

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + discovered, past participle of discover.
