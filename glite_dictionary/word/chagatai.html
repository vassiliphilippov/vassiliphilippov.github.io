Chagatai

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːgətaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːgətaɪ / (BrE)
a Turkic literary language used in central Asia during the medieval and early modern periods, named after Chagatai, son of Genghis Khan
medieval Turkic literary language of central Asia

Examples

The ancient poets composed their verses in Chagatai, enriching the Turkic literary tradition.
Many historical manuscripts written in Chagatai are preserved in Central Asian libraries.
Scholars of Central Asian history often study Chagatai to access original texts from the period.

Etymology

Named after Chagatai, son of Genghis Khan; from Persian and Turkic forms of his name.
