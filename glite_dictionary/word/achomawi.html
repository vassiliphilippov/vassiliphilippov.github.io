Achomawi

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˈtʃoʊmɑːwi / (AmE), / əˈtʃɒməwi / (BrE)
a Native American people of northeastern California who speak a Hokan language
Native American group of northeastern California

Examples

The Achomawi have preserved many of their traditional crafts and stories.
Anthropologists have studied the language and culture of the Achomawi.
A number of Achomawi descendants still live in northern California today.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːtʃəˈmɑːwi / (AmE), / ˌatʃəˈmɑːwi / (BrE)
the Shastan language traditionally spoken by the Achomawi people of northeastern California
a Shastan language of northeastern California

Examples

Linguists have studied Achomawi to help preserve the language for future generations.
There are only a handful of fluent Achomawi speakers remaining today.
She presented her research on Achomawi at a conference on Indigenous languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shastan - family of Native American languages in northern California and southern Oregon

Etymology

From the name of the Achomawi people, an Indigenous group native to northeastern California.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.tʃoʊˈmɑː.wi / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tʃəʊˈmɑː.wi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people from northeastern California
member of Native American people of California

Examples

The Achomawi have maintained their cultural traditions despite many challenges.
An Achomawi elder shared legends of the tribe with local students.
Researchers met with several Achomawi to document their language and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pit River Indian - another term for Achomawi people

Etymology

From the Achomawi term for themselves, meaning 'river people'.
