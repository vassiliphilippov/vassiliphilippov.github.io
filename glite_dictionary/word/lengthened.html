lengthened

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛŋθənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθənd / (BrE)
made longer; drawn out or extended in length.
made longer in length

Examples

The tailor suggested a lengthened hemline to suit the client’s taste.
Due to the renovation, the hallway was considerably lengthened.
After the pause, there was a lengthened silence in the room.

Synset Examples

Picasso's elongated Don Quixote
lengthened skirts are fashionable this year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extended - made longer or larger in space or time
  • Antonym: shortened - made or become shorter in length or duration

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'lengthen,' from Middle English 'lengethen,' based on 'length.'
