Bulgarian

1. ADJ

/ bʌlˈɡɛriən / (AmE), / bʊlˈɡɛəriən / (BrE)
of or relating to Bulgaria, its people, or their language and culture
relating to Bulgaria or its people

Examples

The Bulgarian government announced new policies last week.
She enjoyed tasting traditional Bulgarian dishes during her trip.
Many Bulgarian artists have gained international recognition.

Synset Examples

the Bulgarian capital is Sofia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bulgarian - a person from Bulgaria
  • Synonym: Bulgarian - the language of Bulgaria

Etymology

From Bulgaria, the country in southeastern Europe.

2. NOUN

/ bʌlˈɡɛriən / (AmE), / bʊlˈɡɛːrɪən / (BrE)
the Slavic language spoken in Bulgaria, which is the official language of the country
the Slavic language of Bulgaria

Examples

She is fluent in Bulgarian and often translates documents for her colleagues.
The Bulgarian alphabet is based on the Cyrillic script.
He decided to take a Bulgarian language course before visiting Sofia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - group of related languages of eastern Europe

Etymology

From Bulgaria, the country, ultimately from the Bulgars, a Central Asian Turkic people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06958331-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ bʌlˈɡɛriən / (AmE), / bʊlˈɡeəriən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Bulgaria
person from Bulgaria

Examples

My neighbor is a proud Bulgarian who moved here five years ago.
Many Bulgarians return home each summer to visit their families.
The festival attracts hundreds of Bulgarian expatriates from across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bulgarian - the language of Bulgaria
  • Synonym: Bulgar - a member of a people of central Asia who settled in the Balkans

Etymology

From medieval Latin *Bulgarus*, relating to the Bulgars, a people of central Asia who settled in the Balkans.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09715016-n
Synset POS: n
