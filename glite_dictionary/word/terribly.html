terribly

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛrəbli / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that is very bad, unpleasant, or of poor quality.
in a very bad or unpleasant way

Examples

She sang terribly at the audition and didn't get the part.
The meal was terribly cooked and almost inedible.
He played terribly during the game, missing every shot he took.

Synset Examples

she sings terribly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badly - in a bad or unsatisfactory way
  • Antonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way

Etymology

From 'terrible' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'terribilis' (frightful).

2. ADV

/ ˈtɛrəbli / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəbli / (BrE)
very; to a very great or intense degree (used for emphasis, often with adjectives or adverbs).
(intensifier) very; to a great degree

Examples

The weather was terribly cold last night, so we stayed indoors.
She was terribly upset when she heard the news.
I find this book terribly interesting and hard to put down.

Synset Examples

terribly interesting
I'm awful sorry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awfully - to a great or extreme degree (intensifier)
  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree (intensifier)
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree (used to indicate a lesser intensity)

Etymology

Formed from 'terrible' plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'; originally from Latin 'terribilis'.
