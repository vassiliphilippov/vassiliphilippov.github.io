gathering

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæðəɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of collecting or bringing together people or things
act of collecting or assembling

Examples

The annual gathering of scientists was held in Geneva this year.
She spent the afternoon gathering flowers in the meadow.
The gathering of evidence took several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together in one place for a common purpose
  • Antonym: dispersal - the act of scattering or spreading things or people over a wide area

Etymology

From Middle English 'gaderinge', from 'gaderen' (to gather).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæð.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡað.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A meeting or assembly of people, especially for a social, religious, or political purpose.
a meeting of people

Examples

We hosted a small gathering of friends to celebrate his birthday.
The annual gathering of local artists attracted hundreds of visitors.
After the wedding ceremony, everyone was invited to a gathering at the bride's home.

Synset Examples

they demanded the right of assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together in one place for a common purpose
  • Antonym: dispersion - the action or process of distributing or spreading people or things over a wide area

Etymology

From the verb 'gather', Old English 'gadrianian', meaning to bring together.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæðɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðərɪŋ / (BrE)
A sewing technique in which small folds or puckers are made by pulling a thread tight along a line of stitching.
small folds in fabric made by sewing

Examples

She added a gathering to the waist of the dress to give it more shape.
The sleeves were finished with a neat gathering at the cuffs.
Learning how to sew a gathering is essential for making ruffles and decorative effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleat - a fold in cloth made by doubling material over
