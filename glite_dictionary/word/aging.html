aging

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of growing older and showing the effects of advancing age in living organisms.
process of growing older

Examples

Aging is a natural part of life that affects every living organism.
Research seeks ways to slow the effects of aging on the human body.
Many people try to maintain an active lifestyle to delay the signs of aging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senescence - the biological process of aging and gradual deterioration
  • Antonym: youth - the state or period of being young

Etymology

From the verb 'age' + -ing, denoting the process or result of becoming older.

3. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which something acquires desirable qualities through being left undisturbed for a period of time, as with cheese or wine
process of improving quality over time

Examples

The aging of cheese intensifies its flavor and texture.
Oak barrels are used in the aging of fine wines.
Proper aging of meat produces a more tender and flavorful product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maturation - the process of becoming mature or fully developed, especially in food or drink

Etymology

From age + -ing, denoting the process of becoming older; specialized use in food and material contexts.
