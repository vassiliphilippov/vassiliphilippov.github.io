neuroethics

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈɛθɪks / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈɛθɪks / (BrE)
The study of the ethical, legal, and social implications arising from advances in neuroscience, especially in the treatment of neurological diseases and manipulation of the brain.
ethics of neuroscience and brain treatment
medical

Examples

Advances in neuroscience have led to difficult questions in neuroethics regarding brain enhancement.
She is writing her dissertation on neuroethics and the ethical limits of mind-altering treatments.
An international conference on neuroethics will examine the moral implications of new brain technologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bioethics - the study of ethical issues in the life sciences and healthcare

Etymology

Formed from 'neuro-' (relating to nerves or the nervous system) and 'ethics' (moral principles).
