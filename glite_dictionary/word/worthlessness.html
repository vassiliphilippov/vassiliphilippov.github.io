worthlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrθləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːθləsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having no value, use, or importance
lack of value or usefulness

Examples

She felt a deep sense of worthlessness after losing her job.
The constant criticism reinforced his feelings of worthlessness.
A sense of worthlessness can be a sign of depression.

Synset Examples

the drill sergeant's intent was to convince all the recruits of their worthlessness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: value - the quality that renders something desirable or useful
  • Synonym: uselessness - the quality of being useless
  • Degree Higher: pricelessness - the quality of being extremely valuable

Etymology

From 'worthless' + '-ness', where 'worthless' is from Old English 'weorþ' (worth) + '-less'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝθ.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːθ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no practical use or value
lack of practical use or value

Examples

The old machinery was finally discarded because of its total worthlessness.
After the experiment failed repeatedly, he realized the worthlessness of the current approach.
Her collection of broken gadgets filled her with a sense of worthlessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uselessness - the state of being of no use or benefit

Etymology

From 'worthless' + '-ness'; 'worthless' from 'worth' (value) + '-less' (without).
