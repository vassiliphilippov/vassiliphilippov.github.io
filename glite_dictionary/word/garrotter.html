garrotter

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɑːtər / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒtə / (BrE)
A person who strangles someone to death, especially as a method of murder or execution.
person who strangles to kill

Examples

The notorious garrotter terrorized the city during the late nineteenth century.
After his arrest, the police discovered evidence linking the garrotter to several unsolved murders.
Stories of a garrotter lurking in the shadows spread fear among the townspeople.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangler - a person who kills by strangling

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or a device for strangulation).
