gnocchi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnjɑːki / (AmE), / ˈnjɒki / (BrE)
Small Italian dumplings typically made from potato, flour, or semolina, boiled or baked and served with sauce or grated cheese.
Italian dumpling made from potato, flour, or semolina

Examples

We ordered homemade gnocchi with a creamy pesto sauce at the Italian restaurant.
She likes to make gnocchi from scratch, using fresh potatoes and flour.
The chef topped the baked gnocchi with tomato sauce and parmesan cheese.

Etymology

From Italian 'gnocchi', plural of 'gnocco', meaning 'lump' or 'dumpling'.
