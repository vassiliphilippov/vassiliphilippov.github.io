factualness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tʃu.əl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tʃu.əl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being based on facts or reality
quality of being factual or actual

Examples

The factualness of his report was questioned after inconsistencies were discovered.
Her argument was notable for its clarity and factualness.
We must evaluate the factualness of these statements before drawing conclusions.

Synset Examples

the realm of factuality must be distinguished from the realm of imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factuality - the quality of being actual or factual
  • Synonym: truthfulness - the quality of being true or honest
  • Antonym: fictionality - the quality of being fictional or imagined

Etymology

From factual + -ness, forming nouns denoting a property or state.
