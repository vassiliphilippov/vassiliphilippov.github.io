sternutative

1. ADJ

/ stɚˈnuːtətɪv / (AmE), / stɜːˈnjuːtətɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance) causing sneezing; making someone sneeze when inhaled or applied.
causing sneezing (substance)

Examples

Some spices, such as white pepper, can have a sternutative effect if inhaled.
The laboratory tested several sternutative compounds for their potential use in riot control.
Sneezing caused by a sternutative agent can be very uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

pepper is a sternutatory substance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sternutatory - causing sneezing (alternative form)

Etymology

From Latin 'sternutativus', from 'sternutare' (to sneeze).
