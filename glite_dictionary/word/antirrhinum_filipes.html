Antirrhinum filipes

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntɪˈraɪnəm ˈfɪlɪpiːz / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈraɪnəm ˈfɪlɪpiːz / (BrE)
A plant species native to the southwestern United States, characterized by yellow flowers on stems that twist and twine through other vegetation.
twining plant with yellow flowers native to the US Southwest

Examples

The delicate stems of the Antirrhinum filipes wound gracefully through the surrounding brush.
During spring hikes, botanists enjoy spotting the bright yellow flowers of Antirrhinum filipes.
Conservationists are monitoring wild populations of Antirrhinum filipes in desert habitats.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Antirrhinum', the genus name meaning 'like a snout', and 'filipes', meaning 'thread-stemmed'.
