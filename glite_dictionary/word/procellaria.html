Procellaria

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsɛləriə / (AmE), / prəʊˈsɛləriə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Procellariidae, comprising several species of petrels found in the Southern Hemisphere.
genus of petrels (Procellariidae)

Examples

The genus Procellaria includes several species of petrels that inhabit southern oceans.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Procellaria to better understand seabird ecology.
Members of Procellaria are known for their ability to travel long distances over open ocean.

Etymology

From Latin *procella* (storm), referring to the stormy environments petrels often inhabit.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌsɛˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɛˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
a genus of seabirds in the family Procellariidae, which includes some large petrels
genus of large petrels (seabirds)

Examples

The Procellaria are known for their ability to travel vast distances over the ocean.
Several species in the genus Procellaria are classified as endangered.
Researchers have observed the feeding habits of Procellaria in the Southern Hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrel - a seabird, especially one of the family Procellariidae

Etymology

From Latin *procella* meaning 'storm', referring to the stormy seas where these birds are found.
