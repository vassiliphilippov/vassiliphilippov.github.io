meanwhile

1. ADV

/ ˈminˌwaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːnˌwaɪl / (BrE)
During the period of time between two events; at the same time.
in the intervening time

Examples

I'll start dinner now; meanwhile, could you set the table?
She applied for jobs, and meanwhile, she continued her studies.
The train was delayed, so meanwhile we waited patiently at the station.

Synset Examples

meanwhile I will not think about the problem
meantime he was attentive to his other interests
in the meantime the police were notified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meantime - for the time in between; during the intervening time

Etymology

From Middle English menewhile, from mene (‘middle’) + while (‘period of time’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːnˌwaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːnwʌɪl / (BrE)
the period of time between two events or points in time
time between two events

Examples

She waited in the meanwhile while everyone else prepared for the meeting.
The repairs will take a week, so in the meanwhile, you'll need to find alternative transportation.
Much changed in the meanwhile between their last visit and now.

Synset Examples

meanwhile the socialists are running the government

Etymology

From Middle English menewhile, meaning 'in the intervening time', from 'mean' (middle) + 'while'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15297873-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈminˌwaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːnˌwaɪl / (BrE)
at the same time; during the intervening period, especially in another place or situation
at the same time elsewhere

Examples

I'm cooking dinner; meanwhile, could you please set the table?
She left for the store. Meanwhile, he finished cleaning the kitchen.
The children played outside. Meanwhile, their parents prepared a surprise inside the house.

Synset Examples

meanwhile, back at the ranch …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneously - at the same time

Etymology

From Middle English mein while (in the meantime), from mein (in the meantime) + while (time, period).
