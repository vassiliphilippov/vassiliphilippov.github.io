Sebastes

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈbæstiːz / (AmE), / sɪˈbæstiːz / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish known as rockfishes, found mainly in the North Pacific and valued as food fish
genus of marine rockfishes

Examples

Several species in the genus Sebastes are popular targets for commercial fisheries.
The Sebastes genus includes both shallow and deep-water rockfishes.
Scientists study Sebastes populations to monitor changes in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockfish - a common name for various fish in the genus Sebastes

Etymology

From Greek 'sebastos', meaning 'august' or 'venerable', used as a Latinized genus name.

2. NOUN

/ səˈbæstiz / (AmE), / sɪˈbeɪstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish commonly known as rockfishes, found primarily in the North Pacific Ocean.
genus of rockfishes

Examples

The Pacific Ocean is home to many species within the genus Sebastes.
Scientists are studying the population dynamics of Sebastes due to their ecological importance.
Numerous commercial fisheries target rockfish species classified under Sebastes.

Etymology

From Greek Sebastos (venerable, august), also the root of 'Augustus.'
