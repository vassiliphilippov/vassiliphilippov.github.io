Joseph Smith

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəf smɪθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf smɪθ / (BrE)
(1805–1844) The American religious leader who founded the Mormon Church (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints) in 1830.
founder of the Mormon Church

Examples

Joseph Smith claimed to have translated the Book of Mormon through divine guidance.
Many historians study the legacy of Joseph Smith and the origins of the Latter-day Saint movement.
The city of Nauvoo, Illinois, was once led by Joseph Smith before his death in 1844.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brigham Young - second president and influential leader of the Mormon Church
