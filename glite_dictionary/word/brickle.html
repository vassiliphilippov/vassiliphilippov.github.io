brickle

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪkəl / (BrE)
(dialect) Easily broken, cracked, or snapped; brittle.
easily broken; brittle (dialect)
archaic

Examples

The old vase was so brickle that it shattered at the slightest touch.
She handled the brickle twigs with care, knowing they would snap easily.
After years without rain, the earth became dry and brickle underfoot.

Synset Examples

brittle bones

‘brickle’ and ‘brickly’ are dialectal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brittle - easily broken or snapped; fragile

Etymology

From Middle English brikel, from Old English bryċel (brittle), related to 'break.'
