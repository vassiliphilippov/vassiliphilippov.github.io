Baltic

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔltɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːltɪk / (BrE)
the sea in northern Europe, bordered by several countries including Sweden, Finland, Russia, and the Baltic States
sea in northern Europe

Examples

The Russian navy maintains a major base on the Baltic.
Several countries share coastlines with the Baltic, including Estonia and Poland.
Environmental concerns about pollution in the Baltic have increased in recent years.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Mare Balticum', possibly from a Germanic root meaning 'belt', referencing the sea's shape.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɔl.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.tɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Lithuanian, Latvian, and Old Prussian; related to the Slavic languages and noted for its preservation of archaic features
branch of Indo-European languages (Lithuanian, Latvian, etc.)

Examples

Lithuanian is a living example of a Baltic language still spoken today.
Some linguists study Baltic to reconstruct aspects of Proto-Indo-European.
The similarities between Slavic and Baltic suggest a close historical relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - branch of the Indo-European language family including Russian, Polish, and others

Etymology

From Latin 'Balticus', referring to the Baltic Sea region.
