trepidation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɛpɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrɛpɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of fear or anxiety about something that may happen.
anxiety or fear about the future

Examples

She opened the envelope with trepidation, not knowing what news it would contain.
As the storm approached, a sense of trepidation spread through the village.
Despite her trepidation about starting a new job, she decided to take the leap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease about something with an uncertain outcome
  • Synonym: dread - a strong feeling of fear or apprehension
  • Antonym: calm - a peaceful or tranquil state, especially as opposed to anxiety or fear

Etymology

From Latin trepidatio (trembling, alarm), from trepidare (to be in a state of alarm).
