patzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætsər / (AmE), / ˈpætsə / (BrE)
A poor or unskilled chess player.
an unskilled chess player

Examples

The experienced player easily defeated the patzer in just a few moves.
Many beginners worry about being called a patzer at their first chess tournament.
Despite years of playing, he still considers himself only a patzer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation
  • Antonym: expert - a person who has a comprehensive and authoritative knowledge of or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From German 'Patzer' (blunderer), related to 'patzen' (to make a mess).
