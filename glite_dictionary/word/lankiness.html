lankiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋkinəs / (AmE), / ˈlaŋkɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being tall and thin, often in a way that appears slightly awkward
quality of being tall and thin

Examples

His lankiness made him stand out on the basketball court.
She moved with the awkward lankiness of a teenager still growing into her body.
The model's lankiness was well-suited for high-fashion runways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanky - tall and thin, often awkwardly so

Etymology

From 'lanky' + '-ness', where 'lanky' likely comes from the dialectal 'lank' (slender, long and limp).
