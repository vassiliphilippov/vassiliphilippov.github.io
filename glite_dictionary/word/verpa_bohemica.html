Verpa bohemica

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːpə boʊˈhɛmɪkə / (AmE), / ˈvɜːpə bəʊˈhɛmɪkə / (BrE)
A species of edible fungus known as the early morel, characterized by a wrinkled cap resembling a thimble, appearing in early spring.
species of early morel mushroom

Examples

The distinctive cap of Verpa bohemica makes it easy for mushroom hunters to identify in early spring.
Foragers often collect Verpa bohemica before most other wild mushrooms appear in the forest.
Although Verpa bohemica is considered edible, it should always be cooked before consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible fungus with a honeycomb-like cap

Etymology

From Latin: 'Verpa' (rod, penis) and 'bohemica' (from Bohemia, now part of the Czech Republic).
