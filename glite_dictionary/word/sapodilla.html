sapodilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈdɪlə / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈdɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with rough brownish skin and very sweet, brownish pulp, native to Central America and the Caribbean.
tropical fruit with sweet, brownish pulp

Examples

The fresh sapodilla she bought at the market was sweet and fragrant.
One sapodilla provides a rich source of fiber and natural sugar.
He had never tasted a sapodilla before his trip to the Caribbean.

Etymology

From Spanish zapotillo, diminutive of zapote (soft fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈdɪlə / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈdɪlə / (BrE)
A large tropical American evergreen tree (Manilkara zapota), cultivated for its sweet edible fruit and as a source of chicle gum.
tropical fruit tree; edible fruit; source of chicle

Examples

The farmer harvested a basket of ripe sapodilla from the orchard.
Chicle, used in making chewing gum, is extracted from the sapodilla tree.
I tried sapodilla for the first time while traveling in Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapote - Edible tropical fruit from several species, often confused with sapodilla

Etymology

From Spanish 'zapotilla', diminutive of 'zapote' (sapote), from Nahuatl 'tzapotl' (soft fruit).
