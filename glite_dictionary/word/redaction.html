redaction

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdækʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdækʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of preparing and editing text, especially for publication, by arranging, revising, or removing content to ensure clarity, accuracy, or acceptable form
editing or preparing text for publication

Examples

The redaction of the company's annual report required careful attention to detail.
Before publication, the manuscript underwent significant redaction to clarify its main arguments.
Sensitive information was removed during the redaction of the legal document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editing - the act of making changes to written material

Etymology

From French 'rédaction', from Latin 'redactio' (bringing back, editing).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdækʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdækʃən / (BrE)
the process of editing, arranging, or preparing text for publication or official use
editing or preparing text for publication

Examples

The redaction of the confidential report took several weeks to complete.
Following the redaction, sensitive information was removed from the public release.
Her careful redaction ensured the article was both clear and accurate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editing - the process of preparing written material by correcting or revising
  • Synonym: compilation - the preparation of something by collecting and arranging existing material

Etymology

From Latin 'redactio' (a bringing back, editing), from 'redigere' (to bring back, collect, reduce).
