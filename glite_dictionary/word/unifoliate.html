unifoliate

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuː.nɪˈfoʊ.li.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈfəʊ.li.eɪt / (BrE)
(Botany) Having a single leaf; consisting of one leaflet.
having a single leaf (botany)

Examples

The unifoliate structure of the plant makes it easy to identify in the field.
Some orchids are known for their unifoliate leaves, which distinguish them from other species.
The botanist noted the plant's unifoliate nature in her journal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trifoliate - having three leaves or leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'folium' (leaf).
