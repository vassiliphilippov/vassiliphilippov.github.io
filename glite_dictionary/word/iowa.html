Iowa

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪ.oʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.wə / (BrE)
a state in the midwestern United States, with Des Moines as its capital
a U.S. state in the Midwest

Examples

She grew up in Iowa before moving to New York City.
The Iowa State Fair attracts thousands of visitors every summer.
Many farmers in Iowa produce corn and soybeans.

Etymology

Named after the Iowa people, a Native American tribe; the name may derive from Dakota ayúxba or ayuxwe, meaning 'sleepy ones.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪəwə / (AmE), / ˈaɪəwə / (BrE)
a dialect of the Chiwere language spoken by the Iowa people

Examples

Few people today speak the Iowa dialect fluently.
Linguists have worked to document the Iowa language before it disappears.
The Iowa people are making efforts to revive their ancestral tongue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chiwere - Siouan language spoken by the Iowa, Otoe, and Missouria peoples

Etymology

From the name of the Iowa people, itself from Dakota ayúxba, refers to the Native American tribe.

4. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.oʊ.wə / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.wə / (BrE)
A member of the Siouan Native American people originally living in Iowa, Minnesota, and Missouri.
member of a Siouan Native American people

Examples

The Iowa once inhabited parts of the regions now known as Iowa and Minnesota.
Historians have traced the migration of the Iowa people across the Midwest.
An Iowa elder shared stories of his ancestors with the local schoolchildren.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Otoe - a member of another Siouan people originally from the same region
  • Synonym: Missouri - a member of the Siouan people from Missouri area

Etymology

From Dakota (Siouan language) *ayúxba*.
