tracheid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪkiɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrækɪɪd / (BrE)
A long, thin, tubular cell found in the xylem of vascular plants that helps transport water and provide structural support.
tubular xylem cell in plants
medical

Examples

The pine tree's xylem consists primarily of tracheids rather than vessel elements.
Water moves upward through tracheids by capillary action and adhesion to cell walls.
Unlike vessel elements, tracheid cells are long and tapering, allowing them to contribute to both support and water transport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vessel element - a shorter, wider cell type in the xylem involved in water transport

Etymology

From Greek 'tracheia' (windpipe) + '-id' (resembling), referring to resemblance to trachea.
