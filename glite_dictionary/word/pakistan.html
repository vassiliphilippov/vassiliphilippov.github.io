Pakistan

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɑː.kɪˈstæn / (AmE), / ˈpæk.ɪ.stæn / (BrE)
a country in South Asia, officially known as the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, occupying the Indus River valley and established as an independent nation in 1947 after partition from British India.
country in South Asia; formed in 1947

Examples

After completing his studies, he decided to return to Pakistan to contribute to his country's development.
Relations between Pakistan and its neighbors have fluctuated over the years.
The Indus River flows through several regions of Pakistan, shaping its agriculture and civilization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: India - country in South Asia, shared colonial history until 1947
  • Antonym: Bangladesh - country that was part of Pakistan until 1971, formerly East Pakistan

Etymology

Coined in 1933 from Urdu and Persian words: 'pak' (pure) and 'istan' (land); meaning 'Land of the Pure'.
