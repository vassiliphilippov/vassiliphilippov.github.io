piercing

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪr.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.sɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sensation, sound, or quality) extremely sharp, intense, or penetrating, as if caused by something that pierces.
very sharp or intense (pain, sound, etc.)

Examples

The piercing wind cut through my coat and made me shiver uncontrollably.
She felt a piercing pain in her side after running for ten minutes.
His piercing criticism left her feeling hurt and embarrassed.

Synset Examples

a cutting wind
keen winds
knifelike cold
piercing knifelike pains
piercing cold
piercing criticism
a stabbing pain
lancinating pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabbing - sharp and sudden, like a pointed object
  • Synonym: cutting - sharp or biting (especially of wind or remarks)
  • Antonym: dull - not sharp, lacking intensity (of pain, sensation, etc.)

Etymology

Present participle of 'pierce,' from Old French 'percer', of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪr.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.sɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person's intelligence, observation, or remark) showing the ability to notice and understand things quickly and deeply; perceptive or insightful.
keenly perceptive or insightful

Examples

Her piercing analysis quickly exposed the flaws in the argument.
The reporter's piercing questions left the politician visibly uncomfortable.
With a piercing gaze, she seemed to see right through his excuses.

Synset Examples

an acute observer of politics and politicians
incisive comments
icy knifelike reasoning
as sharp and incisive as the stroke of a fang
penetrating insight
frequent penetrative observations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incisive - clear-thinking and able to express ideas sharply
  • Synonym: penetrating - showing an ability to understand things deeply
  • Antonym: blunt - not perceptive or lacking sharpness in understanding

Etymology

From the verb 'pierce', from Old French 'percer', probably of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
