shiftlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪftləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪftləsnɪs / (BrE)
lack of ambition or initiative; a state of being unwilling or unable to work or be active
lack of ambition or initiative

Examples

His shiftlessness made it difficult for him to keep any job for long.
Many people blamed the town’s problems on the general shiftlessness of its youth.
She grew frustrated with his constant shiftlessness and lack of motivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laziness - unwillingness to work or use energy
  • Antonym: industriousness - the quality of being hardworking and diligent

Etymology

From 'shiftless' (lacking resourcefulness or initiative) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
