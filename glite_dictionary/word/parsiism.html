Parsiism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the religious faith and practices of the Parsi community in India, derived from Zoroastrianism
the religion of the Parsi community in India

Examples

Many members of the community still faithfully practice Parsiism today.
He wrote a thesis on the history and rituals of Parsiism in India.
Parsiism emphasizes the values of truth and good deeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoroastrianism - the ancient Persian religion founded by Zoroaster

Etymology

From Parsi (a member of a Zoroastrian community in India) + -ism (denoting a system or practice).
