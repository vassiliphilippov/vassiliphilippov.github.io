profoundness

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfaʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being physically deep; great depth.
great physical depth

Examples

The profoundness of the canyon made the hikers feel very small.
The profoundness of the ocean is still largely unexplored by humans.
Geologists measured the profoundness of the cave to determine its age.

Synset Examples

the profundity of the mine was almost a mile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profundity - the quality of being very deep, both physically and metaphorically

Etymology

From profound + -ness; ultimately from Latin profundus (deep).
