spandex

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænˌdɛks / (AmE), / ˈspændɛks / (BrE)
a synthetic fiber known for its exceptional elasticity, used especially in sportswear and form-fitting garments
elastic synthetic fabric or fiber

Examples

Many athletic shorts are made from spandex to allow greater flexibility during exercise.
The dancer wore a bright spandex costume that fit her perfectly.
Cyclists often choose spandex clothing for its lightweight and stretchy qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lycra - proprietary name for spandex fiber

Etymology

Alteration of 'expands'. Coined as a brand name in the 1950s.
