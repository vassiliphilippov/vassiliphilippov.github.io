generous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛn.ə.rəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ər.əs / (BrE)
willing to give or share more than is usual or expected; showing a readiness to give more of something, such as time or money, than is strictly necessary or expected.
willing to give or share freely

Examples

Her generous spirit made her beloved by everyone in the community.
He made a generous offer to donate money to the hospital.
The company thanked its generous customers for their continued support.

Synset Examples

a generous donation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charitable - showing kindness by giving money or help to those in need
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Latin 'generosus' (noble in birth) via Old French 'genereus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01114727-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛnərəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəs / (BrE)
(of an amount) larger or more plentiful than is usual or necessary.
more than the usual amount

Examples

He gave me a generous slice of cake that covered the entire plate.
The chef served a generous helping of pasta to each guest.
We were delighted to receive such a generous portion at the restaurant.

Synset Examples

a generous portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ample - more than enough, plentiful
  • Antonym: scanty - small in amount, less than is needed

Etymology

From Latin generosus (noble, of noble birth), from genus (birth, race).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00106981-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛnərəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn ər əs / (BrE)
Having a broad and noble character; willing to overlook small faults and not being petty.
not petty; broad-minded

Examples

Her generous spirit allowed her to forgive even the harshest critics.
He gave a generous interpretation of his colleague's ambiguous remark.
The judge was generous in considering the circumstances of the accused.

Synset Examples

unusually generous in his judgment of people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnanimous - generous or forgiving, especially toward a rival or less powerful person
  • Antonym: petty - of little importance; concerned with trivial matters

Etymology

From Latin 'generosus' (of noble birth, magnanimous).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01118176-a
Synset POS: a
