cellulose

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljuˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊˌləʊs / (BrE)
a complex carbohydrate that forms the main component of the cell walls in plants and is used in the production of paper and textiles
main component of plant cell walls
medical

Examples

Paper is made primarily from wood pulp, which contains a high percentage of cellulose.
Some animals, like cows, have special bacteria to help them digest cellulose.
Scientists studied the structure of cellulose to develop new biodegradable materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starch - a polysaccharide found in plants, used for energy storage
  • Synonym: lignin - a complex organic polymer in plant cell walls, associated with cellulose

Etymology

From French *cellulose*, from *cellule* ('cell').
