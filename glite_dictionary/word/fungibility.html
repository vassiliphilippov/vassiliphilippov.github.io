fungibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌfʌndʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being interchangeable with other individual items of the same kind, especially with respect to goods or assets.
interchangeability of goods or assets

Examples

The fungibility of cash makes it easy to use for a variety of transactions.
Cryptocurrencies are often discussed in terms of their fungibility compared to traditional money.
Because of its fungibility, oil can be traded freely on the global market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeability - the quality of being capable of being exchanged or replaced with equivalent items

Etymology

From Latin 'fungibilis' (interchangeable), from 'fungi' (to perform, enjoy).
