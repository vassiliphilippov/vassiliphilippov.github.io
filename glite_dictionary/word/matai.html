matai

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.taɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.taɪ / (BrE)
A large evergreen coniferous tree (Prumnopitys taxifolia) native to New Zealand and Australia, with fine-grained timber.
coniferous tree of New Zealand and Australia

Examples

The matai is valued for its strong, fine-grained wood used in flooring and furniture.
Many ancient matai trees can be found in native New Zealand forests.
Conservation efforts are helping to restore matai populations reduced by logging.

Etymology

From Maori *mataī* (Prumnopitys taxifolia).
