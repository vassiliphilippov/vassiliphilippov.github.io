phlebothrombosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfliboʊθrɒmˈboʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌflɛbəʊθrɒmˈbəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which a blood clot forms in a vein without preceding inflammation, often linked to slow blood flow or increased blood coagulation.
formation of a blood clot in a vein (no prior inflammation)
medical

Examples

The patient developed phlebothrombosis in her left leg after weeks of immobility following surgery.
Unlike thrombophlebitis, phlebothrombosis occurs without signs of vein inflammation.
Pregnancy is a known risk factor for phlebothrombosis due to hormonal changes and reduced mobility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thrombophlebitis - inflammation of a vein associated with a blood clot

Etymology

From Greek *phlebo-* (vein) and *thrombosis* (clotting).
