anaphasic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈfeɪ.zɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈfeɪ.zɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of anaphase, the stage in mitosis or meiosis when chromosomes separate to opposite poles of the cell.
relating to anaphase stage of cell division
medical

Examples

The scientist observed a distinct anaphasic movement of chromosomes under the microscope.
Errors during the anaphasic stage can lead to genetic disorders.
During cell division, the anaphasic process ensures chromosomes are evenly distributed between daughter cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaphase - stage of cell division when chromosomes separate

Etymology

From 'anaphase' (from Greek ana- 'up' + phasis 'appearance') + '-ic', forming adjectives.
