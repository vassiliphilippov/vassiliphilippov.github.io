lengthiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛŋθinəs / (AmE), / ˈleŋθɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being lengthy, especially in duration or extent
the state of being lengthy

Examples

The lengthiness of the speech bored many members of the audience.
Critics have commented on the lengthiness of the novel, suggesting it could be edited.
She apologized for the lengthiness of her explanation, realizing it had taken up too much time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brevity - the quality of being brief

Etymology

From 'lengthy' + '-ness', both ultimately from Old English 'lengðu' (length).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛŋ.θi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋ.θi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being lengthy or prolonged; great length or extent
the quality of being lengthy

Examples

The lengthiness of the meeting left everyone exhausted by the end.
Critics complained about the lengthiness of the novel, calling it unnecessarily drawn out.
We should consider ways to reduce the lengthiness of our application process.

Synset Examples

the wire has an extension of 50 feet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brevity - the quality of being brief or short in duration
  • Synonym: length - the measurement or extent of something from end to end

Etymology

From 'lengthy' + '-ness', based on 'length'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05140800-n
Synset POS: n
