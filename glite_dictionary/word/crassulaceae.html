Crassulaceae

1. NOUN

/ krəˌsjuːləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / kræsˌjuːləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of succulent shrubs and herbs, including many stonecrops and houseleeks
family of succulent plants

Examples

The Crassulaceae are well-known for their ability to store water in their fleshy leaves.
Many popular houseplants, such as jade plants, belong to the Crassulaceae family.
Botanists often study Crassulaceae because of their remarkable drought resistance.

Etymology

From Latin 'Crassula' (a genus of the family) and '-aceae', a standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ kræˌsjuːləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / kræˌsjʊləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of succulent shrubs and herbs, commonly known as the stonecrop family, found mainly in arid and temperate regions
a family of succulent plants (stonecrop family)
medical

Examples

Many popular houseplants, such as jade plants, belong to the Crassulaceae family.
Succulents in the Crassulaceae family store water in their thick leaves to survive dry climates.
Researchers are studying drought resistance in several species from the Crassulaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonecrop - a common name for many plants in the Crassulaceae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Crassulaceae, based on the genus Crassula.
