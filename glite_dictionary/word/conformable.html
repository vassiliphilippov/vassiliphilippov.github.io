conformable

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɔːrməbəl / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
(of a person) willing or ready to comply with others' wishes or rules; compliant.
willing to comply; compliant

Examples

She was always conformable to the expectations of her superiors.
The new intern proved conformable, quickly adapting to established procedures.
In Shakespeare's play, the queen is described as conformable to her husband's wishes.

Synset Examples

I have been to you a true and humble wife, at all times to your will conformable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliant - willing to obey or conform to rules or others' wishes
  • Antonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or actions

Etymology

From Latin conformabilis, from conformare (to shape, fashion).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
(formal) consistent or in agreement with something; in keeping with a particular standard, rule, or expectation.
consistent with; in keeping with

Examples

The new regulations are conformable with international standards.
Her results are conformable with previous research in the field.
All policies must be conformable to the company's core values.

Synset Examples

salaries agreeable with current trends
plans conformable with your wishes
expressed views concordant with his background

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Antonym: incompatible - not able to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

From Late Latin conformabilis, from Latin conformare (to shape, fashion, conform).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ kənˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
willing or inclined to comply with rules, standards, or the wishes of others
disposed or willing to conform

Examples

The team was made up of highly conformable individuals who adapted easily to new procedures.
She is quite conformable when it comes to following workplace policies and guidelines.
Unlike his rebellious friends, George was naturally conformable and respected his teachers' requests.

Synset Examples

someone amenable to the instruction of others

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amenable - willing to be led or controlled
  • Synonym: compliant - inclined to agree with others or obey rules
  • Antonym: recalcitrant - resisting authority or control

Etymology

From Latin 'conformare' (to shape, form).

Synset POS: s
