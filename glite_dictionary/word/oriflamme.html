oriflamme

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəˌflæm / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪflam / (BrE)
A red or orange-red flag or standard, especially the banner carried as a battle standard by early French kings.
red royal banner of early French kings

Examples

The knight marched beneath the fluttering oriflamme into battle.
Legends say that the oriflamme inspired great courage in the soldiers of France.
The museum displayed a reconstruction of the medieval oriflamme.

Etymology

From Old French *oriflambe*, from Latin *aurea flamma* (golden flame).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪˌflæm / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪˌflam / (BrE)
an inspiring symbol or ideal that serves as a rallying point, especially in a struggle; originally, a battle standard
inspiring symbol or rallying point

Examples

The revolutionary leader became the oriflamme for those seeking change.
Liberty, once accepted, is the oriflamme under which generations fight oppression.
Their community's flag served as an oriflamme in the battle for rights.

Etymology

From Old French 'oriflambe', from Latin 'aurea flamma' (golden flame), referring to a medieval French battle standard.
