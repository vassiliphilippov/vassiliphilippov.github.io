knit

1. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
A type of fabric that is produced by interlacing yarns using the process of knitting, often characterized by its stretch and flexibility.
fabric produced by knitting

Examples

The sweater is made from a soft wool knit.
This shop sells both woven and knit fabrics.
He chose a lightweight knit for the summer top.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woven - fabric made by weaving threads over and under each other

Etymology

From Old English *cnyttan* (to knot).

2. VERB

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
to draw together into small wrinkles or folds; to contract and form creases or furrows
gather or contract into folds or wrinkles

Examples

She knit her brows in concentration as she read the letter.
A worried frown knit his forehead into deep lines.
Stress can often make people knit their features unconsciously.

Synset Examples

She puckered her lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pucker - to contract into wrinkles or small folds
  • Antonym: smooth - to make surface even, remove wrinkles

Etymology

Old English 'cnyttan' meaning 'to tie in a knot'.

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
to make fabric or garments by interlinking yarn with needles or similar tools.
make fabric by interlinking yarn with needles

Examples

She loves to knit warm sweaters for her grandchildren during the winter.

Many people find it relaxing to knit while listening to music.

  • Antonym: weave - to form cloth by interlacing threads

4. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
A piece of fabric or an item created by interlacing yarn with needles or by machine in a series of connected loops.
fabric or item made by knitting

Examples

The sweater's tight knit made it especially warm in winter.
This scarf has a loose knit that gives it a casual look.
She admired the intricate knit of the handmade blanket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knitting - the action or process of knitting yarn into fabric
  • Antonym: weave - a fabric or cloth created by interlacing threads at right angles

Etymology

From Old English 'cnyttan' (to tie in a knot).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
A basic knitting stitch made by inserting the needle through the front of the stitch from the left-hand side.
basic knitting stitch

Examples

After you master the knit, you can learn the purl stitch.
The scarf is made entirely with the knit for a smooth texture.
I practiced the knit until my hands felt comfortable with the motion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purl - a basic knitting stitch made in the opposite manner to a knit stitch

6. VERB

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
to tie or link things or people closely together; to unite or connect closely
unite or link closely together

Examples

Shared experiences can knit a group of strangers into close friends.
The disaster helped knit the divided community together.
Common goals often knit people more closely than differences pull them apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to come or bring together for a common purpose or action
  • Synonym: join - to connect or fasten things together
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to stop being connected

Etymology
