airiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈeə.ri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being light in weight, insubstantial, or spacious; the condition of having an open or light character
quality of being light or spacious

Examples

The airiness of the curtains allowed sunlight to flood the entire room.
She loved the airiness of the soufflé, which made it feel almost weightless on her tongue.
The airiness of the new office design boosted everyone's mood and productivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy
  • Synonym: lightness - the quality of being light in weight or not heavy

Etymology

From 'airy' + '-ness', formed in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈeə.ri.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being light or graceful in movement or manner
lightness or grace in movement

Examples

The dancer's airiness made each leap look effortless.
There was an airiness to her walk that caught everyone's attention.
His speech had an airiness that lifted the mood of the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracefulness - elegant and smooth quality in movement or form
  • Antonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy, sluggish, or lacking lightness

Etymology

From 'airy' (light, graceful) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04640223-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈeə.ri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being spacious and filled with fresh air; an impression of openness and lightness in a place or thing
quality of being spacious and well-aired

Examples

The airiness of the living room was enhanced by the large windows and high ceilings.
She appreciated the airiness of the hotel lobby as soon as she entered.
The designer focused on maximizing light and airiness throughout the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spaciousness - the quality of being large and having a lot of space

Etymology

From 'airy' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state or quality of being airy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05113045-n
Synset POS: n
