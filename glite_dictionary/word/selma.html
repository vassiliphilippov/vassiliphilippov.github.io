Selma

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛlmə / (AmE), / ˈsɛlmə / (BrE)
A town in central Alabama, located on the Alabama River, known for its major role in the American civil rights movement, particularly the 1965 Selma to Montgomery marches.
town in Alabama, civil rights landmark

Examples

Many people visit Selma to learn about the history of the civil rights movement.
The bridge in Selma became a powerful symbol of the struggle for voting rights.
She grew up in Selma, surrounded by reminders of its historic marches.
