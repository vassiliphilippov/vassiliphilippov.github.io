resilient

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪliənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪlɪənt / (BrE)
(of a substance or object) able to spring back into shape after bending, stretching, or being compressed; elastic.
able to return to original shape; elastic

Examples

The foam mattress is particularly resilient and regains its shape quickly after use.
She admired the resilient properties of the new sportswear fabric.
After being compressed, the resilient rubber ball bounced back to its original form.

Synset Examples

clean bouncy hair
a lively tennis ball
as resilient as seasoned hickory
springy turf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elastic - able to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed
  • Synonym: flexible - capable of bending easily without breaking
  • Antonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible

Etymology

From Latin resilire (to spring back, rebound).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪliənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪliənt / (BrE)
able to recover quickly from difficulties, adversity, or change; able to withstand or adapt to challenging conditions.
able to recover quickly from difficulties

Examples

Despite many setbacks, the company remained resilient and eventually flourished.
Children can be remarkably resilient in the face of change and stress.
Her resilient attitude inspired others to keep going through tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; able to withstand or overcome adverse conditions
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged; not strong

Etymology

From Latin resilire (to spring back).
