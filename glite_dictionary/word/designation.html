designation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a name, title, or word used to identify and classify someone or something, distinguishing them from others
name or title used to identify

Examples

Doctor is a professional designation that commands respect worldwide.
She was given the official designation of Chief Operating Officer.
The bird's scientific designation is Turdus migratorius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - a name that describes someone's position or job
  • Synonym: label - a word or phrase used to describe or identify something

Etymology

From Latin designatio (appointment, marking out), from designare (to mark out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of identifying someone or something, or the process of officially assigning a particular status or name
the act of identifying or naming

Examples

The official designation of the park was granted last year by the city council.
His designation as team leader was announced in the meeting.
The designation of this area as a wildlife reserve will help protect endangered species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone

Etymology

From Latin designatio (a marking out, indication), from designare (to mark out, designate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially choosing someone for a job or position that is not filled by election
appointment to a non-elected position

Examples

The committee's designation of a new chairperson was met with unanimous approval.
After his designation as manager, he implemented several new policies.
Her designation by the board was based on her extensive experience and leadership skills.

Synset Examples
