ill-chosen

1. ADJ

/ ɪlˈtʃoʊzən / (AmE), / ɪlˈtʃəʊzən / (BrE)
not appropriate, suitable, or graceful for the context; awkward or inaptly selected, especially in expression or word choice
not appropriate or gracefully chosen

Examples

The politician's ill-chosen words sparked outrage among the public.
Her ill-chosen metaphor confused the listeners rather than clarifying her point.
Sending that email at midnight was an ill-chosen move.

Synset Examples

an awkward prose style
a clumsy apology
his cumbersome writing style
if the rumor is true, can anything be more inept than to repeat it now?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill in manner or movement or performance
  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking grace in movement or posture
  • Antonym: well-chosen - suitably or appropriately selected

Etymology

Formed from 'ill' (badly) + 'chosen' (selected), indicating something badly selected.
