pre-existent

1. ADJ

/ priː.ɪɡˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / priː.ɪɡˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
existing earlier or before something else; having existed previously.
existing prior to something else

Examples

Scientists analyzed the impact of pre-existent conditions on the outcome of the experiment.
The composer incorporated pre-existent melodies into his new symphony.
Certain pre-existent beliefs may influence how people interpret new information.

Synset Examples

variations on pre-existent musical themes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existent - currently existing; having existence
  • Synonym: pre-existing - existing from an earlier time before something else

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'existent' (existing).
