rotisserie

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈtɪsəri / (AmE), / rəʊˈtɪsəri / (BrE)
a restaurant that specializes in roasted or barbecued meats, often cooked on a spit
restaurant specializing in roasted meats

Examples

They dined at a famous rotisserie on the edge of town.
The rotisserie offers a wide selection of spit-roasted chicken and pork.
We reserved a table at the new rotisserie for our family dinner.

Etymology

From French *rôtisserie* (shop selling roasted meats), from *rôtir* (to roast).

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈtɪsəri / (AmE), / rəʊˈtɪsəri / (BrE)
an oven or broiler fitted with a rotating spit where meat is cooked as it turns
oven with a rotating spit for meat

Examples

He roasted the chicken in the rotisserie for a perfectly crispy skin.
Many supermarkets sell pre-cooked rotisserie chickens near the deli section.
The chef demonstrated how to use a rotisserie to cook lamb evenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - a long, thin rod used to hold meat while it is roasted over a fire or in an oven

Etymology

From French 'rôtisserie' (cookshop for roasting), from 'rôtir' (to roast).
