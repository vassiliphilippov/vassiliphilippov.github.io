clayiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being composed of clay or having clay-like characteristics.
quality of being clay-like

Examples

The clayiness of the soil made drainage difficult after heavy rains.
Some plants struggle to grow in areas of high clayiness.
The road construction was delayed due to the unexpected clayiness of the ground.

Synset Examples

During periods of excess moisture, grazing needs to be restricted in many places because of the clayiness of the surface soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clay - a stiff, sticky fine-grained earth used for making bricks, pottery, etc.

Etymology

From 'clay' + '-iness', a suffix forming nouns denoting quality or state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being like clay, especially in texture, thickness, or consistency.
the quality of being like clay

Examples

The clayiness of the soil made it difficult for water to drain properly.
Her sculpture was praised for the realistic clayiness of the model's skin.
The riverbank's clayiness caused our boots to stick with every step.
