to overgrow

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
To grow too large or excessively, often beyond what is normal or desired.
to grow too large or excessively

Examples

The weeds overgrew the entire garden by the end of summer.
If left unchecked, tree roots can quickly overgrow their containers.
The city pond was overgrown with algae after the warm spell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outgrow - to grow too large for something
  • Antonym: to wither - to shrivel or cease growing

Etymology

From prefix 'over-' (excessive) + 'grow' (to increase in size).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to grow beyond or across something, especially so as to cover it completely or spread unchecked
grow across or spread over

Examples

The backyard fence was quickly overgrown with flowering vines.
If left unchecked, weeds will overgrow the vegetable garden by midsummer.
Ancient ruins were almost completely overgrown by moss and ferns.

Synset Examples

The ivy overgrew the patio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to put or spread something over or on top of

Etymology

From over- (beyond) + grow; Old English grōwan (to grow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00233086-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
(of plants) to grow over or cover (something) with excessive or uncontrolled growth; to become covered with plants or vegetation.
become covered with vegetation

Examples

The old garden began to overgrow with weeds after months of neglect.
If left unchecked, bamboo will quickly overgrow the entire backyard.
The path to the river had overgrown so much that it was hardly visible anymore.

Synset Examples

The patio overgrew with ivy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overgrown - covered with plants; grown excessively

Etymology

From over- (above, excessively) + grow (from Old English 'grōwan').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00233215-v
Synset POS: v
