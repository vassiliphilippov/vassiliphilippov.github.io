tetrahydrocannabinol

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˌhaɪdroʊkəˈnæbɪnɒl / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəhʌɪdrəʊkəˈnabɪnɒl / (BrE)
a psychoactive chemical compound found in cannabis plants, responsible for most of the psychological effects of marijuana
psychoactive compound in cannabis
medical

Examples

The psychoactive effects of marijuana are mainly caused by tetrahydrocannabinol.
Scientists are studying tetrahydrocannabinol for its potential medical uses.
Most drug tests are designed to detect traces of tetrahydrocannabinol in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannabidiol - a non-psychoactive compound found in cannabis plants

Etymology

From tetra- (four) + hydro- (hydrogen) + cannabinol (compound from cannabis).
