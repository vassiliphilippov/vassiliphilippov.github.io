Daedal

1. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪ.dəl / (AmE), / ˈdiː.dəl / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, an Athenian inventor who built the labyrinth for King Minos of Crete and escaped using wings he made for himself and his son Icarus.
mythical Greek inventor; builder of the labyrinth

Examples

According to the myth, Daedal built wings of feathers and wax for himself and his son.
The story of Daedal and Icarus is a classic cautionary tale from Greek mythology.
Many artists have depicted Daedal constructing the labyrinth for King Minos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Icarus - son of Daedalus who flew too close to the sun
  • Synonym: labyrinth - maze built by Daedalus for King Minos

Etymology

From Latin Daedalus, from Ancient Greek Daidalos (Δαίδαλος), a legendary craftsman.
