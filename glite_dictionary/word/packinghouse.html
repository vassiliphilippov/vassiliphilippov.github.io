packinghouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a building where food products, especially meat or fruit, are processed and packed for shipment
building for processing and packing food

Examples

The old packinghouse has been converted into a community art center.
She got a summer job sorting apples in the packinghouse near the orchard.
Strict hygiene standards are enforced in every packinghouse to ensure food safety.

Synset Examples

they came from an apple packinghouse

Etymology

Formed from 'packing' + 'house', referring to a place for packaging goods.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a factory where livestock are slaughtered and processed into meat products for packing and distribution
meat processing factory

Examples

After the tour of the packinghouse, the students understood how meat was processed before reaching stores.
The town's economy relied heavily on the large packinghouse that employed dozens of local residents.
Strict regulations are enforced at every packinghouse to ensure food safety and hygiene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughterhouse - a place where animals are killed for food
