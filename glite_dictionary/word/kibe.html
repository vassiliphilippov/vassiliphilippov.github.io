kibe

1. NOUN

/ kaɪb / (AmE), / kaɪb / (BrE)
an ulcerated sore, especially a chilblain on the heel or foot
ulcerated sore (chilblain) on heel or foot
archaic medical

Examples

After walking through the snow in thin boots, a painful kibe developed on his heel.
In Shakespeare’s plays, the word kibe sometimes refers to a sore caused by cold weather.
The doctor examined the infected kibe and recommended rest and bandaging.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *kybe* (swelling; sore), of uncertain origin.
