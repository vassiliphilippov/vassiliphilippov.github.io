alienism

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪliənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪliənɪzəm / (BrE)
an archaic term for the study and treatment of mental illness
archaic term for psychiatry
archaic medical

Examples

In the 19th century, alienism was considered a distinct branch of medicine.
The history of alienism reveals important shifts in attitudes toward mental health.
Although alienism is no longer practiced as such, its influence shaped modern psychiatry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychiatry - the scientific study and treatment of mental illness

Etymology

From French *aliénisme*, from *aliéné* (insane), from Latin *alienatus* (estranged).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.li.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.li.ə.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The state or quality of being foreign, strange, or different; the condition of being alien.
state of being foreign or strange

Examples

In his poetry, the sense of alienism pervades each line, highlighting the author's feelings of exclusion.
Some critics attribute the novel's unsettling tone to its subtle exploration of alienism.
The immigrant struggled with alienism in a society so different from his own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreignness - the quality of being foreign or unfamiliar
  • Synonym: alienation - the state of being isolated or estranged

Etymology

From Latin 'alienus' (belonging to another), with '-ism' forming a noun.
