Herpestes nyula

1. NOUN

/ hərˈpɛstiːz ˈnjuːlə / (AmE), / hɜːˈpɛstiːz ˈnjuːlə / (BrE)
A species of mongoose native to southern Asia, about the size of a ferret and often domesticated.
mongoose species of southern Asia

Examples

The Herpestes nyula is known for its agility and keen eyesight in hunting snakes.
Villagers sometimes keep Herpestes nyula as pets to control pests around the home.
Despite its small size, the Herpestes nyula is a fearless opponent of venomous snakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongoose - a small carnivorous mammal known for eating snakes

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Herpestes' as the genus name and 'nyula' as the species epithet; scientific naming convention.
