hooker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkər / (AmE), / ˈhʊkə / (BrE)
An informal term for a prostitute, especially one who solicits customers in public places such as streets.
street prostitute (informal)
informal

Examples

The police arrested several hookers during the late-night raid.
He was caught trying to pick up a hooker on Main Street.
Many hookers work in the city center after dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʊkə / (BrE)
(in rugby) the player who occupies the central position in the front row of the scrum and whose role is to try to win possession of the ball with the foot.
rugby player in center of scrum

Examples

In the scrum, the team's hooker must be quick to react and win the ball.
The injured hooker was replaced by a substitute after halftime.
He was praised for his excellent performance as hooker during the championship match.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkər / (AmE), / ˈhʊkə / (BrE)
A golfer whose shots typically curve sharply to the left (for right-handed golfers).
golfer whose shots curve left

Examples

As a hooker, he often found himself in trouble on the left side of the fairway.
His coach helped him correct the swing that made him a notorious hooker among his friends.
Even professional golfers can become a hooker if they lose control of their grip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slicer - a golfer whose shots curve to the right (for right-handed golfers)
