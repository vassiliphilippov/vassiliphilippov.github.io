terminology

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrmɪˈnɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌtɜːmɪˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the set of technical or specialized words used in a particular subject, discipline, or area of activity
specialized vocabulary of a discipline

Examples

The report was filled with scientific terminology that was hard for most people to understand.
Doctors often use medical terminology when talking about diseases and treatments.
Legal terminology can be confusing for someone without a background in law.

Synset Examples

legal terminology
biological nomenclature
the language of sociology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a particular group
  • Synonym: nomenclature - the system or set of names used in a particular field

Etymology

From Medieval Latin terminologia, from Latin terminus (end, boundary).
