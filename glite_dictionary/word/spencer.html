Spencer

1. PROPN

/ ˈspɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈspɛnsə / (BrE)
Herbert Spencer (1820–1903), an English philosopher and sociologist known for applying the theory of natural selection to human societies.
English philosopher and sociologist (1820–1903)
american british

Examples

Spencer argued that societies evolve in much the same way as organisms do.
The ideas of Spencer influenced many thinkers during the late nineteenth century.
Many sociologists have debated Spencer's application of natural selection to human communities.
