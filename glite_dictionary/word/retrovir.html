Retrovir

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌvɪr / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌvɪə / (BrE)
A trademarked antiviral drug (also known as zidovudine or AZT) used to treat HIV/AIDS, which may cause side effects such as liver damage and bone marrow suppression.
antiviral drug for HIV/AIDS treatment
medical

Examples

His doctor prescribed Retrovir as part of his antiretroviral therapy regimen.
Some patients on Retrovir have experienced side effects such as fatigue and nausea.
The introduction of Retrovir marked a turning point in the treatment of HIV/AIDS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zidovudine - the generic name for the drug Retrovir
