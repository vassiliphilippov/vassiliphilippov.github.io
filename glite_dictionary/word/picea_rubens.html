Picea rubens

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiː.ə ˈruː.benz / (AmE), / pɪˈsiː.ə ˈruː.benz / (BrE)
A medium-sized spruce tree native to eastern North America, important as a source of lumber and pulpwood.
a spruce tree species native to eastern North America

Examples

The forests of New England are home to the majestic Picea rubens.
Timber companies value Picea rubens as a chief source of high-quality lumber in the region.
Paper mills frequently use the wood of Picea rubens for pulp production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red spruce - common name for Picea rubens, a North American coniferous tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Picea' meaning spruce, 'rubens' meaning reddish, referring to the tree's wood or bark.
