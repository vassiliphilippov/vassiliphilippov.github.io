uninvolved

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈvɑlvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈvɒlvd / (BrE)
not participating or connected with a situation, activity, or relationship; not involved.
not taking part or connected

Examples

Despite the heated debate, Maria remained uninvolved and impartial in her comments.
Many parents prefer to stay uninvolved in their teenagers' disagreements unless absolutely necessary.
The judge must be completely uninvolved to guarantee a fair trial.

Synset Examples

being uninvolved, he remained objective

Related Terms

  • Antonym: involved - taking part or connected with a situation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01520915-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈvɑlvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈvɒlvd / (BrE)
not emotionally affected or showing a lack of emotional interest or engagement; detached.
not emotionally engaged; detached

Examples

She remained uninvolved in the family argument, quietly reading her book.
Despite the chaos around him, his expression was calm and uninvolved.
The judge must be completely uninvolved in the case to ensure fairness.

Synset Examples

adopted a degage pose on the arm of the easy chair
she may be detached or even unfeeling but at least she's not hypocritically effusive
an uninvolved bystander

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detached - not emotionally involved; impartial
  • Antonym: involved - having a part in something; emotionally engaged
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00548375-s
Synset POS: s
