tapeworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪpˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈteɪpˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a long, flat, ribbon-like parasitic worm that lives in the intestines of humans or other animals
parasitic intestinal flatworm

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with a tapeworm after noticing abnormal weight loss and digestive issues.
It is important to cook pork thoroughly to prevent tapeworm infections.
A tapeworm can grow several metres long inside its host's intestine.

Etymology

From tape (referring to its shape) + worm.
