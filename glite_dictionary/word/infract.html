to infract

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfrækt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrækt / (BrE)
To act in violation of a law, rule, contract, or promise; to break or disregard.
break or violate (a law, rule, or promise)

Examples

Anyone who infracts the company's code of conduct may face disciplinary action.
The student was warned not to infract the school's strict uniform policy again.
Repeatedly infracting the community guidelines could result in a ban from the forum.

Synset Examples

offend all laws of humanity
violate the basic laws or human civilization
break a law
break a promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to violate - to break or fail to comply with (a rule, formal agreement, etc.)
  • Antonym: to comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command

Etymology

From Latin infractus, past participle of infringere (to break).
