Zantac

1. NOUN

/ ˈzæn.tæk / (AmE), / ˈzæn.tæk / (BrE)
A trademarked medication used to reduce stomach acid, commonly prescribed for peptic ulcers, gastritis, and acid reflux.
trademarked antacid medication
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Zantac to help control my acid reflux symptoms.
After taking Zantac, his heartburn subsided within an hour.
Many people relied on Zantac for relief before it was withdrawn from the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranitidine - the generic name for the drug sold as Zantac
