bumph

1. NOUN

/ bʌmf / (AmE), / bʌmf / (BrE)
documents or written information, especially considered unnecessary or tedious to read
tedious or unnecessary documents
informal british

Examples

My desk is buried under piles of bumph from the finance department.
She spent her whole afternoon sorting through endless bumph before finding the report she needed.
Most of the mail I get these days is just marketing bumph that goes straight in the bin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paperwork - documents or forms that need to be completed or dealt with
  • Synonym: bureaucracy - excessive administrative procedures or paperwork

Etymology

Originally British slang, short for 'bum-fodder' (toilet paper), a humorous term for useless papers.
