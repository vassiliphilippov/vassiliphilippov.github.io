Discomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskoʊˈmaɪsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəʊˈmɪsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of ascomycete fungi characterized by disk-shaped or cup-shaped fruiting bodies
class of fungi with disk-like fruiting bodies

Examples

The Discomycetes are commonly found on decaying wood in temperate forests.
Researchers are still clarifying the taxonomy of Discomycetes due to their diverse fruiting bodies.
Many edible fungi, such as morels, belong to the group of Discomycetes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascomycetes - a class of fungi bearing spores in sac-like structures

Etymology

From Greek 'diskos' (disk) + 'mykēs' (fungus).
