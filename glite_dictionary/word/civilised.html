civilised

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəlaɪzd / (BrE)
having an advanced level of social, cultural, and technological development; behaving in a manner considered polite or refined.
advanced in culture or manners

Examples

The debate was conducted in a civilised and respectful manner.
Many people believe that education is the foundation of a civilised society.
Advances in medicine are often seen as a mark of a civilised nation.

Synset Examples

terrorist acts that shocked the civilized world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncivilised - lacking cultural and moral development; not polite or refined
  • Synonym: cultured - characterized by refined taste and manners

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to a citizen), via Old French 'civiliser'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪv.əˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.ɪ.laɪzd / (BrE)
marked by refinement, good manners, and polite behavior; having or showing advanced social and cultural development.
refined and polite in manners

Examples

Her civilised manners made a strong impression at the formal dinner.
They discussed their differences in a civilised and respectful way.
The museum reflects the achievements of a civilised society.

Synset Examples

cultivated speech
cultured Bostonians
cultured tastes
a genteel old lady
polite society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultured - showing good education, taste, and manners
  • Synonym: genteel - polite, refined, or respectable in an affected or ostentatious way
  • Antonym: uncivilised - lacking refinement, education, or culture; rude or barbaric

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to a citizen, public life) via French 'civilisé'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01954376-s
Synset POS: s
