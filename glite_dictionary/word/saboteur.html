saboteur

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæbəˈtɜr / (AmE), / ˌsæbəˈtɜː / (BrE)
a person who deliberately damages or destroys property or disrupts activities, especially for political or military advantage
person who commits sabotage

Examples

The factory was shut down after a saboteur tampered with the machinery.
During wartime, a saboteur was caught attempting to destroy crucial supply lines.
Rumors spread that a saboteur was operating within the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabotage - the act of deliberately destroying or damaging something for political or military advantage

Etymology

From French saboteur, from saboter (to sabotage).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæbəˈtɝ / (AmE), / ˌsabəˈtɜː / (BrE)
a person who deliberately destroys, damages, or obstructs something, especially for political or military advantage
person who commits sabotage

Examples

The police arrested the saboteur before any damage could be done to the railway.
During wartime, a single saboteur managed to disrupt communications across the front lines.
The company suspected that a saboteur was behind the factory's repeated equipment failures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabotage - deliberate destruction or obstruction, especially for political or military purposes

Etymology

From French saboteur, from saboter (to sabotage).
