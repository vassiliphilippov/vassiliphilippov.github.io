terminated

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜːrmɪˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of, for example, a contract or position) having been brought to an end; no longer continuing.
brought to an end; finished

Examples

The employee's contract was terminated after several policy violations.
Once the lease is terminated, the tenant must vacate the property.
Her appointment as manager was terminated at the end of the fiscal year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ended - brought to a conclusion; finished
  • Antonym: ongoing - continuing, still in progress

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɝː.məˌneɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪˌneɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Having come to an end; having been brought to a conclusion or stopped.
brought to an end; concluded

Examples

Her contract was terminated after the project was completed.
The meeting abruptly terminated when the fire alarm went off.
Access to the service is terminated after 90 days of inactivity.

Synset Examples

the harvesting was complete
the affair is over, ended, finished
the abruptly terminated interview

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ended - having come to an end
  • Antonym: unfinished - not finished, not brought to an end
