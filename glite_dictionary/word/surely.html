surely

1. ADV

/ ˈʃʊrli / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːli / (BrE)
Without doubt; certainly; used to express that something is definite or confidently expected.
certainly; without doubt

Examples

The new evidence will surely convince the jury of his innocence.
She will surely be promoted after her outstanding performance this year.
If we leave now, we will surely arrive on time for the meeting.

Synset Examples

the results are surely encouraging
she certainly is a hard worker
it's going to be a good day for sure
they are coming, for certain
they thought he had been killed sure enough
he'll win sure as shooting
they sure smell good
sure he'll come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without any doubt; definitely
  • Antonym: doubtfully - in a way that expresses doubt; not certainly

Etymology

From sure + -ly, formed in Middle English after Old French 'seür' and Latin 'securus'.
