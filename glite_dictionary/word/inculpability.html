inculpability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌkʌlpəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkʌlpəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state of being free from blame or guilt; innocence
state of being without blame or guilt

Examples

Despite the accusations, the evidence supported her inculpability in the matter.
The court found that the documents proved his complete inculpability.
She maintained her inculpability throughout the investigation, insisting she had done nothing wrong.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guilt - the state of having done something wrong or blameworthy
  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being free from moral wrong or legal guilt

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + culpabilis (blameworthy).
