burned

1. ADJ

/ bɝːnd / (AmE), / bɜːnd / (BrE)
damaged or destroyed as a result of fire or intense heat
damaged or destroyed by fire

Examples

After the wildfire, only burned trees remained in the forest.
The chef threw away the burned toast and made a fresh batch.
Investigators examined the burned building for clues to the fire’s origin.

Synset Examples

a row of burned houses
a charred bit of burnt wood
a burned-over site in the forest
barricaded the street with burnt-out cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burnt - damaged or destroyed by fire

2. ADJ

/ bɝnd / (AmE), / bɜːnd / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) treated by heating to a high temperature but below its melting or fusing point.
treated by heating (not melted)

Examples

Artists frequently use burned ochre as a pigment for earthy tones.
The ceramic was burned to achieve its distinctive color and texture.
Some metallic finishes are created by applying a burned coating to the surface.

Synset Examples

burnt sienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burnt - (of a substance) treated by heating but not melted; alternative spelling of 'burned' in certain contexts
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01961085-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ bɝːnd / (AmE), / bɜːnd / (BrE)
(of food) spoiled by being overcooked or charred, often turning black or having a bitter taste.
ruined by being overcooked (food)

Examples

The toast was completely burned and tasted bitter.
He accidentally left the pizza in the oven until it was burned.
Nobody wanted to eat the burned cookies from the first batch.

Synset Examples

she served us underdone bacon and burnt biscuits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercooked - (of food) not cooked enough
  • Synonym: burnt - alternative spelling; (of food) spoiled by overcooking or charring
