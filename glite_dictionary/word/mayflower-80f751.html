mayflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a low-growing evergreen shrub (Epigaea repens) of eastern North America, with leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers
north American evergreen shrub with fragrant flowers

Examples

The mayflower is often one of the first wildflowers to bloom in the spring forest.
Hiking along the trail, we spotted several patches of mayflower nestled among the moss.
The delicate scent of mayflower filled the air after the morning rain.

Etymology

From the month of May + flower, referring to its blooming season.
