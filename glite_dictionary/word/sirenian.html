sirenian

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈriːniən / (AmE), / saɪˈriːniən / (BrE)
a large herbivorous aquatic mammal of the order Sirenia, such as a manatee or dugong, characterized by paddle-shaped tails and flipper-like forelimbs
large herbivorous aquatic mammal (order Sirenia)

Examples

The sirenian population has declined sharply due to habitat destruction.
Dugongs, a type of sirenian, are found in warm coastal waters.
Scientists are studying sirenian fossils to learn about ancient marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - an aquatic mammal of the family Trichechidae, related to the dugong
  • Synonym: dugong - a large marine mammal similar to the manatee

Etymology

From modern Latin Sirenia, from Latin Siren (a mythical sea creature).
