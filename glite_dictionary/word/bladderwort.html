bladderwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædɚˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈblædəˌwɜːt / (BrE)
An aquatic carnivorous plant of the genus Utricularia, whose leaves are modified into tiny bladder-like traps to catch small aquatic animals.
aquatic carnivorous plant with trapping bladders

Examples

Bladderwort can be found in ponds and lakes around the world, floating freely or anchored in muddy bottoms.
Scientists study bladderwort to understand how it catches tiny creatures underwater using its bladder-like traps.
The delicate flowers of the bladderwort make it popular among aquatic plant enthusiasts.

Etymology

From bladder + wort, where 'wort' means plant or herb.
