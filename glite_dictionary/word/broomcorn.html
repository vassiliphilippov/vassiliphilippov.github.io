broomcorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːmˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbruːmkɔːn / (BrE)
a tall grass, especially a variety of sorghum, grown for its stiff, brush-like seed heads used in making brooms and brushes
a grass used for making brooms

Examples

Farmers in the region grew broomcorn to supply local broom factories.
The tough panicles of broomcorn are ideal for making sturdy brooms.
She learned how to harvest and dry broomcorn for traditional broom making.

Etymology

From 'broom' (a cleaning tool) + 'corn' (grain), referring to its use in broom-making.
