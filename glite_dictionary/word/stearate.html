stearate

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪəˌreɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester formed from stearic acid, commonly used in cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and as a lubricant.
salt or ester of stearic acid
medical

Examples

Magnesium stearate is commonly used as a flow agent in the production of tablets.
The soap was made by reacting sodium hydroxide with stearate.
Some cosmetics use zinc stearate as an ingredient to improve texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stearic acid - a saturated fatty acid used in making soaps and cosmetics

Etymology

From stearic acid, itself from Greek 'stear' meaning tallow.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiː.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪə.rɛɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of stearic acid, commonly used in chemistry and industry
salt or ester of stearic acid
medical

Examples

Magnesium stearate is often used as a flow agent in the production of tablets.
Sodium stearate acts as a key ingredient in many types of soap.
The chemist explained that stearate compounds are derived from stearic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stearic acid - a saturated fatty acid found in animal and vegetable fats

Etymology

From 'stearic acid', ultimately from Greek 'stear' (fat, tallow).
