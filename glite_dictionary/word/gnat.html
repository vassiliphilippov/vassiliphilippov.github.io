gnat

1. NOUN

/ næt / (AmE), / nat / (BrE)
a very small flying insect, especially one that bites people and animals
small biting flying insect

Examples

A gnat bit my arm while I was hiking in the forest.
Swatting away a single gnat can be surprisingly difficult.
Clouds of gnats hovered over the pond at sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midge - small fly similar to a gnat, often biting
  • Synonym: mosquito - a small flying insect that bites and sucks blood

Etymology

Old English gnat, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch gnat and German Gnitze.

2. NOUN

/ næt / (AmE), / næt / (BrE)
(British) A small flying insect, especially one of various small biting flies such as the mosquito, in British usage.
(Brit.) small biting fly; mosquito
british

Examples

After the rain, clouds of gnats hovered over the marshland.
In summer, I often get bitten by gnats near the river.
The tent was filled with gnats until we zipped it up tightly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - A small flying insect that bites and sucks blood; widespread use in American English

Etymology

Old English gnætt (insect), related to Germanic forms such as German 'Gnitze' (gnat).
