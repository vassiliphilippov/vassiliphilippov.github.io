naysayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪˌseɪər / (AmE), / ˈneɪˌseɪə / (BrE)
a person who habitually expresses negative or pessimistic views or opposes proposals or ideas
person who habitually opposes or is pessimistic

Examples

There will always be at least one naysayer who doubts the project's success.
Despite the naysayers, she went ahead with her innovative plan.
Don't let a single naysayer discourage you from pursuing your dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future or success

Etymology

Formed from 'nay' (no) + 'sayer' (one who says); used since the 17th century.
