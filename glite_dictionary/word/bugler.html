bugler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːɡlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːɡlə / (BrE)
a person who plays the bugle, especially in a military or ceremonial context.
person who plays the bugle

Examples

The army bugler woke the troops at dawn with a rousing call.
At the ceremony, a bugler played the national anthem.
The sound of the bugler echoed through the silent cemetery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter - person who plays the trumpet

Etymology

From 'bugle' (a brass instrument) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
