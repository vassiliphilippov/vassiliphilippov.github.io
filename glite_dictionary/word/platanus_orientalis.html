Platanus orientalis

1. NOUN

/ pləˈteɪnəs ɔːˌrɪənˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / pləˈtænəs ɒˌrɪənˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to southeastern Europe and western Asia, also known as the Oriental plane.
Oriental plane tree species

Examples

The ancient city was lined with majestic Platanus orientalis trees that offered welcome shade.
Botanists studied the bark of the Platanus orientalis for evidence of disease resistance.
A row of Platanus orientalis can often be seen in historic parks across Istanbul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plane tree - a tree of the genus Platanus

Etymology

From Latin: Platanus (plane tree) + orientalis (eastern).
