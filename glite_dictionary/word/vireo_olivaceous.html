Vireo olivaceous

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪrioʊ ˌoʊlɪˈveɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˌvɪərɪəʊ ˌɒlɪˈveɪsiəs / (BrE)
A North American songbird with red irises, an olive-grey back, and white underparts, commonly known as the red-eyed vireo.
a North American songbird; red-eyed vireo

Examples

The Vireo olivaceous is often heard before it is seen, thanks to its distinctive song.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Vireo olivaceous during spring migration.
The olive-grey plumage of the Vireo olivaceous helps it blend into the forest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-eyed vireo - Common name for Vireo olivaceous, a North American songbird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Vireo and species epithet olivaceous (Latin 'olive-colored').
