ascomycete

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈmaɪsit / (AmE), / ˌæskəˈmaɪsiːt / (BrE)
any fungus of the class Ascomycetes (or subdivision Ascomycota), characterized by the formation of spores inside a saclike structure called an ascus.
fungus forming spores in an ascus
medical

Examples

The scientist identified the mold as an ascomycete based on its spore-producing structure.
Many common yeasts, including baker's yeast, belong to the ascomycete group.
Some ascomycetes play important roles in decomposing organic matter in soil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basidiomycete - fungus forming spores on a basidium, distinct from ascomycete

Etymology

From New Latin Ascomycetes, from Greek askos (bag, sac) + mykēs (fungus).
