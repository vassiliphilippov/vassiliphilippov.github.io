Bighorn River

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪɡˌhɔrn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡˌhɔːn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the western United States flowing from central Wyoming northward to join the Yellowstone River in southern Montana
river in Wyoming and Montana, USA

Examples

The Bighorn River is popular among anglers for its abundant trout population.
We camped along the Bighorn River and enjoyed the beautiful mountain views.
The Bighorn River rises in the Rocky Mountains and travels through stunning canyons.

Etymology

Named after the bighorn sheep native to the region through which it flows.
