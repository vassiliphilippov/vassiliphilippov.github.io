calyculate

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪkjələt / (AmE), / kəˈlɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
(Botany) Having a calyculus; possessing a whorl of small bracts resembling a calyx beneath the true calyx.
having a calyculus in botany
medical

Examples

The calyculate involucre supports the delicate petals of the flower.
Some thistle species are easily identified by their calyculate heads.
The botanist noted the calyculate nature of the plant's bracts in her field notes.

Etymology

From New Latin calyculus (diminutive of calyx, meaning 'little cup').
