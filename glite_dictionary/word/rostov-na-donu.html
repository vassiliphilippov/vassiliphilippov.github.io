Rostov-na-Donu

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɒsˌtɔv nə ˈdoʊnu / (AmE), / ˌrɒstɒf nə ˈdɒnuː / (BrE)
a city in the southwestern part of Russia, located as a major seaport on the Don River near the Sea of Azov
major Russian city and port on the Don River

Examples

He grew up in Rostov-na-Donu before moving to Moscow for university.
The team from Rostov-na-Donu reached the semi-finals in the national championship.
Many ships pass through Rostov-na-Donu on their way to the Black Sea.

Etymology

Russian, meaning 'Rostov-on-Don,' from 'Rostov' (the city) and 'na Donu' (on the Don [River]).
