Courtelle

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtɛl / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɛl / (BrE)
A trademarked acrylic fabric that resembles wool, used mainly in clothing and textiles.
a type of acrylic fabric resembling wool

Examples

The sweater is made from Courtelle, making it both warm and easy to care for.
Many school uniforms use Courtelle because of its durability and wool-like feel.
She prefers Courtelle garments since they are softer and less expensive than real wool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic - a synthetic fiber made from polyacrylonitrile, often used in textiles

Etymology

A trademark, originally registered for a brand of acrylic fibre developed in the mid-20th century.
