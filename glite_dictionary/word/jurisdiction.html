jurisdiction

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɝːɪsˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
the official power to make legal decisions and judgments, or the area within which this power applies
the area under legal authority
legal

Examples

The court has no jurisdiction outside the state borders.
Each city operates independently within its own jurisdiction.
Environmental regulations may vary from one jurisdiction to another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders and make decisions

Etymology

From Latin 'jurisdictio', from 'jus' (law) + 'dictio' (saying)

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɝːɪsˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
the official power or right to interpret and apply the law, especially within a particular area or for particular types of cases
legal authority or power
legal

Examples

The federal court does not have jurisdiction over this case.
Different agencies may have overlapping jurisdictions in environmental matters.
The dispute is outside the jurisdiction of local authorities.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
