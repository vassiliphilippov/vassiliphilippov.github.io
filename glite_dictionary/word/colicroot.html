colicroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪkˌruːt / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the genus Aletris, with grasslike leaves and bitter roots, formerly believed to treat colic
a perennial plant (Aletris) with bitter roots

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used the colicroot to make herbal remedies for digestive problems.
The bitter taste of colicroot comes from compounds found in its roots and leaves.
Several colicroot plants grew in the meadow, their slender leaves blending with the surrounding grass.

Etymology

From 'colic' (abdominal pain) + 'root', referring to its historical medicinal use.
