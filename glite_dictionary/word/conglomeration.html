conglomeration

1. NOUN

/ kənˌɡlɑːməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌɡlɒməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a rounded mass or cluster of distinct parts gathered together, especially a lump formed by accumulation
a rounded mass or cluster

Examples

A strange conglomeration of pebbles had formed at the riverbank after the storm.
He examined the conglomeration of minerals under the magnifying glass.
A conglomeration of dust bunnies rolled across the wooden floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluster - a group of similar things or people positioned closely together

Etymology

From Latin 'conglomerare' (to wind into a ball), from 'con-' (together) + 'glomerare' (to wind into a ball).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌɡlɑːməˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌɡlɒməˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a collection or mass formed by the combination of various things, often of different kinds
collection or mass of diverse things

Examples

The city is a conglomeration of cultures, languages, and traditions.
A bizarre conglomeration of furniture was piled in the middle of the room after the move.
His desk was covered with a conglomeration of papers, books, and random objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregation - a group or mass of distinct things gathered together

Etymology

From Latin 'conglomerare' (to wind into a ball), from 'con-' (together) + 'glomerare' (to form into a ball).

3. NOUN

/ kənˌɡlɑː.məˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌɡlɒm.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a collection or mixture of different people, things, or elements gathered together as a whole
a collection or mixture of different things

Examples

The city is a conglomeration of styles, cultures, and languages.
On her desk was a conglomeration of papers, books, and coffee mugs.
The festival featured a conglomeration of musicians from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregation - a group or collection of things or people
  • Synonym: mixture - a combination made by mixing different things together
  • Synonym: assortment - a collection of various kinds

Etymology

From Latin 'conglomeratio' (a massing together), from 'conglomerare' (to wind into a ball).
