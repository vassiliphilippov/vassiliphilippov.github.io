road

1. NOUN

/ roʊd / (AmE), / rəʊd / (BrE)
a way or method for achieving or attaining something, often used figuratively
figurative path to achievement

Examples

He worked tirelessly, believing education was the road out of poverty.
Many artists struggle on the road to recognition and success.
Discipline and hard work paved the road to her achievements.

Synset Examples

the road to fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: path - a course of action or way towards a goal, especially figuratively
  • Synonym: route - a way or course taken to reach a goal, especially in a non-literal sense

2. NOUN

/ roʊd / (AmE), / rəʊd / (BrE)
a wide, hard surface built for vehicles and people to travel on, especially between places
a public way for travel or transport

Examples

The road was blocked by fallen trees after the storm.
They drove down a narrow country road to reach the village.
Repairs will be made to the main road next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: street - a public road in a city or town, usually with buildings on one or both sides
  • Synonym: highway - a main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities
  • Degree Lower: path - a way or track laid down for walking or made by continual treading

Etymology

From Old English 'rād' (a riding, a journey on horseback), related to 'ride'.
