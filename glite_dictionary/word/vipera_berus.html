Vipera berus

1. NOUN

/ viˈpɛrə ˈbɛrəs / (AmE), / vɪˈpɪərə ˈbɜːrəs / (BrE)
a small terrestrial viper native to northern Eurasia, commonly known as the common European adder
small viper found in northern Eurasia

Examples

The bite of a Vipera berus is rarely fatal to humans but can cause significant discomfort.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Vipera berus in the wild.
Hikers in northern Europe are advised to watch out for Vipera berus in grasslands and forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adder - a common name for the Vipera berus, a venomous snake

Etymology

From Latin: 'vipera' (viper) and 'berus' (adder).
