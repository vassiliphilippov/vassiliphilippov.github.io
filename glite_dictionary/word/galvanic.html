galvanic

1. ADJ

/ ɡælˈvænɪk / (AmE), / ɡælˈvanɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or producing electricity by chemical action, especially through the use of a galvanic cell.
producing electricity by chemical action

Examples

The battery relies on a galvanic reaction to generate power.
Engineers studied the galvanic corrosion that occurs when different metals are connected underwater.
A galvanic cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy.

Synset Examples

a galvanic cell
a voltaic (or galvanic) couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltaic - producing electric current by chemical means; used interchangeably with 'galvanic' in technical contexts

Etymology

From Luigi Galvani, Italian physician, who studied electricity produced by chemical reactions (18th century).

2. ADJ

/ ɡælˈvænɪk / (AmE), / ɡælˈvanɪk / (BrE)
producing a sudden and powerful emotional effect, as if by an electric shock; thrilling or energizing.
sudden, powerful, thrilling effect

Examples

The speaker's galvanic presence inspired the entire audience to action.
Her galvanic performance brought the theater to its feet in applause.
The team's comeback had a galvanic impact on their supporters.

Synset Examples

gave an electric reading of the play
the new leader had a galvanic effect on morale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric - having an intense, exciting, or thrilling effect (figurative sense)
  • Synonym: stirring - causing excitement or strong emotion
  • Antonym: unexciting - not causing excitement or strong emotion

Etymology

From Italian 'galvanico', after Luigi Galvani (1737–1798), an Italian scientist known for his work on electricity.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
