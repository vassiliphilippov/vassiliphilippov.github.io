Icterus galbula

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪktərəs ɡælˈbjuːlə / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs ɡælˈbjuːlə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Baltimore oriole, a species of New World songbird known for its bright orange and black plumage
Baltimore oriole species (bird)

Examples

The Icterus galbula is easily recognized by its striking orange and black feathers.
Birdwatchers travel long distances to catch a glimpse of the Icterus galbula during migration.
In scientific literature, the Icterus galbula is often studied for its nesting habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baltimore oriole - common name for Icterus galbula, a brightly colored American songbird

Etymology

From Latin: 'Icterus' (a yellow bird) and 'galbula' (little yellow bird).
