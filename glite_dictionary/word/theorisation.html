theorisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌθiəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɪərəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forming or applying theories, especially in a formal or systematic way
process of forming theories

Examples

Academic debates often revolve around the theorisation of complex social phenomena.
His theorisation of quantum mechanics changed the course of physics.
The theorisation in her latest paper offers a fresh perspective on political power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to theorize - to form a theory or set of theories about something
  • Degree Lower: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
  • Synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence

Etymology

From theory + -isation, modeled on similar formations in English.
