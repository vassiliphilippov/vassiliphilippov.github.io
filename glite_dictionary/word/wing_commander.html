wing commander

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋ kəˈmændər / (AmE), / wɪŋ kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
an officer of the Royal Air Force who ranks above a squadron leader and below a group captain
RAF rank above squadron leader
british

Examples

She was promoted to wing commander after ten years of service in the RAF.
The wing commander led the meeting with calm authority.
He retired as a wing commander and later became an aviation consultant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: group captain - RAF rank above wing commander
  • Degree Lower: squadron leader - RAF rank below wing commander
