THz

1. NOUN

/ ti eɪtʃ zɪ / (AmE), / tiː eɪtʃ zɛd / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one trillion hertz (1,000,000,000,000 Hz), commonly used in physics and electronics
unit of frequency: one trillion hertz

Examples

The spectrometer can detect signals up to 1 THz in frequency.
Researchers use THz radiation to study materials at the molecular level.
Wireless communication at THz frequencies is an emerging area of technology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hertz - a unit of frequency equal to one cycle per second
  • Degree Lower: GHz - a unit of frequency equal to one billion hertz

Etymology

Abbreviation of terahertz: 'tera-' (Greek for 'monster', used in metric system to denote a factor of one trillion) and 'hertz' (after Heinrich Hertz).
