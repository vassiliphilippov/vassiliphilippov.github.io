handmaiden

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌmeɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌmeɪdn̩ / (BrE)
a female personal attendant or maid; a woman who serves another as an assistant
female personal attendant or maid

Examples

The queen's trusted handmaiden attended to her every need throughout the day.
She worked as a handmaiden in the noblewoman's household, performing a variety of tasks.
Literature often features the devoted handmaiden as a symbol of loyalty and service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maid - a female domestic servant

Etymology

From Old English 'handmæġden' (maidservant), from 'hand' + 'maiden'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌmeɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhandˌmeɪdn̩ / (BrE)
A person or thing that serves or is subordinate to another; an auxiliary or supporter in a secondary role.
person or thing in a subordinate or supporting role

Examples

In this system, science became the handmaiden of politics, supporting government agendas.
Historically, art was often seen as the handmaiden to religion, illustrating spiritual lessons.
She resented the idea that her role was merely a handmaiden to his ambitions.

Synset Examples

theology should be the handmaiden of ethics
the state cannot be a servant of the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially as a job
  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps or supports someone else
  • Antonym: master - a person with control or authority over others

Etymology

From Old English handmægden (hand + maiden), originally meaning a female servant.
