Thomas Stearns Eliot

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑːməs stɜrnz ˈɛliət / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs stɜːnz ˈɛliət / (BrE)
(1888–1965) British poet, playwright, and critic, born in the United States, known for influential modernist works such as 'The Waste Land' and for winning the Nobel Prize in Literature.
British poet and Nobel laureate

Examples

Thomas Stearns Eliot is widely recognized for his poem 'The Waste Land', which profoundly influenced modern poetry.
Many consider Thomas Stearns Eliot one of the greatest English-language poets of the twentieth century.
In 1948, Thomas Stearns Eliot was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his outstanding contributions.
