leakiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːkinəs / (AmE), / ˈliːkinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of allowing liquid or gas to pass through leaks or openings
condition of allowing leaks

Examples

The leakiness of the old roof caused constant problems during heavy rainstorms.
Scientists tested the leakiness of various types of containers for safe chemical storage.
Due to its leakiness, the inflatable pool quickly lost most of its water.

Synset Examples

the leakiness of the roof
the heart valve's leakiness
the leakiness of the boat made it dangerous to use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leakage - the act or process of leaking
  • Antonym: tightness - the quality of being sealed against leaks

Etymology

From 'leaky' + suffix '-ness', meaning the state or quality of being leaky.
