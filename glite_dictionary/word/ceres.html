Ceres

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.riːz / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of agriculture, crops, and fertility; the Roman counterpart of the Greek goddess Demeter.
Roman goddess of agriculture

Examples

In ancient Roman religion, Ceres was worshipped as the protector of crops and grain.
The story of Ceres and her daughter's abduction explains the changing seasons in Roman mythology.
Statues of Ceres often depict her holding a sheaf of wheat, symbolizing abundance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Demeter - Greek goddess of agriculture and fertility, counterpart of Ceres

Etymology

From Latin Ceres, the name of the goddess.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, classified as both an asteroid and a dwarf planet
largest asteroid and dwarf planet

Examples

Scientists have discovered water ice on Ceres, suggesting the possibility of past or present life.
Ceres was the first object in the asteroid belt to be discovered and is now also classified as a dwarf planet.
NASA's Dawn spacecraft orbited Ceres to study its surface and composition.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ceres', the Roman goddess of agriculture; chosen by discoverer Giuseppe Piazzi in 1801.
