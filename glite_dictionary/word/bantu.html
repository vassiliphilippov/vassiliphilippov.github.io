Bantu

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.tu / (AmE), / ˈban.tuː / (BrE)
A member of any of a large group of peoples in Central and Southern Africa who speak Bantu languages.
member of a Central/Southern African ethnolinguistic group

Examples

The Bantu migrated across large areas of Africa over thousands of years.
Many Bantu traditions are still practiced in rural communities today.
Archaeologists study the ancient settlements of Bantu peoples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu languages - A group of closely related African languages spoken by the Bantu peoples

Etymology

From the Bantu languages, meaning 'people' (ba- plural prefix + -ntu 'person').

2. ADJ

/ ˈbænˌtuː / (AmE), / ˈban.tuː / (BrE)
relating to the Bantu-speaking peoples of Africa or to their culture or languages
relating to Bantu peoples or culture

Examples

The Bantu languages are spoken across much of central and southern Africa.
Researchers have traced the origins of several Bantu customs to ancient migratory periods.
Traditional Bantu music features rhythmic drumming and group singing.

Synset Examples

the Bantu population of Sierra Leone

Etymology

From Bantu, the self-designation of many Central and Southern African ethnic groups, from the word for 'people' in several Bantu languages.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbænˌtu / (AmE), / ˈbæn.tuː / (BrE)
A family of languages widely spoken in the southern half of the African continent, belonging to the Niger-Congo language group.
large African language family

Examples

Swahili is one of the most widely spoken Bantu languages in Africa.
The Bantu language family includes hundreds of distinct languages and dialects.
Researchers have traced the migration of Bantu speakers across central and southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Niger-Congo - The major language family to which Bantu belongs

Etymology

From the word for 'people' in many Bantu languages (such as Zulu and Xhosa).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07004640-n
Synset POS: n
