Eucalyptus pauciflora

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkæləptəs ˌpɔːsɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪpˈtʌs ˌpɔːsɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized eucalyptus tree native to Australia and Tasmania, with smooth white or light-grey bark that peels in patches or strips; commonly called snow gum
a species of eucalyptus tree (snow gum)

Examples

The Eucalyptus pauciflora thrives in the cold alpine regions of southeastern Australia.
Botanists collected samples of Eucalyptus pauciflora for a study on frost resistance in trees.
The smooth, peeling bark of the Eucalyptus pauciflora makes it easily identifiable in the Tasmanian wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow gum - common name for Eucalyptus pauciflora, an Australian tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'pauciflora' meaning 'few-flowered'.
