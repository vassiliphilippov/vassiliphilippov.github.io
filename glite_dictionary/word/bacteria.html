bacteria

1. NOUN

/ bækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Single-celled microorganisms that lack a nucleus, often found in various environments; some cause disease, while others play important roles in processes such as fermentation and decomposition.
microscopic single-celled organisms
medical

Examples

Some bacteria can cause infections, while others are essential for digestion.
Medical researchers study bacteria to develop new antibiotics and vaccines.
Yogurt contains beneficial bacteria that help maintain a healthy gut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - singular form of bacteria, a single microorganism of this type
  • Antonym: virus - microscopic infectious agent that replicates only in living cells

Etymology

Plural of bacterium, from Greek baktērion (small staff), referring to their shape.
