Jacobean

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒækəˈbiː.ən / (AmE), / ˌdʒækəˈbiː.ən / (BrE)
Relating to, or typical of the period of the reign of James I of England (1603–1625), or to the literature, architecture, or styles of that time.
relating to the reign of James I of England
archaic british

Examples

Many Jacobean plays reflect the complexities of political life during James I's rule.
The Jacobean architecture in this church is famous for its elaborate woodwork.
Her thesis explored the symbolism found in Jacobean literature.

Synset Examples

Jacobean writers

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Jacobeus' (James), from Latin 'Jacobus' (James).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒækəˈbiən / (AmE), / ˌdʒakəˈbiːən / (BrE)
a distinguished person or figure associated with the reign of James I of England (1603–1625)
notable figure from the reign of James I

Examples

Many Jacobeans were influential in shaping the politics of early seventeenth-century England.
The architecture designed by prominent Jacobeans can still be seen today.
Literature from the period often references the values held by the Jacobeans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Elizabethan - notable individual during the reign of Elizabeth I

Etymology

From Latin Iacobus (James) + English -an, referring to James I of England.
