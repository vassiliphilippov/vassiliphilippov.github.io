Trachurus

1. NOUN

/ trəˈkjʊrəs / (AmE), / trəˈkjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Carangidae, commonly known as horse mackerels or scads.
genus of fish (horse mackerels)

Examples

The Atlantic Trachurus is widely fished for food in many countries.
Several species of Trachurus are known as horse mackerels.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the Trachurus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse mackerel - a common name for fish of the genus Trachurus
  • Synonym: scad - another common name for several fish in the genus Trachurus

Etymology

From Greek 'trachys' (rough) and 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ trəˈkjʊrəs / (AmE), / trəˈkjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, commonly known as horse mackerels or scads.
a genus of fish (horse mackerels or scads)

Examples

The Trachurus genus includes several commercially valuable species of horse mackerel.
Marine biologists often study Trachurus populations to assess fishery health.
In Mediterranean cuisine, fish from the Trachurus genus are commonly served grilled or fried.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse mackerel - a common fish species within the genus Trachurus
  • Synonym: scad - common name for fish of the genus Trachurus

Etymology

From Greek *trachys* (rough) + *oura* (tail), referring to the rough texture of the fish's tail.
