sundowner

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdaʊnər / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌdaʊnə / (BrE)
an alcoholic drink taken in the early evening, especially at sundown, as a way to relax at the end of the day
an evening alcoholic drink

Examples

Many safari lodges serve a complimentary sundowner as the sun sets over the savannah.
After a long day of hiking, we enjoyed a cold sundowner on the porch.
He mixed himself a gin and tonic for his usual sundowner ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocktail - an alcoholic mixed drink

Etymology

From 'sundown' (sunset) + '-er', referring to the time the drink is consumed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdaʊnər / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌdaʊnə / (BrE)
(historical, Australia) A tramp or itinerant worker who habitually arrives at a farm at sunset, often seeking food and lodging but offering little work.
a tramp arriving at sunset for food or lodging

Examples

The old farmer complained about the sundowner who showed up every week asking for supper.
During the Depression, many sundowners traveled from station to station in search of work and a meal.
She remembers her grandfather telling stories about sundowners arriving just as the sun was setting.

Etymology

Australian English, from 'sun' + 'downer', referring to arrival at sundown.
