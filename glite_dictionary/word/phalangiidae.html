Phalangiidae

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlændʒiˈɪdiː / (AmE), / fəˌlandʒɪˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of arachnids commonly known as harvestmen or daddy longlegs, found worldwide and characterized by their long, slender legs and small, rounded bodies.
a family of harvestman arachnids

Examples

Members of the Phalangiidae family are often mistaken for spiders due to their appearance.
The distribution of Phalangiidae spans Europe, Asia, and North America.
Scientists have described over 100 species within the Phalangiidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Phalangium' (a genus name) and the standard suffix '-idae' for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ fəˌlændʒiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / fəˌlændʒɪˈɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of arachnids within the order Opiliones, commonly known as harvestmen or daddy longlegs, characterized by long legs and a compact body
a family of harvestman arachnids

Examples

Members of the Phalangiidae family are often mistaken for spiders due to their long legs.
The Phalangiidae are commonly referred to as harvestmen or daddy longlegs in everyday language.
Research on the Phalangiidae has revealed unique adaptations to their habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Phalangiidae, based on genus Phalangium + -idae (zoological family suffix).
