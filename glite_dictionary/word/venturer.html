venturer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.tʃɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tʃər.ər / (BrE)
a person who takes risks, especially someone who is willing to undertake new and daring activities
person who takes risks

Examples

As a seasoned venturer, she never shied away from unpredictable journeys.
The company needed a venturer to lead its expansion into new markets.
Every successful entrepreneur is something of a venturer at heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk-taker - a person who takes risks

Etymology

From venture + -er, denoting one who ventures.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛntʃərər / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tʃər.ə / (BrE)
a person, especially a merchant, who undertakes a commercial venture, often involving overseas trade or investment
person undertaking a risky commercial venture

Examples

As a successful venturer, she invested heavily in new shipping routes to Asia.
The venturer took great risks but reaped substantial profits from his overseas enterprises.
Many early American colonies were established by ambitious venturers seeking new markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrepreneur - a person who organizes and operates a business, taking risks
  • Synonym: merchant - a person involved in wholesale trade, especially with foreign countries

Etymology

From Latin 'ventura', meaning 'about to happen', via Old French 'aventure'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10767810-n
Synset POS: n
