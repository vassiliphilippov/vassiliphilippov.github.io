Cardamine pratensis

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdæmɪni præˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈdamɪni praːˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the mustard family, native to Europe and America, with pale lilac flowers, also known as cuckooflower or lady's smock.
perennial plant with pale lilac flowers

Examples

The delicate flowers of Cardamine pratensis bloom in meadows every spring.
Botanists identified the specimen as Cardamine pratensis during their field survey.
Many gardeners appreciate Cardamine pratensis for its attractive blossoms and natural habitat value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckooflower - common name for Cardamine pratensis
  • Synonym: lady's smock - another common name for Cardamine pratensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cardamine' (a genus of cress) + 'pratensis' (of meadows).
