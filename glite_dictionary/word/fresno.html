Fresno

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛzˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛz.nəʊ / (BrE)
a city in south central California, located in the San Joaquin Valley, known as an important agricultural center and gateway to the Sierra Nevada Mountains
city in south central California

Examples

Fresno is known for its booming agriculture industry and proximity to Yosemite National Park.
She grew up in Fresno before moving to Los Angeles for college.
The climate in Fresno is typically hot and dry during the summer months.

Etymology

From Spanish 'fresno' meaning 'ash tree', named for the abundance of ash trees in the area.
