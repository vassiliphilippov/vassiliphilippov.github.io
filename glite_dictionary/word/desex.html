to desex

1. VERB

/ diˈsɛks / (AmE), / diˈsɛks / (BrE)
to make (an animal or a person) infertile by removing or deactivating the reproductive organs; to sterilize
make infertile; sterilize

Examples

The veterinary clinic will desex your cat to prevent unwanted litters.
Many animal shelters desex pets before they are adopted out to new owners.
Local laws require owners to desex certain breeds of dogs to control the population.

Synset Examples

in some countries, people with genetically transmissible disabilities are sterilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sterilize - to make unable to produce offspring; to make infertile

Etymology

From de- (remove) + sex (biological distinction), meaning to remove sexual function.
