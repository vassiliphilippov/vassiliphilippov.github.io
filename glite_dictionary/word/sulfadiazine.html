sulfadiazine

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌlfəˈdaɪəˌzin / (AmE), / ˌsʌlfəˈdaɪəsiːn / (BrE)
a sulfonamide antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections such as meningitis, pneumonia, and other conditions
sulfonamide antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sulfadiazine to treat his bacterial infection.
Sulfadiazine is often used in combination with other antibiotics for greater effectiveness.
Patients with allergies to sulfa drugs should not take sulfadiazine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfamethoxazole - a related sulfonamide antibiotic drug

Etymology

From 'sulfa-' (relating to sulfonamides) and 'diazine', a type of organic compound.
