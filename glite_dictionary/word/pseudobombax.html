Pseudobombax

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈbɑːmbæks / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈbɒmbaks / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American deciduous shrubs or small trees belonging to the family Malvaceae, known for their large flowers and soft wood.
genus of tropical American shrubs or small trees

Examples

The Pseudobombax ellipticum is admired for its striking white flowers.
Many species of Pseudobombax lose their leaves during the dry season.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Pseudobombax roots in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From pseudo- (false) + Bombax, the related genus name in modern Latin botanical nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ suːdoʊˈbɒmbæks / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˈbɒmbaks / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American deciduous shrubs or small trees belonging to the family Malvaceae, known for their thick trunks and showy flowers.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The Pseudobombax tree is commonly found in South American tropical forests.
Botanists have identified several species within the Pseudobombax genus due to their distinctive flowers.
The thick trunk of the Pseudobombax helps it store water during dry seasons.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and Latin 'Bombax' (a related genus).
