Sciaridae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈarɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small dark-winged flies, commonly called dark-winged fungus gnats, which are often found in damp environments and associated with fungi.
family of dark-winged fungus gnats

Examples

The greenhouse plants were infested with Sciaridae, causing damage to the roots.
Many mushroom farmers use biological controls to reduce the impact of Sciaridae populations.
Scientists collected samples of Sciaridae to study their role in fungus decomposition.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Sciaridae', derived from genus name 'Sciara'.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈærɪˌdi / (AmE), / saɪˈarɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, dark-winged flies commonly known as dark-winged fungus gnats, whose larvae feed on fungi and decaying plant matter
family of dark-winged fungus gnats

Examples

The larvae of Sciaridae can cause significant damage to greenhouse plants.
Entomologists often monitor populations of Sciaridae in mushroom-growing environments.
Sciaridae are sometimes mistaken for fruit flies because of their small size and appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin Sciaridae, based on the type genus Sciara.
