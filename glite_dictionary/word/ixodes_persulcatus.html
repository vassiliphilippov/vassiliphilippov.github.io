Ixodes persulcatus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz pərˌsʌlˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz pəˌsʌlˈkɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of tick known for biting humans and serving as a vector for Lyme disease.
species of tick; vector for Lyme disease
medical

Examples

The Ixodes persulcatus tick is a known carrier of several serious diseases.
Researchers found Ixodes persulcatus in many regions of Eastern Europe and Asia.
Proper tick removal is crucial if bitten by Ixodes persulcatus to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - small arachnid that feeds on blood of mammals, birds, and sometimes reptiles and amphibians

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Ixodes' (tick), species epithet 'persulcatus'.
