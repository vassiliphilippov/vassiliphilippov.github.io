Kandinsky

1. PROPN

/ kænˈdɪnski / (AmE), / kanˈdɪnski / (BrE)
(1866–1944) A Russian painter who was a leading pioneer of abstract art.
Russian abstract painter (1866–1944)

Examples

Kandinsky's paintings are known for their vivid colors and abstract forms.
Many art students study Kandinsky when learning about the origins of modern art.
The museum hosted a special exhibition dedicated to Kandinsky's revolutionary work.

Etymology

From the Russian surname Kandinsky (Кандинский).
