Aleut

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːt / (AmE), / əˈluːt / (BrE)
the language of the Aleut people, spoken in the Aleutian Islands and parts of Alaska.
language of the Aleut people

Examples

She is studying the Aleut language as part of her research into indigenous cultures.
Very few people today are fluent speakers of Aleut.
A new effort aims to preserve Aleut by recording conversations with the elders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aleutian - of or relating to the Aleut people or their language

Etymology

From Russian 'Aleut', itself from native Alaskan languages referring to the indigenous people.

2. NOUN

/ əˈljuːt / (AmE), / ˈæliːuːt / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous people inhabiting the Aleutian Islands and southwestern Alaska
member of Aleutian indigenous people

Examples

Many Aleut still practice traditional fishing and hunting methods handed down through generations.
The Aleut were among the first indigenous peoples encountered by Russian explorers in Alaska.
Belonging to both Russian and American history, the Aleut community maintains a rich cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Inuit - a member of the indigenous people of northern Canada, Alaska, and Greenland
  • Synonym: Eskimo - a member of an indigenous people inhabiting the Arctic regions of Greenland, Canada, and Alaska; considered offensive by some

Etymology

From Russian Aleut, from the Aleut autonym Unangax̂.

3. NOUN

/ əˈluːt / (AmE), / əˈluːt / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people inhabiting the Aleutian Islands and southwestern Alaska, who speak an Eskimo-Aleut language
member of indigenous group in Alaska and Aleutian Islands

Examples

The Aleut have a rich tradition of sea mammal hunting and fishing.
Many Aleut now live both in traditional villages and in larger Alaskan cities.
Anthropologists study the culture and language of the Aleut people.

Synset Examples

the Aleut and the Eskimo are related culturally and linguistically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aleutian - relating to the Aleut people or the Aleutian Islands
  • Synonym: Eskimo - member of indigenous Arctic peoples related to Aleuts culturally and linguistically

Etymology

From Russian 'Aleuty', name for the indigenous people of the Aleutian Islands.
