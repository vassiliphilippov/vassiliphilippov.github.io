Cygnus columbianus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs kəˌlʌm.biˈɑː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs kəˌlʌm.bɪˈɑː.nəs / (BrE)
a species of swan that nests in tundra regions across both the New World and the Old World; commonly known as the tundra swan
species of swan (tundra swan)

Examples

The Cygnus columbianus migrates thousands of miles each year between its breeding and wintering grounds.
Birdwatchers in Alaska observed several Cygnus columbianus feeding by the lake.
Unlike the trumpeter swan, the Cygnus columbianus prefers to nest in Arctic tundra regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tundra swan - common name for Cygnus columbianus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cygnus' meaning 'swan' and 'columbianus' referring to Columbia (historically applied to North America).
