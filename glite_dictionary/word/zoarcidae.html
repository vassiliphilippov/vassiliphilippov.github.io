Zoarcidae

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˈɑrsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / zəʊˈɑːsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, marine fish known as eelpouts, found in cold waters worldwide.
family of marine fish (eelpouts)

Examples

The Zoarcidae are commonly found in deep, cold waters worldwide.
Marine biologists have identified over 300 species within the Zoarcidae family.
Unlike other similar fish, Zoarcidae have elongated bodies and lack pelvic fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelpout - a type of elongated marine fish in the Zoarcidae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Zoarcidae', based on the type genus 'Zoarces'.
