prizefighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪzˌfaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpraɪzˌfaɪtə / (BrE)
A boxer who competes professionally, especially for cash prizes.
professional boxer

Examples

The young prizefighter trained relentlessly for his first championship bout.
Many prizefighters come from humble beginnings and rise to fame through hard work.
The retired prizefighter now coaches aspiring boxers in his hometown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxer - a person who fights as a sport, especially professionally

Etymology

From 'prize' + 'fighter', originally referring to one who fought in contests for monetary prizes, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.
