Grampus griseus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmpəs ɡrɪˈsiːəs / (AmE), / ˈɡræmpəs ɡrɪˈsiːəs / (BrE)
A species of slaty-grey, blunt-nosed dolphin found commonly in northern seas, also known as Risso's dolphin.
a species of blunt-nosed dolphin

Examples

Marine biologists sighted a pod of Grampus griseus during the research expedition.
The characteristic scars on the body help researchers identify Grampus griseus individuals.
Grampus griseus is often seen off the coasts of temperate and tropical waters worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Risso's dolphin - Common name for Grampus griseus, a blunt-nosed dolphin

Etymology

From Latin grampus (a type of sea creature) and griseus (gray).
