Kiwi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːwi / (AmE), / ˈkiːwi / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of New Zealand
a person from New Zealand

Examples

Many Kiwis are proud of their country's beautiful landscapes.
She made friends with a Kiwi while traveling through Europe.
The Kiwis cheered loudly during the rugby match.

Etymology

From the Māori word *kiwi*, originally referring to the native flightless bird of New Zealand, later used to refer to people from New Zealand.
