kiwi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːwi / (AmE), / ˈkiːwi / (BrE)
a nocturnal flightless bird native to New Zealand, with a long slender bill and stout legs, and the only surviving member of the order Apterygiformes.
flightless bird of New Zealand

Examples

The kiwi is recognized as a national symbol of New Zealand.
Because of its unique appearance, the kiwi is easily distinguished from other birds.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the remaining wild kiwi populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - extinct large flightless bird of New Zealand

Etymology

From Maori *kiwi*, imitative of the bird's call.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːwi / (AmE), / ˈkiːwi / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to China and cultivated in New Zealand and elsewhere for its edible, fuzzy-skinned fruit with green flesh.
climbing vine grown for its fruit

Examples

Farmers in New Zealand grow kiwi vines for their tangy and nutritious fruit.
The kiwi plant thrives in temperate climates with plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil.
Botanists studied the rapid growth rate of the kiwi compared to other climbing vines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwifruit - the edible fruit of the kiwi vine

Etymology

From Maori, the name of a native flightless bird, transferred to the fruit and plant.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkiːwi / (AmE), / ˈkiːwi / (BrE)
A small, oval, fuzzy brown fruit with bright green flesh and tiny black seeds, known for its sweet and slightly tart flavor.
fuzzy brown fruit with green flesh

Examples

She sliced a fresh kiwi to add to the fruit salad.
The smoothie tasted delicious with banana and kiwi blended together.
I love the tangy flavor of a ripe kiwi for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese gooseberry - another name for the kiwi fruit

Etymology

From Maori *kīwi*, originally referring to the kiwi bird, adopted in English for the fruit in the early 20th century.
