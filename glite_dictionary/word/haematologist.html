haematologist

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmætəˈlɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / hiːˌmætəˈlɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A doctor who specializes in the study and treatment of diseases of the blood and blood-forming organs.
specialist doctor for blood diseases
medical british

Examples

After being referred by her general practitioner, she scheduled an appointment with the haematologist.
The haematologist explained the blood test results in detail to the patient.
Research by a haematologist led to new treatments for rare blood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematologist - doctor specializing in blood diseases, American spelling

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -logist (one who studies).
