Gossypium

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːˈsɪpiəm / (AmE), / ɡɒˈsɪpiəm / (BrE)
a genus of plants that includes the cotton species, comprising herbs, shrubs, and small trees cultivated for their fiber
genus of cotton plants

Examples

The genus Gossypium contains several species cultivated for cotton fiber production.
Researchers are studying different Gossypium species to improve disease resistance in cotton.
Egyptian cotton is derived from a specific Gossypium variety known for its long fibers.

Etymology

From Latin *gossypium* (cotton), from Arabic *quṭn* (cotton).
