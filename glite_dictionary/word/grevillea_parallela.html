Grevillea parallela

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvɪliə pəˈrælələ / (AmE), / ɡrɛˈvɪliə pəˈralɪlə / (BrE)
A small slender tree native to northern Australia, with usually entire grey-green pendulous leaves and white or cream-colored flowers.
Australian tree with slender, pendulous leaves

Examples

The Grevillea parallela is often planted as an ornamental tree in Australian gardens.
Botanists studied the unique leaf structure of the Grevillea parallela during their field research.
The creamy blossoms of the Grevillea parallela attract native birds and insects throughout the dry season.

Etymology

Genus name from Charles Francis Greville (1749–1809), British collector and patron of botany; species name from Latin 'parallela' meaning 'parallel'.
