extrados

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.strəˌdɑs / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strəˌdɒs / (BrE)
the outer curve or surface of an arch, forming its exterior boundary
outer curve of an arch

Examples

The engineer measured the extrados of the stone arch to ensure its uniform curve.
Decorative carvings adorned the extrados of the cathedral's main entrance arch.
The thickness of the extrados can affect both the strength and appearance of an arch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intrados - the interior curve or surface of an arch

Etymology

From French 'extrados', from Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'dorsum' (back).
