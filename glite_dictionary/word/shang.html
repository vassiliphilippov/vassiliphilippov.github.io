Shang

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑːŋ / (AmE), / ʃæŋ / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from about the 18th to the 12th century BC, known for its advances in bronze casting, writing, and urban development.
ancient Chinese dynasty (18th–12th centuries BC)

Examples

Many archaeologists consider the Shang dynasty the earliest confirmed ruling power in Chinese history.
Artifacts from the Shang period reveal a sophisticated use of bronze.
The Shang established a feudal society and developed early forms of Chinese writing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Zhou - the Chinese dynasty that followed and replaced the Shang dynasty
  • Degree Lower: Xia - the Chinese dynasty preceding the Shang, considered semi-legendary

Etymology

From Mandarin 商 (Shāng), the Chinese name for the dynasty.
