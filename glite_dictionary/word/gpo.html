GPO

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː piː ˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː piː ˈəʊ / (BrE)
an agency of the United States legislative branch that provides printing and binding services for Congress and federal government departments.
U.S. government printing agency
american

Examples

The GPO is responsible for publishing the official documents of Congress and federal agencies.
Visiting the GPO in Washington, D.C., was part of our civics class trip.
The report was ordered through the GPO by several government offices.

Etymology

Acronym of Government Publishing Office (originally Government Printing Office).
