dispiritedness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɪrɪtɪdnɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪərɪtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
a state of feeling discouraged or lacking in enthusiasm and hope
a feeling of being discouraged or downhearted

Examples

There was a sense of dispiritedness among the team after their defeat.
His long period of unemployment contributed to his feelings of dispiritedness.
Despite her dispiritedness, she tried to remain hopeful about the future.

Synset Examples

he felt responsible for her lowness of spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dejection - a feeling of sadness and lack of hope
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - a feeling of being happy and optimistic

Etymology

Formed from 'dispirited' + '-ness', 'dispirited' from Latin 'spiritus' (spirit).
