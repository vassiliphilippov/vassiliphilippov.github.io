tweedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtwidi / (AmE), / ˈtwiːdi / (BrE)
(of textiles) having a rough, woolly surface similar to tweed fabric.
having a rough, tweed-like surface

Examples

She wore a tweedy jacket that complemented the autumn chill perfectly.
The armchair was covered in a tweedy fabric that felt sturdy and rustic.
His favorite scarf was thick and tweedy, ideal for winter days.

Synset Examples

a sweater knitted of nubbly homespun yarns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough - having an uneven or irregular surface; not smooth

Etymology

From 'tweed', the name of a rough woolen fabric, plus the suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtwidi / (AmE), / ˈtwiːdi / (BrE)
Having characteristics associated with country gentry, such as being informal, traditional, clannish, or outdoorsy, often suggesting a fondness for tweed clothing.
resembling country gentry; outdoorsy or traditional

Examples

She always wore tweedy jackets when she visited her cottage in the countryside.
The university had a distinctly tweedy atmosphere, filled with old traditions and autumn walks.
His tweedy style made him stand out among the urban crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country - belonging to or characteristic of the countryside or rural life

Etymology

From 'tweed', a rough woolen fabric, plus the suffix '-y'.

Synset POS: s
