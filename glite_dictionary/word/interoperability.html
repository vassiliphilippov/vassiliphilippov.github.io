interoperability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnt̬ɚˌɑː.pɚ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˌɒp.ər.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The ability of different systems, devices, or software to work together and exchange information effectively.
ability of systems to work together

Examples

The new software was designed for maximum interoperability with different operating systems.
Achieving interoperability between healthcare systems can improve patient care and data accuracy.
The engineers tested the interoperability of their devices with a wide range of networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatibility - ability of systems or components to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

From 'inter-' (between) and 'operate', with the suffix '-bility' forming 'interoperable' and then 'interoperability'.
