lactate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæk.teɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of lactic acid, commonly found in biological processes such as muscle metabolism
salt or ester of lactic acid
medical

Examples

High levels of lactate in the blood can indicate a problem with oxygen delivery to tissues.
Athletes sometimes measure lactate to monitor muscle fatigue during intense exercise.
The laboratory test confirmed an elevated lactate concentration in the sample.

Etymology

From Latin 'lac' (milk), via French 'lactique' (lactic).

2. VERB

/ ˈlæk.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæk.teɪt / (BrE)
(of a woman or female mammal) to secrete milk and feed it to a young offspring; to give suck to.
to secrete milk and breastfeed
medical

Examples

Some mammals continue to lactate for several months after giving birth.
After childbirth, she was able to lactate and nurse her newborn daughter.
Certain medications can affect a woman's ability to lactate successfully.

Synset Examples

The wetnurse suckled the infant
You cannot nurse your baby in public in some places

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse - to feed a baby at the breast
  • Antonym: to wean - to accustom (a baby or young animal) to food other than its mother's milk

Etymology

From Latin 'lactatus', past participle of 'lactare' (to suckle), from 'lac, lactis' (milk).
