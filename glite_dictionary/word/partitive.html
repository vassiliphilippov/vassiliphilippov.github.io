3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪtɪv / (BrE)
a word or phrase that denotes a part or portion of a whole, such as 'some' or 'less'
word indicating part of a whole

Examples

The word 'some' is a common partitive in English grammar.
In the phrase 'some of the cake,' 'some' functions as a partitive.
Certain languages mark the partitive case with specific endings or particles.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'partitivus', from Latin 'partitus' (divided, shared).
