Clusia rosea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkluːʒə roʊˈziːə / (AmE), / ˈkluːziə rəʊˈziːə / (BrE)
A tropical American evergreen tree with thick, leathery leaves and solitary white or rose-colored flowers, often known as the autograph tree.
tropical American tree with leathery leaves and white or rose flowers

Examples

The Clusia rosea thrives in tropical climates and is often used as an ornamental tree.
Gardeners value the thick, shiny leaves of the Clusia rosea for landscaping projects.
The Clusia rosea produces stunning rose-colored flowers during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autograph tree - Common name for Clusia rosea

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Clusia' after botanist Carolus Clusius; 'rosea' meaning 'rose-colored'.
