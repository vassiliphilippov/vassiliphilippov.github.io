whiteface

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌfeɪs / (BrE)
A hardy English breed of cattle, distinguished by its white face, extensively raised in the United States and other countries for beef.
breed of beef cattle with a white face

Examples

The farmer added ten whiteface calves to his herd this spring.
Whiteface cattle are valued for their adaptability to different climates.
Many ranches across the Midwest specialize in breeding whiteface for their high-quality beef.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt.feɪs / (BrE)
a type of clown whose face is covered with white make-up, typically characterized by an elegant costume and precise movements
clown with white make-up

Examples

The whiteface greeted the children with a gentle smile and a graceful bow.
She trained for years to perfect her whiteface clown character for the circus.
Among the clowns, the whiteface is often seen as the most traditional and formal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: auguste - a comedic clown with exaggerated features and colorful make-up
  • Synonym: clown - a comic entertainer in a circus who wears funny clothes and make-up
