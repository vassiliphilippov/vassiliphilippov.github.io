logomach

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈɡɑːmæk / (AmE), / ləʊˈɡɒmak / (BrE)
A person who engages in or is fond of verbal controversies or disputes about words or their meanings.
a person who disputes over words

Examples

The debate was quickly derailed by a notorious logomach who quibbled over every definition.
Rather than focus on the real issue, he revealed himself as a logomach by arguing semantics.
Scholars sometimes worry that being a logomach detracts from academic progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logomachist - a person who disputes about words
  • Synonym: logomachy - a dispute about words

Etymology

From Greek *logomakhos* (*logos* ‘word’ + *makhē* ‘battle’).
