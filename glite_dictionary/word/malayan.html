Malayan

1. ADJ

/ məˈleɪ.ən / (AmE), / məˈleɪ.ən / (BrE)
relating to Malaysia, its people, language, or the Malay Peninsula and western Malay Archipelago.
relating to Malaysia or Malay Peninsula

Examples

The Malayan language has a rich history and unique grammar.
Several Malayan species are found only on the Malay Peninsula.
He studied the cultural traditions of the Malayan people.

Synset Examples

Malay peoples
Malayan syllable structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - of or relating to the Malay people or language

Etymology

From 'Malay' + '-an', referring to the Malay people or region.

2. NOUN

/ məˈleɪən / (AmE), / məˈleɪən / (BrE)
a member of a people native to the northern Malay Peninsula, Malaysia, and parts of the western Malay Archipelago
a person of the Malayan ethnic group

Examples

The Malayan community celebrates many traditional festivals unique to their culture.
Historically, Malayans have played a significant role in the region's trade and politics.
She spoke passionately about her experiences as a Malayan growing up in Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - a member of a people living mainly in Malaysia, Indonesia, and Brunei

Etymology

From Malay 'Melayu', referring to the Malay people, with English suffix '-an'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09732092-n
Synset POS: n
