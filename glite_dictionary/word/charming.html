charming

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑːrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
very pleasant or attractive, giving delight or enjoyment to others
pleasant and attractive

Examples

Everyone found the charming little inn to be a perfect place for a weekend getaway.
Her charming manner quickly won over the entire group at the meeting.
The village has many charming cottages with beautiful gardens.

Synset Examples

endowed with charming manners
a charming little cottage
a charming personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delightful - very pleasant, giving great pleasure
  • Antonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or agreeable

Etymology

From French 'charmer', from Latin 'carmen' (song, verse, incantation).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to, possessing, or using supernatural powers, especially of magic or enchantment.
relating to supernatural or magical powers
archaic

Examples

The witch uttered charming incantations to cast her spell.
Legends speak of charming rituals that protect people from harm.
The artifact was said to possess charming powers beyond mortal understanding.

Synset Examples

charming incantations
magic signs that protect against adverse influence
a magical spell
'tis now the very witching time of night
wizard wands
wizardly powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magical - having or using magic
  • Synonym: enchanted - under the influence of magic

Etymology

From Old French *charmer* (to enchant), from Latin *carmen* (song, incantation).
