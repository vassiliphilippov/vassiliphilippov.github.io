Sisymbrium officinale

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌsɪmbriəm ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəli / (AmE), / sɪˌsɪmbriəm ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəli / (BrE)
A species of wild mustard, known as hedge mustard, with stiff branches and pale yellow flowers, formerly used in herbal medicine.
wild mustard species; hedge mustard

Examples

Sisymbrium officinale is commonly found growing along roadsides and disturbed sites.
Herbalists once used Sisymbrium officinale to treat throat irritations and coughs.
The pale yellow flowers of Sisymbrium officinale bloom from spring to early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedge mustard - Common name for Sisymbrium officinale, a type of wild mustard

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Sisymbrium' and species 'officinale' (medicinal, from 'officina' meaning workshop/apothecary).
