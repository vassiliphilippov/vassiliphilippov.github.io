to behove

1. VERB

/ bɪˈhuv / (AmE), / bɪˈhəʊv / (BrE)
to be appropriate or necessary for someone; to be a duty or responsibility
be appropriate or necessary; be one's duty
british

Examples

It behoves all citizens to act with integrity and honesty.
It behoves the committee to consider all possible outcomes before making a decision.
If you want to succeed, it behoves you to work hard and stay focused.

Synset Examples

It behooves us to reflect on this matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to behoove - to be appropriate or necessary for someone; American spelling and usage

Etymology

From Old English *behoofian* (to be of use, befit), related to *behoof* (benefit, advantage).
