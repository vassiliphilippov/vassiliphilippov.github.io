to welch

1. VERB

/ wɛltʃ / (AmE), / wɛltʃ / (BrE)
to fail to pay a gambling debt or to cheat someone by refusing to honor an agreement, especially in gambling
fail to pay a gambling debt; cheat on a deal
offensive

Examples

He tried to welch on his bet after losing the poker game.
If you welch on your debts, people won't trust you again.
She accused him of attempting to welch out of the agreement at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renege - to go back on a promise or agreement
  • Antonym: to pay up - to pay a debt, especially after delay or reluctance

Etymology

Probably a disparaging reference to Welsh people, from the noun 'Welsh'; origin regarded as offensive by some.
