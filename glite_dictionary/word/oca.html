oca

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.kə / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.kə / (BrE)
A South American plant of the wood sorrel family, cultivated for its small edible tubers, which are a staple food in parts of the Andes.
South American tuberous plant

Examples

The farmers harvested oca along with potatoes and other root vegetables in the Andes.
She roasted oca to bring out its sweet, tangy flavor for dinner.
In Peru, oca is a traditional crop valued for its resilience and nutritional content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - a starchy tuberous root from certain tropical plants, also used as food

Etymology

From Quechua *uqhu* (a tuber plant); adopted into Spanish as 'oca'.
