cruciform

1. ADJ

/ ˈkruːsɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfɔːm / (BrE)
shaped like a cross or having a cross-like form.
shaped like a cross

Examples

The old church was built in a cruciform layout typical of medieval cathedrals.
Under the microscope, the crystals appeared cruciform in structure.
The designer opted for a cruciform table to maximize seating in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-shaped - having the shape of a cross

Etymology

From Latin 'crux' (cross) + '-form' (form, shape), meaning 'cross-shaped'.
