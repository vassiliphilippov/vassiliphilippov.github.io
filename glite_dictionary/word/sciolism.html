sciolism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A superficial show of knowledge; pretentious or shallow learning that lacks true understanding.
superficial or pretentious knowledge

Examples

His sciolism was quickly exposed when he was asked detailed questions about the subject.
The debate was filled with sciolism rather than genuine insight or expertise.
Critics accused the author of sciolism because her arguments were based on shallow understanding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: erudition - deep, extensive learning and knowledge

Etymology

From Latin sciolus ('having knowledge, pretending to knowledge'), diminutive of scius ('knowing').
