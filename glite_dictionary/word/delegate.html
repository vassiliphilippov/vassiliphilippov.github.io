delegate

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪɡət / (BrE)
a person who is chosen or elected to represent a group, especially at a meeting or conference
person chosen to represent others

Examples

Each country sent a delegate to the international climate conference.
The delegate from Ohio addressed the assembly with new proposals.
As a union delegate, she attended all major negotiations on behalf of her coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representative - a person chosen or appointed to act or speak for others

Etymology

From Latin delegatus, past participle of delegare (to send as representative).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛl.əˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.ɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to give a particular job, duty, or responsibility to someone else, especially so that they do work on your behalf
assign a task to someone

Examples

The manager decided to delegate several key tasks to her assistant.
Learning to delegate effectively is an important skill for any leader.
He prefers to delegate responsibility rather than handle every detail himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to allocate a task or responsibility to someone
  • Antonym: to relinquish - to give up or hand over a responsibility or possession

Etymology

From Latin 'delegare' (to send from, commission), from 'de-' (away) + 'legare' (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02396945-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to give responsibility, authority, or power to another person, typically so that they can act on one's behalf
to give power or responsibility to another

Examples

The manager decided to delegate some of her tasks to junior staff members.
Effective leaders know when to delegate authority to others.
He struggled at first to delegate important decisions, preferring to do everything himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a task or responsibility
  • Antonym: to relinquish - to voluntarily give up; to let go of power or a responsibility

Etymology

From Latin *delegare* (to send as a deputy), from *de-* (away) + *legare* (to send, appoint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02400537-v
Synset POS: v
