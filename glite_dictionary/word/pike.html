pike

1. NOUN

/ paɪk / (AmE), / paɪk / (BrE)
a large northern freshwater fish (Esox lucius) with a long snout and sharp teeth, valued for its lean flesh
large northern freshwater fish

Examples

Anglers often travel to remote lakes hoping to catch a giant pike.
The pike is known for its aggressive hunting style and sharp teeth.
He prepared a delicious dish using freshly caught pike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel - a smaller type of freshwater fish closely related to the pike

Etymology

From Middle English *pike*, perhaps related to the Old French *pic* (pointed), referring to the fish’s pointed snout.

2. NOUN

/ paɪk / (AmE), / paɪk / (BrE)
a long spear with a sharp point, formerly used as a weapon by infantry soldiers; also, a sharp point as on a spear
a long spear with a sharp point

Examples

The knight carried a heavy pike into battle.
The soldier kept the pike pointed toward his opponent as he advanced.
Archaeologists uncovered an ancient pike with a rusted iron point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spear - a weapon with a long shaft and a sharp tip
  • Synonym: lance - a long weapon used for thrusting, especially by mounted warriors

Etymology

From Old French *pique* (sharp point, weapon), ultimately from Latin *picus* (woodpecker, pick).

3. NOUN

/ paɪk / (AmE), / paɪk / (BrE)
a large, long-bodied freshwater fish with a pointed snout, found chiefly in northern waters and known for being a fierce predator
long-bodied freshwater predatory fish

Examples

The pike lurked beneath the surface, waiting to ambush its prey.
Anglers visit this lake hoping to catch a giant pike during fishing season.
The northern pike is known for its powerful jaws and sharp teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel - a small pike-like freshwater fish of North America
  • Synonym: muskellunge - a large North American pike species

Etymology

From Middle English pike, possibly related to Old French pique (pike, point) and Medieval Latin *pica* (pike), referring to its pointed snout.

5. NOUN

/ paɪk / (AmE), / paɪk / (BrE)
A long spear-like weapon with a pointed metal head, mounted on a long wooden shaft, used especially by infantry in medieval and early modern warfare.
long pole weapon with a pointed head

Examples

During the battle, the soldiers advanced, each wielding a pike that towered over his head.
The museum displayed a medieval pike alongside other historical weapons.
He learned how to lower his pike to defend against approaching cavalry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spear - a weapon with a pointed tip, used for thrusting or throwing
  • Degree Higher: bayonet - a blade that may be fixed to the muzzle of a rifle and used as a weapon

Etymology

From Middle English 'pik', from Old French 'pic' (point, spike), ultimately from Latin 'picus' (woodpecker; literally, a pic, pike).
