temporalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛmpəɹəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of placing or defining something in terms of time or temporal relations.
placing or defining in time

Examples

The novel's structure relies heavily on the temporalization of its narrative, presenting events out of chronological order.
Philosophers often debate the temporalization of abstract concepts such as being and existence.
The temporalization of history in the documentary allowed viewers to better grasp the sequence of events.

Synset Examples

In contrast to the topos common in Stifter scholarship that his descriptions bring about a standstill of motion and time, I argue that Stifter decidedly works on the temporalization of description.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporal - relating to time

Etymology

From temporal + -ization, patterned after words formed with '-ize' and '-ation'.
