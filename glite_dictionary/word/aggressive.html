aggressive

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
behaving in a hostile or forceful way, showing a readiness to attack or confront; eager to fight or argue
ready to attack or confront; hostile

Examples

The leader's aggressive statements increased tensions between the two countries.
His aggressive behavior made everyone in the meeting uncomfortable.
Their aggressive strategies often lead to conflict with competitors.

Synset Examples

aggressive acts against another country
a belligerent tone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belligerent - hostile and aggressive
  • Antonym: peaceful - not aggressive; inclined to avoid conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'aggressivus' (fit for attack), from 'aggredi' (to attack).

2. ADJ

/ əˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a disease or medical condition) tending to spread quickly and become more severe.
tending to spread quickly (esp. disease)

Examples

The doctors were concerned because the tumor was unusually aggressive and growing rapidly.
She was diagnosed with an aggressive form of breast cancer that required immediate treatment.
Early detection is crucial when dealing with an aggressive illness.

Synset Examples

an aggressive tumor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benign - (of a disease) not harmful in effect

Etymology

From Latin 'aggress-', past participle of 'aggredi' (to attack).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01358991-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
showing determination and energetic pursuit of one's goals, often in a forceful or proactive way
determined and energetic in pursuing goals

Examples

The company hired an aggressive manager to lead their expansion into new markets.
Her aggressive approach to negotiations resulted in a beneficial deal.
The athlete is known for his aggressive style of play on the court.

Synset Examples

an aggressive businessman
an aggressive basketball player
he was aggressive and imperious; positive in his convictions
aggressive drivers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertive - confidently self-assured and direct in expressing views
  • Antonym: passive - accepting or allowing what happens without active response

Etymology

From Latin aggredi (to attack),
