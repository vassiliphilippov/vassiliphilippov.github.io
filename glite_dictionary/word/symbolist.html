symbolist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.bə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.bə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person skilled in interpreting or representing symbols, especially in literature or art
person skilled in interpreting symbols

Examples

As a renowned symbolist, she offered new insights into the painter's use of color.
The novel was analyzed by a symbolist for its multiple layers of meaning.
His work as a symbolist influenced generations of literary critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreter - someone who explains the meaning of something
  • Antonym: literalist - someone who interprets words or works in their most basic sense without symbolism

Etymology

From French symboliste, from symbole (symbol).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlɪst / (BrE)
a member of a late 19th-century artistic and literary movement that sought to express ideas and emotions indirectly through the use of symbolic imagery and suggestion rather than explicit statement
member of a 19th-century artistic movement using symbols

Examples

The poet was a leading symbolist who conveyed emotion through intricate images.
Many symbolists rejected realism in favor of abstract representations.
As a symbolist, she preferred suggestion over direct statement in her writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities, especially as an art movement

Etymology

From French symboliste, from symbole (symbol) + -iste (-ist), popularized in late 19th-century France.
