presciently

1. ADV

/ ˈprɛʃ.ənt.li / (AmE), / ˈprɛs.i.ənt.li / (BrE)
In a manner that shows foresight or the ability to predict what will happen.
with foresight; anticipating future events

Examples

She presciently invested in technology stocks before the market boom.
The author presciently described social changes that would only occur decades later.
He presciently warned of the dangers long before they became apparent to others.

Synset Examples

more presciently than they superiors, these workers grasped the economic situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetically - in a way that predicts what will happen in the future
  • Antonym: retrospectively - in a way that looks back on or deals with past events

Etymology

From Latin praescientem, present participle of praescire (to know beforehand).
