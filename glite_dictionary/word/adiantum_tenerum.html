Adiantum tenerum

1. NOUN

/ ˌædiˈæntəm ˈtɛnərəm / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈæntəm ˈtɛnərəm / (BrE)
A tropical American fern with broad leaflets, commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.
species of tropical American fern

Examples

The delicate leaves of Adiantum tenerum make it a favorite among indoor plant enthusiasts.
Gardeners often choose Adiantum tenerum for shaded areas due to its lush, broad fronds.
Botanists documented the spread of Adiantum tenerum in tropical gardens worldwide.

Etymology

From Latin: Adiantum (genus name) + tenerum (Latin for 'delicate' or 'tender').
