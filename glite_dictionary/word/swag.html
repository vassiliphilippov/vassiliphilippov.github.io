swag

1. NOUN

/ swæɡ / (AmE), / swæɡ / (BrE)
a bundle containing the personal belongings of a swagman, especially as carried while traveling
bundle of a swagman's belongings

Examples

The old swagman tossed his swag over his shoulder and set off down the dusty track.
She carried her swag as she trekked through the Australian outback, just like the pioneers of old.
He rolled out his swag under the gum tree, ready to sleep beneath the stars.

Etymology

Probably from older English dialect 'swag' meaning 'a bulging bag' or 'heavy load'.

2. VERB

/ swæɡ / (AmE), / swæɡ / (BrE)
to sway or move heavily and unsteadily, especially from side to side or under a heavy load
sway or move unsteadily

Examples

The old bridge began to swag under the weight of the heavy truck.
He watched the lantern swag back and forth as the ship rocked.
With every step, the overloaded donkey seemed to swag side to side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sway - to move slowly or rhythmically backward and forward or from side to side

Etymology

Probably imitative of the motion; attested from the 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ swæɡ / (AmE), / swæɡ / (BrE)
(informal) Valuable goods, especially those taken by thieves; loot or stolen property.
loot or stolen goods
informal

Examples

The thieves made off with a bag full of swag after breaking into the jewelry store.
Investigators recovered the stolen swag hidden in an abandoned warehouse.
Pirates often boasted about the swag they plundered from merchant ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - goods taken illegally, especially by force or theft
  • Synonym: booty - valuable stolen goods, especially those taken in war
  • Antonym: property - something owned; possessions

Etymology

Probably from older Scandinavian, related to Norwegian 'svagga' (to sway or stagger), originally referring to a bulging bag.

4. VERB

/ swæɡ / (AmE), / swæɡ / (BrE)
to droop, sink, or sag from or as if from weight or pressure; to lose tautness and hang down unevenly
to droop, sink, or sag (verb)

Examples

The old wooden beams began to swag under the weight of the roof.
Her clothesline swagged in the middle from the heavy blankets.
Over time, the tent canvas may swag if it is not tightened properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sag - to sink, droop, or hang downward
  • Synonym: to droop - to bend or hang downward limply

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse *sveggja* (to swing, sway).
