questionnaire

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɛstʃəˈnɛr / (AmE), / ˌkwɛstʃəˈneə / (BrE)
a written or printed form with a list of questions, used for gathering information from people for surveys or research
form for collecting information via questions

Examples

Every participant was asked to fill out a questionnaire about their shopping habits.
The company distributed a detailed questionnaire to gather feedback from its employees.
Researchers analyzed the results of the questionnaire to understand customer satisfaction levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - an investigation of the opinions or experience of a group of people, based on a series of questions

Etymology

From French 'questionnaire', from 'question' (question) + '-naire', used to denote a form or document.
