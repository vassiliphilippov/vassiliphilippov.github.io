barrack

1. VERB

/ ˈbæɹək / (AmE), / ˈbarək / (BrE)
to house or lodge (people, especially soldiers) in barracks
house in barracks

Examples

The army decided to barrack the new recruits near the base for easier training.
During the winter, workers were barracked in temporary shelters close to the construction site.
Hundreds of soldiers were barracked together in the old fort.

Etymology

From French 'baraque' (hut, temporary shelter), originally from Italian 'baracca'.

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbær.ək / (AmE), / ˈbær.ək / (BrE)
a building or group of buildings used to house soldiers or other military personnel
building for housing soldiers

Examples

The new recruits were assigned to the barrack near the training field.
After lights out, the barrack was silent except for the occasional snore.
The base commander inspected each barrack to ensure cleanliness and order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracks - a building or set of buildings for lodging soldiers (plural form also used as singular)

Etymology

From French 'baraque', from Catalan 'barraca' meaning 'hut, cabin'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02797802-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbær.ək / (AmE), / ˈbær.ək / (BrE)
(chiefly British and Australian) to cheer or shout encouragement to someone, especially at a sporting event.
cheer or encourage (especially at sports)
british

Examples

The fans began to barrack loudly for their team during the final minutes of the match.
She loved to barrack for her brother at his football games.
The players were motivated when the crowd continued barracking from the stands.

Synset Examples

The crowd cheered the demonstrating strikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout in encouragement or praise

Etymology

Originated in British and Australian English; possibly from 19th-century sporting slang.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00860463-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈbærək / (AmE), / ˈbærək / (BrE)
to shout at someone or laugh at them with contempt, especially in order to interrupt or mock them
to jeer or mock loudly

Examples

Some fans began to barrack the referee after the controversial call.
It is unfair to barrack a performer when they are trying their best.
The protesters were barracking the politician as he tried to speak.

Synset Examples

The crowd jeered at the speaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - to make rude and mocking remarks, typically in a loud voice
  • Synonym: boo - to show disapproval or contempt by shouting 'boo', especially at a public event
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00851872-v
Synset POS: v
