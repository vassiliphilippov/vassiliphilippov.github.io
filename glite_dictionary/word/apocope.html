apocope

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːkəpi / (AmE), / əˈpɒkəpi / (BrE)
the omission of the final sound or sounds of a word
omission of a word's final sound(s)

Examples

The word ‘pud’ is formed from ‘pudding’ by apocope.
In poetry, apocope can be used to create a more rhythmic line.
Regional dialects often feature apocope in commonly spoken words.

Synset Examples

the British get ‘pud’ from ‘pudding’ by apocope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncope - omission of sounds or letters from within a word

Etymology

From Late Latin apocope (cutting off), from Greek apokopē (a cutting off).
