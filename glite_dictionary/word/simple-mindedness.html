simple-mindedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a lack of complexity, subtlety, or sophistication in thought or understanding
lack of subtlety or sophistication

Examples

His simple-mindedness made him vulnerable to scams and dishonest people.
Despite her simple-mindedness, she always approached new experiences with enthusiasm.
The children’s simple-mindedness was evident in their innocent questions.

Synset Examples

they took advantage of her simplicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • Antonym: sophistication - the quality of being complex or having worldly experience
