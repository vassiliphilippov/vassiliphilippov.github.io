earnest

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝnɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪst / (BrE)
showing sincere and intense conviction; serious and determined, not joking or playful
serious and sincere in attitude

Examples

She gave an earnest speech about the importance of honesty.
He looked at me with earnest concern, hoping I understood his point.
Their earnest efforts to improve the neighborhood inspired others to join.

Synset Examples

both sides were deeply in earnest, even passionate
an entirely sincere and cruel tyrant
a film with a solemn social message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serious - not joking or playful; thoughtful
  • Synonym: solemn - marked by deep sincerity or seriousness
  • Antonym: frivolous - not having any serious purpose or value; carefree

Etymology

From Middle English 'ernest', from Old French 'erainest', from Latin 'serius' (serious).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜː.nɪst / (BrE)
a sum of money or valuable item given by one party to another as a pledge to bind a contract or agreement
deposit or pledge for a contract
legal

Examples

As an earnest, she gave him five gold coins to secure the land deal.
The earnest was returned when the contract was fulfilled.
They handed over a small earnest to demonstrate their commitment to the agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deposit - a sum of money paid as an initial part of a larger payment or as security for an agreement
  • Synonym: down payment - an initial payment made when something is bought on credit

Etymology

From Old French *aernest*, from Latin *arra* (pledge, earnest money), by way of Old High German *ernust*.

Synset POS: n
