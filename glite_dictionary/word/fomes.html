Fomes

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.miːz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.miːz / (BrE)
a genus of bracket fungi forming hard, woody, perennial shelf-like fruiting bodies, including species that cause heartrot in trees
genus of woody shelf fungi

Examples

The fungus Fomes plays a significant role in the decomposition of dead wood in forests.
Several trees in the park were found to be infected with Fomes, leading to heartrot.
Species of Fomes are commonly seen as hard shelves on the trunks of old trees.

Etymology

From Latin fōmes (tinder), used in scientific taxonomy for fungi producing tinder-like structures.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊmiːz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊmiːz / (BrE)
A genus of bracket fungi that form hard, woody, perennial shelflike fruiting bodies, some of which cause heartrot in trees.
genus of bracket fungi

Examples

The species belonging to Fomes are known for their woody, perennial fruiting bodies.
Several trees in the park were infected by Fomes, leading to extensive heartrot damage.
Mycology students studied Fomes under the microscope to understand its spore structure.

Etymology

From Latin fomes (tinder), used in scientific taxonomy.
