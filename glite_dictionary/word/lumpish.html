lumpish

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌmpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlʌmpɪʃ / (BrE)
slow-witted, dull, or mentally sluggish; lacking alertness or liveliness of mind.
mentally sluggish or dull
archaic

Examples

After a sleepless night, he felt particularly lumpish during the morning meeting.
His lumpish responses made it clear he wasn't following the discussion.
The novel's hero is initially lumpish, but gradually becomes more perceptive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - not intellectually sharp or quick-witted
  • Antonym: quick-witted - mentally alert and sharp

Etymology

From 'lump' + '-ish', originally meaning heavy or clumsy, later extended to mental dullness.
