Anura

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʊrə / (AmE), / əˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of amphibians that comprises frogs, toads, and related species, typically characterized by the absence of a tail in adults.
order of amphibians: frogs and toads
medical

Examples

The class conducted a field study to observe different species within the Anura order.
Biologists debate the evolutionary origins of Anura and their adaptation to diverse habitats.
All modern frogs and toads are classified in the order Anura by scientists.

Etymology

From New Latin Anura, from Greek an- (without) + oura (tail).
