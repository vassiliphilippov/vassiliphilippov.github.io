Melchite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛl.kaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.kʌɪt / (BrE)
A member of an Eastern Christian church in Egypt or Syria who adheres to the Orthodox faith as defined by the Council of Chalcedon in 451, maintaining communion with the Byzantine traditions.
member of an Eastern Orthodox Christian church

Examples

Many Melchite Christians still preserve ancient liturgical traditions.
The Melchite community played a significant role in Middle Eastern religious history.
He was proud of his heritage as a Melchite in modern Syria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orthodox - conforming to the faith and practices of the Eastern Orthodox Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Melchita, from Syriac malkāyā (royal, imperial), from malkā (king), referring to loyalty to the Byzantine emperor.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛl.kaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.kʌɪt / (BrE)
a member of the Eastern Christian churches, especially those who follow the Byzantine rite and are associated with the patriarchates of Alexandria, Antioch, or Jerusalem, either as Orthodox or as part of the Eastern Catholic (Uniate) churches.
member of certain Eastern Christian churches

Examples

Several Melchite communities still preserve ancient traditions in their worship and liturgy.
The Melchite patriarch played a crucial role during the early Christian councils.
My grandfather belonged to a Melchite parish in the old quarter of the city.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Melchita, from Syriac malkāyā (royalist), from malkā (king).
