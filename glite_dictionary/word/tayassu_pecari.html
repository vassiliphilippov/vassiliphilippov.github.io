Tayassu pecari

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈæs.uː pɛˈkɑː.ri / (AmE), / taɪˈæs.uː pɛˈkɑː.ri / (BrE)
a species of large, blackish peccary with whitish cheeks, native to Central and South America
a large blackish peccary species

Examples

A herd of Tayassu pecari moved noisily through the rainforest in search of food.
Unlike other peccaries, Tayassu pecari are often seen in very large groups.
The conservation status of Tayassu pecari is threatened due to deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: peccary - pig-like mammal native to the Americas

Etymology

From genus name 'Tayassu' (from Tupi language) and Latin species epithet 'pecari', from Cariban languages.
