Thunnus alalunga

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌnəs ˌæl.əˈlʌŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈθʌn.əs ˌæl.əˈlʌŋ.ɡə / (BrE)
A species of tuna, also known as albacore, notable for its long pectoral fins and widely fished for canned tuna.
species of tuna (albacore)

Examples

The majority of canned tuna is produced from Thunnus alalunga due to its mild flavor.
Fishermen off the coast of California caught several large Thunnus alalunga this season.
The long pectoral fins are a distinguishing feature of Thunnus alalunga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albacore - another name for Thunnus alalunga, a species of tuna

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Thunnus (tuna) + species name alalunga (long-winged), referring to long pectoral fins.
