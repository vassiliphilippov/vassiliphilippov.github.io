rascality

1. NOUN

/ ˌræˈskæləti / (AmE), / ˌrɑːˈskæləti / (BrE)
the quality or behavior of being mischievous or dishonest, like a rascal
mischievous or dishonest behavior

Examples

His rascality was well known in the village, where he often played tricks on his neighbors.
The rascality of the corrupt officials shocked even the most cynical citizens.
Children sometimes display a harmless rascality that amuses adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or dishonest person

Etymology

From Italian *rascalità*, from *rascal*, referring to a mischievous person.

2. NOUN

/ ræˈskæləti / (AmE), / ræˈskalɪti / (BrE)
reckless or mischievous behavior that causes discomfort or annoyance to others
reckless or mischievous behavior

Examples

His constant rascality kept the neighbors annoyed and suspicious.
The puppy's harmless rascality made everyone in the family laugh.
She was known for her childhood rascality and playful tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Degree Higher: villainy - wicked or criminal behavior

Etymology

From 'rascal' (a mischievous or dishonest person) + '-ity' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌræsˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌræsˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the characteristic or behavior of engaging in mischievous or dishonest pranks or tricks
mischievous or dishonest behavior

Examples

The boy's rascality often got him into trouble at school.
His charming rascality made him popular among his friends despite his pranks.
The children's rascality was evident in the way they played tricks on their teacher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking, often in children
  • Antonym: honesty - quality of being honest; truthfulness, opposite of deceit or trickery

Etymology

From rascal + -ity, influenced by Latin -itas (forming nouns of quality).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
