begonia

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Begonia, grown for its ornamental leaves and bright, colorful flowers
ornamental plant with showy flowers

Examples

She placed a begonia on the windowsill to brighten up the room.
The garden was filled with colorful begonias and ferns.
My grandmother is proud of her begonia collection, especially when they bloom in spring.

Etymology

From modern Latin Begonia, named after Michel Bégon (1638–1710), French patron of botany.
