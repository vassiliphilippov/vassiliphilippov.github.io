to daunt

1. VERB

/ dɔnt / (AmE), / dɔːnt / (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid, less confident, or discouraged
make someone feel afraid or discouraged

Examples

The challenge did not daunt her; she faced it bravely.
He was daunted by the enormity of the task ahead.
Don't let the possibility of failure daunt you from trying.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or make timid
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give someone support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From Old French 'danter', from Latin 'domitare' (to tame, subdue).
