alate

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of seeds, leaves, or insects) having winglike extensions or appendages.
having winglike extensions

Examples

The alate seeds of the maple tree help them travel farther on the wind.
Many ants develop an alate form during their reproductive phase.
The botanist described the alate leaves that aided in dispersal.

Synset Examples

alate leaves
alate seeds of a maple tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged - having wings or structures shaped like wings

Etymology

From Latin alatus, past participle of alare (to furnish with wings), from ala (wing).
