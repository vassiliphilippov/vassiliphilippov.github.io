Gael

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪl / (AmE), / ɡeɪl / (BrE)
a member of a group of Gaelic-speaking people native to Ireland, Scotland, or the Isle of Man
a Gaelic-speaking person from Ireland, Scotland, or the Isle of Man

Examples

The Gael have preserved their language and traditions for centuries.
She studied the poetry and songs of the ancient Gael.
Many Scottish Gael emigrated to North America in the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celt - a member of an ancient Indo-European people

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'Gàidheal' and Irish 'Gael', meaning 'Gaelic-speaking person'.
