flyaway

1. ADJ

/ ˈflaɪ.əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪ.əˌweɪ / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) guided by whim and fancy; capricious or flighty.
capricious; whimsical or flighty

Examples

Her flyaway nature made it hard for her to commit to a single plan.
The flyaway decisions of youth often lead to unexpected adventures.
People described him as flyaway due to his ever-changing whims.

Synset Examples

flighty young girls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flighty - capricious; lacking stability; whimsical
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02128403-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈflaɪəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪəˌweɪ / (BrE)
(of hair or clothing) loose, light, and tending to fly or move about instead of lying flat or staying in place.
loose or untidy (hair, clothing)

Examples

A gust of wind blew her flyaway hair across her face.
He wore a flyaway scarf that fluttered in the breeze.
After running, her flyaway curls escaped from her ponytail.

Synset Examples

her flyaway hair
a flyaway coat

Etymology

Formed from 'fly' + 'away', indicating a tendency to move or lift easily.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
