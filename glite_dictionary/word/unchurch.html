to unchurch

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to exclude someone from membership in a church or religious community
expel from a church

Examples

After years of disagreement with the church's doctrine, they decided to unchurch him.
The bishop threatened to unchurch anyone who broke the sacred rules.
She felt lost after being unchurched by her congregation.

Synset Examples

The gay priest was excommunicated when he married his partner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excommunicate - formally exclude from participation in the sacraments and services of a Christian church

Etymology

from prefix un- (meaning 'to remove') + church (from Old English 'cirice')
