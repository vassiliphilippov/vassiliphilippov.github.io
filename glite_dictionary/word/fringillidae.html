Fringillidae

1. NOUN

/ frɪnˈdʒɪlɪˌdi / (AmE), / frɪnˈdʒɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of small passerine birds including finches, goldfinches, bullfinches, and related species, typically noted for their conical bills and seed-eating habits
family of small seed-eating birds (finches, etc.)

Examples

Many of the songbirds observed in the park belong to the Fringillidae family.
The bright plumage of some Fringillidae species makes them popular among birdwatchers.
Scientists have identified over two hundred species within the Fringillidae family worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird with a short, stout bill

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Fringilla (a genus name), from Latin 'fringilla' meaning 'a finch'.
