neo-Luddite

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːoʊ ˈlʌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ ˈlʌdaɪt / (BrE)
A person who opposes or rejects modern technology, often on social or ethical grounds.
person opposed to modern technology

Examples

Some neo-Luddites argue that rapid technological advances harm society and the environment.
After reading about privacy concerns, he started to feel like a neo-Luddite.
The neo-Luddite movement has gained traction among those worried about artificial intelligence.

Synset Examples

He identifies as a neo-Luddite, rejecting modern technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luddite - person opposed to technological change (original movement)

Etymology

From 'neo-' (new) + 'Luddite,' referring to followers of Ned Ludd, who opposed industrial machinery in early 19th-century England.
