acariosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.əˈraɪ.oʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæk.əˈraɪ.əʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
infestation of the skin or body by itch mites, especially causing irritation or disease
infestation with itch mites
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with acariosis after noticing persistent itching and skin lesions.
Early detection of acariosis can help prevent the spread of mites among livestock.
Human cases of acariosis are rare but can cause significant discomfort and skin irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scabies - skin disease caused by infestation with itch mites

Etymology

From Greek akarēs (mite) + -osis (condition).
