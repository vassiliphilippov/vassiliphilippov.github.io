appraising

1. ADJ

/ əˈpreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
carefully judging or evaluating the value, quality, or significance of something
carefully judging or evaluating

Examples

She gave him an appraising look before deciding whether to trust him.
The manager's appraising glance lingered on the candidate's résumé.
He spoke with an appraising tone, weighing each word carefully.

Synset Examples

looked him over with an appraising eye
the literary judge uses many evaluative terms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaluative - relating to the assessment or appraisal of something

Etymology

From the verb 'appraise', which comes from Old French 'apreisier' (to value, estimate), from Late Latin 'appretiare' (to value).
