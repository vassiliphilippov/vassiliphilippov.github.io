Occidental

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːk.sɪˈdɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɒk.sɪˈdɛn.tl̩ / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of the Occident (the Western world, especially Europe and the Americas)
native or inhabitant of the Western world

Examples

During his travels, he observed the customs of both Occidentals and Orientals.
As an Occidental, she sometimes struggled to adapt to life in East Asia.
The debate focused on the differing views of Occidentals and Easterners regarding tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Westerner - a person from the Western world
  • Antonym: Oriental - a native or inhabitant of the Orient (the Eastern world)

Etymology

From Latin occidentalis (western), from occidens (west, sunset).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑksəˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
a constructed international auxiliary language created by Edgar de Wahl in 1922, intended to facilitate communication between speakers of different native languages
planned international auxiliary language

Examples

The linguist studied Occidental as part of his research on planned languages.
Before learning Occidental, she had already mastered Esperanto and Interlingua.
Interest in Occidental grew during the early 20th century among language enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esperanto - a constructed international auxiliary language created by L. L. Zamenhof
  • Synonym: Interlingua - a planned international auxiliary language developed by the International Auxiliary Language Association

Etymology

From Latin occidentalis (western), likely chosen to evoke Western linguistic roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06910250-n
Synset POS: n
