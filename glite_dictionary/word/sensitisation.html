sensitisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychology) the process by which a person or organism becomes increasingly responsive or sensitive to certain events or stimuli, especially emotional ones.
increased psychological sensitivity to stimuli

Examples

Repeated exposure to violence in media can lead to sensitisation to aggressive behavior.
After the traumatic event, she experienced sensitisation to loud noises and crowds.
Therapists monitor patients for sensitisation during exposure therapy for anxiety disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desensitisation - the process of becoming less sensitive to a stimulus or event

Etymology

From Latin *sensus* (feeling) + English suffix *-isation* (process of making or becoming).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of becoming sensitive to a substance, such as an allergen or antigen, resulting in an increased response on subsequent exposure
increased sensitivity to a substance
medical british

Examples

After repeated exposure to pollen, the patient's sensitisation led to severe allergic reactions.
The sensitisation of the immune system made subsequent contact with the substance dangerous.
Scientists studied the sensitisation process in order to develop better allergy treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitization - the process or state of becoming sensitive to a substance (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'sensitise' (to make sensitive) + '-ation', modelled on French sensibilisation.

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.sə.tɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making an organism sensitive to a substance, especially by repeated injections, often in preparation for an immune response
making an organism sensitive by injections
medical

Examples

The sensitisation of laboratory animals is necessary before testing certain vaccines.
Repeated sensitisation can lead to allergic reactions in some individuals.
Medical researchers monitored the effects of sensitisation over several weeks of treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desensitisation - the process of making less sensitive or nonreactive, often to an allergen

Etymology

From Latin 'sensibilis' (that can be perceived) + English suffix '-ation'.
