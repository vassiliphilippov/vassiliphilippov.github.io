Adirondacks

1. PROPN

/ ˌædɪˈrɒndæks / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈrɒndaks / (BrE)
a mountain range in northeastern New York State, known for its natural beauty and popular as a resort area
mountain range in New York State, USA

Examples

We spent our summer vacation hiking in the Adirondacks and exploring the beautiful lakes.
The Adirondacks attract thousands of tourists every year seeking outdoor adventure.
She photographed the autumn foliage in the Adirondacks for a travel magazine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Appalachians - a larger mountain range in eastern North America

Etymology

From a Mohawk word meaning 'tree eaters', referring to Algonquian-speaking peoples; adapted into English in the 19th century.
