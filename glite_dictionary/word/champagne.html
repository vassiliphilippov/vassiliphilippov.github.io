Champagne

1. PROPN

/ ʃæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ʃamˈpeɪn / (BrE)
a historical region in northeastern France, famous for producing sparkling wine of the same name.
region of northeastern France

Examples

Many tourists visit Champagne to explore its vineyards and learn about wine production.
The rolling hills of Champagne are dotted with picturesque villages and ancient churches.
Historic battles in Champagne have left a rich cultural legacy in the region.

Etymology

From French Champagne, from Latin Campania (level country), related to 'champ' (field).
