champagne

1. NOUN

/ ʃæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ʃamˈpeɪn / (BrE)
a white sparkling wine produced in the Champagne region of France, or any similar sparkling wine
sparkling white wine from Champagne

Examples

They celebrated the New Year by opening a bottle of champagne.
French law protects the right to call sparkling wine champagne if it comes from the region.
The waiter poured champagne into tall, elegant glasses for the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling wine - a wine with bubbles of carbon dioxide

Etymology

From French 'Champagne', the name of the region in northeastern France where the wine originated.
