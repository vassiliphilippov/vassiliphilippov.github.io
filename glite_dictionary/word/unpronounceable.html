unpronounceable

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.prəˈnaʊns.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌn.prəˈnaʊns.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a word or sound) impossible or very difficult to pronounce correctly.
impossible or difficult to pronounce

Examples

The chemical's name was so unpronounceable that no one could remember it.
Many students found the foreign word almost unpronounceable at first.
Her last name is unpronounceable to most people outside her country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pronounceable - able to be pronounced

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'pronounceable', from 'pronounce' plus '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌn.prəˈnaʊns.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌn.prəˈnaʊns.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a word or sound) very difficult or impossible to pronounce correctly.
difficult or impossible to pronounce

Examples

The chef tried to remember the unpronounceable name of the new dish.
Her last name is almost unpronounceable for English speakers.
They laughed at the unpronounceable sequence of consonants in the spelling bee word.

Synset Examples

an unpronounceable foreign word
unutterable consonant clusters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pronounceable - easy to pronounce correctly

Etymology

From un- (not) + pronounceable (able to be pronounced).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503910-s
Synset POS: s
