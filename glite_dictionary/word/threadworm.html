threadworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛdˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdwɜːm / (BrE)
a small parasitic nematode worm, especially one that infests the intestines and rectum of humans, particularly children
parasitic intestinal worm, often in children
medical

Examples

Doctors often prescribe medication to treat threadworm infections in young children.
Poor hygiene can help threadworm spread quickly within households.
Schools sometimes send home information on preventing threadworm infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinworm - another term for the same common intestinal parasite (especially in the US)

Etymology

Formed from 'thread' and 'worm' referring to the worm’s slender, threadlike appearance.
