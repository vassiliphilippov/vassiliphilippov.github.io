Anomalops

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑməlˌɑps / (AmE), / əˈnɒməlɒps / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Anomalopidae, known as flashlight fishes, distinguished by light-producing organs beneath their eyes.
genus of flashlight fishes

Examples

The genus Anomalops is famous for its bioluminescent organs below the eyes.
Researchers observed several species belonging to Anomalops in the coastal waters.
The unique adaptation of Anomalops allows it to attract prey in the dark ocean depths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anomalopidae - the family of flashlight fishes

Etymology

From Greek 'anomalos' (uneven, irregular) + 'ops' (eye).
