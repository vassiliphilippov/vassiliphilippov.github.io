anomalops

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑː.məˌlɒps / (AmE), / əˈnɒm.əˌlɒps / (BrE)
a genus of fish found in warm western Pacific and Puerto Rican waters, noted for having a luminous organ beneath each eye
genus of fish with luminous organs under eyes

Examples

The anomalops uses its bioluminescent organs to find prey in the dark waters at night.
Marine biologists study the unique light-emitting features of the anomalops.
Snorkelers in the Pacific sometimes catch a glimpse of the mysterious anomalops after sunset.

Etymology

From Greek anōmalos (irregular, anomalous) + -ops (eye).
