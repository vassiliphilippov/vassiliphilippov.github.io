receiver

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
the tennis player who receives the serve from the server
player receiving the serve in tennis

Examples

The receiver stood ready to return the powerful serve.
As the receiver, she anticipated the direction of the ball carefully.
The umpire checked to see if the receiver was prepared before starting play.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: server - the player who serves the ball in tennis

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
a person who receives something, such as a letter, message, or gift
person who receives something

Examples

The receiver of the award expressed her gratitude during the ceremony.
Please write the name of the receiver clearly on the envelope.
The receiver signed for the package upon delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recipient - a person who receives something
  • Antonym: giver - a person who gives something

Etymology

From Old French recevoir, from Latin recipere (to receive).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
(in American football) a player whose main role is to catch forward passes thrown by the quarterback.
football player who catches passes

Examples

The quarterback threw a perfect pass to his favorite receiver for a touchdown.
As a talented receiver, she broke several league records during the season.
Each receiver must know the team's passing routes by heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide receiver - a type of receiver positioned near the sidelines, specializing in catching long passes

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
A device that receives radio or television signals and reproduces sound or images.
device receiving radio or TV signals

Examples

The old radio receiver in the attic still works perfectly.
She adjusted the television receiver to get a clearer picture.
Their home theater system has a powerful audio receiver for surround sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transmitter - device that sends radio or TV signals

Etymology

From Middle English 'receyvere', from Anglo-Norman 'receivre', from Latin 'recipere' (to receive).

5. NOUN

/ rɪˈsivər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
a device, especially part of a telephone or radio, that converts electrical signals into audible sound.
device converting signals to sound

Examples

He put the receiver to his ear and listened carefully for any noise.
The old radio's receiver was replaced for better sound quality.
She dropped the receiver when she heard the shocking news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earpiece - small part of a device placed in or over the ear to receive sound
  • Antonym: microphone - a device that converts sound into electrical signals

Etymology

From Old French 'receveor', from Latin 'recipere' (to receive).

6. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
(law) A person, usually appointed by a court, who manages, preserves, or liquidates assets for the benefit of affected parties during legal proceedings.
court-appointed manager of assets
legal

Examples

The court appointed a receiver to oversee the company's assets during bankruptcy proceedings.
As receiver, she was responsible for liquidating the property and distributing the funds to creditors.
The judge ruled that a receiver should be appointed to preserve the estate's value while the dispute is resolved.

Etymology

From Old French 'receveor', from Latin 'recipere' (to receive).
