Ezechiel

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈziːkiəl / (AmE), / ɪˈziːkiəl / (BrE)
a Hebrew prophet of the 6th century BC, exiled to Babylon in 587 BC, whose prophecies are recorded in the Book of Ezekiel in the Bible
6th-century BC Hebrew prophet

Examples

Many of Ezechiel's visions are described in detail in the Old Testament.
The Book of Ezechiel contains vivid prophecies and symbolic imagery.
Ezechiel was among the exiles taken to Babylon by Nebuchadnezzar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ezekiel - alternative spelling of the same prophet

Etymology

From Hebrew Yeḥezqel, meaning 'God will strengthen'.

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈziːkiɛl / (AmE), / ɪˈziːkiəl / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of Ezekiel, referring to the Old Testament book that contains the prophet Ezekiel's visions and prophecies regarding the downfall and restoration of Jerusalem and Judah
alternative spelling of Ezekiel (Old Testament book)

Examples

The Book of Ezechiel describes powerful visions seen by the prophet during the Babylonian exile.
In medieval manuscripts, the prophet Ezechiel is often depicted alongside other major figures of the Old Testament.
Some biblical translations use the spelling Ezechiel instead of the more common 'Ezekiel'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ezekiel - the Old Testament book of the prophet Ezekiel

Etymology

From Latin Ezechiel, from Greek Iezekiel, from Hebrew Yeḥezqē’l (God will strengthen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06450418-n
Synset POS: n
