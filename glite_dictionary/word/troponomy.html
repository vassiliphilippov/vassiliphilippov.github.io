troponomy

1. NOUN

/ troʊˈpɑːnəmi / (AmE), / trəˈpɒnəmi / (BrE)
the study or collection of the place names of a region or language considered as a whole
the collective place names of a region or language

Examples

Her research in troponomy reveals how early settlers chose names for local villages.
The troponomy of Scotland reflects centuries of cultural change in the region.
Linguists use troponomy to trace historical migration patterns and language evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toponymy - the study of place names, their origins, meanings, and use

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place) and '-onymy' (naming); related to 'toponymy'.
