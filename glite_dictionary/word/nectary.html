nectary

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛktəri / (AmE), / ˈnɛktəri / (BrE)
A glandular structure in a flower or plant that secretes nectar.
nectar-secreting plant gland

Examples

Bees are attracted to the flower's nectary for its sweet nectar.
The nectaries are usually found at the base of the petals.
Some plants have nectary glands outside their flowers to attract protective insects.

Etymology

From Latin 'nectarium', from 'nectar'.
