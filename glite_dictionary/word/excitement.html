excitement

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
an event or experience that causes strong feelings of enthusiasm, eagerness, or agitation
an event or thing that excites

Examples

The children could not contain their excitement about the upcoming trip to the zoo.
The city was filled with excitement during the festival.
For some people, roller coasters provide all the excitement they need.

Synset Examples

he looked forward to the excitements of the day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrill - a sudden feeling of excitement or pleasure
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of being bored or uninterested

Etymology

From excite + -ment, based on Latin 'excitare' (to arouse).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of eager enthusiasm and heightened emotional arousal.
A strong feeling of enthusiasm or arousal

Examples

Her voice was shaking with excitement as she told us the news.
The children's excitement grew as Christmas morning approached.
There was a sense of excitement in the air before the big game.

Synset Examples

his face was flushed with excitement and his hands trembled
he tried to calm those who were in a state of extreme inflammation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of strong emotion or excitement

Etymology

From excite + -ment, ultimately from Latin excitare (to rouse).
