beroe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛroʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈbɛrəʊ.i / (BrE)
A genus of delicately iridescent, thimble-shaped comb jellies found in marine environments.
a genus of thimble-shaped comb jellies

Examples

The beroe drifted gracefully through the water, its transparent body shimmering in the light.
Scientists collected several specimens of beroe during the deep-sea expedition.
A beroe feeds on other small ctenophores by engulfing them whole.

Etymology

From Greek Bēroē, a proper name, used in zoological nomenclature.
