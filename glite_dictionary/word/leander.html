Leander

1. PROPN

/ liˈændər / (AmE), / liˈændə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a youth who was the lover of Hero and drowned in the Hellespont while trying to visit her during a storm.
youth in Greek myth, lover of Hero

Examples

The story of Leander and Hero is a classic tale of tragic love from Greek mythology.
Many poets have written about Leander's nightly swims across the Hellespont to reach his beloved.
In the myth, Leander drowns during a violent storm, leaving Hero heartbroken.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λέανδρος (Leandros), meaning 'lion of a man'.
