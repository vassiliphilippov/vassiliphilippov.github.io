trellis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛlɪs / (BrE)
a framework of light wooden or metal bars, chiefly used as a support for climbing plants
framework for supporting climbing plants

Examples

The roses climbed up the trellis along the garden wall.
We installed a wooden trellis to support the growing grapes.
I painted the trellis green to match the rest of the backyard décor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lattice - an interlaced structure or pattern resembling a grid

Etymology

From Old French *treliz*, from Latin *trichila* (arbor for vines).

2. VERB

/ ˈtrɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛlɪs / (BrE)
to train (a plant, especially a vine) to grow on a trellis by tying or guiding its stems or branches
train (a plant) to grow on a trellis

Examples

The gardener carefully trellised the climbing roses along the wooden frame.
It takes skill and patience to trellis grapevines effectively in the vineyard.
They spent the afternoon trellising their new cucumber plants to encourage upward growth.

Etymology

From Old French *treliz*, from Latin *trilix* (three-threaded), referring originally to latticed work.
