occluded

1. ADJ

/ əˈkluːdɪd / (AmE), / əˈkluːdɪd / (BrE)
blocked or closed off, especially referring to a passage or opening such as a blood vessel.
blocked; closed off (medical, technical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon discovered an occluded artery during the procedure.
If the pipe is occluded, water cannot flow through it.
She had difficulty breathing due to an occluded nasal passage.

Synset Examples

an occluded artery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - obstructed or stopped up
  • Antonym: open - not closed or blocked; allowing passage

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere' (to shut up, close up).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkluːdɪd / (AmE), / əˈkluːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) taken into and retained inside another substance, especially in a physical or chemical process.
taken into and retained in another substance

Examples

Palladium can store large amounts of occluded hydrogen in its metal lattice.
The mineral sample contained occluded gases that affected its weight.
Researchers measured the release of occluded oxygen from the solid during heating.

Synset Examples

the sorbed oil mass
large volumes of occluded hydrogen in palladium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbed - (of a substance) taken into and retained by another substance, especially by soaking up liquids
  • Synonym: adsorbed - (of a substance) adhering as a thin film to the surface of another

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere' (to shut up, close up).
