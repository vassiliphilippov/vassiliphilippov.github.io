saucer-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔsər aɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsə aɪd / (BrE)
Having large, round, wide-open eyes, often suggesting surprise or amazement.
having large wide-open eyes

Examples

The child looked up at me with saucer-eyed astonishment.
She was saucer-eyed as she listened to the incredible story.
The cartoon character's saucer-eyed expression made everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide-eyed - having eyes opened wide, typically in surprise or innocence

Etymology

From 'saucer' (a small shallow dish), used metaphorically to describe the roundness of the eyes.
