doomsday

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːmz.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈduːmz.deɪ / (BrE)
(in some religions) the day at the end of the world when God judges all people based on their actions.
the final day of judgment

Examples

Many religious texts speak of doomsday as the time when the fate of every soul will be decided.
Some people fear that technological disasters could one day cause a real-life doomsday.
He spoke in ominous tones about the coming of doomsday and the end of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Judgment Day - the day of God's final judgment of humanity

Etymology

Old English dōmes dæg (day of judgment), from dōm (judgment) + dæg (day).

2. NOUN

/ ˈduːmz.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈduːmz.deɪ / (BrE)
(in some religious beliefs) the last day of the world's existence, when a final judgment will occur and the fate of every soul will be decided.
day of the final judgment

Examples

Many religious texts speak of doomsday as a time of final reckoning for all souls.
Some people believe that signs in the world today indicate that doomsday is near.
The preacher warned his congregation to live righteously in preparation for doomsday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment day - the day on which God will judge all people at the end of the world

Etymology

Old English dōmes dæg, literally 'day of judgment'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈduːmz.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈduːmz.deɪ / (BrE)
the final day of the world's existence or a time of catastrophic destruction or disaster
the end of the world; catastrophic disaster

Examples

Some believe that technological advances could trigger doomsday scenarios in the future.
The movie depicts a post-apocalyptic world after doomsday has occurred.
People have been predicting doomsday for centuries, but the world keeps going.

Synset Examples

everyone was aware of the approaching doom but was helpless to avoid it
that's unfortunate but it isn't the end of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment day - the time of final judgment by God according to some religions
  • Synonym: armageddon - the last and completely destructive battle; a final and catastrophic conflict

Etymology

From Old English dōmes dæg ('day of judgment').
