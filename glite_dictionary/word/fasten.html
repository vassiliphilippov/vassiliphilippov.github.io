to fasten

1. VERB

/ ˈfæsən / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsən / (BrE)
to make something tight or tighter; to secure firmly in place
make something tight or secure

Examples

Before climbing, make sure to fasten your harness tightly.
She fastened the lid to keep the container sealed.
Please fasten the seatbelt before the plane takes off.

Synset Examples

Tighten the wire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less tight or more relaxed
  • Synonym: secure - to fix or attach something firmly

Etymology

From Old English fæstnian (to make firm, secure), related to fæst (firm, fixed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00421085-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfæsn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsn̩ / (BrE)
to attach or join something firmly to another thing
attach or join securely

Examples

She used a safety pin to fasten the name tag to her shirt.
Please fasten your seatbelt before the car starts moving.
The painter fastened the canvas securely to the frame.

Synset Examples

They fastened various nicknames to each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attach - to join or fix something to something else
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate something that is attached

Etymology

From Middle English fastnen, from Old English fæstnian (to make firm), from fæst (firm, fast).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01292736-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfæsən / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsən / (BrE)
to attach or join something firmly so that it will not come loose, open, or move
to fix or attach securely

Examples

Please fasten your seatbelt before the plane takes off.
She fastened the strap securely around her suitcase.
He fastened the lid to prevent any spills during the trip.

Synset Examples

fasten the lock onto the door
she fixed her gaze on the man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attach - to join or fix one thing to another
  • Synonym: secure - to fix or fasten something so it cannot move
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less tight or not firmly attached

Etymology

From Old English fæstnian (make firm, establish), related to 'fast' (firm, fixed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01343121-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈfæsn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsn̩ / (BrE)
to become firmly fixed or secured in place
become firmly fixed

Examples

The collar of his coat fastens at the neck with a small button.
This old window fastens shut automatically when you close it.
Make sure the seat belt fastens properly before you drive.

Synset Examples

This dress fastens in the back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to make something firmly fixed or safe
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less tight or become less firmly fixed

Etymology

From Old English fæstan (to make firm, secure).
