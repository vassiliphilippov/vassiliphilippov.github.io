indiscrete

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈskrit / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskriːt / (BrE)
not separated or divisible into distinct parts; forming a single, undivided mass or entity.
not divided into parts

Examples

The minerals solidified into an indiscrete block, impossible to separate by hand.
Some ancient rocks consist of an indiscrete mixture of various minerals.
After years underground, the chemicals had fused into an indiscrete mass.

Synset Examples

layers that were fused into an indiscrete mass

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discrete - individually separate and distinct

Etymology

From Latin 'indiscretus' meaning not separated.
