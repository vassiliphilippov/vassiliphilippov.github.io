to decrypt

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkrɪpt / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɪpt / (BrE)
to convert coded or encrypted text into its original, readable form; to decode information so that it can be understood
convert coded text to readable form

Examples

It took the team several hours to decrypt the secret message.
Modern software can easily decrypt certain types of data if the password is known.
She helped the police decrypt information from the suspect’s computer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to encrypt - to convert information into code to prevent unauthorized access

Etymology

From de- (reversal) + crypt (from Greek kryptos, meaning hidden).
