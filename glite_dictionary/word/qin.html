Qin

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪn / (BrE)
the Chinese dynasty (221–206 BC) that established the first centralized imperial government in China and began construction of the Great Wall
early Chinese imperial dynasty

Examples

The Qin dynasty unified China for the first time in history.
Many ancient sections of the Great Wall were built during the Qin era.
Legalist ideas formed the basis of government under the Qin dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Han dynasty - successor dynasty to the Qin, consolidated the imperial structure
  • Degree Lower: Zhou dynasty - preceding dynasty to the Qin, ruled much of ancient China

Etymology

From Mandarin 秦 (Qín), the name of the dynasty.
