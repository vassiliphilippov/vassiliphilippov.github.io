sorrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊ / (BrE)
a feeling or event that causes deep distress or unhappiness; grief
something causing deep unhappiness

Examples

The loss of his childhood home was a great sorrow to him.
After hearing the tragic news, she was overcome with sorrow.
For many years, the memory of the accident remained his greatest sorrow.

Synset Examples

her death was a great grief to John

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Old English 'sorg' (grief, trouble); related to Old High German 'sorga', Old Norse 'sorg'.
