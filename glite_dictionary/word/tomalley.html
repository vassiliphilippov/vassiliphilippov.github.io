tomalley

1. NOUN

/ təˈmæli / (AmE), / təˈmæli / (BrE)
A soft, greenish substance found in the body cavity of a lobster, considered a delicacy and eaten as food.
edible green substance in a lobster

Examples

Some chefs use tomalley to add flavor to sauces for seafood dishes.
The tomalley is prized by many lobster lovers for its rich taste.
Although edible, you should avoid tomalley if the lobster's waters were polluted.

Etymology

From French 'tomalley', possibly from Mi'kmaq or another Algonquian language.
