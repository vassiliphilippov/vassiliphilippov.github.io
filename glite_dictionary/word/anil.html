anil

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪl / (AmE), / ˈænɪl / (BrE)
A blue dye obtained from certain plants, especially indigo, or prepared synthetically.
blue dye from plants or synthetic sources

Examples

The fabric was dyed with anil to achieve a rich, blue color.
Historically, anil was extracted from leaves of the indigo plant.
Many artists prefer natural anil over its synthetic counterpart for its unique hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo - a deep blue dye obtained from plants

Etymology

From Spanish 'añil', from Arabic 'an-nīl', from Persian 'nīl' (indigo), from Sanskrit 'nīla' (dark blue).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɪl / (AmE), / ˈænɪl / (BrE)
a shrub native to the West Indies and South America, cultivated as a source of indigo dye
shrub; source of indigo dye

Examples

The farmers planted anil to harvest indigo dye for the textile industry.
Early colonists in the Caribbean relied on anil as a valuable export crop.
Traditional methods for extracting dye from anil involve fermenting its leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo - deep blue dye obtained from certain plants, including anil

Etymology

From Spanish 'anil', from Arabic 'an-nīl', from Sanskrit 'nīla' (dark blue, indigo).
