farseeing

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrˌsiːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːˌsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
Able to see across a great distance; having exceptional vision for distant objects.
able to see far distances

Examples

The farseeing eagle soared above the mountains, spotting prey miles away.
With his farseeing abilities, the lookout warned the crew long before danger arrived.
Scientists have studied animals with farseeing vision to improve surveillance technology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: near-sighted - unable to see distant objects clearly; myopic

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrˌsiɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːˌsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
having the ability to foresee or plan prudently for the future; showing long-term vision or foresight.
able to plan wisely for the future

Examples

Her farseeing approach ensured the company's success for decades to come.
The council praised the mayor's farseeing economic policies.
Only a truly farseeing leader could have anticipated the current challenges.

Synset Examples

large goals that required farsighted policies
took a long view of the geopolitical issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsighted - having or showing foresight; able to anticipate the future
  • Antonym: shortsighted - lacking foresight or consideration for the future

Etymology

From 'far' + 'seeing', meaning seeing far ahead; modeled on the literal sense of ability to see into the distance but used figuratively from the 18th century.
