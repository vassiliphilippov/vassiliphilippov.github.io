glottochronological

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡlɑːtoʊˌkrɑːnəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒtəʊˌkrɒnəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of the evolution of languages from a common source, especially by comparing rates of vocabulary change
relating to language evolution studies

Examples

The linguistics department recently published a glottochronological analysis of several ancient languages.
Many researchers question the accuracy of glottochronological methods in dating language divergence.
Her dissertation focused on the glottochronological development of Indo-European languages.

Synset Examples

glottochronological studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glottochronology - the study of the time relations of languages

Etymology

From glottochronology, from Greek 'glotta' (tongue, language) + 'chronos' (time) + '-logy' (study of) + '-ical'.
