chairmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛr.mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃeə.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or term of being a chairman; leadership of a committee, board, or meeting as a chairman
the role of a chairman

Examples

Her chairmanship of the board was marked by significant growth and innovation.
The company thrived during his ten-year chairmanship.
She was praised for her effective and fair chairmanship of the finance committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairman - the person leading a meeting, committee, or organization

Etymology

From 'chairman' + '-ship', denoting the state or condition of being a chairman.
