kindling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɪndlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of starting a fire
the act of starting a fire

Examples

The kindling of the campfire took only a few minutes with dry twigs.
He watched the kindling of the torch at the ceremony.
The rapid kindling of the flames alarmed the firefighters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition - the act of setting something on fire

Etymology

From Middle English 'kindlen' (to kindle, set on fire), from Old Norse 'kynda'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɪndlɪŋ / (BrE)
Small sticks or other materials that catch fire easily and are used to start a fire.
material used to start a fire

Examples

They gathered some kindling and larger logs to build a campfire.
Before lighting the fireplace, she arranged kindling and paper beneath the logs.
Dry kindling is essential for starting a fire quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinder - material, like dry grass or paper, used to start a fire easily

Etymology

From Middle English 'kindelyn', related to 'kindle' (to set alight), from Old Norse 'kyndill' (torch).
