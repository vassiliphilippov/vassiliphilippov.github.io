repetition

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpɪˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of doing or saying something again; the process of repeating.
the act of repeating

Examples

Her repetition of the speech made a strong impression on the audience.
Too much repetition can make your writing less engaging.
The training involves the constant repetition of the same movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to do or say something again

Etymology

From Latin repetitio (a repeating), from repetere (to repeat).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpɪˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
The repeated use of the same word or phrase as a rhetorical device, often for emphasis or effect.
repeated use of a word or phrase for effect

Examples

The speaker used repetition to emphasize his main points throughout the presentation.
Poets often employ repetition to create a rhythmic effect and reinforce meaning.
The repetition of the phrase 'never again' made the audience remember its significance.

Etymology

From Latin repetitio (a repeating), from repetere (to repeat).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpɪˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
an instance or event of something being repeated
an event that is repeated

Examples

The repetition of last year's mistakes was disappointing to the entire team.
They found the repetition of the lecture tedious and unnecessary.
A repetition of such an error could have serious consequences.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
