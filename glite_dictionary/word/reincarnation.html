reincarnation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˌkɑːrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˌkɑːˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The belief or concept that a soul or spirit is reborn in a new body after death.
the rebirth of a soul in a new body

Examples

Many cultures hold a deep belief in reincarnation and the idea of life after death.
She wondered if her cat's unusual behavior was a sign of reincarnation.
The philosopher wrote extensively on the subject of reincarnation and the soul's journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebirth - the process of being born again into a new body or life

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'incarnare' (to make flesh) + '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˌkɑrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnkɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the embodiment of a person or soul in a new form, especially the belief that after death, one is reborn in another body or form
rebirth in a new body or form

Examples

Many religions teach the concept of reincarnation as part of the cycle of life and death.
She believed that her reincarnation was once a famous artist from the past.
The story explores the idea of reincarnation through characters who relive their past lives.

Synset Examples

his reincarnation as a lion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebirth - the process of being born again in a new body or form
  • Synonym: transmigration - the passage of a soul into another body after death

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'incarnatio' (making flesh), from 'incarnare' (to make flesh).

3. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnkɑrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnkɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(in Hinduism and Buddhism) The belief or doctrine that a person may be reborn in a new body, in one of several forms, based on the moral quality of previous actions.
belief in rebirth after death

Examples

Many Hindus believe in reincarnation and hope to be reborn into a better life.
The concept of reincarnation offers a different perspective on life and death.
Buddhist teachings discuss reincarnation and the cycle of existence known as samsara.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebirth - another term for being born again in a new body

Etymology

From Late Latin reincarnationem (re- + incarnatio, 'the process of being made flesh again').
