epicanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A vertical fold of skin on the upper eyelid covering the inner corner of the eye, common in people of certain ethnicities or medical conditions.
vertical fold of eyelid skin near the nose
medical

Examples

The presence of an epicanthus is often seen in infants and may disappear with age.
Doctors noted that the patient exhibited a prominent epicanthus in addition to other features of the syndrome.
Anthropologists have studied the occurrence of epicanthus across different populations.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'epi-' (upon) and 'kanthos' (corner of the eye).
