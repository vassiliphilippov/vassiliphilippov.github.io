flash-forward

1. NOUN

/ flæʃ ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / flæʃ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
A scene or event in a book, film, or play that interrupts the normal sequence of events to show what will happen in the future.
narrative jump to a future event

Examples

The film uses a flash-forward to reveal the main character's fate early on.
In the novel, a sudden flash-forward disrupts the chronological flow of the story.
The director cleverly inserted a flash-forward to hint at the climax.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flashback - a scene that takes the narrative back in time from the current point

Etymology

Modeled after 'flashback'; 'flash' (sudden) + 'forward' (toward the future).
