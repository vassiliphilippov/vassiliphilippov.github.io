Rajidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.dʒɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.dʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of cartilaginous fish known as skates, typically bottom-dwelling rays found in tropical and temperate seas.
a family of rays known as skates

Examples

The Rajidae family comprises numerous species of skates, found in oceans worldwide.
Marine biologists study Rajidae to understand the evolution of cartilaginous fishes.
Some members of the Rajidae family are known for their flattened bodies and long, slender tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skate - a type of cartilaginous fish in the family Rajidae
  • Synonym: ray - cartilaginous fish of the order Batoidea, related to Rajidae

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'raja', meaning 'ray', plus the zoological suffix '-idae' indicating a family.
