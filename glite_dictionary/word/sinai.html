Sinai

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪˌnaɪ, ˈsiːˌnaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.naɪ / (BrE)
a desert region on the Sinai Peninsula in northeastern Egypt, situated between the Mediterranean Sea and the Red Sea
desert region in northeastern Egypt

Examples

The Sinai is known for its dramatic mountain landscapes and vast stretches of desert.
After crossing the Sinai, the expedition reached the Red Sea.
Ancient trade routes once traversed the Sinai connecting Africa and Asia.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Sīnay* (Mount Sinai), of uncertain origin.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪ.naɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.naɪ / (BrE)
a peninsula in northeastern Egypt, bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the north and the Red Sea to the south, known for its historical and biblical significance.
peninsula in northeastern Egypt

Examples

The Sinai Peninsula is a strategic land bridge between Africa and Asia.
Many tourists visit Sinai for its beautiful beaches and coral reefs.
Tensions have historically arisen in the Sinai region due to its geopolitical importance.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Sīnay', the name as found in the Bible.

3. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪ.naɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.naɪ / (BrE)
A mountain peak in the southern Sinai Peninsula, traditionally regarded as the site where Moses received the Ten Commandments.
mountain in the Sinai Peninsula, Egypt, with biblical significance

Examples

Many pilgrims travel to Sinai to visit the site where Moses is believed to have received the Ten Commandments.
The sunrise over Sinai is considered a breathtaking experience by climbers and tourists alike.
Archaeologists have long debated the exact location of biblical events on Sinai.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mount Sinai - the full name of Sinai, referring to the same mountain in Egypt

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Sinai', of uncertain origin; known from ancient texts as a sacred mountain.
