Ligustrum

1. PROPN

/ lɪˈɡʌstrəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs commonly known as privet, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.
genus of shrubs; privet

Examples

The ornamental shrub in their garden belongs to the Ligustrum genus.
Several species of Ligustrum are widely used to form privacy hedges.
Botanists recently reclassified a number of plants under the Ligustrum group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - a shrub of the Ligustrum genus, often used for hedges

Etymology

From Latin 'ligustrum' (privet), used by classical authors for this plant.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡʌstrəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees known as privet, native to the Old World, often used for hedges.
genus of shrubs: privet

Examples

The Ligustrum hedge in their garden needed pruning every spring.
Many species within the genus Ligustrum are cultivated as ornamental plants.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Ligustrum in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - a common hedge shrub (plant of the genus Ligustrum)

Etymology

From Latin *ligustrum*, meaning 'privet'.
