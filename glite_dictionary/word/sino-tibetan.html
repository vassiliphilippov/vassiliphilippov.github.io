Sino-Tibetan

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪnoʊ tɪˈbɛtən / (AmE), / ˌsaɪnəʊ tɪˈbɛtən / (BrE)
a family of tonal languages spoken in eastern Asia, including Chinese and Tibetan languages
language family including Chinese and Tibetan

Examples

Mandarin and Cantonese are both part of the Sino-Tibetan language family.
Many linguists study the structure of Sino-Tibetan languages to understand their similarities and differences.
The spread of Sino-Tibetan languages has greatly influenced the cultures of eastern Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Indo-European - a major family of languages spoken in Europe and Asia
  • Degree Lower: Tibeto-Burman - a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family

Etymology

From Latin 'Sinae' (China) and Tibet, indicating the geographical regions where these languages are spoken.
