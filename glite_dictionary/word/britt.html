britt

1. NOUN

/ brɪt / (AmE), / brɪt / (BrE)
Small marine crustaceans that form a major food source for right whales.
minute crustaceans eaten by right whales

Examples

Large schools of britt gather in cold Arctic waters, attracting many species of whales.
The diet of the right whale consists mainly of britt and other plankton.
Researchers observed that the reduction in britt could impact marine food chains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krill - small shrimp-like planktonic crustaceans eaten by whales

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Scots 'britt' (small fish).

2. NOUN

/ brɪt / (AmE), / brɪt / (BrE)
The young of a herring, sprat, or similar small fish.
young herring or sprat

Examples

Large schools of britt often attract bigger fish and seabirds.
Fishermen look for britt in the spring, when herring spawn.
Marine biologists study the growth rate of britt in estuaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fry - young, recently hatched fish
