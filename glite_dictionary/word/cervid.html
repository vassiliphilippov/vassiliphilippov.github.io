cervid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.vɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪd / (BrE)
a mammal of the deer family (Cervidae), typically distinguished by males having solid, deciduous antlers
member of the deer family

Examples

A moose is the largest cervid found in North America.
Scientists observed several different cervids in the wildlife reserve, including deer and elk.
The solid, branching antlers are a distinguishing feature of a male cervid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer - a hoofed grazing or browsing animal of the family Cervidae
  • Antonym: bovid - a member of the family Bovidae (e.g., cattle, goats, sheep)

Etymology

From Latin 'cervus' (deer) + '-id' (member of family).
