urban

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝː.bən / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bən / (BrE)
connected with, or characteristic of a city or a town, especially a densely populated area.
relating to cities or towns

Examples

The city's urban population has grown rapidly in the last decade.
She prefers the excitement of urban living to the quietness of rural areas.
The government launched a new plan to revitalize urban neighborhoods.

Synset Examples

urban sociology
urban development

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the town or city

Etymology

From Latin 'urbanus', meaning 'of a city, courteous', from 'urbs' (city).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɜːr.bən / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bən / (BrE)
relating to a city or town, or to city life; characteristic of cities and towns rather than rural areas.
relating to cities or city life

Examples

The city council is focused on improving urban transportation systems.
Many young professionals prefer urban living for easier access to work and amenities.
Urbanization has led to the rapid expansion of urban areas in many countries.

Synset Examples

urban property owners
urban affairs
urban manners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the town or city

Etymology

From Latin urbanus (of or belonging to a city), from urbs (city).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02059737-a
Synset POS: a
