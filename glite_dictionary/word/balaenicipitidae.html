Balaenicipitidae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnɪˌsɪpɪˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɪˌsɪpɪˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, African wading birds consisting of a single species, the shoebill (Balaeniceps rex).
family of birds: shoebill

Examples

The family Balaenicipitidae is represented today by only one living species, the shoebill.
Ornithologists debated whether to classify the Balaenicipitidae alongside herons or storks.
The unique features of Balaenicipitidae set them apart from other waterbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoebill - a large African bird with a shoe-shaped bill

Etymology

From Latin *balaena* (whale) + *caput* (head) + -idae (family suffix).
