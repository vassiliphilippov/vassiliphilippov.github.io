flicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪkər / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
a very brief or sudden flash of light
momentary flash of light

Examples

A sudden flicker of lightning illuminated the dark sky.
There was a flicker from the candle before it went out.
The television emitted a brief flicker before turning off completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of bright light

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, matching forms in Middle English (flikeren).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
A large North American woodpecker with a brownish plumage and spotted underparts, often feeding on the ground.
a large North American woodpecker

Examples

A northern flicker drilled into the tree trunk searching for insects.
We spotted a flicker in the park, recognizable by its spotted breast.
Several flickers were feeding among the fallen leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird with a strong bill for pecking at wood

Etymology

Probably imitative of its flickering call or flight.

3. NOUN

/ ˈflɪk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪk.ə / (BrE)
a quick, unsteady movement or light; the act of moving rapidly back and forth or wavering
quick, unsteady movement or light

Examples

There was a flicker of movement in the shadows behind the curtain.
She noticed a flicker of hesitation before he answered.
The candle gave off only a faint flicker in the dark room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flutter - a quick, light movement or fluttering

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, based on the movement it describes.

/ ˈflɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
to move or shine with quick, small, and unsteady movements or light
move or shine unsteadily or rapidly

Examples

A sudden breeze made the flames flicker for a moment before dying down.

(of light or a flame) to shine unsteadily; to flash on and off rapidly.
shine or flash unsteadily

Examples

A distant lightning storm made the sky flicker throughout the night.

6. VERB

/ ˈflɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
to shine or burn with a wavering or unsteady light
shine unsteadily

Examples

The candles flickered in the gentle evening breeze.
I watched the old streetlamp flicker before it finally went out.
Lightning made the tv screen flicker momentarily during the storm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glow - to produce a steady light without flame

Probably from Middle English 'flikeren', related to Dutch 'flikkeren' and German 'flackern' (to flicker).
