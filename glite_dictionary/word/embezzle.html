to embezzle

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɛzəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛzəl / (BrE)
to steal money or property that you have been trusted with, especially from your employer, by using it for your own purposes illegally
steal entrusted money or property

Examples

The company's treasurer was caught trying to embezzle funds from the employee pension plan.
She went to prison after attempting to embezzle thousands of dollars from her employer.
It is a criminal offense to embezzle government money entrusted to your care.

Synset Examples

The accountant embezzled thousands of dollars while working for the wealthy family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misappropriate - to dishonestly take something, especially money, for your own use
  • Synonym: steal - to take another person's property without permission or legal right
  • Antonym: return - to give back something that was taken

Etymology

From Old French 'embesiller' (to steal), probably from 'besillier' (to torment, destroy) plus en-.
