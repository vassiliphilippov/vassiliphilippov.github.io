housewifery

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌwɪfəri / (AmE), / ˈhaʊswɪfəri / (BrE)
the management of household affairs; the work or skills of a housewife
the work of a housewife

Examples

She excelled in housewifery, keeping her home tidy and her family well cared for.
The art of housewifery was passed down through generations in her family.
He wrote a book on housewifery in the nineteenth century, providing advice for young wives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housekeeping - the management of household affairs

Etymology

Formed from 'housewife' + the suffix '-ry', meaning practice or occupation.
