optionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpʃəˈnælɪti / (AmE), / ˌɒpʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being optional or involving a choice; the possibility of choosing among alternatives.
the quality of being optional

Examples

The optionality built into the agreement allows both parties to adjust the terms later.
Investors appreciate the optionality offered by convertible bonds.
The course structure gives students a degree of optionality in selecting their assignments.

Synset Examples

The usual optionality of these tasks entails the risk of students not fulfilling them in an effective way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - a choice; the act of choosing or possibility of choosing

Etymology

From 'optional' + '-ity', modeled on similar constructions in English.
