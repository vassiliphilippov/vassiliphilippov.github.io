prejudiced

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛdʒədɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɛdʒʊdɪst / (BrE)
(of a statement, action, or attitude) influenced by or resulting from personal prejudice rather than reason or fairness.
influenced by personal prejudice

Examples

Her prejudiced remarks revealed a lack of understanding about the issue.
It's difficult to have a fair discussion with someone holding such prejudiced views.
The review was clearly prejudiced and did not consider all the evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biased - having or showing prejudice
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; unbiased

Etymology

From 'prejudice' + '-ed'. 'Prejudice' from Latin 'praejudicium' (prior judgment).

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɛdʒədɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɛdʒʊdɪst / (BrE)
having or showing an unreasonable dislike or preference for someone or something, especially based on preconceived opinions rather than actual experience or reason.
having an unreasonable bias or preference

Examples

The committee made a decision that seemed prejudiced against younger applicants.
It's important for a judge to avoid being prejudiced in any case.
He denied being prejudiced, but his comments told a different story.

Synset Examples

a prejudiced judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biased - unfairly preferring one person or group over another
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just

Etymology

From the past participle of 'prejudice', from Old French *préjudice*, from Latin *praeiudicium* (previous judgment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00286014-a
Synset POS: a
