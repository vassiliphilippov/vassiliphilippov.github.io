creaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkriːkɪŋ / (BrE)
A prolonged, high-pitched squeaking or groaning sound, typically produced when something is being moved or strained.
squeaking or groaning sound

Examples

The creaking of the old wooden stairs made it impossible to sneak upstairs quietly.
In the silence of the night, the creaking of the door echoed through the house.
She was kept awake by the creaking of the branches outside her window.

Synset Examples

the creak of the floorboards gave him away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creak - a harsh, high-pitched sound like that made by a badly-oiled hinge
