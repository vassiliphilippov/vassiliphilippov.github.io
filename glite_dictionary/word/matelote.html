matelote

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætəˌloʊt / (AmE), / ˈmatələʊt / (BrE)
a highly seasoned French soup or stew made of freshwater fish, typically cooked with wine and stock
French fish stew or soup

Examples

He prepared a classic matelote with eel, perch, and a rich red wine sauce.
The chef recommended the matelote as a traditional specialty of the region.
After tasting the flavorful matelote, she was eager to try more rustic French cuisine.

Etymology

From French 'matelote', originally meaning 'sailor's stew'.
