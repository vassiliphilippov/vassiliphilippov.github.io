holonym

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊləˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈhɒlənɪm / (BrE)
A word that denotes a whole of which another word names a part.
word for a whole (of a part)

Examples

In linguistics, the word holonym describes the whole that contains particular parts.
'Car' is the holonym for 'wheel', 'engine', and 'door'.
The teacher explained that 'tree' is a holonym for 'branch' and 'leaf'.

Synset Examples

‘hat’ is a holonym for ‘brim’ and ‘crown’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meronym - a word that denotes a part of something

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + -onym (name).
