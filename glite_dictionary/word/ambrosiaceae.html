Ambrosiaceae

1. NOUN

/ æmˌbroʊziˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / amˌbrəʊziˈeɪsiˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, sometimes classified separately within Compositae, that includes ragweeds and related species.
plant family including ragweeds

Examples

The Ambrosiaceae family includes many species known to cause seasonal allergies.
Botanists sometimes separate Ambrosiaceae from the broader Compositae family for classification purposes.
Ragweeds are perhaps the most infamous members of the Ambrosiaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asteraceae - the composite or daisy family of flowering plants
  • Synonym: Compositae - alternative name for the aster or composite family of plants

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the genus Ambrosia (ragweed).

2. NOUN

/ æmˌbroʊziˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / amˌbrəʊziˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, sometimes considered a separate family within the Compositae, including the ragweeds.
plant family including ragweeds
medical

Examples

The Ambrosiaceae include several species of ragweed known to cause allergies.
Botanists sometimes classify ragweeds in the Ambrosiaceae rather than the broader Asteraceae family.
Recent studies debate whether Ambrosiaceae should remain a separate family or be merged with Compositae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Compositae - A large family of flowering plants, also called Asteraceae
  • Degree Higher: Asteraceae - The composite family of flowering plants, another name for Compositae

Etymology

From the genus name Ambrosia, which is the type genus of the family, plus the botanical suffix -aceae (family).
