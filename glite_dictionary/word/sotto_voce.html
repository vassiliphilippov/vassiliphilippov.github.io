sotto voce

1. ADV

/ ˌsoʊtoʊ ˈvoʊtʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɒtəʊ ˈvəʊtʃeɪ / (BrE)
in a quiet voice so as not to be heard by others; in an undertone
in a quiet or undertone voice

Examples

She whispered the answer sotto voce so the others would not hear.
During the meeting, he commented sotto voce about the proposal.
The actress delivered the crucial line sotto voce for dramatic effect.

Synset Examples

he uttered a curse sotto voce

Etymology

From Italian, literally 'under voice'.
