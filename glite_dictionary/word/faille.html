faille

1. NOUN

/ faɪl / (AmE), / faɪl / (BrE)
a ribbed woven fabric made from silk, rayon, or cotton, characterized by a slight sheen and a soft, flexible texture
ribbed woven fabric (silk, rayon, or cotton)

Examples

The dress was made of luxurious faille that shimmered in the light.
She chose a navy blue faille for the elegant evening gown.
Many vintage coats from the era were lined with faille for added comfort.

Etymology

From French 'faille', meaning 'fault, flaw', later used for the fabric name.
