relocated

1. ADJ

/ ˌriːˈloʊˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈləʊkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having moved and settled in a new place or location.
having settled in a new location

Examples

After the earthquake, many families were relocated to new housing developments on the outskirts of town.
The company’s relocated headquarters are now much closer to the airport.
They quickly adjusted to their relocated lives in a different country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moved - having changed one’s place or residence

Etymology

From the verb 'relocate', from re- (again) + locate (to put in a place), ultimately from Latin locare.
