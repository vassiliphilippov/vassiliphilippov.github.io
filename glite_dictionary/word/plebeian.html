plebeian

1. NOUN

/ pləˈbiːən / (AmE), / plɪˈbiːən / (BrE)
A member of the common people, especially in ancient Rome; an ordinary person with no special rank or title.
common person; ordinary citizen

Examples

During the Roman Republic, a plebeian could not hold public office.
In his novel, the hero was a humble plebeian from the countryside.
Many reforms in Rome aimed to increase the rights of the plebeians.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrician - a member of the aristocracy, especially in ancient Rome
  • Synonym: commoner - an ordinary person without rank or title

Etymology

From Latin 'plebeius' (of the common people), from 'plebs' (the common people).

2. ADJ

/ pləˈbiː.ən / (AmE), / plɪˈbiː.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of common people, especially in contrast to the elite or privileged.
of the common people; not elite

Examples

Many considered his plebeian background an obstacle in high society.
The politician's plebeian tastes made him popular with ordinary voters.
Some critics dismissed the play's plebeian humor as lacking sophistication.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common - ordinary; not special or elite
  • Synonym: vulgar - lacking sophistication or refinement; common
  • Antonym: aristocratic - belonging to or typical of the aristocracy; elite

Etymology

From Latin 'plebeius' (of the common people), from 'plebs' (the common people).
