Regalecus glesne

1. NOUN

/ rəˈɡeɪləkəs ˈɡlɛsni / (AmE), / rɛˈɡalɪkəs ˈɡlɛsni / (BrE)
a long, thin species of deep-water fish, known as the giant oarfish, distinguished by its ribbon-like body and red dorsal fin, measuring 20 to 30 feet in length
species of giant deep-sea oarfish

Examples

The Regalecus glesne is often called the giant oarfish due to its extraordinary length.
A Regalecus glesne was recently spotted near the coast, sparking fascination among marine biologists.
With its shimmering body and red dorsal fin, the Regalecus glesne is a rare sight in shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarfish - large, elongated deep-sea fish with a ribbon-like body

Etymology

From Latin: Regalecus (genus name), from 'regalis' meaning royal; glesne (species name) after Lake Glesne, Norway.
