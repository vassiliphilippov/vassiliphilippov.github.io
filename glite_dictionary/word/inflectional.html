inflectional

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈflɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving changes in the form of a word to express grammatical distinctions such as tense, number, or case.
relating to word forms expressing grammar

Examples

English is considered an inflectional language because verbs change form for tense and person.
The inflectional endings in Latin indicate the case and number of nouns.
In linguistics class, we studied inflectional morphology and how it shapes meaning.

Synset Examples

inflectional morphology is used to indicate number and case and tense and person etc.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: derivational - relating to the formation of new words by adding affixes
  • Synonym: inflected - (of a word or language) showing grammatical variation by inflection

Etymology

From 'inflection' + '-al', with 'inflection' from Latin 'inflectere' (to bend).
