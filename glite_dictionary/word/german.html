German

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.mən / (BrE)
relating to Germany, its people, or its language.
of Germany or its people/language

Examples

She enjoys studying German history and culture at university.
Many German companies are known for their engineering excellence.
The museum is displaying rare German manuscripts from the 18th century.

Synset Examples

German philosophers
German universities
German literature

Etymology

From Latin 'Germanus' (of Germany), via Old French 'germains'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.mən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or citizen of Germany, or of German descent
a person from Germany

Examples

Her grandfather was a proud German who immigrated to the United States.
Many Germans visit other European countries each summer for vacation.
He spoke fluent English, but his accent revealed he was a German.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German - the language spoken in Germany and Austria

Etymology

From Latin 'Germanus', referring to the people of Germania.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09767053-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːmən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the standard language of Germany, Austria, and parts of Switzerland, belonging to the West Germanic branch of the Indo-European language family
the standard language of Germany and Austria

Examples

Many people learn German as a second language in school.
The novel was originally written in German before being translated into English.
She is fluent in both German and French.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English - the standard language of England and many other countries
  • Synonym: French - the standard language of France and parts of other countries

Etymology

From Latin 'Germania' (land of the Germans), through Old French 'german' and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06963290-n
Synset POS: n
