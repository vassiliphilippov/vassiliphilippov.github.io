stactometer

1. NOUN

/ stækˈtɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / stækˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used to measure the surface tension of liquids by means of the stalagmometric method.
device measuring surface tension of liquids

Examples

The laboratory technician used a stactometer to determine the surface tension of the new liquid solution.
Before beginning her experiment, she carefully calibrated the stactometer.
Research into detergents often relies on data collected with a stactometer.

Synset Examples

The part of the bottom of the stalagmometer is narrowed down to let the fluid fall out from the tube in a shape of drop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalagmometer - device for measuring surface tension using drops

Etymology

From Greek 'staktos' (dripping) + '-meter' (measuring instrument), patterned after 'stalagmometer'.
