parenthesis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛnθəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrɛnθəsɪs / (BrE)
either of the pair of round marks ( ) used in writing to enclose words, phrases, or numbers, usually to add extra information or asides
punctuation mark: ( or )

Examples

Please put your explanation in parentheses to make it clearer.
Numbers in parentheses refer to corresponding notes at the bottom of the page.
The word in parentheses is an optional part of the sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracket - one of the pair of punctuation symbols [ ] used to enclose text

Etymology

From Greek 'parenthesis', meaning 'insertion', from 'para-' (beside) + 'en' (in) + 'tithenai' (to put).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈɹɛnθəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈɹɛnθəsɪs / (BrE)
a remark or passage that is inserted into a speech or piece of writing as an explanation or afterthought, often departing from the main subject
digressive remark or passage

Examples

His comments about the weather were just a parenthesis in an otherwise serious discussion.
The author uses a brief parenthesis to share a personal anecdote with the reader.
During her lecture, she included a humorous parenthesis that lightened the mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing

Etymology

From Greek parenthesis, from para- 'beside' + en 'in' + tithenai 'to place'.
