grafting

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːftɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of attaching one thing to another, especially inserting a shoot or twig into a plant or a piece of tissue into a body so that they unite and grow together
the act of attaching or joining, especially in plants or tissue

Examples

Grafting allows gardeners to combine the qualities of two different plants in a single tree.
Successful grafting requires clean cuts and precise technique.
Skin grafting was necessary to treat the patient's severe burns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graft - to attach or insert (a shoot or tissue) so it unites with another

Etymology

From the verb 'graft', ultimately from Old French 'grafe' (graft, stylus), from Latin 'graphium'.
