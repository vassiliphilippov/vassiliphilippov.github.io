qui vive

1. NOUN

/ ki ˈviv / (AmE), / ki ˈviːv / (BrE)
a state of alertness or watchful readiness for action
state of alert watchfulness

Examples

The soldiers remained on qui vive throughout the night as enemy forces approached.
She was always on the qui vive for any sign of trouble in the neighborhood.
After the break-in, the whole family was on qui vive for several days.

Synset Examples

bombers were put on alert during the crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - state of being watchful and ready to respond
  • Antonym: off guard - not alert or not prepared for something

Etymology

From French 'qui vive?' meaning 'who goes there?', a sentry's challenge.
