vertiginous

1. ADJ

/ vərˈtɪdʒənəs / (AmE), / vəˈtɪdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
causing or suffering from a sensation of whirling and dizziness; extremely high or steep, making one feel dizzy
causing or experiencing dizziness; extremely steep

Examples

The climber stood on the vertiginous edge, looking down at the valley far below.
She avoided the vertiginous staircase because it made her feel dizzy.
The road wound up a vertiginous mountain pass that left drivers' heads spinning.

Synset Examples

had a dizzy spell
a dizzy pinnacle
had a headache and felt giddy
a giddy precipice
feeling woozy from the blow on his head
a vertiginous climb up the face of the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - having or causing a sensation of spinning and unsteadiness
  • Synonym: giddy - feeling or causing a sensation of dizziness or lightheadedness

Etymology

From Latin 'vertiginosus' (dizzy, giddy), from 'vertigo' (a turning, dizziness).
