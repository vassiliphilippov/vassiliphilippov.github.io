once

1. ADV

/ wʌns / (AmE), / wʌns / (BrE)
On one occasion; at a single time in the past or future.
on one occasion

Examples

I've only watched that movie once in my life.
She told me about the accident just once and never brought it up again.
If you visit Paris even once, you'll remember it forever.

Synset Examples

once I ran into her

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: twice - on two occasions

Etymology

From Old English 'ānces' (once, at one time), from 'ān' (one).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00119765-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ wʌns / (AmE), / wʌns / (BrE)
As soon as; immediately after the moment when (used to indicate that something happens immediately after another event).
as soon as; immediately after

Examples

Once you've finished your homework, you can go outside to play.
He called me once everything was ready for the party.
Once the meeting ends, let's meet for coffee.

Synset Examples

once we are home, we can rest

Etymology

From Middle English 'ones', from Old English 'ānēs' (of one, once).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00182828-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ wʌns / (AmE), / wʌns / (BrE)
At a time in the past; formerly.
formerly; at a previous time

Examples

She once lived in Paris before moving to London.
He was a famous athlete once, though few remember now.
I once dreamed of becoming a writer.

Synset Examples

at one time he loved her
her erstwhile writing
she was a dancer once

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formerly - in the past; earlier
  • Antonym: now - at the present time

Etymology

From Middle English 'ones', genitive of 'one', influenced by Old English 'ān' (one).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00119861-r
Synset POS: r
