cartonful

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrtənˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtənˌfʊl / (BrE)
an amount that fills a carton
amount that fills a carton

Examples

She poured a cartonful of milk into the large mixing bowl.
We received a cartonful of books from the publisher yesterday.
He brought a cartonful of eggs to the market for sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxful - the amount that fills a box

Etymology

Formed from 'carton' + 'ful', following the pattern of words like 'spoonful'.
