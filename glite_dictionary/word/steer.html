steer

1. NOUN

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
an indication or piece of advice that suggests a possible opportunity or course of action
advice or suggestion about an opportunity
informal

Examples

She gave me a steer about which companies were hiring this month.
If you want a good restaurant, I can give you a steer.
Thanks for the steer on where to find affordable apartments.

Synset Examples

he got a tip on the stock market
a good lead for a job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a helpful piece of information or advice, especially about a job or opportunity
  • Synonym: lead - information that may help discover an opportunity or find out something valuable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06664322-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
to guide or direct oneself or someone else in a particular direction or towards a particular goal
guide or direct one's course

Examples

She tried to steer clear of any controversial topics during the meeting.
After the accident, he learned to steer himself away from risky behaviors.
Tourists are advised to steer clear of the dangerous area at night.

Synset Examples

Steer clear of him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - to show or lead the way
  • Synonym: to navigate - to plan and direct the course
  • Antonym: to follow - to go in the same direction as someone else

Etymology

From Old English *stēoran* (to guide a ship), related to Old Norse *styra* (to steer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01938963-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
to guide or direct the course of someone or something, especially in a figurative sense
guide or direct; motivate

Examples

Her mentor helped steer her toward a successful scientific career.
Parents often try to steer their children away from bad influences.
The organization aims to steer public opinion in favor of environmental protection.

Synset Examples

The teacher steered the gifted students towards the more challenging courses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - to show or direct the way; to lead
  • Synonym: lead - to cause a person or group to go with one by holding them by the hand, a rope, etc.; to guide

4. NOUN

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
a young male bovine animal that has been castrated and is raised for beef
castrated male cattle

Examples

The rancher separated the steer from the rest of the herd.
Each steer was weighed before being sent to market.
They raised several steers each year for beef production.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - an uncastrated male bovine animal
  • Synonym: ox - a domesticated bovine animal trained as a draft animal, typically castrated
  • Antonym: cow - a fully grown female bovine animal

Etymology

From Old English *steor* (young ox, steer).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
to direct the course of a vehicle, ship, or other moving object; to guide or control the direction in which something moves
to direct or guide the movement of something

Examples

You need to steer the car carefully around the sharp bend.
The captain managed to steer the ship safely through the storm.
She tried to steer the conversation away from politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - to operate and control the direction and speed of a motor vehicle
  • Synonym: guide - to lead or show the way
  • Antonym: to follow - to go or come after in the same direction

Etymology

From Old English *stēoran* (to guide a ship), related to Old Norse *styra* (to control, govern).
