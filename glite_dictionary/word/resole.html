to resole

1. VERB

/ riˈsoʊl / (AmE), / riˈsəʊl / (BrE)
to put a new sole on a shoe or boot
put a new sole on footwear

Examples

I took my boots to the cobbler so he could resole them.
Many climbers prefer to resole their shoes instead of buying new ones.
After years of wear, she finally decided to resole her favorite pair of sandals.

Synset Examples

sole the shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - to restore something damaged to good condition
  • Synonym: to replace - to put something new in place of something old or worn

Etymology

From re- (again) + sole (the bottom part of a shoe or boot), modeled after words like 'rebuild'.
