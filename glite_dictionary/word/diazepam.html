diazepam

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæzəˌpæm / (AmE), / daɪˈazɪpam / (BrE)
a medication of the benzodiazepine class used to treat anxiety, muscle spasms, and seizures, sometimes known by the trade name Valium.
medication for anxiety and muscle spasms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed diazepam to help manage her anxiety symptoms.
Diazepam is sometimes administered to patients experiencing muscle spasms after surgery.
Emergency responders may use diazepam to control seizures caused by poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valium - trade name for diazepam

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: 'diazepine' (a heterocyclic compound) + '-am' (from 'amine').
