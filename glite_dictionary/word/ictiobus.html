Ictiobus

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtaɪ.oʊ.bəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɪə.bəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater fishes commonly known as buffalo fishes, characterized by deep bodies and humped backs.
genus of North American buffalo fishes

Examples

Species of the genus Ictiobus are important for commercial fishing in North America.
The largest member of the Ictiobus genus can weigh over 50 pounds.
Ichthyologists have long studied Ictiobus for their adaptability to various freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'bous' (ox), referring to the robust shape of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪktiˈoʊbəs / (AmE), / ˌɪktiˈəʊbəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater fishes known as buffalo fishes, characterized by large size and humped back.
genus of buffalo fishes

Examples

The Ictiobus species are often found in large rivers and lakes throughout North America.
A biologist studied the feeding habits of several Ictiobus populations.
Fishing regulations may vary depending on the abundance of Ictiobus in different regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo fish - common name for members of the genus Ictiobus

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'bous' (ox), referring to the fish's size and shape.
