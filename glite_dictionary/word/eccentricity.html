eccentricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sənˈtrɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sənˈtrɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(mathematics, physics) The extent to which a curve or orbit deviates from being circular, usually measured by how much the center differs from the center of the circle, or by the numerical value describing the deviation.
deviation from circularity (geometry/physics)

Examples

The oval's eccentricity determines how far its shape deviates from a perfect circle.
Earth's orbit around the sun has a small eccentricity, making it nearly circular.
In engineering, the eccentricity of a rotating shaft can lead to vibrations and noise.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: oval - a shape resembling a flattened or elongated circle
  • Degree Lower: circle - a perfectly round shape with all points equidistant from the center; zero eccentricity

Etymology

From Late Latin *eccentricitas*, from Greek *ekkentros* (out of center): *ek-* (out) + *kentron* (center).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɛnˈtrɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɛnˈtrɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Geometry) A measure of how a conic section deviates from being circular, defined as the ratio of the distance between its foci to the length of its major axis.
ratio describing shape of a conic section

Examples

In mathematics, a circle has an eccentricity of zero while an ellipse has an eccentricity less than one.
The eccentricity of a conic section determines whether it is a circle, ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola.
By calculating the eccentricity, students can compare the shapes of different orbits in astronomy.

Synset Examples

a circle is an ellipse with zero eccentricity

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: circle - a round plane figure whose boundary consists of points equidistant from the center
  • Synonym: ellipse - a regular oval shape, a type of conic section
  • Degree Higher: parabola - a symmetrical open plane curve formed by the intersection of a cone with a plane parallel to its side
  • Degree Higher: hyperbola - an open curve formed by a plane that cuts the base of a right circular cone

Etymology

From Latin eccentricitas, from Greek ekkentros ('out of center').

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɛnˈtrɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɛnˈtrɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being unconventional or showing strange or unusual behavior
strange or unconventional behavior

Examples

His eccentricity was evident in the mismatched shoes he wore to every meeting.
Many artists are known for their eccentricity and unexpected habits.
She regarded his eccentricity as charming rather than bothersome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - a strange or peculiar person or thing
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical

Etymology

From Greek ekkentros (out of the center), via Late Latin eccentricitas.
