Geranium pratense

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm præˈtɛnsi / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪniəm praˈtɛnseɪ / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant with violet-blue flowers, commonly known as the meadow cranesbill, native to northern Europe and Asia and naturalized in North America.
a tall perennial herb with violet-blue flowers

Examples

Many gardeners admire the beautiful Geranium pratense for its vibrant blue flowers.
The Geranium pratense thrives in meadows and along woodland edges in Europe.
Botanists observed that Geranium pratense has spread to new regions in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranesbill - common name for species in the Geranium genus

Etymology

From Latin: 'geranium' (from Greek 'geranos', meaning 'crane') and 'pratense' (meaning 'of the meadow').
