Chenopodium murale

1. NOUN

/ kɛˌnoʊˈpoʊdiəm mjʊˈreɪli / (AmE), / kɛˌnɒˈpəʊdiəm mjʊˈrɑːli / (BrE)
A European annual plant with coarsely toothed leaves, now widespread in the United States and southern Canada.
European annual plant; now widespread in North America

Examples

Chenopodium murale often grows in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.
Gardeners sometimes remove Chenopodium murale because it can become a common weed.
Researchers have studied the spread of Chenopodium murale in North American agricultural areas.

Etymology

From Latin: 'chenopodium' (goosefoot) and 'murale' (of walls).
