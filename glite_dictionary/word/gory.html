gory

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɔri / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːri / (BrE)
covered with blood; stained or splattered with blood.
covered or stained with blood

Examples

The killer cleaned his gory hands before anyone arrived.
The detective recoiled at the sight of the gory weapon lying on the ground.
His shirt was gory after helping the injured man.

Synset Examples

a bloodstained shirt
a gory dagger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodstained - covered or marked with blood

Etymology

From gore (blood, especially clotted blood), from Old English 'gor' (dirt, dung, blood).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɔri / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːri / (BrE)
involving or showing a lot of blood or violence, especially as the result of injury or killing
involving much bloodshed or violence

Examples

The horror movie was too gory for some viewers, featuring graphic scenes of violence.
He avoided watching gory news reports about the war.
The crime scene was one of the most gory the detective had ever encountered.

Synset Examples

this bitter and sanguinary war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - covered in or involving blood, often violently
  • Synonym: sanguinary - involving or causing much bloodshed

Etymology

From English 'gore' (blood, especially clotted blood), + -y (adjectival suffix); 'gore' from Old English 'gor' (dirt, dung, clotted blood).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00249970-s
Synset POS: s
