psychoneurotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkoʊnʊˈrɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊnjuˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
Affected by or relating to a psychoneurosis; suffering from an emotional disorder.
affected by an emotional disorder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as psychoneurotic after displaying persistent anxiety and irrational fears.
Her psychoneurotic symptoms made daily routines increasingly challenging.
Doctors developed new therapies specifically for psychoneurotic disorders in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotic - having or relating to neurosis; emotionally unstable

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + neurotic, relating to nerves and mental disorder.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌnʊˈroʊtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌnjʊəˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
a person who is affected by or suffering from neurosis
person with neurosis
medical

Examples

The therapist worked closely with the psychoneurotic to help manage her anxiety disorders.
Some researchers have argued that a psychoneurotic may benefit from cognitive behavioral therapy.
In past decades, the term psychoneurotic was widely used in psychiatric diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotic - a person with a mild mental disorder characterized by anxiety, phobias, or obsessive behavior

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + neurotic (from Greek neuron 'nerve').
