Aulacorhyncus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔː.lə.kɔːˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌɔː.lə.kɒˈrɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the toucan family (Ramphastidae), native to Central and South America, known as green toucanets.
genus of green toucanets (birds)

Examples

The genus Aulacorhyncus includes several species of small green toucans found in South America.
Researchers studied the vocalizations of Aulacorhyncus birds in their natural habitats.
Several birdwatchers spotted a rare Aulacorhyncus in the cloud forests of Ecuador.

Etymology

From Greek aulax (furrow) + rhynchos (beak).
