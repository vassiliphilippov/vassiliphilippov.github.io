Heliothis zia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈɒθɪs ˈzaɪə / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪˈɒθɪs ˈzaɪə / (BrE)
A species of noctuid moth whose larva is highly destructive to crops, especially corn, cotton, and tomatoes.
a crop-damaging moth species

Examples

Farmers often struggle to protect their crops from the destructive larvae of Heliothis zia.
The infestation of Heliothis zia larvae caused significant losses in the region's cotton fields.
Research focuses on new pesticides that can control Heliothis zia without harming beneficial insects.

Etymology

From modern Latin, following taxonomic convention; genus Heliothis from Greek hēlios (sun) + this (touch).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈoʊθɪs ˈzaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪˈɒθɪs ˈziː.ə / (BrE)
a medium-sized moth whose larvae are known as corn earworms, pests of various crops, especially corn
moth species; corn earworm larva

Examples

Farmers often face extensive damage to crops from the larvae of Heliothis zia.
The life cycle of Heliothis zia has been studied to develop better pest control methods.
Heliothis zia is recognized for its role as the corn earworm in agricultural environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn earworm - the larval stage of Heliothis zia, a common agricultural pest

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Heliothis and species name zia.
