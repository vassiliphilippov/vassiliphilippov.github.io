Bucephela albeola

1. NOUN

/ bjuˈsɛfələ ˌælbiˈoʊlə / (AmE), / bjuːˈsɛfɪlə ˌælbɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
A small North American diving duck, the male of which has distinctive bushy head plumage; also known as the bufflehead.
species of small North American diving duck

Examples

A male Bucephela albeola displays a striking white patch on its head during mating season.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot several Bucephela albeola on the lake this morning.
The Bucephela albeola prefers to nest in tree cavities near ponds or small lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bufflehead - common name for Bucephela albeola, a small North American diving duck

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Bucephala' and species name 'albeola', meaning 'whitish' in Latin.
