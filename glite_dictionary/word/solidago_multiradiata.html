Solidago multiradiata

1. NOUN

/ soʊlɪˈdeɪɡoʊ ˌmʌltɪˌreɪdiˈɑːtə / (AmE), / sɒlɪˈdeɪɡəʊ ˌmʌltɪˌreɪdɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of goldenrod with bristly hairs on the edges of its leaf stalks, found in mountainous regions of western North America.
mountain goldenrod species of western North America

Examples

The slopes were covered with Solidago multiradiata in full bloom, adding a golden hue to the mountain landscape.
Botanists identified Solidago multiradiata by the distinctive bristly hairs on its leaf stalks.
Hikers in the Rockies often encounter patches of Solidago multiradiata along alpine trails.

Etymology

From Latin: 'solidago' (goldenrod genus) and 'multiradiata' meaning 'many-rayed'.
