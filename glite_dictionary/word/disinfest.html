to disinfest

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛst / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛst / (BrE)
to rid a place or object of insects, vermin, or other pests, typically by using chemical or physical means
remove pests or vermin

Examples

The company was hired to disinfest the factory after a mouse infestation.
Farmers must regularly disinfest their storage facilities to protect crops from insects.
Specialists disinfested the old barn using safe, eco-friendly products.

Synset Examples

The exterminator disinfests the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean something to destroy bacteria or viruses
  • Antonym: to infest - to be present in large numbers and cause damage or annoyance

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + infestare (to trouble, disturb).
