Tuamotus

1. PROPN

/ tuˈɑːməˌtuːz / (AmE), / tjuːˈæmətjuːz / (BrE)
a group of about 80 coral atolls in French Polynesia, located in the central South Pacific Ocean
group of coral islands in French Polynesia

Examples

The Tuamotus are famous for their stunning lagoons and thriving marine life.
She spent a summer volunteering on one of the remote Tuamotus.
The pearl industry is a major part of the economy in the Tuamotus.

Etymology

From French 'Tuamotu', itself from the Tuamotuan language meaning 'distant islands'.
