endoscopy

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈdɑːskəpi / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒskəpi / (BrE)
A medical procedure in which the inside of a body organ or cavity is visually examined using an endoscope.
internal medical examination with an endoscope
medical

Examples

The doctor scheduled an endoscopy to investigate the cause of her stomach pain.
During the endoscopy, the physician carefully examined the lining of the patient's esophagus and stomach.
Advances in technology have made endoscopy less invasive and more comfortable for patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonoscopy - examination of the colon with an endoscope
  • Synonym: gastroscopy - examination of the stomach with an endoscope

Etymology

From Greek endo- (within) + -scopy (to look or observe), based on skopein (to look).
