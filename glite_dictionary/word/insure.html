to insure

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to arrange for compensation in the event of damage, loss, or injury by taking out an insurance policy
arrange insurance for protection

Examples

It is wise to insure your house against fire and theft.
Before driving in most countries, you are required to insure your car.
They decided to insure their business after a series of unfortunate incidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ensure - to make certain that something happens

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman *enseurer*, from Old French *asseurer* (make safe).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to protect against loss, damage, or liability by means of insurance
protect by means of insurance

Examples

Many companies insure their employees for health benefits.
They decided to insure the shipment before it left the port.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to make certain that something will happen; to take steps to guarantee or secure an outcome
make certain; secure or guarantee

Examples

She double-checked the doors to insure they were locked before leaving.
Proper training will insure everyone knows what to do in an emergency.
Please insure that all forms are filled out correctly before submitting them.

Synset Examples

See that the curtains are closed
control the quality of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ensure - to make certain that something will happen
  • Synonym: guarantee - to provide a formal assurance
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or do something

Etymology

From late Middle English, from Anglo-Norman French 'ensurer', alteration of Old French 'enseürer', from en- (in) + seür (sure).
