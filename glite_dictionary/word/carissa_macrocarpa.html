Carissa macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪsə ˌmækrəˈkɑrpə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪsə ˌmakrəˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
a large, densely branched South African shrub with forked bright green spines and glossy leaves, known for its edible fruit
South African shrub with spines and shiny leaves

Examples

The Carissa macrocarpa is often grown in gardens for its attractive appearance and edible fruit.
Botanists studied the thorny branches of Carissa macrocarpa during their field trip to South Africa.
Ripe fruit from the Carissa macrocarpa is sometimes used in making jams and preserves.

Etymology

From Latin, Carissa (genus name) and macrocarpa from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'karpos' (fruit).
