paw

1. VERB

/ pɔ / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
(of an animal) to scrape, touch, or hit something repeatedly with its paw or feet.
scrape or touch with the paw

Examples

The dog began to paw at the closed window, wanting to go outside.
The horse pawed the ground impatiently as it waited for its rider.
My cat often paws the blanket before settling down to sleep.

Synset Examples

The bear pawed the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to rub or scrape with the claws or nails
  • Antonym: stroke - to pass the hand or an object gently over something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɔ / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to touch or handle someone or something clumsily or roughly, often with the hands or fingers
touch clumsily or roughly

Examples

He tried to paw her at the crowded party, but she pushed him away.
The child began to paw at the gift, eager to see what was inside.
She disliked how strangers would paw through her belongings without asking.

Synset Examples

The man tried to paw her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - to feel about or search blindly or uncertainly with the hands

Etymology

From Middle English pawe, from Old French poe (animal's foot). The verb sense developed by analogy to an animal's pawing motion.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ pɔ / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
the foot of an animal, especially a four-legged one, that has claws or nails
animal's clawed foot

Examples

The cat tucked its paw under its body as it slept on the windowsill.
After running through the mud, the dog's paws needed a good wash.
The lion raised its massive paw and gently touched its cub.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hoof - the hard foot of some animals, such as horses or cows
  • Antonym: hand - the human appendage analogous to an animal's paw

Etymology

From Old French *powe* (paw), origin uncertain.
