vehemence

1. NOUN

/ ˈviəməns / (AmE), / ˈviːɪm(ə)ns / (BrE)
the quality of being intensely forceful or passionate, especially in expressing feelings or opinions
intensity or forcefulness of expression

Examples

She spoke with such vehemence that the entire room fell silent.
His vehemence during the debate surprised the audience.
The protestors shouted their demands with great vehemence.

Synset Examples

the vehemence of his denial
his emphasis on civil rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion - strong emotion or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: intensity - the quality of being intense or forceful
  • Antonym: mildness - the quality of being gentle or moderate

Etymology

From Latin 'vehementia' (impetuosity, force), from 'vehemens' (impetuous, violent).

2. NOUN

/ ˈviː.ə.məns / (AmE), / ˈviː.ɪ.məns / (BrE)
the quality of being forceful, intense, or marked by strong emotion or energy; intensity or fervor
intensity; strong emotion or force

Examples

Her speech was delivered with such vehemence that the audience was left stunned.
The vehemence of the debate surprised everyone present.
He protested with great vehemence, refusing to back down.

Synset Examples

the storm's violence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - the use of physical force to harm or damage
  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being calm and free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'vehementia' (impetuosity, eagerness).
