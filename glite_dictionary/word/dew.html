dew

1. NOUN

/ du / (AmE), / djuː / (BrE)
Tiny drops of water that form on cool surfaces at night, caused by condensation of water vapor in the air.
moisture condensed on cool surfaces at night

Examples

The petals glittered with dew in the early morning sunlight.
After a warm night, a layer of dew covered the car windows.
He liked to walk barefoot in the grass, feeling the cool dew between his toes.

Synset Examples

in the morning the grass was wet with dew

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frost - a deposit of small white ice crystals on the ground or other surfaces

Etymology

From Old English *dēaw*, related to German *Tau* and Dutch *dauw*.
