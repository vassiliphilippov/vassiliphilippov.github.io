cryogenics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪəˈdʒɛnɪks / (AmE), / ˌkraɪəʊˈdʒɛnɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics concerned with the production and effects of very low temperatures.
science of very low temperatures

Examples

Cryogenics is essential for preserving biological samples at extremely low temperatures.
Researchers in cryogenics have developed new techniques for storing organs for transplantation.
Advances in cryogenics have made it possible to achieve temperatures near absolute zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermodynamics - the branch of physics dealing with heat and temperature and their relation to energy

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (frost) and the suffix '-genics', modeled after 'physics'.
