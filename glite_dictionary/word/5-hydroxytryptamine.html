5-hydroxytryptamine

1. NOUN

/ faɪ ˌhaɪdroʊˌtrɪp.təˈmiːn / (AmE), / faɪ ˌhɪdrəʊˌtrɪp.təˈmiːn / (BrE)
a neurotransmitter in the brain and body that is involved in regulating mood, sleep, appetite, and memory; also known as serotonin
neurotransmitter, serotonin
medical

Examples

The levels of 5-hydroxytryptamine in the brain influence mood and emotional well-being.
Antidepressant medications often work by increasing 5-hydroxytryptamine availability in the synaptic cleft.
Researchers discovered that 5-hydroxytryptamine plays a crucial role in regulating the sleep-wake cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serotonin - another name for 5-hydroxytryptamine, a neurotransmitter

Etymology

From the chemical structure: 5-hydroxy- (five hydroxyl) + tryptamine (derived from tryptophan).
