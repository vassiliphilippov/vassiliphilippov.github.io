hesitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛzɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɛzɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A pause or delay due to uncertainty or indecision, especially in speech or action.
pause caused by indecision

Examples

She answered the difficult question without a moment's hesitation.
His hesitation before signing the contract made everyone uneasy.
There was a brief hesitation in her voice as she spoke to the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not confident
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of certainty or assurance

Etymology

From Latin 'haesitatio', from 'haesitare' (to hesitate or be uncertain).

3. NOUN

/ ˌhɛzɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɛzɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of pausing before doing or saying something, usually because of uncertainty or nervousness
the act of pausing due to uncertainty

Examples

She answered the question without a moment's hesitation.
After a brief hesitation, he stepped onto the stage and began to speak.
There was clear hesitation in his voice as he broke the news.

Synset Examples

there was a hesitation in his speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop or break in speech or action
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or confident

Etymology

From Latin 'haesitatio' (a sticking fast, hesitation), from 'haesitare' (to stick fast, hesitate).
