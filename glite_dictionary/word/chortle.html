chortle

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɔːr.təl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔː.tl̩ / (BrE)
to laugh in a quiet or restrained way, often with a snort or chuckle
laugh quietly or with restraint

Examples

Samantha couldn't help but chortle at the comedian's clever punchline.
He chortled quietly as he read the amusing text message.
The children chortled with delight as the puppy chased its tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly in a restrained manner
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously

Etymology

Coined by Lewis Carroll in 'Through the Looking-Glass' (1871) as a blend of 'chuckle' and 'snort'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɔr.təl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔː.təl / (BrE)
a soft, partly suppressed laugh; a joyful, gleeful chuckle
a soft, partly suppressed laugh

Examples

She let out a little chortle at his silly joke.
His chortle echoed in the quiet room, making everyone smile.
The child suppressed a chortle as he tried not to get caught.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckle - a quiet or suppressed laugh
  • Degree Lower: giggle - a light, silly laugh
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - a loud, boisterous laugh

Etymology

Blend of 'chuckle' and 'snort,' coined by Lewis Carroll in 1871.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
