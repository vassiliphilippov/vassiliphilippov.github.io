Padua

1. PROPN

/ ˈpædjuə / (AmE), / ˈpædjʊə / (BrE)
a city in the Veneto region of northern Italy, known for its historic university and rich cultural heritage
city in northern Italy

Examples

Galileo once taught at the University of Padua, one of Europe's oldest institutions of higher learning.
Tourists often visit Padua for its medieval architecture and beautiful frescoes.
The city of Padua is featured in several works by Shakespeare.

Etymology

From Italian Padova, Latin Patavium.
