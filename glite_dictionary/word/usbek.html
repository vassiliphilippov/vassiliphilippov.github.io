Usbek

1. NOUN

/ uzˈbɛk / (AmE), / ʊzˈbɛk / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken primarily by the Uzbek people in Central Asia.
the language of the Uzbek people

Examples

She has been studying Usbek to better communicate with her colleagues in Tashkent.
Several classic Central Asian poems were originally written in Usbek.
The radio broadcast is available in English, Russian, and Usbek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - a native or inhabitant of Uzbekistan

Etymology

From Russian 'Uzbek', from Uzbek 'Oʻzbek'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06940140-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈʊz.bɛk / (AmE), / ˈʌz.bɛk / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group originating from Uzbekistan and nearby regions.
member of Uzbek ethnic group

Examples

The Usbek people have a rich tradition of music and dance.
As an Usbek, he celebrates Nowruz with his family every spring.
Many Usbeks live in Uzbekistan as well as in neighboring countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - official language of Uzbekistan

Etymology

From Russian 'Uzbek', from Uzbek 'Oʻzbek', referring to the Uzbek people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09756183-n
Synset POS: n
