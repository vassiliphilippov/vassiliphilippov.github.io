Habenaria greenei

1. PROPN

/ ˌhæbəˈnɛriə ˈɡriːniː / (AmE), / ˌhæbənˈɑːriə ˈɡriːniː / (BrE)
A species of stout orchid native to central California to northern Washington, with racemes of white, fragrant, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.
a species of orchid native to western North America

Examples

The rare Habenaria greenei is often spotted in moist meadows during late summer.
Botanists studied the pollination patterns of Habenaria greenei in Washington State.
Hikers were excited to discover a cluster of blooming Habenaria greenei along the trail.

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized form of German botanist H. Habenaria; species named after botanist Edward Lee Greene.
