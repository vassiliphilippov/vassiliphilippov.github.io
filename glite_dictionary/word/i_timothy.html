I Timothy

1. PROPN

/ ˈfaɪrst ˈtɪməˌθi / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈtɪməθi / (BrE)
the first book in the New Testament addressed by Saint Paul to Timothy, offering advice on church leadership and pastoral care
New Testament epistle to Timothy

Examples

The pastor preached on a passage from I Timothy during Sunday's service.
Many scholars debate the authorship of I Timothy in the New Testament.
I Timothy contains practical guidance for church leaders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: II Timothy - the second epistle to Timothy, following I Timothy
  • Synonym: Epistle - a letter, especially a formal or didactic one; often used for the letters in the New Testament

Etymology

From Latin 'Timotheus', from Greek 'Timotheos', the name of the recipient. 'I' denotes the first epistle in sequence.
