antiviral

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈvaɪrəl / (AmE), / ˌantiˈvaɪrəl / (BrE)
A drug or substance that inhibits the growth or replication of viruses, often used to treat viral infections.
drug that inhibits viruses
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiviral to help combat the flu virus.
Patients with shingles may benefit from taking an antiviral early in the course of the disease.
Research is ongoing to develop new antiviral medications for emerging viruses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antibiotic - a drug used to treat bacterial infections
  • Synonym: antifungal - a drug used to treat fungal infections

Etymology

From anti- (against) + viral (relating to a virus).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈvaɪ.rəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈvaɪ.rəl / (BrE)
(of a substance, action, or drug) inhibiting or stopping the growth and reproduction of viruses.
inhibiting or stopping viruses
medical

Examples

This new drug has strong antiviral properties and can treat several infections.
Doctors recommend taking antiviral medications at the first sign of symptoms.
They are developing antiviral coatings for public surfaces to reduce the spread of illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibacterial - preventing or inhibiting the growth of bacteria

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'viral' (relating to a virus), first used in the medical context in the 20th century.
