Pericallis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈkælɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkalɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native to the Canary Islands and commonly known as cineraria.
genus of flowering plants (cineraria)

Examples

Many gardeners choose Pericallis for its vibrant blooms in spring.
The Pericallis hybrid is popular as an ornamental plant in temperate zones.
Botanists classify cineraria under the genus Pericallis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cineraria - another name for Pericallis, especially as a garden plant

Etymology

From Greek 'peri' (around) + 'kallos' (beauty), referring to the ring of ray florets around the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈkælɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkalɪs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as cineraria, native to the Canary Islands and Madeira, valued for their ornamental blooms.
a genus of ornamental flowering plants

Examples

The brilliant purple flowers of Pericallis brightened up the windowsills through spring.
Gardeners often choose Pericallis for its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
Pericallis hybrids are popular in floral arrangements due to their striking colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cineraria - common name for species in the genus Pericallis

Etymology

From Greek 'peri' (around) and 'kallos' (beauty), referring to the attractive appearance of the flowers.
