Amphioxidae

1. NOUN

/ æmˌfiːɑkˈsaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / amˌfiːɒkˈsaɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, elongated marine animals (lancelets), including most species in the subphylum Cephalochordata.
family of lancelets (marine animals)

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species belonging to the Amphioxidae family in the coastal waters of Asia.
The anatomy of Amphioxidae provides valuable insights into the early evolution of vertebrates.
Students studied specimens of Amphioxidae to understand the structural features of lancelets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lancelet - small, elongated marine animals of the Amphioxidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'amphi-' (both) and 'oxys' (sharp), referring to the pointed shape of the animal.

2. NOUN

/ æmˌfaɪ.əksɪˈdiː / (AmE), / æmˌfɪ.əksɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, fish-like marine animals known as lancelets, comprising most species in this group.
family of lancelets (marine animals)
medical

Examples

The Amphioxidae are often studied as the closest living relatives of vertebrates.
Researchers found several new lancelet species in the Amphioxidae family.
Textbooks describe Amphioxidae as a key group for understanding chordate evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lancelet - small, fish-like marine animal in the subphylum Cephalochordata

Etymology

From New Latin *Amphioxus* (the type genus) + suffix -idae (used in naming animal families).
