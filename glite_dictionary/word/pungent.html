pungent

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌn.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈpʌn.dʒənt / (BrE)
having a strong, sharp taste or smell that is often so intensive as to be unpleasant or stimulating
having a strong, sharp taste or smell

Examples

The pungent aroma of garlic immediately filled the kitchen.
She wrinkled her nose at the pungent fumes coming from the cleaner.
Wasabi has a pungent flavor that can make your eyes water.

Synset Examples

the pungent taste of radishes
the acrid smell of burning rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrid - having a very strong, bitter, and unpleasant taste or smell
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong features or flavor

Etymology

From Latin pungens, pungent- (present participle of pungere 'to prick').

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌndʒənt / (AmE), / ˈpʌndʒənt / (BrE)
(of speech, remarks, or writing) sharply expressive or capable of wounding emotionally; caustic or biting.
sharply expressive; caustic

Examples

Her pungent wit often left her audience stunned and silent.
The critic's pungent review stung the playwright deeply.
She delivered a pungent satire of modern politics in her latest column.

Synset Examples

a barbed compliment
a biting aphorism
pungent satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biting - (of remarks, etc.) sharply critical or cutting
  • Synonym: caustic - (of speech or writing) severely critical or sarcastic
  • Antonym: bland - (of remarks, etc.) lacking strong features or characteristics; not sharp

Etymology

From Latin pungens, pungent-, present participle of pungere (to prick, to sting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02086443-s
Synset POS: s
