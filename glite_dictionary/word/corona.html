corona

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊ.nə / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊ.nə / (BrE)
(Anatomy) Any structure in the body that is shaped like or resembles a crown.
crown-shaped anatomical structure
medical

Examples

In anatomy, the corona of the glans penis is the rounded base where it meets the shaft.
The dentist examined the corona of the tooth for signs of decay.
Certain flowers have a corona—a crown-like appendage surrounding their reproductive structures.

Etymology

From Latin 'corona' (crown).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊnə / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊnə / (BrE)
the outermost region of the Sun's atmosphere, visible as a white halo during a solar eclipse
the Sun's outer atmosphere, a halo seen at eclipse

Examples

During the total solar eclipse, the corona was clearly visible as a bright, glowing halo around the darkened Sun.
Scientists study the Sun's corona to understand solar winds and other astronomical phenomena.
The temperature of the corona is much higher than that of the Sun's surface, puzzling astronomers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: photosphere - the visible surface layer of the Sun
  • Degree Lower: chromosphere - the layer of the Sun's atmosphere between the photosphere and the corona

Etymology

From Latin corona (garland, crown), referring to its crown-like appearance.
