budgerigar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdʒəriˌɡɑr / (AmE), / ˈbʌdʒərɪɡɑː / (BrE)
a small Australian parakeet, usually green with black and yellow markings in the wild, but bred in many colors as a pet
small Australian parakeet; pet bird

Examples

The family decided to adopt a budgerigar because of its vibrant colors and friendly nature.
Wild budgerigars are often seen flying in large flocks across the Australian outback.
He taught his budgerigar to mimic simple words and phrases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parakeet - small slender parrots with long tails

Etymology

From Australian Aboriginal Yuwaalaraay 'buda-rigaa' (a type of bird).
