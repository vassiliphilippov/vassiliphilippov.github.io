to embattle

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbætəl / (AmE), / ɛmˈbætəl / (BrE)
to fortify a structure, especially by equipping it with battlements for defense
fortify with battlements
archaic

Examples

The king ordered his soldiers to embattle the city walls against the advancing army.
During the 14th century, many castles were embattled to withstand long sieges.
Architects would sometimes embattle even churches, preparing them for possible attack.

Synset Examples

an embattled castle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works
  • Antonym: to demilitarize - to remove military forces or defenses from a place

Etymology

From Old French 'embataillier', from 'bataille' (battle).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbætəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈbatl / (BrE)
to prepare or arrange (troops, a place, etc.) for battle or conflict; to fortify for defense or confrontation
prepare for battle or conflict

Examples

The general ordered his troops to embattle the city walls before dawn.
As tensions rose, the villagers began to embattle their homes for the impending attack.
The nation had to embattle its borders in response to the threat of invasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works; synonym for embattle

Etymology

From Old French 'embatailler' (to prepare for battle), from 'en-' + 'bataille' (battle).
