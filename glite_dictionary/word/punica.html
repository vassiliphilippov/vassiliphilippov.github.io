Punica

1. PROPN

/ ˈpjuː.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Punicaceae, including the pomegranate.
genus of plants including the pomegranate

Examples

The genus Punica is best known for its edible fruit, the pomegranate.
Researchers studied the flowering habits of Punica species in Mediterranean climates.
The ancient Romans cultivated Punica for both its fruit and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomegranate - a fruit-bearing shrub of the genus Punica

Etymology

From Latin Punica (pomegranate), referring to Carthage (‘Punic’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpjuːnɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Punicaceae, including the pomegranate.
genus of flowering plants (pomegranate)

Examples

The genus Punica is best known for the pomegranate, an ancient fruit cultivated worldwide.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the Punica genus growing in the wild.
Studies of Punica have contributed to our understanding of drought-resistant plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'Punica', referring to Carthaginian (Punic) origin of the pomegranate.
