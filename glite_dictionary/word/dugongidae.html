Dugongidae

1. NOUN

/ duːˈɡɑn.dɪˌdi / (AmE), / djuːˈɡɒn.dɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic mammals in the order Sirenia, which includes dugongs and the extinct Steller's sea cow
family of aquatic mammals including dugongs

Examples

Scientists have classified the dugong in the family Dugongidae.
The extinction of Steller's sea cow marked the loss of a unique member of Dugongidae.
The order Sirenia comprises two families: Dugongidae and Trichechidae.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Dugongidae, based on the genus name Dugong and the suffix -idae (denoting zoological family).

2. NOUN

/ duːˌɡɑːnˈdʒɪdiː / (AmE), / djuːˌɡɒnˈdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic, herbivorous mammals in the order Sirenia, including dugongs and the extinct Steller's sea cow
family of aquatic mammals (dugongs, Steller's sea cow)

Examples

The Dugongidae family includes both living dugongs and extinct Steller's sea cows.
Scientists study the Dugongidae to understand the evolution of marine mammals.
Members of the Dugongidae are distinguished by their unique skull structure and herbivorous diet.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Dugongidae, formed from the genus name Dugong and the suffix -idae used for animal families.
