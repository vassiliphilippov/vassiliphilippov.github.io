applauder

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɔdər / (AmE), / əˈplɔːdə / (BrE)
a person who expresses approval or praise by clapping their hands
someone who applauds

Examples

The enthusiastic applauder caught the performer's attention from the stage.
Every speech was met by at least one loud applauder in the crowd.
At the ceremony, each award recipient had their own dedicated applauder among the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectator - a person who watches at a show, game, or other event
  • Antonym: booer - a person who expresses disapproval by booing

Etymology

From 'applaud' + '-er', forming an agent noun.
