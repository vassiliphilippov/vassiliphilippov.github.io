Panicum capillare

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænɪkəm kəˈpɪlərˌi / (AmE), / ˌpænɪkəm kəˈpɪləri / (BrE)
A North American grass species with slender, brushy flower clusters, often found as a weed on cultivated land.
species of North American grass, a common weed

Examples

In many cornfields across the Midwest, Panicum capillare can be a troublesome weed.
The slender panicles of Panicum capillare give it a distinctive, brushy appearance.
Farmers often remove Panicum capillare to protect their crops from competition.

Etymology

From Latin: 'panicum' (panic grass genus) + 'capillare' (hair-like, referring to its fine panicles).
