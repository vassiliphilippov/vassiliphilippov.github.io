willet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪlɪt / (BrE)
a large North American shorebird with grayish plumage and a distinctive loud call, found along the eastern and Gulf coasts
large North American shorebird

Examples

A group of willets waded along the shoreline searching for food.
During migration, willets gather in large flocks near coastal marshes.
The loud call of the willet is often heard before the bird is seen.

Etymology

From American French 'willet', imitation of its call.
