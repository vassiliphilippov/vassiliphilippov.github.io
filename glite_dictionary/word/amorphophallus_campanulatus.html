Amorphophallus campanulatus

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɔr.foʊˈfæl.əs ˌkæm.pəˈnju.leɪ.təs / (AmE), / əˌmɔː.fəˈfæl.əs ˌkæm.pəˈnjuː.læt.əs / (BrE)
A species of large, putrid-smelling aroid native to southeastern Asia, especially the Philippines, cultivated for its edible tuber.
a southeast Asian edible aroid plant

Examples

The tuber of Amorphophallus campanulatus is often used as a vegetable in Southeast Asian cuisine.
Farmers cultivate Amorphophallus campanulatus for its edible tubers and starchy roots.
Because of its strong odor, Amorphophallus campanulatus is sometimes called the 'stinky yam' in local markets.

Etymology

From Greek 'amorphos' (shapeless) + 'phallos' (phallus), referring to the plant's inflorescence; 'campanulatus' from Latin 'campanula' (little bell).
