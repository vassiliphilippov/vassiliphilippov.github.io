entrenchment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt / (BrE)
a defensive military position or fortification protected by trenches
military fortification with trenches

Examples

The soldiers built a strong entrenchment around their camp to defend against attack.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a nineteenth-century entrenchment near the battlefield.
During the siege, the army relied on a network of entrenchments for protection and cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortification - a military construction for defense

Etymology

From French 'entrenchment' (aménagement de tranchées), based on 'trench'.
