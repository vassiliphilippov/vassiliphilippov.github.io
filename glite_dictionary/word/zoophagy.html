zoophagy

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˈɑːfədʒi / (AmE), / zəʊˈɒfəɡi / (BrE)
The practice or habit of an animal eating other animals; carnivorousness.
animal's eating of other animals
medical

Examples

Some parasites exhibit zoophagy, feeding exclusively on animal hosts to survive.
The evolution of zoophagy has led to several adaptations in predators' teeth and digestive systems.
Researchers have discovered a unique pattern of zoophagy among certain tropical insect species.

Synset Examples

The too obvious fact that a large portion of animals are carnivorous neither proves nor justifies the carnivorousness of the human species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnivory - the practice of eating meat
  • Antonym: herbivory - the practice of eating plants

Etymology

From Greek zōion (animal) + -phagy (eating).
