alloyed

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈlɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a metal) mixed with another, often inferior, substance to reduce its purity or value.
(of metal) mixed with inferior element

Examples

The gold in this ring is alloyed with copper to increase its strength.
Coins are often alloyed to make them more durable and less expensive.
The engineer explained that alloyed metals can lose some of their original value.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unalloyed - (of a metal) not mixed with any other substance; pure

Etymology

From 'alloy' (blend of metals), from Old French 'aloi', from Latin 'alligare' (to bind).

2. ADJ

/ əˈlɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈlɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a metal) combined with one or more other elements to improve or alter its properties.
combined with other metals

Examples

The alloyed steel was chosen for its strength and durability.
Coins are often made from alloyed metals to prevent corrosion.
By using alloyed copper, the manufacturer enhanced the product's conductivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unalloyed - not combined with other metals or elements

Etymology

From 'alloy' (to combine metals), from Old French 'aloi', from Latin 'alligare' (to bind).
