Rudbeckia laciniata

1. PROPN

/ ɹʌdˈbɛkiə ləˌsɪniˈeɪtə / (AmE), / rʊdˈbɛkiə ləˌsɪniˈeɪtə / (BrE)
A tall perennial North American plant with divided leaves and large yellow flower heads with drooping rays, commonly known as cutleaf coneflower.
a tall yellow-flowered North American plant

Examples

In the wildflower meadow, the Rudbeckia laciniata stood out with its bright yellow petals.
Gardeners often plant Rudbeckia laciniata to attract butterflies and add late-summer color.
We spotted a cluster of Rudbeckia laciniata growing along the riverbank during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutleaf coneflower - Another common name for Rudbeckia laciniata, a tall perennial plant with yellow flowers

Etymology

Named for Swedish botanist Olof Rudbeck; from Latin 'laciniata' meaning 'slashed' or 'fringed', referring to the deeply cut leaves.
