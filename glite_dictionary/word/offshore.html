offshore

1. ADV

/ ˌɔfˈʃɔr / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈʃɔː / (BrE)
at a distance from the shore or land; away from the coastline.
away from shore or land

Examples

The boat drifted offshore during the night, far from the harbor lights.
Seabirds often hunt for fish several miles offshore where the water is deeper.
A storm warning was issued for vessels operating offshore.

Synset Examples

cruising three miles offshore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onshore - towards or located on the shore or land
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00141083-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔfˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒfʃɔː / (BrE)
Situated or happening at some distance from the shore, particularly in the sea.
located away from the shore

Examples

The company is exploring new offshore oil fields in the North Sea.
We spent a week on an offshore island that was only accessible by boat.
Strong winds pushed the fishing boats offshore, making their return difficult.

Synset Examples

offshore oil reserves
an offshore island

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onshore - located or happening on or near the shore

Etymology

From off + shore, meaning away from the shore.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01384266-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑfˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈʃɔː / (BrE)
(of winds) blowing from the land towards the sea.
blowing from land to sea (winds)

Examples

The surfers waited for offshore winds, which create ideal wave conditions.
Because the offshore breeze pushed the smoke away from land, the beaches remained clear.
Early morning offshore winds kept the harbor calm for the fishermen.

Synset Examples

offshore winds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onshore - (of winds) blowing from the sea towards the land

Etymology

From 'off' + 'shore', indicating movement away from the shore.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466390-a
Synset POS: a
