vena jugularis

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə dʒʌˈgjulərɪs / (AmE), / ˌveɪnə dʒuːˈgjuːlərɪs / (BrE)
Either of the large veins in the neck that return blood from the head to the heart.
vein in neck returning blood from head
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully located the vena jugularis before proceeding with the operation.
Damage to the vena jugularis can lead to rapid blood loss and requires immediate attention.
In anatomy class, we studied the pathway of the vena jugularis in the human neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jugular vein - the more common English term for the same anatomical structure

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) + 'jugularis' (of the throat or neck).
