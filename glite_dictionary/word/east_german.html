East German

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈdʒɝːmən / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the former republic of East Germany
person from former East Germany

Examples

After the reunification, many East Germans moved to western cities in search of new opportunities.
My neighbor is an East German who remembers life before the Berlin Wall came down.
The struggles faced by East Germans after 1990 are documented in several important books.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West German - a native or inhabitant of the former West Germany

2. ADJ

/ iːst ˈdʒɜːrmən / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of East Germany or its people, especially during the period when it was a separate state (1949–1990)
relating to East Germany or its people

Examples

Many East German athletes competed in the 1988 Seoul Olympics.
He grew up under the strict education system of the East German regime.
The East German border guards were known for their strictness.
