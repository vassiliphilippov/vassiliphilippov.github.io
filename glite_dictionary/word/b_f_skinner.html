B. F. Skinner

1. PROPN

/ ˌbiː ˈɛf ˈskɪnər / (AmE), / ˌbiː ˈɛf ˈskɪnə / (BrE)
(1904–1990) United States psychologist and leading advocate of behaviorism, known for his work on operant conditioning.
American psychologist; leading behaviorist

Examples

B. F. Skinner developed theories that transformed the field of psychology.
Many textbooks discuss the impact of B. F. Skinner on behavioral science.
The concept of operant conditioning is closely associated with B. F. Skinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behaviorism - the theory that human and animal behavior can be explained in terms of conditioning
