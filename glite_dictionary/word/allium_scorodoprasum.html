Allium scorodoprasum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm skəˌroʊdəˈpræsəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm skɒˌrəʊdəʊˈprɑːsəm / (BrE)
A species of European wild leek, cultivated and used similarly to common leeks for culinary purposes.
species of European wild leek

Examples

In the herb garden, Allium scorodoprasum grows alongside onions and garlic.
Chefs sometimes use Allium scorodoprasum as a milder alternative to regular leeks in their recipes.
The purple-flowering Allium scorodoprasum adds a subtle flavor to traditional European dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leek - An edible plant of the onion family, with a long white stem and green leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'Allium' (garlic) and 'scorodoprasum' (garlic-leek, from Greek 'skorodon' garlic + 'prason' leek).
