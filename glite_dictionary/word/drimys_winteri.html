Drimys winteri

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪmɪs ˈwɪntəri / (AmE), / ˈdrɪmɪs ˈwɪntəri / (BrE)
A South American evergreen tree that produces winter's bark and a light, soft wood similar to basswood.
South American evergreen tree, winter's bark source

Examples

The bark of Drimys winteri was once used as a remedy against scurvy.
Botanists recently discovered a mature Drimys winteri in the rainforest.
The wood from Drimys winteri is valued for its softness and lightness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter's bark - the aromatic bark of Drimys winteri, formerly used to prevent scurvy

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'drimys' meaning 'acrid, pungent'; species after William Winter, said to have discovered its anti-scurvy properties.
