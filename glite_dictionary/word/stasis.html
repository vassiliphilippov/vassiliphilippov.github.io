stasis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪsɪs, ˈstæ- / (AmE), / ˈsteɪsɪs, ˈstæ- / (BrE)
A condition in which the normal flow of a liquid, especially blood or other bodily fluids, is slowed or stopped.
abnormal slowing or stoppage of flow
medical

Examples

The patient developed stasis in the leg veins, increasing the risk of a blood clot.
Chronic heart failure can result in pulmonary stasis due to poor circulation.
Doctors monitored for signs of stasis after the operation to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congestion - accumulation of fluid or blood causing swelling or blockage

Etymology

From Greek *stasis* (a standing still, stopping), from *histanai* (to stand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪsɪs, ˈstæsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪsɪs, ˈstɑːsɪs / (BrE)
a state in which there is little or no change, often due to a balance of opposing forces
state of little or no change

Examples

The negotiations reached a stasis, with neither side willing to compromise.
After years of conflict, the region settled into a stagnant stasis of tension.
Economic reform was halted, resulting in political stasis for over a decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equilibrium - a state of balance between opposing forces

Etymology

From Greek stasis (standing, stopping, civil strife).
