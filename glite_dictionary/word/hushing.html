hushing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a fricative, typically sibilant sound made to signal disapproval or to quiet someone
a fricative sound expressing disapproval or for quieting

Examples

A sudden hushing filled the auditorium when the speaker began.
The hushing from the crowd silenced the rowdy group in the back.
The teacher noticed the persistent hushing as students tried to concentrate.

Synset Examples

the performers could not be heard over the hissing of the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hissing - a sharp sibilant sound, often used for disapproval
