proximate

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːksəmət / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪmət / (BrE)
nearest in space, time, order, or relationship; closest, especially in a chain of events or causes.
nearest in space, time, or order

Examples

The proximate cause of the accident was a faulty brake.
Scientists focused on the proximate factors influencing animal behavior.
Her proximate goal was to finish her degree before moving abroad.

Synset Examples

news of his proximate arrival
interest in proximate rather than ultimate goals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ultimate - final or farthest in a sequence; the last or most distant point
  • Synonym: nearest - most close in space or time; closest

Etymology

From Latin proximatus, past participle of proximare (to approach), from proximus (nearest).

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːk.sɪ.mət / (AmE), / ˈprɒk.sɪ.mət / (BrE)
very near in space or time; closest in position or occurrence.
very near in space or time

Examples

The two buildings have proximate entrances, making it easy to move between them.
The proximate cause of the confusion was a poorly worded email.
The store is proximate to the library, so we can visit both easily.

Synset Examples

proximate words
proximate houses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediate - nearest in time or relationship
  • Antonym: remote - far away in space or time

Etymology

From Latin 'prōximātus', past participle of 'prōximāre' (to come near), from 'proximus' (nearest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00452181-s
Synset POS: s
