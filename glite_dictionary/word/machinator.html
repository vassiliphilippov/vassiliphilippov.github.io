machinator

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.ɪˌneɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ɪˌneɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who schemes or plots, especially one involved in a conspiracy
plotter; conspirator

Examples

The detective suspected there was a skilled machinator lurking behind the scenes.
History is filled with stories of machinators attempting to overthrow governments.
He was revealed as the chief machinator behind the corporate scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspirator - a person who takes part in a conspiracy
  • Synonym: plotter - a person who secretly makes plans, usually to do something harmful

Etymology

From Latin machinator, from machinari (to contrive, plot).
