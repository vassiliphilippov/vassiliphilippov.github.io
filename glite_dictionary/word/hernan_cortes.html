Hernan Cortes

1. PROPN

/ erˈnɑn kɔrˈteɪs / (AmE), / ɛˈɾnan kɔːˈteɪs / (BrE)
(1485–1547) A Spanish conquistador who led the expedition that resulted in the fall of the Aztec Empire and the conquest of Mexico.
Spanish conquistador; defeated Aztecs; conquered Mexico

Examples

Many historians regard Hernan Cortes as one of the most influential figures in the history of the Americas.
The conquest led by Hernan Cortes dramatically changed the course of Mexican history.
Some accounts describe Hernan Cortes negotiating alliances with indigenous peoples to overthrow the Aztec Empire.
