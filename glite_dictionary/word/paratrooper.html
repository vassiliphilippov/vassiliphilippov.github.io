paratrooper

1. NOUN

/ ˈpær.əˌtruː.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈpær.əˌtruː.pə / (BrE)
a soldier who is trained to parachute into combat or military operations
soldier trained to parachute into combat

Examples

The paratrooper landed behind enemy lines during the operation.
My grandfather served as a paratrooper in World War II.
Hundreds of paratroopers jumped from the planes at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachutist - a person who jumps from aircraft with a parachute

Etymology

Blend of 'parachute' and 'trooper', first used in World War II.
