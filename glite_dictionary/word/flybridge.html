flybridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, often open deck above the pilothouse on a ship, used for navigation and steering
top navigational deck on a ship

Examples

With a commanding view from the flybridge, the captain steered the yacht through the narrow channel.
We enjoyed cocktails on the flybridge as the sun set over the ocean.
The vessel’s flybridge is equipped with state-of-the-art navigation technology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pilothouse - enclosed area from which a ship is steered

Etymology

From 'fly' (to move in the air; high) + 'bridge' (structure for navigation control).
