complex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pleks / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) a group of repressed emotions, thoughts, and desires that unconsciously influence a person's behavior.
psychological pattern of repressed feelings

Examples

Her inferiority complex made social interactions stressful and difficult.
The therapist believed that childhood trauma had contributed to his guilt complex.
He overcame his fear complex through years of counseling and self-reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurosis - a mild mental illness involving stress and anxiety, but not delusions or hallucinations

Etymology

From Latin complexus (embrace, connection), from complecti (to entwine, embrace).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmplɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
a group of interconnected or related buildings considered as a single whole
group of related buildings

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - an enclosed area with buildings, often related in purpose

Etymology

From Latin *complexus* (embraced, comprehensive), past participle of *complecti* (to entwine together, embrace).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːm.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.plɛks / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A chemical compound in which a central atom, usually a metal, is bonded to a surrounding group of molecules or ions.
chemical compound with central atom and ligands

Examples

The scientist synthesized a nickel complex with four ammonia ligands.
Many transition metals form colored complexes with organic compounds.
The stability of a coordination complex depends on several factors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordination compound - a chemical compound containing a central atom bonded to surrounding ligands

Etymology

From Latin complexus (entwined), from complecti (to entwine, embrace), reflecting the binding of atoms.
