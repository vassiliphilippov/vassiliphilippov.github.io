frolic

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪk / (BrE)
to play or move about energetically and cheerfully, often in a lively or playful way
to play energetically and cheerfully

Examples

The puppies frolic in the yard every afternoon, chasing each other around the trees.
Tourists frolicked on the beach, splashing in the waves and laughing in the sun.
Children love to frolic in the snow after the first big winter storm.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romp - to play or move about in a lively, carefree way
  • Synonym: gambol - to run and jump about playfully

Etymology

From Dutch *vrolijk* (joyful, cheerful).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪk / (BrE)
a playful or lively activity done for amusement or fun
light-hearted playful activity

Examples

The children's laughter echoed during their frolic in the park.
After a long week of work, she enjoyed a carefree frolic by the sea.
The picnic turned into a delightful frolic as everyone joined in the games.

Synset Examples

it was all done in play
their frolic in the surf threatened to become ugly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play - recreational fun activity
  • Synonym: romp - lively or boisterous play

Etymology

From Dutch 'vrolijk' meaning 'joyful, merry'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00512828-n
Synset POS: n
