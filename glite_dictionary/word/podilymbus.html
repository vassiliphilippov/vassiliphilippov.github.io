Podilymbus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈlɪmbəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɪˈlɪmbəs / (BrE)
A genus of grebes in the family Podicipedidae, commonly including the pied-billed grebe.
genus of grebes (birds)

Examples

The pied-billed grebe, belonging to the genus Podilymbus, is widespread in North America.
Scientists observed a Podilymbus species nesting near the edge of the lake.
Within the family Podicipedidae, Podilymbus is known for its distinctive lobed feet.

Etymology

From Latinized Neo-Latin, likely formed from Greek 'pous' (foot) and 'kolymbos' (diver).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdɪˈlɪmbəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɪˈlɪmbəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Podicipedidae, consisting of small grebes found chiefly in the Americas.
genus of grebes in Podicipedidae

Examples

The Podilymbus genus includes the well-known pied-billed grebe.
Researchers studied the distribution of Podilymbus species across North America.
Only two species are recognized under the Podilymbus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - aquatic diving bird of the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Latin podicis (rump) + Ancient Greek limbos (fringe or border), referring to the bird's body shape.
