spinose

1. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪnoʊs / (AmE), / ˈspɪnəʊs / (BrE)
having spines or thorn-like projections.
having spines or thorns

Examples

The cactus has spinose stems that deter herbivores.
Many marine animals, like sea urchins, have spinose shells for protection.
Some insects develop spinose legs as a defensive adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - bearing or covered with spines

Etymology

From Latin 'spinosus' (full of thorns), from 'spina' (thorn).
