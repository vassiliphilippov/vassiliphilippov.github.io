vulture

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a person who preys on others, especially in a ruthless or exploitative way, often seeking to profit from their misfortune
ruthlessly exploitative person

Examples

After the company went bankrupt, financial vultures swooped in to buy its assets at a bargain.
The paparazzi acted like vultures, crowding around the grieving family for photographs.
Some people become vultures in a crisis, looking for ways to take advantage of others' misfortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predator - a person or animal that preys on others
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or support

Etymology

From Latin 'vultur' (vulture), applied metaphorically to describe predatory people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large bird of prey with a bald head and weak claws that feeds mainly on carrion
large carrion-eating bird of prey

Examples

A vulture soared high above the savannah, searching for its next meal.
After spotting a carcass, the vulture descended quickly to feed.
The documentary showed how vultures help keep ecosystems clean by eating dead animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzzard - a type of large broad-winged bird of prey
  • Synonym: eagle - a large bird of prey with a strong beak and keen vision

Etymology

From Late Latin 'vultur' or 'volture', meaning 'a plucker' (because it plucks meat from carcasses).
