corruptness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌptnəs / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking integrity or honesty, especially through willingness to act dishonestly for personal gain
state of being corrupt or dishonest

Examples

The widespread corruptness within the organization led to severe consequences.
Many citizens were disillusioned by the corruptness of their political leaders.
She spoke out against the corruptness that undermined the fairness of the process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power

Etymology

From 'corrupt' + '-ness', where 'corrupt' comes from Latin 'corruptus' (spoiled, corrupt).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌptnəs / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally depraved or dishonest, especially involving bribery or the violation of rules
the state of being corrupt

Examples

The corruptness of the administration was exposed by the public inquiry.
She could not tolerate the corruptness that plagued the organization.
The company's corruptness eventually led to numerous legal troubles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power
  • Antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles

Etymology

From Latin 'corruptus' (spoiled, broken), influenced by the English suffix '-ness'.
