Saktism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːktɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃæktɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A Hindu religious tradition devoted to the worship of Shakti, the divine feminine power.
Hindu sect worshipping Shakti

Examples

Devotees of Saktism gather annually to celebrate the goddess Durga.
Many temples in India are centers of Saktism and rituals honoring the feminine divine.
The influence of Saktism can be seen in various art forms and literature throughout South Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaktism - alternative spelling of Saktism

Etymology

From Sanskrit śakti (power, energy) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːktɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃæktɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The worship of the goddess Shakti as the divine consort of Shiva, especially as practiced in certain Hindu traditions.
worship of Shakti as Shiva's consort in Hinduism

Examples

Many devotees practice Saktism as a path to spiritual enlightenment.
The rituals associated with Saktism honor Shakti as the creative energy of the universe.
Scholars have written extensively about the influence of Saktism on Indian culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaktism - worship of Shakti as the supreme deity in Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Śākta' (worshipper of Shakti), from 'Śakti' (the divine feminine power).
