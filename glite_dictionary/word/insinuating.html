insinuating

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪnjʊeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is intended to please or win someone’s favor, often in a subtle or sly manner
attempting to win favor slyly

Examples

He adopted an insinuating tone, hoping to win her trust.
Her insinuating smile made everyone a little uneasy.
With an insinuating manner, he quickly gained favor with the new manager.

Synset Examples

a smooth ingratiating manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingratiating - trying to win favor or approval by pleasing others

Etymology

From Latin insinuare (to bring in, make one's way into).
