Storeria occipitamaculata

1. NOUN

/ stoʊˈrɪəriə ɑkˌsɪpɪtəˌmækjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / stəˈrɪəriə ɒkˌsɪpɪtəˌmækjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A harmless woodland snake of the southeastern United States, also known as the red-bellied snake.
a harmless woodland snake (red-bellied snake)

Examples

The Storeria occipitamaculata often hides under logs and leaf litter in woodlands.
Herpetologists frequently study the Storeria occipitamaculata due to its unique coloration and docile nature.
The Storeria occipitamaculata is commonly known as the red-bellied snake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-bellied snake - common name for Storeria occipitamaculata

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Storeria (genus named for American naturalist David H. Storer) and Latin occipitamaculata ('spotted at the back of the head': occiput + maculata).
