scarred

1. ADJ

/ skɑrd / (AmE), / skɑːd / (BrE)
marked or damaged by scars, injuries, or rough use, often leaving visible marks or blemishes.
marked by scars or damage

Examples

The old oak desk was scarred by years of use and careless handling.
His face remained scarred after the accident, but he wore it with pride.
The once-beautiful mural was badly scarred by graffiti and weather damage.

Synset Examples

the scarred piano bench
walls marred by graffiti

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unblemished - not marked or damaged; without any scars
  • Synonym: marred - damaged or spoiled to a certain extent

Etymology

From Middle English 'skar' (scar) with past participle ending '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ skɑrd / (AmE), / skɑːd / (BrE)
Deeply affected or marked by physical injury or emotional pain.
marked by injury or emotional trauma

Examples

Even years later, he remained scarred by his experiences in the war.
The accident left her leg scarred for life.
He has a scarred past that still troubles him.

Synset Examples

Could her scarred mind ever be free of fear?
a face scarred by anxiety
the fire left her arm badly scarred

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmarked - not marked or damaged
  • Synonym: damaged - impaired or harmed in structure or function

Etymology

From the noun 'scar' (mark left by a healed wound) + -ed suffix forming adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01483991-s
Synset POS: s
