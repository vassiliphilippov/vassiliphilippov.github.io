bugaboo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡəˌbu / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡəbuː / (BrE)
A cause of persistent fear, worry, or irritation, often an imagined threat or source of anxiety.
source of persistent fear or worry

Examples

For many investors, the possibility of recession is a real bugaboo.
High taxes are often cited as a political bugaboo by certain groups.
The bugaboo of public speaking makes some people nervous for weeks in advance.

Synset Examples

the old bugaboo of inflation still bothers them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogeyman - an imaginary evil spirit or frightening figure, often used to threaten children
  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm

Etymology

Probably from obsolete bugbear (an imaginary creature) and boo (used to startle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡəˌbu / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡəbuː / (BrE)
an imaginary evil creature or monster, often used to frighten children; a source of needless fear or anxiety
imaginary monster; needless fear

Examples

The old stories warned children that a bugaboo would come for them if they misbehaved.
For years, climate change was treated like a distant bugaboo rather than a pressing concern.
She told her little brother there was no such thing as a bugaboo hiding under the bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogeyman - an imaginary evil spirit or monster used to frighten children

Etymology

Probably from obsolete bugbear (object of dread), influenced by 'boo' (as a cry to frighten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09511247-n
Synset POS: n
