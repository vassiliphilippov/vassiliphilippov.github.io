corpus mamillare

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.pəs məˈmɪl.əˌri / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pəs məˈmɪl.əˌreɪ / (BrE)
either of two small round structures on the underside of the brain that serve as terminals of the anterior arches of the fornix
structure at base of the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the corpus mamillare can result in memory disorders.
The neurologist pointed to the corpus mamillare on the diagram during the lecture.
MRI scans revealed atrophy in the patient's corpus mamillare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammillary body - another term for corpus mamillare, small brain structures involved in memory

Etymology

Latin; 'corpus' meaning 'body' and 'mamillare' meaning 'related to the nipple/teat', referring to their shape.
