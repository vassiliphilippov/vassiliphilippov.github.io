etching

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A metal plate decorated with a design that has been incised using acid, commonly used for producing prints.
etched plate for making prints

Examples

The museum displayed a rare etching by Rembrandt in its new gallery.
Careful handling is required due to the fragile nature of the copper etching.
She purchased an etching from the local artist to hang in her living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a plate incised by hand for printing

Etymology

From Dutch *etsen*, meaning 'to eat (away), corrode,' referring to the use of acid in the process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of creating designs on a surface by using acid to cut into the parts of a metal plate, or a print made from such a plate
art of using acid to engrave and print designs

Examples

He collects rare etchings from 19th-century artists.
The museum displayed an etching of a rural landscape.
Students learned how to make an etching using copper plates and acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - the process or art of cutting or carving a design on a hard surface

Etymology

From Dutch 'etsen', meaning to eat (into) by acid.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a print made from a metal plate that has been etched with acid to produce a design
print made from an etched plate

Examples

The museum displayed an etching by Rembrandt in its main gallery.
She purchased a beautiful etching of a cityscape from a local artist.
The etching showed incredible detail in the shading and lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from a design cut into a surface

Etymology

From Dutch 'etsen', from German 'ätzen' (to etch, to corrode).
