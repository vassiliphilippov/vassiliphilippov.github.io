juiceless

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːsləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːslɪs / (BrE)
(of food, especially fruit) lacking juice; dry or without moisture.
lacking juice; dry

Examples

The orange looked fresh but turned out to be juiceless and disappointing.
After several hours in the oven, the meat became tough and juiceless.
The summer drought left most of the berries shriveled and juiceless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry - lacking moisture; not wet or juicy

Etymology

Formed from 'juice' + '-less'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːsləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːslɪs / (BrE)
Lacking interest or vitality; dull, lifeless, or uninspiring.
dull; lacking interest or vitality

Examples

Many readers found the novel juiceless and abandoned it after a few chapters.
The meeting was disappointingly juiceless, with no exciting announcements or new ideas.
Her speech felt juiceless compared to the energetic presentation that followed.

Synset Examples

a dry book
a dry lecture filled with trivial details
dull and juiceless as only book knowledge can be when it is unrelated to … life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Synonym: lifeless - without energy or vitality
  • Antonym: interesting - arousing curiosity or attention

Etymology

From 'juice' + '-less', meaning 'without juice'; used metaphorically for lack of liveliness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02315409-s
Synset POS: s
