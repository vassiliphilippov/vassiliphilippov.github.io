monoreme

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌriːm / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌriːm / (BrE)
an ancient galley with a single bank of oars on each side
ancient galley with one bank of oars

Examples

The Greek navy deployed a monoreme in coastal patrol duties during the early classical era.
Unlike the trireme, the monoreme was simpler in construction and required fewer rowers.
Historians discovered remnants of a monoreme near the ancient harbor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bireme - a galley with two banks of oars
  • Degree Higher: trireme - an ancient galley with three banks of oars

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'erēma' (row, oar).
