Gwyn ap Nudd

1. PROPN

/ ɡwɪn æp nɪð / (AmE), / ˈɡwɪn ap ˈnɪð / (BrE)
(in Welsh mythology) a god and ruler of the Otherworld, associated with the underworld and often regarded as the leader of the wild hunt.
Welsh underworld god

Examples

According to legend, Gwyn ap Nudd leads a band of spectral hounds in the night sky.
In Welsh folklore, Gwyn ap Nudd is known as a guardian of the underworld.
The story of Gwyn ap Nudd is central to many ancient Celtic myths.

Etymology

From Welsh 'Gwyn' (white, blessed) and 'ap Nudd' (son of Nudd, a mythological figure).
