unwitting

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
not aware of what is really happening; not intended or deliberate
not aware; unintentional

Examples

She became an unwitting participant in the fraud.
His unwitting remark offended the guests without him realizing it.
They served as unwitting accomplices in the plan.

Synset Examples

he was completely ignorant of the circumstances
an unknowledgeable assistant
his rudeness was unwitting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking awareness or knowledge
  • Antonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate

Etymology

From Old English 'witan' (to know), with prefix 'un-' meaning 'not'.

2. ADJ

not aware of what is really happening; done without intending or knowing
not aware; unintentional

Examples

He was an unwitting accomplice to the crime.

Synset Examples

an unwitting subject in an experiment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general

Etymology

From Old English 'un-' (not) + 'wit' (knowledge, understanding).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
not done deliberately; not intended or planned
not deliberate or intentional

Examples

She made an unwitting error while filling out the form.
His unwitting remark caused unintended offense among his colleagues.
The journalist became an unwitting participant in the controversial event.

Synset Examples

an unintentional pun
the offense was unintentional
an unwitting mistake may be overlooked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Old English 'unwittig' (unwise, ignorant), from 'un-' (not) + 'wittig' (wise, knowing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01342529-s
Synset POS: s
