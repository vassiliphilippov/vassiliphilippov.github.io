cricket

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪk.ət / (AmE), / ˈkrɪk.ɪt / (BrE)
A team game played with a ball and bat by two teams of eleven players, who take turns batting to score runs while the other team fields.
bat-and-ball team sport

Examples

Many countries, especially in the Commonwealth, are passionate about cricket.
Rain often interrupts matches during the English cricket season.
The rules of cricket can seem complicated to newcomers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseball - A bat-and-ball game played between two teams, similar in some aspects to cricket

Etymology

From Old French *criquet* (goal post, stick) or Middle Dutch *krick(e)* (stick, staff).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪkət / (AmE), / ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a small jumping insect of the family Gryllidae, the male of which produces a characteristic chirping sound by rubbing its forewings together
jumping insect that chirps

Examples

A cricket sang loudly outside my window all night.
Children tried to catch a cricket in the garden for their bug collection.
The male cricket produces sound by rubbing its wings together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - a plant-eating insect with long hind legs for jumping
  • Synonym: locust - a large, often swarming grasshopper

Etymology

From Old French *criquet*, probably of imitative origin.
