plutonium

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈtoʊniəm / (AmE), / pluːˈtəʊniəm / (BrE)
a dense, silvery radioactive metallic element with the symbol Pu and atomic number 94, used especially as a fuel in nuclear reactors and weapons
radioactive element, symbol Pu, atomic number 94

Examples

Scientists handled the plutonium with extreme caution due to its radioactivity.
The discovery of plutonium revolutionized the development of nuclear energy and weapons.
Only tiny amounts of plutonium are found naturally; most is produced in reactors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: uranium - a radioactive metallic element with atomic number 92, used as fuel in nuclear reactors and weapons
  • Degree Higher: americium - a radioactive metallic element with atomic number 95, following plutonium in the periodic table

Etymology

From Pluto, the planet, with '-ium' used for elements; discovered shortly after the planet Pluto.

2. NOUN

/ pluˈtoʊniəm / (AmE), / pluːˈtəʊniəm / (BrE)
a radioactive silvery-grey chemical element of the actinide series, used as a fuel in nuclear reactors and weapons (symbol: Pu)
radioactive chemical element (Pu)

Examples

The scientists handled the plutonium with extreme care due to its radioactivity.
Nuclear reactors often use plutonium as a fuel source for generating energy.
Traces of plutonium were found in the laboratory after the experiment.

Etymology

From Pluto, the planet, with the chemical suffix -ium, named after the planet discovered shortly before the element.
