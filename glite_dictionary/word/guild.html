guild

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪld / (AmE), / ɡɪld / (BrE)
an organization of people with similar interests or occupations, especially one formed for mutual aid or the pursuit of a common aim
organization of people with shared interests

Examples

The artists’ guild provided support and resources to its members.
In medieval times, each trade had its own guild to regulate standards and training.
She joined an online guild dedicated to historical reenactment enthusiasts.

Synset Examples

he joined a golf club
they formed a small lunch society
men from the fraternal order will staff the soup kitchen today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: association - a group of people organized for a joint purpose
  • Synonym: society - an organized group of people with a common purpose or interest
  • Synonym: club - an organization for people with a particular interest or activity

Etymology

From Old English *gild* (association, society), from Proto-Germanic *geldam* (payment, tribute).
