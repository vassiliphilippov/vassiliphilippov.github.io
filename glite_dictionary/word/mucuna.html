Mucuna

1. NOUN

/ muːˈkuːnə / (AmE), / mjuːˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical herbs and woody vines with trifoliate leaves and showy flowers in axillary clusters, found in warm regions.
genus of tropical herbs and vines

Examples

The Mucuna genus includes several species used in traditional medicine.
Researchers are studying the properties of Mucuna for its high L-DOPA content.
Some Mucuna species are grown as cover crops in tropical agriculture.

Etymology

From Latinized form of a name used in the Tupi-Guarani languages of South America.
