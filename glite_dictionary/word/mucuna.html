mucuna

1. NOUN

/ muːˈkuːnə / (AmE), / mjuːˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
Any of several erect or climbing woody plants of the genus Mucuna, widespread in tropical regions of both the Old and New World.
woody climbing plant of genus Mucuna

Examples

The seeds of the mucuna plant are used in traditional herbal medicine.
Some species of mucuna can be found climbing high into the forest canopy.
Farmers cultivate mucuna to improve soil fertility in tropical regions.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Mucuna', the genus name.
