nanometre

1. NOUN

/ ˈnænoʊˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈnanəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a metre (1 × 10⁻⁹ metres), used especially to measure wavelengths and microscopic distances
one billionth of a metre, metric unit of length

Examples

The wavelength of visible light ranges from about 400 to 700 nanometres.
A virus typically measures only a few hundred nanometres in diameter.
Modern computer chips contain features just a few nanometres wide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanometer - a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (US spelling)

Etymology

From nano- (a factor meaning 'one billionth') + metre, following the metric system conventions.
