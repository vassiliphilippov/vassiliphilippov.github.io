unwavering

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈweɪvɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈweɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
not changing or becoming weaker in determination, faith, or support; steady and resolute.
firm and steady; resolute

Examples

She maintained her unwavering belief in his innocence throughout the trial.
His unwavering support helped the team succeed through difficult times.
Despite criticism, the mayor's unwavering commitment to reform inspired many citizens.

Synset Examples

spoke in a level voice
she gave him a level look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
  • Antonym: faltering - losing strength or hesitating

Etymology

From un- (not) + wavering (from Middle English waveren, to sway or flicker).

2. ADJ

not changing or becoming weaker in purpose, belief, or support; steady and resolute.
steady and resolute; not changing

Examples

She remained unwavering in her support for the project despite criticism.
His unwavering dedication to his family inspired everyone around him.
Through hardship and success, her unwavering loyalty never faltered.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful and determined
  • Antonym: faltering - losing strength or confidence; hesitant

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'wavering', from Middle English 'waveren' (to sway).
