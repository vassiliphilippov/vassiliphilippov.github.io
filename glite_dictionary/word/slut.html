slut

1. NOUN

/ slʌt / (AmE), / slʌt / (BrE)
(Archaic) A woman who is dirty or untidy.
archaic: dirty or untidy woman
archaic

Examples

The old tale describes a slut who never cleaned her room or washed her clothes.
In some Victorian novels, the word slut simply meant a woman who was untidy rather than immoral.
She was unfairly called a slut for neglecting her household chores and appearing disheveled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slattern - a dirty or untidy woman

Etymology

Originates in Middle English, of uncertain origin; the sense of 'dirty woman' predates the offensive sexual use.

2. NOUN

/ slʌt / (AmE), / slʌt / (BrE)
A woman who is considered to have many casual sexual partners or to behave in a sexually promiscuous way; this term is highly offensive and vulgar.
offensive term for sexually promiscuous woman
offensive vulgar

Examples

He insulted her by calling her a slut, which was deeply hurtful and untrue.
Some activists are working to reclaim the word slut as a symbol of empowerment.
It's important to recognize that labeling someone as a slut is both offensive and sexist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whore - offensive term for a prostitute or promiscuous woman
  • Antonym: prude - person, especially a woman, regarded as excessively proper or modest in sexual matters

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, of uncertain origin; earlier meanings referred to a dirty or untidy woman.

3. NOUN

/ slʌt / (AmE), / slʌt / (BrE)
A woman who is considered to have many casual sexual partners or is accused of being promiscuous. Offensive.
offensive term for a promiscuous woman
offensive

Examples

Many people criticize the media for labeling women as a slut based on their clothes or behavior.
The word slut is often used offensively and can be deeply hurtful.
She was unfairly called a slut by some classmates, though her personal life was nobody's business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whore - an offensive term for a woman who has many sexual partners or engages in sex for payment
  • Antonym: prude - a person, especially a woman, considered to be excessively proper or modest

Etymology

Middle English, originally meant 'a dirty, untidy woman,' later acquiring a sexual sense.
