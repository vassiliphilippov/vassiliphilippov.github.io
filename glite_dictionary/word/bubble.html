bubble

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to form or produce bubbles, or to emit bubbles of gas or air.
form or emit bubbles

Examples

The pot of water began to bubble as it heated up on the stove.
As the chemicals reacted, the solution started to bubble vigorously.
She watched the mud bubble at the bottom of the hot spring.

Synset Examples

The soup was bubbling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boil - to reach a temperature at which bubbles form and rise to the surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'bubble', possibly imitative of the sound of bubbling.

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl / (BrE)
an economic or financial situation in which the value of assets rises rapidly to levels that are not sustainable, often due to speculative activity, and is likely to collapse suddenly
speculative financial scheme or boom

Examples

The tech bubble of the late 1990s ended with a market crash.
Some economists warn that a housing bubble could be forming in major cities.
Investors lost millions when the financial bubble suddenly burst.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crash - a sudden serious drop in the value of something, especially shares or property prices
