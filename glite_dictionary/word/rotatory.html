rotatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊtəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəˌtəri / (BrE)
of or relating to rotation; tending to rotate or cause rotation, especially around an axis.
relating to rotation or axial turning

Examples

The Earth's rotatory motion causes day and night.
Certain joints in the human body allow for rotatory movements.
The rotatory force of the motor quickly spins the fan blades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotational - relating to or resulting from rotation
  • Antonym: stationary - not moving or not capable of movement

Etymology

From Latin rotatorius (of or pertaining to a wheel or rotation).
