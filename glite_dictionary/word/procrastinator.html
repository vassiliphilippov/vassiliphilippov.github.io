procrastinator

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkræs.tɪˌneɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / prəˈkræs.tɪ.neɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who delays or postpones tasks, especially habitually or out of laziness
someone who habitually delays tasks

Examples

As a lifelong procrastinator, she often finished her assignments at the last possible minute.
The team struggled because their main planner was a notorious procrastinator.
He admitted being a procrastinator when it came to doing household chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawdler - a person who takes more time than necessary; lags behind
  • Antonym: go-getter - a person who is ambitious and energetic

Etymology

From Latin procrastinare (to put off until tomorrow), from pro- (forward) + crastinus (of tomorrow).
