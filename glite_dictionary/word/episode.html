episode

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsoʊd / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsəʊd / (BrE)
an incident or event that is distinct within a series of related happenings
distinct event in a series

Examples

The most exciting episode of the trip was our unexpected hike through the mountains.
During her career, each episode of success was followed by a new challenge.
The book describes a tragic episode in the nation's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incident - an individual occurrence or event
  • Synonym: event - something that happens, especially something important

Etymology

From Greek 'epeisodion' (additional or incidental event).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsoʊd / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsəʊd / (BrE)
an individual part of a television or radio series that is broadcast separately
part of a TV or radio series

Examples

I missed last week's episode of my favorite show, so I watched it online.
The season finale is always the most exciting episode of the series.
Each episode introduces new characters and unexpected twists in the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment - one of a series of parts in a work published or broadcast over time

Etymology

From Greek 'epeisodion', meaning 'addition' or 'incident'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsoʊd / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsəʊd / (BrE)
a section of a film or television programme forming part of a series, especially one consisting of related scenes that develop a particular subject or story
part of a film or TV series

Examples

The documentary was divided into several episodes, each focusing on a different aspect of the artist's life.
The first episode of the new series introduced all the main characters and set up the central mystery.
Each episode in the film explored a different period of the scientist's career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment - a part of a series released separately
  • Synonym: chapter - an individual section of a book or series

Etymology

From Greek 'episodion', meaning 'addition' or 'episode', originally referring to an entry in a play.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04180233-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsoʊd / (AmE), / ˈepɪsəʊd / (BrE)
a part of a story, television or radio series, or other creative work that is one event or installment within a larger connected sequence
part of a series or story

Examples

The third episode of the season revealed new secrets about the main character.
Each episode in the novel contributes to the overall mystery.
They watched an episode of their favorite comedy show before bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment - one part of a broadcast, series, or publication released at regular intervals

Etymology

From Greek 'epeisodion' (an additional entry or event), via Latin 'episodium'.
