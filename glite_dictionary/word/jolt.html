jolt

1. NOUN

/ dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
a sudden, hard shock or movement resulting from a sudden jarring impact
a sudden hard shock or movement

Examples

The train came to a sudden stop with a loud jolt.
He felt a jolt as the elevator shuddered unexpectedly.
The news gave her a jolt she hadn't anticipated.

Synset Examples

the door closed with a jolt
all the jars and jolts were smoothed out by the shock absorbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - a quick, sudden movement
  • Synonym: shock - a sudden and violent impact

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly imitative or from dialectal English 'jowl' (to knock, strike).

2. VERB

/ dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
to startle or disturb the composure of someone, often suddenly or unexpectedly
to disturb or startle someone

Examples

The dramatic news jolted everyone in the office.
Her sharp question jolted him out of his daydream.
The unexpected criticism jolted the team into reconsidering their strategy.

Synset Examples

The audience was jolted by the play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to startle - to cause someone to feel sudden shock or surprise
  • Synonym: to unsettle - to make someone feel anxious or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone relaxed or less disturbed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01766815-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with a sudden, rough, or jerky motion
move with a sudden, jerky motion

Examples

The train jolted as it went over the tracks, waking everyone in the carriage.
A sudden explosion jolted him out of his thoughts.
She jolted the chair back to life with a quick shove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jar - to shake or vibrate from a sudden impact
  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly up and down or from side to side

Etymology

Probably from obsolete jol (to knock, strike), perhaps from Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01868851-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
a sudden, forceful, or jerky movement.
a sudden, jerky movement

Examples

The bus came to a stop with a jolt, throwing passengers forward in their seats.
She felt a jolt of surprise when the car suddenly lurched.
The earthquake sent a jolt through the entire building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - a sudden sharp movement
  • Synonym: shock - a sudden, violent disturbance or impact

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the mid-16th century.
