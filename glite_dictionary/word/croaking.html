croaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
a harsh, hoarse sound or utterance, especially like that made by a frog
the hoarse sound of a frog or similar creature

Examples

The croaking of frogs echoed through the quiet swamp at dusk.
A sudden croaking from the tree startled the hikers as they passed by.
At night, the constant croaking made it hard to sleep near the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croak - to make a harsh, hoarse sound like a frog

Etymology

From the verb 'croak,' which likely imitates the sound itself.
