neurolemma

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈlɛmə / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈlɛmə / (BrE)
the thin membranous sheath surrounding a nerve fiber, also known as the neurilemma
membranous sheath around a nerve fiber
medical

Examples

The neurolemma plays an essential role in the regeneration of injured nerve fibers.
Under the microscope, the neurolemma can be seen enveloping the axon.
Damage to the neurolemma may impede proper nerve function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurilemma - the sheath surrounding a nerve fiber

Etymology

From Greek 'neuro-' (nerve) and 'lemma' (sheath).
