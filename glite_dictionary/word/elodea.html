Elodea

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈloʊdiə / (AmE), / ɪˈləʊdiə / (BrE)
a genus of submerged freshwater perennial plants, commonly found in ponds and aquariums, known for their ribbon-like leaves
a genus of aquatic freshwater plants

Examples

Aquarium owners often use Elodea to oxygenate the water and provide shelter for fish.
The pond was filled with thick strands of Elodea, waving gently with the current.
Students studied the process of photosynthesis using leaves from the Elodea plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterweed - common name for aquatic plants of the genus Elodea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'helōdēs' (marshy).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈloʊdiə / (AmE), / ɛˈləʊdɪə / (BrE)
a genus of submerged freshwater perennial aquatic plants commonly used in aquariums and scientific studies
genus of aquatic plants

Examples

Elodea is often used in classrooms to demonstrate photosynthesis because it releases oxygen bubbles underwater.
The aquarium owner added Elodea to help keep the water clean and provide shelter for fish.
Researchers collected samples of Elodea from the lake to study its effects on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - a similar aquatic plant also called tape grass

Etymology

From Greek 'helôdēs' (marshy) + '-ea', a botanical suffix.
