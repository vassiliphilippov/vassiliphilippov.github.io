vibe

1. VERB

/ vaɪb / (AmE), / vaɪb / (BrE)
to have a mutual understanding, connection, or positive feeling with someone or something; to resonate emotionally, often used informally
to feel a mutual emotional connection
informal

Examples

I just don't vibe with his sense of humor, so we rarely hang out.
They really vibed at the party and ended up talking all night.
Are you vibing with this music, or should we change it?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to form a relationship or feel affinity with someone
  • Synonym: to get along - to have a harmonious relationship
  • Antonym: clash - to be in conflict or not get along

Etymology

Shortened from 'vibration,' popularized in African-American Vernacular English and 20th-century slang.

2. NOUN

/ vaɪb / (AmE), / vaɪb / (BrE)
a distinctive emotional quality or atmosphere that is sensed instinctively about a place, situation, or person
distinctive emotional atmosphere or feeling
informal

Examples

The new café had a relaxed vibe that made everyone feel welcome.
She didn't like the vibe at the party, so she decided to leave early.
His apartment gives off a creative vibe with all the artwork on the walls.

Synset Examples

that place gave me bad vibrations
it gave me a nostalgic vibe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the pervading tone or mood of a place or situation
  • Synonym: aura - a distinctive feeling or character that seems to surround something

Etymology

Shortened from 'vibration', popularized in American slang in the mid-20th century.

3. NOUN

/ vaɪb / (AmE), / vaɪb / (BrE)
a distinctive feeling, atmosphere, or mood, especially one that is positive or relaxed, associated with a place, situation, or type of music
atmosphere or mood of a place or music
informal

Examples

The café had such a welcoming vibe that we stayed for hours.
Her music creates a mellow vibe that helps people relax.
There's a fun vibe at the summer festivals every year.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the pervading tone or mood of a place, situation, or creative work

Shortened form of 'vibration', popularized in American slang in the 1960s.
