Pu

1. NOUN

/ piː juː / (AmE), / pjuː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for plutonium, a radioactive silvery-grey transuranic element used as fuel in nuclear reactors and weapons.
chemical symbol for plutonium

Examples

The isotope Pu-239 is used as fuel in many nuclear reactors.
Scientists detected traces of Pu in the soil near the test site.
Storage of Pu must be handled with extreme caution due to its radioactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plutonium - a radioactive metallic element (atomic number 94), chemical element symbol Pu

Etymology

Symbol: from the first two letters of plutonium.
