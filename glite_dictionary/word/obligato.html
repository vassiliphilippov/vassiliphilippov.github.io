obligato

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːblɪˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a musical motif or part that is persistent but subordinate, often accompanying the principal melody
persistent subordinate musical motif

Examples

In this concerto, the flute plays a beautiful obligato under the main theme.
The violinist performed an intricate obligato alongside the choir's singing.
The composer added a clarinet obligato to enrich the orchestral texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countermelody - a secondary melody played in conjunction with the main melody
  • Synonym: accompaniment - a musical part that supports or partners a solo instrument, voice, or group

Etymology

From Italian obbligato (obligatory), used in 18th-century music to mean a required or indispensable part.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːblɪˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
In music, a part that is essential and must be performed exactly as written, without change or omission, often accompanying the main melody.
essential musical part that must be performed

Examples

The flute obligato added color and brilliance to the orchestral arrangement.
She practiced the violin obligato until she could play it flawlessly every time.
A key feature of the piece is its intricate piano obligato accompanying the soloist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ad libitum - (in music) a part that may be omitted or altered at the performer's discretion

Etymology

From Italian 'obbligato' (obligatory), from Latin 'obligatus' (bound, obliged).
