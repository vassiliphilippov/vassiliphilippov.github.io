to didder

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪdər / (AmE), / ˈdɪdə / (BrE)
to move, shake, or tremble slightly, typically as if with a tremor or from nervousness
move or shake with a tremor
archaic

Examples

The old man began to didder as the cold wind blew through the open door.
Her voice would didder with excitement whenever she recounted the story.
The candle flame seemed to didder in the draft from the window.

Synset Examples

his hands shook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically from emotions or physical weakness
  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly imitative or related to dialectal forms meaning 'to tremble'.
