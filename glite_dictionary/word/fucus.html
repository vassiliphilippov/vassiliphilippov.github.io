fucus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːkəs / (AmE), / ˈfjuːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of brown seaweeds commonly found on rocky seashores, especially in the North Atlantic.
a genus of brown seaweeds

Examples

Researchers collected samples of fucus from the tidal pools for analysis.
The rocky coastline was covered with thick mats of fucus.
Many marine animals find food and shelter among the fronds of fucus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: seaweed - a general term for marine algae

Etymology

From Latin *fucus* (a kind of seaweed).
