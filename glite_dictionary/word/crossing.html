crossing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of traveling across something such as a road, river, or sea
an act of traveling across

Examples

The crossing of the river was difficult due to the strong current.
They made a safe crossing of the busy highway at the pedestrian bridge.
The Atlantic crossing took longer than they had anticipated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passage - the act of moving through, under, over, or past something

Etymology

From the verb 'cross' + -ing; 'cross' from Old French 'crosser', from Latin 'crux' (cross).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
(genetics) The act of breeding different species or varieties of animals or plants to produce hybrids.
breeding different species or varieties to produce hybrids

Examples

The experimental crossing of two rose species resulted in a new, hardier hybrid.
Selective crossing has led to many modern breeds of cattle.
Scientists monitored the crossing of wheat varieties to improve disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybridization - the process of producing hybrids by mating different species or varieties

Etymology

From 'cross' (to breed animals or plants of different varieties) + '-ing'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
A place where two lines, roads, or paths cross or intersect.
intersection point of two paths or lines

Examples

You should be careful when walking at the crossing of busy streets.
The train slowed down as it approached the crossing.
A new pedestrian crossing was built near the school to improve safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intersection - the point where two or more things intersect, especially roads

Etymology

From Old English *crossian* (to make a cross), from Latin *crux* (cross).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
A shallow part of a stream or river where it is possible to walk or drive across.
shallow fordable place in a stream or river

Examples

The wagon train found a safe crossing downstream.
We searched for a crossing where the horses could get across the river.
Early settlers used this crossing when traveling west.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ford - a shallow place in a river or stream allowing one to walk or drive across

Etymology

From the verb 'to cross', ultimately from Old French 'croiser' and Latin 'crux' (cross).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
a place, often marked, where a street, railroad, or similar obstacle can be crossed from one side to the other
a marked place for crossing a road or railroad

Examples

There is a pedestrian crossing near the school for students' safety.
The train slowed down as it approached the railroad crossing.
Always look both ways before using the crossing at a busy intersection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosswalk - a marked part of a road for pedestrians to cross
  • Synonym: footbridge - a bridge designed for pedestrians to cross over
  • Antonym: barrier - an obstacle that prevents crossing

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'cross' + '-ing' (gerund or noun-forming suffix).
