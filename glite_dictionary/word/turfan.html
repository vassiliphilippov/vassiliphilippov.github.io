Turfan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrˌfæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːfæn / (BrE)
an extinct dialect of the Tocharian language, formerly spoken in the region around Qarašähär and Turpan in Central Asia
ancient Tocharian dialect of Turpan region

Examples

Scholars have studied the Turfan dialect to learn more about the history of the Tocharian languages.
Manuscripts in Turfan have provided valuable insights into the culture of ancient Central Asia.
The Turfan variety differs notably from other Tocharian dialects in both vocabulary and grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tocharian - an extinct branch of the Indo-European language family once spoken in Central Asia

Etymology

From the name of Turpan (Turfan), a city and region in present-day Xinjiang, China.
