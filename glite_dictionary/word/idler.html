idler

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪd.lə / (BrE)
a person who is habitually lazy or avoids work; someone who does little or nothing
person who avoids work

Examples

He was known as the village idler, never holding a steady job.
She refused to marry an idler who spent his days lounging around the house.
The old park bench was often occupied by an idler watching the world go by.

Synset Examples

a lazy bum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loafer - someone who is habitually lazy or avoids work
  • Antonym: worker - a person who works, especially one who does manual labor

Etymology

From idle (lazy, inactive) + -er (agent noun suffix).
