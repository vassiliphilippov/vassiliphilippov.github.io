strapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræpər / (AmE), / ˈstræpə / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, who is large, strong, and heavyset
large, strong, heavyset person

Examples

The new security guard is a real strapper who looks like he could handle any trouble.
As a young man, he was a strapper known for his strength on the farm.
They hired a strapper to help move the heavy equipment into the warehouse.

Synset Examples

he was a bull of a man
a thick-skinned bruiser ready to give as good as he got

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruiser - a large, strong, and heavyset man

Etymology

Probably from 'strap' (leather belt or thong), suggesting strength or robustness.
