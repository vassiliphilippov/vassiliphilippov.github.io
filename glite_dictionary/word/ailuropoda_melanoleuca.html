Ailuropoda melanoleuca

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌlʊrəˈpoʊdə ˌmɛlənoʊˈluːkə / (AmE), / aɪˌljuərəˈpəʊdə ˌmɛlənəʊˈluːkə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the giant panda, a large, black-and-white herbivorous mammal native to bamboo forests of China and Tibet.
scientific name for the giant panda

Examples

The Ailuropoda melanoleuca is renowned for its distinctive black and white fur.
Researchers study Ailuropoda melanoleuca to learn more about bamboo forest ecosystems.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Ailuropoda melanoleuca in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant panda - common name for Ailuropoda melanoleuca, a large, black-and-white bear-like mammal

Etymology

From Greek 'ailuros' (cat), 'pous/pod-' (foot), 'melas' (black), and 'leukos' (white), referring to its catlike appearance and color pattern.
