musculus articularis cubiti

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs ɑrˌtɪk.jəˈleɪ.rɪs kjuːˈbaɪ.taɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lʊs ɑːˌtɪk.jʊˈleɪ.rɪs kjuːˈbɪ.taɪ / (BrE)
a small muscle branching from the triceps, inserting into the capsule of the elbow joint
small muscle of the elbow joint
medical

Examples

The musculus articularis cubiti assists with the movement and stabilization of the elbow joint.
In rare cases, injury to the musculus articularis cubiti may cause discomfort during elbow extension.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the musculus articularis cubiti during procedures involving the triceps or elbow capsule.

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle) + 'articularis' (of a joint) + 'cubiti' (of the elbow).
