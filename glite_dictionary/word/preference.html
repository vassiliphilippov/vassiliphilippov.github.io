preference

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛfərəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛf(ə)rəns / (BrE)
a greater liking for one alternative over others; a predisposition in favor of something
greater liking; predisposition

Examples

She has a strong preference for early morning meetings over late-night ones.
The survey asked about your preferences for different types of cuisine.
Personal preference plays a big role in choosing the right career.

Synset Examples

a predilection for expensive cars
his sexual preferences
showed a Marxist orientation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predilection - a preference or special liking for something
  • Antonym: dislike - a feeling of not liking or approving

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'preferentia', from Latin 'praeferre' (to prefer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈprɛf.ər.əns / (BrE)
the right or opportunity to choose between different options or to have one's own wishes considered first
the right or chance to choose

Examples

As a member, you have preference in selecting which seat you want.
Certain groups are given preference when applying for these jobs.
The company gives preference to customers who have been with them for a long time.

Synset Examples

given my druthers, I'd eat cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - an act of selecting or making a decision when faced with two or more possibilities
  • Synonym: option - the power or right to choose among alternatives or a thing that may be chosen

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *praeferentia* (preference, priority), from Latin *praeferre* (to carry before, prefer).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɛfɚəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛfərəns / (BrE)
A greater liking for one alternative over others; a strong liking.
strong liking or greater choice

Examples

She has a preference for tea over coffee in the morning.
My preference is to stay home and read a good book.
Cultural preference can strongly influence people's eating habits.

Synset Examples

my own preference is for good literature
the Irish have a penchant for blarney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penchant - a strong or habitual liking for something
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin 'praeferre' (to prefer), via Medieval Latin 'praeferentia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07513449-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈprɛfərəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛfərəns / (BrE)
a grant of favor or advantage to one party over others, especially in areas like international trade, such as the setting of tariffs or import duties
granting advantage to one party, esp. in trade

Examples

The government offered a trade preference to certain developing nations.
Some countries receive preference when importing goods due to longstanding agreements.
The new law removed the preference that local companies once enjoyed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage granted to a particular person or group

Etymology

From Latin praeferre (to prefer), via Medieval Latin praeferentia (preference) and French préférence.

Source: WordNet
