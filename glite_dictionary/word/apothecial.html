apothecial

1. ADJ

/ əˌpɑːˈθiːʃəl / (AmE), / əˌpɒˈθiːsɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an apothecium, a type of reproductive structure in certain lichens and fungi
relating to an apothecium in lichens/fungi
medical

Examples

The apothecial discs on this lichen are clearly visible to the naked eye.
Researchers studied the apothecial development throughout the fungal reproductive cycle.
The specimen was identified based on its distinct apothecial structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apothecium - a spore-producing structure in some lichens and fungi

Etymology

From 'apothecium', itself from Greek 'apothēkē' (storehouse).
