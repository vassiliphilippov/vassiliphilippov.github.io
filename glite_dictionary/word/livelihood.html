livelihood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪvlihʊd / (AmE), / ˈlʌɪvlɪhʊd / (BrE)
a means of securing the necessities of life, especially by earning money through work
means of earning a living

Examples

Farming has been his family's main livelihood for generations.
The new regulations threaten the livelihood of local fishermen.
Many people lost their livelihoods when the factory closed.

Synset Examples

each child was expected to pay for their keep
he applied to the state for support
he could no longer earn his own livelihood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsistence - the means by which someone maintains their life
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of having little or no money or means of support

Etymology

From Middle English 'lyvelod' (means of living), from 'live' + '-hood'.
