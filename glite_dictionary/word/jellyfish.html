jellyfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛl.i.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛl.i.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A soft-bodied, free-swimming marine animal with a gelatinous, often bell-shaped body and trailing tentacles, found primarily in oceans worldwide.
a gelatinous, tentacled marine animal

Examples

While swimming in the ocean, she was startled by a large jellyfish floating nearby.
The aquarium features several species of jellyfish, glowing softly under the lights.
A jellyfish sting can be painful, so it's wise to watch your step at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medusa - free-swimming sexual form of a coelenterate (esp. a jellyfish)

Etymology

From 'jelly' (referring to its gelatinous body) + 'fish', first used in the late 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛl.i.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛl.i.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a soft-bodied, marine animal with stinging tentacles and a bell-shaped, gelatinous body, often found in oceans worldwide
marine animal with stinging tentacles

Examples

We saw a jellyfish floating near the shore during our beach trip.
Some species of jellyfish can deliver a painful sting if touched.
Researchers are studying how a jellyfish moves gracefully through the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medusa - the free-swimming, bell-shaped stage of a jellyfish
  • Synonym: siphonophore - a group of marine animals related to jellyfish

Etymology

From 'jelly' + 'fish'; so named for its gelatinous appearance.
