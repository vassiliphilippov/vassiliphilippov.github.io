decarboxylation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkɑːrˌbɑksɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɑːbɒksɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of a carboxyl group from a chemical compound, often resulting in the release of carbon dioxide
removal of a carboxyl group (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The decarboxylation of amino acids is a key step in many metabolic pathways.
Heating cannabis causes decarboxylation, which activates its psychoactive compounds.
Scientists studied the rate of decarboxylation in the chemical reaction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carboxylation - the addition of a carboxyl group to a molecule

Etymology

From de- (removal) + carboxyl (the COOH group) + -ation (action or process).
