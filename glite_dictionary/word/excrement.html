excrement

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkskrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɛkskrɪmənt / (BrE)
Waste matter, especially feces, discharged from the body.
waste matter discharged from the body

Examples

Proper disposal of excrement is crucial to maintaining public health.
The animal's cage had to be cleaned regularly to remove accumulated excrement.
Doctors warn that contact with human excrement can lead to the spread of disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feces - solid waste eliminated from the digestive tract
  • Synonym: urine - liquid waste excreted by the kidneys
  • Degree Lower: secretion - substance produced and discharged by a cell or gland

Etymology

From Latin excrementum (that which is separated or cast out), from ex- (out) + cernere (to sift, separate).
