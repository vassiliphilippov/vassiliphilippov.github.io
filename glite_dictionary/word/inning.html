inning

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
In baseball, one of the nine divisions of play during which each team has a turn at batting and fielding.
division of play in baseball

Examples

The game went into extra innings after a tie score in the ninth.
The team scored three runs in the first inning of the match.
He pitched a perfect inning, retiring all three batters.

Etymology

From Old English 'innian' (to get in), related to 'in.'
