Plicatoperipatus

1. NOUN

/ plɪˌkætoʊpəˈrɪpətəs / (AmE), / plɪˌkatəʊpəˈrɪpətəs / (BrE)
A genus of velvet worms belonging to the family Peripatidae, found in certain tropical regions.
genus of velvet worms

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Plicatoperipatus in the rainforest.
The genus Plicatoperipatus is distinguished by its unique leg structures.
Specimens of Plicatoperipatus are often studied to understand evolutionary relationships among velvet worms.

Etymology

From Latin 'plicatus' (folded) and modern Latin 'Peripatus' (a related genus of velvet worms).

2. NOUN

/ ˌplaɪ.kə.toʊˌpɛr.əˈpaɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˌplɪ.kə.təʊˌpɛr.ɪˈpaɪ.təs / (BrE)
A genus of velvet worms in the family Peripatidae, found in the neotropics.
genus of velvet worms

Examples

The species Plicatoperipatus jamaicensis is the only known member of its genus.
Researchers discovered unique features in Plicatoperipatus during their expedition in Jamaica.
Taxonomists debate whether new specimens should be assigned to Plicatoperipatus or another genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Peripatidae - a family of velvet worms to which Plicatoperipatus belongs

Etymology

From New Latin, combining roots from Latin 'plicatus' (folded) and the genus name 'Peripatus'.
