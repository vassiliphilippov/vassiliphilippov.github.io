x86

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ˈeɪti sɪks / (AmE), / ɛks ˈeɪti sɪks / (BrE)
a family of computer architectures based on the Intel 8086 microprocessor, widely used in personal computers and servers
Intel-based computer architecture family

Examples

Most desktop computers still use the x86 architecture for compatibility with legacy software.
Developers often optimize applications specifically for x86 processors to improve performance.
The server runs on an x86 platform rather than ARM.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ARM - a different type of computer architecture used in many mobile devices

Etymology

Named after the Intel 8086 microprocessor, with subsequent compatible processors ending in '86' (e.g., 80286, 80386).
