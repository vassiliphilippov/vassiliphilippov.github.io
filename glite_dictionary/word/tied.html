tied

1. ADJ

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
fastened or secured with string, rope, cord, or similar material.
fastened with string or cord

Examples

She handed him a tied package wrapped in brown paper.
The curtains were tied back to let the sunlight into the room.
Each bundle of newspapers was neatly tied with twine.

Synset Examples

a neatly tied bundle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untied - not fastened or secured with string or cord
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00255612-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
bound or secured with a string, rope, or other fastening so as to be held or kept together
securely fastened by tying

Examples

The package was tied with a thick blue ribbon.
She lay on the ground with her hands tied behind her back.
The newspapers were tied together and stacked neatly by the door.

Synset Examples

the guard was found trussed up with his arms and legs securely tied
a trussed chicken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bound - fastened or tied tightly
  • Antonym: untied - not tied or fastened
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00254735-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
bound together by or as if by a strong rope; especially joined closely by a bond such as affection or relationship
bound or joined together, especially by relationship

Examples

The siblings are tied by a deep sense of loyalty.
Many communities remain tied by strong family traditions.
She felt tied to her hometown, unable to leave her roots behind.

Synset Examples

people tied by blood or marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bound - tied or secured tightly
  • Antonym: unbound - not tied or not fastened together
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02487690-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
(of a score in a contest) having the same number of points, votes, or goals as another; equal in score.
having equal score in a contest

Examples

After the final whistle, both teams were tied at 2-2.
The game remained tied until the last few minutes of play.
If the scores are tied after overtime, there will be a penalty shootout.

Synset Examples

the score is tied

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawn - equal in score, especially in British English contexts
  • Antonym: ahead - having a higher score than the opponent
  • Antonym: behind - having a lower score than the opponent
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00895536-s
Synset POS: s
