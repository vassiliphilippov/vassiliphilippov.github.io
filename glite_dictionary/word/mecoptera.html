Mecoptera

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑːptərə / (AmE), / məˈkɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of carnivorous insects typically having long membranous wings and elongated, beaklike heads with chewing mouthparts at the tip
order of carnivorous insects with beaklike heads
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species of Mecoptera in the remote forest region.
The distinctive beaklike heads make Mecoptera easy for entomologists to identify.
Unlike other insects, Mecoptera adults are often seen feeding on both living prey and dead animals.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek mēkōs (length) + pteron (wing).
