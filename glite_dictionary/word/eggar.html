eggar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡ.ər / (AmE), / ˈɛɡ.ə / (BrE)
A moth of the family Lasiocampidae, characterized by adults with nonfunctional mouthparts and larvae that feed on tree foliage and spin egg-shaped cocoons.
a type of moth with egg-shaped cocoon

Examples

The caterpillar of the eggar is often seen feeding on hawthorn leaves.
Many bird species feed on eggar larvae before they spin their distinctive cocoons.
The eggar moth gets its name from the egg-shaped cocoon spun by its larvae.

Etymology

From the resemblance of their cocoons to eggs.
