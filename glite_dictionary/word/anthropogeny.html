anthropogeny

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɑdʒəni / (AmE), / ˌænθrəˈpɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the study of the origin and development of the human species
origin and development of humans

Examples

The field of anthropogeny explores how humans have evolved from earlier primates.
Her research in anthropogeny examines genetic evidence for the migration of early humans.
Advances in genetics have greatly contributed to our understanding of anthropogeny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropology - the scientific study of humans, human behavior, and societies

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrōpos' (human) and 'genesis' (origin, birth).
