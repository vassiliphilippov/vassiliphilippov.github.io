repentant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpɛntənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛntənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing sincere regret and remorse for a wrongdoing or misdeed
feeling remorse for wrongdoing

Examples

She appeared truly repentant when she apologized for her mistake.
After the scandal, the politician made a repentant speech to the public.
He gave a repentant look, hoping for his friend's forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorseful - feeling deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: unrepentant - not feeling or showing regret for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin 'paenitent-', meaning 'feeling regret'.
