yodh

1. NOUN

/ joʊd / (AmE), / jɒd / (BrE)
the tenth letter of the Hebrew alphabet, corresponding to the sound 'y' as in 'yes'
tenth letter of Hebrew alphabet

Examples

The Hebrew letter yodh is often transliterated as 'y' in English.
Each yodh in the Torah is written with great care by scribes.
Learning the significance of yodh is important in studying Hebrew grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jod - alternative Latinized name for the same Hebrew letter
  • Synonym: yod - alternative spelling for the same Hebrew letter

Etymology

From Hebrew י (yod), ultimately from a Semitic root meaning 'hand'.
