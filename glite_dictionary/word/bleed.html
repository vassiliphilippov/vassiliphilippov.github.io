to bleed

1. VERB

/ bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
to draw blood from a person, especially as a medical procedure practiced in the past
draw blood (formerly as treatment)

Examples

In the 18th century, doctors would often bleed patients to treat fevers.
The surgeon decided not to bleed the patient, arguing it was an outdated practice.
Many believed that to bleed someone could balance their bodily humors.

Synset Examples

In the old days, doctors routinely bled patients as part of the treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodlet - to remove blood from a person as a treatment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00085829-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
to lose blood from the body as a result of injury or illness
lose blood from the body

Examples

He began to bleed after accidentally cutting his finger with a knife.
The patient continued to bleed during the surgery, requiring immediate attention.
If your nose starts to bleed, lean forward to prevent swallowing blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemorrhage - to bleed, especially heavily or internally

Etymology

Old English *blēdan*, from *blōd* (blood).

3. VERB

/ bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
to remove or drain liquid, air, or steam from a container or system, especially to ensure it functions correctly
remove liquid, air, or steam from a system

Examples

Before winter, it's important to bleed the radiators to remove trapped air.
The mechanic had to bleed the brakes to ensure they worked properly.
After repairs, remember to bleed the fuel line to release any air.

Synset Examples

bleed the radiators
the mechanic bled the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - to cause liquid to flow out from something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00450108-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
(of a color or dye) to spread or run into surrounding areas, causing blurring or diffusion.
color or dye spreading or running

Examples

Be careful—if the fabric gets wet, the red dye might bleed onto your other clothes.
Some cheap markers tend to bleed through the paper and stain the next page.
The blue paint started to bleed into the white areas when it rained.

Synset Examples

These dyes and colors are guaranteed not to run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - (of color or dye) to spread or diffuse into surrounding areas

5. VERB

/ bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
to obtain money or possessions from someone, typically in a relentless or unfair way
to extract money or possessions unfairly

Examples

The mechanic tried to bleed me for every penny during the repair.
Over the years, the lawyer bled his clients with endless fees.
If you let them, these companies will bleed you dry with hidden charges.

Synset Examples

They bled me dry — I have nothing left!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to make use of someone or something unfairly for one's own advantage
  • Synonym: drain - to gradually deprive of money or resources
