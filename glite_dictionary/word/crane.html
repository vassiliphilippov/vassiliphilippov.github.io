crane

1. VERB

/ kreɪn / (AmE), / kreɪn / (BrE)
to stretch one's neck in order to see something more clearly or from a better vantage point
stretch the neck to see better

Examples

I had to crane my neck to see the parade floats coming down the street.
She craned over the heads of the crowd to catch a glimpse of her favorite actor.
The children were craning their necks to look out the classroom window.

Synset Examples

The women craned their necks to see the President drive by

Etymology

Figurative use from the long neck of the crane (bird); Middle English from Old French 'cran', from Latin 'grus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00028160-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ kreɪn / (AmE), / kreɪn / (BrE)
a large, long-necked wading bird found in marshes and plains in many parts of the world, known for its graceful appearance and elaborate courtship dances
large long-necked marsh bird

Examples

A flock of cranes migrated across the sky at sunrise.
The crane stood motionless in the shallow water, searching for fish.
Many cultures consider the crane a symbol of longevity and good fortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - another large, long-legged wading bird often found in wetlands

Etymology

From Old English cran, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch kraan and German Kranich.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02015503-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kreɪn / (AmE), / kreɪn / (BrE)
a large machine with a long arm, used to lift and move heavy objects by means of cables suspended from a rotating boom
machine for lifting heavy objects

Examples

The construction site used a crane to hoist steel beams into place.
As the crane rotated, the workers carefully guided the load to the ground.
They rented a large crane to move the shipping containers onto the truck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derrick - a lifting device similar to a crane, often with a fixed horizontal arm

Etymology

From Middle English *cran*, from Old English *cran* (the bird); the mechanical sense developed by analogy with the bird's long neck.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03131358-n
Synset POS: n
