Cephalotus follicularis

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈloʊtəs fɒlɪˈkjʊlərɪs / (AmE), / sɛfəˈləʊtəs fɒlɪˈkjʊlərɪs / (BrE)
a species of carnivorous perennial herb native to western Australia, known for its pitcher-shaped leaves with red-edged lids that trap insects
carnivorous pitcher plant of western Australia

Examples

The Cephalotus follicularis is prized by collectors for its unique insect-trapping leaves.
Botanists studied how the Cephalotus follicularis adapts to nutrient-poor soils.
In her greenhouse, she successfully cultivated several Cephalotus follicularis plants.

Etymology

From Greek *kephalē* (head) and Latin *folliculus* (small sack), referring to the plant's pitcher-shaped traps.
