censorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsensəʃɪp / (BrE)
The practice or system of suppressing or prohibiting parts of books, films, news, or other information that are considered sensitive or objectionable, especially for political or security reasons.
suppression of information or content

Examples

During wartime, government censorship was used to prevent sensitive information from reaching the enemy.
The book was banned by censorship authorities because of its controversial ideas.
Debates around internet censorship have intensified due to concerns over free speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppression - the act of stopping something by force
  • Antonym: freedom of speech - the right to express opinions without censorship

Etymology

From Latin 'censura' (judgment, office of a censor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice of removing or suppressing parts of books, films, letters, or other communications considered objectionable, harmful, or sensitive
suppression or deletion of content

Examples

Government censorship of the media was a major issue in the election.
The novel faced heavy censorship before it could be published in some countries.
Many artists protest against censorship that limits creative freedom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freedom of expression - the right to express opinions without censorship or restraint

Etymology

From Latin 'censura' (judgment, office of a censor); influenced by 'censor.'
