Bosc

1. NOUN

/ bɑsk / (AmE), / bɒsk / (BrE)
A variety of pear with a rough, brownish skin and sweet, greenish-yellow flesh, known for its elongated neck.
a type of pear

Examples

I bought a ripe Bosc at the market for dessert.
Bosc pears are excellent for baking due to their firm texture.
The salad featured slices of Bosc alongside walnuts and blue cheese.

Etymology

Named after the French horticulturist Louis Bosc (1759–1828).
