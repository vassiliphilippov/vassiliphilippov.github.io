gynophore

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪnəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪnəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a stalk that supports and raises the pistil above the receptacle in certain flowering plants
botanical stalk supporting the pistil
medical

Examples

Certain species of Capparis have a long gynophore that separates the ovary from the base of the flower.
The scientist carefully measured the length of the gynophore in each sample.
Development of a gynophore is a distinguishing feature in some plant families.

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman, female) + 'phoros' (bearing).
