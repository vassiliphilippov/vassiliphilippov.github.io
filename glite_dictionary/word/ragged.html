ragged

1. ADJ

/ ˈræɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈraɡɪd / (BrE)
having an uneven or irregular outline or edge; not smooth or straight
having an uneven or irregular outline

Examples

The hikers followed a ragged path through the forest, bordered by wild undergrowth.
She drew a ragged line across the paper to represent the mountain range.
The document had ragged right margins, making the text look uneven.

Synset Examples

text set with ragged right margins
herded the class into a ragged line

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface or outline
  • Synonym: jagged - having sharp, uneven projections at the edge or outline

Etymology

From Middle English ragged, from Old Norse rögg (shaggy hair).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪd / (BrE)
(of clothes or a person) wearing clothes that are worn out or torn; in poor condition due to use or neglect.
worn or torn in appearance (of clothes/person)

Examples

The old man wore a ragged coat that barely kept out the cold.
Children in ragged clothing played by the roadside, laughing despite their poverty.
Her ragged jeans made her stand out at the formal dinner.

Synset Examples

clothes as ragged as a scarecrow's
a ragged tramp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tattered - (of cloth or clothing) old and torn; in poor condition
  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Antonym: neat - tidy and in good order

Etymology

From Middle English ragged, from 'rag' + '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593135-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈræɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) extremely tired, exhausted, or worn out due to stress or strain.
exhausted; physically or emotionally worn out

Examples

After working double shifts all week, she felt completely ragged.
The coach ran the players ragged during the intense training session.
By the end of the semester, most students look a little ragged from all the exams and assignments.

Synset Examples

run ragged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - extremely tired or drained of energy
  • Antonym: refreshed - feeling energetic and no longer tired

Etymology

From 'rag', originally referring to clothes torn into rags, later extended metaphorically to people overtired or worn down, as if torn to rags.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02443029-s
Synset POS: s
