shadiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdi.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dishonest, suspicious, or of doubtful legality
dubious or suspicious conduct

Examples

Some people questioned the shadiness of his business dealings.
There was a clear shadiness about the entire negotiation process.
The journalist exposed the shadiness behind the company’s rapid success.

Synset Examples

they acted with such obvious shadiness that they were instantly recognizable
the shadiness of their transactions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty or integrity
  • Antonym: legitimacy - conformity to the law or to rules

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being shaded or partially dark, caused by blocking light
state of partial darkness or shade

Examples

The dense trees provided welcome shadiness on a hot afternoon.
Photographers often struggle with shadiness when trying to capture clear images in the woods.
The shadiness under the awning made it a pleasant spot to sit in summer.

Synset Examples

it is much cooler in the shade
there's too much shadiness to take good photographs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - an area where direct sunlight is blocked
  • Antonym: brightness - the state of being bright or well lit

Etymology

From 'shady' + '-ness'.
