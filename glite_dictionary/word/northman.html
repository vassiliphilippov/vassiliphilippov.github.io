Northman

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɹθ.mən / (AmE), / ˈnɔːθ.mən / (BrE)
(historical) A male inhabitant of Scandinavia, especially during the Viking Age.
male inhabitant of Scandinavia (historical)

Examples

The legendary Northman sailed across the North Sea in search of new lands.
Many monasteries feared raids from fierce Northmen during the 9th century.
The stories told of the bravery and seafaring skills of every Northman in his village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Viking - a seafaring Scandinavian warrior of the late 8th to early 11th century

Etymology

From Old English norþmann (northman), from norþ (north) + mann (man).
