schema

1. NOUN

/ ˈskiːmə / (AmE), / ˈskiːmə / (BrE)
(In psychology) a cognitive framework or concept that helps organize and interpret information, which can be adjusted with new experiences.
cognitive framework organizing information

Examples

Children develop a new schema when they learn that not all four-legged animals are dogs.
The teacher designed activities to expand the students’ existing schema about plants.
Your schema for social situations changes as you gain more life experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framework - a basic structure underlying a system or concept

Etymology

From Greek 'skhēma' (form, figure), adopted into Latin 'schema'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskiː.mə / (AmE), / ˈskiː.mə / (BrE)
a diagrammatic or preliminary representation of a plan, theory, or system
diagrammatic plan or representation

Examples

The engineer drew a schema of the new bridge to illustrate its key design elements.
Before starting the software project, the team created a schema outlining the main features and workflow.
The printed schema helped the students visualize how the different parts of the experiment fit together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something
  • Synonym: blueprint - a detailed plan or drawing that explains how something will be built or made

Etymology

From Greek skhēma (form, figure, appearance), via Latin schema.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05900179-n
Synset POS: n
