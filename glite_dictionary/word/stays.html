stays

1. NOUN

/ steɪz / (AmE), / steɪz / (BrE)
A woman's close-fitting foundation garment, typically stiffened with whalebone or similar materials, worn to shape the torso; an early form of corset.
historical women's foundation garment
archaic

Examples

She wore a pair of stays tightly laced beneath her dress.
Museum displays often include examples of stays from the eighteenth century.
Before corsets became popular, women used stays to shape their figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corset - a fitted undergarment extending from below the chest to the hips

Etymology

From the plural of 'stay', probably from Old French 'estai' (support), reflecting the garment's structural role.
