clapperboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæp.ɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈklæp.əˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a device consisting of two hinged boards that are clapped together in front of a movie camera to synchronize sound and picture at the start of a take
device for synchronizing sound and video in film

Examples

The director called for the clapperboard before starting the first scene of the movie.
A crew member snapped the clapperboard to mark the beginning of the take.
The sound engineer used the noise from the clapperboard to line up the audio with the video footage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slate - device used in filmmaking to identify takes and scenes, often combined with a clapper

Etymology

From 'clapper' (something that makes a clapping sound) and 'board'.
