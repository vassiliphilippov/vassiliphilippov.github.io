Juncus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial tufted marsh plants in the family Juncaceae, commonly known as rushes, found in temperate regions.
genus of marsh plants (rushes)

Examples

Juncus species are commonly found in wetlands and marshy environments worldwide.
The botanist collected several samples of Juncus for her study on aquatic plants.
Many birds build nests among the Juncus growing along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - common name for members of the Juncus genus, marsh plants with cylindrical stems

Etymology

From Latin 'juncus' meaning 'rush' (the plant).
