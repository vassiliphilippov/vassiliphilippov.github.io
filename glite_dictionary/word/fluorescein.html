fluorescein

1. NOUN

/ fluˈɔrəsiˌin / (AmE), / flʊˈɒrəsiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic yellow or greenish dye that fluoresces under ultraviolet light, commonly used as a tracer or absorption indicator in chemistry and medicine
fluorescent yellow dye used as chemical indicator
medical

Examples

A drop of fluorescein was added to the solution to check for leaks.
The doctor used fluorescein dye to examine the patient's eye injury.
When exposed to UV light, fluorescein emits a strong green fluorescence.

Etymology

From German Fluoresceïn, from Fluoreszenz ('fluorescence') + '-in'.

2. NOUN

/ fluˈɔrəsiˌin / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛsiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic yellow or greenish-yellow dye used as an indicator and in medical diagnostics, known for its bright fluorescence under ultraviolet light
yellow fluorescent dye used as indicator or in diagnostics
medical

Examples

Doctors use fluorescein eye drops to diagnose corneal injuries.
The solution glowed bright green after adding fluorescein and exposing it to UV light.
A small amount of fluorescein was sufficient to track the movement of water in the laboratory experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorescence - the emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light

Etymology

From German 'Fluoresceïn', from 'Fluoreszenz' (fluorescence) + '-in' (chemical suffix).
