plundering

1. ADJ

/ ˈplʌndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of people or actions) inclined to take things by force, especially for gain or greed.
taking by force; looting

Examples

The plundering invaders left little behind in the deserted village.
Many opposed the plundering policies of the corrupt regime.
A plundering army swept through the region, taking whatever they wanted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looting - the act of stealing goods, often during chaos
  • Synonym: robbing - taking property by force or threat

Etymology

From Middle English plundren (to rob), perhaps from Middle Dutch plunderen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of stealing valuable things, especially during a time of war or disorder; looting or pillaging
the act of stealing or looting

Examples

The museum curators condemned the plundering of ancient artifacts during the conflict.
Historians have documented the plundering of cities by invading armies.
The plundering of natural resources has left the region severely degraded.

Synset Examples

the plundering of the Parthenon
his plundering of the great authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looting - the act of stealing goods, typically during a war or riot
  • Synonym: pillaging - the act of robbing a place using violence, especially in wartime

Etymology

From Middle English plundren (to plunder), likely from Middle Low German plunderen (to plunder, rob).
