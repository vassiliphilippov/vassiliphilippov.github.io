alderfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔldɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːldəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a dark-colored insect of the family Sialidae, with aquatic predatory larvae, found near water in Europe and North America
aquatic insect with predatory larvae

Examples

The alderfly is commonly found resting near streams and rivers during the spring.
Fishermen often use alderfly larvae as bait for catching trout.
The aquatic larvae of the alderfly are fierce predators of smaller insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dobsonfly - large aquatic insect related to the alderfly

Etymology

Named for its habit of living near alder trees, combined with 'fly' (insect).
