settlement

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬.əl.mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl.mənt / (BrE)
the act of establishing a colony in a new area; the process of colonizing a place
the act of founding a colony

Examples

The British settlement of Australia began in the late 18th century.
Dutch settlement in South Africa significantly altered the region's development.
Archaeologists studied evidence of ancient settlement patterns along the coast.

Synset Examples

the British colonization of America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonization - the process of establishing control over a foreign area and settling it

Etymology

From Old French 'establement', from Latin 'stabilīmentum', from 'stabilīre' (to make stable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01254867-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtl̩mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛtləmənt / (BrE)
a place where people establish a community and live together, especially in a new region
community where people live together

Examples

The first European settlement in the region was established in 1620.
They built a small settlement near the river where families could farm and trade.
Archaeologists discovered evidence of an ancient settlement beneath the sand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: village - a small group of houses and other buildings in a country area

Etymology

From Old French *setlement* or directly from Medieval Latin *settlementum*, from *settle* (to seat, place) + -ment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08690300-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬.əl.mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl.mənt / (BrE)
a small community or group of houses where people live, typically smaller than a town
small community or village

Examples

The archaeological team discovered a prehistoric settlement near the riverbank.
She grew up in a rural settlement, far from the nearest city.
New houses have been built around the old settlement in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: village - a small group of houses, usually in a rural area
  • Degree Higher: town - larger than a village or settlement, but smaller than a city

Etymology

From Old French 'settlement', from 'settle', from Old English 'setlan'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬.əl.mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl.mənt / (BrE)
the process of concluding a business operation by liquidating assets to pay off liabilities
ending a business by paying off liabilities

Examples

Following the bankruptcy, the court oversaw the company's settlement to ensure all creditors were paid.
The settlement of the partnership involved selling its remaining assets and distributing the proceeds.
Efficient settlement of failed enterprises is essential for a healthy business environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidation - the process of dissolving a business by selling assets and paying debts

Etymology

From Old French *settlement*, from *settle*, ultimately from Old English *setlan* (to seat, settle).

a group of people living in a foreign country who maintain ties with their original homeland, usually not under their home state's government

There was an American settlement in Shanghai during the early 20th century.

Related Terms

6. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬.əl.mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl.mənt / (BrE)
the act of reaching a final agreement or resolution in a dispute or legal case
final resolution of a dispute

Examples

After months of negotiations, the two companies finally reached a settlement out of court.
The lawsuit ended in a financial settlement that satisfied both parties.
The settlement of the contract dispute allowed the project to move forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement
  • Antonym: dispute - a disagreement or argument

Etymology

From Old English *setl* (seat, place) + -ment, influenced by legal Latin *settlementum* (agreement).
