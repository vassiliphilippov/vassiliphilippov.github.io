gritty

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɪti / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪti / (BrE)
showing courage and resolve in difficult or dangerous situations; brave and determined
showing courage and resolve

Examples

Despite the dangers, her gritty determination kept the team moving forward.
The gritty firefighter entered the burning building without hesitation.
He gave a gritty performance under extreme pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'grit' meaning courage or determination, combined with the suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɪti / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪti / (BrE)
having a texture that contains or is covered with small, hard particles; resembling grains or grit.
containing or covered with small particles

Examples

The sandpaper felt gritty in my hand as I began sanding the wood.
After a day at the beach, her feet were gritty from walking in the sand.
The sugar left a gritty residue at the bottom of the cup.

Synset Examples

granular sugar
the photographs were grainy and indistinct
it left a mealy residue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grainy - having a texture of many small particles, like grains
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from roughness

Etymology

From grit + -y, first recorded in the 17th century; 'grit' from Old English 'grytt' (gravel, sand).
