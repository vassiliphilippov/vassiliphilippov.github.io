opportunism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpərˈtuːnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of taking advantage of opportunities with little regard for the consequences for others or for principles
taking advantage of opportunities without regard for others

Examples

The manager's opportunism became clear when he cut corners to impress the board.
Many criticize political opportunism as a betrayal of core values.
Her opportunism helped her succeed, but it cost her some friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfishness - concerned excessively or exclusively with oneself
  • Antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

From French 'opportunisme', from 'opportun' (opportune), originally from Latin 'opportunus' (favorable, convenient).
