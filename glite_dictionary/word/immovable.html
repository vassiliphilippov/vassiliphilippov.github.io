immovable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmuːvəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmuːvəbəl / (BrE)
not able or intended to be moved; fixed in place
not able to be moved

Examples

The heavy statue was completely immovable, no matter how hard they tried.
The debate ended in an immovable stalemate, with neither side willing to compromise.
These ancient rocks have remained immovable for thousands of years.

Synset Examples

the immovable hills

Related Terms

  • Antonym: movable - able to be moved; not fixed in one place

Etymology

From Latin immovabilis (unable to be moved), from in- (not) + movēre (to move).
