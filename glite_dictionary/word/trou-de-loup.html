trou-de-loup

1. NOUN

/ tʁu də ˈluːp / (AmE), / tʁu də ˈluːp / (BrE)
A sloping pit with a pointed stake in the center, used as a defensive obstacle against enemy troops.
defensive pit with stake (military)

Examples

The defenders prepared a series of trous-de-loup along the outer perimeter of the fort.
During the siege, the army fell into several trous-de-loup concealed in the grass.
Engineers constructed trous-de-loup overnight to slow the enemy's advance.

Etymology

From French, literally 'wolf hole'.
