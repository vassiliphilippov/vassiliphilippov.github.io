unexpansive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪkˈspæn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪkˈspæn.sɪv / (BrE)
not showing any tendency or ability to expand; not expansive.
not tending to expand

Examples

The minerals remained unexpansive even when exposed to high heat.
Because the material is unexpansive, it is ideal for applications requiring stability under temperature changes.
He described the fabric as unexpansive and therefore unsuitable for stretch clothing.

Synset Examples

unexpansive bodies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expansive - tending to expand; able to expand

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- meaning 'not' + expansive.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnɪkˈspænsɪv / (AmE), / ʌnɪkˈspansɪv / (BrE)
not inclined to be open, outgoing, or enthusiastic in manner or expression; reserved or restrained.
not open or outgoing; reserved

Examples

Despite the cheerful atmosphere, he remained unexpansive and spoke very little.
Her unexpansive demeanor made it hard for others to get to know her.
The manager seemed unexpansive during the meeting, offering only brief responses.

Synset Examples

an unexpansive man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotion or opinions
  • Antonym: expansive - outgoing and sociable in manner
  • Synonym: withdrawn - not wanting to communicate with others

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'expansive' (outgoing, open); modeled after similar English formations.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02007655-s
Synset POS: s
