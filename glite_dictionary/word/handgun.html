handgun

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈhændˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a small firearm designed to be held and fired with one hand
small one-handed firearm

Examples

The police officer drew his handgun during the confrontation.
She kept a licensed handgun in her bedside drawer for protection.
Strict laws regulate the sale and possession of handguns in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistol - a type of handgun, especially one with a chamber integrated with the barrel
  • Synonym: revolver - a type of handgun with a revolving cylinder
  • Antonym: rifle - a long-barreled firearm designed to be fired from the shoulder

Etymology

From 'hand' + 'gun', indicating a gun designed to be held in one hand.
