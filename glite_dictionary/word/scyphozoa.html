Scyphozoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪfəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌsɪfəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a class of marine coelenterate animals that includes the true jellyfish, characterized by a conspicuous medusa stage and a reduced or absent polyp stage
class of true jellyfish
medical

Examples

Scientists study the life cycles of Scyphozoa to learn more about jellyfish development.
The Scyphozoa class includes species like the moon jelly and the lion's mane jellyfish.
Unlike other cnidarians, Scyphozoa typically lack a prominent polyp stage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Cubozoa - class of box jellyfish distinguished by their cube-shaped medusae

Etymology

From Greek 'skyphos' (cup) + 'zoon' (animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪfəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌsɪfəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a class of marine coelenterates (jellyfish) in which the polyp stage is either absent or much reduced
class of jellyfish (marine animals)

Examples

The Scyphozoa are commonly known as the true jellyfish.
Many Scyphozoa spend most of their life cycle as free-swimming medusae.
Members of the Scyphozoa can be found in oceans worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jellyfish - free-swimming marine animals with a gelatinous bell and tentacles

Etymology

From Greek skuphos (cup) + zōion (animal).
