subfamily

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌfæmɪli / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
A taxonomic category in biological classification ranking below a family and above a genus.
taxonomic rank below family
medical

Examples

The cat subfamily includes both domestic cats and big wild cats like lions and tigers.
Within the rose family, the Rosoideae is a well-known subfamily that contains strawberries and raspberries.
Scientists recently discovered a new subfamily of insects based on genetic differences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: family - a major taxonomic category above subfamily and below order
  • Degree Lower: genus - taxonomic category below subfamily and above species

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'familia' (family).
