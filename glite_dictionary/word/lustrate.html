to lustrate

1. VERB

/ ˈlʌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlʌstreɪt / (BrE)
To purify or cleanse, especially by means of a ritual; also, to subject to political cleansing, especially of officials associated with a previous regime.
purify by ritual or remove officials

Examples

During the ceremony, the priests lustrated the temple to cleanse it of evil influences.
After the revolution, the new government lustrated former officials associated with the previous regime.
In ancient Rome, people would lustrate themselves to achieve spiritual purity before important events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - remove impurities or cleanse

Etymology

From Latin lustratus, past participle of lustrare (to purify).
