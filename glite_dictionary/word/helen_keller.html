Helen Keller

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛlən ˈkɛlər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlən ˈkɛlə / (BrE)
(1880–1968) American lecturer, writer, and advocate who, having been blind and deaf from early childhood, became a symbol of courage and championed the rights of people with disabilities.
American author and disability rights advocate

Examples

Helen Keller overcame the challenges of being both blind and deaf to become a world-renowned author.
The story of Helen Keller and her teacher Anne Sullivan continues to inspire generations.
Many schools teach about Helen Keller as a pioneer in advocating for disability rights.
