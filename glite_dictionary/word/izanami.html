Izanami

1. PROPN

/ iːˌzɑːˈnɑːmi / (AmE), / iːˌzɑːˈnɑːmi / (BrE)
In Japanese mythology, the goddess who, with her brother and consort Izanagi, created the islands and gods of Japan.
Japanese goddess; creator with Izanagi

Examples

In Japanese mythology, Izanami and Izanagi are revered as the divine creators of the islands.
The tragic death of Izanami led to her descent into the underworld.
Artists often depict Izanami alongside her consort Izanagi in paintings of Japanese legends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Izanagi - Izanami's brother and consort in Japanese mythology

Etymology

From Japanese 伊邪那美 (Izanami), meaning 'she who invites'.
