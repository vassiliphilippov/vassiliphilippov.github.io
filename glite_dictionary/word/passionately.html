passionately

1. ADV

/ ˈpæʃ.ə.nətli / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ən.ətli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or expresses intense emotion, enthusiasm, or desire.
with intense emotion or enthusiasm

Examples

He spoke passionately about the need for reform during the meeting.
She passionately defended her choice to pursue a career in art.
They passionately believe in the cause and volunteer every weekend.

Synset Examples

she kissed him passionately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervently - in a way that shows intense and sincere feeling
  • Antonym: calmly - in a way that is relaxed and without strong emotion

Etymology

From 'passionate', which comes from Late Latin 'passionatus' (suffering, feeling), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈpæʃ.ən.ətli / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ən.ət.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows or involves intense emotion, enthusiasm, or strong feeling; in a forceful or fervent manner.
with intense emotion or feeling

Examples

The crowd cheered passionately as the team scored the winning goal.
She spoke passionately about the importance of protecting the environment.
He argued passionately in defense of his beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervently - with intense and passionate feeling
  • Antonym: calmly - in a relaxed and peaceful manner

Etymology

From 'passionate' + '-ly'; 'passionate' from late Middle English via Old French from medieval Latin 'passionatus'.
