compare

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpɛr / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛə / (BrE)
to be as good as or similar in quality, amount, or status to someone or something else
to be comparable in quality or status

Examples

Nothing in the restaurant compares with their signature dessert.
No other city compares to Paris for its romantic atmosphere.
His recent work simply does not compare with his earlier masterpieces.

Synset Examples

This car does not compare with our line of Mercedes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contrast - to show differences when compared

Etymology

From Latin comparare (to couple, match with), from com- (together) + par (equal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02735507-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kəmˈpɛr / (AmE), / kəmˈpeə / (BrE)
to consider or describe someone or something as similar to or equal to someone or something else
consider as similar or equal

Examples

It's not fair to compare the two candidates, as their backgrounds are very different.

In her essay, she compared the two novels to highlight their similarities.

Synset Examples

We can compare the Han dynasty to the Romans
You cannot equate success in financial matters with greed

Related Terms

/ kəmˈpɛr / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛə / (BrE)

Examples
