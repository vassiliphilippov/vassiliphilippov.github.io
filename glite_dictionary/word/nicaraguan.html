Nicaraguan

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪkəˈrɑːɡwən / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəˈræɡjuən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Nicaragua
native or inhabitant of Nicaragua

Examples

The new coffee shop is run by a Nicaraguan who moved here last year.
Many Nicaraguans are proud of their country's cultural heritage.
She became friends with a Nicaraguan during her travels through Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicaragua - a country in Central America

Etymology

From Nicaragua (the country name) + -an (denoting origin or belonging).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɪkəˈrɑːɡwən / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəˈræɡjuən / (BrE)
relating to Nicaragua or its people, culture, or language
from or relating to Nicaragua

Examples

The devastating Nicaraguan earthquakes of the 1970s shaped much of the country’s modern history.
She specializes in Nicaraguan literature and poetry in her research.
The Nicaraguan cuisine is known for its use of fresh, local ingredients.

Synset Examples

Nicaraguan earthquakes

Etymology

From Nicaragua, the name of the country, with the English suffix -an.
