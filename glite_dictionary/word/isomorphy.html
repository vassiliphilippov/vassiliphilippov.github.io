isomorphy

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈmɔrfi / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈmɔːfi / (BrE)
(biology) similarity or identity of form, shape, or structure, especially in biological organisms or structures.
similarity of form or structure (biology)
medical

Examples

Many marine animals display isomorphy in their skeletal structures across different species.
The isomorphy between the adult and larval forms allows scientists to trace evolutionary relationships.
Some plant groups are notable for their isomorphy throughout the different generations in their life cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomorphism - correspondence or similarity in form or structure
  • Antonym: heteromorphy - difference in form or structure

Etymology

From Greek isomorphos (of equal form), from iso- (equal) + morphē (form).
