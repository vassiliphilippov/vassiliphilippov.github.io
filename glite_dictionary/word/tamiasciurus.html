Tamiasciurus

1. NOUN

/ teɪ.mi.əˈsaɪ.jʊ.rəs / (AmE), / teɪ.mi.əˈsaɪ.jʊ.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American red squirrels, including species such as the American red squirrel and Douglas squirrel.
genus of North American red squirrels

Examples

The American red squirrel, a species in the genus Tamiasciurus, is common in northern forests.
Researchers studied the behavior of Tamiasciurus during the winter months.
Several Tamiasciurus species play crucial roles in forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrel - Any small or medium-sized rodent of the family Sciuridae

Etymology

From Greek 'tamias' (steward) + 'sciurus' (squirrel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌteɪmiəˈskaɪərəs / (AmE), / ˌtæmiəˈskaɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American squirrels, commonly known as pine squirrels or American red squirrels, notable for their reddish fur and bushy tails.
genus of North American red squirrels

Examples

The Tamiasciurus species are highly territorial and vocal.
In North America, the Tamiasciurus genus is associated with pine forests.
Researchers studied the foraging behavior of Tamiasciurus in different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red squirrel - a small reddish squirrel found in both Europe and North America

Etymology

From Greek 'tamias' (steward, treasurer) and 'sciurus' (squirrel).
