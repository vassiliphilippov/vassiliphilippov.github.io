infallibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfæləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌfalɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being incapable of making mistakes or being wrong
incapability of making mistakes

Examples

The doctrine of papal infallibility has been debated by theologians for centuries.
Some managers mistakenly believe in their own infallibility, refusing to accept criticism.
Science does not claim infallibility, but rather constantly seeks to improve its understanding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fallibility - the tendency to make mistakes or be wrong
  • Synonym: perfection - the state of being perfect or without flaws

Etymology

From Medieval Latin infallibilitas, from Latin infallibilis (not liable to err).
