pardoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrdənər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdnə / (BrE)
(historical) A medieval cleric who raised money for the church by selling papal indulgences, often viewed with suspicion or criticism.
medieval seller of church indulgences

Examples

The pardoner traveled from village to village selling indulgences to the faithful.
In Chaucer's tale, the pardoner is depicted as a corrupt and greedy figure.
Many viewed the pardoner's practices as a form of church corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulgence - a remission of punishment for sins, granted by the Catholic Church

Etymology

From Old French pardoneor, from pardoner (to grant pardon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrdənər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdənə / (BrE)
a person who pardons, forgives, or excuses a fault or offense; historically, someone authorized to grant pardons, especially for sins
person who pardons or forgives

Examples

The pardoner gave absolution to those who confessed and repented.
In medieval times, a pardoner traveled between towns selling indulgences.
She acted as a pardoner, letting go of old grudges and forgiving past mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgiver - someone who forgives another's fault or offense

Etymology

From Old French pardoner (to forgive), from Medieval Latin pardunare, from Latin per- (through) + donare (to give).
