Capra falconeri

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɹə fælˈkoʊnəri / (AmE), / ˈkaprə falˈkəʊnəri / (BrE)
a large wild goat of the Himalayas and Central Asia, known for its impressive spiraled horns; also called the markhor
large wild Himalayan goat with spiraled horns

Examples

The Capra falconeri is recognized for its long, spiraled horns and impressive climbing ability.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of Capra falconeri from poaching and development.
I saw a documentary about the Capra falconeri roaming the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: markhor - a large wild goat of the Himalayas with twisted horns

Etymology

From Latin: 'Capra' meaning 'goat', and 'falconeri' honoring British naturalist Hugh Falconer.
