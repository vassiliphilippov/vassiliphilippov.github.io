Caelum

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːləm / (AmE), / ˈsiːləm / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near Columba and Eridanus.
a constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Caelum is best viewed during the months of December and January.
Astronomers often find it challenging to locate Caelum because it is so faint.
Caelum lies near the constellations Columba and Eridanus in the southern sky.

Etymology

From Latin 'caelum' meaning 'chisel' or 'heaven'.
