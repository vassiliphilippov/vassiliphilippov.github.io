powder

1. VERB

/ ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
to make (something) into a fine, dry substance by crushing or grinding it
make into a fine dry substance

Examples

The mill is used to powder spices for cooking.
Pharmaceutical companies often powder raw materials for easier formulation.
The artist carefully powdered the pigment before mixing it with oil.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to pulverize - to crush into a powder or dust

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
a cosmetic or medical preparation in the form of a fine, dry, pulverized substance, used for application to the skin
fine cosmetic or medical preparation

Examples

She applied a light layer of powder to her face before leaving the house.
The pharmacist recommended a medicated powder to treat the rash.
After shaving, he sprinkled some talcum powder on his neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ointment - a smooth oily substance used on the skin for medical purposes

Etymology

From Middle English poudere, from Old French poudre (dust, powder), from Latin pulverem (dust).

5. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A mixture of potassium nitrate, charcoal, and sulfur, usually in a 75:15:10 ratio, used as an explosive in gunnery, time fuses, and fireworks; also known as gunpowder.
explosive mixture used in guns and fireworks

Examples

Black powder was a crucial component in early firearms and cannons.
The fireworks display was powered by a carefully measured amount of powder.
Old time fuses were packed with powder to ensure a controlled detonation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunpowder - an explosive powder used in firearms and fireworks

Etymology

From Middle English 'poudre', from Old French, from Latin 'pulvis' (dust).
