gunite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnaɪt / (BrE)
a mixture of cement, sand, and water that is sprayed onto a surface using pneumatic pressure, typically used in construction for lining tunnels or swimming pools
sprayed cement mixture for construction

Examples

The pool walls were constructed using gunite for extra durability.
Workers sprayed gunite on the tunnel walls to create a smooth surface.
Many modern swimming pools are built with a strong foundation of gunite.

Etymology

Blend of 'gun' (for the spraying apparatus) and 'concrete'.
