revere

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɪr / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
A lapel on a woman's garment, usually folded or turned back to show the reverse side of the fabric.
lapel showing reverse fabric side

Examples

The jacket was tailored with a distinctive revere that added elegance to the neckline.
She admired the contrasting fabric on the revere of her new coat.
Fashion designers often highlight the revere to give garments a unique style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapel - the part on each side of a coat or jacket immediately below the collar which is folded back on either side of the front opening

Etymology

From French 'revers' (reverse, lapel), from Latin 'revertere' (to turn back).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɪr / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
to regard someone or something with deep respect, admiration, or awe
regard with deep respect or awe

Examples

Many people revere historical leaders for their contributions to society.
She was revered by her students for her wisdom and kindness.
Some cultures revere ancestors through elaborate rituals and ceremonies.

Synset Examples

Fear God as your father
We venerate genius

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to venerate - to regard with great respect or reverence
  • Antonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for

Etymology

From Latin 'revereri', from 're-' (again) + 'vereri' (to stand in awe of, fear).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈvɪr / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
to feel deep respect or admiration for someone or something, often with awe
regard with deep respect or admiration

Examples

Many people revere the Dalai Lama for his message of compassion.
She was revered as a pioneer in her field by both colleagues and students.
In some cultures, ancestors are revered and honored through special ceremonies.

Synset Examples

Many teenagers idolized the Beatles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to venerate - regard with great respect; revere
  • Synonym: to idolize - admire or love greatly or excessively
  • Antonym: to despise - feel contempt or a deep repugnance for

Etymology

From Latin revereri, from re- (intensive) + vereri (to fear, respect).
