paraprofessional

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəprəˈfɛʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌparəprəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
A person who is trained to assist a qualified professional but does not possess the full qualifications required for full professional status.
trained assistant to a professional

Examples

The paraprofessional worked closely with teachers to support students with additional needs.
Many schools rely on a paraprofessional to assist in classrooms and help manage daily tasks.
She started her career as a paraprofessional before pursuing further education to become a teacher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps another in their job
  • Antonym: professional - a person with full qualifications in a profession

Etymology

From prefix 'para-' (beside, related to) + 'professional'.
