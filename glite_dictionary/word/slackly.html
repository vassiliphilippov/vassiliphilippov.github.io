slackly

1. ADV

/ ˈslækli / (AmE), / ˈslækli / (BrE)
in a loose or relaxed way; not firmly or tightly
in a loose or relaxed manner

Examples

He held the rope slackly in his hands, making no attempt to pull it tight.
The flag hung slackly from the pole on a windless afternoon.
She walked slackly into the room, shoulders relaxed and posture casual.

Synset Examples

his hands lay loosely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosely - in a loose or relaxed way
  • Antonym: tightly - in a firm or rigid manner

Etymology

From 'slack' (not tight) + '-ly', used to form adverbs from adjectives.
