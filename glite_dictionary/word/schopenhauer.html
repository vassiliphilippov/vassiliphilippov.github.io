Schopenhauer

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃoʊpənˌhaʊər / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊpənˌhaʊə / (BrE)
Arthur Schopenhauer (1788–1860), a German philosopher known for his pessimistic philosophy and influence on later thinkers.
German pessimistic philosopher (1788–1860)

Examples

Many scholars have studied Schopenhauer's theories on the nature of human desire.
Nietzsche was greatly influenced by Schopenhauer during his early philosophical career.
The writings of Schopenhauer continue to provoke debate among students of philosophy.

Etymology

Surname of German origin; associated with Arthur Schopenhauer, philosopher.
