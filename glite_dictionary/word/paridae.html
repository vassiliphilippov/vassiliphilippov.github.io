Paridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpærɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small songbirds including the titmice and chickadees, found mainly in the Northern Hemisphere.
family of small songbirds (titmice, chickadees)

Examples

Several species within the Paridae family are common in European woodlands.
The Paridae include well-known birds like the great tit and black-capped chickadee.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Paridae in suburban gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titmouse - Small songbird in Paridae family
  • Synonym: chickadee - Small North American songbird in the Paridae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Paridae, from Parus (type genus of the family).
