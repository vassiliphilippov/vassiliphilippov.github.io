authentication

1. NOUN

/ əˌθɛntɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɛntɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of verifying that something or someone is genuine or valid
verification of authenticity

Examples

The bank requires authentication before granting access to online accounts.
Biometric scans are now widely used for authentication at airports.
Document authentication is essential to prevent fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verification - the process of checking or confirming the truth or accuracy of something
  • Antonym: forgery - the act of producing a copy of a document, signature, or work of art in order to deceive

Etymology

From Medieval Latin authenticationem (act of authenticating), from Greek authentikos (genuine).
