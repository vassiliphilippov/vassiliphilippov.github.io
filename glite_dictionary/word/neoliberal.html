neoliberal

1. ADJ

/ ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəl / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈlɪbərəl / (BrE)
relating to or advocating a modified form of liberalism that emphasizes free-market economic policies, deregulation, and reduced government intervention.
favoring free-market economic policies

Examples

Many critics argue that neoliberal policies worsen inequality in developing countries.
The neoliberal approach favors privatizing public services to increase efficiency.
Under her leadership, the party shifted towards a more neoliberal economic agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberal - favoring individual freedoms and progressive reform

Etymology

Formed from neo- (new) + liberal; popularized in the 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəl / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈlɪbərəl / (BrE)
A person who advocates or supports neoliberalism, especially in economic and political policy.
advocate of neoliberalism

Examples

The economic policies were crafted by prominent neoliberals in the government.
Many critics argue that neoliberals prioritize market efficiency over social welfare.
Over the years, several major parties have adopted neoliberal positions on trade and deregulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neoliberalism - the ideology or policy model favoring free-market capitalism
  • Degree Lower: liberal - a person open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values

Etymology

From neo- (new) + liberal, referring to a revived form of liberal economic policy.
