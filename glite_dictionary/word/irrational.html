irrational

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈræʃ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃ.ən.əl / (BrE)
not based on or using reason or clear thinking; lacking logical explanation or justification.
not based on reason or logic

Examples

He has an irrational fear of spiders that keeps him from going outdoors.
It is irrational to expect success without any preparation.
Her irrational outburst surprised everyone in the meeting.

Synset Examples

irrational fears
irrational animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illogical - not making sense according to logic
  • Antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin irrationalis, from in- (not) + rationalis (rational).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01932775-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
a real number that cannot be written as a ratio of two integers
real number not expressible as a fraction

Examples

The square root of 2 is an irrational and cannot be expressed as a simple fraction.
Pi is a well-known example of an irrational in mathematics.
Some decimal expansions never repeat or terminate because they represent an irrational.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational - a number expressible as a ratio of two integers

Etymology

From Latin irrationalis (not rational).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13752694-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
(of a number) real but not expressible as the ratio of two integers, i.e., not a fraction.
not expressible as a fraction (math)

Examples

The square root of 2 is an irrational number because it cannot be written as a fraction.
Pi is a famous example of an irrational number in mathematics.
Although its decimal expansion goes on forever without repeating, an irrational number is still real.

Synset Examples

irrational numbers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational - (of a number) expressible as the ratio of two integers, i.e., a fraction

Etymology

From Latin 'irrationalis' meaning 'without reason'; in mathematics, adopted in the 16th century for numbers not expressible as fractions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02941396-a
Synset POS: a
