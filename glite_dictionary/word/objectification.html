objectification

1. NOUN

/ əbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / əbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing or representing an abstract concept as a concrete object or example
making an abstract idea concrete

Examples

The statue was an objectification of the nation's ideals of freedom and unity.
Artists often strive for the objectification of complex emotions in their work.
His theory provided a clear objectification of justice through social institutions.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin objectificare (to make into an object), from Latin obiectum (object) + -ficare (to make).

2. NOUN

/ əbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / əbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of regarding or representing something abstract, such as an idea or emotion, as a concrete object.
making an abstraction concrete

Examples

The objectification of love in literature often takes the form of symbolic items or gestures.
Philosophers have debated whether the objectification of emotions adds clarity or diminishes their meaning.
The objectification of abstract ideas can help make complex concepts easier to understand.

Etymology

From objectify + -ation; objectify from object + -ify, with -ation forming a noun of action.
