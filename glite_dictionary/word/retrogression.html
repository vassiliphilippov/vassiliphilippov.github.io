retrogression

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
The process of returning to an earlier, usually worse or less developed state.
a return to a former, usually worse, state

Examples

The country's economic retrogression alarmed investors worldwide.
After years of improvement, there was a sudden retrogression in public health standards.
Scientists warned against the retrogression of environmental protection policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression - the act of going back to a previous state, especially a less advanced one
  • Antonym: progression - the process of developing or moving gradually towards a more advanced state

Etymology

From Latin 'retrogressio' (a going back), from 'retro' (backward) + 'gradior' (to step, go).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a process in which an organism or system reverts from a more complex to a simpler form, especially in biological development or evolution
reversion to simpler biological form

Examples

The fossil record provides evidence of retrogression in certain organisms under extreme environmental pressures.
Some scientists argue that cave-dwelling animals represent cases of evolutionary retrogression.
The species exhibited retrogression, losing many of the complex features seen in its ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression - return to an earlier or less developed state
  • Antonym: progression - advancement from a simpler to a more complex form

Etymology

From Latin 'retrogressio' (a going back), from 'retrogradi' (to go backwards).
