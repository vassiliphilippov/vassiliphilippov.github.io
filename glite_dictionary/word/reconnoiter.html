to reconnoiter

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəˈnɔɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈnɔɪ.tə / (BrE)
to make a preliminary survey of an area, especially to gather military information or to find something or someone
make a preliminary survey, especially for military purposes

Examples

The soldiers were ordered to reconnoiter the area before the main force advanced.
A small group was sent ahead to reconnoiter the enemy's position discreetly.
We need to reconnoiter the mountain trail before tomorrow's expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scout - to explore or observe an area to gather information

Etymology

From French 'reconnoître', based on Latin 'recognoscere' (to recognize).
