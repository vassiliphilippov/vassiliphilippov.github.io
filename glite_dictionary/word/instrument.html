instrument

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
(Law) A formal legal document that records a contractual relationship or grants a right.
legal document stating contract or rights
legal

Examples

The parties signed the instrument before a notary public to make the agreement official.
A will is a type of instrument that distributes property upon death.
All terms and conditions are clearly stated in the legal instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document conveying or transferring property
  • Synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between parties

Etymology

From Latin instrumentum (implement, tool, document), from instruere (to build, instruct).

8. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The semantic role of an entity, typically inanimate, used by an agent to perform an action or initiate a process.
semantic role for means used in an action

Examples

In the sentence 'She cut the bread with a knife', the knife serves as the instrument.
Linguists classify 'with a pen' as an instrument phrase in 'He wrote the letter with a pen'.
The word 'hammer' functions as an instrument in the statement 'He hit the nail with a hammer'.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agent - the semantic role of the doer of an action

Etymology
