Monterey

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈreɪ / (BrE)
A town in western California, south of San Francisco, situated on a peninsula at the southern end of Monterey Bay.
town in western California, USA

Examples

We spent our vacation exploring the historic sites of Monterey and enjoying the coastal scenery.
The aquarium in Monterey is famous for its impressive marine life exhibits.
John moved to Monterey for its mild climate and beautiful ocean views.

Etymology

From Spanish 'monte rey', meaning 'king mountain'. Monterey was named by Spanish explorers in the 17th century.
