vivification

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪvəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪvɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality or act of making something more lively, spirited, or full of life; the process of enlivening or animating.
act of making lively or animated

Examples

The vivification of the old neighborhood brought a sense of energy and hope to the community.
Her speech sparked a remarkable vivification in the atmosphere of the classroom.
The painter's use of color contributed significantly to the vivification of the entire artwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animation - the state of being full of life or vigor
  • Synonym: revitalization - the action of giving new life or vigor to something
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'vivificatio', from 'vivificare' meaning 'to make alive'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɪvəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪvɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of giving life, energy, or vitality to something
the act of giving life or vitality

Examples

The vivification of the city's downtown was evident after the renovation projects.
Music can serve as a means of vivification for a tired soul.
The scientist studied the vivification of cells under different conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revitalization - the action of giving new life or energy to something
  • Synonym: animation - the act of bringing to life or imparting motion
  • Antonym: enervation - the process of draining energy or vitality

Etymology

From Latin vivificatio, from vivificare (to give life), from vivus (alive).
