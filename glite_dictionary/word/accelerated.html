accelerated

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛlɚˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a process or course) made faster or shorter than usual, especially to achieve results more quickly.
made faster than usual; expedited

Examples

She enrolled in an accelerated program to finish her degree in just two years.
The company offers an accelerated training course for experienced professionals.
He completed his coursework under an accelerated schedule to graduate early.

Synset Examples

in an accelerated program in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speeded-up - made faster or given increased speed

Etymology

Past participle of 'accelerate', from Latin 'acceleratus', past participle of 'accelerare' (to hasten).
