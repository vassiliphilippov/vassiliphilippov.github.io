penn'orth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnɚθ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəθ / (BrE)
An amount that can be bought for a penny; a penny's worth.
an amount bought for a penny
british archaic

Examples

She asked the shopkeeper for a penn'orth of sweets.
He only had enough money left for a penn'orth of apples.
Back then, a penn'orth went a lot further than it does today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pennyworth - an amount bought for a penny

Etymology

Contraction of 'pennyworth', from penny + worth.
