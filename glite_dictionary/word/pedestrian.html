pedestrian

1. ADJ

/ pəˈdɛstriən / (AmE), / pəˈdɛstriən / (BrE)
Lacking inspiration or excitement; dull or unimaginative.
dull; lacking inspiration or imagination

Examples

The novel's storyline was rather pedestrian and failed to captivate readers.
Critics dismissed the film as pedestrian and formulaic.
His solutions to the problem were so pedestrian that they surprised no one.

Synset Examples

a pedestrian movie plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uninspired - Lacking in creativity or originality
  • Synonym: prosaic - Lacking poetic beauty; commonplace
  • Antonym: imaginative - Having or showing creativity or inventiveness

Etymology

From Latin 'pedester' meaning 'going on foot', later extended metaphorically to mean 'commonplace'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈdɛstriən / (AmE), / pəˈdɛstriən / (BrE)
a person who is walking, especially in an area where vehicles go
person walking on foot

Examples

The city has built new crosswalks to keep pedestrians safe.
During rush hour, hundreds of pedestrians fill the sidewalks downtown.
A pedestrian was injured when a cyclist ignored the traffic signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walker - a person who walks, especially for exercise
  • Antonym: motorist - a person who drives a car

Etymology

From Latin pedester (going on foot), from pes, pedis (foot).

Synset POS: n
