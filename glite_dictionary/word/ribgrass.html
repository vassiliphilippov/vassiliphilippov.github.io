ribgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈrɪbˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an Old World plantain (Plantago lanceolata) with long, narrow ribbed leaves, widely found in temperate regions
a plantain with narrow ribbed leaves

Examples

The meadow was full of ribgrass, swaying gently in the summer breeze.
Cows often graze on ribgrass because it is common in pastures.
You can identify ribgrass by its long, narrow leaves with prominent veins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a plant of the genus Plantago

Etymology

From 'rib' referring to the leaf veins and 'grass' for its grass-like appearance.
