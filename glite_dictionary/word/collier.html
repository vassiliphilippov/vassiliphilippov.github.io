collier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːljər / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪə / (BrE)
a person who works in a coal mine, especially one who digs and removes coal underground
coal miner

Examples

His grandfather was a collier who spent decades underground in the Welsh coalfields.
The dangerous work of a collier helped fuel the industrial revolution.
Many former colliers struggled to find new work after the mines were closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miner - a person who works in a mine, extracting minerals

Etymology

From Old French 'colier', based on 'col' (coal), and ultimately from Latin 'carbonem'.
