Pseudopleuronectes

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˌplʊəroʊˈnɛktiːz / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˌplʊərəʊˈnɛktiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Pleuronectidae, commonly known as righteye flounders, primarily found in the northwestern Pacific Ocean and with one species in the northwestern Atlantic Ocean.
genus of righteye flounder fish

Examples

The genus Pseudopleuronectes includes important commercial fish such as the olive flounder.
Scientists found that Pseudopleuronectes species are distributed mainly in coastal waters of the northwest Pacific.
A detailed study of Pseudopleuronectes helped clarify the evolutionary history of flatfishes.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + genus name 'Pleuro­nectes', referring to similarity to other flounders.

2. NOUN

A genus of flatfish in the family Pleuronectidae, known as righteye flounders, found mostly in the northwestern Pacific Ocean, with one species in the northwestern Atlantic.
genus of righteye flounders (flatfish)

Examples

The genus Pseudopleuronectes includes several species of righteye flounders found in northern waters.
Marine biologists studied the distribution of Pseudopleuronectes along the Pacific coast.
A notable member of Pseudopleuronectes is the winter flounder, important to commercial fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleuronectes - a related genus of flatfish (righteye flounders)

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and 'pleuronectes' (a type of flatfish), referring to similarity with the genus Pleuronectes.
