superintendent

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pər.ɪnˈtɛn.dənt / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pər.ɪnˈtɛn.dənt / (BrE)
a person who manages and directs an organization, department, or institution, especially in government, police, or education
person who manages an organization

Examples

The school district appointed a new superintendent to oversee educational programs.
As superintendent of the building, she is responsible for all maintenance issues.
The police superintendent gave a press conference about the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • Synonym: director - a person who is in charge of an activity, department, or organization

Etymology

From Latin superintendens, present participle of superintendere (to oversee), from super- (over) + intendere (to direct).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌsjuːpərɪnˈtɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who manages and maintains a building, especially an apartment house, often acting as janitor and rent collector on behalf of the owner
apartment building caretaker and manager
american

Examples

The superintendent fixed the leaking pipes in our apartment.
If you lose your key, contact the superintendent for help.
The superintendent collects rent and handles maintenance requests from tenants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - a person employed to look after a building

Etymology

From Latin 'superintendens,' present participle of 'superintendere' (to oversee).
