proposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of making a suggestion or proposal, especially one put forward for discussion or consideration
the act of making a proposal

Examples

The board carefully considered her proposition before making a decision.
He made a business proposition that intrigued several investors.
During the meeting, she presented a bold proposition to launch a new product line.

Synset Examples

they listened to her proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - the act of suggesting a plan or idea for consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio' (a setting forth, statement), from 'proponere' (to set forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(logic) A statement or assertion that affirms or denies something and is either true or false.
(logic) statement that is true or false

Examples

The philosopher discussed whether the proposition was logically valid.
Mathematicians often prove the truth or falsity of a proposition.
If the proposition is false, then the argument cannot be sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statement - an expression that can be true or false
  • Antonym: question - an utterance seeking an answer rather than stating a fact

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio' (a setting forth, assertion), from 'proponere' (to put forth).

3. NOUN

Related Terms

  • Antonym: breeze - something very easy to do (as antonym/contrast)

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio' (a setting forth, statement), from 'proponere' (to set forth).
