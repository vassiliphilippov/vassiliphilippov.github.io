orpiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrpəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːpɪmənt / (BrE)
a yellow mineral composed of arsenic sulfide, found in nature often with realgar and used as an ore of arsenic
yellow mineral ore of arsenic

Examples

Medieval artists sometimes used orpiment to create vibrant yellow hues in their paintings.
The geologist identified traces of orpiment in the mine’s sulfur-rich veins.
Exposure to orpiment should be handled with care due to its arsenic content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realgar - a red mineral often found with orpiment, also an arsenic sulfide ore

Etymology

From Latin 'orpimentum', from 'auripigmentum' meaning 'gold pigment'.
