Micrococcus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkrəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of spherical, Gram-positive bacteria found widely in the environment, especially in soil, water, and dust.
genus of spherical bacteria
medical

Examples

The laboratory test identified a strain of Micrococcus in the water sample.
Some species of Micrococcus are commonly found on human skin and in soil.
Researchers studied the resistance of Micrococcus to various antibiotics.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek mikros (small) + kokkos (grain, berry).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of spherical, usually harmless bacteria commonly found in soil, water, and human skin.
a genus of spherical bacteria
medical

Examples

The Micrococcus genus includes bacteria that are widespread in the environment.
Scientists isolated Micrococcus cells from samples taken from hospital surfaces.
Micrococcus is generally regarded as non-pathogenic in healthy humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Staphylococcus - a genus of spherical bacteria; some species cause infection

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'kokkos' (berry, grain).
