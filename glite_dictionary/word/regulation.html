regulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛgjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛgjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being controlled or governed by rules or laws.
state of being controlled or governed

Examples

Under strict government regulation, the industry saw a sharp decline in pollution.
The new policy ensures the regulation of financial institutions to prevent fraud.
Environmental regulation is necessary to protect natural resources for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - the power to influence or direct behaviour or events

Etymology

From Latin 'regulatio', from 'regulare' (to control, to rule).
