capstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkæpstəʊn / (BrE)
a stone that forms the top or finishing piece of a wall or building
top stone of a wall or building

Examples

The builders placed a large capstone on the finished wall to protect it from the rain.
The ancient temple's capstone was engraved with mysterious symbols.
During restoration, the missing capstone was carefully replaced to complete the structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keystone - the central stone at the summit of an arch, locking the whole together

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'cap' + 'stone', indicating its position as the topmost stone.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæpˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkæpstəʊn / (BrE)
The final and most important part of something, serving as a crowning achievement or culmination.
final, crowning achievement

Examples

Winning the championship was the capstone of her athletic career.
The novel serves as the capstone to his decades-long writing journey.
The new research facility is seen as the capstone of the university's science program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culmination - the highest or climactic point of something
  • Synonym: milestone - a significant stage or event in development

Etymology

Literally from 'cap' (top) + 'stone', originally referring to the top stone of a structure.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
