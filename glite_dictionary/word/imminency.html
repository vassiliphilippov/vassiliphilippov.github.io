imminency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪm.ə.nən.si / (AmE), / ˈɪm.ɪ.nən.si / (BrE)
the state or quality of being imminent; the condition of something being likely to happen very soon
state of being imminent

Examples

The imminency of the storm forced residents to evacuate quickly.
Many experts warn about the imminency of a major economic downturn.
She felt the imminency of change as the company announced new policies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: immediacy - the state of being immediate or happening at once

Etymology

From Latin 'imminentia', from 'imminere' meaning 'to overhang, be near'.
