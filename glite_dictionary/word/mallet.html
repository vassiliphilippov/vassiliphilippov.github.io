mallet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪt / (AmE), / ˈmælɪt / (BrE)
a light stick with a rounded head, used to strike percussion instruments such as chimes, marimbas, and glockenspiels
a stick used to play percussion instruments

Examples

The percussionist carefully selected a mallet to play the delicate notes on the glockenspiel.
You must use a soft mallet for the marimba to produce a warm tone.
He lost one mallet during the performance and had to finish with just the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumstick - a stick used to strike drums and other percussion instruments

Etymology

From Old French *maillet*, diminutive of *mail* (hammer), from Latin *malleus* (hammer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪt / (AmE), / ˈmalɪt / (BrE)
a hammer-like tool with a large, typically wooden head, used for driving objects or shaping materials without damaging them
tool with a large head used for striking

Examples

The chef used a mallet to tenderize the meat before cooking.
A wooden mallet is often used to assemble furniture without damaging it.
She struck the chisel with a mallet to carefully shape the stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammer - a tool with a heavy metal head used for striking

Etymology

From Old French *maillet* (small hammer), diminutive of *mail* (hammer), from Latin *malleus* (hammer).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪt / (AmE), / ˈmalɪt / (BrE)
a long-handled sports implement with a heavy head, used to hit a ball in games such as polo or croquet
long-handled sports implement for hitting a ball

Examples

In croquet, each player uses a mallet to strike the ball through the hoops.
The polo player's mallet swung gracefully as he aimed at the ball.
She bought a new mallet before joining the club's weekend match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammer - a tool with a heavy head used for striking

Etymology

From Old French *maillet*, diminutive of *mail* (mallet, hammer), from Latin *malleus* (hammer).
