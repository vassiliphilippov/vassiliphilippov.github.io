snugly

1. ADV

/ ˈsnʌɡli / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡli / (BrE)
in a way that fits closely or comfortably, often providing protection or security
in a close, comfortable, or secure manner

Examples

The kitten slept snugly in the basket by the fireplace.
The lid fits snugly onto the container, keeping everything fresh.
She wrapped the scarf snugly around her neck before heading outside.

Synset Examples

concealed snugly in his hideout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: securely - in a safe or secure manner

Etymology

From "snug" (close-fitting or comfortable) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00195117-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈsnʌɡli / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡli / (BrE)
in a way that fits closely and comfortably
in a close, comfortable manner

Examples

The baby was wrapped snugly in a soft blanket on the cold night.
Her new dress fits snugly around her waist and hips.
Make sure the lid closes snugly to keep the food fresh.

Synset Examples

the vest fit snugly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosely - in a way that is not tight or close-fitting

Etymology

From 'snug' (close-fitting, comfortable) with adverbial '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00195026-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈsnʌɡli / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡli / (BrE)
In a way that is warm, comfortable, and sheltered from cold, danger, or discomfort.
warmly and comfortably sheltered

Examples

The cat slept snugly in her basket by the window.
He wrapped the baby snugly in a soft blanket to keep her warm.
Everyone was seated snugly indoors as the snow fell outside.

Synset Examples

sitting snugly by the fireside while the storm raged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfortably - in a comfortable manner

Etymology

From "snug" (meaning comfortable, sheltered) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00195221-r
Synset POS: r
