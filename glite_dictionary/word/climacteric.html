climacteric

1. NOUN

/ klaɪˈmæk.tə.rɪk / (AmE), / klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk / (BrE)
the period in a woman's life when menstruation ceases, typically occurring in middle age; menopause
period marking end of menstruation in women
medical

Examples

Some women experience emotional and physical changes during the climacteric.
The climacteric usually occurs between the ages of 45 and 55.
Doctors can provide support for women going through the climacteric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menopause - the time in a woman's life (typically between 45 and 55 years old) when menstruation ends

Etymology

From Greek klimaktērikos (critical, relating to a turning point), from klimaktēr (critical period, rung of a ladder).

2. NOUN

/ klaɪˈmæk.tɚ.ɪk / (AmE), / klaɪˈmæk.tər.ɪk / (BrE)
A period in a man's life, typically in middle age, marked by physical and emotional changes, often considered as the male counterpart to menopause.
male period of midlife change
medical

Examples

Some doctors believe that men also experience a climacteric marked by hormonal shifts and mood changes.
He went through a climacteric in his late forties, struggling with fatigue and irritability.
The concept of a male climacteric is still debated among medical professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menopause - the time in a woman's life when menstrual periods permanently stop

Etymology

From Latin climactericus, from Greek klimaktērikos (critical point).
