amebiosis

1. NOUN

/ əˌmiːbiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / əˌmiːbiˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infection caused by disease-producing amoebae, typically of the genus Entamoeba
infection by disease-causing amoeba
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with amebiosis after presenting severe gastrointestinal symptoms.
Travelers to certain regions are at increased risk of contracting amebiosis.
Proper sanitation can help prevent outbreaks of amebiosis in communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoebiasis - infection with a pathogenic amoeba, especially Entamoeba histolytica

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'ameba' and '-osis' (condition or disease).
