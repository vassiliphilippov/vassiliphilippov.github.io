amorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈamərəsnəs / (BrE)
a strong feeling of romantic love or desire
romantic love or desire

Examples

His obvious amorousness made everyone around him feel slightly uncomfortable.
There was a touch of amorousness in the way she looked at him across the room.
The poet wrote with a deep amorousness that reflected his passion for life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin amorosus (loving), from amor (love).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.ɚ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæm.ər.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being amorous; strong feelings of love or sexual desire
state of being amorous; sexual or romantic desire

Examples

There was an unmistakable amorousness in the way he looked at her.
The novel is rich with themes of amorousness and passion.
Her amorousness was evident in her affectionate gestures toward her partner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infatuation - an intense but short-lived passion or admiration
  • Antonym: platonic - relating to affectionate but non-sexual love

Etymology

From Latin 'amōrus' (loving) + -ness.
