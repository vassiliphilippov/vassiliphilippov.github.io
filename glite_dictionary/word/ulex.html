Ulex

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːlɛks / (AmE), / ˈjuːlɛks / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian spiny shrubs, commonly known as gorse, with bright yellow flowers and green stems.
genus of spiny shrubs (gorse)

Examples

The hills were covered with dense patches of Ulex in full bloom.
Botanists have classified this plant as belonging to the Ulex genus.
Many insects depend on Ulex flowers for food during early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorse - a spiny evergreen shrub with yellow flowers, also known as furze

Etymology

From Latin 'ūlex' meaning 'a shrub' or 'gorse'.
