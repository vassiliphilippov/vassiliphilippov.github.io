humorist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmərɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmərɪst / (BrE)
a person who writes, speaks, or performs in a way that entertains people by being amusing or funny
a person who uses humor to entertain

Examples

The famous humorist delivered a witty speech that had the audience laughing nonstop.
She aspires to become a professional humorist and write comedic essays for magazines.
Many humorists use satire to highlight social issues in a light-hearted way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comedian - a person whose job is to make people laugh by telling jokes or acting in a funny way

Etymology

From French *humoriste*, from Latin *humor* (liquid, humor).
