Katsuwonidae

1. NOUN

/ kætˌsuːˈoʊnɪdiː / (AmE), / kætˌsuːˈəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of oceanic bonitos, sometimes considered separate in certain fish classifications
family of oceanic bonitos (fish)

Examples

The Katsuwonidae are sometimes classified separately from other mackerel-like fishes.
Some taxonomists recognize Katsuwonidae as a distinct family within oceanic fish.
Research papers often debate whether Katsuwonidae should be merged with Scombridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scombridae - the mackerel family, often including bonitos and tunas

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from genus Katsuwonus + -idae (suffix for animal family names).
