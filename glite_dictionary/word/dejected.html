dejected

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈdʒɛktɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛktɪd / (BrE)
Sad and depressed; dispirited.
sad and dispirited

Examples

She looked dejected after hearing the disappointing news.
Despite his hard work, he felt dejected when he was not selected for the team.
The rain added to the already dejected mood of the group.

Synset Examples

is dejected but trying to look cheerful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despondent - in low spirits from loss of hope
  • Antonym: elated - ecstatically happy

Etymology

From Latin deicere (to cast down), past participle deiectus.
