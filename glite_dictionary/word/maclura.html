Maclura

1. NOUN

/ məkˈlʊrə / (AmE), / məkˈlʊərə / (BrE)
a genus of yellowwood trees or shrubs in the mulberry family, native to North and South America and Asia
genus of yellowwood trees or shrubs

Examples

The wood of Maclura is prized for its hardness and durability.
Some species in the Maclura genus produce fruits resembling oranges.
Maclura trees are often planted for windbreaks and hedges.

Etymology

Named after William Maclure (1763–1840), an American geologist.
