Pythius

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪθiəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪθiəs / (BrE)
an epithet of Apollo, deriving from his slaying of the serpent Python
epithet of Apollo in Greek mythology

Examples

The temple was dedicated to Pythius, celebrating Apollo's triumph over the serpent.
Ancient hymns praised Pythius for his wisdom and strength.
Scholars often refer to Apollo as Pythius when discussing his Delphic aspect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apollo - an important Olympian god in Greek and Roman mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πύθιος (Pythios), referring to Apollo's victory over Python.
