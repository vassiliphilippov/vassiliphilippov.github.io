oblong

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.lɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒb.lɒŋ / (BrE)
A plane figure that is longer in one direction than the other, especially a rectangle with unequal adjacent sides.
elongated rectangular or oval shape

Examples

The architect designed windows in the shape of an oblong.
She drew an oblong on the chalkboard to represent the garden plot.
The tablecloth was patterned with rows of yellow oblongs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectangle - a four-sided figure with opposite sides equal and all angles right angles
  • Antonym: square - a plane figure with four equal sides and four right angles
  • Synonym: oval - a plane curve resembling a stretched circle

Etymology

From Latin *oblongus* meaning 'rather long'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːb.lɔːŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒb.lɒŋ / (BrE)
having a shape that is longer than it is wide, especially with parallel sides
longer than wide; elongated shape

Examples

The plate had an oblong design, making it perfect for serving baguettes.
She picked up an oblong stone from the riverbank and slipped it into her pocket.
The room was oblong, with windows only on the longer walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval - egg-shaped; having a rounded and elongated form
  • Antonym: square - having all sides equal and all angles right angles
  • Antonym: circular - having the shape of a circle

Etymology

From Latin 'oblongus' (rather long, elongated).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑb.lɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒb.lɒŋ / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other object) having an elongated shape with nearly parallel sides, longer than it is wide.
elongated shape with parallel sides

Examples

The plant's oblong leaves are easily distinguished by their parallel sides and uniform width.
She picked up an oblong stone that was longer than it was wide.
The botanist described the specimen's leaves as oblong rather than oval.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oval - egg-shaped or elliptical
  • Degree Higher: rectangular - shaped like a rectangle, with sides at right angles

Etymology

From Latin oblongus, from ob- (in the direction of) + longus (long).
