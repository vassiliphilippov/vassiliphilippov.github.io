cynosure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəˌʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəsjʊə / (BrE)
a person or thing that serves as a guide or focus of attention and admiration
object of guidance or admiration

Examples

Her courage became the cynosure for others facing adversity.
The new museum is the cynosure of the city’s cultural district.
Let your ideals be the cynosure that leads your actions.

Synset Examples

let faith be your cynosure to walk by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guiding star - something that serves as a guide or inspiration

Etymology

From Latin 'cynosura' (the constellation Ursa Minor), from Greek 'kynosoura' meaning 'dog's tail'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.nəˌʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.ə.ʃʊə / (BrE)
a person or thing that attracts a great deal of attention or admiration
center of attention or admiration

Examples

The actress was the cynosure of all eyes as she walked down the red carpet.
His innovative work quickly became the cynosure of the scientific community.
The new sculpture in the park is a cynosure for art lovers and tourists alike.

Synset Examples

if he was the cynosure of all eyes, he didn't notice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerpiece - main or most important feature
  • Synonym: focus - the main point of attention or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'cynosura', from Greek 'kynosoura' (dog’s tail), originally referring to the constellation Ursa Minor.
