seeder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to seed clouds, typically to induce rainfall by dispersing substances into the air
person who seeds clouds

Examples

The seeder prepared the aircraft for cloud seeding operations over the drought-stricken area.
Each seeder on the team was responsible for releasing silver iodide at precise altitudes.
After the experiment, the seeder reported increased rainfall in the targeted zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud seeding - the process of dispersing substances into clouds to induce precipitation

Etymology

From verb 'seed' (to sow or spread seed) + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A kitchen utensil used to remove seeds from fruit.
tool for removing seeds from fruit

Examples

I used a seeder to prepare the cherries for the pie.
This handy seeder makes removing pomegranate seeds much easier.
After slicing the apples, she reached for the seeder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitter - a utensil used to remove pits or stones from fruit

Etymology

From 'seed' + '-er', indicating an agent that removes seeds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04174289-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a machine or device used to sow seed evenly over the ground, especially in agriculture.
machine for sowing seeds

Examples

The farmer used a seeder to plant the wheat efficiently across the field.
After calibrating the seeder, they began to sow clover throughout the newly cleared land.
Our community garden recently purchased a small seeder to make planting easier for volunteers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sower - a person or machine that plants seeds
  • Synonym: planter - a machine for planting seeds or seedlings in the ground

Etymology

From 'seed' + '-er', denoting something that performs the action of seeding.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04174407-n
Synset POS: n
