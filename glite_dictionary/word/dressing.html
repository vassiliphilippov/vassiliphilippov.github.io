dressing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of treating animal hides to convert them into leather
treatment of hides for leather

Examples

After the initial cleaning, the dressing of the hides takes several weeks.
The factory specializes in the dressing of high-quality leather for shoes and bags.
Modern chemical methods have improved the efficiency of dressing animal skins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanning - the process of converting animal hides into leather using tannins

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
a mixture of seasoned ingredients, such as bread, herbs, and spices, used to stuff meat, poultry, or vegetables before cooking
seasoned stuffing for meat or vegetables

Examples

She prepared a delicious sage and onion dressing to serve with the turkey.
The recipe calls for cornbread dressing baked inside the chicken.
Many families have their own special dressing for holiday dinners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffing - mixture used to fill poultry, meat, or vegetables before cooking

Etymology

From dress (to prepare) + -ing; culinary sense dates from the 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
A substance, such as fertilizer or manure, applied to soil to make it more fertile.
fertilizer or manure applied to soil

Examples

Gardeners often add compost as a dressing for their vegetable beds.
The wheat crop responded well to the top dressing of manure applied in early spring.
After sowing, a quick dressing of fertilizer can greatly boost plant growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to increase its fertility

Etymology

From Old French *dresser* (to arrange, prepare), from Vulgar Latin *directiare* (make straight or right).

Examples

It is important to change the dressing regularly to prevent infection.

Related Terms

From the verb 'dress' in the sense 'to treat (a wound)', ultimately from Old French 'dresser' (to arrange, prepare).
