Brunfelsia americana

1. NOUN

/ brʊnˈfɛlziə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑnə / (AmE), / brʊnˈfɛlziə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A West Indian shrub of the nightshade family, known for its fragrant, showy yellowish-white flowers.
West Indian fragrant shrub with yellowish-white flowers

Examples

The botanical garden is home to a rare Brunfelsia americana with remarkable flowers.
Gardeners prize Brunfelsia americana for its showy, fragrant blossoms that bloom at night.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Brunfelsia americana in tropical environments.

Etymology

Genus name from Otto Brunfels, a German botanist; species Latin for 'of America'.
