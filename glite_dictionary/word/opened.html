opened

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊpənd / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənd / (BrE)
(of the mouth or eyes) not closed; in a state of being apart or uncovered.
not closed (of mouth or eyes)

Examples

She stared at him with her opened eyes, wide with surprise.
His opened mouth suggested he was about to speak but hesitated.
The baby slept with her lips slightly opened.

Synset Examples

keep your eyes open
his mouth slightly opened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed - (of the mouth or eyes) not open; shut
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01658803-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊpənd / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənd / (BrE)
having been made open, available, or accessible, often for general use or public access
made open or accessible

Examples

The gallery has a special exhibit for the opened wing.
Only recently opened shops can apply for this permit.
The museum's opened section attracted hundreds of visitors on its first day.

Synset Examples

the newly opened road

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed - not open or accessible
  • Degree Lower: open - to make accessible or cause to be open
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01657577-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈoʊpənd / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənd / (BrE)
Not sealed or having been unsealed; no longer closed.
not sealed or having been unsealed

Examples

She found the opened envelope on her desk and realized someone had read her mail.
Please dispose of any opened containers in the refrigerator before the inspection.
The store refused to accept returns on any opened packages.

Synset Examples

the letter was already open
the opened package lay on the table

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed - not open or sealed; shut
  • Antonym: sealed - closed tightly so that nothing can enter or leave
  • Synonym: open - not closed or blocked
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02094323-s
Synset POS: s
