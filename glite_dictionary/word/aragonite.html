aragonite

1. NOUN

/ əˈræɡəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / əˈraɡənʌɪt / (BrE)
a white or colorless mineral form of crystalline calcium carbonate, occurring naturally in marine and freshwater environments, and dimorphic with calcite
a mineral form of calcium carbonate

Examples

The delicate shells of some marine organisms are composed primarily of aragonite.
Researchers discovered a deposit rich in aragonite beneath the ancient seabed.
Unlike calcite, aragonite is less stable under standard temperature and pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcite - a common mineral form of calcium carbonate, and dimorph of aragonite

Etymology

Named after Aragon, a region in Spain, where it was first discovered in 1797.
