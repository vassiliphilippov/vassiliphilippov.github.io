pheromone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr.əˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfɛrəˌməʊn / (BrE)
a chemical substance released by an animal that affects the physiology or behavior of other animals of the same species
chemical signal between animals of same species

Examples

Certain ants release a pheromone to alert others when food is found.
The moth is attracted by the female's pheromone during mating season.
Researchers studied how the pheromone influenced behavior in the colony.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hormone - a regulatory substance produced in organisms to stimulate specific cells or tissues

Etymology

From Greek 'pherein' (to carry) + 'hormone'. Coined in the 1950s.
