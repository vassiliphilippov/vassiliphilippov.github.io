Aepyceros

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈpɪsəˌrɒs / (AmE), / iːˈpɪsərɒs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes that includes the impala.
genus of African antelopes (impala)

Examples

The genus Aepyceros contains only one living species, the impala.
Researchers studying Aepyceros focus on the unique adaptations of the impala to savanna habitats.
Fossil records show that Aepyceros had a wider historical range across Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impala - a medium-sized African antelope (Aepyceros melampus)

Etymology

From Greek aipos (high) + keras (horn), referring to the impala's long, curved horns.
