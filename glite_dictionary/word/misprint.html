misprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.prɪnt / (BrE)
A mistake in printed text caused by mechanical or typographical error.
an error in printed text

Examples

I noticed a misprint in the first paragraph of the article.
The publisher apologized for the misprint on the book's cover.
Despite several edits, a misprint still appeared in the final edition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typo - a minor typing error in printed or digital text
  • Antonym: correction - a change that rectifies an error in printed material

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrong, incorrect) + 'print'.

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈprɪnt / (AmE), / mɪsˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to print something incorrectly, especially by making a mistake in spelling, layout, or content during printing or publishing
print incorrectly; make an error in printed text

Examples

The publisher accidentally misprinted the author's name on the book cover.
Newspapers often have to issue corrections when they misprint facts in an article.
If you misprint the number, customers may not be able to contact us.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: print - to produce text or images on paper using a machine

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'print'.

Synset POS: v
