to antedate

1. VERB

/ ˈæn.tɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to occur or exist before a certain date or event; to be earlier in time.
occur or exist before a certain date

Examples

The ruins clearly antedate the written records of the city.
This discovery may antedate what we believed about human migration.
Bronze artifacts in the area antedate those found elsewhere by several centuries.

Synset Examples

Stone tools precede bronze tools

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to postdate - to occur or exist after a certain date
  • Synonym: to precede - to come before in time or order

Etymology

From Latin ante- (before) + date (given time).

WordNet ID: oewn-02718318-v

2. VERB

/ ˈæn.t̬iˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti.deɪt / (BrE)
to establish that something happened or existed earlier than another event or date
establish as happening earlier

Examples

The researchers were able to antedate the earliest known use of the tool by several centuries.
Experts antedated the manuscript to the 15th century, earlier than previously believed.
Recent discoveries antidate the appearance of the species by thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to postdate - to establish something occurred after another event

Etymology

From Latin 'ante-' (before) + 'date' (datum, given).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277856-v
Synset POS: v
