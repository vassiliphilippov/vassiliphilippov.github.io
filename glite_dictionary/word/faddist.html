faddist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfædɪst / (AmE), / ˈfadɪst / (BrE)
a person who is excessively concerned with following fads or trends, particularly for a short time
person addicted to fads or trends

Examples

As a known faddist, she often changed her wardrobe to match the latest styles.
Every diet craze seems to attract at least one enthusiastic faddist in our office.
The media sometimes portrays celebrities as faddists chasing after the newest trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trendsetter - a person who leads the way in fashion or ideas
  • Antonym: conservative - a person resistant to change or new trends

Etymology

From 'fad' (a craze) + '-ist' (one who practices or is concerned with something).
