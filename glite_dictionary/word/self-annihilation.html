self-annihilation

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf əˌnaɪəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf əˌnʌɪəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of destroying or killing oneself; self-destruction.
the act of destroying oneself

Examples

Philosophers have debated whether self-annihilation can ever be morally justified.
The story deals with a character struggling with thoughts of self-annihilation.
In some religious traditions, self-annihilation is seen as the ultimate escape from suffering.

Synset Examples

it is a crime to commit suicide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally killing oneself
  • Antonym: self-preservation - the act of keeping oneself alive and safe

Etymology

self- + annihilation, from Latin *annihilare* (to reduce to nothing).
