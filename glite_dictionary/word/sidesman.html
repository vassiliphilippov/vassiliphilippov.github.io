sidesman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdzˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdzmən / (BrE)
(in the Church of England) a man appointed to assist the churchwarden, especially by collecting offerings during a church service.
male church assistant, collector of offerings
british

Examples

During the Sunday service, the sidesman skillfully passed the collection plate down each pew.
He volunteered to serve as a sidesman at his local church, assisting with the offertory collections.
The vicar thanked each sidesman for their faithful support throughout the year.
