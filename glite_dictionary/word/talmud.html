Talmud

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑlmʊd, ˈtɑlməd / (AmE), / ˈtælmʊd, ˈtælməd / (BrE)
the central collection of ancient rabbinic writings, including the Mishna and the Gemara, forming the foundation of Jewish law and tradition
central Jewish religious text

Examples

Scholars around the world have studied the Talmud for centuries.
The Talmud provides detailed commentary on Jewish law and ethics.
She referenced the Talmud to explain an ancient religious tradition.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Talmūdh* (teaching, study), via Late Latin *Talmud*.
