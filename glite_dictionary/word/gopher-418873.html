gopher

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊfər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
a burrowing edible land tortoise found in southeastern North America

Examples

Scientists are studying the gopher population in Georgia's pine forests.
Some communities have traditions of cooking gophers as a local delicacy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.fər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.fər / (BrE)
a burrowing rodent of the family Geomyidae, native to Central America and southwestern North America, known for its large external cheek pouches
a burrowing rodent with cheek pouches

Examples

A gopher popped its head out of the tunnel in the backyard.
Farmers often struggle with gophers damaging their crops and irrigation systems.
The scientist studied the underground networks built by the gopher colony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - a rodent with external cheek pouches, of the family Geomyidae

Etymology

Probably from Canadian French gaufre (honeycomb), referring to its tunnels.

3. NOUN

/ ˈgoʊfər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊfə / (BrE)
a small burrowing rodent with fur-lined cheek pouches, found chiefly in North and Central America, noted for digging extensive tunnel systems and sometimes damaging crops
burrowing rodent with cheek pouches

Examples

The farmer discovered a new gopher tunnel running through his carrot patch.
They set traps to prevent gophers from destroying the vegetable garden.
Watching the gopher pop up from its burrow delighted the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground squirrel - another type of burrowing rodent
  • Synonym: vole - small burrowing or field rodent

Etymology

Probably from French 'gaufre', meaning 'waffle', referring to the animal's honeycomb-like tunnels.

a person who is zealously energetic, especially a salesperson
very energetic person, esp. a salesperson

Examples

The new hire is a real gopher—he never seems to slow down.
As a seasoned gopher, she could outsell anyone in the office.
The company was looking for a gopher to energize their sales team.

Etymology

From 'gopher', the animal known for its constant activity, applied figuratively to a person.
