unseasonable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsizənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːzənəbl / (BrE)
happening or done at an inappropriate or awkward time; badly timed.
badly timed or inappropriate in timing

Examples

His unseasonable comment disrupted the meeting at a crucial point.
I hope you will pardon my unseasonable visit at this late hour.
Their laughter felt unseasonable given the seriousness of the situation.

Synset Examples

an ill-timed intervention
you think my intrusion unseasonable
an untimely remark
it was the wrong moment for a joke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-timed - done at a bad or inappropriate time
  • Synonym: untimely - occurring at an unsuitable or premature time
  • Antonym: timely - done or occurring at a suitable time

Etymology

From un- (not) + seasonable (timely; suitable to the season or occasion), originally from Latin 'sationem'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsiːzənəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːzənəbl / (BrE)
(of weather or events) not typical or suitable for the time of year, often regarded as undesirable.
not typical for the season

Examples

The unseasonable cold forced us to wear winter coats in early May.
Heavy rainfall in July was considered highly unseasonable for the region.
Many tourists were disappointed by the unseasonable heat during their autumn holiday.

Synset Examples

a sudden unseasonable blizzard
unseasonable bright blue weather in November

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seasonal - typical or suitable for a particular season
  • Synonym: unseasonal - not typical or usual for the season (variant form)

Etymology

From un- (not) + seasonable, meaning not appropriate to the season.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01497888-a
Synset POS: a
