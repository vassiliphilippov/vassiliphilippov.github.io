maigre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪɡrə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪɡrə / (BrE)
A large European marine food fish (Argyrosomus regius) of the drum family, valued for its flesh.
large European marine food fish

Examples

The chef prepared grilled maigre with a lemon and herb sauce.
Fishermen reported catching several maigre during the summer season.
The maigre is highly prized in Mediterranean cuisine for its delicate flesh.

Etymology

From French 'maigre', meaning 'lean', possibly referring to the fish's body shape.
