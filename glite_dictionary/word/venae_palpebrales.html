venae palpebrales

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnaɪ ˌpælpəˈbreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈveɪniː ˌpalpɛˈbraːliːz / (BrE)
the veins located in the eyelids that drain blood from the eyelid tissues
eyelid veins in human anatomy
medical

Examples

The venae palpebrales are responsible for draining blood from the upper and lower eyelids.
During the procedure, care was taken not to damage the venae palpebrales.
Inflammation in the venae palpebrales can cause swelling of the eyelids.

Etymology

From Latin 'vēnae' (veins) and 'palpebrales' (of the eyelids).
