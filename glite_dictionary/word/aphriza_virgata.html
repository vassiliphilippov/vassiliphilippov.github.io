Aphriza virgata

1. NOUN

/ əˈfraɪzə vərˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / əˈfraɪzə vɜːˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
A sandpiper-like shorebird found along the Pacific coasts of North and South America, also known as the surfbird.
shorebird of Pacific coasts (surfbird)

Examples

Birdwatchers along the Pacific coast often search for the elusive Aphriza virgata during migration season.
The Aphriza virgata feeds on insects and crustaceans found among coastal rocks.
Researchers tagged several Aphriza virgata to study their migratory patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surfbird - another common name for Aphriza virgata

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Aphriza and species epithet virgata meaning 'striped' in Latin.
