sturdiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɝː.di.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɜː.di.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong, robust, and able to withstand pressure or hardship
strength or robustness

Examples

The sturdiness of the bridge gave travelers confidence during the storm.
She admired the sturdiness of the hiking boots before buying them.
Despite its age, the table's sturdiness was impressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • Antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From sturdy + -ness, with 'sturdy' from Old French 'estourdi' (dazed, stunned).

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɝːdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɜːdi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong, firm, and resolute, especially in character or principles
strength and firmness in character

Examples

Her sturdiness helped her face adversity with courage and optimism.
The sturdiness of his beliefs made him a respected leader in the community.
Despite criticism, she showed remarkable sturdiness in standing by her values.

Synset Examples

sturdiness of moral principle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resoluteness - firm determination
  • Antonym: weakness - lack of strength or firmness in character

Etymology

From sturdy + -ness; 'sturdy' from Old French 'estourdi' meaning 'dazed', later influenced by sense of strong or robust.
