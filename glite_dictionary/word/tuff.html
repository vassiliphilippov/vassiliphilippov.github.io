tuff

1. NOUN

/ tʌf / (AmE), / tʌf / (BrE)
A type of hard, porous rock formed from compacted volcanic ash ejected during eruptions.
porous volcanic rock made of compacted ash

Examples

The layers of tuff in the canyon reveal a history of ancient volcanic eruptions.
Builders sometimes use volcanic tuff as a lightweight construction material.
Yellowstone National Park features dramatic cliffs formed from tuff deposited by past supervolcanoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufa - a porous rock composed of calcium carbonate, often deposited by springs

Etymology

From Italian *tufo*, from Latin *tophus* (stone, tufa).
