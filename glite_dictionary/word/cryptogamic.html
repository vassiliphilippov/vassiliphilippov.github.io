cryptogamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈɡæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈɡamɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of cryptogams (plants such as algae, ferns, mosses, and fungi that reproduce by spores, without flowers or seeds)
relating to cryptogams (spore-producing plants)

Examples

The study focused on cryptogamic plants found in rainforest understories.
Lichens are a cryptogamic association of algae and fungi.
Many cryptogamic species play a vital role in the ecosystem's nutrient cycling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptogam - a plant that reproduces by spores, without flowers or seeds

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cryptogamia' (the group of non-flowering plants), from Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'gamos' (marriage).
