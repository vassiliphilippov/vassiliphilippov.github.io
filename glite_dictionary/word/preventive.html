preventive

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
A substance, device, or measure designed to prevent conception or the occurrence of something undesirable, especially in a medical context.
contraceptive agent or preventative device
medical

Examples

Many couples use a preventive to avoid unwanted pregnancies.
The clinic provides information about different preventive options available to women.
She discussed with her doctor which preventive would best suit her lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraceptive - device or drug preventing pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin 'praeventivus', from 'praevenire' meaning 'to come before, prevent'.

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
A medicine or measure used to stop an illness or disease from occurring or getting worse.
remedy or measure that prevents disease

Examples

The doctor recommended several preventives before the start of the flu season.
Vaccination is considered one of the most effective preventives against infectious diseases.
They used mosquito nets as a simple preventive for malaria.

Synset Examples

the doctor recommended several preventatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophylactic - a medicine or measure to prevent disease
  • Antonym: cure - a treatment that eliminates a disease

Etymology

From Latin 'praeventīvus', from 'praevenire' (to come before, prevent).

3. ADJ

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
Intended to stop something from happening or to hinder its occurrence, especially to avoid disease or problems.
intended to prevent or hinder

Examples

The doctor recommended a preventive treatment to reduce the risk of infection.
Many public health campaigns focus on preventive measures rather than cures.
She believes regular exercise is a preventive strategy against many illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precautionary - intended to prevent harm or risk
  • Antonym: remedial - intended as a remedy

Etymology

From Latin 'praeventivus', from 'praevenire' (to anticipate, prevent).
