striper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪpər / (AmE), / ˈstraɪpə / (BrE)
A marine fish with dark horizontal stripes, valued as a food and game fish, that migrates upriver to spawn, sometimes classified in the genus Morone.
a marine fish with dark horizontal stripes

Examples

Fishermen often hope to catch a large striper during the spring migration.
The chef prepared the fresh striper with lemon and herbs for dinner.
Many anglers consider the striper to be one of the best fighting game fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped bass - a species of striper, Morone saxatilis, known for its dark stripes

Etymology

From 'stripe' + '-er', referring to the fish's striped appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪpər / (AmE), / ˈstraɪpə / (BrE)
a serviceman who wears stripes on the uniform to indicate rank or years of service
serviceman with rank stripes

Examples

After his promotion, he became a respected striper among his peers.
As a four-striper, she held a position of authority on the base.
Only one striper attended the meeting, making the group mostly junior personnel.

Synset Examples

he's a four-striper

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: private - lowest military rank, often without stripes
  • Degree Higher: sergeant - mid-level military rank, typically with more stripes than a private

Etymology

From 'stripe' (rank insignia) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
