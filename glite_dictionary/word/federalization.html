federalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of bringing an institution, system, or area under the control or authority of a federal government.
process of coming under federal control

Examples

The federalization of the police force sparked debate among states' rights advocates.
Recent years have seen the federalization of disaster response policies.
Opponents of federalization argue that local control is more effective for education policy.

Synset Examples

the federalization of postal service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalization - the process of bringing an industry or assets under national government control
  • Antonym: decentralization - the transfer of authority from central to local government

Etymology

From federal + -ization; modeled after the noun 'federalize.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of bringing something under the control of a federal government
transfer to federal government control

Examples

The federalization of the railway system improved efficiency and safety across the country.
Debates continue about the potential federalization of healthcare services in the nation.
Many citizens were concerned about the federalization of local police forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalization - transfer of a private asset or industry into government ownership or control
  • Antonym: privatization - the process of transferring an enterprise or industry from the public sector to the private sector

Etymology

From 'federalize' + '-ation', based on Latin 'foedus' (league, covenant).
