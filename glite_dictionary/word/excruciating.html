excruciating

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskruːʃieɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈskruːʃieɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely painful; causing intense physical or mental suffering
extremely painful

Examples

The patient experienced excruciating pain after the surgery.
He gave an excruciating description of his ordeal during the interview.
My headache became so excruciating that I had to lie down in a dark room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agonizing - causing great physical or mental pain
  • Degree Lower: painful - causing physical or emotional pain
  • Degree Higher: unbearable - impossible to endure; intolerable

Etymology

From Latin excruciare (to torture, torment), from ex- (out of) + cruciare (to crucify, torture).
