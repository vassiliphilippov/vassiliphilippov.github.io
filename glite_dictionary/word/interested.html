interested

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪntrəstɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪntrɪstɪd / (BrE)
showing curiosity, concern, or fascination about something; wanting to know or learn more.
showing curiosity or concern

Examples

She has always been interested in learning new languages.
The children looked interested as the teacher began the science experiment.
If you're interested in joining the club, please sign up here.

Synset Examples

an interested audience
interested in sports
was interested to hear about her family
interested in knowing who was on the telephone
interested spectators

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uninterested - not showing interest or concern; not curious or engaged
  • Synonym: curious - wanting to know or learn something

Etymology

Past participle of 'interest', from Old French 'interest', from Latin 'interesse' (to be between, matter).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.tɚ.stɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tər.ɪstɪd / (BrE)
having a personal involvement, claim, or share in something; affected by or concerned with a situation, especially due to personal benefit or stake.
having a personal stake or involvement

Examples

Only the interested parties were invited to the confidential meeting.
The settlement will be distributed among all interested individuals with a valid claim.
All interested shareholders must attend the annual general meeting.

Synset Examples

a memorandum to those concerned
an enterprise in which three men are concerned
factors concerned in the rise and fall of epidemics
the interested parties met to discuss the business

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disinterested - having no personal involvement or bias; impartial

Etymology

From interest + -ed, formed from the noun 'interest' (from Old French, from Latin 'interesse') plus the adjective suffix '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01519775-s
Synset POS: s
