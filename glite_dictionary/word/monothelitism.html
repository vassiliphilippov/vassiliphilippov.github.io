Monothelitism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈθɛlɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈθɛlɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
the theological doctrine that Christ had only one will despite having two natures, human and divine, which was declared heretical by the Third Council of Constantinople
the doctrine that Christ had one will

Examples

The controversy over Monothelitism was a major issue in the early Christian church.
Historians often reference Monothelitism when discussing debates about Christ's nature.
The doctrine of Monothelitism was officially condemned as heresy in 681 CE.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'thelēma' (will) + '-ism'.
