discarded

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɑːrdɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːdɪd / (BrE)
thrown away or no longer wanted; cast aside or rejected
thrown away; no longer wanted

Examples

Old computers are often discarded when they become obsolete.
She picked up a discarded newspaper from the bench and started reading.
The beach was littered with discarded plastic bottles.

Synset Examples

wearing someone's cast-off clothes
throwaway children living on the streets
salvaged some thrown-away furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast-off - no longer wanted; thrown away
  • Antonym: retained - kept or held on to; not thrown away

Etymology

From the verb 'discard,' itself from Middle French 'descarder', from Old Italian 'scartare' (to reject, discard).
