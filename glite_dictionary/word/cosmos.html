Cosmos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːz.moʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɒs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American plants of the daisy family, cultivated for their colorful, daisy-like flowers.
genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners enjoy growing Cosmos for their vibrant blooms and easy care.
The Cosmos plants added a splash of color to the summer garden.
Butterflies are often attracted to the colorful flowers of Cosmos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - common wildflower of the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Greek 'kosmos' meaning 'order, world, universe,' adapted as the botanical name.
