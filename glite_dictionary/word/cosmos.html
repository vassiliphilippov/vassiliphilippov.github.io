cosmos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑzmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɒs / (BrE)
the universe seen as a well-ordered whole; all of space and everything in it
the universe as an ordered whole

Examples

The telescope allowed scientists to look deeper into the cosmos than ever before.
Many philosophers have pondered the origins of the cosmos.
She gazed up at the night sky, feeling small in the vast cosmos.

Synset Examples

they study the evolution of the universe
the biggest tree in existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universe - all of space, time, and everything contained in it
  • Antonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion (often contrasted with 'cosmos' as order)

Etymology

From Greek kosmos (order, universe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑz.moʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɒs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants native to Mexico and Central America, known for their brightly colored, daisy-like blooms and pinnate leaves, often grown as ornamental annuals
a genus of colorful annual flowers

Examples

The cosmos in her garden bloomed in vibrant pink and orange all autumn long.
Many gardeners plant cosmos because they attract butterflies and require little maintenance.
We admired the tall stems and delicate leaves of the cosmos swaying gently in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a plant with bright yellow or orange flowers, commonly cultivated as an annual ornamental

Etymology

From Greek *kosmos* (order, ornament), referring to the harmonious arrangement of petals.
