Pelecanoididae

1. NOUN

/ pɛlɪˌkænɔɪˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / pɛlɪˌkænɔɪˈdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small diving seabirds known as diving petrels, found in the Southern Hemisphere.
family of diving petrels (birds)

Examples

The Pelecanoididae are specialized seabirds adapted for underwater pursuit of prey.
Researchers studied the distribution of Pelecanoididae off the coasts of New Zealand.
Pelecanoididae differ from other seabird families in their unique diving behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diving petrel - a small seabird that dives for food

Etymology

From New Latin Pelecanoididae, from genus Pelecanoides (type genus, meaning 'pelican-like').

2. NOUN

/ pɛlɪˌkænoʊˈɪdɪdi / (AmE), / pɛlɪˌkænɔɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small seabirds known as diving petrels, found in the Southern Hemisphere, characterized by their compact bodies and diving habits.
family of diving petrels (seabirds)

Examples

The Pelecanoididae family includes several species of diving petrels found near New Zealand and South America.
Members of the Pelecanoididae are well adapted for underwater pursuit of small fish and plankton.
Ornithologists often study Pelecanoididae to understand the evolution of diving behavior in seabirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diving petrel - small seabird closely related to auks; member of Pelecanoididae

Etymology

From modern Latin Pelecanoides (type genus) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for families).
