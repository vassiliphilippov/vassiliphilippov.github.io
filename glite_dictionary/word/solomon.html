Solomon

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑːləmən / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmən / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the son of David and king of Israel, renowned for his wisdom, who ruled in the 10th century BC.
biblical king of Israel known for wisdom

Examples

Solomon is often cited as one of the wisest figures in biblical history.
The tale of Solomon and the two women reveals his renowned sense of justice.
Many proverbs are traditionally attributed to Solomon in the Old Testament.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: David - biblical king of Israel; Solomon's father
  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality for which Solomon is most famous

Etymology

From Hebrew Šəlōmōh, Greek Solomōn, Latin Solomon.
