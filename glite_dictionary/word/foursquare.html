foursquare

1. ADV

/ ˈfɔrˌskwɛr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskwɛː / (BrE)
in a square position or with a square shape; in a solid, unyielding manner
in a square or solid position

Examples

The monument stood foursquare against the horizon, unshaken by the wind.
The house sat foursquare on its foundation, sturdy and immovable.
He placed the crate foursquare on the floor, making sure it wouldn't tip over.

Synset Examples

the building stood foursquare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squarely - in a direct, straight, or square position or manner

Etymology

Formed from 'four' and 'square,' referring to something having four equal sides or a solid, square shape.

2. ADV

/ ˈfɔrˌskwɛr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskwɛə / (BrE)
in a firm, unwavering, and uncompromising manner
with firmness and conviction

Examples

The president stood foursquare against any attempt to undermine civil rights.
She spoke foursquare in support of her beliefs, never backing down under pressure.
The committee acted foursquare in defense of their principles throughout the negotiations.

Synset Examples

he stood foursquare for religious liberty and toleration
dealt straightforwardly with all issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfastly - in a resolutely firm and unwavering manner
  • Antonym: waveringly - in a way that shows hesitation or lack of firmness

Etymology

From Middle English, meaning 'square on all four sides,' figuratively extended to 'firm' or 'steadfast.'

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrˌskwɛr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌskweə / (BrE)
Firm and unwavering in conviction, purpose, or stance; resolutely determined.
firm and unwavering; resolute

Examples

She stood foursquare in her beliefs, undeterred by criticism from others.
The judge delivered a foursquare denial of the allegations, making his position clear.
Their foursquare opposition to the proposal influenced the final decision.

Synset Examples

a foursquare refusal to yield

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • Antonym: wavering - showing indecision or lack of firmness

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'fouresquare' (four + square), meaning literal four-sidedness; later extended metaphorically to steadfastness.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
