fordable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdəbəl / (BrE)
(of a river or stream) shallow enough to be crossed by walking, riding, or driving through it.
shallow enough to be crossed (of water)

Examples

After the rainfall, the river was no longer fordable and travelers had to wait for the water to subside.
We looked for a fordable section before attempting to cross the stream with our horses.
The guide pointed out a fordable spot where vehicles could safely drive through the creek.

Synset Examples

the stream was fordable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impassable - impossible to cross or travel through
  • Synonym: shallow - of little depth (for water)

Etymology

From Old English *ford* (a shallow place in a river) + suffix *-able*.
