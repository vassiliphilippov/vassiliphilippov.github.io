Bk

1. NOUN

/ biːˈkeɪ / (AmE), / biːˈkeɪ / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for berkelium, a radioactive transuranic element
symbol for the element berkelium

Examples

The chemist successfully isolated a sample containing Bk for further study.
Bk has no significant commercial applications due to its rarity and radioactivity.
Element 97, known as Bk, was first synthesized in the 1940s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berkelium - a radioactive transuranic element with symbol Bk and atomic number 97

Etymology

From Berkeley, California, where the element was first synthesized, combined with the standard element symbol '-ium'.
