Soubise

1. NOUN

/ suːˈbiːz / (AmE), / suːˈbiːz / (BrE)
a velouté sauce made with sautéed chopped onions and cream, used especially in French cuisine
onion and cream-based sauce

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate Soubise to accompany the roasted chicken.
A classic Soubise adds rich, sweet onion flavor to many dishes.
We topped the grilled vegetables with a velvet-smooth Soubise for extra depth.

Etymology

French, named after Charles de Rohan, Prince of Soubise, for whom the sauce was created.
