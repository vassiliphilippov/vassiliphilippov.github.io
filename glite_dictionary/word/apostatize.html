to apostatize

1. VERB

/ əˈpɑːstəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒstətaɪz / (BrE)
to abandon one's religious faith or strongly held beliefs or principles
abandon one's beliefs or faith

Examples

After much inner turmoil, he finally decided to apostatize from the faith he was raised in.
Some people apostatize when faced with persecution, while others hold to their convictions.
Throughout history, many have been ostracized for daring to apostatize from mainstream beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - to formally abandon a belief or claim
  • Antonym: convert - to change from one belief or religion to another (opposite direction)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin apostatizāre, from Greek apostatizein (to defect).
