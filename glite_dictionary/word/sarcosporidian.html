sarcosporidian

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑr.kə.spəˈrɪd.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌsɑː.kə.spəˈrɪd.i.ən / (BrE)
a protozoan parasite that infects the muscles of vertebrates, often forming visible cysts within muscle tissue
parasite of vertebrate muscles
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified a sarcosporidian infection in the muscle tissue of the sheep.
Researchers study sarcosporidian parasites to understand how they affect different species of livestock.
Wild boars can serve as hosts for several types of sarcosporidian species.

Etymology

From New Latin Sarcosporidia, from Greek sarx (flesh) + spora (seed, spore).
