terrorisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately causing fear, especially through threats or violence
the act of causing fear

Examples

The terrorisation of the population forced many families to flee the area.
Police condemned the terrorisation of witnesses as a serious crime.
He spoke out against the systematic terrorisation used by the regime to suppress dissent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimidation - the act of frightening someone to make them do what one wants

Etymology

From 'terrorise', patterned after French 'terrorisation' and related to Latin 'terror' (fear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of using violence or threats to create fear, especially for political purposes; an act of terrorism
the act of terrorism

Examples

The country experienced a wave of terrorisation that left citizens afraid to leave their homes.
International organizations condemned the terrorisation of innocent people during the conflict.
Laws were introduced to combat the terrorisation of communities by extremist groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorism - the unlawful use of violence and intimidation, especially against civilians, in the pursuit of political aims

Etymology

From 'terrorise' + '-ation'; patterned after French 'terrorisation'.
