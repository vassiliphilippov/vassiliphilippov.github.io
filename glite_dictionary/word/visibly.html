visibly

1. ADV

/ ˈvɪzəbli / (AmE), / ˈvɪzəbli / (BrE)
In a way that can be seen or perceived; so as to be visible.
in a way that can be seen

Examples

She was visibly nervous before giving her speech.
The stain had visibly faded after several washings.
He was visibly moved by the heartfelt message.

Synset Examples

he was visibly upset

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visibly - in a visible manner
  • Antonym: invisibly - in a way that cannot be seen

Etymology

From 'visible' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'visibilis' (that can be seen).

2. ADV

/ ˈvɪzəbli / (AmE), / ˈvɪzəbli / (BrE)
in a way that can be seen or noticed; so as to be visible to the eye or obvious to perception.
in a way that can be seen

Examples

She was visibly upset when she received the bad news.
His hands were visibly shaking during the interview.
The results were visibly improved after the new treatment.

Synset Examples

the sign was visibly displayed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invisibly - in a way that cannot be seen or is hidden from sight

Etymology

From visible + -ly, modeled after Latin visibilis ('that can be seen') and English '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00195362-r
Synset POS: r
