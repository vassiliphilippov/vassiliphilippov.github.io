fivefold

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪvˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfaɪvfəʊld / (BrE)
having five parts, elements, or times as much of something
having five parts or five times as much

Examples

Profits increased fivefold compared to last year.
The new system is fivefold more efficient than the previous one.
Membership saw a fivefold rise over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: threefold - having three parts or three times as much
  • Degree Higher: tenfold - having ten parts or ten times as much

Etymology

From 'five' + 'fold', similar to other multiplicative adjectives such as 'threefold', 'tenfold'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪv.foʊld / (AmE), / ˈfaɪv.fəʊld / (BrE)
Consisting of five parts, units, or components; having five times as much or many.
having five parts or times

Examples

Our sales increased fivefold from last year, setting a new company record.
The new device offers fivefold protection against electrical surges.
They aim to achieve a fivefold reduction in water usage by 2030.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - twice as much or as many
  • Degree Lower: threefold - having three parts or occurring three times
  • Degree Higher: tenfold - having ten parts or times

Etymology

From Old English fif (five) + -fold (times; multiplied by).
