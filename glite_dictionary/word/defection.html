defection

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving a country, political party, organization, or cause to which one previously belonged, especially to join an opposing one
the act of abandoning allegiance

Examples

The sudden defection of several team members shocked the organization.
Many politicians feared defection from their party before the crucial vote.
During the Cold War, stories of defection to the West were often in the news.

Synset Examples

his abandonment of his wife and children left them penniless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desertion - the act of abandoning a person, cause, or organization
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin defectio (failure, revolt), from deficere (to fail, desert).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of abandoning one's allegiance or loyalty to a group, cause, or religion, often in favor of an opposing one.
act of abandoning loyalty for an opposing cause

Examples

His defection from the party shocked his former allies.
The regime was weakened by the defection of key military leaders.
Reports of mass defection spread panic among the supporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desertion - the act of leaving someone or something without intending to return

Etymology

From Latin defectio (desertion), from deficere (to desert, fail).
