loculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːkjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈlɒkjʊləs / (BrE)
A small compartment or cavity within a plant or animal organ.
small cavity in an organ or plant

Examples

The plant's fruit contains a single loculus filled with seeds.
On the cross-section of the ovary, each loculus is clearly visible.
In some fossils, the loculus structure provides clues about the organism's biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamber - a large room or enclosed space within a structure or organ
  • Synonym: cell - a small compartment or cavity, especially in biology

Etymology

From Latin 'loculus' (small place, compartment), diminutive of 'locus' (place).
