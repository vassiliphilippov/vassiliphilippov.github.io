fungible

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌndʒɪbəl / (BrE)
An item, especially a commodity, that is interchangeable with others of the same kind for practical or legal purposes.
an interchangeable item or commodity
legal

Examples

In banking, gold is considered a fungible because one unit can be substituted for another of equal value.
Legal contracts often specify whether goods are fungibles or non-fungibles to clarify exchange terms.
Oil is traded as a fungible on international markets due to its uniform quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeable - able to be exchanged with each other without difference

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'fungibilis', from Latin 'fungi' (to perform).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌndʒɪbəl / (BrE)
(of goods or commodities) able to be replaced by another identical item; mutually interchangeable, especially to satisfy an obligation.
mutually interchangeable (goods or commodities)
legal

Examples

In commercial transactions, gold is considered a fungible commodity because each unit is essentially identical.
The court ruled that the loan was to be repaid with fungible items of the same quality and quantity.
Cryptocurrencies are typically fungible, meaning one token is exchangeable for another of equal value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeable - able to be exchanged with another of its kind

Etymology

From Medieval Latin fungibilis (that may be performed), from Latin fungi (to perform).

Synset POS: s
