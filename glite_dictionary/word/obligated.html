obligated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑblɪˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒblɪˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
required by law or conscience to do something; having a duty or responsibility to act in a certain way
required or bound to do something

Examples

She felt obligated to attend the meeting even though she was tired.
I am obligated by law to pay my taxes every year.
He doesn't want others to feel obligated to return his favors.

Synset Examples

felt obligated to repay the kindness
was obligated to pay off the student loan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliged - required or morally bound to do something
  • Antonym: free - not constrained or required to do something

Etymology

From Latin obligatus, past participle of obligare, meaning ‘to bind’ or ‘to oblige’.
