Plectranthus amboinicus

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈtrænθəs ˌæm.bɔɪˈnɪ.kəs / (AmE), / plɛkˈtranθəs ˌam.bɔɪˈnɪ.kəs / (BrE)
an aromatic perennial herb native to southern and eastern Africa and widely cultivated elsewhere, used in cooking and traditional medicine
an aromatic culinary and medicinal herb

Examples

Many households grow Plectranthus amboinicus for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional remedies.
The chef added fresh Plectranthus amboinicus to the soup for a unique flavor.
Plectranthus amboinicus thrives in warm climates and is often grown on windowsills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian borage - common English name for Plectranthus amboinicus, a culinary and medicinal herb
  • Synonym: oregano - a similar aromatic herb often used in cooking

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'plektron' (spur) and 'anthos' (flower); species name from Amboina (Ambon Island, Indonesia), where first described.
