Sula bassana

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːlə bəˈsɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌsuːlə bæˈsɑːnə / (BrE)
a very large white seabird, the northern gannet, with black wing tips, found in the North Atlantic
large white seabird (northern gannet)

Examples

Large colonies of Sula bassana can be found nesting on the cliffs of the North Atlantic.
The distinctive black wing tips make Sula bassana easy to identify at sea.
Researchers observed the migration patterns of Sula bassana over the course of the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern gannet - large white seabird with black wingtips

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Sula' (from Norwegian for gannet) and 'bassana' (from the Bass Rock in Scotland, a famous nesting site).
