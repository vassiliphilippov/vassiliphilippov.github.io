chained

1. ADJ

/ tʃeɪnd / (AmE), / tʃeɪnd / (BrE)
bound or secured with chains, often to prevent movement or escape.
bound with chains

Examples

The prisoners were chained together to prevent their escape.
A chained dog barked every time someone walked past the gate.
He stared at the chained bicycle, wondering who had left it there overnight.

Synset Examples

enchained demons strained in anger to gnaw on his bones
prisoners in chains

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unchained - not bound or secured with chains; free
  • Synonym: shackled - restrained with shackles or similar restraints

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'chain', from Old French 'chaine', from Latin 'catena' (chain).
