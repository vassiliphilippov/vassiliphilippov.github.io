somewhere

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈsʌmwɛə / (BrE)
an indefinite or unspecified place; an unknown or unnamed location
an unknown or indefinite place (noun)

Examples

They decided to move to somewhere quiet after retirement.
Her mind wandered off to somewhere far away during the meeting.
He grew up in somewhere along the coast, but never said exactly where.

Synset Examples

they moved to somewhere in Spain

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anywhere - any place; used instead of somewhere in negative statements and questions
  • Antonym: nowhere - no place; not in or at any place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08506775-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˈsʌmˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈsʌmwɛə / (BrE)
in, at, or to an unspecified or unknown place or location.
in an unspecified or unknown place

Examples

She looked for her keys somewhere in the living room.
There's somewhere I'd like to take you this weekend.
He must be hiding somewhere nearby.

Synset Examples

she must be somewhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anywhere - in, at, or to any place at all
  • Antonym: nowhere - not in or at any place
  • Antonym: everywhere - in or to all places
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00025968-r
Synset POS: r
