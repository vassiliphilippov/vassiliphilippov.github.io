Hero of Alexandria

1. PROPN

/ ˈhiːroʊ əv ˌæləɡˈzændriə / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ əv ˌæləɡˈzændriə / (BrE)
An ancient Greek mathematician and inventor renowned for his work in geometry and for describing various mechanical devices in the first century AD.
ancient Greek mathematician and inventor

Examples

Hero of Alexandria invented a primitive steam engine known as the aeolipile.
Many geometry students learn about Hero of Alexandria and his famous formula for the area of a triangle.
The mechanical devices described by Hero of Alexandria influenced engineers for centuries.

Etymology

From the Greek name Hērōn ho Alexandreus, meaning 'Hero the Alexandrian.'
