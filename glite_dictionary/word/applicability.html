applicability

1. NOUN

/ əˌplɪkəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / əˌplɪkəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being relevant or suitable for a particular purpose, situation, or subject
suitability or relevance for a purpose

Examples

The applicability of this law to international cases has been questioned by legal experts.
She doubted the applicability of the research results to real-world situations.
We must consider the applicability of these rules in different contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relevance - the quality of being closely connected or appropriate
  • Antonym: inapplicability - the quality of not being relevant or applicable

Etymology

From 'applicable' + '-ity', influenced by Late Latin 'applicabilitas'.
