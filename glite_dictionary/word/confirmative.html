confirmative

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɜːrmətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmətɪv / (BrE)
serving to support or corroborate; providing confirmation or evidence for something
serving to support or corroborate

Examples

The scientist presented confirmative data to support her hypothesis.
No confirmative evidence was found to prove the suspect’s alibi.
His findings were confirmative of the previous research in the field.

Synset Examples

collateral evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroborative - serving to strengthen or support with evidence

Etymology

From Latin 'confirmativus' (confirming), from 'confirmare' (to strengthen, establish).
