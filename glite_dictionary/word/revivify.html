to revivify

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɪvəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪvɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to give new life, fresh energy, or renewed vigor to someone or something
give new life or energy

Examples

The infusion of new talent helped to revivify the struggling theater company.
A weekend retreat might revivify your enthusiasm for the project.
The rain seemed to revivify the dry and withered plants in the garden.

Synset Examples

A hot soup will revive me
This will renovate my spirits
This treatment repaired my health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - restore to life or consciousness
  • Synonym: to rejuvenate - make young or fresh again

Etymology

From French 'revivifier', from Latin 'revivificare' (to make alive again).
