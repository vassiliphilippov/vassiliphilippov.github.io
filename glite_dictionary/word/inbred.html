inbred

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbrɛd / (BrE)
produced by inbreeding; resulting from the mating of closely related individuals over generations.
produced by inbreeding

Examples

The researchers studied the effects of an inbred population on genetic diversity.
Certain purebred animals are often highly inbred to maintain specific traits.
The inbred mice showed increased susceptibility to disease compared to the control group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outbred - not produced by inbreeding; from unrelated individuals

Etymology

From in- (into) + bred, past participle of breed.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbrɛd / (BrE)
(of a quality) existing in one from birth; innate; natural and not acquired.
existing from birth; innate

Examples

His inbred decency showed even in the most difficult situations.
She had an inbred sense of justice that guided her choices.
The puppy seemed to have an inbred instinct for hunting.

Synset Examples

mankind's connatural sense of the good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innate - existing naturally from birth
  • Antonym: acquired - obtained later, not present from birth

Etymology

From in- + bred, past participle of 'breed', originally meaning 'innate, inborn'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01037835-s
Synset POS: s
