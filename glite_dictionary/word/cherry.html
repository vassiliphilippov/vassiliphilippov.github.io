cherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A small, round, typically red or black fruit with a single hard stone at its center, produced by the cherry tree.
small red fruit with a hard stone

Examples

She picked a ripe cherry from the tree and ate it immediately.
The cake was decorated with a bright red cherry on top.
Fresh cherries are in season during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a fruit similar to a cherry but larger and oval-shaped

Etymology

From Old Northern French *cherise*, from Latin *cerasum* (cherry).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a tree or shrub that produces small, round, edible fruit with a single hard stone and often yields valuable hardwood
tree or shrub bearing round fruit (cherry)

Examples

The cherry in their backyard produces beautiful blossoms each spring.
Many types of cherry are cultivated for both their fruit and timber.
Birds love to eat the ripe cherry from the tree during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a tree producing oval, edible fruit with a stone
  • Synonym: apple - a tree bearing round, red or green fruit with white flesh

Etymology

From Old Northern French *cherise*, from Latin *cerasum* (cherry tree), originally from Ancient Greek *kerasos*.
