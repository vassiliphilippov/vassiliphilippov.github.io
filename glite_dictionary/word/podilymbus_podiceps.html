Podilymbus podiceps

1. PROPN

/ ˌpoʊdɪˈlɪmbəs poʊˈdɪsɛps / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɪˈlɪmbəs pəʊˈdɪsɛps / (BrE)
the scientific name for the pied-billed grebe, a small North American waterbird with a distinctive black-banded whitish bill
the pied-billed grebe (waterbird species)

Examples

The Podilymbus podiceps can be recognized by its unique black-banded bill during breeding season.
Birdwatchers flock to wetlands to catch a glimpse of Podilymbus podiceps swimming among the reeds.
In the guidebook, Podilymbus podiceps is listed as a common resident of North American marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pied-billed grebe - common name for Podilymbus podiceps, a small brown waterbird

Etymology

From Latin: 'podilymbus' referring to a type of diver (water bird), and 'podiceps' meaning 'rump-footed', indicating the bird’s legs are set far back on its body.
