aquilege

1. NOUN

/ ˈækwɪˌlɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈakwɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Aquilegia, known for its irregular, showy spurred flowers, found in temperate regions, especially mountains.
plant of the genus Aquilegia

Examples

Many gardeners plant aquilege to add a splash of color to their spring borders.
The delicate spurred flowers of the aquilege attract hummingbirds and bees alike.
On our hike through the mountains, we found a rare species of aquilege in bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - another common name for plants of the genus Aquilegia

Etymology

From Latin *aquilegia*, the genus name, possibly from 'aquila' meaning 'eagle', referring to the shape of the flower's spurs.
