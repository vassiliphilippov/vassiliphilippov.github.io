Muscicapidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsɪkəˈpɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmʌsɪkəˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small passerine birds, commonly known as the Old World or true flycatchers, found mainly in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
family of Old World flycatchers

Examples

Many species in the Muscicapidae family are renowned for their agile insect-hunting techniques.
A robin is not a member of the Muscicapidae family, despite its flycatcher-like habits.
The forests of Southeast Asia are home to a diverse range of Muscicapidae species.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Muscicapa' (type genus) + '-idae' (family suffix).
