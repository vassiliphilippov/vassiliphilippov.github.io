brewage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbruːɪdʒ / (BrE)
a drink produced by steeping, boiling, and fermenting ingredients, rather than by distillation
fermented drink brewed, not distilled

Examples

The monks perfected their brewage over centuries, creating a unique flavor that draws visitors from all over.
Traditional ales are a classic example of a brewage made through natural fermentation.
He sampled a homemade brewage crafted from local grains and wild yeast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brew - to make a beverage by steeping, boiling, and fermenting

Etymology

From Middle English *brewe* (brew), from Old English *breowan* (to brew) + -age (denoting action or result).
