Sinapis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈneɪpɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈneɪpɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of Old World herbs in the mustard family, often included in the genus Brassica.
a genus of Old World mustard herbs

Examples

The plants classified in the genus Sinapis are commonly known as wild mustards.
Botanists often debate whether Sinapis should be a separate genus or part of Brassica.
Some species of Sinapis are cultivated for their seeds and leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassica - a genus of plants in the mustard family including cabbages and mustards

Etymology

From Latin 'sinapis', from Greek 'sinapi' meaning 'mustard'.
