genomics

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɑːmɪks / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnəʊmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of genetics that studies the structure, function, evolution, and mapping of genomes, which are the complete set of DNA in an organism
the study of genomes in organisms
medical

Examples

Recent advances in genomics have revolutionized our understanding of genetic diseases.
The field of genomics enables scientists to analyze the DNA of entire populations.
Many universities now offer specialized courses in genomics and bioinformatics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetics - the science of heredity and variation in organisms

Etymology

From genome + -ics, modeled after genetics.
