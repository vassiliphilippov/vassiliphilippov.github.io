Illyria

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪriə / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
an ancient region on the eastern coast of the Adriatic Sea, inhabited by the Illyrians, an Indo-European people
ancient region on Adriatic coast

Examples

The legends of Illyria have inspired poets for centuries.
Archaeologists uncovered relics from ancient Illyria during their expedition.
In Shakespeare's 'Twelfth Night,' the action unfolds in the imaginary land of Illyria.

Etymology

From Latin Illyria, from Ancient Greek Ἰλλυρία (Illyría).
