cousin-german

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌzən dʒɚˈmæn / (AmE), / ˈkʌz(ə)n dʒəˈmɑːn / (BrE)
a first cousin; the child of one's aunt or uncle
first cousin (old-fashioned)
archaic

Examples

Her cousin-german visited from France for the family reunion.
In old English literature, the term cousin-german often appears for first cousins.
He inherited the estate jointly with his cousin-german after their uncle's passing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first cousin - the child of your aunt or uncle

Etymology

From Latin germanus (full, own, genuine), referring to a close relative.
