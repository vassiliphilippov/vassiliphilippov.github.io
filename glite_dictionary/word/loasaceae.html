Loasaceae

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈæsəˌsiː / (AmE), / ləʊˈæsɪˌsiː / (BrE)
a family of bristly, often hairy plants, sometimes climbing, native to America, Africa, and southern Arabia
family of bristly, often hairy plants

Examples

The Loasaceae family includes many species with stinging hairs.
Several members of the Loasaceae are native to arid regions of South America.
Botanists recently discovered a new genus within the Loasaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Loasa + Latin -aceae, used in botanical family names.

2. NOUN

/ loʊˌæsəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / ləʊˌæsəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a family of bristly, hairy, sometimes climbing plants found in America, Africa, and southern Arabia
family of bristly plants (botany)

Examples

The Loasaceae are known for their distinctive bristly hairs that often irritate the skin.
Several species within the Loasaceae family are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
Botanists discovered a new member of the Loasaceae in the mountains of South America.

Etymology

From the genus *Loasa*, type genus of the family, with the Latin suffix -aceae used to indicate plant families.
