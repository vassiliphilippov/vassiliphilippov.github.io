anti-American

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən / (BrE)
a person who is opposed to the United States or its policies
a person opposed to the US or its policies

Examples

Some anti-Americans criticize US foreign policy in international forums.
The debate included both anti-Americans and strong supporters of the US.
He was labeled an anti-American because of his outspoken views.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro-American - a person who supports the United States or its policies

Etymology

Formed from anti- (against) + American

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən / (AmE), / ˌænti.əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən / (BrE)
opposed to the United States or its policies, culture, or people
opposed to the United States

Examples

Some politicians in Europe have expressed anti-American views in recent years.
The film was criticized for its perceived anti-American message.
He denied that his speech was anti-American, saying he merely disagreed with U.S. foreign policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro-American - supporting the United States or its policies

Etymology

anti- (against) + American (of or relating to the United States)
