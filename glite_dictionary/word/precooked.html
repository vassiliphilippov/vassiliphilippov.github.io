precooked

1. ADJ

/ priˈkʊkt / (AmE), / priˈkʊkt / (BrE)
(of food) cooked partially or completely in advance, before being packaged or sold.
cooked in advance (of food)

Examples

These precooked sausages only need to be heated before serving.
Most airlines serve precooked meals during flights.
You can buy precooked rice for convenience.

Synset Examples

frozen precooked meals from the supermarket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw - not cooked

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'cooked' (past participle of 'cook').
