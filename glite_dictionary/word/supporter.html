supporter

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɔɹɾɚ / (AmE), / səˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a band, usually elastic, worn around the leg to hold up a stocking, or around the arm to hold up a sleeve
elastic band to hold up stocking or sleeve

Examples

She adjusted her supporter to keep her stockings in place throughout the performance.
The vintage dress came with a matching supporter for the sleeves.
In the early 20th century, men commonly used an arm supporter to keep their shirt sleeves from slipping down.

3. NOUN

/ səˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / səˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a person who supports, champions, or backs a person, cause, or team
a person who supports a person, cause, or team

Examples

The politician thanked every supporter who volunteered for her campaign.
Football matches are always exciting when supporters cheer for their teams in the stands.
As a passionate supporter of animal rights, he donates regularly to various charities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opponent - someone who opposes or competes against another
  • Synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a cause or policy

Etymology

From Old French supporter, from Latin supportare (to carry up, bring, support).
