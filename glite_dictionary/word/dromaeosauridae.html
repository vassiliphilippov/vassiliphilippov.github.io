Dromaeosauridae

1. NOUN

/ droʊˌmiːoʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (AmE), / drəʊˌmɪəʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of swift-running, carnivorous, bipedal dinosaurs that lived during the Cretaceous period, including species like Velociraptor.
family of bipedal predatory dinosaurs

Examples

Scientists believe that Dromaeosauridae were among the most agile predators of their time.
Fossils of Dromaeosauridae have been discovered in both Asia and North America.
The famous Velociraptor belongs to the Dromaeosauridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Velociraptor - a genus within Dromaeosauridae known for its speed and agility

Etymology

From Greek dromaios (running) + sauros (lizard) + Latin -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ droʊˌmiː.oʊˈsɔːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / drəˌmiː.əʊˈsɔːrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of swift-running, bipedal, carnivorous dinosaurs, known for their sickle-shaped claws and often referred to as 'raptors.'
family of swift-running bipedal dinosaurs
medical

Examples

The Dromaeosauridae family includes the famous Velociraptor and Deinonychus.
Fossil evidence shows that members of the Dromaeosauridae were agile and possibly feathered predators.
Paleontologists have discovered new Dromaeosauridae species in Asia and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velociraptor - a well-known genus in the Dromaeosauridae family
  • Degree Higher: dinosaur - a group of reptiles including the Dromaeosauridae family

Etymology

From Greek dromeus (runner) + sauros (lizard), with the family suffix -idae.
