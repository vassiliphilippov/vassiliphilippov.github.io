Helleborus orientalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛləˈbɔrəs ˌɔːriənˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrəs ˌɒriɛnˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A perennial garden plant with deep green leathery leaves and purplish-green flowers, commonly called the Lenten rose.
variety of perennial garden plant; Lenten rose

Examples

Helleborus orientalis is prized for its early spring flowers in many shade gardens.
Gardeners recommend planting Helleborus orientalis under deciduous trees for best results.
The subtle beauty of Helleborus orientalis makes it an excellent choice for woodland borders.

Etymology

Latin binomial: genus Helleborus and species orientalis, meaning 'eastern.'
