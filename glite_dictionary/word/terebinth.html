terebinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.ə.bɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪ.bɪnθ / (BrE)
a small Mediterranean tree (Pistacia terebinthus) of the cashew family, which yields Chian turpentine.
Mediterranean tree yielding turpentine

Examples

The ancient Greeks valued the terebinth for its aromatic resin used in medicine and incense.
A solitary terebinth stood at the edge of the olive grove, shading the shepherds from the sun.
Researchers collected samples of terebinth leaves to study their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin terebinthus, from Greek terebinthos (τερέβινθος), meaning turpentine tree.
