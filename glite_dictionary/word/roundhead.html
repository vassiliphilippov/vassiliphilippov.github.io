Roundhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈraʊndˌhɛd / (BrE)
a supporter of Parliament and Oliver Cromwell during the English Civil War in the 17th century, opposed to the Royalists (Cavaliers)
supporter of Parliament in the English Civil War

Examples

Many Roundhead soldiers fought bravely against the king's forces during the English Civil War.
The parliamentarian cause, championed by the Roundheads, eventually led to the trial and execution of Charles I.
Some considered themselves loyal Roundheads, believing firmly in the authority of Parliament over the monarchy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Cavalier - supporter of King Charles I during the English Civil War

Etymology

From the use of short-cropped hair by these supporters, in contrast to the long ringlets of the Cavaliers.
