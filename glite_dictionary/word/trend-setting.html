trend-setting

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛndˌsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛndˌsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
initiating or popularizing a new fashion, idea, or activity that others subsequently follow
initiating or popularizing a trend

Examples

The designer is known for his trend-setting collections that attract global attention.
Her trend-setting hairstyle was soon copied by celebrities around the world.
This trend-setting technology will shape the industry for years to come.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fashionable - following the current style or trend
  • Antonym: conventional - following accepted customs or practices

Etymology

Formed from 'trend' (current style) + present participle of 'set', meaning to establish.
