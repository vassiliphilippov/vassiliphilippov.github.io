Xenicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈzɛnɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small insectivorous birds native to New Zealand, constituting the type genus of the family Xenicidae.
genus of New Zealand wrens

Examples

The rare Xenicus species are found only in remote New Zealand forests.
Scientists study Xenicus to understand the unique evolution of New Zealand's birdlife.
Loss of habitat threatens some members of the Xenicus genus.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek xenos (strange, foreign) + Latin -icus (suffix forming nouns).
