to advertize

1. VERB

/ ˈædvərˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈadvətaɪz / (BrE)
to make a product or service known to the public, especially in order to sell it; to promote through publicity
promote or publicize a product or service

Examples

The company plans to advertize its latest smartphones on television and social media.
They hired a marketing agency to advertize the new restaurant downtown.
If you want to attract more customers, you should advertize your services online.

Synset Examples

The salesman is aggressively pushing the new computer model
The company is heavily advertizing their new laptops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advertise - to promote goods or services publicly

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'advertise', from Middle English 'advertisen', from Old French 'advertir' (to turn toward).

2. VERB

/ ˈædvərˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈædvətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something known or draw attention to it, especially publicly or generally
announce or draw attention to

Examples

He didn't want to advertize his personal problems to everyone at work.
The organization chose not to advertize the results of the poll.
Please don't advertize that she will be leaving the company soon.

Synset Examples

Please don't advertise the fact that he has AIDS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advertise - make something known; alternative spelling
  • Antonym: to conceal - deliberately keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'advertere' meaning 'to turn toward'. Spelling variation of 'advertise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00977934-v
Synset POS: v
