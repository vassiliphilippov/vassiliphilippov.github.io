to larn

1. VERB

/ lɑrn / (AmE), / lɑːn / (BrE)
(dialect) To learn; to gain knowledge or skill through study or experience.
acquire knowledge or skill (dialect)
archaic british

Examples

He never did larn to swim, despite living by the river all his life.
You’ll larn a lot here if you pay attention to the old folks.
She reckoned she could larn him a thing or two about farming.

Synset Examples

She learned dancing from her sister
I learned Sanskrit
Children acquire language at an amazing rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to learn - to gain knowledge or skills through study or experience

Etymology

Dialect variant of "learn," used especially in some British and Irish regional Englishes.
