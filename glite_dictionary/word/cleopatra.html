Cleopatra

1. PROPN

/ kliˈɑprətə / (AmE), / kliˈɒprətə / (BrE)
The last active queen of Egypt, renowned for her beauty, charisma, and relationships with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony, who died by suicide to avoid capture by Octavian.
Queen of Egypt (69–30 BC), lover of Caesar and Mark Antony

Examples

Many movies have portrayed Cleopatra as a cunning and influential historical figure.
Historians continue to debate the real motivations behind Cleopatra's alliances with Roman leaders.
The dramatic end of Cleopatra's reign marked the fall of the Ptolemaic dynasty in Egypt.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Kleopátrā, meaning 'glory of the father'.
