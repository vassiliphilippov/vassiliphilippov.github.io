Otis

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˈəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large terrestrial birds (bustards) in the family Otididae, including the great bustard, found in Europe and adjacent regions.
genus of European bustards (birds)

Examples

The Otis genus includes the great bustard, one of the heaviest flying birds.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Otis species across Europe and Asia.
Habitat loss has threatened several members of the Otis genus in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large ground-dwelling bird of the family Otididae

Etymology

From Latin 'Otis', a name used by Pliny for a type of bustard.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.tɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Otididae, comprising the European bustards.
type genus of the bustard family

Examples

The Otis genus includes the well-known great bustard of Europe.
Zoologists studied the mating habits of Otis in their natural habitat.
Conservation efforts have focused on the endangered species within the Otis genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Otididae - the bustard family of birds

Etymology

From Latin Otis, the name for a kind of bustard.
