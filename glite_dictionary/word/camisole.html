camisole

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmɪˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæmɪsəʊl / (BrE)
A woman's short, sleeveless undergarment, typically with thin straps, worn under a blouse or dress.
short sleeveless women's undergarment

Examples

She wore a sheer camisole under her blouse for extra comfort.
The lace-trimmed camisole peeked out from beneath her sweater.
You can choose a camisole in various colors to match your outfit.

Etymology

From French 'camisole', from Old Occitan 'camisola', diminutive of 'camisa' (shirt).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmɪˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkæmɪsəʊl / (BrE)
A woman's sleeveless undergarment for the upper body, often lacy or decorative, sometimes worn as a short negligee.
woman's sleeveless undergarment

Examples

She slipped on a camisole before getting ready for bed.
The delicate lace camisole peeked out from under her blouse.
She wore a silk camisole as part of her sleepwear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - a woman's undergarment worn beneath a dress or skirt

Etymology

From French 'camisole', originally from Italian 'camiciola', diminutive of 'camicia' (shirt).
