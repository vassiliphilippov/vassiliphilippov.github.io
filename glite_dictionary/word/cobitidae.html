Cobitidae

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈbɪt.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / kəʊˈbɪt.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, slender freshwater fishes commonly known as loaches, found mainly in Eurasia.
family of freshwater fishes (loaches)

Examples

Many species within the Cobitidae family are popular aquarium fish due to their peaceful nature.
Researchers studied the behavior of several Cobitidae in different freshwater habitats across Asia.
The stream was home to a diverse group of Cobitidae, including some rare loaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loach - common name for fishes in the Cobitidae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cobitis' (a type genus of loach) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoology).
