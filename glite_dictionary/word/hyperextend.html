to hyperextend

1. VERB

/ ˌhaɪ.pɚ.ɪkˈstɛnd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pər.ɪkˈstɛnd / (BrE)
to extend a limb or joint beyond its normal range of motion, often causing injury
extend a joint excessively
medical

Examples

Be careful not to hyperextend your knee during exercise.
She hyperextended her wrist while playing tennis last weekend.
If you hyperextend your back, it may lead to serious muscle strain.

Synset Examples

Don't hyperextend your elbow

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to extend - to make something longer or straighter

Etymology

From 'hyper-' (over, beyond) + 'extend', based on Greek and Latin roots.
