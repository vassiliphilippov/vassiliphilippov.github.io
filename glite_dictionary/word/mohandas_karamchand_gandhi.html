Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈhɑːndɑːs ˌkəræmˈtʃænd ˈɡɑːndi / (AmE), / məʊˈhʌndəs kəˌræmˈtʃʌnd ˈɡɑːndi / (BrE)
(1869–1948) Indian political and spiritual leader who advocated for India's independence from British rule through nonviolent resistance.
Indian leader and advocate of nonviolent resistance

Examples

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi led India to independence using methods of nonviolent civil disobedience.
Many people around the world are inspired by the philosophy of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi.
The legacy of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi continues to influence peace movements globally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mahatma Gandhi - Honorific for Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

Etymology

From his birth name in Hindi: Mohandas (given name) Karamchand (father's name) Gandhi (family name).
