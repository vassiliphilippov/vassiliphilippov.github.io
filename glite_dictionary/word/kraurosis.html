kraurosis

1. NOUN

/ kraʊˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / kraʊˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the skin or a mucous membrane undergoes atrophy and shriveling.
atrophy and shriveling of skin or mucous membrane
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with kraurosis of the vulva after showing symptoms of dryness and shrinking.
Early detection of kraurosis can prevent further complications affecting the mucous membranes.
Doctors are still unsure about the exact causes of kraurosis in some patients.

Etymology

From Greek krauros (dry, shriveled) + -osis (disease condition).
