Fasciola hepatica

1. NOUN

/ fəˌsiːoʊlə həˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / fəˌsɪəʊlə hɛˈpætɪkə / (BrE)
a species of parasitic flatworm that infects the liver and bile ducts of domestic animals and humans, causing liver fluke disease
parasitic flatworm species infecting liver
medical

Examples

Fasciola hepatica is a major cause of liver disease in cattle worldwide.
Veterinarians test livestock for Fasciola hepatica infections during routine examinations.
In some regions, human infection with Fasciola hepatica occurs through contaminated water plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver fluke - common name for parasitic flatworms affecting the liver, including Fasciola hepatica
  • Degree Higher: Schistosoma - genus of parasitic flatworms affecting humans, causing schistosomiasis

Etymology

From Latin: 'fasciola' (little band or ribbon) and 'hepatica' (of the liver).
