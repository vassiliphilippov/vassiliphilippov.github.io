Brutus

1. PROPN

/ ˈbruːtəs / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəs / (BrE)
A Roman statesman (85–42 BC) who, together with Cassius, led the conspiracy to assassinate Julius Caesar.
Roman statesman and Caesar's assassin

Examples

Many historians study Brutus for his role in the downfall of Julius Caesar.
The name Brutus is often invoked as a symbol of betrayal in Western literature.
Shakespeare's play highlights the moral dilemma faced by Brutus during the conspiracy.
