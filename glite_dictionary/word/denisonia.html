Denisonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnɪˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌdɛnɪˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of venomous snakes native to Australia, commonly known as De Vis banded snakes or mud adders
genus of Australian venomous snakes

Examples

The Denisonia genus includes snakes adapted to wetlands in Australia.
Researchers studied the venom composition of several Denisonia species.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect habitats critical for Denisonia populations.

Etymology

Named after Denison, an English surname, combined with scientific Latin suffix -ia, used in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnɪˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌdɛnɪˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes known as De Vis banded snakes or mud adders, native to Australia.
a genus of venomous Australian snakes

Examples

The Denisonia genus includes several species of venomous snakes found in Australia.
Scientists recently studied the habitat preferences of Denisonia species.
Venom from snakes in the Denisonia genus can be dangerous to humans.

Etymology

Named after Sir William Thomas Denison, 19th-century Governor of New South Wales, Australia.
