nonliteral

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈlɪtərəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈlɪtərəl / (BrE)
(of a meaning or interpretation) not literal; involving figures of speech or metaphorical use.
not literal; figurative

Examples

The poem uses a number of nonliteral expressions to convey its deeper meaning.
Children often struggle to understand nonliteral language like metaphors and idioms.
His interpretation was too nonliteral for some readers, who preferred a more straightforward analysis.

Synset Examples

figurative language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figurative - (of a word or phrase) not using its basic or literal meaning
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) and 'literal' (from Latin litteralis, of or pertaining to letters or literal meaning).
