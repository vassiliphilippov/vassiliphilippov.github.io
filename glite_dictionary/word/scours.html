scours

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊərz / (AmE), / ˈskaʊəz / (BrE)
A disease affecting livestock, especially calves and piglets, characterized by diarrhea.
diarrhea disease in livestock

Examples

The farmer lost several calves to scours last winter despite his best efforts.
Veterinarians recommend immediate treatment if any signs of scours appear in young animals.
Prolonged cases of scours can lead to severe dehydration in piglets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diarrhea - frequent loose or liquid bowel movements
