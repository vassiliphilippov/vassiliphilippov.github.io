shegetz

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪɡɛts / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪɡɛts / (BrE)
An offensive term used, especially among some Jewish communities, to refer to a non-Jewish boy or young man.
offensive term for non-Jewish young man
offensive

Examples

He was called a shegetz by the older members of the community.
Some people find the word shegetz deeply offensive and avoid using it.
Growing up, my grandmother would warn me not to date a shegetz.

Synset Examples

why does she like all those shkotzim?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiksa - offensive term for a non-Jewish woman or girl

Etymology

From Yiddish שֵׁיְגֶעץ (sheygets), from Hebrew שֵׁקֶץ (sheqets, abomination).
