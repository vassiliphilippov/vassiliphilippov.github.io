scorcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːtʃə / (BrE)
a ball that is hit or kicked with great force, especially in sports, moving very fast
a very hard-hit ball (sports)

Examples

He hit a scorcher down the first base line and made it to second.
That shot was a real scorcher and gave the goalkeeper no chance.
The crowd erupted as she sent a scorcher over the boundary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blaster - a ball hit with tremendous force (sports slang)
  • Antonym: softie - a ball hit gently or weakly (sports slang)

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːtʃə / (BrE)
An extremely hot day.
an extremely hot day
informal

Examples

Yesterday was an absolute scorcher, with temperatures soaring to 39 degrees Celsius.
We're expecting another scorcher this weekend, so make sure to stay hydrated.
It was a scorcher at the tennis match, making it hard for the players to keep cool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heatwave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather
  • Antonym: cold snap - a sudden brief spell of cold weather

Etymology

From 'scorch' + '-er', denoting something or someone that scorches.
