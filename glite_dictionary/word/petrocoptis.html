Petrocoptis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtrəˈkɒptɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəˈkɒptɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial tussock-forming rock plants native to the Pyrenees and northern Spain, sometimes classified under the genus Lychnis
genus of rock plants from the Pyrenees

Examples

Several species of Petrocoptis are endemic to steep, rocky slopes in the Pyrenees.
Botanists often debate whether Petrocoptis should be grouped with the genus Lychnis.
The delicate flowers of Petrocoptis bloom in the high mountain crevices each spring.

Etymology

From Greek *petra* (rock) + *kopto* (to cut), referring to rocky habitats.
