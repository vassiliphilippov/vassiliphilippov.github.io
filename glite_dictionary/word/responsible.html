responsible

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɑnsəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsəbl̩ / (BrE)
being the person or thing that is the cause or agent of something, especially something negative or undesirable
being the agent or cause of something

Examples

Police are trying to identify the group responsible for the vandalism.
Smoking was found to be responsible for thousands of preventable deaths each year.
The faulty wiring was responsible for the fire that destroyed the building.

Synset Examples

determined who was the responsible party
termites were responsible for the damage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culpable - deserving blame; at fault

Etymology

From Medieval Latin responsibilis (answerable), from Latin respondēre (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00325776-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rəˈspɑnsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or organization) regarded as trustworthy, reliable, or having a good credit rating, especially in financial contexts.
trustworthy or having a good credit rating

Examples

Lenders are more likely to approve loans for responsible applicants with proven payment histories.
To maintain a good reputation, the company always chooses to work with responsible borrowers.
Your credit card limit may increase if you demonstrate responsible financial behavior.

Synset Examples

a responsible borrower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be trusted or depended on

Etymology

From Latin responsus (answered, responsible), past participle of respondere (to respond).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02474578-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɑnsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsəbəl / (BrE)
having the duty to deal with something or having control over someone or something, and being accountable for the outcome; deserving to be trusted to do what is right or required
having duty or accountability; trustworthy

Examples

As a manager, he is responsible for overseeing all daily operations.
Parents are responsible for the safety and well-being of their children.
It is responsible to recycle your waste and reduce environmental impact.

Synset Examples

a responsible adult
responsible journalism
a responsible position
the captain is responsible for the ship's safety
the cabinet is responsible to the parliament

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irresponsible - not showing a proper sense of responsibility
  • Synonym: accountable - required or expected to justify actions or decisions; responsible
  • Synonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful

Etymology

From Old French 'responsable', from Late Latin 'responsabilis' (answerable), from Latin 'respondere' (to respond).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02003477-a
Synset POS: a
