fruitfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfruːtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfruːtfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of producing healthy growth, results, or benefits; the state of being fruitful
the quality of producing growth or results

Examples

The fruitfulness of the land ensured a good harvest every year.
She admired the fruitfulness of his imagination and creativity.
Rainfall plays a vital role in the fruitfulness of the region's soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertility - the ability to produce offspring or growth
  • Antonym: barrenness - the quality of being unproductive or infertile

Etymology

From Old English 'frēotful' (productive) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrut.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfruːt.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the ability to produce many new and original ideas; intellectual productivity or creativity.
intellectual productivity; creativity

Examples

Her fruitfulness as a writer was evident in the dozens of novels she produced over her career.
The fruitfulness of his imagination led to innovations in both science and art.
Teachers try to foster fruitfulness in students by encouraging critical and creative thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creativity - the ability to create new ideas
  • Synonym: fertility - the quality of producing abundantly
  • Antonym: barrenness - the lack of productivity or results

Etymology

From Middle English 'frutful', from Old French 'fruit' + '-ful' and '-ness'.
