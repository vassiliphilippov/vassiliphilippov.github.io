Mucuna deeringiana

1. NOUN

/ muˈkuːnə diːˌɛrɪŋˈiːænə / (AmE), / mjuːˈkuːnə diːˌɪərɪŋˈiːənə / (BrE)
An annual woody vine native to Asia, characterized by long clusters of purplish flowers and densely hairy pods, cultivated in the southern United States as green manure and for grazing.
woody vine cultivated for green manure and grazing

Examples

Many farmers in the southern U.S. plant Mucuna deeringiana to enrich their soil.
The hairy pods of Mucuna deeringiana can cause skin irritation if touched.
Researchers are studying Mucuna deeringiana for its potential benefits as a cover crop.
