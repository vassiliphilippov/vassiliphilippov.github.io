Aptenodytes

1. NOUN

/ æpˌtɛnoʊˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / æpˌtɛnəʊˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
a genus of large penguins, including the emperor and king penguins
genus of large penguins

Examples

The Aptenodytes genus contains some of the largest penguins, such as the emperor penguin.
Both king and emperor penguins belong to the Aptenodytes genus.
Scientists are studying the unique adaptations of Aptenodytes species to extreme cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - flightless seabird found in the Southern Hemisphere
  • Antonym: Pygoscelis - genus of medium-sized penguins, such as the Adélie and chinstrap penguins

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'ptenos' (winged) + '-dytes' (diver).

2. NOUN

/ æpˌtɛnəˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / æpˌtɛnəˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
a genus of large penguins, including the emperor penguin and king penguin, native to Antarctica and subantarctic regions
genus of large penguins

Examples

The emperor penguin, a member of the genus Aptenodytes, is the tallest and heaviest of all penguin species.
Researchers studied the breeding patterns of Aptenodytes colonies during the Antarctic winter.
Both species in the Aptenodytes genus are known for their remarkable ability to survive extreme cold.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'aptēnos' (without wings) + 'dytēs' (diver).
