subsidizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person, organization, or country that provides financial assistance to support an activity or industry by giving a subsidy
one who provides a subsidy

Examples

The government is a major subsidizer of renewable energy programs in the country.
Without a reliable subsidizer, many public health initiatives would struggle to survive.
Several nonprofit organizations act as subsidizers for small businesses in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or help to a person or cause
  • Antonym: recipient - a person or organization receiving a subsidy

Etymology

Formed from 'subsidize' + the agent suffix '-er'. 'Subsidize' from French 'subsidier', from Latin 'subsidium' (support, aid).
