encysted

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪstɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstɪd / (BrE)
enclosed in or as if in a cyst, especially referring to biological tissues or organisms.
enclosed in a cyst
medical

Examples

The parasite remained encysted in the tissue and avoided detection for months.
Doctors found an encysted mass during the patient's abdominal scan.
Some worms become encysted in muscle tissue as part of their life cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cystic - relating to or resembling a cyst

Etymology

From en- (cause to be) + cyst, influenced by medical Latin.
