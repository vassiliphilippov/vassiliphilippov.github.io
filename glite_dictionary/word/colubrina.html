Colubrina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːbrɪnə / (AmE), / kɒˈluːbrɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly tropical American shrubs or small trees with small yellowish flowers and yellow or red fruits.
genus of tropical American shrubs or small trees

Examples

Several species of Colubrina are used for their medicinal properties in traditional practices.
The bright red fruits of the Colubrina tree are attractive to many bird species.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Colubrina growing in the tropical rainforest.

Etymology

From Latin colubrinus (snakelike), referring to the shape of the twigs.

a genus of tropical American shrubs or small trees

Examples

The Colubrina genus includes several species valued for their medicinal properties.
Botanists found a rare Colubrina tree growing in the coastal forest.
The fruits of Colubrina plants attract many birds in the region.

Etymology

From Latin 'colubrinus' (of a snake), referring to the snake-like stems of some species.
