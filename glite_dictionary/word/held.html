held

1. ADJ

/ hɛld / (AmE), / hɛld / (BrE)
(of a place or thing, especially in combination) occupied or controlled by a particular person or group.
occupied or controlled by (in combinations)

Examples

The enemy-held region was heavily guarded by troops.
Much of the coastline remained rebel-held throughout the conflict.
Journalists could not enter the government-held area without special permits.

Synset Examples

enemy-held territory

Etymology

Past participle of 'hold', from Old English 'healdan' (to grasp, keep).
