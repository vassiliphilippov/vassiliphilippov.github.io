Osco-Umbrian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːskoʊ ˈʌmbriən / (AmE), / ˌɒskəʊ ˈʌmbriən / (BrE)
a group of extinct Italic languages once spoken in ancient Italy, later replaced by Latin
group of extinct Italic languages

Examples

The Osco-Umbrian languages were gradually replaced by Latin as the Roman Empire expanded.
Scholars study inscriptions in Osco-Umbrian to learn about Italic cultural history.
Linguistic similarities suggest that Latin and Osco-Umbrian share a common ancestor.

Etymology

From the names of the Oscan and Umbrian peoples of ancient Italy.
