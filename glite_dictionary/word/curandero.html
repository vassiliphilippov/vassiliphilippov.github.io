curandero

1. NOUN

/ kuːɾɑnˈdɛɾoʊ / (AmE), / kʊəɾænˈdɛəɹəʊ / (BrE)
A traditional healer, especially in Mexico or Latin America, who uses folk remedies and spiritual practices.
traditional healer in Latin America

Examples

The village curandero was called upon to treat the child's mysterious illness.
Many people visit the curandero for herbal remedies and spiritual cleansing.
She learned about ancient healing methods from a respected curandero in Oaxaca.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: healer - person who treats illness using traditional or alternative methods
  • Synonym: shaman - person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of spirits

Etymology

From Spanish 'curandero' (healer), from 'curar' (to heal).
