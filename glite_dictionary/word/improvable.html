improvable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruːvəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːvəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being improved or made better
capable of improvement

Examples

The company's processes are highly improvable with the right technology.
Her most recent draft is definitely improvable, but it already shows progress.
Many urban spaces are improvable with just a little investment and care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the act or result of making something better
  • Antonym: perfect - having no flaws or defects; not improvable

Etymology

From improve + -able, after French 'améliorable'.
