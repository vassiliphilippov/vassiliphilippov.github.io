Rhizopogon idahoensis

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌzoʊˈpoʊɡən aɪˌdəˈhoʊˌɛnsɪs / (AmE), / rɪˌzɒpəˈɡɒn aɪˌdəʊˈhəʊˌɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A large species of whitish truffle-like fungus, becoming greyish brown when mature, native to parts of North America.
a species of whitish truffle-like fungus

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with specimens of Rhizopogon idahoensis near the roots of pine trees.
Mycologists discovered Rhizopogon idahoensis during their survey of fungi in western Montana.
As it matures, Rhizopogon idahoensis changes color from whitish to a greyish brown hue.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhizo-' (root) and '-pogon' (beard), combined with a Latinized reference to Idaho, USA.
