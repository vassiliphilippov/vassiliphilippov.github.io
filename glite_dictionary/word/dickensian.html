Dickensian

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkɛnziən / (AmE), / dɪˈkɛnziən / (BrE)
Characteristic of the novels of Charles Dickens, especially with regard to squalid social conditions or poverty.
resembling or relating to Dickens's novels, esp. poverty

Examples

The movie depicted Dickensian scenes of overcrowded slums and destitute families.
Many people in Victorian London lived in Dickensian conditions, plagued by poverty and filth.
The school's outdated rules and grim dormitories create a Dickensian atmosphere.

Etymology

From the name of Charles Dickens (1812–1870), the English novelist, plus suffix -ian.
