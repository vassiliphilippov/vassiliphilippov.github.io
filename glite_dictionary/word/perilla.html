Perilla

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / pəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A small genus of Asiatic herbs in the mint family, including plants cultivated for their aromatic leaves often used in cooking.
genus of Asiatic aromatic herbs

Examples

She sprinkled chopped Perilla leaves over the salad for extra flavor.
The garden features several varieties of Perilla among its herbs.
In Asian cuisine, Perilla is often used to wrap rice and fish.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Latin 'perilla', a diminutive of 'pera' (wallet, pouch), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / pəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs of the mint family, native to Asia, including plants like shiso and perilla mint.
genus of Asiatic herbs in the mint family

Examples

The leaves of Perilla are used as an ingredient in many Asian dishes.
Researchers are studying Perilla for its potential medicinal properties.
You can identify Perilla by its distinctively serrated leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name, derived from Italian perilla (a plant, possibly a variant of parilla).
