transference

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsfɜr.əns / (AmE), / trɑːnsˈfɜː.rəns / (BrE)
the act of moving something from one form, place, or medium to another.
the act of transferring something

Examples

The transference of power was completed after the official ceremony.
Many errors can occur during the transference of data between systems.
The transference of knowledge is essential in education.

Synset Examples

the transfer of the music from record to tape suppressed much of the background noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - the act of moving something from one place or person to another

Etymology

From Latin 'transferre' (to carry across) via French 'transférence'.

2. NOUN

/ trænsˈfɝː.əns / (AmE), / trænsˈfɜː.rəns / (BrE)
the act of transferring ownership, rights, or possession from one person or entity to another
act of transferring ownership

Examples

The transference of property rights must be documented by a legal contract.
He arranged for the transference of his shares to his business partner.
The lawyer ensured that the transference of ownership was conducted properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - the act of moving something from one person or place to another

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ‘transferentia’, from Latin ‘transferre’ (to carry over).

3. NOUN

/ trænsˈfɝːəns / (AmE), / trænsˈfɜːrəns / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) the process in which feelings or desires originally associated with one person, especially a parent, are unconsciously shifted onto another person, such as a therapist.
psychoanalytic shifting of feelings to another
medical

Examples

During her therapy sessions, she experienced intense transference toward her analyst.
The psychologist carefully addressed the issue of transference to help the patient understand her feelings.
Classic cases of transference often involve patients shifting childhood emotions onto their therapists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - the unconscious transfer of one's own feelings to another

Etymology

From Latin transferre (to carry over).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06257311-n
Synset POS: n
