lasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlæʃə / (BrE)
a person who urges animals forward by striking them with a whip
person who drives animals with a whip

Examples

The cattle moved faster as the lasher cracked his whip behind them.
As a lasher, he was skilled at managing teams of oxen through difficult terrain.
The old lasher boasted he never missed his mark when driving the horses.

Etymology

From 'lash' (to strike with a whip) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
