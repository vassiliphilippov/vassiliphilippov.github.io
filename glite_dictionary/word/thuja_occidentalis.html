Thuja occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈθuː.dʒə ˌɑk.sɪˈdɛn.tæl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈθjuː.dʒə ˌɒk.sɪˈdɛn.tæl.ɪs / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree of eastern North America with tiny scalelike leaves on flattened branchlets, commonly known as northern white cedar.
north American evergreen tree; northern white cedar

Examples

The forest was full of Thuja occidentalis, giving the area its characteristic scent.
Gardeners often plant Thuja occidentalis as an ornamental shrub or hedge.
The leaves of Thuja occidentalis are used in some traditional medicines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern white cedar - common name for Thuja occidentalis, evergreen tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Thuja' from Greek 'thuia' (a kind of juniper), 'occidentalis' meaning 'western'.
