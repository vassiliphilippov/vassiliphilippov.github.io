lily-livered

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪli ˌlɪvərd / (AmE), / ˈlɪli ˌlɪvəd / (BrE)
Easily frightened; lacking courage; cowardly.
easily frightened; cowardly
archaic

Examples

The knight was accused of being lily-livered for refusing the challenge.
Only a lily-livered coward would run away in the face of danger.
He tried to act tough, but his lily-livered response gave him away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage or bravery
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage or determination

Etymology

From the fanciful notion that a pale (lily-colored) liver was a sign of cowardice (16th century).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪliˌlɪvɚd / (AmE), / ˈlɪliˌlɪvəd / (BrE)
Very cowardly or lacking courage; timid or faint-hearted.
cowardly; lacking courage
archaic

Examples

She accused her brother of being lily-livered when he refused to speak up for himself.
Politicians are often called lily-livered for avoiding tough decisions.
His lily-livered reaction to the challenge disappointed his teammates.

Synset Examples

Don't be lily-livered; take a stand!
She called him lily-livered for backing down.
His lily-livered excuses annoyed everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage, easily frightened
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage; not afraid

Etymology

First used in the 16th century, from the belief that a pale (lily-colored) liver indicated lack of courage.
