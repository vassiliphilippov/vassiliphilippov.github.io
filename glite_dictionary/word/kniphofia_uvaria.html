Kniphofia uvaria

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈfoʊfiə juːˈvɛriə / (AmE), / nɪˈfɒfiə juːˈvɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A clump-forming perennial plant native to South Africa, noted for its tall spikes of scarlet flowers; commonly known as red hot poker.
South African plant with scarlet flower spikes

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Kniphofia uvaria attract hummingbirds to the garden each summer.
Many gardeners plant Kniphofia uvaria to add a splash of color to their flower beds.
The tall flower spikes of Kniphofia uvaria make it a striking ornamental plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red hot poker - Common name for Kniphofia uvaria, a plant with spike-like scarlet flowers

Etymology

From genus name Kniphofia, honoring Johann Hieronymus Kniphof, German botanist; species uvaria from Latin 'uva' (bunch of grapes).
