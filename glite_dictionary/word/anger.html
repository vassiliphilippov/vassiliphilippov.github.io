anger

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
A strong feeling of displeasure or hostility.
strong feeling of displeasure or hostility

Examples

She tried to control her anger during the heated conversation.
His voice trembled with anger as he responded to the accusation.
Long walks help him release his anger in a healthy way.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rage - violent, uncontrollable anger
  • Degree Lower: irritation - a mild feeling of annoyance
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of anger or agitation

Etymology

From Old Norse *angr* (grief, sorrow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
A strong feeling of displeasure and outrage, often resulting from real or perceived injustice, hurt, or annoyance.
strong feeling of displeasure or outrage

Examples

Her voice was trembling with anger as she confronted the manager about the mistake.
He struggled to control his anger when he found out what had happened.
The news sparked widespread anger among the local community.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rage - intense, violent anger
  • Degree Lower: annoyance - slight anger or irritation
  • Antonym: calm - state of being tranquil or without anger

Etymology

From Old Norse 'angr' (grief, sorrow).

3. VERB

/ ˈæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
(of a person) to become angry; to feel or show anger.
become angry (verb)

Examples

He angers easily when things don't go his way.
Sometimes, unfair criticism can anger even the most patient person.
The new regulations are likely to anger many business owners.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrage - to make very angry
  • Antonym: calm - to become less angry; to become tranquil

Etymology

From Old French 'aner', from Latin 'angere' (to strangle, torment).

5. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
A strong feeling of displeasure and belligerence aroused by a perceived wrong; regarded as one of the seven deadly sins in Christian tradition.
strong feeling of displeasure or rage

Examples

She could not hide her anger when she heard the unfair decision.
His anger grew as he remembered the insult.
In many religions, anger is viewed as a sin to be avoided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rage - intense, uncontrolled anger
  • Antonym: calm - the state of being free from strong emotion or agitation

Etymology

From Old Norse 'angr' (grief, sorrow), via Old French 'angre'.
