Ira Gershwin

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪrə ˈɡɜrʃwɪn / (AmE), / ˈaɪrə ˈɡɜːʃwɪn / (BrE)
(1896–1983) An American lyricist renowned for his collaborations with his brother George Gershwin on numerous popular songs and musicals.
U.S. lyricist, George Gershwin's brother

Examples

Ira Gershwin wrote the lyrics for many of the classic songs composed by his brother George.
The songwriting partnership between George and Ira Gershwin produced some of the greatest hits of the 20th century.
Many music historians credit Ira Gershwin with elevating the art of lyric writing in American musical theater.
