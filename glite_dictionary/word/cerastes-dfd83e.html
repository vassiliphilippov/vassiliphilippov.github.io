Cerastes

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈræstiːz / (AmE), / sɪˈrɑːstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of venomous horned vipers native to desert regions of North Africa and the Middle East.
a genus of horned vipers

Examples

The Cerastes genus is well known for species with horn-like projections above their eyes.
Scientists observed a Cerastes slithering across the hot desert sand.
Many people fear the bite of Cerastes due to their potent venom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - a family of venomous snakes
  • Synonym: horned viper - a viper species with hornlike scales over the eyes

Etymology

From Greek kerastēs (horned), referring to the distinctive horns of these vipers.
