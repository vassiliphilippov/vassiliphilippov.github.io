Wiclif

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪklɪf / (AmE), / ˈwɪklɪf / (BrE)
(1328–1384) An English theologian and reformer whose criticisms of Roman Catholic doctrine anticipated the Protestant Reformation.
English religious reformer (1328–1384)

Examples

Wiclif was a forerunner of later reformers such as Martin Luther.
The teachings of Wiclif challenged many practices of the medieval church.
Scholars often study Wiclif when exploring the roots of the Reformation.
