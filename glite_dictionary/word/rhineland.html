Rhineland

1. PROPN

/ ˈraɪnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈraɪnlænd / (BrE)
a picturesque region of western Germany along the banks of the Rhine river, noted for its landscapes, vineyards, and historical towns
region of Germany along the Rhine river

Examples

We spent a summer holiday touring the Rhineland and tasting wines in small villages.
The castles along the Rhineland are popular tourist attractions for visitors from all over the world.
He has vivid memories of his childhood home in the scenic Rhineland.

Etymology

From German 'Rheinland' (Rhine land), from 'Rhein' (Rhine) + 'Land' (land).
