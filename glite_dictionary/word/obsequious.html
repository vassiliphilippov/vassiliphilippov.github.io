obsequious

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsiːkwiəs / (AmE), / əbˈsiːkwɪəs / (BrE)
excessively eager to please or obey someone, often in a way that seems insincere or servile
excessively eager to please; servile

Examples

The manager disliked obsequious behavior from his staff during meetings.
She found the waiter’s obsequious attention rather uncomfortable.
His obsequious compliments did nothing to win her favor.

Synset Examples

obsequious shop assistants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - willing to obey orders or wishes of others
  • Synonym: servile - showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

From Latin obsequiōsus (compliant, dutiful), from obsequium (compliance, dutiful service).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈsi·kwi·əs / (AmE), / əbˈsiː.kwɪ.əs / (BrE)
excessively eager to please or obey someone important, often in a way that is insincere or overly flattering
excessively fawning; overly eager to please

Examples

He surrounded himself with obsequious assistants who agreed with everything he said.
Her obsequious behavior made her unpopular with colleagues who saw it as manipulative.
The waiter's obsequious attentiveness made the guests feel uncomfortable rather than welcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophantic - behaving in a way that flatters influential people for personal gain
  • Synonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: insubordinate - not obeying orders or showing resistance to authority

Etymology

From Latin obsequiosus (compliant, obliging), from obsequium (compliance, dutiful service).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
