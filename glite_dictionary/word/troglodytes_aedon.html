Troglodytes aedon

1. NOUN

/ ˌtroʊɡləˈdaɪtiːz iːˈdɒn / (AmE), / ˌtrɒɡləˈdaɪtiːz iːˈdɒn / (BrE)
a small North American wren, commonly known as the house wren, often found nesting near human dwellings
species of North American house wren

Examples

The Troglodytes aedon is easily recognized by its bubbly song near suburban gardens.
Researchers studied Troglodytes aedon populations in both urban and rural areas.
We spotted several Troglodytes aedon building nests under the eaves of the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house wren - common name for Troglodytes aedon, a North American wren

Etymology

From Greek 'trōglodutēs' (cave-dweller) and Latin 'aedon' (nightingale).
