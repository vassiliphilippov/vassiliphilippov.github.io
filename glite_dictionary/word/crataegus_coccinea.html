Crataegus coccinea

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs kɑkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs kɒkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
an American species of hawthorn with red fruit and dense clusters of pink-red flowers
American red-fruited hawthorn species

Examples

The garden features several Crataegus coccinea trees that bloom beautifully in spring.
Botanists identified the specimen as Crataegus coccinea because of its distinctive red fruit.
Many birds are attracted to the bright berries of the Crataegus coccinea in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or tree of the genus Crataegus

Etymology

From Latin: *Crataegus* is the genus name for hawthorn, and *coccinea* means 'scarlet' or 'red'.
