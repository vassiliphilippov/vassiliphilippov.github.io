to untune

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtun / (AmE), / ʌnˈtjuːn / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose their composure or emotional stability
cause to lose composure
archaic

Examples

The sudden news was enough to untune even the calmest of minds.
Grief can untune a person's inner harmony, leaving them vulnerable and shaken.
It takes a great shock to untune her normally placid demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unsettle - to cause to feel anxious or disturbed

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (reverse) + 'tune', meaning to put out of harmony.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01793821-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈtuːn / (AmE), / ʌnˈtjuːn / (BrE)
to cause (an instrument, string, or sound) to be out of tune
make out of tune (musically)

Examples

Please be careful not to untune the guitar while adjusting the strings.
Excessive heat can untune a piano rather quickly in an unventilated room.
If you untune the violin, it won't sound right for the next performance.

Synset Examples

Don't untune that string!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tune - adjust to correct musical pitch
  • Synonym: detune - to make an instrument or string out of tune, especially intentionally

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (reversing an action) + tune.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00296344-v
Synset POS: v
