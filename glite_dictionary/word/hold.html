/ hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
to remain firm or intact under pressure, attack, or stress; to resist being overcome or destroyed
remain firm under pressure or attack

Examples

The old dam held through the worst of the storm.
Despite mounting pressure, the defenders held against the enemy's attack.
This new fabric holds up well to rough handling.

Synset Examples

The politician defied public opinion
The new material withstands even the greatest wear and tear
The bridge held

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withstand - to remain undamaged or unaffected by; resist
  • Antonym: collapse - to give way under pressure; fall down or give in

Etymology

From Old English *healdan* (to keep, guard).
