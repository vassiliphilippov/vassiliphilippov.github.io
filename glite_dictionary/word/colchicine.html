colchicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊl.tʃɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.tʃɪˌsiːn / (BrE)
a medication derived from the autumn crocus, used to treat gout and certain other inflammatory conditions
medication for gout and inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed colchicine to help manage her gout flare-up.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking colchicine for a long period of time.
In cases of acute gout, colchicine is often considered an effective treatment option.

Etymology

From French colchicine, from Latin Colchicum (autumn crocus), the plant from which it is derived.
