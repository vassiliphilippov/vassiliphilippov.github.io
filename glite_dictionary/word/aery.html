aery

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
(literary) Characterized by lightness, insubstantiality, or ethereality; as intangible or delicate as air.
light, insubstantial, ethereal

Examples

The ghostly forms took on an aery quality in the fading twilight.
Her aery voice seemed to float effortlessly above the music.
The poem describes dreams as aery visions, fleeting and intangible.

Synset Examples

figures light and aeriform come unlooked for and melt away
aerial fancies
an airy apparition
physical rather than ethereal forms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airy - light; relating to air; delicate or insubstantial
  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light; heavenly or spiritual

Etymology

From Latin 'aerius' (of the air), via Old French 'aerien', related to 'air'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
the lofty nest of a bird of prey, such as a hawk or eagle
nest of a bird of prey

Examples

The golden eagle surveyed the valley from its aery perched high on the rocky cliff.
From his writing desk, he could see an aery tucked among the fir trees.
Their guide pointed out an aery where young hawks had recently hatched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyrie - the nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle; variant spelling

Etymology

From Old French *aire* (nest of a bird of prey), from Latin *area* (open space).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09212054-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛəri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
A dwelling or habitation situated at a high altitude, especially one that is elevated or lofty.
lofty dwelling; high-altitude habitation
archaic

Examples

The castle was perched like an aery atop the mountain, overlooking the entire valley.
From their aery in the skyscraper penthouse, they could see the whole city spread out below them.
The monks built their isolated aery high on the cliffs for solitude and peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerie - the nest of a bird of prey, usually in a high place

Etymology

From Latin 'aeria' (airy, of the air) via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08509364-n
Synset POS: n
