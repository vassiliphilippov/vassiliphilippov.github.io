characid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkarəsɪd / (BrE)
any freshwater fish of the family Characidae, commonly found in Central and South America.
freshwater fish of the family Characidae

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep characid species because of their vibrant colors and active swimming behavior.
The Amazon River is home to hundreds of different characid fish.
Scientists studied the diet of the characid to better understand the river's ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetra - any small, brightly colored freshwater fish in the family Characidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Characidae, from Greek kharax (a pointed stake, also used for a kind of fish).
