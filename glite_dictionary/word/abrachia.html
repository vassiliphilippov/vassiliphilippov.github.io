abrachia

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪkiə / (AmE), / əˈbreɪkɪə / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which a person is born without arms.
congenital absence of arms
medical

Examples

The rare disorder known as abrachia often requires specialized prosthetic solutions for daily activities.
She was born with abrachia and has adapted remarkably to life without arms.
Medical researchers continue to study the genetic causes behind conditions such as abrachia.

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + brachion (arm).
