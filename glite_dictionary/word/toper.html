toper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊpər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊpə / (BrE)
A person who drinks alcohol, especially to excess; a habitual drinker.
heavy or habitual drinker

Examples

Despite warnings from his friends, the old toper continued to frequent the pub every night.
Stories of the famous toper spread throughout the small village over the years.
The character in the novel is portrayed as a jovial toper with little regard for sobriety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - a person who is habitually drunk
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcohol

Etymology

From French 'toper' (to agree, shake hands), possibly influenced by 'top' meaning tipple or drink.
