Justinian the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒʌˈstɪniən ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌsˈtɪnɪən ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
Byzantine emperor (482–565) known for codifying Roman law and strengthening the eastern empire.
Byzantine emperor and law codifier (482–565)

Examples

Justinian the Great is renowned for his ambitious efforts to restore the former territories of the Roman Empire.
The Justinian Code, compiled under Justinian the Great, became the foundation of civil law in many countries.
Historians often credit Justinian the Great for revitalizing the Eastern Roman Empire during the 6th century.
