Ricinus communis

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈsaɪnəs kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪnəs kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
a tall, tropical African and Asian shrub with large lobed leaves and spiny fruit capsules containing seeds used to make castor oil and ricin; commonly called the castor oil plant
castor oil plant, tropical shrub species

Examples

Ricinus communis is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are processed into castor oil.
The leaves of Ricinus communis can be quite dramatic, making it a striking ornamental plant.
Doctors warn that all parts of Ricinus communis are highly poisonous if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor oil plant - common English name for Ricinus communis

Etymology

From Latin: 'ricinus' (tick, referring to the seed shape) and 'communis' (common).
