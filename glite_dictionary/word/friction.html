friction

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another.
resistance to movement between surfaces

Examples

The mechanics explained that too much friction between the moving parts was causing the machine to overheat.
Runners use special shoes to reduce friction and improve their speed on the track.
Applying oil to the hinges helps to minimize friction and prevent squeaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - resistance experienced by an object moving through a fluid
  • Antonym: lubrication - process of reducing friction by applying a lubricant

Etymology

From Latin 'frictio' (a rubbing), from 'fricare' (to rub).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
the resistance that occurs when one object moves over another, usually requiring force due to the pressure between the surfaces
resistance to motion between surfaces

Examples

The friction between the tires and the road allows the car to stop safely.
Applying oil can reduce friction between moving metal parts.
He felt the heat caused by the friction as he rubbed his hands together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - the force opposing motion through a fluid
  • Antonym: slip - movement with little friction or resistance

Etymology

From Latin 'frictio' (rubbing), from 'fricare' (to rub).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)

Examples

Despite initial friction, the team eventually learned to work together.
