pounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndə / (BrE)
(in combination) something that weighs a specified number of pounds.
thing weighing a specified number of pounds

Examples

He landed a massive 20-pounder on his fishing trip last weekend.
The new baby is a healthy eight-pounder according to the hospital records.
Their defensive tackle is a 310-pounder with incredible strength.

Synset Examples

the fisherman caught a 10-pounder
their linemen are all 300-pounders

Etymology

From pound (unit of weight) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndə / (BrE)
a heavy tool, usually with a flat base and a handle, used for grinding and mixing materials such as grain, drugs, or pigments against a slab of stone
tool for grinding or mixing materials

Examples

The apothecary used a stone pounder to crush medicinal herbs.
Ancient artists would prepare pigments with a pounder and a flat slab.
A heavy iron pounder was found among the archaeological relics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pestle - a tool with a rounded end, used with a mortar for grinding substances

Etymology

From the verb 'pound' (to crush or grind), with the agent suffix '-er'.
