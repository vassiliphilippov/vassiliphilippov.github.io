fiddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.lə / (BrE)
a person who fidgets with objects or manipulates things in a nervous or unconscious manner
person who fidgets or manipulates things

Examples

As a chronic fiddler, he was always playing with his pen during meetings.
The teacher asked the fiddler in the back row to focus on the lesson.
You could tell she was nervous, being a constant fiddler with her necklace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidgeter - someone who fidgets or is restless
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.lə / (BrE)
a person who plays the violin, especially one who plays folk or traditional music
person who plays the violin

Examples

The fiddler played a lively tune that got everyone dancing.
She is an accomplished fiddler in the local bluegrass band.
Many Irish folk songs feature a solo by the fiddler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violinist - a person who plays the violin, especially in classical music

Etymology

From Middle English 'fideler', from 'fiddle' (a kind of violin) + '-er' (indicating a person who does an activity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪdlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪdlə / (BrE)
a person, especially an amateur or unskilled one, who tries to repair or adjust something, often without expertise
unskilled fixer or tinkerer

Examples

He's something of a fiddler when it comes to electronics, but he rarely fixes anything.
Our old neighbor was a fiddler who was always messing with his car engine.
Rather than hiring a professional, she let a fiddler attempt to repair the leaky faucet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinkerer - a person who enjoys repairing or improving things, often without formal training

Etymology

From 'fiddle' (to tinker or tamper with), combined with the agent suffix '-er.'
