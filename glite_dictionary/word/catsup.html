catsup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætsəp / (AmE), / ˈkætsʌp / (BrE)
a thick, spicy sauce made from tomatoes, usually used as a condiment with food such as hamburgers and fries; a variant spelling of 'ketchup'
tomato-based condiment; variant spelling of 'ketchup'

Examples

Please pass the catsup so I can put some on my fries.
Some brands still use the term catsup instead of ketchup on their labels.
He added a generous dollop of catsup to his hamburger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ketchup - thick tomato-based condiment commonly used on foods like fries and burgers

Etymology

Variant of 'ketchup', possibly from Malay 'kecap' or Chinese 'ke-tsiap' (a type of sauce).
