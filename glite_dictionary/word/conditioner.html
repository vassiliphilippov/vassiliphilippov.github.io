conditioner

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənər / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənə / (BrE)
a substance added during washing, especially of hair or clothes, to make them softer, smoother, or in better condition
substance used to soften hair or clothes

Examples

After shampooing her hair, she applied conditioner to make it soft and shiny.
Some people use fabric conditioner to give their clothes a fresh scent and smooth feel.
He forgot to buy conditioner, so his hair felt dry after washing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shampoo - cleansing substance for hair
  • Synonym: softener - substance used to make things softer, especially in laundry

Etymology

From 'condition' (to bring to a desired state) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənə / (BrE)
an exercise, activity, or thing that improves physical fitness or conditions the body
exercise or activity that improves fitness

Examples

After months of using the treadmill as a conditioner, her stamina greatly improved.
Swimming is an excellent conditioner for your entire body.
Many athletes rely on weightlifting as their primary conditioner during the off-season.

Synset Examples

farm work can be a good conditioner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exercise - physical activity to improve fitness

Etymology

From 'condition' + '-er', indicating something that conditions or trains the body.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00626791-n
Synset POS: n
