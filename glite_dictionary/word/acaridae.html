Acaridae

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɛrɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / əˈkærɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mites that includes certain grain mites and mole mites
family of mites (incl. grain mites)
medical

Examples

The Acaridae can infest stored grains, causing significant loss in agriculture.
Members of the Acaridae are studied in relation to allergies and food contamination.
Proper storage conditions help prevent Acaridae infestations in warehouses.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'akari' (mite) and the taxonomic suffix '-idae' (family).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / əˈkærɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mites that includes various small species, some of which infest stored grains and other organic materials.
family of mites, includes grain mite

Examples

Mites of the Acaridae family are commonly found in stored grain products.
The scientist identified the pest as belonging to Acaridae, a group known for infesting food supplies.
Some species within Acaridae can cause allergic reactions in humans when present in large numbers.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Acaridae', based on 'Acarus' (a mite genus) and the suffix '-idae' (denoting a zoological family).
