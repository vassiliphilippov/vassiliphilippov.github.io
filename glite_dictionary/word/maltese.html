Maltese

1. NOUN

/ mɔlˈtiz / (AmE), / mɔːlˈtiːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Malta
a person from Malta

Examples

Many Maltese people are proud of their island's rich history.
A group of Maltese gathered in the square to celebrate the festival.
He met several Maltese during his trip to Valletta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maltese - the Semitic language spoken in Malta

Etymology

From Malta, the name of the country, plus -ese (denoting nationality).

/ mælˈtiz / (AmE), / mɔːlˈtiːz / (BrE)
the national language of Malta, a Semitic language historically derived from Arabic and influenced by Italian, Spanish, and Norman-French
the national language of Malta

Examples

She speaks fluent Maltese, as well as English and Italian.
The poem was originally written in Maltese before being translated.
Many residents of Malta use Maltese at home and in everyday life.

Etymology

From Malta, the name of the country, with the English suffix '-ese' indicating language or nationality.

5. NOUN

/ mɔlˈtiz / (AmE), / mɔːlˈtiːz / (BrE)
A domestic cat of any breed with short, bluish-grey fur, especially as the term is used in the United States.
cat with bluish-grey fur

Examples

My neighbor adopted a beautiful Maltese that has striking blue-grey fur.
Many people think the Maltese is a specific breed, but it actually refers to the cat's color.
That shelter has several Maltese cats available for adoption this week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian Blue - a breed of cat known for its short bluish-grey coat

Etymology

Derived from Malta, an island in the central Mediterranean, used in reference to the cat's supposed origin or resemblance to cats from Malta.

Source: WordNet
