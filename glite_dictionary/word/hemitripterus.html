Hemitripterus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈtrɪptərəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˈtrɪptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish known as sea ravens, found in the North Pacific and North Atlantic oceans.
a genus of marine fish (sea ravens)

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Hemitripterus genus off the Alaskan coast.
The distinctive fins of the Hemitripterus help it blend into rocky seabeds.
Many marine biologists study Hemitripterus to learn about adaptations in cold-water fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea raven - a common name for fish in the genus Hemitripterus

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + Greek tripteros (three-winged).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈtrɪptərəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˈtrɪptərəs / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish known as sea ravens, found in the North Pacific Ocean, characterized by spiny fins and rough skin
genus of sea raven fishes

Examples

The Hemitripterus is easily recognized by its spiny fins and coarse appearance.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Hemitripterus genus.
The rough skin of Hemitripterus helps protect it from predators in its cold, northern waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea raven - a marine fish of the genus Hemitripterus, known for spiny fins

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + tripteros (having three wings/fins).
