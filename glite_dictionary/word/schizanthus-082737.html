schizanthus

1. NOUN

/ skɪˈzænθəs / (AmE), / skɪˈzænθəs / (BrE)
A plant of a South American genus, Schizanthus, known for its finely divided leaves and brightly colored, showy flowers often cultivated as an ornamental.
ornamental plant with showy flowers

Examples

The gardener planted schizanthus along the border for a splash of color in early summer.
I admired the delicate, butterfly-like petals of the schizanthus at the flower show.
Many people grow schizanthus in greenhouses due to its vibrant and unusual blooms.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (to split) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the deeply divided petals.
