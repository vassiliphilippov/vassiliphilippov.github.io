Sedum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːdəm / (BrE)
a genus of succulent plants, often grown in rock gardens, with thick, fleshy leaves
a genus of succulent plants with fleshy leaves

Examples

Many gardeners use Sedum as ground cover for its drought-resistant qualities.
The thick leaves of Sedum store water, making the plant ideal for dry climates.
We planted several varieties of Sedum in the rock garden last spring.

Etymology

From Latin 'sedum', used for certain succulents.
