meteorology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmitiəˈrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmiːtiəˈrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the atmosphere, weather, and weather forecasting
study of weather and forecasting

Examples

She decided to study meteorology at university because she was fascinated by storms and weather patterns.
Meteorology helps people prepare for dangerous weather conditions such as hurricanes or blizzards.
Advancements in meteorology have greatly improved the accuracy of weather forecasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climatology - the scientific study of climate and climate changes

Etymology

From Greek 'meteōrologia', meaning 'the study of atmospheric phenomena'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmiːtiəˈrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmiːtiəˈrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the atmosphere and its phenomena, especially relating to weather and weather forecasting.
the study of weather and the atmosphere

Examples

She decided to major in meteorology because she was fascinated by storms.
Advances in meteorology have made weather forecasts more reliable in recent years.
The science museum offered a workshop on meteorology for students interested in learning about weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climatology - the scientific study of climate

Etymology

From Greek 'meteōrologia', meaning 'the study of things in the air'.
