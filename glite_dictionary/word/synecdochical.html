synecdochical

1. ADJ

/ sɪˌnɛkˈdɑkɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪˌnɛkˈdɒkɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by synecdoche, a figure of speech in which a part is used to represent the whole, or vice versa, or a material is used to represent the thing made from it
relating to the use of synecdoche

Examples

The poet's use of synecdochical language added depth to the imagery in the poem.
Referring to a fleet of ships as 'sails' is a classic synecdochical expression.
Her analysis highlighted several synecdochical elements in the novel's narrative.

Synset Examples

to use ‘hand’ for ‘worker’ or ‘ten sails’ for ‘ten ships’ or ‘steel’ for ‘sword’ is to use a synecdochic figure of speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synecdoche - figure of speech in which a part is made to represent the whole or vice versa
  • Degree Lower: metonymic - relating to metonymy, a related figure of speech

Etymology

From synecdoche, which comes from Greek synekdoche, meaning 'simultaneous understanding'.
