tapeline

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪpˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪp.laɪn / (BrE)
A measuring instrument consisting of a long, narrow strip of cloth, plastic, or metal marked with units for measuring length.
flexible ruler for measuring length

Examples

The seamstress always has a tapeline draped around her neck while working.
He pulled out his tapeline to double-check the width of the doorway.
A tapeline is essential for measuring irregular surfaces.

Synset Examples

the carpenter should have used his tape measure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape measure - a flexible ruler used to measure size or distance

Etymology

From 'tape' + 'line,' indicating a linear measuring tape.
