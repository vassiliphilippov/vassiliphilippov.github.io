Viola canadensis

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə kəˌnæˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / vɪˈəʊlə kəˌnæˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A tall North American perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and white flowers marked with purple streaks, also known as Canada violet.
a North American perennial plant (Canada violet)

Examples

The woods behind our house are full of Viola canadensis in the spring.
She pressed a Viola canadensis blossom into her herbarium as a memento of her hike.
Botanists often study Viola canadensis to learn about native flora.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Viola' (violet) + 'canadensis' (of Canada).
