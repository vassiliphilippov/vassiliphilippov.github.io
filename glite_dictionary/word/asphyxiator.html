asphyxiator

1. NOUN

/ əsˈfɪksiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / əsˈfɪksiˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a manually operated device designed to extinguish small fires by cutting off the supply of air
device for extinguishing small fires

Examples

Before the fire spread, the guard used an asphyxiator to smother the flames.
Every laboratory should be equipped with an asphyxiator for small fire emergencies.
The asphyxiator was easy to operate and prevented the fire from causing any damage.

Etymology

From Greek 'asphyxia' (stopping of the pulse) + English agent suffix '-ator'.
