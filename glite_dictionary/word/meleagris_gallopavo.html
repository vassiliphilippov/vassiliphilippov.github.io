Meleagris gallopavo

1. NOUN

/ məˌliːəɡrɪs ˌɡæləˈpeɪvoʊ / (AmE), / mɛlɪˌæɡrɪs ˌɡaləˈpɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
the scientific name for the wild turkey, a large North American bird domesticated for food, recognized by its fan-shaped tail
scientific name for the wild turkey

Examples

The scientific name for the wild turkey is Meleagris gallopavo.
Researchers observed a flock of Meleagris gallopavo in the forest reserve.
Domesticated Meleagris gallopavo are a popular source of meat in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild turkey - a large North American bird that is the same species as Meleagris gallopavo

Etymology

From Greek *Meleagris* (guinea fowl) and Latin *gallopavo* (chicken peacock, i.e., turkey).
