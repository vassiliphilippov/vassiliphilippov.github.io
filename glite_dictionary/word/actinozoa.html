Actinozoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪnoʊˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnəʊˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a large class of sedentary marine coelenterates, including sea anemones and corals, in which the medusoid phase is absent
class of marine animals, includes corals and anemones
medical

Examples

Scientists study Actinozoa to better understand the evolution of corals and sea anemones.
Some of the world's oldest reefs are formed by organisms belonging to Actinozoa.
The suppression of the medusoid stage distinguishes Actinozoa from other related animal groups.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek aktis (ray) + zōon (animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪnəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a large class of sedentary marine coelenterates that includes sea anemones and corals, in which the medusoid phase is entirely suppressed
class of sea anemones and corals

Examples

The Actinozoa are known for their vibrant coral formations on tropical reefs.
Scientists study the Actinozoa to understand the evolution of marine biodiversity.
Members of Actinozoa include both solitary sea anemones and colonial corals.

Etymology

From Greek aktis, aktin- (ray) and zōion (animal).
