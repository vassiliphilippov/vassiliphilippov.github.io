Spermatophyta

1. NOUN

/ spɚˌmætəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / spɜːˌmatəˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
A major division of plants that produce seeds, including angiosperms (flowering plants) and gymnosperms (such as conifers).
division of seed-producing plants

Examples

Members of the Spermatophyta include both angiosperms and gymnosperms, which cover most land plants.
Botanists classify conifers as part of the Spermatophyta due to their seed-producing nature.
The study of Spermatophyta has advanced our understanding of plant evolution and diversity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: angiosperm - flowering plant producing seeds enclosed in an ovary
  • Degree Lower: gymnosperm - seed plant with unenclosed seeds, such as conifers
  • Antonym: Pteridophyta - division of spore-producing vascular plants (ferns and their allies)
  • Antonym: Bryophyta - division of non-vascular, spore-producing plants (mosses, liverworts)

Etymology

From Greek 'sperma' (seed) + 'phyton' (plant).

2. NOUN

/ spɚˌmætəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / spəˌmætəˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
A major division of plants that produce seeds, including both flowering plants (Angiospermae) and gymnosperms (Gymnospermae).
division of seed-producing plants
medical

Examples

The Spermatophyta include all plants that reproduce by seeds, such as conifers and flowering plants.
Evolutionary studies often distinguish Spermatophyta from other plant divisions based on their reproductive strategies.
Botanists consider the Spermatophyta to be the dominant group in most terrestrial plant communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Angiospermae - flowering plants; a major group within Spermatophyta
  • Synonym: Gymnospermae - non-flowering seed plants; a major group within Spermatophyta
  • Antonym: Pteridophyta - division of spore-producing vascular plants
  • Antonym: Bryophyta - division of spore-producing non-vascular plants

Etymology

From Greek sperma (seed) + phyton (plant).
