reredos

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪrˌdoʊs / (AmE), / ˈrɪə.dɒs / (BrE)
a decorative screen, often painted or carved, situated behind and above the altar in a church
ornamental screen behind an altar

Examples

The cathedral's magnificent reredos was carved from marble in the 18th century.
Restorers carefully cleaned the painted reredos behind the main altar.
This church is famous for its intricate wooden reredos depicting scenes from the Bible.

Etymology

From Old French *areredos*, from *arere* (behind) + *dos* (back), ultimately from Latin *dorsum* (back).
