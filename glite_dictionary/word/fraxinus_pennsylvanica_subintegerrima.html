Fraxinus pennsylvanica subintegerrima

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ˌpɛnsɪlˈveɪnɪkə ˌsʌbɪntɪdʒəˈrɪmə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs ˌpen.sɪlˈveɪ.nɪ.kə ˌsʌb.ɪn.tɪ.dʒəˈrɪ.mə / (BrE)
a variety of red ash tree distinguished by its glossy branchlets and shiny lower leaf surfaces
variety of red ash with glossy features

Examples

The park planted several Fraxinus pennsylvanica subintegerrima trees along the walking trail.
Botanists can identify Fraxinus pennsylvanica subintegerrima by its glossy branchlets and shiny leaf undersides.
Due to its attractive appearance, Fraxinus pennsylvanica subintegerrima is often selected for urban landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red ash - a species of ash tree (Fraxinus pennsylvanica)

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Fraxinus' (ash tree), 'pennsylvanica' (referring to Pennsylvania), and 'subintegerrima' meaning 'almost entire' or 'nearly whole'.
