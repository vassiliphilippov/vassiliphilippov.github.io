Neurospora

1. NOUN

/ nʊˈroʊspərə / (AmE), / njʊəˈrɒspərə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi with black perithecia, used extensively in genetic research and sometimes as a cause of spoilage in bakeries
genus of fungi used in genetics research

Examples

Neurospora has played a vital role in advancing the field of genetics.
Scientists often use Neurospora to study gene regulation in eukaryotes.
Some bakeries have experienced spoilage due to orange spore masses of Neurospora.

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) + 'spora' (seed, spore), referring to the threadlike structure of the fungus.

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and 'spora' (spore), referring to the threadlike appearance of the spores.
