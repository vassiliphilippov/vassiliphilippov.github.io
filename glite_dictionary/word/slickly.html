slickly

1. ADV

/ ˈslɪkli / (AmE), / ˈslɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is smooth, clever, and often superficial or insincerely plausible.
in a smooth or superficially plausible way

Examples

He answered all the reporters' questions slickly, but his sincerity was questionable.
The company’s advertisement was slickly produced, yet lacked real information.
She slickly convinced the board to support her plan, despite its obvious flaws.

Synset Examples

he talked glibly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glib - (of words or a person) fluent and voluble but insincere and shallow
  • Degree Lower: smoothly - in a smooth or effortless way

Etymology

From 'slick' + '-ly', where 'slick' is from Middle English 'slicke'.
