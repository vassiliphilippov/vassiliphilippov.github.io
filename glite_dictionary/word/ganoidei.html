Ganoidei

1. NOUN

/ ɡænɔɪˈdiːaɪ / (AmE), / ɡænɔɪˈdiːɪ / (BrE)
an order of mostly extinct primitive bony fishes characterized by armor-like bony scales
order of extinct primitive bony fishes

Examples

Fossils of Ganoidei help scientists understand the evolution of early fishes.
The characteristic bony scales of Ganoidei set them apart from modern fish groups.
Many species within Ganoidei have long been extinct, but their remains tell a fascinating story.

Etymology

From Greek 'ganos' (brightness) + 'eidos' (form), referring to the shiny, bony scales.
