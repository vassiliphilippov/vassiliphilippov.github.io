eelpout

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlˌpaʊt / (AmE), / ˈiːlˌpaʊt / (BrE)
a marine fish resembling an eel, typically found dwelling on the sea bottom in northern seas
marine eellike bottom-dwelling fish

Examples

The eelpout is known for its elongated body and habit of lurking on the ocean floor.
Fishermen in Arctic regions often catch eelpout during the winter months.
Biologists study the eelpout to understand how marine species adapt to cold environments.

Etymology

From Old English *æl* (eel) + *pūte* (pouch or bag), possibly referencing the shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈilˌpaʊt / (AmE), / ˈiːlpaʊt / (BrE)
A long, slender freshwater fish found in northern Europe, Asia, and North America, with barbels around its mouth.
elongate freshwater fish with barbels

Examples

The eelpout lurks in cold freshwater lakes beneath the thick ice.
Fishermen often recognize the eelpout by its long body and chin barbels.
During winter, catching an eelpout can be a rare but exciting event for anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burbot - a species of freshwater cod also called an eelpout

Etymology

From 'eel' + 'pout' (Old French 'pou' - fish), referring to its eel-like shape.
