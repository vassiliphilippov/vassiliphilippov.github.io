Icteridae

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds that includes American orioles, blackbirds, bobolinks, and meadowlarks, native to the Americas.
family of American orioles and blackbirds

Examples

The Icteridae family contains a wide range of colorful birds suited to open habitats in the Americas.
Meadowlarks, members of the Icteridae, are known for their melodious calls.
Researchers observed several species from the Icteridae family during the spring migration.

Etymology

From modern Latin Icteridae, from Greek ikteros (jaundice, yellow bird), referring to the yellow coloration in some species.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of passerine birds including American orioles, blackbirds, bobolinks, and meadowlarks, native to the Americas
family of American orioles and blackbirds

Examples

The Icteridae family includes species such as the red-winged blackbird and the Baltimore oriole.
Many members of the Icteridae are renowned for their bright plumage and melodious songs.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of birds in the Icteridae family across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a medium-sized songbird of the family Icteridae in America or Turdidae in Europe
  • Synonym: oriole - any of various brightly colored, chiefly yellow and black birds of the family Icteridae or Oriolidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Icteridae*, from Greek *ikteros* (a yellow bird, the golden oriole).
