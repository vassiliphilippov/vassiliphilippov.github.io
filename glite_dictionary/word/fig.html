fig

1. NOUN

/ fɪɡ / (AmE), / fɪɡ / (BrE)
A tree native to the Mediterranean region, widely grown for its sweet, edible fruit.
Mediterranean tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fig thrives in warm climates and produces delicious fruit.
They planted a fig in their backyard, hoping for a good harvest next summer.
Some species of fig can reach great heights and provide ample shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig - the fruit of the fig tree

Etymology

From Old French *figue*, from Latin *ficus* (fig tree, fig).

2. NOUN

/ fɪɡ / (AmE), / fɪɡ / (BrE)
A soft, sweet fruit with a thin skin that is usually yellowish or purple, eaten fresh, dried, or preserved.
sweet pear-shaped fruit

Examples

She added dried fig to the granola for extra sweetness.
A fresh fig tastes delicious with a drizzle of honey.
They preserved the fig harvest by making jam.

Etymology

From Old French 'figue', from Latin 'ficus' (fig, fig tree).
