Antennariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.təˌnɛr.iˈaɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌæn.təˌnɛər.ɪˈiː.diː / (BrE)
A family of tropical, spiny-finned marine fishes known as frogfishes, characterized by large, nearly vertical mouths.
family of tropical frogfishes

Examples

The Antennariidae are masters of camouflage, blending seamlessly with their surroundings.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Antennariidae family off the coast of Indonesia.
Many divers are fascinated by the unique hunting methods of the Antennariidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogfish - Common name for fishes in the family Antennariidae

Etymology

From New Latin Antennarius (the type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ ænˌtɛnəˈnɛrɪˌaɪˌdi / (AmE), / ænˌtɛnəˈnɛəriːˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical spiny-finned marine fishes known as frogfishes, characterized by large, nearly vertical mouths; related to toadfishes and anglers.
family of tropical frogfishes

Examples

The Antennariidae are well known for their extraordinary camouflage abilities among coral reefs.
Species in the Antennariidae family use a worm-like lure to attract their prey.
Marine biologists discovered a new member of the Antennariidae along the Indonesian coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogfish - common name for members of Antennariidae
  • Synonym: anglerfish - related family known for similar predatory adaptations

Etymology

From New Latin Antennaria (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
