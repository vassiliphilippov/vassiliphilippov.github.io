Perdicidae

1. NOUN

/ pərˈdɪsɪˌdi / (AmE), / pəˈdɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds comprising the Old World partridges.
family of Old World partridges

Examples

Many species within the Perdicidae family inhabit grasslands and open woodlands.
The Perdicidae are distinct from pheasants, which belong to another family.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of several Perdicidae species in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a game bird of the family Perdicidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *perdix* (partridge) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
