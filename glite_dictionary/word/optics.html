optics

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːptɪks / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
the qualities or properties of a device, system, or material relating to light and vision, especially its ability to transmit, refract, or reflect light
optical properties of something

Examples

Engineers analyzed the optics of the new camera lens to improve image clarity.
The optics of the microscope were carefully adjusted for maximum magnification.
The telescope's optics determine how much detail can be seen in distant objects.

Synset Examples

the optics of a telescope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optical - relating to sight or light

Etymology

From Greek optikē (art of seeing), from optikos (of sight)

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪks / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies the behavior and properties of light, including its interactions with matter and the instruments used to detect it
study of light in physics

Examples

She decided to specialize in optics because she was fascinated by lasers and fiber optics.
Recent advances in optics have led to the development of more powerful microscopes.
The course covered the fundamentals of optics, including reflection, refraction, and diffraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physics - the natural science concerned with the study of matter, energy, and the fundamental forces
  • Synonym: acoustics - the branch of physics dealing with sound

Etymology

From Greek optikē (art of seeing), from optikos (of sight, optical).
