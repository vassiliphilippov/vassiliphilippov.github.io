touchable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃəbl / (BrE)
able to be touched; perceptible by the sense of touch.
able to be touched

Examples

The sculpture is meant to be touchable so that visitors can feel the textures.
Those objects aren't just for display—everything here is touchable.
The velvet fabric was soft and touchable, inviting you to run your fingers across it.

Synset Examples

skin with a tangible roughness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangible - capable of being touched; having physical substance
  • Antonym: intangible - unable to be touched; not having physical presence

Etymology

From the verb 'touch' + suffix '-able', indicating capability.
