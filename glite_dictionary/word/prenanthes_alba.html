Prenanthes alba

1. NOUN

/ priːˈnænθiːz ˈælbə / (AmE), / priːˈnænθiːz ˈælbə / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant native to northeastern North America, with drooping clusters of yellowish-white flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes
herb with drooping yellowish-white flowers

Examples

The woodland was dotted with clumps of Prenanthes alba blooming in late summer.
Botanists studied the unique flower structure of Prenanthes alba in its natural habitat.
Gardeners appreciate Prenanthes alba for its delicate, drooping white blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prenanthes' (a plant genus), 'alba' meaning 'white'.
