alpine

1. ADJ

/ ˈæl.paɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.paɪn / (BrE)
relating to high mountains, especially the Alps, or typical of such regions.
of or relating to high mountains, esp. the Alps

Examples

She enjoys alpine skiing during the winter holidays.
The region is known for its alpine flora and clear mountain lakes.
His team specializes in alpine rescue missions in remote areas.

Synset Examples

alpine sports

Etymology

From Latin 'Alpīnus', from 'Alpēs' (the Alps).

WordNet ID: oewn-03037260-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈæl.paɪn / (AmE), / ˈal.paɪn / (BrE)
(of plants or animals) living or growing above the timber line, especially in high mountain regions.
living or growing above timber line

Examples

Many alpine plants survive extreme cold and strong winds at high elevations.
She studies alpine ecosystems for her biology research.
The garden features a variety of rare alpine flowers that flourish above the timber line.

Synset Examples

alpine flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Alpīnus', meaning 'of the Alps'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
