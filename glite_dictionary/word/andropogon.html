Andropogon

1. NOUN

/ ˌændrəˈpoʊɡən / (AmE), / ˌandrəˈpəʊɡən / (BrE)
A genus of tall annual or perennial grasses with spikelike racemes, commonly found in warm regions.
genus of tall warm-region grasses

Examples

Several species within the Andropogon genus are important for pasturelands in tropical climates.
The feathery seed heads of Andropogon often stand out in prairies during late summer.
Researchers studied Andropogon to understand its role in soil conservation.

Etymology

From Greek andros (man) + pogon (beard), referring to the beardlike appearance of the flower spikes.

2. NOUN

a genus of tall annual or perennial grasses with spikelike racemes, found in warm regions
a genus of tall grasses

Examples

Species in the genus Andropogon are widely distributed across tropical and subtropical regions.
Big bluestem, a member of Andropogon, is a dominant grass in North American prairies.
The diversity within the Andropogon genus makes it important for ecological research.

Etymology

From Greek andros (man) + pogon (beard), referring to the beard-like appearance of the flower clusters.
