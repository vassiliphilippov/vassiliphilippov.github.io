viscounty

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsˌkaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈvɪskaʊnti / (BrE)
A district or territory governed by a viscount or viscountess.
district ruled by a viscount(ess)

Examples

During the Middle Ages, the viscounty grew in both influence and size.
The borders of the old viscounty remain visible in local place names today.
She inherited the estate and the title of the viscounty upon her father's death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchy - the domain controlled by a duke or duchess
  • Synonym: county - region or territory administered as a unit

Etymology

From French 'vicomté', from Medieval Latin 'vicecomitatus' (the office or district of a viscount).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪskaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈvɪskaʊnti / (BrE)
the rank, dignity, or office of a viscount or viscountess
rank of a viscount or viscountess

Examples

The title was elevated from a barony to a viscounty in the mid-seventeenth century.
Inheritance of the viscounty passed to his eldest son upon his death.
She was proud to hold the viscounty, continuing the family’s long legacy of service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscount - a nobleman ranking above a baron and below an earl or count

Etymology

From Middle English viscounté, from Anglo-Norman, from Old French viscomté (see viscount).
