simmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə / (BrE)
A state or temperature just below the boiling point, often used in cooking to allow gentle heating.
temperature just below boiling point

Examples

Let the sauce cook at a simmer until it thickens.
Keep the soup at a gentle simmer so the flavors can meld.
After bringing the water to a boil, reduce the heat to maintain a simmer.

Synset Examples

the stew remained at a simmer for hours

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boil - to reach the temperature at which a liquid turns to vapor
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05021740-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə / (BrE)
to cook or heat (a liquid or food) gently below the boiling point so that small bubbles form slowly and steadily
cook gently below boiling point

Examples

Allow the soup to simmer for thirty minutes before serving.
She let the rice simmer gently in the pot until all the water was absorbed.
Bring the sauce to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer it uncovered.

Synset Examples

simmer the sauce
simmering water

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boil - to heat a liquid until it bubbles vigorously
  • Degree Lower: to poach - to cook food gently in water just below the boiling point

Etymology

Of unknown origin; perhaps imitative of the sound of gently boiling liquid.
