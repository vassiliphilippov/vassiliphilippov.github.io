alkylbenzenesulfonate

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.kəlˌbɛn.ziːnˈsʌl.fəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌæl.kɪlˌbɛn.ziːnˈsʌl.fəˌneɪt / (BrE)
A synthetic organic compound that is a sulfonate derivative of alkyl benzene, commonly used as a surfactant in detergents and cleaning agents.
a sulfonate derivative of alkyl benzene
medical

Examples

Linear alkylbenzenesulfonate is a common ingredient in household laundry detergents.
Scientists studied how alkylbenzenesulfonate breaks down in water treatment plants.
Some industrial cleaners use alkylbenzenesulfonate as a primary surfactant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surfactant - a substance that reduces surface tension, often used in cleaning agents

Etymology

Formed from 'alkyl' + 'benzene' + 'sulfonate,' indicating its molecular structure.
