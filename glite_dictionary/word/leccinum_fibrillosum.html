Leccinum fibrillosum

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈsɪnəm faɪˌbrɪˈloʊsəm / (AmE), / lɛˈsɪnəm fɪˌbrɪˈləʊsəm / (BrE)
An edible mushroom with a dark reddish-brown cap and a wide, light tan stalk that expands toward the base.
species of edible mushroom

Examples

The mushroom hunter was excited to find a patch of Leccinum fibrillosum in the woods.
Many foragers appreciate Leccinum fibrillosum for its delicate flavor and firm texture.
After identifying the Leccinum fibrillosum, she carefully collected several specimens for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boletus - a genus of mushrooms with a thick stem and often a sponge-like underside

Etymology

From Latin; Leccinum, a genus of bolete mushrooms, and fibrillosum, meaning 'fibrous'.
