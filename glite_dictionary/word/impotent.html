impotent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.pə.tənt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pətənt / (BrE)
lacking power or ability to act effectively; powerless or helpless
powerless; lacking ability or strength

Examples

The government appeared impotent in the face of the growing crisis.
He felt impotent to stop the injustice he witnessed.
Many people experience impotent anger when they cannot change a difficult situation.

Synset Examples

Technology without morality is barbarous; morality without technology is impotent
felt impotent rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerless - lacking power or ability
  • Antonym: powerful - having great power or strength

Etymology

From Latin *impotens* (powerless), from *in-* (not) + *potens* (powerful).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.pə.tənt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pətənt / (BrE)
(of a man) unable to achieve or maintain an erection or to copulate.
unable to have sexual intercourse (male)

Examples

The doctor discussed possible treatments for men who are impotent.
He struggled with feelings of inadequacy after realizing he was impotent.
Many physical and psychological factors can cause a man to become impotent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erectile dysfunction - difficulty in achieving or maintaining an erection
  • Antonym: potent - having sexual potency; able to copulate (of a male)

Etymology

From Latin 'impotens', meaning 'powerless, weak'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01831268-a
Synset POS: a
