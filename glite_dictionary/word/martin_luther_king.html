Martin Luther King

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑrtɪn ˈluːθər kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɑːtɪn ˈluːθə kɪŋ / (BrE)
(1929–1968) United States civil rights leader and Baptist minister known for his role in the American civil rights movement and his advocacy of nonviolent protest against racial segregation.
U.S. civil rights leader and minister (1929–1968)

Examples

Every January, Americans honor Martin Luther King with a national holiday.
Martin Luther King delivered his famous 'I Have a Dream' speech in Washington, D.C.
Many schools across the country are named after Martin Luther King.
