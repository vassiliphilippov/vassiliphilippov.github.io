/ ˈnɑkər / (AmE), / ˈnɒkə / (BrE)

Examples

The project didn't need another knocker to bring down morale.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑkər / (AmE), / ˈnɒkə / (BrE)
a metal object fixed to a door and used to knock for attention by lifting and striking it against the door
metal object for knocking on doors

Examples

She lifted the heavy knocker and rapped loudly on the old wooden door.
A brass knocker shaped like a lion's head adorned the entrance.
The sound of the knocker echoed through the empty hallway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doorbell - electronic device for signaling at a door

Etymology

From Middle English knokere, from knock (to strike).

4. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈnɒkə / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly US) A person who is boastful and self-important; someone who shows off their success or status, often with arrogance.
boastful, self-important person
informal american

Examples

Everyone in the office avoided the knocker who bragged constantly about his achievements.
She didn't want to be seen as a knocker, so she stayed humble despite her success.
The club's reputation suffered because its president was such a notorious knocker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braggart - a person who boasts about achievements or possessions
  • Synonym: show-off - a person who behaves in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
  • Antonym: modest person - someone who does not boast or show off

Etymology

Probably from Yiddish, relating to 'knock' as in talking up oneself, and influenced by similar English slang usage.
