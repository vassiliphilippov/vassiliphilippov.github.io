glove

1. NOUN

/ ɡlʌv / (AmE), / ɡlʌv / (BrE)
A padded covering for the hand used in the sport of boxing to protect the hands and soften blows.
padded hand covering for boxing

Examples

The referee checked both fighters' gloves before the match began.
You should always wear proper gloves to protect your hands when boxing.
His old gloves were torn after months of intense training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitt - a type of padded glove, often used in baseball or boxing

Etymology

From Middle English 'glove', from Old English 'glof'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlʌv / (AmE), / ɡlʌv / (BrE)
A piece of clothing that covers the hand and wrist, often with separate parts for each finger, worn to protect the hands or keep them warm.
handwear covering hand and wrist

Examples

He put on a pair of gloves to keep his hands warm in the winter.
Surgeons wear sterile gloves during operations to prevent infection.
She found a leather glove lying on the park bench.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mitten - a type of glove that covers the hand but not the fingers separately

Etymology

From Middle English *glove*, from Old English *glōf* (glove).

3. NOUN

/ ɡlʌv / (AmE), / ɡlʌv / (BrE)
A padded leather piece of handwear with webbing between the thumb and fingers, used by fielders in baseball to catch and field the ball.
baseball fielder's handwear

Examples

He bought a new glove before the baseball season started.
The outfielder stretched out his glove to catch the fly ball.
Every player on the team brought their own glove to practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitt - a baseball glove with extra padding, typically for catchers or first basemen

Etymology

From Middle English 'glof', from Old English 'glōf' (covering for the hand).
