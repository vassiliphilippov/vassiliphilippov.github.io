file

1. NOUN

/ faɪl / (AmE), / faɪl / (BrE)
a container, usually with drawers, used in offices for storing and organizing papers
container for organizing papers

Examples

She placed the documents carefully in the top drawer of the file.
The accountant spent hours searching through the file for last year's receipts.
Each employee has a personal file where all their paperwork is kept.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filing cabinet - a large piece of office furniture with drawers for storing files

Etymology

From Latin filum (thread), originally referring to a string for holding documents together.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03342085-n
Synset POS: n

/ faɪl / (AmE), / faɪl / (BrE)

tool for smoothing or shaping surfaces

Always clean your file after use to maintain its performance.

Related Terms

Etymology
