Latimeria

1. NOUN

/ ˌlætɪˈmɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌlætɪˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of rare deep-sea fishes known as coelacanths, considered living fossils and once thought extinct.
genus of coelacanth fishes

Examples

Scientists were astonished to discover a living Latimeria specimen off the coast of South Africa in 1938.
The Latimeria is often described as a 'living fossil' due to its ancient lineage.
Researchers study Latimeria to learn more about the evolution of vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelacanth - a rare order of ancient lobe-finned fish

Etymology

Named after Marjorie Courtenay-Latimer, the discoverer of the first modern specimen.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlætɪˈmɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌlætɪˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of rare, deep-sea fish known as coelacanths, considered living fossils and the type genus of the family Latimeridae.
genus of living coelacanth fish

Examples

The discovery of a living Latimeria off the coast of South Africa astonished scientists in 1938.
Researchers studied the anatomy of Latimeria to gain insight into the evolution of vertebrates.
A specimen of Latimeria is displayed in the National Museum for educational purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelacanth - A rare order of fish, considered living fossils, to which Latimeria belongs

Etymology

Named after Marjorie Courtenay-Latimer, who first discovered the modern coelacanth.
