tanka

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈtæŋ.kə / (BrE)
a traditional Japanese poem consisting of five lines, with a syllable pattern of 5-7-5-7-7
form of Japanese five-line poetry

Examples

She composed a tanka describing the peaceful morning by the lake.
In our workshop, we learned how to write a traditional tanka.
Many Japanese poets express their emotions through the brief form of the tanka.

Etymology

From Japanese 'tanka' (短歌), meaning 'short poem'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑŋkə / (AmE), / ˈtæŋkə / (BrE)
A Tibetan religious painting made on fabric, often depicting Buddhist deities or scenes, and used for meditation or ceremonial purposes.
Tibetan religious painting on fabric

Examples

The museum recently acquired a beautiful tanka depicting the life of the Buddha.
She hung a brightly colored tanka in her meditation room for inspiration.
Conservators carefully restored the damaged tanka to preserve its intricate details.

Etymology

From Tibetan 'thang ka', meaning 'recorded message' or 'painting'.
