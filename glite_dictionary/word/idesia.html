idesia

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdiːʒə / (AmE), / aɪˈdiːzɪə / (BrE)
A deciduous, roundheaded tree native to Asia, cultivated in mild climates for its ornamental heart-shaped leaves, fragrant yellow-green flowers, and clusters of orange-red berries.
Asiatic ornamental tree with orange-red berries

Examples

The idesia in our garden produces beautiful clusters of orange-red berries each autumn.
Many landscapers recommend idesia for its striking heart-shaped leaves and ornamental value.
An idesia can add a touch of elegance and color to parks and botanical gardens.

Etymology

From the botanical Latin genus name Idesia, named after Japanese botanist Keisuke Ito (surname variant: Ides).
