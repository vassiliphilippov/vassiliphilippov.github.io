Mikhail Kalinin

1. PROPN

/ məˈxil kəˈlinin / (AmE), / məˈxiːl kəˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
(1875–1946) A Soviet statesman who served as the head of state of the USSR.
Soviet statesman and head of USSR

Examples

Mikhail Kalinin played a significant role in the formation of Soviet political institutions.
The city of Kaliningrad was named after Mikhail Kalinin.
Many history books discuss the influence of Mikhail Kalinin on early Soviet leadership.
