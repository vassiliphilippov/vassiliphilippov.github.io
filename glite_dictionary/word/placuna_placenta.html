Placuna placenta

1. NOUN

/ pləˈkuːnə pləˈsɛntə / (AmE), / pləˈkjuːnə pləˈsɛntə / (BrE)
a species of marine bivalve mollusk found in Philippine coastal waters, known for its large, thin, flat, translucent shell, often called the windowpane oyster
species of translucent marine bivalve mollusk
medical

Examples

The delicate shell of the Placuna placenta is prized for making decorative items and windowpanes.
Harvesting Placuna placenta is a significant coastal livelihood in some parts of the Philippines.
Researchers are studying the habitat of Placuna placenta to support conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windowpane oyster - common name for Placuna placenta, a translucent bivalve mollusk

Etymology

From Latin; Placuna is the genus name, meaning 'flat plate', and placenta means 'flat cake'.
