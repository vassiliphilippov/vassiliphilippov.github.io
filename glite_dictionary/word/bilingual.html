bilingual

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈlɪŋɡwəl / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪŋɡwəl / (BrE)
a person who is able to speak two languages with equal fluency.
person fluent in two languages

Examples

Many bilingual employees are in high demand at international companies.
She grew up as a bilingual in both Spanish and English.
Schools often offer programs to help bilingual children succeed academically.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polyglot - a person who knows and can use several languages
  • Degree Lower: monolingual - a person who speaks only one language

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'lingua' (tongue, language).

2. ADJ

/ baɪˈlɪŋɡwəl / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪŋɡwəl / (BrE)
able to use or involving two languages with equal or nearly equal fluency; able to speak two languages.
able to use two languages

Examples

She grew up in a bilingual household, speaking both Spanish and English fluently.
The school offers a bilingual education program for immigrant children.
Many companies now seek bilingual employees to serve a diverse client base.

Synset Examples

bilingual education

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monolingual - able to use only one language
  • Degree Higher: multilingual - able to use more than two languages

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'lingua' (language).

Synset POS: s
