Ursus arctos middendorffi

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜːrsəs ˈɑːrktəs ˌmɪd.ənˈdɔr.fi / (AmE), / ˈɜːsəs ˈɑːktəs ˌmɪd.əndˈɔː.fɪ / (BrE)
a subspecies of brown bear native to coastal Alaska and British Columbia, also known as the Kodiak bear, one of the largest bears in the world
Kodiak bear subspecies of brown bear

Examples

The Ursus arctos middendorffi is renowned for its immense size and strength.
Conservation efforts have helped protect the habitat of Ursus arctos middendorffi on Kodiak Island.
Researchers track the population health of Ursus arctos middendorffi as an indicator of ecosystem stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kodiak bear - common name for Ursus arctos middendorffi
  • Degree Lower: brown bear - the species to which Ursus arctos middendorffi belongs

Etymology

From Latin: *ursus* (bear), *arctos* (bear in Greek), and named after Aleksandr Middendorff, a Russian explorer.
