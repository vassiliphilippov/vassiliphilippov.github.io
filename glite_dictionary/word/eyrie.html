eyrie

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
a nest built in a high place by a bird of prey such as an eagle or hawk
nest of a bird of prey (high place)

Examples

The golden eagle built its eyrie on a rocky cliff high above the valley.
From our campsite, we could see the distant eyrie perched atop the crag.
The naturalist observed two chicks in the eyrie nestled in the pine tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerie - another spelling of 'eyrie', nest of a bird of prey

Etymology

From Old French *aire* (nest of a bird of prey) and Medieval Latin *aeria*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪri, ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri, ˈɛəri / (BrE)
A building, room, or dwelling situated at a very high altitude, often with a commanding view.
dwelling at a high altitude

Examples

He built his mountain retreat as a luxurious eyrie overlooking the valley below.
The radio host broadcasted from his eyrie high above the city skyline.
The monastery's eyrie location made it perfect for contemplation and solitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerie - a large nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle; also a high dwelling

Etymology

From Old French *aire* (nest), from Latin *area* (open space).
