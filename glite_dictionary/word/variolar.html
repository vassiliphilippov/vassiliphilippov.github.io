variolar

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɛriˈoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌværɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of smallpox.
relating to smallpox
medical

Examples

Historians studied the variolar lesions preserved in early illustrations of the disease.
The variolar outbreak devastated whole communities in the eighteenth century.
He examined variolar scars on the patient's face to confirm a past infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - a serious infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

From Latin variola (smallpox) with the suffix -ar.
