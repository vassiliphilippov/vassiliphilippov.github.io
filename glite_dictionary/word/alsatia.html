Alsatia

1. PROPN

/ ælˈseɪʃə / (AmE), / ælˈseɪʃə / (BrE)
A region in northeastern France, known for its wine production and mixed French and German cultural influences.
region in France famous for wines

Examples

Many tourists visit Alsatia each year to explore its picturesque villages and vineyards.
The wines produced in Alsatia are considered among the best in the world.
He studied the unique blend of cultures found in Alsatia during his trip to France.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Alsatia', referring to the historical region.
