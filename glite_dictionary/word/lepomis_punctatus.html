Lepomis punctatus

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈpoʊmɪs pʌŋkˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / lɛˈpəʊmɪs pʌŋkˈtɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of sunfish native to streams in the southeastern United States, valued as a panfish.
species of sunfish found in southeastern U.S.

Examples

The Lepomis punctatus is commonly found in slow-moving streams of the southeast.
Anglers appreciate the Lepomis punctatus for its spirited fight and good taste.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Lepomis punctatus in Georgia rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted sunfish - Common name for Lepomis punctatus, a species of sunfish

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Lepomis' (from Greek 'lepis' meaning 'scale' and 'poma' meaning 'cover') and Latin 'punctatus' meaning 'spotted.'
