khesari

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɑːri / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːri / (BrE)
An annual Eurasian plant (Lathyrus sativus) grown for forage, with seeds used for food in India and as stock feed elsewhere.
forage plant; edible seeds used in India

Examples

In some regions of India, khesari is an important crop for both human consumption and animal feed.
Farmers often use khesari as a forage plant because it grows well in poor soils.
Prolonged consumption of khesari seeds may pose health risks due to certain neurotoxins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass pea - Plant (Lathyrus sativus) cultivated for fodder and edible seeds

Etymology

From Hindi 'khesari', referring to Lathyrus sativus.
