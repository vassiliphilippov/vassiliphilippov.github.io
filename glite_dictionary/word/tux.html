tux

1. NOUN

/ tʌks / (AmE), / tʌks / (BrE)
A man's semiformal evening suit, typically including a jacket with satin or grosgrain facing and matching trousers; short for 'tuxedo'.
semiformal men's evening suit

Examples

He wore a classic black tux to the gala and looked very elegant.
The invitation said 'black tie,' so I had to rent a tux for the event.
All the groomsmen matched in their sharp tuxes at the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuxedo - a semiformal evening suit for men
  • Degree Lower: suit - a set of outer clothes made of the same fabric and designed to be worn together

Etymology

Shortened form of 'tuxedo', originally named after Tuxedo Park, New York.
