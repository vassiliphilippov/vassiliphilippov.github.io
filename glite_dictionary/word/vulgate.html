Vulgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡeɪt / (BrE)
the principal Latin version of the Bible, translated chiefly by St. Jerome in the late 4th century and later adopted as the official text of the Roman Catholic Church
principal Latin version of the Bible

Examples

The Vulgate remained the standard Bible text for the Roman Catholic Church for centuries.
Scholars often refer to the Vulgate when comparing biblical translations.
St. Jerome's Vulgate had a profound influence on Western Christianity.

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgata editio' (commonly used edition).
