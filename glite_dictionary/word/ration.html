ration

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ən / (BrE)
a fixed amount of something, such as food or supplies, officially allowed to each person, especially during a shortage or in wartime
fixed portion allotted, especially during scarcity

Examples

During the war, each family received a weekly ration of meat and butter.
We had to survive on half our usual ration of water in the desert.
Chocolate was considered a luxury and included in a soldier’s ration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - an amount of something given or permitted, often regularly
  • Synonym: quota - a fixed share or portion assigned to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'ratio' (calculation, measure), via French 'ration'.

2. VERB

/ ˈræʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ən / (BrE)
to restrict the consumption or allocation of something, especially when it is in short supply
restrict or limit usage (esp. in scarcity)

Examples

During the drought, the government decided to ration water to ensure there was enough for everyone.
When fuel supplies ran low, the army rationed gasoline to its vehicles.
Parents sometimes ration their children's screen time to encourage other activities.

Synset Examples

Bread was rationed during the siege of the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - set or serve as a restriction
  • Synonym: to withhold - refuse to give or allow something
  • Degree Lower: hoard - accumulate and store goods

Etymology

From Latin *ratio* (reckoning, calculation) via French *ration* (allowance).

3. NOUN

/ ˈræʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ən / (BrE)
a fixed amount of food officially allowed to each person, especially a soldier or sailor, for one day
fixed daily food allowance

Examples

Each soldier was given a ration of bread and cheese for the day.
During the expedition, their ration was reduced to conserve supplies.
The emergency ration included dried fruit and energy bars.

Synset Examples

the rations should be nutritionally balanced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - a fixed quantity of something permitted, especially food or money
  • Synonym: portion - a share or part of a whole, especially food

Etymology

From Latin *ratio* (reckoning, calculation), via French *ration* (ration).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07581290-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈræʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ən / (BrE)
to distribute a fixed amount of something, especially because there is not enough for everyone to have as much as they want
distribute in fixed, limited amounts

Examples

During the war, food supplies were rationed to make sure everyone had enough.
The government decided to ration electricity during the energy crisis.
Each family was rationed only two gallons of water per week.

Synset Examples

Cigarettes are rationed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: hoard - to save or accumulate something secretly or carefully

Etymology

From Latin *rationem* (a reckoning, calculation).
