stressor

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɛsər / (AmE), / ˈstrɛsə / (BrE)
an event, situation, or agent that causes stress to an organism, especially a person
something that causes stress
medical

Examples

Financial problems can be a significant stressor in many people's lives.
Loud noises are a common stressor for people with sensory sensitivities.
It's important to identify your main stressors to manage anxiety effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - a state of mental or emotional strain resulting from adverse circumstances

Etymology

From 'stress' + '-or', formed in analogy with words like 'compressor'.
