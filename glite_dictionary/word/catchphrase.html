catchphrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌfreɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatʃˌfreɪz / (BrE)
A well-known phrase or expression that is frequently used, especially one associated with a particular person, group, or context.
distinctive, often-repeated phrase

Examples

The show's most famous catchphrase quickly became part of popular culture.
He ended every speech with his trademark catchphrase.
Many advertisements rely on a clever catchphrase to be memorable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a short, memorable phrase used in advertising or politics
  • Synonym: catchword - a word or phrase made popular by repeated use

Etymology

Formed from 'catch' (to capture attention) and 'phrase' (a group of words); popularized in the 20th century.
