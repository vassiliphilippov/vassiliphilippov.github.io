pomelo

1. NOUN

/ pəˈmɛloʊ / (AmE), / pəˈmiːləʊ / (BrE)
a large citrus fruit with a thick rind and sweet, dry flesh, similar to a grapefruit but usually larger and less juicy
large citrus fruit similar to grapefruit

Examples

The pomelo is often eaten fresh or used in salads in Southeast Asian cuisine.
She bought a pomelo at the market to try something new.
Despite its size, the pomelo is surprisingly easy to peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapefruit - a large, round, tart citrus fruit related to the pomelo

Etymology

From Dutch 'pompelmoes', likely from Tamil 'pampaḷimasu' or Portuguese 'pomposos limões'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈmɛloʊ / (AmE), / pəˈmiːləʊ / (BrE)
A large citrus fruit with thick rind and juicy flesh, native to Southeast Asia, or the evergreen tree that bears this fruit.
large citrus fruit or its tree

Examples

The pomelo is prized in Southeast Asia for its sweet, mild-tasting flesh.
She used segments of pomelo to make a refreshing citrus salad.
A mature pomelo tree can yield dozens of large, pale green fruits each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapefruit - a large citrus fruit related to the pomelo

Etymology

From Dutch pompelmoes, ultimately from Tamil pampa limāsu (big citrus).
