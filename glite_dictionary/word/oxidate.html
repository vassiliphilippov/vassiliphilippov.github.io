to oxidate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdeɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to combine with oxygen or to be converted into an oxide, especially as a chemical reaction.
to combine with oxygen; convert to an oxide
medical

Examples

Iron will oxidate quickly when exposed to moist air.
Some enzymes can oxidate organic substances in living organisms.
If copper pipes are not coated, they may gradually oxidate and turn green.

Synset Examples

This metal oxidizes easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oxidize - to combine or become combined chemically with oxygen

Etymology

From Latin 'oxidatum', past participle of 'oxidare' (to oxidize), from 'oxygenium' (oxygen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00240207-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to add oxygen to or combine with oxygen, especially in a chemical process
add oxygen to (chemistry)

Examples

Iron will eventually oxidate when it is left exposed to air and moisture.
Some chemical reactions oxidate metals very rapidly, producing rust.
Biologists study how enzymes can oxidate organic molecules during metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oxidize - to combine or become combined chemically with oxygen
  • Antonym: to reduce - to remove oxygen from a compound (chemistry)

Etymology

From Latin 'oxidatus', past participle of 'oxidare', from 'oxygenium' (oxygen).
