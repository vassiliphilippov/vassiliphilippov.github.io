coyote

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈoʊti / (AmE), / kaɪˈəʊti / (BrE)
A forest firefighter who is sent to battle remote and severe forest fires, often for days at a time.
forest firefighter for severe, remote fires

Examples

The team sent a seasoned coyote to tackle the wildfire deep in the mountains.
As a coyote, she spent long days and nights far from civilization.
Only the toughest coyotes volunteered for fighting fires on the most inaccessible slopes.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈoʊti / (AmE), / kaɪˈəʊti / (BrE)
A small wild wolf native to North America, especially the western regions, known for its adaptability and distinctive howl.
small wild wolf of North America

Examples

A coyote was seen crossing the road just before sunrise.
Farmers sometimes hear the distant howling of a coyote at night.
The children watched a coyote hunt for small animals in the field.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wolf - a large wild carnivorous mammal related to the coyote

Etymology

From Spanish 'coyote', from Nahuatl 'coyotl' (a kind of wild dog).

3. NOUN

/ kaɪˈoʊti / (AmE), / kaɪˈəʊti / (BrE)
a person who smuggles illegal immigrants across the border into the United States, especially from Mexico
person who smuggles migrants into the US

Examples

The authorities arrested a coyote who was transporting people across the border.
Many migrants pay a coyote large sums of money for the dangerous journey north.
The newspaper reported that coyotes often abandon people in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - a person who illegally transports people or goods across borders

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'coyote', originally meaning 'animal, trickster', by extension used for human smugglers.
