amputee

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.pjəˈti / (AmE), / ˌam.pjʊˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who has had a limb surgically removed, usually because of injury or disease
person missing a limb due to amputation

Examples

The veteran became an amputee after losing his leg in combat.
As an amputee, she had to learn how to use a prosthetic arm.
The organization offers support for every amputee in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amputation - surgical removal of a limb or part of a limb

Etymology

From Latin 'amputare' (to cut off) + English suffix '-ee' (person affected).
