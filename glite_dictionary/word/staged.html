staged

1. ADJ

/ steɪdʒd / (AmE), / steɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a play, event, or production) adapted for or performed on a theatre stage.
adapted for performance on stage

Examples

The director announced a new staged adaptation of the classic novel for next season.
A staged reading of the script will be held in the theater tomorrow night.
Many literary works have been successfully staged despite their complex narratives.

Synset Examples

a staged version of the novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrical - connected with the theater or performances on the stage
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02302172-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ steɪdʒd / (AmE), / steɪdʒd / (BrE)
deliberately arranged or planned to produce a particular effect, rather than being spontaneous or genuine.
deliberately arranged for effect

Examples

The company denied that the protest was staged to gain publicity.
Many reality TV shows include staged scenes to increase drama.
The photos looked too perfect and clearly staged for the campaign.

Synset Examples

one of those artfully staged photographs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spontaneous - done without planning or premeditation; occurring naturally
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01576262-s
Synset POS: s
