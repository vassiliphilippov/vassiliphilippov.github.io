unconstitutionally

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnˌkɑnˌstɪˈtuʃənəli / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌkɒnˌstɪˈtjuːʃənəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is not allowed or authorized by a constitution, especially a country's fundamental laws.
in a way that violates the constitution

Examples

The president was accused of acting unconstitutionally in bypassing the legislative process.
The law was passed unconstitutionally without proper review by the courts.
Critics argued that the new regulations were imposed unconstitutionally and should be challenged.

Synset Examples

the president acted unconstitutionally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconstitutional - not allowed by a constitution or the fundamental law
  • Antonym: constitutionally - in a way that is allowed by the constitution

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + constitution (body of fundamental principles) + -ally (adverbial suffix).
