chapiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæpɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃapɪtə / (BrE)
the upper part of a column, usually ornate, that supports the entablature in classical architecture; a capital
upper part of a column; capital

Examples

The ornate chapiter was intricately carved with acanthus leaves.
Restoration work included repairing the chapiter of the ancient column.
Each chapiter in the cathedral reflected a different architectural style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capital - the topmost part of a column

Etymology

From Old French *chapitre*, from Latin *capitulum* (head, small head).
