to conjure

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.dʒər / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.dʒə / (BrE)
to call or bring into existence as if by magic; to summon or produce, often using supernatural or mysterious means
summon or bring about as if by magic

Examples

The magician seemed to conjure a rabbit out of thin air.
The author can conjure vivid images with just a few words.
Legends say the priest could conjure spirits to protect the village.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summon - to call for the presence of someone or something, especially with authority or magic
  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring something to mind or recollection, or to summon a spirit

Etymology

From Latin 'conjurare' (to swear together, conspire), via Old French 'conjurer'.
