Euphorbia fulgens

1. NOUN

/ juːˌfɔːrbiə ˈfʊldʒənz / (AmE), / juːˌfɔːbɪə ˈfʊldʒənz / (BrE)
A Mexican shrub of the spurge family, often cultivated for its scarlet-bracted flowers.
Mexican shrub with scarlet-bracted flowers

Examples

The striking blooms of Euphorbia fulgens made it a favorite in botanical gardens.
Gardeners appreciate Euphorbia fulgens for its long-lasting, vibrant red bracts.
She decided to plant Euphorbia fulgens along the walkway for a touch of color.

Etymology

From genus name Euphorbia (named after Euphorbus, a Greek physician) and Latin fulgens (shining, glittering).
