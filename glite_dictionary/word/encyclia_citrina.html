Encyclia citrina

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɪkliə sɪˈtraɪnə / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɪkliə sɪˈtraɪnə / (BrE)
A Mexican epiphytic orchid with glaucous grey-green leaves and partially opened, lemon- to golden-yellow flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Cattleya.
a Mexican epiphytic orchid species

Examples

Encyclia citrina is prized by collectors for its striking yellow blooms and citrus scent.
The glaucous leaves of Encyclia citrina make it easy to distinguish from other orchid species.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Encyclia citrina should be placed in the genus Cattleya.

Etymology

From the genus name Encyclia (from Greek 'enkykleos', encircling) and species epithet citrina (Latin for 'lemon-yellow').
