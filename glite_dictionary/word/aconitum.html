Aconitum

1. NOUN

/ əˈkoʊnɪtəm / (AmE), / əˈkɒnɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous plants native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by hood-shaped flowers; also known as monkshood or wolfsbane.
genus of poisonous plants (monkshood, wolfsbane)

Examples

The beautiful but deadly Aconitum grows in the forests of Northern Europe.
Gardeners should handle Aconitum with care due to its toxic properties.
The distinctive hood-shaped flowers of Aconitum make it easy to recognize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkshood - common name for plants of the Aconitum genus
  • Synonym: wolfsbane - another common name for Aconitum plants

Etymology

From Latin *aconitum*, from Greek *akoniton*, referring to the plant.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkoʊnɪtəm / (AmE), / əˈkəʊnɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous plants found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, known for their helmet-shaped flowers.
a genus of poisonous plants (monkshoods)

Examples

The garden contained several rare species within the genus Aconitum.
Members of Aconitum are also known as monkshood due to their distinctive flower shape.
Botanists warn that all parts of Aconitum plants are highly toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkshood - common name for Aconitum plants
  • Synonym: wolfsbane - common name for Aconitum plants, especially A. lycoctonum

Etymology

From Greek akoniton, the classical name for the plant.
