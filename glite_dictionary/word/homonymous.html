homonymous

1. ADJ

/ həˈmɑː.nɪ.məs / (AmE), / həˈmɒn.ɪ.məs / (BrE)
(of words) having the same spelling or pronunciation but different meanings; relating to or being homonyms.
having same spelling or sound, different meanings

Examples

The word ‘bat’ is homonymous, as it refers to both an animal and a sporting implement.
Confusion can occur with homonymous terms in legal documents.
In crossword puzzles, homonymous clues often make the solutions more challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homonymic - having the nature of a homonym
  • Degree Lower: polysemous - having multiple related meanings

Etymology

From Greek homōnymos, from homos (same) + onyma (name).
