Ionia

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈoʊniə / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊniə / (BrE)
an ancient region on the central coast of western Asia Minor, colonized by the ancient Greeks and known for its rich cultural and historical significance
ancient Greek region in Asia Minor

Examples

The philosophers of Ionia made significant contributions to early Western thought.
Many important cities, such as Ephesus and Miletus, were located in Ionia.
The art and architecture of Ionia greatly influenced later Greek civilization.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ionia', from Ancient Greek 'Ἰωνία' (Iōnía).
