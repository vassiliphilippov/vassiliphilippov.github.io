raccoon

1. NOUN

/ ræˈkun / (AmE), / rəˈkuːn / (BrE)
A small, mainly nocturnal mammal native to North America and Central America, known for its distinctive black facial markings and omnivorous diet.
nocturnal North American mammal

Examples

A raccoon was rummaging through the trash cans behind our house last night.
The raccoon washed its food in the river before eating it.
Zoo visitors enjoyed watching the playful antics of the young raccoons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coati - a related mammal in the same family as the raccoon
  • Synonym: opossum - another omnivorous nocturnal mammal native to the Americas

Etymology

From the Powhatan (an Algonquian language) word 'aroughcun'.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈkun / (AmE), / rəˈkuːn / (BrE)
The fur of the North American raccoon, used especially for making garments.
fur of the raccoon (used in clothing)

Examples

She wore a luxurious coat made of raccoon for the winter gala.
Traditional trappers once supplied raccoon to hat makers across North America.
The designer's new line features collars lined with soft raccoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mink - the fur of the mink, used in high-quality garments

Etymology

From Powhatan (Virginia Algonquian) *aroughcun*, adopted into English in the 17th century.
