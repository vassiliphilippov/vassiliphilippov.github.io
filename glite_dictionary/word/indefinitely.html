indefinitely

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdɛfəntli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnətli / (BrE)
for an unlimited or unspecified period of time
for an unlimited time

Examples

The meeting has been postponed indefinitely due to unforeseen circumstances.
He said he could wait indefinitely for her response.
Construction on the new building was halted indefinitely.

Synset Examples

this could go on indefinitely

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: forever - for all time; without end
  • Degree Lower: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

From 'indefinite' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'indefinitus' (not limited).
