plower

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈplaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who plows fields using a plow, especially as an occupation
someone who plows fields

Examples

The plower guided his horses steadily across the muddy field.
In ancient times, the plower was respected for his essential contribution to the village.
During the spring, every plower prepared his land for planting crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plowman - a man who plows, especially one who plows for a living

Etymology

From the verb 'plow' + agent noun suffix '-er'.
