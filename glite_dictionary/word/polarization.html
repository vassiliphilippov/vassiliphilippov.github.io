polarization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or phenomenon by which waves of light or other radiation are restricted to vibrate in particular directions.
restriction of wave vibrations to a direction
medical

Examples

The polarization of light can be observed using certain types of filters.
Scientists studied the polarization of microwaves in their experiments.
The sunglasses reduce glare by blocking the polarization of reflected light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depolarization - loss or removal of polarization

Etymology

From Latin 'polaris' (of the pole) and English '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition or process in which something acquires or has polarity, especially in physics or chemistry.
the condition of having polarity

Examples

The polarization of water molecules gives it unique properties.
Certain materials allow easy polarization under an electric field.
The experiment measured the degree of polarization in the sample.

Etymology

From French 'polarisation', from 'polaire' (polar) + '-isation'.
