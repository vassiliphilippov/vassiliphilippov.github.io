sideways

1. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.weɪz / (BrE)
in a direction from or toward the side; obliquely or with a sideward movement.
from or toward the side; obliquely

Examples

The car skidded sideways on the icy road, barely missing the curb.
She glanced sideways at him, unsure of his reaction to the news.
The wind blew so strongly that the rain lashed against the windows sideways.

Synset Examples

a picture lit sideways
scenes viewed sidewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliquely - at an angle; not in a direct way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00456343-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.weɪz / (BrE)
toward, to, or at one side; with a side facing forward.
toward or at one side

Examples

She glanced sideways at him before answering the question.
The crab moved sideways across the sand, searching for food.
When the wind blew hard, the rain hit the window sideways.

Synset Examples

darting eyes looking sidelong out of a wizened face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidelong - toward or at one side; slanting or indirect

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'ways', a pattern for indicating direction or manner in English (cf. 'lengthways', 'crossways').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00455713-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.weɪz / (BrE)
In or to the side; toward or from one side rather than straight ahead or back.
toward or from the side

Examples

The rain blew sideways against the windows during the storm.
She glanced sideways at her friend, unsure of what to say.
The bookshelf was too wide, so they turned it sideways to fit through the door.

Synset Examples

the car slipped sideways into the ditch
leaning sideways
a figure moving sidewise in the shadows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laterally - in a lateral or sideways direction

Etymology

From 'side' + 'ways', modeled after 'endways' and similar adverbial forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00456164-r
Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.weɪz / (BrE)
Moving, facing, or directed to or from the side; at or towards an angle rather than straight ahead.
at or towards an angle; not straight ahead

Examples

She glanced sideways at her friend, trying not to laugh.
The car skidded sideways on the icy road.
He moved sideways to let someone pass.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - extending or moving in one direction only, without bending or curving

Etymology

From 'side' + 'ways', patterned after 'lengthways', 'crossways'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01724015-s
Synset POS: s

5. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.weɪz / (BrE)
with one side facing forward or toward the front, rather than the usual forward-facing direction
with one side facing forward

Examples

The car skidded sideways on the icy road before coming to a stop.
He glanced sideways at her, unsure how she would react to the news.
The painting was hanging sideways and needed to be adjusted.

Synset Examples

turned sideways to show the profile
crabs seeming to walk sidewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidewise - with one side forward or to the front

Etymology

From side + ways (adverbial form of 'way'), indicating direction toward the side.
