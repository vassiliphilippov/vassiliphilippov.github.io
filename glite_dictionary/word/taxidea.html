Taxidea

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsaɪdiə / (AmE), / tækˈsaɪdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of the family Mustelidae comprising the American badger and its close relatives.
genus of American badgers

Examples

The American badger belongs to the genus Taxidea.
Researchers studied the distribution of Taxidea in different habitats across North America.
Taxidea species play an important role in the ecosystem as burrowing mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - burrowing carnivorous mammal of the family Mustelidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Taxidea, formed from taxus (badger) + -idea (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ tækˈsaɪdiə / (AmE), / tækˈsɪdiə / (BrE)
a genus of mustelid mammals that includes the American badger
genus of mustelid mammals (American badger)

Examples

The American badger belongs to the genus Taxidea.
Researchers study the behavior of mammals in the genus Taxidea.
Taxidea is classified within the family Mustelidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a burrowing nocturnal mammal of the genus Taxidea or Meles

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Taxidea', based on 'taxus' (badger) with a Latinizing suffix.
