Pelargonium peltatum

1. NOUN

/ pɛlˌɑːrˈgoʊniəm pɛlˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / pɛlˌɑːlˈgəʊniəm pɛlˈteɪtəm / (BrE)
a species of trailing plant native to South America, commonly cultivated for its peltate leaves and clusters of rosy flowers; also known as the ivy-leaved geranium
ivy-leaved geranium species

Examples

The hanging basket was filled with blooming Pelargonium peltatum that cascaded over its edges.
Many gardeners prefer Pelargonium peltatum because of its attractive, glossy leaves and long-lasting flowers.
When grown in containers, Pelargonium peltatum provides a vivid display of color throughout the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivy geranium - common name for Pelargonium peltatum

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pelargonium' (genus name from Greek 'pelargos' meaning stork) and 'peltatum' (Latin for 'shield-shaped', referring to the leaves).
