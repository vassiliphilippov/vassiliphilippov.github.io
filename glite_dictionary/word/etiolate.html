to etiolate

1. VERB

/ ˈiːtiəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːtɪəleɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant) to make or become pale and weak by growing in insufficient light, resulting in a lack of chlorophyll.
to grow pale and weak from lack of light

Examples

When celery is grown in the dark, it can quickly become etiolated and lose its green color.
The gardener noticed that the seedlings had etiolated due to being kept in a shaded greenhouse.
If you etiolate your houseplants by placing them in a closet, they will become weak and yellowish.

Synset Examples

etiolated celery

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pale - lacking color or brightness

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *etiolare* (to make pale), from French *étioler*.

/ ˈiːtiəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːtɪəleɪt / (BrE)
to make or become pale and weak, especially by depriving of light or vitality
to make or become pale and weak

Examples

Long hospital stays can etiolate formerly healthy children.
Excessive indoor living may etiolate one's complexion over time.
The lack of sunlight etiolated the houseplants, turning their leaves pale and limp.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pale - having little color or brightness; lacking vitality

Etymology

From French étioler, originally referring to making plants pale by depriving them of light.

3. VERB

/ ˈiːtiəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːtɪəleɪt / (BrE)
to make weak or pale by preventing normal growth or development, especially by depriving of light
make weak or pale by stunting growth

Examples

Lack of exposure to sunlight can etiolate young seedlings, making them pale and weak.
Constant neglect may etiolate a child’s innate curiosity and creativity.
The long winter months seemed to etiolate the town’s spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make less strong or powerful

Etymology

From French *étioler*, perhaps from a Germanic root related to 'straw'.

4. VERB

/ ˈiːtiəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːtɪəˌleɪt / (BrE)
to cause (a plant) to become pale and weak by growing it without sufficient sunlight
make a plant pale by lack of sunlight
medical

Examples

If you etiolate the seedlings by keeping them in the dark, they will turn yellow and spindly.
Gardeners sometimes etiolate shoots deliberately to produce tender, pale vegetables.
Plants etiolated by lack of sunlight exhibit weak stems and pale leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blanch - to make pale, especially by depriving of light

Etymology

From French *étioler* (to make pale), probably from Old French *estuier* (to shut up, stifle).
