triceratops

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈsɛrəˌtɑps / (AmE), / traɪˈsɛrətɒps / (BrE)
a large herbivorous dinosaur of the late Cretaceous period, having three horns on its head and a large bony frill at the neck
three-horned dinosaur with neck frill

Examples

The museum features a complete triceratops skeleton in its dinosaur exhibit.
A triceratops used its horns and frill for defense against predators.
Children often recognize the triceratops because of its distinctive three horns.

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'keras' (horn) + 'ops' (face), meaning 'three-horned face.'
