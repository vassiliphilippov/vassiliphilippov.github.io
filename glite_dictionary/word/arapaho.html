Arapaho

1. PROPN

/ əˈræpəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / əˈrapəhəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally from the Plains, particularly eastern Colorado and Wyoming, now living mainly in Oklahoma and Wyoming.
a member of a Native American people

Examples

The Arapaho once roamed across the Great Plains alongside other tribes.
Many Arapaho now live on reservations in Wyoming and Oklahoma.
She is studying the language and traditions of the Arapaho people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cheyenne - a member of another Plains Indian tribe closely associated with the Arapaho

Etymology

Probably from Hidatsa arúpahu (trader).

2. NOUN

/ əˈræpəhoʊ / (AmE), / əˈrapəhəʊ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Arapaho people, indigenous to the plains of North America
Algonquian language of the Arapaho people

Examples

He studied the structure of Arapaho for his graduate thesis.
The elders still speak Arapaho during community gatherings to keep the language alive.
A new dictionary has been published to help young people learn Arapaho.

Etymology

From the name of the Arapaho people, of uncertain origin, possibly from a word in another Algonquian language referring to 'traders'.
