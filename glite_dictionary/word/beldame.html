beldame

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.dəm / (BrE)
an old woman, especially one who is considered ugly or frightening
old woman (often ugly or frightening)
archaic

Examples

The villagers whispered that the beldame in the cottage was a witch.
A huddled beldame begged for alms on the icy street corner.
Legends often describe a wise but solitary beldame dwelling deep in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crone - an old woman who is thin and ugly
  • Synonym: hag - an old woman, especially one who is unpleasant or frightening

Etymology

From Old French *belle-dame* (fair lady), used ironically.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌdeɪm / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.deɪm / (BrE)
an old woman, especially one who is ugly or frightening
an ugly or frightening old woman
archaic

Examples

The villagers often spoke of the beldame who lived alone at the end of the lane.
In the story, a beldame curses the young hero with bad luck.
Children would run from the sight of the old beldame, frightened by her sharp voice and wild hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hag - an ugly or unpleasant old woman
  • Synonym: crone - an old woman, usually one who is thin and ugly

Etymology

From Old French 'belle-dame' meaning 'fair lady,' later used ironically.
