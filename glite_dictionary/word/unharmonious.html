unharmonious

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.hɑːrˈmoʊ.ni.əs / (AmE), / ʌn.hɑːˈmɒn.i.əs / (BrE)
Not forming a pleasing or consistent whole; lacking harmony.
not in harmony; discordant

Examples

The colors chosen for the room were strikingly unharmonious and clashed with each other.
Their voices were so unharmonious that the choir director stopped the rehearsal.
The company's departments developed an unharmonious relationship, causing frequent misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discordant - disagreeing or incongruous; inharmonious
  • Antonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + harmonious.
