wormhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːrmˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmˌhəʊl / (BrE)
A small hole made in wood, soil, or another material by a burrowing worm or similar creature.
hole made by a burrowing worm

Examples

The old log was riddled with wormholes left by insects and larvae.
Careful inspection revealed several wormholes in the apple's surface.
The antique table is valued for its character, including the small wormhole marks.

Etymology

From 'worm' + 'hole', meaning a hole created by a worm.
