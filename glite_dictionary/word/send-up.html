send-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛndˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈsɛndˌʌp / (BrE)
a humorous imitation or parody that exaggerates or mocks the style of a person, group, or work
humorous imitation or parody
british

Examples

The new film is a brilliant send-up of classic spy movies.
Her performance was a clever send-up of the famous singer's mannerisms.
The comedian's send-up of politicians had the whole audience laughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - a humorous or exaggerated imitation of a work, person, or style
  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor or exaggeration to criticize people or ideas

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'send up', meaning to mock or parody, originating in British English in the mid-20th century.
