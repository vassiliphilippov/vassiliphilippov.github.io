allantoid

1. ADJ

/ əˈlæntɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈlæntɔɪd / (BrE)
sausage-shaped; having a form resembling a sausage, especially used in biological contexts.
sausage-shaped (biology, anatomy)

Examples

The bacteria formed allantoid cells under certain growth conditions.
Some spores are allantoid in appearance, making them easy to distinguish under the microscope.
The structure was described as allantoid, elongated and slightly curved, in the research paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusiform - spindle-shaped; tapering at both ends
  • Synonym: cylindrical - having the form of a cylinder

Etymology

From Greek allas, allant- (sausage) + -oid (like, resembling).
