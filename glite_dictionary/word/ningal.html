Ningal

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪŋ.gɑl / (AmE), / ˈnɪŋ.gɑːl / (BrE)
An Akkadian goddess, wife of the Mesopotamian Moon god Sin.
Akkadian goddess, wife of Sin

Examples

In ancient Mesopotamian texts, Ningal is often mentioned as the consort of the moon god.
The temple dedicated to Ningal was a significant site of worship in Ur.
Mythological stories describe Ningal as a caring motherly figure among the gods.

Etymology

From Akkadian *Ningal* meaning 'Great Lady'.
