uveitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːviˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌjuːviˈaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the uvea, the middle layer of the eye.
inflammation of the uvea of the eye
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with uveitis after experiencing eye pain and blurred vision.
Chronic uveitis can lead to complications such as glaucoma or cataracts if not treated promptly.
Certain autoimmune diseases increase the risk of developing uveitis in both eyes.

Etymology

From New Latin uvea (the middle layer of the eye) + -itis (Greek for inflammation).
