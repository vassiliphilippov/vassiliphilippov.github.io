Himmler

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɪmlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪmlə / (BrE)
Heinrich Himmler (1900–1945), a leading German Nazi official, chief of the SS and Gestapo, who oversaw the Holocaust.
Nazi chief of SS and Holocaust overseer

Examples

Himmler played a central role in organizing the systematic persecution and extermination of millions during the Holocaust.
The orders given by Himmler shaped the inner workings of the SS throughout Nazi Germany.
After the war, the legacy of Himmler was widely condemned by the international community.
