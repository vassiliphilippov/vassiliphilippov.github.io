racer

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈreɪsə / (BrE)
a fast car designed and built for competing in races
fast car for racing

Examples

The sleek racer sped past its competitors on the track.
He invested a fortune in building his own racer for the championship.
After the crash, the racer had to be towed back to the pit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racecar - a car built or modified for racing

Etymology

From 'race', meaning a contest of speed, + '-er', denoting a thing that performs the action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈreɪsə / (BrE)
an animal, such as a horse or dog, that takes part in races
an animal that races competitively

Examples

The trainer prepared the racer for its first competition.
After the race, the racer was given a thorough check-up by the veterinarian.
She owns three champion racers that compete across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racehorse - a horse bred and trained for racing
  • Synonym: racing dog - a dog bred and trained to race, especially a greyhound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02387510-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈreɪsə / (BrE)
a slender, fast-moving snake found in North America, especially of the genus Coluber
a fast-moving North American snake

Examples

The racer darted across the path and vanished into the tall grass.
We spotted a black racer sunning itself on a warm rock.
Unlike vipers, the racer is not venomous and relies on its speed to escape predators.
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈreɪsə / (BrE)
a person who drives racing cars at high speeds, especially as a sport or profession
person who drives racing cars

Examples

The professional racer prepared his car for the championship race.
She dreamed of becoming a famous racer after watching Formula One on television.
Many young racers compete each year in hopes of winning major titles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driver - person who drives a vehicle

Etymology

From race (contest of speed) + -er (agent suffix).
