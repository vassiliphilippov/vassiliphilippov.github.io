Pinophytina

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪnoʊfaɪˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌpɪnəʊfaɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A taxonomic subdivision comprising most conifers; in some classification systems, considered a class (Pinopsida) or a subdivision of gymnosperms.
taxonomic group of conifers
medical

Examples

Much of the world's timber comes from species classified within Pinophytina.
Botanists debate whether Pinophytina should be considered a class or a subdivision.
The majority of living coniferous plants belong to the group known as Pinophytina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinopsida - class of gymnosperms including conifers; sometimes synonym for Pinophytina
  • Degree Higher: Gymnospermae - group of seed-producing plants including conifers, cycads, etc.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pino-' (from Latin 'pinus', pine) and '-phytina' (from Greek 'phyton', plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.nəˈfaɪ.tɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌpɪn.əˈfaɪ.tɪ.nə / (BrE)
A subdivision of vascular plants that includes most conifers; in some classification systems, it is treated as a class (Pinopsida) and in others as a subdivision of Gymnospermophyta.
major plant group containing most conifers
medical

Examples

The Pinophytina include pine, fir, and spruce trees.
Scientists debate whether Pinophytina should be considered a class or a subdivision.
Members of Pinophytina are important both ecologically and economically worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinopsida - the class containing conifers in some classifications

Etymology

From Latin 'pinus' (pine) and Greek 'phyton' (plant).
