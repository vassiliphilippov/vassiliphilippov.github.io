Arachnida

1. NOUN

/ əˈræknɪdə / (AmE), / əˈraknɪdə / (BrE)
A large class of arthropods that includes spiders, scorpions, ticks, and mites, typically having eight legs and no wings.
class of arthropods including spiders and scorpions

Examples

Scientists have discovered a new species within the Arachnida class inhabiting the rainforest canopy.
Unlike insects, members of the Arachnida have eight legs and lack antennae.
The biology textbook includes a detailed chapter on Arachnida and their life cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arachnid - any member of the class Arachnida

Etymology

From Greek aráchnē (spider) + -ida (suffix denoting class).

2. NOUN

/ əˈræk.nɪ.də / (AmE), / əˈræk.nɪ.də / (BrE)
a large class of arthropods that includes spiders, scorpions, ticks, and related animals, typically having eight legs and no wings or antennae
class of eight-legged arthropods (spiders, scorpions, etc.)

Examples

Spiders, scorpions, and ticks all belong to the Arachnida class.
Unlike insects, members of Arachnida have eight legs and lack antennae.
The biology textbook included detailed diagrams of various Arachnida species.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek arakhnē (spider) + -ida (indicating a zoological class).
