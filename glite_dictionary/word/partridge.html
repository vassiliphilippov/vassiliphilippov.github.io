partridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small game bird of the pheasant family found in Eurasia and North Africa, with short legs and brown, gray, or white plumage
a small Old World game bird

Examples

A flock of partridges settled quietly in the field at dusk.
He enjoyed hunting partridge during the autumn season.
The restaurant served roasted partridge with wild mushrooms and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pheasant - a large game bird, related to the partridge, with long tail feathers

Etymology

From Old French 'pertris', from Latin 'perdix', from Greek 'perdix'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
A medium-sized, short-tailed game bird, valued for hunting, especially in the eastern United States and Canada.
a medium-sized game bird

Examples

We spotted a partridge in the underbrush during our morning hike.
The hunter returned with two partridges for dinner.
A partridge flushed from the field as we approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - another type of game bird

Etymology

From Old French 'pertris', from Latin 'perdix', 'perdicis', from Ancient Greek 'perdix'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a medium-sized game bird with a plump body and short tail, commonly found in Europe, Asia, and North America, often hunted for sport and food.
medium-sized game bird

Examples

A covey of partridge burst out of the underbrush as we walked through the field.
The chef prepared a delicious roast partridge with herbs and vegetables.
Hunters look forward to partridge season every autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - a similar ground-dwelling game bird

Etymology

From Old French 'perdriz,' from Latin 'perdix, perdicem' (partridge).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
A heavy-bodied game bird found in South America that resembles gallinaceous birds but is related to ratite birds.
South American game bird

Examples

The South American partridge is known for its distinctive call.
Scientists have discovered new facts about the diet of the partridge.
Unlike other game birds, this partridge is related to ratite species.

Etymology

From Old French 'pertris', from Latin 'perdix', 'perdicis' meaning 'partridge'.
