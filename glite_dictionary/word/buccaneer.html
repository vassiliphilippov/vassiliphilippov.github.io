buccaneer

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌkəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌbʌkəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a pirate or person who attacks and robs ships at sea, especially one who does so without official authorization
pirate; sea robber

Examples

The notorious buccaneer terrorized the Caribbean during the seventeenth century.
She dressed as a buccaneer for the costume party, complete with a tricorn hat and eye patch.
Historians debate whether the buccaneers were criminals or folk heroes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea

Etymology

From French 'boucanier', originally referring to Caribbean hunters who smoked meat ('boucan').

2. VERB

/ ˌbʌk.əˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌbʌk.əˈnɪə / (BrE)
to act in a piratical or adventurous manner; to live or operate like a buccaneer.
act in a piratical or adventurous manner

Examples

For several years, he decided to buccaneer along the Caribbean, seeking his fortune on the high seas.
They buccaneered their way through the Mediterranean, attacking merchant ships.
The young captain dreamed of buccaneering across exotic oceans, chasing both adventure and treasure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - to commit acts of piracy
