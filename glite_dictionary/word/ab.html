AB

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˈbi / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈbiː / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells carry both the A and B antigens; the fourth main type in the ABO blood group system
a blood group with A and B antigens
medical

Examples

She discovered her blood type was AB after taking a blood test at the hospital.
Donors with AB blood are considered universal plasma recipients.
Both of his parents carry the genes for the AB blood group.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: type A - a blood group whose red cells carry the A antigen
  • Degree Lower: type B - a blood group whose red cells carry the B antigen
  • Degree Lower: type O - the blood group whose red cells carry neither A nor B antigens

Etymology

From the letters representing the A and B antigens in the ABO blood group system, introduced in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈbi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈbiː / (BrE)
a Bachelor of Arts degree, an undergraduate degree awarded for a course in the arts or sciences
Bachelor of Arts degree (academic title)

Examples

She graduated with an AB in history from Harvard University.
An AB is equivalent to a Bachelor of Arts in most universities.
After earning his AB, he applied to law school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: BA - Bachelor of Arts degree (common abbreviation)

Etymology

From Latin 'artium baccalaureus' (Bachelor of Arts).
