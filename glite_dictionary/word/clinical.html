clinical

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɪnɪkl̩ / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkl / (BrE)
relating to a medical clinic, or based on direct observation and treatment of patients rather than theoretical or laboratory studies
relating to clinics or medical observation
medical

Examples

The doctor made a clinical diagnosis based on the patient's symptoms and appearance.
She is conducting a clinical trial to test the effectiveness of the new drug.
His clinical experience helps him provide excellent care to his patients.

Synset Examples

clinical observation
clinical case study

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laboratory - relating to work done in a laboratory or through experimental study

Etymology

From Greek 'klinike' (medical practice at the sickbed), via French 'clinique'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02896833-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɪnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl / (BrE)
showing no emotion or personal involvement; scientifically detached or dispassionate
emotionally detached; dispassionate

Examples

Her analysis of the situation was so clinical that it seemed devoid of any human warmth.
He gave a clinical account of the events, never allowing emotion to influence his words.
The report's clinical tone made it difficult for readers to empathize with the victims.

Synset Examples

he spoke in the clipped clinical monotones typical of police testimony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispassionate - not influenced by strong emotion; rational; objective
  • Synonym: detached - not involved emotionally; aloof or objective
  • Antonym: emotional - displaying strong feelings; not detached or clinical

Etymology

From Greek klinikē (practice at the sickbed), later influenced by the context of clinical observation in medicine.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01619612-s
Synset POS: s
