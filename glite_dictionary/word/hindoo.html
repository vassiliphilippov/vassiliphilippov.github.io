Hindoo

1. NOUN

/ hɪnˈdu / (AmE), / hɪnˈduː / (BrE)
an archaic term for a native or inhabitant of Hindustan or India, or a person of the Hindu faith
archaic term for an Indian or Hindu
archaic

Examples

In nineteenth-century literature, the term Hindoo is often used to refer to Indians.
He studied the customs and beliefs of the Hindoos during his travels.
Many old British texts describe Indian temples as Hindoo places of worship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindu - a follower of Hinduism or a person from India; modern spelling

Etymology

Older English spelling of 'Hindu', from Persian 'Hindū', relating to India.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.duː / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.duː / (BrE)
(archaic) a person who follows Hinduism; an older spelling of 'Hindu'.
archaic term for a follower of Hinduism
archaic

Examples

In nineteenth-century British literature, the word Hindoo was commonly used to describe followers of Hinduism.
The Hindoos celebrated their religious festival with music and vibrant colors.
He wrote extensively on the customs of the Hindoo people in his travel journals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindu - a person who follows Hinduism

Etymology

Dated variant of 'Hindu', from Hindi and Persian *Hindū*, referring to a person from the Indian subcontinent.
