Christianity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstʃiˈænəti / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstiˈænəti / (BrE)
The religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ, originating from the life and teachings presented in the New Testament, and recognizing Jesus as the savior.
the Christian religion

Examples

Many people in Europe and the Americas practice Christianity as their main religion.
The principles of Christianity are rooted in the teachings of Jesus Christ.
Art and music in the Middle Ages were deeply influenced by Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Judaism - the monotheistic religion of the Jewish people, emphasizing the Hebrew Scriptures and traditions
  • Antonym: Islam - the monotheistic religion based on the teachings of Muhammad as detailed in the Qur'an

Etymology

From Greek 'khristianos' (follower of Christ) via Latin 'Christianitas' (Christianity).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪs.tʃiˈæn.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌkrɪs.tiˈæn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the collective body of Christians worldwide, including the beliefs and institutions associated with the Christian faith
the global community of Christians

Examples

Over the centuries, Christianity has spread across many continents and cultures.
She studied the influence of Christianity on European art and history.
The church aims to unite all branches of Christianity in dialogue.

Synset Examples

for a thousand years the Roman Catholic Church was the principal church of Christendom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christendom - the worldwide community of Christianity or Christian peoples

Etymology

From Latin 'Christianitas', from 'Christianus' (Christian), from Greek 'Christianos'.
