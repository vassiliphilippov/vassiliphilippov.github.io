laugh

1. NOUN

/ læf / (AmE), / lɑːf / (BrE)
a humorous remark, story, or event that is intended to make people laugh; a joke or witty anecdote
humorous remark or joke
informal

Examples

He always comes up with a laugh when the mood turns serious.
That story about his trip was an absolute laugh.
Thanks for the laugh; I really needed that.

Synset Examples

he told a very funny joke
he knows a million gags
thanks for the laugh
he laughed unpleasantly at his own jest
even a schoolboy's jape is supposed to have some ascertainable point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to provoke laughter
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06790941-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ læf / (AmE), / lɑːf / (BrE)
the sound that someone makes when they are amused or find something funny
the sound of laughing

Examples

Her loud laugh echoed through the empty hall.
He has a contagious laugh that always makes others smile.
The comedian's joke drew a hearty laugh from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - a light, silly laugh
  • Antonym: cry - the act of shedding tears, often as the opposite emotional expression to laugh

Etymology

From Old English hliehhan, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch and German lachen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07141714-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ læf / (AmE), / lɑːf / (BrE)
to make sounds and movements of the face and body that express amusement or happiness
make sounds showing amusement

Examples

His jokes always make me laugh out loud.
Everyone laughed when the cat jumped onto the table.
She couldn't help laughing at the funny movie.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: giggle - to laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way
  • Degree Lower: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself
  • Antonym: cry - to shed tears, often as an antonym in the phrase 'laugh or cry'

Etymology

From Old English *hlæhhan*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *lachen* and German *lachen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00031813-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ læf / (AmE), / lɑːf / (BrE)
a facial expression showing amusement, pleasure, or derision, typically characterized by a smile and wrinkling of the face
a facial expression of amusement or pleasure

Examples

There was a mischievous laugh on her face as she revealed the surprise.
His laugh was unmistakable, lighting up his entire face even before a sound escaped.
The child's laugh reflected pure joy and innocence.

Synset Examples

his face wrinkled in a silent laugh of derision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smile - a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression

Etymology

From Old English hlæhhan, of Germanic origin.
