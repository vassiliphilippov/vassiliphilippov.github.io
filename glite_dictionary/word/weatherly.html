weatherly

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðərli / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəli / (BrE)
(of a sailing vessel) able to sail close to the wind with little sideways drift (leeway).
able to sail close to the wind

Examples

The yacht proved remarkably weatherly in strong winds, keeping her course with ease.
Compared to older ships, this model is much more weatherly.
Her weatherly qualities allowed her to outpace the competition around the upwind mark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leeward - away from the wind; the opposite direction to weatherly behavior

Etymology

From 'weather' (meaning windward direction at sea) + -ly.
