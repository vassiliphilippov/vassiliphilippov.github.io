Goodeniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɡuːˌdiːniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ɡuːˌdiːniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants native to Australasia and southeast China, known for their sap and distinctive flowers
family of flowering plants from Australasia and SE China
medical

Examples

Members of the Goodeniaceae family display a distinctive fan-shaped flower structure.
Many Goodeniaceae species are found in the arid regions of Australia.
Botanists have classified over 400 species within Goodeniaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Scottish botanist Samuel Goodenough (1743–1827) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant families).

2. NOUN

/ guːˌdiːniˈeɪsii / (AmE), / guːˌdiːniˈeɪsɪi / (BrE)
a family of mainly sappy plants native to Australasia and southeast China, including herbs and shrubs such as the goodenia
family of sappy plants in Australasia and China

Examples

The Goodeniaceae are easily recognized by their unique flower structure.
Many members of the Goodeniaceae family are popular in Australian gardens for their resilience.
Botanists studying plant diversity in China have described new species within Goodeniaceae.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Samuel Goodenough (1743–1827), with Latin family suffix -aceae.
