Sorghum bicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrɡəm baɪˈkoʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəm bʌɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
a species of sorghum cultivated widely for its grain, used as food for humans and animals, with leaves that have sawtooth edges and stems similar to corn
species of sorghum used for food

Examples

Sorghum bicolor is a staple crop in many parts of Africa and Asia.
Farmers planted fields of Sorghum bicolor to provide food for their livestock.
The drought-resistant nature of Sorghum bicolor makes it valuable in arid regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grasses cultivated for grain, especially in warm climates

Etymology

Modern Latin, from sorghum (from Italian sorgo) and bicolor meaning 'two-colored'.
