Crocodilus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈkɑːdələs / (AmE), / krəʊˈkɒdɪləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Crocodylidae, comprising the true crocodiles.
type genus of true crocodiles

Examples

The extinct genus Crocodilus has been reclassified in recent taxonomic systems.
Some ancient fossils initially identified as Crocodilus have since been reassigned to other genera.
19th-century zoologists frequently used the term Crocodilus in their scientific writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crocodylus - genus of true crocodiles, currently accepted spelling

Etymology

From Latin *crocodīlus*, from Ancient Greek *krokódīlos* (crocodile).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊ.kəˈdaɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˌkrɒk.əˈdaɪ.ləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Crocodylidae, including several species of true crocodiles.
type genus of crocodiles

Examples

The Crocodilus genus contains species found in Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
Many museum exhibits feature fossils from the ancient Crocodilus lineages.
Researchers have updated the classification of certain crocodiles based on their relation to Crocodilus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout and powerful jaws

Etymology

From Latin *crocodīlus*, meaning 'crocodile', itself from Ancient Greek *krokódilos*.
