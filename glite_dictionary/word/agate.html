agate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡət / (AmE), / ˈæɡət / (BrE)
A type of quartz characterized by its banded appearance, commonly used as a gemstone and in tools such as mortars and pestles.
banded gemstone, form of quartz

Examples

She wore a necklace set with a polished agate pendant.
The agate inlaid in the ring had beautiful bands of color.
Mortars made from agate are often used in laboratories for grinding substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalcedony - a cryptocrystalline form of silica, related mineral

Etymology

From Greek 'agathē', via Latin 'achates', the name of a river in Sicily where the stone was found.
