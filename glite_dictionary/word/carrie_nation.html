Carrie Nation

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæri ˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(1846–1911) An American activist and leader of the temperance movement, noted for attacking alcohol-serving establishments with a hatchet.
American temperance movement leader

Examples

Carrie Nation became famous for attacking saloons with a hatchet as part of her fight against alcohol.
Many historians credit Carrie Nation with bringing national attention to the temperance movement.
The museum dedicates an exhibit to Carrie Nation and her unusual methods of protest.
