impost

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪmpəʊst / (BrE)
the block or stone at the base of an arch, from which the curve of the arch rises
base block of an arch

Examples

The mason carefully placed the impost before beginning work on the arch itself.
Each side of the gateway featured a carved impost supporting the stone arch above.
Damage to the impost weakened the entire structure of the ancient bridge.

Etymology

From Italian *imposta*, from Latin *imponere* (to place upon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪmˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pəʊst / (BrE)
a tax or duty imposed on imported or exported goods; money collected under a tariff
tax or duty on imports/exports

Examples

The government increased the impost on luxury goods to generate more revenue.
Merchants protested against the new impost, claiming it would harm trade.
Colonial ports often collected an impost on all imported products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tariff - a tax or duty on imported or exported goods
  • Synonym: duty - a government tax, especially on imports

Etymology

From Latin 'impositum', past participle of 'imponere' (to impose).
