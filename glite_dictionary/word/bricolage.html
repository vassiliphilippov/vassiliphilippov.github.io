bricolage

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrikoʊˈlɑʒ / (AmE), / ˌbriːkɒˈlɑːʒ / (BrE)
the creation or construction of something using a diverse range of available materials or resources, especially in a makeshift or improvisational way
improvised construction with available materials

Examples

The artist's studio is filled with works of bricolage made from found objects.
Building the treehouse was a real exercise in bricolage, using leftover wood and spare parts.
Contemporary design often embraces bricolage to create unique and eclectic interiors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of creating something spontaneously or without preparation

Etymology

From French 'bricolage', from 'bricoler' (to tinker).
