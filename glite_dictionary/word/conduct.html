conduct

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dəkt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʌkt / (BrE)
the way in which a person behaves, especially in a particular situation or context
way of behaving

Examples

The principal praised the student's exemplary conduct during the ceremony.
All employees are expected to maintain proper conduct in the workplace.
Her conduct at the meeting reflected her professionalism and respect for others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavior - the way in which one acts or conducts oneself
  • Antonym: misconduct - unacceptable or improper behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'conductus', past participle of 'conducere' meaning 'to lead together'.
