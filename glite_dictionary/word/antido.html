Antido

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.ti.doʊ / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti.dəʊ / (BrE)
an artificial language created as a variant related to Ido, designed for international communication
constructed language related to Ido

Examples

A small group of linguists still studies Antido and its grammatical structure.
Unlike Esperanto, Antido has never gained a large number of speakers.
The creator of Antido hoped it would improve upon the perceived flaws of Ido.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ido - an artificial language based on Esperanto
  • Synonym: Esperanto - a widely known constructed international auxiliary language

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against, in opposition) and 'Ido' (name of the related constructed language).
