colza

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlzə / (AmE), / ˈkɒlzə / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant (Brassica napus) cultivated for its oil-rich seeds and sometimes used as a forage crop.
Eurasian oilseed plant

Examples

Farmers in Europe often grow colza for its valuable oil-rich seeds.
The field was filled with bright yellow colza blossoms in the spring.
Some livestock farmers use colza as a forage crop for their animals.

Etymology

From French 'colza', from Dutch 'koolzaad' (cabbage seed).
