President Eisenhower

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈaɪzənˌhaʊər / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈaɪzənhaʊə / (BrE)
the 34th President of the United States (1953–1961), formerly Supreme Allied Commander in Europe during World War II.
34th U.S. President, WWII general

Examples

President Eisenhower launched the interstate highway system during his administration.
Historians often credit President Eisenhower with maintaining peace during the early years of the Cold War.
Many Americans trusted President Eisenhower because of his leadership in World War II.
