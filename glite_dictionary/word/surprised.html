surprised

1. ADJ

/ sərˈpraɪzd / (AmE), / səˈpraɪzd / (BrE)
feeling or showing sudden wonder or astonishment as a result of something unexpected
feeling or showing sudden astonishment

Examples

She looked genuinely surprised when everyone shouted, 'Happy Birthday!'
I was surprised to find out that the museum was open so late.
His parents were surprised by how well he performed in the competition.

Synset Examples

surprised by her student's ingenuity
surprised that he remembered my name
a surprised expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - feeling extremely surprised or amazed
  • Antonym: unimpressed - not feeling admiration or surprise

Etymology

From past participle of 'surprise', from Old French 'surprendre' (to overtake, to surprise), from Latin 'super-' (over) + 'prehendere' (to seize).
