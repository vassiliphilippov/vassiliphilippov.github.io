indri

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.dri / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dri / (BrE)
a large, short-tailed lemur native to Madagascar, with thick, silky black, white, and fawn fur
large lemur of Madagascar

Examples

The indri is famous for its haunting calls that echo through Madagascar's forests.
Tourists visiting Madagascar often hope to catch a glimpse of the rare indri.
Unlike most lemurs, the indri has only a short, barely visible tail.

Etymology

From Malagasy 'indry' (look!), imitative of its call.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.dri / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dri / (BrE)
a large lemur native to Madagascar, with a short tail and thick black, white, and fawn fur
large lemur of Madagascar

Examples

The indri is known for its loud, haunting calls that can be heard for miles in the rainforest.
Unlike other lemurs, the indri has only a tiny stub of a tail.
Researchers are working to protect the habitat of the endangered indri in Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a nocturnal primate native to Madagascar

Etymology

From Malagasy 'indri', said to be an exclamation meaning 'look there!' mistaken for the animal's name.
