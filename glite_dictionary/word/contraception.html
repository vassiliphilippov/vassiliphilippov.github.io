contraception

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the deliberate use of methods or devices to prevent pregnancy resulting from sexual intercourse
prevention of pregnancy
medical

Examples

Many couples rely on contraception to plan their families according to their needs and preferences.
Access to safe and effective contraception is an important public health issue.
Different forms of contraception include pills, intrauterine devices, and condoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth control - the practice or technique of preventing pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin 'contra-' (against) + 'conceptionem' (conception)
