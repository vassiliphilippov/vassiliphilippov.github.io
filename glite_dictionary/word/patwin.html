Patwin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæt.wɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæt.wɪn / (BrE)
A Native American language of the Copehan family, traditionally spoken by the Patwin people of northern California.
a Native American language of northern California

Examples

The Patwin language is critically endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining.
Linguists are working with the community to document the Patwin language before it disappears.
He studied the grammar and vocabulary of Patwin for his thesis on indigenous languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wintun - a group of Native American peoples and languages in California, related to Patwin

Etymology

From the self-designation of the Patwin people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpætwiːn / (AmE), / ˈpætwiːn / (BrE)
A member of an Indigenous people native to the Sacramento Valley in California.
member of a California Indigenous people

Examples

The Patwin traditionally inhabited areas along the Sacramento River.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient Patwin village sites in Northern California.
Representatives of the Patwin community attended the local tribal council meeting.
