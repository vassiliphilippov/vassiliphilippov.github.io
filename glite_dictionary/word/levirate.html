levirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛv.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈliː.vɪˌreɪt / (BrE)
a custom or law requiring a man to marry the widow of his deceased, childless brother to preserve the brother's family line
custom of marrying a deceased brother's widow

Examples

The practice of levirate was common in certain ancient Middle Eastern societies.
Scholars have debated the significance of levirate obligations in biblical law.
Anthropologists have found variants of levirate in many traditional cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levirate marriage - the custom or institution of levirate
  • Antonym: sororate - custom where a widower marries his deceased wife's sister

Etymology

From Latin levir (husband's brother); used in biblical and anthropological contexts.
