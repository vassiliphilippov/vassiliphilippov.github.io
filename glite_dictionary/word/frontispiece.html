frontispiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.tɪˌspiːs / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪ.spiːs / (BrE)
an ornamental or decorated facade, especially the principal front of a building
ornamental facade of a building

Examples

The palace's frontispiece was adorned with intricate carvings and statues.
Renovations restored the historic frontispiece of the old town hall to its former glory.
An impressive frontispiece greeted visitors as they approached the grand estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - the front of a building, especially an imposing or decorative one

Etymology

From French 'frontispice', from Latin 'frontispicium', meaning the front view or facade.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.tɪˌspis / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪˌspiːs / (BrE)
an illustration or decorative design appearing on the page facing the title page of a book
illustration facing a book's title page

Examples

The novel's frontispiece showed a detailed portrait of the author.
An antique copy of the book included an ornate frontispiece depicting a scene from the story.
He admired the frontispiece before turning to the title page to start reading.

Etymology

From French 'frontispice', from Late Latin 'frontispicium' (front view, facade).
