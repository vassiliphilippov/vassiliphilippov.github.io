pre-empt

1. NOUN

/ priˈɛmpt / (AmE), / priˈɛmpt / (BrE)
(in card games, especially bridge) a high opening bid intended to prevent opponents from making their own higher bids.
high bid to block opponents (bridge)

Examples

She made a daring pre-empt to disrupt her opponents' bidding strategy.
A well-timed pre-empt can put the other team under a lot of pressure.
His aggressive pre-empt left the opponents with few good options.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overcall - a bid made after the opening bid by the opponents (bridge)

Etymology

From pre- +‎ empt, derived from Latin praeemere ‘to buy before’ in reference to acting in advance.
