infantile

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.fən.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fəntaɪl / (BrE)
(of behavior or attitudes) characteristic of or suitable for a young child; very immature or childish.
immature or childish; like an infant

Examples

His infantile behavior made it difficult for others to take him seriously at work.
The debate devolved into infantile name-calling and pointless arguments.
She refused to engage in infantile gossip about her colleagues.

Synset Examples

childish tantrums
infantile behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish - (of behavior) typical of a child; immature
  • Antonym: mature - behaving in a sensible, adult way

Etymology

From Latin infantilis (of an infant), from infans (infant, unable to speak).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.fən.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fən.taɪl / (BrE)
Relating to or typical of an infant; childishly immature or naive.
relating to infants; childishly immature

Examples

He was criticized for his infantile behavior during the meeting.
The argument descended into infantile name-calling and pouting.
These cartoons are too infantile for most teenagers to enjoy.

Synset Examples

infantile games

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish - silly or immature like a child
  • Antonym: mature - fully developed or behaving in an adult way

Etymology

From Latin 'infantilis', from 'infans' (infant).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.fən.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fən.taɪl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of infants or infancy; typical of a baby or young child.
relating to infants or infancy

Examples

Her artwork showed an infantile style, with bright colors and simple shapes.
He suffered from infantile amnesia and could not remember anything from early childhood.
The doctor diagnosed the child with infantile paralysis, which affected his muscle control.

Synset Examples

infantile paralysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish - typical of a child; immature
  • Antonym: mature - fully developed physically; showing adult qualities

Etymology

From Latin 'infantilis', from 'infans' (infant, unable to speak).
