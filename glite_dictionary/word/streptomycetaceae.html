Streptomycetaceae

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛpˌtoʊmaɪsiˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / stɹɛpˌtəʊmaɪsiˈtiːsiˌiː / (BrE)
A family of typically aerobic, soil-dwelling bacteria, many of which are important as sources of antibiotics.
family of antibiotic-producing soil bacteria
medical

Examples

Members of the Streptomycetaceae family are well known for producing natural antibiotics.
Researchers isolated new compounds from a strain belonging to the Streptomycetaceae.
The soil sample showed a high diversity of Streptomycetaceae under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Streptomyces - genus of soil bacteria producing many antibiotics
  • Degree Higher: Actinomycetaceae - another family of actinomycete bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Streptomyces' (the type genus), itself from Greek 'streptos' (twisted) + 'mykēs' (fungus), with the family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ strɛpˌtoʊmaɪˌsiːtəˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / strɛptəʊˌmaɪsiːtəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of higher bacteria, typically aerobic soil saprophytes, known for their role in decomposing organic matter and producing antibiotics
a family of soil-dwelling bacteria
medical

Examples

The Streptomycetaceae are important for their ability to produce natural antibiotics.
Researchers found new species belonging to the Streptomycetaceae family in the soil samples.
Many members of Streptomycetaceae help decompose complex organic materials in the environment.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Streptomyces and the family suffix -aceae.
