abbreviated

1. ADJ

/ əˈbri.vi.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əˈbriː.vi.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of clothing) very short or cut so as to cover only a small part of the body.
very short (of clothing)

Examples

Her abbreviated skirt drew attention from everyone at the party.
The athlete wore an abbreviated tank top during the summer match.
Some fashion designers favor abbreviated styles that reveal more skin.

Synset Examples

an abbreviated swimsuit
a brief bikini

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short - small in length or duration

Etymology

From Latin abbreviatus, past participle of abbreviare (to shorten), via Old French.

2. ADJ

/ əˈbriː.vi.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əˈbriː.vi.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
made shorter in length or duration than usual or expected; cut short.
shortened in duration or length

Examples

Because of time constraints, the ceremony was abbreviated to just thirty minutes.
His vacation was abbreviated when he received an urgent call from work.
We attended an abbreviated meeting that covered only the most important topics.

Synset Examples

the abbreviated speech
her shortened life was clearly the result of smoking
an unsatisfactory truncated conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortened - made or became shorter
  • Synonym: truncated - shortened by cutting off

Etymology

From Latin abbreviatus, past participle of abbreviare (to shorten).

WordNet ID: oewn-01445488-s
