pyrogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪrəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or process) producing or resulting from fever.
fever-producing or caused by fever
medical

Examples

Some bacteria release pyrogenic toxins that can cause a high fever in patients.
The doctor suspected a pyrogenic reaction due to the sudden rise in body temperature.
Researchers are developing tests to detect pyrogenic substances in medical products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: febrile - relating to or characterized by fever

Etymology

From Greek pyros (fire) + -genic (producing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪrəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
Produced or formed by or under conditions of intense heat, especially relating to geological processes.
produced by intense heat

Examples

The area is known for pyrogenic rocks created deep within the earth's crust.
Scientists studied the pyrogenic deposits left after the ancient volcanic eruptions.
Some minerals only form in pyrogenic environments of extreme heat and pressure.

Synset Examples

igneous rock is rock formed by solidification from a molten state; especially from molten magma
igneous fusion is fusion by heat alone
pyrogenic strata

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igneous - (of rock) formed by the solidification of molten magma

Etymology

From Greek pyr (fire) + -genic (producing, formed by).
