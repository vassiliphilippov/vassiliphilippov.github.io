to galvanize

1. VERB

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.naɪz / (BrE)
to coat (metal, especially iron or steel) with a layer of zinc to protect it from rusting
coat metal with zinc

Examples

The company decided to galvanize the steel beams to prevent corrosion.
Many water pipes are galvanized to increase their lifespan and durability.
Engineers recommend galvanizing iron fences for better resistance to weather damage.

Synset Examples

galvanize steel

Etymology

From French *galvaniser*, named after Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.naɪz / (BrE)
To stimulate muscles by applying an electric shock, typically as part of a medical or physiological procedure.
stimulate muscles with electric shock
medical

Examples

The therapist used electrodes to galvanize the patient's leg muscles during the rehabilitation session.
Early physiologists would galvanize frog legs to observe muscular contractions in response to electric currents.
In rare cases, doctors may galvanize certain nerves to restore movement after injury.

Etymology

From the name of Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist, who studied electrical currents and muscle contractions in the 18th century.
