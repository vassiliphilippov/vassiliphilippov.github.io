pomposity

1. NOUN

/ pɑmˈpɑsɪti / (AmE), / pɒmˈpɒsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being pompous; self-importance or exaggerated dignity in manner, style, or language
self-importance; pompous behavior

Examples

Her speech was filled with pomposity, making it hard for the audience to relate.
The professor's pomposity alienated many of his students.
Despite his achievements, he managed to avoid any trace of pomposity in his manner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomposity - quality of being self-important
  • Antonym: humility - modest or low view of one's own importance; antidote to pomposity

Etymology

From Medieval Latin pompositas, from Latin pomposus (pompous).
