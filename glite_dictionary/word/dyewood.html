dyewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.wʊd / (BrE)
any wood from which a dye can be extracted, especially for coloring fabrics or other materials.
wood used for making dye

Examples

The craftsman imported dyewood from the tropics to create vibrant cloth colors.
Historically, dyewood played a vital role in the textile industry before synthetic dyes were invented.
Different types of dyewood can produce a wide range of hues, from reds to purples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - a substance used to change the color of something
  • Synonym: logwood - a type of dyewood used to make red or purple dye

Etymology

From 'dye' + 'wood', indicating wood used for dye-making.
