imbauba

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.bɑːˈuː.bə / (AmE), / ˌɪm.bɑːˈuː.bə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree with large shield-shaped leaves and hollow stems, often found in rainforests.
tropical tree with large leaves and hollow stems

Examples

The imbauba provides shelter for many rainforest animals with its hollow stems.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of young imbaubas in the Amazon region.
Several birds nested in the thick branches of the imbauba.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'embaúba', used in Brazil for various species of the genus Cecropia.
