tach

1. NOUN

/ tætʃ / (AmE), / tætʃ / (BrE)
an instrument that measures and displays the speed of rotation of a shaft or engine, especially in vehicles
instrument for measuring rotational speed

Examples

The mechanic checked the tach to ensure the engine wasn't over-revving.
Many race cars have a digital tach mounted prominently on the dashboard.
My motorcycle's tach showed the RPMs spiking as I accelerated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tachometer - instrument for measuring rotational speed

Etymology

Shortened form of 'tachometer', from Greek 'tachos' (speed) and 'metron' (measure).

2. NOUN

/ tætʃ / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
an informal term for a tachometer, an instrument that measures the rotation speed of an engine
informal term for tachometer
informal

Examples

He glanced at the tach to see if he was pushing the engine too hard.
The tach isn't working, so I have to guess my RPMs.
Always watch the tach when you're accelerating quickly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
