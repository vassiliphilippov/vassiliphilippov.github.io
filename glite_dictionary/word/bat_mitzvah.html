bat mitzvah

1. NOUN

/ bɑt ˈmɪts.və / (AmE), / bæt ˈmɪts.və / (BrE)
In Judaism, a ceremony marking the 12th birthday of a Jewish girl, signifying her religious maturity and responsibilities.
Jewish coming-of-age ceremony for girls

Examples

Her family hosted a large celebration for her bat mitzvah.
After her bat mitzvah, she began taking on more responsibilities in the synagogue.
The rabbi congratulated Emma on her successful bat mitzvah ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar mitzvah - a Jewish boy's coming-of-age ceremony at 13

Etymology

From Hebrew bat (daughter) + mitzvah (commandment).

2. VERB

/ bɑt ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / bæt ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
to confirm a girl in the Jewish faith through the bat mitzvah ceremony, marking her coming of age at 12 or 13
to confirm a girl in Judaism at coming of age

Examples

Her daughter will bat mitzvah next spring at their synagogue.
Many families choose to bat mitzvah their daughters as part of a large celebration.
He helped bat mitzvah his niece by reading a blessing during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar mitzvah - to confirm a boy in the Jewish faith through the bar mitzvah ceremony

Etymology

From Hebrew 'bat' meaning 'daughter' and 'mitzvah' meaning 'commandment'.

3. NOUN

/ bɑt ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / bæt ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a ceremony held to celebrate a Jewish girl's 12th birthday, marking her coming of age and religious responsibilities.
Jewish coming-of-age ceremony for girls

Examples

Sarah celebrated her bat mitzvah with a ceremony at the synagogue.
The family invited friends and relatives to attend their daughter's bat mitzvah.
Preparing for her bat mitzvah, Leah spent months learning her Torah portion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar mitzvah - Jewish coming-of-age ceremony for boys

Etymology

From Hebrew bat (daughter) + mitzvah (commandment). Modeled after bar mitzvah for boys.
