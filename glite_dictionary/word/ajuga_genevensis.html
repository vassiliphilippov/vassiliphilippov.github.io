Ajuga genevensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒuːɡə dʒəˈniːvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːɡə dʒəˈniːvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb with upright stems and bright blue flowers, native to southern Europe.
herbaceous plant with blue flowers

Examples

Ajuga genevensis is often planted in gardens for its dense, bright blue flower spikes.
The upright stems of Ajuga genevensis make it stand out among other ground covers.
Botanists identified several specimens of Ajuga genevensis growing wild in southern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Ajuga' is the genus name, 'genevensis' means 'of Geneva'.
