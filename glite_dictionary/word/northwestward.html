northwestward

1. ADV

/ ˌnɔrθˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
Toward the northwest; in a direction toward the northwest.
toward the northwest (direction)

Examples

The storm moved northwestward across the state, bringing heavy rain.
Migratory birds travel northwestward during certain seasons.
The explorers continued northwestward in search of a new route.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southeastward - in a southeastward direction

Etymology

Formed from 'northwest' + '-ward' (denoting direction).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrθˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
Moving or directed toward the northwest.
toward the northwest

Examples

The hikers set a northwestward course toward the mountains.
A northwestward wind brought cooler air to the region.
She glanced northwestward, searching for the distant lighthouse.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: northward - toward the north
  • Synonym: northwesterly - in a northwest direction

Etymology

Formed from 'northwest' + '-ward', an Old English suffix indicating direction.

3. NOUN

/ ˌnɔrθˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
The direction or compass point situated midway between north and west, corresponding to 315 degrees.
direction between north and west

Examples

The explorers set their course toward the northwestward to reach the base camp.
On the compass, 315 degrees points to the northwestward.
A strong wind was blowing from the northwestward all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northwest - the direction between north and west
  • Antonym: southeastward - the direction between south and east

Etymology

Formed from 'northwest' + '-ward', indicating direction.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
