stylite

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪ.laɪt / (AmE), / ˈstaɪ.laɪt / (BrE)
an early Christian ascetic who lived on top of a pillar, often as a form of extreme devotion
early Christian ascetic living on a pillar

Examples

The most famous stylite was St. Simeon, who reportedly spent decades on his pillar.
Pilgrims traveled great distances to seek the blessing of a renowned stylite in Syria.
Historical accounts describe how food and water were brought up to the stylite by his disciples.

Etymology

From Greek stylitēs (pillar-dweller), from stylos (pillar).
