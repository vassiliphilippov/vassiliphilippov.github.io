pencil

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsl̩ / (BrE)
a cosmetic in the form of a long, thin stick, used for applying color or definition to a specific part of the face, such as the eyebrows or eyelids
cosmetic stick for face application

Examples

She bought a new eyebrow pencil to match her hair color.
The makeup artist sharpened the pencil before applying the eyeliner.
You can use a lip pencil to outline your lips for a fuller look.

Synset Examples

an eyebrow pencil

Etymology

From Old French 'pincel' (brush), from Latin 'penicillus' (little tail, brush).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl / (BrE)
a set of straight lines or light rays that converge or diverge from a single point, especially in geometry or optics
set of lines/rays meeting at a point

Examples

In geometry, a pencil of lines can be constructed through a given point.
The light formed a pencil of rays as it passed through the small opening.
A pencil of intersecting planes defines the axis in this mathematical model.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl / (BrE)
a writing or drawing medium made of graphite or a similar substance, typically used for marking paper or other surfaces
graphite-based writing or drawing medium

Examples

She prefers to sketch in pencil rather than in ink.
The notes were written in faint pencil on the back of the envelope.
Oil, watercolor, and pencil are her favorite artistic mediums.

Synset Examples

the words were scribbled in pencil
this artist's favorite medium is pencil

Etymology

From Latin 'penicillus' (little tail, brush), later referring to a writing instrument.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.səl / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.səl / (BrE)
a thin, usually cylindrical writing instrument made of a rod of graphite or a similar substance encased in wood or another material, used for writing or drawing
writing instrument with a graphite core

Examples

She sketched the portrait with a sharp pencil on plain white paper.
He forgot to bring a pencil to the exam and had to borrow one from a friend.
The artist preferred using a pencil for his initial drawings before adding color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - writing instrument using ink

Etymology

From Old French 'pincel' (brush), from Latin 'penicillus' (little tail, brush).

5. VERB

/ ˈpɛnsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsəl / (BrE)
to write, draw, or trace using a pencil
write or draw with a pencil

Examples

She carefully penciled her signature at the bottom of the drawing.
The designer penciled out a quick sketch of the new building.
He likes to pencil his ideas in a notebook before committing to ink.

Synset Examples

he penciled a figure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draw - to produce a picture by making lines or marks, especially with a pen or pencil

Etymology

From Old French *pincel* (small paintbrush), from Latin *penicillus* (little tail, brush).
