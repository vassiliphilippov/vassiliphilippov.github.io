well-disposed

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛl dɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl dɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
Having a positive, friendly, or supportive attitude toward someone or something.
friendly or supportive in attitude

Examples

His new colleagues were well-disposed towards him and eager to help.
The review committee was well-disposed to funding the innovative project.
She felt well-disposed to her neighbor after their pleasant conversation.

Synset Examples

a government friendly to our interests
well disposed to the good order and happiness of the United States
a relaxed environment well-disposed to the appreciation of good food and fine wine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hostile - having or showing strong dislike or opposition
  • Antonym: unfriendly - not friendly or kind in attitude or behavior
  • Synonym: kindly - having a kind or benevolent attitude

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (meaning 'in a good manner') and 'disposed' (from Latin 'disponere', to arrange).
