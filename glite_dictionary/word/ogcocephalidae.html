Ogcocephalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːgkoʊˌsɛfəˈlaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌɒgkəʊˌsiːfəˈliːdiː / (BrE)
A family of sluggish, bottom-dwelling spiny fishes known as batfishes, found in tropical and subtropical oceans.
family of bottom-dwelling spiny batfishes

Examples

The Ogcocephalidae are often called batfishes due to their unique body shape.
Marine biologists are researching the feeding habits of Ogcocephalidae in Caribbean waters.
Unlike most fish, members of the Ogcocephalidae family walk along the sea floor on their fins.

Etymology

From Greek *ogkos* (mass, bulk) + *kephalē* (head), referring to the distinctive head shape of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːɡkosoʊˈfæləˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɒɡkəʊsɪˈfæləˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of sluggish, bottom-dwelling, spiny fishes commonly known as batfishes, found in marine environments.
family of spiny batfishes
medical

Examples

The Ogcocephalidae are easily recognized by their flattened bodies and broad, spiny heads.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Ogcocephalidae during their ocean floor expedition.
Some members of Ogcocephalidae have developed unique adaptations for walking along the seabed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batfish - common name for various fishes including members of Ogcocephalidae

Etymology

From Greek 'ogkos' (bulk, mass) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to the distinctive head shape in these fishes.
