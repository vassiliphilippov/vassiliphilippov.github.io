Id al-Adha

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːd ælˈɑːdə, ˌɪd ælˈɑːdə / (AmE), / ˌiːd ælˈɑːdə, ˌɪd ælˈɑːdə / (BrE)
A major Islamic festival marking the end of the Hajj, celebrated by ritual animal sacrifice and charity, commemorating Abraham's willingness to sacrifice his son.
Islamic festival of sacrifice

Examples

Every year, millions of Muslims around the world celebrate Id al-Adha with prayers and charitable giving.
During Id al-Adha, families often share meals and distribute meat to those in need.
The story of Abraham's devotion is central to the meaning of Id al-Adha.

Etymology

From Arabic 'ʿīd al-ʾaḍḥā' meaning 'Festival of the Sacrifice'.
