jackstraws

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌstrɔz / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌstrɔːz / (BrE)
A game in which players try to remove individual sticks from a pile without disturbing the others.
traditional pick-up sticks game

Examples

The children played jackstraws quietly in the corner on a rainy afternoon.
My grandfather taught me how to play jackstraws when I was young.
She bought a vintage set of jackstraws at the antique market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pick-up sticks - a game where players remove sticks from a pile without moving others

Etymology

From 'jackstraw', referring to the individual sticks used in the game; 'jack' as a generic term for a small object and 'straw' for the shape.
