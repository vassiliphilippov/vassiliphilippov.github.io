zone

1. NOUN

/ zoʊn / (AmE), / zəʊn / (BrE)
an area or region that is different from others because of a particular feature or characteristic
distinctive area or region

Examples

The city established a pedestrian-only zone in the downtown area.
This parking zone is reserved for residents only.
They traveled through a war zone on their way to safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - a large area of land, usually without exact limits
  • Synonym: area - a part of a place or surface

Etymology

From Greek *zōnē* (belt, girdle), via Latin *zona*.

2. NOUN

/ zoʊn / (AmE), / zəʊn / (BrE)
any of the broad regions of the Earth's surface, typically defined by latitude or longitude, such as the tropical, temperate, or polar zones.
a region of Earth's surface by latitude or longitude

Examples

The Earth is divided into several climatic zones, such as the tropical and polar zones.
Many animals are adapted to survive only in a particular zone of the planet.
The temperate zone experiences moderate weather compared to the extremes of the tropics or poles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - an area or division, especially part of a country or the world

Etymology

From Greek zōnē (belt, girdle) via Latin zona.

6. NOUN

/ zoʊn / (AmE), / zəʊn / (BrE)
(Anatomy) An encircling or beltlike structure in an organ or tissue.
beltlike anatomical structure
medical

Examples

The transition zone between the cortex and the medulla is clearly visible under the microscope.
The prostate gland contains a peripheral zone and a central zone with different tissue characteristics.
Damage to the paracentral zone of the retina may affect peripheral vision.
