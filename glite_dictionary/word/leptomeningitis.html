leptomeningitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛp.toʊˌmɛn.ɪnˈdʒaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təʊˌmɛn.ɪnˈdʒaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the leptomeninges, the two thin layers of tissue covering the brain and spinal cord.
inflammation of the leptomeninges
medical

Examples

The autopsy revealed that the patient had died from acute leptomeningitis.
Symptoms of leptomeningitis include severe headache, fever, and neck stiffness.
Treatment for leptomeningitis typically involves antibiotics and supportive care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meningitis - inflammation of the meninges covering the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (thin) and 'meninx' (membrane), with '-itis' indicating inflammation.
