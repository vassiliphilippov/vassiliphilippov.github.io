to backslap

1. VERB

/ ˈbækˌslæp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌslæp / (BrE)
to behave in an overly friendly or cheerful way, often by enthusiastically patting someone on the back
behave in an overly friendly, hearty manner

Examples

He is always backslapping his colleagues at staff meetings, much to their embarrassment.
At the party, the politician kept backslapping everyone he met to appear more approachable.
Some employees found his constant backslapping insincere and a bit overwhelming.

Synset Examples

he is always backslapping his colleagues at staff meetings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schmooze - to chat in a lively, friendly way, often for personal gain
  • Synonym: fawn - to show excessive affection or attention to win favor
  • Antonym: snub - to ignore or treat with disdain

Etymology

From the practice of literally slapping someone's back in a friendly gesture; formed from 'back' + 'slap'.
