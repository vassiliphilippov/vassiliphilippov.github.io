to ionise

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
(of an atom or molecule) to become converted into ions, typically by losing or gaining an electron.
be converted into ions
british

Examples

Under high energy, the gas will ionise and conduct electricity.
Radiation can cause atoms in the body to ionise and form free radicals.
In some cases, molecules ionise more easily in water than in other solvents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ionize - (of an atom or molecule) to become converted into ions (American spelling)

Etymology

From ion + -ise, after 'ion'.

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to convert (an atom or molecule) into an ion by adding or removing charged particles such as electrons
convert into ions
british

Examples

Chemists often ionise substances in the lab to study their properties.
Ultraviolet radiation can ionise the gas in the upper atmosphere.
The high energy from the laser was sufficient to ionise the sample completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ionize - to convert into ions (American spelling)

Etymology

From ion + -ise, after ionize, modelled on 'ion'.
