uniparous

1. ADJ

/ juːˈnɪpərəs / (AmE), / juːˈnɪpərəs / (BrE)
(of an organism or species) producing only one offspring at a time or during a single reproductive event.
producing one offspring at a time
medical

Examples

The cheetah is a uniparous animal, usually giving birth to just one cub per pregnancy.
Some flowering plants are uniparous, producing a single seed at a time.
In contrast to uniparous species, rabbits are known for being highly multiparous.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multiparous - producing more than one offspring at a time
  • Synonym: primiparous - having borne offspring for the first time

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'parere' (to bring forth, to bear).
