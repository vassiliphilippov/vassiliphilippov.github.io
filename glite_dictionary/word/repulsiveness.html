repulsiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌlsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant or disgusting to the senses or emotions
quality of being extremely unpleasant

Examples

The repulsiveness of the rotten food made everyone leave the room immediately.
She was disturbed by the repulsiveness of the crime scene described in the novel.
He tried to hide the repulsiveness of his injuries with a bandage.

Synset Examples

the vileness of his language surprised us

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attractiveness - the quality of being pleasing or appealing
  • Synonym: vileness - the quality of being morally despicable or abhorrent

Etymology

From 'repulsive' + '-ness'; 'repulsive' from Latin repulsus (driven back, repelled).
