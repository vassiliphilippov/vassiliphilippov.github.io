discontinuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.kənˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kɒnˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of stopping or interrupting something, either temporarily or permanently
the act of stopping or interrupting

Examples

After the discontinuation of the product, customers were left searching for alternatives.
The doctor warned that abrupt discontinuation of the medication could cause withdrawal symptoms.
There was a brief discontinuation in power supply during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - a break in continuity
  • Synonym: cessation - the process of ending or being brought to an end
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of continuing something

Etymology

From Latin 'discontinuatio', from 'discontinuare' (to discontinue).
