Plavix

1. NOUN

/ ˈplævɪks / (AmE), / ˈplævɪks / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used to prevent blood clots by stopping platelets from sticking together, commonly prescribed after mild heart attacks.
blood-thinning medication (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Plavix to help prevent another heart attack.
Patients taking Plavix should inform their dentist before undergoing surgery.
My grandfather has been on Plavix since his heart procedure last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clopidogrel - the generic name for the active ingredient in Plavix
