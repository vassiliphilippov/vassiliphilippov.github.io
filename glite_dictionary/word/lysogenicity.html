lysogenicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪsədʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsəʤəˈnɪsɪti / (BrE)
The state or condition in which a bacterium has incorporated a bacteriophage's genetic material into its own genome, allowing the phage to remain dormant within the host.
incorporation of a phage into bacterial DNA
medical

Examples

The lysogenicity of a bacterium can be determined by testing for the presence of integrated viral DNA.
Certain environmental factors can trigger a switch from lysogenicity to a lytic cycle in bacterial cells.
Understanding lysogenicity is crucial for researchers studying bacteriophage-host interactions.

Synset Examples

when a phage infects a bacterium it can either destroy its host or be incorporated in the host genome in a state of lysogeny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lysogeny - the condition in which a bacteriophage genome is incorporated into a bacterial genome
  • Antonym: lytic - referring to the process by which a virus destroys its host cell

Etymology

From 'lysogenic' (lyso- + -genic, relating to lysis and generation) + -ity.
