Slask

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃlɑːsk / (AmE), / ˈʃlɑːsk / (BrE)
a historical region in central Europe, rich in coal and iron ore deposits, now mainly located in southwestern Poland
region in central Europe, mainly in Poland

Examples

The rich mineral resources of Slask have long played a crucial role in the region's industry.
Many families in Slask have roots stretching back several centuries.
After the border changes, much of Slask became part of Poland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Silesia - a historical region of central Europe, primarily in Poland

Etymology

From Polish 'Śląsk', referring to the region known in English as Silesia.
