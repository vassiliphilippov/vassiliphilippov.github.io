Hylophylax naevioides

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌlɒfɪˌlæks ˌniːviˈoʊɪdiːz / (AmE), / hɪˌlɒfɪˌlæks ˌniːviˈəʊɪdiːz / (BrE)
a species of antbird native to Central America and parts of northwestern South America, known as the spotted antbird
a species of antbird (spotted antbird)

Examples

The Hylophylax naevioides is commonly found in humid forests of Central America.
Researchers observed the Hylophylax naevioides following columns of army ants for food.
The plumage of the Hylophylax naevioides helps it blend into the forest understory.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name Hylophylax and species epithet naevioides, meaning 'spotted' or 'marked'.
