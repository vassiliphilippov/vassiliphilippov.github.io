Menticirrhus littoralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.tɪˈsɪr.əs ˌlɪˈtɔːr.əl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.tɪˈsɪr.əs ˌlɪˈtɒr.æl.ɪs / (BrE)
a dull silvery whiting fish native to the southern Atlantic and Gulf Coasts of the United States
a whiting fish of U.S. southern coasts

Examples

The Menticirrhus littoralis is commonly caught by anglers along the Gulf Coast.
Scientists have studied the diet of Menticirrhus littoralis to understand its ecological role.
Local fishermen often refer to the Menticirrhus littoralis as the Gulf kingfish.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Menticirrhus' (from 'mentum', chin + 'cirrus', curl/filament) and species 'littoralis' (of the shore).
