hominoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.mə.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒm.ɪ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a primate of the superfamily Hominoidea, including all modern apes and humans, as well as their extinct ancestors and relatives
ape or human in the Hominoidea superfamily

Examples

Fossil evidence suggests that the earliest hominoid ancestors lived over twenty million years ago.
Chimpanzees and gorillas are both classified as hominoids.
The discovery of a new species of hominoid has provided insight into human evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ape - a primate of the family Hominidae or Hylobatidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Hominoidea, from Latin homo (man, human being) + -oid (form, likeness).
