valet

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælɪt / (AmE), / ˈvæleɪ; ˈvælɪt / (BrE)
a male servant who acts as a personal attendant to his employer, performing tasks such as dressing, grooming, and looking after clothes
manservant; personal male attendant

Examples

The wealthy gentleman always relied on his valet to help him dress for dinner.
In many classic novels, the valet is a discreet but essential household member.
His father once worked as a valet in a grand London hotel.

Synset Examples

Jeeves was Bertie Wooster's man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manservant - a male servant

Etymology

From French *valet* (servant, attendant).

2. VERB

/ ˈvæl.eɪt, vəˈleɪt / (AmE), / ˈvæl.eɪ, ˈvæl.ɪt / (BrE)
to serve as a personal attendant to someone, especially by performing personal services such as handling clothes or helping with grooming
act as personal attendant

Examples

He used to valet for a wealthy businessman in the city.
During the summer, she valeted for several clients at the country club.
Some hotels offer services where staff will valet for special guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attend - to serve or wait upon someone as an attendant

Etymology

From French valet (servant), ultimately from Old French 'vaslet' (squire, young man).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
