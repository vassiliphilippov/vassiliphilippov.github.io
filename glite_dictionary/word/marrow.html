marrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
the soft, fatty tissue found in the cavities of bones, responsible for producing blood cells
soft fatty tissue inside bones
medical

Examples

Doctors extracted a sample of marrow from his hip bone for testing.
Certain diseases, like leukemia, affect the marrow and its ability to produce blood cells.
The transplant involved replacing damaged marrow with healthy tissue from a donor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone marrow - the soft tissue inside bones that produces blood cells

Etymology

From Old English *mærg*, related to German *Mark* and Old Norse *mergr*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
the soft, fatty tissue found inside bones, especially large ones, which is considered very nutritious and can be eaten as food
soft, edible tissue inside bones

Examples

The chef served roasted bone with a generous portion of marrow spread on toasted bread.
Many cultures consider marrow a delicacy because of its rich flavor and high nutritional value.
She carefully scooped the marrow out of the bone to add it to her soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone marrow - the soft tissue inside bones, especially in animals and humans, which produces blood cells

Etymology

From Old English 'mearg', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'merg' and German 'Mark'.
