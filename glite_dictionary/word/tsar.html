tsar

1. NOUN

/ zɑr / (AmE), / zɑː / (BrE)
a male monarch or emperor, especially one of Russia before 1917
Russian male monarch or emperor

Examples

The last Russian tsar, Nicholas II, abdicated the throne in 1917.
Many Russian peasants viewed the tsar as a father figure, despite their hardships.
Historians still debate the influence of the tsar on early twentieth-century politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: czar - alternative spelling with the same meaning
  • Synonym: emperor - male sovereign ruler of an empire
  • Degree Lower: king - male monarch of a kingdom
  • Antonym: queen - female monarch (comparable title)

Etymology

From Russian 'tsar', via Latin 'Caesar' (title of Roman emperors).
