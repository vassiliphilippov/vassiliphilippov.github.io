Calamintha grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈmɪn.θə ˌɡræn.dɪˈflɔːr.ə / (AmE), / ˌkal.əˈmɪn.θə ˌɡran.dɪˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
A species of aromatic herb with large pink flowers, native to southern and southeastern Europe, Anatolia, and northern Iran.
a species of aromatic herb with pink flowers

Examples

Calamintha grandiflora is prized in gardens for its attractive pink blossoms and aromatic leaves.
Botanists observed that Calamintha grandiflora thrives particularly well in limestone-rich soils.
Herbalists sometimes use Calamintha grandiflora for its mild, minty fragrance in traditional remedies.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus Calamintha with Latin 'grandiflora' meaning 'large-flowered'.
