congregant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnɡrɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrɪɡənt / (BrE)
A member of a congregation, especially in a church or synagogue.
member of a religious congregation

Examples

Each congregant was invited to light a candle during the ceremony.
The church welcomed several new congregants this Sunday.
As a long-standing congregant, she volunteered regularly at the soup kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parishioner - a member of a parish, especially in the Christian church

Etymology

From Latin 'congregare' (to collect into a flock), via 'congregation'.
