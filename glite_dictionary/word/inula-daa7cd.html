inula

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈɪn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Inula, typically with yellow ray flowers and used ornamentally or medicinally
a plant of the genus Inula

Examples

The vibrant yellow flowers of the inula add a splash of color to the garden in midsummer.
Herbalists have long valued inula for its potential medicinal properties.
Several species of inula are grown as ornamentals in parks and public gardens.

Etymology

From Latin *inula*, meaning 'herb' or 'medicinal plant'.
