comradery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmrəˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈkæm.rə.dri / (BrE)
A feeling of trust and friendship among people who spend a lot of time together and share activities or goals.
friendship and trust among a group

Examples

The difficult journey created a strong sense of comradery among the team members.
After just a few days at camp, the children felt a real comradery with each other.
The company's annual retreat is designed to foster comradery in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camaraderie - mutual trust and friendship among people

Etymology

Variant of 'camaraderie', from French 'camaraderie' (companionship), ultimately from Latin 'camera' (chamber, room).
