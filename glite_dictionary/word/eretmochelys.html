Eretmochelys

1. NOUN

/ ɛrɛtˈmɑkəlɪs / (AmE), / ɛrɛtməˈkiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sea turtles commonly known as hawksbills, distinguished by their narrow, pointed beaks and beautifully patterned shells.

Eretmochelys is listed as critically endangered due to habitat loss and exploitation for its shell.

The shell of Eretmochelys is highly valued in the illegal tortoiseshell trade.
