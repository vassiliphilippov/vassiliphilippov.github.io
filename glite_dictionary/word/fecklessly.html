fecklessly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɛkləsli / (AmE), / ˈfɛkləsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows lack of skill, responsibility, or efficiency; in an inept or irresponsible manner.
in an inept or irresponsible way

Examples

Jenny handled the crisis fecklessly, making the situation worse than before.
The company's finances were managed fecklessly for years, leading to bankruptcy.
He fecklessly ignored all advice, resulting in repeated failures.

Synset Examples

he performed his functions ineptly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineptly - in a way that shows lack of skill or competence
  • Antonym: competently - in a capable or efficient manner

Etymology

From 'feckless', itself from Scots 'feck' (effect, value) with suffix '-less'.

2. ADV

/ ˈfɛklɪsli / (AmE), / ˈfɛkləsli / (BrE)
In an irresponsible or incompetent manner; lacking initiative or strength of character.
in an irresponsible manner

Examples

He managed the project fecklessly, resulting in missed deadlines and disorganization.
She spent her inheritance fecklessly, without any thought for the future.
The government was criticized for acting fecklessly during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irresponsibly - in a manner lacking a sense of responsibility
  • Antonym: competently - in an efficient and capable manner

Etymology

Formed from 'feckless' + '-ly'; 'feckless' from Scots 'feck' (effect, value) + '-less'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00165080-r
Synset POS: r
