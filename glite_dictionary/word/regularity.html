regularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
(in geometry) the property of a polygon having all sides and all angles equal.
property of equal sides and angles (geometry)

Examples

The regularity of a square means all its sides and angles are exactly the same.
Mathematicians often discuss the regularity of polygons when classifying different shapes.
In geometry, a figure's regularity is important for understanding its symmetry and structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irregularity - the quality of being uneven or not uniform, especially in shape or form

Etymology

From Latin regularitas (rule, conformity to a rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being consistent, predictable, or occurring at fixed intervals or according to a pattern
consistency; fixed pattern

Examples

She admired the regularity of the train schedule, which made commuting easy.
The regularity of his daily walks contributed to his good health.
Despite the storm, there was a regularity in the ocean waves that was calming to watch.

Synset Examples

he was famous for the regularity of his habits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irregularity - the quality of lacking consistency, predictability, or a fixed pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'regularitas' (rule, regularity), from 'regulus' (rule).
