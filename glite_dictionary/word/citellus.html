Citellus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs / (BrE)
a former genus name for typical ground squirrels found in Eurasia and North America, now classified under the genus Spermophilus.
former genus of ground squirrels

Examples

The genus Citellus was once used for many species of ground squirrels in Europe and Asia.
Researchers found a colony of Citellus living on the grassy plains.
Modern taxonomy has reassigned most Citellus species to the genus Spermophilus.

Etymology

From New Latin, originally a genus name, derived from Latin citellus (ground squirrel).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs / (BrE)
a genus of ground squirrels, comprising typical ground squirrels found in Eurasia and formerly North America.
a genus of ground squirrels

Examples

The Citellus genus includes several species of ground squirrels commonly found in Eurasia.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of different Citellus species across grassland regions.
Members of the Citellus genus are known for their distinctive burrowing behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground squirrel - a burrowing rodent of the squirrel family commonly found in open country

Etymology

From Modern Latin Citellus, adopted as a genus name in zoological classification.
