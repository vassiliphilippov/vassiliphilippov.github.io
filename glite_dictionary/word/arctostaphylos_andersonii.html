Arctostaphylos andersonii

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrktoʊˌstæfɪˈloʊs ændərˈsoʊniː / (AmE), / ɑːktəˌstæfɪˈləʊs ændəˈsəʊniː / (BrE)
A species of erect shrub native to California, characterized by leaves with heart-shaped lobes at the base.
a Californian shrub species

Examples

The Arctostaphylos andersonii is easily recognized by its unique heart-shaped leaves.
Biologists are working to protect Arctostaphylos andersonii from habitat loss in California.
Many gardens in northern California feature the attractive Arctostaphylos andersonii shrub.

Etymology

Genus from Greek arktos (bear) and staphyle (grape). Species named after Charles Lewis Anderson, an American physician and botanist.
