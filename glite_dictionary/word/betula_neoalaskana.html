Betula neoalaskana

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtjuːlə niːoʊəˌlæˈskænə / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə nɪəʊˌəˈlæskənə / (BrE)
A species of birch tree native to Alaska and northwestern Canada, distinguished by its white to pale brown bark.
a birch species native to Alaska and NW Canada

Examples

The Betula neoalaskana thrives in the cold climates of Alaska and Yukon.
Scientists studied the bark of Betula neoalaskana for its unique properties.
Many landscapes in Alaska are dotted with Betula neoalaskana trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a type of tree with thin bark and slender branches

Etymology

From New Latin: Betula (birch genus), neo- (new), alaskana (of Alaska).
