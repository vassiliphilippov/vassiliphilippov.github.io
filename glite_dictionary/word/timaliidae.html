Timaliidae

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈmæliɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / tɪˌmælɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly Old World passerine birds known as babblers, characterized by soft plumage and often social habits.
family of birds known as babblers

Examples

The Timaliidae are known for their melodious calls and gregarious nature.
Many species in the Timaliidae family inhabit dense forests in Asia.
Ornithologists study Timaliidae to understand social behavior in birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babblers - small to medium-sized, often noisy, Old World birds in the Timaliidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Timaliidae, the family name, based on the genus Timalius.
