Kogia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdʒiə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small sperm whales, including the pygmy sperm whale and dwarf sperm whale, found in deep oceans.
a genus of small sperm whales

Examples

The genus Kogia includes two recognized species of small whales.
Scientists have studied Kogia specimens to learn about deep-sea marine life.
Unlike other whales, Kogia species can expel a cloud of ink when threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy sperm whale - a small species of sperm whale, Kogia breviceps
  • Degree Higher: sperm whale - large toothed whale, Physeter macrocephalus

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek kōgē (kind of fish).
