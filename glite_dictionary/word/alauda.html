Alauda

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɔdə / (AmE), / əˈlɔːdə / (BrE)
The genus of birds in the lark family Alaudidae, including the skylarks.
genus of larks (skylarks)

Examples

The Eurasian skylark belongs to the genus Alauda and is well known for its song.
Several species in the Alauda genus are native to Europe and Asia.
Researchers have studied the migration patterns of Alauda across different continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lark - a songbird known for its melodious singing

Etymology

From Latin *alauda* (lark).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɔdə / (AmE), / əˈlɔːdə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Alaudidae, comprising the skylarks.
type genus of skylarks

Examples

The Alauda genus includes several species of skylarks found in Europe and Asia.
Naturalists observed the flight pattern characteristic of birds in the Alauda genus.
The Eurasian skylark, or Alauda arvensis, is celebrated for its melodious song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lark - a small songbird, especially of the genus Alauda

Etymology

From Latin *alauda* (lark).
