apprehender

1. NOUN

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛndər / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛndə / (BrE)
a person who arrests or seizes someone, especially in the name of justice
person who seizes or arrests

Examples

The fugitive was finally caught by the skilled apprehender assigned to the case.
As an experienced apprehender, she has brought many criminals to justice over the years.
The role of an apprehender often involves significant risk and responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrester - a person who detains or arrests another

Etymology

From the verb 'apprehend,' from Latin apprehendere (to seize, to grasp).
