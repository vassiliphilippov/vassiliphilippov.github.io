utility-grade

1. ADJ

/ juˈtɪləti ɡreɪd / (AmE), / juˈtɪləti ɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of beef) usable but of inferior quality, suitable for processing rather than direct sale as cuts of meat.
inferior quality, usable beef

Examples

This cut of utility-grade beef is typically used for processed products rather than steaks.
The butcher explained that utility-grade meat is not usually sold in supermarkets.
Most utility-grade beef ends up in canned or ground meat products.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: commercial-grade - (of beef) higher than utility-grade but still below top quality grades

Etymology

From 'utility' meaning usability and 'grade' meaning category of quality; used in U.S. Department of Agriculture classifications.
