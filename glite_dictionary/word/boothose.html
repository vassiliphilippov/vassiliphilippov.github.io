boothose

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌhoʊz / (AmE), / ˈbuːthəʊz / (BrE)
protective stockings worn over or in place of boots, especially in the past
protective stockings (historical)
archaic

Examples

In the 17th century, gentlemen often wore boothose made of fine linen beneath their boots.
The museum's exhibit includes a pair of embroidered boothose from the Elizabethan era.
He slipped on his boothose before putting on his heavy riding boots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaiter - a covering for the lower leg, similar in function

Etymology

From 'boot' and 'hose', referring to stockings; used from the 16th to 18th centuries.
