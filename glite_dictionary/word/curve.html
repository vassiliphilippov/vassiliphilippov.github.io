curve

1. VERB

/ kɝv / (AmE), / kɜːv / (BrE)
to bend or cause to bend in a smooth, often round shape
to bend in a smooth shape

Examples

The road begins to curve just beyond the old oak tree.
She watched the ribbon curve gracefully through the air.
You must curve the metal strip to fit the shape of the lamp.

Synset Examples

He crooked his index finger
the road curved sharply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - to shape or force something straight into a curve or angle
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From Latin 'curvare' (to bend), from 'curvus' (bent, curved).
