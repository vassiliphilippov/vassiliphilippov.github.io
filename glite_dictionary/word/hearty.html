hearty

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈhɑːti / (BrE)
showing sincere, warm, and enthusiastic friendliness or affection
warm and enthusiastic; friendly

Examples

The guests were greeted with a hearty smile at the front door.
She extended a hearty handshake to welcome the newcomer.
Their hearty laughter filled the room during the celebration.

Synset Examples

a hearty welcome
gave us a hearty reception

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordial - warm and friendly
  • Antonym: cold - lacking warmth or emotion

Etymology

From Middle English 'herti', from 'heart' + '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈhɑːti / (BrE)
(of an appetite or eater) strong, enthusiastic, and involving the consumption of large amounts of food.
strong or enthusiastic (especially of appetite)

Examples

After hiking all day, she had a hearty appetite.
The children enjoyed a hearty breakfast before school.
His hearty eating impressed everyone at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

a hearty (or healthy) appetite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voracious - wanting or devouring great quantities of food

Etymology

From Middle English 'herti', from 'heart' + '-y', originally meaning 'heartfelt'.

/ ˈhɑrti / (AmE), / ˈhɑːti / (BrE)
(of food) providing abundant nourishment; satisfying and filling.
providing abundant nourishment (of food)

Examples

The inn serves hearty soups that are perfect for a cold winter's day.
After hours of hard work, the farmers gathered for a hearty meal together.
