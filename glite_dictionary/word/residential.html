residential

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛzɪˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or suitable for living in; connected with places where people live rather than where they work.
relating to where people live

Examples

The city is planning to build more residential areas to accommodate its growing population.
Most of the neighborhood is residential, with only a few shops and offices.
He moved to a residential college near the university for his studies.

Synset Examples

a residential requirement for the doctorate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonresidential - not used or intended for residences

Etymology

From Latin residēre (to remain, to reside) + English suffix -ial.

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛzɪˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
designed for people to live in; consisting of private homes rather than businesses or factories
relating to places where people live

Examples

They decided to move to a more residential neighborhood to raise their family.
The city has strict residential zoning laws to separate homes from businesses.
There was little traffic in the residential streets after 9 p.m.

Synset Examples

a residential hotel
a residential quarter
a residential college
residential zoning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: commercial - relating to business rather than private residence

Etymology

From resident + -ial, from Latin residēre (to remain, to reside).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00487032-a
Synset POS: a
