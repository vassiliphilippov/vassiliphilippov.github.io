circulatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɜr.kjə.ləˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / səˈkjʊə.lə.tər.i / (BrE)
relating to the circulation of blood or the circulatory system.
of circulation or the circulatory system
medical

Examples

Regular exercise supports a healthy circulatory system.
The doctor explained the importance of circulatory health for overall well-being.
Certain diseases can affect the circulatory function of the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiovascular - relating to the heart and blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'circulatorius', from 'circulare' (to circulate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɝːkjəˌleɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / səˈkjʊː.lə.tər.i / (BrE)
relating to the circulation of fluids, especially blood, within a living organism
relating to bodily circulation

Examples

The human circulatory system consists of the heart, blood, and blood vessels.
Exercise can have significant effects on circulatory health.
Poor circulatory function can lead to numbness in the extremities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiovascular - relating to the heart and blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin circulatorius, from circulare (to circulate).
