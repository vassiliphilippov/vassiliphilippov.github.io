Trichodontidae

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkoʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒdɒnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, elongate, compressed, scaleless fishes with large eyes, inhabiting sandy or muddy sea bottoms, comprising two species.
family of small elongate scaleless fishes

Examples

Many marine biologists study the unique adaptations of Trichodontidae to sandy sea floors.
The Trichodontidae are notable for their large eyes and lack of scales.
Specimens from the Trichodontidae family are rarely caught in commercial fishing operations.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to features of the family, plus '-idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ traɪkoʊˌdɒntəˈdiː / (AmE), / trɪkəʊˌdɒntɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of elongate, compressed, scaleless, large-eyed fishes, typically found living in sand or mud
family of sand-dwelling fishes

Examples

The Trichodontidae are characterized by their compressed bodies and large eyes.
Marine biologists recently conducted a study on the habitat preferences of Trichodontidae.
Members of the Trichodontidae family are often found burrowed in sandy coastal regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to their teeth.
