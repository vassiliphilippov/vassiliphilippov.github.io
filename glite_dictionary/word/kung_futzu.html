K'ung Futzu

1. PROPN

/ kuŋ ˈfuːdsuː / (AmE), / kuŋ ˈfuːtsuː / (BrE)
The Chinese philosopher (circa 551–478 BC) whose teachings formed the foundation of Confucianism.
Chinese philosopher, founder of Confucianism

Examples

The teachings of K'ung Futzu have greatly influenced Eastern philosophy and culture.
Many scholars still study the Analects, a collection of K'ung Futzu's sayings and ideas.
K'ung Futzu emphasized the importance of moral integrity and respect for tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Confucius - Latinized name of K'ung Futzu, Chinese philosopher (551–478 BC)

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 孔夫子 (Kǒng Fūzǐ), meaning 'Master Kong'.
