caste

1. NOUN

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
A hereditary social class in Hindu society, distinguished by relative degrees of ritual purity and social status.
hereditary social class in Hinduism

Examples

The Indian social structure was historically organized around the rigid caste system.
Members of a higher caste were often given privileges denied to others.
He rejected discrimination based on caste and worked toward social equality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a group sharing the same economic or social status
  • Antonym: outcaste - a person who does not belong to any caste

Etymology

From Portuguese 'casta' (race, lineage), originally from Latin 'castus' (pure, chaste).

2. NOUN

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
A social class or group in a society, distinguished from others by hereditary rank, profession, or wealth, often with social or legal restrictions.
hereditary social class or group

Examples

In traditional India, a person's caste determined their social status and occupation.
Efforts to eradicate caste discrimination have been ongoing for decades.
Members of the merchant caste were responsible for trade and commerce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a group of people in a society with the same social or economic status
  • Synonym: estate - a social class in historical European societies
  • Synonym: caste system - a social structure in which classes are determined by heredity

Etymology

From Portuguese 'casta' (race, lineage), from Latin 'castus' (pure).

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
the social status or position a person holds within a rigid system based on class, often determined by birth and not easily changed
social class in a rigid system

Examples

In traditional societies, a person's caste often determined their occupation and marital prospects.
He was born into the highest caste and enjoyed certain privileges.
Marrying outside one's caste was once considered unacceptable in many communities.

4. NOUN

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
In social insects such as ants or bees, a physically distinct group of individuals specialized to perform certain functions within the colony.
social insect subgroup by function or form

Examples

In an ant colony, the worker caste is responsible for foraging and caring for the young.
The soldier caste in termites has powerful jaws for defending the colony against predators.
Some bees belong to the queen caste, which is solely dedicated to reproduction.

Etymology

From Latin 'casta' (lineage, breed), applied in biology to groups with specialized roles.
