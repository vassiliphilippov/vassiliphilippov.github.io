Bos

1. PROPN

/ bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
A genus of wild and domestic cattle, including oxen, cows, and related species.
genus of cattle (animals)

Examples

The genus Bos includes well-known species such as the domestic cow and the wild gaur.
Modern dairy cattle belong to the species Bos taurus.
Ancient cave paintings often depict members of the Bos genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'bos' (ox, cow; cattle), used as the genus name in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
a genus of large ruminant mammals that includes wild and domestic cattle, such as oxen, cows, and yaks
genus including wild and domestic cattle

Examples

The extinct aurochs is a member of the genus Bos and the ancestor of modern cattle.
Biologists classify domestic cows and yaks in the genus Bos.
There are several species within the genus Bos, including the gaur and the banteng.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle - large domesticated bovine animals used for meat or milk
  • Degree Higher: Bovinae - the biological subfamily including cattle and related animals

Etymology

From Latin *bos* (ox, cow).
