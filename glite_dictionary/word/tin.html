tin

1. NOUN

/ tɪn / (AmE), / tɪn / (BrE)
a metal container used for storing dry foods such as tea, flour, or biscuits
metal container for food storage

Examples

She keeps her favorite tea in a decorative tin on the shelf.
The biscuits came in a beautiful tin with a festive design.
Please fill the tin with flour so we can start baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can - a metal container, especially for liquids or preserved food

Etymology

From Old English *tīn*, of Germanic origin, referring to the metal tin.

3. NOUN

/ tɪn / (AmE), / tɪn / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metal, chemical element number 50, known for its resistance to corrosion and use in making alloys and coating other metals.
a silvery-white metallic element (Sn)

Examples

The cans are coated with tin to prevent them from rusting.
Tin is often combined with copper to make bronze.
Large deposits of tin can be found in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - a heavy, soft, bluish-gray metal, chemical element number 82
  • Synonym: aluminum - a light, silvery, corrosion-resistant metal, chemical element number 13

Etymology
