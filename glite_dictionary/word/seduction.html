seduction

1. NOUN

/ səˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone to have sex or to become romantically involved, often by using charm or appeal
act of persuading into romance or sex

Examples

The art of seduction often relies on subtlety and confidence.
His seduction of the actress became the talk of the town.
Stories of seduction are common throughout literature and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allurement - the action of attracting or enticing someone
  • Antonym: rejection - refusal to accept or be persuaded

Etymology

From Latin seductio (act of leading aside), from seducere (to lead aside).

2. NOUN

/ səˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone to do something they would normally consider wrong or unwise, especially in a moral or ethical sense
the act of enticing into wrongdoing

Examples

The seduction of the young man led him to make decisions he later regretted.
She warned him about the seduction of easy money and quick success.
In literature, the theme of seduction often involves moral dilemmas and ethical choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enticement - The act of attracting someone to do something, often wrong or unwise
  • Synonym: temptation - The desire or action of tempting someone into wrongdoing
  • Antonym: resistance - Refusal to be enticed or seduced; the opposite of giving in

Etymology

From Latin seductio (the act of leading aside), from seducere (to lead away).
