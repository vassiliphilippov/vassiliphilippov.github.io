methodology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəˈdɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmɛθəˈdɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a system of methods, principles, and rules used in a particular discipline or field of study
system of methods in a field

Examples

The researchers carefully outlined their methodology to ensure their experiment could be replicated.
A sound methodology is crucial for credible scientific results.
She questioned whether the methodology used in the study was appropriate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a particular way of doing something

Etymology

From French méthodologie, from méthode (method) + -logie (-logy, study of), ultimately from Greek.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəˈdɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmɛθəˈdɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the system of methods and principles used in a particular discipline, especially as analyzed or discussed in philosophy
system of methods used in a discipline

Examples

The professor is an expert in the methodology of scientific research.
Philosophers often debate the appropriate methodology for inquiry in social sciences.
A clear methodology is essential for producing valid and reliable results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method - a way of doing something, especially a systematic way

Etymology

From French 'méthodologie', from Greek 'methodos' (pursuit, method) + '-logia' (study of).
