Oxyura jamaicensis

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑksiˈjʊrə dʒəˌmeɪɪˈsɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈjʊərə dʒəˌmeɪɪˈsɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of small, reddish-brown, stiff-tailed duck native to North America and northern South America, commonly called the ruddy duck.
species of stiff-tailed duck

Examples

Birdwatchers in Canada are always thrilled to spot the rare Oxyura jamaicensis during migration.
Wetland conservation is crucial for maintaining healthy populations of Oxyura jamaicensis.
The distinctive blue bill of the male Oxyura jamaicensis makes it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruddy duck - common name for Oxyura jamaicensis, a stiff-tailed duck

Etymology

From Greek *oxys* (sharp) + *oura* (tail) and Latin *jamaicensis* (of Jamaica), referring to the species' sharp tail and habitat.
