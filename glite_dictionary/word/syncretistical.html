syncretistical

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˌkrɛtɪˈstɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪŋˌkret.ɪˈstɪk.əl / (BrE)
relating to the historical tendency of a language to reduce or simplify its use of inflections.
relating to reduction of inflections in language

Examples

The transition from Old English to Modern English was marked by several syncretistical changes.
Linguists study how syncretistical processes shape grammar over time.
A syncretistical perspective reveals why case endings disappeared in English.

Synset Examples

modern English is a syncretic language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncretic - relating to the merging or combination of different elements, especially in language or religion

Etymology

From 'syncretism' + '-ical'; ultimately from Greek 'synkretismos' (union of different parties or schools).

2. ADJ

/ sɪŋˌkrɛtɪˈstɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪŋˌkriːtɪˈstɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by syncretism; combining different beliefs, schools of thought, or practices.
relating to syncretism

Examples

The philosopher's syncretistical approach embraced elements of both Eastern and Western traditions.
Their syncretistical rituals merge practices from several indigenous religions.
She is known for her syncretistical tendency to blend disparate artistic styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncretic - combining different beliefs or schools of thought

Etymology

From syncretism + -ical, formed on the model of 'syncretic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02818301-a
Synset POS: a
