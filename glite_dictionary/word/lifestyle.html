lifestyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌstaɪl / (BrE)
the way in which a person or group lives, including their habits, attitudes, and values
way of living; habits and values

Examples

He decided to adopt a healthier lifestyle by exercising regularly and eating better.
Traveling frequently is part of her exciting lifestyle.
The documentary explores the rural lifestyle of villagers in northern Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: way of life - the manner in which a person lives; way of living

Etymology

Formed from 'life' and 'style', popularized in the 20th century to describe patterns of living.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfstaɪl / (BrE)
The way in which a person or group lives, including their habits, attitudes, tastes, moral standards, and economic level.
way of living; daily habits and attitudes

Examples

She adopted a healthier lifestyle after her doctor advised her to exercise more.
Many people are seeking a more sustainable lifestyle to help protect the environment.
Traveling frequently became part of his lifestyle after he became a freelance writer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: way of life - a particular manner in which a person lives

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'style', first recorded in English in the early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfstaɪl / (BrE)
The way in which a person or group lives, including their habits, attitudes, and values.
a way of living

Examples

Regular exercise and a balanced diet are important parts of a healthy lifestyle.
After moving to the countryside, her lifestyle became much more relaxed.
He decided to change his lifestyle to reduce stress and improve his well-being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: way of life - a customary manner of living; similar meaning to lifestyle

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'style' in early 20th century English.
