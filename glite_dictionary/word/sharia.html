sharia

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈriːə / (AmE), / ʃəˈrɪə / (BrE)
the religious law forming part of the Islamic tradition, derived from the Quran and the teachings and example of Muhammad
Islamic religious law

Examples

In some countries, sharia influences both criminal and civil law systems.
Debates over the implementation of sharia often arise in multicultural societies.
His research focused on the interpretation of sharia in different Islamic communities.

Synset Examples

sharia is only applicable to Muslims
under Islamic law there is no separation of church and state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic law - law based on Islamic tradition and sources

Etymology

From Arabic šarīʕa (meaning 'way', 'path', 'law').
