bauble

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːbəl / (BrE)
A small, inexpensive, and showy ornament or piece of jewelry, often worn for decoration.
cheap, showy ornament or jewelry

Examples

She wore a cheap bauble on her wrist that glittered in the sunlight.
The store was filled with colorful baubles for the holiday season.
He gave her a pretty bauble, though it held no real value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament or piece of inexpensive jewelry
  • Antonym: gem - a precious or semi-precious stone, especially when cut and polished or engraved

Etymology

From Old French *baubel* meaning 'trinket'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːbəl / (BrE)
a mock scepter, often ornamented, carried by a court jester as a symbol of their role
mock scepter of a court jester

Examples

The jester spun his bauble in the air to amuse the king.
A court jester's bauble often featured a carved head with bells.
In medieval times, the bauble was a recognizable sign of the jester's profession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scepter - an ornamented staff carried by rulers as a symbol of authority

Etymology

From Old French 'bauble' (child's toy, trinket), of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.
