to circumscribe

1. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkəmˌskraɪb / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmskraɪb / (BrE)
(in geometry) to draw a shape around another, touching it at all possible points without intersecting it.
to draw a shape around another in geometry

Examples

In geometry class, the teacher showed us how to circumscribe a circle around a triangle.
To find the minimum area rectangle that can circumscribe a given polygon, you need to follow a specific method.
The engineer needed to circumscribe the square with a circle of precisely calculated radius.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inscribe - to draw a shape inside another so that it touches it at all possible points without crossing the boundary

Etymology

From Latin 'circumscribere' (to draw a line around), from 'circum-' (around) + 'scribere' (to write).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkəmˌskraɪb / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌskraɪb / (BrE)
to limit or restrict something within certain boundaries
to restrict within limits

Examples

Strict policies circumscribe what students can and cannot do on campus.
His creativity was circumscribed by a lack of resources and support.
These new laws will inevitably circumscribe the powers of local government.

Synset Examples

I limit you to two visits to the pub a day
please confine your questions to the topic
our actions are circumscribed by our biology, personality, and by the social and cultural context into which we are born

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrict - to put a limit on; keep under control
  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: free - not limited or restricted

Etymology

From Latin 'circumscribere', from 'circum' (around) + 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00236085-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to draw a line around something, especially to enclose a shape within another so that all corners touch the boundary
draw a line around; enclose within bounds

Examples

The artist carefully circumscribed the area for the mural.
A triangle can be circumscribed by a circle so that all its vertices touch the circle.
They used chalk to circumscribe a section of the playground for the new game.

Synset Examples

another circle is circumscribed about the square

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inscribe - to draw or write inside a shape so that it touches all sides of another shape

Etymology

From Latin 'circumscribere', from 'circum' (around) + 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
