consanguine

1. ADJ

/ kɒnˈsæŋ.ɡwɪn / (AmE), / kɒnˈsæŋ.ɡwɪn / (BrE)
Related by blood; having the same ancestry.
related by blood
archaic

Examples

Their consanguine connection was traced back several generations.
In ancient societies, consanguine relationships often determined inheritance rights.
Even though they lived far apart, their consanguine ties remained strong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consanguineous - related by blood; descended from the same ancestor
  • Synonym: agnate - related through male lineage or on the father's side
  • Synonym: cognate - related through female lineage or on the mother's side
  • Antonym: affinal - related by marriage rather than by blood

Etymology

From Latin 'consanguineus' meaning 'of the same blood' (con- 'together' + sanguis 'blood').
