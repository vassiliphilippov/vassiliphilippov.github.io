analyticity

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˌlɪˈsɪɾəti / (AmE), / ˌæn.əl.ɪˈsɪt.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being analytic, especially in relation to truth, logic, or method.
quality of being analytic

Examples

Philosophers often debate the analyticity of certain statements.
The analyticity of the function allows it to be expanded as a power series.
Linguists examine the analyticity of languages with regard to their sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analytic - involving analysis; logical

Etymology

From analytic + -ity, ultimately from Greek 'analytikos' (able to analyze).
