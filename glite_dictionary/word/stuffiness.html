stuffiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfinəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌfinɪs / (BrE)
a quality of being excessively formal, pompous, or lacking liveliness, often making an atmosphere seem dull or oppressive
pompous or overly formal quality

Examples

He disliked the stuffiness of the boardroom, where everyone took themselves too seriously.
The party was spoiled by the stuffiness of the guests, who refused to relax and have fun.
Her speech was refreshing, breaking through the stuffiness that usually filled such formal events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formality - the quality of being formal or conventional
  • Antonym: informality - the quality of being informal

Etymology

From 'stuffy' + '-ness', with 'stuffy' meaning excessively formal or lacking in freshness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfinəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌfinəs / (BrE)
The condition of having the nose or throat blocked or congested, making breathing difficult.
blocked nose or throat; congestion

Examples

Allergy sufferers often complain about stuffiness during pollen season.
The stuffiness in my nose made it hard to sleep last night.
Drinking hot tea helped relieve the stuffiness in her throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congestion - a state of being blocked, especially in the nose or throat
  • Antonym: clearance - the removal of an obstruction, especially in bodily passages

Etymology

From 'stuffy' (blocked, airless) + '-ness' (state or quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌf.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being airless and poorly ventilated, often resulting in an uncomfortable atmosphere
lack of fresh air; poor ventilation

Examples

After several hours, the stuffiness in the crowded conference room became almost unbearable.
They opened all the windows to get rid of the stuffiness in the apartment.
The stuffiness in the old theater made it difficult to concentrate on the play.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ventilation - the provision of fresh air to a room or building
