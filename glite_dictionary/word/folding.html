folding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a protein molecule assumes its specific, three-dimensional structure essential for its function
formation of a protein's three-dimensional shape

Examples

Scientists study folding to understand how proteins work in the body.
Errors in protein folding can lead to diseases such as Alzheimer's.
Advances in technology have improved our ability to observe the folding of complex molecules.

Synset Examples

understanding protein folding is the next step in deciphering the genetic code

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which rock layers are bent or curved due to stress in the Earth's crust
geological process bending rock layers

Examples

Mountain ranges are often formed by the folding of rock layers over millions of years.
Geologists study folding to understand the history of Earth's crustal movements.
The area exhibits evidence of severe folding due to tectonic activity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faulting - the process of fracturing and displacement of rock layers in the Earth's crust

(of furniture or equipment) designed to be folded for easy storage or transport.
able to be folded for storage or transport

Examples

We brought a folding table to the picnic for extra space.
He sat on a folding chair while waiting for the train.
A folding bike is convenient for commuting and storage in small apartments.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapsible - able to be folded or compressed into a smaller space

4. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of bending or laying one part over another; the action of folding something
the act of folding

Examples

After folding the paper, she created an intricate origami figure.
Proper folding of the laundry helps keep clothes wrinkle-free.
The chef demonstrated the folding of dough to his students.

Synset Examples

he gave the napkins a double fold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfolding - the act or process of opening or spreading out something that was folded

Etymology

From the verb 'fold' + '-ing', forming a noun indicating the action or process.
