superlunary

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈluː.nə.ri / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈluː.nəri / (BrE)
Situated beyond the moon or relating to a region outside the orbit of the moon, often used in literary or philosophical contexts.
beyond the moon; outside lunar orbit

Examples

Some ancient philosophers believed in a superlunary realm untouched by earthly imperfections.
Her poetry aspires to a superlunary beauty, transcending the ordinary world.
In fiction, life on a superlunary planet can represent hope or otherworldliness.

Synset Examples

who can imagine a translunary visitor in Times Square?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sublunary - belonging to this world; under the moon; earthly

Etymology

From Latin superlunaris, from super- (above) + lunaris (of the moon).

2. ADJ

/ suːˈpɜrluːˌnɛri / (AmE), / suːˈpɜːljuːnəri / (BrE)
Relating to things beyond the physical world; unworldly or ethereal.
unworldly; ethereal

Examples

Her poetry was filled with superlunary images of angels and celestial light.
He found solace in superlunary dreams, untouched by the troubles of daily life.
The artist strove to capture a superlunary beauty beyond the material world.

Synset Examples

high translunary dreams

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sublunary - of this earthly world; mundane

Etymology

From Latin superlunaris, from super- (above) + luna (moon).
