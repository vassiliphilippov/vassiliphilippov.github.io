Platonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪ.tən.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈplæt.ən.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the philosophical ideas of Plato or Platonism
follower of Plato's philosophy

Examples

Many scholars consider themselves Platonists because they believe in the existence of ideal forms.
The debate between the Platonists and the Aristotelians shaped much of Western philosophy.
As a Platonist, she emphasized the importance of abstract concepts in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Aristotelian - a supporter of Aristotle's philosophy
  • Synonym: Platonism - the philosophy of Plato

Etymology

From Plato + -ist, denoting an adherent or follower of Plato.
