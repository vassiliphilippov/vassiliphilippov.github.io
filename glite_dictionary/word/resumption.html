resumption

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌmpʃən / (BrE)
The act of starting something again after it has been paused or interrupted.
starting something again

Examples

After a brief interruption, the resumption of the meeting was announced by the chairperson.
The peace talks are expected to see a resumption next week after months of delay.
Following the holiday, there was a resumption of normal business activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restart - an act of starting something again
  • Antonym: suspension - temporary stopping or pausing of something

Etymology

From Latin resumptio, from resumere (to take up again).
