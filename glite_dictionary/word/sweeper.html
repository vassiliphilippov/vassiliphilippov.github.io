sweeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈswipər / (AmE), / ˈswiːpə / (BrE)
a cleaning device equipped with revolving brushes, used to pick up dirt and debris from carpets or floors as it is pushed.
cleaning device with revolving brushes

Examples

She pushed the sweeper over the carpet to pick up crumbs after dinner.
The hotel uses a mechanical sweeper in the hallways to keep the floors clean.
Before vacuum cleaners became common, households relied on a sturdy sweeper for daily cleaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum cleaner - an electrical cleaning device that uses suction to remove dirt from floors and carpets

Etymology

From 'sweep' + '-er', meaning one that sweeps.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswipər / (AmE), / ˈswiːpə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to sweep floors, streets, or similar areas to keep them clean
person employed to sweep floors or streets

Examples

The sweeper started early in the morning to clear the leaves from the street.
Every evening, a sweeper cleans the floors of the office building.
They hired an extra sweeper to help maintain the cleanliness of the train station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: janitor - a person employed to look after a building, especially cleaning
  • Synonym: cleaner - person employed to clean buildings or other places

Etymology

From sweep + -er, denoting someone who sweeps.

3. NOUN

/ ˈswipər / (AmE), / ˈswiːpə / (BrE)
A small nocturnal fish found in warm shallow seas, with an oblong, laterally compressed body.
small nocturnal marine fish

Examples

The reef was teeming with sweepers hiding among the corals at night.
Divers often spot sweepers because of their bright, reflective eyes in the dark.
Sweepers are rarely seen during the day as they shelter in rocks and crevices.
