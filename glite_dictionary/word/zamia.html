zamia

1. NOUN

/ ˈzeɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈzeɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Zamia, a group of small, verdant cycads native to the Americas.
a genus of small, green cycads

Examples

Several species of zamia can be found thriving in the forests of Central America.
The botanist gave a lecture on the unique life cycle of zamia plants.
Gardeners value zamias for their decorative foliage and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a plant of the order Cycadales, characterized by a stout trunk and large compound leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'zamia', used in botanical Latin for these cycads.
