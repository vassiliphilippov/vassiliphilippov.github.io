Monte Pissis

1. PROPN

/ ˌmoʊnteɪ ˈpisis / (AmE), / ˌmɒnteɪ ˈpisis / (BrE)
a mountain in the Andes of Argentina, rising to 22,241 feet (6,793 meters); one of the highest peaks in South America
a high mountain in Argentina

Examples

Many climbers consider Monte Pissis to be one of the ultimate high-altitude challenges in the Andes.
From the summit of Monte Pissis, the view of the surrounding Atacama Desert is stunning.
The harsh, dry climate makes Monte Pissis a demanding ascent for mountaineers.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Monte' (mountain) and the name 'Pissis', after French geologist Pedro José Amadeo Pissis.
