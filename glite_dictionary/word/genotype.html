genotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nə.taɪp / (BrE)
the genetic constitution of an organism, referring to the specific alleles present at particular locations in its DNA
an organism's genetic makeup

Examples

Identical twins have the same genotype but may look slightly different due to environmental factors.
Scientists study a plant's genotype to understand how certain traits are inherited.
The genotype determines which genetic diseases an individual might be predisposed to.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phenotype - the observable characteristics of an organism resulting from the interaction of its genotype with the environment

Etymology

From German Genotypus, from 'geno-' (race, kind) and Greek 'typos' (type, impression).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nə.taɪp / (BrE)
a group of organisms sharing a specific set of genetic characteristics or constitution
group with specific genetic constitution
medical

Examples

Many plants in this field have the same genotype, making them genetically identical.
Scientists studied the genotype of the population to identify hereditary traits.
Different environments can affect how a particular genotype is expressed as observable traits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phenotype - the observable characteristics of an organism resulting from the interaction of its genotype with the environment

Etymology

From German Genotypus, from Geno- (gene) + -type, originally coined in early 20th century scientific literature.
