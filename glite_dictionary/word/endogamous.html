endogamous

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈdɑɡəməs / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒɡəməs / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting the custom of marrying only within a specific group, clan, or tribe.
marrying within one's group

Examples

Anthropologists studied the endogamous practices of several remote tribes.
Some communities remain strongly endogamous to preserve cultural traditions.
The village was historically endogamous, with most people marrying within the same clan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogamous - relating to marrying outside one's group

Etymology

From endogamy, based on Greek 'endon' (within) + 'gamos' (marriage).

2. ADJ

/ ɛnˈdɑɡəməs / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒɡəməs / (BrE)
(of reproduction) characterized by fertilization involving pollen or gametes from another flower or individual of the same species.
having fertilization within the same species

Examples

Some plant species are endogamous, requiring fertilization by pollen from flowers of the same kind.
Research has shown that endogamous breeding promotes genetic stability within isolated populations.
Unlike exogamous plants, endogamous species rarely hybridize with different species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogamous - (of reproduction) involving fertilization from a different species or population

Etymology

From Greek endo- (within) + -gamos (marriage, union).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00875124-a
Synset POS: a
