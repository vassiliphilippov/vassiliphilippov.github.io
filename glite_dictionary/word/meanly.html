meanly

1. ADV

/ ˈminli / (AmE), / ˈmiːnli / (BrE)
in a despicable or ignoble manner; with cruelty or lack of honor
in a despicable or base way

Examples

He spoke meanly of his coworkers behind their backs.
She meanly dismissed the child's sincere efforts.
The dictator meanly oppressed his opponents without a second thought.

Synset Examples

this new leader meanly threatens the deepest values of our society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelly - in a cruel manner
  • Antonym: nobly - in a noble or honorable manner

Etymology

Formed from 'mean' (base, low) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet

2. ADV

/ ˈminli / (AmE), / ˈmiːnli / (BrE)
In a poor or inferior manner; in a way that lacks quality or richness.
in a poor or inferior manner

Examples

The soldiers were meanly clothed for the harsh winter months.
Their house was meanly furnished, lacking even basic comforts.
The guests were meanly treated at the event, receiving almost no attention.

Synset Examples

troops meanly equipped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poorly - in a way that is of low quality or standard
  • Antonym: well - in a good or satisfactory manner
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00399514-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈminli / (AmE), / ˈmiːnli / (BrE)
in a miserly or stingy way; showing or characterized by lack of generosity or luxury
in a miserly or stingy manner

Examples

The couple chose to live meanly, saving every cent for their future.
He dressed meanly despite his great wealth, avoiding any display of luxury.
Even in success, she furnished her home meanly and avoided unnecessary expenses.

Synset Examples

they lived meanly and without ostentation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserly - unwilling to spend money; stingy
  • Antonym: generously - in a generous and giving manner

Etymology

Formed from 'mean' (in the sense of miserly) + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00399370-r
Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˈminli / (AmE), / ˈmiːnli / (BrE)
In a nasty or ill-tempered manner; showing spite or unkindness.
in a nasty, unkind manner

Examples

He spoke meanly to everyone at the meeting, making the atmosphere tense.
She meanly refused to help, even though she clearly could have.
The children complained that their teacher treated them meanly for no reason.

Synset Examples

‘Don't expect me to help you,’ he added nastily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nastily - in a nasty or spiteful manner
  • Antonym: kindly - in a kind or benevolent manner

Etymology

Formed from 'mean' (unkind) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
