camper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæmpə / (BrE)
a motor vehicle or trailer equipped with living facilities for camping while traveling
vehicle equipped for camping

Examples

We rented a camper and drove across the country during the summer break.
The camper was fully stocked with a small kitchen and sleeping quarters.
Many families choose a camper for convenient and comfortable road trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravan - a vehicle equipped for living in, typically towed by a car

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmpər / (AmE), / ˈkæmpə / (BrE)
a person who stays temporarily in a tent, cabin, or similar shelter for recreation, especially as part of a vacation or outdoor activity
person staying in tent or cabin for recreation

Examples

The camper set up his tent by the lake and enjoyed the peaceful scenery.
Every camper is required to follow the safety rules in the park.
As a young camper, she learned useful survival skills during summer camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiker - a person who goes for long walks, especially in the countryside

Etymology

From 'camp' + '-er', denoting someone who camps.
