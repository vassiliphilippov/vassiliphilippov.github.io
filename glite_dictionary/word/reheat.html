to reheat

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈhit / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhiːt / (BrE)
to heat something again, especially food that has previously been cooked
heat again; make warm again

Examples

If the soup gets cold, you can simply reheat it on the stove.
Never reheat rice more than once, as it can cause food poisoning.
He quickly reheated his coffee in the microwave before heading back to work.

Synset Examples

Please reheat the food from last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat - to make something hot or warm

Etymology

From the prefix 're-' (again) + 'heat'.
