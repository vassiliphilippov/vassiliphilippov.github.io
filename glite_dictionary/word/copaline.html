copaline

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.pəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.pəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A fossil resin, partly mineralized copal, often dug from the ground and considered an intermediate stage between copal and amber.
partly mineralized fossil copal

Examples

The geologist discovered a piece of copaline while excavating the ancient riverbed.
Collectors value copaline for its intermediate qualities between copal and amber.
The museum displayed specimens of copal, copaline, and amber to show their progressive mineralization.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: copal - A natural tree resin sometimes used as incense or varnish
  • Degree Higher: amber - A hard, yellowish fossil resin valued for jewelry

Etymology

From 'copal' (a type of resin) with scientific suffix '-ine', indicating a substance related to copal.
