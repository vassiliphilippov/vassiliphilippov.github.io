Decumaria barbara

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛk.juˈmɛr.i.ə bɑːrˈbær.ə / (AmE), / ˌdɛk.juˈmɑːr.i.ə bɑːˈbɑː.rə / (BrE)
A species of woody climbing plant native to the southeastern United States, bearing white flowers in compound terminal clusters.
woody climber with white flowers, southeastern US

Examples

The garden's Decumaria barbara climbed gracefully over the old stone wall.
In the wild, Decumaria barbara thrives in moist forests of the southeastern states.
We planted Decumaria barbara to attract pollinators with its clusters of white blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Decumaria and the species epithet barbara.
