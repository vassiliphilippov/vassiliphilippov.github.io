tarantella

1. NOUN

/ təˌrænˈtɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌtær.ənˈtel.ə / (BrE)
A lively, fast-paced Italian dance for two people, characterized by rapid, whirling movements.
lively Italian folk dance for two

Examples

The dancers performed a spirited tarantella in colorful costumes at the festival.
She learned the tarantella during her trip to Italy and enjoyed its energetic rhythms.
The music teacher explained the origins of the tarantella to her students.

Etymology

From Italian 'tarantella', deriving from 'Taranto', a city in southern Italy.

2. NOUN

/ tɛrənˈtɛlə / (AmE), / tærənˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A lively Italian folk dance in rapid 6/8 time, or the music composed for such a dance.
Italian folk dance/music in 6/8 time

Examples

The orchestra played a spirited tarantella that kept everyone on their feet.
During the festival, dancers performed a traditional tarantella accompanied by tambourines.
He studied the structure of the tarantella for his music theory class.

Etymology

From Italian 'tarantella', named after the city of Taranto in southern Italy.
