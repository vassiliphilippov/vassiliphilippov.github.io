Physostegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪsəˈstiːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌfɪsəˈstiːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs in the mint family, typically bearing tubular, often pink or white flowers.
genus of North American perennial herbs

Examples

The Physostegia grows well in moist, sunny flowerbeds and attracts many pollinators.
Gardeners appreciate the tall spikes of the Physostegia for late summer color.
Several species of Physostegia, such as the obedient plant, are grown as ornamentals.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'physa' (bladder) and 'stege' (cover), referring to aspects of the plant's morphology.
