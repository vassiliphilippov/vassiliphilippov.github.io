Cladrastis

1. NOUN

/ kləˈdræstɪs / (AmE), / kləˈdræstɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the legume family, known as yellowwoods, native to East Asia and eastern North America.
genus of yellowwood trees

Examples

Cladrastis is known for its attractive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring.
The American Cladrastis can be found in the Appalachian region's forests.
Botanists studied the unique leaf arrangement of the Cladrastis tree in the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowwood - common name for trees in the Cladrastis genus and similar species

Etymology

From Greek 'klados' (branch) and 'thraustos' (breakable), referring to the brittle branches.

2. NOUN

/ kləˈdræstɪs / (AmE), / kləˈdrastɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the pea family (Fabaceae), known as yellowwoods, native to eastern Asia and North America.
genus of yellowwood trees

Examples

The Cladrastis genus is highly valued for its beautiful yellow heartwood.
Horticulturists often plant Cladrastis species for their attractive spring flowers.
Several parks in the southern United States feature mature Cladrastis trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowwood - common name for trees in the genus Cladrastis

Etymology

From Greek klados (branch) + rhastos (easily broken), referring to easily broken branches.
