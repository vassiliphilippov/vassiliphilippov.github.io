fascination

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a very strong interest, attraction, or admiration for something remarkable or unusual
intense interest or attraction

Examples

Her lifelong fascination with ancient Egypt inspired her career as an archaeologist.
The children watched the magician with fascination, amazed by every trick.
I've always had a fascination for the mysteries of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captivation - the state of being intensely attracted or interested
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of being uninterested or unengaged

Etymology

From Latin fascinatio (bewitchment), from fascinare (to bewitch).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfæsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely interesting or attractive, often inspiring intense attention or admiration.
the power to intensely attract interest

Examples

The city's fascination lies in its vibrant culture and rich history.
As a child, she had a fascination for dinosaurs and ancient fossils.
His fascination with astronomy led him to become an astrophysicist.

Synset Examples

he held the children spellbound with magic tricks and other fascinations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - the act or power of drawing interest or attention
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of being bored or lacking interest

Etymology

From Latin fascinatio ‘bewitchment’, from fascinare ‘to bewitch’.

3. NOUN

/ ˌfæsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a powerful feeling of being intensely interested or attracted to something, often with a sense of wonder or awe
intense interest or attraction

Examples

Her lifelong fascination with astronomy began when she saw her first eclipse as a child.
The magician's performance held the audience in utter fascination.
Despite the danger, there was a strange fascination that drew them closer to the erupting volcano.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or learn about something
  • Degree Higher: obsession - an idea or thought that continually preoccupies or intrudes on a person's mind
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of being weary and restless through lack of interest

Etymology

From Latin fascinatio, from fascinare (to bewitch).
