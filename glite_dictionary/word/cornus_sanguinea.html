Cornus sanguinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs sæŋˈɡwin.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.nəs sæŋˈɡwiː.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A European deciduous shrub of the dogwood family, notable for its red stems in autumn and clusters of dull white flowers.
a European shrub with red autumn stems and white flowers

Examples

The Cornus sanguinea brightens gardens in autumn with its vivid red stems.
Gardeners often plant Cornus sanguinea for winter color and wildlife attraction.
In spring, the Cornus sanguinea produces small white flowers before its leaves fully emerge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - a common name for shrubs and small trees of the genus Cornus

Etymology

From Latin 'cornus' (dogwood) and 'sanguinea' (bloody, blood-red), referring to the red stems.
