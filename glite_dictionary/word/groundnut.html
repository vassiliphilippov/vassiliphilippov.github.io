groundnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd.nʌt / (BrE)
A nutlike edible tuber of certain North American plants, traditionally an important food for Native Americans.
nutlike tuber; edible plant root

Examples

The Native Americans relied on groundnut tubers as a staple food source during winter.
She cooked a stew by adding sliced groundnut along with wild greens.
Botanists are studying the cultivation of the groundnut to enhance sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut - a leguminous plant producing edible seeds, also called groundnut in some regions

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd.nʌt / (BrE)
A North American vine (Apios americana) bearing fragrant flowers and edible tubers, formerly an important food for some Native American peoples.
North American vine with edible tubers

Examples

The Native Americans cultivated the groundnut for its nutritious tubers and fragrant flowers.
Some wild groundnut vines can still be found growing along riverbanks in New England.
Researchers are studying the potential of groundnut as a sustainable crop for modern agriculture.

Etymology

Formed from 'ground' + 'nut' describing tubers that grow underground.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd.nʌt / (BrE)
the pod of the peanut plant, containing usually two nuts or seeds; in British English, also called 'monkey nut'
pod of the peanut plant
british

Examples

She picked up a groundnut from the bowl and cracked open its shell.
Groundnuts are often roasted and eaten as a snack in many countries.
Many British people refer to peanuts as groundnuts or monkey nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut - pod or seed of Arachis hypogaea, consumed as food

Etymology

From 'ground' + 'nut', referring to nuts that grow underground.
