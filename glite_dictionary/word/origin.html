origin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
the point or place where something begins, arises, or is derived
starting point; place where something begins

Examples

Scientists are studying the origin of life on Earth.
The painting's origin can be traced to 16th-century Italy.
Many words in English have Latin origin.

Synset Examples

communism's Russian root

Related Terms

  • Synonym: source - the point where something is supplied or originates
  • Synonym: root - the basic cause, source, or origin of something

Etymology

From Latin origo (beginning, source), from oriri (to rise, to begin).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔrədʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪdʒɪn / (BrE)

Examples

In the Cartesian plane, the origin is where the x-axis and y-axis meet.
The coordinates of the origin are always (0, 0) in two-dimensional space.
The graph is centered at the origin to simplify calculations.
