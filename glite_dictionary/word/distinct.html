distinct

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɪŋkt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋkt / (BrE)
clearly different or separate in nature or quality; not the same as something else.
clearly different; separate

Examples

The twins have very distinct personalities and interests.
There is a distinct difference between these two proposals.
Her accent is distinct from any I have heard before.

Synset Examples

plants of several distinct types
the word ‘nationalism’ is used in at least two distinct senses
gold is distinct from iron
a tree related to but quite distinct from the European beech
management had interests quite distinct from those of their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: different - not the same as another; dissimilar
  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance in appearance or nature; not different

Etymology

From Latin 'distinctus', past participle of 'distinguere' (to separate, distinguish).
