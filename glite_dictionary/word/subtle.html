subtle

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌt̬l / (AmE), / ˈsʌtl / (BrE)
Not obvious or easy to detect; difficult to notice, analyze, or understand.
not obvious; difficult to detect or analyze

Examples

There was a subtle shift in her tone that few people noticed.
The artist used subtle shading to give depth to the painting.
Sometimes, the most subtle flavors make a dish memorable.

Synset Examples

his whole attitude had undergone a subtle change
a subtle difference
that elusive thing the soul

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear

Etymology

From Old French *sotil*, from Latin *subtilis* (fine, delicate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌt.əl / (BrE)
(of something harmful) working or spreading in a hidden and usually injurious way; not obvious but potentially damaging.
hidden and injurious (of harm or danger)

Examples

The effects of the subtle toxin were not noticed until weeks later.
Some viruses are extremely subtle, making them hard to detect before symptoms appear.
Corruption can have a subtle influence on an organization, slowly undermining its integrity.

Synset Examples

glaucoma is an insidious disease
a subtle poison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insidious - working or spreading harmfully in a gradual or subtle way
  • Antonym: obvious - easily seen, recognized, or understood; not hidden

Etymology

From Old French 'subtil', from Latin 'subtilis' (fine, delicate, precise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01165950-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌt.əl / (BrE)
able to notice or understand fine differences or distinctions; characterized by keen or nuanced perceptiveness
able to make fine distinctions

Examples

She has a subtle mind that catches the smallest nuances in arguments.
His subtle understanding of literature allows him to appreciate the deeper meanings in poems.
The detective's subtle approach to questioning revealed the truth without alarming anyone.

Synset Examples

a subtle mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • Antonym: crude - lacking subtlety or discrimination

Etymology

From Old French 'subtil', from Latin 'subtilis' (fine, delicate, finely woven).
