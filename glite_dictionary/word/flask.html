flask

1. NOUN

/ flæsk / (AmE), / flɑːsk / (BrE)
A bottle with a narrow neck, typically used for holding liquids, especially in laboratories or for carrying alcoholic drinks.
bottle with a narrow neck

Examples

He poured the chemicals into a flask and placed it on the table.
She carried a small flask of whiskey in her pocket.
The scientist labeled each flask for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle - a container with a narrow neck for storing liquids

Etymology

From Middle English flaske, from Old French flasque, from Late Latin flascā, meaning bottle or flask.

2. NOUN

/ flæsk / (AmE), / flɑːsk / (BrE)
An amount of something contained in a flask.
amount held in a flask

Examples

He offered his friend a flask of brandy to warm him up.
She measured out precisely one flask of solution for the experiment.
After a long hike, they shared a flask of water between them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle - the quantity a bottle can hold

Etymology

From Middle English flaske, from Old French flasque (bottle, flask), from Late Latin flasco (bottle).
