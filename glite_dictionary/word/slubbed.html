slubbed

1. ADJ

/ slʌbd / (AmE), / slʌbd / (BrE)
(of textiles) having a rough or uneven surface, typically caused by thick, irregular threads or yarns.
textiles: rough/uneven surface

Examples

She wore a jacket made from slubbed linen, perfect for summer evenings.
The curtain fabric had a distinctive slubbed appearance that added texture to the room.
Designers often choose slubbed materials for a more natural and tactile feel.

Synset Examples

a sweater knitted of nubbly homespun yarns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nubby - (of fabric) having a coarse, irregular surface
  • Antonym: smooth - (of textiles) having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From 'slub', a lump or thick place in yarn or fabric.
