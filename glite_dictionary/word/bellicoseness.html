bellicoseness

1. NOUN

/ bɛlˈɪkoʊsnɪs / (AmE), / bɛlˈɪkəʊsnɪs / (BrE)
a natural tendency to argue, fight, or be aggressive
tendency to be aggressive or combative

Examples

His bellicoseness often got him into trouble at school.
The candidate's bellicoseness was evident during the heated debate.
Historians have debated the underlying causes of the nation's bellicoseness throughout the century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belligerence - a hostile or warlike attitude or disposition
  • Synonym: aggressiveness - readiness or tendency to attack or confront
  • Antonym: peacefulness - the state of being calm or non-violent

Etymology

From the adjective 'bellicose', from Latin 'bellicosus' (warlike).
