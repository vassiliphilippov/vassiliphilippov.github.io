crossjack

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌdʒæk / (BrE)
the lowermost sail set on the mizzenmast of a square-rigged ship
sail on a ship's mizzenmast

Examples

The sailors quickly lowered the crossjack as the wind began to shift.
Maintenance on the crossjack was scheduled before the next long voyage.
He pointed toward the crossjack, instructing the crew to adjust the ropes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mizzenmast - the aftmost mast of a ship

Etymology

From 'cross' (referring to the cross spar) + 'jack', nautical slang for a small sail.
