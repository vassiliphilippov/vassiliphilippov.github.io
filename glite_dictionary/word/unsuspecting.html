unsuspecting

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈspɛk.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈspɛk.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Not aware of the presence of danger, harm, or deceit; not suspicious.
not suspicious or aware of danger

Examples

Tourists are often easy targets for scammers because they are unsuspecting.
The package was left outside the unsuspecting victim’s home.
She smiled warmly, her expression entirely unsuspecting of any deception.

Synset Examples

deceiving the unsuspecting public

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suspicious - having or showing a cautious distrust of someone or something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'suspecting'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈspɛk.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈspɛk.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not aware of possible danger or harm; not expecting anything bad to happen.
not aware of danger or harm

Examples

The unsuspecting tourist was easily tricked by the street magician.
She handed the envelope to her unsuspecting colleague, who had no idea it was a birthday card.
He entered the dark room, unsuspecting of the surprise waiting for him.

Synset Examples

an unsuspecting victim
unsuspecting (or unaware) of the fact that I would one day be their leader

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suspicious - believing that something is wrong or someone is behaving wrongly
  • Synonym: unaware - not knowing about something

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'suspecting', from Latin 'suspectare' (to look at repeatedly, suspect).
