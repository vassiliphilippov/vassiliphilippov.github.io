cere

1. NOUN

/ sɪər / (AmE), / sɪə / (BrE)
the fleshy, often waxy, covering at the base of the upper beak in certain birds, such as hawks and parrots
waxy area at bird's beak base

Examples

The eagle's cere was bright yellow and shone in the afternoon sun.
In many parrot species, the color of the cere indicates the bird's sex.
A falconer will inspect the cere to assess the health of a raptor.

Etymology

From Latin 'cera' meaning 'wax'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01761278-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ sɪr / (AmE), / sɪə / (BrE)
to wrap a body in a cerecloth, typically for burial
wrap in a cerecloth (for burial)
archaic

Examples

In the ancient ritual, they would cere the body before placing it in the tomb.
The monks cered the corpse with great care, following the old tradition.
Historians discovered evidence that the dead were once cered in waxed cloths.

Synset Examples

cere a corpse

Etymology

From Latin 'cera' (wax), referring to waxed cloth used to wrap bodies.

Synset POS: v
