noble-mindedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊbəlˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌnəʊbəlˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having elevated ideals and moral conduct; a belief in pursuing higher principles and values
elevated ideals or moral conduct

Examples

Her noble-mindedness inspired others to act with integrity and compassion.
The leader's noble-mindedness was evident in his fair and just decisions.
In times of crisis, noble-mindedness can help guide a community toward unity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - the practice of pursuing ideals, sometimes unrealistically
  • Antonym: selfishness - lack of consideration for others; opposite of noble-mindedness

Etymology

From 'noble-minded' (having a noble mind) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
