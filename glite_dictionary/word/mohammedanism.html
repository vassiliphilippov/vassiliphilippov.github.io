Mohammedanism

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌhæməˈdænɪzəm / (AmE), / məˌhæmɪˈdænɪzəm / (BrE)
A former term for the religion of Islam; now considered outdated and often offensive.
former term for Islam (now offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

The term Mohammedanism is found in many 19th-century books but is rarely used today.
Scholars now recognize Mohammedanism as a misnomer for Islam.
Muslims generally dislike the term Mohammedanism, preferring the name Islam instead.

Synset Examples

Islam is a complete way of life, not a Sunday religion
the term Muhammadanism is offensive to Muslims who believe that Allah, not Muhammad, founded their religion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islam - the religion of Muslims, based on the teachings of Muhammad and the Koran

Etymology

From "Mohammedan" (follower of Muhammad) + "-ism".
