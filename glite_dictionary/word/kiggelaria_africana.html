Kiggelaria africana

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪɡəˈlɛriə æˈfrɪkənə / (AmE), / ˌkɪɡɪˈlɑːrɪə æˈfrɪkənə / (BrE)
A large evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa, grown primarily for its dense, evergreen foliage.
evergreen shrub or tree of southern Africa

Examples

The Kiggelaria africana is widely used as a shade tree in South African gardens.
Birds are attracted to the fruit of the Kiggelaria africana throughout the year.
The Kiggelaria africana is known for its dense, evergreen leaves and resilience to drought.

Etymology

Genus name from botanist Franz Kiggelaer; 'africana' meaning 'of Africa' in Latin.
