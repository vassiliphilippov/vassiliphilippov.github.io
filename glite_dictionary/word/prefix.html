prefix

1. VERB

/ ˈpriˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈpriːfɪks / (BrE)
to add a prefix to a word or to place something at the beginning of something else
add a prefix to a word

Examples

You can prefix 'un-' to 'happy' to form 'unhappy'.
The teacher asked students to prefix each word with 'pre-'.
In some cases, you must prefix your application number on the form.

Synset Examples

prefixed words

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suffix - to add a suffix to a word

Etymology

From Latin praefixare, from prae- (before) + figere (to fix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01300547-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈpriːˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈpriː.fɪks / (BrE)
a letter or group of letters added to the beginning of a word to change its meaning
an affix placed at the start of a word

Examples

The prefix 'un-' changes the meaning of 'happy' to its opposite.
When you add the prefix 're-' to 'write', it becomes 'rewrite'.
Knowing the meaning of a prefix can help you understand unfamiliar words.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suffix - an affix added at the end of a word
  • Synonym: affix - a morpheme attached to a word to modify its meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'praefixus', past participle of 'praefigere' (to fix in front), from 'prae-' (before) + 'figere' (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06319294-n
Synset POS: n
