accoucheur

1. NOUN

/ əˌkuːˈʃɜr / (AmE), / əˌkuːˈʃɜː / (BrE)
A physician or male medical professional who specializes in obstetrics and assists with childbirth.
physician specializing in obstetrics
medical

Examples

The accoucheur was called immediately when labor began to ensure a safe delivery.
For centuries, only midwives attended births, but the rise of the accoucheur changed medical practices.
Her family hired an experienced accoucheur to oversee the birth of her child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstetrician - a doctor specializing in pregnancy and childbirth

Etymology

From French 'accoucheur', from 'accoucher' meaning 'to deliver (a baby)'.
