dibucaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪbjuˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɪbjʊkeɪn / (BrE)
a powerful local anesthetic used especially for spinal anesthesia, often administered by injection
local anesthetic (injection)
medical

Examples

The surgeon administered dibucaine before beginning the minor procedure.
Allergic reactions to dibucaine are extremely rare, making it a safe choice.
The anesthetist chose dibucaine for its potency and long duration of action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - a drug that causes a loss of sensation

Etymology

From the International Nonproprietary Name system, based on its chemical structure.
