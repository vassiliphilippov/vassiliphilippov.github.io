ut

1. NOUN

/ ʌt / (AmE), / ʌt / (BrE)
the syllable naming the first (tonic) note of any major scale in solmization, now largely replaced by 'do' in modern usage
first note of a scale (solmization)

Examples

In traditional solmization, ut is used for the first note of the scale.
Music historians note that ut was later replaced by 'do' in the modern system.
The hymn 'Ut queant laxis' provided the syllable ut for music pedagogy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do - the syllable naming the first degree of the major scale in solmization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin, taken from the first syllable of the hymn 'Ut queant laxis' used by Guido d'Arezzo.
