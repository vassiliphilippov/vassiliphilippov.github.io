unhappily

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈhæpɪli / (AmE), / ʌnˈhæpɪli / (BrE)
In an unfortunate or regrettable manner; with sadness or lack of happiness.
in an unfortunate or sad way

Examples

She unhappily accepted the decision, knowing there was nothing she could do to change it.
The couple parted unhappily after years of unresolved conflict.
He unhappily admitted that his plan had failed.

Synset Examples

sadly he died before he could see his grandchild

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadly - in a sad or unfortunate manner
  • Antonym: fortunately - in a lucky or fortunate manner

Etymology

Formed from 'unhappy' + '-ly', following standard English adverbial formation.

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈhæpɪli / (AmE), / ʌnˈhæpɪli / (BrE)
In an unpleasant, unfortunate, or sad manner; not happily.
in an unpleasant or sad way

Examples

She sighed unhappily and turned away from the window.
They were unhappily married for many years before finally separating.
He unhappily accepted the decision, realizing he had no other choice.

Synset Examples

they were unhappily married

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happily - in a happy or pleasant way

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'happily', from 'happy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00051094-r
Synset POS: r
