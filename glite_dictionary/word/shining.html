shining

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something smooth and shiny by rubbing or waxing it
the act of polishing or making shiny

Examples

The shining of the floor took several hours but was worth the effort.
She specializes in the shining of antique brass fixtures.
Regular shining of your shoes keeps them looking new.

Synset Examples

the shining of shoes provided a meager living
every Sunday he gave his car a good polishing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polishing - the act of making something smooth and shiny by rubbing

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
reflecting light; bright or gleaming with reflected light
reflecting light; bright, gleaming

Examples

The shining surface of the lake reflected the clear blue sky.
Her shining hair caught the sunlight as she walked through the park.
After polishing, the silverware was left shining on the table.

Synset Examples

glistening bodies of swimmers

lustrous auburn hair

shining white enamel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - smooth and shiny, reflecting light
  • Synonym: lustrous - having a gentle sheen or soft glow
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny or bright; lacking sheen

Etymology
