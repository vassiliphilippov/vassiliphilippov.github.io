fountain

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntən / (BrE)
A device or structure that sends a stream or jet of water into the air, often as an ornamental feature in a garden or public place, or as a fixture providing water for drinking or decorative purposes.
water feature or device with flowing water

Examples

Children gathered around the fountain in the park to cool off on a hot day.
She filled her bottle at the drinking fountain near the entrance.
The city center features a beautiful stone fountain with lights and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring - a natural source of water flowing from the ground

Etymology

From Old French 'fontaine', from Late Latin 'fontana' (spring, fountain), from Latin 'fontanus', from 'fons' (spring, source).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊn.tən / (AmE), / ˈfaʊn.tɪn / (BrE)
A structure that sends a stream of water into the air, often for decorative purposes, usually in gardens, parks, or public squares.
decorative structure jetting water

Examples

The city square was dominated by a beautiful marble fountain where children often played.
After a long walk in the park, they sat beside the fountain to enjoy the cool spray.
Tourists tossed coins into the fountain, hoping their wishes would come true.

Etymology

From Old French fontaine, from Latin fontana (spring, fountain), from fons (spring, source).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊn.tən / (AmE), / ˈfaʊn.tɪn / (BrE)
A natural spring where water flows from the ground.
natural spring; flow of groundwater

Examples

The ancient village was built near a fountain that provided fresh water all year round.
During the hike, we discovered a hidden fountain emerging between the rocks.
Legends say the fountain at the forest's edge has been flowing for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring - A natural source of water that flows from the ground

Etymology

From Old French 'fontaine' (spring, fountain), from Late Latin 'fontana', from Latin 'fontanus' (of a spring), from 'fons' (spring).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntɪn / (BrE)
A structure or device that produces a stream of water which is often used for decorative or drinking purposes.
artificial structure producing a flow of water

Examples

Tourists gathered around the fountain in the center of the square to take photos.
She tossed a coin into the fountain and made a wish, as tradition suggested.
A beautiful marble fountain was installed outside the museum, drawing crowds every evening.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spring - a natural source of water
  • Synonym: jet - a narrow stream of liquid or gas forced out at high pressure

Etymology

From Old French fonteine, from Latin fontana (spring, fountain), from fons (spring).
