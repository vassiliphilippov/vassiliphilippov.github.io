cauline

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔlɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪn / (BrE)
(of plants) having or producing a well-developed stem above ground.
having a stem above ground (botany)

Examples

The cauline leaves of the sunflower are arranged alternately along the stem.
Botanists classify these species as cauline because of their prominent, upright stems.
Unlike basal leaves, cauline leaves appear higher up on the stem.

Etymology

From Latin 'caulis' (stem or stalk of a plant) + '-ine'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːlɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪn / (BrE)
(of leaves or other plant parts) growing on the stem, especially on the upper part of a stem.
growing on a plant stem
medical

Examples

The plant is distinguished by its cauline leaves that grow along the upper stem.
Some species have only basal leaves, while others produce both basal and cauline leaves.
Botanists observed that the cauline flowers appeared later in the growing season.

Synset Examples

cauline leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'caulis' (stem, stalk).
