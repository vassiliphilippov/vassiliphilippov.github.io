disagreeable

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡriːəbəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːəbl̩ / (BrE)
Unpleasant or not to your liking; causing discomfort or annoyance.
unpleasant; not to one's liking

Examples

The weather turned suddenly disagreeable, forcing us to cancel the picnic.
She found his tone of voice rather disagreeable and decided not to respond.
Cleaning the attic was a disagreeable task, but it had to be done.

Synset Examples

a disagreeable situation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

Formed from prefix dis- (expressing negation) + agreeable, from Old French agreer (to please).

not pleasant; not to your liking

3. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡriəbəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːəbl / (BrE)
unpleasant or unfriendly in manner; causing discomfort or dislike in social interactions
unpleasant in manner or interaction

Examples

She found her new colleague to be quite disagreeable during their first meeting.
The weather turned disagreeable just as we started our picnic, forcing us indoors.
He has a disagreeable habit of interrupting people when they are speaking.

Synset Examples

a disagreeable old man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreeable - pleasant or enjoyable to be with

Etymology

From Old French *desagreable*, from des- (expressing reversal) + agréable ('agreeable').
