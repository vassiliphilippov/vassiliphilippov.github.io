exodontics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.soʊˈdɒn.tɪks / (AmE), / ˌɛks.əʊˈdɒn.tɪks / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry that deals with the extraction of teeth
branch of dentistry for tooth extraction
medical

Examples

After completing his studies in exodontics, he specialized in wisdom tooth extractions.
Advances in exodontics have made tooth removal less painful and more efficient.
The dental student attended a seminar on modern techniques in exodontics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dentistry - the science and practice of preventing and treating diseases of the teeth and gums

Etymology

From Greek 'exo-' (out) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth), with '-ics' denoting a field of study.
