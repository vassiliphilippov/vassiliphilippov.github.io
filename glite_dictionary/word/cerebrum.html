cerebrum

1. NOUN

/ səˈriːbrəm / (AmE), / səˈriːbrəm / (BrE)
the largest part of the brain, located in the front part of the skull, consisting of two hemispheres and responsible for thought, voluntary movement, sensation, and consciousness
largest part of the brain; responsible for thought and movement
medical

Examples

The cerebrum controls higher brain functions such as thought and action.
Damage to the left hemisphere of the cerebrum can affect language abilities.
The cerebrum is divided into two main hemispheres, each with specialized functions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brain - the organ inside the skull that controls thoughts, memory, feelings, and activity
  • Antonym: cerebellum - the part of the brain at the back of the skull, important for coordination and balance

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain).
