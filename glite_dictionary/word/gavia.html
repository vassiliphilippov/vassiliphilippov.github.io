Gavia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪ.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.vɪ.ə / (BrE)
The genus of birds comprising the loons, aquatic birds found in the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of aquatic birds, loons

Examples

The genus Gavia includes all species commonly known as loons.
Researchers observed several members of Gavia nesting on the remote northern lake.
Within the family Gavidae, Gavia is distinguished by its streamlined body and diving ability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - a large aquatic bird of the genus Gavia

Etymology

From Latin 'gavia' (a kind of sea-bird).
