pudendum

1. NOUN

/ puˈdɛndəm / (AmE), / pjuːˈdɛndəm / (BrE)
the external genital organs, especially of a female
external female genital organs
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's pudendum for any signs of infection.
In anatomical texts, the term pudendum often refers specifically to female genitalia.
Some historical medical literature used the word pudendum as a general term for external genital organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulva - external female genitalia

Etymology

From Latin 'pudendum' (external genitals), from 'pudere' (to be ashamed).
