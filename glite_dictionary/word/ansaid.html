Ansaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈæn.saɪd / (BrE)
A trademarked nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain and inflammation; taken orally.
a branded NSAID drug taken orally
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Ansaid to reduce the inflammation in my knee.
After a week on Ansaid, her joint pain began to subside.
Patients should not exceed the recommended dosage of Ansaid without consulting their physician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flurbiprofen - the generic name for the drug Ansaid
