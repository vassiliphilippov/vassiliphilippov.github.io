Joel

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊəl / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that contains the prophecies of Joel.
Old Testament book of prophecies

Examples

The book of Joel describes a devastating locust plague as a metaphor for divine judgment.
In Bible study, we discussed the themes of repentance and restoration found in Joel.
Many scholars analyze how Joel connects natural disasters to spiritual renewal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Obadiah - Old Testament book of prophecies
  • Synonym: Amos - Old Testament prophetic book

Etymology

From Hebrew Yoʾel, meaning 'Yahweh is God'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.əl / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet, the second of the twelve minor prophets in the Old Testament; also, the book of the Bible attributed to him.
Hebrew minor prophet; biblical book

Examples

Joel is known for his prophecies concerning a devastating locust plague in Israel.
The Book of Joel is one of the shortest prophetic books in the Old Testament.
Scholars often discuss the historical background of Joel in theological seminars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Book of Joel - A book of the Old Testament attributed to Joel

Etymology

From Hebrew Yoʾēl, meaning 'Yahweh is God'.

Synset POS: n
