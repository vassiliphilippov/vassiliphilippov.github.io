regur

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪˌɡʊr / (AmE), / ˈreɪɡə / (BrE)
a rich black soil of volcanic origin, found in India and valued for its fertility
black fertile Indian soil

Examples

The Deccan plateau is famous for its regur, which supports the cultivation of cotton.
Farmers in central India rely on regur to yield abundant crops.
The black color of regur comes from its high content of iron and humus.

Etymology

From Marathi 'regur', referring to black cotton soil.
