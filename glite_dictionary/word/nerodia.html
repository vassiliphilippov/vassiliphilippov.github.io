Nerodia

1. NOUN

/ nəˈroʊdiə / (AmE), / nɛˈrəʊdiə / (BrE)
A genus of nonvenomous, primarily aquatic snakes commonly known as water snakes, native to North America.
genus of North American water snakes

Examples

Scientists observed several Nerodia basking on the rocks by the river.
The Nerodia species are often mistaken for venomous snakes due to their coloration.
Herpetologists study the habitat preferences of Nerodia across the southeastern United States.

Etymology

From Greek 'neros' (water) and '-odia', referring to snakes.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈroʊdiə / (AmE), / nɪˈrəʊdiə / (BrE)
A genus of North American nonvenomous water snakes commonly found in aquatic habitats.
genus of North American water snakes

Examples

The Nerodia species are commonly found near lakes and slow-moving rivers.
Researchers observed a Nerodia swimming swiftly through the reeds.
Nerodia are frequently mistaken for venomous snakes due to their appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'neros' (water), used as a scientific genus name.
