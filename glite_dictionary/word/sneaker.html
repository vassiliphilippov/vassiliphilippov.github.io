sneaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsniːkər / (AmE), / ˈsniːkə / (BrE)
A light, comfortable shoe with a flexible rubber sole, typically worn for sports or casual activities.
sport/casual shoe with rubber sole
american

Examples

He put on his new sneakers before heading out for a morning run.
My sneaker got soaked in the rain yesterday and took hours to dry.
You can't wear sneakers to the formal dinner; you need dress shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trainer - a shoe designed for sports or exercise, commonly used in British English

Etymology

American English, from 'sneak' + '-er', because the rubber sole made the wearer move quietly.
