audiocassette

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːdioʊkəˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiəʊkəˈsɛt / (BrE)
A small plastic case containing magnetic tape for recording and playing back sound, used especially from the 1970s to the early 2000s.
cassette containing magnetic audiotape

Examples

He found an old audiocassette of his childhood favorite band in the attic.
The library still loans out music on audiocassette for those who have tape players.
She transferred her lecture recordings from audiocassette to digital audio files.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassette - a small, flat case containing tape for recording or playing back sound or pictures

Etymology

From audio (relating to sound) + cassette (from French, meaning 'small box').
