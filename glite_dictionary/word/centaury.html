centaury

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɔː.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tjʊ.ri / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Centaurea, typically with pink or blue flowers, found in Europe and Asia.
a plant of the genus Centaurea

Examples

The herbalist recommended dried centaury for its potential health benefits.
In the meadow, several species of centaury bloomed in late spring.
She identified the blue-flowered plant as a type of centaury common in Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'centaureum', from Greek 'kentaureion', said to be named after the centaur Chiron in Greek mythology.

/ sɛnˈtɔri / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːri / (BrE)
A small herbaceous plant of the genus Centaurium, typically having pink flowers and used in herbal medicine.
a small flowering plant (Centaurium genus)

Examples

The herbalist recommended using centaury to help with digestive problems.
Clusters of pink centaury brightened the edge of the meadow in summer.

From Latin 'centaurea', said to be named after the centaur Chiron in Greek mythology.
