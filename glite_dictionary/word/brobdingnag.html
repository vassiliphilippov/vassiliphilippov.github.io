Brobdingnag

1. PROPN

/ ˌbrɑːb.dɪŋˈnæɡ / (AmE), / ˌbrɒb.dɪŋˈnaɡ / (BrE)
A fictional land in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels' where everything is of enormous size.
fictional land of giants in Swift's works

Examples

In literature, Brobdingnag is the land where Gulliver encounters people and objects of gigantic proportions.
The novelist described the city as a modern Brobdingnag with towering buildings everywhere.
The wonder exhibit was said to rival Brobdingnag in its colossal designs.

Etymology

Coined by Jonathan Swift in 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726) as the name of a land inhabited by giants.
