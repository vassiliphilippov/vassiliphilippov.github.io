savoy

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɔɪ / (AmE), / səˈvɔɪ / (BrE)
a type of cabbage with a compact head of soft, crinkly leaves, often used in cooking
type of cabbage with crinkly leaves

Examples

We added chopped savoy to the soup for extra texture and flavor.
Farmers say that savoy withstands cold weather better than other cabbages.
She sautéed savoy with garlic and olive oil for a quick side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a round vegetable with green, purple, or white leaves

Etymology

From French "chou de Savoie" (cabbage of Savoy), referring to the Savoy region in France.
