Savoy

1. PROPN

/ səˈvɔɪ / (AmE), / səˈvɔɪ / (BrE)
a historical region in southwestern France, western Switzerland, and northwestern Italy, formerly a duchy of major political importance
historical region and former duchy in Europe

Examples

The House of Savoy played a major role in the unification of Italy.
During the Middle Ages, Savoy was a powerful duchy in Europe.
Many tourists visit the picturesque landscapes of Savoy every year.

Etymology

From French Savoie, from Latin Sapaudia, a region in Gaul.
