to detoxicate

1. VERB

/ diˈtɑksɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / diˈtɒksɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to remove poisonous or harmful substances from something, especially to make it safe or healthy
remove poison or toxins from

Examples

Special chemicals were used to detoxicate the contaminated water.
The treatment helps detoxicate the body of harmful substances.
It can take years to fully detoxicate polluted soil.

Synset Examples

detoxify the soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detoxify - to remove toxins or poison from something

Etymology

From Latin 'toxicum' (poison) + 'de-' (removal) + '-ate' (verb forming suffix).
