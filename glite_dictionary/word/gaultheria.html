Gaultheria

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔlˈθɪəriə / (AmE), / ɡɔːlˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly evergreen shrubs, often creeping or upright, widely distributed and including some ornamental and berry-producing species such as wintergreen
a genus of evergreen shrubs, including wintergreen

Examples

Gaultheria is commonly known for its aromatic leaves and edible berries.
Several species of Gaultheria are cultivated as ornamental plants in gardens.
The most well-known member of the Gaultheria genus is the American wintergreen.

Etymology

Named after Jean-François Gaultier, a French-Canadian botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɔlˈθɪəriə / (AmE), / ɡɔːlˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs, some creeping, widely distributed and known for species such as wintergreen.
genus of evergreen shrubs (including wintergreen)

Examples

Several species within the Gaultheria genus are cultivated for their aromatic leaves and attractive berries.
The most famous member of Gaultheria is probably the eastern teaberry, valued for its wintergreen aroma.
Gardeners often choose Gaultheria plants for ground cover in shaded woodland gardens.

Etymology

Named after Jean-François Gaultier, an 18th-century Canadian botanist.
