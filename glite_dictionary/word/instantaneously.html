instantaneously

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.stənˈteɪ.ni.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stənˈteɪ.ni.əs.li / (BrE)
without any delay; immediately or occurring at once.
immediately, without delay

Examples

The reaction occurred instantaneously when the two chemicals were mixed.
She answered the question instantaneously, impressing everyone with her quick thinking.
The lights went out instantaneously after the thunder struck.

Synset Examples

he was killed outright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without delay; at once
  • Synonym: instantly - immediately; at once
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a long delay

Etymology

From Latin 'instantaneus' (instant), with English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
