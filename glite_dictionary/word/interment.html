interment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜːrmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːmənt / (BrE)
the act or ceremony of placing a dead body in a grave or tomb; burial
burial; placing a body in a grave

Examples

The interment of the former president took place at Arlington National Cemetery.
A brief ceremony was held before the interment began.
The family requested a private interment following the funeral service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burial - the act of burying a dead body

Etymology

From French 'enterrement', from 'enterrer' (to bury), from Latin 'in' (in) + 'terra' (earth).
