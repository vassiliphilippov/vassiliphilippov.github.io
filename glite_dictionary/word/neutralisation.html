neutralisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːtrəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A chemical reaction in which an acid and a base react to form a salt and water, usually by combining hydrogen ions with hydroxide ions.
chemical reaction of acid and base to form salt and water

Examples

Neutralisation occurs when hydrochloric acid is mixed with sodium hydroxide, resulting in salt and water.
The process of neutralisation is important in treating acidic industrial waste before disposal.
In a titration, the endpoint indicates complete neutralisation of the acid by the base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to neutralize - to make a substance neutral or to counteract acidity or alkalinity

Etymology

From Latin 'neutralis' (of neuter gender) + '-ation' forming nouns of action; closely related to 'neutralize'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of making a country politically neutral or ensuring it does not participate in a conflict or war
making a country neutral in conflict

Examples

The treaty called for the neutralisation of the disputed region to prevent future conflict.
International bodies proposed the neutralisation of the canal zone during the negotiations.
The neutralisation of certain territories was a common practice in 19th-century diplomacy.

Synset Examples

the neutralization of Belgium

Etymology

From French 'neutralisation', from 'neutraliser' (to neutralize), from Latin 'neutralis' (of neuter gender or neutral).

3. NOUN

/ ˌnuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(euphemism, especially in military or intelligence contexts) The removal of a threat by killing or destroying it, often in a covert operation.
removal of threat, often by killing (euphemism)

Examples

The military announced the neutralisation of several high-profile targets during the operation.
Officials refused to comment on the neutralisation that took place in the border region.
The term neutralisation is often used to obscure the harsh reality of such missions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elimination - the act of removing or getting rid of something or someone

Etymology

From French 'neutralisation', from neutraliser (to neutralize), from neutre (neutral).

4. NOUN

/ nuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / njuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The action of making something ineffective or nullifying its effect, especially by applying an opposing force or influence.
making something ineffective or nullified

Examples

The neutralisation of the toxin prevents any harm to humans.
They focused on the neutralisation of hostile forces in the area.
His quick response led to the neutralisation of the crisis before it escalated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counteraction - the act of opposing or mitigating an effect
  • Antonym: reinforcement - the act of strengthening or supporting an effect

Etymology

From French 'neutralisation' or from neutral + -isation; ultimately from Latin 'neutralis' (neither one nor the other).
