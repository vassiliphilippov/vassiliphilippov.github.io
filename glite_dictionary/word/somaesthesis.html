somaesthesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊməˈɛsθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsəʊməˈiːsθəsɪs / (BrE)
the faculty of bodily perception, including the sensory systems associated with the skin, muscles, joints, and internal organs
faculty of bodily perception
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervous system can impair somaesthesis, affecting how one perceives touch and pain.
Researchers studied somaesthesis to better understand the body’s awareness of its own position in space.
Yoga practice can heighten somaesthesis by improving one’s awareness of internal bodily sensations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - sense of the position and movement of the body
  • Synonym: kinesthesia - sense of bodily movement

Etymology

From Greek 'sōma' (body) and 'aisthēsis' (perception).
