egger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈɛɡə / (BrE)
A moth of the family Lasiocampidae, whose adults have nonfunctional mouthparts and whose larvae feed on tree foliage and spin egg-shaped cocoons.
a type of moth with egg-shaped cocoons

Examples

The caterpillars of the egger can cause significant damage to tree foliage before entering their pupal stage.
An egger moth was found resting on the oak tree's trunk early this morning.
Many gardeners recognize the egger by its distinctive, egg-shaped cocoon suspended from branches.

Etymology

Probably from the shape of the cocoon spun by the larva, resembling an egg.
