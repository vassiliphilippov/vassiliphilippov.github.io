certification

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.t̬ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an official document that states that particular facts are true or that someone has achieved a required standard
official attesting document

Examples

She received a certification confirming her completion of the training program.
The contractor submitted a certification of insurance as part of the requirements.
Employers often ask for copies of your professional certifications during the hiring process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate - an official document attesting a fact

Etymology

From Medieval Latin certificatio, from certificare (to certify).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.t̬ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
official confirmation that something is true or meets certain standards, typically through documentary evidence
official confirmation through documents

Examples

He received certification for completing the safety training program.
The new policy requires all employees to show certification of their qualifications.
Without official certification, the product cannot be sold in the European market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate - an official document that attests to a fact or qualification

Etymology

From Medieval Latin certificatio (making certain), from certificare ('to make certain').

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of verifying or officially recognizing that something or someone is genuine or meets certain standards
official validation; attestation

Examples

The jeweler provided a certification of the diamond's authenticity.
You need official certification before you can start working as a lifeguard.
The company is awaiting certification from the government to launch its new product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authentication - the process of proving something is genuine or valid

Etymology

From Medieval Latin certificatio, from certificare (to certify).

4. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.tə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the official act of confirming or granting formal approval, status, or qualification, especially by an authorized body
official confirmation or granting of status

Examples

The university requires certification of your qualifications before admission to the program.
He received certification as a licensed electrician after passing the exams.
Official certification is necessary before a business can operate as a franchise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - the act of giving official permission or approval
  • Antonym: disqualification - the act of making someone ineligible or removing official approval

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'certificatio' (attestation), from Latin 'certificare' (to certify).
