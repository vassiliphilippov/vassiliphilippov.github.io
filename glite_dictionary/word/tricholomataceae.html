Tricholomataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkoʊˌloʊməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkələʊməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Agaricales, consisting of white-, yellow-, or pink-spored genera not classified in other related families such as Amanitaceae or Lepiotaceae.
a family of fungi in Agaricales

Examples

Many wild mushrooms found in forests belong to the Tricholomataceae family.
The Tricholomataceae are distinguished by their white or pale-colored spores.
Some genera within the Tricholomataceae are important for both culinary and ecological reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricales - an order of fungi containing gilled mushrooms
  • Synonym: Amanitaceae - a related family of fungi in Agaricales

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tricholoma + suffix -aceae, denoting a family.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkoʊˌloʊməˈteɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkələʊməˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of fungi within the order Agaricales, including various genera that have white, yellow, or pink spores and do not belong to Amanitaceae, Lepiotaceae, Hygrophoraceae, Pluteaceae, or Entolomataceae.
family of fungi in the order Agaricales
medical

Examples

Several edible mushrooms belong to the Tricholomataceae family, making them of interest to foragers.
Recent studies have led to the reclassification of some genera previously included in the Tricholomataceae.
Mycologists debate the boundaries of the Tricholomataceae due to its diverse characteristics.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Tricholoma (from Greek 'trichos' meaning hair and 'loma' meaning fringe) + -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names.
