Oxalis corniculata

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈzælɪs kɔrnɪˈkjuleɪtə / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs kɔːnɪˈkjuːlɑːtə / (BrE)
A creeping, mat-forming plant of the wood-sorrel family, found worldwide; often considered a weed in gardens and lawns.
a cosmopolitan creeping weed (wood-sorrel)

Examples

The garden was quickly overrun by Oxalis corniculata, making it difficult to maintain the flower beds.
You can identify Oxalis corniculata by its trifoliate leaves and tiny yellow flowers.
Many gardeners struggle to control the spread of Oxalis corniculata on their lawns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping woodsorrel - common name for Oxalis corniculata

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Oxalis' means 'sour' (referring to the acidic taste), and 'corniculata' means 'with little horns'.
