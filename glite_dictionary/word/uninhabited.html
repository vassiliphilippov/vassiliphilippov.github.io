uninhabited

1. ADJ

/ ʌnɪnˈhæbɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnɪnˈhæbɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) having no people living there; deserted.
having no people living there

Examples

The uninhabited house stood silent at the end of the road.
After the evacuation, the village became completely uninhabited.
They explored an uninhabited island in the Pacific.

Synset Examples

an uninhabited island
gaping doors of uninhabited houses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhabited - (of a place) with people living there
  • Synonym: deserted - (of a place) abandoned, with no people

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'inhabited', from Latin 'inhabitare' (to dwell in).
