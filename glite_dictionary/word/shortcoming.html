shortcoming

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
A fault or weakness that makes someone or something less effective or adequate.
a fault or deficiency

Examples

The report failed to address the most serious shortcoming of the current system.
Despite his many talents, he is well aware of his own shortcomings.
Every plan has a shortcoming, but that does not mean it cannot be improved.

Synset Examples

that interpretation is an unfortunate defect of our lack of information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deficiency - the state of lacking or being inadequate
  • Synonym: fault - a flaw, defect, or imperfection
  • Antonym: strength - a positive quality or attribute

Etymology

From the verb 'short' + 'coming', meaning a coming up short, i.e., a failure to reach a standard.
