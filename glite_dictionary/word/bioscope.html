bioscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əˌskəʊp / (BrE)
(in South Africa) a movie theater; a cinema.
South African movie theater
british

Examples

We went to the bioscope last night to see the new comedy film.
The old bioscope in town is now considered a historic building.
As children, weekends meant a trip to the bioscope for an afternoon matinee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinema - a movie theater, especially in British English
  • Synonym: movie theater - a theater where films are shown, American English

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) + 'skopein' (to look), originally used for early motion picture projectors.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an early type of movie projector used to display moving pictures, especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries
early type of movie projector

Examples

At the exhibition, a restored bioscope was used to show silent films from the 1900s.
Early cinema audiences were fascinated by the images projected by the bioscope.
The museum's collection includes a rare 19th-century bioscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projector - a device that projects an image onto a surface

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -scope (view, instrument for viewing).
