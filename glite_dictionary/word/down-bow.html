down-bow

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌbəʊ / (BrE)
a downward stroke of the bow, moving from the frog (heel) to the tip when playing a stringed instrument
downward stroke of a stringed instrument's bow

Examples

The violinist played the opening notes with a down-bow for a strong, accented sound.
It is important to control your down-bow to maintain a smooth tone throughout the piece.
The sheet music indicates a down-bow at the start of each phrase.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: up-bow - an upward stroke of the bow from tip to heel
