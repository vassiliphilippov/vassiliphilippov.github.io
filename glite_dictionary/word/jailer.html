jailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlə / (BrE)
a person in charge of guarding prisoners in a jail or prison
person who guards prisoners

Examples

The jailer unlocked the cell door for the morning inspection.
After years as a jailer, he knew every corner of the prison.
The jailer made sure all the inmates were accounted for before turning in for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch, especially over prisoners or property
  • Antonym: prisoner - a person who is kept in prison

Etymology

From Old French *jaioleor*, from *jaiole* (cage, prison), ultimately from Latin *cavea* (cage).
