Turdus iliacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrdəs ɪˈlaɪəkəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs ɪˈlaɪəkəs / (BrE)
A small European thrush, commonly known as the redwing, with reddish flanks and a creamy stripe above the eye.
small European thrush (redwing)

Examples

During migration, large flocks of Turdus iliacus are often observed in the British countryside.
The Turdus iliacus is recognized by its distinctive reddish flanks and creamy eyebrow stripe.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Turdus iliacus in the city park last winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwing - common name for Turdus iliacus, a small Eurasian thrush

Etymology

From Latin: 'Turdus' meaning 'thrush', 'iliacus' referring to the flanks (ilium).
