deviltry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəltri / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəltri / (BrE)
reckless or malicious behavior that causes discomfort, annoyance, or harm to others; mischief or wickedness
malicious mischief or wickedness

Examples

The children's deviltry kept the neighbors awake all night.
She was known for her clever deviltry and playful pranks.
Such blatant deviltry was not tolerated by the strict headmaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking, especially in children
  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Middle English 'develtrie', from 'devil' + '-try'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəltri / (AmE), / ˈdɛvltri / (BrE)
Wicked or cruel behavior; actions or conduct intended to harm or cause mischief.
wicked or cruel behavior

Examples

The villagers were frightened by tales of deviltry in the old forest.
His actions were not harmless pranks but calculated deviltry.
Legends spoke of the deviltry committed by the mysterious stranger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devilry - wicked or mischievous behavior
  • Antonym: goodness - the quality of being morally good or virtuous

Etymology

Alteration of 'devilry', from 'devil' + '-ry'.
