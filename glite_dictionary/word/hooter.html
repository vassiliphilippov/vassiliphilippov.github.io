british

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈhuːtər / (AmE), / ˈhuːtə / (BrE)
A nocturnal bird of prey, such as an owl, with a hawk-like beak and claws and a large head with front-facing eyes.
nocturnal bird of prey (owl)
informal

Examples

At dusk, a solitary hooter called from the oak tree behind our house.
We heard a hooter in the forest and looked up to see an owl perched on a branch.
Local legends often feature a wise old hooter watching over the village at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - nocturnal bird of prey with a flat face and large forward-facing eyes
