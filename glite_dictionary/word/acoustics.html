acoustics

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːstɪks / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪks / (BrE)
The scientific study of the physical properties of sound, including its production, transmission, and effects.
study of sound properties

Examples

She is studying acoustics at university to specialize in concert hall design.
The acoustics of the new auditorium were praised by the musicians and audience alike.
Advances in acoustics have improved the sound quality in modern theaters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonics - the science of sound waves, especially their generation and propagation
  • Antonym: optics - the scientific study of light and vision

Etymology

From Greek akoustikos (relating to hearing), from akouein (to hear).
