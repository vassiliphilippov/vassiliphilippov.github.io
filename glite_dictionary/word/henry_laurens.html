Henry Laurens

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈlɔrənz / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈlɔːrənz / (BrE)
(1724–1792), American statesman who served as president of the Continental Congress during the American Revolution.
American revolutionary leader and politician

Examples

Henry Laurens played a pivotal role in guiding the Continental Congress through turbulent times.
The letters of Henry Laurens provide important insights into the challenges of the American Revolution.
Few know that Henry Laurens was captured by the British and held in the Tower of London.
