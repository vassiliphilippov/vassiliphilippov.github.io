cloak

1. NOUN

/ kloʊk / (AmE), / kləʊk / (BrE)
a loose outer garment, often sleeveless and fastening at the neck, that hangs down the back and covers the body
a loose outer garment

Examples

The magician swept his cloak around his shoulders before stepping onto the stage.
She wore a thick woolen cloak to keep warm on the cold winter night.
A shadowy figure in a dark cloak emerged from the alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cape - a sleeveless outer garment that hangs from the shoulders

Etymology

From Old French *cloque*, from Medieval Latin *clocca* (bell, cloak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03049322-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kloʊk / (AmE), / kləʊk / (BrE)
to cover or conceal with or as if with a cloak; to hide or disguise something
to cover or conceal

Examples

The spy tried to cloak his true intentions during the meeting.
Heavy fog cloaked the city, making it difficult to see the buildings.
Her calm demeanor cloaks a keen sense of anxiety.

Synset Examples

cloaked monks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - to hide the true appearance or nature of something
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make known or visible something that was hidden

Etymology

From Old French 'cloque' (traveling cloak), from Medieval Latin 'clocca' (cloak, bell).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

/ kloʊk / (AmE), / kləʊk / (BrE)

cover or conceal as if with clothing

Examples

The organization's true purpose was cloaked in secrecy and rumors.

4. NOUN

/ kloʊk / (AmE), / kləʊk / (BrE)
something that covers or conceals, often to hide the true nature of something
something that conceals or hides

Examples

Corruption was carried out under the cloak of official government business.
The mystery was hidden behind a cloak of silence and secrecy.
The committee acted under the cloak of anonymity to protect its members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that lies over or hides something else
  • Synonym: mask - something that hides or disguises
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make known or visible

Etymology

From Old French 'cloque' (travelling cloak), from Medieval Latin 'clocca' (cloak, bell).

5. VERB

/ kloʊk / (AmE), / kləʊk / (BrE)
to hide or cover something, especially true feelings, motives, or facts, under a false appearance
hide or disguise under a false appearance

Examples

She tried to cloak her nervousness with a polite smile.
The company's true intentions were cloaked in vague promises and empty statements.
He cloaked his anger by pretending to be indifferent.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask - to conceal something, especially emotions, by pretending otherwise
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make known or visible; to show something hidden

Etymology

From Old French *cloque* (cloak, bell), from Medieval Latin *clocca* (bell, cloak).
