Borago

1. NOUN

/ bəˈreɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / bəˈreɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs native to the Mediterranean region, commonly known as borage, with star-shaped blue flowers and rough, hairy leaves
genus of Mediterranean perennial herbs

Examples

Borago is frequently grown in gardens for its edible flowers and leaves.
The most well-known species of Borago is Borago officinalis, commonly called borage.
Herbalists sometimes use Borago extracts for their purported medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borage - an annual herb of the Borago genus often used in salads or as a garnish

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Borago, the genus name, of uncertain origin.
