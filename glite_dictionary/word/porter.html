porter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a dark, sweet variety of beer made from roasted unmalted barley, known for its rich flavor
dark, sweet style of beer

Examples

He ordered a pint of porter to go with his meal.
The brewery is famous for its rich, chocolatey porter.
Many craft beer enthusiasts enjoy a porter during the colder months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stout - a strong, dark beer similar to porter

Etymology

From Old French porteur (carrier); the drink was popular among porters (carriers) in London.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a person employed to carry luggage and other loads, especially at a hotel, railway station, or airport.
person who carries luggage

Examples

The porter offered to carry our bags up to the hotel room.
At the train station, a porter helped me with my heavy suitcases.
We tipped the porter generously for his excellent service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellhop - a hotel employee who carries luggage for guests

Etymology

From Old French porteur, from Latin portare (to carry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10475013-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a person stationed at a door or entrance to guard it or admit people; a doorkeeper or gatekeeper
person who guards or manages an entrance

Examples

The porter stopped us at the entrance to check our identification.
A uniformed porter guarded the main door of the building.
As a porter, she made sure only authorized visitors entered the estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorkeeper - a person who guards or manages a door or entrance
  • Synonym: gatekeeper - a person who controls access at a gate

Etymology

From Old French *portier*, from Latin *portarius*, from *porta* (gate).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a railroad employee who assists passengers, especially by attending to their needs on sleeping cars
railroad employee assisting sleeping car passengers

Examples

The porter greeted passengers as they boarded the sleeping car.
She tipped the porter for helping with her luggage on the overnight train.
The experienced porter made sure every passenger was comfortable before departure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendant - a person who serves or assists others, especially in public places or transportation
  • Synonym: conductor - a railroad employee in charge of a train and its passengers

Etymology

From Old French 'portier' (gatekeeper, doorkeeper), from late Latin 'portarius', from 'porta' (gate, door).
