bonobo

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnoʊboʊ / (AmE), / bəˈnəʊbəʊ / (BrE)
a small, intelligent species of chimpanzee native to the forests of the Democratic Republic of Congo, closely related to the common chimpanzee
species of small African chimpanzee

Examples

The bonobo is known for its peaceful social structure and strong female bonds.
Researchers have observed bonobos using tools and displaying empathy towards each other.
Several conservation organizations are working to protect the endangered bonobo in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - a great ape native to Africa and closely related to the bonobo
  • Degree Higher: gorilla - a large great ape native to Africa

Etymology

From a supposed place name Bolobo (in the area of discovery) in Congo.
