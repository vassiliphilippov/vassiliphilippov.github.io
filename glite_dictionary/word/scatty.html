scatty

1. ADJ

/ ˈskæti / (AmE), / ˈskæti / (BrE)
(British, informal) Easily distracted, absent-minded, or scatterbrained; showing a lack of focus or concentration.
absent-minded; scatterbrained (Brit. informal)
informal british

Examples

My scatty sister forgot her keys at home again this morning.
He gave a rather scatty answer and seemed lost in thought during the meeting.
People say she’s a bit scatty, but she’s actually quite clever beneath it all.

Synset Examples

an absent stare
an absentminded professor
the scatty glancing quality of a hyperactive but unfocused intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatterbrained - having a disorganized or unfocused mind
  • Synonym: absent-minded - frequently forgetting things; not paying attention

2. ADJ

/ ˈskæti / (AmE), / ˈskæti / (BrE)
(informal, British) Absent-minded, disorganized, or lacking in sense; scatterbrained.
scatterbrained; absent-minded (British informal)
informal british

Examples

She can be a bit scatty at times, always forgetting where she left her keys.
The scatty professor arrived late, papers spilling from his briefcase.
His scatty comments made it hard to follow the meeting.

Synset Examples

his rattlebrained crackpot ideas
how rattlepated I am! I've forgotten what I came for

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatterbrained - lacking concentration; disorganized in thought

Etymology

British slang, probably from 'scatter-brained' or 'scattered'.
