Alosa pseudoharengus

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊsə ˌsjuːdoʊˈhɛrɪŋɡəs / (AmE), / əˈləʊsə ˌsjuːdəʊˈhɛrɪŋɡəs / (BrE)
A species of shad-like fish, known as the alewife, that migrates into rivers to spawn and is often used as food, typically salted or smoked.
species of shad-like fish (alewife)

Examples

The sudden increase of Alosa pseudoharengus in the lake impacted the local ecosystem.
Commercial fishermen sought Alosa pseudoharengus during the spring spawning migration.
Traditionally, Alosa pseudoharengus was salted and smoked to preserve it for consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alewife - common name for Alosa pseudoharengus, a shad-like fish

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Alosa' (genus name from French 'alose' shad), 'pseudoharengus' from Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + Latin 'harengus' (herring).
