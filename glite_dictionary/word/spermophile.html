spermophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɜːrməˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈspɜːmɪˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a ground-dwelling rodent of the genus Spermophilus, found in the Old and New Worlds, known for its burrowing habits and often considered an agricultural pest
burrowing ground squirrel-like rodent

Examples

The spermophile is known for digging complex burrows in open fields.
Farmers often consider the spermophile a pest because it can damage crops.
Several species of spermophile are native to both Europe and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground squirrel - a burrowing rodent closely related to spermophiles

Etymology

From Greek *sperma* (seed) + *philos* (loving), referring to its diet.
