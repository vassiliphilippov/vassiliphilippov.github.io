azathioprine

1. NOUN

/ ˌæz.əˈθaɪ.oʊˌpriːn / (AmE), / ˌæz.əˈθaɪ.əʊˌpriːn / (BrE)
an immunosuppressive drug used to prevent organ rejection after transplantation and treat autoimmune diseases
immunosuppressive drug for transplants
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed azathioprine to help prevent organ rejection after the kidney transplant.
Patients taking azathioprine must have their blood monitored regularly for side effects.
Azathioprine is sometimes used to manage symptoms in autoimmune conditions like lupus.

Etymology

From aza- (chemical prefix indicating nitrogen substitution) + thioprine, from 'thio-' (sulfur-containing) and 'purine'.
