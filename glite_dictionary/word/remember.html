to remember

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmɛmbɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
to show appreciation or kindness to someone, especially by giving a gift or making a provision for them (such as in a will)
show appreciation, often by a gift or bequest

Examples

He generously remembered his old friends in his will.
The company remembered loyal employees with a holiday bonus.
She made sure to remember her former teacher by sending a thank-you gift.

Synset Examples

He remembered her in his will

2. VERB

/ rɪˈmɛmbɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
to be able to bring a fact or event back into your mind, or to retain knowledge from the past
to recall or retain information from memory

Examples

After many years, she could still remember her childhood home in vivid detail.
Not everyone can remember all the names from yesterday’s meeting.
He struggled to remember where he had parked the car.

Synset Examples

After the shelling, many people lost the ability to remember
some remember better than others

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to forget - to be unable to recall information or fail to remember
  • Synonym: recall - to remember something; to bring a fact back into your mind

Etymology

From Latin rememorari (to recall to mind), from re- (again) + memor (mindful).
