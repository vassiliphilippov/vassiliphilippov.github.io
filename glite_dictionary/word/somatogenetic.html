somatogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsoʊˌmæt̬əˈdʒɛnətɪk / (AmE), / ˌsəʊˌmatəˈdʒɛnɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease, disorder, or condition) originating from physiological causes as opposed to psychological origins.
originating from bodily (not psychological) causes
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist supported a somatogenetic explanation for the patient's symptoms, focusing on biological rather than emotional factors.
Recent research challenges the purely somatogenetic model of depression, suggesting a complex interplay of mental and physical causes.
Some disorders once thought to be psychogenic are now considered somatogenetic after advances in neuroscience.

Synset Examples

somatogenic theories of schizophrenia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: psychogenic - originating from psychological rather than physiological causes

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) + -genetic (producing, origin).
