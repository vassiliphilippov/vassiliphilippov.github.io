malted

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔltɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːltɪd / (BrE)
(of grain) having been converted into malt by soaking, germinating, and drying, often used in brewing and distilling.
converted into malt

Examples

The brewery uses only malted barley in its traditional recipes.
Chocolate made with malted milk powder has a distinctive flavor.
Many whiskies are distilled from malted barley for a richer taste.

Synset Examples

malted barley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malt - grain, especially barley, that has been germinated and dried for use in brewing or distilling

Etymology

From past participle of the verb 'malt.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔltɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːltɪd / (BrE)
A milkshake made with milk, ice cream, and malt powder, often sweetened and flavored, popular especially in the United States.
milkshake with malt powder
american

Examples

Whenever I go to the diner, I always order a chocolate malted with my burger.
She prefers a malted over a regular milkshake because of its rich, nutty flavor.
They shared a vanilla malted after the movie, sipping it through two straws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkshake - a sweet, cold drink made of milk and ice cream mixed together, often with flavorings

Etymology

From 'malt,' referring to malted barley, with the term used for drinks containing malt powder.
