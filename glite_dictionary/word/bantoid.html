Bantoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæn.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈbæn.tɔɪd / (BrE)
Relating to or designating a group of African languages that share characteristics with Bantu.
relating to a group of African Bantu-like languages

Examples

The linguist specialized in Bantoid languages and their historical development.
Certain Bantoid tongues retain unique phonetic features distinct from standard Bantu.
The conference included a session on the structure of Bantoid dialects.

Synset Examples

Bantoid languages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu - relating to a large group of African languages spoken in central and southern Africa

Etymology

From 'Bantu' (a major African language group) + '-oid' (resembling or like).
