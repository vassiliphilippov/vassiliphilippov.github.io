Panax pseudoginseng

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.æks ˌsuː.doʊˈdʒɪn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpæn.æks ˌsjuː.dəʊˈdʒɪn.sɪŋ / (BrE)
A species of ginseng native to China, with palmately compound leaves, small greenish flowers, and forked aromatic roots that are believed to have medicinal properties.
Chinese herb with medicinal roots

Examples

Traditional Chinese medicine often uses Panax pseudoginseng to promote circulation and treat injuries.
The roots of Panax pseudoginseng are valued for their purported healing properties.
Researchers are studying Panax pseudoginseng for its potential benefits in cardiovascular health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginseng - a plant of the genus Panax with medicinal roots

Etymology

From Latin: 'Panax' meaning 'all-healing' and 'pseudoginseng' meaning 'false ginseng'.
