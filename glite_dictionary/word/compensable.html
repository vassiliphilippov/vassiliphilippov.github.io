compensable

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛnsəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of work or loss) eligible for or entitled to receive monetary compensation.
entitled to compensation

Examples

The injury was deemed compensable under workers' compensation law.
Only compensable hours will be counted toward your weekly wage.
She filed a claim for compensable damages after the accident.

Synset Examples

a paying job
remunerative work
salaried employment
stipendiary services

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remunerative - giving a financial reward; profitable
  • Antonym: uncompensated - not receiving or entitled to compensation

Etymology

From Latin compensare (to weigh together, counterbalance) + -able.
