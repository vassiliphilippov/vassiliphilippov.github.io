to visualize

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something visible, especially to the eye or through technical means; to render perceptible
make visible; render perceptible

Examples

With this new imaging software, doctors can visualize organs in remarkable detail.
The special dye helps visualize blood flow in the brain during the scan.
Advanced telescopes allow astronomers to visualize distant galaxies previously hidden from view.

Synset Examples

With this machine, ultrasound can be visualized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something known or visible that was hidden
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something from being seen; to hide

Etymology

From Latin visus (sight) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00582282-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
(Medicine) To make the outline of an internal body structure visible using X-rays or similar imaging techniques.
make visible with X-rays (med.)
medical

Examples

The technician was able to visualize the blockage in the artery with a special X-ray scan.
Doctors visualized the tumor in her kidney using advanced imaging techniques.
A contrast agent is sometimes used to help visualize blood vessels during the procedure.

Synset Examples

The radiologist can visualize the cancerous liver

Etymology

From Latin 'visualis' (of sight) + English '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02155824-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to form a mental image of something; to imagine what something looks like in the mind
form a mental image; imagine

Examples

She tried to visualize the house based on his description.
Athletes often visualize themselves succeeding before a competition.
Can you visualize the solution to this problem in your mind?

Synset Examples

I can't see him on horseback!
I can see what will happen
I can see a risk in this strategy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something
  • Synonym: to envision - to picture mentally, especially some future event or events
  • Antonym: to forget - to be unable to remember or imagine in the mind

Etymology

From French 'visualiser', from 'visuel' (visual).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01638974-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to form a mental image of something that is not visible or is abstract
form a mental image

Examples

She tried to visualize her dream house in every detail.
Athletes often visualize success before a big competition.
It can help to visualize your goals as clearly as possible.

Synset Examples

Mathematicians often visualize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - form a mental image or concept of something

Etymology

From French 'visualiser', from 'visuel' (visual), ultimately from Latin 'videre' (to see).
