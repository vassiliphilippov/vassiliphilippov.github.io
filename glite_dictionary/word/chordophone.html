chordophone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəˌfəʊn / (BrE)
a musical instrument that produces sound by means of vibrating strings, such as harps, lutes, lyres, and zithers
stringed musical instrument

Examples

The violin is a well-known example of a chordophone used in classical music.
Recent archaeological finds have revealed ancient chordophones dating back thousands of years.
Unlike wind instruments, a chordophone produces sound by vibrating strings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: string instrument - a musical instrument that produces sound from vibrating strings

Etymology

From Greek 'chordē' (string) + 'phōnē' (voice, sound).
