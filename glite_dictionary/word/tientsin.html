Tientsin

1. PROPN

/ tiˈɛn(t)sɪn / (AmE), / tiˈɛn(t)sɪn / (BrE)
a major industrial city in northeastern China, now officially called Tianjin; an alternative historical spelling often used in English sources.
old spelling for the city of Tianjin, China

Examples

Many foreign embassies were once located in Tientsin during the late Qing dynasty.
Trade flourished through the port of Tientsin in the early 20th century.
The city's official name changed from Tientsin to Tianjin after reforms in romanization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tianjin - current official name for the city formerly spelled Tientsin

Etymology

From Chinese 天津 (Tiānjīn), formerly transliterated as Tientsin in English.
