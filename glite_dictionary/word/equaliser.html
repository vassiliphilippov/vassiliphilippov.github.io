equaliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a weight or device used to balance another weight, especially in mechanical systems to produce equilibrium
a balancing weight or device
british

Examples

A counterweight serves as an equaliser to keep the platform steady.
The old clock uses an equaliser to ensure balanced movement of the hands.
In the scale, a small lead bar was added as an equaliser for accuracy.

Etymology

From equal + -iser (British spelling; also spelled 'equalizer' in American English).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪzə / (BrE)
an electronic device that adjusts or reduces frequency distortion to achieve a desired sound profile, especially in audio systems
device for adjusting audio frequencies

Examples

The sound engineer adjusted the equaliser to balance the bass and treble during the concert.
Modern car stereos often come with a built-in equaliser to improve audio quality.
Without a proper equaliser, the speakers produce a distorted sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equalizer - Alternate spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'equalise' + '-er', indicating a device that makes frequencies equal.
