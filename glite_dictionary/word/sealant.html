sealant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːlənt / (AmE), / ˈsiːlənt / (BrE)
a substance used to block the passage of fluids through the surface or joints of materials, forming a hard protective coating
material used to seal surfaces or joints

Examples

After installing the new tiles, she applied a sealant to prevent water from seeping through the grout.
The wood deck was treated with a weatherproof sealant to extend its lifespan.
Before painting, he used a sealant to ensure the surface was properly prepared and protected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealer - a substance used to seal surfaces, especially before painting

Etymology

From 'seal' + '-ant', denoting an agent or substance; modeled on 'seal' (to close tightly).
