paragonite

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈɡoʊˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌparəˈɡəʊnaɪt / (BrE)
a colorless or pale brown mica mineral containing sodium, commonly found in metamorphic rocks
a sodium-rich mica mineral
medical

Examples

The rock sample contained crystals of paragonite intergrown with other micas.
Geologists identified paragonite by its pale brown color and sodium content.
Under the microscope, the thin section revealed flakes of paragonite among the metamorphic minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a group of silicate minerals with layered structure, known for their sheet-like forms

Etymology

From German Paragonit, from Greek paragon (a kind of clay) + -ite (mineral suffix).
