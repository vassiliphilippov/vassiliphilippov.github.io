Emberiza hortulana

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˌbɛrɪzə hɔrˈtʊlənə / (AmE), / ɛmˌbɛrɪzə hɔːˈtʊlənə / (BrE)
A small brownish Old World bunting, commonly known as the ortolan, sometimes eaten as a delicacy.
a brownish bunting bird, the ortolan

Examples

The Emberiza hortulana is protected by law in several countries due to declining populations.
Gourmets have historically regarded the Emberiza hortulana as a rare delicacy.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a Emberiza hortulana in the meadow during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ortolan - common name for Emberiza hortulana, a small bunting bird

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Emberiza' from Medieval Latin for bunting, and 'hortulana' meaning 'of the garden'.
