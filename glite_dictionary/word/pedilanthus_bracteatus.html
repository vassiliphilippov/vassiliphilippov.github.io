Pedilanthus bracteatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdɪˈlænθəs brækˈtiːətəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛdɪˈlanθəs brækˈtiːətəs / (BrE)
A wax-coated shrub native to Mexico, related to Euphorbia antisyphilitica and known for its thick, succulent stems.
Mexican wax-coated shrub related to Euphorbia

Examples

The Pedilanthus bracteatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique waxy stems.
Gardeners in arid regions sometimes choose Pedilanthus bracteatus because it thrives in dry conditions.
Some traditional practices use the wax from Pedilanthus bracteatus as a natural coating for various items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphorbia antisyphilitica - A related wax-producing shrub native to Mexico and the southwestern United States

Etymology

From Latin, 'pedilanthus' meaning 'slipper flower' and 'bracteatus' meaning 'having bracts'.
