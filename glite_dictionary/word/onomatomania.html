onomatomania

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnəˌmætoʊˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˌmatəʊˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
An obsession with a particular word or words, often resulting in their repeated use or persistent intrusion into a person's thoughts.
obsession with a particular word
medical

Examples

Her onomatomania caused her to repeat the same unusual word for days, unable to get it out of her mind.
Sufferers of onomatomania often find particular words intruding upon their consciousness involuntarily.
The linguist described his mild onomatomania as an endless looping of favorite terms in his thoughts.

Etymology

From Greek onoma (name, word) + mania (madness, obsession).
