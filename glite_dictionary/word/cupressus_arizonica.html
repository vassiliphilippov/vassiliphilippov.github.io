Cupressus arizonica

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈprɛsəs ˌærɪˈzoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs ˌærɪˈzəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
a species of cypress tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its bluish silvery foliage and use as timber
a southwestern North American cypress tree

Examples

The Cupressus arizonica thrives in dry, rocky soils throughout Arizona and northern Mexico.
Many landscapers choose Cupressus arizonica for its attractive silvery foliage and drought resistance.
The wood from Cupressus arizonica is valued for its durability in outdoor construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arizona cypress - common name for Cupressus arizonica

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cupressus' (cypress) and 'arizonica', referring to the U.S. state of Arizona where the species is native.
