Crinoidea

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈnoɪdiə / (AmE), / kraɪˈnɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A class of marine animals in the phylum Echinodermata, commonly known as sea lilies or feather stars, characterized by a cup-shaped body and feathery arms.
class of marine echinoderms (sea lilies, feather stars)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species within the Crinoidea during the deep-sea expedition.
Fossilized Crinoidea are frequently found in Paleozoic sedimentary rocks.
The distinctive feathery arms of Crinoidea help them capture plankton from the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lily - a common name for crinoids, marine animals with long stalks and feathery arms
  • Degree Higher: Echinodermata - the phylum of marine animals including sea stars, sea urchins, and crinoids

Etymology

From Greek 'krinon' (lily) + '-eidos' (form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ kraɪnoʊˈiːdiə / (AmE), / kraɪnɔɪˈiːdɪə / (BrE)
A class of marine animals, known as sea lilies or feather stars, characterized by a cup-shaped body and feathery arms.
a class of marine animals (sea lilies)

Examples

The fossil record shows that Crinoidea have existed since the Paleozoic era.
Many species within Crinoidea are commonly called feather stars or sea lilies.
Scientists study Crinoidea to understand ancient marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lily - a common name for members of class Crinoidea

Etymology

From Greek 'krinon' (lily) + 'eidos' (form, shape).
