reshuffle

1. NOUN

/ riˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / riˈʃʌfəl / (BrE)
an act of reorganizing or redistributing, especially the positions of people within an organization or government
reorganization of positions or roles

Examples

After the election, the prime minister announced a reshuffle of the cabinet.
The company underwent a major reshuffle to improve efficiency.
There will be a reshuffle of staff next month to address changing business needs.

Synset Examples

there was a reshuffle of cabinet officers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reorganization - the act of organizing something again
  • Synonym: shake-up - a thorough restructuring or reorganization

Etymology

From re- (again) + shuffle (to mix up cards), originally referring to shuffling a deck of cards, later generalized.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
to shuffle something, especially playing cards, again; to change the order or arrangement of something anew
shuffle or reorganize again

Examples

To make the game fair, the dealer decided to reshuffle the deck.
After the committee meeting, they had to reshuffle the schedule to fit everyone's availability.
The magician quickly reshuffled the cards to impress the audience.

Synset Examples

So as to prevent cheating, he was asked to reshuffle the cards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to mix up the order of (typically cards)

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 're-' (again) to 'shuffle'.
