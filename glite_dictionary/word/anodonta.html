Anodonta

1. NOUN

/ æn.əˈdɑn.tə / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈdɒn.tə / (BrE)
a genus of thin-shelled freshwater mussels found in rivers and lakes.
genus of freshwater mussels

Examples

The Anodonta can often be found buried in the soft sediment at the bottom of lakes.
Several species of Anodonta are considered indicators of clean freshwater ecosystems.
The biologist studied the life cycle of an Anodonta collected from a nearby river.

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + odous, odont- (tooth), referring to the toothless hinge of the shell.
