stock-still

1. ADV

/ stɑkˈstɪl / (AmE), / stɒkˈstɪl / (BrE)
Completely motionless and silent; not moving at all.
completely motionless and silent

Examples

The deer stood stock-still as the hunters approached, blending into the trees.
She froze stock-still when she heard footsteps behind her in the dark hallway.
The children remained stock-still during the hiding game, hardly daring to breathe.

Synset Examples

he sat still as a statue
time stood still
they waited stock-still outside the door
he couldn't hold still any longer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionless - not moving; still
  • Degree Lower: motionless - not moving; still
  • Degree Higher: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible

Etymology

Possibly from 'stock' meaning 'trunk of a tree' (symbolizing stillness).
