Graecophile

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡriːkəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡriːkəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
Having or showing a strong admiration for Greece, its people, or its culture.
admirer of Greek culture or Greece

Examples

The new director is a noted Graecophile who collects ancient Greek manuscripts.
Her Graecophile interests led her to specialize in Hellenic studies.
Many Graecophile societies promote the preservation of Greek heritage and art.

Synset Examples

the Philhellenic Society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philhellene - a person who loves or supports Greece and Greek culture
  • Degree Lower: Francophile - a person who admires France or French culture

Etymology

From Latin Graecus (Greek) + -phile (lover of).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪkəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡriːkəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
A person who admires Greece, its people, culture, or language.
admirer of Greece or Greek culture

Examples

As a self-proclaimed Graecophile, she spent years studying the language and history of ancient Greece.
Many Graecophiles travel to Athens to experience the country's rich cultural heritage firsthand.
The museum's new exhibit was a delight for any Graecophile interested in Hellenistic art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hellenophile - a person who admires ancient Greek culture and language
  • Synonym: Francophile - a person who admires France or French culture
  • Degree Higher: Graecomania - an excessive admiration or obsession with Greek culture

Etymology

From Latin Graecus (Greek) + -phile (lover, admirer), ultimately from Greek Phil- (loving).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10442285-n
Synset POS: n
