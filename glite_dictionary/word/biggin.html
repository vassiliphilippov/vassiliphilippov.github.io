biggin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪɡɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡɪn / (BrE)
A child's close-fitting cap, often tied under the chin, typically worn in the past.
child's close-fitting cap
archaic

Examples

The baby wore a soft biggin to keep her head warm on chilly mornings.
In portraits from the 17th century, children are often shown wearing a biggin.
She carefully tied the strings of the biggin beneath her infant son's chin.

Etymology

From Middle English *bygyn*, possibly from Old French *beguin* (coif, cap).
