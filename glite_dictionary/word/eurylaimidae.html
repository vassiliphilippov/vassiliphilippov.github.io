Eurylaimidae

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌraɪləˈmɪdiː / (AmE), / jʊˌraɪləˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of broadbills, comprising tropical birds found mainly in Southeast Asia, known for their wide, flat bills
family of tropical broadbills (birds)

Examples

The Eurylaimidae are easily recognized by their broad, flattened bills.
Researchers observed several species of Eurylaimidae in the rainforests of Borneo.
The vibrant plumage of birds in the Eurylaimidae family attracts many birdwatchers.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek ‘eurys’ (broad) and ‘laimos’ (throat).

2. NOUN

/ ˌjʊəri.əˈlaɪ.mɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌjʊərɪəˈlaɪmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical birds known as broadbills, coextensive with the suborder Eurylaimi.
family of tropical broadbill birds

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species in the Eurylaimidae family in Southeast Asia.
The colorful broadbills belong to the bird family Eurylaimidae.
Members of Eurylaimidae are known for their wide, flat bills and vivid plumage.

Etymology

From New Latin Eurylaimus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
