Isoptera

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsɑptərə / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒptərə / (BrE)
a former order of social insects comprising the termites, now generally included within the order Blattodea
former order of termites

Examples

Scientists once classified termites in the order Isoptera, though modern taxonomy has changed this grouping.
The study of Isoptera provides insight into the complex social structures of termites.
Many entomologists are familiar with Isoptera from earlier biological literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social insect formerly classified in Isoptera, now in Blattodea

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + pteron (wing).
