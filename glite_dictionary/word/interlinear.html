interlinear

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɪniə / (BrE)
written or printed between the lines of text
written between lines of text

Examples

The ancient manuscript had interlinear notes to explain difficult passages.
She preferred to write interlinear translations so she could compare the original text directly with its meaning.
The student inserted interlinear glosses to better understand the grammar of each sentence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marginal - written in the margin of a text

Etymology

From Latin 'interlinearis', from 'inter-' (between) + 'linea' (line).
