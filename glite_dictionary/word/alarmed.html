alarmed

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɑːrmd / (AmE), / əˈlɑːmd / (BrE)
feeling sudden fear or worry, especially because you think something bad might happen
suddenly fearful or worried

Examples

The sudden noise left her alarmed and on edge for the rest of the night.
He looked alarmed when he heard footsteps approaching from behind.
Neighbors grew alarmed at the sight of thick smoke rising from the house.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From 'alarm' + -ed, originally from Italian 'all'arme' ('to arms').
