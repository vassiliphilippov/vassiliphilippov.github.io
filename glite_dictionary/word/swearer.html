swearer

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛrər / (AmE), / ˈsweərə / (BrE)
a person who habitually uses profanity or offensive language
person who uses profanity

Examples

He is known as a habitual swearer, often shocking people with his language.
At the meeting, the loudest swearer was asked to watch his mouth.
She refused to be friends with a swearer who constantly used bad language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curser - a person who utters curses or uses profane language
  • Synonym: blasphemer - a person who speaks irreverently about God or sacred things
  • Antonym: non-swearer - a person who does not use profanity

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɛrər / (AmE), / ˈsweərə / (BrE)
a person who makes a solemn oath or affirmation, especially in a formal or legal setting
someone who takes a solemn oath

Examples

As the swearer, he was required to recite the oath aloud before the judge.
The swearer must place their hand on the Bible as part of the ceremony.
Each swearer affirmed their commitment to tell the truth in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - person who gives testimony under oath

Etymology

From Middle English 'sweren', related to the verb 'swear'.
