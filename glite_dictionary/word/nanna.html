Nanna

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˈnæn.ə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the wife of the god Balder, known for her devotion and tragic death.
wife of Balder in Norse mythology

Examples

In the legends, Nanna died of grief after Balder's death.
The story of Nanna and Balder illustrates themes of love and loss in Norse mythology.
A statue of Nanna stands among other Norse deities in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Balder - Norse god, husband of Nanna, associated with light and beauty

Etymology

From Old Norse Nanna, a mythological name in Norse legend.

2. PROPN

/ ˈnænə / (AmE), / ˈnanə / (BrE)
A Mesopotamian god of the Moon, worshipped primarily in Sumerian mythology and regarded as the counterpart of the Akkadian Sin.
Mesopotamian moon god

Examples

Ancient Sumerians built great temples to honor Nanna as their principal moon deity.
Mythological texts often mention Nanna alongside other gods of the Mesopotamian pantheon.
The festival dedicated to Nanna was an important event in the city of Ur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sin - Akkadian god of the Moon, equivalent to Sumerian Nanna

Etymology

From Sumerian mythology; related to Akkadian Sin.
