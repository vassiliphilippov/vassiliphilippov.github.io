herpetology

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɜrpəˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌhɜːpɪˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of zoology that deals with the study of reptiles and amphibians
study of reptiles and amphibians

Examples

She decided to specialize in herpetology, focusing on the behavior of snakes.
The museum's new exhibit showcases the importance of herpetology in conservation efforts.
His lifelong interest in frogs and lizards led him to a career in herpetology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zoology - the scientific study of animals

Etymology

From Greek herpeton (creeping animal) + -logy (study of).
