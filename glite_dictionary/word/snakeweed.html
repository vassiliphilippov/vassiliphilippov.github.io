snakeweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk.wiːd / (BrE)
a low-growing sticky subshrub of the southwestern United States, with narrow linear leaves and numerous tiny yellow flower heads
a sticky subshrub of the southwestern US with yellow flowers

Examples

The snakeweed thrives in arid regions of the southwestern United States.
Ranchers often try to control the spread of snakeweed because it can be toxic to livestock.
The small yellow flowers of snakeweed bloom throughout late summer and early autumn.
