viola

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
Any of a genus of small herbaceous plants with heart-shaped leaves and showy flowers, typically purple, yellow, or white.
a small flowering plant of the genus Viola

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming violas in a variety of colors.
She planted a border of violas along the walkway for their delicate beauty.
Some species of viola are prized for their fragrance and vibrant petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple or white flowers, often of the genus Viola

Etymology

From Latin 'viola' (the violet flower).

2. NOUN

/ viˈoʊlə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
A bowed stringed instrument of the violin family, slightly larger than a violin and tuned a fifth lower, producing a deeper sound.
bowed string instrument, deeper than violin

Examples

She prefers playing the viola to the violin because of its richer tone.
The orchestra needed a new viola player for the upcoming concert.
During the quartet, the viola part added warmth to the overall sound.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: violin - a smaller, higher-pitched instrument in the same family as the viola
  • Degree Lower: cello - a larger, lower-pitched member of the violin family

Etymology

From Italian 'viola', meaning 'stringed instrument'.
