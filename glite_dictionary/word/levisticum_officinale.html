Levisticum officinale

1. NOUN

/ ləˈvɪstɪkəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑli / (AmE), / lɛˈvɪstɪkəm ɒfɪsɪˈnɑːli / (BrE)
A herb native to southern Europe, cultivated for its edible stalks, leaves, and seeds, commonly known as lovage.
herb (lovage) with edible stalks and seeds

Examples

The leaves of Levisticum officinale can be used as a flavorful herb in soups and stews.
Gardeners often grow Levisticum officinale for its aromatic qualities and medicinal uses.
Cooks sometimes substitute celery with Levisticum officinale in traditional European recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovage - common name for Levisticum officinale, a herb with edible stalks and seeds

Etymology

From Latin: 'Levisticum' (lovage) and 'officinale' (used in medicine or the apothecary).
