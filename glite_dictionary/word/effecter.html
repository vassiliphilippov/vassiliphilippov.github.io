effecter

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktə / (BrE)
a person or thing that brings about a result or accomplishes a purpose
someone or something that causes an effect

Examples

The new manager proved to be an effective effecter of positive change in the organization.
In the natural world, beavers are well-known as effecters of environmental transformation.
Each policy requires an effecter to ensure its successful implementation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person or thing that takes an active role or produces a specified effect
  • Synonym: cause - something or someone that makes something happen

Etymology

Formed from 'effect' (from Latin 'effectus', accomplishment) + '-er', agent noun suffix.
