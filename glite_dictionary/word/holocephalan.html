holocephalan

1. NOUN

/ hoʊloʊˈsɛfələn / (AmE), / hɒləʊˈsɛfələn / (BrE)
A cartilaginous fish with a highly compressed head and a body that tapers into a long tail, belonging to the subclass Holocephali, such as the chimaeras.
cartilaginous fish with a compressed head and tapering tail

Examples

The holocephalan is notable for its long, slender tail and distinct head shape.
Scientists discovered a new species of holocephalan off the coast of South Africa.
Unlike sharks, a holocephalan has a single gill opening on each side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimaera - another name for member of order Chimaeriformes, cartilaginous fish related to sharks
  • Degree Lower: cartilaginous fish - fish whose skeleton is made of cartilage, not bone

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) + 'kephalē' (head), meaning 'whole head'.
