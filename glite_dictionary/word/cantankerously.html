cantankerously

1. ADV

/ kænˈtæŋkərəsli / (AmE), / kanˈtaŋkərəsli / (BrE)
in a bad-tempered, argumentative, or uncooperative manner
in a bad-tempered manner

Examples

He replied cantankerously to every question she asked during the meeting.
The old man muttered cantankerously about the noise outside his window.
When interrupted, she cantankerously dismissed everyone's suggestions.

Synset Examples

he answered her cantankerously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumpily - in a mood marked by irritation or sulkiness
  • Antonym: amiably - in a friendly and pleasant manner

Etymology

From 'cantankerous' (likely from Middle English, possibly from contank or conteck, meaning 'strife, contention').
