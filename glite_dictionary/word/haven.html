haven

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪvən / (AmE), / ˈheɪvən / (BrE)
A sheltered place, especially a port or harbor, where ships can take refuge or unload and load cargo safely.
sheltered port or harbor

Examples

The fishing boats found a haven from the storm in the small bay.
For centuries, this coastal city served as a haven for trading ships.
After days at sea, the sailors were grateful to enter the safe haven of the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor - a place on the coast where vessels may find shelter
  • Synonym: port - a town or city with a harbor for ships

Etymology

From Old English hæfen (harbor, port), related to Old Norse höfn.

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪvən / (AmE), / ˈheɪvən / (BrE)
a place of safety, refuge, or sanctuary from danger or hardship
a safe or sheltered place

Examples

After the storm, the coastal town became a haven for stranded travelers.
The wildlife reserve is a haven for endangered species.
She found a haven of peace in her quiet garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing shelter or protection

Etymology

From Old English hæfen (harbor, haven), related to Dutch haven, German Hafen.
