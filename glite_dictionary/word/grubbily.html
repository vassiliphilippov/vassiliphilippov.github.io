grubbily

1. ADV

/ ˈɡrʌbɪli / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbɪli / (BrE)
In a dirty or shabby manner; in a way that is not clean or is untidy.
in a dirty or untidy manner

Examples

The children came home grubbily after playing in the garden all afternoon.
He was dressed grubbily, in old jeans and a stained T-shirt.
The room looked grubbily neglected, with dust and smudges everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirtily - in a dirty manner
  • Antonym: cleanly - in a clean manner

Etymology

From 'grubby' + '-ly'. 'Grubby' from 'grub' (larva) suggesting dirtiness.
