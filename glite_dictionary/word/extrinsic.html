extrinsic

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈstrɪnsɪk, ɪkˈstrɪnsɪk / (AmE), / ɛkˈstrɪnsɪk, ɪkˈstrɪnsɪk / (BrE)
not forming an essential part of something; coming from outside rather than from within.
coming from outside; not essential

Examples

The success of a project can depend on extrinsic factors such as market trends and funding.
She valued feedback from her peers more than any extrinsic motivation like prizes or awards.
These decorations are purely extrinsic and do not affect the structure itself.

Synset Examples

extrinsic evidence
an extrinsic feature of the new building
that style is something extrinsic to the subject
looking for extrinsic aid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intrinsic - belonging naturally; essential

Etymology

From Medieval Latin extrinsecus (from the outside), from Latin extrinsecus (outside, external).
