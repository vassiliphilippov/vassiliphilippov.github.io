semiterrestrial

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmi təˈrɛstəriəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi tɛˈrɛstəriəl / (BrE)
(of an animal or organism) primarily living or operating on land but not exclusively so.
partly but not fully terrestrial

Examples

Many amphibians are considered semiterrestrial because they split their time between water and land.
Some crabs exhibit semiterrestrial behavior, venturing onto land but returning to water frequently.
The semiterrestrial lifestyle of these snails allows them to exploit food sources in both habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: terrestrial - living or growing on land
  • Antonym: aquatic - living or growing in water

Etymology

From semi- (Latin, meaning 'half' or 'partly') + terrestrial (from Latin 'terra', meaning 'earth').
