lightning

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
A sudden electrical discharge occurring naturally between clouds or between a cloud and the ground, usually accompanied by a flash of light and thunder.
atmospheric electrical discharge

Examples

The lightning lit up the entire night sky during the storm.
She was frightened after a bolt of lightning struck a tree nearby.
We heard thunder seconds after seeing the lightning flash in the distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunder - the sound caused by lightning

Etymology

From Middle English 'lightening', from 'lighten' (to flash with lightning).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden, bright flash of light in the sky produced by an electric discharge in the atmosphere, especially during a thunderstorm
flash of light during a storm

Examples

A bolt of lightning lit up the entire sky during the storm.
She saw lightning strike the tree outside her window.
The sudden lightning was followed by a loud rumble of thunder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunder - the loud sound that follows lightning due to expansion of air

Etymology

From Old English 'lihting', from 'lēoht' (light).
