trembles

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmblz / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmblz / (BrE)
A disease of livestock, especially cattle, caused by poisoning after eating certain types of snakeroot, characterized by muscular tremors and weakness.
disease in livestock caused by snakeroot poisoning

Examples

Cattle that feed on white snakeroot are at risk of developing trembles.
Farmers in the Midwest once lost entire herds to trembles outbreaks.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow’s symptoms as trembles due to grazing near contaminated plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk sickness - A similar disease in humans caused by drinking milk from affected cattle
