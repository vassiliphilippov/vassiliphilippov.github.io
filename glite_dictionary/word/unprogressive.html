unprogressive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.prəˈɡrɛs.ɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prəˈɡrɛs.ɪv / (BrE)
old-fashioned and unwilling to accept new ideas or changes; not making progress or development
not modern or open to change

Examples

His unprogressive views on technology held the company back for years.
Some critics argue that the policy is unprogressive and fails to address modern challenges.
Despite the changing times, the old rules remain stubbornly unprogressive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new ideas
  • Synonym: conservative - holding to traditional attitudes and values; cautious about change or innovation

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + progressive.
