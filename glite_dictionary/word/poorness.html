poorness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːnəs / (BrE)
the state of having little or no money or few material possessions; poverty
state of being poor

Examples

Growing up in poorness taught her the value of hard work and perseverance.
The film depicted the poorness of the remote village in vivid detail.
Despite his poorness, he remained generous to those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Antonym: wealth - the state of being rich, having a lot of money and possessions

Etymology

From 'poor' + '-ness', using the adjective 'poor' and the noun-forming suffix '-ness'.
