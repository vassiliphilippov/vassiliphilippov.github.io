ilang-ilang

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːlɑːŋ ˈiːlɑːŋ / (AmE), / ˌiːlæŋ ˈiːlæŋ / (BrE)
An essential oil distilled from the flowers of the ilang-ilang tree, used especially in perfumery.
essential oil from ilang-ilang flowers

Examples

The fragrance of ilang-ilang is a key ingredient in many luxury perfumes.
She bought a bottle of pure ilang-ilang oil during her trip to the Philippines.
Some aromatherapy blends use ilang-ilang for its calming scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ylang-ylang - essential oil from flowers of the ylang-ylang tree, used in perfumery

Etymology

From Tagalog 'ilang-ilang', the name of the tree and its flowers in the Philippines.

2. NOUN

/ iˌlɑŋ iˈlɑŋ / (AmE), / iˌlæŋ iˈlæŋ / (BrE)
An evergreen tropical Asian tree (Cananga odorata) with strongly scented greenish-yellow flowers that yield a valuable essential oil, grown widely as an ornamental plant in the tropics.
tropical Asian tree with aromatic flowers

Examples

The sweet fragrance of ilang-ilang filled the garden, attracting bees and butterflies.
Perfume makers often use oil extracted from ilang-ilang flowers for its unique scent.
An ilang-ilang tree was planted near the entrance to provide both beauty and aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ylang-ylang - alternative spelling of the same tree

Etymology

From Tagalog 'ilang-ilang', the local name for the tree.
