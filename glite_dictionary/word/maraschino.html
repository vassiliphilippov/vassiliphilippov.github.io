maraschino

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærəˈskiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmærəˈskiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a cherry preserved in maraschino liqueur or a similar sweet syrup, often used as a garnish in drinks or desserts
cherry preserved in liqueur or syrup

Examples

He placed a bright maraschino on top of the whipped cream.
The recipe called for two maraschino cherries as a garnish.
A single maraschino added a touch of color to her cocktail.

Etymology

From Italian 'maraschino', originally referring to a liqueur made from marasca cherries.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmærəˈskiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmærəˈskiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A sweet, clear liqueur distilled from the fermented juice of bitter wild marasca cherries.
sweet liqueur from marasca cherries

Examples

The bartender added a splash of maraschino to the cocktail for a touch of sweetness.
Traditional recipes call for authentic maraschino made from marasca cherries.
Connoisseurs often prefer maraschino for its distinct cherry aroma and subtle almond notes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cherry liqueur - a liqueur made from cherries; broader category than maraschino

Etymology

From Italian 'maraschino', from 'marasca' (a variety of cherry), ultimately from Slavic root.
