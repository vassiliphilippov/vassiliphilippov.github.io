adsorbent

1. NOUN

/ ædˈsɔrbənt / (AmE), / adˈsɔːbənt / (BrE)
a material that has the ability to adsorb another substance onto its surface
material that adsorbs another substance

Examples

Activated carbon is a common adsorbent used in water purification systems.
The laboratory tested several adsorbent materials to remove toxins from the air.
Zeolites act as an adsorbent in the process of gas separation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorbent - a substance that can absorb liquids or gases

Etymology

From 'adsorb' + '-ent', modeled on 'absorbent'.

2. ADJ

/ ædˈzɔrbənt / (AmE), / ədˈsɔːbənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) having the ability to adsorb other substances onto its surface.
capable of adsorbing substances

Examples

Activated charcoal is a highly adsorbent material used to remove toxins from water.
The laboratory tested several adsorbent surfaces for their effectiveness in trapping pollutants.
Zeolites are naturally adsorbent minerals and play a role in cleaning air and water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorbent - able to soak up or take in fluids or other substances

Etymology

From 'adsorb', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'sorbēre' (to suck in).
