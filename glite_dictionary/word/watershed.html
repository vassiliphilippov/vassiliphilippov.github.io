watershed

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːtərˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌʃɛd / (BrE)
an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers, basins, or seas, or the entire region drained by a river and its tributaries
drainage basin; land area draining to a river

Examples

The Amazon River has the largest watershed in the world, covering millions of square kilometers.
Conservation efforts in the watershed help prevent downstream flooding and pollution.
Many cities rely on the clean water supplied by their local watershed.

Synset Examples

flood control in the Missouri basin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainage basin - the area from which all precipitation flows to a single stream or set of streams

Etymology

From English 'water' + 'shed' (divide, ridge); originally meaning a ridge that diverts water in different directions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːt̬ɚˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌʃɛd / (BrE)
an event or period that marks an important turning point or change in the course of history, affairs, or development
crucial turning point or landmark event

Examples

1990 was a watershed year for the country's political reforms.
The court ruling became a watershed in civil rights history.
Many historians regard the fall of the Berlin Wall as a watershed moment in world affairs.

Synset Examples

the agreement was a watershed in the history of both nations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a moment of decisive change
  • Synonym: milestone - an important event in development or history

Etymology

Originally from the literal meaning, combining 'water' and 'shed' (meaning to divide or separate, here a ridge dividing drainage areas); figurative use since the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəʃɛd / (BrE)
A ridge of land that separates two adjacent river systems.
ridge dividing river systems

Examples

The Rocky Mountains form a major watershed in North America, dividing rivers that flow to the Atlantic and Pacific oceans.
Rainfall on either side of the watershed can end up in completely different river basins.
The hike followed the watershed for several miles, offering stunning views of both valleys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - ridge or elevated region separating river drainages

Etymology

From English 'water' + 'shed' (to divide or split); originally meant a water divide.
