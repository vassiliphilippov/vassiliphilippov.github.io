Pennsylvanian

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.səlˈveɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.sɪlˈveɪ.ni.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Pennsylvania, a state in the United States
person from Pennsylvania

Examples

As a proud Pennsylvanian, she always supported local businesses in her hometown.
Several Pennsylvanians traveled to Washington, D.C. for the annual festival.
He met a fellow Pennsylvanian while studying abroad in Europe.

Etymology

From Pennsylvania, the name of the U.S. state, with the suffix -an forming a demonym.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.səlˈveɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˌpɛnzɪlˈveɪ.nɪ.ən / (BrE)
the period in the late Carboniferous era, about 323 to 299 million years ago, characterized by extensive coal-forming forests and swampy land
geological period in late Carboniferous era

Examples

Coal deposits from the Pennsylvanian provide important clues about Earth’s prehistoric climate.
The lush forests of the Pennsylvanian period created conditions for vast coal formation.
Scientists discovered fossils dating back to the Pennsylvanian in the region’s sedimentary rocks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Carboniferous - the geologic period encompassing both the Mississippian and Pennsylvanian epochs

Etymology

Named after Pennsylvania, where rocks from this period were first studied.
