Trichys lipura

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪkɪs lɪˈpjʊrə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkɪs lɪˈpjʊərə / (BrE)
a species of porcupine found in Borneo and Sumatra, distinguished by its short spines and long tail
porcupine species from Borneo and Sumatra

Examples

The Trichys lipura is notable for its unusually long tail among porcupines.
Researchers in Sumatra documented sightings of Trichys lipura in lowland forests.
Unlike most porcupines, Trichys lipura has relatively short spines.

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Trichys' from Greek 'thrix' (hair), species epithet from Greek 'lipoura' (with a fat tail).
