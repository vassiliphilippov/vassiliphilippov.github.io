Bhutan

1. PROPN

/ buˈtɑn / (AmE), / buːˈtɑːn / (BrE)
a landlocked country in the eastern Himalayas, to the northeast of India, known for its monasteries, fortresses, and dramatic landscapes
country in the Himalayas, northeast of India

Examples

Many tourists visit Bhutan to experience its unique culture and natural beauty.
The government of Bhutan measures the country's progress using Gross National Happiness instead of GDP.
Mountains dominate the landscape of Bhutan, making much of it difficult to access.

Etymology

From the local endonym 'Druk Yul' meaning 'Land of the Thunder Dragon'; 'Bhutan' possibly from Sanskrit 'Bhotanta' ('end of Tibet').
