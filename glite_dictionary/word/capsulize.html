to capsulize

1. VERB

/ ˈkæp.sjəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkap.sjʊ.laɪz / (BrE)
to enclose or put something within a capsule, especially for protection or administration
enclose in a capsule

Examples

Pharmaceutical manufacturers often capsulize powdered medicines to improve dosage accuracy.
The researchers decided to capsulize the new supplement for easier administration.
Some vitamins are specially capsulized to prevent them from dissolving in the stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encapsulate - to enclose in or as if in a capsule; to summarize

Etymology

Formed from 'capsule' + '-ize', indicating the act of making into a capsule.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01583919-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkæp.səˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæp.sjʊ.laɪz / (BrE)
to put something into a short or concise form; to summarize or condense
summarize or condense

Examples

The journalist managed to capsulize the complicated issue in a single paragraph.
Can you capsulize your argument for the meeting later today?
The editor asked her team to capsulize last week's events into a short summary.

Synset Examples

capsulize the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: to condense - to make something more concise by reducing its volume

Etymology

From capsule + -ize, modeled after 'summarize'; likely influenced by the idea of putting content into a small container (capsule).
