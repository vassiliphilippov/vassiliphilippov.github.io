Holocentrus coruscus

1. NOUN

/ həˌlɑːˈsɛntrəs kəˈrʌskəs / (AmE), / hɒləˌsɛntrəs kəˈrʌskəs / (BrE)
A species of fish found on reefs from Bermuda and Florida to northern South America, belonging to the family Holocentridae.
a species of reef fish

Examples

The Holocentrus coruscus is commonly found on coral reefs in warm Caribbean waters.
Researchers observed the distinctive red coloring of Holocentrus coruscus while diving in Bermuda.
Many divers are excited to spot a Holocentrus coruscus hiding among the rocks.

Etymology

From genus Holocentrus and Latin coruscus meaning 'sparkling' or 'glittering'.
