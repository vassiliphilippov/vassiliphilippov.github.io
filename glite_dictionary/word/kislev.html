Kislev

1. NOUN

/ kɪzˈleɪv / (AmE), / kɪzˈlɛv / (BrE)
the third month of the civil year and the ninth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in November and December
month in the Jewish calendar (November–December)

Examples

Hanukkah usually begins in the month of Kislev on the Jewish calendar.
This year, Kislev fell entirely in December, which is uncommon.
Many important events in Jewish history are commemorated during Kislev.

Etymology

From Hebrew כִּסְלֵו (Kislev), the name of the month in the Hebrew calendar.
