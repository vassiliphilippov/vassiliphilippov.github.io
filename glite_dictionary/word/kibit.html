kibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪb.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪb.ɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 bits, used in computing and telecommunications.
unit of 1,024 bits (digital information)

Examples

A kibit is commonly used to measure small amounts of digital data.
The file size was just over one kibit, so it downloaded instantly.
Many protocols specify values in kibit instead of traditional kilobits to avoid confusion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kilobit - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 bits

Etymology

From 'kibi-', a binary prefix derived from 'kilo' and 'binary', defined by the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC), and 'bit', meaning binary digit.
