Calochortus amoenus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɔrtəs əˈmiːnəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈkɔːtəs əˈmiːnəs / (BrE)
A species of globe lily with deep rose-pink or purple egg-shaped flowers, native to the western slopes of the Sierra Nevada in California.
a species of globe lily native to California

Examples

The meadows were dotted with the delicate Calochortus amoenus in full bloom.
Hikers often stop to admire the deep rose hues of Calochortus amoenus along the Sierra trails.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Calochortus amoenus in the San Joaquin Valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globe lily - a common name for species in the Calochortus genus

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'chortos' (grass); species epithet from Latin 'amoenus' (pleasant, delightful).
