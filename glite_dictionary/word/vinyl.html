vinyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪnɪl / (BrE)
a shiny, flexible, and tough plastic material, used especially for floor coverings, upholstery, and records.
shiny, flexible plastic material

Examples

We chose a vinyl floor for the kitchen because it's easy to clean.
The chair was covered in red vinyl that looked almost like leather.
He prefers listening to music on old vinyl records rather than digital files.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PVC - polyvinyl chloride, a common type of vinyl plastic

Etymology

From Latin 'vinum' (wine), because the chemical vinyl group is derived from ethyl alcohol (spirit of wine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪnɪl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group (–CH=CH2) derived from ethylene, found in many organic compounds and plastics
univalent chemical group (–CH=CH2) from ethylene
medical

Examples

Polyvinyl chloride is made by adding chlorine to the vinyl group.
The vinyl moiety is important in many polymerization reactions.
Chemists often discuss the reactivity of the vinyl radical in synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: allyl - a similar univalent chemical group (–CH2CH=CH2)

Etymology

From Latin 'vinum' (wine), referring to ethyl derivatives, and 'yl', a chemical suffix for radicals.
