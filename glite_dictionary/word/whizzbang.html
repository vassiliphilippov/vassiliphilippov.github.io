whizzbang

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzˌbæŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪzˌbæŋ / (BrE)
A small, high-velocity artillery shell that makes a whizzing sound followed by a bang when it explodes.
small artillery shell with a whizzing sound

Examples

The soldiers ducked for cover as a whizzbang screamed overhead.
During the battle, the constant threat of whizzbangs kept everyone on edge.
He described the terrifying noise of a whizzbang as it tore through the air.

Etymology

Imitative, from the whizzing sound the shell makes, followed by a bang.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzˌbæŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪzˌbaŋ / (BrE)
A type of firecracker or shell that emits a whizzing sound followed by a loud explosion.
firecracker with whizz and bang

Examples

The children shrieked with delight when the whizzbang erupted into the night sky.
Be careful when lighting a whizzbang, as it can be unpredictable.
During the festival, several colorful whizzbangs dazzled the crowd with their noise and lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firecracker - explosive device for entertainment, similar in function
  • Synonym: banger - another term for a loud firework

Etymology

Originally military slang from World War I, describing shells that whizzed through the air and exploded with a bang.
