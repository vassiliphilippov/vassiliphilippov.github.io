Eridanus

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛrɪˈdeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈdeɪnəs / (BrE)
a constellation in the southern sky, near Fornax and Cetus, representing a river in Greek mythology
constellation in southern sky

Examples

The constellation Eridanus is best viewed during the months of December and January.
Ancient astronomers associated Eridanus with the mythological river that flowed through the underworld.
Scientists discovered an unusual star cluster at the edge of Eridanus.

Etymology

From Latin 'Eridanus', from Greek 'Eridanos', a mythical river.
