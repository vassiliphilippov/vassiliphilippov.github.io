camwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈkam.wʊd / (BrE)
a small shrubby African tree (Baphia nitida) with hard, red wood used to produce a red dye
African tree yielding red dyewood

Examples

The artisans used camwood to create a vibrant red dye for their fabrics.
Traditional carvings made from camwood are prized for their hardness and deep color.
She explained that camwood has been used in West African cultures for generations.

Etymology

Possibly from Portuguese 'pau de cam' or West African languages; used in English since the 18th century.
