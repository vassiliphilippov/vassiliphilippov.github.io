blushing

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) showing a red face as a result of embarrassment, shame, or strong emotion.
showing a red face from emotion

Examples

She greeted her idol with a blushing smile, unable to hide her excitement.
The blushing bride entered the room to a round of applause from her family.
He was blushing so deeply that everyone noticed his embarrassment.

Synset Examples

the blushing boy was brought before the Principal
her blushful beau
was red-faced with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flushed - having a red or pink face, especially from emotion or exertion
  • Antonym: pale - having less color than usual, especially in the face

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'blush', from Old English *blyscan*.
