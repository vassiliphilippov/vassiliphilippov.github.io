haematoidin

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmætɔɪˈdɪn / (AmE), / hiːˌmatɔɪˈdɪn / (BrE)
an orange-yellow pigment formed from hemoglobin, occurring in tissues and contributing to the yellow coloration seen in jaundice
orange-yellow pigment from hemoglobin
medical british

Examples

The presence of haematoidin crystals is often noted in old hemorrhagic sites.
Jaundice results when haematoidin and related pigments accumulate in the bloodstream.
Researchers examined the liver tissue for haematoidin deposits during the autopsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilirubin - a yellow compound formed from hemoglobin, associated with jaundice

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'oeidēs' (like, resembling).
