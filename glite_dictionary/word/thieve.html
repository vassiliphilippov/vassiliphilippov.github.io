to thieve

1. VERB

/ θiv / (AmE), / θiːv / (BrE)
to steal something, especially secretly or dishonestly
to steal

Examples

He was caught trying to thieve a bicycle from the station.
Some children thieve sweets from the corner shop when no one is looking.
She admitted she had thieved money from her sister's purse.

Synset Examples

Someone snitched my wallet!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From Old English 'thēofian' (to steal), related to 'thief'.
