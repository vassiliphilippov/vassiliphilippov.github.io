morals

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəlz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlz / (BrE)
principles or standards of behavior that are based on ideas of what is right and wrong
standards of right and wrong behavior

Examples

The company was accused of putting profit before morals.
She has strong morals and always tries to do the right thing.
Many people question the morals of politicians these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - moral principles that govern a person's behavior
  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong
  • Antonym: immorality - the state or quality of being immoral; wickedness

Etymology

From Latin moralis (relating to manners or morals), pluralized in English to indicate a set of principles.
