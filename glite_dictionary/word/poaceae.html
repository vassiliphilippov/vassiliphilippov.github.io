Poaceae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / pəʊˈiːsiː / (BrE)
The botanical family of grasses, including herbaceous and some woody plants such as cereals, bamboo, reeds, and sugar cane.
the grass family (plants)

Examples

The Poaceae family includes important crops such as wheat and rice.
Bamboo is a member of the Poaceae family, making it a type of grass.
Many scientists study Poaceae because of its ecological and agricultural significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - common plant of the family Poaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'poa' (grass), adopted as a botanical family name in modern taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / pəʊˈeɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
a large family of chiefly herbaceous plants known as grasses, including cereals, bamboo, reeds, and sugar cane
the grass family of plants

Examples

The Poaceae family includes wheat, rice, and corn, which are staple crops worldwide.
Many ornamental plants in the Poaceae are popular in landscaping for their graceful foliage.
Scientists study Poaceae to improve resistance to disease in important cereal crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gramineae - an alternative name for the Poaceae family

Etymology

From Latin Poa (genus of grasses) + -aceae, a standard suffix for plant families.
