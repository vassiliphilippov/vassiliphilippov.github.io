partitionist

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports the partitioning of a country into separate regions or states, especially for political reasons
advocate of political partition

Examples

The partitionist movement gained momentum during the political negotiations.
Many historians argue that the partitionist approach led to prolonged conflict in the region.
He was labeled a partitionist by his opponents for supporting the division of the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separatist - a person who supports separation of a group from a larger body, especially for political reasons

Etymology

From 'partition' (Latin 'partitio', division) + '-ist' (suffix denoting an advocate or adherent).
