Arctocebus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrk.təˈsiː.bəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːktəˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of small African primates in the family Lorisidae, including the Calabar and golden angwantibos.
genus of African primates (angwantibos)

Examples

The Arctocebus genus consists of two species found in west-central Africa.
Research on Arctocebus has revealed insights into their nocturnal behaviors and specialized diet.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect the rare Arctocebus from habitat loss.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'arktos' (bear) and 'kebos' (long-tailed monkey).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑrk.təˈsiː.bəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːk.təˈsiː.bəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal primates in the family Lorisidae, known as angwantibos, native to central and western Africa.
genus of African angwantibo primates

Examples

The Arctocebus genus includes two species, both known as angwantibos.
Researchers observed Arctocebus feeding on insects in the rainforest canopy.
The unique nose structure of Arctocebus distinguishes it from other lorisid primates.

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) and kebos (long-tailed monkey), forming a Neo-Latin taxonomic name.
