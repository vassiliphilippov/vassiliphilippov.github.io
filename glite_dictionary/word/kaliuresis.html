kaliuresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæliˈjʊrəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈjʊərəsɪs / (BrE)
the excretion of an unusually large amount of potassium in the urine
excess potassium in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient showed signs of kaliuresis after receiving high doses of diuretics.
Doctors monitored levels of kaliuresis to adjust the patient's medication.
A sudden increase in kaliuresis can indicate potential kidney issues.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kali' (potash, potassium) and 'uresis' (urination).
