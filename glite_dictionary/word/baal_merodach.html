Baal Merodach

1. PROPN

/ bɑl ˌmɛrəˈdɑk / (AmE), / bɑːl ˌmɛərəˈdæk / (BrE)
the chief god of Babylonian mythology, regarded as the supreme deity whose consort was Sarpanitu
chief god of Babylonian mythology

Examples

Ancient texts frequently mention Baal Merodach as the supreme god worshipped in Babylon.
Priests in Babylon performed rituals in honor of Baal Merodach during the New Year festival.
The temple dedicated to Baal Merodach stood at the heart of the great city of Babylon.

Etymology

From Hebrew Baʿal Mərōḏaḵ, combining 'Baal' (lord) and 'Merodach', a form of 'Marduk', the principal Babylonian deity.
