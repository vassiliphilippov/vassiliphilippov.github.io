Lupinus

1. PROPN

/ luˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / luˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family, commonly known as lupins, which includes herbaceous plants and shrubs with attractive, often colorful, flowers.
genus of flowering plants (lupin)

Examples

Many gardens feature Lupinus varieties for their tall spikes of colorful blooms.
Botanists have identified several new species within the Lupinus genus.
The seeds of some Lupinus species are used as a food source in Mediterranean countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupin - common name for species in the genus Lupinus

Etymology

From Latin 'lupinus', relating to the wolf, possibly from the belief that the plant exhausted soil like a wolf preys on animals.

2. NOUN

/ luˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / ljuːˈpaɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering herbs or shrubs in the legume family, commonly known as lupins, known for their tall spikes of colorful flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability.
genus of plants; the lupin

Examples

Lupinus species are widely cultivated for their attractive flower spikes.
The seeds of some Lupinus varieties are used as food.
Gardeners often choose Lupinus to enrich the soil with nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupin - a plant of the genus Lupinus with tall colorful flower spikes

Etymology

From Latin 'lupinus' (wolf), the plant was thought to ravenously exhaust soil nutrients.
