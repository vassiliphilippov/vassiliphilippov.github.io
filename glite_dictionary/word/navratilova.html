Navratilova

1. PROPN

/ næv.rə.tɪˈloʊ.və / (AmE), / nav.rə.tɪˈləʊ.və / (BrE)
Martina Navratilova (born 1956), United States tennis player originally from Czechoslovakia, renowned for winning nine Wimbledon women's singles championships.
United States tennis champion (born 1956)

Examples

Navratilova dominated the tennis world throughout the 1980s with her exceptional skill and athleticism.
Many consider Navratilova to be one of the greatest female tennis players in history.
In interviews, Navratilova often speaks about her experiences as an athlete and activist.

Etymology

Czech surname; Martina Navratilova emigrated from Czechoslovakia to the United States.
