serpent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːpənt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːpənt / (BrE)
an obsolete wind instrument with a long, curved, snake-like shape, formerly used as a bass cornet in churches and military bands
obsolete bass wind instrument
archaic

Examples

The church choir was accompanied by a serpent during the performance.
Museums sometimes display a serpent alongside other historical wind instruments.
The sound of the serpent added a deep resonance to the military band's music.

Etymology

From French 'serpent' meaning 'snake', referring to the instrument's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːpənt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːpənt / (BrE)
A type of firework that moves in a winding or curving manner when ignited.
type of winding firework

Examples

The children watched in awe as the serpent wriggled across the pavement, leaving trails of sparks.
He set off a serpent at dusk, and it twisted brightly around the yard.
Among the many fireworks, the serpent was a crowd favorite for its snaking movements.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɚːpənt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːpənt / (BrE)
A large snake, often used in literature or myth to refer to a limbless, scaly, elongate reptile, sometimes considered venomous.
large snake or mythic reptile

Examples

In the myth, the forbidden fruit was offered by a serpent lurking in the tree.
The ancient temple carvings depicted a giant serpent winding around its pillars.
During the hike, they spotted a venomous serpent slithering through the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - a limbless, scaly, elongate reptile

Etymology

From Old French 'serpent', from Latin 'serpens' (snake, creeping thing), from 'serpere' (to creep).
