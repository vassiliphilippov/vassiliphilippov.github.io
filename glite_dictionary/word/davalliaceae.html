Davalliaceae

1. NOUN

/ dəˌvæliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / dəˌvælɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns, formerly part of Polypodiaceae, comprising about 110 species.
a family of ferns

Examples

The Davalliaceae are often known for their creeping rhizomes and delicate fronds.
Several ferns classified under Davalliaceae can be found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Botanists debated whether to separate Davalliaceae from the larger family Polypodiaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Davallia (named for Swiss botanist Edmond Davall) + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

a family of ferns containing about 110 species, distinguished in some classification systems as separate from Polypodiaceae
a family of ferns
medical

Examples

The ferns belonging to the Davalliaceae are often epiphytic and have creeping rhizomes.
You can identify members of the Davalliaceae by their distinctive hairy rootstocks.
Botanists have debated whether the Davalliaceae should be placed within Polypodiaceae or treated as a separate family.

Etymology

From the genus name Davallia, named after Swiss botanist Edmond Davall.
