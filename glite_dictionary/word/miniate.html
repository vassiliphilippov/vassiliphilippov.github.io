to miniate

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪeɪt / (BrE)
to paint or color with red lead or vermilion, especially in the decoration of manuscripts
to paint with red lead or vermilion
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, scribes would miniate the edges of the parchment with bright vermilion.
The ancient manuscript was miniated with delicate red decorations on every page.
Artists sometimes miniated their works to highlight important passages and embellish texts.

Etymology

From Latin *miniatus*, past participle of *miniare* (to rub with red lead), from *minium* (red lead).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪniˌeɪt / (BrE)
to decorate manuscripts with red-colored letters or illustrations, especially by painting them with red pigment
decorate manuscripts with red pigment
archaic

Examples

Medieval scribes would often miniate the initial letters to enhance the manuscripts' beauty.
The artist miniated the margins of the book with intricate red illustrations.
In the restoration process, special care was taken to miniate the faded headings using natural pigments.

Synset Examples

In this beautiful book, all the place names are rubricated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rubricate - to mark or highlight in red, especially in manuscripts

Etymology

From Latin *miniatus*, past participle of *miniare* (to color with minium, red lead).
