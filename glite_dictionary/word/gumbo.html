gumbo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
A fine-grained, silty soil that becomes waxy and very sticky when saturated with water.
sticky, fine-grained silty soil

Examples

The fields were difficult to plow after heavy rainfall because the gumbo turned extremely sticky.
Farmers often avoid areas with gumbo soil because machinery can get stuck.
During the spring, the gumbo in the riverbed became almost impassable for hikers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌm.boʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌm.bəʊ / (BrE)
a plant with long, green, mucilaginous pods, used especially in soups and stews; also called okra.
okra; edible green pod plant

Examples

Fresh gumbo is often added to stews for its thickening properties.
She picked a basket of gumbo from the garden this morning.
The recipe calls for sliced gumbo to give the soup its unique texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okra - the plant and its edible green pods, used in cooking

Etymology

From Bantu languages (e.g., Umbundu *ngombo*, meaning okra), entering English via American southern dialects.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
A stew or thick soup originating in Louisiana, made with meat or seafood, vegetables, and thickened with okra or filé powder.
Louisiana stew with okra or filé powder
american

Examples

The chef prepared a spicy gumbo with shrimp, sausage, and okra for dinner.
Nothing warms me up on a cold day like a bowl of homemade gumbo.
She learned to cook gumbo from her grandmother, who grew up in New Orleans.

Etymology

From Louisiana Creole 'gombo', from West African languages such as Umbundu (ki-ngombo, okra).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
A tall coarse plant of the Old World tropics, widely cultivated for its long green mucilaginous pods used primarily as a vegetable in soups and stews, especially in the southern United States and West Indies; also called okra.
plant with edible green pods (okra)

Examples

The chef added fresh gumbo pods to the sizzling stew.
Farmers in the southern states often grow gumbo for both local use and export.
The distinctive texture of gumbo makes it an important ingredient in many Creole dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okra - the edible green seed pod of the plant Abelmoschus esculentus, also known as gumbo

Etymology

From Bantu (Umbundu) *ngombo* (okra).
