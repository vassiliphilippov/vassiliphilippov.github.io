conic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.nɪk / (BrE)
In geometry, a curve formed by the intersection of a plane with a circular cone; common examples are ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas.
curve from plane intersecting a cone (geometry)

Examples

The ellipse is one type of conic found in geometry.
Understanding each conic helps solve various astronomical problems.
He drew a conic on the board to illustrate the concept to his students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conic section - a curve obtained by intersecting a cone with a plane

Etymology

From Greek *kōnikos* (conical), relating to a cone.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.nɪk / (BrE)
relating to or having the shape of a cone; resembling a cone.
relating to or shaped like a cone

Examples

The mountain's peak had a distinct conic shape visible from miles away.
Many ancient structures were built with conic roofs to efficiently channel rainwater.
The scientist illustrated the crystal's conic formation during the presentation.

Synset Examples

conical mountains
conelike fruit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conical - having the shape of a cone

Etymology

From Greek kōnikos, from kōnos (cone).
