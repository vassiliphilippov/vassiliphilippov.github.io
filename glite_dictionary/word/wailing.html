wailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
loud, prolonged cries expressing grief, pain, or sorrow, typically made while weeping
loud cries while weeping

Examples

There was a persistent wailing from the baby next door all night.
The wailing of the mourners could be heard throughout the village.
His sudden wailing startled everyone in the quiet room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying - the act of shedding tears, especially as an expression of emotion
  • Synonym: sobbing - crying noisily while catching your breath

Etymology

From the verb 'wail', itself from Old Norse 'væla' meaning 'to lament'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing grief or sorrow vocally, or resembling such an expression; mournful or plaintive.
mournfully crying; expressing sorrow

Examples

The wailing sirens echoed through the deserted streets after midnight.
They listened to the wailing bagpipes as the funeral procession passed by.
A wailing wind rattled the old windows during the storm.

Synset Examples

lamenting sinners
wailing mourners
the wailing wind
wailful bagpipes
tangle her desires with wailful sonnets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournful - feeling, expressing, or inducing sadness, regret, or grief
  • Antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From the verb 'wail' (Middle English waylen, from Old Norse væl, 'lament').
