indistinctness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unclear or lacking sharp, definite outlines or features.
unclearness; lack of definite outlines

Examples

The indistinctness of the photograph made it hard to recognize anyone in it.
Through the fog, only the indistinctness of the buildings could be seen.
His speech was marked by a certain indistinctness that made it difficult to follow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to understand
  • Antonym: distinctness - the quality of being easy to recognize or clearly defined
  • Synonym: vagueness - the quality of being unclear or ill-defined

Etymology

from Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'distinct' + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix)
