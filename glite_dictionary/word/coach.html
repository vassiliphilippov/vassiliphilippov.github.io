coach

1. NOUN

/ koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a person who trains and directs athletes or a sports team, helping them improve their skills and performance
person who trains athletes or a team

Examples

The soccer team hired a new coach to improve their performance next season.
As a coach, she motivates her players both on and off the field.
He became a coach after retiring from professional basketball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trainer - person who teaches or prepares others, especially in sports
  • Antonym: athlete - a person trained to compete in sports

Etymology

From French *coche*, originally referring to a carriage; later applied metaphorically to someone who guides or instructs.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a large, enclosed horse-drawn carriage, typically pulled by four horses and driven by a coachman, used for carrying passengers
horse-drawn carriage for passengers

Examples

The royal family traveled to the palace in a magnificent coach.
In the 18th century, wealthy travelers often journeyed long distances by coach.
The old coach was displayed in the museum as an example of luxurious transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a wheeled vehicle for people, usually horse-drawn

Etymology

From French *coche*, from Hungarian *kocsi* (wagon from Kocs).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03059655-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a person who gives individual instruction or training in a particular subject, such as singing or acting
person who gives private instruction

Examples

She hired a coach to help improve her vocal technique.
The young actor worked with an experienced coach before his big audition.
A language coach assisted the cast with their accents for the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tutor - a private teacher, especially one who teaches a single student or a very small group
  • Synonym: instructor - a person who teaches or trains something
  • Antonym: student - a person who is taught by a coach

Etymology

From French 'coche', originally referring to a carriage; figurative sense of 'instructor' dates from the 19th century (students carrying others through exams).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09950876-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a railway carriage or car in which passengers ride, typically one of several attached to a train
railway carriage for passengers

Examples

She reserved a seat in the first-class coach for her trip to Edinburgh.
The train had eight coaches, each filled with passengers and their luggage.
We found our seats near the end of the coach, close to the dining car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a railway passenger vehicle
  • Degree Lower: compartment - a section of a railway coach

Etymology

From French 'coche', via German 'Kutsche', originally from Hungarian 'kocsi', meaning 'of Kocs' (a village where such carriages were first made).

Source: WordNet

large passenger vehicle, often for long distances
