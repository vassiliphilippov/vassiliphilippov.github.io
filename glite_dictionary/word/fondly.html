fondly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɑnd.li / (AmE), / ˈfɒnd.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows affection or love.
with affection or love

Examples

She looked fondly at the old photographs of her family.
He always remembers his childhood friends fondly despite the years apart.
The teacher spoke fondly of her former students at the reunion.

Synset Examples

she spoke to her children fondly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affectionately - in a way that shows affection

Etymology

From 'fond' + '-ly'; 'fond' from Middle English 'fond' (foolish, fond), later 'affectionate'.
