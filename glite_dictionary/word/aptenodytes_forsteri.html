Aptenodytes forsteri

1. NOUN

/ æpˌtɛnəˈdaɪtiːz fɔrˈstɛri / (AmE), / æpˌtiːnəˈdaɪtiːz fɔːˈstɛriː / (BrE)
The emperor penguin, the largest species of penguin, native to Antarctica.
the emperor penguin, Antarctic species

Examples

The Aptenodytes forsteri can survive in temperatures as low as -60°C during the Antarctic winter.
Researchers observed the breeding habits of Aptenodytes forsteri on the sea ice.
Young chicks of Aptenodytes forsteri huddle together for warmth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor penguin - common name for Aptenodytes forsteri, the largest Antarctic penguin

Etymology

From Greek 'apteno-' (without wings) + 'dytes' (diver); species name after Johann Reinhold Forster, German naturalist.
