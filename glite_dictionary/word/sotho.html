Sotho

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A group of closely related southern Bantu languages spoken by the Sotho people in Botswana, South Africa, and Lesotho.
a group of southern Bantu languages

Examples

Many people in Lesotho speak Sotho as their first language.
Sotho has a rich oral tradition and a growing body of written literature.
She is fluent in both Sotho and Zulu, which helps her communicate with a wide range of people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sesotho - the Sotho language spoken in Lesotho and South Africa

Etymology

From Sesotho, the endonym for the people and language.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Bantu ethnic group native to Lesotho, Botswana, and northern South Africa, who speak the Sotho languages.
member of a Bantu ethnic group in southern Africa

Examples

The Sotho are known for their distinctive traditional blankets and rich oral literature.
Many Sotho migrated to urban areas in South Africa during the 20th century.
She is proud of her Sotho heritage and teaches the language to her children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basotho - plural form referring to the Sotho people collectively
  • Synonym: Sotho language - the language spoken by the Sotho people

Etymology

From Sesotho, the name for the language and people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09712768-n
Synset POS: n
