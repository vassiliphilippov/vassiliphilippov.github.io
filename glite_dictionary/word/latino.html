Latino

1. NOUN

/ ləˈtiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ləˈtiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A person, especially a man or boy, who is a native of Latin America or whose family comes from Latin America.
a person from Latin America

Examples

His family identifies as Latino, with roots in Mexico.
Many Latino students attended the university's cultural festival.
The new mayor is the first Latino to hold the office in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latina - A woman or girl of Latin American origin

Etymology

From Spanish 'latino', short for 'latinoamericano' (Latin American), from Latin 'Latinus' (of Latium, Latin).

2. ADJ

/ ləˈtinoʊ / (AmE), / ləˈtiːnəʊ / (BrE)
Relating to people, culture, or things from Latin America, particularly Spanish-speaking countries or communities.
of or relating to Latin American people or culture

Examples

The city hosts a vibrant Latino cultural festival every summer.
She studies Latino literature at university to connect with her heritage.
Many Latino artists have influenced popular music around the world.

Synset Examples

the Hispanic population of California is growing rapidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hispanic - relating to Spanish-speaking people or culture, especially from Spain or Latin America

Etymology

From American Spanish 'latino', short for 'latinoamericano' (Latin American).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
