up-bow

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌbəʊ / (BrE)
an upward movement of the bow from tip to heel when playing a stringed instrument
upward bow movement (music)

Examples

The passage is played with a gentle up-bow to create a smooth sound.
Her technique included a perfectly controlled up-bow on the violin.
Start the phrase with a strong up-bow for greater emphasis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: down-bow - a downward stroke from the heel to the tip of the bow
