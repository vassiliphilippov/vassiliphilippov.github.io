viniculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnɪˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
The cultivation and production of grapes, especially for use in wine-making.
cultivation of grapes for wine

Examples

The region is known for its rich tradition of viniculture, producing some of the finest wines in the country.
Advancements in viniculture have led to increased grape yields and improved wine quality.
He studied viniculture at university to prepare for managing his family's vineyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viticulture - the science, production, and study of grapes

Etymology

From Latin 'vinum' (wine) and 'cultura' (cultivation).
