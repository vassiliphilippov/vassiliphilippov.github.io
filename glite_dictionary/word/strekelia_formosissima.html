Strekelia formosissima

1. NOUN

/ strɛˈkiːliə fɔrˌmoʊˈsɪsɪmə / (AmE), / strɛˈkiːliə fɔːˌmɒˈsɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A Mexican bulbous herb cultivated for its showy, bright red solitary flower, also known as the Aztec lily.
Mexican bulbous herb with red flower

Examples

The brilliant red flower of the Strekelia formosissima makes it a popular choice among gardeners.
Botanists admire the unique structure of Strekelia formosissima blooms found in Mexican habitats.
She planted a bulb of Strekelia formosissima to add a striking color to her garden in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aztec lily - Common name for Strekelia formosissima

Etymology

From Latin, with Strekelia named after Friedrich Adolph August Strecker, a German botanist, and formosissima meaning 'most beautiful'.
