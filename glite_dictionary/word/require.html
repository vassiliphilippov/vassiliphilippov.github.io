to require

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to need something; to have a necessity for something
need; have a necessity for

Examples

This job will require you to travel overseas frequently.
Good communication skills are required for this position.
The plants require plenty of sunlight and water to grow well.

Synset Examples

This piano wants the attention of a competent tuner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: need - to require something because it is essential or important
  • Synonym: demand - to ask for forcibly; to require as necessary

Etymology

From Latin requirere, from re- (again) + quaerere (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01191258-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to make it necessary for someone to do something, often by rule, law, or authority
make necessary; demand action from someone

Examples

The new policy will require all employees to wear identification badges at all times.
Most universities require students to submit applications before a certain deadline.
The job requires you to have at least five years of relevant experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - ask forcefully for something to be done or given
  • Antonym: permit - to allow someone to do something, especially officially
  • Degree Lower: request - ask for something politely or formally

Etymology

From Latin 'requirere' (to seek, ask for).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00753573-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rəˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to need or demand something as necessary, proper, or appropriate; to call for as essential
demand as necessary or proper

Examples

This work requires careful attention to detail.
The recipe requires several unusual ingredients.
Completing the course requires you to pass a final exam.

Synset Examples

It takes nerve to do what she did
success usually requires hard work
This job asks a lot of patience and skill
This position demands a lot of personal sacrifice
This dinner calls for a spectacular dessert
This intervention does not postulate a patient's consent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - to ask for forcefully or insistently
  • Synonym: to call for - to require as necessary or appropriate
  • Antonym: permit - to allow or make possible

Etymology

From Latin 'requirere', from 're-' (again) + 'quaerere' (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02634015-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ rɪˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to make something necessary or obligatory; to demand or expect someone to do something as a condition
to make something necessary or obligatory

Examples

All employees are required to wear identification badges at all times.
The job requires a high level of technical skill and attention to detail.
Passengers are required by law to fasten their seat belts while the vehicle is moving.

Synset Examples

We require our secretary to be on time
Aren't we asking too much of these children?
I expect my students to arrive in time for their lessons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, as a right
  • Synonym: to expect - to regard something as likely or required
  • Antonym: to allow - to permit or make possible

Etymology

From Old French 'requerre' (to seek, ask), from Latin 'requirere' (to seek, ask for, demand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00757790-v
Synset POS: v
