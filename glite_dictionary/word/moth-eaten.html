moth-eaten

1. ADJ

/ mɔθ ˌitən / (AmE), / mɒθ ˌiːtən / (BrE)
old and in poor condition because of long use or neglect; shabby or damaged, especially in a way resembling clothing eaten by moths
old and worn; shabby or damaged

Examples

His old moth-eaten jacket barely kept out the cold.
The theater’s curtains were faded and moth-eaten from years of use.
She refused to throw away her grandmother's moth-eaten quilt.

Synset Examples

a ratty old overcoat
shabby furniture
an old house with dirty windows and tatty curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: tatty - worn and shabby; in poor condition

Etymology

From 'moth' (a type of insect whose larvae eat fabric) + 'eaten' (past participle of 'eat')

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɔθ ˌitn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɒθ ˌiːtn̩ / (BrE)
(especially of fabric) worn or damaged in appearance, as though eaten by moths; shabby or dilapidated.
worn or damaged, as by moths

Examples

The old mansion was filled with moth-eaten carpets and faded wallpapers.
He wore a moth-eaten sweater that looked decades old.
Stacks of moth-eaten books lined the dusty shelves.

Synset Examples

moth-eaten blankets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin

Etymology

From 'moth' (the insect) + 'eaten', referring to the damage moths cause to fabric.

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑθˌitn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌiːtn̩ / (BrE)
(of ideas, things, etc.) lacking originality or freshness; outdated or stale.
stale or outdated; no longer original

Examples

His moth-eaten argument failed to impress the panel of judges.
The museum displayed several moth-eaten traditions that few people observe today.
I wish he would stop repeating those moth-eaten jokes every year.

Synset Examples

moth-eaten theories about race
stale news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stale - no longer fresh or original; uninteresting due to overuse
  • Antonym: original - fresh and not derived from something else; new

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'damaged by moths,' used metaphorically since the 19th century.

damaged or tattered by moths

Examples

The play took place in a moth-eaten theater that had clearly seen better days.
