bema

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːmə / (AmE), / ˈbiːmə / (BrE)
The area around the altar of a church, typically reserved for the clergy and choir and often enclosed by a lattice or railing.
area near altar for clergy and choir

Examples

The choir stood in the bema during the morning service.
Only clergy are permitted to enter the bema in this cathedral.
A decorative railing separates the bema from the nave of the church.

Etymology

From Greek *bēma* (platform, step), originally meaning 'platform' in ancient Greek.
