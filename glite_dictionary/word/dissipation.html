dissipation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of dispersing or scattering something so that it disappears or becomes less concentrated
dispersion or scattering

Examples

The morning sun led to the dissipation of the thick fog over the valley.
Efforts were made to prevent the dissipation of the gases into the atmosphere.
Wind contributed to the rapid dissipation of smoke from the burning building.

Synset Examples

the dissipation of the mist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersion - the act or process of spreading things over a wide area

Etymology

From Latin dissipatio, from dissipare (to scatter, disperse).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the careless or wasteful use or consumption of something, especially resources such as time, energy, or money
careless or wasteful use of something

Examples

The dissipation of public funds angered many voters.
Without careful planning, there can be needless dissipation of energy in the workplace.
Environmentalists warn against the dissipation of vital natural resources.

Synset Examples

if the effort brings no compensating gain, it is a waste
mindless dissipation of natural resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - the careless or unnecessary use or consumption of something valuable

Etymology

From Latin dissipatio (scattering, dispersion), from dissipare (to scatter, disperse).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
excessive indulgence in sensual pleasures; behavior that is morally lax and wasteful
excessive sensual indulgence

Examples

After years of dissipation, he found it hard to return to a disciplined lifestyle.
The novel depicts the dissipation of the wealthy elite in the roaring twenties.
Warnings about the dangers of dissipation fill the moral tracts of the era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauchery - excessive indulgence in sensual pleasures
  • Synonym: vice - immoral or wicked behavior, especially relating to sensual pleasure
  • Antonym: temperance - abstinence from indulgence, especially in alcoholic drink

Etymology

From Latin dissipatio (a scattering, dispersion), from dissipare (to scatter, squander).
