to enslave

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsleɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsleɪv / (BrE)
to make someone a slave; to cause someone to lose their freedom and be controlled as property by another
make someone a slave

Examples

Throughout history, conquerors have sought to enslave populations to increase their power.
It is wrong to enslave others or deprive them of their freedom.
The dictator enslaved the minority group by forcing them into hard labor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to emancipate - to set someone free from slavery or oppression
  • Synonym: to subjugate - to bring under control or domination, especially by conquest

Etymology

From Old French 'en-' (make) + 'esclave' (slave), from Medieval Latin 'sclavus' (slave).
