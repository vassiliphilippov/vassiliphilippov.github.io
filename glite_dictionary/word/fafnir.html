Fafnir

1. PROPN

/ ˈfæfnɪr / (AmE), / ˈfæfnɪə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) a dragon who guarded a hoard of treasure and was slain by the hero Sigurd.
Norse dragon slain by Sigurd

Examples

In the saga, Fafnir transforms into a dragon to guard his ill-gotten treasure.
The legend of Fafnir has inspired many fantasy stories and artworks.
Sigurd became famous after slaying the dragon Fafnir in Norse mythology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sigurd - the Norse hero who slew Fafnir

Etymology

From Old Norse Fáfnir, a name in Norse legend.
