Euonymus fortunei radicans

1. NOUN

/ juːˈɑnəməs fɔːrˈtuːni aɪ ˈrædɪkænz / (AmE), / juːˈɒnɪməs fɔːˈtjuːnaɪ ˈrædɪkænz / (BrE)
A broad, bushy Asiatic evergreen shrub with twining stems and pinkish fruit, cultivated for its attractive foliage; includes various subspecies and varieties.
Asiatic evergreen shrub (variety of Euonymus fortunei)

Examples

Euonymus fortunei radicans is often used as a ground cover due to its dense, spreading habit.
Gardeners appreciate Euonymus fortunei radicans for its tolerance to shade and urban conditions.
The bright pink fruit of Euonymus fortunei radicans adds color to autumn landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Euonymus', species name 'fortunei' (after Robert Fortune, Scottish botanist), and subspecies 'radicans' (Latin for 'rooting').
