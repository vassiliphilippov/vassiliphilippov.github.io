rouleau

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈloʊ / (AmE), / ruːˈləʊ / (BrE)
A roll of coins, especially one wrapped in paper for ease of handling and counting.
roll of coins wrapped in paper

Examples

The cashier handed me a rouleau of quarters for the vending machine.
She collected old coin rouleaux as a hobby.
Bank tellers often prepare each denomination in a separate rouleau for quick distribution.

Etymology

From French *rouleau* (roll), diminutive of *roule* (roll).

2. NOUN

/ ruːˈloʊ / (AmE), / ruːˈləʊ / (BrE)
a roll or coil of ribbon, fabric, or similar material, especially used in sewing or decoration
a roll of ribbon or fabric

Examples

The seamstress reached for a rouleau of satin to finish the dress.
Each invitation was tied with a delicate rouleau of silk ribbon.
She stored her collection of vintage rouleaux in a decorative box.

Etymology

From French 'rouleau' (small roll), from 'rouler' (to roll).
