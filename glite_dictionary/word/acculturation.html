acculturation

1. NOUN

/ əˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an individual or group adopts the cultural traits or social patterns of another group, especially those of the dominant or surrounding society
adoption of another culture's traits

Examples

The acculturation of immigrants often leads to changes in language, food, and customs.
Successful acculturation can help newcomers adapt to a new country while preserving their heritage.
Researchers study acculturation to understand how minority groups adapt to mainstream society.

Synset Examples

the socialization of children to the norms of their culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilation - the process of fully absorbing and integrating into a different culture

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'cultura' (growing, cultivation), via 'acculturate'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adopting or assimilating the cultural traits or social patterns of another group, or incorporating new ideas into an existing cultural or cognitive structure
process of adopting new cultural traits

Examples

The acculturation of immigrants often involves learning new customs and language while retaining some traditions.
Rapid acculturation can sometimes cause tension between generations within a community.
Students discussed the acculturation process when moving to a country with different cultural norms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to something; absorbing new information or traits

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'cultura' (cultivation); originally in anthropology.
