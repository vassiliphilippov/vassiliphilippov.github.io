unsocial

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
not inclined to seek or enjoy the company of others; preferring to be alone or without companions.
not sociable; solitary in nature

Examples

He became increasingly unsocial after moving to the countryside.
Her unsocial habits made it difficult for her to form close friendships.
Despite his unsocial nature, he was content with his solitary lifestyle.

Synset Examples

the unsocial disposition to neglect one's neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antisocial - actively avoiding or hostile to social interaction
  • Antonym: sociable - inclined to seek company; friendly and outgoing

Etymology

From Latin 'socialis' (of companionship), with prefix 'un-' (not).
