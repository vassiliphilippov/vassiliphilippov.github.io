Orontium

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɑnʃiəm / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɒntɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants in the arum family, known commonly as golden club.
genus of aquatic plant (golden club)

Examples

The pond was bordered by clusters of Orontium rising above the water.
Botanists studied the unique characteristics of Orontium during their fieldwork.
Orontium is easily identified by its bright yellow flower spike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden club - common name for the aquatic plant Orontium aquaticum

Etymology

From Greek Orontion, a plant name.

2. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɒntiəm / (AmE), / ɒˈrɒntɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants in the family Araceae, containing the single species known as the golden club.
genus of aquatic plants (golden club)

Examples

The Orontium is noted for its distinctive golden flower spike above water.
Botanists have studied Orontium in its native wetland habitats.
Many gardeners value Orontium for its ornamental appeal in ponds.

Etymology

From Latin Orontium, the original name given by Linnaeus; possibly after the river Orontes in the Middle East.
