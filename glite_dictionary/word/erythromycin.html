erythromycin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, especially those caused by Gram-positive bacteria
antibiotic medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed erythromycin to treat her throat infection.
Some patients may experience stomach upset when taking erythromycin.
Allergic reactions to erythromycin are rare but can occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms

Etymology

From International Scientific Vocabulary, combining Greek 'erythros' (red) and 'mykes' (fungus).
