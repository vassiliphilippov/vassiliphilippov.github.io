beneficence

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnɛfəsəns / (AmE), / bɪˈnɛfɪsəns / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, helpful, or generous; active goodness or charity
kindness or generosity

Examples

The hospital relies on the beneficence of its volunteers to keep running smoothly.
Her acts of beneficence earned her widespread admiration and respect.
Many religions emphasize the importance of beneficence towards strangers and the less fortunate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - generous actions or donations to aid the needy
  • Antonym: malevolence - the state of having or showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin beneficentia (kindness, generosity), from beneficus (doing good).

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈnɛfəsəns / (AmE), / bɪˈnɛfɪsəns / (BrE)
the quality or practice of doing good and showing kindness or charity to others
doing good; kindness

Examples

The hospital staff demonstrated remarkable beneficence toward their patients.
Acts of beneficence are highly valued in medical ethics.
Her beneficence inspired others to participate in charitable work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning; kindness
  • Antonym: malevolence - the state or condition of wishing harm to others

Etymology

From Latin beneficentia (kindness, generosity), from beneficus (doing good).
