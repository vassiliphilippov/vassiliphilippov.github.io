vena facialis anterior

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə fəˈseɪlɪs ænˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ˌviːnə fæˈseɪlɪs ænˈtɪərɪə / (BrE)
A vein of the face that is a continuation of the angular vein and unites with the retromandibular vein before emptying into the internal jugular vein.
facial vein branch; drains into internal jugular vein
medical

Examples

The vena facialis anterior plays a critical role in draining blood from the facial region.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the vena facialis anterior.
The vena facialis anterior merges with the retromandibular vein before entering the internal jugular vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial vein - main vein that drains the face

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'facialis' (of the face) + 'anterior' (in front).
