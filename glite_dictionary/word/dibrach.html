dibrach

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪbræk / (AmE), / ˈdaɪbrak / (BrE)
A metrical unit (foot) consisting of two unstressed syllables.
metrical unit of two unstressed syllables

Examples

The ancient poem features a rare dibrach followed by a spondee in the second line.

In this analysis, the dibrach interrupts the regular iambic rhythm.

From Greek *dibrachys* (twice short).
