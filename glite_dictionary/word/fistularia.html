Fistularia

1. NOUN

/ fɪs.tjuˈlɛəri.ə / (AmE), / fɪs.tjʊˈlɑː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of slender, elongated marine fishes in the family Fistulariidae, commonly known as cornetfishes.
genus of cornetfishes

Examples

The genus Fistularia includes several species of cornetfishes found in tropical and subtropical oceans.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Fistularia genus off the coast of Indonesia.
The long, tube-like snout is characteristic of all members of Fistularia.

Etymology

From Latin 'fistula' (pipe, tube), referring to the tubular snout of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ fɪs.tjuˈlɛəri.ə / (AmE), / fɪs.tjʊˈlɑː.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Fistulariidae, commonly known as cornetfish, characterized by their elongated bodies and tubular snouts.
genus of marine fish (cornetfish)

Examples

The Fistularia are easily recognized by their long, slender bodies and tubular snouts.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the genus Fistularia during the recent expedition.
The diet of Fistularia consists mainly of small fish and invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornetfish - Common name for fish in the genus Fistularia

Etymology

From Latin fistula (pipe or tube), referring to the tubular snout.
