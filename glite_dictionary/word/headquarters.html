headquarters

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌkwɔrtərz / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
The main office or center of operations for an organization, where administrative activities are managed.
main office of an organization

Examples

The company's global headquarters are located in London.
During the crisis, all decisions came directly from military headquarters.
She was transferred to headquarters after five years in regional offices.

Synset Examples

many companies have their headquarters in New York

Related Terms

  • Antonym: branch - a local office or subsidiary of a larger organization

Etymology

From 'head', meaning main or principal, and 'quarters', meaning lodgings or rooms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌkwɔrtərz / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
A military unit composed of a commander and their headquarters staff, responsible for planning and directing operations.
military unit with commander and staff

Examples

The division's headquarters was responsible for coordinating the various battalions.
An order from headquarters instructed the troops to move out at dawn.
He was assigned to headquarters as part of the command staff last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command post - a place from which a military commander issues orders
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌkwɔrtərz / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the place from which military operations are directed and where the commander and their staff are based
command center for military operations

Examples

The general set up a temporary headquarters near the front lines to oversee the battle.
Enemy plans were intercepted before they could reach the headquarters of the division.
The old mansion was converted into the regiment's headquarters during the war.

Synset Examples

the general's headquarters were a couple of large tents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command post - a place from which military commands are issued

Etymology

From 'head' + 'quarters', referring to the main place (quarters) of leadership.
