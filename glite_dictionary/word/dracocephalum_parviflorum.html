Dracocephalum parviflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdreɪkoʊˈkɛfələm ˌpɑrvɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌdrakəˈkɛfələm ˌpɑːvɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A North American herb with sharply toothed lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers, belonging to the mint family.
North American herb with blue-violet flowers

Examples

Dracocephalum parviflorum is commonly found in wet meadows across much of North America.
Botanists admire the vibrant blue flowers of Dracocephalum parviflorum each spring.
Gardeners plant Dracocephalum parviflorum for its ornamental spikes and aromatic leaves.

Etymology

From Latin: 'dracocephalum' meaning 'dragon's head' and 'parviflorum' meaning 'small-flowered'.
