Sunderland

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌndərlənd / (AmE), / ˈsʌndələnd / (BrE)
a port and industrial city in the northeast of England, on the North Sea at the mouth of the River Wear
city in northeastern England

Examples

Sunderland has a rich history of shipbuilding and coal mining.
Many football fans support Sunderland AFC, the local soccer team.
The River Wear flows through Sunderland and out to the North Sea.

Etymology

From Old English 'Sundorland' meaning 'separated land', referring to land set apart.
