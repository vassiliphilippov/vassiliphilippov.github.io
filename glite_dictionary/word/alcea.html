Alcea

1. NOUN

/ ælˈsiːə / (AmE), / ælˈsiːə / (BrE)
A genus of upright herbs native to the Middle East, known for their large, showy flowers; hollyhocks.
genus of flowering plants (hollyhocks)

Examples

The tall Alcea in her garden bloomed vividly throughout the summer.
Many gardeners plant Alcea to create a colorful backdrop along fences and walls.
Researchers studied various species within the Alcea genus for their horticultural potential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollyhock - A tall plant with large, colorful flowers, commonly cultivated in gardens

Etymology

From Latin 'Alcea', from Greek 'alkēa', a plant name related to 'althaia' (mallow).
