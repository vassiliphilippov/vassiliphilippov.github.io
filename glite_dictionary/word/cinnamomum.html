Cinnamomum

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnæməˌmʊm / (AmE), / sɪˈnæmənəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic trees and shrubs native to Asia and Australia, including the sources of cinnamon and camphor.
genus of aromatic trees and shrubs

Examples

Species in the Cinnamomum genus are valued for their aromatic bark and oils.
The most widely known member of Cinnamomum is the cinnamon tree, used as a spice.
Botanists studied several Cinnamomum species to understand their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin 'cinnamomum', from Greek 'kinnamōmon', meaning 'cinnamon'.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˌnæməˈmoʊm / (AmE), / sɪˌnæməˈməʊm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic trees and shrubs of Asia and Australia, including the tree that produces cinnamon.
genus of aromatic trees/shrubs including cinnamon

Examples

Most spices labeled as Cinnamomum in markets come from specific regions of Asia.
The essential oils in the bark of Cinnamomum trees are valued in medicine and cuisine.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within the genus Cinnamomum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - the aromatic spice obtained from the inner bark of trees in the genus Cinnamomum

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek kinnamōmon (cinnamon), the name of the spice.
