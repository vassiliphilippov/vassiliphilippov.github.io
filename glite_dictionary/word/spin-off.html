spin-off

1. NOUN

/ spɪn ˌɔf / (AmE), / spɪn ˌɒf / (BrE)
a secondary product that is produced during the manufacture of another product; a by-product
secondary product; by-product

Examples

The new cleaning solvent was a spin-off from the company's research into plastics.
During the chemical process, several useful spin-offs were created alongside the main compound.
One important spin-off of the project was a more efficient method of recycling materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by-product - a secondary product resulting from a manufacturing process
  • Antonym: main product - the primary product produced in a manufacturing process

Etymology

From the verb 'spin off,' meaning to create something as a by-product or offshoot, originally from 'spin,' referring to the act of turning fibers into thread.
