Trichoceros

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑsəˌrəs / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒsərəs / (BrE)
A small genus of epiphytic or terrestrial orchids native to tropical South America.
genus of South American orchids

Examples

The Trichoceros genus includes several orchid species prized for their unusual floral shapes.
Researchers discovered a new Trichoceros specimen in the rainforests of Ecuador.
Many collectors seek out Trichoceros orchids due to their rarity and beauty.

Etymology

From Greek 'thrix' (hair) + 'keras' (horn), referring to features of the orchid's flower structure.

2. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑːsərəs / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒsərəs / (BrE)
a small genus of epiphytic or terrestrial orchids native to tropical South America
orchid genus from South America

Examples

The Trichoceros genus includes several striking species found in Ecuadorian cloud forests.
Many orchid enthusiasts value Trichoceros for its unusual flower shapes.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Trichoceros in the Andean foothills.

Etymology

From Greek tricho- (hair) and keras (horn), referring to hair-like or horn-shaped structures in the flowers.
