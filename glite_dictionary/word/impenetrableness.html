impenetrableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəb(ə)lnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible to understand or penetrate; the state of being impenetrable, either physically or intellectually.
quality of being impenetrable

Examples

The impenetrableness of the philosopher’s argument left many readers confused.
Due to the impenetrableness of the fog, the ships could not navigate safely.
The novel’s impenetrableness discouraged some readers from finishing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensibility - the quality of being impossible to understand
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of being easy to see through or understand

Etymology

From Latin impenetrabilis (not to be penetrated) + -ness suffix.
