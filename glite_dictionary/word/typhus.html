typhus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈtɪ.fəs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by rickettsial bacteria, transmitted by lice or fleas, and marked by high fever, headache, and a skin rash
infectious disease with fever and rash
medical

Examples

The outbreak of typhus in the refugee camp caused widespread concern among health workers.
During the 19th century, typhus epidemics swept through crowded cities, claiming thousands of lives.
Body lice are known to spread typhus, especially in areas with poor hygiene.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typhoid fever - bacterial disease often confused with typhus, but caused by Salmonella typhi

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'typhos' (fever, stupor).
