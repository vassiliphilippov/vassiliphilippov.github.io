doozy

1. NOUN

/ ˈduzi / (AmE), / ˈduːzi / (BrE)
something outstanding or remarkable, often unusually impressive or difficult
something remarkable or outstanding
informal

Examples

That last question on the exam was a doozy and took me twenty minutes to solve.
The storm last night was a real doozy; trees were knocked down all over town.
His new novel is a doozy, full of unexpected plot twists and vivid characters.

Synset Examples

Watch out for that Newfoundland speed bump; it's a doozy!
Her birthday cake was a doozy, towering over the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilly - something or someone remarkable or unusual

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly an alteration of "daisy" (something excellent) or related to the Duesenberg automobile, once considered extraordinary.
