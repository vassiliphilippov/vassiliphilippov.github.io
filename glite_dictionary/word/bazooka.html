bazooka

1. NOUN

/ bəˈzʊkə / (AmE), / bəˈzuːkə / (BrE)
a lightweight, portable rocket launcher, operated by one person, used as an antitank weapon by infantry
portable antitank rocket launcher

Examples

The soldier aimed his bazooka at the approaching tank.
During World War II, the bazooka became a common infantry weapon.
He displayed a decommissioned bazooka at the military museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocket launcher - a device that launches rockets as weapons

Etymology

From the name of a musical instrument created by American comedian Bob Burns in the 1930s; later applied to the weapon due to its similar appearance.
