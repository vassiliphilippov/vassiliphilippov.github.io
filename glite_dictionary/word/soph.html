soph

1. NOUN

/ sɑf / (AmE), / sɒf / (BrE)
An informal term for a second-year student at a college or university, especially in the United States.
informal term for a second-year college student
informal american

Examples

She became friends with several sophs during her first week on campus.
After finishing their freshman year, the students proudly called themselves sophs.
The university organizes a special orientation program for new sophs every fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sophomore - A student in the second year of college or high school in the US

Etymology

Abbreviated form of 'sophomore', from Greek sophos (wise) and moros (foolish).
