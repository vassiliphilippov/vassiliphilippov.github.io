Lathyrus tuberosus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪɹəs tuːˈbɛɹəsəs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪərəs tjuːˈbɜːrəsəs / (BrE)
A European perennial herb of the pea family, bearing small edible tubers and sometimes used in Scotland to flavor whiskey.
a European herb with edible tubers

Examples

The Lathyrus tuberosus is valued for its small, nutritious tubers.
In some regions, the tubers of Lathyrus tuberosus are collected and eaten as a delicacy.
Whiskey in Scotland is sometimes flavored with the roots of Lathyrus tuberosus.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lathyrus' is the genus name for vetchlings, and 'tuberosus' means 'tuberous' or 'with tubers.'
