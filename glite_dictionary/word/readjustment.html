readjustment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (BrE)
the act of adjusting again, especially to new or changed circumstances
the act of adjusting again

Examples

After losing his job, the period of readjustment was challenging but ultimately rewarding.
The recent merger required significant readjustment by all staff members.
Moving to a different country often involves a difficult readjustment to new customs and routines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - the process of adapting or becoming used to a new situation

Etymology

From re- (again) + adjustment, formed in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (BrE)
the act of adjusting something again, especially to make it conform to a standard or requirement
the act of adjusting again

Examples

After the policy change, a careful readjustment of the procedures was necessary.
Her readjustment to life after college took several months.
The team made a quick readjustment to their strategy during halftime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - the act of making something more suitable or fitting

Etymology

From re- (again) + adjustment, derived from adjust (from Old French 'ajuster', from Late Latin 'adjuxtare').
