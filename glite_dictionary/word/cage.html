cage

1. NOUN

/ keɪdʒ / (AmE), / keɪdʒ / (BrE)
A situation, condition, or thing that limits or restricts freedom or movement, similar to how a physical cage confines.
something that restricts freedom

Examples

She felt that her job was a cage, keeping her from pursuing her true passions.
His fear acted as a cage, preventing him from experiencing new things.
Many people yearn to break free from the invisible cage of social expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building or place where people are legally held as a punishment or while awaiting trial
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants

2. NOUN

/ keɪdʒ / (AmE), / keɪdʒ / (BrE)
In ice hockey, the netted structure that serves as the goal.
goal net in ice hockey

Examples

The puck shot past the goalie and into the cage for a goal.
The defense worked hard to protect their cage throughout the game.
He stood in front of the cage, ready to block any incoming shots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - the net or structure players aim to score into in sports

3. NOUN

/ keɪdʒ / (AmE), / keɪdʒ / (BrE)
a structure of bars or wires in which birds or animals are kept, preventing them from escaping
enclosure of bars for animals or birds

Examples

The parrot sang happily in its cage by the window.
She cleaned the rabbit's cage every morning before breakfast.
Many zoos have spacious cages to keep animals safe yet visible to visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an enclosure for confining animals

Etymology

From Old French 'cage', from Latin 'cavea' (hollow, cavity, cage).
