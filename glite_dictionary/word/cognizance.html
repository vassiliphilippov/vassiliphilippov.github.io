cognizance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːɡnɪzəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪzəns / (BrE)
Knowledge or awareness of something; the state of being aware or informed.
knowledge; awareness

Examples

The company took cognizance of the complaints and promised to investigate.
She had no cognizance of the important changes happening around her.
The judge said he would take cognizance of all the evidence before making a decision.

Synset Examples

he had no awareness of his mistakes
his sudden consciousness of the problem he faced
their intelligence and general knowingness was impressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awareness - knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Old French 'cognissance' and Latin 'cognoscere' (to know).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːɡnɪzəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪzəns / (BrE)
the range or scope of what someone can know or understand
range of what can be known or understood

Examples

That topic is beyond my cognizance, so I can't speak about it with authority.
The accident brought the issue within the cognizance of the authorities.
His cognizance of modern art is surprisingly broad for someone trained in engineering.

Synset Examples

beyond my ken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ken - range of knowledge or understanding

Etymology

From Old French *cognissance*, based on Latin *cognoscere* (to know).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːɡnɪzəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪz(ə)ns / (BrE)
The range or scope of knowledge, awareness, or perception.
range or scope of awareness

Examples

The matter did not come within the cognizance of the committee.
Scientists must broaden their cognizance of emerging issues facing the environment.
Within his cognizance, the events were unprecedented and deeply troubling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awareness - knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Old French 'connaisance' (recognition), from Latin 'cognoscere' (to know).
