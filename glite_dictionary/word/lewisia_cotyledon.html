Lewisia cotyledon

1. NOUN

/ luˈisiə ˌkɒtəˈlidən / (AmE), / luˈwiːsiə ˌkɒtɪˈliːdɒn / (BrE)
an evergreen perennial plant native to southwestern Oregon and northern California, with a basal rosette of spatula-shaped leaves and showy pink or white-striped flowers, often found in cliffs or rock crevices
a flowering perennial plant of southwestern Oregon and northern California

Examples

The gardener planted several Lewisia cotyledon near the rock wall for their striking flowers.
In spring, the Lewisia cotyledon bloomed with vibrant pink and white blossoms.
These cliffs provide the perfect habitat for Lewisia cotyledon to thrive.

Etymology

Genus 'Lewisia' named after explorer Meriwether Lewis; 'cotyledon' from Greek 'kotyledon' (cup-shaped cavity, seed leaf).
