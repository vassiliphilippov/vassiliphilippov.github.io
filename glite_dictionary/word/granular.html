granular

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræn.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
composed of or resembling small grains or particles; having a texture or structure made up of granules.
composed of small grains or particles

Examples

The sandpaper’s granular surface helped smooth the wood efficiently.
The scientist noted a granular texture under the microscope, similar to certain meteorites.
Her voice took on a granular quality as the recording distorted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grainy - having a texture of many small particles or grains

Etymology

From Latin granulum (small grain); related to granum (grain).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡræn.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
composed of or covered with small particles or grains, like meal in texture or consistency.
consisting of or resembling small grains

Examples

The recipe called for granular sugar instead of powdered sugar.
The sandpaper left a granular finish on the surface of the wood.
After evaporation, a granular residue remained in the bottom of the beaker.

Synset Examples

granular sugar
the photographs were grainy and indistinct
it left a mealy residue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grainy - having a rough texture, like containing grains
  • Synonym: mealy - having a texture like meal; granular or powdery
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not granular

Etymology

From Latin granulum, diminutive of granum (grain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02239247-s
Synset POS: s
