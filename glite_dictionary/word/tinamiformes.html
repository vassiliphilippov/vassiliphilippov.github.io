Tinamiformes

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌnæməˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / tɪˌnæmɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of ground-dwelling birds found in Central and South America, comprising the family Tinamidae (tinamous).
order of birds (tinamous)

Examples

The Tinamiformes are native to Central and South America, inhabiting a variety of forest and grassland environments.
Unlike other ground birds, members of the Tinamiformes can fly, though only for short distances.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary relationship between Tinamiformes and other ancient bird lineages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tinamidae - the family of birds known as tinamous

Etymology

From New Latin Tinamiformes, formed from Tinamus (type genus) + Latin -formes ('having the form of').

2. NOUN

/ tɪˌnæmɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / tɪˌnamɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds comprising the tinamous, coextensive with the family Tinamidae, native to Central and South America
order of birds including tinamous

Examples

The Tinamiformes are known for their ground-dwelling habits and secretive behavior.
Researchers studied the distribution of Tinamiformes across different South American habitats.
Museum collections often include eggs of the Tinamiformes due to their unique coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinamou - a bird of the family Tinamidae, native to Central and South America

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Tinamidae' (tinamous) and '-formes' (in the form of).
