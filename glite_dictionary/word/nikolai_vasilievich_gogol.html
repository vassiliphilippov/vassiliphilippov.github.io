Nikolai Vasilievich Gogol

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkoʊlaɪ vəˈsɪliəvɪtʃ ˈɡoʊɡəl / (AmE), / nɪˈkɒlaɪ vəˈsɪliəvɪtʃ ˈɡəʊɡɒl / (BrE)
(1809–1852) A Russian writer noted for introducing realism into Russian literature and for works such as 'Dead Souls' and 'The Overcoat'.
Russian author, pioneer of realism

Examples

Nikolai Vasilievich Gogol is regarded as a founding figure in Russian literature.
The satirical stories of Nikolai Vasilievich Gogol have influenced generations of writers.
Many scholars study Nikolai Vasilievich Gogol to understand the origins of literary realism.
