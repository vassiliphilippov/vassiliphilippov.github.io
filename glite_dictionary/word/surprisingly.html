surprisingly

1. ADV

/ sərˈpraɪzɪŋli / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪzɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes surprise; unexpectedly or astonishingly.
unexpected or astonishingly

Examples

Surprisingly, the small restaurant was fully booked on a Tuesday night.
The results were surprisingly positive, exceeding even our highest expectations.
She surprisingly agreed to join us, even though she usually prefers to stay home.

Synset Examples

amazingly, he finished medical school in three years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazingly - in a way that causes amazement or great surprise
  • Antonym: predictably - in a way that happens as expected, not surprising

Etymology

From 'surprise' + '-ingly', where 'surprise' comes from Old French 'surprendre' (to overtake), from Latin 'super-' (over) + 'prehendere' (to seize).

2. ADV

/ sɚˈpraɪzɪŋli / (AmE), / səˈpraɪzɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes surprise; unexpectedly or in a manner that is not anticipated.
in an unexpected manner

Examples

Surprisingly, the tiny restaurant was completely booked for the evening.
She finished the marathon surprisingly quickly, beating her personal best time.
The test was surprisingly easy considering how much everyone had worried about it.

Synset Examples

he was surprisingly friendly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - in a way that was not expected
  • Antonym: predictably - in a way that is expected

Etymology

Formed from 'surprising' (from 'surprise') + '-ly' adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00146264-r
Synset POS: r
