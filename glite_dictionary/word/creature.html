creature

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈkriːtʃə / (BrE)
A living being, especially an animal or imaginary being, rather than a plant or inanimate object.
a living being, usually animal

Examples

The forest is home to many mysterious creatures that are rarely seen by humans.
She believes that every living creature deserves kindness and respect.
The legend describes a terrifying creature that appeared during the full moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: being - a living creature; existence
  • Degree Lower: animal - a living organism that feeds on organic matter
  • Degree Higher: person - a human being

Etymology

From Latin 'creatura' (something created), from 'creare' (to create).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkriː.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkriː.tʃə / (BrE)
a person who is controlled by others and used to perform unpleasant or dishonest tasks for someone else
person controlled and used by others

Examples

He was merely a creature of the corrupt politician, carrying out orders without question.
She refused to become a creature of her wealthy employer and insisted on her own independence.
Many regarded the new manager as a creature of the board, lacking any original ideas of his own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tool - person used by others to accomplish something, often derogatory
  • Synonym: puppet - person who is controlled by someone else

Etymology

From Latin 'creatura' (a created thing), from 'creare' (to create).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkriːtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkriːtʃə / (BrE)
A living being, especially an animal, that is capable of movement and considered as distinct from plants or inanimate objects.
a living being, especially an animal

Examples

The forest is home to many fascinating creatures that are rarely seen by humans.
Under the microscope, the pond revealed hundreds of tiny creatures swimming about.
The children were delighted to see a strange creature crawling in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal - a living organism that feeds on organic matter and typically has specialized sense organs and nervous system
  • Antonym: plant - a living organism lacking specialized sense organs and voluntary movement

Etymology

From Old French *creature*, from Latin *creatura* (a created thing), from *creare* (to create).
