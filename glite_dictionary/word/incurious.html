incurious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkjʊriəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊəriəs / (BrE)
not interested in learning about things; lacking curiosity or inquisitiveness.
not curious; lacking interest

Examples

Despite the exciting rumors, she remained incurious and barely asked any questions.
He was oddly incurious about the new neighbor's background.
Their incurious attitude surprised the journalists covering the event.

Synset Examples

strangely incurious about the cause of the political upheaval surrounding them

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curious - eager to know or learn something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix in- (not) + curious (from Latin curiosus, 'careful, inquisitive').
