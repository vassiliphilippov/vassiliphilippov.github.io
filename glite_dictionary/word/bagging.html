bagging

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɡɪŋ / (BrE)
a coarse fabric, typically made from jute or similar fibers, used for making bags or sacks
coarse fabric for sacks or bags

Examples

The potatoes were packed in heavy bagging and stacked in the corner.
They bought several yards of bagging to sew their own storage sacks.
The factory specializes in producing bagging for agricultural uses.

Etymology

From 'bag' + '-ing', denoting material used for making bags.
