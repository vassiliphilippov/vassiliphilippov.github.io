disdainfully

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈdeɪnfəli / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪnfəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows contempt or a lack of respect.
in a contemptuous, disrespectful way

Examples

He glanced disdainfully at the messy pile of clothes on the floor.
The judge listened disdainfully to the defendant's excuses.
She rejected the proposal disdainfully, making her disapproval clear to everyone.

Synset Examples

she spoke of him contemptuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuously - in a scornful or disrespectful manner

Etymology

From 'disdainful' + '-ly'; 'disdainful' derives from Old French 'desdaigner'.

2. ADV

/ dɪsˈdeɪn.fəli / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows contempt or lack of respect; scornfully.
in a contemptuous or scornful manner

Examples

She glanced at the proposal disdainfully, clearly unimpressed by the suggestions.
He disdainfully refused to answer any of the reporter’s questions.
The critic disdainfully dismissed the film as unimaginative and dull.

Synset Examples

he treated his staff cavalierly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuously - in a manner showing contempt
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a manner showing respect

Etymology

From 'disdainful' + '-ly'; 'disdainful' from Old French 'desdeignous', from 'desdaigner' (to disdain), from Latin 'dedignari'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00284129-r
Synset POS: r
