headed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of leafy vegetables) having formed a dense cluster or 'head' of leaves.
leafy vegetable with a compact head

Examples

The farmer harvested the headed cabbages in early summer.
Certain varieties of lettuce are bred to grow as headed vegetables.
Only headed broccoli should be selected for shipping to the market.

Synset Examples

headed cabbages

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headless - (of vegetables) lacking a head or compact cluster
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01492570-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of a document, column, or page) having a heading or caption at the top.
having a heading or caption

Examples

The company sends all its letters on headed paper to look professional.
Please write the title in the headed section of the table.
The report was submitted with a properly headed first page.

Synset Examples

a headed column
headed notepaper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unheaded - lacking a heading or caption
Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Having a head of a specified kind, or provided with something serving as a head; often used as the second element in compound adjectives.
having a head of a specified kind (esp. in compounds)

Examples

The story tells of a three-headed monster guarding the cave.
She is a calm and cool-headed leader even in a crisis.
All headed bolts were carefully sorted in the workshop.

Synset Examples

headed bolts
three-headed Cerberus
a cool-headed fighter pilot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: -headed - forming compound adjectives indicating type of head (e.g., curly-headed, cold-headed)

4. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or group) having a heading or proceeding in a specified direction.
going in a specified direction

Examples

Several east-headed trains passed through the station before noon.
The weather forecast mentioned a storm headed north along the coast.
We saw a group of hikers headed up the mountain trail early in the morning.

Synset Examples
