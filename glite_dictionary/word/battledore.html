battledore

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əlˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əlˌdɔː / (BrE)
an early form of a light, long-handled racket used in games such as badminton or battledore and shuttlecock
early lightweight racket (badminton)

Examples

In Victorian times, children often played with a battledore and shuttlecock in the garden.
The museum displayed an antique battledore next to a modern badminton racket.
Before the invention of today's rackets, players used a wooden battledore for the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racket - a bat with a round or oval frame strung with catgut, used in sports such as tennis or badminton

Etymology

From Old French 'batteour' (beater, striker) and Middle English 'batelour.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl.dɔːr / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl.dɔː / (BrE)
an ancient racket game, similar to badminton, played with rackets and a shuttlecock
ancient racket game with shuttlecock

Examples

Children in the nineteenth century often played battledore in the garden.
The origins of badminton can be traced back to the game of battledore.
He collected old battledore rackets as part of his interest in historical sports.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: badminton - a modern racket game descended from battledore

Etymology

From Middle French 'batedouere', apparently from battre (to beat) + d'ouer (of the play).
