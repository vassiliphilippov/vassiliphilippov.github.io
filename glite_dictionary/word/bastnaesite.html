bastnaesite

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstnəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæsnəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
A yellow to brown mineral, consisting mainly of carbonate and fluoride of cerium and other rare earth elements, and serving as a major source of rare earths.
a rare earth mineral

Examples

The newly discovered deposit contains large quantities of bastnaesite, a valuable resource for rare earth elements.
Scientists analyzed the composition of bastnaesite samples from the mine to assess their economic potential.
Most of the world's supply of cerium is extracted from minerals like bastnaesite.

Etymology

Named after Bastnäs, a mine in Sweden where the mineral was originally discovered.
