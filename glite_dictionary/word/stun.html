to stun

1. VERB

/ stʌn / (AmE), / stʌn / (BrE)
to make someone or something senseless or dazed, especially as a result of a blow or a shock
make someone or something senseless or dazed

Examples

The loud explosion managed to stun everyone in the building for a few moments.
The fisherman used an electric current to stun the fish before catching them.
Boxers are trained to recover quickly if they are stunned by a punch in the ring.

Synset Examples

stun fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daze - make someone unable to think clearly
  • Antonym: to revive - restore consciousness or alertness

2. VERB

/ stʌn / (AmE), / stʌn / (BrE)
to shock or greatly surprise someone so that they are temporarily unable to react
to shock or greatly surprise

Examples

The sudden announcement stunned everyone in the office into silence.
I was stunned by the beauty of the landscape when I first saw it.
Her unexpected resignation stunned the entire team.

Synset Examples

The news stunned her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amaze - to surprise greatly; fill with wonder
  • Synonym: to astound - to shock or greatly surprise
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or unsuprised

Etymology

From Old French 'estoner', from Latin 'extonare' (to thunder out).

3. VERB

/ stʌn / (AmE), / stʌn / (BrE)
to hit someone or something so hard that they become temporarily unconscious or incapacitated, as if struck with a sandbag
to knock out or incapacitate by a blow

Examples

The force of the blow managed to stun the attacker for several minutes.
He was stunned by a sudden punch before he could react.
A falling branch struck the animal, temporarily stunning it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to make someone unconscious, esp. by a blow
  • Antonym: to revive - to bring someone back from unconsciousness

Etymology

From Old French *estoner*, from Latin *extonare* (to thunder out).
