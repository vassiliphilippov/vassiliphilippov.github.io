Canavalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈvæliə / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈvalɪə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs or woody vines native to mainly the American tropics and subtropics, including the jack bean and sword bean
a plant genus of tropical herbs or vines

Examples

The species Canavalia ensiformis is commonly known as the jack bean.
Some Canavalia species are used as green manure crops to enrich soil.
Botanists discovered a new Canavalia vine growing in the rainforest.

Etymology

From Latinized form of a Brazilian native name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnævəˈliːə / (AmE), / kəˌnævəˈliːə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical herbs or woody vines in the pea family, native mainly to the Americas
genus of tropical vines or herbs

Examples

Some species of Canavalia are cultivated for their edible beans.
The Canavalia genus includes the well-known jack bean plant.
Botanists recently discovered a new Canavalia species in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Latinized form of a Portuguese or Spanish word related to the jack bean.
