microcrystalline

1. ADJ

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈkrɪstəlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈkrɪstəlaɪn / (BrE)
Consisting of crystals so small that they can only be seen with a microscope.
containing very small crystals visible only under a microscope

Examples

The rock sample has a microcrystalline structure visible only under a powerful microscope.
Some types of wax are produced in a microcrystalline form, giving them unique textures and uses.
The laboratory analysis revealed that the mineral was primarily microcrystalline in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crystalline - composed of or resembling crystals

Etymology

From micro- (small) + crystalline, influenced by Greek 'krystallos' (ice, crystal).
