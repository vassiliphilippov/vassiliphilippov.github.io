Richard Wagner

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ˈvɑːɡnɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ˈvɑːɡnə / (BrE)
(1813–1883) A German composer noted for his operas and for inventing the musical drama, where music, drama, and spectacle are combined.
German opera composer (1813–1883)

Examples

Many critics consider Richard Wagner one of the most influential composers in opera history.
The music of Richard Wagner is renowned for its emotional depth and complexity.
During the nineteenth century, Richard Wagner revolutionized the concept of musical drama.
