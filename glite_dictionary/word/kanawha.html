Kanawha

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnɑːwə / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːwə / (BrE)
A river in West Virginia, United States, which is a major tributary of the Ohio River.
river in West Virginia, USA

Examples

The Kanawha River flows through several towns before joining the Ohio River.
Many species of fish are found in the waters of the Kanawha.
Flooding along the Kanawha has historically affected communities in western West Virginia.

Etymology

From a Native American (Siouan) word, likely from the name for the region or river.
