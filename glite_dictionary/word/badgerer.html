badgerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbædʒərər / (AmE), / ˈbædʒərə / (BrE)
a person who persistently annoys or harasses someone with questions, criticism, or gibes
someone who persistently harasses or annoys

Examples

The persistent badgerer kept interrupting the speaker with challenging questions.
If you give in to a badgerer, they may never stop pestering you for answers.
She confronted her badgerer with calm dignity, refusing to be rattled by his remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tormentor - a person who deliberately annoys or causes suffering to others
  • Synonym: annoyer - someone who causes irritation or annoyance
  • Antonym: comforter - a person who consoles or soothes others

Etymology

From 'badger' (verb), meaning to harass or pester, patterned after the animal's name.
