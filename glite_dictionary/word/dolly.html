dolly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːli / (AmE), / ˈdɒli / (BrE)
A small figure representing a person, used as a child's toy.
a child's toy figure

Examples

She gave her daughter a new dolly for her birthday.
The little girl tucked her favorite dolly into bed every night.
After lunch, the children played with their dolly and toy soldiers on the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doll - a small model of a human figure, used as a child's toy

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑli / (AmE), / ˈdɒli / (BrE)
a small wheeled platform used for moving a camera smoothly during filming
movable camera support platform

Examples

The film crew placed the camera on a dolly to shoot smooth tracking shots.
Using a dolly allowed the director to create fluid movement in the scene.
They rented a dolly for the music video to achieve cinematic camera angles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camera crane - a large device for moving a camera vertically and horizontally

Etymology

From the given name Dolly, originally used for small platforms or carts.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːli / (AmE), / ˈdɒli / (BrE)
a small platform on wheels, used for moving heavy objects easily
wheeled platform for moving objects

Examples

The movers used a dolly to transport the refrigerator into the kitchen.
You can rent a dolly to help move heavy boxes from the truck.
The crew placed the camera on a dolly to film the tracking shot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand truck - two-wheeled cart for moving heavy loads upright
  • Synonym: cart - vehicle with wheels used for carrying loads
