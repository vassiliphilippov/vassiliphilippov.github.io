seizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzər / (AmE), / ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
a person who kidnaps men, often drugging them, to force them into compulsory service on a ship
person who kidnaps men for service on ships

Examples

The notorious seizer prowled the docks at night, searching for sailors to kidnap.
Stories of the seizer and his victims spread fear among the drinking establishments near the harbor.
Many young men were taken by a seizer and woke up at sea, forced into labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shanghaier - a person who forces men into service on ships, often by kidnapping or trickery

Etymology

From 'seize', meaning to take hold of forcibly; used historically in maritime contexts.
