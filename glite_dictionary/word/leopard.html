leopard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəd / (BrE)
a large wild cat with a tawny coat marked with black spots, native to the forests of Africa and Asia
large wild cat with spots

Examples

The leopard stalked its prey silently through the dense jungle foliage.
She was fascinated by the unique patterns on the leopard's coat at the zoo.
Leopards are excellent climbers and often rest in the branches of trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheetah - a large spotted cat native to Africa and parts of Asia, known for its speed
  • Synonym: jaguar - a large spotted cat native to Central and South America
  • Synonym: tiger - a large feline with a striped coat native to Asia

Etymology

From Old French *leupart*, from Late Latin *leopardus*, from Greek *leopardos* (lion-panther).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəd / (BrE)
the skin or pelt of a leopard, especially after it has been removed and prepared for use
the pelt of a leopard

Examples

The king wore a cloak made from leopard to demonstrate his status.
Some traditional ceremonies use leopard as a symbol of power and prestige.
Trading in leopard is now illegal in many countries to protect endangered species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leopard - a large, spotted wild cat native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Old French 'leupart', via Latin 'leopardus', from Greek 'leopardos' (lion + panther).
