Eyck, Jan van

1. PROPN

/ eɪk / (AmE), / eɪk / (BrE)
(1390–1441) Flemish painter who was a founder of the Flemish school of painting and pioneered modern techniques of oil painting.
Flemish painter; pioneer of oil painting

Examples

Many art historians consider Eyck to be a master of realism in painting.
The detailed textures in Eyck's works have fascinated scholars for centuries.
A reproduction of Eyck's 'Arnolfini Portrait' hangs in the museum.

Etymology

Dutch surname, associated with Jan van Eyck, a prominent Early Netherlandish painter.
