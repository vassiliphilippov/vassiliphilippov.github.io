epsilon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛp.sɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛp.sɪ.lɒn / (BrE)
the fifth letter of the Greek alphabet, used in mathematical, scientific, and linguistic contexts to represent various values or sounds
fifth letter of the Greek alphabet

Examples

In set theory, epsilon often denotes an arbitrarily small positive quantity.
Linguists study the pronunciation of epsilon in different dialects of Greek.
On the test, we were asked to identify the symbol epsilon among other Greek letters.

Etymology

From Greek 'epsilon', literally 'simple e' (as opposed to the diphthong 'ai').
