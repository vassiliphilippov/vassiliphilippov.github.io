winkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
A small edible marine snail with a spiral shell, especially the common periwinkle.
small edible marine snail

Examples

Children used to collect winkles from the rocks at low tide.
She added a handful of boiled winkles to the seafood platter.
In some coastal towns, winkle stalls are a popular sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - another name for the common edible winkle

Etymology

Probably a shortened form of "periwinkle," from Old English *pīnewincle* (periwinkle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
A small edible marine snail, often eaten steamed or baked.
small edible marine snail

Examples

We found fresh winkles at the seaside market this morning.
She prefers her winkles baked with a little garlic and butter.
After a long walk on the beach, they collected winkles for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - another name for a small edible marine snail

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *wincle* or related to Old High German *wankal* (shellfish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07798644-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to remove or dislodge someone or something from a place, especially with effort or difficulty
remove or dislodge with effort

Examples

It took a lot of persuasion to winkle the secret out of him.
The investigator managed to winkle the hidden documents from the drawer.
We finally winkled the cat out from under the sofa after an hour of searching.

Etymology

From dialectal English 'winkle', meaning to extract like a periwinkle from its shell.
