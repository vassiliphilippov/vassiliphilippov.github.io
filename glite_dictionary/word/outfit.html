outfit

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
a set of tools, equipment, or gear assembled for a specific activity or purpose
set of equipment for a task

Examples

The camping outfit included a tent, sleeping bag, and cooking gear.
Each firefighter was issued a full safety outfit before heading to the site.
For the scuba diving course, you'll need to rent a special outfit from the shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kit - set of tools or equipment needed for a specific purpose

Etymology

From Middle English 'outfit', originally meaning 'equipment' or 'provision', from out + fit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03624605-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
to provide a person or group with clothing, equipment, or other necessary items, especially for a particular purpose
provide with equipment or supplies

Examples

The army will outfit the soldiers with new uniforms before deployment.
She outfitted her kitchen with the latest appliances to make cooking easier.
Our team was outfitted with specialized gear for the mountain expedition.

Synset Examples

The expedition was equipped with proper clothing, food, and other necessities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to supply with necessary items for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: supply - to provide someone with something that is needed or wanted

Etymology

From Middle English 'outfitten', influenced by the noun 'outfit' (originally meaning an equipment or supply for a journey).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02344409-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
a group, organization, or unit that acts as a single entity, especially a military company or a team
group or organization; military unit

Examples

The elite outfit was deployed to the front lines during the conflict.
She joined a small tech outfit that specialized in software development.
The military outfit conducted training exercises in preparation for the mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - a single group forming part of a larger organization
  • Synonym: organization - a group of people organized for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: individual - a single person, as opposed to a group

Etymology

From Middle English 'outfit' (equipment), originally Scottish; 'out-' + 'fit' (to be equipped).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08291747-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
a set of clothes, often including accessories, worn together, especially for a particular occasion or purpose
set of clothes worn together

Examples

She chose a stylish outfit for the party tonight.
His outfit included a matching hat and shoes.
That outfit is perfect for hiking in the mountains.

Synset Examples

his getup was exceedingly elegant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attire - clothes, especially fine or formal ones
  • Synonym: costume - clothing worn to portray a particular style or for a special occasion

Etymology

From out + fit, originally meaning equipment or provisions for a journey (mid-18th century).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
