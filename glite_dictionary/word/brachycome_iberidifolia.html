Brachycome Iberidifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˌkoʊmi ˌaɪbərɪˈdɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˌkəʊmi ˌaɪbərɪˈdɪfəliə / (BrE)
a western Australian annual plant widely cultivated for its daisy-like flower heads with white, bluish, violet, or variegated rays
Australian annual with showy daisy-like flowers

Examples

Gardeners often choose Brachycome Iberidifolia for its brightly colored blooms and easy care.
The blue and white petals of Brachycome Iberidifolia create a striking contrast in summer borders.
Native to Western Australia, Brachycome Iberidifolia can thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short) and 'kome' (hair), and Latin 'iberidifolia' (with leaves like Iberis).
