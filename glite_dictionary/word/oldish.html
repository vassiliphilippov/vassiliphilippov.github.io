oldish

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊldɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊldɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat old; rather elderly but not yet truly old
somewhat old or elderly

Examples

The house looked oldish but was still sturdy and well maintained.
He’s oldish, with gray hair and a gentle manner, but he’s far from frail.
She wore an oldish coat that reminded me of fashions from decades past.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: old - having lived for a long time; not young
  • Antonym: young - having lived or existed for only a short time; not old
