unmarked

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɑrkt / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːkt / (BrE)
not noticed, observed, celebrated, or recognized; not marked or acknowledged in any way.
not noticed or recognized

Examples

His achievements did not go unmarked by the community.
The anniversary passed unmarked, with no celebration or mention.
The kindness he showed never went unmarked by those around him.

Synset Examples

his retirement was not allowed to go unmarked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marked - noticed or given attention; recognized or acknowledged

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (meaning not) and 'marked'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01613430-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɑrkt / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːkt / (BrE)
not having any identifying mark, label, or distinguishing feature
without any distinguishing mark or label

Examples

The suspect drove away in an unmarked vehicle, making it difficult for witnesses to notice.
She preferred to use unmarked envelopes to keep the contents private.
Officials played with unmarked cards to ensure fairness in the game.

Synset Examples

unmarked cards
an unmarked police car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marked - having an identifying mark

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'marked' (having a mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01484442-a
Synset POS: a
