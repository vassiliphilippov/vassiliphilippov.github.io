to decarbonate

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːˈkɑrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈkɑːbəneɪt / (BrE)
to remove carbon dioxide from a substance, especially in a chemical process
remove carbon dioxide from

Examples

The engineers worked to decarbonate the water before it entered the main supply system.
Certain industrial processes must decarbonate minerals to reduce their carbon footprint.
After heating, the machine efficiently decarbonates limestone by releasing carbon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carbonate - to combine or treat with carbon dioxide

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (removal) and 'carbonate' (to combine or treat with carbon dioxide).
