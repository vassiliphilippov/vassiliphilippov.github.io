peripety

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪpɪti / (AmE), / pəˈrɪpɪti / (BrE)
A sudden and unexpected change of fortune or reversal of circumstances, especially in a literary work.
sudden reversal of fortune (literature)

Examples

The novel's peripety occurs when the protagonist discovers her true heritage.
Classic tragedies often feature a dramatic peripety that changes the hero's fate.
The sudden peripety in the play stunned the audience.

Synset Examples

a peripeteia swiftly turns a routine sequence of events into a story worth telling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peripeteia - sudden reversal of fortune (literature)

Etymology

From Greek peripeteia (sudden change), via Latin peripetia.
