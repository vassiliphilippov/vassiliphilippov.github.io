peewit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːˌwɪt / (BrE)
A small olive-colored woodland flycatcher native to eastern North America.
small woodland flycatcher (bird)

Examples

The peewit is easily spotted by its distinct olive coloring among the trees.
Birdwatchers in the region are always excited to catch a glimpse of the elusive peewit.
I heard the call of a peewit while hiking in the forest.

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of the bird's call.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːwɪt / (BrE)
a large crested Old World plover (Vanellus vanellus), also called the lapwing, noted for its distinctive call and appearance
a large crested Old World plover; lapwing

Examples

The peewit is often seen in wetlands and marshes across Europe.
A flock of peewits circled overhead, making their distinctive calls.
Farmers welcomed the presence of the peewit in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapwing - another name for the peewit, a large crested plover

Etymology

Imitative of the bird's cry.
