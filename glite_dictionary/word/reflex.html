reflex

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈriː.flɛks / (BrE)
(of an action) performed automatically and without conscious thought, especially as a response to a stimulus.
done automatically; involuntary action

Examples

The doctor noticed a reflex jerk in the patient's leg when he tapped the knee.
Blinking when something approaches your eyes is a reflex response.
The baby's grip on a finger is a natural reflex action.

Synset Examples

the automatic shrinking of the pupils of the eye in strong light
a reflex knee jerk
sneezing is reflexive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - done or occurring without conscious thought
  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or intention

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexus', past participle of 'reflectere' (to bend back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
an automatic, instinctive, and involuntary response to a stimulus, not learned or consciously controlled
automatic reaction to a stimulus

Examples

When the doctor tapped my knee, my reflex made my leg kick forward.
Catching the falling glass was just a quick reflex—I didn’t even think about it.
He trained so much that reacting to the ball became a natural reflex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: response - a reaction to something that has happened or been said
  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or free will, not automatic

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexus', past participle of 'reflectere' (to bend back).

Synset POS: n
