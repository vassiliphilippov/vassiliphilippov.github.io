focal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated at a focus; having a focus or being important as a center of activity or attention.
relating to a focus

Examples

The surgeon identified a focal area of infection that needed treatment.
The lens has a focal length of 50 millimeters, ideal for portraits.
The report highlighted the focal issues facing the community.

Synset Examples

focal length

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central - being at the center or most important part
  • Antonym: peripheral - related to or situated on the edge or periphery

Etymology

From Medieval Latin focalis, from Latin focus (hearth, fireplace, focus).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kəl / (BrE)
Situated at, or pertaining to, a focus; having the position or quality of being central or localized at a focus.
centralized at or relating to a focus

Examples

The eyes are the focal point of this painting, immediately drawing your attention.
The doctor detected a focal infection in the patient's right lung.
A focal area of damage was found on the engine's surface.

Synset Examples

focal point
focal infection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central - of or forming the center; of prime importance

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'focalis', from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace, focus).
