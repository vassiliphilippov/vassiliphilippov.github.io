nucleus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
the central structure of the lens in the eye, surrounded by the cortex
central part of the lens in the eye
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully removed the nucleus of the lens to treat the cataract.
During the operation, the nucleus was differentiated from the surrounding cortex.
The density of the nucleus affects the type of lens replacement required.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cortex - the outer layer of the lens of the eye

Etymology

From Latin nucleus, diminutive of nux (nut, kernel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
the central and most important part of a cell, containing DNA and RNA and responsible for growth and reproduction
central cell part containing genetic material

Examples

The nucleus of each cell contains the organism's genetic code.
Scientists observed the nucleus under a powerful microscope to study cell division.
Damage to the nucleus can disrupt a cell's ability to reproduce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell membrane - thin structure surrounding the cell nucleus
  • Antonym: cytoplasm - material within a cell excluding the nucleus

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel, core), diminutive of nux (nut).

4. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The solid central part of a comet, made of ice and frozen gases, which vaporizes as the comet approaches the Sun, forming the coma and tail.
central core of a comet

Examples

The nucleus of a comet contains icy material that vaporizes as it nears the sun.
Astronomers observed the nucleus breaking apart after the comet's close passage to Jupiter.
Only the nucleus is visible when the comet is far from the Sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel or core).
