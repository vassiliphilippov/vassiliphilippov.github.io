integrative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.t̬əˌɡreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.ɡrət.ɪv / (BrE)
serving or tending to combine separate elements into a unified whole
combining parts into a whole

Examples

The school adopted an integrative approach that brought together science, art, and humanities.
Her therapy emphasizes integrative methods, addressing mind, body, and spirit in treatment.
An integrative policy will help unite the various departments into one effective team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disintegrative - tending to break into pieces or destroy unity

Etymology

From Latin integratus (made whole), from integrare (to make whole, renew).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈteɡrətɪv / (BrE)
serving or intended to combine different elements into a unified whole
combining elements into a whole

Examples

Their research takes an integrative approach to mental health, blending biology, psychology, and social factors.
The new curriculum is designed to be integrative, uniting subjects like history, art, and literature.
An integrative therapy might use both traditional medicine and alternative healing practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated - formed or united into a whole

Etymology

From Latin integratus, past participle of integrare (to make whole), from integer (whole).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01333938-a
Synset POS: a
