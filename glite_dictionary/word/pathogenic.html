pathogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæθəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a microorganism or agent) capable of causing disease.
causing disease (esp. microorganisms)
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying pathogenic bacteria to understand how they cause infections.
Only certain strains of E. coli are actually pathogenic to humans.
The water supply was contaminated with pathogenic microorganisms.

Synset Examples

infective agents
pathogenic bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - able to cause infection; spreading disease

Etymology

From Greek pathos (suffering, disease) + -genic (producing).
