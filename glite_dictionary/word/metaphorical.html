metaphorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈfɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
(of language or an expression) expressing one thing in terms usually used for something else, especially as a metaphor; not literal.
not literal; figurative

Examples

Her description of the city as a 'jungle' was a metaphorical way of highlighting its dangers.
Writers often use metaphorical language to convey emotions more vividly.
Calling someone a 'rock' is a metaphorical compliment, not a literal one.

Synset Examples

a metaphorical expression
metaphoric language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literal - using words in their usual, most basic sense

Etymology

From Late Latin metaphoricus, from Greek metaphora (metaphor).
