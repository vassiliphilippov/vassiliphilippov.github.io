Lanius borealis

1. NOUN

/ læn.i.əs bɔˈri.æl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈlæ.ni.əs bɒˈrɪə.lɪs / (BrE)
a species of butcherbird found in northern North America, known for impaling its prey on thorns or barbed wire
a northern North American butcherbird species

Examples

The Lanius borealis is often seen perching on fence posts in the northern forests.
Biologists studied the feeding habits of Lanius borealis during the harsh Canadian winter.
The distinctive call of the Lanius borealis can be heard across the tundra in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcherbird - a bird known for impaling its prey, especially shrikes

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lanius' meaning butcher (referring to its feeding habits), and 'borealis' meaning northern.
