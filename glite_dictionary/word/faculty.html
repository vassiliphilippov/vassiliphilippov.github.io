faculty

1. NOUN

/ ˈfækəlti / (AmE), / ˈfækəlti / (BrE)
an inherent mental or physical power, especially one of the mind's natural abilities such as reasoning, memory, or perception
inherent mental or physical power

Examples

As people age, some may worry about losing their mental faculties.
Imagination is one faculty that distinguishes humans from other animals.
He maintained full control of his faculties until the end of his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ability - the power or skill to do something
  • Antonym: incapacity - lack of ability; inability

Etymology

From Latin facultas (power, ability), from facilis (easy to do).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfækəlti / (AmE), / ˈfækəlti / (BrE)
all the teachers and administrative staff of a school, college, or university considered as a group
teaching and administrative staff at an institution

Examples

The university faculty voted to implement a new curriculum next year.
She is a respected member of the faculty at the medical school.
The faculty and students gathered for the annual awards ceremony.

Synset Examples

the dean addressed the letter to the entire staff of the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - the employees of an organization, especially teaching or support staff in schools
  • Synonym: administration - the group of people who manage or direct an institution, especially in education

Etymology

From Latin 'facultas' (ability, power, skill), via Old French 'faculte'.
