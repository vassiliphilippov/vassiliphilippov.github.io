Sorbonne

1. PROPN

/ sɔrˈbɒn / (AmE), / sɔːˈbɒn / (BrE)
the historic university in Paris, widely regarded as an intellectual and cultural center of France
historic university in Paris

Examples

She spent a semester studying at the Sorbonne in Paris.
The Sorbonne has produced many influential philosophers and scientists.
His dream was to earn a degree from the prestigious Sorbonne.

Etymology

From French 'Sorbonne', named after Robert de Sorbon, its 13th-century founder.
