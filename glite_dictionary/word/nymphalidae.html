Nymphalidae

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfælɪˌdi / (AmE), / nɪmˈfælədiː / (BrE)
a large family of brightly colored butterflies, commonly known as brush-footed butterflies
family of large, colorful butterflies

Examples

The Nymphalidae family includes well-known butterflies such as monarchs and admirals.
Many species in the Nymphalidae family have vibrant patterns and colors on their wings.
Scientists study the migration patterns of Nymphalidae to better understand butterfly behavior.

Etymology

From New Latin Nymphalidae, based on the type genus Nymphalis.
