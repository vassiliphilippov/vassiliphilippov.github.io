breadth

1. NOUN

/ brɛdθ / (AmE), / brɛdθ / (BrE)
the capacity to understand a wide range of subjects or to have comprehensive knowledge
wide range of knowledge or understanding

Examples

The candidate's breadth of experience impressed the interview panel.
A good leader needs both breadth and depth of vision.
Her breadth of knowledge made her an invaluable resource for the team.

Synset Examples

a teacher must have a breadth of knowledge of the subject
a man distinguished by the largeness and scope of his views

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depth - thoroughness and detail in understanding or knowledge

Etymology

From Old English 'brǣdu' (breadth), related to 'broad'.

2. NOUN

/ brɛdθ / (AmE), / brɛdθ / (BrE)
the distance or measurement from one side of something to the other; the width
distance from side to side

Examples

The river's breadth made it difficult to build a bridge across.
The artist admired the breadth of the canvas before starting to paint.
The room's breadth allows for a large dining table in the center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
  • Antonym: narrowness - the quality of being narrow from side to side

Etymology

From Old English 'brǣdu' (breadth, width), related to 'broad'.
