grenadier

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪə / (BrE)
A soldier, especially in the past, who was equipped with grenades or a member of certain elite infantry regiments.
soldier equipped with grenades

Examples

The museum has a uniform worn by a British grenadier during the 18th century.
French grenadiers led the attack in the historic battle.
As a grenadier, he was trained to throw explosives over enemy defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot

Etymology

From French *grenadier*, from *grenade* (a type of explosive), originally referring to troops who threw grenades.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a deep-sea fish with a large head, elongated body, and long tapering tail, found in deep ocean waters
deep-sea fish with a long tail and large head

Examples

The grenadier is commonly found at depths of over one thousand meters in the Atlantic Ocean.
Some fishermen specialize in catching the elusive grenadier during deep-sea expeditions.
A biologist studied the peculiar features of the newly discovered species of grenadier.

Etymology

From French 'grenadier', originally meaning 'grenade-thrower', referring here to the fish's bulging shape.
