Amblyrhynchus cristatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.blɪˈrɪŋ.kəs krɪˈsteɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˌam.blɪˈrɪŋ.kəs krɪˈstaː.təs / (BrE)
a shore-dwelling seaweed-eating lizard native to the Galapagos Islands, commonly known as the marine iguana
marine iguana of the Galapagos Islands

Examples

The Amblyrhynchus cristatus is the only sea-going lizard in the world.
Researchers observed Amblyrhynchus cristatus basking on volcanic rocks to regulate their body temperature.
Tourists to the Galapagos Islands are often fascinated by the unique behavior of the Amblyrhynchus cristatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine iguana - common name for Amblyrhynchus cristatus

Etymology

From Greek 'amblys' (blunt) + 'rhynchos' (snout) and Latin 'cristatus' (crested).
