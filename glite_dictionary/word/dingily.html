dingily

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪŋɪli / (AmE), / ˈdɪŋɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that is dirty, shabby, or lacking brightness or freshness.
in a dirty or shabby way

Examples

The once-beautiful curtains now hung dingily in the abandoned house.
He stared out the window of the motel, the sunlight filtering dingily through stained glass.
With her coat hanging dingily from her shoulders, she hurried down the alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dingy - dirty, dull, or shabby in appearance

Etymology

From "dingy" (dirty or discolored) + adverbial suffix "-ly".
