greediness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdɪnɪs / (BrE)
An excessive or insatiable desire for food.
excessive desire for food

Examples

His greediness at the dinner table was astonishing to everyone present.
The child's greediness for sweets led to an upset stomach.
She was chastised for her greediness, having eaten three servings before anyone else started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating

Etymology

From 'greedy' + '-ness'; ultimately from Old English 'grǣdig'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːdinəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdinɪs / (BrE)
An excessive or insatiable desire, especially for wealth or possessions.
excessive desire for wealth or possessions

Examples

His greediness drove him to manipulate the stock market for personal gain.
Many people criticize the greediness of large corporations.
The lawyer's greediness became apparent when he demanded an outrageous fee.

Synset Examples

the greediness of lawyers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: generosity - quality of being kind and generous; willingness to give

Etymology

From 'greedy' + '-ness'; ultimately from Old English 'grǣdig'.
