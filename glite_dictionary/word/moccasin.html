moccasin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːkəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɒkəsɪn / (BrE)
a soft leather shoe, historically worn by Native Americans, often with a flexible sole and sometimes decorative stitching or beadwork
soft leather shoe; Native American origin

Examples

She slipped on her comfortable moccasins before heading outside.
His handmade moccasins were decorated with colorful beadwork.
Traditional moccasins provide excellent feel and flexibility when walking in the forest.

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian (Powhatan) *makasin*, through French *mocassin*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːkəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɒkəsɪn / (BrE)
a soft leather shoe, traditionally made and worn by Indigenous peoples of North America, often with the sole and sides made of one piece
soft leather shoe, originally worn by Native Americans

Examples

He wore a pair of comfortable moccasins on the long flight.
Traditional moccasins are often decorated with intricate beadwork.
She slipped off her boots and put on her favorite moccasins by the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loafer - a type of slip-on shoe without laces

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *makasin*, via French *mocassin*.
