long-life

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ laɪf / (AmE), / lɒŋ laɪf / (BrE)
(of perishable goods) treated or designed to stay fresh or usable for a longer period than usual.
treated to last longer than usual (goods)

Examples

The supermarket now stocks long-life milk, which doesn't need to be refrigerated before opening.
She always buys long-life batteries for her wireless mouse to avoid frequent replacements.
Many campers prefer long-life foods that can be stored without refrigeration.

Synset Examples

long-life milk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-life - perishable goods that remain usable or fresh for only a short period
