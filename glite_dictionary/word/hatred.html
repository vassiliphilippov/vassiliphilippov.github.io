hatred

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪtrəd / (AmE), / ˈheɪtrɪd / (BrE)
a very strong feeling of dislike or hostility, often so intense that it may provoke action
intense dislike or hostility

Examples

His hatred for injustice fueled his activism throughout his life.
Years of conflict had bred deep hatred between the two communities.
She tried to hide her hatred, but it was evident in her voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loathing - a strong feeling of hatred or disgust
  • Antonym: love - an intense feeling of deep affection; the opposite of hatred
  • Degree Lower: dislike - a feeling of mild aversion
  • Degree Higher: detestation - extreme hatred or abhorrence

Etymology

From Old English hatreden, from hatian (to hate) + -red (condition).
