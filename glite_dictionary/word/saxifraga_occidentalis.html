Saxifraga occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə ˌɒksɪˈdentəlɪs / (BrE)
A species of saxifrage with loose clusters of white flowers on hairy stems and a cluster of basal leaves, native to moist slopes of western North America.
species of saxifrage native to North America

Examples

The Saxifraga occidentalis thrives in moist alpine meadows throughout the western United States and Canada.
Botanists identified the plant as Saxifraga occidentalis by its hairy stems and cluster of white flowers.
Hikers may spot Saxifraga occidentalis blooming on damp, rocky slopes in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - Any plant of the genus Saxifraga

Etymology

From Latin: 'saxifraga' meaning 'stone-breaker', and 'occidentalis' meaning 'western'.
