zephyr

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛfər / (AmE), / ˈzɛfə / (BrE)
a gentle, mild breeze, often refreshing or pleasant
gentle, refreshing breeze

Examples

A cool zephyr drifted through the open window on a summer evening.
We sat on the porch, enjoying the gentle zephyr from the sea.
The picnic was perfect, with sunshine and a soft zephyr rustling the trees.

Synset Examples

the breeze was cooled by the lake
as he waited he could feel the air on his neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeze - a light and gentle wind
  • Degree Higher: gale - a very strong wind

Etymology

From Greek Zephyros, the god of the west wind, via Latin zephyrus.
