Adalia

1. NOUN

/ əˈdeɪliə / (AmE), / əˈdeɪliə / (BrE)
a genus of ladybugs, including species such as Adalia bipunctata and Adalia decempunctata, found primarily in Europe and Asia
a genus of ladybugs

Examples

The Adalia genus includes several familiar species of ladybugs found in gardens.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of Adalia across different European habitats.
Both Adalia bipunctata and decempunctata are known for their helpful role in controlling aphid populations.

Etymology

From New Latin Adalia, assigned as a genus name in scientific classification.
