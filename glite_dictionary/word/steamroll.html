to steamroll

1. VERB

/ ˈstiːmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
to proceed with great force, often disregarding obstacles or opposition
proceed with great force

Examples

The new policy allowed the company to steamroll through regulatory hurdles with ease.
She tends to steamroll over other people's ideas in meetings.
Instead of listening, the manager just steamrolled ahead with his own plan.

Synset Examples

The new teacher tends to steamroller

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bulldoze - to proceed forcefully or insensitively

Etymology

From 'steamroller', a heavy vehicle used to flatten surfaces; used metaphorically since early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02378669-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstiːmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
to make (a surface) level or flat by using a steamroller
level or flatten with a steamroller

Examples

Before laying the asphalt, workers will steamroll the road to ensure a smooth surface.
The crew steamrolled the new path through the park yesterday.
They need to steamroll the site before starting construction on the building.

Synset Examples

steamroll the roads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatten - to make or become flat or flatter

Etymology

From the noun 'steamroller', a machine for flattening surfaces, plus the verb-forming usage of roll.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00464919-v

3. VERB

/ ˈstiːmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
to bring something to a specified state by using overwhelming force or persistent pressure, often crushing opposition
overcome or achieve by overwhelming force

Examples

The committee managed to steamroll the policy changes despite public objections.
She tried to steamroll her proposal through the board without any real discussion.
The company was accused of steamrolling smaller competitors out of the market.

Synset Examples

The Senator steamrollered the bill to defeat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to defeat or overpower completely
  • Synonym: to bulldoze - to coerce or force someone into doing something

Etymology

From 'steamroller', a heavy road roller powered by steam; used figuratively since the early 20th century to indicate overwhelming force.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02511456-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈstiːmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
to defeat or overwhelm someone or something with great force, energy, or determination, often without regard for resistance
overwhelm or defeat with great force

Examples

The home team managed to steamroll its opponents in the second half, winning by a huge margin.
She refused to let the company steamroll the smaller businesses in the area.
The proposal was steamrolled through the committee without any real discussion.

Synset Examples

steamroller the opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to defeat completely and decisively
  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overcome completely or overpower
  • Antonym: yield - to give way under pressure; to surrender (opposite sense)

Etymology

Formed from 'steamroller,' the heavy machine for flattening surfaces, used metaphorically from the late 19th century.
