Freya

1. PROPN

/ ˈfreɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈfreɪ.ə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the goddess of love, beauty, and fertility, daughter of Njorth and sister of Frey.
Norse goddess of love and fertility

Examples

The ancient Norse worshipped Freya as the goddess of love and fertility.
Many stories describe Freya riding her chariot drawn by cats.
People in Scandinavia still honor Freya in local traditions and festivals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Frey - Norse god of fertility, Freya's brother
  • Synonym: Venus - Roman goddess of love

Etymology

From Old Norse *Freyja*, meaning 'lady'.
