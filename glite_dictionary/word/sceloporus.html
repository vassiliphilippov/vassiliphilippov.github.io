Sceloporus

1. NOUN

/ skəˈlɑpərəs / (AmE), / skɛˈlɒpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of North and Central American lizards commonly known as spiny lizards, characterized by rough, keeled scales.
genus of spiny lizards

Examples

The desert is home to the Sceloporus, recognized by its rough scales and rapid movements.
Researchers observed a Sceloporus basking on the rocks early in the morning.
Many species within the Sceloporus genus display vivid blue patches on their bellies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lizard - a common name for lizards of the genus Sceloporus

Etymology

From Greek *skelos* (leg) and *poros* (pore, passage).
