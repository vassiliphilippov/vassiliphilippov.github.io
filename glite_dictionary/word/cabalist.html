cabalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkabəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or practices the Jewish mystical tradition known as the Kabbalah
student of Jewish Kabbalah

Examples

The cabalist spent years deciphering the hidden meanings in ancient texts.
As a respected cabalist, she often taught lessons on Jewish mysticism.
He devoted his life to becoming a true cabalist, seeking spiritual enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kabbalist - a person who studies or practices Kabbalah

Etymology

From French *cabaliste*, from *Kabbale* (Kabbalah), ultimately from Medieval Hebrew *qabbālā* (tradition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkabəlɪst / (BrE)
A person who is a member of a secret group involved in plots or intrigue.
member of a secretive group

Examples

The conspiracy was uncovered after authorities arrested a leading cabalist involved in the plot.
Historians debated whether the king was influenced by a cabalist within his council.
Rumors spread that a cabalist had infiltrated the organization, sowing distrust among its members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabal - a secret group or faction involved in intrigue

Etymology

From French *cabaliste*, from *cabal*, referring to a secret or small group.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09905128-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkabəlɪst / (BrE)
An expert who is highly skilled in obscure, complex, or esoteric matters; one who has secret or specialized knowledge, especially of mystical or occult subjects.
expert in esoteric matters

Examples

The cabalist amazed his audience with his deep understanding of mystical texts.
Only a true cabalist could decipher the hidden meanings in those cryptic writings.
She was known among scholars as a cabalist in the art of mathematical puzzles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: novice - a person who is new to something; beginner

Etymology

From French 'cabaliste', from Medieval Latin 'cabalista' (one versed in the Jewish Kabbalah), from 'Kabbalah'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
