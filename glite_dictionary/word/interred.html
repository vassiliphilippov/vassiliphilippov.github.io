interred

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɜrd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːd / (BrE)
(of a dead body) placed in a grave or tomb after death; buried.
(of a body) placed in a grave; buried

Examples

The remains were interred in a quiet cemetery on the hillside.
Many interred soldiers were later identified and honored by their families.
The interred artifacts revealed much about the ancient civilization.

Synset Examples

the hastily buried corpses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buried - placed in the ground and covered, especially as a form of disposal after death

Etymology

Past participle of 'inter', from Latin 'interrare', from 'in' (in) + 'terra' (earth).
