oligospermia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːlɪɡoʊˈspɜːrmiə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəʊˈspɜːmiə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an insufficient number of spermatozoa (sperm cells) in the semen, which may affect male fertility.
low sperm count condition
medical

Examples

The patient's fertility tests showed evidence of oligospermia after several months of trying to conceive.
Treatment options for oligospermia depend on its underlying cause.
Men with oligospermia may benefit from lifestyle changes and medical therapies to improve sperm count.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: azoospermia - complete absence of sperm in the semen
  • Degree Lower: normospermia - normal concentration of sperm in semen

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + sperma (seed, sperm).
