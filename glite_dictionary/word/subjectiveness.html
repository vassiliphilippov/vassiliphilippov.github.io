subjectiveness

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdʒɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛktɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being based on personal feelings, impressions, or opinions rather than on objective facts.
quality of being subjective

Examples

The judge warned the panel to avoid subjectiveness when evaluating the evidence.
Her review was criticized for its subjectiveness rather than relying on measurable criteria.
Academic research prefers objectivity to subjectiveness in interpreting findings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: objectivity - The quality of being objective and impartial
  • Synonym: subjectivity - The state or quality of being subjective

Etymology

From 'subjective', derived from Medieval Latin 'subjectivus', from Latin 'subjectus' (brought under).
