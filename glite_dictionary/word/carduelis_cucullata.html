Carduelis cucullata

1. PROPN

/ kɑrˈduːəlɪs kuːˈkuːlətə / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs kjuːˈkʊlətə / (BrE)
a South American species of finch, commonly known as the red siskin, with a scarlet body and black head, wings, and tail
South American finch species (red siskin)

Examples

The Carduelis cucullata is renowned for its vibrant scarlet plumage and melodious song.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered Carduelis cucullata in its native habitats.
Many birdwatchers travel to Venezuela hoping to catch a glimpse of the elusive Carduelis cucullata.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Carduelis' meaning goldfinch, and 'cucullata' meaning hooded.
