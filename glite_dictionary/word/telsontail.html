telsontail

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛl.sɒnˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.sɒn.teɪl / (BrE)
A minute, primitive, wingless and eyeless insect with a cone-shaped head, typically found in damp soil or decaying organic matter.
primitive soil-dwelling insect

Examples

The forest floor was teeming with small creatures like the telsontail.
Biologists discovered a new species of telsontail in the damp earth.
Many telsontails thrive in decaying organic matter, helping to break it down.
