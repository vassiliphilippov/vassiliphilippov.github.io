Pidlimdi language

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪd.lɪmˈdi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌpɪd.lɪmˈdi ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Chadic language that uses three tones, spoken in parts of Nigeria
a Chadic language spoken in Nigeria

Examples

Linguists have conducted studies on the tonal system of the Pidlimdi language.
Many children in the region grow up speaking Pidlimdi language as their first language.
Documentation of the Pidlimdi language helps preserve its unique linguistic features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chadic language - a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family, spoken in central Africa
