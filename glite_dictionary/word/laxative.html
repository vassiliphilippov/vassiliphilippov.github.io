laxative

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæk.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈlæk.sət.ɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance) having the effect of stimulating or promoting the evacuation of feces from the body.
promoting bowel movement
medical

Examples

The pharmacist recommended a laxative tea that contains natural herbs.
Certain fruits, such as prunes, are known for their laxative properties.
The doctor warned that overusing laxative medicines can lead to dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constipating - causing or tending to cause constipation

Etymology

From Latin 'laxativus' (loosening), from 'laxare' (to loosen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈlæk.sə.tɪv / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that helps stimulate or facilitate bowel movements, typically used to relieve constipation.
substance that relieves constipation
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a gentle laxative to relieve her discomfort.
After eating too much cheese, he took a laxative as a precaution.
Some herbal teas contain natural laxative ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathartic - a substance that accelerates defecation
  • Antonym: constipating - causing or tending to cause constipation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin laxativus (having the power to loosen), from Latin laxare (to loosen).

Synset POS: n
