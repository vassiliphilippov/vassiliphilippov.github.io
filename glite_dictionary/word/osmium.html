osmium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑzmiəm / (AmE), / ˈɒzmiəm / (BrE)
a hard, brittle, blue-grey or blue-black metallic element that is one of the platinum group; it is the densest naturally occurring element, with the symbol Os.
a dense blue-grey metallic element (Os)

Examples

Because osmium is so dense, it is used in specialized scientific equipment.
The mineral sample contained traces of both platinum and osmium.
Due to its toxicity, care must be taken when handling osmium compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - another element of the platinum group metals

Etymology

From Greek *osme* (smell), in reference to its pungent oxide.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːzmiəm / (AmE), / ˈɒzmiəm / (BrE)
a hard, brittle, blue-grey or blue-black metallic element, the densest of all known metals, used in alloys and electrical contacts
a dense, hard metallic element

Examples

The scientist measured the density of osmium to compare it with other metals.
Because of its durability, osmium is sometimes used in fountain pen tips and electrical contacts.
Osmium has a bluish-silver appearance and is considered the heaviest naturally occurring element.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - a precious grey-white metallic element, often alloyed with osmium

Etymology

From Greek osme (smell), referring to the strong smell of its oxide.
