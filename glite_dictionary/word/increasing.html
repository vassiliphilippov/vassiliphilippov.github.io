increasing

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkriːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkriːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(music) Becoming greater in tempo and/or volume.
growing in tempo or volume (music)

Examples

The conductor signaled for the orchestra to play with increasing tempo until the end of the movement.
The music grew more intense, increasing both in speed and volume as the climax approached.
She listened intently, noticing the increasing dynamics in the final section of the symphony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accelerating - (music) increasing in speed or tempo

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkriːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkriːsɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming greater or larger in amount, number, or degree
becoming greater or larger

Examples

With increasing demand for housing, prices continued to rise throughout the year.
The team faced increasing challenges as the project progressed.
There is increasing evidence that climate change affects global weather patterns.

Synset Examples

increasing prices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decreasing - becoming smaller or less

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'increase', from Latin 'increscere' (to grow in).
