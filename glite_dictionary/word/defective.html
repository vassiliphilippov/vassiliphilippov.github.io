defective

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
Markedly subnormal in structure, function, intelligence, or behavior; not functioning or developing normally.
markedly below normal in structure, function, or ability

Examples

The child was born with defective hearing, which made learning to speak difficult.
Some students have defective vision and require corrective glasses.
Speech therapy was recommended for his defective articulation.

Synset Examples

defective speech

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; regular or expected
  • Synonym: abnormal - deviating from what is normal or usual

Etymology

From Latin defectivus, from defectus (failure, deficiency).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01601822-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
having a fault or imperfection that prevents something from functioning properly or being complete
having a fault or imperfection

Examples

The defective phone would not turn on despite several attempts to charge it.
She received a defective product and immediately contacted customer service for a replacement.
Inspectors removed the defective parts from the assembly line to prevent future malfunctions.

Synset Examples

I returned the appliance because it was defective

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faulty - having faults; not working correctly
  • Antonym: perfect - without any flaws or defects

Etymology

From Latin 'defectivus' (wanting, defective), from 'deficere' (to fail, be lacking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01757421-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
not working properly or having a fault; imperfect or faulty in operation, function, or structure.
not working properly; faulty

Examples

The product was returned because it was defective and did not operate as advertised.
Many defective items were discovered during the quality control inspection.
A defective battery can cause the device to stop working unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

a bad telephone connection
a defective appliance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faulty - having faults; not working correctly
  • Synonym: imperfect - not perfect; having defects or flaws
  • Antonym: working - functioning properly; in good order

Etymology

From Latin defectivus (failing, deficient), from deficere (to fail).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01096265-s
Synset POS: s
