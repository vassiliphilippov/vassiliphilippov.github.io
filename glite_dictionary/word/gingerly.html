gingerly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɪndʒɚli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒəli / (BrE)
in a very careful or cautious way
with great care or caution

Examples

She placed the fragile vase gingerly on the shelf, afraid it might break.
He walked gingerly across the icy pavement, clutching the railing for support.
The kitten gingerly explored its new surroundings, pausing at every unfamiliar sound.

Synset Examples

they proceeded with gingerly footwork over the jagged stones
the issue was handled only in a gingerly way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - with attention or caution
  • Antonym: recklessly - without care or caution

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French *gensor* (delicate, dainty), meaning delicate or cautious.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒɪndʒɚli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒəli / (BrE)
in a careful or cautious manner, showing great care or delicacy
in a careful or cautious way

Examples

She placed the fragile vase gingerly on the shelf, afraid of breaking it.
He stepped gingerly over the icy patch on the sidewalk to avoid falling.
The doctor gingerly touched the bruised area to check for injury.

Synset Examples

gingerly I raised the edge of the blanket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a way that is careful to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: recklessly - acting without thinking about the risks or consequences

Etymology

Possibly from French "gingembre" (ginger) or as an alteration of "gentle" + -ly, influenced by association with being delicate as with ginger.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
