Synaptomys borealis

1. PROPN

/ sɪˌnæptəˌmaɪz bɔːˈreɪəlɪs / (AmE), / sɪˌnaptəˌmɪz bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (BrE)
A small rodent species, commonly known as the northern bog lemming, found in wet alpine and subalpine meadows of Canada and Alaska.
northern bog lemming species

Examples

The Synaptomys borealis population is an important part of the northern wetland ecosystem.
Scientists studying Synaptomys borealis monitor their numbers to assess habitat health.
Unlike other lemmings, Synaptomys borealis prefers moist, grassy meadows at high elevations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern bog lemming - common name for Synaptomys borealis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Synaptomys' as the genus name and 'borealis' meaning 'northern' in Latin.
