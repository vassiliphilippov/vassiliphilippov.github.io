habanera

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːbəˈnɛrə / (AmE), / ˌhabəˈnɛːrə / (BrE)
a Cuban dance and musical style in duple time, characterized by a distinctive rhythm
Cuban dance and musical style

Examples

The orchestra performed a lively habanera at the concert last night.
He taught us the basic steps of the habanera during our dance class.
The famous aria from Bizet's Carmen is based on the rhythm of the habanera.

Etymology

From Spanish 'habanera' (feminine of 'habanero', meaning 'of Havana').

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːbəˈnɛrə / (AmE), / ˌhabəˈneərə / (BrE)
A Cuban dance and the music written for it, characterized by a slow, syncopated rhythm in duple time.
Cuban dance and music with a syncopated rhythm

Examples

The orchestra played a lively habanera that filled the room with energy.
She danced the habanera with skill and grace at the recital.
Many composers have written pieces inspired by the habanera rhythm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tango - A ballroom dance of South American origin, related in rhythm and style

Etymology

Spanish, from 'Habana' (Havana), referring to its place of origin.
