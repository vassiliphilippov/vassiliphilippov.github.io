Louis Isadore Kahn

1. PROPN

/ ˌluːi ɪˈzɑːdɔːr kɑːn / (AmE), / ˌluːi ɪˈzɑːdɔː kɑːn / (BrE)
(1901–1974) a prominent American architect, born in Estonia, known for influential modernist buildings featuring innovative use of space and light.
American architect (1901–1974), noted modernist

Examples

The Salk Institute, designed by Louis Isadore Kahn, is considered a masterpiece of modern architecture.
Many students study the works of Louis Isadore Kahn for inspiration in their architectural projects.
Louis Isadore Kahn was known for his thoughtful use of natural light and monumental structures.
