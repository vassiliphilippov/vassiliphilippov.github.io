Johann Strauss

1. PROPN

/ joʊˈhɑːn ˈstraʊs / (AmE), / jəʊˈhan ˈstraʊs / (BrE)
(1804–1849) an Austrian composer best known for his waltzes, often referred to as the 'father of the waltz'.
Austrian composer of waltzes (1804–1849)

Examples

Many consider Johann Strauss to be the originator of the Viennese waltz tradition.
The orchestra performed a piece composed by Johann Strauss at the summer concert.
When studying Romantic music, students often encounter the works of Johann Strauss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Johann Strauss II - Austrian composer, son of Johann Strauss I, also famous for waltzes

2. PROPN

/ joʊˌhɑn ˈstraʊs / (AmE), / jəʊˌhan ˈstraʊs / (BrE)
(1825–1899) An Austrian composer famous for his waltzes, known as the ‘waltz king’, and the son of Johann Strauss the Elder.
Austrian composer of famous waltzes

Examples

Johann Strauss composed 'The Blue Danube,' one of the most famous waltzes in classical music.
Many orchestras around the world still perform works by Johann Strauss every New Year's Day.
The legacy of Johann Strauss continues to influence composers of dance music and operettas.
