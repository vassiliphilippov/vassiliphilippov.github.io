acetanilid

1. NOUN

/ əˌsɛtəˈnɪlɪd / (AmE), / əˌsiːtəˈnɪlɪd / (BrE)
A white, odorless crystalline compound formerly used as a painkiller and currently used in the manufacture of dyes, rubber accelerators, and other chemicals.
odorless white crystalline chemical compound
medical

Examples

Acetanilid is no longer used medically due to safety concerns, but it remains important in chemical manufacturing.
Early researchers discovered that acetanilid was metabolized into paracetamol in the human body.
The laboratory ordered more acetanilid for use in the dye synthesis process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetaminophen - a common painkiller also known as paracetamol, metabolite of acetanilid

Etymology

From acetic (as in 'acetic acid') + anilid (from 'aniline', a basis for synthetic dyes), formed in scientific nomenclature in the 19th century.
