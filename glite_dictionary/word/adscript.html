adscript

1. ADJ

/ ˈædˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈad.skrɪpt / (BrE)
(of a character or symbol) written or printed immediately following another character and aligned with it.
written after and aligned with a character

Examples

In this linguistic notation, an adscript character indicates a particular type of vowel quality.
The mathematician used an adscript number to distinguish between the variables x and x₁.
Adscript letters are rarely seen in modern printed texts outside specialized fields.

Etymology

From Latin 'adscriptus', past participle of 'adscribere' (to write next to).

2. ADJ

/ ˈædˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈadˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
(of a person) bound to a particular tract of land, with their service or labor transferable along with the property to new owners.
bound to land; tied to an estate
archaic

Examples

The landowner regarded the adscript peasants as part of the estate.
In medieval society, an adscript serf could not leave the land without permission.
Legal reforms eventually freed many adscript workers from hereditary bondage.

Synset Examples

an adscript serf

Etymology

From Latin adscriptus (enrolled, written in addition), from ad- (to) + scriptus (written).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01068308-s
Synset POS: s
