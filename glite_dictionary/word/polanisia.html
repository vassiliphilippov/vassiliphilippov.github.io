Polanisia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.ləˈnɪʒ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.ləˈnɪz.i.ə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs widely distributed in North America, having palmate leaves and creamy white to magenta flowers with many stamens of unequal length
a genus of flowering herbs

Examples

The specimen was identified as a member of the genus Polanisia.
Polanisia species are often recognized by their distinctive clusters of magenta flowers.
Botanists studied the distribution of Polanisia across different habitats.

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed after 'Polanisia dodecandra', the type species.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˌleɪˈnɪʃiə / (AmE), / pɒˌlæˈnɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed herbs with palmate leaves and creamy white to magenta flowers, characterized by many stamens of unequal length.
genus of herbs with palmate leaves and colorful flowers

Examples

Many species in the Polanisia genus are recognized by their showy flowers and distinctive leaf structure.
The Polanisia plants can be found in diverse habitats across North America.
Botanists studied the stamen length variations within the Polanisia genus for taxonomic purposes.

Etymology

From New Latin, apparently formed from Greek elements ‘polys’ (many) and ‘anisos’ (unequal), referring to the unequal stamens.
