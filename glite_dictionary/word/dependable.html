dependable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) able to be relied upon; trustworthy or reliable.
able to be relied upon; trustworthy

Examples

She is a dependable friend who always keeps her promises.
Our company is known for its dependable customer service.
This car has proven to be extremely dependable over the years.

Synset Examples

a reliable source of information
a dependable worker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliable - able to be relied on; trustworthy
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be relied on; not trustworthy

Etymology

From depend + -able, modeled on French 'dépendable'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈpendəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) trustworthy and reliable; able to be relied upon.
trustworthy; reliable

Examples

Everyone knows Sarah is a dependable colleague who always finishes her work on time.
For this challenging trip, you'll need a dependable vehicle that won't break down.
I value friends who are honest and dependable in difficult situations.

Synset Examples

a dependable worker
an honest working stiff
a reliable source of information
he was true to his word
I would be true for there are those who trust me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliable - able to be trusted to do what is expected or to provide correct results
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be trusted or depended on

Etymology

From 'depend' + '-able', patterning after similar English adjectives.

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndəbl / (BrE)
Able to be relied on to do what is expected or to perform consistently well; trustworthy and reliable.
able to be relied on; trustworthy

Examples

Everyone in the office knows that John is very dependable when it comes to meeting deadlines.
Our old car has been remarkably dependable, even after fifteen years of use.
She is a dependable friend who is always there when you need her.

Synset Examples

dependable in one's habits
a steady-going family man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliable - able to be trusted to do what is expected or to be accurate
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be relied on; not trustworthy

Etymology

From 'depend' + '-able', influenced by French 'dépendable' (reliable).
