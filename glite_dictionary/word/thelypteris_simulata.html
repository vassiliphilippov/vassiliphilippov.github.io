Thelypteris simulata

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɪptərɪs sɪˈmjuːlətə / (AmE), / θəˈlɪptərɪs sɪˈmjuːlətə / (BrE)
A species of delicate, feathery shield fern native to the eastern United States, sometimes classified within the genus Thelypteris.
species of shield fern from eastern US

Examples

The damp woodlands of New England are home to the beautiful Thelypteris simulata.
Botanists identified the rare Thelypteris simulata growing along the shaded riverbanks.
The fronds of Thelypteris simulata are finely divided, giving the fern a delicate appearance.

Etymology

From Greek thely- (female) + pteris (fern); simulata from Latin, meaning 'imitated'.
