bandwagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈbændˌwæɡən / (BrE)
an activity, cause, or trend that is attracting growing support, especially because it is fashionable or popular
a popular trend attracting support

Examples

As soon as the new diet became popular, everyone wanted to get on the bandwagon.
Politicians were quick to jump on the bandwagon after seeing the movement gain traction.
Some people join the bandwagon without fully understanding the issue.

Synset Examples

when they saw how things were going everybody jumped on the bandwagon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fad - a practice or interest followed for a short time with exaggerated zeal

Etymology

Originally referred to a wagon carrying a band in a parade; later came to mean anything with growing popular support.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbændˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈbændˌwæɡən / (BrE)
a large, often ornate wagon used for carrying a musical band, especially in parades or circuses
wagon for carrying a musical band

Examples

The circus parade's brightly decorated bandwagon drew cheers from the crowd.
Every summer festival featured a bandwagon at the front of the procession.
Children ran after the bandwagon, captivated by the lively music and colorful banners.

Synset Examples

the gaudy bandwagon led the circus parade

Etymology

Formed from 'band' (musical group) + 'wagon'; originally referring to parade wagons in 19th-century America.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
