employer

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈplɔɪər / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔɪə / (BrE)
a person, company, or organization that hires people to work for them
person or organization that hires workers

Examples

The employer is responsible for ensuring a safe workplace.
Many employers offer health benefits to their staff.
He asked his employer for some time off next week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: employee - a person who works for another person or organization
  • Synonym: boss - a person in charge of workers or an organization

Etymology

From 'employ' + '-er', with 'employ' from Old French 'employer' (to make use of), from Latin 'implicare'.
