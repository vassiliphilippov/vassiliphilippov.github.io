to inhume

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈhjum / (AmE), / ɪnˈhjuːm / (BrE)
to place a dead body in a grave or tomb; to bury
to bury a dead body

Examples

The family chose to inhume their loved one in the village cemetery.
Following the ceremony, the body was inhumed with great solemnity.
It is customary in some cultures to inhume the deceased within 24 hours.

Synset Examples

Stalin was buried behind the Kremlin wall on Red Square
The pharaohs were entombed in the pyramids
My grandfather was laid to rest last Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bury - to put a dead body in the ground
  • Antonym: to exhume - to dig out (a body) from the ground

Etymology

From Latin 'inhumare' (to bury), from 'in-' (in) + 'humus' (ground).
