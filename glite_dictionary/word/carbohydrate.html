carbohydrate

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrboʊˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbəʊˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
a compound consisting of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, found in foods and living tissues, and serving as a major source of energy for animals and an important structural component in plants.
a biological compound; key energy source

Examples

Eating foods high in carbohydrate is important for athletes who need extra energy.
Rice, bread, and pasta are common sources of carbohydrate in many diets.
The label on the cereal box lists the amount of carbohydrate per serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar - a sweet-tasting, soluble carbohydrate
  • Antonym: protein - a molecule made of amino acids, another major class of nutrients
  • Antonym: fat - a macronutrient used by the body for energy and insulation

Etymology

From French *carbohydrate*, from *carbone* (carbon) and *hydrate* (a chemical containing water).
