to convoke

1. VERB

/ kənˈvoʊk / (AmE), / kənˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to call people together for a meeting or assembly, usually formally
formally call together

Examples

The chairman decided to convoke a special session to address the urgent issues.
The school board will convoke a meeting of parents next week.
The president may convoke Parliament in times of crisis.

Synset Examples

The students were convened in the auditorium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to convene - to come together or bring people together for a meeting
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to send people away from a place or meeting

Etymology

From Latin 'convocare' meaning 'to call together', from 'com-' (together) + 'vocare' (to call).
