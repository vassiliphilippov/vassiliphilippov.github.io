Canis rufus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nɪs ˈruː.fəs / (AmE), / ˈkaː.nɪs ˈruː.fəs / (BrE)
A species of reddish-grey wolf native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as the red wolf.
species of red wolf

Examples

The Canis rufus population has become critically endangered in the wild.
Researchers are working to reintroduce Canis rufus into their natural habitat.
Unlike other wolves, Canis rufus has a distinctive reddish-grey coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red wolf - wild canid native to the southeastern United States

Etymology

From Latin: 'Canis' (dog) and 'rufus' (red).
