Dactylorhiza fuchsii

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæktɪləˈraɪzə ˈfʊksi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌdæktɪləˈraɪzə ˈfʊksi.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of European orchid with lance-shaped leaves spotted with purple and flowers ranging in color from pink to white or mauve, marked with deep red or purple spots or lines.
European orchid species with spotted leaves and flowers

Examples

The Dactylorhiza fuchsii is often found growing in meadows and woodland clearings across Europe.
Botanists admire the intricate marking patterns on the Dactylorhiza fuchsii flowers.
She photographed a cluster of Dactylorhiza fuchsii during her hike through the Scottish Highlands.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'daktylos' (finger) and 'rhiza' (root), species named after German botanist Leonard Fuchs.
