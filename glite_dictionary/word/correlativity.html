correlativity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˌlɛˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˌlɛˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a reciprocal or mutual relationship between two or more things.
reciprocal relationship between things

Examples

The correlativity between supply and demand is a fundamental principle in economics.
Scientists study the correlativity of different variables to understand complex phenomena.
There is an undeniable correlativity between exercise and overall well-being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - mutual relationship or connection between two or more things

Etymology

From correlate + -ivity, modeled on words like 'relativity'.
