maniac

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪniˌæk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪniˌak / (BrE)
a person who shows extreme enthusiasm or obsession for something, often in a way that seems excessive or unreasonable
person with obsessive enthusiasm

Examples

He's a football maniac who never misses a game.
My brother is a fitness maniac; he goes to the gym twice a day.
That guy drives like a maniac when he's late for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatic - a person filled with excessive zeal; synonym for obsessed enthusiast
  • Synonym: enthusiast - someone who is very interested in a particular activity or subject

Etymology

From Greek maniakos (mad, frenzied), from mania (madness).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪniˌæk / (AmE), / ˈmanɪak / (BrE)
(of behavior or emotion) characterized by wild disorder or frenzied excitement.
wildly disordered or frenzied (adj)

Examples

Her maniac laughter filled the silent room, startling everyone around her.
The fans erupted into a maniac frenzy when the team scored the winning goal.
He described the storm as having a maniac energy, unpredictable and terrifying.

Synset Examples

a maniacal frenzy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion
  • Synonym: maniacal - showing wild or violent behavior
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Greek *maniakos* (mad, frenzied), from *mania* (madness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02083546-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪniˌæk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪniˌak / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a wildly reckless or violent way, often considered insane or dangerously obsessed
an insane, wildly reckless or obsessed person

Examples

The police warned the public about a maniac on the loose in the city.
She drives like a maniac when she's late for work.
People called him a maniac because of his wild obsession with cleanliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunatic - a person who is insane (offensive, old use)
  • Synonym: fanatic - a person filled with excessive enthusiasm or obsession
  • Antonym: sane person - a person who is of sound mind

Etymology

From Greek *maniakos* (mad, frantic), from *mania* (madness).
