carditis

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdaɪtɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈdaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the heart or its lining, often caused by infection or autoimmune response
inflammation of the heart
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with carditis after reviewing the test results.
Certain bacterial infections can lead to carditis in vulnerable patients.
Early detection and treatment of carditis can prevent serious complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endocarditis - inflammation of the inner lining of the heart
  • Synonym: myocarditis - inflammation of the heart muscle
  • Synonym: pericarditis - inflammation of the outer lining of the heart

Etymology

From Greek kardia (heart) + -itis (inflammation).
