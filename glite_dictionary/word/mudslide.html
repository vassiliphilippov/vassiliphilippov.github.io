mudslide

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌdˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌslaɪd / (BrE)
A large mass of mud that moves suddenly down a mountain or hillside, often after heavy rain.
a landslide consisting of mud

Examples

Heavy rainfall last night caused a mudslide that blocked the main road.
Several houses were destroyed by a massive mudslide in the village.
After days of rain, a mudslide swept through the farmland, leaving destruction in its path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landslide - the sliding down of a mass of earth or rock from a mountain or cliff
