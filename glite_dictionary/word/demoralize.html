to demoralize

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to corrupt the morals or character of someone, especially by encouraging intemperance or sensuality
corrupt morally

Examples

He sought to demoralize his rivals by tempting them with extravagance and vice.
Some argue that violent media can demoralize young viewers over time.
The corrupt regime aimed to demoralize the population by promoting decadence.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to make someone or something morally depraved
  • Antonym: to edify - to improve someone's mind or character

Etymology

From French 'démoraliser', from 'dé-' (de-) + 'moraliser' (to moralize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02585532-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make someone lose confidence, hope, or morale; to lower the spirits of someone
make someone lose confidence or morale

Examples

The constant criticism from her supervisor began to demoralize her over time.
News of the team's defeat didn't demoralize the players, but inspired them to train harder.
High unemployment can demoralize an entire community.

Synset Examples

These news depressed her
The bad state of her child's health demoralizes her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Synonym: to dishearten - cause someone to lose hope or confidence

Etymology

From French démoraliser, from dé- (expressing removal) + moraliser (to moralize), based on Latin moralis (moral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01818363-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone lose confidence, hope, or spirit; to cause someone to feel discouraged or disheartened
cause to lose confidence or spirit

Examples

The team's defeat in the final game completely demoralized them.
Constant criticism can quickly demoralize even the most dedicated workers.
She refused to let her illness demoralize her, staying positive throughout her treatment.

Synset Examples

the boss's behavior demoralized everyone in the office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to encourage - to give hope, confidence, or support to someone
  • Synonym: to dishearten - to make someone lose hope or courage

Etymology

From French 'démoraliser', from 'dé-' (reversal) + 'moral' (morale, spirit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00623876-v
Synset POS: v
