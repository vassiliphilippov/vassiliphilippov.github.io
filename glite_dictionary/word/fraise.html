fraise

1. NOUN

/ freɪz / (AmE), / freɪz / (BrE)
A defensive barrier consisting of a sloping or horizontal rampart of pointed stakes, used to protect fortifications.
barrier of pointed stakes for defense

Examples

The old fortress was once protected by a fraise along its outer walls.
Soldiers constructed a fraise to deter enemy advances during the siege.
Historical records describe the effective use of fraises in 18th-century military architecture.

Etymology

From French "fraise," meaning a hedge of stakes, from Old French "frais" (shredded, jagged).

2. NOUN

/ freɪz / (AmE), / freɪz / (BrE)
A ruffled collar or neckpiece, typically stiff and elaborate, worn in the 16th century.
16th-century ruffled neck collar
archaic

Examples

The queen's elaborate fraise was decorated with pearls and lace.
Portraits from the Elizabethan era often show nobles wearing a distinctive fraise.
He wore a starched white fraise to complete his period costume.

Etymology

From French 'fraise' meaning 'ruff', derived from Latin 'fraga' (ruff).
