T-ammonal

1. NOUN

/ ti ˈæməˌnɔl / (AmE), / ti ˈæmənəl / (BrE)
a type of ammonal explosive that contains trinitrotoluene (TNT) to increase its effectiveness
ammonal containing TNT

Examples

The military used T-ammonal in demolition charges during the operation.
Because T-ammonal contains TNT, it is more powerful than standard ammonal.
Safety procedures are essential when handling T-ammonal due to its increased brisance.

Synset Examples

Trinitrotoluene increases the brisance of T-ammonal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonal - an explosive composed of ammonium nitrate and aluminum powder

Etymology

Blend of TNT (trinitrotoluene) and ammonal (ammonium nitrate and aluminum powder explosive).
