cuckold

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌk.oʊld / (AmE), / ˈkʌk.əʊld / (BrE)
A man whose wife has been unfaithful to him.
man with unfaithful wife

Examples

He felt humiliated and angry at being called a cuckold by his friends.
In medieval literature, the cuckold was often the subject of ridicule and pity.
Despite rumors about his marriage, he refused to see himself as a cuckold.

Etymology

From Old French *cucuault*, from *cucu* (cuckoo), referencing the bird's reputation for laying eggs in other birds' nests.

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌkəld / (AmE), / ˈkʌkəld / (BrE)
(archaic) To be sexually unfaithful to one's husband, thereby making him a cuckold.
be unfaithful to one's husband
archaic

Examples

In the old play, the wife cuckolds her oblivious husband with a visiting nobleman.
Rumors spread quickly that she had cuckolded him while he was away at sea.
He feared his wife might cuckold him as revenge for his neglect.

Synset Examples

She cheats on her husband
Might her husband be wandering?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - be sexually unfaithful to one's partner

Etymology

From Old French *cucuault*, from *coucou* (‘cuckoo’), referring to the bird's habit of laying eggs in other birds’ nests.
