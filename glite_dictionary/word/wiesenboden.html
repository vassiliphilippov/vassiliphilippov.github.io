wiesenboden

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːzənˌboʊdən / (AmE), / ˈviːzənˌbəʊdən / (BrE)
A dark meadow soil rich in organic material, formed in poorly drained, humid grassy or sedge areas.
dark organic meadow soil
medical

Examples

The scientist analyzed a sample of wiesenboden to determine its organic content.
Extensive patches of wiesenboden are found in the valley due to persistent poor drainage.
Farmers must consider the unique properties of wiesenboden when planning their crop rotations.

Etymology

From German 'Wiesenboden', meaning 'meadow soil' ('Wiese' = meadow, 'Boden' = soil, ground).
