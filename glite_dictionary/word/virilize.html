to virilize

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪr.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to cause the development of male physical characteristics, especially by hormonal means
cause development of male traits
medical

Examples

Doctors may virilize female patients through specific hormone treatments.
The condition can virilize women, leading to the growth of facial hair and a deeper voice.
Excess testosterone may virilize young girls with certain endocrine disorders.

Synset Examples

the drugs masculinized the teenage girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to masculinize - to make or become masculine in character, appearance, or behavior
  • Antonym: to feminize - to make or become more feminine in character or appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'virilis' (male, manly) + '-ize'.
