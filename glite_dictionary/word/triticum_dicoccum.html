Triticum dicoccum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪtɪkəm daɪˈkɒkəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪtɪkəm dɪˈkɒkəm / (BrE)
a type of hard red wheat grown mainly in Russia and Germany, and used in the United States as livestock feed; also known as emmer wheat
a type of hard red wheat (emmer wheat)

Examples

Triticum dicoccum is often used as a source of whole grains in artisan breads.
Farmers in Europe have been cultivating Triticum dicoccum for centuries because of its resilience.
In the United States, Triticum dicoccum is primarily grown to feed livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emmer - an old species of wheat; emmer wheat

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Triticum (wheat) and dicoccum (two-grained).
