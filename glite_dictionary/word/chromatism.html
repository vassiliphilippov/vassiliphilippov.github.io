chromatism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.məˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mə.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A hallucinatory perception of colored lights, often occurring as a symptom in certain medical or psychological conditions.
hallucination of colored lights
medical

Examples

Patients experiencing migraines sometimes report episodes of chromatism during their attacks.
The psychiatrist noted that his patient's visual hallucinations were characterized by intense chromatism.
Some epilepsy cases involve chromatism, where the person sees vivid, unreal colors.

Etymology

From Greek 'khrôma' (color) + '-ism' (denoting a condition or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.məˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.məˌtɪz.əm / (BrE)
abnormal or pathological coloration, especially of tissues or skin
abnormal pigmentation (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient's skin chromatism was noted as a symptom of the underlying disease.
Researchers studied the causes of chromatism in certain tropical fish species.
Some plants display chromatism due to environmental stress or genetic mutation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigmentation - coloration of tissues, especially skin

Etymology

From Greek 'khrōma' (color) + '-ism'.
