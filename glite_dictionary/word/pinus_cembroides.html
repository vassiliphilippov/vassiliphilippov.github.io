Pinus cembroides

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs sɛmˈbrɔɪ.deɪz / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs sɛmˈbrɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
A species of small pine tree native to Mexico and southern Texas, having two or three needles per fascicle and producing edible seeds known as pinyon nuts.
species of small pine tree native to Mexico and southern Texas

Examples

The Pinus cembroides thrives in arid regions of northern Mexico and southern Texas.
Many animals depend on the seeds produced by Pinus cembroides for food through the winter.
Researchers studied the distribution of Pinus cembroides across its entire native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon pine - any of several small pines with edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Pinus' (pine) and species 'cembroides', meaning 'similar to the cembra pine'.
