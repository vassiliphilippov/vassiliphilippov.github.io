to wive

1. VERB

/ waɪv / (AmE), / waɪv / (BrE)
to provide with a wife; to marry (someone) to a wife
to provide or marry with a wife
archaic

Examples

The king sought to wive his loyal knight to a lady of the court.
Many noblemen in the past would wive for power rather than for love.
You must wive before you inherit the family estate, said his uncle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marry - to join together in marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'wīfian', related to 'wīf' (woman, wife).

2. VERB

/ waɪv / (AmE), / waɪv / (BrE)
(archaic) To take (someone) as a wife; to marry a woman.
to marry a woman (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In centuries past, a man might wive into a wealthy family to improve his status.
He sought to wive a lady of noble birth, despite his humble origins.
Many a tale spoke of knights who would wive fair maidens after daring quests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marry - to take someone as a spouse; to become legally joined in marriage

Etymology

From Middle English wiven, from Old English wīfian (to marry), from wīf (woman, wife).

3. VERB

/ waɪv / (AmE), / waɪv / (BrE)
(archaic) To marry a woman; to take as a wife.
marry a woman (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In his youth, he dreamed to wive a fair maiden from the village.
The tale tells of a king who sought to wive and secure an heir to the throne.
Should he wive before the harvest, his estate would prosper, or so the elders said.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marry - to become legally joined in marriage

Etymology

From Old English *wīfian* (to take as a wife), from *wīf* (woman, wife).
