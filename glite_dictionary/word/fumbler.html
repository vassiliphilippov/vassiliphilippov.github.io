fumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈfʌmblə / (BrE)
a person who often makes mistakes, especially due to lack of skill or competence
someone who repeatedly makes mistakes

Examples

As a quarterback, he was criticized for being a fumbler in crucial games.
Her reputation as a fumbler made her colleagues hesitant to assign her important tasks.
Despite his enthusiasm, many saw him as a bit of a fumbler in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungler - a person who frequently botches tasks or acts clumsily
  • Antonym: expert - a person highly skilled or knowledgeable in a particular area

Etymology

From 'fumble' + '-er', forming an agent noun meaning someone who fumbles.
