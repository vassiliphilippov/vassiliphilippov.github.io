Acipenser transmontanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˈpɛnsər trænsˌmɒnˈteɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈpɛnsə trænsˌmɒnˈteɪnəs / (BrE)
a large sturgeon native to the marine and freshwater regions of the northwestern coast of North America, valued as a food and game fish
white sturgeon species of North America

Examples

Commercial fishing for Acipenser transmontanus has historically been an important industry in the Pacific Northwest.
The eggs of Acipenser transmontanus are highly prized for making caviar.
Due to overfishing, Acipenser transmontanus populations in many rivers are now closely monitored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white sturgeon - common name for Acipenser transmontanus, a large sturgeon species

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Acipenser' meaning 'sturgeon' and species name 'transmontanus' meaning 'beyond the mountains'.
