Machaeranthera bigelovii

1. NOUN

/ məˌkɛərə́nθərə ˌbaɪgəˈloʊviː / (AmE), / məˌkɜːrənˈθɪərə ˌbɪgəˈləʊviː / (BrE)
A wild aster species native to western Colorado and Arizona, characterized by leafy stems and flower heads with narrow reddish-lavender or purple rays.
a wild aster species of western US

Examples

The hills of Arizona come alive with Machaeranthera bigelovii in late summer.
Botanists observed Machaeranthera bigelovii thriving alongside other native asters.
Her field notes included a detailed sketch of Machaeranthera bigelovii in bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the genus Aster, known for star-shaped flower heads

Etymology

Genus name from Greek machaira (a type of sword) + anthera (anther); species named after American botanist John Bigelow.
