ornamentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnəmɛnˈteɪʃn / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəmɛnˈteɪʃn / (BrE)
the act or process of adding decorative details or adornments to something to enhance its appearance
adding decorative details or adornments

Examples

The palace featured elaborate ornamentation on its ceilings and walls.
Excessive ornamentation can sometimes detract from the clarity of a building's design.
Musicians often use ornamentation to embellish a melody and express their personal style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something added to make something more attractive

Etymology

From Latin 'ornāmentum' (equipment, decoration) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnəmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being decorated or provided with decorative details
the state of being decorated

Examples

The heavy ornamentation on the frame made it the centerpiece of the room.
Classical architecture is known for its elaborate ornamentation and decorative columns.
Many modern designs avoid excessive ornamentation in favor of clean, simple lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - the process or result of making something look more attractive by adding items or patterns
  • Synonym: adornment - the act or process of adding decorations
  • Antonym: plainness - the state of lacking embellishment or ornamentation

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, decoration) via Old French ornementation.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnəmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of decorating, or something that is added to decorate or beautify.
decoration or decorative addition

Examples

The ornamentation on the old building included intricate carvings and gold leaf.
Baroque music is known for its elaborate ornamentation and expressive flourishes.
She preferred a minimalist style, avoiding any unnecessary ornamentation in her home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something used to adorn or beautify

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, decoration), via Old French and Middle English.
