endoergic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛndoʊˈɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
(of a nuclear or chemical reaction) involving the absorption of energy from the surroundings.
involving absorption of energy (reaction)

Examples

The fusion reaction is endoergic, requiring a continuous input of energy to proceed.
In comparison to exoergic reactions, endoergic processes absorb energy from their environment.
An endoergic nuclear process will not occur spontaneously under normal conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exergonic - (of a reaction) releasing energy
  • Synonym: endothermic - (of a reaction) characterized by absorption of heat

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'ergon' (work). Modeled on 'exergic'.
