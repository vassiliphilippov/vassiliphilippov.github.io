to inspissate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈspɪseɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɪseɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to become thick or thicker, or to make something thick or thicker, especially by evaporation or concentration.
become or make thicker

Examples

If the jam is left to cool, it will inspissate and set properly.
The laboratory technician observed the plasma sample slowly inspissate over several hours.
In dry climates, mud can inspissate rapidly as water evaporates from it.

Synset Examples

The sauce thickened
The egg yolk will inspissate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thicken - become or make thick or thicker

Etymology

From Latin inspissatus, past participle of inspissare (to thicken), from in- (in) + spissus (thick, dense).

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈspɪseɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɪseɪt / (BrE)
to make (a liquid or substance) thicker or more dense, or to become thickened
make or become thick or dense

Examples

The solution will inspissate as water evaporates from the mixture.
Certain diseases can inspissate bodily fluids, making them harder to expel.
The chemist needed to inspissate the syrup before proceeding to the next step.

Synset Examples

thicken the sauce by adding flour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thicken - to make or become thick or thicker

Etymology

From Latin 'inspissatus', past participle of 'inspissare' (to thicken), from 'in-' (in, on) + 'spissus' (thick).
