Cuculidae

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkuːlɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / kjuːˈkjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that includes cuckoos, anis, and roadrunners.
bird family: cuckoos, anis, and roadrunners

Examples

The Cuculidae family consists of birds with slender bodies and long tails.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of the Cuculidae to understand their breeding behavior.
Members of the Cuculidae are found on every continent except Antarctica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a bird known for laying eggs in the nests of other birds

Etymology

From Latin *Cuculus* (cuckoo) + -idae (denoting a family in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ kuːˈkuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kjuːˈkuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds including cuckoos, anis, and roadrunners, known for slender bodies and, in many species, brood parasitism
bird family including cuckoos and roadrunners

Examples

The Cuculidae family contains many species that are skilled at mimicking the calls of other birds.
Among the Cuculidae, brood parasitism is especially common in cuckoos.
Roadrunners, which belong to the Cuculidae, are well known in the deserts of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a member of the Cuculidae family, known for laying eggs in other birds’ nests
  • Synonym: roadrunner - a fast-running bird of the Cuculidae family found in North America

Etymology

From New Latin *Cuculidae*, based on Latin *cuculus* (cuckoo).
