cystoplegia

1. NOUN

/ sɪs.toʊˈpliː.dʒə / (AmE), / sɪs.təʊˈpliː.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
Paralysis of the urinary bladder.
paralysis of the bladder
medical

Examples

The patient's cystoplegia required the use of a catheter for bladder management.
Surgery to the pelvic region may cause temporary cystoplegia in some individuals.
Because of his cystoplegia, he needed regular medical supervision to prevent infections.

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) + 'plēgē' (stroke, paralysis).
