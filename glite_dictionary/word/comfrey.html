comfrey

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfri / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfri / (BrE)
A perennial herb of the borage family native to Europe and Asia, often grown for medicinal uses and as groundcover in shaded areas.
a perennial herbaceous plant

Examples

She planted comfrey along the garden border as a natural groundcover.
Traditional herbalists use comfrey leaves to make soothing ointments.
The rapid growth of comfrey helps prevent weeds in shaded areas.

Etymology

From Old English 'comfrege', ultimately from Latin 'confervere' (to grow together), referring to its reputed healing properties.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfri / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfri / (BrE)
A plant of the borage family, with hairy leaves and bell-shaped flowers, whose leaves and roots are sometimes used in herbal medicine and cooking.
herbaceous plant used in herbal medicine and cooking

Examples

The leaves of comfrey are sometimes added to salads for their unique flavor.
Many gardeners grow comfrey to use its leaves as a natural fertilizer.
A hot cup of comfrey tea is thought to have soothing properties.

Etymology

From Old English *cumfrēo*, related to Latin *confervere* (to grow together), referencing its traditional use in healing.
