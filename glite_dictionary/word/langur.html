langur

1. NOUN

/ lænˈɡʊr / (AmE), / ˈlæŋ.ɡə / (BrE)
a slender, long-tailed monkey native to Asia, typically found in forests and known for its agility; also called a leaf monkey
slender long-tailed Asian monkey

Examples

The langur leaped gracefully from branch to branch in the dense forest.
A troop of langurs was spotted near the riverbank, feeding on leaves.
In several parts of India, the langur is considered sacred and protected by local communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf monkey - slender long-tailed monkey found in Asia

Etymology

From Hindi 'langūr', possibly from Sanskrit 'langūla' meaning 'tail'.
