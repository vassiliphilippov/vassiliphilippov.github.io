liberator

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪb.əˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪb.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who frees others from captivity, oppression, or bondage
a person who frees others from captivity or oppression

Examples

The general was hailed as a liberator by the townspeople after freeing the city.
She considered herself a liberator of minds, encouraging free thought and discussion.
Many view Abraham Lincoln as the liberator of the slaves in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oppressor - a person who keeps others in subjection or hardship
  • Synonym: emancipator - a person who sets others free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions

Etymology

From Latin liberator (one who sets free), from liberare (to free).
