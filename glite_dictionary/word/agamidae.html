Agamidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈɡæmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of lizards native to the Old World, comprising many species commonly known as agamas.
family of Old World lizards (agamas)

Examples

The family Agamidae includes the colorful and diverse agamas found throughout Africa and Asia.
Lizards of the Agamidae family often have well-developed limbs and sometimes elaborate crests.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Agamidae to understand lizard diversification in arid regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Agama' (the type genus) + '-idae' (a standard zoological suffix denoting family).

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡæmɪˌdi / (AmE), / əˈɡæmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Old World lizards (reptiles) within the order Sauria, including many species such as agamas and flying dragons.
family of Old World lizards

Examples

The Agamidae family includes a diverse range of colorful lizard species found in Asia and Africa.
Unlike chameleons, members of the Agamidae often have well-developed limbs and crests.
Scientists frequently study Agamidae to understand the evolution of Old World lizards.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Agamidae', formed from genus 'Agama' and suffix '-idae' (indicating animal family).
