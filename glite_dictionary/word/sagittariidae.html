Sagittariidae

1. NOUN

/ səˌdʒɪtəˈraɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sæˌdʒɪtəˈraɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, long-legged African birds of prey, consisting solely of the secretary bird.
family of birds of prey (secretary birds)

Examples

The Sagittariidae are distinguished by their long legs and unique hunting behavior in the African savannas.
Unlike most raptors, members of the Sagittariidae family hunt primarily on foot.
The secretary bird is the only living species in the family Sagittariidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary bird - a large, long-legged predatory bird native to Africa, sole member of Sagittariidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Sagittariidae, based on Sagittarius (genus name, meaning ‘archer’ in Latin).
