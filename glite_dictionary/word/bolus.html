bolus

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊləs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləs / (BrE)
a small rounded mass of a substance, especially chewed food at the moment of swallowing
small rounded mass, esp. chewed food

Examples

After chewing thoroughly, the food formed a bolus ready to be swallowed.
Each bolus passes from the mouth to the esophagus during eating.
The technician analyzed a bolus of chewed bread under the microscope.

Etymology

From Latin bolus (clod, lump), from Greek bōlos (clod, lump of earth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊləs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləs / (BrE)
a large pill or tablet, especially as used in veterinary medicine
large pill used in veterinary medicine
medical

Examples

The veterinarian administered a bolus to treat the cow's infection.
This treatment involves giving the animal a bolus rather than a series of smaller tablets.
A bolus is sometimes used to provide a single, large dose of medication in livestock.

Etymology

From Latin *bolus* (lump, ball), from Greek *bōlos*.
