serjeant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
(historical) An English barrister of the highest rank, formerly holding a position of distinction in the legal profession.
historical English barrister of high rank
legal archaic british

Examples

The distinguished serjeant addressed the court with great authority.
During the seventeenth century, a serjeant would wear a distinctive coif as part of their dress.
He aspired to become a serjeant, the highest honor among barristers at the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in higher courts; UK term

Etymology

From Old French *sergent*, from Latin *serviens* (servant, attendant).
