nutria

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtriə / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtriə / (BrE)
a large, aquatic South American rodent with webbed feet and a long tail, resembling a small beaver, bred for its fur.
aquatic South American rodent, fur animal

Examples

The wetlands are home to many animals, including the invasive nutria.
Farmers are concerned about crop damage caused by nutrias near the riverbanks.
The nutria was once prized for its soft, water-resistant fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coypu - another name for a nutria, same species of rodent
  • Degree Higher: beaver - large semiaquatic rodent; similar in appearance to nutria

Etymology

From Spanish 'nutria' (otter), ultimately from Latin 'lutria'.
