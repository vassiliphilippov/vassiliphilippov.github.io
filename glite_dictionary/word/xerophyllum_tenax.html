Xerophyllum tenax

1. NOUN

/ zɛrəˈfɪləm ˈtɛnæks / (AmE), / zɛrəˈfɪləm ˈtɛnaks / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to western North America, known for its stiff grasslike leaves and spikes of creamy white flowers
North American perennial plant with white flower spikes

Examples

Native Americans have traditionally used Xerophyllum tenax leaves for weaving baskets and mats.
In spring, the Xerophyllum tenax blooms, transforming the meadows with its creamy white flower spikes.
Hikers admire the unique appearance of Xerophyllum tenax during wildflower season in the mountains.

Etymology

From Greek 'xēros' (dry) and 'phyllon' (leaf) plus Latin 'tenax' (tenacious, clinging), referring to the plant's tough leaves.
