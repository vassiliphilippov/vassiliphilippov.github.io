celibate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlɪbət / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪbət / (BrE)
a person who abstains from marriage and sexual relations, especially for religious reasons or because of a vow of chastity
person who abstains from marriage and sex, often for religious reasons

Examples

As a celibate, he devoted his life to spiritual practice and community service.
The monastery was home to dozens of celibates who had taken vows of chastity.
She chose to become a celibate as part of her deep religious commitment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monk - a man who has withdrawn from the world for religious reasons
  • Synonym: nun - a woman who has withdrawn from the world for religious reasons

Etymology

From Latin caelibatus (state of being unmarried), from caelebs (unmarried man).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛləbət / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪbət / (BrE)
abstaining from sexual intercourse, often for religious reasons
abstaining from sexual activity

Examples

Many Catholic priests take a vow to remain celibate throughout their lives.
She chose to stay celibate after her divorce, focusing on personal growth.
The celibate lifestyle can be challenging for some individuals.

Synset Examples

celibate priests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaste - refraining from sexual activity, often for moral or religious reasons

Etymology

From Latin 'caelibatus' (state of being unmarried), from 'caelebs' (unmarried man).
