Allium cepa viviparum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæ.li.əm ˈsiː.pə vɪˈvɪp.ə.rəm / (AmE), / ˈæl.i.əm ˈsiː.pə vɪˈvɪp.ə.rəm / (BrE)
a type of perennial onion cultivated mainly as a curiosity or for early salad onions, characterized by producing bulbils instead of flowers
a perennial onion variety with bulbils

Examples

Gardeners sometimes grow Allium cepa viviparum for its unusual clusters of bulbils.
The Allium cepa viviparum can be added to salads for a mild onion flavor.
Unlike regular onions, Allium cepa viviparum produces small bulbs on its stalks rather than seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree onion - a common name for this variety, also known for producing bulbils

Etymology

From Latin: 'Allium' (garlic/onion genus), 'cepa' (onion), 'viviparum' (bearing young live).
