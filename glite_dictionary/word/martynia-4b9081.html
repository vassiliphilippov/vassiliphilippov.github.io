martynia

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈtɪniə / (AmE), / mɑːˈtɪniə / (BrE)
A sprawling annual or perennial herb native to Central America and the West Indies, characterized by creamy-white to red-purple bell-shaped flowers and distinctive horned fruit.
a Central American herb with horned fruit

Examples

The seeds of martynia are often collected for their unusual appearance.
Botanists are fascinated by the curious horned fruit produced by martynia.
A martynia plant can add an exotic touch to your garden.

Etymology

Named after John Martyn, English botanist.
