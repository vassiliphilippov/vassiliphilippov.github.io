camas

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.əs / (AmE), / ˈkam.əs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Camassia, native to North and South America, with grass-like leaves and star-shaped blue or white flowers.
North American Camassia plant

Examples

Native Americans traditionally harvested camas bulbs for food.
The blue flowers of the camas bloom in late spring across the meadows.
Many wildlife species rely on camas as an important food source in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camassia - the genus of plants to which camas belongs

Etymology

From Nez Perce Native American word *qém’es* (the plant).
