dishonor

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑnər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
Loss or absence of honor, integrity, or respect.
loss or lack of honor or integrity

Examples

His actions brought great dishonor to his family.
The athlete faced public dishonor after the cheating scandal was revealed.
He would rather die than live in dishonor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honor - high respect or esteem
  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect as the result of a dishonorable action

Etymology

From Old French 'deshonor', from Latin 'dishonor' (loss of dignity).

/ dɪsˈɑnər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
A state of shame or loss of respect brought about by one's actions or circumstances.
state of shame or disgrace

Examples

She would rather die than bring dishonor to her family.
The soldier faced dishonor when his actions were revealed.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of respect or honor resulting from a particular event or action
  • Antonym: honor - a state of high respect or esteem; opposite of dishonor

Etymology

From Old French deshonor, from Latin dishonor (loss of honor).

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈɑːnɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
(of a bank or other institution) to refuse to pay or accept a cheque, bill, or other negotiable instrument.
refuse to accept or pay (a cheque, etc.)

Examples

The bank will dishonor any check that is not properly signed.
If your account lacks funds, your bank may dishonor your payment.
Several merchants refused to accept cards from companies known to dishonor drafts.

Synset Examples

dishonor checks and drafts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to accept - to agree to receive or pay a cheque, etc.

Etymology

From Old French deshonorer, from Latin dishonor (loss of honor).
