stirrup

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪr.əp / (AmE), / ˈstɪr.əp / (BrE)
a pair of metal loops attached to the sides of a saddle, supporting the rider's feet and aiding in mounting and balance while riding a horse
metal foot support on a saddle

Examples

She placed her feet firmly in the stirrups before guiding the horse forward.
A broken stirrup can make riding dangerous for even the most experienced riders.
The blacksmith was asked to repair the damaged stirrup after the long journey.

Etymology

From Old English 'stigrāp', from 'stigan' (to climb) + 'rāp' (rope).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈstɪrəp / (BrE)
A small stirrup-shaped bone (the stapes) in the middle ear that transmits sound vibrations from the incus to the cochlea.
ossicle bone of the middle ear
medical

Examples

The stirrup is the smallest bone in the human body and plays a vital role in hearing.
Damage to the stirrup can result in significant hearing loss or deafness.
Doctors examined the patient's middle ear and found that the stirrup was malformed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stapes - the technical anatomical name for the stirrup bone in the ear

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a stirrup, the support for a rider's foot; related to the Latin 'stapes' (stirrup).
