Pipturus argenteus

1. NOUN

/ pɪpˈtɜːrəs ɑrˈdʒɛnti.əs / (AmE), / pɪpˈtjʊərəs ɑːˈdʒɛnti.əs / (BrE)
An Australian shrub or small tree in the nettle family, whose fibers are used in cloth-making.
Australian plant used for fiber

Examples

Traditional weavers use the Pipturus argenteus plant to produce strong fibers for cloth.
The leaves of Pipturus argenteus are soft and silvery, making it easy to recognize in the wild.
Researchers studied how Pipturus argenteus contributes to sustainable textile production.

Etymology

From botanical Latin; 'Pipturus' from Greek roots 'piptein' (to fall) and 'oura' (tail); 'argenteus' means 'silvery' in Latin.
