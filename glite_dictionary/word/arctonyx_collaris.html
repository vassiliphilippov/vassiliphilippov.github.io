Arctonyx collaris

1. NOUN

/ ɑrkˈtoʊ.nɪks kəˈlɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtɒn.ɪks kəˈlæ.rɪs / (BrE)
A species of badger found in Southeast Asia, known for its pig-like snout.
Southeast Asian badger species

Examples

The Arctonyx collaris inhabits forests across Southeast Asia and is recognized by its distinctive snout.
Researchers observed an Arctonyx collaris foraging for food in the undergrowth.
Due to habitat loss, the Arctonyx collaris is considered a species of conservation concern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hog badger - common name for Arctonyx collaris

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) and onyx (claw), with Latin collaris (of the neck).
