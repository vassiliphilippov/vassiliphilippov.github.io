leatherfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛð.ərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɛð.əˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A brightly colored tropical filefish with tough, leathery skin, found especially in warm waters.
a type of tropical filefish

Examples

The diver pointed out a brightly colored leatherfish swimming near the coral reef.
We observed several leatherfish gliding gracefully through the warm waters of the lagoon.
Scientists are studying how the skin of the leatherfish protects it from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filefish - a family of tropical and subtropical fish known for rough skin and unique shape
