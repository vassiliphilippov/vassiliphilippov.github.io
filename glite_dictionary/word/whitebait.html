whitebait

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtbeɪt / (BrE)
the small, edible young of certain fish, especially herrings, sprats, and smelts, typically eaten whole.
young edible fish, especially herrings or sprats

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its crispy whitebait served with tartar sauce.
Fishermen caught buckets of whitebait along the estuary this morning.
She ordered a plate of fried whitebait as an appetizer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtbeɪt / (BrE)
small young fish, such as herring or minnows, especially those caught and cooked whole as food
small fish eaten whole as food

Examples

Fried whitebait is a popular appetizer in many seaside restaurants.
The chef recommended serving the whitebait with lemon and freshly baked bread.
Schools of whitebait can often be seen near river mouths during their migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish, often used as bait or food
