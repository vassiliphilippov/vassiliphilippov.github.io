Anthurium scherzerianum

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθjʊriəm ˌskɛrtsəˈriːənəm / (AmE), / ænˈθjʊəriəm ˌskɜːtsəˈriːənəm / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant in the arum family, popularly grown for its bright scarlet spathe and spadix
a plant with scarlet spathe and spadix

Examples

The Anthurium scherzerianum is prized for its striking red flowers and unique twisted spadix.
Many indoor gardeners prefer Anthurium scherzerianum because it thrives in low light conditions.
She gave her mother a potted Anthurium scherzerianum for her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthurium - a genus of tropical American plants with brightly colored spathes

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Anthurium' from Greek 'anthos' (flower) + 'oura' (tail), species name honors German botanist Karl Scherzer.
