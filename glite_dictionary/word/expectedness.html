expectedness

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛktɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being expected or regarded as likely to happen; the characteristic of being unsurprising or usual
state of being expected; unsurprising

Examples

The expectedness of her reaction made the outcome easy to predict.
There was a certain expectedness to the annual celebration, which followed the same traditions every year.
The movie's ending lacked suspense due to its expectedness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unexpectedness - the quality of being unexpected or surprising

Etymology

From 'expected' + '-ness' (state, quality suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛktɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being expected; ordinariness due to being unsurprising or anticipated
the state of being expected or unsurprising

Examples

The expectedness of her arrival meant no one was particularly excited to see her.
There was a certain expectedness to his reaction that made the outcome feel routine.
He found comfort in the expectedness of daily rituals and regular schedules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unexpectedness - the quality or state of being unexpected; surprise or unpredictability
