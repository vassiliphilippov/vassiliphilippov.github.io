baud

1. NOUN

/ bɔd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
a unit measuring the rate of data transmission, equal to one signal change or symbol per second in telecommunications and computing
unit of data transmission rate

Examples

The modem was capable of a maximum speed of 56,000 baud.
Older communication systems transmitted data at a much lower baud rate.
Engineers debated whether the baud rate or the bit rate was more important for performance.

Etymology

Named after Émile Baudot (1845–1903), French engineer and telegraphy pioneer.
