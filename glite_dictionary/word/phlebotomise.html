to phlebotomise

1. VERB

/ fləˈbɑːtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / flɪˈbɒtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to draw blood from a person, typically as part of a medical procedure
draw blood medically
medical british

Examples

The nurse skillfully phlebotomised the patient to obtain a blood sample for testing.
In historical medicine, practitioners would often phlebotomise patients to treat various ailments.
Before the surgery, the technician phlebotomised several donors for transfusions.

Synset Examples

In the old days, doctors routinely bled patients as part of the treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to phlebotomize - to draw blood (American English spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'phlebotomía' (venesection), from 'phleb-' (vein) and 'tomia' (cutting).
