mephistophelean

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛfɪsˌtoʊfəˈliən / (AmE), / ˌmɛfɪsˌtɒfəˈliːən / (BrE)
characteristic of Mephistopheles; showing cunning, ingenuity, or wickedness typical of a devil.
devilishly cunning or wicked

Examples

His mephistophelean plan to manipulate his rivals was both cunning and ruthless.
She arched an eyebrow with a mephistophelean smile, hinting at her hidden intentions.
The villain's mephistophelean laughter echoed through the dark, empty hall.

Synset Examples

devilish schemes
the cold calculation and diabolic art of some statesmen
the diabolical expression on his face
a mephistophelian glint in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - characteristic of the devil; extremely evil or cruel
  • Antonym: angelic - resembling an angel in purity or kindness

Etymology

From Mephistopheles, the name of the devil in German legend and Goethe's 'Faust', with the adjectival suffix '-ean'.
