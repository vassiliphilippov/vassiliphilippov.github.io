Pinyin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːn.jɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.jɪn / (BrE)
The official system for transcribing the pronunciation of Mandarin Chinese characters into the Latin alphabet.
Romanization system for Mandarin Chinese

Examples

She used Pinyin to help her learn how to pronounce Mandarin words correctly.
Many Chinese textbooks include both characters and Pinyin for each lesson.
The student typed her essay in Pinyin before adding Chinese characters.

Etymology

From Chinese 拼音 (pīnyīn), meaning 'spell sound'.
