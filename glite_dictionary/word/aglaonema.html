Aglaonema

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡliəˈniːmə / (AmE), / ˌæɡliəˈniːmə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian plants, commonly called Chinese evergreens, with thick, fleshy leaves and unisexual flowers, often grown as houseplants
genus of tropical Asian plants; Chinese evergreen

Examples

We decided to add an Aglaonema to our living room for its attractive foliage and easy care.
The leaves of the Aglaonema are thick, glossy, and beautifully patterned.
Indoor gardeners often recommend Aglaonema for its tolerance of low light conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese evergreen - common name for Aglaonema plants

Etymology

From Greek aglaos (bright) + nema (thread), referring to plant features.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.ləˈniː.mə / (AmE), / ˌaɡ.ləˈniː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asian plants with thick, fleshy, oblong leaves and unisexual flowers, commonly known as Chinese evergreens.
genus of tropical Asian plants

Examples

Many offices use Aglaonema as a decorative plant because of its tolerance to low light.
The Aglaonema is prized for its attractive, easy-to-care-for foliage.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Aglaonema genus in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese evergreen - common name for plants of the genus Aglaonema

Etymology

From Greek aglaos (bright) and nema (thread), referring to the plant's appearance.
