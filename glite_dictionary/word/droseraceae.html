Droseraceae

1. NOUN

/ droʊˌsɛrˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / drəʊˌsɛrəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of carnivorous plants, including sundews and related genera, characterized by leaves adapted to trapping insects
family of carnivorous plants

Examples

Many species in the Droseraceae family are known for their unique ability to trap and digest insects.
Researchers have discovered a new genus within the Droseraceae while exploring Australian wetlands.
The Droseraceae include both the sundews and the aquatic waterwheel plant.

Etymology

From modern Latin Droseraceae, from genus Drosera, named for Greek drósos (dew) due to the dew-like drops on sundew leaves.

2. NOUN

/ droʊˌsɛrəˈsiː / (AmE), / drɒˌsɛrəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of carnivorous herbs and shrubs, including the sundews, with leaves adapted to trap insects
family of carnivorous plants (sundews, etc.)

Examples

The Droseraceae family is known for its unique insect-trapping mechanisms.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Droseraceae in Australia.
Plants in the Droseraceae family often grow in nutrient-poor, boggy soils.

Etymology

From New Latin Droseraceae, based on the type genus Drosera.
