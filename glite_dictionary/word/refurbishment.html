refurbishment

1. NOUN

/ riˈfɝːbɪʃmənt / (AmE), / riˈfɜːbɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the process or result of cleaning, repairing, and improving something so that it is in good condition again
the process of restoring or improving

Examples

After an extensive refurbishment, the museum reopened with new exhibits and improved facilities.
The hotel is closed for refurbishment and will welcome guests again in June.
A major refurbishment of the city's library provided modern amenities and preserved historic features.

Synset Examples

the inn was a renovation of a Colonial house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the action of restoring to a good state
  • Synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original condition

Etymology

From 'refurbish', from Old French 'forbir' (to polish), with 're-' (again) + 'furbish'.
