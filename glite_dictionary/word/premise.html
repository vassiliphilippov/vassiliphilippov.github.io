premise

1. VERB

/ ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
to base an argument or statement on a particular idea, fact, or principle that is assumed to be true
base on an assumed idea or fact

Examples

The theory premises its conclusions on the idea that human nature is constant.
Her argument premised that all people seek happiness above all else.
We must first premise that the data are accurate before proceeding with further analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assume - to take for granted or accept without proof

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *praemissa*, past participle of *praemittere* (to send before); originally a noun, used as a verb from the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
to state or introduce (something) at the beginning, especially as a basis for argument or explanation
state beforehand as basis for argument

Examples

He premised his argument with a definition of key terms.
The author premised the discussion on the assumption that all men are rational.
Before presenting her proposal, she premised it with a personal story.

Synset Examples

He premised these remarks so that his readers might understand

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praemissa', past participle of 'praemittere' (to send before).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
A statement or idea that is assumed to be true and that forms the basis for a logical argument or conclusion.
statement forming basis of an argument

Examples

Every argument must start with at least one premise that is accepted as true.
If your premise is incorrect, your conclusion will also be flawed.
The lawyer questioned whether the premise of the prosecution's argument was actually valid.

Synset Examples

on the assumption that he has been injured, we can infer that he will not play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true without proof
  • Antonym: conclusion - a judgment or opinion formed after considering the premises

Etymology

From Latin praemissa (things sent before), from praemittere (to send before).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
