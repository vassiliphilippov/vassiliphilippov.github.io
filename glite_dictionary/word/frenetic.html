frenetic

1. ADJ

/ frəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / frəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Involving a lot of excited movement or activity; fast and energetic in a rather wild and uncontrolled way.
wildly energetic or frantic

Examples

After the alarm sounded, there was a frenetic rush to the exits.
The company's frenetic pace of expansion worried some investors.
She spoke in a frenetic manner, barely stopping to catch her breath.

Synset Examples

frantic with anger and frustration
frenetic screams followed the accident
a frenzied look in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Greek phrenētikos (delirious), from phren (mind).
