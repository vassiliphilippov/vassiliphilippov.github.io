Fumimaro Konoye

1. PROPN

/ fuː-miː-ˈmɑː-roʊ koʊ-ˈnoʊ-jeɪ / (AmE), / fuː-miː-ˈmɑː-rəʊ kə-ˈnəʊ-jeɪ / (BrE)
(1891–1945) Japanese statesman who served as Prime Minister and played a key role in setting Japan's expansionist policies and forming alliances with Germany and Italy before World War II.
Japanese Prime Minister and statesman, 1891–1945

Examples

Fumimaro Konoye was instrumental in shaping Japan's foreign policy during the late 1930s.
Historians often discuss the impact of Fumimaro Konoye's government on the outbreak of World War II.
During his tenure as prime minister, Fumimaro Konoye sought to strengthen ties with Axis powers.
