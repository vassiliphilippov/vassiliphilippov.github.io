mentum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.təm / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təm / (BrE)
a projection resembling a chin, formed by the sepals and base of the column in some orchids
projecting part in some orchids

Examples

The orchid's mentum gives the flower its unique chin-like appearance.
Botanists use the shape of the mentum to help identify orchid species.
You can see the prominent mentum at the base of this flower's column.

Etymology

From Latin mentum (chin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.təm / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təm / (BrE)
a projection below the mouth of certain mollusks that resembles a chin
chin-like projection in mollusks

Examples

The mentum of this snail species is especially prominent and easily visible during movement.
Researchers examined the structure of the mentum to differentiate between mollusk families.
When threatened, the clam retracts its mentum along with the rest of its soft tissues.

Etymology

From Latin 'mentum' (chin).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.təm / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təm / (BrE)
the projecting part of the lower jaw, especially the chin, in humans and some animals
the projecting part of the lower jaw or chin
medical

Examples

A pronounced mentum can give the appearance of a strong chin.
The surgeon examined the structure of the patient's mentum before proceeding.
In insects, the mentum forms part of the mouthparts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chin - the part of the face below the mouth, formed by the lower jaw

Etymology

From Latin mentum (chin).
