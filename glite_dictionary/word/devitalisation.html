devitalisation

1. NOUN

/ diˌvaɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌvaɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of eliminating or reducing the vitality or life force of something, especially in a biological or medical context
reduction or removal of vitality

Examples

The devitalisation of the tissue was necessary before the surgical procedure could continue.
Urban development has led to the devitalisation of many local ecosystems.
Excessive use of chemicals can cause the devitalisation of plants, making them more prone to disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devitalization - American English spelling of 'devitalisation'

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (removal, reversal) + 'vitalis' (of life), via French and English; see also 'devitalize'.
