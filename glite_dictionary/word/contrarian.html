contrarian

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtrɛriən / (AmE), / kɒnˈtreəriən / (BrE)
An investor who deliberately acts against the prevailing opinion or market trends.
person who opposes prevailing investor sentiment

Examples

A true contrarian often buys stocks when everyone else is selling them.
Some analysts view Warren Buffett as a successful contrarian investor.
During market bubbles, acting as a contrarian can sometimes lead to significant profits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herd - group thinking or crowd followers in investing

Etymology

From Latin 'contra' (against) + English suffix '-arian' (denoting a person who advocates a principle).
