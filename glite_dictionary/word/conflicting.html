conflicting

1. ADJ

/ kənˈflɪktɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈflɪktɪŋ / (BrE)
in disagreement, opposed, or incompatible with one another; contradictory.
incompatible or contradictory

Examples

The two reports present conflicting accounts of what happened during the incident.
She was struggling with conflicting emotions about her decision.
There is conflicting evidence on whether the treatment is effective.

Synset Examples

the figures are at odds with our findings
contradictory attributes of unjust justice and loving vindictiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Synonym: contradictory - mutually opposed or inconsistent

Etymology

From verb 'conflict' + '-ing'. 'Conflict' from Latin 'confligere' (to strike together).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈflɪktɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈflɪktɪŋ / (BrE)
incompatible or opposed to each other; being in disagreement or at variance
opposed or incompatible; in disagreement

Examples

The committee was unable to reach a decision due to conflicting viewpoints.
He felt conflicting emotions about leaving his hometown for a new job.
Reports about the incident are conflicting, making it difficult to know what really happened.

Synset Examples

they were usually at odds over politics
conflicting opinions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Synonym: contradictory - mutually opposed or inconsistent
