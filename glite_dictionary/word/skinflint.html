skinflint

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪn.flɪnt / (AmE), / ˈskɪn.flɪnt / (BrE)
a person who is extremely reluctant to spend money; a miser
a very miserly person

Examples

My uncle is such a skinflint that he refuses to buy air conditioning even during heatwaves.
The company's skinflint approach to employee benefits caused widespread dissatisfaction.
Nobody wanted to dine out with Tom, since he had a reputation as a skinflint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible
  • Antonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money extravagantly and irresponsibly

Etymology

Originally referring to someone who would even scrape a flint (for fire) to save money; attested from the 17th century.
