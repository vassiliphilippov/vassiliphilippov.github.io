surprise

1. NOUN

/ səˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
the act of making someone feel sudden wonder or astonishment, often by something unexpected
the act of surprising someone

Examples

The party was a complete surprise to her, and she couldn't stop smiling.
His sudden arrival was such a surprise that it took me a moment to react.
She planned a surprise for her friend's birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden unexpected event causing strong emotional impact
  • Antonym: expectation - the anticipation of something happening

Etymology

From Old French 'surprise', from 'surprendre' (to overtake, surprise), from Latin 'super-' (over) and 'prehendere' (to seize).
