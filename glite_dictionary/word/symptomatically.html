symptomatically

1. ADV

/ ˌsɪmp.təˈmæt.ɪk.li / (AmE), / ˌsɪmp.təˈmæt.ɪk.li / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is characterized by symptoms; by means of symptoms.
in a manner related to symptoms

Examples

The patient was treated symptomatically while doctors awaited further test results.
Some infections can be identified symptomatically before laboratory confirmation is available.
She improved symptomatically, although the underlying cause remained unclear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symptom - a physical or mental feature indicating a condition or disease
  • Antonym: asymptomatically - in a way that shows no symptoms

Etymology

From 'symptomatic' (from Greek sūmptōmatikos) + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).
