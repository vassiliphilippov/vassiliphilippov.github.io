Pinus virginiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs vɚˌdʒɪn.iˈæn.ə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs vɜːˌdʒɪn.iˈæn.ə / (BrE)
a small, shrubby pine tree native to the eastern United States, with short needles in pairs and often twisted branches, commonly known as Virginia pine
a shrubby pine tree native to eastern US

Examples

The Pinus virginiana is easily recognized by its twisted branches and paired needles.
Many reforestation projects in the Appalachian region use Pinus virginiana due to its rapid growth.
Local wildlife often use Pinus virginiana stands for shelter during winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia pine - common name for Pinus virginiana, a shrubby pine of eastern US

Etymology

From Latin: Pinus (pine) + virginiana (of Virginia).
