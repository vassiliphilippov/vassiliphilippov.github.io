nationality

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌnaʃəˈnalɪti / (BrE)
the legal status or condition of belonging to a particular nation, either by birth or by naturalization
status of belonging to a nation

Examples

She applied for Italian nationality after living in Rome for several years.
His nationality was listed as Canadian on his passport.
The school has students of many different nationalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citizenship - legal status of being a member of a country

Etymology

From French 'nationalité', from nation + -alité (from Latin -alitas).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌnaʃəˈnalɪti / (BrE)
A group of people sharing common origins or traditions, often forming a nation or having a common identity.
people of common origins or traditions

Examples

Immigrants of the same nationality often form close communities in new countries.
He is proud of his nationality and celebrates its traditions.
The competition is open to people of any nationality.

Synset Examples

immigrants of the same nationality often seek each other out
such images define their sense of nationality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethnicity - the fact or state of belonging to a social group with a common national or cultural tradition

Etymology

From French 'nationalité', from 'nation'.
