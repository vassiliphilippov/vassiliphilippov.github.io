axon

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.sɑn / (AmE), / ˈæk.sɒn / (BrE)
a long, slender nerve fiber that transmits electrical impulses away from the cell body of a neuron
nerve fiber transmitting impulses from neurons
medical

Examples

The electrical signal travels along the axon to reach other neurons or muscle cells.
Damage to the axon can interfere with nerve function.
Each motor neuron typically has one long axon that controls muscle movements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dendrite - branched extension of a neuron that receives impulses
  • Synonym: nerve fiber - any elongated process of a nerve cell

Etymology

From Greek 'áxōn' meaning 'axis' or 'axle'.
