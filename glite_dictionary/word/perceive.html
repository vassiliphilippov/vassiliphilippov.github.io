to perceive

1. VERB

/ pərˈsiv / (AmE), / pəˈsiːv / (BrE)
to become aware of or conscious of something; to realize or understand
become aware or conscious of

Examples

It took her a while to perceive the true meaning behind his words.
He finally perceived the reason for their disappointment.
They began perceiving a change in the atmosphere as the meeting progressed.

Synset Examples

She finally perceived the futility of her protest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notice - become aware of something; observe
  • Synonym: to detect - discover or identify the presence of something
  • Antonym: to ignore - fail to notice or recognize

Etymology

From Latin 'percipere', from 'per-' (through) + 'capere' (to take).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00592914-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pərˈsiv / (AmE), / pəˈsiːv / (BrE)
to become aware of something through the senses, especially sight or hearing
become aware through the senses

Examples

From the deck, you could perceive the outline of mountains in the distance.
Some animals can perceive sounds at frequencies that are inaudible to humans.
She immediately perceived the faint scent of perfume as she entered the room.

Synset Examples

I could perceive the ship coming over the horizon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense - to become aware of something using the senses
  • Synonym: notice - to become aware of something, especially visually

Etymology

From Latin percipere 'to seize, understand', from per- 'thoroughly' + capere 'to take'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02110960-v
Synset POS: v
