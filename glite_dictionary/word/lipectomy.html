lipectomy

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɛktəmi / (AmE), / lɪˈpɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of fatty tissue, particularly for cosmetic or medical reasons
removal of fatty tissue by surgery
medical

Examples

The patient opted for a lipectomy to improve their appearance after significant weight loss.
A lipectomy may be recommended for individuals with large fatty deposits unresponsive to dieting.
Advancements in surgical techniques have made lipectomy safer and more effective over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liposuction - a cosmetic surgical procedure to remove fat from under the skin

Etymology

From Greek 'lipos' (fat) + '-ectomy' (removal by cutting).
