heartbreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtbreɪk / (BrE)
Intense emotional pain or sorrow, especially caused by the loss of a loved one.
intense sorrow from loss of a loved one

Examples

She suffered unbearable heartbreak after losing her husband in the accident.
The novel captures the heartbreak of saying goodbye to someone you love.
Despite her heartbreak, she found the strength to move on with her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - deep sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From 'heart' + 'break', referring metaphorically to the heart being broken by grief or loss.
