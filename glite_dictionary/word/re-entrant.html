re-entrant

1. ADJ

/ ˌriˈɛntrənt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛntrənt / (BrE)
(of an angle) pointing inward; forming an indent or notch into a shape, rather than projecting outward.
(of an angle) pointing inward

Examples

Some fortresses were designed with re-entrant angles to improve defensive coverage.
The coastline features several re-entrant bays that cut deep into the land.
A re-entrant corner in a polygon often requires special consideration in structural design.

Synset Examples

a polygon with re-entrant angles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salient - (of an angle) pointing outward; projecting

Etymology

From the Latin prefix re- (again, back) and 'entrant' (entering).
