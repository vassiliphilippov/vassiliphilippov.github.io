Cardiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrˈdiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdɪˈɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of somewhat heart-shaped, sand-burrowing bivalve mollusks, commonly known as cockles
family of heart-shaped bivalve mollusks

Examples

The Cardiidae are distributed worldwide, especially in sandy coastal regions.
Biologists studied the burrowing behavior of several Cardiidae species.
Many members of the Cardiidae family are valued as a food source in various cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockle - a common name for edible bivalve mollusks of this family

Etymology

From New Latin Cardiidae, from genus name Cardium (from Greek kardia, heart).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑr.diˈaɪ.di.i / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.dɪˈaɪ.di.iː / (BrE)
A family of bivalve mollusks, commonly known as cockles, characterized by their somewhat heart-shaped shells and habit of burrowing in sand.
family of heart-shaped cockle mollusks

Examples

Many species in the Cardiidae family are found in coastal sand and mud flats worldwide.
The unique heart-shaped shells of Cardiidae make them easily recognizable among other bivalve mollusks.
Marine biologists study Cardiidae to understand their ecological role in sandy seashore habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockle - a common name for edible bivalve mollusks in the family Cardiidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cardiidae', based on Greek 'kardia' (heart), referring to the heart-shaped shell.
