Pleione

1. NOUN

/ plaɪˈoʊni / (AmE), / plaɪˈəʊniː / (BrE)
A small genus of dwarf orchids native to India, Thailand, and Taiwan.
genus of dwarf orchids

Examples

The Pleione orchid is prized for its delicate and colorful blooms.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Pleione genus in northern Thailand.
My collection now includes several hybrids of Pleione, each with unique patterns.

Etymology

Named after Pleione, a figure in Greek mythology, mother of the Pleiades.

/ plaɪˈoʊni / (AmE), / pliˈəʊni / (BrE)

a genus of dwarf orchids

Examples

The Pleione orchids bloom spectacularly in spring and are highly prized by collectors.
Several species of Pleione are cultivated for their vibrant flowers and compact growth.
Native to Asia, the Pleione thrives in cool, mountainous regions.

Etymology

Named after Pleione, a figure in Greek mythology, mother of the Pleiades.
