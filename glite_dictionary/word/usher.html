usher

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə / (BrE)
an official stationed at the entrance of a courtroom or legislative chamber, responsible for showing people to their seats and maintaining order
court or legislative official; attendant

Examples

The usher guided the witnesses to their seats in the courtroom.
A uniformed usher stood by the entrance of the legislative chamber.
Before the session began, the usher ensured that all visitors were seated properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendant - a person who assists or serves others in a place

Etymology

From Latin 'ostiarius' (doorkeeper), via Old French 'ussier'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to show people to their seats, especially in a theater, church, or at a public event
person who shows people to seats

Examples

The usher guided us to our seats before the performance began.
She worked as an usher at the local cinema during the summer holidays.
At the wedding, a usher greeted each guest and handed out programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendant - a person employed to provide service to the public, especially in a theater or a museum
  • Synonym: guide - a person who leads or directs other people on a journey

Etymology

From Old French *ussier*, from Latin *ostiarius* (doorkeeper), from *ostium* (door).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10761478-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə / (BrE)
to escort or guide someone to their seat or designated place, especially in a theater or public event
escort or guide to seats

Examples

The attendant ushered the guests to their reserved seats in the front row.
She ushered us quietly into the auditorium just as the performance began.
He smiled politely as he ushered the audience into the theater.

Synset Examples

he ushered us to our seats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - to accompany someone to a place
  • Synonym: lead - to show someone the way

Etymology

From Latin 'ustiarius' (doorkeeper), via Old French 'ussier', Middle English 'usshere'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
