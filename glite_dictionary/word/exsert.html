to exsert

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɝːt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɜːt / (BrE)
to thrust or extend outwards
thrust or extend out

Examples

The flower's stamens exsert beyond the petals, attracting pollinators.
The turtle cautiously exserted its head from its shell to look around.
Some insects exsert their stingers only when threatened.

Synset Examples

He held out his hand
extend a hand
the bee exserted its sting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extend - to stretch out in length or space
  • Synonym: to protrude - to stick out or project

Etymology

From Latin exserere (to thrust out), from ex- (out) + serere (to join, put).
