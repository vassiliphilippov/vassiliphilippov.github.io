unfailing

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
always reliable or constant; never weakening or tiring
always reliable; never weakening

Examples

Her unfailing determination inspired everyone in the team to keep going.
I rely on his unfailing support during difficult times.
The dog's unfailing loyalty touched the hearts of the whole family.

Synset Examples

unfailing loyalty
unfailing good spirits
unflagging courtesy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - happening all the time or regularly; unchanging
  • Antonym: inconstant - frequently changing; not dependable

Etymology

From un- (not) + failing (from fail).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
always able to continue providing or giving something; never ending or diminishing
always able to supply or provide

Examples

She was an unfailing source of encouragement whenever things were tough.
His unfailing optimism inspired everyone around him to keep trying.
Their unfailing dedication to the project ensured its success.

Synset Examples

an unfailing source of good stories
a subject of unfailing interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexhaustible - unable to be used up; abundant

Etymology

From un- (not) + failing, from Old French faillir (to fail).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
always reliable or effective; not likely to fail.
always reliable; not failing

Examples

Her unfailing optimism inspired everyone around her.
His unfailing memory made him an excellent witness in court.
We appreciated her unfailing support during difficult times.

Synset Examples

a foolproof identification system
the unfailing sign of an amateur
an unfailing test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infallible - incapable of making mistakes or being wrong
  • Synonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted
  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From un- (not) + fail + -ing, meaning 'not failing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00968365-s
Synset POS: s
