Petunia hybrida

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtuniə hɪˈbraɪdə / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuːniə hɪˈbrɪdə / (BrE)
A widely cultivated hybrid group of petunias, with single or double flowers in various colors ranging from white to purple, derived from crossing Petunia axillaris and Petunia integrifolia.
cultivated hybrid group of petunias

Examples

The vibrant Petunia hybrida in her garden bloomed in shades of pink and purple all summer.
Many gardeners prefer Petunia hybrida for their resistance to pests and wide range of colors.
New cultivars of Petunia hybrida are introduced each year at the flower show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petunia - garden plant with colorful funnel-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name Petunia with the Latin hybrida (hybrid), indicating its hybrid origin.
