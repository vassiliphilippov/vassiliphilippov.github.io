ordinal

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrdənl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪn(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a number or position) indicating the order of something in a series, such as first, second, or third.
indicating order in a series

Examples

In the list, each item is assigned an ordinal number.
The contestants finished in ordinal order from first to last.
He explained the difference between ordinal and cardinal numbers to the students.

Synset Examples

ordinal numbers
held an ordinal rank of seventh

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cardinal - a number indicating quantity rather than order

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ordinalis (relating to order), from Latin ordo (order).

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrdnəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to or denoting a taxonomic order in the classification of organisms.
relating to a taxonomic order (biology)

Examples

The ordinal classification of these species places them in the Carnivora order.
Some ordinal distinctions can only be made using genetic analysis in modern taxonomy.
Recent studies have led to changes in the ordinal names of several plant groups.

Synset Examples

family and ordinal names of animals and plants

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'ordinalis', meaning 'pertaining to order'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03007974-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrdənl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnəl / (BrE)
a number that shows the position of something in a sequence, such as first, second, or third
number showing position in sequence

Examples

The teacher asked each student to write the ordinal next to their name.
In the list, the ordinal for her position was fourth.
Children learn to count both cardinal and ordinal numbers in early mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cardinal - a number that shows quantity rather than position (one, two, three, etc.)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ordinalis, from Latin ordo (order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13618523-n
Synset POS: n
