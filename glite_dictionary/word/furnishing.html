furnishing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of providing a room or house with furniture and other removable items such as shelves, carpets, or appliances
the act of supplying furniture and household items

Examples

The furnishing of the new apartment took several weeks to complete.
She enjoyed every part of the furnishing process, from picking curtains to choosing light fixtures.
After the renovation, the couple prioritized the furnishing of their living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furniture - large movable equipment, such as tables and chairs, used to make a room ready for use

Etymology

From furnish + -ing; 'furnish' from Old French 'fournir', meaning 'to supply'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an item of furniture, appliance, or other movable accessory that makes a home or space livable, usually used in the plural
movable items in a room or home

Examples

The living room furnishings included a plush sofa, a coffee table, and thick velvet curtains.
We need to buy new furnishings before we move into our apartment.
Hospital furnishings must be easy to clean and durable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furniture - large movable articles in a room, such as tables and chairs

Etymology

From furnish + -ing, meaning items used to furnish a space.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
