A. testudineus

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌtɛs.tuˈdɪn.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌtɛs.tjʊˈdɪn.i.əs / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish native to India and Southeast Asia, known as the climbing perch, whose gills are adapted for breathing air and which can travel short distances on land using its spiny pectoral fins
air-breathing climbing perch fish

Examples

The A. testudineus is famous for its ability to travel across land in search of new water sources.
Researchers observed an A. testudineus breathing air at the water's edge during low tide.
Fishermen in rural India often catch A. testudineus in rice paddies after heavy rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climbing perch - common name for Anabas testudineus, an air-breathing freshwater fish

Etymology

Abbreviation of Anabas testudineus, from Latin; 'testudineus' meaning 'of a tortoise' (possibly referencing its ability to survive out of water).
