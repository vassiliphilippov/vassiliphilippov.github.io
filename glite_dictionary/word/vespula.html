Vespula

1. NOUN

/ vɛsˈpjuːlə / (AmE), / vɛsˈpjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of social wasps, sometimes regarded as a subgenus of Vespa, which includes several common yellowjacket species.
genus of social wasps

Examples

The Vespula genus includes the common yellowjacket found in North America.
Researchers studied the nesting behavior of several Vespula species.
A Vespula colony can contain thousands of individuals by the end of summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vespa - a genus of wasps including large hornets

Etymology

From Latin vespula, diminutive of vespa (wasp).

2. NOUN

/ vɛsˈpjuːlə / (AmE), / vɛsˈpjuːlə / (BrE)
a genus of social wasps sometimes considered a subgenus of Vespa, including species such as the common wasp and yellowjackets
a genus of social wasps

Examples

Some species in the genus Vespula are commonly known as yellowjackets in North America.
The nests built by Vespula wasps can often be found in the ground or in wall cavities.
Biologists are studying Vespula populations to understand their role in local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vespa - genus of wasps, including hornets

Etymology

From Latin 'vespula', diminutive of 'vespa' (wasp).
