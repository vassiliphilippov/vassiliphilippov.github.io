Passeres

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsəˌriːz / (AmE), / ˈpæsəˌriːz / (BrE)
A former taxonomic suborder comprising typical songbirds; often used as an alternative name for the order Passeriformes.
suborder of typical songbirds

Examples

The order Passeriformes, sometimes called the Passeres, includes more than half of all bird species.
Scientists used to classify most singing birds under the suborder Passeres.
Field guides often list Passeres among the most common types of birds worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passeriformes - the order of perching birds or songbirds

Etymology

From Latin 'passer', meaning 'sparrow'.
