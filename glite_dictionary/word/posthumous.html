posthumous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːst͡ʃəməs / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊməs / (BrE)
Occurring or awarded after the death of the person concerned.
occurring after death

Examples

The author received a posthumous Pulitzer Prize for his novel.
She published a posthumous collection of essays by her father.
Their child, born a month after the accident, was a posthumous son.

Synset Examples

a posthumous award
a posthumous book
a posthumous daughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postmortem - occurring or done after death

Etymology

From Latin posthumus (after death), from post (after) + humus (ground, earth).
