partner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person's husband, wife, or long-term companion in a romantic relationship
husband, wife, or romantic companion

Examples

She arrived at the dinner with her partner of ten years.
Many people introduce their partner rather than specifying 'husband' or 'wife'.
His partner supported him throughout his illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spouse - a husband or wife

Etymology

From Middle English *partener* (companion, sharer), from Old French *partenaire*, from *part* (part).

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑrt.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːt.nə / (BrE)
to assign or provide (someone) with a partner; to join or associate as a partner or partners
to provide with a partner; to associate together

Examples

Each actor was partnered with a dancer for the competition.
The teacher partnered the students for the science project.
She partnered with a local charity to organize the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pair - to arrange in pairs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02337082-v

3. VERB

/ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
to act as the partner of someone in an activity or relationship
act as someone's partner

Examples

Fred wanted to partner with a local company to expand his business.
She partnered her brother in the mixed doubles tennis tournament.
In the dance competition, each contestant must partner a member of the opposite team.

Synset Examples

Astaire partnered Rogers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work jointly on an activity, especially to produce or create something

Etymology

From Middle English partener, from Anglo-Norman and Old French, from Latin 'partitio' (a sharing, division).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02376775-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person who shares ownership or responsibility in a business or legal partnership with one or more other people
member of a business partnership

Examples

After ten years at the firm, she finally became a partner.
Each partner is responsible for contributing capital to the business.
He and his partner opened a new restaurant downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - a partner or colleague in a business
  • Antonym: rival - a person or business competing against a partner

Etymology

From Middle English 'partener', from Old French 'parçonier', from Latin 'partitio' (a sharing, division).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person who shares or is associated with another in an activity, business, or relationship
person involved with another in an activity

Examples

He became my business partner when we started the company together.
She is looking for a tennis partner to play with on weekends.
The scientist and her partner worked for years on the research project.

Synset Examples

the musician and the librettist were collaborators
sexual partners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - a person joined with another in business or work
  • Synonym: collaborator - a person who works jointly with others
  • Antonym: rival - a person competing with another

Etymology

From Middle English partener, from Old French partenaire, from partir (to divide, share).
