Cucurbita mixta

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkɝːbɪtə ˈmɪkstə / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜːbɪtə ˈmɪkstə / (BrE)
A species of squash plant with round to oval fruit, characterized by grey, green, and white striped warty rinds.
species of squash plant

Examples

Farmers in Mexico often grow Cucurbita mixta for its edible seeds and flavorful flesh.
The fruit of Cucurbita mixta is known for its unique striped and warty rind.
Botanists classify several cultivated squashes under the species Cucurbita mixta.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cucurbita - The genus of plants including squashes, pumpkins, and gourds.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cucurbita' (gourd or pumpkin) and 'mixta' (mixed).
