unflawed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈflɔd / (AmE), / ʌnˈflɔːd / (BrE)
Without any imperfections, mistakes, or defects.
without flaws or defects

Examples

The diamond was remarkably unflawed and shone brilliantly under the light.
Her logic in the debate was unflawed, leaving no room for rebuttal.
They admired the unflawed craftsmanship of the antique clock.

Synset Examples

a flawless gemstone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flawless - without any flaws or defects; perfect
  • Antonym: flawed - having errors, imperfections, or defects

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'flawed'; 'flaw' from Old Norse 'flaga' (slab).
