Zea saccharata

1. NOUN

/ ziːə sækəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˈziːə sakəˈraːtə / (BrE)
a variety of maize developed for its young, sweet-tasting ears, commonly known as sweet corn
a type of corn with sweet edible ears

Examples

Farmers often plant Zea saccharata because consumers prefer its sweet flavor over field corn.
The young ears of Zea saccharata are harvested while still tender and served as a vegetable.
Researchers are studying Zea saccharata to improve disease resistance in sweet corn crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet corn - a variety of maize with high sugar content eaten as a vegetable

Etymology

From Latin: 'Zea' (genus of maize) and 'saccharata' (sugary, from 'saccharum' meaning sugar).
