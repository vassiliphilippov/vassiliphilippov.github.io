rejoicing

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
Great joy or celebration; a feeling or expression of great happiness.
a feeling or expression of great happiness

Examples

There was much rejoicing in the streets after the victory was announced.
The news of their safe arrival was met with rejoicing by family and friends.
After years of hardship, the community was finally filled with rejoicing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness
  • Antonym: misery - a state or feeling of great distress or discomfort

Etymology

From Middle English rejoicen, from Old French resjoir, from re- (again) + joir (to rejoice), from Latin gaudēre (to rejoice).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the expression of great joy, often through loud sounds, actions, or celebrations
expression of great joy

Examples

There was much rejoicing in the streets after the team won the championship.
The news of their safe arrival was met with loud rejoicing.
Their wedding was a time of rejoicing for family and friends alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jubilation - a feeling or expression of great joy
  • Antonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment

Etymology

From Middle English rejoicynge, from Old French resjoiss, related to 'rejoice' meaning 'to feel joy'.
