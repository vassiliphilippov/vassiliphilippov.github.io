Strombus

1. PROPN

/ ˈstrɑmbəs / (AmE), / ˈstrɒmbəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Strombidae, comprising various species of marine conchs or sea snails
genus of marine conchs (sea snails)

Examples

The shell of the Strombus is prized by collectors for its intricate design.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Strombus in the Caribbean Sea.
Many Strombus snails play an important role in maintaining the health of coral reefs.

Etymology

From Latin 'strombus', from Ancient Greek 'στρόμβος' (strombos, 'spiral shell').

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɑmbəs / (AmE), / ˈstrɒmbəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine gastropod mollusks, known as conchs, which is the type genus of the family Strombidae.
a genus of conchs (marine snails)

Examples

The shells of the Strombus genus are prized by collectors for their beauty.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Strombus genus along the reef.
The Strombus conch is noted for its distinctive long, curved shell.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Strombidae - the family of conchs to which Strombus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'strombus', from Ancient Greek 'στρόμβος' (strombos) meaning 'a kind of sea snail'.
