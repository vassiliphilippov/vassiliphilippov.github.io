Ho Chi Minh

1. PROPN

/ hoʊ tʃiː mɪn / (AmE), / həʊ tʃiː mɪn / (BrE)
(1890–1969) Vietnamese communist leader and statesman, known for leading the struggle for Vietnamese independence and serving as president of North Vietnam.
Vietnamese communist leader (1890–1969)

Examples

Ho Chi Minh is remembered as the founding father of modern Vietnam.
During the Vietnam War, Ho Chi Minh became a symbol of national unity and resistance.
Many streets in Vietnam are named after Ho Chi Minh to honor his legacy.
