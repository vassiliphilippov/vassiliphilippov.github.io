Elaphe

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪfi / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪfi / (BrE)
A genus of nonvenomous snakes commonly known as rat snakes, native to North America and parts of Eurasia.
genus of nonvenomous rat snakes

Examples

The Elaphe genus includes several species that are popular among reptile enthusiasts.
Researchers found that Elaphe snakes help control the rodent population in many rural areas.
Some Elaphe species display striking color patterns that make them attractive to collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat snake - common name for snakes of the genus Elaphe and related genera

Etymology

From Greek elaphē (a kind of snake).
