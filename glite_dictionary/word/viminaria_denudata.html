Viminaria denudata

1. NOUN

/ vɪməˌnɛriə dɛˈnudətə / (AmE), / vɪmɪˌnɑːriə dɪˈnjuːdətə / (BrE)
An Australian leafless shrub resembling broom, with small yellow flowers, commonly known as swamp wattle.
Australian leafless shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The Viminaria denudata thrives in wetlands across southern Australia.
Scientists studied the unique flowering patterns of Viminaria denudata for habitat restoration projects.
Gardeners appreciate Viminaria denudata for its delicate yellow blooms and resilience in poor soils.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vimen' meaning 'osier, twig' and 'denudata' meaning 'bare, leafless'.
