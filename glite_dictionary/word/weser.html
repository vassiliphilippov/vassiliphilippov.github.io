Weser

1. PROPN

/ ˈveɪzər / (AmE), / ˈveːzə / (BrE)
a river in northwestern Germany that flows northward to the North Sea near Bremerhaven
river in northwestern Germany

Examples

The Weser is an important waterway for inland shipping in Germany.
Historic towns like Bremen are situated along the banks of the Weser.
During the river cruise, we admired the scenic landscapes of the Weser valley.

Etymology

From German 'Weser', ultimately from Old High German 'Wesera', from Latin 'Visurgis'.
