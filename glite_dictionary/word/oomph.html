oomph

1. NOUN

/ ʊmf / (AmE), / ʊmf / (BrE)
energy, enthusiasm, or attractiveness, especially in a person's manner or style.
energy; liveliness; appeal
informal

Examples

Her presentation had a lot of oomph and kept everyone engaged.
The team needs more oomph if they want to win the championship.
This old car just doesn’t have the oomph it used to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pep - energy or liveliness
  • Synonym: vigor - physical strength and good health
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or energy

Etymology

Originally imitative, possibly influenced by German 'umf' (used as a sound of force or impact).

2. NOUN

/ ʊmf / (AmE), / ʊmf / (BrE)
sexual appeal, attractiveness, or an exciting quality that draws attention, especially of a person.
attractiveness or sexual appeal

Examples

The actress has a lot of oomph and lights up every scene she's in.
He felt his outfit needed more oomph for the party.
Her performance added the extra oomph that the show was missing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex appeal - sexual attractiveness or charm
  • Synonym: charisma - compelling attractiveness or charm

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a forceful impact, transferred to mean vitality or sex appeal in early 20th-century American English.
