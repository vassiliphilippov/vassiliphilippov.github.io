realised

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈrɪəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(Of a hope, ambition, or plan) successfully completed or brought to fulfilment.
achieved or fulfilled (goal, ambition, etc.)

Examples

Her lifelong dream of owning a bakery was finally realised.
The artist's vision was realised in his most famous painting.
The realised ambition brought him immense satisfaction.

Synset Examples

his mission accomplished, he took a vacation
the completed project
the joy of a realized ambition overcame him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplished - successfully completed or achieved
  • Antonym: unrealised - not achieved or fulfilled; not brought to completion

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'realise', from French 'réaliser' (to make real), from Latin 'realis'.
