Hydnocarpus

1. NOUN

/ hɪd.nəˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / hɪd.nəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large trees native to Indonesia and Malaysia, some species of which yield seeds used in traditional medicine.
genus of Indonesian and Malaysian trees

Examples

The seeds of Hydnocarpus are traditionally used in the treatment of leprosy.
One species of Hydnocarpus is valued for its medicinal oil.
Botanists are studying the ecological role of Hydnocarpus trees in Southeast Asian forests.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydno-' (truffle) and 'karpos' (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ hɪdˈnɑːkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / hɪdˈnɒkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large trees native to Indonesia and Malaysia, some species of which are valued for their medicinal oil.
genus of Indonesian and Malaysian trees

Examples

Several species of Hydnocarpus have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
Oil extracted from the seeds of Hydnocarpus trees was historically used to treat leprosy.
The lush forests of Sumatra are home to various members of the Hydnocarpus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydor' (water) + 'karpos' (fruit), used in scientific Latin.
