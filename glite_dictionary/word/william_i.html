William I

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(1027–1087) The first Norman King of England, also known as William the Conqueror, who invaded England from Normandy, defeated Harold II at the Battle of Hastings in 1066, and introduced many Norman customs to England.
first Norman King of England (1027–1087)

Examples

William I is often remembered for his decisive victory at the Battle of Hastings in 1066.
The Domesday Book was commissioned by William I to assess the wealth of his new kingdom.
William I's reign marked the beginning of significant Norman influence on English culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William the Conqueror - another name for William I of England
  • Antonym: Harold II - the king defeated by William I at the Battle of Hastings

Etymology

From the Old French given name Guillaume, from Germanic Wilhelm (wil 'will' + helm 'helmet, protection').
