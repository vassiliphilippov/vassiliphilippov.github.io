strategy

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræt̬.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of military science concerned with the planning, directing, and execution of large-scale operations and the overall conduct of a war.
military science of war planning

Examples

The general was renowned for his innovative strategy during the campaign.
Understanding the strategy of opposing forces was critical to the outcome of the battle.
Military academies often teach the theory and application of strategy in warfare.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tactics - the art of arranging and directing immediate military operations

Etymology

From Greek stratēgia (office of a general), from stratēgos (general, commander).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
a detailed plan or method designed to achieve a specific goal, especially in situations involving competition or conflict
detailed plan to achieve a goal

Examples

The company's new strategy aims to double its market share in the next five years.
Developing a clear strategy is essential for success in competitive games like chess.
They changed their strategy midway through the project to adapt to unexpected challenges.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tactic - a specific action or method used to achieve a short-term goal within a strategy
  • Synonym: plan - an intention or decision about what one is going to do
  • Antonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing spontaneously, without a plan

Etymology

From Greek stratēgia (generalship), from stratēgos (general).

Synset POS: n
