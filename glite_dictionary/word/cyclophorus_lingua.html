Cyclophorus lingua

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.kləˈfoʊ.rəs ˈlɪŋ.ɡwə / (AmE), / sɪ.kləˈfɒr.əs ˈlɪŋ.ɡwə / (BrE)
An East Asian fern with fronds shaped like tongues, sometimes classified in the genus Cyclophorus.
species of East Asian fern

Examples

The fronds of the Cyclophorus lingua resemble tongues, making it distinctive among ferns.
Botanists debated whether Cyclophorus lingua should remain in the Cyclophorus genus or be reclassified.
The lush green leaves of Cyclophorus lingua can be found in shaded East Asian forests.

Etymology

From Modern Latin; 'Cyclophorus' from Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'phoros' (bearing), 'lingua' from Latin meaning 'tongue'.
