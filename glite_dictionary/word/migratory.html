migratory

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪɡrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈmɪɡrətəri / (BrE)
(of animals) moving from one place to another at different times of the year, usually to feed or breed.
moving seasonally (of animals)

Examples

Millions of migratory birds travel between continents every year.
The marshes provide a vital stopover for migratory waterfowl during their long journeys.
Some whales are migratory and swim thousands of miles to reach breeding grounds.

Synset Examples

migratory birds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resident - (of animals) remaining in the same area all year round

Etymology

From Latin migrare (to move from one place to another).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪɡrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / mʌɪˈɡreɪtəri / (BrE)
(of a person or group) habitually moving from place to place, especially to find seasonal work.
habitually moving from place to place

Examples

During the harvest season, many migratory workers come to the region for temporary jobs.
The migratory lifestyle can be challenging for families with young children.
Farmers depend on migratory labor to pick crops at the right time.

Synset Examples

appalled by the social conditions of migrant life
migratory workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - wandering from place to place without a fixed home
  • Antonym: settled - having a permanent place of residence

Etymology

From Latin migratorius, from migrare (to move from one place to another).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02135196-s
Synset POS: s
