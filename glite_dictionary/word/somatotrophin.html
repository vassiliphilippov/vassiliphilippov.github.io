somatotrophin

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌmætəˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / səʊˌmætəˈtrəʊfɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth in humans and animals
growth-promoting pituitary hormone
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that somatotrophin is essential for normal growth in children.
Researchers are studying the effects of recombinant somatotrophin on livestock to increase productivity.
A deficiency in somatotrophin can result in growth disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth hormone - another term for somatotrophin, hormone that stimulates growth

Etymology

From Greek 'soma' (body) and 'trophē' (nourishment), indicating its role in body growth.
