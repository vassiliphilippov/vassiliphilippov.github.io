woollen

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊlən / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.ən / (BrE)
Made of wool or relating to wool.
made of or relating to wool

Examples

She wore a woollen scarf to stay warm during the winter.
The woollen blanket was soft and cozy.
My grandmother knitted me a beautiful woollen hat.

Synset Examples

a woolen sweater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - fibrous material from sheep used in textiles

Etymology

From Old English wull (wool) + -en (adjectival suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlən / (AmE), / ˈwʊlən / (BrE)
A fabric made from the hair of sheep, especially one with a soft, fuzzy texture, typically used for clothing.
fabric made from sheep's wool

Examples

The shop sells high-quality woollen imported from Scotland.
She wore a light woollen to keep warm in the chilly weather.
During winter, I prefer woollen over synthetic fabrics for their comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - the soft, curly hair from sheep or similar animals
  • Antonym: cotton - soft, white, fibrous substance from cotton plants, used for fabric

Etymology

From Old English wull (wool), with the suffix -en indicating made of.

WordNet ID: oewn-04606562-n
Synset POS: n
