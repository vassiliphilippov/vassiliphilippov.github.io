keratitis

1. NOUN

/ kɛrəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / kɛrəˈtɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the cornea, often causing pain, redness, and blurred vision
inflammation of the cornea
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with keratitis after she complained of severe eye pain and blurred vision.
Contact lens wearers have an increased risk of developing keratitis if proper hygiene is not followed.
If left untreated, keratitis can lead to serious complications such as vision loss.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn, in reference to the cornea) + -itis (inflammation).
