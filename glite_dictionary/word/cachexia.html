cachexia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkɛksiə / (AmE), / kəˈkɛksɪə / (BrE)
A general state of physical wasting and loss of weight, muscle, and strength, often occurring in patients with chronic illness.
wasting syndrome due to chronic disease
medical

Examples

The patient developed cachexia after months of battling cancer.
Chronic diseases such as HIV/AIDS are often associated with cachexia.
Effective treatment of cachexia remains a major challenge in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasting - progressive loss of weight and muscle mass

Etymology

From Greek *kakhexia* (bad condition), from *kakos* (bad) + *hexis* (condition).
