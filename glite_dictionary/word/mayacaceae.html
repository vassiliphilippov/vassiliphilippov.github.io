Mayacaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.əkəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əkəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of monocotyledonous bog plants in the order Xyridales, known for their grasslike appearance.
family of monocot bog plants

Examples

The Mayacaceae are typically found in wet habitats such as bogs and marshes.
Botanists have long debated the classification of the Mayacaceae within the order Xyridales.
Species in the Mayacaceae family exhibit adaptations to their waterlogged environments.

Etymology

From the genus Mayaca, which is the type genus of this family, plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.ə.kəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.ə.kəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of monocotyledonous bog plants of the order Xyridales, native to tropical and subtropical regions
a family of bog plants in order Xyridales

Examples

The Mayacaceae are often found in wet, boggy environments in South America.
Botanists have conducted genetic studies on species within the Mayacaceae to determine their evolutionary relationships.
Because Mayacaceae plants thrive in marshy soils, they are sometimes used in constructed wetlands.

Etymology

From the type genus Mayaca, plus the standard botanical Latin suffix -aceae.
