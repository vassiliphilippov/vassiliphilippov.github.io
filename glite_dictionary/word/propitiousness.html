propitiousness

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɪʃ.əsnɪs / (AmE), / prəˈpɪʃ.ə.snəs / (BrE)
the quality of being favorable or likely to lead to success
quality of being favorable for success

Examples

The propitiousness of the weather boosted everyone's confidence about the outdoor event.
Investors were impressed by the propitiousness of the economic forecasts this quarter.
We could not ignore the propitiousness of the signs pointing to a successful outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorableness - the quality of being favorable
  • Antonym: unfavorableness - the quality of being unfavorable

Etymology

From Latin 'propitius' (favorable, gracious) via Middle English 'propitious.'
