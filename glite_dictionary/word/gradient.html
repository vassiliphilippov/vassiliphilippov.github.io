gradient

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪdiənt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdiənt / (BrE)
the degree to which a surface or a line slopes away from the horizontal; a measure of steepness
slope or incline of a surface or line

Examples

The hikers climbed a steep gradient to reach the top of the hill.
The road has a gentle gradient, making it suitable for cyclists.
Engineers measured the gradient of the railway track before construction began.

Synset Examples

a five-degree gradient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slope - the steepness of a line or surface
  • Synonym: incline - an upward or downward slope

Etymology

From Latin 'gradiens', present participle of 'gradi' (to step, to walk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪdiənt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdiənt / (BrE)
a gradual change in the value or amount of something, especially the rate of such change in physical quantity or dimension
a gradual change in value or amount

Examples

The temperature gradient across the metal plate indicates how heat is distributed.
After the rain, the gradient of the river's flow became more pronounced.
The color gradient on the map shows elevation changes in the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slope - the steepness or degree of incline of a line or surface

Etymology

From Latin 'gradiens' (stepping, proceeding), present participle of 'gradi' (to step).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
