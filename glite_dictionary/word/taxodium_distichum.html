Taxodium distichum

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsoʊdiəm dɪˈstɪkəm / (AmE), / tækˈsəʊdiəm dɪsˈtɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of cypress tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its flared trunk base and growth in coastal swamps and river bottoms.
species of cypress tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The Taxodium distichum can be recognized by its distinctive flared trunks in flooded swamplands.
Many photographers travel to capture the beauty of old Taxodium distichum draped in Spanish moss.
Conservation efforts are helping to restore Taxodium distichum populations in southern wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - common name for Taxodium distichum
  • Degree Lower: Taxodium ascendens - pond cypress, related species

Etymology

From Latin: Taxodium (genus name), from 'taxus' (yew) + '-odium'; distichum from Greek 'distichos' (in two rows), referring to leaf arrangement.
