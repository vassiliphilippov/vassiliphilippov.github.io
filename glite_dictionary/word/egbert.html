Egbert

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛɡ.bɚt / (AmE), / ˈɛɡ.bət / (BrE)
(died 839) King of Wessex whose victories established Wessex as the most powerful kingdom in England.
King of Wessex, died 839

Examples

Egbert united much of southern England under his rule by the end of his reign.
Many historians regard Egbert as the first king of all England.
The military campaigns led by Egbert dramatically changed the balance of power in early medieval Britain.

Etymology

From Old English *Ecgbeorht*, meaning 'bright sword'.
