Manilkara zapota

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪlˈkɛrə zəˈpoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌmanɪlˈkɑːrə zəˈpəʊtə / (BrE)
A large tropical American evergreen tree whose latex yields chicle gum and whose fruit is edible; also known as sapodilla.
tropical tree; source of chicle and edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Manilkara zapota is sweet and has a malty flavor.
In some countries, the latex from Manilkara zapota trees is used to make chewing gum.
Farmers in Central America often cultivate Manilkara zapota for both its fruit and chicle gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapodilla - a common name for Manilkara zapota

Etymology

From the genus name 'Manilkara,' originally from South American languages, and 'zapota,' Spanish from Nahuatl 'tzapotl' (fruit).
