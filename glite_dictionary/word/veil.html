veil

1. NOUN

/ veɪl / (AmE), / veɪl / (BrE)
a thin membrane covering attached to the young fruiting body of some mushrooms, which later ruptures as the fungus matures
membranous covering on young mushrooms

Examples

The veil was still attached to the edge of the young mushroom's cap.
As the mushroom grew, its veil broke apart, leaving remnants on the stem.
In some species, the veil creates a ring around the stalk after it ruptures.

Etymology

From Old French 'veil', from Latin 'velum' (sail, curtain).
