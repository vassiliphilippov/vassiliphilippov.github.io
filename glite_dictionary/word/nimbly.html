nimbly

1. ADV

/ ˈnɪmbli / (AmE), / ˈnɪmbli / (BrE)
in a quick, light, and agile manner; moving with ease and skill
in a quick and agile manner

Examples

The gymnast moved nimbly across the balance beam, impressing the judges.
The cat nimbly jumped from the sofa to the windowsill.
She nimbly dodged the obstacles in her path during the race.

Synset Examples

nimbly scaling an iron gate
leaped agilely from roof to roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deftly - in a skillful and quick way
  • Antonym: clumsily - in a way that lacks skill or grace

Etymology

From "nimble," originally from Old English *nǣmel* (quick to seize).
