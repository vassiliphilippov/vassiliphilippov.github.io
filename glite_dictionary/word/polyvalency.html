polyvalency

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈveɪlənsi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈveɪlənsi / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The state of having a valency greater than two.
valency greater than two (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Some transition metals display polyvalency, allowing them to form compounds with different oxidation states.
The concept of polyvalency is important in understanding certain chemical reactions and bonding patterns.
Researchers studied the polyvalency of the element to determine its potential uses in new alloys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyvalence - The property of being polyvalent (chemistry)
  • Antonym: monovalency - The state of having a valency of one (chemistry)

Etymology

From poly- (many) + valency, reflecting multiple chemical bonds.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈvælənsi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈvælənsi / (BrE)
the capacity of a substance, especially an antibody or antivenom, to counteract more than one toxin, antigen, or type of microorganism
capacity to neutralize multiple toxins or antigens
medical

Examples

The polyvalency of this antivenom makes it effective against bites from several snake species.
Because of its polyvalency, the vaccine provides protection against multiple strains of the virus.
Researchers are developing drugs with increased polyvalency to treat a broader range of infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyvalent - having the ability to counteract multiple toxins or antigens

Etymology

From poly- (many) + valency (capacity to combine), patterned after 'polyvalent'.
