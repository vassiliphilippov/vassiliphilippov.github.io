honours

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑnɚz / (AmE), / ˈɒnəz / (BrE)
An academic distinction awarded for a university degree in recognition of above-average achievement, especially in British and Commonwealth universities.
university degree with honors
british

Examples

She graduated with first-class honours from Cambridge University.
After three years of hard work, he received his degree with honours.
A master's degree with honours can open many career opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honors - an academic distinction for a university degree, chiefly in American English

Etymology

From Latin 'honor' via Old French 'honor'. The academic use developed in British universities in the 18th–19th centuries.
