e'en

1. ADV

/ in / (AmE), / iÀên / (BrE)
Archaic contraction of 'even', chiefly used in poetry and older texts.
archaic contraction of 'even'
archaic

Examples

The poet wrote, 'So fair a face was never seen as shone that starry e'en.'
In Shakespeare's plays, one might encounter the word e'en instead of 'even'.
The ancient ballad sang, 'At e'en she sat by the glowing ember.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: even - at, or to, an equal degree; flat; or in the evening time

Etymology

Contraction of 'even', often used in poetic or archaic English.
