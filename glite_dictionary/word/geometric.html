geometric

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒioʊˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
(of design or decoration) characterized by simple shapes such as squares, circles, or triangles, often arranged in a regular pattern.
characterized by simple shapes in design

Examples

The rug features a beautiful geometric pattern of triangles and diamonds.
She wore a dress with bold geometric prints that caught everyone's eye.
Ancient pottery often displayed geometric designs using circles and straight lines.

Synset Examples

a buffalo hide painted with red and black geometric designs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - not representing external reality, but using shapes and colors

Etymology

From Latin geometricus, from Greek geometrikos (pertaining to geometry).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiː.əˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to geometry or to the principles and properties of geometry
relating to geometry

Examples

The artist used geometric shapes to create a sense of order in the painting.
Their designs have a strong geometric influence with sharp lines and angles.
The solution involved a simple geometric calculation of area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematical - relating to mathematics

Etymology

From Latin 'geometricus', from Greek 'geōmetrikos' (pertaining to geometry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02745944-a
Synset POS: a
