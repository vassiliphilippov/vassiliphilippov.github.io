exbibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛksbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,152,921,504,606,846,976 bits (2^60 bits), commonly used in computing to express very large data sizes.
unit of 2^60 bits (information)
medical

Examples

A data center might store several exbibits of information to support global operations.
Transfer speeds are now measured in exbibit per second on cutting-edge networks.
The file archive exceeded half an exbibit in total data.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pebibit - a unit of information equal to 2^50 bits
  • Degree Higher: zebibit - a unit of information equal to 2^70 bits

Etymology

Formed from 'exbi-' (from 'exabyte' and 'binary') + 'bit'.
