Dasyproctidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsɪˈprɑktɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈprɒktɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, tropical American rodents that includes agoutis and pacas.
family of rodents including agoutis and pacas

Examples

The family Dasyproctidae includes several species adapted to life in tropical forests.
Researchers observed that members of the Dasyproctidae play a crucial role in seed dispersal.
Agoutis, which belong to the Dasyproctidae family, are known for their speed and agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agouti - a short-haired, burrowing rodent of Central and South America
  • Synonym: paca - a large nocturnal rodent found in Central and South America

Etymology

From Modern Latin Dasyproctidae, based on the genus Dasyprocta (agouti) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdæsiˌproʊkˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌdasiˌprɒkˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of rodents comprising the agoutis and pacas, native to Central and South America.
family of rodents (agoutis and pacas)
medical

Examples

The Dasyproctidae family includes both agoutis and pacas.
Scientists study the Dasyproctidae to learn more about rodent evolution in tropical forests.
Several species within Dasyproctidae are important for seed dispersal in their native habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Dasyproctidae, based on the type genus Dasyprocta, from Greek dasys (hairy) and proktos (anus).
