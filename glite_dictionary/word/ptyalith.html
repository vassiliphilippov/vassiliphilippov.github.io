ptyalith

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.əˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪ.əˌlɪθ / (BrE)
A stone or calculus formed within a salivary gland.
stone in a salivary gland
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a ptyalith that was blocking the patient's salivary duct.
A large ptyalith can cause swelling and pain in the mouth.
Radiographs revealed the presence of a ptyalith in the submandibular gland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sialolith - a stone or calculus in a salivary gland or duct

Etymology

From Greek ptyalon (saliva) + lithos (stone).
