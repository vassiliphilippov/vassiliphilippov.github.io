to reexamine

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪɡˈzæmɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːɪɡˈzæmɪn / (BrE)
to look at or consider something again, especially in order to make changes or reach a new conclusion
look at again; examine again

Examples

The court decided to reexamine the evidence before reaching a verdict.
After hearing new testimony, the committee agreed to reexamine the issue.
She had to reexamine her own beliefs after the incident.

Synset Examples

let's review your situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to examine or assess something again
  • Synonym: to reconsider - to think about again, especially with changed opinions or intentions

Etymology

From re- (again) + examine, from Latin 'examinare' (to weigh, consider).
