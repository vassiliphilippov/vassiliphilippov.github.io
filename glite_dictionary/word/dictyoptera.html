Dictyoptera

1. NOUN

/ dɪkˈtiːəptərə / (AmE), / dɪkˈtɪəptərə / (BrE)
a former order or taxonomic group of insects, including cockroaches and mantids, sometimes treated as a superorder that encompasses the orders Blattodea and Mantodea
insect group: cockroaches and mantids

Examples

The order Dictyoptera includes both cockroaches and praying mantises in some classifications.
Recent studies have reassigned many species formerly placed in Dictyoptera to more specific insect orders.
The anatomy of Dictyoptera provides insights into the evolutionary history of winged insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Blattodea - the order of insects comprising cockroaches
  • Synonym: Mantodea - the order of insects comprising mantids

Etymology

From Greek diktyon (net) + pteron (wing), referring to the network of veins in the wings.

2. NOUN

/ dɪk.tiˈɑp.tə.rə / (AmE), / dɪk.tiˈɒp.tərə / (BrE)
a former order of insects that included cockroaches and mantids, now usually divided into the orders Blattodea and Mantodea
group of insects: cockroaches and mantids

Examples

The classification of Dictyoptera has changed as entomologists learned more about insect relationships.
Earlier textbooks often grouped cockroaches and mantids under the order Dictyoptera.
Recent studies have divided Dictyoptera into separate orders for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Blattodea - order of insects including cockroaches
  • Synonym: Mantodea - order of insects including mantids

Etymology

From Greek diktyon (net) + pteron (wing), referring to the network of veins in the wings.
