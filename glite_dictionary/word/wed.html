to wed

1. VERB

/ wɛd / (AmE), / wɛd / (BrE)
to perform a marriage ceremony or to marry someone; to get married
perform a marriage ceremony; get married

Examples

The couple plan to wed in an intimate ceremony next spring.
They were wedded in a small chapel by the sea.
The priest will wed them at noon on Saturday.

Synset Examples

The minister married us on Saturday
We were wed the following week
The couple got spliced on Hawaii

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marry - to join in marriage; to become the spouse of
  • Antonym: divorce - to legally end a marriage with someone

Etymology

Old English 'wed(d)an' (to pledge, engage to marry).

2. VERB

/ wɛd / (AmE), / wɛd / (BrE)
to marry someone; to take as a spouse in a formal ceremony
to marry; take as a spouse

Examples

They decided to wed after five years of dating.
The couple was wedded in a beautiful outdoor ceremony.
He plans to wed his childhood sweetheart next spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marry - to become the husband or wife of someone
  • Antonym: divorce - to legally end a marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'weddian' (to pledge, marry), related to 'wedd' (pledge, agreement).
