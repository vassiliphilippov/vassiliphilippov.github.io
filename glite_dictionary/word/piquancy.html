piquancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkənsi / (AmE), / ˈpiːkənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasantly stimulating, interesting, or exciting to the mind or senses
agreeably stimulating quality

Examples

The piquancy of his conversation made the evening unforgettable.
Her novel's piquancy captivates readers with every surprising twist.
A little piquancy in the discussion kept everyone engaged and entertained.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dullness - the quality of lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From French 'piquance', from 'piquant' (pricking, stimulating).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkənsi / (AmE), / ˈpiːkənsi / (BrE)
a pleasantly sharp, spicy, or appetizing flavor or quality; the quality of being stimulating or exciting to the senses
pleasantly sharp or spicy flavor; stimulating quality

Examples

The piquancy of the salsa made it the highlight of the meal.
She enjoyed the piquancy of the cheese, which left a tingling sensation on her tongue.
The writer's wit added a certain piquancy to his articles, making them a pleasure to read.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blandness - lacking strong flavor or stimulation
  • Synonym: spiciness - the quality of being spicy or peppery

Etymology

From French 'piquant' (pricking, stimulating), based on 'piquer' (to prick).
