Senecio

1. NOUN

/ səˈniːsioʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈniːsɪəʊ / (BrE)
A large and diverse genus of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, vines, and herbs, many of which are weeds.
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

Several species in the Senecio genus are grown as ornamental plants in gardens.
Ragwort, a common weed, belongs to the Senecio genus.
Researchers discovered a new Senecio species in the Andes mountains.

Etymology

From Latin 'senecio', meaning 'old man', referring to the white, hair-like pappus of some species' seeds.

2. NOUN

/ səˈniːʃioʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈniːsiəʊ / (BrE)
a large and diverse genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, including herbs, shrubs, vines, and trees, found worldwide
genus of flowering plants (daisy family)

Examples

The Senecio genus contains many species often grown as ornamental plants in gardens.
Some Senecio species are considered invasive weeds in certain regions.
Scientists recently discovered a new herb belonging to the Senecio genus in South America.

Etymology

From Latin *senecio* (old man), originally referring to the plant's white, hairlike pappus resembling an old man's beard.
