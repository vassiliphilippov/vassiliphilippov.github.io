Wampanoag

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɑːmpəˈnoʊ.æɡ / (AmE), / ˌwɒmpəˈnəʊ.aɡ / (BrE)
A member of an Indigenous people of Rhode Island and Massachusetts, historically known for greeting the Pilgrims in the 17th century.
Indigenous people of Rhode Island and Massachusetts

Examples

Many members of the Wampanoag tribe maintain their cultural traditions today.
The Wampanoag played a significant role during the settlers' first winter.
She is proud of her heritage as a Wampanoag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - A group of Native American peoples and languages of North America

Etymology

From Wôpanâak, their own name for themselves, meaning 'Easterners' in the Massachusett language.
