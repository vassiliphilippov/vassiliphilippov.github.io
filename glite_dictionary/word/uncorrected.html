uncorrected

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.kəˈrɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌn.kəˈrɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
not having been corrected, changed, or disciplined; remaining in its original or faulty state.
not corrected or disciplined

Examples

The manuscript was published in its uncorrected form, full of errors.
His uncorrected behavior eventually led to bigger problems at school.
If flaws are left uncorrected, they can cause serious issues later on.

Synset Examples

let her children grow up uncorrected

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corrected - altered to remove errors or faults

Etymology

From un- (not) + corrected (past participle of correct).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəˈrɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈrɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
not corrected; still containing faults, errors, or deficiencies.
not corrected; still faulty

Examples

The report was returned because it was still uncorrected.
Patients with uncorrected vision problems may have difficulty reading.
He submitted the essay with several uncorrected mistakes.

Synset Examples

uncorrected astigmatism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corrected - made right or free from error
