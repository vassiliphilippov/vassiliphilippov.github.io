enantiomorphism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnæn.ti.əˈmɔːr.fɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɪˌnantɪəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
the property or relation of being mirror images of each other, especially in crystals or molecules that cannot be superimposed
being a non-superimposable mirror image

Examples

The enantiomorphism of these crystals results in different optical activities.
In organic chemistry, enantiomorphism explains how certain molecules can exist as left- and right-handed forms.
Researchers studied the enantiomorphism in quartz to understand its physical properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirality - property of a molecule that is not superimposable on its mirror image
  • Antonym: symmetry - balanced proportions or correspondence in size and shape on opposite sides

Etymology

From Greek 'enantios' (opposite) + 'morphē' (form) + English '-ism'.
