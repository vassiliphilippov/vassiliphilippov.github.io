Nat Turner

1. PROPN

/ næt ˈtɝːnər / (AmE), / næt ˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
(1800–1831) An enslaved African American who led a slave rebellion in Virginia in 1831, resulting in his capture and execution.
leader of 1831 slave rebellion in Virginia

Examples

The story of Nat Turner is often discussed in American history classes.
Historians continue to debate the impact of Nat Turner's rebellion on the abolition movement.
A new biography explores the life and motivations of Nat Turner.
