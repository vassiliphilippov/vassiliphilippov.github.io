socle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒkl / (BrE)
a plain, usually projecting base or plinth that supports a wall, column, or statue
a base or plinth supporting a wall or column

Examples

The ancient building rests on a sturdy socle of stone that has endured centuries.
Marble columns rose from a simple socle at the entrance of the museum.
The statue was mounted on a granite socle to elevate it above the square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plinth - a heavy base supporting a statue or vase

Etymology

From French 'socle', from Italian 'zoccolo' (clog, base), from Latin 'socculus' (small shoe).
