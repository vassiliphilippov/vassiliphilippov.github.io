plumbago

1. NOUN

/ pləmˈbeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / plʌmˈbeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Plumbago, typically with blue or white flowers, often grown as ornamentals.
a plant of the genus Plumbago

Examples

The plumbago in the corner of the garden has vibrant blue blossoms.
They planted several plumbagos along the fence to attract butterflies.
Her favorite ornamental plant is the white-flowered plumbago.

Etymology

From Latin 'plumbago' (leadwort), from 'plumbum' (lead), referring to a supposed resemblance or medicinal use.

2. NOUN

/ pləmˈbeɪgoʊ / (AmE), / plʌmˈbeɪgəʊ / (BrE)
A form of graphite, used as a lubricant and in the moderation of nuclear reactors.
form of graphite; industrial use

Examples

The engineer applied plumbago to lubricate the machinery's moving parts.
In nuclear reactors, plumbago can serve as a neutron moderator.
The pencils were originally made using plumbago instead of modern graphite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphite - a soft black carbon mineral used as a lubricant and for pencils

Etymology

From Latin plumbago (a resemblance to lead), from plumbum (lead).
