Plumbago

1. NOUN

/ plʌmˈbeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / plʌmˈbeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs, herbs, and woody vines, commonly known as leadwort, native to warm regions.
a genus of leadwort plants

Examples

The garden features several varieties of Plumbago for their striking blue flowers.
Botanists classify leadwort species under the genus Plumbago.
Plumbago thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Etymology

From Latin *plumbago* (lead ore), referring to the plant's lead-colored flowers and sap.
