rose-colored

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊz ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
having the color of a rose; pinkish-red in hue.
having the color of a rose

Examples

She wore a beautiful rose-colored dress to the party.
The morning sky was painted in soft rose-colored clouds.
He prefers rose-colored walls in his living room for their warm, soothing effect.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color; also used to describe similar hues
  • Degree Higher: red - the color at the end of the spectrum next to orange

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊz ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having an unduly optimistic or hopeful outlook; viewing things in an unrealistically positive way.
overly optimistic; unrealistically hopeful

Examples

She tends to have a rose-colored view of every situation, even when things are difficult.
Politicians often present their plans in a rose-colored light to win support.
It's important to be hopeful, but not everything can be seen through rose-colored glasses.

Synset Examples

a rosy future
looked at the world through rose-colored glasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimistic - hopeful and confident about the future
  • Antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are; practical

Etymology

Refers to the color rose, with figurative usage dating from the 19th century relating to seeing the world tinted pleasantly pink.
