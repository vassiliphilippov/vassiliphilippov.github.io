unjustified

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd / (BrE)
Not having, or not shown to have, a good reason or legitimate excuse; not justified or authorized.
lacking justification or authorization

Examples

The judge ruled that the search was unjustified because there was no evidence of wrongdoing.
Her criticism of the staff was entirely unjustified and hurtful.
Many employees felt that the new restrictions were unjustified and unnecessarily harsh.

Synset Examples

desire for undue private profit
unwarranted limitations of personal freedom

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason or legitimate excuse
  • Synonym: unwarranted - not justified or authorized

Etymology

From un- (not) + justified, from justify, from Old French justifier.
