to misunderstand

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.ˌʌn.dɚˈstænd / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ʌn.dəˈstænd / (BrE)
to fail to interpret or comprehend something correctly; to interpret in the wrong way
fail to interpret correctly

Examples

Please don’t misunderstand my intentions; I only want to help.
Mark clearly misunderstood the rules and made a mistake on the test.
If you misunderstand the instructions, you might assemble the furniture incorrectly.

Synset Examples

Don't misinterpret my comments as criticism
She misconstrued my remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misinterpret - to interpret something incorrectly
  • Antonym: to understand - to perceive the intended meaning of something

Etymology

From Old English 'understandan' (to understand) with prefix 'mis-' meaning wrongly.
