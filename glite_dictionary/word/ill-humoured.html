ill-humoured

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪlˈhjuːmɚd / (AmE), / ˌɪlˈhjuːməd / (BrE)
having a surly, unfriendly, or irritable disposition; easily annoyed or inclined to be brusque.
having a surly or irritable temper

Examples

Her ill-humoured tone made it clear she didn't want to continue the conversation.
Despite his ill-humoured remarks, his colleagues knew he meant no real harm.
The ill-humoured waiter provided little warmth or welcome during our meal.

Synset Examples

crusty remarks
a crusty old man
his curmudgeonly temper
gruff manner
a gruff reply

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good-humoured - cheerful and friendly in temperament
  • Synonym: crusty - irritable in manner; curt and surly
  • Synonym: curmudgeonly - bad-tempered and difficult

Etymology

Formed from 'ill-' (badly) + 'humoured' (having a particular mood or temperament), modelled on 'good-humoured'.
