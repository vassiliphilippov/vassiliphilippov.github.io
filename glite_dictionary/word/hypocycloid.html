hypocycloid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊˈsaɪ.klɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəʊˈsʌɪ.klɔɪd / (BrE)
A plane curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle that rolls without slipping inside a larger fixed circle.
type of mathematical curve

Examples

The gear teeth were designed based on the shape of a hypocycloid.
Mathematicians often study the properties of the hypocycloid and its applications.
The curve drawn inside the circle was identified as a hypocycloid in his research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epicycloid - a curve traced by a point on a circle rolling outside another circle

Etymology

From French *hypocycloïde*, from Greek *hypo-* (under) and *kyklos* (circle).
