detestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛtəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛtɪˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
intense hatred and disgust; the feeling of abhorrence.
intense hatred and disgust

Examples

Her detestation of cruelty was evident in everything she did.
He spoke of the corrupt leader with absolute detestation.
There was mutual detestation between the rival teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loathing - strong feeling of hatred or disgust
  • Synonym: abhorrence - a feeling of repulsion; disgusted loathing
  • Antonym: love - a strong feeling of affection or deep attachment

Etymology

From Middle French 'détestation', from Latin 'detestatio', meaning 'denunciation, curse'.
