achromatin

1. NOUN

/ əˈkroʊmətɪn / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmətɪn / (BrE)
The part of a cell nucleus that does not take up colored stains or dyes, and appears lightly colored or uncolored under a microscope.
unstained part of cell nucleus
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the achromatin appeared as a pale area in the cell nucleus.
Scientists study achromatin to understand its role in genetic regulation.
In stained tissue sections, achromatin can be distinguished from darker, chromatin-rich regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'chroma' (color) + '-in' (a chemical suffix).
