baa-lamb

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːˌlæm / (AmE), / ˈbɑːˌlæm / (BrE)
a child's word for a sheep or lamb, especially one that is young; commonly used in speaking to or by children
child's word for sheep or lamb

Examples

The little girl hugged the baa-lamb at the petting zoo.
In the storybook, the lost baa-lamb finds its way home.
He dressed as a baa-lamb for the school play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamb - a young sheep
  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant animal with a thick woolly coat

Etymology

Imitative of the sound 'baa' made by sheep, combined with 'lamb'.
