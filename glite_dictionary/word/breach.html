breach

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
The act of breaking or failing to observe a law, agreement, or code of conduct; a failure to fulfill an obligation or promise.
violation of an agreement or promise

Examples

The company was sued for a breach of contract.
Any breach of the rules may lead to disqualification.
There was a serious breach of trust between the two parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violation - the act of breaking a law or agreement
  • Antonym: fulfillment - the act of carrying out or satisfying an obligation

Etymology

From Old French *breche*, from *bruch* (break) in Germanic origin.

5. NOUN

/ briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
an opening or gap, especially one made in a wall, barrier, or defense, typically by force
an opening or gap in a barrier or defense

Examples

The storm caused a breach in the seawall, leading to flooding in the village.
Soldiers rushed through the breach in the enemy's fortifications to capture the stronghold.
Engineers worked overnight to repair the breach in the dam before more water escaped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an opening or space in something that separates things or allows passage
  • Antonym: closure - the act or process of closing something, or the state of being closed

Etymology

From Old French *breche* 'breach, opening', from Old English *bryċe* 'act of breaking'.
