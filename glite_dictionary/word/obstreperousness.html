obstreperousness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstrɛpɚəsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstrɛpərəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being noisy and difficult to control; stubborn defiance marked by loud or unruly behavior.
noisy defiance; unruliness

Examples

His obstreperousness in meetings often derailed productive discussion.
The teacher struggled to manage the obstreperousness of the rowdiest students.
Tired of the constant obstreperousness from the crowd, the speaker decided to leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstreperous - noisy and difficult to control

Etymology

From obstreperous + -ness; 'obstreperous' from Latin obstreperus, from obstrepere (to make a noise against).
