damourite

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmʊrˌaɪt / (AmE), / dəˈmʊə.raɪt / (BrE)
a fine-grained, pale-colored variety of muscovite mica, sometimes found in soft, powdery masses
variety of muscovite mica

Examples

Geologists discovered a pocket of damourite during the mineral survey in the mountain range.
The pale, powdery appearance of damourite distinguishes it from other types of mica.
Collectors prize damourite for its softness and distinctive coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscovite - a common silicate mineral in the mica group

Etymology

Named after French mineralogist Jean-Pierre Damour (1808–1908), who described it.
