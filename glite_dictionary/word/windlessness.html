windlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪndləsnəs / (BrE)
The state or condition of having no wind; complete calm.
absence of wind; calmness

Examples

The windlessness of the afternoon made the air feel even hotter.
Sailors dreaded periods of windlessness that could leave their ships stranded for days.
A sense of stillness settled over the lake, interrupted only by the windlessness that muffled all sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - a period or state of tranquillity without wind or disturbance
  • Antonym: breeze - a gentle wind
  • Antonym: gale - a very strong wind

Etymology

Formed from 'windless' (without wind) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
