chamfer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.fə / (BrE)
A sloping edge or corner formed by cutting off the edge of a material, where two surfaces meet at an angle other than 90 degrees.
a sloping edge or surface, not at a right angle

Examples

The craftsman created a smooth chamfer along the edge of the shelf for a neater look.
You should use a file to make a slight chamfer on the metal before assembly.
This tool allows you to cut a precise chamfer on wooden boards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bevel - A slope from the horizontal or vertical in carpentry or stonework.

Etymology

From French 'chanfrein', from 'chanfreiner' (to bevel).

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃæmfər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːmfə / (BrE)
to cut a sloping edge or groove along the edge or corner of a material, such as wood, stone, or metal
cut a sloping edge on a material

Examples

The carpenter carefully chamfered the edges of the table for a smoother finish.
Stone columns are often chamfered to enhance their appearance and prevent sharp corners.
Before assembly, each metal piece must be chamfered according to the blueprints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bevel - to cut or shape at an angle other than 90 degrees

Etymology

From Old French *chanfrein* (beveled edge), from *chanfreiner*.

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃæm.fər / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.fə / (BrE)
to cut or shape (an edge or surface) with a beveled edge, typically at a 45-degree angle
cut or shape an edge to a bevel

Examples

Make sure to chamfer the edges to prevent splintering.
The craftsman carefully chamfered the corners for a smoother finish.
You should chamfer the ends of the pipe before assembling them.
