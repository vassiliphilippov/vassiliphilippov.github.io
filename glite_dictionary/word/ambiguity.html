ambiguity

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbɪˈɡjuːəti / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪˈɡjuːəti / (BrE)
The quality of being open to more than one interpretation; uncertainty or inexactness in meaning.
uncertainty or double meaning

Examples

There is a lot of ambiguity in the new government policy, which makes it difficult to implement.
Literary critics often debate the ambiguity in modernist poetry.
Her answer was full of ambiguity, leaving everyone confused about her true intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivocation - the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin ambiguitas (double meaning, doubt), from ambiguus (uncertain).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæmbɪˈɡjuːəti / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪˈɡjuːɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being open to more than one interpretation; an expression or statement whose meaning is not clear or is uncertain, often because it can be understood in more than one way
uncertainty or double meaning in language

Examples

The ambiguity of his answer left everyone confused about his true intentions.
Legal documents are often rewritten to avoid any ambiguity in interpretation.
She enjoys the ambiguity in poetry, where multiple meanings can coexist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being uncertain
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'ambiguitas', from 'ambiguus' meaning 'doubtful'.
