scummer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌmə / (BrE)
an insult for a person considered dishonest, contemptible, or of low character
insult for a contemptible person

Examples

He was called a scummer after he lied about his intentions.
Many fans consider their rivals to be nothing but scummers.
She shouted at him, "You're a scummer for betraying your friends!"

Synset Examples

She called him a scummer for cheating on the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scum - a contemptible or worthless person
  • Synonym: villain - a person who does evil things
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for noble qualities

Etymology

From 'scum' (referring to something vile or worthless) plus the agentive suffix '-er'.
