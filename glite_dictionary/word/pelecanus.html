Pelecanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Pelecanidae, comprising large water birds known as pelicans.
genus of pelicans

Examples

The genus Pelecanus includes all living species of pelicans worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new fossil species belonging to the Pelecanus genus.
Members of the Pelecanus genus are known for their distinctive throat pouches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelican - large water bird with a long beak and pouch

Etymology

From Latin 'pelecanus', from Ancient Greek 'πελεκανός' (pelekanós, “pelican”).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Pelecanidae, which includes pelicans
type genus of Pelecanidae (pelicans)

Examples

The genus Pelecanus contains all modern species of pelican found worldwide.
Scientists debated whether to classify the brown pelican as part of the genus Pelecanus.
Specimens of Pelecanus have been discovered in ancient fossil deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelican - large water bird with a distinctive throat pouch

Etymology

From Latin *pelecanus* (pelican), from Ancient Greek *pelekan*.
