green-blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌblaɪnd.nɪs / (BrE)
A type of color blindness in which there is reduced sensitivity to green light, making it difficult to distinguish greens from purplish-reds.
inability to distinguish green shades
medical

Examples

Her green-blindness makes it hard for her to tell green apples from red ones.
Genetic testing revealed his green-blindness, explaining his trouble with certain traffic lights.
People with green-blindness often have difficulty choosing matching clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors
