dextrocardia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkstɹoʊˈkɑːrdiə / (AmE), / ˌdɛkstrəˈkɑːdɪə / (BrE)
an abnormal condition in which the heart is located on the right side of the chest instead of the left
heart located on right side of chest
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with dextrocardia, James had several tests to determine if he had any other organ abnormalities.
Doctors discovered her dextrocardia during a routine chest X-ray.
Although dextrocardia is rare, it may be associated with other congenital conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'dexter' (right) and Greek 'kardia' (heart).
