to sing

1. VERB

/ sɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪŋ / (BrE)
to produce musical sounds with the voice, especially with a set tune and words; to perform a song
produce musical sounds with the voice

Examples

She loves to sing in the shower every morning.
The birds sing beautifully at dawn each spring.
He sang his favorite song at the talent show.

Synset Examples

The nightingale was singing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chant - to sing or say something repeatedly in a monotonous tone
  • Antonym: talk - to speak

Etymology

Old English *singan* (to sing), of Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ sɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪŋ / (BrE)
(of objects) to make a whining, ringing, or high-pitched whistling sound.
emit a whining or ringing sound (of objects)

Examples

As the water reached boiling point, the kettle began to sing on the stove.
The bullet sang through the air, barely missing his head.
In the silent workshop, the saw sings as it slices through the wood.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistle - emit a high-pitched sound by blowing or moving rapidly
  • Degree Lower: hum - make a low continuous sound

Etymology

5. VERB

/ sɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪŋ / (BrE)
to disclose confidential or secret information, especially to authorities; to betray a secret
divulge secrets or confidential information
informal

Examples

The suspect finally decided to sing after hours of questioning by the police.
Nobody thought Rachel would sing, but she ended up confessing everything.
Under pressure, he was bound to sing about what really happened at the meeting.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spill the beans - reveal secret or confidential information
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - refrain from divulging information

Etymology

Figurative use from the literal meaning 'to produce musical tones with the voice', referring to revealing information freely.
