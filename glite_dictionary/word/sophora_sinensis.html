Sophora sinensis

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈfɔːrə saɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / səˈfɔːrə sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to China and Japan, notable for its rounded crown, dark green compound leaves, and numerous fragrant creamy-white flowers.
a tree native to China and Japan with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The Sophora sinensis produces beautiful clusters of creamy-white flowers each spring.
Botanists are studying the characteristics of Sophora sinensis in its native habitat.
Gardeners value Sophora sinensis for its elegant shape and fragrant blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Sophora' is the genus name derived from Arabic, and 'sinensis' means 'from China' in Latin.
