to prognosticate

1. VERB

/ prɑgˈnɑstəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / prɒgˈnɒstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to predict or foretell a future event, especially using present signs or indications
predict or foretell a future event

Examples

Many analysts attempt to prognosticate how the stock market will perform each year.
The gray clouds seemed to prognosticate the arrival of a heavy storm.
It is difficult to prognosticate the outcome of such a complex negotiation.

Synset Examples

Call the outcome of an election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to foresee - to know about something before it happens

Etymology

From Latin prognosticatus, past participle of prognosticare (to foretell), from Greek prognōstikos (foreknowing).

2. VERB

/ prɑgˈnɑstəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / prɒgˈnɒstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to indicate or predict a future event, especially as a sign or omen
predict or indicate by a sign or omen

Examples

Many experts attempt to prognosticate the outcome of the election based on early voting data.
Dark clouds on the horizon often prognosticate a coming storm.
The economic indicators prognosticate a period of slow growth ahead.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to foretell - to tell what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin prognosticatus, past participle of prognosticare (to foretell), from Greek prognōstikos (knowing beforehand).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
