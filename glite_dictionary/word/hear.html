to hear

1. VERB

/ hɪr / (AmE), / hɪə / (BrE)
to listen to someone or something, especially with attention or to consider what they say
listen and pay attention

Examples

You should hear both sides before making a decision.
We will hear from the witnesses at tomorrow’s meeting.
Let’s hear what Sarah has to say.

Synset Examples

Listen to your father
We must hear the expert before we make a decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to listen - to give one's attention to sound or to someone speaking

Etymology

From Old English *hieran*, *hȳran* (to hear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02175483-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hɪr / (AmE), / hɪə / (BrE)
(of a judge or court) to examine or consider (evidence or a case) in a formal judicial process.
consider a case in court
legal

Examples

The Supreme Court will hear the case next month.
The judge agreed to hear new evidence presented by the defense.
The tribunal will hear appeals from both sides before making a decision.

Synset Examples

The jury had heard all the evidence
The case will be tried in California

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to examine and judge a legal case in court
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02506551-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hɪr / (AmE), / hɪə / (BrE)
to become aware of information or learn about something, often by chance or indirectly
become aware of information

Examples

Did you hear that Jessica got a new job downtown?
I just heard about the upcoming changes to the schedule.
He often hears rumors long before anyone else.

Synset Examples

I learned that she has two grown-up children
I see that you have been promoted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to learn - to gain knowledge or information about something
  • Degree Lower: to overhear - to hear information not intended for you

Etymology

From Old English *hieran*, *heran* (to hear); related to German *hören*, Dutch *horen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00600349-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ hɪr / (AmE), / hɪə / (BrE)
to perceive sound with the ears
perceive sound with the ears

Examples

Can you hear the birds singing outside the window?
She couldn't hear what he was saying because of the loud music.
I heard a strange noise coming from the basement last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to listen - to give attention in order to hear
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately not pay attention (to sound, information, etc.)

Etymology

From Old English *hieran* or *hȳran* (to hear, listen), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02174146-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ hɪr / (AmE), / hɪə / (BrE)
to receive communication from someone, especially by letter, phone, or other means
receive communication from someone

Examples

We were relieved to finally hear from her after so many months.
Let me know if you hear anything from the company about your application.
It's been years since I've heard from my childhood friend.

Synset Examples

We heard nothing from our son for five years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hear from - to receive a communication from (someone)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02111896-v
