misogynic

1. ADJ

/ mɪˌsɑːˈdʒɪnɪk / (AmE), / mɪˌsɒˈdʒɪnɪk / (BrE)
(Of a person, especially a man) having a deep-seated distrust or hatred of women.
having a deep distrust or hatred of women

Examples

The author's misogynic remarks sparked outrage among readers and critics alike.
He was accused of holding deeply misogynic views due to his persistent distrust of female colleagues.
Her research explores how misogynic attitudes have shaped social policies throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misogynistic - showing or characterized by hatred of women
  • Synonym: misogyny - hatred or prejudice against women
  • Antonym: philogynous - having a fondness or love for women

Etymology

From Greek 'misos' (hatred) + 'gynē' (woman).
