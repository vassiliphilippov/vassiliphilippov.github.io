Gasherbrum

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡæʃərˌbrum / (AmE), / ˈɡæʃəbruːm / (BrE)
a mountain massif in the Karakoram range of northern Kashmir, known for several high peaks including Gasherbrum I and II, with the highest reaching 8,068 meters (26,470 feet)
mountain massif in the Karakoram, Kashmir

Examples

The climbers set out to conquer Gasherbrum despite challenging weather conditions.
Gasherbrum is renowned among mountaineers for its breathtaking scenery and technical difficulty.
After months of preparation, the team reached the summit of Gasherbrum in late July.

Etymology

From Balti, meaning 'beautiful mountain' or 'shining wall'.
