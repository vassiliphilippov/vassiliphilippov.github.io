peacemaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who tries to create or maintain peace, especially by settling conflicts or disputes
person who seeks to bring about peace

Examples

As a peacemaker, she negotiated a truce between the two neighboring communities.
The school counselor often acted as a peacemaker among students after arguments.
He was widely respected for his role as a peacemaker during the labor dispute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - person who helps others resolve disputes
  • Antonym: agitator - person who stirs up conflict or trouble

Etymology

Formed from 'peace' + 'maker'; first used in English in the 16th century.
