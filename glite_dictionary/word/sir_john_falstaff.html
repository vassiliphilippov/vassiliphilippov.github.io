Sir John Falstaff

1. PROPN

/ sɝ dʒɑn ˈfælstæf / (AmE), / sə dʒɒn ˈfɔːlstɑːf / (BrE)
a fat, witty, and dissolute character appearing in several plays by Shakespeare, known for his boastful and comical behavior
boastful, witty character in Shakespeare's plays

Examples

He laughed heartily, reminding everyone of Sir John Falstaff in both girth and wit.
Many critics consider Sir John Falstaff the greatest comic character in English literature.
The actor delivered a memorable performance as Sir John Falstaff in the production of Henry IV.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Falstaffian - resembling or characteristic of Falstaff, especially in being jovial or dissolute

Etymology

Name of a character created by William Shakespeare, first appearing in 'Henry IV, Part 1' (1597).
