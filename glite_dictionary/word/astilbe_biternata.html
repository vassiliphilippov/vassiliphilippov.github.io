Astilbe biternata

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪlbi baɪtərˈneɪtə / (AmE), / əˈstɪlbi baɪtɜːˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A North American species of astilbe with panicles of creamy white flowers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
North American astilbe plant species

Examples

The woodland garden features several clusters of Astilbe biternata blooming in late spring.
Gardeners appreciate Astilbe biternata for its delicate, creamy white flowers and elegant foliage.
Botanists identified the plant as Astilbe biternata due to its distinctive flower panicles and native habitat.

Etymology

Astilbe from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'stilbos' (shining), referring to the dull leaves; 'biternata' from Latin, meaning 'twice-ternately divided'.
