unseen

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsin / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːn / (BrE)
not seen, noticed, or perceived; not visible or detected.
not seen or noticed

Examples

The thief managed to escape unseen into the night.
There are many unseen forces at work in the natural world.
She left the room quietly, hoping to remain unseen by anyone.

Synset Examples

unseen natural resources

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen
  • Synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; concealed

Etymology

From un- (not) + seen, past participle of see.

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsin / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːn / (BrE)
not noticed, observed, or perceived; not seen by anyone
not noticed or observed

Examples

The thief made his way through the house unseen by the sleeping family.
Strange, unseen forces seemed to be guiding their decisions.
She wanted her kindness to remain unseen and anonymous.

Synset Examples

managed to slip away unobserved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invisible - unable to be seen; not visible to the eye
  • Synonym: unnoticed - not observed or remarked on
  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen

Etymology

From un- (not) + seen; see and un- from Old English.
