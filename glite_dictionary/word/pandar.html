pandar

1. NOUN

/ ˈpændɑːr / (AmE), / ˈpændɑː / (BrE)
a person who procures clients for prostitutes; a pimp, especially in historical or literary contexts.
procurer for prostitutes (historical/literary)
archaic

Examples

In the old play, the pandar arranges secret meetings between the lovers.
He was scorned as a pandar by those who knew of his dealings.
The poet satirized the city's elite, calling them little better than a pandar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pimp - a person who procures clients for prostitutes, typically controlling them
  • Synonym: ponce - British English term for a pimp

Etymology

From Middle English 'pander', after Pandarus, a character in Chaucer and Shakespeare who facilitates a lover's affair.
