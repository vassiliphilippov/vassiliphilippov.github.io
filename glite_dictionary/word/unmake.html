to unmake

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈmeɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmeɪk / (BrE)
to deprive (something) of its existing characteristics or qualities; to reverse or undo the making of something
deprive of certain characteristics

Examples

Recent revelations may unmake his reputation as an honest politician.
It is far easier to unmake a plan than to create one from scratch.
The crisis threatened to unmake the fragile peace that had been established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undo - to reverse or annul an action or effect

Etymology

From un- (prefix indicating reversal) + make (verb).
