to crenellate

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɛnəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɹɛn.ɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to provide (a wall, building, etc.) with battlements or a notched parapet for defensive purposes
provide with battlements

Examples

The castle walls were crenellated to allow archers to defend the stronghold.
During the renovation, they decided to crenellate the roofline for historical accuracy.
Many medieval cities chose to crenellate their gates for added protection against attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *crenellare*, from *crenella* (notch).
