Crassostrea

1. NOUN

/ kræsəˈstriə / (AmE), / kræsɒsˈtriːə / (BrE)
A genus of oysters, including commercially important species such as the Virginia oyster.
genus of oysters

Examples

The eastern oyster, known scientifically as Crassostrea virginica, is native to the Atlantic coast of North America.
Researchers are studying the population decline of Crassostrea species due to environmental changes.
Many coastal communities depend on Crassostrea for both economic and ecological reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - A bivalve mollusk found in marine or brackish waters

Etymology

From Latin 'crassus' (thick, fat) and Greek 'ostreon' (oyster).
