fiber-optic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfaɪbər ˈɑptɪk / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbə ˈɒptɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using thin flexible fibers of glass or plastic to transmit light signals, especially for telecommunications or medical instruments.
relating to fiber optic technology

Examples

The hospital upgraded its network to a fiber-optic system for faster data transmission.
Engineers installed fiber-optic cables throughout the campus for high-speed internet access.
The procedure was performed using a fiber-optic camera that provided clear internal images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optical fiber - a thin flexible fiber of glass or plastic used to transmit light signals

Etymology

Formed from 'fiber' and 'optic', referring to the technology of transmitting information as light through fibers.
