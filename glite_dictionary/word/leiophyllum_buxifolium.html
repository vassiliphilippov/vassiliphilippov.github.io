Leiophyllum buxifolium

1. NOUN

/ leɪ.ˈaɪ.əˌfɪl.əm ˌbʌk.sɪˈfoʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / leɪ.ˈaɪ.əˌfɪl.əm ˌbʌk.sɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States, valued for its white star-shaped flowers and glossy leaves
a low evergreen shrub with star-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden featured several Leiophyllum buxifolium near the front entrance.
Horticulturists admire Leiophyllum buxifolium for its glossy foliage and delicate white flowers.
Wild populations of Leiophyllum buxifolium are important for local ecosystems in the southeastern United States.

Etymology

From Greek leios (smooth) + phyllon (leaf); Latin buxifolium (boxwood-leaved).
