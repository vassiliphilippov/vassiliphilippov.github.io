fork

1. NOUN

/ fɔrk / (AmE), / fɔːk / (BrE)
an act or instance of branching or dividing into two or more parts or directions
the act of branching or dividing

Examples

A river forms a wide fork as it splits around the island.
There is a fork in the road just past the old church.
The discussion took a fork when new evidence was introduced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a division or subdivision of the stem or axis of a tree, shrub, or other plant

Etymology

From Old English 'forc', from Latin 'furca' (pitchfork, forked instrument).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ fɔrk / (AmE), / fɔːk / (BrE)
(in chess) to attack two or more enemy pieces at the same time with one move.

Examples

If you aren't careful, your opponent might fork your two major pieces.
