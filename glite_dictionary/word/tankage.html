tankage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋ.kɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtaŋ.kɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process or act of storing a substance in tanks, especially large containers for liquids such as oil or chemicals
storage of substances in tanks

Examples

The company increased its tankage capacity to handle more crude oil.
Proper management of tankage is vital to prevent leaks and contamination.
The port's tankage facilities allow for the storage of various chemicals before distribution.

Etymology

Formed from 'tank' + '-age' (denoting action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee or charge for storing goods or liquids in tanks
fee for storage in tanks

Examples

The company paid tankage for the storage of crude oil at the port facility.
After calculating the tankage, the total cost of storing chemicals became clear.
Our contract includes the tankage for all liquid materials held over thirty days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage fee - a charge for storing goods in a facility

Etymology

From 'tank' + '-age', a suffix forming nouns indicating associated action or fee.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the quantity contained in or the storage capacity of a tank or tanks, especially for liquids such as oil or chemicals
quantity or capacity of a tank or tanks

Examples

The oil company's tankage was increased to store the extra crude arriving at the port.
Due to limited tankage, the facility could not accept additional chemical shipments this month.
They calculated the tankage required to ensure the plant could operate without interruptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacity - the maximum amount that something can contain
  • Synonym: volume - the amount of space that a substance or object occupies

Etymology

From 'tank' + '-age', a suffix forming nouns meaning a related action or result.
