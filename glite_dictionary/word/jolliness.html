jolliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒl.i.nəs / (BrE)
a cheerful and lively feeling; the quality of being full of good humor and joy
a feeling of cheerfulness and good humor

Examples

Her jolliness made everyone at the party feel welcome and at ease.
Despite the bad weather, his jolliness never diminished during the entire trip.
You can't help but be infected by the jolliness of the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloominess - a feeling of darkness or depression

Etymology

Formed from 'jolly' (perhaps from Old French 'jolif', festive or merry) plus the suffix '-ness'.
