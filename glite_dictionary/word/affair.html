affair

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛr / (AmE), / əˈfeə / (BrE)
a sexual relationship, often secret or illicit, especially when at least one partner is married to someone else
secret or illicit sexual relationship

Examples

Rumors spread quickly when news of their affair became public.
She ended her marriage after discovering her husband's secret affair.
The novel centers around an affair between two colleagues at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relationship - a romantic or sexual involvement with someone
  • Degree Lower: fling - a short, casual sexual relationship

Etymology

From Old French 'afaire', from Latin 'ad' (to) + 'facere' (to do).

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛr / (AmE), / əˈfeə / (BrE)
a formal or official event, gathering, or ceremony, especially a social one
formal or official event; social gathering

Examples

The awards dinner was quite an affair, with speeches and dancing lasting all night.
Their wedding was a lavish affair held at the town's grandest hotel.
The committee organized a fundraising affair to support local charities.

Synset Examples

the party was quite an affair
an occasion arranged to honor the president
a seemingly endless round of social functions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - a planned public or social occasion
  • Synonym: occasion - a particular time when something happens, especially a social event

3. NOUN

/ əˈfɛr / (AmE), / əˈfeə / (BrE)
A matter or concern, especially one that is unspecified or of little detail.
a matter or concern

Examples

She assured him that the whole affair was under control and nothing would go wrong.
The meeting was a private affair, so only a select few attended.
He didn't want to get involved in an affair that didn't concern him.

Synset Examples

several matters to attend to
it is none of your affair
things are going well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matter - a subject or situation under consideration
  • Synonym: business - a person's concern or responsibility
  • Synonym: concern - a matter of interest or importance

Etymology

From Old French 'afaire', from 'à faire' (to do); related to Latin 'facere' (to do, make).
