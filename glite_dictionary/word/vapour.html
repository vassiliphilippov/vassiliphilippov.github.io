vapour

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.pə / (BrE)
The process of becoming a gas, especially from a liquid or solid state; the action or state of vaporizing.
process of becoming gas/vapor
british

Examples

After the rain, the sun quickly turned the puddles into vapour.
The process of boiling involves the formation of vapour from liquid water.
Fog is formed when water vapour condenses in the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vapor - process of becoming a gas (American spelling)
  • Antonym: condensation - process of a gas becoming a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, heat, exhalation).

2. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpər / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə / (BrE)
A visible suspension of fine particles of liquid or solid in the air, such as mist or smoke.
suspension of particles in air
british

Examples

In the early morning, a thin vapour rose from the surface of the lake.
The chemist demonstrated how the substance turned into vapour when heated.
You could see the vapour escaping from the kettle as it boiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vapor - Visible suspension in air (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, exhalation).
