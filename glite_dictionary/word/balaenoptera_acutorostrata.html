Balaenoptera acutorostrata

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnəpˈtɛrə ˌækjətəˈrɑstrətə / (AmE), / bəˌliːnəpˈtɛərə ˌækjətɒˈrɒstrətə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the minke whale, a small species of baleen whale found in coastal waters of the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
scientific name for the minke whale

Examples

Scientists recently studied the migration patterns of Balaenoptera acutorostrata in the North Atlantic.
The Balaenoptera acutorostrata is commonly known as the minke whale among marine biologists.
Threats to Balaenoptera acutorostrata populations include climate change and commercial fishing activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minke whale - common name for Balaenoptera acutorostrata, a small baleen whale

Etymology

From Latin: 'balaena' (whale) and 'ptera' (fin or wing); 'acutorostrata' meaning 'sharp-snouted'.
