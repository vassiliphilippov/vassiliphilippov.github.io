Delonix

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈloʊnɪks / (AmE), / dɪˈləʊnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen or deciduous trees native to tropical Africa and India, noted for their flamboyant flowers.
genus of tropical trees

Examples

The Delonix trees in full bloom add vibrant color to the city park.
Botanists often study the unique flowering pattern of Delonix species.
A Delonix is commonly planted as an ornamental tree in tropical climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'delos' (visible) and 'onyx' (claw), referring to the shape of the petals.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈloʊ.nɪks / (AmE), / dɪˈləʊ.nɪks / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen or deciduous trees native to tropical Africa and India, including the flame tree.
genus of tropical trees (flame tree, etc.)

Examples

The Delonix genus is well known for the striking flowers of the flame tree.
Several species in the Delonix genus are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
Botanists recently discovered a new Delonix species in Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flame tree - a common name for Delonix regia, known for its bright red flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'delos' (evident) and 'onyx' (claw), referring to the shape of the flower's petal.
