dacoit

1. NOUN

/ dəˈkɔɪt / (AmE), / dəˈkɔɪt / (BrE)
a member of an armed gang of robbers, especially in India or South Asia
member of armed robbers gang (esp. South Asia)

Examples

The notorious dacoit was finally captured by the police after months on the run.
Villages in the region lived in fear of dacoits raiding their homes at night.
Legend has it that he once confronted a dacoit single-handedly and saved the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandit - an outlaw who robs people, often by force
  • Synonym: robber - a person who commits robbery

Etymology

From Hindi ḍākait, from Sanskrit ḍākā (bandit, robber).
