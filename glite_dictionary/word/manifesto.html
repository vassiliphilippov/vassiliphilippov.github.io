manifesto

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænəˈfɛstoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈfɛstəʊ / (BrE)
A public declaration of the intentions, motives, or views of an individual or group, especially a political party or government.
public declaration of intentions

Examples

The political party released its manifesto ahead of the election, outlining key policy proposals.
Artists often publish a manifesto to define the principles of a new movement.
His manifesto called for radical economic and social reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proclamation - an official public announcement
  • Synonym: declaration - a formal or explicit statement or announcement
  • Antonym: retraction - the withdrawal of a statement, opposite of declaration

Etymology

From Italian 'manifesto,' from Latin 'manifestus' (obvious, clear).
