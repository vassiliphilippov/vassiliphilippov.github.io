batwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætˌwʊm.ən / (AmE), / ˈbætwʊm.ən / (BrE)
(historical, British) A female orderly assigned to serve a British military officer, responsible for personal and domestic duties.
female military orderly (British, historical)
british archaic

Examples

During World War II, the officer relied heavily on his batwoman for daily assistance.
The duties of a batwoman included managing laundry and preparing uniforms.
As a batwoman, she ensured the captain's quarters were always kept impeccably tidy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batman - male orderly assigned to serve a British officer

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'batman' (military orderly + -woman).
