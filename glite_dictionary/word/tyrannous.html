tyrannous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪrənəs / (AmE), / ˈtɪərənəs / (BrE)
Showing unjustly severe, cruel, or arbitrary use of power; oppressive or dictatorial.
oppressively cruel or severe

Examples

The tyrannous rule of the dictator left the citizens in constant fear.
Reformers fought against the tyrannous laws imposed by the regime.
His tyrannous attitude alienated even his closest allies.

Synset Examples

the oppressive government
oppressive laws
a tyrannical parent
tyrannous disregard of human rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrannical - exercising power in a cruel or arbitrary way
  • Synonym: oppressive - unjustly harsh or tyrannical
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly

Etymology

From Old French *tyrannous*, from Latin *tyrannus* (tyrant).
