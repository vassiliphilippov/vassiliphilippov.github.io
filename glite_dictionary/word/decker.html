decker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkə / (BrE)
(often used in compounds) something constructed with a specified number of levels or decks.
thing with multiple levels (esp. in compounds)

Examples

The old hotel was a famous three-decker overlooking the seaside boulevard.
We rode the city in a red double-decker for the full tour experience.
The new parking garage is a multi-decker structure with plenty of space.

Synset Examples

they rode in a double-decker bus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double-decker - a vehicle or object with two levels or decks
  • Degree Higher: triple-decker - a thing with three levels or decks

Etymology

From 'deck' meaning a floor or level, plus the suffix '-er', indicating something that has decks.
