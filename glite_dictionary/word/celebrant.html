celebrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.ə.brənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ɪ.brənt / (BrE)
a person who is celebrating, especially at a ceremony or festivity
person participating in a celebration

Examples

As the celebrant blew out the candles, everyone applauded and cheered.
The celebrant wore a bright sash to mark her special day.
Dozens of celebrants gathered in the park for the annual festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrator - a person who celebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'celebrant-', present participle of 'celebrare' (to celebrate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.ə.brənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ɪ.brənt / (BrE)
a priest or minister who officiates at the celebration of the Eucharist or another religious ceremony
officiating priest or minister at a religious ceremony

Examples

The celebrant raised the chalice and offered prayers for the congregation.
Only the celebrant is permitted to give the final blessing during the Mass.
The celebrant led the procession into the church at the start of the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: officiant - a person who officiates at a religious ceremony

Etymology

From Latin celebrant-, celebrans, present participle of celebrare (to celebrate).
