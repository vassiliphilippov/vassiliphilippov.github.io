pastel

1. ADJ

/ pæˈstɛl / (AmE), / pæˈstɛl / (BrE)
delicate and pale in color, especially used to describe colors that are soft and light rather than bright or dark.
delicate, pale in color

Examples

The nursery was decorated in pastel shades of blue and green.
She wore a beautiful pastel yellow dress to the summer party.
Artists often use pastel colors for soft, dreamy landscapes.

Synset Examples

pastel pink

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vivid - strong and intense in color

Etymology

From French 'pastel', referring to a crayon made from powdered pigments, originally from Italian 'pastello'.

2. NOUN

/ pæˈstɛl / (AmE), / pæˈstɛl / (BrE)
a soft, pale color; any of various light shades, especially as used in art, fashion, or design
a soft, pale color

Examples

She prefers decorating her room with pastel shades for a calming effect.
The artist used soft pastel colors to create a delicate landscape.
Spring fashion collections often feature clothes in cheerful pastel tones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vivid - having strong, bright colors
  • Synonym: pale - light in color or shade; lacking intensity or brightness

Etymology

From French 'pastel' (crayon made of powdered pigment)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04981842-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ pæˈstɛl / (AmE), / pæˈstɛl / (BrE)
(of a quality, style, or effect) lacking in strength, body, or vigor; pale or subdued in impact.
lacking in strength or vigor

Examples

The artist's new collection has a distinctly pastel feel, more serene than striking.
Her performance was criticized for being too pastel, lacking the energy the role demanded.
The novel evokes a pastel emotional landscape, gentle and understated rather than intense.

Synset Examples

faded pastel charms of the naive music

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vivid - having strong, striking, or intense impact

Etymology

From French 'pastel' (pastel; crayon), ultimately from Late Latin 'pastellus'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
