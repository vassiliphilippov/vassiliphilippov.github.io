Acer glabrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ˈɡleɪbrəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə ˈɡleɪbrəm / (BrE)
A species of small maple tree native to northwestern North America, also known as the Rocky Mountain maple.
a small maple tree native to northwestern North America

Examples

The Acer glabrum thrives in the mountainous regions of the western United States.
In autumn, the leaves of Acer glabrum turn brilliant shades of yellow and orange.
Botanists identified the tree as Acer glabrum during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a deciduous tree with lobed leaves, including many species

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Acer' meaning 'maple' and 'glabrum' meaning 'smooth' or 'hairless'.
