paraphilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈfɪl.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈfɪl.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by atypical or extreme sexual interests or behaviors considered outside of societal norms, often involving unusual objects, activities, or situations.
atypical sexual interest or behavior
medical

Examples

The psychologist explained that paraphilia can manifest in many different ways, some of which may require treatment.
Research about the origins of paraphilia remains a topic of debate among mental health professionals.
Individuals with a paraphilia may feel isolated or reluctant to seek help due to social stigma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetishism - sexual attraction to objects or body parts not typically viewed as sexual

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside, beyond) and 'philia' (love, attraction).
