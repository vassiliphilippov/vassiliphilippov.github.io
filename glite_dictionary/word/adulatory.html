adulatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈædʒəˌlɛtəri / (AmE), / əˈdjuːlətəri / (BrE)
excessively praising or flattering; showing or expressing insincere or obsequious admiration
excessively flattering or admiring

Examples

His adulatory remarks towards his boss seemed insincere to everyone in the room.
The author's adulatory preface praised the critics in glowing terms.
She was uncomfortable with the adulatory attention she received after her performance.

Synset Examples

they listened with flattering interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattering - giving praise or compliments, sometimes insincerely
  • Antonym: critical - expressing disapproval or judgement, opposite of complimentary or adulatory

Etymology

From Latin 'adulaturus', future participle of 'adulari' (to flatter).
