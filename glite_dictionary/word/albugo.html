Albugo

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjuːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Albuginaceae that cause white rust diseases in plants.
genus of fungi causing white rusts

Examples

Albugo infections are commonly known as white rusts, affecting a variety of cultivated plants.
The presence of Albugo on the leaves caused distinctive white pustules on the surface.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Albugo to develop better strategies for managing plant diseases.

Etymology

From Latin 'albugo' meaning 'whiteness' or 'white spot'.
