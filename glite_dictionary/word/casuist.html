casuist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuˌɪst / (AmE), / ˈkazjʊɪst / (BrE)
a person who uses careful but often misleading or overly subtle reasoning, especially in the analysis of moral problems
person using subtle, often specious reasoning

Examples

The seasoned casuist found a loophole in every regulation put before him.
She accused the politician of being a casuist who twists arguments to suit his goals.
History remembers him as a casuist skilled in the art of moral justification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sophist - a person who uses clever but fallacious reasoning

Etymology

From French 'casuiste', from Spanish 'casuista', from Latin 'casus' (case, occurrence).
