author

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔθɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːθə / (BrE)
A person or thing that originates, causes, or initiates something.
originator or initiator of something

Examples

The CEO was the author of the new company policy that stirred debate.
He is seen as the author of the recent changes in the industry.
The scientist became the author of a breakthrough that transformed medical research.

Synset Examples

he was the generator of several complaints

Related Terms

  • Synonym: originator - a person who creates or initiates something
  • Synonym: source - the place, person, or thing from which something originates

Etymology

From Latin auctor (originator, creator).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10145714-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɔθɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːθə / (BrE)
to be the creator or writer of a book, article, play, or other written work
write or create (a written work)

Examples

She authored this groundbreaking novel in just six months.
The professor has authored several influential papers on climate change.
He hopes to author a book about his experiences in the Arctic.

Synset Examples

She authored this play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write - compose or produce written material
  • Synonym: to compose - create a literary, musical, or artistic work

Etymology

From Latin 'auctor' (originator, creator) via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01708311-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔθɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːθə / (BrE)
A person who writes books, articles, or other written works, especially as a profession.
person who writes books or other works

Examples

The author of the novel won a prestigious literary prize last year.
She became a bestselling author after her first book was published.
Each author brings a unique perspective to their chosen subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writer - person who writes books or articles or stories

Etymology

From Latin 'auctor' (originator, promoter), from 'augere' (to increase).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10813654-n
Synset POS: n
