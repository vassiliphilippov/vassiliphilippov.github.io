pravastatin

1. NOUN

/ ˌprævəˈstætɪn / (AmE), / ˌprævəˈstætɪn / (BrE)
a drug used to reduce levels of cholesterol and other fatty substances in the blood, often prescribed after heart attacks
cholesterol-lowering drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed pravastatin to help lower her cholesterol after the cardiac event.
Many patients tolerate pravastatin well with few side effects.
He researched the benefits and risks of taking pravastatin before starting the medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atorvastatin - a drug for reducing cholesterol, also a statin
  • Synonym: simvastatin - a cholesterol-lowering statin drug

Etymology

From the prefix 'prava-' (referring to its chemical structure) and 'statin' (a class of cholesterol-lowering drugs).
