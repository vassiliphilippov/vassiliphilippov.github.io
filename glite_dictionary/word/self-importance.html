self-importance

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ɪmˈpɔrtəns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪmˈpɔːtəns / (BrE)
an exaggerated sense of one's own significance or worth, often with feelings of superiority over others
exaggerated sense of one's importance

Examples

Her constant self-importance made it difficult for others to work with her.
He spoke with such self-importance that everyone in the room felt uncomfortable.
The manager’s self-importance was obvious in the way he dismissed others’ opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotism - excessive sense of self-importance or self-centeredness
  • Antonym: modesty - lack of self-importance or pretentiousness

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'importance', both from Middle English roots.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.ɪmˈpɔɹ.təns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.ɪmˈpɔː.təns / (BrE)
An exaggerated sense of one's own value or significance; having an excessively high opinion of oneself.
exaggerated opinion of one's own importance

Examples

His self-importance made him unpopular with his colleagues.
She carried herself with such self-importance that people found her difficult to approach.
The manager's self-importance was obvious in every meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - an attitude of superiority manifested in an overbearing manner
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being unassuming or moderate in the estimation of one's abilities

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'importance', in use since the 18th century.
