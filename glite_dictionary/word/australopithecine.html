australopithecine

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːstrələˈpɪθəˌsin / (AmE), / ˌɒstrələˈpɪθəsiːn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the extinct hominid genus Australopithecus
relating to the genus Australopithecus

Examples

The australopithecine fossils were discovered in South Africa in the early twentieth century.
Scientists have studied australopithecine bones to learn more about early human evolution.
This site yielded several australopithecine skulls, helping to clarify our ancestral tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus - the extinct hominid genus to which australopithecine refers

Etymology

From Latin 'australis' (southern) and Greek 'pithekos' (ape), via scientific Latin 'Australopithecus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔstrələˈpɪθɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˌɒstrələˈpɪθɪˌsiːn / (BrE)
an extinct member of the genus Australopithecus, a group of bipedal primates that lived in Africa from about 4 to 1 million years ago and are among the earliest known hominids
extinct early humanlike bipedal primate
medical

Examples

Fossil evidence suggests that australopithecines were among the first hominids to walk upright.
Paleoanthropologists have discovered several skulls belonging to australopithecine species in East Africa.
The brain of an australopithecine was significantly smaller than that of modern humans, but it showed signs of development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus - genus of extinct early hominids

Etymology

From Latin 'australis' (southern) + Greek 'pithēkos' (ape), referring to 'southern ape.'
