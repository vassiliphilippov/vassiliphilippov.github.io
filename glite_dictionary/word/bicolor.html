bicolor

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌkʌlə / (BrE)
having two colors or marked by two distinct colors.
having two colors

Examples

The company's new logo features a bicolor design of blue and orange.
She admired the bicolor roses blooming in the garden.
The artist wore a striking bicolor scarf during the interview.

Synset Examples

a bicolor flower
a bicolored postage stamp

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tricolor - having three colors

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + color (color).
