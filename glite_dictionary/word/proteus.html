Proteus

1. PROPN

/ ˈproʊtiəs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a prophetic sea god who served Poseidon and had the ability to change his shape at will.
mythological sea god capable of changing shape

Examples

In the myth, Proteus could evade capture by transforming into various animals and elements.
The poet referenced Proteus as a symbol of constant change and adaptability.
Scholars often discuss the elusive nature of Proteus when describing shapeshifting characters.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πρωτεύς (Prōteus), name of a sea god.

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiəs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiəs / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic salamanders, the type genus of the family Proteidae.
genus of aquatic salamanders

Examples

The olm, a species within the genus Proteus, is found in subterranean waters of the Dinaric Alps.
Researchers studied the adaptations of Proteus to its unique cave environment.
Some species in the Proteus genus are completely blind due to their subterranean habitat.

Etymology

From Greek 'Prōteus', name of a sea god able to change shape, from the animal's changing or elusive nature.
