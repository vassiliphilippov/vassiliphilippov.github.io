tomograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.məˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈtɒ.məˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A machine that uses X-rays and a computer to generate a detailed image of a specific slice or plane through an object, commonly used in medical imaging.
X-ray imaging device for planar sections
medical

Examples

The hospital recently acquired a new tomograph to improve the accuracy of its diagnostic procedures.
A tomograph can create cross-sectional images of organs that help doctors detect abnormalities.
Using a tomograph, specialists were able to identify the tumor's exact location within the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CT scanner - machine that uses computed tomography for imaging
  • Antonym: MRI scanner - machine that uses magnetic resonance imaging for slice images

Etymology

From Greek 'tomos' (a slice, section) + '-graph' (something written or drawn).
