canty

1. ADJ

/ ˈkænti / (AmE), / ˈkanti / (BrE)
lively, cheerful, and brisk; full of energy, often used in Scottish and northern English dialects.
lively and cheerful (dialect)
archaic

Examples

Her canty manner brightened up the entire room.
The canty children danced around the bonfire, laughing and singing.
Despite his age, he kept a canty pace on his morning walks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: dull - lacking energy or liveliness

Etymology

Scots origin, from Old Norse 'kǫttr' (cat), referring to being lively as a playful cat.
