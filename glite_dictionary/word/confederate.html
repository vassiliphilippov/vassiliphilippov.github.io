Confederate

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɛdərət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət / (BrE)
relating to the southern states that seceded to form the Confederacy during the American Civil War (1861–1865)
of the southern Confederacy in the American Civil War

Examples

Many Confederate flags are displayed in museums illustrating Civil War history.
The Confederate army was ultimately defeated by Union forces in 1865.
He wore a replica Confederate uniform to the reenactment event.

Synset Examples

Confederate soldiers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union - relating to the northern states during the American Civil War

Etymology

From Latin 'confoederatus' (allied), via Late Middle English 'confederate' (joined by treaty).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdəɹət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd(ə)ɹət / (BrE)
a supporter of the Confederate States of America, especially during the American Civil War (1861–65)
supporter of the Confederate States (Civil War)

Examples

During the Civil War, many Confederate soldiers fought bravely for their cause.
A monument in the town square honors the memory of Confederate veterans.
He discovered that his great-great-grandfather had been a Confederate during the 1860s.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Unionist - a supporter of the Union (northern states) during the American Civil War

Etymology

From Latin 'confederatus' (allied together), via French 'confédéré', meaning 'joined by a treaty'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09973094-n
Synset POS: n
