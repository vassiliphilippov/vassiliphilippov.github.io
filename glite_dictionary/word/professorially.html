professorially

1. ADV

/ prəˌfɛˈsɔriəli / (AmE), / prəˌfɛˈsɔːriəli / (BrE)
In a manner characteristic of a professor, especially in being scholarly, didactic, or authoritative.
in the style of a professor

Examples

The new manager spoke professorially at every meeting, explaining each point in detail.
She corrected our essays professorially, offering thorough feedback as if she were teaching a class.
He answered the interview questions professorially, using precise language and referencing scholarly sources.

Synset Examples

she behaved very professorially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: didactically - in a didactic or instructive manner
