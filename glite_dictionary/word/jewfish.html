jewfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large marine food fish found in Australian waters, closely related to the maigre, prized for its size and value as seafood
large marine food fish of Australia

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious fillet of jewfish for dinner last night.
Fishermen in northern Australia often hope to catch a jewfish because of its impressive size.
The jewfish is almost indistinguishable from the maigre in appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maigre - a large marine fish related to the jewfish
  • Synonym: grouper - a large family of fish including the jewfish

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, dark-colored grouper with a thick head and rough scales, found in warm seas
large dark grouper fish

Examples

The fisherman was thrilled to catch a massive jewfish on his trip to Florida.
In some areas, the jewfish is a protected species due to overfishing.
Divers often encounter the imposing jewfish among the coral reefs.
