soy

1. NOUN

/ sɔɪ / (AmE), / sɔɪ / (BrE)
an Asian plant (Glycine max) with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, cultivated for its edible seeds, oil, and forage
Asian plant grown for edible seeds and oil

Examples

The farmer planted soy in the fields to improve the soil and harvest nutritious seeds.
Asian cuisines often use soy products as a staple protein source.
Researchers developed a new variety of soy that is more resistant to pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - the edible seed of the soy plant

Etymology

From Japanese ‘shōyu’ (soy sauce), via obsolete Dutch ‘soja’.

2. NOUN

/ sɔɪ / (AmE), / sɔɪ / (BrE)
A plant of East Asian origin grown for its edible seeds, which are used as food and fodder, especially as a source of protein.
edible seed/plant; protein-rich food source

Examples

Many people choose soy products as a source of protein instead of meat.
The farmer planted several acres of soy this year to meet growing demand.
Soy is a key ingredient in many vegetarian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - the seed or plant itself

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce), via Dutch 'soja', referring to the soybean plant.

3. NOUN

/ sɔɪ / (AmE), / sɔɪ / (BrE)
A thin, dark sauce made from fermented soybeans, used especially in East Asian cooking.
thin sauce from fermented soybeans

Examples

He splashed a little soy over his rice for extra flavor.
Do you have any low-sodium soy to go with the sushi?
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of soy to enhance the dish's umami.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soy sauce - a dark, salty sauce made from fermented soybeans and wheat, used in Asian cuisine

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce), via Dutch 'soja', from Portuguese 'soja', ultimately from Japanese.

4. NOUN

/ sɔɪ / (AmE), / sɔɪ / (BrE)
a plant of the pea family, grown for its edible seeds and used as a source of oil, animal feed, soil improvement, and food products.
edible plant used for oil, food, and feed

Examples

Soy is a major crop used to produce oil and protein-rich foods around the world.
Vegetarian diets often include soy as a healthy source of protein.
Farmers rotate their crops with soy to improve soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - the seed of the soy plant, used in food and industry

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu', meaning soy sauce, borrowed via Chinese 'jiàng yóu'.
