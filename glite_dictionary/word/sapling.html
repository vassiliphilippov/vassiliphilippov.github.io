sapling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaplɪŋ / (BrE)
a young tree, especially one with a slender trunk
a young tree

Examples

The gardener planted a sapling in the backyard to replace the old oak.
After only a few years, the sapling had grown into a sturdy tree.
The forest was full of saplings sprouting up after the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seedling - a young plant, especially one grown from seed

Etymology

From Middle English sapling, from sap (young shoot) + -ling (diminutive suffix).
