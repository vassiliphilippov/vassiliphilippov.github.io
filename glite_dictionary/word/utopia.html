utopia

1. NOUN

/ juˈtoʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / juːˈtəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
an imagined place or state of things in which everything is perfect, especially in social, political, and moral aspects
perfect imagined society or state

Examples

Many authors have depicted a utopia where poverty and war no longer exist.
Her vision of a future utopia inspired political activists around the world.
While some see technology as the road to utopia, others warn of unforeseen consequences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dystopia - an imagined world where everything is unpleasant or bad
  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of great happiness or beauty

Etymology

Coined from Greek 'ou' (not) + 'topos' (place), meaning 'no place'; popularized by Sir Thomas More's 1516 book 'Utopia'.

2. NOUN

/ juˈtoʊpiə / (AmE), / juːˈtəʊpiə / (BrE)
a fictional or literary work that describes an imagined perfect or ideal society
a literary work about an ideal society

Examples

Her novel is a fascinating utopia set in a world without poverty or crime.
Students were assigned to read a classic utopia for their literature class.
Many 19th-century writers imagined their own utopia as a response to social problems of their time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dystopia - a fictional work describing a nightmare or oppressed society

Etymology

From 'Utopia', the title of a 1516 book by Thomas More, from Greek 'ou' (not) + 'topos' (place), meaning 'no place'.
