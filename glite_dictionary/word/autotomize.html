to autotomize

1. VERB

/ ɑːˈtɑtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒtəˌmaɪz / (BrE)
(of an animal or body part) to cause a body part to undergo autotomy; to deliberately shed a part, typically for self-defense.
cause a body part to be shed (autotomy)
medical

Examples

Some lizards can autotomize their tails to distract predators and escape.
The crab was observed to autotomize its limb when threatened by a larger animal.
Scientists studied how certain insects autotomize legs as a survival mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autotomy - the spontaneous shedding of a body part by an animal, usually to escape a predator

Etymology

From autotomy + -ize. 'Autotomy' from Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'tomy' (cutting).
