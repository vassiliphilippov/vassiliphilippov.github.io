Scolytidae

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈlɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / skɒˈlɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of bark-boring or wood-boring beetles, many species of which are destructive pests of forest and fruit trees.
family of bark-boring beetles
medical

Examples

The Scolytidae family includes species responsible for massive destruction of pine forests.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Scolytidae to develop pest control methods.
Infestations by Scolytidae can severely damage commercial fruit orchards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark beetle - any beetle of the family Scolytidae or related species that bores into tree bark

Etymology

From New Latin Scolytidae, based on the genus Scolytus, from Greek 'skolytos' meaning bark beetle.
