peach

1. NOUN

/ pitʃ / (AmE), / piːtʃ / (BrE)
a pale pinkish-yellow color, resembling the skin of a ripe peach
a pale pinkish-yellow color

Examples

She chose a peach dress for the summer party, which complemented her skin tone beautifully.
The walls of the nursery were painted a soft peach to create a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
He handed me a folder in a pale peach shade, different from the usual office colors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color
  • Degree Higher: apricot - a yellowish-orange color

Etymology

Named after the color of the peach fruit, from Middle English peche, from Old French, from Medieval Latin persica.

2. NOUN

/ pitʃ / (AmE), / piːtʃ / (BrE)
A round, soft fruit with sweet yellow or white flesh, a slightly furry skin, and a stone in the center, cultivated in temperate regions.
a soft, sweet stone fruit

Examples

She bit into a ripe peach and let the juice run down her chin.
The orchard behind our house is famous for its peach trees.
He added slices of peach to the fruit salad for extra sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nectarine - a smooth-skinned variety of peach
  • Degree Lower: plum - a smooth-skinned stone fruit similar to a peach but usually smaller

Etymology

From Middle English peche, from Old French pesche, from Medieval Latin pesca, from Latin persica, from Greek persikē (Persian apple).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ pitʃ / (AmE), / piːtʃ / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes offensive) An attractive or seductive woman.
attractive woman (informal)
informal

Examples

Everyone at the party agreed that Clara was a real peach in that dress.
He called her a peach, but she rolled her eyes at the old-fashioned compliment.
In the 1960s, movie magazines often referred to glamorous actresses as "Hollywood peaches."

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ugly - unattractive, not pleasing in appearance

Originally from the sweet, pleasing qualities associated with the fruit 'peach'.

british archaic

5. NOUN

/ pitʃ / (AmE), / piːtʃ / (BrE)
a round, juicy fruit with soft, downy skin and sweet yellowish or whitish flesh, commonly eaten fresh or used in cooking
a soft, sweet, juicy fruit

Examples

She sliced a ripe peach and added it to her fruit salad.
The tree in our backyard produces delicious peaches every summer.
A warm peach cobbler is one of my favorite desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nectarine - a smooth-skinned variety of peach
  • Degree Lower: plum - a similar but distinct juicy fruit with smooth skin
  • Degree Lower: apricot - a related, smaller and less juicy fruit

Etymology

From Middle English 'peche', from Old French 'pesche', from Medieval Latin 'persica', ultimately from Latin 'persicum' (Persian apple).
