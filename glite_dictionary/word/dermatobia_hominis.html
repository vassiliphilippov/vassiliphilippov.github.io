Dermatobia hominis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜrməˈtoʊbiə ˈhɑmənɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜːməˈtəʊbɪə ˈhɒmɪnɪs / (BrE)
A large tropical American fly whose larvae are parasitic on humans and other mammals.
species of parasitic tropical American fly
medical

Examples

The larvae of Dermatobia hominis can cause painful skin swellings in humans.
Farmers in South America often have to protect their cattle from Dermatobia hominis infestations.
Doctors identified the growing lump as the result of a Dermatobia hominis larva beneath the skin.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) and 'bios' (life), with 'hominis' from Latin meaning 'of humans'.
