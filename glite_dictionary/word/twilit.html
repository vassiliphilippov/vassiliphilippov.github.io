twilit

1. ADJ

/ ˈtwaɪ.lɪt / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪ.lɪt / (BrE)
Dimly lit as by twilight; having a subdued light like that at twilight.
dimly lit like twilight

Examples

She wandered through the twilit garden, lost in her thoughts.
A twilit calm settled over the small coastal town as evening approached.
The painting captured a twilit landscape, full of mysterious shadows and fading colors.

Synset Examples

The dusky night rides down the sky / And ushers in the morn
the twilight glow of the sky
a boat on a twilit river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the soft diffused light just after sunset or before sunrise

Etymology

Formed from 'twilight' + English past participle suffix '-lit'.
