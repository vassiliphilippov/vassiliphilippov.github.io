allogamy

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑɡəmi / (AmE), / əˈlɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the fertilization of a plant's ovules by pollen from a genetically different individual of the same species
cross-fertilization in plants
medical

Examples

Certain species rely on allogamy to promote genetic diversity within their populations.
In agricultural practice, allogamy can result in hybrid vigor among crop plants.
The study examined factors influencing allogamy versus self-pollination in wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autogamy - self-fertilization in plants
  • Synonym: cross-pollination - fertilization with pollen from another plant

Etymology

From Greek allo- (other) + -gamy (marriage, union).
