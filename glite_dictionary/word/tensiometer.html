tensiometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnsiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌtɛnsiˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the tension in a wire, fiber, or beam.
instrument for measuring tension

Examples

The engineer used a tensiometer to check the tension in the suspension cables.
Modern laboratories often include a digital tensiometer for precise measurements.
Before installation, the technician adjusted the tensiometer to ensure accurate readings.

Etymology

From Latin 'tensio' (stretching) + Greek 'metron' (measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnsiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌtɛnsiˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used to measure the moisture content or tension of water in soil.
instrument for measuring soil moisture

Examples

The scientist inserted a tensiometer into the ground to monitor the soil's moisture content.
Farmers use a tensiometer to decide when to irrigate their crops.
Regular readings from the tensiometer help ensure optimal growing conditions for plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygrometer - instrument for measuring humidity in the atmosphere

Etymology

From Latin 'tensio' (tension) + Greek '-meter' (measurer).

3. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnsiˈɑmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌtɛnsiˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the surface tension of liquids
instrument for measuring liquid surface tension

Examples

The scientist used a tensiometer to determine the surface tension of the solution.
Regular calibration of the tensiometer is necessary for accurate measurements.
Advanced laboratories often rely on an electronic tensiometer for precise results.

Etymology

From French 'tensiomètre', from 'tension' and Greek 'metron' (measure).
