Antheraea polyphemus

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθiːriə pəˈlɪfəməs / (AmE), / ænˈθɪəriə pɒlˈɪfɪməs / (BrE)
a very large North American silkworm moth with yellowish-brown wings and distinctive eyespots on its hind wings; its larvae feed on various fruit and shade trees
large North American silkworm moth with eyespots

Examples

The caterpillar of the Antheraea polyphemus can be found on many backyard trees during summer.
He photographed a stunning Antheraea polyphemus resting on the window at dusk.
Many gardeners appreciate the beauty of the adult Antheraea polyphemus moths, especially their large eyespots.

Etymology

From genus name Antheraea (Neo-Latin) and Polyphemus, after a giant in Greek mythology with one eye, referring to the moth's eyespots.
