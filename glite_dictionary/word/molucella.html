Molucella

1. NOUN

/ məˈluːsələ / (AmE), / məˈljuːsɪlə / (BrE)
a small genus of aromatic herbs native to Mediterranean regions, often cultivated as ornamental plants
genus of aromatic Mediterranean herbs

Examples

Molucella is popular among gardeners for its unique green bell-shaped calyces.
The florist included Molucella in her arrangements for its delicate fragrance.
Botanists often study Molucella to understand aromatic properties in Mediterranean plants.

Etymology

From modern Latin "Molucella," based on Molucca Islands, once thought to be the origin.

2. NOUN

/ məˈluːsələ / (AmE), / məˈluːsələ / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic herbs native to Mediterranean regions, often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
genus of aromatic Mediterranean herbs

Examples

Molucella is popular in gardens for its distinctive green flowers.
Several species of Molucella are grown for ornamental use.
The genus Molucella includes the well-known Bells of Ireland.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the genus name in botanical Latin, based on New Latin taxonomy.
