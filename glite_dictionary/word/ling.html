ling

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋ / (AmE), / lɪŋ / (BrE)
A low, evergreen shrub of the heath family (Calluna vulgaris), commonly known as heather, found widely in northern Europe and Asia.
low evergreen shrub; heather

Examples

The hills were covered with blooming ling in early autumn.
She pressed a sprig of ling into her diary as a keepsake.
Much of the moorland is dominated by ling and other heath plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - another name for the same plant (Calluna vulgaris)

Etymology

From Old Norse *lyng* (heather), related to other Germanic languages.

2. NOUN

/ lɪŋ / (AmE), / lɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of water chestnut whose spiny fruit has two rather than four prongs, especially Trapa bicornis.
a water chestnut with two-pronged fruit

Examples

The spiny fruit of the ling is often used in Asian cuisine.
Botanists collected samples of ling from the muddy pond.
The lings are harvested for their edible seeds in the late summer.

3. NOUN

/ lɪŋ / (AmE), / lɪŋ / (BrE)
an elongated freshwater fish found in northern Europe, Asia, and North America, with barbels around its mouth; also known as burbot.
an elongate freshwater cod-like fish

Examples

Fishermen in Canada often catch ling in the cold northern rivers.
The ling is prized for its delicate white flesh in many traditional recipes.
Biologists studied the unique habits of the ling during its spawning season.

Etymology

From Old Norse *langr* (long), referring to the fish's shape.

4. NOUN

/ lɪŋ / (AmE), / lɪŋ / (BrE)
An elongated marine fish found in Greenland and northern Europe, often salted and dried as food.
elongated marine food fish

Examples

Dried ling is a delicacy in several Scandinavian countries.
The fishermen caught several large ling off the coast of Iceland.
She prepared a traditional stew using salted ling and potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish, related and sometimes compared to ling

Etymology

From Old Norse *langr* (long), referencing the fish's shape.

5. NOUN

/ lɪŋ / (AmE), / lɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of fish belonging to the hake family, especially found in American waters.
a type of American hake (fish)

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a large ling that he caught off the coast of Maine.
Many restaurants serve dishes made with fresh ling, prized for its delicate flavor.
The Atlantic ling is often confused with other hakes due to its similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hake - a marine fish related to the cod, of which several kinds are commercially important as food
