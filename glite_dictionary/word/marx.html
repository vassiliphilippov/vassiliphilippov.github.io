Marx

1. PROPN

/ mɑːrks / (AmE), / mɑːks / (BrE)
Karl Marx (1818–1883), German philosopher, economist, and political theorist, known as the founder of modern communism and co-author of The Communist Manifesto.
Karl Marx, philosopher and founder of communism

Examples

Many historians credit Marx with shaping socialist theory and modern political thought.
Marx wrote The Communist Manifesto together with Friedrich Engels in 1848.
The ideas of Marx have influenced political movements around the world.
