digitate

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪdʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a part, especially a leaf or anatomical structure) shaped like or spreading out from a central point like the fingers of a hand.
shaped like or spread out like fingers

Examples

The horse chestnut has digitate leaves that radiate from a single point.
Some sea stars have digitate arms, which look like fingers extending from their bodies.
The digitate projections on the microscope slide were immediately noticed by the students.

Synset Examples

digitate leaves of the horse chestnut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmate - (of leaves) having lobes that spread out from a central point, like a palm

Etymology

From Latin 'digitatus', from 'digitus' meaning 'finger'.
