to dabble

1. VERB

/ ˈdæbəl / (AmE), / ˈdæbəl / (BrE)
to dip a foot or hand briefly into a liquid
dip part of body in liquid

Examples

She let her toes dabble in the cool stream as she sat on the bank.
He likes to dabble his fingers in the fountain on hot days.
The child reached out to dabble his hand in the puddle after the rain.

Etymology

From Dutch *dabbelen* (to paw with the hands or feet).

2. VERB

/ ˈdæbəl / (AmE), / ˈdæbəl / (BrE)
(of a bird) to bob forward and dip the head under water to feed, especially in shallow water.
dip the head under water to feed (as a bird)

Examples

Ducks often dabble in shallow ponds in search of food.
The mallards were dabbling along the shore, heads bobbing underwater.
Some waterfowl dabble to reach seeds and insects beneath the surface.

Synset Examples

dabbling ducks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dive - to plunge completely under water, often to feed (as a diving duck)

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdæbəl / (AmE), / ˈdæbəl / (BrE)
to take part in an activity in a casual or superficial way, especially without serious commitment
take part casually in an activity

Examples

She likes to dabble in painting whenever she has free time.
Many people dabble in foreign languages without becoming fluent.
He began to dabble in gardening after moving to the countryside.

Synset Examples

She dabbles in astronomy
He plays around with investments but he never makes any money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experiment - to try something new to discover what it is like
  • Antonym: to specialize - to focus on a particular activity or area with deep expertise

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *dabbelen* (to paddle in water).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02421160-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈdæbəl / (AmE), / ˈdæbəl / (BrE)
to splash or play in water lightly or casually, especially as children do.
splash or play in water

Examples

The children love to dabble their feet in the cool stream on hot summer days.
She watched the ducks dabble in the shallow pond near the park.
After the rain, the toddler began to dabble in the puddles outside the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to scatter water about in drops or blobs
  • Synonym: paddle - to move hands or feet gently in water

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly imitative or related to Dutch 'dabbelen' (to dip in water).
