cecropia

1. NOUN

/ səˈkroʊpiə / (AmE), / sɪˈkrəʊpiə / (BrE)
A large North American silkworm moth (Hyalophora cecropia) with brown and red markings; its larvae feed on the leaves of forest trees.
a large North American silkworm moth

Examples

The cecropia is the largest native moth in North America, with a wingspan of up to six inches.
As a hobbyist, she raised several cecropia caterpillars in her garden each spring.
The stunning patterns on a cecropia moth's wings help it avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, typically nocturnal
  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with large, colorful wings

Etymology

From Latin 'Cecropia', named after Cecrops, a legendary king of Athens.
