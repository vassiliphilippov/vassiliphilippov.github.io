unpalatableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈpælətəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈpælətəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unacceptable or difficult to accept to the mind or emotions.
the state of being mentally unacceptable

Examples

The sheer unpalatableness of the proposal made it difficult for anyone to support it.
He was surprised by the unpalatableness of the truth when it was finally revealed.
The report's unpalatableness forced the committee to reconsider their original stance.

Synset Examples

the policy's unpalatability caused an uproar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpalatability - the quality of being unacceptable to the mind or taste

Etymology

From unpalatable + -ness, where 'palatable' comes from Latin 'palatum' (palate).

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈpæləˌtəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈpalətəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unpleasant or unacceptable to the taste.
quality of being unpleasant to the taste

Examples

The unpalatableness of the medicine made it difficult for the child to swallow.
Some vegetables are avoided by children because of their perceived unpalatableness.
He complained about the unpalatableness of the overcooked fish at the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpalatable - not pleasant to taste; difficult to accept

Etymology

From 'unpalatable' + '-ness'. 'Unpalatable' from 'palatable', itself from Latin 'palatum' (palate).
