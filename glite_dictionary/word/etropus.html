Etropus

1. NOUN

/ iˈtroʊpəs / (AmE), / iˈtrəʊpəs / (BrE)
A genus of flatfishes in the family Bothidae, known as large-tooth flounders, native to the coastal waters of the Americas.
a genus of large-tooth flounders

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Etropus off the coast of Florida.
Fishermen occasionally catch Etropus while trawling in shallow coastal waters.
A field guide to marine life includes several illustrations of Etropus species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːtrəpəs / (AmE), / ˈiːtrəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of large-tooth flounders in the family Bothidae, native to the coastal waters of the Americas.
genus of large-tooth flounders

Examples

Several species within the genus Etropus inhabit the shallow coastal waters of the Gulf of Mexico.
Researchers discovered a new member of Etropus along the Atlantic coast.
Fish from the genus Etropus are recognized by their large teeth and flat bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bothidae - the family of fish that includes lefteye flounders, to which Etropus belongs
