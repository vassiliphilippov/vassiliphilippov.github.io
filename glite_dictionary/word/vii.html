VII

1. NUM

/ viː ˈaɪ ˈaɪ / (AmE), / viː ˈaɪ ˈaɪ / (BrE)
the Roman numeral representing the number seven (7)
the Roman numeral for seven

Examples

She labeled the section as VII in her outline for clarity.
The Roman emperor Augustus reigned during the year VII BCE.
The clock face showed the hour as VII using Roman numerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seven - the cardinal number that is the sum of six and one

Etymology

From Latin numeral 'VII' (seven), using Roman numeral notation.
