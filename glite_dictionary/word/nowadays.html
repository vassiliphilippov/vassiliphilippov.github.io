nowadays

1. ADV

/ ˈnaʊəˌdeɪz / (AmE), / ˈnaʊədeɪz / (BrE)
at the present time; in these times
at the present time

Examples

Many people work from home nowadays thanks to advances in technology.
Children nowadays spend less time outdoors than previous generations did.
It's rare to see handwritten letters nowadays; most communication is digital.

Synset Examples

it is solely by their language that the upper classes nowadays are distinguished
we now rarely see horse-drawn vehicles on city streets
today almost every home has television

Related Terms

  • Synonym: today - on or in the present day
  • Antonym: formerly - in the past; previously

Etymology

From Middle English 'now a days', meaning 'in these days'.
