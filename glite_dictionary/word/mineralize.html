to mineralize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪnɚəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert or be converted into a mineral substance, especially by the infiltration of minerals
convert to a mineral substance

Examples

Certain chemicals can mineralize organic matter during the fossilization process.
Bones begin to mineralize as minerals are deposited in their structure.
The company developed a technique to mineralize wastewater, making it cleaner and safer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fossilize - to convert into a fossil

Etymology

From mineral + -ize, patterned after French 'minéraliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00586782-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a metal) to convert into an ore by naturally occurring or artificial processes.
convert a metal into an ore

Examples

Copper deposits can mineralize under the right geological conditions.
Geologists study how metals naturally mineralize to locate new ore bodies.
Scientists aim to mineralize trace metals for more efficient extraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ore - a naturally occurring solid material from which a metal can be profitably extracted

Etymology

From French *minéraliser*, from 'minéral' (mineral), ultimately from Latin *minerale*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00586683-v
Synset POS: v
