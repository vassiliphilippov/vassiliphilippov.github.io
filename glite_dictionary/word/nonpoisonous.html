nonpoisonous

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈpɔɪzənəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈpɔɪzənəs / (BrE)
Not containing poison; safe to eat, touch, or use and not harmful to health.
not poisonous; safe to eat or touch

Examples

The berries are nonpoisonous and safe to eat in the wild.
Most garden snakes are nonpoisonous and harmless to humans.
Check the label to ensure the paint is nonpoisonous before using it in the nursery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poisonous - containing poison; capable of causing harm or death

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'poisonous' (containing poison).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈpɔɪzənəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈpɔɪzənəs / (BrE)
Not producing or containing poison; not harmful or toxic if touched or consumed.
not producing or containing poison

Examples

The berries on this plant are nonpoisonous and safe for children to eat.
He was relieved to learn that the snake was nonpoisonous and posed no threat.
Many popular houseplants are nonpoisonous to pets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poisonous - containing or producing poison
  • Antonym: toxic - harmful or deadly because of poisonous substances
  • Synonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'poisonous' (containing poison).

Synset POS: s
