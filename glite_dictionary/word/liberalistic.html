liberalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɪbərəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɪbərəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of liberalism; supporting or advocating individual freedom, civil liberties, and the protection of rights by law
relating to liberalism and civil liberties

Examples

Her liberalistic views often spark lively debates at family gatherings.
The party’s liberalistic platform emphasizes individual rights and freedoms for all citizens.
He admired the liberalistic principles that inspired the nation’s founding documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberal - favoring civil liberties and political freedom

Etymology

From 'liberal' + '-istic', modeled after 'liberalism'. Rooted in Latin 'liberalis' (of freedom).
