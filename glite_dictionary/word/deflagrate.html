to deflagrate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛfləˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfləɡreɪt / (BrE)
to cause to burn rapidly, especially with intense, sudden heat and flame, without detonation
to cause rapid combustion
medical

Examples

Careful measures must be taken when chemicals are deflagrated in the laboratory.
The technician used a controlled spark to deflagrate the substance safely.
Because the powder can deflagrate violently, only experienced personnel are allowed to handle it.

Synset Examples

care must be exercised when this substance is to be deflagrated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to set on fire; to cause to begin burning
  • Antonym: to explode - to burst or cause to burst violently as a result of rapid chemical change

Etymology

From Latin deflagratus, past participle of deflagrare (to burn down).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛfləˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfləɡreɪt / (BrE)
To burn rapidly with intense heat and light, often with a sudden, vigorous flame.
to burn rapidly with intense heat

Examples

The chemicals began to deflagrate, producing a blinding flash in the laboratory.
If exposed to a flame, the powder will deflagrate rather than detonate.
Engineers carefully controlled the environment so that the gas would deflagrate safely.

Synset Examples

the powder deflagrated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire

Etymology

From Latin deflagrare (to burn down), from de- (down) + flagrare (to burn).
