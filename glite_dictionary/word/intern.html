intern

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜrn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to work as an intern, especially to gain practical experience in a professional field
work as an intern

Examples

Many students intern at major companies during the summer to gain work experience.
She is interning at a design firm while completing her degree.
After medical school, he interned at a hospital in New York.

Synset Examples

The young doctor is interning at the Medical Center this year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprentice - to work as an apprentice
  • Degree Lower: volunteer - to work without pay, often to gain experience

Etymology

From French *interne*, from Latin *internus* (inward, internal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02451684-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜrn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to confine or detain someone, especially during wartime, without trial, usually for political or military reasons
confine or detain, especially during war

Examples

During the war, the government decided to intern all enemy nationals living near the coast.
Many political dissidents were interned without trial during that period.
Authorities may intern civilians if they are suspected of posing a threat to security.

Synset Examples

During WW II, Japanese were interned in camps in the West

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detain - to keep someone in official custody
  • Synonym: to imprison - to put someone in prison
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free

Etymology

From French *interner* (to confine), from Latin *internus* (internal, inside).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02501036-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tɝn / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɜːn / (BrE)
a graduate or advanced student in medicine who is gaining supervised practical experience in a hospital or clinic before obtaining full qualification
medical graduate in practical training

Examples

As an intern, she was responsible for monitoring patients during her hospital rotations.
Every intern must work overnight shifts as part of their training.
He completed his year as an intern before starting his residency.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: resident - a doctor receiving advanced training in a hospital after completing an internship

Etymology

From French 'interne', ultimately from Latin 'internus' (inward, internal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10230873-n
Synset POS: n
