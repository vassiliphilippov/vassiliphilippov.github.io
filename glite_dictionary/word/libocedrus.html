Libocedrus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪboʊˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ˌlɪb.əˈsiː.drəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family (Cupressaceae), native to the Southern Hemisphere and resembling cedars
a genus of cypress-like trees

Examples

The forests in southern Chile are home to the rare Libocedrus trees.
Botanists studied the cones of Libocedrus to understand its evolutionary history.
Some species of Libocedrus are valued for their fragrant wood and attractive form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - any of several coniferous trees with fragrant, durable wood
  • Synonym: cypress - any coniferous tree belonging to the Cupressaceae family

Etymology

From Greek 'leíbo' (to pour, flow) and 'kedros' (cedar), referring to its resin and cedar-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪboʊˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ˌlɪbə(ʊ)ˈsiːdrəs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the cypress family (Cupressaceae) that resemble cedars, native to the Southern Hemisphere.
genus of cypress-like trees

Examples

The Libocedrus genus is found primarily in New Zealand and South America.
Botanists study Libocedrus for its unique adaptation to the Southern Hemisphere climate.
Some species in the Libocedrus genus are often mistaken for true cedars due to their similar appearance.

Etymology

From Greek *libanos* (frankincense) and *kedros* (cedar), reflecting the resinous quality and cedar-like appearance.
