Megadermatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌdɜrˈmætɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡədɜːˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large-eared, insect-eating bats commonly known as Old World false vampire bats, found mainly in Africa, Asia, and Australia.
family of Old World false vampire bats

Examples

The Megadermatidae are distinguished by their large ears and lack of lower incisors.
Zoologists have discovered a new species within the Megadermatidae family in Southeast Asia.
Unlike true vampire bats, Megadermatidae feed primarily on insects and small animals.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'mega-' (large) + 'derma' (skin) + '-idae' (denoting a family in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌdɜːrməˈtɪːdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˌdɜːməˈtɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of bats, commonly known as Old World false vampire bats, found in parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.
a family of Old World false vampire bats

Examples

The Megadermatidae are known for their large ears and distinctive nose-leafs.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Megadermatidae in Southeast Asia.
Many members of Megadermatidae are carnivorous, feeding on insects and small vertebrates.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'mega' (large) and 'derma' (skin), referring to the large skin flaps on their ears and face.
