Clitocybe inornata

1. NOUN

/ klɪtəˈsaɪbi ɪˈnɔrnətə / (AmE), / klɪtəˈsaɪbi ɪˈnɔːnətə / (BrE)
A species of fungus distinguished by a creamy grey cap that turns brown with age and a whitish stalk that stains yellow when handled.
a species of mushroom with brown-aging cap

Examples

The identification of Clitocybe inornata can be tricky due to its subtle coloring.
When young, Clitocybe inornata has a creamy grey cap that gradually darkens.
Mycologists noted the yellow stains on the stalks of Clitocybe inornata after handling.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Clitocybe' meaning 'sloping head' and Latin 'inornata' meaning 'plain' or 'unadorned'.
