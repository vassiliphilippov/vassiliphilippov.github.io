disproportion

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsprəˈpɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
An imbalance or lack of proper relationship in size, amount, or degree between the parts of something.
imbalance; lack of proportion

Examples

There was a clear disproportion between the amount of work he did and the pay he received.
Some critics highlighted the disproportion of funding given to certain departments.
The disproportion in the population's age groups can affect social policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imbalance - a state of being out of equilibrium or proportion
  • Antonym: proportion - a proper or equal relationship between things or parts

Etymology

From Latin 'disproportio', from 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'proportio' (proportion).
