smoker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkə / (BrE)
a railway passenger car or compartment where smoking is permitted
railway car for smoking passengers

Examples

In the early 20th century, many trains had a smoker for passengers who wanted to light up.
He accidentally sat in the smoker, not realizing it was reserved for those who wished to smoke.
With new regulations, the old smoker cars have been converted into standard passenger compartments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-smoker - a railway car where smoking is not permitted

2. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkə / (BrE)
a person who smokes tobacco, especially habitually
person who smokes tobacco

Examples

The hotel offers special rooms for smokers and non-smokers.
As a longtime smoker, he struggled to quit the habit.
Many restaurants now prohibit smokers from lighting up indoors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonsmoker - a person who does not smoke tobacco
  • Synonym: smoking - the act of inhaling and exhaling the smoke of burning tobacco

Etymology

From smoke + -er, with -er denoting a person who performs an action.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkə / (BrE)
a social event or party attended only by men, traditionally involving smoking, drinking, and entertainment considered suitable for men
men-only social party

Examples

The fraternity organized a smoker to welcome the new members.
For decades, the annual smoker was a highlight for the club's male members.
He recalled attending a political smoker back in the 1950s.
