uncivil

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɪvəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɪvəl / (BrE)
not polite or lacking in good manners; rude or discourteous.
not polite; rude

Examples

The manager reprimanded the employee for his uncivil behavior toward customers.
Her uncivil remarks made the conversation quite uncomfortable for everyone present.
Online discussions can quickly become uncivil if participants lack respect for one another.

Synset Examples

want nothing from you but to get away from your uncivil tongue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rude - impolite or disrespectful in manner or speech
  • Antonym: polite - having or showing good manners and respect for others

Etymology

From Latin 'incivilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'civilis' (courteous, of citizens).
