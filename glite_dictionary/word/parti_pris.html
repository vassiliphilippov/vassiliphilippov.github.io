parti pris

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˌti ˈpri / (AmE), / ˌpɑːti ˈpriː / (BrE)
a preconceived opinion or bias, especially one formed without sufficient evidence
preconceived opinion; bias

Examples

Her argument was weakened by the obvious parti pris she brought to the discussion.
The critic's parti pris was evident in every review he wrote.
It is important to recognize one's own parti pris before making judgments.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preconception - an idea or opinion formed before having the evidence
  • Synonym: bias - preference or inclination, especially one that inhibits impartial judgment
  • Antonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict or bias

Etymology

From French 'parti pris' (prejudice, bias), past participle of 'prendre parti' (to take a side).
