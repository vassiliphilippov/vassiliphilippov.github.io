Sagitta

1. PROPN

/ səˈdʒɪtə, səˈgɪtə / (AmE), / səˈdʒɪtə, səˈgɪtə / (BrE)
a small constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located between Cygnus and Aquila and crossed by the Milky Way
constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Sagitta is easy to spot with binoculars on a clear night.
Ancient astronomers named Sagitta because of its arrow-like shape.
Sagitta lies along the path of the Milky Way between Aquila and Cygnus.

Etymology

From Latin 'sagitta' meaning 'arrow'.

2. NOUN

/ səˈdʒɪtə / (AmE), / səˈdʒɪtə / (BrE)
A chief genus of marine arrowworms in the phylum Chaetognatha, including some of the largest species of arrowworms.
genus of marine arrowworms

Examples

The species in the genus Sagitta are among the largest of all arrowworms.
Recent research described a new specimen belonging to the genus Sagitta from the Pacific Ocean.
Many plankton samples contain multiple representatives of the genus Sagitta.

Etymology

From Latin sagitta (arrow), referring to the arrow-like shape of the animals.
