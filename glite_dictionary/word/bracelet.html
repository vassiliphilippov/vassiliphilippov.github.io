bracelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪ.slət / (AmE), / ˈbreɪ.slɪt / (BrE)
An ornamental band, hoop, or chain worn around the wrist, typically as jewelry.
ornamental band worn on the wrist

Examples

She wore a delicate silver bracelet on her left wrist.
The jeweler offered to engrave the bracelet as a special gift.
He received a charm bracelet for his birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bangle - a rigid, ring-shaped bracelet

Etymology

From Old French 'bracelet', from Latin 'bracchiale' (of the arm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪslət / (AmE), / ˈbreɪslɪt / (BrE)
A band of cloth, leather, or metal links that attaches to a wristwatch and is worn around the wrist.
wristwatch strap or band

Examples

He replaced the leather bracelet on his vintage watch with a metal one.
The gold bracelet matched perfectly with her designer wristwatch.
This type of bracelet is adjustable to fit any wrist size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strap - a strip of leather, cloth, or other material used for fastening or holding things together
  • Synonym: band - a flat, thin strip or loop that is put around something, often for securing or attaching

Etymology

From Old French *bracelet* (small arm), from *bracel* (armlet), from Latin *bracchiale* (of the arm).

Synset POS: n
