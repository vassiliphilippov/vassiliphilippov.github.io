martagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtəɡən / (BrE)
a lily (Lilium martagon) native to northwestern Europe and northwestern Asia, having small, dull purple, turk's-cap-shaped flowers
a lily with purple turk's-cap flowers

Examples

The martagon is prized in gardens for its unique turk’s-cap blossoms and graceful stems.
Walking through the meadow, she was delighted to spot a wild martagon growing among the grasses.
Botanists often seek the martagon because of its distinctive purple flowers and historical significance.

Etymology

From the French 'martagon', a type of lily, possibly from Turkish 'mārtagān', meaning a kind of turban (referring to the flower shape).
