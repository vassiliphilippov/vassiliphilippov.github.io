gleba

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡliːbə / (AmE), / ˈɡliːbə / (BrE)
the spore-bearing, fleshy inner mass found in certain fungi, such as puffballs and stinkhorns
spore-bearing mass inside some fungi
medical

Examples

The gleba of the puffball is soft and white when young, turning powdery as it matures.
Scientists examined the gleba to study the spore development of the mushroom.
The unpleasant odor of the stinkhorn comes from its slimy gleba.

Etymology

From Latin *gleba* (clod, lump of earth).
