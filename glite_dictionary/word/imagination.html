imagination

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmædʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmadʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the faculty or action of forming new ideas, images or concepts of external objects not present to the senses
the ability to form mental images or ideas

Examples

Children often demonstrate a vivid imagination when they play pretend games.
Great works of literature are often born from the author's imagination.
He used his imagination to solve the puzzle that no one else could.

Synset Examples

he could still hear her in his imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creativity - the use of imagination or original ideas to create something
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin imaginatio (a picture, idea, imagination), from imaginari (to picture to oneself).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmadʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The ability to form mental images or concepts of things that are not present to the senses or have not been experienced.
the ability to imagine things not present

Examples

Her imagination turned the simple story into a magical adventure.
The artist's imagination soared as he created his latest masterpiece.
A child's imagination can transform an empty box into a castle.

Synset Examples

popular imagination created a world of demons
imagination reveals what the world could be

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creativity - the use of imagination or original ideas to create something
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin 'imaginatio' (the act of imagining, a fancy), from 'imaginari' (to picture to oneself).
