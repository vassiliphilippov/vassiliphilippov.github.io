lure

1. NOUN

/ lʊr / (AmE), / lʊə / (BrE)
an object or substance used to attract and entice animals, especially fish, into a trap or danger
object used to attract animals, especially in fishing or hunting

Examples

He attached a shiny lure to his fishing line to attract the trout.
Hunters often use a scent lure to draw animals toward their traps.
The brightly colored lure fooled the fish into thinking it was food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bait - food or substance used to entice animals, especially fish, as prey

Etymology

From Old French *leurre* (bait, decoy), originally referring to a device for training falcons.

2. NOUN

/ lʊr / (AmE), / ljʊə / (BrE)
an attractive quality or feature that tempts or entices someone by promising some kind of reward
appealing quality or feature

Examples

The lure of easy money can be hard to resist for some people.
Many people are drawn to the city by the lure of fame and fortune.
The lure of adventure inspired him to travel to distant lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - the action or power of evoking interest, pleasure, or liking
  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully and mysteriously attractive or fascinating
  • Antonym: repulsion - a feeling of intense distaste or disgust

Etymology

From Old French *leurre* (bait), originally from Frankish *lōthr* (device for luring), related to the verb 'to lure'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04697009-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ lʊr / (AmE), / ljʊə / (BrE)
to tempt or persuade someone to go somewhere or do something, especially by offering something attractive or making false promises
tempt or persuade with promises

Examples

She tried to lure the cat inside with a bowl of milk.
The company lured new employees with promises of high salaries and flexible hours.
He lured me into joining the risky investment scheme.

Synset Examples

He lured me into temptation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entice - to attract by offering pleasure or advantage
  • Synonym: to tempt - to try to persuade someone to do something that might not be wise or good

Etymology

From Middle English *luren*, from Old French *luere*, from Frankish *lōn* (reward).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ lʊr / (AmE), / ljʊə / (BrE)
A thing that tempts or attracts a person or animal to do something, especially by offering some reward or advantage.
something that entices or attracts

Examples

The promise of quick riches was a powerful lure for many investors.
Many fish cannot resist the colorful lure on the end of the line.
The bright city lights act as a lure for people from small towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enticement - The act of enticing or something that entices
  • Synonym: temptation - The desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
  • Antonym: repellent - Something that repels or drives away

Etymology

From Old French *lourre* (bait, device for luring birds), possibly of Germanic origin.
