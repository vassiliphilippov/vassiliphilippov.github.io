Kaopectate

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊəˈpɛkteɪt / (AmE), / ˌkaʊəˈpɛkteɪt / (BrE)
A trademarked over-the-counter medicine used to treat diarrhea and upset stomach, which contains kaolin and pectin as active ingredients.
trade-name antidiarrheal medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended taking Kaopectate to help with my digestive discomfort.
I always keep a bottle of Kaopectate in my medicine cabinet for emergencies.
After eating something bad, she reached for the Kaopectate to soothe her stomach.

Etymology

Formed from the components kaolin and pectin, the main active ingredients.
