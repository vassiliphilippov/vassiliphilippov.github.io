to vaporize

1. VERB

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪz / (BrE)
to turn or be turned into vapor or gas, especially by heating
to turn into vapor or gas

Examples

When water reaches 100°C, it will vaporize quickly.
The chemist used heat to vaporize the solvent in the flask.
Some cleaning sprays instantly vaporize when used on hot surfaces.

Synset Examples

The substance gasified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaporate - to turn from liquid into vapor

Etymology

From French 'vaporiser', from 'vapeur' (vapor); ultimately from Latin 'vapor'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00443222-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪz / (BrE)
to destroy or kill someone or something suddenly and completely, especially as if by a powerful weapon or energy burst
destroy or kill instantly, as by energy

Examples

The spaceship's laser can vaporize enemy ships in seconds.

3. VERB

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪz / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to lose its water content by converting it into vapor, leaving a more concentrated residue behind
remove liquid by turning it to vapor

Examples

You can vaporize the solution to obtain a more concentrated syrup.
The chef vaporized the milk to create a thicker base for the sauce.
If heated too long, the water will vaporize completely, leaving only the residue behind.

Synset Examples

evaporate milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaporate - to turn from liquid into vapor

Etymology

From vapor + -ize; ultimately from Latin vapor (steam, exhalation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00367511-v
Synset POS: v
