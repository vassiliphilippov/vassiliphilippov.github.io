masquerader

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪdər / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪdə / (BrE)
a person who wears a disguise or costume, especially as part of a masquerade or masked event
person in disguise at a masquerade

Examples

Scores of masqueraders filled the ballroom, each hidden behind elaborate masks and costumes.
At the carnival, every masquerader followed the music through the crowded streets.
The guilty masquerader was finally revealed when she removed her mask.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masker - a person wearing a mask, often at a celebration

Etymology

From 'masquerade' (from French 'mascarade', Italian 'mascherata') + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
