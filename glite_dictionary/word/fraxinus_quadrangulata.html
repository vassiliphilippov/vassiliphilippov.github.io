Fraxinus quadrangulata

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ˌkwɒdræŋˈɡjuːlətə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs ˌkwɒdræŋˈɡjuːlətə / (BrE)
A species of ash tree native to central and southern United States, known for its bluish-green foliage and hard brown wood.
North American ash tree species

Examples

The Fraxinus quadrangulata is often referred to as blue ash due to its distinctive bark color.
Many furniture makers value the hard wood of Fraxinus quadrangulata for its durability.
Arboretums in the Midwest feature specimens of Fraxinus quadrangulata among their native tree collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue ash - Common name for Fraxinus quadrangulata, a species of North American ash tree
  • Degree Higher: ash - Common name for trees of the genus Fraxinus, with compound leaves and winged fruits

Etymology

From Latin: 'fraxinus' meaning ash tree and 'quadrangulata' meaning four-angled, referring to the angled twigs.
