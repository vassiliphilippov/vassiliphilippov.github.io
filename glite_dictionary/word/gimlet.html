gimlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪmlət / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmlɪt / (BrE)
A small hand tool with a pointed screw tip and a cross handle, used for boring holes, especially in wood.
small tool for boring holes

Examples

He drilled a neat hole in the plank using a gimlet.
Before power tools, carpenters relied on the gimlet for small holes in wood.
The old toolbox contained a rusty gimlet among other traditional tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auger - a tool for boring larger holes in wood or earth

Etymology

From Old French *guimbelet*, from Middle Dutch *wimmel* (‘auger, drill’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪm.lət / (AmE), / ˈɡɪm.lɪt / (BrE)
A cocktail made with gin or vodka and lime juice, often served with ice.
cocktail of gin or vodka and lime juice

Examples

She ordered a gimlet at the bar to start her evening.
His favorite drink is a classic gimlet with fresh lime juice.
Would you like a gimlet or something sweeter?

Etymology

Probably from the drink's association with the tool 'gimlet', hinting at its sharp taste.
