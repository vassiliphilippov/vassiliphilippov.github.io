chamosite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæməˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæməʊˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a greenish-grey or black mineral that is a silicate of iron and aluminum, typically found in metamorphic rocks
a greenish-grey iron-aluminum silicate mineral

Examples

The distinct greenish hue of chamosite makes it easy to distinguish from other minerals in the field.
A sample containing chamosite was found in the sedimentary rocks near the river.
Geologists analyzed the composition of chamosite to better understand the rock's metamorphic history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorite - any of a group of green, flaky minerals related to chamosite

Etymology

Named after Chamoson, a locality in Switzerland where it was first described.
