backdrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌdrɒp / (BrE)
a painted cloth or set of scenery hung at the back of a stage as a background for the action; any background against which events are perceived.
scenery hung at the back of a stage

Examples

The painted backdrop created a magical forest on stage for the play.
Crew members quickly changed the backdrop between scenes to set a new location.
Against the city skyline backdrop, the performers sang their final number.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background - the area or scenery behind the main object of contemplation, especially in a picture, photograph, or stage

Etymology

From back + drop; originally referring to a painted cloth that was dropped at the back of a stage.
