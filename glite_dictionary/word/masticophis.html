Masticophis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪˈkoʊfɪs / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈkəʊfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, fast-moving snakes commonly known as whip snakes, native to North and Central America.
genus of whip snakes

Examples

Several species within the Masticophis genus are known for their remarkable speed and agility.
The coachwhip, a member of the Masticophis group, is found throughout the southern United States.
Biologists studied the diet of Masticophis to understand their ecological role.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastix' (whip) and 'ophis' (snake), referring to the shape and agility of these snakes.
