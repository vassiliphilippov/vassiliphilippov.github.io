Noah Webster

1. PROPN

/ ˈnoʊ.ə ˈwɛb.stər / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.ə ˈwɛb.stə / (BrE)
(1758–1843) An American lexicographer, grammarian, and language reformer, best known for compiling dictionaries that influenced American English spelling and usage.
American lexicographer (1758–1843)

Examples

Noah Webster is often credited with shaping the way American English is spoken and written today.
The first American dictionary was compiled by Noah Webster in the early nineteenth century.
Many people recognize Noah Webster's influence on standardizing American English spelling.
