hypnotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪp.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nə.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The act or practice of inducing a trance-like state of heightened suggestibility in a person, known as hypnosis.
the act of inducing hypnosis

Examples

He became interested in hypnotism after attending a demonstration at a science fair.
The therapist used hypnotism to help her patient quit smoking.
Skeptics often question whether stage hypnotism is real or just an act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - a trance-like state with heightened suggestibility

Etymology

From French hypnotisme, from Greek hūpnos (sleep).
