Brachychiton acerifolius

1. NOUN

/ brəˌkaɪkɪˌtɑn ˌæsəˈrɪfəliəs / (AmE), / brəˌkaɪkɪˌtɒn ˌæsəriːˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
A tall Australian tree, also known as the Illawarra flame tree, noted for its large panicles of bright scarlet flowers.
Australian tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Brachychiton acerifolius is renowned for its stunning red blossoms that appear in late spring.
Many gardeners plant Brachychiton acerifolius to add vibrant color to their landscapes.
During flowering season, the Brachychiton acerifolius transforms into a striking blaze of scarlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Illawarra flame tree - Common name for Brachychiton acerifolius

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short) + 'chiton' (tunic), referring to the seed covering; 'acerifolius' from Latin 'acer' (maple) + 'folium' (leaf), referring to the maple-like leaves.
