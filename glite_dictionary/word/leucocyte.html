leucocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːkoʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈluːkəsʌɪt / (BrE)
A type of white blood cell that engulfs and digests bacteria and fungi, playing a key role in the body's immune response.
white blood cell in immune system
medical

Examples

An increased number of leucocytes in the blood may indicate an infection.
The doctor explained that leucocytes are crucial for fighting off bacterial invasions.
A blood test showed a normal leucocyte count, suggesting a healthy immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white blood cell - another term for leucocyte; a cell of the immune system involved in protecting the body

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'kytos' (cell).
