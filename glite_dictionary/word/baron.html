baron

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærən / (AmE), / ˈbærən / (BrE)
A member of the lowest rank of the British nobility.
British noble of lowest rank
british

Examples

The old estate had once belonged to a powerful baron in medieval times.
He was made a baron by the Queen in recognition of his service.
Several barons attended the ceremony at the House of Lords.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lord - a British peer; a nobleman
  • Degree Higher: duke - a British peer of the highest rank
  • Degree Higher: earl - a British peer of rank above baron

Etymology

From Old French *baron* (man, warrior), from Late Latin *baro* (man, servant, vassal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbær.ən / (AmE), / ˈbær.ən / (BrE)
a member of the lowest rank of the nobility, especially in Britain and some other European countries
lowest rank of nobility

Examples

He was made a baron by the king for his loyal service.
Several barons attended the Parliament in medieval England.
As a baron, he owned vast estates and held local power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobleman - a man who belongs to the nobility; an aristocrat
  • Antonym: commoner - a person who is not a member of the nobility

Etymology

From Old French baron (man, vassal, nobleman), from Late Latin baro (man, servant, soldier).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbærən / (AmE), / ˈbærən / (BrE)
A very wealthy or powerful person in a particular business or industry.
wealthy or powerful businessperson

Examples

He became a baron in the steel industry before the age of forty.
Many considered him an oil baron because of his vast energy empire.
The new tech barons wield enormous influence over modern society.

Synset Examples

an oil baron

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tycoon - a wealthy, powerful person in business or industry
  • Synonym: magnate - a person of great influence or importance in a particular field, especially business
