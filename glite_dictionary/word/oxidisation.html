oxidisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a substance combines with oxygen, often involving the loss of electrons, especially as part of a chemical reaction
chemical process of combining with oxygen

Examples

The oxidisation of iron causes it to rust over time.
Copper pipes can corrode due to oxidisation when exposed to moisture and air.
The process of food spoilage is often accelerated by oxidisation of fats and oils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxidation - the process of oxidizing; the addition of oxygen to a compound with a loss of electrons
  • Antonym: reduction - the chemical reaction involving the gain of electrons, often accompanying oxidation

Etymology

From oxidise + -ation, modelled on French oxidation from Latin 'oxygenium'.
