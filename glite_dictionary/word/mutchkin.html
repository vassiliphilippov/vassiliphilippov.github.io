mutchkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌtʃ.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌtʃ.kɪn / (BrE)
A Scottish unit of liquid measure equal to approximately 0.9 US pint (about 0.426 liters), formerly used chiefly for spirits.
Scottish liquid measure, about 0.9 US pint

Examples

In 18th-century Scotland, a bottle containing a mutchkin was a common sight at taverns.
The old recipe calls for a mutchkin of whisky to be added to the mixture.
He poured everyone a generous mutchkin of ale, following the local tradition.

Etymology

From Scots, likely from 'mutch' (cap), possibly referencing a cap or measure covering.
