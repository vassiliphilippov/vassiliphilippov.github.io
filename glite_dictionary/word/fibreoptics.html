fibreoptics

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪbərˈɑptɪks / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbəˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
The technological process or field involving the transmission of light signals through fine, flexible glass or plastic fibers, usually for telecommunications or data transfer.
transmission of light via glass fibers
british

Examples

Fibreoptics has revolutionized high-speed internet connections in many countries.
Medical instruments often use fibreoptics to view inside the human body.
Recent advances in fibreoptics make data transmission much faster and more reliable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber optics - The transmission of light signals through glass fibers (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'fibre' (British spelling of 'fiber') and 'optics' (the science of light).
