Tsuga mertensiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈtsuːɡə mərˌtɛnsiˈænə / (AmE), / ˈtsuːɡə mɛːˌtɛnsiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A large evergreen conifer, commonly known as the mountain hemlock, native to western North America with wood harder than other hemlocks.
mountain hemlock tree species

Examples

The forests of the Pacific Northwest are home to the Tsuga mertensiana.
Wood from Tsuga mertensiana is prized for its strength and durability.
We identified the tall trees on the slope as Tsuga mertensiana after examining their needles and cones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain hemlock - Alternative common name for Tsuga mertensiana
  • Degree Lower: western hemlock - A related species, Tsuga heterophylla, native to western North America

Etymology

From New Latin: Tsuga (genus name) + mertensiana, after Karl H. Mertens, German botanist.
