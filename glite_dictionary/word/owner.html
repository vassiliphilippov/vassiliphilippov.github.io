owner

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈəʊnə / (BrE)
a person who has legal possession of something
person who possesses something

Examples

The owner of the restaurant greeted us at the door.
As a pet owner, she takes great care of her animals.
He became the proud owner of a vintage motorcycle last year.

Synset Examples

they are searching for the owner of the car
who is the owner of that friendly smile?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprietor - person who owns a business or property
  • Antonym: tenant - person who rents or occupies property from an owner

Etymology

From 'own' + '-er', with 'own' from Old English *āgan* (to possess).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈəʊnə / (BrE)
a person who legally possesses something such as a business, property, or item
legal possessor; holder of ownership

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally became the owner of her own bakery.
The owner is responsible for maintaining the building and ensuring it's up to code.
Many owners choose to sell their businesses when they retire.

Synset Examples

he is the owner of a chain of restaurants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprietor - person who has the legal right or exclusive title to something
  • Antonym: tenant - person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord

Etymology

From Old English āgnian (to possess, own), related to 'own'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10408623-n
Synset POS: n
