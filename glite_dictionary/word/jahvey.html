Jahvey

1. NOUN

/ jɑːˈveɪ / (AmE), / jɑːˈveɪ / (BrE)
A transliteration of YHVH, the name for God in the Old Testament, used especially in theological texts.
name for God in the Old Testament

Examples

Many ancient texts refer to Jahvey as the supreme deity in Hebrew tradition.
Some scholars debate the correct pronunciation of the name Jahvey.
In theological discussions, Jahvey is often considered synonymous with Jehovah or Yahweh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yahweh - another transliteration of the Hebrew name for God
  • Synonym: Jehovah - alternative form of the Hebrew name for God

Etymology

Transliteration of the Hebrew consonants YHVH, representing the divine name.
