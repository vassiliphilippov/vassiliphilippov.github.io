IJssel

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəl / (BrE)
A river in the central Netherlands flowing northward into the IJsselmeer.
a river in the Netherlands

Examples

The IJssel is an important waterway for shipping in the Netherlands.
Flood risks along the IJssel have prompted large-scale river management projects.
We took a scenic drive along the IJssel to admire the picturesque towns and landscapes.

Etymology

Dutch, from Old Dutch *Īsala (name of the river).

2. PROPN

/ ˈaɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəl / (BrE)
a river in central Netherlands that flows north into the IJsselmeer
river in the Netherlands

Examples

The IJssel splits from the Rhine and winds its way north through the Netherlands.
Several picturesque towns lie along the banks of the IJssel.
Heavy rainfall caused the IJssel to overflow its banks in the spring.

Etymology

From Dutch 'IJssel', ultimately from Old Dutch 'Isala'.
