didanosine

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdænəˌsin / (AmE), / dɪˈdænəʊsiːn / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used as a reverse transcriptase inhibitor in the treatment of HIV infection
antiviral drug for HIV treatment
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed didanosine as part of the patient's antiretroviral therapy for HIV.
Resistance to didanosine can develop if doses are missed regularly.
Side effects of didanosine may include nausea and abdominal pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ddI - abbreviation for didanosine, the antiviral drug
  • Synonym: reverse transcriptase inhibitor - a class of drugs that inhibit reverse transcriptase enzyme
  • Synonym: zidovudine - another reverse transcriptase inhibitor used against HIV

Etymology

From 'di-' (two) + 'deoxyinosine', reflecting its chemical structure and naming conventions.
