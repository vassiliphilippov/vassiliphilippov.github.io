anaphor

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈænəfəː / (BrE)
A word or phrase, such as a pronoun, that refers back to another word or phrase earlier in a sentence or context to avoid repetition.
a word referring back to an earlier word or phrase

Examples

In the sentence 'Lisa lost her keys and she is upset,' the word she is an anaphor referring to Lisa.
An anaphor like 'his' in 'John finished his work' refers to the previously mentioned John.
Linguists study how an anaphor links to its antecedent in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that takes the place of a noun

Etymology

From Greek anaphora (a carrying back), from ana- (back) + pherein (to carry).
