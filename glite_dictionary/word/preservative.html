preservative

1. ADJ

/ prəˈzɝː.və.tɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to preserve or protect against decay or change
having the ability to preserve

Examples

This paint has preservative properties that help wood last longer.
They added a preservative coating to the metal to prevent rust.
The museum uses preservative measures to protect ancient artifacts.

Synset Examples

timbers should be treated with a preservative substance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protective - intended to protect or shield

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praeservativus, from praeservare (to preserve).

WordNet ID: oewn-01894653-s

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɜrvətɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
a substance, especially a chemical, added to food, wood, or other products to prevent decay or spoilage
substance that prevents decay or spoilage

Examples

Many processed foods contain a preservative to extend their shelf life.
This wood has been treated with a preservative to prevent rot and insect damage.
Benzene is sometimes used as a preservative in laboratory samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additive - a substance added to something in small quantities for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: antioxidant - a substance that inhibits oxidation, often used to prevent spoilage

Etymology

From Latin praeservare (to guard beforehand).
