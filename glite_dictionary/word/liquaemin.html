Liquaemin

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪk.wəˈɛm.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌlɪk.wəˈɛm.ɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for heparin, a polysaccharide anticoagulant used to prevent blood clots, especially in the treatment of thrombosis and during heart surgery.
trade name for heparin, an anticoagulant
medical

Examples

The patient was administered Liquaemin to reduce the risk of blood clots during surgery.
Doctors chose Liquaemin as the preferred anticoagulant in this case.
After receiving Liquaemin, the patient's coagulation levels were closely monitored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heparin - an anticoagulant substance used to prevent blood clotting
