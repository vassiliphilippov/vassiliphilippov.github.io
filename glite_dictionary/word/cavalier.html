cavalier

1. NOUN

/ ˌkævəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌkævəˈlɪə / (BrE)
a gallant or courtly gentleman, especially one who is attentive to women or displays chivalrous behavior
gallant or courtly gentleman

Examples

Donning his finest clothes, the cavalier swept into the ballroom with effortless grace.
In old English tales, a cavalier would often defend the honor of a lady at great personal risk.
The nobleman's manners were those of a perfect cavalier, charming everyone he met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallant - a man who pays special attention to women; a chivalrous man
  • Synonym: gentleman - a man of good birth or high social position, or behaving in a courteous way

Etymology

From French 'cavalier', from Italian 'cavaliere', from Late Latin 'caballarius' (horseman).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkævəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌkævəˈlɪə / (BrE)
showing a lack of proper concern; displaying a haughty disregard for others or for important matters
showing haughty disregard; unconcerned

Examples

He made a cavalier remark about people's concerns, dismissing them entirely.
Her cavalier attitude towards safety regulations alarmed her colleagues.
The manager's cavalier approach to employee feedback frustrated the staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disdainful - showing contempt or lack of respect
  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference

Etymology

From French 'cavalier' (horseman), from Italian 'cavaliere', from Latin 'caballarius' (horseman).
