Cavalier

1. NOUN

/ ˌkævəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌkævəˈlɪə / (BrE)
A supporter of King Charles I and the royalist cause during the English Civil War (1642–1651).
royalist supporter in English Civil War

Examples

During the English Civil War, the Cavaliers fought fiercely for King Charles I against the Parliamentarians.
Many Cavalier officers wore elaborate clothes and long hair as a symbol of their loyalty to the king.
The Cavaliers were ultimately defeated by Oliver Cromwell's forces at the Battle of Naseby.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Roundhead - a supporter of Parliament and opponent of Charles I during the English Civil War

Etymology

From French 'cavalier', from Italian 'cavaliere', from Latin 'caballarius' (horseman, knight).
