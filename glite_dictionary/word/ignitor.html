ignitor

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
A substance that is used to start or kindle a fire; a material that initiates combustion.
a substance that starts a fire

Examples

The technician added a small amount of ignitor to the mixture to ensure it would burn evenly.
This type of ignitor is commonly used in model rocket engines.
Always handle the ignitor carefully, as it is highly flammable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igniter - a device or substance used to initiate combustion

Etymology

From the verb 'ignite', which comes from Latin 'ignis' (fire).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to ignite fuel, charges, or fires by producing a spark or flame
device for lighting fuel or fires

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty ignitor in the furnace to restore heat to the building.
A gas stove may use an electric ignitor instead of matches for greater convenience.
Be sure to check that the barbecue ignitor works before starting to grill.

Synset Examples

do you have a light?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lighter - a portable device for lighting cigarettes, stoves, etc.
  • Antonym: extinguisher - a device used to put out fires

Etymology

From ignite, based on Latin 'ignīre' (to set fire) + -or (agent suffix).
