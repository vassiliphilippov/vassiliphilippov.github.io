quintuple

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtuːpəl / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtjuːpəl / (BrE)
A set or group of five similar things considered as a single unit.
set of five similar things

Examples

The award ceremony ended with each quintuple being honored on stage.
He ordered a quintuple of espresso shots to help him stay awake.
The museum displayed a quintuple of rare coins from the same period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintet - a group of five people or things, especially musicians

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) and '-plus' (more).

2. ADJ

/ kwɪnˈtʌpəl, ˈkwɪn.tə.pəl / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtjuː.pəl, ˈkwɪn.tjʊ.pəl / (BrE)
Consisting of five parts, units, or components.
having five parts or components

Examples

The company announced a quintuple expansion of its production facilities.
Each flower had a quintuple arrangement of petals.
They celebrated their quintuple victory in five major tournaments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintet - a group of five people or things
  • Degree Lower: quadruple - having four units or components
  • Degree Higher: sextuple - having six units or components

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) + 'plus' (fold).

3. VERB

/ kwɪnˈtʌpəl / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtjuːpəl / (BrE)
to increase or make something increase to five times its original amount or size
increase fivefold

Examples

The city's population quintupled over the last decade due to rapid urbanization.
Sales of the product are expected to quintuple if the new campaign is successful.
If you invest wisely, your money could quintuple within a few years.

Synset Examples

The population of China quintupled

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - to make or become twice as much
  • Degree Lower: triple - to make or become three times as much
  • Degree Lower: quadruple - to make or become four times as much

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) + '-uple' (as in 'double', 'triple', etc.), modeled after similar multiplicative terms.
