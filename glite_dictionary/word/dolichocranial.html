dolichocranial

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɑlɪkoʊˈkreɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkəʊˈkreɪnɪəl / (BrE)
Having a relatively long head, with a cephalic index under 75.
having a long head (cephalic index under 75)
medical

Examples

Human populations with predominantly dolichocranial skulls are found in certain regions of Africa and Europe.
The researcher classified the specimen as dolichocranial based on its cephalic index measurements.
A dolichocranial head shape is contrasted with brachycephalic in cranial anthropometry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brachycephalic - having a broad, short head (cephalic index over 80)

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + kranion (skull).
