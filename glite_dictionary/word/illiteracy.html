illiteracy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪt̬.ə.rə.si / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪt.ər.ə.si / (BrE)
the inability to read or write
inability to read or write

Examples

Efforts to reduce illiteracy have become a major focus of recent educational reforms.
In some rural areas, illiteracy rates remain alarmingly high.
The program helps adults overcome illiteracy and improve their job opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literacy - the ability to read and write

Etymology

From Latin illitteratus ('unlettered, ignorant').

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪt̬.ɚ.ə.si / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪt.ər.ə.si / (BrE)
the inability to read or write; lack of knowledge or education resulting from not being able to read
inability to read or write

Examples

The high rate of illiteracy in the region has become a major concern for local governments.
Programs to reduce illiteracy have brought positive changes to the community.
Her illiteracy made it difficult for her to find employment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literacy - the ability to read and write

Etymology

From Medieval Latin illiteratus (unlettered), from Latin in- (not) + litteratus (literate).
