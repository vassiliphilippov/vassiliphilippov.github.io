dormant

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔrmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmənt / (BrE)
temporarily inactive but able to become active or develop in the future
temporarily inactive, able to activate

Examples

The volcano has been dormant for centuries, but scientists believe it could erupt again.
Her artistic talent lay dormant until she enrolled in a painting class.
Some seeds remain dormant in the soil for years before they finally sprout.

Synset Examples

her feelings of affection are dormant but easily awakened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaged in action or ready for activity
  • Synonym: latent - existing but not yet developed or manifest; hidden or concealed
  • Synonym: inactive - not active; not engaging in activity

Etymology

From Latin dormire (to sleep), via French dormant (sleeping).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɔːrmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmənt / (BrE)
(of a volcano or other natural phenomena) temporarily inactive but not extinct.
temporarily inactive (esp. volcano)

Examples

The dormant volcano hasn't erupted in over a century, but scientists believe it could become active again.
Seeds can remain dormant for years before conditions are right for them to sprout.
The park is home to several dormant geysers that occasionally spring back to life.

Synset Examples

a dormant volcano

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - currently showing activity (e.g. of a volcano)
  • Antonym: extinct - no longer active or able to erupt (e.g. of a volcano)

Etymology

From Latin 'dormire' (to sleep), via Old French 'dormant' (sleeping).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00040908-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɔrmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmənt / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, or part) in a state of rest or inactivity, with vital functions slowed down or suspended.
in a state of rest or inactivity

Examples

The seeds remain dormant until conditions are right for growth.
During the winter, the volcano appeared dormant but was still monitored closely.
Many trees become dormant in cold weather to survive harsh conditions.

Synset Examples

dormant buds
a hibernating bear
torpid frogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inactive - not active; not engaging in activity
  • Antonym: active - engaging or ready to engage in activity

Etymology

From Latin dormire (to sleep), via Old French dormant (sleeping).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00189643-s
Synset POS: s
