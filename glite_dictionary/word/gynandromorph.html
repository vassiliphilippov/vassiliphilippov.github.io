gynandromorph

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡaɪˈnændrəˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˌɡaɪˈnændrəˌmɔːf / (BrE)
an individual animal that exhibits both male and female sexual characteristics and organs, typically as a result of abnormal development
organism with both male and female traits

Examples

The butterfly was identified as a gynandromorph, possessing both male and female coloring on its wings.
In rare cases, a bird may hatch as a gynandromorph with one side of its body male and the other female.
Scientists observed a crab that was a natural gynandromorph, displaying characteristics of both sexes.

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman) + 'anēr' (man) + 'morphē' (form).
