Mustela vison

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛlə ˈvaɪsən / (AmE), / mʌˈstiːlə ˈviːsɒn / (BrE)
the American mink, a semiaquatic mammal native to North America, usually with dark brown fur
American mink, a semiaquatic mammal

Examples

The fur industry once heavily relied on the pelt of the Mustela vison for luxurious coats.
Researchers tagged several Mustela vison to study their movement along the river banks.
Unlike other mustelids, Mustela vison is highly adapted to aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mink - a semiaquatic mustelid mammal valued for its fur; can refer to several species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Mustela' meaning 'weasel' and 'vison', adopted from French, used as a species name.
