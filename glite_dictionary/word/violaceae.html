Violaceae

1. NOUN

/ vaɪ.əˈleɪ.si.i / (AmE), / vaɪ.əˈleɪ.sɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes violets and their relatives, characterized by herbaceous or shrubby plants often with showy flowers
the violet family of flowering plants

Examples

The Violaceae family consists of various species commonly known as violets and pansies.
Botanists have identified over 800 species within the Violaceae.
Many members of the Violaceae are grown in gardens for their attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Viola - the genus including violets and pansies

Etymology

From Latin 'Viola', the type genus, with the suffix '-aceae' denoting a plant family.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.ələˈsi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əʊˈleɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes violets and related genera such as Viola, Hybanthus, Hymenanthera, and Melicytus
the violet family of flowering plants

Examples

The Violaceae family is widely distributed across temperate and tropical regions worldwide.
Many garden plants, such as pansies, are members of the Violaceae.
Botanists study the unique floral structures found in the Violaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - any plant of the genus Viola

Etymology

From genus name Viola (violet) + standard botanical suffix -aceae (Latin, denoting a family).
