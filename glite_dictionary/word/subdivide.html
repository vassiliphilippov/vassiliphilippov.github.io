to subdivide

1. VERB

/ ˌsʌb.dɪˈvaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.dɪˈvaɪd / (BrE)
to divide something into smaller parts; to form subdivisions
divide into smaller parts or sections

Examples

For efficiency, the company decided to subdivide the project into three main phases.
As the embryo develops, its cells subdivide rapidly to form tissues.
We plan to subdivide the land into smaller plots for housing.

Synset Examples

The cells subdivided

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups

Etymology

From Latin 'subdividere': 'sub-' (under, below) + 'dividere' (to divide).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌsʌb.dɪˈvaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.dɪˈvaɪd / (BrE)
to divide something into smaller parts or sections, or to separate into even smaller parts repeatedly
divide into smaller parts

Examples

The large plot of land was subdivided into several smaller lots for new houses.
The recipe says you should subdivide the dough into twelve equal pieces before baking.
This area cannot be subdivided further due to zoning regulations.

Synset Examples

This apartment cannot be subdivided any further!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • Synonym: partition - to divide into parts or sections, often with boundaries

Etymology

From Latin 'subdividere', from 'sub-' (under, below) + 'dividere' (to divide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00333876-v
Synset POS: v
