heavily

1. ADV

/ ˈhɛvəli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvɪli / (BrE)
In a heavy-footed or weighty manner; with great weight, effort, or force.
with weight or great force

Examples

After working all day, he walked heavily towards his apartment.
The rain fell heavily all night, flooding the streets by morning.
She sighed heavily as she tried to solve the difficult problem.

Synset Examples

he walked heavily up the three flights to his room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightly - in a light or gentle manner

Etymology

From 'heavy' + '-ly', Middle English 'hevyly'.

Source: WordNet

3. ADV

/ ˈhɛvɪli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvɪli / (BrE)
With great force or weight; in a manner involving a large amount of physical impact.
with great force

Examples

He pounded the door heavily until someone answered.
The boxer fell heavily to the floor after the knockout punch.
She leaned heavily against the wall, exhausted from the effort.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From 'heavy' + '-ly', with 'heavy' from Old English 'hefig' (having weight).
