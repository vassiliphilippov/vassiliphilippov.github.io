xanthophyll

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænθəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈzanθəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a yellow carotenoid pigment found in green plants, animal fats, and egg yolks, responsible for the yellow color in some plant tissues
yellow plant pigment; a carotenoid

Examples

The bright yellow color of fall leaves is often caused by xanthophyll pigments becoming visible as chlorophyll breaks down.
Egg yolks owe part of their hue to xanthophyll present in the chicken's diet.
Researchers study xanthophyll to understand its role in photosynthesis and plant health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotenoid - any class of yellow, orange, or red pigments found in plants and some animals

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + phyllon (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈzænθəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈzanθəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a yellow carotenoid pigment found in plants, animal fats, and egg yolks, involved in the process of photosynthesis
yellow carotenoid pigment in plants and animal products

Examples

Autumn leaves get their color from pigments such as xanthophyll and carotene.
The presence of xanthophyll in egg yolks contributes to their yellow hue.
Scientists study xanthophyll to understand its role in photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotenoid - any of a class of yellow, orange, or red pigments in plants

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + phullon (leaf).
