foul-spoken

1. ADJ

/ faʊlˈspoʊkən / (AmE), / faʊlˈspəʊkən / (BrE)
Using foul, offensive, or obscene language.
using offensive or obscene language

Examples

The coach reprimanded the foul-spoken player for shouting insults during the match.
Her foul-spoken remarks shocked everyone at the formal dinner.
He was known as a blunt and foul-spoken critic of the local government.

Synset Examples

noisy foul-mouthed women all shouting at once

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foul-mouthed - using offensive or abusive language
  • Antonym: polite - showing good manners and respect for others in speech

Etymology

Formed from 'foul' (offensive) + 'spoken' (past participle of 'speak').
