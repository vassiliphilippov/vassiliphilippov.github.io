grandfather

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn(d)ˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræn(d)ˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
the father of one's father or mother
father of one's parent

Examples

My grandfather told us stories from his childhood every evening.
After retiring, he moved to the countryside to live closer to his grandfather.
She keeps a photo of her grandfather in her wallet for good luck.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grandmother - the mother of one's father or mother
  • Degree Higher: parent - a father or mother; a person's mother or father

Etymology

From Middle English 'graundfader', from Anglo-Norman 'graunt' (great) + 'fader' (father).
