finitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnəˌtud / (AmE), / ˈfɪn.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
The state or quality of having limits or bounds; the condition of being finite.
state of being finite; having limits

Examples

Philosophers have long debated the finitude of human life.
She struggled to accept the finitude of her own existence in the face of mortality.
The artwork explores themes of infinity and finitude through its use of space and boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infinitude - the state or quality of being infinite
  • Synonym: finiteness - the property of having limits; being finite

Etymology

From Latin 'finitudo' meaning 'finiteness', from 'finis' (end, limit).
