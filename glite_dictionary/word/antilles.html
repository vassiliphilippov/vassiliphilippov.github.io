Antilles

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtɪliz / (AmE), / ænˈtɪliːz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the West Indies, including the Greater Antilles and Lesser Antilles, extending from the tip of Florida to the northern coast of South America
group of islands in the West Indies

Examples

Many cruise ships visit the Antilles for their beautiful beaches and warm climate.
Hurricanes often pass through the Antilles during the summer months.
The cultures of the Antilles reflect influences from Africa, Europe, and indigenous peoples.

Etymology

From French 'Antilles', of uncertain origin; first recorded in 16th century in connection with Spanish exploration.
