to recalculate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈkælkjəleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to calculate again, especially in order to correct or update a previous calculation
calculate again

Examples

After discovering a mistake in the data, the engineer had to recalculate the total cost.
Please recalculate the figures to ensure accuracy before submitting your report.
The bank will automatically recalculate your monthly payments if interest rates change.

Synset Examples

The costs had to be recalculated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calculate - to determine by mathematical methods

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) and calculate (from Latin calculare, to count).
