automaton

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈtɑməˌtɑn / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒmətən / (BrE)
a person who acts or responds in a mechanical, unemotional, or automatic way, as if devoid of feeling or thought
person who acts mechanically or without emotion

Examples

She moved through her daily routine like an automaton, hardly noticing the world around her.
He answered every question in the meeting with the emotionless precision of an automaton.
After weeks of exhaustion, Maria felt as if she had become an automaton, simply going through the motions.

Synset Examples

only an automaton wouldn't have noticed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robot - a machine resembling a human being and able to replicate certain human movements and functions automatically

Etymology

From Greek automatos (self-moving) via Latin automaton.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtəˈmeɪtən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmeɪtən / (BrE)
a machine or mechanism that moves or acts automatically, often designed to imitate a human or animal
a self-operating machine

Examples

The museum displayed an intricate automaton that played a tiny piano.
Early automatons fascinated audiences with their lifelike movements in the 18th century.
Some clocks from the Renaissance included small automata that would chime the hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robot - a machine resembling a human and able to perform complex actions automatically

Etymology

From Greek 'automatos' (acting of itself).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02764397-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɔˈtɑməˌtɑn / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒmətən / (BrE)
a machine that moves and operates automatically, often designed to imitate human actions; a self-operating robot or device
self-operating machine or robot

Examples

The museum features a medieval automaton that rings a bell every hour.
In science fiction, automata often serve as household helpers or companions.
She watched the automaton parade gracefully across the stage, mimicking a real dancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robot - a machine capable of carrying out complex actions automatically

Etymology

From Greek automatos (self-moving).
