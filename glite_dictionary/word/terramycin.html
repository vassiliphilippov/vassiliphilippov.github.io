Terramycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈmɪsɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked yellow antibiotic drug obtained from a soil bacterium, used to treat bacterial and rickettsial infections.
antibiotic drug (trademark)
medical

Examples

The veterinarian prescribed Terramycin to treat the calf's eye infection.
You should not take Terramycin without consulting your doctor first.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to Terramycin over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxytetracycline - a broad-spectrum antibiotic derived from Terramycin

Etymology

From trademark Terramycin, combining Latin 'terra' (earth) and 'mycin' (from Greek 'mykes', fungus).
