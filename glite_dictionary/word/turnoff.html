turnoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌɒf / (BrE)
a road or path that leaves the main road to go in another direction
road leaving a main road

Examples

Take the next turnoff to get to the lake.
We almost missed the turnoff in the thick fog last night.
There's a small turnoff just past the farmhouse where you can park.

Synset Examples

I missed the turnoff and went 15 miles out of my way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit - a road leading off a main road, especially from a highway
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04507999-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn.ɒf / (BrE)
something that causes someone to lose interest, feel repelled, or become disinterested
something that causes loss of interest or attraction
informal

Examples

For me, arrogance is a major turnoff in any relationship.
Bad manners at the dinner table can be a real turnoff for guests.
His constant complaining was such a turnoff that she stopped returning his calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put-off - something that discourages or repels someone
  • Antonym: attraction - something that draws interest or attention
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05836587-n
Synset POS: n
