Yeats

1. PROPN

/ jeɪts / (AmE), / jeɪts / (BrE)
William Butler Yeats (1865–1939), an Irish poet and playwright, one of the foremost figures of 20th-century literature.
Irish poet and dramatist (1865–1939)

Examples

Yeats is known for his evocative poetry and involvement in the Irish literary revival.
The works of Yeats are studied in literature courses around the world.
Many consider Yeats a key influence on modern English poetry.
