blackberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɛri / (BrE)
A large, sweet, black or very dark purple edible fruit made up of small drupelets, produced by various plants of the genus Rubus.
edible dark purple or black fruit

Examples

She picked a ripe blackberry from the bush and ate it.
The pie was filled with sweet blackberry filling.
In late summer, the blackberry harvest is at its peak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a small, soft red or pink edible fruit from the genus Rubus
  • Degree Lower: strawberry - a sweet, soft, red fruit with a seed-studded surface

Etymology

From Old English blæcberie, literally 'black berry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbəri / (BrE)
a wild bush with prickly stems that produces small, sweet, edible black or dark purple berries
sweet dark purple or black berry; the bramble plant

Examples

We went to the woods to pick fresh blackberries for the pie.
The blackberry bush behind the shed is full of ripe fruit every summer.
She added a handful of blackberries to her morning cereal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a similar edible berry with a red or pink color

Etymology

From Old English 'blæcberie', a compound of 'blæc' (black) + 'berie' (berry).
