eschatology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛskəˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛskəˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the part of theology concerned with death, judgment, and the final destiny of humankind or the world
theology of final things or end times

Examples

Modern Christian eschatology explores the concepts of resurrection and the end of the world.
His research interests include biblical prophecy and eschatology.
Many religions have developed their own eschatology to explain what happens after death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious belief

Etymology

From Greek eschatos (last) + -logy (study of).
