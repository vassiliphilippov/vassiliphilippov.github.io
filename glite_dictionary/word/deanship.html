deanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːnˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈdiːnʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or term of a dean, especially at a college or university
position or office of a dean

Examples

After a long career as a professor, she was promoted to the deanship of the medical school.
His deanship was marked by major reforms in the curriculum and faculty diversity.
The board praised her leadership during her five-year deanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dean - the head of a college, faculty, or cathedral chapter

Etymology

From dean + -ship, the noun-forming suffix indicating position or office.
