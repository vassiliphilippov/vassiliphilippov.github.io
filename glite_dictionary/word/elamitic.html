Elamitic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæmɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɛˈlæmɪtɪk / (BrE)
an extinct language formerly spoken by the Elamites, with no known relation to other language families
extinct ancient language of the Elamites

Examples

Scholars study Elamitic texts to better understand the history of ancient Elam.
The Elamitic language was written in a cuneiform script distinct from those of its neighbors.
Few modern linguists can read Elamitic fluently due to its long extinction.

Etymology

From Elam, an ancient region in present-day Iran, plus the suffix -itic.
