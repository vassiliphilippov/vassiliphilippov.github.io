aftersensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæf.tɚ.sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑːf.tə.sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a sensation or perceptual image that continues to be experienced after the original stimulus has ended, especially a lingering image seen after looking at a bright object
lingering sensation after stimulus ends

Examples

After looking at the bright light, she experienced a green aftersensation lingering in her vision.
A strong flavor can sometimes leave an aftersensation in the mouth long after you have finished eating.
Researchers studied how long the visual aftersensation lasted following the flashing images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterimage - a visual image that continues to appear in one's vision after the exposure to the original image has ceased

Etymology

From 'after-' (following in time) + 'sensation' (feeling or perception).
