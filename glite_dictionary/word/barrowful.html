barrowful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæroʊˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊˌfʊl / (BrE)
an amount or quantity that fills a barrow (wheelbarrow)
quantity that fills a barrow

Examples

He carried a barrowful of soil to the vegetable patch.
After the storm, we removed several barrowfuls of debris from the garden.
It took only one barrowful to finish filling the flower bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovelful - the amount a shovel can hold
  • Synonym: cartload - the quantity that fills a cart

Etymology

From 'barrow' (wheelbarrow) + '-ful' (suffixed to nouns to mean 'as much as will fill').
