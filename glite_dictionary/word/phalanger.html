phalanger

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlæŋdʒər / (AmE), / fəˈlandʒə / (BrE)
A small, furry, tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia and New Guinea, typically with a long, prehensile tail.
small arboreal Australian marsupial

Examples

The phalanger uses its prehensile tail to grasp branches as it moves through the canopy.
Several species of phalanger are native to the forests of New Guinea.
At night, the phalanger emerges to forage for leaves and fruit in the treetops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possum - another name for some phalanger species, especially in Australia

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Phalanger', genus name, from Greek 'phalangion' meaning 'spider's web' (referring to webbed toes).
