movable

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmuːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
(law) A tangible item of personal property, as opposed to real estate; any object that can be moved, such as furniture, vehicles, or animals.
tangible item of personal property
legal

Examples

The estate consisted of both land and several valuable movables.
Farm equipment and livestock are typical movables listed in the inventory.
Legal disputes sometimes arise over the ownership of inherited movables.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real property - land and anything permanently attached to it
  • Synonym: chattel - an item of personal, movable property

Etymology

From Latin movibilis, from movere (to move).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmuːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmuːvəb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of personal property) able to be moved from place to place, especially by hand, as distinct from real estate.
able to be moved; not fixed in place
legal

Examples

Because the paintings are considered movable property, they can be transported to another gallery.
All movable assets should be listed separately from real estate in the inventory.
According to the contract, only movable items such as furniture and electronics are included in the sale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immovable - not able to be moved; fixed in place (especially in legal property terms)

Etymology

From Late Latin *movibilis*, from Latin *movēre* (to move).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01530181-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmuvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmuːvəb(ə)l / (BrE)
able to be moved or carried from one place to another.
able to be moved

Examples

All the movable furniture was taken out of the room during cleaning.
The exhibition displays only movable artworks that can be easily transported.
Emergency shelters are often equipped with movable partitions to create private spaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immovable - impossible to move; fixed in place

Etymology

From Latin 'movibilis', from 'movere' (to move).
