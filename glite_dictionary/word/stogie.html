stogie

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊ.ɡi / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ.ɡi / (BrE)
a cheap, usually long and thin cigar, often of low quality
a cheap cigar

Examples

He pulled a stogie from his coat pocket and lit it with a match.
The old men sat on the porch, each puffing on a stogie after dinner.
She wrinkled her nose at the smell of his cheap stogie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cigar - a cylindrical roll of tobacco for smoking

Etymology

Originally an American term, possibly from Conestoga, a type of wagon, as wagon drivers were known for smoking such cigars.
