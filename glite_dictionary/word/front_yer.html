front yer

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt jɪr / (AmE), / frʌnt jəː / (BrE)
The Cyrillic letter Ь/ь, historically used to represent a short front vowel in Old Church Slavonic and now indicating a palatalized consonant in several Slavic languages.
historical Cyrillic letter representing reduced front vowel

Examples

In Old Church Slavonic, the front yer often appeared at the end of words.
The loss of the front yer significantly changed Slavic phonology.
Modern linguists study how the front yer affected the evolution of Cyrillic orthography.

Synset Examples

The yers in weak position were lost, although not in some instances before palatalizing the preceeding consonant by the front yer b.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back yer - Cyrillic letter representing a short back vowel in Old Church Slavonic

Etymology

From Old Church Slavonic linguistic terminology; 'yer' derives from Russian ер (jer), a term for certain reduced vowels.
