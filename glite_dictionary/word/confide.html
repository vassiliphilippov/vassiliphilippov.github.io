to confide

1. VERB

/ kənˈfaɪd / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪd / (BrE)
to share a personal secret or private matter with someone, trusting them not to repeat it to others
share a secret trustingly

Examples

She felt she could confide her worries to her best friend without fear of judgment.
Many employees confide in their managers when they experience workplace difficulties.
After much hesitation, he finally confided his secret to his brother.

Synset Examples

The messenger was entrusted with the general's secret
I commit my soul to God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entrust - to give someone responsibility for something important or valuable
  • Synonym: to reveal - to make known, especially something secret or hidden
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin confidere (to have full trust), from con- (together) + fidere (to trust).

2. VERB

/ kənˈfaɪd / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪd / (BrE)
to tell someone a secret or private matter, trusting them not to repeat it to others
to tell someone something in confidence

Examples

I decided to confide in my best friend about my fears.
She would never confide such sensitive information to a stranger.
Sometimes it's a relief to confide your worries to someone you trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disclose - make secret or new information known
  • Antonym: to conceal - keep something secret; not tell

Etymology

From Latin 'confidere' meaning 'to trust fully'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00938497-v
Synset POS: v
