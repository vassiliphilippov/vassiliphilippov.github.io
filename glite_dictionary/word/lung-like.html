lung-like

1. ADJ

/ lʌŋˌlaɪk / (AmE), / lʌŋˌlaɪk / (BrE)
Having characteristics or functions similar to those of lungs, especially in enabling the exchange of gases such as oxygen and carbon dioxide.
similar to a lung in function or structure

Examples

Some amphibians have lung-like structures that enable them to breathe on land.
Certain fish possess a lung-like organ that assists with supplemental oxygen intake.
The device was designed to mimic lung-like gas exchange for medical testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulmonary - relating to the lungs
