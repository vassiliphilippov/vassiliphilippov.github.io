skirt

1. NOUN

/ skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
(in mycology) A membranous ring or collar on the stalk of some mushrooms, forming a remnant of the partial veil.
ring-like structure on mushroom stems

Examples

The edible mushroom has a distinct skirt near the top of its stem.
Look for the skirt when identifying different species of mushrooms.
The skirt protects the spores as the mushroom matures.

4. NOUN

/ skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
A piece of clothing that hangs from the waist and covers all or part of the legs, usually worn by women and girls.
women's or girls' waist-hanging garment

Examples

She wore a bright red skirt to the summer party.
The school uniform includes a navy blue skirt and a white shirt.
Long skirts became popular again in the spring collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - a one-piece garment for a woman or girl

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skyrta' (shirt), influenced by Old English 'scyrte' (shirt).
