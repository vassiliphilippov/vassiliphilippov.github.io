Iberis

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈbɪərɪs / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, commonly known as candytuft, with small white, pink, or purple flowers.
genus of plants (candytuft)

Examples

The rock garden was dotted with blooming Iberis along the borders.
She planted several Iberis to add a splash of white to her spring display.
Iberis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candytuft - Common name for various species in the genus Iberis

Etymology

From Latin Iberis, referring to Iberia (Spain), where the plant was first described.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈbɪərɪs / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, commonly known as candytuft, native to southern Europe and western Asia.
genus of flowering plants (candytuft)

Examples

The garden was bordered by blooming Iberis, adding a splash of white and purple.
Several species of Iberis are popular in rock gardens for their delicate flowers.
I planted Iberis in my flowerbed because they attract butterflies and bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candytuft - a common name for plants in the genus Iberis

Etymology

From Latin Iberis, from Greek Iberis, likely referencing Iberia (Spain/Portugal), where the plants are native.
