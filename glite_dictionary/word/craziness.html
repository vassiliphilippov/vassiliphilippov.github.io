craziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.zi.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being mentally deranged or exhibiting wild, foolish, or erratic behavior; insanity or extreme foolishness
insanity; wild or foolish behavior
informal

Examples

The level of craziness in the office during the party was unbelievable.
Her craziness sometimes makes everyday life a bit more interesting.
They were drawn to the craziness of the impromptu street performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - the state of being seriously mentally ill; madness
  • Antonym: sanity - the ability to think and behave in a normal and rational manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'crazy' + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪzinəs / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzinɪs / (BrE)
The quality of acting in a rash, foolish, or reckless manner.
rash or foolish behavior

Examples

It would be pure craziness to go hiking during the severe thunderstorm warning.
Her craziness made the trip unpredictable, but also a lot of fun.
The manager realized the craziness of approving such a risky plan.

Synset Examples

trying to drive through a blizzard is the height of folly
adjusting to an insane society is total foolishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - lack of good sense; foolishness
  • Antonym: prudence - the quality of being cautious and wise

Etymology

From 'crazy' + '-ness', with 'crazy' derived from the verb 'craze' (to shatter or break), originally from Old Norse 'krasa'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi.nəs / (BrE)
foolish, wild, or senseless behavior; the quality of being crazy.
foolish or wild behavior

Examples

No one expected such craziness at the usually quiet meeting.
Her craziness inspired everyone to be more spontaneous and creative.
The craziness of the plan made everyone laugh, even though it worked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - state of being seriously mentally ill, madness
  • Synonym: madness - the state of being mad; insanity; extreme foolishness
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind; soundness of mental faculties

Etymology

From "crazy" + "-ness" (forming nouns indicating a state or quality). "Crazy" likely from Middle English "craze" (to crack).
