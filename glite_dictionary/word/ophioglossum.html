Ophioglossum

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiəˈɡlɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˌəʊfiəˈɡlɒsəm / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Ophioglossaceae, commonly known as adder's-tongues.
genus of ferns (adder's-tongues)

Examples

The genus Ophioglossum includes some of the most unique ferns, known for their simple leaves and spore stalks.
Botanists discovered a new species of Ophioglossum in the temperate forests of Asia.
Many garden enthusiasts are fascinated by Ophioglossum due to its unusual appearance and rarity.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the shape of the frond.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.fi.oʊˈɡlɑː.səm / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.fi.əˈɡlɒs.əm / (BrE)
A genus of ferns, known as adder's-tongue ferns, and the type genus of the family Ophioglossaceae.
a genus of adder's-tongue ferns

Examples

Many species in the genus Ophioglossum are notable for their unusual leaf structure.
The spores of Ophioglossum can be found in damp meadows and forests.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Ophioglossum genus last year.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the tongue-like appearance of the plant.
