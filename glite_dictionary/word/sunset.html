sunset

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.sɛt / (BrE)
the time in the evening when the sun begins to go below the horizon and daylight starts to fade
time when sun goes below horizon

Examples

We planned to arrive at the beach before sunset to watch the beautiful colors in the sky.
The park closes every day at sunset, so we need to leave soon.
During the summer, sunset happens much later than in the winter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunrise - the time in the morning when the sun appears above the horizon

Etymology

From Old English sunne (sun) + settan (to set).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of an industry or technology) in decline; no longer growing and likely to disappear or become obsolete
in decline; becoming obsolete (industry, technology)

Examples

Many governments are reluctant to subsidize sunset industries that are no longer competitive.
As a result of technological progress, the company shifted its focus from sunset products to emerging markets.
Analysts warn that investing in sunset sectors carries higher risks due to their uncertain future.

Synset Examples

sunset industries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunrise - (of an industry or technology) new and growing rapidly

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on the sun setting, indicating an ending or decline.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01644845-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of a law, policy, or provision) specifying or providing for automatic termination after a fixed period unless extended or renewed.
providing for automatic termination
legal

Examples

The new tax credit has a sunset provision and will expire in five years unless renewed.
Many lawmakers support the inclusion of sunset clauses in regulatory programs to ensure periodic review.
Each emergency power was granted only for a sunset period, requiring legislative reauthorization to continue.

Synset Examples

a program with a sunset provision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunset clause - a provision in a law or contract that sets an expiration date unless renewed
  • Antonym: perpetual - continuing without end or termination

Etymology

Metaphorically derived from 'sunset' as the end of the day, indicating the ending of something official.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01017440-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (BrE)
the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon, and the sky often glows with light and color
evening disappearance of the sun

Examples

We watched the sunset together from the beach.
The colors at sunset were breathtaking and painted the sky in orange and pink.
By sunset, the temperature had dropped and the city lights began to twinkle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dusk - the darker stage of twilight following sunset
  • Antonym: sunrise - the time in the morning when the sun appears above the horizon
  • Degree Lower: twilight - the time before sunrise and after sunset when the sky is partially lit

Etymology

From Old English sunne (sun) + set (the act of setting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11538209-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnsɛt / (BrE)
the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon and the sky begins to darken
the daily disappearance of the sun below the horizon

Examples

We watched the sunset from the beach, enjoying the changing colors in the sky.
The park closes at sunset, so make sure to leave on time.
A beautiful sunset painted the clouds with shades of orange and pink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunrise - the daily event of the sun appearing above the horizon

Etymology

From Old English 'sunne' (sun) + 'settan' (to set).

Source: WordNet
