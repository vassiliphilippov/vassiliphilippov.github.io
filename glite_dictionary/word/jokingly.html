jokingly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒoʊkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊkɪŋli / (BrE)
in a manner intended as a joke; not meant to be taken seriously
in a joking or playful manner

Examples

She jokingly suggested we take a trip to the moon for the weekend.
He jokingly called himself the king of the office after getting a promotion.
The teacher jokingly warned the students that homework would be doubled if they kept talking.

Synset Examples

I asked him jokingly whether he thought he could drive the Calcutta-Peshawar express

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriously - in a serious manner

Etymology

Formed from 'joking' + '-ly', denoting the manner of joking.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒoʊkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊkɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that is intended as a joke; not seriously.
in a joking or playful way

Examples

She jokingly suggested that we should all wear costumes to the meeting.
He called his friend a genius, but only jokingly.
If I say something rude, please know that I mean it jokingly.

Synset Examples

I meant it facetiously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriously - in a serious manner; not jokingly
  • Synonym: facetiously - in a way that treats serious issues with deliberately inappropriate humor

Etymology

From 'joking' + '-ly', where 'joking' is from 'joke' (possibly from Latin 'jocus' meaning 'jest, joke').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086017-r
Synset POS: r
