accreditation

1. NOUN

/ əˌkrɛdəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkrɛdɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
official approval or recognition that an organization, institution, or program meets certain standards, especially in education or professional practice.
official recognition of meeting standards

Examples

The university received its accreditation from the national education board.
Without proper accreditation, the program's degrees would not be recognized by employers.
The commission is responsible for the accreditation of all medical schools in the region.

Synset Examples

a commission is responsible for the accreditation of medical schools

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certification - official process of recognizing someone as qualified
  • Antonym: disqualification - removal or denial of official approval or status

Etymology

From Medieval Latin accreditationem, from Latin 'accreditare' (to entrust).
