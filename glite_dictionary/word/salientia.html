Salientia

1. NOUN

/ sæˈliːnʃiə / (AmE), / sæˈliːnʃiə / (BrE)
the taxonomic order that includes frogs, toads, and related amphibians
order of frogs and toads

Examples

The order Salientia encompasses all modern frogs and toads.
Scientists study the anatomy of Salientia to understand amphibian evolution.
There are thousands of species within the Salientia group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anura - scientific order of tailless amphibians (frogs and toads)

Etymology

From Latin 'salire' (to leap), referring to the jumping ability of frogs and toads.
