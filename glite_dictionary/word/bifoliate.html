bifoliate

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈfoʊliət / (AmE), / bɪˈfəʊliət / (BrE)
(of a plant or organ) having two leaves or leaflets.
having two leaves

Examples

The orchid is bifoliate, bearing exactly two leaves at the top of each stem.
Many species in this region are bifoliate and can be easily identified by their paired leaves.
This plant's bifoliate structure distinguishes it from its trifoliate relatives.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: unifoliate - having a single leaf or leaflet
  • Degree Higher: trifoliate - having three leaves or leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'folium' (leaf).
