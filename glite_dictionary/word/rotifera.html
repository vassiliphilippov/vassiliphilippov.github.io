Rotifera

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈtɪfərə / (AmE), / rəʊˈtɪfərə / (BrE)
A phylum of microscopic aquatic animals, commonly known as rotifers, found in freshwater and some saltwater environments.
phylum of microscopic aquatic animals

Examples

Scientists discovered several species of Rotifera in the pond sample.
The phylum Rotifera is important in aquatic food webs.
Many members of Rotifera have a wheel-like structure used for feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotifer - a microscopic aquatic animal belonging to the phylum Rotifera

Etymology

From New Latin 'Rotifera', from Latin 'rota' (wheel) + 'fer' (bearing), referring to the wheel-like ciliated organs.
