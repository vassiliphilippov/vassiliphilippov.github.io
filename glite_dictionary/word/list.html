list

1. NOUN

/ lɪst / (AmE), / lɪst / (BrE)
an ordered series of items, names, or topics, typically written or printed together, often for reference or organization
an ordered collection of items or names

Examples

Please add your name to the list at the front desk if you wish to attend.
She checked the grocery list before heading to the supermarket.
The teacher posted a list of students who received awards on the bulletin board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalog - a complete list of items, often with details
  • Synonym: inventory - a list of items, especially in stock or possession

Etymology

From Old French *liste* (border, strip), later meaning a record or catalog.

3. NOUN

/ lɪst / (AmE), / lɪst / (BrE)
a leaning or tilting to one side, especially of a ship or upright structure
a tilt or slant to one side

Examples

The old cargo ship developed a heavy list to port after the storm.
With its pronounced list, the leaning tower shocked tourists.
After the collision, the yacht had a noticeable list to starboard.

Synset Examples

the tower had a pronounced tilt
the ship developed a list to starboard
he walked with a heavy inclination to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - a sloping position or movement
  • Synonym: inclination - a tendency to move, act, or feel in a particular way or direction, or the fact of sloping
  • Antonym: uprightness - the state of being perfectly vertical or not leaning

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse 'lista' (to bend or tilt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05075249-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ lɪst / (AmE), / lɪst / (BrE)
(of a ship or boat) to lean or tilt to one side, especially unintentionally.
tilt or lean to one side (of a vessel)

Examples

The old ferry began to list dangerously as passengers rushed to one side.
When water flooded the cargo hold, the ship started to list to port.
In heavy winds, the yacht may list until the sails nearly touch the water.

Synset Examples

The balloon heeled over
the wind made the vessel heel
The ship listed to starboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heel - (of a ship or boat) to tilt over to one side

Etymology

Perhaps from earlier 'list' meaning border or edge; origin uncertain, but in this sense recorded since the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02042636-v
Synset POS: v

(of a ship or object) to lean or cause to lean to one side, typically as a result of imbalance or external force.

Examples

  • Synonym: lean - to rest or cause to rest at an incline
