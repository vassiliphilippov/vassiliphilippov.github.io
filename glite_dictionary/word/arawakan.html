Arawakan

1. ADJ

/ əˈrɑːwəkən / (AmE), / əˈraʊəkən / (BrE)
relating to the peoples who speak the language of the Arawak, or to their languages
of the Arawak-speaking peoples or their languages

Examples

The Arawakan languages are widely distributed across northern South America.
Anthropologists study the Arawakan cultures to understand pre-Columbian history.
The Arawakan population was profoundly impacted by European colonization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arawak - a member of a group of indigenous peoples of South America and the Caribbean

Etymology

From Arawak, the name of the indigenous people and their language of northern South America and the Caribbean, with the suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈwɑːkən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈwɑːkən / (BrE)
A member of a large family of South American Indigenous languages formerly spoken in a broad region of northeastern South America.
family of South American Indigenous languages

Examples

Several tribes in the Amazon basin spoke Arawakan languages before European contact.
Linguists have studied the influence of Arawakan languages on modern South American dialects.
The Arawakan language family includes a wide variety of dialects spread across different countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tupi-Guarani - another major family of South American Indigenous languages

Etymology

From Arawak, the name of one of the languages in the family and its people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06930540-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈwɑːkən / (AmE), / ˌarəˈwakən / (BrE)
a member of a group of Indigenous peoples inhabiting northeastern South America and parts of the Caribbean
Indigenous peoples of South America and the Caribbean

Examples

Anthropologists have studied several Arawakan communities along the Amazon River.
The Arawakans developed complex agricultural systems before European contact.
Many Caribbean islands were originally inhabited by Arawakan peoples.

Etymology

From Spanish 'arahuaco', of Indigenous origin, referring to the group.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09822670-n
Synset POS: n
