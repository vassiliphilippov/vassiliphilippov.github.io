Botaurus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtɔːrəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large bitterns found in wetlands, belonging to the heron family.
genus of large bittern birds

Examples

The American Botaurus is known for its distinctive booming call during the breeding season.
Wetlands conservation efforts help protect the habitat of the rare Botaurus populations.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Botaurus among the reeds at dawn.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Latin 'būtio' (a kind of bird) and possibly 'taurus' (bull), referring to the deep booming calls of these birds.

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈtɔːrəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large wading birds in the heron family, known as bitterns, found in temperate regions.
genus of large bitterns (birds)

Examples

The Botaurus is well known for its deep, booming call during mating season.
Several species within the Botaurus genus inhabit marshlands across North America and Europe.
Zoologists observed a Botaurus blending almost perfectly into the reeds.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'bous' (ox) + Latin 'taurus' (bull), possibly referring to the booming sound these birds make.
