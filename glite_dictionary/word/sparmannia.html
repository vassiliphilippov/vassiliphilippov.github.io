Sparmannia

1. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈmæniə / (AmE), / spɑːˈmænɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African shrubs in the mallow family, known for their large leaves and showy flowers.
genus of tropical African shrubs

Examples

The Sparmannia is prized in botanical gardens for its unusual foliage and flowers.
We studied the pollination mechanisms of various Sparmannia species during our research trip.
A specimen of Sparmannia can be found in the greenhouse section of the city’s botanical garden.

Etymology

Named after Anders Sparrman, a Swedish naturalist.

2. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈmɑːniə / (AmE), / spɑːˈmánɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African shrubs in the mallow family, known for their ornamental foliage and flowers.
genus of African shrubs

Examples

Several species in the Sparmannia genus are valued for their decorative flowers.
Botanists discovered a new Sparmannia shrub during the African expedition.
The leaves of Sparmannia are often large and softly hairy.

Etymology

Named after Anders Sparrman, Swedish naturalist (1748–1820).
