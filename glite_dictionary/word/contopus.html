Contopus

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtoʊpəs / (AmE), / kɒnˈtəʊpəs / (BrE)
a genus of small insect-eating birds known as pewees, found in the Americas
genus of birds called pewees

Examples

The Contopus genus includes several species of pewees widely distributed across North and South America.
Bird watchers spotted a rare Contopus flycatcher near the lake early this morning.
Many members of the Contopus genus migrate seasonally between North and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pewee - a small North and South American flycatcher bird

Etymology

From Greek kontos (pole, staff) + pous (foot), referring to the shape of the bird's legs.
