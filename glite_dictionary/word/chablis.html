Chablis

1. NOUN

/ ʃæˈbli / (AmE), / ʃaˈbli / (BrE)
A dry white wine from Chablis in France, or a similar wine made elsewhere.
dry white wine from Chablis, France

Examples

She ordered a glass of Chablis to go with her seafood dinner.
This restaurant offers an excellent selection of French Chablis.
They toasted their anniversary with a bottle of crisp, chilled Chablis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chardonnay - white wine grape variety and the main grape of Chablis wines

Etymology

From French Chablis, the name of the region in Burgundy, France, where the wine is produced.
