Oread

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːriˌæd / (AmE), / ˈɔːriˌad / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a mountain nymph; a female spirit believed to inhabit mountains and rocky hills.
mountain nymph in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient poetry, an Oread was depicted as dancing gracefully among the mountain peaks.
The statue in the garden was inspired by the legend of the Oread.
Scholars often debate whether an Oread could be considered a minor goddess or a nature spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nymph - a minor female nature deity in Greek and Roman mythology

Etymology

From Greek 'oreias' (mountain nymph), from 'oros' (mountain).
