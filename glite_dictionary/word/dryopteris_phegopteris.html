Dryopteris phegopteris

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑːptərɪs fiːˈgɒptərɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs fiːˈgɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A species of fern, commonly known as beech fern, found in North America and Eurasia.
species of beech fern

Examples

The Dryopteris phegopteris thrives in moist woodland areas in northern Europe and North America.
Botanists often study Dryopteris phegopteris for its distinctive triangular fronds.
Gardeners value Dryopteris phegopteris for its delicate appearance and shade tolerance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech fern - A common name for Dryopteris phegopteris and related species of ferns

Etymology

From Greek: 'dryas' (oak nymph) and 'pteris' (fern), with 'phegopteris' referring to beech trees (pēgon).
