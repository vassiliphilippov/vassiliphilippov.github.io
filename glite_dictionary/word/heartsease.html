heartsease

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtsˌiz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtsˌiːz / (BrE)
A violet of the Pacific coast of North America, having white petals tinged with yellow and deep violet; also known as a wild pansy.
Pacific coast violet with multicolored petals

Examples

The delicate heartsease flourished in the coastal gardens all spring long.
She pressed a heartsease between the pages of her favorite book as a keepsake.
Botanists have identified several distinct patterns on the petals of the heartsease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild pansy - another name for heartsease, a species of violet

Etymology

Heartsease is an English compound; so named from its traditional use to relieve heartbreak or sadness.

/ ˈhɑrtsˌiz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtsˌiːz / (BrE)
A small wild European plant of the violet family, typically having flowers of yellow, white, and purple; also known as wild pansy or Viola tricolor.
a small wild European plant (Viola tricolor)

Examples

Heartsease grows wild in meadows and along roadsides throughout much of Europe.
Gardeners sometimes plant heartsease for its attractive tricolored blossoms.
The old herbalists believed that heartsease could soothe troubled feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pansy - a cultivated garden viola with large colorful flowers
