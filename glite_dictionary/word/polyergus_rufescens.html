Polyergus rufescens

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɪərɡəs ruːˈfɛsɛnz / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪəɡəs ruːˈfɛsɛnz / (BrE)
a small reddish species of ant known for enslaving ants of other species to work in their colonies
a slave-making reddish ant species

Examples

The colony of Polyergus rufescens invaded a neighboring nest to capture new workers.
Researchers observed that Polyergus rufescens relies on other ant species to maintain its colony.
The distinctive reddish color of Polyergus rufescens makes it easy to identify in the field.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Polyergus' is the genus name from Greek meaning 'much working', 'rufescens' means 'reddish' in Latin.
