Eucalyptus citriodora

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkʌlɪptəs sɪˌtrioʊˈdɔrə / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs sɪˌtrɪəʊˈdɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of Australian tree with smooth bark and lemon-scented leaves, often called the lemon-scented gum.
lemon-scented Australian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The avenue was lined with majestic Eucalyptus citriodora, their scent filling the air after the rain.
She planted a Eucalyptus citriodora in her garden for its fragrant leaves and decorative bark.
Many essential oils are derived from the leaves of Eucalyptus citriodora due to their strong lemon aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon-scented gum - common name for Eucalyptus citriodora

Etymology

From Latinized genus Eucalyptus (from Greek eu- 'well' + kalyptos 'covered') and species citriodora (Latin for 'lemon-scented', from 'citrus' and 'odor').
