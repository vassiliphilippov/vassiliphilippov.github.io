cottonwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌwʊd / (BrE)
A tree of the Allegheny region, also known as the American basswood, valued for its soft, light wood and heart-shaped leaves.
tree (Allegheny region), American basswood

Examples

The forests of the region abound with cottonwood and maple trees.
Craftsmen often use cottonwood for carving due to its soft wood.
A large cottonwood shaded the path behind the farmhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basswood - a North American linden tree with soft light wood

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌwʊd / (BrE)
a North American tree of the poplar family, especially Populus deltoides, with seeds that have tufts of cottony hairs
North American poplar tree with cottony seeds

Examples

The cottonwood trees along the river provide valuable shade for hikers.
In spring, the seeds of the cottonwood drift through the air like fluffy snow.
A large cottonwood stood at the edge of the old farmland, its branches swaying in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a tree of the genus Populus, related to the cottonwood

Etymology

Named for its seeds' resemblance to cotton, combining 'cotton' and 'wood'.
