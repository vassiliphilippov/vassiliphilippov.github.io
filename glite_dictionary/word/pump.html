pump

1. VERB

/ pʌmp / (AmE), / pʌmp / (BrE)
To move up and down repeatedly and forcefully, especially in a regular or rhythmic manner.
move up and down repeatedly

Examples

He pumped iron every morning to build his strength.
The children pumped their legs to swing higher in the playground.
She pumped her arms as she ran up the hill.

Synset Examples

The athlete pumps weights in the gym

2. VERB

/ pʌmp / (AmE), / pʌmp / (BrE)
to move or transfer liquid or gas by using a pump device
move liquid/gas with a pump

Examples

Workers began to pump water out of the flooded basement immediately.
Engineers used a generator to pump oil from the underground reservoir.
She had to pump air into the tires before riding her bike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siphon - to draw liquid from one container to another using a tube

Etymology

From Middle English *pompe*, borrowed from Middle Dutch *pomp*, of imitative origin.
