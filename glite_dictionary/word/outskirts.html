outskirts

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌskɝts / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌskɜːts / (BrE)
the outer parts of a town or city, farthest from the center
outer areas of a city or town

Examples

Many factories are located on the outskirts of the city.
She preferred to live on the outskirts rather than in the busy downtown area.
The hotel is situated in the outskirts, offering a peaceful environment away from the center.

Synset Examples

they lived on the outskirts of Houston
they mingled in the outskirts of the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suburb - an area on the edge of a city, usually residential
  • Antonym: center - the central part of a city or town

Etymology

From out + skirt, originally meaning the border or fringes of something.
