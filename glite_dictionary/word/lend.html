to lend

1. VERB

/ lɛnd / (AmE), / lɛnd / (BrE)
(figurative) To give or impart a particular quality, characteristic, or atmosphere to something.
impart a quality or character

Examples

Her calm voice lent an air of dignity to the ceremony.
The old paintings lend the room a sense of history.
His humor lends warmth to the team's meetings.

Synset Examples

Her presence lends a certain cachet to the company
The music added a lot to the play
She brings a special atmosphere to our meetings
This adds a light note to the program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impart - to give a quality to something or someone
  • Synonym: to bring - to cause a quality or atmosphere to be present

Etymology

From Old English *lēnan* (to lend), related to the concept of giving temporarily.

2. VERB

/ lɛnd / (AmE), / lɛnd / (BrE)
to give something to someone for a short time, expecting it to be returned
give temporarily, expecting return

Examples

Can you lend me your notes for the class?
She lent her umbrella to a coworker during the storm.
I'm always happy to lend a hand when someone needs help.

Synset Examples

I will lend you my car
loan me some money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to borrow - to take and use something belonging to someone else with the intention of returning it
  • Synonym: loan - to lend something, especially money, to someone

Etymology

From Old English 'lǣnan' (to lend, let have), related to 'loan'.

Synset POS: v
