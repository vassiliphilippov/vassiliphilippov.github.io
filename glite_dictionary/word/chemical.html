chemical

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or used in chemistry, or involving the properties or reactions of substances
relating to chemistry

Examples

The chemical structure of water consists of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
She works as a chemical engineer in a pharmaceutical company.
Strict safety protocols are required when handling chemical substances.

Synset Examples

chemical engineer
chemical balance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physical - relating to physics or physical properties, often contrasted with 'chemical' in sciences

Etymology

From French *chimique*, from Latin *chimicus* (alchemist), ultimately from Greek *khēmia* (alchemy).

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
A substance produced by or used in a chemical process, especially one created or manipulated for specific use.
substance produced by or used in chemistry

Examples

Always store chemicals in properly labeled containers away from food.
The laboratory tested the water for toxic chemicals after the spill.
Many household cleaning products contain strong chemicals that can irritate your skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - a substance formed from two or more elements chemically united

Etymology

From French *chimique*, from New Latin *chemicus* (chemist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14831008-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, derived from, or involving the use of substances created through or used in chemical reactions.
relating to substances formed by chemistry

Examples

Many farmers now prefer chemical fertilizers to improve crop yields.
The factory uses several chemical processes to manufacture plastics.
We studied the chemical properties of water in science class.

Synset Examples

chemical fertilizer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic - relating to or derived from living matter or carbon compounds, often contrasted with chemical in everyday use

Etymology

From French *chimique*, from Late Latin *chimicus* (alchemist), from Arabic *kīmiyāʾ* (alchemy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02703703-a
Synset POS: a
