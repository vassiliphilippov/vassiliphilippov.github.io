T-bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌbɑː / (BrE)
A type of ski lift consisting of a T-shaped bar attached to a moving cable, which skiers place behind their hips or between their legs to be pulled uphill.
T-shaped ski lift for pulling skiers uphill

Examples

The ski resort installed a new T-bar to help skiers reach the higher slopes.
When the crowds were large, the T-bar moved more people uphill than the chairlift.
It took her a few tries to get comfortable using the T-bar without falling.

Etymology

Named for the T-shaped bar that skiers hold while being pulled uphill.
