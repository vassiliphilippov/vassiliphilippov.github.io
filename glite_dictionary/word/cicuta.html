Cicuta

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjuːtə / (AmE), / sɪˈkjuːtə / (BrE)
a small genus of highly poisonous perennial herbs, known as water hemlocks, with tuberous roots
genus of poisonous plants (water hemlock)

Examples

The roots of Cicuta contain a potent toxin that can be deadly if ingested.
Many cases of livestock poisoning are traced to Cicuta growing near streams and wetlands.
Botanists warn that Cicuta is often mistaken for edible plants in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hemlock - a common name for plants of the Cicuta genus
  • Synonym: hemlock - a similar but distinct poisonous plant

Etymology

From Latin Cicuta (hemlock), the classical name for the plant.
