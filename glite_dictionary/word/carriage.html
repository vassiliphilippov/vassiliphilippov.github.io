carriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
the way in which a person holds and moves their body; posture or bearing
way of carrying the body; posture

Examples

The dancer's graceful carriage impressed everyone in the audience.
Good carriage is important for making a strong impression at a job interview.
He walked into the room with a confident carriage that drew everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

stood with good posture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posture - the position in which someone holds their body
  • Synonym: bearing - a person's way of standing or moving

Etymology

From Old Northern French 'cariage', related to 'carier' meaning 'to carry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part of a machine that supports and moves another component, especially in devices such as typewriters or printers
machine part that supports/moves another component

Examples

The typewriter's carriage moved smoothly as she typed each letter.
The printer jam was caused by an obstruction in the carriage assembly.
Technicians replaced the carriage to restore proper function to the plotter.

Etymology

From Old Northern French 'cariage', from 'carier' (to carry).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
A vehicle with wheels, usually pulled by one or more horses, used for carrying people.
horse-drawn passenger vehicle

Examples

The queen rode through the streets in a splendid carriage pulled by four white horses.
They hired a horse-drawn carriage for their wedding day.
Children gathered on the sidewalk to watch the old-fashioned carriage pass by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - a large, closed, four-wheeled horse-drawn carriage
  • Synonym: wagon - a vehicle with four wheels, used for transporting goods or passengers

Etymology

From Old Northern French 'cariage', from 'carier' (to carry).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small vehicle with four wheels in which a baby or young child is pushed around
baby stroller or pram

Examples

We took the baby for a walk in his carriage around the neighborhood.
Please fold up the carriage before getting on the bus.
The old-fashioned carriage had large wheels and a canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pram - a British term for a baby carriage
  • Synonym: stroller - an American term for a type of baby carriage (upright, often collapsible)

Etymology

From Old Northern French 'cariage' (act of carrying), related to 'carier' (to carry).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a separate vehicle or compartment of a train for carrying passengers
passenger compartment of a train
british

Examples

The train was composed of several carriages, each reserved for different classes of passengers.
She found an empty seat in the last carriage and settled in for the journey.
Smoking is prohibited in every carriage on this service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - a railway carriage, especially one for passengers
  • Antonym: freight car - a railcar for carrying goods rather than passengers

Etymology

From Old Northern French cariage (act of carrying), from carier (to carry).
