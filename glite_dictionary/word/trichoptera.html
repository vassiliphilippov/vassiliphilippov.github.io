Trichoptera

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑp.tə.rə / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒp.tə.rə / (BrE)
an order of insects comprising the caddis flies, characterized by hairy wings and aquatic larvae
order of insects including caddis flies

Examples

The larvae of Trichoptera are well known for building protective cases from materials found in freshwater habitats.
Entomologists often study Trichoptera to assess water quality in streams and rivers.
A diversity of Trichoptera species can be found in temperate and tropical freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddis fly - a common name for insects of the order Trichoptera

Etymology

From Greek trikhos (hair) + pteron (wing), referring to the hairy wings of these insects.
