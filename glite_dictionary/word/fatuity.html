fatuity

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtuː.ə.ti / (AmE), / fəˈtjuː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
foolishness, stupidity, or a ludicrously foolish action or idea
foolishness; ludicrous folly

Examples

The fatuity of his argument was apparent to everyone in the room.
She regretted her moment of fatuity during the heated debate.
Historians now look back on that era's grandiose schemes as pure fatuity.

Synset Examples

the crowd laughed at the absurdity of the clown's behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - foolishness; lack of good sense
  • Synonym: absurdity - something ridiculously unreasonable or foolish
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

From Latin fatuus (foolish, silly).
