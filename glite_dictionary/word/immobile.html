immobile

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbaɪl / (BrE)
securely fixed in place; not able to be moved.
securely fixed; unable to move

Examples

The sculpture was completely immobile on its stone pedestal.
Heavy frost made the car's wheels immobile overnight.
After the repairs, the fence post was immobile and sturdy.

Synset Examples

the post was still firm after being hit by the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stationary - not moving; not intended to be moved
  • Antonym: mobile - able to move or be moved freely or easily

Etymology

From Latin 'immobilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'mobilis' (movable).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbaɪl / (BrE)
unable to move or be moved; not moving or motionless.
unable to move or be moved

Examples

After the accident, he was immobile for weeks while his injuries healed.
A large, immobile statue stood in the center of the garden.
She sat immobile with shock, unable to respond to the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionless - not moving; still
  • Synonym: immovable - impossible to move; firmly fixed
  • Antonym: mobile - able to move or be moved freely or easily

Etymology

From Latin immōbilis (not movable), from in- (not) + mōbilis (movable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01529235-a
Synset POS: a
