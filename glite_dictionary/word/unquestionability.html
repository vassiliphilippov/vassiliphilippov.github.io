unquestionability

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˌkwɛstʃənəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ʌnˌkwɛstʃənəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to doubt or dispute
quality of being beyond doubt

Examples

The unquestionability of his reputation made him a trusted leader.
Many admired the unquestionability of her scientific findings.
The lawyer emphasized the unquestionability of the evidence to the jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or sure
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

Formed from 'unquestionable' + '-ity', by analogy with similar abstract nouns.
