to discolour

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkʌlər / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to change or cause to change in color, usually in an undesirable way
change in color, especially undesirably
british

Examples

If the curtains are exposed to too much sunlight, they may discolour over time.
The chemicals in the water can discolour your white clothes.
Her silver ring started to discolour after a few months of use.

Synset Examples

The shirts discolored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to lose brightness or vividness of color
  • Synonym: stain - to mark or discolor with something that is not easily removed
  • Degree Higher: bleach - to make something whiter or lighter in color

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (removal) + 'color'.
