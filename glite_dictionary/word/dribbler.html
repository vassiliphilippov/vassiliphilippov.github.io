dribbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪb.lə / (BrE)
a player who moves the ball along by repeatedly bouncing or tapping it, especially in basketball or soccer
player advancing ball by dribbling

Examples

The point guard was an excellent dribbler who could move past any defender.
As a young soccer player, he developed into a skillful dribbler.
The coach praised her for being the fastest dribbler on the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribble - to move a ball by repeated small bounces or taps

Etymology

From 'dribble' (to move a ball by repeated small bounces or taps) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪb.lə / (BrE)
a person, especially a player, who dribbles; someone who moves a ball by repeatedly bouncing or controlling it, or a person (such as a baby) who lets saliva or liquid drip from their mouth
person who dribbles a ball or saliva

Examples

The young soccer player was an excellent dribbler and could get past any defender.
Her baby is quite a dribbler and always needs to wear a bib.
Among the basketball team, Jordan is the best dribbler with the ball.

Synset Examples

that baby is a dribbler; he needs a bib

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribble - to move or let fall in drops, or to control the ball repeatedly in sports

Etymology

From the verb 'dribble' + '-er', denoting one who performs the action.
