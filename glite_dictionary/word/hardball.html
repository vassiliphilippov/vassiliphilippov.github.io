hardball

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A form of baseball played with a hard ball, used especially to distinguish it from softball.
baseball, as opposed to softball

Examples

The school offers both hardball and softball leagues for students.
After playing hardball for years, she found softball to be much easier.
Many professional athletes started their sports careers playing hardball as children.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdbɔːl / (BrE)
a tough and uncompromising approach, especially in business or politics
tough, uncompromising approach

Examples

When it comes to negotiations, she always plays hardball and never backs down.
The company decided to take a hardball approach during the contract talks.
Sometimes you have to play hardball to get what you want in politics.

Synset Examples

they play hardball in the Senate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softball - a more conciliatory approach, especially in business or politics

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense in baseball, referring to playing with a standard hard ball as opposed to softball.
