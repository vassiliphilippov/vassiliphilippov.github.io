bruxism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌk.sɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈbrʌk.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The involuntary or unconscious clenching or grinding of the teeth, usually during sleep.
involuntary teeth grinding
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed him with bruxism after noticing excessive wear on his teeth.
Night guards are often prescribed to patients suffering from bruxism.
Chronic bruxism can lead to jaw pain and headaches.

Etymology

From French 'bruxisme', from Greek 'brychein' (to gnash the teeth).
