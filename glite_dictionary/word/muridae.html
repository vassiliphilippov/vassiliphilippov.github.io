Muridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjʊrɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈmjʊərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the largest family of rodents, including Old World rats and mice, now found worldwide, distinguished from related families by typically lacking cheek pouches
family of rodents including rats and mice

Examples

The Muridae are the most diverse family of mammals, with species adapted to many environments.
Researchers studied the genetic variations present in several Muridae species found in Southeast Asia.
Unlike other rodent families, Muridae typically lack cheek pouches, distinguishing them from the Cricetidae.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Cricetidae - another family of rodents distinguished by having cheek pouches

Etymology

From New Latin Muridae, from Latin mus (mouse) + -idae (taxonomic family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjʊrɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of rodents including true mice and rats, originally native to the Old World but now found worldwide, typically lacking cheek pouches
family of rodents including mice and rats
medical

Examples

Biologists have identified several new species within the Muridae family in Southeast Asia.
The Muridae are the largest family of mammals, comprising over 700 species of rodents.
Members of the Muridae lack cheek pouches, unlike their relatives in the Cricetidae family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Cricetidae - a family of rodents including hamsters and voles, often possessing cheek pouches

Etymology

From New Latin Muridae, from Latin mus, muris (mouse).
