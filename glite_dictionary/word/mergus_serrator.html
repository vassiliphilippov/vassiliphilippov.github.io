Mergus serrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrɡəs səˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːɡəs səˈreɪtə / (BrE)
A widely distributed species of merganser duck, known as the red-breasted merganser, found in North America, Europe, and Asia.
species of merganser duck

Examples

The Mergus serrator is recognizable by its spiky crest and slender, serrated bill.
During migration, flocks of Mergus serrator can often be seen along the coastal waters.
The feeding habits of Mergus serrator include diving for fish in both freshwater and marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-breasted merganser - common name for Mergus serrator

Etymology

From Latin: mergus (diver, a kind of waterbird) and serrator (one who saws), referring to the bird's serrated bill.
