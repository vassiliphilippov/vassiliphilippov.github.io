giving

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of voluntarily transferring property or money to another without expecting anything in return; especially charitable donations.
the act of making voluntary donations or gifts

Examples

Many organizations rely on giving from the public to fund their work.
The annual giving campaign raised a record amount this year.
Her family's tradition of giving inspired her to donate to several charities.

Synset Examples

the alumni followed a program of annual giving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something given to a charity or cause
  • Synonym: gift - something given voluntarily without payment
  • Antonym: receiving - the act of getting something from someone

Etymology

From the verb 'give', originating from Old English 'giefan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00091599-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of providing something to someone, especially as a gift or as an act of generosity
the act of providing or donating

Examples

Her generosity is shown through her selfless giving to those in need.
The holiday season is often associated with giving and kindness.
They recognized his years of giving to community organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - the act of giving money or goods to help a person or organization
  • Antonym: receiving - the act of accepting or getting something that is given

Etymology

From Middle English 'yiven' (to give), from Old English 'giefan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01088005-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of imparting news, information, promises, or similar intangible things to others
the act of imparting news or promises

Examples

The giving of his word meant a lot to everyone in the room.
She celebrated the giving of the good news with her friends.
The ceremony included the formal giving of several important announcements.

Synset Examples

he gave us the news and made a great show of the giving
giving his word of honor seemed to come too easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presentation - the act of presenting something, such as information or news

Etymology

From Old English *giefan* (to give), with suffix *-ing* forming the noun.

Source: WordNet

/ ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) generous, willing to give or share freely with others.
generous; willing to share or give

Examples

She is a very giving person who always puts others before herself.
His giving nature made him beloved by everyone in the community.
Volunteering at the shelter brings out the most giving side of people.

Related Terms
