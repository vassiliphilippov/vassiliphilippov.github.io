ending

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the final part of a word, especially a suffix or inflection that is added to change its form or meaning
final part of a word, suffix or inflection

Examples

The plural ending -s is common in English.
She explained the grammatical function of the verb's ending.
The word 'happiness' has the ending '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to a close or an end; the conclusion or termination of something
the act of concluding or finishing

Examples

The concert's ending was met with a standing ovation from the audience.
After hours of negotiations, the ending of the meeting was abrupt and tense.
Their long friendship had a bitter ending when they finally parted ways.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termination - the act of ending something
  • Antonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts

Etymology

From Old English endung, formed from end + -ing (verbal noun suffix).
