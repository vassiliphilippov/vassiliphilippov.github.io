Balanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæ.lə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbæ.lə.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of barnacles belonging to the family Balanidae, commonly found attached to rocks and ship hulls in marine environments.
genus of barnacles

Examples

Balanus species are frequently studied for their ability to attach to hard surfaces in the ocean.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the genus Balanus on the rocky coast.
The hull of the ship was encrusted with Balanus barnacles after months at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a marine crustacean that attaches to surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'balanus', meaning 'acorn', due to the acorn-like shape of the shells.
