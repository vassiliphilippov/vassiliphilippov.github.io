sarin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrɪn / (AmE), / ˈsærɪn / (BrE)
a highly toxic, colorless, and odorless nerve agent used as a chemical weapon, which inhibits cholinesterase activity in the nervous system.
highly toxic nerve agent
medical

Examples

The use of sarin in warfare is considered a violation of international law.
Exposure to even a small amount of sarin can be fatal to humans.
Scientists have developed antidotes to counteract the effects of sarin poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve gas - a chemical weapon that disrupts the transmission of nerve impulses

Etymology

From German Sarin, an acronym formed from the names of the chemists Schrader, Ambros, Rüdiger, and Van der Linde who discovered it.
