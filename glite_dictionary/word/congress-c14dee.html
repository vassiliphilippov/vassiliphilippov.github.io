congress

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
a formal meeting or assembly of elected or appointed representatives, especially to discuss or make decisions on important matters
formal meeting of representatives

Examples

A national congress was held to debate constitutional reforms.
Delegates from each region attended the congress to represent their constituents.
The annual scientific congress attracts researchers from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together for a common purpose
  • Synonym: session - a formal meeting for discussion or decision-making

Etymology

From Latin 'congressus' (a meeting), from 'congredi' (to meet), from 'com-' (together) + 'gradi' (to step, go).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋ.ɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.ɡrɛs / (BrE)
a formal meeting or gathering of people for discussion, decision-making, or some common purpose
formal meeting or assembly

Examples

Delegates gathered at the annual congress to debate policy changes within the organization.
After months of planning, a congress was held to unify the different environmental groups.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
The national legislative body of a country, especially the legislative branch of the United States government.
national legislative assembly

Examples

The new policy was debated extensively in congress before it was passed.
He was elected to Congress in 2018 and served two terms.
Every year, congress convenes to address the nation’s most pressing issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parliament - the highest legislative authority in a country, similar to congress

Etymology

From Latin 'congressus' (a meeting, encounter), from 'congredi' (to meet, come together).
