rollicking

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑːlɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɒlɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
lively, cheerful, and full of fun; behaving in an exuberant or high-spirited way
exuberantly lively; high-spirited

Examples

The children enjoyed a rollicking afternoon at the park, laughing and chasing each other for hours.
We watched a rollicking comedy that had the whole audience roaring with laughter.
Their rollicking performance brought energy and joy to the entire festival.

Synset Examples

frolicsome students celebrated their graduation with parties and practical jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolicsome - playful and full of fun

Etymology

Probably from 'frolic' plus -ing, with influence from 'rollick,' a variant blending 'roll' and 'frolic.'
