brickbat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪkˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪkˌbæt / (BrE)
A fragment of brick, especially one used as a weapon to throw at someone or something.
fragment of brick used as a weapon

Examples

During the protest, someone hurled a brickbat at the police line.
The broken window was caused by a brickbat thrown from the street.
He dodged a brickbat that was aimed directly at his head.

Etymology

From 'brick' + 'bat', with 'bat' meaning a piece or lump.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪkˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪkˌbæt / (BrE)
a critical or insulting remark; a sharp or blunt criticism
critical or insulting remark

Examples

The politician received the usual brickbats from his critics after the debate.
Instead of praise, her remarks attracted a volley of brickbats.
He shrugged off the brickbats and focused on improving his work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compliment - an expression of praise, approval, or admiration
  • Synonym: insult - an offensive remark or action

Etymology

Originally meant a fragment of a brick used as a missile; figurative use as 'criticism' dates from the 19th century.
