chimneysweep

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪmniˌswip / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmniˌswiːp / (BrE)
a person whose job is to clean soot and ash from chimneys
person who cleans chimneys

Examples

The chimneysweep visited our house every winter to clean the fireplace.
In Victorian England, a chimneysweep was a common sight in the city streets.
After the chimneysweep finished his work, the living room smelled of soot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney sweep - person who cleans chimneys; alternate spacing

Etymology

From 'chimney' (from Old French 'cheminée') + 'sweep' (to clean, from Old English 'swāpan').
