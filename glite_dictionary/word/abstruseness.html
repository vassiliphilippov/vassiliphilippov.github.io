abstruseness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstruːsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstruːsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being difficult to understand; profound and obscure complexity.
difficulty of understanding; obscurity

Examples

The abstruseness of the philosopher's writing made it challenging for many students to follow.
Critics admired the abstruseness in her poetry, saying it revealed deeper meanings upon closer reading.
Some people appreciate the abstruseness of advanced mathematics, while others find it frustrating.

Synset Examples

the anthropologist was impressed by the reconditeness of the native proverbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'abstrusus' (concealed, hidden), from 'abstrudere' (to hide).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈstruːsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstruːsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being difficult to understand; obscurity or complexity in meaning or expression.
the quality of being hard to understand

Examples

The abstruseness of the professor's lecture left many students feeling confused.
Some literary critics enjoy the abstruseness of experimental novels, while others find it frustrating.
The abstruseness of the instructions made assembling the furniture a real challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unclear or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being easy to understand

Etymology

From 'abstruse' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'abstrusus' (hidden, concealed).
