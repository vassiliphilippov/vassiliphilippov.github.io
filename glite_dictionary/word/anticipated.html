anticipated

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪtɪd / (BrE)
regarded as likely or expected to happen, often with a sense of hopeful expectation
expected or awaited, often with hope

Examples

The highly anticipated movie premiere drew a huge crowd to the theater.
Her performance at the concert exceeded everyone's anticipated expectations.
The company's new product launch is much anticipated by technology enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unexpected - not expected or foreseen
  • Antonym: unanticipated - not expected or predicted
  • Synonym: expected - regarded as likely to happen

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'anticipate', from Latin 'anticipare' (to take before).
