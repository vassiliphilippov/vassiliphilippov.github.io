Talinum spinescens

1. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪnəm spaɪˈnɛsɛnz / (AmE), / təˈlaɪnəm spaɪˈnɛsns / (BrE)
A low cushion-forming plant native to the southwestern United States, with rose to crimson-magenta flowers and leaf midribs that become spines when the leaves die.
a southwestern US plant with spiny leaf midribs

Examples

In the arid hills, Talinum spinescens is easily recognized by its spiny leaf midribs that remain after leaves fall.
Botanists surveyed an area populated by Talinum spinescens, noting its vivid magenta flowers.
Gardeners value Talinum spinescens for its resilience in dry, rocky soils.

Etymology

From genus name Talinum (from Latin/Greek origins) and species name spinescens (Latin 'becoming spiny').
