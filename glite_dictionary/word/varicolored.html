varicolored

1. ADJ

/ ˈværɪˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈværɪˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having several different colors, often in distinct patches or sections.
having various different colors

Examples

The artist painted a varicolored mask for the festival.
She wore a stunning, varicolored dress decorated with bold geometric patterns.
The bird's feathers were varicolored, blending greens, reds, and yellows in a beautiful display.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color
  • Synonym: particolored - having different colors in patches; variegated

Etymology

From Latin varius (different) + colored.

2. ADJ

/ ˈverɪˌkʌlərd / (AmE), / ˈvɛərɪˌkʌləd / (BrE)
having a variety of different colors; multicolored.
having many different colors

Examples

The artist created a varicolored mural on the side of the building.
Her garden was filled with varicolored tulips blooming in the spring sunlight.
The varicolored feathers of the parrot caught everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many different colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color

Etymology

From Latin varius (varied) + English colored.
