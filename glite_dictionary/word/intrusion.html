intrusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the act of entering a place, especially someone else's property, without right or permission
unauthorized entry onto property

Examples

The police responded quickly to the alarm that signaled an intrusion at the warehouse.
Burglary is considered an unlawful intrusion into a private residence.
Security cameras were installed to help deter intrusion onto the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespass - entry onto someone's property without permission

Etymology

From Latin 'intrudere' meaning 'to thrust in'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the act of entering a place or situation where one is not wanted or has not been before, especially in a forceful or unwelcome way
unwanted or forcible entry

Examples

The sudden intrusion of hundreds of tourists changed the peaceful village atmosphere.
Scientists were concerned about the intrusion of non-native species into the local ecosystem.
The intrusion of loud noises disrupted the wildlife in the area.

Synset Examples

an invasion of tourists
an invasion of locusts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasion - the act of entering a place with the intent to occupy it, often by force

Etymology

From Latin intrudere (to thrust in) via Late Latin intrusionem (a forcing in).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the act of entering a place or situation by force or without permission, often unwelcome
unwelcome or forced entry

Examples

The security system detected an intrusion and immediately alerted the police.
Any intrusion into someone's privacy should be avoided whenever possible.
She considered the questions an unwelcome intrusion into her personal life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasion - an act of entering in large numbers, often with force
  • Synonym: trespass - the act of unlawfully entering someone's property
  • Antonym: invitation - the act of inviting someone to enter or participate

Etymology

From Latin 'intrusio' (a thrusting in), from 'intrudere' (to thrust in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00050289-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the process or result of molten rock being forced into cracks or between layers of existing rock formations
geological: molten rock entering fissures

Examples

The granite intrusion formed deep beneath the Earth's surface millions of years ago.
Geologists studied the intrusion to understand the area's volcanic history.
Basalt intrusion can significantly alter the composition of the surrounding rock layers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrusion - the process of molten rock flowing onto the Earth's surface

Etymology

From Latin intrudere (to thrust in).

5. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
A mass of igneous rock formed when magma cools and solidifies beneath the Earth's surface.
mass of igneous rock formed underground

Examples

The granite intrusion is visible along the northern edge of the valley.
Geologists studied the structure of the ancient intrusion to understand the region's volcanic history.
This mountain range was largely shaped by a massive intrusion millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrusion - rock formed when magma cools on the surface

Etymology

From Latin intrudere (to thrust in).
