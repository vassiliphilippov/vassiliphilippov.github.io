domestically

1. ADV

/ dəˈmɛstɪkli / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to home or family life.
in a way related to home or family

Examples

She prefers to focus her energy domestically rather than on her career.
His achievements are significant both internationally and domestically.
The film depicts life domestically, highlighting the importance of family relationships.

Synset Examples

the housewife bored us with her domestically limited conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domestic - relating to home or family

Etymology

From 'domestic' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'domus' (house).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00322759-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ dəˈmɛstɪkli / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the internal affairs or issues of a country or government
relating to internal affairs of a country

Examples

Domestically, the president proposes a more moderate economic policy.
The government faced criticism for its actions domestically, even as it received praise abroad.
While the company is successful domestically, it struggles to compete in international markets.

Synset Examples

domestically, the president proposes a more moderate economic policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internationally - in a way that concerns two or more countries

Etymology

From 'domestic' (from Latin domesticus, from domus ‘house’) + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00322917-r
Synset POS: r
