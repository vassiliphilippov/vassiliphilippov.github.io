con

1. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
an argument or reason against a particular proposal or course of action
argument against something

Examples

Let’s weigh every con before deciding on the new policy.
He listed several cons related to the proposed changes.
One con of moving to the city is the higher cost of living.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro - an argument in favor of something

Etymology

From Latin 'contra' (against, opposite).

2. ADV

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
in opposition to a proposition, opinion, or argument
in opposition; against (an argument)

Examples

They weighed the pros and cons carefully before making a decision.
Much was written both pro and con during the public debate.
In the discussion, she presented the con side with convincing arguments.

Synset Examples

much was written pro and con

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro - in support of a proposition or argument

Etymology

From Latin 'contra' meaning 'against'.

3. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
a person who is serving a sentence in jail or prison; a convict
prisoner; convict
informal

Examples

The con had already spent five years behind bars before the parole hearing.
After escaping from prison, the con evaded authorities for months.
That building over there houses over a hundred cons serving long sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convict - a person found guilty of a criminal offense and serving a sentence
  • Synonym: inmate - a person confined to an institution such as a prison or hospital

Etymology

Shortened form of 'convict'.

4. VERB

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
to trick someone into giving you something, especially money, by deceiving them
to deceive and cheat someone
informal

Examples

He managed to con elderly people out of thousands of dollars.
Tourists are often warned not to let anyone con them on the street.
She realized too late that she had been conned by a fake charity.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain advantage

Etymology

Possibly from 19th-century slang, shortening of 'confidence (trick)', as in 'confidence man'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02578034-v
Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
a trick or scam in which someone is deceived, especially to cheat at gambling or to persuade someone to buy worthless property
a trick or scam to cheat someone

Examples

He fell victim to a con and lost all his savings.
The lottery was just another con designed to trick people.
Police warned residents about a new con targeting the elderly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
  • Synonym: swindle - a scheme to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception for financial gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful

Etymology

Short for 'confidence trick' or 'confidence game'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00780744-n
Synset POS: n
