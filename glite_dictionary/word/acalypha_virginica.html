Acalypha virginica

1. NOUN

/ əˈkæləfə vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / əˈkælɪfə vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A weedy annual herb native to eastern North America, often found in disturbed soils.
weedy North American herb

Examples

Acalypha virginica commonly grows along roadsides and in open fields.
Botanists have noted an increase in Acalypha virginica in disturbed habitats.
Gardeners often consider Acalypha virginica to be a weed due to its rapid spread.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Acalypha' and Latin 'virginica' meaning 'of Virginia'.
