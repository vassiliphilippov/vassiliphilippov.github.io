Tomistoma schlegeli

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈmɪstoʊmə ʃlɛˈgɛli / (AmE), / təˈmɪstəmə ʃlɛˈɡɛli / (BrE)
A large freshwater crocodilian species native to Southeast Asia, also known as the false gharial, with a long, slender snout.
a crocodilian species of Southeast Asia

Examples

The Tomistoma schlegeli is often mistaken for the true gharial due to its elongated snout.
Conservation efforts have been launched to protect the endangered Tomistoma schlegeli in Malaysian wetlands.
Unlike most crocodilians, the Tomistoma schlegeli rarely poses a threat to humans.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Tomistoma and species name honoring Hermann Schlegel, a German zoologist.
