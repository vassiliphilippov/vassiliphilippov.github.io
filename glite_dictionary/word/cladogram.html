cladogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈklædəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈklædəɡræm / (BrE)
a branching diagram showing the evolutionary relationships among different species or groups based on their shared characteristics
diagram of evolutionary relationships

Examples

The biology textbook included a cladogram to show the relationships between various animal groups.
Scientists used DNA evidence to revise the cladogram of flowering plants.
A well-constructed cladogram can help clarify evolutionary histories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylogenetic tree - a diagram showing inferred evolutionary relationships among species

Etymology

From Greek 'klados' (branch) + '-gram' (something written or drawn).
