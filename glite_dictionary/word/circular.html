circular

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːkjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊlə / (BrE)
a printed advertisement or official notice intended for wide distribution, usually in the form of a leaflet or page
printed advertisement or notice for wide distribution

Examples

The company sent out a circular to inform customers of the new store opening.
Every household received a promotional circular advertising the latest discounts.
He threw the advertising circular into the recycling bin without reading it.

Synset Examples

he mailed the circular to all subscribers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flyer - a small leaflet advertising an event or product
  • Synonym: brochure - a small booklet or pamphlet advertising or providing information

Etymology

From Latin circulāris (circular; round); in the sense of a notice, likely from the idea of being circulated.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɜːrkjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊlə / (BrE)
having the shape or movement of a circle; relating to or resembling a circle.
having the shape or movement of a circle

Examples

The dancers formed a circular pattern on the stage as the music played.
Most planets follow a nearly circular orbit around the sun.
She drew a perfect circular shape on the whiteboard to demonstrate the concept.

Synset Examples

the circular motion of the wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round - having the shape of a circle or sphere
  • Antonym: rectangular - having the shape of a rectangle

Etymology

From Latin 'circularis', from 'circulus' meaning 'circle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00679539-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɝː.kjə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə / (BrE)
having the shape or form of a circle; round
shaped like a circle

Examples

The park had a large circular fountain at its center.
Please arrange the desks in a circular pattern for the group discussion.
She wore a pendant with a simple circular design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round - shaped like or approximately like a circle
  • Antonym: square - having the shape of a square

Etymology

From Latin 'circularis', from 'circulus' (circle).
