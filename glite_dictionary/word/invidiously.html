invidiously

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈvɪdiəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪdiəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is likely to cause resentment or anger in others; unfairly or offensively.
in a way causing resentment

Examples

The law seemed to apply invidiously to certain groups, which sparked widespread criticism.
He was invidiously compared to his predecessor in a way that felt deeply unjust.
Making distinctions invidiously can erode trust and unity in a community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfairly - in a way that is not fair or just
  • Antonym: justly - in a fair and morally right way

Etymology

From Latin 'invidiosus' (envious) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
