dispiriting

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspɪrɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪrɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to lose enthusiasm, hope, or confidence; making someone feel discouraged.
causing discouragement or loss of hope

Examples

The team's sudden loss was especially dispiriting after weeks of hard training.
It can be dispiriting to face repeated rejection when looking for a job.
The bleak weather had a dispiriting effect on everyone's mood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraging - giving someone support or confidence

Etymology

From the verb 'dispiriting', formed from 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'spirit'.
