Valerianella olitoria

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɛriˈænələ oʊˌlɪˈtɔriə / (AmE), / vəˌlɛːrɪˈanɛlə ɒlɪˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a small annual plant of the honeysuckle family, often cultivated for its edible leaves used in salads and sometimes considered a weed
a plant grown for salad leaves

Examples

I added fresh Valerianella olitoria to the spring salad for a delicate flavor.
Farmers often grow Valerianella olitoria alongside other leafy greens in cool climates.
Wild patches of Valerianella olitoria can sometimes be found growing as weeds among crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn salad - another name for Valerianella olitoria, especially in culinary contexts
  • Synonym: lamb's lettuce - common English name for Valerianella olitoria

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus name Valerianella and species epithet olitoria, meaning relating to vegetables.
