rational

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ən.əl / (BrE)
a number that can be expressed as an integer or the quotient of two integers
number expressible as fraction or integer

Examples

Every integer is a rational, since it can be written as a fraction with denominator one.
The fraction 3/4 is a rational because both the numerator and denominator are integers.
Zero is considered a rational in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrational - a number that cannot be expressed as a fraction of integers

Etymology

From Latin rationalis (reasonable), from ratio (reason, reckoning).

3. ADJ

/ ˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
(Mathematics) Capable of being expressed as a quotient or fraction of two integers.
can be written as a fraction of integers (math)

Examples

Every rational number can be written in the form a/b where a and b are integers and b≠0.
The fraction 3/4 is a rational number because it represents a ratio of integers.
Pi is not a rational number, as it cannot be expressed exactly as a fraction of two integers.

Synset Examples

rational numbers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrational - (Mathematics) not capable of being expressed as a quotient of integers.

Etymology

From Latin rationalis (of reason or reasoning), here referring to the property of being a ratio of integers.
