Portunidae

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtjuːnəˌdiː / (AmE), / pɔːˈtjuːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine crabs known as swimming crabs, characterized by their flattened hind legs adapted for swimming
family of swimming crabs

Examples

The Portunidae family includes some of the fastest swimming crabs in the ocean.
Blue crabs, a member of the Portunidae, are highly valued for their meat.
Scientists have conducted studies on the unique swimming abilities of Portunidae.

Etymology

From Latin Portunus (a genus of crabs), named after the Roman god Portunus.

2. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtuːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pɔːˈtjuːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of swimming crabs characterized by having flattened hind legs adapted for swimming, found in marine environments worldwide
family of swimming crabs

Examples

The Portunidae are easily recognized by their paddle-shaped hind legs, which help them swim.
Many commercially important crabs, like the blue crab, belong to the Portunidae family.
Marine biologists study the diversity of Portunidae in coastal waters worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portunus - type genus of the Portunidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Portunus', the name of a Roman sea god, combined with the zoological suffix '-idae' denoting a family.
