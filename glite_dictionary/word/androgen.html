androgen

1. NOUN

/ ˈændɹəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈændɹəˌdʒɛn / (BrE)
A male sex hormone produced in the testes and responsible for the development and maintenance of male sexual characteristics.
male sex hormone
medical

Examples

An imbalance of androgen levels can lead to various health issues in both men and women.
The doctor explained how androgen plays a crucial role in male puberty.
Excess androgen production may cause symptoms such as increased body hair and acne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testosterone - the primary male sex hormone
  • Antonym: estrogen - female sex hormones

Etymology

From Greek 'andr-', meaning man, and '-gen', meaning producing.
