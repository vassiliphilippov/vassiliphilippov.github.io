warehouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛrˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈweə.haʊs / (BrE)
A large building where goods and merchandise are stored before distribution or sale.
a building for storing goods

Examples

The company rented a warehouse to store its products before shipping them to retailers.
Fire safety regulations require all exits in the warehouse to remain clear at all times.
They visited the old warehouse that had been converted into modern office space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depot - a place for the storage of large quantities of equipment, food, or goods
  • Synonym: storehouse - a building where things are stored

Etymology

From Middle English 'warehous', from 'ware' (goods) + 'house'.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɛrˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwɛəˌhaʊs / (BrE)
to store goods or materials in a warehouse, typically for later distribution or sale
to store in a warehouse

Examples

Many retailers warehouse their products before they hit the stores.
The company warehoused excess inventory during the slow season.
Goods are often warehoused in bulk to save on shipping costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to put something in a place where it is kept for future use
