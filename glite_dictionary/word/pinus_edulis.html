Pinus edulis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪnəs ˈɛdʒʊlɪs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪnəs ˈɛdjuːlɪs / (BrE)
A small, compact pine tree native to the southwestern United States, valued for its edible seeds known as pine nuts.
a nut-producing pine tree of the Southwest US

Examples

The seeds of the Pinus edulis are harvested and sold as pine nuts across the United States.
Many birds and small mammals rely on the Pinus edulis for both food and shelter in arid landscapes.
Due to climate change, the distribution of Pinus edulis may shift northward in the coming decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon pine - common English name for Pinus edulis

Etymology

Latin; 'Pinus' meaning pine, 'edulis' meaning edible, referring to the edible seeds.
