raring

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
very eager and enthusiastic to do something, especially used in 'raring to go'.
full of eagerness; eager to start

Examples

After months of training, the team was raring to compete in the championship.
As soon as the bell rang, the children were raring to run outside and play.
She was raring to get started on her new project right away.

Synset Examples

impatient to begin
raring to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something
  • Synonym: keen - wanting to do something very much; eager
  • Antonym: reluctant - unwilling and hesitant; not eager

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'roaring', referring to the idea of someone making a noise out of excitement.
