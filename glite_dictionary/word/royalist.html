Royalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəlɪst / (BrE)
a supporter of Charles I and the monarchy during the English Civil War (1642–1651)
supporter of the king during English Civil War

Examples

He was a loyal Royalist, fighting for the king throughout the conflict.
Many Royalists fled to France after their defeat in the Civil War.
The debate between Royalist and Parliamentarian ideals shaped England's future.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Parliamentarian - a supporter of Parliament against Charles I during the English Civil War

Etymology

Formed from 'royal' (from Old French, from Latin *regalis*) + '-ist'.
