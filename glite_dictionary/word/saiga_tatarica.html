Saiga tatarica

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪɡə təˈtɑrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsaɪɡə təˈtɑːrɪkə / (BrE)
A species of goat-like antelope native to central Eurasia, known for its distinctive swollen nose resembling a short trunk.
Eurasian antelope with a distinctive nose

Examples

The Saiga tatarica is recognized for its unusual, bulbous nose that helps filter dusty air.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Saiga tatarica from extinction due to poaching and habitat loss.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Saiga tatarica across the steppes of Kazakhstan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saiga - Another term for Saiga tatarica, referring to the same antelope

Etymology

From Russian 'saiga', the name of the animal, and the specific epithet from the Tatar people.
