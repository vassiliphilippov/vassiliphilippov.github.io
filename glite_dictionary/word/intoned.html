intoned

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtoʊnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtəʊnd / (BrE)
spoken or sung with a monotonous or chant-like cadence or rhythm, as in chanting.
spoken in a chant-like monotone

Examples

She spoke the poem in an intoned manner, making it sound like a ritual chant.
The intoned prayers echoed through the silent hall, calming everyone present.
His intoned response made it difficult to tell how he really felt.

Synset Examples

their chantlike intoned prayers
a singsong manner of speaking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotone - a single unvaried musical tone or speaking without variation in pitch
  • Synonym: singsong - characterized by a rhythmic, repetitive, and often monotonous pattern of speech
  • Antonym: varied - characterized by change or variety in tone or pitch

Etymology

From the verb 'intone', itself from Latin 'intonare' (to thunder).
