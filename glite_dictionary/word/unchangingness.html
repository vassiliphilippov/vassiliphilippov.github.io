unchangingness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of remaining unchanged or not varying
quality of remaining unchanged

Examples

The unchangingness of the landscape gave her a sense of peace and stability.
He admired the unchangingness of his friend's moral principles, which never wavered over time.
Many people are comforted by the unchangingness of certain traditions in their culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable; fixedness
  • Antonym: changeability - the quality of being changeable; tendency to change

Etymology

From unchanging + -ness (Old English -nes, forming nouns from adjectives).
