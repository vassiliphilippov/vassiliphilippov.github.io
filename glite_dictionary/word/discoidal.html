discoidal

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskɔɪdl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈskɔɪdəl / (BrE)
having the shape of a flat disc; round and flattened
having a flat, disc-like shape

Examples

The scientist observed discoidal red blood cells under the microscope.
The fossil showed a discoidal imprint on the ancient rock surface.
Some tablets are manufactured in a discoidal form for easy swallowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disk-shaped - having the shape of a disk

Etymology

From Modern Latin discoidalis, from Greek diskos (disc) + -oid (resembling) + -al (adjective suffix).
