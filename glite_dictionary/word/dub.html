dub

1. VERB

/ dʌb / (AmE), / dʌb / (BrE)
to give an unofficial name or nickname to someone or something
give a nickname

Examples

The press dubbed her the 'Queen of Pop' after the release of her new album.
Fans dubbed the team 'The Invincibles' after their unbeaten season.
He was dubbed 'Mr. Fix-It' because he always solved our problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - a familiar or humorous name given instead of a real name
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01030649-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dʌb / (AmE), / dʌb / (BrE)
new sounds or dialogue that are added to a soundtrack, especially in film, television, or music production.
added soundtrack sounds or dialogue

Examples

The film included a dub of several characters' voices to improve clarity.
In reggae music, producers often create a dub version by remixing and adding special effects.
She noticed the dub didn't quite match the actors' mouth movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubbing - the process of adding new sounds to a soundtrack

Etymology

Shortened from 'dubbing,' from the verb 'to dub' (to add or record new sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05728468-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dʌb / (AmE), / dʌb / (BrE)
to replace the original soundtrack of a film or television program with a soundtrack in another language
replace original soundtrack with foreign language

Examples

Many animated films are dubbed into several languages for international audiences.
We watched the Spanish dubbed version of the sci-fi movie last night.
The studio will dub the English dialogue so it can be released in France.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subtitle - display the translation as text on screen instead of replacing audio
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00962594-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dʌb / (AmE), / dʌb / (BrE)
to make someone a knight, especially by a formal ceremony involving the touch of a sword on the shoulders
to make someone a knight

Examples

The queen will dub him a knight at the ceremony next week.
During the ceremony, he was dubbed Sir Arthur by the monarch.
Many famous individuals have been dubbed knights for their contributions to society.

Synset Examples

The Beatles were knighted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knight - a man granted an honorary title of knighthood

Etymology

From Old French *aduber* (to equip with armor), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
