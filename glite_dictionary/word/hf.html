HF

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (BrE)
the range of radio frequencies from 3 to 30 megahertz, known as high frequency, used in various communication systems
radio frequency range: 3–30 MHz

Examples

Many amateur radio operators use HF bands to communicate over long distances.
The HF spectrum is ideal for international broadcasting because it supports ionospheric propagation.
Weather reports for ships are often transmitted on HF frequencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high frequency - radio frequencies between 3 and 30 megahertz

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'high frequency.'
