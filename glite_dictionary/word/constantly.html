constantly

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑːn.stənt.li / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that happens all the time or very frequently, without interruption or change.
all the time; without interruption

Examples

The baby cried constantly throughout the night, keeping everyone awake.
She is constantly checking her phone for messages.
Water is constantly flowing from the mountain spring.

Synset Examples

constantly kind and gracious
he always arrives on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: always - at all times; on every occasion
  • Antonym: never - not at any time; not ever

Etymology

From Latin 'constanter' (steadily), from 'constans' (standing firm).

in a way that happens all the time or very frequently, without stopping
all the time; without stopping

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occasionally - sometimes but not frequently or regularly

Etymology
