glacial

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡleɪ.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪ.si.əl / (BrE)
(of a person's manner or expression) extremely cold, unfriendly, or showing disdain.
extremely cold or unfriendly (of manner)

Examples

She gave him a glacial look that ended the conversation immediately.
Despite his apology, her response remained glacial and distant.
The board greeted the proposal with a glacial silence, making their disapproval clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icy - cold and unfriendly in manner

Etymology

From Latin glaciālis (icy), from glaciēs (ice).

extremely cold

Examples

She hurried across the glacial street to escape the biting cold.
Despite the glacial air in the conference room, the meeting dragged on for hours.
