Tazicef

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæzɪˌsɛf / (AmE), / ˈtazɪˌsɛf / (BrE)
A trade name for a parenteral cephalosporin antibiotic (ceftazidime) used to treat moderate bacterial infections.
trade name for an antibiotic (ceftazidime)
medical

Examples

The hospital administered Tazicef to treat the patient's severe respiratory infection.
After a positive culture, the doctor prescribed Tazicef for the bacterial meningitis.
Some strains of bacteria have developed resistance to Tazicef over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceftazidime - a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic
