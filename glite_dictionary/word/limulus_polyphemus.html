Limulus polyphemus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmjələs ˌpɑːlɪˈfiːməs / (AmE), / ˈlɪmjʊləs ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the horseshoe crab, a large marine arthropod found along the Atlantic coast of North America, known for its domed, horseshoe-shaped carapace and stiff, pointed tail.
scientific name for the horseshoe crab

Examples

The blood of Limulus polyphemus is used in medical research for detecting bacterial endotoxins.
Several Limulus polyphemus were observed crawling across the tidal flats at dusk.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Limulus polyphemus populations along the eastern seaboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - A marine arthropod with a horseshoe-shaped domed shell

Etymology

From Latin: 'Limulus' meaning 'a little askew' (alluding to shape); 'polyphemus' from Greek mythology, referencing the giant Polyphemus, possibly due to size or appearance.
