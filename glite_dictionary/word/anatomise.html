to anatomise

1. VERB

/ əˈnæt̬.ə.maɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæt.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to dissect a body or organ in order to study its structure; to examine and analyse in detail
dissect or analyse in detail

Examples

The scientist decided to anatomise the frog to learn more about its internal organs.
In his review, the critic carefully anatomised the novel’s plot and themes.
Medical students often anatomise cadavers to understand human anatomy in detail.

Synset Examples

anatomize the bodies of the victims of this strange disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissect - to cut apart (a body or plant) for study
  • Synonym: to analyse - to examine in detail to understand something better

Etymology

From Greek 'anatomē' (dissection) via Late Latin 'anatomizare', Medieval Latin 'anatomisare'.
