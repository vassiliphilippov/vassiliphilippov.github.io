suppressant

1. NOUN

/ səˈprɛsənt / (AmE), / səˈprɛsənt / (BrE)
A drug that reduces or suppresses appetite.
drug that reduces appetite
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an suppressant to help control her appetite.
Some over-the-counter suppressants claim to reduce hunger, but their effectiveness varies.
Before taking an appetite suppressant, consult with a healthcare professional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appetite suppressant - a substance or drug that decreases the desire to eat

Etymology

From suppress + -ant, modeled after French and Latin agent nouns.
