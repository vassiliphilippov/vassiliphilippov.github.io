spitefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪtf(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of wanting to hurt, upset, or annoy others; malicious ill will
the quality of being spiteful

Examples

Her spitefulness was evident when she laughed at her classmate's mistake.
He acted out of pure spitefulness, determined to ruin her plans.
The spitefulness in his comments hurt everyone in the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil; ill will
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning; kindness

Etymology

From 'spiteful' + '-ness'; 'spiteful' from Old French 'despit' (contempt), from Latin 'despectus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪtfʊlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being mean, malicious, or showing a desire to hurt, annoy, or offend others.
quality of being spiteful or malicious

Examples

Her spitefulness was evident in the cruel remarks she made about her colleagues.
He acted out of pure spitefulness, ruining the surprise out of jealousy.
The spitefulness in his actions alienated him from his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - desire to harm others or see others suffer
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, or considerate

Etymology

Formed from 'spiteful' + '-ness'; 'spiteful' from Middle English 'despit' (spite).
