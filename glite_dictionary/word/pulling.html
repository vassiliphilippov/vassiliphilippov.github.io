pulling

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of exerting force to draw or move something toward oneself or in a particular direction
the act of drawing or moving something by force

Examples

The pulling of the heavy sled left him exhausted.
Continuous pulling on the rope finally brought the boat to shore.
Frequent pulling of weeds kept the garden tidy.

Synset Examples

the pull up the hill had him breathing harder
his strenuous pulling strained his back

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pushing - the act of exerting force to move something away from oneself

Etymology

From the verb 'pull,' ultimately from Middle English 'pullen,' from Old English 'pullian.'
