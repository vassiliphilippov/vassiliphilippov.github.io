architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a particular building or group of buildings, or the style in which they are designed and constructed
the design or style of buildings

Examples

The city is famous for its modern architecture.
We admired the innovative architecture of the museum.
The architecture along the river dates back to the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: building - a structure with a roof and walls, such as a house, school, or factory

Etymology

From Latin 'architectura', from Greek 'arkhitekton' (chief builder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02737730-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the art and profession of designing buildings and other structures, especially with regard to aesthetic effect
the profession of designing buildings

Examples

She decided to study architecture at university so she could design innovative buildings.
Frank Lloyd Wright made a lasting impact on modern architecture around the world.
The conference covered trends and new technologies in sustainable architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architect - a person who designs buildings
  • Degree Lower: engineering - the application of scientific principles to design and build structures

Etymology

From Latin 'architectura', from Greek 'arkhitektōn' (chief builder, architect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00612006-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪtɛktʃə / (BrE)
(computing) The design and organization of a computer's hardware or system software.
structure of computer hardware or software

Examples

The company's new processor uses a completely different architecture from previous models.
Understanding the software architecture is essential for debugging complex systems.
Their engineers are developing an innovative architecture for high-performance servers.

Synset Examples

the architecture of a computer's system software

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framework - an essential supporting structure for a system or concept (in computing, a structural foundation)
  • Synonym: design - the arrangement of elements or structure in computing systems

Etymology

From Latin 'architectura', from Greek 'arkhitekton' (chief builder), adapted for computing in the mid-20th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the art and science of designing and constructing buildings, especially those that are aesthetically pleasing and structurally sound
design and construction of fine buildings

Examples

She studied architecture at university, focusing on sustainable building design.
The museum's architecture blends modern elements with traditional motifs.
Many people travel to Europe to appreciate the historic architecture of its cities.

Synset Examples

architecture and eloquence are mixed arts whose end is sometimes beauty and sometimes use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering - the application of scientific principles to design and build structures or machines

Etymology

From Latin architectura, from Greek arkhitektōn (chief builder).
