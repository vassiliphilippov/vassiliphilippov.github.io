sleepy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsliːpi / (AmE), / ˈsliːpi / (BrE)
feeling ready to fall asleep; drowsy or inclined to sleep.
drowsy; ready to sleep

Examples

After a long day at work, I always feel sleepy around 9 p.m.
The sleepy child yawned and rubbed his eyes during the car ride home.
She was too sleepy to finish her book and decided to go to bed early.

Synset Examples

beginning to feel sleepy
a sleepy-eyed child with drooping eyelids
sleepyheaded students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drowsy - feeling sleepy or lethargic
  • Antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive

Etymology

From Middle English slepi, from Old English slǣpīg (sleepy), from slǣp (sleep).
