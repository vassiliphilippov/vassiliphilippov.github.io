Bligh

1. PROPN

/ blaɪ / (AmE), / blaɪ / (BrE)
William Bligh (1754–1817), a British admiral best known as the captain of the HMS Bounty during the 1789 mutiny.
British admiral; captain during the Bounty mutiny

Examples

The leadership style of Bligh has been widely debated by historians.
After the mutiny, Bligh managed a remarkable voyage to Timor.
Many films depict the dramatic relationship between Fletcher Christian and Bligh.
