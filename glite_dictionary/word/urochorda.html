Urochorda

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈkɔrdə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a subphylum of marine animals, commonly known as tunicates, characterized by a sac-like body and a tough outer covering
group of marine animals; tunicates

Examples

Scientists have discovered several new species within the Urochorda in coastal waters.
Members of the Urochorda, such as sea squirts, filter feed by drawing water through their bodies.
In marine biology, the Urochorda are studied for their unique developmental patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tunicate - marine invertebrate animal of the subphylum Urochorda

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (tail) + 'chorda' (cord, string).
