nescient

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛʃiənt / (AmE), / ˈnɛsiənt / (BrE)
holding that only material phenomena can be known and that knowledge of spiritual matters or ultimate causes is impossible; relating to or characterized by agnosticism or ignorance of higher truths
believing only material phenomena can be known

Examples

Her nescient view led her to dismiss all discussions of the divine as unknowable.
The philosopher adopted a nescient stance, focusing solely on observable phenomena.
A nescient perspective leaves no room for knowledge of ultimate causes or spiritual matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agnostic - a person who believes that nothing is known or can be known of the existence or nature of God

Etymology

From Latin 'nesciens' (not knowing), present participle of 'nescire' (to not know).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɛʃiənt / (AmE), / ˈnɛsɪənt / (BrE)
Lacking knowledge or awareness; ignorant, especially in a general or fundamental way.
lacking knowledge; ignorant

Examples

She remained nescient of the political changes happening beyond her small village.
His nescient comments during the seminar showed he hadn't read the assigned material.
Despite his intelligence, he was surprisingly nescient about modern technology.

Synset Examples

an ignorant man
nescient of contemporary literature
an unlearned group incapable of understanding complex issues
exhibiting contempt for his unlettered companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - Lacking knowledge or awareness in general; uneducated

Etymology

From Latin 'nesciens' (not knowing), from 'ne-' (not) + 'sciens' (knowing).
