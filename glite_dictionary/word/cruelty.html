cruelty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːəlti / (AmE), / ˈkruːəlti / (BrE)
the quality of being cruel; behavior that deliberately causes pain or suffering to others
deliberate causing of pain or suffering

Examples

The cruelty of the punishment shocked everyone in the courtroom.
He was known for his cruelty towards animals, which made people avoid him.
Stories of cruelty during the war are still difficult to listen to.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate; opposite of cruelty

Etymology

From Old French cruauté, from Latin crudelitas (cruelty, harshness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːlti / (AmE), / ˈkruːəlti / (BrE)
behavior or actions that deliberately cause pain, suffering, or distress to others.
deliberate infliction of pain or suffering

Examples

The animal shelter rescued dogs that had suffered terrible cruelty at the hands of their former owners.
Acts of cruelty against prisoners are condemned by international law.
She was shocked by the cruelty he showed toward innocent people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Synonym: brutality - savage physical violence; great cruelty

Etymology

From Old French 'cruelté', from Latin 'crudelitas' (cruelty, harshness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkruːəlti / (AmE), / ˈkruːəlti / (BrE)
A feeling or attitude of extreme heartlessness or delight in causing pain or suffering in others.
extreme heartlessness; delight in causing pain

Examples

The defendant's cruelty was evident in the complete lack of remorse he showed.
There was a certain cruelty in her laugh that made everyone uncomfortable.
He could not understand the cruelty behind such hateful words.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From Old French cruelté, from Latin crudelitas, from crudelis meaning 'cruel'.
