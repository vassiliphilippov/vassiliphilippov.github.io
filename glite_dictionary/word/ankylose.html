to ankylose

1. VERB

/ ˈæŋ.kəˌloʊz / (AmE), / ˈaŋ.kɪ.ləʊz / (BrE)
to cause ankylosis, especially by surgical means; to stiffen a joint by causing its bones to fuse
cause fusion of a joint by surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon had to ankylose the damaged joint to restore the patient's mobility.
In rare cases, doctors intentionally ankylose a joint to relieve severe chronic pain.
After the infection, the only option was to ankylose the knee surgically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankylosis - stiffening or fusion of a joint

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (crooked, bent) and -osis (condition).

2. VERB

/ ˈæŋ.kəˌloʊz / (AmE), / ˈæŋ.kɪˌləʊz / (BrE)
(of joints or bones) to become stiff or fused together due to abnormal adhesion and rigidity of the bones of a joint.
become abnormally stiff or fused (joints)
medical

Examples

If left untreated, the joint may ankylose and limit movement permanently.
In severe cases of arthritis, fingers can ankylose over time.
Doctors warned that the elbow could ankylose if it remains immobilized too long.

Synset Examples

joints ankylose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankylosis - stiffening or fusion of a joint

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (bent, crooked), via French ankyloser.
