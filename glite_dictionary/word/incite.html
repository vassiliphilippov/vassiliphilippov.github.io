to incite

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to encourage, urge, or provoke someone to do something, especially something violent, illegal, or harmful
urge or provoke (especially to action)

Examples

The speaker's words were intended to incite the crowd to protest.
It is illegal to incite violence or hatred against others.
She was accused of trying to incite unrest within the organization.

Synset Examples

incite a riot
set off great unrest among the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate a reaction, usually strong or unwelcome
  • Antonym: to deter - to discourage someone from taking action

Etymology

From Latin incitare (to urge on, set in motion), from in- (toward) + citare (to arouse).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to act, especially in a forceful or violent way
encourage or urge to act

Examples

The leader's speech was meant to incite the crowd to protest against the new law.
Some posts on social media can incite violence and spread disinformation.
She was accused of trying to incite others to join the movement.

Synset Examples

This moved me to sacrifice my career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate or give rise to a reaction or emotion, typically a strong or unwelcome one
  • Synonym: urge - to strongly encourage someone to do something
  • Antonym: to deter - to discourage someone from taking an action

Etymology

From Latin 'incitare' (to set in motion, urge on).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01653541-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to encourage or stir up (violent or unlawful behavior or strong feelings)
to encourage or provoke action

Examples

He was arrested for trying to incite a riot during the protest.
Leaders who incite violence are often held responsible for the consequences.
The speaker's words were meant to incite anger among the crowd.

Synset Examples

The other children egged the boy on, but he did not want to throw the stone through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to deliberately cause a reaction or feeling
  • Synonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Antonym: to deter - to discourage someone from taking action

Etymology

From Latin incitare (to urge on).
