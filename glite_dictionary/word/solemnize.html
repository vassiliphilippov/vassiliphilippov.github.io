to solemnize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɑːləmˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to perform a wedding ceremony with the proper formalities or ceremonies
perform a wedding ceremony formally

Examples

The priest will solemnize the marriage on Saturday afternoon.
Only a registered official may solemnize a legal wedding ceremony in this country.
They invited their families and friends to witness the minister solemnize their union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to officiate - to act as an official in charge at a ceremony, especially a wedding

Etymology

From Old French 'solemniser', from Latin 'solemnizare' (to celebrate solemnly).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɑːləmˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to observe or perform (a ceremony or event) with formal dignity or seriousness
perform or observe with dignity

Examples

The community gathered to solemnize the anniversary of the town's founding.
They decided to solemnize their marriage in a traditional church ceremony.
Each year, we solemnize this day to remember those who sacrificed their lives.

Synset Examples

The King solemnized this day of mourning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something, often with a ceremony
  • Synonym: to celebrate - to observe or honor (a significant event) with festivities

Etymology

From Old French 'solemniser', from Latin 'solemnizare' (to celebrate solemnly).

3. VERB

/ ˈsɑː.ləmˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.əm.naɪz / (BrE)
To perform or mark with a formal or dignified ceremony; to make or render solemn.
perform or mark with ceremony

Examples

The couple chose to solemnize their marriage with a traditional ceremony.
The community gathered to solemnize the memory of the fallen heroes.
The leader's words solemnized the occasion, making it deeply meaningful for everyone present.

Synset Examples

This ceremony solemnized our hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of with a ceremony
  • Synonym: to celebrate - to honor or mark with a ceremony or festivities
  • Antonym: to trivialize - to make seem less serious or important

Etymology

From Old French 'solenniser', from Latin 'sollemnizare', from 'sollemnis' (customary, solemn).

Synset POS: v
