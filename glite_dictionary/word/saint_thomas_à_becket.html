Saint Thomas à Becket

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈtɑːməs ə ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtɒməs ə ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(c.1118–1170) The Archbishop of Canterbury from 1162 to 1170, venerated as a saint after his murder due to his conflict with King Henry II over the rights of the Church.
English archbishop and martyr (c.1118–1170)

Examples

The story of Saint Thomas à Becket is often cited as an example of martyrdom for religious principles.
Pilgrims traveled to Canterbury to visit the shrine of Saint Thomas à Becket after his canonization.
Many historians debate the political implications of the murder of Saint Thomas à Becket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thomas Becket - Alternative name for Saint Thomas à Becket, Archbishop of Canterbury

Etymology

From Old French 'Bequet'. Name includes honorific 'Saint' and the French preposition 'à'.
