bluestocking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌstɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌstɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
an intellectual or literary woman, especially one regarded as being pedantic or unfeminine
intellectual or literary woman

Examples

She has always been considered something of a bluestocking because of her love of poetry and philosophy.
In the nineteenth century, being called a bluestocking was not always a compliment.
The salon was filled with writers, artists, and bluestockings engaged in lively debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - a person possessing a highly developed intellect

Etymology

From 'Blue Stocking Society', a group of learned women in 18th-century England, named after the blue worsted stockings worn by some members.
