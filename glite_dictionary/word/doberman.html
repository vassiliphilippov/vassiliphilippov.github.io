Doberman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.bɚ.mən / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.bə.mən / (BrE)
A medium to large breed of dog of German origin, known for its glossy black and tan coat and commonly used as a watchdog.
breed of dog of German origin

Examples

The family adopted a Doberman to help protect their property.
A well-trained Doberman is both a loyal companion and a reliable guard dog.
During the dog show, the judge praised the Doberman for its athletic build and intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rottweiler - large, powerful breed of dog also of German origin, known for guarding ability

Etymology

Named after German breeder Karl Friedrich Louis Dobermann (1834–1894), who developed the breed.
