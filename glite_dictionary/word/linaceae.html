Linaceae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈneɪəsiˌi / (AmE), / lɪˈneɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, widely distributed, that includes the flax and related species.
family of flowering plants (flax family)

Examples

The Linaceae family includes the flax plant, which is used to make linen fabric.
Botanists study the characteristics of Linaceae to classify new plant species.
Seeds from several members of Linaceae are valued for their nutritional properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flax - plant cultivated for its fibre and seeds; member of Linaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'Linum' (flax) with the standard botanical suffix '-aceae' denoting a family.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈneɪəsiː / (AmE), / lɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed flowering plants that includes flax and related species
family of flowering plants including flax

Examples

The Linaceae family includes the well-known plant flax, used for making linen.
Some members of the Linaceae have medicinal properties valued in traditional remedies.
Botanists study Linaceae to understand the diversity of flowering plants.

Etymology

From the genus name Linum and the Latin suffix -aceae, used in botanical family names.
