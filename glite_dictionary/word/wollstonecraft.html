Wollstonecraft

1. PROPN

/ ˈwʊl.stən.kræft / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.stən.krɑːft / (BrE)
Mary Wollstonecraft (1759–1797), an English writer and early advocate for women's rights, known for her works promoting equal education for women and challenging male supremacy.
English writer and early feminist (1759–1797)

Examples

Many scholars credit Wollstonecraft with laying the foundations of modern feminism.
I recently read a biography of Wollstonecraft and was inspired by her life story.
The ideas of Wollstonecraft remain influential in discussions about women's education.

Etymology

English surname of uncertain origin; famous bearer Mary Wollstonecraft.
