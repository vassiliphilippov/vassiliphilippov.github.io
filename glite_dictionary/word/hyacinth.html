hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (BrE)
A red to reddish-brown transparent variety of zircon used as a gemstone.
red zircon gemstone

Examples

The jeweler displayed an exquisite hyacinth set in a gold ring.
She admired the deep red hue of the polished hyacinth in the necklace.
Collectors prize the rarest hyacinth gemstones for their clarity and brilliance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zircon - a mineral used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Latin *hyacinthus*, from Greek *hyakinthos* (the flower hyacinth; also used for precious stones of blue or red color).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəsɪnθ / (BrE)
a bulbous plant of the genus Hyacinthus, cultivated for its fragrant, typically blue, purple, or white flowers that appear in spring.
spring-flowering bulbous plant

Examples

The hyacinth bloomed early this year, filling the garden with its sweet scent.
She arranged a vase of hyacinths on the dining table for a touch of spring color.
Gardeners often plant hyacinth bulbs in autumn for vibrant flowers in April.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip - spring-flowering bulbous plant with bright cup-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin hyacinthus, from Greek hyakinthos (a blue larkspur or sapphire).
