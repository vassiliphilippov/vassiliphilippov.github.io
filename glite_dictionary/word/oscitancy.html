oscitancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑsɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪtənsi / (BrE)
an involuntary act of yawning, often caused by fatigue or boredom
the act of yawning

Examples

He apologized for his oscitancy during the meeting, blaming a sleepless night.
The professor’s dull lecture produced widespread oscitancy among the students.
Oscitancy is often contagious; one yawn can trigger a roomful of them.

Synset Examples

he could not suppress a yawn
the yawning in the audience told him it was time to stop
he apologized for his oscitancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yawn - an involuntary intake of breath with the mouth open, usually due to tiredness or boredom

Etymology

From Latin ‘oscitantia’, from ‘oscitare’ (to yawn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːsɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪtənsi / (BrE)
A state of drowsiness or dullness, often shown by frequent yawning.
drowsiness marked by yawning

Examples

There was an air of oscitancy in the warm afternoon lecture, as many students struggled to stay awake.
The doctor's notes mentioned the patient's persistent oscitancy as a symptom of his illness.
His oscitancy became obvious when he began yawning repeatedly during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - lack of energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'oscitantia' (yawning, drowsiness), from 'oscitare' (to yawn).

Synset POS: n
