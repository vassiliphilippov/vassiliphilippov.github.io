ormolu

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.məˌlu / (AmE), / ˈɔː.mə.luː / (BrE)
A gold-colored alloy of copper and zinc, often used for decorating furniture and ornaments.
gold-coloured brass for decoration

Examples

The antique clock was adorned with ormolu decorations that shimmered in the light.
Many 18th-century cabinets featured intricate ormolu mounts to enhance their elegance.
The collector specialized in furniture embellished with ormolu detailing.

Etymology

From French 'or moulu' (ground gold), ultimately from Latin 'aurum' (gold).
