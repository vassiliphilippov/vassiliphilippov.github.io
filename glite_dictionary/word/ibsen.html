Ibsen

/ ˈɪb.sən / (AmE), / ˈɪb.sən / (BrE)
Henrik Ibsen (1828–1906), a Norwegian playwright known for his realistic plays addressing social and political themes.
Norwegian playwright Henrik Ibsen

Examples

Many scholars consider Ibsen the father of modern drama.
The play "A Doll's House" by Ibsen sparked controversy for its portrayal of marriage.
He wrote his thesis on the influence of Ibsen in contemporary theatre.

Etymology

From the Norwegian surname Ibsen.
