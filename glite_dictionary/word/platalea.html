Platalea

1. NOUN

/ pləˈteɪliə / (AmE), / pləˈteɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large, long-legged wading birds known as spoonbills, characterized by their distinctive spoon-shaped bills, and the type genus of the family Plataleidae.
genus of wading birds (spoonbills)

Examples

The genus Platalea includes all the true spoonbills found worldwide.
Ornithologists studied nesting patterns within the Platalea populations in the wetlands.
Members of the Platalea genus are easily recognized by their unique spoon-shaped bills.

Etymology

From Latin Platalea, from Ancient Greek plataleia (spoonbill), from platys (broad).
