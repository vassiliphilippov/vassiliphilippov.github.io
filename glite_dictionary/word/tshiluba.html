Tshiluba

1. PROPN

/ ˌtʃiˈluːbə / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪˈluːbə / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken in southeastern Democratic Republic of the Congo.
Bantu language of southeastern Congo

Examples

Many people in the Kasai region speak Tshiluba as their first language.
She is studying Tshiluba at university to better understand local cultures.
The meeting was conducted in French, but translations were provided in Tshiluba.

Etymology

From the native name in the language itself, Tshiluba.
