bombardment

1. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbɑrdmənt / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːdmənt / (BrE)
an attack in which bombs are dropped over a place, especially in a military context
attack by dropping bombs

Examples

During World War II, the city suffered a relentless bombardment that destroyed much of its infrastructure.
The bombardment lasted for hours, keeping everyone in the shelters on edge.
After the bombardment, the survivors emerged to assess the damage to their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelling - an attack using artillery shells

Etymology

From French 'bombardement', from 'bombarder' (to bombard).

2. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbɑrdmənt / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːdmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of subjecting a material or substance to the impact of high-energy particles, such as electrons or alpha rays, especially in scientific experiments.
exposure to high-energy particles

Examples

The bombardment of the metal surface with alpha particles led to a new discovery.
After prolonged bombardment by neutrons, the isotope began to decay rapidly.
Scientists studied the effects of gamma ray bombardment on crystal structures.

Etymology

From French 'bombardement', from 'bombarder' (to bomb or bombard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00980666-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbɑrdmənt / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːdmənt / (BrE)
a continuous and intense attack with artillery fire intended to saturate a wide area rather than target a specific position
heavy artillery attack over an area

Examples

The city endured a relentless bombardment that lasted through the night.
During the bombardment, civilians sought shelter underground.
The bombardment was intended to weaken enemy defenses before the infantry advanced.

Synset Examples

they laid down a barrage in front of the advancing troops
the shelling went on for hours without pausing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid and continuous delivery of artillery fire to protect troops or saturate an area
  • Synonym: shelling - firing shells from artillery, especially in a sustained manner

Etymology

From French 'bombardement', from 'bombarder' (to bombard), based on 'bombarde' (cannon).

4. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbɑrdmənt / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːdmənt / (BrE)
a rapid and continuous stream of spoken or written communication, such as questions, messages, or criticisms
continuous stream of communication

Examples

After the press conference began, the politician faced a bombardment of questions from reporters.
The customer service team received a bombardment of emails regarding the product recall.
She was unprepared for the bombardment of criticism she received on social media.

Synset Examples

a barrage of questions
a bombardment of mail complaining about his mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid outpouring of something, especially words or questions

Etymology

From French *bombardement*, from *bombarder* (to bombard), ultimately from Italian *bombarda* (mortar).
