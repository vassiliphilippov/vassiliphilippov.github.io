Victor Emmanuel III of Italy

1. PROPN

/ ˌvɪktər ɪˈmænjuəl ðə θɜrd əv ɪˈtæli / (AmE), / ˌvɪktə ɪˈmænjuəl ðə ˈθɜːd ɒv ɪˈtæli / (BrE)
(1869–1947) King of Italy from 1900 to 1946, who appointed Mussolini as prime minister and abdicated when the monarchy was abolished.
King of Italy, 1900–1946

Examples

Victor Emmanuel III of Italy played a pivotal role in the events leading up to the rise of Mussolini.
The monarchy in Italy ended when Victor Emmanuel III of Italy abdicated in 1946.
Historians often debate the decisions made by Victor Emmanuel III of Italy during World War II.
