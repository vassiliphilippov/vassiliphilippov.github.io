bandlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænd.lət / (AmE), / ˈbænd.lɪt / (BrE)
A small molding in the form of a ring, often found at the top of a column in classical architecture.
small ring-shaped molding (architecture)

Examples

The Corinthian column was adorned with a carved bandlet just below the capital.
Restoration work included repairing the damaged bandlets that circled the top of the pillars.
Ancient builders often used a bandlet to add a decorative touch above the shaft of a column.

Etymology

From Italian 'bandletta', diminutive of 'banda' (band).
