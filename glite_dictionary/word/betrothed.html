betrothed

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtroʊðd / (AmE), / bɪˈtrəʊðd / (BrE)
the person to whom one is engaged to be married
person one is engaged to marry

Examples

He introduced me to his betrothed at the engagement party.
Her betrothed sent her a letter every day while he was away.
The young couple looked lovingly at each other, each grateful for their betrothed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiancé - a man to whom someone is engaged to be married
  • Synonym: fiancée - a woman to whom someone is engaged to be married
  • Degree Higher: spouse - a husband or wife; a person's married partner

Etymology

From Old English 'betrothian', from 'be-' (about) + 'troth' (faith, pledge).

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈtroʊðd / (AmE), / bɪˈtrəʊðd / (BrE)
Formally promised or pledged to be married.
formally engaged to marry

Examples

The betrothed couple announced their wedding date to friends and family.
She wore the ring proudly, happy to finally be betrothed.
Both families celebrated the official dinner for their betrothed children.

Synset Examples

the engaged couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engaged - formally agreed to marry
  • Antonym: single - not married or in a romantic commitment
  • Degree Higher: married - having a spouse; united in marriage

Etymology

From Middle English betrothed, from be- (intensive prefix) + troth (faith, fidelity, truth).
