reliquary

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪˌkwɛri / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪˌkwəri / (BrE)
a container, often decorative, used for storing or displaying sacred religious relics, especially those of saints
decorative container for religious relics

Examples

The jeweled reliquary held the saint's finger bone in the cathedral.
A medieval reliquary often took the shape of an ornate casket or statue.
Museum visitors admired the intricately carved reliquary on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrine - a place regarded as holy because of its associations with a sacred person or relic

Etymology

From Latin 'reliquiarium', from 'reliquiae' (remains, relics).
