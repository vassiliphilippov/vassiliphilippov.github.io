constricted

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or feeling) affected by tension or restricted emotional expression; tense or inhibited.
affected by tension; tense

Examples

She spoke in a constricted voice, barely able to hide her anxiety.
His constricted posture revealed how nervous he felt about the interview.
After the argument, the constricted atmosphere in the room was hard to ignore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tense - stretched tight or showing nervousness
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety

Etymology

Past participle of 'constrict', from Latin 'constringere' (to bind together, compress).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
made narrower or tighter by being squeezed or compressed; by extension, restricted or limited in scope or freedom.
narrowed or restricted; squeezed

Examples

The doctor explained that the pain was due to a constricted artery in his leg.
Her constricted breathing made it hard for her to speak clearly.
He had a very constricted outlook on life, seeing only problems and no solutions.

Synset Examples

a constricted blood vessel
a constricted view of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restricted - limited in extent, number, or scope
  • Antonym: expanded - made larger or wider

Etymology

From Latin 'constrictus', past participle of 'constringere' (to bind together).
