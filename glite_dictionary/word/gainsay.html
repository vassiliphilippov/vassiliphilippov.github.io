to gainsay

1. VERB

/ ˌɡeɪnˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌɡeɪnˈseɪ / (BrE)
to deny or contradict a fact or statement; to declare to be untrue or invalid
deny or contradict

Examples

No one dared to gainsay the evidence presented in court.
It is difficult to gainsay the importance of education in modern society.
She calmly gainsaid every accusation made against her.

Synset Examples

She challenged his claims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deny - to state that something is not true
  • Synonym: to contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement
  • Antonym: to affirm - to state that something is true

Etymology

From Middle English 'geynseien', from Old English 'gēansecgan', with 'gēan-' (against) + 'secgan' (to say).
