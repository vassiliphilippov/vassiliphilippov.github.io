unrecognized

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈrɛk.əɡˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈrɛk.əɡ.naɪzd / (BrE)
Not officially acknowledged or having failed to gain notice or a secure reputation.
not officially acknowledged or widely known

Examples

Many talented scientists remain unrecognized throughout their careers.
The artist's work was largely unrecognized until after her death.
Several unrecognized musicians performed at the festival, hoping to gain attention.

Synset Examples

short stories by unrecognized writers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unknown - not known or familiar
  • Antonym: recognized - officially acknowledged or accepted

Etymology

From un- (not) + recognized, from Latin recognoscere (to acknowledge, to know again).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈrɛkəɡnaɪzd / (BrE)
not identified as being known or acknowledged; not recognized.
not identified or acknowledged

Examples

Because of the heavy makeup, the actor was unrecognized by his fans at the event.
Her accomplishments went unrecognized for many years before she finally received credit.
He entered the city unrecognized, wearing a simple disguise to avoid attention.

Synset Examples

he was unrecognized in his disguise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recognized - identified as known or acknowledged

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'recognized', past participle of 'recognize', from Latin 'recognoscere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00029329-s
Synset POS: s
