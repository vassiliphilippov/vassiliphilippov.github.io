pontifex

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːn.təˌfɛks / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.tɪ.fɛks / (BrE)
A member of the chief council of priests in ancient Rome.
Roman high priest

Examples

Julius Caesar was elected as a pontifex before becoming dictator of Rome.
The pontifex played a key role in conducting public religious ceremonies.
Membership in the pontifex council was considered a great honor in ancient Rome.

Etymology

From Latin 'pontifex', meaning 'bridge-maker', originally referring to priests who oversaw bridges.
