capsid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæp.sɪd / (BrE)
the protein shell that surrounds the genetic material of a virus
protein shell of a virus
medical

Examples

The capsid protects the viral RNA from being degraded by enzymes.
Researchers studied how the capsid assembles around the virus's genetic material.
Each virus has a unique capsid shape that helps determine how it infects cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envelope - the outermost layer surrounding some viruses, outside the capsid

Etymology

From Latin 'capsa' (box).
