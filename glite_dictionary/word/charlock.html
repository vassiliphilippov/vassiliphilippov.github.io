charlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrˌlʌk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlək / (BrE)
a weedy Eurasian plant of the mustard family (Sinapis arvensis), often considered a pest in grain fields
a weedy Eurasian plant

Examples

The wheat farmer found charlock growing thickly among his crops, threatening the harvest.
A field overrun with charlock can be difficult to clear without chemical herbicides.
She recognized the yellow flowers of charlock nodding in the breeze beside the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mustard - another common name for charlock, a yellow-flowering weed

Etymology

From Middle English 'cherlok', of uncertain origin.
