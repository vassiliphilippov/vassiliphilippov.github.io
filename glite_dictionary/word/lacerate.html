3. VERB

/ ˈlæsəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæsəreɪt / (BrE)
to cut or tear something, especially flesh, roughly or irregularly
cut or tear roughly

Examples

The sharp wire lacerated his arm as he climbed over the fence.
She lacerated her hand on the broken glass but managed to bandage it quickly.
Doctors cleaned and stitched the dog's lacerated paw after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or rip; to damage by pulling
  • Synonym: gash - to make a deep, long cut
