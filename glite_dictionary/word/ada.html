ADA

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ diː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ diː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an enzyme in mammals that catalyzes the conversion of adenosine to inosine and ammonia
enzyme converting adenosine to inosine
medical

Examples

ADA deficiency is known to cause severe immune system disorders in humans.
Researchers measured ADA levels in the patient's blood as part of the diagnostic tests.
The discovery of ADA has greatly enhanced our understanding of genetic diseases.

Synset Examples

ADA deficiency can lead to one form of severe combined immunodeficiency disease
the gene encoding ADA was one of the earlier human genes to be isolated and cloned for study

Etymology

Acronym from Adenosine Deaminase.
