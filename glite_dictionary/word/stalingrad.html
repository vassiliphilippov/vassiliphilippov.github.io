Stalingrad

1. PROPN

/ ˈstælɪnˌɡræd / (AmE), / ˈstælɪnˌɡræd / (BrE)
A former name of the city now called Volgograd in Russia, famous as the site of a major World War II battle between Soviet and German forces in 1942–43.
former name of Volgograd, site of WWII battle

Examples

The Battle of Stalingrad is considered one of the turning points of World War II.
Many documentaries have examined the siege and destruction caused in Stalingrad.
After 1961, Stalingrad was officially renamed Volgograd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Volgograd - current name of the city formerly called Stalingrad

Etymology

From Russian Сталинград (Stalingrad), meaning 'Stalin's city', named after Joseph Stalin.
