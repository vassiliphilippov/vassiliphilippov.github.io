tenfold

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtɛnˌfəʊld / (BrE)
By ten times as much; increased or multiplied by ten.
by ten times as much

Examples

Profits have increased tenfold since we launched the new product.
The number of tourists grew tenfold over the last decade.
Her investment returned tenfold after only five years.

Synset Examples

the population increased tenfold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ten times - multiple of ten; by ten times as much
  • Degree Lower: twofold - by two times as much
  • Degree Higher: hundredfold - by one hundred times as much

Etymology

From Old English 'tienfeald', from 'ten' + 'fold' (meaning 'times').

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.fəʊld / (BrE)
Consisting of ten parts or elements, or being ten times as great or as many.
consisting of ten parts; ten times as much

Examples

The company's profits increased tenfold over the past decade.
He received a tenfold return on his investment in technology.
Membership in the club grew tenfold after the new program launched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ten times - ten times as much or as many
  • Degree Lower: single - one-fold; consisting of one part
  • Degree Lower: double - two-fold; twice as much
  • Degree Higher: hundredfold - a hundred times as much or many

Etymology

From Old English 'tienfeald', combining 'ten' and 'fold' (meaning times as much).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɛnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtɛnˌfəʊld / (BrE)
Consisting of ten parts or elements; being ten times as much or as many.
consisting of ten parts or times

Examples

The population of the city increased tenfold over the last century.
Profits rose to tenfold their original amount after the new product launch.
She experienced tenfold improvement in her performance through consistent practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ten times - multiplied by ten

Etymology

From Old English 'tēonfeald', from 'tēon' (ten) + 'feald' (fold).
