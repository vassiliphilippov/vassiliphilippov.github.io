lexeme

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛkˌsiːm / (AmE), / ˈlɛksiːm / (BrE)
A basic unit of meaning in a language's vocabulary, encompassing all forms and variants of a word.
basic unit of vocabulary

Examples

All the different forms 'run', 'runs', 'ran', and 'running' belong to the same lexeme.
Linguists study how each lexeme functions across various grammatical constructions.
The dictionary entry lists the lexeme rather than every inflected form separately.

Etymology

From French *lexème*, formed from Greek *lexis* (word, speech).
