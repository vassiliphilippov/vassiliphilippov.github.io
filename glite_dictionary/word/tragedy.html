tragedy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrædʒədi / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒədi / (BrE)
an event causing great suffering, destruction, or distress
event causing great loss or suffering

Examples

The sinking of the ship was a national tragedy that affected millions.
Losing his family in the fire was the greatest tragedy of his life.
The town came together to support each other after the tragedy struck.

Synset Examples

the whole city was affected by the irremediable calamity
the earthquake was a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or loss
  • Synonym: catastrophe - an event causing sudden and great damage or suffering
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and often sudden damage or distress
  • Antonym: blessing - a beneficial thing for which one is grateful; something that brings well-being

Etymology

From Greek tragōidia (goat song), via Latin tragoedia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrædʒədi / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒədi / (BrE)
A serious play, film, or other dramatic work in which the main character is brought to ruin or suffers extreme sorrow, especially as a consequence of a tragic flaw, moral weakness, or inability to cope with unfavorable circumstances.
serious drama with a sorrowful or disastrous ending

Examples

Shakespeare's tragedy 'Macbeth' depicts the downfall of a brave but flawed hero.
Many ancient Greek plays are classic examples of tragedy.
The performance was a powerful tragedy that left the audience in tears.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comedy - a genre of drama intended to amuse or entertain; typically with a happy ending

Etymology

From Greek tragōidia, from tragos (goat) + ōidē (song), originally 'goat song'.
