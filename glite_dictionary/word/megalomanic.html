megalomanic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛgələˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛgələˈmænɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing an obsession with power and grandiose ideas of personal importance or omnipotence; suffering from megalomania.
obsessed with power or delusions of grandeur

Examples

The megalomanic ruler demanded absolute loyalty from everyone in his kingdom.
Critics described the CEO's megalomanic ambitions as dangerous to the company's stability.
Her megalomanic plans for world domination were met with skepticism by her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megalomaniac - a person suffering from delusions of grandeur or omnipotence

Etymology

From 'megalomania' (delusions of grandeur) with the suffix '-ic' forming adjectives.
