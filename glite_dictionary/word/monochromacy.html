monochromacy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.nəˈkroʊ.mə.si / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əˈkrəʊ.mə.si / (BrE)
the condition of complete color blindness, in which colors are distinguished only by their brightness
complete color blindness
medical

Examples

People with monochromacy cannot distinguish colors and see the world in shades of gray.
Certain animals, such as some deep-sea fish, display monochromacy throughout their lives.
Researchers study monochromacy to better understand the mechanisms of color vision.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dichromacy - color vision deficiency with two types of functional cones
  • Degree Higher: trichromacy - normal color vision with three types of cones

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'chroma' (color) + '-cy' (state or condition).
