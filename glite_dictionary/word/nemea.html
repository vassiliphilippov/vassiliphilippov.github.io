Nemea

1. PROPN

/ nəˈmiːə / (AmE), / nɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
A valley in southeastern Greece, historically known as the site where the Nemean Games were held in ancient times.
valley in Greece; site of ancient Nemean Games

Examples

The archaeologists discovered ancient ruins near Nemea in the Peloponnese.
During their tour of Greece, they visited the famous valley of Nemea.
The Nemea region is also renowned for its vineyards and wine production today.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Νεμέα (Nemea), the name of the region.
