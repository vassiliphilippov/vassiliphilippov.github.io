given

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') having a tendency or inclination toward something; habitually disposed to behave in a particular way.
inclined or disposed toward

Examples

He is given to daydreaming during long meetings.
She became given to sudden bouts of melancholy in winter.
The author is given to using complex metaphors in his writing.

Synset Examples

he is apt to ignore matters he considers unimportant
I am not minded to answer any questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prone - inclined or likely to do something
  • Synonym: inclined - disposed or willing to do something
  • Antonym: averse - having a strong dislike of or opposition to something

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'give', used in phrases like 'given to'.

2. ADP

/ ˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
taking into account; considering (used to introduce a condition or fact that affects the situation)
considering; taking into account

Examples

Given the weather, our picnic will likely be postponed.
Given her experience, she was the obvious choice for team leader.
Given his age, he is remarkably active.

Synset Examples

given the engine's condition, it is a wonder that it started

Related Terms

  • Synonym: considering - taking into account; in view of
  • Synonym: provided - on the condition that; if

Etymology

From past participle of 'give', Old English 'giefan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00028393-s
Synset POS: s
