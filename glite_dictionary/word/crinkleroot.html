crinkleroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪŋkləˌrut / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkləˌruːt / (BrE)
a North American herb (Cardamine diphylla) with pungent, scaly or toothed roots, often found in moist woods
North American herb with pungent roots

Examples

In early spring, hikers often spot crinkleroot blooming in shaded forest areas.
The pungent roots of crinkleroot were once used as a spice by Native Americans.
You can identify crinkleroot by its toothed leaves and delicate white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothwort - another name for the same or similar plant
