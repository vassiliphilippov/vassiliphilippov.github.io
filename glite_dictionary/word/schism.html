schism

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪzəm, ˈsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪz.əm, ˈskɪz.əm / (BrE)
A formal division or split within a church or religious organization, especially due to doctrinal disagreements.
formal split in a church

Examples

The Great schism in the Catholic Church had lasting effects on European history.
A deep schism developed within the congregation over differing interpretations of scripture.
Efforts to heal the schism between the two sects have so far been unsuccessful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - the action or result of separating or being separated into parts
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole

Etymology

From Greek 'skhisma' (division, split).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəm / (BrE)
a split or division between strongly opposed parties or sections, often within a religious, political, or other organization
division into opposing groups

Examples

The debate created a schism within the committee, with members taking opposing sides.
Throughout history, religious schisms have shaped entire civilizations.
A deep schism developed in the party over the controversial policy changes.

Synset Examples

another schism like that and they will wind up in bankruptcy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the act of separating into parts or groups
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole

Etymology

From Greek *skhizma* (cleft, division) via Late Latin *schisma*.
