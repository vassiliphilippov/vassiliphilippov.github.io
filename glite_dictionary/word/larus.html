Larus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛə.rəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.rəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Laridae, comprising most of the typical gull species.
genus of gulls (birds)

Examples

Many species classified under the genus Larus are found along coastlines worldwide.
The herring gull, known scientifically as Larus argentatus, is a familiar sight in many harbors.
Biologists debated whether certain gulls should be grouped in the genus Larus or separated into other genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a common seabird with long wings and usually white or grey plumage

Etymology

From Latin *larus*, meaning a sea bird or gull.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛərəs / (AmE), / ˈlar.əs / (BrE)
A genus of gulls, serving as the type genus of the family Laridae.
genus of gulls (Laridae family)

Examples

Many species in the Larus genus inhabit coastal areas around the world.
The herring gull is classified within the Larus genus.
Researchers studied variations among the different Larus species.

Etymology

From Latin 'larus' (a kind of sea bird, gull).
