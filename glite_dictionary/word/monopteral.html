monopteral

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑptərəl / (AmE), / məˈnɒptərəl / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having a circular arrangement of columns supporting a roof, typically without walls.
having a circular colonnade (architecture)

Examples

The ancient temple is monopteral, with a circular colonnade supporting its domed roof.
The architect was inspired by monopteral designs from classical antiquity.
A monopteral rotunda stood in the center of the classical garden, visible from every angle.

Etymology

From Greek *monopteros* (single-winged: ‘mono-’ single + ‘pteron’ wing), used in architectural terminology.
