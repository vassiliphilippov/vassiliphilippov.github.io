puniness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːninəs / (AmE), / ˈpjuːninəs / (BrE)
the quality of being small or weak in size, strength, or importance
smallness or weakness

Examples

His puniness made him look even younger than his years.
Despite her puniness, she was quick and agile on the field.
The puniness of the kitten made everyone protective of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puny - small and weak
  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong

Etymology

From 'puny', possibly from Old French 'puisné' (born later), meaning younger or inferior in rank.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.ni.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unimportant, insignificant, or small; lack of strength or significance
the state of being small or insignificant

Examples

The puniness of their complaints made everyone ignore them in the meeting.
He was disappointed by the puniness of his achievements compared to his ambitions.
The novel highlights the puniness of personal worries in the face of larger world events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificance - the quality of being unimportant or trivial
  • Antonym: importance - the quality of being important or significant

Etymology

From 'puny' + '-ness', 'puny' from French 'puisné' (later born, inferior).
