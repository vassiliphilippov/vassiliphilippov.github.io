Antigonus I Monophthalmus

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtɪɡənəs waɪ ˌmɒnəfˈθælməs / (AmE), / ænˈtɪɡənəs waɪ ˌmɒnɒfˈθælməs / (BrE)
(c. 382–301 BC) A Macedonian general under Alexander the Great who later became king of Macedonia; known for losing one eye and for being killed at the Battle of Ipsus.
general and king in Ancient Macedonia

Examples

Antigonus I Monophthalmus rose to power following the death of Alexander the Great.
The defeat of Antigonus I Monophthalmus at the Battle of Ipsus marked the end of his ambitions for empire.
Studying the actions of Antigonus I Monophthalmus offers insight into the power struggles of early Hellenistic rulers.

Etymology

From Greek Ἀντίγονος Μονόφθαλμος (Antigonos Monophthalmos, 'Antigonus the One-Eyed').
