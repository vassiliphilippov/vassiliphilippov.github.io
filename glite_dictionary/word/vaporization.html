vaporization

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪpɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveɪpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a substance changes from a liquid or solid state to a vapor
process of becoming a vapor

Examples

The vaporization of water requires a considerable amount of heat energy.
A sudden vaporization of liquid nitrogen creates a cloud of mist in the laboratory.
The vaporization process is essential in distillation techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaporation - the process by which liquid turns into vapor, especially at temperatures below boiling point
  • Antonym: condensation - the process of a substance changing from vapor to liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam) and English '-ization' (forming nouns of action).

2. NOUN

/ ˌveɪpɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveɪpərəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of annihilating or destroying something by converting it into vapor, especially by intense heat.
annihilation by turning into vapor

Examples

The vaporization of the enemy outpost left nothing behind but a cloud of smoke.
Science fiction often depicts total vaporization as a result of futuristic weapons.
Some believe the energy release from instant vaporization could be catastrophic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaporation - the process of turning from liquid into vapor without necessarily annihilation
  • Synonym: destruction - the act of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Etymology

From Latin vapor (steam) + -ization (forming nouns of action or process).
