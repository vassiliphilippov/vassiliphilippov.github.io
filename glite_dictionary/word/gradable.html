gradable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪdəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a quality, property, or word) capable of being measured or described in degrees or levels.
able to be measured in degrees or levels

Examples

Temperature is a gradable quality, as it can be described as hot, warm, or cold.
Adjectives like 'happy' or 'sad' are often gradable, allowing for degrees of intensity.
The test scores were gradable on a simple numerical scale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-gradable - not capable of being measured or compared in degrees

Etymology

From grade + -able; grade from Latin gradus (step, rank).
