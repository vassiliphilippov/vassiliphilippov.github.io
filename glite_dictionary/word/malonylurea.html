malonylurea

1. NOUN

/ məˌloʊnɪlˈjʊriə / (AmE), / məˌləʊnɪlˈjʊərɪə / (BrE)
A white crystalline acid derived from pyrimidine, used as a base in the preparation of barbiturate drugs.
acid used to make barbiturates
medical

Examples

Malonylurea is an important intermediate in the synthesis of several barbiturate drugs.
The laboratory ordered a fresh supply of malonylurea for experimental purposes.
Chemists first identified malonylurea while studying the properties of pyrimidine derivatives.

Etymology

From malonyl (referring to malonic acid) + urea, reflecting its chemical structure.
