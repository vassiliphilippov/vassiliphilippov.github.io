swage

1. NOUN

/ swɛdʒ / (AmE), / sweɪdʒ / (BrE)
a tool used to shape, thicken, or spread the end of a bar, rivet, or other metal object by forging, hammering, or swaging
tool for shaping or thickening metal by forging

Examples

The blacksmith selected a swage to shape the end of the metal rod.
With a swage, you can efficiently create a rounded edge on a rivet.
After heating the metal, he placed it in the swage and struck it with a hammer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - a device for shaping material in manufacturing

Etymology

From Old French *souage*, probably of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ swɛɪdʒ / (AmE), / sweɪdʒ / (BrE)
to shape or form metal using a swage, typically by hammering or pressing
form metal using a swage

Examples

The blacksmith expertly swaged the iron bar to create a decorative pattern.
Engineers often swage metal rods to provide a tighter fit in machinery.
He spent the afternoon swaging copper tubes for the custom plumbing system.

Etymology

From Old French *souage*, likely of Germanic origin, related to Swedish *svagga* (to sway, bend).
