cross-dresser

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈdrɛsər / (AmE), / krɒs ˈdrɛsə / (BrE)
a person who wears clothes typically worn by the opposite sex, often for expression, comfort, or personal preference
person who wears clothes of the opposite sex

Examples

The documentary follows a cross-dresser exploring self-expression in a conservative community.
As a cross-dresser, he enjoys experimenting with different styles and personas.
Many cross-dressers participate in events that celebrate diversity and gender expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transvestite - a person who dresses in clothes associated with the opposite sex

Etymology

From 'cross-' (meaning across) + 'dresser,' denoting one who dresses; originally from mid-20th century English.
