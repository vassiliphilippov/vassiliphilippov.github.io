journalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.nə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites, or prepares news to be broadcast.
writer or reporter for news media

Examples

She has worked as a journalist for several major newspapers over the past decade.
The journalist conducted interviews with witnesses at the scene of the accident.
As a journalist, he is committed to reporting the truth without bias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reporter - a person who reports news or conducts interviews

Etymology

From French 'journaliste', from 'journal' (newspaper or daily record).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.nə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who keeps a diary or journal; someone who regularly records their personal thoughts or experiences.
person who keeps a diary or journal

Examples

In her youth, she was an avid journalist, filling notebooks with daily observations and reflections.
Before becoming famous, he was known as a meticulous journalist who detailed every event in his private diaries.
Many historians rely on the records left by dedicated journalists of the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diarist - a person who writes a diary

Etymology

From French journaliste (one who keeps a journal), from journal (daily record, diary).

Synset POS: n
