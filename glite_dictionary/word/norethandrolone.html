norethandrolone

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˌrɛθænˈdroʊloʊn / (AmE), / nɔːˌrɛθænˈdrəʊləʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic progestational hormone used in oral contraceptives and in the treatment of endometriosis
synthetic progestational hormone
medical

Examples

Norethandrolone is sometimes prescribed to help manage symptoms of endometriosis.
The contraceptive pill contains norethandrolone as one of its active ingredients.
Doctors evaluate the risks and benefits before recommending norethandrolone therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: norethisterone - a synthetic progestin similar to norethandrolone
  • Antonym: estrogen - a group of sex hormones, antonym as estrogen promotes female characteristics, not androgenic ones

Etymology

From chemical name components: nor- (–CH3 group removed), ethinyl, and androlone (derived from androgen and ketone).
