gassing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡasɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate act of poisoning a person or animal with gas, especially as a method of execution or chemical attack
deliberate poisoning with gas

Examples

The prisoners were victims of gassing during the war.
There are strict international laws against the gassing of civilians.
The report documented cases of gassing as a form of illegal execution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoning - the act of causing harm or death by a toxic substance

Etymology

From the verb 'gas' + -ing, referring to using gas as a weapon or poison.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡasɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of exposing something to a gas, especially for a chemical or industrial purpose
exposure to gas; process of applying gas

Examples

The gassing of the laboratory samples is essential for this experiment.
Improper gassing can lead to hazardous conditions in the plant.
Industries use gassing procedures to sterilize certain products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasification - the process of converting solid or liquid into gas

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'gas' + -ing (verbal noun suffix).
