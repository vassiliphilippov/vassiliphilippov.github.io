Gbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bits or 1,000 megabits
unit of 1,000,000,000 bits

Examples

The new network supports speeds up to 1 Gbit per second.
Transferring a large file requires at least a 10 Gbit connection for efficiency.
Most ISPs now offer plans with 2 Gbit internet speeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigabit - unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bits

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'gigabit' (giga- meaning 'one billion,' from Greek 'gigas', and 'bit').
