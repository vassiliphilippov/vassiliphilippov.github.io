ripe

1. ADJ

/ raɪp / (AmE), / raɪp / (BrE)
(of a person or group) fully prepared or ready for something, especially for change or a particular action; eager.
fully prepared or eager

Examples

After months of political turmoil, the public was ripe for reform.
The students were ripe for a new challenge after mastering the material.
With rumors spreading across the company, employees were ripe for a major announcement.

Synset Examples

the colonists were ripe for revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - prepared for something to happen or to do something

2. ADJ

/ raɪp / (AmE), / raɪp / (BrE)
(of fruit or food) fully developed or matured and ready to be eaten or used.
fully matured and ready to eat or use

Examples

The ripe tomatoes were picked and used in the salad.
You should wait until the bananas are ripe before eating them.
The vineyard produces wine only from ripe grapes harvested in the late summer.

Synset Examples

ripe peaches
full-bodied mature wines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unripe - not fully developed or mature; not ready to be eaten or used
  • Synonym: mature - having reached full development or growth

Etymology

From Old English rīpe (ready to harvest).
