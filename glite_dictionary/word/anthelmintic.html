anthelmintic

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəlˈmɪntɪk / (AmE), / ˌænθɛlˈmɪntɪk / (BrE)
a drug or substance used to expel or destroy parasitic intestinal worms in the body
drug to expel intestinal worms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anthelmintic to treat the patient's intestinal infection.
Many anthelmintic drugs are effective against a wide variety of parasitic worms.
Farmers regularly use anthelmintic treatments to protect livestock from worm infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermicide - a substance that kills worms, especially parasitic ones

Etymology

From Greek *anti-* (against) + *helmins, helminthos* (worm).

2. ADJ

/ ˌænθəlˈmɪntɪk / (AmE), / ˌanθɛlˈmɪntɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) capable of expelling or destroying parasitic worms, especially in the intestines.
destroying or expelling parasitic worms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anthelmintic drug to treat the patient's parasitic infection.
Veterinarians often use anthelmintic medicines to protect livestock from intestinal worms.
Resistance to certain anthelmintic treatments has become a concern in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermifuge - a substance that expels parasitic worms

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + helminth (worm).
