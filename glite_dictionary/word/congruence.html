congruence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈɡruəns / (AmE), / kənˈɡruːəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of being in agreement, harmony, or appropriateness with something else
agreement; harmony; suitability

Examples

There is a clear congruence between her values and her actions.
The architect aimed for congruence between the building and its natural surroundings.
A lack of congruence in the data raised questions about the experiment's validity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - the state of having the same opinion or feeling
  • Synonym: compatibility - the ability to exist or work together without conflict
  • Antonym: incongruity - the state of being out of place or not in harmony

Etymology

From Latin congruentia (agreement, harmony), from congruere (to come together, agree).
