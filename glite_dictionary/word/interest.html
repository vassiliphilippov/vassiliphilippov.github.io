interest

1. VERB

/ ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
to attract the attention or curiosity of someone; to cause someone to want to know more about something
attract or engage someone's curiosity

Examples

The new art exhibit really interested her, so she decided to visit the museum.
He tried to interest his children in classical music at an early age.
The science program is designed to interest young students in environmental issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fascinate - to attract and hold the attention of someone intensely
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or weary

Etymology

From Old French *interest*, from Latin *interest* (it concerns, it makes a difference), from *interesse* (to be between, to be of importance).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
(usually plural) A social group or organization whose members have common aims and control some area of activity, especially to promote or protect their shared business or professional concerns.
group with common aims in an activity

Examples

Powerful financial interests played a key role in shaping the legislation.
Several interests collaborated to support the environmental initiative.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobby - a group seeking to influence legislators on a particular issue

Etymology

From Old French *interest*, from Latin *interest* ('it concerns, it is of importance'), from *interesse* ('to be between, to be of importance').

8. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
money paid regularly at a particular rate as a charge for borrowing money or as profit from lending money or investing
charge for borrowing money

Examples

The bank charges a high rate of interest on personal loans.
Most credit cards charge interest if you do not pay the full balance.
He invested his savings to earn interest over time.

Synset Examples

how much interest do you pay on your mortgage?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: principal - the original sum of money lent or invested
  • Degree Lower: dividend - a sum of money paid regularly by a company to its shareholders from its profits
  • Degree Higher: usury - the illegal action or practice of lending money at unreasonably high rates of interest

Etymology

From Old French *interest* (damage, expense), from Latin *interesse* (to be between, to concern, matter), originally meaning compensation for loss.
