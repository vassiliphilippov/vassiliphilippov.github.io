lovebird

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈlʌvˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored parrot native to Australia, often kept as a pet and known for its affectionate behavior
small, colorful Australian parrot

Examples

The couple bought a pair of lovebirds for their daughter's birthday.
A green and yellow lovebird sat on its perch, nibbling seeds.
Many people adore lovebirds because they form strong bonds with their mates.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvˌbɜrd / (AmE), / ˈlʌvˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored African parrot known for its strong pair bonds and affectionate behavior toward its mate
small African parrot; affectionate with mate

Examples

The pet store had several colorful lovebirds chirping in a large cage.
Many people admire the way lovebirds cuddle and preen each other.
She gave her grandparents a pair of lovebirds as an anniversary gift.
