allusive

1. ADJ

/ əˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / əˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
referring to something indirectly; characterized by allusions
making indirect references

Examples

The author's allusive style challenges readers to catch literary references throughout the novel.
Her speech was highly allusive, making subtle nods to classic mythology.
I found his comments allusive rather than direct, leaving much open to interpretation.

Synset Examples

allusive speech is characterized by allusions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail

Etymology

From Latin alludere (to play beside, to allude), via French allusif.
