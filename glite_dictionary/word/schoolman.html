schoolman

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈskuːlmən / (BrE)
A medieval scholar versed in scholastic philosophy, especially one associated with the universities of the Middle Ages.
medieval scholar versed in scholasticism

Examples

The influence of the schoolman was evident in medieval academic debates.
As a devoted schoolman, he spent years studying Aristotle's works.
Many schoolmen contributed to the development of European philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholastic - a philosopher or theologian of medieval scholasticism

Etymology

Formed from 'school' + 'man', referring to men associated with schools (universities) in medieval Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌman / (BrE)
a male scholar, especially one skilled in academic argument or associated with scholastic philosophy
male academic or scholastic philosopher
archaic

Examples

During the Middle Ages, a schoolman often participated in lengthy philosophical debates.
The arguments presented by the schoolman were rooted in classical logic and theology.
Today, the methods used by a schoolman may seem overly formal to modern scholars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholastic - a person, especially in the Middle Ages, engaged in scholastic philosophy and disputation

Etymology

From Middle English 'scoleman', from 'school' + 'man'.
