Schoolman

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌman / (BrE)
a scholar of the Middle Ages, especially one versed in scholasticism; a thinker or academic of a medieval university
medieval scholar skilled in scholasticism

Examples

The Schoolman devoted years to the study of Aristotle's philosophy.
Many Schoolmen wrote treatises reconciling faith and reason.
Thomas Aquinas is perhaps the most famous Schoolman of the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholastic - a medieval philosopher or theologian who used the method of scholasticism

Etymology

From 'school' + 'man'; refers to scholars of medieval schools or universities.
