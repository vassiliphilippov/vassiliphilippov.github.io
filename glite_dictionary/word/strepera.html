Strepera

1. NOUN

/ strəˈpɛrə / (AmE), / strɪˈpɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of large, crow-like Australian birds known as bell magpies.
a genus of Australian bell magpies

Examples

Strepera species are often observed in woodlands throughout southeastern Australia.
The currawong, belonging to the genus Strepera, is known for its melodious call.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of several Strepera individuals during the breeding season.

Etymology

From Latin 'strepera', meaning 'noisy', referring to their loud calls.

2. NOUN

/ strəˈpɪrə / (AmE), / strɪˈpɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of passerine birds in the family Artamidae, commonly called bell magpies, native to Australasia.
genus of bell magpies (birds)

Examples

The pied Strepera is commonly seen in southeastern Australia.
Researchers studied the vocal patterns of several Strepera species.
Within the Strepera genus, the currawong is perhaps the most recognizable.

Etymology

From Latin 'strepera', meaning 'noisy', referring to the birds' loud calls.
