Sidon

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdən / (BrE)
an ancient port city of Phoenicia, now in Lebanon, known as a principal center of trade and culture.
ancient Phoenician city (now in Lebanon)

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts in Sidon, shedding light on life in ancient Phoenicia.
The prosperous merchants of Sidon traded goods across the Mediterranean Sea.
In antiquity, Sidon competed with nearby Tyre as a leading commercial hub.

Etymology

From Greek Sidōn, from Phoenician ṣydn.
