croquet

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / krəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
A lawn game in which players use mallets to hit wooden balls through a series of hoops, aiming to be the first to complete the course and strike a central peg.
outdoor game with mallets and hoops

Examples

We enjoyed a relaxing afternoon playing croquet on the lawn.
The garden was large enough for a lively croquet match with friends.
She taught her children the rules of croquet during their summer holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polo - a sport played on horseback with long-handled mallets

Etymology

From French 'croquet', meaning 'little hook', referring to the curved stick used in early versions of the game.

2. VERB

/ kroʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / krəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
In the game of croquet, to drive (another player's ball) away by striking it with one's own ball.
hit another player's ball in croquet

Examples

She managed to croquet her opponent's ball across the lawn with a precise shot.
If you croquet another ball into a difficult position, you gain a strategic advantage.
During the match, he expertly croqueted his rival's ball out of bounds.

Synset Examples

croquet the opponent's ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roquet - to hit another ball with one's own in croquet

/ kroʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / krəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
to play the game of croquet, in which players use mallets to hit balls through hoops embedded in a lawn
play the game of croquet

She learned how to croquet during the summer holidays at her grandparents' house.
The club members croqueted competitively throughout the spring tournament.

Related Terms
