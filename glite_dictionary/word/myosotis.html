Myosotis

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːsətɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈɒsətɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, mostly blue-flowered plants in the borage family, commonly called forget-me-nots.
genus of flowering plants (forget-me-nots)

Examples

The border of the garden was filled with colorful Myosotis.
Many gardeners adore the delicate blue flowers of Myosotis.
Botanists often study Myosotis for its interesting floral structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forget-me-not - common name of plants in the Myosotis genus

Etymology

From Greek 'myosotis', meaning 'mouse's ear', referring to the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˈsoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪəˈsəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the borage family, known commonly as forget-me-nots, with small, usually blue flowers.
genus of forget-me-nots

Examples

The delicate blue flowers of the Myosotis brighten many gardens in early spring.
Several species within the Myosotis genus are popular in ornamental horticulture.
She planted Myosotis along the edge of the pond for a splash of color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forget-me-not - common name for plants of the genus Myosotis

Etymology

From Greek myos (mouse) + ōtis (ear), referring to the shape of the leaves.
