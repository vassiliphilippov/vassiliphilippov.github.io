Gnetales

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / nɛˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly tropical or xerophytic woody plants, sometimes considered close to the ancestral line of flowering plants
order of tropical or dry-region woody plants

Examples

The Gnetales are a small order of plants often found in tropical regions.
Scientists study Gnetales to understand the evolution of flowering plants.
Some members of the Gnetales are adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments.

Etymology

From the genus name Gnetum + Latin '-ales', a suffix denoting orders in botanical nomenclature.
