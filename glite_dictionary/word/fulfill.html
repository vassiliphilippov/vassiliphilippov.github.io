to fulfill

1. VERB

/ fʊlˈfɪl / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪl / (BrE)
to meet the requirements or expectations of a duty, promise, or condition
meet requirements or expectations

Examples

She worked hard to fulfill the requirements for her degree.
The company did not fulfill its contractual obligations to the client.
His performance failed to fulfill our high expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satisfy - meet the requirements or needs of
  • Antonym: to violate - fail to meet or break a requirement or promise

Etymology

From Old English *fullfyllan* (to fill full).

2. VERB

/ fʊlˈfɪl / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪl / (BrE)
to satisfy or meet a need, requirement, condition, or restriction; to carry out something as promised or expected
satisfy a need or requirement

Examples

I don't think this proposal will fulfill all the requirements set by the committee.
The new facility was built to fulfill the growing needs of the community.
She hopes her work will fulfill the company's expectations for this project.

Synset Examples

does this paper meet the requirements for the degree?
This job doesn't match my dreams
meet a need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satisfy - to meet the needs or requirements of something
  • Synonym: meet - to achieve or fulfill a requirement or condition
  • Antonym: break - to fail to fulfill or satisfy (a promise, condition, etc.)

Etymology

From Old English 'fullfyllan' (to fill full); influenced by Latin 'fulfil(l)ere' (to fill up, accomplish).

3. VERB

/ fʊlˈfɪl / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪl / (BrE)
to carry out or put (a promise, duty, plan, etc.) into effect; to execute or perform as required
to carry out or put into effect

Examples

The company failed to fulfill its promise to deliver the product on time.
She was asked to fulfill the requirements set by the board.
After months of planning, he was finally able to fulfill his duty to the team.
