olde worlde

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊld ˈwɜːrld / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
attractively old-fashioned, especially in a way that suggests charm rather than authenticity
attractively old-fashioned (often not authentic)

Examples

The restaurant exudes an olde worlde charm, with its wooden beams and candlelit tables.
She loves shopping for olde worlde furniture at flea markets.
Tourists are drawn to the olde worlde streets lined with cobblestones and gas lamps.

Synset Examples

houses with quaint thatched roofs
a vaulted roof supporting old-time chimney pots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quaint - attractively unusual or old-fashioned

Etymology

Mock archaic spelling of 'old world', used to evoke the appearance or atmosphere of the past.
