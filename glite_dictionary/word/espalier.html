espalier

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈspæliər / (AmE), / ɛˈspaljeɪ / (BrE)
A framework or trellis on which a tree or shrub is trained to grow flat against a surface.
trellis for training trees or shrubs flat

Examples

The apple trees were trained against an espalier to save space in the garden.
She admired the roses blooming along the old brick wall and their espalier.
Building an espalier takes patience, but it creates a stunning garden feature.

Etymology

From French *espalier*, from Italian *spalliera* (shoulder support), relating to plants trained against a support.
