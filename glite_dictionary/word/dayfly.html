dayfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A slender insect with delicate, membranous wings whose aquatic larval stage is followed by a brief terrestrial adult life, usually lasting less than two days; also called a mayfly.
a type of insect with a very short adult life

Examples

The dayfly emerged from the water and fluttered briefly before settling on a nearby leaf.
Because the adult dayfly lives for such a short time, its arrival is often seen as a sign of a clean river.
Scientists studied the dayfly population to monitor the health of the aquatic ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - a similar insect known for its short adult span

Etymology

Formed from 'day' and 'fly', referring to the short lifespan of the adult stage.
