Mysidacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪsɪˈdeɪsiə / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈdeɪsɪə / (BrE)
An order of small, shrimp-like crustaceans commonly known as opossum shrimp, found in marine and freshwater environments.
order of shrimp-like crustaceans

Examples

Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Mysidacea near the coral reef.
The Mysidacea play an important role in aquatic food chains as prey for many fish species.
Studying Mysidacea helps scientists understand changes in freshwater and marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum shrimp - Common name for shrimp-like crustaceans of the order Mysidacea

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek mysid (a kind of shrimp) + -acea (denoting orders in zoological classification).
