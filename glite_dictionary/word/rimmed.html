rimmed

1. ADJ

/ rɪmd / (AmE), / rɪmd / (BrE)
having a rim or surrounded by a rim, especially of a particular kind or material.
having a rim or border

Examples

She prefers rimmed glasses because they feel sturdier on her face.
The dessert was served in a gold-rimmed bowl at the fancy restaurant.
He admired the snow-rimmed peaks glistening in the morning sun.

Synset Examples

do you wear rimmed or rimless glasses?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rimless - having no rim or border

Etymology

From the noun 'rim' + suffix '-ed', indicating possession of a rim.
