Bairdiella

1. NOUN

/ bɛrˈdiːliə / (AmE), / bɛːˈdɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sciaenidae, including species such as the armed croaker, silver perch, and swordspine croaker.

Examples

The genus Bairdiella is recognized for several species of croakers found in coastal waters.

Bairdiella species serve as important indicators of environmental health in their habitats.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɛərdiˈɛlə / (AmE), / ˌbɛːdɪˈɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sciaenidae, comprising several species including the armed croaker and silver perch, found in the Americas.
a genus of croaker fish

Examples

The genus Bairdiella includes several species commonly known as croakers.
Researchers identified a new specimen belonging to the Bairdiella genus along the Gulf coast.
The silver perch, a member of Bairdiella, is popular among local fishermen.

Etymology

Named after Spencer Fullerton Baird, an American naturalist.
