brant

1. NOUN

/ brænt / (AmE), / brɑːnt / (BrE)
a small, dark species of goose (Branta bernicla) that breeds in northern regions and migrates south for the winter
a small, dark goose

Examples

During their migration, thousands of brant can be seen flying in tight formation along the coast.
The wetlands provide an important habitat for brant during the winter months.
We spotted a flock of brant feeding on eelgrass near the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck, webbed feet, and a short broad bill

Etymology

From Old Norse brandgás (brant-goose); related to Old English brandgōs.
