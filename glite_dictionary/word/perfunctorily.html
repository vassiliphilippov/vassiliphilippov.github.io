perfunctorily

1. ADV

/ pərˈfʌŋktərəli / (AmE), / pəˈfʌŋktərɪli / (BrE)
In a way that shows little interest, care, or enthusiasm; done as a formality or routine rather than with real attention.
without genuine interest or care

Examples

She shook his hand perfunctorily, barely glancing in his direction.
The students completed their assignments perfunctorily, clearly lacking motivation.
After years at the job, he now greeted customers perfunctorily rather than with genuine warmth.

Synset Examples

they answered my letter pro forma
he kissed her cheek perfunctorily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanically - in a routine, automatic way, without understanding or thought
  • Synonym: dutifully - in a manner motivated by duty, not enthusiasm
  • Antonym: enthusiastically - with strong excitement or interest; the opposite of perfunctorily

Etymology

From Latin perfunctorius (done carelessly, negligent), from perfungi (to get through, discharge).
