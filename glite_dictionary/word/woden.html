Woden

1. PROPN

/ ˈwoʊdən / (AmE), / ˈwəʊdən / (BrE)
the chief god in Anglo-Saxon mythology; counterpart of Norse Odin and Teutonic Wotan
chief god in Anglo-Saxon mythology

Examples

Many early English legends speak of Woden as a powerful and wise deity.
The day Wednesday is named after Woden in Anglo-Saxon tradition.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts depicting Woden at several ancient sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odin - chief god in Norse mythology
  • Synonym: Wotan - chief god in Teutonic mythology

Etymology

Old English Wōden, from Proto-Germanic *Wōdanaz; related to Old Norse Óðinn (Odin).
