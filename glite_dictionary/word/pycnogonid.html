pycnogonid

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˈnɑɡənɪd / (AmE), / pɪkˈnɒɡənɪd / (BrE)
any of a group of small marine arthropods, also known as sea spiders, characterized by long, slender legs and small bodies
marine arthropod (sea spider)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of pycnogonid in the deep sea.
The pycnogonid moves slowly across the ocean floor on its long legs.
Marine biologists often study pycnogonids to learn more about arthropod evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea spider - another term for pycnogonid, a marine arthropod with long legs

Etymology

From Greek *pyknos* (dense, thick) + *gonu* (knee, joint), referring to their jointed legs.
