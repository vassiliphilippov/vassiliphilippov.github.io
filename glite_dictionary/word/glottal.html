glottal

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɑt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒt.əl / (BrE)
of or relating to the glottis, or produced by the glottis (the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them)
relating to the glottis or produced by it

Examples

The glottal stop is a common sound in many spoken languages.
Some English dialects use a glottal sound in place of the letter 't' in certain words.
The linguistics professor explained how to articulate glottal consonants.

Synset Examples

glottal stops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glottis - the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them
  • Degree Lower: velar - articulated with the back of the tongue against the soft palate

Etymology

From glottis (from Greek glōttis, from glōtta meaning 'tongue') + -al (adjectival suffix).
