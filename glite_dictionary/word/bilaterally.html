bilaterally

1. ADV

/ bɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl.i / (AmE), / bɪˈlæt.ər.əl.i / (BrE)
Involving or affecting two sides; in a way that concerns both sides or parts.
involving two sides or parts

Examples

The agreement was reached bilaterally, with both countries participating equally in the negotiations.
Patients often experience symptoms bilaterally, affecting both arms or both legs at the same time.
The treaty must be accepted bilaterally to become effective.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilaterally - done by or affecting only one side

Etymology

From 'bilateral' (from Latin bis 'twice' + lateralis 'of the side') + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ baɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl.i / (AmE), / baɪˈlæt.ər.əl.i / (BrE)
involving or affecting two parties, especially countries, equally or mutually
in a way involving two parties or countries

Examples

The two countries agreed to resolve the dispute bilaterally rather than involve a third party.
Trade issues were discussed bilaterally at the summit between the neighboring nations.
They decided to negotiate bilaterally to reach a mutually acceptable solution.

Synset Examples

they worked out an agreement bilaterally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilaterally - involving or decided by only one party

Etymology

From 'bilateral' + '-ly'; 'bilateral' from Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'lateralis' (of the side).
