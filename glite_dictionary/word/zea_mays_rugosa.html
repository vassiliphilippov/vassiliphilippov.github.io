Zea mays rugosa

1. NOUN

/ zi ə meɪz ruˈɡoʊsə / (AmE), / ziː ə meɪz ruˈɡəʊsə / (BrE)
A variety of maize with young ears that are sweet and edible, commonly known as sweet corn.
botanical name for sweet corn

Examples

Many gardeners choose to plant Zea mays rugosa for its exceptionally sweet kernels.
The agricultural exhibition displayed several varieties of Zea mays rugosa from around the world.
Research on Zea mays rugosa has contributed to improvements in modern sweet corn breeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet corn - A type of corn with high sugar content, eaten as a vegetable

Etymology

From Latin: Zea (a kind of grain) + mays (corn/maize) + rugosa (wrinkled), referencing the plant's botanical classification.
