Harkat-ul-Jihad-e-Islami

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɑrkət ʊl dʒɪˌhɑd iː ɪsˈlɑmi / (AmE), / ˌhɑːkət ʊl dʒɪˌhɑːd iː ɪsˈlɑːmi / (BrE)
an Islamist militant group based in Pakistan, known for seeking to establish an Islamic government and for its links and cooperation with the Taliban in Afghanistan.
Pakistani Islamist militant group

Examples

The government banned Harkat-ul-Jihad-e-Islami due to its involvement in terrorist activities.
Investigations found that Harkat-ul-Jihad-e-Islami had collaborated with groups in Afghanistan.
Authorities increased surveillance on Harkat-ul-Jihad-e-Islami after a series of coordinated attacks.

Etymology

From Arabic, meaning 'Movement of Islamic Jihad'.
