asunder

1. ADV

/ əˈsʌndɚ / (AmE), / əˈsʌndə / (BrE)
Into pieces; apart or divided.
into pieces; apart

Examples

The ancient statue was smashed asunder by vandals.
After years of conflict, their partnership was torn asunder.
Thunder split the silence asunder in the dead of night.

Synset Examples

he took his father's watch apart
split apart
torn asunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apart - separated by distance or into pieces

Etymology

From Old English 'onsundran', meaning 'apart, separately'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈsʌndɚ / (AmE), / əˈsʌndə / (BrE)
apart; widely separated or divided into pieces.
apart; widely separated

Examples

The ancient church was torn asunder by civil war.
After years of disagreement, their friendship was finally split asunder.
Lightning struck the old oak tree and broke it asunder.

Synset Examples

as wide asunder as pole from pole

Related Terms

  • Antonym: together - with or in proximity to another or others

Etymology

From Old English 'sundran' (apart, separately), from 'sundor' (apart, different).
