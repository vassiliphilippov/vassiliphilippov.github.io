arteria alveolaris

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə ælˌviːəˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ælˌviːəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
an anatomical branch of the maxillary artery that supplies blood to the alveolar process of the jaw
artery branch supplying the alveolar process
medical

Examples

During dental surgery, the arteria alveolaris may need to be carefully avoided to prevent excessive bleeding.
The arteria alveolaris plays a crucial role in supplying oxygenated blood to the upper jaw.
Damage to the arteria alveolaris can lead to complications in oral health procedures.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) and 'alveolaris' (of the alveolus), referring to its anatomical location.
