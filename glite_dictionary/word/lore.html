lore

1. NOUN

/ lɔr / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
traditional knowledge or beliefs passed from generation to generation, often by word of mouth.
traditional knowledge or belief

Examples

Local myths and animal lore have been handed down for generations.
The book is full of fascinating lore about medicinal plants used by indigenous tribes.
She studied the ancient lore of the sea, learning about tides, winds, and legends.

Synset Examples

early peoples passed on plant and animal lore through legend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tradition - the transmission of customs or beliefs from generation to generation

Etymology

From Old English lār (instruction, teaching, doctrine).
