countinghouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntɪŋˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a room or office in a business where financial accounts are kept and managed, especially in former times
office for business accounting (historical)

Examples

The old merchant's countinghouse was tucked away behind the warehouse.
Ledgers and invoices filled the dusty shelves of the countinghouse.
He worked as a clerk in the countinghouse before becoming a partner in the firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: office - room or building where people work at desks for business

Etymology

From 'counting' (the act of adding up) + 'house' (building or room); in use from the 15th century for places where financial records were kept.
