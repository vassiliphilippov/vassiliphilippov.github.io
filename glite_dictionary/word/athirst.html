athirst

1. ADJ

/ əˈθɜrst / (AmE), / əˈθɜːst / (BrE)
Desiring something very eagerly; extremely eager, especially for something such as knowledge or power.
extremely eager or desirous
archaic

Examples

She was athirst for knowledge, reading every book she could find on the subject.
The explorer was athirst for adventure, always seeking new lands to discover.
He grew athirst for recognition after years of working in obscurity.

Synset Examples

athirst for knowledge
hungry for recognition
thirsty for information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thirsty - having a strong desire for something
  • Antonym: fulfilled - satisfied, no longer desiring something

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'a-' (on, in, at) + 'thirst', meaning to be in a state of thirst; earliest uses in Middle English.
