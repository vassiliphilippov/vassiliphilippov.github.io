Portulaca oleracea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːrtʃəˈleɪkə ˌɑːlərˈeɪsiə / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtjʊˈlɑːkə ˌɒlərˈeɪsiə / (BrE)
A mat-forming herb with small yellow flowers, cultivated for its edible, mildly sour leaves, commonly used in Indian, Greek, and Middle Eastern cuisine.
edible herb with yellow flowers, known as purslane

Examples

Portulaca oleracea thrives in sunny gardens and is often used in salads for its crunchy texture.
Chefs prize Portulaca oleracea for its slightly tart flavor in Mediterranean dishes.
You can easily recognize Portulaca oleracea by its succulent leaves and bright yellow blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purslane - common name for Portulaca oleracea, edible leafy herb

Etymology

Latin scientific name: genus Portulaca, species oleracea, meaning 'vegetable-like' or 'kitchen garden plant'.
