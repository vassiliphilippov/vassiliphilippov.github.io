aculeus

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːliəs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːlɪəs / (BrE)
a sharp, pointed structure, especially the sting of a hymenopterous insect such as a bee or wasp
sharp-pointed anatomical structure; insect sting

Examples

The bee extended its aculeus in defense when threatened by the intruder.
Some wasps have a potent aculeus capable of delivering a painful sting.
The microscope revealed the sharp aculeus at the tip of the insect's abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - a sharp organ used by some animals to inject venom

Etymology

From Latin *aculeus* (sting, prickle), diminutive of *acus* (needle).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːliəs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːlɪəs / (BrE)
a stiff, sharp-pointed projection or process, especially one occurring on a plant
sharp-pointed plant projection

Examples

The cactus is covered in stiff aculei that protect it from herbivores.
Scientists examined the sharp aculeus on the stem of the wild rose.
A plant's aculeus can help deter insects and animals from feeding on it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spine - a stiff, sharp-pointed structure on a plant or animal
  • Synonym: thorn - a stiff, sharp, woody projection from a plant

Etymology

From Latin *aculeus* (sting, sharp point), diminutive of *acus* (needle).
