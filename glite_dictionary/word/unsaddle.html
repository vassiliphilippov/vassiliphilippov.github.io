to unsaddle

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈsædəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsæd(ə)l / (BrE)
to remove the saddle from a horse or another animal
remove a saddle from a horse

Examples

After a long ride, she carefully unsaddled her horse and led it to the stable.
The cowboys returned to the ranch to unsaddle their tired mounts.
You should unsaddle the pony as soon as you finish riding to keep it comfortable.

Synset Examples

They unsaddled their mounts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saddle - to put a saddle on a horse or animal

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + saddle; first used in Middle English.
