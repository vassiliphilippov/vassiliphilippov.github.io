intermediately

1. ADV

/ ˌɪntɚˈmiːdiətli / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiətli / (BrE)
to an intermediate degree; in a way that is between two extremes
to a moderate or middle degree

Examples

The solution was intermediately concentrated, not too strong or too weak.
She placed the lamp so the room was intermediately lit, balancing brightness and shade.
The contestants scored intermediately in the first round, ranking in the middle of the group.

Synset Examples

intermediately hot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderately - to a moderate degree

Etymology

From 'intermediate' + '-ly', following the pattern of adverb formation in English.
