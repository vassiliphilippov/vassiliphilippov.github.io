mojo

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.dʒoʊ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dʒəʊ / (BrE)
a magic charm, talisman, or spell; a quality that attracts good luck or success
magic power or spell; charm

Examples

Some say the musician lost his mojo after the accident and never performed the same again.
She carries a lucky coin as her personal mojo to help her through tough times.
After a long slump, the team finally regained their mojo and started winning games again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - an object or quality believed to bring good luck or protection
  • Antonym: hex - a magic spell, especially one that brings bad luck

Etymology

Possibly from Gullah, from African origin, related to West African 'moco', 'mojuba' (prayer, magic).
