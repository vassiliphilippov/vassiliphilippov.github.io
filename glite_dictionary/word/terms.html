terms

1. NOUN

/ tɝmz / (AmE), / tɜːmz / (BrE)
the status of the relationship between people or groups, especially as determined by their attitudes and dealings with each other
status of a relationship between people or groups

Examples

She has always tried to remain on good terms with her neighbors.
After the argument, they were no longer on speaking terms.
The company and the union are negotiating to get back on better terms.

Synset Examples

on good terms with her in-laws
on a friendly footing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relations - the way in which two or more people or groups feel and behave towards each other

2. NOUN

/ tɝmz / (AmE), / tɜːmz / (BrE)
conditions, especially the amount of money required to buy something or the arrangement of payments
monetary conditions or payment arrangement

Examples

We were offered better terms if we paid in cash.
She bought the apartment on favorable terms over five years.
The terms of the contract require a deposit before delivery.
