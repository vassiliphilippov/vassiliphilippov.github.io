abaxial

1. ADJ

/ æbˈæksiəl / (AmE), / æbˈæksiəl / (BrE)
(of a surface or part) facing away from the axis of an organ or organism, especially the underside of a leaf.
facing away from an axis (biology)
medical

Examples

The abaxial surface of the leaf was covered in tiny hairs.
Stomata are often more numerous on the abaxial side of many plant leaves.
In botanical illustrations, both the adaxial and abaxial surfaces of a leaf are frequently shown.

Synset Examples

the abaxial surface of a leaf is the underside or side facing away from the stem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adaxial - facing toward the axis of an organ; the opposite of abaxial

Etymology

From Latin 'ab-' (away from) + 'axis' (axis).
