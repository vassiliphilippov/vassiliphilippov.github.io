zodiac

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.di.æk / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.di.æk / (BrE)
A circular diagram representing the 12 signs of astrology, each linked to a constellation, used to predict or explain personality and events.
astrological diagram of 12 signs

Examples

The astrologer drew my zodiac on a piece of parchment.
She studied the zodiac to learn more about her star sign.
The zodiac is divided into twelve equal parts, each named after a constellation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horoscope - astrological chart based on the positions of celestial bodies at a particular time

Etymology

From Greek *zōidiakos* (circle of little animals), via Latin *zodiacus*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊdiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈzəʊdiˌæk / (BrE)
an imaginary belt in the sky, divided into twelve equal parts or constellations, through which the sun, moon, and planets appear to move, and which is used in astrology to predict events or describe personality traits
imaginary belt of constellations used in astrology

Examples

Many people believe their zodiac sign influences their personality and fate.
The constellation Leo is one of the twelve zodiac signs.
Astrologers often consult the zodiac when making predictions about the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrology - study of the movements of celestial bodies and their influence on human affairs
  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'zodiacus', from Greek 'zōidiakos (kuklos)' meaning 'circle of little animals'.
