Hedysarum coronarium

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛdɪˈsɛrəm ˌkɔrəˈnɛəriəm / (AmE), / ˌhɛdɪˈsarɪəm ˌkɒrəˈnɛːrɪəm / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant of southern Europe cultivated for animal forage and for its nectar-rich pink flowers, which are valued as a honey crop.
a forage plant with nectar-rich pink flowers

Examples

Hedysarum coronarium is often grown in Mediterranean regions for both grazing and honey production.
The bright pink flowers of Hedysarum coronarium attract bees, making it valuable to local beekeepers.
Farmers prefer Hedysarum coronarium because it improves soil fertility and provides nutritious forage for livestock.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Hedysarum' and species epithet 'coronarium', meaning 'crown-like'.
