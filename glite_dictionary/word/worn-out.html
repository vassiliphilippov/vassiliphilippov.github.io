worn-out

1. ADJ

/ wɔrn ˈaʊt / (AmE), / wɔːn ˈaʊt / (BrE)
too old or damaged from much use to be still usable; no longer useful because of being worn or exhausted
too old or damaged from use

Examples

After years of heavy use, his worn-out boots finally split at the seams.
She threw away the worn-out sofa and bought a new one.
This worn-out textbook is missing several pages and needs replacing.

Synset Examples

battered trumpets and raddled radios
worn-out shoes with flapping soles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battered - damaged by repeated use or blows
  • Antonym: new - recently made or not yet used; the opposite of worn-out

Etymology

Formed from 'worn', past participle of 'wear', and 'out', meaning 'completely' or 'to exhaustion'.

2. ADJ

/ wɔrnˈaʊt / (AmE), / wɔːnˈaʊt / (BrE)
Extremely tired or exhausted; no longer effective or usable due to long use or fatigue.
extremely tired or no longer effective

Examples

By the end of the week, I was completely worn-out and needed to rest.
These worn-out shoes should be thrown away because they have holes in the soles.
She looked worn-out after working two double shifts in a row.

Synset Examples

the day's shopping left her exhausted
he went to bed dog-tired
was fagged and sweaty
the trembling of his played out limbs
felt completely washed-out
only worn-out horses and cattle
you look worn out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - extremely tired; drained of strength or energy
  • Antonym: fresh - full of energy; not tired or used up
