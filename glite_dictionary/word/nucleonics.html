nucleonics

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːkliˈɑnɪks / (AmE), / ˌnjuːkliˈɒnɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies the structure, properties, and reactions of atomic nuclei
branch of physics studying atomic nuclei

Examples

She specialized in nucleonics during her postgraduate studies.
Advances in nucleonics have greatly improved nuclear reactor technology.
The conference brought together leading experts in the field of nucleonics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear physics - the physics dealing with the nucleus of atoms and nuclear reactions

Etymology

From 'nucleon' (a constituent particle of the atomic nucleus) + '-ics', patterned after 'electronics'.
