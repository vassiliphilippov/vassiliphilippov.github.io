whipcord

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.kɔːd / (BrE)
A closely twisted, hard cord, typically used for making the lashes of whips or for strong, durable cordage.
closely twisted hard cord

Examples

The craftsman selected a length of whipcord to repair the worn lash on the riding crop.
Her boots were fastened with sturdy whipcord laces for extra durability.
He examined the whipcord, noting its tightly twisted strands and resilience.

Etymology

Formed from 'whip' + 'cord', indicating cord used in whips.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪp.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.kɔːd / (BrE)
a strong, closely woven fabric, usually of worsted or cotton, with a characteristic diagonal rib, used especially for making uniforms, trousers, or outerwear
strong fabric with a diagonal rib

Examples

The trousers were made of durable whipcord that resists wear and tear.
He chose a jacket of navy blue whipcord for hiking because of its toughness.
Many military uniforms are traditionally made from whipcord fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twill - a fabric with a diagonal weave pattern
  • Degree Lower: gabardine - a tightly woven fabric with a diagonal rib on one side

Etymology

Named for its original use in making whips and similar cords.
