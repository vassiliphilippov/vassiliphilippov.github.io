friskiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪski.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪski.nɪs / (BrE)
lively and playful behavior or attitude; high-spirited playfulness.
lively, playful behavior

Examples

The puppy’s friskiness brought a smile to everyone’s face at the park.
There was a noticeable friskiness in his step after hearing the good news.
Spring often brings out a sense of friskiness in both children and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playfulness - the quality of being light-hearted or full of fun
  • Antonym: soberness - the quality of being serious and not playful

Etymology

From 'frisky' + '-ness', where 'frisky' is from early modern English, likely related to 'frisk' (to move in a lively way).
