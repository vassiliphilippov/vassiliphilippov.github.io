Ashkenazi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑʃkəˈnɑzi / (AmE), / ˌaʃkəˈnɑːzi / (BrE)
A Jew of eastern European or German descent, especially one whose ancestors lived in central or eastern Europe.
a Jew of eastern European or German descent

Examples

Many Ashkenazi Jews settled in the United States during the late nineteenth century.
Traditional Ashkenazi cuisine includes dishes such as gefilte fish and matzo ball soup.
The Ashkenazi community has made significant cultural and intellectual contributions to Jewish life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sephardi - a Jew of Spanish, Portuguese, or North African descent

Etymology

From Hebrew אַשְׁכְּנַזִּי (Ashk'nazí), meaning 'German,' originally referring to Jews living in the Rhineland and northern France.
