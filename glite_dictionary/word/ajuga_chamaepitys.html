Ajuga chamaepitys

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒuːɡə kæmiˈpɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːɡə kæmiˈpɪtɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing annual plant with yellow flowers speckled with red, faintly aromatic of pine resin, native to Europe, the British Isles, and North Africa.
low annual with yellow-red flowers, Europe/North Africa

Examples

Ajuga chamaepitys is often found in dry, sandy soils across southern Europe.
Botanists admire the distinctive yellow and red flowers of Ajuga chamaepitys.
The faint scent of pine resin made it easy to identify Ajuga chamaepitys in the field.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name Ajuga, species epithet chamaepitys from Greek khamai (on the ground) + pitys (pine), referring to its ground-hugging habit and pine-like aroma.
