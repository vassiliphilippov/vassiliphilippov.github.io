catalytically

1. ADV

/ ˌkæt̬əˈlɪkli / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or involves catalytic action or a catalyst.
involving or relating to catalysis

Examples

The enzyme acted catalytically to increase the rate of the reaction.
Some industrial processes are catalytically enhanced to improve efficiency.
Hydrogen peroxide decomposes catalytically in the presence of certain metals.

Synset Examples

catalytically stabilized combustion of propane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalytic - relating to catalysis or a catalyst

Etymology

From catalytic + -ally, after the pattern of adverb formation in English.
