2. NOUN

/ bɛnˈɡɑli / (AmE), / bɛnˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A member of an ethnolinguistic group native to Bangladesh and the eastern region of India, particularly West Bengal, who speak the Bengali language.
person from Bangladesh or eastern India

Examples

Many Bengalis celebrate both Hindu and Muslim festivals throughout the year.
The Bengali community has a rich tradition of literature and art.
She grew up in a family of Bengali origin in Kolkata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bangladeshi - person from Bangladesh

Etymology

From Hindi 'Banga' (Bengal) or Bengali 'Bangali'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08500364-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ bɛnˈɡɑli / (AmE), / bɛnˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
The Indo-Aryan language spoken by the Bengali people, and the official language of Bangladesh and the Indian state of West Bengal.
the main language of Bangladesh and West Bengal

Examples

He can speak fluent Bengali after living in Dhaka for five years.
The poet composed his verses in Bengali to reach a wider audience in his country.
They are offering Bengali language classes at the community center this fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bangla - Another name for the Bengali language

Etymology

From Bengali বাংলা (Bāṅlā), referring to both the language and the people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06983620-n
Synset POS: n
