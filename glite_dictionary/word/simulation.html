simulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of pretending or giving a false appearance, especially in order to deceive
act of pretending; false appearance

Examples

The magician's trick relied on careful simulation of impossible feats.
His politeness was only a simulation to win her trust.
She detected the simulation of sadness in his voice.

Synset Examples

his conformity was only pretending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - the act of making something appear true or real when it is not
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or genuine

Etymology

From Latin simulatio (pretence, feigning), from simulare (to imitate, feign).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
in computer science, a computerized imitation of real-world processes or systems used for analysis, experimentation, or training
computerized imitation of real-world processes

Examples

The new traffic system was tested through a simulation before being implemented in the city.
Flight schools use advanced computer simulation to train pilots in handling emergencies.
Scientists ran a weather simulation to predict the impact of climate change over the next decade.

Synset Examples

a simulation should imitate the internal processes and not merely the results of the thing being simulated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a simplified representation used to explain or predict real-world processes
  • Synonym: emulation - the process of imitating another system, especially in computing

Etymology

From Latin simulatio (an imitation, feigning), from simulare (to imitate, feign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05674544-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a model or imitation of a real-world process, system, or situation, often used for study, training, or testing purposes
imitation of a process or system

Examples

The flight simulation helped pilots practice for real emergencies.
In science class, the teacher used a computer simulation to show how earthquakes happen.
The company created a virtual simulation of the new product before manufacturing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a representation, usually on a smaller scale, of an object or system
  • Synonym: replica - an exact copy of something, often on a different scale

Etymology

From Latin simulatio (pretence, resemblance), from simulare (to imitate).

4. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of imitating the operation or behavior of a real-world process or system, often for study, training, or testing purposes
act of imitating a process or system

Examples

The flight simulation allowed new pilots to practice landing in challenging conditions.
Medical students used a virtual simulation to learn how to respond to emergencies.
The company developed a computer simulation to test the factory's workflow before construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a simplified representation of a system or phenomenon
  • Synonym: replica - an exact copy or reproduction, especially of something complex
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin simulatio, from simulare (to imitate).
