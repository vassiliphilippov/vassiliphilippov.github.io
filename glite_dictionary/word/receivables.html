receivables

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvəblz / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvəblz / (BrE)
sums of money owed to a business by its customers and expected to be received, typically as a result of sales made on credit
money owed to a business, expected to be received

Examples

The company's receivables increased significantly after the holiday sales period.
Managing receivables efficiently is crucial for maintaining healthy cash flow.
Most of their receivables are expected to be collected within 30 days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accounts receivable - money owed to a business by its customers

Etymology

From the adjective 'receivable' (capable of being received), ultimately from Latin 'recipere' (to receive).
