Rg

1. NOUN

/ ɑr dʒi / (AmE), / ɑː dʒiː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for roentgenium, a radioactive transuranic element with atomic number 111
symbol for roentgenium (element 111)

Examples

Scientists first synthesized Rg in 1994 by bombarding bismuth with nickel ions.
The atomic structure of Rg is difficult to study due to its short half-life.
Because Rg is highly unstable, it has no practical applications outside scientific research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roentgenium - a radioactive element with atomic number 111

Etymology

From Latinized form of Wilhelm Röntgen's surname (the discoverer of X-rays), combined with -ium.
