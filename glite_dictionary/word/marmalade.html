marmalade

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrməˌleɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.mə.leɪd / (BrE)
a sweet preserve made from the pulp and peel of citrus fruits, especially oranges, boiled with sugar
citrus fruit preserve with rind

Examples

She spread a thick layer of marmalade on her toast each morning for breakfast.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of orange marmalade to enhance the sauce.
We bought a jar of homemade marmalade at the farmers' market last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - fruit preserve made from crushed fruit and sugar
  • Synonym: jelly - transparent fruit spread made from juice and sugar

Etymology

From Portuguese 'marmelada', meaning quince jam, from 'marmelo' (quince).
