talipes

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæ.ləˌpiːz / (AmE), / ˈtæ.lɪ.piːz / (BrE)
A congenital deformity in which the foot is twisted out of its normal position, often resulting in a curled or turned appearance of the ankle, heel, and toes.
congenital foot deformity; twisted foot
medical

Examples

The infant was diagnosed with talipes shortly after birth and referred to a specialist for treatment.
Surgery and corrective casting are common methods used to manage talipes in young children.
Doctors explained that talipes can range from mild to severe, affecting either one or both feet.

Etymology

From Latin 'talus' (ankle) + 'pes' (foot).
