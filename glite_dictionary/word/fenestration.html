fenestration

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛnəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement, design, or pattern of windows and other openings in a building or structure
arrangement of windows in buildings

Examples

The modern house features a striking fenestration that allows for plenty of natural light.
Architects paid special attention to the fenestration to enhance the building's symmetry.
Historical homes are often recognized by their distinctive fenestration styles.

Etymology

From Latin 'fenestra' meaning 'window', via modern French 'fenestration'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɛnəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a new opening is made into the cochlea to restore hearing lost due to osteosclerosis
surgical creation of an opening in the cochlea
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a fenestration to help the patient regain some hearing ability.
Advances in fenestration techniques have improved outcomes for people with otosclerosis.
After his fenestration surgery, John noticed a significant improvement in his hearing.

Etymology

From Latin 'fenestra' meaning 'window', referring to the creation of an opening.
