go-between

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.bɪˌtwin / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.bɪˌtwiːn / (BrE)
a person who acts as an intermediary or negotiator between two people or groups
person who mediates between others

Examples

She acted as a go-between for the two companies negotiating the deal.
During the peace talks, a trusted go-between conveyed messages between the rival leaders.
As a go-between, he tried to resolve the conflict without taking sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermediary - a person who acts as a mediator between two parties
  • Synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement

Etymology

Formed from 'go' and 'between', first recorded in the 18th century to mean someone who goes between two parties.
