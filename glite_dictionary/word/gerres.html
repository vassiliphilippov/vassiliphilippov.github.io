Gerres

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛriz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛəriːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Gerreidae, comprising certain tropical and subtropical marine fish known as mojarras.
genus of fish in the Gerreidae family

Examples

The genus Gerres includes many small fish commonly found in shallow coastal waters.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of several Gerres species along the tropical shoreline.
The family Gerreidae is typified by the genus Gerres, known for their silvery appearance.

Etymology

From Latin *Gerres*, name used by ancient authors for a type of fish.

/ ˈɡɛr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈɡɛr.iːz / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Gerreidae, known as mojarras, found in coastal waters of tropical and subtropical regions.
a genus of fish (mojarras)
medical

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Gerres genus.
