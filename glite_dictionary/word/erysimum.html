Erysimum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈsɪməm / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈsɪməm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the cabbage family, comprising annual or perennial herbs with brightly colored flowers, commonly known as wallflowers.
genus of flowering plants (wallflowers)

Examples

Many gardeners plant Erysimum for its rich array of colors and long flowering period.
Erysimum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight conditions.
Several species of Erysimum are native to Europe and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - common name for many Erysimum species, known for their fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'erysimon', the name of a kind of cress.

2. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɪsɪməm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪsɪməm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, including annual and perennial herbs, some cultivated for their flowers or evergreen leaves
genus of flowering plants in the mustard family

Examples

Many species in the Erysimum genus are prized for their bright yellow or orange flowers.
The gardener added Erysimum to the border for its long-lasting blooms and attractive foliage.
Several members of the Erysimum genus are native to both Europe and North America.

Etymology

From Greek erysimon, a name for a plant in this genus.
