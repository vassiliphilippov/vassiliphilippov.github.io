sulphurous

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfjərəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfjʊərəs / (BrE)
(of air or weather) extremely hot and humid; oppressive, stifling, or suffocating.
oppressively hot and humid (of weather/air)

Examples

The sulphurous air made it hard to breathe during the heatwave.
We sat through a sulphurous afternoon before the thunder finally broke.
The tropical greenhouse was filled with a sulphurous humidity that clung to our skin.

Synset Examples

the summer was sultry and oppressive
the stifling atmosphere
the sulfurous atmosphere preceding a thunderstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sultry - (of weather) uncomfortably hot and humid
  • Synonym: stifling - (of air/weather) making it difficult to breathe; very hot or close
  • Antonym: refreshing - (of air or weather) pleasantly cool and invigorating

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphurosus', from 'sulphur' (brimstone).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfərəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfjʊərəs / (BrE)
containing or relating to sulfur; derived from or characteristic of sulfur, especially in smell or composition.
containing or related to sulfur

Examples

The volcano emitted a sulphurous cloud that hung over the valley for days.
A sulphurous odor filled the laboratory after the chemical reaction.
Medieval writers often described hell as a sulphurous abyss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfurous - containing or relating to sulfur (alternative spelling, American)

Etymology

From Latin sulfur (brimstone) + -ous (forming adjectives).

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfjərəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfjərəs / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) harsh, caustic, or scathing in tone or language.
harsh or caustic in tone

Examples

The politician's sulphurous speech left many listeners shocked and defensive.
Her reviews were famous for their sulphurous wit and biting sarcasm.
He delivered a sulphurous attack on his opponents during the debate.

Synset Examples

an acerbic tone piercing otherwise flowery prose
a barrage of acid comments
her acrid remarks make her many enemies
bitter words
blistering criticism
caustic jokes about political assassination, talk-show hosts and medical ethics
a sulfurous denunciation
a vitriolic critique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - (of speech or writing) severely critical or sarcastic
  • Synonym: vitriolic - filled with bitter criticism or malice
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong features or characteristics; mild (especially in speech)

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphur' (brimstone, sulfurous matter).
