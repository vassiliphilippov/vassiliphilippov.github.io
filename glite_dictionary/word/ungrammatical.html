ungrammatical

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˌɡræməˈtɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˌɡraməˈtɪkəl / (BrE)
Not conforming to the grammatical rules or accepted usage of a language.
not following grammar rules

Examples

The student's essay contained several ungrammatical sentences.
Using double negatives is often considered ungrammatical in standard English.
Her message was difficult to understand due to its many ungrammatical phrases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grammatical - following the rules of grammar or accepted usage
  • Synonym: incorrect - not correct; wrong

Etymology

From un- (not) + grammatical, from Late Latin grammaticalis (relating to grammar).
