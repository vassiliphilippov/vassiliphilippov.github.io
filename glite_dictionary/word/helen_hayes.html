Helen Hayes

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛlən ˈheɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɛlən ˈheɪz / (BrE)
(1900–1993) An acclaimed American actress of stage and screen, widely regarded as one of the greatest in theatre and film history.
acclaimed American actress (1900–1993)

Examples

Helen Hayes won the Academy Award for Best Actress for her role in 'The Sin of Madelon Claudet.'
The theater community often refers to Helen Hayes as the 'First Lady of American Theatre.'
A Broadway theater was renamed the Helen Hayes Theatre in her honor.
