squeaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːkər / (AmE), / ˈskwiːkə / (BrE)
an object that makes a sharp, high-pitched squeaking sound when used or operated
an object that squeaks when used

Examples

The old toy was a real squeaker when the kids played with it.
She traced the annoying noise to a squeaker in her running shoes.
Every time he opened the door, the squeaker hinge let out a loud noise.

Synset Examples

those sneakers are squeakers
which hinge is the squeaker?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeak - a short, high-pitched sound or cry

Etymology

From 'squeak,' imitative of the sound, plus the agent suffix '-er.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːkər / (AmE), / ˈskwiːkə / (BrE)
an event, result, or achievement that is decided or obtained by a very narrow margin; a close call
something decided by a narrow margin

Examples

The election turned out to be a real squeaker, with less than 1% separating the candidates.
That basketball game was a squeaker—they won by just one point at the last second.
It was a squeaker getting to the airport on time, but we made it just before the gate closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close call - a situation where disaster or failure is narrowly avoided
