frangible

1. ADJ

/ ˈfræn.dʒə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfræn.dʒɪ.bəl / (BrE)
easily broken; fragile or brittle.
easily broken; fragile

Examples

The lab technician handled the frangible vials with extreme care.
Because the artifact was frangible, it was displayed behind protective glass.
Please ensure that all frangible items are properly cushioned during transport.

Synset Examples

the museum stored all frangible articles in locked showcases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: durable - able to withstand wear or damage; not easily broken

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'frangibilis', from Latin 'frangere' (to break).
