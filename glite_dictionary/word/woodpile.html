woodpile

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌpaɪl / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌpaɪl / (BrE)
a stack or pile of wood, especially one kept for use as fuel
a stack of wood for fuel

Examples

After chopping the trees, he stacked the logs neatly in the woodpile.
In the winter, the woodpile behind the shed kept the fireplace burning.
A snake was hiding under the woodpile in the backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logpile - a stack or heap of logs
