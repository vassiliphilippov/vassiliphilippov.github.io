step-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛp ɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɛp ɪn / (BrE)
A short, one-piece undergarment for women or children, covering the lower body and usually held up by straps over the shoulders.
short one-piece undergarment

Examples

In the 1920s, many women preferred to wear step-in underwear under their dresses.
She bought several pairs of lace-trimmed step-in for her daughter.
Vintage fashion enthusiasts often seek out original silk step-in from antique shops.

Etymology

From the phrase 'step in', referring to the way the garment is put on by stepping into it.
