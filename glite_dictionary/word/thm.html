ThM

1. NOUN

/ tiː eɪtʃ ɛm / (AmE), / tiː eɪtʃ ɛm / (BrE)
A master's degree in theology, often pursued after a Bachelor of Divinity or Master of Divinity.
Master of Theology degree

Examples

After completing his ThM, he decided to pursue doctoral studies in biblical languages.
Applicants to the program must already hold a ThM or equivalent qualification.
She received her ThM from a prominent seminary in 2016.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: BD - Bachelor of Divinity degree
  • Degree Higher: DTh - Doctor of Theology degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Theologiae Magister' (Master of Theology).
