racialist

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that one racial group is inherently superior to others, often resulting in prejudice or discrimination
person who believes one race is superior

Examples

The politician was accused of being a racialist by several advocacy groups.
Many civil rights leaders spoke out against racialist ideologies in the 1960s.
A true democracy should have no place for racialist beliefs or practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racist - a person who shows or feels discrimination or prejudice against people of other races

Etymology

Formed from "racial" + "-ist" (suffix forming agent nouns), late 19th century.
