to x-ray

1. VERB

/ ˈɛksˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛks.reɪ / (BrE)
to take an x-ray photograph of part of the body, usually for medical examination
to take an x-ray photograph (verb)

Examples

The dentist decided to x-ray my teeth to look for cavities.
Every patient with a suspected fracture must be x-rayed as soon as possible.
They are x-raying the packages at the airport for security reasons.

Synset Examples

The doctor x-rayed my chest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiograph - make an x-ray photograph of something

Etymology

Formed from 'x-ray' (noun), itself from 'X' (the unknown quantity) and 'ray'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01005930-v

2. VERB

/ ˈɛksˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛksˌreɪ / (BrE)
to examine or photograph the inside of something or someone using x-rays, especially to diagnose medical conditions
examine using x-rays

Examples

The doctor decided to x-ray my chest to check for pneumonia.
After the accident, they x-rayed her arm to see if it was broken.
Veterinarians often x-ray animals to diagnose injuries or illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiograph - to obtain an image using x-rays

Etymology

From x-ray (noun), after the discovery of X-rays by Wilhelm Röntgen in 1895.
