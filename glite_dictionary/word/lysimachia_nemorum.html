Lysimachia nemorum

1. NOUN

/ laɪsɪˈmeɪkiə nɪˈmɔːrəm / (AmE), / laɪsɪˈmeɪkɪə nɪˈmɒrəm / (BrE)
A trailing evergreen plant native to Europe, with yellow flowers, commonly known as yellow pimpernel.
European evergreen with yellow flowers

Examples

The shady woodland path was lined with Lysimachia nemorum, its yellow blossoms catching the sunlight.
Botanists identified the trailing plant as Lysimachia nemorum, commonly known as yellow pimpernel.
Gardeners often plant Lysimachia nemorum as ground cover in moist, shaded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow pimpernel - Common name for Lysimachia nemorum, a European evergreen plant with yellow flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, after Lysimachus (Greek name), and Latin 'nemorum' from 'nemus' (grove, wood).
