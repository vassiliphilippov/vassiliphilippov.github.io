nursing

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɜːsɪŋ / (BrE)
the profession or practice of caring for the sick, injured, or infirm, especially as a trained occupation
work of caring for the sick or injured

Examples

After studying for four years, she finally began her career in nursing.
Good nursing can greatly improve a patient's recovery time.
Many people enter nursing out of a desire to help others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caregiving - providing care or assistance to others, especially the sick or elderly

Etymology

From Middle English 'nursyng', from Old French 'nourir' (to nourish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɝːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɜːsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of feeding a baby with milk from the breast; breastfeeding.
feeding a baby at the breast

Examples

Many mothers find nursing to be a bonding experience with their babies.
Some women experience difficulties during nursing in the first few weeks.
Proper nursing techniques can help prevent sore nipples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breastfeeding - the act of feeding a baby from the mother's breast

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɜːsɪŋ / (BrE)
the profession or practice of providing care for the sick, especially in a hospital
the profession of a nurse

Examples

She has been working in nursing for over twenty years.
The hospital is known for its excellent nursing staff and patient care.
He chose a career in nursing to help others in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine - the science or practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing illness
  • Synonym: doctoring - the practice of medicine by a doctor

Etymology

From Middle English 'noursen' (to nurture), from Old French 'nourrir', from Latin 'nutrire' (to suckle, nourish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
