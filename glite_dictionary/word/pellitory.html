pellitory

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪtəri / (BrE)
A small Mediterranean plant, especially Anacyclus pyrethrum, formerly used for its pungent root to relieve toothache.
small plant used medicinally for toothache

Examples

The herbalist recommended pellitory as a traditional remedy for toothache.
Dried pellitory roots were once common in European apothecaries.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of pellitory in ancient texts.

Etymology

From Old French 'peletre', from Medieval Latin 'pellitorius', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛləˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪtəri / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Parietaria or Anacyclus, typically found growing in crevices, with long narrow leaves and small pink, petal-less flowers.
herbaceous plant with narrow leaves and pink flowers

Examples

The old stone walls were covered in pellitory, thriving in the narrow cracks between the bricks.
Gardeners often find pellitory growing wild in shaded areas near buildings.
She brushed aside the pellitory to reveal a tiny gecko hiding beneath the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietaria - a genus of plants often referred to as pellitory

Etymology

From Old French *pelitoire*, from Medieval Latin *parietāria*, linked to Latin *paries* (wall), referencing its typical growing places.
