blabbermouth

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæbərˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈblæbəməʊθ / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who talks too much or reveals secrets, especially gossiping indiscreetly
someone who reveals secrets or gossips
informal

Examples

Everyone knows Karen is a blabbermouth who can't keep a secret for five minutes.
If you weren't such a blabbermouth, no one would know about our surprise party.
Calling him a blabbermouth hurt his feelings, but he promised to keep quiet next time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - someone who habitually reveals personal or sensational facts about others
  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial matters
  • Antonym: silent type - a person who is habitually quiet or reserved

Etymology

From 'blabber' (to talk excessively or reveal secrets) + 'mouth' (person who speaks).
