waggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwæɡl̩ / (BrE)
a movement in which something is moved repeatedly from side to side or up and down
side-to-side or up-and-down movement

Examples

The puppy gave a quick waggle of its tail when it saw its owner.
She performed a small waggle of her hips during the dance routine.
The golfer practiced his waggle before taking the first swing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiggle - a small, quick movement up and down or from side to side
  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly up and down or from side to side
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00348478-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈwæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwæɡl̩ / (BrE)
to move unsteadily or with short quick movements from side to side or up and down
move with a quick side-to-side motion

Examples

The puppy would waggle its tail excitedly whenever someone entered the room.
He tried to waggle the loose tooth with his tongue during class.
The fisherman began to waggle the bait to attract a bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wag - move (something) rapidly back and forth or up and down

Etymology

Probably a frequentative formation related to 'wag', meaning to move back and forth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01875314-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈwæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwæɡl̩ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move quickly from side to side or up and down, usually repeatedly
move from side to side

Examples

The puppy would waggle its tail excitedly every time it saw its owner.
She tried to waggle her loose tooth until it finally came out.
The bee began to waggle in a figure-eight pattern to communicate with the hive.

Synset Examples

The happy dog wagged his tail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiggle - to move with small movements from side to side or up and down
