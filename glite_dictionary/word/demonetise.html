to demonetise

1. VERB

/ diˈmʌnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈmɒnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
To officially remove the status of legal tender from a coin, note, or currency; to deprive something of its value as money.
remove status as legal currency

Examples

After the government decided to demonetise the old banknotes, they could no longer be used for transactions.
The silver coin was demonetised when the country's currency system changed.
Some countries demonetise coins as a way to combat counterfeiting and encourage the use of electronic payments.

Synset Examples

demonetize a coin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to monetise - to convert into or express in the form of currency

Etymology

From French 'démonétiser', from 'dé-' (removal) + 'monétiser' (to monetize), based on Latin 'moneta' (money).
