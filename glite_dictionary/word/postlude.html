postlude

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌluːd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌluːd / (BrE)
a piece of music, often played on the organ, performed at the end of a religious service
music played at end of a service

Examples

The organist played a magnificent postlude as the congregation exited the church.
After the final hymn, everyone sat quietly to listen to the postlude.
Her favorite part of the service was the uplifting postlude performed on the pipe organ.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prelude - a piece of music played at the beginning of a service or musical work

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after) + 'ludus' (play).
