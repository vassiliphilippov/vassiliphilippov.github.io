disrespect

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
rude or impolite behavior or remarks that show a lack of respect for someone or something.
rude behavior; lack of respect

Examples

His blatant disrespect for the rules surprised everyone in the meeting.
Teachers will not tolerate disrespect from their students in class.
By interrupting the speaker, she showed obvious disrespect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration or regard
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
rude or contemptuous behavior or lack of respect towards someone or something.
rude or contemptuous behavior; lack of respect

Examples

His repeated disrespect for the rules eventually got him suspended from school.
I won't tolerate any disrespect towards my friends in this house.
Many people were shocked by the disrespect shown during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respect - polite or admiring regard
  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless

Etymology

Formed by combining the prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) with 'respect', from Latin respectus (regard, consideration).

3. VERB

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to show a lack of respect for someone or something; to treat with contempt
to show a lack of respect

Examples

He felt hurt when his colleagues openly disrespected him during the meeting.
It's important not to disrespect other people's opinions, even if you disagree.
She apologized for disrespecting the rules of the organization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respect - to admire or show regard for someone or something
  • Antonym: esteem - to regard with respect or admiration

Etymology

From the noun 'disrespect' (lack of respect), formed with the prefix 'dis-' and the verb 'respect'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrəˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
a lack of respect or courtesy; a feeling or attitude that shows little regard for someone or something
lack of respect or courtesy

Examples

He spoke to his teacher with obvious disrespect in his voice.
Such disrespect for the rules cannot be tolerated in this school.
His disrespect towards authority figures got him into trouble more than once.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is beneath consideration; scorn

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'dis-' (denoting negation) + 'respect', from Latin 'respectus' (regard, consideration).
