conscious

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
done deliberately and with full awareness; intentional.
intentional; deliberate

Examples

She made a conscious decision to change her career path.
There has been a conscious effort within the company to promote diversity.
He's trying to be more conscious of his spending habits.

Synset Examples

a conscious effort to speak more slowly
a conscious policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • Antonym: unintentional - not done deliberately

Etymology

From Latin 'conscius' (knowing, aware), from 'con-' (together) + 'scio' (to know).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
aware of and able to perceive or experience one's surroundings, sensations, or thoughts; awake and responsive to the environment.
aware; awake; having perception

Examples

She remained conscious throughout the entire operation, astonishing the medical staff.
He was always conscious of the impression he made on others.
After fainting briefly, I became conscious again to the sound of voices around me.

Synset Examples

remained conscious during the operation
conscious of his faults
became conscious that he was being followed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: unconscious - not awake and not aware of and responding to one's environment

Etymology

From Latin 'conscius' (knowing, aware), from 'con-' (with) + 'scire' (to know).

WordNet ID: oewn-00572854-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
aware of and able to perceive or notice something; having realization or recognition (especially followed by 'of').
aware; noticing or recognizing something

Examples

She was fully conscious of her responsibilities at work.
He seemed barely conscious of the people around him at the party.
As a new driver, Mia was always conscious of the rules of the road.

Synset Examples

few voters seem conscious of the issue's importance
conscious of having succeeded
the careful tread of one conscious of his alcoholic load

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact

Etymology

From Latin conscius (knowing, aware), from com- (with) + scire (to know).
