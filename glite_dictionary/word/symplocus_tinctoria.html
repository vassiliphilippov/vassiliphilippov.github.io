Symplocus tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈploʊkəs tɪŋkˈtɔːriə / (AmE), / sɪmˈpləʊkəs tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a small yellowwood tree native to the southern United States, with fragrant white flowers and leaves and bark that yield a yellow dye
a small yellowwood tree of the southern US

Examples

The bark of Symplocus tinctoria was once prized for its use as a natural dye.
We spotted several Symplocus tinctoria trees in bloom along the woodland trail.
Botanists study Symplocus tinctoria for its ecological benefits and distinctive yellowwood.

Etymology

From New Latin, 'Symplocus' being the genus and 'tinctoria' meaning 'used for dye' in Latin.
