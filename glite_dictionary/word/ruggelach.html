ruggelach

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːɡələx / (AmE), / ˈrʊɡələx / (BrE)
A small crescent-shaped pastry made with cream cheese dough and filled with ingredients such as nuts, raisins, cinnamon, chocolate, or preserves.
crescent-shaped filled pastry

Examples

She served homemade ruggelach filled with chocolate and walnuts at the party.
Many bakeries feature ruggelach among their selection of traditional pastries.
I've never tasted ruggelach as delicious as those from the deli downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry - baked dough-based food, often sweet or filled

Etymology

From Yiddish *rugelakh*, plural of *rugele* (twist or roll), ultimately from East Slavic.
