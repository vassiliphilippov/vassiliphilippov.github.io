tenor

/ ˈtɛnər / (AmE), / ˈtɛnə / (BrE)
the general meaning, sense, or substance of something spoken or written

Examples

/ ˈtɛnɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛnə / (BrE)
of or relating to the highest natural adult male singing voice, or close in range to it.
of or relating to a tenor voice

Examples

  • Degree Higher: countertenor - of or relating to the adult male singing voice higher than tenor

Examples

He has a powerful tenor that captivates audiences worldwide.
The choir was looking for a new tenor to fill out the sound section.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alto - the adult singing voice below soprano and above tenor, usually female or boy's unchanged voice

Etymology

From Italian 'tenore', from Latin 'tenor' (holder, course), originally meaning 'one who holds'.
