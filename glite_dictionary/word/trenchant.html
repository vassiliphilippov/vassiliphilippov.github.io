trenchant

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛntʃənt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛn.tʃənt / (BrE)
(of language or a person) vigorous, incisive, and keen; clearly or sharply expressed, often in a forceful or penetrating way.
incisive and forceful; keen

Examples

The journalist was known for her trenchant analysis of political issues.
His trenchant remarks left no doubt about where he stood on the matter.
The review offered a trenchant critique of the film's weaknesses.

Synset Examples

searching insights
trenchant criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incisive - clear and direct; sharply focused
  • Antonym: blunt - not sharp or incisive; lacking in subtlety or force

Etymology

From Old French 'trenchant' (cutting), present participle of 'trencher' (to cut), from Latin 'truncare' (to cut).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛntʃənt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛn.tʃənt / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) vigorous, forceful, and clear; expressing strong, effective criticism or opinions.
forceful, vigorous in expression

Examples

Her trenchant criticism of the government's policy sparked a nationwide debate.
The journalist's trenchant analysis revealed flaws in the proposed reforms.
He is known for delivering trenchant commentary during televised debates.
