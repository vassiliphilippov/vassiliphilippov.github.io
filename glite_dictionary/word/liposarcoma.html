liposarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪ.poʊ.sɑːrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌlɪp.əʊ.sɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor that develops from fat cells, typically occurring in deep soft tissue
malignant tumor of fat cells
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the mass was actually a liposarcoma requiring immediate removal.
After her diagnosis with liposarcoma, she received chemotherapy and regular follow-up scans.
A liposarcoma can sometimes grow quite large before causing noticeable symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcoma - a type of malignant tumor of connective or other nonepithelial tissue
  • Antonym: lipoma - a benign tumor made of fat tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'lipos' (fat) + 'sarcoma' (fleshy tumor).
