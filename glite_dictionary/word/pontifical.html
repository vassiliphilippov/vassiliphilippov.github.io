pontifical

1. ADJ

/ pɑnˈtɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, governed by, or denoting a bishop or bishops; episcopal.
relating to a bishop or bishops

Examples

The cathedral still preserves its pontifical traditions dating back centuries.
He wore the ornate vestments reserved for pontifical ceremonies.
The pontifical authority was central to organizing the diocese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: episcopal - relating to a bishop or bishops

Etymology

From Latin 'pontificalis', from 'pontifex' meaning 'bishop'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02731793-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ pɑnˈtɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a person's manner or style) characterized by pompous self-importance or excessive solemnity.
pompous or self-important (of style or manner)

Examples

His pontifical tone irritated everyone at the meeting.
She gave a pontifical speech that made her sound detached from reality.
The manager's pontifical attitude annoyed his staff.

Synset Examples

a grandiloquent and boastful manner
overblown oratory
a pompous speech
pseudo-scientific gobbledygook and pontifical hooey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pompous - affectedly grand, solemn, or self-important
  • Synonym: grandiloquent - using lofty or inflated language
  • Antonym: modest - not overly proud or self-important

Etymology

From Latin 'pontificalis' (of a pontiff), via Old French and Late Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01855888-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ pɑnˈtɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, ordered by, or subject to the pope or papacy; papal.
relating to the pope or papacy

Examples

The new law was issued as a pontifical decree from the Vatican.
He questioned whether the pontifical authority extended to secular matters.
The cardinals awaited a pontifical decision before taking further action.

Synset Examples

papal dispensation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papal - relating to the pope or papacy

Etymology

From Latin pontificālis, from pontifex (priest, bishop).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02890942-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ pɑnˈtɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
the vestments and other insignia worn by a bishop or pontiff during liturgical ceremonies
bishop's ceremonial vestments and insignia

Examples

The bishop entered the cathedral in full pontifical for the important ceremony.
His ornate pontifical reflected the significance of the occasion.
During the procession, the priests admired the ancient pontifical displayed with reverence.

Etymology

From Latin pontificalis, from pontifex (high priest).

Source: WordNet
