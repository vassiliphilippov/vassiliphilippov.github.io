gelidity

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / dʒɛˈlɪdɪti / (BrE)
the state of being extremely cold; intense coldness, especially due to a cold environment
intense coldness; extreme cold

Examples

The gelidity of the Arctic air made every breath feel sharp and painful.
The mountaineers endured the gelidity of the high-altitude night without complaint.
Despite the gelidity outside, the cabin remained cozy and inviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coldness - the state of being cold
  • Antonym: warmth - the state of being warm

Etymology

From Latin 'geliditas' (coldness), from 'gelidus' (icy, cold).
