confused

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuzd / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
being in a state of disorder, disarray, or lacking clear arrangement; not organized or tidy
in a state of disorder or disarray

Examples

The room was in such a confused state that I struggled to find my keys.
A confused stack of papers littered the desk after the meeting.
Their reports created a confused picture of what had really happened.

Synset Examples

a confused mass of papers on the desk
the small disordered room
with everything so upset

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disordered - lacking order or organization
  • Antonym: organized - arranged in a systematic way; orderly

Etymology

From Latin 'confūsus', past participle of 'confundere' (to mingle together, confuse).

/ kənˈfjuzd / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
unable to think clearly or to understand what is happening, especially with regard to time, place, or personal identity.
