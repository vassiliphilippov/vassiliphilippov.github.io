polarography

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlərəˈɡræfi / (AmE), / ˌpəʊlərəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
an electrochemical method of chemical analysis that measures the current flowing through a solution as a function of an applied voltage to determine the concentration of substances.
electrochemical analysis technique
medical

Examples

The chemist used polarography to determine the concentration of metal ions in the solution.
Advances in polarography have improved the detection limits for trace elements.
Many environmental laboratories rely on polarography as a precise analytic technique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltammetry - electroanalytical method measuring current as a function of potential
  • Antonym: spectroscopy - method of analyzing substances by interaction with electromagnetic radiation

Etymology

From 'polar' (relating to electrodes) and '-graphy' (writing, recording), coined in the early 20th century.
