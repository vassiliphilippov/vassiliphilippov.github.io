pivot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪvət / (AmE), / ˈpɪvət / (BrE)
a short shaft or pin on which something turns or oscillates, serving as an axis of rotation
axis or shaft enabling rotation

Examples

The entire mechanism rotates around a central pivot.
He oiled the pivot to ensure the wheel would spin smoothly.
The clock's hands are attached to a small pivot in the center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinge - a movable joint or mechanism on which a door, gate, or lid swings

Etymology

From French 'pivot', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪvət / (AmE), / ˈpɪvət / (BrE)
the person in a formation or rank around whom others turn or maneuver
person around whom a group maneuvers

Examples

In the drill, the pivot remained stationary while the rest of the line wheeled around.
The pivot plays a crucial role in the coordinated movement of the marching band.
During the parade, the officer acted as the pivot for the entire formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center - the middle point or part of something, especially a group

Etymology

From French 'pivot', of uncertain origin, first used in English in the 17th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪvət / (AmE), / ˈpɪvət / (BrE)
an act of turning or rotating around a central point; a movement resembling the turning of a body on a pivot
an act of turning around a point

Examples

The dancer executed a perfect pivot in the middle of the stage.
During the drill, each player must practice their pivot to improve agility.
The golfer adjusted his pivot to achieve a better swing angle.

Synset Examples

the golfer went to the driving range to practice his pivot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - the action of rotating around an axis or center
  • Synonym: spin - a rapid turning movement around a central point

Etymology

From French *pivot*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old French *pivoter* (to turn).

Related Terms

to make a significant change in strategy, direction, or approach, especially in business or career contexts.
make a major strategic change
