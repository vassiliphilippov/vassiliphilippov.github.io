beforehand

1. ADV

/ bɪˈfɔrˌhænd / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔːhænd / (BrE)
before a particular event or action; in advance or in anticipation
in advance; ahead of time

Examples

You should prepare your notes beforehand to avoid last-minute stress.
Did you call to confirm the reservation beforehand?
It's better to arrange transportation beforehand when traveling to a new city.

Synset Examples

when you pay ahead (or in advance) you receive a discount
We like to plan ahead
should have made reservations beforehand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afterwards - at a later or subsequent time

Etymology

From Middle English 'be-foren-hond', literally meaning 'before in the hand' (in one’s possession beforehand).

2. ADV

/ bɪˈfɔrˌhænd / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔːhænd / (BrE)
before the time when something happens; in advance
in advance; ahead of time

Examples

She had prepared dinner beforehand so she wouldn't be rushed when guests arrived.
It’s helpful to read the instructions beforehand to avoid mistakes.
Please let me know beforehand if you are unable to attend the meeting.

Synset Examples

gave advance warning
was beforehand with her report

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in advance - ahead of a scheduled time; earlier than expected

Etymology

From Middle English 'be-foren-hand', meaning 'at the hand before'.
