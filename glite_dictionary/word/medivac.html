medivac

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdiˌvæk / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪˌvæk / (BrE)
the transportation of sick or injured people to a medical facility, typically by helicopter or aircraft, for emergency treatment
air evacuation for medical care

Examples

After the accident, a medivac helicopter airlifted the injured hikers to the nearest hospital.
Military units were prepared for a rapid medivac if casualties occurred during the operation.
The doctor called for a medivac as the patient's condition was too critical to wait for a road ambulance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airlift - transport of people or supplies by aircraft, especially in emergencies

Etymology

Blend of 'medical' and 'evacuation'.
