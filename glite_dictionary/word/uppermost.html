uppermost

1. ADV

/ ˈʌpɚˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈʌpəməʊst / (BrE)
in or into the highest position; at or towards the top.
at or towards the top

Examples

After the earthquake, only a few blocks remained uppermost in the rubble.
She placed the folder uppermost on the pile so it would be seen first.
The bird soared uppermost above the treetops, almost out of sight.

Synset Examples

the blade turned uppermost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topmost - occupying the highest position or place
  • Antonym: lowermost - in or into the lowest position

Etymology

From Middle English, formed from 'upper' + '-most'.

2. ADV

/ ˈʌpɚˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈʌpəməʊst / (BrE)
in or into the most prominent or important position; foremost in attention or consideration
most prominent or important position

Examples

When making decisions under pressure, the safety of her team came uppermost in her mind.
Honesty should always remain uppermost when dealing with others.
Whatever thoughts were uppermost at the time, he spoke them freely.

Synset Examples

say what comes uppermost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foremost - most important or prominent
  • Antonym: last - coming after all others in order or importance

Etymology

From Middle English 'uppermost', from 'upper' + 'most', patterned on Old English 'ufemest'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00492876-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈʌpɚˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈʌpəməʊst / (BrE)
at or nearest to the top; highest in position or rank
highest or topmost in position

Examples

The uppermost shelf was too high for her to reach without a ladder.
The painting hung on the uppermost part of the wall, catching everyone's attention.
Snow had gathered on the uppermost branches of the trees after the storm.

Synset Examples

the uppermost book in the pile
on the topmost step

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topmost - at the very top, highest in position
  • Antonym: lowermost - at or nearest to the bottom, lowest in position

Etymology

Formed from 'upper' + 'most', patterned after 'utmost' and 'foremost'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02449665-s
Synset POS: s
