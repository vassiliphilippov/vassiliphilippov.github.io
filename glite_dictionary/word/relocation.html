relocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of moving to a new place to live or work, or the process of changing one's place of residence or business
the act of moving to a new place

Examples

The company's relocation to the new office was completed last month.
After many years in the city, she found the relocation to the countryside refreshing.
The military offered assistance with relocation expenses for transferred personnel.

Synset Examples

they say that three moves equal one fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - the act of changing location or residence

Etymology

From Latin 'relocare' (to place again) + English suffix '-tion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.loʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.ləʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of moving people or a group to a new place to live, especially after being forced to leave their home
the act of moving people to a new place

Examples

The government ordered the relocation of the residents after the flooding destroyed their homes.
After years of conflict, relocation became necessary for thousands of families.
The relocation of the entire village was completed within a few months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resettlement - the process of settling people in a new place after being forced to leave their previous home

Etymology

From re- (again) + location, first attested early 20th century.
