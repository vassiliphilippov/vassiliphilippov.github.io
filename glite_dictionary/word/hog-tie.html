to hog-tie

1. VERB

/ hɑɡ taɪ / (AmE), / hɒɡ taɪ / (BrE)
to tie together someone's hands and feet, especially with the hands and feet behind the back, so that movement is extremely restricted
to tie someone's limbs together tightly

Examples

The police hog-tied the suspect to prevent him from escaping.
Cowboys would hog-tie calves during rodeos as part of the competition.
She felt as though her parents had hog-tied her with their strict rules.

Synset Examples

The prisoner was hog-tied

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bind - to tie or fasten something tightly

Etymology

From the practice of tying the limbs of a hog for restraint; 'hog' (animal) + 'tie'.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑɡ.taɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡ.taɪ / (BrE)
to tie together the limbs of a person or animal tightly so that movement is difficult or impossible; to restrain someone or something very securely
tie tightly to prevent movement; restrain securely

Examples

The rancher had to hog-tie the calf to keep it from escaping.
She threatened to hog-tie her brothers if they didn't stop fighting.
Police officers sometimes hog-tie unruly suspects to prevent injury.

Synset Examples

He joked about needing to hog-tie the kids for bedtime.
They had to hog-tie the unruly goat to keep it still.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bind - to tie or fasten something tightly
  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent someone or something from moving freely

Etymology

From the practice of tying all four legs of a hog together for restraint.

3. VERB

/ hɑːɡ taɪ / (AmE), / hɒɡ taɪ / (BrE)
to tie all four legs of an animal together, especially a calf, to prevent movement
restrain an animal by tying its legs together

Examples

The cowboy demonstrated how to hog-tie a calf during the rodeo.
She learned to hog-tie a calf as part of the ranch work.
After catching the animal, they quickly hog-tied it to keep it still.

Synset Examples

The cowboy showed us how to hog-tie a calf properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truss - to tie up tightly to prevent movement

Etymology

From 'hog' (pig) and 'tie'; originally referred to tying pigs' legs.
