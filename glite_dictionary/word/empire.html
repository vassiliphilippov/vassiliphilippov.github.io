empire

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.paɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
a group of diverse companies or organizations controlled and managed by a single owner or group
large business organization or group

Examples

His media empire includes newspapers, television stations, and film companies.
She built a fashion empire starting from a single boutique shop.
The technology empire expanded rapidly by acquiring several start-up companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conglomerate - a large corporation formed by the merging of separate and diverse firms

Etymology

From Latin imperium (command, authority, empire).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
a monarchy or sovereign state ruled by an emperor or empress
a monarchy ruled by an emperor

Examples

The Roman empire was one of the most powerful civilizations in history.
Her research focuses on how the British empire influenced global trade.
After the fall of the empire, several smaller nations were established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - a country, state, or territory ruled by a king or queen
  • Antonym: republic - a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives

Etymology

From Latin imperium (command, authority, empire), from imperare (to command).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
A group of countries or regions controlled by a single ruler or government.
group of countries under one authority

Examples

The Roman empire was one of the most powerful in history.
At its height, the British empire spanned several continents.
The emperor governed the vast empire from his capital city.

Synset Examples

the British created a great empire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - a country ruled by a king or queen
  • Synonym: colony - a country or region controlled by another country
  • Antonym: republic - a country governed by elected representatives and a president, not by a king or queen

Etymology

From Latin 'imperium' (command, authority, empire).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛmˌpaɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
A large group of countries or regions that are controlled by one ruler or government, especially one ruled by an emperor or empress.
domain ruled by an emperor or empress

Examples

The Roman empire stretched across three continents at its height.
She studied the rise and fall of the Ottoman empire in her history class.
The British empire once controlled vast territories around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - a country or territory ruled by a king or queen
  • Antonym: republic - a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives

Etymology

From Latin 'imperium' (command, authority, empire), via Old French and Middle English.
