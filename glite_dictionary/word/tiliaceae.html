Tiliaceae

1. NOUN

/ tɪliˈeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / tɪlɪˈeɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
a former family of chiefly tropical and temperate trees and shrubs, including the lindens and related genera, now usually included in Malvaceae.
former plant family including lindens and jutes

Examples

Recent genetic studies have led botanists to merge Tiliaceae with the Malvaceae family.
Linden trees, once classified under Tiliaceae, are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
The Tiliaceae included economically important plants such as jute and some ornamental trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Malvaceae - the mallow family, containing many species including those formerly in Tiliaceae

Etymology

From Latin *Tilia* (linden tree) plus standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
