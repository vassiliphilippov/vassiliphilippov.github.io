intractableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtræktəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtraktəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being difficult to manage, influence, or control.
quality of being hard to control

Examples

The intractableness of the disease made treatment extremely challenging for the doctors.
Some teachers struggle with the intractableness of certain students' behavior in the classroom.
Negotiators were frustrated by the intractableness of the political situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intractability - the quality of being difficult to manage or control

Etymology

Formed from 'intractable' + '-ness'. 'Intractable' comes from Latin intractabilis (not manageable).
