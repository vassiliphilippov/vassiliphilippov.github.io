monovular

1. ADJ

/ ˌmoʊnəˈvjuːlər / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈvjuːlə / (BrE)
(of twins) derived from a single egg or ovum; identical.
derived from a single egg (twins)
medical

Examples

The doctors explained that the twins were monovular and therefore genetically identical.
Researchers studied several cases of monovular twins to understand inherited traits.
A rare medical condition was found to occur more often in monovular than in dizygotic twins.

Synset Examples

identical twins are monovular

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dizygotic - (of twins) derived from two separate eggs, fraternal

Etymology

From mono- (single) + Latin ovulum (small egg, diminutive of ovum).
