leporid

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpəˌrɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɛpərɪd / (BrE)
A mammal of the family Leporidae, which includes rabbits and hares.
animal in the rabbit/hare family

Examples

The biologist specializes in the study of leporids such as rabbits and hares.
Many leporids can be found in grasslands and forests worldwide.
Several species of leporid are vital to the ecosystem as prey animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small mammal with long ears and a short tail
  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running mammal similar to a rabbit

Etymology

From modern Latin Leporidae, based on Latin lepus (hare).
