vasodilative

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪsoʊdaɪˈleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌveɪsəʊdaɪˈleɪtɪv / (BrE)
a drug or substance that causes blood vessels to widen, increasing blood flow
drug causing blood vessels to widen
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a vasodilative to help manage the patient's high blood pressure.
Some headaches can be treated with vasodilative medications to improve blood flow.
Certain vasodilatives are used in emergency rooms to relieve chest pain caused by heart conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasodilator - a substance that causes blood vessels to widen
  • Antonym: vasoconstrictor - a substance that causes blood vessels to narrow

Etymology

From Latin 'vas' (vessel) and modern term 'dilate', with suffix '-ive'.
