penpusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnˌpʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɛnˌpʊʃə / (BrE)
a person, especially a clerk, whose work consists mostly of routine or tedious paperwork
person who does routine paperwork
informal british

Examples

He quit his job as a penpusher because he wanted something more exciting.
The company is run by penpushers who make all the decisions from behind their desks.
She refused to become just another penpusher in a government office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper-pusher - a person whose job involves mainly routine paperwork

Etymology

From 'pen' meaning a writing instrument, and 'pusher' meaning one who pushes; originally referring to those whose work mainly involved writing.
