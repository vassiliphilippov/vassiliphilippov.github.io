combine

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪn / (BrE)
a large machine that harvests, threshes, and cleans grain crops as it moves across a field
machine that harvests and threshes grain

Examples

The farmer used his new combine to harvest the wheat efficiently.
Modern combines can process dozens of acres in a single day.
She watched as the massive combine moved steadily across the golden field.

Etymology

Shortened from 'combine harvester', named for its ability to combine multiple harvesting processes.

5. NOUN

/ kəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌɪn / (BrE)
a group of independent companies or organizations that join together to control the production and distribution of a product or service and reduce competition
group of companies controlling production and competition

Examples

The oil combine was accused of fixing prices across several countries.
A number of smaller firms united to form a powerful combine in the steel industry.
Government intervention broke up the tobacco combine to encourage greater competition.

Synset Examples

they set up the trust in the hope of gaining a monopoly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartel - a group of businesses that work together to fix prices or limit production
  • Synonym: trust - a large group of businesses joined together to control the market

Etymology

From French *combiner*, from Latin *combinare* (to unite). The noun use for business groups dates from the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
