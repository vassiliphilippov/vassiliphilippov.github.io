syncretical

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈkrɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by syncretism; combining different beliefs, systems, or practices.
relating to syncretism

Examples

The region's religion is highly syncretical, blending local traditions with imported beliefs.
A syncretical approach to philosophy seeks to unite Eastern and Western thought.
Her art displays a syncretical fusion of various cultural styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncretic - relating to or characterized by syncretism

Etymology

Formed from 'syncretism' + '-ical'; 'syncretism' from modern Latin syncretismus.

2. ADJ

/ sɪŋˈkrɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to a historical tendency in a language to reduce or merge inflectional forms, especially in grammar.
tending to reduce grammatical inflections

Examples

Linguists describe modern English as a syncretical language due to its simplified inflectional system.
Old English had many more inflections than its syncretical descendant, modern English.
Some scholars debate how syncretical tendencies have shaped English grammar over the centuries.

Synset Examples

modern English is a syncretic language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analytic - pertaining to languages that use few or no inflections
  • Antonym: inflectional - pertaining to languages that make extensive use of inflections

Etymology

From Greek *synkretismos* (union of different things) via Late Latin *syncretismus* and English *syncretism*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02818598-a
Synset POS: a
