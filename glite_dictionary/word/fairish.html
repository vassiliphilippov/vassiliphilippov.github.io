fairish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfeərɪʃ / (BrE)
moderately fair in amount, degree, or quality; not excessive or extreme
moderately fair; not excessive

Examples

He earns a fairish wage working at the factory.
We had a fairish chance of winning the game, despite being the underdogs.
The weather was fairish for our picnic—neither perfectly sunny nor particularly rainy.

Synset Examples

a fairish income
reasonable prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fair - moderately good, acceptable, or just
  • Synonym: reasonable - not exceeding reasonable limits; moderate

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix '-ish' to 'fair', indicating 'somewhat fair'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɛɹɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfeərɪʃ / (BrE)
Somewhat fair in color; moderately pale or light, especially of hair or skin.
somewhat pale or light in color (hair or skin)

Examples

She has fairish hair that shines in the sunlight.
After a summer indoors, his complexion remained rather fairish.
Their child was born with fairish skin, much lighter than his parents expected.

Synset Examples

a fair complexion

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fair - light in color, especially of hair or skin
