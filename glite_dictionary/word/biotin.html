biotin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.ə.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.ətɪn / (BrE)
a B-complex vitamin that is essential for body growth and metabolism
a B-complex vitamin
medical

Examples

Egg yolks and organ meats are rich sources of biotin.
Many hair and nail supplements contain biotin to promote growth and strength.
A deficiency in biotin can lead to skin problems and thinning hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B7 - another name for biotin
  • Degree Higher: vitamin B12 - another B-complex vitamin

Etymology

From Greek biotos (life) + -in (a chemical suffix).
