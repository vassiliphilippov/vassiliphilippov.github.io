Curcuma domestica

1. NOUN

/ kɜrˈkuːmə doʊˈmɛstɪkə / (AmE), / kɜːˈkuːmə dəʊˈmɛstɪkə / (BrE)
A tropical plant native to India, with yellow flowers and a deep yellow aromatic rhizome, widely cultivated as a source of turmeric used as a condiment and dye.
plant used for turmeric and as a dye

Examples

Curcuma domestica is prized for its rhizome, which is often ground into the spice turmeric.
Farmers in India have cultivated Curcuma domestica for centuries for its culinary and medicinal uses.
The yellow dye extracted from Curcuma domestica is traditionally used in fabrics and religious ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmeric - common name for the spice and the plant's rhizome

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'Curcuma' (the genus, from Arabic 'kurkum' meaning saffron or turmeric) and 'domestica' (meaning domestic or cultivated).
