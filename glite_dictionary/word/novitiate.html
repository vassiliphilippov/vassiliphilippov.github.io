novitiate

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈvɪʃiət / (AmE), / nəʊˈvɪʃɪət / (BrE)
a person who has entered a religious order as a novice but has not yet taken final vows
person in a religious order not fully admitted

Examples

The young novitiate was eager to begin his spiritual journey within the monastery.
During her year as a novitiate, she followed a strict routine of prayer and study.
The abbess spoke kindly to the new novitiate about the challenges of the religious life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field, especially a new member of a religious order

Etymology

From Latin noviciatus (period of being a novice), from novicius (new, novice).

2. NOUN

/ noʊˈvɪʃi.ət / (AmE), / nəʊˈvɪʃɪət / (BrE)
the period during which a person is a novice, especially in a religious order, undergoing training and preparation before taking vows
period of training as a novice

Examples

During her novitiate, she learned the rituals and disciplines required to become a full member of the convent.
The monastery has strict rules for those in their novitiate to help them adjust to religious life.
He reflected on his challenging novitiate as a time of personal growth and deep spiritual learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person who is new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin novitiatus (state of being a novice), from Latin novicius (new, novice).
