Fagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees commonly known as beeches, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of beech trees

Examples

The genus Fagus includes several species of beech trees found in Europe, Asia, and North America.
Botanists discussed the characteristics distinguishing Fagus from other genera in the Fagaceae family.
Leaves of Fagus are simple and arranged alternately on the branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech - deciduous tree producing nuts, of the genus Fagus

Etymology

From Latin 'Fagus' (beech tree), from Greek 'phagos' (oak or beech).
