dollar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
the main unit of currency in the United States, Canada, Australia, and several other countries, equal to 100 cents
currency unit equal to 100 cents

Examples

He paid five dollars for the book at the store.
The Australian dollar has strengthened against the U.S. dollar this week.
She found a dollar bill on the sidewalk while walking her dog.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cent - one hundredth of a dollar

Etymology

From early Dutch 'daler', short for 'rijksdaalder', from German 'Thaler' (a silver coin named after Joachimsthal, a town in Bohemia).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
A piece of paper money worth one dollar, especially in the currency of the United States or other countries that use the dollar as their unit of currency.
a one-dollar bill or note

Examples

He handed me a crisp dollar to pay for the drink.
I found a dollar lying on the sidewalk this morning.
She slipped a dollar into the charity donation box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dollar bill - a banknote worth one dollar (U.S. currency)
  • Synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money used as currency
  • Antonym: coin - a piece of metal money

Etymology

From Low German *daler*, short for *Joachimsdaler*, a coin from St Joachimsthal (now Jáchymov in the Czech Republic).
