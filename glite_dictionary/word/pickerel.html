pickerel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkərəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkərəl / (BrE)
The flesh of a young or small pike, used as food.
flesh of a young or small pike (as food)

Examples

The restaurant served grilled pickerel with lemon and herbs.
Fishermen in the area often catch pickerel for local markets.
She prepared a dish using freshly caught pickerel from the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - large predatory freshwater fish of the genus Esox

Etymology

From Middle English pikerel, diminutive of pike (the fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkərəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkərəl / (BrE)
any of several small, slender pike native to North America, especially of the genera Esox or related genera
a small North American pike

Examples

The lake is known for its large population of pickerel and bass.
Anglers often use live bait to catch pickerel in shallow waters.
A young pickerel darted through the weeds as we paddled by.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pike - a large freshwater fish of the family Esocidae
  • Degree Higher: muskellunge - a large North American pike

Etymology

From Middle English 'pikerel', diminutive of 'pike' (the fish).
