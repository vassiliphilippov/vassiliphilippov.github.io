Schefflera actinophylla

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈflɛrə ˌæktɪnoʊˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / ʃɪˈflɛrə ˌæktɪnəʊˈfʌɪlə / (BrE)
An upright evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia and northern New Guinea, known for its palmately compound leaves and often grown as an ornamental plant.
evergreen tree or shrub native to Australia and New Guinea

Examples

The Schefflera actinophylla thrives in bright, indirect sunlight, making it popular as a houseplant.
In its native habitat, Schefflera actinophylla can grow up to fifteen meters tall.
Gardeners admire the glossy, hand-shaped leaves of the Schefflera actinophylla for their striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella tree - another common name for Schefflera actinophylla

Etymology

Schefflera: Named after German botanist Jacob Christian Scheffler; actinophylla: from Greek 'aktis' (ray) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to radiating leaflets.
