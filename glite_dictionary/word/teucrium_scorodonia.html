Teucrium scorodonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːkriəm skoːˈrɒdoniə / (AmE), / ˈtjuːkriəm skɒˈrɒdɒnɪə / (BrE)
A European species of germander with one-sided racemes of yellow flowers, naturalized in North America.
a European species of germander plant

Examples

The woodland undergrowth was dotted with Teucrium scorodonia in full bloom.
Gardeners often cultivate Teucrium scorodonia for its attractive yellow flowers.
Teucrium scorodonia has become naturalized in parts of North America.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Teucrium' is the genus name and 'scorodonia' is the species epithet.
