uniting

1. NOUN

/ juˈnaɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / juˈnaɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of joining together separate people or things to form a single unit
the act of joining into one

Examples

The uniting of the two companies created a more powerful corporation.
Many hope that the uniting of the communities will bring lasting peace and cooperation.
The uniting of our efforts is crucial if we want to succeed in this project.

Synset Examples

the union of opposing factions
he looked forward to the unification of his family for the holidays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the action or fact of joining together; the state of being united
  • Antonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart

2. NOUN

/ juˈnaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / juˈnaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of combining two or more commercial companies into one organization
combination of companies

Examples

The uniting of the two firms created the largest corporation in the region.
There was much debate about whether the uniting of these businesses would benefit consumers.
After years of negotiation, the official uniting of the companies was announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merger - the joining together of two or more companies into one
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01240989-n
Synset POS: n
