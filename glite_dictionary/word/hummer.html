hummer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌmə / (BrE)
a person who sings a tune by humming, without opening the lips or forming words
person who sings by humming

Examples

Whenever she was nervous, she became a quiet hummer in the back of the classroom.
The gentle hummer filled the room with a soft, soothing melody.
As a child, he was known as a constant hummer, inventing tunes wherever he went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - to make a continuous low sound or sing without words

Etymology

From the verb 'hum' (to make a low, continuous sound) + -er (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌmə / (BrE)
(Baseball, US) A pitch thrown with maximum velocity; a very fast pitch, especially a fastball.
very fast baseball pitch
american

Examples

The pitcher threw a hummer right past the batter for a strike.
His hummer was so fast that few hitters could even make contact.
Fans cheered when she unleashed her signature hummer in the final inning.

Synset Examples

he swung late on the fastball
he showed batters nothing but smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastball - a baseball pitch thrown at high speed
