luminosity

1. NOUN

/ luːməˈnɑːsəti / (AmE), / luːmɪˈnɒsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being luminous; the state of emitting or reflecting light, especially as measured in astronomy or physics
quality of emitting or reflecting light

Examples

Astronomers calculate a star's luminosity to compare its light output with that of our sun.
The luminosity of the painting made it stand out in the dimly lit gallery.
Researchers studied the luminosity of various minerals under ultraviolet light.

Synset Examples

its luminosity is measured relative to that of our sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightness - the quality or state of being bright
  • Antonym: dimness - the quality of being only faintly luminous

Etymology

From Latin 'luminosus' (full of light), with suffix '-ity'.
