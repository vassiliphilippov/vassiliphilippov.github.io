light-year

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtˌjɪr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌjɪə / (BrE)
the distance that light travels in a vacuum in one year, approximately 5.88 trillion miles (9.46 trillion kilometers)
unit of astronomical distance

Examples

The nearest star system to Earth is over four light-years away.
Astronomers measure vast cosmic distances in light-years rather than miles or kilometers.
It takes about 100,000 light-years for light to travel across the Milky Way galaxy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsec - another astronomical unit of distance equal to about 3.26 light-years

Etymology

Formed from 'light' + 'year', first used in the 19th century as an astronomical unit of measurement.
