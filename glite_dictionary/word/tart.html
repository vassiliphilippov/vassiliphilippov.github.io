tart

1. NOUN

/ tɑrt / (AmE), / tɑːt / (BrE)
A small open pastry case filled with fruit or other sweet ingredients.
small open fruit pie

Examples

She baked a delicious apple tart for dessert last night.
The bakery window displayed several colorful fruit tarts.
I ordered a lemon tart with my afternoon tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pie - baked dish with a pastry crust and filling

Etymology

From Old French *tarte* (flat, open-topped pastry).

2. NOUN

/ tɑrt / (AmE), / tɑːt / (BrE)
A small pastry case filled with fruit, custard, or other sweet or savoury ingredients, typically without a top crust.
small open pastry with a filling

Examples

She ordered a raspberry tart for dessert after her meal.
The bakery displayed dozens of colorful fruit tarts in the window.
He baked a lemon tart with a buttery crust for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pie - dish of pastry with a sweet or savoury filling and a top crust

Etymology

From Old French *tarte*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Medieval Latin *tarta*.

3. ADJ

/ tɑrt / (AmE), / tɑːt / (BrE)
(of a remark or tone) marked by being sharp, biting, or harsh.
sharp or harsh (of speech)

Examples

Her tart reply made everyone fall silent.
He delivered a tart criticism of the proposed policy.
The debate became heated with increasingly tart exchanges.

Synset Examples

sharp criticism
a sharp-worded exchange
a tart remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp - (of remarks or tone) harsh or cutting
  • Synonym: biting - (of remarks) harshly critical or sarcastic
  • Antonym: gentle - (of remarks) mild or kind, the opposite of harsh

Etymology

From Old French 'tart' (sharp to the taste), extended figuratively to describe tone or speech.

5. ADJ

/ tɑrt / (AmE), / tɑːt / (BrE)
having a sharp or sour taste, similar to that of a lemon or unripe fruit
having a sharp, sour taste

Examples

The lemonade was too tart for my taste, so I added more sugar.
She prefers tart apples to sweet ones for baking pies.
The sauce had a pleasantly tart flavor that complemented the fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour - having an acidic taste like lemon or vinegar
  • Antonym: sweet - having the taste characteristic of sugar or honey

Etymology

From Old French 'tart' (sharp to the taste), of Germanic origin.
