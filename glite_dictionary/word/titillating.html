titillating

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
sexually exciting or arousing; stimulating sexual interest or excitement.
sexually arousing; exciting interest

Examples

The novel was full of titillating scenes that attracted adult readers.
His titillating comments made the audience blush and laugh at the same time.
The advertisement was considered too titillating for daytime television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulating - exciting interest or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting or exciting

Etymology

From Latin titillare (to tickle), via the late Middle English titillate (excite, tickle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
interesting or exciting in a way that is pleasant but often superficial, sometimes especially by being mildly sexual or provocative.
pleasantly exciting or stimulating

Examples

The magazine published a titillating story that drew a lot of attention.
She found the jokes more titillating than actually funny.
His presentation included some titillating details to keep the audience engaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin titillare (to tickle).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing laughter or twitching movements by lightly touching or stimulating; giving a tickling sensation.
causing laughter by light touch; tickling

Examples

The kitten found the feather so titillating that it could not stop squirming.
He laughed uncontrollably as she ran a titillating finger across his bare foot.
That titillating sensation on my arm made me shiver with delight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticklish - easily made to laugh or react by gentle touch; sensitive to tickling

Etymology

From Latin titillare (to tickle).
