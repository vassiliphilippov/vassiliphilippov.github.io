Crangon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræn.ɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈkræŋ.ɡɒn / (BrE)
a genus of small shrimps, including the common sand shrimp, that are found in coastal waters
genus of small coastal shrimps

Examples

The Crangon shrimp is commonly found along sandy shores in temperate regions.
Researchers identified several species within the Crangon genus during the coastal survey.
Fishermen often catch Crangon for use as bait in local waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crangonidae - the family of shrimps to which Crangon belongs

Etymology

From Greek *krangōn* (a kind of shellfish).
