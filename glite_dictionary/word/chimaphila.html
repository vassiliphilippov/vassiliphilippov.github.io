Chimaphila

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmæfɪlə / (AmE), / kɪˈmæfɪlə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen herbs with creeping rootstocks and glossy leaves, native to North America, Europe, and eastern Asia.
a genus of evergreen herbs

Examples

Chimaphila species are often found in shady woodlands and forest floors.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Chimaphila as part of their research on native plants.
The vibrant green leaves of Chimaphila remain through the winter months.

Etymology

From Greek *cheima* (winter) and *philos* (loving), referring to the plant's evergreen nature.

/ ˌkaɪməˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkɪməˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen herbs with long creeping rootstocks and shiny leaves, found in North America, Europe, and East Asia.
genus of evergreen herbs

Examples

The Chimaphila genus includes several low-growing, evergreen herbs often found in woodland habitats.
Botanists have identified new species within the Chimaphila genus in eastern Asia.
The shiny leaves of Chimaphila make it easy to spot in a forest understory.

Etymology

From Greek *cheimaphilos* meaning 'fond of winter', referring to its evergreen nature.
