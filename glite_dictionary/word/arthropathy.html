arthropathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrˈθrɑpəθi / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɒpəθi / (BrE)
a disease or abnormal condition affecting a joint
disease or abnormality of a joint
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with arthropathy after examining his swollen knee joint.
Certain infections can lead to arthropathy and lasting joint problems if not treated early.
People with diabetes are at increased risk of developing various forms of arthropathy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - painful inflammation and stiffness of the joints

Etymology

From Greek 'arthron' (joint) + 'pathos' (disease).
