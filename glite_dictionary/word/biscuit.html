biscuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪs.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪs.kɪt / (BrE)
A small, flat, sweet baked cake, typically crisp and eaten as a snack, especially in British English.
small, sweet, flat baked cake (British)
british

Examples

Could you please pass me a biscuit to have with my tea?
He offered his guests a plate of homemade biscuits.
The chocolate-covered biscuit was her favorite afternoon treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookie - a sweet baked food, usually flat and round (mainly American sense)

Etymology

From Old French *bescuit*, from Medieval Latin *biscoctus* ('twice-cooked'), referring to how the original biscuits were baked.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪs.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪs.kɪt / (BrE)
A small, round, soft bread, typically leavened with baking powder or baking soda rather than yeast.
small, soft leavened bread
american

Examples

I like to eat a warm biscuit with butter and honey for breakfast.
The restaurant serves their fried chicken with homemade biscuits on the side.
She baked a batch of biscuits for the holiday dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scone - A similar baked good, often sweeter and more common in British English

Etymology

From Old French *bescuit*, based on Latin *bis* (twice) + *coquere* (to cook); originally referred to twice-baked bread.
