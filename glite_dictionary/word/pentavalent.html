pentavalent

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛntəˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element or compound) having a valence of five; capable of combining with or replacing five atoms of hydrogen or other monovalent element.
having a valence of five (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Phosphorus is commonly found in both trivalent and pentavalent states.
The chemist synthesized a pentavalent compound during the experiment.
Arsenic exhibits a pentavalent form in some of its oxides.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trivalent - having a valence of three (chemistry)
  • Degree Higher: hexavalent - having a valence of six (chemistry)

Etymology

Formed from 'penta-' (Greek for 'five') and 'valent' (from Latin 'valentia' meaning 'strength, capacity').
