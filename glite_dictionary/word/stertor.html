stertor

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrtər / (AmE), / ˈstɜːtə / (BrE)
a harsh, snoring, or gasping sound made in breathing, especially during sleep or in certain medical conditions
heavy snoring or gasping sound in breathing
medical

Examples

The patient's loud stertor was audible down the hallway.
Doctors noted a persistent stertor in his breathing during the examination.
Her restless sleep was punctuated by bouts of heavy stertor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snoring - the act of making a snorting or grunting sound while sleeping

Etymology

From Latin 'stertor' (snoring), possibly imitative in origin.
