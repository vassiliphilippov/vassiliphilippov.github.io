Lilium martagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm mɑːrˈtɑːɡən / (AmE), / ˈlɪlɪəm mɑːˈtɑːɡən / (BrE)
A species of lily with small, dull purple flowers native to northwestern Europe and northwestern Asia.
a species of lily with purple flowers

Examples

The Lilium martagon blooms in summer, filling the woodland with subtle purple hues.
Gardeners value Lilium martagon for its elegant, downward-facing flowers and hardiness in temperate climates.
During our hike, we spotted several clusters of Lilium martagon growing along the forest edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant of the genus Lilium; large prominent flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Lilium' (lily) and presumed epithet 'martagon', possibly from Medieval Latin.
