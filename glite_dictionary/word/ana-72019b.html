ana

1. NOUN

/ ˈænə / (AmE), / ˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A collection of anecdotes or literary gossip relating to a particular person, place, or subject.
collection of anecdotes about a person or topic

Examples

The biography included a fascinating ana recounting witty remarks made by the author.
Literary scholars often consult the ana of famous writers for insights into their personal lives.
She compiled an ana of her travels, collecting amusing tales from each city she visited.

Etymology

From Latin -ana, a suffix meaning 'things about' or 'related to', used with proper names (as in Scaligerana, things about Scaliger).
