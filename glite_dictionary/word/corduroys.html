corduroys

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.dəˌrɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.djʊˌrɔɪz / (BrE)
Trousers made of corduroy fabric, typically characterized by a ridged, velvet-like texture.
trousers made of corduroy fabric

Examples

He wore a pair of brown corduroys to the party last night.
My grandfather always preferred corduroys over jeans for their comfort and warmth.
The store has a new collection of colorful corduroys for autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corduroy - a thick cotton fabric with parallel ridges

Etymology

From 'corduroy', referring to the fabric, itself from French 'corde du roi' (cord of the king), originally describing a type of cloth.
