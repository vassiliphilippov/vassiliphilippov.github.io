save

1. VERB

/ seɪv / (AmE), / seɪv / (BrE)
to prevent the need for spending money, effort, or time; to make something unnecessary
prevent need for expense or effort

Examples

Buying in bulk can save you a significant amount of money over time.
This shortcut will save you from having to repeat the entire process.
Arriving early can save you the trouble of waiting in a long line.

Synset Examples

This will save money
I'll save you the trouble
This will save you a lot of time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to spend - to pay out money or use effort or time

Etymology

From Old French 'sauver', from Latin 'salvare' (to make safe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00550632-v
Synset POS: v
