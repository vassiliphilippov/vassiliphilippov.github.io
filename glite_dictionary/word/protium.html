Protium

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiəm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical American trees with fragrant wood, producing gum elemi.
genus of tropical trees yielding gum elemi

Examples

The rainforest is rich in species from the Protium genus, valued for their aromatic gum.
Many indigenous communities harvest gum elemi from Protium trees for medicinal purposes.
Botanists recently classified a new species within the Protium genus found in South America.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek *prṓtios* (first), named as a genus in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiəm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiəm / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly tropical American trees known for their fragrant wood and production of gum elemi
genus of tropical trees yielding gum elemi

Examples

The genus Protium is widely distributed throughout Central and South America.
Several species in the Protium group are valued for their aromatic resin.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Protium genus in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek prōtos (first), used as a taxonomic name.
