bolete

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːt / (AmE), / bəˈliːt / (BrE)
a mushroom of the family Boletaceae, typically having a thick stem and sponge-like pores beneath the cap instead of gills
a type of mushroom in the Boletaceae family

Examples

The forest was full of boletes during the autumn mushroom season.
Careful identification is important before eating any wild bolete.
She photographed a rare bolete she found near the old oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - the spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus; commonly edible fungi

Etymology

From French 'bolet', from Latin 'boletus' (mushroom).
