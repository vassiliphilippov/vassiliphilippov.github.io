dyspepsia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpɛp.si.ə / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛp.si.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person experiences discomfort or pain in the upper abdomen, often with symptoms such as nausea, heartburn, or bloating.
discomfort or pain in digestion
medical

Examples

After eating a heavy meal, he often suffers from dyspepsia and discomfort.
Many people with dyspepsia experience a burning sensation in their chest after eating.
Some over-the-counter medicines can help relieve mild dyspepsia symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigestion - discomfort in the stomach caused by difficulty in digesting food

Etymology

From Greek dyspepsia, from dys- (bad) + pepsis (digestion).
