decedent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsiːdən̩t / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːdnt / (BrE)
a person who has died, especially as referred to in legal contexts
person who has died (legal term)
legal

Examples

The lawyer explained the terms of the decedent's will to the family.
Assets owned by the decedent will be distributed according to state law if there is no will.
The identity of the decedent was established through dental records.

Synset Examples

I wonder what the dead person would have done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceased - a person who has died
  • Synonym: testator - person who has made a will

Etymology

From Latin 'decedentem', present participle of 'decedere' (to die, to depart).
