wale

1. NOUN

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
A thick plank or band running lengthwise along the side of a wooden ship, serving as a structural reinforcement or protection.
plank or ridge on a wooden ship's side

Examples

The old sailing vessel still had its original wale intact along the starboard side.
Shipbuilders repaired the damaged wale to strengthen the hull before setting sail again.
A thick wale protected the ship from scrapes against the dock.

Etymology

From Middle English 'wale', from Old English 'walu' (ridge, streak, welt).

2. NOUN

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
a raised mark or ridge on the skin, especially one caused by a blow or allergic reaction
raised mark on the skin

Examples

The child's arm had a wale where the branch had struck him.
After the allergic reaction, large red wales appeared on her back.
The old photograph showed a wale left by the lash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weal - a raised red mark on the skin
  • Synonym: welt - a red, swollen mark on the skin

Etymology

From Old English 'walu' (ridge, mark of blows).
