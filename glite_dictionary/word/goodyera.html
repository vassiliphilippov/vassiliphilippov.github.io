Goodyera

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡuːdiˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˌɡuːdɪˈɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of small orchids found in the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by creeping rhizomes, stalked ovate leaves, and small flowers
a genus of small northern orchids

Examples

The rare Goodyera orchid can be found in shaded woodland areas.
Researchers surveyed a population of Goodyera to monitor its growth patterns.
Some species in the Goodyera genus are commonly called rattlesnake plantains.

Etymology

Named after John Goodyer (1592–1664), English botanist.
