callousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkæləsnəs / (BrE)
lack of feeling or empathy; insensitivity to the feelings of others or to suffering.
insensitivity; lack of empathy

Examples

His callousness toward the victim shocked everyone in the courtroom.
Many criticized the company's callousness in laying off workers without warning.
Her callousness made it difficult for her to form close relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitivity - lack of concern for others' feelings
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others' suffering

Etymology

From callous + -ness; 'callous' from Latin callosus (thick-skinned).
