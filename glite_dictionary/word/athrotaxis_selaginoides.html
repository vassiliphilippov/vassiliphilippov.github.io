Athrotaxis selaginoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθ.roʊˈtæksɪs ˌsɛl.ə.dʒɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌæθ.rəʊˈtæksɪs ˌsɛlə.dʒɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the mountains of Tasmania, with sharp-pointed, inward-curving leaves
a Tasmanian evergreen conifer

Examples

Athrotaxis selaginoides can be found in the cool, moist forests of Tasmania.
The unique leaves of Athrotaxis selaginoides help distinguish it from other conifers.
Conservation efforts have increased to protect the endangered Athrotaxis selaginoides from logging.

Etymology

From Greek 'athroos' (crowded) + 'taxis' (arrangement) and modern Latin 'selaginoides' (resembling Selaginella).
