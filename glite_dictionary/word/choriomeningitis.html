choriomeningitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔriˌoʊmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒriəʊˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A type of cerebral meningitis characterized by inflammation and cellular infiltration of the meninges.
cerebral meningitis with cellular infiltration
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with choriomeningitis after examining the cerebrospinal fluid.
Recent research has focused on the neurological complications of viral choriomeningitis.
Prompt treatment of choriomeningitis can significantly improve patient outcomes.

Etymology

From Greek 'chorion' (membrane) + 'meningitis' (inflammation of the meninges).
