serial

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
Occurring or arranged in a series or in regular succession without gaps.
in regular succession; arranged as a series

Examples

The orchestra held a serial concert series each month during the summer.
The painter produced a serial set of images documenting every phase of the project.
His serial meetings with the director ensured no details were overlooked.

Synset Examples

serial concerts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutive - following continuously; in unbroken sequence
  • Antonym: intermittent - occasional; not continuous or steady

Etymology

From Late Latin serialis, from Latin series (row, succession).

4. ADJ

/ ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
(of a process or operation) performed or occurring in a sequence, one after another, rather than simultaneously.
performed in sequence, one after another

Examples

The serial execution of tasks ensures that each one is completed before the next begins.
In computing, serial processing means instructions are handled one after the other.
Unlike parallel systems, serial approaches may be slower but are simpler to design.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parallel - (of computing) involving the simultaneous execution of operations

Etymology

From Latin 'seria' (series), via French 'sériel'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
a publication released in successive parts at regular intervals, such as a magazine or journal
publication issued in regular parts

Examples

The university library subscribes to many serials in print and online formats.
She catalogued each serial by its publication cycle and subject area.
Academic journals are a type of serial valued for their regular research updates.

Related Terms
