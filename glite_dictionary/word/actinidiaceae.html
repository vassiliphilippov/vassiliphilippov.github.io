Actinidiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ækˌtɪnɪdiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnɪdiːˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical trees, shrubs, or woody vines, including the kiwifruit, found mainly in Asia and the Pacific region
family of tropical trees, shrubs, or vines
medical

Examples

The Actinidiaceae family includes plants such as the kiwifruit and hardy kiwi.
Many members of the Actinidiaceae are native to East Asia and are valued for their edible fruit.
Botanists often study the Actinidiaceae to better understand tropical plant evolution.

Etymology

From genus name Actinidia, from Greek 'aktis, aktinos' (ray, beam) + New Latin family suffix '-aceae'.
