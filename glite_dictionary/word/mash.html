mash

1. NOUN

/ mæʃ / (AmE), / mæʃ / (BrE)
A mixture of crushed malt grains and hot water, used in brewing to extract sugars.
mixture of crushed malt and water in brewing

Examples

The brewer combined barley and hot water to make the mash for fermentation.
Stirring the mash ensures even extraction of sugars from the grains.
The temperature of the mash must be carefully controlled during brewing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wort - the liquid extracted from mash during brewing, containing sugars for fermentation
  • Degree Lower: grist - ground malt or grain used in brewing before mixing with water

Etymology

From Middle English 'mashe', from Old English 'mæsc' (soft mixture, mash in brewing), related to 'mæscan' (to mash).
