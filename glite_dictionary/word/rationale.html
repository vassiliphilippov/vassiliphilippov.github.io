rationale

1. NOUN

/ ˌræʃəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌræʃəˈnɑːl / (BrE)
a set of reasons or a logical basis given to explain a particular course of action or belief
reasoned explanation or justification

Examples

She carefully explained the rationale behind her decision to resign.
The scientists outlined the rationale for using this particular method in their study.
Understanding the rationale behind the new policy helped employees accept the changes.

Synset Examples

the rationale for capital punishment
the principles of internal-combustion engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justification - the action of showing something to be right or reasonable
  • Antonym: pretext - a reason given in justification that is not the real reason

Etymology

From Latin 'rationalis' (rational), from 'ratio' (reason, calculation).
