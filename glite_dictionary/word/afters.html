afters

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚz / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.təz / (BrE)
A sweet dish served as the last course of a meal; dessert.
sweet dish or dessert served last
british

Examples

For afters, we had a delicious chocolate cake.
Would you like some fruit for afters or perhaps some ice cream?
I'm too full for afters, but the pudding looks tempting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dessert - sweet course eaten at the end of a meal

Etymology

Chiefly British English; from 'after' (later in time), referring to the course after the main meal.
