Cocopa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊˌpɑː / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəˌpɑː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people living near the mouth of the Colorado River in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico
member of a Native American people

Examples

A group of Cocopa gathered for the annual Yuman language festival to share their traditions.
The Cocopa have maintained their cultural identity through storytelling and ceremonies.
Several Cocopa families live on reservations near the lower Colorado River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cocopah - variant spelling of 'Cocopa', referring to the same people

Etymology

From Cocopa self-designation, possibly meaning 'river people' in their own language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.kəˌpɑ / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.əˌpɑː / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Cocopa people, indigenous to regions in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico
a Yuman language of the Cocopa people

Examples

The Cocopa language is currently spoken by only a small number of elders in the community.
Linguists have been working to document Cocopa to help preserve it for future generations.
Classes in Cocopa are being offered to children interested in learning their ancestral language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of indigenous languages of the southwestern US and northwestern Mexico
