razorback

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzərˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈreɪzəbæk / (BrE)
A baleen whale of the family Balaenopteridae, characterized by longitudinal throat grooves and a small, pointed dorsal fin.
a type of baleen whale

Examples

The razorback is easily distinguished by the ridges along its throat.
We spotted a razorback surfacing near the boat during our whale-watching tour.
Unlike other whales, the razorback has a distinctive dorsal fin set far back on its body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin whale - a large, slender baleen whale of the family Balaenopteridae

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪzərˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈreɪzəbæk / (BrE)
Having a sharp, narrow, or ridged back, like the edge of a razor.
having a sharp, narrow back

Examples

The wild boar's razorback shape made it easy to identify from a distance.
Farmers reported that razorback hogs can be aggressive and hard to contain.
The old horse, thin and razorback, grazed quietly in the field.

Synset Examples

a razor-backed horse
razorback hogs

Etymology

From 'razor' + 'back', referring to the resemblance to a razor's edge.

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzərˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈreɪzəbæk / (BrE)
a wild or semi-wild hog with a thin body, long legs, and a ridged back, found chiefly in the southeastern United States
wild hog with a ridged back, southeastern U.S.
american

Examples

The old farmer spotted a razorback rooting near the edge of his property.
Razorbacks are known for their adaptability and tough survival skills in the wild.
Hunters in the Southeast often come across razorbacks while exploring the dense forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feral hog - a wild hog descended from domesticated pigs
  • Synonym: boar - a wild pig or hog

Etymology

From the combination of 'razor' and 'back', referring to the animal's sharply ridged spine.
