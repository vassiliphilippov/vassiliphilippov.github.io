Paul-Henri Thiry

1. PROPN

/ ˌpoʊl ɑnˈri ˈti ri / (AmE), / ˌpɔːl ɒ̃ˈʁi ˈtiː ri / (BrE)
(1723–1789) French philosopher known for his pioneering atheism and influential works on materialism and skepticism.
French philosopher, early atheist

Examples

Paul-Henri Thiry was one of the first European philosophers to openly declare his atheism.
The writings of Paul-Henri Thiry had a significant influence on Enlightenment thought.
Many modern scholars cite Paul-Henri Thiry when discussing the origins of secular philosophy.
