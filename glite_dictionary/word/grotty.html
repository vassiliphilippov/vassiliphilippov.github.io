grotty

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɑti / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒti / (BrE)
Unpleasant, dirty, or of poor quality; shabby or squalid, especially in an informal or British context.
dirty or unpleasant (informal)
informal british

Examples

The restaurant looked rather grotty, so we decided to eat elsewhere.
She rented a grotty flat above the shop, but at least it was cheap.
After the festival, the park was left in a grotty state.

Synset Examples

a grotty little play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition because of long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: dirty - not clean
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

Probably a blend of 'grotesque' and 'grotty', or from British slang 'grotty', recorded from the 1960s.
