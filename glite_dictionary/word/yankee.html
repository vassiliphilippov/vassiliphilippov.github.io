Yankee

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋki / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki / (BrE)
(in the southern United States) A person from a northern state of the US, especially a Union soldier during the Civil War.
Northerner, especially Union soldier (US South, historical)
american

Examples

During the Civil War, many in the South viewed every Yankee as an enemy.
My grandfather used to tell stories about fighting against the Yankees.
Some locals still use the term Yankee to refer to people from the North.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southerner - A person from the southern United States

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the Dutch name Janke (diminutive of Jan).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋki / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki / (BrE)
A person from the United States, especially as referred to by people from outside the country.
an American, especially to non-Americans

Examples

Many Europeans are curious about what life is like for a Yankee in New York City.
The tourist laughed when the local called him a Yankee.
To outsiders, every Yankee seems obsessed with baseball and fast food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American - a native or citizen of the United States

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'Janke', a diminutive of 'Jan' (John), later used by the British as a term for Americans.

3. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋki / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki / (BrE)
An American who lives in the northern United States, especially a Union supporter during the American Civil War.
Northerner in the U.S., especially Civil War era

Examples

During the Civil War, the term Yankee was often used to describe soldiers from the North.
As a proud Yankee, he felt strongly about preserving the Union.
The Yankees faced many challenges as they fought against the Confederate forces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southerner - A person from the southern United States, especially during the Civil War era

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Dutch 'Janke' (diminutive of Jan); used as a nickname for Americans.

4. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋki / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki / (BrE)
A person from New England in the United States, especially regarded as a typical Northerner.
an American from New England

Examples

Many Yankees value their New England heritage and traditions.
During the Civil War, Southerners often referred to Union soldiers as Yankees.
As a proud Yankee, she enjoys autumns in Vermont and clam chowder by the sea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southerner - a person from the southern United States

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'Janke', diminutive of the given name Jan (John).
