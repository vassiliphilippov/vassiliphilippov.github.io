to fantasize

1. VERB

/ ˈfæn.təˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.saɪz / (BrE)
to imagine or think about something that is very unlikely or not real, often as a form of wishful thinking
imagine unreal or unlikely things

Examples

As a teenager, she would often fantasize about traveling the world.
It's normal to fantasize about success when starting a new job.
He fantasizes that one day he will win the lottery.

Synset Examples

he is fantasizing when he says he plans to start his own company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dream - to form ideas or images in the mind that are not present or real

Etymology

From fantasy + -ize, first attested in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈfæntəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæntəsaɪz / (BrE)
to imagine or form mental images of things that are not present or real, often as a form of wishful thinking
to imagine things not real or present

Examples

She would often fantasize about traveling the world during long meetings.
As a child, he used to fantasize entire adventures before falling asleep.
Many people fantasize about winning the lottery, even if they know it's unlikely.

Synset Examples

he is fantasizing the ideal wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something
  • Synonym: daydream - to indulge in a series of pleasant thoughts not connected with the present moment
  • Antonym: to realize - to become aware of or bring into reality

Etymology

From fantasy + -ize; influenced by French 'fantasiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01640401-v
Synset POS: v
