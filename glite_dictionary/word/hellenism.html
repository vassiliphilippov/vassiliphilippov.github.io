Hellenism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛləˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhɛlɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
the principles, ideals, and culture associated with classical Greek civilization, especially as spread or adopted in other regions
Greek principles and ideals; culture of ancient Greece

Examples

Many Renaissance artists were inspired by Hellenism in their work.
The spread of Hellenism influenced art, philosophy, and politics across the ancient world.
Scholars study Hellenism to understand its impact on Western civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hellenistic - relating to Greek history, culture, or art after Alexander the Great

Etymology

From Greek Hellēnismos (imitation of the Greeks), from Hellēn (a Greek).
