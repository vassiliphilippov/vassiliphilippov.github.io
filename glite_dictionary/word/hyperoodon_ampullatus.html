Hyperoodon ampullatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈroʊədɑn ˌæmpjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpərəʊˈɒdɒn ˌæmpʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
a large beaked whale of the northern Atlantic Ocean with a prominent bulbous forehead, commonly called the northern bottlenose whale
northern bottlenose whale species

Examples

The Hyperoodon ampullatus is renowned for its intelligence and deep-diving ability.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the Hyperoodon ampullatus in the North Atlantic.
The Hyperoodon ampullatus can be identified by its distinct bulbous forehead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern bottlenose whale - common name for Hyperoodon ampullatus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Hyperoodon' and species name 'ampullatus', from Latin 'ampulla' (flask), referring to the whale's bulbous head shape.
