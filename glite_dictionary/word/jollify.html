to jollify

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑːlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒlɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to celebrate in a lively and noisy way, often with drinking and merrymaking
celebrate noisily; make merry

Examples

The townspeople gathered in the square to jollify after their team's big win.
They jollified deep into the night, singing and dancing together.
Every year, friends come together to jollify during the summer festival.

Synset Examples

The members of the wedding party made merry all night
Let's whoop it up — the boss is gone!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to observe or honor with festivities
  • Synonym: carouse - to drink and celebrate noisily

Etymology

From 'jolly' + '-ify', patterned after similar English verb formations.
