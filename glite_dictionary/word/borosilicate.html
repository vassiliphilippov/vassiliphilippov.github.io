borosilicate

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɔːroʊˈsɪlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌbɒrəʊˈsɪlɪkeɪt / (BrE)
a salt formed from boric acid and silicic acid, especially used to refer to compounds in borosilicate glass
salt of boric and silicic acids

Examples

Laboratory glassware is often made from borosilicate to resist thermal shock.
The mineral contains traces of borosilicate in its crystalline structure.
Engineers selected borosilicate for its durability and chemical resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicate - a salt derived from silicic acid

Etymology

From boron and silicate, reflecting its components boric acid and silicic acid.
