issuer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪʃuər / (AmE), / ˈɪsjʊə / (BrE)
an organization or institution that formally produces and publishes something, such as securities, currency, or official documents
organization that issues securities, currency, etc.

Examples

The government is the sole issuer of legal currency.
Each bond issuer must provide a prospectus to investors.
A newspaper issuer is responsible for editorial standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publisher - an organization or person that prepares and issues books, journals, or music

Etymology

From 'issue' (to put forth), ultimately from Latin 'exire' (to go out).
