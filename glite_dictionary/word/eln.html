ELN

1. NOUN

/ i ɛl ɛn / (AmE), / iː ɛl ɛn / (BrE)
a Marxist guerrilla group founded in 1963 in Colombia, known for its armed conflict with the government and involvement in kidnappings and other criminal activities
Colombian Marxist guerrilla group

Examples

The ELN claimed responsibility for the attack on the oil pipeline.
Peace talks between the government and the ELN have frequently stalled.
The ELN is considered a terrorist organization by several countries.

Synset Examples

ELN kidnappers target foreign employees of large corporations

Etymology

Acronym of Spanish 'Ejército de Liberación Nacional' (National Liberation Army).

WordNet ID: oewn-08052354-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ i ɛl ɛn / (AmE), / iː ɛl ɛn / (BrE)
a Bolivian terrorist organization operating as an umbrella for various indigenous subversive groups, originally established by Che Guevara in the 1960s with Marxist-Leninist ideologies
Bolivian Marxist-Leninist terrorist organization

Examples

The Bolivian government launched operations to dismantle the ELN and its affiliated groups.
Many indigenous activists were allegedly recruited by the ELN during the 1970s.
The ELN traced its origins back to Che Guevara's revolutionary efforts in Bolivia.

Etymology

Acronym for Spanish Ejército de Liberación Nacional (National Liberation Army).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08051986-n
Synset POS: n
