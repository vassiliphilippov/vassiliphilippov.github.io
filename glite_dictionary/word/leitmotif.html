leitmotif

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtˌmoʊˌtif / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt.məʊˌtiːf / (BrE)
a recurrent theme or melodic phrase in a musical composition, especially one that accompanies the reappearance of a person, idea, or situation, prominently used in opera
recurring theme in music or art

Examples

The composer assigned a distinct leitmotif to each character in the opera.
The film's main leitmotif recurs whenever the protagonist faces a moral dilemma.
In Wagner's works, the leitmotif subtly signals the presence of a particular emotion or character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motif - a recurring musical or thematic element

Etymology

From German Leitmotiv (guiding motif): leiten (to lead) + Motiv (motive).
