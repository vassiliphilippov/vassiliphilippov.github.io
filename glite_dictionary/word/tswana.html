Tswana

1. NOUN

/ ˈtswɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈtswɑːnə / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu ethnic group living mainly in Botswana and western South Africa
a member of a southern African ethnic group

Examples

The Tswana are known for their rich traditions and cultural heritage.
She met a Tswana man while studying in Botswana.
Many Tswana live both in Botswana and nearby areas of South Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu - a group of indigenous African peoples sharing related languages

Etymology

From the Tswana word 'Batswana' (the Tswana people, plural of Motswana).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈtwɑːnə / (BrE)
A Bantu language spoken chiefly by the Tswana people in Botswana and South Africa.
language of the Tswana people

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Tswana.
The Tswana language is taught in many schools in Botswana.
He used a traditional greeting in Tswana during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sotho - Bantu language closely related to Tswana

Etymology

From the name of the Tswana ethnic group in southern Africa.
