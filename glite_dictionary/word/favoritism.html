favoritism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvərɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪtɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The practice of giving unfair preferential treatment to one person or group at the expense of others.
unfair preferential treatment

Examples

The manager was accused of favoritism when he promoted his friend.
Parents should try to avoid showing favoritism among their children.
The company has a strict policy against favoritism in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - A tendency to favor one group, person, or thing over another, often unfairly
  • Antonym: impartiality - The quality of treating all people and groups equally, without bias

Etymology

From 'favorite' + '-ism', patterned after French 'favoritisme'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪ.vəˌrɪ.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.vər.ɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
unfair support shown to one person or group, often based on personal feelings or prejudice, rather than merit
unfair preference for one person or group

Examples

Accusations of favoritism damaged the manager’s reputation among employees.
The teacher was careful to avoid any hint of favoritism in grading.
Many people believe that favoritism in politics leads to corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against something
  • Synonym: nepotism - favoritism shown to relatives, especially in professional contexts
  • Antonym: impartiality - fairness; equal treatment of all rivals or disputants

Etymology

Formed from 'favorite' + '-ism', first attested in early 18th century.
