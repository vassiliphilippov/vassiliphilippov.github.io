Gerald R. Ford

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛrəld ɑːr fɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrəld ɑː fɔːd / (BrE)
the 38th President of the United States, who served from 1974 to 1977 after being appointed vice president and succeeding Richard Nixon upon his resignation
38th U.S. President (1974–77)

Examples

Gerald R. Ford took office after the resignation of President Nixon in 1974.
The library honoring Gerald R. Ford is located in Grand Rapids, Michigan.
Historians often debate the political legacy of Gerald R. Ford.
