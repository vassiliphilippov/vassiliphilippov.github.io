uncritical

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
not tending to judge or evaluate carefully; lacking critical or analytical thinking.
lacking critical judgment

Examples

The manager gave an uncritical acceptance of the proposal, ignoring potential risks.
She tends to be uncritical when reviewing her friends' ideas.
His uncritical reading of the report led to several mistakes.

Synset Examples

news sources reflected uncritical estimates of the number of juvenile addicts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: critical - judging with careful analysis
  • Antonym: discerning - able to judge well

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + critical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00653373-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
not expressing or involving any criticism or analytical judgment; accepting without questioning or evaluating
not critical or analytical

Examples

He accepted the report in an uncritical manner, without asking any questions.
Many readers gave the book their uncritical praise, overlooking its flaws.
She was an uncritical supporter of the policy, never challenging its assumptions.

Synset Examples

a devoted and almost uncritical admirer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: critical - tending to find fault or judge analytically

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + critical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00651838-a
Synset POS: a
