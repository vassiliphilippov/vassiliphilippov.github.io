biting

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of wind or cold) causing a sharply painful or stinging sensation; intensely cold or penetrating.
intensely cold or stinging (of wind or cold)

Examples

A biting wind swept across the deserted field, making everyone shiver.
The biting cold made it difficult to stay outside for more than a few minutes.
She pulled her coat tighter to shield herself from the biting air.

Synset Examples

bitter cold
a biting wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piercing - (of wind or cold) extremely cold or penetrating
  • Antonym: mild - (of climate or sensation) moderate, not severe or sharp

2. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of words, remarks, etc.) harsh, sharply critical, or capable of causing hurt or offense; caustic.
sharply critical or caustic (of words, remarks)

Examples

The author's biting criticism of the policy left many officials stunned.
She delivered a biting remark that silenced everyone in the room.
His biting wit made him both feared and respected among his peers.

Synset Examples

a barbed compliment
a biting aphorism
pungent satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - sarcastic in a scathing and bitter way
  • Synonym: scathing - severely critical; scornful
  • Antonym: mild - gentle or moderate, not harsh or sharp

Etymology

Present participle of 'bite', from Old English 'bītan'.
