Populus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.pjʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.pjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees of the willow family, including poplars and aspens, native to the Northern Hemisphere
genus of trees including poplars and aspens

Examples

Many forests in Europe are dominated by Populus species, particularly along riverbanks.
The botanist identified the tree as belonging to the Populus genus due to its distinctive leaves.
Several varieties in the Populus group are valued for their fast growth and timber quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a deciduous tree of the genus Populus
  • Synonym: aspen - a type of tree in the genus Populus, with trembling leaves

Etymology

From Latin *populus* (poplar tree), used as the genus name in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələs / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees commonly known as poplars, native to the Northern Hemisphere and belonging to the family Salicaceae
genus of poplar trees

Examples

The Populus genus includes cottonwoods, aspens, and true poplars.
Many species in the Populus group are valued for their rapid growth and use in timber production.
A botanist can easily identify a Populus tree by its distinctive leaves and catkins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a tree of the genus Populus, with slender branches and catkins

Etymology

From Latin *populus* (poplar tree).
