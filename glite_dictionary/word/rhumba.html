rhumba

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmbə / (AmE), / ˈrʌmbə / (BrE)
A type of ballroom dance of Cuban origin, characterized by a slow rhythm and sensuous movements.
a ballroom dance of Cuban origin

Examples

They performed a passionate rhumba at the dance competition.
The rhumba is known for its slow, romantic movements.
She learned how to dance the rhumba while on vacation in Havana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a ballroom dance of Cuban origin, also spelled this way

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish 'rumba', meaning 'party' or 'spree'. Spelling 'rhumba' adopted in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmbə / (AmE), / ˈrʌmbə / (BrE)
A lively dance of Cuban origin, characterized by complex footwork and strong, rhythmic movements, usually performed in duple time.
Cuban dance with complex footwork

Examples

The couple performed a passionate rhumba during the competition.
She learned the basic steps of the rhumba in her dance class.
His swift footwork made his rhumba the highlight of the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - A similar Cuban dance and music genre; often used interchangeably with 'rhumba'.

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish 'rumba' (party, dance); influenced by Spanish and African traditions.
