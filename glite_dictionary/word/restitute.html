to restitute

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛstəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪˌtjuːt / (BrE)
to restore something to its original or a better condition
restore to original condition

Examples

After the fire, the museum worked tirelessly to restitute the damaged paintings.
The court ordered the company to restitute the land to its previous owners.
Efforts are being made to restitute the historic building to its former glory.

Synset Examples

They renovated the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to return something to a former condition or position
  • Synonym: repair - to fix something that is broken or damaged

Etymology

From Latin restituere (to restore, give back).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛstɪˌtut / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪˌtjuːt / (BrE)
to restore something lost or stolen to its proper owner; to return something to its original state or position
return or restore to original state or owner

Examples

The court ordered the company to restitute the funds to the victims.
After the investigation, the museum agreed to restitute the ancient artifact to its country of origin.
He promised to restitute the borrowed books as soon as possible.

Synset Examples

Restore the stolen painting to its rightful owner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to return something to a former place or condition
  • Antonym: forfeit - to lose or give up something as a penalty

Etymology

From Latin restitut-, past participle of restituere (to restore).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02315462-v
Synset POS: v
