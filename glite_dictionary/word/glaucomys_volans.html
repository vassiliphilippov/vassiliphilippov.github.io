Glaucomys volans

1. NOUN

/ ɡlawˈkoʊmɪs voʊˈlænz / (AmE), / ɡlaʊˈkəʊmɪs vəʊˈlænz / (BrE)
A small, large-eyed nocturnal flying squirrel native to the eastern United States, known for its ability to glide between trees.
southern flying squirrel species

Examples

The Glaucomys volans can glide across distances of more than 20 meters between trees.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Glaucomys volans in the Appalachian forests.
Unlike other squirrels, Glaucomys volans is nocturnal and rarely seen during the daytime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southern flying squirrel - common name for Glaucomys volans, a North American flying squirrel

Etymology

From Latin: Glaucomys (genus) and volans (flying).
