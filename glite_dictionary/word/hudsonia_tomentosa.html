Hudsonia tomentosa

1. NOUN

/ hʌdˈsoʊniə toʊˈmɛn.toʊ.sə / (AmE), / hʌdˈsəʊniə təʊˈmɛn.tə.sə / (BrE)
a small heath-like plant covered with white down, found on beaches in northeastern North America
a small downy plant native to northeastern North America beaches

Examples

The endangered Hudsonia tomentosa thrives in sandy coastal habitats with little disturbance.
We found several patches of Hudsonia tomentosa while hiking along the dunes.
Botanists are researching the adaptability of Hudsonia tomentosa to climate change.

Etymology

Hudsonia: named after William Hudson, English botanist (1730–1793); tomentosa: from Latin 'tomentosus', meaning 'covered with matted hairs'.
