equerry

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɛri / (BrE)
an officer of the British royal household who assists members of the royal family, often acting as a personal attendant
royal household attendant, British context
british

Examples

After his years of military service, he was appointed as an equerry to the queen.
The equerry organized the royal schedule and accompanied the prince to official events.
During the ceremony, the equerry stood nearby, ready to assist any member of the royal family.

Etymology

From French 'écurie' (stable), via Old French, related to 'écuyer' (squire, groom).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɛri / (BrE)
an official in a royal or noble household responsible for the care of horses, or, in modern usage, a personal attendant to a member of the royal family
royal official in charge of horses or as an attendant

Examples

As an equerry, he oversaw the care and training of the royal horses.
The equerry accompanied the prince to all official functions.
Historically, an equerry played an important role in the daily affairs of the palace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groom - a person responsible for the care of horses

Etymology

From French 'écurie' (stable), from Old French 'escuerie', related to 'écuyer' (squire, horseman).
