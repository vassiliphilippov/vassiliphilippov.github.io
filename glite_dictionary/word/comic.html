comic

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of comedy; intended to make people laugh.
relating to comedy; humorous

Examples

The movie features several comic scenes that had the audience laughing out loud.
He plays the comic hero who always finds himself in absurd situations.
Her comic timing is impeccable, making even simple jokes hilarious.

Synset Examples

comic hero

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humorous - funny or making you laugh
  • Antonym: tragic - relating to tragedy, causing sadness or distress

Etymology

From Latin 'comicus', from Greek 'kōmikos' (pertaining to comedy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02984937-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
a professional performer whose job is to tell jokes and entertain people, often by performing comical acts
stand-up comedian or performer of jokes

Examples

The comic entertained the audience with a hilarious routine about daily life.
Last night, a famous comic performed at the local comedy club.
Many comics rely on personal experiences as material for their acts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comedian - a person who entertains an audience with jokes, humorous stories, or comic acts

Etymology

From Latin 'comicus', from Greek 'kōmikos' (of or belonging to comedy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09959604-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
intended to make people laugh; causing amusement or laughter.
intended to provoke laughter

Examples

His comic timing kept the entire audience in stitches throughout the show.
The movie had several comic moments that lightened the otherwise serious plot.
She wore a comic hat to the party that made everyone smile.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funny - causing laughter or amusement
  • Antonym: serious - not intended to amuse; solemn
  • Synonym: amusing - entertaining and likely to make people laugh

Etymology

From Greek kōmikos (of or relating to comedy), from kōmos (revel, merrymaking).
