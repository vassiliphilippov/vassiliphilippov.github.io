draftswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræftsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːftsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female artist or technician skilled at drawing or preparing technical plans or designs
female technical artist or drafter

Examples

As a draftswoman, she contributed detailed architectural plans to the building project.
The engineering firm hired a talented draftswoman to create blueprints for the new bridge.
Her portfolio demonstrated her expertise as a draftswoman in both artistic and technical drawing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draftsman - an artist or technician skilled at drawing or technical plans (male or gender-neutral)

Etymology

Formed from 'draftsman' + 'woman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdræftsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːftsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to make detailed technical drawings of buildings, machinery, or other structures
female technical drawing specialist

Examples

The architectural firm hired a highly experienced draftswoman to prepare the blueprints for their new project.
As a skilled draftswoman, she was known for the precision of her engineering diagrams.
The company encouraged her to develop her talents as a draftswoman in a traditionally male-dominated field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draftsman - a person whose job is making detailed technical drawings
  • Synonym: drafter - someone who prepares technical drawings

Etymology

Formed from 'draftsman' (a person who makes technical drawings) with 'woman' replacing 'man'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-83111438-n
Synset POS: n
