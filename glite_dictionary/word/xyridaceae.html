Xyridaceae

1. NOUN

/ zaɪrɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / zaɪrɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants found mainly in tropical to temperate regions, typically growing in wet places.
family of wetland flowering plants

Examples

Members of the Xyridaceae are often found in marshes and boggy areas.
Botanists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Xyridaceae family.
The distinctive yellow flowers of Xyridaceae make them easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From the genus Xyris + Latin -aceae (suffix denoting plant family).

2. NOUN

/ zaɪˌrɪdəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / zaɪˌrɪdəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants typically found in wet habitats in tropical to temperate regions, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass family
family of wetland flowering plants

Examples

The wetlands in Brazil are home to several species in the Xyridaceae family.
Botanists conducted research on the unique flower structures within Xyridaceae.
Members of the Xyridaceae are often known as yellow-eyed grasses.

Etymology

From genus name Xyris + Latin -aceae, denoting botanical family.
