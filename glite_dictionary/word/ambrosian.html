Ambrosian

1. ADJ

/ æmˈbroʊʒiən / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziən / (BrE)
of or relating to Saint Ambrose or his works, particularly the style of liturgy and chant associated with him
relating to Saint Ambrose or his traditions

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful Ambrosian chant during the service.
Researchers studied the influence of Ambrosian traditions on early Christian music.
The cathedral is known for its unique Ambrosian liturgy.

Synset Examples

Ambrosian chants

Etymology

From Latin 'Ambrosius' (Ambrose), the name of the 4th-century bishop of Milan.
