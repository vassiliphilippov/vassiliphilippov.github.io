ambrosian

1. ADJ

/ æmˈbroʊʒən / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziən / (BrE)
Extremely pleasing to the taste or smell; sweet and fragrant, like ambrosia.
extremely pleasing; sweet and fragrant

Examples

The chef prepared an ambrosian dessert that delighted everyone's senses.
As soon as I entered the bakery, the ambrosian scent of fresh pastries filled the air.
Her grandmother's secret recipe produced an ambrosian stew everyone raved about.

Synset Examples

a nectarous drink
ambrosial food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nectarous - pleasantly sweet or fragrant, especially relating to taste or smell
  • Synonym: ambrosial - extremely pleasing to the taste or smell; divine

Etymology

From Latin 'ambrosius', from Greek 'ambrosios' (immortal), used in reference to the food of the gods in Greek mythology.

2. ADJ

/ æmˈbroʊziən / (AmE), / amˈbrəʊziən / (BrE)
Exceptionally pleasing; worthy of the gods, especially in taste or smell.
exceptionally pleasing (as if for the gods)

Examples

The chef prepared an ambrosian feast that delighted every guest.
The scent of the blooming flowers was truly ambrosian on that spring morning.
She described the chocolate cake as an ambrosian dessert worthy of royalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - of, from, or like God or a god; heavenly
  • Synonym: delicious - highly pleasing to the taste

Etymology

From Latin ambrosius, from Greek ambrosios (immortal, divine).
