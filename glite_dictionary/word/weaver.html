weaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːvər / (AmE), / ˈwiːvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make fabric by weaving threads together
person who weaves fabric

Examples

The weaver skillfully created beautiful patterns using colorful threads.
My grandmother was a talented weaver who sold her fabrics at the market.
Traditional villages often have a weaver who supplies cloth for the whole community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loom operator - a person who operates a loom to weave fabric
  • Antonym: tailor - a person who makes or repairs clothes, especially suits and jackets

Etymology

From Old English wefan (to weave).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːvər / (AmE), / ˈwiːvə / (BrE)
a type of African or Asian bird known for building elaborate woven nests, belonging to the family Ploceidae
African or Asian bird that weaves nests

Examples

The colony of weavers worked together to build dozens of nests in the tall acacia tree.
Many weaver species create intricate hanging nests woven from grasses and twigs.
Birdwatchers travel to see the brightly colored weaver during its nesting season.
