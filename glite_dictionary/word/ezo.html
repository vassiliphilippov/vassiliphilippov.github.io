Ezo

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːzəʊ / (BrE)
the former name for Hokkaido, the northernmost of Japan's four main islands
historical name for Hokkaido, Japan

Examples

The island of Ezo played a significant role in Japan's northern expansion.
During the Edo period, explorers traveled to Ezo to map its territory.
Ainu culture has deep roots in the region once called Ezo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hokkaido - the second largest and northernmost main island of Japan

Etymology

From Japanese 蝦夷 (Ezo), historically used to refer to Hokkaido and surrounding regions.
