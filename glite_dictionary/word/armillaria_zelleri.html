Armillaria zelleri

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.mɪˈlɛr.i zəˈlɛr.aɪ / (AmE), / ɑː.mɪˈlɛə.ri zɛˈlɛə.raɪ / (BrE)
a large species of fungus with a sticky cap that dries and turns brown, and off-white gills
species of mushroom with sticky brown cap

Examples

The distinctive cap of Armillaria zelleri is often sticky when young and turns brown as it ages.
Mycologists identified the sample as Armillaria zelleri based on its off-white gills.
Photographers are drawn to Armillaria zelleri because of its large, striking appearance in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey fungus - common name for fungus species in the Armillaria genus

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Armillaria and commemorative specific epithet ‘zelleri’, honoring mycologist Sanford Myron Zeller.
