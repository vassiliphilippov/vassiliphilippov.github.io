cashbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʃˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈkæʃˌbɒks / (BrE)
a strong container or box used to store cash, especially in businesses or at events
strong container for holding cash

Examples

The cashier kept the day’s earnings in the cashbox under the counter.
During the school event, all ticket sales were stored in a locked cashbox.
After counting the money, she carefully placed it in the cashbox for safekeeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safe - a strong secure box used for storing valuables

Etymology

From 'cash' (money) + 'box'.
