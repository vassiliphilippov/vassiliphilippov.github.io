introductoriness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈdʌktəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdʌktəri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being introductory or serving as an introduction
quality of being introductory

Examples

The introductoriness of the first chapter sets the tone for the entire book.
Critics noted the introductoriness of the presentation, wishing for more in-depth analysis.
She found the essay's introductoriness helpful for newcomers to the field.

Synset Examples

Instead this sequel redoubled the already marked introductoriness of its predecessor, introducing it in turn by subjecting it to preliminary interrogation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introductory - serving as an introduction; preliminary

Etymology

Formed from introductory + -ness (noun suffix indicating state or quality).
