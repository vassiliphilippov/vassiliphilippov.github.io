Serpasil

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɜːrpəˌsɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.pə.sɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked name for an antihypertensive drug containing reserpine, extracted from the plant Rauwolfia serpentina, formerly used to treat high blood pressure.
antihypertensive drug (reserpine/brand name)
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed Serpasil to help control her blood pressure.
Doctors rarely use Serpasil today due to its potential side effects.
After reading about Serpasil, he became interested in the history of antihypertensive drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserpine - alkaloid used as an antihypertensive and antipsychotic drug

Etymology

Trademark; from Latin 'serpens' (snake), due to the plant's name Rauwolfia serpentina.
