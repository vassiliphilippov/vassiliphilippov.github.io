Poecilogale

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊsɪloʊˈɡeɪli / (AmE), / ˌpəʊsɪlɒˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the weasel family, comprising the striped polecat of Africa.
genus of African striped polecat

Examples

The genus Poecilogale contains just one species, commonly known as the striped polecat.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Poecilogale in southern Africa.
Unlike other mustelids, Poecilogale has a distinctive black-and-white pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped polecat - African mustelid mammal, member of genus Poecilogale
  • Degree Lower: Ictonyx - genus of zorillas, related to Poecilogale

Etymology

From Greek 'poikilos' (varied) + 'galē' (weasel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpiːsɪˈlɒɡəli / (AmE), / ˌpiːsɪˈlɒɡəli / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the family Mustelidae, commonly known as muishonds, native to Africa.
genus of small African carnivorous mammals

Examples

The Poecilogale genus includes species commonly known as African striped weasels.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Poecilogale in southern Africa.
Unlike many other mustelids, members of Poecilogale are mostly nocturnal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muishond - small African carnivorous mammal in the weasel family

Etymology

From Greek *poikilos* (varied) and *gale* (weasel).
