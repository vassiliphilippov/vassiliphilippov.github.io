varsity

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑrsəti / (AmE), / ˈvɑːsəti / (BrE)
(British) An informal abbreviation of 'university', especially used to refer to Oxford or Cambridge University.
(Brit.) abbreviation for university (esp. Oxbridge)
informal british

Examples

She dreamed of attending varsity after finishing her A-levels.
The annual varsity rugby match between Oxford and Cambridge draws large crowds.
He wore his old varsity scarf with pride at the reunion.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'university', from Medieval Latin 'universitas'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːr.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈvɑː.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
the principal team representing a college or university, especially in sports competitions
main college or university sports team

Examples

She made the varsity soccer team as a freshman.
The school's varsity basketball team won the state championship last year.
He practices every day to secure a spot on the varsity squad.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: junior varsity - a secondary college or high school sports team

Etymology

Shortened from 'university,' originally used at Oxford and Cambridge in the 19th century.
