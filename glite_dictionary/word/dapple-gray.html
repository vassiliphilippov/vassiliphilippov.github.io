dapple-gray

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæpəl ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdæpəl ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
A gray animal, especially a horse, with a mottled pattern of darker gray spots or markings.
gray animal with mottled darker markings

Examples

The dapple-gray stood out in the herd because of its striking mottled coat.
She rode a beautiful dapple-gray into the sunset.
Several dapple-gray horses competed in the show.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gray - having a color between black and white

Etymology

From 'dapple' (mottled spot) + 'gray', referring to the animal's coloration.
