halobacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæloʊbækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌhæləʊbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a group of microorganisms that thrive in highly saline environments, such as salt flats or the Dead Sea
microorganisms thriving in high-salt conditions
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered new species of halobacteria in the salt ponds.
The Dead Sea is known for its unique halobacteria populations.
Some halobacteria produce red pigments that color salt flats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halophile - an organism that thrives in salty environments

Etymology

From Greek 'hals' (salt) and 'bakterion' (small rod).
