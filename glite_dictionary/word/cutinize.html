to cutinize

1. VERB

/ ˈkjuːtəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkjuːtɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
(of plant cells or tissues) to convert into or become impregnated with cutin.
become or turn into cutin (in plants)
medical

Examples

The outer cell walls cutinize to form a protective barrier against water loss.
Under certain conditions, leaves may cutinize more extensively to adapt to dry climates.
Researchers observed that some tissues failed to cutinize properly in mutant plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutin - a waxy, waterproof substance found in plant cuticles

Etymology

From 'cutin' (a waxy plant substance) + '-ize' (forming verbs).
