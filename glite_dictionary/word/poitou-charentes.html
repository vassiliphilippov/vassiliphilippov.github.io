Poitou-Charentes

1. PROPN

/ pwɑːˌtuː ʃɑːˈrɑːnt / (AmE), / pwɑːˌtuː ʃaˈʁɑ̃t / (BrE)
a low-lying region in west central France on the Bay of Biscay, formerly an administrative region.
region of west central France

Examples

She traveled through Poitou-Charentes during her summer vacation in France.
The wine from Poitou-Charentes is renowned throughout the country.
Many tourists visit Poitou-Charentes for its scenic coastline and tranquil countryside.

Etymology

French, from the historic provinces of Poitou and Charente.
