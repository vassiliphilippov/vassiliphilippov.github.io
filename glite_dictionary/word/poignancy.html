poignancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪnjənsi / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪɡnənsi / (BrE)
the quality of evoking a keen sense of sadness, pity, or deep emotion
quality of evoking deep emotion or sadness

Examples

The poignancy of the farewell scene moved many audience members to tears.
She spoke with poignancy about the struggles faced by refugees.
His memoir is filled with both humor and poignancy.

Synset Examples

the film captured all the pathos of their situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathos - the quality in something that evokes pity or sadness
  • Antonym: blandness - lack of emotional effect or strong feeling

Etymology

From Old French 'poignancie', from Latin 'pungere' (to prick, sting).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪ.njən.si / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪ.njən.si / (BrE)
a quality that evokes a keen sense of sadness or emotional distress
deep emotional distress, sorrow

Examples

The poignancy of the scene brought tears to everyone's eyes.
She spoke with a poignancy that touched the whole audience.
There was a certain poignancy in his voice as he shared the story.

Synset Examples

a moment of extraordinary poignancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathos - the quality in art or literature that evokes pity or sadness
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Old French 'poignance', from Latin 'pungere' (to prick).
