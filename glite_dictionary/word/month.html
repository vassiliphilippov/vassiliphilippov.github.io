month

1. NOUN

/ mʌnθ / (AmE), / mʌnθ / (BrE)
One of the twelve periods, each lasting from about 28 to 31 days, that make up a year in the calendar.
calendar period of about four weeks

Examples

Her birthday is next month, so we are planning a party.
Rent is usually due at the beginning of each month.
They traveled to three different countries in just one month.

Synset Examples

he paid the bill last month

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: week - a period of seven days
  • Degree Higher: year - a period of twelve months

Etymology

From Old English 'mōnath', related to the word for moon, reflecting the month's original basis on lunar cycles.

2. NOUN

/ mʌnθ / (AmE), / mʌnθ / (BrE)
A period of approximately four weeks or 30 days, used as a unit of time, typically corresponding to one of the twelve divisions of a calendar year.
unit of time, about 30 days

Examples

She plans to visit her family next month.
Rent for the apartment must be paid every month.
It took almost a month to finish the renovations.

Synset Examples

he was given a month to pay the bill

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: week - a period of seven days
  • Degree Higher: year - a period of twelve months

Etymology

From Old English *monath*, related to moon, from Proto-Germanic *mēnōth-*.
