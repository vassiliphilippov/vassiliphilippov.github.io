floatation

1. NOUN

/ floʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / fləʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of remaining on the surface of a liquid without sinking; the phenomenon of floating
the act or state of floating on liquid

Examples

The floatation of the wooden raft allowed it to carry several passengers safely across the river.
Saltwater increases the floatation of swimmers due to its higher density.
Proper floatation devices are essential for safety while boating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flotation - the act or condition of floating; an alternative spelling

Etymology

From float + -ation, modeled after analogous formations like flotation.

2. NOUN

/ floʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / fləʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of financing a company by issuing shares or bonds to the public for the first time
act of issuing shares or bonds to raise capital

Examples

The company's floatation on the stock market attracted many new investors.
After a successful floatation, the business expanded rapidly across Asia.
The board approved the floatation of bonds to fund new development projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flotation - alternative spelling, means the same as floatation

Etymology

From 'float' + '-ation'; related to the analogy of launching a company onto the market, as one launches a vessel onto water.

Synset POS: n
