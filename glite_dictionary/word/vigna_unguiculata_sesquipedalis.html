Vigna unguiculata sesquipedalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡnə ˌʌŋɡwɪˈkjuleɪtə sɛsˌkwɪpɪˈdeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə ˌʌŋɡwɪˈkjʊlətə sɛsˌkwɪpɪˈdeɪlɪs / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the legume family, known for its very long, edible pods, commonly called yardlong bean or asparagus bean.
tropical bean plant with long pods

Examples

Farmers in Southeast Asia widely cultivate Vigna unguiculata sesquipedalis for its tender, lengthy pods.
The nutritional value of Vigna unguiculata sesquipedalis makes it popular in vegetarian cooking.
You can find Vigna unguiculata sesquipedalis in many local markets, sold as yardlong beans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yardlong bean - another name for Vigna unguiculata sesquipedalis

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Vigna' (the genus), 'unguiculata' meaning 'with small claws', and 'sesquipedalis' meaning 'a foot and a half long.'
