baiza

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪzə / (AmE), / ˈbaɪzə / (BrE)
a monetary unit of Oman, equal to one thousandth of a riyal
unit of Omani currency

Examples

The price of the postcard was 500 baiza at the local shop.
He paid for the snacks with a few baiza coins.
One Omani riyal is made up of 1,000 baiza.

Etymology

From Arabic بَيْضَة‎ (bayḍa), meaning 'egg', used as a name for the coin.
