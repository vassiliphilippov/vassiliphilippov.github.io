trestle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛsəl / (BrE)
One of a pair of frames, typically with sloping legs, used to support a tabletop or other horizontal surface.
supporting frame for tabletops, etc.

Examples

We set up a workbench using two trestles and a wooden board.
The painter balanced his canvas on a pair of sturdy trestles.
Carpenters often use trestles to create a temporary table on construction sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawhorse - frame for supporting wood for sawing

Etymology

From Old French *trestel*, from Vulgar Latin *transtellum*, diminutive of Latin *transtrum* (crossbeam).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛsəl / (BrE)
A supporting framework consisting of a horizontal beam held up by two pairs of sloping legs, used especially to support a bridge or a table.
supporting framework, often for bridges

Examples

The old railway bridge relies on a massive trestle to span the valley safely.
Workers inspected the wooden trestle for signs of wear before allowing any trains to cross.
The engineer explained how each trestle supports the bridge's overall structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that bears the weight of another thing

Etymology

From Old French *trestel* (crossbeam), from Vulgar Latin *transtellum*, diminutive of Latin *transtrum* (crossbeam).
