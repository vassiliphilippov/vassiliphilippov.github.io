unbeknown

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnbɪkˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪkˈnəʊn / (BrE)
(usually with 'to') Existing or happening without a specified person knowing about it.
without someone's knowledge

Examples

The decision was made unbeknown to the staff members involved.
Unbeknown to him, his friends had organized a surprise party for his birthday.
The funds were transferred, unbeknown to the company's management.

Synset Examples

a crisis unbeknown to me
she had been ill for months, unbeknownst to the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unknown - not known or familiar

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' + archaic form 'beknown' (known).

2. ADV

/ ˌʌnbɪˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈnəʊn / (BrE)
(to someone) Without the knowledge of the person mentioned; unknown to someone.
without someone's knowledge

Examples

Unbeknown to her parents, she had already left the house.
Unbeknown to the guests, a surprise was being prepared in the next room.
Unbeknown to the team leader, the project had already been completed.

Synset Examples

unbeknownst to me, she made all the arrangements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbeknownst - without someone's knowledge; alternative form
  • Antonym: known - having knowledge of; recognized

Etymology

From un- (not) + known (past participle of 'know').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00486260-r
Synset POS: r
