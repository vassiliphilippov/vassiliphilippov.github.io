eurhythmy

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɪðmi / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɪðmi / (BrE)
A system of harmonious bodily movements performed to music, especially for interpreting the rhythm of musical compositions or as a teaching method for musical understanding.
harmonious bodily movement to music

Examples

The children practiced eurhythmy to learn how music and movement are connected.
Many schools incorporate eurhythmy into their curriculum to foster creativity and musicality.
She was captivated by the graceful eurhythmy performance on stage.

Etymology

From Greek 'eurhythmia', from 'eu-' (good, well) + 'rhythmos' (rhythm).
