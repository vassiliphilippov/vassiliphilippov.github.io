Solanopteris bifrons

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌlæn.ɑːpˈtɛr.ɪs ˈbaɪ.frɒnz / (AmE), / səʊˌlan.ɒpˈtɛr.ɪs ˈbʌɪ.frɒnz / (BrE)
A small epiphytic fern native to South America, characterized by tuberous swellings along its rhizomes.
South American epiphytic fern species

Examples

The Solanopteris bifrons thrives in the humid forests of the Andes.
Botanists identified the unusual tuberous swellings as a trait of Solanopteris bifrons.
Collectors value Solanopteris bifrons for its unique appearance and epiphytic habit.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Solanopteris' (from Greek 'solanum' and 'pteris', fern); species epithet 'bifrons' meaning 'two-faced' in Latin.
