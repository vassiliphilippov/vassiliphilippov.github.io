wire

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
(in horse racing) the finishing line on a racetrack, traditionally marked by a wire stretched above the track.
finishing line in horse racing

Examples

The horse surged ahead right before the wire to claim victory.
It was a close race all the way to the wire.
Fans cheered as the runners sprinted for the wire.

2. NOUN

/ waɪɚ / (AmE), / waɪə / (BrE)
a thin, flexible strand or rod of metal used to carry electricity over a distance
metal conductor for electricity

Examples

The electrician replaced the damaged wire to restore power to the house.
You should never touch a live wire with your bare hands.
They ran a wire along the wall to connect the light fixture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cable - a thick insulated wire or group of wires for carrying electricity

Etymology

From Old English *wīr* (metal thread, wire), of Germanic origin.
