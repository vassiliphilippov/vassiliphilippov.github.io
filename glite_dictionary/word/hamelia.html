Hamelia

1. NOUN

/ həˈmiːliə / (AmE), / həˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen tropical American shrubs or small trees, often grown for their ornamental flowers.
tropical American shrub genus

Examples

The bright red flowers of Hamelia attract hummingbirds in tropical gardens.
Several species of Hamelia are popular as ornamental plants in warm climates.
He specializes in the cultivation of Hamelia for botanical gardens.

Etymology

Named after Henri-Louis Duhamel du Monceau, French botanist (1700–1782).
