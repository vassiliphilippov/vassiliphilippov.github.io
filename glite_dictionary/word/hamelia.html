hamelia

1. NOUN

/ həˈmiːliə / (AmE), / həˈmiːliə / (BrE)
Any of several flowering tropical or subtropical shrubs of the genus Hamelia, known for their bright, tubular flowers.
a genus of tropical flowering shrubs

Examples

The gardener planted a hamelia near the patio to attract hummingbirds.
Many tropical gardens include hamelia for its showy red and orange flowers.
The hamelia thrives in warm climates with plenty of direct sunlight.

Etymology

Named after Henri-Louis Duhamel du Monceau, a French botanist.
