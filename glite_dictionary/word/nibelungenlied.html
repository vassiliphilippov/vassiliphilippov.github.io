Nibelungenlied

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːbəˈlʊŋənˌliːd / (AmE), / ˌniːbɛˈlʊŋənliːd / (BrE)
an epic poem written in Middle High German around 1200, telling the story of Siegfried, the Burgundians, and legendary events rooted in Germanic mythology
medieval German epic poem

Examples

Many scholars consider the Nibelungenlied a central work of German epic poetry.
Wagner's famous operas were inspired in part by characters from the Nibelungenlied.
The Nibelungenlied recounts the legendary exploits of Siegfried and the fall of the Burgundians.

Etymology

From German Nibelungenlied, meaning 'Song of the Nibelungs'.
