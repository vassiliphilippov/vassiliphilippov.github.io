highboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a tall chest of drawers with two sections, typically standing on four legs
tall chest of drawers on legs

Examples

The antique highboy in the hallway belonged to my great-grandmother.
They restored the oak highboy to match the vintage decor of the room.
He opened the top drawer of the highboy to look for his missing watch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowboy - a low chest of drawers, usually with short legs

Etymology

From 'high' + 'boy', used in 18th-century America for tall chests of drawers.
