Polemonium boreale

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌlɛməniəm bɔːˈreɪliː / (AmE), / pəˌlɛməniəm bɔːˈriːliː / (BrE)
a perennial, erect herb with white flowers, native to circumpolar Arctic regions
Arctic herb with white flowers

Examples

Polemonium boreale thrives in the Arctic tundra, where few other flowering plants can survive.
Scientists conducted a survey to map populations of Polemonium boreale across Svalbard.
The delicate white flowers of Polemonium boreale bloom during the brief polar summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polemonium - genus of flowering plants commonly known as Jacob's ladder

Etymology

From Latin; 'Polemonium' from Greek polemonion (a plant name), and 'boreale' meaning 'northern'.
