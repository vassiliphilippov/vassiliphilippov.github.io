anovulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.oʊ.vjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌan.əʊ.vjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
failure to ovulate, usually due to hormonal imbalance, illness, or other conditions.
absence of ovulation
medical

Examples

Irregular menstrual cycles can sometimes be a sign of anovulation.
Certain medications and stress may lead to temporary anovulation in women.
Doctors often test for anovulation when investigating causes of infertility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ovulation - the process of releasing an egg from an ovary

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'an-' (not) + English 'ovulation', from Latin 'ovulum' (little egg).
