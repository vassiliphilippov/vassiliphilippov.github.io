cofounder

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊˈfaʊndər / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
a person who jointly establishes a company or organization with one or more others
person who jointly establishes something

Examples

As cofounder of the tech startup, she played a key role in developing its first product.
The documentary features interviews with every living cofounder of the organization.
He became a cofounder after investing early and helping shape the company's vision.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: founder - a person who establishes an institution or settlement

Etymology

From co- (meaning 'jointly' from Latin) + founder (from Old French *fondeur*)
