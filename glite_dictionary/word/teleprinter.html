teleprinter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌprɪntə / (BrE)
an electromechanical typewriter device used for transmitting and receiving typed messages over telegraph or telephone lines
electromechanical message-sending typewriter

Examples

The news agency sent urgent reports over the teleprinter late into the night.
Before modern computers, companies relied on the teleprinter for fast long-distance communication.
She watched as the teleprinter clattered out lines of text from across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teletype - another term for teleprinter, often used in North America
  • Degree Higher: facsimile - a device for sending copies of printed material (fax), a successor technology

Etymology

Formed from 'tele-' (distance) and 'printer', indicating a device for distant printing.
