intoxication

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɑksəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The condition of having one's body or mind affected by a poison or other toxic substance.
state caused by poisoning

Examples

The patient showed clear symptoms of intoxication after ingesting the contaminated food.
Acute intoxication can be fatal without immediate medical attention.
Lead intoxication is particularly dangerous to children and pregnant women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoning - the condition caused by ingesting, inhaling, or absorbing a poison

Etymology

From Latin 'intoxicatio', from 'intoxicare' (to poison).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɑksɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A temporary state in which a person's physical and mental control is diminished due to excessive consumption of alcohol.
state caused by excessive alcohol consumption

Examples

Her speech was slurred due to intoxication after the party.
The driver was arrested for operating a vehicle while in a state of intoxication.
Symptoms of alcohol intoxication include impaired judgment and loss of coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inebriation - the state of being drunk; intoxication
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober; not affected by alcohol

Etymology

From Latin 'intoxicatio', from 'intoxicare' (to poison).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɑksɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of heightened excitement or elation, often experienced beyond normal bounds of self-control or moderation.
extreme excitement or elation

Examples

She felt an overwhelming intoxication as she stepped on stage to accept the award.
The intoxication of victory made the team forget their earlier doubts.
In his memoir, he describes the intoxication of newfound freedom after years of restrictions.

Synset Examples

the intoxication of wealth and power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhilaration - a feeling of lively excitement and happiness
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober or moderate; absence of excitement or excess

Etymology

From Latin intoxicatio (poisoning), from toxicum (poison).
