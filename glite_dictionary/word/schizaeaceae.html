Schizaeaceae

1. NOUN

/ skaɪziˈeɪəsiː / (AmE), / skɪziːˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a small family of mainly tropical ferns with unique spore-bearing structures, belonging to the order Schizaeales
family of tropical ferns

Examples

The Schizaeaceae are most commonly found in humid, tropical regions around the world.
Many botanists study Schizaeaceae to understand fern evolution and diversification.
Some members of the Schizaeaceae family have been used in traditional medicine.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'skhizein' (to split), referring to the split sporangia of these ferns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌskiːziˌeɪˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌskɪziːˈiːsiːaɪ / (BrE)
A small family of mainly tropical ferns distinguished by their unique spore arrangements.
family of tropical ferns

Examples

Several species in the Schizaeaceae family are prized among fern enthusiasts for their unique appearance.
The Schizaeaceae are most commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
Botanists recently discovered a new member of the Schizaeaceae in the rainforest.

Etymology

From the genus name Schizaea, from Greek 'skhizein' meaning 'to split', referring to the appearance of the fronds, and standard botanical Latin family suffix -aceae.
