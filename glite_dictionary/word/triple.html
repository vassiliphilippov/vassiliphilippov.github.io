triple

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəl / (BrE)
An amount or quantity that is three times as great as another or as usual.
a quantity three times as great as another

Examples

Many athletes receive a triple of the standard allowance for their achievements.
The company's profits reached a triple compared to last year.
The recipe calls for a triple of the usual amount of sugar for extra sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - a quantity that is twice as great as another
  • Degree Higher: quadruple - a quantity that is four times as great as another

Etymology

From Latin 'triplus', from 'tri-' (three) + 'plus' (fold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəl / (BrE)
(in baseball) a hit that allows the batter to reach third base safely on a single play.
baseball hit for three bases

Examples

The crowd cheered as he hit a triple in the bottom of the ninth.
She scored a triple that helped her team take the lead.
Despite the pitcher’s best efforts, the batter managed a triple to center field.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - (baseball) a hit that allows the batter to reach second base
  • Degree Higher: home run - (baseball) a hit that allows the batter to circle all bases and score
