Eriogonum allenii

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˈɒɡənəm ˈælɪniː / (AmE), / ˌɛriˈɒɡənəm ˈælɪniː / (BrE)
A late-blooming perennial plant native to the shale barrens of Virginia, noted for its flat-topped clusters of yellow flowers.
species of wild buckwheat from Virginia

Examples

The rare Eriogonum allenii blooms late in the season, adding color to the shale barrens.
We spotted several Eriogonum allenii growing in the rocky landscape of western Virginia.
Botanists are working to conserve the habitat of Eriogonum allenii due to its limited range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild buckwheat - common name for plants in the genus Eriogonum

Etymology

Named after botanist E. J. Allen; Eriogonum from Greek 'erion' (wool) and 'gonu' (knee/joint), referring to the plant's woolly joints.
