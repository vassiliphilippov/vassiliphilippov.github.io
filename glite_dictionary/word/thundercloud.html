thundercloud

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndɚˌklaʊd / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəˌklaʊd / (BrE)
a large, dark cloud charged with electricity, often bringing thunderstorms and associated with heavy rain, thunder, and lightning
a dark, stormy cloud that produces thunder

Examples

A thundercloud loomed overhead, signaling the start of the storm.
The sky darkened quickly as a massive thundercloud approached from the west.
Lightning flashed repeatedly within the thundercloud, lighting up the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumulonimbus - a type of heavy, towering cloud producing thunderstorms

Etymology

Formed from 'thunder' and 'cloud'.
