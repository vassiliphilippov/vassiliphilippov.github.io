to prepare

1. VERB

/ prɪˈpɛr / (AmE), / prɪˈpeə / (BrE)
to arrange or organize something through careful planning and coordination, often involving several people
arrange by systematic planning

Examples

The committee will prepare a detailed proposal before the end of the month.
They worked together to prepare the plan for the school fundraiser.
It took months to prepare the logistics for the festival.

Synset Examples

machinate a plot
organize a strike
devise a plan to take over the director's office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange or structure systematically
  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought

Etymology

From Latin praeparare (to make ready beforehand), from prae- (before) + parare (to make ready).
