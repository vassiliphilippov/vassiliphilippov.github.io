fantastic

1. ADJ

/ fænˈtæstɪk / (AmE), / fanˈtastɪk / (BrE)
Fanciful, not based on reality, and so unlikely or foolish.
fanciful or unrealistic; foolish

Examples

His plan to build a city on the moon was dismissed as a fantastic vision.
She was criticized for proposing such a fantastic solution to a serious problem.
It was a fantastic tale, full of improbable events and imaginary creatures.

Synset Examples

a fantastic idea of his own importance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity; inventive
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things as they really are

Etymology

From Greek phantastikos (able to make visible), via Latin and Old French.

/ fænˈtæstɪk / (AmE), / fænˈtæstɪk / (BrE)
Extremely good, impressive, or enjoyable; extraordinarily good or great.
extraordinarily good or impressive

Examples

She received a fantastic job offer from a leading tech company.
Their new restaurant serves fantastic food that keeps customers coming back.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremendous - very great in amount, scale, or intensity
  • Antonym: terrible - very bad, of poor quality
  • Synonym: incredible - difficult to believe; extraordinarily good

Etymology

From Greek phantastikos ('able to imagine, make visible'), via Latin and Old French.
