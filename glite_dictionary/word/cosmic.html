cosmic

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑzmɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɪk / (BrE)
Extremely large or vast, especially so immense that it cannot be easily measured or comprehended; relating to immeasurable extent in space or time.
immeasurably vast; extremely large

Examples

The Hubble telescope allows us to glimpse the cosmic scale of the universe.
Their ambitions were not merely grand—they were truly cosmic in scope.
Even a single grain of sand is insignificant compared to the cosmic distances between stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantic - extremely large; enormous
  • Antonym: minute - extremely small; tiny

Etymology

From Greek kosmikos (relating to the universe), from kosmos (order, universe).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑzmɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the universe or cosmos, especially as distinct from the Earth; vast or universal in scope.
relating to the universe or cosmos

Examples

The discovery of cosmic rays led to major advancements in physics.
Philosophers often ponder the cosmic significance of human life.
A cosmic collision billions of years ago is believed to have created our moon.

Synset Examples

cosmic laws
cosmic catastrophe
cosmic rays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universal - relating to or done by all people or things in the world or in a particular group

Etymology

From Greek kosmikos (of the world), from kosmos (world, universe).
