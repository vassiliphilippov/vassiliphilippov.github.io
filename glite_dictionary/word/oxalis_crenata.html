Oxalis crenata

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈzælɪs krəˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs krɛˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of South American wood sorrel cultivated for its edible tubers, often used as a food crop in the Andes.
Andean tuber-producing wood sorrel

Examples

Oxalis crenata is widely cultivated in the Andes for its tangy, edible tubers.
Farmers rely on Oxalis crenata as a traditional crop to supplement potatoes.
The tubers of Oxalis crenata add a unique flavor to many Peruvian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oca - Common name for Oxalis crenata, an Andean tuber crop

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Oxalis' (wood sorrel) and species 'crenata' (notched, scalloped).
