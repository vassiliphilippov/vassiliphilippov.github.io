oddity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːdəti / (AmE), / ˈɒdəti / (BrE)
a strange or unusual habit, mannerism, or way of thinking
strange habit or mannerism

Examples

Her tendency to talk to plants was considered an oddity by her neighbors.
Among his many oddities was a habit of collecting bottle caps from every country he visited.
No one minded her little oddity of humming while she worked, as it brightened the mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quirk - a peculiar behavioral habit
  • Synonym: eccentricity - an unusual habit or behavior
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal

Etymology

From odd + -ity, modeled after Latin-derived nouns with -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.dɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈɒd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a person or thing that is strange or unusual; something out of the ordinary, sometimes considered worth noticing or collecting
unusual or strange person or thing

Examples

The museum displayed several oddities collected from around the world.
She was considered an oddity in her small town because of her peculiar hobbies.
Among his collections, the rare coin was the most prized oddity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curiosity - an unusual or interesting object or fact
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or usual

Etymology

From odd + -ity, from Middle French -ité, Latin -itas (suffix forming abstract nouns).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːd.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈɒd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a strange or unusual quality or trait, especially one that is hard to explain; eccentricity
unusual or eccentric quality

Examples

The artist was well known for his many oddities and unconventional habits.
Living alone in the forest was seen as an oddity by his neighbors.
Her oddity made her stand out in a crowd, but she embraced her uniqueness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentricity - unusual or unconventional behavior
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical

Etymology

From odd + -ity; modeled after Latin-origin abstract nouns ending in -ity.
