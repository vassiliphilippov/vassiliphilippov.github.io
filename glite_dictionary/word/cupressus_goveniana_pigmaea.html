Cupressus goveniana pigmaea

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈprɛsəs ɡoʊˌvɛniˈɑːnə pɪɡˈmiːə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs ɡəʊˌvɛniˈɑːnə pɪɡˈmiːə / (BrE)
a rare, small cypress tree native to northern California, sometimes regarded as a variety of the Gowen cypress
rare cypress of northern California

Examples

The Cupressus goveniana pigmaea is found in the unique pygmy forests of Mendocino County.
Botanists have debated whether Cupressus goveniana pigmaea should be classified as a separate species.
Conservationists are working to protect Cupressus goveniana pigmaea due to its limited natural range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gowen cypress - a species of cypress tree native to California (Cupressus goveniana)

Etymology

Cupressus from Latin for 'cypress'; goveniana and pigmaea are botanical Latin, with pigmaea meaning 'dwarf or pygmy'.
