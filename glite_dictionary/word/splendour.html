splendour

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɛn.də / (BrE)
Impressive beauty, magnificence, or grandeur.
magnificent or impressive beauty
british

Examples

The palace was known throughout the land for its splendour and beauty.
Tourists come from all over the world to witness the natural splendour of the mountains.
Her gown glittered with such splendour that all eyes were drawn to her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificence - impressive beauty or greatness
  • Synonym: grandeur - splendid or impressive quality; grandness
  • Antonym: drabness - dullness; lacking brightness or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'splendor' (brightness, brilliance), via Old French 'splendour'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɛn.də / (BrE)
great beauty or an impressive quality, especially something that is magnificent or glorious.
magnificent beauty or impressiveness
british

Examples

The palace was renowned for the splendour of its architecture and gardens.
Tourists marveled at the natural splendour of the mountain landscape in spring.
The festival was celebrated with such splendour that it left everyone in awe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliance - exceptional brightness or impressive quality
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or vividness

Etymology

From Latin 'splendor' (brightness, brilliance), via Old French 'splendeur'.
