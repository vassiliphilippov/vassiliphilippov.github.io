Lespedeza sericea

1. NOUN

/ lɛs.pəˈdiː.zə səˈriː.si.ə / (AmE), / lɛs.pɛˈdiː.zə səˈriː.si.ə / (BrE)
A perennial plant widely cultivated for forage and as a hay crop, especially on poor soils; also known as sericea lespedeza.
perennial forage and hay crop plant

Examples

Lespedeza sericea is often planted on reclaimed mining land to improve soil quality.
Farmers use Lespedeza sericea as a nutritious hay crop for livestock.
Areas with poor soil fertility benefit from introducing Lespedeza sericea as a forage plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sericea lespedeza - Another common name for Lespedeza sericea

Etymology

From scientific Latin: Lespedeza (genus name) + sericea (from Latin 'sericeus', silky).
