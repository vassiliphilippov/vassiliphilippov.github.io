Callorhinus ursinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈraɪ.nəs ɝːˈsaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈraɪ.nəs ɜːˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a species of fur seal native to the North Pacific, from Alaska to California, commonly known as the northern fur seal
a species of North Pacific fur seal

Examples

Researchers have studied the migration patterns of the Callorhinus ursinus across the Pacific Ocean.
The Callorhinus ursinus population has suffered declines due to commercial hunting in the past.
Unlike other seals in the region, Callorhinus ursinus has a thick, luxurious fur coat that once attracted fur traders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern fur seal - common name for Callorhinus ursinus

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus Callorhinus (from Greek kallos 'beauty' + rhinos 'nose') and species epithet ursinus (from Latin 'of a bear') due to its bear-like fur.
