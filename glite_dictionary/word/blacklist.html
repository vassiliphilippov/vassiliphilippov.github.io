blacklist

1. VERB

/ ˈblækˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈblæk.lɪst / (BrE)
to put (a person or thing) on a list of people or things that are disapproved of, banned, or boycotted
to ban or boycott by listing

Examples

The government threatened to blacklist any company that broke the new regulations.
Several artists were blacklisted for their political beliefs in the 1950s.
The publisher was blacklisted by major retailers after the controversy.

Synset Examples

many books were blacklisted by the Nazis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boycott - to refuse to deal with as a form of protest
  • Synonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈblak.lɪst / (BrE)
a list of people or entities who are disapproved of, banned, or excluded by an authority or group
list of people banned or disapproved of

Examples

Several companies were placed on the blacklist for violating regulations.
Being added to the blacklist made it difficult for him to find work in the industry.
Journalists discovered the existence of a secret blacklist used to bar dissenters from public events.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whitelist - a list of people or things that are approved or accepted
  • Synonym: boycott - to refuse to deal with as a form of protest

Etymology

Originally from 'black' + 'list', first used in this sense in the 17th century.
