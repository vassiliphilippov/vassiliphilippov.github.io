WNW

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juː ɛn ˈdʌbəl.juː / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl.juː ɛn ˈdʌbəl.juː / (BrE)
the direction, or the point on a compass, that is halfway between west and northwest, at an angle of 292.5° clockwise from north
compass point between west and northwest

Examples

The wind was blowing steadily from the WNW all afternoon.
The ship changed course and headed WNW to avoid the storm.
According to the map, the next village lies directly WNW of here.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: west - the direction to the left when facing north
  • Degree Higher: northwest - the direction halfway between north and west

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'west-northwest'.
