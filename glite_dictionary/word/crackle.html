crackle

1. VERB

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
(of a surface or material) to become, or to cause to become, covered with a network of small cracks.
become covered with small cracks

Examples

Over time, the old paint began to crackle in the summer heat.
The pottery glaze will crackle as it cools, creating a unique pattern.
Intense cold can cause asphalt to crackle and break apart.

Synset Examples

The blazing sun crackled the desert sand

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of cracking or crackling; related to 'crack.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00122456-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
(of a surface) having a network of fine cracks, especially as in glazes on pottery or ceramics.
decorated with fine surface cracks

Examples

The antique vase has a beautiful crackle glaze that adds to its charm.
She admired the crackle finish on the old porcelain plate.
The artist used a special technique to create a crackle effect on the surface of the bowl.

Synset Examples

a crackle glaze
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01799969-s

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkræk.əl / (BrE)
a series of short, sharp, snapping or popping sounds
series of sharp snapping sounds

Examples

The crackle of the burning logs filled the room with a cozy atmosphere.
She listened to the crackle on the radio as the signal faded in and out.
There was a crackle of static before the announcer started speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - a sudden, sharp cracking noise
  • Synonym: pop - a short, sharp sound like a small explosion

Etymology

From Middle English crakelen, frequentative form of crack.

fine cracks in glaze of ceramics

5. VERB

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
to make a series of short, sharp noises, often as something is crushed, burned, or broken
make a series of sharp, snapping noises

Examples

The fire crackled warmly in the fireplace as they talked.
As he walked through the frosty grass, it crackled under his boots.
Old vinyl records sometimes crackle when played on a turntable.

Synset Examples

his shoes were crunching on the gravel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - make a sudden, sharp cracking sound
  • Synonym: pop - make a quick, sharp sound, like a small explosion
  • Synonym: crunch - make a loud, crushing noise, especially when being pressed or crushed

Etymology

From Middle English 'crakelen', frequentative of 'cracken' (to crack).

6. VERB

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
to make a series of short, sharp, popping sounds
make popping or crackling sounds

Examples

The fire began to crackle as the wood caught alight.
I love to listen to bacon crackle in the frying pan.
The old vinyl record would crackle every time the needle passed over a scratch.

Synset Examples

My Rice Krispies crackled in the bowl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - to make a sudden sharp cracking sound
  • Synonym: pop - to make a short, sharp, explosive sound
  • Antonym: hiss - to make a sibilant sound like that of the letter 's'

Etymology

From Middle English 'crakelen' (to make slight cracking noises), frequentative of 'cracken' (to crack).
