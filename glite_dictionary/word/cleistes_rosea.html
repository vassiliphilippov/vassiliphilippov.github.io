Cleistes rosea

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪstiːz ˈroʊziə / (AmE), / ˈklaɪstiːz ˈrəʊziə / (BrE)
An orchid of central and northern South America, with 1- to 3-blossomed racemes of large, showy rose-colored flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Pogonia.
South American orchid with rose-colored flowers

Examples

The rare Cleistes rosea is prized among orchid enthusiasts for its vivid blossoms.
Botanists documented several specimens of Cleistes rosea in the rainforest region last year.
I photographed a beautiful Cleistes rosea while hiking in northern Brazil.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Cleistes', from Greek 'kleistos' (closed), and Latin 'rosea' (rose-colored).
