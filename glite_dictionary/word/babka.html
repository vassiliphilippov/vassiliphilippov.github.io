babka

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbkə / (AmE), / ˈbab.kə / (BrE)
a sweet, leavened cake of Eastern European origin, typically flavored with orange rind and often containing raisins and almonds
Eastern European sweet cake with fruit and nuts

Examples

We served babka at the brunch, and everyone loved its citrus and raisin filling.
My grandmother taught me how to bake a traditional babka for the holidays.
Slices of babka were arranged on the dessert table beside other pastries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffee cake - a cake, often with fruit or nuts, usually eaten with coffee

Etymology

From Polish 'babka' (little grandmother), referring to the cake's shape.
