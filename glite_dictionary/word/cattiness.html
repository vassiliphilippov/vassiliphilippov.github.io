cattiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæti nəs / (AmE), / ˈkæti nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being spiteful, malicious, or unpleasant, often in a subtle or indirect way
spiteful or malicious quality

Examples

Her cattiness during the meeting made everyone uncomfortable.
There's no need for cattiness when giving constructive criticism.
Despite their friendship, a sense of cattiness sometimes crept into their conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From 'catty' (malicious or spiteful), likely from the comparison to the sly or spiteful behavior attributed to cats, plus '-ness' (forming nouns from adjectives).
