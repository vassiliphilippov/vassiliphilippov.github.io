Pleuronectes platessa

1. NOUN

/ plʊˌrɒnɛktiz pləˈtɛsə / (AmE), / pljʊərəˌnɛktiːz pləˈtiːsə / (BrE)
a large European flatfish, commonly known as the European plaice, valued as a food fish
a European flatfish; European plaice

Examples

The North Sea is home to a large population of Pleuronectes platessa, making it important for local fisheries.
Many chefs consider Pleuronectes platessa one of the finest fish for traditional European seafood recipes.
Overfishing has caused a decline in Pleuronectes platessa stocks in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaice - the common name for Pleuronectes platessa, a European edible flatfish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus Pleuronectes (from Greek 'pleura' side + 'nektēs' swimmer), and species 'platessa'.
