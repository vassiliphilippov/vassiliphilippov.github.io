goaltender

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊlˌtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊlˌtɛndə / (BrE)
a player in sports such as hockey or soccer who is assigned to defend the goal and prevent the opposing team from scoring
player who defends the goal in sports

Examples

The goaltender made an impressive save in the final seconds of the match.
Despite the pressure, the goaltender remained calm and focused throughout the game.
Our team's goaltender was awarded player of the match for her outstanding performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goalkeeper - player who guards the goal, especially in soccer

Etymology

Formed from 'goal' + 'tender' (one who tends or guards). Used primarily in North American sports contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊlˌtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊlˌtɛndə / (BrE)
the player in sports such as ice hockey, soccer, or lacrosse whose main job is to prevent the opposing team from scoring by guarding the goal
player who guards the goal in sports

Examples

The team relied heavily on their goaltender to keep the score close.
After making a spectacular save, the goaltender received a standing ovation from the crowd.
Both teams praised the goaltender for his quick reflexes and calm under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goalkeeper - player who guards the goal in sports; especially used in soccer
  • Degree Lower: defender - player whose main role is to stop the opposition from scoring

Etymology

Formed from 'goal' + 'tender' (one who tends or guards).
