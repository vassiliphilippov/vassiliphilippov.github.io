Pekinese

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkəˌniz / (AmE), / ˌpiːkɪˈniːz / (BrE)
A small dog of a Chinese breed, having short legs, a long silky coat, and a flat, broad muzzle.
small Chinese dog breed with silky coat

Examples

The Pekinese sat proudly on the cushion, surveying its surroundings.
Many owners prize the Pekinese for its elegance and loyalty.
Her grandmother has a pair of Pekineses that she often takes for walks in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pekingese - a Chinese breed of small dogs with a silky coat and a flat face

Etymology

From Peking, the old name for Beijing, China, where the breed originated.
