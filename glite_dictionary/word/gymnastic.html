gymnastic

1. ADJ

/ dʒɪmˈnæstɪk / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnastɪk / (BrE)
involving or displaying vigorous physical activity or agility, especially as in gymnastics
vigorously active, as in gymnastics

Examples

The children performed a series of gymnastic exercises during their physical education class.
Her dance routine was highly gymnastic, featuring flips and leaps that amazed the audience.
He admired the gymnastic feats of the trapeze artists at the circus.

Synset Examples

an acrobatic dance
an athletic child
athletic playing
gymnastic exercises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrobatic - involving spectacular gymnastic feats or agility
  • Synonym: athletic - physically active and strong

Etymology

From Greek *gymnastikos* (fond of athletic exercises), via Latin *gymnasticus*.

2. ADJ

/ dʒɪmˈnæstɪk / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnastɪk / (BrE)
relating to or used in gymnastics or exercises designed to develop strength and agility
relating to gymnastics or exercises

Examples

She demonstrated remarkable gymnastic abilities during the floor routine.
The new equipment is suitable for all kinds of gymnastic exercises.
A gymnastic coach supervised the children's training session.

Synset Examples

gymnastic horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gymnastic - relating to gymnastics or exercises
  • Synonym: gymnastics - the sport involving exercises to develop strength and flexibility

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnastikos' (fond of or skilled in bodily exercise) via French 'gymnastique'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02860617-a
Synset POS: a
