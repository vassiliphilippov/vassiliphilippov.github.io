adjective

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈadʒɪktɪv / (BrE)
A word used to describe a noun by expressing an attribute such as quality, quantity, or state.
a word describing a noun

Examples

In the sentence 'the red apple,' 'red' is an adjective describing the noun 'apple'.
The adjective 'happy' tells us about the boy's mood.
Her essay required at least five adjectives to describe the main character.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun - a word used to identify people, places, or things
  • Antonym: verb - a word used to describe an action, state, or occurrence
  • Antonym: adverb - a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb

Etymology

From Latin 'adiectivum' (added), from 'adiicere' (to add to).

2. NOUN

/ ˈædʒɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈadʒɪktɪv / (BrE)
A word that describes a noun by giving more information about its qualities, such as size, color, or number.
word describing a noun

Examples

In the phrase 'blue sky,' the word blue is an adjective that describes the noun 'sky'.
She asked the students to underline every adjective in the sentence.
Words like 'happy,' 'large,' and 'interesting' are all examples of an adjective.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun - a word class denoting persons, places, or things
  • Antonym: adverb - a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb

Etymology

From Latin 'adjectivum' (added, qualifying), from 'ad' (to) + 'iacere' (throw).

Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈædʒɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈadʒ.ɪk.tɪv / (BrE)
(of law) relating to court practice and procedure as opposed to the principles of law; procedural rather than substantive.
relating to procedure in law
legal

Examples

The court distinguished between adjective and substantive law in its ruling.
He is an expert on adjective law, especially court procedures.
The bill proposed several adjective changes to streamline litigation processes.

Synset Examples

adjective law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procedural - relating to the rules of a court or legal procedure
  • Antonym: substantive - relating to the essential principles of law, not procedure

Etymology

From Latin adjectivus 'added', from adicere 'to add to'. Legal use relates to something added to the main body of law.
