Nymphaea caerulea

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə siːˈruːliə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfiːə siəˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
A water lily species native to Egypt, with blue flowers, historically revered by ancient Egyptians.
a blue-flowered Egyptian water lily

Examples

The ancient Egyptians often depicted the Nymphaea caerulea in their art and religious ceremonies.
Botanists study Nymphaea caerulea for its unique blue petals and medicinal properties.
A pond covered in Nymphaea caerulea creates a stunning and tranquil landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue lotus - common name for Nymphaea caerulea
  • Degree Higher: Nymphaea - the genus of aquatic plants known as water lilies

Etymology

From Latin: Nymphaea (water lily) + caerulea (blue).
