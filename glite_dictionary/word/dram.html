dram

1. NOUN

/ dræm / (AmE), / dræm / (BrE)
the official currency and basic monetary unit of Armenia, equal to 100 luma.
Armenian currency unit

Examples

The price of bread in Yerevan is about 150 dram per loaf.
She exchanged her dollars for Armenian drams at the airport.
Tourists often ask how many drams are needed for a taxi ride in Armenia.

Etymology

From Armenian դրամ (dram), meaning 'money' or 'coin'.

2. NOUN

/ dræm / (AmE), / dræm / (BrE)
a unit of apothecary weight equal to one eighth of an ounce or to 60 grains
unit of apothecary weight
medical

Examples

The prescription called for one dram of the medication to be administered daily.
In apothecary measurements, a dram is equivalent to sixty grains.
The old bottle was labeled as containing six drams of tincture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ounce - a unit of weight equal to about 28 grams
  • Degree Lower: grain - a unit of weight equal to 1/7000 of a pound

Etymology

From Late Latin 'dragma', from Greek 'dragma' (a handful, a weight).

3. NOUN

/ dræm / (AmE), / dræm / (BrE)
A unit of weight equal to 1/16 of an ounce or approximately 1.771 grams.
unit of weight; 1/16 ounce

Examples

The pharmacist measured out a dram of the powder for the prescription.
In some old recipes, a dram is used as a small measure of weight.
They discovered a scale that could weigh quantities as light as a single dram.

Etymology

From Old French *drame*, from Late Latin *dragma*, from Greek *drakhmē* (a weight, a coin).
