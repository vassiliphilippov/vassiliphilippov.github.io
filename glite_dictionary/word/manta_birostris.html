Manta birostris

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæntə baɪˈrɒstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈmantə baɪˈrɒstrɪs / (BrE)
a very large species of oceanic manta ray, reaching up to 22 feet across, found in warm waters worldwide
giant oceanic manta ray species

Examples

Divers were thrilled to observe a majestic Manta birostris gliding silently over the reef.
The Manta birostris is known for its enormous size and graceful movements in the open ocean.
Conservationists are working to protect Manta birostris from threats like overfishing and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manta ray - large marine ray with triangular pectoral fins, typically called the 'manta'

Etymology

From Latin, with 'manta' meaning blanket and 'birostris' meaning two-snouted.
