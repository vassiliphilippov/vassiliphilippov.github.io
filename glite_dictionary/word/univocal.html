univocal

1. ADJ

/ juːˈnɪvoʊkəl / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəkəl / (BrE)
having only one possible meaning or interpretation; clear and unambiguous.
having a single, unambiguous meaning

Examples

The contract was written in univocal terms to avoid any confusion.
His response was univocal, leaving no room for misinterpretation.
Researchers prefer univocal definitions to ensure precise communication.

Synset Examples

unequivocal evidence
took an unequivocal position
an unequivocal success
an unequivocal promise
an unequivocal (or univocal) statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unequivocal - leaving no doubt; clear and unambiguous
  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one clear meaning

Etymology

From Late Latin univocus (having one meaning), from Latin unus (one) + voc-, vox (voice).
