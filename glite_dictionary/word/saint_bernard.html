Saint Bernard

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt bərˈnɑrd / (AmE), / seɪnt bəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a large, strong breed of dog originally from the Swiss Alps, known for its thick coat and use in mountain rescue work
large Swiss Alpine rescue dog breed

Examples

The Saint Bernard was famous for rescuing lost travelers in the mountains.
Her family owns a gentle Saint Bernard that loves children.
Saint Bernards require plenty of space and regular exercise due to their size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Newfoundland - large working dog breed known for strength and swimming ability
  • Synonym: St. Bernard - alternate written form and abbreviation of 'Saint Bernard'

Etymology

Named after the Great St Bernard Pass in the Alps, where the breed was used for rescue.
