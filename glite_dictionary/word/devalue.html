to devalue

1. VERB

/ diˈvælju / (AmE), / diˈvæljuː / (BrE)
to reduce or remove the value or worth of something; to cause something to be considered less important or valuable
reduce or remove the value of

Examples

Rapid inflation can devalue a country's currency.
He felt the feedback unfairly devalued his hard work and achievements.
New evidence may devalue the historical significance of the artifact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depreciate - to diminish in value over time or to reduce the worth of something
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to increase the value of something

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + value, modeled on French dévaluer.

2. VERB

/ diˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / diˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) to lose in value; to become less valuable, especially in terms of exchange rates or worth.
to lose in value

Examples

Many currencies tend to devalue during periods of economic instability.
If the company continues to perform poorly, its stock may devalue significantly.
Experts warned that the policy could cause the local currency to devalue against the dollar.

Synset Examples

The dollar depreciated again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depreciate - to decrease in value over time
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to increase in value over time

Etymology

From de- (indicating reversal) + value, modeled after French dévaluer.

3. VERB

/ diˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / diˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
to reduce the value or quality of something; to make something less important, valuable, or respected.
reduce value or quality

Examples

A significant scratch can devalue a car considerably.
Many people believe that mass production has devalued the uniqueness of traditional crafts.
Constant criticism will only devalue her confidence over time.

Synset Examples

The tear devalues the painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depreciate - to decrease in value over time or through action
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to increase in value over time or through action
  • Synonym: to debase - to reduce in quality or value; to degrade

Etymology

From de- (expressing removal or reversal) + value, modeled after French dévaluer.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
