angina

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒaɪnə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle.
chest pain from reduced heart blood flow
medical

Examples

The doctor warned that untreated angina could lead to serious heart problems.
Many people experience angina during physical exertion or times of stress.
Her persistent angina required her to take medication and change her lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heart attack - sudden and severe heart condition caused by blocked blood flow
  • Synonym: chest pain - pain or discomfort in the chest

Etymology

From Latin 'angina', meaning 'infection of the throat', from 'angere' meaning 'to strangle'.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒaɪ.nə / (BrE)
A condition in which severe, constricting pain occurs, especially in the chest or throat, often caused by reduced blood flow or inflammation.
severe constricting pain, often in chest or throat
medical

Examples

The patient was admitted to the hospital after complaining of severe angina attacks.
Early treatments reduced the symptoms of angina and prevented further complications.
She had difficulty swallowing due to angina affecting her throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinsy - an abscess in the tissue around a tonsil

Etymology

From Latin 'angina' (quinsy, choking, sore throat), from 'angere' (to strangle).
