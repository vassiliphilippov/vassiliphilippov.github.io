aneurysm

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jəˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈæn.jʊ.rɪz.əm / (BrE)
A localized, sac-like widening of an artery caused by a weakening of the arterial wall.
abnormal artery widening
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the aneurysm could rupture if left untreated.
An aneurysm was detected during a routine brain scan.
He underwent surgery to repair a dangerous aneurysm in his aorta.

Etymology

From Greek 'aneurysma', meaning 'dilation'.
