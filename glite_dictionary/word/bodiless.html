bodiless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːdi.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi.lɪs / (BrE)
Lacking a trunk or main body, consisting only of a part, such as a head without its body.
lacking a trunk or main body

Examples

The ancient helmet was perched atop a bodiless head on the table.
A bodiless statue stood in the corner, only the limbs were left.
The tale described a bodiless spirit that drifted through the halls.

Synset Examples

a bodiless head

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headless - without a head or main part

Etymology

From body + -less (suffix meaning 'without').

2. ADJ

/ ˈboʊdiˌlɪs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊdiˌlɪs / (BrE)
not having a physical or material body; incorporeal.
lacking a physical body

Examples

The legend told of bodiless apparitions haunting the castle.
She felt a bodiless presence in the empty room.
The poem described a bodiless voice whispering in the darkness.

Synset Examples

bodiless ghosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorporeal - not having a physical body; immaterial
  • Antonym: corporeal - having a physical, material body

Etymology

From 'body' + '-less', the suffix meaning 'without'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00633881-s
Synset POS: s
