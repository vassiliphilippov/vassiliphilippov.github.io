multiplicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltɪˈplɪsɪti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈplɪsɪti / (BrE)
The state of being multiple or having a large number or variety of parts, aspects, or elements.
state of being multiple; variety

Examples

The multiplicity of choices made it difficult to decide on a single option.
She was fascinated by the multiplicity of cultures represented at the festival.
Quantum mechanics deals with a multiplicity of possible outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse; a range of different things
  • Antonym: singularity - the state of being singular, unique, or one of a kind

Etymology

From Latin multiplicitas (multiplicity), from multiplex (having many folds or parts).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltɪˈplɪsɪti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈplɪsɪti / (BrE)
A large number or variety of things.
a large number or variety

Examples

The city offers a multiplicity of cultural experiences for visitors.
There was a multiplicity of reasons why she chose to move abroad.
Scientists are investigating the multiplicity of factors affecting climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - a number of different types of the same thing
  • Synonym: diversity - the state of being diverse; variety
  • Antonym: singularity - the state of being singular or unique

Etymology

From Old French *multiplicite*, from Latin *multiplicitas* (great number, multiplicity), from *multiplex* (having many parts).
