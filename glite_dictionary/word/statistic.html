statistic

1. NOUN

/ stəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / stəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
a single numerical fact or datum, often forming part of a set used for analysis or interpretation
a single numerical datum

Examples

The company uses every statistic to measure customer satisfaction.
One alarming statistic is that unemployment has doubled in the past year.
She cited a surprising statistic about literacy rates in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statistics - the science of collecting and analyzing numerical data

Etymology

From New Latin statisticus (of state affairs), from Italian statistico (of politics), related to 'statistics'.
