Galega

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈliːɡə / (AmE), / ɡəˈliːɡə / (BrE)
A small genus of Eurasian herbaceous plants in the pea family, known as goat's rue.
genus of Eurasian plants (goat's rue)

Examples

The genus Galega includes species commonly known as goat's rue.
Botanists have studied Galega for its potential use as fodder.
Several medicinal compounds have been isolated from Galega plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goat's rue - a Eurasian herbaceous plant used in herbal medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'galega', from Greek 'galegē', meaning 'milkwort'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈliːɡə / (AmE), / ɡəˈliːɡə / (BrE)
A small genus of Eurasian herbaceous plants in the pea family, commonly known as goat's rue.
genus of Eurasian herbs (goat's rue)

Examples

The genus Galega includes species such as goat's rue, which is valued for its medicinal properties.
Researchers studied the distribution of Galega in Eastern Europe.
Members of the Galega genus are often cultivated as forage plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goat's rue - a common plant of the genus Galega

Etymology

From Latin Galega, from Ancient Greek gale (milk) and ēgein (to lead), referring to its reputed ability to increase milk production.
