RAIU

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr eɪ aɪ juː / (AmE), / ɑː eɪ aɪ juː / (BrE)
A medical test that measures the amount of radioactive iodine absorbed by the thyroid gland to evaluate thyroid function.
thyroid function iodine uptake test
medical

Examples

The RAIU is often ordered when doctors suspect abnormal thyroid function.
After receiving the RAIU, the patient was given instructions to avoid close contact with pregnant women for a few days.
An elevated RAIU may indicate hyperthyroidism or certain types of thyroiditis.

Etymology

Initialism of radioactive iodine uptake.
