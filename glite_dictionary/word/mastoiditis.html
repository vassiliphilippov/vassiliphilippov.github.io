mastoiditis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.tɔɪˈdaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌmas.tɔɪˈdʌɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the mastoid bone, usually caused by infection.
inflammation of the mastoid bone
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with mastoiditis following a severe ear infection.
Antibiotics are often required to treat mastoiditis and prevent complications.
Early symptoms of mastoiditis include pain and swelling behind the ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otitis media - inflammation of the middle ear

Etymology

From 'mastoid' (from Greek 'mastos' meaning breast, for its shape) + '-itis' (Greek for inflammation).
