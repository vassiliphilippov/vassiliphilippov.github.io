Geothlypis

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːˈɒθlɪpɪs / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒθlɪpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small New World warblers known as yellowthroats, typically found in North and Central America.
genus of yellowthroat warblers

Examples

Many species within the genus Geothlypis are recognized for their distinctive yellow throat patches.
The common yellowthroat, a member of the Geothlypis genus, is widespread across North America.
Researchers studied migration patterns of several Geothlypis species in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowthroat - any of several small warblers of the genus Geothlypis

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'thlypis' (a kind of small bird).

2. NOUN

/ dʒiːˈɑːθlɪpɪs / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒθlɪpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small New World warblers known as yellowthroats, found throughout the Americas.
genus of New World yellowthroat warblers

Examples

Several species within the Geothlypis genus are known for their distinctive yellow throats.
The common yellowthroat, a member of the Geothlypis genus, is widespread across North America.
Ornithologists have studied the migratory patterns of Geothlypis species for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowthroat - a small songbird of the genus Geothlypis with a bright yellow throat

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'geō-' (earth) and 'thlypis' (a small bird).
