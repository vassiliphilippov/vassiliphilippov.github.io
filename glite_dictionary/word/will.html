will

1. NOUN

/ wɪl / (AmE), / wɪl / (BrE)
the power of making conscious choices or decisions; the faculty to choose or decide deliberately
the power to choose or decide

Examples

She showed remarkable will in overcoming her illness.
His strong will helped him to reach his goals.
With enough will, people can change their circumstances.

Synset Examples

the exercise of their volition we construe as revolt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volition - the act of making a conscious choice or decision
  • Antonym: instinct - an innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in animals in response to certain stimuli

Etymology

From Old English 'willan' (to wish, desire, will), related to German 'Wille'.

Synset POS: n
