Rorschach

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔrˌʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈrɔːʃæk / (BrE)
a psychological test in which subjects' perceptions of a series of symmetrical inkblots are analyzed to assess personality and emotional functioning
psychological test using inkblots

Examples

The psychologist administered a Rorschach to better understand the patient's thought patterns.
Many people are familiar with the Rorschach, even if they've never taken the test themselves.
Debate continues over the scientific validity of the Rorschach in modern clinical practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rorschach test - psychological test using inkblots to assess personality

Etymology

Named after Swiss psychiatrist Hermann Rorschach (1884–1922), who developed the test.
