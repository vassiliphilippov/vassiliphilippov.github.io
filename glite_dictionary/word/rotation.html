rotation

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a single complete turn around an axis or center, especially by a physical object or a celestial body
a single complete turn

Examples

The spinning top completed one rotation before falling over.
One full rotation of the Earth on its axis takes about 24 hours.
The athlete measured how many rotations the discus made in the air.

Synset Examples

the plane made three rotations before it crashed
the revolution of the earth about the sun takes one year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolution - a single complete orbit or turn, especially in astronomy

Etymology

From Latin rotatio (a turning), from rotare (to rotate).

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of turning around a central point or axis
the act of turning around an axis

Examples

The rotation of the Earth causes day and night.
The rotation of the wheels was smooth and continuous during the test.
The gymnast performed a perfect rotation during her routine.

Synset Examples

the rotation of the dancer kept time with the music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolution - a complete turn around an axis or center
  • Antonym: stillness - the state of not moving or being motionless

Etymology

From Latin 'rotatio' (a turning about), from 'rotare' (to turn, revolve).

3. NOUN

/ roʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A transformation that turns a figure or coordinate system around a fixed point, usually the origin, by a certain angle.
mathematical transformation involving turning around a point

Examples

The rotation of the coordinate axes changes the values of the variables but preserves distances.
This equation describes a rotation of 90 degrees about the origin in the plane.
In three dimensions, a rotation can occur about any axis passing through the origin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflection - a transformation flipping a figure over a line
  • Antonym: translation - a transformation moving a figure without rotating it
  • Synonym: rotation - the general act of turning around a center point

Etymology

From Latin rotatio (a turning), from rotare (to turn), from rota (wheel).

4. NOUN

/ roʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a planned recurring sequence or schedule, especially of crops, personnel, or duties, so that each appears or occurs regularly in turn
planned recurring sequence (of crops, personnel, etc.)

Examples

The company implements a staff rotation to ensure fairness in weekend shifts.
Farmers practice crop rotation to preserve soil nutrients and prevent disease.
The team's starting pitcher rotation changed after the star player's injury.

Synset Examples

crop rotation makes a balanced demand on the fertility of the soil
the manager had only four starting pitchers in his rotation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - a plan of activities or events and when they will happen

Etymology

From Latin 'rotatio' (a turning), from 'rotare' (to turn).
