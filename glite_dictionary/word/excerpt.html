excerpt

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌsɝpt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɜːpt / (BrE)
a short extract from a larger written work, piece of music, or film
a selected passage or extract

Examples

The essay includes a brief excerpt from the author's first novel.
She read aloud an excerpt from a famous speech to inspire the audience.
The article began with an excerpt from an unpublished letter.

Synset Examples

he presented excerpts from William James' philosophical writings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - a passage taken from a text, film, or piece of music

Etymology

From Latin 'excerptum', past participle of 'excerpere' (to pick out, select).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06412153-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈsɝpt / (AmE), / ˈɛksɜːpt / (BrE)
to take a short extract from a text, film, or piece of music and use it elsewhere, especially for citation or reference
take or cite part from a work

Examples

The teacher excerpted several passages from the novel to discuss in class.
The article excerpts key sections from the original report for analysis.
She excerpted a few lines from the poem for her essay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quote - to repeat or copy a group of words from a text, typically with an indication of the original author

Etymology

From Latin 'excerptum', past participle of 'excerpere' (to pick out, select).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00678940-v
Synset POS: v
