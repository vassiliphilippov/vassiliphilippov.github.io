Pediculus humanus

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈdɪkjələs hjuˈmeɪnəs / (AmE), / pɛˈdɪkjʊləs hjuːˈmɑːnəs / (BrE)
A species of parasitic louse that infests humans, commonly known as the head louse or body louse.
species of human-infesting louse
medical

Examples

The doctor identified the parasite as Pediculus humanus during the examination.
Infestations of Pediculus humanus are common in crowded or unsanitary living conditions.
Treatment for Pediculus humanus typically involves topical insecticides and improved hygiene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head louse - parasitic louse living on the human scalp
  • Synonym: body louse - parasitic louse living on human body and clothes

Etymology

From Latin: pediculus (little louse) + humanus (human).
