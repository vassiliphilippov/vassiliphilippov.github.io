fibrin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbrɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪbrɪn / (BrE)
a white, insoluble fibrous protein formed from fibrinogen during blood clotting, which forms a network that traps blood cells to form a clot
protein involved in blood clotting
medical

Examples

When a blood vessel is injured, fibrin forms a mesh that helps stop bleeding.
Doctors studied the role of fibrin in the healing of surgical wounds.
Deficiencies in fibrin production can lead to problems with blood clotting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrinogen - the soluble protein that is converted into fibrin during blood clotting

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (fiber), through French 'fibrine'.
