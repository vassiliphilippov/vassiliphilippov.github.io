adult

/ əˈdʌlt, ˈædʌlt / (AmE), / əˈdʌlt, ˈædʌlt / (BrE)
(of entertainment or content) intended for adults, especially because it contains sexually explicit material.

Examples

Websites with adult content are blocked on this computer.
She accidentally rented an adult video, not realizing its explicit nature.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdʌlt, ˈædʌlt / (AmE), / ˈædʌlt, əˈdʌlt / (BrE)
a person who is fully grown or developed, typically considered to be beyond the age of childhood or adolescence
fully grown person

Examples

When you become an adult, you have many responsibilities that children do not.
Only adults are allowed to attend the conference after 8 p.m.
As an adult, she decided to move to a new city and start fresh.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: child - a young human below the age of puberty
  • Antonym: minor - a person under the age of legal adulthood
  • Synonym: grown-up - another term for an adult

Etymology

From Latin 'adultus', past participle of 'adolescere' (to grow up).

3. NOUN

/ əˈdʌlt, ˈæˌdʌlt / (AmE), / ˈædʌlt, əˈdʌlt / (BrE)
A fully grown or mature animal, as opposed to a juvenile.
fully grown animal

Examples

The adult elephant protected the calves from predators.
Frogs begin life as tadpoles and develop into adult amphibians.
An adult butterfly emerges from the chrysalis after metamorphosis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: juvenile - an animal that is not yet fully grown
  • Degree Lower: larva - immature form of an animal, especially before metamorphosis

Etymology

From Latin adultus, past participle of adolescere (to grow up).

4. ADJ

/ əˈdʌlt, ˈæd.ʌlt / (AmE), / ˈæd.ʌlt, əˈdʌlt / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) fully grown or developed; mature.
fully grown or developed (animal or person)

Examples

The adult elephant can weigh thousands of kilograms.
Many birds are not easy to identify until they reach adult plumage.
Only adult tigers have a fully developed mane.

Synset Examples

an adult animal
a grown woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: juvenile - not yet fully grown or developed; young
  • Synonym: mature - fully developed physically or mentally; grown up

Etymology

From Latin 'adultus' (grown up, mature), past participle of 'adolescere' (to grow up).
