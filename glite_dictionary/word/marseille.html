marseille

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈseɪl / (AmE), / mɑːˈseɪl / (BrE)
A strong cotton fabric with a raised pattern, often used for bedspreads.
type of patterned cotton fabric

Examples

The antique bedspread was made from marseille, showcasing an intricate raised design.
She chose marseille fabric for her new set of decorative pillows.
Traditional marseille is prized for its durability and textured appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matelassé - a type of fabric with a raised, quilted pattern

Etymology

Named after Marseille, a city in France known for textile production.
