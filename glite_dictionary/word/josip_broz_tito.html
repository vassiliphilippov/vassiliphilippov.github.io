Josip Broz Tito

1. PROPN

/ ˈjoʊsɪp ˈbroʊz ˈtiːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈjɒsɪp ˈbrɒz ˈtiːtəʊ / (BrE)
(1892–1980) Yugoslav statesman and military leader who led the resistance against German occupation during World War II and later established and ruled a communist state in Yugoslavia.
Yugoslav leader and statesman (1892–1980)

Examples

Josip Broz Tito was a central figure in the politics of Yugoslavia during the mid-20th century.
Many Yugoslavs credited Josip Broz Tito with preserving peace among the nation's ethnic groups.
World leaders often visited Josip Broz Tito to discuss non-alignment during the Cold War.
