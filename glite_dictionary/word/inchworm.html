inchworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntʃˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɪntʃˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A small, greenish caterpillar of various moths, especially of the family Geometridae, that moves by looping its body and appears to measure out an inch at a time.
type of caterpillar that moves in a looping motion

Examples

The inchworm arched its body as it slowly made its way across the leaf.
Children watched in fascination as a green inchworm looped along a branch.
Gardeners often find inchworms feeding on the leaves of their plants in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caterpillar - the larval stage of butterflies and moths
  • Synonym: geometer moth - a moth belonging to the family Geometridae, whose larvae are inchworms

Etymology

Formed from "inch" and "worm", referring to its way of locomotion and shape.
