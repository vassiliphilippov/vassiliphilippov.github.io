/ ˈreɪdiəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl / (BrE)

Examples

The artist used radial lines in the design, creating a sense of movement.
Radial heat is generated when energy spreads outward in all directions from a hot object.

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl / (BrE)
A pneumatic tire constructed with the plies arranged at 90 degrees to the direction of travel, providing improved performance and durability.
type of tire with radial-ply casing

Examples

Many drivers prefer radials for their superior traction and fuel efficiency.
The shop specialized in high-performance radials for sports cars and motorcycles.
After switching to radials, the ride felt much smoother and more stable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bias-ply - a type of tire with crisscrossed textile cords

Etymology

From Latin radius (spoke of a wheel, ray), reflecting the arrangement of the tire's plies.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
