threonine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθriːəˌniːn / (AmE), / ˈθriːəniːn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid that is a constituent of most proteins and is necessary for human nutrition
an essential amino acid found in proteins
medical

Examples

Dietary sources rich in threonine include eggs, fish, and dairy products.
The structure of threonine allows it to play a unique role in protein stability.
People with threonine deficiency may experience problems with their immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - an organic compound containing both an amino and a carboxyl group, forming proteins

Etymology

From threo-, an isomeric prefix, and -ine, from chemical nomenclature.
