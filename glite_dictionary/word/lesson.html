lesson

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
a piece of knowledge or moral principle that can be learned from a story or an event
meaning or moral of a story/event

Examples

The lesson of the tale is that honesty is always the best policy.
Every mistake offers a new lesson to be learned for the future.
The film's tragic ending carries an important lesson about forgiveness.

Synset Examples

the moral of the story is to love thy neighbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moral - the lesson or principle of a story

Etymology

From Old French 'leçon', from Latin 'lectio' (a reading, lesson).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
a task given to a student for individual study, especially as part of schoolwork
task assigned for study

Examples

She always completes her lesson before dinner each night.
After finishing his lesson, he went outside to play.

4. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsən / (AmE), / ˈlɛsən / (BrE)
a punishment given to someone in order to serve as a warning to others; an action taken to teach a deterrent example
punishment as a warning to others

Examples

The harsh sentence was meant to serve as a lesson to others considering similar crimes.
They made an example of the student to teach the whole class a lesson.
The organization was fined heavily as a lesson to other companies that might break the law.

Synset Examples

they decided to make an example of him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: example - a person or thing used to show what is to be avoided, especially by punishment

Etymology

From Latin lectio (a reading), from legere (to read), later extended in sense to learning and experience.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06685497-n
Synset POS: n
