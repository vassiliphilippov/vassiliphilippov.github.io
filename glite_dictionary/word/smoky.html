smoky

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmoʊki / (AmE), / ˈsməʊki / (BrE)
Having a flavor or taste suggestive of smoke.
tasting of smoke

Examples

The cheese had a smoky flavor that lingered on the palate.
She prefers smoky barbecue sauces over the regular kind.
The restaurant is famous for its smoky ribs and grilled meats.

Synset Examples

smoky sausages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoked - preserved or flavored with smoke

2. ADJ

/ ˈsmoʊki / (AmE), / ˈsməʊki / (BrE)
filled with, producing, or resembling smoke; marked by the presence of smoke.
full of or producing smoke

Examples

The cabin had a smoky fireplace that gave the air a distinct scent.
After the fire, a smoky haze lingered over the city for days.
We tried to avoid sitting near the smoky end of the barbecue pit.

Synset Examples

smoky rafters
smoky chimneys
a smoky fireplace
a smoky corridor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smokeless - free from smoke; not emitting smoke
  • Degree Lower: hazy - filled with haze; somewhat similar as less intense than smoky
