phosphoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑːsfoʊˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a protein molecule that contains one or more chemically bound phosphate groups, usually as a result of biological processes such as phosphorylation
protein with bound phosphate groups
medical

Examples

The scientist analyzed the function of a phosphoprotein involved in cell signaling.
Changes in phosphoprotein levels can indicate alterations in cellular activity.
Detection of a specific phosphoprotein is crucial for studying certain diseases.

Etymology

From phospho- (relating to phosphate) + protein.
