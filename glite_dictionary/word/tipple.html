tipple

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪpəl / (BrE)
a small amount of alcoholic drink, especially one drawn from a keg or cask
a serving of alcoholic drink

Examples

He enjoyed a tipple in the evening after work.
They each had a tipple before heading home from the pub.
Would you care for a tipple with your meal?

Synset Examples

they served beer on draft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - a liquid that can be drunk, especially alcoholic liquor
  • Synonym: draft - beer or other alcoholic drink served from a barrel or tank rather than a bottle or can

Etymology

Probably from English dialect 'tip' (to touch, taste) + diminutive suffix '-le'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07899955-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪpəl / (BrE)
to drink alcohol, especially in small quantities, regularly or habitually
drink alcohol regularly but moderately

Examples

He likes to tipple a little wine with dinner every evening.
They tippled their way through the tasting menu at the vineyard.
She had been tippling for years but was never truly drunk.

Synset Examples

We tippled the cognac

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - to consume a liquid, especially alcohol

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'tip' (to touch lightly, taste) + diminutive '-le'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01174411-v
Synset POS: v
