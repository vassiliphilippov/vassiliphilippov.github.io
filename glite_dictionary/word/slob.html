slob

1. NOUN

/ slɑb / (AmE), / slɒb / (BrE)
a person who is messy, lazy, or behaves in a coarse or obnoxious way
messy or obnoxious person
informal

Examples

He can be such a slob when it comes to keeping his room tidy.
After the party, the house looked like a group of slobs had moved in.
She refused to date him because he was a total slob about his appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloven - a person who is habitually messy or careless
  • Antonym: neat freak - a person excessively concerned with cleanliness and order

Etymology

Perhaps from Irish Gaelic slab (a big, fat person).

2. NOUN

/ slɑb / (AmE), / slɒb / (BrE)
a person who is very untidy and careless about their appearance or habits
very untidy person

Examples

He's such a slob that his room is always a mess and he never puts things away.
Don't be a slob; clean up after yourself once in a while.
Even though she works hard, her desk makes her look like a total slob.

Synset Examples

He always leaves his clothes everywhere; he's such a slob.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloven - a person who is habitually messy or careless
  • Antonym: neat freak - a person who is excessively tidy and orderly

Etymology

Probably of Irish origin, from Irish slab (mud, slimy substance).
