aphrodisiac

1. ADJ

/ ˌæf.rəˈdiː.zi.æk / (AmE), / ˌæf.rəˈdɪz.i.æk / (BrE)
(of a substance or action) arousing or increasing sexual desire.
arousing sexual desire

Examples

Some people believe oysters have aphrodisiac qualities.
The candle was scented with aphrodisiac oils to set a romantic mood.
Certain herbs are famous for their aphrodisiac effects in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arousing - causing excitement or sexual desire
  • Antonym: anaphrodisiac - reducing or suppressing sexual desire

Etymology

From Greek Aphrodisiakos, relating to Aphrodite, the goddess of love.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfɹəˈdiziˌæk / (AmE), / ˌæfɹəˈdɪziˌæk / (BrE)
A substance, typically a drug or food, that increases sexual desire, arousal, or pleasure.
substance stimulating sexual desire

Examples

Oysters are sometimes considered a natural aphrodisiac by many cultures.
He bought a special aphrodisiac hoping to spice up their romantic evening.
Some believe that certain herbs can act as a mild aphrodisiac.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaphrodisiac - a substance that suppresses sexual desire

Etymology

From Greek Aphrodisiakos, relating to Aphrodite, the goddess of love.
