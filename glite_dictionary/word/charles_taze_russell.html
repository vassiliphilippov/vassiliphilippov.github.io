Charles Taze Russell

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz teɪz ˈrʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz teɪz ˈrʌsəl / (BrE)
(1852–1916) a United States religious leader who founded the group now known as Jehovah's Witnesses.
founder of Jehovah's Witnesses

Examples

Charles Taze Russell played a key role in the early development of what became Jehovah's Witnesses.
Many religious scholars have studied the leadership style of Charles Taze Russell.
The teachings of Charles Taze Russell continue to influence the beliefs of Jehovah's Witnesses today.
