dropout

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who withdraws or disengages from a social group, activity, or environment, especially before completion or fulfillment
person who withdraws from a group or environment

Examples

After leaving college in his second year, he was labeled a dropout by his peers.
The community center hosts events for youth at risk of becoming dropouts.
She became a dropout from the corporate world, seeking a simpler life in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quitter - a person who gives up easily or does not finish a task

Etymology

From 'drop' + 'out', first used in American English in the 1930s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who leaves school, college, or university before finishing their course of study
person who leaves school before graduating

Examples

Many teenagers become dropouts due to difficult family circumstances.
As a high school dropout, he later returned to finish his education.
The program helps dropouts reclaim their academic goals and build careers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graduate - a person who has successfully completed a course of study

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'drop out', meaning to withdraw, especially from school or a course.
