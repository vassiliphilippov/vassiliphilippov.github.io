thermostat

1. VERB

/ ˈθɜr.moʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məʊ.stæt / (BrE)
to control or regulate the temperature of a space or device by means of a thermostat.
regulate temperature using a thermostat

Examples

The laboratory is carefully thermostatted to ensure accurate results.
Many modern buildings thermostat their climate control systems automatically.
He forgot to thermostat the greenhouse before leaving for vacation.

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' (warm) and 'statos' (standing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02517090-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːrmoʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌstæt / (BrE)
a device that automatically regulates temperature by turning heating or cooling systems on or off to maintain a set temperature
device for regulating temperature

Examples

The thermostat quickly turned off the furnace when the room reached the set temperature.
She adjusted the thermostat to make the house warmer during the winter months.
Modern smart thermostats can be controlled using your phone from anywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulator - a device used to control or maintain a variable, such as pressure or temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'thermē' (heat) + 'statēs' (one that causes to stand or make firm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04430010-n
Synset POS: n
