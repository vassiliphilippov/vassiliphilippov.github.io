tasting

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking a small amount of food or drink into the mouth to test its quality or flavor
the act of sampling food or drink

Examples

We attended a tasting event at the local winery.
The chef offered a tasting of his new dessert menu.
After the tasting, she decided which cheese she liked best.

Synset Examples

cooking was fine but it was the savoring that he enjoyed most

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sampling - the act of trying a small amount to evaluate

Etymology

From the verb 'taste', from Old French 'taster', from Latin 'taxare' (to touch, estimate, judge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
an event or instance at which small amounts of food or drink, especially wine, are sampled for evaluation or enjoyment
event for sampling food or drink

Examples

We attended a wine tasting at the local vineyard last weekend.
The chef invited guests to a cheese tasting before the dinner.
She offered a tasting of her homemade desserts at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sampling - the act of trying a small amount of something for evaluation

Etymology

From the verb 'taste' + noun-forming suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05830972-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈteɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
an event or occasion when people sample and compare the flavors of different foods or drinks, especially for evaluation or enjoyment
event for sampling flavors

Examples

We attended a cheese tasting at the local market last weekend.
The restaurant offers a wine tasting every Friday evening.
He discovered his favorite tea at a tasting organized by the club.

Synset Examples

a wine tasting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sampling - the process of trying a small amount of something
  • Antonym: smelling - the act of sensing odors with the nose

Etymology

From Middle English 'tasten' (to taste) + -ing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00884380-n
Synset POS: n
