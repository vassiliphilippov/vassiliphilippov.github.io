declarer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛɹɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈkleərə / (BrE)
a person who makes a statement or announces something as true, especially formally or publicly.
person who makes a formal statement

Examples

The declarer stood before the audience and announced the results with confidence.
As the official declarer of the findings, she held great responsibility.
In the debate, he took on the role of declarer for his team's position.

Synset Examples

a bold asserter
a declarer of his intentions
affirmers of traditional doctrine
an asseverator of strong convictions
an avower of his own great intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asserter - person who states something confidently
  • Antonym: denier - person who denies something

Etymology

From Latin 'declarare' (to make clear), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛrər / (AmE), / dɪˈkleərə / (BrE)
(in contract bridge) the player who wins the bidding and declares the contract, playing both their own and partner's cards.
player in bridge who declares and plays contract

Examples

As the declarer, she needed to carefully plan every move to fulfill the contract.
The declarer chose no trumps, surprising everyone at the table.
After the bidding phase, he became the declarer and took charge of the play.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defender - a player in bridge who is not the declarer and tries to prevent declarer's contract from succeeding

Etymology

From 'declare' + '-er', indicating the person performing the action.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
