Dianthus latifolius

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs lætəˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs latɪˈfəʊliʊs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the carnation family, known for its much-branched stems and clustered pink flowers; closely related to sweet William.
species of flowering plant with clustered pink flowers

Examples

Gardeners often plant Dianthus latifolius for its attractive clusters of pink blossoms.
The Dianthus latifolius thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
Botanists have noted that Dianthus latifolius is closely related to sweet William.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet William - a related species of Dianthus with similar pink or red flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus name "Dianthus" (from Greek dios "divine" + anthos "flower") and species epithet "latifolius" meaning "broad-leaved."
