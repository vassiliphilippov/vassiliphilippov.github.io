crenelation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɛnəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɛnɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of constructing ramparts or walls with regularly spaced gaps (crenels) for firing through; the arrangement of battlements with open spaces for defense.
the construction of battlements with gaps

Examples

The castle's crenelation allowed defenders to observe and fire upon attackers safely.
During restoration, architects studied ancient techniques of crenelation to preserve the fortress's authenticity.
The ruined tower still shows traces of its original crenelation along the top walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlement - a parapet at the top of a wall, usually with gaps for shooting through

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *crenelatio*, from Old French *creneler* (to furnish with battlements).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɛnəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɛnɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A defensive wall at the top of a castle or fort, typically featuring a regular pattern of open spaces (crenels) for shooting or observation.
castle wall with gaps for defense

Examples

The ancient fortress was topped with impressive crenelations that overlooked the valley below.
Tourists admired the detailed crenelations that gave the castle its iconic silhouette.
During the siege, archers took position behind the crenelations to defend the stronghold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlement - parapet with alternating high and low sections on a castle

Etymology

From Old French 'creneler' (to furnish with battlements), from 'crenel' (notch).
