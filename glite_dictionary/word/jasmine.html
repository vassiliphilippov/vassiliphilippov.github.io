jasmine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæz.mɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒæz.mɪn / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Jasminum, with fragrant white or yellow flowers, chiefly native to warm regions of Eurasia and Africa
fragrant flowering plant

Examples

The jasmine in her garden filled the air with a sweet perfume each evening.
He picked a small bouquet of jasmine blossoms to give to his mother.
The delicate scent of jasmine drifted through the open window on the summer breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardenia - a fragrant flowering plant often confused with jasmine
  • Antonym: rose - a different type of popular flowering plant

Etymology

From Persian 'yasmin' via Arabic 'yāsamīn', meaning 'gift from God'.
