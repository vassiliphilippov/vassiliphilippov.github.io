Graf Zeppelin

1. NOUN

/ ɡræf ˈzɛpəˌlɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf ˈzɛpəlɪn / (BrE)
A large rigid German airship, notable for its passenger flights and long-distance journeys in the late 1920s and 1930s.
famous large German airship (1920s–30s)

Examples

The Graf Zeppelin completed a historic round-the-world flight in 1929.
Many spectators gathered to watch the Graf Zeppelin land after its transatlantic journey.
The Graf Zeppelin was considered a marvel of engineering in its time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airship - a powered, steerable aircraft that is lighter than air
  • Synonym: dirigible - a lighter-than-air craft that can be steered and propelled

Etymology

From German, named after Count (Graf) Ferdinand von Zeppelin, the airship's designer.
