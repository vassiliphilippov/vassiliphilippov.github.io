lethality

1. NOUN

/ liˈθæləti / (AmE), / lɪˈθæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being deadly or capable of causing death
the state of being deadly

Examples

The lethality of the new virus variant has alarmed health officials worldwide.
Some chemicals are strictly regulated due to their extreme lethality.
The military studied the lethality of various weapons in combat scenarios.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadliness - the quality of causing death; synonym of lethality
  • Antonym: harmlessness - the quality of not causing harm or death; antonym of lethality

Etymology

From Latin 'letalis' (deadly) + English suffix '-ity'.
