2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈdɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈdɪniəm / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater dinoflagellates belonging to the family Peridiniidae, known for their distinctive cellulose plates and important ecological roles in aquatic environments.
genus of freshwater dinoflagellates

Examples

Peridinium species are commonly found in nutrient-rich freshwater lakes around the world.
The scientist studied the unique cellulose armor of Peridinium under the microscope.
A bloom of Peridinium can significantly alter the oxygen levels in a pond.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'dinos' (whirling), referencing the organism's rotation in movement.
