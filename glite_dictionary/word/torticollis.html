torticollis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɔrtɪˈkɑlɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɔːtɪˈkɒlɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which the head is tilted to one side due to muscle contraction on that side of the neck.
head tilting due to neck muscle contraction
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with torticollis after observing the abnormal tilt of his head.
Physical therapy was recommended to help manage her torticollis symptoms.
Infants can sometimes develop torticollis if they always sleep in the same position.

Etymology

From Latin 'tortus' (twisted) + 'collum' (neck).
