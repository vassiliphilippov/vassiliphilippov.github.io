omissive

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / əˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
characterized by or involving omission; tending to omit or leave things out
characterized by omission

Examples

The report was criticized for its omissive treatment of key data.
Certain laws address both active and omissive types of criminal behavior.
His analysis was thorough but somewhat omissive regarding environmental impact.

Synset Examples

omissive crimes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omissible - able to be omitted; capable of being left out

Etymology

From Latin omissivus (related to omission), from omittere (to let go, neglect).
