gelechiid

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈliːkiɪd / (AmE), / dʒɪˈliːkɪɪd / (BrE)
A small, slender-winged moth of the family Gelechiidae, many of whose larvae are agricultural pests.
a moth of the Gelechiidae family

Examples

The cotton fields suffered heavy damage from the larvae of the gelechiid.
Scientists are studying the behavior of gelechiids to improve pest control methods.
A gelechiid was identified as the source of the infestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to the butterfly; most moths are nocturnal

Etymology

From New Latin Gelechiidae, the scientific name of the moth family.
