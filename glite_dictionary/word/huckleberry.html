huckleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhʌkəlbəri / (BrE)
a small dark-purple berry similar to the blueberry, belonging to certain plants of the genus Gaylussacia, commonly found in North America
a small dark-purple berry, similar to a blueberry

Examples

We picked huckleberries from the bushes along the trail.
She made a delicious pie filled with fresh huckleberries.
The huckleberry harvest is an eagerly anticipated event in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - a small blue edible berry from plants of the genus Vaccinium

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, round, blue-black berry similar to a blueberry or bilberry, native to North America, especially the eastern United States.
small blue-black berry, like a blueberry

Examples

She picked a handful of ripe huckleberries on her morning hike.
The pie was filled with fresh huckleberry jam.
Bears are known to forage for huckleberry bushes in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - A small, sweet blue-black berry
  • Synonym: bilberry - A small, dark blue edible berry from northern Europe and western Asia

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect *hurtleberry* or alteration of *whortleberry*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhʌk.əlˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A North American shrub of the genus Gaylussacia that bears small, dark, edible berries similar to blueberries.
North American berry-bearing shrub

Examples

We picked a bucket of huckleberries while hiking through the forest.
The pie was made with fresh huckleberries gathered from the mountain trails.
Huckleberry bushes thrive best in acidic soil and cooler climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - Edible berry from shrubs of the genus Vaccinium, similar to huckleberries

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect *hurtleberry* (whortleberry), influenced by 'huckle'.
