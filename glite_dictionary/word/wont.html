wont

1. NOUN

/ wɑnt / (AmE), / wɒnt / (BrE)
A usual or habitual way of doing something; an established custom or practice.
customary habit or practice

Examples

She rose at dawn, as was her wont during her years on the farm.
He greeted his neighbors with a cheerful wave, following his daily wont.
It is not her wont to be late for important meetings.

Synset Examples

it was their habit to dine at 7 every evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habit - a regular practice or custom

Etymology

From Old English gewunod ('accustomed, wont'), from Proto-Germanic *wunōną ('to be content, to dwell').

2. NOUN

/ wɑnt / (AmE), / wɒnt / (BrE)
a person's customary behavior or habitual practice
customary habit or practice
archaic

Examples

It was wont for him to take a long walk every evening.
She rose early, as was her wont, and prepared breakfast for the family.
Despite the changes, he continued with his old wont of reading the newspaper at dawn.

Synset Examples

It was his wont to help others in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habit - a regular tendency or practice
  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving
  • Degree Lower: quirk - a peculiar behavioral habit

Etymology

From Middle English 'wont' (accustomed), from Old English 'gewunod' (accustomed, used to).
