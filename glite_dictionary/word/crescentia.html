Crescentia

1. NOUN

/ krəˈsɛn(t)ʃiə / (AmE), / krɛˈsɛn.tsi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Bignoniaceae, characterized by short trunks, crooked limbs, and drooping branches.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The fruit of Crescentia trees is used to make traditional utensils in some cultures.
Botanists have documented several species within the Crescentia genus.
Crescentia trees are notable for their distinctive gourd-like fruits and spreading branches.

Etymology

From Latin 'crescentia' (growth, increase), used as a genus name.

2. NOUN

/ krɛˈsɛnʃiə / (AmE), / krɛˈsɛnʃiə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees in the family Bignoniaceae, characterized by a short trunk, crooked limbs, and drooping branches, best known for the calabash tree.
genus of tropical American trees (Bignoniaceae)

Examples

The genus Crescentia is native to tropical regions of the Americas.
One of the best-known species in Crescentia is the calabash tree, valued for its fruit.
Botanists classify several distinctive trees under the genus Crescentia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calabash tree - a tree of the genus Crescentia known for its hard-shelled fruit

Etymology

Named after Pietro Crescentio (Latinized as Crescentius), an Italian botanist.
