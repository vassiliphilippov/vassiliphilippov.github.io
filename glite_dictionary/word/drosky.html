drosky

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːski / (AmE), / ˈdrɒski / (BrE)
an open, four-wheeled horse-drawn carriage, formerly used in Poland and Russia
a type of Polish or Russian carriage

Examples

Tourists took a ride in a brightly painted drosky through the historic streets of the city.
In nineteenth-century Russia, the drosky was a common means of urban transport.
The old photograph showed a nobleman sitting comfortably in his drosky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a vehicle with wheels, drawn by horses, for carrying people

Etymology

From Russian 'drozhki', the plural of 'drozhok' (a small cart).
