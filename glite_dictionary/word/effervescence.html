effervescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛfərˈvɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɛfəˈvɛsəns / (BrE)
the process of bubbling or fizzing as gas escapes from a liquid, often resulting in visible bubbles or foam
the process of bubbling as gas escapes

Examples

The effervescence in carbonated drinks is caused by dissolved carbon dioxide escaping as bubbles.
Chemists observed the effervescence when the acid reacted with the marble chips.
The gentle effervescence of champagne makes it a popular choice for celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fizz - bubbles of gas in a liquid that make it seem lively or tingly

Etymology

From Latin effervescere, meaning 'to boil up' or 'to bubble up'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛfərˈvɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɛfəˈvɛsəns / (BrE)
the process or state of giving off bubbles; the rapid escape of gas from a liquid, often seen as fizzing or bubbling
the process of bubbling or fizzing

Examples

The effervescence of sparkling water makes it a refreshing drink on a hot day.
As the chemical reacted with the acid, effervescence was visible in the form of tiny bubbles.
The freshly poured champagne showed a lively effervescence in the glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fizz - bubbling sound or effect in a liquid
  • Antonym: flatness - the absence of bubbles or fizz, especially in a beverage

Etymology

From Latin 'effervescere' (to boil up), from 'ex-' (out) and 'fervescere' (to begin to boil).
