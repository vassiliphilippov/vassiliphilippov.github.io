twinjet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪnˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈtwɪnˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
A jet aircraft powered by two jet engines.
jet plane with two engines

Examples

The new airline purchased a twinjet for short- to medium-haul routes.
Engineers praised the fuel efficiency of the latest twinjet models.
After takeoff, the twinjet climbed rapidly into the cloudy sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trijet - a jet aircraft with three engines
  • Degree Higher: quadjet - a jet aircraft with four engines
  • Degree Lower: singlejet - a jet aircraft with one engine

Etymology

Formed from "twin" (two) and "jet" (jet engine).
