Opisthocomus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌpɪsθəˈkoʊməs / (AmE), / əʊˌpɪsθəˈkəʊməs / (BrE)
The genus of the hoatzin, a tropical bird found in South America and the only genus in the family Opisthocomidae.
genus of the hoatzin bird

Examples

The Opisthocomus is distinguished by its unique digestive system, which ferments vegetation in its crop.
Evolutionary biologists are fascinated by the placement of Opisthocomus within the bird family tree.
The chick of the Opisthocomus is famous for its clawed wings, allowing it to climb branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoatzin - a South American bird with blue facial skin and a spiky crest

Etymology

From Greek 'opisthen' (behind) and 'koma' (hair), referring to the bird's crest.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊpɪsθəˈkoʊməs / (AmE), / ˌɒpɪsθəˈkəʊməs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Opisthocomidae, represented by the hoatzin, a South American bird known for its distinctive digestive and nesting habits.
genus of birds including the hoatzin

Examples

The hoatzin is the only surviving member of the genus Opisthocomus.
Ornithologists study Opisthocomus to understand the evolution of digestive systems in birds.
Fossil evidence suggests that Opisthocomus has ancient origins in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoatzin - a South American bird known for its unique digestive system

Etymology

From Greek 'opistho-' (behind) + 'komē' (hair), referring to the bird's crest.
