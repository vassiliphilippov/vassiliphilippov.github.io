Sechuana

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtʃwɑːnə / (AmE), / sɛˈtʃwɑːnə / (BrE)
the Bantu language of the Tswana people, spoken chiefly in Botswana and South Africa; also called Setswana.
language of the Tswana people

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Sechuana.
The novel was originally written in Sechuana before being translated.
Students are encouraged to learn Sechuana to connect with local culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Setswana - the Bantu language of the Tswana people

Etymology

From the name of the Tswana people, with a prefix se- denoting language in Bantu linguistic tradition.
