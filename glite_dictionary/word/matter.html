matter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmætə / (BrE)
written content, especially in books, magazines, or other printed materials
written content in publications

Examples

She always carried some matter with her to read on the subway.
The magazine was filled with interesting reading matter for all ages.
He found the matter in the textbook too technical to enjoy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: material - written content, text in publications

3. NOUN

/ ˈmædər / (AmE), / ˈmætə / (BrE)
something of consequence or importance (often used with a negative, as in 'no matter')
thing of consequence or importance

Examples

It was no matter to him whether they stayed or left.
In the end, the mistake was of little matter.
'No matter who comes,' she said, 'I'll be ready.'

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: importance - the state of being important or significant
  • Antonym: triviality - something with little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin materia (substance, subject), later extended to mean 'subject of discourse, affair'.
