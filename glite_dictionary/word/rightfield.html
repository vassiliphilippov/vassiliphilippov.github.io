rightfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌfiːld / (BrE)
the area of the outfield in baseball or softball that is to the right when facing from home plate, or the fielding position in this area
baseball outfield area on the right

Examples

He made an incredible catch in rightfield during the final inning.
The ball sailed over the rightfield fence for a home run.
She was assigned to play rightfield in today's game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leftfield - the outfield area to the left from home plate in baseball
  • Synonym: centerfield - the outfield area in the center from home plate in baseball

Etymology

Formed from 'right' + 'field,' indicating the right side of the playing field.
