hajji

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈhædʒi / (BrE)
A general term used by foreign, especially American, soldiers to refer to an Iraqi or, more broadly, to Arabs or Muslims, often regarded as offensive.
offensive term for Iraqis or Arabs by soldiers
offensive

Examples

Some soldiers referred to the local population simply as hajji during deployments in Iraq.
He was criticized for using the word hajji to describe civilians in the region.
The documentary explored how terms like hajji contribute to dehumanization in conflict zones.

Synset Examples

to American soldiers, the hajji are the alien people from whom the enemy emerges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haji - a Muslim who has made the Hajj to Mecca (original sense)

Etymology

From Arabic ḥājjī (one who has made the Hajj); in this sense, adopted and repurposed by English-speaking soldiers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈhædʒi / (BrE)
A Muslim who has completed the pilgrimage to Mecca; used as a term of respect.
Muslim who made pilgrimage to Mecca

Examples

He was honored as a hajji after returning from Mecca.
Many people in the community addressed her as hajji in recognition of her pilgrimage.
The title hajji is highly respected in Islamic cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilgrim - a person who makes a journey to a holy place for religious reasons

Etymology

From Arabic ḥājjī, meaning 'one who has made the pilgrimage (ḥajj)'.
