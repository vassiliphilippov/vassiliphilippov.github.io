azote

1. NOUN

/ ˈæzoʊt / (AmE), / ˈazəʊt / (BrE)
an obsolete name for the chemical element nitrogen
obsolete name for nitrogen
archaic

Examples

In early chemistry texts, nitrogen is often referred to as azote.
Lavoisier proposed the term azote because the gas did not support life.
The name azote was eventually replaced by the modern term nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrogen - a chemical element with symbol N and atomic number 7

Etymology

From French 'azote', coined by French chemist Antoine Lavoisier from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'zōē' (life), as nitrogen does not support life.
