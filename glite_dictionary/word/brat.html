brat

1. NOUN

/ bræt / (AmE), / bræt / (BrE)
a small pork sausage, especially a bratwurst
small pork sausage
american

Examples

We're grilling some brats for the barbecue tonight.
He bought a package of brats at the supermarket for the party.
She served brat with mustard and sauerkraut at the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bratwurst - a type of German sausage made from pork, beef, or veal

Etymology

Shortened from German 'Bratwurst', where 'Brat' means 'finely chopped meat' and 'Wurst' means 'sausage'.

2. NOUN

/ bræt / (AmE), / bræt / (BrE)
a child, typically one who is badly behaved or annoying
badly behaved child

Examples

The spoiled brat threw a tantrum when he didn't get what he wanted.
Don't be such a brat—share your toys with your sister.
She was known as the class brat because she was always causing trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urchin - a mischievous child, especially one who is poorly or raggedly dressed
  • Antonym: angel - a well-behaved child (opposite of brat in this sense)

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'brat' (cloak, rag, garment), later used for a child, possibly originally meaning a child in rags.
