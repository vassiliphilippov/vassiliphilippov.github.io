widowhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊˌhʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being a widow or widower, especially after the death of a spouse and before any remarriage
state of being a widow or widower

Examples

She struggled with the loneliness of widowhood after her husband passed away.
His family supported him through the challenges of widowhood.
Many find new purpose during widowhood by volunteering or taking up new hobbies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widower - a man whose spouse has died and has not remarried

Etymology

From Old English 'widuwanhad', combining 'widuwe' (widow) + '-had' (state, condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊˌhʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being a widow
the state of being a widow

Examples

After her husband's passing, she faced the challenges of widowhood with courage.
She spent many years of her life in widowhood, surrounded by family and friends.
His mother entered widowhood at a relatively young age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widowerhood - the state or period of being a widower

Etymology

From Old English 'wuduhād' (widowhood), from 'wuduwe' (widow) + '-hād' (state, condition).
