dibranchiate

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈbræŋkiˌeɪt / (AmE), / daɪˈbraŋkɪət / (BrE)
A cephalopod mollusc having two gills, as opposed to four; includes squids and octopuses.
cephalopod with two gills

Examples

The dibranchiate molluscs, such as squids and octopuses, possess only two gills.
Unlike the dibranchiates, nautiluses belong to a separate subclass with four gills.
Marine biologists study dibranchiate anatomy to understand cephalopod evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tetrabranchiate - cephalopods with four gills

Etymology

From Latin di- (two) + Greek branchia (gills).
