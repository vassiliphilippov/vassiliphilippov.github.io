antipathetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
having a strong aversion or opposition to something; strongly opposed (usually followed by 'to').
strongly opposed or averse to

Examples

He was antipathetic to any changes in the company’s traditional policies.
Many people remain antipathetic to genetically modified foods despite scientific assurances.
Her antipathetic attitude toward authority figures often led to conflict.

Synset Examples

antipathetic to new ideas
averse to taking risks
loath to go on such short notice
clearly indisposed to grant their request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: averse - having a strong dislike of or opposition to something

Etymology

From Greek antipatheia (opposition of feeling), from anti- (against) + pathos (feeling).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
Feeling or showing a strong dislike or opposition to someone or something.
having a strong dislike or opposition

Examples

Many voters were antipathetic to the new tax proposal, fearing it would hurt the economy.
He always seemed antipathetic toward his colleague’s ideas, rarely offering support.
The two families remained antipathetic to each other for generations after the dispute.

Synset Examples

slaves antagonistic to their masters
antipathetic factions within the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostile - Feeling or showing enmity, antagonism, or opposition
  • Antonym: sympathetic - Feeling or showing understanding or compassion for others

Etymology

From Greek antipatheia ('opposition of feeling, aversion') via French antipathetique.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01248012-s
Synset POS: s
