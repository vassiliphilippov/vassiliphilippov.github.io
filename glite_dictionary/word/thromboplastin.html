thromboplastin

1. NOUN

/ ˌθrɑːmboʊˈplæstɪn / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəʊˈplæstɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme released from blood platelets that helps to convert prothrombin into thrombin during blood clotting
enzyme involved in blood clotting
medical

Examples

The laboratory used thromboplastin to measure the patient's blood clotting time.
A deficiency in thromboplastin can lead to disorders involving excessive bleeding.
The release of thromboplastin initiates an important step in the coagulation cascade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prothrombin - a plasma protein converted into thrombin in the clotting process
  • Synonym: thrombin - an enzyme that converts fibrinogen to fibrin in the clotting process

Etymology

From Greek *thrombos* (clot) + *plastos* (formed).
