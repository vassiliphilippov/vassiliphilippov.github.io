vicarship

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪkərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈvɪkəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, position, or religious institution under the authority of a vicar
office or position of a vicar

Examples

He was appointed to the vicarship of St. Mary's parish in 1850.
During her tenure, the vicarship expanded its charitable activities.
After years of study, he was finally offered a vicarship in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicariate - the office or district of a vicar

Etymology

Formed from 'vicar' + '-ship', where 'vicar' is from Latin 'vicarius' (substitute).
