haunt

1. NOUN

/ hɑnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
a place often visited by a particular person or group
frequently visited place

Examples

This coffee shop is my favorite haunt on weekends.
Artists often find inspiration at their regular haunt downtown.
The old library was a popular haunt for local students.

Etymology

From Old French 'hanter' (to frequent, visit regularly).

2. VERB

/ hɑnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
to visit a place frequently or regularly, often as part of a habit
visit a place frequently

Examples

She likes to haunt the local coffee shop on weekends.
Artists used to haunt this old jazz club in the 1960s.
He still haunts the library, even after all these years.

Synset Examples

Etymology

3. VERB

/ hɑnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
to repeatedly appear in someone's mind or to persistently trouble or follow someone, especially in a way that causes anxiety or unease
recur constantly or trouble persistently

Examples

The memory of that tragic night still haunts him years later.
She felt as if her mistakes would forever haunt her.
His strange behavior began to haunt her thoughts every day.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalk - to pursue or approach stealthily or obsessively
  • Synonym: to harass - to bother or trouble someone persistently

4. VERB

/ hɔnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
(of a memory, fear, or feeling) to continually cause anxiety or trouble to someone; (of a ghost) to appear regularly in a place.
to trouble persistently; to appear as a ghost

Examples

The fear of failure can haunt even the most successful people.

5. VERB

/ hɑnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
(of a ghost or supernatural being) to regularly appear or manifest in a place, often disturbing or troubling those present.
(of ghost) regularly visit a place

Examples

People said the ghost continued to haunt the old library long after it was abandoned.
Legends claim that a headless rider haunts the country roads at night.
She was convinced the empty cottage had been haunted for years.

Synset Examples
