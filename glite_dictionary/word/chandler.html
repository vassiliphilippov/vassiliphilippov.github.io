chandler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃændlər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn(d)lə / (BrE)
a person or business that sells provisions, supplies, or equipment, especially for ships.
seller of provisions and supplies

Examples

The ship's crew visited the chandler to stock up on food and tools before departure.
She inherited her family's chandler business, supplying local fishermen with everything they needed.
A skilled chandler ensured the fleet was fully equipped for the long voyage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provisioner - a person or company that supplies food and other necessary items

Etymology

From Old French *chandelier* (maker or seller of candles), later extended to dealers in supplies.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃændlər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːndlɐ / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is to make or sell candles, soap, oils, and sometimes paints
a maker or seller of candles and soap

Examples

The village chandler supplied everyone with candles and soap throughout the year.
Before electric lighting, a good chandler was essential to running a large estate.
He apprenticed as a chandler, learning to mix oils for lamps and molds for candles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candlemaker - a person who makes candles

Etymology

From Old French *chandelier* (maker or seller of candles), from Latin *candela* (candle).
