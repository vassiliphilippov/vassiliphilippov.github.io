pitchfork

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃˌfɔrk / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃˌfɔːk / (BrE)
a long-handled tool with widely spaced, sharp prongs, used for lifting and tossing hay or other materials
long-handled tool for lifting hay

Examples

The farmer used a pitchfork to toss hay onto the wagon.
After the storm, she grabbed her pitchfork to clear debris from the yard.
At the barn, an old rusted pitchfork leaned against the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fork - a tool with two or more prongs used for lifting or digging

Etymology

From Middle English 'pichefork', from Old French 'pique-fourche' (forked pike).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪtʃˌfɔrk / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃˌfɔːk / (BrE)
to lift or move (something) using a pitchfork
lift or move with a pitchfork

Examples

Every morning, she would pitchfork hay into the wagon for the horses.
The workers pitchforked the compost onto a large pile behind the barn.
With practiced ease, he pitchforks straw into the loft above.

Synset Examples

pitchfork hay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fork - to lift or move with a forked instrument (verb)

Etymology

From 'pitchfork' (noun), meaning a tool for lifting and tossing hay or similar materials.

Synset POS: v
