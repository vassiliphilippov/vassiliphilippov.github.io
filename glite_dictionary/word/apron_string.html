apron string

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪprən ˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪprən ˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
(usually apron strings) Used metaphorically to refer to dependence on one's mother or parental control, especially as excessive or inappropriate for an adult.
dependence on parental control (metaphor)

Examples

Even as an adult, he couldn't quite cut the apron strings and move out on his own.
She accused him of being tied to his mother's apron strings whenever he refused to make decisions by himself.
Some people leave for college eager to escape their parents' apron strings.

Synset Examples

It's time for him to let go of those apron strings.
He still clings to his mother's apron strings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independence - freedom from the control or influence of others
  • Antonym: self-reliance - reliance on one's own powers and resources
