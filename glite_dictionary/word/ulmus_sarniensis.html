Ulmus sarniensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs sɑːrˈniːɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs sɑːˈnɪensis / (BrE)
a variety of the English elm tree distinguished by its erect branches and broader leaves than typical English elms
a variety of English elm tree

Examples

The Ulmus sarniensis is renowned for its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
Gardeners appreciate Ulmus sarniensis for its upright form and attractive foliage.
After the storm, the ancient Ulmus sarniensis in the park remained standing.

Etymology

From Latin: Ulmus (elm) and sarniensis ('of Guernsey', from the Latin name for Guernsey, Sarnia); named for its presence on the island of Guernsey.
