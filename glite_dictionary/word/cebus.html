Cebus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
a genus of New World monkeys, known as capuchin monkeys, native to Central and South America
genus of capuchin monkeys

Examples

Researchers have observed that Cebus monkeys use tools in the wild.
The Cebus genus includes several species of capuchin monkeys.
Conservation efforts are crucial for protecting many members of the Cebus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capuchin monkey - a common name for monkeys in the genus Cebus

Etymology

From Latin cebus, from Greek kēbos (long-tailed monkey).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of New World monkeys known as capuchin monkeys, native to Central and South America; the type genus of the family Cebidae.
genus of capuchin monkeys

Examples

The Cebus genus includes several species of highly intelligent capuchin monkeys.
Researchers studied the behavior of Cebus monkeys in different forest habitats.
The Cebus genus is distinguished by its rounded head and prehensile tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capuchin monkey - a small New World monkey of the genus Cebus

Etymology

From Latin 'Cebus,' modern scientific Latin, from Greek kēbos (meaning 'a kind of monkey').
