to vilipend

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪləˌpɛnd / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪpɛnd / (BrE)
to regard or treat as of little worth; to belittle or disparage
regard or treat as of little worth

Examples

It is unfair to vilipend someone's achievements out of jealousy.
She refused to vilipend her opponent, choosing instead to focus on her own strengths.
Writers who vilipend the classics often fail to appreciate their lasting value.

Synset Examples

The teacher should not deprecate his student's efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - to make (someone or something) seem unimportant
  • Antonym: to extol - to praise highly; glorify

Etymology

From Latin 'vilipendere', from 'vilis' (cheap, vile) + 'pendere' (to weigh).
