weakling

1. NOUN

/ ˈwik.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːk.lɪŋ / (BrE)
A person who is physically weak and lacks strength or effectiveness.
a physically weak person

Examples

As a child, he was a weakling, often picked last for team sports.
Nobody wants to be seen as a weakling in front of their peers.
Despite being called a weakling, he proved everyone wrong with his determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wimp - a person who lacks courage or is unduly afraid
  • Antonym: strongman - a physically strong or powerful person

Etymology

From 'weak' + '-ling', an Old English diminutive suffix.
