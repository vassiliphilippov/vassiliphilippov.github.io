scatological

1. ADJ

/ ˌskætəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌskætəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by an interest in excrement or excretory functions, especially in a coarse or indecent way
dealing with excrement or excretory functions

Examples

The comedian's jokes were criticized for their scatological content.
Some Renaissance literature is surprisingly scatological in its humor.
He dismissed the film as mere scatological nonsense, lacking any real wit.

Synset Examples

scatological literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality

Etymology

From Greek *skōr*, *skat-* (dung) + -logical (relating to study or discourse).
