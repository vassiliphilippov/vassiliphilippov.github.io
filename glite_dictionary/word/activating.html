activating

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.təˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈakt.ɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of causing something to have energy or become active
the act of making active or energetic

Examples

The activating of the chemicals must be carefully controlled in the laboratory.
Proper activating of the alarm system is crucial for security.
Experts discussed the activating of community programs to encourage youth participation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activation - the action or process of making something active or operative

Etymology

From the verb 'activate', from Latin 'activare' (to make active).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæk.təˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
causing something to start operating, moving, or becoming active; initiating motion, action, or change.
causing to become active or start operating

Examples

The researcher added an activating agent to the solution to speed up the reaction.
Exposure to sunlight has an activating effect on the plant's growth.
The activating mechanism in the engine triggered the process automatically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulating - encouraging activity or arousing action
  • Synonym: initiating - causing to begin; starting something
  • Antonym: inhibiting - preventing or restraining an action or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'activare' (to make active), from 'activus' (active).
