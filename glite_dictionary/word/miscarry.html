to miscarry

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈkæri / (AmE), / mɪsˈkæri / (BrE)
to have a miscarriage; to lose a pregnancy before the fetus can survive outside the womb
suffer a miscarriage
medical

Examples

She was devastated to learn that she would miscarry in her first trimester.
Doctors explained that it is not uncommon for women to miscarry early in pregnancy.
After several attempts, she sadly miscarried again last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abort - to end a pregnancy early
  • Antonym: to conceive - to become pregnant

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + carry, referring to unsuccessful carrying of pregnancy.

WordNet ID: oewn-00059540-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈkæri / (AmE), / mɪsˈkæri / (BrE)
(of a plan, attempt, etc.) to fail to achieve the intended result; to be unsuccessful.
to fail; to be unsuccessful

Examples

If the negotiations miscarry, both countries risk further conflict.
Plans for the new shopping center miscarried due to lack of funding.
Their scheme to improve attendance miscarried despite their best efforts.

Synset Examples

Where do today's public schools fail?
The attempt to rescue the hostages failed miserably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal or the desired result

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + carry; from Old French 'carier' (to transport) and mis- (badly or wrongly).
