demonstrable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɑnstrəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrəbəl / (BrE)
able to be proven or clearly shown to be true by evidence or argument
able to be proven or shown true

Examples

There is no demonstrable connection between the two incidents.
The results provided demonstrable proof of his theory.
Her innocence was demonstrable in the face of all the evidence.

Synset Examples

demonstrable truths

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provable - able to be proved true; capable of being demonstrated
  • Antonym: indemonstrable - not able to be demonstrated or proved

Etymology

From Late Latin demonstrabilis (that can be demonstrated), from Latin demonstrare (to demonstrate).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɑːnstrəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrəbəl / (BrE)
able to be shown or proven clearly and convincingly
able to be shown or proven

Examples

There is no demonstrable evidence linking the suspect to the crime.
The benefits of the new method must be demonstrable before we adopt it.
Her claims are not demonstrable by any scientific standards.

Synset Examples

a demonstrable lack of concern for the general welfare
practical truth provable to all men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provable - capable of being proved true or correct

Etymology

From Latin demonstrabilis, from demonstrare (to demonstrate).
