subaqueous

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈeɪkwiəs / (AmE), / sʌbˈeɪkwɪəs / (BrE)
existing, growing, or taking place under water.
existing or occurring under water

Examples

The researchers studied subaqueous plants that thrive at the bottom of the lake.
Divers discovered an ancient subaqueous cave filled with unusual rock formations.
Many aquatic animals have adapted to life in a strictly subaqueous environment.

Synset Examples

viewing subaqueous fauna from a glass-bottomed boat
submerged leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submerged - completely covered or under water
  • Antonym: subaerial - existing or taking place on or near the surface of the earth (as opposed to under water)

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + aqua (water).
