Araucaria columnaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌær.əˈkɛəri.ə ˌkɒl.əmˈnɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈkeə.ri.ə ˌkɒl.əmˈnɛː.rɪs / (BrE)
A very tall evergreen tree native to New Caledonia and the New Hebrides, resembling the Norfolk Island pine.
tall evergreen tree of New Caledonia

Examples

The slender silhouette of the Araucaria columnaris makes it a striking feature in landscaped gardens.
Many coastal towns in the Pacific plant Araucaria columnaris along streets for their ornamental value.
Botanists compared the growth patterns of Araucaria columnaris and Norfolk Island pine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norfolk Island pine - a similar species of Araucaria native to Norfolk Island

Etymology

From genus name Araucaria and species epithet columnaris, meaning 'column-shaped' in Latin.
