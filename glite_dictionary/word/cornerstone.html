cornerstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəstəʊn / (BrE)
A stone, often inscribed with a date, set at the corner of a building, usually during a formal ceremony and marking the start of construction.
inscribed stone marking start of building

Examples

The new museum's cornerstone was laid in a grand ceremony attended by city officials.
An inscription on the cornerstone marks the year the school was established.
Architects pointed out the original cornerstone at the base of the historic courthouse.

Etymology

From Middle English 'corner' + 'stone', referring to the physical stone placed at a building's corner.

/ ˈkɔrnərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəstəʊn / (BrE)
an essential or foundational principle or element on which something is based or depends
fundamental principle or basis

Honesty remains the cornerstone of our company's values.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a stone placed at the outer corner of two intersecting masonry walls, often marking the foundation or ceremonial start of a building
stone at the corner of intersecting walls

Examples

The building's cornerstone was laid in 1892 during a public ceremony.
A time capsule was placed inside the cornerstone of the new library.
Architects inspected the ancient cornerstone for signs of structural damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation stone - a stone that forms the base of a building; similar ceremonial or structural concept

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'corner' and 'stone'.
