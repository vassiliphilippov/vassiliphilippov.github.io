disqualification

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˌkwɑləfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of declaring someone ineligible to participate, especially due to not meeting certain requirements or rules
act of declaring ineligible

Examples

The athlete's false start led to her disqualification from the race.
Failure to provide the required documents resulted in immediate disqualification.
His aggressive behavior brought about his disqualification from the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualification - the act of qualifying or the state of being qualified

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'qualificare' (to make of a certain quality).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˌkwɑlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being declared unfit or ineligible to participate, often due to rules, regulations, or conduct.
state of being made ineligible

Examples

His actions led to immediate disqualification from the competition.
Any sign of cheating will result in disqualification.
After reviewing the evidence, the judges confirmed her disqualification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualification - the process or fact of qualifying or being qualified

Etymology

From disqualify + -ation, based on Latin roots dis- (apart, away) and qualificare (to make suitable).
