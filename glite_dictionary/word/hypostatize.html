to hypostatize

1. VERB

/ haɪˈpɑstəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒstətaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or regard an abstract concept as if it were a real, concrete entity; to attribute actual existence to a conceptual or non-physical thing
regard an abstract concept as real

Examples

The philosopher warned against attempts to hypostatize ideas such as 'justice' or 'freedom'.
It is a mistake to hypostatize the notion of intelligence as a physical entity within the brain.
Some critics argue that social constructs are often hypostatized in modern theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reify - to make something abstract more concrete or real

Etymology

From Greek 'hypostatoun', from 'hypostasis' (substance, essence).
