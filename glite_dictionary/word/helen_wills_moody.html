Helen Wills Moody

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛlən wɪlz ˈmuːdi / (AmE), / ˈhɛlən wɪlz ˈmuːdi / (BrE)
(1905–1998) United States tennis player who dominated women's tennis in the 1920s and 1930s.
famous American tennis player (1905–1998)

Examples

Helen Wills Moody won a total of 19 Grand Slam singles titles during her career.
Sports historians often cite Helen Wills Moody as one of the greatest female tennis players of all time.
In the 1920s and 1930s, Helen Wills Moody was a dominant presence at Wimbledon.
