mastication

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of biting and grinding food in the mouth to prepare it for swallowing
the act of chewing food
medical

Examples

Proper mastication is essential for good digestion and nutrient absorption.
Some animals spend much longer on mastication than humans do.
The dentist explained that insufficient mastication can lead to stomach problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chewing - the act of biting and grinding with teeth

Etymology

From Latin masticatio, from masticare (to chew), from Greek mastikhan (to gnash the teeth).
