Hemitripterus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈtrɪptərəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˈtrɪptərəs əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnəs / (BrE)
A large sculpin of the western Atlantic Ocean, known for inflating itself when caught; also called the sea raven.
species of Atlantic sculpin fish

Examples

The Hemitripterus americanus is often caught by anglers along the northeastern coast of North America.
When disturbed, the Hemitripterus americanus inflates its body as a defensive behavior.
Marine biologists study Hemitripterus americanus to better understand its unique adaptations.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Hemitripterus', meaning 'half fin' (Greek hemi- ‘half’ + Greek tripteros ‘with three wings/fins’), plus the Latin 'americanus', referring to America.
