Hygrophorus turundus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑfərəs tjuːˈrʌndəs / (AmE), / hɪˌɡrɒfərəs tjʊˈrʌndəs / (BrE)
a small mushroom with an orange cap and yellow gills, typically found in sphagnum bogs
a small orange-capped mushroom

Examples

Hygrophorus turundus can often be found growing among sphagnum moss in wet bogs.
The bright orange cap of Hygrophorus turundus makes it easy to identify for mushroom hunters.
Mycology students observed Hygrophorus turundus during their field trip to the peat bog.

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (wet, moist) and 'phoros' (bearing); 'turundus' is the species name assigned in scientific classification.
