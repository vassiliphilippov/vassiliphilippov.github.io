articulated

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) consisting of segments connected by joints that allow movement between them.
having segments joined by joints

Examples

The articulated limbs of insects allow them to move with great flexibility.
She observed the articulated structure of the train as it navigated the sharp curve.
Crabs have articulated legs that enable them to walk sideways with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jointed - having joints or being jointed

Etymology

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare 'to divide into joints'.
