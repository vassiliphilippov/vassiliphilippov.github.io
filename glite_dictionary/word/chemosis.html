chemosis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A swelling (edema) of the mucous membrane covering the eyeball and lining the eyelids, often giving the conjunctiva a swollen, jelly-like appearance.
swelling of the eye's conjunctiva
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her sudden eye discomfort as chemosis caused by an allergic reaction.
Severe chemosis can make it difficult to close the eyelids completely.
After the injury, the patient developed significant chemosis in the affected eye.

Etymology

From Greek kēmosis (swelling of the eyelids), from kēma (swelling, tumor).
