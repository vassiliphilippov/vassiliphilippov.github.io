apanage

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈapənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a customary and rightful privilege or perquisite associated with a particular rank or office
customary privilege of rank or office

Examples

For centuries, the title of duke was considered an apanage reserved for members of the royal family.
Education was once the apanage of the privileged classes, not accessible to the general public.
The use of elaborate robes was seen as an apanage of religious leaders in medieval society.

Synset Examples

for thousands of years the chair was an appanage of state and dignity rather than an article of ordinary use

Etymology

From French 'apanage', from Medieval Latin 'appanagium', meaning 'that which is provided as bread', i.e., provision for younger children of noble families.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæp.ə.nɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈap.ənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a grant of land, title, or resources by a sovereign or legislature to support a dependent member of a ruling family
grant for royal family support

Examples

As a younger prince, he was given an apanage in the southern provinces.
Many historians study the system of apanage in medieval France for clues about power distribution.
The bishopric was treated as an apanage for the king's second son.

Synset Examples

bishoprics were received as appanages for the younger sons of great families

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money or other resources given for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'apanage', from Medieval Latin 'apanaticum', from 'apanare' (to provide with means of subsistence).
