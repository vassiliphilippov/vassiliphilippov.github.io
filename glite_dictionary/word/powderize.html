to powderize

1. VERB

/ ˈpaʊ.dəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.də.raɪz / (BrE)
to become or cause to become powder or dust
become powder or dust

Examples

The rock began to powderize after years of exposure to the elements.
When heated to a high temperature, the minerals will eventually powderize.
The old documents had powderized and fell apart when touched.

Synset Examples

When it was blown up, the building powderized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pulverize - to reduce to fine particles; to crush or grind into powder or dust
  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-ize', following the pattern of forming verbs meaning 'to make' or 'become'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpaʊdəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəraɪz / (BrE)
to make into a powder by crushing, grinding, or other means; to reduce to dust
reduce something to powder by crushing

Examples

Modern mills can powderize corn in seconds for use in food production.
Pharmaceutical companies powderize certain medicines to make them easier to administer.
The chef needed to powderize the dried spices before adding them to the dish.

Synset Examples

pulverize the grains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pulverize - to crush into a powder or dust

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-ize', indicating the process of making into powder.

Synset POS: v
