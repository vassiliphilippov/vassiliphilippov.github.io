Trigonella ornithopodioides

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪɡəˈnɛlə ɔrnɪθoʊˌpoʊdiˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌtrɪɡənɛlə ɔːˌnɪθəʊpəʊdɪˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
An Old World herb of the pea family, related to fenugreek, native to Mediterranean regions.
Old World herb related to fenugreek

Examples

Trigonella ornithopodioides is often found in Mediterranean meadows and grasslands.
The leaves of Trigonella ornithopodioides resemble those of its relative, fenugreek.
Botanists study Trigonella ornithopodioides because of its unique seed and flower characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fenugreek - an aromatic plant used as a herb and spice

Etymology

From Latin; 'Trigonella' means 'little triangle' (referring to the shape of leaflets), 'ornithopodioides' means 'resembling bird’s foot'.
