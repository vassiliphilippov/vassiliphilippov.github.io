circadian

1. ADJ

/ sɚˈkeɪdiən / (AmE), / sɜːˈkeɪdiən / (BrE)
Relating to biological processes or cycles that occur approximately every 24 hours.
occurring every 24 hours (biology)

Examples

Researchers study circadian rhythms to understand how sleep patterns are regulated.
Jet lag occurs when your circadian clock becomes misaligned with a new time zone.
The circadian cycle influences body temperature, hormone release, and eating habits.

Synset Examples

circadian rhythms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daily - Happening every day or relating to a day

Etymology

From Latin 'circa' (about) + 'dies' (day), meaning 'about a day.'
