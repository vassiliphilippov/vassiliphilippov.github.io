to nominate

1. VERB

/ ˈnɑː.mə.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɒ.mɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to formally appoint or assign someone to a position, role, or task
formally appoint or assign

Examples

The board will nominate a new chairperson at tomorrow’s meeting.
She was nominated to lead the fundraising committee this year.
Each department is asked to nominate one representative for the task force.

Synset Examples

nominate a committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign a job or role to someone formally
  • Synonym: designate - to officially choose someone for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin nominare (to name), from nomen (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02401347-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnɑːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to formally propose someone for appointment to a position, office, or honor
formally propose for a position or honor

Examples

The board will nominate several candidates for the new position.
She was nominated for the award by her colleagues in recognition of her achievements.
Each party can nominate one representative to serve on the committee.

Synset Examples

The President nominated her as head of the Civil Rights Commission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign someone to a position or job officially
  • Antonym: remove - to take someone out of a position or office

Etymology

From Latin nominare (to name), from nomen (name).
