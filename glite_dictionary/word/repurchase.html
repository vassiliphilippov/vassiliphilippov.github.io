repurchase

1. NOUN

/ riˈpɜrtʃəs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
the act of buying back something that was previously sold
the act of buying something back

Examples

The company announced a repurchase of its own shares to boost their value.
After the sale, there was an option for the repurchase of the property within two years.
A repurchase agreement is commonly used in financial markets to raise short-term capital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buyback - the act of buying something back, especially shares or stock

Etymology

From re- (again) + purchase; based on Latin roots.

2. VERB

/ riˈpɜrtʃəs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
to buy back something that was previously sold, lost, or given away
buy something again, especially after selling it

Examples

The company decided to repurchase its shares from investors.
After years abroad, she managed to repurchase her family home.
He regretted selling his guitar and wanted to repurchase it from its new owner.

Synset Examples

He bought back the house that his father sold years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to buy back - to purchase something again after selling it

Etymology

From re- (again) + purchase, ultimately from Latin 'purchasare' (to buy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02212672-v
Synset POS: v
