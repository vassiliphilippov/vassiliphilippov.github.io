chrysoberyl

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsəˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəˈbɛrəl / (BrE)
a rare hard mineral consisting of beryllium aluminate, typically yellow-green in color, used as a gemstone
rare yellow-green mineral gemstone

Examples

The jeweler displayed a stunning chrysoberyl set in a gold ring.
Due to its hardness, chrysoberyl is highly valued among gemstone collectors.
She admired the pale yellow-green color of the chrysoberyl in her necklace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alexandrite - a variety of chrysoberyl that changes color under different light
  • Synonym: beryl - a different mineral gemstone (beryllium aluminium cyclosilicate)

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) + *beryllos* (beryl).
