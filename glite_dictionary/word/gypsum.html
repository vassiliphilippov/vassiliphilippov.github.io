gypsum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪp.səm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪp.səm / (BrE)
A soft white or colorless mineral consisting of hydrated calcium sulfate, used to make plaster, cement, and fertilizers.
a white mineral used in plaster and cement

Examples

The builders mixed gypsum with water to create plaster for the walls.
Natural deposits of gypsum are found in many parts of the world.
Artists often use gypsum to make detailed sculptures and casts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster of Paris - a fine white powder made from gypsum, used for plastering and casting

Etymology

From Latin 'gypsum', from Greek 'gýpsos' (chalk, gypsum).
