sensualism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnʃuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛnʃʊəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The ethical doctrine that feelings or sensations are the only valid basis for determining what is good.
philosophical doctrine: feeling as ethical criterion

Examples

Some philosophers defend sensualism as the foundation of moral judgment.
The debate between rationalism and sensualism shaped much of Enlightenment thought.
Critics of sensualism argue that it neglects the importance of reason in ethical decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedonism - the ethical theory that pleasure is the highest good

Etymology

From Latin 'sensualis' (pertaining to sense or feeling) + '-ism'

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tjʊ.əˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
the pursuit of or devotion to sensual pleasures; the doctrine that pleasure or sensations are the highest good
the pursuit of sensual pleasures

Examples

He was often criticized for his sensualism and indulgence in worldly pleasures.
Some philosophers have debated the merits of sensualism versus asceticism as a way of life.
Her writing explores the tension between spiritual longing and sensualism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedonism - the pursuit of pleasure as the highest good
  • Antonym: asceticism - the practice of self-discipline and abstention from indulgence

Etymology

From Latin 'sensualis' (sensual) + '-ism'.
