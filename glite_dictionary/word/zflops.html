ZFLOPS

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːˌflɑps / (AmE), / ˈzɛt.əˌflɒps / (BrE)
A unit of measurement for computer processing speed, equal to one sextillion (10²¹) floating-point operations per second.
unit of computer processing speed (10²¹ FLOPS)

Examples

Supercomputers are expected to reach ZFLOPS capability within the next decade.
Simulating global climate models accurately requires systems that can perform at ZFLOPS speeds.
The lab is competing to build the world's first ZFLOPS computer for advanced scientific research.

Synset Examples

Erik P. DeBenedictis of Sandia National Laboratories theorizes that a zettaFLOPS (ZFLOPS) computer is required to accomplish full weather modeling of two week time span.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: FLOPS - floating-point operations per second; unit for measuring computer performance
  • Degree Lower: EFLOPS - one quintillion (10¹⁸) floating-point operations per second; lower than ZFLOPS

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'zetta floating-point operations per second', where zetta- denotes a factor of 10²¹.
