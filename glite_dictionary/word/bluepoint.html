bluepoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
A type of oyster originally harvested from Long Island Sound, now from the northeastern US coast, commonly eaten raw.
type of northeastern US oyster

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its fresh bluepoint oysters served on ice.
She ordered a dozen bluepoints as a starter for the table.
Many seafood lovers prefer bluepoint oysters for their mild, briny flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - an edible bivalve mollusk with a rough irregular shell

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
A small edible oyster, typically harvested from the southern shore of Long Island, prized for its flavor.
small edible oyster from Long Island

Examples

The restaurant offered fresh bluepoint oysters on the half shell.
She ordered a dozen bluepoints as a starter for the table.
Many chefs prefer bluepoint oysters for their briny and slightly sweet taste.
