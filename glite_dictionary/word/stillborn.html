stillborn

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɪlˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈstɪl.bɔːn / (BrE)
(of a baby) born dead, showing no signs of life at birth.
born dead (of a baby)

Examples

The doctor gently explained that the baby was stillborn.
She mourned the loss of her stillborn child with deep sorrow.
A memorial service was held for families of stillborn infants.

Synset Examples

a stillborn baby

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liveborn - born alive

Etymology

From Middle English, from still (motionless, dead) + born (past participle of bear), reflecting the condition at birth.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɪlˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈstɪlˌbɔːn / (BrE)
(of an idea, plan, or project) failing to develop or come to fruition; unsuccessful from the outset.
failing to succeed or develop

Examples

The company's ambitious merger was ultimately a stillborn effort due to regulatory issues.
Despite months of planning, their proposal proved to be stillborn when funding was withdrawn.
The peace talks were stillborn, collapsing before they even began.

Synset Examples

an abortive revolt
a stillborn plot to assassinate the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abortive - failing to produce the intended result
  • Antonym: successful - accomplishing the intended result

Etymology

From still + born, originally referring to an infant born dead; figurative use extended to unrealized attempts or plans.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01086402-s
Synset POS: s
