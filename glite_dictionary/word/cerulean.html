cerulean

1. ADJ

/ səˈruːliən / (AmE), / sɪˈruːliən / (BrE)
Deep blue in color, resembling the clear sky on a bright day, sometimes with a purplish tint.
deep sky-blue color

Examples

The artist painted the sea in a vivid cerulean that captivated everyone.
She wore a cerulean dress that matched the clear sky.
The mountain lake reflected the cerulean hue of an autumn afternoon.

Synset Examples

October's bright blue weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - bright blue in color, like a cloudless sky
  • Degree Lower: blue - color between green and violet in the spectrum

Etymology

From Latin 'caeruleus' (sky blue), from 'caelum' (sky).

2. NOUN

/ səˈruːliən / (AmE), / sɪˈruːlɪən / (BrE)
a deep, sky-blue or light blue color
a light shade of blue

Examples

The painter chose cerulean for the sky in her landscape.
She wore a dress of soft cerulean that brought out her eyes.
The lake reflected the cerulean of the afternoon sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - a bright blue color, like the sky on a clear day
  • Degree Lower: navy blue - a very dark shade of blue
  • Degree Lower: turquoise - a blue-green color

Etymology

From Latin 'caeruleus' (sky blue), from 'caelum' (sky).

Synset POS: n
