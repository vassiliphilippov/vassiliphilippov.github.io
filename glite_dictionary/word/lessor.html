lessor

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈsɔr / (AmE), / lɛˈsɔː / (BrE)
a person or organization that grants the use of property under a lease
one who grants a lease
legal

Examples

The lessor is responsible for property maintenance according to the lease agreement.
Under the contract, the lessor must provide written notice before entering the premises.
A commercial lessor often requires tenants to purchase liability insurance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lessee - the person who receives the use of property under a lease

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *lessour*, from Old French *lesser* (to lease).
