antiferromagnetism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.fɛr.oʊˈmæɡ.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌanti.fɛrəʊˈmaɡ.nɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A phenomenon in which the magnetic moments of atoms or ions in a material are aligned in parallel but opposite directions, resulting in no net magnetization.
magnetic arrangement with opposing spins

Examples

Some materials display antiferromagnetism only at very low temperatures.
Understanding antiferromagnetism is crucial for developing advanced magnetic storage devices.
The discovery of antiferromagnetism led to new insights in solid-state physics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ferromagnetism - magnetic phenomenon with aligned spins and strong magnetization

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'ferromagnetism', from Latin 'ferrum' (iron) + 'magnetism'.
