Morchella crassipes

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈkɛl.ə ˈkræs.ɪˌpiːz / (AmE), / mɔːˈkɛl.ə ˈkræs.ɪˌpiːz / (BrE)
a species of edible morel mushroom with a conical cap marked by deep and irregular pits
a species of edible morel mushroom

Examples

The spring forager was thrilled to find a patch of Morchella crassipes in the woods.
Chefs prize Morchella crassipes for its rich, earthy flavor in gourmet dishes.
Identifying Morchella crassipes requires careful attention to the deep pits on its cap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom of the genus Morchella with a honeycombed cap

Etymology

From Latin 'Morchella' (morel) and 'crassipes' (thick-footed).
