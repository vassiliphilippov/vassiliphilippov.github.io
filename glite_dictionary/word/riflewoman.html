riflewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈraɪflˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female soldier equipped with and trained to use a rifle.
female rifle-bearing soldier

Examples

The army recently promoted its first riflewoman to the rank of sergeant.
During her training, the riflewoman demonstrated excellent marksmanship.
Modern militaries now include both riflemen and riflewomen in their infantry units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rifleman - a soldier armed with a rifle

Etymology

From 'rifle' (firearm) + 'woman'. Modeled after 'rifleman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈraɪf(ə)lˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is skilled in the use of a rifle, especially as a member of the armed forces or in shooting sports
woman skilled with a rifle

Examples

The skilled riflewoman quickly took her position during the competition.
As the only riflewoman in her unit, she became a role model for younger recruits.
The team's leading riflewoman demonstrated remarkable accuracy in every round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rifleman - a man skilled in the use of a rifle

Etymology

Formed from 'rifle' + 'woman,' parallel to 'rifleman.'

Synset POS: n
