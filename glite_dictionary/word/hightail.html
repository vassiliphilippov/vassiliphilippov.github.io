to hightail

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪ.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.teɪl / (BrE)
to leave or move away very quickly, especially to escape or retreat
leave in a hurry; run away quickly
informal american

Examples

As soon as the alarm went off, the thieves hightailed out of the building.
We'd better hightail it if we want to catch the last bus.
After the embarrassing mistake, she hightailed out of the meeting room.

Synset Examples

The actress high-tailed to her villa when reporters began to follow her to the restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from danger or pursuit
  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw quickly from a position or situation
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay longer; to be slow in leaving

Etymology

Probably from the image of an animal lifting its tail high when fleeing; originally American English, 19th century.
