trifoliate

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈfoʊliət / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊlɪət / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having three leaflets.
leaf with three leaflets

Examples

The plant is easily identified by its trifoliate leaves.
Some legumes, such as clover, display a characteristic trifoliate pattern.
The trifoliate structure is an adaptation that helps certain plants reduce water loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pinnate - (of a leaf) with leaflets arranged on either side of the stem, typically in pairs

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'folium' (leaf).
