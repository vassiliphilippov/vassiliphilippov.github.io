Elettaria cardamomum

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛtəriə kɑrˌdæməˈmʌm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛtəriə kɑːˌdaməˈmʌm / (BrE)
A tropical plant native to India, known for its aromatic seeds used as a spice in cooking and baking.
aromatic spice plant (cardamom)

Examples

The seeds of Elettaria cardamomum are often ground and used in traditional Indian desserts.
Farmers in Kerala cultivate Elettaria cardamomum as a major cash crop.
The distinct flavor of Elettaria cardamomum enhances many curries and baked goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamom - aromatic spice from the seeds of Elettaria cardamomum

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name Elettaria (from Malayalam elathari) and the species cardamomum (from Greek kardamōmon, cardamom).
