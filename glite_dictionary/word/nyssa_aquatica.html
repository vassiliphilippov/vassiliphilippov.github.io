Nyssa aquatica

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪsə əˈkwɑːtɪkə / (AmE), / ˈnɪsə əˈkwɒtɪkə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous tree native to the swamps and floodplains of southeastern and midwestern North America, known for its columnar form and pale, soft wood.
tree species native to North American swamps

Examples

The Nyssa aquatica thrives in the wet, swampy soils along southern rivers.
Lumber from Nyssa aquatica is valued for its softness and workability.
Researchers studied the ecosystem role of Nyssa aquatica in floodplain forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water tupelo - common name for Nyssa aquatica

Etymology

From Latin: 'Nyssa' (a genus of trees) + 'aquatica' meaning 'of water' or 'aquatic'.
