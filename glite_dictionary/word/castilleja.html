Castilleja

1. NOUN

/ kæˈstil.jeɪ.ə / (AmE), / kaˈstɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants native to western North and South America, often partly parasitic on the roots of grasses; known as Indian paintbrush.
genus of partly parasitic plants (Indian paintbrush)

Examples

The wildflower Castilleja is commonly known for its vibrant red and orange bracts.
Many species of Castilleja are native to high-altitude meadows and prairies.
Ecologists study how Castilleja species interact with surrounding grasses in their ecosystem.

Etymology

Named after Domingo Castillejo, an 18th-century Spanish botanist.
