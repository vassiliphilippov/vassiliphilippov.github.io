Centaurium calycosum

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔːriəm ˌkælɪˈkoʊsəm / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪəm ˌkælɪˈkəʊsəm / (BrE)
An erect plant with small clusters of pink, trumpet-shaped flowers, native to the southwestern United States.
species of plant with pink, trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

Centaurium calycosum thrives in arid regions with well-drained soils and plenty of sunlight.
The delicate flowers of Centaurium calycosum appear in clusters during the spring and attract pollinators.
Botanists surveyed the landscape to document native species, including Centaurium calycosum.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Centaurium, species calycosum.
