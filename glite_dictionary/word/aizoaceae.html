Aizoaceae

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌzoʊˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / aɪˌzəʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of succulent herbs or small shrubs, mainly found in South Africa, including the carpetweeds and fig marigolds
family of succulent plants, mostly South African

Examples

The Aizoaceae family is known for its drought-resistant, succulent plants.
Many species in the Aizoaceae are cultivated as ornamental ground covers.
Collectors of rare succulents often seek out unusual members of the Aizoaceae.

Etymology

From modern Latin Aizoaceae, based on the type genus Aizoon.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˌzoʊˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / aɪˌzəʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of succulent herbs or small shrubs, chiefly native to South Africa but also found in New Zealand and North America, which includes carpetweeds and fig marigolds
family of succulent herbs, mostly South African

Examples

Many species in the Aizoaceae family are grown as ornamental plants due to their colorful flowers.
Succulents from the Aizoaceae family thrive in arid environments because they can store water in their leaves.
The botanical garden has a special section devoted to members of the Aizoaceae.

Etymology

From the type genus Aizoon, from Greek 'aizóōn' meaning 'ever-living.'
