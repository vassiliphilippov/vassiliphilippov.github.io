Nelumbo lutea

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈlʌmboʊ luˈtiə / (AmE), / nɪˈlʌmbəʊ luːˈtiːə / (BrE)
A water lily native to eastern North America, known for its pale yellow flowers and edible, nut-like seeds.
North American water lily species

Examples

The pond was covered in beautiful Nelumbo lutea blossoms in early summer.
Native Americans traditionally harvested the seeds of Nelumbo lutea for food.
We identified the plant as Nelumbo lutea by its pale yellow flowers and large leaves.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Nelumbo nucifera - Asian counterpart, the sacred lotus

Etymology

From Latin 'Nelumbo', the genus name, and 'lutea', meaning 'yellow'.
