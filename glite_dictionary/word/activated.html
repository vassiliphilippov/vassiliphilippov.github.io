activated

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.təˌveɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a military unit or device) set up and placed on active duty or made operational.
placed on active duty or made operational

Examples

The activated unit responded quickly to the emergency call.
Once activated, the system continuously monitors for threats.
Three activated brigades will be deployed by the end of the month.

Synset Examples

a newly activated unit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deactivated - made inactive or taken off active status

Etymology

From the verb 'activate', formed from Latin 'activare' (to make active), based on 'activus' (active).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tɪˌveɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
made active or rendered capable of functioning, especially by chemical, physical, or biological means (such as being made radioactive, luminescent, or conductive).
made active by physical, chemical or biological means

Examples

After exposure to the radiation, the material became activated and needed special handling.
The sensor only works when it is activated by a light source.
Certain enzymes are activated in response to high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deactivated - made inactive or rendered unable to function

3. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tɪˌveɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of sewage or sludge) treated by aeration and the addition of bacteria to accelerate decomposition of organic matter.
sewage treated with bacteria and aeration
medical

Examples

The activated sludge process is widely used for wastewater treatment in large cities.
After being activated, the sewage undergoes further processing to remove harmful substances.
During the cleaning process, activated biosolids help break down organic pollutants efficiently.

4. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a molecule or substance) made reactive or more reactive, especially as the result of a chemical or physical process.
made reactive or more reactive

Examples

The enzyme becomes activated only when it binds to its specific substrate.
Activated charcoal is often used to remove toxins from the body.
A photoreceptor is activated by exposure to light, triggering a nerve signal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inert - lacking the ability to react chemically
  • Synonym: reactive - tending to react chemically

Etymology

From activate + -ed, meaning "made active".
