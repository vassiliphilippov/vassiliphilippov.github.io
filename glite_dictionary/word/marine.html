Marine

1. NOUN

/ məˈrin / (AmE), / məˈriːn / (BrE)
a member of a branch of the United States armed forces trained for service on land and at sea, especially as part of the United States Marine Corps
member of the US Marine Corps

Examples

His son is a proud Marine serving overseas.
After years as a Marine, she found civilian life quite different.
The Marine saluted the flag during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army
  • Antonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship

Etymology

From Latin marinus (of the sea); in this sense, specifically used for corps members since the 18th century.
