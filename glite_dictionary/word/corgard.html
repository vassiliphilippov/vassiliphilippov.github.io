Corgard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
A trademarked beta-adrenergic blocking drug (nadolol) used to treat high blood pressure and angina.
beta-blocker drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Corgard to help manage her hypertension.
Patients with angina may benefit from taking Corgard regularly as directed.
If you are allergic to beta-blockers, inform your physician before starting Corgard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nadolol - the generic drug name for Corgard
