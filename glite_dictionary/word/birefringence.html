birefringence

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪrəˈfrɪndʒəns / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.rɪˈfrɪn.dʒəns / (BrE)
the property of a material that causes a ray of light passing through it to split into two rays polarized at right angles to each other
optical property causing light to split into two rays

Examples

Calcite crystals exhibit strong birefringence, which makes them useful for optical experiments.
The birefringence of the sample was measured to determine its internal structure.
Polarized light microscopy reveals birefringence in muscle fibers and other biological tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double refraction - the splitting of a ray of light into two rays as it passes through certain types of material
  • Antonym: isotropy - the property of being uniform in all directions (opposite of birefringence in materials)

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'refringere' (to break up), referring to double refraction.
