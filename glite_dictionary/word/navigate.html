to navigate

1. VERB

/ ˈnævəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnævɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to travel in a boat or ship, especially by steering or controlling its course, often on water
to steer or travel in a boat or ship

Examples

The captain will navigate the ship through the narrow channel at dawn.
They learned how to navigate the river using only a compass and a map.
Modern sailors use GPS to navigate their vessels across the open sea.

Synset Examples

The QE2 will sail to Southampton tomorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sail - to travel in a boat with sails, especially as a sport or pastime
  • Synonym: steer - to guide or control the course of a vehicle, vessel, or aircraft

Etymology

From Latin navigare, from navis (ship) + agere (to drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01850262-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnævɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnavɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to act as the navigator in a vehicle such as a car, plane, or ship by planning and directing its route and position
direct a vehicle's course as navigator

Examples

Could you navigate while I drive through the city?
He learned to navigate ships across difficult waters during his training.
She offered to navigate the route when we traveled through the mountains.

Synset Examples

Is anyone volunteering to navigate during the trip?
Who was navigating the ship during the accident?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - to operate and control the course of an aircraft or boat

Etymology

From Latin navigare (to sail), from navis (ship) + agere (to drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01937276-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnævəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnævɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to direct the course of, especially carefully or safely; to find or plan the way to a place or through a situation.
direct carefully and safely

Examples

She learned how to navigate busy city streets on her own.
The captain skillfully navigated the ship through the storm.
He had to navigate a complicated series of interviews to get the job.

Synset Examples

He navigated his way to the altar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steer - to guide the path or course of a vehicle or vessel
  • Synonym: pilot - to act as the person in control of a plane or boat
  • Antonym: to misdirect - to guide wrongly or incorrectly

Etymology

From Latin 'navigare' (to sail), from 'navis' (ship) + 'agere' (to drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01936675-v
Synset POS: v
