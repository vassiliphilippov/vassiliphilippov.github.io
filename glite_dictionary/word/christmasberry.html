Christmasberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsməs ˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsməs ˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub of the Pacific coast of the United States, with large white flowers and red, berry-like fruits, often grown as an ornamental plant
ornamental shrub with red berry-like fruits

Examples

The garden was brightened by a row of Christmasberry shrubs, their red fruit brilliant against the green leaves.
Many landscapers plant Christmasberry along fences for its colorful appeal in winter.
The Christmasberry is prized for its ability to attract birds to the garden.

Etymology

Refers to the plant's red fruits, which resemble berries used in Christmas decorations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsməsˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsməsˌbɛri / (BrE)
A spiny evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, bearing blue or mauve flowers and red berries.
spiny evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The Christmasberry brightens the winter garden with its vibrant red fruit.
Birds are attracted to the Christmasberry for its colorful berries in late autumn.
Gardeners often plant Christmasberry shrubs for ornamental purposes and wildlife appeal.
