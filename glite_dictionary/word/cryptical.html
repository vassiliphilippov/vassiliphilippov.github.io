cryptical

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptɪkəl / (BrE)
having a secret or hidden meaning; mysterious or obscure in meaning
having a secret or hidden meaning

Examples

The lecturer's cryptical remarks left many students confused about the main point.
She sent me a cryptical message that took hours to interpret.
The old manuscript was filled with cryptical references to alchemy and lost sciences.

Synset Examples

cabalistic symbols engraved in stone
cryptic writings
thoroughly sibylline in most of his pronouncements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) via 'cryptic' + -al suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptɪkəl / (BrE)
having a meaning that is obscure, mysterious, or hard to understand; enigmatic.
obscure or mysterious in nature

Examples

His explanation was so cryptical that nobody understood what he meant.
The cryptical clues in the crossword puzzled even experienced solvers.
She gave me a cryptical smile, hinting at a secret I couldn't grasp.

Synset Examples

the new insurance policy is written without cryptic or mysterious terms
a deep dark secret
the inscrutable workings of Providence
in its mysterious past it encompasses all the dim origins of life
rituals totally mystifying to visitors from other lands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure
  • Synonym: inscrutable - impossible to understand or interpret
  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion

Etymology

From Greek kryptikos (fit for concealing), via 'cryptic' + suffix -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00942713-s
Synset POS: s
