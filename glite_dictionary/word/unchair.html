to unchair

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtʃɛr / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃɛː / (BrE)
to remove someone from the position of chairperson or a position of authority
remove from position of chairperson

Examples

After the scandal, the board voted to unchair the president immediately.
A heated debate erupted after members moved to unchair the committee leader.
It is difficult to unchair someone who has strong support from the members.

Synset Examples

The decision to unchair her sparked controversy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dethrone - remove from a position of power or authority
  • Synonym: to depose - remove from office suddenly and forcefully

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'chair' (to serve as chairperson).
