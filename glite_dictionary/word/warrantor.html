warrantor

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrənˌtɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɒrənˌtɔː / (BrE)
a person or party who gives a formal guarantee, especially in a legal contract, that certain facts or conditions are true, or undertakes to remedy any defects
person who gives a formal guarantee
legal

Examples

The company acted as a warrantor of the product's quality and offered a full refund if it failed.
As the warrantor in the agreement, she was responsible for any breach of warranty.
Each warrantor must provide written proof of the guarantee to the customer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantor - person who guarantees to pay a debt if the original borrower defaults

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French garantor, from garantir (to guarantee).
