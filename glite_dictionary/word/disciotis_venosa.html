Disciotis venosa

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsaɪˈoʊtɪs vɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / dɪˌsaɪˈəʊtɪs vɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
a species of edible cup fungus with a wrinkled, cup- or saucer-shaped fruiting body up to 20 cm wide, resembling some inedible mushrooms.
a species of edible cup fungus

Examples

The forager was delighted to find a patch of Disciotis venosa in the damp woodland.
You should be cautious when collecting Disciotis venosa because it can be mistaken for toxic fungi.
The fruiting body of Disciotis venosa has distinctive wrinkles that radiate from the center of the cup.

Etymology

From modern Latin, with 'Disciotis' referring to the genus and 'venosa' meaning 'veined' in Latin.
