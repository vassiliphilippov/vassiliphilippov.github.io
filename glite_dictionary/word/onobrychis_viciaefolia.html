Onobrychis viciaefolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.nəˈbraɪ.kɪs ˌvɪ.si.əˈfoʊl.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌɒn.əˈbraɪ.kɪs ˌvɪ.si.əˈfəʊl.i.ə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb with pale pink flowers and curved pods, cultivated as a forage crop and honey source, especially in Britain and North America.
Eurasian perennial forage herb

Examples

Onobrychis viciaefolia is widely grown for animal fodder due to its high nutritional value.
The fields were bright with the pale pink flowers of Onobrychis viciaefolia in early summer.
Beekeepers in the region rely on Onobrychis viciaefolia as an important source of nectar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sainfoin - Common name for Onobrychis viciaefolia, a forage crop.

Etymology

From genus name Onobrychis and species epithet viciaefolia, meaning 'having leaves like vetch' (from Latin vicia, 'vetch', and folium, 'leaf').
