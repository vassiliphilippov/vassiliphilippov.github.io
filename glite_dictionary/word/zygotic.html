zygotic

1. ADJ

/ zaɪˈɡɑːtɪk / (AmE), / zaɪˈɡɒtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a zygote, especially in reference to its formation or development.
relating to a zygote
medical

Examples

The zygotic stage is crucial for the development of multicellular organisms.
Researchers observed changes in gene expression during the zygotic phase.
The zygotic genome activation marks the beginning of embryonic transcription.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zygote - a cell formed by the union of two gametes; the fertilized egg cell

Etymology

From New Latin 'zygotes' (joined), from Greek 'zygon' (yoke).
