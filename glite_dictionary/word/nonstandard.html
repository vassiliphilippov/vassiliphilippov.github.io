nonstandard

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈstændɚd / (AmE), / nɒnˈstændəd / (BrE)
(of language or usage) not conforming to the established or accepted rules or forms of a language, especially as used by a prestige group within a community.
not conforming to standard language use

Examples

Some teachers discourage the use of nonstandard grammar in formal writing.
Her accent features several nonstandard pronunciations common in the local dialect.
Linguists study both standard and nonstandard varieties of English.

Synset Examples

a nonstandard dialect is one used by uneducated speakers or socially disfavored groups
the common core of nonstandard words and phrases in folk speech

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard - conforming to established language norms
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02305966-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈstændɚd / (AmE), / nɒnˈstændəd / (BrE)
not conforming to an accepted standard or norm, especially in form, quality, or usage.
not conforming to a standard

Examples

Some airlines charge extra fees for nonstandard luggage sizes.
The architect designed a building with several nonstandard window shapes.
Using nonstandard parts can lead to compatibility issues in manufacturing.

Synset Examples

nonstandard windows
envelopes of nonstandard sizes
nonstandard lengths of board

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard - conforming to a recognized norm or model
  • Synonym: atypical - not representative of a type, group, or class

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'standard'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02304841-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ nɑnˈstændɚd / (AmE), / nɒnˈstændəd / (BrE)
not conforming to the accepted or established standard; not regarded as a model of excellence or correctness.
not conforming to the accepted standard

Examples

The report was rejected because it used nonstandard formatting throughout.
They spoke in a nonstandard dialect that was unfamiliar to many listeners.
This equipment requires a nonstandard power adapter, which may not be easy to find.

Synset Examples

a nonstandard text

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard - conforming to the recognized norm or model

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'standard' (an accepted norm or model).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02303542-a
Synset POS: a
