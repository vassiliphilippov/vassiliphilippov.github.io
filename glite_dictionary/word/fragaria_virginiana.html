Fragaria virginiana

1. NOUN

/ frəˈgɛriə vɚˌdʒɪniˈænə / (AmE), / frəˈɡɑːriə vɜːˌdʒɪnɪˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of wild strawberry native to North America, noted for its sweet scarlet fruit and as a source of cultivated strawberries.
North American wild strawberry species

Examples

Fragaria virginiana can often be found growing wild along forest edges throughout North America.
The sweet fruit of Fragaria virginiana was enjoyed by Indigenous peoples long before the modern strawberry was cultivated.
Many hybrid strawberries owe their hardiness and flavor to Fragaria virginiana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild strawberry - any uncultivated species of strawberry, especially those native to North America or Europe
  • Degree Higher: cultivated strawberry - the common garden strawberry, bred from wild species including Fragaria virginiana

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fragaria' meaning 'strawberry', and 'virginiana' referring to Virginia, U.S.A., indicating its origin.
