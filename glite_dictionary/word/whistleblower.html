whistleblower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪsəlˌbloʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəlˌbləʊə / (BrE)
A person who exposes secret or illegal activity within an organization, typically to stop wrongdoing.
person who exposes organizational misconduct

Examples

The whistleblower submitted documents to the authorities revealing financial fraud in the company.
The government enacted new laws to protect whistleblowers from retaliation.
After raising safety concerns, the whistleblower lost her job at the factory.

Synset Examples

the law gives little protection to whistleblowers who feel the public has a right to know what is going on
the whistleblower was fired for exposing the conditions in mental hospitals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - a person who provides information, especially to authorities
  • Synonym: accuser - a person who claims someone has done something wrong
  • Antonym: perpetrator - a person who commits a wrongdoing

Etymology

Formed from 'whistle' and 'blower,' alluding to someone who blows a whistle to draw attention to wrongdoing, similar to a referee in sports.
