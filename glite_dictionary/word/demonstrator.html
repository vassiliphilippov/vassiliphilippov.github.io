demonstrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmənˌstreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛmənstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to show or explain how a product works to potential customers
person showing how a product works

Examples

A demonstrator showed the new vacuum cleaner's features at the department store.
The company hired a demonstrator to attract customers at the trade fair.
She worked as a demonstrator, explaining the benefits of kitchen appliances to shoppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salesperson - someone who sells goods or services

Etymology

From Latin demonstrare (to show, point out).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmənˌstreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɛmənstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person, especially a teacher or assistant, who shows students how something works or how to do a particular activity in an educational setting
person who demonstrates in teaching

Examples

The biology demonstrator prepared the experiment for the students in the laboratory.
As a demonstrator, she carefully explained each step of the dissection to the class.
The university hired a new demonstrator to assist with the physics practicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructor - someone who teaches or instructs, especially in a practical subject
  • Synonym: lecturer - someone who gives lectures, especially in a college or university

Etymology

From Latin 'dēmōnstrātor' (one who points out), from 'dēmōnstrāre' (to demonstrate).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmənˌstreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛmənstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a public protest or march to show support for or opposition to something
person who protests or marches publicly

Examples

Thousands of demonstrators gathered in the city square demanding change.
Police peacefully dispersed the group of demonstrators blocking the main road.
Several demonstrators carried banners and chanted slogans during the march.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protester - a person who publicly expresses strong objection to something
  • Degree Lower: supporter - a person who backs or advocates for a cause, often without public protest

Etymology

From demonstrate + -or, modeled after Latin agent nouns.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
