Holocephali

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊloʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌhɒləʊˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A subclass of cartilaginous fishes that includes chimaeras and their extinct relatives.
subclass of cartilaginous fishes

Examples

Fossil records show that Holocephali have existed since the Devonian period.
Scientists study the unique anatomy of Holocephali to understand the evolution of cartilaginous fishes.
Modern chimaeras are the only living members of the subclass Holocephali.

Etymology

From Greek holo- (whole) + kephalē (head), referring to the fused upper jaw and skull.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊloʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.ə(ʊ)ˈsɛf.ə.laɪ / (BrE)
A subclass of cartilaginous fishes that includes the chimaeras and their extinct relatives.
subclass of cartilaginous fishes (chimaeras, extinct forms)

Examples

The Holocephali are represented today only by the chimaeras, sometimes called ghost sharks.
Fossil records show that Holocephali were much more diverse during the Paleozoic era.
Scientists study the evolution of Holocephali to understand early vertebrate development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimaera - a group of marine cartilaginous fish included in Holocephali

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + kephalē (head), referring to the fused head structure.
