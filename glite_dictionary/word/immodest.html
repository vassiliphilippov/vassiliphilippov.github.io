immodest

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɑdəst / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdɪst / (BrE)
having or showing an exaggerated opinion of your own importance, ability, etc.; lacking humility or modesty
lacking modesty; boastful

Examples

His immodest claims of success made him unpopular with colleagues.
She gave an immodest account of her achievements during the interview.
The athlete's immodest boasting irritated fans and opponents alike.

Synset Examples

brash immodest boasting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modest - having a humble opinion of one's own importance
  • Synonym: conceited - having an excessively high opinion of oneself

Etymology

From Latin immodestus, meaning 'immoderate, excessive'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɑdəst / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdɪst / (BrE)
lacking in decency or propriety; not conforming to accepted standards of behavior or dress, especially in regard to modesty
lacking decency or propriety

Examples

Some people considered her dress immodest at the conservative event.
It would be immodest of me to claim all the credit for this project.
His immodest remarks offended several members of the audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modest - having or showing a moderate or humble estimate of one's merits; not showy
  • Synonym: indecent - not conforming with generally accepted standards of behavior or propriety; obscene

Etymology

From Latin immodestus (immoderate, shameless), from in- (not) + modestus (moderate, modest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01543026-a
Synset POS: a
