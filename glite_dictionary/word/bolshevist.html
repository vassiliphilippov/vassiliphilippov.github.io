Bolshevist

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃɪˌvɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Bolshevism or the Bolsheviks.
relating to Bolshevism or the Bolsheviks

Examples

The Bolshevist government implemented sweeping changes across Russia after the revolution.
Many intellectuals were accused of having Bolshevist sympathies in the early twentieth century.
The film depicts the rise of Bolshevist ideology during the Russian Civil War.

Synset Examples

Bolshevik Revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bolshevik - A member of the majority faction of the Russian Social Democratic Party, later the Communist Party

Etymology

From Russian bol'shevik (member of the majority), after bol'shoy (large, majority).

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəˌvɪst / (BrE)
A member of the Bolshevik party, the left-wing majority group that followed Lenin and became the Russian Communist Party.
member of the Bolshevik party (Lenin's followers)

Examples

After the revolution, the Bolshevist agenda quickly transformed Russian society.
He studied the theories and tactics of the Bolshevists in political science class.
The rise of the Bolshevist movement dramatically influenced global politics in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bolshevik - Member of Leninist Russian Communist party; variant form or synonym

Etymology

From Russian 'bol'shevik' (majority), from 'bol'shoy' (great).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09883342-n
Synset POS: n
