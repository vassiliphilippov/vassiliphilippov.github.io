spiller

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪlər / (AmE), / ˈspɪlə / (BrE)
a long fishing line with many shorter lines and hooks attached, usually suspended between buoys for catching fish
long fishing line with attached short lines and hooks

Examples

The fishermen checked their spiller early in the morning for a fresh catch.
A spiller can cover a wide area when suspended between buoys.
He learned to rig a spiller from his grandfather on weekends.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɪlə / (BrE)
a person who sheds or spills blood, often in the context of violence or hunting
one who sheds or spills blood

Examples

The notorious spiller of blood was feared throughout the kingdom.
Legends spoke of a spiller whose blade had never missed its mark.
As a hunter, he was known as a ruthless spiller of animal blood.

Synset Examples

a great hunter and spiller of blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killer - a person or thing that kills

Etymology

From the verb 'spill', meaning to cause or allow (a liquid) to flow over the edge, ultimately from Old English 'spillan'.
