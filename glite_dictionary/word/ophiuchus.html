Ophiuchus

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊ.fiˈjuː.kəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.fɪˈjuː.kəs / (BrE)
A large constellation in the equatorial region of the sky, situated between Hercules and Scorpius.
constellation between Hercules and Scorpius

Examples

The constellation Ophiuchus is sometimes referred to as the thirteenth sign of the zodiac.
Many bright stars can be seen in Ophiuchus during the summer months in the Northern Hemisphere.
Astronomers observe Ophiuchus to study a variety of interesting stellar phenomena.

Etymology

From Latin Ophiūchus, from Greek Ophioukhos meaning 'serpent-bearer'.
