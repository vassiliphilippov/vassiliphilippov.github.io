solacement

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɒl.əs.mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪs.mənt / (BrE)
comfort or consolation given in times of disappointment or misery
comfort in distress
archaic

Examples

She found solacement in her friends after the passing of her beloved dog.
The quiet of the garden brought him solacement during his troubled times.
Literature often provides solacement to those enduring hardship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solace - comfort or consolation in a time of distress or sadness
  • Synonym: consolation - comfort received by a person after a loss or disappointment

Etymology

From Middle English, based on 'solace' from Old French 'solas', from Latin 'solacium' (comfort).
