II Timothy

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈtɪməθi / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈtɪməθi / (BrE)
the second book in the New Testament addressed by Saint Paul to Timothy, containing advice on church leadership and pastoral care
New Testament book; Paul's second letter to Timothy

Examples

Many pastors look to II Timothy for guidance on spiritual leadership.
The letter of II Timothy is often cited for its advice on perseverance and faith.
In Bible study, we discussed themes from II Timothy last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: I Timothy - the first epistle of Paul to Timothy in the New Testament

Etymology

From Latin 'Timotheus', from Greek 'Timótheos', meaning 'honoring God'. The Roman numeral II indicates it is the second such letter.
