Stalinist

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑː.lə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈstɑː.lɪ.nɪst / (BrE)
Relating to Joseph Stalin, his policies, or the period when he was leader of the Soviet Union.
relating to Stalin or his era

Examples

The country adopted Stalinist policies of centralized control and repression.
Her research focuses on Stalinist purges during the 1930s.
The architecture of the era features the characteristic Stalinist style.

Etymology

From Stalin, the surname of Joseph Stalin (Soviet leader 1878–1953) + -ist.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02813299-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːlɪnɪst / (AmE), / ˈstælɪnɪst / (BrE)
a supporter or follower of Joseph Stalin or the principles of Stalinism, especially relating to Soviet politics and ideology
supporter of Stalinism

Examples

The party split when the Stalinists clashed with those who favored reform.
As a committed Stalinist, he defended the purges of the 1930s.
Historians debate how many true Stalinists remained after his death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - a person who supports or believes in the principles of communism

Etymology

From the name of Joseph Stalin (Soviet leader from 1924 to 1953) + -ist, denoting supporter or follower.

Synset POS: n
