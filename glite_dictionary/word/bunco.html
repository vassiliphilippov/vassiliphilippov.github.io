bunco

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkəʊ / (BrE)
A swindle, especially one involving cheating at gambling or persuading someone to buy worthless property.
a swindle or con scheme

Examples

He fell victim to a classic bunco scheme and lost all his savings.
The police arrested the man for running a bunco operation targeting elderly people.
That investment offer was nothing more than a bunco designed to trick unsuspecting buyers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: con - a trick to get someone's money by deception
  • Synonym: swindle - a fraudulent scheme or scam

Etymology

From American Spanish 'banco', meaning a card game or scheme.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkəʊ / (BrE)
to swindle or cheat someone by means of deceit
swindle or cheat by deceit
slang american

Examples

The con artists tried to bunco elderly residents out of their savings.
He was arrested for attempting to bunco tourists with a fake lottery scheme.
She realized too late that she had been buncoed by a smooth-talking stranger.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property

Etymology

Possibly from Spanish "banco" (a card game, also source of 'bunco game').
