decimalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsɪməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛsɪməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting a system, especially currency or measurement, to a decimal-based system
conversion to a decimal system
british

Examples

The decimalisation of British currency occurred in 1971.
After decimalisation, prices were easier to calculate.
Many countries have undergone decimalisation of their coinage systems.

Synset Examples

the decimalization of British currency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimalization - spelling variant, especially in American English

Etymology

From decimal + -isation, after French 'décimalisation'.
