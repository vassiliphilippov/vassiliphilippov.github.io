to condone

1. VERB

/ kənˈdoʊn / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊn / (BrE)
to accept or allow behaviour that is morally wrong or offensive to continue; to overlook or forgive without protest
to overlook or allow wrongdoing

Examples

The company cannot condone any form of workplace bullying.
Many believe that ignoring the issue is the same as condoning it.
By remaining silent, the community seemed to condone the illegal activity.

Synset Examples

excuse someone's behavior
She condoned her husband's occasional infidelities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful towards someone for an offense, flaw, or mistake
  • Synonym: excuse - to seek to remove blame from someone for a fault or offense
  • Antonym: to condemn - to express complete disapproval of, typically in public

Etymology

From Latin 'condonare' (to remit, forgive), from 'con-' (altogether) + 'donare' (to give).
