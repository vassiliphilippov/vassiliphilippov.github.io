riata

1. NOUN

/ riˈɑtə / (AmE), / riˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a long rope with a noose, used especially in North America for catching cattle or horses.
long noosed rope for catching animals

Examples

The cowboy swung his riata expertly, aiming for the horse's neck.
She learned how to coil the riata after a long day on the ranch.
A well-made riata can last for many years if properly cared for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lariat - a rope used to catch animals, especially cattle and horses; similar to riata

Etymology

From Spanish 'riata', meaning lariat or rope.
