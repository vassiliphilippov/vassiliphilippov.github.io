to institutionalise

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃə.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to be placed or admitted into an institution, such as a hospital, prison, or care facility
place someone in an institution

Examples

The doctor decided to institutionalise the patient for ongoing care.
After repeated offenses, he was institutionalised by court order.
Her family struggled with the decision to institutionalise her for her own safety.

Synset Examples

After the second episode, she had to be committed
he was committed to prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commit - to send someone officially to a hospital, prison, etc.

Etymology

From institution + -alise, formed in British English (American: institutionalize).
