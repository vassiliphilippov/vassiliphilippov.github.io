fare-thee-well

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛr ði ˈwɛl / (AmE), / ˌfeə ði ˈwel / (BrE)
the highest or utmost degree; a state of perfection
utmost degree; perfection

Examples

The chef prepared the meal to a fare-thee-well, leaving nothing to chance.
Her performance was rehearsed to a fare-thee-well, flawless in every detail.
He cleaned the house to a fare-thee-well before the guests arrived.

Synset Examples

they polished the furniture to a fare-thee-well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfection - the state of being perfect or most excellent
  • Synonym: peak - the highest or maximal point of something
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the state of being average or ordinary

Etymology

Derived from the phrase 'fare thee well,' originally a wish for someone to fare well, later used idiomatically for completeness or extremity.
