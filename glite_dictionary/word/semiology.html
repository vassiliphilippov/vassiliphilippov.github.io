semiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːmiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study or theory of signs and symbols and their use or interpretation, especially in language and communication
theory of signs and symbols

Examples

Ferdinand de Saussure is often credited as a founder of semiology.
Semiology examines how meaning is constructed and understood through signs.
Her research focuses on the practical applications of semiology in mass media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semiotics - the study of signs and symbols and their use or interpretation

Etymology

From French 'sémiologie', from Greek 'sēmeion' (sign) + '-logy' (study).
