Argiope

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒaɪəpi / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒaɪəpi / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored orb-weaving spiders belonging to the family Araneidae, known for their large webs and distinctive markings.
genus of orb-weaving spiders

Examples

Researchers observed an Argiope weaving its intricate web in the garden.
The striking coloration of the Argiope makes it easy to spot among the vegetation.
An Argiope can often be identified by the zigzag pattern in the center of its web.

Etymology

From Greek Argiope, a name in Greek mythology, sometimes interpreted as 'silver-faced'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.dʒiˈoʊ.pi / (AmE), / ˌɑː.dʒiˈəʊ.pi / (BrE)
A genus of orb-weaving spiders known for their distinctive web patterns and bright markings, including the common garden spider.
genus of orb-weaving spiders

Examples

The striking yellow and black markings identify spiders of the Argiope genus.
Many gardeners welcome Argiope spiders for their role in controlling insect pests.
The web structure of an Argiope is often easily recognized by its zigzag pattern.

Etymology

From Greek 'Argiope', a mythological figure, literally meaning 'silver-faced' (argos 'shining' + ōps 'face').
