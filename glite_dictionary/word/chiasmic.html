chiasmic

1. ADJ

/ kaɪˈæzmɪk / (AmE), / kaɪˈæzmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a chiasm; especially referring to an intersection or crossing, such as in anatomical structures or rhetorical figures.
relating to a chiasm or crossing

Examples

The optic nerves meet at a chiasmic region known as the optic chiasm.
Researchers observed chiasmic patterns in the genetic crossover processes.
The chiasmic structure in the poem brings balance and symmetry to its verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chiasmatic - relating to a chiasm

Etymology

From chiasm (from Greek 'khiásma' – a crossing) + -ic.
