evensong

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːvənˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːvənˌsɒŋ / (BrE)
A daily evening service of prayers and psalms, especially in the Anglican Church, typically following a set liturgy.
Anglican evening prayer service

Examples

We attended evensong at the cathedral last Sunday evening.
The choir beautifully sang hymns during evensong.
Many visitors find the peaceful atmosphere of evensong comforting and inspiring.

Etymology

From Middle English 'evensonge' (evening song), referring to the evening service.

2. NOUN

/ ˈivənˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːvənˌsɒŋ / (BrE)
a Christian service held in the early evening, especially the Anglican service of evening prayers, psalms, and canticles; originally the sixth of the seven canonical hours.
evening Christian service (esp. Anglican)

Examples

The choir performed evensong beautifully in the historic cathedral.
Many visitors attend evensong on Sundays for its peaceful atmosphere and choral music.
He found comfort in attending evensong after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vespers - evening prayer service in the Christian Church

Etymology

From Old English æfnung (evening), influenced by 'song' for musical services.
