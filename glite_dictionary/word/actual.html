actual

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
existing in fact; real and not merely possible, imagined, or supposed
real; existing in fact

Examples

The actual cost was much higher than we expected.
Despite rumors, the actual story was quite different.
We finally saw the actual painting in the museum.

Synset Examples

rocks and trees … the actual world
actual heroism
the actual things that produced the emotion you experienced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: real - actually existing or occurring; not imagined or supposed
  • Antonym: imaginary - existing only in the imagination; not real

Etymology

From Latin 'actualis' (active, practical), from 'actus' (act, deed).
