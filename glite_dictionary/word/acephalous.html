acephalous

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / eɪˈsiːfələs / (BrE)
Lacking a head or a distinct head; headless.
lacking a head or distinct head

Examples

Some species of worms are described as acephalous because they lack a distinctive head region.
The fossil displayed an acephalous body, making it difficult for scientists to classify.
In certain literature, the term acephalous is used metaphorically for organizations without clear leadership.

Synset Examples

acephalous worms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headless - lacking a head; without a head

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + kephalē (head).
