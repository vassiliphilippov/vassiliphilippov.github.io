Ramadan

1. NOUN

/ ˈræməˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈræmədæn / (BrE)
The ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims as a month of fasting from sunrise to sunset, prayer, reflection, and community.
Muslim month of fasting

Examples

Millions of Muslims around the world observe Ramadan by fasting during daylight hours.
During Ramadan, families gather each evening to break their fast together.
Schools sometimes modify schedules to accommodate students participating in Ramadan.

Etymology

From Arabic 'ramaḍān', the ninth month of the Islamic year.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræməˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈræmədæn / (BrE)
the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims as a month of fasting, prayer, and reflection; regarded as the holiest month in Islam.
the Islamic month of fasting

Examples

During Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn until sunset each day.
The city organizes special events and prayers throughout Ramadan.
Many people donate to charity during Ramadan as an act of goodwill.

Etymology

From Arabic 'Ramaḍān', the ninth month of the Muslim year.
