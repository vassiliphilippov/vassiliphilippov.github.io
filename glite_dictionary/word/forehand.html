forehand

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːhænd / (BrE)
(in sports) a stroke in which the palm of the hand faces in the direction of the stroke, especially in tennis, badminton, or squash.
sports stroke with palm facing forward

Examples

He hit the ball across the court with a powerful forehand.
Her forehand is the strongest part of her game.
To improve your forehand, focus on your grip and wrist movement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: backhand - a stroke made with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke, as in tennis

Etymology

From 'fore-' (before) + 'hand', indicating the front of the hand.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌhænd / (BrE)
(of a racket stroke) performed with the palm of the hand facing in the direction of the stroke.
stroke with palm facing forward

Examples

He hit a powerful forehand shot that landed just inside the baseline.
Her forehand return was faster and more accurate than her backhand.
The coach suggested he improve his forehand technique for better control.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: backhand - (of a racket stroke) made with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke

Etymology

From 'fore-' (before, in front) + 'hand', referring to the front or palm side of the hand.
