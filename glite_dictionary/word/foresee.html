to foresee

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈsi / (AmE), / fɔːˈsiː / (BrE)
to realize or know about something before it happens; to anticipate
predict or anticipate

Examples

It is difficult to foresee how technology will change our lives in the next decade.
No one could foresee that the peaceful protest would turn violent.
She foresaw the potential risks in investing in that company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something
  • Synonym: to predict - to say what will happen before it occurs
  • Antonym: to ignore - to fail to acknowledge or consider

Etymology

From Latin 'praevidere', from 'prae-' (before) + 'videre' (to see).

2. VERB

/ fɔrˈsi / (AmE), / fɔːˈsiː / (BrE)
to be aware of or anticipate something before it happens; to predict or expect in advance
anticipate or predict in advance

Examples

It is difficult to foresee all the challenges we might encounter in this project.
The company failed to foresee the sudden change in consumer preferences.
Scientists try to foresee potential risks related to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something
  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future

Etymology

From Old French for-, fore- (before) + see; influenced by Latin 'prǒvidēre' (to look ahead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02571406-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ fɔrˈsi / (AmE), / fɔːˈsiː / (BrE)
to imagine or picture something as a possible or likely outcome; to envision
to imagine as possible; to envision

Examples

It is difficult to foresee how technology will shape our lives in the next decade.
She could not foresee any major problems arising from the plan.
No one could foresee the dramatic changes that would come so quickly.

Synset Examples

I cannot envision him as President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to envision - to picture something in your mind
  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something
  • Synonym: to predict - to say what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Old French 'forseoir', from Latin 'prævidēre' (to see beforehand).
