acantha

1. NOUN

/ əˈkænθə / (AmE), / əˈkanθə / (BrE)
A sharply pointed projection, especially one found in the anatomy of animals or plants.
sharply pointed projection (anatomy/biology)
medical

Examples

The hedgehog's back is covered with acantha that protect it from predators.
Researchers studied the fish's acantha under a microscope to observe its structure.
Certain cacti have an acantha at the end of each stem, deterring herbivores.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine).
