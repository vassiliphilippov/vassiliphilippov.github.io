eusporangiate

1. ADJ

/ juːˌspɔːrændʒiˈeɪt / (AmE), / juːˌspɒrændʒɪˈeɪt / (BrE)
(of ferns) having sporangia that develop from a group of epidermal cells, rather than from a single cell.
describes ferns with sporangia from multiple epidermal cells
medical

Examples

Many primitive ferns are classified as eusporangiate due to the structure of their sporangia.
The Ophioglossaceae family consists of eusporangiate ferns with distinctive reproductive features.
Researchers studied the development of eusporangiate sporangia in early-diverging fern lineages.

Synset Examples

eusporangiate ferns of the families Ophioglossaceae and Marattiaceae

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leptosporangiate - having sporangia that arise from a single initial cell (type of fern)

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' meaning 'good' or 'well' and 'sporangium', referring to a well-developed sporangium.
