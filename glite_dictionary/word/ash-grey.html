ash-grey

1. ADJ

/ ˈæʃˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʃˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a pale or light grey colour, resembling the colour of ashes
pale grey colour

Examples

The sky was an ash-grey after the storm had passed.
She chose an ash-grey suit for the interview, seeking a neutral look.
The walls were painted in a subtle ash-grey to create a calm atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey - colour between black and white

Etymology

From 'ash' (the remains of something burned) and 'grey', referring to the typical colour of ashes.
