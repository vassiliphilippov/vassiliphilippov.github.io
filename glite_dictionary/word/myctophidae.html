Myctophidae

1. NOUN

/ maɪkˈtɑːfəˌdiː / (AmE), / mɪkˈtɒfɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, deep-sea fishes known as lanternfishes, characterized by light-producing organs along their bodies.
family of deep-sea lanternfishes
medical

Examples

The Myctophidae are among the most abundant fishes in the world's oceans, especially in deep-sea environments.
Scientists study Myctophidae to understand their role in marine food webs and bioluminescence.
A variety of Myctophidae species migrate from the depths at night to feed near the ocean surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanternfish - common name for fishes in the family Myctophidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Myctophidae, based on Greek myktēr (snout) and -phidae (used in taxonomic family names).

2. NOUN

/ maɪkˈtɑːfəˌdiː / (AmE), / mɪkˈtɒfɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, deep-sea fishes commonly known as lanternfishes, notable for their light-producing organs.
family of lanternfishes

Examples

Most species in the Myctophidae family use bioluminescence to help them survive in deep ocean waters.
Researchers found several unidentified Myctophidae specimens during their expedition.
The distinctive glow of Myctophidae makes them easy to spot in the dark ocean depths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanternfish - small deep-sea fish with light organs belonging to the family Myctophidae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek myktē (nose) and phos (light), due to their luminous organs.
