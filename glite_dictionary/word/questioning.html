questioning

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɛs.tʃə.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing or showing doubt or uncertainty; inclined to ask questions or challenge information
expressing doubt or uncertainty

Examples

She gave him a questioning look, unsure if he was telling the truth.
The journalist adopted a questioning stance during the interview.
His questioning mind always seeks evidence before accepting a claim.

Synset Examples

a skeptical attitude
a skeptical listener

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - having or expressing doubt; not easily convinced
  • Antonym: credulous - willing to believe or trust too readily

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛs.tʃə.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of asking questions; a process or period during which questions are asked to gather information.
the act of asking questions

Examples

The detective's questioning revealed several inconsistencies in the suspect's story.
After hours of questioning, the witness finally recalled a crucial detail.
His persistent questioning made everyone uncomfortable during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogation - formal or systematic questioning, especially by authorities

Etymology

From the verb 'question,' which comes from Old French 'question,' from Latin 'quaestio' (seeking, inquiry).
