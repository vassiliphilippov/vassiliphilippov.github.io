haricot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhærɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhæ.rɪ.kəʊ / (BrE)
A variety of bean, especially a French green bean or its edible seed, often used in cooking.
French variety of bean or its seed

Examples

The soup was flavored with haricots and fresh herbs picked from the garden.
She prepared a dish of sautéed haricots for the dinner party.
In the market, I found fresh haricot beans perfect for making cassoulet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - an edible seed or pod of various plants of the legume family

Etymology

From French 'haricot' (bean).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhærɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈharɪkəʊ / (BrE)
A small, usually light-colored bean of a variety often used dried in cooking, such as in French cuisine.
variety of small edible bean

Examples

The traditional French dish cassoulet features haricot beans as a main ingredient.
Soak the haricot beans overnight before adding them to the stew.
We bought a pound of dried haricot beans at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various plants of the legume family

Etymology

From French 'haricot', meaning 'bean'.
