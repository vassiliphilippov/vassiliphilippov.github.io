dyspeptic

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from indigestion or digestive discomfort
person with indigestion
medical

Examples

The dyspeptic complained bitterly about every meal he ate.
During the medical seminar, a dyspeptic described his daily struggles with digestion.
In the novel, the author portrays one character as a perpetual dyspeptic who is never satisfied with his food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalid - a person made weak or disabled by illness or injury

Etymology

From Greek dyspeptos (hard to digest), from dys- (bad) + peptos (digested).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from or related to indigestion (dyspepsia).
suffering from indigestion
medical

Examples

After a heavy meal, he felt quite dyspeptic for the rest of the evening.
The dyspeptic patient described his discomfort to the doctor.
Certain foods can trigger dyspeptic symptoms in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigestion - discomfort or pain in the stomach associated with difficulty in digesting food

Etymology

From Greek 'dyspeptos' (hard to digest), from 'dys-' (bad) + 'peptos' (digested).

3. ADJ

/ dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (BrE)
Irritable or bad-tempered, as if suffering from indigestion.
irritable or bad-tempered

Examples

After missing breakfast, he grew increasingly dyspeptic as the morning wore on.
Her dyspeptic mood made conversation difficult during the meeting.
The critic’s dyspeptic remarks soured the atmosphere at the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: amiable - having a friendly and pleasant manner

Etymology

From Greek dyspeptos, from dys- (bad) + peptos (digested).
