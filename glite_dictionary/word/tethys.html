Tethys

1. PROPN

/ ˈtiːθɪs / (AmE), / ˈtiːθɪs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a Titaness and sea goddess, the wife of Oceanus.
Titaness and sea goddess in Greek mythology

Examples

According to myth, Tethys was the mother of many river gods and ocean nymphs.
In ancient texts, Tethys is often associated with the nourishment of the earth's waters.
Artists have long depicted Tethys as a majestic figure alongside Oceanus in classical art.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Τηθύς (Tēthýs).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːθɪs / (AmE), / ˈtiːθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sea slugs in the family Aplysiidae, comprising marine mollusks notable for their soft bodies and often large size.
genus of sea slugs in Aplysiidae

Examples

The genus Tethys includes several strikingly large sea slugs found in warm waters.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Tethys genus off the Mediterranean coast.
The study focused on the feeding habits of Tethys and related aplysiid mollusks.

Etymology

From Greek Tēthys, the name of a sea goddess in mythology.
