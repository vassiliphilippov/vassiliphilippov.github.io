os tarsi fibulare

1. NOUN

/ ɑs ˈtɑrsi fɪˈbjʊləri / (AmE), / ɒs ˈtɑːsi fɪˈbjʊləri / (BrE)
the largest tarsal bone in the foot, forming the human heel
largest heel bone (calcaneus)
medical

Examples

A fracture of the os tarsi fibulare often results from a fall or car accident.
An X-ray confirmed damage to the os tarsi fibulare in her left foot.
The surgeon explained that the os tarsi fibulare supports much of the body’s weight when standing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcaneus - the large bone forming the heel of the foot

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'fibular tarsal bone'.
