myocarditis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˌkɑrˈdaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪəkɑːˈdaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the muscular tissue of the heart (myocardium), often caused by infection or immune response.
inflammation of heart muscle
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that myocarditis could cause chest pain and irregular heartbeat.
After his viral infection, he was diagnosed with myocarditis and required hospitalization.
Athletes recovering from myocarditis are often advised to avoid strenuous exercise until cleared by a cardiologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pericarditis - inflammation of the pericardium (membrane enclosing the heart)
  • Synonym: endocarditis - inflammation of the inner lining of the heart

Etymology

From Greek 'myo-' (muscle) + 'kardia' (heart) + '-itis' (inflammation).
