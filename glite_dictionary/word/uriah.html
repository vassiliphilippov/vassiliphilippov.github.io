Uriah

1. PROPN

/ jʊˈraɪə / (AmE), / jʊˈraɪə / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) the husband of Bathsheba and a soldier who was sent to die in battle by King David.
Old Testament soldier, husband of Bathsheba

Examples

The story of Uriah and Bathsheba is central to several biblical lessons.
King David ordered Uriah to the front lines, leading to his death.
In sermons, Uriah is often cited as a figure of loyalty and tragedy.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Uryah*, meaning 'Yahweh is my light'.
