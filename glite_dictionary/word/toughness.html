toughness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʌfnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong and durable, or able to withstand hardship or stress
strength and resilience

Examples

Her toughness impressed everyone during the harsh mountain climb.
You need emotional toughness to succeed in a demanding job like this.
The toughness of the material makes it perfect for outdoor use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resilience - the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties
  • Antonym: weakness - the state of lacking physical or mental strength

Etymology

From tough + -ness, dating from Middle English.

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʌfnəs / (BrE)
The ability of a material, especially a metal, to absorb energy and resist fracturing, combining both elasticity and hardness.
material's ability to absorb energy before cracking

Examples

The toughness of steel allows it to be used in the construction of skyscrapers and bridges.
Engineers test the toughness of metals to ensure they can withstand high impacts.
Improving the alloy increased the material's toughness and made it less likely to crack under stress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brittleness - the tendency of a material to crack or break without significant deformation
  • Synonym: hardness - the resistance of a material to deformation or scratching

Etymology

From tough + -ness; 'tough' from Old English 'tōh' (strong, durable).
