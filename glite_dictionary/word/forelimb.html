forelimb

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌlɪm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlɪm / (BrE)
the front limb of an animal, especially one corresponding to the arm, wing, or flipper in various species
front limb of an animal

Examples

The cat injured its forelimb while jumping over the fence.
Birds use their forelimbs, which are adapted as wings, for flight.
The dinosaur's forelimbs were much shorter than its hind limbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hindlimb - the back limb of an animal

Etymology

From 'fore-' meaning 'front' and 'limb' meaning an appendage.
