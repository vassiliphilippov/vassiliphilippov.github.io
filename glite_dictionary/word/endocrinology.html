endocrinology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊ.krəˈnɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊ.krɪˈnɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of medicine and biology concerned with the endocrine glands, their secretions (hormones), and related disorders.
study of the endocrine system and hormones
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in endocrinology because she was fascinated by hormone disorders.
Recent breakthroughs in endocrinology have improved treatments for diabetes and thyroid problems.
The hospital hosts an annual conference on advances in endocrinology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a chemical substance produced in the body that controls and regulates the activity of certain cells or organs

Etymology

From French endocrinologie, from endo- (inside) + Greek krinein (to separate) + -logy (study of).
