to proselytise

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːs.ə.lɪ.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ɪ.lɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to try to persuade someone to change their religious faith or beliefs
try to convert someone's religion

Examples

Some missionaries travel to remote areas specifically to proselytise among indigenous communities.
He was criticized for trying to proselytise his colleagues at work.
The organization does not proselytise, but offers help to anyone in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change someone's religious beliefs

Etymology

From Late Latin 'proselytizare', from Greek 'prosēlytos' (convert).
