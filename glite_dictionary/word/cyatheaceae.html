Cyatheaceae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌæθiˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / saɪˌæθiˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical tree ferns, recognized for their large, arborescent forms and widespread presence in rainforests
family of tropical tree ferns

Examples

The Cyatheaceae are commonly found in the rainforests of Central and South America.
Several members of the Cyatheaceae family grow to impressive heights resembling palm trees.
Botanists studied the unique reproductive strategies of Cyatheaceae in their native habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kyatheion' (a little cup), referring to a characteristic feature of the ferns.
