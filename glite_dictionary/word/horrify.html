to horrify

1. VERB

/ ˈhɔːrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɒrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel great shock, fear, or disgust
cause shock or fear

Examples

The graphic footage on the news will horrify many viewers.
She was horrified to discover that her favorite restaurant had closed.
His comments horrified everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

I was horrified at the thought of being late for my interview
The news of the executions horrified us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to cause someone to feel surprised and upset
  • Synonym: to appall - to fill with horror or dismay
  • Antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy

Etymology

From French horrifier, from Latin horrere (to shudder, to bristle) + -fy (to make).
