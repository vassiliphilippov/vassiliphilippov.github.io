Esperanto

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛspəˈrɑntoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɛspəˈræntəʊ / (BrE)
an artificial international language created in the late 19th century, designed to be easy to learn and based on vocabulary common to European languages
artificial international language

Examples

She learned to speak Esperanto in just a few months.
Many people use Esperanto to communicate with friends around the world.
The Esperanto movement promotes international understanding and peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constructed language - a language that has been artificially created

Etymology

From the pseudonym 'Dr. Esperanto' (meaning 'one who hopes' in the language), used by its creator L.L. Zamenhof.
