altogether

1. ADV

/ ˌɔl.təˈɡɛð.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌɔːl.təˈɡɛð.ə / (BrE)
including everything; when everything or everyone is considered as a whole.
with everything included or counted

Examples

Altogether, the trip cost us less than we expected.
There were fifteen people altogether at the meeting.
His debts amount to five thousand dollars altogether.

Synset Examples

altogether he earns close to a million dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totally - completely; wholly
  • Antonym: partially - not fully; only in part

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English eall þæġeder (all together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00064168-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˌɔl.təˈɡɛð.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌɔːl.təˈɡɛð.ə / (BrE)
when everything is considered; overall, generally speaking
considering everything; overall

Examples

Altogether, I think the project was a success despite some setbacks.
The report wasn't perfect, but altogether, it gave a good overview.
Altogether, it's probably best that we waited until today to make the decision.

Synset Examples

altogether, I'm sorry it happened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all in all - as a whole; considering everything

Etymology
