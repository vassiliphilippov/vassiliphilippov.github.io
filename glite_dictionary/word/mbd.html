MBD

1. NOUN

/ ɛm bi di / (AmE), / ɛm biː diː / (BrE)
a medical condition, mainly in boys, characterized by behavioral and learning disorders; historically referred to as minimal brain dysfunction.
minimal brain dysfunction; behavioral/learning disorder
medical

Examples

Some doctors in the 1960s diagnosed hyperactive children as having MBD.
Research into MBD laid the groundwork for our understanding of attention disorders today.
Although the term MBD is now outdated, it was once widely used in psychiatry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attention deficit hyperactivity disorder - a neurodevelopmental disorder with symptoms of inattentiveness, hyperactivity, and impulsiveness

Etymology

Acronym of 'Minimal Brain Dysfunction'.
