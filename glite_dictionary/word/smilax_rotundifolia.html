Smilax rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪlæks roʊˌtʌndəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈsmaɪlæks rəʊˌtʌndɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a very prickly woody vine native to eastern North America, with round stems, shiny leathery leaves, greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible shiny black berries
prickly vine of eastern North America

Examples

The thickets of Smilax rotundifolia made it difficult to walk through the forest.
Gardeners often try to remove Smilax rotundifolia because of its tough, sprawling vines and sharp thorns.
Clusters of shiny black berries hang from the tangled stems of Smilax rotundifolia in late summer.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Smilax' (a genus of climbing plants) + 'rotundifolia' (round-leaved).
