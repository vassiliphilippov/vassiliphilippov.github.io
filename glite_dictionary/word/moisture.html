moisture

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪstʃə / (BrE)
Very small amounts of water or other liquid in or on something, causing slight wetness.
slight wetness caused by water

Examples

After the heavy rain, you could feel the moisture in the air.
Wipe away any excess moisture before painting the surface.
Plants need some moisture in the soil to grow properly.

Synset Examples

drops of wet gleamed on the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wetness - the condition of being wet

Etymology

From Old French 'moiste' (damp) and Latin 'muscidus' (moldy, musty).
