indetermination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˌtɜɹ.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The quality of being vague, unclear, or lacking precise definition.
quality of being unclear or vague

Examples

The report's indetermination left readers unsure about the actual results.
There was a sense of indetermination in the committee's decision, making future plans difficult.
Legal documents should avoid indetermination to ensure clarity and certainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indefiniteness - quality of being indefinite or unclear; synonym for indetermination
  • Synonym: vagueness - lack of clear or precise description; synonym for indetermination
  • Antonym: precision - the quality of being exact; antonym of indetermination

Etymology

From Latin 'indeterminatio', from 'in-' (not) + 'determinatio' (determination).
