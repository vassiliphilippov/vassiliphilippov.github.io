Scophthalmus aquosus

1. NOUN

/ skoʊfˈθælməs æˈkwoʊsəs / (AmE), / skɒfˈθælməs akˈwəʊsəs / (BrE)
A species of very thin, translucent flounder found along the Atlantic coast of North America, commonly known as the windowpane.
a thin, translucent North American flounder

Examples

The Scophthalmus aquosus is prized by some anglers for its delicate texture and mild flavor.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of Scophthalmus aquosus along the coastal waters.
Because of its nearly transparent body, Scophthalmus aquosus is easily overlooked on sandy sea floors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windowpane - Common name for Scophthalmus aquosus, a thin, translucent flounder

Etymology

From Greek skophthalmos (with projecting eyes) + Latin aquosus (watery), referring to its appearance.
