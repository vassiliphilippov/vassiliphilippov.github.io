diploid

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of an organism or cell) having two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent.
organism or cell with two sets of chromosomes
medical

Examples

Humans are diploid organisms, inheriting a set of chromosomes from each parent.
The scientist examined the diploid cells under a microscope to study their chromosome structure.
After fertilization, the zygote becomes diploid, containing two sets of genetic material.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: haploid - having a single set of unpaired chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek 'diplous' (double) + '-oid' (resembling).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent.
having two sets of chromosomes

Examples

Most human cells are diploid, containing a total of 46 chromosomes.
The fertilized egg is the first diploid cell of an organism.
In plants, the diploid phase alternates with the haploid phase during the life cycle.

Synset Examples

diploid somatic cells

Related Terms

  • Antonym: haploid - having a single set of chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek 'diploos' (double) + '-oid' (form, likeness).

Synset POS: a
