to sexualize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛkʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something sexual in character or quality; to regard or present something or someone in sexual terms
make sexual or attribute sexuality to

Examples

Some media outlets often sexualize young celebrities to attract more viewers.
The curriculum was criticized for sexualizing children's behaviors unnecessarily.
Historically, certain works of art have sexualized religious figures, prompting controversy.

Synset Examples

The god was sexualized and married to another god
Some languages sexualize all nouns and do not have a neuter gender

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desexualize - to remove or deprive of sexual character or quality

Etymology

From 'sexual' + '-ize', following English word-formation patterns.
