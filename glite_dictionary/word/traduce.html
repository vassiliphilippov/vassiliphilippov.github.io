to traduce

1. VERB

/ trəˈdus / (AmE), / trəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to speak very unfavorably or falsely about someone to damage their reputation; to slander or defame.
to speak badly of someone; slander

Examples

The politician accused his opponent of trying to traduce his character during the debate.
She felt betrayed after learning her coworkers had traduced her in front of management.
Journalists should verify facts before they traduce someone in print.

Synset Examples

She badmouths her husband everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - to make false spoken statements that damage someone's reputation
  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the good reputation of someone by making false statements
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone

Etymology

From Latin traducere (to lead across, transfer, expose to shame), from trans- (across) + ducere (to lead).
