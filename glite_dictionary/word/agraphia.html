agraphia

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡræfiə / (AmE), / əˈɡræfɪə / (BrE)
A loss of the ability to write or express thoughts in writing due to a brain lesion.
loss of ability to write (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient developed agraphia after suffering a stroke in the left hemisphere of the brain.
Neurologists often assess for agraphia when diagnosing disorders affecting language and communication.
Her sudden agraphia was distressing, as she had always enjoyed writing letters to her family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dysgraphia - impaired ability to write, less severe than agraphia

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'graphō' (to write).
