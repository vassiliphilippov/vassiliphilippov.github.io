orchis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrkɪs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪs / (BrE)
either of the two male reproductive glands (testes) that produce sperm and secrete androgens
male reproductive gland, testis
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the left orchis for signs of damage after the accident.
Inflammation of the orchis can cause pain and swelling in the area.
The function of the orchis is crucial for male fertility and hormone production.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.kɪs / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪs / (BrE)
any of various terrestrial orchid plants of the genus Orchis, having fleshy underground tubers and spikes of typically colorful flowers
a type of terrestrial orchid plant

Examples

The orchis blooms in early spring along the forest edges.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare species of orchis in the meadow.
Gardeners often cultivate orchis for their striking flower spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - any plant of the family Orchidaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'orchis', from Greek 'órkhis' (testicle), referring to the shape of the tubers.
