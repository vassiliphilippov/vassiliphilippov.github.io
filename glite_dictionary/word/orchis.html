Orchis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrkɪs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪs / (BrE)
A genus of hardy terrestrial orchids found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of hardy temperate orchids

Examples

Several species within the genus Orchis can be found flowering in European woodlands.
The roots of Orchis plants have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety within the Orchis genus in the mountains.

Etymology

From Greek *orkhis* (testicle), referring to the shape of the tubers.
