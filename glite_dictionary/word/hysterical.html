hysterical

1. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
characterized by or arising from hysteria, especially involving uncontrolled emotion or psychoneurotic symptoms
affected by hysteria; uncontrollably emotional

Examples

She burst into hysterical laughter when she heard the news.
The patient's hysterical reactions made diagnosis difficult for the doctors.
During the crisis, some people experienced hysterical behavior that seemed uncontrollable.

Synset Examples

during hysterical conditions various functions of the human body are disordered
hysterical amnesia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing agitation or strong emotions
  • Synonym: hysteric - a person suffering from hysteria or displaying hysteria

Etymology

From Greek *hystera* (womb), via Latin *hystericus*; originally related to the belief that hysteria was linked to the uterus.

2. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
extremely or uncontrollably emotional, often to the point of being unreasonable or frenzied; very funny in an extreme way.
overcome by uncontrollable emotion

Examples

She became hysterical when she heard the unexpected news.
The comedian's performance was so hysterical that the audience couldn't stop laughing.
During the emergency, a hysterical crowd rushed to the exits.

Synset Examples

hysterical laughter
a mob of hysterical vigilantes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing strong emotions
  • Synonym: emotional - showing strong emotions

Etymology

From Greek *hystera* (womb), via Latin *hystericus* and French *hystérique*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00087291-s
Synset POS: s
