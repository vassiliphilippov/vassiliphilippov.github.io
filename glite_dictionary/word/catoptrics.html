catoptrics

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtɑptrɪks / (AmE), / kəˈtɒptrɪks / (BrE)
the branch of optics concerned with the formation of images by mirrors
optics of image formation by mirrors

Examples

The study of catoptrics involves understanding how mirrors reflect light to form images.
Early telescopes relied on the principles of catoptrics for their design.
His research in catoptrics contributed to the development of better optical instruments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dioptrics - branch of optics dealing with image formation by lenses

Etymology

From Greek 'katoptron' (mirror) + '-ics' (science or study of).
