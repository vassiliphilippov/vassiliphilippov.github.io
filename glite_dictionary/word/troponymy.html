troponymy

1. NOUN

/ trəˈpɑːnəmi / (AmE), / trəˈpɒnəmi / (BrE)
(in linguistics) the semantic relationship in which one verb refers to a specific manner of performing the action of another verb.
semantic relation: manner of action

Examples

In lexical semantics, troponymy helps organize verbs according to their manner of action.
The verb 'whisper' stands in a relation of troponymy to 'speak', as it denotes a specific manner of speaking.
Linguists often study troponymy to analyze the fine distinctions between related verbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyponymy - semantic relation where a word denotes a subclass of another word

Etymology

From Greek tropos (a turn, manner) + -onymy (relating to names or words).
