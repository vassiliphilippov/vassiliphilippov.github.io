Lythrum hyssopifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθrəm ˌhɪsəpɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈlɪθrəm ˌhɪsəʊpɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
An annual plant with small solitary pink flowers, native to Europe but widely naturalized in moist habitats.
a pink-flowered annual plant species

Examples

Lythrum hyssopifolia is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.
Gardeners sometimes plant Lythrum hyssopifolia for its attractive pink flowers.
The spread of Lythrum hyssopifolia in North America has prompted biological surveys.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Lythrum' and species epithet 'hyssopifolia', meaning 'with hyssop-like leaves'.
