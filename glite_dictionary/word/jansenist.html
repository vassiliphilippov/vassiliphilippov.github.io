Jansenist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæn.sən.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒan.sən.ɪst / (BrE)
A supporter or adherent of Jansenism, a theological movement within Catholicism emphasizing original sin, human depravity, and predestination.
a supporter of Jansenism

Examples

He became known as a staunch Jansenist during the controversies in 17th-century France.
Many Jansenists faced opposition from the Catholic Church for their beliefs.
Her writings reflect the influence of Jansenist thought on spiritual matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jansenism - the theological movement within Catholicism emphasizing predestination and original sin

Etymology

From French 'janséniste', after Cornelius Jansen (1585–1638), Dutch theologian.
