marly

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrli / (AmE), / ˈmɑːli / (BrE)
(of soil or rock) containing, resembling, or rich in marl.
containing or resembling marl

Examples

The soil in this region is particularly marly, making it ideal for certain crops.
Geologists classified the layer as marly limestone due to its rich mineral content.
Farmers avoid planting in marly fields because the drainage is often poor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clayey - having the properties or abundance of clay

Etymology

From marl + -y, where 'marl' comes from Middle English and Old French *marle*.
