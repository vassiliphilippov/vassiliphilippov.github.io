rehabilitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.həˌbɪl.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.həˌbɪl.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of helping someone to return to normal life or to a useful place in society, especially after imprisonment, illness, or addiction
restoration to normal life or society

Examples

After his release from prison, he entered a rehabilitation program to help him adjust to society.
Effective rehabilitation is crucial for patients recovering from serious injuries or addiction.
The government invested in rehabilitation centers to reduce recidivism and support social reintegration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recovery - the process of becoming healthy after an illness or injury

Etymology

From Latin rehabilitare (to restore).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making wasteland suitable for habitation or cultivation
conversion of wasteland for use

Examples

The city's rehabilitation of the old industrial area has greatly improved the quality of life for residents.
Successful rehabilitation of desert land made it possible to grow crops where nothing grew before.
Environmental agencies invested heavily in the rehabilitation of the abandoned mining site.

Etymology

From Latin 'rehabilitatio', from 'rehabilitare' (to make able again).

3. NOUN

/ ˌriː.əˌbɪl.əˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.həˌbɪl.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of restoring someone's reputation or honor after it has been damaged or questioned, especially by officially clearing their name
restoration of someone's reputation or honor

Examples

After new evidence came to light, the court ordered the rehabilitation of the wrongly accused scientist.
His rehabilitation in the eyes of the public took years of persistent effort.
The official rehabilitation of her reputation followed a long legal battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vindication - the action of clearing someone of blame or suspicion

Etymology

From Latin rehabilitatio (restoration), from re- (again) + habilitare (to make able)

4. NOUN

/ ˌriː.həˌbɪ.ləˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.həˌbɪl.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of helping someone to recover physical abilities after illness or injury, especially through treatment such as massage, exercises, or electrotherapy
treatment to restore physical abilities
medical

Examples

After his surgery, he underwent rehabilitation to regain movement in his leg.
The hospital offers comprehensive rehabilitation programs for stroke patients.
She credits her speedy rehabilitation to the dedicated physical therapy team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physiotherapy - treatment of disease or injury by physical methods
  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original state

Etymology

From Latin 'rehabilitare', meaning to make fit again.
