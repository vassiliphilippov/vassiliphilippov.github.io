Euonymus alatus

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑnɪməs əˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / juːˈɒnɪməs əˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
a bushy deciduous shrub native to northeastern Asia and central China, known for its corky winged branches and brilliant red foliage in autumn
shrub with winged branches and red autumn leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Euonymus alatus turn a vivid scarlet in the fall, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
A row of Euonymus alatus shrubs borders their front yard, adding striking color each autumn.
Botanists value Euonymus alatus for its distinctive corky winged stems and ornamental appeal.

Etymology

From Greek eu- (well) + onoma (name); 'alatus' from Latin meaning 'winged'.
