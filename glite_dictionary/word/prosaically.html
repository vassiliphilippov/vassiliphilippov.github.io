prosaically

1. ADV

/ proʊˈzeɪɪkli / (AmE), / prəˈzeɪɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is matter-of-fact and lacking in imagination; in an ordinary or straightforward manner
in a matter-of-fact or unimaginative way

Examples

She described the situation prosaically, without any emotional embellishment.
The facts were presented prosaically by the spokesperson.
Rather than romanticizing the past, he talks about it prosaically.

Synset Examples

I applied my attention prosaically to my routine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosaic - lacking imagination; dull and ordinary

Etymology

From 'prosaic' + '-ally', with 'prosaic' from Latin 'prosa' (prose).
