tumult

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.mʌlt / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.mʌlt / (BrE)
A loud, confused noise, especially caused by a large group of people; a state of confusion or disorder.
noisy disturbance or confusion

Examples

The crowd's tumult outside the stadium could be heard from miles away.
After the announcement, tumult erupted in the meeting room as everyone started shouting their opinions.
The city streets were in a state of tumult following the unexpected blackout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a loud, confused noise, especially from a crowd
  • Antonym: calm - peaceful, quiet state; absence of noise or disorder

Etymology

From Latin tumultus (commotion, disturbance, uprising).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.mʌlt / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.mʌlt / (BrE)
A state of noisy confusion or disorder; violent agitation or commotion in a crowd or situation.
noisy confusion or violent agitation

Examples

The stadium erupted in tumult as the winning goal was scored.
After the announcement, there was a tumult of shouting and applause.
She struggled to make herself heard above the tumult in the crowded hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - a period or state of peace and tranquility

Etymology

From Latin tumultus (commotion, uprising).
