Salpichroa rhomboidea

1. NOUN

/ sælˌpɪkroʊ.ə ˌrɒmbɔɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / sælˌpɪkrəʊ.ə ˌrɒmbɔɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
A species of weedy vine native to Argentina, characterized by solitary white flowers and egg-shaped white or yellow fruit.
Argentinian vine with white flowers and fruit

Examples

Salpichroa rhomboidea is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in some regions.
Gardeners often struggle to control the spread of Salpichroa rhomboidea in their yards.
The white berries of Salpichroa rhomboidea stand out against its dark green foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin, with genus Salpichroa and species name from Latin rhomboideus, meaning 'diamond-shaped'.
