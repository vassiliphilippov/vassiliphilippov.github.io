Tetragonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the fig-marigold family, including New Zealand spinach, often grown as an edible leafy vegetable.
genus of plants; New Zealand spinach

Examples

Tetragonia is often used as a substitute for true spinach in summer vegetable gardens.
The leaves of Tetragonia are rich in vitamins and can be cooked or eaten raw.
Chefs prize Tetragonia for its mild flavor and heat tolerance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Zealand spinach - Edible plant, Tetragonia tetragonoides, used as a leafy green

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'gonia' (angle), referring to the angled shape of the plant's fruit.

2. NOUN

/ tɛtrəˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / tɛtrəˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of succulent plants in the family Aizoaceae, including New Zealand spinach, cultivated for use as a leafy vegetable.
genus of succulent plants; New Zealand spinach

Examples

The leaves of Tetragonia are often used in salads and cooked as a spinach substitute.
Botanists classify New Zealand spinach in the genus Tetragonia.
Gardeners sometimes grow Tetragonia in warm climates where true spinach struggles.

Etymology

From Greek 'tetragonos' (having four angles), referring to the shape of the fruit.
