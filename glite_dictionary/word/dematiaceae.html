Dematiaceae

1. NOUN

/ diˌmætiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / diˌmætiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by dark-colored hyphae or conidia, known as imperfect mushrooms
family of fungi with dark hyphae/conidia
medical

Examples

The Dematiaceae are noted for their darkly pigmented hyphae, giving them a characteristic appearance.
Many species within the Dematiaceae can cause plant diseases and are studied by plant pathologists.
Identification of Dematiaceae often relies on the color and structure of their conidia.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'demat-', meaning 'bundle' or 'thread', referencing the filamentous structures.

2. NOUN

/ dɛˌmeɪtiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / dɪˌmætiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of imperfect fungi characterized by dark-colored hyphae or conidia
a family of dark-colored fungi
medical

Examples

The Dematiaceae produce spores with a distinctive dark coloration visible under a microscope.
Many members of the Dematiaceae are associated with plant decay and soil environments.
Some Dematiaceae species can cause opportunistic infections in immunocompromised individuals.

Etymology

From Greek 'demas' (body), relating to the dark coloration; used in taxonomic Latin.
