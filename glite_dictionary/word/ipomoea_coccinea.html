Ipomoea coccinea

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.poʊˈmiː.ə kɒkˈsɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə kɒkˈsɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
An annual climbing plant native to the eastern United States, known for its bright scarlet flowers.
climbing plant with scarlet flowers

Examples

The bright red blooms of Ipomoea coccinea attract hummingbirds to the garden each summer.
We planted Ipomoea coccinea along the fence, and it quickly covered it with lush vines and flowers.
Unlike some other morning glories, Ipomoea coccinea thrives in full sun and poor soil.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ipomoea' (morning glory genus) + 'coccinea' (scarlet).
