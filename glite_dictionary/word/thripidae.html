Thripidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɪpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈθrɪpɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of thrips insects, containing over 2,000 species in more than 290 genera
family of thrips insects

Examples

The Thripidae family includes many species that are pests of crops.
Researchers have discovered a new genus within the Thripidae.
Members of the Thripidae are found in diverse habitats around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus 'Thrips' and the suffix '-idae' used for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɪpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈθrɪpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a large family of tiny, slender insects known as thrips, including over 2,000 described species
family of thrips insects

Examples

Many species in the Thripidae family are important agricultural pests due to their feeding on crops.
Researchers discovered a new genus of Thripidae in the tropical rainforest.
The Thripidae are distinguished by their fringed wings and small size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - tiny, slender insects of the order Thysanoptera

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Thrips + the family suffix -idae.
