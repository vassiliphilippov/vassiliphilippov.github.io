Limulidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmjuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈmjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine arthropods commonly known as horseshoe crabs, characterized by hard carapaces and long tails
family of horseshoe crabs

Examples

The family Limulidae includes several species of horseshoe crabs found along the coasts of Asia and North America.
Researchers study Limulidae to understand the evolution of marine arthropods.
Blood extracted from members of the Limulidae family is crucial in medical testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - a marine arthropod with a hard, horseshoe-shaped shell

Etymology

From Latin 'Limulus', the type genus, and '-idae', a suffix for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈmjuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈmjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine arthropods known as horseshoe crabs, characterized by a hard carapace and long tail spine
family of horseshoe crabs

Examples

The Limulidae have survived largely unchanged for hundreds of millions of years.
Researchers study Limulidae to better understand evolutionary history among arthropods.
The blood of some Limulidae species is used in medical testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - a marine arthropod with a domed shell, member of Limulidae

Etymology

From Limulus (the type genus) + -idae, the standard suffix for zoological family names in Latin.
