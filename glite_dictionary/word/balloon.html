balloon

1. VERB

/ bəˈluːn / (AmE), / bəˈluːn / (BrE)
to travel in or ride by means of a hot-air balloon
travel in a hot-air balloon

Examples

She dreams of the day when she can balloon over the countryside at sunrise.
They ballooned across the open desert, enjoying the breathtaking views.
Last summer, we ballooned for hours, marveling at the scenery below.

Synset Examples

He tried to balloon around the earth but storms forced him to land in China

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlun / (AmE), / bəˈluːn / (BrE)
a large flexible bag filled with gas or heated air, which rises and floats in the atmosphere
large bag filled with gas or heated air

Examples

The hot air balloon drifted slowly over the countryside at sunrise.
She let go of the helium balloon, and it floated up into the sky.
The festival featured a giant balloon shaped like a dinosaur that attracted many visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airship - powered aircraft that is kept buoyant by gas, typically rigid
  • Synonym: blimp - nonrigid airship often used for advertising or observation

Etymology

From French 'ballon' (large ball), from Italian 'ballone' (large ball), augmentative of 'balla' (ball).

3. NOUN

/ bəˈlun / (AmE), / bəˈluːn / (BrE)
a small, thin, inflatable bag, usually made of rubber, with a narrow neck, typically filled with air or gas and used as a toy or decoration
inflatable rubber toy or decoration

Examples

The children laughed as the balloon floated away into the sky.
She tied a red balloon to each chair for the birthday party.
He accidentally popped the balloon with a pin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blimp - a non-rigid airship, similar in shape to a balloon
  • Synonym: airship - a powered aircraft that is kept buoyant by a body of gas, contrasting with a balloon
  • Synonym: parachute - a device used to slow a person or object when falling, which resembles a balloon when inflated

Etymology

From French 'ballon' (large ball), from Italian 'ballone','balla' (ball).

4. VERB

/ bəˈluːn / (AmE), / bəˈluːn / (BrE)
(of an object or a quantity) to become swollen, puffed out, or rapidly increase in size or volume, like a balloon being inflated.
to become inflated or rapidly increase

Examples

Rising prices have caused the budget to balloon in recent years.
As the wind caught them, the curtains began to balloon outwards.
Her workload ballooned after her colleague left the company.

Synset Examples

The sails ballooned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expand - to increase in size, number, or importance
  • Antonym: shrink - become or make smaller in size or amount

Etymology

From French 'ballon', from Italian 'ballone', an augmentative of 'balla' meaning 'ball'.
