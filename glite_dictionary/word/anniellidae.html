Anniellidae

1. NOUN

/ æn.iˈɛl.ɪ.di / (AmE), / æn.iˈɛl.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of legless lizards native to western North America, commonly known as American legless lizards.
family of legless lizards

Examples

The Anniellidae are commonly referred to as American legless lizards.
Researchers have discovered several new species within the Anniellidae family.
Unlike snakes, members of the Anniellidae family have movable eyelids and are not venomous.

Etymology

From New Latin Anniella (type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæniˈɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌaniːˈɛlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of legless lizards native to western North America, characterized by their elongate bodies and burrowing lifestyle.
family of legless lizards

Examples

The Anniellidae family includes species commonly known as California legless lizards.
Researchers studied the burrowing behavior unique to Anniellidae in their natural habitat.
Unlike most lizards, members of the Anniellidae family lack limbs and are adapted for life underground.

Etymology

From genus name 'Anniella' with the standard zoological family suffix '-idae'.
