covalent

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
relating to or denoting chemical bonds formed by the sharing of electron pairs between atoms
relating to bonds formed by sharing electrons

Examples

A water molecule is formed by two covalent bonds between hydrogen and oxygen atoms.
Carbon atoms can form multiple covalent bonds with other elements.
The strength of a covalent bond makes many compounds stable.

Synset Examples

covalent bond

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ionic - relating to chemical bonds formed by the transfer of electrons

Etymology

From 'co-' (together) and 'valence,' referring to the shared valence electrons in a bond.
