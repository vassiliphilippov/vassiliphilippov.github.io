Scandentia

1. NOUN

/ skænˈdɛntiə / (AmE), / skænˈdɛntiə / (BrE)
an order of small mammals comprising the tree shrews, sometimes classified as primates or insectivores
order of mammals including tree shrews

Examples

Scandentia is a unique mammalian order that includes only the tree shrews.
Some taxonomists debate whether Scandentia should be classified with primates or insectivores.
The evolutionary history of Scandentia has fascinated zoologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree shrew - small, squirrel-like mammal in Scandentia

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Latin scandens (climbing).
