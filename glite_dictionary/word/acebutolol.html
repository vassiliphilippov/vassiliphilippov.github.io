acebutolol

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪsɪˈbjuːtəˌlɔl / (AmE), / ˌeɪsɪˈbjuːtəlɒl / (BrE)
A beta blocker drug, used orally to treat high blood pressure and certain heart conditions.
beta blocker drug for hypertension
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed acebutolol to help control her blood pressure.
If you experience dizziness while taking acebutolol, inform your physician immediately.
Many patients tolerate acebutolol well, even if they have side effects from other beta blockers.

From chemical nomenclature, based on its molecular structure.
