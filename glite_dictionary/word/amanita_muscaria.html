Amanita muscaria

1. NOUN

/ əˌmænɪtə mʌsˈkɛriə / (AmE), / əˌmanɪtə mʌsˈkɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a poisonous but rarely fatal woodland mushroom, recognizable by its bright red cap with white spots and white gills
a poisonous red-capped mushroom with white spots

Examples

The famous red-capped Amanita muscaria is often depicted in fairy tale illustrations.
Despite its beauty, Amanita muscaria should never be eaten due to its poisonous properties.
Mushroom enthusiasts easily recognize Amanita muscaria by its distinctive white-spotted cap.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Amanita' and Latin 'muscaria' (of flies), traditionally believed to attract or kill flies.
