3. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
a sweet-tasting, white, crystalline substance obtained from various plants, especially sugar cane and sugar beet, used as a sweetener and preservative in food and drink
sweet-tasting crystalline substance; sweetener

Examples

She always puts a spoonful of sugar in her tea.
Too much sugar in your diet can be unhealthy.
The baker sprinkled sugar over the freshly baked cookies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetener - a substance used to make food or drink taste sweet
  • Antonym: salt - a white crystalline substance used to season and preserve food, opposite in taste to sugar

Etymology

From Old French sucre, from Medieval Latin succarum, from Arabic sukkar, from Persian shakar, from Sanskrit śarkarā (gravel, ground or candied sugar).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈsʊɡə / (BrE)
a sweet crystalline substance obtained from various plants, used as a sweetener, or any of a class of soluble, usually sweet-tasting carbohydrates found in living cells that are a source of energy for animals and plants; classified by the number of monosaccharide units they contain
sweet crystalline substance; class of carbohydrates

Examples

Glucose is a simple sugar that is vital for cellular respiration.
Consuming too much sugar can increase the risk of certain health problems.
Plant cells produce sugar through the process of photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbohydrate - any of a large group of organic compounds including sugars, starch, and cellulose

Etymology

From Arabic *sukkar*, from Persian *shakar*, from Sanskrit *śarkarā* (grit, sugar).
