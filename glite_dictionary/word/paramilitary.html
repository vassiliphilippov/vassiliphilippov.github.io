paramilitary

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈmɪlɪˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌparəˈmɪlɪtəri / (BrE)
a group of civilians organized in a military style, often to supplement or act in place of a regular army
civilian group organized like a military force

Examples

The government accused the paramilitary of destabilizing the region through illicit activities.
Several armed paramilitary groups operate in remote areas outside government control.
The peace process stalled after a resurgence of violence by paramilitaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militia - a military force raised from the civilian population to supplement a regular army
  • Antonym: army - the official armed forces of a nation

Etymology

From para- (beside, alongside) + military, meaning beside or auxiliary to the military.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpær.əˈmɪl.ɪˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈmɪl.ɪ.təri / (BrE)
relating to a group of civilians organized to function like or support a military unit
relating to civilian groups with military functions

Examples

The government banned all paramilitary organizations operating in the region.
The police responded to the unrest with paramilitary tactics and equipment.
Some countries rely on paramilitary groups to supplement their armed forces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: military - relating to soldiers or armed forces

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'para-' (beside, alongside) and 'military'.
