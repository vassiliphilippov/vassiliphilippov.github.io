revenge

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
the act of harming or punishing someone because they have harmed you or someone you care about
punishment or harm in return for an injury

Examples

She plotted her revenge carefully, waiting for the perfect moment to act.
Many stories are built around the powerful desire for revenge.
He took revenge on those who betrayed his trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vengeance - the act of taking revenge; punishment for a wrong
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving someone for a wrong; showing mercy

Etymology

From Old French 'revenge', from 'revengier', from Latin 'vindicare' (to claim, avenge).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
to punish or hurt someone in return for an injury or wrong suffered
punish or hurt in return for a wrong

Examples

After being cheated, he vowed to revenge himself on those responsible.
She could not resist the urge to revenge the insult inflicted on her family.
They plotted to revenge the defeat they suffered last year.

Synset Examples

He wants to avenge the murder of his brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avenge - to inflict punishment in return for a wrong, especially to uphold justice
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense or mistake

Etymology

From Old French *revengier* (to avenge), from Latin *vindicare* (to claim, avenge).
