lecturer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛktʃərər / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃərə / (BrE)
a person who gives lectures, especially as a profession at a college or university
person who gives lectures

Examples

The lecturer delivered a fascinating talk on astrophysics last night.
Many students lined up after class to ask the lecturer questions about the material.
She was promoted from assistant lecturer to senior teaching staff within five years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: professor - a senior academic at a college or university
  • Degree Lower: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school

Etymology

From Latin 'lectura' (a reading), via 'lecture', meaning a spoken lesson.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛk.tʃər / (BrE)
a person who gives lectures, especially as an academic staff member at a university or college
university teacher giving lectures

Examples

The university lecturer explained the complex theory in a way everyone could understand.
She was promoted from graduate assistant to lecturer after completing her doctorate.
A well-known lecturer visited campus last week to speak on neuroscience.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: professor - a senior university academic
  • Synonym: instructor - a person who teaches, especially in a college or university

Etymology

From Latin 'lectus', past participle of 'legere' (to read), via Old French and late Middle English.
