Ligularia

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡjuːləriə / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjuːləriə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native to Europe and Asia, known for their large leaves and yellow or orange flower spikes; also called leopard plants.
genus of tall flowering plants (leopard plants)

Examples

Many gardeners choose Ligularia for its showy foliage and striking yellow flowers.
The damp shade in her backyard was ideal for planting Ligularia species.
As a hardy perennial, Ligularia can brighten up any woodland garden.

Etymology

From Latin 'ligula' (a small tongue), referring to the shape of the flowers, with the New Latin suffix '-aria'.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡjuːləriə / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjuːləriə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants in the daisy family, native to Europe and Asia, known as leopard plants.
a genus of leopard plants

Examples

The Ligularia in her garden bloom with bright yellow flower spikes each summer.
Gardeners value Ligularia for its bold foliage and ability to thrive in damp, shady areas.
Some species of Ligularia are commonly known as leopard plants due to their spotted leaves.

Etymology

From Latin ligularis, meaning 'strap-shaped', referring to the shape of the flowers.
