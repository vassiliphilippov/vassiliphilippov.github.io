porphyry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːr.fɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.fər.i / (BrE)
an igneous rock consisting of large crystals embedded in a fine-grained groundmass
a type of igneous rock with large crystals

Examples

This cliff is made of porphyry, which glistens in the sunlight due to its crystals.
Ancient civilizations often carved statues and columns from porphyry because of its hardness and color.
The museum's geology exhibit includes samples of porphyry from different volcanic regions.

Etymology

From Greek *porphyra* (purple), referring to the rock's typical color.
