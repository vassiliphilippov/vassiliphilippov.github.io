accessary

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
a person who helps another to commit a crime, typically without being present when the crime is committed
person aiding a crime
legal

Examples

The court found the driver to be an accessary to the robbery, even though he did not enter the bank.
She was charged as an accessary after the fact for helping the suspect escape.
In some jurisdictions, being an accessary to a crime carries a severe penalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - person who aids in committing a crime (alternate spelling, more common)

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *accessarie*, related to 'access' and ultimately Latin *accessus* (approach).

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
A person who helps someone else commit a crime but is not present when the crime is actually committed.
person who helps in a crime
legal

Examples

She was accused of being an accessary to the robbery, though she never entered the bank.
The lawyer argued that his client was merely an accessary and not the main perpetrator.
He was found guilty as an accessary after helping plan the burglary.

Synset Examples

he was charged with being accessory to the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - a person who aids a crime but is not present at its commission; common variant spelling

Etymology

From Medieval Latin accessarius, from Latin accessus (approach, access).
