slightly

1. ADV

/ ˈslaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtli / (BrE)
To a small degree or extent; not greatly or seriously.
to a small degree or extent

Examples

The soup was slightly too salty for my taste.
She seemed slightly nervous before the interview.
The temperature dropped slightly overnight.

Synset Examples

his arguments were somewhat self-contradictory
the children argued because one slice of cake was slightly larger than the other

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: somewhat - to a moderate extent or by a moderate amount
  • Degree Lower: barely - only just; by the smallest amount

Etymology

From 'slight' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix), originally Middle English.
