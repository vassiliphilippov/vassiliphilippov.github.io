Chaetodipterus faber

1. NOUN

/ kiˌtoʊdəˈdɪptərəs ˈfeɪbər / (AmE), / kiˌiːtəʊdɪˈptɛərəs ˈfɑːbə / (BrE)
A deep-bodied, disk-shaped food fish found in warmer western Atlantic coastal waters; also known as the Atlantic spadefish.
species of Atlantic food fish

Examples

Fishermen caught several Chaetodipterus faber off the coast of Florida yesterday.
The diet of Chaetodipterus faber consists mainly of small invertebrates and plankton.
Local seafood restaurants often serve freshly grilled Chaetodipterus faber during the summer season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefish - common name for Chaetodipterus faber, a disk-shaped marine fish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Chaetodipterus' meaning 'bristle-fin', species name 'faber' meaning 'smith' in Latin.
