cheekpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃikˌpis / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːkˌpiːs / (BrE)
either of the two straps on a bridle that connect the bit to the headpiece
strap on a bridle between the bit and headpiece

Examples

The cheekpiece must be adjusted so the bit sits comfortably in the horse's mouth.
She replaced the worn leather cheekpiece on her bridle before the competition.
Both cheekpieces should be the same length to keep the bit even.

Etymology

From 'cheek' + 'piece', referring to its position beside the horse's cheek.
