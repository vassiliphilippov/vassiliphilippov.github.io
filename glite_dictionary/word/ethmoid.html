ethmoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθˌmɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.mɔɪd / (BrE)
a light, spongy bone at the base of the skull, located between the eyes, that forms part of the nasal cavity and the orbits
bone at skull base between eyes
medical

Examples

The ethmoid bone helps to form the walls of the nasal cavity.
A fracture to the ethmoid can affect both the nose and the eyes.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the ethmoid during invasive nasal procedures.

Etymology

From Greek *ēthmoeidēs*, from *ēthmos* (sieve) + *-eidēs* (form, shape), referring to its sieve-like structure.
