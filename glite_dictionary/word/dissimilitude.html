dissimilitude

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌsɪmɪˈlɪtjuːd / (AmE), / dɪˌsɪmɪˈlɪtjuːd / (BrE)
The quality or state of being dissimilar; lack of resemblance or similarity between two or more things.
lack of similarity; dissimilarity

Examples

The two paintings, despite being by the same artist, showed remarkable dissimilitude in style and technique.
There was a clear dissimilitude between her gentle manner and his brash demeanor.
Scientists noted a striking dissimilitude between the chemical properties of the samples.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike
  • Antonym: resemblance - the state of resembling or being alike

Etymology

From Latin dissimilitudo, from dissimilis (unlike, dissimilar).
