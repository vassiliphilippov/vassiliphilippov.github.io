Arilus cristatus

1. NOUN

/ əˈraɪləs krɪsˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌæ.rɪ.ləs krɪsˈteɪ.təs / (BrE)
a large predatory North American bug, also known as the wheel bug, which feeds on the blood of other insects
large predatory North American bug

Examples

The Arilus cristatus is easily recognized by the gear-shaped crest on its back.
Gardeners appreciate Arilus cristatus for its role in controlling pest insects.
If threatened, Arilus cristatus can deliver a painful bite to humans.

Etymology

From Latin; Arilus is the genus name and cristatus means 'crested' in Latin, referring to the wheel-like crest on its thorax.
