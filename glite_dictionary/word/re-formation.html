re-formation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː fɔːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː fɔːˈmeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of forming again, especially with improvements or after correcting defects; renewal or reconstitution
the act of forming again; renewal

Examples

After the merger, the company underwent a complete re-formation of its management structure.
The re-formation of the team brought together new talent and fresh strategies.
Social movements often call for the re-formation of existing institutions to address inequalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - to make changes for improvement

Etymology

From 're-' meaning 'again' + 'formation' (from Latin formatio).
