parody

1. VERB

/ ˈpærədi / (AmE), / ˈparədi / (BrE)
to imitate the style or content of someone or something in a humorous or satirical way, often exaggerating certain characteristics for comic effect
imitate humorously to mock or satirize

Examples

The comedian loves to parody popular songs by changing the lyrics.
They parodied the politician’s speech in the latest television skit.
Many YouTube channels parody movie trailers for comedic effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satirize - to use satire to mock or criticize
  • Synonym: lampoon - to publicly criticize by using ridicule, irony, or sarcasm

Etymology

From Greek parōidia (burlesque poem), from para- (beside) + ōidē (song).

2. NOUN

/ ˈperədi / (AmE), / ˈpærədi / (BrE)
A humorous or satirical imitation of a serious piece of literature, music, or other work.
humorous or satirical imitation

Examples

The film is a clever parody of classic horror movies.
He wrote a parody of the president's speech for the comedy show.
Online, many users shared a hilarious parody of the viral advertisement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize
  • Synonym: spoof - a humorous imitation or send-up
  • Antonym: homage - respectful imitation or tribute

Etymology

From Greek parōidia (burlesque song or poem), from para- (beside) + ōidē (song).

/ ˈpɛr.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.di / (BrE)
to imitate someone or something in a humorous or satirical way, especially exaggerating its characteristic features
imitate humorously or satirically

Examples

The comedian parodied a famous politician during the show, drawing lots of laughter from the audience.
Many YouTube creators parody popular music videos to entertain their subscribers.
The play parodies classic fairy tales by giving them unexpected twists and modern humor.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of, often by imitating
  • Antonym: to celebrate - to praise or honor, often the opposite of mocking or ridiculing

Etymology

From Greek parōidia (a burlesque song or poem).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.di / (BrE)
a piece of writing, music, or other work that humorously imitates the style of someone or something else
humorous imitation of a style or work

Examples

The play was a clever parody of classic fairy tales.
He wrote a parody of the famous politician's speech that made everyone laugh.
That song is actually a parody of an old rock anthem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor or exaggeration to criticize people or ideas
  • Synonym: pastiche - an artistic work in a style that imitates another

Etymology

From Greek parōidia (burlesque poem), from para- (beside, parallel) + oide (ode, song).
