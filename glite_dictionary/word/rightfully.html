rightfully

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪtfəli / (AmE), / ˈraɪtfəli / (BrE)
in a way that is morally or legally correct; by right
in a rightful or just manner

Examples

He was rightfully proud of his achievements after years of hard work.
She rightfully demanded an apology for the unfair treatment.
The property was rightfully returned to its original owner.

Synset Examples

baseball rightfully is the nation's pastime

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wrongfully - in a way that is morally or legally wrong

Etymology

From the adjective 'rightful' + '-ly'.
