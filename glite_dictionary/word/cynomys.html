Cynomys

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəˌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.əʊ.mɪs / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing rodents known as prairie dogs, native to North America.
genus of prairie dogs (rodents)

Examples

Scientists observed how the Cynomys colonies communicated with each other using distinctive alarm calls.
The black-tailed Cynomys is the most widespread species of prairie dog in North America.
Habitat loss remains a significant threat to several Cynomys species across the plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie dog - a burrowing rodent native to North America

Etymology

From Greek kynos (dog) + mys (mouse), referring to the animal's dog-like bark and rodent status.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈnoʊmɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈnəʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing rodents native to North America, known as prairie dogs.
genus of prairie dogs

Examples

Several colonies of Cynomys can be found in the grasslands of North America.
Scientists studied the social behaviors of Cynomys in their natural habitat.
The genus Cynomys includes several species, such as the black-tailed and white-tailed prairie dog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie dog - a small burrowing rodent of North American grasslands

Etymology

From Greek 'kynos' (dog) and 'mys' (mouse), meaning 'dog mouse'.
