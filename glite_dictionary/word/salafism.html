Salafism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæ.ləˌfɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsæ.ləˌfɪz.əm / (BrE)
A movement within Sunni Islam advocating a return to what participants believe are the original teachings and practices of the first three generations of Muslims (the salaf); it emphasizes strict adherence to Islamic law and often rejects modern interpretations of Islam.
conservative Islamic movement within Sunni Islam

Examples

Salafism emphasizes a strict interpretation of Islamic texts and the practices of the earliest Muslim community.
Some governments have expressed concern about the spread of Salafism and its potential influence on young people.
He wrote a detailed analysis of the historical origins and development of Salafism in the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wahhabism - An ultraconservative branch of Sunni Islam closely associated with Saudi Arabia

Etymology

From Arabic salaf (predecessors, ancestors), referring to the earliest generations of Muslims.
