trust

1. NOUN

/ trʌst / (AmE), / trʌst / (BrE)
firm belief in the reliability, truth, or ability of someone or something, often based on past experience
firm belief in reliability or truth

Examples

After working together for years, there was a deep trust between the partners.
He placed his trust in the guide, believing she knew the safest path.
It's difficult to rebuild trust once it has been broken.

Synset Examples

he wrote the paper with considerable reliance on the work of other scientists
he put more trust in his own two legs than in the gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliance - dependence on or trust in someone or something
  • Antonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence

Etymology

From Old Norse *traust* (help, confidence, firmness), related to Old English *treowe* (faithful, trustworthy).
