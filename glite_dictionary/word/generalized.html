generalized

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an organism or part) not specialized for a particular function or environment; adapted to a broad range of conditions.
not specialized; broadly adapted

Examples

The generalized structure of the animal allows it to adapt to various habitats.
Primitive mammals, like the hedgehog, are often described as generalized rather than specialized.
A generalized limb structure means the animal can perform a wide range of movements.

Synset Examples

the hedgehog is a primitive and generalized mammal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specialized - adapted to a specific function or environment

Etymology

From 'general', based on Latin 'generalis' (of a whole class) + '-ize' + '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an organism or part) not adapted to a specific function or environment; lacking biological specialization.
not biologically specialized

Examples

The platypus is considered a generalized mammal due to its mixture of primitive traits.
Early amphibians were more generalized than modern species, allowing them to adapt to varied environments.
In evolutionary terms, a generalized limb can perform multiple functions but is less efficient at any single task.

Synset Examples

the hedgehog is a primitive and generalized mammal

  • Antonym: specialized - adapted for a specific function or environment
