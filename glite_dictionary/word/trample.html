trample

1. VERB

/ ˈtræmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtræmpəl / (BrE)
to tread or stomp heavily and noisily, often causing damage or showing disregard
tread heavily or roughly

Examples

The crowd began to trample the flowers in their excitement.
Please do not trample on the newly planted grass.
The protesters trampled over the barriers set up by the police.

Synset Examples

The soldiers trampled across the fields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - bring down one’s foot forcefully, often as an expression of anger
  • Synonym: tread - to step or walk on or over

Etymology

From Middle English 'trampelen', frequentative of 'trampen' meaning 'to stamp, tread'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01933420-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈtræmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtræmpəl / (BrE)
to injure someone or something by stepping heavily on them repeatedly
step heavily to injure

Examples

The tourists panicked and accidentally trampled several people at the entrance.
Cattle sometimes trample crops when moving through the fields.
The child was nearly trampled by the crowd at the festival.

Synset Examples

The passerby was trampled by an elephant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or is damaged
  • Synonym: stamp - to bring down one's foot heavily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00070210-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈtræmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtræmpəl / (BrE)
a sound made by heavy, continuous footsteps or stamping, often of a group of people or animals
sound of heavy footsteps or stomping

Examples

We could hear the trample of boots echoing down the hallway.
The trample of hooves filled the air as the horses charged across the field.
From above, the trample of feet revealed that the parade had begun.

Synset Examples

he heard the trample of many feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - a heavy, forceful step or the sound it makes
  • Synonym: tread - the sound of a person's footstep

Etymology

From the verb 'trample,' which is from Middle English tramplen, frequentative of trampen, related to 'tramp.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07413259-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈtræm.pəl / (AmE), / ˈtræm.pəl / (BrE)
to tread heavily so as to crush or flatten something beneath one's feet
tread heavily and crush underfoot

Examples

The parade trampled the fresh grass, leaving muddy footprints everywhere.
Please be careful not to trample the wildflowers along the trail.
Crowds of tourists sometimes trample over sensitive habitats, causing damage.

Synset Examples

tramp down the grass
trample the flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it is damaged or destroyed
  • Synonym: to flatten - to make or become level or smooth

Etymology

From Middle English trampelen, a frequentative of trampen (to stamp, tread).
