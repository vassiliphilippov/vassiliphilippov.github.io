Paulo Afonso

1. PROPN

/ ˌpaʊloʊ əˈfɔnsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpaʊləʊ əˈfɒnsəʊ / (BrE)
a major waterfall on the São Francisco River in northeastern Brazil, known for its height and hydroelectric power facilities.
a waterfall in northeastern Brazil

Examples

Tourists flock to Paulo Afonso each year to see its impressive waterfalls and natural beauty.
The hydroelectric dam at Paulo Afonso plays a crucial role in Brazil's energy supply.
We took a boat tour to get a closer view of Paulo Afonso and the surrounding landscape.

Etymology

Portuguese origin: 'Paulo' (Paul) + 'Afonso' (Alfonso), named after a historical figure.
