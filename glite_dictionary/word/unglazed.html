unglazed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡleɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡleɪzd / (BrE)
(of windows, doors, etc.) not fitted with glass.
not fitted with glass (esp. windows)

Examples

The old cottage had several unglazed windows that let in the cool night breeze.
During renovations, the builders left the upper windows unglazed for ventilation.
Some historic greenhouses featured unglazed openings to increase airflow for delicate plants.

Synset Examples

windows were unglazed to admit as much light and air as possible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glazed - fitted or covered with glass or a glossy surface

Etymology

Formed by combining the prefix 'un-' (not) with 'glazed' (fitted with glass).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡleɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡleɪzd / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) not having a shiny or glassy coating; matte or unfinished.
not having a shiny coating

Examples

The artist preferred using unglazed pottery for its natural, rustic appearance.
These cookies are delicious even when they are unglazed and simple.
We chose unglazed tiles to create a softer look in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

unglazed paper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glazed - having a shiny or glossy coating

Etymology

From un- (not) + glazed, where 'glaze' comes from Middle English glasen (to fit with glass or make shiny).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01124160-a
Synset POS: a
