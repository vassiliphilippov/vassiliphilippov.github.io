Celosia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈloʊsiə / (AmE), / sɪˈləʊsiə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial herbs and vines, native to tropical and subtropical regions of America, Asia, and Africa, known for their brightly colored, plume-like or crested flowers
a genus of tropical herbs with bright flowers

Examples

The gardens were full of Celosia with their vivid, flame-like blossoms attracting butterflies.
Many florists use Celosia for striking arrangements due to its unique flower shapes.
She planted several varieties of Celosia to add bursts of color to her summer beds.

Etymology

From Greek kēleos (burned) due to the flame-like flower heads.
