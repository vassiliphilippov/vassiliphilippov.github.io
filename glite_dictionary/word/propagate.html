to propagate

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːpəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəɡeɪt / (BrE)
to cause (a plant) to reproduce, especially by means such as grafting or layering
cause a plant to reproduce

Examples

Gardeners often propagate plants by taking cuttings from healthy specimens.
You can propagate roses successfully using the layering technique in spring.
Many fruit trees are propagated by grafting rather than from seed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breed - to produce offspring, especially in plants and animals
  • Synonym: plant - to put (a seed or plant) in the ground to grow

Etymology

From Latin propagare (to multiply by layers, increase, extend).
