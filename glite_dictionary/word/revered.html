revered

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɪrd / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪəd / (BrE)
deeply respected or admired, especially because of age, wisdom, or character
deeply respected; highly honored

Examples

As a revered leader, she inspired generations with her wisdom and integrity.
The professor was revered by his students for his dedication to teaching.
Gandhi is revered around the world as a symbol of peace and nonviolence.

Synset Examples

revered holy men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respected - admired for abilities, qualities, or achievements
  • Antonym: despised - regarded with contempt or scorn

Etymology

Past participle of 'revere,' from Latin 'revereri' (to stand in awe of).
