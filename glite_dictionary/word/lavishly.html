lavishly

1. ADV

/ ˈlævɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃli / (BrE)
In a rich, elaborate, or generous manner.
in a rich or elaborate way

Examples

The ballroom was lavishly decorated for the wedding reception.
She spent lavishly on gifts for her friends and family.
The king lived lavishly, with feasts and celebrations every night.

Synset Examples

lavishly decorated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generously - in a generous manner
  • Antonym: sparingly - in a restricted or meager manner

Etymology

From the adjective "lavish" plus suffix "-ly"; "lavish" from Middle French *lavasse* (deluge), from *laver* (to wash).

2. ADV

/ ˈlævɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃli / (BrE)
in a way that involves spending or giving generously or extravagantly, often more than is necessary
in an extravagant or generous way

Examples

The ballroom was decorated lavishly with gold ornaments and fresh flowers.
She spent money lavishly on gifts for her friends and family.
The company rewarded its top employees lavishly at the annual gala.

Synset Examples

the United States, up to the 1920s, used fuel lavishly, mainly because it was so cheap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagantly - in an excessive or wasteful manner
  • Antonym: frugally - in a way that avoids waste; sparingly

Etymology

From 'lavish' (from Middle English, probably via Old French 'lavasse', from Latin 'lavare', to wash), with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
