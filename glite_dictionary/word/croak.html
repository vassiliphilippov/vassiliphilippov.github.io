croak

1. NOUN

/ kroʊk / (AmE), / krəʊk / (BrE)
a harsh, hoarse sound, especially the deep, rattling call of a frog or raven
harsh hoarse sound (esp. of a frog)

Examples

The croak of frogs echoed across the pond at dusk.
A loud croak startled me as I approached the old tree.
The raven's harsh croak sent a chill down my spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caw - a harsh, loud sound made by a crow or similar bird
  • Antonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects

Etymology

Imitative origin; mimics the sound made by frogs and certain birds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07140075-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kroʊk / (AmE), / krəʊk / (BrE)
to make a deep, rough, hoarse sound similar to that made by a frog or a raven
make a hoarse, rough sound

Examples

The old frog began to croak loudly near the pond at sunset.
After shouting all night, his voice could only croak the next morning.
Ravens croak ominously in the trees before a storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squawk - make a loud, harsh noise (esp. birds)
  • Antonym: chirp - make a short, sharp sound (esp. small birds)

Etymology

Imitative of the natural sound produced by frogs and some birds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01066423-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kroʊk / (AmE), / krəʊk / (BrE)
to make low, complaining remarks or noises, often under one's breath
make low, complaining remarks

Examples

Tired of his chores, he began to croak about the unfairness of it all.
She would croak every morning about the early meetings at work.
Instead of voicing their opinions openly, the students just croaked quietly to each other.

Synset Examples

she grumbles when she feels overworked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain in a quiet or low voice
  • Synonym: mutter - to speak or complain in a low, indistinct voice
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound made by frogs and crows.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911172-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kɹoʊk / (AmE), / krəʊk / (BrE)
(informal, often humorous or irreverent) to die; to cease living, especially used in a casual or slangy context.
(slang) to die
informal slang

Examples

The old man finally croaked last night after a long illness.
He joked that he'd croak if he ever had to run a marathon.
Everyone thought the villain would croak at the end of the movie, but he survived.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to cease living; stop being alive
  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - (idiom) to die
  • Antonym: live - to remain alive; to continue existing

Etymology

Imitative of the harsh sound made by a frog or raven, later extended to mean 'to die' (slang, late 19th century).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
