Gazella

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈzɛlə / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of antelopes comprising the typical gazelles of Africa and Asia, known for their slender build and swift movements.
genus of typical gazelles

Examples

The genus Gazella includes several well-known species found in Africa and Asia.
Researchers observed the migration patterns of Gazella in the savanna.
Members of the Gazella genus are admired for their agility and grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazelle - a small, graceful antelope known for its speed

Etymology

From Latin 'gazella', from Arabic 'ghazāl' (gazelle).

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈzɛlə / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of small to medium-sized antelopes known as typical gazelles, native to Africa and Asia
genus of typical gazelles

Examples

The Gazella genus includes several species known for their speed and agility.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Gazella populations in the wild.
Many animals in the Gazella genus inhabit arid grasslands and open plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazelle - a graceful antelope of Africa and Asia with slender legs and curved horns

Etymology

From Latin 'gazella', from Arabic 'ghazāl' (gazelle).
