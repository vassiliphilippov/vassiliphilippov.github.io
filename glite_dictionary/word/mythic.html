mythic

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, resembling, or characteristic of myth; having the quality or nature of a myth.
relating to or like a myth

Examples

The painting depicts figures with a mythic presence, blending history and fantasy.
His achievements on the football field reached mythic proportions among fans.
She told a story with mythic themes of heroism and transformation.

Synset Examples

a novel of almost mythic consequence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythical - relating to or existing only in myths

Etymology

From Greek muthikos (pertaining to myth), from muthos (myth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03007480-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling myth; based on or told of in traditional stories, especially lacking factual basis or historical validity.
relating to myths or traditional stories

Examples

The ancient city was said to be founded by mythic heroes whose deeds inspired generations.
The mythic creature terrified villagers in stories passed down through centuries.
Some believe the origins of the river are mythic rather than historical.

Synset Examples

mythical centaurs
the fabulous unicorn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythical - existing only in myths; not real or factual

Etymology

From Greek mȳthikos (pertaining to myths), from mȳthos (myth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01942776-s
Synset POS: s
