Ashur

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈɑːʃʊə / (BrE)
the chief god of the Assyrians, associated with military power and empire, and identified with the Babylonian god Anshar
chief god of the Assyrians

Examples

The ancient Assyrians built temples to honor Ashur, their chief deity.
Reliefs from Nineveh often depict Ashur as a powerful figure guiding the Assyrian kings.
Scholars believe that the worship of Ashur played a crucial role in unifying the Assyrian Empire.

Etymology

From Akkadian Aššur, the name of the Assyrian city and its patron deity.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɑːʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈaʃʊə / (BrE)
an ancient Assyrian city on the Tigris River, which served as the traditional capital of Assyria, located just south of present-day Mosul in Iraq
ancient Assyrian city on the Tigris in Iraq

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered numerous artifacts from Ashur that reveal details about ancient Assyrian culture.
The ancient city of Ashur was once the political and religious heart of the Assyrian Empire.
Today, the ruins of Ashur are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
