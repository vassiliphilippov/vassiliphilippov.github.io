financier

1. VERB

/ fɪˈnænsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / fɪˈnænsiːeɪt / (BrE)
to conduct or manage financial operations; to provide funding or operate as a financier.
conduct or manage financial operations

Examples

The multinational corporation financiers several large infrastructure projects each year.
He made his fortune by shrewdly financiering startups during the tech boom.
Bankers were accused of illegally financiering political campaigns under false pretenses.

Etymology

From French 'financier' (one who manages finances), ultimately from 'finance' (to end or settle a debt).

WordNet ID: oewn-02422085-v

2. NOUN

/ fɪˌnænˈsɪr / (AmE), / ˌfɪn.ænˈsɪə / (BrE)
a person skilled in managing large-scale financial operations or investments, especially in banking or business.
expert in large-scale finance

Examples

The billionaire financier donated large sums to charity each year.
She built her career as a successful financier in New York's investment sector.
Rumors about the financier's next big deal spread quickly in the business world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banker - a person who manages or owns a bank

Etymology

From French 'financier', from 'finance' meaning 'finance, endowment'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10109789-n
Synset POS: n
