to forswear

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈswɛr / (AmE), / fɔːˈswɛə / (BrE)
to formally and publicly renounce or reject a previously held belief, statement, or practice, especially under pressure
formally renounce a belief or practice

Examples

After joining the new faith, she decided to forswear her former religious beliefs.
Many politicians are urged to forswear dishonest practices when they take office.
He solemnly forswore any involvement with the controversial group.

Synset Examples

He retracted his earlier statements about his religion
She abjured her beliefs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abjure - to renounce or reject something formally
  • Synonym: to recant - to withdraw a statement or belief formally

Etymology

From Old English 'forswerian', from 'for-' (away, renounce) + 'swerian' (to swear).
