emulsion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmʌlʃən / (BrE)
a light-sensitive coating on photographic paper or film, consisting of fine grains of silver bromide suspended in gelatin, used to capture images
photographic coating of silver bromide in gelatin

Examples

The photographic film has a thin layer of emulsion on its surface.
Handle negatives carefully to avoid damaging the emulsion.
The quality of the emulsion determines the sharpness of the final image.

Etymology

From Latin *emulsio* (milking out), from *emulgere* (to milk out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmʌlʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A mixture of two liquids that do not normally mix, where one liquid contains tiny droplets dispersed in the other.
mixture of two unmixable liquids

Examples

The salad dressing separated because the emulsion broke down.
Milk is a natural emulsion of fat in water.
Pharmaceutical creams often rely on the stability of their emulsion for effectiveness.

Synset Examples

an oil-in-water emulsion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suspension - mixture in which particles are dispersed throughout a fluid but are large enough to settle out

Etymology

From Latin 'emulgere' (to milk out), from 'e-' (out) + 'mulgere' (to milk).
