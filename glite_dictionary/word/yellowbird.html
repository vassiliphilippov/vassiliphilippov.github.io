yellowbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌbɜːd / (BrE)
an American finch whose male has bright yellow plumage in summer, especially the American goldfinch.
American finch with yellow plumage

Examples

We spotted a bright yellowbird perched on the fence this morning.
The male yellowbird is most colorful during the summer breeding season.
Children watched as a flock of yellowbirds fluttered through the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American goldfinch - a small North American finch with yellow summer plumage

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌbɜrd / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A small American songbird with a yellow throat, typically a wood warbler.
American yellow-throated wood warbler

Examples

A brightly colored yellowbird perched on the willow branch, singing early in the morning.
The garden was alive with the cheerful calls of the yellowbird during spring.
We spotted a rare yellowbird while hiking through the Appalachian woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood warbler - a small insectivorous songbird of the family Parulidae
