to hasten

1. VERB

/ ˈheɪsən / (AmE), / ˈheɪsən / (BrE)
to make something happen sooner or more quickly; to accelerate the progress of something
make happen more quickly

Examples

The government hopes to hasten the recovery by introducing new policies.
We must hasten our preparations if we want to finish on time.
Strong winds hastened the spread of the wildfire.

Synset Examples

This should expedite the process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expedite - to make an action or process happen sooner or more quickly
  • Antonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned or slower

Etymology

From Old French 'hastier', based on haste (fast movement), possibly from Frankish or Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈheɪsən / (AmE), / ˈheɪsən / (BrE)
to do something quickly or to make something happen sooner
act quickly or make happen sooner

Examples

Realizing they were late, she began to hasten her pace toward the station.
In order to meet the deadline, we must hasten our preparations.
His apology did little to hasten the reconciliation between the two friends.

Synset Examples

We have to rush!
hurry — it's late!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with speed
  • Synonym: rush - to move or act rapidly
  • Antonym: delay - to postpone or slow down an action

Etymology

From Old French 'haster', from Frankish *haistjan (to hasten, urge on).

3. VERB

/ ˈheɪsən / (AmE), / ˈheɪsən / (BrE)
to cause something to happen more quickly; to accelerate the occurrence of something
make something happen sooner

Examples

The crisis seemed to hasten the government's decision-making process.
Their reckless spending could hasten the company's bankruptcy.
A poor diet may hasten the onset of certain diseases.

Synset Examples

the infection precipitated a high fever and allergic reactions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accelerate - to cause to happen faster; to increase speed or rate
  • Antonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned; to defer

Etymology

From Middle English *hasten*, from Old French *haste* (haste).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01648064-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈheɪsən / (AmE), / ˈheɪsən / (BrE)
to move quickly or act with haste
move quickly; act with haste

Examples

We need to hasten if we want to catch the last train.
She hastened through the crowded market to meet her friends on time.
Not wanting to be late, he hastened his steps as the rain began to fall.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move slowly or waste time

Etymology

From Middle English hasten, from Old French haster, from Frankish *hastjan (to hurry).
