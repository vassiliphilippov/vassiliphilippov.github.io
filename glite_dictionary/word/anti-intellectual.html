anti-intellectual

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌɪntl̩ɛkˈtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌɪntl̩ɛkˈtjuːəl / (BrE)
A person who is opposed to or uninterested in intellectual activities or pursuits.
person opposed to intellectual pursuits

Examples

The new policy appealed to the anti-intellectual, who dismissed expert advice as unnecessary.
Many anti-intellectuals argue that practical experience is more valuable than academic learning.
Being labeled an anti-intellectual can sometimes stifle productive debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intellectual - a person who engages in critical study and thought

Etymology

Formed from anti- (against) and intellectual.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10442776-n

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌɪn.təlˈɛk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
opposed or indifferent to intellectual, artistic, or cultural values; showing hostility or lack of interest in knowledge and culture
opposed to intellectual or cultural values

Examples

Some political movements in history have taken an openly anti-intellectual stance.
His anti-intellectual comments alienated many of his colleagues at the university.
The new regime implemented anti-intellectual policies that discouraged critical thinking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intellectual - relating to or appealing to the intellect

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' meaning against, and 'intellectual', from Latin intellectus (understanding).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01336838-s
Synset POS: s
