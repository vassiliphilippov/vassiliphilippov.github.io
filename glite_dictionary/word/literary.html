literary

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri / (BrE)
used in or appropriate to formal written works rather than everyday speech or writing; characteristic of literature.
suitable for literature, formal written style

Examples

Her essay had a very literary tone, filled with poetic language and complex metaphors.
Some people find literary works harder to understand than everyday conversation.
He chose a literary style for his speech, quoting Shakespeare and using formal expressions.

Synset Examples

when trying to impress someone she spoke in an affected literary style

Related Terms

  • Antonym: colloquial - used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary

Etymology

From Latin 'literarius' (relating to letters or literature).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri / (BrE)
relating to literature, books, or the writing, study, or appreciation of literary works.
relating to literature or writing

Examples

She is well-known for her literary criticism of 20th-century novels.
The festival celebrates the literary achievements of local authors.
He took a great interest in literary history and classic works.

Synset Examples

literary criticism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonliterary - not relating to literature
  • Synonym: bookish - fond of books or studying; literary in nature

Etymology

From Latin litterarius, from littera (letter, writing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02841386-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬.ə.rer.i / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əri / (BrE)
having knowledge of or being well-read in literature; relating to literature or written works in general.
knowledgeable about literature; relating to literature

Examples

Although he's an engineer by trade, he is remarkably literary in his interests and reading habits.
She joined a literary society to discuss classic novels with fellow enthusiasts.
His literary background helped him write insightful book reviews.

Synset Examples

a literary style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookish - fond of reading and studying, scholarly
  • Antonym: unliterary - not having or showing knowledge of literature

Etymology

From Latin litterarius (relating to letters or literature), from littera (letter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01424241-s
Synset POS: s
