greenway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌweɪ / (BrE)
a strip of protected parkland, open space, or rural land, often surrounding or running through a town or city, used for recreation or environmental preservation
belt of parkland around or through a city

Examples

The city council proposed building a greenway to connect the parks and neighborhoods.
Cyclists and runners regularly use the greenway for exercise and commuting to work.
A new greenway along the river will help protect local wildlife habitats.

Etymology

Formed from 'green' (land, open space) + 'way' (route, path); term arose in late 20th-century urban planning.
