Echeneis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈniːɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪˈniːɪs / (BrE)
The genus of fish comprising the typical remoras, which are known for attaching themselves to larger marine animals.
genus of typical remoras (fish)

Examples

The Echeneis genus includes the well-known remora, famous for hitching rides on sharks and turtles.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Echeneis genus in the Indian Ocean.
Members of Echeneis have specialized suction discs on their heads for attaching to larger marine animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a suckerfish that attaches to larger fish or ships

Etymology

From Greek 'ekheneis', meaning 'remora' or 'a fish that holds ships back'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈniːɪs / (AmE), / ˌiːkɪˈniːɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Echeneididae, including fishes commonly known as remoras.
a genus of remoras (fish)

Examples

The Echeneis genus includes fish that attach themselves to sharks and ships.
Scientists studied the unique suction disc of Echeneis species.
The common remora, or Echeneis naucrates, can be seen in tropical oceans worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a fish known for attaching itself to other animals

Etymology

From Greek *ekhene* (remora, sea lamprey), from *ekhein* (to hold) + *naus* (ship).
