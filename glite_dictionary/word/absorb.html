to absorb

1. VERB

/ əbˈzɔrb / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːb / (BrE)
to take in or soak up (a substance or information), often by chemical or physical action; to assimilate or incorporate ideas or influences
to take in or soak up; assimilate

Examples

The towel will absorb most of the spilled juice if you act quickly.
Plants absorb sunlight during the process of photosynthesis.
She tried to absorb all the details of the painting during her visit.

Synset Examples

The sponge absorbs water well
She drew strength from the minister's words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assimilate - to take in and fully understand or integrate information or ideas
  • Antonym: release - to let go or allow to escape

Etymology

From Latin absorbere, from ab- (away) + sorbere (to suck in).
