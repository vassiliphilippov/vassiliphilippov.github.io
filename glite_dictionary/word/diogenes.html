Diogenes

1. PROPN

/ daɪˈɑːdʒəˌniz / (AmE), / daɪˈɒdʒɪniːz / (BrE)
(circa 400–325 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher and founder of Cynicism, known for rejecting social conventions and advocating a simple, ascetic lifestyle.
ancient Greek philosopher, founder of Cynicism

Examples

The teachings of Diogenes continue to influence modern philosophy today.
When asked about luxury, Diogenes famously replied that he owned nothing but his cloak and cup.
In class, we discussed how Diogenes challenged the values of Athenian society.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Διογένης (Diogenēs), meaning 'born of Zeus'.
