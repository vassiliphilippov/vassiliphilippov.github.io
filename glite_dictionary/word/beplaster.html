to beplaster

1. VERB

/ bɪˈplæstər / (AmE), / bɪˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
to cover a surface thickly or conspicuously, especially by pasting or coating something over it
cover thickly or conspicuously

Examples

The walls in the old house were beplastered with faded wallpaper and notices.
After the birthday party, the children's faces were beplastered with cake and frosting.
She loves to beplaster her notebook covers with stickers and drawings.

Synset Examples

The demonstrators plastered the hallways with posters
She let the walls of the apartment be beplastered with stucco

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - to cover or coat with plaster

Etymology

From be- (an intensifying prefix) + plaster (to cover or coat with plaster); modelled after similar be- compounds in English.
