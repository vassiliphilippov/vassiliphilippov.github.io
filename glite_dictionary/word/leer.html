leer

1. NOUN

/ lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
a suggestive, sly, or sneering look or smile, often with unpleasant or sexual intent
a suggestive or sneering look

Examples

She caught his leer from across the bar and immediately felt uncomfortable.
His leer made everyone in the office uneasy.
The villain's leer as he closed the door sent chills down her spine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glare - an angry or fierce stare

Etymology

From Middle English *leren* (to teach), later influenced by Old High German *līran* (to learn); the noun sense stems from the verb.

2. VERB

/ lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
to look or gaze at someone in an unpleasant, sly, or sexually suggestive way
look in a sly or suggestive way

Examples

He couldn't help but leer at the woman across the room, making her uncomfortable.
The old man sat in the corner, leering at everyone who passed by.
She felt uneasy as a group of strangers leered at her from the bar.

Synset Examples

The men leered at the young women on the beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ogle - to stare at in a lecherous manner
  • Degree Lower: gaze - to look steadily and intently

Etymology

Probably from Middle English lere (cheek, face), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
an unpleasant, often sideways look or facial expression showing contempt, scorn, or slyness, typically with a curled upper lip
scornful or sly facial expression

Examples

He gave her a cold leer as she walked past.
With a mocking leer, he turned away from his opponent.
The villain's leer made everyone in the room uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneer - a contemptuous or mocking smile, remark, or tone

Etymology

From Middle English *leren* (to teach), later influenced by Dutch *leren* (to look sideways).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06729422-n
Synset POS: n
