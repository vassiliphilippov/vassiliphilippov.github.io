seesaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌsɔː / (BrE)
A long, narrow board balanced in the middle so that, as one end goes up, the other goes down, used as a playground toy for children.
playground apparatus with up-and-down board

Examples

The children spent hours laughing on the seesaw at the park.
She pushed off the ground and soared upward on the seesaw.
One end of the seesaw touched the sand as the other lifted into the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teeter-totter - another term for seesaw, especially in American English

Etymology

Reduplication of 'saw', imitating the back-and-forth motion; used since the late 17th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈsiˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌsɔː / (BrE)
to move repeatedly up and down or backwards and forwards, as if on a seesaw
move up and down repeatedly

Examples

The stock market continued to seesaw throughout the day, making investors nervous.
The children seesawed on the balance beam, laughing as they tried to keep their balance.
Her mood seemed to seesaw between excitement and anxiety before the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock - to move gently back and forth or from side to side
  • Antonym: to stabilize - to make or become steady; stop fluctuating

3. VERB

/ ˈsiˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈsiːsɑː / (BrE)
to move unsteadily up and down or back and forth, with a rocking or alternating motion.
move with a rocking or alternating motion

Examples

The boat began to seesaw as the waves grew stronger.
Her emotions seesawed between hope and despair throughout the ordeal.
The ladder seesawed dangerously when he tried to climb it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock - to move gently back and forth or to and fro
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become steady or stable

Etymology

From the reduplication of 'see' (possibly from the French 'ci' = here) and 'saw' (possibly from 'ça' = there); imitates the back-and-forth motion of a teeter-totter.

Source: WordNet

/ ˈsiˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈsiːsɔː / (BrE)
to ride on a long, narrow board balanced in the middle so that, as one end goes up, the other goes down, especially as a children's activity
ride on a plank balanced in the middle

Examples

We seesawed together until sunset, laughing the whole time.
Will you seesaw with me while we wait for the others?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teeter-totter - to ride up and down on a balanced plank; American English synonym
