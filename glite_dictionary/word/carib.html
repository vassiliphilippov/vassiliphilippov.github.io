Carib

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪb / (AmE), / ˈkærɪb / (BrE)
the family of languages spoken by the Carib people, indigenous to parts of Central and South America
a family of indigenous South American languages

Examples

The linguist devoted her research to the study of the Carib languages.
Several dialects within the Carib family are endangered today.
The Carib language has influenced local place names throughout the Guianas.

Etymology

From Spanish Caribe, the name of an indigenous people of the Caribbean region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪb, kəˈriːb / (AmE), / ˈkærɪb, kəˈriːb / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous peoples originally living in northeastern South America and the Lesser Antilles
member of an indigenous people of South America and the Caribbean

Examples

The Carib were known for their seafaring skills in the Caribbean islands.
Some Carib traditions are still practiced in Dominica today.
Anthropologists have studied the language and culture of the Carib people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Arawak - member of another indigenous people of northeastern South America and the Caribbean

Etymology

From Spanish 'caribe', from the indigenous name for themselves; related to the origin of 'Caribbean'.
