covey

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvi / (AmE), / ˈkʌvi / (BrE)
a small flock of birds, especially partridges or grouse
small flock of birds (esp. partridges, grouse)

Examples

A covey of quail scattered as we walked through the field.
We caught sight of a covey of partridges hidden in the long grass.
During the hunt, they flushed a covey of grouse from the underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds

Etymology

From Old French *covee* (brood, hatch), from Latin *cubare* (to lie down).

Examples
