Nitrospan

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtroʊˌspæn / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəʊˌspæn / (BrE)
A trade name for nitroglycerin, used as a medication to widen blood vessels and treat heart conditions such as angina.
trade name for nitroglycerin medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Nitrospan to help control his chest pain during episodes of angina.
Patients should take Nitrospan exactly as directed to avoid possible side effects.
She always carried her Nitrospan tablets in case of a sudden heart attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitroglycerin - an oily, explosive liquid used in explosives and as a heart medication
