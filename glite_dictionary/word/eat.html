to eat

1. VERB

/ it / (AmE), / iːt / (BrE)
To have a meal; to take food at a regular meal time.
to have a meal

Examples

We usually eat at six o'clock in the evening.
She didn't eat before the meeting, so she was hungry.
They invited us to eat with them after the ceremony.

Synset Examples

We did not eat until 10 P.M. because there were so many phone calls
I didn't eat yet, so I gladly accept your invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine - to eat dinner or the main meal of the day
  • Antonym: fast - to abstain from eating food

Etymology

From Old English *etan*, related to German *essen* and Dutch *eten*.
