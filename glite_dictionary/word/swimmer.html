swimmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪmər / (AmE), / ˈswɪmə / (BrE)
a person who moves through water by swimming, either for sport, recreation, or survival
person who swims

Examples

Michael Phelps is a world-famous swimmer who has won multiple Olympic gold medals.
She is a strong swimmer and enjoys competing in local races.
The lake was crowded with swimmers enjoying the warm summer day.

Synset Examples

he is not a good swimmer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diver - a person who dives into water

Etymology

From Old English 'swimman' (to swim) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈswɪmə / (BrE)
a person, especially a trained athlete, who takes part in swimming competitions
trained athlete who competes in swimming

Examples

As an Olympic swimmer, she practices for hours every day.
The school hired a new swimmer to coach their team.
He aspired to become a world-class swimmer after watching the championships.

Synset Examples

he was an Olympic swimmer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimming - the sport or activity of moving through water using the limbs

Etymology

From swim + -er, formed from Old English 'swimman' (to swim)

Synset POS: n
