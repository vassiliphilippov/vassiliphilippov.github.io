transposon

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈpoʊˌzɑn / (AmE), / trænzˈpəʊzɒn / (BrE)
a segment of DNA that can move to different positions within a genome, especially in bacteria, often affecting the function of genes when inserted
mobile DNA segment
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered that a transposon had inserted itself into the gene, causing a mutation.
The movement of a transposon can disrupt the normal function of cellular DNA.
Some antibiotics resistances are spread by transposons in bacterial populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumping gene - alternative term for a mobile DNA segment

Etymology

From 'transpose' (to change position) + '-on' (suffix used in genetics for particles or units, as in 'codon' or 'operon').
