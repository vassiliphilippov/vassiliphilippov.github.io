colonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.lə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ə.nɪst / (BrE)
a person who settles in a new colony or region, often as part of a group from a foreign country
person who settles in a colony

Examples

The first colonist arrived on the island in the early 1600s.
Many colonists faced harsh winters and food shortages.
She traced her ancestors back to a colonist who sailed on the Mayflower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area and makes a home there
  • Antonym: aboriginal - a person native to a place, especially as contrasted with colonists

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, colony) via French coloniste.
