trabecular

1. ADJ

/ trəˈbɛkjələr / (AmE), / trəˈbiːkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or resembling trabeculae, the small supporting fibers or beams found in organs or tissues, especially in bone
relating to trabeculae or supporting fibers
medical

Examples

The trabecular structure of the bone provides strength without excessive weight.
Osteoporosis leads to a decrease in trabecular bone mass, making fractures more likely.
Researchers studied the trabecular patterns in the vertebrae to understand bone health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trabecula - a small supporting fiber or beam in body tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'trabecula' (a small beam), diminutive of 'trabs' (beam).
