Laguncularia

1. NOUN

/ ləˌɡʌŋkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ləˌɡʌŋkjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical mangrove trees in the family Combretaceae, commonly known as white mangroves
a genus of mangrove trees

Examples

Laguncularia is an important component of many tropical coastal ecosystems.
The white mangrove, classified as Laguncularia, is found throughout the Americas.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Laguncularia in salt marsh habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove - tropical tree growing in coastal saline water

Etymology

From Latin 'laguncula' (a little flask), referring to the flask-shaped fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ləˌɡʌŋ.kjəˈlɛə.ri.ə / (AmE), / ləˌɡʌŋ.kjʊˈlɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical mangrove trees in the family Combretaceae, found in coastal regions of the Americas, especially known for the white mangrove species.
genus of tropical mangrove trees

Examples

The white mangrove, classified as Laguncularia racemosa, is common in tropical coastal wetlands.
Researchers studied Laguncularia to understand its role in stabilizing shoreline ecosystems.
The genus Laguncularia contains important species for mangrove restoration projects.

Etymology

From Latin 'laguncula' meaning 'small flask', referring to the flask-shaped fruit of some species.
