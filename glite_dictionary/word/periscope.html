periscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈper.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈper.ɪ.skəʊp / (BrE)
An optical instrument consisting of a tube with mirrors or prisms, used to see over, around, or through an obstacle or object.
optical instrument for seeing over obstacles

Examples

The captain used a periscope to observe enemy ships from below the surface.
Children at the museum enjoyed looking through a periscope to see over the tall wall.
Modern submarines are equipped with a sophisticated periscope for surface observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telescope - an optical instrument for making distant objects appear closer
  • Synonym: microscope - an optical instrument used for viewing very small objects

Etymology

From French 'périscope', from Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'skopein' (to look).
