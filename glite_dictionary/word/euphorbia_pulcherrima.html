Euphorbia pulcherrima

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔːrbiə pʌlˈkɛrəmə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə pʌlˈkɛrɪmə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub with poisonous milky sap and showy, usually scarlet, petal-like leaves (bracts) surrounding small yellow flowers; commonly known as the poinsettia.
poinsettia plant; showy red-bracted tropical shrub

Examples

Every December, many people decorate their homes with the bright red Euphorbia pulcherrima.
The sap of Euphorbia pulcherrima can cause skin irritation if not handled carefully.
Euphorbia pulcherrima is widely grown as an ornamental plant for its colorful bracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poinsettia - common English name for Euphorbia pulcherrima

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Euphorbia' (from Euphorbus, Greek physician), species name 'pulcherrima' meaning 'most beautiful'.
