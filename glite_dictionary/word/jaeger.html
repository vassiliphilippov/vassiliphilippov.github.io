jaeger

1. NOUN

/ ˈjeɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈjeɪɡə / (BrE)
A large, predatory seabird of the genus Stercorarius that often pursues and harasses weaker birds to force them to drop their prey.
predatory seabird that chases other birds

Examples

On our trip to the Arctic, we spotted a jaeger harassing a group of terns.
The jaeger soared above the waves, searching for an opportunity to steal food from other birds.
Naturalists observed a jaeger swooping in to snatch a fish dropped by a puffin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - another name for birds of the genus Stercorarius

Etymology

From German *Jäger* meaning 'hunter.'
