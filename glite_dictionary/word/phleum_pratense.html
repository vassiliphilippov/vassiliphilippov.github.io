Phleum pratense

1. NOUN

/ ˈfliːəm præˈtɛns / (AmE), / ˈfliːəm præˈtɛns / (BrE)
A species of grass with long cylindrical flower spikes, commonly grown in northern United States and Europe as hay for cattle and horses.
a grass species grown for hay

Examples

Phleum pratense is often used as a primary hay crop in regions with cool climates.
The farmer planted several acres of Phleum pratense to provide feed for his livestock.
In botanical studies, Phleum pratense is recognized for its resilience and nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timothy - a common name for Phleum pratense, a type of grass grown for hay

Etymology

From Latin: 'Phleum' (a genus of grasses); 'pratense' (of meadows).
