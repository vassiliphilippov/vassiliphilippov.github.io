skyward

1. ADV

/ ˈskaɪwərd / (AmE), / ˈskaɪwəd / (BrE)
in the direction of the sky; toward the sky
toward the sky

Examples

The birds flew skyward as the dog ran toward them.
She pointed her telescope skyward to watch the stars.
Smoke rose skyward from the burning building.

Synset Examples

look skywards!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: earthward - toward the earth; in the direction of the earth

Etymology

From 'sky' + 'ward', with 'ward' used to form adverbs indicating direction.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00262350-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈskaɪwɚd / (AmE), / ˈskaɪwəd / (BrE)
Directed or moving toward the sky or upward.
toward the sky; upward direction

Examples

The birds soared skyward as dawn broke over the forest.
She tilted her head skyward, watching the clouds drift by.
The rocket shot skyward with a brilliant trail of fire.

Synset Examples

the soul in its heavenward flight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downward - directed toward a lower place or ground

Etymology

From 'sky' + suffix '-ward', meaning 'toward'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02500849-s
Synset POS: s
