Lasiocampidae

1. NOUN

/ læsi.oʊˈkæmpɪˌdi / (AmE), / læsi.əʊˈkæmpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of moths that includes tent caterpillars, eggars, and lappet moths, known for their hairy caterpillars and stout-bodied adults
family of moths including tent caterpillars

Examples

The family Lasiocampidae includes several species known for their tent-building caterpillars.
A scientist identified the moth as a member of the Lasiocampidae family.
Many Lasiocampidae larvae are covered with dense hairs that protect them from predators.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'lasios' (hairy) and 'kampē' (caterpillar, caterpillar's curve), referencing the hairy larvae.
