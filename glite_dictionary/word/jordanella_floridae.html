Jordanella floridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɔːrdəˈnɛlə flɔːrɪˈdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒɔːdəˈnɛlə flɒrɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A small North American fish with a dark-blue back and whitish sides marked with red stripes, native to swamps and streams of Florida.
a small colorful fish native to Florida

Examples

The aquarium featured several Jordanella floridae swimming among the aquatic plants.
Researchers observed Jordanella floridae in the shallow streams of central Florida.
Due to its vibrant colors, Jordanella floridae is popular among freshwater fish enthusiasts.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Jordanella' (after American ichthyologist David Starr Jordan) and the species name 'floridae' referring to Florida.
