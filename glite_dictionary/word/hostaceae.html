Hostaceae

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈsteɪ.siː.iː / (AmE), / hɒˈstæ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the genus Hosta, recognized in some botanical classification systems but not widely accepted
plant family; includes genus Hosta

Examples

Some botanists classify hostas in the family Hostaceae rather than Liliaceae.
The Hostaceae includes only the genus Hosta in most modern systems.
Debate continues over whether Hostaceae should be separated from Liliaceae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Liliaceae - the lily family, a large plant family sometimes divided to form Hostaceae
  • Synonym: Hosta - a genus of shade-loving perennial plants, also known as plantain lilies

Etymology

From the genus name Hosta with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ hoʊˈsteɪsi.i / (AmE), / hɒˈstæsi.i / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the genus Hosta, recognized in some botanical classification systems but not widely accepted
plant family including Hosta

Examples

The Hostaceae are known for their shade-tolerant foliage plants commonly called hostas.
Some botanists consider Hostaceae to be a separate family distinct from the Liliaceae.
The classification of Hostaceae has been debated among plant taxonomists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Liliaceae - a large family of flowering plants (the lily family) sometimes including Hosta

Etymology

From the genus name Hosta, after Austrian botanist Nicolaus Thomas Host (1761–1834), plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
