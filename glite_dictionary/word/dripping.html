dripping

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the quiet, repeated sound made when liquid falls in drops, often onto a surface
sound of liquid falling drop by drop

Examples

The steady dripping from the faucet kept her awake all night.
We heard the faint dripping of rainwater echoing in the hallway.
The dripping of coffee into the pot was the only sound in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

the constant sound of dripping irritated him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drip - the sound or action of a single drop falling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07397923-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A small amount of liquid that falls in drops, especially from something wet or leaking.
liquid falling in drops

Examples

Water dripping from the roof soaked the ground beneath the eaves.
There was a constant dripping from the leaky faucet in the kitchen.
After the storm, the steady dripping of rainwater could be heard everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drip - a single drop of liquid that falls from something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07422059-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈdrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
used as an adverb to mean extremely wet or saturated with liquid, especially in the phrase 'dripping wet'.
extremely wet (adverb, as in 'dripping wet')

Examples

After the sudden downpour, her clothes were dripping wet.
The dog came inside dripping after jumping in the lake.
He handed me an umbrella that was dripping wet from the rain.

Synset Examples

dripping wet
soaking wet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaking - extremely wet (adverb, as in 'soaking wet')
