statuesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌstætʃuˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌstatjuˈɛsk / (BrE)
(especially of a woman) attractively tall and dignified in a way suggestive of a statue.
tall and dignified like a statue

Examples

She walked into the room with a statuesque elegance that turned every head.
The statuesque model stood poised at the end of the runway, radiating confidence.
Her statuesque presence made her perfect for the lead role in the production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - having elegant movement or form
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or elegance

Etymology

From statue + -esque (French -esque, from Italian -esco, meaning 'in the style of').

2. ADJ

/ ˌstætʃuˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌstatjʊˈɛsk / (BrE)
(especially of a woman) attractively tall, graceful, and dignified, in a way that is reminiscent of a statue.
attractively tall and dignified

Examples

She walked into the room with a statuesque presence that drew everyone's attention.
The model's statuesque figure made her stand out on the runway.
A statuesque woman posed in the center of the portrait, commanding admiration from all viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - having elegance or beauty of movement or form
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease in movement or manner

Etymology

From statue + -esque (resembling), modeled on French 'statuesque'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02147208-s
Synset POS: s
