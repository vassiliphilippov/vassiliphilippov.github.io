Pelecanus erythrorhynchos

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs ˌɛrɪθroʊˈraɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˈkɑːnəs ˌɛrɪθrəʊˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A large North American pelican with white plumage and black wing feathers, known as the American white pelican.
American white pelican species

Examples

A group of Pelecanus erythrorhynchos soared gracefully above the lake, searching for fish.
The distinctive black flight feathers make Pelecanus erythrorhynchos easy to identify in the wild.
During migration, thousands of Pelecanus erythrorhynchos stop at inland lakes to feed and rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American white pelican - A large white pelican native to North America

Etymology

From Latin: Pelecanus (pelican) + erythrorhynchos (red-billed), from Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'rhynchos' (bill).
