egregious

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈɡriːdʒiəs / (AmE), / ɪˈɡriːdʒɪəs / (BrE)
Extremely bad in a way that is very noticeable or shocking; remarkably bad or flagrant.
outstandingly bad or shocking

Examples

His disregard for safety regulations was an egregious oversight that endangered everyone on site.
The report detailed several egregious violations of ethical standards.
Calling her late to the meeting was a minor issue, but lying to the team was truly egregious.

Synset Examples

a crying shame
an egregious lie
flagrant violation of human rights
a glaring error
gross ineptitude
gross injustice
rank treachery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagrant - conspicuously bad or offensive
  • Synonym: outrageous - shockingly bad or excessive
  • Degree Lower: minor - of lesser seriousness or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'egregius' (outstanding, distinguished), later used ironically in English to mean 'remarkably bad'.
