ELISA

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlaɪzə / (AmE), / ɪˈlaɪzə / (BrE)
a laboratory test that uses enzymes and color change to detect the presence of a substance, usually an antigen or antibody, in a sample
immunological laboratory assay
medical

Examples

The ELISA test is widely used to detect HIV antibodies in blood samples.
Researchers relied on ELISA to measure protein levels after treatment.
Doctors often recommend an ELISA to screen for certain infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoassay - a test that uses an immune response to detect a substance
  • Synonym: Western blot - a laboratory method used to detect specific proteins in a sample

Etymology

Acronym from Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay.
