GPS

1. NOUN

/ dʒi pi ɛs / (AmE), / dʒiː piː ɛs / (BrE)
a system that uses satellites and computers to determine the precise position of a receiver on Earth
satellite-based navigation system

Examples

Many hikers rely on their GPS to navigate remote trails safely.
The delivery driver used a GPS to find the quickest route through the city.
My phone's GPS signal was lost when I entered the tunnel.

Etymology

Abbreviation for 'Global Positioning System'.
