ordering

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of arranging things in a particular sequence or order
arranging in sequence

Examples

She was responsible for the ordering of the books on the shelves.
Improper ordering can make the instructions difficult to follow.
The ordering of the files was alphabetical for easy reference.

Synset Examples

there were mistakes in the ordering of items on the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the action of putting things into a neat, attractive, or required order

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'order,' from Latin 'ordinare' (to arrange, put in order).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdərɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement of separate elements in a logical or comprehensible sequence
logical arrangement of elements

Examples

The ordering of topics in the syllabus helps students build knowledge step by step.
Her systematic ordering of files saved everyone a lot of time.
Changing the ordering of the steps can lead to confusion in the procedure.

Synset Examples

we shall consider these questions in the inverse order of their presentation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which things follow each other
  • Antonym: disarray - a lack of order or organization

Etymology

From the verb 'order', ultimately from Latin 'ordinare' (to put in order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08474004-n
Synset POS: n
