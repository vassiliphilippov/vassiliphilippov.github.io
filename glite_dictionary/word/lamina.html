lamina

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmɪnə / (AmE), / ˈlamɪnə / (BrE)
a thin plate or layer, especially of bone or mineral
thin plate or layer (esp. bone, mineral)

Examples

The fossil was preserved between a lamina of shale and a layer of sandstone.
Each vertebra has a thin lamina forming part of the vertebral arch.
Microscopic examination revealed a delicate lamina of bone tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - a sheet, quantity, or thickness of material, typically one of several, covering a surface or body

Etymology

From Latin lamina (thin plate, layer).
