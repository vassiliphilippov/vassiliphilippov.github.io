beamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːmi / (AmE), / ˈbiːmi / (BrE)
emitting or appearing to emit rays of light; radiant or shining
radiating or shining with light

Examples

The beamy glow from the lamp filled the small room with warmth.
She smiled with a beamy expression, lighting up the entire gathering.
A beamy ray of moonlight broke through the clouds and illuminated the garden.

Synset Examples

the beaming sun
the effulgent daffodils
a radiant sunrise
a refulgent sunset

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant - shining or glowing brightly
  • Synonym: effulgent - shining brightly; radiant

Etymology

From 'beam' (ray of light) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbiːmi / (AmE), / ˈbiːmi / (BrE)
broad or wide in the beam, especially of a ship or boat.
broad in the beam (width), esp. of a vessel

Examples

The new yacht was noticeably beamy, providing extra stability on the water.
Because the vessel was so beamy, it offered generous cabin space for the passengers.
A beamy fishing boat is ideal for rough seas and heavy loads.

Synset Examples

a beamy cargo ship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad - having a wide distance from side to side
  • Antonym: narrow - having a small distance from side to side; not wide

Etymology

From "beam" (nautical: the width of a ship) + -y, forming adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02571744-s
Synset POS: s
