élan vital

1. NOUN

/ eɪˌlɑn viˈtɑl / (AmE), / eɪˌlɑ̃ viːˈtal / (BrE)
(biology, philosophy) the vital force or impulse, once hypothesized to drive the development and evolution of living organisms, especially as proposed by Henri Bergson.
hypothetical vital force in organisms (Bergson)

Examples

Some early biologists believed that an élan vital was needed to explain life's origin.
Bergson's theory of élan vital has been influential in discussions of consciousness and evolution.
While modern science rejects the idea of élan vital, it remains a part of philosophical history.

Etymology

French, meaning 'vital force' or 'vital impetus', coined by Henri Bergson.
