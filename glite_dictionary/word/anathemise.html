to anathemise

1. VERB

/ əˈnæθəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæθəmaɪz / (BrE)
to formally curse or denounce someone or something; to express strong condemnation or invoke harm.
formally curse or denounce

Examples

The religious council decided to anathemise the heretic for his controversial teachings.
She refused to anathemise her former friend, despite the betrayal.
In medieval times, to anathemise someone was considered a fate worse than death.

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to invoke harm or misfortune upon
  • Antonym: to bless - to invoke good or protection upon

Etymology

From Late Latin anathematizare, from Greek anathematizein (to devote to evil, to curse).

2. VERB

/ əˈnæθəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnaθ.ɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
To curse or declare (someone or something) to be evil or accursed, especially with a formal denunciation or threat of divine punishment.
curse or condemn formally

Examples

The church leaders chose to anathemise those who had rejected the doctrine.
He was anathemised by his peers for his heretical beliefs.
Some religious groups continue to anathemise modern scientific theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anathema - a person or thing cursed or condemned
  • Antonym: to bless - to ask for divine favor or protection

Etymology

From Late Latin anathema (ban, curse) via Medieval Latin anathematizare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00866574-v
Synset POS: v
