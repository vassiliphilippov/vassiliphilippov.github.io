superfamily

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic rank below an order and above a family, used in the classification of organisms.
taxonomic rank below order, above family
medical

Examples

In zoological classification, the superfamily contains several related families.
The butterfly belongs to the superfamily Papilionoidea, which includes many colorful species.
Researchers debated where to place the new genus within the existing superfamily.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: family - taxonomic rank below superfamily, above genus
  • Degree Higher: order - taxonomic rank above superfamily, below class

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (above) and 'family,' used in taxonomy since the 19th century.
