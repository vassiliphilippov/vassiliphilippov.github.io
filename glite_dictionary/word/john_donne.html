John Donne

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn dʌn / (AmE), / dʒɒn dʌn / (BrE)
(1572–1631) An English clergyman, metaphysical poet, and celebrated preacher known for his complex and inventive poetry.
English metaphysical poet and preacher

Examples

Many literary scholars have analyzed the John Donne poem 'Death, be not proud' for its complex metaphors.
John Donne is often cited as the leading figure among metaphysical poets in English literature.
The sermons of John Donne remain influential in the study of church history and religious rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: metaphysical poet - a poet belonging to a group of 17th-century English poets whose work is characterized by intellectual playfulness and elaborate metaphors
