to disembowel

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbaʊ.əl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbaʊ.əl / (BrE)
to remove the internal organs, especially the intestines, from the body of a person or animal
remove internal organs

Examples

The chef skillfully disemboweled the fish before cooking it.
In ancient rituals, priests would sometimes disembowel animals as sacrifices.
The investigators found evidence that the victim had been disemboweled.

Synset Examples

draw a chicken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eviscerate - to remove the internal organs of a person or animal

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + embowel, from Old French emboueler (put entrails into), from en- + boel (bowel).
