pridefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪd.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpraɪd.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having an excessively high opinion of oneself; excessive pride.
the quality of being excessively proud

Examples

His pridefulness often prevented him from accepting help from others, even when he needed it.
The leader’s pridefulness blinded her to the concerns of her team.
Critics attributed the downfall of the company to the CEO’s excessive pridefulness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction in one's achievements
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness

Etymology

Formed from 'prideful' + '-ness'; 'pride' from Old English *prȳde* (proud, haughty).
