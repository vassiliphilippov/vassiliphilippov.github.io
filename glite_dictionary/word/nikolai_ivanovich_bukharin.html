Nikolai Ivanovich Bukharin

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkoʊlaɪ ˌivaˈnoʊvɪtʃ buˈkɑrɪn / (AmE), / nɪˈkɒlaɪ ˌivəˈnəʊvɪtʃ bʊˈkɑːrɪn / (BrE)
(1888–1938) A prominent Bolshevik leader in Russia, known for advocating gradual collectivization of agriculture and later executed during Stalin's purges.
Russian Bolshevik revolutionary and theorist

Examples

As a leading Bolshevik, Nikolai Ivanovich Bukharin played a key role in the early Soviet government.
Historians often debate whether Nikolai Ivanovich Bukharin could have shaped Soviet policies differently if he had survived.
Stalin ordered the execution of Nikolai Ivanovich Bukharin during the Great Purge in 1938.
