Albert Schweitzer

1. PROPN

/ ˈæl.bɚt ˈʃwaɪ.t͡sɚ / (AmE), / ˈal.bət ˈʃvaɪ.t.sə / (BrE)
(1875–1965) A French philosopher, physician, and organist, known for his lifelong humanitarian work as a medical missionary in Gabon, Africa.
French philosopher and medical missionary (1875–1965)

Examples

Albert Schweitzer devoted much of his life to providing medical care in Africa.
The hospital founded by Albert Schweitzer continues to serve patients in Gabon today.
Many scholars admire Albert Schweitzer for his philosophy of 'Reverence for Life.'
