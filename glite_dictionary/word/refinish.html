to refinish

1. VERB

/ riˈfɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
to give a new surface to something, especially by removing the old finish and applying a new one
give a new surface, especially to furniture

Examples

We decided to refinish the hardwood floors instead of replacing them.
She spent all weekend refinishing the old kitchen cabinets.
The company offers a service to refinish worn-out antique tables.

Synset Examples

refinish the dining room furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - repair or renovate something to return it to its original condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + finish (bring to a surface or end).
