Himantopus mexicanus

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəpəs ˌmɛksɪˈkænəs / (AmE), / hɪˌmantəpəs ˌmɛksɪˈkanəs / (BrE)
A species of stilt found in the southwestern United States to northern South America, characterized by black plumage extending from the head down the back of the neck.
a stilt bird of the Americas with black neck and back

Examples

The wetlands are home to many bird species, including the distinctive Himantopus mexicanus.
Researchers observed a flock of Himantopus mexicanus wading through the shallow water in search of food.
Due to habitat loss, the population of Himantopus mexicanus has become a concern among conservationists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-necked stilt - common English name for Himantopus mexicanus

Etymology

From Latin: genus Himantopus, species mexicanus, named for Mexico.
