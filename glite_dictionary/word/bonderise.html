to bonderise

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːndəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɒndəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to coat a metal, especially steel, with a protective phosphate layer to prevent corrosion
coat metal to prevent corrosion

Examples

The manufacturer decided to bonderise the steel sheets for extra protection.
If you bonderise the metal parts, they will last much longer in harsh environments.
Engineers recommend that all exposed steel be bonderised before assembly.

Synset Examples

bonderize steel

Etymology

From the trade name 'Bonderite', a brand for phosphate coating processes.
