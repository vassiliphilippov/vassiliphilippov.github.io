confessor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛsər / (AmE), / kənˈfɛsə / (BrE)
a priest who hears confessions and grants absolution to penitents
priest who hears confessions

Examples

The confessor listened patiently as parishioners revealed their sins.
After the ceremony, the confessor offered words of comfort and guidance.
He sought out a confessor before embarking on his journey of repentance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: penitent - a person who confesses sins and seeks forgiveness from a priest

Etymology

From Latin confessor, meaning 'one who confesses' or 'one who hears confession'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛsər / (AmE), / kənˈfɛsə / (BrE)
a person who admits or reveals information, especially one disclosing facts that may be damaging to themselves
person who confesses to something

Examples

After hours of questioning, the police finally got a confessor to admit to the crime.
The confessor revealed details that only the perpetrator could have known.
Sometimes a confessor seeks forgiveness after coming forward with the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who gives testimony, often in legal settings

Etymology

From Latin 'confessor', from 'confiteri' (to confess).
