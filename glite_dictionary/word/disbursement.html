disbursement

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈbɜrsmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːsmənt / (BrE)
the payment of money, especially from a fund or account, often for goods or services
the act of paying out money

Examples

The disbursement of funds will occur at the end of the fiscal year.
Please submit all receipts to ensure proper disbursement to staff members.
Each disbursement must be documented for auditing purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the act of paying money
  • Antonym: reimbursement - money paid back after an expense

Etymology

From Old French 'desbourser' (to disburse), from 'des-' (away) + 'bours' (purse).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈbɜrsmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːsmənt / (BrE)
the act of paying out or distributing money, especially from a fund or account
the act of paying out money

Examples

The quarterly disbursement of funds to the departments occurs in April.
She handled the disbursement of grants to all successful applicants.
Before making any disbursement, the accountant double-checked all invoices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the act of paying money
  • Antonym: receipt - the act of receiving money

Etymology

From Old French 'desbourser' (to pay out), from 'des-' (out) + 'bours' (purse)
