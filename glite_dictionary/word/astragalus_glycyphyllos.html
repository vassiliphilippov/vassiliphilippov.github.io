Astragalus glycyphyllos

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈɡeɪləs ˌɡlɪsɪˈfɪloʊs / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈɡeɪləs ˌɡlɪsɪˈfɪlɒs / (BrE)
A European perennial plant of the pea family, commonly known as wild liquorice, with compound leaves and yellowish flowers.
European perennial plant (wild liquorice)

Examples

Astragalus glycyphyllos is commonly found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.
Herbalists have traditionally used Astragalus glycyphyllos for its reputed medicinal properties.
The distinctive yellowish flowers of Astragalus glycyphyllos appear in late spring.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Astragalus and species epithet glycyphyllos, from Greek 'glykys' (sweet) + 'phyllon' (leaf).
