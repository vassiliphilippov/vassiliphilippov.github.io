skyway

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌweɪ / (BrE)
a designated route used by airplanes when flying from one airport to another
designated route for airplane flights

Examples

Pilots must stick to the assigned skyway during their flights between the two cities.
A congested skyway can delay arrivals and departures at busy airports.
Modern navigation systems help aircraft stay on the correct skyway throughout their journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airway - an official route in the sky for aircraft

Etymology

Formed from 'sky' + 'way', modeled after 'highway'.
