relic

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪk / (BrE)
an object kept because of its sentimental value or association with someone or something important from the past
object kept for sentimental value

Examples

He kept his grandmother's watch as a relic of his childhood.
The old photograph album is a precious relic from their family history.
Her wedding dress became a relic cherished by future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keepsake - an object kept in memory of the person who gave it or originally owned it
  • Synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a place, event, or person

Etymology

From Latin 'reliquiae' (remains), from 'relinquere' (to leave behind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪk / (BrE)
An object from the past that has survived to the present, often valued for its historical or cultural significance.
object surviving from the distant past

Examples

The ancient vase on display is a relic from the Roman Empire.
Many tourists visit the temple to marvel at the relic of its original foundation stones.
The museum's collection includes a relic of medieval armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquity - an object from ancient times, especially one of archaeological or historical interest
  • Synonym: heirloom - a valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations

Etymology

From Latin 'reliquiae' (remains), from 'relinquere' (to leave behind).
