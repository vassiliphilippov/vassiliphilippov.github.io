diversionist

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvɝːʒənɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who commits sabotage or deliberately causes disruption, especially to divert attention from an intended target
person who commits sabotage or disruption

Examples

The authorities arrested a diversionist suspected of orchestrating the attack on the railway.
During wartime, a diversionist might be sent behind enemy lines to disrupt communications.
The police believe the train derailment was caused by a diversionist acting on behalf of a rival group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saboteur - a person who deliberately destroys or damages property

Etymology

From 'diversion' (distraction or turning aside) + '-ist' (person who practices).
