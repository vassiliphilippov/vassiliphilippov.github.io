outset

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.sɛt / (BrE)
the beginning or start of something
beginning; start

Examples

We made our plans clear at the outset of the meeting.
At the outset of her career, she moved to London to seek better opportunities.
It's important to be honest from the very outset of any relationship.

Synset Examples

they got an early start
she knew from the get-go that he was the man for her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the point in time at which something begins
  • Antonym: end - the final part of something

Etymology

From the phrase 'to set out,' meaning to begin a journey or undertaking, from Middle English.
