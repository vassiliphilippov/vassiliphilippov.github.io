carafe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræf / (AmE), / kəˈræf / (BrE)
a glass bottle, often with a flared lip, used for serving water or wine, typically without a stopper
glass bottle for serving drinks

Examples

The waiter brought a carafe of water to the table.
She poured the red wine from the carafe into the glasses.
A glass carafe sat in the center of the breakfast table, filled with fresh orange juice.

Etymology

From French 'carafe', from Italian 'caraffa'.
