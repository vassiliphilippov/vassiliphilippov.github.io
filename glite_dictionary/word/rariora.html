rariora

1. NOUN

/ rɑːriˈɔːrə / (AmE), / rɑːrɪˈɔːrə / (BrE)
Rare objects or items that are especially sought after by collectors, typically those with significant value, age, or uniqueness.
rare collector's items

Examples

The museum's new exhibit features several rariora from the sixteenth century.
Collectors travel from around the world to acquire rariora for their private collections.
Auction houses regularly handle rariora, including rare books, manuscripts, and antiques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rarity - something that is rare

Etymology

From Latin 'rariora', neuter plural of 'rarior', meaning 'rarer things'.
