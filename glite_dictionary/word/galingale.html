galingale

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælɪnˌɡeɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪnɡeɪl / (BrE)
A European sedge with rough-edged leaves, reddish flower spikelets, and aromatic roots, commonly found in wet habitats.
European sedge with aromatic roots

Examples

The galingale thrived along the marshy banks, its aromatic roots prized by herbalists.
She identified the plant as galingale, noting its distinctive reddish flowers.
Local folklore claims that galingale has medicinal properties when used in traditional remedies.

Etymology

From Old French 'galingal', from medieval Latin 'galingala', from Arabic 'khalanjān' (galangal), though unrelated to culinary galangal.
