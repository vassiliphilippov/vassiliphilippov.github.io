to freshen

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃən / (BrE)
to make something feel or become fresh or fresher
make or become fresh

Examples

Opening the windows will freshen the air in this room.
She sprayed lemon-scented cleaner to freshen the kitchen.
A quick shower in the morning helps to freshen me before work.

Synset Examples

The cool water refreshed us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refresh - to give new strength or energy to; to reinvigorate

Etymology

From 'fresh' + '-en' (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
to make or become fresh or fresher; to restore to a fresher state or appearance
make or become fresh again

Examples

She opened the window to freshen the air in the room.
I took a quick shower to freshen up before the meeting.
They added lemon slices to the water to freshen its taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refresh - to make someone less tired or restore energy; to revive or reinvigorate

Etymology

From 'fresh' + '-en', a verb-forming suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024642-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃən / (BrE)
to make oneself or something feel or look clean, cool, or refreshed again
make or become fresh or clean again

Examples

I need to freshen up before dinner after working outside all day.
She always likes to freshen her makeup before a meeting.
After a long drive, we stopped at the rest area to freshen ourselves.

Synset Examples

She freshened up after the tennis game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refresh - to restore strength or energy; to revive

Etymology

From English 'fresh' plus the suffix '-en' (forming verbs).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
