bioattack

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊ.əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊ.əˌtæk / (BrE)
an attack in which bacteria, viruses, or toxins are used to harm people, animals, or food supplies
attack using biological agents

Examples

The government raised the alert level after intelligence suggested a possible bioattack on major cities.
Scientists are developing new vaccines to help protect the population in case of a bioattack.
A bioattack could have devastating effects on agriculture and livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bioterrorism - the use of biological agents to intimidate or terrorize
  • Antonym: chemical attack - an attack using chemical agents

Etymology

Formed from 'bio-' (from Greek 'bios', meaning 'life') + 'attack'.
