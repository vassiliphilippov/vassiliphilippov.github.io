accurate

1. ADJ

/ ˈækjərət / (AmE), / ˈækjʊrət / (BrE)
exact, correct in all details, and free from error; precisely conforming to fact or truth.
correct and exact; free from error

Examples

The report contained accurate information based on reliable sources.
It's important to use accurate measurements in scientific experiments.
Her accurate description helped the police find the missing child.

Synset Examples

a precise image
a precise measurement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precise - exact, clear, and correct in detail
  • Antonym: inaccurate - not correct or exact; containing errors

Etymology

From Latin 'accuratus', past participle of 'accurare' (to take care), from 'ad-' (to) + 'cura' (care).
