lazily

1. ADV

/ ˈleɪzəli / (AmE), / ˈleɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a slow, relaxed, or unenergetic manner, as if unwilling to put in effort.
in a slow or unenergetic manner

Examples

She stretched lazily on the sofa, enjoying her afternoon nap.
The cat lazily batted at the toy without much interest.
Clouds drifted lazily across the summer sky, undisturbed by the gentle breeze.

Synset Examples

I watched the blue smoke drift lazily away on the still air

Related Terms

  • Antonym: energetically - in a way that shows a lot of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

Formed from 'lazy' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Lazy' possibly from Low German 'lasich' (feeble, weak).

2. ADV

/ ˈleɪzɪli / (AmE), / ˈleɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a slow, relaxed, or idle manner, without energy or motivation.
in a slow or idle manner

Examples

He lazily stretched out on the couch and watched the clouds drift by.
The cat lazily batted at the string but soon lost interest.
She lazily flipped through the magazine, barely glancing at the articles.

Synset Examples

this is what I always imagined myself doing in the south of France, sitting idly, drinking coffee, watching the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idly - in a lazy or inactive manner
  • Antonym: actively - in an energetic or active manner

Etymology

From 'lazy' (of uncertain origin, possibly from Low German *lasich* 'sluggish') + '-ly', an adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00363577-r
Synset POS: r
