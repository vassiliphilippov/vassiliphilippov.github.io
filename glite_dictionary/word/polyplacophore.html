polyplacophore

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈplækəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈplækəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a primitive elongated marine mollusk with bilateral symmetry, having a mantle covered with eight calcareous plates; a member of the class Polyplacophora (chitons)
marine mollusk with eight shell plates

Examples

The polyplacophore crawled slowly across the rocky tide pool, its plates gleaming under the water.
Many biologists study the polyplacophore for clues about the evolution of mollusks.
The shell of a polyplacophore is made up of eight distinct plates, allowing it to flex over rough surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chiton - a marine mollusk of the class Polyplacophora

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'plax' (plate) + '-phoros' (bearing), meaning 'bearing many plates'.
