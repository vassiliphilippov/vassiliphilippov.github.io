Chang Jiang

1. PROPN

/ tʃɑŋ dʒjɑŋ / (AmE), / tʃæŋ dʒæŋ / (BrE)
the longest river in Asia, flowing east from Tibet to the East China Sea near Shanghai; also known as the Yangtze River
the longest river in Asia, also known as the Yangtze

Examples

The Chang Jiang is a critical waterway for transportation and commerce in China.
Pollution levels in the Chang Jiang have become a major environmental concern.
Many historic cities are located along the banks of the Chang Jiang.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yangtze River - the longest river of Asia, also called Chang Jiang

Etymology

From Chinese 長江 (Cháng Jiāng), meaning 'Long River'.
