instability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being unstable, likely to change or fail suddenly.
state of being unstable

Examples

The economic instability in the region has caused concern among investors.
Political instability can lead to unpredictable changes in government policies.
Sudden instability in the weather forced us to postpone the event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable
  • Synonym: precariousness - the state of being dangerously likely to fall or collapse

Etymology

From Latin 'instabilitas', from 'in-' (not) + 'stabilis' (stable).
