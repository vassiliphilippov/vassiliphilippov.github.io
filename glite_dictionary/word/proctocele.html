proctocele

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑk.təˌsil / (AmE), / ˈprɒk.təˌsiːl / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the rectum bulges or herniates into the vagina, often due to weakened pelvic muscles
rectal herniation into the vagina
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a proctocele following persistent pelvic discomfort.
A proctocele may develop after multiple difficult childbirths due to weakened pelvic muscles.
Surgical repair is sometimes necessary when a proctocele causes significant symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'proktos' (anus, rectum) + '-cele' (swelling, hernia).
