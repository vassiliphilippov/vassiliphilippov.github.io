bilabiate

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈleɪ.bi.ɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪ.bɪ.ət / (BrE)
(of a flower or corolla) having two lips or lip-like parts, typically referring to the form of certain flowers.
having two lips (esp. in botany)

Examples

The snapdragon's bilabiate flowers are easily recognized by their two-part structure.
Several members of the mint family possess bilabiate corollas that attract pollinators.
The botanist described the specimen's bilabiate shape as a distinguishing feature.

Synset Examples

the corolla of a snapdragon is bilabiate

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'labium' (lip).
