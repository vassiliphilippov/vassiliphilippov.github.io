dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌklɔːroʊdaɪˌfɛnaɪltrɪˌklɔːroʊˈiːθeɪn / (AmE), / daɪˌklɔːrədəɪˌfɛnaɪltrɪˌklɔːrəʊˈiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a highly toxic synthetic insecticide, known as DDT, formerly used widely but now banned in many countries due to its harmful environmental and health effects
a once-common toxic insecticide (DDT)
medical

Examples

The widespread use of dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane led to serious environmental damage in the mid-20th century.
Exposure to dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane has been linked to negative health effects in humans and wildlife.
Many countries have banned dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane due to its persistence in the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DDT - abbreviation for dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane, the pesticide

Etymology

From chemical terminology: dichloro- (two chlorines), diphenyl (two phenyl groups), trichloroethane (ethane with three chlorines).
