settle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl̩ / (BrE)
a long wooden bench with a back, often with arms and sometimes with a storage compartment beneath the seat, traditionally used in old-fashioned homes or inns
long wooden bench with a back

Examples

The antique settle in the hallway added a rustic charm to the cottage.
Guests would often rest on the sturdy oak settle by the fireplace.
He inherited a carved wooden settle from his grandparents' old farmhouse.

Etymology

From Old English setl (seat, bench), related to 'set'.
