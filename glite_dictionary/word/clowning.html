clowning

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklaʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the performance or enactment of comic acts, often by a clown or someone imitating clownish behavior
comic acts or performance, clown-like behavior

Examples

During the party, his clowning had everyone in stitches with laughter.
The director discouraged unnecessary clowning during serious rehearsal scenes.
Years of practicing clowning have made her an expert in physical comedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffoonery - silly or ridiculous behavior, foolish acts intended to amuse
  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking, sometimes humorous

Etymology

From 'clown' (NOUN) + '-ing' (suffix forming nouns indicating action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklaʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of behaving in a silly or comical way like a clown, especially to amuse others
acting like a clown; silly behavior

Examples

His constant clowning made everyone in the class laugh.
After a bit of clowning around, the children settled down to do their homework.
She scolded him for all his clowning during the important meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffoonery - foolish or silly behavior
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious and not joking

Etymology

From 'clown' + -ing (verbal noun suffix).
