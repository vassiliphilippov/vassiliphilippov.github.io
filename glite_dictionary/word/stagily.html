stagily

1. ADV

/ ˈsteɪdʒɪli / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒɪli / (BrE)
In an overly theatrical or exaggerated manner, as if on a stage.
in an exaggerated, theatrical way

Examples

He raised his eyebrows stagily during the performance to amuse the audience.
She sighed stagily, drawing everyone's attention to her supposed distress.
He always speaks stagily when telling stories, as if acting on a stage.

Synset Examples

‘I cannot show my face at her house,’ he declared theatrically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrically - in a manner suggesting the theater; exaggerated
  • Antonym: naturally - in a natural, unaffected manner

Etymology

From 'stagy' + '-ly', with 'stagy' meaning 'theatrical' from 'stage'.
