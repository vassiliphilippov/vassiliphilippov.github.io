daminozide

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmaɪnəˌzaɪd / (AmE), / dəˈmaɪnəˌzaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical used as a plant growth regulator, especially sprayed on fruit trees to control growth and synchronize harvest time
plant growth regulator chemical

Examples

The use of daminozide in apple orchards helped ensure the fruit matured uniformly.
Many growers stopped applying daminozide after concerns were raised about its safety.
Regulations surrounding daminozide have changed significantly over the years due to health studies.
