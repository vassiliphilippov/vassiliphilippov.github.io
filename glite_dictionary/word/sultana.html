sultana

1. NOUN

/ səlˈtænə / (AmE), / sʌlˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
A pale yellow, seedless variety of grape grown for making raisins and wine.
pale yellow seedless grape for raisins and wine

Examples

Many fruitcakes are packed with sultanas and nuts.
She bought a bag of sultanas for a healthy snack.
The vineyard is famous for its quality sultana grapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raisin - a dried grape

Etymology

From Italian 'sultana' (feminine of sultano, sultan), possibly referring to Turkish origins or methods.

2. NOUN

/ sʌlˈtænə / (AmE), / sʌlˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
A small, sweet, pale golden dried seedless grape, often used in baking and cooking.
a type of dried seedless grape

Examples

She added a handful of sultanas to the cake mixture for extra sweetness.
The fruit salad included sultanas, apples, and oranges.
You can substitute sultanas for raisins in most recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raisin - dried grape, generally dark in color, used in baking

Etymology

From Italian ‘sultana’ or French ‘sultane’, probably from Turkish ‘sultana’ (feminine of ‘sultan’), reflecting origins of the grape variety.
