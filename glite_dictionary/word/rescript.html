rescript

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈriːskrɪpt / (BrE)
an official or legally binding command or decision, especially one issued by a court or judge and recorded in the court record
a legally binding court command or decision
legal

Examples

The judge issued a rescript that finalized the case and required immediate compliance.
The legal team reviewed the rescript to ensure all details were properly recorded.
After months of deliberation, the Supreme Court released a rescript clarifying the law.

Synset Examples

a friend in New Mexico said that the order caused no trouble out there

Etymology

From Latin rescriptum (a written reply), from rescribere (to write back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈriːskrɪpt / (BrE)
A formal written reply by the Pope to an inquiry about a point of law or morality.
Papal reply to an inquiry

Examples

The bishop received a rescript from the Vatican clarifying the matter of doctrine.
In the nineteenth century, many disputes were settled through a papal rescript.
He submitted his question to Rome and awaited the official rescript addressing his concerns.

Etymology

From Latin rescriptum (something written back), from re- (again) + scribere (to write).

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈriːskrɪpt / (BrE)
a new version of a text or document that has been rewritten or revised
a rewritten text or document

Examples

The editor asked the author to submit a rescript after the initial draft was rejected.
Following the feedback, she produced a rescript which addressed all the reviewers' concerns.
The committee reviewed several rescripts before selecting the final version for publication.

Synset Examples

4. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈriːˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
the act of rewriting or revising a text, document, or other written work
the act of rewriting something

Examples

After reviewing the comments, the editor requested a complete rescript of the article.
The play underwent a significant rescript before its final production.
A thorough rescript of the policy document improved its clarity and effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rewrite - to write something again in a different way

Etymology

From Latin rescriptum (an official answer, something written back), from re- 'again' + scribere 'to write'.
