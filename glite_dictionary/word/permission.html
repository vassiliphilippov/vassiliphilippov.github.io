permission

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially allowing someone to do something, especially through a formal or written authorization
official authorization or consent

Examples

Employees must obtain permission from their manager before taking leave.
The school requires written permission from parents for students to go on field trips.
He entered the restricted area without permission, violating company policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval
  • Antonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something by law or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'permissio', from 'permittere' (to allow, to permit).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of allowing someone to do something; official approval or consent
authorization or consent to do something

Examples

You must obtain permission from your supervisor before taking leave.
The children need their parents' permission to go on the school trip.
He was granted permission to enter the restricted area after showing his ID.

Synset Examples

he asked permission to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consent - agreement or permission for something to happen
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of declining to give consent or permission

Etymology

From Latin 'permissio' (allowance, permission), from 'permittere' (to permit).
