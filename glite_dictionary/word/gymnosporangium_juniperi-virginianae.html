Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae

1. NOUN

/ d͡ʒɪm.nə.spəˈræn.dʒi.əm d͡ʒuːˌnɪ.pə.ri vɜːr.d͡ʒɪ.niˈeɪ.iː / (AmE), / d͡ʒɪm.nə.spəˈræn.dʒi.əm d͡ʒuːˌnɪ.pə.ri vɜː.d͡ʒɪ.niˈɑː.iː / (BrE)
a species of rust fungus that causes cedar-apple rust, resulting in orange-brown spots on apples, pears, and related plants
a rust fungus affecting apples and pears
medical

Examples

Orchards often struggle with Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae, which causes unsightly spots on apple leaves and fruit.
The spread of Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae can be reduced by removing nearby juniper hosts.
Researchers are studying how Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae infects both cedar and apple trees during its life cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rust fungus - a group of parasitic fungi that cause rust disease in plants
  • Synonym: cedar-apple rust - the common name for disease caused by Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae

Etymology

From Latin; 'Gymnosporangium' refers to the genus, meaning 'naked spore vessel', and 'juniperi-virginianae' references the host (Juniperus virginiana).
