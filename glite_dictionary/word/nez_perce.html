Nez Perce

1. NOUN

/ nɛz ˈpɜrs / (AmE), / nɛz ˈpɜːs / (BrE)
the Shahaptian language spoken by the Nez Perce people of the northwestern United States
language of the Nez Perce people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Nez Perce as a first language.
She is studying Nez Perce at university as part of her research on Native American cultures.
The dictionary includes a comprehensive list of words from Nez Perce.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Shahaptian - a family of Native American languages including Nez Perce and Sahaptin

Etymology

From French 'nez percé' (pierced nose), used by early French-speaking explorers referring to the Nez Perce people; also used for their language.

2. NOUN

/ nɛz ˈpɜrs / (AmE), / nɛz ˈpɜːs / (BrE)
A member of a Native American tribe of the Shahaptian people indigenous to the Pacific Northwest region of the United States.
member of a Native American tribe

Examples

The Nez Perce resisted forced removal from their ancestral lands in the 19th century.
She is proud of her heritage as a Nez Perce and works to preserve tribal traditions.
A noted Nez Perce leader was Chief Joseph, known for his eloquence and courage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nez Perce - the language spoken by the Nez Perce people

Etymology

French, literally 'pierced nose', referring to an early custom reported by French explorers.
