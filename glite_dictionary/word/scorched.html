scorched

1. ADJ

/ skɔrtʃt / (AmE), / skɔːtʃt / (BrE)
dried out or burned as a result of intense heat or excessive exposure to sunlight
dried or burned by intense heat

Examples

Much of the grass was scorched after weeks without rain.
They walked across the scorched earth where the fire had passed.
The plants looked scorched and wilted in the midday sun.

Synset Examples

a vast desert all adust
land lying baked in the heat
parched soil
the earth was scorched and bare
sunbaked salt flats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - very dry, especially because of hot weather
  • Synonym: sunbaked - heated and dried by the sun
  • Antonym: moist - slightly wet; not dry

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'scorch', of unknown origin; possibly related to Old French *escorcher* (to strip the skin).

2. ADJ

/ skɔrtʃt / (AmE), / skɔːtʃt / (BrE)
(of land or objects) burned or charred, especially so completely as to prevent use or recovery, often as a result of deliberate destruction.
burned, charred, or destroyed

Examples

The army left a scorched landscape behind them after retreating from the village.
Farmers surveyed the scorched fields, worried about the impact on their harvest.
The invaders implemented a scorched earth policy, leaving nothing useful for their enemies.

Synset Examples

Sherman's scorched earth policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burned - damaged or destroyed by fire or heat

Etymology

Past participle of 'scorch,' from Middle English 'scorchen.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00740055-s
Synset POS: s
