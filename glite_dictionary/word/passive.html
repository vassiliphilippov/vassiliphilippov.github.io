passive

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪv / (BrE)
Having little energy, initiative, or will; inclined to accept or submit rather than take action.
lacking energy or initiative

Examples

Laura's passive attitude made it difficult for her to stand up for herself.
The patient's passive behavior frustrated the nurses who wanted him to engage in therapy.
John remained passive during the heated discussion, refusing to voice his opinion.

Synset Examples

Much benevolence of the passive order may be traced to a disinclination to inflict pain upon oneself

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - showing energy, initiative, or readiness to act

Etymology

From Latin passivus, from passus, past participle of pati (to suffer).

WordNet ID: oewn-00039705-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv / (BrE)
(grammar) the form or voice of a verb in which the subject is acted on rather than performing the action, as in 'The ball was thrown by the boy.'
grammatical voice with acted-upon subject

Examples

Many students have trouble identifying the passive in complex sentences.
Writers are often advised to avoid the passive in favor of the active voice.
The passive can sometimes make writing seem less direct or clear.

Synset Examples

‘The ball was thrown by the boy’ uses the passive voice
‘The ball was thrown’ is an abbreviated passive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - the grammatical voice where the subject performs the action

Etymology

From Latin passivus (capable of suffering or feeling), from pati (to suffer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13825643-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb or sentence) expressing that the subject is affected by the action, rather than performing it.
grammatical form where subject receives action

Examples

In the sentence 'The cake was eaten by the children,' the verb is in the passive voice.
Some writers prefer using passive constructions to emphasize the object of the action.
The teacher explained how to change an active sentence into a passive one.

Synset Examples

academics seem to favor passive sentences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - describing a verb or sentence where the subject performs the action

Etymology

From Latin passivus (capable of suffering), from pati (to suffer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00042449-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪv / (BrE)
accepting or allowing what happens or what others do, without active response or resistance; especially peacefully resisting injustice without active confrontation
not actively resisting, especially nonviolent

Examples

The protesters adopted a strategy of passive resistance to avoid violent confrontation with the police.
Many historical movements succeeded through passive means, such as boycotting and peaceful marches.
She chose a passive approach, refusing to fight back but standing her ground firmly against injustice.

Synset Examples

passive resistance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaging or ready to engage in physically energetic pursuit, especially in contrast to passive

Etymology

From Latin passivus (capable of suffering or feeling), from passus, past participle of pati (to suffer).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
