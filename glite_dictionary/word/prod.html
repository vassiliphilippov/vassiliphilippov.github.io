prod

1. NOUN

/ prɑd / (AmE), / prɒd / (BrE)
a pointed stick or instrument used to poke, push, or drive animals or people into action
pointed instrument for poking

Examples

The farmer used a prod to guide the cattle into the barn.
He gave the tangled machine a sharp prod with a stick to get it working again.
Her impatient prod with her umbrella hurried us along the crowded street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goad - a pointed stick used to drive cattle or other animals

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'prodden' (to poke).

2. VERB

/ prɑd / (AmE), / prɒd / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to take action, often by reminding them or applying gentle pressure
urge or prompt to take action

Examples

Her friends tried to prod her into applying for the new job.
He needed to be prodded into finishing his homework before dinner.
Sometimes a gentle prod is all it takes to get someone moving.

Synset Examples

The other children egged the boy on, but he did not want to throw the stone through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Synonym: prompt - to cause someone to act
  • Antonym: to discourage - persuade someone not to do something

5. VERB

/ prɑd / (AmE), / prɒd / (BrE)
to push someone or something gently with a finger, stick, or similar object
push gently with a finger or object

Examples

She prodded him in the side to get his attention.
The farmer prodded the cow with a stick to make it move.
He gently prodded the sleeping dog to see if it would wake up.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to jab or prod someone or something
  • Synonym: jab - to push or poke something sharply or quickly

Etymology

Probably from late Middle English and related to the word 'proud', originally meaning to thrust forward.
