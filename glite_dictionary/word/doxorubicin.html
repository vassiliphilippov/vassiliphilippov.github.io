doxorubicin

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːksəˈruːbɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌdɒksəˈruːbɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic drug used in chemotherapy to treat various cancers
anticancer antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

Doxorubicin is commonly used as part of combination chemotherapy regimens for breast cancer.
The patient experienced hair loss as a side effect of doxorubicin treatment.
Doctors closely monitor heart function in patients receiving doxorubicin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daunorubicin - an antibiotic used as an anticancer drug, related compound

Etymology

From Italian *doxorubicina*, based on the antibiotic *daunorubicin* and the Latin *rubidus* ('red').
