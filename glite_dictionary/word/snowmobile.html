snowmobile

1. VERB

/ ˈsnoʊmoʊˌbiːl / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊməʊˌbiːl / (BrE)
to ride or travel by snowmobile, especially over snow-covered terrain
to ride a snowmobile

Examples

We decided to snowmobile across the frozen lake early in the morning.
Last winter, she snowmobiled through the forest during her trip to Canada.
He spends every weekend snowmobiling with his friends in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - to operate and control the direction and speed of a vehicle

Etymology

Formed from 'snow' and 'mobile', modelled after the noun 'snowmobile'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01961132-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊmoʊˌbiːl / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊməʊˌbiːl / (BrE)
a motorized vehicle designed for travel on snow, typically having skis at the front and tracks at the rear
motor vehicle for travel on snow

Examples

We rented a snowmobile to explore the frozen landscape.
Heavy snowfall meant that only a snowmobile could reach the remote cabin.
The guide led our group across the icy lake on his snowmobile.

Etymology

Formed from 'snow' and 'automobile', first used in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04259187-n
Synset POS: n
