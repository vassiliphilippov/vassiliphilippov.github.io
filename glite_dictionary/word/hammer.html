hammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmər / (AmE), / ˈhamə / (BrE)
A padded striker inside a piano that hits the strings to produce sound when a key is pressed.
padded striker in a piano

Examples

Each time you press a key, a hammer inside the piano strikes the string.
The technician replaced a worn hammer to improve the piano's tone.
If a hammer in the piano is misaligned, some notes may sound muted.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhamə / (BrE)
a hand tool with a heavy, rigid head attached to a handle, used for striking objects
hand tool for striking objects

Examples

He used a hammer to drive the nail into the wall.
The carpenter picked up his hammer and began working on the frame.
Make sure to hold the hammer firmly to avoid hitting your fingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallet - tool with a large, usually wooden head for striking
  • Degree Higher: sledgehammer - large, heavy hammer used for demolition
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light or gentle striking movement

Etymology

Old English *hamor* (hammer; gavel), related to Old Norse *hamarr* (stone, hammer).

9. NOUN

the small bone of the middle ear that is attached to the eardrum and transmits sound vibrations to the anvil (incus); also called the malleus
bone of the middle ear (malleus)
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the hammer transfers vibrations from the eardrum to the inner ear.
A loud noise can sometimes dislodge the hammer in the middle ear, affecting hearing.
Damage to the hammer can result in conductive hearing loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleus - the small bone in the middle ear, also known as the hammer
  • Degree Higher: anvil - the middle ear bone (incus) that the hammer connects to

Etymology

From Old English hamor (hammer), so named for its shape; the anatomical term comes from Latin malleus (hammer).
