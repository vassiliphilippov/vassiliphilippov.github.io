Sphecius

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfiːʃiəs / (AmE), / ˈsfiːsiəs / (BrE)
a genus of large solitary wasps known as cicada killers, which prey on cicadas
genus of cicada killer wasps

Examples

The Sphecius genus is well known for its members' habit of hunting cicadas.
One of the most common Sphecius species in North America is the eastern cicada killer wasp.
Scientists study Sphecius to better understand solitary wasp behavior.

Etymology

From Greek 'sphex' (wasp), used in taxonomy for related genera.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspiːʃiəs / (AmE), / ˈspiːsɪəs / (BrE)
a genus of large solitary wasps known as cicada killers, whose members prey on cicadas; native to North America and other regions.
a genus of cicada-killer wasps

Examples

The Sphecius genus includes the well-known eastern cicada killer wasp.
Researchers observed Sphecius species burrowing in sandy soils to create nesting chambers.
Several members of Sphecius are crucial for controlling cicada populations in their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada killer - a large solitary wasp that hunts cicadas; member of the genus Sphecius
  • Degree Higher: wasp - a stinging insect of the order Hymenoptera

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sphecius', derived from Greek 'sphex' (wasp).
