hiddenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪdənnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɪdənnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being concealed or not easily seen or understood
the state of being hidden or concealed

Examples

The hiddenness of the cave made it the perfect place for a secret meeting.
Some philosophers debate the hiddenness of God as a challenge to faith.
She was fascinated by the hiddenness of motives behind human actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or hidden
  • Antonym: openness - the state of being open, not concealed

Etymology

From 'hidden' (past participle of 'hide') + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting states or qualities).
