preexistent

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
existing before a particular event, period, or condition; previously present or in existence.
existing previously or before something

Examples

The theory is based on preexistent knowledge passed down through generations.
They incorporated preexistent structures into the new design of the building.
Variations on preexistent musical themes are common in classical compositions.

Synset Examples

variations on pre-existent musical themes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preexisting - existing previously or before something

Etymology

From Latin 'praeexistēns', past participle of 'praeexistere' (to exist before).
