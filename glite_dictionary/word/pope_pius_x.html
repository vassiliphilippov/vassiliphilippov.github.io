Pope Pius X

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˈpaɪ.əs ˌtɛnθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˈpaɪ.əs ˌtɛnθ / (BrE)
the Pope from 1903 to 1914, known for condemning religious modernism and being canonized as a saint in 1954 for his concern for the poor
Pope from 1903–1914; canonized saint

Examples

Pope Pius X was canonized in 1954, recognizing his dedication to the poor and the Church.
Many traditional Catholics admire the reforms implemented by Pope Pius X during his papacy.
The encyclicals issued by Pope Pius X had a lasting influence on Catholic doctrine.
