Branchiopoda

1. NOUN

/ bræn.kiˈɑ.pə.də / (AmE), / bræŋ.kɪˈɒ.pə.də / (BrE)
a class of primitive aquatic crustaceans found mainly in freshwater, including fairy shrimps, brine shrimps, tadpole shrimps, and water fleas
class of primitive freshwater crustaceans

Examples

Scientists discovered a rare species of Branchiopoda in the alpine lake.
Many Branchiopoda play a vital role in the food web of freshwater ecosystems.
The aquarium's exhibit on Branchiopoda features several types of tiny swimming crustaceans.

Etymology

From Greek 'branchia' (gills) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to their gill-footed structure.

2. NOUN

/ brænkiˈɑpədə / (AmE), / bræŋkɪˈɒpədə / (BrE)
a subclass of small, primitive aquatic crustaceans found mainly in fresh water, including fairy shrimps, brine shrimps, tadpole shrimps, and water fleas
subclass of freshwater crustaceans

Examples

The researcher studied several specimens of Branchiopoda collected from the pond.
Fairy shrimps and water fleas are typical members of the Branchiopoda subclass.
Environmental changes can greatly affect the populations within Branchiopoda communities.

Etymology

From Greek *branchia* (gill) + *pous, podos* (foot), referring to their gill-bearing legs.
