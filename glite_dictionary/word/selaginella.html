Selaginella

1. NOUN

/ sɛləˈdʒɪnələ / (AmE), / sɛləˈdʒɪnɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of moss-like evergreen plants, known as spike moss or little club moss, belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.
genus of spike moss or little club moss

Examples

The greenhouse features a variety of Selaginella species, all thriving in the humid conditions.
Botanists are studying how Selaginella adapts to changes in moisture and light.
Some gardeners use Selaginella as ground cover in shaded, damp areas.

Etymology

From New Latin, from the obsolete genus name Selago plus -inella (diminutive).

2. NOUN

/ sɛləˌdʒɪˈnɛlə / (AmE), / sɪləˌdʒɪˈnɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of moss-like, evergreen plants, commonly known as spike moss or little club moss, and the only genus in the family Selaginellaceae
genus of moss-like spike moss plants

Examples

Many species in the Selaginella genus are popular in terrariums due to their unique appearance.
The botanist collected several Selaginella samples during her expedition in the rainforest.
Some Selaginella varieties can survive periods of drying out, reviving when watered again.

Etymology

From New Latin Selaginella, diminutive of Selago, the name of a related plant genus.
