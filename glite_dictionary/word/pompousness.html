pompousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːmpəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being pompous; excessive self-importance, arrogance, or affected dignity.
quality of being pompous or self-important

Examples

His pompousness often alienated colleagues who preferred a more modest approach.
The speech was criticized for its pompousness and lack of genuine content.
She masked her insecurity with an air of pompousness that failed to impress anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomposity - The quality of being pompous, self-important, or pretentious

Etymology

From 'pompous' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'pomposus' (stately, magnificent).
