invalidity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.vəˈlɪdə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vəˈlɪdə.ti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being illogical or not valid, especially in an argument where the conclusion does not logically follow from the premises.
state of being illogical or not valid

Examples

The invalidity of his argument was clear once we analyzed the logical steps.
Law students are trained to recognize the invalidity of faulty syllogisms.
They discussed the invalidity of the conclusion based on the given evidence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: validity - the quality of being logically sound or cogent

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidus' (not strong; powerless).
