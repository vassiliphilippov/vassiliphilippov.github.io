Ebenaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːbəˈneɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌiːbɪˈneɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of fruit and timber trees found in tropical and warm regions, including ebony and persimmon trees.
family of trees including ebony and persimmon

Examples

Many valuable woods are produced by trees in the Ebenaceae family, such as ebony.
The Ebenaceae are important in tropical forestry for both their timber and fruit.
Persimmons, which belong to the Ebenaceae family, are cultivated in several warm regions around the world.

Etymology

From the genus name Ebenus, from Latin, ultimately from Greek 'ébenos' (ebony), with '-aceae' denoting a plant family.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛbəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɛbəˈneɪsiː-iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants comprising trees and shrubs, including ebony and persimmon, native to tropical and warm regions.
a plant family including ebony and persimmon

Examples

The Ebenaceae family includes many valuable timber trees such as ebony.
Persimmon trees, which belong to the Ebenaceae, are grown for their edible fruit.
Botanists studied the natural distribution of the Ebenaceae across tropical climates.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Ebenus' (ebony), with the standard botanical suffix -aceae, forming family names.
