bilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
having or affecting two sides, parts, or parties; involving two groups or countries
having two sides or involving two parties

Examples

The countries signed a bilateral trade agreement to enhance cooperation.
He experienced bilateral knee pain after running the marathon.
Bilateral symmetry is common in many animal species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilateral - affecting or involving only one side or party

Etymology

From Latin 'bilateralis', from 'bi-' (two) + 'lateralis' (sided).

2. ADJ

/ baɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
involving or affecting two sides, parties, or countries equally; undertaken by two parties
involving two sides or parties

Examples

The two countries signed a bilateral trade agreement to promote economic cooperation.
Bilateral talks between the companies resulted in a successful merger.
A bilateral contract requires both parties to fulfill their obligations.

Synset Examples

a bilateral agreement between the United States and Japan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilateral - performed by or affecting only one side or party

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'lateralis' (of the side)

3. ADJ

/ baɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
Having identical parts on each side of a central axis; involving both sides equally.
having identical parts on both sides

Examples

The human body is bilateral in structure, with similar organs on both sides.
Many animals possess bilateral symmetry, which aids in movement and coordination.
A leaf often displays bilateral symmetry along its central vein.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilateral - involving or affecting only one side

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'lateralis' (of the side).
