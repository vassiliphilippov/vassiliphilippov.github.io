dinginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪn.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪn.i.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being dirty, dull, or discolored; lack of brightness or cleanliness
the state of being dirty or discolored

Examples

The dinginess of the curtains made the whole room look gloomy.
Despite scrubbing, the dinginess of the old bathtub remained.
Years of smoke had added to the dinginess of the wallpaper.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean
  • Synonym: dirtiness - the state of being dirty

Etymology

From dingy + -ness; 'dingy' is of uncertain origin, perhaps from English dialect, meaning 'dirty or discolored'.
