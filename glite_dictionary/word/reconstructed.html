reconstructed

1. ADJ

/ ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.tɪd / (BrE)
having been adapted, revised, or fundamentally changed in response to social, political, or economic change.
adapted or revised due to change

Examples

After studying abroad, he became a reconstructed advocate for social justice.
Many traditional companies have hired reconstructed leaders to modernize their business models.
She described herself as a reconstructed environmentalist after learning about climate change.

Synset Examples

a reconstructed feminist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reformed - having been improved or changed for the better
  • Antonym: unchanged - not altered or modified

Etymology

From 'reconstruct' (to build again), from Latin 're-' (again) + 'construere' (to build).
