ascocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈæskoʊˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈæskəʊˌkɑːp / (BrE)
The mature, spore-producing fruiting body of a fungus belonging to the group Ascomycota.
fruiting body of an ascomycete fungus
medical

Examples

The ascocarp develops as the fungus matures, eventually releasing spores.
Researchers examined the structure of the ascocarp under a microscope.
Some edible fungi, such as morels, produce a distinctive ascocarp.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basidiocarp - fruiting body of a basidiomycete fungus

Etymology

From Greek askos (bag, sac) + karpos (fruit).
