emboldened

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈboʊldənd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbəʊldənd / (BrE)
having been made more confident or courageous to take action
made more confident or brave

Examples

The public support emboldened the politician to push for new reforms.
After hearing her friend's story, she felt emboldened to speak up.
The team's recent victory has emboldened them for the upcoming match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or characterized by courage or bravery
  • Antonym: timid - lacking in courage or confidence

Etymology

From the verb 'embolden', based on 'bold' and the prefix 'em-'.
