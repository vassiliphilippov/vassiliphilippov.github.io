Brassica rapa pekinensis

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈreɪpə ˌpɛkɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈreɪpə ˌpɛkɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of Chinese cabbage with an elongated head and broad-stalked leaves, commonly used as a vegetable in East Asian cuisine.
variety of Chinese cabbage

Examples

Brassica rapa pekinensis is often pickled to make kimchi in Korean cuisine.
Farmers harvest Brassica rapa pekinensis in the early autumn for export.
Stir-fried Brassica rapa pekinensis is a typical side dish in many Chinese households.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napa cabbage - common name for Brassica rapa pekinensis
  • Synonym: Chinese cabbage - general term for several leafy Asian cabbages including Brassica rapa pekinensis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Brassica (Latin for 'cabbage'), species rapa ('turnip'), and pekinensis (from 'Peking', old spelling of Beijing, China).
