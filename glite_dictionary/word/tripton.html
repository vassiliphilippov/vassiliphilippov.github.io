tripton

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪp.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.tɒn / (BrE)
Inorganic particulate matter suspended in bodies of water, such as lakes and seas, typically composed of mineral fragments.
inorganic particles in water

Examples

The presence of tripton in the lake can affect water clarity and light penetration.
Samples revealed a high concentration of tripton following the heavy rainfall.
Unlike plankton, tripton consists entirely of inorganic particles suspended in the water column.
