jurist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊrɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊərɪst / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in law, especially in civil law or international law; a legal scholar
legal scholar or expert in law

Examples

The conference brought together leading jurists from around the world to discuss constitutional reform.
She became a renowned jurist after publishing her influential work on civil rights law.
As a prominent jurist, he advised governments on matters of international law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal scholar - a person with specialized knowledge of law
  • Synonym: lawyer - a person who practices law as a profession

Etymology

From Latin 'jurista', from 'jus' (law).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊrɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊərɪst / (BrE)
a person, especially a judge or legal scholar, who is an expert in law and qualified to interpret or apply it, particularly as a public official in a court of justice
expert in law; a judge or legal scholar
legal

Examples

The jurist delivered a landmark ruling that changed national law.
As a respected jurist, she was often consulted on matters of constitutional rights.
The conference brought together leading jurists from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - a public official with authority to decide legal cases

Etymology

From Latin 'iurisconsultus' (legal expert), from 'ius' (law).
