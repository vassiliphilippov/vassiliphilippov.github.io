twelve

1. NUM

/ twɛlv / (AmE), / twɛlv / (BrE)
The number equal to 11 plus 1; represented as 12 in numerals.
the number 12

Examples

There are twelve months in a year.
The clock struck twelve at midnight, signaling the start of a new day.
She bought twelve eggs at the grocery store.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eleven - the number 11
  • Degree Higher: thirteen - the number 13

Etymology

From Old English *twelf*, from Proto-Germanic *twaliƀiz*.

2. NUM

/ twɛlv / (AmE), / twɛlv / (BrE)
the cardinal number equal to eleven plus one; 12.
the number 12

Examples

There are twelve months in a year.
She bought twelve roses for the anniversary.
The clock struck twelve at midnight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eleven - the cardinal number equal to ten plus one; 11
  • Degree Higher: thirteen - the cardinal number equal to twelve plus one; 13

Etymology

From Old English 'twelf', from Proto-Germanic *twaliƀ, meaning 'two left (over ten)'.

3. NUM

/ twɛlv / (AmE), / twɛlv / (BrE)
being one more than eleven; the number following eleven and preceding thirteen
number: one more than eleven

Examples

There are twelve students in the class today.
She baked twelve cookies for the party.
The clock struck twelve at midnight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eleven - the number one less than twelve
  • Degree Higher: thirteen - the number one more than twelve

Etymology

From Old English 'twelf', from Proto-Germanic '*twaliƀ' (two left/after ten).
