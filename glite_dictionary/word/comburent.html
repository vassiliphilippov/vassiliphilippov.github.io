comburent

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈbjʊrənt / (AmE), / kəmˈbjʊərənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) supporting combustion; able to cause or maintain the burning of another material.
supporting combustion

Examples

Oxygen is a classic example of a comburent gas used in industrial processes.
A good fire extinguisher must isolate the fuel from any comburent materials.
Because chlorine acts as a comburent, it increases the flammability of certain organic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxidizing - causing or supporting combustion by supplying oxygen

Etymology

From Latin 'comburens', present participle of 'comburere' (to burn up).
