saloon

1. NOUN

/ səˈlun / (AmE), / səˈluːn / (BrE)
A room or establishment where alcoholic drinks are served, especially one with a counter for serving drinks.
drinking establishment or bar

Examples

After work, they all met at the saloon for a few drinks and conversation.
The old western saloon had swinging doors and a long wooden bar.
Many small towns in the nineteenth century had at least one saloon on Main Street.

Synset Examples

he drowned his sorrows in whiskey at the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a place where alcoholic drinks are served
  • Synonym: pub - a public house for drinking, especially in the UK
  • Synonym: tavern - an establishment for the sale of beer and other drinks; sometimes also food

Etymology

From French 'salon' (large hall), Italian 'salone', ultimately from Latin 'sala' (hall).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlun / (AmE), / səˈluːn / (BrE)
A car with a fixed roof, four doors, and separate compartments for passengers and luggage; known as a sedan in American English.
type of car with four doors and fixed roof
british

Examples

He decided to buy a spacious saloon for his family trips.
The new model is available as a hatchback or a four-door saloon.
She prefers a saloon over an SUV for city driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedan - a car with a closed body and four doors (American English)

Etymology

From French *salon* (large hall), developed in British English to refer to cars with a closed body.

3. NOUN

/ səˈlun / (AmE), / səˈluːn / (BrE)
A public house or bar where alcoholic drinks are served, often with light meals and separate rooms for patrons.
public house or bar serving drinks and light meals

Examples

The old western saloon was crowded with cowboys and travelers seeking a drink.
She ordered a sandwich and a beer at the local saloon after work.
Some saloons in the 19th century also offered gambling and live music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a counter or establishment where alcoholic drinks are served
  • Synonym: pub - a British public house licensed to sell alcoholic drinks
  • Synonym: tavern - an establishment for the sale of beer and other drinks; sometimes also food

Etymology

From French 'salon' (large room), via Italian 'salone', augmentative of 'sala' (hall).
