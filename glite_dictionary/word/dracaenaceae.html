Dracaenaceae

1. NOUN

/ drəˌsiːnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / drasɪˌiːnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a former family or subfamily of tropical plants that includes dragon trees and related species, now usually placed within the Asparagaceae or Agavaceae families in modern classification systems
plant family (now mostly included in Asparagaceae or Agavaceae)

Examples

Many species formerly classified in the Dracaenaceae are now placed under the family Asparagaceae.
Botanists debated whether Dracaenaceae should be treated as a separate family or a subfamily within Agavaceae.
The dragon tree, a well-known ornamental plant, once belonged to the Dracaenaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus name Dracaena, from Greek drakaina (female dragon), with the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ drəˌsiːnəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / drəˌsiːnəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a subfamily of flowering plants that includes genera such as Dracaena, classified by some systems within or apart from the Agavaceae family
a subfamily of flowering plants

Examples

The Dracaenaceae are widely distributed throughout tropical and subtropical regions.
Some botanists classify the genus Dracaena in the Dracaenaceae rather than Agavaceae.
Research on the Dracaenaceae provides insight into the evolution of monocots.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from the genus name Dracaena + -aceae (plant family suffix).
