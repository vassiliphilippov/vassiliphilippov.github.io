ne'er-do-well

1. NOUN

/ nɛr.duˌwɛl / (AmE), / ˈnɪə.duˌwɛl / (BrE)
a person who is lazy, irresponsible, or not likely to succeed; an idle or worthless individual
idle or worthless person

Examples

His uncle considered him a ne'er-do-well who would never amount to anything.
The old mansion had once belonged to a notorious ne'er-do-well from town.
Despite his family's efforts, Jack developed a reputation as the village ne'er-do-well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loafer - a person who avoids work or effort
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person

Etymology

Contraction of 'never-do-well,' first recorded in the 18th century.
