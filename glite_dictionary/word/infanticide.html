infanticide

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfæntɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfæntɪsaɪd / (BrE)
A person who kills an infant.
a person who kills an infant

Examples

The ancient city's laws prescribed severe penalties for anyone found to be an infanticide.
The judge described the infanticide as a person who committed a particularly heinous crime.
Historians have documented several cases where an infanticide was brought to trial in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who commits murder

Etymology

From Latin 'infanticida': 'infans' (infant) + 'cida' (killer; from 'caedere', to kill).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfæntəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfæntɪsaɪd / (BrE)
The crime of killing an infant, especially by a parent.
the killing of an infant

Examples

The court case involved a disturbing accusation of infanticide against the young mother.
Throughout history, certain cultures practiced infanticide during times of famine or crisis.
Modern laws severely punish infanticide, recognizing it as one of the most serious crimes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: homicide - the killing of one person by another
  • Synonym: parricide - the killing of one's parent
  • Synonym: filicide - the killing of one's child

Etymology

From Latin 'infanticidium', from 'infans' (infant) + 'caedere' (to kill).
