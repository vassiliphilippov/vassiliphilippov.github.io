gargle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːɡl / (BrE)
a medicated liquid used to rinse the mouth and throat by gargling
medicated mouth or throat rinse

Examples

The doctor prescribed a special gargle to soothe my sore throat.
This herbal gargle can help reduce mouth infections.
After the dental procedure, she was told to use a medicated gargle twice a day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouthwash - a liquid used to rinse the mouth, often for hygiene or medicinal purposes

Etymology

From the French 'gargouiller' (to gargle), probably imitative of the sound.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɑr.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.ɡl̩ / (BrE)
to rinse the mouth and throat with a liquid, typically by holding it in the throat and blowing air through it with a bubbling sound
rinse mouth/throat with liquid

Examples

She was told to gargle with salt water to soothe her sore throat.
The dentist recommended that I gargle daily for better oral hygiene.
He filled his mouth with mouthwash and began to gargle loudly.

Synset Examples

gargle with this liquid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rinse - to wash lightly, especially by pouring water over or through something

Etymology

From Old French *gargouiller* (to gargle), from a reduplication imitating the sound.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːɡl / (BrE)
a bubbling or gurgling sound made while bathing the throat with a liquid
sound produced while gargling

Examples

The gargle echoed in the bathroom as she rinsed her mouth after brushing her teeth.
He was startled by the strange gargle coming from the sink late at night.
The child's attempts at singing turned into a playful gargle of laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurgle - a bubbling or rippling sound like water flowing

Etymology

From Old French *gargouiller* (to gargle), influenced by imitative sounds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07398623-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɡɑrɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːɡl / (BrE)
to utter words or sounds with a gargling or burbling quality
utter in a gargling or burbling way

Examples

She tried to gargle a reply, but her mouth was full of water.
The actor gargled his lines for comedic effect during the rehearsal.
He gargled something unintelligible and stormed out of the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurgle - make a bubbling sound in the throat

Etymology

From Old French *gargouiller*, meaning 'to gurgle' or 'to bubble'; related to 'gargoyle'.
