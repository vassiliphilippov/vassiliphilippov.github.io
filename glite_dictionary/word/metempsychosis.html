metempsychosis

1. NOUN

/ məˌtɛmp.saɪˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiː.tɛm.psaɪˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that after death the soul passes into another human or animal body and begins a new cycle of existence.
soul's transmigration after death

Examples

Many ancient philosophers debated the validity of metempsychosis as an explanation for the soul's journey after death.
The doctrine of metempsychosis is central to certain Eastern religions, including forms of Hinduism and Buddhism.
She wrote a book exploring the cultural significance of metempsychosis throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reincarnation - the rebirth of a soul in a new body

Etymology

From Greek metempsychōsis, from meta- (change) + empsychoun (to animate).
