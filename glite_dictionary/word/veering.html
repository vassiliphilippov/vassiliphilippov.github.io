veering

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of changing direction or turning aside suddenly, especially in movement or course
the act of changing direction suddenly

Examples

The car's sudden veering to the left startled the passengers.
There was a noticeable veering of the wind just before the storm struck.
His veering from the original plan caused confusion among the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to turn aside abruptly from a straight course
  • Antonym: straightening - the act of becoming or making straight again after a turn

Etymology

From the verb 'veer', which comes from Old French 'virer' (to turn).
