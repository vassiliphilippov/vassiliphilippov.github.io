Parophrys

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːfrɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrɒfrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Soleidae, which includes the English sole.
genus of flatfish (including English sole)

Examples

The English sole, classified under the genus Parophrys, is found along the Pacific coast.
Scientists recently published a study on the habitat preferences of fish in the Parophrys genus.
Fisheries data show that Parophrys species are important for commercial harvesting in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solea - another genus of flatfish in the family Soleidae

Etymology

From Greek para- (beside) and ophrys (eyebrow or ridge), a reference to the physical features of the fish.
