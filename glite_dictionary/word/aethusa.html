Aethusa

1. NOUN

/ iˈθjuːsə / (AmE), / iˈθjuːsə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous plants in the parsley family, commonly known as fool's parsley.
a genus of poisonous plants (fool's parsley)

Examples

Aethusa is often mistaken for edible parsley, but it is highly toxic.
Botanists identified the specimen as Aethusa during their survey of the meadow.
Children should be taught to avoid picking Aethusa because of its poisonous qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's parsley - a common name for Aethusa cynapium, a poisonous plant

Etymology

From Greek Aethousa, an ancient name for several plants.

2. NOUN

/ iˈθjusə / (AmE), / iˈθjuːsə / (BrE)
A small genus of plants in the parsley family, including fool's parsley.
genus of plants including fool's parsley

Examples

Aethusa is a genus of plants best known for its species fool's parsley.
The weed known as fool's parsley belongs to the genus Aethusa.
Botanists classify several poisonous herbs under Aethusa in the parsley family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's parsley - a poisonous herbaceous plant of the genus Aethusa

Etymology

From Greek aithousa, a type of plant.
