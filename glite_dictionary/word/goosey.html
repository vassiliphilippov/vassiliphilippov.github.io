goosey

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡuːsi / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsi / (BrE)
Silly or foolish; showing a lack of good judgment or intelligence.
silly; foolish

Examples

She made a rather goosey remark during the meeting, which made everyone laugh.
His goosey grin showed he hadn’t understood the instructions at all.
Sometimes, I feel a bit goosey when trying something completely new.

Synset Examples

ridiculous anserine behavior
a dopey answer
a dopey kid
some fool idea about rewriting authors' books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - showing lack of good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From 'goose' + '-y', based on the reputation of geese as being foolish or silly in behavior.
