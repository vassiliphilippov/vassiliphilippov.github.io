taguan

1. NOUN

/ təˈɡwɑːn / (AmE), / təˈɡwɑːn / (BrE)
A large flying squirrel native to Southeast Asia, especially India and the Malay Peninsula, known for gliding between trees.
East Indian flying squirrel

Examples

The taguan uses a membrane between its limbs to glide from tree to tree in the rainforest.
Researchers studied the nocturnal habits of the taguan in its natural habitat.
The dense forests of Southeast Asia are home to the elusive taguan.

Etymology

From Tagalog 'tagwan', referring to the species.
