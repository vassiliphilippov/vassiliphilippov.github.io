flowchart

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊˌtʃɑrt / (AmE), / ˈfləʊˌtʃɑːt / (BrE)
a diagram that shows the sequence and connections of steps in a process, such as in a computer program or business procedure
diagram showing steps in a process

Examples

The engineer created a flowchart to illustrate how the software processes user input.
Before implementing the system, the team reviewed the project flowchart.
Learning to read a flowchart is essential for new programmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a drawing that explains how something works or shows the relationship between parts

Etymology

Formed from 'flow' and 'chart', first used in the early 20th century in engineering and computing.
