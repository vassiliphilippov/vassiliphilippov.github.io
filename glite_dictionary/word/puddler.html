puddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌd.lə / (BrE)
a worker who converts pig iron into wrought iron by stirring it in a furnace to remove impurities (puddling)
worker converting pig iron to wrought iron

Examples

The skilled puddler spent his days at the furnace, turning molten pig iron into high-quality wrought iron.
Young apprentices watched the experienced puddler as he expertly managed the heat and stirred the bubbling metal.
In the 19th century, being a puddler was a respected but physically demanding job in many steel mills.

Etymology

From 'puddle' (in metallurgy: to stir molten metal) + '-er'.
