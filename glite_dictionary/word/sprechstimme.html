sprechstimme

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃprɛkˌstɪmə / (AmE), / ˈʃprɛxˌstɪmə / (BrE)
A style of dramatic vocalization in which the performer uses a technique between singing and speaking.
musical vocal style between singing and speaking

Examples

The soprano performed the piece in sprechstimme as instructed by the composer.
Many twentieth-century composers experimented with sprechstimme to create new effects.
Her use of sprechstimme added a haunting quality to the performance.

Etymology

From German Sprechstimme, from sprechen (to speak) + Stimme (voice).
