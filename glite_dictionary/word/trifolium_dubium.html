Trifolium dubium

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəm ˈduːbiəm / (AmE), / trɪˈfəʊliəm ˈdjuːbiəm / (BrE)
A species of clover native to Ireland with small yellowish flowers; widely regarded as the true or original shamrock.
species of clover; true shamrock

Examples

Trifolium dubium is often identified as the plant used for the traditional Irish shamrock symbol.
Botanists confirmed that the yellow-flowered Trifolium dubium is widespread throughout Ireland.
Gardeners sometimes include Trifolium dubium in their wildflower mixes to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamrock - a symbol of Ireland; a plant with three-lobed leaves
  • Synonym: clover - any of various plants of the genus Trifolium

Etymology

From Latin: 'trifolium' meaning 'three-leaved', and 'dubium' meaning 'doubtful' or 'dubious'.
