resignedly

1. ADV

/ rɪˈzaɪndɪdli / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪnɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows acceptance of something unpleasant or unavoidable without protest.
in a resigned manner

Examples

She resignedly agreed to take on extra work, knowing she had little choice.
He handed over his keys resignedly, accepting the end of his shift.
The child resignedly finished his vegetables, knowing dessert would come after.

Synset Examples

resignedly, I telegraphed back that it was all right with me if he insisted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctantly - in a hesitant or unwilling way
  • Antonym: defiantly - in a manner showing resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From 'resigned' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'resignare' (to give up).

2. ADV

/ rɪˈzaɪndɪdli / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪnɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows acceptance of something unpleasant or inevitable without protest.
in a resigned or accepting way

Examples

She sighed resignedly and began to gather her belongings.
He nodded resignedly, realizing there was nothing he could do to change the situation.
The dog settled resignedly into its kennel after a long day.

Synset Examples

she shrugged her shoulders abjectly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptingly - in a tolerant or accepting manner
  • Antonym: defiantly - in a manner showing resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From 'resigned' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
