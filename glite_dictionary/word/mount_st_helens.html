Mount St. Helens

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt sənt ˈhɛlənz / (AmE), / maʊnt sənt ˈhɛlənz / (BrE)
an active volcano in the Cascade Range in southwestern Washington State, USA, known for its major 1980 eruption
active volcano in Washington State, USA

Examples

Many scientists study Mount St. Helens to better understand volcanic eruptions and their effects on the environment.
After the dramatic eruption in 1980, Mount St. Helens became a major tourist attraction in the region.
The ash cloud from Mount St. Helens traveled across several states, impacting air travel and agriculture.

Etymology

Named after Alleyne Fitzherbert, 1st Baron St Helens, a British diplomat.
