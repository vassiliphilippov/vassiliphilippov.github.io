exacting

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːktɪŋ / (BrE)
demanding great accuracy, care, or effort; making careful and precise demands of someone or something.
demanding accuracy or effort

Examples

The role of editor is an exacting one that requires constant attention to detail.
She is an exacting supervisor who demands perfection from her team.
Preparing for the competition involved months of exacting training.

Synset Examples

an exacting job
became more exigent over his pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demanding - requiring much effort or attention
  • Antonym: easygoing - not demanding, relaxed or tolerant

Etymology

From exact + -ing, meaning 'demanding precision or accuracy'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːktɪŋ / (BrE)
demanding in terms of accuracy, attention, or requirements; requiring special care or effort, especially with reference to conditions or needs.
demanding or requiring special care

Examples

The researcher found that the bacteria were exacting in their need for specific nutrients.
Growing orchids can be exacting, as they require precise humidity and temperature.
Only exacting standards will ensure the accuracy of these scientific experiments.

Synset Examples

fastidious microorganisms
certain highly specialized xerophytes are extremely exacting in their requirements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demanding - requiring much effort or attention
  • Synonym: strict - requiring strict attention or rules
  • Antonym: easygoing - not demanding; relaxed or tolerant

Etymology

From English 'exact' + '-ing', from Latin 'exigere' (to demand, require).

3. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːktɪŋ / (BrE)
making great demands on one's skill, attention, or resources; requiring strict attention to detail or precision.
demanding strict attention or effort

Examples

The job of an air traffic controller is exacting and requires maximum concentration at all times.
She is an exacting editor who insists on accuracy in every detail.
The construction of the bridge was an exacting task that demanded both skill and patience.

Synset Examples

an exacting instructor
a stern disciplinarian
strict standards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demanding - requiring much effort or attention
  • Antonym: lenient - not strict; permissive or tolerant

Etymology

From verb 'exact' (to demand and obtain), from Latin 'exigere' (to drive out, demand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00714774-s
Synset POS: s
