defoliant

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfoʊliənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfəʊliənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance that, when applied to plants, causes their leaves to fall off
chemical that causes leaves to drop

Examples

The government banned the use of the defoliant due to its harmful environmental effects.
Farmers sometimes apply a defoliant before harvesting cotton to make the process easier.
The use of defoliant in warfare led to long-term damage to local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbicide - chemical that destroys unwanted plants

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (removal) + 'folium' (leaf) + '-ant' (agent noun suffix).
