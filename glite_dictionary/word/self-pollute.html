to self-pollute

1. VERB

/ sɛlf pəˈluːt / (AmE), / sɛlf pəˈluːt / (BrE)
to cause harm to oneself or one's own environment, either mentally, physically, or by contaminating one's surroundings
to contaminate oneself or one's own environment

Examples

If you constantly dwell on negative thoughts, you may self-pollute your mind.
She was determined not to self-pollute her body with harmful substances any longer.
Some people unknowingly self-pollute their immediate environment through careless habits.

Synset Examples

He realized his habits were causing him to self-pollute his mind.
Avoiding junk food can help us not self-pollute our bodies.
We must stop self-polluting our environment with plastic waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pollute - to make dirty or impure

Etymology

Formed by combining 'self-' (indicating oneself) and 'pollute' (from Latin 'polluere', to defile or contaminate).
