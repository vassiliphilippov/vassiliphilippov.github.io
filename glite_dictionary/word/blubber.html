blubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌbə / (BrE)
a thick layer of fat under the skin of whales and other large marine mammals, used to keep them warm in cold water and as a source of oil
thick fat layer on marine mammals

Examples

The whale's blubber helps it survive in freezing temperatures.
Researchers analyzed the blubber of seals to monitor pollution levels in the ocean.
In the past, people hunted whales for their blubber to produce oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fat - soft greasy tissue found in animals and humans
  • Synonym: oil - substance obtained from blubber by processing, used as fuel or lubricant

Etymology

From Middle English bluberen (to bubble, foam). The noun sense developed from the verb's application to swelling or bulging forms.

2. VERB

/ ˈblʌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌbə / (BrE)
to cry noisily and uncontrollably, often with snuffling or sobbing sounds.
cry noisily and uncontrollably

Examples

He began to blubber when he realized his toy was broken.
There's no use blubbering over a little mistake—just fix it and move on.
She blubbered so loudly that everyone in the room turned to look.

Synset Examples

Stop snivelling — you got yourself into this mess!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sob - to cry noisily, making convulsive gasps
  • Degree Lower: whimper - to make a series of low, feeble sounds expressing fear or pain

Etymology

From Middle English bluberen (to bubble, foam up), imitative of the sound of sobbing.

3. VERB

/ ˈblʌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌbə / (BrE)
to say something while crying in a noisy and uncontrolled way
to speak while crying noisily

Examples

She tried to blubber an apology, but her words were lost in her sobs.
He blubbered out his feelings after hearing the sad news.
The little boy blubbered that he wanted his mother as tears streamed down his face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sob - to cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps

Etymology

From Middle English bluberen (to bubble, to weep noisily), akin to the sound made while crying.

cry noisily and uncontrollably
