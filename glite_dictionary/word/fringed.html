fringed

1. ADJ

/ frɪndʒd / (AmE), / frɪndʒd / (BrE)
having a decorative edging made of hanging threads, cords, or strips
decorated with hanging threads or strips

Examples

She wore a fringed leather jacket to the concert.
The fringed edge of the curtain gave it a vintage look.
Her scarf was beautifully fringed with golden threads.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfringed - not having a decorative edging or fringe
  • Synonym: fringe - a decorative border of hanging threads or strips
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00059081-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ frɪndʒd / (AmE), / frɪn(d)ʒd / (BrE)
having a border or edge decorated with or resembling a fringe; often used to describe something surrounded or bordered by another feature.
having a border or edge like a fringe

Examples

A beautiful, fringed shawl hung over the chair in the hallway.
The path was fringed with wildflowers, making the walk especially pleasant.
Palm trees fringed the white sandy beach, creating a tropical paradise.

Synset Examples

a large suburban community … fringed by an industrial area
a grass-fringed stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bordered - having a border or boundary; edged
  • Antonym: unfringed - not having a fringe or border

Etymology

From the verb 'fringe,' ultimately from Old French 'frenge' and Latin 'fimbria' (border).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00259453-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ frɪndʒd / (AmE), / frɪndʒd / (BrE)
having an edge decorated or marked by fine projections or irregular cuts; often referring to a border that appears trimmed or adorned with narrow strips.
having an irregular, slashed edge

Examples

The leaves of this plant are fringed with delicate threads along their margins.
She wore a shawl with fringed edges that fluttered in the wind.
Some sea anemones have fringed tentacles that wave gracefully underwater.

Synset Examples

a laciniate leaf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laciniate - having edges irregularly slashed or jagged (esp. in botany)

Etymology

From fringe + -ed, denoting 'having a fringe'.
