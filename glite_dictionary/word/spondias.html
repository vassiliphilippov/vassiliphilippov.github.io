Spondias

1. NOUN

/ spɒnˈdaɪ.əs / (AmE), / spɒnˈdeɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees bearing one-seeded, edible fruits, including species such as the hog plum and ambarella.
genus of tropical fruit trees

Examples

The Spondias tree is cultivated in many tropical regions for its tart fruit.
Some farmers grow Spondias for commercial export and as a food crop.
The fruits of Spondias are enjoyed fresh or used in beverages and preserves.

Etymology

From Latin 'Spondias', the name used by Pliny for a similar fruit, ultimately of Greek origin.
