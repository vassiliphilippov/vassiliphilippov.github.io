underpayment

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
an amount paid that is less than what is required or expected
payment less than required or expected

Examples

The company discovered an underpayment in several employees' salaries.
Any underpayment of taxes can lead to penalties and interest charges.
She was upset to notice the underpayment after checking her pay slip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overpayment - a payment greater than what is required or expected

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning 'below or less than usual') and 'payment'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the act of paying less money than is required, expected, or owed
paying less than required

Examples

The company was fined for the underpayment of wages to its employees.
An underpayment on your tax return may result in a penalty.
She discovered an underpayment in her last month's paycheck and reported it to HR.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overpayment - the act of paying more than required

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'insufficiently') and 'payment'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01124809-n
Synset POS: n
