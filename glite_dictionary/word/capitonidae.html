Capitonidae

1. NOUN

/ kæpɪˈtoʊnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kæpɪˈtəʊnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical birds known as barbets, characterized by stout bills and brightly colored plumage
family of tropical birds (barbets)

Examples

The Capitonidae are easily recognized by their thick, bristled bills and bold colors.
Scientists studying South American forests often encounter several species from the Capitonidae family.
Members of the Capitonidae play a key role in seed dispersal in tropical ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbet - a type of tropical bird with stout bill; member of Capitonidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Capitonidae', based on genus name 'Capito'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪˈtɒnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪˈtəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical birds known as barbets, characterized by stout bills and brightly colored plumage, found mainly in Africa, Asia, and Central and South America.
family of birds known as barbets

Examples

The colorful Capitonidae are found throughout tropical forests, where they feed mainly on fruit and insects.
Scientists discovered a new species of Capitonidae in the rainforests of South America.
Some members of the Capitonidae family have distinctive bristles around their beaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbet - Tropical bird with a stout bill from the family Capitonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Capitonidae, from Capito (a genus) and the suffix -idae indicating family in zoological taxonomy.
