warp

1. NOUN

/ wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
A moral or mental distortion that affects someone's character or thinking.
moral or mental distortion

Examples

The traumatic events in his childhood left a lasting warp in his personality.
She explained his cruelty as a warp in his moral judgment.
The novel explores the psychological warp that can develop from years of loneliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the action of distorting, or the state of being distorted

2. NOUN

/ wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
the set of threads running lengthwise in a woven fabric, held in tension on a loom and crossed by the weft (woof) during weaving
lengthwise threads on a loom

Examples

The artisan carefully arranged the warp on the loom before starting to weave.
If the warp threads are not tight enough, the fabric may lose its shape.
The silk warp contrasted beautifully with the colored wool weft.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weft - the threads that are woven across the warp in a fabric

Etymology

From Old English 'wearp' (that which is thrown away, warp in weaving), related to German 'Wurf'.

3. VERB

/ wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
to bend or twist out of shape, especially due to heat, pressure, damp, or stress
to bend or twist out of shape

Examples

The wooden floor began to warp after the pipe burst and flooded the basement.
Don't leave your vinyl records in the sun or they might warp.
Repeated exposure to heat can warp plastic containers.

Synset Examples

The highway buckled during the heat wave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distort - to twist out of the true meaning or shape
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make straight or restore to original shape

Etymology

Old English *weorpan* (to throw), later meaning 'to turn' or 'to twist'.
