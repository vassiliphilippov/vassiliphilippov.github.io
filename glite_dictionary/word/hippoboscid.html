hippoboscid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈbɑsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈbɒsɪd / (BrE)
a bloodsucking fly of the family Hippoboscidae, parasitic on birds and mammals
parasitic bloodsucking fly

Examples

The hippoboscid is known for its flattened body, which helps it remain attached to its host.
Researchers discovered several hippoboscids infesting the nests of wild birds.
Some species of hippoboscid flies are commonly found on sheep and horses.

Etymology

From modern Latin Hippoboscidae, from Greek hippos (horse) + boskō (to feed).
