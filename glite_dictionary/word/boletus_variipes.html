Boletus variipes

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs ˌvɛriˈaɪpiːz / (AmE), / bəˈliːtəs ˌvɑːrɪˈaɪpiːz / (BrE)
A species of edible fungus found on soil under hardwood trees, characterized by a dry convex cap, whitish under surface, and a netted (reticulate) stalk.
species of edible woodland fungus

Examples

Mycologists identified the mushroom as Boletus variipes growing near the oak trees.
While Boletus variipes is edible, it is not considered a top choice among mushroom hunters.
The dry cap and whitish pores helped him recognize Boletus variipes during his woodland walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boletus - a genus of mushrooms with pores instead of gills

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Boletus' (a genus of mushrooms) and Latin 'variipes' (variegated foot/stalk).
