tangent

1. NOUN

/ ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒənt / (BrE)
a straight line or plane that touches a curve or curved surface at a single point, but does not cross it at that point
line touching a curve at one point

Examples

The tangent to the circle at point A is drawn in red.
In calculus, finding the tangent to a curve at a given point is a fundamental concept.
The architect explained how the tangent line affected the building's design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secant - a straight line that cuts a curve at two or more points

Etymology

From Latin 'tangens', present participle of 'tangere' (to touch).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒənt / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The trigonometric function that gives the ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the adjacent side in a right-angled triangle.
trigonometric ratio; opposite/adjacent in a right triangle

Examples

The tangent of a 45-degree angle is exactly one.
To solve the problem, you need to calculate the tangent of the given angle.
She explained how the tangent function is used in right triangle trigonometry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cotangent - the reciprocal of the tangent function in trigonometry
  • Degree Lower: sine - trigonometric function; ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse in a right triangle
  • Synonym: secant - trigonometric function; reciprocal of cosine, related function

Etymology

From Latin tangens, present participle of tangere (to touch), originally related to the tangent line in geometry.
