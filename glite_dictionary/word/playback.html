playback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌbæk / (BrE)
the part of an audio or video recorder or system responsible for reproducing previously recorded sounds or images
device component that reproduces recordings

Examples

This high-end tape recorder has an advanced playback section for better sound quality.
During playback, you can hear every detail of the original performance.
The technician repaired the playback head so the tape could be heard clearly again.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recording - the process or result of storing sounds or images for later reproduction

Etymology

From 'play' + 'back', originally referring to playing a recording in reverse direction for reproduction.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌbæk / (BrE)
the act of reproducing previously recorded sound or images
reproduction of recorded sound or images

Examples

The playback of the concert allowed fans to relive the best moments.
She checked the playback to ensure the interview had been recorded properly.
After the performance, the band listened to a playback of their latest song.

Synset Examples

he was allowed to hear the playback of his testimony

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recording - the process of storing sound or images on tape, film, etc.

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'play back' (to play again), first recorded in the mid-20th century with the spread of audio technology.
