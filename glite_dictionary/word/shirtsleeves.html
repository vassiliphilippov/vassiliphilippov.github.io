shirtsleeves

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɜrtsˌsliːvz / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːtsˌsliːvz / (BrE)
the state of wearing only a shirt without a jacket or coat
wearing only a shirt (no jacket)

Examples

He greeted his guests in his shirtsleeves, making everyone feel at ease.
Because of the heat, most of the audience sat in their shirtsleeves.
In the office, it was common to see people working in their shirtsleeves during summer.

Synset Examples

‘in your shirtsleeves’ means you are not wearing anything over your shirt
in hot weather they dined in their shirtsleeves
