dehiscence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈhɪsəns / (AmE), / dɪˈhɪsəns / (BrE)
(biology, medicine) the splitting or bursting open of an organ, tissue, or wound to release contents, especially at maturity or after surgery.
bursting open of organ or wound
medical

Examples

The fruit undergoes dehiscence to release its seeds when mature.
Improper wound care can result in surgical dehiscence, leading to complications.
Some plants rely on dehiscence for effective seed dispersal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rupture - a break or tear in tissue or organ, often sudden or accidental
  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or line of breakage

Etymology

From Latin dehiscere (to split open).
