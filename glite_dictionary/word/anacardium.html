Anacardium

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈkɑːrdiəm / (AmE), / ˌanəˈkɑːdɪəm / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Anacardiaceae, including the cashew tree and related species.
genus of trees including the cashew

Examples

The cashew belongs to the genus Anacardium within the family Anacardiaceae.
Botanists studied several species from the Anacardium genus to better understand cashew cultivation.
The fruit of Anacardium occidentale is widely consumed around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anacardiaceae - the botanical family of cashew and sumac trees

Etymology

From Greek ana- (upwards) + kardion (heart), referring to the shape of the cashew.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈkɑːrdiəm / (AmE), / ˌanəˈkɑːdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae, best known for the cashew tree.
genus of plants including the cashew

Examples

The Anacardium genus is valued for producing the edible cashew nut.
Researchers studied various species within the Anacardium genus for medicinal properties.
A common member of Anacardium is the cashew tree, widely grown in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashew - the tropical tree or its nut, belonging to genus Anacardium

Etymology

From Greek 'ana-' (up) and 'kardia' (heart), referring to the heart-shaped fruit stalk.
