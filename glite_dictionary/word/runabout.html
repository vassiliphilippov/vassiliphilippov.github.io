runabout

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈrʌnəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
an open automobile with a front seat and a rumble seat, typically used in the early 20th century
open car with front and rumble seats

Examples

The collector proudly displayed his restored runabout at the vintage car show.
In the early 1900s, a runabout was an affordable choice for young drivers.
They took a leisurely drive in their classic runabout along the countryside roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadster - open two-seater car, especially one without a fixed roof
