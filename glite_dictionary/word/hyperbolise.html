to hyperbolise

1. VERB

/ haɪˈpɝːbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜːbəlaɪz / (BrE)
To exaggerate or represent something as greater or more dramatic than it really is.
exaggerate; overstate something
british

Examples

Politicians often hyperbolise the impact of their policies to win public approval.
He tends to hyperbolise his travel stories to make them more entertaining.
The news report hyperbolised the severity of the storm, causing unnecessary panic.

Synset Examples

tended to romanticize and exaggerate this ‘gracious Old South’ imagery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - To represent as greater than is actually the case
  • Antonym: to understate - To represent as less important or smaller than it is

Etymology

From Greek 'hyperbolē' (excess, exaggeration) via French and late Latin.
