two-bit

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌbɪt / (BrE)
worthless or insignificant; of little value or importance
of little value; insignificant
informal american

Examples

The detective scoffed at the two-bit thief who attempted the robbery.
He refused to take orders from a two-bit manager with no real authority.
They dismissed her criticism as just another two-bit complaint.

Synset Examples

That two-bit actor couldn't land a decent role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or use
  • Synonym: insignificant - not important or noteworthy
  • Antonym: valuable - having considerable worth or importance

Etymology

Originally from the value of two bits (a quarter dollar) in early American currency, indicating something cheap.
