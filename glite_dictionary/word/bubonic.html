bubonic

1. ADJ

/ buːˈbɑːnɪk / (AmE), / bjuːˈbɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by buboes, especially as seen in cases of plague
relating to or showing buboes
medical

Examples

Doctors identified the disease as bubonic after observing the swollen lymph nodes.
During the Middle Ages, the bubonic plague caused millions of deaths across Europe.
Symptoms of bubonic infections can include fever, chills, and swollen glands.

Synset Examples

bubonic plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - a serious infectious disease that spreads quickly and often causes death
  • Degree Lower: pneumonic - relating to or affecting the lungs, especially a form of plague

Etymology

From Greek 'boubōn' (groin, swelling of the groin).
