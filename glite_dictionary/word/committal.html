committal

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / kəˈmɪt.əl / (BrE)
the official act of sending someone to a place of confinement, such as a prison or mental hospital, by legal process.
act of sending someone to prison or institution
legal

Examples

The judge ordered the committal of the defendant to the state mental hospital.
His committal to prison followed a lengthy trial.
The family was saddened by the sudden committal of their relative to an institution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commitment - the act of sending someone officially to prison or psychiatric hospital

Etymology

From commit + -al, meaning the act of committing.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪɾl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtəl / (BrE)
the act of committing a crime or offence.
the act of committing a crime

Examples

The lawyer argued there was no proof of committal of the alleged theft.
After the committal of the crime, the suspect fled the country.
He was arrested for his committal of multiple offenses last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commission - the act of committing a crime
  • Antonym: omission - the failure to do something, especially a crime

Etymology

From commit + -al, influenced by Late Latin committalis.
