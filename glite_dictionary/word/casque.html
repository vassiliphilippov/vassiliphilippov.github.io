casque

1. NOUN

/ kæsk / (AmE), / kɑːsk / (BrE)
A piece of armor for the head, usually ornate and without a visor, worn in the 15th and 16th centuries.
ornate helmet without a visor (15th–16th c.)

Examples

The knight wore a beautifully engraved casque that gleamed in the sunlight.
Early Renaissance soldiers often favored the casque for its ornate design.
In the museum's armor exhibit, I admired a 16th-century casque encrusted with gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmet - a protective covering for the head

Etymology

From French 'casque', from Italian 'casco' meaning 'helmet'.
