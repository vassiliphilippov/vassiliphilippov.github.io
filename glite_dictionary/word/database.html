database

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtəˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtəˌbeɪs / (BrE)
A structured set of data held in a computer or other medium, especially one that is accessible in various ways.
structured collection of data

Examples

All customer records are stored in a secure database on the server.
The company is building a database of medical research information.
To find the article you want, search the online database.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repository - a place where data or information is stored
  • Synonym: archive - a collection of historical records or data

Etymology

Formed from 'data' + 'base', first used in the context of computer science in the 1960s.
