clover

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a small plant of the genus Trifolium, typically with three-lobed leaves and often grown as fodder or for its flowers.
a small three-lobed-leaf plant

Examples

A patch of clover grew in the meadow behind our house.
She picked a four-leaf clover and made a wish.
Cows love to graze on fresh clover in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trefoil - a plant with three-lobed leaves, especially clover

Etymology

Old English 'clāfre', related to Dutch 'klaver' and German 'Klee'.
