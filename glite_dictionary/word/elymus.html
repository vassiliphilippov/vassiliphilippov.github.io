Elymus

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlɪməs / (AmE), / ˈiːlɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of tall tufted perennial grasses, including lyme grass and wild rye, found in temperate regions.
genus of perennial grasses

Examples

Several species in the Elymus genus are important for stabilizing sand dunes along coastlines.
The botanist collected samples of Elymus from the prairie for her research.
Elymus is commonly known as wild rye in many parts of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyme grass - a species of grass in the genus Elymus
  • Synonym: wild rye - a common name for grasses in the genus Elymus

Etymology

From Latin 'elymus', from Ancient Greek 'elymos', a type of grass.
