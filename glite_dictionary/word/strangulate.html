to strangulate

1. VERB

/ ˈstræŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of a hollow organ or vessel) to constrict so tightly as to stop the flow of blood or air.
to constrict a vessel or organ tightly
medical

Examples

A hernia may strangulate the intestine, cutting off blood supply.
The surgeon was careful not to strangulate any blood vessels during the procedure.
Left untreated, a twisted bowel can strangulate and cause tissue death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to constrict - to make narrower by pressure
  • Synonym: compress - to squeeze together or press

Etymology

From Latin strangulatus, past participle of strangulare (to strangle).

2. VERB

/ ˈstræŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to kill by squeezing or compressing someone's throat so as to cut off their air supply
kill by blocking the airway by squeezing

Examples

The murderer attempted to strangulate his victim during the robbery.
Doctors confirmed the cause of death was that the woman had been strangulated.
The suspect confessed to strangulating his opponent after a heated argument.

Synset Examples

he tried to strangulate his opponent
A man in Boston has been strangling several dozen prostitutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strangle - kill by squeezing the throat and cutting off air
  • Degree Lower: to suffocate - die or kill through lack of air

Etymology

From Latin strangulatus, past participle of strangulare (to strangle), from Greek strangaloun (to choke).

3. VERB

/ ˈstræŋgjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstræŋgjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or a passage) to become constricted so that blood, air, or another flow is stopped or hindered.
become constricted (medical, especially passage or organ)
medical

Examples

If the blood supply to the tissue is cut off, the organ may strangulate and become necrotic.
In rare cases, an umbilical hernia can strangulate and cause severe complications.
Doctors acted quickly because the intestine began to strangulate due to the blockage.

Synset Examples

The hernia will strangulate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to constrict - to make narrower, especially by encircling pressure

Etymology

From Latin strangulatus, past participle of strangulare “to choke, strangle.”
