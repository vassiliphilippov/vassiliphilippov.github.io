accoutered

1. ADJ

/ əˈkuːtɚd / (AmE), / əˈkuːtəd / (BrE)
provided with the necessary clothing, equipment, or accessories, especially for a specialized or military purpose.
equipped or outfitted (often for a purpose)

Examples

The soldiers were accoutered in full combat gear before sunrise.
He arrived at the expedition accoutered with high-tech climbing equipment.
The knights rode onto the field, splendidly accoutered for the tournament.

Synset Examples

troops accoutered for battle
properly accoutered for the trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equipped - provided with the necessary tools or items
  • Antonym: unprepared - not ready or lacking necessary equipment

Etymology

From Middle French *accoutrer* (to equip), altered from Old French *acoutrer* (to arrange, dress).
