Bryonia

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈoʊniə / (AmE), / brɪˈəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of climbing perennial herbs in the gourd family, commonly known as bryony, found in Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.
genus of climbing perennial herbs (bryony)

Examples

The genus Bryonia includes several species of climbing herbs often found in hedgerows.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Bryonia in traditional remedies.
Wild Bryonia plants can be toxic if ingested by humans or animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bryony - any plant of the genus Bryonia

Etymology

From New Latin Bryonia, from Greek bryōnia (bryony).
