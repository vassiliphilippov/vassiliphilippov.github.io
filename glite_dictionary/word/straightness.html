straightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being free from curves, bends, or angles; the state of being straight.
the quality of being straight

Examples

The straightness of the road made it easy to see for miles ahead.
She admired the straightness of the shelf after adjusting it carefully.
Engineers tested the straightness of the metal beams before construction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curvature - the quality of being curved

Etymology

From 'straight' + '-ness', where 'straight' comes from Old English 'streht' (stretched, direct).

3. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtnəs / (BrE)

lack of curliness in hair

Examples

She admired the straightness of her friend's long black hair.
Over the years, he envied the straightness of his brother's hair compared to his own curls.
Many people use flat irons to achieve a greater straightness in their hair.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curliness - the degree to which hair forms curls or waves

Etymology

From straight + -ness, denoting the state or quality of being straight.

the quality of being direct or true in course toward a goal
trueness or directness of course or aim

Examples

The straightness of her drive toward success impressed everyone at the company.
Pilots must constantly adjust to maintain the straightness of their flight path.
He admired the straightness of her answer, never dodging the question.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward or direct in action or course
  • Antonym: deviation - a turning aside from a course or standard

From straight + -ness, where 'straight' comes from Middle English 'stret', from Old English 'streċe' (straight, stretched).
