Limenitis

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈmɛnɪtɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈmɛnɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mainly dark, northern butterflies, notable for having white bars across their wings.
genus of dark butterflies with white wing bars

Examples

The Limenitis butterflies are easily recognized by the white bands on their dark wings.
Researchers observed a rare Limenitis specimen in the northern forest last summer.
Many collectors admire the beauty of the Limenitis genus for its striking coloration.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'limenitis' (of harbours), used as a genus name in zoological classification.
