aliyah

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˈliːjə / (AmE), / aˈliːjə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) the immigration of Jews to Israel as a return to their ancestral homeland.
Jewish immigration to Israel

Examples

After graduating, David decided to make aliyah and start a new life in Israel.
The government's programs are designed to encourage aliyah from North America and Europe.
Many families consider aliyah not only a physical journey but also a spiritual one.

Synset Examples

students making aliyah

Etymology

From Hebrew עֲלִיָּה‎ (*ʿaliyá*) meaning 'ascent' or 'going up'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːˈliːə / (AmE), / ɑːˈliːə / (BrE)
(Judaism) the honor of being called up in the synagogue to recite or read a portion of the Torah during a service.
being called to read from the Torah in synagogue

Examples

Each week, different members of the congregation are given an aliyah during the service.
She felt deeply honored when she was invited for an aliyah at her nephew's bar mitzvah.
It is customary for those celebrating a special occasion to receive an aliyah in the synagogue.

Synset Examples

he was called on for an aliyah

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Torah reading - The public recitation of the Torah in synagogue services

Etymology

From Hebrew עֲלִיָּה (*ʿaliyāh*), meaning 'ascent' or 'going up'.
