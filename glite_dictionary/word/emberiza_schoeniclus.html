Emberiza schoeniclus

1. NOUN

/ ɛm.bəˈraɪ.zə skoʊˈnɪk.ləs / (AmE), / ɛm.bəˈraɪ.zə skɪˈnɪk.ləs / (BrE)
A species of European bunting, commonly known as the reed bunting, that inhabits marshy or wetland areas.
European marsh-dwelling reed bunting

Examples

The Emberiza schoeniclus is often observed flitting among reeds and grasses in European marshlands.
During migration season, birdwatchers look for the distinctive markings of the Emberiza schoeniclus.
Recent studies have tracked the habitat preferences of Emberiza schoeniclus across the British Isles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed bunting - Common name for Emberiza schoeniclus, a bird species found in wetlands

Etymology

From Latin: 'Emberiza' (a bunting genus) and 'schoeniclus' (reed-dwelling).
