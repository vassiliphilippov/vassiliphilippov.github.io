eternity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
The state of existence that continues forever, especially believed in some religions to characterize life after death.
endless existence, often the afterlife

Examples

Many religions promise eternity to the faithful after death.
The philosopher wrote about the soul's journey into eternity.
She wondered what it would be like to spend eternity in peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - without end, for all time
  • Synonym: infinity - the state or quality of being infinite or endless
  • Antonym: mortality - the state of being subject to death

Etymology

From Latin 'aeternitas' (eternity, immortality).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
Time that has no end; an infinite or unending period of existence.
time without end

Examples

Some cultures believe the soul lives on for all eternity.
Staring at the clock, it felt as though an eternity had passed.
The monuments seem built to last for eternity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinity - limitless or endless time, space, or quantity

Etymology

From Latin aeternitas (eternity), from aeternus (eternal).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
a period of time that seems to last forever, especially when waiting or enduring something
a seemingly endless period of time

Examples

The line moved so slowly that it felt like an eternity before it was my turn.
Waiting for the results seemed to take an eternity.
She stared at the clock, each minute dragging on like an eternity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - an endless or seemingly endless period of time
  • Antonym: moment - a very short period of time

Etymology

From Latin aeternitas, meaning 'infinite time' or 'age'.
