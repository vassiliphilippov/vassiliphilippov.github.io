malachite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæləˌkaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæləkʌɪt / (BrE)
a bright green or blue mineral consisting of copper carbonate hydroxide, used as an ore of copper and for making decorative objects
green or blue copper mineral

Examples

The ornate box was made of polished malachite and gold.
Miners discovered rich veins of malachite deep beneath the surface.
A stunning malachite necklace caught her eye at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azurite - a blue copper mineral often found with malachite

Etymology

From Middle French malachite, from Latin molochitis, from Ancient Greek malakhē (mallow) due to its leaf-green color.
