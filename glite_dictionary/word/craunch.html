to craunch

1. VERB

/ krɔntʃ / (AmE), / krɔːntʃ / (BrE)
To press, crush, or grind something with a harsh, crunching noise.
press or grind with a crushing noise
archaic

Examples

The carriage wheels craunched over the gravel driveway as it approached the mansion.
She craunched the dry leaves beneath her boots with each step through the forest.
I could hear bones craunching as the wolf devoured its prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch - to crush or grind with a noisy sound

Etymology

Variant of 'crunch', of imitative origin.
