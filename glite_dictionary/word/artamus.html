Artamus

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈteɪməs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɑːməs / (BrE)
The genus of birds known as woodswallows, forming the type genus of the family Artamidae.
genus of woodswallow birds

Examples

The genus Artamus includes several species of woodswallows found in Australasia.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Artamus in the Australian outback.
The black-faced woodswallow, part of the genus Artamus, is known for its communal roosting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodswallow - any bird of the family Artamidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Artamus', derived from Greek 'artamos' meaning 'butcher', referring to the bird's feeding habits.

2. NOUN

/ ɑˈtɑːməs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɑːməs / (BrE)
A genus of passerine birds known as woodswallows, belonging to the family Artamidae.
genus of woodswallows (birds)

Examples

The Artamus genus is native to Australasia and nearby islands.
Several species within Artamus are known for their aerial agility.
Researchers studied the feeding behaviors of Artamus in open woodlands.

Etymology

From Greek 'artamos', meaning butcher, referring to their feeding habits.
