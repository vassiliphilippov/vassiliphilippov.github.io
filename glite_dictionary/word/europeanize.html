to Europeanize

1. VERB

/ jʊˌroʊˈpiːəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊˈpiːənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more like European customs, culture, or ideas
make more European in character or customs

Examples

The government sought to Europeanize the country's institutions after joining the European Union.
Many local restaurants started to Europeanize their menus to attract international tourists.
His desire to Europeanize traditional art sparked controversy among cultural purists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to westernize - to make more like Western (especially Western European or American) culture

Etymology

From 'European' + '-ize', following common English verb formation.

2. VERB

/ jʊˌroʊpiəˈnaɪz / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊpiəˈnaɪz / (BrE)
To denationalize and place (a territory) under the supervision or control of a European community of nations.
place under European community supervision

Examples

The treaty sought to Europeanize the administration of the disputed region.
Moves to Europeanize certain eastern territories faced both support and resistance from local communities.
After the conflict, authorities attempted to Europeanize the governance of the area by placing it under EU oversight.

Etymology

From 'European' + '-ize', meaning to make or become European in some respect.
