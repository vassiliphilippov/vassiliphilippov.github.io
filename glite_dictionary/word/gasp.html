gasp

1. NOUN

/ ɡæsp / (AmE), / ɡɑːsp / (BrE)
A short, sudden intake of breath, often caused by surprise, shock, or pain.
sudden intake of breath

Examples

There was a collective gasp as the magician revealed his trick.
She let out a gasp when she saw the unexpected guest at the door.
The crowd's gasp echoed through the stadium after the shocking announcement.

Synset Examples

she gave a gasp and fainted

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sigh - a long, deep audible breath expressing emotion

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound of the breath.

2. VERB

/ ɡæsp / (AmE), / ɡɑːsp / (BrE)
to breathe in suddenly and noisily, often because of surprise, pain, or exhaustion
breathe in suddenly or noisily

Examples

She began to gasp for air after running up the stairs.
The audience gasped in shock when the magician disappeared.
He lay on the ground, gasping after the strenuous workout.

Synset Examples

The runners reached the finish line, panting heavily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pant - to breathe quickly and noisily, usually after exertion
  • Synonym: wheeze - to breathe with a whistling or rattling sound in the chest

Etymology

From Middle English gaspen (to open the mouth), possibly of Scandinavian origin.
