Andropogon furcatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌændrəˈpoʊɡɒn fɜrˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌændrəˈpəʊɡən fɜːˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A tall grass native to North America with smooth bluish leaf sheaths, cultivated for hay in the United States.
North American tall grass used for hay

Examples

The fields were full of Andropogon furcatus swaying in the late summer breeze.
Farmers in the Midwest often harvest Andropogon furcatus for high-quality hay.
Ecologists study Andropogon furcatus as an important species in native prairie restoration.

Etymology

From Greek 'andros' (man) + 'pogon' (beard), referring to the beard-like appendages of the grass; 'furcatus' is Latin for 'forked'.
