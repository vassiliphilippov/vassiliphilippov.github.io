gagster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡ.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæɡ.stə / (BrE)
a person who writes jokes or comic material for public performers, such as comedians or entertainers
writer of jokes or comedy routines

Examples

She hired a talented gagster to add humor to her late-night talk show.
The gagster was known for his quick wit and clever one-liners.
Many comedians rely on a gagster behind the scenes to write their funniest routines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gag writer - a person who writes jokes or humorous content, especially for others
  • Synonym: comedian - a person who entertains an audience with jokes and comic acts

Etymology

From 'gag' (slang for joke or comic line) + '-ster' (agent noun forming suffix).
