fearfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪə.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being afraid, especially in anticipation of danger or pain
state of being afraid

Examples

Her fearfulness was evident when she refused to enter the dark room alone.
The fearfulness of the situation made everyone tense and alert.
He overcame his fearfulness and spoke confidently in front of the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm
  • Antonym: bravery - the quality of being courageous

Etymology

From 'fearful' + '-ness', where 'fearful' is from Old English 'fǣrfull', and '-ness' is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪə.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being afraid or easily frightened
the trait of being afraid

Examples

Her fearfulness made it difficult for her to try new things.
The dog's fearfulness around strangers was obvious during walks in the park.
He recognized his own fearfulness and decided to work on becoming more confident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timidity - the quality of lacking courage or confidence
  • Antonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Antonym: fearlessness - the lack of fear; bravery

Etymology

From 'fearful' + '-ness', ultimately from Old English 'fǣran' (to frighten).
