jackstones

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌstoʊnz / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.stəʊnz / (BrE)
Small six-pointed metal pieces used in the game of jacks, which players pick up while bouncing a ball.
game pieces used in jacks

Examples

The children scattered the jackstones and began their game on the sidewalk.
She lost one of her jackstones, so the set was incomplete.
Traditional sets included metal jackstones and a small rubber ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacks - a children's game involving picking up objects while bouncing a ball

Etymology

From 'jack' (the game) + 'stone'; historically, jacks were originally played with small pebbles or bones.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌstoʊnz / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.stəʊnz / (BrE)
a traditional children's game in which small objects (jacks) are thrown and picked up in specific patterns between bounces of a ball
a children's game with jacks and a bouncing ball

Examples

The children played jackstones on the sidewalk until it was time for dinner.
She learned how to play jackstones from her grandmother.
In the old days, kids used real stones instead of metal pieces when playing jackstones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacks - a game played with a ball and small objects called jacks

Etymology

From 'jacks' (the small pieces used in the game) and 'stones', originally referring to actual stones before metal or plastic jacks were used.
