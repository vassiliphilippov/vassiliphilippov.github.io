gros point

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊ ˈpwæ̃ / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ ˈpwæ̃ / (BrE)
A type of needlepoint embroidery made with large, coarse stitches, often used for upholstery or decorative work.
needlepoint embroidery with large stitches

Examples

The tapestry was worked in gros point, which gave it a bold and textured appearance.
Her grandmother taught her how to do gros point for decorative pillows.
Museum visitors admired the antique chair covered in elegant gros point embroidery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petit point - needlepoint embroidery with very small, fine stitches

Etymology

From French 'gros point', literally 'large stitch'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡroʊ ˈpwæn(t) / (AmE), / ɡrɒ pwæ̃ / (BrE)
A type of needlepoint stitch that covers two horizontal and two vertical threads, producing a coarser texture.
coarse needlepoint stitch

Examples

She used gros point to give the tapestry a bold, textured appearance.
The antique chair was upholstered with an intricate gros point design.
Learning gros point is a good starting point for beginners in needlepoint.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: petit point - a fine needlepoint stitch covering a single horizontal and vertical thread

Etymology

From French 'gros point' meaning 'large stitch'.
