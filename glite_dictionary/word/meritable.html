meritable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛr.ɪ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛr.ɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
deserving reward or praise; worthy of merit
deserving reward or praise

Examples

The committee agreed that her years of meritable volunteer work should be publicly recognized.
It was a meritable achievement, earning him the admiration of his peers.
The judges are searching for the most meritable entry in the competition.

Synset Examples

a lifetime of meritorious service
meritorious conduct

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meritorious - deserving reward or praise
  • Antonym: blameworthy - deserving blame or censure

Etymology

From Medieval Latin meritabilis (‘worthy, deserving’), from Latin meritum (‘merit’).
