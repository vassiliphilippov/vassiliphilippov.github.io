arccosecant

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkˈkoʊsiːkənt / (AmE), / ˌɑːkˈkəʊsiːkənt / (BrE)
the inverse trigonometric function of cosecant; the angle whose cosecant is a given number.
inverse trigonometric function of cosecant

Examples

You can find the angle by taking the arccosecant of the number.
In trigonometry, the arccosecant is used to determine the angle when the value of cosecant is known.
The arccosecant function is less common than arcsine or arccosine but is sometimes needed in advanced calculations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcsecant - inverse trigonometric function of secant
  • Antonym: cosecant - trigonometric function; reciprocal of sine

Etymology

From Latin arcus (bow, arc) and cosecant (from co- + secant).
