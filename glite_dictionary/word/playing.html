playing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of performing a part or role in a drama
performance of a dramatic role

Examples

Her playing of Lady Macbeth was highly acclaimed by the critics.
The actor's playing brought a unique perspective to the character.
The director praised the cast's playing during the dress rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a role or piece
  • Synonym: acting - the art or practice of performing in plays

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of taking part in a game, sport, or other form of recreation
participation in games or recreation

Examples

The children's laughter echoed through the park while they were playing outside.
Regular playing helps athletes stay in good physical condition.
She injured her knee during playing in the soccer match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play - to engage in a game or activity for enjoyment

3. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of performing on a musical instrument
performing on a musical instrument

Examples

Her playing on the piano brought tears to everyone's eyes.
The audience admired his skillful playing of the violin.
She received several awards for her outstanding playing during the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing music for an audience

Etymology

Present participle of 'play', used as a noun since at least the 17th century.
