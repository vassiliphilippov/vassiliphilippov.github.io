Tl

1. NOUN

/ tiː ɛl / (AmE), / tiː ɛl / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for thallium, a soft gray malleable metallic element that discolors on exposure to air and is highly toxic, used in pest poisons
chemical symbol for thallium (element)

Examples

The chemical symbol Tl stands for thallium on the periodic table.
Industrial chemists must handle Tl with extreme caution due to its toxicity.
Old rodent poisons often contained Tl as their active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thallium - a soft, malleable, and highly toxic metallic element

Etymology

Symbol from New Latin *thallium*, named by its discoverer after the green line (Greek *thallos*: a green shoot) seen in its spectrum.
