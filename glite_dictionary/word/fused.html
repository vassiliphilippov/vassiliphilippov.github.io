fused

1. ADJ

/ fjuzd / (AmE), / fjuÀêzd / (BrE)
joined together to form a single entity or mass
joined together as a whole

Examples

The artists created a sculpture in which different metals were fused seamlessly together.
The committee was formed after several teams were fused into one group.
Their ideas gradually fused into a single, comprehensive proposal.

Synset Examples

the amalgamated colleges constituted a university
a consolidated school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merged - combined into one, united
  • Antonym: separate - not joined or connected; kept apart

Etymology

Past participle of 'fuse', from Latin 'fundere' (to melt).
