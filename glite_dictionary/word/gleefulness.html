gleefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡliːfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡliːfəlnəs / (BrE)
a feeling or state of great joy and merriment
great joy or merriment

Examples

Her gleefulness was evident when she heard the good news.
The children's gleefulness filled the playground with laughter.
He couldn't hide his gleefulness at winning the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glee - great delight or joy
  • Synonym: mirth - amusement or happiness
  • Antonym: sadness - the state of being unhappy or sorrowful

Etymology

From the adjective 'gleeful' + suffix '-ness'.
