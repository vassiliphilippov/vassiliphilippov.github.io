Bradypus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrædɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈbrædɪpəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Bradypodidae, comprising the three-toed sloths native to Central and South America.
type genus of three-toed sloths

Examples

The Bradypus genus includes the brown-throated three-toed sloth.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Bradypus genus.
Bradypus sloths are known for their extremely slow movement and tree-dwelling habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-toed sloth - a sloth with three toes on each foot, genus Bradypus
  • Degree Lower: sloth - a slow-moving arboreal mammal of South and Central America

Etymology

From Greek bradys (slow) + pous (foot), referring to the animal's slow movement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪdɪˌpʊs / (AmE), / ˈbrædɪpəs / (BrE)
A genus of slow-moving mammals known as the three-toed sloths, native to Central and South America.
genus of three-toed sloths

Examples

The Bradypus genus includes all species of three-toed sloths.
Researchers studied the behavior of Bradypus in its natural habitat.
The most common member of the Bradypus genus is Bradypus variegatus, the brown-throated three-toed sloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - a slow-moving tropical American mammal

Etymology

From Greek bradys (slow) + pous (foot).
