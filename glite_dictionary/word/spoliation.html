spoliation

1. NOUN

/ ˌspoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The intentional destruction or alteration of a document or evidence, impairing its value or usefulness in a legal context.
intentional destruction of legal evidence
legal

Examples

The court imposed sanctions for the spoliation of crucial documents in the case.
Lawyers argued that the company's data deletion amounted to spoliation of evidence.
To prevent spoliation, parties are instructed to preserve all relevant emails and files.

Etymology

From Late Latin spoliatio (plundering), from Latin spoliare (to strip, plunder).

2. NOUN

/ ˌspoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking goods or property by force, especially wrongfully or illegally; the act of plundering or despoiling
the act of taking by force; plundering

Examples

The museum was accused of spoliation of artifacts during the war.
Spoliation of natural resources can have devastating environmental consequences.
The court handled a case involving the spoliation of official documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in time of war or civil disorder

Etymology

From Latin spoliatio (taking away, plundering), from spoliare (to plunder).
