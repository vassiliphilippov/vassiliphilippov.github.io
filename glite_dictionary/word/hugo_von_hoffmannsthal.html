Hugo von Hoffmannsthal

1. PROPN

/ ˈhuːgoʊ vɒn ˈhɔːfmanˌʃtɑːl / (AmE), / ˈhuːɡəʊ fɒn ˈhɒfmanʃtɑːl / (BrE)
(1874–1929) An Austrian poet, dramatist, and essayist, known for writing libretti for operas by Richard Strauss.
Austrian poet and librettist (1874–1929)

Examples

Hugo von Hoffmannsthal collaborated closely with Richard Strauss on many famous operas.
Many consider Hugo von Hoffmannsthal one of the leading figures of early 20th-century Austrian literature.
The works of Hugo von Hoffmannsthal had a profound influence on European theatre and poetry.
