to transgress

1. VERB

/ trænzˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / trænzˈɡres / (BrE)
(of the sea) to spread over land, especially as shoreline subsides or sea level rises.
(of sea) spread over land

Examples

In prehistoric times, the ocean would often transgress far into the coastal plains.
Geologists studied how the sea continues to transgress inland due to shifting tectonic plates.
If sea levels rise significantly, the water may transgress onto farmlands near the coast.

Synset Examples

The sea transgresses along the West coast of the island

Etymology

From Latin transgredi (to step across), from trans- (across) + gradi (to step).

2. VERB

/ trænzˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / trænzˈɡrɛs / (BrE)
to do something that is not allowed by law, custom, or moral principle; to violate a rule or boundary
violate a law or moral rule

Examples

If you transgress the rules of the club, you may be asked to leave.
He felt guilty after he transgressed against his own principles.
Those who continually transgress may eventually face serious consequences.
