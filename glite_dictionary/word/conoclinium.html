Conoclinium

1. NOUN

/ ˌkəʊnəˈklɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈklɪniəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the aster family, commonly known as mistflowers.
genus of flowering plants (mistflower)

Examples

The garden’s border was a sea of purple thanks to the blooming Conoclinium.
Many butterflies are attracted to Conoclinium for its abundant nectar.
Botanists recently reclassified several species under the genus Conoclinium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistflower - a common name for species of Conoclinium

Etymology

From Greek 'kōnos' (cone) and 'klinion' (little bed), referring to the shape of the plant's flower clusters.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.nəˈklɪni.əm / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.nəˈklɪni.əm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as mistflowers.
genus of plants called mistflowers

Examples

Conoclinium is native to North and Central America and is valued for its attractive blue flowers.
Many gardeners plant Conoclinium to attract butterflies to their gardens.
The distinctive flowers of Conoclinium appear in late summer and early autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistflower - common name for species in the genus Conoclinium

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'konos' (cone) and 'klinion' (little bed).
