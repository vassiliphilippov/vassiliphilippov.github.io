munggo

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʊŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
The edible seed of the mung bean plant, commonly used as food, especially in Filipino and Southeast Asian cuisines.
seed of the mung bean used as food

Examples

She cooked a delicious stew using munggo and vegetables for dinner.
In the Philippines, munggo is often eaten on Fridays as a meat substitute.
The market sells both dried munggo and already-sprouted beans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the plant Vigna radiata or its seed used in various cuisines

Etymology

From Filipino 'munggo', borrowed from Hokkien Chinese 'mēng-ngu̍t' (mung bean).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmuŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
the seed of the mung bean plant, used as food, especially in Filipino cuisine
seed of the mung bean plant

Examples

My grandmother served a delicious stew made with munggo.
We bought a bag of munggo to make a traditional Filipino soup.
The munggo sprouted quickly and was ready to eat in a few days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the edible seed or the plant Vigna radiata

Etymology

From Filipino "munggo", itself from Spanish "monggo", both ultimately from the Hindi "moong" via Malay "munggu".
