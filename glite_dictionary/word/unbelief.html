unbelief

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.bɪˈlif / (AmE), / ˌʌn.bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
lack of religious belief or refusal to believe; absence or rejection of faith or trust.
absence or rejection of belief

Examples

His unbelief was evident in the way he questioned every doctrine.
The novel explores themes of faith and unbelief in modern society.
Even in the face of miracles, her unbelief did not waver.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: belief - acceptance that something exists or is true, especially without proof
  • Synonym: disbelief - inability or refusal to accept that something is true or real

Etymology

From un- (not) + belief; Middle English 'unbileve'.
