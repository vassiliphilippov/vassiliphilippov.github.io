sui generis

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.i ˈdʒɛn.ər.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌsuː.i ˈdʒɛn.ər.ɪs / (BrE)
constituting a class or type of its own; unique; unlike anything else
unique; of its own kind

Examples

The artist's new sculpture is truly sui generis and unlike anything the gallery has displayed before.
Her writing style is so sui generis that it defies easy classification.
This theory stands as a sui generis contribution to modern philosophy.

Synset Examples

a history book sui generis
sui generis works like Mary Chestnut's Civil War diary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unique - being the only one of its kind; unlike anything else
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Latin 'sui generis', meaning 'of its own kind or class'.
