loganberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ɡənˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ɡənˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
a hybrid bramble fruit, deep red in color, developed from the crossing of a raspberry and a blackberry, native to the western United States.
hybrid red bramble fruit

Examples

I added fresh loganberries to the fruit salad for extra flavor.
The loganberry bush in our garden produced a large crop this year.
She served homemade jam made from loganberries at breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a small, soft red or black fruit with many small seeds
  • Synonym: blackberry - a soft black or dark purple fruit

Etymology

Named after James Harvey Logan, who first cultivated it in California in the late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ɡənˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ɡənˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A large, tangy red or reddish-purple berry, the fruit of a bramble (Rubus × loganobaccus), developed as a hybrid between a raspberry and a blackberry.
a hybrid red berry fruit

Examples

We made delicious jam from the fresh loganberries we picked yesterday.
She planted a loganberry bush in her garden beside the raspberries and blackberries.
The tart flavor of the loganberry makes it perfect for pies and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - an edible soft fruit related to the loganberry
  • Synonym: blackberry - an edible dark purple-black fruit, one parent of the loganberry

Etymology

Named after James Harvey Logan, the American judge who first cultivated it in the late 19th century.
