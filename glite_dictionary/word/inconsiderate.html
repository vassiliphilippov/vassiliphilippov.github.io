inconsiderate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kənˈsɪd.ə.rət / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈsɪd.ər.ət / (BrE)
not caring about the rights, feelings, or needs of others; thoughtlessly causing inconvenience or harm
lacking concern for others

Examples

It was inconsiderate of him to play loud music late at night.
She found his inconsiderate remarks hurtful.
Leaving your trash for others to clean up is extremely inconsiderate.

Synset Examples

shockingly inconsiderate behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtless - not showing consideration for the needs of other people
  • Antonym: considerate - careful not to cause inconvenience or hurt to others

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + considerate (careful, thoughtful), via Late Latin consideratus.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈsɪdərət / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈsɪdərət / (BrE)
done without careful thought, consideration, or reflection; lacking due regard for the consequences.
done without careful thought

Examples

His comments were inconsiderate and hurtful to the people involved.
It was inconsiderate to make such a decision without consulting your team.
She apologized for her inconsiderate behavior during the meeting.

Synset Examples

slovenly inconsiderate reasoning
unconsidered words
prejudice is the holding of unconsidered opinions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtless - lacking consideration for others or for consequences
  • Antonym: considerate - showing careful thought or regard for others

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'consideratus' (deliberated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02429531-s
Synset POS: s
