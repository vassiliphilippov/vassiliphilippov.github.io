sanitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of keeping places free from dirt, infection, and disease by removing waste and sterilizing surfaces.
the act of keeping places clean and hygienic

Examples

Proper sanitation is essential to prevent the spread of infectious diseases.
After the flood, workers focused on sanitation to ensure the water was safe to drink.
The lack of adequate sanitation in the area led to a public health crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygiene - practices to maintain health and prevent disease
  • Antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances causing harm

Etymology

From Latin 'sanitas' (health) + English suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsænəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of keeping places clean and hygienic to promote health and prevent disease.
the state of being clean and hygienic

Examples

Access to proper sanitation is essential for preventing the spread of diseases.
The city invested millions in improving water supply and sanitation facilities.
Poor sanitation in the area led to a serious health crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygiene - practices to maintain health and prevent disease, especially through cleanliness

Etymology

From Latin 'sanitas' (health) via French 'sanitation'.
