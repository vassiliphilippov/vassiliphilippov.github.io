cardinal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːrdɪnl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnl̩ / (BrE)
(of a number) expressing quantity rather than order, such as one, two, or three (as opposed to first, second, third).
expressing numerical quantity, not order

Examples

Most children learn the cardinal numbers before they understand ordinal numbers.
In linguistics, a cardinal numeral indicates quantity but not position.
The teacher explained the difference between cardinal and ordinal numbers.

Synset Examples

cardinal numbers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordinal - denoting position or order, not quantity, as in first, second, third

Etymology

From Latin 'cardinalis' (principal, chief), from 'cardo' (hinge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdɪnl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl / (BrE)
a senior official of the Roman Catholic Church, appointed by the Pope to serve as a principal advisor and to participate in the election of new Popes.
senior Catholic official assisting the Pope

Examples

The cardinal traveled to Rome to attend the papal conclave.
A new cardinal was appointed by the Pope last week.
Many cardinals gathered for the important church ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bishop - senior member of the Christian clergy, generally in charge of a diocese

Etymology

From Latin cardinalis (principal, chief), from cardo (hinge).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A number representing the quantity of elements in a set, irrespective of their order.
number denoting quantity in a set (math)
medical

Examples

In set theory, the cardinal of a finite set is simply its number of elements.
Mathematicians use the concept of cardinal to compare the sizes of infinite sets.
The cardinal of the empty set is zero.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordinal - a number indicating position or order
  • Synonym: cardinal number - a number that expresses quantity (not order)

Etymology

From Latin 'cardinalis' (principal, chief), from 'cardo' (hinge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13618828-n
Synset POS: n

6. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdənl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnl / (BrE)
a North American songbird with a distinctive crest and a thick bill, the male of which has bright red plumage
a crested North American songbird

Examples

A bright red cardinal perched on the fence, singing cheerfully in the morning sun.
It's easy to spot a male cardinal in winter against the snowy landscape.
Several cardinals visit our backyard bird feeder every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern cardinal - another name for the cardinal bird, especially Cardinalis cardinalis

Etymology

From Latin *cardinalis*, used in Modern Latin as genus name *Cardinalis* for the bird.
