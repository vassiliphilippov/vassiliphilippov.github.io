vigour

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
physical or mental strength, energy, or enthusiasm; the capacity for active and forceful action.
physical or mental energy and strength
british

Examples

After his illness, he regained his vigour and returned to work with renewed enthusiasm.
The children played in the garden with remarkable vigour even in the heat.
She attacked the problem with her usual vigour and determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - American spelling of vigour
  • Antonym: languor - lack of physical or mental energy

Etymology

From Latin 'vigorem' (liveliness, activity), via Old French 'vigueur'.
