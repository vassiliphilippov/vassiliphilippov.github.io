vindictive

1. ADJ

/ vɪnˈdɪktɪv / (AmE), / vɪnˈdɪktɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a strong or unreasoning desire for revenge; showing malice or a tendency to seek retribution.
having a desire for revenge

Examples

Her criticism sounded vindictive, as if she wanted to hurt his feelings.
Some people become vindictive after being wronged, seeking ways to get even.
He denied making any vindictive remarks about his former colleagues.

Synset Examples

a despiteful fiend
a truly spiteful child
a vindictive man will look for occasions for resentment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiteful - showing or caused by malice
  • Synonym: revengeful - eager for revenge; inclined to retaliate
  • Antonym: forgiving - willing to forgive; not seeking revenge

Etymology

From Latin vindicta (revenge, punishment), via French vindicte.

2. ADJ

/ vɪnˈdɪktɪv / (AmE), / vɪnˈdɪktɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a strong or unreasoning desire for revenge
having a tendency to seek revenge

Examples

She became vindictive after the betrayal and sought ways to make him suffer.
His vindictive attitude made it difficult for anyone to trust him again.
The punishment seemed excessively vindictive rather than just or fair.

Synset Examples

more vindictive than jealous love
punishments … essentially vindictive in their nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiteful - having or showing a desire to harm, anger, or defeat someone
  • Antonym: forgiving - willing or able to forgive; not seeking revenge

Etymology

From Latin 'vindicta' (revenge) via French 'vindictif'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01045316-s
Synset POS: s
