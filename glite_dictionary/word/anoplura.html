Anoplura

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈplʊrə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈplʊərə / (BrE)
An order of parasitic insects known as sucking lice, which live on mammals and feed on their blood.
order of sucking lice
medical

Examples

Veterinarians often treat mammals that have been infested with Anoplura.
Researchers studied the transmission of disease among animals carrying Anoplura.
The presence of Anoplura can be an indicator of poor hygiene in livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small parasitic insect, singular form of lice

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek an- (not) + hoplon (weapon) + oura (tail), referring to their anatomy.
