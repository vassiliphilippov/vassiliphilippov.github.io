Syringa emodi

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə ˈɛm.əˌdaɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə ˈɛməʊdi / (BrE)
a robust upright shrub native to the mountains of northern India, with oblong-elliptic leaves and pale lilac or white malodorous flowers
a northern Indian lilac shrub species

Examples

The Syringa emodi is prized in gardens for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
Botanists noted that Syringa emodi produces a distinctive, somewhat unpleasant fragrance when in bloom.
On our trek through the Himalayas, we spotted numerous Syringa emodi shrubs among the rocky slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - a type of shrub with fragrant flowers, genus Syringa

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Syringa' from Greek 'syrinx' meaning 'pipe' (referring to the plant’s hollow stems) and 'emodi' referring to the Himalayas (Emodus).
