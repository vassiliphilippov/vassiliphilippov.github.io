Viola conspersa

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə kənˈspɜrsə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə kənˈspɜːsə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to eastern North America, having pale violet to white flowers.
a North American violet species

Examples

The delicate flowers of the Viola conspersa can be seen carpeting shady woodlands in the spring.
Gardeners appreciate Viola conspersa for its subtle beauty and its ability to thrive in moist soils.
During the nature walk, we identified several clusters of Viola conspersa along the forest path.

Etymology

From Latin: viola (violet) + conspersa (besprinkled, scattered).
