Schlesien

1. PROPN

/ ʃleˈziːən / (AmE), / ʃleˈziːən / (BrE)
The German name for Silesia, a historical region in central Europe known for its coal and iron ore deposits, much of which is now in Poland.
German name for Silesia, European region

Examples

Much of modern-day Schlesien now lies within the borders of Poland.
The coal mines of Schlesien played a crucial role in European industry.
After World War II, millions of Germans left Schlesien when it became part of Poland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Silesia - A historical region of central Europe, mostly in Poland

Etymology

German form of Silesia, from medieval Latin Silesia, likely from the Ślęza river or Mount Ślęża in Poland.
