laevulose

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvjuˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈliːvjʊˌləʊs / (BrE)
a simple sugar, also known as fructose, found in honey and many ripe fruits
a sugar found in fruit and honey
medical

Examples

Laevulose is abundant in ripe apples and pears, contributing to their sweetness.
The laboratory analysis confirmed the presence of laevulose in the honey sample.
Some people find that laevulose is easier to digest than other types of sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fructose - a simple sugar found in fruit and honey

Etymology

From Latin 'laevus' (left) and '-ose' (sugar). Named for its left-handed optical rotation.
