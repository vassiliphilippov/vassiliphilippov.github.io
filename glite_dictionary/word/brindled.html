brindled

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪn.dəld / (AmE), / ˈbrɪn.dld / (BrE)
having a grey or brown streaked or patchy coloring, especially used to describe the fur or coat of animals such as cats or dogs
streaked or patchy coloring (esp. animal fur)

Examples

The brindled cat curled up on the windowsill, its fur a mix of browns and greys.
She adopted a brindled puppy from the shelter, loving its unusual coat pattern.
A herd of brindled cattle grazed peacefully in the early morning light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brindle - a brownish or grayish streaked or patchy color, especially in animal fur

Etymology

From 'brindle' (an 18th-century term for a streaked or patterned coloration, especially in animals).
