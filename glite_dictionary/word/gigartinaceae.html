Gigartinaceae

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˌɡɑːrtɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / dʒɪˌɡɑːtɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of red algae in the order Gigartinales, found primarily in marine environments
family of red algae

Examples

Species in the Gigartinaceae family are important sources of carrageenan.
Scientists collected several samples of Gigartinaceae from the intertidal zone.
The identification of Gigartinaceae requires microscopic examination of their reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red algae - a group of algae that have a reddish color due to their pigments

Etymology

From the genus name Gigartina plus the family suffix -aceae, following botanical Latin conventions.
