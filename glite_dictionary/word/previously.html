previously

1. ADV

/ ˈpriːviəsli / (AmE), / ˈpriːviəsli / (BrE)
At an earlier time; before now or before the time mentioned.
at an earlier time, before

Examples

I had previously visited the museum, so I knew what to expect.
The rules were previously unclear, but now they have been clarified.
She previously worked in advertising before changing careers.

Synset Examples

she had previously lived in Chicago
he was previously president of a bank
better than anything previously proposed
a previously unquestioned attitude
antecedently arranged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beforehand - in advance; ahead of time
  • Synonym: formerly - in the past; previously
  • Antonym: subsequently - afterward; later

Etymology

From 'previous' + '-ly'. 'Previous' from Latin 'prævius', meaning 'going before'.
