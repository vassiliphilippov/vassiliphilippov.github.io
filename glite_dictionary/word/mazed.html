mazed

1. ADJ

/ meɪzd / (AmE), / meɪzd / (BrE)
bewildered or confused by many conflicting situations or statements; perplexed or lost in thought
bewildered or confused
archaic

Examples

He looked mazed when they told him the truth about his past.
The mazed traveler wandered the winding paths for hours, unable to find the exit.
She stood in the center of the bustling market, feeling mazed by the unfamiliar sights and sounds.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewildered - confused and puzzled
  • Synonym: perplexed - very puzzled or confused
  • Antonym: clear-headed - thinking in a calm and sensible way

Etymology

From Middle English *mesed* (bewildered, confused), related to 'maze' (a network of paths designed as a puzzle).
