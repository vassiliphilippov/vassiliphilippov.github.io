Anomia ephippium

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊ.mi.ə əˈfɪp.i.əm / (AmE), / əˈnəʊ.mi.ə ɪˈfɪp.i.əm / (BrE)
a thin-shelled bivalve mollusk with the right valve deeply notched, commonly found attached to rocks or shells in marine environments.
species of thin-shelled bivalve mollusk

Examples

Researchers discovered a colony of Anomia ephippium attached to the rocks along the shoreline.
The distinctive notch in the right valve helps identify Anomia ephippium among similar bivalves.
In marine biology class, we studied the habitat preferences of Anomia ephippium.

Etymology

From Latin *Anomia* (a genus of bivalve mollusks) and *ephippium* (Latin for 'saddle', referring to the shape of the shell).
