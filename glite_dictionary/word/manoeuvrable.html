manoeuvrable

1. ADJ

/ məˈnuːvərəbl̩ / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvərəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of being easily steered or moved into different positions
able to be easily steered or moved

Examples

The new drone is highly manoeuvrable, allowing it to avoid obstacles easily.
A smaller car tends to be more manoeuvrable on narrow city streets.
Despite its size, the yacht remained remarkably manoeuvrable in the crowded harbor.

Synset Examples

a highly maneuverable ship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agile - able to move quickly and easily
  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement, not easily controlled

Etymology

From French 'manoeuvrable', from 'manoeuvrer' (to maneuver), ultimately from Latin 'manus' (hand) + 'opera' (work).
