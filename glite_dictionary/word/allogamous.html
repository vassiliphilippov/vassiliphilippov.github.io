allogamous

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɑːɡəməs / (AmE), / əˈlɒɡəməs / (BrE)
(of a plant) reproducing by cross-fertilization with pollen from another individual of the same species.
relating to cross-fertilization in plants
medical

Examples

Many flowering plants are allogamous, requiring pollen from different individuals for successful reproduction.
Researchers found that allogamous species showed greater genetic diversity than self-pollinating plants.
In agricultural settings, allogamous crops may need pollinators like bees to ensure fertilization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autogamous - self-fertilizing in plants

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) and 'gamos' (marriage).
