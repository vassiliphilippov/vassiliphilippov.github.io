systema lymphaticum

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈstɛmə lɪmˈfætɪkəm / (AmE), / sɪˈstɛmə lɪmˈfatɪkəm / (BrE)
the interconnected system of vessels and spaces in the body through which lymph circulates; the lymphatic system
the lymphatic system (medical term)
medical

Examples

In medical anatomy, the systema lymphaticum plays a crucial role in immune defense.
Diseases affecting the systema lymphaticum can lead to swelling and impaired immunity.
Textbooks often refer to the systema lymphaticum when describing lymphatic circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphatic system - the network of tissues and organs that help rid the body of toxins, waste and other unwanted materials

Etymology

From Latin: 'systema' meaning system, and 'lymphaticum' pertaining to lymph.
