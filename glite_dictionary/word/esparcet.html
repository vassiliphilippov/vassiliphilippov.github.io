esparcet

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈspɑːrsɛt / (AmE), / ɛˈspaːsɛt / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb of the pea family, with pale pink flowers and curved pods, grown as a forage crop and honey source, especially in calcareous soils.
forage crop with pink flowers

Examples

Farmers often grow esparcet to improve soil nitrogen and provide high-quality fodder for livestock.
Honey produced from esparcet flowers is highly prized in some regions for its delicate flavor.
The botanist studied the adaptation of esparcet to chalk and limestone grasslands.

Etymology

From French 'esparcette', from Provençal 'esparceta'.
