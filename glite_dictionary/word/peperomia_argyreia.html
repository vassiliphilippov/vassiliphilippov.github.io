Peperomia argyreia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛpəˈroʊmiə ɑːrˈdʒɪriə / (AmE), / ˌpɛpəˈrəʊmiə ɑːˈdʒɪəriə / (BrE)
a species of South American houseplant with fleshy, silvery-striped leaves, commonly grown for ornamental purposes
South American houseplant with striped leaves

Examples

The Peperomia argyreia is popular for its striking, watermelon-like leaf pattern.
She placed the Peperomia argyreia on her windowsill to brighten the room.
Because of its low maintenance, Peperomia argyreia is ideal for beginner plant owners.

Etymology

From the genus name Peperomia, derived from Greek 'peperi' (pepper) and 'homoios' (resembling), and the species name 'argyreia' from Greek 'argyros' (silver), referring to the silvery leaves.
