Tropaeolum minus

1. NOUN

/ troʊˈpiːəliəm ˈmaɪnəs / (AmE), / trəˈpiːəliəm ˈmaɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of annual flowering plant, known as the dwarf or small nasturtium, with deep yellow flowers smaller than those of the common garden nasturtium.
a species of yellow-flowered nasturtium

Examples

Tropaeolum minus adds a splash of color to summer gardens with its delicate yellow blossoms.
Gardeners often choose Tropaeolum minus for container displays due to its compact growth habit.
The leaves and flowers of Tropaeolum minus are both edible and often used in salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasturtium - a genus of flowering plants with edible, brightly colored flowers

Etymology

From Latin tropaeolum (diminutive form indicating small size) and minus (smaller).
