Chanukah

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːnʊkə / (AmE), / ˈxanʊkə / (BrE)
A Jewish festival lasting eight days that commemorates the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC, also known as Hanukkah.
Jewish festival of lights

Examples

Each year, they light the menorah to celebrate Chanukah with their family.
Schools often teach children about the story and traditions associated with Chanukah.
During Chanukah, families gather to play dreidel and eat special foods like latkes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanukkah - alternative spelling of the Jewish festival Chanukah

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥănukkāh (dedication).
