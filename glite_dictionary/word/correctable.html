correctable

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be corrected or put right.
able to be corrected

Examples

The error in the data was correctable, so she quickly fixed it.
Fortunately, the patient's vision problem was correctable with glasses.
Most grammatical mistakes in the essay are correctable by careful proofreading.

Synset Examples

an amendable flaw

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorrigible - not able to be corrected or improved
  • Synonym: amendable - capable of being amended or improved

Etymology

From Latin corrigere (to correct) + -able.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈrɛktəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be corrected or restored to a proper or original condition; not necessarily permanent.
able to be corrected

Examples

Fortunately, the error in the document was correctable before printing began.
These minor defects are correctable with a simple adjustment.
Doctors assured him that his vision problem was correctable with surgery.

Synset Examples

a correctable image

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorrigible - not able to be corrected or reformed
  • Synonym: fixable - able to be fixed or repaired

Etymology

Formed from 'correct' + '-able', from Latin 'corrigere' (to correct) + English suffix '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01763604-s
Synset POS: s
