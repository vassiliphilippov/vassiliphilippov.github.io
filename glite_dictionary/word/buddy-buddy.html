buddy-buddy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌdi ˈbʌdi / (AmE), / ˈbʌdi ˈbʌdi / (BrE)
(informal) behaving in a very friendly or intimate way, especially in a way that suggests a close personal relationship.
very friendly; on intimate terms
informal

Examples

They've become quite buddy-buddy since working together on that project.
Some colleagues are almost too buddy-buddy with the boss.
The film stars are so buddy-buddy off-screen that fans suspect they're best friends in real life.

Synset Examples

the bartender was chummy with the regular customers
the two were thick as thieves for months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chummy - friendly, on close terms
  • Synonym: thick as thieves - very close or friendly with someone
  • Antonym: distant - not close or friendly

Etymology

From 'buddy' (informal for friend or companion), reduplicated for emphasis.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌdi ˌbʌdi / (AmE), / ˈbʌdi ˌbʌdi / (BrE)
(informal) overly friendly or intimate, often in a way that is viewed as suspicious or inappropriate.
overly friendly, often suspiciously
informal american

Examples

Ever since the team-building retreat, they've been buddy-buddy with each other.
I'm not sure why she acts so buddy-buddy with the new manager.
People started wondering when he became buddy-buddy with the competition.

Synset Examples

I don't trust them; they're too buddy-buddy with the boss.
They became buddy-buddy during the project.
They got all buddy-buddy after working together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chummy - friendly in a familiar or intimate way
  • Antonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant

Etymology

Formed from reduplication of 'buddy,' an American English term for friend.
