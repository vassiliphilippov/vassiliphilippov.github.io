jabot

1. NOUN

/ ʒæˈboʊ / (AmE), / ʒaˈboʊ / (BrE)
A decorative ruffle or frill attached to the front of a woman's blouse or a man's shirt, often used in formal or historical clothing.
decorative ruffle on front of blouse or shirt

Examples

The judge wore a black robe and a white jabot around her neck.
Her blouse was embellished with a delicate lace jabot.
In the painting, he is depicted in a shirt with a prominent jabot.

Etymology

From French 'jabot', originally meaning 'bird's crop' or throat, later used for the ruffled decoration.
