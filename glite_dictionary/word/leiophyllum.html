Leiophyllum

1. NOUN

/ leɪ.iˈɑfɪləm / (AmE), / leɪ.ɪˈɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, consisting of a single species known as sand myrtle, native to the eastern United States.
genus of shrub; sand myrtle

Examples

The Leiophyllum blossoms add a splash of white to the rocky landscape each spring.
Botanists studied the habitat requirements of Leiophyllum in the southern Appalachians.
Gardeners appreciate Leiophyllum for its tolerance to sandy, acidic soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand myrtle - Evergreen shrub and only species in the genus Leiophyllum

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) and 'phyllon' (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ liː.oʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / liː.ɒˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, containing only one species, the sand myrtle.
genus of evergreen shrubs (sand myrtle)

Examples

The Leiophyllum plant is native to eastern North America.
Botanists identified the shrub as a member of the Leiophyllum genus.
Gardeners appreciate Leiophyllum for its delicate, evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the smooth leaves of the plant.
