Erica perspicua

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪkə pərˈspɪkjuə / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪkə pəˈspɪkjʊə / (BrE)
A South African shrub of the heath family, grown for its profuse white flowers.
South African shrub with white flowers

Examples

Gardeners value Erica perspicua for its showy clusters of white blooms.
We planted Erica perspicua along the border to add year-round interest.
Erica perspicua thrives in well-drained, acidic soils typical of the South African fynbos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - a shrubland plant of the genus Erica or Calluna

Etymology

From Latin: 'Erica' (heath) + 'perspicua' (transparent, clear).
