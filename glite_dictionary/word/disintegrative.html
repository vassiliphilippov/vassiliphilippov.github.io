disintegrative

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈɪntɪɡrətɪv / (BrE)
tending to cause breaking up or decomposition into parts or elements.
tending to cause breakup into parts

Examples

The scientist observed that the chemical had a disintegrative effect on the material.
Long-term stress can have a disintegrative impact on a person's mental health.
The disintegrative influence of rumors threatened the unity of the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrative - tending to bring parts together into a whole

Etymology

From disintegrate (to break into parts) + -ive (forming adjectives).
