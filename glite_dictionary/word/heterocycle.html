heterocycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛt̬.ər.oʊ.saɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ərəʊ.saɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains a ring structure composed of atoms of at least two different elements, typically carbon and at least one other element such as nitrogen, oxygen, or sulfur.
ring molecule with atoms of more than one element
medical

Examples

The synthesis of a new heterocycle can lead to novel pharmaceuticals.
Many natural products, such as vitamins, contain a heterocycle in their structure.
The presence of nitrogen atoms in a heterocycle can significantly alter its reactivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carbocycle - a ring composed entirely of carbon atoms

Etymology

From hetero- (different) + cycle (ring, circle), reflecting a ring of differing atoms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛt̬ɚ.oʊˌsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ər.əʊˌsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A chemical compound whose molecules contain a ring structure composed of atoms of at least two different elements.
compound with a ring of mixed elements
medical

Examples

Many pharmaceuticals are based on heterocycles due to their unique chemical properties.
The presence of nitrogen in the heterocycle makes it react differently from simple hydrocarbons.
Researchers synthesized a new heterocycle with potential antibacterial activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterocyclic - Containing a ring structure with different elements

Etymology

From hetero- (different) + cycle (ring, circle).
