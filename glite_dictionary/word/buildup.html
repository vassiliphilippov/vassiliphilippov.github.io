buildup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɪldˌʌp / (BrE)
an increase in amount, size, or intensity of something over time, especially due to gradual accumulation
gradual accumulation or increase

Examples

The buildup of dust on the shelves became noticeable after a few weeks.
There is a significant buildup of traffic around the stadium before big events.
Doctors warned about the buildup of plaque in the arteries.

Synset Examples

the buildup of leaves blocked the drain pipes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulation - the process or result of collecting or gathering over time

Etymology

Formed from 'build' + 'up', first recorded in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlˌdʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɪldʌp / (BrE)
intensive and enthusiastic publicity or praise intended to promote someone or something.
intensive publicity or praise

Examples

The movie received a huge buildup before its release, but it failed to impress critics.
Her performance lived up to the buildup in the press.
The team benefited from a strong buildup by their public relations manager.

Synset Examples

his letter of recommendation gave her a terrific buildup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promotion - the activity of supporting or actively encouraging something to increase its popularity or sales
  • Synonym: hype - extravagant or intensive publicity or promotion

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɪldʌp / (BrE)
a gradual increase or accumulation of something, especially to a significant or noticeable amount
gradual accumulation or increase

Examples

There was a significant buildup of traffic near the construction site.
Doctors warned that a buildup of cholesterol could lead to health problems.
The buildup to the festival included numerous events and promotions.

Synset Examples

I envied his rapid buildup of assets
a military buildup in preparation for the invasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulation - an amount of something that has built up or been gathered
