monkish

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌŋkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkɪʃ / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of a monk, especially in being austere or inclined to self-denial.
like a monk; self-denying or austere

Examples

He led a monkish existence, devoted to study and prayer.
Her monkish habits included early rising and complete abstinence from luxury.
The writer lived in a monkish retreat, seeing almost no one for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austere - severely simple or strict in manner or appearance
  • Synonym: ascetic - practicing strict self-denial as a measure of personal and especially spiritual discipline

Etymology

Formed from 'monk' + '-ish', meaning like or resembling a monk.
