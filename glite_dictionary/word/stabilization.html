stabilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsteɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsteɪbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something steady, secure, or unlikely to change suddenly
the act of making something stable

Examples

The government introduced stabilization measures to control inflation and support the economy.
Environmental stabilization of the riverbanks prevented further erosion after the heavy storms.
Technological advances have led to the stabilization of power supply in remote areas.

Synset Examples

he worked for price stabilization for farm products
wage stabilization is necessary for industrial peace
stabilization means that the product can be handled under atmospheric conditions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destabilization - the act of making something unstable

Etymology

From stabilize + -ation, modeled on French 'stabilisation' (19th century).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsteɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstəbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something, such as a vessel or aircraft, more stable and less likely to overturn or lose balance
act of making something more stable

Examples

The addition of ballast improved the stabilization of the ship during rough seas.
New technology has enhanced the stabilization systems in modern aircraft.
Effective stabilization is critical to prevent small boats from capsizing in strong winds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destabilization - the process of making something less stable
  • Synonym: stability - the state of being stable and not easily overturned

Etymology

From stabilize + -ation; stabilize from French stabiliser, from Latin stabilis (firm, stable).
