unreal

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈriəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪəl / (BrE)
lacking material form or substance; not existing in physical reality.
not having physical substance

Examples

The shimmering shape on the lake looked completely unreal from a distance.
The magician conjured up an unreal image that amazed the audience.
The colors in the painting were so vibrant they appeared almost unreal.

Synset Examples

as insubstantial as a dream
an insubstantial mirage on the horizon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insubstantial - lacking substance or reality
  • Antonym: real - existing or occurring as fact

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'real' from Latin 'realis' (actual).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00628492-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈriəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪəl / (BrE)
not real; lacking in reality, substance, or genuineness; not corresponding to facts or accepted standards
not real; lacking substance

Examples

The movie created an unreal atmosphere with its special effects and haunting soundtrack.
The story sounded so exaggerated that it seemed almost unreal.
After hearing the verdict, he felt as if everything around him was unreal.

Synset Examples

ghosts and other unreal entities
unreal propaganda serving as news

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring; genuine, not imagined or artificial
  • Synonym: fictitious - not real or true; imaginary or fabricated

Etymology

From un- (not) + real, ultimately from Latin realis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01941146-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈriəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːəl / (BrE)
not real; strange, dreamlike, or seeming imaginary rather than genuine or factual
strange, imagined, or dreamlike; not real

Examples

After he won the lottery, the experience felt completely unreal to him.
The landscape looked so beautiful that it seemed almost unreal in its perfection.
There was an unreal silence following the announcement.

Synset Examples

this conversation is getting more and more unreal
the fantastically unreal world of government bureaucracy
the unreal world of advertising art

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring; not imagined or supposed
  • Synonym: imaginary - existing only in the imagination

Etymology

From un- (not) + real.

Source: WordNet
