Grammatophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræməˈtoʊfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌɡramətəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of large orchids native to southeast Asia and the Pacific, known for their long, narrow leaves and drooping flower clusters that can reach up to 6 feet
genus of large Asian orchids

Examples

The Grammatophyllum orchid can produce enormous flower spikes, sometimes exceeding six feet in length.
Botanists discovered a wild Grammatophyllum growing high in the rainforest canopy.
She specializes in cultivating rare orchids, including the impressive Grammatophyllum species.

Etymology

From Greek 'gramma' (letter) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to markings on the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡræmətoʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌɡræmətəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a small genus of large epiphytic or terrestrial orchids native to southeastern Asia and Polynesia, known for their long, narrow leaves and extremely large drooping flower clusters
a genus of large Southeast Asian and Polynesian orchids

Examples

The Grammatophyllum orchid can produce flower clusters that reach lengths of up to six feet.
Several specimens of Grammatophyllum thrive in the botanical garden's tropical collection.
Horticulturists admire Grammatophyllum for its impressive size and striking blooms.

Etymology

From Greek 'gramma' (letter) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the markings on the leaves.
