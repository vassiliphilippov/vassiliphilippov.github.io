anthophyllite

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈfɪlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌanθəˈfɪlaɪt / (BrE)
a typically brown to greenish-brown mineral of the amphibole group, consisting of magnesium iron silicate
a brown amphibole mineral

Examples

The rocks in this region are rich in anthophyllite, which is used as an indicator of metamorphic processes.
Exposure to anthophyllite asbestos can pose health risks if fibres are inhaled.
A geologist identified the brown-green mineral sample as anthophyllite during the field survey.

Etymology

From Greek anthos (flower) and phyllon (leaf), referring to its leaf-like crystal formations.
