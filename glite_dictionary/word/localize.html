to localize

1. VERB

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to restrict something to a particular area or region; to confine within limits
restrict to a particular area

Examples

The doctors were able to localize the infection and prevent it from spreading.
Firefighters managed to localize the fire to a single building.
Strict measures were taken to localize the contamination in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep something within limits; restrict
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or spread over a wide area

Etymology

From French 'localiser', from 'local' (relating to a place).

2. VERB

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to concentrate or restrict something to a particular place or spot
concentrate in a particular place

Examples

The doctors were relieved when the tumor localized and did not spread further.
During the inspection, the engineers managed to localize the source of the leak.
The pain has localized mainly in her lower back over the past week.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From French localiser, from local (place).
