Alsace

1. PROPN

/ ælˈsæs / (AmE), / ælˈsæs / (BrE)
a region in northeastern France, noted for its white wines and unique blend of French and German cultural influences
region of northeastern France, famous for its wines

Examples

Many travelers visit Alsace to sample its renowned Riesling and Gewürztraminer wines.
The villages of Alsace are known for their charming half-timbered houses and scenic landscapes.
Throughout history, Alsace has shifted between French and German control, enriching its cultural heritage.

Etymology

From French 'Alsace', possibly from Germanic origins via Old High German 'Ali-saz' meaning 'foreign settlement'.
