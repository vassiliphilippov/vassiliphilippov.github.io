Osteostraci

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑstioʊˈstræsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəˈstrɑːsaɪ / (BrE)
an extinct group of armored jawless vertebrates known from the Silurian and Devonian periods, with uncertain taxonomic classification
extinct group of armored jawless vertebrates

Examples

Fossils of the Osteostraci reveal the intricate armor plating that protected these ancient creatures.
Scientists believe that Osteostraci played a crucial role in the evolution of early vertebrates.
The discovery of Osteostraci fossils provided new insights into the diversity of Paleozoic marine life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jawless fish - fish lacking jaws, such as lampreys and hagfish

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) and ostrakon (shell), reflecting their bony armored head shields.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑsti.oʊˈstræsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒsti.əˈstrɑːsaɪ / (BrE)
an extinct taxonomic suborder of armored, jawless fish-like vertebrates from the Paleozoic era; taxonomy is not clearly established
extinct armored jawless fish suborder

Examples

Fossils of Osteostraci provide important insights into the early evolution of vertebrates.
Researchers have debated the precise classification of Osteostraci within jawless fish.
The distinctive armored head shields of Osteostraci set them apart from other Paleozoic fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cephalaspida - another group of extinct jawless fish, closely related

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + ostrakon (shell), referring to their bony armored head shields.
