Wurzburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈvʊrts.bɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈvʊəts.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
A city in south central Germany, known for its baroque and rococo architecture, located on the Main River.
a city in south central Germany

Examples

Many tourists visit Wurzburg to see its renowned Residenz palace.
The Main River runs through the historic city of Wurzburg.
She studied wine production in Wurzburg, a region famous for its vineyards.

Etymology

From German 'Würzburg', first attested in Old High German as 'Virteburch'.
