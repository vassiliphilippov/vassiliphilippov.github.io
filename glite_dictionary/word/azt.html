AZT

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ zi ˈti / (AmE), / ˌeɪ zɛd ˈtiː / (BrE)
an antiviral drug, also known as zidovudine, used in the treatment of HIV/AIDS
antiviral drug for HIV/AIDS
medical

Examples

After his diagnosis, the doctor prescribed AZT to help manage the viral infection.
The use of AZT significantly improved the prognosis for many patients with HIV in the 1980s.
Researchers continue to study the long-term effects of AZT therapy on liver and bone marrow health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zidovudine - antiviral drug also known as AZT, used to treat HIV infection
  • Degree Higher: antiviral - a drug that reduces the effects or reproduction of viruses

Etymology

Acronym of azidothymidine, based on its chemical structure.
