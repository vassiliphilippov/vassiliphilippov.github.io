Echeneis naucrates

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈkiːnɪs nɔːˈkreɪtiːz / (AmE), / ɛˈkiːnɪɪs nɔːˈkreɪtiːz / (BrE)
a species of remora, a fish often found attached to sharks and other large marine animals
species of remora fish

Examples

The Echeneis naucrates can often be seen riding alongside sharks in tropical seas.
Marine biologists studied the symbiotic relationship between Echeneis naucrates and its host animals.
Fishermen in some cultures consider the Echeneis naucrates a sign of good luck when spotted near their boats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a fish known for attaching itself to larger sea animals

Etymology

From Latin *echeneis* (a kind of fish that attaches to ships) and Greek *naukratēs* (ship-holder).
