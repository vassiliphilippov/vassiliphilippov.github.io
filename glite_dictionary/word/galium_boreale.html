Galium boreale

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪ.li.əm bɔːˈriː.æl.i / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.li.əm bɔːˈriː.ɑː.li / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant of the bedstraw family, with small white flowers, sometimes grown as an ornamental.
a perennial plant with white flowers

Examples

Galium boreale thrives in moist meadows and open woods across North America.
Gardeners often choose Galium boreale for its delicate, star-shaped white flowers.
The scientific name Galium boreale refers to northern bedstraw, a member of the Rubiaceae family.

Etymology

From Latin: 'galium' (bedstraw genus); 'boreale' meaning 'northern'.
