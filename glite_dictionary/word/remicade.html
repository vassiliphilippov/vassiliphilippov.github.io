Remicade

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛməˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛmɪˌkeɪd / (BrE)
A proprietary drug containing infliximab, used to treat autoimmune diseases such as Crohn's disease and rheumatoid arthritis by inhibiting tumor necrosis factor (TNF).
a drug for autoimmune diseases (infliximab)
medical

Examples

After several failed medications, her doctor prescribed Remicade to manage her Crohn's disease symptoms.
Many patients receive Remicade infusions at a clinic every few months.
The effectiveness of Remicade in treating rheumatoid arthritis has been demonstrated in multiple studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infliximab - the generic form of Remicade, an anti-TNF monoclonal antibody
