to allow

1. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ / (BrE)
to deduct or give a specified sum as a discount or trade-in value, especially when accepting something in exchange
deduct as a discount or trade-in

Examples

The shop allowed me $100 on my old phone when I traded it in.
They allowed a 10% discount for early payment of the invoice.
The dealership allowed us a generous sum for our old car as part of the new purchase.

Synset Examples

The camera store owner allowed me $50 on my old camera
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02324649-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ / (BrE)
to make it possible for something to happen, especially as a result of not preventing it
make possible; not prevent

Examples

The new software will allow users to access their files remotely.
Leaving the gates open could allow animals to escape from the enclosure.
Wearing a raincoat allowed her to walk in the storm without getting wet.

Synset Examples

This permits the water to rush in
This sealed door won't allow the water to come into the basement
This will permit the rain to run off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - to make something possible or give permission for something
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening

Etymology

From Old French 'alouer', from Latin 'adlaudare' meaning 'to praise', later influenced by Latin 'allocare' meaning 'to assign'.

3. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ / (BrE)

Examples
