anergy

1. NOUN

/ ˈænərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈænəːdʒi / (BrE)
a condition in which the body shows a reduced or absent immune response to a particular antigen or stimulus
reduced or absent immune response
medical

Examples

The patient's anergy made it difficult to diagnose tuberculosis using the skin test.
Researchers are exploring how anergy contributes to chronic infections and autoimmune diseases.
A lack of immune reaction in the test was attributed to anergy, not the absence of the disease.

Etymology

From Greek anergos (inactive), from an- (without) + ergon (work, activity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈænɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈænəːdʒi / (BrE)
A state of inactivity or lack of energy, especially in a physiological or medical context.
lack of energy; physiological inactivity
medical

Examples

The patient's anergy made it difficult for her to participate in everyday activities.
Scientists studied the effects of chronic illness on immune anergy in the test subjects.
After weeks of stress, he felt a deep sense of anergy and struggled to focus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or concern
  • Synonym: lethargy - a state of sluggishness or laziness
  • Antonym: energy - the capacity for vigorous activity

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + ergon (work, activity).
