permit

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmɪt / (AmE), / pəˈmɪt / (BrE)
an official document giving someone authorization to do something
official authorization document

Examples

You need a permit to camp in the national park.
She applied for a building permit before starting construction.
Without a parking permit, you may get a ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - an official permission to do something, often driving or operating something
  • Antonym: to prohibit - to formally forbid by law, rule, or other authority

Etymology

From Latin 'permittere' (to allow), via Old French 'permettre'.

2. VERB

/ pərˈmɪt / (AmE), / pəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allow someone to do something or to allow something to happen, especially by not preventing it
allow; give permission

Examples

No food or drink is permitted inside the lecture hall.
My parents will not permit me to stay out late on weekdays.
The city recently permitted music events in public spaces.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allow - to let someone do something or let something happen
  • Antonym: to forbid - to order someone not to do something; to refuse to allow

Etymology

From Latin 'permittere' (to let go through, allow), from 'per-' (through) + 'mittere' (to send).
