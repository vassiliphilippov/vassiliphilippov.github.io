Sir Barton

1. PROPN

/ sɜr ˈbɑrtən / (AmE), / sɜː ˈbɑːtən / (BrE)
the thoroughbred racehorse that won the American Triple Crown in 1919, becoming the first horse to do so.
first horse to win the American Triple Crown (1919)

Examples

Sir Barton became the first official winner of the American Triple Crown in 1919.
Many racing enthusiasts remember Sir Barton as a trailblazer in horse racing history.
The legacy of Sir Barton continues to inspire trainers and jockeys today.
