benzyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn.zɪl / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.zɪl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical radical (C6H5CH2–) derived from toluene, often found in organic compounds
chemical radical from toluene
medical

Examples

The benzyl group can be used to protect alcohols during chemical synthesis.
A benzyl chloride reaction is common in organic chemistry laboratories.
The compound contains a benzyl radical attached to the nitrogen atom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenyl - the univalent radical C6H5–, derived from benzene

Etymology

From benzoin (from German Benzoin) + -yl, a standard chemical suffix.
