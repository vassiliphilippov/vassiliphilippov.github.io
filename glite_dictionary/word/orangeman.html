Orangeman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪndʒˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒmən / (BrE)
a member of a Protestant fraternal society founded in Ireland in 1795 to uphold Protestantism and allegiance to the British crown
member of Protestant society in Ireland

Examples

The Orangemen organized a parade to celebrate their heritage.
As an Orangeman, he participated in annual demonstrations.
The history of the Orangeman is closely linked to Irish religious and political conflicts.

Etymology

Named after William of Orange, Protestant king of England, Scotland, and Ireland.
