knob

1. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
an ornament, typically ball-shaped, on the hilt of a sword or dagger.
ball-shaped ornament on sword hilt

Examples

The intricate knob at the end of the dagger's hilt caught the candlelight.
He commissioned a sword with a golden knob encrusted with gemstones.
Collectors often examine the craftsmanship of the knob when assessing antique weapons.

2. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
a rounded lump or protuberance, especially one that is thickened or enlarged
a rounded lump or protuberance

Examples

A strange knob had formed on the tree branch after the injury.
Some fungi have a knob at the tip of their stalks.
The end of the bone developed a small knob due to repeated stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bump - a rounded swelling or protuberance on a surface
  • Degree Lower: knoblet - a small knob
  • Synonym: nodule - a small swelling or aggregation of cells

Etymology

From Middle English knobbe (lump, knot), possibly of Low German or Dutch origin.

3. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
a rounded handle or control switch that is turned to operate something
a round handle or control switch

Examples

She turned the knob on the door and entered the room quietly.
Please adjust the volume by twisting the knob on the radio.
The dresser had shiny brass knobs on all its drawers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - part of an object designed to be held or operated by hand
  • Antonym: lever - a rigid bar used to operate or control something

Etymology

From Middle English knobbe (lump, knob), probably from a Low German or Dutch source.

4. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
a rounded handle or a small, usually circular, projection that sticks out from a surface, often used for opening, adjusting, or holding something
a rounded handle or projection

Examples

She turned the knob to open the door quietly.
The old radio had a large volume knob in the center.
There was a brass knob on the top drawer of the cabinet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - a part by which something is held or operated
  • Degree Lower: button - a small device pressed to operate something

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *knobbe* (lump, knob).
