unsaturated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈsætʃərətɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution) capable of dissolving more of a substance at a given temperature; not saturated.
able to dissolve more solute

Examples

The chemist prepared an unsaturated salt solution for the experiment.
If more sugar can be dissolved, the solution is still unsaturated.
Heating the water helps maintain the unsaturated state while adding more solute.

Synset Examples

an unsaturated salt solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saturated - (of a solution) unable to dissolve more of a substance at a given temperature

Etymology

From Latin prefix un- (not) + saturated, from Latin saturatus (full, saturated).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a color) not pure in hue; diluted or mixed with white, grey, or black, making it less vivid.
not pure in color; diluted

Examples

The designer preferred using unsaturated colors for a softer look in the living room.
This painting features both intense and unsaturated shades to create visual contrast.
When too much white is added, the red becomes unsaturated and muted.

Synset Examples

an unsaturated red

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saturated - (of a color) pure and intense; not diluted

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'saturated' (from Latin saturare 'to fill, saturate').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00394913-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound, especially one containing carbon) having atoms that share more than one double or triple bond, not fully saturated with hydrogen.
not fully saturated; contains double or triple bonds (chemistry)

Examples

Avocados are a good source of unsaturated fats, which are considered healthier than saturated fats.
Many vegetable oils are rich in unsaturated compounds, making them liquid at room temperature.
Chemists study unsaturated hydrocarbons like alkenes and alkynes for their reactivity.

Synset Examples

unsaturated fats

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saturated - containing the greatest possible number of hydrogen atoms, no double or triple bonds

Etymology

From Latin 'saturatus' (saturated) with prefix 'un-' (not).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00760953-a
Synset POS: a
