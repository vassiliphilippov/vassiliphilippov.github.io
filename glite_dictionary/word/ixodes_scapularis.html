Ixodes scapularis

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈksoʊdiːz skæpˈjʊlərɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz skæpˈjʊlərɪs / (BrE)
a species of tick, also known as the black-legged tick or deer tick, that transmits Lyme disease, especially in eastern North America
species of tick, Lyme disease vector
medical

Examples

Researchers found that the Ixodes scapularis tick was responsible for most Lyme disease cases in the region.
Preventing bites from Ixodes scapularis is an important part of outdoor safety in the northeastern United States.
The lifecycle of Ixodes scapularis involves several hosts, including small mammals and deer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer tick - common name for Ixodes scapularis

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Ixodes', meaning 'tick', and species name 'scapularis', meaning 'of the shoulder'.
