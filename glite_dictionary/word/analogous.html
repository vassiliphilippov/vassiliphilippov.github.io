analogous

1. ADJ

/ əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
similar or comparable in certain respects, typically in a way that makes clearer the nature of the things compared, though otherwise different.
similar in some respects; comparable

Examples

The structure of a whale’s fin and a human hand are analogous in their bone arrangement.
His explanation used an analogous situation to help clarify the point.
Internet search engines are analogous to libraries in providing access to information.

Synset Examples

brains and computers are often considered analogous
salmon roe is marketed as analogous to caviar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparable - similar in some aspects; able to be likened to another
  • Antonym: different - not the same as another; dissimilar

Etymology

From Latin analogus, from Greek analogos (proportionate).

2. ADJ

/ əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
(especially in biology) having similar function but a different evolutionary origin or structure.
similar in function but different in origin

Examples

In biology, the fins of a fish and the flippers of a whale are considered analogous structures.
The engineer explained that these two mechanisms are analogous in how they solve similar problems.
The wings of bats and the wings of insects are analogous, not homologous.

Synset Examples

the wings of a bee and those of a hummingbird are analogous

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homologous - having the same evolutionary origin but a different or similar function

Etymology

From Greek 'analogos' meaning 'proportionate'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01223961-a
Synset POS: a
