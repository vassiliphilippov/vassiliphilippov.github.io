Nero

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnɪərəʊ / (BrE)
(AD 37–68) The fifth Roman Emperor, notorious for his extravagance and cruelty, who reigned from 54 to 68 and is often associated with the Great Fire of Rome.
Roman Emperor known for extravagance and cruelty

Examples

The reign of Nero is infamous for its excess and alleged persecution of Christians.
Some historians debate whether Nero really started the Great Fire of Rome.
In popular culture, Nero is often portrayed as a symbol of tyranny and decadence.

Etymology

From Latin Nero, the emperor's personal name.
