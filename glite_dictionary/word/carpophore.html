carpophore

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.pəˌfɔr / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɒ.fɔː / (BrE)
(Botany) A slender stalk that supports or acts as an axis for a carpel or fruit segment in certain plants.
slender stalk supporting a carpel (botany)
medical

Examples

The carpophore is visible between the two seed halves of the plant.
Without a well-developed carpophore, the fruit may not disperse properly.
Botanists examined the carpophore to identify the species of the herbarium specimen.

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + phorein (to bear).
