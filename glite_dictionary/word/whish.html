to whish

1. VERB

/ wɪʃ / (AmE), / wɪʃ / (BrE)
to move or pass by quickly, making a soft, sweeping or rushing sound
move quickly with a soft rush sound

Examples

The train whished through the tunnel, leaving only a faint echo.
A flock of birds whished overhead as we walked by the river.
The arrow whished past the target, missing it by inches.

Synset Examples

The car whished past her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - move quickly with a rushing sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01838302-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɪʃ / (AmE), / wɪʃ / (BrE)
to make or cause a soft, swift, sibilant sound, like that of something moving quickly through the air
make a sibilant or rushing sound

Examples

The arrow whished past my ear, barely missing me.
A gust of wind whished through the trees at night, stirring the branches.
She listened as the silk curtain whished softly against the window frame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - to move or cause to move with a rushing sound
  • Synonym: hiss - to make a sharp sibilant sound like that of the letter 's'

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, similar to 'whoosh' and 'whiz'.
