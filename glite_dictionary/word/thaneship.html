thaneship

1. NOUN

/ ˈθeɪnˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈθeɪnʃɪp / (BrE)
the rank, status, or office of a thane in ancient Scotland or England
the rank or position of a thane
archaic

Examples

His promotion to thaneship brought newfound respect among his peers and neighbors.
The responsibilities that accompanied thaneship were significant in the medieval Scottish court.
Many legends recount the bravery required to attain thaneship in ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thane - a man, often a noble, holding land from a king or lord in Scotland or England

Etymology

Formed from 'thane' (Old English 'þegn', servant, noble) + '-ship' (denoting state or condition).
