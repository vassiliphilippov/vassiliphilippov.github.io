patronisingly

1. ADV

/ ˌpætɹəˈnaɪzɪŋli / (AmE), / ˌpatɹəˈnaɪzɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that shows that you think you are more intelligent or important than someone else; in a condescending manner.
in a condescending way

Examples

He spoke patronisingly to the interns, assuming they knew little about the project.
She smiled patronisingly, as if amused by his lack of experience.
The manager addressed the staff patronisingly, which irritated many of them.

Synset Examples

he treats his secretary condescendingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condescendingly - in a condescending or superior manner

Etymology

From 'patronise' + '-ing' + '-ly'. 'Patronise' from Latin 'patronus' (protector, master).
