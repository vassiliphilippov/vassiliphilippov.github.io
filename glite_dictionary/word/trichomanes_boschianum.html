Trichomanes boschianum

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkoʊˈmeɪniːz bɒsˈkiːənəm / (AmE), / trɪˌkəʊˈmɑːniːz bɒsˈkiːənəm / (BrE)
A species of bristle fern found in moist, shaded habitats, known for its delicate, translucent fronds.
species of bristle fern

Examples

Trichomanes boschianum thrives in damp, well-shaded rock crevices of temperate forests.
The delicate fronds of Trichomanes boschianum make it a favorite among fern enthusiasts.
Researchers discovered a rare population of Trichomanes boschianum during their botanical survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristle fern - type of delicate, hair-like fern

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) + 'manes' (madness), genus name; 'boschianum' honors botanist Roelof Benjamin van den Bosch.
