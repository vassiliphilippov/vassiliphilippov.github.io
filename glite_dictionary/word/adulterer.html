adulterer

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltɚɚ / (AmE), / əˈdʌltərə / (BrE)
a person who commits adultery; a married person who has a sexual relationship with someone other than their spouse
person who commits adultery

Examples

The secret finally came out, and her husband was revealed as an adulterer.
Throughout history, societies have punished the adulterer for breaking marital vows.
He was labeled an adulterer by the press after his extramarital affair became public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulteress - a woman who commits adultery
  • Antonym: faithful - remaining loyal to a spouse or partner

Etymology

From Latin adulterare (to corrupt or commit adultery) via Old French adultérer.
