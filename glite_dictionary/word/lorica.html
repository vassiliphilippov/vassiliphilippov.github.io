lorica

1. NOUN

/ lɔːˈriːkə / (AmE), / lɒˈriːkə / (BrE)
a hard protective covering secreted by certain protoctists and other microorganisms
protective shell of some microorganisms

Examples

The ciliate organism forms a lorica that shields it from predators in its aquatic environment.
Scientists observed the lorica under a microscope to study its structural properties.
Some protoctists build their loricae out of sand grains and organic material.

Etymology

From Latin 'lorica' (body armor, cuirass).
