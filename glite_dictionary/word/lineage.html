lineage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniɪdʒ / (BrE)
the line of descent or ancestry of a person, family, or group
direct line of descent or ancestry

Examples

She was proud of her noble lineage and family history.
Scientists studied the genetic lineage of the ancient population.
Many people research their lineage to learn more about their ancestors.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - family or ancestral descent
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Old French 'ligne', from Latin 'linea' (line, thread, lineage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪɪdʒ / (BrE)
the inherited characteristics or ancestry shared by members of the same family or bloodline
family ancestry or inherited traits

Examples

Many noble families proudly trace their lineage back to medieval times.
The scientist studied the lineage of the endangered species to understand its genetic traits.
He was interested in discovering more about his family's lineage and heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪɪdʒ / (BrE)
the line of descent or ancestry of a person or group; the kinship relation between an individual and their ancestors
ancestry; line of descent

Examples

Her family's lineage can be traced back to the early settlers of the region.
The prince took great pride in his royal lineage and noble heritage.
Genealogists study lineage to uncover family histories and connections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ancestral descent
  • Antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Old French *lignage*, from Latin *linea* (line).
