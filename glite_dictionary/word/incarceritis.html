incarceritis

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɑrsərˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɑːsəˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
a feeling of restlessness or irritability caused by being confined for a long period
restlessness from being confined
informal

Examples

After a week of quarantine, I started showing signs of incarceritis.
The children had a severe case of incarceritis after three days stuck indoors during the storm.
Too much time in the office gave him a bout of incarceritis.

Synset Examples

After weeks in lockdown, everyone felt a touch of incarceritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabin fever - restlessness or irritability from being stuck indoors or isolated

Etymology

Formed from 'incarcerate' (to imprison) and '-itis' (denoting a condition or disease).
