deputation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of people appointed to represent others, often for a specific purpose or to deliver a message.
group of appointed representatives

Examples

A deputation of students met with the principal to discuss the new school policy.
The mayor received a deputation from the local business community seeking support for the market.
A deputation visited Parliament to present their concerns about environmental regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - a group of representatives with similar duties

Etymology

From Medieval Latin deputatio (appointment of a deputy), from Latin deputare (to depute).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of authorizing someone, especially a subordinate, to act or make decisions on one's behalf
act of assigning authority to another

Examples

The manager's deputation of tasks allowed her team to operate more independently.
By formal deputation, the committee chair empowered members to approve routine requests.
Without proper deputation, subordinates lacked the authority to implement important changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - the act of giving someone else responsibility or authority to perform a task

Etymology

From Late Latin deputatio (appointment, delegation), from Latin deputare (to assign, appoint).
