diatom

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.əˌtɑm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.tɒm / (BrE)
a microscopic single-celled alga found in marine or freshwater environments, with a silica-rich cell wall and often forming colonies
microscopic unicellular alga with silica cell wall

Examples

Diatom populations are indicators of water quality in many lakes and rivers.
Scientists examined the intricate patterns in the shells of each diatom under the microscope.
Many toothpaste brands use powdered diatom shells as a mild abrasive.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alga - a simple non-flowering plant, such as seaweed or pond scum
  • Synonym: phytoplankton - microscopic plant-like organisms that float in water

Etymology

From Greek diatomos (cut through), from dia- (through) and temnein (to cut).
