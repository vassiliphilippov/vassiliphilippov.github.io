globally

1. ADV

/ ˈɡloʊ.bə.li / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊ.bəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or affects the whole world; worldwide.
worldwide; relating to the whole world

Examples

Climate change is a problem that must be addressed globally, not just locally.
The brand has expanded its presence globally over the past decade.
Decisions made here will have consequences globally as well as nationally.

Synset Examples

She is internationally known
this is globally significant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worldwide - existing or happening throughout the world
  • Antonym: locally - in a particular area or region, not worldwide

Etymology

From the word 'global' (relating to the whole world) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈɡloʊbəli / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or affects the whole world.
involving the whole world

Examples

We need to consider how our decisions will impact people globally as well as locally.
The company aims to expand globally over the next five years.
Issues like climate change must be addressed globally rather than individually.

Synset Examples

think globally, not locally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: locally - in a way that relates to a specific place or area

Etymology

From 'global' + '-ly', with 'global' from Latin 'globus' (sphere, globe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00113197-r
Synset POS: r
