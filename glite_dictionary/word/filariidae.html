Filariidae

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlɛriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / fɪˌlærɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of slender, threadlike parasitic roundworms, some of which can cause diseases in humans and animals.
family of threadlike parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

Several diseases in tropical regions are caused by worms belonging to the Filariidae family.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Filariidae to find better treatments for filariasis.
The threadlike appearance makes members of the Filariidae distinct among parasitic worms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filaria - A type of parasitic roundworm in the Filariidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'filaria' (threadlike worm) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological nomenclature).
