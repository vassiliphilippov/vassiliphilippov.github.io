wicket-keeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkət ˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪt ˌkiːpə / (BrE)
(in cricket) a player who stands behind the wicket to catch balls missed by the batter and to attempt to dismiss batters.
player behind wicket in cricket

Examples

The wicket-keeper made an incredible dive to take the catch and dismiss the batsman.
She was appointed as the team's wicket-keeper due to her quick reflexes and sharp eyesight.
A skilled wicket-keeper can change the course of a match with effective stumpings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catcher - player who catches balls behind the batter (in baseball)

Etymology

From 'wicket' (the cricket stumps) + 'keeper' (one who keeps or guards).
