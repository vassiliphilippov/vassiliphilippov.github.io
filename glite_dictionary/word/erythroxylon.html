Erythroxylon

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɑkˈsaɪlən / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɒkˈsɪlən / (BrE)
A genus of South American shrubs and small trees in the family Erythroxylaceae, including species such as the coca plant.
genus of South American shrubs and trees
medical

Examples

The genus Erythroxylon includes several species commonly found in South America.
Researchers have studied Erythroxylon for its medicinal properties.
The coca plant, known scientifically as Erythroxylon coca, is the source of cocaine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coca - plant from the genus Erythroxylon, source of cocaine

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) and 'xylon' (wood).
