wattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
A framework made of interwoven sticks or branches, used especially for fencing or building walls.
interwoven stick or branch framework

Examples

The farmers constructed a wattle fence to keep the sheep inside the pasture.
Traditional houses in the area often use wattle and daub walls for insulation.
She admired the skill it took to weave a sturdy wattle from thin branches.

Etymology

From Old English *watol*, related to weaving and binding.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈwɒt.əl / (BrE)
a fleshy, wrinkled, and often brightly colored fold of skin that hangs from the neck or throat of certain birds (such as chickens and turkeys) or some lizards
fleshy skin fold on bird's or lizard's neck

Examples

The rooster's wattle grew more vibrant as mating season approached.
Turkeys have a noticeable red wattle hanging beneath their beaks.
Some lizards also display a loose, hanging wattle as part of their throat anatomy.

Etymology

From Old English *watel* (band, wrap, hurdle), though sense of skin fold developed later.

3. VERB

/ ˈwɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
to weave or interlace sticks, twigs, or branches to form a structure, such as a fence or wall
interlace sticks to form a structure

Examples

The early settlers would wattle branches together to create simple fences.
They spent the afternoon wattling hazel rods into a sturdy garden divider.
The craftsmen wattled the walls before applying a layer of mud to complete the hut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weave - to make by interlacing threads or materials together

4. VERB

/ ˈwɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
to build or make (a structure) using wattle (interwoven rods and twigs)
build using interwoven rods and twigs

Examples

In traditional building, they would wattle the walls before applying mud plaster.
The villagers wattled simple fences to keep animals out of the garden.
Historically, craftsmen wattled lightweight partitions from flexible branches.

Etymology

From Old English *watel* meaning 'hurdle, wall of interwoven rods', later used as a verb.

5. NOUN

/ ˈwɑt.əl / (AmE), / ˈwɒt.əl / (BrE)
Any of various Australasian trees, especially acacias, that produce slender stems or branches used for making fences, shelters, or similar structures.
Australasian tree yielding slender branches

Examples

The farmer cut several wattle poles to repair the old stockyard fence.
Many traditional Australian shelters were constructed using local wattle trees.
Some wattle species have become important in land restoration projects due to their fast growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acacia - genus of trees and shrubs often called wattles; many yield suitable poles

Etymology

From Old English *watol* (bandage, wrap).
