prothorax

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / prəʊˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
the front section of an insect's thorax, which supports the first pair of legs
front part of an insect's thorax
medical

Examples

The prothorax of the beetle is often covered by a hard shield.
Damage to the prothorax can affect the insect's ability to walk.
Scientists observed distinctive markings on the prothorax to identify the species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thorax - the chest or midsection of an insect's body

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'pro-' (before) and Greek 'thōrax' (breastplate, chest).
