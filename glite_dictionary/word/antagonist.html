antagonist

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənɪst / (BrE)
a muscle that opposes the action of another muscle by relaxing when the other contracts
muscle that opposes another's action
medical

Examples

When you straighten your arm, the triceps act as the antagonist to the biceps.
In physical therapy, understanding which muscle is the antagonist can help prevent injury.
During a biceps curl, the triceps muscle serves as the antagonist.

Synset Examples

when bending the elbow the triceps are the antagonist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agonist - a muscle that contracts to cause a movement

Etymology

From Greek antagonistes (opponent), from anti- (against) + agonizesthai (to contend).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənɪst / (BrE)
a substance that blocks or reduces the physiological effect of another substance, especially a drug
a drug that counteracts another drug
medical

Examples

Naloxone is an antagonist that can reverse the effects of opioid overdose in minutes.
The doctor prescribed a beta antagonist to help control her heart rate.
Certain antihistamines act as an antagonist by blocking allergic reactions in the body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agonist - a drug or substance that stimulates a physiological response by binding to a receptor

Etymology

From Greek antagōnistēs (opponent), from antagōnizesthai (to struggle against).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02717794-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ænˈtæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənɪst / (BrE)
a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something; an adversary
someone who opposes or competes with another

Examples

In the novel, the antagonist tries to thwart the hero's efforts at every turn.
During the debate, she was a fierce antagonist who challenged every argument.
The antagonist in the film is just as compelling as the protagonist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protagonist - the main character or leading figure, especially in a literary work
  • Synonym: opponent - someone who competes against or fights another

Etymology

From Greek antagōnistēs, from antagōnizesthai 'to contend against'.
