laissez faire

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛ.seɪ ˈfɛr / (AmE), / ˌlæ.seɪ ˈfɛə / (BrE)
the principle that the government should allow commercial and business activities to operate without interference or regulation
economic policy of minimal government intervention

Examples

Many economists argue that laissez faire policies can foster innovation and economic growth.
The period was marked by a laissez faire approach, with little government regulation of industry.
Critics of laissez faire believe some oversight is necessary to prevent abuses and protect workers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interventionism - policy of government intervention in economic affairs

Etymology

From French 'laissez faire', meaning 'let do', reflecting the idea of letting people act freely.
