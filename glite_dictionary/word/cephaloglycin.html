cephaloglycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛf.ə.loʊˈɡlaɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɛf.ə.ləʊˈɡlɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic related to cephalosporins, formerly used to treat bacterial infections but now largely discontinued
antibiotic related to cephalosporins
medical

Examples

Cephaloglycin was prescribed to treat specific infections before being replaced by newer antibiotics.
Some older medical texts mention cephaloglycin as an option for bacterial respiratory infections.
Resistance to cephaloglycin contributed to its decline in clinical use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a class of broad-spectrum antibiotics

Etymology

From 'cephalo-' (relating to cephalosporins) and 'glycin', referring to its chemical structure.
