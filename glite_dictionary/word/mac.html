mac

1. NOUN

/ mæk / (AmE), / mæk / (BrE)
a waterproof raincoat, typically made of rubberized fabric
waterproof raincoat
british

Examples

She put on her mac before heading out into the pouring rain.
A yellow mac hung by the door in case of sudden showers.
The tourists bought cheap macs from a street vendor during the unexpected downpour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackintosh - a waterproof raincoat, especially made of rubberized fabric

Etymology

Short for 'mackintosh', from Charles Macintosh (1766–1843), Scottish chemist and inventor of waterproof fabric.
