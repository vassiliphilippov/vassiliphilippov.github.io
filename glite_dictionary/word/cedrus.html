Cedrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛdrəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːdrəs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the pine family, known as true cedars, native to the mountains of the western Himalayas and the Mediterranean region.
genus of true cedars (trees)

Examples

The Cedrus species are prized for their durable, fragrant wood.
Botanists studied how Cedrus adapts to various mountain climates.
The ancient forests contain several mature Cedrus trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - any of several coniferous trees of the genus Cedrus or related species

Etymology

From Latin 'cedrus', from Ancient Greek 'kedros' (cedar tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːdrəs / (BrE)
a genus of coniferous trees in the pine family Pinaceae, commonly known as true cedars, native to mountainous regions of the western Himalayas and the Mediterranean.
genus of true cedar trees

Examples

The ancient forests contained several magnificent Cedrus trees towering above the landscape.
Researchers studied the disease resistance of Cedrus species in Mediterranean climates.
The Cedrus genus is prized for its aromatic wood and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar - a common name for trees of the genus Cedrus and some unrelated species

Etymology

From Latin Cedrus, from ancient Greek kēdrós, meaning cedar tree.
