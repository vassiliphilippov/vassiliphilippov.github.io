Gentianales

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnʃiəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnʃɪəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a large order of dicotyledonous plants characterized by flowers with fused petals, including families such as Gentianaceae and Apocynaceae
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Gentianales order includes many tropical species with medicinal properties.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity within the Gentianales to better understand plant evolution.
The family Apocynaceae, belonging to the Gentianales, contains several ornamental flowering plants.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the family name Gentianaceae, ultimately from Gentiana (the gentian genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃiəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃɪəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a botanical order of dicotyledonous plants characterized by gamopetalous flowers, including families such as Gentianaceae, Apocynaceae, and Oleaceae.
an order of flowering plants

Examples

Many medicinal plants belong to the Gentianales order, particularly those in the Apocynaceae family.
Botanists often study the diversity of floral structures within the Gentianales.
The Gentianales are recognized for their distinctive fused petals and opposite leaves.

Etymology

From the genus name Gentiana, type genus of the family Gentianaceae, with the taxonomic suffix -ales.
