watercress

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔtərˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəkrɛs / (BrE)
Of a moderate yellow-green color that is greener and deeper than moss green and yellower and darker than pea green.
a moderate yellow-green color

Examples

The designer chose a watercress shade for the walls to create a calming effect.
Her scarf was dyed a rich watercress color, complementing her green eyes.
Painters often mix their own watercress hue for vibrant landscape scenes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌkrɛs / (BrE)
a small, edible plant with pungent leaves that grows in clear, running water and is often used in salads or as a garnish
edible aquatic plant used in salads

Examples

She added fresh watercress to the salad for a peppery flavor.
Wild watercress can often be found growing along the banks of streams.
Soup made from watercress is a traditional dish in many European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - a small, edible herb with pungent leaves

Etymology

From Old English 'wætercærse', combining 'water' and 'cress'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəkrɛs / (BrE)
a small, pungent-leaved aquatic plant, often used in salads, that grows in running water and belongs to the cress family
edible aquatic plant used in salads

Examples

She added fresh watercress to the salad for an extra peppery flavor.
Watercress grows quickly in clean streams and is often harvested in the wild.
A sandwich made with cream cheese and watercress is a popular teatime snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - a plant with edible peppery-tasting leaves

Etymology

From Middle English 'watercresse', from Old English 'wætercærse' (water + cress).
