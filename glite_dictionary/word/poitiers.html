Poitiers

1. PROPN

/ pwɑːˈtjeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpwɑːtjeɪ / (BrE)
the battle in 1356 during the Hundred Years' War in which the English, led by the Black Prince, defeated the French
battle in 1356, English defeated French

Examples

The Battle of Poitiers was a turning point in the Hundred Years' War.
Many historians study Poitiers to understand medieval warfare tactics.
After Poitiers, English influence in France grew significantly.

Etymology

From the name of the city Poitiers, in western France, where the battle took place.
