moistly

1. ADV

/ ˈmɔɪstli / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪstli / (BrE)
In a slightly wet or damp manner.
in a slightly wet manner

Examples

He spoke so close that she could feel his breath moistly on her cheek.
The leaves glistened moistly after the early morning rain.
The towels were folded moistly, still not quite dry from the wash.

Synset Examples

a scarf was tied round her head but the rebellious curl had escaped and hung damply over her left eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damply - in a damp or slightly wet manner

Etymology

From 'moist' + '-ly', forming the adverb.
