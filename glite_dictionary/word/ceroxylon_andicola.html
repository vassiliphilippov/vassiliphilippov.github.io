Ceroxylon andicola

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrɒksɪˌɒn ændɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / sɛˌrɒksɪˌɒn ændɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
a species of tall palm native to the Andes, known for producing a resinous wax that is mixed with tallow to make candles
Andean palm yielding wax for candles

Examples

The Ceroxylon andicola can reach impressive heights in its native Andean forests.
Local communities historically harvested wax from the Ceroxylon andicola to make candles.
Scientists study the Ceroxylon andicola for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: *keros* (wax) + *xylon* (wood); "andicola" from Latin for 'dweller of the Andes.'
