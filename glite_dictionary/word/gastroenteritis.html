gastroenteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˌɛntərˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˌentəˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the stomach and intestines, typically resulting in vomiting and diarrhea.
inflammation of stomach and intestines
medical

Examples

Her gastroenteritis was caused by a viral infection, leading to severe dehydration.
Many travelers take precautions to avoid gastroenteritis when visiting new countries.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with gastroenteritis after reviewing his symptoms of vomiting and diarrhea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomach flu - common informal term for gastroenteritis

Etymology

From Greek 'gaster' (stomach) + 'enteron' (intestine) + '-itis' (inflammation).
