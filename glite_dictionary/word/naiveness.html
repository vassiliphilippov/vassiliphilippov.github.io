naiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking experience, sophistication, or worldliness; innocence or simplicity in outlook or behavior
lack of sophistication or worldliness

Examples

Her naiveness made her vulnerable to scams during her first few months in the city.
The movie explores the naiveness of youth and the lessons learned from hardship.
Despite his naiveness, he approached every new challenge with enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naivety - innocence or lack of experience; alternate noun form

Etymology

From naive + -ness, modeled after French *naïveté*.
