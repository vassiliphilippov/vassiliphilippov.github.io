healer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlər / (AmE), / ˈhiːlə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at curing or treating physical or mental illnesses, often using a particular method or tradition
person skilled at curing illness

Examples

She visited a traditional healer for her chronic back pain.
Many people believe that a skilled healer can address both the body and the spirit.
Modern medicine and natural healers both play important roles in community health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapist - a person trained to treat illness, especially by therapy

Etymology

From Old English hǣlan (to heal) + -er (agent suffix).
