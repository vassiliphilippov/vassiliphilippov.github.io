Stilton

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪltən / (AmE), / ˈstɪltən / (BrE)
a strong English blue cheese with a crumbly texture and distinctive flavor, made from cow's milk
English blue cheese

Examples

She served the salad with crumbled Stilton on top for extra flavor.
I bought a wedge of Stilton at the market to pair with pears.
Stilton is traditionally enjoyed with a glass of port after dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roquefort - a French blue cheese made from sheep's milk
  • Synonym: Gorgonzola - an Italian blue cheese made from cow's milk

Etymology

Named after the village of Stilton in Cambridgeshire, England, where it was first sold.
