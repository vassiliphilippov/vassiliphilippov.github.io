eutrophication

1. NOUN

/ juˌtroʊfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / juːˌtrɒfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a body of water becomes overly enriched with nutrients, leading to excessive growth of plants and algae, depletion of oxygen, and death of animal life.
over-enrichment of water with nutrients

Examples

The lake suffered from eutrophication due to agricultural runoff and improper waste disposal.
Many fish species disappeared as eutrophication depleted the oxygen levels in the water.
Scientists are studying ways to prevent eutrophication in freshwater reservoirs.

Synset Examples

he argued that the controlling factor in eutrophication is not nitrate but phosphate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eutrophic - having waters rich in nutrients that promote plant growth
  • Antonym: oligotrophication - the process of water becoming low in nutrients

Etymology

From Greek 'eutrophia' (well-nourished), via French 'eutrophique'.
