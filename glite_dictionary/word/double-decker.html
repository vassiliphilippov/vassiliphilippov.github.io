double-decker

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlˈdɛkər / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əlˈdɛk.ə / (BrE)
a bus or other vehicle with two levels for carrying passengers, one above the other
bus or vehicle with two passenger levels

Examples

We rode a double-decker through the city to enjoy the view from the top deck.
The famous red double-decker buses are a symbol of London.
She prefers traveling by double-decker because it offers more seating.

Synset Examples

he always rode the bus to work

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-decker - a bus or vehicle with only one passenger level

Etymology

From 'double' meaning two, and 'decker' from 'deck' (floor or platform), indicating two levels.
