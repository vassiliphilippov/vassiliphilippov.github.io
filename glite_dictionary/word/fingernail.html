fingernail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəneɪl / (BrE)
the hard, slightly curved covering at the tip of each finger on the human hand
nail at the end of a finger

Examples

He trimmed his fingernail carefully to avoid cutting the skin.
She painted each fingernail a different color for the party.
Dirt collected underneath his fingernail after a day of gardening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toenail - the nail on the tip of a toe

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'finger' and 'nail'.
