sylph

1. NOUN

/ sɪlf / (AmE), / sɪlf / (BrE)
a slender and graceful young woman, especially one considered ethereal or delicate in appearance
a slender, graceful young woman

Examples

She moved across the stage like a sylph, capturing everyone's attention with her elegance.
At the party, the hostess was a sylph in a flowing silver gown.
He described the dancer as a sylph with the grace and lightness of the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waif - a thin, often fragile-looking young woman
  • Synonym: nymph - a beautiful, graceful young woman, often in mythology

Etymology

From New Latin 'sylpha' (airy spirit), coined by Paracelsus in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ sɪlf / (AmE), / sɪlf / (BrE)
A mythological spirit of the air, often depicted as a slender, graceful woman.
mythical air spirit

Examples

In the ancient legend, a sylph glided through the morning sky, unseen by human eyes.
The dancer moved with the grace of a sylph, captivating the entire audience.
Victorian poets often described a sylph as a symbol of ethereal beauty and delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprite - a small or elusive supernatural being; a fairy
  • Synonym: nymph - a mythological spirit of nature imagined as a beautiful maiden

Etymology

From New Latin 'sylphus', likely from Latin 'silva' (forest); popularized by Paracelsus in the 16th century.
