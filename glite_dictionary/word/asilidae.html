Asilidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪlɪˌdi / (AmE), / əˈsɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of predatory flies, commonly known as robber flies, characterized by strong, bristly bodies and known for their aggressive predation on other insects.
family of predatory flies (robber flies)

Examples

The Asilidae are known for their voracious predation on other insects in various habitats.
Researchers collected several specimens of Asilidae from the forest edge for further study.
Members of the Asilidae family can often be seen resting on twigs, waiting to ambush prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robber fly - common name for insects in the family Asilidae

Etymology

From New Latin Asilidae, derived from Latin asilus (gadfly), from Greek ásilos (horsefly).
