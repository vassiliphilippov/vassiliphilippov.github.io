chaenactis

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈnæktɪs / (AmE), / kiːˈnæktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the daisy family, characterized by long stalks and funnel-shaped white or yellow flowers.
genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

The dry slopes of California are home to several species of chaenactis.
Botanists studied the unique flower structure of chaenactis in the field.
The white blossoms of chaenactis contrasted beautifully with the desert landscape.

Etymology

From Greek *chainein* (to gape) + *aktis* (ray), referring to the form of the flower.
