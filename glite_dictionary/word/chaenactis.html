Chaenactis

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈnæktɪs / (AmE), / kiːˈnaktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering herbs native to the western United States, commonly known as pincushions.
genus of flowering herbs (pincushions)

Examples

Several species of Chaenactis thrive in the deserts of the southwestern United States.
The botanist collected samples of Chaenactis for further study in the lab.
Chaenactis plants are recognized for their distinctive pincushion-like flower heads.

Etymology

From Greek khainein (to gape) + aktis (ray), referring to the flower structure.
