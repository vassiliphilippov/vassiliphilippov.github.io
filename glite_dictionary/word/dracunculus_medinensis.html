Dracunculus medinensis

1. NOUN

/ drəˌkʌŋkjələs ˌmɛdɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / drəˌkʌŋkjʊləs ˌmɛdɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a parasitic roundworm found in India and Africa that infects humans and other vertebrates, living in the abdomen or beneath the skin
a parasitic roundworm infecting humans
medical

Examples

Dracunculus medinensis is responsible for causing Guinea worm disease in humans.
The life cycle of Dracunculus medinensis involves contaminating water sources.
Doctors in endemic regions are actively working to eradicate Dracunculus medinensis infections.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dracunculus' means 'little dragon'; 'medinensis' refers to Medina, a region associated with the worm's prevalence.
