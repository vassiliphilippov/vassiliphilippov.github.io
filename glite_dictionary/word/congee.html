congee

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒi / (BrE)
a type of Chinese rice porridge, often eaten for breakfast or as a light meal
Chinese rice porridge

Examples

She served a comforting bowl of congee with pickled vegetables for breakfast.
Many people eat congee when they are feeling unwell because it is easy to digest.
The restaurant offers a variety of toppings for their signature congee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porridge - a dish made by boiling grains in water or milk until thick

Etymology

From Chinese (Cantonese jūk or Hokkien kanji), possibly via Portuguese 'canja'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒi / (BrE)
A concave molding used in architecture, typically with a hollowed, curved profile.
concave architectural molding

Examples

The decorative congee ran along the top edge of the door frame.
Restorers carefully recreated the original congee in the historic hall.
A congee can add a graceful detail to classical columns.

Etymology

From French *congé* (fluting), itself from Latin *concha* (shell, conch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03095054-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkɑn.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒi / (BrE)
(archaic) To depart after obtaining formal permission, especially from someone in authority or of higher social status.
formally depart (archaic usage)
archaic

Examples

After the council meeting, he congeed with great ceremony before the assembled nobles.
The ambassador congeed from the queen as a mark of respect.
She congeed quietly, taking her leave without drawing attention to herself.

Synset Examples

He has congeed with the King

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person

Etymology

From French 'congier' (to take leave), from Latin 'commeatus' (leave of absence).

Source: WordNet
