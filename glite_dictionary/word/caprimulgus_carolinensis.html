Caprimulgus carolinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprɪˈmʌlɡəs ˌkæroʊlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈmʌlɡəs ˌkærəlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a large nocturnal bird of the southern United States, similar to the whip-poor-will, also known as the Chuck-will's-widow
large nocturnal bird of the southern U.S.

Examples

The Caprimulgus carolinensis is often heard on warm southern nights, calling its distinctive song.
Birdwatchers travel to the Southeast to catch a glimpse of the elusive Caprimulgus carolinensis.
Unlike the smaller whip-poor-will, the Caprimulgus carolinensis has a larger body and a deeper call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chuck-will's-widow - common name for Caprimulgus carolinensis; large nocturnal bird of the southern United States
  • Degree Lower: whip-poor-will - a related nocturnal bird found in North America (Antrostomus vociferus)

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Caprimulgus' (goat-milker), species 'carolinensis' (of Carolina).
