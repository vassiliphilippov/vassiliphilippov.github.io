primateship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmeɪtˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪmətˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of a primate, especially in certain Christian churches
the office of a church primate

Examples

The election to the primateship brought new responsibilities and challenges.
His tenure in the primateship was marked by significant reforms in the church.
After the death of the previous leader, the primateship remained vacant for several months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primate - a senior bishop or archbishop in some Christian churches

Etymology

Formed from 'primate' (from Latin 'primas', 'chief') + 'ship', denoting office or position.
