discouraged

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɜrɪdʒd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒd / (BrE)
lacking confidence or enthusiasm because of difficulties or setbacks
lacking hope or enthusiasm

Examples

After several failed attempts, she felt completely discouraged and wanted to give up.
The team became discouraged when they started losing so many games in a row.
He tried not to seem discouraged, but his disappointment was obvious to everyone.

Synset Examples

desperate demoralized people looking for work
felt discouraged by the magnitude of the problem
the disheartened instructor tried vainly to arouse their interest

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraged - having more hope, confidence, or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: disheartened - having lost hope or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Old French *descoragier*, from des- (expressing reversal) + corage (courage).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɝːrɪdʒd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒd / (BrE)
having lost confidence, hope, or enthusiasm, often because of difficulties or setbacks
having lost confidence or enthusiasm

Examples

After several failed attempts, she felt too discouraged to keep trying.
Many workers became discouraged by the lack of progress on the project.
Despite being discouraged by bad reviews, the author published another novel.

Synset Examples

the accident left others discouraged about going there

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraged - having increased motivation or confidence
  • Synonym: disheartened - having lost confidence or determination

Etymology

From the verb 'discourage,' from Old French 'descourager' (to dishearten), from Latin prefix 'dis-' + 'cor' (heart).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01999500-s
Synset POS: s
