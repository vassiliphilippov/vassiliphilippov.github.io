toga

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊɡə / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɡə / (BrE)
a loose, one-piece cloak worn by men in ancient Rome, typically draped over the shoulder and around the body
cloak worn by men in ancient Rome

Examples

At the costume party, Marcus wore a white toga and a laurel wreath.
The historian described the significance of the toga in Roman political life.
Students dressed in togas to celebrate Latin Week at school.

Etymology

From Latin toga (cloak, mantle).
