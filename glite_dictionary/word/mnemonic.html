mnemonic

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈmɑːnɪk / (AmE), / nɪˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
a word, phrase, rhyme, or other device used to help remember something
memory aid or device

Examples

The teacher taught us a clever mnemonic to remember the planets' order.
'ROY G. BIV' is a popular mnemonic for recalling the colors of the rainbow.
He invented a new mnemonic to help him memorize chemical formulas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory aid - device that helps someone remember something

Etymology

From Greek mnēmonikos (of memory), from mnēmōn (mindful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00172328-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ nəˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / nɪˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or designed to assist the memory.
aiding or relating to memory

Examples

The teacher introduced a mnemonic trick to help students remember the planets in order.
She used a simple mnemonic rhyme to recall the steps in the process.
A mnemonic chart made memorizing chemical elements much easier.

Synset Examples

mnemonic device

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory aid - something used to help remember information

Etymology

From Greek mnēmonikos (of memory), from mnēmōn (mindful).
