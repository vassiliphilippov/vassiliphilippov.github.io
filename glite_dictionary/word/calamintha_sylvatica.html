Calamintha sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈmɪnθə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈmɪnθə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial mint-scented herb found in central and southern Europe, belonging to the mint family.
a mint-scented European perennial herb

Examples

Calamintha sylvatica thrives in the woodlands and meadows of southern Europe.
The leaves of Calamintha sylvatica give off a pleasant minty aroma when crushed.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Calamintha sylvatica for its attractive flowers and scent.

Etymology

From Latin: 'calamintha' (mint genus), 'sylvatica' meaning 'of the woods'.
