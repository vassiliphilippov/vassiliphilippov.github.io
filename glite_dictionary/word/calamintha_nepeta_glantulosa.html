Calamintha nepeta glantulosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈmɪnθə nəˈpiːtə ˌɡlæn.tʃuˈloʊsə / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈmɪn.θə nɪˈpiː.tə ˌɡlæn.tjʊˈləʊ.sə / (BrE)
A low-growing, strongly aromatic perennial herb native to southern Europe and Great Britain, and naturalized in the United States; a subspecies of calamint.
aromatic perennial herb; a calamint subspecies

Examples

The garden included Calamintha nepeta glantulosa for its pleasant scent and culinary uses.
Botanists are studying the spread of Calamintha nepeta glantulosa in American wildlands.
Bees are especially attracted to the flowers of Calamintha nepeta glantulosa during mid-summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamint - Any of several aromatic herbs in the genus Calamintha

Etymology

From Latin: 'Calamintha' (a genus name), 'nepeta' (referring to catnip), 'glantulosa' (with glands).
