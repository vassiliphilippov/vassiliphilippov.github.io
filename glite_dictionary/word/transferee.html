transferee

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌtrɑːnsfəˈriː / (BrE)
a person who transfers or is transferred from one place, position, or institution to another
person who is transferred

Examples

The new policy provides extensive support to each transferee during their transition period.
As a recent transferee from another department, she quickly adapted to her new role.
The company hosted a welcome event for every transferee joining the headquarters this year.

Synset Examples

the best student was a transfer from LSU

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transferor - a person who makes a transfer to someone else

Etymology

From transfer + -ee, following the pattern of Latin-derived agent nouns indicating one who receives an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌtrænsfəˈriː / (BrE)
(law) a person to whom a title, property, or right is transferred by another party.
person to whom property or rights are transferred
legal

Examples

The transferee must sign all documents to complete the ownership change.
Once the contract is executed, the transferee becomes responsible for the property.
In the case of a share sale, the seller is the transferor and the buyer is the transferee.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transferor - the person who transfers a title, property, or right to another

Etymology

From transfer + -ee, by analogy with other legal terms like 'lessee' or 'assignee', indicating the person to whom something is transferred.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10744206-n
Synset POS: n
