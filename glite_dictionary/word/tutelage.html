tutelage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
The act of guarding, caring for, or having responsibility for someone, often involving protection and supervision.
care, guardianship, or protection

Examples

The young heir was placed under the tutelage of a trusted advisor after his parents' death.
The organization operates under the tutelage of government regulators to ensure public safety.
During her trip abroad, she was happy to be in the tutelage of an experienced guide.

Synset Examples

he is in the care of a bodyguard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - the provision of what is necessary for the health, welfare, maintenance, and protection of someone
  • Synonym: guardianship - the position of protecting or defending something

Etymology

From Latin tutela (protection, care), from tutus (safe, protected).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of teaching or instructing, especially when done individually by a tutor; guidance or supervision in study or learning
individual teaching or supervision

Examples

Under the tutelage of his mentor, he quickly mastered the basics of chess.
She owes her impressive academic progress to the personal tutelage she received.
During my summer holidays, I learned French under private tutelage at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuition - private teaching or instruction, often for individuals or small groups
  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge
  • Synonym: guidance - advice or instruction aimed at resolving a problem or difficulty, especially as given by someone in authority

Etymology

From Latin tutela (protection, guardianship), from tutus (safe).
