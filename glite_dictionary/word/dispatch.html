dispatch

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)
the act of sending someone or something to a place for a particular purpose
the act of sending off something

Examples

The urgent dispatch of medical supplies saved many lives after the earthquake.
She arranged the dispatch of the documents by courier yesterday.
A team was awaiting the dispatch of new equipment from the main office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipment - the act of sending goods to a destination

Etymology

From Italian *dispacciare* (to hasten), from Old French *despechier* (to hasten, dispatch).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)

Examples
