Tarpon atlanticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːrpən ætˈlæntɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈtɑːpən ætˈlæntɪkəs / (BrE)
A large, silvery game fish of warm Atlantic coastal waters, especially off Florida, known for its size and strength.
large silvery Atlantic game fish

Examples

The Tarpon atlanticus is among the most sought-after trophies for sport fishers in Florida.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the migration of Tarpon atlanticus along the Gulf Coast.
Catching a Tarpon atlanticus requires special tackle due to its size and fighting strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarpon - large game fish of the genus Megalops

Etymology

From 'tarpon' (unknown origin) and 'Atlanticus', Latin for 'of the Atlantic'.
