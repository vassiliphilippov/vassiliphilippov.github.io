inquiringly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈkwaɪɚɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner showing curiosity, interest, or a desire to know more.
in a curious or questioning manner

Examples

She glanced inquiringly at her boss, hoping for permission to speak.
The child looked inquiringly at the strange machine, eager to know what it did.
He raised his eyebrows inquiringly, waiting for her response.

Synset Examples

Tom Swift looked at his cabin mates inquiringly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curiously - in a way that shows curiosity or interest
  • Degree Lower: suspiciously - in a way that shows suspicion

Etymology

Formed from 'inquiring' + adverbial suffix '-ly', based on 'inquire' from Latin 'inquirere'.
