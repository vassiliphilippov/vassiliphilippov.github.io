pandeism

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdiːɪzəm / (AmE), / pænˈdiːɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that God created the universe by becoming the universe itself, and that the universe is now the only manifestation of God.
doctrine: God became the universe

Examples

Many scholars have studied pandeism as a unique blend of pantheism and deism.
Followers of pandeism believe that the divine became the universe itself after creation.
His philosophical writings frequently reference pandeism in the context of comparative religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantheism - belief that God and the universe are identical
  • Synonym: deism - belief that God created the universe but does not intervene in it

Etymology

Blend of 'pantheism' and 'deism' (see also their etymologies).
