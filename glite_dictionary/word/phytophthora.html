Phytophthora

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈtɑfθərə / (AmE), / faɪˈtɒfθərə / (BrE)
a genus of destructive parasitic microorganisms resembling fungi, causing diseases such as brown rot in plants
genus of plant pathogens causing brown rot
medical

Examples

Phytophthora infestans was responsible for the Irish potato famine in the mid-nineteenth century.
Farmers are taking steps to protect their crops from Phytophthora infections this season.
The presence of Phytophthora in the soil poses a serious threat to many ornamental plants.

Etymology

From Greek phyto- (plant) + phthora (destruction).

2. NOUN

/ faɪˈtɒfθərə / (AmE), / faɪˈtɒfθərə / (BrE)
A genus of destructive parasitic oomycete fungi that cause diseases such as brown rot in plants.
a genus of plant-pathogenic fungi
medical

Examples

Potato crops can be devastated by Phytophthora infestations, leading to significant agricultural losses.
The scientist identified Phytophthora in the roots, which explained the widespread plant dieback.
Many nursery plants must be regularly inspected for signs of Phytophthora infection.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyton' (plant) + 'phthora' (destruction/corruption).
