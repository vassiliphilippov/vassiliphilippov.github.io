innumerate

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnuːmərət / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːmərət / (BrE)
lacking basic knowledge or understanding of mathematical concepts and methods
lacking mathematical understanding

Examples

Many adults remain innumerate despite years of schooling.
Being innumerate can make everyday tasks like budgeting much more challenging.
The company offers training for employees who are functionally innumerate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: numerate - having a good basic knowledge of mathematics

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'illiterate,' from Latin numeratus (counted) with prefix in- (not).
