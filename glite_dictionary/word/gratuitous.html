gratuitous

1. ADJ

/ ɡrəˈtuɪtəs / (AmE), / ɡrəˈtjuːɪtəs / (BrE)
Done without good reason or justification; uncalled for.
done without good reason or cause

Examples

His comment was a gratuitous insult that served no purpose.
There was a scene of gratuitous violence in the film that many found disturbing.
The manager's criticism seemed entirely gratuitous and unfair.

Synset Examples

a gratuitous insult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwarranted - not justified or authorized
  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason for something

Etymology

From Latin gratuitus (given freely, spontaneous), from gratus (pleasing, thankful).

2. ADJ

/ ɡrəˈtuːɪtəs / (AmE), / ɡrəˈtjuːɪtəs / (BrE)
(of something such as an action or remark) not necessary or justified; done without a good reason.
unnecessary; done without reason

Examples

The violence in the film was criticized for being gratuitous and excessive.
She was angered by his gratuitous comment about her appearance.
The movie included a gratuitous plot twist that added nothing to the story.

Synset Examples

a strikers' tent camp … was burned with needless loss of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unnecessary - not needed; not essential
  • Antonym: justified - having or shown to have a good reason; warranted

Etymology

From Latin 'gratuitus' (given freely, spontaneous), from 'gratus' (pleasing).
