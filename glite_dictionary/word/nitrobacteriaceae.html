Nitrobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ naɪtroʊˌbæktɪəriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / naɪtrəʊˌbaktɪəriːˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of rod-shaped bacteria that oxidize ammonia or nitrites, important in the nitrogen cycle.
family of bacteria that oxidize ammonia or nitrites
medical

Examples

The Nitrobacteriaceae play a vital role in the conversion of ammonia to nitrates in soil.
Researchers isolated several species of Nitrobacteriaceae from the agricultural field samples.
An imbalance in Nitrobacteriaceae populations can disrupt the nitrogen cycle in aquatic systems.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on type genus Nitrobacter + family suffix -aceae.
