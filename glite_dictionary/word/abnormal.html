abnormal

1. ADJ

/ æbˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / æbˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
much greater than what is usual or normal; exceeding the normal range or amount.
much greater than normal

Examples

The company reported abnormal profits for the quarter, surprising many analysts.
His abnormal ambition drove him to achieve what few thought possible.
Doctors found abnormal levels of the hormone in her blood test results.

Synset Examples

abnormal profits
abnormal ambition

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal - usual, typical, or standard

Etymology

From Latin abnormis, from ab- (away from) + norma (rule).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01537654-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ æbˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / æbˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
deviating from what is normal, usual, or expected, especially in a way that may be undesirable or worrying
deviating from the normal or usual

Examples

The test results showed an abnormal level of hormones in his blood.
Her behavior became increasingly abnormal after the accident.
Doctors are monitoring the patient for any abnormal signs or symptoms.

Synset Examples

they were heartbroken when they learned their child was abnormal
an abnormal personality

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected
  • Synonym: anomalous - deviating from what is standard, normal, or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'abnormis' (irregular), from 'ab-' (away from) + 'norma' (rule, pattern).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01603174-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ æbˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / æbˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
different from what is normal or usual, often in a way that is worrying or undesirable.
not normal; unusual

Examples

Her abnormal behavior worried her friends and family.
The doctor noted an abnormal increase in his heart rate.
Meteorologists reported abnormal weather patterns throughout the spring.

Synset Examples

abnormal amounts of rain
abnormal circumstances
an abnormal interest in food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected
  • Synonym: unusual - not usual, uncommon
