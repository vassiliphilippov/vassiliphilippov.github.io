anagram

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈanəɡram / (BrE)
a word or phrase formed by rearranging the letters of another word or phrase
word rearranged from another

Examples

The word anagram comes from the Greek, meaning to transcribe letters differently.
'Listen' is an anagram of 'silent'.
She loves solving anagram puzzles in her free time.

Etymology

From Greek 'anagrammatismos' (transposition of letters), from 'ana-' (again) + 'gramma' (letter).

2. VERB

/ ˈænəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈænəɡræm / (BrE)
to rearrange the letters of a word, phrase, or text to form a new word or phrase, often to discover a hidden meaning
rearrange letters to discover a hidden meaning

Examples

She tried to anagram the word ‘listen’ and found ‘silent’.
The puzzle required us to anagram each clue before finding the answer.
He anagrammed the author’s name to create a clever pseudonym.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unscramble - to rearrange something so that it is no longer confused or jumbled

Etymology

From Greek anagrammatismos, from ana- (again) + gramma (letter).
