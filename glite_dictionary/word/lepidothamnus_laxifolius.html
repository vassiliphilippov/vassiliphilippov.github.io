Lepidothamnus laxifolius

1. NOUN

/ lɛpɪdoʊˈθæmnəs læksɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / lɛpɪdəʊˈθamnəs læksɪˈfəʊlɪəs / (BrE)
A low-growing or prostrate shrub with slender trailing branches, native to New Zealand.
New Zealand prostrate shrub

Examples

The Lepidothamnus laxifolius is one of the smallest conifers in the world.
Botanists discovered a large patch of Lepidothamnus laxifolius growing in the alpine region.
Conservation efforts have helped protect the rare Lepidothamnus laxifolius in New Zealand.

Etymology

From Greek *lepis* (scale) + *thamnos* (shrub) and Latin *laxus* (loose) + *folium* (leaf).
