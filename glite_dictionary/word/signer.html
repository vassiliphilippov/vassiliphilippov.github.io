signer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who uses sign language to communicate, especially someone who translates spoken language into sign language for the deaf
person using sign language

Examples

The signer at the conference interpreted every speech for the deaf audience.
A signer stood beside the stage, making the concert accessible to everyone.
She became a skilled signer after taking several courses in American Sign Language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreter - a person who translates spoken language into another language, including sign language

Etymology

From 'sign' (gesture used for communication) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who signs a document and is thereby bound by its contents
a person who signs a document

Examples

The signer of the contract agreed to all the listed terms and conditions.
Each signer must present identification before their signature is accepted.
The document requires a signer who is at least eighteen years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subscriber - a person who signs a document, especially a contract or official paper
  • Synonym: witness - a person who observes the signing of a document and attests to it

Etymology

From sign + -er, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
