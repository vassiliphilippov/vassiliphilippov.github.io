phenobarbitone

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːnoʊˈbɑːrbɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnəˈbɑːbɪtəʊn / (BrE)
A long-acting barbiturate drug used as a sedative and to control seizures.
barbiturate drug used as sedative
medical british

Examples

The patient was prescribed phenobarbitone to manage her epilepsy.
Doctors sometimes use phenobarbitone as a sedative in certain medical situations.
Long-term use of phenobarbitone requires careful monitoring due to potential side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenobarbital - the American term for the same barbiturate drug

Etymology

From 'pheno-' (as in phenyl group) and 'barbitone', an earlier name for barbituric acid derivatives.
