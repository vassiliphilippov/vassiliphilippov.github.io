anthropogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænθroʊpəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌanθrəpəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(especially of environmental change or pollution) caused or produced by human activity.
caused by human activity

Examples

Many scientists are concerned about anthropogenic climate change and its effects on the planet.
The majority of new emissions are anthropogenic in origin rather than the result of natural processes.
Anthropogenic factors frequently contribute to habitat destruction and loss of biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature, not made or caused by humankind

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpos (human being) + -genic (produced by).
