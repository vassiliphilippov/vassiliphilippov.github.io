Ptolemy I Soter

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑːləm.i waɪ ˈsoʊ.tər / (AmE), / ˈtɒl.ə.mi wʌn ˈsəʊ.tə / (BrE)
(circa 367–285 BC) The king of Egypt who founded the Macedonian dynasty in Egypt and was a close associate and general of Alexander the Great.
founder of the Macedonian dynasty in ancient Egypt

Examples

Ptolemy I Soter became ruler of Egypt after Alexander the Great's death.
The dynasty established by Ptolemy I Soter lasted nearly three centuries.
Historians regard Ptolemy I Soter as a founder of Hellenistic rule in Egypt.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πτολεμαῖος (Ptolemaios); Soter means 'saviour' in Greek.
