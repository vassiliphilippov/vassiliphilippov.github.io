botfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A stout-bodied, hairy fly whose larvae are parasitic on humans and other mammals.
parasitic fly species

Examples

A botfly can lay its eggs on the skin of mammals, including humans.
The presence of a botfly larva under the skin can cause irritation and swelling.
Veterinarians often treat cattle to prevent infestations by the botfly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble fly - another species of large fly whose larvae also parasitize mammals

Etymology

From 'bot,' an old term for a maggot or larva, plus 'fly.'
