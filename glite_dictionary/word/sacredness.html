sacredness

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪkrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sacred, holy, or worthy of religious veneration
the quality of being sacred or holy

Examples

The sacredness of the temple is respected by everyone in the community.
She spoke about the sacredness of life during the ceremony.
Many traditions emphasize the sacredness of certain rituals and objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holiness - the state of being holy
  • Antonym: profaneness - the quality of being profane or not sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'sacer' (sacred, holy) + English suffix '-ness'.
