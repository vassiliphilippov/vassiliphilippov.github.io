Holocentrus bullisi

1. NOUN

/ hoʊləˈsɛntrəs bʊˈlɪsaɪ / (AmE), / hɒl.əˈsɛn.trəs bʊˈlɪsaɪ / (BrE)
A species of squirrelfish found in the western Atlantic, from South Carolina to Bermuda and the Gulf of Mexico.
a species of squirrelfish

Examples

The vibrant hues of the Holocentrus bullisi make it a popular species among marine enthusiasts.
Researchers spotted several Holocentrus bullisi near the coral reefs off the Bermuda coast.
The Holocentrus bullisi prefers shallow waters and is often active at night.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Holocentrus from Greek 'holos' (whole) + 'kentron' (spine), species after American ichthyologist Harold Bullis.
