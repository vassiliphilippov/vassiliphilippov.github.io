Hegira

1. NOUN

/ həˈdʒirə, hɪˈdʒaɪrə / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒaɪrə, həˈdʒɪərə / (BrE)
The flight of Muhammad from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE, marking the beginning of the Muslim era and Islamic calendar.
Muhammad's flight from Mecca in 622 CE

Examples

The Hegira marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
Many historians regard the Hegira as a pivotal event in world history.
Muslims celebrate the Hegira as the moment when their community was first established.

Etymology

From Arabic hijra (emigration, flight).
