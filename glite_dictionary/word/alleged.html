alleged

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɛdʒd / (AmE), / əˈlɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of an incident or a fact) said, without proof, to have taken place or to be the case.
declared but not proven

Examples

The report details the alleged involvement of several officials in the scandal.
He denied all the alleged violations cited in the lawsuit.
Police are investigating the alleged theft of confidential documents from the office.

Synset Examples

alleged abuses of housing benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supposed - generally assumed or believed to be true, often without certain proof
  • Antonym: proven - shown to be true or real with evidence; established beyond doubt

Etymology

From the verb 'allege', from Old French *alegier*, from Medieval Latin *allegare* (to declare, cite).

2. ADJ

/ əˈlɛdʒd / (AmE), / əˈlɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of something) claimed to be true or to have taken place, especially when this has not been proved and is regarded with doubt or suspicion.
claimed without proof; suspect

Examples

The alleged thief was arrested but has not yet been charged.
Several alleged sightings of the rare bird have been reported in the area.
She denied all alleged involvement in the fraud scheme.

Synset Examples

these so-called experts are no help

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supposed - assumed or believed to be true without proof
  • Antonym: proven - demonstrated or established as true or real

Etymology

From late Middle English, past participle of 'allege' (to assert or declare).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01922936-s
Synset POS: s
