Hypsiprymnodon moschatus

1. NOUN

/ haɪpsɪˌprɪmnədɒn mɒˈskeɪtəs / (AmE), / hɪpsɪˌprɪmnədɒn mɒsˈkætəs / (BrE)
A small species of kangaroo, commonly known as the musky rat-kangaroo, native to northeastern Australia.
small kangaroo species of northeastern Australia

Examples

The Hypsiprymnodon moschatus is the smallest member of the kangaroo family.
Researchers studied the habits of Hypsiprymnodon moschatus in the rainforests of Queensland.
Unlike larger kangaroos, Hypsiprymnodon moschatus moves using all four limbs.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining Greek 'hypsi-' (high), 'prymnos' (hind part), and species name from Latin 'moschatus' (musky).
