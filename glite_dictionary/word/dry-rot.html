to dry-rot

1. VERB

/ ˈdraɪ ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌrɒt / (BrE)
(of wood or other organic matter) to become decayed by dry rot, or to cause to decay in this way.
to decay or cause decay by dry rot

Examples

The old beams in the attic began to dry-rot after years of damp conditions.
Neglected buildings are often allowed to dry-rot before being demolished.
Improper sealing can cause window frames to dry-rot much faster than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - to rot or decompose naturally

Etymology

From the noun 'dry rot,' itself attested since the 18th century.
