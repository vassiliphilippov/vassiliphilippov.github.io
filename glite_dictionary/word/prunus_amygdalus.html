Prunus amygdalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs əˈmɪɡdələs / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs æmɪɡˈdaləs / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Asia and North Africa, cultivated for its edible nuts (almonds) and pink blossoms.
almond tree; source of almonds

Examples

The Prunus amygdalus is famous for its beautiful spring blossoms and abundant almond harvest.
Farmers in California widely cultivate Prunus amygdalus for commercial almond production.
Botanists studied the drought resistance of Prunus amygdalus in Mediterranean climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond tree - tree that produces almonds; common name for Prunus amygdalus
  • Synonym: almond - the edible nut produced by Prunus amygdalus

Etymology

From Latin: genus Prunus (plum trees) and species amygdalus (almond) from Greek amygdalos (almond tree).
