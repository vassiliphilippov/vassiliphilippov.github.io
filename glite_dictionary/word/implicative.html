implicative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪkətɪv / (BrE)
tending to imply or suggest something rather than state it directly.
tending to imply or suggest

Examples

The lawyer's implicative remarks hinted at the client's possible motive.
His response was implicative rather than direct, leaving much to interpretation.
The novel uses implicative language to address controversial topics.

Synset Examples

artifacts suggestive of an ancient society
an implicative statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestive - tending to suggest or imply something

Etymology

From Latin implicatus (entangled, involved), past participle of implicare (to entangle).
