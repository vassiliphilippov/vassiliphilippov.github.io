regionalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
loyalty to or advocacy for the interests of a particular region, often as opposed to those of a central authority
loyalty to a region's interests

Examples

Political regionalism has grown in response to the government's central policies.
The party's platform embraces regionalism, supporting greater autonomy for local governments.
Many believe that strong regionalism can hinder national unity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nationalism - loyalty or devotion to one's nation

Etymology

From 'region' + '-alism,' patterned after 'nationalism.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a word, expression, pronunciation, or custom that is characteristic of a particular region
feature characteristic of a region

Examples

The word 'pop' for soft drink is a regionalism common in the Midwest.
His accent includes several regionalisms that reveal he is from the South.
Certain culinary traditions are considered regionalism unique to northern France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a particular form of a language specific to a region or social group

Etymology

From 'region' + '-alism', modeled on similar '-ism' nouns.

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A policy or principle that prioritizes the interests of a particular geographic region, especially in international affairs.
foreign policy focusing on specific regions

Examples

The country's regionalism influenced its decision to strengthen ties with neighboring states.
Critics argue that excessive regionalism can undermine broader international cooperation.
Her thesis explores the impact of regionalism on economic development in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalism - advocacy of political independence for a particular nation

Etymology

From region + -alism, modeled after similar formations such as nationalism.
