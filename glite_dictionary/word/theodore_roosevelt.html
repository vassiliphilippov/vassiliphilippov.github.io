Theodore Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˈθiədoʊɹ ˈɹoʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈθiːədɔː ˈɹəʊzəˌvɛlt / (BrE)
(1858–1919) the 26th President of the United States, noted for his leadership in the Spanish-American War, progressive domestic policies, and for overseeing the construction of the Panama Canal.
26th U.S. President (1858–1919)

Examples

Theodore Roosevelt was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for mediating the end of the Russo-Japanese War.
As president, Theodore Roosevelt championed progressive reforms and conservation efforts.
Many Americans remember Theodore Roosevelt for his famous phrase, 'Speak softly and carry a big stick.'
