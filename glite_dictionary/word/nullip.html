nullip

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌlˌɪp / (AmE), / ˈnʌl.ɪp / (BrE)
a woman who has never given birth to a child
woman who has never given birth
medical

Examples

After the seminar, several nullips shared their perspectives on societal expectations.
She identified as a nullip, focusing on her career instead of motherhood.
Doctors may use the term nullip in medical records to denote a patient's obstetric history.

Synset Examples

She identified as a nullip, focusing on her career instead of motherhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nullipara - a woman who has never borne a child
  • Antonym: primipara - a woman giving birth for the first time

Etymology

Shortened from medical Latin 'nullipara', from Latin 'nullus' (none) + 'parere' (to give birth).
