confession

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the act of admitting one's sins to a priest as part of the sacrament of penance, seeking forgiveness.
admitting sins to a priest (religious)

Examples

After making his confession, he felt a great sense of relief and peace.
Every Saturday afternoon, parishioners line up for confession at the cathedral.
She encouraged her son to go to confession before Easter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - the act of admitting something
  • Antonym: denial - refusal to admit the truth or reality

Etymology

From Latin 'confessionem' (acknowledgement, disclosure), from 'confiteri' (to confess).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
a statement in which someone admits that they have done something wrong or committed a fault or crime
admission of fault or wrongdoing

Examples

After hours of questioning, he finally made a confession to the police.
Her honest confession surprised everyone in the room.
The letter contained a heartfelt confession of his past mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - the act of admitting something, especially a fact or fault
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or that one has not done something

Etymology

From Latin 'confessionem' (acknowledgment, admission), from 'confiteri' (to acknowledge).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
a written statement in which someone admits to having committed an offense, typically signed by the person making it
a written admission of guilt

Examples

The suspect signed a confession in the presence of his lawyer.
Police presented the court with a written confession detailing the events of the crime.
Without a valid confession, the prosecution had little evidence to rely on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - a statement acknowledging the truth of something (especially wrongdoing)

Etymology

From Latin 'confessio', from 'confiteri' meaning 'to acknowledge'.

4. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
a public declaration or formal statement of religious faith, especially in a religious ceremony or creed
public declaration of faith

Examples

During the ceremony, she made a solemn confession of her faith before the entire congregation.
The Nicene Creed is an ancient confession recited by many Christian churches worldwide.
His personal confession as a Christian was a turning point in his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creed - a formal statement of religious belief
  • Antonym: denial - the act of declaring something to be untrue, especially faith

Etymology

From Latin confessio (acknowledgment, declaration), from confiteri (to acknowledge, confess).

5. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a formal written statement describing the beliefs and doctrines of a particular church, especially those created during the Reformation period
formal doctrinal statement of a church

Examples

The Augsburg Confession is one of the most important documents of the Lutheran tradition.
Many Presbyterian churches adhere to the Westminster Confession as a summary of their doctrine.
That denomination revised its confession in the 19th century to reflect new theological views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creed - a formal statement of religious beliefs
  • Synonym: catechism - a summary of religious doctrine often in question-and-answer form

Etymology

From Latin 'confessio' (acknowledgment, confession), used for formal declarations of faith in Christian tradition.
