actionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.ʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈæk.ʃən.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Giving sufficient cause for legal action; subject to a lawsuit.
subject to a lawsuit
legal

Examples

The company discovered several actionable breaches of contract in the report.
His remarks were considered actionable under defamation law.
If the client's claim is actionable, the lawyer will proceed with a lawsuit.

Synset Examples

slander is an actionable offense

Etymology

From Medieval Latin actionabilis, from Latin actio (a legal action, a doing).
