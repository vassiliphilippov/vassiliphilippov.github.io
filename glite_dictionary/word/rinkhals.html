rinkhals

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌhɑlz / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌhɑːlz / (BrE)
a highly venomous snake native to southern Africa, known for its ability to spit venom over several feet
African spitting cobra-like snake

Examples

The rinkhals can rear up and display its distinctive hood when threatened.
Unlike some cobras, the rinkhals prefers to feign death rather than attack.
Wildlife rangers were trained to handle rinkhalses during snake relocation operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a highly venomous snake known for its hood

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'rinkhals', meaning 'ring-neck'.
