Lewisia rediviva

1. NOUN

/ luˈiːziə ˌrɛdɪˈvaɪvə / (AmE), / luˈiːziə ˌrɛdɪˈvaɪvə / (BrE)
a low-growing succulent plant native to the Rocky Mountains, noted for its deep to pale pink flowers and fleshy, starchy roots; also known as the Montana state flower
a succulent plant, Montana state flower

Examples

The Lewisia rediviva blooms in spring across the dry slopes of Montana.
Many gardeners admire the delicate flowers of Lewisia rediviva for their beauty and resilience.
The fleshy roots of Lewisia rediviva were traditionally eaten by Native Americans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterroot - common name for Lewisia rediviva

Etymology

Named after explorer Meriwether Lewis; 'rediviva' from Latin, meaning 'revived'.
