sortition

1. NOUN

/ sɔrˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / sɔːˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
the practice of making decisions or selecting individuals by drawing lots or similar random methods
decision-making by drawing lots

Examples

In ancient Athens, political offices were often assigned through sortition rather than elections.
The organization decided to use sortition as a fair way to choose committee members.
Some scholars believe that sortition can help reduce bias and corruption in the selection process.

Etymology

From Latin 'sortitio' (allotment by lot), from 'sortiri' (to draw lots).
