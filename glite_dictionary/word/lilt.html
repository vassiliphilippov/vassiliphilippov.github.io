lilt

1. NOUN

/ lɪlt / (AmE), / lɪlt / (BrE)
a lively or cheerful rhythm or cadence, especially in music or speech
lively rhythm or cadence

Examples

The cheerful lilt of the music made everyone want to dance.
She spoke with a gentle lilt in her voice, enchanting the entire room.
The pianist played the melody with a distinct lilt that brightened the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - a rhythmic feel or groove in music
  • Antonym: monotone - a lack of variation in pitch or rhythm

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative of a rhythmic sound or from Scots lilt (to sing or sound cheerfully).

2. VERB

/ lɪlt / (AmE), / lɪlt / (BrE)
to speak, sing, or play in a light, rhythmic, and graceful manner; to articulate with a lively or musical rhythm
speak or sing with a rhythmic swing

Examples

Her voice began to lilt as she recited the poem to the children.
He would lilt every sentence, making even ordinary words sound musical.
The singer lilted the melody, giving it a playful, uplifting quality.

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian 'lilta' (to trudge, to lollop).
