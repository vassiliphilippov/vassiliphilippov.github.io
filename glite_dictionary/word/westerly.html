westerly

1. ADV

/ ˈwɛstərli / (AmE), / ˈwɛstəli / (BrE)
in a westward direction
towards the west (direction)

Examples

The hikers continued westerly along the ridge until sunset.
The ship turned westerly to avoid the storm clouds on the horizon.
Birds migrate westerly in search of a milder climate during the autumn.

Synset Examples

we began to steer away westerly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: westward - toward the west
  • Antonym: easterly - in an eastward direction

Etymology

From Old English 'wester', meaning 'western', with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərli / (AmE), / ˈwɛstəli / (BrE)
A wind that blows from the west, especially one occurring in the temperate zones of the Earth.
wind blowing from the west

Examples

During spring, a strong westerly brought rain to the valley.
The sailors prepared for the arrival of the afternoon westerly.
Farmers rely on the westerly to bring cool, moist air during the hot season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easterly - A wind that blows from the east.
  • Synonym: westerlies - Prevailing winds from the west found in middle latitudes.

Etymology

From 'west' + '-erly', meaning 'toward' or 'coming from' the west.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11546047-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɛstərli / (AmE), / ˈwɛstəli / (BrE)
(of wind) blowing from the west.
(of wind) from the west

Examples

A strong westerly wind brought clouds and rain to the coast.
The sailors adjusted their course to take advantage of the westerly breeze.
Farmers often worry about crop damage when a cold westerly arrives in spring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easterly - (of wind) blowing from the east
  • Antonym: southerly - (of wind) blowing from the south
  • Antonym: northerly - (of wind) blowing from the north

Etymology

From 'west' + '-erly', formed in analogy to other compass-point adjectives.
