hackamore

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæk.əˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈhæk.əmɔː / (BrE)
A type of headgear for a horse, made of rope or canvas, used for leading or controlling the horse without a bit.
rope or canvas horse headgear

Examples

The cowboy used a hackamore instead of a bridle for the young horse.
She bought a brightly colored hackamore for her pony at the tack shop.
Some trainers prefer starting colts with a hackamore to avoid mouth injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halter - a strap or rope placed around the head of a horse to lead or tie it

Etymology

From Spanish 'jáquima', meaning halter or headstall.
