dilute

1. VERB

/ daɪˈluːt / (AmE), / dɪˈluːt / (BrE)
to make a liquid or solution weaker or less concentrated by adding water or another solvent
make a liquid less concentrated

Examples

She decided to dilute the juice with some water for the children.
If the paint is too thick, you can dilute it to get the desired consistency.
They had to dilute the solution before using it in the experiment.

Synset Examples

cut bourbon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentrate - to make a liquid or substance stronger by removing water or another diluting agent
  • Synonym: to weaken - to reduce in strength, force, or intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'dilutus', past participle of 'diluere' (to wash away, dissolve).

Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ daɪˈluːt / (AmE), / daɪˈluːt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) made weaker or less concentrated by adding another substance, especially a solvent such as water.
made weaker or less concentrated

Examples

The chemist prepared a dilute acid solution for the experiment.
Even dilute bleach must be handled with care to avoid skin irritation.
The flavor was very mild because the syrup was too dilute.

Synset Examples

diluted alcohol
a dilute solution
dilute acetic acid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentrated - (of a substance) having a higher strength or concentration

Etymology

From Latin 'dilutus', past participle of 'diluere' (to wash away, dissolve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00759612-a
Synset POS: a

3. VERB

/ daɪˈluːt / (AmE), / daɪˈluːt / (BrE)
to make something impure or of lower quality by adding a foreign or inferior substance, often by replacing valuable parts with less valuable ones
make impure by adding inferior substances

Examples

Some companies dilute their products with cheaper ingredients to cut costs.
The winemaker was accused of trying to dilute the wine with water.
Adding fillers can dilute the purity and quality of the medicine.

Synset Examples

adulterate liquor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulterate - to make something impure by adding inferior substances

Etymology

From Latin 'dilutus', past participle of 'diluere' ('to wash away, dissolve').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00488684-v
Synset POS: v
