amblyopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæm.bliˈoʊ.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌam.blɪˈəʊ.pɪk / (BrE)
relating to amblyopia, a visual impairment where one or both eyes have reduced vision without detectable damage to the eye itself
relating to impaired vision (amblyopia)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child as amblyopic after a series of vision tests.
An amblyopic patient may struggle to see clearly despite having healthy eyes.
Early treatment is crucial for children with amblyopic conditions to improve their vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amblyopia - visual impairment due to poor development of vision in one eye

Etymology

From Greek amblyōps (dull-eyed), from amblys (dull) + ōps (eye).
