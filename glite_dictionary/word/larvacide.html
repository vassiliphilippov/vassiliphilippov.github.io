larvacide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrvəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɑːvəˌsaɪd / (BrE)
A substance or agent used to kill the larvae of insects.
substance that kills insect larvae
medical

Examples

The city sprayed larvacide in stagnant water to control the mosquito population.
Most larvacide treatments target the early life stages of pests before they become adults.
Using a larvacide can reduce the need for adult insecticides in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insecticide - a substance used to kill insects in general
  • Degree Higher: pesticide - a substance used for destroying pests in general

Etymology

From Latin 'larva' (ghost, mask, but also used for insect larval stage) + '-cide' (from Latin 'caedere', to kill).
