eryngo

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Eryngium, typically having spiny leaves and often blue or purple flower heads.
spiny-leaved plant of the genus Eryngium

Examples

The eryngo thrives in sandy soils and is popular in coastal gardens.
Several species of eryngo are cultivated for their ornamental blue flowers.
Often mistaken for thistles, eryngo has unique, spiky leaves and stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea holly - any of several plants of the genus Eryngium, especially those resembling holly

Etymology

From Spanish "eryngo", from Latin "Eryngium", the genus name.
