yen

1. NOUN

/ jɛn / (AmE), / jen / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Japan, equal to 100 sen.
the basic unit of Japanese currency

Examples

The price of the book is 1,500 yen in Tokyo.
She exchanged her dollars for yen before traveling to Japan.
The value of the yen fluctuated significantly last year.

Etymology

From Japanese 'en', meaning 'round (object)' or 'circle'.

3. NOUN

/ jɛn / (AmE), / jɛn / (BrE)
a strong desire or craving for something
a strong desire or craving

Examples

After years in the city, he developed a yen for the quiet of the countryside.
She always had a yen to travel the world and explore new cultures.
A sudden yen for chocolate made him stop by the bakery on his way home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craving - an intense desire for something
  • Synonym: longing - a strong feeling of wanting something or someone
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Chinese (Cantonese) 'yin' (craving, desire), originally referring to opium craving.
