Congregationalist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a member of the Congregational Church, a Protestant Christian denomination emphasizing the autonomy of each local congregation
member of the Congregational Church

Examples

As a devoted Congregationalist, he attended services every Sunday.
Her grandfather was a prominent Congregationalist minister in the community.
Several early American settlers were Congregationalists seeking religious freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Presbyterian - a member of a Protestant church governed by presbyters
  • Synonym: Episcopalian - a member of the Episcopal Church

Etymology

From 'congregation' + '-alist', denoting adherence to Congregational church principles.

2. ADJ

/ kɑːnɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / kɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a Congregational church or the Congregationalist movement
relating to a Congregational church

Examples

The Congregationalist tradition places authority in the individual congregation rather than in a central hierarchy.
Her ancestors were prominent members of a Congregationalist church in New England.
The building was originally constructed as a Congregationalist meetinghouse in the late eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Presbyterian - relating to the Presbyterian church or system
  • Synonym: Episcopalian - relating to the Episcopal Church or episcopal polity

Etymology

From Congregational + -ist; Congregational referring to a Protestant denomination with church government by congregation.
