Sorbus americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrbəs əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbəs əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (BrE)
A species of mountain ash native to eastern North America, known for its pinnate leaves and clusters of bright red berries.
species of mountain ash tree

Examples

The Sorbus americana is prized for its vibrant autumn foliage and clusters of red berries.
Birds are attracted to the bright berries produced by Sorbus americana in the fall.
Gardeners often plant Sorbus americana as an ornamental tree in parks and yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain ash - a tree of the genus Sorbus, especially those with clusters of bright berries

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Sorbus' (the genus for rowans and mountain ashes) and 'americana' meaning 'of America'.
