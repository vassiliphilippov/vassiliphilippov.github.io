amnestic

1. ADJ

/ æmˈnɛstɪk / (AmE), / æmˈnɛstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by amnesia; associated with memory loss.
relating to amnesia or memory loss
medical

Examples

The patient displayed an amnestic syndrome following the accident.
Some medications can have amnestic side effects, resulting in temporary memory loss.
The study focused on the amnestic effects of head injuries in athletes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesia - loss of memory

Etymology

From Greek amnēstikos (causing forgetfulness), from amnēstia (forgetfulness).
