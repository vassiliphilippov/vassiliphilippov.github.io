replaceable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpleɪsəbl / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪsəbl / (BrE)
able to be replaced by something or someone else; capable of being substituted.
able to be replaced

Examples

The battery in this device is easily replaceable if it stops working.
Most parts in the car are replaceable, making repairs more convenient.
She feared that in her job, she was entirely replaceable and not valued.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreplaceable - impossible to replace

Etymology

From 'replace' + '-able', the latter from Latin '-abilis' meaning 'able to be'.
