pied

1. ADJ

/ paɪd / (AmE), / paɪd / (BrE)
having sections or patches of different colors, often bright or contrasting
having differently colored patches

Examples

The pied wagtail hopped across the lawn, its black and white feathers striking against the grass.
She admired the pied daisies scattered throughout the wildflower meadow.
The jester wore a pied costume made of bright, patchwork fabric.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particolored - having different colors in patches
  • Synonym: piebald - having irregular patches of two colors, typically black and white

Etymology

From Middle English 'pied', from 'pie' (magpie), referring to the bird's black and white coloring.
