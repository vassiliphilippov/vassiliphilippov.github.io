steatite

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiəˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstiː.ə.taɪt / (BrE)
a soft, heavy, compact variety of talc with a soapy feel, used for making hearths, tabletops, and decorative ornaments
soft, heavy variety of talc (mineral)

Examples

The sculpture was carved from steatite, giving it a smooth, polished appearance.
Many ancient seals from the Indus Valley Civilization were made using steatite.
Because of its heat resistance, steatite is often used for laboratory countertops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapstone - a soft rock consisting chiefly of talc, similar to steatite

Etymology

From Greek steatitis, from stear (fat, tallow), referencing the mineral's greasy feel.
