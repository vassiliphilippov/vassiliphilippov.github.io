oppositionality

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɑːzɪʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / əˌpɒzɪʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of opposing or being in active conflict with something, especially in a political, social, or psychological context.
the state of being oppositional; political or social conflict

Examples

Contemporary art often expresses its oppositionality to mainstream political views.
The oppositionality between the two parties led to frequent deadlocks in parliament.
In family therapy, a child's oppositionality may manifest as persistent defiance toward authority figures.

Synset Examples

At present the axis of political oppositionality is centered on the nuke issue, which however is defined by the power relations inherent in entire politics and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposition - the action of opposing or resisting
  • Antonym: concord - agreement or harmony between people or groups

Etymology

From 'oppositional' + '-ity', patterned on 'oppositional' (opposing) + '-ity' (state or quality).
