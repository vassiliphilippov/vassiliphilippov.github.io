Kuchean

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈtʃiːən / (AmE), / kuːˈtʃiːən / (BrE)
a dialect of the Tocharian language once spoken in the regions of Kucha, Qarašähär, and Turpan in Central Asia
dialect of Tocharian from Kucha region

Examples

Linguists have studied Kuchean to better understand the history of Indo-European languages in Central Asia.
The manuscripts discovered near the Silk Road contain texts written in Kuchean.
He specialized in translating ancient Kuchean documents found in the Tarim Basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tocharian - an extinct branch of the Indo-European language family

Etymology

From Kucha (an ancient city in Central Asia) + -an (forming adjectives of origin).
