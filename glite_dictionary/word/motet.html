motet

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈtɛt / (AmE), / məʊˈtɛt / (BrE)
A short piece of unaccompanied choral music with a sacred text, typically sung as part of a church service.
sacred unaccompanied choral composition

Examples

The choir performed a Renaissance motet at the Sunday service.
One of Bach's most famous works is the motet 'Jesu, meine Freude'.
During the concert, several medieval motets were included in the program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthem - a choral composition with English words, often for church use

Etymology

From Old French 'motet', diminutive of 'mot' (word), referring to the use of words in the polyphonic composition.
