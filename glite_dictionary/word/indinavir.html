indinavir

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪnəˌvɪr / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪnəvɪə / (BrE)
a protease inhibitor drug used in the treatment of HIV infection, sold under the trade name Crixivan
HIV protease inhibitor drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed indinavir as part of the patient's antiretroviral therapy.
Recent studies show that indinavir can reduce viral load in HIV-positive individuals.
Some patients experience kidney stones as a side effect of indinavir treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crixivan - trade name for indinavir

Etymology

From the international nonproprietary name, combining elements for 'inhibitor' and 'protease'.
