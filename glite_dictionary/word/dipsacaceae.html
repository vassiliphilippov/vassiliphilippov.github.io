Dipsacaceae

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˌsækəˈsiːi / (AmE), / dɪpˌsakəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly southern European herbs with flowers typically arranged in dense cymose heads, also known as the teasel family
family of European flowering herbs (teasel family)

Examples

The Dipsacaceae family includes plants such as the teasel and scabious.
Botanists have long debated the classification of Dipsacaceae within flowering plants.
Many members of Dipsacaceae are valued for their ornamental flowers and unique heads.

Etymology

From modern Latin Dipsacaceae, based on Dipsacus, the type genus.

2. NOUN

/ dɪpˌsækəˈsiːi / (AmE), / dɪpˌsakəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly southern European herbs with flowers usually in dense cymose heads, commonly known as the teasel family
a family of flowering herbs (teasel family)

Examples

The Dipsacaceae include several species with distinctive prickly flower heads.
Some gardeners cultivate members of the Dipsacaceae for their unusual floral arrangements.
Biologists have recently reclassified many plants formerly in the Dipsacaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - a plant in the genus Dipsacus, member of the Dipsacaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin Dipsacaceae, based on genus Dipsacus, from Greek dipsakos (teasel, thistle).
