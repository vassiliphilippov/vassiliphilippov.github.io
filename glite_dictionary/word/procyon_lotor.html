Procyon lotor

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌsaɪ.ɒn ˈloʊ.tɔr / (AmE), / prəʊˌsʌɪ.ɒn ˈləʊ.tɔː / (BrE)
The scientific name for the North American raccoon, a medium-sized mammal native to North America, known for its dexterous front paws and facial mask.
scientific name for raccoon

Examples

The scientific name for the raccoon is Procyon lotor.
Researchers observed several Procyon lotor foraging near the riverbank.
Many wildlife guides refer to the North American raccoon as Procyon lotor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raccoon - a medium-sized omnivorous mammal native to North America

Etymology

From Latin; 'Procyon' meaning 'before the dog', and 'lotor' meaning 'washer', referring to the raccoon's habit of washing food.
