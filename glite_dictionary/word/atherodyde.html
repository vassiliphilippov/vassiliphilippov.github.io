atherodyde

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈrɒdaɪd / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈrɒdaɪd / (BrE)
a type of simple jet engine that operates only at high speeds and requires an initial high-speed launch to function
simple jet engine requiring high-speed launch

Examples

The atherodyde was tested on experimental aircraft in the 1950s.
Unlike a conventional jet engine, an atherodyde cannot start from rest.
Researchers are developing a new atherodyde design to improve high-speed flight efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramjet - a jet engine that operates by taking in air at high speed, compressing it, and mixing it with fuel
