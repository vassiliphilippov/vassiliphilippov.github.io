barefooted

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrˌfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌfʊtɪd / (BrE)
not wearing any shoes or other covering on the feet.
without shoes

Examples

The children ran barefooted across the cool grass in the morning.
She often prefers to work in the garden barefooted during the summer months.
After losing his shoes in the mud, he walked barefooted the rest of the way home.

Synset Examples

the barefoot boy
shoeless Joe Jackson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barefoot - not wearing any shoes or foot covering

Etymology

Formed from 'barefoot' + '-ed'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02164101-s

2. ADV

/ ˈbɛrˌfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌfʊtɪd / (BrE)
without wearing any shoes or socks on the feet; with bare feet.
without wearing shoes or socks

Examples

The children ran barefooted along the sandy beach, laughing loudly.
She prefers to walk barefooted in the grass on warm summer days.
After losing his shoes in the mud, he continued hiking barefooted.

Synset Examples

he chased her barefoot across the meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barefoot - without shoes or socks; with bare feet (adj/adv form)

Etymology

Formed from 'barefoot' + '-ed'.
