eolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈioʊlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈiːəlɪθ / (BrE)
a roughly chipped stone, once believed to be an early human tool but now generally considered to be naturally formed
primitive stone believed to be an early tool

Examples

Some archaeologists once believed that eoliths represented the earliest human technology.
The authenticity of eolith discoveries has sparked debates within the scientific community.
Museum exhibits sometimes include eoliths as examples of primitive stone artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artifact - an object made or modified by humans, typically of cultural or historical interest

Etymology

From Greek ‘ēōs’ (dawn) + ‘lithos’ (stone), meaning 'dawn stone'.
