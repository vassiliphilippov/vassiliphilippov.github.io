waldmeister

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaltˌmaɪstər / (AmE), / ˈvaltˌmaɪstə / (BrE)
A fragrant perennial herb (Galium odoratum) native to Europe and northern Asia, used as a flavoring and cultivated as a ground cover.
a fragrant European perennial herb

Examples

The traditional May punch in Germany is flavored with waldmeister.
Waldmeister is often used to scent potpourri and sachets.
Gardeners plant waldmeister as an attractive ground cover beneath trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet woodruff - another common name for Galium odoratum

Etymology

From German 'Waldmeister' (literally ‘master of the woods’), referring to its woodland habitat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaltˌmaɪstər / (AmE), / ˈvaltˌmaɪstə / (BrE)
A fragrant herb (sweet woodruff) with dark green leaves, used especially to flavor May wine and other drinks.
fragrant herb used to flavor drinks

Examples

The traditional German May wine is flavored with waldmeister for its unique taste.
Bottles of syrup made from waldmeister are popular in Berlin cafes.
He added dried waldmeister leaves to the punch before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet woodruff - a fragrant plant used as a flavoring, especially in Europe

Etymology

From German Waldmeister, literally 'master of the woods', referring to the plant's woodland habitat.
