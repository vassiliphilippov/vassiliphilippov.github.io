Vallisneria spiralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌvælɪsˈnɛriə spaɪˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˌvalɪsˈnɪərɪə spɪˈreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with long, ribbonlike leaves, native to the Old World and Australia, commonly known as eelgrass and used in aquariums
submerged aquatic plant with ribbonlike leaves

Examples

Many aquarists choose Vallisneria spiralis for its graceful, ribbonlike leaves and ease of care.
In some regions, Vallisneria spiralis is used to help oxygenate ponds and lakes.
The rapid growth of Vallisneria spiralis makes it an excellent choice for freshwater aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - common name for various ribbonlike aquatic plants including Vallisneria spiralis

Etymology

Genus name 'Vallisneria' named after Italian biologist Antonio Vallisneri; specific epithet 'spiralis' refers to its spiral-shaped flower stalk.
