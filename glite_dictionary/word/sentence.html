sentence

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
the period of time that a person is required by a court to spend in prison as punishment for a crime
period of imprisonment as punishment

Examples

The judge gave him a sentence of ten years in prison.
She was released after serving half her sentence for theft.
The lawyer argued for a reduced sentence based on mitigating circumstances.

Synset Examples

he served a prison term of 15 months
his sentence was 5 to 10 years
he is doing time in the county jail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: term - a fixed period of imprisonment or other official punishment

Etymology

From Latin 'sententia' (opinion, judgment), referring to a judicial decision.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.təns / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.təns / (BrE)
(Law) The formal judgment declared by a court in a criminal case, stating the punishment assigned to a person found guilty.
punishment imposed by a court
legal

Examples

The judge will announce the sentence at tomorrow's hearing.
After pleading guilty, he received a five-year sentence in prison.
Her sentence was reduced because she cooperated with the authorities.

Synset Examples

the conviction came as no surprise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they were charged
  • Synonym: verdict - the formal decision or finding made by a jury or judge

Etymology

From Latin sententia (opinion, judgment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01191772-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
to officially declare the punishment given to someone found guilty of a crime in a court of law
declare official punishment in court
legal

Examples

The judge will sentence the defendant tomorrow after reviewing all the evidence.
He was sentenced to five years in prison for fraud.
After the guilty verdict, the court must sentence her according to the law.

Synset Examples

He was condemned to ten years in prison

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acquit - to declare someone not guilty in a court of law
  • Synonym: convict - to find someone guilty of a crime in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin *sententia* (opinion, judgment).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
A set of words that expresses a complete thought, typically containing a subject and a predicate, and following the grammatical rules of a language.
grammatically complete statement

Examples

Please write a sentence that uses all of the vocabulary words.
Every sentence begins with a capital letter and ends with a period, question mark, or exclamation point.
She struggled to form a complete sentence in French during the exam.

Synset Examples

he always spoke in grammatical sentences

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: clause - a grammatical unit smaller than a sentence, usually containing a subject and verb

Etymology

From Latin 'sententia' (thought, opinion, judgment), from 'sentire' (to feel).
