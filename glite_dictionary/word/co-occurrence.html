co-occurrence

1. NOUN

/ koʊ əˈkɝːəns / (AmE), / kəʊ əˈkʌrəns / (BrE)
an event or fact of happening together or at the same time as something else, especially in connection with another event or circumstance
simultaneous or linked occurrence

Examples

There is a strong co-occurrence of smoking and alcohol consumption in the population.
Researchers studied the co-occurrence of certain symptoms among the patients.
The co-occurrence of these two species in the same habitat suggests possible interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincidence - the occurrence of events at the same time by chance

Etymology

From 'co-' meaning 'together' and 'occurrence' (from Latin 'occurrere', to run to meet).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈkɝː.əns / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈkʌr.əns / (BrE)
The fact or occurrence of two or more things happening at the same time or in the same place.
simultaneous occurrence of events

Examples

The co-occurrence of drought and high temperatures worsened the agricultural crisis.
Linguists often analyze the co-occurrence of certain words in spoken language.
Their study focused on the co-occurrence of specific symptoms in patients with the disease.

Synset Examples

the interval determining the coincidence gate is adjustable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincidence - a remarkable concurrence of events or circumstances
  • Synonym: simultaneity - the fact of occurring at the same time

Etymology

Prefix 'co-' (from Latin 'com-', 'with, together') + 'occurrence' (from Latin 'occurrere', to occur).
