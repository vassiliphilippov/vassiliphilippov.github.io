Anemone cylindrica

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni sɪˈlɪndrɪkə / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni sɪˈlɪndrɪkə / (BrE)
A species of anemone native to North America, characterized by cylindrical clusters of fruit that resemble thimbles.
North American anemone species with thimble-shaped fruit

Examples

The prairie was dotted with Anemone cylindrica, its fruit clusters standing upright like tiny thimbles.
Gardeners appreciate Anemone cylindrica for its resilience and unique appearance in wildflower meadows.
During summer hikes, you might notice Anemone cylindrica blooming along woodland edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thimbleweed - another common name for Anemone cylindrica

Etymology

From Greek anemōnē (windflower) and Latin cylindricus (cylindrical), referring to the plant's shape.
