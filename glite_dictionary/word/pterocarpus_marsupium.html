Pterocarpus marsupium

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛroʊˈkɑrpəs mɑrˈsuːpiəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkɑːpəs mɑːˈsuːpiəm / (BrE)
an East Indian tree whose resin or extract is used medicinally and in tanning
East Indian medicinal tree

Examples

The extract of Pterocarpus marsupium is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to manage diabetes.
A large Pterocarpus marsupium tree grew on the outskirts of the village, valued for its medicinal resin.
Researchers studied Pterocarpus marsupium to explore new natural compounds for pharmaceutical use.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + karpos (fruit) and Latin marsupium (pouch), referring to the shape of the fruit pod.
