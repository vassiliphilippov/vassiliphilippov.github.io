impertinent

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɜrtənənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːtɪnənt / (BrE)
Not relevant or pertinent to the matter under consideration; irrelevant.
irrelevant; not pertinent

Examples

He brought up several impertinent details that had nothing to do with the discussion.
Please avoid making impertinent comments during the meeting.
Most of the questions in the survey were impertinent to our actual concerns.

Synset Examples

an issue extraneous to the debate
the price was immaterial
mentioned several impertinent facts before finally coming to the point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevant - not related to the matter at hand
  • Antonym: pertinent - relevant to the matter under consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'impertinens', from 'im-' (not) + 'pertinens' (pertinent, relevant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01983596-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɚtɪnənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːtɪnənt / (BrE)
Having a lively, bold, and exuberant quality, often in a way that is playfully disrespectful.
lively or boldly playful in manner

Examples

Her impertinent sense of humor kept the party lively and unexpected.
There was an impertinent charm in his quick wit and ready smile.
The child's impertinent questions made everyone in the room laugh.

Synset Examples

a certain irreverent gaiety and ease of manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pert - lively, bold, or cheeky in a playful way
  • Synonym: irreverent - showing a lack of respect in a playful way

Etymology

From Latin 'impertinens' (not pertinent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02287824-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɝː.tən.ənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜː.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
rude or disrespectful, especially by being boldly forward in a way that is not appropriate
rudely bold or disrespectful

Examples

It was impertinent of her to question the teacher's decision in front of everyone.
The reporter's impertinent questions angered the politician.
Children can sometimes be impertinent without realizing they are being disrespectful.

Synset Examples

don't be fresh with me
impertinent of a child to lecture a grownup
an impudent boy given to insulting strangers
Don't get wise with me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudent - not showing due respect for another person; impertinent
  • Antonym: pertinent - relevant or appropriate to a particular matter

Etymology

From Latin 'impertinens' (not belonging), from 'in-' (not) + 'pertinens' (pertinent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00206887-s
Synset POS: s
