to mythologize

1. VERB

/ mɪˈθɑːlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to create or treat something as a myth, often by exaggerating or idealizing facts or events
to make or treat as a myth

Examples

The new biography tends to mythologize the achievements of the artist, ignoring her flaws.
Historians warn against attempts to mythologize national leaders of the past.
Over time, people began to mythologize his life story, turning ordinary events into legends.

Synset Examples

The poet mythologized that the King had three sons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something more appealing or interesting than it really is
  • Antonym: to debunk - to expose the falseness or hollowness of a myth, idea, or belief

Etymology

From Greek mythologein (to relate myths), via French mythologiser.

2. VERB

/ mɪˈθɑl.əˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒl.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
To make something into a myth or to treat it as legendary, often giving it exaggerated, symbolic, or idealized status.
make into a myth

Examples

The film helped to mythologize the life of the famous musician.
Writers often mythologize historical events to make them more compelling.
Over time, people began to mythologize his achievements, turning him into a cultural icon.

Synset Examples

The Europeans have mythicized Rte. 66

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or interesting than it really is
  • Antonym: to demythologize - to remove mythical elements from a narrative or belief

Etymology

From Greek mythologein (to relate myths) via French mythologiser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00134821-v
Synset POS: v
