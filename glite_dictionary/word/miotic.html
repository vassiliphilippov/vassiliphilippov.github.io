miotic

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈɒtɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing constriction of the pupil of the eye
causing constriction of the pupil
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a miotic agent to help constrict the patient's pupils.
Pilocarpine is a commonly used miotic drug in ophthalmology.
Exposure to certain chemicals can have a miotic effect on the eyes.

Synset Examples

a miotic drug

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriatic - causing dilation of the pupil of the eye

Etymology

From Greek 'meiōtikos' (diminishing), related to 'myein' (to close).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
a drug that causes constriction of the pupil of the eye
drug that constricts the pupil
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a miotic to reduce the patient's intraocular pressure.
After receiving the miotic, the patient's pupils became noticeably smaller.
Ophthalmologists may use a miotic in the treatment of glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriatic - a drug that causes dilation of the pupil

Etymology

From Greek 'meioun' (to lessen) and '-tic' (forming nouns and adjectives).

WordNet ID: oewn-03778414-n
Synset POS: n
