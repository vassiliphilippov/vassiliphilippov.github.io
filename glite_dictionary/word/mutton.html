mutton

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌtən / (AmE), / ˈmʌtən / (BrE)
the flesh of a mature sheep used as food
meat from a mature sheep

Examples

The restaurant specializes in slow-cooked mutton with aromatic spices.
He prefers the flavor of mutton to lamb in traditional stews.
After the festival, the family prepared a feast with roast mutton.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lamb - meat from a young sheep

Etymology

From Old French *moton* (sheep), from Medieval Latin *multo, multonem* (ram, sheep).
