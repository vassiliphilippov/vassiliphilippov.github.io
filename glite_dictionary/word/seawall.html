seawall

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌwɔːl / (BrE)
A strong wall or embankment built along the shore to protect land against erosion or flooding from the sea.
wall to protect against sea erosion

Examples

The old seawall was reinforced to protect the town from storm surges.
Waves crashed against the seawall during last night's storm.
Local officials inspected the cracks in the seawall before planning repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakwater - A barrier built out into the sea to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves
  • Synonym: levee - An embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'wall', indicating its function as a barrier against the sea.
