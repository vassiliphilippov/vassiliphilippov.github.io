Edgar

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.ɡɑr / (AmE), / ˈɛd.ɡɑː / (BrE)
(c. 943–975) King of England from 959 to 975, known as Edgar the Peaceful; younger brother of Edwy.
King of England (959–975); known as Edgar the Peaceful

Examples

Edgar was crowned King of England in 973 at Bath.
Historians often refer to Edgar as 'the Peaceful' due to his stable reign.
The coins minted during Edgar's rule are among the finest of their era.

Etymology

From Old English 'Ēadgār', meaning 'rich, prosperous spear'.
