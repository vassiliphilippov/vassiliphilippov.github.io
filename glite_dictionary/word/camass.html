camass

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæs / (AmE), / kəˈmas / (BrE)
A North American plant of the genus Camassia, with grass-like leaves and star-shaped blue or purple flowers, cultivated for its edible bulb.
a plant of the genus Camassia

Examples

The indigenous people harvested camass bulbs as a staple food for centuries.
Camass fields bloom with blue flowers in the spring across the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists study the distribution of camass in wet meadows and grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - plant of the genus Camassia with edible bulbs

Etymology

From Nez Perce Native American word 'qém’es'.
