personnel

1. NOUN

/ pɝː.səˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.səˈnɛl / (BrE)
the department within an organization that is responsible for hiring, training, and managing employees and policies related to staff
department managing employees

Examples

If you have any questions about your contract, please contact the personnel department.
The company’s personnel office handles all employee benefits and training programs.
After the merger, several changes were made in the personnel department’s policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human resources - the department dealing with hiring and employee relations

Etymology

From French 'personnel' (staff), from 'personne' (person).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɝː.səˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.səˈnel / (BrE)
the people who are employed in an organization or engaged in an activity, especially those who are subject to orders or directives
people employed or involved in an organization

Examples

The company is hiring new personnel to meet the demands of its growing business.
All personnel must attend the safety training session this Friday.
Military personnel were deployed to the region to assist with disaster relief efforts.

Synset Examples

a public force is necessary to give security to the rights of citizens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - the employees of an organization
  • Synonym: workforce - all the people working for a company or country
  • Antonym: management - the people in charge of an organization, contrasted with personnel

Etymology

From French 'personnel', from Latin 'personalis' (personal).
