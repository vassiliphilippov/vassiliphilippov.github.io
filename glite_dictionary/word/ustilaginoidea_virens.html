Ustilaginoidea virens

1. NOUN

/ juːˌstɪlədʒɪˈnɔɪdiə vəˈrɛnz / (AmE), / juːˌstɪlədʒɪˈnɔɪdiːə vɪˈrɛnz / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes green smut disease in rice plants
fungus causing green smut in rice
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of Ustilaginoidea virens have been linked to reduced rice harvests in several regions.
Researchers are developing new methods to control Ustilaginoidea virens infection in paddy fields.
The rice crop turned yellow after being affected by the fungus Ustilaginoidea virens.

Etymology

From Modern Latin genus name Ustilaginoidea (from Ustilago, a smut fungus genus) + species name virens (Latin for 'green').
