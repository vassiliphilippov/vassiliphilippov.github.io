Centranthus

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrænθəs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtranθəs / (BrE)
A genus of southern European herbs and subshrubs, including several ornamental species such as the red valerian.
genus of southern European herbs and subshrubs

Examples

Centranthus is often grown in gardens for its attractive flowers.
Some species in the Centranthus genus are popular among pollinators like bees and butterflies.
The most widely cultivated Centranthus is commonly known as red valerian.

Etymology

From Greek kentron (spur) and anthos (flower).

2. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrænθəs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtranθəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native to southern Europe, including herbs and subshrubs such as the red valerian.
genus of southern European herbs and subshrubs

Examples

The Centranthus genus includes several species admired for their ornamental flowers.
Gardeners often plant Centranthus along borders for its long-lasting blooms.
Species in the Centranthus group are well suited to dry, rocky soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'kentron' (spur) + 'anthos' (flower), referring to the shape of the flower.
