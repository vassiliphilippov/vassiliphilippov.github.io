blueweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌwiːd / (BrE)
a coarse, bristly European plant with spikes of blue flowers, now also found in North America
a bristly blue-flowered European weed

Examples

The fields were dotted with patches of blueweed in full bloom.
She identified the prickly stemmed plant as blueweed, a common sight along rural roadsides.
Efforts to remove blueweed have increased since it began invading local grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper's bugloss - another common name for Echium vulgare
