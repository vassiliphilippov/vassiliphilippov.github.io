marketable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrkətəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be sold or suitable for sale in a market.
able to be sold; fit for sale

Examples

The company's latest product is highly marketable and appeals to a wide audience.
Only marketable crops were harvested for export, while the rest were used for local consumption.
She took steps to make her artwork more marketable by adapting to current trends.

Synset Examples

the marketable surplus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sellable - able to be sold
  • Antonym: unsellable - not able to be sold

Etymology

From 'market' + '-able', modeled after Late Latin 'mercatibilis' (fit to be bought).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrkətəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkətəbəl / (BrE)
(of a skill, quality, or product) able to be sold easily or in demand, especially by employers.
in demand or easily sold

Examples

The company is looking for employees with marketable skills.
His experience and certifications make him highly marketable in the job market.
She took extra courses to ensure her degree would be marketable.

Synset Examples

marketable skills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saleable - able to be sold; fit for sale
  • Antonym: unmarketable - not able to be sold or not in demand

Etymology

From market + -able, dating from the late 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02069402-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrkətəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtəbəl / (BrE)
fit to be offered for sale; able or suitable to be sold
suitable to be sold

Examples

The company grows only marketable fruits to maximize their profits.
Many antiques were restored to a marketable condition before being auctioned.
Due to excessive bruising, the apples were not considered marketable.

Synset Examples

marketable produce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salable - able to be sold or fit for sale

Etymology

From 'market' + '-able', after French 'marchandisable'.
