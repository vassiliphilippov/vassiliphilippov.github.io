Skinnerian

1. ADJ

/ skɪˈnɛriən / (AmE), / skɪˈnɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of B. F. Skinner or his theory of behaviorist psychology
relating to Skinner's behaviorist psychology

Examples

The psychologist employed Skinnerian techniques to encourage positive behaviors in his patients.
Many classrooms still use Skinnerian principles, such as reward and repetition, to shape student behavior.
Her approach to therapy is rooted in Skinnerian theories of conditioning and response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behaviorist - adhering to the psychological school of behaviorism

Etymology

Formed from the name of B. F. Skinner (1904–1990), American psychologist, with the adjectival suffix -ian.

WordNet ID: oewn-03039052-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ skɪˈnɛriən / (AmE), / skɪˈnɪəriən / (BrE)
a person who follows or supports the theories or methods of B. F. Skinner, especially in psychology or behaviorism
follower of B. F. Skinner's theories

Examples

As a dedicated Skinnerian, she focused her research on observable behavior rather than unconscious motives.
Some Skinnerians argue that reinforcement is the key to effective teaching.
Among psychologists, the debate between Freudians and Skinnerians has shaped the field for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behaviorist - person who studies or favors behaviorism
  • Antonym: Freudian - follower of Sigmund Freud's theories

Etymology

From the name of B. F. Skinner (1904–1990), American psychologist, + -ian (denoting followers or advocates).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10625016-n
Synset POS: n
