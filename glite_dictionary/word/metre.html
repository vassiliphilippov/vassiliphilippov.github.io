metre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
the basic unit of length in the International System of Units, equal to 100 centimetres or approximately 39.37 inches
unit of length (SI)

Examples

The Olympic pool must be exactly 50 metres long.
He ran a distance of one hundred metres in record time.
The building stands over thirty metres tall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - alternative spelling, chiefly American English, for the unit of length

Etymology

From French *mètre*, from Greek *metron* (measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
The rhythmic arrangement of beats or syllables in poetry or music, typically marked by regular intervals or patterns.
poetic or musical rhythm pattern
british

Examples

The poem follows a strict metre that enhances its musical quality.
Composers often experiment with different metres to create unique rhythms in their music.
Understanding the metre is essential for reading classical poetry aloud correctly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the American English spelling of 'metre'

Etymology

From Greek 'metron' (measure) via Latin and Old French.
