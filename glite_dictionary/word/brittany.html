Brittany

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪt̬əni / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtəni / (BrE)
a former province in northwestern France, located on a peninsula between the English Channel and the Bay of Biscay, known for its distinct cultural identity and Celtic heritage.
former province in northwestern France

Examples

She traveled to Brittany to explore its rugged coastline and charming villages.
The traditional music of Brittany reflects its strong Celtic roots.
Many people visit Brittany for its beautiful beaches and fresh seafood.

Etymology

From French 'Bretagne', from Latin 'Britannia', originally referring to Britain but later applied to this region of France settled by Britons.
