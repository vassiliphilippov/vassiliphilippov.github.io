rabato

1. NOUN

/ rəˈbeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / rəˈbɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a wired or starched collar made of elaborate lace, popular in the 17th century
17th-century lace collar
archaic

Examples

The queen appeared at court wearing a magnificent rabato adorned with pearls.
In the portrait, the gentleman's rabato frames his face in delicate lace.
Fashion of the era often included a stiff rabato to complement elaborate gowns.

Etymology

From Italian rabatto, meaning 'turned down', referring to the folded collar.
