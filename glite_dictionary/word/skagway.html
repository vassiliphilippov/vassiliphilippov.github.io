Skagway

1. PROPN

/ ˈskæɡ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈskæɡ.weɪ / (BrE)
A town in southeastern Alaska, historically known as a gateway to the Klondike during the Alaska Gold Rush.
town in southeastern Alaska; gold rush gateway

Examples

The historic town of Skagway attracts thousands of tourists each summer for its gold rush heritage.
During the Klondike Gold Rush, Skagway was a bustling entry point for miners heading north.
A ferry from Juneau will take you directly to Skagway along the scenic Inside Passage.
