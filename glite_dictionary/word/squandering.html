squandering

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːndɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of spending resources lavishly and wastefully
wasteful spending of resources

Examples

The squandering of valuable resources sparked outrage among environmentalists.
Her squandering of time and money led to serious consequences.
He regretted the squandering of opportunities he once took for granted.

Synset Examples

more wasteful than the squandering of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - the careless use of resources resulting in loss
  • Antonym: conservation - the act of protecting and carefully using resources

Etymology

From verb squander (origin unknown, possibly from obsolete 'squander' meaning to scatter).
