naphthoquinone

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæfθoʊkɪˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌnafθəʊkɪˈnəʊn / (BrE)
a chemical compound that acts as a fat-soluble vitamin, important for blood clotting
compound; vitamin involved in blood clotting
medical

Examples

The presence of naphthoquinone in leafy greens makes them beneficial for blood health.
Scientists are studying naphthoquinone derivatives for potential medicinal uses.
A deficiency in naphthoquinone can lead to problems with blood clotting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin K - a fat-soluble vitamin essential for blood clotting

Etymology

From naphthalene and quinone, combining chemical names.
