Nahum

1. PROPN

/ ˈneɪəm / (AmE), / ˈneɪəm / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet of the 7th century BC; also, the book of prophecy attributed to him in the Old Testament.
Hebrew minor prophet of the 7th century BC

Examples

The Book of Nahum is part of the Old Testament's minor prophets.
Biblical scholars continue to debate the historical context of Nahum's prophecies.
Many churches include readings from Nahum during their study of the Hebrew prophets.

Etymology

From Hebrew ‎נַחוּם‎ (Naḥūm), meaning 'comforter'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈneɪəm / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.əm / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecy of Nahum concerning the fall of Nineveh.
Old Testament book; prophecy of Nahum

Examples

My favorite passage comes from the book of Nahum in the Old Testament.
Scholars debate the historical accuracy of Nahum's prophecy about Nineveh.
During Bible study, we discussed the themes of justice and retribution found in Nahum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old Testament - The first part of the Christian Bible, based mainly on the Hebrew Scriptures

From Hebrew 'Naḥūm', meaning 'comforter'.
