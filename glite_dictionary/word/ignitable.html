ignitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈnaɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪtəbəl / (BrE)
capable of catching fire and burning easily; combustible.
capable of catching fire

Examples

The solvent is highly ignitable and must be stored carefully.
Old newspapers are very ignitable, so keep them away from open flames.
Firefighters inspected the warehouse for ignitable materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combustible - able to catch fire and burn easily
  • Synonym: inflammable - easily set on fire
  • Antonym: nonflammable - not able to catch fire easily

Etymology

From Latin 'ignis' (fire) + English suffix '-able'.
