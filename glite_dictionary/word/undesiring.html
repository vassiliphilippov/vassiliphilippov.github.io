undesiring

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈzaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈzaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
having or feeling no desire for something; lacking wish or inclination for something
lacking desire or wish

Examples

She remained undesiring of any further attention after the long meeting.
An undesiring attitude toward fame is rare in the entertainment industry.
He looked at the dessert table, undesiring anything sweet after a heavy meal.

Synset Examples

a very private man, totally undesirous of public office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desiring - having or feeling desire

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'desiring' (present participle of 'desire').
