unarmored

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑrmɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːməd / (BrE)
(of a person, vehicle, or structure) not provided with protective armor.
without protective armor

Examples

The convoy was vulnerable because it included several unarmored vehicles.
He entered the tournament unarmored, relying solely on his skill and speed.
The historian explained that early warships were often unarmored and easily damaged by enemy fire.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armored - provided with protective armor

Etymology

Formed by the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'armored' (provided with armor).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːrmərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːməd / (BrE)
(of an animal or object) not having a protective covering or armor.
without protective covering or armor

Examples

The unarmored fish was more vulnerable to predators in the open water.
Some prehistoric species evolved from armored to unarmored forms over time.
Unlike turtles, frogs are unarmored and rely on camouflage for protection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armored - having a protective covering or armor
  • Synonym: bare - without covering; lacking protection

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'armored' (having protective armor).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
