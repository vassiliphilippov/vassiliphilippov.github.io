sibilant

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪbɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced by forcing air through a narrow channel, resulting in a hissing or hushing sound, as in 's', 'z', or 'sh'.
speech sound with hissing or hushing quality

Examples

The teacher demonstrated how the sibilant 's' sound can be confusing for language learners.
Her accent was characterized by a strongly sibilant pronunciation of certain consonants.
The word 'hiss' contains a highly sibilant sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fricative - speech sound produced by forcing air through a narrow passage

Etymology

From Latin sibilant-, sibilans, present participle of sibilare (to hiss).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪbələnt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪlənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound characterized by a hissing or hushing noise, such as that produced by 's', 'sh', or 'z'
a hissing consonant sound (e.g. 's', 'sh')

Examples

In English, the letter 's' usually represents a sibilant sound.
The child's lisp made the sibilant at the start of 'snake' sound softer.
Linguists study how different languages use the sibilant in their phonetic systems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fricative - a consonant produced by forcing air through a narrow opening
  • Antonym: vowel - a speech sound produced without significant constriction of airflow

Etymology

From Latin 'sibilant-', present participle of 'sibilare' (to hiss).
