Erythrina variegata

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɪnə ˌværiˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɪnə ˌværɪˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized thorny tree native to tropical Asia and northern Australia, known for its dense clusters of scarlet or crimson flowers and black seeds
ornamental Asian and Australian tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Erythrina variegata is often planted along roadsides for its striking appearance.
Local birds are attracted to the Erythrina variegata when it blooms in spring.
The wood of Erythrina variegata is sometimes used for light construction work.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: *erythros* (red), referring to its bright red flowers, and *variegata* (variegated).
