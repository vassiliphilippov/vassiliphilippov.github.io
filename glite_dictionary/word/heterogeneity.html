heterogeneity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬.ə.roʊ.dʒəˈniː.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.ər.əʊ.dʒəˈniː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being diverse in character or content; the presence of different elements or types within a group
diversity or variety within a group

Examples

There is considerable heterogeneity in the treatment approaches used by different hospitals.
Environmental heterogeneity can lead to greater biodiversity in ecosystems.
The study accounted for the heterogeneity of the patient population.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homogeneity - the quality or state of being all the same or very similar

Etymology

From Greek heterogenēs (of different kinds), from hetero- (other, different) + genos (kind, race) via Medieval Latin heterogeneitas.
