Ardisia paniculata

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈdɪziə pəˌnɪkjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɪziə pəˌnɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub or small tree with brown wood and dark berries, belonging to the genus Ardisia.
tropical American shrub or small tree

Examples

The garden featured a rare Ardisia paniculata flourishing in the shade.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Ardisia paniculata for its traditional uses.
The dark berries of Ardisia paniculata attract many birds in the region.

Etymology

From genus name Ardisia, from Greek 'ardis' (point); 'paniculata' from Latin 'panicula' (tuft, panicle).
