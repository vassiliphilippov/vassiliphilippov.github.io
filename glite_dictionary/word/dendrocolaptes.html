Dendrocolaptes

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊkoʊˈlæptiːz / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrəkəʊˈlæptiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the bird family Dendrocolaptidae, comprising the woodcreepers found in Central and South America.
genus of woodcreeper birds

Examples

The genus Dendrocolaptes includes several species commonly known as woodcreepers.
Ornithologists have studied the nesting habits of Dendrocolaptes in Amazonian forests.
A Dendrocolaptes was spotted scaling the bark of a tree in search of insects.

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + kolapto (to peck), referring to tree-creeping habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊkoʊˈlæptiːz / (AmE), / ˌdendrəkəʊˈlæptiːz / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Dendrocolaptidae, known as woodcreepers, native to Central and South America.
genus of woodcreeper birds

Examples

Several species in the genus Dendrocolaptes are important for maintaining forest ecosystems.
The Amazonian Dendrocolaptes builds its nest in tree cavities high above the ground.
Ornithologists often study Dendrocolaptes to understand adaptations to arboreal life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcreeper - any bird of the subfamily Dendrocolaptinae, to which Dendrocolaptes belongs
  • Degree Higher: Dendrocolaptidae - the bird family including the genus Dendrocolaptes

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + kolaptes (pecker).
