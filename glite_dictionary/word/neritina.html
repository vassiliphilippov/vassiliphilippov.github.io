Neritina

1. NOUN

/ nɛrɪˈtiːnə / (AmE), / nɛrɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized aquatic snails in the family Neritidae.
genus of aquatic snails

Examples

Several species in the genus Neritina are popular in freshwater aquariums due to their attractive shells.
The Neritina genus is widely distributed in both fresh and brackish water habitats.
Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Neritina genus in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Neritidae - the family of snails to which Neritina belongs

Etymology

From New Latin Neritina, diminutive of Nerita, a related genus name, ultimately from Greek Nērítēs (a sea snail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɛrɪˈtiːnə / (AmE), / ˌnɛrɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a genus of ornately marked and brightly colored snails found in brackish waters, often admired for their decorative shells
a genus of colorful aquatic snails

Examples

The aquarium enthusiast introduced several Neritina snails to help keep the glass clean.
Brackish riverbanks may host Neritina species with intricately patterned shells.
Collectors prize certain Neritina varieties for their bright, ornamental appearances.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the Latin 'Nerita', a sea snail, originally from Greek 'Nerites'.
