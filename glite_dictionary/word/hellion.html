hellion

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛliən / (AmE), / ˈhɛliən / (BrE)
a rowdy, mischievous, or troublemaking person, especially a child or young man
rowdy or mischievous person

Examples

The three hellions next door are always getting into some kind of mischief.
She was a real hellion as a child, causing chaos wherever she went.
Teachers found it difficult to keep the group of hellions under control during recess.

Synset Examples

he chased the young hellions out of his yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person, especially a child or young man
  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who causes trouble, especially through mischief or unruly behavior
  • Antonym: angel - a well-behaved or exceptionally good person

Etymology

Probably alteration of dialectal 'hallion', from Irish 'allúin' (wretch, clown).
