banksia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋksiə / (AmE), / ˈbaŋksɪə / (BrE)
A shrub or tree of the genus Banksia, native to Australia, with leathery leaves, showy yellow flower spikes, and conelike fruit bearing winged seeds.
Australian shrub or tree with showy yellow flowers

Examples

Several species of banksia grow along the Australian coast, easily identified by their striking flower spikes.
Gardeners often plant banksia for its attractive blooms and appeal to native birds.
The banksia seed pods are used in crafts because of their unusual, woody texture.

Etymology

Named after Sir Joseph Banks (1743–1820), the English botanist who first collected specimens in Australia.
