supermarket

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpərˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a large self-service store that sells groceries, dairy products, and household goods, typically organized into aisles
large self-service grocery store

Examples

I'm going to the supermarket to pick up some groceries for dinner.
Many people prefer shopping at a supermarket because of the wide variety of products available.
After work, she stopped by the supermarket to buy milk and bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grocery store - a retail store where food and related products are sold
  • Degree Lower: convenience store - a small retail store that stocks a range of everyday items
  • Degree Higher: hypermarket - an extensive retail store combining a supermarket and a department store

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (large, above) and 'market' (place for buying goods); in use since the 1930s.
