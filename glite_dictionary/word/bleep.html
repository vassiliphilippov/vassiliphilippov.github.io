bleep

1. NOUN

/ bliːp / (AmE), / bliːp / (BrE)
a short, high-pitched sound used as a signal or warning, especially from an electronic device
short electronic warning sound

Examples

The microwave made a bleep when the timer finished.
He woke up to the bleep of his alarm clock every morning.
A loud bleep signaled that someone was trying to call on the walkie-talkie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beep - a short, high sound as made by a horn or electronic device
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07391682-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ bliːp / (AmE), / bliːp / (BrE)
(of an electronic device) to emit a single short, high-pitched sound or signal.
emit a short high-pitched signal (electronic device)

Examples

The alarm will bleep if you press this button.
My phone bleeped to notify me of a new message.
Every time the timer finishes, it starts bleeping loudly.

Synset Examples

The computer bleeped away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beep - to make a short, high-pitched sound (typically by a vehicle or device)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02191617-v
Synset POS: v
