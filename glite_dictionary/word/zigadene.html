zigadene

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪɡəˌdin / (AmE), / ˈzɪɡədiːn / (BrE)
any of several North American plants of the genus Zigadenus, with grass-like leaves and clusters of mostly white, poisonous flowers
poisonous plant of the genus Zigadenus

Examples

Many wild pastures contain zigadene, which can be hazardous to grazing livestock.
Botanists collected a specimen of zigadene for further study of its toxic properties.
The white flowers of the zigadene stand out against its blue-green leaves.

Etymology

From the New Latin genus name Zigadenus, derived from Greek 'zigon' (yoke) and 'aden' (gland).
