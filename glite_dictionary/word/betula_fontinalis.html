Betula fontinalis

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtjuːlə fɒnˈtaɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə fɒnˈtaɪnælɪs / (BrE)
A species of birch native to the western United States, resembling the paper birch but with brownish bark.
a birch species of the western US

Examples

The Betula fontinalis grows along streams in the Rocky Mountains.
Unlike other birches, Betula fontinalis is easily recognized by its brownish bark.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Betula fontinalis in Colorado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper birch - a related species of birch with white bark

Etymology

From Latin: 'Betula' (birch) and 'fontinalis' (of springs or fountains), referencing its typical habitat.
