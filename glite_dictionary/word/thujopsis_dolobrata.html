Thujopsis dolobrata

1. PROPN

/ θuˈdʒɑpsɪs ˌdoʊloʊˈbreɪtə / (AmE), / θjuːˈdʒɒpsɪs ˌdəʊləˈbreɪtə / (BrE)
a slow-growing, medium-large evergreen tree native to Japan, grown as an ornamental for its attractive foliage
Japanese ornamental evergreen tree

Examples

The garden's centerpiece is a beautiful Thujopsis dolobrata that stands out among the other evergreens.
Many landscapers choose Thujopsis dolobrata for its unique foliage and resistance to pests.
Botanists admire the slow growth and ornamental value of Thujopsis dolobrata in Japanese gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborvitae - a type of evergreen tree often used ornamentally; related conifer

Etymology

From the genus name 'Thujopsis' (Greek 'thuja' + '-opsis' meaning 'resembling') and the species name 'dolobrata'.
