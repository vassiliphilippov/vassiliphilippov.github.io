garpike

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrˌpaɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːˌpaɪk / (BrE)
A large, primitive predatory fish native to North America, with a long snout and hard, diamond-shaped scales.
long-snouted North American fish

Examples

The garpike is known for its long, needlelike teeth and armored scales.
Fishermen on the Mississippi River often encounter garpike in the shallow waters.
Biologists study the garpike to learn about the evolution of fishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gar - primitive predatory fish with elongated jaws, also known as garpike

Etymology

From 'gar' (an old name for the fish) + 'pike' (a type of predatory fish).
