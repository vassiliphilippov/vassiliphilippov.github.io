2. NOUN

/ haɪˌmɛnˈænθərə / (AmE), / hɪˌmɛnˈænθərə / (BrE)
A genus of slender evergreen shrubs native to Australia and New Zealand.
genus of shrubs from Australia and New Zealand

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species within the Hymenanthera genus in New Zealand forests.
The stiff, narrow leaves are characteristic of Hymenanthera shrubs.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Hymenanthera for its evergreen foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a woody plant smaller than a tree, often with multiple stems

Etymology

From Greek 'hymen' (membrane) + 'anthera' (anther), referencing the botanical features of the flowers.
