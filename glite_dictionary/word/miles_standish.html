Miles Standish

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪlz ˈstændɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlz ˈstandɪʃ / (BrE)
(1584–1656) An English military officer and colonist who served as a leader and military adviser of the Pilgrims in the Plymouth Colony in early America.
English military leader of early Plymouth Colony

Examples

Many historians regard Miles Standish as instrumental in the survival of the Plymouth Colony.
The story of Miles Standish is taught in American history classes across the country.
Longfellow wrote a poem about Miles Standish and his experiences in the New World.
