sponsor

1. VERB

/ ˈspɑnsər / (AmE), / ˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
to take responsibility for or to support and present an idea, project, or proposal, often in an official or formal way
to officially support or back a proposal or project

Examples

The senator agreed to sponsor the new environmental legislation in Congress.
If no one is willing to sponsor the proposal, it cannot be considered for funding.
Several countries have decided to sponsor the peace talks in the region.

Synset Examples

The senator announced that he would sponsor the health care plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or backing to something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist something formally

Etymology

From Latin sponsor (guarantor), from spondere (to promise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02224930-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈspɑnsər / (AmE), / ˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
to provide support for a person, activity, or organization, often by giving money, or to take responsibility for something or someone
to support or take responsibility for

Examples

The company agreed to sponsor the local football team for the next season.
She decided to sponsor a child in need through a charitable organization.
Several major brands sponsor the annual music festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or backing to
  • Synonym: to patronize - to give financial or other support to a person or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'spondere' (to pledge), via Late Latin 'sponsorem' (guarantor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02224722-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɑnsər / (AmE), / ˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
a person or organization that supports, finances, or champions somebody or something, especially by providing funds or resources
person or group supporting or funding something

Examples

The marathon would not have been possible without a generous sponsor to cover the expenses.
As a sponsor, the company provided scholarships to dozens of students last year.
Each participant was required to find a sponsor for their charity fundraising ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patron - a person who gives financial or other support to a person, organization, or cause
  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or other help to a person or cause
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who opposes or competes against another

Etymology

From Latin 'spondere' (to promise), via Old French and Late Latin 'sponsor' (guarantor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10427699-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈspɑnsər / (AmE), / ˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
a person who formally presents or recommends someone for an award, degree, position, or introduction
person who presents or recommends someone

Examples

Dr. Bennett was proud to be the sponsor for the new member of the academic society.
At the ceremony, her sponsor gave a heartfelt speech about her achievements.
Each candidate must have a sponsor who can vouch for their character and qualifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends another
  • Degree Lower: nominee - a person who is nominated for something

Etymology

From Latin sponsor (guarantor, surety), from spondere (to pledge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10485764-n
Synset POS: n
