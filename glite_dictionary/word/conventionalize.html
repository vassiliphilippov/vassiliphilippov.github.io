to conventionalize

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or make something according to conventional styles or norms, especially in art or ideas
make conform to conventions or norms

Examples

Many artists conventionalize natural forms to create patterns in their work.
The architect chose to conventionalize the design, adhering to industry standards.
Over time, language tends to conventionalize expressions that were once novel.

Synset Examples

a stylized female head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stylize - to design or represent something according to a style rather than naturalistic depiction

Etymology

From 'conventional' + '-ize', indicating to make or render conventional.

WordNet ID: oewn-01691464-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something conform to established customs or conventions
make conventional; adapt to established customs

Examples

The new dance form was quickly conventionalized to suit traditional audiences.
Some artists refuse to conventionalize their work, preferring innovative techniques.
Company protocol has conventionalized even casual conversations among employees.

Synset Examples

conventionalized behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conform - to comply with rules, standards, or laws
  • Antonym: to individualize - to make something distinctive or individual

Etymology

Formed from 'conventional' + '-ize', following a common English verb-forming pattern.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00528447-v
Synset POS: v
