duologue

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː.əˌlɔːɡ / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.əˌlɒɡ / (BrE)
A conversation or dialogue between two people, especially in a play or literary work.
conversation between two people

Examples

The actors prepared a duologue for the audition, showcasing their chemistry.
The film opens with a gripping duologue between the hero and his rival.
During drama class, we were asked to perform a duologue of our choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialogue - a conversation between two or more people

Etymology

From duo (Latin, 'two') + dialogue, patterned on model of 'dialogue'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈduːəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəlɒɡ / (BrE)
a conversation or part of a play or script involving only two characters or actors
scripted conversation between two actors

Examples

The play opens with a tense duologue between the main characters.
The director praised the actors' chemistry during the duologue in the second act.
Writing a compelling duologue requires an ear for natural conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people or characters

Etymology

From French 'duologue', from Latin 'duo' (two) + Greek 'logos' (speech).
