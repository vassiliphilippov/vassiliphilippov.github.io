firebrand

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌbrænd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbrænd / (BrE)
a piece of wood that is burning or has been burned, especially one used to start a fire
burning or burned piece of wood

Examples

He waved a firebrand over his head to light the way.
The villagers used a firebrand to light the bonfire.
A firebrand was thrown into the pile of dry leaves, setting them ablaze.

Etymology

From Old English fȳr (fire) + brand (sword, torch, or piece of burning wood).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbrænd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbrand / (BrE)
a person who deliberately tries to cause unrest or provoke trouble, especially one who incites others to protest or rebel.
person who deliberately causes unrest or trouble

Examples

The political firebrand urged the crowd to demand justice.
She gained a reputation as a firebrand in her university days, leading protests and challenging authority.
The company had to address concerns after a firebrand employee incited a strike.

Synset Examples

she was the instigator of their quarrel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitator - a person who urges others to protest or rebel
  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who habitually causes trouble

Etymology

Originally referred to a piece of burning wood; figurative sense of 'agitator' developed in the 17th century.
