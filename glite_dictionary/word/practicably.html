practicably

1. ADV

/ ˈpræktɪkəbli / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is feasible or capable of being done; in a practicable manner.
in a feasible or workable manner

Examples

It is not practicably possible to complete the work by tomorrow.
All safety measures must be implemented as far as practicably possible.
The law requires employers to act practicably to protect their workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practically - almost; nearly (sometimes confused in meaning or spelling)

Etymology

From 'practicable' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'practicabilis' (able to be done).
