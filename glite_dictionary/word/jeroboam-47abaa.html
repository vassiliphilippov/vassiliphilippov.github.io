jeroboam

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛrəˈboʊəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛrəˈbəʊəm / (BrE)
a large wine bottle holding approximately 3 liters or 4/5 of a gallon
large wine bottle (4/5 gallon)

Examples

The sommelier brought out a jeroboam of champagne for the celebration.
They decided to buy a jeroboam for the wedding reception to serve all their guests.
Collectors covet rare vintages in a jeroboam because of their impressive size and aging ability.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: magnum - a wine bottle holding about 1.5 liters
  • Degree Higher: rehoboam - a wine bottle even larger than a jeroboam (about 4.5 liters; often used for Champagne)

Etymology

Named after Jeroboam, a biblical king of Israel, possibly as a humorous reference to largeness.
