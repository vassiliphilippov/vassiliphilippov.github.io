proved

1. ADJ

/ pruvd / (AmE), / pruÀêvd / (BrE)
demonstrated to be true or correct beyond doubt; established by evidence or facts
established as true beyond doubt

Examples

His innocence was finally proved after a lengthy investigation.
The defendant's guilt remained proved in the eyes of the court.
She has a proved track record in the technology sector.

Synset Examples

a proven liar
a Soviet leader of proven shrewdness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proven - demonstrated to be true or correct beyond doubt (synonym as another adjective form)

Etymology

Past participle of 'prove,' from Latin 'probare' (to test, approve).
