Persea borbonia

1. NOUN

/ pɚˌsiːə bɔːrˈboʊniə / (AmE), / pəˌsiːə bɔːˈbəʊniə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to the southern United States, noted for its aromatic leaves and dark red heartwood
small evergreen U.S. tree with red heartwood

Examples

The leaves of the Persea borbonia emit a strong aroma when crushed.
Many cabinetmakers prize the dark red heartwood of Persea borbonia for fine woodworking projects.
Wildlife in the southeastern U.S. often feed on the fruit of the Persea borbonia tree.

Etymology

From the genus name Persea and species epithet borbonia, referring to Bourbon Island (Réunion).
