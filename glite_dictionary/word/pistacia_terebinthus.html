Pistacia terebinthus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsteɪʃiə tɛrəˈbɪnθəs / (AmE), / pɪˈsteɪsɪə tɛrəˈbɪnθəs / (BrE)
a small Mediterranean tree of the cashew family that produces Chian turpentine, known for its aromatic resin and compound leaves
Mediterranean tree yielding turpentine

Examples

The resin obtained from the Pistacia terebinthus tree has been valued since ancient times.
In Mediterranean forests, Pistacia terebinthus is recognized for its aromatic leaves and fruit.
Chian turpentine, an important export, is produced by the Pistacia terebinthus.

Etymology

From the genus Pistacia (from Latin, from Greek pistakion) and species terebinthus (from Latin terebinthus, from Greek terebinthos, denoting the turpentine tree).
