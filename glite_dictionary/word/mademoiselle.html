mademoiselle

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædəmwɑˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌmadəmwɑˈzɛl / (BrE)
a small silvery drumfish (Leiostomus xanthurus) found along the coasts of the United States from New York to Mexico, often mistaken for white perch
a small silvery drumfish

Examples

Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often catch mademoiselle when looking for white perch.
The restaurant's special featured grilled mademoiselle with lemon and herbs.
It's easy to mistake a mademoiselle for a white perch unless you know the differences.

Etymology

From French 'mademoiselle', sometimes applied to this fish due to its delicate appearance.
