lychnis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪk.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪk.nɪs / (BrE)
a genus of mostly perennial herbs with sticky stems, often grown for their showy flowers, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
a genus of perennial flowering herbs

Examples

The vivid red blooms of the lychnis brightened the garden in early summer.
Some species of lychnis have sticky stems that can trap tiny insects.
Gardeners often choose lychnis for border plantings because of its hardiness and color.

Etymology

From Greek lýchnis (lamp), originally referring to a plant used as lamp wicks.
