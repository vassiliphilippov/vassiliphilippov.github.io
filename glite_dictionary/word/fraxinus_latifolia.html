Fraxinus latifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ˌlætɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs ˌlætɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of ash tree native to western North America, valued for its hard, light wood.
species of ash tree (western North America)

Examples

The Fraxinus latifolia is commonly found along riverbanks in the Pacific Northwest.
Furniture makers often prize the wood of Fraxinus latifolia for its strength and lightness.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the growth of Fraxinus latifolia populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - a type of deciduous tree of the genus Fraxinus
  • Degree Higher: red ash - a close relative species of Fraxinus known for its reddish wood

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning 'ash tree' and 'latifolia' meaning 'broad-leaved'.
