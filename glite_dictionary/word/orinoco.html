Orinoco

1. PROPN

/ ɔːrɪˈnoʊkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪˈnəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
a major river in northern South America, approximately 1,500 miles (2,400 km) long, flowing through Venezuela and Colombia before emptying into the Atlantic Ocean.
major river in northern South America

Examples

The Orinoco is known for its extensive delta and diverse wildlife.
Explorers have sailed up the Orinoco to reach the heart of Venezuela.
Flooding along the Orinoco can affect entire communities in the rainy season.

Etymology

From Spanish Orinoco, possibly from an indigenous Warao word meaning 'place to paddle'.
