clam

1. VERB

/ klæm / (AmE), / klæm / (BrE)
to gather clams by digging in sand along the seashore.
gather clams by digging on the shore

Examples

Every summer, we clam on the beaches during low tide.
She spent the afternoon clamming with her family along the coast.
Local fishers often clam early in the morning before tourists arrive.

2. NOUN

/ klæm / (AmE), / klam / (BrE)
The edible flesh of a hard-shell or soft-shell clam, often used as food.
edible flesh of a clam

Examples

The chowder was packed with fresh clam and potatoes.
She ordered a bowl of steamed clam with garlic butter.
He added chopped clam to the pasta for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - an aquatic mollusk with two hinged shells

Etymology

From Old English 'clam' meaning 'pincers' or 'clamp', probably referring to the shell's action.

3. NOUN

/ klæm / (AmE), / klæm / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a shell that closes tightly, living buried in sand or mud, especially along the seashore
burrowing marine mollusk with a shell

Examples

We dug in the sand and found a large clam near the shoreline.
The fishermen caught several clams to sell at the market.
She ordered a bowl of clam chowder at the seaside restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a different type of bivalve mollusk, often found in freshwater or saltwater habitats
  • Synonym: oyster - a marine bivalve mollusk with a rough shell, cultivated for food and pearls

Etymology

Probably from Old English 'clam' (vice, clamp), referring to the firmness with which the shell closes.
