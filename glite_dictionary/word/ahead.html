ahead

1. ADV

/ əˈhɛd / (AmE), / əˈhɛd / (BrE)
before a particular time; in anticipation or advance.
before a time; in advance

Examples

The company asks customers to pay ahead to receive a discount.
You should book your tickets ahead to avoid disappointment.
We like to plan ahead to make sure everything runs smoothly.

Synset Examples

when you pay ahead (or in advance) you receive a discount
We like to plan ahead
should have made reservations beforehand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beforehand - in advance; ahead of time
  • Antonym: afterward - at a later time; subsequently
