acidity

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being acidic; the degree to which a substance has acid properties
the quality of being acidic

Examples

The acidity of lemon juice makes it taste sour.
Doctors measure the acidity of the stomach to diagnose certain conditions.
Soil acidity can affect which plants grow best in a region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalinity - the property of having a basic (alkaline) quality

Etymology

From Latin 'acidus' (sour) + '-ity' (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a pH below 7, indicating the presence of acid; the degree of acid in a substance.
degree of acid (pH below 7)

Examples

The acidity of lemon juice makes it taste sour and sharp.
Soil acidity can affect which plants will grow successfully in a garden.
Doctors often check stomach acidity to diagnose digestive issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalinity - the quality of having a pH above 7, indicating the presence of base

Etymology

From Latin acidus (sour) + -ity.

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
A sour or sharp taste present when something acidic is in the mouth.
sour or sharp taste

Examples

The acidity of the lemonade made my mouth pucker.
Some wines have a strong acidity that balances their sweetness.
Excessive acidity in fruit can make it taste unpleasantly sharp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourness - having a tart or acid taste
  • Antonym: sweetness - having a sweet taste

Etymology

From Latin aciditas (sourness), from acidus (sour).
