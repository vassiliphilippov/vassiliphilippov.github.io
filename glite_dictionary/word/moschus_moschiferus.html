Moschus moschiferus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑskəs ˌmɑskɪˈfɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒskəs ˌmɒskɪˈfɪərəs / (BrE)
A small, heavy-limbed deer of central Asia, the male of which produces musk valued in perfumery.
species of Siberian musk deer

Examples

The Moschus moschiferus is hunted for the musk produced by the male's scent gland.
Moschus moschiferus inhabits forested and mountainous regions of central Asia.
Conservationists are concerned about declining populations of Moschus moschiferus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musk deer - common name for small, musk-producing deer of the genus Moschus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek moskhos (musk) and Latin moschifer (musk-bearing).
