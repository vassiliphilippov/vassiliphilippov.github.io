acetum

1. NOUN

/ əˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / əˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
A dilute solution of acetic acid, sometimes used as a solvent, especially in pharmacology or chemistry.
dilute solution of acetic acid
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician prepared an acetum solution for testing.
Historically, certain tinctures used acetum as a solvent instead of alcohol.
Doctors sometimes prescribed medicines dissolved in acetum for easier absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinegar - a sour liquid containing acetic acid, used in food and cleaning

Etymology

From Latin 'acetum' (vinegar, sour wine).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / əˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
A sour-tasting liquid, especially vinegar, produced by the oxidation of alcohol in wine or cider and used as a condiment or preservative.
sour liquid; vinegar
archaic

Examples

Historically, acetum was produced by allowing wine to sour naturally.
The cook added a dash of acetum to the salad for extra flavor.
Apothecaries once used acetum in medicinal preparations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinegar - a sour liquid made by fermenting wine or cider, used as a condiment or preservative

Etymology

From Latin acetum (vinegar), from acere (to be sour).
