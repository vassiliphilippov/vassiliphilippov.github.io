psyop

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪˌɑp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪˌɒp / (BrE)
a military operation intended to influence the perceptions, emotions, and behavior of individuals, groups, or foreign governments
military operation to influence attitudes or perception

Examples

The government carried out a psyop to sway public opinion during the crisis.
Some soldiers specialized in psyop missions targeting enemy morale.
There are debates about the ethics of conducting psyop campaigns in international relations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propaganda - information spread to influence public opinion or obscure the truth

Etymology

Shortened from 'psychological operation', combining 'psycho-' (mind) and 'operation'.
