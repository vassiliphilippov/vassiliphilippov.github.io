to persevere

1. VERB

/ ˌpɜrsəˈvɪr / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
to continue trying to achieve something despite difficulties or discouragement
continue despite difficulty

Examples

Even when faced with setbacks, she chose to persevere and eventually achieved her goals.
If you persevere, you will eventually master this challenging subject.
He had to persevere through months of physical therapy after the accident.

Synset Examples

he persisted to call me every night
The child persisted and kept asking questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in an opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: to give up - to cease making an effort; stop trying

Etymology

From Latin perseverare (to persist, continue steadfastly).
