phylloquinone

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪloʊkwɪˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌfɪləkwɪˈnəʊn / (BrE)
a form of vitamin K (vitamin K1) found chiefly in green plants, important for blood clotting and other bodily functions
a form of vitamin K1 found in plants
medical

Examples

Spinach is a good source of phylloquinone, which is essential for healthy blood clotting.
Many vitamin supplements contain phylloquinone to meet daily recommended intake.
Researchers studied the absorption of phylloquinone in patients with digestive disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin K - a group of fat-soluble vitamins needed for blood clotting

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'quinone' (chemical class), referring to its occurrence in plants.
