to choose

1. VERB

/ tʃuz / (AmE), / tʃuːz / (BrE)
to select or pick out someone or something from a number of alternatives
to select from alternatives

Examples

You may choose any dessert from the menu.
After much thought, she chose the blue dress for the party.
Please choose your seats before the show starts.

Synset Examples

Take any one of these cards
Choose a good husband for your daughter
She selected a pair of shoes from among the dozen the salesgirl had shown her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to carefully choose as the best or most suitable option
  • Synonym: pick - to choose someone or something from a group
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose someone or something

Etymology

From Old English *ceosan* (to choose), related to Dutch *kiezen* and German *kiesen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00676526-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃuz / (AmE), / tʃuːz / (BrE)
to decide to do something, especially after considering various options
decide to do something

Examples

She chose to ignore the invitation and stayed home instead.
You are free to choose how you spend your free time.
He chose not to speak during the meeting.

Synset Examples

She chose not to attend classes and now she failed the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decide - to make a choice or judgment about something
  • Antonym: refuse - to show unwillingness or say no to something offered or requested

Etymology

From Old English *ceōsan*, related to Old High German *kiosan* (to choose), Latin *gustare* (to taste).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00681856-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tʃuz / (AmE), / tʃuːz / (BrE)
to select one person or thing from two or more possibilities
to select one from several options

Examples

You must choose a password with at least eight characters.
She couldn't choose between the blue dress and the red one.
After much thought, we chose to stay at home for the holidays.

Synset Examples

I always choose the fish over the meat courses in this restaurant
She opted for the job on the East coast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to carefully choose as being the best or most suitable
  • Synonym: pick - to choose someone or something from a group of options
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose

Etymology

From Old English *ceosan* (to choose), from Proto-Germanic *kiusan*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00681308-v
Synset POS: v
