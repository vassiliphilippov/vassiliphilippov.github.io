Exopterygota

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɑp.təˈrɪɡ.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɒp.təˈrɪɡ.ə.tə / (BrE)
A subclass of insects that undergo gradual and usually incomplete metamorphosis, with externally developing wings.
insect subclass with gradual metamorphosis

Examples

Grasshoppers and cockroaches belong to the Exopterygota subclass due to their gradual metamorphosis.
The wings of Exopterygota insects develop on the outside of their bodies.
Researchers studied several species within Exopterygota to better understand incomplete metamorphosis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Endopterygota - subclass of insects with complete metamorphosis and internal wing development

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + pterygota (winged).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkˌsɑptəˈrɪɡətə / (AmE), / ˌɛksɒptəˈrɪɡətə / (BrE)
A subclass of insects characterized by gradual and usually incomplete metamorphosis, with wings developing externally.
subclass of insects with external wing development

Examples

The grasshopper is a member of the Exopterygota, undergoing gradual metamorphosis.
Researchers studied the developmental stages typical of Exopterygota in field insects.
Unlike butterflies, insects within the Exopterygota subclass do not have a pupal stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Endopterygota - insect subclass with complete metamorphosis and internal wing development

Etymology

From Greek 'exo-' (outside) + 'pterygota' (winged), referring to insects with wings developing externally.
