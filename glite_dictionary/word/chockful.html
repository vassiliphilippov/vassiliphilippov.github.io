chockful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑk.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒk.fʊl / (BrE)
Filled to the top; packed full to capacity.
filled to capacity

Examples

The basket was chockful of ripe strawberries fresh from the garden.
Her new book is chockful of helpful advice for young entrepreneurs.
By the time we arrived, the auditorium was chockful and there were no seats left.

Synset Examples

chowder chockablock with pieces of fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crammed - filled completely or to excess
  • Synonym: stuffed - filled so much that nothing more can be added
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled

Etymology

From earlier 'chock-full', possibly from 'choke' or from Middle English 'chokke' (block).
