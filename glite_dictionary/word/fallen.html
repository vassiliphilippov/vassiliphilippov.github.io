fallen

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑlən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlən / (BrE)
Killed in battle or as a result of war, especially used to describe soldiers.
killed in battle (of soldiers)

Examples

Every year, the memorial service commemorates the fallen who gave their lives for their country.
The names of the fallen are inscribed on the monument in the town square.
During the ceremony, the soldiers saluted in honor of their fallen comrades.

Synset Examples

to honor fallen soldiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead - no longer alive
  • Antonym: surviving - continuing to live or exist; not killed in battle

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fall', from Old English 'feallan'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑlən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlən / (BrE)
(of a woman) having lost her chastity, especially regarded as morally degraded or dishonored.
having lost chastity (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In Victorian novels, a fallen woman was often excluded from polite society.
Society unfairly judged her as fallen after learning of her past.
The character is portrayed as fallen, yet still deserving of compassion.

Synset Examples

a fallen woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chaste - pure; abstaining from unlawful sexual intercourse

Etymology

Past participle of 'fall,' used figuratively since the 15th century to mean 'having succumbed to sin.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00363253-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɔlən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlən / (BrE)
having dropped to the ground as a result of gravity, especially after detachment from a usual position
having dropped to the ground

Examples

The ground was blanketed in fallen leaves after the storm.
We sat together on a fallen log by the riverbank.
A fallen branch blocked the narrow hiking trail.

Synset Examples

fallen leaves covered the forest floor
sat on a fallen tree trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dropped - let go or fell to a lower place
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not having fallen

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fall', from Old English *feallan*.

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɑlən / (AmE), / ˈfɒlən / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) having collapsed to the ground; having fallen down.
collapsed; fallen down (of object or structure)

Examples

The crew inspected the site of the fallen building for safety hazards.
A fallen tree blocked traffic after last night's storm.
Rescue teams searched the rubble for survivors after the fallen roof caused panic.

Synset Examples

a fallen building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapsed - having suddenly fallen down or given way

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fall', from Old English 'feallan' (to fall).
