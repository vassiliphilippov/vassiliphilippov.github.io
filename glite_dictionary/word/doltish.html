doltish

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊltɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊltɪʃ / (BrE)
slow-witted, foolish, or showing a lack of intelligence
stupid or slow-witted

Examples

He made a doltish remark during the meeting that left everyone speechless.
Her doltish behavior in class frustrated even the most patient teachers.
They dismissed his ideas as doltish, refusing to take him seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupid - showing a lack of intelligence or common sense
  • Antonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise things

Etymology

From 'dolt' (fool) + '-ish'. 'Dolt' origin uncertain, perhaps from Middle English 'dulte' (stupid).
