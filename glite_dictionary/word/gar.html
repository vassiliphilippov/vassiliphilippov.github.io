gar

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑr / (AmE), / ɡɑː / (BrE)
a primitive predatory North American freshwater fish with hard scales and long jaws lined with needlelike teeth
predatory North American freshwater fish

Examples

The ancient gar can often be found lurking in slow-moving rivers and lakes.
A fisherman caught a huge gar near the mouth of the bayou.
The bony scales of the gar serve as a natural armor against predators.

Etymology

Possibly from an Old English word *gār* meaning 'spear', referring to the fish's long shape.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɑr / (AmE), / ɡɑː / (BrE)
a long, slender predatory fish of European coastal waters with long toothed jaws, belonging to the family Belonidae.
long, slender European predatory fish

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed the gar he had caught near the shoreline.
In the shallow coastal waters, schools of gar are often seen hunting smaller fish.
The gar's long jaws are lined with sharp teeth, making it a formidable predator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlefish - long, slender predatory fish, family Belonidae

Etymology

From Middle English 'gare', possibly from Old English 'gar' meaning 'spear', referring to its shape.
