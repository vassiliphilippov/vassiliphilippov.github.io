nitrochloromethane

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊklɔːroʊˈmɛθeɪn / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊklɔːrəʊˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
A chemical compound, the gaseous form of chloropicrin, used as a tear gas.
chemical; tear gas compound

Examples

The laboratory handled nitrochloromethane with extreme care due to its use as a tear gas.
Exposure to nitrochloromethane can cause severe eye and respiratory irritation.
During chemical warfare in the past, nitrochloromethane was dispersed to incapacitate enemy troops.

Etymology

From nitro-, indicating the presence of a nitro group, and chloromethane, a compound containing chlorine and methane.
