Marxism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrkˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑːksɪzəm / (BrE)
The political and economic theories developed by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, which argue that class struggle and economic forces shape society, and that capitalism will be replaced by communism.
theories of Karl Marx advocating socialism and eventual communism

Examples

Many university courses explore the principles of Marxism and their influence on modern politics.
He wrote a book that provides a critical analysis of Marxism and its impact on twentieth-century revolutions.
Debates about Marxism often focus on the relationship between economic forces and social change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialism - a political and economic system where the means of production are owned collectively or by the state
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets
  • Degree Higher: communism - a political and economic system where all property is publicly owned and each person works and is paid according to their abilities and needs

Etymology

From the name of Karl Marx (1818–1883), German political theorist.
