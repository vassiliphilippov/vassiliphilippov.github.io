diversification

1. NOUN

/ dəˌvɜrsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˌvɜːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of becoming more varied or diverse
the condition of being varied

Examples

The diversification of the local economy helped protect jobs during the recession.
Critics argue that there is a lack of diversification in the company's investment portfolio.
The school's curriculum is undergoing diversification to include more arts and science courses.

Synset Examples

that restaurant's menu lacks diversification: every day it is the same

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse

Etymology

From French 'diversification', from Latin 'diversus' (turned different ways, diverse).

2. NOUN

/ dəˌvɜrsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˌvɜːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of increasing the variety of products, investments, or activities to reduce risk or improve outcomes
increasing variety to reduce risk

Examples

The company's diversification into new markets helped boost its profits.
Prudent diversification is essential for investors seeking to minimize risk.
Through diversification of crops, the farmers hoped to ensure a more stable income.

Synset Examples

my broker recommended a greater diversification of my investments
he limited his losses by diversification of his product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse; the absence of uniformity
  • Antonym: concentration - focus on a single area or activity, as opposed to diversification

Etymology

From Latin diversificare (to make diverse) + English suffix -ation.
