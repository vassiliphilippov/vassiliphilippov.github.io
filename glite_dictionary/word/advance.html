advance

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvæns / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːns / (BrE)
a sum of money paid before it is due or earned, typically as part of a contract or salary
sum paid before it is due

Examples

He received an advance on his salary to help cover moving expenses.
The publisher offered the author a generous advance before the book was written.
You must return the advance if you do not complete the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prepayment - a sum paid in advance of when it is due

Etymology

From Old French 'avancier', from Late Latin 'abantiāre', from Latin 'abante' (before).
