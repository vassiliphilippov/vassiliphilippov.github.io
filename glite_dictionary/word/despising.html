despising

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈspaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
A feeling of strong scorn and hatred toward someone or something.
scornful hatred or contempt

Examples

His despising of authority figures was evident in every conversation he had.
You could sense her despising for her former employer in her tone.
The novel is full of the protagonist's despising of hypocrisy and falsehood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless
  • Synonym: hatred - intense dislike or hostility

Etymology

From the verb 'despise', from Old French *despis*, from Latin *despicere* (to look down on).
