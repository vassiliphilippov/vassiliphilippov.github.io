Podocarpus spicata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs spaɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈkɑːpəs spɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A species of evergreen coniferous tree native to Australia and New Zealand, known for its dense foliage and often found in moist forests.
species of coniferous tree of Australia and New Zealand

Examples

The dense canopy of the Podocarpus spicata helps maintain moisture in the forest ecosystem.
Scientists discovered a rare specimen of Podocarpus spicata deep in the national park.
Timber from the Podocarpus spicata is valued for its durability in construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Podocarpus - a genus of coniferous trees and shrubs in the family Podocarpaceae

Etymology

From genus name Podocarpus (from Greek 'pous' meaning 'foot' and 'karpos' meaning 'fruit'), and Latin 'spicata' meaning 'bearing spikes'.
