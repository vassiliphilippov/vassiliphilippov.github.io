Halcyon

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæl.si.ən / (AmE), / ˈhals.i.ən / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) a woman, originally named Alcyone, who was transformed into a kingfisher bird.
mythical woman changed into a kingfisher

Examples

In the ancient story, Halcyon was changed into a bird after losing her husband at sea.
Many poets refer to Halcyon in describing periods of peace and calm.
According to Greek legend, Halcyon calmed the seas during her nesting period.

Etymology

From Greek Alkyone (Ἁλκυόνη), a figure in Greek mythology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæl.si.ˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈhæl.sɪ.ən / (BrE)
a genus of large, brightly colored kingfishers native to Africa, Asia, and Australasia
genus of large kingfishers

Examples

The Halcyon genus includes several striking species found in Africa and Asia.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Halcyon kingfishers in coastal wetlands.
One of the best-known species in the Halcyon group is the woodland kingfisher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a brightly colored bird known for catching fish

Etymology

From Greek 'alkyōn' (kingfisher), also associated in legend with calm seas.
