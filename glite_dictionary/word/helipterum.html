Helipterum

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈlɪptərəm / (AmE), / hɛˈlɪptərəm / (BrE)
A genus of South African and Australian herbs or shrubs, many of which are grown as everlastings; some species have been reassigned to other genera.
genus of everlastings (plants)

Examples

Several species of Helipterum are popular in dried flower arrangements.
Researchers noted that some Helipterum species are now classified under Hyalosperma.
The Australian Helipterum thrives in sandy soils and full sunlight.

Etymology

From Greek 'helios' (sun) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to features of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈlɪptərəm / (AmE), / hɪˈlɪptərəm / (BrE)
a genus of South African and Australian herbs or shrubs, often grown as everlastings; many species have been reassigned to other genera such as Pteropogon and Hyalosperma
genus of South African and Australian herbs or shrubs

Examples

Several species formerly classified under Helipterum are now assigned to other genera.
Gardeners appreciate Helipterum for its long-lasting, papery flowers that are prized in dried arrangements.
The sandy soils of Western Australia provide the perfect habitat for wild Helipterum to thrive.

Etymology

From Greek 'helios' (sun) and 'pteron' (wing), relating to the sun-like or winged appearance of the flowers.
