diaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəpə / (BrE)
A fabric, usually cotton or linen, woven with a repeating geometric pattern, often used historically for towels and table linens.
fabric with repeating geometric pattern

Examples

The altar cloth was made from fine diaper with an intricate geometric design.
Historians noted that medieval nobles often used towels of white diaper at banquets.
You can recognize authentic diaper fabric by its small, diamond-shaped patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damask - A rich patterned woven fabric, often featuring elaborate designs

Etymology

From Old French *diapre* (ornamental fabric), from Medieval Latin *diaprum*, of Greek origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.pə / (BrE)
A soft, absorbent garment worn by babies or incontinent people, fastened at the waist and between the legs to absorb excrement.
absorbent garment for babies or incontinent persons

Examples

The mother quickly changed the baby's diaper after she noticed it was wet.
Disposable diapers are convenient for travel and outings.
He learned how to put a clean diaper on his newborn son.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nappy - British term for diaper (absorbent garment for babies)

Etymology

From Old French 'diapre' (ornamental cloth), originally referring to the fabric.
