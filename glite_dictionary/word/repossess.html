to repossess

1. VERB

/ ˌriːpəˈzɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːpəˈzɛs / (BrE)
(of a lender) to take back possession of property, especially when the buyer defaults on payments.
to take back property after default

Examples

The bank threatened to repossess his car after he missed three payments.
If you fail to pay your mortgage, the lender may repossess your house.
They were forced to repossess several vehicles due to loan defaults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to foreclose - take possession of a mortgaged property when the mortgagor defaults
  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something suddenly or by force
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up possession of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + possess (from Latin possidēre, to own or occupy).

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / ˌriː.pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
to take possession of something again, especially when someone fails to make payments as required
take back possession, often for nonpayment

Examples

The bank threatened to repossess her car after she missed several payments.
If you default on your loan, the company can repossess the property.
They were devastated when the lender decided to repossess their home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something suddenly and forcibly
  • Antonym: forfeit - to lose or give up something as a consequence

Etymology

From re- (again) + possess, from Latin possessus (held, owned).

Synset POS: v
