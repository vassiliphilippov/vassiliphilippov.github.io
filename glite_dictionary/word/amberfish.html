amberfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbɚˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæmbəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
any of several warm-water carangid fish with amber to coppery coloring and a forked tail, found chiefly in tropical and subtropical seas.
a warm-water amber-colored carangid fish

Examples

The fisherman was proud of the massive amberfish he caught off the Florida coast.
Grilled amberfish is a delicacy in many seaside restaurants.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the amberfish in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amberjack - a species of large amber-colored game fish related to amberfish
