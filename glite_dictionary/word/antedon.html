Antedon

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪ.dɑn / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.dɒn / (BrE)
A genus of marine echinoderms in the family Antedonidae, commonly known as feather stars.
genus of marine echinoderms (feather stars)

Examples

The Antedon is often found in shallow coastal waters attached to rocks.
Researchers studied the unique feeding habits of Antedon species.
The delicate arms of Antedon resemble feathers as they wave in the current.

Etymology

From Greek anti (against) + odon (tooth), referring to features of the oral structure.
