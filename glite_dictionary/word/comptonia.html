Comptonia

1. NOUN

/ kɑmpˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / kɒmpˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of North American shrubs in the family Myricaceae, including the sweet fern (Comptonia peregrina), known for its aromatic, fern-like leaves.
genus of aromatic North American shrubs (sweet fern)

Examples

Comptonia is easily recognized by its slender, deeply lobed leaves resembling those of a fern.
Botanists collected samples of Comptonia during their fieldwork in North America.
The aromatic leaves of Comptonia are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet fern - an aromatic shrub, Comptonia peregrina, native to eastern North America

Etymology

Named after Henry Compton, an 18th-century English bishop and plant collector.

2. NOUN

/ kɑmpˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / kɒmpˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Myricaceae, consisting of a single species, Comptonia peregrina (commonly known as sweet fern), native to eastern North America.
a plant genus in Myricaceae (sweet fern)

Examples

Comptonia is well known for its aromatic leaves, which resemble those of ferns.
Botanists identified the shrub as a member of the genus Comptonia.
The only North American species belonging to Comptonia is commonly called sweet fern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet fern - a fragrant North American shrub, the only species in the genus Comptonia

Etymology

Named after Henry Compton, an English botanist.
