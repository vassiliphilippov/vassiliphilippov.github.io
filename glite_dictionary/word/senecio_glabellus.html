Senecio glabellus

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈniːsioʊ ɡləˈbɛləs / (AmE), / sɛˈniːsiəʊ ɡləˈbɛləs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the daisy family, commonly known as American ragwort, with yellow flowers native to North America.
species of American ragwort with yellow flowers

Examples

Senecio glabellus can often be found growing along roadsides in the southeastern United States.
The yellow blossoms of Senecio glabellus attract many pollinators during the spring.
Gardeners sometimes remove Senecio glabellus to prevent it from spreading in wildflower meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragwort - common name for several species of plants in the genus Senecio

Etymology

From Latin: 'Senecio' meaning 'old man' (referring to the white hairs on seeds), 'glabellus' meaning 'smooth' or 'hairless'.
