Crex crex

1. NOUN

/ krɛks ˈkrɛks / (AmE), / krɛks ˈkrɛks / (BrE)
A species of rail, commonly known as the corncrake, found in Eurasian grain fields.
Eurasian rail bird species (corncrake)

Examples

The distinctive call of the Crex crex can be heard across European meadows in spring.
Wildlife enthusiasts were thrilled to spot a Crex crex among the tall grasses.
Due to habitat loss, the population of Crex crex has significantly declined in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corncrake - Common name for Crex crex, a Eurasian rail
  • Degree Higher: rail - Any bird of the family Rallidae to which Crex crex belongs

Etymology

From the scientific (Linnaean) Latin name, imitative of the bird's call.
