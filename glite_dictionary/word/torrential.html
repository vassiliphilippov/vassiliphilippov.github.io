torrential

1. ADJ

/ tɔːˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / tɒˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of rain or water) falling or flowing rapidly and in great quantity; pouring in abundance.
falling or flowing in great quantity

Examples

The village was flooded after torrential rain fell for hours.
Drivers were warned about dangerous conditions due to torrential downpours.
The picnic was cancelled because of the torrential storms.

Synset Examples

torrential rains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy - having great weight or amount; used to describe intense rain

Etymology

From Latin 'torrent-', meaning 'rushing, roaring'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00016570-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ tɔˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / tɒˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of rain, applause, abuse, etc.) resembling a torrent in its force or abundance; falling or coming in a great quantity and with great force.
falling in great force or abundance

Examples

The match was canceled due to torrential rain that flooded the field.
The politician faced torrential criticism after his controversial remarks.
There was torrential applause as the performer took the stage.

Synset Examples

torrential applause
torrential abuse
the torrential facility and fecundity characteristic of his style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pouring - falling heavily (especially of rain)

Etymology

From French *torrentiel*, from Latin *torrentem* (rushing, roaring).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00603894-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ tɔˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / tɒˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by the rapid, forceful flow characteristic of a torrent; especially used in scientific contexts to describe processes or features shaped by rushing water.
relating to a torrent or its action

Examples

The steep valleys showed signs of torrential erosion after the flash flood.
Some plant species have developed torrential adaptations to survive in rapidly flowing streams.
Engineers studied the area’s torrential landscape before planning the new dam.

Synset Examples

torrential erosion
torrential adaptations seen in some aquatic forms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torrent - a strong and fast-moving stream of water or other liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'torrens' (rushing stream) via French 'torrentiel'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02828735-a
Synset POS: a
