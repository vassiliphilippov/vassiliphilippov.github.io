Satureja nepeta

1. NOUN

/ səˈtʊəriə nɪˈpiːtə / (AmE), / səˈtjʊəriə nɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
A low-growing, strongly aromatic perennial herb native to southern Europe and Great Britain, and naturalized in the United States, commonly used as a culinary and medicinal herb.
aromatic perennial herb, also called savory

Examples

The chef garnished the dish with fresh Satureja nepeta to enhance its flavor.
Gardeners value Satureja nepeta for its hardiness and aromatic leaves.
Herbal teas made from Satureja nepeta are believed to aid digestion.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Satureja', species 'nepeta', used in botanical nomenclature.
