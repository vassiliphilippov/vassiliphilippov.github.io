ten-membered

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛn ˈmɛmbɚd / (AmE), / ˌtɛn ˈmɛmbəd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having a ring consisting of ten atoms or groups.
having a ring of ten atoms (chemistry)

Examples

The compound has a ten-membered ring at its core, making it unusually flexible.
Scientists have synthesized a ten-membered lactone for use in fragrance chemistry.
A ten-membered cyclic structure can influence the biological activity of the molecule.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: six-membered - having a ring of six members (chemistry)
  • Degree Higher: twelve-membered - having a ring of twelve members (chemistry)
