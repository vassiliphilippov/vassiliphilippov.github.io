Falcatifolium falciforme

1. NOUN

/ fælˌkeɪtɪˈfoʊliəm fælˈsɪfɔːrm / (AmE), / falˌkatɪˈfɔːliəm falˈsɪfɔːmi / (BrE)
A small tropical rainforest tree native to Indonesia and Malaysia, belonging to the conifer family Podocarpaceae.
tropical tree of Indonesia and Malaysia

Examples

The rare Falcatifolium falciforme thrives in the damp lowland forests of Borneo.
Botanists discovered a grove of Falcatifolium falciforme during their expedition in Sumatra.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the natural habitat of Falcatifolium falciforme.

Etymology

From Latin: 'falcatifolium' meaning 'sickle-shaped leaf', and 'falciforme' meaning 'sickle-shaped'. Botanical Latin.
