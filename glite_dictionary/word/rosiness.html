rosiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzi.nɪs / (BrE)
a pinkish or reddish color or glow, especially in the cheeks, typically indicating good health or cheerfulness
pinkish glow, especially in cheeks

Examples

She admired the rosiness of the children's cheeks after they played outside.
His rosiness faded as the illness progressed.
A healthy diet can contribute to the natural rosiness in your complexion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pallor - paleness, especially of the face

Etymology

From 'rosy' (like a rose) + '-ness' forming a noun for the state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzinəs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzinəs / (BrE)
a healthy, reddish color or glow, especially in the cheeks; the quality of being rosy
healthy reddish color or glow

Examples

Her cheeks had a natural rosiness after coming in from the cold.
The rosiness of his complexion made him look energetic and youthful.
A slight rosiness appeared on her face as she blushed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance

Etymology

From 'rosy' + '-ness'. 'Rosy' from rose, referring to the color of a rose.

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzinəs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzinɪs / (BrE)
A pinkish color resembling that of a rose; a dusty or subtle shade of pink.
a pinkish or dusty rose color

Examples

The gentle rosiness of the evening sky made the sunset especially beautiful.
Her cheeks had a natural rosiness after walking in the cold air.
The rosiness of the wallpaper added warmth to the room's decor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color
  • Synonym: rose - a moderate purplish-red to pink color

Etymology

From 'rosy' (like a rose) + '-ness' (noun suffix denoting state or quality).
