splicing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsplaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A point or section where two pieces, such as paper, film, or tape, have been joined together.
junction where items are joined

Examples

The editor repaired the damaged film by adding a splicing near the middle of the reel.
Any weakness at the splicing could cause the audio tape to break during playback.
Technicians checked each splicing to ensure a seamless connection between magnetic tape segments.

Synset Examples

the break was due to an imperfect splice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splice - a joining together of two things (noun)

Etymology

From splice (verb), likely of Dutch origin 'splissen' (to splice rope).
