Cecropia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkroʊpiə / (AmE), / sɪˈkrəʊpiə / (BrE)
a large genus of fast-growing tropical American trees, known for their use in cordage, tanning, and rubber production
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The leaves of the Cecropia tree are easily recognized in the rainforests of South America.
Certain species of Cecropia are vital to local wildlife, providing both shelter and food.
The bark from Cecropia trees has traditionally been used for making rope and leather goods.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Cecrops, a legendary king of Athens.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈkroʊpiə / (AmE), / sɛˈkrəʊpiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees whose bark is used in tanning, bast fibers for cordage, and milky sap for caoutchouc.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The rainforests of Central America are home to several species of Cecropia.
Fibers from Cecropia trees are traditionally used to make strong ropes.
The bark of the Cecropia tree has been used in leather tanning processes.

Etymology

Named after Cecrops, a mythical king of Athens, from New Latin.
