scut

1. NOUN

/ skʌt / (AmE), / skʌt / (BrE)
a short, erect tail, especially that of a rabbit, deer, or similar animal
a short upright animal tail

Examples

The white scut of the fleeing rabbit was visible in the long grass.
A deer flicked its scut as it bounded away into the trees.
Hunters spotted only the scut of their quarry disappearing over the hill.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English scut, perhaps of Flemish or Middle Dutch origin.

2. NOUN

/ skʌt / (AmE), / skʌt / (BrE)
an idle, lazy, or unhelpful person, often used in a derogatory way
a lazy or unhelpful person (slang)
slang

Examples

You can’t expect a scut like him to finish the project on time.
Stop acting like a scut and clean up your mess.
The rest of the team resented having a scut who never pulled his weight.
