unacceptability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnəkˌsɛptəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnəkˌsɛptəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being acceptable or not meeting approved standards.
state of being unacceptable

Examples

The unacceptability of his behavior was clear to everyone at the meeting.
Many residents voiced concern about the unacceptability of the proposed changes to the neighborhood.
She emphasized the unacceptability of lowering safety standards at the factory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptability - the quality of being acceptable
  • Synonym: unsatisfactoriness - the state of being unsatisfactory

Etymology

From un- (not) + acceptability, based on Latin acceptare (to accept).
