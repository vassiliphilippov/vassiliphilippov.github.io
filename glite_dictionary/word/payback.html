payback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌbæk / (BrE)
the financial return gained from an investment, especially when it equals the original amount invested
return on investment; recouping of initial outlay

Examples

The project's payback period is estimated at just two years, making it very attractive to investors.
Investors were pleased to see a quick payback on their initial outlay.
We calculated the payback to ensure the investment would be worthwhile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - the profit from an investment
  • Synonym: profit - financial gain from business activity

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'pay back,' meaning to return money, used as a noun since the mid 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌbæk / (BrE)
the act of punishing or harming someone in return for something they have done, especially as revenge
revenge or act of retaliation

Examples

After years of being bullied, he finally got his payback when he stood up for himself.
She believed that fate would deliver payback to those who wronged her family.
Many movies revolve around the theme of payback for betrayal or injustice.

Synset Examples

Vengeance is mine; I will repay, saith the Lord
For vengeance I would do nothing. This nation is too great to look for mere revenge
he swore vengeance on the man who betrayed him
the swiftness of divine retribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the action of inflicting hurt in return for harm received
  • Synonym: retribution - punishment inflicted as vengeance for a wrong or criminal act
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the action of stopping blaming or being angry with someone for something wrong

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'pay back,' meaning to return money or a favor, extended to mean return an injury.
