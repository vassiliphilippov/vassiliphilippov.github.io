Alleghenies

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪˈɡɛniːz / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡiːniːz / (BrE)
the western part of the Appalachian Mountains, extending from northern Pennsylvania to southwestern Virginia
mountain range in the eastern US

Examples

We camped in the Alleghenies during the summer to enjoy the mountain scenery.
Streams in the Alleghenies are known for their clear, cold water.
The fall colors in the Alleghenies attract many tourists every year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Appalachians - the entire Appalachian mountain system in eastern North America

Etymology

From Allegheny, likely derived from the Lenape (Delaware) Indian word for the river or region.
