Ammotragus lervia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈtreɪɡəs ˈlɜr.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˌæməˈtreɪɡəs ˈlɜː.vi.ə / (BrE)
a species of wild sheep native to northern Africa, also known as the Barbary sheep
wild sheep of northern Africa

Examples

The Ammotragus lervia inhabits the rocky mountains of northern Africa.
Zoologists studied the Ammotragus lervia to learn more about its adaptation to arid environments.
Visitors to the reserve were excited to spot an Ammotragus lervia among the cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbary sheep - a common name for Ammotragus lervia

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Ammotragus' (from Greek 'ammos' sand + 'tragos' goat) and species name 'lervia'.
