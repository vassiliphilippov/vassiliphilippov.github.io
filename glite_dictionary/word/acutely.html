acutely

1. ADV

/ əˈkjutli / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtli / (BrE)
In a way that is intensely or sharply felt or perceived.
intensely or sharply felt or perceived

Examples

She became acutely aware of the silence in the room.
The patient was acutely sensitive to any changes in temperature.
He acutely felt the loss of his childhood friend.

Synset Examples

he was keenly aware of his own shortcomings
she pitied her sister acutely
acutely aware

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keenly - in an intense or sharp manner

Etymology

From acute + -ly; 'acute' from Latin acutus, meaning 'sharp'.

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ əˈkjuːtli / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtli / (BrE)
(medical) With a rapid and sudden onset (of a disease or symptom).
with rapid onset (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient became acutely ill within a few hours of infection.
Doctors are trained to recognize acutely presenting conditions in emergency settings.
Some viruses can cause acutely severe symptoms in otherwise healthy individuals.

Synset Examples

an acutely debilitating virus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronically - In a way that persists for a long time or constantly recurs (medical)

Etymology

From 'acute' (from Latin 'acūtus', sharp) plus adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00141793-r
Synset POS: r

/ əˈkjuːtli / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtli / (BrE)
in a perceptive, shrewd, or highly sensitive manner
in a sharp or highly sensitive manner

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
