pneumococcal

1. ADJ

/ ˌnjuːmoʊˈkɑːkəl / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈkɒkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by bacteria of the genus pneumococcus.
of or caused by pneumococcus bacteria
medical

Examples

The pneumococcal vaccine protects against infections caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae.
Children and elderly people are at risk for pneumococcal pneumonia.
Doctors recommend pneumococcal immunization for certain high-risk groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumococcus - a bacterium of the genus causing diseases such as pneumonia

Etymology

From New Latin 'pneumococcus' (the bacterial genus) + English '-al' (adjectival suffix).
