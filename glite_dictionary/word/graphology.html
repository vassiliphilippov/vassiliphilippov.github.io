graphology

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈfɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡrəˈfɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of handwriting, especially as a means of analyzing the writer's character or personality
study of handwriting for character analysis

Examples

She has a keen interest in graphology and often analyzes her friends' handwriting.
Some employers use graphology to assess a candidate's suitability for a job, though its reliability is debated.
The field of graphology claims to uncover aspects of personality through handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handwriting analysis - analysis of handwriting to infer personality or authenticity

Etymology

From French graphologie, from Greek graphē (writing) + -logia (study).
