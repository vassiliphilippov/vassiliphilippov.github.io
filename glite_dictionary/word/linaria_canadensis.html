Linaria canadensis

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnɛəri.ə ˌkænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈneəriə ˌkænəˈdensɪs / (BrE)
A North American plant species with racemes of blue-violet flowers, also known as blue toadflax.
North American plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with clusters of Linaria canadensis during the spring.
She identified the delicate blue flowers as Linaria canadensis on her botanical walk.
Researchers are studying how Linaria canadensis adapts to different soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue toadflax - Common name for Linaria canadensis

Etymology

From Latin: Linaria (a genus in the plant family Plantaginaceae), canadensis indicating 'of Canada'.
