foxhunt

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑksˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈfɒksˌhʌnt / (BrE)
to hunt foxes, typically on horseback and with dogs, as a traditional sport
to hunt foxes as a sport

Examples

On weekends, the club would foxhunt across the rolling countryside.
He has foxhunted since childhood, following the pack on horseback.
Many rural traditions include the annual foxhunting season as an important event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunt - to chase and kill wild animals for food or sport
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01147490-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑksˌhʌnt / (AmE), / ˈfɒksˌhʌnt / (BrE)
an organized sport in which riders on horseback follow hounds in pursuit of a fox
sport of hunting foxes with hounds and horses

Examples

Every autumn, a traditional foxhunt takes place in the English countryside.
She participated in her first foxhunt last weekend and found it challenging.
The debate over banning the foxhunt continues to divide opinion in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox hunting - the practice or sport of hunting foxes with trained hounds
