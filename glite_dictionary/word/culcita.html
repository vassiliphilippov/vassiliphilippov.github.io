Culcita

1. NOUN

/ kʌlˈsaɪtə / (AmE), / kʌlˈsiːtə / (BrE)
A small genus of terrestrial ferns resembling bracken, found in tropical America, Malaysia, Australia, Polynesia, southwestern Europe, and the Atlantic islands.
genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

The rare Culcita fern thrives on the shaded slopes of the Canary Islands.
Botanists recently discovered a new population of Culcita in tropical America.
Gardens featuring Culcita are prized by collectors of unusual ferns.

Etymology

From Latin *culcita* (a cushion), referencing the cushion-like base of the fern's fronds.

2. NOUN

/ kʌlˈsiːtə / (AmE), / kʌlˈkiːtə / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns resembling bracken, found in tropical America, Malaysia to Australia and Polynesia, and parts of southwestern Europe and Atlantic islands.
a genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

Culcita is known for its cushion-like shape and large, divided leaves.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Culcita genus in the Atlantic islands.
Unlike bracken, the Culcita fern prefers more humid and shaded environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dicksonia - a related genus of tree ferns

Etymology

From Latin *culcita* (cushion, mattress), possibly referring to the shape or growth habit of the fern.
