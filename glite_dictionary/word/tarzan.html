Tarzan

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑrˌzæn / (AmE), / ˈtɑːzæn / (BrE)
A fictional character, a man raised by apes in the African jungle and known as the hero of a series of novels by Edgar Rice Burroughs.
man raised by apes; literary character

Examples

When I was a child, I loved reading about Tarzan swinging through the jungle on vines.
The actor played Tarzan in the latest big-budget adaptation of the story.
Many people associate the wild jungle with the image of Tarzan leaping from tree to tree.

Etymology

Created by Edgar Rice Burroughs for his 1912 novel; the name is said to mean 'white-skin' in the fictional ape language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːr.zæn / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.zæn / (BrE)
(sometimes used ironically) a man of great physical strength and agility, after the hero in the novels by Edgar Rice Burroughs.
a very strong and agile man (figurative)

Examples

He swung across the obstacle course like a real Tarzan.
They called him Tarzan at the gym because of his amazing strength.
Trying to impress us with your Tarzan moves won't work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hercules - a man of great strength, from Greek mythology
  • Antonym: weakling - a person lacking physical strength

Etymology

From the name of Tarzan, the fictional hero created by Edgar Rice Burroughs.
