Prunus cerasus austera

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌsɛˈreɪsəs ɔːˈstɪrə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˌsɛˈreɪsəs ɒˈstɪərə / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of sour cherry tree known for bearing fruit with dark skin and dark juice
cultivated sour cherry tree with dark fruit

Examples

The Prunus cerasus austera produces cherries favored for making rich preserves and liqueurs.
Gardeners often choose Prunus cerasus austera for its resistance to disease and flavorful fruit.
During harvest season, the dark juice of the Prunus cerasus austera often stains the hands of pickers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prunus cerasus - species of sour cherry tree

Etymology

From Latin; 'Prunus' for plum genus, 'cerasus' meaning cherry, and 'austera' meaning severe or dark.
