Huxley

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌksli / (AmE), / ˈhʌksli / (BrE)
Aldous Huxley (1894–1963), English writer best known for his novel 'Brave New World' and his depiction of a scientifically controlled utopia.
English writer, author of 'Brave New World'

Examples

Huxley is often cited for his visionary insights into the future of society.
Many students read Huxley in their literature courses, especially his work 'Brave New World.'
The philosophical ideas of Huxley continue to influence debates about science and society.

Etymology

English surname, from a place name in Cheshire, England.
