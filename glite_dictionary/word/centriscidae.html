Centriscidae

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrɪsɪˌdi / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, slender marine fishes commonly known as shrimpfishes, characterized by a laterally compressed body and long, tubular snout
family of marine fishes (shrimpfishes)

Examples

The family Centriscidae includes species found in shallow coastal waters of the Indo-Pacific region.
Centriscidae are known for their unusual way of swimming vertically, head downward.
Marine biologists often study Centriscidae to learn about adaptive camouflage in fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimpfish - a slender marine fish with a tubular snout, member of the family Centriscidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Centriscidae*, based on the type genus *Centriscus*.

2. NOUN

/ sɛnˌtrɪsɪˈdiː / (AmE), / sɛnˌtrɪsɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of small marine fishes, known as shrimpfishes, characterized by their slender bodies and long, tubular snouts.
family of shrimpfishes

Examples

The Centriscidae are notable for their unique, laterally compressed bodies found in tropical seas.
Researchers observed several species belonging to the Centriscidae family near coral reefs.
Aquariums sometimes exhibit Centriscidae due to their fascinating vertical swimming behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimpfish - a small, slender fish of the family Centriscidae

From New Latin 'Centriscidae', based on the type genus 'Centriscus'.
