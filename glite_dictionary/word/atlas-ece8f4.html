atlas

1. NOUN

/ ˈætləs / (AmE), / ˈatləs / (BrE)
the first cervical vertebra of the spine, which supports the skull
first cervical vertebra
medical

Examples

The atlas allows your head to move up and down.
Damage to the atlas can lead to neck stiffness or pain.
The neurosurgeon explained the function of the atlas in supporting the skull.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: axis - the second cervical vertebra of the spine, below the atlas

Etymology

From Greek Atlas, the mythological Titan who supported the heavens, referencing the vertebra's support of the skull.

2. NOUN

/ ˈætləs / (AmE), / ˈatləs / (BrE)
A book of maps or charts, typically of the whole world or a particular region.
book of maps or charts

Examples

She bought a new atlas to help her plan the road trip across Europe.
The teacher used the classroom atlas to show us where the Amazon River is located.
After moving, I found an old atlas filled with detailed maps of every continent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: map - a visual representation of an area

Etymology

Named after Atlas, a Titan in Greek mythology, because early map books were often illustrated with his image.

3. NOUN

/ ˈætləs / (AmE), / ˈatləs / (BrE)
A sculpted figure of a man used as a supporting column or pilaster in classical architecture.
male figure as a supporting column

Examples

The entrance was flanked by an imposing atlas supporting the architrave.
Several ornate atlases lined the facade of the neoclassical building.
In ancient Greek architecture, the atlas is often paired with a caryatid.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: caryatid - a sculpted female figure serving as an architectural support

Etymology

From Greek Atlas, the Titan condemned to hold up the heavens, whose image was used in classical architecture.
