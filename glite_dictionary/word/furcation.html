furcation

1. NOUN

/ fɝˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the point or place at which something splits or divides into branches, commonly used in anatomy and dentistry
point where something splits into branches

Examples

The dentist identified a furcation defect in the patient's molar during the examination.
Root furcation often makes dental treatment more challenging due to the complexity of cleaning the area.
The river's furcation created several small streams flowing through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bifurcation - the division of something into two branches or parts

Etymology

From Latin 'furca' (fork) + '-ation'.
