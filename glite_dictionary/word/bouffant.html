bouffant

1. NOUN

/ buːˈfɑnt / (AmE), / buːˈfɒnt / (BrE)
A woman's hairstyle in which the hair is styled to give a rounded, puffy appearance, often with backcombing or teasing for volume.
puffy, rounded women's hairstyle

Examples

She wore her hair in a dramatic bouffant for the wedding.
The actress became famous for her signature bouffant in the 1960s.
A stylist created a retro bouffant for the fashion show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beehive - a woman's hairstyle with hair piled high and shaped like a beehive

Etymology

From French 'bouffant' (swelling, puffed out), present participle of 'bouffer' (to puff).

2. ADJ

/ buːˈfɑnt / (AmE), / buːˈfɑ̃ / (BrE)
(of a hairstyle or clothing) puffed out in a rounded shape; having fullness and volume.
puffed out in shape (hair or clothing)

Examples

She styled her hair in a bouffant for the party, giving it extra height and volume.
The actress wore a striking bouffant skirt that stood out on the red carpet.
Many 1960s fashion icons popularized the bouffant hairstyle.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From French 'bouffant', present participle of 'bouffer' (to puff, to swell).
