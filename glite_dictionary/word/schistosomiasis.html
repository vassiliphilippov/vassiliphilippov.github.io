schistosomiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪs.tə.soʊˈmaɪ.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌskɪs.tə.səʊˈmaɪ.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with parasitic worms of the genus Schistosoma, typically contracted in tropical or subtropical waters, leading to symptoms that vary depending on the affected organs.
disease caused by Schistosoma parasites
medical

Examples

Doctors identified the patient's chronic liver problems as being caused by schistosomiasis.
Preventing schistosomiasis requires avoiding contact with freshwater in certain tropical regions.
Many health campaigns in Africa focus on treating and reducing cases of schistosomiasis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilharzia - another term for schistosomiasis

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Schistosoma' (the genus name for the parasite) and '-iasis' (disease condition).
