Libocedrus bidwillii

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪboʊˈsiːdrəs bɪdˈwɪliː / (AmE), / ˌlɪb.əˈsiː.drəs bɪdˈwɪliː / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to New Zealand, resembling the kawaka, with scale-like leaves and coniferous cones
evergreen New Zealand tree

Examples

The forest reserve protects stands of Libocedrus bidwillii unique to the region.
Several conservation projects focus on preserving Libocedrus bidwillii due to its limited distribution.
We encountered a towering Libocedrus bidwillii during our hike in New Zealand.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'libo' (to pour) and 'kedros' (cedar); species named after botanist John Bidwill.
