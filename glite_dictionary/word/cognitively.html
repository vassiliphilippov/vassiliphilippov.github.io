cognitively

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑːɡ.nə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.nɪ.tɪv.li / (BrE)
In a way that relates to mental processes such as thinking, understanding, learning, and remembering.
in relation to cognition or mental processes

Examples

Children develop cognitively at different rates depending on many factors.
The experiment measured how participants responded cognitively to new information.
He is highly skilled cognitively, especially in problem-solving tasks.

Synset Examples

cognitively skillful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognitive - relating to mental processes of acquiring knowledge

Etymology

From 'cognitive' + '-ly', where 'cognitive' comes from Latin 'cognitio' (knowledge, recognition).
