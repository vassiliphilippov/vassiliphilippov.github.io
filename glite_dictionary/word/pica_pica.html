Pica pica

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkə ˈpaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪkə ˈpiːkə / (BrE)
The Eurasian magpie, a black-and-white bird of the crow family native to Europe and much of Asia.
Eurasian magpie species

Examples

The Pica pica is often seen collecting shiny objects to decorate its nest.
Unlike other corvids, Pica pica has a striking black-and-white appearance and a long tail.
Ornithologists study the behavior and intelligence of Pica pica across different regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magpie - a long-tailed bird in the crow family, known for its black-and-white plumage and noisy chattering

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'magpie magpie' (doubling the genus and species).
