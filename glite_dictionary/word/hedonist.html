hedonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːdənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhiːdənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that the pursuit of pleasure is the most important goal of life, especially pleasure of the senses
person who seeks pleasure as main goal

Examples

As a self-proclaimed hedonist, he spent his weekends exploring new gourmet restaurants and spas.
The philosopher was criticized as a hedonist for suggesting that pleasure is the highest good.
Despite her image as a hedonist, she valued intellectual pursuits as much as physical enjoyment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epicurean - person devoted to sensual enjoyment, especially that derived from fine food and drink
  • Antonym: ascetic - person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from pleasure

Etymology

From Greek *hēdonē* (pleasure) + -ist.
