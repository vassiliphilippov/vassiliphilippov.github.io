Lubavitch

1. PROPN

/ luˈbɑːvɪtʃ / (AmE), / luˈbævɪtʃ / (BrE)
a major Hasidic Jewish movement known for its outreach, hospitality, optimism, and focus on religious study
Hasidic Jewish movement

Examples

The Lubavitch community is known for its warm hospitality to visitors.
A Lubavitch rabbi opened a new center in the city to offer educational programs.
Many Lubavitch followers dedicate themselves to outreach and religious study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chabad - another name for the Lubavitch Hasidic movement

Etymology

From the town of Lubavitch in Russia, where the movement was founded.
