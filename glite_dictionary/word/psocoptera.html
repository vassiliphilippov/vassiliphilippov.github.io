Psocoptera

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈkɑp.tə.rə / (AmE), / səˈkɒp.tə.rə / (BrE)
an order of small insects that includes booklice and bark-lice, characterized by soft bodies and chewing mouthparts
order of insects including booklice

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Psocoptera order in South America.
Psocoptera are commonly known as booklice and are often found in old books or paper goods.
The classification and evolutionary relationships of Psocoptera have been debated by entomologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - common name for certain insects in the order Psocoptera
  • Synonym: bark-louse - insect in the order Psocoptera that lives on bark

Etymology

From Greek psōk (to rub, to grind) + pteron (wing).

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈkɑːptərə / (AmE), / səʊˈkɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of small insects, including booklice and barklice, characterized by chewing mouthparts and soft bodies
order of insects including booklice and barklice

Examples

Scientists identified several species of Psocoptera living under the loose bark of trees.
The Psocoptera are often referred to as booklice because some species are found among old books.
Recent studies have revised the classification of the Psocoptera within the insect world.

Etymology

From Greek 'psōkos' (rubbed, gnawed) + 'ptera' (wings).
