convexness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛksnəs / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛksnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being convex; the property of having a surface or outline that curves outward like the exterior of a sphere or circle
being convex; outward curvature

Examples

The convexness of the lens helps focus light onto the retina.
Architects admired the convexness of the dome, which gave the building a unique silhouette.
When drawing three-dimensional objects, the convexness of surfaces creates the illusion of depth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concavity - the property possessed by a concave shape; inward curvature
  • Synonym: convexity - the quality of being convex; another term for convexness

Etymology

From Latin 'convexus' (vaulted, arched, convex) + English '-ness'.
