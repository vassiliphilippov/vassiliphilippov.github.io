Kalmia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælmiə / (AmE), / ˈkælmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, including mountain laurel, native to North America and known for their attractive flowers.
genus of evergreen shrubs, mountain laurel

Examples

The garden featured several Kalmia bushes in full bloom along the walkway.
Kalmia is often planted for its striking clusters of pink or white flowers.
Botanists have identified new hybrids within the Kalmia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain laurel - common name for Kalmia latifolia, a species in this genus

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Pehr Kalm, who collected North American plants in the 18th century.
