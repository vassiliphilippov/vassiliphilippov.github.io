senior

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːniər / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə / (BrE)
a person who is older than someone else, often used to indicate seniority by age
an older person; person of greater age

Examples

My senior in the company gave me valuable advice about my career.
She always respects her senior at family gatherings.
At the meeting, each senior was asked to share their experience with the younger members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: junior - a person who is younger than someone else

Etymology

From Latin senior, comparative of senex (old).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːniɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə / (BrE)
having a higher rank, greater age, or longer length of service than others; superior in status or position.
greater in rank or age

Examples

He was appointed as the senior manager in the department.
Despite her young age, she quickly became a senior engineer at the firm.
The senior members of the team guided the new recruits.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin senior, comparative of senex (old).
