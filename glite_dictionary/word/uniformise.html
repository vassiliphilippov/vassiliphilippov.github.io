to uniformise

1. VERB

/ ˈjuː.nɪ.fər.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.fɔː.maɪz / (BrE)
to make or become uniform; to cause something to be consistent, regular, or standardized
make or become uniform
british

Examples

The company decided to uniformise its branding across all markets.
Government policies aim to uniformise health and safety regulations in the industry.
They hope to uniformise the quality of their products through new procedures.

Synset Examples

the data have been uniformized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - make something conform to a standard; make uniform
  • Antonym: to diversify - make more diverse or varied (opposite of making uniform)

Etymology

From uniform + -ise, modelled after French uniformiser.
