bloodshed

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌʃɛd / (BrE)
the killing or wounding of people, especially during violent conflict or war; the shedding of human blood, often on a large scale
killing or violence causing blood to be shed

Examples

The long civil war resulted in unimaginable bloodshed throughout the country.
Leaders are calling for an end to the cycle of bloodshed and revenge attacks.
Historians still debate what led to so much bloodshed during that period.

Synset Examples

a bloodbath took place when the leaders of the plot surrendered
ten days after the bloodletting Hitler gave the action its name
the valley is no stranger to bloodshed and murder
a huge prison battue was ordered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt or kill
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance and violence

Etymology

From Middle English 'blood' + 'shed' (the act of spilling); literally, the shedding of blood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdʃɛd / (BrE)
the killing or wounding of people, typically in a violent conflict or murder, resulting in the shedding of blood
violent killing or wounding; shedding of blood

Examples

The civil war led to years of unbearable bloodshed and suffering among the population.
The government promised to end the cycle of bloodshed and restore order to the region.
Protesters marched in the streets, calling for an end to the bloodshed and violence.

Synset Examples

he avenged the bloodshed of his kinsmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt or kill
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; absence of war or violence

Etymology

From 'blood' + 'shed', indicating the spilling of blood.
