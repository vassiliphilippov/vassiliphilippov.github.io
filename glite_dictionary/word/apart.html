apart

1. ADV

/ əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
(of people or things) separated by a distance in space or time; not together.
separately; at a distance from others

Examples

After the argument, the two friends began to drift apart.
Please keep the dogs apart until they calm down.
They have lived apart since their divorce.

Synset Examples

they grew apart over the years
kept apart from the group out of shyness
decided to live apart

Related Terms

  • Antonym: together - being with each other, not apart

Etymology

From Old French a part (to one side), from Latin ad partem (to the part).
