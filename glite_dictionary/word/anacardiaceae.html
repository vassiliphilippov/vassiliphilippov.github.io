Anacardiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˌkɑrdiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌanəˌkɑːdɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, and vines with resinous or sometimes poisonous sap; members include the cashew, mango, pistachio, poison ivy, and sumac.
family of plants including cashew and poison ivy

Examples

The poison ivy plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, along with mango and cashew.
Botanists studying the Anacardiaceae have discovered many new species of sumac in recent years.
Several members of the Anacardiaceae are cultivated for their edible fruits and nuts.

Etymology

From modern Latin Anacardiaceae, based on Anacardium (the type genus, from Greek ‘upward’ + ‘heart’ referring to the shape of the fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəkɑrdiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌænəkɑːdɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, and vines with resinous or sometimes poisonous sap, including the cashew, mango, pistachio, poison ivy, and sumac.
plant family including cashew and mango

Examples

The Anacardiaceae family includes familiar plants like mangoes and cashews.
Poison ivy, a member of the Anacardiaceae family, can cause skin irritation.
Many species in the Anacardiaceae are valued for their edible nuts and fruits.

Etymology

From botanical Latin, based on the genus Anacardium (the cashew).
