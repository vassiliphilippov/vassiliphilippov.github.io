inoffensive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnəˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Not likely to cause anger, annoyance, or offense; harmless or unobjectionable.
not likely to offend; harmless

Examples

The comedian's jokes were inoffensive and suitable for all ages.
She tried to choose inoffensive topics during the dinner conversation.
His inoffensive demeanor made everyone feel at ease.

Synset Examples

inoffensive behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offensive - causing someone to feel hurt, upset, or angry
  • Synonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'offensivus' (hurtful, offensive).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnəˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Not likely to cause offense; harmless or unobjectionable.
not likely to offend; harmless

Examples

The committee chose an inoffensive name that everyone could agree on.
Her comments were always polite and inoffensive during meetings.
He preferred to keep his wardrobe simple and inoffensive to avoid attracting attention.

Synset Examples

a quiet inoffensive man
a refreshing inoffensive stimulant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offensive - causing someone to feel hurt, upset, or angry; unpleasant

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'offensivus' (offending), meaning 'not offending'.
