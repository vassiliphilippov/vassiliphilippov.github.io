true-blue

1. ADJ

/ truː ˈbluː / (AmE), / truː ˈbluː / (BrE)
completely loyal and faithful; unwavering in support or allegiance
unswervingly loyal or faithful

Examples

She is a true-blue friend who has always stood by me through difficult times.
His true-blue devotion to the team made him a fan favorite for many years.
Only a true-blue supporter would volunteer every weekend without fail.

Synset Examples

a true-blue American
a reliable true-blue country club conservative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyal - faithful and devoted to a cause, organization, or individual
  • Synonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast
  • Antonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties or affections

Etymology

Originally from the blue cloth of Coventry, England, famed in the 16th century for its unwavering color, metaphorically transferred to mean faithful or loyal.
