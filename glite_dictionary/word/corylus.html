Corylus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.ɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ɪ.ləs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees, commonly known as hazels, that produce edible nuts and have separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
genus of hazel trees and shrubs

Examples

Corylus species such as the common hazel are valued for their nutritious nuts.
The botanical garden features several varieties from the Corylus genus.
Researchers studied the pollen of Corylus to understand its role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazel - a shrub or small tree producing edible nuts; member of genus Corylus

Etymology

From Latin 'corylus', meaning hazel tree.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɔːrɪləs / (AmE), / ˈkɒrɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of small trees and shrubs known as hazels, which produce edible nuts and have deciduous leaves
genus of hazel trees and shrubs

Examples

The nuts produced by Corylus species are commonly known as hazelnuts or filberts.
Several varieties of Corylus are cultivated for their edible nuts and ornamental value.
Botanists studied the distribution of Corylus in temperate regions across Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazel - the common name for species within Corylus

Etymology

From Latin *corylus* (hazel tree), from Ancient Greek *korylos* (hazel).
