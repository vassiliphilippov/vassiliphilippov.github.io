wood

1. NOUN

/ wʊd / (AmE), / wʊd / (BrE)
a golf club with a long shaft designed for hitting long-distance shots, originally made with a wooden head but now often made of metal or other materials
type of golf club for long shots

Examples

Many professional golfers prefer to use a wood for their tee shots on long holes.
He carried three woods and eight irons in his golf bag.
With the introduction of metal woods, golfers can hit the ball farther than ever before.

Synset Examples

metal woods are now standard

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iron - a type of golf club with a metal head, used for shorter or more controlled shots

2. NOUN

/ wʊd / (AmE), / wʊd / (BrE)
a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth; a forest or grove
a large area of trees; forest or grove

Examples

We went for a walk in the wood to enjoy the fresh air and birdsong.
A deer emerged from the wood and paused by the river.
The cottage is surrounded by a dense wood on all sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Degree Lower: grove - a small group of trees, especially one cleared for planting or as a natural feature
  • Degree Higher: jungle - an area of land overgrown with dense forest and tangled vegetation, typically in the tropics

Etymology

From Old English 'wudu' (tree, wood, forest).

3. NOUN

/ wʊd / (AmE), / wʊd / (BrE)
A wind instrument, such as a flute, clarinet, or oboe, that is not classified as a brass instrument.
type of wind musical instrument

Examples

The orchestra rehearsed with all the wood and brass instruments present.
She played a wood in the school band, choosing the clarinet as her specialty.
The conductor signaled for the wood section to begin the melody.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brass - a group of wind instruments made of brass, such as trumpet and trombone

4. NOUN

/ wʊd / (AmE), / wʊd / (BrE)
the hard, fibrous substance that forms the trunk and branches of trees beneath the bark, used as a material for fuel, building, and making things
hard substance forming trunks/branches of trees

Examples

The cabin was built entirely out of wood from the surrounding forest.
He carved a beautiful statue from a single block of wood.
We collected some wood to start a fire at the campsite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timber - wood prepared for use in building and carpentry

Etymology

Old English wudu (tree, wood, forest).
