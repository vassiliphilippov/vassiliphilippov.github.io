incapableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being incapable, either physically, intellectually, or legally.
quality of being incapable

Examples

His incapableness in handling complex machinery became apparent during training.
There was a general incapableness among the candidates to address the legal issues involved.
She struggled with a sense of incapableness after her injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incapability - the quality or state of being incapable
  • Antonym: capableness - the quality or state of being capable

Etymology

Formed from 'incapable' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəblnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being incapable; lack of ability or potential for development or achievement.
lack of ability or potential

Examples

Despite his efforts, his constant failures revealed an underlying incapableness for the leadership role.
The incapableness of the old machinery has become a significant obstacle to the company's growth.
Their incapableness to adapt to new technologies left the team behind in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incapacity - inability; lack of ability
  • Antonym: capableness - the quality of being capable

Etymology

From 'incapable' + '-ness', patterned after similar abstract nouns.
