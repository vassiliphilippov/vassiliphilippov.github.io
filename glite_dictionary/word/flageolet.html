flageolet

1. NOUN

/ flædʒ.oʊˈlɛt / (AmE), / ˌflæʒ.əˈlɛt / (BrE)
a variety of small, light green French bean, typically dried and used in cooking
a small, light green French bean

Examples

The recipe calls for a cup of cooked flageolet to add flavor to the soup.
We served lamb with a side of flageolet beans and fresh herbs.
Dried flageolets are popular in French cuisine, especially in stews and salads.

Etymology

From French 'flageolet', diminutive of 'flageole', meaning bean.

2. NOUN

/ ˌflædʒəˈlɛt / (AmE), / ˌfladʒəˈlɛt / (BrE)
a small wind instrument resembling a recorder, having a fipple mouthpiece and usually six finger holes
small fipple flute with six holes

Examples

The musician played a haunting melody on the flageolet during the concert.
She collects rare woodwind instruments, including an 18th-century flageolet.
Learning to play the flageolet helped him appreciate early music traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recorder - a woodwind instrument with a fipple mouthpiece and finger holes

Etymology

From French 'flageolet', diminutive of 'flageol', from Old Provençal 'flaujol' (flute).
