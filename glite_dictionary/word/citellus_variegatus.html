Citellus variegatus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs ˌvɛriˈeɪɡətəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs ˌvɛriˈeɪɡətəs / (BrE)
A large grey ground squirrel found in rocky areas of the southwestern United States, also known as the rock squirrel.
large grey ground squirrel of the US Southwest

Examples

The Citellus variegatus is often seen sunning itself on rocky outcrops in Arizona.
Researchers observed the burrowing habits of Citellus variegatus in its natural habitat.
The Citellus variegatus, or rock squirrel, is well adapted to arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock squirrel - common name for Citellus variegatus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Citellus and species name variegatus, meaning 'variegated' or 'diversely colored.'
