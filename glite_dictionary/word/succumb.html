to succumb

1. VERB

/ səˈkʌm / (AmE), / səˈkʌm / (BrE)
to die or fail to resist an overwhelming force, pressure, illness, or temptation
fail to resist, often fatally

Examples

After a long battle with cancer, he finally succumbed to the disease.
Many plants succumb to drought when water is scarce for extended periods.
Despite her determination, she succumbed to exhaustion and had to rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - give way to force, pressure, or temptation
  • Antonym: to resist - withstand the action or effect of something

Etymology

From Latin 'succumbere', from 'sub-' (under) + 'cumbere' (to lie down).

2. VERB

/ səˈkʌm / (AmE), / səˈkʌm / (BrE)
to give in or consent to something reluctantly or after resistance
consent reluctantly, yield

Examples

After hours of bargaining, she finally succumbed to their demands.
He tried to resist, but he succumbed to the temptation of a second dessert.
The pressure was overwhelming, and even the most stubborn opponent eventually succumbed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of
  • Synonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'succumbere' (to lie under, yield), from sub- (under) + -cumbere (to lie down).
