ischaemia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskiːmiə / (AmE), / ɪsˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which an area of the body, typically tissue or an organ, receives insufficient blood supply, often due to constricted or blocked blood vessels.
insufficient blood supply to a body part
medical british

Examples

The surgeon explained that the tissue damage was caused by ischaemia following a blocked artery.
Untreated ischaemia can lead to permanent loss of function in the affected limb.
Prompt treatment is critical to restore blood flow and reduce the effects of ischaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ischemia - lack of blood supply (American spelling)
  • Degree Higher: infarction - tissue death due to lack of blood supply

Etymology

From Greek 'iskhainō' (to restrain) and 'haima' (blood).
