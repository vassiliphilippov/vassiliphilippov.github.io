undogmatical

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɔɡˈmæt.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɒɡˈmæt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
not inclined to accept authority or dogma, especially in religious matters; open-minded or questioning
not accepting authority or dogma

Examples

Her undogmatical approach to philosophy encouraged lively debates in class.
Many scientists value an undogmatical attitude toward established theories.
He is admired for his undogmatical stance on religious teachings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dogmatic - tending to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true
  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced

Etymology

From un- (not) + dogmatical, itself from Greek dogmatikos (of doctrines).
