to plea-bargain

1. VERB

/ ˈpliˌbɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˈpliːˌbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
to agree to plead guilty to a lesser charge in order to receive a lighter sentence or avoid trial
plead guilty for a lesser charge
legal american

Examples

The defendant decided to plea-bargain rather than risk a lengthy trial.
Prosecutors often encourage suspects to plea-bargain when the evidence is strong.
She plea-bargained and received a much shorter sentence than she might have otherwise.

Synset Examples

If he plea-bargains, he will be sent to a medium-security prison for 8 years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plea bargain - an agreement in a criminal case to plead guilty for lesser charges

Etymology

Formed from 'plea' (legal statement of innocence or guilt) and 'bargain' (agreement); originated in American legal context in the 20th century.
