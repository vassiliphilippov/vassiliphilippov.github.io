to brainwash

1. VERB

/ ˈbreɪnˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɒʃ / (BrE)
to make someone adopt radically different beliefs by using systematic and often forcible pressure or persuasion
forcibly indoctrinate someone with new beliefs

Examples

The cult attempted to brainwash its members into abandoning their families.
She worried that her son would be brainwashed by extremist propaganda online.
It can be difficult to brainwash someone with strong beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indoctrinate - to teach a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • Antonym: deprogram - to free someone from brainwashing or forced indoctrination

Etymology

Formed in English, modeled after Chinese 洗脑 (xǐ nǎo, 'wash brain'), popularized during the Korean War.

2. VERB

/ ˈbreɪnˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
to make someone adopt radically different beliefs by using systematic and often forcible pressure or manipulation
indoctrinate by coercion or manipulation

Examples

The cult tried to brainwash new members into total obedience.
Media outlets have the power to brainwash the public with constant misinformation.
He feared that someone would attempt to brainwash the children while they were abroad.

Synset Examples

The propaganda brainwashed many people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indoctrinate - to teach someone to accept beliefs uncritically
  • Degree Lower: to persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something through reasoning or argument

Etymology

From "brain" + "wash", first used in English in the 1950s as a translation of Chinese xǐ năo (洗脑, wash brain).

3. VERB

/ ˈbreɪnwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɒʃ / (BrE)
to make someone adopt radically different beliefs by using systematic and often forcible pressure or manipulation
manipulate someone to change beliefs

Examples

The cult leaders tried to brainwash new members into abandoning their families.
The government used propaganda to brainwash the public during the war.
She was afraid her son would be brainwashed by extremist groups online.

Synset Examples

He worried the cult would try to brainwash him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indoctrinate - to teach someone to fully accept the ideas of a particular group
  • Degree Lower: to persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something by giving reasons
  • Degree Higher: to coerce - to force someone to do something against their will

Etymology

Formed in the 1950s by calque from Chinese 洗脑 (xǐ nǎo), meaning 'wash brain'.
