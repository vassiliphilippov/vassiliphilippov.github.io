pertness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːtnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtnəs / (BrE)
bold or inappropriate playfulness; impudent liveliness or forwardness, especially in speech or manner.
bold or cheeky playfulness

Examples

Her pertness during the meeting surprised everyone, as she usually spoke so quietly.
The child’s pertness made the adults laugh, even though she was a bit too cheeky.
He answered the teacher with unexpected pertness, earning both a smile and a warning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudence - rude boldness or lack of respect
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest; shyness or reserve

Etymology

From 'pert' (bold, impudent) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix). 'Pert' from Middle English, probably from Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrtnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being lively, confident, and sometimes slightly disrespectful or cheeky
lively or cheeky confidence

Examples

Her pertness made her stand out in the group interview.
There was a charming pertness in his response that made everyone laugh.
He admired the pertness of her quick retorts.

Synset Examples

there was a pertness about her that attracted him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheekiness - the quality of being rude in a lively or amusing way
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'pert' + '-ness', with 'pert' from Middle English, from Old French 'apert' (open, frank).
