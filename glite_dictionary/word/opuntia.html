Opuntia

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈpʌnʃə / (AmE), / əˈpʌntɪə / (BrE)
A large genus of cacti native to the Americas, commonly known as prickly pears, characterized by flat, jointed stems and often edible fruit.
genus of cacti (prickly pears)

Examples

The Opuntia thrives in arid climates across North and South America.
Many farmers grow Opuntia for its edible fruit known as prickly pear.
You can often see wild Opuntia along desert hiking trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly pear - common name for several species of Opuntia cacti

Etymology

From Latin, from the name of the Greek town Opus, where a plant of similar appearance grew.
