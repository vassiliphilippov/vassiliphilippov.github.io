primed

1. ADJ

/ praɪmd / (AmE), / praɪmd / (BrE)
ready and prepared to take a particular action or experience; strongly disposed or on the verge of doing something (usually followed by 'to' or 'for').
ready and prepared for action

Examples

The team was primed for victory after months of hard training.
She is primed to deliver her speech at a moment's notice.
Markets were primed for a sharp reaction following the announcement.

Synset Examples

in no fit state to continue
fit to drop
laughing fit to burst
she was fit to scream
primed for a fight
we are set to go at any time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - prepared and willing for something to happen
  • Synonym: set - in a state of readiness or preparedness

Etymology

Past participle of 'prime', from Latin 'primus' meaning 'first'.
