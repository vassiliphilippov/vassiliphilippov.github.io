dogfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a primitive, long-bodied carnivorous freshwater fish with a very long dorsal fin, found in sluggish waters of North America
primitive North American freshwater fish

Examples

The dogfish is often found lurking near the muddy bottoms of slow-moving rivers.
Anglers sometimes mistake the dogfish for other similar freshwater species.
Scientists are studying the unique hunting behaviors of the dogfish in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burbot - a related type of freshwater fish, sometimes called dogfish
  • Synonym: bowfin - a North American freshwater fish sometimes called dogfish

Etymology

From the resemblance of its predatory habits to those of a dog.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
Any of several small sharks found in coastal waters, typically with a slender body and sharp, spiny fins.
a small shark species

Examples

Fishermen often catch dogfish while looking for cod in shallow waters.
The dogfish is sometimes used in fish and chips in some parts of the UK.
Marine biologists study dogfish to learn more about shark behavior and evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - a large fish with a cartilaginous skeleton and sharp teeth

Etymology

From 'dog' + 'fish', possibly referring to the aggressive nature of some species.
