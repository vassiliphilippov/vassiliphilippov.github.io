plication

1. NOUN

/ plaɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / plaɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of folding or being folded into parallel folds, especially for structural or medical purposes
the act of folding in parallel folds

Examples

The surgeon performed a plication to reduce the size of the stomach.
The fabric's neat plication gave the curtain an elegant look.
Geological plication can result in fascinating rock formations.

Etymology

From Latin plicatio (a folding), from plicare (to fold).

2. NOUN

/ plaɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / plaɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an angular or rounded fold or crease formed by folding a material, often referring to fabric or tissue
a fold made by folding

Examples

The intricate plication on the dress made it look elegant and unique.
Surgeons performed a gastric plication to reduce the size of his stomach.
The artist admired the plication of the paper in the origami sculpture.

Synset Examples

a fold in the napkin
a crease in his trousers
a plication on her blouse
a flexure of the colon
a bend of his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - a crease or line on a surface formed by folding

Etymology

From Latin 'plicatio', from 'plicare' meaning 'to fold'.
