spinnaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnəkər / (AmE), / ˈspɪnəkə / (BrE)
a large, usually triangular sail set forward of the mainsail on a yacht, used when sailing downwind
large sail for sailing downwind

Examples

The yacht unfurled its spinnaker to catch the strong breeze during the race.
Learning how to handle a spinnaker is essential for many competitive sailors.
With the spinnaker billowing, the boat picked up impressive speed downwind.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sail - a piece of fabric on a boat or ship for catching the wind

Etymology

Perhaps from the phrase 'spin akers', a joking reference made by sailors in the late 19th century.
