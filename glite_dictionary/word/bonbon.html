bonbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑnˌbɑn / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.bɒn / (BrE)
A small, round sweet or confection, typically with a soft, creamy, fruity, or nutty center coated in chocolate or fondant.
small sweet with a coated center

Examples

She offered me a bonbon from the colorful box on the table.
The shop specializes in handmade chocolate bonbons filled with fruit or nuts.
He unwrapped a bonbon and popped it into his mouth, savoring the creamy center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chocolate - a sweet food made from roasted cocoa beans, often used as a coating for sweets
  • Synonym: truffle - a type of rich chocolate confection, sometimes similar to a bonbon

Etymology

From French *bonbon*, from *bon* meaning 'good'.
