captopril

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.təˌprɪl / (AmE), / ˈkæp.təˌprɪl / (BrE)
a drug used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure by relaxing blood vessels; an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor
antihypertensive drug (ACE inhibitor)
medical

Examples

Her doctor prescribed captopril to help lower her blood pressure.
Patients with heart failure may benefit from treatment with captopril.
Always take captopril exactly as directed by your healthcare provider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enalapril - another ACE inhibitor used to treat hypertension

Etymology

Blend of caproic acid, proline, and -il (a chemical suffix).
