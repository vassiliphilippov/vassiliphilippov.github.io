storeyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɔːr·id / (AmE), / ˈstɔː.rid / (BrE)
(usually in combination) having a specified number of floors or stories, especially used of buildings.
having a specified number of floors
british

Examples

The city is filled with elegant storeyed buildings from the Victorian era.
They moved into a newly built two-storeyed house in the suburbs.
A six-storeyed office block stands at the corner of the main street.

Synset Examples

a six-storied building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storied - having a specified number of stories (variant spelling, chiefly US)

Etymology

From 'storey' (British for 'floor of a building'), influenced by the suffix -ed, meaning 'provided with.'
