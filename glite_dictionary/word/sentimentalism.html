sentimentalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.t̬əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency to express or be guided by excessive emotion or sentiment, especially in art, literature, or behavior.
tendency toward excessive sentimentality

Examples

The movie was criticized for its sentimentalism and lack of realism.
Her writing indulges in sentimentalism, often focusing on childhood memories and bittersweet nostalgia.
He rejected sentimentalism in favor of a more rational approach to problem solving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Antonym: cynicism - an inclination to believe that people are motivated purely by self-interest

Etymology

From sentimental + -ism; sentimental from French "sentimental", ultimately from Latin "sentire" (to feel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.təl.ɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmɛn.təl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The tendency to express or be influenced by excessive or affected feelings of tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia.
excessive expression of tender feelings

Examples

The film was criticized for its sentimentalism and lack of subtlety.
There is an appealing honesty in her poetry that resists easy sentimentalism.
His speech drifted into sentimentalism as he remembered his childhood home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - the quality of being overly sentimental
  • Antonym: stoicism - the endurance of pain or hardship without the display of feelings

Etymology

From sentimental + -ism, influenced by French 'sentimentalisme'.
