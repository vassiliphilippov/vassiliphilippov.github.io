miasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪæzəm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪazəm / (BrE)
An unpleasant or unhealthy vapor or atmosphere, especially one that was formerly believed to carry disease.
unhealthy vapor or atmosphere

Examples

The swamp gave off a foul miasm that made people feel sick.
A thick miasm of smoke filled the dimly lit bar.
They believed illness spread through the city by an invisible miasm rising from the river.

Synset Examples

the miasma of the marshes
a miasma of cigar smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miasma - unhealthy or unpleasant vapor, poison in the air

Etymology

From Greek miasma (defilement, pollution).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪˌæzəm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪˌazəm / (BrE)
A noxious or unpleasant atmosphere, often considered to be harmful or unhealthy.
unpleasant or unhealthy atmosphere

Examples

A miasm of despair seemed to hang over the abandoned house.
The city was shrouded in a miasm of pollution and neglect.
He described the political climate as a miasm that stifled innovation.

Synset Examples

the novel spun a miasma of death and decay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miasma - an oppressive or unpleasant atmosphere that surrounds or emanates from something

Etymology

From Greek miasma (defilement, pollution).
