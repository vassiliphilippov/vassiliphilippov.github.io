sympathetically

1. ADV

/ ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or relates to the sympathetic nervous system.
involving the sympathetic nervous system
medical

Examples

The drug acted sympathetically to increase the patient's heart rate.
Stimulation occurred sympathetically, indicating involvement of the autonomic nervous system.
The muscles responded sympathetically to the neural signals during the experiment.

Synset Examples

the stimulus acted sympathetically

Etymology

From 'sympathetic' (relating to the sympathetic nervous system) + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that shows sympathy and understanding for someone else's feelings or situation
in a sympathetic manner

Examples

She listened to her friend's problems sympathetically and offered advice.
The teacher responded sympathetically when the student explained why his homework was late.
He smiled sympathetically as she told her difficult story.

Synset Examples

she listened to him sympathetically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassionately - in a compassionate manner

Etymology

From 'sympathetic' + '-ally'; 'sympathetic' from Greek 'sympatheia' (fellow feeling).

Synset POS: r
