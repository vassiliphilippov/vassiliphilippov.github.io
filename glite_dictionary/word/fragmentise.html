to fragmentise

1. VERB

/ ˈfræɡ.mənˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.məntaɪz / (BrE)
to break or cause to break into fragments or small pieces
break into small pieces
british

Examples

The bomb explosion fragmentised the old building instantly.
Over time, exposure to harsh conditions can fragmentise ancient pottery.
The document was fragmentised into several unrelated sections after the editing process.

Synset Examples

The plate fragmented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off something
  • Synonym: to shatter - to break suddenly into many small pieces
  • Antonym: to unify - to make or become united or whole

Etymology

From 'fragment' + '-ise', based on Latin fragmentum (a broken piece).
