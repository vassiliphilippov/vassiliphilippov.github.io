Gauguin

1. PROPN

/ ɡoʊˈɡæ̃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊˈɡæ̃ / (BrE)
Paul Gauguin (1848–1903), a French Post-Impressionist painter known for his bold colors and work in the South Pacific.
French Post-Impressionist painter

Examples

Many art collectors seek works by Gauguin due to his distinctive style.
Gauguin spent several years living and painting in Tahiti.
The museum's new exhibition explores Gauguin's influence on modern art.

Etymology

From the surname of Paul Gauguin, French artist.
