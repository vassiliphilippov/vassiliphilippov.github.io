Tautoga

1. NOUN

/ taʊˈtoʊɡə / (AmE), / tɔːˈtəʊɡə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the wrasse family, which includes tautogs.
a genus of marine fish (tautogs)

Examples

The genus Tautoga includes several species found along the Atlantic coast.
Scientists have studied the behavior and habitat of fish in the Tautoga genus.
Members of the Tautoga genus are commonly known for their robust bodies and strong jaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tautog - an edible marine fish native to the Atlantic coast of North America

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on 'tautog', a Native American term for the fish.
