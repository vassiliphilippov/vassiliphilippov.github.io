Pythoness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪθənəs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪθənɪs / (BrE)
(in ancient Greek mythology) the priestess of Apollo at Delphi, believed to deliver prophecies inspired by Apollo.
priestess of Apollo at Delphi

Examples

In ancient Greece, the Pythoness was consulted by leaders seeking guidance from Apollo.
Legends say the Pythoness spoke in riddles that priests interpreted for the followers.
The temple at Delphi is famous for its Pythoness and mysterious rituals.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pȳthia', from Greek 'Pythiā' (priestess of Apollo at Delphi, named after the python slain by Apollo).
