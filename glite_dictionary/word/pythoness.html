pythoness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪθənˌɛs / (AmE), / ˈpɪθənɪs / (BrE)
a woman regarded as a witch or prophetess, especially one with powers of divination; an oracle
female diviner or prophetic witch
archaic

Examples

In the ancient tale, the pythoness foretold the downfall of the mighty king.
The villagers whispered that the old woman who lived on the hill was a pythoness with uncanny powers.
Legends spoke of a pythoness who delivered prophecies from deep within the temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracle - a priest or priestess acting as a medium through whom advice or prophecy was sought from the gods
  • Synonym: witch - a woman thought to have magic powers, especially evil ones

Etymology

From Latin pythonissa (priestess of Apollo at Delphi), from Greek Pythōnissa, derived from Pythō (Delphi).
