Moirai

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪraɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪraɪ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the three goddesses of fate who control the destiny of gods and humans.
the three Greek goddesses of fate

Examples

In ancient poetry, the Moirai are depicted as spinning the threads of life and fate.
According to legend, not even the gods could change the decisions of the Moirai.
Scholars often compare the roles of the Moirai in Greek mythology to similar figures in other cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fates - the three goddesses of destiny in Greek and Roman mythology
  • Synonym: Norns - the three Norse goddesses of destiny

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μοῖραι (Moĩrai), meaning 'the apportioners' or 'the fates'.
