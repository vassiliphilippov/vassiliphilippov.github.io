antiphrasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈfræ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌantɪˈfrɑːsɪs / (BrE)
the use of a word or phrase in a sense opposite to its usual meaning, often for ironic or humorous effect
ironic use of opposite meaning

Examples

Calling a very tall person "Shorty" is an example of antiphrasis.
Writers often use antiphrasis to inject humor into their stories.
The teacher explained antiphrasis as a way to communicate sarcasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irony - the expression of one's meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite

Etymology

From Greek antiphrasis, from anti- (against) + phrasis (expression).
