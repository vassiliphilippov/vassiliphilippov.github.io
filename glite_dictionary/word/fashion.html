fashion

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfæʃ.ən / (BrE)
the popular or prevailing style in clothing, accessories, or consumer goods at a given time
prevailing style in clothing, etc.

Examples

She always keeps up with the latest fashion and wears trendy clothes.
This summer's fashion features bright colors and loose-fitting dresses.
Many designers showcased their new fashion collections at the city’s annual runway show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing, especially in style
  • Synonym: style - a distinctive appearance, typically determined by the principles according to which something is designed

Etymology

From Old French *façon* (manner, appearance), from Latin *faciō* (I make, do).

/ ˈfæʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfæʃ.ən / (BrE)
to make or shape something by combining materials or parts, often in an improvised or creative way
make or shape (especially creatively)

Examples

She expertly fashioned a hat out of colorful ribbons for the parade.
During the power outage, they fashioned makeshift lanterns from jars and candles.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæʃən / (AmE), / ˈfaʃ.ən / (BrE)
The latest style or trend in clothing, cosmetics, behavior, or appearance, especially one admired and followed by many people.
latest trend in style or appearance

Examples

Paris is often considered the capital of fashion and style.
That jacket is out of fashion this season, but it might come back.
She always tries to keep up with the latest fashion trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trend - A general direction in which something is developing or changing, especially in style or behavior
  • Synonym: style - A distinctive appearance, typically determined by the principles according to which something is designed
  • Antonym: tradition - A long-established custom or belief

Etymology

From Old French 'façon' (manner, appearance), from Latin 'factio' (a making, doing).
