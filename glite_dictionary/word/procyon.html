Procyon

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsaɪ.ɒn / (AmE), / prəʊˈsaɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Procyonidae, which includes raccoons.
genus of raccoons

Examples

The North American Procyon is an adaptable creature found in both forests and cities.
Scientists have studied the behavior of Procyon species to understand their intelligence.
Members of the genus Procyon are known for their dexterous front paws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raccoon - a medium-sized mammal in the genus Procyon

Etymology

From Greek prokyon (before the dog), referring to the animal's dog-like appearance.

2. PROPN

/ ˈproʊsiˌɒn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊsɪɒn / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Canis Minor, visible in the northern hemisphere.
brightest star in Canis Minor

Examples

Procyon is one of the closest bright stars to Earth and is easily visible in the winter sky.
Ancient astronomers named Procyon because it rises before Sirius.
Through the telescope, Procyon appears as a brilliant white point of light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sirius - the brightest star in the constellation Canis Major

Etymology

From Greek prokyon (before the dog), referring to its rising before Sirius (the Dog Star).
