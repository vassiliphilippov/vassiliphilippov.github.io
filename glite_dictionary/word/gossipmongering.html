gossipmongering

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːsɪpˌmʌŋərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪpˌmʌŋərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of spreading personal or sensational information about other people, often in a harmful or indiscreet way
spreading personal or sensational information

Examples

Their constant gossipmongering eventually damaged the reputation of several colleagues.
She warned her friends to avoid gossipmongering at the office party.
Some tabloids profit from gossipmongering about celebrities and public figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - idle talk or rumors about people's affairs

Etymology

From 'gossip' (idle talk or rumors) + 'mongering' (the activity of promoting or spreading something).
