clomipramine

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˈmɪprəˌmin / (AmE), / kləʊˈmɪprəmiːn / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat obsessive-compulsive disorder and depression
a tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed clomipramine for her obsessive-compulsive disorder.
Research has shown that clomipramine can be effective in treating depression.
Patients taking clomipramine should be monitored for possible side effects.

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: derived from 'chloro' (chlorine), 'imipramine' (a related drug).
