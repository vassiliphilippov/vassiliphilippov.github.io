Arianist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛriənɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛːrɪənɪst / (BrE)
A follower of Arianism, the Christian belief that Jesus Christ was not of the same substance as God and therefore not fully divine.
follower of Arianism (belief Christ is not fully God)

Examples

In the fourth century, an Arianist could be found debating fiercely with followers of orthodox doctrine.
Some historians argue that the emperor’s support for an Arianist bishop shaped the early church.
The council condemned the teachings of every prominent Arianist in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arian - a follower of Arianism; relating to Arius or Arianism

Etymology

From Arian, after Arius (c. 250–336), Christian priest, with suffix -ist indicating follower.
