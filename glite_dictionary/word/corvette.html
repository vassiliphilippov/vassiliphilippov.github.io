corvette

1. NOUN

/ kɔɹˈvɛt / (AmE), / kɔːˈvɛt / (BrE)
a small, highly maneuverable warship used as an escort, typically smaller than a destroyer
small maneuverable escort warship

Examples

The navy launched a new corvette designed for coastal defense missions.
During the war, corvettes were often used to escort merchant ships.
A corvette is smaller and faster than most other vessels in the fleet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: destroyer - a fast, maneuverable warship larger than a corvette
  • Degree Higher: frigate - a medium-sized warship, generally larger than a corvette

Etymology

From French 'corvette', possibly from Dutch 'korvet', referring to a small warship.
