tucked

1. ADJ

/ tʌkt / (AmE), / tʌkt / (BrE)
(of fabric, bedding, or similar items) folded or gathered in and secured neatly, often to give a neat or decorative effect.
folded or gathered in and secured neatly

Examples

The sheets were always tucked so tightly that it was hard to get into bed.
She wore a crisp, white blouse with a tucked front for the interview.
Their shirts remained neatly tucked into their trousers throughout the day.

Synset Examples

tightly tucked blankets
a fancy tucked shirt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untucked - not tucked in or secured

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'tuck', originally from Middle English 'tukken' (to pull up, gather).
