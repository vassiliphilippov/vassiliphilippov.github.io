scurfy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɜrfi / (AmE), / ˈskɜːfi / (BrE)
Having a rough or flaky surface; covered with or resembling scurf or scales.
rough or flaky; covered with scales

Examples

The scurfy bark of the old tree made it easy to peel away pieces.
Certain plants have scurfy leaves, giving them a dull and rough texture.
After weeks without grooming, the dog's fur appeared scurfy and unhealthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaly - covered with or resembling scales

Etymology

From 'scurf' (skin flakes) + '-y', forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɝːfi / (AmE), / ˈskɜːfi / (BrE)
covered with or producing scurf; having a flaky or scaly surface or texture.
having a flaky or scaly surface

Examples

After recovering from the rash, her skin remained scurfy and dry for several weeks.
A scurfy deposit built up on the inside of the neglected water tank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaly - covered with or resembling scales

Etymology

From scurf + -y, dating back to the late 16th century.
