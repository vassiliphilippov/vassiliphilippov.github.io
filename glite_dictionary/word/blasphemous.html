blasphemous

1. ADJ

/ ˈblæsfəməs / (AmE), / ˈblɑːsfəməs / (BrE)
showing disrespect or lack of reverence for something considered sacred or holy
irreverent or disrespectful toward the sacred

Examples

His speech was condemned as blasphemous by religious leaders.
The artist's new painting was criticized for being blasphemous by many in the community.
Many consider it blasphemous to joke about sacred texts.

Synset Examples

blasphemous rites of a witches' Sabbath
profane utterances against the Church
it is sacrilegious to enter with shoes on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrilegious - showing disrespect for something sacred
  • Antonym: reverent - showing deep respect for something sacred

Etymology

From Late Latin blasphemosus, from Greek blasphemos (slanderous, blasphemous).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblæsfəməs / (AmE), / ˈblasfəməs / (BrE)
using or containing language that shows disrespect or irreverence towards religion or something sacred.
showing disrespect for religion or sacred things

Examples

Many people found his blasphemous comments offensive during the ceremony.
The book was banned for its blasphemous depiction of sacred figures.
Her jokes were considered blasphemous by the church community.

Synset Examples

foul-mouthed and blasphemous
blue language
profane words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profane - showing disrespect for religion or sacred things
  • Synonym: sacrilegious - violating or profaning what is sacred

Etymology

From Late Latin 'blasphemus' (speaking evil), from Greek 'blasphemos'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00426521-s
Synset POS: s
