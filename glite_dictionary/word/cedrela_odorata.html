Cedrela odorata

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈdrɛlə oʊdəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / sɪˈdriːlə əʊdəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
a tropical American tree with fragrant wood, often used for making boxes and other products
fragrant tropical American tree species

Examples

The cigar box was made from the aromatic wood of Cedrela odorata.
Farmers in Central America grow Cedrela odorata for its valuable timber.
Cedrela odorata trees are commonly found in tropical forests and are prized by woodworkers.

Etymology

From Latin genus Cedrela and species epithet odorata, meaning 'fragrant'.
