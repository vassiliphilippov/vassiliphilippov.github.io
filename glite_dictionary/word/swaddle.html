to swaddle

1. VERB

/ ˈswɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˈswɒdəl / (BrE)
to wrap a baby tightly in cloth or blankets to keep them warm and restrict movement
wrap a baby in cloth or blankets

Examples

She carefully swaddled her newborn to help him feel safe and warm.
Nurses often swaddle infants to help them sleep better in the hospital nursery.
The baby's father practiced swaddling for the first time under the nurse's guidance.

Synset Examples

swaddled the infant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrap - to cover or enclose something in material

Etymology

From Middle English swathlen, related to swath (a band or strip).
