Belamcanda chinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛləmˈkændə kɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbɛləmˈkændə kɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial garden plant whose fruit capsule, when ripe, reveals a cluster of seeds resembling a blackberry; also known as the blackberry lily.
a garden plant with blackberry-like seeds

Examples

The vibrant orange flowers of Belamcanda chinensis make it a popular choice for summer gardens.
After blooming, Belamcanda chinensis develops seed capsules that resemble blackberries.
Gardeners appreciate Belamcanda chinensis for both its decorative flowers and its unusual fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry lily - common name for Belamcanda chinensis

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining the genus Belamcanda and species chinensis, indicating Chinese origin.
