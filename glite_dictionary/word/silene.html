Silene

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈliːni / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as campion or catchfly, with showy flowers in various colours.
genus of flowering plants (campion, catchfly)

Examples

The wildflowers blooming by the roadside included several species of Silene.
Gardeners often cultivate Silene for its vibrant, showy petals.
Many Silene plants are known as 'catchfly' because of their sticky stems that can trap insects.

Etymology

From Latin 'Silene', possibly referring to Silenus, a companion of Bacchus in Greek mythology, due to the sticky secretion of some species resembling the frothy wine often associated with Silenus.
