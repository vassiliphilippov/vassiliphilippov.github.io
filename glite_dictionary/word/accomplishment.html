accomplishment

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɑmplɪʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmplɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of achieving or completing something successfully
the act of achieving something

Examples

Winning the championship was a major accomplishment for the young team.
She felt a deep sense of accomplishment after finishing the marathon.
Completing the project ahead of schedule was a significant accomplishment for the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something achieved, typically by effort, skill, or courage

Etymology

From Old French 'acomplissement', from 'acomplir' (to accomplish), related to Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'complere' (to fill up, complete).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkɑːmplɪʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmplɪʃmənt / (BrE)
an ability or skill that has been acquired through training or practice
acquired skill or ability

Examples

Singing is an accomplishment she developed through years of practice.
He listed piano as one of his accomplishments on his résumé.
Language is an accomplishment that opens doors to new cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well, usually as a result of experience and training
  • Synonym: achievement - a thing done successfully, often through effort or skill

Etymology

From Old French accomplissement, from accomplir (to accomplish).

Synset POS: n
