alpha

1. ADJ

/ ˈæl.fə / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə / (BrE)
(of a product) relating to an early stage in development, especially a version released for initial testing and feedback.
early testing stage in product development

Examples

The software is currently in its alpha stage, so users should expect bugs.
We released an alpha version to gather early feedback from testers.
Only a small group of employees have access to the alpha build.

Synset Examples

alpha version

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beta - the stage following alpha, used for wider testing before release

Etymology

From the first letter of the Greek alphabet, used to indicate the initial stage.

3. ADJ

/ ˈælfə / (AmE), / ˈælfə / (BrE)
being the most important, powerful, or dominant person or thing in a group; first in rank or status.
most important or dominant; first in rank

Examples

In many animal groups, the alpha individual leads and makes decisions for the rest.
She has an alpha personality and naturally takes charge in meetings.
The alpha star in the constellation Orion is easily visible to the naked eye.

Synset Examples

the alpha male in the group of chimpanzees
the alpha star in a constellation is the brightest or main star

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beta - second in importance or rank; subordinate to alpha
  • Antonym: omega - last or least in importance or order; often contrasted with alpha

Etymology

From Greek alpha, the first letter of the Greek alphabet, signifying primacy or importance.

Source: WordNet
