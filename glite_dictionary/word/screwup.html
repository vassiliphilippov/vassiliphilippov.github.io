screwup

1. NOUN

/ ˈskruːˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈskruːˌʌp / (BrE)
a situation that has been completely mismanaged or mishandled; a serious mistake or blunder
serious mistake; mismanagement
informal

Examples

The new software launch was a total screwup and customers complained for weeks.
He got fired after one costly screwup at work.
Their vacation plans turned into a screwup when the airline lost their luggage.

Synset Examples

a typical bureaucratic screwup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Synonym: fiasco - a complete failure, especially in a humiliating way
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'screw up' (to make a mistake), plus nominalization with '-up'.
