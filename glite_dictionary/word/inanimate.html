inanimate

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnænɪmət / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪmət / (BrE)
not alive; lacking the qualities of living things such as movement or consciousness.
not alive; not animate

Examples

The scientist explained how inanimate matter could never possess consciousness.
She arranged the inanimate objects on the shelf with great care.
During the demonstration, the magician seemed to bring the inanimate statue to life.

Synset Examples

the inorganic world is inanimate
inanimate objects

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animate - having life; alive

Etymology

From Latin inanimatus, from in- (not) + animatus (alive, animated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00119049-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnænɪmət / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪmət / (BrE)
appearing dead; not breathing or showing any sign of life.
appearing lifeless or dead

Examples

The victim lay inanimate on the ground until help arrived.
After hours underwater, the diver was pulled out, inanimate and pale.
The doctor checked for a pulse but found the body completely inanimate.

Synset Examples

an inanimate body
pulseless and dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeless - without life; dead or apparently dead
  • Degree Higher: dead - no longer alive
  • Degree Lower: unconscious - not awake and aware of and responding to one's environment

Etymology

From Latin inanimatus, from in- (not) + animatus (alive, animated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00097448-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈnænɪmət / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪmət / (BrE)
(of a noun) denoting a nonliving thing rather than a person or animal.
denoting nonliving things

Examples

In some languages, nouns are classified as inanimate or animate.
Grammatical rules may differ for inanimate objects in certain languages.
The word ‘rock’ is considered inanimate in English grammar.

Synset Examples

the word ‘car’ is inanimate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animate - denoting a living thing, especially a person or animal

Etymology

From Latin inanimatus, from in- (not) + animatus (alive, animated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00121221-a
Synset POS: a
