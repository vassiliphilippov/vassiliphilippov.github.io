tactfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæktfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈtæktfəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being careful not to upset or offend others; showing sensitivity and consideration in dealing with people.
consideration in dealing with others

Examples

Her tactfulness helped to resolve the conflict without anyone feeling hurt.
He answered the awkward question with impressive tactfulness.
A bit more tactfulness in feedback can improve workplace relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or difficult issues
  • Antonym: bluntness - the quality of being direct or abrupt to the point of rudeness

Etymology

From 'tact' (Latin tactus, meaning 'sense of touch') + '-fulness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæktfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaktfəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of showing sensitivity and skill in dealing with others or with difficult issues, so as to avoid offense.
sensitivity in dealing with others

Examples

Her tactfulness made her an excellent mediator during team disputes.
He handled the criticism with admirable tactfulness, avoiding any offense to his colleagues.
Diplomacy requires both honesty and tactfulness in sensitive negotiations.

Synset Examples

In its opinion, the court's 6-3 majority took pains to note that they were not ruling on the wisdom or tactfulness of the governor's decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues
  • Antonym: bluntness - the quality of being uncompromisingly forthright

Etymology

From 'tactful' + '-ness'; 'tactful' from Latin 'tactus' (sense of touch).
