virtually

1. ADV

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.ə.li / (BrE)
in essence or effect but not in fact; almost or nearly as described, though not completely or according to strict definition.
almost; in effect but not in fact

Examples

After the renovations, the building was virtually unrecognizable from its original form.
She has virtually finished her thesis, with just a few edits left to make.
The team was virtually unstoppable during the championship, winning every game except one.

Synset Examples

the strike virtually paralyzed the city
I'm virtually broke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly, but not quite
  • Antonym: actually - in fact; really

Etymology

From Latin 'virtus' meaning 'virtue, strength' + '-ally'.

2. ADV

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tjʊ.ə.li / (BrE)
Almost or nearly, but not completely or exactly; practically.
almost; nearly

Examples

Virtually everyone in the office attended the meeting this morning.
The storm destroyed virtually every building in the village.
The two computers are virtually identical in terms of performance and features.

Synset Examples

the job is (just) about done
the baby was almost asleep when the alarm sounded
we're almost finished
the car all but ran her down
he nearly fainted
talked for nigh onto 2 hours
the recording is well-nigh perfect
virtually all the parties signed the contract
I was near exhausted by the run
most everyone agrees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly, but not completely
  • Synonym: nearly - not quite; almost

Etymology

From Late Latin 'virtualis' (potential, possible), from Latin 'virtus' (power, virtue).
