Fucales

1. NOUN

/ fjuˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / fjuˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of brown algae, coextensive with the family Fucaceae, found mainly in marine habitats.
order of brown seaweeds (algae)

Examples

Many species within the Fucales order are important for marine ecosystems.
Researchers are studying the role of Fucales in coastal environments.
The distinctive air bladders on some Fucales help them stay afloat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fucaceae - family of brown algae related to Fucales

Etymology

From New Latin Fucales, based on the genus Fucus.
