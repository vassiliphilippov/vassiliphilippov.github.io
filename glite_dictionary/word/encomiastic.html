encomiastic

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˌkoʊmiˈæstɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˌkəʊmɪˈastɪk / (BrE)
Formally expressing praise; highly complimentary, especially in official or elaborate language.
formally expressing praise

Examples

The historian's encomiastic speech honored the achievements of the national hero.
Her encomiastic review was published in several prestigious literary journals.
The letter contained several encomiastic remarks about the retiring principal's dedication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudatory - expressing praise or commendation
  • Antonym: critical - expressing adverse or disapproving comments

Etymology

From Greek 'enkomiastikos' (praising), from 'enkomion' (eulogy).
