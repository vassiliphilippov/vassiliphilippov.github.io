aggressiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being inclined to behave in a hostile or forceful way toward others
quality of being hostile or forceful

Examples

Her aggressiveness sometimes intimidates her colleagues during meetings.
The dog's aggressiveness made it difficult for the family to keep him as a pet.
Coaches often value aggressiveness in players who need to win possession of the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly behavior
  • Antonym: meekness - gentle, submissive, or non-aggressive quality

Etymology

From aggressive + -ness, with 'aggressive' from Latin aggressīvus ('fit to attack').

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being bold, energetic, and enterprising, often in pursuit of a goal
quality of being bold and enterprising

Examples

Her aggressiveness in negotiations helped the company secure several major contracts.
Some people view aggressiveness as a strength, while others see it as a flaw.
His aggressiveness on the basketball court intimidated the opposing team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - the quality of being confident and courageous
  • Antonym: timidity - the quality of being shy or lacking in courage

Etymology

From aggressive + -ness. Ultimately based on Latin aggredi (to attack).

3. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡresɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ready or willing to attack or confront; hostile or forceful behavior or attitude
hostile or confrontational attitude

Examples

The player's aggressiveness on the field intimidated his opponents.
Some animals show more aggressiveness when they feel threatened.
Her aggressiveness during negotiations helped secure a better deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly behavior
  • Antonym: passiveness - lack of active response or resistance

Etymology

From 'aggressive' + '-ness'; 'aggressive' from Latin aggredi (to attack).
