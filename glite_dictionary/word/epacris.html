epacris

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpækrɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpækrɪs / (BrE)
Any evergreen shrub of the genus Epacris, grown for its dense spikes of small bell-shaped or tubular flowers.
a genus of evergreen shrubs with showy flowers

Examples

Several varieties of epacris are popular in ornamental gardens for their vibrant flowers.
The epacris thrives in well-drained, acidic soil, making it ideal for rockeries.
Botanists discovered a new species of epacris in the mountainous region.

Etymology

From Greek 'epakris', the name of a plant.
