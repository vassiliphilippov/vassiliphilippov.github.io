Epacris

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpækrɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpækrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of Australian shrubs in the heath family, Epacridaceae, notable for their small bell-shaped flowers
a genus of Australian heath shrubs

Examples

Several species of Epacris thrive in the acidic soils of southeastern Australia.
The delicate pink blossoms of Epacris are a striking feature in the native landscape garden.
Botanists collected specimens of Epacris for study during their expedition.

Etymology

From Greek epakris (an unknown plant), used as a genus name in botanical Latin.
