Xylopia aethiopica

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈloʊpiə iˌθiˈɑpɪkə / (AmE), / zaɪˈləʊpɪə iːˌθiːˈɒpɪkə / (BrE)
A tropical West African evergreen tree whose pungent, aromatic seeds are used as a spice and in traditional medicine.
West African tree with aromatic seeds

Examples

In West African cuisine, Xylopia aethiopica seeds add a distinctive spicy flavor to soups and stews.
The medicinal benefits of Xylopia aethiopica are valued in many traditional healing practices.
Researchers are studying Xylopia aethiopica for its potential antibacterial properties.

Etymology

From the genus name Xylopia, derived from Greek xylon (wood) + ops (appearance); aethiopica from Latin, meaning 'Ethiopian', referring to Africa.
