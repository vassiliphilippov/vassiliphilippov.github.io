barbed

1. ADJ

/ bɑrbd / (AmE), / bɑːbd / (BrE)
having or covered with sharp points or barbs, such as those on wire, feathers, or certain animals and plants.
having sharp points or barbs

Examples

The farmer installed barbed wire around his property for security.
The hedgehog's barbed spines protect it from predators.
Some fish have barbed fins to deter attackers.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - covered with spines or thorns
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, flat surface; not rough or sharp

2. ADJ

/ bɑrbd / (AmE), / bɑːbd / (BrE)
(of words or remarks) intended to hurt or criticize; cutting and sharp in manner or tone.
cutting; intended to hurt or criticize

Examples

She gave a barbed reply that left him speechless.
His review was full of barbed comments about the director's choices.
The debate became heated with several barbed exchanges between the panelists.

Synset Examples

a barbed compliment
a biting aphorism
pungent satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - (of remarks) cruel or intended to hurt
  • Antonym: blunt - (of remarks) uncompromisingly forthright or direct

Etymology

From 'barb', meaning a sharp projection, originally from Middle French 'barbe' (beard, point).
