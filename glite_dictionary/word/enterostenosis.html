enterostenosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.tə.roʊ.stəˈnoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛn.tə.rəʊ.stəˈnəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
Abnormal narrowing of the intestine.
abnormal narrowing of the intestine
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with enterostenosis after experiencing severe abdominal pain.
Surgery may be required to treat enterostenosis if the blockage is significant.
Chronic inflammation can sometimes lead to enterostenosis in certain gastrointestinal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenosis - the abnormal narrowing of a passage in the body

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'stenosis' (narrowing).
