muddled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌdld / (AmE), / ˈmʌdld / (BrE)
(of thinking, explanation, or situation) confused and lacking clear order or understanding.
confused or unclear, especially in thought

Examples

After a sleepless night, his muddled thoughts made it hard to focus at work.
The instructions were so muddled that nobody could figure out what to do next.
Her muddled explanation left everyone more confused than before.

Synset Examples

muddleheaded ideas
your addled little brain
woolly thinking
woolly-headed ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - lacking clarity or order; bewildered
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand or free from confusion

Etymology

From the verb 'muddle', likely from Middle English 'mud' (soft, wet earth), extended to mean 'to mix up' or 'to confuse'.
