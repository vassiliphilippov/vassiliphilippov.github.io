to deepen

1. VERB

/ ˈdiːpən / (AmE), / ˈdiːpən / (BrE)
to make or become deeper
make or become deeper

Examples

The city decided to deepen the harbor to accommodate larger ships.
His feelings for her began to deepen over time.
Heavy rainfall can deepen the grooves in a dirt road.

Synset Examples

They deepened the lake so that bigger pleasure boats could use it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shallow - to make or become shallower
  • Synonym: to intensify - to make stronger or more intense

Etymology

From deep + -en (verb-forming suffix), Old English deopan.

/ ˈdiːpən / (AmE), / ˈdiːpən / (BrE)

Examples

  • Synonym: to intensify - to make or become more intense

Synset Examples

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ ˈdiːpən / (AmE), / ˈdiːpən / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) to become lower in pitch or tone.
become lower in pitch or tone

Examples

As he spoke, his voice began to deepen with emotion.
After the operation, her voice started to deepen unexpectedly.
With age, most boys' voices naturally deepen during puberty.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raise - to make (a sound or voice) higher in pitch

Etymology

From deep +‎ -en, with -en forming verbs meaning 'to make or become', dating back to Middle English.
