harpy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːr.pi / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.pi / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a fierce, winged monster with a woman's head and bird's body; sometimes used figuratively for a predatory or shrewish woman.
monster from Greek mythology; part-bird, part-woman

Examples

In the ancient myth, the harpy snatched away wrongdoers and delivered them to punishment.
The statue in the museum depicts a terrifying harpy with sharp talons and a woman's face.
In some stories, sailors believed a harpy brought storms and bad luck to their ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorgon - another mythical female monster from Greek mythology
  • Synonym: siren - mythical creature part woman and part bird, luring sailors to destruction

Etymology

From Latin Harpyia, from Greek Harpyia, name of a mythological monster.

/ ˈhɑr.pi / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.pi / (BrE)
A fruit bat of the genus Nyctimene, notable for having nostrils shaped into diverging tubes.
a type of fruit bat (Nyctimene genus)

Examples

The harpy is easily recognized by its unique tubular nostrils.
Researchers observed a harpy feeding on ripe figs in the rainforest.
Several species of harpy inhabit the forests of Papua New Guinea.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrpi / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpi / (BrE)
A woman regarded as cruel, grasping, or malicious, especially one with a fierce or unpleasant temper.
malicious or fierce-tempered woman

Examples

The gossip columns described her as a harpy who tormented those around her.
He left his job, saying his manager was a harpy who made everyone's life miserable.
Some critics dismissed the character as a mere harpy, lacking any redeeming qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a woman of violent temper and speech
  • Synonym: virago - a domineering, violent, or bad-tempered woman
  • Antonym: angel - a kind, gentle, and benevolent person (figuratively, especially referring to a woman)

Etymology

From Greek 'Harpyia', mythical winged monster, adopted via Latin 'Harpyia'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːr.pi / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.pi / (BrE)
a large, powerful black-and-white eagle of tropical American forests, known for its prominent crest
large tropical American eagle

Examples

The harpy is one of the largest and most powerful predatory birds in the world.
Researchers tracked a harpy in the Amazon rainforest for conservation purposes.
With its striking crest, the harpy is easily recognizable among tropical eagles.

Etymology

From Greek 'harpyia,' a mythical creature, later applied to the eagle for its fierce reputation.
