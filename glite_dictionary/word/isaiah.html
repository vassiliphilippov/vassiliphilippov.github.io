Isaiah

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈzeɪ.ə / (AmE), / aɪˈzeɪ.ə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament in the Bible, consisting of prophecies attributed to the prophet Isaiah.
book of prophecies in the Old Testament

Examples

The Book of Isaiah contains some of the most famous passages in the Bible.
She studied Isaiah for her course on prophetic literature.
Many scholars discuss the historical context of Isaiah when interpreting biblical prophecy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jeremiah - A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Jeremiah
  • Synonym: Ezekiel - A prophetic book of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew Yəsha‘yāhū (Yahweh is salvation), via Late Latin and Greek.

2. PROPN

/ aɪˈzeɪ.ə / (AmE), / aɪˈzeɪ.ə / (BrE)
A major Hebrew prophet of the 8th century BC, regarded as the first of the major prophets in the Old Testament.
major Hebrew prophet of the Old Testament

Examples

Many scholars have studied the prophecies of Isaiah for their historical and theological significance.
The Book of Isaiah is considered one of the most important texts in the Old Testament.
In his sermon, the pastor referenced Isaiah to highlight the theme of hope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jeremiah - second major Hebrew prophet of the Old Testament
  • Synonym: Ezekiel - third major Hebrew prophet of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew *Yəsha‘Yāhū* meaning 'Yahweh is salvation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11094130-n
Synset POS: n
