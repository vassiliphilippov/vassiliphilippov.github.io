Brahmi

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmi / (BrE)
an ancient script of India, dating from around the 7th century BC, from which many later Indian scripts developed
ancient Indian script

Examples

Scholars have studied the Brahmi script to understand the origins of South Asian writing systems.
The oldest known inscriptions in Brahmi were discovered on ancient stone pillars.
Brahmi gradually evolved into various regional scripts across India.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Brāhmī' (script), traditionally associated with Brāhma, meaning sacred or Brahmanic.
