ultrasound

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌltrəˌsaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəsaʊnd / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal body structures, commonly used to monitor fetal development or examine organs
medical imaging using sound waves
medical

Examples

The doctor used an ultrasound to check the baby's development.
She had an ultrasound to examine her kidneys.
During my pregnancy, I looked forward to each ultrasound appointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonogram - an image produced by ultrasound scanning

Etymology

From ultra- (beyond) + sound, referring to sound waves beyond human hearing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌl.trəˌsaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈʌl.trə.saʊnd / (BrE)
Sound waves with a frequency higher than the upper limit of human hearing, especially as used in medical imaging and examination.
very high frequency sound waves (medical imaging)
medical

Examples

The doctor used ultrasound to examine the development of the baby.
Veterinary clinics often perform ultrasound scans on animals to diagnose internal issues.
Modern ultrasound machines can generate detailed images of soft tissue organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultrasonography - the technique of using ultrasound waves to visualize internal organs
  • Antonym: infrasound - sound waves with frequencies below the limit of human hearing

Etymology

From ultra- (beyond) + sound, indicating frequencies beyond the audible range.
