seismologist

1. NOUN

/ saɪzˈmɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / saɪzˈmɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A scientist who studies earthquakes and the physical properties of the Earth's structure.
scientist who studies earthquakes

Examples

The seismologist presented new findings about the recent earthquake during the conference.
A team of seismologists installed sensors along the fault line to monitor seismic activity.
After the tremor, a seismologist explained its magnitude and possible aftershocks on television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geophysicist - scientist specializing in the physical processes of the Earth
  • Synonym: volcanologist - scientist who studies volcanoes

Etymology

From Greek 'seismos' (earthquake) + '-logist' (one who studies).
