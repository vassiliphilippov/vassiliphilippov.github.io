unlocked

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɑkt / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɒkt / (BrE)
not fastened with a lock; not secured or made inaccessible by a lock.
not secured by a lock

Examples

She left the front door unlocked while she went to get the mail.
Make sure the windows remain unlocked in case of emergency.
The safe was accidentally left unlocked overnight.

Synset Examples

an unbarred door
went through the unlatched gate into the street
an unlocked room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: locked - fastened with a lock
  • Synonym: open - not closed or fastened

Etymology

from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'locked', from Middle English 'locken', from Old English 'loccian'.
