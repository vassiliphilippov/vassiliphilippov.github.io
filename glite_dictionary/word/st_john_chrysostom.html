St. John Chrysostom

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt dʒɑn krɪˈsɒstəm / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn krɪˈsɒstəm / (BrE)
(c. 347–407) An early Church Father, celebrated preacher, and Archbishop of Constantinople, recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
early Church Father and Archbishop of Constantinople

Examples

Many sermons of St. John Chrysostom are still read in Christian liturgy today.
As Archbishop of Constantinople, St. John Chrysostom became renowned for his eloquent homilies.
The feast day of St. John Chrysostom is celebrated by several Christian denominations.

Etymology

From Greek 'Chrysostomos' meaning 'golden-mouthed', referring to his eloquence in preaching.
