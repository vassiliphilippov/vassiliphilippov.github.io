protractedly

1. ADV

/ proʊˈtræktɪdli / (AmE), / prəˈtræktɪdli / (BrE)
In a slow, leisurely, or prolonged manner; lasting longer than usual or expected.
in a prolonged or extended way

Examples

The negotiations dragged on protractedly, frustrating both parties involved.
He explained his reasoning protractedly, making sure not to leave out any details.
The ceremony concluded protractedly, with many speeches and slow transitions.

Synset Examples

her voice was swift, yet ever the last words fell lingeringly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingeringly - in a way that lasts for a long time or is slow to end

Etymology

From 'protracted' (from Latin 'protractus' meaning 'extended'), plus '-ly'.
