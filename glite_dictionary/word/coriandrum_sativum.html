Coriandrum sativum

1. NOUN

/ kɔːriˈændrəm səˈtɪvəm / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.iˈæn.drəm səˈtɪv.əm / (BrE)
An Old World herb with aromatic leaves and seeds, widely used in cooking; also known as coriander or cilantro.
herb; coriander; cilantro

Examples

Coriandrum sativum is commonly grown for its fragrant leaves and seeds used in many cuisines worldwide.
The chef sprinkled fresh leaves of Coriandrum sativum over the spicy curry just before serving.
In some regions, Coriandrum sativum is called cilantro, while in others, it's known as coriander.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - the common name for Coriandrum sativum, especially the seeds
  • Synonym: cilantro - the culinary name for the leaves of Coriandrum sativum

Etymology

From Latin: Coriandrum (coriander) + sativum (cultivated).
