Lucrezia Borgia

1. PROPN

/ luːˈkreɪtsiə ˈbɔrdʒə / (AmE), / luːˈkreɪtsiə ˈbɔːdʒiə / (BrE)
(1480–1519) An Italian noblewoman and patron of the arts, known for her influential role in Renaissance Italy and as a member of the prominent Borgia family.
Italian noblewoman and Renaissance patron

Examples

Lucrezia Borgia is remembered for her connections to several powerful families during the Italian Renaissance.
Many historians have reevaluated the life of Lucrezia Borgia, separating myth from fact.
Paintings and letters provide insight into the courtly life led by Lucrezia Borgia in Ferrara.
