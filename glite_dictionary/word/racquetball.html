racquetball

1. NOUN

/ ˈrækɪtˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈrakɪtbɔːl / (BrE)
A small, hollow rubber ball used in the game of racquetball.
ball used in the sport of racquetball

Examples

Make sure you use a blue racquetball for tournament play.
I bought a new racquetball because the old one lost its bounce.
The racquetball bounced off the wall and rolled across the court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racquetball - an indoor game using rackets and a hollow rubber ball

2. NOUN

/ ˈrækɪtˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈrækɪtˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A game played with short-handled rackets and a hollow rubber ball on an indoor court with walls, similar to handball.
indoor game with rackets and a rubber ball

Examples

He enjoys playing racquetball twice a week at the local gym.
The racquetball court was fully booked for the evening.
After a strenuous racquetball match, they relaxed in the clubhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - a similar indoor racket sport played on a four-walled court

Etymology

From 'racquet' (variant of 'racket') + 'ball'; term popularized in the United States in the mid-20th century.
