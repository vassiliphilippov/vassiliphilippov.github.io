empty-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛmptiˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛmptiˈhændɪd / (BrE)
Not carrying or bringing anything, especially when something was expected.
carrying or bringing nothing

Examples

He returned from the store empty-handed because they were out of stock.
Despite searching for hours, she came back empty-handed.
The burglar fled empty-handed when the alarm went off.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hands-free - not needing to be held in the hands
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01090989-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛmpti ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛmpti ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
having gained or obtained nothing; without achieving one's aim or desired result
having gained nothing

Examples

She left the store empty-handed because nothing caught her eye.
Despite hours of searching, they returned empty-handed.
He applied for several jobs but remained empty-handed after all the interviews.

Synset Examples

they returned from the negotiations empty-handed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsuccessful - not having achieved success
  • Antonym: successful - having achieved a desired result
