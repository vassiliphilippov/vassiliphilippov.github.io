arteria ileocolica

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə ˌaɪliːoʊˈkɒlɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌɪliːəʊˈkɒlɪkə / (BrE)
a branch of the superior mesenteric artery that supplies blood to the terminal ileum, cecum, appendix, and ascending colon
artery supplying ileum, cecum, appendix, and ascending colon
medical

Examples

In surgery, care must be taken not to damage the arteria ileocolica when removing the appendix.
The arteria ileocolica branches off from the superior mesenteric artery.
A blockage in the arteria ileocolica can compromise blood supply to the cecum and appendix.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) and 'ileocolica' (relating to the ileum and colon).
