deceit

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsit / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːt / (BrE)
the act of deliberately causing someone to believe something that is not true; deception or trickery
the act of deceiving; deception

Examples

He used deceit to gain the trust of his colleagues.
The politician's career was ruined by accusations of deceit and dishonesty.
It is hard to rebuild a relationship once deceit has been discovered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone; trickery or fraud
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free of deceit

Etymology

From Old French *deceite*, from Latin *deceptus* (deceived), from *decipere* (to deceive).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈsit / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːt / (BrE)
the act or practice of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth; dishonest behavior or trickery
dishonest behavior; deception

Examples

He was known for his deceit in business dealings and lost many clients because of it.
Their relationship was destroyed by lies and deceit.
The detective soon uncovered the deceit that was at the heart of the scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving; tricking or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

From Old French *deçait*, from Latin *deceptum*, past participle of *decipere* (to deceive).
