Fumaria claviculata

1. PROPN

/ fuːˈmɛəriə klævɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / fjuːˈmɑːriə klævɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
An annual climbing plant with finely divided leaves and racemes of yellow and pink flowers, belonging to the fumitory family.
a flowering annual vine with yellow and pink flowers

Examples

Fumaria claviculata is often found growing on rocky slopes and fields in the Mediterranean region.
The delicate yellow and pink flowers of Fumaria claviculata make it a distinctive member of the fumitory family.
Botanists documented the spread of Fumaria claviculata across new habitats in southern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin: Fumaria (the genus name), from Latin 'fumus' (smoke), and claviculata (having tendrils), from Latin 'clavicula' (small key or tendril).
