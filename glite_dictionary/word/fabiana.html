Fabiana

1. NOUN

/ fəˈbaɪ.ən.ə / (AmE), / faˈbiː.ə.nə / (BrE)
A genus of South and Central American evergreen shrubs with heath-like foliage, belonging to the nightshade family.
genus of South/Central American evergreen shrubs

Examples

The medicinal properties of Fabiana have been studied by researchers in Argentina.
Several species within the genus Fabiana are used in traditional herbal remedies.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Fabiana in the foothills of the Andes.

Etymology

From Latinization of the name of Francisco Fabiano, an 18th-century Spanish botanist.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈbiːənə / (AmE), / fəˈbiːənə / (BrE)
A genus of heath-like evergreen shrubs native to South and Central America, belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).
genus of South American evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Fabiana genus includes several shrubs with medicinal properties.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Fabiana genus in Argentina.
Fabiana plants are adapted to dry, mountainous regions in South America.

Etymology

Named after Fabián, possibly in honor of a person or place; origin of the scientific name from Latinized form.
