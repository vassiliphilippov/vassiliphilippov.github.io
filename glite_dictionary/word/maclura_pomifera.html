Maclura pomifera

1. NOUN

/ məˈklʊrə ˌpoʊməˈfɪrə / (AmE), / məˈklʊərə ˌpɒmɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of the mulberry family, native to the south-central United States, known for its spiny branches, glossy leaves, inedible fruit resembling an orange, and hard yellow-orange wood traditionally used for making bows.
deciduous tree with inedible orange-like fruit, native to the U.S.

Examples

The hard wood of the Maclura pomifera was prized for making bows by Native Americans.
A tall hedge of Maclura pomifera bordered the old farm property.
The inedible, wrinkled fruit of the Maclura pomifera is often mistaken for an orange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Osage orange - Common name for Maclura pomifera, a deciduous tree with inedible fruit and hard wood

Etymology

From Latin; 'Maclura' named after American geologist William Maclure, and 'pomifera' meaning 'fruit-bearing' from Latin 'pomum' (fruit) + 'ferre' (to bear).
