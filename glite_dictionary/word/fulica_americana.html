Fulica americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuː.lɪ.kə əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈfjuː.lɪ.kə əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nə / (BrE)
The species of coot native to North America, known as the American coot, a medium-sized water bird with a white bill and dark plumage.
species of North American coot

Examples

The wetlands are home to many birds, including the Fulica americana.
During migration, large flocks of Fulica americana can be seen on inland lakes.
Field guides often list Fulica americana under its common name, the American coot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American coot - the common name for Fulica americana, a water bird found across North America

Etymology

From Latin: Fulica (coot) and americana (of America).
