macromolecular

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæk.roʊ.məˈlɛk.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊ.məˈlɛk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of very large molecules, especially those important in biology such as proteins, nucleic acids, or synthetic polymers.
of or relating to large molecules
medical

Examples

The researchers studied the macromolecular structure of the protein to understand how it works.
Many synthetic fibers are created through macromolecular processes in industrial chemistry.
The macromolecular weight of DNA makes it difficult to analyze without specialized equipment.

Synset Examples

macromolecular compounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macromolecule - a very large molecule, such as a protein or polymer

Etymology

From 'macromolecule,' from Greek makros (large) + molecule.
