papism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpɪzəm / (BrE)
(historical, often offensive) The doctrines, beliefs, and practices of the Roman Catholic Church, especially as viewed by opponents.
doctrines and practices of the Roman Catholic Church
archaic offensive

Examples

The rise of papism in the region alarmed many Protestant leaders.
During the Reformation, fierce debates erupted over accusations of papism.
He was accused of secretly promoting papism among his followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholicism - the faith, practice, and system of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From French papisme, from pape (pope) + -isme (-ism).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpɪzəm / (BrE)
An offensive term referring to the practices, beliefs, or system of the Roman Catholic Church, especially as used by its opponents.
offensive term for Roman Catholicism
offensive archaic

Examples

The pamphlet was filled with attacks on the so-called papism of his rivals.
Some reformers denounced holidays as remnants of papism corrupting true faith.
In seventeenth-century England, accusations of papism carried serious political weight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholicism - The faith, practices, and church order of the Roman Catholic Church
  • Antonym: Protestantism - The faith, practices, and system of Protestant Christian churches

Etymology

From French papisme, from pape (pope), ultimately from Latin papa (pope).
