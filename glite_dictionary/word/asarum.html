Asarum

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛərəm / (AmE), / əˈsɑːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants commonly known as wild ginger, found in temperate regions and noted for their heart-shaped leaves and underground stems.
genus of wild ginger plants

Examples

The leaves of Asarum are often heart-shaped and have a pleasant spicy scent.
Gardeners sometimes plant Asarum in shaded woodland gardens for its ground cover.
Many herbalists use the root of Asarum as a traditional remedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild ginger - common name for plants of the genus Asarum

Etymology

From Greek 'asarion', diminutive of 'ason', a plant name.
