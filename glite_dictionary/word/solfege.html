solfege

1. NOUN

/ sɑlˈfɛdʒ / (AmE), / sɒlˈfɛdʒ / (BrE)
A method of teaching singing and pitch using syllables (do, re, mi, etc.) to denote the notes of a scale.
music teaching method using syllables

Examples

Many music teachers introduce students to solfege to help them develop a sense of pitch.
She practiced her solfege daily, singing the scale from do to do.
Understanding solfege can make it easier to sight-read musical notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfeggio - alternative term for solfege

Etymology

From French 'solfège', from Italian 'solfeggio', from 'solfa' (sol-fa syllables).

2. NOUN

/ soʊlˈfɛdʒ / (AmE), / sɒlˈfɛdʒ / (BrE)
A system of vocal exercises in which musical scales are sung to specific syllables, often used to teach pitch and sight singing.
singing musical scales with syllables

Examples

The choir began their warm-up with solfege to practice pitch accuracy.
She learned to read music more easily by practicing daily solfege sessions.
Many music students find solfege helpful for developing a better ear for melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfeggio - singing exercises using sol-fa syllables

Etymology

From French 'solfège', from Italian 'solfeggio', related to the sol-fa syllables used in music.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
