Bombus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑmbəs / (AmE), / ˈbɒmbəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, hairy bees commonly known as bumblebees, characterized by their robust bodies and important role in pollination.
genus of bumblebees

Examples

Several species within the genus Bombus are vital pollinators for wildflowers and crops.
Entomologists conducted a survey of Bombus populations across European meadows.
The decline of Bombus colonies has raised concerns about biodiversity and agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumblebee - a large, fuzzy bee of the genus Bombus

Etymology

From Latin 'bombus' (a buzzing or booming sound), adopted as a genus name in biological classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑmbəs / (AmE), / ˈbɒmbəs / (BrE)
A genus of hairy, robust bees commonly known as bumblebees, found in temperate climates and known for their ability to pollinate flowers by vibrating them.
genus of bumblebees

Examples

The Bombus genus is renowned for its role in pollinating crops and wildflowers.
Several species within Bombus are adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.
Researchers are studying how Bombus populations respond to environmental changes.

Etymology

From Latin bombus (a buzzing or humming sound), used in taxonomy for bumblebees.
