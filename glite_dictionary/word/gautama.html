Gautama

1. PROPN

/ ɡaʊˈtɑmə / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊtəmə / (BrE)
the founder of Buddhism, who lived in ancient India around 563–483 BC, also known as Siddhartha Gautama or the Buddha.
founder of Buddhism

Examples

Many followers of Buddhism regard Gautama as the Enlightened One who shared his insights with the world.
Scholars have studied the teachings of Gautama to better understand early Buddhist philosophy.
Paintings often depict Gautama seated in meditation beneath the Bodhi tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddha - title given to Siddhartha Gautama, the founder of Buddhism

Etymology

From Sanskrit Gautama (गौतम), the family name of Siddhartha, the Buddha.
