Himantopus himantopus

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəpəs hɪˌmæntəpəs / (AmE), / hɪˌmantəpəs hɪˌmantəpəs / (BrE)
A species of stilt found in Europe, Africa, and Asia, with mainly white plumage and black wings.
species of stilt with black wings

Examples

The wetland reserve is home to the rare Himantopus himantopus during the breeding season.
A group of birdwatchers spotted several Himantopus himantopus feeding in the shallow waters.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Himantopus himantopus in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-winged stilt - Common name for Himantopus himantopus

Etymology

From Greek himantopus, meaning 'strap-foot', referring to the stilt's long legs.
