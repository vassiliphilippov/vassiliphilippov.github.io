dissilient

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪliənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪliənt / (BrE)
Bursting open with force, as do some ripe seed pods or capsules, especially to release seeds.
bursting open forcefully (of seed pods)

Examples

The dissilient pods of the plant scatter seeds several meters from the parent plant.
Some species use a dissilient mechanism to disperse their seeds explosively.
Botanists observed the dissilient fruits rapidly releasing seeds when touched.

Etymology

From Latin dissilīre (to leap apart, burst asunder), from dis- (apart) + salīre (to leap).
