multiphase

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈfeɪz / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈfeɪz / (BrE)
(of an electrical system) using or producing two or more alternating voltages of the same frequency but different phase angles.
using two or more alternating voltages in different phases

Examples

The new factory installed a multiphase power system to handle heavy machinery.
A multiphase generator supplies consistent power to large industrial plants.
Engineers checked the wiring to ensure the multiphase circuit was correctly connected.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-phase - having or using only one alternating voltage or current
  • Degree Higher: three-phase - an electrical system using three alternating currents of the same frequency but different phases

Etymology

From multi- (many) + phase (from Greek phasis, appearance, phase).
