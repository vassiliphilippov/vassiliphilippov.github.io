coloration

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the choice and use of colors, especially by an artist or in a work of art
use of color by an artist

Examples

The vivid coloration of the painting caught everyone's attention in the gallery.
Impressionist artists are well known for their unique approach to coloration.
Her designs are admired for their bold and innovative coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color scheme - an arrangement or combination of colors, especially as used in interior decoration or art
  • Antonym: monochrome - a work of art having only one color or shades of one color

Etymology

From Latin 'coloratio' (a coloring).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The appearance or pattern of colors on the surface of something, especially as it affects its overall look.
appearance or pattern of colors

Examples

The vivid coloration of the butterfly helps it to ward off predators.
Her cheeks had a rosy coloration after the brisk walk in the cold air.
Scientists studied the coloration patterns of tropical fish to understand their behavior.

Synset Examples

her healthy coloration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - the property possessed by an object of producing different sensations on the eye as a result of the way it reflects or emits light
  • Synonym: hue - a particular shade or tint of a given color

Etymology

From Latin 'coloratio' (act of coloring), from 'colorare' (to color).

musical timbre or tone color

Examples

Some amplifiers add their own coloration to the sound, changing how instruments are perceived.
The subtle coloration of her voice added warmth to the song's melody.

Related Terms
