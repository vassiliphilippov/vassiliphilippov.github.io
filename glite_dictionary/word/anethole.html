anethole

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌθoʊl / (AmE), / ˈanɪˌθəʊl / (BrE)
an aromatic compound, widely used as a flavoring substance, especially in licorice, anise, and fennel
aromatic flavoring compound

Examples

Anethole gives licorice its characteristic sweet flavor and aroma.
Many alcoholic drinks, such as ouzo and absinthe, are flavored with anethole.
Some candies and baked goods use anethole as a natural sweetener and flavoring agent.

Synset Examples

Anethole is an aromatic, unsaturated ether related to lignols.

Etymology

From French anéthol, from Latin anethum (dill, or anise).
