destiny

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛstəˌni / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪni / (BrE)
A person's overall circumstances or condition in life, including everything that happens to them.
circumstances or condition in life

Examples

Sometimes hard work can change your destiny for the better.
He accepted his destiny without complaint, believing everything happens for a reason.
She wondered if her difficult childhood was simply her destiny.

Synset Examples

whatever my fortune may be

has a happy lot
the luck of the Irish
a victim of circumstances
success that was her portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control; fate
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external, controlling force in life

Etymology

From Latin destinata, from destinare (to make firm, establish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛs.tɪ.ni / (AmE), / ˈdɛs.tɪ.ni / (BrE)
the supposed force or power that determines future events and is believed to be beyond human control
preordained fate or power controlling events

Examples

Many people believe that their destiny is written in the stars.
She felt it was her destiny to become a great leader.
No one can escape their destiny, no matter how hard they try.

Synset Examples

we are helpless in the face of destiny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • Antonym: chance - the occurrence of events in the absence of any obvious intention or cause

Etymology

From Latin 'destinata', past participle of 'destinare' (to make firm, establish).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛstəni / (AmE), / ˈdɛstəni / (BrE)
the events that will inevitably happen to a particular person or thing in the future; the power believed to control future events
the inevitable course of future events

Examples

Many people believe that true love is their destiny and will happen no matter what.
He worked hard, convinced that his destiny was to become a leader.
Some say it was destiny that brought the two old friends back together after so many years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events outside a person's control, considered determined by a supernatural power
  • Synonym: doom - a negative or disastrous fate or destiny
  • Antonym: chance - events that happen unpredictably; the absence of deliberate planning or premeditation

Etymology

From Latin 'destinare' (to make firm, establish), via Old French 'destinee' (purpose, fate).
