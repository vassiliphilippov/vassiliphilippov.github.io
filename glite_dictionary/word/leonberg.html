Leonberg

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɒnˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈliːɒnˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a large breed of dog, usually with a golden coat, developed by crossing a St Bernard and a Newfoundland.
large breed of dog, golden coat

Examples

The Leonberg is known for its gentle temperament and impressive size.
His family adopted a Leonberg puppy from a reputable breeder.
Because of its heritage, the Leonberg is both strong and friendly, making it a popular companion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St Bernard - a large working dog breed from the Swiss Alps
  • Synonym: Newfoundland - a large breed of working dog from Newfoundland, Canada

Etymology

Named after the town of Leonberg in southwestern Germany, where the breed was originally developed.
