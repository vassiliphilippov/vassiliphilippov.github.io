cocoanut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkənʌt / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of 'coconut', a large hard-shelled oval nut with a fibrous husk, thick white edible flesh, and a central cavity with liquid
alternative spelling of coconut (the large tropical nut)
archaic

Examples

The explorer noted the abundance of cocoanuts along the tropical shoreline.
In the old recipe book, it is spelled as cocoanut instead of coconut.
She broke open a cocoanut to drink its fresh milk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coconut - the standard modern spelling for cocoanut

Etymology

Altered spelling of 'coconut', from Portuguese 'coco' (grinning face, coconut) + English 'nut'.
