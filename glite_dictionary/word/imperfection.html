imperfection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmpərˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmpəˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
The state or quality of being imperfect; a flaw, weakness, or defect in something or someone.
state of being imperfect or having a flaw

Examples

She accepted her imperfection and learned to appreciate her unique qualities.
The diamond had a tiny imperfection that was barely noticeable.
No one is without imperfection, and that's what makes us human.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfection - the state of being perfect, without flaws or defects
  • Synonym: flaw - a mark, fault, or other imperfection that mars a substance or object

Etymology

From Latin 'imperfectionem', from 'im-' (not) + 'perfectio' (completeness, perfection).
