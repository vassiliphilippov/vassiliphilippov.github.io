garbology

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈbɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈbɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of a society or culture by examining its refuse and waste material
study of society via garbage analysis

Examples

The city hired experts in garbology to learn about residents' consumption habits.
Her thesis in garbology helped reveal changing food trends over the decades.
As a pioneer in garbology, he examined landfills for clues about urban life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From English 'garbage' + '-ology' (the study of).
