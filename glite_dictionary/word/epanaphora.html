epanaphora

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈnæfərə / (AmE), / ˌɛpəˈnafərə / (BrE)
The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences, used as a rhetorical device.
repetition at start of clauses (rhetoric)

Examples

The speech used epanaphora to emphasize key themes in each paragraph.
Writers often employ epanaphora to create rhythm and build emotional power in their prose.
In poetry, epanaphora can turn simple lines into memorable refrains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaphora - the repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses

Etymology

From Greek epanaphora, meaning 'repetition.'
