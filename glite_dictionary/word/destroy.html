to destroy

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstrɔɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪ / (BrE)
to defeat someone completely and humiliatingly, especially in a contest or competition
defeat utterly and humiliatingly
informal

Examples

The visiting team was completely destroyed in the second half.
She destroyed her opponent in the debate with sharp arguments.
Their new marketing strategy destroyed the competition this year.

Synset Examples

The home team demolished the visitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - win a victory over; overcome in a contest
  • Synonym: to trounce - defeat heavily in a contest
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01085766-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈstrɔɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪ / (BrE)
to put an animal to death, especially for humane reasons or by official order
put an animal to death

Examples

The veterinarian had to destroy the injured raccoon because it could not be saved.
Regulations require officials to destroy livestock that test positive for certain diseases.
The animal shelter sometimes has no choice but to destroy severely ill animals.

Synset Examples

The customs agents destroyed the dog that was found to be rabid
the sick cat had to be put down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to euthanize - to put (a living creature) to death humanely
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01329210-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈstrɔɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪ / (BrE)
to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be used or repaired
to completely ruin or put an end to

Examples

The storm destroyed several buildings in the town last night.
If you drop your phone in water, it might destroy the device completely.
The old documents were destroyed to protect people's privacy.

Synset Examples

The fire destroyed the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - to severely damage so that no longer works or is valuable
  • Synonym: to demolish - to completely destroy a building
  • Antonym: to create - to bring something into existence

Etymology

From Latin destruere (to unbuild, demolish) via Old French destruire.
