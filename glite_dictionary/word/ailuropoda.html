Ailuropoda

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌlʊərəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / aɪˌljuərəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a genus of mammals in the bear family, including only the giant panda
genus including the giant panda

Examples

The giant panda is the sole living species within the genus Ailuropoda.
Researchers debated whether Ailuropoda should be placed in its own family, Ailuropodidae.
Fossil evidence suggests that ancient members of Ailuropoda once ranged across much of Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant panda - large black and white bear native to China

Etymology

From Greek 'ailuros' (cat) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the cat-like feet.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˌlʊəroʊˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / aɪˌljuːərəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A genus of bears that includes only the giant panda, sometimes placed in its own family, Ailuropodidae.
genus of the giant panda

Examples

The only living species in the genus Ailuropoda is the giant panda.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Ailuropoda fossils found in China.
Conservationists are working to protect Ailuropoda habitats from deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant panda - large black-and-white bear native to China

Etymology

From Greek 'ailuros' (cat) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to panda's cat-like feet.
