Exaeretodon

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɪərəˈtoʊdən / (AmE), / ɛkˌsɪərɪˈtəʊdən / (BrE)
A genus of extinct reptiles in the division Cynodontia, known from the Late Triassic period.
extinct genus of Cynodont reptiles

Examples

Fossils of Exaeretodon have been discovered in South America and India.
The genus Exaeretodon provides important clues about the evolution of early mammals.
Paleontologists classify Exaeretodon as a member of the Cynodontia division.

Etymology

From Greek exairetos (remarkable, distinguished) and odon (tooth).

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˈsɪərətoʊdɒn / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɪərətədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of extinct cynodont reptiles that lived during the Triassic period, known from fossil remains in South America and India.
extinct cynodont reptile genus

Examples

Fossils of Exaeretodon have provided important insights into the evolution of early mammals.
The Exaeretodon genus was first described in the early 20th century from Triassic deposits.
Paleontologists discovered a nearly complete skull of Exaeretodon in Argentina.
