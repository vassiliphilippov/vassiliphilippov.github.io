bouquet

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / buːˈkeɪ / (BrE)
A carefully arranged collection of flowers, typically given as a gift or carried on special occasions.
arrangement of flowers given as a gift

Examples

She received a beautiful bouquet of roses for her birthday.
The bride held a white bouquet as she walked down the aisle.
He handed her a bouquet to celebrate her promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posy - a small bunch of flowers

Etymology

From French 'bouquet' (bunch of flowers).

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / buːˈkeɪ / (BrE)
a distinct and pleasant scent, especially the characteristic fragrance of wine or perfume
distinctive scent, especially of wine or perfume

Examples

The wine's bouquet was rich with notes of cherry and oak.
She chose a perfume for its delicate floral bouquet.
A complex bouquet filled the kitchen as the spices simmered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aroma - a distinctive, typically pleasant smell
  • Synonym: odor - a particular smell, especially one that is distinctive
  • Antonym: stench - a strong and very unpleasant smell

Etymology

From French 'bouquet' (a bunch of flowers, fragrance).
