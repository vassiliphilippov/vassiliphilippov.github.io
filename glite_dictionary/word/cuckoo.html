cuckoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːku / (AmE), / ˈkʊkuː / (BrE)
a bird of the family Cuculidae, found in Europe and North America, with pointed wings and a long tail, known for its distinctive call and for laying its eggs in the nests of other birds
a bird with a distinctive call; family Cuculidae

Examples

We heard a cuckoo calling from the woods early this morning.
The cuckoo is famous for laying its eggs in other birds’ nests.
During spring, the distinctive call of the cuckoo signals the change of seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo clock - a clock that announces the hours with a sound like a cuckoo's call

Etymology

From French *coucou*, based on the bird's call.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuˌku / (AmE), / ˈkʊkuː / (BrE)
(informal) A man who is regarded as a fool or as being stupid or incompetent.
foolish or incompetent man (informal)
informal

Examples

Everyone thought he was a cuckoo for trying that dangerous stunt.
Don't pay attention to Mike; he's a bit of a cuckoo sometimes.
Why are you acting like such a cuckoo today?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: simpleton - a person lacking intelligence or common sense
  • Antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual ability

Etymology

From the name of the bird, whose repetitive call and perceived foolishness inspired the figurative use.
