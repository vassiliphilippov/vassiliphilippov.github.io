marijuana

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈwɑːnə / (BrE)
a drug derived from the dried leaves and flowers of the hemp plant, typically smoked or ingested for its intoxicating effects
drug from hemp plant, used for intoxication

Examples

The police seized several bags of marijuana during the raid.
She supports legalizing marijuana for medical purposes.
He was arrested for possessing a small amount of marijuana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannabis - the plant from which marijuana is derived or drugs made from it

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'marihuana', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrɪˈwɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈwɑːnə / (BrE)
the dried leaves and flowers of the hemp plant, used as a drug, especially for its intoxicating and medicinal effects
plant used as a drug or medicine

Examples

The doctor prescribed marijuana to help ease his chronic pain.
Many states have legalized marijuana for medical use.
She was arrested for possessing marijuana in her backpack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannabis - the genus of plants including marijuana, also used to mean the drug made from them
  • Synonym: hemp - the plant Cannabis sativa, source of fiber and marijuana

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'marihuana', of uncertain origin.
