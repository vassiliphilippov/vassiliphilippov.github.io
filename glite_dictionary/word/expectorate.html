to expectorate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛktəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəreɪt / (BrE)
to cough up and discharge phlegm or sputum from the lungs and out of the mouth
cough up and spit out phlegm
medical

Examples

The doctor instructed the patient to expectorate into a sterile cup for testing.
During a chest infection, some people may frequently expectorate thick mucus.
She managed to expectorate the phlegm that had been bothering her all morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to force saliva or other substances from the mouth

Etymology

From Latin *expectoratus*, past participle of *expectorare* (to expel from the chest), from *ex-* (out) + *pectus* (chest).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛktəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəreɪt / (BrE)
to cough up and expel phlegm, mucus, or other material from the lungs or throat
to spit out phlegm from the respiratory tract
medical

Examples

The doctor advised him to expectorate any mucus instead of swallowing it.
Certain medications help patients expectorate phlegm from their lungs more easily.
He found it difficult to expectorate due to a severe sore throat.

Synset Examples

This drug expectorates quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to force saliva or another substance from the mouth
  • Antonym: swallow - to cause or allow something to pass down the throat

Etymology

From Latin expectoratus, from ex- (out) + pectus (chest).
