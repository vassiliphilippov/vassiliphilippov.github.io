Saarinen

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑːrɪnən / (AmE), / ˈsɑːrɪnən / (BrE)
Eero Saarinen (1910–1961), Finnish-American architect known for designing prominent structures such as the Gateway Arch in St. Louis and the TWA Flight Center in New York.
Finnish-American architect (1910–1961)

Examples

The Gateway Arch in St. Louis is one of Saarinen's most recognizable works.
Many architects study Saarinen's innovative use of curves and open space.
The TWA Flight Center at JFK Airport was designed by Saarinen in the 1960s.

Etymology

Finnish surname; most often refers to Eero Saarinen, noted architect.
