flukey

1. ADJ

/ ˈfluːki / (AmE), / ˈfluːki / (BrE)
Happening by chance in a way that is uncertain or unpredictable; resulting from luck rather than skill or planning.
resulting from chance or luck
informal

Examples

His promotion to manager was a flukey outcome that surprised everyone in the office.
They won the championship on a flukey last-minute goal.
The wind was so flukey that even experienced sailors had trouble predicting its direction.

Synset Examples

a chancy appeal at best
getting that job was definitely fluky
a fluky wind
an iffy proposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chancy - subject to chance or uncertainty
  • Synonym: lucky - having or bringing good luck
  • Antonym: planned - deliberately arranged or intended

Etymology

From 'fluke' (an accidental stroke of good luck), with the suffix '-y'.
