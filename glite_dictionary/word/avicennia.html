Avicennia

1. NOUN

/ ˌævɪˈsɛniə / (AmE), / ˌavɪˈsɛniə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical shrubs or trees, commonly known as black mangroves, found in coastal regions of the tropics and subtropics.
genus of coastal mangrove shrubs or trees

Examples

The roots of Avicennia help stabilize coastal shorelines and provide habitats for marine life.
Researchers studied how Avicennia species tolerate high salinity in estuarine environments.
The leaves of Avicennia often excrete excess salt, a unique adaptation among mangroves.

Etymology

Named after the Persian philosopher and physician Avicenna (Ibn Sina), reflecting Linnaean naming conventions.
