Stylomecon heterophyllum

1. NOUN

/ staɪloʊˈmiːkɒn ˌhɛtəˈrɒfɪləm / (AmE), / staɪləˈmiːkɒn ˌhɛtərəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A species of wild poppy native to California, noted for its bright red flowers.
a poppy species with bright red flowers, native to California

Examples

The Stylomecon heterophyllum blooms in late spring, adding a vibrant red to the hillsides.
Botanists often study Stylomecon heterophyllum for its unique petal structure.
In native plant gardens, Stylomecon heterophyllum is valued for its brilliant red flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California poppy - another species of poppy native to California (Eschscholzia californica)

Etymology

From Greek 'stylos' (pillar) and 'mecon' (poppy), combined with Neo-Latin specific epithet 'heterophyllum' meaning 'different leaves'.
