secularist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsekjələrɪst / (AmE), / ˈsekjʊlərɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates secularism, especially the separation of religion from government and education
advocate of secularism

Examples

As a committed secularist, she argued for keeping religious instruction out of public schools.
The debate featured a secularist and a religious leader discussing faith in politics.
Many secularists support the idea that government policies should not be influenced by religious beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secularism - the principle of separating religion from government and education

Etymology

From 'secular' + '-ist', where 'secular' comes from Latin 'saecularis' (worldly).
