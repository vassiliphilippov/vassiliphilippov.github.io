Serratia

1. NOUN

/ səˈreɪʃiə / (AmE), / səˈreɪtɪə / (BrE)
a genus of motile, Gram-negative, rod-shaped bacteria, some species of which can cause infections in humans.
genus of Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

Serratia species are often associated with hospital-acquired infections, especially in immunocompromised patients.
The laboratory identified Serratia marcescens as the causative agent of the outbreak.
Some Serratia bacteria are known for producing a characteristic red pigment.

Etymology

Named after Italian pharmacist Serafino Serrati (1745–1817), with '-ia' used in genus names in Neo-Latin.

2. NOUN

/ səˈreɪtiə / (AmE), / sɛˈreɪtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped, Gram-negative, motile bacteria, some species of which can cause infection in humans.
genus of motile rod-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Serratia can be found in water, soil, and some food products.
The hospital reported an outbreak of Serratia infections in the intensive care unit.
Researchers studied how Serratia resists common antibiotics.

Etymology

Named after Italian pharmacist Serafino Serrati (1743–1817).
