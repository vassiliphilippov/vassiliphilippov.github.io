guanabana

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑːˈnɑːbənə / (AmE), / ɡwɑːˈnɑːbənə / (BrE)
A large tropical fruit with spiny skin and tart white pulp, related to the custard apple, also called soursop.
tropical fruit with spiny skin and tart pulp

Examples

The guanabana is prized in many tropical countries for its sweet and tangy flavor.
She added chunks of guanabana to the smoothie for an exotic twist.
The guanabana tree can grow up to thirty feet tall in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soursop - another name for the same fruit
  • Synonym: cherimoya - a related fruit, also called custard apple

Etymology

Spanish, from Taíno (indigenous Caribbean language).
