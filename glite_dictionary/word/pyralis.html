Pyralis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪərəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Pyralidae, commonly known as snout moths.
genus of snout moths

Examples

The Pyralis genus includes several species known for their distinctive snout-like mouthparts.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Pyralis genus in the rainforest.
Many Pyralis moths are attracted to light and can be found near outdoor lamps at night.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek pur (fire), possibly referring to the moth's attraction to flame.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈrælɪs / (AmE), / pɪˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of moths that is the type genus of the family Pyralidae.
type genus of moth family Pyralidae

Examples

The Pyralis genus contains several notable species, including the meal moth.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Pyralis in controlled laboratory conditions.
Specimens belonging to Pyralis are common in stored food environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pyralidae - a family of moths including Pyralis and related genera

Etymology

From Latin Pyralis, a type of moth, from Greek puralis, said to mean 'a fly living in fire'.
