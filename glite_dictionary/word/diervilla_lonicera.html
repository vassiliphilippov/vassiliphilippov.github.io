Diervilla lonicera

1. NOUN

/ diˌɜrˈvɪlə ləˈnɪsərə / (AmE), / daɪˌɜːˈvɪlə ləˈnɪsərə / (BrE)
A spreading shrub native to northeastern North America, known for its small clusters of fragrant green and yellow flowers.
North American flowering shrub

Examples

Gardeners often choose Diervilla lonicera for its hardiness and attractive yellow flowers.
The Diervilla lonicera blooms from late spring through midsummer, brightening up shady areas.
Native bees are frequently attracted to Diervilla lonicera during its flowering season.

Etymology

From the genus name Diervilla, honoring French surgeon M. Dierville, and the species name lonicera, from Latin for honeysuckle.
