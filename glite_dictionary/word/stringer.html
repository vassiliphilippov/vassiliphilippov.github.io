stringer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who strings or threads items, such as beads or tennis rackets
person who threads or strings items

Examples

The workshop employed a skilled stringer to prepare the bead necklaces.
As a stringer of tennis rackets, she developed remarkable dexterity.
Each stringer follows a precise pattern to ensure the beads are evenly spaced.

Synset Examples

a stringer of beads

Etymology

From 'string' + '-er', denoting a person who strings.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋə / (BrE)
A long, narrow structural brace running lengthwise to strengthen the fuselage of an aircraft or the hull of a boat.
longitudinal structural brace in vehicles/boats

Examples

The aluminum stringer adds rigidity to the airplane's fuselage without much extra weight.
The damaged stringer was replaced during the boat's restoration to ensure structural integrity.
Engineers inspected every stringer inside the hull for signs of corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rib - crosswise structural member in aircraft or boats

Etymology

From 'string' + '-er'; technical sense from analogy with items that run in a straight line.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋə / (BrE)
a long horizontal piece of timber or metal used to connect uprights or support structures, especially in buildings or stairways.
horizontal timber or beam connecting supports

Examples

The carpenter installed a stringer along the base to support the new railing.
A broken stringer can weaken the entire structure of the staircase.
They used steel stringers to reinforce the bridge's walkway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used in construction

4. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋə / (BrE)
a member of a sports team, especially in reference to their ranking in the squad, such as a first or second string player
member of a ranked sports team squad

Examples

As a first stringer, she started every game of the season.
He was promoted from a second stringer to the starting lineup after his impressive performance.
The coach rotated several stringers to keep the team fresh during the tournament.

Synset Examples

a first stringer
a second stringer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starter - a player who is part of the team's regular or starting lineup
  • Antonym: substitute - a player who replaces another during a game

Etymology

From 'string' in sports, referring to a level or ranking of players on a team.
