gymnosophy

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑːsəfi / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒsəfi / (BrE)
(historical) The doctrine or practice of certain ancient Hindu ascetic philosophers who advocated nudity, asceticism, and meditation.
doctrine of ancient Hindu ascetics practicing nudity

Examples

The practice of gymnosophy involved strict ascetic disciplines, including the rejection of clothing.
Ancient accounts described followers of gymnosophy meditating unclothed beneath the Indian sun.
Some Greek philosophers were fascinated by the mystical elements of gymnosophy during their travels.

Etymology

From Greek gymnos (naked) + -sophy (wisdom).
