bypass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.pɑːs / (BrE)
a passage created surgically to divert the flow of blood or other bodily fluids around a blocked or damaged section
surgically created shunt
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a bypass to restore blood flow to his heart.
Doctors recommended a gastric bypass procedure to help her lose weight.
A coronary artery bypass can significantly reduce the risk of heart attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shunt - a tube or passage that diverts fluid from one path to another

Etymology

From the verb 'bypass', meaning to go around; in medical use since the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪpɑːs / (BrE)
a road built around a town or city to allow traffic to avoid its center
road around a town or city

Examples

The new bypass has reduced traffic congestion in the city center.
They built a bypass to divert trucks away from the residential area.
It takes about five minutes longer if you don't use the bypass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring road - a road that goes around the edge of a town or city
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪpɑːs / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with something, especially something unpleasant or difficult, by using a different method or route
avoid dealing with (something unpleasant or difficult)

Examples

Many travelers try to bypass long security lines by enrolling in fast-track programs.
She found a way to bypass the complicated application process.
New technology may allow us to bypass the need for manual checks entirely.

Synset Examples

You cannot bypass these rules!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with something unpleasant

4. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪpɑːs / (BrE)
An electrical conductor placed in parallel with another device in a circuit to divert a portion of the current away from it.
electrical conductor that diverts current

Examples

The engineer installed a bypass to prevent the resistor from overheating.
A bypass is often used to stabilize voltage across sensitive components.
Without a bypass, too much current would flow through the main circuit path.

Etymology

From 'by' + 'pass', originally indicating a secondary route; technical sense developed in electrical engineering.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
