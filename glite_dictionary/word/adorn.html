to adorn

1. VERB

/ əˈdɔrn / (AmE), / əˈdɔːn / (BrE)
to decorate or add beauty to something; to make more attractive
to decorate; make more attractive

Examples

The bride's hair was adorned with delicate white flowers.
Paintings and tapestries adorn the walls of the old castle.
Sparkling lights adorned the city streets during the holiday season.

Synset Examples

Flowers adorned the tables everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding things to it
  • Synonym: to beautify - to improve the appearance of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin adornare, from ad- ‘to’ + ornare ‘to equip, decorate’.

2. VERB

/ əˈdɔrn / (AmE), / əˈdɔːn / (BrE)
to make something or someone more attractive by decorating or adding ornaments
decorate; add beauty to

Examples

The bride chose to adorn her hair with fresh flowers for the ceremony.
Historic buildings are often adorned with intricate carvings and statues.
He loves to adorn his workspace with photographs and souvenirs.

Synset Examples

Decorate the room for the party
beautify yourself for the special day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by putting things on it
  • Synonym: to beautify - to improve the appearance of something or someone
  • Antonym: to disfigure - to spoil the appearance of something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'adornare' (to equip, decorate), from 'ad-' (to) + 'ornare' (to furnish, adorn).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
