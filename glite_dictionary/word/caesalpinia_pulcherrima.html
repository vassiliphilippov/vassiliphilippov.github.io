Caesalpinia pulcherrima

1. NOUN

/ siːˌzælˈpɪniə pʌlˈkɛrɪmə / (AmE), / siːˌsælˈpɪniə pʌlˈkɛrɪmə / (BrE)
a tropical shrub or small tree with bright yellow, orange, or red flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant; also known as the 'peacock flower' or 'pride of Barbados'
ornamental tropical shrub with bright flowers

Examples

The garden is famous for its vibrant Caesalpinia pulcherrima blooming throughout the summer.
We planted a Caesalpinia pulcherrima to add color to our backyard.
The bright orange flowers of Caesalpinia pulcherrima attract butterflies and hummingbirds.

Etymology

From Latin; named after Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino, and Latin 'pulcherrima' meaning 'most beautiful.'
