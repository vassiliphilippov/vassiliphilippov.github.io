unkind

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaɪnd / (BrE)
not friendly, generous, or considerate; lacking kindness.
not kind or considerate

Examples

It was unkind of you to laugh at her mistake.
His unkind words hurt her feelings deeply.
Sarah tried not to be unkind, even when she was upset.

Synset Examples

a thoughtless and unkind remark
the unkindest cut of all

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kind - friendly and considerate
  • Degree Higher: cruel - willing to cause pain or suffering
  • Synonym: thoughtless - without consideration for others

Etymology

From Old English un- (not) + kind (friendly, well-disposed).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaɪnd / (BrE)
not friendly, generous, or considerate; lacking in kindness or compassion.
not kind; lacking compassion

Examples

It was very unkind of her to leave without saying goodbye.
The teacher's unkind comments hurt the student's feelings.
Although his words were unkind, she chose to forgive him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kind - friendly, generous, or considerate
  • Degree Higher: cruel - willfully causing pain or suffering to others
  • Synonym: mean - unkind or spiteful

Etymology

From Old English 'uncynde' (unnatural), from 'un-' (not) + 'kind' (friendly, natural).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01267289-s
Synset POS: s
