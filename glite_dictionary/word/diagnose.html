to diagnose

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪəgˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəɡnəʊz / (BrE)
To identify the nature or cause of a problem or illness by examination and analysis.
identify nature of a problem or illness

Examples

The doctor was able to diagnose her condition after reviewing the test results.
It can be difficult to diagnose engine problems without proper equipment.
She spent years going from one specialist to another before finally being diagnosed with the rare disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnosis - the identification of the nature of an illness or problem

Etymology

From Greek 'diagnōsis' (discernment, distinguishing), from 'dia-' (apart) + 'gnōsis' (knowledge).

2. VERB

/ ˈdaɪəɡˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəɡnəʊz / (BrE)
to identify the nature of an illness or problem, especially by examining symptoms or analyzing information
identify an illness or problem by analysis
medical

Examples

Doctors are trained to diagnose diseases by evaluating symptoms and medical history.
It took several tests before the mechanic could diagnose the problem with my car engine.
Early screening can help health professionals diagnose cancer at a treatable stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnosis - the identification of an illness or problem after analysis

Etymology

From Greek diagnōsis (discernment, distinguishing), from dia- (apart) + gignōskein (to know).
