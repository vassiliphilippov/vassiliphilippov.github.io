audible

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəbl / (BrE)
able to be heard; loud enough to be perceived by the ear.
able to be heard

Examples

Her voice was barely audible over the noise of the crowd.
There was an audible sigh of relief when the exam ended.
The announcement was clearly audible throughout the entire building.

Synset Examples

he spoke in an audible whisper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inaudible - impossible to hear; not able to be heard

Etymology

From Latin 'audībilis' from 'audīre' (to hear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪbəl / (BrE)
In American football, a play that is changed verbally by the quarterback after both teams are set at the line of scrimmage.
American football play change spoken at the line
american

Examples

The quarterback called an audible when he saw the defense shift.
Manning is famous for his ability to recognize coverage and use an audible to exploit weaknesses.
Sometimes, the coach allows the quarterback to call an audible at the line of scrimmage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to make a decision or communicate a play in sports
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
