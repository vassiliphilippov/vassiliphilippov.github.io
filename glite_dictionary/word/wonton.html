wonton

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑn.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈwɒn.tɒn / (BrE)
A Chinese dumpling filled with spiced minced meat, typically served in soup.
Chinese dumpling, often in soup

Examples

She ordered a bowl of wonton soup for lunch.
The restaurant makes their own wonton wrappers by hand.
He filled the wonton with pork and vegetables before boiling them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumpling - a small piece of dough, often filled, cooked and eaten as food

Etymology

From Cantonese 雲吞 (wan4 tan1), literally 'swallowing clouds.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑnˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈwɒn.tɒn / (BrE)
A small Chinese dumpling filled with meat, seafood, or vegetables, often served in soup.
Chinese dumpling, often in soup

Examples

She ordered a bowl of wonton soup with her lunch.
The chef prepared homemade wonton filled with pork and shrimp.
You can find frozen wonton at most Asian grocery stores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumpling - small ball of dough, often with a filling
  • Synonym: wonton soup - soup containing wonton dumplings

Etymology

From Cantonese 雲吞 (wàhn tān), literally 'swallowing clouds'.
