Corvus brachyrhynchos

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔːr.vəs brəˈkɪr.ɪn.kəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.vəs brəˈkɪr.ɪn.kɒs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the American crow, a common species of crow found throughout North America
scientific name for the American crow

Examples

The Corvus brachyrhynchos is often seen in fields and urban areas across the United States.
Researchers have studied the intelligence of Corvus brachyrhynchos for decades.
Farmers sometimes view Corvus brachyrhynchos as pests due to their fondness for crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American crow - common large black bird native to North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'corvus' meaning 'crow' and 'brachyrhynchos' from Greek meaning 'short-billed'.
