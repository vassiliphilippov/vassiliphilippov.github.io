to vitalise

1. VERB

/ ˈvaɪtəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more lively, energetic, or vigorous
make more lively or energetic

Examples

The new manager's enthusiasm vitalised the entire team.
Fresh air and exercise can vitalise both body and mind.
The festival is intended to vitalise the community spirit in the town.

Synset Examples

The treatment at the spa vitalized the old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to
  • Synonym: animate - to bring to life or make lively
  • Synonym: to enliven - to make more lively or cheerful

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) + '-ise' (forming verbs).

2. VERB

/ ˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪt(ə)lʌɪz / (BrE)
to give life, energy, or vigour to; to make lively or more effective
give life or energy to
british

Examples

The coach's speech vitalised the exhausted team.
Spring sunshine always seems to vitalise the garden after winter.
These vitamins can help to vitalise your overall health.

Synset Examples

The eggs are vitalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vitalize - to give life, energy, or strength to
  • Antonym: to deaden - to make something less lively or energetic

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix), influenced by French 'vitaliser'.
