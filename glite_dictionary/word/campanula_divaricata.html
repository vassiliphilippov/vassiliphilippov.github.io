Campanula divaricata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæm.pəˈnjuː.lə ˌdaɪˌvɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌkam.pəˈnjuː.lə dɪˌvaː.rɪˈkeɪ.tə / (BrE)
A bellflower of the southeastern United States, from Maryland to Georgia, with pale blue flowers.
a bellflower species native to southeastern US

Examples

Campanula divaricata is commonly found growing in rocky woodlands throughout the Appalachian region.
The gardener planted Campanula divaricata to add native blue flowers to her summer border.
Pollinators are frequently attracted to the pale blossoms of Campanula divaricata.

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' means 'little bell' (referring to the bell-shaped flower), and 'divaricata' meaning 'spreading' or 'divergent'.
