Melilotus alba

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈloʊtəs ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈləʊtəs ˈalbə / (BrE)
A biennial plant of the pea family, commonly known as white sweet clover, valued as a honey plant and for forage.
white sweet clover; biennial plant

Examples

Melilotus alba is often planted to improve soil and provide forage for livestock.
Beekeepers favor Melilotus alba because it produces a high yield of nectar for honey production.
The tall, fragrant spikes of Melilotus alba attract pollinators to wild meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - any plant of the genus Melilotus, often called sweet clover

Etymology

From Latin Melilotus (genus name, from Greek 'meli' honey + 'lotos' clover); alba meaning 'white' in Latin.
