Peronospora tabacina

1. NOUN

/ pɛrəˌnɒspərə təˈbeɪsɪnə / (AmE), / pɛrəˌnɒspərə təˈbæˌsɪnə / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes a serious disease in tobacco plants, producing bluish-grey mildew on the undersides of the leaves
fungus causing tobacco mildew
medical

Examples

The outbreak of Peronospora tabacina devastated tobacco crops in the region last year.
Farmers use fungicides to protect their fields from Peronospora tabacina infections.
Bluish-grey mildew on tobacco leaves is a sign that Peronospora tabacina is present.

Etymology

From genus name Peronospora (from Greek 'perónē', buckle, and 'spora', seed) + specific epithet relating to tobacco.
