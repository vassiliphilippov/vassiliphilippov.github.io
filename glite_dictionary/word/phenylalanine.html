phenylalanine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːnəlˈæləˌniːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɪlˈaləniːn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid found in proteins, necessary for growth and protein metabolism in humans, and usually converted to tyrosine in the body
an essential amino acid in proteins
medical

Examples

Foods like eggs and milk are rich in phenylalanine, which is crucial for growth and health.
Patients with phenylketonuria need to avoid foods high in phenylalanine.
Phenylalanine is converted into tyrosine, another important amino acid, within the body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tyrosine - an amino acid derived from phenylalanine

Etymology

From phenyl (chemical group) + alanine (another amino acid), denoting its structure.
