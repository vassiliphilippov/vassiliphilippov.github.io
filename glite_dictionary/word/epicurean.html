epicurean

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈkjʊriən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkjʊəriən / (BrE)
Devoted to the pursuit of pleasure, especially in relation to fine food, drink, and comfort.
devoted to pleasure or luxury

Examples

She lived an epicurean lifestyle, savoring every gourmet meal she could find.
His epicurean tastes made him a regular at the city's finest restaurants.
They designed their vacation to maximize epicurean pleasures with fine food and luxurious surroundings.

Synset Examples

a hedonic thrill
lives of unending hedonistic delight
epicurean pleasures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedonistic - relating to or characterized by pleasure-seeking

Etymology

From Latin Epicureus, from Greek Epikoureios, relating to Epicurus, an ancient Greek philosopher who advocated pleasure as the highest good.

Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈkjʊriən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkjʊəriən / (BrE)
a person who takes particular pleasure in fine food and drink
person devoted to fine food and pleasure

Examples

As an epicurean, she often traveled to new cities just to sample their finest restaurants.
The club attracted epicureans who gathered weekly to enjoy elaborate dinners together.
His epicurean tastes made selecting the perfect wine an important part of every meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gourmet - a person who has a discerning palate and enjoys fine food and drink
  • Synonym: hedonist - a person who pursues pleasure as a goal

Etymology

From Greek 'Epikoureios', pertaining to Epicurus, whose teachings advocated the pursuit of pleasure and refined enjoyment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10081464-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈkjʊriən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkjʊəriən / (BrE)
relating to or displaying luxury and indulgence in sensual pleasures, especially in food, comfort, or style
luxurious; indulging sensual pleasures

Examples

They hosted an epicurean banquet featuring rare wines and exotic dishes.
The hotel is known for its epicurean suites, complete with private spas and gourmet service.
Her epicurean taste in home décor was admired by all her friends.

Synset Examples

an epicurean banquet
enjoyed a luxurious suite with a crystal chandelier and thick oriental rugs
Lucullus spent the remainder of his days in voluptuous magnificence
a chinchilla robe of sybaritic lavishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - characterized by luxury and comfort
  • Antonym: ascetic - practicing severe self-discipline and abstention from indulgence

Etymology

From Greek 'Epikoureios', relating to the philosopher Epicurus, who taught the pursuit of pleasure.
