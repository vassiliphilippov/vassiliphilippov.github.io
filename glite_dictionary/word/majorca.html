Majorca

1. PROPN

/ məˈjɔrkə / (AmE), / məˈjɔːkə / (BrE)
the largest of the Balearic Islands, located in the Mediterranean Sea and part of Spain, known for its beaches and resorts
largest Balearic Island of Spain

Examples

She spent her summer vacation on Majorca, enjoying the beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife.
Flights to Majorca are popular with tourists from all over Europe.
The cuisine of Majorca reflects the island's rich Mediterranean heritage.

Etymology

From Catalan 'Mallorca', possibly from Latin 'insula maior' (larger island).
