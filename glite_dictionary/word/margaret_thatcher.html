Margaret Thatcher

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrɡərət ˈθætʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːɡərət ˈθætʃə / (BrE)
(1925–2013) British stateswoman who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1979 to 1990, and was the first woman to hold this office.
British Prime Minister (1979–1990)

Examples

Margaret Thatcher was the first woman to become Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.
Many policies introduced by Margaret Thatcher are still debated today.
People often refer to Margaret Thatcher as the 'Iron Lady' for her strong leadership style.
