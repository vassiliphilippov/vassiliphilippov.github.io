cantaloup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.təˌluːp / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tə.luːp / (BrE)
A variety of muskmelon with a tan rind and orange, sweet flesh, commonly eaten as a fruit.
type of muskmelon with orange flesh

Examples

She served chilled cantaloup slices for breakfast on a hot summer morning.
The aroma of ripe cantaloup filled the kitchen as he prepared a fruit salad.
At the market, the vendor offered tastes of his sweetest cantaloup to passing customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantaloupe - another spelling of cantaloup, a type of muskmelon

Etymology

From French cantaloup, named after Cantalupo, a town near Rome where the fruit was introduced to Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæntəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈkantələʊp / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized melon with a rough rind and sweet, yellowish or orange flesh.
a type of sweet melon

Examples

She sliced the cantaloup and served it with breakfast.
The garden produced an unusually sweet cantaloup this summer.
Ripe cantaloups have a fragrant aroma and slightly soft skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - large, juicy fruit with sweet flesh and a hard rind

Etymology

From French 'cantaloup,' named after Cantalupo, a town near Rome where this melon was once cultivated.

Synset POS: n
