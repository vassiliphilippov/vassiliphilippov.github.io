sharper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑrpər / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpə / (BrE)
a person, especially a professional card player, who lives by cheating in games of chance
a professional card cheat
archaic

Examples

In the 18th century, a notorious sharper might be found at any fashionable card table.
The gambler was exposed as a sharper after he was caught marking the aces.
She lost her fortune to a skilled sharper who used elaborate tricks to win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - someone who behaves dishonestly to gain advantage
  • Synonym: swindler - a person who cheats others out of money or assets
  • Antonym: honest player - a person who plays games of chance fairly and without cheating

Etymology

From 'sharp' (as in clever or cunning) with the agentive suffix '-er'; used since the 17th century for cheats.
