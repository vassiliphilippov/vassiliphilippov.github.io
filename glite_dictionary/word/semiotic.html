semiotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsiːmiˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiːmɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to signs and symbols, especially as an element of communication or the study of meaning (semiotics)
relating to signs and symbols; semiotics

Examples

The professor introduced semiotic theories to explain how meanings are constructed in films.
Her research focuses on the semiotic analysis of advertising imagery.
Students explored the semiotic dimensions of language and communication in the course.

Synset Examples

semiotic analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semiotics - the study of signs and symbols and their use or interpretation

Etymology

From Greek sēmeiōtikos (‘observant of signs’), from sēmeion (‘sign’).
