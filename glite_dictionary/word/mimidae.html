Mimidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of songbirds that includes mockingbirds, catbirds, and thrashers, native to the Americas.
family of songbirds (mockingbirds, catbirds, thrashers)

Examples

The Mimidae are well known for their remarkable ability to mimic other birds’ songs.
Many species in the Mimidae family are native to North and South America.
Researchers study the vocalizations of Mimidae to understand avian mimicry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockingbird - a bird known for its ability to mimic sounds

Etymology

From Latin 'mimus' (mimic, actor), referencing the birds' mimicry abilities, and modern taxonomic suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪməˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds that includes mockingbirds, catbirds, and thrashers, sometimes considered a subfamily of Troglodytidae.
family of birds: mockingbirds, catbirds, thrashers

Examples

The Mimidae are renowned for their remarkable ability to imitate the calls of other birds.
Mockingbirds, members of the Mimidae family, are famous for their diverse vocalizations.
Scientists have studied several species within the Mimidae to understand the evolution of bird song.

Etymology

From New Latin Mimidae, from Latin mimus (mime, mimic), referring to the birds' mimicking ability.
