Northern

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðɚn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən / (BrE)
the dialect of Middle English spoken in northern England and southern Scotland, which developed into Scottish Lallans.
Middle English dialect of northern England and Scotland

Examples

The Northern dialect shows distinct grammatical features compared to southern varieties of Middle English.
Linguists trace many characteristics of Scots back to Northern.
In medieval literature, the influence of Northern can often be detected in vocabulary and spelling.
