subcortical

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌbˈkɔr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈkɔː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Located beneath or relating to areas below the cerebral cortex in the brain.
beneath the cerebral cortex
medical

Examples

The MRI scan revealed abnormalities in the subcortical regions of her brain.
Research suggests that subcortical structures play an important role in regulating emotion.
Damage to subcortical areas can affect movement and coordination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cortical - relating to the cerebral cortex

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'cortex' (bark, rind, here meaning cerebral cortex).
