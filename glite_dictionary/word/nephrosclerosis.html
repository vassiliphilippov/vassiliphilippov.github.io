nephrosclerosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛfroʊsklɚˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrəʊsklɪəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A kidney disease characterized by hardening of the renal arterioles, typically associated with hypertension, which can reduce blood flow and lead to kidney and heart failure.
hardening of kidney arterioles (disease)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that nephrosclerosis was causing his decreased kidney function.
Patients with untreated hypertension are at increased risk for developing nephrosclerosis.
Advanced nephrosclerosis may eventually lead to chronic kidney failure.

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + sclerosis (hardening).
