facultative

1. ADJ

/ fəˈkʌltəˌtɪv / (AmE), / fəˈkʌltətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or concerning the mental faculties or powers of the mind.
relating to mental faculties

Examples

The neurologist studied the facultative abilities affected by the injury.
Some philosophers debate whether imagination is a facultative process distinct from reasoning.
The professor delivered a lecture on the facultative nature of intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognitive - related to the mental processes of perception, memory, judgment, and reasoning

Etymology

From Medieval Latin facultātīvus, from Latin facultās (faculty, power, ability).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfælkjəˌteɪtɪv / (AmE), / fækˈʌltətɪv / (BrE)
(of a law, rule, or action) giving permission or the power to do or not do something; optional rather than compulsory.
optional; granting permission or choice

Examples

The university offers several facultative courses that students can choose to take beyond their core requirements.
The new legislation includes a facultative clause, allowing cities to adopt the policy at their discretion.
Participation in the workshop is facultative, not a mandatory part of the program.

Synset Examples

a facultative enactment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optional - not compulsory; left to choice
  • Antonym: compulsory - required by law or a rule; obligatory

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'facultativus', from 'facultas' (power, ability).

4. ADJ

/ ˈfæk.əlˌteɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / fəˈkʌl.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of an organism or process) able to function, survive, or exist under more than one set of conditions, not restricted to a single mode of life or activity.
able to exist in more than one condition

Examples

Unlike obligate anaerobes, facultative bacteria can survive in both the presence and absence of oxygen.
Some fungi are facultative parasites, capable of living off both living hosts and dead organic matter.
Scientists studied the facultative adaptations that allow certain plants to thrive in dry and wet environments.

Synset Examples

a facultative parasite can exist as a parasite or a saprophyte

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obligate - restricted to a single condition or mode of life

Etymology

From Latin facultātīvus ('optional, contingent'), from facultās ('power, ability').
