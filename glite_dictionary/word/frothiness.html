frothiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔːθi nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɒθi nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being frothy; the condition of producing or containing bubbles or foam.
the quality of being frothy or bubbly

Examples

The frothiness of the cappuccino made it particularly enjoyable to drink.
Champagne is prized for its frothiness and lively bubbles.
After shaking the drink vigorously, the bartender admired the frothiness on top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothy - full of small bubbles; foamy

Etymology

Formed from 'frothy' + '-ness', where 'frothy' derives from 'froth' (Middle English from Old Norse 'frotha').
