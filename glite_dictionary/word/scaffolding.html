scaffolding

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæfəldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskafəldɪŋ / (BrE)
temporary structures made of boards and metal poles, used by workers while building, repairing, or cleaning buildings; a system of scaffolds.
temporary structure for workers

Examples

The workers assembled the scaffolding around the building before beginning the repairs.
Old wooden scaffolding was replaced with new metal structures for safety reasons.
Painters spent hours on the scaffolding restoring the church's exterior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaffold - a temporary platform for workers during construction

Etymology

From 'scaffold' + '-ing'. 'Scaffold' from Old French 'eschaffaut', from Italian 'schaffaldo', of Germanic origin.
