Cystophora cristata

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌstɑfərə krɪˈsteɪtə / (AmE), / sɪˌstɒfərə krɪˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
A medium-sized blackish-grey seal with a large inflatable sac on its head, native to Arctic and northern Atlantic waters; also known as the hooded seal.
hooded seal of Arctic and Atlantic waters

Examples

The Cystophora cristata is known for the male's distinctive inflatable nasal sac.
Marine biologists have tracked the migration patterns of Cystophora cristata between Greenland and Canada.
The Cystophora cristata faces threats from climate change and sealing in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hooded seal - common name for Cystophora cristata, the seal with an inflatable sac on its head

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the inflatable sac; 'cristata' from Latin for 'crested'.
