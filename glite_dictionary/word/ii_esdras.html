II Esdras

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈɛzdrəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈɛzdrəs / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book containing angelic revelations, sometimes included in certain versions of the Bible
an Apocryphal book of revelations

Examples

Many scholars study II Esdras for its unique insights into Jewish apocalyptic literature.
In some Christian traditions, II Esdras is considered part of the biblical Apocrypha.
The visions described in II Esdras have influenced both theology and literature throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apocrypha - biblical or related writings not forming part of the accepted canon of Scripture

Etymology

From Latin 'Esdras', from Greek 'Esdras', a rendering of the Hebrew name 'Ezra'.
