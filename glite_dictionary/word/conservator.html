conservator

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.veɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tə / (BrE)
a person appointed by a court to manage the affairs of a minor or of someone who is legally incapable of managing their own affairs
court-appointed guardian or manager
legal

Examples

The court appointed a conservator to oversee the financial affairs of the elderly woman.
As her conservator, he was responsible for making all legal and medical decisions on her behalf.
After the accident, a conservator was named to protect his interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guardian - a person who is legally appointed to care for another person, especially a child
  • Antonym: ward - a person, especially a minor, who is under the protection or care of a guardian or conservator

Etymology

From Latin conservator (one who preserves), from conservare (to preserve).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.veɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.teə / (BrE)
a person responsible for the care and preservation of a collection, such as in a museum or library
person in charge of preserving a collection

Examples

The conservator carefully restored several rare manuscripts in the library's collection.
Each painting was examined by a conservator before being displayed in the new exhibition.
As a conservator, her expertise in historic textiles was essential to the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curator - a person in charge of a museum, library, or other collection

Etymology

From Latin conservator (preserver), from conservare (to preserve).
