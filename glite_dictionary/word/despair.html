despair

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɛr / (AmE), / dɪˈspeə / (BrE)
to lose all hope; to stop believing that something good will happen
to lose all hope

Examples

Even when things seem bleak, try not to despair.
She began to despair of ever finding her lost cat.
He despaired after hearing the disappointing news.

Synset Examples

Don't despair — help is on the way!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hope - to want something to happen or be true and to believe it is possible or likely

Etymology

From Old French 'desperer', from Latin 'desperare' (to lose hope), from 'de-' (without) + 'sperare' (to hope).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɛr / (AmE), / dɪˈspeə / (BrE)
the complete loss or absence of hope
complete loss of hope

Examples

The news left him in a state of despair for several days.
Despite her despair, she managed to keep moving forward.
He tried to hide his despair from his friends and family.

Synset Examples

they moaned in despair and dismay
one harsh word would send her into the depths of despair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hope - a feeling of expectation and desire for a certain thing to happen
  • Synonym: hopelessness - the state of having no hope

Etymology

From Old French 'desperer', from Latin 'desperare' (to lose hope), from 'de-' (without) + 'sperare' (to hope).
