roadhog

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdˌhɑg / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌhɒg / (BrE)
a driver who selfishly obstructs other vehicles on the road, often by taking up more than one lane or refusing to let others pass
selfish or obstructive driver

Examples

The roadhog refused to let anyone overtake him, causing a long line of cars behind.
I couldn't believe what a roadhog that truck driver was during rush hour.
It's dangerous to be a roadhog on busy city streets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courteous driver - a driver who is considerate to others on the road

Etymology

Formed from 'road' + 'hog', likening such a driver to a hog that greedily takes more than its share.
