turn-on

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝn ɑn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn ɒn / (BrE)
something that causes excitement or stimulates interest or desire, especially in a sexual context or more generally.
source of excitement or stimulation
informal

Examples

Intelligent conversation is a real turn-on for him.
For many people, risk-taking is more of a turn-on than a deterrent.
His confidence, not just his looks, was the biggest turn-on.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: turn-off - something that causes a loss of interest or excitement
  • Synonym: attraction - something that draws interest, attention, or admiration

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'turn on', meaning to activate or stimulate, especially popularized in the 20th century.
