motif

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈtif / (AmE), / məʊˈtiːf / (BrE)
a distinctive or recurring theme or idea in a literary, artistic, or musical work
recurrent idea in art or literature

Examples

One recurring motif in her paintings is the image of a lone tree.
The story’s central motif is the struggle between good and evil.
Classical music often uses a short melodic motif to unify a composition.

Synset Examples

it was the usual ‘boy gets girl’ theme

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme - the central subject or topic in a literary or artistic work

Etymology

From French 'motif', from medieval Latin 'motivus' (serving to move).

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈtif / (AmE), / məʊˈtiːf / (BrE)
a short musical idea or theme that is repeated or developed throughout a piece of music
recurring theme in music

Examples

The composer introduced a haunting motif that recurred throughout the symphony.
Each movement of the suite features a different motif that ties the work together.
The film's soundtrack is unified by a simple piano motif that appears in several scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme - the main subject or melodic idea in music
  • Synonym: leitmotif - a recurrent musical phrase associated with a particular person, idea, or situation

Etymology

From French motif (motive, theme), from Latin motivus (moving, motive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07043169-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ moʊˈtif / (AmE), / məʊˈtiːf / (BrE)
a decorative design or pattern, especially one that is repeated in architecture, textiles, or art
repeated decorative design or pattern

Examples

The wallpaper featured a repetitive floral motif in shades of blue and gold.
Greek temples often display a key motif along their friezes.
The dress was decorated with a geometric motif that caught everyone's eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pattern - a repeated decorative design

Etymology

From French motif (motive, theme), from Latin motivus (serving to move).
