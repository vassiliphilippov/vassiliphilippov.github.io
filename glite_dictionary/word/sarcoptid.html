sarcoptid

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkɑp.tɪd / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒp.tɪd / (BrE)
A mite belonging to the family Sarcoptidae, known for infesting the skin of humans and animals.
mite of Sarcoptidae family
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified the sarcoptid as the cause of the dog's intense itching.
Infestations by sarcoptids can result in conditions such as scabies in humans.
Researchers are studying different treatments for sarcoptid infestations in livestock.

Synset Examples

itch mites cause scabies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scabies mite - mite that causes the skin disease scabies

Etymology

From New Latin Sarcoptidae, derived from Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'koptein' (to cut), referring to their skin-burrowing behavior.
