Assyrian

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
A person who was an inhabitant of ancient Assyria, a historical region in Mesopotamia.
inhabitant of ancient Assyria

Examples

The ancient Assyrian empire was known for its impressive architecture and military prowess.
The museum displayed artifacts uncovered from a lost Assyrian city.
He studied the language and customs of the Assyrian people for his thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonian - an inhabitant of ancient Babylonia
  • Synonym: Egyptian - an inhabitant of ancient Egypt

Etymology

From Latin Assyrius, from Greek Ass Syrianos, from Assyria.

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language once spoken by the Assyrians of ancient Mesopotamia
extinct language of ancient Mesopotamia

Examples

Scholars have deciphered ancient inscriptions written in Assyrian to learn more about early Mesopotamian culture.
The Assyrian language employed cuneiform script for administrative records and royal decrees.
Few people today can read or translate Assyrian, since the language has been extinct for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Akkadian - the extinct Semitic language from which Assyrian descended
  • Synonym: Babylonian - an ancient variant of the Akkadian language; contemporary with Assyrian

Etymology

From Latin Assyrius, from Greek Assyrios, from Akkadian Aššur (the Assyrian city and chief deity).

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
the modern Neo-Aramaic language spoken by Assyrian people, primarily in northern Iraq and surrounding regions
Neo-Aramaic language of Assyrians

Examples

She is fluent in both Arabic and Assyrian, which she learned from her grandparents.
The Assyrian language has many dialects still spoken in northern Iraq and neighboring countries.
Efforts are being made to preserve Assyrian by teaching it in community schools abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neo-Aramaic - modern Aramaic languages spoken by various communities including Assyrians

Etymology

From Latin Assyrius (of Assyria), from Ancient Greek Assurios, from Assyria.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
