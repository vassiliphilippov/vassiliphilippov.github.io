improvidently

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈprɑːvədəntli / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒvɪdəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of foresight or planning for the future.
without foresight; carelessly for the future

Examples

She spent her inheritance improvidently and soon found herself in debt.
If you handle resources improvidently, you may regret it when times are tough.
They managed the company's finances improvidently, leading to its collapse.

Synset Examples

he lived improvidently for the moment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prudently - in a prudent manner; with careful planning for the future
  • Synonym: carelessly - without care or attention; heedlessly

Etymology

From 'improvident' (Latin improvidens: unprepared for the future) + '-ly'.
