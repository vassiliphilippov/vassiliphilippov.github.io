skillessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈskɪləsnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of lacking skill; ineptness or incompetence.
lack of skill; ineptness

Examples

Despite his enthusiasm, his skillessness was evident to everyone watching the demonstration.
The team's repeated failures were not due to laziness, but to sheer skillessness.
She admitted her skillessness at chess, preferring to observe rather than play.

Synset Examples

Here he lays his skillessness, or his vaulting laziness, one or the other, right in front of you and dares you to not see what is there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability
  • Antonym: skill - the ability to do something well

Etymology

From 'skilless' (without skill) plus the noun suffix '-ness'.
