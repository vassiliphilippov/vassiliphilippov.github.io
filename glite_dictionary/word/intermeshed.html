intermeshed

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈmɛʃt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛʃt / (BrE)
entangled or caught as if in a mesh; closely intertwined.
entangled; caught in a mesh

Examples

The two companies became so intermeshed that their finances were impossible to separate.
Caught and intermeshed in bureaucracy, the project never moved forward.
Her life became intermeshed with his, making independence difficult.

Synset Examples

enmeshed in financial difficulties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enmeshed - entangled or caught in a net or mesh

Etymology

from 'inter-' (between) and 'mesh' (a network of threads or wires)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256319-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈmɛʃt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛʃt / (BrE)
(of toothed parts or gears) interlocked and working together by fitting into each other.
fitted parts working together

Examples

Each engine drives a pair of intermeshed gears that turn the main rotor.
The designer ensured that the intermeshed rotors would never collide during operation.
You can see the intermeshed cogs spinning inside the transparent clock case.

Synset Examples

the gears are engaged
meshed gears
intermeshed twin rotors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meshed - (of gears) engaged or fitted together
  • Antonym: disengaged - not interlocked or not working together (of gears)

Etymology

From 'inter-' (between) and 'mesh' (to fit together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01104510-s
Synset POS: s
