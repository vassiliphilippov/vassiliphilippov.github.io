Nerita peloronta

1. NOUN

/ nəˈraɪtə pɛˈlɔrɒntə / (AmE), / nɛˈrɪtə pɛˈlɔːrəntə / (BrE)
A species of marine snail native to the Caribbean, known for its shell with reddish tooth-like projections around the opening.
Caribbean marine snail with toothed shell opening

Examples

The Nerita peloronta can be found along rocky Caribbean shores, where its distinctive shell makes it easy to spot.
Scientists collected several Nerita peloronta specimens to study their unique toothlike shell projections.
Nerita peloronta is prized by shell collectors for its beautiful coloration and unusual shell structure.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Nerita' and species name 'peloronta'.
