servitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the state of being subject to someone more powerful, especially as a slave or through forced labor, often as a punishment
state of forced labor or subjection

Examples

After the trial, he was sentenced to ten years of servitude for his crimes.
Many people lived in servitude under oppressive regimes throughout history.
The abolition of servitude was a significant step toward human rights.

Synset Examples

penal servitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slavery - state of being a slave
  • Antonym: freedom - state of being free, not under control of another

Etymology

From Latin servitūdō (slavery), from servus (slave).
