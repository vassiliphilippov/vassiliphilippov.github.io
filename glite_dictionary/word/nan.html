nan

1. NOUN

/ nɑn / (AmE), / nɑːn / (BrE)
A type of leavened, oven-baked flatbread from India, typically shaped like a teardrop.
Indian leavened flatbread

Examples

We ordered butter chicken and a basket of nan to share at the restaurant.
The chef brushed garlic on the freshly baked nan before serving it.
She learned to make nan in a traditional clay tandoor oven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naan - Leavened flatbread from India (variant spelling)

Etymology

From Hindi नान (nān) and Persian نان (nān) meaning 'bread'.

2. NOUN

/ næn / (AmE), / næn / (BrE)
(especially in British English) a child's word for their grandmother.
informal term for grandmother
informal british

Examples

I used to spend every Sunday at my nan's house when I was a child.
My nan makes the most delicious apple pie in the world.
When I was sick, my nan always knew how to comfort me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandmother - the mother of one's father or mother

3. NOUN

/ næn / (AmE), / næn / (BrE)
(British, informal) your grandmother; the mother of your father or mother.
(British, informal) grandmother
informal british

Examples

My nan used to tell wonderful stories about her childhood.
We're visiting my nan this weekend for her birthday.
When I was a child, my nan would bake the best apple pies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandmother - the mother of your father or mother
