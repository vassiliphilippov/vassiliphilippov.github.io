pemican

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛmɪkən / (AmE), / ˈpɛmɪkən / (BrE)
Lean dried meat pounded finely and mixed with melted fat, traditionally used as a high-energy food by North American Indigenous peoples.
traditional North American Indian preserved meat food

Examples

During their expedition, the travelers survived on pemican for several weeks.
Many Indigenous cultures made pemican to store food for the winter months.
The museum displayed a sample of traditional pemican alongside other Native American artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerky - meat that has been cured and dried in thin strips

Etymology

From Cree *pimîkan* (pemi-: grease, -kan: thing).
