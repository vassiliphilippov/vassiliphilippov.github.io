brainwave

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪnˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnˌweɪv / (BrE)
A sudden clever idea or realization, especially one that resolves a complex situation.
sudden clever idea; realization

Examples

She had a brainwave and suddenly knew how to solve the problem.
After hours of fruitless searching, Tom finally had a brainwave about where the keys might be.
The team was stuck until Maria had a brainwave that changed their approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insight - the capacity to gain an accurate and deep understanding of something
  • Synonym: epiphany - a moment of sudden and great revelation or realization

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪnwəv / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɛɪv / (BrE)
A rapid fluctuation of electrical activity between parts of the brain's cortex, detectable by an electroencephalograph.
electrical activity in the brain
medical

Examples

The EEG showed abnormal brainwave patterns during the patient's seizure.
Different types of brainwaves, such as alpha and beta waves, are observed during sleep and wakefulness.
Meditation can alter the frequency of your brainwaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain rhythm - a rhythmic fluctuation of electrical activity in the brain

Synset POS: n
