tribalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪ.bə.lɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtraɪ.bə.lɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of organizing or uniting people into tribes or tribal groups, or adopting tribal characteristics
the formation or organization of tribes

Examples

The tribalization of the region has led to complex political alliances.
Social media has been accused of encouraging tribalization in public discourse.
Some historians study the tribalization of societies during periods of migration.

Etymology

From tribe + -al + -ization, modeled on similar noun formations (e.g., civilization).
