Pseudopleuronectes americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˌplʊroʊˈnɛktiːz əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˌplʊərəˈnɛktiːz əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of flatfish found along the Atlantic coast of North America, valued as a food fish and commonly known as the winter flounder
an Atlantic food fish species (winter flounder)

Examples

The Pseudopleuronectes americanus is prized by commercial fisheries along the northeastern United States.
Scientists monitor populations of Pseudopleuronectes americanus to assess the health of coastal ecosystems.
Many seafood restaurants serve dishes made from Pseudopleuronectes americanus during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter flounder - common name for Pseudopleuronectes americanus

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudes' (false) + 'pleuronectes' (a genus of flatfishes), and Latin 'americanus' (American).
