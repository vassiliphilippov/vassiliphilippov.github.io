chaise

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪz / (AmE), / ʃeɪz / (BrE)
a long chair designed for reclining, typically with an extended seat to support the legs
long reclining chair

Examples

She stretched out on the chaise in the sunroom, enjoying her book.
The hotel pool was lined with comfortable chaise chairs for guests to relax on.
He fell asleep on the chaise by the window after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaise longue - a long reclining chair, often upholstered

Etymology

From French 'chaise longue' (long chair).

2. NOUN

/ ʃeɪz / (AmE), / ʃeɪz / (BrE)
A light open carriage with two wheels and a folding hood, drawn by a single horse.
light two-wheeled horse-drawn carriage

Examples

The wealthy merchant arrived in a stylish chaise drawn by a single gray mare.
She admired the elegant lines of the restored chaise on display at the museum.
Traveling by chaise was both fashionable and practical in the early 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a vehicle with wheels drawn by horses, used in the past

Etymology

From French 'chaise' meaning 'chair', originally applied to this style of carriage.
