Desmanthus illinoensis

1. NOUN

/ dɛsˈmænθəs ɪˌlɪnoʊˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / dɛsˈmanθəs ɪˌlɪnəʊˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to North American prairies, with dense heads of small white flowers; also called prairie mimosa
a prairie herbaceous plant with white flowers

Examples

The roots of Desmanthus illinoensis are sometimes studied for their chemical properties.
Desmanthus illinoensis thrives in well-drained soils across Midwestern prairies.
Conservationists are working to protect Desmanthus illinoensis as part of native prairie restoration projects.

Etymology

From genus Desmanthus (Greek 'desmos' meaning 'bond' and 'anthos' meaning 'flower') and 'illinoensis,' indicating association with Illinois.
