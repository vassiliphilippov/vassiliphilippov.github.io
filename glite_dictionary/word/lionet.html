lionet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.ə.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.nɪt / (BrE)
a small or young lion
a small or young lion

Examples

The novelist described the young cub as a curious lionet exploring the grasslands.
A lionet depends on its mother for protection during the first months of life.
The children watched a playful lionet tumble near the pride.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lion - a large wild cat native to Africa and India

Etymology

From lion + -et, a diminutive suffix from Old French -et.
