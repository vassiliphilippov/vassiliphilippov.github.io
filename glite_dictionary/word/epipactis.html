Epipactis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈpæktɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of hardy orchids with leafy-bracted racemes of greenish or purplish irregular flowers, commonly known as helleborines.
genus of hardy orchids

Examples

Many rare species of Epipactis grow in the damp woodlands of Europe.
The broad-leaved helleborine, a common Epipactis, can be found in shaded areas.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Epipactis near the riverbank.

Etymology

From Greek epipaktis (a kind of plant, possibly a type of orchid).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs / (BrE)
a genus of hardy orchids with leafy-bracted racemes of greenish or purplish irregular flowers
a genus of hardy orchids

Examples

Several species within the genus Epipactis are found throughout Europe and Asia.
The marsh helleborine is a beautiful member of the Epipactis genus.
Botanists discovered a new variety belonging to the Epipactis genus in the wetlands.

Etymology

From Latin *epipactis*, originally from Greek, meaning a plant used to curdle milk.
