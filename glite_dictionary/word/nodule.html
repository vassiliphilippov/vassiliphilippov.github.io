nodule

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈnɒd.juːl / (BrE)
A small, rounded lump or swelling, often found on roots or stems of plants, especially caused by bacteria or other growths.
small rounded protuberance on a plant

Examples

The scientist observed several nodules forming on the roots of the legume plant.
Pea plants often develop nodules where beneficial bacteria help them fix nitrogen.
Small nodules on the stem indicate a healthy plant response to infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass of a substance

Etymology

From Latin nodulus, diminutive of nodus (knot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈnɒd.juːl / (BrE)
a small, rounded lump or swelling, especially on or in the body, plants, or rocks
small lump or swelling

Examples

The doctor discovered a nodule on her thyroid gland during a routine examination.
Certain bean plants develop a nodule on their roots to help fix nitrogen from the air.
Geologists found a manganese nodule on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: node - a point of intersection or small swelling; general term for a knot or lump

Etymology

From Latin 'nodulus', diminutive of 'nodus' (knot).

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɑdʒul / (AmE), / ˈnɒdjuːl / (BrE)
a small rounded lump of mineral substance, often harder than the surrounding rock or sediment, especially found in geological formations
small rounded mineral lump in rock

Examples

The geologist carefully examined the nodule embedded in the sandstone layer.
Some manganese nodules on the ocean floor contain valuable metals like nickel and copper.
Over time, a nodule can form as minerals precipitate around a small core in sediment.

Etymology

From Latin nodulus, diminutive of nodus (knot, lump).
