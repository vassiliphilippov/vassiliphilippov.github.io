Diplazium pycnocarpon

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpleɪziəm ˌpɪknoʊˈkɑrpən / (AmE), / dɪˈpleɪziəm ˌpɪknəʊˈkɑːpən / (BrE)
A North American fern species with narrow fronds and yellowish leafstalks, commonly found in moist woodland areas.
North American fern species

Examples

The shady ravines in the park are home to Diplazium pycnocarpon and other rare ferns.
Botanists documented a large population of Diplazium pycnocarpon along the creek.
The narrow fronds of Diplazium pycnocarpon make it easy to distinguish from other local fern species.

Etymology

From Greek 'diplazion' (double) and 'pycnocarpon' (dense-fruited); used in scientific Latin nomenclature.
