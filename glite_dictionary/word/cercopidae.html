Cercopidae

1. NOUN

/ sərˈkɑpɪˌdi / (AmE), / səˈkɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects in the order Hemiptera, commonly known as froghoppers or spittlebugs, characterized by their jumping ability and the foamy spittle-like masses produced by their larvae
family of insects called froghoppers or spittlebugs

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species of Cercopidae in the Amazon rain forest.
The distinctive foam produced by Cercopidae larvae protects them from predators.
Farmers monitor Cercopidae populations due to their potential impact on crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: froghopper - small jumping insect from the family Cercopidae
  • Synonym: spittlebug - insect known for larvae that produce foamy masses

Etymology

From New Latin Cercopidae, based on the type genus Cercopis and the suffix -idae used for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ sɚˈkɑːpɪdiː / (AmE), / sɜːˈkɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as froghoppers or spittlebugs, characterized by their ability to jump and by the frothy spittle-like substance their nymphs produce.
family of froghoppers or spittlebugs

Examples

The Cercopidae are commonly known for the frothy masses their nymphs produce on plants.
Many species in the Cercopidae family can be found in meadows and gardens worldwide.
Scientists study Cercopidae to understand how their jumping mechanisms work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: froghopper - Small jumping insect from the Cercopidae family
  • Synonym: spittlebug - Insect nymph that produces frothy spittle masses

Etymology

From New Latin Cercopidae, from genus Cercopis, from Greek kerkōps (with long tail, a kind of insect).
