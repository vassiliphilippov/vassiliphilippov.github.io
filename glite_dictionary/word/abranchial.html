abranchial

1. ADJ

/ əˈbræn.ki.əl / (AmE), / əˈbræŋ.kɪ.əl / (BrE)
(of an organism) having no gills.
having no gills (biology)

Examples

Certain amphibians are abranchial throughout their adult stage, breathing entirely through their skin and lungs.
The fossil showed no evidence of gill structures, suggesting it was abranchial.
Unlike most fish, lamprey larvae are temporarily abranchial during part of their development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: branchiate - having gills

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + branchia (gills).
