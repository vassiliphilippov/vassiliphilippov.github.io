canalicular

1. ADJ

/ kəˌnælɪˈkjulər / (AmE), / kəˌnalɪˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
relating to, resembling, or having a canaliculus (a small channel or duct, especially in anatomical contexts)
relating to a small channel or duct
medical

Examples

Liver diseases can sometimes be identified by changes in canalicular bile flow.
The canalicular structure of bones facilitates the transport of nutrients.
Pathologists examined the tissue for signs of canalicular injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canaliculus - a small channel or duct in the body

Etymology

From French 'canaliculaire', from Latin 'canaliculus' (small channel).
