to traumatize

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɔːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrɔːmətaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to experience severe emotional or psychological distress as a result of a shocking or distressing event
cause severe emotional or psychological distress

Examples

The accident traumatized her for years to come.
Loud explosions during the war deeply traumatized many children.
They fear that reliving the event could traumatize the victims again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - suddenly disturb the mind or emotions
  • Antonym: to heal - make or become healthy again after injury or trauma

Etymology

From Greek trauma (wound) + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
