to accomplish

1. VERB

/ əˈkɑmplɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈkɒmplɪʃ / (BrE)
to achieve or complete something successfully, often through effort
to achieve or complete successfully

Examples

After months of hard work, she finally accomplished her dream of opening a bakery.
It took teamwork to accomplish such a challenging project on time.
You can accomplish a lot if you stay focused and determined.

Synset Examples

she achieved her goal despite setbacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to achieve - to successfully bring about or reach a desired objective or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French 'acomplir', from Latin 'ad' (to) + 'complere' (to fill up, complete).

2. VERB

/ əˈkɑmplɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈkʌmplɪʃ / (BrE)
to succeed in doing or completing something, especially after a lot of effort; to carry out or put into effect
succeed in doing or carrying out

Examples

She was determined to accomplish her goals despite the difficulties.
The new director hopes to accomplish major changes within the department.
After months of planning, they finally accomplished the project successfully.

Synset Examples

carry out a task
execute the decision of the people
He actioned the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to achieve - to successfully bring about or reach by effort
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in accomplishing something

Etymology

From Old French accomplir, from Latin ad- (to) + complēre (to fill up, complete).
