engraved

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡreɪvd / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪvd / (BrE)
(of a design, lettering, or pattern) cut or impressed into a surface.
cut or impressed into a surface

Examples

The couple sent out beautifully engraved wedding invitations to all their guests.
The museum displayed an engraved silver plate from the 18th century.
His name was engraved on the inside of the ring.

Synset Examples

an incised design
engraved invitations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incised - cut into a surface
  • Antonym: embossed - raised in relief above the surface

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'engrave,' from Middle English engraven, from en- (make) + grave (to carve).
