anancasm

1. NOUN

/ əˈnænˌkæzəm / (AmE), / əˈnæŋ.kæz.əm / (BrE)
An obsolete term for a ritualistic act or sequence of acts, especially performed by a person with obsessive-compulsive neurosis.
obsolete term for obsessive ritual acts
archaic medical

Examples

The psychiatrist explained that the patient's repetitive hand-washing was an example of anancasm.
In historical texts, anancasm is often mentioned in relation to early studies of obsessive-compulsive disorder.
Researchers discussed whether anancasm should be revived as a useful diagnostic term in psychiatry.

Synset Examples

The conceptual history of ‘anancasm’ in psychiatry remains almost unexplored and this article will help to remove this deficit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsession - persistent unwanted thought or urge in obsessive-compulsive disorder
  • Synonym: compulsion - an irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, especially against one's conscious wishes

Etymology

From Greek anankasmos (compulsion), related to anankē (necessity, compulsion).
