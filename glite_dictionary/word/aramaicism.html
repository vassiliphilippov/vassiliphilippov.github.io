Aramaicism

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪmiəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈreɪmiəˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a word or linguistic feature characteristic of Aramaic that appears in another language
Aramaic feature in another language

Examples

The presence of an Aramaicism in the text suggests influence from ancient Aramaic culture.
Scholars debate whether this phrase is a true Aramaicism or a result of later translation.
The use of certain idioms in the manuscript points to multiple Aramaicisms scattered throughout the work.

Synset Examples

Even Joachim Jeremias, one of the prime champions of this criterion, recognized that the mere presence of an Aramaism could not prove the authenticity of an independent unit of translation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aramaism - a word, phrase, or idiom characteristic of Aramaic

Etymology

From Aramaic, referring to the Semitic language, plus -ism (denoting a characteristic or feature).
