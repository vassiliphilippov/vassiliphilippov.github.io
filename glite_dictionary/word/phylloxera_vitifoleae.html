Phylloxera vitifoleae

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌlɒk.sɪ.rə ˌvɪ.tɪˈfoʊ.li.i / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒk.sɪərə ˌvɪ.tɪˈfɒl.i.i / (BrE)
a species of tiny aphid-like insect that attacks and destroys various grape plants, causing serious damage to vineyards
insect pest of grape plants

Examples

The devastation caused by Phylloxera vitifoleae led to a major crisis in European vineyards in the 19th century.
Grape growers introduced resistant rootstocks to combat the threat of Phylloxera vitifoleae.
Research continues into natural predators of Phylloxera vitifoleae to help protect grape yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylloxera - another term for Phylloxera vitifoleae, the species as a general grape pest

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'xēros' (dry), combined with modern Latin species name 'vitifoleae'.
