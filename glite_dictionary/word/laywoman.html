laywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈleɪˌwʊm.ən / (BrE)
a woman who is not a member of the clergy or a particular profession, especially one without specialized or professional knowledge
woman who is not clergy or specialist

Examples

She spoke as a laywoman, bringing a fresh perspective to the theological discussion.
As a laywoman, she found some medical terms difficult to understand.
The committee welcomed input from any laywoman with an interest in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layman - a man who is not a member of the clergy or a specialist
  • Antonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy

Etymology

From 'lay' (from Old French 'lai', meaning non-clerical) + 'woman'.
