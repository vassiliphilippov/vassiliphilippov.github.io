Malayo-Polynesian

1. ADJ

/ məˌleɪoʊˌpɑːləˈniːʒən / (AmE), / məˌleɪəʊˌpɒlɪˈniːʒən / (BrE)
relating to the Malayo-Polynesian branch of the Austronesian language family, which includes languages spoken in Southeast Asia, the Pacific, and parts of Madagascar
of the Malayo-Polynesian language family

Examples

Many languages spoken in Indonesia and the Philippines are classified as Malayo-Polynesian.
The Malayo-Polynesian languages are known for their complex systems of verb affixation.
Scholars have studied the migration patterns of Malayo-Polynesian speakers across the Pacific islands.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Austronesian - relating to the larger Austronesian language family

Etymology

From 'Malay' (the language) and 'Polynesian,' reflecting the geographic and linguistic scope of the branch.

2. NOUN

/ məˌleɪoʊˌpɑliˈniʒən / (AmE), / məˌleɪəʊˌpɒlɪˈniːʒən / (BrE)
the branch of the Austronesian language family spoken across a region from Madagascar to the central Pacific, including languages such as Malay, Tagalog, and Maori
language branch within Austronesian family

Examples

Many languages spoken in Indonesia belong to the Malayo-Polynesian group.
Scholars have traced the migration patterns of Malayo-Polynesian peoples across the Pacific.
Madagascar's main language, Malagasy, is part of the Malayo-Polynesian family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Austronesian - the wider language family encompassing Malayo-Polynesian and other branches

Etymology

Formed from 'Malay' and 'Polynesian' to denote the languages spoken in these regions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06950545-n
Synset POS: n
