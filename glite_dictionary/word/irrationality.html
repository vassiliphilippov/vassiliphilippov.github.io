irrationality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌræʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌræʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being irrational; lack of reason or logic.
lack of reason or logic

Examples

His sudden outburst was a sign of irrationality rather than careful thought.
The debate questioned the irrationality inherent in some traditions.
Scientists strive to avoid irrationality in their research methods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rationality - the state of being rational; using reason or logic
  • Synonym: unreason - lack of reason; irrational thought or behavior

Etymology

From Latin irrationalitas, from irrationalis (irrational).
