Gerald Ford

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛrəld fɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrəld fɔːd / (BrE)
(1913–2006) the 38th President of the United States, who became president after Richard Nixon's resignation.
38th U.S. president (1974–1977)

Examples

Gerald Ford became president after Richard Nixon resigned in 1974.
The museum is dedicated to the legacy of Gerald Ford.
Many historians have analyzed the decisions made by Gerald Ford during his presidency.
