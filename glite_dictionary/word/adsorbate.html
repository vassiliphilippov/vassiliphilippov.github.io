adsorbate

1. NOUN

/ ædˈsɔrˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ədˈsɔːˌbeɪt / (BrE)
A substance that has been adsorbed onto the surface of another material.
substance adsorbed onto a surface
medical

Examples

The activated carbon acted as an adsorbate in the purification process.
Once an adsorbate is bound to the surface, it can alter the catalyst's reactivity.
Different adsorbate molecules interact with the metal surface in unique ways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adsorbent - material that causes another substance to be adsorbed onto its surface

Etymology

Formed from 'adsorb' (from Latin ad- ‘to’ + sorbere ‘suck in’) and the suffix '-ate'.
