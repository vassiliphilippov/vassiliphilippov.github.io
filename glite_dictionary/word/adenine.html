adenine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.əˌnin / (AmE), / ˈad.ɪ.niːn / (BrE)
a purine base found in DNA and RNA, where it pairs with thymine in DNA and with uracil in RNA
purine base in DNA and RNA
medical

Examples

Adenine is one of the four nitrogenous bases in DNA, essential for storing genetic information.
In RNA, adenine pairs with uracil instead of thymine.
Researchers analyzed how adenine mutations can affect the stability of genetic material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guanine - a purine base found in DNA and RNA, pairs with cytosine
  • Antonym: thymine - a pyrimidine base found in DNA, pairs with adenine

Etymology

From German 'Adenin', from Greek 'aden' (gland) and '-in' (chemical suffix).
