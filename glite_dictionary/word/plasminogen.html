plasminogen

1. NOUN

/ plæzˈmɪnədʒən / (AmE), / plazˈmɪnədʒən / (BrE)
an inactive protein in blood plasma that is converted into plasmin, an enzyme involved in dissolving blood clots
inactive blood protein, precursor to plasmin
medical

Examples

Researchers measured the level of plasminogen in the patient's blood after the surgery.
A deficiency of plasminogen can lead to impaired wound healing and thrombotic conditions.
The drug works by activating plasminogen, which helps dissolve dangerous clots in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasmin - an enzyme that dissolves blood clots by breaking down fibrin

Etymology

From 'plasmin' (an enzyme) + '-ogen' (a suffix meaning precursor or generator).
