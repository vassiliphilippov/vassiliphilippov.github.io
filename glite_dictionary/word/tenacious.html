tenacious

1. ADJ

/ təˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / tɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having a strong ability to remember things; highly retentive.
having a strong memory

Examples

Her tenacious memory allowed her to recall details that everyone else had forgotten.
With his tenacious mind, he mastered several languages with ease.
A detective needs a tenacious memory to keep track of countless facts and clues.

Synset Examples

a retentive mind
tenacious memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retentive - having a good memory; able to retain information easily

Etymology

From Latin tenax (holding fast), from tenere (to hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02012852-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ təˈneɪ.ʃəs / (AmE), / tɪˈneɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
Able to stick firmly to something, especially by adhering strongly or clinging tightly.
clinging or adhesive

Examples

The tenacious glue held the two parts firmly together for weeks.
Velcro is designed to be tenacious, making it perfect for items that need to stay attached.
The tenacious weeds clung persistently to the garden wall.

Synset Examples

two coherent sheets
tenacious burrs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - able to stick fast to a surface or object; sticky
  • Synonym: cohesive - characterized by or causing cohesion; tending to stick together

Etymology

From Latin tenax, tenac- (holding fast), from tenere (to hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00053624-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ təˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / tɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
Not easily discouraged or stopped; holding firmly to an idea, goal, or object.
holding firmly; persistent

Examples

The detective was tenacious in pursuing every lead until the case was solved.
Her tenacious grip on her beliefs made it hard to change her mind.
Only the most tenacious runners managed to finish the grueling marathon.

Synset Examples

dogged persistence
dour determination
the most vocal and pertinacious of all the critics
a mind not gifted to discover truth but tenacious to hold it
men tenacious of opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Synonym: stubborn - refusing to change one's mind or actions despite persuasion
  • Antonym: yielding - ready to give way or to be influenced

Etymology

From Latin tenax (holding fast), from tenere (to hold).
