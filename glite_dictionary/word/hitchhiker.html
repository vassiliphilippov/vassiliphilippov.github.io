hitchhiker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪtʃˌhaɪkər / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃˌhaɪkə / (BrE)
a person who travels by getting free rides from passing vehicles, usually by standing at the roadside and asking passing drivers for a lift
person traveling by free rides from passing cars

Examples

We picked up a hitchhiker on the way to the next town.
The young hitchhiker carried just a small backpack and a map.
Many people used to work as a hitchhiker across Europe to save money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hitchhike - to travel by getting free rides from passing vehicles

Etymology

From 'hitchhike', formed from 'hitch' (to lift or move suddenly) + 'hike' (a long walk), with '-er' as an agent noun suffix.
