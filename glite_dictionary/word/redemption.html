redemption

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the action of saving or being saved from sin, error, or evil, often by a saviour.
salvation from sin or evil (theology)

Examples

The concept of redemption lies at the heart of many Christian teachings.
He prayed for redemption after years of living in sin.
Many hymns celebrate mankind's redemption through sacrifice and forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salvation - deliverance from sin or its consequences (theology)
  • Antonym: damnation - condemnation to eternal punishment (theology)

Etymology

From Latin redemptio (a buying back, ransom), from redimere (to buy back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of buying back something that was previously sold or pledged, especially in financial or legal contexts
act of buying something back

Examples

He paid the necessary fee for the redemption of his pawned watch.
The redemption of government bonds takes place after a set period.
After negotiating with the lender, the redemption of the property was finalized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repurchase - the act of buying something again that was previously owned or sold

Etymology

From Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back), from 'redimere' (to buy back).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of repaying or recovering the principal amount of a debt or security, especially by paying off a loan or repurchasing issued shares or bonds
repayment of debt or repurchase of securities

Examples

The company announced the redemption of its outstanding bonds ahead of schedule.
Early redemption of the loan saved the business thousands in interest payments.
Shareholders were notified about the redemption of preferred stock later this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repayment - the act of paying back money previously borrowed
  • Synonym: purchase - the act of buying something, especially in finance or business

Etymology

From Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back), from 'redimere' (to buy back).
