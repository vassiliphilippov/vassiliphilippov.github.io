maltha

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælθə / (AmE), / ˈmælθə / (BrE)
a thick, black, sticky form of natural bitumen that is intermediate in consistency between petroleum and asphalt
a tar-like bitumen between petroleum and asphalt

Examples

The ancient builders used maltha to waterproof their walls and foundations.
During geological surveys, thick deposits of maltha can sometimes be found near oil fields.
The sticky consistency of maltha makes it useful in some industrial sealing applications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: asphalt - a black, sticky substance used for road surfaces
  • Degree Lower: petroleum - a naturally occurring liquid mixture of hydrocarbons

Etymology

From Latin maltha (a kind of bitumen), via Greek máltē (pitch, resin).
