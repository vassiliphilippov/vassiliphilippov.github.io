kleptomaniac

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɛp.təˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (AmE), / ˌklɛp.təˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (BrE)
a person who has a recurrent, irresistible urge to steal, typically without an economic or personal motive
person with impulse to steal

Examples

The store owner suspected that the frequent shoplifter was a kleptomaniac rather than a petty thief.
Some kleptomaniacs steal small items not because they need them, but due to their uncontrollable impulses.
She was diagnosed as a kleptomaniac after repeatedly taking things from friends' houses without reason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals; general term for someone who commits theft
  • Synonym: kleptomania - a mental disorder characterized by a recurring urge to steal
  • Antonym: law-abiding - obeying the law; not prone to criminal behavior

Etymology

Formed from Greek kleptein (to steal) and -mania (madness, obsession).
