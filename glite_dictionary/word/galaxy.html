galaxy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.əksi / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.əksi / (BrE)
A system of millions or billions of stars, together with gas and dust, held together by gravitational attraction.
large system of stars in space

Examples

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our solar system.
Astronomers discovered a new galaxy over ten billion light-years away.
Each galaxy can contain billions of stars and massive clouds of gas.

Synset Examples

‘extragalactic nebula’ is a former name for ‘galaxy’

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: universe - all of space and everything in it, including all galaxies
  • Degree Lower: star system - a group of stars which may form part of a galaxy

Etymology

From Greek galaxias (milky), via Latin galaxia, originally referring to the Milky Way.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ˈɡaləksi / (BrE)
A distinguished group or assemblage, especially of illustrious or notable people.
distinguished group of notable people

Examples

The awards gala brought together a galaxy of sports stars and celebrities.
A galaxy of influential writers attended the international book festival.
She was honored to perform before a galaxy of political leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of notable people or things

Etymology

From Greek galaxias (milky), via Latin galaxia, originally referring to the Milky Way, later used metaphorically.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ˈɡælək(siː) / (BrE)
A perennial evergreen herb (Galax urceolata) native to the southeastern United States, with spikes of tiny white flowers and round to heart-shaped glossy leaves that turn reddish or purplish in autumn.
a perennial evergreen herb with white flowers and glossy leaves

Examples

The galaxy is prized by gardeners for its attractive foliage that turns a deep maroon in autumn.
In shaded woodland gardens, galaxy thrives as a ground cover with its glossy, heart-shaped leaves.
After planting galaxy in spring, you can expect clusters of delicate white flowers by early summer.

Etymology

From the genus name *Galax*, likely derived from Greek *gala* (milk), referring to the white flowers.
