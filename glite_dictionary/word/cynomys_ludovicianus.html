Cynomys ludovicianus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌnoʊmɪs ˌluːdəˈvɪʃiənəs / (AmE), / sɪˌnəʊmɪs ˌluːdəˈvɪsɪənəs / (BrE)
A species of prairie dog native to North America, known for its black-tipped tail.
black-tailed prairie dog

Examples

The Cynomys ludovicianus colony covered hundreds of acres across the grasslands.
Researchers observed the behavior of Cynomys ludovicianus in response to predators.
Many wildlife photographers visit the plains to capture images of Cynomys ludovicianus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie dog - A burrowing North American rodent

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cynomys' meaning 'dog mouse' and 'ludovicianus' referring to Louisiana (type locality).
