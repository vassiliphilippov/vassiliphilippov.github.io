unlined

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪnd / (BrE)
(of skin or surface) smooth, without lines, wrinkles, or creases.
smooth, without lines or wrinkles

Examples

Despite his age, his unlined face gave him a youthful appearance.
Her hands were remarkably unlined for someone who worked outdoors all her life.
The actor's unlined cheeks made him perfect for the role of a young prince.

Synset Examples

his cheeks were unlined
his unseamed face

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lined - marked with lines or wrinkles
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02245722-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a garment, container, or similar item) not having a lining.
not having a lining

Examples

She wore an unlined coat that was perfect for spring evenings.
The notebook was unlined, giving her plenty of freedom to sketch.
His face appeared unlined despite his age.

Synset Examples

a thin unlined jacket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lined - (of a garment or item) provided with a lining

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'lined' (furnished with a lining).
