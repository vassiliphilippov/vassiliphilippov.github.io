gracious

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪ.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
Showing charm, elegance, or generous and courteous behavior, especially towards others.
charming and generous in manner

Examples

She was always gracious to her guests, making everyone feel welcome and comfortable.
The ambassador accepted the honor in a gracious and dignified manner.
Despite the criticism, he remained gracious and thanked everyone for their feedback.

Synset Examples

gracious even to unexpected visitors
gracious living
he bears insult with gracious good humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courteous - polite and respectful in manner
  • Antonym: rude - impolite or lacking in graciousness

Etymology

From Old French gracieux, from Latin gratiosus (enjoying favour, kind), from gratia (favor, grace).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪʃəs / (BrE)
Courteous, kind, and pleasant; showing politeness and consideration towards others.
courteous and polite

Examples

She was always gracious to guests, making everyone feel welcome in her home.
The actor gave a gracious acceptance speech, thanking everyone who helped along the way.
Even after losing the match, he remained gracious toward his opponent.

Synset Examples

a nice gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courteous - polite, respectful, or considerate in manner
  • Antonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From Old French gracieux, from Latin gratiosus (enjoying favor, obliging).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a person, especially someone of high rank) showing kindness, warmth, and courteous behavior towards others, often with a sense of generosity or magnanimity.
showing kind and courteous behavior, especially by people of rank

Examples

The gracious queen welcomed all her subjects with warmth and respect.
He received us in a gracious manner that made us feel truly honored.
The ambassador was known for his gracious hospitality towards foreign guests.

Synset Examples

our benignant king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benignant - kindly and benevolent, especially in authority
  • Synonym: kind - having a friendly or generous nature
  • Antonym: harsh - lacking kindness or gentleness in treatment or attitude

Etymology

From Old French gracieux ‘pleasing, agreeable’, from Latin gratiosus ‘enjoying favour, esteemed’.

4. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪʃəs / (BrE)
showing kindness, courtesy, or a willingness to bestow favors or mercy
showing kindness or favor

Examples

The host was gracious, welcoming every guest with a warm smile and a kind word.
She accepted the award in a gracious manner, thanking everyone who helped her succeed.
The king was gracious enough to pardon the prisoner's mistake.

Synset Examples

thanks to the gracious gods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kind - considerate and helpful toward others
  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more than is usual or expected
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Old French 'gracieux', from Latin 'gratia' (favor, kindness).
