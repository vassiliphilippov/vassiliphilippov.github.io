desire

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzaɪɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪə / (BrE)
A strong feeling of wanting to have something or wishing for something to happen.
strong feeling of wanting something

Examples

His desire for knowledge led him to read constantly.
She tried to ignore her desire to eat more chocolate.
The desire to travel can be very powerful.

Synset Examples

a man of many desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wish - a hope for something to happen
  • Synonym: craving - an intense desire for something
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Old French 'desir', from Latin 'desiderium' (an earnest wish or longing).
