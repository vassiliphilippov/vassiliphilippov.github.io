scabbard

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæbɚd / (AmE), / ˈskæbəd / (BrE)
a sheath, typically made of leather or metal, for holding a sword, dagger, or bayonet
sheath for a sword or dagger

Examples

The knight drew his sword from its scabbard before entering the arena.
Antique scabbards are often ornately decorated and highly valued by collectors.
He slid the dagger smoothly back into its leather scabbard at his belt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheath - a cover for the blade of a knife or sword

Etymology

From Old French *escauberc*, from a Germanic source related to 'sheathe'.
