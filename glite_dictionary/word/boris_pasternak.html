Boris Pasternak

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɔːrɪs pæs.tərˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈbɒr.ɪs ˌpæst.əˈnæk / (BrE)
(1890–1960) Russian writer and poet, best known for his novel Doctor Zhivago, which was banned in the Soviet Union but published internationally.
Russian writer and poet (1890–1960)

Examples

Boris Pasternak won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1958, which he was later forced to decline.
Many readers around the world have been moved by Boris Pasternak's poetry and novels.
Boris Pasternak's Doctor Zhivago remains a classic work of twentieth-century literature.
