faery

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈfeə.ri / (BrE)
An imaginary or enchanted realm inhabited by fairies; fairyland.
enchanted realm of fairies

Examples

The novel takes place in a magical faery where time flows differently.
Legends speak of a hidden faery beyond the edge of the old forest.
Children dreamt of entering the mysterious faery described in their grandmother's stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairyland - the imaginary land where fairies live

Etymology

From Old French *faerie* (enchantment, fairyland), ultimately from Latin *fāta* (the Fates).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri / (BrE)
A small, often humanlike creature in folklore, believed to possess magical powers and a playful nature; a fairy.
magical, humanlike creature in folklore

Examples

Legends say that a faery can grant wishes to those deemed worthy.
In the moonlit garden, a delicate faery danced among the flowers.
The old tale describes how a mischievous faery switched the children’s shoes at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small, magical, humanlike being in folklore

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'fairy,' from Old French 'faerie' (enchantment, fairyland), ultimately from Latin 'fāta' (fate).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
