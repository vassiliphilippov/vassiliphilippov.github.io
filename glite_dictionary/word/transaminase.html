transaminase

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsæməˌneɪs / (AmE), / trænˈsæmɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the transfer of an amino group from an amino acid to another compound, playing a key role in amino acid metabolism
enzyme transferring amino groups
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered tests to check the level of transaminase enzymes in his blood.
Elevated transaminase activity can indicate liver damage or disease.
Researchers study how each transaminase facilitates amino acid transfer in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aminotransferase - another term for transaminase, often used in medical contexts

Etymology

From 'transamination' + '-ase', following the pattern for enzyme names in biochemistry.
