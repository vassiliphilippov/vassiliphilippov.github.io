Peramelidae

1. NOUN

/ pɛrəˌmɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / pɛrəˌmɛlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized marsupial mammals known as bandicoots, native to Australia and nearby regions.
family of marsupials (bandicoots)

Examples

The Peramelidae family contains several species of bandicoots native to Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists study Peramelidae fossils to learn about marsupial evolution.
Members of Peramelidae play an important role in their native ecosystems by turning over soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandicoot - any of several small marsupial mammals of the family Peramelidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on type genus Perameles + -idae (family suffix).
