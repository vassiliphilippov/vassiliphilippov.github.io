schmooze

1. NOUN

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
an informal, friendly conversation, often intended to gain favor or network
informal, friendly conversation
informal

Examples

We had a long schmooze over coffee before the meeting started.
The annual dinner is a chance for members to enjoy a friendly schmooze.
She always loves a good schmooze with colleagues after work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - an informal conversation
  • Synonym: gossip - casual conversation about others, sometimes unkind

Etymology

From Yiddish shmuesn (to chat), from Hebrew shemu'oth (news, things heard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07149558-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
to talk informally and in a friendly, sometimes ingratiating way, often to gain an advantage
talk casually and amiably
informal

Examples

She loves to schmooze with her colleagues at office parties.
The sales manager spent the evening schmoozing potential clients.
He schmoozed his way into an invitation to the exclusive event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about the personal lives of others, often informally

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmuesn* (to chat), from Hebrew *shmu'ot* (talks, rumors).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01041034-v
Synset POS: v
