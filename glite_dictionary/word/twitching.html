twitching

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an involuntary sudden muscle spasm, especially one caused by a nervous condition
sudden involuntary muscle spasm

Examples

He felt a twitching in his left eyelid all day.
The doctor examined her for possible causes of the muscle twitching.
Stress often results in twitching of the limbs or face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
  • Degree Higher: cramp - a painful involuntary contraction of a muscle

Etymology

From Middle English twicchen, meaning 'to pluck or pull suddenly'.
