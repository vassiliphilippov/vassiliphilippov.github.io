velodrome

1. NOUN

/ vɛl.əˌdroʊm / (AmE), / vəˈlɒd.rəʊm / (BrE)
a banked, oval track specially designed for bicycle or motorcycle racing
banked racing track for bicycles

Examples

The Olympic finals were held at the new velodrome on the outskirts of the city.
She trains every weekend at the local velodrome, practicing her sprint starts.
The steeply banked curves of a velodrome challenge even experienced cyclists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle track - a track for bicycle racing, usually outdoors and less steep than a velodrome

Etymology

From French vélodrome, from vélo (bicycle) and -drome (racecourse).
