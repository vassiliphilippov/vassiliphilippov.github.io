abstrusity

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstruːsəti / (AmE), / əbˈstruːsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being abstruse; profound difficulty or complexity that makes something hard to understand
state of being difficult to understand

Examples

The abstrusity of the philosopher's writings discourages many casual readers.
Despite its abstrusity, the lecture was deeply rewarding to those who persevered.
Critics often debate whether complexity adds meaningful abstrusity to literary works.

Synset Examples

the anthropologist was impressed by the reconditeness of the native proverbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconditeness - the quality of being obscure or difficult to understand
  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown, inconspicuous, or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From abstruse + -ity, patterned after Latin abstract nouns.
