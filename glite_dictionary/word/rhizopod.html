rhizopod

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪzəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈraɪzəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a protozoan of a group characterized by the presence of pseudopodia, used for movement and feeding
type of protozoan with pseudopods
medical

Examples

Scientists observed a rhizopod extending its pseudopodia to engulf food particles.
The rhizopod is commonly found in freshwater environments.
Under the microscope, the rhizopod appeared to change shape as it moved.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referencing their root-like extensions.
