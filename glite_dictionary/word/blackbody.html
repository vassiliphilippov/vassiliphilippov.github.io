blackbody

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an idealized physical body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation, regardless of frequency or angle of incidence
ideal absorber of radiation (physics)

Examples

A blackbody emits radiation with a spectrum that depends only on its temperature.
In physics classes, students often study the properties of a blackbody to understand thermal radiation.
The Sun approximates a blackbody, which allows scientists to calculate its temperature from the light it emits.

Synset Examples

a black body maintained at a constant temperature is a full radiator at that temperature because the radiation reaching and leaving it must be in equilibrium

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whitebody - an idealized body that reflects all incident radiation

Etymology

From 'black' + 'body'; used in physics since the 19th century to describe ideal radiators.
