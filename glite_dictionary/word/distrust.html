distrust

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈtrʌst / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrʌst / (BrE)
Lack of trust or confidence in someone or something, often due to doubts about honesty or reliability.
lack of trust or confidence

Examples

His growing distrust of the government made him question every official statement.
There is a deep distrust between the two rival companies, leading to constant suspicion.
Years of broken promises bred a sense of distrust among the employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistrust - lack of trust or confidence in someone or something
  • Antonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or truth of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *destruster*, based on Latin *dis-* (expressing reversal) + *trust*.
