infusorian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fjuˈsɔː.ri.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fjʊˈsɔː.ri.ən / (BrE)
a microscopic, water-dwelling organism of the former subclass Infusoria, mainly comprising ciliates
microscopic aquatic organism (mainly ciliates)

Examples

Under the microscope, the culture teemed with numerous infusorians darting about.
Some aquarists use infusorians as the first food for young fish.
Natural pond water is often home to a variety of infusorians and other microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ciliate - a single-celled organism covered with cilia, part of the phylum Ciliophora

Etymology

From New Latin 'Infusoria', based on Latin 'infusio' (infusion), originally referring to organisms found in infusions of decaying matter.
