roiling

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) moving in a violently agitated or turbulent way.
violently agitated or turbulent (liquid)

Examples

The storm left behind a roiling sea that battered the coast for days.
Steam rose from the roiling pot as the soup boiled furiously.
The divers hesitated before entering the roiling waters beneath the waterfall.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; (of liquid or air) moving unsteadily or violently

Etymology

Present participle of 'roil', likely from Old French 'rouiller' (to make muddy).
