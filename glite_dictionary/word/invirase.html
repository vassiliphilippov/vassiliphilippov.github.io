Invirase

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvaɪr.eɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪə.reɪz / (BrE)
A trademarked brand name for saquinavir, a weak protease inhibitor used to treat HIV infection.
HIV drug (saquinavir; brand name)
medical american

Examples

After being diagnosed with HIV, her doctor prescribed Invirase as part of her treatment regimen.
The effectiveness of Invirase depends on taking it together with other antiretroviral drugs.
Some patients may experience mild side effects when they start Invirase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saquinavir - an antiretroviral drug used to treat HIV/AIDS
