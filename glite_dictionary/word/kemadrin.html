Kemadrin

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmædrɪn / (AmE), / kɛˈmædrɪn / (BrE)
A proprietary name for procyclidine, a drug used to help reduce tremors and muscle stiffness in Parkinson's disease.
drug for Parkinson's disease symptoms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Kemadrin to help manage the patient's tremors.
Some people with Parkinson's find that Kemadrin helps improve their muscle control.
After starting Kemadrin, she noticed a reduction in muscle stiffness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procyclidine - the generic drug also known as Kemadrin
