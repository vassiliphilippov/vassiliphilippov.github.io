Paleolithic

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaliəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
The early period of the Stone Age, lasting from about 2.5 million years ago to around 8,500 BC, characterized by the development of the first stone tools.
early Stone Age period

Examples

The Paleolithic era marked the beginning of human tool use.
Researchers discovered cave paintings dating back to the Paleolithic period.
During the Paleolithic, humans survived as hunter-gatherers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Neolithic - later Stone Age period with more advanced stone tools and agriculture

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + lithos (stone).
