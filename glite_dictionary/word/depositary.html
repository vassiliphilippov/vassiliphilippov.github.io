depositary

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑzɪˌtɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪtəri / (BrE)
a person, organization, or place entrusted with something for safekeeping or storage
person or place for safekeeping

Examples

The museum is a depositary for many rare manuscripts.
He placed the valuable documents in a secure depositary to ensure their safety.
As a trusted depositary, the bank guarantees the protection of clients' assets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repository - a place where things are stored or can be found

Etymology

From Latin depositarius (one with whom something is deposited), from depositum (a deposit).
