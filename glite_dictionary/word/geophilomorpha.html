Geophilomorpha

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːoʊˌfɪləˈmɔːrfə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˌfɪl.əˈmɔː.fə / (BrE)
an order of small, elongated centipedes found in soil and under stones, typically possessing more than 30 pairs of legs
order of soil-dwelling centipedes

Examples

The Geophilomorpha are known for their elongated bodies and numerous legs.
Researchers discovered several new species of Geophilomorpha in the forest soil.
Unlike other centipedes, the Geophilomorpha prefer to burrow deep beneath stones and logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centipede - predatory arthropod with many body segments and legs

Etymology

From Greek geo- (earth) + philo- (loving) + morphe (form, shape); referring to earth-loving centipedes.

2. NOUN

/ dʒiˌɑːfəloʊˈmɔːrfə / (AmE), / dʒiˌɒfɪləʊˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
an order of small, elongate centipedes that live in soil and under stones and have more than 30 pairs of legs
an order of soil-dwelling centipedes

Examples

Many species in the Geophilomorpha order are adapted to burrowing in loose soil.
The Geophilomorpha are often mistaken for worms because of their numerous legs and elongated bodies.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Geophilomorpha under the rocks during the field survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centipede - a predatory, many-legged arthropod of the class Chilopoda

Etymology

From Greek 'ge' (earth) + 'philos' (loving) + 'morphe' (form), referring to earth-loving form.
