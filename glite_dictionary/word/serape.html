serape

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɑːpeɪ / (AmE), / səˈrɑːpeɪ / (BrE)
a long, brightly colored shawl, typically worn by Mexican men
Mexican brightly colored shawl

Examples

He wore a vibrant serape draped over his shoulders during the festival.
The shop displayed serapes in many different colors and patterns.
As the sun set, she wrapped a thick serape around herself to keep warm.

Etymology

From Spanish 'sarape', referring to the traditional garment.
