Prionotus

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈɑːnətəs / (AmE), / prʌɪˈɒnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Triglidae, commonly known as sea robins, characterized by armored heads and fingerlike pectoral fin rays.
a genus of marine sea robin fish

Examples

A new species within the genus Prionotus was recently discovered along the Atlantic coast.
The distinctive pectoral fins of Prionotus species help them "walk" along the ocean floor.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Prionotus in shallow coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea robin - common name for fishes in the genus Prionotus

Etymology

From Greek 'prion', meaning 'saw', and 'noton', meaning 'back'.

2. NOUN

/ praɪˈɑː.nə.təs / (AmE), / praɪˈɒ.nə.təs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Triglidae, commonly known as sea robins, characterized by their large pectoral fins and spiny heads.
genus of sea robin fishes

Examples

The genus Prionotus includes several species commonly referred to as sea robins.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Prionotus genus off the Atlantic coast.
Unlike many bottom-dwelling fish, Prionotus species can 'walk' on the sea floor using their pelvic fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea robin - a common name for fishes of the genus Prionotus

Etymology

From Greek *prion* (saw) and *notos* (back), referring to features of the fish.
