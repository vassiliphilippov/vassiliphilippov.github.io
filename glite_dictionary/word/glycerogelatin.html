glycerogelatin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɪsəroʊˈdʒɛlətɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡlɪsərəʊˈdʒɛlətɪn / (BrE)
A medicinal preparation made from a mixture of glycerin and gelatin, used as a base for skin treatments or dressings.
medicated glycerin-gelatin skin preparation
medical

Examples

The pharmacist prepared a glycerogelatin dressing for the patient's wound.
Glycerogelatin is sometimes used to deliver medication through the skin in a controlled manner.
Doctors may prescribe glycerogelatin formulations to treat certain skin conditions requiring topical therapy.

Etymology

From glycerin + gelatin.
