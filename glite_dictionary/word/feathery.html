feathery

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛðəri / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəri / (BrE)
having a light, delicate appearance or texture, like that of feathers; resembling or suggesting feathers.
resembling or suggesting feathers

Examples

The artist used a feathery brushstroke to create a soft background.
She wore a feathery scarf that felt light around her neck.
The morning fog had a feathery quality as it drifted across the fields.

Synset Examples

feathery palm trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featherlike - resembling a feather
  • Synonym: downy - covered with soft fine feathers

Etymology

From 'feather' + '-y', a suffix forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɛðəri / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəri / (BrE)
adorned with or resembling feathers or plumes; having a light, delicate, or feathery quality.
having or resembling feathers or plumes

Examples

She wore a feathery scarf that added elegance to her outfit.
The artist painted a feathery pattern across the canvas, giving it a soft appearance.
Light, feathery clouds drifted across the summer sky.

Synset Examples

a feathered hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathered - covered with or made of feathers

Etymology

From 'feather' + '-y', forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of.'

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɛðəri / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəri / (BrE)
covered with or resembling feathers; having the light, soft, or delicate qualities of feathers.
covered with or resembling feathers

Examples

Morning dew clung to the feathery ferns lining the garden path.
The artist painted clouds with a feathery texture against the blue sky.
The owl's feathery wings allowed it to fly almost silently through the night.

Synset Examples

the feathery congregation of jays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathered - covered with feathers
  • Degree Lower: hairy - covered with hair
  • Synonym: plumaged - having a covering of plumage; feathered

Etymology

From 'feather' + '-y', a suffix meaning 'characterized by'.
