chlorenchyma

1. NOUN

/ klɔːˈrɛŋ.kɪ.mə / (AmE), / klɔːˈrɛŋ.kɪ.mə / (BrE)
Plant tissue composed of parenchyma cells containing chloroplasts, responsible for photosynthesis.
plant tissue with chloroplasts
medical

Examples

The palisade layer of leaves is composed mainly of chlorenchyma to carry out photosynthesis.
In aquatic plants, chlorenchyma helps to keep the stems buoyant while providing cells for photosynthesis.
Botanists observed that the chlorenchyma cells were especially abundant near the surface of the leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parenchyma - fundamental tissue in plants and animals consisting of unspecialized cells

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) + 'enchyma' (infusion, tissue).
