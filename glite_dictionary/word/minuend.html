minuend

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnjuˌɛnd / (AmE), / ˈmɪnjʊˌɛnd / (BrE)
A number from which another number (the subtrahend) is to be subtracted.
number from which another is subtracted

Examples

In the equation 10 - 3 = 7, the number 10 is the minuend.
To find the difference, subtract the subtrahend from the minuend.
The teacher asked the students to identify both the minuend and the subtrahend in each problem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subtrahend - the number that is subtracted from the minuend

Etymology

From Latin minuendus, gerundive of minuere (to lessen).
