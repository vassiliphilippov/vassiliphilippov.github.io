cyclonic

1. ADJ

/ saɪˈklɑnɪk / (AmE), / saɪˈklɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a cyclone or the atmospheric conditions around a low-pressure center, often involving rotating winds.
relating to a cyclone or low-pressure system

Examples

The weather forecaster explained that the cyclonic conditions would bring heavy rain and strong winds to the area.
Satellite images revealed a distinctive cyclonic swirl over the ocean.
The region often experiences cyclonic storms during the summer months.

Synset Examples

cyclonic cloud pattern

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticyclonic - relating to the atmosphere around a high-pressure center, opposite of cyclonic
  • Synonym: cyclone - a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From 'cyclone', which comes from Greek 'kyklōn' (moving in a circle, whirling).

2. ADJ

/ saɪˈklɑnɪk / (AmE), / saɪˈklɒnɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to a cyclone or characteristic of violent tropical storms, particularly involving strong winds and destructive force
relating to a cyclone or violent storm

Examples

The area suffered cyclonic winds that toppled trees and damaged houses.
Hurricanes are one of the most dangerous cyclonic phenomena in the world.
Meteorologists warned of a possible cyclonic disturbance approaching the coastline.

Synset Examples

cyclonic destruction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticyclonic - relating to an anticyclone; opposite atmospheric characteristics to cyclonic movement

Etymology

From cyclone + -ic; cyclone from Greek 'kyklōn' (moving in a circle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03069354-a
Synset POS: a
