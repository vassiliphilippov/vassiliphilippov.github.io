elver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlvər / (AmE), / ˈɛlvə / (BrE)
a young eel, especially during its stage of migration from the sea to fresh water
young eel

Examples

Local fishermen caught several elvers as they swam upriver in the spring.
Young elvers are often considered a delicacy in some coastal cuisines.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of elver populations along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eel - long, snake-like fish

Etymology

Originated in the 16th century, perhaps a contraction of 'eel fare', meaning eel journey.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛlvər / (AmE), / ˈɛlvə / (BrE)
a young eel, especially one that has reached the stage of migrating from the sea into fresh water
a young eel

Examples

During the spring, fishermen catch thousands of elvers in coastal rivers.
The elver migration is a crucial event for the local ecosystem.
She studied the life cycle of the elver as part of her biology research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: eel - a long, thin, snake-like fish

Etymology

Originated in the 17th century, probably a contraction of 'eel-fare' (the migration of young eels).
