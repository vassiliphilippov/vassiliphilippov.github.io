two-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuːˌhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌhændɪd / (BrE)
requiring the use of both hands to operate or perform; designed to be used by two hands or by two people working together
needing both hands or two people

Examples

She swung the two-handed sword with surprising strength.
The carpenter used a two-handed saw to cut through the thick log.
Playing the piano is a two-handed activity that requires coordination.

Synset Examples

a two-handed sledgehammer
a two-handed crosscut saw
a machine designed for bimanual operation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-handed - requiring the use of only one hand
  • Antonym: single-handed - done by one person without help
