cleistogamous

1. ADJ

/ klaɪˈstɑɡəməs / (AmE), / klaɪˈstɒɡəməs / (BrE)
(of a flower or plant) producing or relating to flowers that self-pollinate without opening.
self-pollinating without opening (in flowers)

Examples

Some grass species produce both cleistogamous and chasmogamous flowers for reproductive flexibility.
The cleistogamous flowers never open and rely entirely on self-pollination.
Researchers found that cleistogamous plants often thrive in habitats with fewer pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chasmogamous - relating to flowers that open and are cross-pollinated

Etymology

From Greek 'kleistos' (closed) + 'gamos' (marriage).
