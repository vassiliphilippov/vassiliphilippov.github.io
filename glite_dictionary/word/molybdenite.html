molybdenite

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪbdəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / məˈlɪbdɪnaɪt / (BrE)
A soft, lead-gray mineral that resembles graphite and is the chief ore of molybdenum.
chief ore of molybdenum

Examples

Geologists discovered a rich vein of molybdenite deep within the mountain.
Molybdenite is often mined for its valuable molybdenum content.
The chemical structure of molybdenite makes it useful in certain industrial applications.

Etymology

From molybdenum + -ite, indicating a mineral.
