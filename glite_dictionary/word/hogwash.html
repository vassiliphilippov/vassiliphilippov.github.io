hogwash

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːɡˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
nonsense; absurd or untrue statements or ideas.
nonsense; absurd statements
informal

Examples

Don't believe a word he says—it's all hogwash from start to finish.
She dismissed the rumors as utter hogwash and refused to comment further.
The politician's explanation was criticized as pure hogwash by the press.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - ideas or statements that are absurd or meaningless
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

Originally referred to kitchen scraps fed to pigs; from 'hog' (pig) + 'wash' (waste water).
