stipendiary

1. ADJ

/ stɪˈpɛnd.iˌɛri / (AmE), / stɪˈpɛnd.i.əri / (BrE)
Relating to or receiving a stipend or regular allowance.
relating to a stipend or allowance

Examples

Many positions in the organization are stipendiary rather than voluntary.
She received a stipendiary grant to support her research.
The foundation created stipendiary funds to assist young artists.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salaried - receiving a wage or salary
  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or without payment

Etymology

2. ADJ

/ stɪˈpɛndiˌɛri / (AmE), / stɪˈpɛndiəri / (BrE)
(of a person or office) receiving a regular payment or salary, especially for a job or position normally unpaid or voluntary.
receiving a regular payment or salary

Examples

The organization hired a stipendiary secretary to manage its daily operations.
In the past, many magistrates were unpaid, but now some are stipendiary.
Serving as a stipendiary official, she received a modest monthly allowance.

Synset Examples

salaried workers
a stipendiary magistrate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salaried - receiving a regular salary
  • Antonym: voluntary - working without pay or compensation

Etymology

From Latin stipendium (payment, salary), via late Latin stipendiarius (related to payment).

(of a job, position, or service) receiving a fixed regular payment; paid a stipend or salary.
paid; receiving a stipend or salary

Examples

He accepted a stipendiary post as the organization's treasurer.
Unlike voluntary judges, stipendiary magistrates receive a regular salary.
Many charities rely on both stipendiary and volunteer workers.

4. NOUN

/ stɪˈpɛn.diˌɛri / (AmE), / stɪˈpɛndiəri / (BrE)
(in the United Kingdom, historical) a paid magistrate, especially one appointed by the Home Secretary to deal with police cases, as opposed to an unpaid lay magistrate.
a paid magistrate (UK, historical)
british legal archaic

Examples

The court was presided over by a stipendiary who handled the most complex cases.
Many metropolitan areas once employed a stipendiary to manage a high volume of police cases.
He served as a stipendiary in London before the role was replaced by district judges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magistrate - a civil officer or judge who administers the law

Etymology

From Latin stipendarius (receiving pay).
