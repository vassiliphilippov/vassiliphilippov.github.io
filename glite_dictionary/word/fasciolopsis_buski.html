Fasciolopsis buski

1. NOUN

/ fæsiəˈlɑpsɪs ˈbʌski / (AmE), / fasɪəˈlɒpsɪs ˈbʌski / (BrE)
a large intestinal fluke that infects humans and pigs, commonly found in eastern and southern Asia
a parasitic intestinal fluke of humans and pigs
medical

Examples

Infection with Fasciolopsis buski can cause severe inflammation of the intestines.
Farmers in certain regions are at risk of contracting Fasciolopsis buski from contaminated water plants.
The life cycle of Fasciolopsis buski involves both aquatic snails and pigs as intermediate hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluke - a parasitic flatworm

Etymology

From Latin 'fasciola' (a small band or strip) + Greek 'opsis' (appearance); named by scientific convention after XIX-century classification
