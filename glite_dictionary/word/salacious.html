salacious

1. ADJ

/ səˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
having or showing an excessive or inappropriate interest in sexual matters; arousing or intended to arouse sexual desire
sexually suggestive or lustful

Examples

The celebrity sued the magazine for publishing salacious rumors about her private life.
Some novels were banned for their salacious content and explicit descriptions.
He was known for making salacious jokes that made everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

eluding lubricious embraces
prurient literature
prurient thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prurient - having or encouraging an excessive interest in sexual matters

Etymology

From Latin 'salax', 'salacis' meaning 'lustful' or 'lecherous'.

2. ADJ

/ səˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having or conveying undue or inappropriate interest in sexual matters; lewd or obscene.
sexually indecent; lewd or obscene

Examples

The tabloid published a salacious story about the celebrity's private life.
Some comedians rely on salacious humor that borders on the offensive.
He raised his eyebrow at the salacious details revealed during the trial.

Synset Examples

lewd pictures
obscene telephone calls
salacious limericks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lewd - crude and offensive in a sexual way
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality and decency

Etymology

From Latin salax, salac- (lustful), from salire (to leap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00427259-s
Synset POS: s
