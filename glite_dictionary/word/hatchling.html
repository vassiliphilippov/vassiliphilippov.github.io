hatchling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhætʃ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a young animal that has just emerged from its egg, especially a bird
newly hatched animal, especially a bird

Examples

The turtle hatchling struggled toward the ocean moments after emerging from its egg.
Researchers observed the behavior of each hatchling in the nest.
A robin will diligently feed every hatchling until they are ready to leave the nest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chick - a young bird, especially a baby chicken
  • Degree Higher: fledgling - a young bird that has just acquired its feathers and is learning to fly

Etymology

From 'hatch' (to emerge from an egg) + '-ling' (diminutive or young form).
