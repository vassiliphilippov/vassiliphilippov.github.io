Chloromycetin

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːroʊmaɪˈsiːtɪn / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəmɪˈsiːtɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked antibiotic drug (chloramphenicol) used to treat serious infections, especially typhoid fever.
antibiotic drug; trade name for chloramphenicol
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Chloromycetin to treat the patient's typhoid fever.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to Chloromycetin over the years.
Due to potential side effects, Chloromycetin is used with caution in clinical practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloramphenicol - broad-spectrum antibiotic drug

Etymology

Trade name, combining 'chloro-' (chlorine) and 'mycetin' (from Greek mykēs, fungus), named for its chemical structure and origin.
