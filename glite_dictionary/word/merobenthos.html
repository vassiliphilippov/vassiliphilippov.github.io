merobenthos

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛroʊˈbɛnθoʊs / (AmE), / ˌmɛərəʊˈbɛnθɒs / (BrE)
organisms that spend the early stages of their life in the benthos (sediment or bottom of a water body) and become planktonic as adults
organisms that are benthic in early stages

Examples

Recent research on coastal ecosystems has increased our understanding of the merobenthos and its ecological significance.
The life cycle of some marine worms includes a merobenthos stage before they transition to free-swimming plankton as adults.
Scientists collected samples of merobenthos from the sediment to study larval development in estuarine environments.

Synset Examples

More recent studies highlighted the role of merobenthos as cysts of planktonic organisms that spend part of their life quiescent in the sediments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holobenthos - organisms that spend all stages of life in the benthos

Etymology

From Greek 'meros' (part) + 'benthos' (depth of the sea).
