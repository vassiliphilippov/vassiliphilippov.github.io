noisy

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔɪzi / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzi / (BrE)
full of or characterized by loud or unpleasant sounds; making a lot of noise.
full of loud or unpleasant sounds

Examples

The noisy construction outside made it hard to concentrate.
Our neighbors have a noisy party every weekend.
She prefers a quiet workspace to a noisy office.

Synset Examples

a noisy cafeteria
a small noisy dog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
  • Synonym: loud - producing much noise

Etymology

From noise + -y, formed in English in the 15th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɔɪzi / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzi / (BrE)
(of clothing or things) attracting attention by being showy or brightly colored; conspicuous.
showy or conspicuous (of color/clothes)

Examples

His noisy tie made him stand out at the formal dinner.
The artist painted the room in noisy colors that caught everyone's eye.
She wore a noisy sweater with bold stripes and vivid patterns.

Synset Examples

a noisy sweater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loud - (of colors or patterns) very bright or showy
  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or showy, especially of colors or designs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00408843-s
Synset POS: s
