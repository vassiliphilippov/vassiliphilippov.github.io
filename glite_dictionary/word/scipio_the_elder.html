Scipio the Elder

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɪpioʊ ði ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪpɪəʊ ði ˈɛldə / (BrE)
(circa 237–183 BC) Roman general who led the invasion of Carthage during the Second Punic War and defeated Hannibal at Zama.
Roman general; defeated Hannibal at Zama

Examples

Scipio the Elder is renowned for his victory over Hannibal at the Battle of Zama.
Many historians credit Scipio the Elder with saving Rome during the Second Punic War.
The strategic brilliance of Scipio the Elder has been studied by military leaders for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scipio Africanus - Roman general, also known as Scipio the Elder, famous for his victory over Hannibal
  • Antonym: Hannibal - Carthaginian general defeated by Scipio at Zama
