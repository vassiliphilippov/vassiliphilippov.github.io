Harkat ul-Mujahedeen

1. PROPN

/ hɑːrˈkɑːt ʊl muːˈdʒɑːhədin / (AmE), / hɑːˈkɑːt ʊl mʊˈdʒɑːhədiːn / (BrE)
an Islamic militant group based in Pakistan, originally active in fighting the Soviet Union in Afghanistan in the 1980s, now considered a terrorist organization primarily operating in Kashmir and seeking the region's accession to Pakistan
Islamic militant group active in Kashmir and Pakistan

Examples

The Harkat ul-Mujahedeen was involved in cross-border activities in Kashmir throughout the 1990s.
The government has designated Harkat ul-Mujahedeen as a terrorist organization due to its extremist operations.
Intelligence agencies monitor groups like Harkat ul-Mujahedeen for threats to regional security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lashkar-e-Taiba - another armed Islamist group based in Pakistan, active in Kashmir
  • Synonym: Al-Qaeda - a global militant Islamist organization
  • Synonym: Taliban - an Islamic fundamentalist group primarily operating in Afghanistan

Etymology

From Arabic: 'Harkat' meaning 'movement', 'ul' meaning 'of the', and 'Mujahedeen' meaning 'those engaged in jihad'.
