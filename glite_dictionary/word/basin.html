basin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsən / (BrE)
an area of land from which all surface water flows to a single river and its tributaries, draining into a common outlet
area drained by a river system

Examples

The Mississippi basin covers an enormous part of North America.
Intensive farming can affect the ecology of an entire basin.
Dams built in the river basin help control periodic flooding.

Synset Examples

flood control in the Missouri basin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watershed - an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers or basins

Etymology

From Old French 'bacin', from Late Latin 'baccinus' (dish or basin).

5. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsən / (BrE)
a natural depression in the earth's surface, typically containing water or forming a drainage area for lakes or rivers
natural land depression, often with water

Examples

The Colorado River flows through a vast basin surrounded by mountains and plateaus.
The Nile basin is home to millions of people and covers several countries.
Rainwater collected in the natural basin, eventually forming a small lake at the bottom.

Synset Examples

the basin of the Great Salt Lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley - a low area between hills or mountains, often with a river running through it
  • Antonym: plain - a large area of flat, often treeless land

Etymology

From Old French 'bacin', from Medieval Latin 'bacchinus'.
