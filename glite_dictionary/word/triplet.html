triplet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪplət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɪt / (BrE)
a set of three similar things considered as a single unit
set of three similar things

Examples

The necklace was decorated with a triplet of pearls in the center.
A triplet of matching vases stood proudly on the mantelpiece.
The scientist observed a triplet of galaxies clustered closely together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trio - a group of three people or things

Etymology

Formed from 'triple' (meaning 'threefold'), patterned after 'doublet' and 'quintet'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪplət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɪt / (BrE)
one of three children or animals born at the same birth to the same mother
one of three siblings born together

Examples

She was delighted to learn she would be giving birth to triplet girls.
Each triplet had a distinctive personality, even though they looked alike.
The hospital specializes in caring for triplet and other multiple births.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twin - one of two children born at the same birth
  • Degree Higher: quadruplet - one of four offspring born at the same time

Etymology

From triple (threefold) + -et (diminutive suffix), influenced by French 'triplet'.
