fleshly

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɛʃli / (AmE), / ˈflɛʃli / (BrE)
relating to bodily appetites, desires, or passions, especially those considered sensual or carnal rather than spiritual.
relating to sensual or bodily desires

Examples

He struggled to overcome his fleshly desires and remain true to his beliefs.
The monks rejected all fleshly pleasures in pursuit of a spiritual life.
She regarded music as a pure joy, untainted by fleshly temptation.

Synset Examples

animal instincts
carnal knowledge
fleshly desire
a sensual delight in eating
music is the only sensual pleasure without vice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnal - relating to physical, especially sexual, needs and activities
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than physical things

Etymology

From Middle English fleschly, from Old English flǣsclic (of or pertaining to flesh).
