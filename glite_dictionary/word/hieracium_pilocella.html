Hieracium pilocella

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɛəriˈækiəm pɪloʊˈsɛlə / (AmE), / hɪəˌræsiˈʌm pɪləʊˈsɛlə / (BrE)
A species of European hawkweed with soft, hairy leaves, sometimes classified in the genus Hieracium.
species of European hawkweed

Examples

The meadows were dotted with Hieracium pilocella, easily recognized by its soft, hairy leaves.
Gardeners often mistake Hieracium pilocella for other common hawkweeds.
Researchers study Hieracium pilocella to understand the spread of non-native plants.

Etymology

From Latin: Hieracium (the genus name, from Greek 'hierax' meaning 'hawk') and pilocella (diminutive of Latin 'pilus', meaning 'hair').
