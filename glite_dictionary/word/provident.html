provident

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːvɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒvɪdənt / (BrE)
making or indicative of timely preparation for the future; careful in planning for future needs
careful in planning for the future

Examples

It is wise to be provident and save some money for emergencies.
Her provident planning ensured the family never lacked for anything during hard times.
A provident person considers how today's actions affect tomorrow's well-being.

Synset Examples

wild squirrels are provident
a provident father plans for his children's education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - acting with care and thought for the future
  • Antonym: improvident - not having or showing foresight; spendthrift or thoughtless

Etymology

From Latin 'provident-', present participle of 'providere' (to foresee, provide).

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑvədənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒvɪdənt / (BrE)
careful in planning for the future, especially by saving money or making provisions; showing prudent foresight.
prudent and foresighted

Examples

Her provident habits ensured she always had enough savings for emergencies.
It is wise to be provident and put aside money for retirement early in your career.
The provident squirrel collects extra food to prepare for the winter.

Synset Examples

the prudent use and development of resources
wild squirrels are provident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - acting with care and thought for the future
  • Antonym: improvident - not having or showing foresight; spendthrift or thoughtless

Etymology

From Latin 'provident-', meaning 'foreseeing, forethoughtful', from 'providere' (to foresee).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01905041-s
Synset POS: s
