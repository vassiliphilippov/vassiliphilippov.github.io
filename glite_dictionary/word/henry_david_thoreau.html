Henry David Thoreau

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈdeɪvɪd θəˈroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈdeɪvɪd θəˈrəʊ / (BrE)
(1817–1862) An American writer, philosopher, and social critic, best known for his book 'Walden' and his advocacy of civil disobedience.
American writer and social critic

Examples

Henry David Thoreau wrote about his experience living simply in a cabin near Walden Pond.
The philosophy of Henry David Thoreau continues to inspire environmentalists and activists today.
Many students are introduced to Henry David Thoreau through his essays on nature and civil disobedience.
