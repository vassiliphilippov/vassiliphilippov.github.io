seidr

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪˌðər / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.ðər / (BrE)
an Old Norse form of sorcery or magic practiced in Norse society during the Late Scandinavian Iron Age, often associated with prophecy and altering reality
Old Norse magic or sorcery

Examples

Practitioners of seidr were often believed to have the power to influence fate and communicate with spirits.
The saga described how the queen employed seidr to foresee the outcome of the battle.
Modern neopagans sometimes study seidr as part of reconstructing Norse spiritual practices.

Synset Examples

Of all the reconstructed systems of archaic magickal practice, Seiðr seems to be one of the most misunderstood.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'seiðr' (sorcery, divination).
