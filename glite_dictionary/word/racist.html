racist

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪst / (BrE)
discriminating against or prejudiced toward people because of their race; showing or based on racism.
discriminatory based on race
offensive

Examples

His comments were widely criticized as racist and unacceptable.
The company adopted strict policies against racist behavior in the workplace.
A racist attitude can cause deep harm to communities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti-racist - opposing or acting against racism
  • Synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust based on prejudice

Etymology

From race + -ist, originally in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that one race is superior to others and discriminates or is prejudiced against people of other races
person with racist beliefs

Examples

The politician was widely condemned as a racist after making offensive remarks about immigrants.
Many activists are focused on challenging the views held by every racist in their community.
He denied being a racist, but his actions told a different story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigot - a person who is intolerant toward those holding different opinions
  • Antonym: antiracist - a person opposed to racism

Etymology

From race + -ist, formed in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10522535-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈreɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪst / (BrE)
Showing or characterized by the belief that one race is superior to others, or by prejudice, discrimination, or antagonism toward people of other races.
characterized by racial prejudice or intolerance

Examples

His boss was fired for making several racist comments in a meeting.
The new law was criticized as being blatantly racist by civil rights groups.
Many people rallied against the use of racist language in the media.

Synset Examples

racist remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust based on prejudice
  • Antonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow the existence of opinions or behavior different from one's own

Etymology

From race (in the sense of 'ethnic group') + -ist (forming nouns and adjectives).
