to sentimentise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.tə.mən.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪ.mən.taɪz / (BrE)
to act in a sentimental manner or indulge in sentimental thoughts or expressions
act in a sentimental manner

Examples

She tends to sentimentise about the past whenever we visit her old hometown.
It's easy to sentimentise childhood memories, forgetting the difficult moments.
Please don't sentimentise the reality of war; it is harsh and tragic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sentimentalize - to indulge in sentiment or express in a sentimental way

Etymology

From 'sentiment' + '-ise', modeled on similar English formations from French or Latin roots.
