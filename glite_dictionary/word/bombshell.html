bombshell

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌʃɛl / (BrE)
an explosive bomb or artillery shell, especially one used in warfare
explosive bomb or artillery shell

Examples

The old fortress was destroyed by a single bombshell during the assault.
Fragments from the bombshell were found scattered across the field.
Soldiers ducked for cover as another bombshell exploded nearby.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌʃɛl / (BrE)
an extremely attractive and glamorous woman, especially one who has a sensational or stunning effect; commonly used to describe female entertainers
glamorous, strikingly attractive woman

Examples

The young actress became a Hollywood bombshell overnight after her breakout role.
In the 1950s, Marilyn Monroe was considered the ultimate bombshell.
Her transformation for the role turned her into a real bombshell on screen.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex symbol - a famous person widely regarded as sexually attractive

Etymology

Originally referring to an explosive shell, the figurative use for an attractive woman became popular in the early 20th century.
