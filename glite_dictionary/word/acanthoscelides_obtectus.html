Acanthoscelides obtectus

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊsəˈlaɪdiːz ɑbˈtɛktəs / (AmE), / əˌkanθəʊsˈsiːliːdiːz ɒbˈtɛktəs / (BrE)
a small beetle of the family Chrysomelidae, whose larvae infest and feed on growing or stored beans, often considered an agricultural pest
bean-infesting beetle species

Examples

The Acanthoscelides obtectus is a major pest affecting bean storage facilities worldwide.
Farmers often struggle to control Acanthoscelides obtectus infestations in their crops.
Proper storage conditions can help prevent Acanthoscelides obtectus from damaging harvested beans.

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn) and 'skelis' (leg), and Latin 'obtectus' (covered).
