Erolia minutilla

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊliə mɪˈnuːtɪlə / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊlɪə mɪˈnjuːtɪlə / (BrE)
A small North American sandpiper species, also known as the least sandpiper, recognized as the smallest shorebird in the region.
smallest North American sandpiper

Examples

The Erolia minutilla migrates in large flocks along the Atlantic coast each autumn.
Many bird watchers hope to spot the elusive Erolia minutilla during spring migration.
Researchers tagged several Erolia minutilla to better understand their migratory patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: least sandpiper - common name for Erolia minutilla, the smallest North American sandpiper

Etymology

From Modern Latin, where 'Erolia' is a bird genus name and 'minutilla' means 'very small'.
