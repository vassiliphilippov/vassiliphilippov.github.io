sticktight

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪkˌtʌɪt / (BrE)
Any of several plants of the genus Bidens, having yellow flowers and prickly fruits that cling to fur and clothing.
prickly plant clinging to fur or clothes

Examples

I spent an hour picking sticktight seeds off my dog's fur after our hike.
The field was filled with sticktight plants, their yellow flowers bright under the sun.
After walking through the woods, her socks were covered in sticktight burrs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beggartick - another common name for plants in the genus Bidens

Etymology

From 'stick' + 'tight', referring to the plant's fruits that tightly adhere.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪk.tʌɪt / (BrE)
A small parasitic insect, especially a type of flea, that attaches itself firmly to the skin of animals or birds, particularly chickens.
parasitic insect, esp. affecting chickens

Examples

The farmer checked his chickens for sticktights after noticing signs of irritation.
Veterinarians recommend treating poultry houses to prevent sticktight infestations.
A heavy burden of sticktights can weaken young chicks and cause health problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small wingless jumping insect that feeds on blood

Etymology

From 'stick' + 'tight', referring to the flea's tendency to cling tenaciously to its host.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02190552-n
Synset POS: n
