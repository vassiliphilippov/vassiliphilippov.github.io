shellac

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈlæk / (AmE), / ʃəˈlæk / (BrE)
A resin obtained from refined lac, typically in thin flakes of orange or yellow color, used especially in varnishes and finishes.
a purified resin used in varnish and finish

Examples

The craftsman coated the table with several layers of shellac to achieve a glossy finish.
Traditional phonograph records were made from shellac before vinyl became popular.
She prefers to use shellac as an environmentally friendly wood finish.

Etymology

From shell + lac, indicating lac in thin plate (shell) form.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈlæk / (AmE), / ʃəˈlak / (BrE)
a thin varnish made by dissolving lac in alcohol, used to finish and protect wood surfaces
a type of wood varnish

Examples

He applied a coat of shellac to give the table a glossy finish.
The antique chair was restored with layers of shellac.
Shellac records, made of shellac, were popular before the advent of vinyl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - a liquid coating that dries to form a hard, protective finish

Etymology

From shell + lac, with lac from Persian lākh or Hindi lākh (the resinous substance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04198193-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ʃəˈlæk / (AmE), / ʃəˈlak / (BrE)
to cover or coat (something) with shellac, especially to give a protective finish or shine
to coat with shellac

Examples

After sanding the surface, she decided to shellac the table for a glossy finish.
The craftsman skillfully shellacked the cabinets to enhance their appearance and durability.
You should always shellac wooden furniture to protect it from moisture and damage.

Synset Examples

She wanted to shellac the desk to protect it from water spots

Etymology

From 'shellac' (the resin used as a varnish), which is from French 'laque en écailles' ('lac in thin pieces').
