fan

1. VERB

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
to blow something away or off using a current of air, typically by moving a fan or similar object
blow away with air

Examples

He used a piece of paper to fan the smoke away from his face.
The farmer fanned the grains to separate the chaff.
She fanned the dust off the old book with her hand.

Synset Examples

winnow chaff
The speaker ceased to be an amusing little gnat to be fanned away and was kicked off the forum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winnow - to blow a current of air through grain to remove the chaff

Etymology

From Old English fann (fan, winnowing device), from Latin vannus (winnowing fan).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00177994-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
a person who is very enthusiastic about and devoted to a particular person, group, team, or activity
enthusiastic admirer or supporter

Examples

I've been a huge fan of her music since I was a teenager.
Thousands of fans cheered as the team walked onto the field.
She's a big fan of science fiction movies and rarely misses a new release.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - a person who admires someone or something
  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a person, group, or cause
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or points out faults

Etymology

Shortened from 'fanatic', from Latin 'fanaticus' (inspired by a deity, frenzied).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10097373-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
A person who is very enthusiastic about and devoted to a particular sport, team, or entertainer.
enthusiastic supporter of sport or entertainer

Examples

She has been a loyal fan of the football team for over ten years.
Hundreds of fans waited outside the stadium to catch a glimpse of their favorite player.
The concert was sold out because so many fans wanted to see the band perform live.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a team or person
  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is highly interested in a subject or activity
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who competes against or disagrees with another

Etymology

Shortened from 'fanatic', first recorded in the late 19th century in this sense.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10659323-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
A device with blades or surfaces that rotates or moves to create a flow of air.
device for creating air flow

Examples

The ceiling fan kept the room cool on the hot summer day.
She bought a small desk fan for her office.
The old fan made a rattling noise but still worked well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilator - a device that circulates air, often mechanically

Etymology

From Latin 'vannus' (winnowing fan), via Old English 'fann'.

5. VERB

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
to make a feeling, especially a negative one such as anger or hatred, become stronger or more intense
intensify a feeling or emotion

Examples

The politician's speech only served to fan the flames of public outrage.
Tabloids often fan rumors to create more controversy.
His harsh words fanned the anger already simmering among the workers.

Synset Examples

fan hatred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflame - to provoke or intensify strong feelings, especially anger or violence
  • Antonym: calm - to make (someone or something) less angry or excited

Etymology

Metaphorically from the literal sense 'to increase the strength of a fire by blowing air on it.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
(In baseball) to strike out a batter by making them miss three pitches.
strike out a batter (baseball)
slang

Examples

The pitcher managed to fan twelve batters in last night's game.
He stepped up to the mound determined to fan the next hitter.
When she fanned the cleanup batter, the crowd erupted in applause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike out - (baseball) to be declared out after three strikes while batting

Etymology

From the motion of swinging a bat like a fan; baseball slang from early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

7. VERB

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
to move air with a fan or by waving something to create a current of air
move air by waving a fan or object

Examples

She used a magazine to fan herself in the heat.
The servant began to fan the embers to get the fire going.
He fanned his face with his hat, hoping to cool down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to waft - to cause something, such as air or a smell, to go gently through the air

Etymology

From Old English *fann* (a basket or fan for winnowing grain), ultimately from Latin *vannus* (winnowing fan).
