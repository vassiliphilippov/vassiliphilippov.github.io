Plumbaginaceae

1. NOUN

/ plʌmˌbæɡəˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / plʌmˌbaɪɡəˈniːsiː / (BrE)
a family of perennial herbs, shrubs, and lianas found worldwide, especially in saltwater areas; includes plants such as leadworts and sea lavenders
family of salt-tolerant flowering plants

Examples

Many species in the Plumbaginaceae family thrive in saline coastal environments.
Gardeners often cultivate members of the Plumbaginaceae for their attractive flowers and hardiness.
Ecologists study Plumbaginaceae to understand plant adaptations to saltwater habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Plumbago (genus name), from Latin plumbum (lead), referring to leadwort.

2. NOUN

/ plʌmˌbædʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / plʌmˌbadʒɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of perennial herbs, shrubs, and lianas, known as the leadwort family, found worldwide, especially in saline or coastal areas
family of leadwort herbs and shrubs

Examples

The Plumbaginaceae are often recognized by their simple leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers.
Many species within the Plumbaginaceae family thrive in salty coastal environments.
Gardeners value several Plumbaginaceae for their resilience and attractive blooms.

Etymology

From New Latin Plumbaginaceae, based on Plumbago (the type genus) and -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
