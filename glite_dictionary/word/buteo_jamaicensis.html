Buteo jamaicensis

1. NOUN

/ buːˌteɪ.oʊ dʒəˌmeɪˈɪkɛnsɪs / (AmE), / bjuːˌtiː.əʊ dʒəˌmeɪˈɪkənsɪs / (BrE)
A large North American hawk species with dark brown plumage and a characteristic reddish-brown tail, commonly known as the red-tailed hawk.
red-tailed hawk; large North American bird of prey

Examples

The Buteo jamaicensis is easily recognized by its broad wings and rust-colored tail.
Birdwatchers often spot Buteo jamaicensis soaring over open fields in search of prey.
Conservationists monitor Buteo jamaicensis populations to assess ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-tailed hawk - common English name for Buteo jamaicensis

Etymology

From Latin; 'Buteo' meaning 'buzzard' or 'hawk' and 'jamaicensis' indicating association with Jamaica (where it was first described).
