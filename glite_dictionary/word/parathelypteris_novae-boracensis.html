Parathelypteris novae-boracensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌθɛlɪpˈtɛrɪs ˌnoʊviˈbɔrəsɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˌθɛlɪpˈtɛrɪs ˌnəʊviːˈbɒrəsɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A slender shield fern found in moist woods of eastern North America, also known as the New York fern.
slender fern of eastern North America

Examples

The Parathelypteris novae-boracensis commonly grows in shaded, humid forests.
Gardeners appreciate the delicate foliage of Parathelypteris novae-boracensis for woodland gardens.
During our hike, we spotted several clusters of Parathelypteris novae-boracensis near the stream.

Etymology

From New Latin: Parathelypteris from Greek 'para-' (beside) and 'thelypteris' (fern); 'novae-boracensis' meaning 'of New York' in Latin.
