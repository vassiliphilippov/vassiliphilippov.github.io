canard

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɑrd / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a false or deliberately misleading story or report, especially one intended to deceive
deliberately misleading story or fabrication

Examples

The rumor about the new policy was just a canard spread by competitors.
She dismissed the accusations as a political canard with no basis in fact.
Many people believed the canard, but it was soon proven to be false.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabrication - a lie or made up story; something invented
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true; reality

Etymology

From French 'canard' (duck), later used figuratively in journalism for a false story.
