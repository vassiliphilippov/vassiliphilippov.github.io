Nuphar

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːfɑr / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfɑː / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants commonly known as spatterdocks, with large yellow flowers and floating leaves, found in temperate regions.
genus of aquatic plants (spatterdocks)

Examples

The pond was filled with graceful Nuphar leaves floating on the surface.
Scientists collected samples of Nuphar for their aquatic plant study.
Nuphar is often found growing in shallow lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nymphaea - genus of water lilies closely related to Nuphar

Etymology

From Greek nymphē (water-lily), through Latin 'nuphar.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.fɑr / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.fɑː / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants, commonly known as spatterdocks, with large, floating leaves and yellow flowers, found in temperate regions.
genus of aquatic plants (spatterdocks)

Examples

Several species within the Nuphar genus inhabit ponds and slow-moving rivers in North America.
The large, round leaves of Nuphar provide shelter for small aquatic animals.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of various Nuphar species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatterdock - an aquatic plant of the genus Nuphar

Etymology

From Greek nuphar, a water lily, used as a genus name in botanical Latin.
