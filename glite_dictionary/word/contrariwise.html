contrariwise

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑːn.trɛr.i.waɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.rɪ.waɪz / (BrE)
in the opposite way; with the order or meaning reversed
in the opposite or reversed way

Examples

His explanation was confusing; contrariwise, hers was clear and straightforward.
The news was shocking to some, but, contrariwise, met with relief by others.
He believed the solution was simple; contrariwise, it was actually quite complex.

Synset Examples

she hates him and vice versa

Etymology

From Middle English 'contrariwise', from 'contrary' + '-wise'.

2. ADV

/ kənˈtrɛr.i.waɪz / (AmE), / kənˈtrɑː.rɪ.waɪz / (BrE)
In the opposite way; on the contrary.
on the contrary; in the opposite way

Examples

She expected him to be angry; contrariwise, he was delighted by the news.
You might think this approach would fail, but, contrariwise, it proved quite effective.
Although the first experiment succeeded, the second, contrariwise, did not yield any useful results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversely - in an opposite or contrary way

Etymology

From Middle English 'contrarywise', from 'contrary' + 'wise' (manner).

3. ADV

/ ˈkɑːn.trɛr.i.waɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.rɪ.waɪz / (BrE)
In a manner or sense opposite to what has been stated or expected; on the contrary.
on the contrary; in an opposite way

Examples

Everyone expected the experiment to succeed; contrariwise, it failed spectacularly.
It was thought to be a curse, but contrariwise it brought unexpected blessings.
Most people hated the new policy; contrariwise, a few saw its benefits.

Synset Examples

he didn't stay home; on the contrary, he went out with his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversely - in an opposite manner, used to introduce a contrasting idea
  • Synonym: on the contrary - used to introduce a statement that means the opposite of the previous statement

Etymology

From Middle English, from Latin contrārius (opposite) + -wise.
