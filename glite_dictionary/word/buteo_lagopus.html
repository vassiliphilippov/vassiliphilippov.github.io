Buteo lagopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti.oʊ ˈlæɡəpəs / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪəʊ ˈlæɡəpəs / (BrE)
A large hawk of the Northern Hemisphere, also known as the rough-legged buzzard, that feeds mainly on small rodents and is beneficial to farmers.
species of large Northern Hemisphere hawk

Examples

The Buteo lagopus is often seen soaring over open fields during the winter months.
Farmers appreciate the presence of Buteo lagopus because it helps control rodent populations.
During migration, Buteo lagopus can be observed in large numbers in northern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Buteo' (a kind of hawk), 'lagopus' (hare-footed), referring to the feathered legs.
