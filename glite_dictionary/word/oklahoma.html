Oklahoma

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊkləˈhoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌəʊkləˈhəʊmə / (BrE)
A state in the south central region of the United States, known for its diverse landscape and Native American history.
U.S. state in south central region

Examples

I was born and raised in Oklahoma and have many fond memories of my childhood there.
The University of Oklahoma is well-known for its academic and athletic programs.
Every spring, tornadoes are a common occurrence in Oklahoma.

Etymology

From the Choctaw words 'okla' meaning 'people' and 'humma' meaning 'red', referring to 'Red People'.
