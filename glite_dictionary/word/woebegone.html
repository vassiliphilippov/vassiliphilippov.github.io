woebegone

1. ADJ

/ ˈwoʊ.bɪˌɡɔn / (AmE), / ˈwəʊ.bɪˌɡɒn / (BrE)
looking or feeling very sad or miserable; full of sorrow or woe.
looking very sad or miserable

Examples

The puppy sat in the corner, looking woebegone after being scolded for chewing the shoes.
After the bad news, her woebegone expression told everyone how she felt.
He wandered through the empty streets, a woebegone figure lost in his own sorrows.

Synset Examples

his sorrow … made him look … haggard and … woebegone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserable - feeling extremely unhappy or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Middle English wo begon (beset with woe), from woe (sorrow) + begon (beset, surrounded).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwoʊ.biˌɡɔn / (AmE), / ˈwəʊ.bɪˌɡɒn / (BrE)
looking very sad, miserable, or worn out; in a poor or broken condition due to hard use or distress
worn out or looking sad

Examples

The woebegone furniture in the abandoned house told the story of many hard years.
She wore a woebegone expression after hearing the disappointing news.
The woebegone old barn looked as if it might collapse in the next storm.

Synset Examples

a creaky shack
a decrepit bus … its seats held together with friction tape
a flea-bitten sofa
a run-down neighborhood
a woebegone old shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrepit - worn out or ruined by age or neglect
  • Synonym: run-down - in poor condition from neglect or overuse
  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin

Etymology

From Middle English 'wo begon', meaning 'beset or surrounded by woe'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
