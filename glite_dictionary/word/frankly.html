frankly

1. ADV

/ ˈfræŋkli / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkli / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that what you are saying is sincere, honest, or direct, even if it might offend or surprise someone.
in a sincere, direct manner

Examples

Frankly, I think the project is doomed from the start.
She spoke frankly about her disappointment with the results.
Frankly, I don't see why we should change the plan now.

Synset Examples

honestly, I don't believe it
candidly, I think she doesn't have a conscience
frankly, my dear, I don't give a damn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honestly - used to emphasize sincerity or truthfulness
  • Synonym: candidly - speaking in an open and honest way

Etymology

From Middle English *frankly*, from Old French *franc* (free, outspoken).
