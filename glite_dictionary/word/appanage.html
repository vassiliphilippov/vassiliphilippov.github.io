appanage

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈapənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a customary right, privilege, or possession associated with a particular rank or status, especially by tradition or inheritance
customary right or privilege

Examples

For centuries, hunting grounds were considered an appanage of the nobility.
Education was seen as an appanage of the upper class in that era.
A grand office became a necessary appanage for people of power and influence.

Synset Examples

for thousands of years the chair was an appanage of state and dignity rather than an article of ordinary use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage given to a person or group

Etymology

From French 'apanage', from Medieval Latin 'appanaticum', from 'appanare' (to provide with bread/means).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈapənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a grant of land, money, or other resources by a sovereign or legislative body to support a dependent member of a royal or noble family, especially in historical contexts
grant of land or resources to a royal dependent

Examples

During the Middle Ages, a younger prince might receive an appanage to govern as his domain.
The duchy served as an appanage for the king's second son.
Without an appanage, junior members of the royal family often struggled to maintain their lifestyle.

Synset Examples

bishoprics were received as appanages for the younger sons of great families

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - act of giving something, especially by a government or authority

Etymology

From French 'apanage', from Medieval Latin 'appanaticum', from 'appanare' (to provide with bread or sustenance).
