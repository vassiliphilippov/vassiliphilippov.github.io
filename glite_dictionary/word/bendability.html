bendability

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛndəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌbɛndəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be bent easily without breaking.
ability to be bent without breaking

Examples

The bendability of the metal makes it ideal for crafting intricate jewelry.
Engineers tested the bendability of the new alloy under extreme conditions.
Choosing a material with high bendability ensures longer durability in flexible applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking

Etymology

Formed from 'bend' + '-ability', modeled after patterns in English such as 'flexibility'.
