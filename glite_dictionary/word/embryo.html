embryo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.bri.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bri.əʊ / (BrE)
An animal or human organism in the early stages of development before birth or hatching, especially before the major organs and body systems have formed.
early-stage developing organism

Examples

The scientist studied the development of the embryo under a microscope.
At this stage, the embryo is just a cluster of cells.
Many animals have distinct embryo stages before they are born or hatched.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fetus - a later stage of development in mammals after the embryo stage

Etymology

From Greek *embryon* (fetus, young one), from *en* (in) + *bryein* (to swell).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.bri.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bri.əʊ / (BrE)
(Botany) A minute undeveloped plant contained within a seed or an archegonium, capable of growing into a mature plant.
undeveloped plant inside a seed (botany)

Examples

The embryo inside the seed begins to grow when optimal moisture and temperature conditions are reached.
A pine seed contains both stored food and an embryo ready to develop into a sapling.
Under a microscope, scientists examined the early stages of the plant embryo.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: seedling - a young plant that has recently germinated from a seed

Etymology

From Greek *embryon* (young one, fetus), from *en* (in) + *bryein* (to swell).
