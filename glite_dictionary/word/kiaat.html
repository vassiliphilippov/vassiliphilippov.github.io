kiaat

1. NOUN

/ kiˈɑt / (AmE), / kiˈɑːt / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to southern Africa, known for its large pinnate leaves, fragrant orange-yellow flowers, and durable timber.
South African deciduous tree with durable wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from kiaat, prized for its strength and attractive grain.
During spring, the kiaat produces a display of bright orange-yellow flowers.
Many local communities value kiaat trees for both shade and high-quality timber.

Etymology

From Afrikaans, ultimately from Khoikhoi origin.
