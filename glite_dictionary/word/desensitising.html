desensitising

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnsɪtaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something less sensitive to physical or emotional stimuli, especially by repeated exposure.
making less sensitive to stimuli
british

Examples

Repeated exposure to violent media can have a desensitising effect on viewers.
After years of working in the emergency room, he found the constant trauma oddly desensitising.
Some therapies are aimed at desensitising patients to their specific fears.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensitising - making more sensitive to stimuli
  • Synonym: desensitizing - making less sensitive to stimuli (American spelling)

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + sensitise (make sensitive).
