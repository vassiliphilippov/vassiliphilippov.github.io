Umbrina roncador

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˈbriːnə rɑn.kə.dɔr / (AmE), / ʌmˈbriːnə rɒŋ.kə.dɔː / (BrE)
a species of fish found along the Pacific coast of North America, commonly known as the roncador croaker.
a Pacific coast fish species

Examples

The Umbrina roncador is often found in shallow waters along the California coast.
Fishermen prize the Umbrina roncador for its delicate flavor and fight when hooked.
Researchers studied the spawning patterns of the Umbrina roncador to better manage its population.

Etymology

From Latin 'Umbrina' (a fish genus), and Spanish 'roncador' (snorer), referring to the sound the fish makes.
