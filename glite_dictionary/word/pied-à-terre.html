pied-à-terre

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjɛd ɑː ˈtɛr / (AmE), / ˌpiː.eɪ dæ ˈtɛə / (BrE)
a small flat or house kept for occasional or secondary use, typically in a city away from the main home
secondary residence for occasional use

Examples

After retiring, they bought a pied-à-terre in Paris to visit during the summers.
She keeps a pied-à-terre in the city for business trips.
Their pied-à-terre is just a short walk from the theatre district.

Synset Examples

they bought a pied-a-terre in London

Etymology

From French, literally 'foot on the ground'.
