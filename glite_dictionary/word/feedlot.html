feedlot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfid.lɑt / (AmE), / ˈfiːd.lɒt / (BrE)
a confined area or facility where livestock, such as cattle or pigs, are fattened before being sent to market
area where livestock are fattened for market

Examples

The cattle spent several months in the feedlot before they were shipped to the slaughterhouse.
New regulations require better waste management at every feedlot in the state.
After being raised on pasture, the animals are moved to a feedlot for finishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stockyard - an area or facility where livestock are temporarily kept

Etymology

From 'feed' + 'lot', referring to an area (lot) where animals are fed intensively.
