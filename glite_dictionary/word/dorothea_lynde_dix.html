Dorothea Lynde Dix

1. PROPN

/ dɔːrˈoʊθiə lɪnd dɪks / (AmE), / dɒrˈəʊθɪə lɪnd dɪks / (BrE)
(1802–1887) A United States social reformer known for her pioneering work in the reform of prisons and the treatment of the mentally ill, and for serving as superintendent of women army nurses during the American Civil War.
U.S. social reformer for prisons and mental health

Examples

Dorothea Lynde Dix campaigned tirelessly for better conditions in mental health institutions across the United States.
Many historians credit Dorothea Lynde Dix with transforming the approach to mental illness in the 19th century.
As superintendent of nurses, Dorothea Lynde Dix played a critical role during the American Civil War.
