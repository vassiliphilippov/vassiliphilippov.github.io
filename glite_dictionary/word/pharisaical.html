pharisaical

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɛrɪˈseɪɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfarɪˈseɪɪkəl / (BrE)
Excessively or hypocritically pious or self-righteous.
excessively or hypocritically pious

Examples

The principal adopted a pharisaical tone when criticizing the students' behavior.
Many were put off by his pharisaical insistence on following every rule to the letter.
Her pharisaical remarks about charity exposed her own hypocrisy.

Synset Examples

a sickening sanctimonious smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior to others
  • Degree Lower: pious - devoutly religious
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine, honest in feeling

Etymology

From Pharisee, referring to a member of an ancient Jewish sect known for strict observance of religious law, plus the suffix -al.
