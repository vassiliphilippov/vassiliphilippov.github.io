flypaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
Paper coated with a sticky or poisonous substance, used to trap and kill flies.
sticky paper for trapping flies

Examples

The old bakery had strips of flypaper hanging from the ceiling to catch insects.
She replaced the used flypaper in the kitchen every week during summer.
A few flies were stuck to the flypaper near the window.
