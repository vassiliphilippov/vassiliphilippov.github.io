extraction

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
the action of removing something, especially by applying effort or force
the act of removing something by force

Examples

The dentist scheduled her extraction for Friday morning.
Oil extraction from deep underground requires advanced technology and equipment.
The extraction of the artifact from the ruins took several hours of careful work.

Synset Examples

the dentist gave her a local anesthetic prior to the extraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or off
  • Antonym: insertion - the action of putting something into something else

Etymology

From Latin extractio, from extrahere (to draw out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræk.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of obtaining a substance from a mixture or compound by chemical, physical, or mechanical means
process of obtaining a substance from a mixture

Examples

The extraction of caffeine from coffee beans is a common industrial process.
Oil extraction techniques have improved significantly over the last decade.
The laboratory focused on the extraction of DNA from blood samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the process of separating substances

Etymology

From Latin extractio (a drawing out), from extrahere (to draw out).
