lxvii

1. NUM

/ ɛl ɛks vi aɪ aɪ / (AmE), / ɛl ɛks vɪ aɪ aɪ / (BrE)
The Roman numeral for 67.
Roman numeral for 67

Examples

The inscription was dated in the year LXVII of the reign.
Each chapter was numbered with Roman numerals, so chapter 67 appeared as LXVII.
The ancient manuscript ends at page LXVII, which corresponds to page 67 in modern editions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 67 - the Arabic numeral for sixty-seven

Etymology

From Latin numerals: L (50) + X (10) + V (5) + II (2).
