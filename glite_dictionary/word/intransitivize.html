to intransitivize

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrænzətɪˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænzɪtɪvaɪz / (BrE)
(of a verb) to make or become intransitive; to change a verb so that it does not take a direct object.
make or become intransitive (of verbs)

Examples

The grammarian explained how dropping the direct object would intransitivize certain verbs.
It is common in some languages to intransitivize verbs through specific morphological changes.
Removing the reflexive pronoun can sometimes intransitivize the verb in certain contexts.

Synset Examples

removing the object will intransitivize the verbs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to transitivize - to make or become transitive (of verbs)

Etymology

From intransitive (not taking a direct object) + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
