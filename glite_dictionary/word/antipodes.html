antipodes

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪˌpoʊdz / (AmE), / ænˈtɪp.əˌdiːz / (BrE)
any two places or regions on opposite sides of the Earth, especially with reference to their relationship as being diametrically opposed.
two places on opposite sides of the Earth

Examples

Some ancient maps marked the antipodes as mysterious, unexplored lands.
If you dig a hole straight through the earth, you'll end up in the antipodes of your starting point.
Australia and England were often called each other's antipodes in 19th-century literature.

Synset Examples

the North Pole and the South Pole are antipodes

Etymology

From Greek antipodes (opposite feet), via Late Latin.
