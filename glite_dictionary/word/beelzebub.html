Beelzebub

1. PROPN

/ biˈɛlzəˌbʌb / (AmE), / biːˈɛlzɪbʌb / (BrE)
(in Judeo-Christian and Islamic tradition) the chief demon; a powerful evil spirit considered adversary of God and master of Hell.
chief demon; adversary of God

Examples

The villagers believed Beelzebub was behind the strange happenings at night.
In medieval literature, Beelzebub is often portrayed as the prince of demons.
He warned his followers about the temptations of Beelzebub and the importance of faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Satan - the chief evil spirit; adversary of God in Christian belief
  • Synonym: Lucifer - another name for the devil; the chief fallen angel
  • Antonym: God - the supreme being; creator and ruler of the universe

Etymology

From Hebrew *Ba‘al Zəvūv* (lord of the flies), via Greek and Latin.
