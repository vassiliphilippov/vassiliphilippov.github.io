unenlightened

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪnˈlaɪ.tn̩d / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪnˈlaɪ.tn̩d / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, understanding, or awareness about a particular subject or in general; uninformed or ignorant.
lacking knowledge or awareness

Examples

Many people in the region remain unenlightened about basic health practices.
His unenlightened remarks revealed a lack of understanding of the issue.
It is our duty to educate the unenlightened members of the community.

Synset Examples

lamentably unenlightened as to the laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: informed - having or showing knowledge of a particular subject

Etymology

From un- (not) + enlightened, from enlighten (make aware or informed).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪnˈlaɪ.tənd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪnˈlaɪ.tənd / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, understanding, or awareness; ignorant or not informed about modern ideas or values.
lacking knowledge or awareness; ignorant

Examples

The villagers remained unenlightened about the new health practices promoted by the government.
Some unenlightened attitudes persist in even the most advanced societies.
He made several unenlightened remarks during the discussion, revealing his lack of understanding.

Synset Examples

the devices by which unenlightened men preserved the unjust social order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: enlightened - having or showing a rational, modern, and well-informed outlook

Etymology

From un- (not) + enlightened, from enlighten (to give knowledge or spiritual insight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00887528-a
