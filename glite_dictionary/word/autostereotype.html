autostereotype

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈstɛrɪəˌtʌɪp / (BrE)
a standardized and oversimplified mental image that members of a cultural group hold about themselves and their own behavior
a group's stereotyped self-image

Examples

Members of the community often reinforce their autostereotype through stories and traditions.
The study explored how a positive autostereotype can influence collective self-esteem.
She challenged the negative autostereotype by highlighting the group's diversity and achievements.

Synset Examples

Just as the Malays made ingroup-favoring attributions, so their autostereotype is very positive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereotype - a widely held but oversimplified idea of a particular type of person or thing
  • Antonym: heterostereotype - a standardized mental image about another group, held by outsiders

Etymology

From auto- (self) + stereotype (from French stéréotype, from Greek stereos ‘solid’ + typos ‘impression’).
