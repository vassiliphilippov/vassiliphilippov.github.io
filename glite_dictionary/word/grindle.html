grindle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪn.dəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪn.dəl / (BrE)
A primitive, long-bodied carnivorous freshwater fish of North America, with a very long dorsal fin, typically found in sluggish waters.
North American carnivorous freshwater fish

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed the large grindle he caught in the bayou.
In certain rivers of the Midwest, you can often find a grindle lurking near the reeds.
Although not often eaten, the grindle is recognized by its long dorsal fin and predatory habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowfin - A primitive freshwater fish found in North America, also known as grindle.
