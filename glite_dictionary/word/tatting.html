tatting

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæt̬.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæt.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the art or activity of making lace by hand with a shuttle or needle and thread, creating intricate patterns of knots and loops
the craft of making lace by hand

Examples

She spent her evenings doing tatting to create delicate lace for the wedding dress.
A display of antique linens featured beautiful examples of handmade tatting.
Learning tatting requires patience and skill, but the results can be stunningly intricate.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtædɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtatɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of needlework for making lace by looping and knotting a single thread with a small shuttle or needle.
handmade lace needlework

Examples

She learned tatting from her grandmother, creating intricate lace for handkerchiefs.
Vintage shawls often include beautiful bands of tatting along their edges.
In the craft fair, a vendor demonstrated the art of tatting to a curious audience.

Etymology

From the verb 'tat' (to make lace by hand), origin uncertain, first recorded in the mid-19th century.
