unveiled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈveɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪld / (BrE)
made known or revealed, especially by the removal of a veil or cover; not hidden or secret.
revealed or made known

Examples

The museum proudly displayed the unveiled paintings for the first time.
After years of secrecy, the company's new product was finally unveiled to the public.
She felt free among the unveiled women, embracing a new sense of identity.

Synset Examples

a new generation of unveiled women in Iran
applauding the unveiled statue of Winston Churchill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veiled - covered with a veil; concealed or disguised

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'veiled' (covered with or as if with a veil).
