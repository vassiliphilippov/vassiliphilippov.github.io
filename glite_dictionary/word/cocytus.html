Cocytus

1. PROPN

/ koʊˈsaɪtəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈsiːtəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a river in Hades believed to be a tributary of the Acheron, associated with lamentation and wailing.
river of lamentation in Hades (Greek myth)

Examples

In Dante's Inferno, Cocytus is depicted as a frozen lake in the ninth circle of Hell.
The souls of the dead were said to wander along the banks of Cocytus in eternal mourning.
Many ancient poets describe Cocytus as the river of wailing and lamentation in the underworld.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acheron - another river of Hades in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κωκυτός (Kōkytós), meaning 'the river of wailing or lamentation'.
