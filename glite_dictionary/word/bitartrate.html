bitartrate

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈtɑrˌtreɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈtɑːˌtreɪt / (BrE)
an acid salt of tartaric acid containing one replaceable hydrogen atom; known as hydrogen tartrate.
an acid salt of tartaric acid
medical

Examples

The medication contains potassium bitartrate as an active ingredient.
Cream of tartar is chemically known as potassium hydrogen bitartrate.
Researchers studied the solubility of sodium bitartrate in water at different temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tartrate - a salt or ester of tartaric acid
  • Degree Higher: tartaric acid - a white crystalline organic acid

Etymology

From 'bi-' (two) and 'tartrate', relating to tartaric acid.
