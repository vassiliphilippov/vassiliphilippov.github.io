agile

1. ADJ

/ ˈædʒaɪl / (AmE), / ˈædʒaɪl / (BrE)
able to move quickly and easily; nimble or light-footed.
able to move quickly and easily

Examples

The gymnast was remarkably agile on the balance beam.
Thanks to her agile movements, she dodged the falling branches with ease.
At 80 years old, her grandfather is still surprisingly agile.

Synset Examples

sleek and agile as a gymnast
as nimble as a deer
nimble fingers
quick of foot
the old dog was so spry it was halfway up the stairs before we could stop it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nimble - quick and light in movement or action
  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things

Etymology

From Latin 'agilis', from 'agere' (to do, to act).

2. ADJ

/ ˈædʒaɪl, ˈædʒəl / (AmE), / ˈædʒaɪl, ˈædʒɪl / (BrE)
able to think and understand quickly and easily; mentally quick and resourceful.
mentally quick and resourceful

Examples

She is known for her agile mind and creative problem solving.
The debate champion's agile thinking impressed the judges.
His agile responses showed he was always one step ahead of the conversation.

Synset Examples

an agile mind
nimble wits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nimble - quick and light in movement or action; also mentally quick

Etymology

From Latin 'agilis' (nimble, quick), from 'agere' (to do, act).
