hemorrhoidectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛm(ə)ˌrɔɪˈdɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌhɛm(ə)rɔɪˈdɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove hemorrhoids by excising or tying them off
surgical removal of hemorrhoids
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a hemorrhoidectomy to treat the patient's severe discomfort.
After her hemorrhoidectomy, she needed a few days of rest before returning to work.
A hemorrhoidectomy is sometimes necessary when less invasive treatments fail.

Etymology

From 'hemorrhoid' + Greek 'ektomē' (excision, cutting out).
