D. W. Griffith

1. PROPN

/ di ˈdʌbəl.ju ˈɡrɪfɪθ / (AmE), / di ˈdʌbəl.juː ˈɡrɪfɪθ / (BrE)
(1875–1948) An American film director and producer, known for pioneering cinematic techniques such as flashbacks and fade-outs.
pioneering American film director

Examples

D. W. Griffith is regarded as one of the most influential figures in early film history.
The silent film "The Birth of a Nation" was directed by D. W. Griffith in 1915.
Cinematic techniques like the flashback were popularized by D. W. Griffith's groundbreaking movies.
