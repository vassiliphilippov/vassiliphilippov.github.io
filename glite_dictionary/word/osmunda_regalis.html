Osmunda regalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑzˈmʊn.də rɪˈɡeɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ɒzˈmʌn.də rɪˈɡeɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a large fern with upright, bipinnate fronds, widely distributed across the world, also known as the royal fern.
a large, widespread fern species (royal fern)

Examples

The lush banks of the river were covered in Osmunda regalis, lending a majestic appearance to the landscape.
Gardeners prize Osmunda regalis for its decorative, feathery fronds and resilience in damp conditions.
In wet woodlands, you can often spot Osmunda regalis towering over other ferns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal fern - common name for Osmunda regalis

Etymology

From Latin, where Osmunda is the genus name and regalis means 'royal'.
