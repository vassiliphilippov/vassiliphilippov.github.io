Pipra

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪprə / (AmE), / ˈpɪprə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American birds in the family Pipridae, containing the typical manakins.
genus of manakins (birds)

Examples

The genus Pipra includes several strikingly colorful manakin species native to South America.
Over time, some species were reclassified and removed from the original Pipra grouping.
Researchers study Pipra to understand the evolution of courtship displays in tropical birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manakin - a small, brightly colored bird of the family Pipridae

Etymology

From New Latin Pipra, a genus name created by Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪprə / (AmE), / ˈpɪprə / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Pipridae, comprising the typical manakins of Central and South America
genus of typical manakins (birds)

Examples

The Pipra genus includes several species of colorful manakins found in South American forests.
Researchers studied the mating behavior of birds within the Pipra genus.
The male Pipra birds are known for their elaborate courtship displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manakin - small, vibrantly colored bird of the family Pipridae

Etymology

From New Latin, used in Linnaean taxonomy; possibly from Greek 'pipra', a small bird mentioned by Aristotle.
