Pythoninae

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈθɑnɪniː / (AmE), / pɪˈθəʊnɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of nonvenomous Old World snakes known as pythons, sometimes considered a separate family from the Boidae.
a subfamily of Old World pythons

Examples

Scientists studying Southeast Asian reptiles often focus on members of the Pythoninae subfamily.
Taxonomists debate whether Pythoninae should be a subfamily or elevated to full family status.
The Pythoninae includes some of the largest snakes found in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: python - a large nonvenomous snake of the genus Python
  • Degree Higher: boid - a member of the family Boidae, including boas and pythons

Etymology

From New Latin, rooted in 'Python' (the type genus) + '-inae' (a zoological suffix for subfamilies).

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈθɑnɪni / (AmE), / paɪˈθəʊnɪni / (BrE)
A subfamily of nonvenomous snakes known as pythons, native to the Old World, sometimes classified as a separate family from Boidae.
subfamily of Old World pythons (snakes)

Examples

The Pythoninae subfamily includes some of the largest snakes in the world.
Researchers studied various species within the Pythoninae to understand their evolution.
Unlike boas, members of the Pythoninae lay eggs rather than giving birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Boidae - the family of snakes including boas and pythons

Etymology

From Latinized form combining 'Python' (the type genus) + -inae (a zoological suffix for subfamilies).
