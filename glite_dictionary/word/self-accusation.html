self-accusation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ˌækjəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ˌækjʊˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of accusing oneself or admitting one's own shortcomings or failures
the act of blaming oneself

Examples

Her constant self-accusation made it difficult for her to move past her mistakes.
In his memoir, he described nights filled with self-accusation and regret.
The therapist encouraged him to replace self-accusation with self-compassion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: self-absolution - the act of forgiving oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'accusation', from Latin 'accusatio' (an accusation).
