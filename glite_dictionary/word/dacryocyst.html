dacryocyst

1. NOUN

/ ˈdækri.oʊˌsɪst / (AmE), / ˈdakrɪəˌsɪst / (BrE)
the dilated end of the lacrimal duct at the nasal corner of the eye that collects tears from the lacrimal glands
dilated end of the lacrimal (tear) duct
medical

Examples

The surgeon operated on the dacryocyst to relieve the patient's chronic tear duct blockage.
Inflammation of the dacryocyst can result in pain and excessive tearing.
During examination, the doctor found swelling near the patient's left dacryocyst.

Etymology

From Greek dakryon (tear) + kystis (sac).
