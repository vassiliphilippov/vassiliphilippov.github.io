Eupatorium cannabinum

1. NOUN

/ juːˌpeɪtɔːriəm kəˈnæbɪnəm / (AmE), / juːˌpatɔːriːəm kanəˈbɪnəm / (BrE)
a coarse European perennial herb with palmately divided leaves and clusters of small reddish-purple flower heads; commonly known as hemp agrimony
a European herb with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with Eupatorium cannabinum thriving in the damp soil near the pond.
Botanists often identify Eupatorium cannabinum by its distinctively divided leaves and purple flower heads.
Eupatorium cannabinum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental flowers and wildlife value.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'eupatorion', after Mithridates Eupator; species name from Latin 'cannabinum' (hemp-like).
