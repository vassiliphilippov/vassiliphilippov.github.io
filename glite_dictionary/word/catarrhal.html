catarrhal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈtɑrəl / (AmE), / kəˈtarəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by catarrh, especially involving inflammation of mucous membranes with increased mucus secretion
relating to catarrh; mucous inflammation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a catarrhal inflammation of the nasal passages.
Veterinarians reported several cases of catarrhal fever among the sheep on the farm.
A catarrhal cough often produces an excess of mucus in the throat.

Synset Examples

catarrhal fever is any of several respiratory or oral diseases of livestock such as bluetongue in horses and sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catarrh - inflammation of mucous membranes with excessive mucus

Etymology

From Greek 'katarrhoos' (flowing down), via Late Latin 'catarrhalis'.
