hall

1. NOUN

/ hɔl / (AmE), / hɔːl / (BrE)
A large building used by a college or university for teaching or research purposes.
large university or college building for teaching/research

Examples

Every morning, students gathered in the main hall for lectures.
The new science hall houses several state-of-the-art laboratories.
Medical students often spend long hours in the anatomy hall working on research projects.

Synset Examples

halls of learning

Etymology

From Old English 'heall' (large house, building), related to Old Norse 'hǫll', German 'Halle'.
