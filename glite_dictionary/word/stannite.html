stannite

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstænaɪt / (BrE)
a dark gray mineral with a metallic luster, composed of tin, copper, and iron sulfide, and important as an ore of tin
a mineral and tin ore

Examples

Geologists discovered a large deposit of stannite in the mountainous region.
The extraction of tin from stannite requires careful separation from other minerals.
Samples of stannite are often identified by their distinctive metallic luster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassiterite - another mineral that is a major source of tin

Etymology

From Latin 'stannum' (tin), with the mineral suffix '-ite'.
