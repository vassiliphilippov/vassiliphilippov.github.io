mutafacient

1. ADJ

/ ˌmjuːtəˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtəˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
capable of causing or inducing genetic mutation (especially of intracellular agents)
able to cause genetic mutation
medical

Examples

Some chemicals present in cigarette smoke are considered highly mutafacient to human lung cells.
Researchers tested several compounds to determine their mutafacient effects in bacteria cultures.
This virus acts as a mutafacient agent within the host's genome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutagenic - causing genetic mutation

Etymology

From Latin 'mutare' (to change) and English '-facient' (making, producing).
