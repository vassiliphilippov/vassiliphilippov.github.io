unaerated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɛrˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈeərˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid, especially blood) not supplied or mixed with oxygen; lacking aeration.
not supplied with oxygen (e.g., blood)

Examples

Venous blood is typically unaerated as it returns to the heart.
The unaerated condition of the water made it unsuitable for fish.
Doctors observed that the tissues were damaged by prolonged exposure to unaerated blood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aerated - (of a liquid, etc.) supplied or mixed with oxygen or air

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + aerated (provided with air or oxygen), from Latin 'aer' (air).
