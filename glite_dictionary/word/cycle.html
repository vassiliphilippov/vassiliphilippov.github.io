cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a series of events that are regularly repeated in the same order, forming a complete sequence
a regularly recurring sequence of events

Examples

A lunar cycle lasts about 29.5 days from new moon to new moon.
The water cycle describes how water moves through the environment again and again.
Each cycle of the washing machine takes approximately 40 minutes.

Synset Examples

a year constitutes a cycle of the seasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a set of related events or things that follow each other in a particular order

Etymology

From Greek kyklos (circle, wheel).

7. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)

Examples

Electrical equipment is rated by how many cycles per second it can safely handle.
In alternating current, a cycle represents a full sequence from zero to peak, back to zero, and to negative peak.
