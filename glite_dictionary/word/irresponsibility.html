irresponsibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪr.ɪˌspɑn.səˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˌspɒn.səˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking a sense of responsibility and not being accountable for one's actions.
lack of responsibility; unaccountability

Examples

His irresponsibility cost the company thousands of dollars in lost revenue.
She was criticized for her irresponsibility in handling the project deadlines.
Repeated acts of irresponsibility can damage your reputation at work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: responsibility - The state or fact of being accountable or to blame for something
  • Synonym: accountability - The fact or condition of being accountable; responsibility

Etymology

From prefix 'ir-' (not) + 'responsibility', patterned after 'irresponsible'.
