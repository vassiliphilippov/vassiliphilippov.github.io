Peronosporaceae

1. NOUN

/ pɛrəˌnɒspoʊˈreɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / pɛrəˌnɒspɒˈreɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic fungi commonly known as downy mildews, which infect plants, causing various diseases.
family of parasitic fungi (downy mildews)
medical

Examples

The Peronosporaceae family is responsible for several devastating plant diseases in agriculture.
Researchers studied the genetic structure of Peronosporaceae to better understand downy mildew infections.
Preventing the spread of Peronosporaceae is crucial for maintaining healthy crops.

Etymology

From the genus name Peronospora, itself from Greek 'peronē' (pin, brooch) and 'spora' (seed).
