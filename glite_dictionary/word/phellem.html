phellem

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛlɛm / (AmE), / ˈfɛlɛm / (BrE)
(Botany) The outer tissue of a plant's bark, composed of dead cells, forming a protective layer.
outer protective bark tissue (botany)

Examples

The phellem acts as a barrier protecting the tree from pathogens and water loss.
Botanists studied the structure of phellem under a microscope to understand bark formation.
In some species, the phellem layer becomes particularly thick to improve resistance to fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cork - outer bark tissue obtained from certain trees

Etymology

From Greek *phellos* (cork).
