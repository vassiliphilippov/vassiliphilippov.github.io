egg

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡ / (AmE), / ɛɡ / (BrE)
An oval or round object produced by female birds, reptiles, fish, and some other animals, containing the developing embryo, usually with a protective shell or covering.
animal reproductive body, especially bird egg

Examples

The hen laid a fresh egg in the nest every morning.
She cracked an egg into the bowl to make an omelette.
The scientist carefully observed the development of the chick inside the egg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ovum - a single female reproductive cell in animals

Etymology

From Old Norse 'egg', related to Old English 'æg'.
