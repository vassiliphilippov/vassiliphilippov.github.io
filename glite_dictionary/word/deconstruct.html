to deconstruct

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to analyze or interpret a text, artwork, etc., by exposing its underlying assumptions and contradictions, often using the method of deconstruction
analyze a text using deconstruction

Examples

Literary theorists often deconstruct novels to reveal hidden assumptions in the narrative.
The painting was deconstructed by critics to expose its political symbolism.
She spent years deconstructing the cultural biases present in traditional fairy tales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine in detail for purposes of explanation and interpretation

Etymology

Based on 'deconstruction', from French 'déconstruire' (to deconstruct).

2. VERB

/ diː.kənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / diː.kənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to analyze something by breaking it down into its components in order to better understand its meaning or structure, especially in literary or cultural works.
analyze by breaking down

Examples

The professor encouraged us to deconstruct the novel's themes during class discussion.
Many critics attempt to deconstruct popular songs to uncover hidden messages within the lyrics.
She enjoys deconstructing movies to understand how their plots are structured.

Synset Examples

He likes to deconstruct popular songs to analyze their lyrics.
In class, we will deconstruct the poem's meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine something methodically for explanation or interpretation
  • Antonym: construct - to build something by putting parts together

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'de-' and 'construct'; influenced by French 'déconstruire', from the theories of philosopher Jacques Derrida.
