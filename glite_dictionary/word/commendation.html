commendation

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
an official or formal statement of praise or approval
expression of praise or approval

Examples

She received a commendation from the mayor for her outstanding volunteer work.
His letter of commendation helped me get the job.
The soldier was awarded a commendation for bravery during the rescue mission.

Synset Examples

words of approval seldom passed his lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • Synonym: approval - officially favorable opinion or judgment
  • Antonym: criticism - expression of disapproval or unfavorable opinion

Etymology

From Latin commendatio (recommendation, praise), from commendare (to entrust, praise).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.mənˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.mənˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an official award or statement given publicly to praise someone, especially for bravery or service
official public award or praise

Examples

She received a commendation for her courageous actions during the rescue.
The police officer was given an official commendation after saving a child's life.
Employees who show outstanding service may be awarded a commendation by the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - something given as a prize or honor for achievement
  • Antonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism

Etymology

From Latin commendatio (recommendation, praise), from commendare (to commit to, to praise).
