to investigate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛstəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to carry out a systematic or scientific inquiry, especially to discover facts or information
conduct a scientific or formal inquiry

Examples

The researchers will investigate how sleep affects memory retention in adults.
Scientists continue to investigate the causes of climate change in greater depth.
Linguists often investigate the structure and function of different languages.

Synset Examples

Let's investigate the syntax of Chinese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or study carefully
  • Synonym: study - to investigate or examine scientifically or academically

Etymology

From Latin investigare, from in- ‘into’ + vestigare ‘track, trace’.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛstɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to examine a matter carefully in order to discover the truth about it, especially as part of an official process
examine carefully to find out facts

Examples

The police began to investigate the cause of the accident after interviewing the witnesses.
Reporters investigated allegations of corruption within the city council.
Scientists are investigating how climate change is affecting local wildlife.

Synset Examples

The district attorney's office investigated reports of possible irregularities
inquire into the disappearance of the rich old lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to look at or consider carefully
  • Synonym: to inquire - to seek information by asking questions
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin investigare, from in- (in) + vestigare (to track).
