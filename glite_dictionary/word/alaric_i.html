Alaric I

1. PROPN

/ ˈælərɪk ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈalərɪk ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 370–410) King of the Visigoths, best known for capturing and sacking Rome in 410 AD.
Visigoth king who sacked Rome

Examples

Alaric I is remembered as the leader who led the Visigoths during the sack of Rome in 410.
Many historians cite Alaric I as a turning point in the decline of the Western Roman Empire.
The legacy of Alaric I influenced European history for centuries.

Etymology

From the Gothic name Alareiks, meaning 'ruler of all'.
