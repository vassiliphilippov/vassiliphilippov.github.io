roc

1. NOUN

/ rɑk / (AmE), / rɒk / (BrE)
a mythical bird of prey of enormous size and strength, often featured in Middle Eastern legends and tales
mythical giant bird of prey

Examples

The tales of Sindbad describe encounters with the legendary roc, whose wings blocked out the sun.
In ancient stories, the roc was said to carry off elephants in its talons.
Some scholars believe early sailors mistook giant birds for the mythical roc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gryphon - mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head and wings of an eagle

Etymology

From Arabic 'ruḵḵ', popularized in European languages via tales such as Sinbad the Sailor.
