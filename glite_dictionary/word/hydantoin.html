hydantoin

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdæntɔɪn / (AmE), / hɪˈdantɔɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of anticonvulsant drugs used to treat epilepsy and related conditions
type of anticonvulsant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hydantoin to help control her epilepsy.
Long-term use of hydantoin may cause certain side effects that require monitoring.
Some patients respond better to hydantoin than to other anticonvulsant medications.

Etymology

From the chemical name of its core structure, combining 'hydra-' (water) and 'antoin', referring to its chemical relation to allantoin.
