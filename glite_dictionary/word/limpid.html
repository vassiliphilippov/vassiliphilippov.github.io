limpid

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪmpɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid, eyes, or atmosphere) completely clear and bright; easily seen through.
completely clear and bright

Examples

The mountain lake was so limpid that you could see the fish swimming at the bottom.
Her limpid blue eyes seemed to sparkle in the sunlight.
After the rain, the air felt especially limpid and fresh.

Synset Examples

the liquid air of a spring morning
eyes shining with a liquid luster
limpid blue eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - easy to see through; not cloudy or opaque
  • Antonym: murky - dark or cloudy; not clear

Etymology

From Latin limpĭdus (clear, transparent).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪmpɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or atmosphere) completely clear and transparent; easily seen through with clarity.
clear and transparent (liquid or air)

Examples

The limpid waters of the lake reflected the mountains perfectly.
Her eyes were as limpid as crystal, revealing every emotion.
We enjoyed swimming in the limpid pool, where every stone was visible on the bottom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pellucid - very clear; transparent
  • Antonym: murky - dark and difficult to see through

Etymology

From Latin limpidus (clear, transparent).

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɪmpɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpɪd / (BrE)
(of language) clear and easy to understand; transparent in meaning or expression.
clear and easily understood (language)

Examples

Her limpid prose captivated readers with its simplicity and grace.
The instructions were written in such limpid language that everyone could follow them easily.
He prefers authors whose style is limpid, rather than dense and confusing.

Synset Examples

writes in a limpid style
lucid directions
a luculent oration
pellucid prose
a crystal clear explanation
a perspicuous argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucid - (of language) expressed clearly; easy to understand
  • Synonym: pellucid - clear and easy to understand (language or style)
  • Antonym: obscure - not clearly expressed or easily understood

Etymology

From Latin 'limpidus' meaning 'clear, transparent'.
