Meles

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːliːz / (AmE), / ˈmiːliːz / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian badgers in the family Mustelidae, chiefly represented by the European badger.
genus of Eurasian badgers

Examples

The European badger, scientifically known as Meles meles, is widespread across Europe.
Researchers have noted behavioral differences among Meles species in different habitats.
Several fossils have been attributed to extinct members of the genus Meles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a burrowing nocturnal mammal of the family Mustelidae

Etymology

From Latin *meles* (badger).
