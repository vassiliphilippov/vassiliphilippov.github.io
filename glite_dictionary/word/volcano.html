volcano

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈkeɪnoʊ / (AmE), / vɒlˈkeɪnəʊ / (BrE)
A mountain or hill with a crater or vent through which lava, rock fragments, hot vapor, and gas are or have been erupted from the earth's crust.
mountain through which lava erupts

Examples

The eruption of the volcano forced nearby residents to evacuate their homes.
Mount Fuji is a famous volcano in Japan.
Scientists monitor the volcano for signs of increased volcanic activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain - large natural elevation of the earth's surface

Etymology

From Italian 'vulcano', from Latin 'Vulcanus', the Roman god of fire.

2. NOUN

/ voʊlˈkeɪnoʊ / (AmE), / vɒlˈkeɪnəʊ / (BrE)
An opening in the earth's crust through which molten lava, ash, and gases are ejected, often forming a mountain.
opening in earth emitting lava and gases

Examples

Mount Fuji is an active volcano that last erupted in the 18th century.
The island was created by an underwater volcano millions of years ago.
Lava poured down the sides of the volcano during the eruption.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mountain - large natural elevation of the earth's surface
  • Synonym: crater - bowl-shaped depression at the top of a volcano

Etymology

From Italian 'vulcano', from Latin 'Vulcanus', the Roman god of fire.
