Saint Andrew the Apostle

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈændru ði əˈpɑstl̩ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈændruː ði əˈpɒstl̩ / (BrE)
one of the twelve disciples of Jesus in the New Testament, brother of Peter, and patron saint of Scotland
disciple of Jesus; patron saint of Scotland

Examples

Every year, Scots celebrate Saint Andrew the Apostle on November 30th with various traditions.
In Christian art, Saint Andrew the Apostle is often depicted with a diagonal cross.
According to the New Testament, Saint Andrew the Apostle was the brother of Saint Peter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Peter - disciple of Jesus and brother of Andrew
