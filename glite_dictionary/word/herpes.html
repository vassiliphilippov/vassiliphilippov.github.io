herpes

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːpiːz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz / (BrE)
A group of viral diseases marked by painful skin or mucous membrane eruptions, especially those caused by herpes simplex or herpes zoster viruses.
viral disease causing skin or mucous eruptions
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the skin rash as a form of herpes and prescribed antiviral medication.
Oral herpes commonly causes cold sores around the mouth and lips.
He worried about the possibility of contracting herpes after learning about its symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek *herpēs* (a creeping), from *herpein* (to creep).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɜːrpiːz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses causing painful blisters or sores on the skin or mucous membranes, especially around the mouth or genitals.
virus causing painful blisters or sores
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the cluster of blisters as a possible case of herpes.
Cold sores around the lips are often caused by the herpes virus.
She learned that herpes can be managed with antiviral medications and good health practices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: herpes simplex - a specific type of herpes virus causing oral or genital sores
  • Degree Higher: herpes zoster - a herpes virus causing shingles

Etymology

From Greek *herpēs* (creeping), referring to the spreading nature of the lesions.
