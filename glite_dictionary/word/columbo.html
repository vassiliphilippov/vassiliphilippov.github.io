columbo

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmboʊ / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb of the genus Frasera, found in dry upland areas of western North America
a tall perennial herb (Frasera)

Examples

The roots of columbo have been traditionally used for medicinal purposes.
We spotted several columbo plants during our hike in Oregon's uplands.
Columbo thrives in well-drained soil and open, sunny areas.

Etymology

From the genus name Frasera; possibly altered from Colombo root or related medicinal plants.
