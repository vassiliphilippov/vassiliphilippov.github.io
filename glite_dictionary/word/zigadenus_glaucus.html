Zigadenus glaucus

1. PROPN

/ zɪɡəˈdinəs ˈɡlaʊkəs / (AmE), / zɪɡəˈdiːnəs ˈɡlaʊkəs / (BrE)
A plant native to eastern and central North America, with creamy white flowers tinged with brown or purple; poisonous, especially to grazing animals.
a poisonous North American plant species

Examples

Cattle have to be kept away from Zigadenus glaucus because it is highly poisonous.
The fields in spring were dotted with Zigadenus glaucus in bloom.
Researchers studied the effects of Zigadenus glaucus on local grazing animal populations.

Etymology

From the genus name Zigadenus, coined from Greek 'zigon' (yoke) and 'aden' (gland), and species epithet 'glaucus' meaning 'bluish-green, gray' in Latin.
