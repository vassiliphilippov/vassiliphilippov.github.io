Tevet

1. NOUN

/ teɪˈvɛt / (AmE), / teɪˈvɛt / (BrE)
the fourth month of the civil year and the tenth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Hebrew calendar, occurring in December and January
month in the Hebrew calendar

Examples

The fast of the Tenth of Tevet is a significant day of mourning in Jewish tradition.
She was born in the month of Tevet according to the Hebrew calendar.
Hanukkah sometimes extends into Tevet, depending on the lunar cycle.

Etymology

From Hebrew טֵבֵת (tevet), the name of the month in the Jewish calendar.
