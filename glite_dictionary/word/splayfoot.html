splayfoot

1. ADJ

/ ˈspleɪˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈspleɪˌfʊt / (BrE)
(of a person) having feet that turn outward.
having feet that turn outward

Examples

The doctor noted that her splayfoot condition caused her to walk slightly differently.
Children who are splayfoot may need special shoes for comfort.
He moved with a distinctive splayfoot gait since childhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pigeon-toed - having feet that turn inward

2. NOUN

/ ˈspleɪˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈspleɪˌfʊt / (BrE)
A foot with a fallen arch resulting in an abnormally flattened and widened shape.
foot with fallen arch; flattened, widened shape
medical

Examples

After years of wearing the wrong shoes, he developed a noticeable splayfoot.
Children with splayfoot may need special insoles for support.
A splayfoot can lead to discomfort and difficulty walking if not treated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatfoot - a condition in which the arch of the foot collapses, with the entire sole coming into complete or near-complete contact with the ground

Etymology

From 'splay' (to spread out) and 'foot', indicating the spreading of the foot's shape.
