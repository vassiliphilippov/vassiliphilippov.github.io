to homologize

1. VERB

/ hoʊˈmɑːləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒl.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
to make homologous or to demonstrate the homology of two or more things, especially in scientific contexts such as biology or chemistry
make or demonstrate as homologous

Examples

The scientist attempted to homologize the bones of the whale with those of land mammals.
Researchers often homologize genes across species to understand evolutionary relationships.
Chemists can homologize hydrocarbons by adding repeating units to their structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to differentiate - to distinguish or make different
  • Degree Lower: to analogize - to make or perceive an analogy

Etymology

From Greek homólogos (agreeing, of common descent) + -ize.

2. VERB

/ həˈmɑːləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to show or recognize as having the same evolutionary origin or structure, especially in biological terms
recognize as having similar origin or structure
medical

Examples

Scientists homologize the bones of a whale's flipper with those of the human arm.
In comparative anatomy, experts often homologize structures to trace evolutionary relationships.
Geneticists can homologize genes between different species to study their functions.

Synset Examples

A person's arms homologize with a quadruped's forelimbs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to analogize - to show similarity by analogy

Etymology

From Greek homologos (agreeing, of one mind).
