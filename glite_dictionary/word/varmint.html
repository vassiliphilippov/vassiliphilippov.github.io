varmint

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːrmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvɑːmɪnt / (BrE)
a wild animal considered undesirable, especially a predatory or nuisance species such as a coyote or fox
undesirable or nuisance wild animal

Examples

The farmer set traps to catch any varmint that might raid his chicken coop.
Coyotes are often considered varmints by ranchers because they prey on livestock.
He spotted a varmint sneaking around the trash cans late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermin - animals or insects regarded as pests

Etymology

From variant of 'vermin,' influenced by regional American English and earlier French 'vermine.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑrmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvɑːmɪnt / (BrE)
(informal) An irritating, troublesome, or obnoxious person.
irritating or obnoxious person (informal)
informal

Examples

That varmint next door plays his music too loud every night.
She's always calling her little brother a mischievous varmint.
Get out of my garden, you pesky varmint!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person

Etymology

Variant of 'vermin', from Middle English 'vermin', from Old French, from Latin 'vermis' (worm).
