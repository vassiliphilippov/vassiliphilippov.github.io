Second Epistle to the Thessalonians

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl tə ði ˌθɛsəˈloʊniənz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ɪˈpɪsəl tə ði ˌθɛsəˈləʊniənz / (BrE)
A book of the New Testament containing Saint Paul's second letter to the Christians in Thessalonica.
New Testament book; Paul's second letter to Thessalonians

Examples

Many scholars debate the authorship of the Second Epistle to the Thessalonians.
The Second Epistle to the Thessalonians addresses issues of faith and perseverance.
During the sermon, the pastor quoted from the Second Epistle to the Thessalonians.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Epistle to the Thessalonians - Paul's first letter to the Thessalonians; preceding book in the New Testament

Etymology

Epistle from Greek *epistolē* (letter), Thessalonians from the ancient city Thessalonica.
