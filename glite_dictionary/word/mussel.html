mussel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
a marine or freshwater bivalve mollusk with a dark elongated shell, typically found attached to rocks or submerged surfaces.
marine or freshwater bivalve mollusk

Examples

We harvested fresh mussels from the rocky shoreline at dawn.
The restaurant served a delicious dish of steamed mussels in white wine sauce.
Many mussel species help filter water and improve aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a bivalve mollusk similar to mussels
  • Synonym: oyster - an edible bivalve mollusk often eaten raw

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'musculus', diminutive of Latin 'mus' meaning 'mouse', referring to the shell's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
A type of edible marine bivalve mollusc, typically with a dark bluish-black shell, often cooked by steaming.
edible marine bivalve mollusc

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish of steamed mussels with garlic and parsley.
We gathered mussels from the rocky shoreline at low tide.
Fresh mussels are often served in white wine sauce in many coastal restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - another type of edible bivalve mollusc
  • Synonym: oyster - a different kind of edible bivalve, often eaten raw or cooked

Etymology

From Late Latin *muscula*, diminutive of Latin *musculus* (little mouse), referring to the shape.
