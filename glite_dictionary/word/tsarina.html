tsarina

1. NOUN

/ zɑːˈriːnə / (AmE), / tsɑːˈriːnə / (BrE)
the wife or widow of a czar, or a female czar; a Russian empress
wife or widow of a czar; Russian empress

Examples

The last tsarina of Russia was Alexandra Feodorovna, wife of Nicholas II.
Historians have researched the role the tsarina played in the Russian Revolution.
During the lavish ball, the guests caught a glimpse of the elegant tsarina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: czarina - wife or widow of a czar; variant spelling
  • Synonym: czar - male ruler or emperor of Russia

Etymology

From Russian цари́ца (tsarítsa), from царь (tsar, czar) with the feminine suffix -itsa.
