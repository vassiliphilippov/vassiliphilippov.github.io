Thrinax morrisii

1. NOUN

/ ˈθraɪ.næks ˈmɔːr.ɪsi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈθraɪ.naks ˈmɒr.ɪsi.aɪ / (BrE)
A small, stocky species of fan palm native to southern Florida and Cuba.
species of fan palm

Examples

The Thrinax morrisii is commonly found along the coasts of southern Florida.
Because of its resilience to salt, Thrinax morrisii is often used in seaside landscaping.
Botanists study Thrinax morrisii to understand adaptation in tropical palms.

Etymology

The genus name 'Thrinax' is from Greek θριναξ (thrinax), meaning 'trident', alluding to the shape of the leaves. 'Morrisii' commemorates a person named Morris.
