neb

1. NOUN

/ nɛb / (AmE), / nɛb / (BrE)
the beak or bill of a bird; a projecting horny mouthpart
beak or bill of a bird

Examples

The hawk sharpened its neb upon a rock before swooping down on its prey.
In old poetry, a raven's black neb was often a portent of bad luck.
The fledgling opened its neb wide, waiting to be fed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beak - the hard, pointed mouth of a bird
  • Synonym: bill - the beak of a bird, especially one that is flat or broad

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *nebb* (face, nose, beak), related to Old Norse *nef* (nose).

2. NOUN

/ nɛb / (AmE), / nɛb / (BrE)
a long or projecting part of an animal's head, especially the nose or beak
animal's projecting nose or beak
archaic

Examples

The bird's sharp neb gleamed in the sunlight as it searched for insects.
He tapped the horse on its neb to calm it down before setting off.
Old tales describe monsters with enormous, gaping nebs ready to snap up their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beak - the hard, pointed part of a bird's mouth
  • Synonym: bill - the beak of a bird

Etymology

From Middle English *nebbe*, from Old English *nebb* (face, beak), related to Old Norse *nef* (nose, beak).
