octosyllable

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑktoʊˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˌɒktəʊˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A line of verse containing exactly eight syllables, or a poem composed of such lines.
line of verse with eight syllables

Examples

The poet used the octosyllable to create a brisk, rhythmic tone in her verses.
Many medieval French poems are written in octosyllable to maintain a consistent meter.
Writing an octosyllable can be challenging due to the strict syllabic structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hexasyllable - a line of verse containing six syllables
  • Degree Higher: decasyllable - a line of verse containing ten syllables

Etymology

From French 'octosyllabe', from Latin 'octo' (eight) + 'syllaba' (syllable).
