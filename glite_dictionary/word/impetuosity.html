impetuosity

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛtʃuˈɑsəti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛtjuˈɒsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of acting or doing something quickly and without thinking carefully; rash impulsiveness
rash impulsiveness; hasty action

Examples

Her impetuosity often led her to make decisions she later regretted.
The impetuosity of youth can sometimes result in careless mistakes.
He admired her impetuosity, though it sometimes got them into trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act on sudden urges
  • Antonym: prudence - careful good judgment that allows someone to avoid danger or risks

Etymology

From Late Latin impetuositas, from Latin impetuosus (impetuous).
