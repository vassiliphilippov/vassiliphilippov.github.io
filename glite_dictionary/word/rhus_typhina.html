Rhus typhina

1. NOUN

/ ruːs taɪˈfaɪnə / (AmE), / ruːs tɪˈfiːnə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, known for its compound leaves that turn bright red in autumn, dense clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, and crimson acidic berries.
shrubby North American tree with red fall leaves and crimson berries

Examples

The vibrant red leaves of Rhus typhina stand out beautifully every autumn.
Many birds are attracted to the crimson berries of Rhus typhina in the winter months.
Gardeners often plant Rhus typhina for its ornamental foliage and wildlife value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn sumac - common name for Rhus typhina, a shrub with red fall leaves and crimson berries

Etymology

From Latinized form of the ancient Greek name for sumac (rhus) and typhina, meaning 'like Typha' (cattail), from resemblance of fruit clusters.
