laissez passer

1. NOUN

/ læs.eɪ pɑːˈseɪ / (AmE), / læseɪ pɑːˈseɪ / (BrE)
an official document that allows a person to travel freely or pass through certain areas, typically issued by a government or international organization
official travel permit or pass document

Examples

The delegate presented his laissez passer at the border checkpoint.
United Nations officials were issued a laissez passer for their mission.
After showing her laissez passer, she was allowed to enter the restricted area.

Synset Examples

the media representatives had special passes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - a permit allowing access or entry
  • Synonym: visa - an official document permitting entry to a country

Etymology

From French 'laissez passer', meaning 'let pass', from 'laisser' (to let) and 'passer' (to pass).
