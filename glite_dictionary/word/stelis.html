Stelis

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˈstiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small tufted orchids native to tropical America.
genus of small orchids of tropical America

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Stelis genus in the Amazon rainforest.
The delicate flowers of Stelis orchids attract many orchid enthusiasts each year.
Botanists study the unique pollination mechanisms of Stelis in tropical climates.

Etymology

From Greek *stēlis* (post, pillar), used in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˈstiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small tropical American orchids characterized by long, slender racemes bearing many small to minute flowers.
genus of small tropical American orchids

Examples

The botanical garden's collection includes several rare Stelis species from Ecuador.
Researchers are studying how Stelis orchids adapt to humid, shaded environments in the rainforest.
Each Stelis plant produces a delicate raceme with dozens of tiny flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name given in botanical nomenclature.
