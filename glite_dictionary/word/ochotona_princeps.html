Ochotona princeps

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.kəˈtoʊ.nə ˈprɪn.sɛps / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.kəˈtəʊ.nə ˈprɪn.sɛps / (BrE)
the scientific name for the North American pika, a small mammal found in mountainous regions of western North America
scientific name for the North American pika

Examples

Researchers observed several Ochotona princeps gathering food among the rocky slopes.
Climate change poses a significant threat to the habitat of Ochotona princeps.
The distinctive whistle of Ochotona princeps can often be heard in alpine meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - a small mountain mammal related to rabbits

Etymology

From genus name Ochotona (from Russian 'ochoton') and Latin 'princeps' (chief, leader).
