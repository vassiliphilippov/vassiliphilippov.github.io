rainmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈreɪnˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
an executive or partner who is exceptionally successful at bringing new business, clients, or revenue into a company or firm
executive skilled at generating business

Examples

The new partner quickly proved herself a rainmaker, bringing in several high-profile clients within her first year.
Every firm wants a rainmaker who can consistently secure major accounts.
As a rainmaker, he was instrumental in doubling the company's annual revenue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal-maker - person skilled at negotiating and closing business deals
  • Synonym: client generator - one who brings in new clients

Etymology

Originally from Native American ceremonial term for one who brings rain; later adopted in business for someone who achieves remarkable success.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈreɪnˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
(historical, chiefly North American) A medicine man among Native American peoples who attempts to bring rain by performing rituals or ceremonies.
medicine man who tries to make it rain

Examples

The tribe's rainmaker performed a ceremony each summer in hopes of bringing rain to the fields.
Legends tell of a powerful rainmaker who could summon storms with his chants.
In times of drought, the community relied on their rainmaker to appeal to the spirits for water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine man - a traditional healer or spiritual leader among some Indigenous peoples of the Americas
