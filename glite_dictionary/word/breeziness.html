breeziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriziˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈbriːziːnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being mildly windy or characterized by gentle breezes
the state of being mildly windy

Examples

The breeziness of the beach made our walk especially refreshing.
She enjoyed the breeziness that drifted through the open window on summer evenings.
There was a pleasant breeziness in the garden after the afternoon rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breezy - having a light, gentle wind
  • Antonym: stillness - absence of wind or movement in the air

Etymology

From 'breezy' + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbriːzinəs / (AmE), / ˈbriːzinɪs / (BrE)
a quality of being lively, cheerful, and relaxed in manner or style
lively, cheerful quality

Examples

Her breeziness put everyone at ease during the meeting.
There was a certain breeziness in his tone that made the conversation light and pleasant.
The breeziness of her writing style made the article enjoyable to read.

Synset Examples

a delightful breeziness of manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacity - the quality of being attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: liveliness - animated or energetic character; spiritedness
  • Antonym: soberness - seriousness in manner or attitude

Etymology

From 'breezy' + '-ness', formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04641154-n
Synset POS: n
