prophetess

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fə.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A woman who is regarded as a prophet, especially one who claims to speak for a deity or foresee the future.
female prophet

Examples

The ancient temple was said to house a famous prophetess who interpreted dreams and omens.
Many people traveled great distances to hear the words of the prophetess.
In the story, a wise prophetess predicted the downfall of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophet - a person who claims to speak for a deity or foresee the future

Etymology

From Old French 'prophetesse', from Latin 'prophetissa', feminine of 'propheta' (prophet).
