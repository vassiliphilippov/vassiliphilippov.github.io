carry-forward

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæri ˌfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˌfɔːwəd / (BrE)
an amount, such as profits or losses, from a previous accounting period that is transferred to the next period, especially after deducting dividends and reserves
accumulated profit or loss transferred to next accounting period

Examples

The company's carry-forward increased this year due to higher retained earnings.
Tax regulations allow you to use a carry-forward of losses to offset future profits.
The board discussed the carry-forward and how it might impact next year's financial planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carryover - an amount transferred to the next period or item

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'carry forward', meaning to transfer an amount to a subsequent period.
