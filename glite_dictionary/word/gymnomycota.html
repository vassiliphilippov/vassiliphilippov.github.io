Gymnomycota

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.noʊ.maɪˈkoʊ.tə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəʊ.maɪˈkəʊ.tə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of slime molds characterized by a noncellular, multinucleate vegetative phase and a spore-producing stage, including Myxomycetes and Acrasiomycetes.
group of slime molds (taxonomy)

Examples

Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within Gymnomycota to better understand the evolution of slime molds.
The Gymnomycota display a unique life cycle distinct from true fungi.
In some modern classifications, Gymnomycota is considered part of the kingdom Protoctista rather than Fungi.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'mykes' (fungus), referring to 'naked fungi'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnoʊmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnəʊmɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a division of slime molds characterized by a noncellular, multinucleate vegetative phase and a spore-producing stage, including Myxomycetes and Acrasiomycetes
division of slime molds (biological)
medical

Examples

Gymnomycota are studied for their unique life cycles and role in decomposing organic material.
Scientists observed the spore-producing stages of Gymnomycota under the microscope.
The Gymnomycota division includes organisms that can exist in a multinucleate, creeping form.

Etymology

From Greek *gymnos* (naked) + *mykes* (fungus), referring to exposed spore-bearing structures.
