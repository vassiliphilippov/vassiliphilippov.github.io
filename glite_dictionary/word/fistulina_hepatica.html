Fistulina hepatica

1. NOUN

/ fɪsˈtuːlɪnə hɛˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / fɪsˈtjuːlɪnə hɛˈpætɪkə / (BrE)
A species of edible fungus, known as the beefsteak fungus, with a cap resembling raw meat in color and texture, found particularly in southeastern United States and Europe.
a species of edible fungus (beefsteak fungus)

Examples

The cap of Fistulina hepatica is often bright red, resembling a slice of raw beef.
Foragers in Europe prize Fistulina hepatica for its unique texture and tangy flavor.
During autumn, Fistulina hepatica can be found growing on oak trees in moist woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beefsteak fungus - common name for Fistulina hepatica

Etymology

From Latin: 'fistula' (pipe, tube) and 'hepatica' (of the liver).
