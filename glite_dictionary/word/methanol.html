methanol

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˈmiːθənɒl / (BrE)
a light, volatile, flammable, and poisonous liquid alcohol, used especially as an antifreeze, solvent, fuel, and denaturant for ethyl alcohol
a poisonous, flammable alcohol used as a solvent or fuel

Examples

Exposure to high levels of methanol can cause serious health problems.
Methanol is commonly used as a solvent in laboratories.
The car runs on a fuel mixture that includes methanol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood alcohol - another name for methanol, especially when derived from wood distillation
  • Antonym: ethanol - the type of alcohol found in alcoholic drinks

Etymology

From 'methylene' + 'alcohol'; originally coined in the 19th century.
