glycerine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəˌrin / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsəriːn / (BrE)
A sweet, thick, colorless liquid used in making medicines, food, and cosmetics, and obtained by saponification of fats and oils.
sweet, thick liquid from fats and oils

Examples

Glycerine is often used in skincare products to keep the skin moisturized.
The laboratory purchased a bottle of glycerine for use in experiments.
Cakes sometimes contain glycerine to maintain their softness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycerol - another name for the same chemical compound

Etymology

From French *glycérine*, from Greek *glykys* (sweet).
