Hogmanay

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːɡ.məˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɒɡ.məˈneɪ / (BrE)
The celebration of New Year's Eve in Scotland, often accompanied by festivities and traditional customs.
Scottish New Year's Eve celebration
british

Examples

Many people travel to Edinburgh to celebrate Hogmanay with fireworks and street parties.
First-footing is an important tradition during Hogmanay in Scottish households.
We exchanged small gifts as part of the Hogmanay customs.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from Old French hoguinané (New Year's gift), or related to French aguillanneuf.
