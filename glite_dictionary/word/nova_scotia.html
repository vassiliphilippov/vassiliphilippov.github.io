Nova Scotia

1. PROPN

/ ˌnoʊvə ˈskoʊʃə / (AmE), / ˌnəʊvə ˈskəʊʃə / (BrE)
A peninsula and province in eastern Canada, located between the Bay of Fundy and the Atlantic Ocean.
Canadian province in eastern Canada

Examples

She traveled to Nova Scotia to visit the famous coastal towns and lighthouses.
The seafood in Nova Scotia is renowned throughout Canada for its freshness and flavor.
Many families in Nova Scotia trace their ancestry back to Scottish settlers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Nova Scotia' meaning 'New Scotland'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌnoʊvə ˈskoʊʃə / (AmE), / ˌnəʊvə ˈskəʊʃə / (BrE)
a province in eastern Canada, located on a peninsula and including Cape Breton Island, known for its maritime history and as the former home of French settlers called Acadians.
Canadian province in the Maritimes

Examples

My grandparents moved to Nova Scotia after they retired.
The rugged coastline of Nova Scotia is famous for its scenic beauty and lighthouses.
Nova Scotia played a key role in Canadian maritime history.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'New Scotland.'
