Anatolia

1. PROPN

/ ˌænəˈtoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈtəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southwestern Asia forming the Asian part of Turkey, historically significant as a crossroads of civilizations
peninsula, Asian part of Turkey

Examples

Many ancient civilizations, including the Hittites and Greeks, once thrived in Anatolia.
Today, Anatolia is home to some of Turkey's most important cities, such as Ankara and Konya.
Archaeologists continue to uncover significant ruins throughout Anatolia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asia Minor - alternative historical name for Anatolia

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀνατολή (Anatolḗ), meaning 'east' or 'sunrise'.
