snowfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌfiːld / (BrE)
a large area of land covered with snow throughout the year, especially on mountains or in polar regions
a permanent expanse of snow

Examples

We could see the vast snowfield stretching out beneath the mountain peak.
The climbers crossed a dangerous snowfield on their way to the summit.
Due to climate change, the snowfield has been shrinking over the past decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glacier - a large mass of ice slowly moving over land

Etymology

From 'snow' + 'field'; attested from the late 19th century.
