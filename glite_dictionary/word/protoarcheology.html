protoarcheology

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˌɑrkiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of prehistoric human artifacts and fossils, typically focusing on periods before the advent of written records
study of prehistoric artifacts and fossils

Examples

Researchers in protoarcheology analyze stone tools dating back thousands of years.
The field of protoarcheology sheds light on how early humans lived before written records.
She is pursuing a career in protoarcheology to discover more about prehistoric societies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: archaeology - the scientific study of historic or prehistoric peoples and their cultures

Etymology

From Greek 'protos' (first) and 'archaiologia' (study of ancient things).
