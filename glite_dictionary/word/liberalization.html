liberalization

1. NOUN

/ lɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪbəraɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making rules, laws, or controls less strict, especially in economic or political contexts
the process of making less strict

Examples

The government announced a policy of liberalization to encourage economic growth.
Since the liberalization of trade, imports have increased significantly.
Many citizens have benefited from the liberalization of travel restrictions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deregulation - the removal of government controls from an industry or sector

Etymology

From liberal + -ization, influenced by French libéralisation.
