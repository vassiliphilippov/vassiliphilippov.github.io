Upanishads

1. NOUN

/ uˈpɑːnɪˌʃɑdz / (AmE), / uːˈpænɪʃædz / (BrE)
A group of ancient Hindu sacred texts composed in Sanskrit that explore mystical and philosophical ideas, forming the basis of later Hindu thought.
ancient Hindu sacred texts

Examples

Many philosophical concepts in Hinduism are discussed extensively in the Upanishads.
The Upanishads are considered a key text in the study of Eastern philosophy.
Scholars often analyze the mystical teachings found within the Upanishads.

Synset Examples

the Vedanta philosophy developed from the pantheistic views of the Upanishads

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Vedas - the most ancient Hindu scriptures, preceding the Upanishads
  • Degree Higher: Bhagavad Gita - a later Hindu scripture that also discusses philosophical themes

Etymology

From Sanskrit upaniṣad (sitting down near, i.e., at the feet of a teacher).
