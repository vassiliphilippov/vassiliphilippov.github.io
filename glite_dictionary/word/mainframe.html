mainframe

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌfreɪm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪn.freɪm / (BrE)
a large and powerful computer, typically used by organizations for bulk data processing and capable of serving many users simultaneously
large, powerful computer for multiple users

Examples

The company upgraded its mainframe to handle more transactions efficiently.
Many banks rely on a mainframe for their core processing needs.
The data was processed overnight by the central mainframe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supercomputer - an extremely fast and powerful computer used for complex calculations

Etymology

From 'main' (principal) + 'frame' (structure): originally referred to the main structural frame of a computer system.
