Missouria

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈzʊəriə / (AmE), / mɪˈzʊəriə / (BrE)
a member of a Siouan-speaking Native American people formerly inhabiting the valley of the Missouri River in Missouri, United States
a Native American people of Missouri

Examples

The Missouria were known for their agricultural skills and trade networks along the Missouri River.
Anthropologists have studied the language and customs of the Missouria.
Few direct descendants of the Missouria remain today, but their legacy lives on in the region.

Etymology

From the name of the Missouri River, itself from an Algonquian language meaning 'people with wooden canoes.'
