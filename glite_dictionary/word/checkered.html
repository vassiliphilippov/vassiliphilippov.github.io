checkered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛkɚd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəd / (BrE)
having a pattern of alternating squares of different colors, usually in a regular arrangement
patterned with squares of color

Examples

The tablecloth had a checkered pattern of red and white.
She wore a checkered shirt to the picnic.
The racing flag is famously checkered with black and white squares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checked - having a pattern of squares; similar patterned textile

Etymology

From Middle English 'chekered', based on 'checker' (a chessboard pattern), from Anglo-Norman 'escheker'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛkɚd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəd / (BrE)
marked by periods of varying fortune or character, often with both successes and failures or good and bad experiences.
marked by mixed fortune or character

Examples

Her checkered political career included both impressive victories and public controversies.
Despite his checkered past, he managed to rebuild his reputation over the years.
The company has had a checkered history of occasional breakthroughs and frequent setbacks.

Synset Examples

a checkered business career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eventful - full of interesting or important events; having a lot happening
  • Antonym: unbroken - not interrupted, continuous, especially of experience or history

Etymology

From ‘checkered’, originally meaning marked with a checker pattern; figurative use relates to the varied nature of a checkered design.
