Arctocebus calabarensis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrkˈtoʊsɛbəs kəˌlɑbəˈrɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtəʊsɪbəs kəˌlɑːbəˈrensɪs / (BrE)
A species of small, nocturnal primate found in Central and West Africa, commonly known as the Calabar angwantibo or golden angwantibo.
species of nocturnal African primate

Examples

The Arctocebus calabarensis is notable for its slow movements and nocturnal habits.
Researchers in Cameroon recently spotted an Arctocebus calabarensis in the wild.
Unlike other primates, the Arctocebus calabarensis has a short tail and distinctive golden fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angwantibo - A common name for primates of the genus Arctocebus

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Arctocebus' from Greek 'arktos' (bear) and 'kebos' (long-tailed monkey); species 'calabarensis' refers to Calabar region in Nigeria.
