xylosma

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɒzmə / (AmE), / zɪˈlɒzmə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree with neat evergreen leaves and fragrant late flowers, grown as an ornamental in mild climates; native to China and parts of Asia.
evergreen ornamental shrub or small tree

Examples

The gardener chose xylosma for its evergreen foliage and easy maintenance.
A mature xylosma can provide dense screening in a landscaped yard.
In spring, the xylosma produces a pleasant fragrance from its small flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood) and possibly an alteration based on taxonomic nomenclature conventions.
