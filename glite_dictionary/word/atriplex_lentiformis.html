Atriplex lentiformis

1. NOUN

/ ˈætrɪˌplɛks lɛnˈtɪfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / ˈætrɪplɛks lɛnˈtɪfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A spiny shrub with silvery, scurfy leaves, native to alkaline plains of the southwestern United States and Mexico.
spiny shrub of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Atriplex lentiformis thrives in the saline soils of desert valleys.
Farmers often use Atriplex lentiformis as forage for livestock in arid regions.
Researchers studied the water retention abilities of Atriplex lentiformis in southwestern ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Atriplex' is a plant genus, and 'lentiformis' means 'lens-shaped'.
