amine

1. NOUN

/ əˈmiːn / (AmE), / ˈæm.iːn / (BrE)
a compound derived from ammonia by replacing one or more hydrogen atoms with hydrocarbon groups
compound derived from ammonia

Examples

An amine group is often present in many biologically important molecules.
Caffeine is classified as an amine due to its nitrogen atoms.
The synthesis of this drug involves attaching an amine to a carbon backbone.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ammonia - a colorless gas with a strong smell, consisting of nitrogen and hydrogen

Etymology

From French amine, based on ammonia, with the suffix -ine.
