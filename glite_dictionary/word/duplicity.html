duplicity

1. NOUN

/ duːˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / djuːˈplɪsəti / (BrE)
deceitful behavior intended to mislead or trick someone; double-dealing.
deceitful double-dealing

Examples

The politician's duplicity eventually led to a scandal that ruined her career.
She was shocked by the duplicity of someone she had trusted for so long.
Business partners must avoid duplicity in order to maintain trust and collaboration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful

Etymology

From Latin 'duplicitas' (doubleness), from 'duplex' (double).

2. NOUN

/ duˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / djuːˈplɪsəti / (BrE)
dishonest behavior that is intended to deceive someone, especially by pretending to have one set of feelings or intentions while acting differently
deceptive or double-dealing behavior

Examples

The politician's duplicity was finally exposed by investigative journalists.
In business, duplicity can quickly destroy trust between partners.
She was hurt to discover the duplicity behind his charming words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin duplicitas (doubleness), from duplex (double).
