dagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæɡər / (AmE), / ˈdaɡə / (BrE)
a short, pointed knife designed for piercing or stabbing, often used as a weapon
short pointed knife used as a weapon

Examples

The assassin concealed a dagger in his cloak before entering the hall.
She collected ancient daggers from different cultures.
He threatened them with a sharp dagger when they tried to escape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - a cutting instrument consisting of a blade and handle
  • Degree Higher: sword - a long-bladed weapon for cutting or thrusting

Etymology

From Old French 'dague', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæɡər / (AmE), / ˈdæɡə / (BrE)
a typographical symbol (†) used in printing to indicate a second cross-reference or footnote when the asterisk has already been used
typographical symbol for a footnote or cross-reference

Examples

In the article, a dagger was used to mark the second footnote at the bottom of the page.
Please refer to the dagger for additional information about this point.
If you see a dagger after a word, check the corresponding note for further details.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: asterisk - symbol (*) used for a first footnote or cross-reference
  • Degree Higher: double dagger - symbol (‡) used for a third footnote or cross-reference

Etymology

From Old French 'dague' (dagger), referring to the symbol's resemblance to a small sword.
