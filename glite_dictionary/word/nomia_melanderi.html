Nomia melanderi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.mi.ə məˈlæn.dəˌraɪ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.mi.ə mɛˈlæn.dəri / (BrE)
A solitary bee species significant for pollinating alfalfa crops in the western United States.
solitary bee pollinating alfalfa in western US

Examples

Nomia melanderi is vital for the pollination of alfalfa crops throughout California.
Researchers studied the nesting behavior of Nomia melanderi to better understand solitary bee species.
Farmers encourage Nomia melanderi populations to increase the yield of their alfalfa fields.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'nomia' (pasture); species name honors American entomologist Charles T. Melander.
