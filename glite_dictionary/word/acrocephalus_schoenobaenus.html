Acrocephalus schoenobaenus

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈsɛfələs ˌskiːnəˈbinəs / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˈsɛfələs ˌskiːnəˈbiːnəs / (BrE)
A small European warbler of the family Acrocephalidae, known for breeding among reeds and sedges and migrating to Africa for the winter; also called the Sedge Warbler.
species of European warbler (Sedge Warbler)

Examples

The Acrocephalus schoenobaenus is known for its varied and complex song during the breeding season.
Each spring, birdwatchers eagerly await the arrival of Acrocephalus schoenobaenus in the marshes.
The migratory route of Acrocephalus schoenobaenus extends from Europe to sub-Saharan Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sedge Warbler - Common English name for Acrocephalus schoenobaenus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Acrocephalus' from Greek 'akros' (topmost, extreme) + 'kephalē' (head); species name 'schoenobaenus' from Greek 'schoinos' (reed) + 'bainō' (to go), referring to its reed-dwelling habits.
