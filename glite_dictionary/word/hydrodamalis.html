Hydrodamalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊdəˈmælɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊdəˈmalɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, extinct marine mammals in the family Dugongidae, comprising only Steller's sea cow.
genus containing Steller's sea cow

Examples

The extinct Hydrodamalis was once found in the cold waters of the North Pacific.
Scientists study fossilized bones of Hydrodamalis to learn about its behavior and environment.
The discovery of Hydrodamalis remains provided new insights into the history of marine mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steller's sea cow - an extinct large marine mammal, sole species in Hydrodamalis

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) + 'damalis' (heifer or young cow).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊdəˈmælɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪdrəʊdəˈmælɪs / (BrE)
a genus of large marine mammals in the family Dugongidae, known for including Steller's sea cow, now extinct.
genus of large extinct sea cows

Examples

The extinct Hydrodamalis is best known for Steller's sea cow, which once inhabited the Bering Sea.
Research into Hydrodamalis fossils sheds light on the evolution of marine mammals.
Members of the genus Hydrodamalis were massive, herbivorous sea creatures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dugongidae - the family to which Hydrodamalis belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'damalis' (heifer, young cow).
