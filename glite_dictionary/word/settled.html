settled

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛt̬.əld / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
(of a situation, condition, or period) stable and unlikely to change; established or fixed.
stable; not likely to change

Examples

After days of rain, we finally enjoyed a week of settled weather.
Her routine became more settled after she moved to the countryside.
The political situation remains settled, with no major changes expected.

Synset Examples

a period of settled weather

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unstable - likely to change or not firmly fixed
  • Synonym: steady - firm and unwavering; regular and not likely to change
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00304279-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛt̬.əld / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
(of a place) inhabited or established by colonists or permanent residents.
inhabited by colonists or residents

Examples

The eastern coast was quickly settled by European colonists in the 17th century.
Many indigenous communities were displaced when the region became settled.
The settled areas had more developed infrastructure than the surrounding wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsettled - not inhabited by colonists or permanent residents

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'settle,' from Old English 'setlan' (to seat, place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01314560-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɛt̬.əld / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
established in a particular place or position; having a fixed or stable residence or situation and not moving about.
established; not moving about

Examples

After moving so many times, it felt good to finally be settled in one place.
The family had a settled routine after several years in the same neighborhood.
She didn't consider herself truly settled until she unpacked the last box.

Synset Examples

nomads … absorbed among the settled people
settled areas
I don't feel entirely settled here
the advent of settled civilization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nomadic - moving about, not settled in one place
  • Synonym: resident - someone who lives permanently in a place; more specific type of settled person

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'settle', from Old English setlan (cause to sit, place firmly), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *setjanan.

4. ADJ

/ ˈsɛtəld / (AmE), / ˈsɛtld / (BrE)
established or decided so that there is no longer any doubt or dispute
established, no longer in doubt

Examples

Now that the question has been settled, we can proceed with the project.
The couple felt relieved when the dispute over the contract was finally settled.
Once the plans were settled, everyone felt much more relaxed.

Synset Examples

with details of the wedding settled she could now sleep at night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolved - (of an issue or problem) settled or determined
  • Antonym: uncertain - not known or fixed; not settled

Etymology

From the verb 'settle', from Old English *setlan* (to seat, place), ultimately from Proto-Germanic roots.
