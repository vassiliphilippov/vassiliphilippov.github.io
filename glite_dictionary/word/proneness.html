proneness

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊn.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being likely or disposed to do or experience something, especially something undesirable.
inclination or tendency to do something

Examples

Her proneness to worry made it hard for her to relax in stressful situations.
Researchers studied the proneness of certain individuals to develop allergies.
He was aware of his proneness to forget appointments and set multiple reminders.

Synset Examples

accident proneness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tendency - an inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior
  • Synonym: inclination - a person's natural tendency to act or feel in a particular way
  • Antonym: resistance - the refusal to accept or comply with something; the attempt to prevent something

Etymology

Formed from 'prone' + '-ness'; 'prone' from Latin 'pronus' (bent forward, inclined).
