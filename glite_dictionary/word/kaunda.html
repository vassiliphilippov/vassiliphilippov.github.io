Kaunda

1. PROPN

/ kaʊˈʊndə / (AmE), / kaʊˈʊndə / (BrE)
Kenneth Kaunda (1924–1999), Zambian statesman who led the country to independence and served as its first president.
Zambia's first president, 1924–1999

Examples

Kaunda was widely respected for his leadership in the movement for Zambia's independence.
In 1991, Kaunda peacefully transferred power after losing the presidential election.
Many streets in Lusaka are named after Kaunda in honor of his legacy.
