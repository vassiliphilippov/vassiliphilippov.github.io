Lycopsida

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / laɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of vascular plants that includes club mosses and their relatives, such as Lycopodiales, Isoetales, Selaginellales, and the extinct Lepidodendrales.
class of vascular plants (club mosses)
medical

Examples

Fossils of Lycopsida provide evidence of Earth's ancient forests.
Modern members of Lycopsida are much smaller than their giant prehistoric ancestors.
Botanists study Lycopsida to understand the evolution of vascular plants.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek lykos (wolf) and opsi (appearance), via botanical naming conventions.

Examples

The class Lycopsida includes both living and extinct species of club mosses.
Researchers found fossils belonging to the Lycopsida in ancient rock formations.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'lyx', wolf, and 'pous', foot, referring to the appearance of some species.
