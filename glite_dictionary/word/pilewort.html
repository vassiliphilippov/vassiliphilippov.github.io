pilewort

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a perennial European herb (Ficaria verna) with heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, whose tuberous roots have been used as a poultice to treat piles (hemorrhoids)
European herb used to treat piles

Examples

The roots of pilewort were traditionally used in folk medicine for their soothing properties.
In spring, the banks of the river were dotted with bright yellow pilewort flowers.
Botanists studied the spread of pilewort beyond its native range in Europe.

Etymology

From 'pile' (hemorrhoid) + 'wort' (old word for plant).
