Pythiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪθiˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌpɪθiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by having sporangia usually borne successively and singly at the tips of branching sporangiophores
family of fungi with distinctive sporangia

Examples

Many members of the Pythiaceae family are found in soil and water habitats.
Scientists have studied the Pythiaceae to understand their role in plant pathology.
The unique sporangiophores of Pythiaceae species help distinguish them from other fungi.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Pythium and the suffix -aceae used for plant and fungal families.

2. NOUN

/ pɪθiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pɪθɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi characterized by sporangia borne successively and singly at the tips of branching sporangiophores.
family of certain fungi

Examples

The Pythiaceae family includes several important plant pathogens.
Researchers studied the life cycle of fungi in the Pythiaceae.
Diseases caused by Pythiaceae often affect crops and garden plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - Any group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter

Etymology

From the genus name Pythium, the type genus of the family, combined with the standard taxonomic suffix -aceae for families.
