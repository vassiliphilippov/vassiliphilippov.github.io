stamper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæmpər / (AmE), / ˈstæmpə / (BrE)
a machine or power tool used for stamping materials into a particular shape or pattern, typically by pressing or imprinting
power tool for stamping or imprinting

Examples

The factory used a stamper to shape the metal sheets quickly and efficiently.
After purchasing a new stamper, the company increased its production capacity.
A heavy-duty stamper is required to imprint the company's logo onto steel plates.

Synset Examples

a metal stamper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press - a machine that applies pressure to shape or imprint materials

Etymology

From 'stamp' + '-er', denoting one who or that which stamps.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstæmpər / (AmE), / ˈstæmpə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to shape or cut materials by pressing them with a mold or die, either manually or using a stamping machine
person who shapes or cuts material with a mold or die

Examples

The stamper ensured each metal sheet was pressed precisely to meet the specifications.
As a stamper in the factory, he operated heavy machinery to cut out parts for cars.
Experienced stampers are skilled at quickly switching between different molds for various products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamping machine operator - person who operates a stamping machine
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10666607-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈstæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæmpə / (BrE)
a person who walks with heavy, noisy steps or stamps on the ground
person who walks noisily or stamps

Examples

The upstairs neighbor is a notorious stamper, thumping along the hall every morning.
During the parade, the little boy became a cheerful stamper, stomping his feet in rhythm with the music.
If you want to sneak up on someone, don't be such a stamper!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - to bring one's foot down heavily on the ground

Etymology

From the verb 'stamp' (to strike the ground with the foot), plus the agent suffix '-er'.
