digenesis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
(Biology) The alternation of sexual and asexual generations in the life cycle of an organism.
alternation of sexual and asexual generations

Examples

Certain parasitic worms exhibit digenesis as part of their complex life cycles.
The study focused on the triggers that cause digenesis in protozoan organisms.
Understanding digenesis is important for controlling the spread of some infectious diseases.

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + genesis (origin, creation).
