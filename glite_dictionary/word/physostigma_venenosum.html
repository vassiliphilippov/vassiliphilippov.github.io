Physostigma venenosum

1. NOUN

/ faɪ.səˈstɪɡ.mə vəˈniː.nə.səm / (AmE), / fɪ.səˈstɪɡ.mə vɛˈnɛn.ə.səm / (BrE)
A tropical African woody vine producing Calabar beans, which contain the toxic compound physostigmine.
African vine producing Calabar beans

Examples

Physostigma venenosum is native to the tropical regions of West Africa.
Scientists extract physostigmine from the seeds of Physostigma venenosum for use in medicine.
The poisonous Calabar bean comes from the Physostigma venenosum plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calabar bean - seed of Physostigma venenosum, toxic and medicinal use

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'physa' (bladder) and 'stigma' (mark), and Latin 'venenosum' (poisonous).
