Philaenus spumarius

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈleɪnəs spjuːˈmɛəriəs / (AmE), / fɪˈleɪnɪs spjʊˈmɑːrɪəs / (BrE)
a widespread species of spittlebug found in North America and Europe, known for damaging grasses and various crops
a species of spittlebug damaging grasses

Examples

The Philaenus spumarius can cause significant damage to turfgrass and other crops.
Farmers monitor their fields for signs of Philaenus spumarius infestations each spring.
Researchers study the life cycle of Philaenus spumarius to find ways to control its spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow spittlebug - common name for Philaenus spumarius

Etymology

From Latin: 'Philaenus' is the genus name; 'spumarius' means 'of foam', referring to the insect's foam nests.
