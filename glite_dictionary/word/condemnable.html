condemnable

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdɛm.nə.bəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm.nə.bəl / (BrE)
deserving strong disapproval or severe criticism; worthy of being condemned
deserving strong disapproval

Examples

The company's failure to address the safety issues was truly condemnable.
Such neglect of the environment is condemnable in any responsible society.
Many considered his actions morally condemnable and demanded accountability.

Synset Examples

a criminal waste of talent
a deplorable act of violence
adultery is as reprehensible for a husband as for a wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprehensible - deserving blame or strong criticism
  • Synonym: deplorable - deserving strong condemnation; shockingly bad
  • Antonym: commendable - deserving praise or approval

Etymology

From Late Latin condemnabilis (worthy of condemnation), from Latin condemnare (to condemn).
