e

1. NOUN

/ i / (AmE), / iː / (BrE)
the fifth letter of the English alphabet
the letter E

Examples

The word 'elephant' begins with the letter e.
On her report card, she got an e in music.
Please write a capital E at the top of the page.

Etymology

From Latin letter E, from Greek epsilon.

2. NOUN

/ i / (AmE), / iː / (BrE)
the base of the natural logarithms, approximately equal to 2.71828
mathematical constant, base of natural logarithms

Examples

The mathematical constant e appears frequently in calculus and complex analysis.
Natural logarithms are taken to the base e.
Many growth processes, both in nature and finance, can be modeled using e.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pi - mathematical constant representing the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter

Etymology

From the initial letter of 'exponential', introduced by the mathematician Leonhard Euler.
