dialectician

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˌlɛkˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪə.lɛkˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a person skilled in dialectic, especially one adept at logical argumentation or debate
expert in dialectic or logical argumentation

Examples

The philosopher was known as a brilliant dialectician who excelled in public debates.
As a trained dialectician, she could quickly expose flaws in her opponent's reasoning.
Throughout history, many great thinkers have been admired as master dialecticians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logician - a person skilled in logic or logical reasoning

Etymology

From French dialecticien, based on Greek dialektikos (skilled in discussion).
