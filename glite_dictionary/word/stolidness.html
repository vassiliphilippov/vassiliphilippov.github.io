stolidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːlɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɒlɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing little or no emotion or sensitivity to pleasure or pain; impassiveness
lack of emotional response or sensitivity

Examples

His stolidness in the face of bad news surprised everyone at the meeting.
The stolidness of the guards made it impossible to guess what they were thinking.
She admired his stolidness during the crisis, but wished he would show some feeling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassiveness - lack of emotional expression or reaction
  • Antonym: sensitivity - capacity to respond to emotional or physical stimuli

Etymology

From Latin 'stolidus' (dull, stupid) + '-ness' (forming nouns).
