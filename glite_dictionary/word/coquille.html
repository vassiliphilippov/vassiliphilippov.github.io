coquille

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkiːl / (AmE), / kɒˈkiːj / (BrE)
A dish, often made of china or metal, shaped like a scallop shell and used especially for serving certain seafood dishes.
dish shaped like a scallop shell

Examples

The seafood was served in a beautiful coquille that resembled a real shell.
She bought a set of porcelain coquilles for her dinner parties.
The classic dish 'Coquilles Saint-Jacques' is traditionally baked in a coquille.

Etymology

From French 'coquille' (shell, especially of a scallop).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkiːl / (AmE), / kɒˈkiːl / (BrE)
A dish of seafood, often scallops, served in a scallop shell, typically with a creamy sauce and breadcrumbs.
seafood dish served in a scallop shell

Examples

The restaurant's signature coquille features scallops baked in a rich cream sauce.
He ordered a delicious coquille as a starter before the main course arrived.
Their version of coquille uses shrimp and mushrooms, topped with grated cheese.

Etymology

From French *coquille* (shell).
