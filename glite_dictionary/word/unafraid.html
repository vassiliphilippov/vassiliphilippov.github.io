unafraid

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
not feeling fear, or calmly facing danger or difficulty without being frightened.
not afraid; fearless

Examples

The children were unafraid to speak their minds at the meeting.
She stood unafraid in the face of the approaching storm.
Despite the risks, he felt completely unafraid of the challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearless - lacking fear; brave
  • Antonym: afraid - feeling fear or anxiety
  • Synonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'afraid' (from Old French 'afradier', from Vulgar Latin).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
not feeling fear; not afraid or worried about something
not feeling fear or worry

Examples

She walked into the dark alley completely unafraid of what might happen.
Despite criticism from others, he remained unafraid to voice his opinions.
The child was surprisingly unafraid of the thunderstorm.

Synset Examples

he was secure that nothing will be held against him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearless - without fear; very brave
  • Antonym: afraid - feeling fear or worry

Etymology

From un- (not) + afraid.
