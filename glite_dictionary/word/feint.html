feint

1. VERB

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
To make a deceptive or pretended movement, especially in order to trick an opponent.
deceive by a mock action

Examples

The boxer feinted left before delivering a punch to the right side.
She feinted a pass, causing her opponent to move out of position.
The chess player feinted an attack on the queen's side to distract his rival.

Synset Examples

The midfielder feinted to shoot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - to make a false movement to deceive an opponent

Etymology

From Old French *feint* (feigned), past participle of *feindre* (to feign), from Latin *fingere* (to shape, pretend).

2. NOUN

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
A deceptive or pretended movement, especially one made to distract an opponent in sports, combat, or games.
deceptive or distracting movement

Examples

The boxer used a quick feint to trick his opponent into lowering his guard.
During the chess match, she made a feint with her knight before launching her real attack.
The army's sudden feint on the eastern front diverted attention from their true objective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - A simulated or false action intended to deceive
  • Antonym: attack - A direct and genuine assault or offensive action

Etymology

From French 'feinte', from Old French, from feindre (to pretend), from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, to feign).

Synset POS: n
