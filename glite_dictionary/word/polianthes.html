Polianthes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈænθiːz / (AmE), / ˌpəʊliˈænθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial tuberous herbs native to Mexico, known for their lily-like fragrant flowers, sometimes placed in the family Amaryllidaceae.
genus of tuberous flowering plants

Examples

Polianthes is admired for its fragrant and attractive white blossoms.
Several species in the Polianthes genus are popular in ornamental gardening.
The best-known member of Polianthes is the tuberose, used in perfumery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuberose - a fragrant plant (Polianthes tuberosa) in this genus

Etymology

From Greek polios (white, referring to the flowers) + anthos (flower).

2. NOUN

a genus of perennial tuberous herbs native to Mexico, having lily-like flowers, sometimes classified in the family Amaryllidaceae
a genus of tuberous herbs with lily-like flowers

Examples

The most famous species in the genus Polianthes is the tuberose, widely grown for its fragrant flowers.
Botanists have debated whether to place Polianthes in the Amaryllidaceae family or in Agavaceae.
Many gardens feature plants from the Polianthes genus due to their attractive blossoms and sweet scent.

Etymology

From Greek 'polios' (white) and 'anthos' (flower).
