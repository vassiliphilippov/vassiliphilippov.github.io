callisthenics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləsˈθɛnɪks / (AmE), / ˌkælɪsˈθɛnɪks / (BrE)
physical exercises that use one's own body weight, usually performed rhythmically and with minimal equipment, to improve strength, flexibility, and overall fitness
body-weight fitness exercises

Examples

He starts every morning with a routine of callisthenics to stay fit.
The physical education class included twenty minutes of callisthenics before games.
Many people practice callisthenics because it requires little or no special equipment.

Synset Examples

several different calisthenics were illustrated in the video

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerobics - vigorous exercises to strengthen the heart and lungs
  • Synonym: gymnastics - physical exercises performed on apparatus or the floor

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'sthenos' (strength).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæləsˈθɛnɪks / (AmE), / ˌkælɪsˈθɛnɪks / (BrE)
exercises consisting of a variety of movements, often rhythmical and using body weight, intended to increase strength and flexibility
bodyweight fitness exercises

Examples

Daily callisthenics can help improve your balance, strength, and flexibility.
The coach introduced a new callisthenics routine to warm up the team.
Many people practice callisthenics at home without any special equipment.

Synset Examples

calisthenics is recommended for general good health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gymnastics - physical exercises designed to develop strength and coordination

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + sthenos (strength).
