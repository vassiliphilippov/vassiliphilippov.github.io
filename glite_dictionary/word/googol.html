googol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuː.ɡɔl / (AmE), / ˈɡuː.ɡɒl / (BrE)
a number equal to ten raised to the power of one hundred (1 followed by 100 zeros)
number equal to 10 to the 100th power

Examples

A googol is much larger than the number of atoms in the observable universe.
The term googol was invented to name an unimaginably large number.
If you wrote out a googol, it would take many pages to list all the zeros.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: googolplex - a number equal to 10 raised to the power of a googol

Etymology

Coined by nine-year-old Milton Sirotta, nephew of American mathematician Edward Kasner, in 1938.
