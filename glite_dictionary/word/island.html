island

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
A piece of land completely surrounded by water, smaller than a continent.
land mass surrounded by water

Examples

We visited a beautiful island in the Caribbean during our vacation.
Birds nest on the island every spring, returning year after year.
The city is connected to the island by a long suspension bridge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continent - a very large continuous landmass

Etymology

From Old English *īegland* (īeg ‘island’ + land ‘land’), reinforced by Old French *isle*. The silent 's' was influenced by the spelling of French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
An area of something that is surrounded by something else, but not necessarily water; a zone or patch resembling an island.
an area resembling an island

Examples

A forested island stood in the middle of the vast, open plain.
The city park featured an island of wildflowers amid the concrete paths.
Within the crowded marketplace, a calm island of benches offered a place to rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patch - a small area differing from its surroundings
