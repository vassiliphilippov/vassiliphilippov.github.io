chemiluminescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmiˌluːməˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌkɛmiˌljuːmɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the emission of light as a result of a chemical reaction, without significant heat
light emission from a chemical reaction
medical

Examples

The glowing bands in the experiment were produced by chemiluminescence.
Fireflies use chemiluminescence to attract mates on warm summer nights.
Some forensic methods detect blood traces through the chemiluminescence caused by certain chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bioluminescence - light emission by living organisms
  • Degree Lower: phosphorescence - light emission after exposure to radiation
  • Degree Lower: fluorescence - emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light

Etymology

From Latin 'chemia' (alchemy, chemistry) and 'luminescentia' (light emission).
