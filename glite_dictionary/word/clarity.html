clarity

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrəti / (AmE), / ˈklærəti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear, especially in appearance, such as water that is free from cloudiness, impurities, or turbidity
quality of being clear (appearance, e.g. water)

Examples

The clarity of the lake allowed us to see the fish swimming near the bottom.
After being filtered, the water achieved remarkable clarity and was safe to drink.
Marine biologists measured the clarity of the ocean to monitor environmental changes.

Synset Examples

when she awoke the clarity was back in her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearness - the quality of being clear, especially easy to see or understand

Etymology

From Latin claritas (clearness, brightness), from clarus (clear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæ.rə.ti / (AmE), / ˈklær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being clear and easy to understand.
quality of being clear and understandable

Examples

Her presentation was praised for its clarity and logic.
Legal documents should be written with clarity to avoid misunderstandings.
The clarity of the lake's water amazed the visitors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obscurity - the quality of being difficult to understand
  • Synonym: lucidity - clearness of thought or style

Etymology

From Latin claritas (clearness, brightness), from clarus (clear).
