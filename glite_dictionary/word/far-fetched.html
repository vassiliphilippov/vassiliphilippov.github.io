far-fetched

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑr ˌfɛtʃt / (AmE), / ˌfɑː ˈfɛtʃt / (BrE)
(of ideas, stories, or explanations) unlikely and unconvincing; difficult to believe because they are too improbable.
improbable or hard to believe

Examples

Her story about being late sounded a bit far-fetched to everyone in the office.
The scientist dismissed the hypothesis as too far-fetched to be taken seriously.
Although the movie's plot was entertaining, some scenes were simply too far-fetched to be believable.

Synset Examples

a farfetched excuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implausible - not seeming reasonable or probable; failing to convince
  • Antonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing

Etymology

From 'far' + 'fetch', originally meaning brought from a distance, later figuratively extended to unlikely ideas.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrˌfɛtʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɑːˌfɛtʃt / (BrE)
(of an idea or story) very unlikely and difficult to believe; improbable or exaggerated to the point of seeming unrealistic.
very unlikely; improbable or unrealistic

Examples

Her excuse for being late sounded rather far-fetched to the teacher.
The story about aliens stealing his homework is clearly far-fetched.
Some of the movie's plot twists were just too far-fetched to take seriously.

Synset Examples

He was always full of far-fetched plans, none of which ever worked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implausible - not seeming reasonable or probable; failing to convince
  • Antonym: believable - able to be believed; credible

Etymology

From 'far' + 'fetched', originally meaning 'brought from afar', later used figuratively for unlikely stories.
