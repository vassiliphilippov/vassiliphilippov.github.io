reflexive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈflɛksɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv / (BrE)
(of an action or response) occurring automatically without conscious thought or volition, especially as part of a physiological reaction.
automatic, unconscious response

Examples

Sneezing is often a reflexive response to irritants in the nose.
Pupils contract in a reflexive manner when exposed to bright light.
Her reflexive flinch saved her from the flying ball.

Synset Examples

the automatic shrinking of the pupils of the eye in strong light
a reflex knee jerk
sneezing is reflexive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - (of an action) done spontaneously or unconsciously
  • Antonym: voluntary - done by intention or conscious decision

Etymology

From New Latin reflexivus (from Latin reflectere, to bend back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛksɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv / (BrE)
a pronoun formed with '-self' or '-selves' (such as 'myself', 'yourself'), used to show that the subject of the verb is also its object
a pronoun like 'myself' or 'himself'

Examples

In the sentence 'She blamed herself,' the pronoun is a reflexive.
The word 'themselves' is a reflexive used for plural subjects.
English learners often confuse when to use a reflexive instead of a regular pronoun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflexive pronoun - a pronoun referring back to the subject (e.g. himself, myself)

Etymology

From Latin reflexivus (bent back).

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈflɛksɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase, especially a pronoun) referring back to the subject of the clause or sentence; (of an action or process) directed back on itself.
referring back to itself; self-referential

Examples

In the sentence "She taught herself," "herself" is a reflexive pronoun.
Some verbs, like "enjoy oneself," are considered reflexive because they refer back to the subject.
The entire process became reflexive as each step influenced the next in a feedback loop.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reciprocal - (of pronouns or actions) indicating mutual relation or action

Etymology

From Late Latin reflexivus (turned back), from Latin reflectere (to bend back).

Source: WordNet
