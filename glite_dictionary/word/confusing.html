confusing

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone to feel bewildered or unclear; difficult to understand or follow.
causing confusion; hard to understand

Examples

The new software interface is quite confusing for first-time users.
All the different instructions made the task more confusing than it needed to be.
I found the plot of the movie extremely confusing and hard to follow.

Synset Examples

a confusing jumble of road signs
being hospitalized can be confusing and distressing for a small child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; not confusing
  • Synonym: perplexing - causing confusion or puzzlement

Etymology

From 'confuse' + -ing, derived from Latin 'confundere' (to mingle together).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
not clear; difficult to understand and likely to cause confusion or misunderstanding
difficult to understand; unclear

Examples

The instructions were so confusing that nobody knew what to do next.
Her explanation of the policy was confusing and left everyone asking questions.
I found the plot of the movie confusing, especially in the second half.

Synset Examples

sent confusing signals to Iraq
perplexing to someone who knew nothing about it
a puzzling statement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; not confusing
  • Synonym: puzzling - causing one to feel confused or perplexed

Etymology

From confuse + -ing, formed from Latin 'confundere' (to mingle together) and the English present participle suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00432472-s
Synset POS: s
