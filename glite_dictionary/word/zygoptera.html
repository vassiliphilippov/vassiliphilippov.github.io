Zygoptera

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈɡɑptərə / (AmE), / zaɪˈɡɒptərə / (BrE)
the suborder of insects comprising the damselflies, characterized by slender bodies and wings of equal size and shape
suborder of insects: damselflies
medical

Examples

Scientists observed several species of Zygoptera near the riverbank.
Unlike dragonflies, members of Zygoptera hold their wings together when at rest.
The colorful wings of Zygoptera make them easy to spot in wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Anisoptera - the suborder of insects comprising dragonflies

Etymology

From Greek zygon (yoke) + pteron (wing), referring to the paired, similarly shaped wings.
