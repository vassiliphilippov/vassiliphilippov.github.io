Trapa natans

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪpə ˈneɪtænz / (AmE), / ˈtreɪpə ˈneɪtænz / (BrE)
A species of aquatic plant, commonly known as water chestnut, with floating leaves and edible seeds, native to Eurasia and Africa.
species of water chestnut (aquatic plant)

Examples

Lakes overrun with Trapa natans can suffer from reduced oxygen levels and decreased biodiversity.
The seeds of Trapa natans are edible and have been used as a food source in Asia for centuries.
Researchers study how Trapa natans spreads quickly in freshwater environments, threatening native plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water chestnut - aquatic plant producing edible nuts, sometimes used for Trapa species

Etymology

From Latin: 'trapa' (a kind of caltrop), 'natans' (swimming or floating).
