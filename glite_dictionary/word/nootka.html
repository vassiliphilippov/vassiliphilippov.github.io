Nootka

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtkə / (AmE), / ˈnuːtkə / (BrE)
A member of the Wakashan Indigenous people living on Vancouver Island and in the Cape Flattery region of northwestern Washington.
a member of the Wakashan Indigenous people

Examples

The Nootka have a rich tradition of whale hunting and cedar carving.
Anthropologists have studied the culture of the Nootka for more than a century.
Many Nootka now refer to themselves as Nuu-chah-nulth.

Etymology

From Nuu-chah-nulth, their own name for themselves, originally given as 'Nootka' by early European explorers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtkə / (AmE), / ˈnuːtkə / (BrE)
the Wakashan language spoken by the Nootka people of Vancouver Island and the surrounding area in Canada
the Wakashan language of the Nootka people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Nootka, but efforts are underway to revitalize the language.
The museum displays several artifacts labeled in both English and Nootka.
Linguists have studied the grammar of Nootka to better understand the structure of Wakashan languages.

Etymology

From the name of the Nootka people, likely from the indigenous Nuu-chah-nulth term.
