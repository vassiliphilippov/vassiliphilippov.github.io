peristylar

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrɪˈstaɪlər / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈstɪlə / (BrE)
(of a building or area) having a row of columns completely surrounding it.
having columns all around (architecture)

Examples

The ancient temple was peristylar, with elegant columns enclosing the central shrine.
The architect designed a peristylar courtyard to evoke classical traditions.
Each side of the peristylar building was lined with marble pillars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peristyle - a row of columns surrounding a space or building

Etymology

From Greek peristylon (colonnade all around); peri- (around) + stylos (pillar).
