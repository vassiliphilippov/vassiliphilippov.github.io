addict

1. VERB

/ əˈdɪkt / (AmE), / əˈdɪkt / (BrE)
to cause someone or oneself to become dependent on a substance or activity, especially a narcotic drug
cause to become dependent or hooked

Examples

Many painkillers can addict patients if not taken as prescribed.
She never imagined a simple prescription would addict her to opioids.
He worried that video games might addict his younger brother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hook - to cause someone to become addicted or strongly attracted to something

Etymology

From Latin 'addictus', meaning 'assigned, devoted to'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01167606-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈædɪkt / (AmE), / ˈadɪkt / (BrE)
a person who is unable to stop using a substance or engaging in an activity, especially one that is harmful, due to a physical or psychological dependence
person dependent on substance or activity

Examples

After years of substance abuse, he finally admitted he was an addict.
Many former addicts credit support groups with helping them recover.
She became an addict after experimenting with prescription painkillers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependent - a person who relies on or needs something
  • Antonym: abstainer - a person who deliberately avoids something, especially alcohol or drugs

Etymology

From Latin addictus, meaning 'assigned, devoted', originally referring to someone given over as a slave to a creditor.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09788253-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈædɪkt / (AmE), / ˈædɪkt / (BrE)
a person who is extremely enthusiastic about a particular activity or interest, often in a way that is compared to addiction
person overly devoted to an activity

Examples

My brother is a real addict when it comes to video games; he can play for hours on end.
She's become a fitness addict since joining the new gym downtown.
As a news addict, he checks his phone for updates every few minutes.

Synset Examples

a golf addict
a car nut
a bodybuilding freak
a news junkie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is highly interested in a particular activity or subject
  • Synonym: fanatic - a person filled with excessive and single-minded zeal
  • Degree Lower: casual - a person with a mild interest, not passionate

Etymology

From Latin addictus, past participle of addicere (to devote, surrender).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09788499-n
Synset POS: n
