cruse

1. NOUN

/ kruːz / (AmE), / kruːz / (BrE)
a small jar or container used to hold liquids, especially oil or water
small jar for holding liquid
archaic

Examples

The widow's cruse of oil miraculously never ran dry.
He poured water gently from a cruse onto the thirsty plant.
An ancient cruse was discovered during the archaeological dig.

Etymology

From Old English 'crūs' (pot, pitcher), related to Old Norse 'krúsa' (jug).
