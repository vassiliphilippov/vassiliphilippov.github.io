batter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætər / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
a player in baseball who is currently batting, trying to hit the ball pitched by the pitcher
baseball player who is batting

Examples

The batter stepped up to the plate, ready to face the next pitch.
Each batter on the team gets a chance during the inning.
The crowd cheered as the batter hit a home run into the stands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pitcher - the baseball player who throws the ball to the batter
  • Synonym: hitter - another term for a player who hits the ball in baseball
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09863364-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
a liquid or semiliquid mixture of ingredients such as flour, eggs, and milk, used in cooking, especially for making cakes, pancakes, or coating foods before frying.
mixture for baking or frying

Examples

She poured the pancake batter onto the hot griddle.
Beer is sometimes added to fish batter to make it crispier.
Let the batter rest for ten minutes before frying the fritters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dough - thick, malleable mixture of flour and liquid used for baking bread or pastries

Etymology

From Old French *batre* (to beat), later influenced by Middle English, referring to the mixture being beaten.

3. VERB

/ ˈbæɾɚ / (AmE), / ˈbatə / (BrE)
to damage by making dents or impressions on a surface, typically through repeated hitting or striking
damage by making dents or impressions

Examples

The hail battered the car roof, leaving lots of small dents.
He battered the old suitcase until its corners were misshapen.
Years of storms have battered the wooden fence, making it look worn and dented.

Synset Examples

dinge a soft hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dent - to make a hollow or impression in the surface of something
  • Synonym: bruise - to injure or mark by striking or pressure

Etymology

From Middle English 'batre', from Old French 'batre', from Latin 'battuere' (to beat).

/ ˈbætər / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
to strike repeatedly and forcefully; to hit with heavy blows

Examples

She watched the rain battering against the window.

6. VERB

/ ˈbætər / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
to cover food, especially meat or vegetables, with a mixture of flour, eggs, and liquid before cooking it, typically by frying.
coat food with batter before cooking

Examples

Before frying, make sure to batter the chicken thoroughly for a crispier texture.
Many recipes instruct cooks to batter the chicken before deep-frying.
He forgot to batter the chicken, so the coating didn't stick well.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover something with a layer of a substance

Etymology

From Old French 'batre' (to beat), from Latin 'battuere'.
