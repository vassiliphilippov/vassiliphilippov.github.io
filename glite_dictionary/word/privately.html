privately

1. ADV

/ ˈpraɪvətli / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvətli / (BrE)
by or involving individuals or organizations that are not part of the government; by private persons or interests rather than public authorities
by a private person or organization

Examples

The company was privately owned before it went public.
Many hospitals are run privately rather than by the state.
The museum's new wing was privately funded through donations.

Synset Examples

a privately financed campaign

Related Terms

  • Antonym: publicly - by or involving the government or the public

Etymology

From private + -ly, parallel to public/publicly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163844-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈpraɪvətli / (AmE), / ˈprɪvətli / (BrE)
in a way that is intended to be secret or not shared with other people; not publicly
not openly; in private

Examples

The matter was discussed privately between the director and the manager.
She admitted privately that she was nervous about the decision.
Although they smiled in public, privately they resented each other.

Synset Examples

it was discussed privately between the two men
privately, she thought differently
some member of his own party hoped privately for his defeat
he was questioned in private

Related Terms

  • Antonym: publicly - openly; in a public or open manner

Etymology

From 'private' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'privatus' (set apart).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163336-r
Synset POS: r
