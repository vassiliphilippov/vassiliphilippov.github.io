morocco

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑkoʊ / (AmE), / məˈrɒkəʊ / (BrE)
A soft, durable leather made from goatskin, typically with a pebble-grained finish, used especially for making shoes and book bindings.
high-quality goatskin leather

Examples

The old book was bound in elegant morocco that had deepened in color with age.
He ordered a pair of custom shoes made from fine red morocco.
Collectors prize rare volumes still covered in original morocco leather.

Etymology

From the country name Morocco, where this type of leather was originally produced or exported.
