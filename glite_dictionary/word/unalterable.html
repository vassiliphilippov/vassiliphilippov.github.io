unalterable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔltərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːltərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to change or modify; not able to be altered.
impossible to change

Examples

The judge handed down an unalterable sentence that could not be appealed.
Some traditions have become so deeply rooted that they seem unalterable.
Her decision was firm and unalterable, no matter what arguments were made.

Synset Examples

an unalterable death sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutable - unable to be changed
  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed or altered

Etymology

From un- (not) + alterable, from Latin alterare (to change).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔltɚəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːltərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to change or modify; remaining the same over time.
impossible to change

Examples

His views on tradition were unalterable, no matter how much anyone argued.
The constitution contains certain unalterable provisions to protect individual rights.
She made an unalterable decision to pursue her dream of becoming a doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invariable - never changing; always the same
  • Synonym: immutable - unable to be changed
  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed

Etymology

From un- (not) + alterable (able to be changed), from Latin 'alterare' (to change).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔltərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːltərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to change or alter; fixed and unchangeable
impossible to change

Examples

His unalterable decision surprised his friends and family.
The document's terms are unalterable without the agreement of both parties.
She faced her fate with unalterable calm and dignity.

Synset Examples

unalterable resolve
an unalterable ground rule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutable - not able to be changed; unchangeable
  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed; not fixed

Etymology

From un- (not) + alterable, itself from Latin 'alterare' (to change).

Source: WordNet
