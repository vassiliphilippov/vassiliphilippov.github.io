holloa

1. NOUN

/ həˈloʊ / (AmE), / həˈlɔː / (BrE)
a loud shout or cry used to attract attention, often associated with calling out to someone or animals
a loud shout or cry
archaic

Examples

The huntsman gave a loud holloa to signal the start of the chase.
She heard a distant holloa echoing through the woods.
With a sudden holloa, he tried to get their attention across the field.

Synset Examples

his bellow filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halloo - a shout to attract attention or encourage (esp. hunting dogs)

Etymology

Alteration of 'halloo' or 'hallo', likely from Old French 'haler' (to haul, pull), originally used to urge dogs on in the hunt.
