zalcitabine

1. NOUN

/ zælˈsɪtəbiːn / (AmE), / zælˈsɪtəbiːn / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection; a nucleoside analog reverse transcriptase inhibitor formerly used in antiretroviral therapy
antiviral drug for HIV treatment (NARTI)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed zalcitabine as part of a combination therapy for his HIV-positive patients.
Researchers studied the long-term effects of zalcitabine on immune system function.
Due to side effects, zalcitabine is now rarely used in modern HIV treatment protocols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: didanosine - an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection; also a nucleoside analog reverse transcriptase inhibitor
  • Synonym: stavudine - an antiviral drug used to treat HIV; a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor

Etymology

Blend of chemical elements from its structure and 'cytidine' (a nucleoside); introduced as a drug name in the 1990s.
