Quercus borealis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs bɔːˈrɪːəlɪs / (BrE)
A large deciduous oak tree native to eastern North America, also known as the northern red oak, with broad leaves featuring spiny-tipped lobes and coarse-grained wood.
species of northern red oak tree

Examples

The Quercus borealis is prized for its shade and autumn foliage in many parks across the northeastern United States.
Furniture made from Quercus borealis is attractive but generally less durable than that from white oak species.
Leaf identification guides often highlight the pointed lobes of the Quercus borealis as a distinguishing feature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern red oak - common name for Quercus borealis
  • Synonym: Quercus rubra - often used as a synonym in taxonomy

Etymology

From Latin: 'quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'borealis' meaning 'northern'.
