Spizella

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈzɛlə / (AmE), / spɪˈzɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small North American sparrows, including the chipping sparrow and field sparrow.
genus of North American sparrows

Examples

The Spizella genus includes several familiar backyard birds, such as the chipping sparrow.
Scientists examined the habitat preferences of three Spizella species in different regions.
Because Spizella sparrows often look alike, careful observation is needed for accurate identification.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'spiza' (a kind of finch) and Latin diminutive '-ella'.
