trunk

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋk / (AmE), / trʌŋk / (BrE)
the enclosed compartment at the back of a car, used for storing luggage, shopping, or tools
storage compartment at back of a car
american

Examples

He put his suitcase in the trunk before leaving for the airport.
After grocery shopping, she loaded the bags into the trunk of her car.
The spare tire is stored in the trunk along with the jack and tools.

Synset Examples

he put his golf bag in the trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boot - the enclosed compartment at the back of a car used for storage (British English)

Etymology

From Latin truncus (stem, trunk of a tree), later used for main body or box; applied to vehicles in US in early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ trʌŋk / (AmE), / trʌŋk / (BrE)
the main woody stem of a tree, typically covered with bark and supporting the branches and leaves
main stem of a tree

Examples

The old oak's trunk was thick and deeply grooved with age.
The logger measured the trunk before preparing to cut it for lumber.
Moss and fungi grew along the base of the trunk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bole - the main stem of a tree, especially the trunk

Etymology

From Old French 'tronc', from Latin 'truncus' meaning 'stem, stock, trunk of a tree'.
