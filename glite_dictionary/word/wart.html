wart

1. NOUN

/ wɔrt / (AmE), / wɔːt / (BrE)
A small, hard, raised growth on the skin, typically caused by a virus.
small hard skin growth

Examples

He went to the doctor to have a wart removed from his finger.
Children sometimes develop warts on their hands and feet.
A wart can be contagious if touched or scratched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw on the skin or surface

Etymology

Old English *wearte*, related to Dutch *wrat* and German *Warze*.

2. NOUN

/ wɔrt / (AmE), / wɔːt / (BrE)
a small, hard, rounded lump that grows on the skin or on the surface of some plants and animals
small rounded lump on skin or plant

Examples

He found a small wart on the surface of the leaf while gardening.
Some frogs have visible warts on their backs as a natural feature.
The dermatologist examined the wart to determine if it needed to be removed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bump - a round swelling on a surface; a lump
  • Synonym: nodule - a small swelling or aggregation of cells in the body or on a plant

Etymology

From Old English *wearte*, related to Dutch *wrat* and German *Warze* (wart).

3. NOUN

/ wɔrt / (AmE), / wɔːt / (BrE)
a small imperfection or unattractive feature in someone or something, especially when likened to the growth on skin
small imperfection or unattractive feature

Examples

Despite its many successes, the new system has a few warts that need to be addressed.
She loves him, warts and all, accepting every little wart in his character.
The novel's plot was compelling, though critics noted several warts in the narrative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaw - an imperfection or weakness in something that makes it less effective or valuable
  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or imperfection that makes something less beautiful or perfect
  • Antonym: virtue - a good or admirable quality

Etymology

Old English *wearte* (hard growth, wart), related to Old High German *warza*.

4. NOUN

/ wɔrt / (AmE), / wɔːt / (BrE)
a small, hard, benign growth on the skin caused by a virus
small growth on the skin

Examples

The doctor examined the wart and suggested a simple treatment.
She decided to have the wart on her hand removed.
Warts can appear anywhere on the body, but my wart was on my knee.

Synset Examples

He was embarrassed by the wart on his finger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mole - a small, dark skin mark or growth, not caused by a virus
  • Synonym: verruca - a wart, especially on the sole of the foot
