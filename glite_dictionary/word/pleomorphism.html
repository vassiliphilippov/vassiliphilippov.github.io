pleomorphism

1. NOUN

/ ˌpliːəˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpliːəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
the property of a chemical compound to exist in more than one crystalline form
existence of multiple crystal forms of a compound
medical

Examples

Some minerals exhibit pleomorphism, forming distinct crystal shapes under different environmental conditions.
The pleomorphism of this compound was studied using advanced imaging techniques.
Researchers were surprised by the degree of pleomorphism found in the synthetic crystals.

Etymology

From Greek pleo- (more) + -morphism (form).

2. NOUN

/ ˌplioʊˈmɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpliːəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
(biology) The occurrence of two or more distinct forms by the same organism during its life cycle.
existence of multiple forms in an organism's life cycle
medical

Examples

The fungus exhibits pleomorphism, alternating between yeast and mold forms.
Certain bacteria show pleomorphism under different environmental conditions.
Understanding pleomorphism is important for diagnosing some infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymorphism - the occurrence of different forms among individuals of a species

Etymology

From Greek pleios (more) + morphē (form).
