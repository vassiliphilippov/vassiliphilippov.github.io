dysphonia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈfoʊniə / (AmE), / dɪsˈfəʊniə / (BrE)
A disorder of the voice characterized by difficulty in speaking due to problems with phonation.
voice disorder; difficulty speaking
medical

Examples

The singer struggled with dysphonia after her illness, making it hard to perform.
His dysphonia was particularly noticeable during stressful situations when his voice became very weak.
After months of vocal therapy, her dysphonia gradually improved.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aphonia - loss of voice; inability to produce voice
  • Synonym: hoarseness - rough or harsh sounding voice

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'phōnē' (voice).
