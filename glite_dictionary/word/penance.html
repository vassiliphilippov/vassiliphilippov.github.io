penance

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəns / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəns / (BrE)
a feeling of regret or remorse for one's past actions, often expressed by undertaking an act of self-punishment or self-discipline
regret for past actions; act of self-punishment

Examples

He did extra volunteer work at the shelter as a form of penance for his mistakes.
She felt a deep penance after realizing how her actions had hurt her friend.
As penance, he apologized and promised to change his behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: repentance - sincere regret or remorse for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old French *penance*, from Latin *paenitentia* (repentance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəns / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəns / (BrE)
An act or expression of self-punishment carried out to show repentance or atone for wrongdoing, especially as a religious duty.
voluntary self-punishment for atonement

Examples

As penance for his mistake, he volunteered at the shelter every weekend for a month.
She walked barefoot to the shrine as an act of penance.
He confessed his lies and performed penance as instructed by the priest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atonement - the act of making amends for wrongdoing
  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old French *penance*, from Latin *paenitentia* (repentance).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəns / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəns / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a sacrament in which a member confesses sins to a priest and receives absolution and spiritual guidance for repentance and atonement.
Catholic sacrament of confession and absolution

Examples

After committing the sin, he went to church to perform penance.
The sacrament of penance offers Catholics a path to forgiveness and spiritual renewal.
She confessed her wrongdoing to the priest and received penance as part of her atonement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - the act of admitting sins, especially in a religious context
  • Synonym: absolution - formal forgiveness of sins, especially by a priest

Etymology

From Old French *penance*, from Latin *paenitentia* (repentance).
