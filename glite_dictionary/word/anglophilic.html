Anglophilic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæŋɡloʊˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
Liking or admiring England, its people, or its culture.
liking or admiring England or its culture

Examples

His Anglophilic tendencies were apparent in his love of British literature and afternoon tea.
The Anglophilic atmosphere of the café attracted many expatriates living in London.
She decorated her apartment in an Anglophilic style, complete with Union Jack pillows and vintage London posters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglophile - a person who admires England or English customs
  • Antonym: Anglophobic - showing dislike or fear of England or English customs

Etymology

Formed from 'Anglo-' (relating to England or English) and '-philic' (having a fondness for).
