misconduct

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈkɑnˌdʌkt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkɒndʌkt / (BrE)
improper or dishonest behaviour, especially by a person in a position of responsibility or trust
improper or dishonest behaviour

Examples

The committee is investigating allegations of misconduct by senior executives.
Financial misconduct led to the dismissal of several employees.
There were reports of misconduct within the organization that damaged its reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malfeasance - wrongdoing by a public official
  • Antonym: probity - the quality of having strong moral principles

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + conduct, influenced by Latin roots con- (together) and ducere (to lead).

2. NOUN

/ mɪsˈkɑn.dʌkt / (AmE), / mɪsˈkɒn.dʌkt / (BrE)
Unacceptable or improper behavior, especially by a professional person or in a public office; activity that violates moral or civil law.
improper or unlawful behavior

Examples

The company was fined after an investigation revealed misconduct by several employees.
Allegations of academic misconduct led to the professor's suspension.
She was accused of professional misconduct for violating ethical guidelines.

Synset Examples

he denied any wrongdoing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrongdoing - illegal or immoral behavior
  • Antonym: propriety - behavior that is correct and socially acceptable

Etymology

From mis- (expressing negation) + conduct, from Latin 'conductus' (led, guided).
