Kentucky

1. PROPN

/ kənˈtʌki / (AmE), / kənˈtʌki / (BrE)
A state in the east central United States, known for its horse breeding and as a former border state during the American Civil War.
a U.S. state in east central United States
american american

Examples

The Kentucky Derby is one of the most prestigious horse races in the world.
We drove through Kentucky on our way to Tennessee last summer.
Kentucky has a rich tradition of bluegrass music and scenic landscapes.

Etymology

Named after the Kentucky River, possibly from an Iroquoian word meaning 'meadow' or 'prairie'.
