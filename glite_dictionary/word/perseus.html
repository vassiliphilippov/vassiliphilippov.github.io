Perseus

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɜr.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.sɪ.əs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the son of Zeus who beheaded Medusa and rescued Andromeda from a sea monster.
Greek mythological hero, slayer of Medusa

Examples

In the myth, Perseus used a mirrored shield to defeat Medusa.
Many ancient artworks depict Perseus rescuing Andromeda from the sea monster.
The constellation Perseus is named after the legendary Greek hero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Medusa - A Gorgon slain by Perseus in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Andromeda - Princess rescued by Perseus in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Zeus - King of the Greek gods, father of Perseus

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Περσεύς (Perseús).

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɜr.sjuːs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.sjuːs / (BrE)
a conspicuous constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, situated between Auriga and Cassiopeia and crossed by the Milky Way
a constellation in the northern sky

Examples

The constellation Perseus is best viewed during the autumn months in the Northern Hemisphere.
Many amateur astronomers search for the Perseid meteor shower, which appears to radiate from Perseus.
Legend says that Perseus was named after the Greek hero who slew Medusa.

Etymology

From Latin Perseus, from the Greek mythological hero Perseús.
