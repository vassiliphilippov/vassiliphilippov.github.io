mickle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪkəl / (BrE)
A large amount or great quantity (now chiefly archaic or literary).
large amount; great quantity (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The project required a mickle of patience and determination.
He inherited a mickle of land from his ancestors, stretching as far as the eye could see.
In days of old, villagers would gather and share a mickle of grain during the harvest festival.

Etymology

From Old English micel (great, much), related to Old Norse mikill (great).
