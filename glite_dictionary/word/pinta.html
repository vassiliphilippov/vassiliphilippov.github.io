pinta

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.tə / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.tə / (BrE)
a pint-sized serving of a beverage, especially beer or, informally, ice cream
pint-sized serving (beer or ice cream)
informal british

Examples

He ordered a pinta of beer at the local pub.
They bought a pinta of ice cream to share after dinner.
After work, she always enjoys a pinta with her friends.

Synset Examples

He ordered a pinta of beer during the game.
We shared a large pinta of ice cream on hot days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pint - a unit of volume, especially for liquids, equal to 0.568 litre in the UK or 0.473 litre in the US
  • Degree Lower: half-pint - a serving or container holding half the amount of a pint

Etymology

Variant of 'pint' (from Old French 'pinte'), commonly used in British and Irish English.
