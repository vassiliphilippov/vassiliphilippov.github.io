to pursue

1. VERB

/ pərˈsu / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː / (BrE)
to carry out or be involved in an activity, interest, or course of action over a period of time
carry out or participate in an activity

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in medicine despite the challenges.
Many students pursue extracurricular activities outside of their academic work.
He spent years pursuing his interest in classical music.

Synset Examples

She pursued many activities
They engaged in a discussion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engage in - take part in or become involved in an activity
  • Antonym: abandon - to stop doing or supporting an activity

Etymology

From Old French 'poursuir', from Latin 'prosequi' (to follow, accompany).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02380222-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pərˈsu / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː / (BrE)
to follow someone or something in order to catch or attack them, or to go after someone or something with determination
to follow in order to catch or chase

Examples

The detectives decided to pursue the fleeing suspect through the crowded streets.
For years, the young athlete pursued his dream of becoming an Olympic champion.
The dog barked loudly as it pursued the squirrel across the yard.

Synset Examples

The police car pursued the suspected attacker
Her bad deed followed her and haunted her dreams all her life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow - move behind someone or something
  • Antonym: to flee - run away from someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *poursuivre*, from Latin *prosequi* (to follow, pursue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02004900-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pərˈsu / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː / (BrE)
to follow or search for something with the purpose of achieving or obtaining it
seek to achieve or obtain

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in medicine despite the many challenges.
The leopard continued to pursue its prey through the dense jungle.
He moved to New York to pursue his dream of becoming an artist.

Synset Examples

pursue a hobby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch or catch up with
  • Synonym: to follow - to move behind or go after someone or something
  • Antonym: abandon - to give up completely; to stop pursuing

Etymology

From Old French *poursuir*, from Latin *prosequi* (to follow, accompany).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01320193-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pərˈsu / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː / (BrE)
to carry out or continue with (an activity, plan, or investigation); to advance something further
carry out or advance something

Examples

The committee decided to pursue the investigation despite the obstacles.
She hopes to pursue a career in scientific research.
We must pursue all possible options before making a final decision.

Synset Examples

Can you act on this matter soon?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or process
  • Antonym: abandon - to give up or stop pursuing something

Etymology

From Old French 'poursuir', from Latin 'prosequi' (to follow, to pursue).
