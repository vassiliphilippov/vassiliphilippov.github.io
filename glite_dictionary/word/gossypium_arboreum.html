Gossypium arboreum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɑːˈsɪpiəm ˌɑːrbɔːˈriəm / (AmE), / ˌɡɒsɪpiəm ˌɑːbərˈiːəm / (BrE)
a species of cotton plant from South Asia, cultivated for its pale yellow to deep purple blossoms and sometimes used ornamentally
a species of Indian cotton plant

Examples

The gardeners planted Gossypium arboreum for its striking purple flowers.
Gossypium arboreum is valued in India both for its beauty and its cotton fibers.
She admired the lush blossoms of Gossypium arboreum in the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton plant - a plant producing cotton fibers, Genus: Gossypium

Etymology

From Latin 'gossypium' (cotton) and 'arboreum' (tree-like).
