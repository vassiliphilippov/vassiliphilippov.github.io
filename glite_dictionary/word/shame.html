shame

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ʃeɪm / (BrE)
an unfortunate or disappointing situation; something to be regretted
an unfortunate or regrettable situation

Examples

It's a shame that we missed the beginning of the concert.
What a shame it rained on your wedding day!
It would be a real shame to throw away such a beautiful vase.

Synset Examples

it's a pity he couldn't do it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pity - a cause for regret or disappointment
  • Degree Higher: tragedy - an event causing great suffering
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ʃeɪm / (BrE)
to bring shame or dishonor upon someone or something
bring shame or dishonor upon

Examples

He was shamed by his actions and could not face his friends.
Their betrayal shamed the country and angered its people.

Synset Examples

he dishonored his family by committing a serious crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonor - to bring disgrace or loss of respect to someone or something

5. NOUN

/ ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ʃeɪm / (BrE)
A painful feeling or state caused by a loss of respect, honor, or disgrace.
state of dishonor or disgrace

Examples

The scandal brought deep shame upon the entire organization.
He felt a sense of shame after betraying his friend's trust.
There is no shame in admitting that you made a mistake.

Synset Examples

one mistake brought shame to all his family
suffered the ignominy of being sent to prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect, especially as the result of a dishonorable action
  • Antonym: honor - high respect or great esteem; the opposite state of respect or dignity

Etymology

Old English scamu, sceamu (shame, disgrace); related to Old High German scama.

7. NOUN

/ ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ʃeɪm / (BrE)
A painful feeling of distress or humiliation caused by the awareness of wrong or foolish behavior.
painful emotion from guilt or inadequacy

Examples

He felt a deep shame after lying to his friends.
There is no shame in admitting you made a mistake.
Her face flushed with shame when she realized her error.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness or awkwardness
  • Synonym: guilt - a feeling of responsibility or remorse for an offense
  • Antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's achievements

Etymology

From Old English 'scamu', related to Proto-Germanic 'skamo' (shame).
