Doolittle

1. PROPN

/ ˈduˌlɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈduːlɪtl / (BrE)
(James Harold Doolittle, 1896–1993) United States Air Force general and aviation pioneer who led the famous 1942 Doolittle Raid on Tokyo during World War II.
American general and aviation pioneer

Examples

The daring raid led by Doolittle boosted American morale during World War II.
General Doolittle was awarded the Medal of Honor for his leadership and bravery.
Many books and films have chronicled the achievements of Doolittle and his squadron.
