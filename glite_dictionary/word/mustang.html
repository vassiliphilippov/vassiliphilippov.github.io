mustang

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstæŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌstæŋ / (BrE)
A small, hardy wild horse of the western plains of North America, descended from horses brought by the Spanish.
wild horse of North America

Examples

Wild mustangs still roam the open plains in some parts of the western United States.
She photographed a herd of mustangs running freely across the desert.
The mustang is known for its stamina and ability to survive harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - a large domesticated animal with solid hooves and a flowing mane and tail

Etymology

From Spanish 'mestengo' or 'mesteño' meaning 'stray animal', from 'mesta' (association of cattle owners).
