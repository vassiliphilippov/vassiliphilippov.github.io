apophysis

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːfəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈpɒfɪsɪs / (BrE)
(Botany) A natural swelling or enlargement at the base of the stalk or seta in certain mosses, or on the cone scale of some conifers.
botanical swelling or enlargement

Examples

The apophysis at the base of the moss capsule is often bulbous.
In some pine cones, a prominent apophysis appears on each scale as the cone matures.
The botanist noted the presence of a large apophysis on the specimen under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek apophysis (a growth), from apo- (away) + physis (growth, nature).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑfəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈpɒfəsɪs / (BrE)
A natural outgrowth or projection from an organ or body part, especially a process of a bone such as a vertebra.
outgrowth or projection on a bone
medical

Examples

The apophysis of the vertebra serves as an attachment point for muscles.
Damage to the lateral apophysis can affect joint movement in adolescents.
On the X-ray, the doctor identified a detached apophysis near the knee.

Etymology

From Greek apophysis (a growth, offshoot), from apo- (away from) + physis (growth).
