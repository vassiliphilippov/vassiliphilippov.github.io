crystal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.təl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.təl / (BrE)
the transparent protective cover over the face of a watch or clock
protective cover of a watch face

Examples

The crystal on my watch got scratched when I dropped it.
Many luxury watches use sapphire crystal to protect their faces.
He replaced the broken crystal to restore the watch to its original appearance.

Etymology

From Greek *krystallos* (ice, crystal), referring to the transparency of early covers, often made from rock crystal.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl / (BrE)
A solid substance in which the atoms are arranged in a highly ordered, repeating pattern, often resulting in a regular geometric shape.
solid with a regular atomic structure

Examples

Salt forms beautiful crystals when water evaporates from a solution.
A diamond is a natural crystal composed entirely of carbon atoms.
Snowflakes are unique crystals of ice that develop in the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amorphous - a solid without a regular atomic structure

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'krustallos' (ice, crystal), via Latin 'crystallum'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl / (BrE)
A solid material in which the atoms are arranged in a highly ordered, repeating pattern, often forming faceted external shapes.
solid with regular internal structure

Examples

The snowflakes formed intricate crystal patterns on the window pane.
Each salt crystal is shaped like a tiny cube under a microscope.
Quartz is a common mineral that often appears as a clear crystal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amorphous - a solid not having a regular internal atomic structure
  • Degree Higher: gemstone - a precious or semi-precious stone, often crystalline

Etymology

From Greek *krustallos* (ice, crystal), via Latin *crystallum*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl / (BrE)
Fine, high-quality glassware made of clear, transparent glass, often containing lead and cut for decorative effect.
fine, transparent glassware

Examples

The table was set with elegant crystal and fine china.
She carefully polished the crystal goblets until they sparkled.
We received a beautiful set of crystal glasses as a wedding gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glassware - objects made from glass, especially for table use

Etymology

From Greek 'krustallos' meaning 'ice, crystal'; through Latin 'crystallum'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl / (BrE)
a crystalline material, especially a piece of quartz, used as a component in electronic devices such as oscillators, radios, and watches, due to its ability to precisely control frequencies
quartz component in electronics

Examples

The radio uses a crystal for frequency stabilization.
If the crystal in your watch fails, it will stop keeping time accurately.
A damaged crystal can cause the circuit to malfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillator - an electronic circuit that generates a repetitive signal

Etymology

From Greek *krustallos* (ice, crystal), later referring to the mineral quartz.

6. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.təl / (BrE)
a high-quality, colorless glass made from almost pure silica, often used for fine glassware and ornaments
fine, clear glass made from pure silica

Examples

The wine sparkled in the delicate crystal glass under the candlelight.
She received a set of fine crystal tumblers for her wedding.
Dusting the old crystal vase, he admired its clarity and intricate patterns.

Related Terms

From Ancient Greek *krystallos* (ice, crystal), via Old French *cristal* and Latin *crystallum*.
