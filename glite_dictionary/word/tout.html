tout

1. NOUN

/ taʊt / (AmE), / taʊt / (BrE)
a person who sells tips or advice, especially about betting or gambling, typically at a racetrack
person who sells betting advice

Examples

The tout offered secret tips on which horse would win the next race.
Many consider the advice of a tout to be unreliable at best.
He was banned from the track for acting as a tout without a license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipster - person who gives or sells advice about betting or gambling

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'touten' (to watch or look out), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ taʊt / (AmE), / taʊt / (BrE)
a person who solicits customers in a bold, intrusive, or aggressive manner, often for tips or commission
person who aggressively seeks customers
british

Examples

The stadium was full of touts selling tickets at double the original price.
A tout tried to persuade us to join his tour as we walked by.
Local authorities are cracking down on ticket touts at major events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawker - person who sells goods aggressively, often in the street
  • Synonym: vendor - person who sells goods, especially in a market or street

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *tuten* (to look out), perhaps influenced by Old French *toter* (to peep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10737998-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ taʊt / (AmE), / taʊt / (BrE)
to attempt to promote or publicize something or oneself in a forceful or showy way, often to attract attention or support
promote or show off enthusiastically

Examples

The company continued to tout its new product in every advertisement.
He loved to tout his recent achievements at every opportunity.
Politicians often tout their accomplishments during election campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: to understate - describe or represent as being smaller or less important than it actually is

Etymology

From Middle English 'touten' (to look out for), possibly from Old French 'tooter' (to look).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ taʊt / (AmE), / taʊt / (BrE)
a person who buys tickets for an event and resells them at a profit, often illegally or without authorization
person reselling tickets for profit

Examples

The police arrested a tout outside the stadium for selling tickets above face value.
He bought the last two tickets from a tout minutes before the concert started.
Many fans criticized the presence of touts who made it harder for them to get seats at a fair price.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scalper - person who resells tickets at a profit (especially American English)
