spanker

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈspaŋkə / (BrE)
a fore-and-aft sail set on the aftermost lower mast, usually the mizzenmast, of a sailing vessel
sail on the mizzenmast of a vessel

Examples

The crew adjusted the spanker to catch more wind during the tack.
A damaged spanker can make steering a large ship much more challenging.
The captain ordered the spanker hoisted as they approached the harbor.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈspaŋkə / (BrE)
a person who strikes someone, especially a child, with an open hand as a form of punishment
person who delivers a spanking

Examples

Her uncle was the designated spanker in the family, much to the children's dismay.
As a teacher, he refused to play the role of spanker when discipline was needed.
Stories of the old school spanker are less common in modern education.

Synset Examples

someone slapped me on the back and I turned to see who the slapper was
my father was the designated spanker in our family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slapper - a person who slaps someone with the open hand

Etymology

From the verb 'spank' (to strike with the open hand), origin unknown, plus '-er' (agent noun suffix).
