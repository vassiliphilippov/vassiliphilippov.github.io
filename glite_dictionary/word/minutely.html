minutely

1. ADV

/ maɪˈnuːtli / (AmE), / maɪˈnjuːtli / (BrE)
in great detail; paying attention to small or precise aspects
in great detail

Examples

The contract was minutely examined by the legal team before signing.
He described the experiment minutely, leaving out no detail.
The report minutely records every stage of the procedure.

Synset Examples

our inability to see everything minutely and clearly is due merely to the infirmity of our senses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulously - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Antonym: superficially - in a way that is not thorough or detailed

Etymology

From minute + -ly, tracing to Latin minutus (small, made small).
