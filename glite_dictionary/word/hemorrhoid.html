hemorrhoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.əˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.ər.ɔɪd / (BrE)
A swollen or inflamed vein in the region of the anus, which may cause discomfort or bleeding.
swollen vein near the anus
medical

Examples

Many adults experience hemorrhoids at some point in their lives, especially with prolonged sitting.
The doctor prescribed a cream to relieve the discomfort caused by hemorrhoids.
Chronic constipation can increase the risk of developing hemorrhoids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piles - another term for hemorrhoids

Etymology

From Greek haimorrhoïdes, from haima (blood) and rhein (to flow).
