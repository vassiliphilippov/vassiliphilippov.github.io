priority

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈɔrəti / (AmE), / praɪˈɒrəti / (BrE)
The fact or condition of being regarded or treated as more important than others; the right to take precedence in order, importance, or urgency.
the condition of being more important or urgent

Examples

Our top priority is to ensure everyone's safety during the event.
Rapid response was given priority because the issue was urgent.
She struggled to balance work commitments with family priority in her busy life.

Synset Examples

… its precedence as the world's leading manufacturer of pharmaceuticals
national independence takes priority over class struggle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precedence - the condition of being considered more important than someone or something else; priority in importance, order, or rank
  • Antonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in importance or quality

Etymology

From Middle French 'priorité', from Medieval Latin 'prioritas', from Latin 'prior' (former, superior).
