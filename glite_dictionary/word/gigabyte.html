gigabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪɡ.ə.baɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡ.ə.baɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 mebibytes or 1,073,741,824 bytes (2^30 bytes), commonly used to measure computer storage capacity.
unit of digital information (1,073,741,824 bytes)

Examples

My new laptop has a 512 gigabyte solid-state drive for storage.
Downloading a high-definition movie can require several gigabytes of data.
External hard drives often come with capacities of one or two gigabytes or more.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: megabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,048,576 bytes (2^20 bytes)
  • Degree Higher: terabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,099,511,627,776 bytes (2^40 bytes)

Etymology

From giga- (prefix meaning 'billion', from Greek 'gigas') + byte (unit of digital information).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪɡəˌbaɪt, ˈdʒɪɡəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡəbaɪt, ˈdʒɪɡəbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bytes (10^9 bytes) or 1000 megabytes.
unit of digital information (1,000,000,000 bytes)

Examples

My smartphone has 128 gigabytes of storage for apps and photos.
You need at least one gigabyte of RAM to run this program efficiently.
The movie file was over two gigabytes in size, so it took a while to download.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: megabyte - A unit of information equal to 1,000,000 bytes or 10^6 bytes
  • Degree Higher: terabyte - A unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000,000 bytes or 10^12 bytes

Etymology

From giga- (meaning 'billion') and byte (unit of digital information).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
