Coniferopsida

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɪfəˈrɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / kəˌnɪfəˈrɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
a class of cone-bearing gymnosperm plants, including most conifers and their extinct relatives; it dates from the Carboniferous period
class of cone-bearing gymnosperm plants
medical

Examples

The fossil record provides evidence of Coniferopsida flourishing during the Carboniferous period.
Modern pine trees belong to the class Coniferopsida.
Some extinct orders in Coniferopsida have been identified from well-preserved plant fossils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinopsida - class containing the typical conifers

Etymology

From Latin 'conifer' (cone-bearing) + Greek 'opsis' (appearance, form), used in botanical classification.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnɪfərˈɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / kəˌnɪfəˈrɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
a class of cone-bearing gymnosperms, mainly substantial trees, including conifers and related extinct and surviving plant groups
class of cone-bearing gymnosperms

Examples

The ancient forests were dominated by Coniferopsida species that towered above the undergrowth.
Fossil evidence shows that Coniferopsida has existed since the Carboniferous period.
Modern members of the Coniferopsida include pines, firs, and yews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinopsida - class of true conifers (pines, spruces, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin 'conus' (cone) + '-fer' (bearing) + '-opsida' (class, from Greek 'opsis', appearance).
