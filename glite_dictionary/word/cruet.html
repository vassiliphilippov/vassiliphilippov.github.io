cruet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːət / (AmE), / ˈkruːɪt / (BrE)
a small bottle or container used for holding liquids such as oil, vinegar, or wine, especially for serving at the table.
small bottle for oil or vinegar

Examples

The salad was served with a cruet of balsamic vinegar on the side.
She reached for the cruet to drizzle olive oil over her bread.
Each table in the restaurant had a matching cruet set for oil and vinegar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carafe - a wide-mouthed glass container for serving liquids

Etymology

From Old French 'crue' (earthen pot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruː.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈkruː.ɪt / (BrE)
A small container or bottle, typically with a handle and spout, used for holding liquids such as oil, vinegar, or wine for use at the dining table.
small bottle for oil, vinegar, etc.

Examples

She poured a little olive oil from the cruet onto her salad.
The cruet set on the table contained both vinegar and oil for the guests.
He accidentally knocked over the cruet, spilling vinegar onto the tablecloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decanter - a vessel used to hold the decantation of a liquid (such as wine) which may contain sediment

Etymology

From Old French *crue*, meaning ‘earthen pot’, possibly from Medieval Latin *crus* (jar).
