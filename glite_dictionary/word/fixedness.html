fixedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪkst.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being firmly fastened or attached and not able to move or change position
state of being firmly attached or unchanging

Examples

The fixedness of the statue ensured it would not topple during the storm.
She admired the fixedness of his gaze as he focused on the task.
The fixedness of the shelves made them ideal for storing heavy objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobility - state of not moving or being unable to move
  • Antonym: movability - the ability to be moved or changed

Etymology

From 'fixed' + '-ness', both of English origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firmly in place or not changing; immovability or stability
the state of being fixed or unchanging

Examples

His determination and fixedness allowed him to overcome every obstacle.
The fixedness of the statue in the garden gave it an air of permanence.
Some people are admired for their fixedness of purpose, while others see it as stubbornness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobility - the state of not moving; lack of motion
  • Antonym: flexibility - the ability to bend or change easily

Etymology

From 'fixed' + '-ness', with 'fixed' originating from Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).
