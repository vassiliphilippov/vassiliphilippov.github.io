antiferromagnet

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.fɛr.oʊˈmæɡ.nət / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.fɛr.əʊˈmæɡ.nət / (BrE)
a material in which adjacent atoms or ions have magnetic moments that are aligned in opposite directions, resulting in no overall magnetization
material with antiferromagnetic properties

Examples

The scientist studied the properties of the antiferromagnet under extreme temperatures.
Chromium is a well-known example of an antiferromagnet in condensed matter physics.
Unlike a ferromagnet, an antiferromagnet does not retain magnetization when the external field is removed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ferromagnet - a material with strong, aligned magnetic moments

Etymology

From 'anti-' (opposite) + 'ferromagnet' (magnet exhibiting strong magnetic properties).
