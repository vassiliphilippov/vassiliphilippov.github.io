Javanese

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɑːvəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɑːvəˈniːz / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken mainly on the island of Java in Indonesia
Indonesian language spoken on Java

Examples

Many people in central and eastern Java speak Javanese as their native language.
She decided to study Javanese to better understand Indonesian history and culture.
Translations of the book are available in both Javanese and Indonesian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indonesian - the official language of Indonesia

Etymology

From Java (the island) + -nese (forming demonyms and names of languages).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɑːvəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɑːvəˈniːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Indonesian island of Java
native or inhabitant of Java

Examples

Many Javanese people continue to live by traditional customs and beliefs.
The island's cuisine reflects the diverse cultures of the Javanese.
A group of Javanese arrived in the city seeking better opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indonesian - a native or inhabitant of Indonesia

Etymology

From Java (island in Indonesia) + -ese (suffix denoting inhabitants or language).

relating to the island of Java, its people, or their language

The Javanese language is spoken by millions of people in Indonesia.
