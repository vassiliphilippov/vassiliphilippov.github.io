perdurability

1. NOUN

/ pɚˌdʊrəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / pəˌdjʊərəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to last or endure for a very long time; extreme durability
capacity for lasting a long time

Examples

The perdurability of ancient Roman roads continues to amaze engineers today.
Materials with exceptional perdurability are sought after for constructing spacecraft.
Artists often choose stones known for their perdurability for outdoor sculptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage

Etymology

From Latin 'perdurabilis' (lasting), from 'perdurare' (to endure).
