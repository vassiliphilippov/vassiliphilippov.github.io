ultraviolet

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
(of electromagnetic radiation) having wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, beyond the violet end of the visible spectrum.
beyond violet in the spectrum; shorter than visible light

Examples

Special glasses are needed to protect your eyes from ultraviolet rays.
The scientists conducted experiments using ultraviolet light to sterilize the lab.
Excessive exposure to ultraviolet radiation can cause sunburn.

Synset Examples

ultraviolet radiation
an ultraviolet lamp

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infrared - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light

Etymology

From Latin 'ultra' (beyond) + 'violet' (referring to the color at the end of the visible spectrum).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, typically invisible to the human eye
type of electromagnetic radiation

Examples

Many insects can see ultraviolet, allowing them to detect patterns on flowers invisible to humans.
The scientist explained that ultraviolet can cause sunburn even on cloudy days.
Special lamps emit ultraviolet to sterilize medical equipment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infrared - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light

Etymology

From Latin ultra (beyond) + violet, referencing its shorter wavelength than visible violet light.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11542373-n
Synset POS: n
