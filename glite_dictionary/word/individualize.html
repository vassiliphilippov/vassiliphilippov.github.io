to individualize

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something distinct or treat as an individual; to give something a particular or personal character
make or treat as individual

Examples

The teacher tried to individualize the lessons to suit each student's interests.
Each patient's treatment plan was carefully individualized based on their medical history.
Designers individualize products by offering color and material options.

Synset Examples

The sounds were individualized by sharpness and tone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to personalize - to design or tailor something to suit an individual
  • Antonym: to generalize - to make less specific or apply broadly

Etymology

From 'individual' + '-ize', modeled after French 'individualiser'.

2. VERB

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something suitable for the needs of a particular person; to tailor or personalize
make personal or personalize

Examples

The teacher worked to individualize instruction for each student in her classroom.
They can individualize your order based on your specific preferences.
Efforts to individualize care are essential in modern healthcare settings.

Synset Examples

personalized service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to personalize - to make something personal or distinctive for an individual

Etymology

From Medieval Latin individualis (indivisible; personal) + -ize.
