Charles

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz / (BrE)
a river in eastern Massachusetts, USA, that flows into Boston Harbor and separates Cambridge from Boston.
river in eastern Massachusetts, USA

Examples

The annual regatta on the Charles River attracts rowers from around the world.
Joggers enjoy running along the banks of the Charles in the early morning.
The sunset over the Charles offers a beautiful view of Boston's skyline.

2. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz / (BrE)
the eldest son of Elizabeth II, former Prince of Wales, and heir to the English throne, born in 1948; became King Charles III in 2022.
eldest son of Elizabeth II, English monarch
american british

Examples

Charles succeeded Elizabeth II as the King of the United Kingdom in 2022.
Many people watched the coronation of Charles on television across the world.
Charles was known as the Prince of Wales for much of his life.

Etymology

From the Germanic name Karl, meaning 'man' or 'free man'.
