pagoda

1. NOUN

/ pəˈɡoʊdə / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊdə / (BrE)
a temple or sacred building, especially in East and Southeast Asia, usually with a tall, tiered structure and curved roofs
an Asian tiered temple or tower

Examples

The ancient pagoda towered above the city, offering a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains.
Tourists visited the famous Shwedagon pagoda during their trip to Myanmar.
A large bell hung from the top floor of the pagoda, ringing softly in the evening breeze.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'pagode', probably from Persian 'butkada' (temple of idols) or Sanskrit 'bhagavati' (goddess).
