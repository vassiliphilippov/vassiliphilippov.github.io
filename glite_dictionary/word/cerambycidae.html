Cerambycidae

1. NOUN

/ səˌræmˈbɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / sɛrˌæmbɪˈsɪːdiː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles, commonly known as long-horned beetles, characterized by their long antennae, often longer than their bodies.
family of long-horned beetles

Examples

The Cerambycidae are known for their extremely long antennae, which can exceed their body length.
Many species in the Cerambycidae family play important roles in forest ecosystems by decomposing dead wood.
A field guide can help you identify different types of Cerambycidae during a nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longhorn beetle - common name for beetles of the family Cerambycidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cerambycidae, based on the genus Cerambyx, from Greek kerambyx (a horned beetle).
