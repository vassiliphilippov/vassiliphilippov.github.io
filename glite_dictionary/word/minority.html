minority

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈnɔrəti / (AmE), / mɪˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
A group of people whose race, ethnicity, religion, or political beliefs differ from those of a larger group in the same society, often resulting in less power or representation.
group differing from the majority

Examples

The new policy was designed to support the minority communities in the city.
As a member of a religious minority, he sometimes felt isolated at school.
The organization advocates for equal rights for all racial minority groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: majority - the greater part or number; the group with the largest number of members

Etymology

From Latin minoritas (the state of being smaller or lesser), from minor (less).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈnɔrəti / (AmE), / mɪˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
The period of life before a person reaches the legal age of adulthood.
period before legal adulthood
legal

Examples

He inherited the estate while still in his minority.
During her minority, her uncle acted as her legal guardian.
The court protected his interests throughout his minority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: majority - the period of life after reaching the legal age of adulthood

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ məˈnɔːrəti / (AmE), / maɪˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
the smaller number or part; the group or party with fewer members or votes than the majority in a situation involving division or votes
the smaller number or part in a group

Examples

The bill was opposed by a minority of the committee members.
She found herself in a minority when the final decision was made.
Although the minority lost the vote, their views were still respected.

Synset Examples

when the vote was taken they were in the minority
he held a minority position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: majority - the greater number or part; more than half of a total

Etymology

From Latin minoritas (smaller in number or younger age), from minor (smaller).
