to resile

1. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪl / (BrE)
(formal) to withdraw from a promise, agreement, or statement, especially officially or legally.
formally withdraw from an agreement or statement

Examples

The company decided to resile from its earlier agreement due to unforeseen circumstances.
He cannot legally resile from the contract without facing penalties.
Once the promise is made, it is difficult to resile from it without damage to one's reputation.

Synset Examples

The landlord cannot resile from the lease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from an agreement, position, or situation
  • Synonym: to renege - to go back on a promise or agreement

Etymology

From Latin resilire (to spring back, rebound).

WordNet ID: oewn-02386369-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪl / (BrE)
to spring back or recoil, especially after being bent, pressed, or having an impact
spring or bounce back after impact

Examples

After being stretched, the elastic band will resile to its original shape.
The shock caused the metal rod to resile slightly.
Not all materials resile efficiently after being deformed.

Synset Examples

The rubber ball bounced
These particles do not resile but they unite after they collide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - to bounce back after hitting a surface
  • Synonym: recoil - to suddenly spring or flinch back in fear, horror, or disgust

Etymology

From Latin resilire (to spring back).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌɪl / (BrE)
Formally to abandon, withdraw from, or renounce a position, belief, or agreement, especially under pressure or on reconsideration.
formally withdraw from a belief or position

Examples

Under pressure, the witness chose to resile from his original testimony.
The politician refused to resile from her controversial statements despite public scrutiny.
He found it difficult to resile from positions he had defended for years.

Synset Examples

He retracted his earlier statements about his religion
She abjured her beliefs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recant - formally withdraw a statement or belief
  • Synonym: to abjure - solemnly renounce a belief, cause, or claim

Etymology

From Latin resilire (to spring back, rebound).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪl / (BrE)
(of an object or material) to spring back or return to its original shape or position after being stretched, compressed, or bent.
spring back to original shape or state

Examples

The rubber ball was able to resile quickly after being squished.
Certain polymers resile better than others when subjected to stretching.
After compression, the foam mattress slowly resiled to its original shape.

Synset Examples

The rubber tubes resile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - spring back through force of impact or elasticity
  • Synonym: recoil - spring back after impact or force
  • Antonym: yield - give way under pressure; opposite of retaining original shape

Etymology

From Latin resilīre (to spring back, rebound).
