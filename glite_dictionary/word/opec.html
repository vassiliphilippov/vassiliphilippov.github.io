OPEC

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊˌpiːˌiːˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊˌpiːˌiːˈsiː / (BrE)
an international organization of oil-exporting countries formed in 1960 to coordinate and unify petroleum policies among member nations
Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries

Examples

The latest OPEC meeting resulted in an agreement to reduce oil production.
Many analysts watch OPEC decisions closely because they impact global energy prices.
Several new countries are seeking membership in OPEC to influence oil policy.

Etymology

Acronym from Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries.
