acerb

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɜrb / (AmE), / əˈsɜːb / (BrE)
having a sour or bitter taste; sharp or harsh in flavor
having a sour or bitter taste

Examples

The grapefruit had an acerb bite that made her wince.
Some wines, especially those with little sugar, can taste rather acerb.
He described the sauce as unexpectedly acerb due to the unripe lemons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acerbic - sharp or sour in taste or tone

Etymology

From Latin acerb(us) meaning 'sour, harsh.'
