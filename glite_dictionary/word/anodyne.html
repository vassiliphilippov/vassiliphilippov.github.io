anodyne

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈanədʌɪn / (BrE)
a medicine or drug used to relieve pain
pain-relieving medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anodyne to ease her discomfort.
Many ancient remedies included natural anodynes derived from plants.
After surgery, he requested an anodyne for the severe pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain

Etymology

From Greek anodynos (free from pain), from an- (without) + odynē (pain).
