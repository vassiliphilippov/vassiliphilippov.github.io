bouffe

1. NOUN

/ buf / (AmE), / buːf / (BrE)
A light, comic opera, typically with a happy ending and some spoken dialogue.
comic opera with spoken parts

Examples

She prefers lively performances, so an evening of bouffe suits her perfectly.
The theater is staging a rarely performed nineteenth-century bouffe this season.
Even critics who dislike serious opera sometimes enjoy the light-hearted tone of bouffe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operetta - a short, light opera with spoken dialogue

Etymology

From French 'bouffe', short for 'opéra bouffe', itself from Italian 'opera buffa' (comic opera).
