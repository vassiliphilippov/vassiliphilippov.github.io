3. NOUN

/ kin / (AmE), / kiːn / (BrE)
a wailing lament for the dead, traditionally sung at a funeral, especially in Irish or Scottish culture
funeral lament with wailing

Examples

During the traditional Irish funeral, a woman sang a sorrowful keen that echoed through the church.
The haunting sound of the keen moved everyone attending the wake to tears.
Scholars have studied the origins and patterns of the keen in Celtic mourning rituals.

Etymology

From Irish *caoin* (to weep, to wail), related to Scottish Gaelic *caoineadh*.
