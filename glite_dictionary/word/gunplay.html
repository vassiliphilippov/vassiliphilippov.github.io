gunplay

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌpleɪ / (BrE)
the use of guns in a violent fight or confrontation, especially with the intent to kill or intimidate
the use of guns in violent fights

Examples

The film's climax is a dramatic scene of gunplay between rival gangs.
Police rushed to the scene after reports of gunplay downtown.
He described the old western town as a place notorious for bar fights and gunplay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shootout - a fight involving shooting between two or more people or groups
  • Antonym: melee - a confused fight, often involving physical struggle rather than shooting

Etymology

From 'gun' + 'play', meaning the use or display of firearms.
