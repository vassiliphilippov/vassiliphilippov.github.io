alight

1. VERB

/ əˈlaɪt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪt / (BrE)
(of a bird or flying object) to come down and settle, especially after flight.
come down and settle after flight

Examples

A flock of pigeons alighted on the town square.
The butterfly alighted gently on the blooming flower.
As we watched, several starlings alighted on the telephone wire.

Synset Examples

the birds alighted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to come down and rest on a surface after flight

Etymology

From Old English *ālīhtan* (to descend, dismount).

2. VERB

/ əˈlaɪt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪt / (BrE)
(of a bird, insect, or other object) to come to rest or settle after flying or moving.
come to rest or settle after moving

Examples

As dusk fell, several birds alighted on the telephone wire.
A butterfly alighted gently on the blooming flower.
Curiosity alighted upon her as she listened to the mysterious tale.

Synset Examples

Misfortune lighted upon him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to come down and rest on a surface after moving through the air

Etymology

From Old English 'ālīhtan', meaning 'to descend, dismount'.

Source: WordNet

lit up or burning
