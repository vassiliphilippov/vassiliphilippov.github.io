syndic

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪn dɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪn dɪk / (BrE)
an official appointed to represent a city, university, or corporation in business transactions or legal matters
representative of a city, university, or corporation

Examples

The syndic was responsible for overseeing the financial affairs of the university.
Each town appointed a syndic to act on its behalf in the regional council.
As syndic of the guild, she ensured fair trade practices among its members.

Etymology

From French syndic, from Late Latin syndicus (delegate of a corporation), from Greek syndikos (advocate, representative).
