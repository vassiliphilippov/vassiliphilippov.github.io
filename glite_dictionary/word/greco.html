Greco

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡreɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛkəʊ / (BrE)
The name adopted by Doménikos Theotokópoulos (1541–1614), a Spanish painter of Greek origin, known for his religious works featuring elongated figures and expressive color.
Spanish painter of Greek origin, 1541–1614

Examples

The paintings of Greco adorn many of the cathedrals in Spain.
Many art students study Greco for his unique use of light and color.
A new exhibition features the dramatic works of Greco alongside his contemporaries.

Etymology

From Spanish 'El Greco' (the Greek), adopted as the nickname of Doménikos Theotokópoulos.
