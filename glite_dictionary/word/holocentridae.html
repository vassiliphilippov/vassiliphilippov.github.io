Holocentridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊ.loʊˈsɛn.trɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əˈsɛn.trɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fish known as squirrelfishes and soldierfishes, distinguished by their large eyes and spiny fins, found in tropical reefs
family of squirrelfishes and soldierfishes

Examples

Fish of the Holocentridae family are easily identified by their bright red coloring and large eyes.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Holocentridae during their expedition to the coral reef.
Many aquarists appreciate members of the Holocentridae for their beauty and interesting social behavior.

Etymology

From Greek holo- (whole) + kentron (sting) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
