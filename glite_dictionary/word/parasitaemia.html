parasitaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəsaɪˈθiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌpærəsaɪˈtiːmiə / (BrE)
The presence of parasites in the blood, especially as observed in diseases such as malaria.
presence of parasites in the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed the diagnosis of malaria after detecting parasitaemia in the patient's blood sample.
High levels of parasitaemia can lead to severe complications if not treated promptly.
Regular screening for parasitaemia is essential in regions where tropical diseases are common.

Etymology

From Greek parasitos (parasite) + -aemia (from Greek haima, blood).
