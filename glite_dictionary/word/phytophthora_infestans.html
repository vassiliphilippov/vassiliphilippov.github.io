Phytophthora infestans

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈtɑfθərə ɪnˈfɛstænz / (AmE), / faɪˈtɒfθərə ɪnˈfɛstænz / (BrE)
a species of fungus-like organism that causes late blight in tomatoes and potatoes, leading to serious crop diseases
fungus causing late blight in crops

Examples

Phytophthora infestans devastated potato crops in Ireland during the 1840s, contributing to the Great Famine.
Farmers monitor their fields regularly to detect early signs of Phytophthora infestans infection.
The spread of Phytophthora infestans can be limited by using disease-resistant plant varieties.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyton' (plant) and 'phthora' (destruction); 'infestans' is Latin for 'attacking'.
