wipeout

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪpˌaʊt / (BrE)
an event that causes the total destruction or elimination of something
total destruction; complete elimination

Examples

The financial crash led to a wipeout of many small businesses in the area.
After the hurricane, the village was nothing short of a wipeout.
The team's defeat in the championship was a complete wipeout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annihilation - complete destruction or obliteration
  • Synonym: devastation - severe and widespread destruction or damage
  • Antonym: survival - the state of continuing to exist

Etymology

Formed from the verb phrase 'wipe out,' meaning to destroy or eliminate.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪpˌaʊt / (BrE)
a sudden, often dramatic fall or crash during a sporting activity, such as from a bicycle, skis, or surfboard
a fall or crash in sports

Examples

He took a massive wipeout while surfing the big waves this morning.
The video replay showed her dramatic wipeout just before the finish line.
Despite an early wipeout, he got back on his bike and finished the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - a fall, especially from a vehicle or in sports
  • Synonym: crash - a violent fall or collision, especially in sports or vehicles

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'wipe out', with the sense of being completely knocked down or eliminated.

Synset POS: n
