to expiate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛkspiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkspɪeɪt / (BrE)
to make amends or reparation for (guilt or wrongdoing)
make amends for wrongdoing

Examples

He tried to expiate his crime by doing community service for several years.
The monk fasted for forty days to expiate his sins.
She sought to expiate her guilt through acts of kindness and charity.

Synset Examples

expiate one's sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to atone - to make amends or reparation
  • Synonym: to redeem - to make up for past faults or sins

Etymology

From Latin expiare (to atone for), from ex- (out) + piare (to appease, atone).
