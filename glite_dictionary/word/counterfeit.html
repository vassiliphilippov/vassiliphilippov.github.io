counterfeit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəfɪt / (BrE)
a copy of something, especially money or an official document, made to look exactly like the original in order to deceive people
a fake imitation presented as original

Examples

The police seized a large number of counterfeit bills from the suspect's house.
She unknowingly purchased a counterfeit handbag at the market.
Experts can usually distinguish a genuine painting from a counterfeit after a close inspection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgery - the crime of making a false copy of a document, signature, or work of art

Etymology

From Old French 'contrefait', past participle of 'contrefaire' (to counterfeit), from Latin 'contra-' (against) + 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəfɪt / (BrE)
to make an exact imitation of something, especially money or documents, with the intention to deceive or defraud
make a fake copy to deceive

Examples

It is illegal to counterfeit passports or identity cards.
The gang planned to counterfeit hundreds of $100 bills.
He was arrested for attempting to counterfeit official documents.

Synset Examples

he faked the signature
they counterfeited dollar bills
She forged a Green Card

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - to make a fake copy of something, especially a document, with intent to deceive
  • Synonym: fake - to make something look real in order to deceive
  • Antonym: genuine - real; actually what it is said to be

Etymology

From Old French contrefaire, from contre- (against) + faire (to make), ultimately from Latin contra (against) and facere (to make).

3. ADJ

/ ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəfɪt / (BrE)
made to look exactly like something else, usually to deceive; not genuine.
not genuine; fake or imitative

Examples

The store was fined for selling counterfeit designer handbags to unsuspecting customers.
He was arrested for paying with counterfeit bills at the restaurant.
A counterfeit passport can be difficult for officials to detect without close inspection.

Synset Examples

counterfeit emotion
counterfeit money
counterfeit works of art
a counterfeit prince

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; made to look like something else
  • Antonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine

Etymology

From Old French 'contrefait' (imitated), from 'contre-' (counter-) + 'faire' (to make), ultimately from Latin 'contra' (against) + 'facere' (to make).
