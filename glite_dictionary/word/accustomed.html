accustomed

1. ADJ

/ əˈkʌstəmd / (AmE), / əˈkʌstəmd / (BrE)
usual; often used or practiced; familiar through repeated experience.
usual or habitual

Examples

She left the office at her accustomed time every day.
The cat waited for its accustomed meal by the kitchen door.
He spoke with his accustomed confidence during the meeting.

Synset Examples

his accustomed thoroughness
took his customary morning walk
his habitual comment
with her wonted candor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customary - according to the customs or usual practices associated with a particular society, place, or set of circumstances
  • Synonym: habitual - done or doing constantly or as a habit
  • Antonym: unaccustomed - not familiar or usual; out of the ordinary

Etymology

From Middle English 'accustomed', from Anglo-Norman 'accustumer', from Old French 'acostumer', from 'ac-' (to) + 'costume' (custom).
