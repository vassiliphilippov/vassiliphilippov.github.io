grapple

1. NOUN

/ ˈgræpəl / (AmE), / ˈgræpəl / (BrE)
a tool with several hooks, attached to a rope, used for grasping or holding things, especially to catch or drag objects
hooked tool for grasping or dragging

Examples

The sailors used a grapple to haul in the floating crate.
Firefighters threw a grapple onto the roof to secure the safety line.
He attached a rope to the grapple before tossing it over the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grappling hook - a hook attached to a rope, thrown for catching or holding objects

Etymology

From Old French 'grapel', diminutive of 'grape' (hook, claw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræpəl / (AmE), / ˈɡræpəl / (BrE)
a close hand-to-hand struggle or fight, especially in wrestling or combat
close hand-to-hand struggle

Examples

The two boxers were locked in a fierce grapple in the final round of the match.
During the grapple, no one could separate the angry rivals until security arrived.
The crowd cheered as the wrestlers started a grapple in the center of the ring.

Synset Examples

they had a fierce wrestle
we watched his grappling and wrestling with the bully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestle - engage in close hand-to-hand combat, especially as a sport
  • Synonym: scuffle - a short, confused fight or struggle at close quarters

Etymology

From Middle English *grapil* (small hook), from Old French *grapil*, diminutive of *grape* (hook).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡræpəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrapəl / (BrE)
a dredging bucket with hinged jaws, typically resembling a clam shell, used for digging or lifting materials from underwater or difficult-to-reach places
dredging bucket with hinged jaws

Examples

The construction crew lowered the grapple into the river to collect debris from the bottom.
A large grapple was used to remove sediment during the dredging process.
The operator carefully maneuvered the grapple to scoop up rocks from the harbor bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamshell bucket - a type of bucket with two hinged jaws used for digging or lifting materials

Etymology

From Middle English *grapil*, from Old French *grapel*, a diminutive of *grape* meaning 'hook'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03041093-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɡræpəl / (AmE), / ˈɡræpəl / (BrE)
to seize hold of someone or something, especially in a firm struggle or wrestling match
to seize or struggle with someone

Examples

The two boxers grappled in the center of the ring, each trying to gain the upper hand.
During the scuffle, he grappled with the intruder to prevent him from escaping.
As the sailors grappled with each other on deck, the captain tried to break up the fight.

Synset Examples

the two men grappled with each other for several minutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestle - to struggle or fight physically with someone, especially in sport
  • Antonym: release - to let go or free from physical hold or grip

Etymology

From Middle English *graplen*, from Old French *grappiller* (to seize), from *grappe* (hook, handful).

Synset POS: v
