policy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
a written contract or certificate of insurance stating the terms, coverage, and conditions provided
insurance contract or certificate

Examples

She keeps her car insurance policy in a safe place at home.
The details of an accident must be reported according to the terms of your policy.
Before signing the policy, he carefully reviewed all the coverage information.

Synset Examples

you should have read the small print on your policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance - protection against possible financial loss

Etymology

From Old French 'policie' (policy, governance), later specialized to the insurance sense in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A course or principle of action adopted or proposed by a government, organization, or individual, especially as a rational basis for decisions.
course of action or principle adopted by an authority

Examples

The government's new policy on education aims to improve access for all students.
The company reviewed its policy on remote work to accommodate employee needs.
Healthcare policy often becomes a major topic during election campaigns.

Synset Examples

they debated the policy or impolicy of the proposed legislation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term aim
  • Synonym: tactic - an action or strategy carefully planned to achieve a specific end
  • Synonym: principle - a fundamental truth or proposition used as a basis for reasoning or action

Etymology

From Old French politique and Latin politia (civil administration).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
a course or principle of action adopted or proposed by an individual, organization, or government
plan of action adopted by a group or person

Examples

The government announced a new policy on climate change to reduce emissions.
Many companies have a strict privacy policy to protect user data.
His policy of transparency has earned him the trust of voters.

Synset Examples

it was a policy of retribution
a politician keeps changing his policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim
  • Synonym: principle - a general law or rule adopted or professed as a guide to action
  • Synonym: practice - the actual application or use of an idea or policy

Etymology

From Old French *policie* (civil administration), from Late Latin *politia* (state, government), from Greek *politeia* (citizenship, government).
