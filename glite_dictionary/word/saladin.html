Saladin

1. PROPN

/ ˈsælədɪn / (AmE), / ˈsælədɪn / (BrE)
(1137–1193) Sultan of Egypt and Syria who led the Muslim military campaign against the Crusader states in the Levant, notably recapturing Jerusalem in 1187.
Muslim sultan; recaptured Jerusalem (12th century)

Examples

Saladin was renowned for his chivalry and leadership during the Crusades.
The city of Jerusalem fell to Saladin in 1187 after the Battle of Hattin.
Historians often debate the tactics used by Saladin against Richard the Lionheart.

Etymology

From Arabic Ṣalāḥ ad-Dīn (صلاح الدين), meaning 'righteousness of the faith'.
