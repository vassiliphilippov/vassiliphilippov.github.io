revolutionist

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɑːluːʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒljuːʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who actively supports or participates in a political or social revolution
person supporting or participating in a revolution

Examples

The revolutionist inspired others to rise up against the oppressive government.
As a committed revolutionist, she risked her life to distribute pamphlets calling for change.
History often remembers the revolutionist as both a hero and a threat, depending on one's perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - person who advocates or engages in revolution
  • Antonym: counterrevolutionary - person who opposes revolution or seeks to reverse it

Etymology

From French révolutionniste, from révolution (revolution).
