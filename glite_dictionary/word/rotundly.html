rotundly

1. ADV

/ roʊˈtʌnd.li / (AmE), / rəˈtʌnd.li / (BrE)
in a full, rich, or sonorous manner, especially of speech or sound
in a full or sonorous manner

Examples

The orator spoke rotundly, his voice ringing through the hall.
The choir sang rotundly, filling the cathedral with harmonious sound.
She read the poem rotundly, every word clear and rich in tone.

Synset Examples

the congregation consisted chiefly of a few young folk, who snored sonorously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonorously - in a sonorous, resonant, or full-sounding manner

Etymology

From Latin 'rotundus' (round, full-toned), via 'rotund' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
