nardoo

1. NOUN

/ nɑːrˈduː / (AmE), / nɑːˈduː / (BrE)
An Australian clover fern (Marsilea), with leaves resembling a four-leaf clover, traditionally used as a food source by Indigenous Australians.
Australian clover fern plant

Examples

The explorers attempted to survive by eating nardoo seeds during their journey.
Nardoo grows abundantly in the wetlands of southeastern Australia.
Researchers studied the nutritional value of the nardoo plant in traditional diets.

Etymology

From a word in a New South Wales Australian Aboriginal language.
