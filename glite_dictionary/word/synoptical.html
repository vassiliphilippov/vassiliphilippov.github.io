synoptical

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈnɑptɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒptɪkəl / (BrE)
presenting or taking the same point of view, especially with reference to the first three Gospels of the New Testament, which are similar in content and structure
having the same point of view; especially of the first three Gospels

Examples

The scholars conducted a synoptical analysis of Matthew, Mark, and Luke.
In theological discussions, synoptical comparisons reveal both similarities and differences among the Gospels.
The book provides a synoptical overview of early Christian writings.

Synset Examples

synoptic sayings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synoptic - giving a general summary or overview; having a similar viewpoint, especially of the Gospels

Etymology

From Greek synoptikos (giving a comprehensive view), from synopsis (general view) + -ical.
