interrupted

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd / (BrE)
happening with pauses or breaks in continuity; starting and stopping intermittently.
intermittently stopping and starting

Examples

He suffered from interrupted sleep throughout the night because of the noise.
The meeting was marked by interrupted discussions due to frequent technical issues.
Her interrupted breathing made the doctor concerned about her condition.

Synset Examples

fitful (or interrupted) sleep
off-and-on static

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - happening without interruption or breaks
  • Synonym: fitful - occurring in irregular bursts; not steady

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd / (BrE)
temporarily stopped or discontinued; having a break or pause in continuity
temporarily stopped or paused

Examples

After an interrupted phone call, she dialed the number again to finish their conversation.
The interrupted power supply caused the computers to shut down unexpectedly.
The hikers resumed their journey after an interrupted lunch break due to rain.

Synset Examples

we resumed the interrupted discussion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - without interruption or pause; unbroken
  • Antonym: uninterrupted - not stopped; continuous or unbroken

Etymology

From Latin 'interruptus', past participle of 'interrumpere' (to break apart).
