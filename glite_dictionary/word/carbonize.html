to carbonize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːr.bəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
to unite or cause to unite with carbon, especially in a chemical or metallurgical process
unite with carbon (chemistry/metallurgy)

Examples

Modern steel production often requires manufacturers to carbonize the metal to improve its hardness.
Some industrial processes carbonize organic materials at high temperatures to create carbon-rich products.
The engineers developed a technique to carbonize the surface of the alloy for enhanced durability.

Synset Examples

carburize metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carburize - to combine or impregnate with carbon, especially in metals

Etymology

From carbon + -ize, patterning after scientific verbs meaning to cause to become or treat with (carbon).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to convert or be converted into carbon, especially by heating or burning; to char
convert into carbon by heating or burning

Examples

Industrial processes can carbonize wood to produce charcoal.
If you carbonize organic waste, it can be used as a soil amendment.
Too much heat will carbonize the surface of the meat, leaving it charred.

Synset Examples

carbonize coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: char - to partially burn so as to blacken the surface; to convert to charcoal
  • Synonym: to incinerate - to burn something completely to ashes

Etymology

From French 'carboniser', from 'carbone' (carbon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00370281-v
Synset POS: v
