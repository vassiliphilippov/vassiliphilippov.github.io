ghetto

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtəʊ / (BrE)
A part of a city, especially in Europe from the 16th to the 20th centuries, where Jews were required by law to live.
historical Jewish quarter in European cities

Examples

Many Jewish families struggled to survive in the ghetto of Warsaw during the Second World War.
The Venetian ghetto was one of the first areas in Europe where Jews were segregated.
Historians have studied daily life in the Prague ghetto before emancipation.

Synset Examples

the Warsaw ghetto

Etymology

From Italian 'ghetto', originally denoting the Jewish quarter of Venice.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛt.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛt.əʊ / (BrE)
a segregated environment or way of living or working, caused by social bias or stereotyping, which confines a group of people
segregated environment due to bias

Examples

She felt trapped in a ghetto of low expectations at her workplace.
The art world can sometimes become a ghetto for non-traditional artists.
Community leaders worked hard to break free from the social ghetto created by stereotypes.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtəʊ / (BrE)
a poor, densely populated urban area where a minority ethnic group lives, often due to social or economic pressure
poor city district with a minority group

Examples

Many immigrants settled in the Jewish ghetto on the east side of the city.
She grew up in a ghetto, facing daily hardships and limited opportunities.
Urban renewal projects have aimed to improve living conditions in the ghetto.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a thickly populated, run-down urban area inhabited by poor people
  • Antonym: suburb - an outlying district of a city, typically residential

Etymology

From Italian 'ghetto', originally denoting the part of Venice where Jews were compelled to live.
