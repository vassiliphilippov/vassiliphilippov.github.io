visionariness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒənɛri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒənəri.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being visionary, especially having imagination, creativity, or a tendency toward the imaginary or illusory.
the quality of being visionary or imaginative

Examples

The artist's visionariness was evident in her surreal paintings that seemed to depict dreams.
The novel stands out for its visionariness, taking readers on a journey beyond the limits of reality.
Critics praised the director's visionariness in creating a world so different from our own.

Synset Examples

In Real, science fiction and ghost stories intermingle in a tale whose first half has the precise visionariness of David Lynch, and whose second half develops into a specifically Japanese melancholy, as it speaks of childhood, the sea and the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty of forming new ideas or images
  • Antonym: realism - the quality or fact of representing a person or situation accurately or in a way that is true to life

Etymology

From 'visionary' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the quality of being visionary.
