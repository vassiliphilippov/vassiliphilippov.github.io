Haemopis

1. NOUN

/ hiˈɑːməpɪs / (AmE), / hiˈæməpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, freshwater leeches commonly found in Europe and North America.
genus of freshwater leeches

Examples

Haemopis leeches are often mistaken for medicinal leeches due to their size and appearance.
Among freshwater invertebrates, Haemopis exhibits particularly swift movements when hunting prey.
Researchers collected several Haemopis specimens from the river for their ecological study.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + ops (appearance), referring to its blood-sucking nature.

2. NOUN

/ hiˈɑːmɪpɪs / (AmE), / hiˈəʊmɪpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of leeches in the family Haemopidae, comprising several species found mainly in freshwater environments.
genus of freshwater leeches

Examples

Haemopis species are often found in ponds and slow-moving streams across Europe and North America.
Scientists collected samples of Haemopis to study their behavior in different aquatic environments.
Unlike some parasitic leeches, most Haemopis feed on small invertebrates rather than on blood.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'opis' (appearance or face).
