three-mover

1. NOUN

/ ˈθriˌmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈθriːˌmuːvə / (BrE)
A chess problem in which White is required to checkmate Black in no more than three moves, regardless of how Black defends.
chess problem solved in three moves

Examples

The grandmaster published an elegant three-mover in the latest chess magazine.
It took me hours to solve the challenging three-mover in yesterday’s puzzle section.
He prefers composing three-movers over longer or shorter chess problems.

Synset Examples

Accordingly, the solver usually takes it for granted that a three-mover has a threat, unless the setting definitely suggests a block position.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: two-mover - a chess problem requiring mate in two moves
  • Degree Higher: four-mover - a chess problem requiring mate in four moves
