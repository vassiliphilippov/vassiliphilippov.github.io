tarry

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑri / (AmE), / ˈtɑːri / (BrE)
resembling or containing tar; having the characteristics of pitch or tar.
resembling or containing tar

Examples

The workers wore gloves to avoid the tarry residue left by the old roof.
The air had a strong, tarry smell after the road was repaired.
His shoes stuck to the tarry surface of the newly sealed driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitchy - resembling pitch; black or sticky like tar or pitch
  • Synonym: tar-like - having qualities or appearance similar to tar

Etymology

From Middle English, from tar (the substance) + -y (adjectival suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛri / (AmE), / ˈtæri / (BrE)
to stay in a place longer than intended or to remain or linger aimlessly
to linger or stay somewhere
archaic

Examples

We had planned to leave at once, but John tarried by the gate, lost in thought.
Please do not tarry on your way; we are already late for the appointment.
They tarried in the old town square, waiting for the market to open.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around idly or without apparent purpose

Etymology

From Old English *terian* (to delay), related to Old French *tarier* (to delay, hinder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645159-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtæri / (AmE), / ˈtɑːri / (BrE)
to remain or linger in a place, often delaying departure
linger or delay leaving a place

Examples

She chose to tarry at the party, even after most of the guests had left.
Do not tarry on your way home, as it will soon get dark.
He tarried outside the door, uncertain whether to enter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
  • Antonym: to depart - to leave a place

Etymology

From Old English *tærian* (to delay), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
