Tichodroma muriaria

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈkɒdrəʊmə mjuːˈriːəriə / (AmE), / tɪˈkɒdrəmə mjʊəˈrɪərɪə / (BrE)
A small crimson-and-grey songbird (the wallcreeper) native to town walls and mountain cliffs in southern Eurasia and northern Africa.
species of crimson-and-grey songbird (wallcreeper)

Examples

The Tichodroma muriaria can often be seen clinging to sheer cliffs in the Alps.
Ornithologists are excited when they spot a Tichodroma muriaria in urban ruins.
The unique foraging behavior of Tichodroma muriaria makes it fascinating to birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallcreeper - common English name for Tichodroma muriaria

Etymology

From New Latin; Tichodroma from Greek 'teikhos' (wall) + 'dromos' (runner), muriaria from Latin 'murus' (wall).
