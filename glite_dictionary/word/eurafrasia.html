Eurafrasia

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌræfəˈreɪʒə / (AmE), / jʊˌrafəˈreɪzɪə / (BrE)
The combined continental landmass of Europe, Africa, and Asia, as known to Europeans before the discovery of the Americas.
Europe, Africa, and Asia as a single region

Examples

Many ancient civilizations developed across Eurafrasia, trading goods and ideas along vast overland routes.
The concept of Eurafrasia highlights the interconnected histories of Europe, Africa, and Asia.
Before the Age of Exploration, maps often depicted only Eurafrasia in detail, with little knowledge of other continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old World - regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa as known before the discovery of the Americas
  • Antonym: New World - the Americas, as contrasted with the Old World

Etymology

Formed from 'Europe', 'Africa', and 'Asia', combining the names of the three continents.
