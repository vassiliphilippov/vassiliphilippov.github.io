dormitory

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔːrməˌtɔːri / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmɪt(ə)ri / (BrE)
A large room with several beds, used especially for sleeping accommodations in schools, institutions, or hostels.
large sleeping room with several beds

Examples

The children slept in a large dormitory with rows of identical beds.
Noise echoed through the dormitory as everyone settled in for the night.
Each summer camp dormitory housed up to twenty campers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracks - building or room providing sleeping quarters for a number of people

Etymology

From Latin 'dormitorium' (a place for sleeping), from 'dormire' (to sleep).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔːrməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmɪtri / (BrE)
a building in a college or university where students live while studying
student housing at a college or university

Examples

After her classes, she returned to her dormitory to study with friends.
Most freshmen are required to live in a dormitory during their first year at college.
The university renovated its oldest dormitory to provide more comfortable accommodations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residence hall - a college or university building for student accommodation

Etymology

From Latin dormitorium (sleeping place), from dormire (to sleep).
