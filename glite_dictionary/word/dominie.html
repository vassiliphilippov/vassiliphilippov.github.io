dominie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.ni / (BrE)
(chiefly Scottish) A clergyman, especially a settled minister or parson.
a (Scottish) clergyman or minister
archaic british

Examples

The dominie delivered a moving sermon on Sunday morning.
As a young boy, he often visited the dominie for advice and wisdom.
The village respected their dominie for his guidance and kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches
  • Synonym: parson - a parish priest or clergyman, especially in the Anglican Church

Etymology

From Scottish, alteration of Latin 'domine', vocative of 'dominus' (master, lord).
