demiglace

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.iˈɡlɑs / (AmE), / ˌdɛmiˈɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A rich brown sauce made by reducing a mixture of Espagnole sauce and beef stock, often with wine or sherry, used in French cuisine.
rich brown French sauce

Examples

The chef prepared a classic demiglace to serve with the roasted beef.
Making a proper demiglace requires several hours of careful simmering and reduction.
He finished the dish by drizzling a spoonful of demiglace over the vegetables.

Etymology

From French 'demi-glace' meaning 'half glaze'.
