dishonourableness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑnərəbəlnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not deserving honor or respect; the state of being dishonourable.
lack of honor or respectability

Examples

The dishonourableness of his actions was condemned by all who knew him.
She recoiled at the dishonourableness associated with cheating and deceit.
No one wanted to be linked to the dishonourableness of the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonour - loss or lack of honor or respect
  • Synonym: dishonourable - not deserving honor or respect
  • Antonym: honourableness - the quality of deserving honor or respect

Etymology

From 'dishonourable' + '-ness'.
