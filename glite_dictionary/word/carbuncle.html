carbuncle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrˌbʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbʌŋkəl / (BrE)
A deep-red garnet, especially one cut in a smooth, rounded (cabochon) form without facets.
deep-red garnet gemstone

Examples

The queen's necklace was set with a large carbuncle at its center.
A fine carbuncle shone brilliantly in the antique ring.
Collectors prize the deep color and unique cut of a true carbuncle.

Etymology

From Latin *carbunculus* (a small coal; precious stone of a fiery color).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrˌbʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbʌŋkəl / (BrE)
A severe, painful infection of the skin composed of a cluster of boils, with several openings for the discharge of pus.
large skin infection with multiple pus openings
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the swollen, red lump on his neck as a carbuncle requiring immediate treatment.
She suffered from a painful carbuncle on her shoulder that took several weeks to heal.
A carbuncle often needs to be drained by a medical professional to prevent further infection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boil - a painful, pus-filled swelling of the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'carbunculus', meaning 'little coal', due to its red appearance.
