tweed

1. NOUN

/ twid / (AmE), / twiÀêd / (BrE)
a rough, thick woolen fabric, typically of mixed flecked colors, originally made in Scotland, used especially for jackets, coats, and suits
thick woolen fabric for clothing

Examples

He wore a distinctive jacket made of tweed to the countryside event.
Traditional tweed suits are often associated with British academic style.
The tailor offered several patterns of tweed for her new coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - the fiber from the fleece of sheep, used in making fabric

Etymology

From River Tweed, a river in Scotland where the fabric was first produced.
