promissory

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.məˌsɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.sər.i / (BrE)
containing or implying a promise; having the character of a promise
containing or implying a promise

Examples

The company issued a promissory note to guarantee the payment of the loan.
His words had a promissory tone, assuring us that improvements would be made soon.
A promissory contract requires the parties to fulfill certain obligations.

Synset Examples

promissory note

Etymology

From Medieval Latin promissorius, from Latin promissum (a promise).
