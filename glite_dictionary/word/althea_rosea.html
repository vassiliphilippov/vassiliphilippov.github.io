Althea rosea

1. NOUN

/ ælˈθiː.ə ˈroʊ.zi.ə / (AmE), / ælˈθiː.ə ˈrəʊ.zi.ə / (BrE)
a tall plant with clusters of showy white, pink, or purple flowers, commonly known as the hollyhock, often grown in English cottage gardens
a garden plant, the hollyhock

Examples

The Althea rosea flourished along the cottage fence, its blossoms attracting bees and butterflies.
Gardeners prize the Althea rosea for its tall stems and vibrant flowers in midsummer.
She planted Althea rosea seeds in early spring to brighten her garden with classic hollyhocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollyhock - tall garden plant with large showy flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Althea' (an old genus name) with Latin 'rosea' meaning 'rosy' or 'pink'.
