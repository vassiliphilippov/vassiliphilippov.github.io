density

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
the amount of something measured per unit of space, area, or volume
amount per unit of space, area, or volume

Examples

The city has a high population density compared to rural areas.
We measured the density of the metal to determine its composition.
Data density in this region makes it difficult to analyze individual points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - the amount of a substance in a given volume
  • Antonym: sparsity - the property of being sparse or scattered

Etymology

From Latin 'densitas', from 'densus' meaning 'thick, dense'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being crowded or compact; the degree to which something is filled with people or things within a given area or volume
the state of being crowded or packed

Examples

The density of the city’s population makes public transport essential.
The forest has a high density of trees per square mile.
As the event began, the density of the crowd increased near the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compactness - the quality of being closely packed together
  • Antonym: sparsity - the quality of being thinly dispersed or scattered

Etymology

From Latin 'densitas', from 'densus' meaning thick, dense.

Synset POS: n
