sawdust

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːˌdʌst / (AmE), / ˈsɔːˌdʌst / (BrE)
small particles of wood produced by sawing, sanding, or otherwise working wood
fine wood particles from sawing

Examples

After cutting the planks, the floor was covered with sawdust.
Carpenters always sweep up sawdust at the end of the day.
He used sawdust to soak up the spill in the workshop.

Etymology

Compound of 'saw' (the tool) and 'dust' (fine particles), first recorded in the 17th century.
