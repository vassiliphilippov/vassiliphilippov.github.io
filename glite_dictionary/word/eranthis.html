Eranthis

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrænθɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈranθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small flowering plants in the buttercup family, known for producing yellow flowers in early spring, commonly called winter aconite.
genus of flowering plants (winter aconite)

Examples

Eranthis blooms are among the first signs of spring in many temperate gardens.
The bright yellow flowers of Eranthis often appear while snow still covers the ground.
Gardeners appreciate Eranthis for its hardiness and early flowering season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter aconite - common name for Eranthis

Etymology

From Greek 'er' (spring) + 'anthos' (flower).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrænθɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈranθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), commonly known as winter aconite, noted for their early spring yellow flowers.
genus of plants (winter aconite)

Examples

Eranthis blooms early in the year, often when snow is still on the ground.
Gardeners prize Eranthis for its bright yellow flowers and hardy nature.
The woodlands were carpeted with Eranthis during the first weeks of spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter aconite - common name for plants in the Eranthis genus

Etymology

From Greek 'er' (spring) and 'anthos' (flower).
