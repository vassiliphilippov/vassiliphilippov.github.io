disadvantage

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsədˈvɑːntɪdʒ / (BrE)
an unfavorable condition or position that reduces chances of success or effectiveness; the quality or state of being disadvantaged
unfavorable condition; inferior position

Examples

Coming from a small town can be a disadvantage in the competitive job market.
His lack of experience put him at a disadvantage compared to other candidates.
The team's injuries gave their opponents a clear disadvantage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position

Etymology

From Old French desavantage (disadvantage), from des- (dis-) + avantage (advantage).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsədˈvɑːntɪdʒ / (BrE)
to put in an unfavorable position; to hinder or harm someone or something compared to others
to put in an unfavorable position

Examples

Strict deadlines can disadvantage students who work part-time jobs.
Changing the regulations may disadvantage small businesses compared to larger corporations.
Some policies disadvantage people living in rural areas.

Synset Examples

This rule clearly disadvantages me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone to do something
  • Synonym: harm - to cause injury or damage to someone or something
  • Antonym: advantage - to put in a favorable position

Etymology

From Middle French 'desavantage', from 'des-' (expressing reversal) + 'avantage' (advantage).
