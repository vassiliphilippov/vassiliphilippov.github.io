frontage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the direction in which a building or structure faces
the direction a building faces

Examples

The frontage of the house overlooks the park.
Shops with a southern frontage often receive more sunlight.
The new office building was designed with its frontage facing the main street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orientation - the positioning or alignment of something relative to a direction

Etymology

From Old French ‘frontage’, from ‘front’ (face, forehead); see also Latin ‘frons’ (forehead, front).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌntɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part or length of a property that borders a street, river, or other public way
length of property abutting a street or water

Examples

The property boasts a wide frontage along the lake, making it highly desirable.
Shops with greater street frontage tend to attract more foot traffic.
They bought a house with 100 feet of river frontage for their boats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: façade - the front of a building, especially an imposing or decorative one

Etymology

From French 'frontage', from 'front' (face, front of a building).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the front part or face of a building, especially as it faces a street or open space
the front face of a building

Examples

The shop has a wide frontage facing the main street.
The old house's frontage was decorated with climbing ivy.
They renovated the building's frontage to improve its curb appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - the front of a building, especially an imposing or decorative one

Etymology

From French 'frontage', from 'front' (front), influenced by Latin 'frons' (forehead, front).
