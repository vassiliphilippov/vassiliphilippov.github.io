perversely

1. ADV

/ pərˈvɜrsli / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a deliberate desire to behave unreasonably or contrary to what is expected or desired.
in a deliberately unreasonable or contrary manner

Examples

He perversely refused to help, even though everyone expected his support.
Perversely, she enjoyed doing the exact opposite of what she was told.
They acted perversely, challenging every rule simply for the sake of rebellion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinately - in a stubborn or unyielding manner
  • Antonym: obediently - in an obedient manner

Etymology

From 'perverse', from Latin perversus (turned away, contrary).

2. ADV

/ pərˈvɜrsli / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is deliberately unreasonable, contrary, or obstinate, especially when such behavior goes against what is expected or accepted.
in a deliberately unreasonable or contrary way

Examples

She perversely refused to follow the advice, knowing it was in her best interest.
Even when proven wrong, he perversely insisted he was right.
Some people perversely enjoy doing the opposite of what is expected.

Synset Examples

his perversely erotic notions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinately - in a stubborn and unyielding manner
  • Synonym: contrarily - in an opposing or contrary manner

Etymology

From 'perverse' + '-ly'; 'perverse' from Latin perversus (turned away, contrary).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
