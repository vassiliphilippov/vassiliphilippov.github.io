pursuance

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsu·əns / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː.əns / (BrE)
the continued carrying out of an action or process with the intention of bringing it to completion
the act of continuing something to completion

Examples

The company acted in pursuance of its new sustainability policy.
He remained diligent in his pursuance of a college degree despite many challenges.
Funds were allocated for the pursuance of medical research in rare diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursuit - the act of following or striving to obtain something

Etymology

From Old French *poursuance* (a following, continuation), from *poursuivre* (to pursue).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsuəns / (AmE), / pəˈsjʊəns / (BrE)
The act of pursuing or striving to achieve something, such as a goal or objective.
the act of pursuing a goal

Examples

The new policy was enacted in pursuance of the government's commitment to improve public health.
Her pursuance of academic excellence was evident in her consistent top grades.
Many sacrifices were made in the pursuance of their shared dream.

Synset Examples

the pursuit of love
life is more than the pursuance of fame
a quest for wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursuit - the act of following or trying to achieve something
  • Synonym: quest - a long search for something that is difficult to find

Etymology

From Old French 'poursuance', from 'poursuivre' (to pursue).

Synset POS: n
