Degas

1. PROPN

/ deɪˈgɑ / (AmE), / deɪˈgɑ / (BrE)
Edgar Degas (1834–1917), a French impressionist painter known for his works depicting dancers, scenes of everyday life, and innovative use of movement and light.
French impressionist painter (1834–1917)

Examples

The museum features several masterpieces by Degas in its permanent collection.
Students studied the graceful ballet scenes painted by Degas during art class.
Many art lovers admire the unique techniques that Degas employed in his renderings of dancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Impressionism - an art movement focusing on capturing light and movement

Etymology

French surname, from Edgar Degas, the artist's full name.
