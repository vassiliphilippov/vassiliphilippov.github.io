Examples

4. NOUN

/ ˈkrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrækɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of breaking down large hydrocarbon molecules into smaller ones, especially in oil refining, to produce products such as gasoline.
chemical process in oil refining

Examples

The refinery invested in new cracking equipment to increase its gasoline output.
Thermal cracking was widely used before catalytic methods became popular.
Advances in cracking have improved the efficiency of turning crude oil into usable fuels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reforming - a chemical process that rearranges hydrocarbons in petroleum refining
  • Synonym: distillation - the process of purifying a liquid by heating and cooling

Etymology

From 'crack' (to break), referring to the breaking of molecular bonds in hydrocarbons.
