tonicity

1. NOUN

/ təˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / təˈnɪsɪti / (BrE)
the state of normal elastic tension in living muscles or tissues that enables them to respond to stimuli
normal tension in muscles or tissues
medical

Examples

The doctor measured the tonicity of the patient's arm muscles.
Certain medical conditions can reduce muscle tonicity, making movement more difficult.
Physical therapy is sometimes used to improve the tonicity of weakened muscles.

Synset Examples

the doctor tested my tonicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone - the normal level of firmness or tension in body tissues, especially muscles

Etymology

From Greek tonos (tension) + -icity (quality).
