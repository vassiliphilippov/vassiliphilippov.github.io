Rynchops

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋkɑps / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkɒps / (BrE)
A genus of birds, known as skimmers, in the family Rynchopidae, characterized by their unique beaks with a longer lower mandible used to skim water surfaces for food.
genus of skimmer birds

Examples

The Rynchops is easily recognized by its elongated lower mandible, which helps it catch fish while flying over water.
Researchers studied the feeding behavior of Rynchops in South American wetlands.
Unlike most seabirds, the Rynchops feeds by skimming the water's surface with its bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimmer - a bird species of the genus Rynchops, known for skimming water with its beak

Etymology

From Greek 'rhynchos' (snout, beak) and 'ops' (face), referring to the characteristic beak.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪn.kɑps / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ.kɒps / (BrE)
A genus of birds known as skimmers, characterized by their unique long, thin lower bills and found mainly near water.
a genus of skimmer birds

Examples

The Rynchops genus is easily recognized by its striking bill shape.
Researchers observed several Rynchops feeding along the river at dusk.
Unlike most birds, Rynchops catch their prey by skimming the water's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimmer - a bird of the genus Rynchops with a long lower bill

Etymology

From Greek *rhynchos* (beak, snout) + *ops* (face), referring to the bird's distinctive bill.
