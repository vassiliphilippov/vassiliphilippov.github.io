hatching

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a technique of shading in drawing or engraving consisting of multiple intersecting sets of parallel lines
shading with intersecting lines in art

Examples

The artist used hatching to create shadows and depth in the drawing.
In etching, hatching is an essential technique for modeling light and shade.
Through careful hatching, she achieved a sense of texture on the bird’s feathers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crosshatching - shading with two or more sets of intersecting lines

Etymology

From 'hatch' in the sense of 'to mark with lines', dating from Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which young animals emerge from an egg
young animals emerging from an egg

Examples

The hatching of the chicks occurred early in the morning.
Scientists observed the hatching process in several bird species.
After three weeks of incubation, the turtles began hatching from their eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incubation - the process of keeping eggs warm so they develop properly
  • Degree Lower: laying - the act of producing eggs by an animal

Etymology

From 'hatch' (to emerge from an egg) + '-ing' (verbal noun suffix).
