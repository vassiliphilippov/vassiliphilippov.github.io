sick-abed

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪk əˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɪk əˌbɛd / (BrE)
Confined to bed because of illness.
confined to bed by illness
archaic

Examples

He was sick-abed for a week after catching the flu.
The doctor visited his sick-abed patients each morning.
She wrote letters to her sick-abed father from college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedridden - unable to get out of bed because of illness

Etymology

Formed from 'sick' + 'abed' (in bed). Dated usage, primarily literary or historical.
