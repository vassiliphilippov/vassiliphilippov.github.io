to tyrannize

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɪrənaɪz / (BrE)
to rule or exercise power over someone in a cruel and autocratic manner
exercise cruel or oppressive power

Examples

The dictator continued to tyrannize the people, banning free speech and locking up opponents.
Since joining the team, he has tried to tyrannize his colleagues with unreasonable demands.
For years, the corrupt official would tyrannize anyone who dared to challenge her authority.

Synset Examples

her husband and mother-in-law tyrannize her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
  • Synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone perceived as vulnerable
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression

Etymology

From Greek *turannos* (a tyrant), via Latin *tyrannizare*.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɪrənaɪz / (BrE)
to rule or treat someone in a cruel and oppressive way, especially as a tyrant
rule cruelly or oppressively

Examples

The dictator continued to tyrannize the population for years, showing no mercy.
Some leaders tyrannize their people by restricting basic freedoms and punishing dissent.
History remembers those who tyrannized entire nations with fear and harsh laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship unjustly
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set free from oppression

Etymology

From French *tyranniser*, from Latin *tyrannus* (tyrant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593331-v
Synset POS: v
