tenderness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.dɚ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.də.nɪs / (BrE)
a gentle feeling of concern, care, or affection, especially towards someone who is vulnerable or in need
gentle concern or affection

Examples

She spoke with great tenderness as she comforted the frightened child.
His tenderness toward animals is evident in the way he treats his pets.
There was a look of tenderness in her eyes as she cared for her elderly mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a feeling of liking or loving someone or something
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of concern or interest

Etymology

From Middle French tendresse, from Old French tendre (tender).
