burked

1. ADJ

/ bɜrkt / (AmE), / bɜːkt / (BrE)
Suppressed or prevented quietly or indirectly; stifled without open action.
suppressed or stifled quietly

Examples

The committee burked the proposal before it could be discussed publicly.
Concerns about the project were quietly burked by the administration.
Any criticism of the policy was swiftly burked by management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to forcibly; to prevent from being expressed
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From the verb 'burke,' named after William Burke, a 19th-century criminal, meaning to smother or suppress without open violence.
