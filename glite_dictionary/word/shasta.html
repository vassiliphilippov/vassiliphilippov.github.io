Shasta

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæstə / (AmE), / ˈʃæstə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of northern California and southern Oregon
member of Native American group (California/Oregon)

Examples

The Shasta traditionally lived in small villages along the rivers of northern California.
Anthropologists have studied the culture and language of the Shasta people.
A Shasta elder shared stories about their tribe's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yurok - another Native American people of northern California

2. PROPN

/ ˈʃæstə / (AmE), / ˈʃæstə / (BrE)
a volcanic mountain peak in the Cascade Range of northern California, rising to 14,162 feet (4,317 meters)
volcanic mountain in northern California

Examples

Hundreds of hikers attempt to summit Shasta every summer.
The snow-capped Shasta dominates the northern California skyline.
Legends about Shasta are prominent in local Native American culture.

Etymology

Named after the Shasta people, a Native American tribe indigenous to the area.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃæstə / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːstə / (BrE)
the language of the Shasta people, belonging to the Shastan branch of the Hokan language family, formerly spoken in northern California and southern Oregon.
Indigenous language of the Shasta people

Examples

The last fluent speaker of Shasta passed away in the twentieth century.
Linguists have worked to document the grammar and vocabulary of Shasta.
Efforts are underway to revitalize Shasta among the descendants of the Shasta people.

Etymology

From the name of the Shasta people, possibly from their own language or an exonym used by neighboring peoples.
