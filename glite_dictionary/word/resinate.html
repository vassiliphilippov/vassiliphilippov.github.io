to resinate

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛzɪneɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪneɪt / (BrE)
to impregnate a substance, especially wine, with resin to produce a distinctive flavor
add resin for flavor (esp. wine)

Examples

Some traditional Greek wines are resinated to give them their characteristic taste.
To achieve the authentic flavor, the winemaker decided to resinate the batch using pine resin.
The process of resinating food items can impart a unique aroma and taste.

Synset Examples

Greek wines are often resinated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavor - a distinctive taste, especially of food or drink

Etymology

From 'resin' + '-ate', a suffix forming verbs.
