cryptology

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of codes, ciphers, and methods for secure communication, including the analysis and deciphering of encrypted information
the science of codes and ciphers

Examples

He chose to study cryptology because of his fascination with secret codes.
Advances in cryptology have greatly improved data security in recent years.
Her groundbreaking work in cryptology helped the military to intercept enemy communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptography - the practice and study of writing codes and ciphers

Etymology

From Greek kryptós (hidden) + -logy (study of).
