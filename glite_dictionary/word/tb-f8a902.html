TB

1. NOUN

/ tiː biː / (AmE), / tiː biː / (BrE)
An infectious disease, also known as tuberculosis, caused by the tubercle bacillus, typically affecting the lungs but sometimes other parts of the body.
tuberculosis; infectious lung disease
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that TB is still a major health problem in some countries.
She was hospitalized after being diagnosed with TB.
Routine screening for TB is recommended for people in high-risk occupations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuberculosis - infectious disease mainly affecting the lungs

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'tuberculosis', from Latin 'tuberculum' (small swelling) and '-osis' (condition).

2. NOUN

/ ti bi / (AmE), / tiː biː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 gibibytes or 1,099,511,627,776 bytes; terabyte.
unit of digital information (terabyte)
informal

Examples

The external hard drive has a capacity of 2 TB, which is suitable for backing up large files.
Many cloud storage plans now offer users at least 1 TB of space.
Upgrading to a laptop with a 4 TB SSD can greatly improve performance and storage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gigabyte - a unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 megabytes
  • Degree Higher: petabyte - a unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 terabytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'terabyte', from 'tera-' (meaning trillion) and 'byte'.

3. NOUN

/ tiː biː / (AmE), / tiː biː / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 gigabytes or 10^12 bytes, commonly used to measure digital storage capacity
unit of digital information storage (terabyte)
informal

Examples

My new external hard drive has a capacity of 2 TB, which is enough for all my videos.
The average laptop now comes with at least 1 TB of storage space.
Cloud storage providers often advertise plans with multiple TB available for backups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 gigabytes
  • Degree Lower: GB - a unit of information equal to 1,000 megabytes (gigabyte)
  • Degree Higher: PB - a unit of information equal to 1,000 terabytes (petabyte)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'terabyte', from Greek 'teras' (monster) and 'byte'.
