Haggada

1. NOUN

/ hɑˈgɑdə / (AmE), / həˈɡɑːdə / (BrE)
The non-legal part of Jewish rabbinical literature, comprising legends, parables, and ethical teachings in the Talmud.
non-legal Jewish rabbinical literature

Examples

The rabbi explained a story from the Haggada during the class.
Unlike the Halacha, the Haggada focuses on moral and spiritual teachings.
Many Jewish holiday traditions are described in the Haggada.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Halacha - the legal part of Jewish rabbinical literature

Etymology

From Hebrew *haggādāh* (narration, telling).
