harrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæriər / (AmE), / ˈhæriə / (BrE)
a breed of hound resembling a foxhound but smaller, bred and used chiefly for hunting hares and rabbits
a breed of hunting hound

Examples

The pack of harriers chased the rabbit through the brush.
She adopted a harrier because of its friendly temperament and energy.
The harrier is known for its skill in hunting hares over open countryside.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: foxhound - a large breed of hound used for fox hunting
  • Degree Lower: beagle - a small breed of hound used for hunting

Etymology

From Middle English *harier* (hound used for hunting hares), from *hare* + -ier.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæriər / (AmE), / ˈhæriə / (BrE)
a person or thing that persistently attacks or annoys others
persistent attacker or annoyer

Examples

She became known as a relentless harrier of corrupt officials.
Online, he acted as a harrier, always criticizing and finding faults in others.
The team played the role of harrier, constantly pressuring their opponents through the match.

Synset Examples

the harassers were not members of the regular army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harasser - someone who persistently annoys or attacks others
  • Antonym: defender - someone who protects rather than attacks

3. NOUN

/ ˈhæriər / (AmE), / ˈhæriə / (BrE)
a hawk of the genus Circus, which hunts over open land and marshes, preying on small animals.
a type of hawk that hunts over open ground

Examples

The marsh harrier glided low above the reeds, searching for its next meal.
Several harriers were spotted circling over the flooded meadows at dawn.
A harrier relies on its keen eyesight to spot small mammals hiding in the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey with sharp talons and hooked beak

Etymology

From Middle English *harier*, derived from *harrow*, meaning to harry or plunder.
