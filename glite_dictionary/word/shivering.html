shivering

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪvɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
A bodily reaction involving involuntary shaking or trembling, often caused by cold or the onset of a fever.
involuntary trembling due to cold or fever

Examples

The patient experienced shivering before the fever set in.
Severe shivering can sometimes indicate the start of an infection.
She wrapped herself in blankets to stop the shivering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chills - a feeling of coldness often with shivering, typically due to illness

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪvɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
trembling or shaking slightly, typically as a result of cold, fear, or excitement
trembling or shaking slightly

Examples

She sat by the fire, still shivering from the chilly wind outside.
A shivering child stood at the door, asking to come inside.
Even in the warm room, he felt a shivering sensation along his spine.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trembling - shaking involuntarily, typically from anxiety, excitement, or frailty
  • Synonym: quaking - shaking or trembling, often from fear or cold
  • Antonym: steady - not shaking or trembling; stable

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'shiver', from Middle English 'shiveren', of Germanic origin.
