miscarriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
the spontaneous loss of a pregnancy before the fetus can survive outside the womb
loss of pregnancy
medical

Examples

She suffered a miscarriage during her first trimester, which was a very difficult time for her.
Doctors explained that a miscarriage can occur for many different reasons, most of them beyond anyone's control.
He comforted his partner after the devastating news of their miscarriage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abortion - the deliberate termination of a pregnancy

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + carriage (carrying), originally meaning 'mismanagement'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌkɛrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
an unsuccessful outcome or failure of a plan, action, or process; the act or instance of failing to achieve a desired result
failure of a plan or action

Examples

The miscarriage of the project was a disappointment to everyone involved.
Critics blame poor management for the miscarriage of the new policy.
After a miscarriage of the negotiations, both companies walked away from the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French 'mescarriage', from 'mes-' (wrongly) + 'carriage' (carrying)
