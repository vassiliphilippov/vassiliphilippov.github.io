fo'c'sle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊksəl / (AmE), / ˈfəʊksəl / (BrE)
the living quarters located in the forward part of a ship, typically used by the crew
crew's quarters at the bow of a ship

Examples

The crew gathered in the fo'c'sle to escape the storm raging above deck.
He spent much of his early career sleeping in cramped fo'c'sle bunks on fishing boats.
Strong waves pounded the fo'c'sle as the ship sailed through rough seas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forecastle - the forward part of a ship's upper deck; the crew's quarters

Etymology

Contraction of 'forecastle', from Middle English 'forecastel' (forward castle, structure at the bow of a ship).
