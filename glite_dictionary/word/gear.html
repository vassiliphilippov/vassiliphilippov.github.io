gear

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪə / (BrE)
The equipment or apparatus needed for a particular activity or purpose.
equipment needed for an activity

Examples

Make sure you pack all your gear before going on the camping trip.
The players arrived early to check their gear before the match began.
He spent a lot of money on hiking gear last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: kit - a set of articles or equipment needed for a hobby or activity

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gervi' (clothing, gear, adornment).

2. NOUN

/ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪə / (BrE)
a mechanism or system of parts for transmitting motion and force in a machine, especially for a specific function such as steering in a vehicle
mechanism transmitting motion in machines

Examples

The steering gear of the car failed, causing a loss of control.
Engineers inspected the elevator's gear mechanism for signs of wear and tear.
A malfunction in the cooling gear forced the machine to stop operating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gearbox - a metal case containing a set of gears in a vehicle
  • Synonym: transmission - a system in a vehicle that transmits power from engine to wheels

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gervi' (equipment, apparatus).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03436153-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪə / (BrE)
a toothed wheel that meshes with another to change the speed or direction of movement in a machine
toothed wheel in machinery

Examples

The bicycle's lowest gear makes it easier to pedal up steep hills.
The mechanic replaced the worn gear inside the gearbox.
You can hear the gear clicking into place when you change speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cog - a tooth on the rim of a wheel or a wheel with such teeth, especially in machinery
  • Degree Lower: wheel - a circular object that revolves on an axle

Etymology

From Old Norse 'geiri' (equipment), Middle English 'gere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03435461-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪə / (BrE)
to adjust or design something so that it is suitable for a particular purpose, audience, or situation
adjust for a purpose or audience

Examples

The workshop is geared towards beginners with no prior experience.
We gear our marketing strategies to the needs of young professionals.
The curriculum has been geared specifically for advanced students.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - to direct something toward a particular group or purpose

5. NOUN

/ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪə / (BrE)
a system of connected rotating wheels with teeth (gears) that transmit force or change motion and torque in machinery
interconnected rotating wheels in machinery

Examples

The mechanic inspected the gear inside the engine to find the source of the noise.
If a single gear in the transmission fails, the whole system can stop working.
Engineers use complex gear assemblies to control movement in robotic arms.

Synset Examples

the fool got his tie caught in the geartrain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gear train - a connected series of gears that transmits motion

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gerð' (a task, affair, gear), related to Old English 'gearwe' (equipment, apparatus).
