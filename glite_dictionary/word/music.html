music

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
The art of arranging sounds in time to produce a composition through the elements of melody, harmony, rhythm, and timbre, often expressing ideas or emotions.
art of arranging sounds

Examples

I like to listen to classical music while studying.
The band played live music at the festival last night.
She has a deep passion for composing music that moves her audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song - a musical composition with lyrics intended to be sung
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of sound or music

Etymology

From Greek 'mousikē' (art of the Muses) via Latin 'musica' and Old French 'musique'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.zɪk / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.zɪk / (BrE)
The art or practice of arranging sounds produced by voices or instruments in a way that is pleasing or meaningful, often following a particular rhythm, melody, or harmony.
art of arranging sounds; musical sounds

Examples

She enjoys listening to classical music while studying.
Live music filled the air during the festival.
He can play several instruments and composes his own music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melody - a sequence of musical notes that are perceived as a single entity
  • Antonym: noise - unpleasant or unwanted sound

Etymology

From Old French musique, from Latin musica, from Greek mousikē (art of the Muses).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05726882-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
any sound or combination of sounds that is pleasant or harmonious to hear, not necessarily created as art.
pleasing or harmonious sounds

Examples

She found the music of the rain soothing after a long day.
The gentle music of laughter filled the garden during the party.
There was a certain music in the rustling leaves outside his window.

Synset Examples

he fell asleep to the music of the wind chimes

Etymology

From Greek mousikē (art of the Muses), via Latin musica.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmjuː.zɪk / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.zɪk / (BrE)
The activity or art of producing sounds with the voice or instruments, especially in a way that is expressive or enjoyable.
the act of producing musical sounds

Examples

He devoted his life to music and performed in orchestras around the world.
The sound of children making music filled the classroom with joy.
She finds comfort in listening to or creating music after a stressful day.

Synset Examples

his music was his central interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song - a short musical composition with words
  • Antonym: silence - absence of sound or music

Etymology

From Greek mousikē (art of the Muses), via Latin musica and Old French musique.
