to threaten

1. VERB

/ ˈθrɛtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛtn̩ / (BrE)
to pose a danger to someone or something; to be likely to cause harm or damage
pose danger to; endanger

Examples

The invasive species threatens native plants in the region.
Rising sea levels threaten many coastal communities around the world.
Unchecked deforestation threatens the survival of several animal species.

Synset Examples

The pollution is endangering the crops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endanger - to put someone or something at risk or in danger

Etymology

From Old English 'þreatian', meaning to urge or to menace, from 'þreat' (threat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02702962-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈθrɛt.ən / (AmE), / ˈθrɛt.ən / (BrE)
(of something bad or unpleasant) to seem likely to happen or be imminent.
seem likely to happen (esp. something bad)

Examples

Dark clouds threaten heavy rain for the afternoon.
Economic recession threatens several industries this year.
A storm threatens to disrupt tomorrow's outdoor event.

Synset Examples

The clouds threaten rain
Danger threatens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menace - to seem likely to cause harm or danger

Etymology

From Old English 'threatian', from 'threat' + '-en'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873444-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈθrɛtən / (AmE), / ˈθrɛtən / (BrE)
to state one's intention to cause harm or punishment to someone, often in order to intimidate or pressure them
state intent to cause harm or punishment

Examples

She told the police that her neighbor had threatened her after an argument about the fence.
The manager threatened to fire anyone who arrived late again.
He was arrested because he threatened the witnesses before the trial.

Synset Examples

He threatened me when I tried to call the police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menace - to threaten, especially as a danger or with harm
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less afraid or anxious

Etymology

From Old English þreatian, from þreat (threat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873067-v
Synset POS: v
