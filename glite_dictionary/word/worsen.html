to worsen

1. VERB

/ ˈwɜrsən / (AmE), / ˈwɜːsən / (BrE)
to make or become worse; to deteriorate in quality, condition, or situation
become or make worse

Examples

The patient's symptoms began to worsen after he stopped his medication.
If tensions worsen, there could be further unrest in the city.
Adding more debt will only worsen the company’s financial situation.

Synset Examples

This drug aggravates the pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aggravate - to make a problem, injury, or situation worse
  • Antonym: to improve - to make or become better

Etymology

Formed from 'worse' with the suffix '-en', in analogy with similar verbs like 'soften'.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɜːrsən / (AmE), / ˈwɜːsən / (BrE)
to become worse or make something worse
become worse; make worse

Examples

Doctors fear the patient's condition may worsen overnight.
Heavy rainfall will worsen the flooding in some areas.
If you don't address the problem now, it will only worsen with time.

Synset Examples

Conditions in the slum worsened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to improve - to become better or make something better
  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse

Etymology

Formed from 'worse' + the verb-forming suffix '-en'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00204228-v
Synset POS: v
