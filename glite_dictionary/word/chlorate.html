chlorate

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːreɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɔːreɪt / (BrE)
a salt derived from chloric acid, containing the anion ClO₃⁻
salt of chloric acid
medical

Examples

Sodium chlorate is commonly used as a herbicide in agriculture.
The laboratory ordered a shipment of potassium chlorate for use in chemical experiments.
Mixing chlorate compounds with organic material can be extremely dangerous.

Etymology

From French 'chlorate,' from 'chlore' (chlorine) + '-ate' (a salt or ester suffix).
