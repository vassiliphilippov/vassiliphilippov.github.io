Abel Janszoon Tasman

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪbəl ˈjænzun ˈtæzmən / (AmE), / ˈeɪbəl ˈjænzən ˈtæzmən / (BrE)
(1603–1659) Dutch explorer and navigator, the first European known to reach Tasmania and New Zealand.
Dutch navigator; explorer of Tasmania and New Zealand

Examples

Abel Janszoon Tasman charted the coastlines of Tasmania and New Zealand during his 17th-century voyages.
The island of Tasmania is named after the explorer Abel Janszoon Tasman.
Historians recognize Abel Janszoon Tasman as a pioneer in the European exploration of Australasia.
