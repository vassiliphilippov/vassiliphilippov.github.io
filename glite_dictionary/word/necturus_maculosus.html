Necturus maculosus

1. NOUN

/ nɛkˈtʊrəs məˈkjuloʊsəs / (AmE), / nɛkˈtjʊərəs məˈkjuːləsəs / (BrE)
An aquatic North American salamander with red feathery external gills, commonly known as the mudpuppy.
species of aquatic salamander (mudpuppy)

Examples

The Necturus maculosus spends its entire life in water and is often found under rocks in streams or lakes.
Biologists conducted a study on the population decline of Necturus maculosus in the Great Lakes region.
Unlike many amphibians, the Necturus maculosus retains its external gills throughout adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mudpuppy - common name for Necturus maculosus, an aquatic salamander

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Necturus and species epithet maculosus meaning 'spotted' in Latin.
