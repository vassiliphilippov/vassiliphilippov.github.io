officialdom

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɪʃəldəm / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəldəm / (BrE)
the officials collectively, especially those considered as a group within government or administration
the body of officials in government

Examples

Critics argue that officialdom is resistant to change and innovation.
She struggled with officialdom when applying for a business license.
The report highlights the influence of officialdom in shaping public policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucracy - the administrative system governing a state, often seen as complex or rigid

Etymology

Formed from 'official' + '-dom' (denoting domain, collection, or condition) in late 19th-century English.
