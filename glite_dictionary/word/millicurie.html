millicurie

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪˈkjʊri / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈkjʊəri / (BrE)
a unit of radioactivity equal to one thousandth of a curie, commonly used in measuring radioactive substances
unit of radioactivity (1/1000 curie)

Examples

The radioactive sample measured 5 millicuries, making it suitable for medical use.
Researchers used a dose of 0.2 millicurie in the experiment.
Safety guidelines recommend not handling sources above ten millicuries without protection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: curie - a unit of radioactivity equal to the radioactivity of one gram of radium-226

Etymology

Formed from 'milli-' (meaning one thousandth) and 'curie' (named after Marie and Pierre Curie, pioneers in radioactivity).
