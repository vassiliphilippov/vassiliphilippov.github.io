Sihasapa

1. NOUN

/ siː.hɑːˈsɑː.pə / (AmE), / siː.hɑːˈsɑː.pə / (BrE)
A member of a division of the Teton Sioux people, traditionally living in the western Dakotas on the Great Plains.
member of a Teton Sioux tribal division

Examples

The Sihasapa maintained a distinct cultural identity among the Teton Sioux.
Several Sihasapa leaders played important roles in negotiations during the 19th century.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions of the Sihasapa for decades.

Etymology

From the Lakota language, literally meaning 'Blackfoot'.
