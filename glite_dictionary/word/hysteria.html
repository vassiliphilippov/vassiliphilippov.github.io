hysteria

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
a state of uncontrolled excitement or emotion, especially extreme fear or panic that cannot be reasoned with
uncontrollable fear or excitement

Examples

When the fire alarm sounded, hysteria quickly spread through the office.
Reports of the disease outbreak led to widespread hysteria in the community.
The crowd erupted into hysteria as the band took the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - a state of tranquility or a lack of strong emotion

Etymology

From Greek 'hystera' (womb), reflecting an outdated belief about its origin.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A state of extreme excitement or uncontrollable emotion, often characterized by irrational or excessive behavior.
extreme emotional agitation

Examples

The crowd erupted into hysteria when the band appeared on stage.
Reports of the disease spread hysteria throughout the small town.
Her reaction bordered on hysteria as she kept shouting uncontrollably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden overpowering fear, often affecting groups
  • Antonym: calm - state of being quiet and peaceful

Etymology

From Greek hystera (womb), via Latin hysteria, originally relating to a supposed medical disorder in women.

3. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A neurotic disorder marked by violent emotional outbreaks and disturbances of sensory and motor functions, historically used in psychiatry.
neurotic disorder with emotional and motor disturbances
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with hysteria after her repeated emotional collapses.
In the nineteenth century, women with unexplained physical symptoms were often labeled as suffering from hysteria.
Modern psychiatry has largely replaced the diagnosis of hysteria with more precise medical terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversion disorder - psychiatric disorder with neurological symptoms not explained by medical conditions

Etymology

From Greek *hystera* (womb), based on ancient theories about its cause.
