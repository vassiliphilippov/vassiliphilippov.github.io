Anjou

1. PROPN

/ ænˈʒu / (AmE), / ænˈʒuː / (BrE)
A former province of western France located in the Loire valley, known for its historical significance.
former province of western France

Examples

The vineyards of Anjou are famous for producing excellent wines.
During the Middle Ages, Anjou played a crucial role in French and English history.
Many castles in Anjou attract tourists interested in French heritage.

Etymology

From the French 'Anjou', originally the name of the medieval county and province.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.dʒu / (AmE), / ˈæn.ʒuː / (BrE)
A variety of pear with firm flesh and green skin, commonly eaten fresh or used in cooking.
variety of pear with green skin

Examples

She picked a ripe Anjou from the fruit basket for her breakfast.
The salad was made with slices of Anjou for extra sweetness and crunch.
At the market, green Anjou pears were displayed next to the red varieties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pear - an edible fruit produced by the pear tree

Etymology

From French 'Anjou', the name of a region in western France associated with this type of pear.
