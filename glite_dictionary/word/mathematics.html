mathematics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmatɪks / (BrE)
the abstract science of number, quantity, and space, either as abstract concepts (pure mathematics) or as applied to other disciplines (applied mathematics)
the study of numbers, quantity, and space

Examples

He has always been fascinated by mathematics and hopes to become a professor.
Mathematics is essential for fields such as physics, engineering, and economics.
Despite its complexity, mathematics provides a universal language for solving problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arithmetic - the branch of mathematics dealing with numbers and basic operations
  • Synonym: geometry - the branch of mathematics concerning the properties of space
  • Synonym: mathematician - a person skilled or specializing in mathematics

Etymology

From Latin mathematica, from Greek mathēmatikē (the science of learning); related to mathēma (knowledge, learning).
