Cnemidophorus

1. NOUN

/ nɛməˈdɑfərəs / (AmE), / nɛmɪˈdɪfəˌrəs / (BrE)
A genus of lizards known as whiptails, native to the Americas and recognized for their long, slender tails and active behavior.
a genus of whiptail lizards

Examples

The biologist studied several species of Cnemidophorus in their natural habitat.
Many Cnemidophorus can reproduce through parthenogenesis, making them unique among lizards.
The Cnemidophorus genus is characterized by distinctive color patterns and swift movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiptail lizard - common name for lizards of the Cnemidophorus genus

Etymology

From Greek 'knēmē' (leg) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to characteristic scales on the legs.

2. NOUN

/ nɛmɪˈdɒfərəs / (AmE), / nɛmɪˈdɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of whiptail lizards native to the Americas, formerly used for a large group of species now reclassified into several genera.
a genus of whiptail lizards

Examples

The genus Cnemidophorus includes several species of fast-moving whiptail lizards.
Researchers recently reclassified many species formerly placed within Cnemidophorus.
Naturalists have observed Cnemidophorus in desert and grassland habitats throughout the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiptail - common name of lizards in this group

Etymology

From Greek *knēmīdophóros* meaning 'greave-wearing', referring to the armored appearance of the lizards' legs.
