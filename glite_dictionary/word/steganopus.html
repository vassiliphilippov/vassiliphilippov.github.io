Steganopus

1. PROPN

/ stiˈɡænəpəs / (AmE), / stiˈɡanəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Phalaropidae, known for species such as the Wilson's phalarope.
genus of birds in Phalaropidae

Examples

The Wilson's phalarope is the only North American species in the genus Steganopus.
Ornithologists frequently study Steganopus for its unique breeding behavior.
The migratory patterns of Steganopus have fascinated bird watchers for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phalaropus - another genus in the family Phalaropidae (phalaropes)

Etymology

From Greek steganos (covered) + pous (foot), referring to the structure of the bird's feet.
