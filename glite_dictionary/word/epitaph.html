epitaph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌtæf / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪtɑːf / (BrE)
a short statement written in memory of a dead person, typically inscribed on a tombstone
inscription in memory of the dead

Examples

The epitaph on her gravestone reads, 'Beloved mother and friend.'
He spent hours composing a fitting epitaph for his late uncle.
Many famous writers have written their own epitaphs before they died.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obituary - a published notice of a person's death, often with a biography

Etymology

From Greek epitaphion (funeral oration), from epi (upon) + taphos (tomb).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌtæf / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪtɑːf / (BrE)
An inscription on a tombstone or monument in memory of the person buried there.
inscription on a tombstone

Examples

The old churchyard was filled with graves, each marked by a unique epitaph.
She wrote a heartfelt epitaph for her late husband, capturing his love of life.
Many visitors stopped to read the epitaph engraved on the ancient stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obituary - a published notice of a person's death, often with a brief biography
  • Synonym: eulogy - a speech or piece of writing that praises someone highly, especially after death

Etymology

From Greek epitaphion (funeral oration), from epi (upon) + taphos (tomb).
