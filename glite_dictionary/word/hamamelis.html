Hamamelis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈmiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees, commonly known as witch hazel, native to North America and Asia.
genus of shrubs (witch hazel)

Examples

The garden featured a variety of Hamamelis that bloomed in late autumn.
Many traditional remedies use extracts from the Hamamelis plant.
Botanists studied the unique flowering cycle of Hamamelis species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - deciduous shrub; plant of the genus Hamamelis

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hamamelis', based on Greek 'hama' (together) + 'mēlon' (apple) for simultaneous fruit and flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈmiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees known as witch hazels, native to North America and Asia.
genus of witch hazels (shrubs or small trees)

Examples

The Hamamelis in the garden blooms with bright yellow flowers in late winter.
Botanists studied several species within the Hamamelis genus to understand their medicinal properties.
Many gardeners plant Hamamelis for their attractive, fragrant flowers and fall foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - a shrub or small tree in the genus Hamamelis with medicinal bark and twigs

Etymology

From Greek *hamamelis*, meaning 'a kind of medlar or service tree'.
