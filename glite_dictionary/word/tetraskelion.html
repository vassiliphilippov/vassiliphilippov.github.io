tetraskelion

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈskɛliən / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈskɛlɪən / (BrE)
A figure consisting of four stylized human arms, legs, or bent lines radiating symmetrically from a central point.
figure with four bent arms radiating from center

Examples

The ancient coins featured a tetraskelion at their center, symbolizing movement and unity.
A tetraskelion design is often found in Mediterranean archaeological sites.
The logo was inspired by the tetraskelion, representing the four directions.

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) + skelos (leg).
