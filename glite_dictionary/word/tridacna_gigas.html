Tridacna gigas

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈdæknə ˈdʒaɪɡəs / (AmE), / traɪˈdæknə ˈdʒɪɡəs / (BrE)
a species of giant clam found on coral reefs of the Indo-Pacific region, often weighing up to 500 pounds
giant clam species of Indo-Pacific reefs

Examples

Marine biologists studied the massive Tridacna gigas specimens on the coral reef.
The Tridacna gigas can live for more than a hundred years in its natural habitat.
Divers were amazed by the colorful mantle of a Tridacna gigas embedded in the coral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant clam - very large marine clam of the genus Tridacna

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Tridacna' from Greek 'tridákna' (kind of shellfish), species name 'gigas' meaning 'giant' in Latin.
