insinuatingly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪnjʊeɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
in a manner that suggests or implies something, often slyly or indirectly
in a sly or suggestive manner

Examples

She smiled insinuatingly, leaving me to wonder what she really meant.
The salesman leaned forward insinuatingly, hinting at a secret offer.
He spoke insinuatingly about their past, making everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

the art book has art to sell, insinuatingly, and for a purpose, like the American muse, which has in fact a tradition to sell, and one which doesn't exist, in painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestively - in a manner that suggests something indirectly, especially something improper or hidden

Etymology

From 'insinuating' (from Latin insinuare 'to introduce by windings') + '-ly'.
