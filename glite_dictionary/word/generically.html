generically

1. ADV

/ dʒəˈnɛrɪkli / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrɪkli / (BrE)
Without referring to a specific brand name or trademark, usually in relation to products or pharmaceuticals.
without a brand or trademark name

Examples

Many medicines are sold generically after their patent expires.
Customers often choose products that are marketed generically to save money.
The company decided to package their cereal generically rather than creating a branded label.

Synset Examples

we market these drugs generically

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: generically - in a general or non-specific way

2. ADV

/ dʒəˈnɛrɪkli / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to a genus; in terms of belonging to the same genus in biological classification.
in terms of genus (biology)

Examples

The two species are not related generically, though they appear similar.
Plants in this family are classified generically rather than by their physical traits alone.
The animals cannot be grouped generically because they belong to different genera.

Synset Examples

these animals are not related generically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specifically - in a way relating to a particular species, not genus

Etymology

From generic + -ally; 'generic' from Latin *genus* (race, kind).
