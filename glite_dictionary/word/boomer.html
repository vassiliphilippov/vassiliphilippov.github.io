boomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːmər / (AmE), / ˈbuːmə / (BrE)
a person born during the baby boom, especially in the years following the Second World War, typically between 1946 and 1964
member of the baby boom generation

Examples

Many boomers are now approaching retirement age.
The government developed new policies to address the needs of boomers.
As a boomer, she remembers the social changes of the 1960s.

Synset Examples

they expanded the schools for a generation of baby boomers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby boomer - someone born during the baby boom period (1946–1964)

Etymology

From 'baby boom' plus the agent noun suffix '-er'.
