well-read

1. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈrɛd / (AmE), / wɛl ˈrɛd / (BrE)
having a lot of knowledge because of reading a lot of books and literature
knowledgeable from extensive reading

Examples

He is a well-read scholar who can discuss a wide range of topics.
Many consider her the most well-read person in the department.
She became well-read in philosophy after years of study.

Synset Examples

respect for a well-read man
well-read in medieval history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learned - having a lot of knowledge that is usually acquired by studying
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness; uninformed

2. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈrɛd / (AmE), / wɛl ˈrɛd / (BrE)
having acquired much knowledge from reading; knowledgeable and educated through extensive reading
knowledgeable from extensive reading

Examples

She is a well-read historian who can cite a vast number of sources.
Despite his young age, Tom is already well-read in both classic and modern literature.
The well-read professor captivated the class with his insights into Renaissance poetry.

Synset Examples

knowing instructors
a knowledgeable critic
a knowledgeable audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowledgeable - having or showing knowledge or intelligence; well-informed
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness; uninformed
