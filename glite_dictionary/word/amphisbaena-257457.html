amphisbaena

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fɪzˈbiː.nə / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪzˈbiː.nə / (BrE)
A mythical serpent, typically described in classical mythology as having a head at each end of its body.
mythical serpent with a head at each end

Examples

In ancient texts, the amphisbaena is feared for its ability to attack from either end.
The statue in the museum depicts a coiled amphisbaena with two fierce heads.
According to legend, the blood of an amphisbaena was believed to have magical properties.

Etymology

From Latin amphisbaena, from Greek amphisbaina, from amphis (both ways) + bainein (to go), describing the creature's supposed ability to move in either direction.
