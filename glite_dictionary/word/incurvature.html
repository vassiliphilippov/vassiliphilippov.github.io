incurvature

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɜːrvjər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːvjʊə / (BrE)
a shape or state in which something curves or bends inward
an inward curve or bend

Examples

The incurvature of the leaf's edge was pronounced, giving it a unique appearance.
Some medical conditions lead to the incurvature of the spine, requiring specialized treatment.
The artist emphasized the incurvature of the sculpture to create a sense of movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvature - the act or state of being curved
  • Antonym: excavation - a hollow or depression formed by removing material (opposite direction of incurvation)
  • Antonym: protrusion - a shape that extends or bulges outward (opposite meaning)

Etymology

From Latin incurvatura, from incurvare (to bend inward) + -ura (noun suffix).
