ergotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɜrɡəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɜːɡəˈtrəʊpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting physiological processes, especially neural, associated with energy expenditure or activity, as opposed to restorative or rest processes.
relating to energy-expending activity in physiology
medical

Examples

The ergotropic response prepares the body for action and increased metabolic demand.
During stress, the nervous system may shift into a more ergotropic state.
Certain medications can affect ergotropic processes by stimulating energy expenditure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trophotropic - relating to physiological states or nervous system activity associated with rest, restoration, or energy conservation

Etymology

From Greek ergon (work) + -tropic (turning toward, from tropos).
