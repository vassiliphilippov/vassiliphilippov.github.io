infructescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfrəkˈtɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrʌkˈtɛsəns / (BrE)
The collective fruit structure that develops from the flowering part of a plant after flowering, forming the fruiting stage of an inflorescence.
fruiting stage of an inflorescence

Examples

The pineapple is an example of an infructescence, since it forms from a cluster of flowers.
After pollination, the infructescence develops as the flowers turn into fruit.
The botanist studied the infructescence to identify the plant species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflorescence - the complete flower head of a plant including stems, stalks, bracts, and flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'infructescentia', from 'in-' (in, on) + 'fructescere' (to bear fruit).
