railroading

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌroʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of designing, constructing, and operating railroads
the business of building and running railroads

Examples

He developed a lifelong passion for railroading after visiting the local train yard as a child.
The golden age of American railroading transformed the nation’s economy and transportation.
She studied railroading at university, specializing in the design of high-speed rail lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway engineering - the technical field of designing railways

Etymology

From 'railroad' + '-ing', denoting the activity or process.
