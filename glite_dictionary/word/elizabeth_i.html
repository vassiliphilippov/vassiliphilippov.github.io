Elizabeth I

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(1533–1603) Queen of England and Ireland from 1558 to 1603, known for restoring Protestantism, defeating the Spanish Armada, and presiding over a period of English cultural flourishing.
Queen of England, 1558–1603

Examples

Historians regard Elizabeth I as one of England’s greatest monarchs.
The defeat of the Spanish Armada occurred during the reign of Elizabeth I.
Shakespeare wrote many of his plays while Elizabeth I was on the throne.

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəˌbɛθ ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
Queen of England from 1558 to 1603, known for restoring Protestantism and leading England during a period of prosperity and cultural achievement.
Queen of England, 1558–1603

Examples

Many consider Elizabeth I to be one of England's greatest monarchs due to her leadership and vision.
The defeat of the Spanish Armada occurred during Elizabeth I's reign in 1588.
Elizabeth I was known for her intelligence and support of artists like Shakespeare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mary I - Queen of England preceding Elizabeth I, known for her Catholic restoration
  • Synonym: Elizabeth II - Queen of the United Kingdom from 1952 to 2022

3. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəˌbɛθ ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
the Queen of England and Ireland from 1558 to 1603, known for re-establishing Protestantism and presiding over a period of English cultural flourishing.
Queen of England (1533-1603)

Examples

Many historians regard Elizabeth I's reign as a golden age for England.
Shakespeare wrote several of his greatest plays during the time of Elizabeth I.
The defeat of the Spanish Armada was a major victory for Elizabeth I and England.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mary I - Queen of England before Elizabeth I, also known as 'Bloody Mary'
  • Degree Higher: Elizabeth II - current or recent Queen of the United Kingdom (reigned 1952-2022)
