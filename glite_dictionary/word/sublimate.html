sublimate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌblɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌblɪˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a substance that has been formed by the process of sublimation, especially when a solid turns directly into vapor and then back into a solid without passing through the liquid state
a solid formed by sublimation

Examples

The chemist collected the sublimate from the cold surface of the glass tube.
Purple sublimate formed during the reaction as the solid changed directly to vapor and cooled.
After the process was completed, only a trace of sublimate remained in the apparatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sublimation - the process of a solid turning directly into vapor without becoming liquid

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sublīmātum, past participle of Latin sublīmāre (to raise, to elevate).
