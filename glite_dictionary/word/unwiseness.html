unwiseness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwaɪznəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwaɪznɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unwise or lacking in good judgment
lack of wisdom or good judgment

Examples

His unwiseness in handling the negotiations led to significant losses.
The history book chronicled the unwiseness of the era's leaders.
Many regret decisions made in unwiseness during their youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of being wise

Etymology

Formed from 'unwise' + '-ness'.
