Guaiacum officinale

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡwaɪ.ə.kəm ˌɑː.fɪˈsaɪ.nəli / (AmE), / ˌɡwaɪ.əkəm ˌɒfɪˈsaɪnəli / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, known for producing lignum vitae wood, one of the hardest commercial timbers, and a medicinal resin.
species of Caribbean evergreen tree

Examples

The dense wood of Guaiacum officinale has long been prized for its durability and strength.
Traditional remedies often include resin extracted from Guaiacum officinale for its purported medicinal properties.
Many coastal gardens feature Guaiacum officinale for its attractive evergreen foliage and blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lignum vitae - very hard, dense wood produced by Guaiacum species

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Guaiacum' derived from the Taíno word 'guayacán'; 'officinale' denotes its use in medicine.
