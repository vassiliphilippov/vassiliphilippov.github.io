psammoma

1. NOUN

/ sæmˈoʊmə / (AmE), / sæmˈəʊmə / (BrE)
a type of tumor arising from fibrous tissue of the meninges, choroid plexus, or similar brain-associated structures, often containing sand-like calcified particles
tumor with sand-like particles (medical)
medical

Examples

The biopsy showed that the tumor was a psammoma with characteristic sand-like calcifications.
A psammoma often arises in the brain’s meninges or choroid plexus.
Microscopic examination revealed the presence of multiple psammomas in the tissue sample.

Etymology

From Greek psammos (sand) + -oma (tumor).
