adenomyosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.əˌnoʊ.maɪ.oʊ.sɑːrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌad.əʊ.maɪ.əʊ.sɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor composed of both glandular (adeno-) and muscular (myo-) tissue elements, often found in certain organs and characterized by aggressive growth
malignant tumor with glandular and muscle elements
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with an adenomyosarcoma after a biopsy of the tumor.
Early detection of adenomyosarcoma can improve treatment outcomes for affected individuals.
Researchers are studying new therapies for aggressive cancers like adenomyosarcoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue

Etymology

From adeno- (relating to glands), myo- (relating to muscle), and sarcoma (malignant tumor of connective tissue), from Greek sarkōma.
