woodsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊd.zi / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.zi / (BrE)
Having the characteristics or scent of woods or forests; suggestive of woodland.
having the qualities of woods or forests

Examples

The candle has a pleasant woodsy aroma that reminds me of hiking in the forest.
They bought a cabin in a woodsy area far from the city.
Her perfume has subtle woodsy notes mixed with hints of spice.

Synset Examples

a fresh woodsy fragrance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with woods or trees
  • Synonym: earthy - reminiscent of earth, soil, or nature

/ ˈwʊd.zi / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.zi / (BrE)
having many trees or characteristic of the woods; abounding in trees.
having many trees; like the woods

Examples

We found a woodsy cabin at the edge of the lake.
Her perfume had a woodsy scent, reminding me of pine forests.
They chose a woodsy spot for their picnic, surrounded by tall trees.

Synset Examples
