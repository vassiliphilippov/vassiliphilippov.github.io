save-all

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈseɪvˌɔːl / (BrE)
a device or receptacle used to collect waste products so they can be used or recycled later
device for catching waste for reuse

Examples

The factory installed a save-all to reduce material waste during production.
After pouring the excess paint into the save-all, she was able to use it for touch-ups later.
In papermaking, a save-all is essential for capturing valuable fiber that would otherwise be lost.

Etymology

From 'save' + 'all', referring to saving all waste materials.

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪv ˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈseɪv ɔːl / (BrE)
a net hung between a ship and the pier when loading, used to catch items that may fall overboard
net hung between ship and pier to catch falling items

Examples

The crew secured the save-all before unloading the cargo to ensure nothing was lost overboard.
A sturdy save-all is essential for preventing tools and supplies from falling into the harbor during loading.
As the dockworkers operated the crane, a box landed on the save-all instead of splashing into the water.

Etymology

From 'save' + 'all', describing its function to save all items that might otherwise be lost.
