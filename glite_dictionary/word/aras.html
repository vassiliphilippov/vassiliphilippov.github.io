Aras

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːrɑːs / (AmE), / ˈɑːrɑːs / (BrE)
a river in southwestern Asia that rises in northeastern Turkey and flows eastward through Armenia and Azerbaijan into the Caspian Sea; historically known as the Araxes.
river in Turkey, Armenia, and Azerbaijan

Examples

The Aras River forms part of the natural border between Armenia and Turkey.
Many ancient historians wrote about the significance of the Aras in regional trade.
Flooding along the Aras can affect communities in both Armenia and Azerbaijan.

Etymology

Historically known as Araxes in ancient Greek and Latin sources.
