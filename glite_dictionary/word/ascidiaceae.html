Ascidiaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌsɪdiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / əˌsɪdɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of simple marine animals known as sea squirts, sometimes classified as an order, found in shallow waters.
family of sea squirts (marine animals)

Examples

Members of the Ascidiaceae are filter-feeding marine animals commonly known as sea squirts.
Some species in the Ascidiaceae family attach themselves to rocks or ship hulls.
Marine biologists study the Ascidiaceae to understand their evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea squirt - marine invertebrate with a saclike body

Etymology

From Latin ascidium (a kind of flask or bottle) + -aceae (a standard family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ əˌsɪdiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / əˌsɪdɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of sea squirts, sometimes classified as an order, consisting of sessile marine filter-feeders with sac-like bodies.
a family of sea squirts (marine animals)

Examples

Researchers collected specimens from the Ascidiaceae family to study their unique reproductive strategies.
Some members of the Ascidiaceae are known to form dense colonies on rocky seafloors.
The Ascidiaceae are important filter feeders, helping to maintain water clarity in marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea squirt - a simple marine animal with a sac-like body

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ascidiaceae', based on 'Ascidiacea' (the type genus 'Ascidia').
