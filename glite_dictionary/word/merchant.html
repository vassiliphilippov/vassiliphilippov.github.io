merchant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝː.tʃənt / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tʃənt / (BrE)
a person or company involved in the buying and selling of goods, especially one engaged in trade on a large scale or retail business
person engaged in buying and selling goods

Examples

The village merchant sold everything from spices to silks.
As a respected merchant, she built a thriving business in the city center.
Early explorers often partnered with a merchant to finance their voyages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trader - person who buys and sells goods or assets
  • Antonym: customer - person who purchases goods or services

Etymology

From Old French *marcheant*, from Late Latin *mercatantem*, from Latin *mercari* (to trade, to deal).
