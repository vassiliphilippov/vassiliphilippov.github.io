ropebark

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊpˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpˌbɑːk / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its tough, flexible branches and pliable bark, and bearing small yellow flowers.
deciduous shrub of eastern North America

Examples

The tough bark of the ropebark shrub was once used to make cords and rope.
In spring, the ropebark produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
Botanists studying eastern forests often encounter the distinctive ropebark along riverbanks.
