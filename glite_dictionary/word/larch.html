larch

1. NOUN

/ lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
The wood of a larch tree, valued for its strength and resistance to decay.
wood from a larch tree

Examples

The boat was built from sturdy larch to withstand cold northern waters.
Furniture made of larch is both attractive and durable.
They chose larch for the deck because it weathers well outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - wood from a pine tree

Etymology

From Latin *larix*, *laricis* (larch tree).

2. NOUN

/ lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous coniferous tree of the genus Larix, with needle-like leaves that are shed annually
deciduous coniferous tree (Larix genus)

Examples

In autumn, the needles of the larch turn golden before shedding.
The old larch by the lake has grown steadily for decades.
Many cabins are built using tough, durable larch wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - an evergreen conifer of the genus Abies
  • Synonym: pine - an evergreen conifer of the genus Pinus

Etymology

From Middle High German 'larche', from Latin 'larix'.
