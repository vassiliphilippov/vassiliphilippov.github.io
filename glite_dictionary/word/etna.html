etna

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛtnə / (AmE), / ˈɛtnə / (BrE)
a type of gas burner used in laboratories, equipped with an air valve to control the mix of gas and air
laboratory gas burner with air valve

Examples

The chemistry teacher demonstrated the reaction using an etna to heat the solution.
Be sure to adjust the air valve on the etna for a clean blue flame.
Several etnas were placed on each bench in the laboratory for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bunsen burner - a common laboratory gas burner with an adjustable air supply

Etymology

Named after Mount Etna, possibly by analogy to its fiery nature.
