to exhort

1. VERB

/ ɪgˈzɔrt / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɔːt / (BrE)
to strongly encourage or urge someone to do something
strongly urge or encourage

Examples

The coach exhorted the players to give their best effort in the final match.
She exhorts her students to strive for academic excellence.
During the meeting, he exhorted everyone to support the new policy.

Synset Examples

I urged him to finish his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urge - to encourage or try to persuade someone earnestly

Etymology

From Latin exhortari (to encourage), from ex- (out) + hortari (to encourage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00767694-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɔrt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːt / (BrE)
to strongly encourage or urge someone to do something, especially by words or shouts
strongly urge or encourage

Examples

The coach exhorted his team to give their best effort throughout the game.
She exhorted the students to never give up on their dreams during her speech.
At the rally, the leader kept exhorting the crowd to remain peaceful and united.

Synset Examples

The crowd cheered the demonstrating strikers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - to give someone support, confidence, or hope
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Latin 'exhortari', from ex- (out) + hortari (to encourage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00860463-v
Synset POS: v
