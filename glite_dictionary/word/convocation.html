convocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of people assembled in response to a summons, especially for a formal or official meeting
formal gathering in response to a summons

Examples

The university held its annual convocation to welcome new students and faculty.
A convocation of bishops was called to discuss church reforms.
Delegates from all regions attended the convocation at the capital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: dispersal - the action or process of distributing things or people over a wide area

Etymology

From Latin convocatio (a calling together), from convocare (to call together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of calling people together for a formal meeting or assembly
formal gathering or assembly

Examples

The university held a convocation to welcome new students and faculty.
The bishop addressed the annual convocation of the clergy on important church matters.
A special convocation was called to discuss the urgent issue facing the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'convocatio', from 'convocare' (to call together), from 'con-' (together) + 'vocare' (to call).
