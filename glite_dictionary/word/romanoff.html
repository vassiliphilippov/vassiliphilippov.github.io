Romanoff

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊ.məˌnɔf / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.məˌnɒf / (BrE)
the Russian imperial dynasty that ruled from 1613 to 1917
the Russian royal family (1613–1917)

Examples

The last Tsar of the Romanoff dynasty was Nicholas II.
Many treasures once owned by the Romanoff family can be seen in museums today.
The fall of the Romanoff dynasty marked the end of imperial Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanov - alternative spelling of the Russian imperial dynasty's name

Etymology

From Russian Рома́нов (Romanov), the family name of the Tsars of Russia.

2. PROPN

/ ˈroʊ.məˌnɑf / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.məˌnɒf / (BrE)
a member of the imperial family that ruled Russia from 1613 to 1917.
member of Russia's former imperial family

Examples

After the revolution, the last surviving Romanoff escaped with the help of loyal supporters.
Many treasures belonging to the Romanoff family were lost during the Russian Revolution.
She claims to be a descendant of the Romanoff dynasty, though there is little evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanov - spelling variant of the same ruling Russian dynasty

Etymology

From Russian Рома́нов (Románov), the family name of the dynasty.
