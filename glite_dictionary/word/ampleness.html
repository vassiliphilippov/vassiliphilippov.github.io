ampleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.pəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈam.pl̩.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being ample; the condition of more than sufficient amount or extent
the quality of being ample

Examples

The ampleness of the storage space surprised the new tenants.
She admired the ampleness of the garden, perfect for hosting parties.
The ampleness of his explanation left no unanswered questions.

Synset Examples

the ampleness of her servings more than satisfied his hunger

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insufficiency - the state of not being enough
  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'amplus' (large, spacious) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmpəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈæmp(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being impressive in size, amount, or extent; spaciousness or largeness
impressive largeness or spaciousness

Examples

The ampleness of the banquet hall took everyone's breath away.
He appreciated the ampleness of her generosity in supporting the charity.
The ampleness of the library's collection impressed visiting scholars.

Synset Examples

he admired the ampleness of its proportions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

Formed from 'ample' (from Latin 'amplus', meaning large or spacious) with the noun suffix '-ness'.
