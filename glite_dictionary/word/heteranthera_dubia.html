Heteranthera dubia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəˈrænθərə ˈduːbiə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrænθərə ˈdjuːbiə / (BrE)
A North American aquatic plant with grass-like leaves and yellow star-shaped flowers, commonly found in slow-moving freshwater habitats.
aquatic plant with yellow star-shaped flowers

Examples

The pond featured several clusters of Heteranthera dubia along the shoreline.
Researchers have observed that Heteranthera dubia thrives in clean, slow-moving rivers.
Botanists identified the yellow blossoms as those of Heteranthera dubia.

Etymology

From Latin heteranthera (different anthers) and dubia (doubtful, uncertain); scientific name.
