Dipsacus sativus

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˈseɪkəs səˈtaɪvəs / (AmE), / dɪpˈsɑːkəs səˈtɪvəs / (BrE)
A species of teasel widespread in Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, with prickly flower heads once used for raising the nap on cloth.
species of teasel plant

Examples

Dipsacus sativus was commonly cultivated for use in wool processing during the 18th century.
The spiny flower heads of Dipsacus sativus are distinctive and easily recognized by botanists.
In some regions, Dipsacus sativus has become naturalized outside its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - spiny-leaved plant with prickly flower heads

Etymology

From Latin 'dipsacus' (teasel) and 'sativus' (cultivated).
