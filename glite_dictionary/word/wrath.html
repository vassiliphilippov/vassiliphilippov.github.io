wrath

1. NOUN

/ ræθ / (AmE), / rɒθ / (BrE)
Extreme or intense anger, especially of a vengeful or punitive nature.
intense or vengeful anger

Examples

The king's wrath was feared throughout the kingdom.
She trembled at the wrath in his eyes after hearing the news.
The ancient text speaks of the gods' wrath against humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure
  • Synonym: fury - wild or violent anger
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of anger or agitation

Etymology

From Old English *wræththu* (anger), related to Old High German *reid* (to rage).

2. NOUN

/ ræθ / (AmE), / rɒθ / (BrE)
Extreme anger or rage, often regarded as a deadly sin or a powerful emotion resulting from a real or perceived wrong.
intense anger; deadly sin

Examples

Her wrath was evident when she spoke about the injustice done to her family.
In ancient texts, the gods often unleash their wrath upon mortals who defy them.
He struggled to control his wrath after hearing the unfair accusation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Synonym: fury - wild or violent anger
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from anger or agitation

Etymology

From Old English wræththu (anger, wrath), related to Old Norse reiði (wrath).
