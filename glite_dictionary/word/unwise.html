unwise

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwaɪz / (AmE), / ʌnˈwaɪz / (BrE)
not sensible or not appropriate to a particular situation or purpose; showing poor judgment.
not sensible; showing poor judgment

Examples

It would be unwise to invest all your savings in a single company.
Leaving your car unlocked in this neighborhood is extremely unwise.
She realized it was unwise to speak without knowing all the facts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wise - having or showing good judgment; sensible
  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From un- (not) + wise (having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwaɪz / (AmE), / ʌnˈwaɪz / (BrE)
showing a lack of good judgment or sense; not sensible or prudent.
lacking good judgment; imprudent

Examples

It would be unwise to invest all your savings in a single company.
She made the unwise decision to ignore the weather warnings.
His unwise comments caused unnecessary confusion among the team.

Synset Examples

an unwise investor is soon impoverished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: prudent - showing care and thought for the future

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'wise' (showing good sense, from Old English 'wis').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02583095-s
Synset POS: s
