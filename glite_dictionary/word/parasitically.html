parasitically

1. ADV

/ ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that resembles or relates to the behavior of a parasite, often by living off another organism or relying on others without giving back.
in a parasitic manner

Examples

The fungus spreads parasitically on the roots of trees, feeding off their nutrients.
He lived parasitically off his relatives, never contributing to the household expenses.
Some plants obtain their food parasitically rather than through photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitic - having the characteristics of a parasite; living off another
  • Antonym: independently - in an independent manner, without relying on others

Etymology

From 'parasitic' + '-ally,' relating to parasites (from Greek 'parasitos', 'person eating at another's table').
