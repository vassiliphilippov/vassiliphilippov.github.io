arrowworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈærəʊˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a small, transparent marine worm of the phylum Chaetognatha, having horizontal lateral and caudal fins and a row of curved spines on each side of the mouth
transparent marine worm (Chaetognatha)

Examples

Scientists observed an arrowworm swimming rapidly near the coral reef.
The transparent body of the arrowworm makes it difficult to spot in open water.
Most arrowworms are voracious predators, feeding on small planktonic animals.

Etymology

Formed from 'arrow' and 'worm', referring to its slender, arrow-like shape.
