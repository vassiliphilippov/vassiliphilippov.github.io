Temnospondyli

1. NOUN

/ tɛm.nəˈspɑn.dɪ.laɪ / (AmE), / tɛm.nɒˈspɒn.dɪ.laɪ / (BrE)
an extinct group of large amphibians from the Carboniferous and Permian periods, characterized by vertebrae with partly separate elements
extinct group of ancient amphibians

Examples

The fossils of Temnospondyli have been found across several continents.
Many paleontologists study Temnospondyli to understand early amphibian evolution.
Extinct for millions of years, the Temnospondyli left behind a rich fossil record.

Etymology

From Greek temno- (to cut) + spondylos (vertebra).
