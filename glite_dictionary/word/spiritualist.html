spiritualist

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɪr.ɪ.tʃu.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈspɪr.ɪ.tjʊə.lɪst / (BrE)
relating to the belief or practice of spiritualism, especially the idea that the spirits of the dead can communicate with the living
relating to spiritualism

Examples

The spiritualist community held séances to try to contact the spirits of loved ones.
This literature is filled with spiritualist beliefs and references to communication with the afterlife.
She attended several spiritualist meetings out of curiosity about the supernatural.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritualistic - relating to or characteristic of spiritualism

Etymology

From 'spiritualism' + '-ist', modelled on 'spiritual'; dating from mid-19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02923189-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtʃʊəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who claims to act as an intermediary between the living and the spirits of the dead, often communicating with the dead through séances or other practices
person communicating with the dead

Examples

After her husband's death, she visited a spiritualist hoping to receive a message from him.
The spiritualist claimed she could sense the presence of lost loved ones in the room.
Many people attended the spiritualist's séance in search of comfort and answers.

Synset Examples

he consulted several mediums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medium - a person who claims to communicate with the spirits of the dead

Etymology

From 'spiritual' + '-ist', referring to belief in spirits and their communication.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10325946-n
Synset POS: n
