velvet

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛl.vɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛl.vɪt / (BrE)
Having a smooth and soft surface, like velvet.
smooth and soft like velvet

Examples

The baby's cheeks felt as soft as velvet against my hand.
The calm lake had a velvet surface under the moonlight.
Her velvet voice charmed everyone in the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky - having a smooth, fine texture like silk
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface

Etymology

From Old French 'veluet', diminutive of 'velu' (hairy), from Latin 'villosus' (hairy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛl.vət / (AmE), / ˈvɛl.vɪt / (BrE)
a soft, closely woven fabric with a thick, short pile on one side, giving it a smooth, luxurious feel
soft, thick-piled fabric

Examples

The curtains were made of rich, red velvet that shimmered in the sunlight.
She wore a beautiful velvet dress to the party, admiring its luxurious texture.
The jeweler displayed the necklace on a piece of black velvet for an elegant effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velour - a plush, knitted fabric similar to velvet
  • Degree Lower: satin - a smooth, glossy fabric
  • Degree Higher: brocade - a rich fabric with a raised pattern

Etymology

From Old French 'veluotte', from ‘velu’ (hairy), from Latin 'villosus' (hairy, shaggy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04532333-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɛlvət / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt / (BrE)
smooth and soft, especially in a way that is pleasant to sight, hearing, touch, or taste; resembling velvet in texture or quality
smooth and soft in texture or quality

Examples

She spoke in a velvet voice that soothed everyone in the room.
A velvet darkness settled over the quiet town as night fell.
The chef described the dessert's texture as velvet on the tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky - smooth, soft, and shiny; resembling silk
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough, jarring, or grating in texture or sound

Etymology

From Old French 'veluau' (velvet), from Vulgar Latin '*villosum' (hairy), from Latin 'villōsus'.

Synset POS: s
