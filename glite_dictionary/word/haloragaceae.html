Haloragaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæləˌræˈgeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌhæləˌræˈgeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous flowering plants in the order Myrtales, including water milfoils and their relatives
family of flowering plants in Myrtales

Examples

The Haloragaceae family includes both aquatic and terrestrial species found worldwide.
Botanists have debated the classification of Haloragaceae within the order Myrtales.
Some members of the Haloragaceae can be invasive in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Haloragis + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæloʊrəˈɡeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhalərəˈɡeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous plants in the order Myrtales, commonly known as the water milfoil family, which includes aquatic and marsh plants
a botanical family of aquatic and marsh plants

Examples

The Haloragaceae family contains several aquatic species adapted to freshwater habitats.
Researchers discovered a new plant species belonging to the Haloragaceae near the riverbank.
Members of the Haloragaceae often have finely divided leaves and small flowers.

Etymology

From the type genus Haloragis, from Greek hals (sea) and rhax (grape), combined with the botanical family suffix -aceae.
