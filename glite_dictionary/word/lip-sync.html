to lip-sync

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪpˌsɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.sɪŋk / (BrE)
To move the lips in synchronization with recorded speech or music, so that it appears one is speaking or singing.
move lips in time with recorded audio

Examples

Many artists lip-sync during live concerts to ensure a flawless performance.
She had to lip-sync her part because she lost her voice before the show.
The actor was expertly lip-syncing to the prerecorded dialogue in the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - to imitate speech or singing without sound for effect

Etymology

From 'lip' and 'sync', a shortening of 'synchronize'.
