to retouch

1. VERB

/ riˈtʌtʃ / (AmE), / riˈtʌtʃ / (BrE)
to apply touch-ups to (hair), especially by coloring the roots or refreshing previously applied hair color
apply touch-ups to hair, especially roots

Examples

My stylist will retouch my roots every six weeks to keep the color fresh.
She went to the salon to retouch her highlights before the wedding.
It's important to retouch your hair dye regularly to avoid noticeable regrowth.

Synset Examples

retouch the roots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to touch up - to make small improvements or repairs to something
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ riˈtʌtʃ / (AmE), / riˈtʌtʃ / (BrE)
to improve or alter a photograph, picture, or other image, especially by making slight changes to its appearance
make small changes to improve appearance

Examples

The artist decided to retouch the portrait to make the colors more vivid.
Many magazines retouch photographs to remove small imperfections from the models' faces.
He spent hours retouching the old family photographs on his computer.

Synset Examples

This photograph has been retouched!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material by correcting, revising, or adapting
  • Synonym: to enhance - to improve the quality, amount, or strength of something
  • Antonym: damage - to harm or spoil something

Etymology

From French retoucher, from re- (again) + toucher (to touch).
