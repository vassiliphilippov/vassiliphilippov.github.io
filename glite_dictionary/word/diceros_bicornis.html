Diceros bicornis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛɹəs baɪˈkɔːrnɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪərɒs bʌɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
the black rhinoceros, a large, endangered species of African rhinoceros distinguished by two horns on its snout
African black rhinoceros species

Examples

The Diceros bicornis population has declined dramatically due to poaching.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the Diceros bicornis.
The Diceros bicornis is also known as the black rhino.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black rhinoceros - large, endangered African rhinoceros with two horns

Etymology

From Greek 'dikeras' (two-horned) and Latin 'bicornis' (two-horned).
