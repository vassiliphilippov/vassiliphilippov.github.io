Hippobosca

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈbɑskə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈbɒskə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flies in the family Hippoboscidae, commonly known as louse flies or keds, which infest mammals and birds.
genus of parasitic flies (louse flies)

Examples

The genus Hippobosca includes several species of louse flies that parasitize horses and cattle.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Hippobosca to understand how it spreads among hosts.
Specimens of Hippobosca were collected from migratory birds in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hippoboscidae - the family of parasitic flies including louse flies and keds

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'boskein' (to feed), referencing the flies' infestation of horses.
