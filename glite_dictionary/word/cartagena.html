Cartagena

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑrtəˈheɪnə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtəˈheɪnə / (BrE)
a port city in northwestern Colombia on the Caribbean Sea, known for its colonial architecture and historic significance
major port city in Colombia

Examples

The historic center of Cartagena is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting visitors from around the world.
Many cruise ships stop at Cartagena because of its beautiful beaches and rich history.
She studied Spanish in Cartagena for three months while exploring Colombia.

Etymology

From Spanish Cartagena, named after Cartagena, Spain.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːrtəˈheɪnə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtəˈheɪnə / (BrE)
a port city in southeastern Spain on the Mediterranean Sea, known for its naval base and historical significance
port city in southeastern Spain

Examples

The cruise ship docked at Cartagena, allowing passengers to explore its ancient Roman ruins.
Cartagena is famous for its impressive natural harbor and rich maritime history.
While traveling through Spain, we spent three days in Cartagena enjoying the local cuisine and museums.

Etymology

From Latin 'Carthago Nova', meaning 'New Carthage', named by the Carthaginians.
