Solanopteris

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌlæn.ɑpˈtɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / səʊˌlæ.nɒpˈtɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American epiphytic ferns characterized by rhizomes with tubers and roots as well as scales.
genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

The rainforest biologist discovered several specimens of Solanopteris growing high in the canopy.
Members of the Solanopteris genus are known for their distinctive tuberous rhizomes.
Conservationists study Solanopteris to understand epiphytic fern diversity in South America.

2. NOUN

/ soʊˌlæn.əpˈtɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / səʊˌlan.ɒpˈtɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American epiphytic ferns characterized by rhizomes with tubers, roots, and scales.
genus of tropical American epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Solanopteris species thrive in the humid forests of Central America.
Botanists recently discovered a new Solanopteris with unusually large tubers.
Gardeners value Solanopteris for its unique epiphytic growth and attractive fronds.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Latin 'Solanum' (a plant genus) and Greek 'pteris' (fern).
