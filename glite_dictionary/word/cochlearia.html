Cochlearia

1. NOUN

/ koʊkˈlɪəriə / (AmE), / kɒkˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), commonly known as scurvy-grass, noted for their fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and use as a source of vitamin C.
genus of plants called scurvy-grass

Examples

Cochlearia thrives along coastal areas where the salty environment suits its growth.
The leaves of Cochlearia were once carried by sailors to prevent scurvy during long sea voyages.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Cochlearia in treating nutritional deficiencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurvy-grass - a plant of the genus Cochlearia, historically used to treat scurvy

Etymology

From Latin cochlearia (spoonwort), from cochleare (spoon), referring to the leaf shape.

2. NOUN

/ koʊkˈlɪəriə / (AmE), / kɒkˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the cabbage family (Brassicaceae), commonly known as scurvy-grass, found mainly in coastal regions of Europe and used historically to prevent scurvy.
genus of plants known as scurvy-grass

Examples

The leaves of Cochlearia were once used by sailors to prevent scurvy on long voyages.
Several species of Cochlearia thrive along the rocky shores of northern Europe.
Botanists recently discovered a new population of Cochlearia in coastal Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurvy-grass - common name for plants in the genus Cochlearia

Etymology

From Latin *cochlearia* (spoon-shaped), referring to the shape of the leaves.
