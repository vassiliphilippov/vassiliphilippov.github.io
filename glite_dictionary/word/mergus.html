Mergus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrɡəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish-eating ducks known as mergansers, characterized by slender, serrated bills.
genus of fish-eating ducks (mergansers)

Examples

The genus Mergus includes several species of fish-eating ducks found in the Northern Hemisphere.
Researchers have documented the migration patterns of Mergus across North America and Eurasia.
The distinctive serrated bills of Mergus species help them catch slippery fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merganser - a species of duck belonging to the genus Mergus

Etymology

From Latin *mergus* (diver), used in scientific taxonomy.
