Micropterus

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑːptərəs / (AmE), / mɪˈkrɒptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of American freshwater fishes commonly known as black basses, including the largemouth and smallmouth bass.
genus of American black basses

Examples

The largemouth bass is a species in the genus Micropterus and is popular among anglers.
Researchers studied several types of Micropterus to understand their habitat preferences.
The introduction of Micropterus species has impacted native fish populations in some lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black bass - any species of bass in the genus Micropterus

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'pteron' (wing), referencing the small fins.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑːptərəs / (AmE), / mɪˈkrɒptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of American freshwater fish known as black basses, including species such as the largemouth and smallmouth bass.
genus of American freshwater black basses

Examples

The Micropterus genus includes both largemouth and smallmouth bass.
Anglers in North America often seek out species of Micropterus for sport fishing.
Scientists study the distribution of Micropterus in various freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black bass - a common name for several freshwater fish in the genus Micropterus

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + pteron (wing, fin).
