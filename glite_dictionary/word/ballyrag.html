to ballyrag

1. VERB

/ ˈbæliˌræɡ / (AmE), / ˈbæliˌræɡ / (BrE)
to treat someone in a bossy, bullying, or intimidating manner; to harass persistently
bully or intimidate persistently
archaic

Examples

The older boys used to ballyrag the younger students after class.
He was known to ballyrag his staff whenever he was in a bad mood.
Tired of being ballyragged by her peers, she finally stood up for herself.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to habitually seek to harm or intimidate
  • Synonym: to harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation

Etymology

Probably from Irish *balbh* (stammering, muttering) + *rag* (to scold).
