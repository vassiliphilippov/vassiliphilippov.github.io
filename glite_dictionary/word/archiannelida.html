Archiannelida

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˌænəˈlɪdə / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˌænɪˈliːdə / (BrE)
A former class of small, simple marine worms within the phylum Annelida, now usually regarded as a group of primitive polychaetes.
primitive marine worms (Annelida class)
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species of Archiannelida along the Mediterranean coast.
The simple body plan of Archiannelida makes them important for evolutionary studies.
Marine biologists debated the classification of Archiannelida within the annelids.

Etymology

From Greek 'archi-' (chief, primitive) + 'Annelida' (from Latin 'anellus', little ring).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.ki.ænˈɛl.ɪ.də / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ki.ænˈɛl.ɪ.də / (BrE)
a former class of small, primitive, marine worms within the phylum Annelida, now generally included in the class Polychaeta.
group of primitive marine annelid worms

Examples

Recent studies have suggested that Archiannelida should be reclassified under Polychaeta.
Most Archiannelida species inhabit shallow marine environments.
The anatomy of Archiannelida provides insights into early annelid evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polychaeta - class of annelid worms with bristles, current classification for many former Archiannelida species

Etymology

From Greek 'archi-' (beginning) + 'Annelida' (the segmented worms).
