packing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋ / (BrE)
material used to wrap, protect, or cushion objects, especially during transportation or storage.
material used to wrap or protect objects

Examples

Be sure to use plenty of packing to protect the fragile items in the box.
All the packing from our online orders is piling up at home.
The vase arrived safely because there was enough packing inside the crate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrapping - material used to cover or enclose something
  • Antonym: unpacking - the process of removing packing material from something

Etymology

From the verb 'pack' + -ing, dating from the late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of carrying items, especially in a pack on one's back.
carrying items in a backpack

Examples

Packing all the necessary gear for the hike took almost an hour.
The packing of heavy equipment is required for expeditions in remote areas.
She enjoys packing her backpack and exploring the mountains every weekend.

Synset Examples

the backpacking of oxygen is essential for astronauts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backpacking - traveling or hiking with a backpack

Etymology

from the verb 'pack', from Middle English pak, related to carrying or arranging items for transport

3. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of enclosing something in a package, box, or other container to protect or transport it
action of enclosing in a package or box

Examples

The packing of the fragile items took several hours to ensure their safety.
He finished the packing and labeled each box before moving day.
Proper packing of products can prevent damage during shipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packaging - the materials used to wrap or protect goods

Etymology

From the verb 'pack' + -ing suffix, meaning the act or process.
