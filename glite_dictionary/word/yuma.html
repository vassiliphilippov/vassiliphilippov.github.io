Yuma

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuːmə / (AmE), / ˈjuːmə / (BrE)
a town in southwestern Arizona, located on the Colorado River near the California border
a town in Arizona, United States

Examples

Many tourists visit Yuma each winter to enjoy its sunny weather.
The Colorado River flows near the city of Yuma.
The train stopped for a brief layover in Yuma before continuing west.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːmə / (AmE), / ˈjuːmə / (BrE)
The Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Yuma people of the southwestern United States.
the Yuman language of the Yuma people

Examples

Fewer and fewer people are fluent in Yuma today.
She is working to document the grammar of the Yuma language.
The dictionary includes many words from Yuma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of languages of southwestern US and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Yuma people, possibly from Quechan 'Yuumaa', meaning 'people.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈjuːmə / (AmE), / ˈjuːmə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people living in southwestern Arizona and adjacent regions of Mexico and California.
member of a Native American people

Examples

Many Yuma still live in the area along the lower Colorado River.
The Yuma were known for their irrigation and farming skills.
She is studying the traditions of the Yuma people for her research project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechan - a Native American people also referred to as Yuma
