matricide

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmætrɪsaɪd / (BrE)
the act of killing one's mother
killing of one's mother

Examples

The court case involved a shocking matricide that gripped the nation for weeks.
She studied famous cases of matricide as part of her research on family violence.
Historians often mention matricide when discussing complex royal successions in ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parricide - the killing of a parent or close relative
  • Synonym: patricide - the act of killing one's father
  • Synonym: filicide - the act of killing one's child

Etymology

From Latin 'matricida', from 'mater' (mother) + '-cida' (killer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmætrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmætrɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
a person who kills their own mother
person who kills their mother

Examples

The shocking evidence revealed that the accused was a matricide who had carefully planned the crime.
Throughout history, the act of matricide has been condemned as one of the gravest offenses.
Detectives suspected a case of matricide based on the forensic evidence found at the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parricide - a person who kills a parent or close relative
  • Synonym: patricide - a person who kills their father

Etymology

From Latin 'matricidium' (killing of a mother), from 'mater' (mother) + '-cidium' (killing).
