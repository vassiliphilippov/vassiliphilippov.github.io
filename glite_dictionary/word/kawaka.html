kawaka

1. NOUN

/ kəˈwɑːkə / (AmE), / kəˈwɑːkə / (BrE)
a New Zealand timber tree (Libocedrus plumosa) resembling the cypress, valued for its wood
New Zealand timber tree similar to cypress

Examples

The kawaka is prized in New Zealand for its durable timber and attractive foliage.
You can find the kawaka growing in the damp, sheltered forests of the North Island.
Carpenters often use kawaka wood for making traditional Māori carvings.

Etymology

From Māori 'kawaka,' the native name for the tree.
