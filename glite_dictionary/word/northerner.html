Northerner

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðɚnɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðənə / (BrE)
a person who comes from or lives in the northern part of a country, especially the north of England or the northern United States
person from the northern region

Examples

She is proud to call herself a Northerner and often talks about her hometown.
As a Northerner, he was not used to the hot, humid climate of the South.
Many Northerners have strong regional accents that are easy to recognize.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southerner - a person from the southern part of a country

Etymology

From 'northern' + '-er', meaning 'one from the North'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrðərnər / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðənə / (BrE)
an American who lives in or comes from the northern United States, especially during the American Civil War
person from the northern United States

Examples

During the Civil War, a Northerner might have held very different views from a Southerner.
As a proud Northerner, he often spoke about his family's history in New York.
Many Northerners moved west after the end of the war.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Southerner - an American living in the South, especially during the Civil War

Etymology

From 'north' + agent suffix '-er', indicating a person from the North.
