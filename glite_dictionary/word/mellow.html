mellow

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛləʊ / (BrE)
(of food or drink) having a full, smooth, and pleasing taste, especially as a result of proper aging.
rich and smooth in flavor from aging

Examples

The mellow flavor of the cheese comes from being aged for several months.
After years in the cellar, the wine developed a mellow richness that pleased everyone at dinner.
This type of coffee is known for its mellow aroma and smooth finish.

Synset Examples

a mellow port
mellowed fruit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harsh - having a sharp or unpleasant taste, especially of food or drink

Etymology

Probably from Old English *melu* (soft, tender).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛləʊ / (BrE)
(of fruit, wine, etc.) to become softer, sweeter, or richer; to make something milder or more pleasant in flavor, character, or mood.
become or make softer or more pleasant

Examples

The apples need to mellow for another week before they are sweet enough to eat.
Time had mellowed his once sharp temper.
If you let the cheese mellow, its flavor becomes much richer.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ripen - to become fully developed or mature, especially in flavor or color

Etymology
