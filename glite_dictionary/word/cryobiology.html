cryobiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪ.oʊ.baɪˈɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌkraɪ.əʊ.baɪˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the branch of biology that studies the effects of very low temperatures on living tissues, organs, or organisms
study of low temperatures in biology

Examples

Cryobiology has led to breakthroughs in organ preservation for transplantation.
She decided to specialize in cryobiology to study how animals survive in extreme cold.
Research in cryobiology has enabled the long-term storage of blood, eggs, and embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryonics - the practice of preserving people or animals at low temperatures after death with the hope of revival

Etymology

From Greek kryos (frost) + biology, meaning the study of life at low temperatures.
