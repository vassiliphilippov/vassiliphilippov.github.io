First Epistle to the Thessalonians

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɝst ɪˈpɪsəl tu ði ˌθɛsəˈloʊniənz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ɪˈpɪsəl tuː ði ˌθɛsəˈləʊniənz / (BrE)
the first of two letters in the New Testament, attributed to Saint Paul, addressed to the Christian community in Thessalonica
New Testament letter by Saint Paul

Examples

Many scholars consider the First Epistle to the Thessalonians to be the earliest surviving Christian text.
The First Epistle to the Thessalonians addresses themes of faith, love, and hope.
During Bible study, they discussed passages from the First Epistle to the Thessalonians.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Second Epistle to the Thessalonians - the second letter from Paul to the Thessalonians in the New Testament

Etymology

Epistle from Latin 'epistola', from Greek 'epistolē' (letter/message); Thessalonians refers to the people of Thessalonica in ancient Macedonia.
