Varuna

1. PROPN

/ vəˈruːnə / (AmE), / vəˈruːnə / (BrE)
In ancient Indian religion, the god of the night sky, moral order, and cosmic law, who watches over human conduct and judges good and evil.
Hindu god of the night sky and moral order

Examples

In ancient hymns, Varuna is invoked as the protector of truth and justice.
During the festival, devotees offer prayers to Varuna to seek forgiveness for their wrongdoings.
Mythology describes Varuna as the deity who governs the night sky and cosmic waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mitra - Another Vedic deity, often paired with Varuna as upholders of cosmic order

Etymology

From Sanskrit वरुण (Varuṇa), a major Vedic deity.
