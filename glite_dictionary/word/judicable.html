judicable

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːdɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdɪkəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be judged or decided, especially in a legal context.
capable of being judged or decided

Examples

Only judicable disputes can be taken before the court.
The panel determined whether the complaint was judicable under the organization's rules.
Legal scholars debated if such matters were truly judicable or belonged in the political sphere.

Synset Examples

judicable issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justiciable - subject to trial in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin 'judicare' (to judge) + '-able' (able to be).
