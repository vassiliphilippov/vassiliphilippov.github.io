tether

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛðə / (BrE)
to tie (an animal or object) with a rope, chain, or similar restraint to restrict its movement to a certain area
tie with a rope or chain to restrict movement

Examples

She carefully tethered the goat to a post in the yard.
During the storm, they tethered the boat to keep it from drifting away.
Please tether your horse outside the barn before coming in.

Synset Examples

tether horses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untether - to release from a tether; to set free from binding

Etymology

From Old Norse *tjōðr* (tether, rope), related to Middle English *tether*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01292612-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛðər / (AmE), / ˈtɛðə / (BrE)
a rope, chain, or similar restraint used to tie an animal so that it can only move within a certain area
restraint for tying an animal

Examples

The horse was secured to a post with a tether.
After grazing, the goat chewed through its tether and wandered off.
They found a broken tether in the field where the cow had been tied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leash - a cord or strap to control an animal, especially a dog
  • Antonym: untether - to release from a tether

Etymology

From Old Norse *tjǫðr* (tether, bind), related to Old English *tēodan*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03658258-n
Synset POS: n
