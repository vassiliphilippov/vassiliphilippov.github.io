congenital

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɛnətl̩ / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or physical abnormality) present from birth, though not necessarily inherited.
existing at birth (not always inherited)
medical

Examples

The baby was diagnosed with a congenital heart defect shortly after birth.
Some congenital conditions can be detected with prenatal screening.
Her congenital blindness did not stop her from leading a full life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hereditary - (of a condition) passed from parent to offspring by genetic inheritance

Etymology

From Latin 'congenitus' (born together, innate), from 'con-' (together) + 'genitus' (born, from 'gignere' to beget).
