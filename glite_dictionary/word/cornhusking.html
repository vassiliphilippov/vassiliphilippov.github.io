cornhusking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌhʌskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌhʌskɪŋ / (BrE)
a social gathering at which people come together to remove the husks from ears of corn, often accompanied by festivity and communal activities
social gathering for removing corn husks

Examples

Every autumn, the town holds a cornhusking to bring the community together.
During the cornhusking, neighbors exchanged stories and laughter as they worked.
The tradition of cornhusking dates back to early rural American life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husking bee - a social event where people gather to husk corn

Etymology

From 'corn' + 'husking', referring to the removal of husks from corn.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌhʌskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnhʌskɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing the husks from ears of corn
removal of husks from corn

Examples

The annual cornhusking festival attracts visitors from all over the region.
He spent the afternoon cornhusking with his family on the farm.
Traditional cornhusking competitions were a popular rural pastime in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husking - the act of removing the husk from corn or other plants

Etymology

From 'corn' + 'husking', meaning to remove the husk of corn.
