Andrew Carnegie

1. PROPN

/ ˈændru ˈkɑrnəɡi / (AmE), / ˈændru ˈkɑːnəɡi / (BrE)
(1835–1919) A Scottish-American industrialist and philanthropist, known for leading the expansion of the American steel industry and for his extensive charitable work, particularly in supporting libraries, education, and research.
American industrialist and philanthropist (1835–1919)

Examples

Andrew Carnegie donated millions to fund the construction of public libraries across the United States.
The legacy of Andrew Carnegie continues through the many foundations he established to support education and research.
As a leading figure of the Gilded Age, Andrew Carnegie was both admired for his philanthropy and criticized for his business practices.
