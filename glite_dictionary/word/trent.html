Trent

1. PROPN

/ trɛnt / (AmE), / trɛnt / (BrE)
a river in central England that flows northeast to join the Ouse and form the Humber estuary
river in central England

Examples

The Trent often floods the low-lying fields during heavy rainfall in the region.
Many towns along the Trent have a long tradition of river-based trade.
The Ouse and the Trent merge to form the Humber estuary.

city in northern Italy; site of Council of Trent

Examples

The historic city of Trent attracts visitors interested in Renaissance architecture and church history.
Many reforms of the Catholic Church were enacted at the Council of Trent.
