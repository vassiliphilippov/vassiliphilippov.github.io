voluminosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌvoʊ.ljə.məˈnɑː.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˌvɒl.jʊ.mɪˈnɒs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very large in volume or bulk
greatness of volume or bulk

Examples

The voluminosity of the sculpture made it the centerpiece of the exhibition.
Scientists measure the voluminosity of gases to compare their expansion under different temperatures.
The voluminosity of his written work surprised even his closest colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - the amount of space that a substance or object occupies

Etymology

Formed from 'voluminous' + '-ity', patterned after Latin roots.
