filled

1. ADJ

/ fɪld / (AmE), / fɪld / (BrE)
(of time) completely occupied or taken up; full of activity or events.
completely occupied (of time)

Examples

Her days were filled with meetings and responsibilities.
They always ensured their weekends were filled with fun activities.
The manager likes to keep our schedules filled so we stay productive.

Synset Examples

well-filled hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busy - actively and fully occupied
  • Antonym: empty - not occupied or having nothing scheduled (of time)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01627925-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ fɪld / (AmE), / fɪld / (BrE)
(of orders, requests, positions, etc.) having been supplied, completed, or satisfied as required.
supplied, completed, or satisfied

Examples

The store confirmed that my order was filled and ready for pickup.
By the end of the day, all backlogged requests had been filled.
Due to high demand, only some prescriptions were filled immediately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfilled - not supplied, completed, or satisfied
  • Synonym: complete - fully finished or supplied
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03161743-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ fɪld / (AmE), / fɪld / (BrE)
Containing as much of something as possible; full, especially to the point of being unable to hold any more, often followed by 'with'.
full or occupied with something

Examples

The basket was filled with ripe strawberries, ready to be eaten.
Her notebook was filled with sketches and ideas for her next project.
After the storm, the street was filled with debris and fallen branches.

Synset Examples

theirs was a house filled with laughter
a large hall filled with rows of desks
fog-filled air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full - Containing as much or as many as possible; having no empty space
