riyal-omani

1. NOUN

/ riːˈɑːl oʊˈmɑːni / (AmE), / riːˈɑːl əʊˈmɑːni / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Oman, equal to 1,000 baiza.
currency of Oman

Examples

Many tourists exchange their dollars for riyal-omani upon arriving in Oman.
The price was listed as 5 riyal-omani per ticket.
He saved several hundred riyal-omani during his trip to Muscat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riyal - the basic monetary unit of several Middle Eastern countries

Etymology

From Arabic riyāl (ريال), from Spanish real (royal coin) + Omani (relating to Oman).
