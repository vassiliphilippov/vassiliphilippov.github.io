downrightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌraɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being open, direct, and straightforward in speech or behavior
quality of being direct or straightforward

Examples

Her downrightness sometimes surprises people who are used to more subtle communication.
I admire his downrightness, even if it occasionally comes across as harsh.
In business, downrightness can help to avoid misunderstandings and build trust.

Synset Examples

what some people take for rudeness is really straightforwardness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest
  • Synonym: frankness - the quality of being frank; openness
  • Antonym: evasiveness - the quality of being elusive or indirect

Etymology

Formed from 'downright' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
