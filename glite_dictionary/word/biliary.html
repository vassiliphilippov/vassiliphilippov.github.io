biliary

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪliˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɪlɪəri / (BrE)
relating to bile, the bile ducts, or the gallbladder, especially in medical contexts.
of or relating to bile or bile ducts
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a biliary obstruction that required immediate treatment.
During the surgery, the doctors examined the biliary tract for any signs of infection.
Gallstones can cause blockages in the biliary system, leading to pain and jaundice.

Etymology

From Latin *bilis* (bile) + English adjectival suffix -ary.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪliːˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.i.ə.ri / (BrE)
relating to bile or the organs and ducts by which bile is secreted and transported in the body.
relating to bile or its transport
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with a biliary obstruction that required immediate treatment.
The biliary tract includes the gallbladder and bile ducts.
Doctors performed surgery to remove a biliary stone blocking the patient's duct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bile - a bitter greenish-brown fluid secreted by the liver

Etymology

From Latin 'bilis' (bile) + -ary (relating to).
