Wagram

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɑːɡrəm / (AmE), / ˈvɑːɡram / (BrE)
a battle during the Napoleonic Wars in 1809, near Vienna, where Napoleon defeated the Austrians
Napoleonic battle, 1809

Examples

The Battle of Wagram marked a turning point in the Napoleonic Wars.
Historians often examine the strategies used at Wagram to understand Napoleon's military genius.
After his victory at Wagram, Napoleon imposed harsh terms on Austria.

Etymology

From the name of the village Wagram, near Vienna, Austria, where the battle took place.
