Hasidim

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈsiːdɪm / (AmE), / hɑːˈsiːdɪm / (BrE)
Members of a sect of Orthodox Jews originating in eastern Europe in the 18th century, known for strict observance of Jewish law and distinctive religious practices.
sect of Orthodox Jews from eastern Europe

Examples

Many Hasidim live in Brooklyn and maintain their own communities and traditions.
The Hasidim are known for their joyous religious celebrations and spiritual teachings.
Some Hasidim follow their rebbe's guidance in all aspects of daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidic - relating to or characteristic of the Hasidim or their movement

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥăsīdīm (the pious ones), plural of ḥāsīd (pious, devout).
