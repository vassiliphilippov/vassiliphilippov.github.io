excitative

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtəˌtɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtətɪv / (BrE)
(of a drug or agent) able to produce excitement or stimulation, especially in a biological or physiological context.
producing excitement or stimulation
medical

Examples

Some excitative agents can increase heart rate and alertness in patients.
The doctor warned that the medication has excitative effects on the nervous system.
Caffeine is an excitative substance commonly found in coffee and tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
  • Antonym: depressant - a drug that reduces functional or nervous activity

Etymology

From Latin excitativus (exciting), from excitare (to excite).
