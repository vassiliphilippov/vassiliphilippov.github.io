Sarah

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛrə / (AmE), / ˈseərə / (BrE)
(In the Old Testament) the wife of Abraham and mother of Isaac, regarded as a matriarch in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.
wife of Abraham in the Old Testament

Examples

Many religious texts tell the story of Sarah and her faith in difficult times.
In the Old Testament, Sarah gave birth to Isaac at an advanced age.
The names Abraham and Sarah are often mentioned together in discussions of biblical history.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Śārāh' meaning 'princess'.
