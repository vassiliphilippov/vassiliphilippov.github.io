iota

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊtə / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊtə / (BrE)
an extremely small amount; a tiny or scarcely detectable quantity
a very small amount

Examples

There isn't an iota of truth in those rumors.
She didn't seem to care one iota about the consequences.
His story didn't change an iota after hours of questioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small amount
  • Synonym: trace - a slight amount or indication
  • Synonym: speck - a tiny spot or particle

Etymology

From Greek iōta, the name of the ninth letter of the Greek alphabet, which was the smallest letter.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊtə / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊtə / (BrE)
The ninth letter of the Greek alphabet (Ι, ι), corresponding to the letter 'i' in English.
ninth letter of the Greek alphabet

Examples

The word begins with an iota, which is similar to the English 'i'.
In classical Greek, the iota represents a short 'i' sound.
Each Greek vowel, including the iota, has a unique sound and symbol.

Etymology

From Greek ἰῶτα (iōta), the name of the letter iota.
