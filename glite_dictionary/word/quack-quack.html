quack-quack

1. NOUN

/ kwæk kwæk / (AmE), / kwæk kwæk / (BrE)
a word used by or to children to refer to a duck, imitating the sound it makes
child's word for a duck

Examples

The baby squealed with delight when she saw the quack-quack at the pond.
He pointed to the picture book and said, "Look, a quack-quack!"
We fed bread to the quack-quacks at the park last Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet
