tarpan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːr.pæn / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.pæn / (BrE)
A small, grayish wild horse of eastern Europe and western Asia, extinct since the early 20th century.
extinct European wild horse

Examples

The tarpan once roamed freely across the steppes of Eurasia.
Several conservation projects attempt to recreate the appearance of the extinct tarpan.
Historians believe the tarpan influenced the genetics of some modern horse breeds.

Etymology

From Russian 'tarpan', from Kazakh or Kyrgyz 'tarpan' meaning wild horse.
