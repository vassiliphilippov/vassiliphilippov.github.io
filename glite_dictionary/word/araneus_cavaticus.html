Araneus cavaticus

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪniəs kæˈvætɪkəs / (AmE), / əˈreɪniəs kæˈvætɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of orange and tan spider with darkly banded legs, known for spinning an orb web daily; commonly called the barn spider.
species of barn spider

Examples

The Araneus cavaticus is often found spinning its web in barns during late summer.
Many children recognize the Araneus cavaticus as Charlotte from the famous novel 'Charlotte's Web'.
Farmers appreciate the presence of Araneus cavaticus because it helps control flying insect populations.

Synset Examples

the barn spider was made famous in E. B. White's book ‘Charlotte's Web’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn spider - common name for Araneus cavaticus

Etymology

From Latin: Araneus meaning 'spider' and cavaticus meaning 'of the cave' or 'dwelling in a cave'.
