choreographer

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈræɡrəfər / (AmE), / kɒˈrɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who invents and arranges the movements and steps in dance performances
someone who creates dances

Examples

The choreographer spent weeks perfecting the ballet's final scene.
She worked closely with the choreographer to ensure every movement conveyed emotion.
Many Broadway shows rely on a skilled choreographer to design their dance sequences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dancer - someone who performs dances
  • Synonym: choreography - the art or practice of designing dance sequences

Etymology

From French 'choréographe', from Greek 'khoros' (dance) + 'graphos' (writer).
