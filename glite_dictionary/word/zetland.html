Zetland

1. PROPN

/ ˈzɛtlənd / (AmE), / ˈzɛtlənd / (BrE)
a former name for the Shetland Islands, an archipelago of about 100 islands in the North Atlantic off the north coast of Scotland
former name for the Shetland Islands

Examples

The map from 1750 labels the islands as Zetland, not Shetland.
Historical documents often refer to the Shetland Islands as Zetland.
He traced his ancestry back to settlers in Zetland in the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shetland Islands - an archipelago in the North Atlantic off the north coast of Scotland; modern name for Zetland

Etymology

From Old Norse *Hjaltland*, anglicized as Zetland in historical usage.
