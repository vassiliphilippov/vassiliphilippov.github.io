dysaphia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈeɪfiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈeɪfiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the sense of touch is disturbed or impaired.
impaired sense of touch (medical)
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed her with dysaphia after she reported numbness and tingling in her fingers.
Patients suffering from dysaphia may have difficulty perceiving even light touch on the skin.
Traumatic brain injury can occasionally result in dysaphia as part of the neurological symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anaphia - absence of the sense of touch
  • Degree Higher: hyperesthesia - increased sensitivity to touch or other senses

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + haphē (touch).
