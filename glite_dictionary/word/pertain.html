to pertain

1. VERB

/ pərˈteɪn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪn / (BrE)
to be a part, attribute, or relevant aspect of something; to relate to or concern
relate to; concern; be a part of

Examples

The documents pertain to last year's financial records.
Much of the legislation pertains only to businesses in the city.
His research pertains to climate change and its effects on agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relate - to have a connection to something
  • Synonym: to belong - to be rightly placed in a specified position or environment

Etymology

From Latin pertinere, from per- ('through') + tenere ('to hold').

2. VERB

/ pərˈteɪn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪn / (BrE)
to be relevant or applicable to something; to relate directly to a particular subject or situation
to be relevant or related

Examples

The new regulations pertain only to commercial properties, not residential homes.
Her comments did not pertain to the issue under discussion.
The documents pertaining to the case were reviewed by the lawyer.

Synset Examples

There were lots of questions referring to her talk
My remark pertained to your earlier comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relate - to have a connection to something
  • Synonym: to refer - to mention or allude to something; to direct attention to
  • Antonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something

Etymology

From Latin 'pertinere' (to belong to, to concern).

Synset POS: v
