Thamnophis

1. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɒfɪs / (AmE), / θæmˈnɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of non-venomous colubrid snakes commonly known as garter snakes, found throughout North and Central America.
genus of garter snakes

Examples

The genus Thamnophis includes many species of commonly seen garter snakes.
Biologists are studying the behavior of Thamnophis in wetland habitats.
Many people keep Thamnophis as pets due to their mild temperament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garter snake - a common name for snakes of the genus Thamnophis

Etymology

From Greek thamnos (bush) and ophis (snake).

2. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɑːfɪs / (AmE), / θamˈnɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of harmless North American snakes commonly known as garter snakes.
genus of North American garter snakes

Examples

Many species within the genus Thamnophis are found throughout North America.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Thamnophis in various wetland areas.
The Thamnophis group includes some of the most widespread snakes in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garter snake - a common name for snakes of the genus Thamnophis

Etymology

From Greek 'thamnos' (bush) and 'ophis' (snake).
