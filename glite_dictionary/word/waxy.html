waxy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwæksi / (AmE), / ˈwæksi / (BrE)
having the appearance, texture, or quality of wax; made of or covered with wax.
having the quality of wax

Examples

After polishing, the table had a waxy shine that made it look almost new.
Her hands felt waxy after handling the candles all afternoon.
Be careful—the hallway floor is still waxy and could be slippery.

Synset Examples

waxen candles
careful, the floor is waxy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxen - made of or covered with wax; wax-like in appearance or texture

Etymology

From 'wax' + '-y', meaning 'resembling or pertaining to wax'.

Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈwæksi / (AmE), / ˈwæksi / (BrE)
having a pale appearance resembling that of wax, especially in reference to skin or complexion
having pale, wax-like appearance

Examples

She grew waxy as the news sank in, her face drained of color.
After the long illness, his skin appeared almost waxy under the hospital’s harsh lights.
The child’s waxy complexion alarmed the doctor, who ordered immediate tests.

Synset Examples

the poor face with the same awful waxen pallor
the soldier turned his waxlike features toward him
a thin face with a waxy paleness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - having little color or intensity, especially of the face or skin
  • Synonym: livid - discolored from a bruise; pale or ashen

Etymology

From 'wax' (the substance), with suffix '-y' forming an adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00407572-s
Synset POS: s
