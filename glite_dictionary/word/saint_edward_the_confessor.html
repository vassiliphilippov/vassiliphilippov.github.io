Saint Edward the Confessor

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈɛdwərd ðə kənˈfɛsər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈɛdwəd ðə kənˈfɛsə / (BrE)
(c. 1003–1066) King of England from 1042 to 1066, known for his piety; founder of Westminster Abbey and later canonized as a saint.
English king and saint (c. 1003–1066)

Examples

Pilgrims once visited the tomb of Saint Edward the Confessor in Westminster Abbey.
Many historians regard Saint Edward the Confessor as one of England's last Anglo-Saxon kings.
The feast day of Saint Edward the Confessor is celebrated on October 13th.
