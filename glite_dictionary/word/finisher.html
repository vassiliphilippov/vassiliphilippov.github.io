finisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃə / (BrE)
a worker who performs the final step in a manufacturing or production process, ensuring completion or quality.
worker completing last step in a process

Examples

The finisher inspected each table before it left the factory.
As a skilled finisher, she ensured every garment met strict quality standards.
Each car went through several stages before reaching the finisher for a final polish.

Etymology

From English 'finish' + '-er', denoting a person who finishes.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃə / (BrE)
an animal that wins a race or contest of speed
animal winning a race

Examples

The thoroughbred was the clear finisher in yesterday's derby.
As a strong finisher, the greyhound set a new track record.
Despite a slow start, the horse proved to be an impressive finisher.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃə / (BrE)
a competitor, especially in a race, who successfully completes the event
one who completes a race or event

Examples

After the grueling marathon, only half the runners were classified as finishers.
She was proud to be a finisher in her first triathlon, despite her slow time.
In every Formula 1 Grand Prix, being a finisher is considered an achievement due to the challenging conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winner - a competitor who takes first place in a race or competition
  • Antonym: non-finisher - a competitor who does not complete a race or event

Etymology

From finish +‎ -er, denoting a person who finishes.
