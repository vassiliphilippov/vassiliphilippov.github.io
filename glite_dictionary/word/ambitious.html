ambitious

1. ADJ

/ æmˈbɪʃəs / (AmE), / æmˈbɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
having a strong desire to succeed or to achieve something, typically requiring determination and hard work
having a strong desire to achieve success

Examples

The company is looking for ambitious individuals who are eager to advance their careers.
Her ambitious plans include starting her own business before she turns thirty.
Although he was young, his ambitious goals impressed everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspiring - having ambitions to achieve something
  • Antonym: unambitious - lacking ambition or a strong desire to achieve

Etymology

From Latin ambitiosus (eager for favor), from ambitio (a going around, canvassing for favor).

2. ADJ

/ æmˈbɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / æmˈbɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
(of a plan, idea, or task) requiring a great deal of skill, effort, or resources to achieve.
requiring great skill or effort

Examples

Completing the ambitious project within a month required the whole team's dedication.
The company announced an ambitious expansion plan covering three new countries.
He took on an ambitious schedule that challenged everyone involved.

Synset Examples

ambitious schedule
performed the most challenging task without a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demanding - requiring much effort or attention
  • Antonym: unambitious - not requiring much effort or skill; modest in scope

Etymology

From Latin ambitiosus (eager for honor or office), from ambitio (ambition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00749254-s
Synset POS: s
