scam

1. NOUN

/ skæm / (AmE), / skæm / (BrE)
a dishonest or illegal plan, especially one for making money by deceiving people
fraudulent scheme; swindle

Examples

The online store turned out to be a scam that never delivered any products.
He lost all his savings in a cryptocurrency scam.
The police issued a warning about a new phone scam targeting elderly residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • Synonym: swindle - a fraudulent scheme or action for personal gain
  • Antonym: legitimate business - a lawful and honest commercial activity

Etymology

Probably a shortening and alteration of 'scamp.' Slang, 1960s, U.S. origin.

2. VERB

/ skæm / (AmE), / skæm / (BrE)
to cheat someone out of money or possessions by using deception
to cheat by deception
informal

Examples

They tried to scam tourists by selling fake tickets.
She realized too late that her friend had scammed her out of thousands of dollars.
The company was accused of scamming its customers with false promises.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to obtain money or property by deceit or fraud
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage

Etymology

Probably of unknown origin, popularized in American slang from mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
