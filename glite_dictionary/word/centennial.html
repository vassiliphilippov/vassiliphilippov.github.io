centennial

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɛniəl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɛniəl / (BrE)
the 100th anniversary of a significant event, or the celebration of it
100th anniversary or its celebration

Examples

The city held a grand parade to mark the centennial of its founding.
This park was created in honor of the country's centennial in 1876.
Many alumni returned to their university for the centennial celebration.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bicentennial - the 200th anniversary of an event or its celebration
  • Degree Higher: sesquicentennial - the 150th anniversary of an event or its celebration

Etymology

From Latin 'centum' (hundred) + 'annus' (year).

2. ADJ

/ sɛnˈtɛniəl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɛn.i.əl / (BrE)
relating to a period of 100 years or to a hundredth anniversary
relating to 100 years or a hundredth anniversary

Examples

The city held a centennial parade to mark its hundredth year of incorporation.
The new museum exhibit was organized as part of the university's centennial celebrations.
A centennial time capsule was buried to be opened in another hundred years.

Synset Examples

centennial celebration

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bicentennial - relating to a period of 200 years or a two-hundredth anniversary
  • Degree Lower: annual - relating to a period of one year

Etymology

From Latin 'centum' (hundred) + English '-ennial' (relating to years, as in 'biennial').
