executability

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɛkjʊtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɛkjʊtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the capability of being carried out or put into effect
capability of being executed

Examples

Software developers must always consider the executability of their code.
Legal teams debated the executability of the proposed settlement terms.
The project's executability depends on having enough resources and skilled personnel.

Synset Examples

this recommendation has executability problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to carry out or put into effect
  • Antonym: infeasibility - the quality of being impossible to do or carry out

Etymology

From execute + -ability, modeled after Latin executio (carrying out) and English -ability (capacity or suitability).
