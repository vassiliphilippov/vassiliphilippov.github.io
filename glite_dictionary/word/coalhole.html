coalhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a compartment, bin, or small cellar in a building used for storing coal, often with an external cover at street level
container or cellar for coal storage

Examples

The old house still had a coalhole next to the kitchen for storing fuel.
He lifted the cover of the coalhole and shoveled in a fresh delivery of coal.
During winter, the family relied on the coalhole to keep their stove supplied.

Etymology

Formed from 'coal' (fuel) + 'hole' (an opening or compartment).
