thick-skinned

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɪkˈskɪnd / (AmE), / ˌθɪkˈskɪnd / (BrE)
not easily hurt or offended by criticism or insults; insensitive to criticism
not easily offended by criticism

Examples

You need to be thick-skinned to work in customer service, as people can be rude.
Although she was criticized harshly, her thick-skinned nature helped her remain calm.
Politicians often have to be thick-skinned in the face of public scrutiny.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensitive - easily affected by criticism or the feelings of others
  • Synonym: tough - able to withstand adverse conditions or criticism
