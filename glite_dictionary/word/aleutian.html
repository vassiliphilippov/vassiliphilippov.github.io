Aleutian

1. ADJ

/ əˈluːʃən / (AmE), / əˈluːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
relating to the Aleut people, their language, or their culture
of the Aleut people or culture

Examples

The museum features an impressive collection of Aleutian artifacts dating back hundreds of years.
He is studying the Aleutian language in order to understand the culture better.
Traditional Aleutian music is an important part of local festivals.

Etymology

From Aleut, the name of the indigenous people of the Aleutian Islands, plus the suffix -ian.

2. NOUN

/ əˈluːʃən / (AmE), / əˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a member of the Indigenous people inhabiting the Aleutian Islands and southwestern Alaska
member of an Indigenous group in Aleutian Islands/Alaska

Examples

The Aleutian people have a rich tradition of seafaring and hunting.
Many Aleutians continue to speak their native language alongside English.
Artifacts from ancient Aleutian settlements are displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aleut - a member of the Indigenous people of the Aleutian Islands and nearby areas

Etymology

From Russian 'Aleuty', plural of 'Aleut', their self-designation; adopted into English in the 18th century.

Synset POS: n
