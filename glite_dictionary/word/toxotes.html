Toxotes

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈsoʊtiːz / (AmE), / tɒkˈsoʊtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical fish, known as archerfishes, that are the type genus of the family Toxotidae.
genus of archerfishes

Examples

The genus Toxotes includes several species of fish famous for their spit-hunting abilities.
Biologists often study Toxotes to understand how archerfishes shoot water at insects above the surface.
The aquarium displayed a remarkable specimen of Toxotes with its distinctive flat body and sharp snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archerfish - a fish of the genus Toxotes, known for shooting water to catch prey

Etymology

From Greek *toxotes* (archer), referring to the fish's method of shooting water to hunt prey.

2. NOUN

/ toʊˈksoʊtiːz / (AmE), / tɒkˈsəʊtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical fish, known as archerfish, that are able to shoot jets of water to knock prey into the water.
genus of archerfish

Examples

The Toxotes genus includes several species famous for their remarkable hunting skills.
Researchers studied the behavior of Toxotes in both wild and aquarium settings.
The unique ability of Toxotes to shoot down insects with water jets has fascinated scientists for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archerfish - Common name for fish of the genus Toxotes

Etymology

From Greek *toxotes* (archer), referring to the fish's ability to 'shoot' down prey.
