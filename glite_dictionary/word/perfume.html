perfume

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.fjuːm / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fjuːm / (BrE)
a pleasant and distinctive smell or fragrance, especially one that is sweet or appealing
pleasant or distinctive fragrance

Examples

The perfume of blooming roses filled the garden every morning.
She recognized his presence by the subtle perfume that lingered after he left the room.
A lovely perfume from the bakery drifted out onto the street, enticing passersby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Antonym: stench - a strong and very unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'perfumare' meaning 'to smoke through'.

2. VERB

/ pərˈfjum / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːm / (BrE)
to apply perfume to something, especially to give it a pleasant fragrance
apply fragrance; scent

Examples

She likes to perfume her linens with lavender spray.
He perfumed the room before the guests arrived.
The breeze perfumed the air with the scent of blooming flowers.

Synset Examples

She perfumes herself every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scent - to impart a pleasant smell to something
  • Antonym: to deodorize - to remove or conceal an unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'perfumare' (to smoke through), from 'per' (through) + 'fumus' (smoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00043211-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.fjuːm / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fjuːm / (BrE)
A fragrant liquid typically made from essential oils and alcohol, used to give a pleasant scent to a person's body.
fragrant liquid for scenting the body

Examples

She sprayed a little perfume on her wrists before leaving the house.
The store carries an exclusive line of French perfume.
His gift to her was a bottle of expensive perfume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - A pleasant, sweet smell; a product used for scent
  • Synonym: cologne - A type of light perfume

Etymology

From Latin 'perfumare' (to smoke through), from 'per-' (through) + 'fumare' (to smoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03922150-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ pərˈfjuːm / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːm / (BrE)
(verb) to fill or impregnate (a place or the air) with a pleasant scent.
to fill with a pleasant scent

Examples

The blooming lilacs perfumed the entire street in spring.
After she baked cookies, the kitchen was perfumed with the smell of chocolate and vanilla.
A gentle breeze perfumed the evening air with the scent of nearby roses.

Synset Examples

orange blossoms perfumed the air in the garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scent - to impart a pleasant smell to something

Etymology

From Latin 'perfumare', meaning 'to smoke through' (per- 'through' + fumare 'to smoke').
