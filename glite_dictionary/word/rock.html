rock

1. NOUN

/ rɑk / (AmE), / rɒk / (BrE)
a hard, solid mass formed from minerals, typically found on the Earth's surface or underground
hard mass of mineral matter

Examples

She picked up a rock and skipped it across the lake.
Heavy rocks blocked the path after the landslide.
A geologist studies the layers in a rock to learn about Earth's history.

Synset Examples

he threw a rock at me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - a hard, solid nonmetallic mineral matter

Etymology

From Old French *roque* or Medieval Latin *rocca* (rock, stone).
