bracteal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræk.ti.əl / (AmE), / ˈbræk.tɪ.əl / (BrE)
relating to, resembling, or functioning as a bract; of or pertaining to a bract
relating to or resembling a bract

Examples

The bracteal leaves are found just below the plant’s flowers.
This species is notable for its large, brightly colored bracteal structures.
Botanists observed that the bracteal formations served to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bract - a modified leaf associated with a reproductive structure in plants

Etymology

From Latin bractea (a thin plate, especially of metal; later applied to plant parts) + -al.
