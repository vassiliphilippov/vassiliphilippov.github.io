hobo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊbəʊ / (BrE)
A person, especially during the early 20th century in the United States, who travels from place to place looking for temporary work, often by hopping freight trains.
travelling worker; itinerant laborer

Examples

During the Great Depression, many hoboes traveled across the country looking for work.
He spent his youth as a hobo, jumping trains from state to state.
The novel follows the journey of a hobo riding the rails in search of opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tramp - a person who travels from place to place, often seeking work or begging
  • Synonym: drifter - a person who moves from place to place without a fixed home or job

Etymology

Probably from American English slang in the late 19th century, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊboʊ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊbəʊ / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place seeking work, often living as a vagrant or homeless person
homeless vagrant or itinerant worker

Examples

During the Great Depression, many hobos traveled the railways searching for work and food.
The old man claimed he had once been a hobo, riding freight trains across the country.
In stories and movies, the life of a hobo is sometimes romanticized as one of freedom and adventure.

Synset Examples

a homeless tramp
he tried to help the really down-and-out bums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tramp - a homeless person who travels about begging or doing odd jobs
  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or job who wanders from place to place
  • Synonym: drifter - a person who moves from place to place without a permanent home or employment

Etymology

Probably of American origin, possibly from the greeting 'Ho, boy!' used by railway workers and vagrants in the late 19th century.

a homeless person who travels from place to place, often in search of work, especially one who does so by riding freight trains
homeless person who travels, often seeking work

Examples

During the Great Depression, many hobos traveled the railways looking for work.
The hobo shared stories of his journeys with anyone who would listen.
