Tahitian

1. NOUN

/ təˈhiːʃən / (AmE), / təˈhiːʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Tahiti, an island in the central South Pacific
person from Tahiti

Examples

The Tahitian greeted us warmly when we arrived on the island.
Many Tahitians continue to practice traditional crafts and dances.
She learned about Tahitian history and culture during her visit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polynesian - a native or inhabitant of Polynesia

Etymology

From Tahiti, the name of the island, with the English suffix -an.

2. ADJ

/ təˈhiːʃən / (AmE), / təˈhiːʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to the island of Tahiti, its people, their language, or their culture.
relating to Tahiti, its people, language, or culture

Examples

She wore a beautiful Tahitian dress embroidered with traditional patterns.
The museum features a collection of Tahitian art and artifacts.
We enjoyed listening to the melodic sounds of Tahitian music during our visit.

Etymology

From Tahiti (island in French Polynesia) + -an (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03133889-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ təˈhiːʃən / (AmE), / təˈhiːʃən / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken on the island of Tahiti and nearby islands in French Polynesia
Oceanic language of Tahiti

Examples

She is fluent in both French and Tahitian.
The Tahitian word for 'hello' is 'ia ora na'.
Researchers have published a new dictionary of the Tahitian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maori - the Polynesian language spoken by the Maori people of New Zealand
  • Synonym: Hawaiian - the Polynesian language spoken in Hawaii

Etymology

From Tahiti, the largest island in French Polynesia, plus the English suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06951071-n
Synset POS: n
