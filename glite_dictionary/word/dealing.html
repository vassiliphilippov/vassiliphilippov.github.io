dealing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of conducting business transactions, especially between individuals or groups
act of conducting business transactions

Examples

He was known for his fairness in all dealing with clients and colleagues.
Honesty plays an important role in international dealing.
Their business dealing ended in mutual benefit for both parties.

Synset Examples

no transactions are possible without him
he has always been honest in his dealings with me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transaction - an instance of buying or selling something; a business deal
  • Synonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement

Etymology

From the verb 'deal,' from Old English 'dælan' (to divide, distribute).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the way in which a person behaves or conducts themselves when interacting with others, especially in matters of fairness or honesty
manner of conduct toward others

Examples

Her reputation for dealing with clients fairly earned her many loyal customers.
The company's honest dealing set it apart from its competitors.
In all his dealing with staff, he was known for his kindness and integrity.

Synset Examples

honest dealing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavior - the way someone acts, especially toward others

Etymology

From Middle English delen (to distribute), from Old English dǣlan, related to 'deal'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01137693-n
Synset POS: n
