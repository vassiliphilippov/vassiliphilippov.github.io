limp

1. NOUN

/ lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
an uneven or abnormal way of walking, typically caused by injury or weakness in a leg or foot
uneven walk, usually from injury

Examples

After twisting his ankle, he developed a noticeable limp for several days.
Her limp became more pronounced each time she stood up from her chair.
The doctor observed the patient's limp while she walked across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobble - to walk in an awkward way, usually because of pain or injury
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
soft or lacking firmness or rigidity; not stiff or firm.
not firm, soft, lacking rigidity

Examples

The lettuce had become limp after sitting in the fridge for too long.
His handshake was so limp that it felt as if he had no strength in his hand.
After a long run, her muscles felt limp and exhausted.

3. VERB

/ lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
to walk with difficulty, typically because of an injury or physical problem affecting the leg or foot
walk with difficulty due to injury

Examples

He started to limp after twisting his ankle during the football match.
She limped across the parking lot, wincing at every step.
Despite his injury, Thomas was determined and limped to the finish line.
