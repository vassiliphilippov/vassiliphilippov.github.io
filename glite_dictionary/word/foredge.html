foredge

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌɛdʒ / (BrE)
the edge of a book opposite the spine, which faces outward when the book is shelved
edge of a book opposite the spine

Examples

Carefully gild the foreedge to protect the pages from moisture.
The librarian noticed some writing on the foreedge of the old volume.
As the book was pulled from the shelf, dust fell from its foreedge.

Etymology

From 'fore-' (meaning 'front') + 'edge'; first recorded in the 17th century.
