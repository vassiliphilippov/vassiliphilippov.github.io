overpressure

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vərˌprɛʃ.ər / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəˌprɛʃ.ə / (BrE)
a sudden or temporary increase in air pressure above the normal atmospheric level, often resulting from an explosion or shock wave
transient excess air pressure

Examples

The sound of the explosion created a powerful overpressure in the area.
Military vehicles are often designed to withstand sudden overpressure caused by nearby blasts.
Emergency protocols require evacuation if overpressure in the facility exceeds safe limits.

Synset Examples

the overpressure of the blast kills by lethal concussion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underpressure - pressure lower than the surrounding atmospheric pressure

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (above, excessive) + 'pressure', following standard English compounding patterns.
