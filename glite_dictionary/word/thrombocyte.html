thrombocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːm.boʊ.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈθrɒm.bəʊ.saɪt / (BrE)
a small cell fragment in the blood of vertebrates that is essential for blood clotting; also called a platelet
blood cell fragment for clotting
medical

Examples

A low thrombocyte count can increase the risk of excessive bleeding during surgery.
Doctors monitor thrombocyte levels in patients receiving chemotherapy.
A healthy person has between 150,000 and 450,000 thrombocytes per microliter of blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platelet - another term for thrombocyte, a blood cell fragment involved in clotting

Etymology

From Greek 'thrombos' (clot) + 'kytos' (cell).
