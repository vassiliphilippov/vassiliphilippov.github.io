Johann Strauss I

1. PROPN

/ joʊˌhɑn ˈstraʊs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / jəʊˌhan ˈstraʊs ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1804–1849) An Austrian composer known for composing waltzes and popular dance music; father of Johann Strauss II.
Austrian composer of waltzes (1804–1849)

Examples

Many of Johann Strauss I's waltzes became favorites at Viennese balls.
The rivalry between Johann Strauss I and his son influenced the world of classical music.
Johann Strauss I is credited with popularizing the waltz in nineteenth-century Austria.
