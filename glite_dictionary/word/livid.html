livid

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪd / (BrE)
(of a person’s face or lips) unusually pale or ashen, as from illness, shock, or strong emotion.
pale or ashen from illness or shock

Examples

Her face turned livid when she heard the terrible news.
After hours in the hospital, his lips were livid with exhaustion.
The shock left him livid and unable to speak for several minutes.

Synset Examples

a face turned ashen
the invalid's blanched cheeks
tried to speak with bloodless lips
a face livid with shock
lips … livid with the hue of death
lips white with terror
a face white with rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashen - pale or gray in color, especially from illness or fear
  • Synonym: bloodless - lacking color, as from illness or fear

Etymology

From Latin lividus (bluish, of a leaden color), from livere (to be bluish).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪd / (BrE)
extremely angry; furious
furiously angry

Examples

He became livid when he found out he had been lied to.
Her livid reaction surprised everyone in the meeting.
The teacher was livid over the students' reckless behavior.

Synset Examples

willful stupidity makes him absolutely livid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furious - extremely angry
  • Synonym: enraged - very angry, in a rage
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling or showing anger

Etymology

From Latin lividus (bluish, envious, spiteful); originally referring to bluish color, later extended to describe extreme anger.

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪd / (BrE)
(of light or color) having a pale, deathlike, or ghostly luminosity; bluish-grey or pallid in appearance, often suggesting unnaturalness or eeriness.
pale, ghostly light or color

Examples

The storm brought livid clouds that glowed eerily against the night sky.
The old photograph captured a livid light falling across the empty room.
Her face was illuminated by the livid glow of the television, casting strange shadows on the wall.

Synset Examples

livid lightning streaked the sky
a thousand flambeaux … turned all at once that deep gloom into a livid and preternatural day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallid - pale, typically due to poor health or fear

Etymology

From Latin *lividus* (‘bluish’), from *livere* (‘to be blue’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00273038-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪd / (BrE)
(Of the skin) discolored by bruising, especially with a bluish or dark mark caused by blood beneath the skin.
discolored by bruising

Examples

He had a livid bruise on his arm after falling off his bike.
Her leg was livid where she had bumped it against the table edge.
Doctors examined the livid marks left by the accident.

Synset Examples

beaten black and blue
livid bruises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruised - having a mark or discoloration from an injury under the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'lividus', meaning 'bluish', from 'livere' (to be blue).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
