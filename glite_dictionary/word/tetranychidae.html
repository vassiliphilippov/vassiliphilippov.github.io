Tetranychidae

1. NOUN

/ tɛtrəˈnɪkɪdi / (AmE), / tɛtrəˈnɪkɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small plant-feeding mites, some of which are important agricultural pests known as spider mites.
family of plant-feeding mites
medical

Examples

Many crops are threatened by infestations of Tetranychidae, which can damage leaves and reduce yields.
The red spider mite, a member of the Tetranychidae, is a common pest in greenhouses.
Researchers study Tetranychidae to develop better methods of controlling agricultural pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider mite - a common name for members of the family Tetranychidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'tetra-' (four) + 'onyx' (claw), referring to their leg claws.
