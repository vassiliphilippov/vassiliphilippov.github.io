Thaddaeus

1. PROPN

/ θəˈdeɪəs / (AmE), / θəˈdeɪəs / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the Twelve Apostles, sometimes identified as the brother of St. James and invoked as the patron saint of desperate or lost causes.
one of the Twelve Apostles in the New Testament

Examples

Many people pray to Thaddaeus when faced with seemingly impossible situations.
The story of Thaddaeus is often told in Christian teachings about faith and perseverance.
According to some traditions, Thaddaeus is considered the patron saint of hopeless cases.

Etymology

From Greek Thaddaios, from Aramaic Thaddai.
