lenience

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːniəns / (AmE), / ˈliːniəns / (BrE)
the quality of being merciful, tolerant, or not strict in judging or punishing others
quality of being merciful or tolerant

Examples

The judge showed lenience and reduced the sentence for the first-time offender.
Her parents' lenience allowed her more freedom than most children her age.
He pleaded for lenience, hoping the committee would forgive his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected
  • Antonym: severity - the quality of being strict or harsh

Etymology

From Latin lenientia (softness, gentleness), via French "lenience".

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːniəns / (AmE), / ˈliːniəns / (BrE)
the quality of being less severe or more tolerant than expected; mercy or tolerance shown in judging or punishing someone
mercy or tolerance in punishment

Examples

The judge showed unexpected lenience and reduced the defendant’s sentence.
Parents sometimes offer lenience to children who admit their mistakes.
The teacher’s lenience allowed several students to retake the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leniency - alternative spelling meaning the same quality of mercy or tolerance
  • Antonym: harshness - the quality of being severe or strict

Etymology

From Latin 'lenis' (mild, gentle), via French 'lenité' and Late Latin 'lenientia'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːniəns / (AmE), / ˈliːniəns / (BrE)
the quality of being gentle and allowing, especially by yielding to others' wishes or showing tolerance
tendency to be gentle or permissive

Examples

The principal showed lenience toward the students who confessed their mistakes.
Too much lenience from parents can lead to spoiled children.
The judge’s lenience surprised both the defendant and the attorneys.

Synset Examples

too much indulgence spoils a child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strictness - the quality of being strict or inflexible
  • Synonym: indulgence - the act or quality of being permissive or lenient

Etymology

From Latin 'lenientia' (gentleness), from 'lenis' (soft, gentle).
