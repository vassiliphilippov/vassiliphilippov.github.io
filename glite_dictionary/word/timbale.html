timbale

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈtɪm.bɑːl / (BrE)
A small pastry shell, often filled with creamy mixtures of minced foods such as meat, fish, or vegetables and baked as a savory dish.
small pastry shell for savory fillings

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious timbale filled with seafood and a creamy sauce.
She served spinach and mushroom timbales as a starter at the dinner party.
The golden pastry of the timbale was perfectly crisp and flaky.

Etymology

From French 'timbale', originally meaning 'kettledrum', referring to the dish's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmˌbæl, tɪmˈbɑl / (AmE), / ˈtɪm.bæl / (BrE)
A dish consisting of minced meat, fish, or vegetables in a rich, creamy sauce, baked in a small pastry mold or shell.
savoury dish baked in a pastry mold

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious timbale filled with salmon and spinach for the dinner party.
Each guest was served an individual timbale as the appetizer course.
Her favorite brunch dish was a mushroom and cheese timbale, elegantly baked in a pastry shell.

Etymology

From French 'timbale' (drum; drum-shaped mold or dish), from Italian 'timbala', ultimately from Arabic 'ṭunbāl' (drum).
