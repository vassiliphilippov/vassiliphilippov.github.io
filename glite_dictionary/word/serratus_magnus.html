serratus magnus

1. NOUN

/ səˌreɪtəs ˈmæɡnəs / (AmE), / səˌreɪtəs ˈmæɡnəs / (BrE)
a large muscle on the side of the chest that helps rotate the scapula and elevate the rib cage
muscle that rotates scapula, elevates ribs
medical

Examples

During the physical exam, the doctor checked the serratus magnus for signs of muscle weakness.
Physical therapists often target the serratus magnus in rehabilitation exercises for shoulder injuries.
The serratus magnus plays a key role in lifting the arms above the head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serratus anterior - muscle of the chest wall that moves the scapula forward

Etymology

From Latin 'serratus' (saw-shaped) and 'magnus' (great, large).
