zymotic

1. ADJ

/ zaɪˈmɑtɪk / (AmE), / zaɪˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to fermentation or caused by fermentation processes, especially in context of infectious diseases formerly believed to be caused by fermentative action.
relating to fermentation or fermentative diseases

Examples

The scientist studied the zymotic processes that transform sugar into alcohol.
In the 19th century, many diseases were believed to have a zymotic origin.
The winemaker monitored the zymotic activity during the initial stages of fermentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fermentative - causing or relating to fermentation

Etymology

From Greek *zumōtikos* (causing fermentation), from *zumoō* (to ferment).

2. ADJ

/ zaɪˈmɑtɪk / (AmE), / zaɪˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by infection, especially by contagious or infectious diseases.
related to infectious disease

Examples

The rapid spread of the zymotic disease alarmed public health officials.
During the 19th century, many epidemics were classified as zymotic fevers.
Doctors believed the water supply was responsible for the zymotic outbreak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - likely to be transmitted to people, organisms, etc., through the environment

Etymology

From Greek *zumōtikos* (causing fermentation), from *zumoun* (to ferment).
