kinesthetically

1. ADV

/ kɪˌnɛsˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / kɪˌnɛsˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to or involves kinesthesia—the sense of body movement and position.
in a kinesthetic manner

Examples

She learns most effectively kinesthetically, through activities that involve movement and touch.
The test measured the ability to solve problems kinesthetically as well as visually.
The dancer expressed the story kinesthetically, using her body rather than words.

Synset Examples

he can perceive shapes kinesthetically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinesthetic - relating to the sensation of movement or position

Etymology

Formed from 'kinesthetic,' which is from Greek 'kinesis' (movement) and 'aisthēsis' (perception, sensation), with the adverbial suffix '-ally.'
