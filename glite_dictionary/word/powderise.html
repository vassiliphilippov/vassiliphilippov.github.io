to powderise

1. VERB

/ ˈpaʊdɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəraɪz / (BrE)
to make or become a powder by breaking up into fine particles
reduce to powder
british

Examples

The pharmacist needed to powderise the tablets before mixing them into the solution.
Certain recipes require you to powderise whole spices for better flavor.
The rocks were powderised by years of erosion and harsh weather.

Synset Examples

pulverize the grains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pulverize - to reduce to powder or dust by crushing or grinding

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-ise', British spelling variant of 'powderize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpaʊdɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəˌaɪz / (BrE)
to become powder or dust; to be reduced to fine particles
become powder or dust

Examples

The rocks began to powderise after years of exposure to the wind.
If you overheat certain chemicals, they can powderise and spread through the air.
The old manuscript slowly powderised in the damp environment.

Synset Examples

When it was blown up, the building powderized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to powderize - to reduce to powder; alternative spelling

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-ise', patterned after other verbs meaning 'to make or become.'
