outpouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌpɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
An act or instance of something, especially a liquid, being poured out in a large quantity.
a flowing out of a fluid

Examples

A sudden outpouring of lava covered the hillside after the eruption.
The heavy rain caused an outpouring of water onto the streets.
There was an outpouring of champagne from the bottle as it was opened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flow - a steady movement of liquid

Etymology

From out- + pouring, formed in English from 'pour'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌpɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden flow or stream of something, especially liquid or emotion, poured out rapidly and in large amounts
sudden rapid flow or stream

Examples

The storm caused an outpouring of water down the hillside within minutes.
There was an outpouring of tears when the sad news was announced.
A sudden outpouring of lava threatened the nearby villages.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gush - a sudden rapid flow, especially of liquid

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'outpour' (from 'out' + 'pour') plus -ing.

3. NOUN

/ aʊtˈpɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / aʊtˈpɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden and large amount of something that flows out, especially expressed emotions or responses
a large flow or expression of something

Examples

There was an outpouring of sympathy from people around the world.
The accident triggered an outpouring of support for the victims' families.
An outpouring of emotion swept through the audience after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overwhelming quantity or volume of something
  • Degree Lower: trickle - a small, slow flow

Etymology

From the verb 'outpour', formed from 'out-' + 'pour'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌpɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden and intense flow or expression of something, especially words, feelings, or responses
sudden, intense flow or expression

Examples

The announcement triggered an outpouring of emails from concerned citizens.
Her speech was met with an outpouring of support and gratitude from the audience.
After the news broke, there was an outpouring of comments on social media.

Synset Examples

a barrage of questions
a bombardment of mail complaining about his mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid, overwhelming outpouring, especially of words or questions

Etymology

From the verb 'outpour', meaning to pour out, first recorded in English in the 16th century.
