Podicipedidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdɪˈsɪpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɪˈsɪpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic diving birds known as grebes, found worldwide.
family of aquatic diving birds (grebes)

Examples

The Podicipedidae family includes all known species of grebes.
Methods of identification for Podicipedidae often involve observing their distinctive plumage and diving behavior.
Conservation efforts have helped stabilize some populations within the Podicipedidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - a diving water bird belonging to the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Podicipedidae, based on Latin 'podicipes' (grebe, literally 'rump-footed').

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɒdɪˌsɪpɪˈdaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɪˌsɪpɪˈdiːdiː / (BrE)
the family of aquatic birds known as grebes, coextensive with the order Podicipitiformes
a family of aquatic birds (grebes)

Examples

Grebes belong to the Podicipedidae, a family of highly aquatic birds found worldwide.
The Podicipedidae exhibit unique courtship dances that distinguish them among waterfowl families.
Scientists have studied the distribution of Podicipedidae to understand their migratory patterns.

Etymology

From New Latin Podicipedidae, based on genus Podiceps (from Latin 'podicis' (rump) + 'pes' (foot)) and standard zoological family suffix '-idae'.
