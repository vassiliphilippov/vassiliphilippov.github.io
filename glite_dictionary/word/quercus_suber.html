Quercus suber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˈsuːbər / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈsuːbə / (BrE)
a medium-sized evergreen oak tree native to southern Europe and northern Africa, known for its thick, corky bark that is harvested commercially to produce cork
cork oak tree, source of commercial cork

Examples

The bark of Quercus suber is harvested sustainably to produce natural cork stoppers for wine bottles.
In its native habitat, Quercus suber supports a unique ecosystem of wildlife and plants.
Scientists are studying Quercus suber for its resilience to drought and role in preventing soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cork oak - common name for Quercus suber, an evergreen oak tree producing cork

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak', and 'suber' meaning 'cork' or 'cork oak'.
