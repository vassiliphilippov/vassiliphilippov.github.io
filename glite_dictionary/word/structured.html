structured

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃəd / (BrE)
Organized in a definite, systematic, and arranged way.
organized or arranged in a system

Examples

The company offers a structured training program for new employees.
Children benefit from a structured environment where routines are clear.
Her day is carefully structured to maximize productivity and minimize stress.

Synset Examples

a structured environment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unstructured - not organized or arranged in a systematic way

Etymology

From 'structure' + '-ed', meaning provided with structure.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01676037-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃəd / (BrE)
having a clearly organized or arranged form, especially in a way that resembles the organization of a living organism.
clearly organized; arranged like an organism

Examples

The new curriculum will be more structured to address different learning needs.
Their company relies on a highly structured approach to project management.
Modern cities are structured to support millions of residents efficiently.

Synset Examples

society as an integrated whole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organized - arranged or structured in a systematic way

Etymology

From Latin 'structura' (a fitting together, building).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01684403-s
Synset POS: s
