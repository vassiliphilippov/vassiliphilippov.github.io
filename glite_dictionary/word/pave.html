to pave

1. VERB

/ peɪv / (AmE), / peɪv / (BrE)
to cover a surface, especially a road or sidewalk, with a hard material such as stone, concrete, or asphalt to make it suitable for use
cover with hard material (road, etc.)

Examples

The city decided to pave Main Street to make it safer for drivers.
Workers started to pave the old gravel path with concrete last week.
Many historic towns still have cobblestones instead of paved roads.

Synset Examples

pave the roads in the village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - to provide (a road, street, etc.) with a smooth hard covering

Etymology

From Latin 'pavire' (to beat, ram, tread down).
