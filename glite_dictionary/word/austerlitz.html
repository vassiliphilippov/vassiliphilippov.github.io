Austerlitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔstərˌlɪts / (AmE), / ˈɔːstəlɪts / (BrE)
a town in eastern Czech Republic, famous as the site of the Battle of Austerlitz in 1805, where Napoleon won a decisive victory
town in Czech Republic, site of 1805 battle

Examples

Historians often refer to Austerlitz when discussing Napoleon's military genius.
The fields around Austerlitz are now quiet, but they once echoed with the sounds of battle.
She visited Austerlitz to learn more about its significant role in European history.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɔːstərlɪts / (AmE), / ˈɔːstəlɪts / (BrE)
the decisive 1805 battle during the Napoleonic Wars in which Napoleon's French army defeated Russian and Austrian forces
historic Napoleonic battle, 1805

Examples

The Battle of Austerlitz marked Napoleon's greatest victory against the combined Russian and Austrian forces.
Many historians consider Austerlitz a turning point in European history.
Napoleon's strategy at Austerlitz is still studied in military academies today.

Etymology

Named after the town of Austerlitz (now Slavkov u Brna) in the Czech Republic, near where the battle took place.
