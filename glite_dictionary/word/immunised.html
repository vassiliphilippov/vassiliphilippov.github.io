immunised

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjun.aɪzd / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuː.naɪzd / (BrE)
Having been rendered immune to a particular disease, typically through vaccination.
rendered immune to a disease
medical british

Examples

All schoolchildren must be immunised against measles before starting classes.
After traveling abroad, she was immunised for several tropical diseases.
The immunised population had a much lower risk of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunized - Having been rendered immune to a disease (American spelling)
  • Antonym: susceptible - Likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing

Etymology

From 'immunise' (British spelling of 'immunize'), from Latin 'immunis' (exempt, free from).
