virus

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
A harmful or corrupting influence that spreads, often used metaphorically to describe ideas or attitudes.
harmful or corrupting influence (figurative)

Examples

The virus of misinformation can quickly infect entire communities.
Some believe that hatred behaves like a virus, quietly spreading from person to person.
She warned that the virus of cynicism could undermine the team's morale.

Synset Examples

bigotry is a virus that must not be allowed to spread
the virus of jealousy is latent in everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - something that causes continual trouble or distress, especially when spreading
  • Synonym: blight - something that has a damaging effect

Etymology

From Latin 'virus' meaning 'poison', 'slimy liquid'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
An extremely small infectious agent that can multiply only within the living cells of an organism, often causing disease.
microscopic infectious agent; causes disease

Examples

Many people became ill after being exposed to the virus.
Scientists are researching new ways to combat the virus.
The cold is caused by a virus, not by bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bacterium - a single-celled microorganism, distinct from a virus
  • Synonym: pathogen - an agent that causes disease

Etymology

From Latin 'virus' meaning 'poison, slime'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331343-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a computer program that can copy itself and often causes harm to files or other programs on the same computer
malicious self-replicating computer program

Examples

The company's network was infected by a virus that deleted important files.
He installed new security software to protect his computer from a virus.
Opening email attachments from unknown senders might expose your system to a virus.

Synset Examples

a true virus cannot spread to another computer without human assistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malware - software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise computers or networks
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06597992-n
Synset POS: n
