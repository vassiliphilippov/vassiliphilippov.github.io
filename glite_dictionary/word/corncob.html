corncob

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌkɑb / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌkɒb / (BrE)
the hard, cylindrical core that holds the kernels in an ear of corn
core of an ear of corn

Examples

After eating the sweet corn, she threw the corncob into the compost bin.
The farmer fed the leftover corncobs to his livestock.
John carved a pipe from a dried corncob as a hobby project.
