Gallinago gallinago

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡoʊ ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡəʊ ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A medium-sized wading bird found in Eurasia and Africa, known as the common snipe, with a long straight bill and cryptic plumage.
common snipe species of Eurasia and Africa

Examples

The wetlands are home to the elusive Gallinago gallinago, which is often heard rather than seen.
During migration season, hundreds of Gallinago gallinago stop at this reserve to rest and feed.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a group of Gallinago gallinago foraging along the muddy banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common snipe - a medium-sized, long-billed wading bird found in wet habitats

Etymology

From Latin: gallina (hen) and -ago (resembling), used in scientific naming.
