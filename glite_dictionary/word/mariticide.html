mariticide

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈtaɪsaɪd / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈtɪsɪd / (BrE)
The act of killing one's husband.
killing of a husband by his wife
legal

Examples

The court found her guilty of mariticide after a lengthy trial.
In literature, mariticide often serves as a dramatic turning point in the plot.
Historically, mariticide has been treated as a distinct criminal offense in some legal systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uxoricide - the act of killing one's wife
  • Degree Higher: homicide - the act of killing a human being

Etymology

From Latin 'maritus' (husband) + '-cide' (killer).
