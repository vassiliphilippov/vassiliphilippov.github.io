Arctostaphylos

1. NOUN

/ ɑrktoʊˈstæfɪloʊs / (AmE), / ɑːktəʊˈstæfɪlɒs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs in the heath family commonly known as bearberry and manzanita, native to North America.
genus of shrubs (bearberry, manzanita)

Examples

The Arctostaphylos species are widespread in the chaparral ecosystems of California.
Gardeners often plant Arctostaphylos for their attractive red bark and evergreen leaves.
Some bears feed on the berries of Arctostaphylos shrubs during autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearberry - a common name for some species of Arctostaphylos
  • Synonym: manzanita - common name for several species of Arctostaphylos

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) and staphyle (bunch of grapes), referring to the plant's berries.
