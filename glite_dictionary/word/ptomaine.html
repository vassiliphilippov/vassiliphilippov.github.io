ptomaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊmeɪn / (BrE)
a toxic compound formed in decaying animal and vegetable matter, once believed to cause food poisoning; now an obsolete scientific term
obsolete term for food poisoning toxin
archaic medical

Examples

In the nineteenth century, cases of food poisoning were often blamed on ptomaine contamination.
The old textbooks referred to ptomaine poisoning before bacteria were widely understood.
She feared that eating the spoiled meat would result in ptomaine poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living organisms
  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes illness or death when taken into the body

Etymology

From French *ptomaïne*, from Greek *ptōma* (a fallen body, corpse).

2. NOUN

/ toʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / təˈmeɪn / (BrE)
any of various toxic amines (such as putrescine or cadaverine) produced by bacteria during the decomposition of animal or plant matter
toxic compound formed during decomposition
medical

Examples

Certain species of bacteria produce ptomaine compounds when decomposing animal tissue.
The presence of ptomaine in spoiled food can cause severe illness if ingested.
Researchers studied the role of ptomaine in early theories of foodborne poisoning.

Etymology

From Italian 'ptomaine', from Greek 'ptōma' (fall, corpse).
