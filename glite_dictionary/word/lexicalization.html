lexicalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛksələlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɛksəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process by which a concept or meaning is expressed by a single word in a language
creation of a word for a concept

Examples

The lexicalization of new concepts occurs constantly as languages evolve.
Some languages have a lexicalization for ideas that require a phrase in English.
The word 'blog' is an example of the lexicalization of a previously unexpressed idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicon - the vocabulary of a person, language, or branch of knowledge

Etymology

From 'lexical' + '-ization', ultimately from Greek lexis (word) and Latin -alis (relating to).
