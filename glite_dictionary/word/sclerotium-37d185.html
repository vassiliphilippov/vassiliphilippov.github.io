Sclerotium

1. NOUN

/ skləˈroʊtiəm / (AmE), / skləˈrəʊtiəm / (BrE)
a genus of fungi lacking sexual reproduction, some species of which produce sclerotia and cause disease in plants
genus of imperfect fungi

Examples

Sclerotium species are known to cause root rot in numerous crops worldwide.
The hard, resting bodies produced by Sclerotium help the fungus survive adverse conditions.
Researchers identified the fungus as Sclerotium, which is notorious for damaging greenhouse plants.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek sklērotiós (hardness), referring to the hard resting structures (sclerotia) these fungi form.
