Scottish

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɑː.tɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɒt.ɪʃ / (BrE)
relating to Scotland, its people, culture, or languages
of or relating to Scotland or its people

Examples

He is proud of his Scottish heritage and often visits Edinburgh with his family.
The Scottish Highlands are known for their breathtaking scenery and rugged beauty.
She wore a traditional Scottish tartan for the celebration.

Synset Examples

Scots Gaelic
the Scots community in New York
‘Scottish’ tends to be the more formal term as in ‘The Scottish Symphony’ or ‘Scottish authors’ or ‘Scottish mountains’
‘Scotch’ is in disfavor with Scottish people and is used primarily outside Scotland except in such frozen phrases as ‘Scotch broth’ or ‘Scotch whiskey’ or ‘Scotch plaid’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots - referring to the language or people of Scotland

Etymology

From 'Scot' (a native of Scotland) + -ish (adjective suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɑː.tɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɒt.ɪʃ / (BrE)
the dialect of English spoken in Scotland, distinct from standard British English and Scots
dialect of English spoken in Scotland

Examples

He enjoys studying Scottish to better understand the culture of Scotland.
Some words in Scottish differ significantly from standard English.
Her grandparents still speak Scottish at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scots - the Germanic language related to English, spoken in Lowland Scotland
