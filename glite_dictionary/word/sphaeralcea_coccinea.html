Sphaeralcea coccinea

1. NOUN

/ sfɛrˈælsiə kɒkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / sfɪəˈrælsiə kɒkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to the western United States, also known as the false mallow, characterized by racemose red flowers.
species of flowering plant (false mallow) in western U.S.

Examples

Sphaeralcea coccinea thrives in dry prairies across the western United States.
Gardeners appreciate Sphaeralcea coccinea for its vibrant racemose red flowers and drought tolerance.
Unlike some other mallows, Sphaeralcea coccinea blooms abundantly from late spring to midsummer.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Sphaeralcea' from Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and 'alkea' (mallow), species name 'coccinea' from Latin (scarlet).
