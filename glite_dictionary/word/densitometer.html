densitometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛn.sɪˈtɑː.mɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˌdɛn.sɪˈtɒ.mɪ.tə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the density or specific gravity of a substance.
instrument measuring density

Examples

The laboratory uses a densitometer to measure the concentration of substances in solution.
A densitometer can help assess bone density in medical testing.
He calibrated the densitometer before analyzing the photographic negatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrometer - instrument for measuring the density of liquids
  • Synonym: spectrophotometer - instrument measuring intensity of light to determine concentrations

Etymology

From Latin "densus" (thick) and Greek "metron" (measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛn.səˈtɑː.mə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌdɛn.sɪˈtɒm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the optical or photographic density of a material
instrument for measuring optical density

Examples

A densitometer can be used to ensure proper exposure in photographic prints.
The technician calibrated the densitometer before measuring the film's density.
In quality control, a densitometer helps monitor the consistency of printed materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrophotometer - an instrument used to measure the intensity of light at different wavelengths

Etymology

From Latin 'densus' (thick, dense) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
