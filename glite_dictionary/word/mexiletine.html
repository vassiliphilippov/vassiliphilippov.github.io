mexiletine

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛk.sɪˈliː.tin / (AmE), / ˌmɛk.sɪˈliː.tiːn / (BrE)
An antiarrhythmic drug used to treat serious irregular heart rhythms, especially ventricular arrhythmias.
drug for heart rhythm disorders
medical

Examples

The physician prescribed mexiletine to manage the patient's ventricular arrhythmia.
After starting mexiletine, she experienced fewer irregular heartbeats.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking mexiletine for arrhythmia.
