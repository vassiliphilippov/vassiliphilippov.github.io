Cynoscion

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɑːskiˌɒn / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒsɪˌɒn / (BrE)
a genus of marine fishes in the family Sciaenidae, commonly known as sea trout or weakfish, found along the coasts of the Americas
a genus of marine fishes (sea trout)

Examples

The Atlantic coast is home to several species of Cynoscion prized by anglers.
Researchers studied the spawning habits of Cynoscion populations in the Gulf of Mexico.
Commercial fisheries often refer to Cynoscion species as sea trout or weakfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea trout - a common name for various species in the genus Cynoscion

Etymology

From Greek 'kynos' (dog) + 'skion' (shadow), possibly alluding to features of the fish.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɑːsiˌɑn / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒsiˌɒn / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the drum family, commonly known as sea trouts, found in warm coastal waters of the Americas.
a genus of marine fish (sea trouts)

Examples

The Cynoscion species are prized by anglers for their fighting ability and flavor.
Biologists have identified several new members of the Cynoscion genus in recent years.
Many coastal fisheries depend on healthy populations of Cynoscion for their economic success.

Etymology

Modern Latin, possibly from Greek 'kyon' (dog) and 'skion' (a fish), referring to dog-like teeth.
