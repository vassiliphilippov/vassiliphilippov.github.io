Filicinae

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪsɪniː / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪsɪniː / (BrE)
A former botanical subclass comprising all ferns.
subclass of ferns (botany)

Examples

Many species within the Filicinae are valued as ornamental plants.
Paleobotanists study fossil records to trace the evolution of the Filicinae.
In older botanical texts, ferns were often placed in the subclass Filicinae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - Any of numerous nonflowering vascular plants with fronds and reproducing by spores

Etymology

From Latin 'filix' (fern), used in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪsɪniː / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪsɪniː / (BrE)
A group of primitive, non-flowering vascular plants known as ferns, characterized by their leafy fronds and reproduction via spores.
group of vascular plants: ferns

Examples

The Filicinae are commonly found in moist, shaded forests worldwide.
Botanists studied the reproduction process of Filicinae in a controlled environment.
Many garden enthusiasts grow various species of Filicinae for their ornamental foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - any of a group of non-flowering vascular plants with feathery fronds

Etymology

From Latin 'filix' (fern) with the suffix '-inae' denoting a taxonomic group.
