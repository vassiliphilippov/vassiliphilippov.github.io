dielectrolysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪɪlɛkˈtrɑləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪɪlɛkˈtrɒləsɪs / (BrE)
the movement of charged particles within a colloid under the influence of an electric field, where positively charged particles migrate to the cathode and negatively charged particles to the anode
movement of charged particles in a colloid by electric field

Examples

The scientist studied dielectrolysis to understand how colloidal particles move under electrical forces.
A lab demonstration showed dielectrolysis causing different particles to separate when voltage was applied.
Research on dielectrolysis has applications in the purification of certain colloidal solutions.

Etymology

From Greek 'dia-' (through) + 'electrolysis', referring to the process involving electrical forces.
