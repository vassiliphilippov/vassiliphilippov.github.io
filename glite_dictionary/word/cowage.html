cowage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the pods of the cowage plant or the stinging hairs that cover them, used as a vermifuge when mixed with substances such as honey
pods or stinging hairs of the cowage plant, used medicinally
medical

Examples

The powdered cowage was traditionally used in folk medicine to expel intestinal worms.
He handled the cowage pods carefully to avoid being stung by their hairs.
Ancient healers sometimes mixed cowage with honey as a natural remedy.

Etymology

From Hindi kawā̃ch, referring to the plant Mucuna pruriens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
an annual climbing plant native to Asia, with long clusters of purple flowers and hairy pods, cultivated for green manure and grazing
hairy Asian vine used as green manure and fodder

Examples

The leaves and pods of cowage are sometimes used as fodder for livestock.
Farmers in southern states plant cowage to enrich their soil through green manure.
The densely hairy pods of cowage can cause skin irritation if handled without gloves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucuna - a genus of tropical legumes including cowage

Etymology

From Hindi *kawāñch*, referring to the plant's name in India.
