arrack

1. NOUN

/ əˈræk / (AmE), / əˈræk / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic spirit distilled from the fermented sap of toddy palms, rice, or from fermented molasses, commonly produced in South and Southeast Asia.
a strong spirit made from palm sap or molasses

Examples

We sampled the local arrack during our travels in Sri Lanka.
The bartender recommended a cocktail made with coconut arrack for a unique taste.
Some traditional dishes are flavored with a splash of arrack to enhance their aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rum - a distilled alcoholic drink made from sugarcane products
  • Synonym: vodka - a clear distilled alcoholic beverage of Russian origin

Etymology

From Arabic 'araq' (sweat, juice, spirit), adopted via Hindi and Malay.
