historiography

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌstɔriˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / hɪˌstɒriˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study or writing of history, especially with regard to the methods and principles used by historians
the writing or study of history

Examples

The historiography of the American Revolution has changed dramatically over the last century.
She specializes in historiography, focusing on how historical narratives are constructed.
Recent debates in historiography have explored the impact of bias in historical writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: history - the study of past events

Etymology

From Greek 'historiographia', from 'historia' (history) + 'graphia' (writing).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˌstɔriˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / hɪˌstɒriˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the body of historical writings on a particular topic or period
body of historical writings

Examples

The historiography of the French Revolution has changed dramatically over the centuries.
Debates within the historiography reflect new approaches to colonial history.
Recent scholarship has expanded the historiography to include more diverse perspectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: history - the study of past events, especially in human affairs

Etymology

From Greek historiographia, from historia (history) + -graphia (writing).
