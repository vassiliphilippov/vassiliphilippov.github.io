water-carrying

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌkæriɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌkæriɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of performing menial or unrecognized tasks, or acting as an unquestioning supporter for someone, especially in a subordinate or supportive role
performing menial tasks or uncritical support

Examples

Many interns found themselves doing water-carrying for the senior staff instead of more meaningful projects.
His role on the team had turned into endless water-carrying for others' ideas.
Critics accused the spokesperson of mere water-carrying for the company's policies.

Synset Examples

Her job felt more like water-carrying than a real contribution.
He was accused of water-carrying for the management.
In politics, water-carrying often goes unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt work - menial or thankless tasks
  • Synonym: sycophancy - obsequious behaviour towards someone powerful

Etymology

From the literal task of carrying water as a lowly or supportive job, extended figuratively to such roles in organizations and politics.
