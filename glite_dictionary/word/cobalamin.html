cobalamin

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈbæləˌmɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˈbæləmɪn / (BrE)
a B vitamin (vitamin B12) essential for the production of red blood cells and the proper functioning of the nervous system
vitamin B12; essential B vitamin
medical

Examples

People with a deficiency in cobalamin may develop anemia and neurological problems.
Many vegetarians take supplements to ensure they get enough cobalamin in their diet.
Cobalamin injections are often prescribed to treat pernicious anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B12 - another common name for cobalamin

Etymology

From cobalt (because the vitamin contains cobalt) + -amin (related to 'amine', a chemical group).
