eyelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɪt / (BrE)
Fabric decorated with small holes that have neatly stitched edges, forming an ornamental pattern.
ornamental fabric with small stitched holes

Examples

The dress was trimmed with delicate eyelet along the hem.
She chose curtains made from white eyelet for a light and airy look.
This pillowcase features intricate eyelet embroidery as its main decoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broderie anglaise - embroidery featuring decorative patterns of small eyelets

Etymology

From Old French 'oillet', diminutive of 'oil' (eye), ultimately from Latin 'oculus' (eye).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɪt / (BrE)
a small metal ring or edge used to reinforce a hole, especially for threading a cord or lace through
small ring reinforcing a hole for cords/laces

Examples

The curtain was hung using metal eyelets that allowed the rod to pass through easily.
She laced her boots, threading the cords through each eyelet securely.
The sail had reinforced eyelets at the corners for attaching ropes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grommet - a ring or edge strip inserted into a hole through thin material

Etymology

From Old French 'oillet' (little eye), diminutive of 'oil' (eye) from Latin 'oculus' (eye).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03137089-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.lət / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.lɪt / (BrE)
a small round hole, typically finished with stitching or a metal ring, in cloth or leather for the passage of a cord, lace, or hook
small finished hole in fabric or leather

Examples

She threaded the ribbon through each eyelet on the corset.
The shoes have metal eyelets for the laces to pass through.
To reinforce the fabric, a brass eyelet was installed at each corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grommet - a metal or plastic ring used to reinforce a hole in fabric or leather

Etymology

From Old French oeillet, diminutive of oeil (eye), from Latin oculus (eye).

Source: WordNet
