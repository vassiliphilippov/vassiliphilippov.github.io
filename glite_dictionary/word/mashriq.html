Mashriq

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːʃ.rɪk / (AmE), / ˈmaʃ.rɪk / (BrE)
The eastern part of the Arab world, usually referring to the region comprising the Levant and parts of the Arabian Peninsula, as distinct from North Africa.
eastern Arab world region

Examples

The cultural traditions of the Mashriq are distinct from those of North Africa.
Many dialects spoken in the Mashriq share common features with Levantine Arabic.
Scholars often study the literary movements of the Mashriq and Maghreb separately.

Etymology

From Arabic مشرق (mashriq, 'east' or 'place of sunrise').
