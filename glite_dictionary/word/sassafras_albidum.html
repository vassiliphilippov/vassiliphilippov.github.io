sassafras albidum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæsəˌfræs ælˈbaɪdəm / (AmE), / ˌsæsəˌfræs ælˈbɪdəm / (BrE)
A North American tree with aromatic leaves and bark, the primary source of sassafras oil, and known for its brittle yellowish wood.
a North American tree used for sassafras oil

Examples

The leaves of sassafras albidum are often used to make traditional teas in some regions.
Researchers study sassafras albidum for its unique aromatic compounds found in the bark.
The forest path was lined with several mature sassafras albidum trees.

Etymology

From Latin: 'sassafras' (the genus name, possibly from Spanish or French via Timucua) and 'albidum' ('whitish').
