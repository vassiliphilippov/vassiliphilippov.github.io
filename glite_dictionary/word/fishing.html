fishing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of catching fish, especially for pleasure as a leisure pursuit.
catching fish for pleasure

Examples

He spent the weekend fishing at the lake with his friends.
Many people enjoy fishing as a way to relax and connect with nature.
The river is famous for its excellent fishing opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angling - the sport or pastime of fishing with a rod and line

Etymology

From Old English 'fiscian' (to fish) + '-ing' (forming a verb noun).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or occupation of catching fish, especially for commercial purposes
occupation of catching fish

Examples

She has worked in fishing since she was a teenager, like many people in her village.
The government regulates fishing to protect marine life and ensure sustainable industry.
Commercial fishing is one of the most dangerous jobs in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fishery - a place where fish are reared for commercial purposes

Etymology

Old English 'fiscian' (to fish) + '-ing', meaning catching fish.
