academism

1. NOUN

/ əˈkædəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkadəmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a style of painting and sculpture characterized by conventional technique and subject matter, influenced by the teachings of European academies of art
art style shaped by European academies

Examples

The exhibition showcased works representing both realism and academism.
Her painting style was criticized for excessive academism and lack of innovation.
Many 19th-century artists rebelled against the strict norms of academism.

Synset Examples

The setting and treatment of the subject here are typical of his artistic approach, which lies between the idealized academicism of Bouguereau and the social realism of Daumier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic art - a style of painting and sculpture influenced by official art academies

Etymology

From French 'académisme', based on 'académie' (academy).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkædəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkadəmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
rigid adherence to formal academic rules, traditions, or scholarly orthodoxy, often regarded as stifling innovation or creativity
rigid academic orthodoxy

Examples

The painter's later works were criticized for their academism and lack of originality.
Some argue that academism discourages creative thinking in scientific research.
His writing style reflected a certain academism that distanced it from general audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodoxy - conformity to established or traditional doctrines, especially in religion or academia
  • Synonym: dogmatism - tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence
  • Antonym: innovation - the introduction of new ideas or methods

Etymology

From French 'académisme', ultimately from 'académie' (academy).
