glumly

1. ADV

/ ˈɡlʌmli / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌmli / (BrE)
in a sullen, dejected, or depressed manner
in a sullen or dejected manner

Examples

She stared glumly out of the window as rain poured down.
He glumly accepted the bad news without saying much.
The children walked home glumly after losing the match.

Synset Examples

he sat in his chair dourly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomily - in a gloomy or depressed manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic manner

Etymology

From 'glum' (mid 18th century), likely from Scots or Low German, meaning 'moody' or 'sullen' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
