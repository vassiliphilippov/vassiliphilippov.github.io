persnickety

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsnɪkəti / (AmE), / pəˈsnɪkəti / (BrE)
Placing excessive emphasis on trivial details; overly fussy or particular.
overly fussy; paying too much attention to detail

Examples

The chef was so persnickety about every garnish that he delayed the meal.
She has a persnickety attitude when organizing documents, insisting everything be in order.
His persnickety nature made group work challenging, as he corrected even the smallest errors.

Synset Examples

a persnickety job
a persnickety school teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fussy - paying too much attention to small details; hard to please
  • Synonym: fastidious - very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'pernickety', of unknown origin.

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsnɪk.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / pəˈsnɪk.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly North American) Paying too much attention to minor details; fussy or overly particular. Sometimes used to describe someone who is conceited or arrogant about small matters.
overly fussy or particular, sometimes conceited
informal american

Examples

She is so persnickety about her desk that everything must be perfectly aligned.
Don't be so persnickety—no one cares if the towels aren't folded exactly.
His persnickety attitude made it hard to enjoy the simple things.

Synset Examples

a snotty little scion of a degenerate family
they're snobs — stuck-up and uppity and persnickety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fussy - excessively concerned with detail
  • Synonym: fastidious - very attentive to accuracy and detail

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly an alteration of 'pernickety' (chiefly British), itself of unknown origin.
