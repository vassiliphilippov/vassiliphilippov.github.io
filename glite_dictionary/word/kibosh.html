to kibosh

1. VERB

/ kaɪˈbɑʃ / (AmE), / kɪˈbɒʃ / (BrE)
(informal) To put a stop to something; to prevent something from happening or developing.
to prevent or stop something
informal

Examples

The city council decided to kibosh the construction plans after environmental concerns were raised.
Her parents quickly kiboshed her idea of staying out late on a school night.
A sudden downpour could kibosh our plans for an outdoor wedding.

Synset Examples

Block his election
Halt the process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement, progress, or success
  • Synonym: halt - to bring or come to an abrupt stop
  • Antonym: to allow - to permit or let happen

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Yiddish or Irish caip bháis (cap of death).
