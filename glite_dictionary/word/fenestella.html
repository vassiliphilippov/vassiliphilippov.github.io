fenestella

1. NOUN

/ fɛnəˈstɛlə / (AmE), / fɛnɛˈstɛlə / (BrE)
An oval or circular opening in a dome or vault, intended to let in light.
oval or circular architectural opening

Examples

The architect designed a fenestella at the apex of the dome to illuminate the space below.
Restorers carefully cleaned the ancient fenestella, allowing even more sunlight to enter the chamber.
A single fenestella cast a dramatic beam of light onto the floor of the cathedral.

Etymology

From Latin *fenestella* (a small window), diminutive of *fenestra* (window).
