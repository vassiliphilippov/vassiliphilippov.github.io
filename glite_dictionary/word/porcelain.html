porcelain

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːr.səlɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.səlɪn / (BrE)
a hard, white, translucent ceramic material, often used for making dishes, vases, and decorative objects
hard, translucent ceramic material

Examples

The museum displayed an exquisite collection of Ming dynasty porcelain.
She prefers drinking tea from delicate porcelain cups.
The artist crafted each vase by hand using fine porcelain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: china - ceramic ware made of fine, translucent material
  • Antonym: earthenware - ceramic ware made from less refined clay, typically opaque and porous
  • Degree Lower: stoneware - a type of pottery fired at high temperatures, less fine than porcelain

Etymology

From Italian 'porcellana', ultimately from Latin 'porcella' (young sow), referring to the shell's smoothness and resemblance.
