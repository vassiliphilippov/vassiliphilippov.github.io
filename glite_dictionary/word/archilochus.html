Archilochus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈloʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈlɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of hummingbirds in the family Trochilidae, including species such as the ruby-throated hummingbird.
genus of hummingbirds

Examples

The Archilochus genus includes the well-known ruby-throated hummingbird found in North America.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Archilochus species.
Birdwatchers are often excited to spot members of the Archilochus genus during the spring.

Etymology

Named after the ancient Greek poet Archilochus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈloʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈləʊkəs / (BrE)
A genus of hummingbirds in the family Trochilidae, including species such as the ruby-throated hummingbird.
a genus of hummingbirds

Examples

The genus Archilochus includes the ruby-throated and black-chinned hummingbirds.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Archilochus species across North America.
Several species within the genus Archilochus are known for their long migratory routes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hummingbird - small, brightly colored, fast-flying nectar-feeding bird

Etymology

Named after the ancient Greek poet Archilochus.
