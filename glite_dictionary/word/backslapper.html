backslapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌslæpər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌslæpə / (BrE)
a person who shows excessive or noticeably enthusiastic friendliness or goodwill, often in a hearty or demonstrative way
an overly enthusiastic or demonstrative person

Examples

Everyone knows Tom as the office backslapper, always greeting people with loud compliments and hearty laughs.
Some politicians are notorious backslappers, making a show of their friendliness to win support.
His backslapper attitude felt insincere to those who preferred more genuine interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone to gain advantage
  • Antonym: introvert - a person who is shy, reserved, or introspective

Etymology

Formed from 'backslap' (to slap someone's back) + '-er', denoting a person who does this; 'backslap' refers to a gesture of congratulation or friendliness.
