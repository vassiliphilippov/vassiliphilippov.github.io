fairness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˈfeənəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a naturally light or pale complexion
lightness of complexion

Examples

The fairness of her skin was admired throughout the kingdom.
He inherited the fairness of his mother's complexion.
In the portrait, the artist emphasized the fairness of the subject's face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleness - the quality of being pale in color or complexion

Etymology

Formed from 'fair' (light-skinned, beautiful) + noun suffix '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˈfeənəs / (BrE)
the quality of treating people equally or in a way that is right and reasonable, or of conforming to rules or standards
quality of being fair or just

Examples

The principal tried to ensure fairness in awarding scholarships to all students.
Employees value fairness in the workplace when it comes to promotions and raises.
The referee’s commitment to fairness helped maintain order during the game.

Synset Examples

the judge recognized the fairness of my claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justice - the quality of being just or fair
  • Synonym: impartiality - the quality of being unbiased and neutral
  • Antonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group

Etymology

From Old English 'fægernes' (beauty, fairness), from 'fæger' (fair, beautiful).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˈfeənɪs / (BrE)
the quality of making judgments that are free from bias, discrimination, or dishonesty
impartiality; freedom from bias

Examples

The judge was respected for her fairness and integrity in every case she handled.
Employees appreciated the fairness with which promotions were awarded within the company.
Public trust depends on the fairness of our legal and political systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the state of treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • Antonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group
  • Synonym: justice - the quality of being just or fair

Etymology

From 'fair' (just, impartial) + '-ness,' Old English 'fæger' (pleasant, attractive).
