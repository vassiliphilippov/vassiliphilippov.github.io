Artemisia spinescens

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə spaɪˈnɛs.ɛnz / (AmE), / ɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə spaɪˈnɛs.ɛnz / (BrE)
A perennial shrub native to the western United States, valued as forage for sheep; commonly known as budsage.
a western US shrub used as sheep forage

Examples

Ranchers often rely on Artemisia spinescens as an important early spring food source for their sheep.
Ecologists study Artemisia spinescens to understand its role in supporting native wildlife in arid regions.
The resilience of Artemisia spinescens makes it a valuable plant for revegetation projects in the western United States.

Etymology

Genus from Greek goddess Artemis; species Latin 'spinescens' meaning 'becoming spiny'.
