blues

1. NOUN

/ bluːz / (AmE), / bluːz / (BrE)
a style of music originating among African Americans in the early 20th century, characterized by a melancholy sound, specific chord progressions, and expressive lyrics
melancholy American music genre

Examples

She loves listening to old blues records on vinyl.
The guitarist played a soulful blues solo that captivated the audience.
He started singing the blues whenever he felt sad about life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - a genre of music that developed from blues and ragtime
  • Antonym: cheerful - feeling or expressively lighthearted and happy (the opposite mood)

Etymology

From 'blue devils' (feelings of depression), later shortened to 'blues'.

2. NOUN

/ bluːz / (AmE), / bluːz / (BrE)
a feeling of sadness or mild depression, often with no clear cause
a mild state of depression

Examples

Every winter, I get a touch of the blues when the days are so short.
Listening to cheerful music helps lift me out of the blues.
She tried to shake off the blues by meeting friends for coffee.

Synset Examples

he had a bad case of the blues

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: depression - a more severe long-term state of sadness or despair
  • Degree Lower: sadness - the feeling of being sad

Etymology

Originally from 'blue devils', an old phrase for melancholy or depression.
