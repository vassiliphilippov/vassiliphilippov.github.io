rasp

1. NOUN

/ ræsp / (AmE), / rɑːsp / (BrE)
a coarse file with a rough surface of sharp, pointed projections, used for shaping wood or other materials
coarse file with sharp projections

Examples

He used a rasp to smooth the rough edges of the wood.
A metalworker's rasp is essential for shaping hard materials.
The carpenter reached for his rasp to finish the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: file - a tool with a rough surface used for smoothing or shaping material

Etymology

From Middle French *raspe*, from Old French *rasper* (to scrape), from Frankish *raspon* (scraper).

2. VERB

/ ræsp / (AmE), / rɑːsp / (BrE)
to scrape or shape a surface with a coarse file or rasp tool
scrape or file with a rasp

Examples

The carpenter began to rasp the edges of the wooden plank to smooth them.
He used a metal tool to rasp away the paint on the doorframe.
After shaping the clay, she carefully rasped its surface to create a rough texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: file - to smooth or shape with a file tool

Etymology

From Middle English *raspen*, from Old French *rasper* 'to scrape', ultimately from Latin *raspare*, from *radere* (to scrape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01389560-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ræsp / (AmE), / rɑːsp / (BrE)
to speak or say something in a harsh, grating voice
speak in a harsh voice

Examples

He managed to rasp a warning through his clenched teeth.
Her voice began to rasp after hours of shouting at the concert.
With a sore throat, he could only rasp out a few words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - to utter a low, guttural sound, often in anger
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly using the breath

Etymology

From Old French 'rasper' (to scrape), from Latin 'raspare', related to scraping or making a harsh sound.

4. NOUN

/ ræsp / (AmE), / rɑːsp / (BrE)
a harsh or grating sound made when speaking, especially in an irritated or annoyed tone
harsh or irritated utterance

Examples

She spoke with a rasp in her voice, betraying her irritation.
His angry rasp cut through the silence in the room.
A faint rasp escaped him as he struggled to maintain his composure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - a low, guttural sound, especially made in anger
  • Antonym: whisper - speaking very softly without vibration of the vocal cords

Etymology

From Old French *rasper* (to scrape), likely imitative of the sound.
