Zeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈziː.aɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈziː.ɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish, including the John Dory, characterized by compressed bodies and long dorsal fins, in the order Zeiformes.
family of marine fish (John Dory and relatives)

Examples

The Zeidae are known for their distinctive oval-shaped bodies.
Scientists study the Zeidae family to understand adaptations in marine fish.
The John Dory is a well-known member of the Zeidae.

Etymology

From New Latin Zeidae, from genus Zeus + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
