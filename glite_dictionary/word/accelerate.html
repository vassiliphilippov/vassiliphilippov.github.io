to accelerate

1. VERB

/ əkˈsɛlɚˌeɪt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛləˌreɪt / (BrE)
to begin to move or happen more quickly; to increase in speed or rate
to move or happen faster

Examples

The new train can accelerate to 200 kilometers per hour in less than a minute.
The economy began to accelerate after the government introduced new policies.
When the light turned green, she pressed the pedal to accelerate quickly.

Synset Examples

The car accelerated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decelerate - to reduce speed; to slow down
  • Synonym: to speed up - to increase in speed; synonym for accelerate

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'celer' (swift).

2. VERB

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛləreɪt / (BrE)
to cause to move or happen more quickly; to increase the speed of something
make something go faster

Examples

You should accelerate gradually when merging onto the highway.
The teacher accelerated the lesson to fit everything into one class.
Improvements in technology have accelerated the pace of communication worldwide.

Synset Examples

He accelerated the car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decelerate - to reduce the speed of; to slow down
  • Synonym: to speed up - to increase the speed of

Etymology

From Latin accelerare, from ad- (to) + celer (swift)

WordNet ID: oewn-00440298-v
Synset POS: v
