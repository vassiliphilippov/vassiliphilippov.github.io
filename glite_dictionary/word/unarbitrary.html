unarbitrary

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑːrbəˌtrɛri / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːbɪtrəri / (BrE)
not determined by personal judgment, preference, or chance; based on objective or fixed principles
not subject to personal whim or randomness

Examples

Scientific results should be based on unarbitrary methods of data analysis.
The law must have unarbitrary standards that apply equally to everyone.
Their selection process is transparent and unarbitrary, relying on strict criteria.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arbitrary - based on personal choice or random decision, not on reason or system

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'arbitrary', from Latin 'arbitrarius' (dependent on the will, arbitrary).
