fandango

1. NOUN

/ fænˈdæŋgoʊ / (AmE), / fanˈdaŋgəʊ / (BrE)
A lively Spanish courtship dance in triple time, traditionally performed by a man and a woman playing castanets.
Spanish courtship dance in triple time

Examples

The couple performed a passionate fandango to the applause of the audience.
She learned the fandango while traveling through southern Spain.
The rhythmic clacking of castanets is characteristic of a traditional fandango.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamenco - a style of Spanish music and dance

Etymology

Spanish, of uncertain origin, possibly from Andalusian dialect.
