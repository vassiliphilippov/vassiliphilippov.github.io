overthrow

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈθroʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈθrəʊ / (BrE)
to remove a ruler or government from power, often by force
remove a ruler or government by force

Examples

The military plotted to overthrow the dictator in a secret coup.
Rebels eventually overthrew the corrupt regime after months of unrest.
He was accused of trying to overthrow the elected government.

Synset Examples

The Czar was overthrown
subvert the ruling class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to topple - to cause to fall, especially a government or leader
  • Synonym: to depose - to remove a leader from office or power
  • Antonym: to install - to place in a position of power or authority

Etymology

From Old English 'oferþrowan', from 'ofer' (over) + 'þrowan' (to throw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌθroʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌθrəʊ / (BrE)
the removal of a ruler or government, especially by force
removal of a ruler or government by force

Examples

The military staged an overthrow of the corrupt regime.
Civil unrest led to the overthrow of the monarchy.
Several leaders fled the country after the sudden overthrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coup - a sudden, violent, and illegal seizure of power from a government
  • Synonym: revolution - forcible overthrow of a government or social order
  • Antonym: restoration - the return of a ruler or regime to power after being overthrown

Etymology

From Middle English overthrowen (to overturn), from over- + throw.
