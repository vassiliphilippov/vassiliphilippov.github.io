Anasa

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑːsə / (AmE), / əˈnɑːsə / (BrE)
A genus of bugs in the family Coreidae, commonly known as squash bugs, which are pests of squash and related plants.
a genus of squash bugs

Examples

The Anasa species are notorious pests in pumpkin and squash crops.
Farmers often monitor their fields for signs of Anasa infestation during the growing season.
Entomologists classify several common squash bugs under the Anasa genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash bug - a common name for several bugs in the genus Anasa that feed on squash plants

Etymology

From New Latin Anasa, the genus name assigned in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ əˈneɪsə / (AmE), / əˈneɪsə / (BrE)
A genus of bugs in the family Coreidae, commonly known as squash bugs, that feed on plants such as squash and pumpkins.
a genus of squash bugs

Examples

The Anasa genus includes several species destructive to pumpkin crops.
Many gardeners take measures to control Anasa populations in their fields.
The Anasa bugs are known for their piercing-sucking mouthparts that damage squash plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash bug - a common name for bugs in the genus Anasa

Etymology

Formed from Neo-Latin as a taxonomic genus name, origin in scientific classification.
