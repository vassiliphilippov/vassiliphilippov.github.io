tariff

1. NOUN

/ ˈter.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈtær.ɪf / (BrE)
A tax or duty imposed by a government on imported or exported goods.
government tax on imports or exports

Examples

The government introduced a new tariff on steel imports to protect domestic manufacturers.
Many countries negotiate trade agreements to reduce or eliminate tariffs between them.
Raising the tariff on agricultural products sparked a heated political debate.

Synset Examples

they signed a treaty to lower duties on trade between their countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duty - a government tax, especially on imports
  • Antonym: subsidy - government financial support to an industry (opposite of a tax)

Etymology

From Italian 'tariffa', from Arabic 'taʕārīf' (inventory, notification).

WordNet ID: oewn-13337021-n

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛrəf / (AmE), / ˈtærɪf / (BrE)
to impose a tariff or duty on goods, especially imports
impose a tariff or duty

Examples

The government decided to tariff luxury vehicles to protect local manufacturers.
If the country were to tariff steel imports, prices would likely rise.
They have tariffed certain agricultural products to support domestic farmers.

Synset Examples

tariff imported goods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - to impose a tax; to make someone pay a tax

Etymology

From Italian *tariffa*, from Arabic *taʕārīf* (inventory, list of fees).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02312973-v
Synset POS: v
