Begoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ bɪˌɡoʊniˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / bɪˌɡəʊniˈeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of monoecious succulent herbs or shrubs found chiefly in tropical and warm regions, especially in America, including the begonia.
family of tropical and warm-region plants, including begonias

Examples

The Begoniaceae are well known for their vibrant and decorative foliage.
Many species within the Begoniaceae thrive in shady, humid environments.
Gardeners often select members of the Begoniaceae family for ornamental purposes.

Etymology

From the genus name Begonia, named after Michel Bégon, a French official, with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˌɡoʊniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bɪˌɡəʊniˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of monoecious succulent herbs or shrubs native to tropical and warm regions, especially in the Americas, including the begonias
plant family including begonias

Examples

Many species within the Begoniaceae family are prized for their attractive foliage and flowers.
Gardeners often grow members of the Begoniaceae in greenhouses or as houseplants.
The Begoniaceae are widespread in tropical regions, particularly throughout Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Begonia - a plant genus within the family Begoniaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Begonia, named after French patron of botany Michel Bégon, and the botanical family suffix -aceae.
