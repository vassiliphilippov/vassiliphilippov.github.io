clunking

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklʌŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the sound made by horse's hooves striking a hard surface, producing a heavy, dull noise
sound of horse's hooves hitting a hard surface

Examples

The steady clunking of the carriage horses echoed down the cobblestone street.
He listened to the clunking outside and knew the farrier was arriving with the team.
Through the quiet morning air came the repeated clunking of hooves on the pavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clopping - the sound of hoofs hitting a hard surface

Etymology

From the verb 'clunk', imitative of a dull, heavy sound.
