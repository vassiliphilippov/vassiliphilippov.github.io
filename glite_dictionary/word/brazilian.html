Brazilian

1. NOUN

/ brəˈzɪliən / (AmE), / brəˈzɪliən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Brazil
native or inhabitant of Brazil

Examples

Many Brazilians are passionate about football and celebrate their team's victories enthusiastically.
The restaurant is owned by a Brazilian who moved to the city ten years ago.
At the festival, we enjoyed talking to Brazilians about their culture and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazil - the largest country in South America

Etymology

From Brazil + -ian, denoting nationality or origin.

2. ADJ

/ brəˈzɪliən / (AmE), / brəˈzɪliən / (BrE)
relating to Brazil or its people, language, or culture
of or relating to Brazil

Examples

The Brazilian rainforest is home to a vast diversity of wildlife.
She enjoys listening to Brazilian music at the local café.
The restaurant specializes in Brazilian cuisine with authentic flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazilian - a person from Brazil

Etymology

From Brazil + -ian, a common suffix denoting origin or association.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02978374-a
Synset POS: a
