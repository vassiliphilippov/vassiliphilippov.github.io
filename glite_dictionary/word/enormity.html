enormity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɔrməti / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːmɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely wicked or morally wrong; great wickedness
extreme wickedness; moral outrage

Examples

The enormity of his crimes shocked the entire nation.
Many struggled to comprehend the enormity of the dictator's actions.
Historians debate the enormity of the injustice that occurred during that period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong
  • Antonym: virtue - moral excellence; goodness

Etymology

From Old French *enormité* and Latin *enormitas*, from *enormis* (out of rule, irregular).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɔrməti / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːmɪti / (BrE)
a very great crime or act of extreme wickedness; the quality or state of being shockingly evil
extreme wickedness or a very great crime

Examples

The enormity of the dictator's crimes shocked the entire world.
She was appalled by the enormity of his betrayal and cruelty.
Historians still debate the enormity of the events that unfolded during the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocity - an extremely wicked or cruel act, often involving physical violence or injury
  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality and wickedness
  • Antonym: goodness - the quality of being morally good or virtuous

Etymology

From Latin 'enormitas' (‘irregularity, monstrousness’), from 'enormis' (‘out of rule’).

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɔrməti / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːmɪti / (BrE)
the great size or extent of something; vastness (usage sometimes considered incorrect, as 'enormity' more often refers to wickedness).
great size or extent; vastness

Examples

The enormity of the project became clear when we saw the blueprints.
She was overwhelmed by the enormity of the mountain range stretching before her.
Historians debated the enormity of the challenge faced by early settlers.

Synset Examples

in careful usage the noun ‘enormity’ is not used to express the idea of great size
universities recognized the enormity of their task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immensity - the extremely large size, scale, or extent of something
  • Antonym: wickedness - the quality of being morally wrong or evil (the original sense of 'enormity')

Etymology

From Latin 'enormitas' (irregularity, monstrosity), from 'enormis' (out of rule, excessive).
