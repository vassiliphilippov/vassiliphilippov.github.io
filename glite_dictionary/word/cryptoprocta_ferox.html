Cryptoprocta ferox

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈprɒktə ˈfɪərɒks / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈprɒktə ˈfɪərɒks / (BrE)
A large carnivorous mammal native to Madagascar, resembling both cats and civets, known as the fossa.
large carnivore of Madagascar (fossa)

Examples

The Cryptoprocta ferox is the top predator in Madagascar's forests.
Few tourists have seen the elusive Cryptoprocta ferox in the wild.
Researchers are studying how Cryptoprocta ferox hunts its prey across different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossa - the common name for Cryptoprocta ferox, a carnivorous mammal of Madagascar
  • Degree Lower: civet - a small, catlike carnivorous mammal, sometimes used for comparison to the fossa
  • Degree Lower: cat - a carnivorous mammal of the family Felidae, sometimes used for comparison to the fossa

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + proktos (anus) and Latin ferox (fierce), referring to physical features and temperament.
