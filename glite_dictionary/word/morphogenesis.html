morphogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔr.foʊˈdʒɛn.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəʊˈdʒɛn.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the process by which the structure of an organism or a part develops and takes shape through cellular differentiation and growth
development of structure in organisms
medical

Examples

The study focused on the morphogenesis of leaves in various plant species.
Abnormalities in morphogenesis can result in developmental disorders.
Understanding morphogenesis is crucial for advances in regenerative medicine.

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form) + genesis (origin, creation).
