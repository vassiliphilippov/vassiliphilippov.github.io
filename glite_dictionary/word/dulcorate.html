to dulcorate

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌl.kəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʌl.kəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to make sweeter in taste; to sweeten
make sweeter; sweeten

Examples

The chef chose to dulcorate the tart sauce with a touch of honey.
Pharmacists sometimes dulcorate medicines to make them more palatable for children.
Adding vanilla extract will dulcorate the mixture and enhance its flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweeten - to make sweet or sweeter

Etymology

From Latin dulcoratus, past participle of dulcorare (to sweeten), from dulcor (sweetness).
