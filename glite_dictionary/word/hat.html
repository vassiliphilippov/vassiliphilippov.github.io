2. NOUN

/ hæt / (AmE), / hæt / (BrE)
A covering for the head, often with a shaped crown and brim, worn for warmth, protection, or as a fashion item.
covering for the head, often with a brim

Examples

She wore a bright red hat to the wedding.
He took off his hat as he entered the room out of respect.
A strong wind blew the hat right off her head.

Etymology

From Old English hæt, related to German Hut and Dutch hoed.

/ hæt / (AmE), / hæt / (BrE)
an informal term for a particular role or position that someone holds, especially when referring to switching between different areas of responsibility
informal: role or position held by someone
informal

Examples

As a manager, she wears many hats in the company.
Today I'm putting on my teacher hat to help with the school project.
He changed his hat from director to consultant during the meeting.
