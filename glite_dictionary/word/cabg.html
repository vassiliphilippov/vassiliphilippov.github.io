CABG

1. NOUN

/ siː eɪ biː dʒiː / (AmE), / siː eɪ biː dʒiː / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a blood vessel is grafted to bypass a blocked or narrowed coronary artery and improve blood flow to the heart
surgical heart bypass procedure
medical

Examples

The cardiologist recommended a CABG to restore adequate blood flow to the patient's heart.
Many patients recover well after undergoing CABG surgery for severe coronary artery disease.
Several risk factors can increase the likelihood of needing a CABG later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass surgery - surgery to create a new path for blood flow, often referring to coronary arteries
  • Antonym: angioplasty - surgical repair or unblocking of a blood vessel, especially a coronary artery

Etymology

Acronym from 'coronary artery bypass graft'.
