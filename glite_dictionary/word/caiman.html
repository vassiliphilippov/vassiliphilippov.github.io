caiman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.mən / (BrE)
A semiaquatic reptile native to Central and South America, similar to an alligator but with a more heavily armored belly.
a Central/South American alligator-like reptile

Examples

We saw a caiman lurking by the riverbank during our trip to the Amazon.
The biologist explained the differences between a caiman and an alligator to the students.
Several small caimans were basking in the sun near the edge of the swamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator - a large semiaquatic reptile of the family Alligatoridae, native to the Americas and China
  • Synonym: crocodile - a large predatory aquatic reptile known for its long snout and powerful jaws

Etymology

From Spanish 'caimán', likely from Carib or Taíno.
