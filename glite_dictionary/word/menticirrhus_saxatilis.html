Menticirrhus saxatilis

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˌtɪˈsɪrəs sækˈsætɪlɪs / (AmE), / mɛnˌtɪˈsɪrəs sakˈsatɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of whiting found along the east coast of the United States, closely resembling the king whiting
a species of whiting fish

Examples

The Menticirrhus saxatilis is commonly caught by anglers along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers are studying how pollution affects Menticirrhus saxatilis populations in estuaries.
Commercial fisheries report a steady increase in Menticirrhus saxatilis harvested over recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiting - a name for various species of fish in the cod family or similar fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name Menticirrhus (from Latin mentum 'chin' + cirrhus, from Greek 'kirrhos', meaning 'tendril'), species epithet saxatilis from Latin 'saxatilis' meaning 'found among rocks'.
