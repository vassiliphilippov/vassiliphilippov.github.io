headdress

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌdrɛs / (BrE)
a covering or ornament worn on the head, especially as part of ceremonial or traditional attire
covering or ornament for the head

Examples

The bride wore a delicate floral headdress that complemented her gown.
Many Native American tribes have distinct styles of ceremonial headdress.
The museum displayed an ancient Egyptian headdress made of gold and precious stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headgear - any kind of clothing worn on the head

Etymology

From Old English 'heafod' (head) + 'dress' (adornment, clothing).
