Sarpedon

1. PROPN

/ sɑːrˈpiːdən / (AmE), / sɑːˈpiːdən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a son of Zeus who became king of Lycia and fought for the Trojans during the Trojan War, killed by Patroclus.
son of Zeus in Greek mythology

Examples

In the Iliad, Sarpedon is depicted as a noble and courageous warrior.
The death of Sarpedon was a turning point for the Trojan allies.
Many ancient artists depicted Sarpedon being carried from the battlefield by gods.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Σαρπηδών (Sarpēdōn).
