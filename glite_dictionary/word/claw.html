claw

1. NOUN

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
a mechanical device with a curved or hooked part, used to grip, hold, or pull something
mechanical gripping or pulling device

Examples

The crane's claw lifted the debris from the construction site.
He used the hammer's claw to pull out the bent nail.
The vending machine's claw failed to grab the toy.

Etymology

From Old English *clawu* (claw, talon, hoof).

to scratch, tear, or attack with or as if with claws
attack or tear with claws

4. NOUN

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
a pincer-like grasping structure found on the limb of a crustacean or other arthropods, used for catching and holding things
pincer on a crustacean or arthropod's limb

Examples

The crab used its large claw to defend itself from predators.
Lobsters have a powerful claw that can easily crack shells.
Scientists studied the claw structure of various arthropods under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pincer - a pair of hinged claws, especially of a crab or lobster

Etymology

From Old English *clawu* (claw, talon), related to Old Norse *kló*.

7. NOUN

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
A sharp, curved, horny structure at the end of the toe of a bird, some mammals, or reptiles, used for grasping or tearing.
sharp curved structure on animal toe

Examples

The hawk gripped its prey tightly with its powerful claw.
My cat scratched the sofa with her sharp claws.
The lizard's claws helped it climb smoothly up the tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talon - a claw, especially one belonging to a bird of prey
  • Antonym: hoof - the hard foot of an ungulate mammal

Etymology

Old English 'clawu', related to Old Norse 'klō' (claw).
