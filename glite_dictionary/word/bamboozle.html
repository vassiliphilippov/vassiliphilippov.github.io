to bamboozle

1. VERB

/ bæmˈbuːzəl / (AmE), / bæmˈbuːzəl / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone, especially by pretending to have good intentions or by using elaborate schemes
deceive or trick by elaborate methods

Examples

The magician tried to bamboozle the audience with his sleight of hand.
She was completely bamboozled by the complicated sales pitch.
Don't let yourself get bamboozled by promises that sound too good to be true.

Synset Examples

He bamboozled his professors into thinking that he knew the subject well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: fool - trick or deceive someone; dupe
  • Synonym: to mislead - cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: to enlighten - give someone greater knowledge and understanding

Etymology

Origin unknown, but first recorded in early 18th-century English slang.
