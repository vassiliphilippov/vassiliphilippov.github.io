rheumatic

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ruːˈmatɪk / (BrE)
a person suffering from rheumatism
person suffering from rheumatism
medical archaic

Examples

The clinic provides special care for elderly rheumatics during the winter months.
As a rheumatic, he found the damp climate made his symptoms worse.
In Victorian times, many rheumatics sought relief at seaside resorts.

Etymology

From Latin *rheumaticus*, from Greek *rheumatikos* (subject to a flow or flux), with reference to bodily fluids.

2. ADJ

/ ruˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ruˈmatɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by rheumatism or arthritis, especially characterized by pain or stiffness in the joints or muscles.
relating to rheumatism or arthritis
medical

Examples

His rheumatic knees always ached more during the cold winter months.
She takes medication to help relieve her rheumatic pain.
The rheumatic symptoms made it difficult for him to walk long distances.

Synset Examples

my creaky old joints
rheumy with age and grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritic - relating to arthritis; suffering from arthritis

Etymology

From Latin 'rheumaticus', from Greek 'rheumatikos' (subject to flow or flux, referring to bodily fluids), from 'rheuma' (a flux, a flow).
