effector

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that ends on a muscle or gland, stimulating it to contract or secrete
nerve fiber stimulating muscle or gland
medical

Examples

The motor neuron acts as an effector by triggering muscle contraction.
Damage to an effector can impair glandular secretion.
Each effector receives signals from the central nervous system.

Etymology

From Latin effectus (accomplished), from efficere (to accomplish, effect).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes something to happen or brings about a particular result
one who or that which brings about a result

Examples

The manager acted as the main effector behind the company's rapid growth.
She was recognized as the effector of positive change in her community.
Every successful project needs a dedicated effector to turn plans into reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts or has the power to act
  • Synonym: cause - someone or something that brings about an effect

Etymology

From Latin effector (one who accomplishes), from efficere (to accomplish, effect).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktə / (BrE)
an organ or tissue, such as a muscle or gland, that responds to a nerve impulse by carrying out an action
organ that responds to nerve signals
medical

Examples

When a person touches a hot stove, the muscle effector quickly contracts to pull the hand away.
The salivary glands serve as effectors by releasing saliva in response to the smell of food.
A simple reflex arc consists of a receptor, a sensory neuron, and an effector.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receptor - a cell or organ that receives stimuli and transmits a signal to the nervous system

Etymology

From Latin effectus (accomplished, performed), from efficere (to accomplish, effect).
