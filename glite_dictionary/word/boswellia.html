Boswellia

1. NOUN

/ bɑzˈwɛliə / (AmE), / bɒzˈwɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of trees native to North Africa and India, known for producing frankincense resin used in incense and perfumes.
genus of trees producing frankincense

Examples

Boswellia trees are valued for the aromatic resin they produce, commonly known as frankincense.
The medicinal properties of Boswellia extracts are being studied for use in arthritis treatment.
Some traditional incenses are made using resin harvested from Boswellia species.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist John Boswell (1710–1780).
