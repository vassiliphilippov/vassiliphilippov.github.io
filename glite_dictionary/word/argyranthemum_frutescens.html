Argyranthemum frutescens

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.dʒɪˌræŋ.θə.məm fruːˈtɛs.ənz / (AmE), / ɑː.dʒɪˌræn.θɪ.məm fruːˈtes.enz / (BrE)
a perennial subshrub native to the Canary Islands, with pale yellow, daisy-like flowers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
ornamental Canary Islands shrub with daisy-like flowers

Examples

Argyranthemum frutescens is a favorite among gardeners for its long-lasting blooms.
Many public parks feature Argyranthemum frutescens in their flowerbeds for a splash of color.
The pale yellow flowers of Argyranthemum frutescens brighten gardens from spring through autumn.

Etymology

From Greek argyros (silver) and anthemon (flower), with Latin frutescens (becoming shrubby).
