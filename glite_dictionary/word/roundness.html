roundness

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being curved or circular rather than angular or straight
the quality of being round; curved shape

Examples

The roundness of the moon was clearly visible in the night sky.
Designers admired the roundness of the sculpture's edges, which lacked any sharp angles.
Measuring the roundness of the wheel ensured it would roll smoothly on the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvature - the amount by which something is curved
  • Antonym: angularity - the quality of having angles or sharp corners

Etymology

From 'round' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being round.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being round in shape, especially referring to the fullness or smooth curves of a body or object
the quality of being round or rounded

Examples

The roundness of the sculpture made it pleasing to the eye.
He admired the natural roundness of her cheeks.
The artist emphasized the roundness of the fruit in his painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curviness - having many curves or rounded shapes, often used about the body
  • Antonym: flatness - the quality of being flat rather than curved or rounded

Etymology

From Middle English 'roundnesse', from 'round' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
the characteristic of being a round number, especially one that is whole or approximate rather than exact
the quality of being a round number

Examples

The roundness of figures like 100 or 1,000 makes them easier to remember.
She appreciated the roundness of the estimate, even though it lacked precision.
The roundness of the price was convenient for quick calculations.

Synset Examples

the quality of a tone of voice that is full, rich, and smooth

Examples

A singer's roundness in tone can make a performance truly memorable.
