meteoric

1. ADJ

/ ˌmiːtiˈɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːtiˈɒrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, consisting of, or produced by meteors or meteoroids.
of or relating to meteors or meteoroids

Examples

The meteoric debris burned brightly as it entered Earth's atmosphere.
Scientists studied the meteoric particles collected from the upper atmosphere.
A meteoric shower illuminated the night sky with countless streaks of light.

Synset Examples

meteoric shower
meteoric impacts

Etymology

From Greek meteōrikos (high in the air), from meteōros (raised, lofty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02876597-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌmiːtiˈɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːtiˈɒrɪk / (BrE)
(of development, success, etc.) Very rapid or brilliant, but likely to be short-lived, like a meteor.
very rapid and brilliant, but short-lived

Examples

Thanks to social media, her meteoric ascent in the modeling world surprised everyone.
The company experienced a meteoric growth in profits during its first year.
His meteoric career unfortunately faded as quickly as it began.

Synset Examples

a meteoric rise to fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapid - happening very quickly
  • Antonym: slow - not quick; taking a long time

Etymology

From meteor + -ic, relating to meteors (from Greek meteoros, 'high in the air').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00982425-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌmiːtiˈɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːtiˈɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to atmospheric phenomena, especially weather and weather conditions.
relating to weather or atmospheric phenomena

Examples

The scientist studied meteoric disturbances to predict potential storms.
Accurate forecasts depend on monitoring meteoric data from satellites and ground stations.
The textbook covers various meteoric phenomena, including rainfall patterns and cloud formations.

Synset Examples

meteorological factors
meteorological chart
meteoric (or meteorological) phenomena

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteorological - relating to the science of weather and atmospheric phenomena

Etymology

From Greek meteōrikos (high in the air), related to meteoros (raised from the ground).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02876761-a
Synset POS: a
