Tragulus kanchil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræɡjʊləs ˈkænʧɪl / (AmE), / ˈtræɡjʊləs ˈkænʧɪl / (BrE)
A small species of chevrotain, also known as the lesser mouse-deer, native to southeastern Asia.
lesser mouse-deer of SE Asia

Examples

The Tragulus kanchil is the smallest hoofed mammal in the world.
Researchers observed a Tragulus kanchil foraging quietly in the forest undergrowth.
A Tragulus kanchil can easily slip through dense vegetation thanks to its tiny size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chevrotain - small, deer-like hoofed mammal, also called mouse-deer

Etymology

From Latin 'Tragulus' (a genus of chevrotains) and 'kanchil', Malay name for the animal.
