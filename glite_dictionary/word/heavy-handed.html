heavy-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛvi ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛvi ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
clumsy or lacking skill, especially in using the hands or performing physical tasks
clumsy; lacking manual skill

Examples

The heavy-handed apprentice dropped several tools during the repair job.
Her heavy-handed approach to painting left smudges all over the canvas.
Despite his heavy-handed efforts, the delicate vase slipped and shattered.

Synset Examples

a bumbling mechanic
a bungling performance
ham-handed governmental interference
could scarcely empty a scuttle of ashes, so handless was the poor creature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things
  • Antonym: deft - skillful and quick in movement

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɛviˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɛviˈhændɪd / (BrE)
using unnecessary force, harshness, or authority in handling situations or people
unnecessarily forceful or domineering

Examples

The police response to the protest was criticized as heavy-handed by many observers.
Her heavy-handed management style discouraged employees from sharing their ideas.
Many argue that censorship laws are too heavy-handed and restrict freedom of expression.

Synset Examples

incensed at the government's heavy-handed economic policies
a manager who rode roughshod over all opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overbearing - unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering
  • Antonym: lenient - more merciful or tolerant than expected

Synset POS: s
