Indo-Germanic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndoʊ dʒɚˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ dʒɜːˈmanɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the Indo-European language family; often used in historical or linguistic contexts.
of the Indo-European language family

Examples

Early linguists used the term Indo-Germanic when classifying ancient European and Asian languages.
Some textbooks still reference the Indo-Germanic roots of modern languages such as English and Hindi.
The Indo-Germanic language family includes most languages spoken in Europe and parts of Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indo-European - of or relating to the Indo-European language family

Etymology

From German Indogermanisch, combining Indo- (India) and Germanic, reflecting early theories about the family's origins.
