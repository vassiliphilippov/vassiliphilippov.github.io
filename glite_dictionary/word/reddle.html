reddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛdəl / (BrE)
a red iron oxide powder used chiefly for marking sheep and in dyeing
red iron oxide powder for marking or dyeing

Examples

The shepherd marked his sheep with reddle to identify them in the hills.
Miners in the region used reddle as a cheap dye for fabrics.
A faint streak of reddle was visible on the old stone wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red ochre - a natural red earth containing iron oxide, used as a pigment

Etymology

From redd (to make red) + -le, influenced by dialectal forms.
