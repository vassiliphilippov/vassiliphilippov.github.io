supposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
An idea or statement that is believed to be true, although it may not be proved; an assumption or hypothesis.
an assumption or hypothesis

Examples

Her entire argument is based on the supposition that the data is accurate.
It is mere supposition to say that he was involved in the crime.
Without evidence, any claim remains a supposition and not a fact.

Synset Examples

any society is built upon certain assumptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true without proof
  • Synonym: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence

Etymology

From Late Latin suppositionem (an assuming), from Latin supponere (to suppose).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of believing or assuming something is true without certain proof; an assumption or hypothesis
an assumption or hypothesis

Examples

His argument was based on the supposition that everyone agreed with his views.
The entire theory rests on a supposition that hasn't been proven yet.
Without evidence, her claim remains a mere supposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true without proof
  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or known

Etymology

From Late Latin suppositionem (assumption), from Latin supponere (to suppose).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsʌp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsʌp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
An idea or opinion that is formed without complete evidence or proof; an assumption.
an assumption or belief without full evidence

Examples

Her theory is based on pure supposition rather than solid evidence.
The detective dismissed his initial supposition after uncovering new facts.
Without proof, any accusation remains a supposition rather than a fact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - something accepted as true without proof
  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion based on incomplete information
  • Antonym: certainty - something known for sure or with complete confidence

Etymology

From Late Latin suppositionem (an assumption), from Latin supponere (to suppose).
