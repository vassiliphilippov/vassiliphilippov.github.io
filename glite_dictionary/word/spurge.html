spurge

1. NOUN

/ spɝdʒ / (AmE), / spɜːdʒ / (BrE)
any of numerous plants of the genus Euphorbia, typically having milky, often poisonous sap
plant of the genus Euphorbia

Examples

The garden was filled with different varieties of spurge, each displaying unique flowers and foliage.
Some species of spurge are considered weeds because they spread quickly and crowd out other plants.
Wearing gloves is recommended when handling spurge due to its irritating milky sap.

Etymology

From Old French *espurge*, from Latin *epurgea* (a kind of spurge), likely from Greek *euphorbion* (the plant name).
