paretic

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
a person suffering from paresis (partial paralysis)
person with partial paralysis
medical

Examples

The clinic specializes in rehabilitation for paretics and other neurological patients.
Many paretic individuals show improvement with dedicated physical therapy.
Support groups for paretics can provide emotional and practical assistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paresis - partial paralysis, weakness of voluntary movement

Etymology

From Greek parētikos (relating to paralysis), from parēinai (to let go, relax).
