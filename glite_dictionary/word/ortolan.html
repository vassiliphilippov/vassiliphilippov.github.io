ortolan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrtələn / (AmE), / ˈɔːtələn / (BrE)
a small brownish Old World bunting (Emberiza hortulana) often eaten as a delicacy, especially in France
small songbird eaten as a delicacy

Examples

The chef prepared the ortolan in the traditional French style, which is both controversial and rare.
In some countries, catching and eating the ortolan is now strictly forbidden by law.
The tiny ortolan hid among the tall grasses, blending in with its brownish feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a small songbird of the family Emberizidae

Etymology

From French 'ortolan', from Italian 'ortolano', from Latin 'hortulanus' (gardener, of a garden), originally referring to the bird's fondness for garden habitats.
