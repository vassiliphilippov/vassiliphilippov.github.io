digitoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a drug derived from foxglove plants, used especially to treat heart failure or certain arrhythmias
drug for heart failure or arrhythmia
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed digitoxin for her persistent heart arrhythmia.
Patients treated with digitoxin require close monitoring for side effects.
Because of its narrow therapeutic range, digitoxin dosages must be carefully controlled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitalis - another drug from foxglove plants for heart conditions
  • Synonym: digoxin - a related drug from digitalis plants, also for heart conditions

Etymology

From 'digitalis' (the plant genus) + 'toxin', referring to its origin and properties.
