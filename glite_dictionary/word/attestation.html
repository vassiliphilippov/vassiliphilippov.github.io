attestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæt̬.əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæt.əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bearing witness or providing evidence of something, often in a formal or official context
the act of bearing witness

Examples

The contract required the attestation of two witnesses before it could be finalized.
His signature provided official attestation to the accuracy of the report.
Historical documents often include an attestation to confirm their authenticity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - a formal written or spoken statement, especially in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin attestatio, from attestari (to bear witness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæt̬.əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæt.əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
evidence or proof confirming that something is true or has occurred, especially in a formal or official context.
evidence or proof that something is attested

Examples

The certificate serves as an attestation of your membership in the organization.
His signature on the document is an attestation to its authenticity.
The court required an attestation from a third-party witness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - formal statement or evidence given as proof

Etymology

From Latin attestatio (testimony, witness), from attestari (to bear witness).

Synset POS: n
