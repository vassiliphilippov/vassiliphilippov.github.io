nomadic

1. ADJ

/ noʊˈmædɪk / (AmE), / nəʊˈmædɪk / (BrE)
(of people or groups) moving from place to place rather than settling permanently in one location; migratory.
migratory; not settled in one place

Examples

The tribe remained nomadic for centuries, never settling in one region for too long.
As a nomadic worker, she travels to different cities every few months.
Their nomadic lifestyle allowed them to adapt to changing environments.

Synset Examples

a restless mobile society
the nomadic habits of the Bedouins
believed the profession of a peregrine typist would have a happy future
wandering tribes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sedentary - settled; living in one place

Etymology

From French 'nomade', from Greek 'nomados' (roaming, wandering).
