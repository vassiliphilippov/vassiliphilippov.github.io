Hemerocallis flava

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛm.ə.roʊˈkæl.ɪs ˈfleɪ.və / (AmE), / ˌhiː.mə.rəˈkæl.ɪs ˈfleɪ.və / (BrE)
A species of day lily with yellow flowers, often cultivated as a garden plant.
yellow-flowered day lily species

Examples

Many gardeners plant Hemerocallis flava to add bright yellow blooms to their flower beds.
The Hemerocallis flava blooms open in the morning and wither by nightfall.
Botanists admire Hemerocallis flava for its resilience and striking color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: day lily - any plant of the genus Hemerocallis

Etymology

From Greek *hēmera* (day) + *kallos* (beauty), the genus name Hemerocallis; species name *flava* is Latin for 'yellow'.
