Taxus baccata

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksəs bəˈkɑtə / (AmE), / ˈtaksəs bəˈkætə / (BrE)
the European yew, a long-lived, slow-growing conifer native to Europe and western Asia, known for its dense, dark-green foliage and toxic red berries
European yew tree species

Examples

The ancient churchyard was dominated by a massive Taxus baccata that had stood there for centuries.
Botanists are fascinated by the resilience and longevity of Taxus baccata in harsh European climates.
Many traditional longbows were crafted from the wood of Taxus baccata due to its strength and flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree or shrub with red berry-like fruits

Etymology

From Latin, where 'taxus' means yew and 'baccata' means berried.
