tedious

1. ADJ

/ ˈtiːdiəs / (AmE), / ˈtiːdiəs / (BrE)
So boring and monotonous as to cause mental weariness or fatigue.
boring, monotonous, wearisome

Examples

The long, tedious lecture made it hard for the students to stay awake.
Filling out all the paperwork can be a tedious process.
She found the repetitive task both tedious and exhausting.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; dull
  • Antonym: interesting - holding attention; arousing curiosity or interest
  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Synonym: tiresome - causing tiredness or boredom

Etymology

From Latin *taediosus* (wearisome, tedious), from *taedium* (weariness).
