savagely

1. ADV

/ ˈsævɪdʒli / (AmE), / ˈsavɪdʒli / (BrE)
In a fierce, violent, or brutal manner; like a wild animal.
in a fierce or violent manner

Examples

The storm savagely tore through the coastline, leaving devastation behind.
He replied savagely to the criticism, shocking everyone in the room.
The dog barked savagely at the stranger approaching the gate.

Synset Examples

she cried out savagely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutally - in a cruel or violent manner
  • Antonym: gently - in a gentle or mild manner

Etymology

From 'savage' + '-ly'; 'savage' from Old French 'sauvage', from Latin 'silvaticus' (wild).

2. ADV

/ ˈsævɪdʒli / (AmE), / ˈsavɪdʒli / (BrE)
In a violent, cruel, or fierce manner.
violently or fiercely

Examples

The animal attacked the intruder savagely when it felt threatened.
He was savagely criticized for his unpopular decision.
The storm ripped through the coastal village savagely, leaving destruction in its wake.

Synset Examples

he was viciously attacked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viciously - in a violent or cruel manner

Etymology

From 'savage' (from Old French 'sauvage', from Latin 'silvaticus', meaning 'of the woods' or 'wild') + '-ly'.
