Abies amabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz əˈmæb.ɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz əˈmæb.ɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of medium to tall fir tree native to western North America, with a conical crown, tiered branches, and leaves that smell of orange when crushed.
North American fir tree species

Examples

The forests in this region are dominated by Abies amabilis and other conifers.
Botanists have noted that Abies amabilis leaves emit a citrus-like scent when crushed.
The wood of Abies amabilis is commonly used in construction and paper production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - a type of evergreen tree in the genus Abies

Etymology

From Latin: 'Abies' (fir) and 'amabilis' (lovely).
