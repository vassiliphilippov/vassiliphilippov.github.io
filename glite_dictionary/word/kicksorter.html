kicksorter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkˌsɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈkɪkˌsɔːtə / (BrE)
a scientific instrument consisting of an electronic circuit that allows only voltage pulses of a specific height to pass
electronic circuit for filtering voltage pulses

Examples

The kicksorter was calibrated to allow only the strongest pulses to be measured.
A technician adjusted the kicksorter to filter out unwanted voltage spikes.
Researchers used a kicksorter in their experiment to analyze specific electrical pulses.
