Lower Normandy

1. PROPN

/ ˌloʊɚ ˈnɔrməndi / (AmE), / ˌləʊə ˈnɔːməndi / (BrE)
a former administrative region of northwest France, comprising the western part of Normandy, merged with Upper Normandy in 2016 to form the region Normandy.
former region of northwest France

Examples

Many tourists visit Lower Normandy for its picturesque coastal villages and rich history.
The dairy products from Lower Normandy are renowned throughout France.
After 2016, Lower Normandy became part of the unified Normandy region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Upper Normandy - the eastern counterpart of Lower Normandy, also merged into Normandy in 2016

Etymology

From the French 'Basse-Normandie', meaning 'Lower Normandy'.
