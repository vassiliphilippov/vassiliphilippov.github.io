lectureship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛktʃərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or post of a lecturer, especially at a college or university
position or post of a lecturer

Examples

She was awarded a lectureship in English literature at the university.
After finishing his doctorate, he applied for a lectureship in biology.
The university announced several new lectureships to expand their academic staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecturer - someone who gives lectures, especially at a university or college

Etymology

Formed from 'lecture' + 'ship' (suffix denoting state or condition).
