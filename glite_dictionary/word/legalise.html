to legalise

1. VERB

/ ˈliːɡəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something legal by passing a law or official decision.
make something legal
british

Examples

The government decided to legalise same-sex marriage after years of debate.
Many activists are campaigning to legalise cannabis for medical use.
If gambling were legalised, it could boost the economy.

Synset Examples

Marijuana should be legalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to legalize - make something legal (American spelling)
  • Antonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit something

Etymology

From French 'légaliser', from 'légal' (legal), ultimately from Latin 'legalis'.
