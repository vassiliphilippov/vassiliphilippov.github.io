Kant

1. PROPN

/ kænt / (AmE), / kant / (BrE)
Immanuel Kant (1724–1804), a German philosopher whose work in epistemology, ethics, and metaphysics had a profound effect on modern philosophy.
German idealist philosopher (1724–1804)

Examples

Many scholars consider Kant one of the greatest thinkers in Western philosophy.
Kant's Critique of Pure Reason revolutionized the field of epistemology.
The influence of Kant can still be seen in contemporary discussions of ethics and metaphysics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged in philosophy
