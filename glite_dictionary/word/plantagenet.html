Plantagenet

1. PROPN

/ plænˈtædʒənət / (AmE), / plænˈtadʒɪnɪt / (BrE)
the family name of a royal house of English kings who ruled from 1154 to 1485
royal house of English kings, 1154–1485

Examples

The Plantagenet dynasty produced some of England's most famous monarchs, including Richard the Lionheart.
Many wars and political changes occurred during the rule of the Plantagenet kings.
Shakespeare’s history plays often feature characters from the Plantagenet family.

Etymology

From Old French Plantagenet, possibly from Latin planta genista (sprig of broom), a badge worn by Geoffrey, Count of Anjou.
