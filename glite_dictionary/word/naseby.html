Naseby

1. PROPN

/ ˈneɪzbi / (AmE), / ˈneɪzbi / (BrE)
the site of a decisive 1645 battle during the first English Civil War, where Parliamentarian forces defeated the Royalists
site of a decisive 1645 English Civil War battle

Examples

Historians consider Naseby the turning point of the first English Civil War.
The outcome at Naseby marked the decline of the Royalist cause in England.
Many books detail the strategies used at the Battle of Naseby in 1645.

Etymology

Named after the village of Naseby in Northamptonshire, England, near where the battle was fought.
