pinole

1. NOUN

/ piˈnoʊleɪ / (AmE), / pɪˈnəʊleɪ / (BrE)
A traditional meal made from finely ground corn, often mixed with sugar and spices, commonly used in Latin American cuisine.
finely ground corn meal with sugar and spices

Examples

The runner fueled up on pinole before beginning the long trek.
Traditional Mexican recipes sometimes call for pinole as a key ingredient in desserts.
She enjoyed a warm bowl of pinole on a cold morning.

Etymology

From Spanish 'pinole', from Nahuatl 'pinolli' meaning ground, toasted corn.
