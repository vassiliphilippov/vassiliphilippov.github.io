verdigris

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdəˌɡris / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪˌɡriːs / (BrE)
a green or bluish deposit that forms on copper, brass, or bronze surfaces when they are exposed to air or moisture for extended periods
green patina on copper, brass, or bronze

Examples

Over many years, a layer of verdigris developed on the old copper statue.
The artisan used chemicals to quickly produce a verdigris effect on the bronze sculpture.
Rainwater exposure caused the brass handles to acquire a bright verdigris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patina - a green or brown film on the surface of metals from oxidation

Etymology

From Old French 'vertegrez', from 'vert-de-Grèce' meaning 'green of Greece'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdɪˌɡris / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪɡriːs / (BrE)
A blue or green powder used as a pigment, typically composed of copper salts formed by the action of acetic acid on copper, and historically used in paints and dyes.
blue-green pigment made from copper salts

Examples

The artist mixed verdigris into the paint to achieve a rich turquoise hue.
Copper roofs often develop a layer of verdigris after years of exposure to the elements.
In medieval manuscripts, verdigris was commonly used to create vibrant green colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patina - a green or brown film on the surface of bronze or similar metals, produced by oxidation

Etymology

From Old French 'verte-gres', from Latin 'viride aeris' (green of copper).
