facetious

1. ADJ

/ fəˈsiʃəs / (AmE), / fəˈsiːʃəs / (BrE)
treating serious issues with deliberately inappropriate humor; cleverly amusing or humorous in tone.
amusingly humorous or playful

Examples

His facetious comments lightened the mood during the tense meeting.
Sometimes a facetious tone can offend people if the topic is too serious.
She gave a facetious reply when asked about her age.

Synset Examples

a bantering tone
facetious remarks
tongue-in-cheek advice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witty - showing quick and inventive verbal humor
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or not joking

Etymology

From French 'facetieux', from 'facétie' (jest, witticism), from Latin 'facetia'.
