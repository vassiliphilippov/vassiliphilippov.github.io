fuse

1. VERB

/ fjuz / (AmE), / fjuːz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to become liquid or plastic through the application of heat.
become liquid or plastic when heated

Examples

At high temperatures, the metal will fuse and become part of the alloy.
Some ceramics fuse when exposed to extreme heat in the kiln.
The two compounds fuse at approximately 1200°C during the production process.

Synset Examples

The substances fused at a very high temperature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melt - to become liquid as a result of heating
  • Antonym: to solidify - to become solid or hard

Etymology

From Latin 'fundere' (to pour, melt).

2. NOUN

/ fjuz / (AmE), / fjuːz / (BrE)
a device consisting of a cord or tube filled with combustible material, used to ignite explosives or propellants
cord or tube for igniting explosives

Examples

The technician lit the fuse and quickly moved to a safe distance.
Make sure the fuse is securely attached to the firework before lighting it.
A slow-burning fuse was used to control the timing of the explosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detonator - a device used to set off an explosive

Etymology

From Latin 'fusus' (spindle); modern sense influenced by Italian 'fuso' (spindle) used for the shape of the cord.

5. NOUN

/ fjuːz / (AmE), / fjuːz / (BrE)
a safety device consisting of a wire or strip that melts and interrupts an electric circuit when the current becomes too strong
device interrupting electric current when overloaded

Examples

The electrician quickly replaced the blown fuse to restore power to the kitchen.
If you overload the socket, the fuse will melt and cut off the electricity.
Always use the correct rating for a fuse to prevent electrical hazards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuit breaker - a device for stopping the flow of current in an electric circuit as a safety measure

Etymology

From Latin 'fusus' (spindle), via French 'fusée' (spindle, fuse).

/ fjuz / (AmE), / fjuːz / (BrE)
to make or become liquid or plastic by applying heat, especially for the purpose of melting and joining together
make liquid or plastic by heating

Examples

They used a blowtorch to fuse the metal parts into one solid bar.
Certain types of plastic fuse when exposed to intense heat.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melt - to change from a solid to a liquid by heat
  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid
