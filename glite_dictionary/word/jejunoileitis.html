jejunoileitis

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˌdʒunoʊˌɪliˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / dʒɪˌdʒuːnəʊˌɪliˈaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the jejunum and the ileum, which are parts of the small intestine
inflammation of parts of the small intestine
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with jejunoileitis after experiencing severe abdominal pain and weight loss.
Crohn's disease can sometimes present as jejunoileitis in affected individuals.
Long-term jejunoileitis may lead to complications such as malabsorption and intestinal obstruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enteritis - inflammation of the intestine, especially the small intestine

Etymology

From Latin 'jejunum' (empty) + 'ileum' (intestine) + '-itis' (inflammation).
