sober

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.bə / (BrE)
Serious, sensible, and not playful or humorous.
serious; not playful

Examples

The teacher's sober manner made the children focus on their work.
Her speech had a sober tone, without any trace of humor or levity.
Despite the festive occasion, his sober expression never changed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serious - not lighthearted or playful
  • Antonym: humorous - funny or playful in spirit

Etymology

From Latin 'sober' via Old French 'sobre', meaning temperate, not intoxicated.

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊbər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbə / (BrE)
to make someone less drunk or less affected by strong feelings, especially by calming them or bringing them back to a sensible state
make less drunk or more sensible

Examples

Coffee helped sober him before his morning meeting.
She tried to sober her friend with some cold water after the party.
The news quickly sobered everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

A sobering thought

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to intoxicate - make someone drunk, opposite of sober (verb)

Etymology

From Latin sobrius (not drunk), by way of Old French sobre.

/ ˈsoʊbər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbə / (BrE)

not under the influence of alcohol or drugs

Examples

Related Terms

/ ˈsoʊbər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbə / (BrE)
(of a person or mood) to become more realistic, sensible, or serious, especially after being excited or unaware of reality.

Examples

The harsh criticism sobered him and made him reconsider his strategy.
We all sobered as we listened to the serious news.

Related Terms

From Latin sobrius (not drunk), later extended metaphorically to 'serious, sensible'.
