winter

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə / (BrE)
to spend the winter, typically in a particular place
spend the winter in a place

Examples

They chose to winter in Florida to avoid the harsh northern weather.
Many birds winter in warmer regions each year.
The expedition team had to winter at a remote research station in Antarctica.

Synset Examples

We wintered on the Riviera
Shackleton's men overwintered on Elephant Island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwinter - to spend or survive the winter, especially in a particular place

Etymology

From Old English 'winter' (winter, time of water), ultimately of Proto-Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə / (BrE)
the coldest season of the year, occurring between autumn and spring
the coldest season of the year

Examples

Many animals hibernate or migrate during winter to survive the cold weather.
We built a snowman after the first heavy snowfall of winter.
The days are much shorter in winter than in summer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: summer - the warmest season of the year
  • Antonym: spring - the season after winter and before summer
  • Antonym: autumn - the season between summer and winter

Etymology

Old English 'winter', of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'winter' and German 'Winter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15262579-n
Synset POS: n
