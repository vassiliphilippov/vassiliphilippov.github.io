Lucifer

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːsɪfər / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪfə / (BrE)
in Judeo-Christian and Islamic belief, the chief spirit of evil and adversary of God, also known as Satan or the Devil, regarded as the tempter of humankind and ruler of Hell
chief spirit of evil; adversary of God

Examples

Many religious texts describe Lucifer as the rebellious angel cast down from heaven.
In literature, Lucifer is often portrayed as a cunning tempter or fallen angel.
The figure of Lucifer has inspired countless interpretations in art and popular culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Satan - the chief evil spirit, figure representing the Devil
  • Antonym: God - the principal deity in monotheistic traditions; opposite of evil

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucifer' (morning star), from 'lux' (light) + 'ferre' (to bring), originally referring to the planet Venus as the dawn star.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːsɪfər / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪfə / (BrE)
the planet Venus when it appears in the sky just before sunrise; the morning star
Venus as the morning star

Examples

Ancient poets often referred to Venus as Lucifer, the bringer of light before dawn.
Just before sunrise, Lucifer shone brightly in the eastern sky.
The shepherds watched as Lucifer heralded the arrival of morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning star - the planet Venus when visible before sunrise
  • Antonym: Hesperus - the planet Venus when it appears in the evening

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucifer', meaning 'light-bringer', used for Venus as the morning star.
