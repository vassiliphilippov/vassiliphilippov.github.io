lucifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːsəfər / (AmE), / ˈljuːsɪfə / (BrE)
an old-fashioned type of match, consisting of a thin stick of wood or cardboard tipped with a combustible chemical, ignited by friction
archaic term for a type of match
archaic

Examples

He struck a lucifer and held it to the candlewick.
In Victorian times, people often carried a box of lucifers in their pockets.
She lit the fire with a lucifer she found in the old tin.

Synset Examples

he always carries matches to light his pipe
as long you've a lucifer to light your fag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - a small stick for making fire by striking; modern usage

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucifer' meaning 'light-bringer'; the name was applied to early friction matches in the 19th century.
