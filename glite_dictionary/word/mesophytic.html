mesophytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛsəˈfaɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːsəˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of plants or environments) adapted to or thriving in a moderately moist environment, between dry (xeric) and wet (hydric) conditions.
adapted to moderate moisture environments
medical

Examples

The forest was dominated by mesophytic species that required neither constant dryness nor excessive moisture.
Researchers studied how mesophytic conditions influenced plant diversity on the island.
Most lawns in temperate climates are maintained with mesophytic grasses that thrive with moderate watering.

Synset Examples

mesophytic habitats
mesophytic plants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xerophytic - adapted to dry or arid environments
  • Antonym: hydrophytic - adapted to very wet or aquatic environments

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'phyton' (plant), indicating intermediate moisture preference.
