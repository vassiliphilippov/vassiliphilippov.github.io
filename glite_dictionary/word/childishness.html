childishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being like a child, especially in showing immaturity or lack of sophistication
quality of being like a child

Examples

Her childishness often made her colleagues question her professionalism.
There is a certain childishness in the way he refuses to admit his mistakes.
Despite her age, a streak of childishness still appeared when she played with her grandchildren.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish - acting in a way like a child, especially immaturely
  • Antonym: maturity - the state of being fully developed or grown; responsibility

Etymology

From 'childish' + '-ness'; 'childish' from Old English 'cildisc'.
