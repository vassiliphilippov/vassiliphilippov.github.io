Gadiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡædəˈfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˌɡadɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of fishes that includes cods, haddocks, and grenadiers; in some classifications, considered equivalent to the order Anacanthini.
order of fishes including cods and haddocks

Examples

Fishes in the order Gadiformes are important for commercial fisheries worldwide.
The Gadiformes include familiar species such as cod and haddock.
Researchers studied the evolutionary relationships within Gadiformes using genetic analysis.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Gadus (cod) + -iformes (suffix meaning 'in the form of').

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡædəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌɡadɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of ray-finned fish including cods, haddocks, and related species
order of fishes including cod and haddock

Examples

The Gadiformes include commercially important fish such as cod and haddock.
Scientists study the Gadiformes to understand patterns of marine biodiversity.
Many species within the Gadiformes are valued in global fisheries.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Gadus' (cod) + '-iformes' (having the form of) used in zoological classification.
