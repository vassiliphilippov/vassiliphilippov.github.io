Euphorbia helioscopia

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔːrbiə ˌhiːliəˈskoʊpiə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˌhiːliəˈskəʊpiə / (BrE)
A species of spurge native to Europe and Asia, known for its small yellow-green flowers that turn toward the sun.
a species of spurge with sun-following flowers

Examples

The Euphorbia helioscopia blooms in early spring and can be found in fields and gardens.
Farmers often remove Euphorbia helioscopia because it spreads quickly among crops.
The small yellow flowers of Euphorbia helioscopia turn to face the sun throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - a plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Euphorbia' and species 'helioscopia', from Greek 'hēlios' (sun) and 'skopos' (watcher).
