lightheadedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪdnɪs / (BrE)
a feeling of dizziness or faintness, as if you might fall or lose balance
feeling dizzy or faint

Examples

She complained of lightheadedness after standing up too quickly from her chair.
The doctor asked if he had experienced any lightheadedness or loss of balance recently.
Dehydration can often lead to feelings of lightheadedness, especially in hot weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizziness - a sensation of spinning or losing one's balance
  • Synonym: faintness - a state of feeling weak or close to losing consciousness

Etymology

From 'lightheaded' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state of being lightheaded.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪdnɪs / (BrE)
A frivolous or careless lack of prudence or seriousness.
lack of prudence or seriousness
archaic

Examples

Her lightheadedness at the meeting shocked the more serious members of the board.
He regretted his lightheadedness only after the consequences became clear.
The novel portrays a society afflicted by lightheadedness and a lack of responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessness - a lack of concern for the consequences of actions
  • Antonym: prudence - the quality of being cautious and wise

Etymology

Formed from 'lightheaded' (carefree, thoughtless) + noun suffix '-ness'.
