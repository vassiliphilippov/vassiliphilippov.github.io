Ailurus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈlʊərʌs / (AmE), / aɪˈljʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals that includes the red panda, native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China.
genus of mammals including the red panda

Examples

The red panda belongs to the genus Ailurus, which is unique within its family.
Scientists have classified only one living species under Ailurus.
Modern zoology recognizes Ailurus as the sole genus of the family Ailuridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red panda - a small mammal of Asia with reddish-brown fur, member of genus Ailurus

Etymology

From Greek 'ailouros' (cat).

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈluːrəs / (AmE), / aɪˈljʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals consisting of the red panda, a small arboreal animal native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China.
genus containing the red panda

Examples

The red panda is the only living member of the genus Ailurus.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Ailurus to understand its unique adaptations.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitat of Ailurus in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red panda - a small mammal native to the Himalayas and southwestern China, member of Ailurus

Etymology

From Greek 'ailouros' (cat), referencing cat-like appearance.
