sceptered

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛp.tɚd / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.təd / (BrE)
endowed with legal power or sovereign authority, especially as symbolized by holding a scepter
invested with sovereign authority

Examples

In Shakespeare's play, England is described as a sceptered isle, highlighting its royal heritage.
The sceptered monarch presided over the ceremony with great dignity.
Her sceptered presence commanded respect from all in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sovereign - holding supreme power or authority

Etymology

From 'scepter', ultimately from Greek 'skēptron' (staff, scepter).
