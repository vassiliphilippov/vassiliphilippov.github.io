torpor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːpə / (BrE)
a state of physical or mental inactivity, often with a partial suspension of awareness or feeling
state of inactivity; mental or physical

Examples

After days of fever, he sank into a torpor from which it was difficult to rouse him.
The torpor of the hot summer afternoon made it hard to work.
Economic torpor gripped the region following the financial crisis.

Synset Examples

he fell into a deep torpor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vigor - active bodily or mental strength; energy

Etymology

From Latin torpor (numbness, sluggishness), from torpēre (to be numb or sluggish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrpər / (AmE), / ˈtɔːpə / (BrE)
a state of physical or mental inactivity, often marked by a lack of energy or enthusiasm
state of inactivity or sluggishness

Examples

After weeks of torpor, the city finally buzzed with activity when spring arrived.
The patient's torpor worried the doctors, as he showed no interest in his surroundings.
A sense of torpor settled over the office during the hot afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health

Etymology

From Latin torpor (numbness, sluggishness), from torpēre (to be numb or sluggish).
