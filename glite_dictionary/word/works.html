works

1. NOUN

/ wɝks / (AmE), / wɜːks / (BrE)
the internal parts of a device or machine, especially those that make it function
internal mechanism of a device

Examples

The watch stopped because dust got into its works.
She showed us the works of the old-fashioned typewriter.
A skilled technician can repair the works of almost any clock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanism - a system of parts working together in a machine
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04610198-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ wɝks / (AmE), / wɜːks / (BrE)
a place, often consisting of buildings and equipment, where industrial manufacturing or other large-scale operations are carried out
industrial buildings/facilities

Examples

The new steel works employs hundreds of local residents.
Many old textile works have been converted into modern offices.
They visited the water works to learn how the city’s water is purified.

Synset Examples

they built a large plant to manufacture automobiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factory - a building or buildings where goods are manufactured
  • Synonym: plant - an industrial site where manufacturing takes place
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ wɝks / (AmE), / wɜːks / (BrE)
actions performed as part of one's moral or religious duty, especially good deeds regarded as contributing to salvation or spiritual merit.
moral or religious acts; good deeds

Examples

The church teaches that works alone cannot bring salvation without faith.
She devoted her life to charitable works in her community.
Debates continue on the importance of faith versus works in religious doctrine.

Synset Examples

salvation by deeds
the reward for good works

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deeds - moral or religious actions; acts
  • Synonym: acts - actions carried out intentionally
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
