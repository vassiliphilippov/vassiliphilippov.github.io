pyrexia

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɛksiə / (AmE), / paɪˈrɛksiə / (BrE)
an abnormally high body temperature; fever, usually as a result of infection
medical term for fever
medical

Examples

The patient was admitted with a high pyrexia and signs of infection.
Persistent pyrexia may indicate an underlying medical condition requiring treatment.
Her pyrexia subsided after the antibiotics took effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - abnormally high body temperature; common symptom of illness

Etymology

From Greek 'pyretos' (fever), from 'pyr' (fire).
