distemper

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
A painting technique in which pigment is mixed with water and a binder, such as glue, and applied to a surface.
painting using pigment, water, and binder

Examples

The museum displayed an exquisite distemper of a pastoral scene from the 18th century.
Artists often preferred distemper in mural painting because it dried quickly and was less expensive than oil paint.
She restored a faded distemper by carefully cleaning the delicate surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempera - A painting technique using pigments and egg yolk as binder

Etymology

From Middle English distemperen, from Old French destemprer, from Latin distemperare (to disturb). The painting sense dates from the 16th century.

/ dɪsˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
a method of painting in which pigments are mixed with water and a binder, such as glue, used for painting posters, murals, or stage scenery
method of painting with pigments and binder

Examples

The artist used distemper to achieve a matte finish in the mural.
Traditional distemper requires careful mixing of pigments with glue as a binder.
Much of the stage scenery was painted using distemper techniques for quick drying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempera - a painting method where pigments are mixed with egg yolk or another binder

Etymology

From Middle French 'distemper' (to mix), from Latin 'distemperare' (to mix thoroughly).

5. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɛmpər / (AmE), / dɪˈstɛmpə / (BrE)
a type of paint made by mixing pigments with water and a binding agent, used especially for walls and early artworks
paint of pigment, water, and binder

Examples

The artist chose to use distemper for its matte finish and versatility on large wall surfaces.
Restoring the old mural required carefully matching the original distemper paint used centuries ago.
Unlike oil paint, distemper dries quickly and creates a softer effect on plastered walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempera - paint made by mixing pigments with egg yolk or another binder

Etymology

From Middle English distemperen (to mix or blend); from Old French destemprer, based on Latin dis- (apart) + temperare (to mix).
