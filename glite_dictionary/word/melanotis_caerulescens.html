Melanotis caerulescens

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnoʊtɪs ˌsɛəˌruːˈlɛskɛnz / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈnəʊtɪs ˌsɛəˌruːˈlɛskɛnz / (BrE)
A species of mockingbird native to Mexico, known for its striking blue plumage.
Mexican blue mockingbird species

Examples

The Melanotis caerulescens is easily identified by its vivid blue feathers and melodic calls.
Birdwatchers in Mexico often seek out the elusive Melanotis caerulescens for their life lists.
Habitat loss poses a threat to the Melanotis caerulescens population in its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockingbird - a bird known for its mimicry of sounds and songs

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) + 'otis' (ear); 'caerulescens' from Latin meaning 'bluish'.
