gushing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
flowing out rapidly and in large amounts; pouring forth with force
flowing out rapidly, in large amounts

Examples

A gushing stream ran through the valley after the heavy rain.
The gushing oil from the broken pipe caused an emergency shutdown.
He tried to stop the gushing water with his hands, but it was too powerful.

Synset Examples

a gushing hydrant
pouring flood waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pouring - flowing rapidly in a continuous stream
  • Degree Lower: trickling - flowing slowly in a small amount

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing feelings or praise in an excessively enthusiastic or effusive manner
excessively expressive or effusive

Examples

The critics found her acceptance speech far too gushing for their taste.
He wrote a gushing review of the artist's latest exhibition.
Her gushing compliments began to sound insincere after a while.

Synset Examples

insincere and effusive demonstrations of sentimental friendship
a large gushing female
write unrestrained and gushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effusive - expressing feelings in an unrestrained or heartfelt manner
  • Antonym: restrained - showing control over one's emotions or expressions

Etymology

From the verb 'gush', meaning to flow out quickly and with force, figuratively used for emotion since the late 18th century.

expressing feelings or praise in an unrestrained and enthusiastic way
expressing strong, unrestrained enthusiasm

Examples
