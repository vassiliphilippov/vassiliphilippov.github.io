Homaridae

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmærɪˌdiː / (AmE), / həʊˈmærɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large-clawed lobsters found in marine environments, including commercially important species such as the American and European lobsters.
family of large-clawed lobsters

Examples

Species within the Homaridae family are prized for their sweet, tender meat.
The Homaridae include the American lobster and the European lobster, both valued commercially.
Marine biologists study the Homaridae to understand their evolutionary adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and large claws

Etymology

From modern Latin Homaridae, based on Greek 'homaros' (lobster).
