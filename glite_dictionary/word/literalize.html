to literalize

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪt̬əˌrəlˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to interpret or express something in a literal manner; to make something literal rather than figurative or metaphorical
make or interpret literally

Examples

Some readers tend to literalize every detail in religious texts.
It's important not to literalize metaphors, or their meaning may be lost.
The teacher cautioned the class not to literalize the story's symbolism.

Synset Examples

literalize metaphors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
  • Antonym: figurative - departing from a literal use of words; metaphorical

Etymology

Formed from 'literal' + '-ize', following Latin root 'literalis' (of letters).
