Ziphiidae

1. NOUN

/ zɪˈfaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / zɪˈfaɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine mammals known as beaked whales, characterized by their elongated beaks and deep-diving habits.
family of beaked whales

Examples

The Ziphiidae are distinguished from other cetaceans by their elongated beaks and unique teeth.
Recent studies have improved our understanding of how Ziphiidae species dive and forage at great depths.
Unlike other whales, members of the Ziphiidae family are rarely seen at the ocean's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaked whale - any whale of the family Ziphiidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Ziphius + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
