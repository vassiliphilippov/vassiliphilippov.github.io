stumpknocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmpˌnɑkər / (AmE), / ˈstʌmpˌnɒkə / (BrE)
A small freshwater sunfish (Lepomis punctatus), native to streams in the southeastern United States, valued as a panfish.
a small freshwater sunfish (Lepomis punctatus)
american

Examples

We spent the afternoon fishing for stumpknocker in the creek behind the house.
The stumpknocker is a favorite catch among local anglers in Florida.
Grilled stumpknocker makes for a delicious southern meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted sunfish - another common name for Lepomis punctatus
  • Synonym: panfish - small fish commonly caught for food

Etymology

American regional English; possibly from 'stump' + 'knocker', referring to the fish's behavior around submerged tree stumps.
