to comprehend

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑːm.prəˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.prɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to include or contain something as part of a larger whole; to encompass within limits or scope
to include or encompass

Examples

The new legislation comprehends all previous amendments under one law.
The syllabus comprehends both theoretical and practical skills.
His responsibilities comprehend several different departments within the company.

Synset Examples

This group encompasses a wide range of people from different backgrounds
this should cover everyone in the group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encompass - to include comprehensively; cover or surround
  • Synonym: to include - to contain as part of the whole; comprise
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out; not include

Etymology

From Latin 'comprehendere' meaning 'to grasp, seize, include'.

2. VERB

/ ˌkɑːm.prəˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.prɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to understand or grasp the meaning or nature of something
understand; grasp the meaning

Examples

I could not comprehend why she was so upset about the news.
It took years for scientists to fully comprehend the implications of the discovery.
Do you comprehend the instructions, or should I explain them again?

Synset Examples

Do you comprehend the meaning of this letter?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to understand - to perceive or comprehend the meaning of something

Etymology

From Latin 'comprehendere' meaning 'to seize, grasp, understand'.
