dogtooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌtuθ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌtuːθ / (BrE)
A decorative, carved pyramid-shaped ornament often used in 13th-century English architecture, especially in moldings and arches.
pyramidal ornament in medieval architecture

Examples

The archway was adorned with a row of carved dogtooth ornaments.
Historians studied the intricate dogtooth pattern above the cathedral's entrance.
The restoration included replacing several missing dogtooth carvings from the original design.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌtuθ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌtuːθ / (BrE)
one of the four pointed conical teeth (two in each jaw) located between the incisors and the premolars; also called canine tooth
pointed tooth between incisors and premolars

Examples

The dentist explained that the dogtooth is important for tearing food.
After losing his dogtooth, the dog had trouble chewing meat.
Humans and many animals have a prominent dogtooth on each side of the mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine tooth - a pointed tooth between the incisors and premolars

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌtuθ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌtuːθ / (BrE)
A perennial woodland plant that flowers in spring, especially species of the genus Erythronium, widely cultivated for its attractive flowers.
a spring-flowering perennial woodland plant

Examples

The dogtooth blooms early in the spring, adding color to woodland gardens.
Gardeners often plant dogtooth for its elegant and nodding flowers.
Several species of dogtooth thrive in shaded, moist soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - another name for a plant of the genus Erythronium
