non pros

1. NOUN

/ nɑn ˈprɑs / (AmE), / nɒn ˈprɒs / (BrE)
a judgment entered in favor of the defendant when the plaintiff fails to pursue their case or appear in court
judgment for defendant if plaintiff does not continue case
legal

Examples

The court entered a non pros when the plaintiff failed to appear for the hearing.
After several delays, the defendant moved for a non pros due to lack of prosecution.
If you do not pursue your claim diligently, your case may be dismissed by non pros.

Etymology

Shortened from Latin 'non prosequitur', meaning 'he does not pursue'.
