Meitner

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪtnər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtnə / (BrE)
Lise Meitner (1878–1968), a Swedish physicist born in Austria who helped develop the concept of nuclear fission alongside Otto Hahn and Otto Frisch.
Austrian-Swedish physicist, co-discovered nuclear fission

Examples

Lise Meitner made significant contributions to understanding nuclear reactions.
Few people realize that Meitner was overlooked for the Nobel Prize despite her crucial work on nuclear fission.
The element meitnerium was named in honor of Meitner.
