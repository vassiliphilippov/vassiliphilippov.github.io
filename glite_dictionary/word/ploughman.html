ploughman

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊmən / (AmE), / ˈplaʊmən / (BrE)
a man who plows fields, especially by driving a plough drawn by horses, oxen, or other animals
man who plows fields

Examples

The ploughman guided his horses steadily across the field from dawn until dusk.
In old English poetry, the life of a humble ploughman is often portrayed as noble and honest.
The village held a festival in honor of the local ploughman and his hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - person who owns or manages a farm

Etymology

From Old English *plōhman*, meaning 'man who ploughs'.
