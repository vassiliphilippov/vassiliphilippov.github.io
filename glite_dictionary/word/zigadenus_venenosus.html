Zigadenus venenosus

1. NOUN

/ zɪɡə'diːnəs vəˌnɛnəˈsoʊs / (AmE), / zɪɡə'diːnəs vɪˌniːnəˈsəʊs / (BrE)
A poisonous plant native to western North America and Mexico, known to be especially toxic to grazing animals; also called meadow death camas.
poisonous North American plant; meadow death camas

Examples

Livestock ranchers are especially wary of Zigadenus venenosus growing in their pastures.
Accidental ingestion of Zigadenus venenosus can be fatal to sheep and cattle.
Researchers studied the distribution of Zigadenus venenosus across various habitats in the western United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death camas - any of several poisonous plants of western North America

Etymology

From the genus name Zigadenus, of Greek origin (zigos, 'yoke' + adenos, 'gland').
