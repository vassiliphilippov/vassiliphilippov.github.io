blinking

1. VERB

/ ˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
rapidly opening and closing the eyes repeatedly and quickly, usually involuntarily
rapidly closing and opening the eyes

Examples

The bright camera flash left her blinking in surprise.
He kept blinking as he adjusted to the dim room.
After staring at the screen too long, I start blinking more frequently.

Synset Examples

he stood blinking in the bright sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to close and open one’s eyes quickly

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the rapid closing and opening of the eyelids, usually as a reflex
rapid closing and opening of eyelids

Examples

Blinking helps to keep your eyes moist and free from dust.
Frequent blinking can sometimes indicate eye irritation or discomfort.
The doctor observed her blinking during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to close and open the eyes quickly

Etymology

From 'blink' (Middle English 'blenken') + -ing suffix.
