inconstant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
frequently changing, especially without reason or reliability; not steady or consistent
frequently changing; not steady

Examples

Her inconstant moods made her difficult to please.
The weather in spring is notoriously inconstant in this region.
He had an inconstant commitment to his studies, often losing focus.

Synset Examples

inconstant affections
an inconstant lover
swear not by … the inconstant moon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties or affections
  • Antonym: constant - remaining the same over time; steadfast

Etymology

From Latin inconstans (not firm, not steadfast), from in- (not) + constans (constant).
