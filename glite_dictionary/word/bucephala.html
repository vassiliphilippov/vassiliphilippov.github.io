Bucephala

1. NOUN

/ bjuːˈsɛfələ / (AmE), / bjuːˈsɛfələ / (BrE)
A genus of diving ducks that includes buffleheads and goldeneyes.
genus of diving ducks (buffleheads, goldeneyes)

Examples

The genus Bucephala includes several species of diving ducks found in North America and Eurasia.
Wildlife researchers observed a flock of Bucephala feeding on a northern lake.
Both the bufflehead and the common goldeneye belong to the Bucephala genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bufflehead - a small North American diving duck of the genus Bucephala
  • Synonym: goldeneye - a medium-sized diving duck of the genus Bucephala

Etymology

From Greek 'bous' (ox) + 'kephalē' (head), referencing the shape of the ducks' head.
