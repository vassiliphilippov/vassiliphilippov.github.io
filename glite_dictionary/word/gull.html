gull

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌl / (AmE), / ɡʌl / (BrE)
A medium to large, mainly white seabird with long pointed wings, short legs, and usually a harsh wailing call.
a type of seabird

Examples

A gull stole a sandwich from my hand by the beach.
We saw a flock of gulls circling the fishing boats in the harbor.
The gull soared effortlessly above the crashing waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seagull - another common term for a gull, a white seabird

Etymology

From Old Norse *gulr* via Middle English *gulle*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02043900-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌl / (AmE), / ɡʌl / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or tricked; someone who is gullible and easy to take advantage of
a gullible or easily deceived person

Examples

The clever con artist quickly identified a gull among the tourists.
Don't be such a gull; you can't believe everything you hear on the internet.
They took him for a gull and tricked him out of his savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • Synonym: victim - someone who is harmed or tricked
  • Antonym: trickster - a person who deceives or cheats others

Etymology

Perhaps from gull (verb), meaning 'to deceive', which may be of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09940867-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɡʌl / (AmE), / ɡʌl / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone; to fool or hoax
to deceive or trick

Examples

He tried to gull investors with a fake business scheme.
Tourists are often gulled by street vendors selling counterfeit goods.
She warned her friends not to be gulled by emails claiming they had won a lottery.

Synset Examples

The immigrant was duped because he trusted everyone
You can't fool me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick, especially by misleading
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone, often as a joke or for personal gain
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding, opposite of deceive

Etymology

Perhaps from a dialect variant of gull (n.), meaning 'simpleton'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00856586-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɡʌl / (AmE), / ɡʌl / (BrE)
to trick or deceive someone; to make a fool of.
to trick or deceive

Examples

She managed to gull him into believing her story.
Many investors were gulled by the scheme's promises of quick riches.
He was clever enough not to be gulled by their flattery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick, especially by gaining another's trust
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or outwit someone

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *gull* (a simpleton).
