despicableness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɪkəblnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪkəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being deserving of contempt or scorn; the state of being despicable.
quality of being despicable

Examples

The despicableness of his actions shocked even his oldest friends.
Her novel explores the despicableness lurking beneath a seemingly perfect community.
They could not overlook the sheer despicableness of the crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despicable - deserving contempt or scorn

Etymology

Formed from 'despicable' + '-ness', with 'despicable' from Latin 'despicari' (to look down on).
