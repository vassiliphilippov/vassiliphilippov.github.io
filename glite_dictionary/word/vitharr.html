Vitharr

1. PROPN

/ ˈviːθɑr / (AmE), / ˈviːθɑːr / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the Aesir; the son of Odin who avenges his father by slaying the wolf Fenrir at Ragnarok.
Norse god, son of Odin

Examples

In Norse mythology, Vitharr is best known for slaying the monstrous wolf Fenrir during Ragnarok.
Legends say that Vitharr will avenge Odin when he faces Fenrir in the final battle.
Many ancient sources describe Vitharr as the silent and steadfast son of Odin.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Víðarr', a name in Norse mythology.
