earthbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝːθˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
Confined to the earth; unable to fly or leave the ground.
confined to the earth

Examples

The earthbound creatures gazed up at the sky, longing for the freedom of flight.
Her earthbound concerns kept her too busy to dream of traveling to space.
The novel explores both earthbound realities and imaginative flights of fancy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: airborne - in the air; flying or carried in the air

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'bound', indicating being tied to the earth.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɜrθˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
lacking imagination or originality; mundane or unadventurous.
lacking imagination; mundane

Examples

His earthbound style of writing failed to capture the readers' imagination.
Many critics found the film's plot rather earthbound and predictable.
She longed for adventure, but her plans always remained decidedly earthbound.

Synset Examples

a pedestrian movie plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedestrian - lacking inspiration or excitement; dull
  • Antonym: imaginative - having a creative or inventive mind

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'bound', originally meaning 'restricted to the earth', later figurative.
