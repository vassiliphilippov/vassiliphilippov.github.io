slacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈslækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslakɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of avoiding work or responsibility; shirking one's duties
evasion of work or duty

Examples

The manager warned the team that slacking would not be tolerated during the busy season.
His repeated slacking nearly cost him his job at the factory.
Group projects suffer when one member keeps slacking instead of contributing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shirking - avoiding work or responsibility
  • Antonym: diligence - persistent work and effort

Etymology

From the verb 'slack', meaning to be negligent or lazy, itself from Middle English 'slak' (loose, negligent).
