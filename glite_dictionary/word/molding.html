molding

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
A preliminary sculpture in wax or clay from which a finished work can be cast or copied.
preliminary sculpture model

Examples

The artist created a molding in clay before casting the final statue in bronze.
Each molding serves as a prototype for the finished sculpture.
The delicate details were preserved in the molding, ensuring the final piece was accurate.

Etymology

From Old French *mouler* (to form), from Latin *modulus* (a small measure, model).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
A decorative strip, typically of wood, plaster, or other material, used for ornamentation or finishing on walls, ceilings, or furniture.
decorative strip for ornamentation or finishing

Examples

The crown molding in the living room adds a touch of elegance to the space.
He installed a decorative molding along the ceiling to hide the wiring.
The cabinet's intricate molding makes it stand out in the dining room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornice - a horizontal decorative molding at the top of a building or wall

Etymology

From Middle English moulding, related to 'mold' (form, shape).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
a decorative strip, usually of wood, plaster, or similar material, used to cover transitions between surfaces or for ornamentation on edges or walls
decorative strip on an edge or wall

Examples

The ornate molding along the ceiling gave the room an elegant touch.
They installed new molding around the windows to enhance the interior.
Centuries-old houses often feature intricate wood molding on their staircases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornice - a molded projection that crowns or finishes the part to which it is affixed
  • Synonym: trim - a finishing strip used to cover edges or joints

Etymology

From Latin 'modulus' (a small measure or model), via Old French 'modeler'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊl.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊl.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of forming a material or object by casting it in a mold
forming by casting in a mold

Examples

The factory specializes in the molding of plastic components for automotive parts.
Their company has revolutionized the molding of lightweight metal structures.
Advances in technology have made the molding process faster and more precise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casting - the act of shaping material by pouring it into a mold

Etymology

From the verb 'mold' + -ing; 'mold' from Old French 'modle', from Latin 'modulus' (measure, model).
