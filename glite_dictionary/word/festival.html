festival

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛstəvəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛstɪvəl / (BrE)
A day or period marked by gatherings, celebrations, and often feasting in honor of a particular event, occasion, or tradition.
celebratory day or period

Examples

The city hosts an annual festival to celebrate its rich cultural heritage.
During the summer, many tourists visit for the famous music festival.
Families gather together to enjoy food and games during the spring festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holiday - a day of festivity or recreation when no work is done

Etymology

From Latin 'festivus' (festive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛs.tə.vəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛs.tɪ.vəl / (BrE)
an organized series of acts and performances, often held in one place, usually to celebrate a particular art, theme, or occasion
organized performance event

Examples

Every summer, the city hosts a popular jazz festival that attracts visitors from around the world.
Tickets for the literary festival sold out within days of being announced.
The arts festival features theater, dance, and visual exhibitions throughout the week.

Synset Examples

a drama festival

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnival - a public festival or celebration, often involving music, dancing, and costumes
  • Synonym: concert - a musical performance given in public, often as part of a festival

Etymology

From Old French 'festival' (of a feast day), from Medieval Latin 'festivalis'.
