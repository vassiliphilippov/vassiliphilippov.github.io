accompanied

1. ADJ

/ əˈkʌmpənid / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpənid / (BrE)
performed, sung, or played with musical or vocal accompaniment
with musical or vocal accompaniment

Examples

The singer was accompanied by a full orchestra during the performance.
She prefers playing the violin accompanied by her brother on piano.
All choral pieces were accompanied last night, unlike the solos.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaccompanied - performed or sung without musical or vocal accompaniment

2. ADJ

/ əˈkʌmpənid / (AmE), / əˈkʌmp(ə)nid / (BrE)
Having one or more companions or an escort; not alone.
having companions or an escort

Examples

Guests are not allowed into the building unless they are accompanied by a resident.
The children were accompanied by their parents on the school trip.
She never walked home accompanied after dark for safety reasons.

Synset Examples

there were lone gentlemen and gentlemen accompanied by their wives

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaccompanied - having no companions or escort; alone

Etymology

From accompany + -ed, ultimately from Old French accompagner.
