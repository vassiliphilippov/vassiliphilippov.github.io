amnesia

1. NOUN

/ æmˈniːʒə / (AmE), / æmˈniːziə / (BrE)
A partial or total loss of memory, often resulting from injury, illness, or psychological trauma.
loss of memory
medical

Examples

After the accident, she suffered from amnesia and could not recall her own name.
The film explores a detective's struggle with amnesia after a traumatic event.
Doctors diagnosed him with retrograde amnesia, meaning he could not remember anything before the injury.

Synset Examples

he has a total blackout for events of the evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgetfulness - inability to remember things
  • Antonym: reminiscence - remembering and recalling the past

Etymology

From Greek amnēsia, from a- (not) + mnēsis (memory).
