to recommit

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to commit again, especially to send someone or something back to a previous place or state, often used in legal or procedural contexts.
commit again; send back

Examples

After reviewing new evidence, the judge decided to recommit the case to the jury.
The patient was recommitted to the rehabilitation facility for further treatment.
Parliament voted to recommit the bill for further consideration.

Synset Examples

It was recommitted into her custody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commit - to send someone to a place, especially for confinement or official care

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) + commit, from Latin committere (to bring together, entrust).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˌriːkəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to commit again, especially to repeat committing an offense or crime
commit again, repeat an offense

Examples

After serving his sentence, he vowed never to recommit the same crime.
Studies show that many offenders recommit similar offenses after being released.
If a parolee chooses to recommit a crime, the penalties can be much harsher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commit - to carry out or perpetrate (a crime, error, or immoral act)

Etymology

From re- (again) + commit, ultimately from Latin committere (to bring together, to entrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02589126-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send (a bill, proposal, etc.) back to a committee for further consideration
send back to a committee, esp. in legislation

Examples

If the committee raises new concerns, the chair may recommit the resolution for further debate.
After much discussion, the council decided to recommit the proposal to the finance committee.
The Senate voted to recommit the bill, delaying its passage until next session.

Synset Examples

The bill was recommitted three times in the House

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refer - send (a matter) to a committee or authority for decision

Etymology

From re- (again) + commit, following parliamentary usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01957050-v
Synset POS: v
