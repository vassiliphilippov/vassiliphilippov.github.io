shirttail

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɝːtˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːtˌteɪl / (BrE)
An additional short note or comment at the end of a newspaper article.
brief addendum to a news article

Examples

The editor added a shirttail to the story clarifying a minor detail.
Please check if there is a shirttail before sending the article to print.
Sometimes, a shirttail provides context that didn't fit in the main body of the piece.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɝːtˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːtˌteɪl / (BrE)
The lower, usually curved or pointed, part of a shirt that hangs below the waist and is often tucked into trousers or a skirt.
lower part of a shirt below the waist

Examples

His shirttail kept coming untucked during the meeting.
She noticed a stain near the bottom of her shirttail.
He tucked his shirttail neatly into his pants before leaving the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hem - the edge of a piece of cloth that is folded over and sewn

Etymology

Formed from 'shirt' + 'tail', referring to the resemblance to an animal's tail.
