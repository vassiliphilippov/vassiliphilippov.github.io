Tucson

1. PROPN

/ ˈtuːsɑːn / (AmE), / ˈtuːsɒn / (BrE)
A city in southeastern Arizona, US, known for its mild winters and proximity to mountain ranges.
city in Arizona, US
american

Examples

She moved to Tucson for its warm climate and beautiful desert scenery.
The University of Arizona is located in Tucson.
Every spring, tourists flock to Tucson to enjoy the vibrant wildflower displays.

Etymology

From Spanish, after the Tohono O'odham village name *Chuk Shon* meaning 'at the base of the black hill'.
