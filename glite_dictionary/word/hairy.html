hairy

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛri / (AmE), / ˈheəri / (BrE)
having a lot of hair on the body or surface; covered with hair.
covered with hair

Examples

The hairy caterpillar crawled slowly along the branch.
Despite the summer heat, he wore a sweater to hide his hairy arms.
Some plants have hairy leaves to help protect them from insects.

Synset Examples

Jacob was a hairy man
a hairy caterpillar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hairless - lacking hair; without any hair

Etymology

From hair + -y, originally Middle English hery.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛri / (AmE), / ˈheəri / (BrE)
informal; dangerous or frightening, especially in a way that causes anxiety or excitement.
dangerous or frightening (informal)
informal

Examples

We had a hairy drive down the icy mountain road last night.
It got pretty hairy when the wind picked up during our climb.
Some of the negotiations became hairy, but we managed to reach an agreement.

Synset Examples

hairy moments in the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scary - causing fear or alarm
  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or danger
