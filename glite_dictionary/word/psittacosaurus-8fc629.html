psittacosaurus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌtækəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / sɪˌtækəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a small, primitive genus of dinosaur from the Early Cretaceous period, distinguished by a beak-like jaw, long hind limbs, and a rudimentary frill, but lacking horns.
a primitive hornless dinosaur genus

Examples

The fossil of a psittacosaurus was discovered in Mongolia's Gobi Desert by paleontologists.
With its parrot-shaped beak, the psittacosaurus stands out among early ceratopsians.
Museum exhibits often display the psittacosaurus because of its unique frill and bipedal posture.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek psittakos (parrot) and sauros (lizard), referring to its parrot-like beak.
