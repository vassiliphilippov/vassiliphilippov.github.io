systema alimentarium

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈstɛm.ə ˌæl.ɪ.mɛnˈtɛr.i.əm / (AmE), / sɪˈstɛ.mə ˌæl.ɪ.mɛnˈtɛː.rɪ.əm / (BrE)
the system of organs responsible for the digestion and absorption of food in the body
digestive system; food absorption system
medical

Examples

The systema alimentarium includes the mouth, esophagus, stomach, intestines, and associated glands.
In medical terminology, the systema alimentarium is vital for nutrient processing and absorption.
Damage to the systema alimentarium can lead to serious digestive and metabolic issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digestive system - the organs responsible for digestion and absorption of food

Etymology

From Latin 'systema' (system) and 'alimentarium' (pertaining to food or nourishment).
