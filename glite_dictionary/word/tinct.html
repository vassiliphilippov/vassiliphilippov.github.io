to tinct

1. VERB

/ tɪŋkt / (AmE), / tɪŋkt / (BrE)
to color or tinge slightly; to give a slight tint to
color or tinge slightly

Examples

The artist chose to tinct the paper with a faint wash of blue.
Morning light would tinct the old stone walls with a rosy hue.
She liked to tinct her letters with a delicate shade of lavender ink.

Synset Examples

her greying hair was tinged blond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinge - to color slightly; to give a slight shade or trace of color

Etymology

From Latin tinctus, past participle of tingere (to dye, color).
