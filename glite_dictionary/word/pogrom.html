pogrom

1. NOUN

/ pəˈɡrɑm / (AmE), / ˈpɒɡrəm / (BrE)
An organized massacre or violent attack against a particular ethnic group, especially Jews, often sanctioned or condoned by authorities.
organized violent attack against an ethnic group

Examples

The history class discussed the tragic pogrom that devastated the Jewish community in the early 20th century.
Waves of violence and pogroms forced many families to flee their homeland for safety.
International outrage followed reports of a new pogrom against the minority population.

Etymology

From Russian 'pogrom' (devastation, destruction), from 'gromit'' (to destroy).
