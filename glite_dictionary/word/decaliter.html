decaliter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌlitər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəˌliːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to 10 liters.
metric unit equal to 10 liters

Examples

The recipe calls for one decaliter of water to be added to the mixture.
A single decaliter equals exactly ten liters, which makes conversions simple.
The tank has a capacity of three decaliters, making it ideal for larger experiments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - a metric unit of capacity equal to one cubic decimeter

Etymology

From French 'décalitre', from 'déca-' (ten) + 'litre' (liter).
