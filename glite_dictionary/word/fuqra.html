Fuqra

1. PROPN

/ fuːˈkrɑː / (AmE), / fuːˈkrɑː / (BrE)
A militant Islamic group founded in the 1980s, known for advocating violence to purify Islam and operating discreetly in North America and the Caribbean.
militant Islamic group (1980s)

Examples

The U.S. government launched an investigation into Fuqra's activities within the country.
Members of Fuqra have been linked to several plots targeting other Muslim communities.
Authorities believe Fuqra operates in isolated cells across North America.

Etymology

From Arabic 'fuqarāʾ' (plural of 'faqīr', meaning 'poor' or 'religious mendicant'); adopted as a group name.
