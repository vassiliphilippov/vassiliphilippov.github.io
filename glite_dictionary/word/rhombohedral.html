rhombohedral

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɒm.boʊˈhi.drəl / (AmE), / ˌrɒm.bəʊˈhiː.drəl / (BrE)
(of a crystal system) having threefold symmetry; relating to or shaped like a rhombohedron.
having threefold symmetry (crystals)

Examples

The mineral calcite commonly forms rhombohedral crystals, giving it a distinctive shape.
Quartz can crystallize in both hexagonal and rhombohedral forms depending on the environmental conditions.
Microscopic analysis revealed a rhombohedral structure in the newly synthesized compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigonal - relating to the crystal system characterized by threefold symmetry

Etymology

From rhombohedron + -al, meaning 'pertaining to a rhombohedron'.
