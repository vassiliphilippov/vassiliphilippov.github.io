ataxy

1. NOUN

/ əˈtæksi / (AmE), / əˈtæksi / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an inability to coordinate voluntary muscle movements, resulting in unsteady movements and a staggering gait.
inability to coordinate muscle movements (medical)
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with ataxy following several tests of balance and coordination.
Symptoms such as ataxy and difficulty walking suggested damage to the cerebellum.
Physical therapy was recommended to help reduce the effects of ataxy in her daily activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ataxia - loss of full control of bodily movements

Etymology

From Greek ataxia (disorder, lack of order).
