swath

1. NOUN

/ swɑːθ / (AmE), / swɒθ / (BrE)
A broad strip or path of land that has been cut, especially by a scythe or mowing machine.
a strip cut by a scythe or mower

Examples

The mower left a clean swath through the thick grass.
As he swung his scythe, a wide swath was carved into the wheat field.
The machine can cut a swath up to two meters wide in a single pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: row - a linear arrangement of cut plants or objects

Etymology

Old English *swæth* (track, trace), related to Swedish *svad* (swath).

2. NOUN

/ swɑːθ, swɑθ / (AmE), / swɒθ, swɔːθ / (BrE)
a strip or broad path cut or cleared, especially by a scythe or a mowing machine
a strip cut by mowing

Examples

The mower left a wide swath of grass across the field.
A tornado can cut a destructive swath through a town, leaving debris in its wake.
The hikers followed a narrow swath cleared through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - a long narrow piece of land or surface

Etymology

From Old English swæth (track, trace, footprint), related to 'swathe' (to wrap, bind).
