somber

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbə / (BrE)
serious, sad, or gloomy in character, mood, or appearance
serious or gloomy; not cheerful

Examples

The news cast a somber tone over the entire city.
She wore a somber dress to the memorial service.
The room felt somber after everyone had left.

Synset Examples

a suit of somber black
a somber mood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or causing sadness; downcast in mood
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From French sombre (dark, gloomy), from Old French sombres, from Latin subumbrāre (to shadow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːmbər / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbə / (BrE)
(of color or appearance) dark or dull in tone; lacking brightness or vividness.
dark or dull in color

Examples

The artist chose a somber palette for his painting, giving it a subdued atmosphere.
She wore a somber gray dress that matched the cloudy day.
Their living room was decorated in somber tones, with shades of brown and black throughout.

Synset Examples

drab faded curtains
sober Puritan grey
children in somber brown clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - dull, lacking brightness or color
  • Antonym: vivid - bright, intense in color

Etymology

From French sombre (dark, gloomy), from Latin subumbrare (to shade).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00406256-s
Synset POS: s
