sidestroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.strəʊk / (BrE)
a swimming stroke performed on one's side, in which the arms move alternately and the legs use a scissors kick
a swimming stroke performed on one's side

Examples

She used the sidestroke to cross the lake efficiently.
Children often learn the sidestroke as one of their first swimming techniques.
During the rescue, the lifeguard performed a sidestroke while towing the swimmer to safety.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: breaststroke - a swimming stroke in which both arms move symmetrically in a half-circle in front of the swimmer
  • Degree Higher: freestyle - a swimming stroke in which the swimmer may use any method, usually the front crawl, considered faster than sidestroke

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'stroke' (referring to the position and motion in swimming).
