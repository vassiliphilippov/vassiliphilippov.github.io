sinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnər / (AmE), / ˈsɪnə / (BrE)
a person who commits sins, especially viewed in a moral or religious context
person who commits sins

Examples

Many people believe that every sinner can seek forgiveness.
He was labeled a sinner by the strict members of the community.
Stories of redemption often focus on the journey of a sinner to find salvation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saint - a person regarded as holy or virtuous, often canonized
  • Synonym: wrongdoer - a person who does something illegal or immoral

Etymology

From Old English syngere (one who sins), from 'sin' + '-er'.
