retractile

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtræktaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈtræktaɪl / (BrE)
(of a part of an animal or plant) able to be drawn back or withdrawn into itself or into a sheath or cavity.
able to be drawn back

Examples

The retractile claws of a cat help it climb trees and catch prey.
Some species of turtle have a retractile head, which they pull into their shell for protection.
Unlike most dogs, felines have retractile claws that are hidden when not in use.

Synset Examples

cats have retractile claws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retractable - able to be drawn back or withdrawn
  • Antonym: protruding - sticking out from a surface

Etymology

From Latin retractilis, from retractus (drawn back).
