Palaemonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæliˈɛmənɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪˈiːmənɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized shrimp-like crustaceans, commonly known as prawns, found in both fresh and saltwater.
family of prawns (crustaceans)

Examples

Many species in the Palaemonidae family are vital for commercial prawn fisheries.
The scientist identified the shrimp as part of the Palaemonidae family due to its distinctive features.
Members of Palaemonidae can be found in a variety of aquatic habitats worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Palaemon, from Greek Palaimōn (a sea deity in mythology).
