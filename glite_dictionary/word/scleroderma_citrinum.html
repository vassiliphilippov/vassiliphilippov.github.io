Scleroderma citrinum

1. NOUN

/ sklɛr.əˈdɝː.mə sɪˈtraɪ.nəm / (AmE), / sklɪə.rəˈdɜː.mə sɪˈtrɪn.əm / (BrE)
A species of earthball fungus with a dingy brownish-yellow outer layer, a dark purplish interior, and a peridium covered with small warts.
a species of earthball fungus

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Scleroderma citrinum among the fallen leaves.
Researchers identified the fungus as Scleroderma citrinum due to its unique warty peridium.
You should not eat Scleroderma citrinum as it is considered inedible or mildly toxic.

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'derma' (skin), referring to the tough outer layer; 'citrinum' is Latin for 'lemon-colored.'
