Trichomanes

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkəˈmeɪniːz / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈmeɪniːz / (BrE)
A genus of delicate ferns, commonly called bristle ferns or kidney ferns, found chiefly in tropical and subtropical regions.
a genus of delicate bristle ferns

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species within the Trichomanes genus in a remote rainforest.
The delicate fronds of Trichomanes require a humid environment to thrive.
Many collectors prize Trichomanes for its unique lacy appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering plant with feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

From Greek *trikhos* (hair) and *manos* (thin, sparse), referring to the hair-like appearance of parts of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪk.oʊˈmeɪn.iːz / (AmE), / ˌtrɪk.əˈmeɪniːz / (BrE)
A genus of delicate ferns, commonly known as bristle ferns or kidney ferns, found mostly in humid, tropical regions.
genus of delicate bristle ferns

Examples

Some species in the Trichomanes genus are cultivated for their delicate appearance in terrariums.
The Trichomanes ferns thrive in the moist understory of tropical rainforests.
Researchers discovered a new Trichomanes species in the Andes mountains.

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) and 'manos' (thin, sparse).
