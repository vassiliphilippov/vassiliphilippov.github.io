rutted

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌtɪd / (AmE), / ˈrʌtɪd / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially a road or track) full of long, deep grooves made by the repeated passage of wheels.
full of ruts (grooves in a surface)

Examples

The old country lane was so rutted that driving became difficult after rain.
We cycled along a rutted path through the forest.
Heavy farm equipment had left the field rutted and uneven.

Synset Examples

rutty farm roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface; free from ruts or bumps

Etymology

From noun 'rut' (groove in a surface) + suffix '-ed'.
