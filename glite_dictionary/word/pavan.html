pavan

1. NOUN

/ pəˈvɑn / (AmE), / pəˈvɑːn / (BrE)
a slow, stately court dance popular in Europe during the 16th and 17th centuries
stately Renaissance court dance

Examples

The noble couples moved gracefully through the hall, performing the pavan.
The composer wrote a beautiful pavan to evoke the elegance of the Renaissance.
In the dance workshop, students learned the intricate steps of the pavan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pavane - an alternative spelling of the same stately Renaissance dance

Etymology

From Italian 'pavana', possibly named after the city of Padua (Italian: Padova).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈvɑn / (AmE), / pəˈvɑːn / (BrE)
A slow, stately piece of music composed for the pavane, a Renaissance dance.
Renaissance dance music

Examples

The orchestra performed a beautifully haunting pavan by a sixteenth-century composer.
Composers of the Renaissance often wrote a pavan to accompany social dances.
The elegant steps of the pavane were matched by the somber melody of the pavan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pavane - a slow processional dance of the Renaissance period

Etymology

From Italian 'pavana', from 'Pava' (Pavia), a city in Italy.
