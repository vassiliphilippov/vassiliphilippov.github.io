topography

1. NOUN

/ təˈpɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / təˈpɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the arrangement of the natural and artificial physical features of an area
physical features arrangement of an area

Examples

The mountain's topography makes hiking both challenging and rewarding.
Urban planners studied the topography before constructing new roads and buildings.
Satellite images reveal changes in the topography caused by erosion over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrain - the physical features of a tract of land

Etymology

From Greek topographia, from topos (place) + -graphia (writing, description).

2. NOUN

/ təˈpɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / təˈpɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the detailed study or description of the surface features and shape of an area or region, including its mountains, valleys, and other physical characteristics
study or description of land surface features

Examples

The topography of the island includes mountains, valleys, and numerous streams.
Satellite images help scientists understand the topography of remote regions.
Changes in river flow can significantly alter the topography over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrain - the physical features of a stretch of land
  • Synonym: relief - the variation in elevation and slope of the land surface

Etymology

From Greek 'topographia', from 'topos' (place) + 'graphia' (writing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06132185-n
Synset POS: n
