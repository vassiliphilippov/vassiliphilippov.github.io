Britishism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪ.t̬ɪʃ.ɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈbrɪ.tɪʃ.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or custom characteristic of British English or the United Kingdom.
word, phrase, or custom peculiar to Britain

Examples

The word 'lorry' is a classic Britishism not widely used in American English.
Many Americans enjoy adopting the occasional Britishism in their speech, such as 'cheers' or 'mate'.
He wrote an article about everyday Britishisms that confuse foreigners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Americanism - a word, phrase, or custom characteristic of American English or the United States

Etymology

From 'British' + '-ism', modeled after terms like 'Americanism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪt.ɪ.ʃɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.ɪ.ʃɪz.əm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or usage characteristic of British English, especially as distinct from American English.
term characteristic of British English

Examples

The word 'lorry' is a classic Britishism unfamiliar to most Americans.
Her novel is full of Britishisms, which add authenticity to the dialogue.
When writing for an American audience, he tries to avoid using too many Britishisms.

Etymology

From 'British' + '-ism', modeled after 'Anglicism'.
