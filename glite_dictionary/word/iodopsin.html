iodopsin

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.əˈdɑp.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əˈdɒp.sɪn / (BrE)
a violet photopigment found in the retinal cones of most vertebrates, important for daylight vision
a photopigment in retinal cones for daylight vision
medical

Examples

Researchers studied how iodopsin contributes to color vision in birds.
A deficiency of iodopsin could impair a person's ability to see in bright light.
Unlike rhodopsin, iodopsin is most active during daylight conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rhodopsin - a photopigment in retinal rods, responsible for night vision

Etymology

From Greek 'ioeides' (violet-colored) + 'ops' (eye) + '-in' (chemical suffix).
