mandibula

1. NOUN

/ mænˈdɪbjələ / (AmE), / mænˈdɪbjʊlə / (BrE)
The jaw in vertebrates that is hinged to open the mouth, especially referring to the lower jaw.
the hinged lower jaw in vertebrates
medical

Examples

In mammals, the mandibula forms the framework of the lower jaw and holds the lower teeth in place.
A fracture of the mandibula may affect a person's ability to chew and speak.
Birds have a unique mandibula structure compared to other vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'mandibula' (jawbone), from 'mandere' (to chew).
