line-shooter

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnˌʃuːtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnˌʃuːtə / (BrE)
a person who is excessively boastful and talks a lot, often making exaggerated claims
boastful, talkative person

Examples

Everyone at the meeting was rolling their eyes as the line-shooter told yet another exaggerated story.
You can't always believe what a line-shooter says about their accomplishments.
At parties, he's known as a real line-shooter, always boasting about himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braggart - a person who boasts a lot
  • Synonym: blowhard - someone who talks too much in a boastful way
  • Antonym: modest person - someone who is not boastful

Etymology

From 'line' in the sense of a story or tale, and 'shooter' meaning one who tells or delivers.
