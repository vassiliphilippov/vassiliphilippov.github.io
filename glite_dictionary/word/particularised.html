particularised

1. ADJ

/ pɚˈtɪkjə.lɚ.aɪzd / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊləraɪzd / (BrE)
made specific or directed toward a particular person, thing, or purpose; focused on details rather than the general case.
made specific; focused on details

Examples

The report provides a particularised analysis of the company's financial performance.
Legal arguments must often be supported by particularised evidence rather than general assertions.
Her particularised approach to the problem addressed every nuance in the data.

Synset Examples

particularized thinking as distinct from stereotyped sloganeering

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalized - made general or broad in application; not specific

Etymology

From 'particular', from Latin 'particularis', with the suffix '-ise' (to make) and '-ed' for past participle.
