parsimonious

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːrsəˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːsɪˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
unwilling to spend money or use resources; extremely frugal or stingy
extremely reluctant to spend money

Examples

Her parsimonious habits ensured she rarely spent money on anything unnecessary.
The company became parsimonious after the recession, strictly limiting new expenditures.
Although he was wealthy, his parsimonious nature made him unpopular with friends and family.

Synset Examples

parsimonious thrift relieved by few generous impulses
lived in a most penurious manner — denying himself every indulgence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingy - unwilling to spend money; mean
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give more than is necessary or expected
  • Degree Lower: frugal - sparing with money or resources

Etymology

From Latin 'parsimonia' meaning 'frugality, thrift'
