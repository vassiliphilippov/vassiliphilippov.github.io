precedential

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɛsɪˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːsɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
serving as or relating to a precedent; having precedence or authority based on earlier example or decision
serving as a precedent

Examples

The judge relied on precedential rulings to guide her decision.
They argued that the policy lacked any precedential support in case law.
Promotion in the company was based on precedential treatment of long-serving employees.

Synset Examples

precedential treatment for senior members of the firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precedent - an earlier event or decision serving as an example or rule

Etymology

From precedent + -ial, after Late Latin praecedentia (precedence).
