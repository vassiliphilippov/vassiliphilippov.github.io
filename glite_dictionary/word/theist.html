theist

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːɪst / (AmE), / ˈθiːɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in the existence of a god or gods, especially a creator who intervenes in the universe
person believing in a god or gods

Examples

She identifies as a theist and attends services regularly.
Many theists find comfort in prayer and religious rituals.
The debate between an atheist and a theist lasted for hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - a person who does not believe in the existence of a god or gods
  • Synonym: deist - a person who believes that God created the universe but does not intervene in it
  • Antonym: agnostic - a person who believes that it is impossible to know whether there is a god

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + -ist (a person who practices or is concerned with something).

2. ADJ

/ ˈθiɪst / (AmE), / ˈθiːɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially as part of a religious doctrine.
relating to belief in a god or gods

Examples

The debate was influenced by several theist perspectives on the origin of the universe.
Certain theist traditions emphasize the personal nature of the divine.
His theist arguments were met with thoughtful responses from both believers and skeptics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - not believing in the existence of any god or gods
  • Synonym: deist - believing in a god who does not intervene in the universe

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + -ist (denoting an adherent of a system).
