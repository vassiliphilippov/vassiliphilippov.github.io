Logania

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈɡeɪniə / (AmE), / ləʊˈɡeɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of Australian and New Zealand shrubs in the family Loganiaceae, sometimes grown for their flowers
genus of shrubs in Loganiaceae family

Examples

Several species of Logania are popular in native Australian gardens because of their delicate white blooms.
The botanist identified the unusual shrub as belonging to the genus Logania.
Logania can be found in various regions throughout Australia and New Zealand.

Etymology

Named after James Logan (1674–1751), Scottish colonial statesman and botanist.
