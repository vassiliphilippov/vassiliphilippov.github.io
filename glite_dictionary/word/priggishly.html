priggishly

1. ADV

/ ˈprɪɡɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈprɪɡɪʃli / (BrE)
In a self-righteously moral or proper manner, in the style of a prig.
in a priggish or self-righteous manner

Examples

She priggishly corrected everyone's table manners at the dinner party.
The new manager priggishly enforced every rule, no matter how minor.
He priggishly disapproved of their casual approach to dress code.

Synset Examples

this professor acts so priggishly — like a moderator with a gavel!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-righteously - in a way that shows an attitude of moral superiority

Etymology

Formed from 'priggish' + '-ly'. 'Priggish' from 'prig' (a self-righteous person).
