psychedelic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (BrE)
(of a mental state) characterized by intense and distorted perceptions, hallucinations, and feelings of euphoria or sometimes despair.
characterized by vivid, hallucinatory mental states

Examples

He described his first psychedelic experience as both overwhelming and enlightening.
The vivid, swirling patterns recalled a psychedelic dream.
After taking the medication, she entered a deeply psychedelic state filled with strange visions.

Synset Examples

a psychedelic experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinatory - relating to or causing hallucinations
  • Antonym: lucid - clear-thinking; having a clear mind

Etymology

From Greek psykhe (mind) and delos (manifest), coined in the 1950s to describe hallucinogenic experiences.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (BrE)
(of art, design, etc.) having very bright colors and strange patterns typical of the visual effects experienced during a psychedelic state.
showing vivid colors and bizarre patterns

Examples

The artist used psychedelic colors to create a sense of movement and energy.
She wore a psychedelic dress with swirling neon patterns to the party.
The walls were decorated with psychedelic posters from the 1960s.

Synset Examples

a psychedelic painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trippy - resembling the effects of psychedelic drugs, especially in colors and imagery
  • Synonym: hallucinogenic - causing hallucinations or relating to hallucinations, especially through drug use

Etymology

Coined in the 1950s from Greek psyche (mind) and dēlos (manifest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00404896-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdɛlɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug, experience, or artwork) producing vivid and distorted sensory perceptions, feelings, or altered states of consciousness.
producing altered sensory perceptions

Examples

The artist's latest exhibition was filled with psychedelic colors and swirling shapes.
Some people report profound experiences after taking psychedelic substances like LSD or psilocybin.
The concert featured a psychedelic light show that mesmerized the audience.

Synset Examples

psychedelic drugs like psilocybin and mescaline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogenic - (of a drug) causing hallucinations

Etymology

From Greek *psychē* (mind) + *dēlos* (manifest, visible), coined in the 1950s to refer to mind-expanding drugs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01782367-s
Synset POS: s
