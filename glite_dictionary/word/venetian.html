Venetian

1. ADJ

/ vəˈniʃən / (AmE), / vəˈniːʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Venice, its people, or its culture; characteristic of Venice.
relating to Venice or its people

Examples

The art in the gallery includes several exquisite Venetian paintings from the Renaissance era.
We enjoyed a romantic evening along the Venetian canals.
The Venetian glass chandelier was the centerpiece of the dining room.

Synset Examples

Venetian glass
Venetian canals

Etymology

From Italian Veneziano (of Venice), from Venezia (Venice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02982365-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ vəˈniːʒən / (AmE), / vəˈniːʃən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Venice, Italy.
resident of Venice, Italy

Examples

As a proud Venetian, she loves telling visitors about her city's history.
Many Venetians rely on boats for their daily commutes.
The Venetian greeted us warmly as we arrived at the bustling marketplace.

Etymology

From Latin Venetia (Venice) + English suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09736850-n
Synset POS: n
