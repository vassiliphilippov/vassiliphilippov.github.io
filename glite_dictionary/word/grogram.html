grogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːɡrəm / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒɡrəm / (BrE)
a coarse fabric made of silk mixed with wool or mohair, often stiffened with gum for added rigidity
a stiff, coarse fabric of silk and wool or mohair

Examples

The admiral wore a coat made of grogram during the voyage.
In the eighteenth century, grogram was commonly used for both men's and women's outerwear.
She admired the unique texture of the vintage grogram dress.

Etymology

From French 'gros-grain' (coarse grain), referring to the texture of the fabric.
