Salvinia auriculata

1. NOUN

/ sælˈvɪniə ɔːˌrɪkjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / sælˈvɪniə ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of free-floating aquatic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas, often found in ponds and slow-moving waters.
species of floating aquatic fern

Examples

Salvinia auriculata can quickly cover the surface of a pond, blocking sunlight for other plants.
Gardeners often struggle to control the spread of Salvinia auriculata in backyard water features.
The dense mats formed by Salvinia auriculata provide shelter for small aquatic animals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Salvinia - a genus of free-floating aquatic ferns

Etymology

From the genus name Salvinia (named after Italian scientist Antonio Salvini) and Latin 'auriculata' meaning 'eared' or 'with small ears'.
