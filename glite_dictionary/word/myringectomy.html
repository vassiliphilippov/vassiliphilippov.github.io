myringectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪrɪŋˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌmɪrɪŋˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of the eardrum
removal of the eardrum (surgical)
medical

Examples

The patient required a myringectomy to treat chronic ear infections.
Following his myringectomy, he experienced temporary hearing loss in the affected ear.
A myringectomy may be performed to access the middle ear for further surgical procedures.

Etymology

From Latin 'myringa' (eardrum) + Greek '-ektomia' (excision, removal).
