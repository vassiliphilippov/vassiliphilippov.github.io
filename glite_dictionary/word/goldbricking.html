goldbricking

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌbrɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌbrɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of avoiding work or duty, especially while pretending to be busy
evasion of work or duty
informal

Examples

The manager warned that goldbricking would not be tolerated in the office.
There are new software tools designed to detect goldbricking among remote workers.
His constant goldbricking frustrated coworkers who had to pick up the slack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shirking - avoiding assigned work or responsibilities
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent effort or work

Etymology

From 'goldbrick' (a person or thing that appears valuable but is actually worthless), originally from the practice of coating bricks with gold to swindle buyers.
