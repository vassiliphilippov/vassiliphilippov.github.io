plaguily

1. ADV

/ ˈpleɪɡɪli / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡɪli / (BrE)
In an annoying, troublesome, or pernicious manner.
in an annoying or troublesome way
archaic

Examples

The weather turned plaguily damp as evening approached.
He remained plaguily persistent in his questioning.
That mosquito buzzed plaguily around my ear all night.

Synset Examples

it's so plaguey cold!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaguey - annoying or troublesome; vexatious
  • Synonym: annoyingly - in a manner that causes annoyance

Etymology

From 'plaguey' (troublesome, annoying) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
