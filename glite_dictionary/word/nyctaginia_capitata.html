Nyctaginia capitata

1. NOUN

/ nɪk.təˈdʒɪ.ni.ə kəˈpɪ.tə.tə / (AmE), / nɪk.təˈdʒɪ.ni.ə kəˈpɪ.tə.tə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with sticky stems, tuberous roots, and deep red flowers, belonging to the genus Nyctaginia.
species of perennial plant with red flowers

Examples

Nyctaginia capitata produces vivid red flowers that bloom in the heat of summer.
The roots of Nyctaginia capitata are tuberous, helping the plant survive in arid regions.
Botanists documented a new population of Nyctaginia capitata near the border of Texas and Mexico.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Nyctaginia' (from Greek 'nyx, nyktos' meaning 'night') and the species epithet 'capitata' (Latin, meaning 'having a head'), referring to the clustered flowers.
