to soften

1. VERB

/ ˈsɔfən / (AmE), / ˈsɒfən / (BrE)
to make something less severe, harsh, or strict
make less severe or harsh

Examples

The teacher tried to soften her criticism to avoid discouraging the student.
His angry expression softened when he heard the explanation.
The company decided to soften its policy after receiving feedback.

Synset Examples

He moderated his tone when the students burst out in tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - make less extreme, severe, or violent
  • Antonym: to harden - make or become more severe or harsh

Etymology

From Middle English *softnen*, from *soft* + *-en*.
