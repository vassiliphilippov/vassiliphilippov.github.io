Seljuk

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlˌdʒuk / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.dʒʊk / (BrE)
a member of any of several Turkish dynasties that ruled parts of Central Asia and the Middle East from the 11th to the 13th centuries, notable for their conquests and defense against the Crusaders
member of medieval Turkish dynasty

Examples

The Seljuks established a powerful empire across Persia and Anatolia.
Historians often study the art and architecture of the Seljuk period.
The rise of the Seljuks marked a turning point in medieval Middle Eastern history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ottoman - Member of the Turkish dynasty that succeeded the Seljuks in ruling much of the Middle East

Etymology

From Turkish Selçuk, the name of the dynasty's founder.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛlˌdʒuk / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.dʒuːk / (BrE)
relating to the Seljuks, a Turkish dynasty that ruled parts of Central Asia and the Middle East from the 11th to 13th centuries
relating to the Seljuk dynasty or people

Examples

The ancient city contains many Seljuk architectural features that attract visitors from around the world.
She specialized in studying Seljuk pottery for her thesis on medieval Islamic art.
Several Seljuk monuments in Anatolia are designated as UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ottoman - relating to the Ottoman Empire or its people

Etymology

From Turkish Selçuk, the name of the dynasty's founder.
