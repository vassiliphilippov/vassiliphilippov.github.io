Bennettitis

1. NOUN

/ bəˌnɛˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / bəˌnɛˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct seed plants belonging to the order Bennettitales, known from the fossil record.
genus of extinct seed plants

Examples

Fossils of Bennettitis have been discovered in Jurassic rock formations.
The structure of Bennettitis leaves is similar to that of cycads.
Scientists believe Bennettitis played a significant role in ancient plant ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bennettitales - an extinct order of seed plants from the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From the name 'Bennett' (after British paleobotanist J.J. Bennett) + scientific suffix '-itis', used in botanical genus names.

2. NOUN

/ bɛˌnɛˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / bɛˌnɪˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct seed plants belonging to the order Bennettitales, known from fossil records.
genus of extinct seed plants

Examples

The fossilized specimens of Bennettitis provide insights into Jurassic-era plant life.
Scientists have debated the evolutionary relationship between Bennettitis and modern flowering plants.
Several species within the genus Bennettitis have been identified in Mesozoic deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bennettitales - an order of extinct seed plants to which Bennettitis belongs

Etymology

From Bennettitales, named in honor of British botanist John Joseph Bennett (1801–1876), with -itis as a taxonomic suffix.
