Micronesia

1. PROPN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈniː.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəˈniː.zi.ə / (BrE)
A region comprising numerous small islands in the northwestern part of Oceania, including the Caroline, Marshall, Mariana, and Gilbert Islands.
region of small islands in Oceania

Examples

The islands of Micronesia are known for their rich marine biodiversity.
He plans to travel throughout Micronesia to study its unique cultures.
Micronesia consists of thousands of small islands spread across the Pacific Ocean.

Etymology

From Greek mikros ('small') + nēsos ('island'), meaning 'small islands'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈniː.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈniː.zɪə / (BrE)
a country in the western Pacific Ocean, consisting of islands scattered over Micronesia, with a constitutional government in free association with the United States; independent since 1986.
country in the western Pacific Ocean

Examples

Micronesia consists of more than 600 islands spread across the Pacific Ocean.
The capital of Micronesia is Palikir, located on the island of Pohnpei.
After achieving independence in 1986, Micronesia established diplomatic relations with many countries.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'nēsos' (island), referring to the region of small islands.
