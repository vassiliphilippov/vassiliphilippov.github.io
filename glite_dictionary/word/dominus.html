dominus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.mɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a clergyman or minister in charge of a parish, especially in historical or ecclesiastical contexts
parish clergyman or minister (historical)

Examples

The new dominus was welcomed warmly by the villagers.
Records state that the dominus officiated at several important ceremonies during the 17th century.
As dominus, he took great care in attending to the spiritual needs of his parish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parson - a clergyman responsible for a parish
  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches

Etymology

From Latin 'dominus' meaning 'lord' or 'master'.
