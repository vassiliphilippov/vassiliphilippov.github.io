bloomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːmər / (AmE), / ˈbluːmə / (BrE)
a plant or flower that blooms at a particular time or in a particular manner
a flower or plant that blooms in a certain way

Examples

The cactus is a well-known bloomer in arid regions.
This variety is a late bloomer, showing its flowers in early autumn.
The garden features a collection of night bloomers that open after sunset.

Synset Examples

a night bloomer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - the reproductive structure of a flowering plant

Etymology

From 'bloom' (to produce flowers) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈbluːmə / (BrE)
an embarrassing or foolish mistake
an embarrassing mistake
british informal

Examples

I made a real bloomer at the meeting by forgetting my boss's name.
That typo was quite a bloomer in yesterday's newspaper.
He told a funny story about his biggest bloomer as a young politician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - a social or diplomatic error
  • Antonym: triumph - a great success or achievement (opposite of mistake)
