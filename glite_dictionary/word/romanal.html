Romanal

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.məˌnæl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.məˌnæl / (BrE)
a constructed international auxiliary language developed by Dr. Alfred Michaux in 1909
constructed international auxiliary language (1909)

Examples

Although Romanal never gained widespread use, it is studied by linguists interested in constructed languages.
The development of Romanal was inspired by the idea of promoting international communication.
A rare copy of the original Romanal grammar book was found in the library archives.

Etymology

Formed from 'Roman' plus ending '-al', coined by its creator Dr. Alfred Michaux.
