Gymnogyps

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑdʒɪps / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒdʒɪps / (BrE)
a genus of birds containing only the California condor.
genus containing the California condor

Examples

The Gymnogyps genus consists solely of the California condor, making it monotypic.
Recent conservation efforts focus on preserving Gymnogyps californianus in the wild.
Scientists are studying genetic diversity within the Gymnogyps population to aid in its recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California condor - a large vulture native to North America, the sole species in Gymnogyps

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'gyps' (vulture).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑːdʒɪps / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒdʒɪps / (BrE)
a genus of vultures containing only the California condor, a large New World vulture native to North America
genus of vulture (California condor)

Examples

The Gymnogyps genus currently includes only the endangered California condor.
Scientists study the Gymnogyps to understand the evolutionary history of New World vultures.
Conservation efforts have focused on the single surviving species within the Gymnogyps genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California condor - a large New World vulture species, the only member of Gymnogyps

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'gyps' (vulture), referring to the bare head of the bird.
