adequateness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.ɪ.kwət.nəs / (AmE), / ˈad.ɪ.kwət.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being adequate to fulfill a need or requirement satisfactorily
quality of being adequate

Examples

The adequateness of the shelter was questioned during the inspection.
He doubted the adequateness of his preparation for the exam.
The survey measured the adequateness of public services in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adequacy - the state or quality of being adequate
  • Antonym: inadequacy - the state of being insufficient or not enough

Etymology

From 'adequate' + '-ness', modeled on 'adequacy'. 'Adequate' from Latin 'adaequatus' (made equal to).
