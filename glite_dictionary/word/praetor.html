praetor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriːtər / (AmE), / ˈpriːtə / (BrE)
an annually elected magistrate of the ancient Roman Republic, ranking below a consul and mainly responsible for administration of justice
Roman magistrate; ancient Roman judge

Examples

The Roman praetor presided over legal disputes in the busy forum.
Julius Caesar served as a praetor before rising to higher office.
In 68 BCE, the praetor issued new laws to improve urban safety.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: consul - one of the two annually elected chief magistrates who jointly ruled the Roman Republic
  • Synonym: magistrate - a civil officer or judge with authority to administer the law

Etymology

From Latin praetor (leader, magistrate).
