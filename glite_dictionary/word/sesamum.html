Sesamum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛsəˌmʌm / (AmE), / ˈsiːsəməm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical African and Indian herbs, including sesame, known for their oil-rich seeds
genus of tropical herbs including sesame

Examples

Sesamum species are cultivated for their seeds, which are rich in oil and widely used in cooking.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of various Sesamum plants.
The most economically important member of the Sesamum genus is sesame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesame - an edible seed and plant of the species Sesamum indicum

Etymology

From Latin 'sesamum', from Greek 'sēsamon', referring to the sesame plant.
