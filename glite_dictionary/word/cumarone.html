cumarone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːməˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmərəʊn / (BrE)
A colorless, oily chemical compound obtained from coal tar, used in the production of synthetic resins.
chemical compound from coal tar, used in resins

Examples

The factory uses cumarone as a key ingredient in producing synthetic resins.
Researchers studied the reaction of cumarone with other compounds to create new materials.
Improper handling of cumarone can pose health risks due to its chemical properties.

Etymology

From cumaric acid and -one (chemical suffix).
