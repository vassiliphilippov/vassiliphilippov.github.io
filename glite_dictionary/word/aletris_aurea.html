Aletris aurea

1. NOUN

/ əˈliːtrɪs ɔːˈriːə / (AmE), / əˈliːtrɪs ɔːˈriːə / (BrE)
A species of colicroot native to the southeastern United States, with yellow-bracted racemose flowers, smaller than Aletris farinosa.
species of colicroot with yellow flowers (southeastern US)

Examples

The rare Aletris aurea blooms each spring in the pine forests of Alabama.
Botanists distinguish Aletris aurea from similar species by its yellow-bracted racemes.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Aletris aurea found in the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aletris farinosa - a related species of colicroot with white flowers

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Aletris' is the genus name and 'aurea' means 'golden', referring to its yellow flowers.
