radicchio

1. NOUN

/ rəˈdikioʊ / (AmE), / raˈdiːkioʊ / (BrE)
A variety of chicory with round heads of red leaves, often used in salads for its slightly bitter flavor.
variety of chicory with red leaves

Examples

The salad was topped with thinly sliced radicchio and a light vinaigrette.
Grilled radicchio adds a delicious smoky flavor to the dish.
She bought a head of radicchio for her Mediterranean recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - a plant with bitter leaves used in salads and as a coffee substitute

Etymology

From Italian 'radicchio'.
