dramatization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdræmətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdræmətʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of adapting a story, event, or real-life occurrence into a dramatic form, such as a play, film, or radio program
conversion into dramatic form

Examples

The dramatization of the novel was praised for its fidelity to the source material.
Many historical events receive a dramatization for television documentaries.
She is working on the dramatization of her life story for the stage.

Synset Examples

the play was a dramatization of a short story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of changing something to suit a new purpose, especially a story for a different medium
  • Antonym: original - something in its initial or unadapted form

Etymology

From Greek drama (action, play) via French dramatisation and English -ization.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdræmətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdræmətɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of adapting something into a dramatic form, or a work created in this way, such as a play or film based on a book or real events
a work presented as drama; adapting into drama

Examples

The BBC released a new dramatization of the classic novel to great acclaim.
Many historical events have inspired a dramatization for television or film.
The dramatization captured the emotions of the original story perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - a work adapted from another source

Etymology

From Greek drama (action, play) via French 'dramatisation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdræmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdræmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adapting a story or event into a dramatic form, such as a play, film, or television production
adaptation into dramatic form

Examples

The dramatization of the classic novel was well received by critics.
She watched a dramatization of the true story on television.
The museum featured a dramatization to bring the historical event to life.

Synset Examples

the play was a dramatization of a short story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - a version of a work adapted for a new form or medium

Etymology

From English 'drama' and '-ization', meaning 'to make into drama'.
