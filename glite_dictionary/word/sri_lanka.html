Sri Lanka

1. PROPN

/ ʃri ˈlɑːŋkə / (AmE), / ʃriː ˈlæŋkə / (BrE)
an island country in the Indian Ocean, southeast of India, known for its diverse landscapes and cultural heritage
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

She traveled to Sri Lanka to visit its ancient Buddhist temples and beautiful beaches.
The economy of Sri Lanka relies heavily on tourism and tea exports.
After a long civil war, Sri Lanka has become a popular destination for international travelers.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'śrī' (venerable) and 'laṅkā' (island).

2. PROPN

/ ʃri ˈlɑːŋkə / (AmE), / sri ˈlæŋkə / (BrE)
an island country in South Asia, located in the Indian Ocean to the southeast of India; officially known as the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.
island country in South Asia

Examples

She spent two weeks exploring the ancient temples and beaches of Sri Lanka.
The official languages of Sri Lanka are Sinhala and Tamil.
Exports of tea are vital to the economy of Sri Lanka.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ceylon - the former name for Sri Lanka before 1972

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'śrī' (venerable, holy) and 'Lanka' (island), meaning 'Resplendent Island'.
