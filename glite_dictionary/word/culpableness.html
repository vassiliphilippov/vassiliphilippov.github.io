culpableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlpəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlpəblnəs / (BrE)
The state of being deserving of blame or responsibility for a wrongdoing; blameworthiness.
state of being blameworthy

Examples

The court must determine the culpableness of each defendant before passing judgment.
Debate about the culpableness of the executive continued throughout the hearing.
Her lawyer argued that her culpableness was less than that of her accomplices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culpability - state of being responsible for a fault or wrong
  • Antonym: innocence - state of being not guilty or blameless

Etymology

From Latin 'culpabilis' (worthy of blame) + English suffix '-ness'.
