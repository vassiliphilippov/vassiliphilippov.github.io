to append

1. VERB

/ əˈpɛnd / (AmE), / əˈpɛnd / (BrE)
to add something to the end of a written document, list, or other collection
add to the end of something

Examples

Please append your signature to the end of the letter.
The author appended several notes to clarify the main text.
You can append additional information to the report if necessary.

Synset Examples

He appended a glossary to his novel where he used an invented language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attach - to join or fasten something to another thing
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away from its place

Etymology

From Latin 'appendere', from 'ad-' (to) + 'pendere' (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331387-v

2. VERB

/ əˈpɛnd / (AmE), / əˈpɛnd / (BrE)
to attach or add something to the end of a document or another item
attach or add to something

Examples

Please append your signature to the end of the letter.
The document allows you to append additional notes if necessary.
She decided to append a list of sources at the end of her essay.

Synset Examples

append a charm to the necklace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attach - to fasten or join one thing to another
  • Antonym: to detach - to remove or separate something that is attached

Etymology

From Latin 'appendere' (to hang upon), from 'ad-' (to) + 'pendere' (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331195-v
Synset POS: v
