to remunerate

1. VERB

/ rəˈmjunəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːnəreɪt / (BrE)
to pay someone for work or services; to compensate financially
pay or compensate (formally)

Examples

All employees will be remunerated fairly for their overtime work.
The company failed to remunerate him for his contributions to the project.
She was generously remunerated for her consulting services.

Synset Examples

My efforts were not remunerated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compensate - to give someone something, usually money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or work
  • Synonym: reward - to give something to someone in recognition of service, effort, or achievement

Etymology

From Latin 'remuneratus', past participle of 'remunerare' (to reward)
