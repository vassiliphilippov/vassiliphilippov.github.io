to shoplift

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɑp.lɪft / (AmE), / ˈʃɒp.lɪft / (BrE)
to steal goods from a store while pretending to be a customer
steal from a store as a customer

Examples

The teenager was caught trying to shoplift a pair of jeans from the local store.
She was arrested after she attempted to shoplift several items from the pharmacy.
Security cameras are often used to catch people who shoplift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right

Etymology

Blend of 'shop' and 'lift' (slang for steal); American English, late 19th century.
