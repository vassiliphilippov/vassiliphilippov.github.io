protraction

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / prəˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the act of prolonging or extending something in time; prolongation
the act of prolonging or extending

Examples

The protraction of the negotiations frustrated both parties involved.
Doctors warned against the unnecessary protraction of the patient's hospitalization.
She criticized the protraction of the decision-making process in the committee.

Synset Examples

there was an indefinite prolongation of the peace talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolongation - the act of making something last longer
  • Antonym: shortening - the act of making something shorter in duration

Etymology

From Latin protractio, from protractus, past participle of protrahere (to draw forth, prolong).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / prəˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of making something last longer than usual; the prolongation of something in time
the act of lengthening duration

Examples

The protraction of the negotiations frustrated everyone involved.
Continuous protraction of the project means increased costs for the company.
We must avoid unnecessary protraction of the meeting and stick to the agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolongation - the act of extending in time or duration
  • Antonym: abbreviation - a shortening of duration or length

Etymology

From Latin 'protractio', from 'protrahere' meaning 'to draw out'.
