oropharynx

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔroʊˈfærɪŋks / (AmE), / ˌɔːrəˈfærɪŋks / (BrE)
the part of the pharynx that lies at the back of the mouth, behind the oral cavity and above the larynx
part of the throat behind the mouth
medical

Examples

A doctor examined the patient's oropharynx for signs of infection.
Swelling in the oropharynx can make swallowing difficult.
The surgeon inspected the oropharynx before beginning the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nasopharynx - the upper part of the pharynx, connecting with the nasal cavity
  • Degree Lower: laryngopharynx - the lower part of the pharynx, leading to the esophagus and larynx

Etymology

From Greek 'oros' (mouth) + 'pharynx' (throat).
