Salmo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsalməʊ / (BrE)
The genus of fish including the salmon and trout, belonging to the family Salmonidae.
genus of salmon and trout

Examples

The genus Salmo includes both the Atlantic salmon and several species of trout.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Salmo genus in Eastern Europe.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect wild Salmo populations from extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - A large fish with pink flesh, found in the northern Atlantic and Pacific Oceans
  • Synonym: trout - A common name for several species of freshwater fish closely related to salmon

Etymology

From Latin *salmo* (salmon). Used in scientific classification.
