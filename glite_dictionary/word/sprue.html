sprue

1. NOUN

/ spru / (AmE), / spru / (BrE)
A chronic disease in which the small intestine is unable to absorb nutrients properly, leading to symptoms such as chronic diarrhea and weight loss; it includes both tropical and non-tropical forms.
intestinal malabsorption disease
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with sprue after prolonged weight loss and digestive problems.
Persistent diarrhea and nutrient deficiencies can be signs of sprue.
Treatment of sprue involves dietary changes and medical management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malabsorption - inability of the intestine to absorb nutrients
  • Synonym: celiac disease - immune reaction to eating gluten causing intestinal damage

Etymology

From Portuguese 'espru', meaning 'sprue' or chronic diarrhea.
