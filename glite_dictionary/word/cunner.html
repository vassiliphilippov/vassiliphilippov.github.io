cunner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌnər / (AmE), / ˈkʌnə / (BrE)
a small North Atlantic fish (Tautogolabrus adspersus), common along the northeastern coast of the United States, with a thick-set body and usually brownish color
a small North Atlantic fish

Examples

We spotted a group of cunner swimming near the rocky shoreline.
Anglers often catch cunner while fishing for larger species in the Atlantic.
The aquarium features a large tank filled with local species, including the cunner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrasse - a family of marine fishes that includes cunner

Etymology

Perhaps from Cornish *conner* (a type of fish) or related to Old Norse *kunngr* (known, familiar).
