rhesus

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.səs / (AmE), / ˈriː.səs / (BrE)
a macaque monkey native to southern Asia, commonly used in medical research
a southern Asian macaque monkey

Examples

The rhesus monkey is often studied for its close genetic relationship to humans.
Scientists used rhesus macaques to test the new vaccine’s effectiveness.
A colony of rhesus monkeys lives in the forests of northern India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaque - a type of monkey to which the rhesus belongs

Etymology

From French 'rhesus', from Latin 'Rhesus', the name of a mythical king; applied in zoology to this species.
