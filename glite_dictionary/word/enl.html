ENL

1. NOUN

/ i ɛ n ɛl / (AmE), / i ɛ n ɛl / (BrE)
an inflammatory complication of leprosy characterized by painful skin lesions on the arms, legs, and face
leprosy complication with painful skin lesions
medical

Examples

Patients with ENL often experience sudden episodes of fever and painful nodules on their skin.
Treatment of ENL may require corticosteroids to manage the inflammation.
Doctors monitored the leprosy patient closely for signs of ENL during therapy.

Etymology

Abbreviation of erythema nodosum leprosum (Latin: erythema = redness; nodosum = nodular; leprosum = of leprosy).
