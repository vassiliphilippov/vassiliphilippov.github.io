perspicaciousness

1. NOUN

/ pɚˌspɪˈkeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / pəˌspɪˈkeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of having keen insight and good judgment; astuteness.
keen insight; astuteness

Examples

Her perspicaciousness allowed her to anticipate market trends before her competitors noticed them.
The manager admired his assistant's perspicaciousness in identifying potential risks.
A leader's perspicaciousness is invaluable when making critical business decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astuteness - the quality of being clever and perceptive
  • Synonym: insight - the capacity to gain an accurate and deep understanding of something
  • Antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'perspicax' (sharp-sighted) + '-ous' + '-ness'.
