dawdler

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔdlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːdlə / (BrE)
a person who moves or acts slowly, often wasting time or lingering when promptness is expected
person who lingers or wastes time

Examples

If you keep being a dawdler, we'll be late for the movie.
The tour guide urged the dawdler at the back to catch up with the group.
As a child, she was always a bit of a dawdler on the walk to school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laggard - a person who makes slow progress and falls behind others
  • Antonym: speedster - a person who moves very quickly

Etymology

From the verb 'dawdle', of uncertain origin, possibly a variant of 'daddle' (to walk unsteadily).
