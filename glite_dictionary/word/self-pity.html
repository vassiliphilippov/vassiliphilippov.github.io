self-pity

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˈpɪti / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈpɪti / (BrE)
Excessive or self-indulgent sorrow or unhappiness about one's own troubles or misfortunes.
excessive sorrow for oneself

Examples

She fell into self-pity after losing her job, refusing to see her friends.
His constant self-pity made it difficult for others to sympathize with him.
Overcoming self-pity is essential to moving forward after a setback.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: self-compassion - kindness and understanding towards oneself in hardship
  • Antonym: self-reproach - harsh criticism or blame towards oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'pity', first recorded in the early 19th century.
