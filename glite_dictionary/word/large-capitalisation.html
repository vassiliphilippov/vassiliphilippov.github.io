large-capitalisation

1. ADJ

/ lɑrdʒ ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ˌkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(of stocks) relating to companies with a market capitalisation of five billion dollars or more.
having a market value over $5 billion (of stocks)

Examples

Many institutional investors prefer large-capitalisation stocks due to their stability.
The company's strong profits helped it achieve large-capitalisation status on the stock exchange.
Investors often diversify by including both small and large-capitalisation companies in their portfolios.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small-capitalisation - of stocks of companies with relatively low market value
  • Degree Lower: mid-capitalisation - of stocks of companies with a moderate market value

Etymology

Formed from 'large' plus 'capitalisation' (from Latin 'capitalis', relating to the head or principal, and 'ize').
