to bemock

1. VERB

/ bɪˈmɑk / (AmE), / bɪˈmɒk / (BrE)
to treat with contempt or ridicule; to mock or deride
to mock or treat with contempt
archaic

Examples

The cruel crowd began to bemock the old storyteller's accent.
In his letters, he would often bemock the traditions of his rivals.
The play was meant to bemock the follies of the aristocracy.

Synset Examples

The new constitution mocks all democratic principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule

Etymology

From prefix be- + mock, following Early Modern English formations.
