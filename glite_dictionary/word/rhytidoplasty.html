rhytidoplasty

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈtɪdəˌplæsti / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪdəˌplasti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove wrinkles and other signs of aging from the face by tightening the skin
surgical removal of facial wrinkles
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended rhytidoplasty to reduce the appearance of deep facial wrinkles.
After her rhytidoplasty, she looked noticeably younger and more refreshed.
Rhytidoplasty is often considered by people seeking a more youthful facial appearance.

Synset Examples

some actresses have more than one face lift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facelift - a cosmetic surgery to improve the appearance of the face by removing wrinkles

Etymology

From Greek 'rhytís' (wrinkle) + 'plasty' (molding, forming).
