equitable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛkwɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
fair and impartial; treating all people or groups equally and according to reason or justice
fair and impartial

Examples

The manager aimed to ensure an equitable distribution of resources among all departments.
We must find an equitable solution that benefits everyone involved.
Laws are designed to provide equitable treatment regardless of social status or background.

Synset Examples

equitable treatment of all citizens
an equitable distribution of gifts among the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fair - treating people equally without favoritism or discrimination
  • Antonym: unfair - not treating people equally or justly

Etymology

From Late Latin aequitabilis (fair, just), from Latin aequitas (equity).
