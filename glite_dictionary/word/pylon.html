pylon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.lɑn / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.lɒn / (BrE)
A tall structure, often a tower, used for guiding pilots or marking a turning point in a race.
tower guiding pilots or marking a turning point

Examples

The race cars swerved tightly around the pylon marking the first turn.
A red pylon stood at the end of the runway to guide incoming planes.
During the air show, pilots looped their planes around each marked pylon with precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marker - an object used to indicate a place, position, or route
  • Synonym: tower - a tall structure, sometimes used for guidance or observation

Etymology

From Greek 'pylon' meaning 'gateway,' via French 'pylône'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.lɑn / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.lɒn / (BrE)
a tall metal tower supporting overhead power lines
tower supporting power lines

Examples

The high-voltage cables stretched between each pylon across the countryside.
During the storm, a pylon was struck by lightning, causing a major power outage.
Engineers inspected every pylon to ensure the electrical grid was safe after the earthquake.

Synset Examples

power pylons are a favorite target for terrorists

Etymology

From Greek pylon (gateway), originally meaning monumental gateways, later applied to tower-like structures.
