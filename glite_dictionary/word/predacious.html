predacious

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
seeking to exploit or victimize others for personal gain; predatory.
seeking to exploit others; predatory

Examples

The company was criticized for its predacious business practices that hurt small competitors.
Some species exhibit predacious behavior, capturing and consuming other animals to survive.
He warned investors to beware of predacious schemes offering unrealistic returns.

Synset Examples

predatory capitalists
a predatory, insensate society in which innocence and decency can prove fatal
a predacious kind of animal — the early geological gangster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - seeking to exploit or oppress others
  • Synonym: parasitic - taking advantage of others without giving anything in return

Etymology

From Latin praedari (to plunder), from praeda (prey).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) hunting and killing other animals for food; predatory.
hunting and killing for food

Examples

The tiger is a predacious animal, stalking its prey with stealth and patience.
Some insects are predacious, feeding on other insects to survive.
The ecosystem is balanced by both herbivorous and predacious species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - preying on other animals for food; predator-like

Etymology

From Latin praedari (to plunder), from praeda (prey, booty).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
