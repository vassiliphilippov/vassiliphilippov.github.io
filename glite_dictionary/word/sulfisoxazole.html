sulfisoxazole

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌl.fəˈsoʊk.səˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ˌsʌl.fɪˈsɒk.səˌzəʊl / (BrE)
a sulfonamide antibiotic used to treat infections, especially of the urinary tract
a sulfonamide antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sulfisoxazole to treat her urinary tract infection.
Allergic reactions to sulfisoxazole can occur in some patients.
Before taking sulfisoxazole, tell your physician about any allergies you may have.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sulfonamide - a group of synthetic antimicrobial agents

Etymology

From 'sulfonamide' and chemical elements in its structure; the suffix -azole indicates a chemical ring compound.
