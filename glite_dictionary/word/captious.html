captious

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæp.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈkæp.ʃəs / (BrE)
Tending to find fault or raise petty objections; marked by a readiness to point out trivial faults.
inclined to find trivial faults

Examples

The reviewer was so captious that she found flaws in even the best performances.
His captious comments annoyed everyone at the dinner table.
Working with a captious supervisor made every project stressful.

Synset Examples

a captious pedant
an excessively demanding and faultfinding tutor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - inclined to judge or find fault
  • Antonym: complimentary - expressing approval or praise; the opposite of critical

Etymology

From Latin 'captiosus' (deceitful, sophistical), from 'captio' (a taking, a catch).
