Hyoscyamus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɑːsɪˈæməs / (AmE), / haɪˌɒsɪˈaməs / (BrE)
a genus of poisonous herbs in the nightshade family, including henbane, with sticky leaves and funnel-shaped flowers
genus of poisonous herbs (henbane)

Examples

Hyoscyamus plants contain toxic alkaloids that can cause hallucinations if ingested.
The most well-known species in the Hyoscyamus genus is commonly called henbane.
Botanists carefully handle Hyoscyamus because all parts of the plant are poisonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henbane - a poisonous plant in the genus Hyoscyamus

Etymology

From Greek hyoskyamos, meaning 'hog-bean'.

2. NOUN

/ haɪəˈskaɪəməs / (AmE), / hɪəˈskɪəməs / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous herbaceous plants in the nightshade family, including the henbane.
genus of poisonous plants (henbane)

Examples

The genus Hyoscyamus is known for its toxic properties and medicinal uses in ancient cultures.
Henbane, belonging to Hyoscyamus, was historically used as a sedative and hallucinogen.
Botanists studied several species within the Hyoscyamus genus to better understand their alkaloid content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henbane - a poisonous plant of the genus Hyoscyamus

Etymology

From Latin *Hyoscyamus*, from Ancient Greek *hyoskyamos* (literally 'hog-bean').
