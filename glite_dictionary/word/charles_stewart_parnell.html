Charles Stewart Parnell

1. PROPN

/ ˈʧɑrlz ˈstuːərt pɑrˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˈʧɑːlz ˈstjuːət pɑːˈnɛl / (BrE)
(1846–1891) An Irish nationalist political leader known for his efforts to promote Irish home rule and his influence on British and Irish politics in the late 19th century.
Irish nationalist leader (1846–1891)

Examples

Charles Stewart Parnell played a major role in the campaign for Irish home rule during the late nineteenth century.
Many historians regard Charles Stewart Parnell as one of the most influential Irish political figures of his time.
The downfall of Charles Stewart Parnell marked a turning point in Irish politics.
