Angraecum

1. NOUN

/ ænˈɡriːkəm / (AmE), / ænˈɡriːkəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical epiphytic orchids native to the Old World, known for their showy and sometimes unusual flowers
genus of tropical epiphytic orchids

Examples

The Angraecum orchids are prized by collectors for their striking appearance.
Some species of Angraecum produce flowers with long, elegant spurs.
She cultivated a rare Angraecum in her greenhouse, hoping it would bloom for the orchid show.

Etymology

From French Angraecum, from Latinized form of Malay 'anggrek' (orchid).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈɡriːkəm / (AmE), / æŋˈɡriːkəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical epiphytic orchids native to the Old World, known for their showy and sometimes unusual flowers.
genus of tropical epiphytic orchids

Examples

Several species in the Angraecum genus are prized by orchid collectors for their striking blooms.
Charles Darwin studied the Angraecum sesquipedale to understand pollination by moths.
The unique shape of many Angraecum flowers attracts specific pollinators in their native habitats.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Malgache (Malagasy) 'angraecum', used in botanical nomenclature.
