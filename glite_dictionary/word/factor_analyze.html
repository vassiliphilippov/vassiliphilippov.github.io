to factor analyze

1. VERB

/ ˈfæk.tɚ ˌæn.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ˌæn.əˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to perform a factor analysis on a set of data, especially to identify underlying variables by examining correlations among measured variables
perform factor analysis on data

Examples

The experimenters decided to factor analyze the survey responses to uncover latent variables.
If you factor analyze the test scores, you may find underlying dimensions of student ability.
Statisticians often factor analyze large datasets to identify patterns in psychological traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor analysis - the statistical method of identifying underlying relationships between variables

Etymology

Formed from 'factor' and 'analyze', based on the statistical method 'factor analysis'.
