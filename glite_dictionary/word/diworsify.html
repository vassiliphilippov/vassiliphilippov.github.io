to diworsify

1. VERB

/ daɪˈwɜːrsɪfaɪ / (AmE), / daɪˈwɔːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something worse by diversifying, especially by adding investments or projects that reduce overall quality or performance
make worse by poor diversification
informal

Examples

Many companies diworsify when they acquire businesses outside their core area.
You risk your returns if you diworsify your investments without careful research.
Analysts warned that continued attempts to diworsify would hurt the firm's reputation.

Synset Examples

His tendency to diworsify projects caused delays.
Don't diworsify your portfolio with unrelated assets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diversify - to increase variety by adding different elements
  • Antonym: to improve - to make something better

Etymology

Blend of "diversify" and "worse".
