Giulio de' Medici

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːlioʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːliəʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1478–1534) Italian pope, known as Clement VII, who ruled from 1523 to 1534 and is noted for breaking with Henry VIII after Henry's divorce from Catherine of Aragon and marriage to Anne Boleyn.
Italian pope (Clement VII), 1523–1534

Examples

Giulio de' Medici became Pope Clement VII at a time of great political turmoil in Europe.
The reign of Giulio de' Medici marked a significant turning point in the history of the English church.
Artists and scholars were active in Rome during the papacy of Giulio de' Medici.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clement VII - papal name of Giulio de' Medici as pope
