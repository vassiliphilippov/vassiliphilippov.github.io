sunbaked

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌnˌbeɪkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbeɪkt / (BrE)
Baked or hardened by exposure to sunlight, especially referring to surfaces or materials that become dry and firm from the heat of the sun.
hardened or dried by the sun

Examples

The sunbaked ground cracked under their feet as they walked through the desert.
We sat on the sunbaked terrace enjoying the warmth and a cool drink.
After months without rain, the fields were hard and sunbaked.

Synset Examples

sunbaked adobe bricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - extremely dry due to heat or drought

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + past participle 'baked', indicating something baked by the sun.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌnˌbeɪkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbeɪkt / (BrE)
dried out or heated intensely by exposure to sunlight
dried out by sunlight

Examples

The sunbaked streets shimmered in the midday heat.
We picnicked on the sunbaked grass, enjoying the warm summer afternoon.
The old farmhouse stood alone in the sunbaked countryside.

Synset Examples

a vast desert all adust
land lying baked in the heat
parched soil
the earth was scorched and bare
sunbaked salt flats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - very dry, especially due to heat
  • Synonym: scorched - burned or dried by intense heat
  • Antonym: humid - containing a high amount of water vapor or moisture
