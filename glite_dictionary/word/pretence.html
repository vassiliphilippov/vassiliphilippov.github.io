pretence

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈtɛns / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true; a false claim or display
false claim or display
british

Examples

She made a pretence of being interested in the conversation.
His apparent joy was only a pretence to hide his disappointment.
They dropped all pretence of politeness and argued openly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - American spelling of 'pretence', meaning a false claim or display
  • Antonym: truth - the true or actual state of a matter; opposite of falsehood or pretence

Etymology

From Middle English pretens, from Old French pretense, from Medieval Latin praetēnsa (alleged excuse), from Latin praetendere (to pretend).

2. NOUN

/ priˈtɛns / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
the act of imagining or pretending; especially imaginative intellectual play or make-believe
imaginative play or make-believe

Examples

As children, much of our afternoon was spent in pretence, building castles out of cushions and ruling imaginary kingdoms.
Actors must often rely on their sense of pretence to fully become their characters.
The teacher encouraged pretence in the classroom to help students develop their creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - alternative US spelling of 'pretence'

Etymology

From Old French 'pretensse', from Medieval Latin 'praetensus', from Latin 'praetendere' (to stretch forth, pretend).
