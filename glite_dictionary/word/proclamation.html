proclamation

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊkləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒkləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially announcing something to the public, especially by a formal or public statement
formal public announcement

Examples

The government issued a proclamation declaring a national holiday.
The mayor's proclamation was read aloud at the ceremony.
A royal proclamation made the new law official across the country.

Synset Examples

his promulgation of the policy proved to be premature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declaration - an official statement, announcement, or proclamation
  • Synonym: announcement - a public or formal statement about something

Etymology

From Old French 'proclamation', from Latin 'proclamatio', from 'proclamare' (to shout out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːkləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒkləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
an official or formal public announcement made by a person in authority
formal public announcement

Examples

The king issued a proclamation declaring a national holiday.
The mayor's proclamation was read aloud at the city square.
After the war, a proclamation of peace was welcomed by the public.

Synset Examples

the government made an announcement about changes in the drug war
a declaration of independence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declaration - a formal announcement or statement
  • Synonym: announcement - a public or formal statement on a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin 'proclamatio', from 'proclamare' (to shout out).
