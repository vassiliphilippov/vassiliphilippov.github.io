Galium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪ.li.əm / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.li.əm / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbs commonly known as bedstraw or cleavers, found in temperate regions.
genus of herbs (bedstraw, cleavers)

Examples

Many woodlands are covered with Galium, which forms a soft green carpet in spring.
The botanist identified the plant as a species of Galium native to Europe.
Gardeners often remove Galium because it can quickly spread and cover the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedstraw - any plant of the genus Galium, with whorled leaves and small flowers
  • Synonym: cleavers - a common name for Galium aparine, a species of Galium

Etymology

From Latin 'galium', from Greek 'gala' meaning 'milk', due to some species' use in curdling milk.
