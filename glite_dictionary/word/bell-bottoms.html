bell-bottoms

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛl ˌbɑtəmz / (AmE), / ˈbɛl ˌbɒtəmz / (BrE)
trousers with legs that flare out from the knee downward, especially fashionable in the 1960s and 1970s and originally worn by sailors.
trousers with flared legs

Examples

She found a vintage pair of bell-bottoms at the thrift store.
In the 1970s, bell-bottoms were a staple of youth fashion.
He wore his navy bell-bottoms as part of his sailor uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flares - trousers with wide flared legs similar to bell-bottoms

Etymology

From 'bell' (shape) + 'bottoms' (trouser legs), referencing the bell-like flare at the bottom.
