neanderthal

1. ADJ

/ niˈæn.dərˌtɑl / (AmE), / niˈæn.dəˌtɑːl / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) crude, uncivilized, or boorish; lacking in refinement or sophistication.
crude or uncivilized (person/behavior)

Examples

He was criticized for his neanderthal attitudes toward women in the workplace.
Her manager's neanderthal comments made everyone uncomfortable during the meeting.
Don't be such a neanderthal—hold the door open for others.

Synset Examples

was boorish and insensitive
the loutish manners of a bully
her stupid oafish husband
aristocratic contempt for the swinish multitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boorish - rude and insensitive
  • Antonym: refined - elegant or cultured in manner or taste

Etymology

From the name of the Neanderthal hominin, originally named after the Neander Valley in Germany.
