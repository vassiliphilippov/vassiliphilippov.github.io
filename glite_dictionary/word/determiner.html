determiner

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːmɪnər / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnə / (BrE)
a word, usually placed before a noun, that limits or clarifies its reference, such as 'the', 'a', 'some', or 'my'
word limiting or identifying a noun

Examples

'The' is a common determiner in English, used to refer to something specific.
Possessive pronouns like 'my' or 'your' function as a determiner before nouns.
Each noun phrase in the sentence must have a determiner to be grammatically correct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: article - a type of determiner (e.g. 'the', 'a', 'an')

Etymology

From Latin determinare (to limit, to bound), via the verb 'determine'.
