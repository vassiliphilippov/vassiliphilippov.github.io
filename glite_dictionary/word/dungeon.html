dungeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌndʒən / (AmE), / ˈdʌndʒən / (BrE)
an underground prison cell, especially one in a castle, used to confine prisoners
underground prison cell, especially in a castle

Examples

The prisoner was thrown into the dungeon beneath the castle for his crimes.
Stories of knights often include dark dungeons filled with mysterious captives.
The old fortress still has a dungeon that visitors can tour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small room in a prison or jail

Etymology

From Old French *donjon* (great tower of a castle), later associated with prison cells in castles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈdʌn.dʒən / (BrE)
the main tower within the walls of a medieval castle or fortress, often serving as the strongest part of the structure
main tower of a medieval castle

Examples

The king ordered his guards to retreat to the dungeon if the castle walls were breached.
Historians studied the architecture of the dungeon to understand medieval defense strategies.
From the highest window of the dungeon, you could see the whole valley below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep - main fortified tower of a castle

Etymology

From Old French donjon (great tower of a castle), from Gallo-Roman domnionem, influenced by Latin dominus (lord).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
