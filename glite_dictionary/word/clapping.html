clapping

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklæpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of applauding by striking the palms of the hands together to show approval or appreciation
act of applauding by striking hands

Examples

The clapping grew louder as the speaker finished her address.
After the performance, the audience broke into enthusiastic clapping.
Sudden clapping echoed through the hall, signaling approval from the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applause - approval shown by clapping hands

Etymology

From the verb 'clap', which comes from Middle English 'clappen', of Germanic origin.
