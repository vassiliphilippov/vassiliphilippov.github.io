broth

1. NOUN

/ brɑθ / (AmE), / brɒθ / (BrE)
a thin liquid food made by boiling meat, fish, or vegetables in water, often used as a base for soups and sauces
thin soup made from meat, fish, or vegetables

Examples

She cooked the vegetables in a rich broth for extra flavor.
The chicken broth simmered on the stove all afternoon, filling the house with a comforting aroma.
Many traditional recipes start with a simple broth as the base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - liquid made by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables, often thicker and richer than broth
  • Degree Higher: consommé - clear soup made from richly flavored stock or broth

Etymology

Old English 'broth', from Proto-Germanic *bruþą (liquid in which something has been boiled).

2. NOUN

/ brɑːθ / (AmE), / brɒθ / (BrE)
A savory liquid in which meat, bones, vegetables, or grains have been simmered, often used as a base for soups or sauces.
liquid for soups/sauces from simmered meat or vegetables

Examples

She added chicken broth to the pot to make the soup more flavorful.
The recipe calls for two cups of vegetable broth as a base.
After simmering the meat and vegetables, she strained the broth and used it in a sauce.

Synset Examples

she made gravy with a base of beef stock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - flavored liquid prepared by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables, used as a base for soups and sauces

Etymology

From Old English *broth* (liquid in which something has been boiled), related to Old High German *brod* and German *Brühe*.
