inoperable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɑːpərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɒpərəbəl / (BrE)
(of a medical condition or tumor) not suitable for surgical operation.
not suitable for surgery
medical

Examples

The doctors determined that the tumor was inoperable due to its location near vital organs.
After several tests, they concluded that her heart condition was inoperable.
Unfortunately, the cancer was discovered at an inoperable stage.

Synset Examples

metastasis has rendered the tumor inoperable

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'operari' (to work, operate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01173484-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɑpərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒpərəbəl / (BrE)
not able to function or be used as intended; incapable of operating normally.
not able to function or be used

Examples

The machine became inoperable after the power surge damaged its circuits.
Due to a major system error, the software was completely inoperable.
After the flood, most of the equipment in the lab was inoperable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unusable - not able to be used

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'operabilis' (workable, able to be worked).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02132579-s
Synset POS: s
