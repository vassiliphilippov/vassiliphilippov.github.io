simply

1. ADV

/ ˈsɪmpli / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpli / (BrE)
absolutely; really; used to emphasize what is being said.
absolutely; really; for emphasis

Examples

The restaurant was simply amazing; I've never tasted food like that before.
I'm simply exhausted after such a long day at work.
He was simply the best candidate for the job, without question.

Synset Examples

we are simply broke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; totally
  • Synonym: totally - completely; absolutely
  • Antonym: hardly - scarcely; only just (opposite sense)

Etymology

From Latin simplex (single, simple) via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɪmpli / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpli / (BrE)
(used for emphasis) absolutely; completely; used to emphasize the strength or clarity of a statement.
absolutely; completely (emphatic adverb)

Examples

  • Synonym: utterly - completely; totally (emphatic adverb)
  • Antonym: barely - only just; hardly

4. ADV

/ ˈsɪmpli / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpli / (BrE)
In a simple manner; without extravagance, complication, or embellishment.
in a simple manner

Examples

They decorated the hall simply, with just a few flowers and candles.
She prefers to dress simply rather than in fancy clothes.
The meal was prepared simply, using only fresh ingredients.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elaborately - in a detailed and decorated way, with embellishments

From the adjective 'simple' plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
