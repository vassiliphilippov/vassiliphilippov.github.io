sickly

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪkli / (AmE), / ˈsɪkli / (BrE)
often ill or appearing weak and unhealthy; inclined to become sick frequently.
often ill; unhealthy-looking

Examples

The sickly child missed a lot of school days due to frequent colds.
She gave him a sickly smile as she struggled to sit up in bed.
The old building had a sickly smell that made us hurry our visit.

Synset Examples

is unwell and can't come to work

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - in good physical or mental condition; not sick
  • Synonym: unwell - not well; ill

Etymology

From Middle English sikly, from sick + -ly.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪkli / (AmE), / ˈsɪkli / (BrE)
appearing pale or unhealthy; having an appearance of being often ill
looking unhealthy or frequently ill

Examples

After weeks of illness, his sickly appearance worried his family.
She gave me a sickly smile before excusing herself to rest.
The sickly child rarely played outside with the others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - in good health; not sickly
  • Synonym: pale - having little color, often suggesting illness

Etymology

From Old English *seoclic* (in a sickly manner), from *seoc* (sick) + *-lic* (like).
