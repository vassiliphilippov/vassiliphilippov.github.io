to tousle

1. VERB

/ ˈtaʊzəl / (AmE), / ˈtaʊzəl / (BrE)
to make something, especially hair, untidy or disordered by rough handling
make (hair, etc.) untidy or messy

Examples

After the nap, his mother gently tousled his hair to wake him up.
The playful puppy tousled the blanket, leaving it in a heap on the floor.
A sudden breeze tousled her carefully arranged hairstyle just before the photo.

Synset Examples

The strong wind tousled my hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dishevel - to make (hair or clothing) untidy or disordered
  • Antonym: smooth - to make (hair or something else) flat and tidy

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Middle English *towsen* (to pull, tug).
