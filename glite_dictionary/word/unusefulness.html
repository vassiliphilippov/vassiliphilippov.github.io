unusefulness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈjus.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈjuːs.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of having no practical use or being not useful.
quality of having no practical use

Examples

The unusefulness of outdated technology became apparent as the company tried to compete in the market.
He argued about the unusefulness of bringing more paperwork into an already inefficient process.
After much evaluation, the committee concluded the project was marked by total unusefulness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uselessness - the state or quality of being useless
  • Antonym: usefulness - the quality of being useful

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'useful' + '-ness' (noun suffix).
