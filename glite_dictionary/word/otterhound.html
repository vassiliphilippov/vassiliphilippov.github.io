otterhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑt̬ɚˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɒtəˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a large, hardy British breed of hunting dog with long, drooping ears and a rough, shaggy coat, originally bred for hunting otters
breed of British hunting dog

Examples

The otterhound is known for its keen sense of smell and love of water.
Their family adopted an otterhound because of its gentle temperament and playful nature.
At the dog show, the otterhound impressed everyone with its shaggy coat and friendly demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hound - a type of dog bred for hunting

Etymology

From 'otter' + 'hound', indicating a hound bred to hunt otters.
