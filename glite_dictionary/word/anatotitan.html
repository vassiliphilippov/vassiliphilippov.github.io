Anatotitan

1. PROPN

/ ˌænətoʊˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / ˌanətəʊˈtʌɪtən / (BrE)
a genus of large duck-billed dinosaurs that lived during the late Cretaceous period
genus of large duck-billed dinosaurs

Examples

The Anatotitan roamed North America during the late Cretaceous period.
Fossils of Anatotitan have helped scientists understand hadrosaur anatomy.
Unlike other duck-billed dinosaurs, the Anatotitan had an exceptionally long skull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hadrosaurus - another genus of duck-billed dinosaurs
  • Antonym: Tyrannosaurus - a genus of large carnivorous dinosaurs from the same period

Etymology

From Greek 'anāto-' (duck) and 'titan' (giant), meaning 'giant duck.'
