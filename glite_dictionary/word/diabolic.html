diabolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈbɑl.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈbɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
Extremely evil or cruel; characteristic of the devil or hell.
extremely evil; devilish

Examples

The villain's diabolic plot threatened to destroy the entire city.
She gave him a diabolic smile, full of mischief and malice.
The diabolic cruelty of the torture devices shocked even the hardened guards.

Synset Examples

something demonic in him — something that could be cruel
fires lit up a diabolic scene
diabolical sorcerers under the influence of devils
a fiendish despot
hellish torture
infernal instruments of war
satanic cruelty
unholy grimaces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - Extremely evil; characteristic of the devil
  • Antonym: angelic - Exceptionally good or innocent, like an angel
  • Synonym: satanic - Relating to or characteristic of Satan; extremely evil

Etymology

From Late Latin diabolicus, from Greek diabolikos, from diabolos (devil).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
Very evil or wicked with the cunning, ingenuity, or cruelty typically associated with the devil.
devilish; extremely wicked or cunning

Examples

The villain hatched a diabolic plan to take over the city.
She recognized a diabolic glint in his eyes as he spoke.
Their plot was so diabolic that it left everyone in shock.

Synset Examples

devilish schemes
the cold calculation and diabolic art of some statesmen
the diabolical expression on his face
a mephistophelian glint in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - very evil or cruel, like a devil
  • Synonym: devilish - like a devil; wicked or mischievous
  • Antonym: angelic - having qualities of an angel; very good and kind

Etymology

From Late Latin diabolicus, from Greek diabolikos, from diabolos meaning 'devil'.
