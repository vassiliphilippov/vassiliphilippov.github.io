Hydromys

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑmɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈdrɒmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic rodents found primarily in Australia and New Guinea, commonly known as water rats.
genus of aquatic water rats

Examples

The Hydromys are well adapted to a semi-aquatic lifestyle in Australian rivers and streams.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of Hydromys in their natural habitat.
Unlike typical rats, Hydromys have partially webbed feet to help them swim.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'mys' (mouse, rat).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːmɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈdrɒmɪs / (BrE)
a genus of semiaquatic rodents, commonly known as water rats, native to Australia and New Guinea
genus of semiaquatic rodents (water rats)

Examples

The genus Hydromys is well adapted for swimming in rivers and streams.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Hydromys genus in Papua New Guinea.
Members of Hydromys are commonly referred to as water rats due to their aquatic habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water rat - a common name for rodents of the genus Hydromys

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'mys' (mouse).
