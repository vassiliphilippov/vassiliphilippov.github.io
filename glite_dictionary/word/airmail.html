airmail

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈeəˌmeɪl / (BrE)
a system for sending mail by aircraft, especially as a faster means of delivery between distant places
system of sending mail by aircraft

Examples

He decided to send the documents by airmail to ensure they arrived quickly.
The package took only three days to arrive from France by airmail.
Before the age of email, airmail was the preferred method for international communication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surface mail - mail transported by land or sea, not by air

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'mail' to designate mail carried by aircraft.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈeəˌmeɪl / (BrE)
letters and packages sent by air, especially by aircraft, rather than by land or sea
mail sent by aircraft

Examples

Please send the documents by airmail to ensure they arrive quickly.
She paid extra to have her package sent via airmail.
The letter was marked airmail and delivered within three days.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surface mail - letters and packages transported by land or sea

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'mail', indicating mail carried by air (early 20th century).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06635500-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɛrˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈeə.meɪl / (BrE)
to send or transport (a letter or package) by air, especially using a postal service for faster international delivery.
send by air postal service

Examples

I need to airmail this package to Canada to ensure it arrives quickly.
She airmailed the signed documents to her lawyer overseas.
We are airmailing the invitations to our friends abroad.

Synset Examples

Letters to Europe from the U.S. are best airmailed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mail - to send (a letter or package) by post

Etymology

Formed from 'air', referring to air transport, and 'mail', referring to postal delivery. First used in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01033999-v
Synset POS: v
