cismontane

1. ADJ

/ sɪsˈmɑːn.teɪn / (AmE), / sɪsˈmɒn.teɪn / (BrE)
on the same side of the mountains as the speaker or point of reference; lying on this side of the mountains.
on this side of the mountains

Examples

The Romans considered Gaul cismontane because it was on their side of the Alps.
Early settlers in California distinguished between cismontane and transmontane regions of the state.
The valley's cismontane climate is milder than areas beyond the mountains.

Synset Examples

a contest in Virginia between a cismontane and a tramontane people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transmontane - on the other side of the mountains

Etymology

From Latin cis- (on this side of) + montānus (of the mountains).
