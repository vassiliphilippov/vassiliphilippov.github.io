prudish

1. ADJ

/ ˈpruːdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpruːdɪʃ / (BrE)
Easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity; excessively concerned with propriety or modesty.
excessively proper or modest

Examples

Her prudish attitude made her blush at even the slightest hint of impropriety.
Some critics called the film too prudish for its refusal to show any romance scenes.
He was considered prudish because he disapproved of swimwear at the beach.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modest - not willing to draw attention to oneself or one's body; showing restraint or humility
  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values

Etymology

From 'prude' (French, meaning a person who is or claims to be easily shocked), plus English '-ish'.
