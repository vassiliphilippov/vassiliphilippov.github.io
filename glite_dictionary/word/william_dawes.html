William Dawes

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm dɔːz / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm dɔːz / (BrE)
(1745–1799) An American patriot who rode with Paul Revere to warn colonists of the advancing British troops before the battles of Lexington and Concord.
American patriot and Revolutionary War rider

Examples

William Dawes played a crucial role in alerting the colonial militia before the battles of Lexington and Concord.
Many people know about Paul Revere, but William Dawes made the same midnight ride to warn the colonists.
The ride of William Dawes is an important but often overlooked event in American Revolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paul Revere - American patriot who rode to warn of advancing British troops
