WHO

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juːˌeɪtʃˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl.juːˌeɪtʃˈəʊ / (BrE)
An agency of the United Nations responsible for coordinating international public health activities and assisting governments in improving health services.
UN agency for international public health

Examples

The WHO has declared a global health emergency in response to the outbreak.
Governments often rely on guidelines issued by the WHO for disease prevention.
Researchers submitted their findings to the WHO to help improve vaccine distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Health Organization - United Nations public health agency, usually called WHO

Etymology

Acronym of World Health Organization, established 1948.
