W.H.O.

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juː.eɪtʃ.oʊ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl.juː.eɪtʃ.əʊ / (BrE)
an international agency of the United Nations responsible for coordinating global health efforts and supporting government health services.
World Health Organization (UN agency for global health)

Examples

The W.H.O. has declared a global health emergency in response to the outbreak.
Many countries rely on guidelines from the W.H.O. to manage disease prevention.
The W.H.O. works with governments worldwide to improve health outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Health Organization - UN agency for global public health, commonly abbreviated as W.H.O.

Etymology

Abbreviation of World Health Organization, established in 1948 under the United Nations.
