coreferent

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈrɛfərənt / (AmE), / kəˈrɛfərənt / (BrE)
(of two or more expressions) referring to the same object or individual in a given context.
expressions referring to the same entity

Examples

The pronouns 'he' and 'his' are coreferent in the sentence 'John said he would bring his laptop.'
In logical analysis, two terms are coreferent if they refer to the same individual.
The detective noted that 'the victim' and 'the woman' were coreferent in the suspect's statement.

Synset Examples

two expressions are coreferent if they denote the same object or individual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaphoric - referring back to an earlier word or phrase

Etymology

From the prefix co- (together) + referent (something referred to).
