imposture

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɑst͡ʃər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒstʃə / (BrE)
the act of deceiving others by assuming a false identity or pretending to be someone else
deceptive act of pretending to be someone else

Examples

The criminal's elaborate imposture fooled everyone at the bank.
He was eventually exposed for his imposture as a wealthy businessman.
Her convincing imposture allowed her to enter the private event unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended for personal gain
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or sincere

Etymology

From French 'imposture', from Latin 'impostura' (a deception), from 'imponere' (to impose).
