diazoxide

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæzɒksaɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈazɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a vasodilator drug used to treat severe hypertension, also known by the trade name Hyperstat.
a drug for treating severe hypertension
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed diazoxide to help control the patient's dangerously high blood pressure.
In rare cases, diazoxide can cause fluid retention and should be monitored carefully.
Some hospitals keep diazoxide on hand for emergencies involving hypertensive crises.
