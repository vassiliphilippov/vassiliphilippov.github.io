chew

1. NOUN

/ tʃu / (AmE), / tʃuː / (BrE)
The act of biting and grinding food in the mouth to make it soft enough to swallow.
the act of biting and grinding food

Examples

She took a long chew of the tough steak before swallowing.
A good chew is important for proper digestion.
He savored every chew of his favorite chewing gum.

2. NOUN

/ tʃu / (AmE), / tʃuː / (BrE)
a wad of something chewable, especially a portion of tobacco prepared for chewing
wad of something chewable (esp. tobacco)

Examples

He took a large chew of tobacco and spat into the grass.
She offered me a chew of her favorite gum.
He kept a chew tucked in his cheek throughout the baseball game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quid - a portion of something, especially tobacco, held in the mouth and chewed

3. VERB

/ tʃu / (AmE), / tʃuː / (BrE)
to bite and grind food with the teeth to make it easier to swallow
bite and grind food with teeth

Examples

You should always chew your food thoroughly before swallowing.
She absentmindedly chewed on the end of her pencil during the exam.
The cows chew grass for hours every day in the pasture.

Synset Examples

He jawed his bubble gum
Chew your food and don't swallow it!
The cows were masticating the grass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to masticate - to chew (food)

Etymology

Old English *ceowan* (to chew), related to German *kauan*.
