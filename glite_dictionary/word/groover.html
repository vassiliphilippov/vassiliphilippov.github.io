groover

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːvər / (AmE), / ˈɡruːvə / (BrE)
A device or tool used for making grooves by cutting or punching.
tool for making grooves

Examples

The carpenter used a groover to create precise channels in the wood panel.
Tile installers often rely on a groover when working with vinyl flooring.
He invested in a new groover for making consistent grooves in plasterboards.

Etymology

From 'groove' + '-er', denoting an agent noun.
