deftly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɛftli / (AmE), / ˈdɛftli / (BrE)
In a skillful and quick manner; with dexterity.
in a skillful, quick way

Examples

She deftly folded the paper into an intricate origami crane.
He deftly avoided answering the tricky question during the interview.
The chef deftly chopped the vegetables with impressive speed.

Synset Examples

dextrously he untied the knots

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsily - in a way that shows lack of skill or dexterity

Etymology

From 'deft' (late Middle English, meaning gentle or meek), possibly related to Old English 'gedæfte' (mild, gentle), with '-ly' forming the adverb.

2. ADV

/ ˈdɛft.li / (AmE), / ˈdɛft.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows skill or dexterity; with quick, precise, and efficient movement.
in a skillful or dexterous manner

Examples

The magician deftly shuffled the cards, impressing the audience with his speed.
She deftly avoided the obstacles on the sidewalk without breaking her stride.
The chef deftly sliced the vegetables into thin, even strips.

Synset Examples

Lois deftly removed her scarf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skillfully - in a skillful way; with skill
  • Antonym: clumsily - in a clumsy or awkward way

Etymology

Formed from 'deft' (skillful), from Middle English 'deft', possibly from Old English 'gedæfte' (gentle, meek).

Synset POS: r
