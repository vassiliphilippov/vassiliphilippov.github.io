Trichostema dichotomum

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌkɑːstiːmə daɪˈkɑːtəməm / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒstiːmə dʌɪˈkɒtəməm / (BrE)
an aromatic plant native to the eastern United States, known for its slender stems and blue flowers
aromatic plant of eastern U.S.

Examples

Trichostema dichotomum thrives in sandy soils along the eastern coast of the United States.
The slender stems and blue flowers of Trichostema dichotomum make it popular among native plant gardeners.
Researchers studied the aromatic properties of Trichostema dichotomum for potential medicinal uses.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'stema' (stamen), referring to hairy stamens; 'dichotomum' from Greek for 'divided in two'.
