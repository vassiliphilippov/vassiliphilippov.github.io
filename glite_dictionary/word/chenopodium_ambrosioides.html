Chenopodium ambrosioides

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛnoʊˈpoʊdiəm ˌæmbrəˈsɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌkɛnəˈpəʊdiəm ˌambrəˈsɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the amaranth family with a strong odor, commonly known as epazote, used in cooking and traditional medicine.
tropical American plant (epazote)

Examples

Many traditional Mexican dishes include Chenopodium ambrosioides as a seasoning to enhance flavor.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Chenopodium ambrosioides for its potential health benefits.
The pungent aroma of Chenopodium ambrosioides can easily be recognized in local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epazote - common name for Chenopodium ambrosioides

Etymology

From Latin: 'chenopodium' (goose-foot) and 'ambrosioides' (resembling ambrosia).
