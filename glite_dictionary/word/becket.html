Becket

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(1118–1170) A leading English churchman and archbishop of Canterbury, murdered after opposing King Henry II’s efforts to restrict the powers of the church.
archbishop of Canterbury, murdered in 1170
american british archaic

Examples

Many pilgrims traveled to Canterbury Cathedral to honor Becket after his canonization.
The conflict between King Henry II and Becket had far-reaching consequences for the English church.
Alfred found a biography of Becket while studying medieval history.
