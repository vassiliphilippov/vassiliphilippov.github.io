screed

1. NOUN

/ skrid / (AmE), / skriːd / (BrE)
an accurately leveled strip of material applied to a wall or floor to guide the even application of plaster or concrete
level guide strip for plaster/concrete

Examples

The builder placed a screed on the floor to ensure the concrete was level.
Before plastering the wall, a screed was fixed along its length for accuracy.
A metal screed can significantly improve the finish of a cement floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - something that serves to direct or indicate

2. NOUN

/ skrid / (AmE), / skriːd / (BrE)
a long, often monotonous piece of writing or speech, typically one regarded as tedious or ranting
long, tedious piece of writing or speech

Examples

After reading his endless screed on bureaucracy, I lost interest halfway through.
She published a lengthy screed criticizing the government’s handling of the crisis.
The internet is full of angry screeds about recent political events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
  • Synonym: rant - a long, passionate, or angry speech

Etymology

From Middle English 'screde', from Old English 'scrēade' (strip, shred), related to 'shred'.

3. NOUN

/ skrid / (AmE), / skriːd / (BrE)
a long, tedious piece of writing or speech, usually spoken or written in a critical or complaining manner
long monotonous speech or writing

Examples

He delivered a lengthy screed about the government's failures that bored everyone.
The editor returned my article, calling it a pointless screed rather than a news piece.
After ten minutes of her screed, most of the audience had tuned out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harangue - a lengthy and aggressive speech
  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
  • Synonym: rant - a lengthy, loud, or angry speech

Etymology

Probably from Old English *scrēad* (a strip or fragment), later applied to writing or discourse.
