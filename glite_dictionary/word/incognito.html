incognito

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌkɑːɡˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒɡˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
concealing one's identity so as not to be recognized
with identity concealed

Examples

The famous actor traveled incognito to avoid attracting attention from fans.
She entered the party incognito, wearing a wig and sunglasses so no one would recognize her.
Going incognito helped the journalist get an unbiased view of the situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymous - with a name or identity that is not known

Etymology

From Italian 'incognito' (unknown), from Latin 'incognitus' (unknown, unrecognized).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˌkɑːɡˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒɡˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
in a way that conceals one's identity; without revealing who one truly is
concealing one's identity

Examples

The famous actor traveled incognito to avoid the paparazzi.
She often goes incognito when visiting crowded public places.
Detectives sometimes work incognito to gather information for their cases.

Synset Examples

in Holland he lived incognito as a carpenter in the shipyards of the East India company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymous - not identified by name; of unknown name

Etymology

From Italian incognito (unknown), from Latin incognitus, meaning 'unknown'.
