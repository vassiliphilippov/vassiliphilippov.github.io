inefficient

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnəˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
lacking the ability or skill to achieve desired results with minimum effort, time, or resources; not effective or productive.
not achieving effective results; not productive

Examples

The company's outdated machines made the entire process inefficient.
Because of his inefficient approach, the project was delayed by several weeks.
New management replaced the inefficient workers to improve productivity.

Synset Examples

an ineffective administration
inefficient workers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efficient - able to achieve desired results with little wasted effort or resources
  • Synonym: effective - successful in producing a desired result

Etymology

From Latin 'inefficientem', from 'in-' (not) + 'efficientem' (producing, effective).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnəˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
Not working in a well-organized or productive way; wasting time, energy, or resources.
not effective or wasteful

Examples

The old heating system is inefficient and wastes a lot of energy.
Government bureaucracy can sometimes be slow and inefficient.
Because of its inefficient design, the machine requires frequent repairs.

Synset Examples

an inefficient campaign against drugs
outdated and inefficient design and methods

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efficient - working in a well-organized and productive way

Etymology

From in- (not) + efficient, from Latin efficiens (accomplishing, effective).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00844441-a
Synset POS: a
