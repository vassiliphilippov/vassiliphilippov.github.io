traitorously

1. ADV

/ ˈtreɪt̬ɚəsli / (AmE), / ˈtreɪtərəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows betrayal or disloyalty.
in a disloyal or betraying manner

Examples

He acted traitorously, betraying his closest friends for personal gain.
The prisoner had escaped with help from guards who behaved traitorously.
Accused of plotting traitorously against the king, he faced prison.

Synset Examples

he behaved treacherously
his wife played him false

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treacherously - in a manner that betrays trust

Etymology

From 'traitorous' + '-ly'; 'traitorous' from Anglo-French *traitour* (traitor).
