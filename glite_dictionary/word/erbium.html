erbium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜr.bi.əm / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bi.əm / (BrE)
a silvery-white metallic chemical element of the rare earth group, symbol Er, used in alloys and as a doping agent in lasers and optical fibers
a rare earth metallic chemical element

Examples

The glass was doped with erbium to improve its optical properties.
Researchers discovered a new use for erbium in telecommunications technology.
The mineral sample contained traces of erbium and other rare earth elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yttrium - another rare earth element found with erbium

Etymology

Named after Ytterby, a village in Sweden where its ore was first discovered.
