fatherless

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑðɚləs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðələs / (BrE)
Having no living father.
having no living father

Examples

After her father's death, she was left fatherless at a young age.
The charity supports fatherless children in the community.
Growing up fatherless shaped his outlook on life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orphan - a child whose parents are dead
  • Synonym: motherless - having no living mother

Etymology

From Middle English fatherles, from Old English fæderlēas.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑðɚlɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðəlɪs / (BrE)
Having no living, known, or legally responsible father.
without a known or responsible father

Examples

After his father died in the war, the boy grew up fatherless.
The charity focuses on helping fatherless children in underprivileged areas.
She felt fatherless after her father's abandonment, despite having family support.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orphan - a child whose parents are dead

Etymology

From 'father' + '-less' (Old English and Germanic roots denoting 'without').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01411107-s
Synset POS: s
