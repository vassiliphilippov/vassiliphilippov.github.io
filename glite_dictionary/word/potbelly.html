potbelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌbɛli / (BrE)
A rounded, protruding stomach, especially one that is large or fat.
rounded, protruding stomach
informal

Examples

After too many late-night snacks, he developed a noticeable potbelly.
The old man patted his potbelly and laughed heartily.
Regular exercise helped her get rid of her potbelly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paunch - a large or protruding belly

Etymology

From 'pot' (a rounded cooking vessel) + 'belly', likening the stomach shape to a pot.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌbɛli / (BrE)
a type of stove with a rounded, bulging body, traditionally made of cast iron, used for burning wood or coal for heat
bulbous cast-iron stove

Examples

A cast iron potbelly stood in the corner, warming the small cabin all winter.
They cooked beans on the old potbelly stove in the general store.
The antique potbelly has become a collector's item in rural farmhouses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stove - an apparatus that burns fuel or uses electricity to provide heat

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a rounded or bulging belly; 'pot' + 'belly'.
