designatum

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌzaɪˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / dɪˌzaɪˈnɑːtəm / (BrE)
A thing or entity—real or imagined—that is referred to or named by a linguistic expression.
entity referred to by an expression

Examples

The designatum of the word 'unicorn' does not exist in the real world.
Philosophers debate whether a fictional character can have a genuine designatum.
In semantics, the designatum may refer to something purely hypothetical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referent - the actual thing or entity a word or expression refers to

Etymology

From Latin designatum, neuter of designatus, past participle of designare (to designate).
