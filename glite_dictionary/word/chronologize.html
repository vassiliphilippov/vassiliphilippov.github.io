to chronologize

1. VERB

/ krəˈnɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / krəˈnɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or establish the order of events in the sequence in which they occurred in time
establish the order of events in time

Examples

The historian needed to chronologize the major events of the war to provide a clear timeline.
After finding several old diaries, she worked carefully to chronologize the family history.
In order to solve the mystery, the detective tried to chronologize every clue they had gathered.

Synset Examples

The archivist chronologized the documents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronological - arranged in the order of time
  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which related events or things follow each other

Etymology

From Greek 'chronos' (time) + English '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).
