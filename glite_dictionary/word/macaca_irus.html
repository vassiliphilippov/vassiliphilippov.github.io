Macaca irus

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑːkə ˈaɪrəs / (AmE), / məˈkɑːkə ˈɪərəs / (BrE)
a species of monkey native to Southeast Asia, Borneo, and the Philippines, also known as the long-tailed macaque or crab-eating macaque.
a Southeast Asian monkey species

Examples

The Macaca irus is commonly found in mangrove forests throughout Indonesia.
Scientists observed a group of Macaca irus using tools to open shells for food.
In the Philippines, the Macaca irus often interacts with local communities near coastal villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-tailed macaque - another common name for Macaca irus
  • Degree Lower: macaque - any primate of the genus Macaca, to which Macaca irus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'Macaca' (genus name for macaques) and specific epithet 'irus'.
