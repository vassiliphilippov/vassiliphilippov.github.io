absentee

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.sənˈti / (AmE), / ˌæb.sənˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who is not present at a place where they are expected or required to be
someone who is absent

Examples

The manager was disappointed to see so many absentees at the meeting.
Repeated absentee workers may face disciplinary action under the new policy.
The teacher marked each absentee in the register before starting the lesson.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present - being at a place; not absent

Etymology

From Latin 'absens', present participle of 'abesse' (to be away).
