supercritical

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a substance or system, especially nuclear material) able to sustain a chain reaction such that the rate of reaction increases rapidly.
able to sustain an increasing chain reaction

Examples

The reactor became supercritical when the control rods were removed too quickly.
If a mass of uranium is supercritical, the resulting reaction can lead to an explosion.
Scientists carefully monitor the conditions to ensure the system does not become supercritical.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subcritical - not able to sustain a chain reaction; reaction dies out
  • Degree Lower: critical - at the minimum state required to sustain a chain reaction

Etymology

From prefix super- (above, beyond) + critical, referring to exceeding the critical point needed for a reaction.
