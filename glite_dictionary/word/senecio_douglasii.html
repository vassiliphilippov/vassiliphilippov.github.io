Senecio douglasii

1. PROPN

/ səˈniːsiˌoʊ ˌdʌɡˈlæsi.aɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈniːsɪəʊ ˌdʌɡˈlæsi.aɪ / (BrE)
A bushy, bluish-green plant covered with white wool, bearing branched clusters of yellow flower heads; native to the southwestern United States and known to be toxic to livestock.
a bushy, woolly southwestern U.S. plant, toxic to livestock

Examples

Senecio douglasii is commonly found in sandy soils across the southwestern United States.
Ranchers are cautious of Senecio douglasii because it is toxic to cattle and sheep.
The bluish-green leaves and woolly texture make Senecio douglasii easily identifiable in the wild.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *senecio* (old man), alluding to the white hairs on the seeds; species named after Scottish botanist David Douglas.
