salsilla

1. NOUN

/ sælˈsiːjə / (AmE), / sælˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical vine native to the West Indies and northern South America, having pink-and-yellow flowers with purple spots and edible roots sometimes used as a potato substitute.
tropical vine with edible roots

Examples

In rural villages, the roots of the salsilla are often boiled instead of potatoes.
The rare salsilla vine displays beautiful pink-and-yellow flowers with spotted purple markings.
Chefs in South America may use salsilla in traditional dishes due to its edible roots.

2. NOUN

/ sælˈsiːjə / (AmE), / sælˈsiːjə / (BrE)
A tropical vine native to Colombia, bearing clusters of small purple flowers and edible roots sometimes used as a potato substitute.
tropical vine with edible roots, Colombia

Examples

In rural Colombian markets, you can often find salsilla sold alongside potatoes and yucca.
The chef prepared a hearty stew using salsilla roots as a flavorful base.
People sometimes boil salsilla roots and serve them as a substitute for potatoes.

Etymology

From Spanish 'salsilla', diminutive of 'salsa' (sauce), likely referring to culinary use.
