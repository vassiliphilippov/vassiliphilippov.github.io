Veratrum

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrætrəm / (AmE), / vəˈrætrəm / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, poisonous perennial herbs belonging to the lily family, native to temperate regions.
genus of poisonous perennial herbs

Examples

Veratrum species are highly toxic and should not be ingested under any circumstances.
The botanist collected samples of Veratrum from the mountain meadows for further study.
Traditional medicines once used extracts of Veratrum, despite the plant's dangerous toxicity.

Etymology

From Latin 'veratrum', meaning 'hellebore', used for this genus in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ vəˈrætrəm / (AmE), / vəˈratrəm / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, poisonous perennial herbs found in temperate regions, sometimes placed in the subfamily Melanthiaceae.
genus of poisonous perennial herbs

Examples

Some species in the Veratrum genus are known for their toxic properties.
Botanists have debated the classification of Veratrum within the Melanthiaceae family.
The medicinal use of Veratrum requires caution due to its poisonous compounds.

Etymology

From Latin *veratrum*, meaning 'hellebore'.
