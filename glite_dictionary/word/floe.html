floe

1. NOUN

/ floʊ / (AmE), / fləʊ / (BrE)
a large, flat sheet of floating ice, usually smaller than an ice field, found on the surface of the sea or a lake
large, flat floating ice sheet

Examples

During the Arctic expedition, the ship was trapped between a massive floe and the shoreline.
Seals often rest on a floe while hunting for fish beneath the ice.
The scientist measured the thickness of the floe before collecting water samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iceberg - a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier

Etymology

From Norwegian 'flo' meaning 'flat layer' or Swedish 'flö' meaning 'thin layer of ice'.
