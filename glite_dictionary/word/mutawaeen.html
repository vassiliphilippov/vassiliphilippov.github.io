Mutawa'een

1. NOUN

/ muːtəˈwɑːin / (AmE), / muːtəˈwɑːiːn / (BrE)
religious police in Saudi Arabia responsible for enforcing Islamic codes of conduct and public morality.
Saudi Arabia's religious police

Examples

The Mutawa'een patrol public spaces to ensure that everyone complies with Saudi Arabia's strict moral codes.
Foreign visitors are sometimes surprised by the presence and authority of the Mutawa'een in Saudi Arabia.
The role of the Mutawa'een has been reduced in recent years, but they still maintain a visible presence in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: religious police - an enforcement body monitoring religious observance and morality

Etymology

From Arabic مُطَوَّعِين‎ (*muṭawwaʕīn*), plural of *muṭawwaʕ* (one who volunteers for religious duty).
