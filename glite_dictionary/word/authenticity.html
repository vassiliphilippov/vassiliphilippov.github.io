authenticity

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌθɛnˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɛnˈtɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being genuine or real, and able to be believed or accepted as true
genuineness; credibility; realness

Examples

The authenticity of the ancient manuscript was confirmed by experts.
Many collectors value authenticity over rarity when choosing pieces for their collections.
Before investing, she questioned the authenticity of the business’s claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuineness - the quality of being genuine; authenticity
  • Antonym: falseness - the state of being untrue or insincere

Etymology

From Medieval Latin authenticitas (genuineness, authenticity), from Greek authentikos (genuine, principle).
