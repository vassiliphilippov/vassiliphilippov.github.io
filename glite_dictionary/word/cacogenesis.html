cacogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkækoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkakəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
The inability to produce hybrids that are both viable and fertile.
failure to produce viable, fertile hybrids

Examples

Many animal species exhibit cacogenesis, preventing the successful breeding of interspecies hybrids.
Horticulturists often struggle with cacogenesis when trying to cross certain plant varieties.
The principle of cacogenesis ensures that some hybrid offspring do not threaten the integrity of species.

Etymology

From Greek kakos (bad) + genesis (origin, birth).
