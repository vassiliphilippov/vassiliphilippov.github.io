peninsula

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / pɪˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
A large area of land that is almost completely surrounded by water but connected to the mainland on one side.
land almost surrounded by water

Examples

The Iberian peninsula contains both Spain and Portugal.
Several cities are located on the Florida peninsula.
We explored the beautiful beaches along the peninsula's rugged coastline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: island - a piece of land completely surrounded by water

Etymology

From Latin 'paeninsula', from 'paene' (almost) + 'insula' (island).
