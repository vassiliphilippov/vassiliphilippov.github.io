tizzy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪzi / (AmE), / ˈtɪzi / (BrE)
a state of nervous excitement or agitation, typically over something not very important
a state of nervous excitement or fuss
informal

Examples

She got herself into a tizzy over what to wear to the party.
The news sent the whole office into a tizzy.
There's no need to get in a tizzy about such a small mistake.

Synset Examples

he was in a dither
there was a terrible flap about the theft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dither - a state of indecisive agitation
  • Synonym: flap - a state of panic or fuss

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the 1930s in American English.
