Hydrastis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdræstɪs / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrastɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs with thick rhizomes, palmate leaves, and solitary flowers, native to northeastern United States and Japan.
a genus of perennial herbs

Examples

Hydrastis is commonly known as goldenseal and valued for its medicinal properties.
Researchers have studied the antimicrobial effects of Hydrastis extracts.
In her garden, she cultivated several species, including Hydrastis.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek hydor (water) and drasis (action), referring to the plant's medicinal properties.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈdræstɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɑːstɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs with rhizomes, palmate leaves, and small solitary flowers, native to northeastern United States and Japan.
genus of perennial herbs

Examples

Hydrastis is best known for its species Hydrastis canadensis, commonly called goldenseal.
Botanists have studied several medicinal properties of plants within the Hydrastis genus.
The distribution of Hydrastis includes forests in the northeastern United States and parts of Japan.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydrastis,' the name used in botanical Latin.
