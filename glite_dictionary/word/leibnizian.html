Leibnizian

1. ADJ

/ ˌlaɪbˈnɪtsiən / (AmE), / ˌlaɪbˈnɪtsiən / (BrE)
relating to Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz, his mathematics, or his philosophy.
relating to Leibniz or his philosophy

Examples

Her argument follows a distinctly Leibnizian approach to the nature of reality.
He published a book on Leibnizian metaphysics in the eighteenth century.
Many debates in modern logic can be traced back to Leibnizian ideas.

Etymology

Formed from the name of Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646–1716), German mathematician and philosopher, with the suffix -ian.
