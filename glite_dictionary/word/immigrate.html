to immigrate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪməˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to come to live permanently in a country that is not your own
come to settle in a new country

Examples

After years of waiting, her family finally immigrated to Canada.
Many scientists immigrate to the United States to pursue research opportunities.
She immigrated as a child and quickly adapted to her new environment.

Synset Examples

Many people immigrated at the beginning of the 20th century

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to emigrate - to leave one's country to settle in another
  • Synonym: to migrate - to move from one region to another, often temporarily or periodically

Etymology

From Latin immigrare, meaning 'to go into, move in'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɪməˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to come to a country or place to live there permanently
come to live in a new country

Examples

After years of planning, her family finally decided to immigrate to Canada.
Many people immigrate to countries with better job opportunities.
The new policies made it easier for skilled workers to immigrate legally.

Synset Examples

only few plants can immigrate to the island

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to emigrate - to leave one country and settle in another
  • Synonym: to migrate - to move from one region or habitat to another

Etymology

From Latin 'immigrare' meaning 'to go into'.
