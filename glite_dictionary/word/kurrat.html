kurrat

1. NOUN

/ kʊˈrɑt / (AmE), / kʊˈrɑːt / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of leek native to Egypt and the eastern Mediterranean, grown for its edible leaves rather than its bulb.
cultivated variety of leek for edible leaves

Examples

Farmers in Egypt commonly grow kurrat for its flavorful leaves, which are used in traditional cooking.
The chef sprinkled chopped kurrat over the soup to add a mild onion flavor.
Unlike leeks, kurrat is primarily harvested for its abundant green leaves rather than its bulb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leek - an edible plant related to onion, with long white stalks and green leaves

Etymology

From Arabic 'kurrāth' (leek).
