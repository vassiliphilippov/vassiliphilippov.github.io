iodoform

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊdəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊdəˌfɔːm / (BrE)
a yellowish crystalline compound with a strong odor, sometimes used as an antiseptic dressing
yellowish antiseptic compound
medical

Examples

The wound was treated with iodoform to prevent infection.
A faint medicinal smell of iodoform lingered in the clinic.
Historically, iodoform was widely used as a topical antiseptic.

Etymology

From French 'iodoforme', from 'iode' (iodine) and 'forme' (form), referring to its chemical composition.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊdəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊdəˌfɔːm / (BrE)
a yellow, sweet-smelling crystalline solid compound (CHI₃), formerly used as an antiseptic
yellow sweet-smelling crystalline chemical (CHI₃)
medical

Examples

The wound was treated with iodoform to prevent infection.
Laboratory shelves held bottles of chemicals, including a jar of iodoform.
Iodoform has a characteristic medicinal odor that is easily recognized.

Etymology

From French iodoforme, from 'iode' (iodine) and 'forme' (form), indicating its iodine content and relation to formic acid.
