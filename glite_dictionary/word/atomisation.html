atomisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæt̬əməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌætəməˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of reducing something to atoms or fine particles; annihilation or disintegration to an extremely small scale.
process of reducing to atoms or fine particles
british

Examples

The atomisation of the solid substance left virtually nothing behind.
Modern warfare saw the atomisation of entire cities by new weapons.
The process led to the atomisation of the compound, rendering it completely unrecognizable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomization - process of breaking something into atoms or fine particles (US spelling)

Etymology

From atomize + -ation; ultimately from Greek atomos (indivisible).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæt̬əˌmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌætəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of breaking something into very fine particles or droplets, often by spraying or dispersing.
separation into fine particles or droplets
british

Examples

The atomisation of the fuel ensures thorough combustion.
Modern inhalers rely on the atomisation of medication for effective delivery to the lungs.
Advances in atomisation technology have improved paint spraying methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomization - American spelling of atomisation

Etymology

From atom + -isation (British spelling of atomization), ultimately from Greek 'atomos' (indivisible).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
