hatchery

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃ.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈhætʃ.ər.i / (BrE)
a place where eggs are artificially incubated and hatched, especially those of fish or poultry
place where eggs are artificially hatched

Examples

The conservationists released thousands of trout from the hatchery into the river.
Several fish farms rely on a hatchery to maintain their stock.
The new hatchery boosted the region’s aquaculture industry.

Synset Examples

the park authorities operated a trout hatchery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incubator - a device or place used to keep eggs or organisms in conditions favorable for development

Etymology

From 'hatch' (to come out from an egg) + '-ery' (denoting a place for a particular activity).
