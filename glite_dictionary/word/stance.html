stance

1. NOUN

/ stæns / (AmE), / stɑːns / (BrE)
The way in which someone stands, especially when deliberately adopted for a particular purpose.
body posture or way of standing

Examples

The coach corrected his stance to improve his balance.
Her defensive stance helped her block the opponent's attack.
You should adopt a wider stance when lifting heavy weights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posture - the position in which someone holds their body

Etymology

From Old French *estance* (standing, position), from Latin *stantia* (a standing, station).

2. NOUN

/ stæns / (AmE), / stɑːns / (BrE)
a way of thinking about something; a mental attitude or position taken on an issue
attitude or position on an issue

Examples

Her political stance on climate change is well known.
He adopted a firm stance against corruption in the company.
The government's stance on education has changed in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: position - a point of view or attitude about something
  • Synonym: posture - a particular way of dealing with or considering something; an approach or attitude
  • Synonym: opinion - a belief or judgment about something not necessarily based on fact or knowledge

Etymology

From Scottish English 'stance' (standing place), related to Old French 'estans', from Latin 'stantia' (standing).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
