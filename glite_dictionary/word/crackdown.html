crackdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrækˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkræk.daʊn / (BrE)
a series of severe or repressive actions taken to enforce laws or regulations, often to suppress undesirable activities
severe enforcement actions

Examples

The government announced a crackdown on illegal gambling operations across the city.
There was an intense crackdown on protesters during the demonstration.
Police launched a crackdown this week aimed at reducing street crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clampdown - a sudden restriction or suppression of undesirable activities

Etymology

Formed from 'crack' (as in 'to take forceful action') + 'down', mid-20th century.
