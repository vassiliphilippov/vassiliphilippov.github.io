Bennettitales

1. NOUN

/ bəˌnɛtɪˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / bəˌnɛtɪˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of fossil gymnospermous plants, mainly from the Carboniferous period, known for their palm-like appearance
an extinct order of fossil gymnosperms

Examples

The discovery of Bennettitales fossils helped scientists understand plant evolution during the Mesozoic era.
Many Bennettitales specimens have been found alongside dinosaur remains in ancient rock formations.
Because Bennettitales resembled modern cycads, they were once mistakenly classified as members of that group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a related order of gymnosperms with palm-like leaves, still existing today

Etymology

From the genus name Bennettites, named after British botanist John Joseph Bennett, plus the standard botanical order suffix '-ales'.
