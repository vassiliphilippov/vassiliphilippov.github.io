raggedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having an irregular, uneven, or rough surface or edge
irregular or uneven surface or edge

Examples

The raggedness of the old fence made it look abandoned and unsafe.
She could feel the raggedness of the torn fabric against her skin.
The mountain path was difficult to navigate due to the raggedness of the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth or even

Etymology

From 'ragged' + '-ness', where 'ragged' comes from Middle English, related to 'rag' (a torn piece of cloth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being torn, shabby, or dressed in rags
state of being ragged or shabby

Examples

The raggedness of his clothes made it clear that he had fallen on hard times.
Despite the raggedness of their appearance, the children were smiling and cheerful.
The raggedness of the blankets offered little protection from the cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabbiness - the quality of being in poor condition through long use or lack of care

Etymology

From 'ragged' + '-ness', indicating the state or quality of being ragged.
