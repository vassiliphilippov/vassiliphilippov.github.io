Pilea microphylla

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈliːə ˌmaɪkrəˈfɪlə / (AmE), / paɪˈliːə ˌmaɪkrəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the nettle family that is stingless and known for its explosive release of pollen.
species of tropical American nettle

Examples

The Pilea microphylla is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage.
Gardeners enjoy the unique pollen mechanism of Pilea microphylla, which can surprise the uninitiated.
In tropical climates, Pilea microphylla can spread rapidly in shaded areas.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pilea' (genus name), from Latin 'pileus' (felt cap); 'microphylla' from Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
