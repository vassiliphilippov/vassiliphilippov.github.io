feterita

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtɛrɪtə / (AmE), / fəˈtɛrɪtə / (BrE)
a variety of sorghum from Sudan that has unusually large, soft white grains
Sudanese sorghum with large white grains

Examples

Farmers in the region prefer growing feterita because of its high yield and large grains.
The bread made from feterita flour has a distinctive soft texture.
Researchers are studying feterita to develop improved sorghum varieties suitable for dry climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a cereal grass cultivated for its grain, used as food and fodder
