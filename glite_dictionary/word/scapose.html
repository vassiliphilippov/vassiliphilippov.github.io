scapose

1. ADJ

/ ˈskeɪ.poʊs / (AmE), / ˈskeɪ.pəʊs / (BrE)
(of a plant or plant part) having a long, bare, leafless stalk (scape) rising directly from the ground.
having a bare, leafless stalk

Examples

Many lilies produce scapose stems that rise directly from bulbs beneath the soil.
Dandelions are known for their scapose flower stalks.
The scapose structure of certain wildflowers distinguishes them from leafy-stemmed varieties.

Synset Examples

tulips are scapose flowers
a scapose stalk
a scapose column
the scapose portion of a feather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acaulescent - (of a plant) having no visible stem above ground

Etymology

From Latin scapōsus, from scapus meaning 'stalk'.
