ejection

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of expelling or forcing someone or something out; the process of ejecting
the act of expelling or ejecting

Examples

The pilot's quick ejection from the cockpit saved his life during the crash.
After the brawl, his ejection from the stadium was inevitable.
The volcano's sudden ejection of ash surprised the nearby residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave a place
  • Antonym: insertion - the act of putting something into something else

Etymology

From Latin 'ejectio', from 'eicere' meaning 'to throw out'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of forcing someone or something out; expulsion
the act of forcing out

Examples

The referee ordered the ejection of two players for unsportsmanlike conduct.
During the emergency, the pilot initiated the ejection procedure to escape the aircraft safely.
The ejection of unauthorized visitors from the premises was handled quickly by security staff.

Synset Examples

the ejection of troublemakers by the police
the child's expulsion from school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the act of depriving someone of membership or involvement in a group
  • Antonym: inclusion - the action or state of including or being included within a group or structure

Etymology

From Latin ejectio (a throwing out), from eicere (to cast out).
