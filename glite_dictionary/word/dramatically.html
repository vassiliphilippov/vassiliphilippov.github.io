dramatically

1. ADV

/ drəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to drama or theatrical effect; with a quality that is striking, intense, or evokes strong emotion.
in a dramatic or theatrical manner

Examples

Her performance changed dramatically from act to act, keeping the audience engaged.
The room was dramatically lit, creating an intriguing atmosphere for the scene.
The story is dramatically different in the movie adaptation than in the original novel.

Synset Examples

the play was dramatically interesting, but the direction was bad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrically - in a theatrical or showy manner
  • Antonym: subtly - in a subtle or understated manner
Source: WordNet

2. ADV

/ drəˈmæt̬ɪkli / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is very impressive, noticeable, or exciting
in a very impressive or striking way

Examples

Her health improved dramatically after the new treatment started.
Sales increased dramatically during the holiday season last year.
The weather changed dramatically within a matter of hours.

Synset Examples

your performance will improve dramatically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressively - in a way that evokes admiration or respect
  • Antonym: modestly - in a way that is not large, impressive, or extreme

Etymology

From 'dramatic' + '-ally', with 'dramatic' from Greek 'dramatikos' (pertaining to drama).

3. ADV

/ drəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪkli / (BrE)
In a dramatic or striking manner; in a way that is intended to attract attention or produce a strong effect.
in a dramatic manner

Examples

The stock market fell dramatically after the announcement of the new policy.
She entered the room dramatically, capturing everyone's attention at once.
Rainfall has increased dramatically in this region over the last decade.

Synset Examples

he confessed dramatically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrically - in a theatrical manner, resembling theatrical performance
  • Antonym: subtly - in a subtle way; not obvious or showy

Etymology

From 'dramatic' + '-ally', formed from Greek 'dramatikos' (relating to drama).

Synset POS: r
