grosz

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊʃ / (AmE), / ɡrɒʃ / (BrE)
A monetary unit of Poland, equal to one one-hundredth of a zloty.
Polish monetary unit (1/100 zloty)

Examples

He found a single grosz in his pocket and smiled at the memory of his trip to Poland.
The cashier gave me my change in groszy, which I kept as a souvenir.
In Poland, prices are often written to the nearest grosz.

Etymology

From Polish 'grosz', itself from Middle High German 'groschen', a type of coin.
