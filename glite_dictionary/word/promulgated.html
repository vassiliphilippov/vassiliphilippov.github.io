promulgated

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.məl.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.əl.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
formally declared or made public, especially by an official or authoritative source
formally made public

Examples

The new law was promulgated by the government last year.
Regulations are only effective once they have been promulgated in an official bulletin.
A promulgated policy ensures all stakeholders are aware of the new procedures.

Synset Examples

published accounts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: published - officially announced or made available to the public

Etymology

From Latin 'promulgare' (to make known publicly, to promulgate) via late Middle English.
