carbomycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrboʊˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbəʊˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
a colorless basic antibiotic that inhibits the growth of Gram-positive bacteria, used in clinical and research settings
antibiotic effective against Gram-positive bacteria
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed carbomycin to treat the resistant infection.
Researchers are studying the effects of carbomycin on various bacterial strains.
Carbomycin is classified as a macrolide antibiotic.
