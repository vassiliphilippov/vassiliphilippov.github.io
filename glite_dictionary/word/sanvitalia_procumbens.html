Sanvitalia procumbens

1. NOUN

/ sænˌvɪtəliə prəˈkʌmbənz / (AmE), / sanˌvɪtəliə prəˈkʌmbənz / (BrE)
A low, spreading annual plant with leafy branches and flower heads resembling zinnias, native to the southwestern United States, Mexico, and Guatemala.
a low, spreading annual plant with zinnia-like flowers

Examples

Sanvitalia procumbens is a popular choice for ground cover in rock gardens.
The bright yellow flowers of Sanvitalia procumbens attract bees and butterflies throughout the summer.
Gardeners in the Southwest often plant Sanvitalia procumbens for its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms.

Etymology

From the genus name Sanvitalia and Latin procumbens ('lying down, trailing').
