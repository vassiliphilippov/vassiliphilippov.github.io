sow

1. NOUN

/ saʊ / (AmE), / saʊ / (BrE)
an adult female pig, especially one that is kept for breeding
adult female pig

Examples

The sow gave birth to a large litter of piglets on the farm.
Farmers often separate the sow from other pigs before she farrows.
The veterinarian examined the sow to ensure she was healthy during her pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boar - an adult male pig

Etymology

Old English *sugu*, from Proto-Germanic *sugō* (female pig).

2. VERB

/ soʊ / (AmE), / səʊ / (BrE)
to plant seeds in or on the ground for future growth
plant seeds

Examples

In spring, farmers sow wheat in their fields.
If you sow the seeds too deeply, they may not sprout.
She sowed wildflowers along the border of the garden.

Synset Examples

She sowed sunflower seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - to put seeds or plants in the ground to grow

Etymology

From Old English *sawan*, from Proto-Germanic *sæaną*, from Proto-Indo-European *seh₁- (to sow)
