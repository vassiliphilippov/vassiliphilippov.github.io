gonioscopy

1. NOUN

/ ˌgoʊniˈɑːskəpi / (AmE), / ˌgəʊniˈɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical examination of the front part of the eye, especially to inspect the angle where the iris meets the cornea, used to diagnose types of glaucoma
eye exam to inspect iris-cornea angle for glaucoma
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist performed a gonioscopy to determine the type of glaucoma the patient had.
A detailed gonioscopy can reveal abnormalities in the drainage angle of the eye.
Doctors may recommend a gonioscopy if you are at risk for angle-closure glaucoma.

Etymology

From Greek *gōnia* (angle) + *-scopy* (to look or examine).
