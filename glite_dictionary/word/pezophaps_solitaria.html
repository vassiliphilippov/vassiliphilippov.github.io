Pezophaps solitaria

1. NOUN

/ pɛzəˈfæps soʊˈlɪtəriə / (AmE), / pɛzəˈfæps səˈlɪtəriə / (BrE)
an extinct flightless bird related to the dodo that was native to Rodrigues Island in the Indian Ocean
extinct flightless bird related to the dodo

Examples

Fossils of the Pezophaps solitaria have provided insight into the evolution of flightless birds.
The Pezophaps solitaria was once found only on Rodrigues Island before it became extinct in the 18th century.
Researchers believe that the Pezophaps solitaria was closely related to the dodo of Mauritius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodo - an extinct flightless bird of Mauritius, closely related to Pezophaps solitaria

Etymology

From the scientific Latin binomial; 'Pezophaps' from Greek 'pezos' (walking) and 'phaps' (pigeon), 'solitaria' from Latin (solitary).
