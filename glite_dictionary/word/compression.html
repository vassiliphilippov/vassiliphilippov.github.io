compression

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of increasing the density of something by pressing or squeezing it together
increase in density by pressing together

Examples

The compression of the material caused it to become much denser.
Sound waves travel faster in a medium with high compression.
Engineers study how the compression of gases affects performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decompression - the process of reducing pressure or density

Etymology

From Latin 'compressio', from 'comprimere' meaning 'to press together'.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of making something smaller or being pressed together, especially by force
process of being made smaller by pressure

Examples

The doctor explained that the pain was due to nerve compression in her spine.
File compression reduces the size of digital data for easier storage and transfer.
During the experiment, increasing compression caused the gas to become significantly denser.

Synset Examples

the contraction of a gas on cooling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraction - the process of becoming smaller or shorter
  • Antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From Latin 'compressio', from 'comprimere' (to press together).

3. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of pressing or squeezing something so that it occupies less space
the act of pressing or squeezing

Examples

The compression of the gas caused it to heat up rapidly.
Doctors used an elastic bandage to provide compression and reduce swelling.
The engine works by the compression of air and fuel before ignition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decompression - the process of releasing pressure or expanding to previous volume
  • Synonym: compaction - the process of making something more compact by pressing

Etymology

From Latin 'compressio', from 'comprimere' (to press together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00357616-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of encoding information using fewer bits or less bandwidth, especially to reduce data size for storage or transmission
reducing data size by encoding

Examples

The video file was too large to send by email, so she used compression to make it smaller.
Modern web browsers support image compression to help websites load faster.
Without proper compression, audio files can quickly fill up your device’s memory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decompression - restoring data to its original form after compression
  • Synonym: encoding - converting data into a particular form

Etymology

From Latin 'comprimere' (to press together).
