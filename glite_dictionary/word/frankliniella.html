Frankliniella

1. NOUN

/ fræŋk.lɪˈniː.lə / (AmE), / fraŋk.lɪˈniː.lɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small insects known as thrips, including the tobacco thrips, which are common plant pests.
genus of thrips (insects)

Examples

The greenhouse was infested with Frankliniella that damaged the young tomato plants.
Pesticides are sometimes used to control Frankliniella populations in commercial crops.
Researchers found that Frankliniella can transmit viruses to a variety of plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - tiny, slender insects, many of which are plant pests

Etymology

Named after American entomologist H. Franklin (1854–1922).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfræŋklɪˈnɪlə / (AmE), / ˌfraŋklɪˈnɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of small insects in the family Thripidae, commonly known as thrips, which includes species such as the tobacco thrips.
a genus of thrips insects

Examples

The tobacco thrips, a member of the Frankliniella genus, is a common pest in southern crops.
Researchers identified several new species within the Frankliniella group.
Damage caused by Frankliniella can be significant for tobacco farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - tiny, slender insects that feed on plants

Etymology

Named after American entomologist H. T. Franklin.
