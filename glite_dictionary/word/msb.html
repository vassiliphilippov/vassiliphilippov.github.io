MSB

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ɛs bi / (AmE), / ɛm ɛs bi / (BrE)
A state-chartered savings bank that is owned by its depositors and managed by a board of trustees.
mutual savings bank (finance)

Examples

The local MSB has served our community for over fifty years.
Depositors have a voice in the operations of an MSB through representation on the board of trustees.
Unlike commercial banks, an MSB is owned by its depositors rather than shareholders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual savings bank - a type of bank owned by its depositors

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'mutual savings bank'.
