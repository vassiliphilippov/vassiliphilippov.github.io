James Augustus Murray

1. PROPN

/ dʒeɪmz ɔːˈɡʌstəs ˈmɜːri / (AmE), / dʒeɪmz ɔːˈɡʌstəs ˈmʌri / (BrE)
(1837–1915) Scottish philologist and lexicographer who served as the principal editor of the first Oxford English Dictionary.
Scottish lexicographer, chief editor of OED

Examples

James Augustus Murray devoted decades of his life to compiling the Oxford English Dictionary.
The contributions of James Augustus Murray greatly shaped the field of English lexicography.
Historians often credit James Augustus Murray for establishing rigorous standards in dictionary editing.
