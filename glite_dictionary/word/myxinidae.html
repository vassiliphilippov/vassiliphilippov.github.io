Myxinidae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mɪkˈsɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of slime-producing, jawless marine fish known as hagfishes.
family of slime-producing jawless fish

Examples

The Myxinidae are often studied for their unique slime production mechanisms.
Researchers encountered several species of Myxinidae while exploring the ocean floor.
Unlike most fish, members of the Myxinidae family lack jaws and true vertebrae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - slimy, eel-shaped jawless fish (member of Myxinidae)

Etymology

From New Latin 'Myxinidae', based on 'Myxine', the type genus, from Greek 'myxa' (slime).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈksɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / mɪkˈsɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of slime-producing marine animals known as hagfishes, which are jawless, eel-like fish found in deep sea environments
family of marine slime-producing hagfishes

Examples

Researchers collected specimens from the Myxinidae family for their study on slime production.
The Myxinidae are unique among fish due to their jawless mouths and ability to excrete large amounts of mucus.
Marine biologists believe that the ancient lineage of Myxinidae dates back hundreds of millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - a slime-producing, eel-like jawless fish

Etymology

From the genus name Myxine, derived from Greek myxa meaning 'mucus' or 'slime.'
