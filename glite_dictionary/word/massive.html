massive

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈmasɪv / (BrE)
consisting of or forming a large mass; containing a great quantity of matter and often very heavy or bulky
having great mass or weight

Examples

The boulder was so massive that it took several machines to move it.
Astronomers discovered a massive star at the edge of the galaxy.
The massive oak tree had stood in the field for centuries.

Synset Examples

Earth is the most massive of the terrestrial planets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy - of great weight; difficult to lift or move
  • Antonym: light - of little weight; not heavy

Etymology

From Middle French 'massif' (solid, massive), from Old French 'masse' (mass).

4. ADJ

/ ˈmæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈmæsɪv / (BrE)
(of a material object) made entirely of the same substance throughout; not plated or hollow.
solid, same material throughout

Examples

The antique spoon was crafted from massive silver rather than being just silver-plated.
She admired the massive gold band of the ring, impressed by its weight and quality.
For technical applications, massive copper is preferred over coated wires for better conductivity.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solid - made wholly of a single substance, not hollow or plated
