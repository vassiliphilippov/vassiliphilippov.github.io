specie

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːʃi / (AmE), / ˈspiːʃi / (BrE)
Money in the form of coins rather than notes or credit.
money in coin form

Examples

The country decided to pay its debts in specie rather than in paper money.
Due to the gold rush, the banks found themselves short of specie.
All payments must be made in specie to guarantee authenticity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin - a flat, typically round piece of metal used as money

Etymology

From Latin 'in specie' meaning 'in kind' or 'in the actual form'.
