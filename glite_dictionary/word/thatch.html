thatch

1. NOUN

/ θætʃ / (AmE), / θætʃ / (BrE)
dried plant stalks, such as straw or reeds, used to cover roofs as a material for traditional roofing.
material for roofing made from plant stalks

Examples

The cottage was topped with a thick layer of thatch to keep out the rain.
Villagers gathered reeds from the marsh to make thatch for their roofs.
The old barn's thatch needed replacing after many years of wind and weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thatching - the act or process of making a roof with thatch

Etymology

Old English *theccan* (to cover).

2. NOUN

/ θætʃ / (AmE), / θatʃ / (BrE)
A thick or unruly mass of hair, resembling thatched roofing material.
a thick mass of hair

Examples

His unruly thatch of blond hair made him stand out in the crowd.
She pushed the thatch of curls from her forehead and smiled.
Time had streaked his once-dark thatch with patches of gray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mane - a long or thick growth of hair, especially on the head

Etymology

From Old English *thæc*, related to 'covering' or 'roof'.

3. NOUN

/ θætʃ / (AmE), / θætʃ / (BrE)
A roof covering made from layers of dried plant material such as straw, reeds, or grass.
roof of straw or similar plant material

Examples

The cottage had a neat thatch that blended beautifully with the countryside.
After the storm, part of the old thatch needed to be repaired by the skilled roofer.
Traditional English villages often feature houses with a straw thatch instead of tiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roof - covering for the top of a building

Etymology

From Old English *þæc* (thatch, roof covering), related to Dutch *dak* (roof).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04424807-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ θætʃ / (AmE), / θætʃ / (BrE)
to cover a roof or structure with thatch (a layer of straw, reeds, or similar material) to form a roof or covering.
cover with straw or reeds as a roof

Examples

Local craftsmen were hired to thatch the new cottages in the village.
Many traditional houses in the region are still thatched by hand every few decades.
They decided to thatch the hut using reeds collected from the nearby marsh.

Synset Examples

thatch the roofs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roof - to cover or provide with a roof

Etymology

From Old English *þæccean* (to cover), related to *þæc* (thatch, roof, cover).
