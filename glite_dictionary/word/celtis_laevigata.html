Celtis laevigata

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.tɪs ˌleɪ.vɪˈɡeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˈkɛl.tɪs ˌliː.vɪˈɡɑː.tə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the southern United States, known for its small black berries and soft yellowish wood; commonly called sugarberry.
a deciduous tree of the southern US; sugarberry

Examples

The Celtis laevigata thrives in moist soils along riverbanks in the southern United States.
Carpenters sometimes use the soft wood of the Celtis laevigata to make furniture.
Wildlife such as birds feed on the small black berries produced by Celtis laevigata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugarberry - common name for Celtis laevigata

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Celtis* and species epithet *laevigata* meaning 'smooth', referencing the tree's smooth leaves or bark.
