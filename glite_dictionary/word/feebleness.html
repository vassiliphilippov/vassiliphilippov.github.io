feebleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːb.l̩.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːb.lɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking strength, force, intensity, or substance.
lack of strength, intensity, or substance

Examples

The feebleness of his argument was apparent to everyone in the room.
There was a certain feebleness in her voice that suggested she was unwell.
Critics pointed out the feebleness of the evidence presented at the trial.

Synset Examples

a shrill yet sweet tenuity of voice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strength - the quality of being strong or powerful

Etymology

From Middle English febelnesse, from Old French feblesce, from Latin flebilis (weak).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːblnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being physically weak, especially as a result of old age or illness
state of physical weakness

Examples

The feebleness of his voice made it difficult to hear him across the room.
After her long illness, she struggled with a persistent feebleness in her limbs.
Age had brought a certain feebleness to his step, but not to his spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - the state of being weak; lack of strength
  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong

Etymology

From Middle English febilnesse, from Old French feblesce, from Latin debilis (weak).
