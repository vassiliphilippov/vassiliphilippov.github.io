Tympanuchus

1. NOUN

/ tɪm.pəˈnuː.kəs / (AmE), / tɪm.pəˈnjuː.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds known as prairie chickens, belonging to the grouse family.
genus of prairie chickens (birds)

Examples

Biologists studied the booming call of the Tympanuchus during the mating season.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of several Tympanuchus species.
The greater prairie chicken is a well-known member of the Tympanuchus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie chicken - a North American grouse native to grasslands, member of the Tympanuchus genus

Etymology

From Greek *tympanon* (drum) and *ēchos* (sound), referring to the drumming sound made by some species.

2. NOUN

/ tɪm.pəˈnuː.kəs / (AmE), / tɪm.pəˈnjuː.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American grouse known as prairie chickens, comprising several species found in grassland habitats.
genus of prairie chickens
medical

Examples

The Tympanuchus genus includes both the greater and lesser prairie chicken.
Many conservation efforts focus on preserving habitats for Tympanuchus species.
Scientists studied the mating dances of birds in the Tympanuchus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie chicken - A species of grouse native to North American grasslands

Etymology

From Greek *tympanon* (drum) + *-uchus* (having), referring to the bird's booming sound.
