louse

1. NOUN

/ laʊs / (AmE), / laʊs / (BrE)
a small, wingless parasitic insect that lives on the skin of mammals and birds, feeding on their blood or skin.
parasitic bloodsucking insect

Examples

After spending a week at camp, she discovered a louse in her hair.
The veterinarian found a louse clinging to the dog's ear.
Proper hygiene can help prevent a louse infestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lice - plural form of louse, referring to multiple such insects

Etymology

From Old English lūs, related to German Laus and Dutch luis.

2. NOUN

/ laʊs / (AmE), / laʊs / (BrE)
Any of several small insects, especially aphids, that feed by sucking the sap from plants.
plant-feeding insect; aphid

Examples

The wheat crop was damaged by a swarm of lice feeding on the young stems.
Aphids, sometimes called plant lice, can be a serious problem for gardeners.
Farmers often use insecticides to control louse populations on their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small sap-sucking insect often called greenfly or blackfly

Etymology

Old English lūs, related to Old Norse lús and German Laus.

3. NOUN

/ laʊs / (AmE), / laʊs / (BrE)
A small, wingless, parasitic insect with mouthparts adapted for biting, often found on birds and other animals.
a small parasitic wingless insect

Examples

The veterinarian found a louse in the bird's feathers during the examination.
Lice, the plural of louse, are common parasites among wild birds.
A single louse can lay dozens of eggs, leading to infestations if not treated quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small wingless jumping insect that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds
  • Synonym: tick - a small arachnid parasite that feeds on the blood of mammals, birds, and sometimes reptiles and amphibians

Etymology

From Old English lūs, related to German Laus and Dutch luis.

4. NOUN

/ laʊs / (AmE), / laʊs / (BrE)
an unpleasant or contemptible person, especially one who acts unethically or is not deserving of respect
an unpleasant or contemptible person

Examples

After stealing from his friends, everyone agreed he was a real louse.
Don't let that louse trick you with his lies again.
She called her ex-boyfriend a louse for cheating on her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat - a contemptible or treacherous person
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities

Etymology

Originally from Old English *lus*, referring to the parasitic insect; later used figuratively for a despicable person.
