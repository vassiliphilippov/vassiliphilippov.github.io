Carduelis hornemanni

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈduːəlɪs hɔrˈnɛməni / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs hɔːˈnɛməni / (BrE)
A small finch of the Arctic redpoll species, characterized by a red crown and found in northern regions.
species of Arctic redpoll finch

Examples

Birdwatchers in Greenland were excited to spot a flock of Carduelis hornemanni among the birches.
The pale plumage and red crown make Carduelis hornemanni easy to distinguish from similar finches.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the breeding habits of Carduelis hornemanni.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redpoll - a small finch with a red forehead from northern regions

Etymology

From Latin: 'Carduelis' (goldfinch genus) and commemorating Danish botanist Jens Wilken Hornemann.
