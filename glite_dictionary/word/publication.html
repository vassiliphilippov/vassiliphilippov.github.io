publication

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of making printed or digital material available to the public
the act of making something public in print or online

Examples

The publication of her novel was met with great excitement.
Following publication, the findings were discussed at length in the scientific community.
Certain information requires approval before publication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - making something available to the public

Etymology

From Latin 'publicatio' (making public), from 'publicare' (to make public).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a copy of a book, magazine, newspaper, or other printed work made available for distribution
printed work issued for distribution

Examples

She picked up the latest publication from the display rack.
Each publication must include a copyright notice on the cover.
Back issues of the publication are available for research purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: issue - a single copy or edition of a periodical
  • Synonym: edition - a particular version of a printed work

Etymology

From Latin 'publicatio' (making public), via Old French and Middle English.

4. NOUN

/ ˌpʌbləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the business or industry of producing and distributing printed materials, such as books, newspapers, or magazines, for sale or circulation

Examples

She has worked in the publication industry for over twenty years.
Digital publication has transformed the way magazines reach their audience.
The publication of textbooks is a highly competitive business.
