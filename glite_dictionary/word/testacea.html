Testacea

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈsteɪsiə / (AmE), / tɛˈsteɪsiə / (BrE)
A former group of amoeboid protozoa characterized by having shells or tests, especially testacean rhizopods.
shell-bearing amoeboid protozoa

Examples

Scientists collected several Testacea from the freshwater pond for microscopic examination.
Early researchers classified certain shelled amoebas as Testacea due to their distinctive tests.
The diversity of Testacea in soil environments can provide clues about the ecosystem's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testacean - an organism pertaining to or resembling Testacea

Etymology

From Latin testacea (shellfish), from testa (shell).
