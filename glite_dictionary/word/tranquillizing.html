tranquillizing

1. ADJ

/ træŋˈkwɪlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlʌɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
having a calming or soothing effect; making someone feel relaxed or peaceful
calming or soothing

Examples

The medicine had a tranquillizing effect on the anxious patient.
Listening to the sound of gently falling rain can be incredibly tranquillizing.
After a stressful week, she found the spa's atmosphere deeply tranquillizing.

Synset Examples

valium has a tranquilizing effect
took a hot drink with sedative properties before going to bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - having a calming or sleep-inducing effect
  • Antonym: stimulating - making one feel energetic or excited

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (calm, quiet) plus suffix '-ize' and '-ing'.
