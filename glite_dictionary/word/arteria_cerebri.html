arteria cerebri

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə səˈriːbraɪ / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə səˈriːbrɪ / (BrE)
any of the arteries that supply blood to the cerebral cortex of the brain.
artery supplying blood to the brain cortex
medical

Examples

Blockage of the arteria cerebri can lead to a stroke in the affected part of the brain.
Doctors studied the branches of the arteria cerebri using detailed imaging techniques.
Certain neurological disorders are linked to problems in the arteria cerebri circulation.

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery) and 'cerebri' (of the brain).
