somaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊməˈθiːziə / (AmE), / ˌsəʊməˈθiːziə / (BrE)
the perception of bodily sensations, including touch, proprioception, and internal organ sensations
perception of bodily sensations
medical

Examples

Some animals depend heavily on somaesthesia for spatial awareness and navigation.
Training in mindfulness can heighten one's somaesthesia and internal awareness.
Certain neurological disorders can impair somaesthesia, leading to difficulties in movement and coordination.

Synset Examples

he relied on somesthesia to warn him of pressure changes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somatosensory - relating to sensory signals from the body

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) + aisthēsis (perception, sensation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊməˈθiːziə / (AmE), / ˌsəʊməˈθiːziə / (BrE)
the perception of bodily sensations, including touch, proprioception, and awareness of internal organs
perception of bodily sensations
medical

Examples

The study focused on how damage to the nervous system can alter somaesthesia.
Disorders of somaesthesia may lead to difficulties in processing touch or body awareness.
Accurate somaesthesia is essential for coordinated movement and balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somesthesia - alternative spelling of somaesthesia; perception of bodily sensations
  • Degree Lower: proprioception - sense of the relative position and movement of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'sōma' (body) + 'aisthēsis' (perception).
