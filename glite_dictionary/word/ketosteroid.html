ketosteroid

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːtoʊˈstɛrˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌkiːtəʊˈstɛərˌɔɪd / (BrE)
a steroid that contains a ketone group in its structure
steroid with a ketone group
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a ketosteroid is important for diagnosing certain endocrine disorders.
Researchers measured the levels of ketosteroid metabolites in the urine samples.
Some ketosteroid hormones play critical roles in regulating metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steroid - class of organic compounds including many hormones

Etymology

From keto- (relating to a ketone) + steroid.
