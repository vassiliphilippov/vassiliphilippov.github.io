Tropaeolaceae

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌpiːoʊˈleɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / trəʊˌpiːəʊˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants comprising the genus Tropaeolum, known for plants such as nasturtiums
family of flowering plants (nasturtiums)

Examples

The Tropaeolaceae are easily recognized by their brightly colored, spurred flowers.
Gardeners often plant members of the Tropaeolaceae for their attractive foliage and edible flowers.
The genus Tropaeolum, within the Tropaeolaceae family, includes the well-known nasturtium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tropaeolum - type genus of the family Tropaeolaceae (nasturtium)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Tropaeolum.
