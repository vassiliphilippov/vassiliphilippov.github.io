hacienda

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈsiːəndə, æˈsiːəndə / (AmE), / hæˈsiːəndə / (BrE)
a large estate or plantation in a Spanish-speaking country, often with a dwelling house and farmland
large estate, typically Spanish or Latin American

Examples

The old hacienda was surrounded by rolling fields and groves of orange trees.
During their vacation in Mexico, they stayed at a historic hacienda transformed into a boutique hotel.
He inherited a sprawling hacienda from his grandparents in rural Argentina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantation - a large farm, especially one in a tropical or subtropical country, where crops are grown for sale

Etymology

From Spanish 'hacienda' meaning estate, from Latin 'facienda' (things to be done).

2. NOUN

/ hɑːˈsiːən.də / (AmE), / hæˈsiː.əndə / (BrE)
The main house of a large estate or ranch, especially in Spanish-speaking countries.
main house on a large estate or ranch

Examples

The old hacienda stood at the heart of the sprawling ranch.
Tourists visited the restored hacienda to learn about its history and architecture.
The family gathered every summer at their ancestral hacienda in Mexico.

Etymology

From Spanish 'hacienda' (estate, property), from Latin 'facere' (to make, do).
