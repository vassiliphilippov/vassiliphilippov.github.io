walk-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌʌp / (BrE)
a building or apartment in a building without an elevator, requiring occupants to use stairs
building or apartment without an elevator

Examples

They rented a walk-up in the heart of the city.
After moving to a fifth-floor walk-up, she got used to climbing stairs every day.
The old brownstone is a classic New York walk-up.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌʌp / (BrE)
an apartment or building that has no elevator, requiring residents to use the stairs
apartment or building without an elevator

Examples

She rented a walk-up in the heart of the city because the price was affordable.
Living in a fifth-floor walk-up can be challenging when you have groceries to carry.
The old building is a classic New York walk-up with narrow staircases and no lift.
