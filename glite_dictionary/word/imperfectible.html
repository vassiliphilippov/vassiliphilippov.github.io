imperfectible

1. ADJ

/ ɪm.pɚˈfɛk.tɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ɪm.pɜːˈfɛk.tɪ.bəl / (BrE)
Capable of being made imperfect; able to become or be rendered less than perfect.
capable of being made imperfect

Examples

Even the most carefully designed system is still imperfectible, as unforeseen errors may arise.
Some philosophers argue that human nature is inherently imperfectible.
The artwork was believed to be imperfectible, susceptible to decay over time despite preservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfectible - capable of becoming perfect

Etymology

From Latin 'imperfectibilis', from 'im-' (not) + 'perfectibilis' (able to be perfected).
