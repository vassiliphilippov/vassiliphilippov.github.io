quenched

1. ADJ

/ kwɛntʃt / (AmE), / kwɛntʃt / (BrE)
(of a feeling, movement, or event) subdued, suppressed, or brought to an end; overcome or extinguished.
subdued or suppressed

Examples

The protest was quickly quenched by the authorities before it gained momentum.
Rumors of a scandal were quenched as soon as the official statement was released.
Her curiosity was quenched when she finally found out the truth.

Synset Examples

the quelled rebellion
an uprising quenched almost before it started
a squelched rumor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressed - forcibly put an end to, subdued
  • Synonym: subdued - overcome or brought under control

Etymology

From Middle English 'quenchen' (to extinguish), ultimately from Old English 'cwencan' (cause to go out, extinguish).

2. ADJ

/ kwɛntʃt / (AmE), / kwɛntʃt / (BrE)
(of thirst, a desire, or a feeling) satisfied or extinguished; no longer felt or intense.
satisfied (of thirst or desire)

Examples

After several hours in the sun, his quenched thirst brought him relief and renewed energy.
The team celebrated with quenched excitement after winning the championship.
Her curiosity was finally quenched when she found the answer she had been seeking.

Synset Examples

his thirst quenched, he was able to continue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsatisfied - not fulfilled or satisfied; the opposite state

Etymology

Past participle of "quench," from Old English *cwencan* (to extinguish).
