double-crossing

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of betraying someone, especially after pretending to cooperate or be loyal
an act of betrayal

Examples

His double-crossing of his partner shocked everyone in the company.
She discovered the double-crossing before the plan could go any further.
Stories of double-crossing have always fascinated readers of crime novels.

Synset Examples

he gave us the old double cross
I could no longer tolerate his impudent double-crossing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayal - the act of betraying someone or something
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal

Etymology

From 'double-cross' (verb), meaning to betray, originally from early 20th-century American slang.
