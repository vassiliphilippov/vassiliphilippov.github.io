parenesis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛnɪsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈriːnɪsɪs / (BrE)
(in rhetoric) advice or exhortation, especially in a moral or religious context; admonition.
rhetorical advice or exhortation

Examples

The preacher's sermon was filled with parenesis urging the congregation to lead virtuous lives.
Biblical texts often contain sections of parenesis alongside narrative elements.
In ancient rhetorical practice, parenesis was used to inspire moral improvement among listeners.

Synset Examples

In Deuteronomy it is often impossible to separate parenesis from history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admonition - a warning or reprimand; counsel or advice against something
  • Synonym: exhortation - an address or communication emphatically urging someone to do something

Etymology

From Greek 'parainesis' (exhortation), from 'parainein' (to advise, exhort).
