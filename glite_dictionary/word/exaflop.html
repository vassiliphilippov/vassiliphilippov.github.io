exaflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈɛksəˌflɒp / (BrE)
A unit of computing speed equal to one quintillion (10¹⁸) floating-point operations per second.
one quintillion floating-point operations per second

Examples

The new supercomputer is capable of performing more than one exaflop of processing power.
Advancements in technology have made achieving an exaflop speed a major milestone for scientists.
Global leaders are competing to build the first system to reach an exaflop in computing performance.

Synset Examples

Cray, Inc. announced in December 2009 a plan to build a 1 EFLOPS supercomputer before 2020.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petaflop - one quadrillion floating-point operations per second

Etymology

Formed from 'exa-' (prefix meaning one quintillion) and 'flop' (floating-point operations per second).
