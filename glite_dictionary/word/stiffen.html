to stiffen

1. VERB

/ ˈstɪfən / (AmE), / ˈstɪfən / (BrE)
to make or become stiff or stiffer
make or become stiff

Examples

If you stiffen the cream with gelatin, it will hold its shape better.
The child's posture stiffened as she heard the sudden noise behind her.
You can stiffen fabric by soaking it in a starch solution.

Synset Examples

Stiffen the cream by adding gelatine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - to make or become hard or harder
  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become soft or softer

Etymology

From stiff + -en (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈstɪfən / (AmE), / ˈstɪfən / (BrE)
to become or make something become stiff or stiffer
become or make stiff

Examples

She tried to stiffen her resolve before making the announcement.
His back would stiffen with pain after a long day of work.
The mixture will stiffen as it cools, so stir constantly.

Synset Examples

He stiffened when he saw his boss enter the room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or stiff
  • Synonym: to harden - to become harder or more solid

Etymology

Formed from 'stiff' + the verb-forming suffix '-en'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419488-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈstɪfən / (AmE), / ˈstɪfən / (BrE)
to make something, such as a rule or regulation, more strict or restrictive
make rules or restrictions stricter

Examples

The government decided to stiffen regulations on financial institutions after the crisis.
In response to repeated violations, the school board voted to stiffen its disciplinary policies.
Some countries may stiffen their entry requirements to prevent illegal immigration.

Synset Examples

tighten the rules
stiffen the regulations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tighten - to make stricter, especially rules or control
  • Antonym: to relax - to make less strict or severe (of rules or restrictions)

Etymology

From stiff + -en; modeled after 'to make stiff'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419690-v
Synset POS: v
