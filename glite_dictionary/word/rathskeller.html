rathskeller

1. NOUN

/ ˈræθˌskɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈræθˌskɛlə / (BrE)
A restaurant or tavern, often below street level, serving beer and sometimes food, originally modeled after German basement establishments.
underground tavern or restaurant, esp. German style

Examples

The city's oldest rathskeller boasts a wide selection of imported German beers and traditional folk music.
We spent the evening in a cozy rathskeller sharing stories over pretzels and lagers.
Many university campuses have a rathskeller where students can relax after classes.

Etymology

From German 'Ratskeller', literally 'council's cellar', referring to basement restaurants in German town halls.
