Picardie

1. PROPN

/ piˈkɑːr.di / (AmE), / piˈkɑːr.di / (BrE)
a region in northern France bordering the English Channel, known for its history, agriculture, and Gothic cathedrals
region in northern France

Examples

Many tourists visit Picardie to see its famous cathedrals and historical landmarks.
The cuisine of Picardie features dishes made from local produce and seafood.
After traveling through Normandy, they continued their journey into Picardie.

Etymology

French 'Picardie', from 'Picard', the name of the inhabitants, of uncertain origin.
