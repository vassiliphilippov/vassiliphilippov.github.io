to oppress

1. VERB

/ əˈprɛs / (AmE), / əˈprɛs / (BrE)
to treat someone in a cruel or unjust way, especially by using power to prevent them from having rights or freedom
treat cruelly or unjustly; keep down

Examples

The tyrannical regime continued to oppress dissenters for decades.
Many activists spoke out against laws that oppress minority groups.
History books detail how the king used force to oppress his people.

Synset Examples

Some religious groups are persecuted in some countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, especially because of their beliefs

Etymology

From Latin opprimere (to press against, crush, overwhelm).

2. VERB

/ əˈprɛs / (AmE), / əˈprɛs / (BrE)
to treat a person or group harshly or unfairly, especially by using power or authority in an unjust way
treat harshly with unjust power

Examples

The regime oppresses minorities by restricting their rights and freedoms.
Many workers were oppressed by harsh labor laws in the early 20th century.
He spoke out against leaders who oppress their citizens for speaking the truth.

Synset Examples

The government oppresses political activists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, especially because of their race or beliefs
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially oppression

Etymology

From Latin 'opprimere' meaning 'to press against, to overwhelm'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02429499-v
Synset POS: v
