Asteroidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstəˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌæstəriˈɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A class of echinoderms that comprises the sea stars, marine animals with typically five radiating arms.
class of sea stars (starfish)

Examples

Scientists collected several Asteroidea specimens from the rocky tide pools for their study.
Members of the Asteroidea class are easily recognized by their star-shaped bodies.
The aquarium's new exhibit features a variety of Asteroidea from different ocean habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starfish - common name for marine animals of the Asteroidea class
  • Degree Higher: echinoderm - marine animals with spiny skin, the phylum including sea stars

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek astēr (star) + -oidēs (form, likeness) + -a (scientific suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæstəˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌæstəriˈɔːdɪə / (BrE)
a taxonomic class of echinoderms comprising the sea stars, marine animals with typically five radiating arms
class of echinoderms (sea stars)

Examples

The Asteroidea are known for their remarkable ability to regenerate lost arms.
Scientists have classified over 1,500 species within the Asteroidea.
Unlike some other echinoderms, members of Asteroidea are almost exclusively found in marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starfish - common name for members of Asteroidea

Etymology

From Greek 'asteroeidēs', meaning 'star-like'.
