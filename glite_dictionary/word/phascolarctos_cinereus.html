Phascolarctos cinereus

1. NOUN

/ fæskoʊˈlɑːrktəs sɪˈnɪriəs / (AmE), / fæskoʊˈlɑːktɒs sɪˈnɪərɪəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the koala, a sluggish, tailless Australian tree-dwelling marsupial with grey furry ears and coat, which feeds on eucalyptus leaves and bark.
scientific name for the koala

Examples

The Phascolarctos cinereus spends most of its life sleeping in eucalyptus trees.
Researchers observed a group of Phascolarctos cinereus feeding exclusively on certain species of eucalyptus.
Unlike many other marsupials, the Phascolarctos cinereus rarely comes down from the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: koala - common name for Phascolarctos cinereus, an Australian marsupial

Etymology

From Greek 'phaskolos' (pouch) + 'arktos' (bear), and Latin 'cinereus' (ash-colored).
