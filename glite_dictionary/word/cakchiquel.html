Cakchiquel

1. NOUN

/ kakˈtʃikɛl / (AmE), / kakˈtʃikɛl / (BrE)
a member of the Maya people living in south central Guatemala
Mayan people of south central Guatemala

Examples

The Cakchiquel have preserved many of their traditional customs and language.
A group of Cakchiquel leaders gathered to discuss community issues.
He is proud of his Cakchiquel heritage and teaches it to his children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maya - indigenous peoples of Mesoamerica

Etymology

From the Spanish 'Cakchiquel', derived from the group's own name in their native language.

2. NOUN

/ kɑk.tʃiˈkɛl / (AmE), / kak.tʃɪˈkɛl / (BrE)
a Mayan language spoken primarily by the Cakchiquel people in the highlands of Guatemala.
a Mayan language of Guatemala

Examples

Many linguists study Cakchiquel to understand the structure of Mayan languages.
He grew up speaking both Spanish and Cakchiquel at home in Guatemala.
There are several radio broadcasts in Cakchiquel for native speakers in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kʼicheʼ - a closely related Mayan language spoken in Guatemala

Etymology

From the native name Kaqchikel, the self-designation of the people and their language.
