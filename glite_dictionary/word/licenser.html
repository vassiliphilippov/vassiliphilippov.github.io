licenser

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsənsər / (AmE), / ˈlʌɪsənsə / (BrE)
an official or authority who issues licenses or grants formal permission, especially for publications or activities requiring regulation.
official who issues licenses or permissions

Examples

The licenser must review each application thoroughly before granting approval.
Only a recognized licenser can authorize the publication of sensitive material.
The company had to submit all necessary documents to the licenser to obtain an export license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licensor - person or entity that grants a license
  • Antonym: licensee - person or party receiving a license

Etymology

From 'license' (noun) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
