apparently

1. ADV

/ əˈpɛr.ənt.li / (AmE), / əˈpær.ənt.li / (BrE)
Used to say that something seems to be true based on what is known or observed, although it may not actually be so.
seemingly; as far as one knows or can see

Examples

Apparently, the train was delayed due to a signal failure.
He hadn't called ahead, so apparently he wasn't expecting company.
The restaurant is apparently very popular, as it's always busy.

Synset Examples

the answer is obviously wrong
she was in bed and evidently in great pain
he was manifestly too important to leave off the guest list
it is all patently nonsense
she has apparently been living here for some time
I thought he owned the property, but apparently not
You are plainly wrong
he is plain stubborn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seemingly - Appearing to be true but not necessarily so; synonym for 'apparently'
  • Antonym: obviously - Plainly and clearly; antonym in some contexts for apparent uncertainty

Etymology

From Latin 'apparens' (appearing) via French 'apparent'.

2. ADV

/ əˈpɛrəntli / (AmE), / əˈparəntli / (BrE)
used to say that something seems to be true based on what is known or seen, although it may not actually be so
seemingly, as far as one can tell

Examples

Apparently, there was a mistake with the reservation, so our table isn't ready yet.
The road was apparently closed due to construction, although no workers were in sight.
She is apparently very successful in her new job, judging by the way she talks about it.

Synset Examples

irrigation often produces bumper crops from apparently desert land
the child is seemingly healthy but the doctor is concerned
had been ostensibly frank as to his purpose while really concealing it
on the face of it the problem seems minor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seemingly - in a way that appears to be true but may not be
  • Synonym: ostensibly - as appears or is stated to be true, but not necessarily so
  • Antonym: actually - in fact, as the truth or facts of a situation

Etymology

From Latin 'apparēre' (to appear), via Old French and Middle English.
