Erysimum arkansanum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ɪˈsɪm.əm ˌɑːr.kənˈsæn.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈsɪm.əm ˌɑː.kənˈsæn.əm / (BrE)
A biennial or short-lived perennial plant, also known as prairie rocket, with orange-yellow flowers, found in western North America from Minnesota to Kansas; sometimes classified in the genus Cheiranthus.
prairie rocket plant of western North America

Examples

Botanists recently discovered a large population of Erysimum arkansanum growing in a protected prairie reserve.
The orange-yellow blooms of Erysimum arkansanum are a distinctive feature of spring in the Great Plains.
Erysimum arkansanum prefers open grasslands and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Etymology

From Latin Erysimum (a plant genus) and arkansanum, referencing Arkansas in the United States.
