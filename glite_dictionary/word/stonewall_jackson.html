Stonewall Jackson

1. PROPN

/ ˈstoʊnwɔl ˈdʒæksən / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnwɔːl ˈdʒæksən / (BrE)
(1824–1863) A famous general in the Confederate Army during the American Civil War, known for his troops' steadfastness at the First Battle of Bull Run.
Confederate general (American Civil War)

Examples

Historians often cite Stonewall Jackson's tactics as some of the most brilliant of the Civil War.
The troops, inspired by Stonewall Jackson, held their ground under heavy fire.
Stonewall Jackson earned his famous nickname at the First Battle of Bull Run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Robert E. Lee - Leading Confederate general during the American Civil War

Etymology

Nickname 'Stonewall' given for his steadfast defense in battle; born Thomas Jonathan Jackson.
