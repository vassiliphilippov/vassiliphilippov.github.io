Kaiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaɪzə / (BrE)
the title used for the emperors of Germany, Austria, or the Holy Roman Empire, especially up to 1918
emperor of Germany or Austria (historical)

Examples

During World War I, the German Kaiser played a crucial role in military decisions.
The abdication of the last Kaiser marked the end of the German Empire.
Many people associated the word Kaiser with both power and grandeur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor - a sovereign ruler of great power and rank, especially one ruling an empire
  • Synonym: czar - title for the emperor of Russia (historical)

Etymology

From German Kaiser, from Latin Caesar (emperor).
