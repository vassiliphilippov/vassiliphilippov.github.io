Bithynia

1. PROPN

/ bɪˈθɪniə / (AmE), / bɪˈθɪniə / (BrE)
an ancient region in northwestern Asia Minor, now part of modern Turkey, incorporated into the Roman Empire by the 1st century BC
ancient region in Asia Minor (now Turkey)

Examples

Historical records indicate that Bithynia played a key role in regional trade routes.
The Roman conquest of Bithynia marked a turning point in the area's history.
Archaeologists have discovered ancient ruins thought to be from Bithynia near modern Istanbul.

Etymology

From Latin Bithynia, from Ancient Greek Βιθυνία (Bithynía).
