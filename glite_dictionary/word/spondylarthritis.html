spondylarthritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɑːn.dɪ.lɑːrˈθraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌspɒn.dɪ.lɑːˈθraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A type of arthritis that affects one or more of the joints between the vertebrae in the spine, often causing pain and stiffness.
arthritis affecting spinal joints
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with spondylarthritis after experiencing chronic back pain.
Early treatment of spondylarthritis can help prevent severe spinal damage.
Researchers are studying new medications to manage spondylarthritis symptoms more effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of joints
  • Degree Lower: osteoarthritis - degeneration of joint cartilage and bone, especially in older people
  • Degree Higher: ankylosing spondylitis - a specific type of spondylarthritis causing fusion of spinal vertebrae

Etymology

From Greek spondylos (vertebra) + arthritis (joint inflammation).
