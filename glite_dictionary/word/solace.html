solace

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑl.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪs / (BrE)
comfort or consolation in a time of distress or sadness.
comfort in distress or sadness

Examples

During her illness, books were a great source of solace.
He found solace in the quiet beauty of nature after his loss.
Friends often offer words of solace during difficult times.

Synset Examples

his presence was a consolation to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint
  • Synonym: consolation - the comfort received by a person after a loss or disappointment
  • Antonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain (opposite state)

Etymology

From Old French *solas*, from Latin *solacium* (comfort, consolation).

/ ˈsɑləs / (AmE), / ˈsɒləs / (BrE)
comfort or consolation in a time of distress or sadness.
comfort in sadness or distress

Examples

He found solace in reading poetry during his difficult times.
After her loss, music provided her with some much-needed solace.
Friends can be a great source of solace when life is challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint; also emotional support
  • Synonym: consolation - the comfort received by a person after a loss or disappointment
  • Antonym: anguish - severe mental or physical pain or suffering

Etymology

From Old French 'solas', from Latin 'solacium' (comfort, consolation).

3. VERB

/ ˈsɑːləs / (AmE), / ˈsɒləs / (BrE)
to give comfort or moral support to someone in a time of distress or sadness
give comfort in distress

Examples

He tried to solace his friend after the loss of her pet.
Music can often solace the troubled mind during difficult times.
She solaced herself by reading comforting letters from home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - to make someone feel less sad, worried, or disappointed
  • Synonym: console - to sympathize with and offer reassurance to someone who is sad or disappointed

Etymology

From Old French *solacier* (to comfort), ultimately from Latin *solacium* (comfort).
