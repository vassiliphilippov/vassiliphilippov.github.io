anointer

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɔɪntər / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪntə / (BrE)
a person who anoints someone with oil or another substance as part of a religious ceremony or ritual
person who anoints in a religious ritual

Examples

The anointer gently marked the candidate's forehead with oil during the ceremony.
In ancient traditions, the role of the anointer was usually reserved for high priests.
She was chosen as the official anointer for the coronation ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anoint - to smear or rub with oil, often as part of a religious ceremony

Etymology

From anoint + -er, denoting a person who performs the action.
