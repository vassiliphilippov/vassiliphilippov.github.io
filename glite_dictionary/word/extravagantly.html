extravagantly

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli / (BrE)
In a manner that is excessive, lavish, or spending more than is necessary.
in an excessive or lavish manner

Examples

She decorated the entire hall extravagantly for the celebration, with flowers covering every wall.
He extravagantly praised his team for their hard work, leaving everyone feeling valued.
The couple spent extravagantly on their honeymoon, staying in luxury hotels and dining at the best restaurants.

Synset Examples

they were abundantly supplied with food
he thanked her profusely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a rich or elaborate manner
  • Antonym: frugally - in a careful or sparing manner regarding resources

Etymology

From extravagant + -ly; 'extravagant' from Latin 'extravagari' (to wander outside).

2. ADV

/ ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is wasteful, using more of something than is necessary or reasonable.
In a wasteful way

Examples

She decorated her house extravagantly for the holidays, with lights and ornaments everywhere.
They spent extravagantly on their wedding, inviting hundreds of guests and hiring famous musicians.
During the boom years, many people lived extravagantly without worrying about the future.

Synset Examples

the United States, up to the 1920s, used fuel lavishly, mainly because it was so cheap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a generous or wasteful manner
  • Antonym: frugally - in an economical or sparing manner

Etymology

From 'extravagant' + '-ly'; 'extravagant' from Latin 'extravagari' (to wander outside).

3. ADV

/ ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrævəɡəntli / (BrE)
In an excessive, lavish, or luxurious manner; spending or using more than is necessary or reasonable.
in a lavish or excessive manner

Examples

She decorated her home extravagantly for the holiday season, sparing no expense.
They dined extravagantly at the city's finest restaurants every weekend.
The movie was extravagantly produced, with stunning visuals and expensive special effects.

Synset Examples

lavishly decorated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a very generous or extravagant manner
  • Antonym: moderately - in a way that is not excessive or extreme, within reasonable limits

Etymology

From 'extravagant' (from Latin 'extravagari', to wander outside or stray), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00189129-r
Synset POS: r
