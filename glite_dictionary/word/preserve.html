preserve

1. VERB

/ prəˈzɝv / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
to keep someone or something safe from harm, injury, or danger; to maintain in a protected or unchanged state
keep safe from harm or danger

Examples

It is important to preserve endangered species so they do not become extinct.
The treaty was created to preserve peace between the two nations.
She worked hard to preserve her family's traditions for future generations.

Synset Examples

May God keep you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to protect - to shield from harm or danger
  • Antonym: to endanger - to put in danger or at risk

Etymology

From Latin praeservare (to guard beforehand), from prae- (before) + servare (to keep, save).
