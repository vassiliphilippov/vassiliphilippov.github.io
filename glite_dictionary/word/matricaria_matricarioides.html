Matricaria matricarioides

1. NOUN

/ məˌtrɪkəriə ˌməˌtrɪkəriˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / məˌtrɪkəriə ˌməˌtrɪkəriˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
An annual aromatic weed found in Pacific coastal areas of the United States and northeastern Asia, characterized by bristle-pointed leaves and yellow, rayless flowers.
aromatic weed with rayless yellow flowers

Examples

Matricaria matricarioides is commonly found along sandy beaches on the Pacific coast.
The leaves of Matricaria matricarioides emit a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
Botanists monitor populations of Matricaria matricarioides to track the health of local ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Matricaria and species epithet from Latin, patterned after botanical Latin conventions.
