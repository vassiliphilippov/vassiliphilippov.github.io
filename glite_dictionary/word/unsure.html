unsure

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
not confident about oneself or one's abilities; lacking self-confidence
not confident or self-assured

Examples

She felt unsure about speaking in front of the large audience.
His unsure manner made people question his leadership.
Even though he answered, his voice sounded unsure.

Synset Examples

stood in the doorway diffident and abashed
problems that call for bold not timid responses
a very unsure young man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • Synonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite

Etymology

From un- (not) + sure, from Latin securus (safe, sure).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃʊə / (BrE)
not certain about something or lacking confidence or assurance about a decision, feeling, or outcome.
not certain or lacking confidence

Examples

She felt unsure about accepting the job offer and asked for more time to decide.
He gave an unsure smile when asked about his plans for the weekend.
The team was unsure how to proceed after the sudden change in leadership.

Synset Examples

uncertain of his convictions
unsure of himself and his future
moving with uncertain (or unsure) steps
an uncertain smile
touched the ornaments with uncertain fingers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certain - confident and without doubt
  • Degree Higher: confident - feeling or showing certainty
  • Synonym: doubtful - not feeling sure; uncertain
  • Synonym: hesitant - unsure or slow in acting or speaking

Etymology

From un- (not) + sure (from Old French seur, from Latin securus meaning 'free from care').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00338302-a
Synset POS: a
