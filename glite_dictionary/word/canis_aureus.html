Canis aureus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nɪs ˈɔːr.i.əs / (AmE), / ˈka.nɪs ˈɔː.rɪ.əs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the golden jackal, a medium-sized canid native to Europe and Asia with a golden-colored coat
scientific name for the golden jackal

Examples

The scientific name of the golden jackal is Canis aureus.
Many wildlife documentaries feature Canis aureus in their natural Eurasian habitats.
Researchers observed a pack of Canis aureus near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden jackal - a medium-sized jackal native to southeastern Europe and South Asia

Etymology

From Latin: 'canis' (dog) and 'aureus' (golden).
