airbrake

1. NOUN

/ ˈerˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈeəˌbreɪk / (BrE)
a device, such as a small parachute or movable flap, used to reduce the speed of an aircraft in flight
device to slow down an aircraft

Examples

The pilot deployed the airbrake to slow down before landing.
Many modern jets are equipped with advanced airbrakes for safer descents.
When the airbrake failed, the landing had to be aborted.

Etymology

From 'air' + 'brake', referring to a brake used in the air.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈeəˌbreɪk / (BrE)
a brake on a vehicle that is operated by compressed air, especially used on heavy vehicles such as trucks and buses
vehicular brake using compressed air

Examples

Most large trucks are equipped with airbrakes for improved stopping power.
The mechanic checked the airbrake system before the bus left the depot.
In heavy vehicles, a malfunctioning airbrake can be extremely dangerous.
