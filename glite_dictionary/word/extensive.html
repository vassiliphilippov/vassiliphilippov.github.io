extensive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (BrE)
(of agriculture) involving the production of crops or livestock across large areas with relatively minimal input of labor or capital per unit area.
large-scale, low-input farming

Examples

The region is known for its extensive agriculture, with large fields requiring little manual labor.
Switching from intensive to extensive farming can help preserve soil fertility over time.
Cattle are often raised under extensive systems, roaming freely over vast grasslands.

Synset Examples

producing wheat under extensive conditions
agriculture of the extensive type

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intensive - (of agriculture) involving concentrated effort and inputs on a small area for higher productivity

Etymology

From Latin extensivus, from extendere (to stretch out).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (BrE)
covering or affecting a large area or a wide range; broad in scope or content.
broad in scope or content

Examples

The company launched an extensive review of its environmental policies.
The library offers an extensive collection of historical documents.
She has extensive knowledge of European literature.

Synset Examples

across-the-board pay increases
an all-embracing definition
blanket sanctions against human-rights violators
an invention with broad applications
a panoptic study of Soviet nationality
granted him wide powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - including or dealing with all or nearly all elements or aspects of something
  • Antonym: narrow - limited in extent, amount, or scope
  • Antonym: limited - restricted in size, amount, or extent

Etymology

From Latin 'extensus' (stretched out), past participle of 'extendere'.

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (BrE)
covering a large area; having a wide range or scope; large in amount or degree
large in area, scope, or amount

Examples

The storm caused extensive damage to the village, destroying many homes and roads.
After months of extensive research, the scientists published their findings.
The museum features an extensive collection of ancient artifacts from around the world.

Synset Examples

an extensive Roman settlement in northwest England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - including or dealing with all elements or aspects; broad in scope
  • Antonym: limited - restricted in size, amount, or extent; not extensive
  • Synonym: expansive - covering a wide area in terms of space or scope

Etymology

From Latin extensivus, from extendere (to stretch out).
