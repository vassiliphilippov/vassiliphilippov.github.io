Cyamopsis tetragonolobus

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.əˈmɒp.sɪs ˌtɛ.trəˌɡɒn.əˈloʊ.bəs / (AmE), / saɪ.əˈmɒp.sɪs ˌtɛ.trəˌɡɒn.əˈləʊ.bəs / (BrE)
A drought-tolerant leguminous herb cultivated for forage and for its seeds, which yield guar gum used as a thickening or sizing agent.
drought-tolerant legume, source of guar gum

Examples

Cyamopsis tetragonolobus is widely cultivated in India for its valuable guar gum.
Farmers often choose Cyamopsis tetragonolobus because it thrives in arid conditions and poor soils.
The seeds of Cyamopsis tetragonolobus are processed to produce a gum used in the food industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guar - common name for Cyamopsis tetragonolobus, the plant producing guar gum
  • Synonym: guar gum - gum obtained from Cyamopsis tetragonolobus used as a thickening agent

Etymology

From Greek 'kyamos' (bean) + '-opsis' (appearance) and Latin roots referring to 'four-angled pod'.
