doubloon

1. NOUN

/ dəˈbluːn / (AmE), / dʌˈbluːn / (BrE)
a former Spanish gold coin used mainly in the 16th to 19th centuries
former Spanish gold coin

Examples

The pirate captain claimed the chest was filled with doubloons and jewels.
In the museum exhibit, a doubloon from the 18th century was proudly displayed.
He found a rare golden doubloon on the beach after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin - piece of metal used as money

Etymology

From Spanish 'dublón', augmentative of 'doble' (double), referring to the value of the coin.
