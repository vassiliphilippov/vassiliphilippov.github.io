amah

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɑ / (AmE), / əˈmɑː / (BrE)
(historical) A female domestic servant, especially in South or East Asia.
female domestic servant (historical)

Examples

The wealthy family hired an amah to take care of their children and household chores.
She grew up in Hong Kong, where her amah played a significant role in her upbringing.
In colonial Singapore, an amah was often responsible for managing the kitchen and the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanny - a person employed to care for children in a household
  • Synonym: maid - a female domestic worker

Etymology

From Portuguese 'ama' (nurse, wet nurse), used in South and East Asia via colonial influence.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɑ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmɑː / (BrE)
a woman employed to breastfeed and care for another person's child, especially in Asian households
woman hired as a wet nurse or nursemaid

Examples

The wealthy family hired an amah to care for their newborn daughter.
Her amah raised her alongside her own children in the household.
In colonial Hong Kong, it was common for British families to employ an amah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wet nurse - a woman who breastfeeds another's child
  • Synonym: nursemaid - a woman or girl employed to look after a young child

Etymology

From Portuguese 'ama', meaning 'nurse' or 'wet nurse', itself from Latin 'amma' (nurse, mother). Widely used in South and East Asia during colonial periods.
