procedural

1. ADJ

/ prəˈsidʒɚəl / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdʒərəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with a procedure, especially an established or official way of doing something.
relating to procedure or process

Examples

The committee was accused of a procedural error during the vote.
The court dismissed the case due to a procedural technicality.
The company is updating its procedural guidelines to improve efficiency.

Synset Examples

a procedural violation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substantive - having a firm basis in reality; important, meaningful, or considerable

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ prəˈsidʒərəl / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdʒərəl / (BrE)
relating to the rules or methods that govern the operation of courts, as distinct from the substantive principles of law.
relating to court procedures or methods
legal

Examples

The judge explained that the issue was purely procedural and did not affect the outcome of the case.
There are both substantive and procedural aspects to criminal law.
A procedural error led to the dismissal of the lawsuit.

Synset Examples

adjective law

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substantive - relating to the actual principles or rights at issue in law, as opposed to procedure

Etymology

From procedure + -al, ultimately from French 'procédure'.
