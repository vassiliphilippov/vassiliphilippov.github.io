lupine

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːˌpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Lupinus, typically with tall spikes of purplish-blue flowers
plant with tall, colorful flower spikes

Examples

The lupine bloomed early this spring, filling the meadow with color.
Gardeners planted rows of lupines along the fence to attract bees and butterflies.
Blue and purple lupine flowers are a common sight in the mountains during summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'lupinus', meaning 'of a wolf', the genus name.

2. ADJ

/ ˈluːpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈluːpaɪn / (BrE)
of or relating to, or characteristic of wolves
relating to wolves

Examples

The animal exhibited lupine tendencies, stalking its prey with silent patience.
Her lupine eyes glinted in the moonlight, giving her an otherworldly appearance.
The novel describes a man whose lupine instincts make him both feared and respected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolfish - resembling or characteristic of a wolf

Etymology

From Latin 'lupinus' (of a wolf), from 'lupus' (wolf).
