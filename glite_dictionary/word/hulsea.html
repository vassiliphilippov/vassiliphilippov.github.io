Hulsea

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌlsiə / (AmE), / ˈhʌlsiːə / (BrE)
a small genus of erect, balsam-scented herbs native to the Pacific coast of the northwestern United States

Several species of Hulsea are found in the high mountain regions of California.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌl.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈhʌl.si.ə / (BrE)
a small genus of erect, balsam-scented herbs native to the Pacific coast of the northwestern United States.
genus of herbs native to northwestern U.S.

Examples

Several species in the Hulsea genus are prized for their striking yellow flowers.
The rocky slopes of California are a typical habitat for Hulsea plants.
Botanists often study Hulsea for its unique balsam scent and adaptability.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Hulme, a 19th-century American botanist.
