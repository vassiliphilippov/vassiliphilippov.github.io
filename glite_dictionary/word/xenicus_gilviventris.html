Xenicus gilviventris

1. NOUN

/ zɛnɪkəs ɡɪlˈvɪvɛntrɪs / (AmE), / zɛˈniːkəs ɡɪlˈvɪvɛntrɪs / (BrE)
a small, short-tailed bird native to New Zealand, resembling a wren and belonging to the family Acanthisittidae
a small New Zealand bird, wren-like

Examples

The Xenicus gilviventris was spotted moving quickly among the mossy rocks.
Scientists are studying the habitat of the Xenicus gilviventris to understand its decline.
The distinctive call of the Xenicus gilviventris is a familiar sound in New Zealand’s alpine forests.

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Xenicus' from Greek 'xenos' (stranger), species name 'gilviventris' from Latin 'gilvus' (pale yellow) and 'venter' (belly).
