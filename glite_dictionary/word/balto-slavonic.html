Balto-Slavonic

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæltoʊ sləˈvɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌbæltəʊ sləˈvɒnɪk / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family that includes both the Slavic and Baltic languages.
Indo-European language family branch

Examples

The Balto-Slavonic languages share many linguistic features, reflecting their common ancestry.
Lithuanian and Russian are both classified under the Balto-Slavonic branch.
Scholars study the history of Balto-Slavonic to learn about the development of Eastern European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - pertaining to the group of languages including Russian, Polish, Czech, etc.
  • Synonym: Baltic - pertaining to the Baltic language group, including Lithuanian and Latvian

Etymology

From 'Baltic' and 'Slavonic,' referring to the two groups of languages within the branch.
