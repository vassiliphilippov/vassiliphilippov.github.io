Samia cynthia

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.mi.ə ˈsɪn.θi.ə / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.mi.ə ˈsɪn.θi.ə / (BrE)
A large moth native to Asia, introduced into the United States, whose larvae feed on the ailanthus tree.
large Asian moth species

Examples

The larvae of Samia cynthia are commonly found on ailanthus trees.
Samia cynthia was introduced into North America in the mid-19th century.
Entomologists study Samia cynthia for its impact on local ecosystems.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin naming conventions in taxonomy; 'Samia' as genus, 'cynthia' as species epithet.

2. NOUN

/ ˌseɪmiə ˈsɪnθiə / (AmE), / ˌseɪmɪə ˈsɪnθiə / (BrE)
A species of large green silkworm, the larval stage of the cynthia moth, often cultivated for silk production.
a large green silkworm species

Examples

The Samia cynthia is often cultivated for its silk-producing abilities.
Entomologists studied the life cycle of Samia cynthia in a controlled environment.
The lush mulberry leaves were quickly consumed by the hungry Samia cynthia larvae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cynthia moth - a moth species whose larva is Samia cynthia

Etymology

New Latin, from genus name 'Samia' + species modifier 'cynthia'.
