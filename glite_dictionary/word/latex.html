latex

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tɛks / (BrE)
a milky fluid produced by certain plants, which coagulates when exposed to air and is the source of natural rubber and other products
milky plant fluid; source of natural rubber

Examples

Some plants release latex as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
Natural latex is harvested to produce products like gloves and balloons.
When exposed to air, the latex quickly thickens and forms a solid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rubber - elastic material made from latex
  • Synonym: resin - solid or semisolid plant substance exuded by some trees

Etymology

From Latin *latex* (fluid, liquid).

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tɛks / (BrE)
A type of water-based paint in which the binder is latex, used especially for painting walls and ceilings.
water-based paint with latex binder

Examples

We decided to use latex for the living room walls because it dries quickly and is easy to clean.
After spilling coffee on the wall, she was glad it was painted with latex and could be wiped off.
Many artists prefer latex over oil paint for its low odor and fast drying time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic paint - water-based paint in which the binder is acrylic resin

Etymology

From Latin 'latex' (fluid, liquid).
