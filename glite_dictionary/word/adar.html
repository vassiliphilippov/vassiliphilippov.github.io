Adar

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɑr / (AmE), / əˈdɑː / (BrE)
the sixth month of the civil year and the twelfth month of the ecclesiastic year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in February or March
month in the Jewish calendar

Examples

The festival of Purim always takes place in Adar on the Jewish calendar.
This year, Adar falls mostly in March, overlapping with some spring holidays.
Many important events in Jewish history are commemorated during the month of Adar.

Etymology

From Hebrew אֲדָר (*Adār*), the name of the month in the Jewish calendar.
