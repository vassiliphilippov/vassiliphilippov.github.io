to accurse

1. VERB

/ əˈkɝːs / (AmE), / əˈkɜːs / (BrE)
to curse, declare to be under a curse or doom, often invoking divine punishment
declare to be under a curse
archaic

Examples

They believed the land was accursed by the gods for its past sins.
In the tale, the witch accursed the village, bringing misery upon its people.
The king threatened to accurse anyone who defied his command.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - invoke harm or evil upon someone
  • Antonym: to bless - confer or invoke divine favor upon someone

Etymology

From Middle English 'acursen', from Old English 'ācyrcian'.
