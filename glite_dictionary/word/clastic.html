clastic

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæs.tɪk / (BrE)
(of an object or model) capable of being taken apart and reassembled.
able to be taken apart (of objects)

Examples

The professor used a clastic anatomical model to demonstrate the structure of the brain.
She bought a clastic puzzle that can be disassembled and put back together easily.
Engineers examined the clastic prototype to understand its internal components.

Synset Examples

the professor had a clastic model of the human brain

Etymology

From Greek klastos (broken), from klan (to break).

WordNet ID: oewn-00163513-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈklæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæs.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a rock or sediment) composed of fragments of older rocks that have been transported and deposited by mechanical means.
composed of rock fragments
medical

Examples

The formation contains a significant amount of clastic material such as sandstone and siltstone.
Researchers identified the rock as clastic based on its composition and texture.
Many oil reservoirs are found in clastic rocks that were formed from ancient river deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedimentary - (of rock) formed from sediment deposited by water or air

Etymology

From Greek *klastos* (broken), from *klan* (to break).
