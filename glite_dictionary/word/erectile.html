erectile

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛk.tl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛk.taɪl / (BrE)
capable of being raised to an upright position, especially by natural processes in the body or by muscles.
capable of being made upright

Examples

Some birds use their erectile feathers to appear larger when threatened.
The caterpillar displayed erectile hairs as a defense mechanism.
A porcupine has erectile quills that stand up when it feels threatened.

Synset Examples

erectile feathers

Etymology

From Latin erectilis, from erigere (to set up).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛk.taɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛk.taɪl / (BrE)
(of tissue or an organ) capable of becoming rigid and distended, typically as a result of being filled with blood.
capable of becoming rigid with blood (biology, medicine)
medical

Examples

The penis is an erectile organ that can become rigid during arousal.
Some animals have erectile tissues in their tails for display purposes.
Damage to erectile tissue can lead to difficulties with sexual function.

Synset Examples

erectile tissue
the penis is an erectile organ

Etymology

From Latin *erectilis* (that may be erected), from *erigere* (to set up, raise).

Synset POS: s
