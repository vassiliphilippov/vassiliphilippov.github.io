digs

1. NOUN

/ dɪɡz / (AmE), / dɪɡz / (BrE)
a site where excavation is carried out for the discovery of ore, precious stones, or archaeological remains
site of excavation for mining or archaeology

Examples

The team of archaeologists set up camp near the digs in Egypt.
He spent his summer volunteering at the digs where ancient pottery was uncovered.
Many valuable fossils have been unearthed at these digs over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavation - the process of digging up an area or the site itself

2. NOUN

/ dɪɡz / (AmE), / dɪɡz / (BrE)
Informal living quarters, especially ones that are temporary or rented.
temporary or informal living quarters
informal british

Examples

Their new digs were a tiny apartment near the university.
I'm still looking for some decent digs before classes start next month.
She put up a few posters to brighten her digs and make them feel more like home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place to live or stay
  • Synonym: quarters - rooms or lodgings, especially temporary
  • Antonym: home - a person's permanent place of residence

Etymology

Probably from 'diggings', originally referring to lodgings or mining claims in Australia and America, later shortened to 'digs' in British student slang.
