Cnemidophorus tigris

1. PROPN

/ kniːˌmɪdɪˈfɔːrəs ˈtaɪɡrɪs / (AmE), / kniːˌmɪdɪˈfɔːrəs ˈtaɪɡrɪs / (BrE)
A species of active lizard with a network of dark markings, found in semiarid regions from Oregon and Idaho to Baja California.
species of active lizard in western North America

Examples

The Cnemidophorus tigris is known for its speed and agility in escaping predators.
Researchers observed the Cnemidophorus tigris basking on sunlit rocks in the desert.
The unique markings on the Cnemidophorus tigris help it blend into its surroundings.

Etymology

From Greek *knemidophoros* (greave-wearing) and Latin *tigris* (tiger), referring to its markings.
