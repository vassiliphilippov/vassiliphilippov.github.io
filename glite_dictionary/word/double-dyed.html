double-dyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌbəl daɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l daɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) absolute; complete; used to intensify negative qualities, especially in criticism or insult.
utter; absolute (esp. pejorative)

Examples

He was a double-dyed scoundrel who cheated everyone he met.
Only a double-dyed fool would try to cross the river in that weather.
The journalist called the dictator a double-dyed tyrant in her scathing article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoroughgoing - absolute; thorough in every respect
  • Synonym: utter - complete; absolute

Etymology

From the literal process of dyeing fabric twice for greater intensity; figuratively used since the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌb.əl.daɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl.daɪd / (BrE)
(of fabric) dyed twice to achieve greater depth of color or dyed using two different colors.
fabric dyed twice or in two colors

Examples

This shirt is double-dyed to ensure the color lasts even after many washes.
The artisan uses a double-dyed method to create unique fabric designs.
You can notice the difference in tone between regular and double-dyed materials.

Synset Examples

The fabric is double-dyed for a richer color.
That double-dyed scarf was a great purchase.
