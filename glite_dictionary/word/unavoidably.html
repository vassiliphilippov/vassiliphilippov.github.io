unavoidably

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnəˈvɔɪdəbli / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈvɔɪdəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be avoided; by necessity or inevitably.
inevitably; in a way that cannot be avoided

Examples

The two companies were unavoidably drawn into a legal dispute.
His absence from the meeting was unavoidably due to illness.
The deadline was unavoidably postponed because of technical difficulties.

Synset Examples

the situation slid inescapably toward disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - in a way that cannot be avoided or prevented

Etymology

From 'unavoidable' + '-ly'.
