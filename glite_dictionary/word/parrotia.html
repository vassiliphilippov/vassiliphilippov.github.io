Parrotia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːtiə / (AmE), / pəˈrɒtɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous trees in the witch-hazel family, native to Iran and the Caucasus, especially known for the Persian ironwood (Parrotia persica).
genus of trees, incl. Persian ironwood

Examples

The Parrotia tree is prized for its ornamental bark and brilliant autumn foliage.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Parrotia in its native habitat.
A mature Parrotia can add striking color to any garden in the fall.

Etymology

Named after German naturalist Friedrich Parrot (1792–1841).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːtiə / (AmE), / pəˈrɒtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the family Hamamelidaceae, known for the iron tree (Parrotia persica), valued for its hard wood and ornamental foliage.
genus of deciduous trees (iron tree)

Examples

The Parrotia tree is prized for its vibrant autumn leaves and attractive bark.
Botanists classify the iron tree under the genus Parrotia.
Gardeners often plant Parrotia for its colorful foliage and resilience.

Etymology

Named after German naturalist Friedrich Parrot (1791–1841).
