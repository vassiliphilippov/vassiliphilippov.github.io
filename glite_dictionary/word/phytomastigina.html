Phytomastigina

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪtoʊmæˈstɪdʒɪnə / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪtəʊmasˈtɪdʒɪnə / (BrE)
A group of plantlike flagellates containing chlorophyll, often considered a class of unicellular algae.
class of plantlike flagellates (algae)
medical

Examples

Some species classified as Phytomastigina play an important role in aquatic ecosystems.
Researchers are studying Phytomastigina to understand photosynthetic processes in unicellular organisms.
Traditionally, Phytomastigina were grouped with algae due to their chlorophyll content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unicellular algae - algae consisting of single cells

Etymology

From Greek 'phyto-' (plant) + 'mastix' (whip, referring to flagella) + Latin '-ina' (used in taxonomic names).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪtoʊˌmæstɪˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtəʊˌmæstɪˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A subclass of plantlike flagellates containing chlorophyll, often classified as unicellular algae.
subclass of plantlike flagellates (unicellular algae)

Examples

Members of the Phytomastigina subclass are important in aquatic ecosystems as primary producers.
Scientists study Phytomastigina to understand the evolution of photosynthetic organisms.
The unique structure of Phytomastigina flagella aids in their movement and nutrient acquisition.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyto-' (plant) + 'mastix' (whip) + '-ina' (suffix for taxa), referring to whip-like flagella.
