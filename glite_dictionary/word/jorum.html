jorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːrəm / (BrE)
a large bowl used for serving drinks, especially alcoholic beverages such as punch
large drinking bowl
archaic

Examples

The hostess filled the jorum with wine and passed it around the table.
At the party, they drank punch from a massive jorum adorned with silver engravings.
A heavy crystal jorum dominated the center of the banquet table, brimming with ale.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from the character Jorum in the Bible (Jeremiah 35).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːrəm / (BrE)
a large bowl or container, especially one used for serving beverages or soup
large bowl or container

Examples

At the center of the table stood a steaming jorum filled with rich chicken broth.
Grandmother always prepared a jorum of soup for family gatherings in winter.
After the hike, we gladly finished an entire jorum between us.

Synset Examples

A jorum of soup awaited us at the feast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowl - a round, deep dish used as a container for food or liquid

Etymology

Perhaps from 'Joram', a biblical king, in a jocular reference to a big drinking vessel.
