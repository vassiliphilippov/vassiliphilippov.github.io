insurmountable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsɚˈmaʊntəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈmaʊntəbl / (BrE)
impossible to overcome or deal with successfully
impossible to overcome

Examples

The team faced what seemed like an insurmountable challenge at the start of the project.
Debt can become insurmountable without careful financial planning.
Despite many insurmountable obstacles, she managed to reach her goal.

Synset Examples

insurmountable disadvantages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insuperable - impossible to overcome
  • Antonym: surmountable - able to be overcome

Etymology

From in- (not) + surmount + -able; ultimately from Old French 'surmonter', from Latin 'supermontāre'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsɚˈmaʊntəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈmaʊntəbl / (BrE)
(of a problem, difficulty, or obstacle) too great to be overcome.
impossible to overcome

Examples

They faced what seemed to be an insurmountable challenge but refused to give up.
The project was abandoned due to insurmountable technical issues.
Her determination helped her overcome insurmountable odds and achieve her goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbeatable - impossible to defeat or overcome
  • Synonym: insuperable - impossible to overcome (formal/literary)
  • Antonym: surmountable - able to be overcome

Etymology

From Latin 'insurmontabilis', from in- (not) + surmontare (to surmount, to overcome).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00572489-s
Synset POS: s
