titration

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / taɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of gradually adding a measured amount of one solution to another solution of known volume until a reaction is complete, in order to determine the concentration of a substance
chemical method to determine concentration
medical

Examples

The chemist performed a titration to find the concentration of the unknown acid.
Accurate titration requires careful measurement and a clear endpoint.
During the laboratory session, each student was assigned a different solution for titration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to titrate - to measure the concentration of a substance using titration

Etymology

From French 'titration', from 'titre' (proportion, standard).
