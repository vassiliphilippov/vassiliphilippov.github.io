emission

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act or process of discharging a substance, such as water, gas, or radiation, from a source
act of discharging a substance

Examples

The emission of water from the broken pipe flooded the basement.
Regulations now limit the emission of pollutants into rivers and lakes.
A sudden emission of steam alarmed the workers at the plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discharge - the act of releasing a substance from a container or source

Etymology

From Latin 'emissio' (a sending forth), from 'emittere' (to send out).

5. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the process by which electrons are released from atoms, typically as a result of energy absorption or excitation
release of electrons from atoms

Examples

X-rays are produced by the emission of electrons when certain metals are struck by high-energy particles.
The emission of electrons from the surface of the cathode is known as thermionic emission.
Researchers measured the emission rate of electrons under different experimental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ejection - the act of being thrown out or expelled
  • Antonym: absorption - the process by which something is taken in or absorbed

Etymology

From Latin emissio, emissionis (a sending out), from emittere (to send out).
