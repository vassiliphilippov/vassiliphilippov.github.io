invalidated

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvælɪˌdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈvalɪdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having lost legal force or effectiveness; officially declared null and void.
declared legally null or void
legal

Examples

The court order was invalidated after evidence of misconduct was revealed.
Any invalidated contract is unenforceable by law.
The results of the election were invalidated due to widespread voting irregularities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - having no legal force or effect
  • Antonym: valid - having legal force; officially acceptable

Etymology

Past participle of 'invalidate', from Latin 'invalidare' (to weaken, make invalid).
