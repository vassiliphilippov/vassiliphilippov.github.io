incoherent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.koʊˈhɪr.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəʊˈhɪə.rənt / (BrE)
(Physics) of waves, having no stable or definite phase relationship with each other.
not having a definite phase relation (physics, waves)

Examples

Lasers produce coherent light, while ordinary bulbs emit incoherent waves.
Because sunlight is incoherent, its waves do not reinforce each other in interference experiments.
Radio emissions from distant galaxies are often incoherent due to variable origins and timing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coherent - (physics) of waves, having a constant phase relationship with each other

Etymology

From Latin incoherens, present participle of incohaerere (to not cling together); see also 'coherent'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkoʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
unable to express yourself clearly or fluently, especially due to emotion, confusion, or illness.
unable to speak clearly or fluently

Examples

After hearing the shocking news, she became incoherent with shock and couldn't say a word.
His incoherent explanation only added to the confusion in the meeting.
Grief can make a person incoherent, struggling to find the right words.

Synset Examples

felt tongue-tied with embarrassment
incoherent with grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue-tied - unable to speak easily because of shyness or embarrassment
  • Antonym: articulate - able to express thoughts and feelings clearly and easily

Etymology

From Latin incoherens, from in- 'not' + cohaerens, present participle of cohaerere 'stick together'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00153372-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkoʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
Not expressed in a clear, logical, or understandable way; lacking connection or consistency in thought or structure.
lacking clarity or logical connection

Examples

Her speech became increasingly incoherent as she grew more nervous.
The essay was so incoherent that the main argument was difficult to follow.
His explanation was incoherent, leaving everyone confused about what actually happened.

Synset Examples

a turgid incoherent presentation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coherent - logical and consistent, clear and understandable
  • Synonym: illogical - lacking sense or clear, sound reasoning

Etymology

From Latin 'incoherens', from 'in-' (not) + 'coherens' (cohering, sticking together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466953-a
Synset POS: a
