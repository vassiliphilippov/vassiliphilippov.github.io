febricity

1. NOUN

/ fəˈbrɪsəti / (AmE), / fɛˈbrɪsɪti / (BrE)
A rise in the body's temperature, often as a symptom of infection; fever.
elevated body temperature; fever
medical

Examples

The patient presented with persistent febricity and chills over the past three days.
Doctors monitored his febricity after the surgery for any signs of infection.
Unexplained febricity can sometimes indicate an underlying disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - elevated body temperature, often due to illness or infection

Etymology

From Latin febris (fever) + -ity (forming nouns denoting quality or state).
