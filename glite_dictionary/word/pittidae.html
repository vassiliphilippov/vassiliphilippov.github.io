Pittidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, ground-dwelling birds found in the tropics of Asia, Africa, and Australasia, known as pittas.
family of brightly colored tropical birds

Examples

The Pittidae are recognized for their vivid plumage and secretive habits in dense forests.
Many birdwatchers travel to Southeast Asia to observe rare members of the Pittidae family.
Unlike other passerines, Pittidae often forage on the ground for insects and small invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitta - a brightly colored bird of the family Pittidae

Etymology

From New Latin Pittidae, based on the genus name Pitta.
