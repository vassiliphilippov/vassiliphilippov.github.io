photoelectricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
electricity produced or influenced by the action of light, especially as a result of the photoelectric effect
electricity generated or affected by light

Examples

Solar panels generate photoelectricity when exposed to sunlight.
The study of photoelectricity has contributed to advances in modern physics.
Einstein won the Nobel Prize for his explanation of the photoelectricity phenomenon.

Etymology

From 'photo-' (relating to light) and 'electricity'.
