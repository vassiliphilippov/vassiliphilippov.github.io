Zingiber officinale

1. NOUN

/ zɪnˈdʒɪbɚ ˌɑfəˈsɪnəleɪ / (AmE), / ˈzɪndʒɪbə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnaleɪ / (BrE)
The tropical Asian plant whose rhizome is commonly known as ginger, cultivated for its pungent root used as a spice and in herbal medicine.
plant source of gingerroot

Examples

The medicinal properties of Zingiber officinale have been studied for centuries in both Asia and the West.
Many chefs prefer fresh Zingiber officinale when preparing authentic curries and stir-fries.
Powdered Zingiber officinale is commonly used as a spice in baking and cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - the spicy root or product derived from Zingiber officinale

Etymology

From Latin genus Zingiber (from Greek zingiberis), and Latin officinale meaning 'of the storeroom', indicating medicinal use.
