agog

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡɑɡ / (AmE), / əˈɡɒɡ / (BrE)
very eager or excited to hear or see something; filled with anticipation or curiosity
filled with eagerness or excitement

Examples

The children were agog with anticipation as the magician prepared his finale.
She listened agog to the latest celebrity gossip at the café.
Reporters were agog for news after the surprising announcement from City Hall.

Synset Examples

I've been agog all afternoon, waiting for the next episode in your mystery story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excited - very enthusiastic or thrilled
  • Antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Old French en gogues (in good humour, in fun)
