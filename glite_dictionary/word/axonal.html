axonal

1. ADJ

/ ækˈsoʊnəl / (AmE), / ækˈsəʊnəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling an axon, the long, slender part of a nerve cell that conducts electrical impulses
relating to an axon (nerve fiber)
medical

Examples

The study found an increase in axonal damage in patients with the disease.
Researchers measured axonal growth to assess nerve regeneration.
Certain toxins can impair axonal transport in neurons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axon - the long threadlike part of a nerve cell

Etymology

From 'axon', with the suffix '-al' forming adjectives.
