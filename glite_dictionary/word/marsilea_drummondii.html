Marsilea drummondii

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈsɪliə drəˈmʌndiː / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɪlɪə drʌˈmɒndiː / (BrE)
A species of aquatic fern native to Australia, commonly known as clover fern, characterized by its clover-like leaves.
Australian aquatic fern species

Examples

The wetlands were covered in Marsilea drummondii, giving the area a lush, green appearance.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Marsilea drummondii along the riverbanks.
Farmers sometimes use Marsilea drummondii as fodder for livestock in drought-prone regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover fern - Common name for various species of aquatic ferns in the Marsilea genus

Etymology

Genus named after Italian naturalist Luigi Ferdinando Marsili; species named after British-born Australian botanist James Drummond.
