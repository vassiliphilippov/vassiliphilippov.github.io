conger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡə / (BrE)
a large, dark-colored, scaleless marine eel found in temperate and tropical coastal waters, some species of which are used for food.
large scaleless marine eel

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a massive conger he had caught off the coast.
Conger is commonly served grilled in some Mediterranean cuisines.
While diving, we spotted a conger eel hiding in the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eel - a long, slender fish of the order Anguilliformes

Etymology

From Latin 'conger', from Greek 'gongros' (sea eel).
