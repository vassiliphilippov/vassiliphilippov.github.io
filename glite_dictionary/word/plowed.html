plowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈplaʊd / (AmE), / ˈplaʊd / (BrE)
(of land) broken up and turned over with a plow, especially to prepare it for planting crops.
land turned over with a plow

Examples

The plowed fields stretched as far as the eye could see.
After the spring rains, the soil in the plowed land was rich and dark.
Farmers prefer to plant seeds in plowed ground for better crop yields.

Synset Examples

plowed fields

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplowed - (of land) not having been turned over with a plow
