egress

1. VERB

/ ˈiːˌɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈiːɡrɛs / (BrE)
(formal) To go out or emerge from a place.
to go out or emerge

Examples

The firefighters managed to egress from the burning building safely.
In case of emergency, all personnel should egress through the rear doors.
The crew will egress the spacecraft after it lands.

Synset Examples

Water issued from the hole in the wall
The words seemed to come out by themselves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit - to go out or leave a place
  • Synonym: to emerge - to come into view, especially from concealment

Etymology

From Latin *egressus*, past participle of *egredi* (to go out).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈiːɡrɛs / (BrE)
(astronomy) The reappearance of a celestial body after being obscured during an eclipse or occultation.
reappearance after an eclipse (astronomy)

Examples

The egress of the moon was visible just after midnight.
Astronomers carefully recorded the time of egress during the solar eclipse.
The crowd waited with anticipation for the egress of the planet from behind the sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ingress - the first appearance of a celestial body at the start of an eclipse

Etymology

From Latin 'egressus', past participle of 'egredi' meaning 'go out, come forth'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07336738-n
Synset POS: n

the act of going out or leaving a place; an exit or means of departure
the act of going out or an exit

Examples

Related Terms
