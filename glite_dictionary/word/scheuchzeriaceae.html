Scheuchzeriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ʃuːkˌtsɛriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ʃuːkˌtsɛriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of monocotyledonous bog herbs in the order Alismatales, including the genus Scheuchzeria.
family of bog herbs (botany)
medical

Examples

The Scheuchzeriaceae family includes perennial herbs that thrive in wetland habitats.
Scientists study Scheuchzeriaceae to understand plant adaptation in boggy environments.
Only a few genera, such as Scheuchzeria, belong to the Scheuchzeriaceae family.

Etymology

Named after the genus Scheuchzeria, itself named for Swiss naturalist Johann Jakob Scheuchzer.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɔɪxˌzɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ʃɔɪxˌzɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (BrE)
a family of perennial, marsh-dwelling monocotyledonous herbs, typically found in boggy areas, belonging to the order Alismatales.
family of bog herbs (botanical)

Examples

The marshes were rich with members of the Scheuchzeriaceae family, easily identified by their slender stems.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Scheuchzeriaceae in preserving bog environments.
Botanists debate the evolutionary relationships between Scheuchzeriaceae and other monocot families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Juncaginaceae - a similar family of aquatic and marsh plants

Etymology

Named after Johann Jakob Scheuchzer, a Swiss naturalist, with the standard Latin botanical suffix -aceae.
