Eschscholtzia californica

1. NOUN

/ ɛʃˈʃɔltziə ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / ɛʃˈʃɒltziə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A plant species native to the Pacific coast of North America, widely cultivated for its bright yellow to red flowers; commonly known as the California poppy.
plant known as the California poppy

Examples

Eschscholtzia californica is the state flower of California and is celebrated each year during California Poppy Day.
Gardeners often plant Eschscholtzia californica for its vibrant blooms and drought resistance.
The golden fields of Eschscholtzia californica are a familiar sight along the Pacific coast in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California poppy - common name for Eschscholtzia californica

Etymology

Named after German botanist Johann Friedrich von Eschscholtz; genus and species name introduced in 1820.
