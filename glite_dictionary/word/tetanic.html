tetanic

1. ADJ

/ təˈtænɪk / (AmE), / tɪˈtænɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to or causing tetanus, or resembling the muscle spasms associated with tetanus
relating to or causing tetanus
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited tetanic muscle contractions following the infection.
Certain toxins are known to produce tetanic symptoms in mammals.
A tetanic reaction can result in prolonged and rigid muscle spasms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetanus - a serious bacterial disease causing muscle spasms

Etymology

From Greek tetanos (tension, stiffness), related to medical term tetanus.

2. ADJ

/ təˈtænɪk / (AmE), / tɛˈtænɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing tetany, a condition marked by muscular spasms
relating to or causing tetany
medical

Examples

The patient displayed a tetanic response after the nerve was stimulated.
A drop in blood calcium levels may lead to tetanic muscle contractions.
Some toxins have a tetanic effect on the muscular system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetany - a medical condition with muscle spasms due to low calcium
  • Synonym: tetanus - a bacterial disease causing muscle stiffness and spasms

Etymology

From Greek tetanos (rigid tension), related to tetany and tetanus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03135869-a
Synset POS: a
