Titaness

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈtæn.ɪs / (AmE), / taɪˈtæn.ɪs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) any of the primordial giant goddesses, daughters of Uranus and Gaea.
female Titan in Greek mythology

Examples

In the old myths, each Titaness represented a different force of nature.
Rhea, a famous Titaness, was the mother of several Olympian gods.
The museum’s exhibit explores the stories of every known Titaness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Titan - primordial giant gods of Greek mythology

Etymology

From Titan + -ess, denoting female; Titan from Greek Τιτάν (Titán).
