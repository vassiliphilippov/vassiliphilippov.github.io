prolapsus

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈlæp.səs / (AmE), / prəʊˈlæp.səs / (BrE)
the slipping or falling out of place of an organ from its usual position, especially used in medical contexts
medical term for organ displacement
medical

Examples

The surgeon discussed the potential causes of prolapsus of the uterus with the patient.
If left untreated, prolapsus can lead to discomfort and complications.
A rectal prolapsus requires prompt medical evaluation and management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolapse - the falling or slipping of a body part from its usual position

Etymology

From Latin 'prolapsus' (a falling forward), from 'prolabi' (to fall forward).
