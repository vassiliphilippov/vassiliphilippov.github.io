conessi

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈnɛsi / (AmE), / kəʊˈnɛsi / (BrE)
a tropical Asian tree (Holarrhena antidysenterica) with hard white wood and bark formerly used as a remedy for dysentery and diarrhea
tropical Asian medicinal tree
medical

Examples

In traditional medicine, the bark of the conessi is valued for treating digestive disorders.
The conessi grows mainly in tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.
Researchers are studying extracts from conessi for potential antimicrobial properties.

Etymology

From Hindi 'kunai' or 'kureya', via Sanskrit 'kutaja'.
