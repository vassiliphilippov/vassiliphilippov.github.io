Marattia salicina

1. NOUN

/ məˈræti.ə səˈlɪsɪnə / (AmE), / məˈræti.ə səˈlɪsɪnə / (BrE)
A large evergreen fern native to Australasia, having an edible rhizome sometimes used as a vegetable by indigenous peoples.
Australasian evergreen fern with edible rhizome

Examples

The rhizomes of Marattia salicina were traditionally baked as a food source by indigenous communities.
Botanists have documented the habitats where Marattia salicina thrives in New Zealand.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of Marattia salicina in certain forested regions.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Marattia' (honoring Italian botanist C. Maratti) and the Latin 'salicina', meaning 'of or pertaining to willows', referring to the shape of the fronds.
