wrench

1. VERB

/ rɛntʃ / (AmE), / rɛntʃ / (BrE)
to twist or pull something violently or suddenly, especially to remove it from its place or attachment
twist or pull violently, often to remove

Examples

She managed to wrench the bag out of his hands and ran away.
With a sudden movement, he wrenched his arm free from the restraint.
A painful sob was wrenched from her as she heard the news.

Synset Examples

wrench a window off its hinges
wrench oneself free from somebody's grip
a deep sigh was wrenched from his chest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to turn something, especially repeatedly, or to change shape by turning
  • Synonym: to yank - to pull something suddenly and forcefully

Etymology

From Old English *wrencan* (to twist, turn), related to Old Norse *hrækja* (to jerk, twist).
