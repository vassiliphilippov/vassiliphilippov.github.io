Salix amygdalina

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks æmˌɪɡdəˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsalɪks amɪɡdaˈliːnə / (BrE)
A species of willow native to the Old World, known for its light green leaves and often cultivated for basketry.
species of willow used in basketry

Examples

Salix amygdalina is prized in some regions for the flexibility of its branches in traditional basket weaving.
Many craftspeople grow Salix amygdalina to harvest its shoots annually for their workshops.
Unlike other willows, Salix amygdalina has distinctly light green leaves that make it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - any tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning 'willow,' and 'amygdalina' referencing 'almond-like,' from 'amygdala' (almond).
