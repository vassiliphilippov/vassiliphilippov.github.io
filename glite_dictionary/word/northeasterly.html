northeasterly

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrθˈistərli / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈiːstəli / (BrE)
(of a place or direction) situated in or oriented toward the northeast.
oriented toward the northeast

Examples

The hikers took the northeasterly trail through the mountains.
They built the house on the northeasterly side of the lake for better sunlight.
A northeasterly window lets in the morning light.

Synset Examples

the northeasterly part of the island

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: northeast - the direction between north and east
  • Degree Higher: east-northeast - the direction halfway between northeast and east

Etymology

Formed from 'northeast' + '-erly' (suffix forming adjectives indicating direction or orientation).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrθˈistɚli / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈiːstəli / (BrE)
(especially of wind or direction) coming from or directed toward the northeast.
coming from the northeast

Examples

Strong northeasterly winds battered the coastline for hours.
The boat struggled against the northeasterly current as it sailed toward the harbor.
Tomorrow's forecast predicts a shift to northeasterly breezes in the afternoon.

Synset Examples

northeasterly winds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southwesterly - coming from the southwest
  • Synonym: northwesterly - coming from the northwest

Etymology

Formed from 'northeast' + suffix '-erly' meaning 'of or pertaining to.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01606345-s
Synset POS: s
