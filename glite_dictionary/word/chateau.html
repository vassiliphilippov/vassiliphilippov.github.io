chateau

1. NOUN

/ ʃæˈtoʊ / (AmE), / ʃaˈtoʊ / (BrE)
a large and impressive country house or castle, typically one found in France
large French country house or castle

Examples

The family owns a beautiful chateau in the Loire Valley.
During their vacation, they toured an ancient chateau set among vineyards.
The dinner was held in the grand hall of the chateau.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castle - a large fortified building or group of buildings

Etymology

From French 'château', from Latin 'castellum' (castle, fortress).
