reach-around

1. NOUN

/ ˈritʃ əˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈriːtʃ əˌraʊnd / (BrE)
A dubious or unethical method used to gain favor or advantage, especially in political or business contexts.
underhanded method to gain favor
informal slang

Examples

Many suspected the executive used a reach-around to obtain the lucrative contract.
The senator's opponents accused him of a reach-around to get key endorsements.
It was clear from the report that some sort of reach-around had occurred behind the scenes.

Synset Examples

He offered a reach-around to secure the deal.
The politician's reach-around was criticized in the media.
The movie included a funny reach-around scene.
