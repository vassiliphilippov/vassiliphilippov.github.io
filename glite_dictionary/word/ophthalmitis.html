ophthalmitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑf.θælˈmaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒf.θælˈmaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
severe inflammation of the eye, particularly the conjunctiva or the whole eyeball
severe inflammation of the eye
medical

Examples

The patient developed ophthalmitis following cataract surgery.
Prompt treatment is essential in cases of ophthalmitis to prevent vision loss.
Severe redness and pain can be signs of ophthalmitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunctivitis - inflammation of the conjunctiva of the eye

Etymology

From Greek ophthalmos (eye) and -itis (inflammation).
