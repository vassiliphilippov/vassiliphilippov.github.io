cygnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪg.nət / (AmE), / ˈsɪg.nɪt / (BrE)
a young swan, especially one that is not yet fully fledged
a young swan

Examples

The mother swan protected her cygnet from predators near the lake.
As the cygnet matured, its grey feathers turned white.
Several cygnets followed their parents across the calm water.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: swan - large waterbird with a long neck

Etymology

From Old French 'cignet', diminutive of 'cigne' (swan), from Latin 'cygnus'.
