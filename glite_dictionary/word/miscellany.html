miscellany

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəˌleɪni / (AmE), / mɪˈsɛləni / (BrE)
a collection or group of various different items or types, especially written pieces
a collection of various things

Examples

The museum features a fascinating miscellany of ancient artifacts and modern art.
She published a miscellany of short stories, poems, and essays in her new book.
His desk was cluttered with a miscellany of papers, photographs, and mementos.

Synset Examples

a great assortment of cars was on display
he had a variety of disorders
a veritable smorgasbord of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assortment - a collection of different things or people
  • Synonym: variety - several different sorts or types within a group

Etymology

From Latin miscellanea (mixtures), from miscere (to mix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəˌleɪni / (AmE), / mɪˈsɛləni / (BrE)
a collection of various literary works, such as poems, stories, or essays, often by different authors
a collection of literary pieces

Examples

The magazine featured a miscellany of poems, essays, and short stories from emerging writers.
She kept a miscellany of quotes and anecdotes in her journal for inspiration.
The library's rare books section houses a 17th-century miscellany compiled by an anonymous author.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing
  • Synonym: collection - a group of literary or artistic works gathered together

Etymology

From Latin miscellanea (mixtures), from miscellaneus (mixed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06604903-n
Synset POS: n
