Phaseolus angularis

1. NOUN

/ feɪˈsoʊləs æŋˈɡjʊlərɪs / (AmE), / fəˈsiːələs æŋˈɡjʊlərɪs / (BrE)
A bushy annual plant widely cultivated in East Asia for its edible red seeds, also known as adzuki beans, used in flour and cooking.
East Asian bean plant; adzuki bean

Examples

Phaseolus angularis is valued in Japan for the sweet paste made from its beans.
Farmers rotate crops with Phaseolus angularis to enrich the soil.
The seeds of Phaseolus angularis are ground into flour for traditional desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adzuki bean - common name for the seeds of Phaseolus angularis

Etymology

From Latin: 'phaseolus' (bean) and 'angularis' (angular, referring to shape).
