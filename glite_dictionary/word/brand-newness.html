brand-newness

1. NOUN

/ brændˈnuː.nəs / (AmE), / brændˈnjuː.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being completely new and unused
the state of being completely new

Examples

The brand-newness of the car was obvious from its perfect paint and spotless interior.
Some people are drawn to the brand-newness of gadgets, always wanting the latest model.
He missed the excitement and brand-newness of starting at a new school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newness - the quality of being new
  • Synonym: freshness - the quality of being fresh or recently made

Etymology

Formed from 'brand-new' + '-ness', where 'brand-new' dates from Middle English, originally meaning 'fresh from the fire' (as in a new sword or tool).
