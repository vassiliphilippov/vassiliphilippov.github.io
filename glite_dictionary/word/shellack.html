to shellack

1. VERB

/ ʃəˈlæk / (AmE), / ʃəˈlæk / (BrE)
to cover or coat something with shellac, especially to provide a protective or shiny finish
to coat with shellac for protection or shine

Examples

They decided to shellack the old wooden table to restore its original shine.
After sanding the cabinet, she carefully shellacked every surface for extra protection.
It takes several hours for each coat to dry when you shellack a piece of furniture.

Synset Examples

She wanted to shellac the desk to protect it from water spots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - to cover with a clear, protective coating
  • Degree Lower: paint - to cover a surface with a colored liquid

Etymology

Variant of 'shellac,' from French 'laque' (lacquer), ultimately from Hindi 'lākh' (lac, resin).
