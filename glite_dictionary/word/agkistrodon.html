Agkistrodon

1. NOUN

/ æɡˈkɪstrədən / (AmE), / æɡˈkɪstrədɒn / (BrE)
a genus of venomous snakes found in North America and Asia, including copperheads and cottonmouths.
a genus of venomous snakes

Examples

The genus Agkistrodon includes several species of venomous snakes found in North America.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Agkistrodon to better understand their ecological impact.
A bite from some species in the Agkistrodon genus can be dangerous and requires immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copperhead - a common name for venomous snakes of the Agkistrodon genus
  • Synonym: cottonmouth - a common name for Agkistrodon piscivorus, a water moccasin snake

Etymology

From Greek 'ankistron' (fish-hook) and 'odōn' (tooth), referring to the hooked fangs.

2. NOUN

/ æɡˈkɪstrədən / (AmE), / æɡˈkɪstrədən / (BrE)
a genus of venomous snakes found in North and Central America, including species commonly known as copperheads and cottonmouths (also called water moccasins)
genus of venomous snakes (copperheads, cottonmouths)

Examples

The Agkistrodon genus includes both the copperhead and cottonmouth species found in the southeastern United States.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of snakes in the Agkistrodon genus.
Bites from Agkistrodon are medically significant and require immediate attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copperhead - a common name for snakes in the genus Agkistrodon, especially Agkistrodon contortrix
  • Synonym: cottonmouth - a common name for Agkistrodon piscivorus, a species within this genus

Etymology

From Greek agkistron (fishhook), referring to the shape of the snake's fangs, and odon (tooth).
