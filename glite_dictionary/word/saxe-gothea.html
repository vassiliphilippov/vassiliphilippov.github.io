Saxe-gothea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæks ɡəˈθiːə / (AmE), / ˌsaks ɡɒˈθiːə / (BrE)
A genus of South American coniferous trees containing only one species, Prince Albert's yew, native to southern Chile and Argentina.
a genus of South American coniferous trees

Examples

The endangered Saxe-gothea is found primarily in the temperate forests of southern Chile.
Botanists are working to conserve Saxe-gothea due to its limited distribution.
Unlike most conifers, Saxe-gothea has flattened, yew-like leaves.

Etymology

Named after Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha, consort of Queen Victoria.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈɡoʊθiə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈɡəʊθɪə / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the family Podocarpaceae, containing a single species known as Prince Albert's yew.
genus of coniferous tree (Prince Albert's yew)

Examples

The Saxe-gothea is native to the cool, humid forests of southern South America.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Saxe-gothea in different environmental conditions.
The distinctive foliage of Saxe-gothea makes it easy to identify among other conifers.

Etymology

Named after Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha.
