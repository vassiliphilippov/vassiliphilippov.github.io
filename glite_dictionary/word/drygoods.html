drygoods

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌɡʊdz / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌɡʊdz / (BrE)
Textiles, clothing, and related merchandise, especially as sold in a store.
textiles and clothing merchandise

Examples

The department store has a wide selection of drygoods, including fabrics and linens.
She found work managing a small drygoods shop in the city.
Many historic general stores sold both groceries and drygoods to their customers.

Etymology

From 'dry' + 'goods', originally referring to goods other than food and liquids.
