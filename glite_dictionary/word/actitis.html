Actitis

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ækˈtɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, including the common sandpiper and the spotted sandpiper.
genus of wading birds (sandpipers)

Examples

The genus Actitis includes both the common sandpiper and the spotted sandpiper.
Researchers observed the migration patterns of birds belonging to the Actitis genus.
Although similar in appearance, species of Actitis are found on different continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpiper - a type of small wading bird found near water; member of Scolopacidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aktis, aktinos meaning 'ray' or 'beam'.
