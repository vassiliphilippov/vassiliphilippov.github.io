rakishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.kɪʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.kɪʃ.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being confident, lively, and slightly disreputable or unconventional, often in a charming or attractive way.
charming unconventional quality

Examples

There was a certain rakishness about him that made him irresistible to many.
Her rakishness was evident in the jaunty tilt of her hat and the sparkle in her eyes.
The actor brought a touch of rakishness to the otherwise conventional role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffishness - a carefree disregard for convention, sometimes disreputable charm
  • Antonym: respectability - the quality of being socially acceptable or conventional

Etymology

From 'rakish', itself ultimately from 'rake' (a libertine or dissolute person), likely from obsolete rakel (rash, reckless).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪkɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪkɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being stylish and confidently attractive, especially in a slightly unconventional or bold way
stylish and confidently attractive quality

Examples

The actor's rakishness made him stand out on the red carpet.
She arranged her scarf with a touch of rakishness before heading out.
His rakishness was complemented by an easy charm and witty conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegance - graceful and stylish appearance
  • Antonym: slovenliness - messy and careless appearance

Etymology

From 'rakish', originally from 'rake' (a fashionable or debauched man), with -ish and -ness suffixes.
