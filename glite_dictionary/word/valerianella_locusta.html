Valerianella locusta

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɛriəˈnɛlə loʊˈkʌstə / (AmE), / vəˌlɪəriˈɛnələ lɒˈkʌstə / (BrE)
A small leafy annual plant grown for its mild-tasting leaves, often used in salads; also known as lamb's lettuce or corn salad.
a salad plant; lamb's lettuce

Examples

She sprinkled fresh Valerianella locusta on top of the salad for a delicate, nutty flavor.
Farmers often grow Valerianella locusta as a winter salad green in temperate climates.
Although sometimes dismissed as a weed, Valerianella locusta is a prized ingredient in French cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamb's lettuce - mild-flavored leafy green used in salads; alternative common name for Valerianella locusta
  • Synonym: corn salad - another common name for Valerianella locusta

Etymology

From the genus name 'Valerianella' and species name 'locusta', from Latin 'locusta' meaning ‘locust’ or ‘lobster’, referring to the plant's leaves.
