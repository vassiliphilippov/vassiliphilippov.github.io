oculist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.kjə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈɒk.jʊ.lɪst / (BrE)
a medical doctor who specializes in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases of the eye
medical doctor specializing in eye diseases
medical archaic

Examples

After experiencing blurred vision, she made an appointment with an oculist for a thorough eye examination.
The oculist prescribed new medication to treat his recurring eye infections.
Though the term is less common today, an oculist was once the standard word for an eye doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmologist - a medical doctor who specializes in the treatment of eye diseases and surgery
  • Synonym: optometrist - a person trained to examine eyes and prescribe glasses, but not a medical doctor

Etymology

From Latin oculus (eye) + -ist (one who practices).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːkjəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊlɪst / (BrE)
a person skilled in examining eyes for defects in vision and prescribing corrective lenses; an eye specialist, especially an ophthalmologist or optometrist (now largely archaic)
eye specialist; tester and prescriber of glasses
archaic

Examples

After noticing blurred vision, she scheduled an appointment with the oculist for an eye examination.
The oculist recommended new glasses to correct his astigmatism.
In nineteenth-century novels, a visit to the oculist was often suggested for reading difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmologist - a medical doctor specializing in the treatment of eye diseases
  • Synonym: optometrist - a specialist trained to test eyesight and prescribe glasses

Etymology

From Latin 'oculus' (eye) via French 'oculiste'.
