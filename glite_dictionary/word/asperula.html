Asperula

1. NOUN

/ æˈspɛrjʊlə / (AmE), / æˈspɛrjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae commonly known as woodruff, including species with whorled leaves and small flowers.
a plant genus; woodruff

Examples

The sweet-smelling leaves of Asperula are used in traditional herbal remedies.
In early spring, Asperula carpets the woodland floor with delicate white flowers.
Gardeners often plant Asperula for its low-growing, attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodruff - a common name for plants in the Asperula genus

Etymology

From Latin 'asper' meaning 'rough', referring to the rough leaves of some species.

a genus of flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae, commonly known as woodruff, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa
a plant genus, woodruff

Examples

The garden was carpeted with Asperula, giving off a delicate scent in the spring air.
Many species within the Asperula genus are valued for their ornamental foliage.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Asperula extracts in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodruff - a perennial herbaceous plant of the genus Asperula or Galium

Etymology

From Latin 'asper' meaning 'rough,' referring to the texture of the leaves.
