unwholesomeness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈhoʊlsəmnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈhəʊlsəmnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unhealthy or morally harmful.
quality of being unhealthy or harmful

Examples

There was a sense of unwholesomeness surrounding the abandoned building.
Critics often point out the unwholesomeness of fast food diets.
The novel explores the unwholesomeness of unchecked greed and corruption.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesomeness - the quality of being healthy or morally good

Etymology

From 'unwholesome' (not wholesome) plus the suffix '-ness' denoting a state or quality.
