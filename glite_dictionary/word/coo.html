coo

1. NOUN

/ kuː / (AmE), / kuː / (BrE)
the soft murmuring sound made by a dove or pigeon
sound made by a pigeon or dove

Examples

A faint coo came from the dovecote as the birds settled in for the night.
The gentle coo of a pigeon echoed through the quiet courtyard.
Children listened to the soft coo and watched the pigeons gather on the windowsill.

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by pigeons, first recorded in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07395929-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kuː / (AmE), / kuː / (BrE)
to speak in a soft, gentle, or loving voice, often in a way that expresses affection or delight
speak softly or lovingly

Examples

She gently cooed to her baby as she rocked him to sleep.
He cooed words of affection into her ear during the dance.
The couple sat by the fire, cooing sweet nothings to each other.

Synset Examples

The mother who held her baby was cooing softly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisper - speak very softly with breath but no vocalization
  • Synonym: to croon - sing or say something in a soft, low voice

Etymology

Imitative of the soft sounds made by doves or pigeons.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911953-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kuː / (AmE), / kuː / (BrE)
(of a pigeon or dove) to make a soft, murmuring sound; to speak in a soft, gentle, or loving way.
make a soft, murmuring sound (pigeons)

Examples

I heard a dove coo outside my window early this morning.
The baby cooed softly in her mother's arms.
Pigeons coo to each other as they nest on the rooftop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - to make a soft, low sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by pigeons.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911849-v
Synset POS: v
