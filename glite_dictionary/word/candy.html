candy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændi / (AmE), / ˈkændi / (BrE)
a sweet food made from sugar, often mixed with fruit or nuts and formed into various shapes
sweet food made from sugar

Examples

My grandmother always keeps a jar of candy on the kitchen counter for visitors.
He handed out pieces of candy to the children during the parade.
I prefer chocolate candy with nuts and caramel inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet - a small piece of sweet food, especially a piece of candy or chocolate (British English)
  • Synonym: confectionery - sweets and chocolates considered collectively

Etymology

Probably from Arabic 'qandi' (made of sugar), via French 'sucre candi'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkændi / (AmE), / ˈkændi / (BrE)
to coat or preserve (fruit, nuts, etc.) with sugar or syrup, often until crystallized
to coat or preserve with sugar

Examples

We decided to candy some orange peels for the holiday dessert.
My grandmother taught me how to candy ginger to use in baking.
It takes patience to candy fruits properly, but the result is delicious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar-coat - to cover with a layer of sugar or something sweet

Etymology

From Old French *çucre candi* (sugar candy), from Arabic *qandī* (candied), ultimately from Sanskrit *khaṇḍa* (piece, fragment of sugar).
