lost

1. ADJ

/ lɔst / (AmE), / lɒst / (BrE)
no longer in one's possession or control; unable to be found or recovered
unable to be found or recovered

Examples

The police helped search for the lost child in the crowded park.
She felt sad about the lost opportunities of her youth.
He was relieved when his lost wallet was finally returned.

Synset Examples

a lost child
lost friends
his lost book
lost opportunities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misplaced - temporarily lost, especially due to being put in a wrong place
  • Antonym: found - having been discovered or recovered after being lost

Etymology

Past participle of 'lose', from Old English *losian* (to be lost).
