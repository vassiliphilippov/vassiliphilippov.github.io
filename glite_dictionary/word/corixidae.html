Corixidae

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈrɪksɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪksɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic insects commonly known as water boatmen, found in freshwater habitats.
family of aquatic insects (water boatmen)

Examples

Scientists collected several Corixidae specimens from the pond for further study.
The Corixidae are often called water boatmen because of the way they swim.
Members of the Corixidae family can be found in freshwater habitats around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water boatman - common name for insects of the family Corixidae

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from the type genus Corixa plus the zoological suffix -idae.
