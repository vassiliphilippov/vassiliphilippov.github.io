appurtenant

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɜːr.tən.ənt / (AmE), / əˈpɜː.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
providing added support; supplementary or accessory to something else, especially as a rightful belonging or adjunct
supplementary; accessory; providing added support

Examples

The hotel comes with several appurtenant facilities, such as a pool and a gym.
The rights to use the parking area are appurtenant to the building.
The storage shed is considered appurtenant to the main house in the property deed.

Synset Examples

an ancillary pump
an adjuvant discipline to forms of mysticism
The mind and emotions are auxiliary to each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary - providing supplementary or additional help and support
  • Synonym: ancillary - providing necessary support to the primary activities or operation
  • Antonym: principal - main; most important

Etymology

From Anglo-French *apurtenir* (to belong), from Old French *apartenir*, from Latin *appertinere* (to pertain to).
