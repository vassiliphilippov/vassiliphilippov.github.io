Nephelium lappaceum

1. NOUN

/ nəˈfiliəm læpəˈsiəm / (AmE), / nɛˈfɛlɪəm læpəˈsiːəm / (BrE)
A Malayan tropical tree bearing spiny red fruit known as rambutan.
Malayan tree with spiny red fruit

Examples

The orchard includes several Nephelium lappaceum trees, which produce luscious rambutan fruit.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Nephelium lappaceum in different soil conditions.
Farmers export Nephelium lappaceum to countries where the rambutan is considered a delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambutan - the edible fruit of Nephelium lappaceum

Etymology

From genus name Nephelium, from Greek *nephelion*, diminutive of *nephelē* (cloud); species epithet from Latin *lappaceus* (burred, rough).
