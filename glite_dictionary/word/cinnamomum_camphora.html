Cinnamomum camphora

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌnæməˌnʌm kæmˈfɔrə / (AmE), / sɪˌnæmənəm kamˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to East Asia, whose aromatic wood produces camphor, an important chemical used in medicine and industry.
tree producing camphor

Examples

The oil extracted from Cinnamomum camphora is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
In southern China, the Cinnamomum camphora can grow up to 40 meters tall.
Camphor is obtained by distilling the wood of Cinnamomum camphora trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camphor tree - Common name for Cinnamomum camphora

Etymology

From Latinized genus name from Greek 'kinnamomon' (cinnamon) + Neo-Latin species name from Malay 'kapur' (camphor).
