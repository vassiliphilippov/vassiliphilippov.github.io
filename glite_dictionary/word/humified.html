humified

1. ADJ

/ ˈhjuːməˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of organic matter) converted into humus through decomposition.
converted to humus

Examples

The forest floor was covered with a layer of humified leaves.
Agronomists studied the humified content of the soil samples.
Composting speeds up the process by which organic matter becomes humified.

Synset Examples

humified soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humus - the organic component of soil formed by the decomposition of leaves and plant material

Etymology

From 'humus' (decayed organic matter) + '-ify' (to make) + '-ed' (past participle).
