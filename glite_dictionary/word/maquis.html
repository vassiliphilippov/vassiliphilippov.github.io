Maquis

1. NOUN

/ mɑːˈki / (AmE), / maˈki / (BrE)
A French resistance movement that fought against the German occupation during World War II.
French WWII resistance group

Examples

The Maquis played a crucial role in sabotaging enemy operations during the war.
Many young men joined the Maquis to fight for the liberation of France.
Stories of the bravery of the Maquis are still told in French villages today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Resistance - Organized opposition to occupying or controlling forces, especially in wartime

Etymology

French, literally 'thicket, scrubland', referring to where members often hid.
