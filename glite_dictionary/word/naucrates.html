Naucrates

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˈkreɪtiːz / (AmE), / nɔːˈkreɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Carangidae, including the commonly known pilot fish.
genus of fish (pilot fish)

Examples

The Naucrates genus includes species renowned for their association with larger marine animals.
One of the most well-known fishes in the Naucrates genus is the pilot fish.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of Naucrates in tropical waters.

Etymology

From Greek 'naukratēs' meaning 'ruler of ships', referring to the fish's behavior of following ships.

a genus of fish in the family Carangidae, which includes the species commonly known as the pilot fish
a genus of fish (family Carangidae)

Examples

The genus Naucrates includes the well-known pilot fish that often swims alongside sharks.
Researchers observed several species within the genus Naucrates in tropical waters.
Naucrates is distinguished by its streamlined body and bold striping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot fish - a species of fish (Naucrates ductor) known for swimming alongside sharks and ships

Etymology

From Greek 'naus' (ship) and 'krates' (ruler), referring to the pilot fish's habit of following ships.
