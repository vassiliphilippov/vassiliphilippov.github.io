Kangchenjunga

1. PROPN

/ kæn.tʃɛnˈdʒʊŋ.gə / (AmE), / kæn.tʃɛnˈdʒʌŋ.gə / (BrE)
a mountain in the Himalayas, on the border between Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim, the third highest peak in the world at 28,208 feet (8,598 meters)
mountain in the Himalayas; third highest in the world

Examples

Climbers from around the world dream of summiting Kangchenjunga despite its challenging conditions.
The snow-capped peak of Kangchenjunga is visible from many parts of eastern India.
Local communities hold Kangchenjunga sacred and consider it the guardian deity of the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Everest - the highest mountain in the world, located in the Himalayas
  • Degree Lower: Lhotse - a mountain in the Himalayas, the fourth highest in the world

Etymology

From Tibetan 'gangs chen mdzod lnga', meaning 'Five Treasures of the Snow'.
