Neisseria gonorrhoeae

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈsɪəriə ɡəˌnɔrioʊˈiː / (AmE), / naɪˈsiːəriə ɡɒnˌɒriːˈiː / (BrE)
a species of bacteria that causes the sexually transmitted infection gonorrhea
bacterium that causes gonorrhea
medical

Examples

Neisseria gonorrhoeae is responsible for one of the most common sexually transmitted infections worldwide.
Laboratory tests confirmed the presence of Neisseria gonorrhoeae in the patient's sample.
Antibiotic resistance in Neisseria gonorrhoeae has become a major public health concern.

Etymology

Named after German bacteriologist Albert Neisser; from genus name 'Neisseria' and species name 'gonorrhoeae,' referring to gonorrhea.
