Samuel Beckett

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæmjuəl ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsamjʊəl ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(1906–1989) an Irish playwright and novelist, best known for his plays associated with the theatre of the absurd, who lived and worked in France.
Irish playwright and novelist (1906–1989)

Examples

Samuel Beckett is renowned for his play 'Waiting for Godot', a masterpiece of twentieth-century theatre.
Many scholars have analyzed the minimalist style of Samuel Beckett in both his novels and plays.
After moving to France, Samuel Beckett wrote much of his work in French as well as English.
