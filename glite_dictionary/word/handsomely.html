handsomely

1. ADV

/ ˈhæn.səm.li / (AmE), / ˈhæn.səm.li / (BrE)
In an attractive, impressive, or elegant manner; in a way that is pleasing or dignified.
in an attractive or impressive manner

Examples

The lobby was handsomely decorated for the awards ceremony.
She was handsomely rewarded for her years of hard work.
The new book was handsomely bound in leather with gold lettering.

Synset Examples

the volume was handsomely bound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beautifully - in a beautiful manner
  • Antonym: plainly - in a plain or simple manner

2. ADV

/ ˈhæn.səm.li / (AmE), / ˈhæn.səm.li / (BrE)
in a generous, impressive, or substantial manner; to a notable degree.
in a generous or impressive way

Examples

The foundation rewarded the scientists handsomely for their groundbreaking discoveries.
She was paid handsomely for her consulting work with the company.
The team performed handsomely under pressure, impressing everyone with their skill and composure.

Synset Examples

India has responded handsomely by providing 3,000 men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generously - in a way that shows a readiness to give more of something than is necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stingily - in a mean or ungenerous way

Etymology

From 'handsome' + '-ly', with 'handsome' meaning 'generous, noble, or substantial' since the 16th century.
