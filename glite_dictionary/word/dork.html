dork

1. NOUN

/ dɔrk / (AmE), / dɔːk / (BrE)
an informal term for a dull, awkward, or socially inept person, often used as a mild insult
informal, socially awkward or dull person
informal

Examples

I felt like such a dork when I tripped in front of everyone.
Stop being a dork and just ask her out already.
He used to be called a dork in high school, but now he owns his own company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerd - a person seen as overly intellectual or socially awkward
  • Synonym: geek - a person with excessive enthusiasm for a specialized subject
  • Antonym: cool - a fashionable, popular person

2. NOUN

/ dɔrk / (AmE), / dɔːk / (BrE)
an awkward, socially inept, or unfashionable person, often with obsessive interests
awkward or socially inept person
informal

Examples

Even as an adult, he still feels like a total dork at parties.
She called me a dork for wearing mismatched socks, but I didn't mind.
Growing up, he was a dork who preferred books to sports.

Synset Examples

He’s such a dork for loving comic books.
Don’t be such a dorkus; join us for the game!
He's such a nerd, always reading science fiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerd - a person who is very interested in a particular subject, especially computers, and is often socially awkward
  • Synonym: geek - a person who is knowledgeable and enthusiastic about a specific subject, often to the point of being socially awkward
  • Antonym: cool - fashionable and socially adept
