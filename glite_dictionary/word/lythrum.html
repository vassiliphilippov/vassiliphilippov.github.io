Lythrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪθrəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪθrəm / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants in the loosestrife family, known for their spiked clusters of purple, pink, or white flowers
genus of flowering plants (loosestrife)

Examples

Lythrum species are often found growing in wetland habitats throughout Europe and North America.
Gardeners sometimes plant Lythrum for its attractive summer blooms.
The invasive purple loosestrife, a member of Lythrum, poses a threat to native wetland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosestrife - common name for plants in the Lythrum genus and related taxa

Etymology

From Greek lythron (gore, blood), possibly referring to the reddish color of some species' flowers.
