faker

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be something they are not, or who deceives others by making false claims or appearances
person who deceives by pretending

Examples

She realized too late that the art dealer was a faker who sold forgeries.
Many online profiles are run by fakers pretending to be someone else.
The magician exposed the so-called psychic as a faker on live television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposter - a person who pretends to be someone else
  • Synonym: phony - a person who is not genuine; a fraud
  • Antonym: genuine person - someone who is authentic and not pretending

Etymology

From 'fake' (to pretend, to forge) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
