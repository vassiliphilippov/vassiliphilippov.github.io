to immure

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmjʊr / (AmE), / ɪˈmjʊə / (BrE)
to enclose or confine (someone) against their will, especially within walls or a restricted space; to imprison
confine or imprison someone

Examples

He threatened to immure his enemy in the dungeon forever.
The rebels were immured in a dark cell beneath the palace.
She felt immured by the strict rules of her boarding school.

Synset Examples

The suspects were imprisoned without trial
the murderer was incarcerated for the rest of his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imprison - to confine in prison

Etymology

From Medieval Latin immurare, from Latin in- (in) + murus (wall).
