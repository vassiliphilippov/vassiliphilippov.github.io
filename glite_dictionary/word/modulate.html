to modulate

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to vary the frequency, amplitude, phase, or other characteristic of an electromagnetic wave, especially to encode information
vary electromagnetic wave to encode information

Examples

Engineers carefully modulate radio signals to prevent interference with other frequencies.
Most cell phones modulate their signals to transmit voice data efficiently.
The technician used a circuit to modulate the amplitude of the electromagnetic wave.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demodulate - to extract the original information from a modulated carrier wave

Etymology

From Latin modulatus, past participle of modulare (to measure, regulate), from modulus (small measure).

/ ˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)

change musical key

Examples

The composer decided to modulate from C major to G major in the second movement.
As the song progressed, the band chose to modulate the melody for added excitement.
In classical music, it's common to modulate to a related key before returning to the original.
