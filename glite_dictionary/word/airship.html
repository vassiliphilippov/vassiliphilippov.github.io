airship

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈeəʃɪp / (BrE)
A large, steerable, self-propelled aircraft that is kept aloft by a gas lighter than air, such as hydrogen or helium.
self-propelled lighter-than-air aircraft

Examples

The airship floated gracefully over the city, attracting the attention of onlookers below.
During the early 20th century, traveling by airship was considered a luxurious experience.
Engineers are working on new designs to make the modern airship more efficient and environmentally friendly.

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'ship' in the late 19th century.
