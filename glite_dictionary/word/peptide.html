peptide

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛp.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.tʌɪd / (BrE)
a compound consisting of two or more amino acids linked in a chain by amide bonds, formed between the amino group of one amino acid and the carboxyl group of another
chain of amino acids joined by amide bonds
medical

Examples

Insulin is a peptide hormone essential for regulating blood sugar levels.
Scientists are studying how synthetic peptides can be used to treat diseases.
The laboratory synthesized a new peptide with potential antimicrobial properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: protein - large biomolecules made of one or more long chains of amino acids
  • Degree Lower: amino acid - building block of peptides and proteins

Etymology

From Greek 'peptos' (digested) via German 'Peptid'.
