Enterobius

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈroʊbiəs / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈrəʊbiəs / (BrE)
A genus of small parasitic nematode worms, especially known for causing pinworm infections in humans.
genus of pinworms (parasitic worms)
medical

Examples

Enterobius infections are especially common among school-aged children worldwide.
The most prevalent species in the genus Enterobius is Enterobius vermicularis.
Doctors often diagnose a patient with Enterobius after detecting the characteristic eggs in stool samples.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'bios' (life).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈroʊbiəs / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈrəʊbiəs / (BrE)
A genus of small parasitic nematode worms, commonly known as pinworms, which infect the intestines of humans and other animals.
genus of parasitic pinworms
medical

Examples

The most common species of the genus Enterobius is Enterobius vermicularis, responsible for pinworm infections in humans.
Children are particularly susceptible to infection by Enterobius, especially in crowded environments.
A stool sample can help detect the presence of Enterobius eggs in patients showing symptoms of itching and discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinworm - small parasitic worm infecting intestines (common name)

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'entero-' (intestine) and Greek 'bios' (life), referring to its intestinal habitat.
