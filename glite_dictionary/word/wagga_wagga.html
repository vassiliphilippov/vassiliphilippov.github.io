Wagga Wagga

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɑːɡə ˈwɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈwɒɡə ˈwɒɡə / (BrE)
a town in southeastern Australia on the Murrumbidgee River in New South Wales, known as an agricultural trading center
town in New South Wales, Australia

Examples

Many people travel to Wagga Wagga each year to attend the popular agricultural show.
She spent her childhood in Wagga Wagga before moving to Sydney for university.
Farmers near Wagga Wagga often supply produce to markets across Australia.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri, an Aboriginal language, meaning 'place of many crows'.
