Polyplacophora

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌplækəˈfɔrə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌplækəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A class of marine mollusks commonly known as chitons, characterized by their eight-plated shells; sometimes considered an order within Amphineura.
class of marine mollusks (chitons)
medical

Examples

The Polyplacophora are easily recognized by their distinctive, armored shells made up of eight plates.
Biologists study Polyplacophora to understand the evolution of molluscan body plans.
Several species within the Polyplacophora inhabit the rocky shores of temperate oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chiton - A marine mollusk with a shell composed of eight overlapping plates

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'plak-' (plate) + '-phora' (bearing), referring to the animal's many-plated shell.
