Parnassia palustris

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈnæsiə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / pɑːˈnasɪə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant with ovate leaves in a basal rosette and white, star-shaped flowers marked with green veins, commonly found in moist habitats.
a flowering plant with white star-like flowers

Examples

The delicate white flowers of Parnassia palustris brightened the edges of the marsh.
Botanists admire Parnassia palustris for its intricate veining and star-shaped bloom.
Parnassia palustris thrives in wet meadows and boggy soils throughout northern Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Parnassia,' after Mount Parnassus in Greece, and 'palustris' meaning 'of marshes.'
