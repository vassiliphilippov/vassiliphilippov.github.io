monestrous

1. ADJ

/ moʊˈnɛstrəs / (AmE), / məʊˈnɛstrəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) having only one estrous cycle per year.
having one estrous cycle per year

Examples

Bears are considered monestrous animals, experiencing only one breeding season each year.
Because the wolf is monestrous, its reproductive cycle is strictly seasonal.
The veterinarian explained that foxes are monestrous and mate only once annually.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyestrous - having more than one estrous cycle per year

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'oestrus' (frenzy, rut).
