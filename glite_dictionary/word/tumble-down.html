tumble-down

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌm.bəlˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.bəl.daʊn / (BrE)
in a very poor condition, especially because of neglect or lack of repair; dilapidated.
dilapidated or in disrepair

Examples

They bought a tumble-down cottage and spent years restoring it to its former glory.
The school was housed in a tumble-down building at the edge of town.
Many of the tumble-down barns in the area are now being used for storage.

Synset Examples

a street of bedraggled tenements
a broken-down fence
a ramshackle old pier
a tumble-down shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramshackle - badly built or in bad condition
  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin
  • Synonym: broken-down - no longer in good working or living condition

Etymology

From 'tumble' (to fall) and 'down', used figuratively since the 19th century.
