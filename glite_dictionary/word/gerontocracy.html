gerontocracy

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛrənˈtɑkrəsi / (AmE), / dʒɛrɒnˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A form of government or political system in which power is held by older men.
rule by old men; elders governing

Examples

Some analysts argue that the country's leadership is essentially a gerontocracy, with policies reflecting the perspectives of the elderly.
Over time, the gerontocracy struggled to connect with younger generations, leading to social unrest.
Critics claim that a gerontocracy often resists necessary reforms in favor of maintaining the status quo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plutocracy - government by the wealthy
  • Antonym: aristocracy - government by a privileged class
  • Antonym: meritocracy - government by people selected on merit

Etymology

From Greek gerōn (old man) + -kratia (rule, power).
