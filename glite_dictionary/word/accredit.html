to accredit

1. VERB

/ əˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / əˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to officially recognize or ascribe an achievement, quality, or origin to someone or something
to ascribe or assign credit

Examples

The film's director was accredited with the innovative visual effects.
Historians accredit the invention to an anonymous artisan from the 14th century.
Many people accredit their success to hard work and perseverance.

Synset Examples

She was not properly credited in the program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credit - to ascribe an achievement or quality to someone
  • Antonym: blame - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong

Etymology

From Latin accredere (to trust to), from ad (to) + credere (to believe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00729753-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / əˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to officially recognize or authorize someone or something as meeting certain standards, especially by granting credentials or approval
to officially recognize or authorize

Examples

The university was accredited by a national education board last year.
Only certified inspectors are accredited to evaluate the site for safety regulation compliance.
The program is currently seeking to be accredited by a recognized professional association.

Synset Examples

The Regents officially recognized the new educational institution
recognize an academic degree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to certify - to formally attest or confirm as true or meeting a standard
  • Synonym: to authorize - to give official permission for something
  • Antonym: discredit - to harm the reputation of or deny credibility

Etymology

From Latin accredītāre, from ad- (to) + crēdere (to believe, trust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02480958-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈkrɛd.ɪt / (AmE), / əˈkrɛd.ɪt / (BrE)
to give an envoy or ambassador official credentials, authorizing them to represent a government or organization
officially authorize (envoy/ambassador)

Examples

The government decided to accredit a new ambassador to the United Nations.
She was officially accredited as envoy to the European Union last month.
Many countries accredit diplomats to foreign capitals to strengthen diplomatic ties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to authorize - to give official permission or approval for

Etymology

From Latin 'accreditare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'credere' (to believe, trust).
