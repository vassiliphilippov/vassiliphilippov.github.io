unprofitableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈprɑːfɪtəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɒfɪtəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not yielding profit, benefit, or gain
lack of profitability; unprofitability

Examples

The unprofitableness of the project became apparent after several months of mounting losses.
They reconsidered their investment due to the proven unprofitableness of the business model.
Despite their efforts, the company could not overcome the unprofitableness of their new product line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprofitability - the quality or state of being unprofitable
  • Antonym: profitableness - the quality of being profitable or yielding profit
  • Antonym: profitability - the quality or state of being profitable

Etymology

From unprofitable + -ness, where -ness forms nouns indicating a state or quality.
