pentastomid

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.təˈstoʊ.mɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.təˈstəʊ.mɪd / (BrE)
A wormlike arthropod with two pairs of hooks beside the mouth, parasitic in the nasal sinuses of mammals.
parasitic arthropod in mammals' nasal sinuses
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified a pentastomid infection in the dog's nasal cavity.
Certain reptiles and mammals can serve as hosts for pentastomid parasites.
A pentastomid is rarely encountered in routine clinical practice outside tropical regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the structure of the mouth region.
