stagy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒi / (BrE)
(of behaviour, speech, etc.) excessively theatrical or artificial, as if meant for performance on stage.
excessively theatrical or artificial

Examples

The politician's apology was so stagy that few believed it was sincere.
Her stagy gestures during the presentation distracted the audience from her main points.
He delivered his speech in a stagy manner, as if performing for a crowd.

Synset Examples

stagy heroics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrical - exaggerated or staged in manner
  • Antonym: natural - not artificial or affected; genuine

Etymology

From stage + -y (suffix forming adjectives).
