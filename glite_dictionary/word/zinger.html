zinger

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈzɪŋə / (BrE)
a witty, striking, or caustic remark intended to surprise or amuse
witty or striking remark

Examples

The comedian's best zinger had the entire audience laughing out loud.
During the debate, she delivered a clever zinger that left her opponent speechless.
He couldn't resist ending the conversation with one final zinger.

Synset Examples

he always greeted me with a new zinger
she tried to think of some killer of an argument, a real zinger that would disarm all opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quip - a witty remark
  • Synonym: retort - a quick, sharp reply

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'zing' (to move or make a high-pitched sound quickly), with the '-er' suffix indicating an agent or thing that performs the action.
