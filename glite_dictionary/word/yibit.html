yibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈjaɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,208,925,819,614,629,174,706,176 (2^80) bits, or 1024 zebibits
unit of information (2^80 bits)

Examples

The new data center is capable of storing one yibit of information, an enormous capacity for scientific research.
Modern cryptographic systems could theoretically require up to several yibits of memory for maximum security.
A yibit is not commonly used, but it represents an important standard for binary data measurement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zebibit - a unit of information equal to 2^70 bits
  • Degree Lower: bit - the basic unit of information in computing

Etymology

From the prefix 'yobi-' (denoting 2^80 in binary multiples) and 'bit' (binary digit).
