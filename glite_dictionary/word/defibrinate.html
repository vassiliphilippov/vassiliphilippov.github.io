to defibrinate

1. VERB

/ diˈfɪbrɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪbrɪneɪt / (BrE)
to remove fibrin from (blood), typically to prevent clotting
remove fibrin from blood
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician needed to defibrinate the blood sample before running the test.
Certain medical procedures require doctors to defibrinate blood to prevent clot formation.
He explained how to properly defibrinate blood during a demonstration to the medical students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrin - an insoluble protein in blood involved in clotting

Etymology

From Latin prefix de- (removal) and fibrin (a protein involved in blood clotting).
