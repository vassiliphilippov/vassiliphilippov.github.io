grotesque

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
a style of decorative art featuring fantastical or mythical figures, often combining human, animal, and plant forms in incongruous ways
decorative art with fantastic mixed forms

Examples

The cathedral's ceiling was adorned with elaborate grotesques of mythical creatures and vines.
Renaissance artists often painted grotesques to decorate the walls and vaults of palaces.
He was fascinated by the intricate grotesque found on the ancient manuscript's border.

Etymology

From Italian 'grottesca', from 'grotta' (cave), referring to such designs discovered in ancient Roman ruins.

2. ADJ

/ ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
strange or unnatural in a way that is comically or repulsively distorted; ludicrously odd
comically or repulsively strange

Examples

The statue had a grotesque face that startled everyone who passed by.
The comedian wore a grotesque costume during the performance, making the audience burst into laughter.
The shadows on the wall created grotesque shapes in the dim candlelight.

Synset Examples

Hamlet's assumed antic disposition
fantastic Halloween costumes
a grotesque reflection in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to the usual standard

Etymology

From French 'grotesque', from Italian 'grottesca', originally referring to cave (grotto)-like Roman murals with strange figures.

distorted and hideous in appearance
