diaphysial

1. ADJ

/ daɪ.əˈfɪz.i.əl / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈfɪz.i.əl / (BrE)
relating to the diaphysis, the shaft or central part of a long bone
of the shaft of a long bone
medical

Examples

The X-ray images showed a clear diaphysial fracture of the femur.
Bone growth is typically measured in the diaphysial region during development.
The study focused on the diaphysial structure and healing process after injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphyseal - alternative spelling: relating to the diaphysis of a bone
  • Antonym: epiphysial - relating to the epiphysis (end part) of a bone

Etymology

From Greek diaphysis (growth between), referring to the shaft of a long bone.
