Sorex cinereus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrɛks sɪˈnɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɛks sɪˈnɪəriəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the masked shrew, the most common shrew species inhabiting moist areas in North America
a species of North American shrew

Examples

The Sorex cinereus is frequently found in damp meadows and forest floors across Canada and the northern United States.
Scientists studied the diet of Sorex cinereus to understand its role in the ecosystem.
During the survey, several specimens of Sorex cinereus were captured for population analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masked shrew - common name for Sorex cinereus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sorex' meaning 'shrew', 'cinereus' meaning 'ash-colored'.
