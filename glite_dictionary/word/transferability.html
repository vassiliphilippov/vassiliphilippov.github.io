transferability

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsfərəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌtrɑːnsfərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be transferred from one person, place, or situation to another
quality of being able to be transferred

Examples

The transferability of credits makes it easier for students to change universities.
Some skills have broad transferability across different industries.
The transferability of shares is restricted by company policy.

Synset Examples

sterling transferability affords a means of multilateral settlement for … trade between nondollar countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transferable - able to be transferred

Etymology

From 'transferable' + '-ity'; 'transfer' from Latin transferre (to carry across).
