strenuous

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɛn.ju.əs / (AmE), / ˈstrɛn.ju.əs / (BrE)
requiring or using great effort or energy; taxing to the utmost
requiring great effort or exertion

Examples

Running a marathon is a strenuous challenge that tests both physical and mental endurance.
The hikers faced a strenuous climb up the rocky mountain trail.
After several hours of strenuous work, the team finally completed their project.

Synset Examples

his final, straining burst of speed
a strenuous task
your willingness after these six arduous days to remain here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; requiring little effort

Etymology

From Latin 'strenuus' (active, vigorous).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstrɛn.ju.əs / (AmE), / ˈstrɛn.ju.əs / (BrE)
requiring or involving a lot of energy and effort; very active or determined
requiring a lot of effort or energy

Examples

Training for the marathon involved strenuous exercise every day.
After a strenuous climb, we finally reached the top of the mountain.
The job can be physically strenuous at times, especially during peak season.

Synset Examples

strenuous exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - requiring great effort and endurance
  • Antonym: effortless - not requiring much effort

Etymology

From Latin 'strenuus' (brisk, prompt, active).
