parchisi

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈtʃiːzi / (AmE), / pɑːˈtʃiːzi / (BrE)
an ancient board game, originating in India, played on a cross-shaped board, and resembling backgammon.
a cross-shaped board game from India

Examples

My family enjoys playing parchisi together on rainy afternoons.
The rules of parchisi are simple but mastering the strategy can take years.
He bought a vintage parchisi board at the antique shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ludo - a simplified version of parchisi popular in the UK
  • Synonym: backgammon - a board game with dice, similar in goals and gameplay to parchisi

Etymology

From Hindi 'pachisi', referring to the number twenty-five, a key aspect of the game.
