secretly

1. ADV

/ ˈsiː.krət.li / (AmE), / ˈsiː.krɪt.li / (BrE)
In a manner that is not intended to be known or seen by others; in secret.
in a hidden or private manner

Examples

He secretly planned a surprise party for his friend.
She secretly hoped to win the competition, though she didn't show it.
The two met secretly to discuss their future plans.

Synset Examples

they were secretly delighted at his embarrassment
hoped secretly she would change her mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: openly - in a manner that is not secret; without concealment

Etymology

From Middle English *secretly*, from Old French *secret*, from Latin *secretus* (separated, hidden).

2. ADV

/ ˈsiːkrətli / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrətli / (BrE)
In a way that is kept hidden from others; not openly or visibly.
in a hidden or concealed way

Examples

He secretly slipped the letter into her bag without anyone noticing.
The two leaders met secretly in a remote location.
She secretly dreamed of traveling the world someday.

Synset Examples

met secretly to discuss the invasion plans
the children secretly went to the movies when they were supposed to be at the library
they arranged to meet in secret

Related Terms

  • Antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret; publicly
  • Synonym: covertly - in a concealed or disguised manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'secret', from Latin 'secretus' (set apart, hidden), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00167727-r
Synset POS: r
