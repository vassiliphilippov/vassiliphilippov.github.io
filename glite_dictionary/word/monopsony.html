monopsony

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑpsəni / (AmE), / məˈnɒpsəni / (BrE)
(Economics) a market situation in which there is only one buyer for a product or service, despite there being multiple sellers.
single buyer market (economics)

Examples

The defense industry has characteristics of a monopsony because the government is often the only buyer of weapons.
In a monopsony, workers may have little power to negotiate higher wages because only one employer is hiring.
The concept of monopsony helps economists explain why wages remain low in certain sectors where just one company dominates hiring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monopoly - a market with only one seller and many buyers

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'opsōnia' (purchase of provisions).
