oxidoreductase

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪdoʊrɪˈdʌkˌteɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪdəʊrɪˈdʌk.teɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes oxidation-reduction reactions, transferring electrons from one molecule to another
enzyme for oxidation-reduction reactions
medical

Examples

The oxidoreductase family includes many enzymes involved in cellular respiration.
Researchers discovered a novel oxidoreductase that helps plants withstand drought.
Deficiency in a specific oxidoreductase can lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a biological catalyst that speeds up chemical reactions in organisms

Etymology

From oxido-, indicating oxidation, and reductase, an enzyme that reduces.
