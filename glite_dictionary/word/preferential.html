preferential

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɛfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌprɛfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
giving an advantage or showing preference to a particular person or group over others
showing preference or advantage

Examples

The new policy offers preferential treatment to long-term employees.
Children from low-income families receive preferential access to the program.
Some companies have preferential hiring practices for veterans.

Synset Examples

a discriminatory tax
preferential tariff rates
preferential treatment
a preferential shop gives priority or advantage to union members in hiring or promoting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminatory - showing prejudice or bias in favor of or against certain people or groups
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all people equally; not favoring one side over another

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praeferentialis', from Latin 'praeferre' (to prefer).
