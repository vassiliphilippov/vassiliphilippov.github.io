Louis Jacques Mande Daguerre

1. PROPN

/ luːˈi ʒæk ˈmɑːnd deɪˈɡɛər / (AmE), / luːˈiː ʒak mɑ̃d daɡɛʁ / (BrE)
(1789–1851) French artist and inventor who developed the daguerreotype, the first practical photographic process.
French inventor of the daguerreotype

Examples

Louis Jacques Mande Daguerre revolutionized photography with his invention of the daguerreotype.
Many consider Louis Jacques Mande Daguerre the father of practical photography.
A museum exhibit showcased the achievements of Louis Jacques Mande Daguerre and his contemporaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nicéphore Niépce - French inventor who collaborated with Daguerre on early photography
  • Synonym: daguerreotype - an early photographic process invented by Daguerre
