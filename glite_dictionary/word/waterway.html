waterway

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəweɪ / (BrE)
a river, canal, or other body of water that is deep and wide enough for boats or ships to travel through
navigable body of water

Examples

The Panama Canal is an important waterway connecting the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
The city developed rapidly because of its location on a major waterway.
Barges and cargo ships frequently travel along this busy waterway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canal - artificial waterway for navigation or irrigation
  • Synonym: river - large natural stream of water

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a channel, canal, or river through which water flows, especially one used for transportation
channel or route for flowing water

Examples

The city is famous for its extensive network of waterways and picturesque bridges.
Cargo ships use the main waterway to transport goods between the two ports.
They spent the afternoon kayaking along a peaceful waterway surrounded by trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canal - an artificial waterway constructed to allow the passage of boats or ships
  • Synonym: river - a natural wide flow of water across the land into the sea, a lake, or another river
