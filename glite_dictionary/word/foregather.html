to foregather

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈɡæðər / (AmE), / fɔːˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to come together or assemble in one place; to gather or congregate
to assemble; to gather together
archaic

Examples

Every year, the villagers foregather at the old hall to celebrate the harvest festival.
Writers from across the country foregathered to discuss their latest projects.
The alumni will foregather for the annual reunion next weekend.

Synset Examples

We assembled in the church basement
Let's gather in the dining room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together in a group; to collect
  • Synonym: to assemble - to come together as a group for a common purpose

Etymology

From Middle English forgaderen, from for- (together) + gather.
