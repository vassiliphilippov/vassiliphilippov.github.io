rowdyism

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊdiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈraʊdiɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
rude, noisy, or disorderly behavior; rowdy conduct.
noisy or disorderly behavior

Examples

The teacher had no tolerance for rowdyism in the classroom.
The match was marred by rowdyism among some of the fans.
There have been increased efforts to reduce rowdyism during public celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowdiness - the quality of being noisy, rough, and disorderly

Etymology

From 'rowdy' + '-ism', modeled on similar formations denoting a behavior or practice.
