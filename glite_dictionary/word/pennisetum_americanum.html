Pennisetum americanum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm əˌmɛrɪˈkænəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm əˌmɛrɪˈkanəm / (BrE)
A tall grass species with cattail-like spikes, grown for its grain in Africa and Asia and for forage in the United States.
species of tall grass; agricultural use

Examples

Pennisetum americanum is widely grown in arid regions due to its drought tolerance.
The farmer relies on Pennisetum americanum as a key forage crop for livestock.
Traditional beer in some African communities is made using Pennisetum americanum grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearl millet - another common name for Pennisetum americanum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Pennisetum' (meaning bristly grass) and species name 'americanum'.
