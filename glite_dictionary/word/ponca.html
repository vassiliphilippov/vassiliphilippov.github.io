Ponca

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋ.kə / (BrE)
the Dhegiha dialect of the Siouan language family spoken by the Ponca people, a Native American group originally from the Midwest United States
Native American language of the Ponca people

Examples

Very few people today are fluent speakers of Ponca.
Linguists have worked to preserve the traditional stories told in Ponca.
He learned Ponca to connect more deeply with his heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Omaha - the related Dhegiha Siouan dialect spoken by the Omaha people

Etymology

From the name of the Ponca people, a Native American tribe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːŋkə / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋkə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of the Siouan language family, originally from the Missouri River valley in northeastern Nebraska
member of a Native American people from Nebraska

Examples

The Ponca have a rich tradition of storytelling and ceremonial dance.
In the nineteenth century, many Ponca were forcibly relocated to what is now Oklahoma.
A Ponca elder spoke at the ceremony, sharing the tribe's history with the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a member of the group of Native American peoples to which the Ponca are related

Etymology

From the tribe’s own name for themselves, Ponka, in the Siouan language.
