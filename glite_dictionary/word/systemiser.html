systemiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəməˌzaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəməˌzaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who arranges things into a systematic or orderly structure
person who organizes things systematically

Examples

As a natural systemiser, Maya always brought order to chaotic projects.
The company's success depended on its chief systemiser to streamline procedures.
Aristotle is often regarded as a great systemiser of philosophical ideas.

Synset Examples

Aristotle was a great orderer of ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges or systematizes things
  • Antonym: disorganizer - a person who causes disorder or confusion

Etymology

From 'system' + '-iser', on analogy with words like 'organiser'; '-iser' from Latin '-izare', influenced by French '-iser'.
