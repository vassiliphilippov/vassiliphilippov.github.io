flammability

1. NOUN

/ flæməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / flæməˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The ability of a substance to be easily set on fire and burn quickly.
ability to catch fire easily

Examples

You should always check the flammability of materials before using them in a construction project.
Government regulations require labels to indicate the flammability of clothing.
Scientists test the flammability of different chemicals to ensure laboratory safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflammability - the ability to catch fire easily
  • Antonym: nonflammability - the inability to catch fire easily

Etymology

From Latin 'flammare' (to set on fire) + English '-bility' (ability).
