bagasse

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡæs / (AmE), / bəˈɡas / (BrE)
the dry, fibrous pulp left after extracting juice from sugar cane or similar plants, often used as fuel or in the production of paper and building materials
fibrous pulp left after extracting juice from sugar cane

Examples

The factory burns bagasse to produce steam for electricity generation.
Paper made from bagasse is an eco-friendly alternative to traditional wood pulp paper.
After juicing the sugar cane, only bagasse was left behind.

Etymology

From French 'bagasse', meaning refuse or waste from pressed sugar cane, probably from Spanish.
