derisively

1. ADV

/ dəˈraɪ.sɪv.li / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪ.sɪv.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows ridicule or scorn; mockingly or contemptuously
in a mocking or scornful manner

Examples

She laughed derisively at his suggestion, making him feel foolish.
The critics responded derisively to the film, calling it a disaster.
He shook his head derisively, clearly unimpressed by their efforts.

Synset Examples

‘Sorry,’ she repeated derisively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockingly - in a manner intended to make fun of or ridicule
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a manner showing respect or esteem

Etymology

From Latin deridere (to deride), via French dérisif and English derisive + -ly.
