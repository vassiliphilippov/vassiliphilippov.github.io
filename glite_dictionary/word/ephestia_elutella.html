Ephestia elutella

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈfɛstiə ɛˌlutɛlə / (AmE), / ɛˈfɛstiə ɛˌljuːtɛlə / (BrE)
A small moth whose larvae feed on tobacco and other dried plant products, known as a pest in stored goods.
species of small moth pest

Examples

The warehouse suffered a severe infestation of Ephestia elutella that damaged the stored grain.
Tobacco manufacturers take measures to prevent Ephestia elutella from contaminating their products.
Scientists are studying the lifecycle of Ephestia elutella to develop better pest control methods.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin scientific nomenclature: genus name 'Ephestia' and species epithet 'elutella'.
