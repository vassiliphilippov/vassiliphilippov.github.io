eightsome

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtsʌm / (AmE), / ˈeɪtsəm / (BrE)
A group of eight people considered as a single unit.
group of eight people

Examples

We managed to put together an eightsome for tonight's dinner party.
The eightsome spent the whole weekend at the lakeside cabin.
During the tournament, each eightsome played at a different table.

Etymology

Formed from 'eight' + '-some' (meaning a group of a specified number).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtsəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪtsəm / (BrE)
a Scottish reel performed by eight dancers, often as a traditional group dance
Scottish dance for eight people

Examples

The band started playing an eightsome at the ceilidh, inviting everyone to the dance floor.
He practiced the eightsome steps in preparation for the upcoming folk festival.
During the wedding, the guests joined in a lively eightsome that lasted almost half an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - a lively Scottish or Irish folk dance

Etymology

From 'eight' + '-some', indicating a group of eight.
