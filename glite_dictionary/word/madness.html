madness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædnəs / (AmE), / ˈmædnəs / (BrE)
(Obsolete) Legal insanity; the state of being mentally ill as recognized by law.
obsolete term for legal insanity
archaic

Examples

In past legal documents, madness was often cited as grounds for exemption from responsibility.
She was declared to be suffering from madness under the law of the time.
The defendant's plea of madness was accepted by the court in the trial records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - the state of being seriously mentally ill; madness
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of being of sound mind; opposite of insanity

Etymology

From Old English 'gemǣdness' (insanity, foolishness), from 'gemǣd' (mad) + '-ness'.
