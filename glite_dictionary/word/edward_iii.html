Edward III

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.wɚd ðə θɜrd / (AmE), / ˈɛd.wəd ðə θɜːd / (BrE)
(1312–1377) King of England from 1327 to 1377, known for initiating the Hundred Years' War with France and overseeing major events such as the Black Death and the rise of the House of Commons.
King of England, 1327–1377

Examples

Edward III was crowned King of England after his father's abdication in 1327.
During the reign of Edward III, the Hundred Years' War began between England and France.
A severe outbreak of the Black Death occurred while Edward III was on the throne.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Edward IV - Later King of England named Edward
