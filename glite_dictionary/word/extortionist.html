extortionist

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɔrʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who obtains money or other benefits from someone by threatening them, especially by threatening to reveal private or damaging information
a person who commits extortion

Examples

The extortionist threatened to release the photos unless he was paid a large sum of money.
After months of fear, the victim finally reported the extortionist to the authorities.
Police managed to track down the extortionist by following the money trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmailer - a person who demands money by threatening to reveal secrets
  • Antonym: victim - a person who suffers from extortion or crime

Etymology

From 'extortion' (Latin extortio) with suffix '-ist' indicating a person who does an action.
