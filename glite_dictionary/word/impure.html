impure

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpjʊr / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊə / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) morally corrupt or obscene; lacking purity in thoughts or actions.
morally corrupt or obscene

Examples

The teacher warned the students against harboring impure thoughts.
She avoided movies she felt contained impure content.
Some religious traditions prohibit impure behavior to promote spiritual well-being.

Synset Examples

impure thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure - free from moral fault or impurity

Etymology

From Latin 'impurus', meaning unclean or foul, from 'in-' (not) + 'purus' (pure).

WordNet ID: oewn-01911556-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpjʊr / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊə / (BrE)
not clean or pure, especially not meeting the requirements of religious, moral, or ceremonial standards; contaminated or defiled by something considered wrong or forbidden
not clean; morally or ritually unclean

Examples

The food was considered impure according to the dietary laws of the community.
Certain animals were labeled impure in ancient religious texts and forbidden to eat.
He believed his hands were impure until he performed the cleansing ritual.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure - free from contamination or moral blemish

Etymology

From Latin impurus, from in- (not) + purus (pure).

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpjʊr / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊə / (BrE)
mixed with other, usually unwanted, substances; not clean or pure.
not pure; mixed with other substances

Examples

The laboratory rejected the impure sample because it contained traces of other metals.
Drinking impure water can lead to various health problems.
The gold was found to be impure, so it required further refining.

Related Terms

Etymology
