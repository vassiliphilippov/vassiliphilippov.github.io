Loiseleuria procumbens

1. NOUN

/ loʊ.ɪˈslɜːr.i.ə proʊˈkʌm.bənz / (AmE), / ləʊ.ɪˈslɜː.rɪ.ə prəʊˈkʌm.bənz / (BrE)
a creeping, mat-forming evergreen shrub found in high mountain regions of the Northern Hemisphere, grown for its clusters of rose-pink flowers; also called alpine azalea
creeping alpine shrub with rose-pink flowers

Examples

The delicate pink flowers of the Loiseleuria procumbens can be seen carpeting alpine slopes in late spring.
Gardeners admire Loiseleuria procumbens for its ability to thrive in rocky, high-altitude environments.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Loiseleuria procumbens in the northern mountains.

Etymology

From the genus 'Loiseleuria,' named in honor of French botanist Jean-Louis-Auguste Loiseleur-Deslongchamps, and Latin 'procumbens' meaning 'lying down' or 'prostrate'.
