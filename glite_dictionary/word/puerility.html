puerility

1. NOUN

/ pjʊˈɪr.ə.lə.ti / (AmE), / pjʊəˈrɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being childish or immature, especially in thought or behavior
childishness; immaturity

Examples

The philosopher criticized the puerility of the debate, calling for more serious discussion.
Her constant giggling and jokes revealed a certain puerility that others found tiresome.
Literary critics often dismiss such novels as displays of mere puerility rather than true insight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childishness - the state of being childish
  • Antonym: maturity - the state of being mature

Etymology

From Latin 'puerilis' (boyish, childish), from 'puer' (boy).

2. NOUN

/ pjʊˈɪr.əl.ə.ti / (AmE), / pjʊəˈrɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the period or condition of being a child, especially between infancy and adolescence
state or period of childhood

Examples

The puerility of his behavior surprised everyone at the meeting.
Many novels explore the transition from puerility to adulthood.
Some critics argued that the comedy relied too much on jokes rooted in puerility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childhood - the period of being a child

Etymology

From Latin 'puerilis' (boyish, youthful), from 'puer' (boy).
