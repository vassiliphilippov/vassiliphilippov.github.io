Pteridospermaphyta

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrɪdoʊˌspɜːrməˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪdəʊˌspɜːməˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
a group of extinct seed-producing plants, often called seed ferns, classified as fossil gymnosperms and coextensive with the order Cycadofilicales
group of extinct seed ferns (fossil plants)

Examples

The Pteridospermaphyta were dominant during the late Paleozoic era.
Fossils of Pteridospermaphyta provide important evidence of early seed plant evolution.
Scientists debate the classification of Pteridospermaphyta within gymnosperms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed fern - common name for plants of Pteridospermaphyta

Etymology

From Greek 'pteris' (fern), 'sperma' (seed), and 'phyton' (plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrɪˌdoʊˌspɜrməˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪdəʊˌspɜːməˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a group of extinct gymnosperms, known as seed ferns, primarily coextensive with the order Cycadofilicales, recognized in some taxonomic classifications based on fossil evidence
group of extinct seed ferns (fossil plants)

Examples

Fossils of Pteridospermaphyta indicate these plants were widespread during the late Paleozoic era.
The classification of Pteridospermaphyta has been subject to revision as new fossil evidence emerges.
Scientists believe Pteridospermaphyta played a crucial role in the evolution of early seed plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cycadofilicales - an order of extinct seed ferns within Pteridospermaphyta

Etymology

From Greek pteris (fern) + sperma (seed) + phyton (plant), denoting seed-bearing plants resembling ferns.
