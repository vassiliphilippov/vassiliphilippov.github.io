Proteus anguinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtiəs ænˈɡwaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtiːəs æŋˈɡwiːnəs / (BrE)
A European aquatic salamander, also known as the olm, with permanent external gills, living in caves and adapted to complete darkness.
European cave-dwelling aquatic salamander

Examples

The Proteus anguinus is uniquely adapted to life in the pitch-black caves of the Balkans.
Scientists study Proteus anguinus to learn about evolution in isolated environments.
Unlike most salamanders, Proteus anguinus retains its gills throughout its life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olm - Common name for Proteus anguinus, a cave-dwelling salamander

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Proteus' is a sea god and 'anguinus' means 'of a snake'.
