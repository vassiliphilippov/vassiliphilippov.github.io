to expect

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛkt / (BrE)
(of a woman or couple) to be pregnant or to be awaiting the birth of a child.
be pregnant; awaiting a child's birth

Examples

She's expecting her second child in September.
Many people guessed that they were expecting when they made the announcement.
Are you expecting again, or is it just a rumor?

Synset Examples

She is expecting in March

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to look forward to the probable or likely occurrence of something; to anticipate that something will happen
look forward to the likely occurrence of

Examples

We expect heavy rain by the end of the afternoon.
She didn't expect the meeting to last so long.
They are expecting a big announcement from the company later this week.

Synset Examples

We were expecting a visit from our relatives
She is looking to a promotion
he is waiting to be drafted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or predict; to look forward to

Etymology

From Latin 'expectare' (to look out for, await), from 'ex-' (out) + 'spectare' (to look).
