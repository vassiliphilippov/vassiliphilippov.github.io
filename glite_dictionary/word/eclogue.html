eclogue

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.lɔg / (AmE), / ˈɛk.lɒg / (BrE)
a short poem in the form of a conversation or monologue that typically describes rural or pastoral life
pastoral poem or dialogue

Examples

The poet composed an eclogue to capture the beauty of the countryside.
Her favorite literary form is the eclogue, reflecting peaceful rural life.
Many Renaissance writers experimented with the eclogue in their early works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - a poem dealing with shepherds or rural life

Etymology

From Latin 'ecloga' (selection, short poem), from Greek 'eklogē' (selection).
