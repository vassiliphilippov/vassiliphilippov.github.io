bother

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːðər / (AmE), / ˈbɒðə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes trouble, difficulty, or annoyance
source of trouble or annoyance

Examples

The broken lock was a real bother every time I tried to open the door.
It's not much of a bother to help you with your homework.
Carrying all those bags home from the store was quite a bother.

Synset Examples

a bit of a bother
he's not a friend, he's an infliction
washing dishes was a pain in the neck before we got a dish washer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a person, thing, or situation causing annoyance or inconvenience
  • Synonym: annoyance - something that causes slight anger or irritation
  • Antonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment

Etymology

Probably from earlier verb sense, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Irish bodhar (to annoy).
