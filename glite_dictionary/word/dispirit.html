to dispirit

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
to make someone lose enthusiasm, hope, or confidence; to discourage or demoralize
make someone lose enthusiasm or hope

Examples

The constant criticism began to dispirit her, making her doubt her abilities.
We must not let failure dispirit us from trying again.
The team's defeat did nothing to dispirit their loyal fans.

Synset Examples

These news depressed her
The bad state of her child's health demoralizes her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to encourage - give support or confidence to someone
  • Synonym: to demoralize - cause someone to lose confidence or hope
  • Synonym: to dishearten - cause someone to lose hope or confidence

Etymology

From Latin 'spiritus' (spirit) with the prefix 'dis-' indicating reversal or removal.
