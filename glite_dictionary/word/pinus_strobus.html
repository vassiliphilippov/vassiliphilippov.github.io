Pinus strobus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈstroʊ.bəs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈstrəʊ.bəs / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to eastern North America, with soft, flexible needles and brown bark with longitudinal fissures when mature; valued for its timber
tall North American pine tree; timber source

Examples

The Pinus strobus is prized for its lightweight and durable wood, commonly used in construction.
Forests of Pinus strobus once covered much of northeastern America before extensive logging began.
Gardeners appreciate Pinus strobus for its fine, soft needles and graceful, pyramid shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern white pine - another common name for Pinus strobus
  • Antonym: Pinus sylvestris - Scots pine, a different pine species native to Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Pinus' meaning 'pine' and 'strobus' an ancient name for a kind of pine.
