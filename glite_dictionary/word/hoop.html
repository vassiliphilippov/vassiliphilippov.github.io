hoop

1. VERB

/ huːp / (AmE), / huːp / (BrE)
to bind or fasten (something) with a hoop, especially to strengthen or secure it
bind or fasten with a hoop

Examples

The cooper carefully hooped the barrel before filling it with wine.
They used metal bands to hoop the large wooden vats in the factory.
Each tub was hooped for strength to prevent it from falling apart.

2. NOUN

/ huːp / (AmE), / huːp / (BrE)
In basketball, a horizontal circular metal ring supporting a net, through which players attempt to score points by throwing the ball.
basketball ring with net

Examples

He made an impressive shot through the hoop to win the game.
The hoop was slightly bent after the match, making shots more difficult.
Kids spent hours practicing their throws at the basketball hoop in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket - the entire basketball goal structure, including the hoop and net

/ huːp / (AmE), / huːp / (BrE)

/ huːp / (AmE), / huːp / (BrE)
a rigid circular band, typically made of metal, wood, or other material, used for holding together or fastening objects, or for hanging or pulling things
rigid circular band for fastening or holding

Examples

He attached the bucket to the hoop and lowered it into the well.
The old wooden barrel was held together by iron hoops.
A metal hoop stuck out from the wall for hanging tools.
