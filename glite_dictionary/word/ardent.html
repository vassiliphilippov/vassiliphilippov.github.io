ardent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrdənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːdnt / (BrE)
glowing or shining like fire; showing strong enthusiasm or passion often associated with a fiery appearance.
glowing or shining like fire

Examples

Her ardent gaze seemed to illuminate the entire room.
The actor's ardent expression captivated the audience, burning with an inner intensity.
The painting captured the ardent glow of the sunset on the horizon.

Synset Examples

from rank to rank she darts her ardent eyes
frightened by his ardent burning eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiery - having the color or quality of fire
  • Synonym: fervent - having or displaying passionate intensity

Etymology

From Latin ardēns, ardent-, present participle of ardēre (to burn).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑrdənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːdənt / (BrE)
displaying or characterized by strong enthusiasm, passion, or intense feeling
passionate; enthusiastic

Examples

Her ardent support for the arts has inspired many young musicians.
He is an ardent fan of classic literature and attends every book club meeting.
Despite the setbacks, their ardent belief in the project kept the team motivated.

Synset Examples

ardent revolutionaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief
  • Synonym: enthusiastic - having or showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: lukewarm - showing little enthusiasm, interest, or conviction

Etymology

From Latin ardent-, ardēns (burning, glowing), from ardēre (to burn).
