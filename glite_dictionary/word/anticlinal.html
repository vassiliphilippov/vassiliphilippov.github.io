anticlinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈklaɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈklaɪ.nəl / (BrE)
sloping downward away from a central ridge or crest, especially said of rock layers or plant cell walls
sloping away from a crest

Examples

The anticlinal structure of the mountain range explains the distribution of its rivers.
Geologists identified the anticlinal folds in the rock formations during their survey.
The plant’s anticlinal cell walls are important in determining tissue organization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synclinal - sloping downward toward a common trough

Etymology

From French anticlinal, from Greek anti- (against) + klinein (to lean).
