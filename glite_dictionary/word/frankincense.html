frankincense

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋ.kɪnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈfræŋ.kɪn.səns / (BrE)
an aromatic gum resin from certain Arabian or East African trees, used in incense, perfumes, and formerly in religious rites and embalming
aromatic resin used in incense and rituals

Examples

The church was filled with the rich scent of frankincense during the ceremony.
Traders brought frankincense and myrrh along ancient caravan routes.
Archaeologists discovered traces of frankincense in early Egyptian tombs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incense - a substance burned for its fragrant smoke, often for ceremonial purposes

Etymology

From Old French 'franc encens' meaning 'high-quality incense' (literally 'free' or 'pure' incense).
