Prunus subhirtella

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs sʌbˌhɜːrˈtɛlə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs sʌbˌhɜːˈtɛlə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to Japan, cultivated as an ornamental for its rose-pink flowers
ornamental Japanese cherry tree or shrub

Examples

The Prunus subhirtella blooms early in spring, brightening gardens with pink blossoms.
Botanists often recommend Prunus subhirtella for landscaping because of its ornamental value.
We planted a Prunus subhirtella in the front yard to enjoy its graceful flowers each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry blossom - flower of any cherry tree, especially those in the genus Prunus
  • Degree Higher: Prunus - the genus of trees and shrubs which includes cherries, plums, almonds, and apricots

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' (plum, cherry) and 'subhirtella' (somewhat hairy, referring to the plant's features).
