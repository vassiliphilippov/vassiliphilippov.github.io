chelonian

1. ADJ

/ kəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊniən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of turtles and tortoises
relating to turtles or tortoises

Examples

Scientists studied the chelonian features that distinguish turtles from other reptiles.
Herpetologists monitor chelonian populations to understand their migration patterns.
The chelonian shell provides significant protection against predators.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Chelonia (the order of turtles), from Greek khelōnē (tortoise).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊniən / (BrE)
A reptile of the order Chelonia, including turtles, tortoises, and terrapins.
reptile of the order Chelonia

Examples

The giant Galápagos tortoise is an endangered chelonian species found only on the islands.
Many freshwater chelonians are threatened by habitat loss and pollution.
A chelonian shell is composed of a carapace on top and a plastron underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - a reptile of the order Chelonia, especially one that lives in water
  • Synonym: tortoise - a land-dwelling reptile of the order Chelonia

Etymology

From Greek khelōnē (tortoise) + -ian.

3. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊniən / (BrE)
a reptile of the order Chelonia, including turtles, tortoises, and terrapins, characterized by a bony or cartilaginous shell
reptile of the turtle and tortoise group

Examples

The Galápagos tortoise is a giant chelonian that can live for over a century.
Marine biologists discovered an endangered chelonian nesting on the remote beach.
Both the turtle and terrapin are classified as chelonians by scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - a reptile with a hard shell that lives in water and on land
  • Synonym: tortoise - a land-dwelling chelonian reptile

Etymology

From Greek 'khelōnē' meaning 'tortoise'.
