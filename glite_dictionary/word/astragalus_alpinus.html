Astragalus alpinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈɡeɪləs ælˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈɡeɪləs ælˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
A perennial flowering plant of mountainous areas in Eurasia and North America, belonging to the pea family.
mountainous perennial plant species

Examples

The rocky slopes were dotted with blooming Astragalus alpinus throughout the short summer season.
Scientists studying arctic ecosystems frequently encounter Astragalus alpinus among the native flora.
Hikers admired the delicate purple flowers of Astragalus alpinus high above the treeline.

Etymology

From Latin 'Astragalus' (a genus of plants) and Latin 'alpinus' (alpine, of the Alps).
