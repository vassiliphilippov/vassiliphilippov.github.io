Chou En-lai

1. PROPN

/ ʧoʊ ɛnˈlaɪ / (AmE), / ʧəʊ ɛnˈlaɪ / (BrE)
(1898–1976) Chinese revolutionary and leading communist statesman who served as premier of the People's Republic of China from 1949 to 1976.
Chinese communist leader and premier (1898–1976)

Examples

Chou En-lai played a key role in shaping modern China's foreign policy.
The negotiations were led by Chou En-lai during the 1954 Geneva Conference.
Many historians regard Chou En-lai as one of the most influential Chinese leaders of the 20th century.
