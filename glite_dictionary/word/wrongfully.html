wrongfully

1. ADV

/ ˈrɔŋ.fəli / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋ.fəli / (BrE)
In an unjust, unfair, or illegal manner.
in an unjust or illegal way

Examples

Many people have been wrongfully convicted on the basis of unreliable evidence.
The company denied that any workers had been wrongfully terminated.
She was wrongfully accused of theft and spent months clearing her name.

Synset Examples

the employee claimed that she was wrongfully dismissed
people who were wrongfully imprisoned should be released

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unjustly - in a way that is not morally right or fair
  • Antonym: justly - in a fair or morally correct way

Etymology

From "wrongful" + "-ly", formed from "wrong" (Old English *wrang*) + "-ful" and the adverbial suffix "-ly".
