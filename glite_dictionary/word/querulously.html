querulously

1. ADV

/ ˈkwɛr.ə.ləs.li / (AmE), / ˈkwɛr.jʊ.ləs.li / (BrE)
in a complaining or whining manner, often expressing dissatisfaction or annoyance
in a complaining or whiny way

Examples

She spoke querulously about the unfairness of the new policy.
The child querulously demanded attention from his parents.
He querulously questioned every decision made by the committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peevishly - in an irritable or fretful manner

Etymology

From Latin 'querulus' (complaining), from 'queri' (to complain).
