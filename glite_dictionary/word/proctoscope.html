proctoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑk.təˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈprɒk.təˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument used for examining the rectum
instrument for rectal examination
medical

Examples

The doctor used a proctoscope to check for abnormalities in the patient's rectum.
A proctoscope allows for direct visual examination of the lower digestive tract.
Many colorectal surgeons rely on the proctoscope during routine check-ups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for examining the interior of a hollow organ

Etymology

From Greek 'proktos' (anus) and 'skopein' (to look or examine).
