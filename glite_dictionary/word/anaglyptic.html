anaglyptic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈɡlɪptɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡlɪptɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by ornamentation in relief, especially patterns or designs raised above a surface, often by printing or embossing.
relating to relief ornamentation

Examples

The designer used anaglyptic wallpaper to add texture and depth to the living room walls.
The museum displayed anaglyptic prints that featured intricate raised patterns.
Architects often favor anaglyptic details in ceilings to create a sense of luxury and dimension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaglyphic - related to anaglyphs or relief work

Etymology

From Greek 'anaglyptos' (carved in relief), from 'ana-' (up) + 'glyphein' (to carve).
