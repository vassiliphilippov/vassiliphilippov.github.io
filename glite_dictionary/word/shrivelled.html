shrivelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
(especially of vegetation) having lost all moisture; dry and withered.
dry and withered, especially of vegetation

Examples

The hot sun left the garden full of shrivelled plants and dry soil.
She picked up a shrivelled apple that had rolled beneath the table weeks ago.
Most of the flowers were shrivelled by the lack of rain.

Synset Examples

dried-up grass
the desert was edged with sere vegetation
shriveled leaves on the unwatered seedlings
withered vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - dry and shriveled, especially from loss of moisture
  • Synonym: sere - (literary) dry or withered, especially of vegetation
  • Synonym: dried-up - having lost all moisture; no longer fresh

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrivelen', related to 'shrivel'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
(of quality, force, or feeling) reduced in strength, vitality, or effectiveness; diminished or weakened.
diminished in strength or intensity

Examples

After months without funding, the research team's shrivelled ambitions were clear to everyone.
His confidence shrivelled after repeated setbacks in the negotiations.
Public interest in the campaign shrivelled as new issues captured attention.

Synset Examples

our shriveled receipts during the storm
as the project wore on she found her enthusiasm shriveled
the dollar's shrunken buying power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminished - made smaller, weaker, or less important
  • Antonym: intensified - become stronger or more intense

Etymology

Past participle of 'shrivel', origin uncertain; possibly from Middle English 'shrivele' or related to Old Norse.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
lean, shrunken, and wrinkled, especially as a result of age, illness, or drying
shrunken and wrinkled, often by age or drying

Examples

The raisin is nothing more than a shrivelled grape.
Her hands were so shrivelled from years of work that they looked ancient.
After a week in the sun, the peppers had become dry and shrivelled.

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - lean and wrinkled, often because of loss of moisture or vitality
  • Synonym: wizened - shrunken and wrinkled, especially with age
  • Antonym: plump - having a full, rounded shape, the opposite of shrunken

Etymology

From the verb 'shrivel', possibly of Scandinavian origin; related to Swedish 'skryvla' (to wrinkle).
