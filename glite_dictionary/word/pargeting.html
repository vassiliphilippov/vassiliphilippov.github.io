pargeting

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrdʒɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdʒɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Plaster used for coating the outside walls of buildings or lining chimneys, often with ornamental patterns.
ornamental plaster for walls or chimneys

Examples

The restoration team carefully repaired the damaged pargeting on the building's east facade.
Historic houses in Suffolk often display elaborate pargeting on their exterior walls.
The old chimney revealed traces of decorative pargeting beneath layers of soot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - a hard covering of a surface with a smooth substance

Etymology

From Middle English 'pargetynge', from Old French 'pargeter' (to plaster a wall).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrdʒɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdʒɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
decorative or ornamental plasterwork applied to the walls or ceilings of buildings, often found on exterior walls
ornamental plasterwork on buildings

Examples

The old manor's façade was covered in elaborate pargeting depicting scenes from local history.
Artisans spent months restoring the intricate pargeting on the cathedral's outer walls.
Traditional pargeting can be found on many historic buildings in East Anglia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stucco - fine plaster used for coating wall surfaces or molding into architectural decorations
  • Synonym: plasterwork - work or decoration made with plaster

Etymology

From Middle English 'parget', from Old French 'pargeter' (to throw on, to coat).
