Mordva

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrdvə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdvə / (BrE)
a member of the Mordvin people, an agricultural ethnic group living in the central Volga region of European Russia
a member of a Volga region ethnic group in Russia

Examples

The Mordva are known for their distinctive traditional clothing and crafts.
Several villages in the Volga region are inhabited mainly by Mordva.
As a Mordva, he takes great pride in his cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mordvin - a member of a Finnic ethnic group in Russia (synonym)

Etymology

From Russian 'Мордвá' (Mordvá), the self-designation of the Mordvin peoples.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrdvə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdvə / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Mordvinians, an ethnic group in Russia
Finnic language of the Mordvinians

Examples

The Mordva language is spoken by a significant minority in central Russia.
Many ancient texts have been preserved in Mordva.
Her research focused on the grammatical structure of Mordva.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - family of languages including Finnish and Estonian

Etymology

From Russian Мордвá (Mordvá), referring to the Mordvin people and their language.
