Periploca

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈploʊkə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈpləʊkə / (BrE)
a genus of woody vines native to warm regions of the Old World, belonging to the family Apocynaceae
genus of woody vines

Examples

The genus Periploca includes several species of woody vines found in southern Europe and Asia.
Botanists studying Periploca are interested in its unique climbing mechanisms and medicinal properties.
Some species within the Periploca genus are cultivated as ornamental plants in warm climates.

Etymology

From Greek periplokē (an intertwining, a twining plant), from peri- (around) + ploke (twining).
