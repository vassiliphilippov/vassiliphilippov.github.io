criterium

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / kraɪˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A bicycle race held on a short course, typically less than 5 km (3 miles).
short-course bicycle race

Examples

The city will host a criterium downtown this weekend as part of the cycling festival.
She won her first criterium after an intense race around the closed circuit.
Many professional cyclists started their careers by racing local criteriums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: race - a competition of speed, typically among runners, horses, or vehicles
  • Degree Higher: road race - a bicycle race held on roads, usually longer distance

Etymology

From French 'critérium', originally used for sports competitions.
