Selenarctos

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌlɛnˈɑːrktəs / (AmE), / sɪˌlɛnˈɑːktəs / (BrE)
a former genus name for Asiatic black bears, now usually included in the genus Ursus
former genus name for Asiatic black bears

Examples

The Asiatic black bear was once classified under the genus Selenarctos.
Most modern zoologists now place species once labeled Selenarctos in the genus Ursus.
Older scientific texts often use Selenarctos to refer to the Asiatic black bear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - the genus that now includes Asiatic black bears

Etymology

From Greek *selēnē* (moon) and *arktos* (bear), referring to the moon-shaped mark on the bear's chest.

2. NOUN

/ sɛlɪˈnɑːrktəs / (AmE), / sɛlɪˈnɑːktɒs / (BrE)
A former scientific genus of Asiatic black bears; sometimes not distinguished from the genus Ursus.
former genus of Asiatic black bears

Examples

The Selenarctos classification is used by some zoologists for Asiatic black bears.
Recent studies have placed the Selenarctos species within the larger genus Ursus.
The debate over whether Selenarctos is a valid genus continues among taxonomists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - the genus including most bears, including the brown bear

Etymology

From Greek 'selēnē' (moon) + 'arktos' (bear), referring to the moon-shaped mark on the bear's chest.
