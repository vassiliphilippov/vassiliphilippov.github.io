brahmin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (BrE)
A member of the social and cultural elite in New England, especially one descended from an old and distinguished family.
member of elite New England family

Examples

Many old families still consider themselves part of the Boston brahmin class.
She married into a prominent brahmin family with deep roots in New England.
Despite his academic success, he never felt accepted by the local brahmins.

Synset Examples

a Boston brahman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocrat - a member of a privileged or noble class
  • Antonym: nouveau riche - a person newly rich, not from established elite

Etymology

From Sanskrit *brāhmaṇa*, originally meaning a member of the priestly caste in Hindu society; adopted in the U.S. for Boston's cultural elite.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (BrE)
A member of the highest Hindu caste, traditionally priests or scholars, regarded as the priestly class in the caste system.
member of highest Hindu caste

Examples

He was born into a brahmin family known for their scholarly traditions.
Many brahmins became prominent figures in Indian religious and cultural life.
As a brahmin, she was expected to study sacred texts and perform rituals.

Etymology

From Sanskrit brāhmaṇa (member of the priestly caste), related to Brahman, the ultimate reality in Hinduism.
