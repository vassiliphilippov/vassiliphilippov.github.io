chancery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃænsəri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnsəri / (BrE)
An office or court where public or ecclesiastical records are kept; a place responsible for maintaining official archives.
office or court of public records
legal

Examples

All original deeds are filed in the county chancery for future reference.
The old chancery holds centuries of church documents and parish registers.
Researchers visited the chancery to examine historical property records for their study.

Etymology

From Old French *chancelerie* (office of a chancellor), from Medieval Latin *cancellaria*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæn.sə.ri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn.sər.i / (BrE)
a court of equity, historically responsible for administering justice according to principles of fairness, distinct from courts of common law
a court of equity
legal

Examples

The dispute was settled in chancery rather than in a court of law.
Historic cases of trusts and estates were often decided by chancery.
He studied the procedures unique to chancery during his legal training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equity court - a court that decides cases on principles of equity
  • Antonym: court of law - a court that applies statutes and legal precedents rather than principles of equity

Etymology

From Old French *chancellerie*, from Medieval Latin *cancellaria* (office of a chancellor).
