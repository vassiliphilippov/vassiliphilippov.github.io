bullet-headed

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊlɪt ˌhɛdəd / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt ˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
having a small, round, and often thick or blunt head.
having a small, round head

Examples

The coach was a bullet-headed man with a commanding voice.
She described the guard as short and bullet-headed, with piercing eyes.
In the cartoon, the villain was drawn as a bullet-headed thug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round-headed - having a head that is round in shape
  • Antonym: square-headed - having a square-shaped head

Etymology

Formed in English from 'bullet' (projectile, from Middle French *boulet*) + 'headed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʊlət ˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt ˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) stubborn and unintelligent; obstinate and foolish.
stubborn and unintelligent (person)

Examples

Despite all evidence to the contrary, the bullet-headed manager refused to change his mind.
He can be so bullet-headed that working with him is exhausting.
Her bullet-headed insistence on a single approach led to the project's failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • Synonym: stupid - lacking intelligence or common sense
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
Source: WordNet
