podzol

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑdˌzɑl / (AmE), / ˈpɒd.zɒl / (BrE)
A type of acidic soil found in temperate and cold, moist climates, characterized by an organic layer over a leached, grayish subsurface, typically formed under coniferous or heath vegetation.
acidic soil found under coniferous forests

Examples

The acidic nature of a podzol can make it challenging for many crops to grow.
Forest ecosystems with conifer trees often develop a characteristic podzol beneath the leaf litter.
Scientists collected soil samples from the podzol layer to study nutrient cycling.

Etymology

From Russian 'podzól', meaning 'under-ash', referring to its ash-grey layer.
