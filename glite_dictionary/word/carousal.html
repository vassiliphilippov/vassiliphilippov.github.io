carousal

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraʊzəl / (AmE), / kəˈraʊzəl / (BrE)
a lively and noisy drinking party, usually involving revelry and merrymaking
lively drinking party; revelry

Examples

After the wedding feast, the night ended with a joyful carousal among friends.
Their loud carousal kept the neighbors awake well into the morning hours.
Stories of wild carousal at the tavern spread quickly through the small town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revelry - lively and noisy festivities

Etymology

From French 'carrousel', from Italian 'carosello', meaning 'a merry-go-round'; shifted in meaning in English to festive drinking.
