sovereign

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːvɚən / (AmE), / ˈsɒvərən / (BrE)
a monarch or other supreme ruler of a nation, especially one who holds the position by hereditary right
hereditary monarch or supreme ruler

Examples

The newly crowned sovereign addressed the nation from the royal balcony.
During the ceremony, the sovereign received ambassadors from neighboring countries.
For centuries, the British sovereign has played a symbolic role in government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch - a king, queen, or emperor
  • Antonym: subject - a person under the rule of a sovereign

Etymology

From Old French souverain, from Vulgar Latin *superānus* (chief, principal), from Latin *super* (over, above).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːvərə(n) / (AmE), / ˈsɒvərɪn / (BrE)
having the highest status, authority, or power; supreme or paramount.
supreme in status or authority

Examples

The court acts with sovereign authority in constitutional matters.
Her sovereign power over the organization was never questioned by her peers.
The committee made a sovereign decision that could not be appealed.

Synset Examples

a supreme tribunal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supreme - highest in rank or authority

Etymology

From Old French souverain, from Vulgar Latin *superānus (chief, principal), from Latin super (over).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00796196-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɑvərəɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɒvərɪn / (BrE)
(of a state or political body) having independent authority and not being controlled by any other state or external power.
independent and self-governing (of a state)

Examples

Switzerland is known as a sovereign nation, with full control over its internal affairs.
The new constitution reaffirmed the country's status as a sovereign state.
Some regions are seeking to become sovereign entities, independent from central government control.

Synset Examples

an autonomous judiciary
a sovereign state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomous - (of a state or institution) having self-government
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on another for support or authority

Etymology

From Old French souverain, based on Latin superanus (chief, above).
