stevedore

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːvəˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈstiːvədɔː / (BrE)
a person employed at a port to load and unload ships' cargo
a port laborer loading ships

Examples

The stevedore guided the crane operator as heavy containers were lifted onto the ship.
After twenty years as a stevedore, he knew every inch of the docks.
At sunrise, a team of stevedores began unloading the cargo from the freighter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dockworker - a person employed on the docks to load or unload cargo
  • Synonym: longshoreman - a person who works at loading and unloading ships at a dock

Etymology

From Spanish 'estibador', from 'estibar' (to stow), ultimately from Latin 'stipare' (to pack).
