nonmetal

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
Not containing, resembling, or characteristic of a metal.
not containing or resembling metal

Examples

The container is made from nonmetal materials to prevent rust.
These pipes are coated with a nonmetal finish for added safety.
Plastic is a common nonmetal alternative in electrical insulation.

Synset Examples

nonmetallic elements

Related Terms

  • Antonym: metallic - containing or resembling metal

Etymology

From the prefix 'non-' (not) + 'metal'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑnˌmɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
A chemical element that lacks the typical properties of metals, such as electrical conductivity, malleability, and luster.
element without metallic properties

Examples

Unlike copper, which is a metal, sulfur is classified as a nonmetal.
Oxygen, a nonmetal, is an essential component of the air we breathe.
The periodic table groups elements into categories such as metals and nonmetals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: metal - a chemical element with metallic properties

Etymology

Prefix 'non-' (not) + 'metal', meaning not a metal.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14651424-n
Synset POS: n
