carmine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmaɪn / (BrE)
a vivid red color, sometimes with a purplish or bluish tinge, used especially as a pigment or dye
vivid red color or pigment

Examples

The painter chose carmine for the roses to make them stand out.
A deep carmine accented her lips, giving her a bold look.
The curtains were dyed a rich shade of carmine to match the vintage seats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a deep purplish-red color
  • Degree Lower: scarlet - a bright red color with a slight orange tinge
  • Degree Higher: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet color

Etymology

From Medieval Latin carminium, from Arabic qirmiz (red), relating to the insect used for the dye.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːrmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmaɪn / (BrE)
of a vivid red color, similar to the color of blood, cherries, or rubies
bright red color

Examples

The artist used a bold stroke of carmine to highlight the sunset in her painting.
His tie was a striking shade of carmine that stood out in the crowd.
Roses of deep carmine bloomed along the garden path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a deep red color inclining to purple
  • Synonym: scarlet - a brilliant red color with a slightly orange tinge

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'carminus', from Arabic 'qirmiz' (crimson dye produced from scale insects).

Synset POS: s
