to apportion

1. VERB

/ əˈpɔɹʃən / (AmE), / əˈpɔːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
to divide and distribute something among people or groups according to a plan or proportional share
to distribute or allocate in shares

Examples

The committee will apportion the funds among the various departments.
It is important to apportion blame fairly when mistakes are made.
Resources must be apportioned according to the needs of each project.

Synset Examples

I am allocating a loaf of bread to everyone on a daily basis
I'm allocating the rations for the camping trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to set aside or designate for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or share among a number of recipients

Etymology

From Old French 'apportionner', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'portio' (a share, portion).

2. VERB

/ əˈpɔr.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈpɔː.ʃən / (BrE)
to divide and distribute something among several people or groups
divide and distribute

Examples

The manager must apportion resources fairly among all departments.
After the meal, they apportioned the remaining food equally.
It is difficult to apportion blame when both sides are at fault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to assign or set apart for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or hand out to a number of people
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give or grant something

Etymology

From Old French *apportionner*, from Latin *ad-* (to) + *portio* (portion, share).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02299140-v
Synset POS: v
