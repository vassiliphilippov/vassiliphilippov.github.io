Stenocarpus sinuatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstiːnoʊˈkɑːrpəs ˌsɪnjuˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌstiːnəˈkɑːpəs ˌsɪnjʊˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
an eastern Australian tree valued for its glossy leaves and circular clusters of showy red to orange-scarlet flowers, often grown as a shade tree
eastern Australian shade tree with red-orange flowers

Examples

The Stenocarpus sinuatus is admired for its vibrant flowers and is often planted along city streets in Australia.
Gardeners prize Stenocarpus sinuatus for its shade and attractive, glossy foliage.
In summer, the Stenocarpus sinuatus bursts into clusters of bright red flowers, attracting birds to the garden.

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the plant's slender fruit; specific epithet from Latin 'sinuatus' (wavy), describing the leaf margins.
