architectonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪtɛkˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the principles or practice of architecture or building structure.
relating to architecture or construction

Examples

The museum's design is praised for its architectonic clarity and innovative use of space.
Her paintings have an architectonic quality, with structures reminiscent of cathedrals and bridges.
The novel's architectonic structure mirrors the complexity of a Gothic cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architectural - relating to architecture or the design of buildings

Etymology

From Greek *arkhitektonikos* (pertaining to a master builder) via Late Latin *architectonicus*.
