Tingidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnɡɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋɡɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, plant-feeding insects known as lace bugs, characterized by delicate, lace-like patterns on their wings and bodies.
family of insects known as lace bugs

Examples

Many species in the family Tingidae cause visible damage to ornamental plants.
The delicate, reticulated wings of Tingidae are unique among plant bugs.
Some Tingidae can become serious pests in greenhouse environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace bug - a small insect of the family Tingidae with lace-like wing patterns

Etymology

From Tingis, the type genus, combined with the standard zoological family suffix -idae.
