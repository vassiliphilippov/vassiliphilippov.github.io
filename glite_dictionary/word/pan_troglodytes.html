Pan troglodytes

1. NOUN

/ pæn troʊˈɡlɒdəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / pan trɒɡˈlɒdɪtiːz / (BrE)
a species of intelligent arboreal ape native to equatorial African forests, commonly known as the common chimpanzee
the common chimpanzee species

Examples

The Pan troglodytes can use tools, making it one of the most intelligent non-human animals.
Researchers observe Pan troglodytes in the wild to study social behavior.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect Pan troglodytes from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - a species of great ape native to Africa, also known as Pan troglodytes

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Pan' and species name 'troglodytes' meaning 'cave-dweller'.
