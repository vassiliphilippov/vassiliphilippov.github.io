to monish

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑː.nɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ɪʃ / (BrE)
to warn or advise someone, especially with regard to their conduct or behavior
to warn or advise in matters of behavior
archaic

Examples

The teacher did monish her students against cheating on the exam.
He was monished that his reckless actions might bring disaster upon the group.
You would do well to monish your friend before he makes that decision.

Synset Examples

I warned him not to go too far
I warn you against false assumptions
She warned him to be quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warn - to inform someone in advance of possible danger or problem
  • Synonym: to advise - to offer suggestions or recommendations about what should be done

Etymology

From Latin 'monere' (to warn, advise), via Middle English and Old French forms.
