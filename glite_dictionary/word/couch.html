couch

1. NOUN

/ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
a long, narrow bed on which a patient lies during psychiatric or psychoanalytic treatment
a bed used for psychoanalytic treatment

Examples

The patient lay silently on the couch as the therapist took notes.
Sigmund Freud is famous for using a couch during psychoanalysis sessions.
The traditional psychoanalyst’s office features a comfortable couch for clients to recline on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divan - a long low sofa without a back or arms, often used as a couch

/ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
A flat layer of paint or varnish applied as a primer by artists before painting.
layer of paint or varnish used as a primer

Examples

The artist applied a thin couch before starting on the detailed work.
A proper couch can help colors adhere more effectively to the canvas.
Before adding highlights, she waited for the couch to dry completely.

3. NOUN

/ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
A long, comfortable upholstered seat with a back and arms, designed to seat more than one person.
upholstered seat for several people

Examples

She fell asleep on the couch while watching television.
They bought a new leather couch for their living room.
The cat curled up at one end of the couch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a similar upholstered seat usually with arms and a back
  • Degree Lower: armchair - a comfortable chair with sides that support your arms

Etymology

From Old French *couche* (a bed, lair), from Latin *collocare* (to lay down).

4. VERB

/ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to express something in a particular way; to phrase or word (especially carefully or deliberately)
to express in a particular way

Examples

The report was couched in very formal language.
She tried to couch her criticism as diplomatically as possible.
His offer was couched in terms that suggested he was doing us a favor.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
