penultimate

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnʌltɪmət / (AmE), / pɪˈnʌltɪmət / (BrE)
the next to last syllable in a word, especially in linguistic analysis.
next to last syllable in a word

Examples

In the word 'banana', the penultimate is 'na'.
Stress usually falls on the penultimate in Polish words.
Linguists refer to the penultimate when discussing syllable stress patterns.

Etymology

From Latin 'paenultima' (next to last), from 'paene' (almost) + 'ultima' (last).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈnʌltəmət / (AmE), / pɪˈnʌltɪmət / (BrE)
last but one in a series; second to last.
second to last

Examples

The story reached its climax in the penultimate scene, leaving only the conclusion to follow.
She finished second in the penultimate race of the season.
The penultimate chapter sets up the novel’s unexpected ending.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ultimate - last in a series; final

Etymology

From Latin paenultimus, from paene (almost) + ultimus (last).
