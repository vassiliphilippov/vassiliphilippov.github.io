goodish

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʊdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdɪʃ / (BrE)
fairly large in amount, size, or degree; somewhat considerable.
fairly large in amount or degree

Examples

We had to walk a goodish distance to reach the village.
She spent a goodish sum on her new car.
The project will require a goodish amount of time to complete.

Synset Examples

it cost a considerable amount
a goodly amount
received a hefty bonus
a respectable sum
a tidy sum of money
a sizable fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: considerable - large in amount or degree
  • Synonym: sizable - fairly large in amount or extent
  • Antonym: small - of limited size or amount

Etymology

From English 'good' + suffix '-ish', indicating 'somewhat' or 'rather'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡʊdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdɪʃ / (BrE)
fairly good; moderately good of its kind
moderately good

Examples

The meal was goodish, but not outstanding.
He paid a goodish sum for the bicycle, hoping it would last.
Her singing voice is goodish, though not professional calibre.

Synset Examples

a goodish wine

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: good - of high quality or standard
  • Degree Lower: fair - adequate or moderately good quality
