wile

1. NOUN

/ waɪl / (AmE), / waɪl / (BrE)
a trick or strategy intended to deceive or ensnare someone, often for personal gain
deceptive trick or strategy

Examples

The salesman used every wile to convince customers to buy the overpriced product.
She relied on her feminine wiles to win over her opponents.
With a mixture of charm and wile, he managed to escape the accusations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - a cunning or deceitful action
  • Synonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

From Old English wīl, from Proto-Germanic *wīlō (trick, ruse).
