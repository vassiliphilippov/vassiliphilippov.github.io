attenuate

1. VERB

/ əˈtɛnjuˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈtɛnjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to become weaker in strength, value, or intensity
become weaker or less intense

Examples

The signal will attenuate as it travels through dense walls.
His interest in the project began to attenuate over time.
During the experiment, scientists watched the intensity of the light gradually attenuate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Latin attenuare (to make thin, diminish), from ad- (to) + tenuis (thin).

3. VERB

/ əˈtɛnjuˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈtɛnjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to weaken the strength, thickness, or consistency of a substance, especially a chemical solution
weaken or dilute a substance

Examples

Carefully add water to attenuate the chemical solution before use.
The acid was attenuated to reduce its corrosive properties.
This process attenuates the color intensity of the dye in the mixture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilute - make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent

Etymology

From Latin attenuatus, past participle of attenuare (to make thin, weaken), from ad- (to) + tenuis (thin).
