assemblywoman

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛmbliˌwʊmən / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbliˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female member of a legislative assembly, especially in the United States
female member of legislative assembly

Examples

The state elected its first assemblywoman after a closely contested vote.
As an assemblywoman, she sponsored several major reform bills.
Reporters asked the assemblywoman about her plans for education policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemblyman - a male member of a legislative assembly

Etymology

Formed from 'assembly' + 'woman', modeled after 'assemblyman'.
