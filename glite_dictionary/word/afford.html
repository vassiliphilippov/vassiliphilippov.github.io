to afford

1. VERB

/ əˈfɔrd / (AmE), / əˈfɔːd / (BrE)
to have enough money, time, or resources to be able to do or give something without difficulty
to have enough (money, time, etc.) for something

Examples

I can't afford to take another day off work right now.
Can we really afford to lose any more time on this project?
She was too busy and simply couldn't afford the luxury of a long vacation.

Synset Examples

I can't afford to spend two hours with this person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to succeed in doing something despite difficulties
  • Synonym: spare - to give or allow something such as time or money

Etymology

From Old English aforthian, related to 'forth'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01195161-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈfɔrd / (AmE), / əˈfɔːd / (BrE)
to have enough money to pay for something or to be able to buy or do something without difficulty
to have enough money to buy or do something

Examples

We can't afford to go on vacation this year.
She wondered whether she could afford the monthly payments on the new apartment.
Not everyone is able to afford private health insurance.

Synset Examples

We can't afford to send our children to college
Can you afford this car?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to be able to deal with something successfully, often despite difficulty

Etymology

From Old English *geforthian* (to further, to accomplish), related to *faran* (to go, travel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02750326-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈfɔrd / (AmE), / əˈfɔːd / (BrE)
to provide or supply (a result, opportunity, or resource); to yield or offer
to provide or supply

Examples

The new technology affords an opportunity for quicker communication.
This window affords a beautiful view of the mountains.
His generosity afforded us a chance to travel abroad.

Synset Examples

He gave me a lot of trouble
Our meeting afforded much interesting information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provide - to make available or supply
  • Synonym: offer - to present or supply something

Etymology

From Old English 'aforðan' (to further), from 'forðan' (to further, promote).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02344167-v
Synset POS: v
