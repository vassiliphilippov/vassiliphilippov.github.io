Erica lusitanica

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪkə ˌluːsɪˈtænɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪkə ˌluːsɪˈtænɪkə / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub native to western Iberia, with dense foliage and abundant white or pink flowers; also found in southwestern England.
a species of heather shrub

Examples

The hillside was covered in blooming Erica lusitanica, creating a sea of pale pink and white.
Gardeners in southwestern England often plant Erica lusitanica to add winter colour to their landscapes.
Erica lusitanica thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is valued for its hardiness.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Erica' (heather) and 'lusitanica' (pertaining to Lusitania, an ancient region roughly corresponding to modern Portugal).
