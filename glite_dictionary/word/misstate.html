to misstate

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈsteɪt / (AmE), / mɪsˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to express or report something inaccurately or incorrectly
state something incorrectly

Examples

If you misstate a fact in your report, the results could be misleading.
He apologized after realizing he had misstated the date of the event.
Lawyers may challenge testimony if a witness misstates important details.

Synset Examples

You misstated my position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distort - to give a misleading or false account or impression of something
  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • Antonym: state - to express something in speech or writing, especially clearly and carefully

Etymology

From prefix mis- (wrongly) + state (to express in words).
