descendent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who is descended from a particular ancestor
a person descended from an ancestor

Examples

She is a direct descendent of the first settlers in the region.
Many Native Americans are proud descendents of ancient tribes.
The antique was passed down from one descendent to another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor (variant spelling)
  • Antonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended

Etymology

From Latin 'descendentem', present participle of 'descendere' (to descend).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
coming or derived from an ancestor; proceeding by descent from a previous source
coming from an ancestor

Examples

The family tree showed that many members had a descendent relationship to the royal lineage.
A descendent gene can be traced back to a common ancestor.
These traditions are descendent from ancient rituals practiced centuries ago.

Synset Examples

descendent gene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestral - inherited or originating from ancestors
  • Antonym: ascendant - rising or coming from a predecessor, often used as an antonym in lineage context

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere' (to descend).
