dog-iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡ ˌaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ ˌaɪən / (BrE)
a metal support, usually one of a pair, for holding logs in a fireplace; an andiron.
metal support for logs in a fireplace

Examples

He placed the logs carefully on the dog-iron before lighting the fire.
The antique dog-irons in the old house were made of ornate cast iron.
After years of use, the dog-iron had developed a rich patina from the flames.

Synset Examples

the andirons were too hot to touch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: andirons - metal supports for logs in a fireplace

Etymology

From 'dog' + 'iron,' possibly referring to the shape or function resembling a dog's form or tenacity.
