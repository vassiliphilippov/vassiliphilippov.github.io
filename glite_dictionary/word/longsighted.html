longsighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Able to see distant objects clearly; having good distance vision.
able to see distant objects clearly

Examples

As a longsighted driver, he could spot road signs from a great distance.
Birds of prey are famously longsighted, helping them find food from far above.
Her longsighted vision made her the best scout in the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsighted - able to see distant objects clearly
  • Antonym: nearsighted - able to see close objects clearly

Etymology

From 'long' + 'sighted', patterning after similar constructions in English.

/ ˈlɔŋˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
having the ability to consider and plan prudently for the future; showing foresight.
showing foresight, planning for the future

Examples

Her longsighted approach helped the company avoid many financial pitfalls.
Governments need longsighted leaders to address emerging global challenges.
A longsighted strategy can bring lasting benefits to future generations.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsighted - able to plan prudently for the future; showing foresight
