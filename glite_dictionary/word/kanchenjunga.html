Kanchenjunga

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːntʃənˈdʒʌŋɡə / (AmE), / ˌkæn.tʃənˈdʒʌŋ.ɡə / (BrE)
the third highest mountain in the world, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim; elevation 28,208 feet (8,586 meters)
mountain on Nepal-India border, third highest in the world

Examples

The climbers set out to conquer Kanchenjunga in the spring of 2022.
Clouds frequently shroud the peak of Kanchenjunga, making it difficult to see from a distance.
Many trekkers visit Sikkim for breathtaking views of Kanchenjunga at sunrise.

Etymology

From Tibetan 'Kangchen Dzönga', meaning 'The Five Treasures of Snow'.
