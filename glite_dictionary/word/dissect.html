to dissect

1. VERB

/ daɪˈsɛkt, dɪˈsɛkt / (AmE), / daɪˈsɛkt, dɪˈsɛkt / (BrE)
to cut open or apart (especially a dead body or plant) in order to study its internal parts
cut open for scientific study

Examples

In biology class, we had to dissect a frog to study its anatomy.
The scientists dissected the specimen carefully to examine the disease's effects.
She watched closely as the professor began to dissect the plant under the microscope.

Synset Examples

dissect the bodies for analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine in detail, especially to break down into components

Etymology

From Latin dissectus, past participle of dissecare (to cut apart), from dis- (apart) + secare (to cut).

2. VERB

/ daɪˈsɛkt, dɪˈsɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkt, daɪˈsɛkt / (BrE)
to examine or analyze something in detail by breaking it down into its component parts
analyze in detail; break into parts

Examples

The professor asked the students to dissect the poem line by line.
She dissected every argument in his essay to find flaws in his reasoning.
Economists often dissect financial trends to understand the causes behind market changes.

Synset Examples

analyze a specimen
analyze a sentence
analyze a chemical compound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine methodically by separating into parts

Etymology

From Latin dissectus, past participle of dissecare (to cut apart), from dis- (apart) + secare (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00645135-v
Synset POS: v
