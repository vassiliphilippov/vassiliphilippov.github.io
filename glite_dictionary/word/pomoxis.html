Pomoxis

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈmɑːksɪs / (AmE), / pəˈmɒksɪs / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater fish in the sunfish family, including crappies.
genus of freshwater fish (crappies)

Examples

The angler was excited to catch a large Pomoxis specimen in the lake.
Scientists studied the distribution of Pomoxis in various freshwater habitats.
Both black crappie and white crappie belong to the genus Pomoxis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crappie - common name for fish of the genus Pomoxis

Etymology

From Greek pōma (cover) and oxys (sharp), referring to the fish's gill cover.
