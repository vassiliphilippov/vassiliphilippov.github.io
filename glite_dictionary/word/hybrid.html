hybrid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.bɹɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.brɪd / (BrE)
a car that is powered by both an internal combustion engine and an electric motor, typically to improve fuel efficiency and reduce emissions
car using two types of power sources

Examples

More drivers are choosing a hybrid to save on fuel costs and reduce their carbon footprint.
The new hybrid offers a smooth transition between electric and gasoline power.
She traded in her old sedan for a hybrid last summer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electric car - a car powered exclusively by electricity
  • Synonym: to hybridize - to produce a hybrid; to combine two different things

Etymology

From Latin 'hybrida', meaning 'offspring of a tame sow and a wild boar; mongrel'.

thing made from mixed origins

3. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪbrɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪbrɪd / (BrE)
A word formed by combining elements from different languages, such as a prefix from one language and a root from another.
word made from parts of different languages

Examples

The term 'television' is a classic hybrid with roots in Greek and Latin.
Some linguists caution against coining unnecessary hybrids in technical vocabulary.
'Automobile' is another hybrid, combining Greek and Latin elements.

Etymology

From Latin *hybrida* (offspring of a tame sow and wild boar).

4. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪ.brɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.brɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) produced by crossbreeding different species, varieties, or breeds.
produced by crossbreeding

Examples

This new variety of corn is a hybrid developed for greater resistance to disease.
Many garden flowers are hybrid forms, created to enhance color and fragrance.
Some dog breeds, such as the Labradoodle, are intentionally hybrid to combine desirable traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbred - produced by breeding two different varieties or species
  • Antonym: purebred - bred from parents of the same breed or variety

Etymology

From Latin 'hybrida' (offspring of a tame sow and wild boar, mongrel).

5. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.brɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.brɪd / (BrE)
An organism that is the offspring of parents of different species, breeds, varieties, or genetic stocks, especially as a result of breeding.
offspring of genetically different parents

Examples

The hybrid was developed by crossing two different species of roses.
A mule is a classic example of a hybrid between a horse and a donkey.
Modern agriculture often relies on hybrid crops for higher yields.

Synset Examples

a mule is a cross between a horse and a donkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbreed - an animal or plant that is a mix of two different breeds or varieties
  • Antonym: purebred - an animal bred from parents of the same breed or variety

Etymology

From Latin 'hybrida', meaning offspring of a tame sow and wild boar.
