scattergun

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæt̬ɚˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈskætəˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a shotgun, especially a double-barreled smoothbore firearm designed to fire shot at short ranges
double-barreled shotgun

Examples

He rested his scattergun on the porch rail, watching for any sign of movement in the woods.
The hunter loaded both barrels of his scattergun before entering the field.
In old Westerns, the scattergun is often the weapon of choice for close-range defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shotgun - a firearm that fires shot rather than a single bullet

Etymology

Formed from 'scatter' (to disperse) and 'gun', referring to the widespread shot pattern.
