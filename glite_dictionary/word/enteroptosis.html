enteroptosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.tə.roʊpˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌen.tə.rɒpˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the intestines are abnormally displaced downward within the abdominal cavity
downward displacement of intestines
medical

Examples

Chronic fatigue can sometimes be attributed to enteroptosis in certain patients.
The physician diagnosed the patient with enteroptosis after reviewing the abdominal X-rays.
Treatment for enteroptosis may involve physical therapy and supportive garments.

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'ptosis' (a falling or displacement).
