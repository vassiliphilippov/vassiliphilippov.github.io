hymenopterous

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪməˈnɑptɚəs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪmɪˈnɒpt(ə)rəs / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to insects of the order Hymenoptera, such as bees, wasps, and ants
of or relating to Hymenoptera insects

Examples

Bees and ants are both classified as hymenopterous insects by entomologists.
The hymenopterous wings of the wasp are thin and transparent.
Their study focused on hymenopterous species that are major pollinators in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymenoptera - order of insects including bees, wasps, and ants

Etymology

From modern Latin Hymenoptera (name of the order) + -ous.
