megabucks

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəbʌks / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəbʌks / (BrE)
a very large amount of money, especially as earnings or profit
a very large sum of money
informal american

Examples

The company invested megabucks in the new advertising campaign.
She made megabucks after selling her startup to a major corporation.
It takes megabucks to live in that exclusive neighborhood.

Synset Examples

she made a bundle selling real estate
they sank megabucks into their new house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - a very large sum of money
  • Antonym: pittance - a very small amount of money

Etymology

From 'mega-' (meaning 'million' or 'large') + 'buck' (slang for dollar), first used in the US in the 1960s.
