Pope Benedict XV

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˌbɛnəˈdɪkt ˌfɪfˈtiːnθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˌbɛnɪˈdɪkt ˌfɪfˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
(1854–1922) Head of the Roman Catholic Church from 1914 to 1922, known for his efforts to promote peace during World War I and for founding the Vatican service for prisoners of war.
Roman Catholic pope (1914–1922), peace advocate

Examples

Pope Benedict XV sought to mediate peace between the warring nations during World War I.
The humanitarian efforts of Pope Benedict XV provided aid to prisoners of war across Europe.
Historians often credit Pope Benedict XV for his diplomatic approach in a time of global conflict.
