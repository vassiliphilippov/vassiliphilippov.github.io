cusp

1. NOUN

/ kʌsp / (AmE), / kʌsp / (BrE)
A point formed by the intersection of two curved lines or arcs, often seen in the inner curve of a Gothic arch.
point where two arcs meet, esp. in arches

Examples

The ornate window featured a cusp at the top where the two arches converged.
In Gothic architecture, a cusp often adds decorative detail to windows and doorways.
The restoration revealed several intricate cusps missing from the original stonework.

Etymology

From Latin 'cuspis' (point, tip).

2. NOUN

/ kʌsp / (AmE), / kʌsp / (BrE)
A small, pointed elevation on the biting or grinding surface of a tooth.
pointed part of a tooth's surface
medical

Examples

The dentist pointed out that the cusp of her molar was slightly worn.
A broken cusp can affect how your teeth come together when you bite.
Molars usually have more than one cusp on their chewing surface.

Etymology

From Latin cuspis (point, tip).

3. NOUN

/ kʌsp / (AmE), / kʌsp / (BrE)
A thin, triangular flap forming part of a heart valve, allowing blood to flow in one direction.
triangular flap of a heart valve
medical

Examples

The mitral valve contains two cusps that control blood flow between the heart's chambers.
A damaged cusp can lead to a leaky heart valve and impaired circulation.
Surgeons examined the aortic valve's cusps for any signs of abnormality during the procedure.

Etymology

From Latin 'cuspis' meaning 'point' or 'tip'.
