Pisanosaurus

1. NOUN

/ piˌsɑnoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / piˌsænəʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of primitive ornithischian dinosaur from the Late Triassic period, discovered in Argentina
a genus of early ornithischian dinosaur

Examples

The fossilized remains of Pisanosaurus were found in Late Triassic rocks in Argentina.
Some paleontologists consider Pisanosaurus to be one of the earliest ornithischian dinosaurs.
Research on Pisanosaurus has provided insight into the early evolution of herbivorous dinosaurs.

Etymology

From Greek 'pisanos' (pea) + 'sauros' (lizard), named by paleontologist José Bonaparte in 1967.

2. NOUN

/ piːˌsɑːnoʊˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / piːˌsænəʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of small, primitive ornithischian dinosaur found in Argentina, dating from the Late Triassic period
genus of primitive dinosaur from Argentina

Examples

Scientists discovered a fossil of Pisanosaurus in northwestern Argentina.
Pisanosaurus is believed to be one of the earliest known ornithischian dinosaurs.
The skeletal remains of Pisanosaurus helped paleontologists understand dinosaur evolution.

Etymology

From the Latinized form of Argentine paleontologist Juan Arnaldo Pisano and Greek 'sauros' (lizard).
