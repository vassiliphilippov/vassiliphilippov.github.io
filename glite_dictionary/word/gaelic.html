Gaelic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈɡælɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈɡælɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Gaels or their languages, culture, or characteristics, especially those associated with Scotland and Ireland.
of the Gaels; relating to Gaelic culture or languages

Examples

Traditional Gaelic music is an important part of Scottish heritage.
The Gaelic languages include Scottish Gaelic, Irish, and Manx.
He wore a Gaelic kilt to the festival celebrating Irish culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celtic - relating to the Celts or their languages/cultures

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic and Irish 'Gàidhlig', meaning the Gaelic language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈɡælɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈɡalɪk / (BrE)
any of several related Celtic languages spoken in Ireland and Scotland, especially Irish and Scottish Gaelic
Celtic languages of Ireland and Scotland

Examples

She learned to speak Gaelic while living in the Highlands.
The ancient texts were originally written in Gaelic.
There has been a recent revival of interest in Gaelic culture and language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish - the Celtic language spoken in Ireland
  • Synonym: Scottish Gaelic - the Celtic language spoken in Scotland

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic Gàidhlig, related to Old Irish Goídelc.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06973306-n
Synset POS: n
