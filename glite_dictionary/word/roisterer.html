roisterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪstərər / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪstərə / (BrE)
a person who celebrates in a noisy, cheerful, and unruly way; a boisterous merrymaker
noisy, unrestrained partygoer

Examples

The roisterer led the crowd in a raucous toast that echoed through the hall.
During the festival, every street corner had at least one roisterer singing at the top of his voice.
As midnight struck, the roisterers grew louder, filling the neighborhood with laughter and song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveler - one who takes part in lively festivities
  • Synonym: carouser - a person who drinks and celebrates noisily
  • Antonym: party pooper - a person who dampens the fun

Etymology

From the verb "roister" (to behave in a noisy or boisterous way), which derives from Old French "ruistre" (rude or boisterous fellow).
