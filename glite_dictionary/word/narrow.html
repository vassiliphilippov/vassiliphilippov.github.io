narrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
a narrow strait of water connecting two larger bodies of water
a narrow waterway or strait

Examples

The ship carefully navigated the narrow between the two islands.
Fishing boats sailed through the narrow during the early morning fog.
The explorers mapped the narrow that separated the bay from the open sea.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
having a small distance from one side to the other; not wide.
not wide; small in width

Examples

We crossed a narrow wooden bridge over the stream.
She drew a narrow line with her pencil across the page.
The path through the forest was so narrow that only one person could walk at a time.

Synset Examples

a narrow line across the page

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wide - having a great distance from side to side

Etymology

From Old English 'nearu' (of little width), related to Old High German 'narwa' (narrow).
