lampoon

1. NOUN

/ læmˈpun / (AmE), / lamˈpuːn / (BrE)
A written work or speech that satirically imitates and ridicules a person, group, or style, often in a humorous way.
satirical imitation; mocking composition

Examples

The magazine published a lampoon of the politician's latest speech, drawing laughter and controversy.
His clever lampoon of celebrity culture became a viral sensation online.
She wrote a lampoon targeting the clichés often found in romantic comedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of a writer or artist with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize

Etymology

From French 'lampon', likely of obscure or imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ læmˈpun / (AmE), / læmˈpuːn / (BrE)
to publicly criticize or ridicule someone or something using satire.
mock or ridicule with satire

Examples

The cartoonist often lampoons celebrities in his weekly illustrations.
They lampooned the mayor for his extravagant spending in the latest skit.
Satirical magazines are known to lampoon public figures relentlessly.

Synset Examples

The writer satirized the politician's proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to satirize - to attack or criticize with satire
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From French 'lampon', possibly from lamper (to gulp down).
