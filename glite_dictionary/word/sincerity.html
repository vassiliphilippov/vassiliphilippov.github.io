sincerity

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɛrɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being genuine, honest, and free from deceit or hypocrisy
genuineness; honesty; lack of deceit

Examples

She admired his sincerity when he apologized for his mistake.
There was a clear sincerity in her voice that convinced everyone of her intentions.
We appreciated the sincerity of his offer to help.

Synset Examples

his sincerity inspired belief
they demanded some proof of my sincerity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
  • Antonym: deceit - the act of misleading or being untruthful

Etymology

From Latin 'sinceritas', from 'sincerus' (clean, pure, sound).

2. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɛr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being free from pretense, deceit, or hypocrisy; genuine honesty and earnestness
the quality of being sincere

Examples

Her sincerity was evident in the heartfelt way she apologized.
We appreciated his sincerity when he congratulated the winner.
The audience was touched by the sincerity of her speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest
  • Antonym: insincerity - the quality of not being sincere

Etymology

From Old French sincérité or directly from Latin sinceritas (genuineness, purity).

3. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɛrɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being genuine, honest, and free from pretense or affectation
genuineness; freedom from pretense

Examples

Her sincerity came through in every word she spoke during the interview.
The sincerity of his apology convinced everyone that he truly regretted his mistake.
Listeners appreciate the sincerity of folk singers who share real stories in their music.

Synset Examples

the simple sincerity of folk songs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit
  • Antonym: insincerity - the quality of not expressing true feelings; pretense

Etymology

From Latin 'sinceritas' (genuineness, purity), from 'sincerus' (pure, honest).
