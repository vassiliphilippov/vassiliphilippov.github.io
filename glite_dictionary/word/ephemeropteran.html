ephemeropteran

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌfɛmərəˈpɪtərən / (AmE), / ɪˌfiːmərəˈpɪtərən / (BrE)
An insect of the order Ephemeroptera, commonly known as a mayfly, typically characterized by a very short adult life span.
an insect of the order Ephemeroptera (mayfly)

Examples

The scientist collected several ephemeropterans near the river during their brief mating season.
Because ephemeropterans live only a few hours as adults, their emergence is often dramatic.
Mayflies are the most well-known ephemeropterans and are important indicators of aquatic ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - an aquatic insect with a very short adult life; common name for ephemeropterans

Etymology

From modern Latin Ephemeroptera, from Greek ephemeros (lasting a day) + pteron (wing).
