Montaigne

1. PROPN

/ mɒnˈtæn; French: mɔ̃.tɛɲ / (AmE), / mɒnˈtæn; French: mɔ̃.tɛɲ / (BrE)
Michel de Montaigne (1533–1592), French writer and philosopher, regarded as the originator of the modern essay.
French essayist and philosopher

Examples

Many students read Montaigne in philosophy courses for his pioneering essays.
Her style of introspective writing was inspired by Montaigne's work.
In his essays, Montaigne reflected deeply on human nature and society.

Etymology

From the French surname Montaigne, after Michel de Montaigne.
