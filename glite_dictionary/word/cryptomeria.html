Cryptomeria

1. NOUN

/ krɪp.təˈmɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / krɪp.təˈmɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A large evergreen coniferous tree native to Japan, also called Japanese cedar, widely grown for timber and as an ornamental.
Japanese evergreen coniferous tree

Examples

The garden features several majestic Cryptomeria trees surrounding the small pond.
Farmers in Japan often plant Cryptomeria for its valuable timber.
The distinctive bark of the Cryptomeria peels in long strips, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese cedar - Another common name for Cryptomeria, an evergreen coniferous tree native to Japan

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'meros' (part), alluding to obscure features in the genus' flowers.

2. NOUN

/ krɪp.toʊˈmɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / krɪp.təˈmɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the cypress family, especially known for the Japanese cedar (sugi).
genus of coniferous Japanese cedar trees

Examples

The Cryptomeria grove near the shrine is over 400 years old.
Many traditional Japanese buildings are constructed with durable Cryptomeria wood.
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity of Cryptomeria to improve its resistance to disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese cedar - A large evergreen tree native to Japan, species of Cryptomeria

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + meros (part), referring to its concealed seeds.
