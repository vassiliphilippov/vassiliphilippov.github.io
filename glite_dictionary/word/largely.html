largely

1. ADV

/ ˈlɑrdʒli / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒli / (BrE)
mainly; for the most part; chiefly
mainly; for the most part

Examples

The team’s success was largely due to their hard work and determination.
The book is largely concerned with environmental issues.
Attendance at the conference was largely limited to industry professionals.

Synset Examples

These accounts are largely inactive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainly - for the most part; chiefly
  • Synonym: chiefly - primarily; mostly
  • Antonym: partly - in part; not completely

2. ADV

/ ˈlɑrdʒli / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒli / (BrE)
on a large scale; to a great extent or degree.
to a great extent; on a large scale

Examples

The concert was largely attended by students from the university.
The policy changes are largely the result of public pressure.
Her research has been largely ignored by the academic community.

Synset Examples

the sketch was so largely drawn that you could see it from the back row

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainly - for the most part; chiefly
  • Antonym: partly - to some extent; not completely

Etymology

From 'large' + '-ly', where 'large' comes from Old French 'large' (broad, wide), from Latin 'largus' (abundant).
