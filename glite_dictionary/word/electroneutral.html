electroneutral

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈnuːtrəl / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈnjuːtrəl / (BrE)
Having no net electric charge; electrically neutral.
having no net electric charge

Examples

A solution is considered electroneutral when the total positive and negative charges are balanced.
In physiological conditions, blood remains electroneutral despite fluctuations in ion concentration.
The electroneutral state of the atom ensures its stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutral - having no net electric charge

Etymology

Formed from 'electro-' (relating to electricity) and 'neutral' (having no charge).
