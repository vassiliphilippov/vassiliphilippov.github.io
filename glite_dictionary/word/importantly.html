importantly

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɔrtəntli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtəntli / (BrE)
In an important or significant manner.
in an important manner

Examples

The CEO spoke importantly about the new direction of the company.
Jellies have figured importantly among English desserts for centuries.
The evidence importantly changed the outcome of the trial.

Synset Examples

for centuries jellies have figured importantly among English desserts, particularly upon festive occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significantly - in a way that is important or has a noticeable effect
  • Antonym: trivially - in a manner that is unimportant or insignificant

2. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɔrtəntli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtntli / (BrE)
in a way that is significant or of great importance; to an important degree
in a significant or important way

Examples

Most importantly, we must remember to listen to each other's opinions with respect.
The project, importantly, received approval from the board last week.
He spoke importantly about the changes that would affect everyone in the company.

Synset Examples

more importantly, Weber held that the manifold meaning attached to the event by the social scientist could alter his definition of the concrete event itself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significantly - in a meaningful or important way
  • Antonym: trivially - in a way that is of little importance

Etymology

From 'important' + '-ly', following standard adverb formation in English.
