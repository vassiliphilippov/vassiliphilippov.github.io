skillful

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪl.fəl / (AmE), / ˈskɪl.fəl / (BrE)
done with great skill, delicacy, or expertise.
done with skill or expertise

Examples

The surgeon's skillful hands made the operation a complete success.
She gave a skillful performance that impressed the entire jury.
His skillful use of language won him the debate.

Synset Examples

a nice bit of craft
a job requiring nice measurements with a micrometer
a nice shot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having or showing skill; skilled at something
  • Antonym: unskilled - lacking skill or ability

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈskɪlfʊl / (BrE)
having or showing skill, ability, or expertise; demonstrating competence or dexterity in doing something
showing skill or expertise

Examples

The skillful surgeon performed the operation with remarkable precision.
She is a skillful negotiator who always finds a satisfactory compromise.
His skillful handling of the delicate situation impressed everyone in the team.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient at something
  • Antonym: incompetent - lacking necessary skill or ability

Etymology

From 'skill' + '-ful', Middle English 'skilful', after Old Norse 'skilfullr' (discerning).
