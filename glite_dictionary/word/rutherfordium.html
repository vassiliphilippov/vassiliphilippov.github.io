rutherfordium

1. NOUN

/ ˌrʌðərˈfɔrdiəm / (AmE), / ˌrʌðəˈfɔːdiəm / (BrE)
a synthetic, radioactive chemical element with the symbol Rf and atomic number 104
synthetic radioactive element (Rf, atomic number 104)

Examples

Scientists first created rutherfordium in a laboratory in the 1960s.
The properties of rutherfordium are still being studied by researchers.
Because rutherfordium is highly radioactive and unstable, it has no practical applications yet.

Etymology

Named after physicist Ernest Rutherford.
