indubitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈduːbɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːbɪtəbəl / (BrE)
Impossible to doubt; unquestionable; certain.
impossible to doubt; unquestionable

Examples

His honesty is indubitable, and no one has ever questioned his integrity.
There is indubitable evidence linking the suspect to the crime.
The outcome of the experiment provided indubitable proof of the theory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doubtful - not certain; open to question or doubt
  • Antonym: questionable - open to doubt or challenge; not certain
  • Synonym: certain - known to be true; having no doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'indubitabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'dubitabilis' (doubtful).
