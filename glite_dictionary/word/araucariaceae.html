Araucariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəwˌkɛriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌarɔːˌkɛəriˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tall evergreen, cone-bearing trees native to South America and Australia, with broad, leathery leaves; includes species such as the monkey puzzle tree and the Norfolk Island pine
family of tall evergreen trees (botanical)

Examples

The Araucariaceae family includes the well-known monkey puzzle tree.
Some members of the Araucariaceae are cultivated as ornamental trees in gardens.
Fossil records show that Araucariaceae once flourished across much of Gondwana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinaceae - the pine family; a family of coniferous trees including pines, firs, and spruces

Etymology

From the genus name *Araucaria*, which comes from Arauco, a region in Chile, plus the botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ əˌraʊ.kəˌriː.əˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / əˌraʊ.kəˌrɪəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of tall evergreen coniferous trees native to South America and Australia, characterized by broad, leathery leaves and cone-bearing branches.
family of evergreen coniferous trees

Examples

The Araucariaceae family includes the famous monkey puzzle tree of South America.
Many species within the Araucariaceae are cultivated as ornamental trees.
Fossils reveal that Araucariaceae were widespread during the Jurassic period.

Etymology

From genus name Araucaria, after the Arauco region in Chile, with New Latin suffix -aceae for plant families.
