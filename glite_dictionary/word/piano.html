piano

1. NOUN

/ piˈænoʊ / (AmE), / piˈanəʊ / (BrE)
A large musical instrument with a keyboard, in which pressing keys causes hammers to strike strings and produce musical notes.
large keyboard musical instrument

Examples

She practiced the piano for two hours every evening.
The grand piano dominated the center of the living room.
He learned to play the piano when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyboard - electronic instrument played by pressing keys

Etymology

From Italian *piano* (short for *pianoforte*), meaning 'soft' (and 'loud'), referring to the instrument's ability to play at different volumes.

2. NOUN

/ piˈænoʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
(music) In musical notation, a direction to play softly or with low volume.
play softly (music direction)

Examples

The composer marked the passage piano to create a gentle, intimate atmosphere.
The soloist played the melody piano, barely audible over the accompaniment.
Musicians must pay attention to when to shift from piano to louder dynamics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forte - (music) play loudly; musical direction for high volume

Etymology

From Italian 'piano' meaning 'soft'.
