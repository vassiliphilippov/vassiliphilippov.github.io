Aztecan

1. NOUN

/ æzˈtɛkən / (AmE), / azˈtɛkən / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Aztec people, also known as Nahuatl.
the language of the Aztec people

Examples

The ancient manuscripts were written in Aztecan and have been studied by historians for decades.
She learned Aztecan as part of her research into pre-Columbian civilizations.
Fascinated by indigenous languages, he took a university course on Aztecan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nahuatl - the language historically spoken by the Aztec and related peoples

Etymology

From 'Aztec', referring to the Central Mexican people, with '-an' denoting language or related aspect.
