groundbreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a person who pioneers or introduces new ideas, methods, or innovations in a particular field
innovator or pioneer in a field

Examples

As a groundbreaker in renewable energy, she developed technology that changed the industry.
The artist is hailed as a groundbreaker for her uniquely modern style.
Many consider him a groundbreaker in computer science due to his early inventions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pioneer - a person who is among the first to explore or develop a new area
  • Synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new methods, ideas, or products

Etymology

From the words 'ground' + 'breaker', referring to breaking new ground metaphorically.
