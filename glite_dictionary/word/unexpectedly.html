unexpectedly

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnɪkˈspɛktɪdli / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈspɛktɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that surprises because it was not anticipated or foreseen.
in an unanticipated or surprising way

Examples

The meeting ended unexpectedly after only half an hour due to a technical issue.
She unexpectedly received a promotion at work yesterday.
Rain unexpectedly began to fall during the outdoor ceremony.

Synset Examples

her brother showed up at the wedding out of the blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suddenly - quickly and without warning

Etymology

Formed from 'unexpected' (from un- 'not' + expected, from Latin expectare 'to await') and the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˌʌnɪkˈspɛktɪdli / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈspɛktɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that is not anticipated or foreseen; without warning or advance planning.
without prior warning or planning

Examples

The weather changed unexpectedly, forcing us to cancel the picnic.
She unexpectedly received a letter from an old friend.
The lights went out unexpectedly during the meeting.

Synset Examples

they met accidentally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suddenly - quickly and without warning
  • Antonym: predictably - as expected; in a predictable way

Etymology

Formed from 'unexpected' + '-ly'. 'Unexpected' from un- (not) + expect + -ed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00040959-r
Synset POS: r
