Mediterranean

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛd.ə.təˈreɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˌmɛd.ɪ.təˈreɪ.ni.ən / (BrE)
the sea surrounded by southern Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia, known as the largest inland sea in the world
the sea between Europe, Africa, and Asia

Examples

Many ancient civilizations flourished along the shores of the Mediterranean.
The climate around the Mediterranean is famous for its warm, dry summers and mild, wet winters.
She sailed across the Mediterranean on a cruise ship, visiting several countries along the way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mediterranean Sea - the body of water known as the Mediterranean

Etymology

From Latin 'Mediterraneus' (inland, middle of the land), from 'medius' (middle) + 'terra' (land).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪniən / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪniən / (BrE)
connected with the Mediterranean Sea or the countries surrounding it, or having characteristics typical of this region.
relating to the Mediterranean Sea or its region

Examples

They spent their vacation in a Mediterranean resort by the sea.
The Mediterranean climate is known for its hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.
She prepared a delicious Mediterranean salad with olives and feta cheese.

Synset Examples

Mediterranean countries

Etymology

From Latin 'Mediterraneus', meaning 'inland' (from 'medius' middle + 'terra' land).
