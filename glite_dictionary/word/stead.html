stead

1. NOUN

/ stɛd / (AmE), / stɛd / (BrE)
the position, role, or function that is filled by someone or something else; place of another
place or role of another

Examples

She sent her assistant in her stead to attend the meeting.
He accepted the award in his father's stead.
If I am unable to go, my colleague will take my stead.

Synset Examples

can you go in my stead?
took his place
in lieu of

Related Terms

  • Synonym: place - the role or position of someone or something
  • Degree Higher: steadfast - firm and unwavering (different POS, but similar etymological root)

Etymology

From Old English 'stede' (place, position).
