chantarelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃæn.təˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˌʃæn.təˈrɛl / (BrE)
a widely distributed edible mushroom with a rich yellow color, a smooth cap, and a pleasant apricot aroma
yellow edible mushroom with apricot aroma

Examples

We found several chantarelles while hiking in the forest after the rain.
The chef prepared a delicious risotto with fresh chantarelles and herbs.
Her favorite autumn dish is pasta with wild chantarelles and cream sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanterelle - yellow edible mushroom; alternative spelling

Etymology

From French 'chanterelle', diminutive of 'chanter' (to sing), referring to the mushroom's shape resembling a small bell or trumpet.
