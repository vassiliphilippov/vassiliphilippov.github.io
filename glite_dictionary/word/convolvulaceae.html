Convolvulaceae

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvɒlvjuˈleɪsi.i / (AmE), / kɒnˌvɒlvjuːˈleɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants with trumpet-shaped flowers and a climbing or twining habit, including the morning glory, bindweed, and sweet potato.
family of trumpet-flowered climbing plants

Examples

The sweet potato belongs to the Convolvulaceae family of plants.
Many species in the Convolvulaceae are known for their beautiful, trumpet-shaped flowers.
Several invasive weeds, such as bindweed, are part of the Convolvulaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family known for its trumpet-shaped flowers
  • Synonym: bindweed - a climbing plant in the Convolvulaceae family

Etymology

From the genus name Convolvulus, from Latin convolvere (to wind together), with botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ kənˌvɑːlvjəˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kənˌvɒlvjuːˈleɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including morning glories, bindweeds, and sweet potatoes, typically having trumpet-shaped flowers and a climbing or twining habit
a family of flowering plants including morning glory and bindweed

Examples

The sweet potato belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many climbing plants.
Bindweed, a member of the Convolvulaceae, is considered a troublesome weed in many gardens.
Many species in the Convolvulaceae family have beautiful, trumpet-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'convolvulus' (bindweed), the type genus of the family, with the scientific family suffix '-aceae'.
