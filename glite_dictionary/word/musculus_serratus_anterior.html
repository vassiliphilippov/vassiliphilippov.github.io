musculus serratus anterior

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkjulus səˈreɪtəs ænˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / mʌsˈkjʊlʊs səˈreɪtəs ænˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a muscle located on the side of the thorax that rotates the scapula and helps elevate the rib cage during respiration
muscle that rotates scapula, elevates ribs
medical

Examples

The musculus serratus anterior plays a crucial role in allowing you to lift your arms above your head.
Weakness in the musculus serratus anterior can lead to difficulty pushing objects away from the chest.
Proper breathing techniques can help engage the musculus serratus anterior during strenuous activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serratus anterior - a muscle that rotates the scapula and elevates the ribs

Etymology

From Latin, 'musculus' (muscle), 'serratus' (saw-shaped), 'anterior' (front).
