to embroider

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbrɔɪdər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪdə / (BrE)
to decorate cloth or fabric with patterns or images using needlework
decorate with needlework

Examples

She spent hours carefully embroidering flowers onto her dress.
The pillowcases were beautifully embroidered with intricate designs.
He plans to embroider his initials on his handkerchief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sew - to join or attach with a needle and thread

Etymology

From Old French 'embroder', from 'broder' (to embroider) and ultimately of Germanic origin.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbrɔɪdər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪdə / (BrE)
to add extra details or embellishments to a story or account, often exaggerating beyond the truth
add extra (often exaggerated) details

Examples

He tends to embroider the facts when telling stories about his childhood.
The journalist was accused of embroidering the details to make the article more sensational.
She couldn't help but embroider her tale of how she met the celebrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem more impressive, important, or worse than it really is

Etymology

From Old French 'broder', from Middle High German 'brodeln' (to braid, embellish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00958437-v
Synset POS: v
