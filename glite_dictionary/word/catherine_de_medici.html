Catherine de' Medici

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæθrɪn də ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈkæθrɪn də ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1519–1589) Queen of France as the wife of Henry II and regent during the reign of her son Charles IX, noted for her influence on French politics during the Renaissance.
Queen of France and regent (1519–1589)

Examples

Historians still debate the legacy of Catherine de' Medici and her role in the French Wars of Religion.
Many believe Catherine de' Medici was a master of political intrigue in Renaissance France.
During her regency, Catherine de' Medici helped maintain the power of the French monarchy.

Etymology

From Italian Caterina de' Medici, of the powerful Medici family of Florence.
