Planera

1. NOUN

/ pləˈnɛrə / (AmE), / pləˈnɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees of the elm family (Ulmaceae), native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as water elm
genus of trees (water elm)

Examples

The wetlands were filled with tall Planera trees, their leaves shimmering in the sunlight.
During our field trip, we identified a rare Planera near the riverbank.
The Planera is often mistaken for a true elm because of its similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water elm - common name for Planera aquatica

Etymology

From New Latin, after the genus name Planera.

2. NOUN

/ pləˈnɛrə / (AmE), / pləˈnɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees in the family Ulmaceae, native to the southeastern United States, notable for the water elm (Planera aquatica)
a genus of deciduous trees in Ulmaceae (water elms)

Examples

The Planera is often found growing in low, swampy areas of the southeastern United States.
Botanists classify the water elm as part of the genus Planera.
Unlike true elms, trees in the Planera genus have unique, rough-textured fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water elm - a species of Planera, also called Planera aquatica
  • Synonym: Ulmaceae - the elm family of flowering plants

Etymology

Named after J.B. Planer, an 18th-century German botanist.
