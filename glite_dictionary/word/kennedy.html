Kennedy

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɛnədi / (AmE), / ˈkɛnədi / (BrE)
the 35th President of the United States (1961–1963), known for establishing the Peace Corps and being assassinated in Dallas
35th U.S. President (1961–1963); JFK
american british

Examples

Many Americans still admire Kennedy for his vision of the New Frontier.
The assassination of Kennedy in 1963 shocked the entire world.
Historians continue to debate Kennedy's foreign policy decisions during the Cold War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John F. Kennedy - full name of Kennedy, 35th President of the United States
  • Antonym: Lyndon B. Johnson - 36th President of the United States, succeeded Kennedy

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɛnədi / (AmE), / ˈkɛnədi / (BrE)
A large international airport in New York City, officially named John F. Kennedy International Airport.
major airport in New York City

Examples

We landed at Kennedy after a long overnight flight from London.
Many international travelers pass through Kennedy each year.
She took a taxi from Kennedy to Manhattan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: JFK - abbreviation for John F. Kennedy International Airport in New York

Etymology

Named after John F. Kennedy, the 35th President of the United States.
