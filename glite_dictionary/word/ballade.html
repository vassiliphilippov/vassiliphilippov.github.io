ballade

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɑd / (AmE), / bəˈlɑːd / (BrE)
A poem with three stanzas and a final short stanza (envoy), typically following a strict rhyme scheme; especially associated with French medieval poetry.
poem form with 3 stanzas and an envoy

Examples

She composed a ballade in honor of her favorite poet.
The medieval ballade often featured themes of courtly love.
Students were asked to write a ballade as part of their poetry assignment.

Etymology

From Old French *ballade* (dancing song), from *baller* (to dance).
