suggestibility

1. NOUN

/ səˌdʒɛstəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / səˌdʒɛstɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily influenced by the suggestions or opinions of others
susceptibility to influence or suggestion

Examples

Advertising often relies on the suggestibility of consumers to encourage them to buy products.
Children are known for their high suggestibility, which is why they can be easily influenced.
The therapist evaluated the patient's level of suggestibility before beginning hypnosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gullibility - the quality of being easily deceived or tricked
  • Antonym: resistance - the ability to resist or withstand influence or suggestion

Etymology

From 'suggest' + '-ibility', influenced by the pattern of words like 'accessibility'.
