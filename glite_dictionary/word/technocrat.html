technocrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛknəˌkræt / (AmE), / ˈtɛknəkræt / (BrE)
A person who has technical expertise and who is involved in the management or administration of an organization, especially within government or industry.
an expert in charge, especially in government or industry

Examples

The new minister is seen as a technocrat with little political experience.
During the crisis, several technocrats were appointed to key positions in the government.
The company was led by a team of brilliant technocrats who valued efficiency over tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucrat - an official in a government or other organization, especially one perceived as adhering to fixed rules

Etymology

From French *technocrate*, from Greek *tekhnē* (skill) + *-kratia* (rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛk.nəˌkræt / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.nə.kræt / (BrE)
A person who advocates or supports government or control by technical experts, especially scientists or engineers.
proponent of rule by technical experts

Examples

The country's new government is dominated by technocrats with backgrounds in engineering and science.
As a committed technocrat, she believes policy decisions should be guided by data and expertise.
Many voters see technocrats as out of touch with the public's concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technocracy - Government or control of society by technical experts

Etymology

From French 'technocrate', from Greek 'tekhnē' (skill) + 'kratos' (power, rule).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10716168-n
Synset POS: n
