aweigh

1. ADJ

/ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of an anchor) clear of the sea bottom and no longer supporting the weight of the ship, usually just as it is being raised.
(nautical) of anchor, clear of sea bottom

Examples

The ship could not move forward until the anchor was aweigh.
Once the anchor is aweigh, the vessel can begin its journey out to sea.
Sailors cheered when the captain announced the anchor was aweigh.

Synset Examples

anchors aweigh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weigh anchor - to raise the anchor of a ship in preparation for departure

Etymology

From the phrase 'anchors aweigh,' with 'aweigh' based on weigh (to raise anchor).

2. ADJ

/ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of an anchor) just clear of the sea bottom; no longer resting on the bottom and ready to be hoisted.
(anchor) just clear of the bottom

Examples

The ship could not move until the anchor was aweigh.
"Anchors aweigh!" shouted the captain as the vessel began to drift from the harbor.
The crew worked together to bring the anchor aweigh before setting sail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afloat - floating on the surface of water

Etymology

From 'a-' (on) + 'weigh', from Old English 'wegan' (to lift, to carry), as in to weigh anchor.
