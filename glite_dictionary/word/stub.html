stub

1. NOUN

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
the small part of a ticket that is torn off and given back to the holder, serving as a receipt or proof of purchase
torn part of a ticket kept as a receipt

Examples

Please keep your stub in case you need to re-enter the theater.
He lost his ticket but still had the stub as proof of purchase.
The usher collected the ticket and handed back the stub to the guest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receipt - written or printed statement acknowledging something was received

Etymology

From Old English *stybb* (stump, log); related to German *Stumpf*.

2. VERB

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
to accidentally strike one's toe against an object
strike one's toe accidentally

Examples

Be careful not to stub your toe as you walk barefoot through the hallway.
She stubs her toe every time someone moves the furniture in her room.

3. NOUN

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
the part of a check, ticket, or similar document that is kept as a record after the main portion has been removed or used
part retained from a check or ticket

Examples

She carefully filed each stub after writing a check for her monthly bills.
Please keep the stub for your records in case you need to verify this payment.
He compared the stub with the canceled check to confirm the donation amount.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfoil - the part of a check or ticket kept as a record, especially in British English

4. NOUN

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
a small piece or part that remains after the main part has been used or removed, especially the end of something such as a pencil or cigarette
small remaining part or piece

Examples

He could barely write with the stub of his pencil.
There was only a stub of candle left after the power outage.
She flicked the stub of her cigarette into the ashtray.

Synset Examples

a nub of coal
a stub of a pencil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nub - a small lump or piece; the crux of something

Etymology

Old English stubb (tree stump); related to Dutch stubbe and German Stumpf.

5. NOUN

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
a short piece remaining on a plant, especially where a branch has been lost or cut off from a trunk or stem
short piece left after branch removed

Examples

The gardener left a stub where the old branch had been cut.
After the storm, only a stub remained of the once-tall sapling.
Be careful not to injure the stub while pruning the tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stump - the base part of a plant or tree left after the main part is cut or broken

Etymology

From Old English *stybb* (stump, stub), related to German *Stube* (stub, stump).

Source: WordNet

6. NOUN

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
the small unused part remaining at the end of something, especially at the end of a cigarette after smoking
small remaining part, e.g. of a cigarette

Examples

He flicked the stub of his cigarette into the ashtray before leaving the room.
There were several stubs of pencils and cigarette ends on the table.
She picked up the stub and threw it away, careful not to leave any mess behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butt - the end part of a cigarette after most has been smoked
  • Synonym: stump - the small remaining part of something that has been cut or used

Etymology

Probably from Old English *stybb* (stump of a tree)
