Finnish

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
the Finno-Ugric language spoken as the official language of Finland.
language of Finland

Examples

She decided to learn Finnish before traveling to Helsinki for her studies.
Many words in Finnish have no direct equivalents in English.
The novel was originally written in Finnish and later translated into several languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finn - a person from Finland or of Finnish descent

Etymology

From Swedish 'finsk' and Finnish 'suomi' (the Finns' own name for the language).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06970995-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Finland, its people, or their language and culture
relating to Finland or its people

Examples

The new museum features Finnish architecture and design prominently.
She attended a Finnish language course while visiting Helsinki.
The Finnish climate can be quite cold and snowy in the winter.

Synset Examples

Finnish architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finn - a person from Finland

Etymology

From Finland, with the suffix -ish forming adjectives denoting national or ethnic group.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
