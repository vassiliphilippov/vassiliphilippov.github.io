Gallus gallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləs ˈɡæləs / (AmE), / ˈɡæləs ˈɡæləs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the red junglefowl, a bird native to southeastern Asia and regarded as the ancestor of the domestic chicken
scientific name for the red junglefowl

Examples

The domesticated chicken can trace its ancestry back to Gallus gallus, a wild bird from Asia.
Researchers studied the behavior of Gallus gallus to understand early domestication.
Conservation efforts aim to protect the natural habitat of Gallus gallus in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red junglefowl - the common name for Gallus gallus, a wild bird from Southeast Asia
  • Synonym: chicken - the domesticated descendant of Gallus gallus

Etymology

From Latin, meaning ‘rooster, cock’ (Gallus, gallus).
