tradition

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A custom, belief, or practice that has been passed down within a group or society for a long period of time.
long-established custom or practice

Examples

It is a family tradition to gather for dinner every Sunday.
The festival is rooted in an ancient tradition that has been celebrated for centuries.
Wearing costumes at Halloween has become a popular tradition in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something
  • Antonym: innovation - the introduction of new ideas or methods

Etymology

From Latin 'traditio' (handing over, delivery), from 'tradere' (to hand over).

2. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
A belief, custom, or way of doing something that has existed for a long time among a particular group of people and is passed down from one generation to another.
custom passed through generations

Examples

It is a tradition in our family to have a big meal together on New Year's Eve.
The festival celebrates a centuries-old tradition of music and dance.
Passing down stories orally is an important tradition in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a habitual practice, especially a long-established one
  • Antonym: innovation - a new method, idea, or product

Etymology

From Latin 'traditio' (delivery, surrender), from 'tradere' (to hand over).
