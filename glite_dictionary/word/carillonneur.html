carillonneur

1. NOUN

/ kəˌrɪl.oʊˈnɜr / (AmE), / kəˌrɪl.əˈnɜː / (BrE)
a person who plays a carillon, a musical instrument consisting of a set of bells played using a keyboard or other mechanism
person who plays a bell carillon

Examples

The cathedral's carillonneur played a beautiful melody that echoed across the city square.
Each Sunday, the carillonneur attracts a crowd with his intricate performances.
She trained for years to become an accomplished carillonneur at the university chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carillon - a set of stationary bells played using a keyboard

Etymology

From French 'carillonneur', from 'carillon' (a set of bells).
