mute

1. NOUN

/ mjuːt / (AmE), / mjuːt / (BrE)
a device that reduces the volume or alters the timbre of a musical instrument, especially a brass or string instrument
device for softening musical instrument's tone

Examples

He inserted the mute into his trumpet before the soft passage.
The violinist used a mute to create a more mellow sound for the piece.
Different types of mutes can dramatically change the color of an instrument's tone.

Etymology

From Latin 'mutus' meaning 'silent, unable to speak'.

3. VERB

/ mjut / (AmE), / mjuːt / (BrE)
to make a sound or noise quieter, especially by covering or wrapping the source
make a sound quieter, especially by covering

Examples

He tried to mute the noise by placing a towel over the alarm.
The orchestra muted the brass section for a softer sound.
She quickly muted her phone to avoid disturbing the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - to make a sound or noise less harsh or loud
