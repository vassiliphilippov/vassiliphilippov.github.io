4-dimensional

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɔr.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or involving four dimensions, especially including time as the fourth dimension beyond the three spatial dimensions.
relating to four dimensions (esp. incl. time)

Examples

The concept of a 4-dimensional spacetime is fundamental in Einstein's theory of relativity.
Some artists try to depict 4-dimensional shapes using clever visual tricks.
In science fiction, characters sometimes travel through a 4-dimensional universe where time is as accessible as space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three-dimensional - having or relating to three dimensions (length, width, height)
