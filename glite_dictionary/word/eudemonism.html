eudemonism

1. NOUN

/ juˈdiməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / juːˈdiːmənɪzəm / (BrE)
An ethical theory that evaluates actions in terms of their contribution to personal well-being, particularly as achieved through living in accordance with reason.
ethical system based on personal well-being

Examples

The philosopher devoted his work to defending eudemonism as the foundation of moral action.
Many ancient theories of ethics, such as Aristotle's, are based on the principles of eudemonism.
Critics argue that eudemonism places too much emphasis on individual happiness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hedonism - the ethical theory that pleasure is the highest good

Etymology

From Greek eudaimonismos, from eudaimon (happy, fortunate).
