Quercus incana

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ɪnˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ɪnˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A small, semi-evergreen shrubby oak tree native to the southeastern United States, with hairy young branches and leaves that taper to a slender bristly point.
a shrubby oak tree of the southeastern US

Examples

The leaves of the Quercus incana are easily recognized by their bristly tips.
Botanists conducted a study on the distribution of Quercus incana in the wild.
The Quercus incana thrives in sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, producing acorns

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak', and 'incana' meaning 'grayish' or 'hoary'.
