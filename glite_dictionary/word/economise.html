to economise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to spend less or use fewer resources in order to avoid waste or save money
spend less; avoid waste
british

Examples

The family had to economise after their income was reduced.
To avoid debt, she decided to economise on groceries and utilities.
The company encouraged employees to economise wherever possible to cut costs.

Synset Examples

This move will save money
The less fortunate will have to economize now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to keep and not spend or waste (money, resources, etc.)
  • Antonym: waste - to use more of something than is necessary or useful

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (management of a household).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to use resources carefully and avoid waste so as to save money, effort, or time
use resources carefully to avoid waste

Examples

In order to pay off her debt, Emma decided to economise on daily expenses.
During the drought, everyone was encouraged to economise on water usage.
We need to economise our resources if we want them to last through the winter.

Synset Examples

I try to economize my spare time
conserve your energy for the ascent to the summit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to avoid spending money or using resources unnecessarily
  • Antonym: waste - to use resources carelessly or extravagantly

Etymology

From French 'économiser', from Greek 'oikonomia' (household management).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02273988-v
Synset POS: v
