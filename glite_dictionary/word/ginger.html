ginger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒə / (BrE)
A pungent, aromatic spice made from the dried and ground root of the ginger plant, used in cooking and baking.
dried ground spice from gingerroot

Examples

Add a teaspoon of ginger to the cake batter for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for ground ginger as well as fresh spices.
She sprinkled some ginger over the roasted vegetables before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gingerroot - the fresh root of the ginger plant, used as a spice or medicine

Etymology

From Old English 'gingifer', from Medieval Latin 'gingiber', from Greek 'zingiberis', from Sanskrit 'śṛṅgavēra' (ginger).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒə / (BrE)
A perennial plant (Zingiber officinale) with thick, branching, aromatic rhizomes and leafy, reedlike stems, widely cultivated for its pungent, spicy root used in cooking and medicine.
aromatic perennial plant; source of spicy root

Examples

Fresh ginger is often used in Asian cuisine to add flavor and aroma to dishes.
She planted ginger in her garden for both culinary and medicinal uses.
The tea was made with slices of ginger and a touch of honey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger root - the rhizome of the ginger plant used as a spice

Etymology

From Middle English gingivere, from Old English gingifer, from Medieval Latin gingiber, from Greek zingiberis, from Prakrit siṅgavera.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
