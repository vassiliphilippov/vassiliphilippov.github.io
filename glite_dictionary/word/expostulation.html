expostulation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɑstʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɒstjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an expression of strong disagreement, protest, or remonstrance, especially one made verbally
expression of strong protest or objection

Examples

Her expostulation fell on deaf ears, as he refused to change his mind.
Despite my expostulation, they went ahead with the risky decision.
She delivered a lengthy expostulation against the new school policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection or disapproval
  • Synonym: remonstrance - a forcefully reproachful protest

Etymology

From Latin expostulatio (a demand, complaint), from expostulare (to demand earnestly).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɑstʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɒstjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing strong disagreement or protest, especially in an earnest or reasoned way
earnest protest or objection

Examples

Her expostulation fell on deaf ears as he continued with his plan.
The teacher's expostulation against cheating reminded the students of their responsibilities.
Despite his friend's expostulation, Tom decided to drop out of college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - the act of expressing strong objection or disapproval
  • Synonym: remonstration - an expression of protest or opposition

Etymology

From Latin expostulatio (complaint, remonstrance), from expostulare (to demand, argue).
