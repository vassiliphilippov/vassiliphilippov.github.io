limnologist

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈnɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / lɪmˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the biology, chemistry, and ecology of inland waters such as lakes and ponds
scientist specializing in freshwater bodies

Examples

The limnologist collected samples from the lake to monitor water quality.
She became a renowned limnologist after years of studying freshwater ecosystems.
Many limnologists work to understand the impact of pollution on lakes and ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrologist - expert in the science of water; often including rivers, groundwater, etc.

Etymology

From French 'limnologie', from Greek 'limnē' (lake, marsh) + '-logy' (study of).
