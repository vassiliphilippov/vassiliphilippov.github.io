Tilia cordata

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪliə kɔrˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈtɪliə kɔːˈdɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of large, spreading European linden tree with small, dark green leaves, often grown as an ornamental or shade tree.
a European linden tree species

Examples

The magnificent Tilia cordata lined both sides of the old avenue, providing cool shade in summer.
Garden designers often recommend Tilia cordata for its attractive shape and dense foliage.
Many city parks across Europe feature mature specimens of Tilia cordata as centerpiece trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linden - a deciduous tree of the genus Tilia with heart-shaped leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tilia' (linden tree genus) and 'cordata' (heart-shaped, referring to the leaves).
