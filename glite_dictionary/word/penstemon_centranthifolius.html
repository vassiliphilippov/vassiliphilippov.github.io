Penstemon centranthifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnstəˌmɒn sɛnˌtrænθɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛnstɪˌmɒn sɛnˌtranθɪˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
a perennial plant with bright red tubular flowers arranged in long narrow clusters near the tips of upright stems, native to the coastal ranges from central California southward
California wildflower with red tubular flowers

Examples

The bright red flowers of Penstemon centranthifolius attract hummingbirds in California gardens.
We spotted Penstemon centranthifolius blooming along the sunny roadside.
Wildflower enthusiasts often seek out Penstemon centranthifolius during spring hikes in the coastal ranges.

Etymology

From genus name Penstemon, of Greek origin, and Latinized species epithet centranthifolius, meaning 'with leaves like Centranthus'.
