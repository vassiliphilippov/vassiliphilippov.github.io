intrenchment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt / (BrE)
a fortification or defensive position protected by trenches, especially one constructed during warfare
a defensive fortification with trenches

Examples

The soldiers took cover behind the intrenchment during the battle.
Archaeologists discovered remnants of an old intrenchment near the river.
The general ordered the construction of a new intrenchment to defend the position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrenchment - a defensive fortification with trenches

Etymology

Variant of 'entrenchment', based on the verb 'intrench' (to dig a trench).
