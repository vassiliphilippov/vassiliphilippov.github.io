codpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑdˌpis / (AmE), / ˈkɒdˌpiːs / (BrE)
a pouch or flap covering the crotch of men's close-fitting breeches, worn especially in the 15th and 16th centuries
flap covering men's breeches crotch (15th-16th c.)
archaic

Examples

In portraits from the Renaissance, noblemen are often shown wearing a prominent codpiece.
The museum displayed a 16th-century codpiece adorned with jewels and embroidery.
He wrote a paper on the symbolism of the codpiece in Elizabethan fashion.

Etymology

From Middle English 'cod' (scrotum) + 'piece'.
