initiation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of beginning something or introducing something new for the first time
beginning or introduction of something new

Examples

The initiation of the new program brought excitement to the entire team.
He took part in the initiation of the scientific project.
Her initiation into the club marked the start of many new friendships.

Synset Examples

she looked forward to her initiation as an adult
the foundation of a new scientific society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement - the beginning or start of something
  • Antonym: completion - the act of finishing something

Etymology

From Latin initiatio, from initiare (to begin, to admit).

/ ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)

the action of beginning something

Examples

We are all waiting for the initiation of the negotiations to resolve the conflict.
Her research examined the initiation of chemical reactions under high pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement - the beginning of something
  • Antonym: conclusion - the end or finish of an event or process

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the formal act or process of being introduced or admitted into an organization, office, or group, often involving a special ceremony or rite
entry into a group or office

Examples

After his initiation into the fraternity, David felt like he truly belonged.
The company's initiation ceremony for new employees included a welcome speech and team activities.
Her initiation into the club required answering questions about its history.

Synset Examples

his initiation into the club
he was ordered to report for induction into the army
he gave a speech as part of his installation into the hall of fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction - formal admission to an organization or position
  • Synonym: installation - formal placement in an office or position

Etymology

From Latin 'initiatio' (a beginning, an admission), from 'initiare' (to begin).
