electromyogram

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmaɪəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈmaɪəˌɡræm / (BrE)
a record or display of the electrical activity produced by skeletal muscles, typically made by a diagnostic instrument during muscle contraction
record of muscle electrical activity
medical

Examples

The doctor reviewed the electromyogram to diagnose possible nerve damage in the patient's arm.
An electromyogram can help identify disorders that affect muscle tissue or nerve function.
Researchers used an electromyogram to measure the participants' muscle responses during exercise.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electroencephalogram - record of electrical activity of the brain

Etymology

From electro- (electric) + myo- (muscle) + -gram (record, from Greek gramma).
