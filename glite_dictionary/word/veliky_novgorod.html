Veliky Novgorod

1. PROPN

/ vəˈliːki ˈnɔvɡərɒd / (AmE), / vəˈliːki ˈnɒvɡərɒd / (BrE)
a historic city in northwestern Russia on the Volkhov River, regarded as Russia's oldest city and an important medieval trading center
historic city in northwestern Russia

Examples

Many tourists visit Veliky Novgorod to admire its ancient Kremlin and historic churches.
Trade routes connecting Europe and Asia once passed through Veliky Novgorod, contributing to its prosperity in the Middle Ages.
The city of Veliky Novgorod played a pivotal role in the formation of the Russian state.

Etymology

From Russian Великий Новгород (Velikiy Novgorod), meaning 'Great New City.'
