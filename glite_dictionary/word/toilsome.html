toilsome

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔɪl.səm / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪl.səm / (BrE)
Requiring hard or exhausting effort; involving much toil and difficulty.
requiring exhausting effort

Examples

Climbing the mountain was a toilsome journey, testing the strength of all involved.
The reconstruction of the old cathedral was a toilsome process that took years to complete.
She found the daily commute to be toilsome during the harsh winter months.

Synset Examples

worked their arduous way up the mining valley
a grueling campaign
hard labor
heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring
  • Synonym: laborious - requiring considerable effort and time
  • Antonym: easy - achieved without great effort; presenting few difficulties

Etymology

From Middle English 'toil', from Old French 'toiler' (to work hard).
