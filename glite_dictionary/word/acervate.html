acervate

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɜrˌveɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːveɪt / (BrE)
(of fungi) forming or relating to a heaped-up mass or cluster, especially of spores or fruiting bodies.
heaped-up (esp. fungal) mass

Examples

The acervate arrangement of the fungal spores was clearly visible under the microscope.
Some plant diseases are characterized by acervate clusters of fungal fruiting bodies on the leaves.
A mycologist described the new species by its distinctive acervate sporophores.

Synset Examples

acervate fungous sporophores

Etymology

From Latin acervatus, past participle of acervare, from acervus (heap, mass).
