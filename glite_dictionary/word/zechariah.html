Zechariah

1. NOUN

/ ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (AmE), / ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament, consisting of the prophecies attributed to the prophet Zechariah, mainly concerning the renewal of Israel after the Babylonian Captivity
Old Testament book of prophecies

Examples

Many scholars have studied the symbolism in Zechariah to understand its references to the coming Messiah.
The Book of Zechariah is often quoted in discussions about the restoration of Israel.
Among the Minor Prophets, Zechariah stands out for its visionary passages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malachi - last book of the Old Testament with similar prophetic content

Etymology

From Hebrew Zĕkharyāh, meaning 'Yahweh has remembered'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (AmE), / ˌzɛkəˈraɪə / (BrE)
a Hebrew minor prophet of the late 6th century BC, known for his prophecies recorded in the Book of Zechariah in the Old Testament.
Hebrew minor prophet of 6th century BC

Examples

Many scholars have studied the visions of Zechariah recorded in the Hebrew Bible.
Zechariah's prophecies encouraged the Israelites during the rebuilding of the Second Temple.
The Book of Zechariah is one of the shorter books among the Old Testament prophets.

Etymology

From Hebrew Zəkharyāh, meaning 'Yahweh has remembered.'
