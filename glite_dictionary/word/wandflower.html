wandflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwændˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A tufted perennial herb with spikes of small white flowers and glossy, round to heart-shaped leaves that change color in autumn.
ornamental plant with white flower spikes

Examples

The wandflower adds year-round interest to shady garden beds with its changing foliage.
In autumn, the leaves of the wandflower turn a striking coppery-red color.
Gardeners often choose the wandflower for its delicate spikes of white blooms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwændˌflaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈwɒndˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A showy, often-cultivated plant with tawny yellow, often purple-spotted flowers, typically grown for ornamental purposes.
ornamental plant with yellow, purple-spotted flowers

Examples

The wandflower brings a splash of color to gardens in late summer.
Several species of wandflower are prized by gardeners for their striking blooms.
Her favorite perennial in the border is the tall, elegant wandflower.
