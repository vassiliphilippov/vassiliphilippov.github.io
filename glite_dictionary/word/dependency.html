dependency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənsi / (BrE)
a region or territory that is controlled by a distant country but is not fully sovereign
territory governed by a distant country

Examples

The island is a dependency of the United Kingdom.
Several Caribbean territories remain dependencies of European countries.
Greenland is an autonomous dependency within the Kingdom of Denmark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a country or area under the full or partial political control of another country
  • Synonym: protectorate - a state that is controlled and protected by another

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down, be dependent).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənsi / (BrE)
The state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else for support or existence.
state of relying on someone or something

Examples

Her financial dependency on her parents made it difficult for her to move out on her own.
The company’s dependency on a single supplier created risks for their supply chain.
Many people are trying to break free from their dependency on smartphones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independence - the state of being free from outside control; self-sufficiency
  • Synonym: reliance - dependence on or trust in someone or something

Etymology

From Latin dependere (to hang down, depend) via French dépendance and English dependent.

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənsi / (BrE)
A state of needing or being abnormally tolerant to a habit-forming substance, especially drugs or alcohol.
psychological or physical reliance on a substance
medical

Examples

Overcoming a drug dependency often requires medical intervention and strong social support.
The clinic specializes in treating alcohol dependency with counseling and group therapy.
Her struggle with opioid dependency affected every aspect of her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addiction - the condition of being addicted to a particular substance or activity
  • Antonym: independence - the state of not depending on something, especially a substance or drug

Etymology

From depend + -ency, after Latin dependentia (hanging down).
