tangle

1. VERB

/ ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to make something twisted or disordered; to disarrange or dishevel, especially hair, threads, or similar things
make twisted or disordered

Examples

The kitten managed to tangle the ball of yarn in just a few minutes.
If you don't brush your hair, the wind will tangle it badly.
She tried not to tangle her necklace while packing for the trip.

Synset Examples

The strong wind tousled my hair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to untangle - to straighten out or resolve something that is tangled
  • Synonym: to entangle - to twist together or ensnare

Etymology

From Middle English 'tanglen', probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'tangla' (to entangle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01476664-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡəl / (BrE)
a confused mass or complicated situation in which things are mixed together and difficult to separate or understand
confused mass; complicated situation

Examples

The cables behind the desk formed a tangle that took hours to straighten out.
After the debate, the politicians were left in a tangle of conflicting priorities.
She found herself caught in a tangle of emotions she couldn't easily explain.

Synset Examples

a tangle of government regulations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a situation that is dirty or badly organized
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged methodically

Etymology

From Middle English *tanglen*, likely related to Old Norse *tangl* (seaweed, entanglement).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

Examples

Related Terms
