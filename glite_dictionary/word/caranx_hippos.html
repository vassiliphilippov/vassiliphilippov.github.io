Caranx hippos

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræŋks ˈhɪpoʊs / (AmE), / kəˈræŋks ˈhɪpɒs / (BrE)
A large marine fish of the family Carangidae found in the western Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico, also known as the crevalle jack.
species of large western Atlantic fish

Examples

Anglers in Florida often catch Caranx hippos while fishing near the coast.
The diet of Caranx hippos consists mainly of smaller fish and invertebrates.
A school of Caranx hippos can be seen chasing baitfish in shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crevalle jack - Another common name for Caranx hippos

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Caranx is the genus name and hippos is Greek for 'horse'.
