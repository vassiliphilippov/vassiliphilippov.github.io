Sarracenia purpurea

1. NOUN

/ sæɹəˈsiːniə pɚˈpjʊəriə / (AmE), / sarəˈsiːniə pjʊˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
A perennial carnivorous plant with dark red flowers and broad, decumbent, tubular leaves (pitchers) forming a rosette, native to northeastern North America and naturalized in parts of Europe.
carnivorous plant with pitcher-shaped leaves

Examples

The Sarracenia purpurea is often called the purple pitcher plant due to its colorful leaves.
Researchers studied how Sarracenia purpurea traps and digests insects in its natural habitat.
Gardeners in Ireland have successfully cultivated Sarracenia purpurea in specially prepared bog gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - carnivorous plant with hollow, tubular leaves for trapping insects
  • Degree Higher: Sarracenia - genus of North American pitcher plants

Etymology

From modern Latin, Sarracenia (genus name) + purpurea (Latin for 'purple').
