Malva sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæl.və sɪlˈvɛs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈmal.və sɪlˈvɛs.trɪs / (BrE)
a species of mallow, an erect or spreading perennial plant with rosy-purple flowers, native to Europe and Asia and introduced elsewhere
a species of mallow plant with purple flowers

Examples

The roadside was lined with blooming Malva sylvestris, adding color to the landscape.
Gardeners often cultivate Malva sylvestris for its attractive rosy-purple flowers.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Malva sylvestris in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a group of plants in the Malva genus, known for their showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin malva (mallow) + sylvestris (of the woods, sylvan)
