sortie

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːti / (BrE)
(military) A single operational flight made by one aircraft during a mission or military operation.
military aircraft mission

Examples

The fighter jet completed its sortie over enemy territory without encountering resistance.
During the conflict, each pilot averaged two sorties per day.
Weather conditions forced the cancellation of several scheduled sorties yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mission - an assigned task or operation for military personnel or aircraft

Etymology

From French 'sortie' (a going out), from 'sortir' (to go out), from Latin 'sortīrī'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːti / (BrE)
a sudden attack by troops coming out from a defensive position, especially from a besieged place
military attack by troops leaving a defensive position

Examples

The troops launched a sortie against the enemy's position at dawn.
After several days of siege, the garrison attempted a desperate sortie.
The general ordered a surprise sortie to disrupt the attackers' lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sally - a sudden charge out of a besieged place against the enemy

Etymology

From French 'sortie' (an exit, a sally), from 'sortir' (to go out), ultimately from Latin 'sortīrī' (to cast lots, allot).
