risk

1. NOUN

/ rɪsk / (AmE), / rɪsk / (BrE)
a situation or source involving the possibility of danger, harm, or loss
possibility of danger or loss

Examples

Investing in stocks carries a certain risk of losing money.
Smoking increases your risk of developing heart disease.
He was aware of the risk but decided to proceed anyway.

Synset Examples

drinking alcohol is a health hazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger

Etymology

From Italian 'risco', probably from Latin 'resecāre' (to cut off).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14565454-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɪsk / (AmE), / rɪsk / (BrE)
the probability of being exposed to danger, harm, or loss, especially to an infectious agent
probability of exposure to danger or harm

Examples

Frequent travel increases your risk of being exposed to new viruses.
The risk of infection rises in crowded places.
Doctors assess a patient's risk before recommending vaccination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger or harm

Etymology

From Italian *risco* (danger), probably from Old French *risque*, from Latin *risicum* (uncertain origin).

3. NOUN

/ rɪsk / (AmE), / rɪsk / (BrE)
the possibility that something bad, dangerous, or harmful may happen; a situation involving exposure to danger or loss
exposure to danger or loss

Examples

Climbing the mountain in winter involves a significant risk of avalanches.
She was willing to take a risk to achieve her dreams.
Investing all your money in one company carries a high risk.

Synset Examples

he saw the rewards but not the risks of crime
there was a danger he would do the wrong thing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger
  • Synonym: hazard - a potential source of harm or adverse effect

Etymology

From Italian 'risco', possibly from Latin 'resicum' (danger, hazard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00803899-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ rɪsk / (AmE), / rɪsk / (BrE)
to do something that involves the possibility of loss, danger, or failure in the hope of a successful result
expose to possibility of loss or danger

Examples

She decided to risk starting her own business despite the economic downturn.
You can't risk driving without a valid license.
Many investors are willing to risk their money for a chance at higher returns.

Synset Examples

When you buy these stocks you are gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - to take risky action in the hope of a desired result
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid taking risks

Etymology

From Italian 'risco' (danger), perhaps via French 'risque', from Spanish 'riesgo'.

6. VERB

/ rɪsk / (AmE), / rɪsk / (BrE)
to expose (someone or something valued) to danger, harm, or the possibility of loss
expose to danger or loss

Examples

He would never risk his reputation for a quick profit.
If you risk everything on one idea, you could lose it all.
She risked offending her colleagues by speaking out against the proposal.

Synset Examples

We risked losing a lot of money in this venture
Why risk your life?
She laid her job on the line when she told the boss that he was wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endanger - to put someone or something at risk; to expose to danger
  • Synonym: to jeopardize - to put someone or something in danger of being harmed or lost
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone or something safe from harm or danger

Etymology

From French *risque*, from Italian *risco*, of uncertain origin.
