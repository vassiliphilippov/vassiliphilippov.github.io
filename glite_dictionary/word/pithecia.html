Pithecia

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈθiːsiə / (AmE), / pɪˈθiːsiə / (BrE)
A genus of South American monkeys known as sakis, characterized by their long bushy tails and shaggy fur.
a genus of South American sakis (monkeys)

Examples

Several species in the genus Pithecia inhabit the rainforests of South America.
The white-faced saki is a member of the Pithecia genus.
Researchers observed unique vocalizations in Pithecia populations during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saki - a small South American monkey with a long bushy tail, of the genus Pithecia

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'pithēkos' meaning 'ape' or 'monkey'.
