faithless

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪθləs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθləs / (BrE)
disloyal or untrustworthy; likely to betray trust or allegiance.
disloyal; untrustworthy

Examples

He was considered faithless after he betrayed his closest allies.
The faithless actions of the spy shocked everyone who trusted him.
She could not forgive his faithless behavior during the crisis.

Synset Examples

the faithless Benedict Arnold
a lying traitorous insurrectionist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • Synonym: treacherous - guilty of or involving betrayal or deception

Etymology

From Middle English 'faithless', formed from 'faith' + '-less'.
