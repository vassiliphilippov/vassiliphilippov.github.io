decathlon

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkæθ.lɒn / (AmE), / dɪˈkaθ.lən / (BrE)
An athletic competition in which each contestant takes part in ten different track and field events.
athletic competition of ten events

Examples

He trained for months to compete in the decathlon at the Olympics.
Winning the decathlon requires skill in a wide range of athletic disciplines.
Her brother specializes in the decathlon and has won several medals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: heptathlon - athletic contest with seven events

Etymology

From French 'décathlon', from Greek 'deka' (ten) and 'athlon' (contest).
