Tupinambis

1. NOUN

/ tuːpɪˈnæmbɪs / (AmE), / tjuːpɪˈnæmbɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, terrestrial lizards native to South America, commonly known as tegus or tejus.
genus of South American lizards (tegus)

Examples

The Tupinambis is often found in forests and grasslands across South America.
Scientists studied the diet of the Tupinambis in its natural habitat.
Some people keep Tupinambis as exotic pets due to their docile nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tegu - a large, South American lizard of the Tupinambis genus

Etymology

From Tupi (an indigenous language of Brazil), used in Modern Latin scientific naming.

2. NOUN

/ tuːpɪˈnæmbɪs / (AmE), / tjuːpɪˈnæmbɪs / (BrE)
a genus of large South American lizards, commonly called teju lizards, known for their robust bodies and active habits
genus of large South American teju lizards

Examples

The Tupinambis is often kept as a pet due to its intelligence and adaptability.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Tupinambis in the wild.
Farmers sometimes consider the Tupinambis a pest because it raids chicken coops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tegu - any member of the genus Tupinambis, large South American lizard

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Tupinambá,' the name of an indigenous people in Brazil, applied to the lizard genus by early taxonomists.
