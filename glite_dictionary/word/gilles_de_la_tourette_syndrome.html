Gilles de la Tourette syndrome

1. NOUN

/ ʒil də lɑ tuˈrɛt ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ʒil də lɑ tʊəˈrɛt ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a neurological disorder marked by involuntary tics, vocalizations, and often compulsive utterance of inappropriate words or phrases (coprolalia)
neurological disorder with tics and vocalizations
medical

Examples

People with Gilles de la Tourette syndrome may experience sudden, involuntary movements and sounds.
The doctor diagnosed the child with Gilles de la Tourette syndrome after observing a range of motor and vocal tics.
Research into Gilles de la Tourette syndrome has advanced our understanding of complex neurological conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tourette syndrome - another common term for Gilles de la Tourette syndrome

Etymology

Named after French neurologist Georges Gilles de la Tourette (1857–1904), who first described the disorder.
