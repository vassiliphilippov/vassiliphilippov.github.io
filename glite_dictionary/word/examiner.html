examiner

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzæmɪnər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæmɪnə / (BrE)
a person who inspects, investigates, or observes something carefully, especially in order to assess, evaluate, or find information
person who investigates or inspects

Examples

The examiner carefully studied the documents for any signs of forgery.
An examiner must pay close attention to even the smallest details.
The police examiner arrived promptly to survey the scene of the crime.

Synset Examples

the examiner searched for clues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigator - a person who carries out a detailed inquiry or investigation

Etymology

From Latin 'examinator', from 'examinare' (to weigh, to examine).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzæmənər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæmɪnə / (BrE)
a person who administers a test or examination, especially to judge qualifications or ability
person who administers a test

Examples

The driving examiner watched carefully as she performed the parking maneuver.
An external examiner will assess the students' final projects.
The board appointed an independent examiner to oversee the tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspector - person who inspects or examines
  • Antonym: candidate - person being examined or tested

Etymology

From Latin 'examinator', from 'examinare' (to weigh, judge)
