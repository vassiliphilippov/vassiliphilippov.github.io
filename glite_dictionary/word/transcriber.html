transcriber

1. NOUN

/ trænˈskaɪbɚ / (AmE), / trænˈskraɪbə / (BrE)
a person who makes a written or printed record of spoken material or speech
person who writes down spoken material

Examples

The transcriber worked late into the night typing up the interviews.
As a transcriber, she ensures that court proceedings are accurately recorded.
We hired a professional transcriber to convert the audio files into written documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transcriptionist - person who transcribes speech to text, especially in professional settings

Etymology

From Latin 'transcribere' (to copy out), via transcribe + -er.

2. NOUN

/ trænˈskraɪbər / (AmE), / trænˈskraɪbə / (BrE)
a musician who adapts a composition for different voices, instruments, or performance styles
musician who adapts compositions

Examples

The transcriber skillfully adapted the piano piece for a string quartet.
Many jazz performances feature a transcriber who refashions classic tunes for modern ensembles.
As a renowned transcriber, she brought forgotten works to new audiences through imaginative arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arranger - a person who adapts music for particular voices or instruments

Etymology

From Latin 'transcribere' (to copy out in writing), via transcribe + -er.

3. NOUN

/ trænˈskraɪbər / (AmE), / trænˈskraɪbə / (BrE)
a person who converts spoken language, especially its sounds, into written or phonetic notation
person who transcribes speech sounds

Examples

The transcriber used the International Phonetic Alphabet to annotate the speaker's pronunciation.
Each transcriber in the research study was assigned a different dialect to document.
As a transcriber, she was meticulous in representing each sound faithfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transcriptionist - a person who transcribes spoken words, especially audio recordings, into written form

Etymology

From transcribe + -er, based on the Latin 'transcribere' (to write across).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ trænˈskaɪbər / (AmE), / trænˈskaɪbə / (BrE)
a person who writes out spoken words or text, especially converting them into a different script or format
person who converts text or speech into writing

Examples

The transcriber carefully converted the spoken interview into a written document.
Ancient manuscripts were preserved by a skilled transcriber who rewrote them in Latin script.
As a transcriber, she ensures every detail is faithfully recorded, even when changing from one script to another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribe - a person who copies written documents
  • Synonym: translator - a person who translates text into another language

Etymology

From 'transcribe' + '-er'. 'Transcribe' from Latin 'transcribere' (to copy, write across).
