Magnoliidae

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊlɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A large subclass of flowering plants considered among the most primitive, including magnolia and buttercup families, characterized by well-developed perianths and apocarpous ovaries.
subclass of primitive flowering plants

Examples

Many botanists consider Magnoliidae to represent the earliest branch of flowering plants.
The family Magnoliaceae is a prominent member of the Magnoliidae subclass.
Some classifications treat Magnoliidae as a superorder rather than a subclass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Magnoliaceae - the magnolia family, a constituent family within Magnoliidae
  • Synonym: Ranunculaceae - the buttercup family, another family within Magnoliidae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Magnolia (type genus) + -idae (taxonomic suffix).

2. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊli.aɪˈdiː / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊli.aɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a subclass of flowering plants, considered among the most primitive, including the magnolia family and related groups, characterized by well-developed perianths and separate carpels
subclass of primitive flowering plants

Examples

Many botanists regard Magnoliidae as the most primitive group of living flowering plants.
The families within Magnoliidae share several morphological characteristics, such as separate carpels and well-developed perianths.
Research on Magnoliidae has improved our understanding of early angiosperm evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Magnolia (a genus of flowering plants) + -idae (a taxonomic suffix)
