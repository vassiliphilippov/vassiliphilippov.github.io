chaetal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈkiːtəl / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of chaetae (bristles or setae, especially in animals such as annelids)
relating to bristles or setae
medical

Examples

The worm's chaetal bands are important for movement through the soil.
Scientists observed chaetal patterns to distinguish between different species of polychaetes.
A close-up revealed the chaetal structure lining the segmented body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setaceous - having bristles or hair-like structures
  • Synonym: bristly - having or covered with bristles

Etymology

From Greek khaítē (long hair, mane), as used in scientific Latin.
