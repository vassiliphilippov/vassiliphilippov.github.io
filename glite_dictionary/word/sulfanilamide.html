sulfanilamide

1. NOUN

/ sʌlˌfænɪˈlæmaɪd / (AmE), / sʌlˌfænɪˈlæmaɪd / (BrE)
a white, odorless crystalline compound used as an antibacterial sulfa drug and as the parent compound of most sulfa drugs
antibacterial sulfa drug compound
medical

Examples

The discovery of sulfanilamide marked a major breakthrough in the treatment of bacterial infections.
Doctors prescribed sulfanilamide to combat the spread of strep throat before the advent of penicillin.
As the parent compound, sulfanilamide paved the way for the development of other sulfa drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfa drug - any synthetic antibacterial drug containing the sulfonamide group

Etymology

From 'sulfanilic acid' and 'amide', referring to its chemical structure.
