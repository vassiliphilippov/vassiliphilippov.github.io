Panax ginseng

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.æks ˈdʒɪn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpæn.æks ˈdʒɪn.sɪŋ / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to East Asia, valued for its forked aromatic root, which is used in traditional medicine.
East Asian medicinal plant (ginseng)

Examples

Traditional medicine often uses Panax ginseng to improve vitality and relieve stress.
Researchers are studying the effects of Panax ginseng on cognitive function in older adults.
Many herbal supplements contain extracts from Panax ginseng roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginseng - A plant root used as a herbal remedy, especially in East Asian medicine

Etymology

From genus name Panax (from Greek 'panakos' meaning 'all-healing') + ginseng (from Chinese rénshēn).
