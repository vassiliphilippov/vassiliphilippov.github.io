ineffective

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tɪv / (BrE)
Not producing the desired result or intended effect; lacking effectiveness.
not producing intended effect

Examples

The new policy proved to be ineffective in reducing crime rates.
His arguments were ineffective against the strong evidence presented by the opposition.
Many people found the mosquito repellant to be ineffective during their camping trip.

Synset Examples

an ineffective teacher
ineffective legislation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effective - successful in producing a desired result

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + effective (capable of producing a result).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00839148-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
Not producing any significant or intended effect; lacking the ability or skill to achieve results.
not producing intended effect

Examples

The new advertising campaign proved to be ineffective at increasing sales.
Despite several attempts, his efforts remained ineffective against the stubborn stain.
Many voters were frustrated by what they saw as an ineffective government response to the crisis.

Synset Examples

an ineffective administration
inefficient workers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effective - producing a desired or intended result

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + effective, from effectivus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00513664-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪˈfek.tɪv / (BrE)
lacking the ability or power to produce the desired effect; not effective or not forceful.
not producing the desired effect

Examples

The new policy proved ineffective in reducing crime rates across the city.
Despite several attempts, his arguments remained ineffective in persuading the committee.
The medicine was found to be ineffective against the latest strain of the virus.

Synset Examples

an ineffectual ruler
like an unable phoenix in hot ashes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effective - having the intended result; successful in producing a desired outcome
  • Synonym: ineffectual - lacking the ability to produce the intended result

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + effectivus (effective).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01830798-s
Synset POS: s
