deceleration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːsɛləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːsɛlɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A reduction in the speed, rate, or pace of something.
reduction in speed or rate

Examples

The deceleration of economic growth worried financial analysts.
Scientists measured the deceleration of the spacecraft as it approached the planet.
There has been a noticeable deceleration in population growth over the last decade.

Synset Examples

the deceleration of the arms race

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceleration - an increase in the speed, rate, or pace of something

Etymology

From Latin prefix de- (down, away) + acceleration.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdiːsɛləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛl.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A decrease in the rate of speed or velocity; the process of slowing down.
slowing down; decrease in velocity
medical

Examples

The car's deceleration was smooth as it approached the red light.
Scientists measured the deceleration of the spacecraft as it entered the planet's atmosphere.
A sudden deceleration can be dangerous for passengers if they are not wearing seat belts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceleration - an increase in the rate of speed or velocity

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down) + 'celeratio' (quickening).
