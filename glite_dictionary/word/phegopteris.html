Phegopteris

1. NOUN

/ fiːˈɡɑːptərɪs / (AmE), / fiːˈɡɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of ferns known as beech ferns, sometimes classified within the genera Dryopteris or Thelypteris.
a genus of beech ferns

Examples

The woodland understory was dotted with Phegopteris, lending a delicate green to the shade.
Botanists debate whether Phegopteris should be included in the genus Dryopteris or classified separately.
She identified the fern as Phegopteris because of its distinctive triangular fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dryopteris - a related genus of wood ferns
  • Synonym: Thelypteris - a related genus of marsh ferns

Etymology

From Greek phēgos (beech) + pteris (fern).

2. NOUN

/ fiːˈɡɑp.tɚ.ɪs / (AmE), / fiːˈɡɒp.tər.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of ferns known as beech ferns, sometimes regarded as a synonym of Dryopteris or included in the genus Thelypteris.
a genus of beech ferns

Examples

The Phegopteris genus is often referred to as beech ferns due to their preferred habitat.
Some botanists debate whether Phegopteris should be a separate genus or part of Dryopteris.
In shady woodlands, Phegopteris species can form dense colonies of delicate fronds.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'phegos' (beech) and 'pteris' (fern).
