kitten-tails

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtn̩ teɪlz / (AmE), / ˈkɪtn teɪlz / (BrE)
a North American plant of the genus Besseya, having fluffy spikes of small flowers, resembling a kitten's tail
a North American plant with fluffy flower spikes

Examples

Several kitten-tails bloomed along the edge of the meadow, their fluffy spikes swaying in the breeze.
The botanist carefully identified the plant as a species of kitten-tails belonging to the genus Besseya.
Gardeners admire kitten-tails for their delicate, tail-like clusters of tiny flowers.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its flower spikes to a kitten's tail.
