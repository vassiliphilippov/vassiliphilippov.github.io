backdoor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈbæk.dɔː / (BrE)
a secret or unofficial way of gaining access to a place, organization, or position, often by circumventing normal procedures or rules
secret or unofficial means of access

Examples

The hacker found a backdoor into the company's internal network.
He was accused of using a backdoor to secure the contract.
By using personal connections, she gained entry to the club through a backdoor.

Synset Examples

he got his job through the back door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortcut - an alternative and faster way of doing something
  • Synonym: loophole - means of evading a rule or regulation
  • Antonym: front door - open, official, or direct means of access
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00282135-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌdɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌdɔː / (BrE)
An undocumented or secret means of gaining access to a computer system or its data, bypassing normal security measures.
secret access to a computer system

Examples

The hackers gained entry through a backdoor left in the server’s operating system.
Security experts warn that any backdoor can be exploited by malicious actors.
The software developer denied intentionally installing a backdoor in the program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapdoor - a concealed means of access, especially in computing or cryptography
  • Synonym: exploit - a software tool that takes advantage of flaws to bypass security
  • Antonym: firewall - a system that prevents unauthorized access to or from a network
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06365888-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈbæk.dɔː / (BrE)
a door situated at the rear of a building, typically used as a secondary entrance or exit
door at the rear of a building

Examples

I forgot to lock the backdoor before leaving the house.
Delivery drivers usually leave packages at the backdoor to avoid being seen from the street.
She quietly slipped out the backdoor to go to the garden.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front door - the main door at the front of a building
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02771749-n
Synset POS: n
