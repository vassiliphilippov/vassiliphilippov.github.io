naturalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃ(ə)rəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes that everything in the world can be explained by natural or scientific laws, without recourse to the supernatural.
person who advocates scientific naturalism

Examples

As a naturalist, she argued that all phenomena have scientific explanations.
Many Enlightenment thinkers were naturalists who rejected supernatural causes.
The debate between the naturalist and the spiritualist was lively and well-attended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: materialist - person who believes only material things exist
  • Antonym: supernaturalist - person who believes in the supernatural

Etymology

From Latin 'natura' (nature) + '-ist', denoting one who practices or is concerned with something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃ(ə)rəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or is an expert in natural history, especially botany or zoology
a person knowledgeable in natural history

Examples

Charles Darwin was a renowned naturalist who studied species during his voyage on the HMS Beagle.
The naturalist led a group of students on a walk through the forest to observe wildlife.
As a naturalist, she spent much of her career documenting new plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biologist - a person specializing in the science of biology

Etymology

From Latin natura (nature) + -ist (denoting someone who practices or is concerned with something).
