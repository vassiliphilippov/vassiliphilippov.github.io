squalid

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈskwɒlɪd / (BrE)
(of actions or places) morally degraded; showing or involving a lack of moral standards or decency.
morally degraded or sordid

Examples

The squalid details of the scandal shocked the entire community.
He grew up in a squalid environment of crime and corruption.
The film depicts the squalid underworld of gambling and betrayal.

Synset Examples

a seedy district
the seamy side of life
sleazy characters hanging around casinos
sleazy storefronts with … dirt on the walls
the sordid details of his orgies stank under his very nostrils
the squalid atmosphere of intrigue and betrayal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sordid - involving immoral or dishonest behavior
  • Synonym: seedy - morally degraded or disreputable
  • Synonym: sleazy - morally low or sordid
  • Antonym: virtuous - showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'squalidus' (rough, filthy, neglected), from 'squalere' (to be dirty).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈskwɒlɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) extremely dirty and unpleasant, especially as a result of poverty or neglect.
extremely dirty and unpleasant

Examples

The refugees were forced to live in squalid camps on the outskirts of the city.
He was appalled by the squalid conditions in the abandoned warehouse.
The investigative report exposed the squalid reality behind the luxurious facade.

Synset Examples

a flyblown bar on the edge of town
a squalid overcrowded apartment in the poorest part of town
squalid living conditions
sordid shantytowns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filthy - extremely dirty
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

Etymology

From Latin 'squalidus' (rough, dirty, filthy).
