to disburden

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːdn / (BrE)
to relieve or remove a burden from someone or something
remove a burden

Examples

It took several hours to disburden the truck after the long journey.
She tried to disburden her mind of worries before going to sleep.
The volunteers helped disburden the refugees of their heavy luggage.

Synset Examples

unburden the donkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unburden - to relieve someone or something of a burden

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing removal, reversal) + burden.
