qadi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdi / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdi / (BrE)
a judge in Islamic law, especially in a Sharia court
Islamic judge

Examples

The qadi issued a verdict after carefully reviewing the evidence presented in court.
During the Ottoman Empire, a qadi often played a key role in local administration.
People brought disputes about inheritance before the qadi to seek a fair resolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - a public official authorized to decide legal cases

Etymology

From Arabic 'qāḍī' (judge), from root 'qaḍā' (to decide, to judge).
