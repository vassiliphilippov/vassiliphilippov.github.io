tangential

1. ADJ

/ tænˈdʒɛnʃəl / (AmE), / tanˈdʒɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or acting along the direction of a tangent; touching a curve or surface at a single point without crossing it.
relating to a tangent or tangency

Examples

The engineer explained how tangential forces act on the spinning wheel.
The moon's tangential velocity prevents it from falling into the Earth.
In geometry class, we learned how a line can be tangential to a circle at just one point.

Synset Examples

tangential forces

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radial - relating to or arranged like rays or radii; extending from or converging to a center

Etymology

From Latin tangens, tangentis (present participle of tangere 'to touch') + English suffix -ial.

2. ADJ

/ tænˈdʒɛnʃəl / (AmE), / tanˈdʒɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of a remark, idea, etc.) only slightly or indirectly related to the main topic; not closely connected.
only slightly relevant

Examples

Her comments were interesting but ultimately tangential to the main discussion.
The professor made a tangential reference that was only loosely connected to the lecture topic.
Your question is tangential; let's focus on the core issue.

Synset Examples

a digressive allusion to the day of the week
a tangential remark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to the matter at hand
  • Synonym: digressive - departing from the main subject

Etymology

From Medieval Latin tangentialis, from Latin tangere (to touch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01983424-s
Synset POS: s
