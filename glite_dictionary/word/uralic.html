Uralic

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɑlɪk / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken in northern Eurasia, including Finnish, Hungarian, and Estonian
language family including Finnish and Hungarian

Examples

Most speakers of Uralic languages live in northern and eastern Europe.
Hungarian and Finnish are both members of the Uralic language family.
Linguists study the origins and differences among Uralic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finno-Ugric - another major grouping within the Uralic family
  • Antonym: Indo-European - a different major language family

Etymology

From the Ural Mountains, the region associated with the origin of these languages, with the suffix -ic.
