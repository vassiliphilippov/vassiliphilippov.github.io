cystine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstin / (AmE), / ˈsɪstiːn / (BrE)
a sulfur-containing amino acid that occurs in proteins, especially in keratin, and is important in protein structure
a sulfur-containing amino acid
medical

Examples

Keratin in hair contains substantial amounts of cystine, which gives it strength.
Researchers analyzed the cystine levels in the patients' blood samples.
Bladder stones were once a major source for the extraction of cystine in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cysteine - a related sulfur-containing amino acid found in proteins, can convert to cystine

Etymology

From cyst- (referring to the bladder, as it was first found in bladder stones) + -ine, from Greek 'kystis' (bladder) and chemical suffix '-ine'.
