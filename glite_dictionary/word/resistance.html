resistance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
an electrical device, such as a resistor, that reduces the flow of electric current in a circuit
device that opposes electric current

Examples

The engineer replaced the broken resistance to restore the circuit's function.
Adjusting the resistance changes the brightness of the lamp.
We measured the resistance using an ohmmeter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistor - an electrical component that offers resistance to current

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the opposition that a material offers to the flow of electric current, measured in ohms
opposition to electrical current

Examples

The copper wire has a low resistance, making it ideal for electrical wiring.
Increasing the length of the wire increases its resistance to electrical current.
The engineer measured the resistance of the circuit with a multimeter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conductance - ability of a material to conduct electric current, reciprocal of resistance

Etymology

From Latin resistentia (opposition), from resistere (to withstand).
