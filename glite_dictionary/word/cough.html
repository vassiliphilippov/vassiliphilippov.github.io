cough

1. NOUN

/ kɑf / (AmE), / kɒf / (BrE)
A sudden, forceful expulsion of air from the lungs, usually to clear the air passages.
sudden expulsion of air from lungs

Examples

His persistent cough kept him awake all night.
The doctor listened to her cough before suggesting any treatment.
She covered her mouth to muffle a sudden cough during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack - a dry, harsh cough

Etymology

From Middle English 'coughe', from Old English 'cohhian' (to cough).

2. VERB

/ kɑf / (AmE), / kɒf / (BrE)
to expel air suddenly and noisily from the lungs, usually to clear the throat or as a symptom of illness
expel air noisily from the lungs

Examples

He tried not to cough during the quiet performance, but couldn't hold it in.
Allergy season makes her cough more than usual.
The doctor asked if he had coughed up any phlegm.

Synset Examples

The smoker coughs all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack - to make a short, dry sound in the throat or cough noisily
  • Antonym: sneeze - to expel air forcibly from the nose and mouth due to irritation of one’s nostrils

Etymology

From Middle English 'coughe', from Old English 'cohhian' (to cough).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00005815-v
Synset POS: v
