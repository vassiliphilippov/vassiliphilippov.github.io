suddenly

1. ADV

/ ˈsʌd(ə)nli / (AmE), / ˈsʌd(ə)nli / (BrE)
Quickly and unexpectedly; without warning.
quickly and without warning

Examples

The lights went out suddenly, plunging the house into darkness.
She suddenly realized she had forgotten her keys at home.
The weather can change suddenly in the mountains, so be prepared.

Synset Examples

he stopped suddenly
suddenly she felt a sharp pain in her side
he decided to go to Chicago on the spur of the moment
he made up his mind suddenly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptly - quickly and unexpectedly
  • Antonym: gradually - taking place slowly and steadily over time

Etymology

From the adjective "sudden" + -ly.
