collet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.lɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ɪt / (BrE)
A band or collar that securely holds an individual stone in a jewelry setting.
band or collar holding a stone in jewelry

Examples

The diamond was set in a gold collet to enhance its brilliance.
The jeweler inspected the collet to ensure the stone was held firmly.
Vintage rings often feature a distinctive collet setting for the gemstone.

Etymology

From Old French 'collet', diminutive of 'col' (neck), Late Latin 'collum' (neck).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪt / (BrE)
a metal cap or band fitted around the end of a wooden pole or tool to prevent splitting
metal band to prevent splitting of wood

Examples

A collet should be installed at the end of the broom handle to stop it from cracking.
The craftsman secured the axe head with a brass collet for extra durability.
Without a collet, wooden stakes can easily split during use.

Etymology

From Old French *collet* (collar), diminutive of *col* (neck).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlət / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪt / (BrE)
A cone-shaped device used to hold a cylindrical object firmly in place in a lathe or other machine tool.
cone-shaped chuck for holding objects

Examples

The machinist inserted the rod into the collet before tightening it securely.
A collet provides a precise grip on the workpiece during high-speed rotation.
Different sizes of collets are available for various diameters of rods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuck - a device for holding a workpiece in a machine tool

Etymology

From Old French collet (neck), diminutive of col (neck), referring to the shape.
