Phegopteris connectilis

1. NOUN

/ fiːˌgɑp.tər.ɪs kəˈnɛk.tɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / fiːˌgɒp.tər.ɪs kəˈnɛk.tɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of beech fern native to North America and Eurasia, growing in moist, shaded woodlands.
species of beech fern

Examples

The Phegopteris connectilis thrives in damp woodlands across North America.
Botanists identified the fern as Phegopteris connectilis during their survey of Eurasian forests.
You can recognize Phegopteris connectilis by its delicate, triangular fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech fern - A common name for ferns of the genus Phegopteris

Etymology

From Greek 'phegós' (beech) and 'pteris' (fern), denoting 'beech fern'; species name from Latin 'connectilis' (connected).
