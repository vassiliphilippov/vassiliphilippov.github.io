symmachy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.ə.ki / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.ə.ki / (BrE)
An alliance for joint military action, especially among states or political entities.
military alliance for joint action

Examples

The ancient city-states formed a symmachy to defend against common threats.
Historians often debate the effectiveness of the Peloponnesian symmachy in balancing power within Greece.
A symmachy can sometimes dissolve when the interests of the allies no longer align.

Synset Examples

In technical language the Peloponnesian League was a hegemonic symmachy

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'συμμαχία' (symmakhía), meaning 'alliance', from 'σύμμαχος' (sýmmachos), 'allied in war'.
