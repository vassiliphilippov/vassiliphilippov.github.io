fruticulose

1. ADJ

/ fruːˈtɪkjəˌloʊs / (AmE), / fruːˈtɪkjʊləʊs / (BrE)
(Botany) of, relating to, or resembling a shrub; shrubby in form.
shrub-like; shrubby (botany)

Examples

The botanist identified several fruticulose lichens growing in the rocky area.
Some plants in the region have a fruticulose appearance, making them hard to distinguish from true shrubs.
The fruticulose formation of the bushes provided excellent cover for small animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrubby - resembling or consisting of shrubs

Etymology

From Latin fruticulosus (full of shrubs), from fruticulum ‘little shrub’.
