to alliterate

1. VERB

/ əˈlɪtəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈlɪtəreɪt / (BrE)
to use alliteration, especially in poetry or prose, by repeating the same initial consonant sound in adjacent or closely connected words
use alliteration in speech or writing

Examples

The poet skillfully chose words that alliterate to create a musical effect in the stanza.
Writers often alliterate to make their language more memorable and engaging.
Children love to alliterate when inventing tongue-twisters for fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliteration - the occurrence of the same letter or sound at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words

Etymology

From Latin alliteratus, past participle of alliterare, from ad- (to) + littera (letter).
