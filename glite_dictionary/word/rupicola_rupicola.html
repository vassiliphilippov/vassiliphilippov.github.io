Rupicola rupicola

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈpɪkələ ruːˈpɪkələ / (AmE), / ruːˈpɪkələ ruːˈpɪkələ / (BrE)
A species of tropical bird found in northern South America, known for the male's brilliant red or orange plumage and distinctive disk-shaped crest; commonly called the Guianan cock-of-the-rock.
South American bird with bright plumage

Examples

The Rupicola rupicola is admired for the vibrant coloration of the male birds.
Wildlife enthusiasts often travel to observe the elaborate mating displays of Rupicola rupicola.
Biologists study the habitat preferences of Rupicola rupicola in South American rainforests.

Etymology

From Latin: 'rupes' (rock) and 'cola' (dweller), referring to its habitat among rocky outcrops.
