Armoracia rusticana

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrməˈreɪʃə rʌˈstɪkænə / (AmE), / ˌɑːməˈreɪsɪə rʌsˈtɪkənə / (BrE)
a coarse Eurasian plant cultivated for its thick, white, pungent root, commonly known as horseradish
species of plant, horseradish

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Armoracia rusticana for its spicy root, which is used to make condiment sauces.
The thick root of Armoracia rusticana adds a sharp flavor to traditional dishes.
Chefs often grate Armoracia rusticana to prepare fresh horseradish sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseradish - the common name for Armoracia rusticana, particularly referring to its root

Etymology

From Latin; 'Armoracia' is the genus name, and 'rusticana' means 'of the country' or 'rustic'.
