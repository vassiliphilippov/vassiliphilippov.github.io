Mimosa

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A genus of spiny shrubs or trees in the pea family, notable for their sensitivity to touch and movement.
genus of sensitive spiny shrubs or trees

Examples

The Mimosa reacts to touch by rapidly folding its leaves.
Several species of Mimosa are cultivated for their unusual sensitivity to movement.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Mimosa genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'mimosa', from Greek 'mimos' meaning 'mimic' or 'imitator', referring to its sensitivity.
