mimosa

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a cocktail made with champagne and orange juice, typically served at brunch
cocktail of champagne and orange juice

Examples

She ordered a mimosa to enjoy with her brunch.
At weddings, mimosas are often served as a light and festive drink.
The recipe calls for equal parts of champagne and orange juice for a perfect mimosa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buck's fizz - a similar cocktail of champagne and orange juice, typically using more orange juice

Etymology

Named after the yellow-flowered mimosa plant, reflecting the drink's color.

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to Australasia, with white or silvery bark, feathery young leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers.
Australasian tree with yellow flowers

Examples

The gardens were bordered with tall mimosa trees, their yellow blossoms brightening the landscape.
In spring, the mimosa fills the air with a sweet, delicate fragrance.
She admired the silvery bark of the mimosa during her walk in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wattle - another term for several Australian acacias, including some mimosa species

Etymology

From Latin mimosa, feminine of mimosus (‘mimic’), so named for the plant's leaf movements.

3. NOUN

/ mɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a tropical shrub or tree of the genus Mimosa, often with yellow flowers and compound leaves
tropical shrub or tree of the genus Mimosa

Examples

The mimosa in our garden blooms with delicate yellow flowers every spring.
Scientists studied the unique leaf movements of the mimosa as part of their research.
She admired the feathery foliage of the mimosa during her walk through the park.

Etymology

From Latin Mimosa (name of the plant), from mimus (mimic), referring to the sensitivity of some species to touch.
