modiolus

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈdaɪ.əl.əs / (AmE), / məˈdiː.əl.əs / (BrE)
the central conical bony pillar of the cochlea around which the cochlear canals are spiraled
central pillar of the cochlea
medical

Examples

The modiolus provides structural support for the spiral canals of the cochlea.
Damage to the modiolus can affect hearing function by impacting the cochlear nerves.
The central nerves pass through the modiolus to reach the auditory brain centers.

Etymology

From Latin *modiolus* (small cylindrical vessel, nave of a wheel).
