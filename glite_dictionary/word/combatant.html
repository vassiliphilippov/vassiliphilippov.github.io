combatant

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈbætənt / (AmE), / kəmˈbætənt / (BrE)
engaged in or ready to fight in a battle or conflict
engaged in combat; ready for battle

Examples

The combatant troops advanced under cover of darkness.
He was regarded as a combatant member of the resistance.
All combatant forces must report to their commanding officers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belligerent - hostile and aggressive; engaged in war or conflict
  • Antonym: noncombatant - not engaged in fighting during a conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'combattre', from com- (together) + battre (to fight).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈbætənt / (AmE), / kəmˈbætənt / (BrE)
a person who takes part in fighting, especially during a war or conflict
person engaged in fighting

Examples

During the conflict, each combatant wore a uniform to distinguish themselves from civilians.
She was recognized as a brave combatant on the battlefield.
The international law protects both prisoners of war and wounded combatants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fighter - a person who fights
  • Antonym: civilian - a person not in the armed forces or police

Etymology

From French 'combatant', present participle of 'combattre' (to fight), from Late Latin 'combattere'.
