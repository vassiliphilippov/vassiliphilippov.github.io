negatively

1. ADV

/ ˈnɛɡ.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡ.ətɪv.li / (BrE)
In a way that causes harm or has a damaging effect.
in a harmful or damaging way

Examples

The new policy negatively impacted small businesses in the area.
Pollution negatively affects both human health and the environment.
He tends to interpret feedback negatively, even when it's meant to be constructive.

Synset Examples

he was negatively affected

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positively - in a beneficial or affirmative way

Etymology

Formed from negative + -ly (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00004669-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈnɛɡ.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡ.ətɪv.li / (BrE)
In a way that expresses or shows disapproval, denial, or the absence of something; in a negative manner.
in a negative manner

Examples

She reacted negatively to the feedback, refusing to make any changes.
The proposal was received negatively by the board members, who raised several concerns.
If you think about yourself negatively, it can affect your self-esteem.

Synset Examples

he was negatively inclined

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positively - in a positive way; expressing approval or affirmation

Etymology

From 'negative' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'negativus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00004565-r
Synset POS: r
