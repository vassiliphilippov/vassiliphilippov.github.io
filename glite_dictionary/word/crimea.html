Crimea

1. PROPN

/ kraɪˈmiːə / (AmE), / kraɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
a peninsula in southeastern Europe between the Black Sea and the Sea of Azov, politically and geographically significant.
Ukrainian peninsula by the Black Sea

Examples

The political status of Crimea has been the subject of international dispute.
Several cities in Crimea attract tourists due to their beautiful beaches and resorts.
The Crimean mountains form a dramatic backdrop to the southern coast of Crimea.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Crimea', from Crimean Tatar 'Qırım'.
