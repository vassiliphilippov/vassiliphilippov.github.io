Alligator sinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪˈɡeɪtər sɪˈnɛnˌsɪs / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪtə sɪˈnɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
a small, endangered alligator species native to the Yangtze valley of China, identified by its unwebbed digits
Chinese alligator species

Examples

The Alligator sinensis is one of the world's rarest crocodilian species.
Conservationists work tirelessly to protect the natural habitat of the Alligator sinensis.
Unlike its American cousin, the Alligator sinensis has unwebbed digits and a shorter snout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese alligator - Alternative common name for Alligator sinensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'alligator' (alligator) + 'sinensis' (of China), indicating its Chinese origin.
