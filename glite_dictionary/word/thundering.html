thundering

1. ADJ

/ ˈθʌndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərɪŋ / (BrE)
making a loud, deep, rumbling sound like thunder
making a sound like thunder

Examples

A thundering noise echoed through the empty hallways.
The horses crossed the field with thundering hooves.
A thundering applause erupted after the performance.

Synset Examples

the thundering herd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roaring - making a loud, deep, continuous sound
  • Synonym: thunderous - very loud and powerful, like thunder

2. ADJ

/ ˈθʌndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely great in degree; remarkably impressive or intense
extraordinarily impressive or intense

Examples

The team achieved a thundering victory in the championship game.
Her novel was met with thundering applause at the book launch.
Despite his thundering speech, the audience remained unconvinced.

Synset Examples

a thundering success
the thundering silence of what was left unsaid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size or extent
  • Synonym: tremendous - very great in amount, scale, or intensity
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size or amount

Etymology

From the verb 'thunder' (to make a loud noise), used figuratively for emphasis.
