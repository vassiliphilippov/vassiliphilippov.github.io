harrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈharəʊ / (BrE)
A farming implement with spikes or disks used to break up and smooth out soil, especially after plowing.
farm tool for breaking soil

Examples

The farmer used a harrow to prepare the field for planting.
After plowing, she ran the harrow over the soil to make it smooth.
The old harrow was rusty but still effective at breaking up clods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plow - a farm implement used for cutting and turning earth

Etymology

Old English *hergian* (to harrow, devastate), later used for the agricultural implement.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhærəʊ / (BrE)
to break up and smooth the surface of (land) with a harrow after plowing
to break up soil with a harrow

Examples

The farmer will harrow the field to prepare it for planting.
After plowing, they carefully harrowed the land to break up large clods of soil.
It is essential to harrow the ground evenly for a successful crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plow - to turn over the earth with a plow

Etymology

From Old English *hearge* (a harrow), possibly of Germanic origin.
