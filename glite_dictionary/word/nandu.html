nandu

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːn.du / (AmE), / ˈnand.uː / (BrE)
A large, fast-running, flightless bird native to South America, similar to the ostrich but smaller and three-toed.
South American flightless bird related to ostrich

Examples

The nandu is well adapted to the open grasslands of South America.
Unlike ostriches, the nandu has three toes rather than two.
Farmers sometimes raise nandus for their meat and eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhea - another name for the nandu, especially in scientific contexts

Etymology

From Guaraní 'ñandú', taken into Spanish and then into English.
