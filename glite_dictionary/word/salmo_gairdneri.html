Salmo gairdneri

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælmoʊ ɡɛərdˈnɛri / (AmE), / ˌsælməʊ ɡɛədˈnɪəri / (BrE)
a species of trout, commonly known as the rainbow trout, found in Pacific coastal waters and streams from California to Alaska
rainbow trout species

Examples

The Salmo gairdneri is highly prized by anglers for its fighting ability and beauty.
Conservation programs have been established to protect native populations of Salmo gairdneri in Pacific streams.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Salmo gairdneri in relation to water temperature changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainbow trout - a common name for Salmo gairdneri, a species of trout

Etymology

From Latin 'Salmo' (salmon) and the specific epithet honoring Dr. Meredith Gairdner.
