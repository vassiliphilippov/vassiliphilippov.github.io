repute

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpjut / (AmE), / rɪˈpjuːt / (BrE)
the opinion generally held about someone or something; especially the state of being highly respected or esteemed
high reputation or esteem

Examples

The university is of great repute throughout the country for its research.
He is a lawyer of considerable repute in international law.
That artist’s repute grew after her work was showcased in major galleries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reputation - overall opinion or regard in which a person or thing is held
  • Antonym: disrepute - the state of being held in low esteem

Etymology

From Latin 'reputare' (to think over, consider), via Old French 'reputer'.

2. VERB

/ rɪˈpjut / (AmE), / rɪˈpjuːt / (BrE)
to consider or regard (someone or something) as having a particular quality; to think of as.
consider or regard as

Examples

The expert is reputed to have solved the case single-handedly.
She is reputed to speak six languages fluently.
That mansion is reputed to be haunted by several ghosts.

Synset Examples

she looked on this affair as a joke
He thinks of himself as a brilliant musician
He is reputed to be intelligent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regard - to consider or think of in a specified way
  • Synonym: to deem - to judge or consider in a particular way
  • Synonym: to consider - to think about carefully, especially to make a decision

Etymology

From Latin reputare (to consider, estimate).
