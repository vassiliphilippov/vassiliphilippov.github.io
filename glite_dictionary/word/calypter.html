calypter

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪptər / (AmE), / kəˈlɪptə / (BrE)
a scalelike structure between the base of the wing and the halter of a two-winged fly
wing structure in two-winged flies

Examples

The presence of a prominent calypter helps distinguish certain species of flies from others.
In entomological studies, the calypter is observed for its role in flight stability.
Damage to the calypter can affect a fly's ability to maneuver effectively.

Etymology

From Greek kalupter (covering, lid), from kalúptō (to cover).
