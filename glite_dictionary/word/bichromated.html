bichromated

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈkroʊ.meɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / bʌɪˈkrəʊ.meɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) treated or combined with bichromate, especially in chemical or photographic processes.
treated with bichromate

Examples

The bichromated paper process was popular in early photographic printing techniques.
He experimented with bichromated gelatin to create detailed images.
These prints were created using a bichromated solution for added contrast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromated - treated or combined with chromate

Etymology

From 'bi-' meaning 'two' and 'chromate', referring to a chemical compound with two chromium atoms.
