gallamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.əˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈɡal.ə.miːn / (BrE)
A synthetic compound used as a muscle relaxant during anesthesia by blocking nerve impulses at neuromuscular junctions.
muscle relaxant used in anesthesia
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered gallamine to ensure the patient's muscles were fully relaxed during surgery.
Because of its long duration of action, gallamine was once preferred for certain surgical procedures.
Allergic reactions to gallamine are rare but must be considered when planning anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Flaxedil - Trade name for gallamine used as a muscle relaxant
