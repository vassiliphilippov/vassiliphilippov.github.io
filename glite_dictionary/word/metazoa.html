Metazoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A major group of animals whose bodies are composed of multiple cells differentiated into tissues and organs, typically possessing a digestive cavity and nervous system.
multicellular animals group

Examples

The Metazoa comprise all animals except for simple single-celled organisms like protozoa.
Many researchers study the evolutionary relationships among different groups of Metazoa.
Sponges represent some of the most primitive forms of Metazoa known to science.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Protozoa - unicellular, mostly microscopic animals

Etymology

From Greek 'meta' (after, beyond) + 'zōion' (animal).
