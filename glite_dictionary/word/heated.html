heated

1. ADJ

/ ˈhiːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhiːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a situation or discussion) marked by intense emotion, anger, or excitement; vehement.
emotionally intense or vehement

Examples

The debate became heated as each side refused to compromise.
We had a heated discussion about politics last night.
She regretted her heated response after she calmed down.

Synset Examples

a heated argument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions
  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief

Etymology

From the verb 'heat', meaning 'make or become hot', extended figuratively to strong emotion.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhiːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhiːtɪd / (BrE)
made warm or hot, especially by means of external energy such as fire, electricity, or sunlight.
made warm or hot

Examples

The water in the heated pool stayed comfortable all evening.
She placed her coffee mug on a heated coaster to keep it warm.
After working out in the gym, his face was heated and flushed.

Synset Examples

a heated swimming pool
wiped his heated-up face with a large bandana
he was all het up and sweaty

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hot - having a high temperature
  • Antonym: cold - having a low temperature

Etymology

From the past participle of 'heat,' from Old English *hǣtan* (to make hot).
