pachydermatous

1. ADJ

/ pæk.ɪˈdɜr.mə.təs / (AmE), / pak.ɪˈdɜː.mə.təs / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of pachyderms (thick-skinned animals such as elephants, rhinoceroses, and hippopotamuses); having thick skin.
Relating to pachyderms; thick-skinned

Examples

The pachydermatous hide of the elephant protects it from thorns and biting insects.
Her criticism was so blunt that only a pachydermatous person would not be offended.
Scientists studied the pachydermatous qualities of the rhinoceros's skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pachyderm - A thick-skinned, hoofed mammal (elephant, rhino, etc.)

Etymology

From Greek 'pakhydermatos', from 'pakhys' (thick) + 'derma' (skin).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpæk.ɪˈdɝː.mə.təs / (AmE), / ˌpæk.ɪˈdɜː.mə.təs / (BrE)
(figurative) emotionally hardened; showing an insensitive or callous attitude toward others.
emotionally hardened or insensitive

Examples

Despite the team's criticism, he remained pachydermatous and unmoved.
Her pachydermatous response to the news shocked everyone in the room.
In politics, many leaders become pachydermatous to public opinion over time.

Synset Examples

a callous indifference to suffering
cold-blooded and indurate to public opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
  • Antonym: sensitive - easily affected by the feelings or circumstances of others

Etymology

From Greek 'pachys' (thick) + 'derma' (skin), originally referring to animals with thick skin.
