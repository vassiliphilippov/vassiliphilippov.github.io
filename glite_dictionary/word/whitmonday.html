Whitmonday

1. NOUN

/ wɪtˈmʌn.deɪ / (AmE), / wɪtˈmʌn.deɪ / (BrE)
The day after Whitsunday, observed as a legal holiday in England, Wales, and Ireland.
Monday after Whitsunday; holiday in UK and Ireland
british

Examples

Many people used to enjoy picnics and fairs on Whitmonday in 19th-century Britain.
The banks will be closed tomorrow for Whitmonday.
He always remembers family gatherings on Whitmonday when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Whitsunday - The seventh Sunday after Easter, also known as Pentecost

Etymology

From 'Whit', related to Whitsunday (Pentecost), and 'Monday'.
