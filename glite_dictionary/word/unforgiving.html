unforgiving

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.fərˈɡɪ.vɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fəˈɡɪv.ɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing or able to forgive or show mercy towards others; holding resentment or harshness.
not willing to forgive; harsh

Examples

Her unforgiving nature made it hard for her to maintain friendships over time.
The unforgiving look in his eyes made everyone uncomfortable.
He faced the consequences of his mistake in an unforgiving manner.

Synset Examples

a surly unforgiving old woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy or pity
  • Antonym: forgiving - willing to forgive or show mercy

Etymology

un- (not) + forgiving, from forgive (Old English forgiefan, to give, grant, pardon).
