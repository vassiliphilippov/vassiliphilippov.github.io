cleavage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkliːvɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or state of splitting or being split, especially along a natural line or division
the act of splitting or dividing

Examples

The mineral shows a distinct cleavage along its crystal planes.
During cell division, the process of cleavage produces two separate cells.
The geologist explained the rock's natural cleavage and how it affects its structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - the act of breaking or dividing something into parts

Etymology

From 'cleave' + '-age', indicating the act or process of cleaving.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkliːvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkliːvɪdʒ / (BrE)
(Embryology) The repeated division of a fertilized egg cell into smaller cells during early development.
cell division in embryology
medical

Examples

After fertilization, the zygote undergoes rapid cleavage to form a multicellular embryo.
The pattern of cleavage varies among different species during embryonic development.
Scientists observed the process of cleavage under the microscope to study cell division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitosis - cell division resulting in two identical cells
  • Antonym: meiosis - cell division that produces gametes with half the number of chromosomes

Etymology

From 'cleave' (to split) + '-age', originally referring to the act of splitting.
