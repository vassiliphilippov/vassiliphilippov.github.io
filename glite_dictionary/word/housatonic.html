Housatonic

1. PROPN

/ ˌhuːsəˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhuːsəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
a river in western Massachusetts that flows south through Connecticut and empties into Long Island Sound.
river in Massachusetts and Connecticut

Examples

The Housatonic River is popular for fishing and boating in the summer.
Historic mills once lined the banks of the Housatonic in Connecticut.
She enjoyed a scenic drive along the Housatonic during the autumn foliage season.

Etymology

From the Mohican language, said to mean 'beyond the mountain place' or 'river of the mountain place'.
