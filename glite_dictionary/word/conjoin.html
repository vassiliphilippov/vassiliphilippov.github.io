to conjoin

1. VERB

/ kənˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to join or come together; to unite or combine two or more things
to join or unite

Examples

In the ceremony, the two families were conjoined by marriage.
The streams conjoin at the base of the mountain to form a river.
Researchers hope to conjoin the findings from both studies for a broader analysis.

Synset Examples

The two roads join here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to bring things together or merge them
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be moved apart

Etymology

From Latin 'conjungere', from 'con-' (together) + 'jungere' (to join).
