torque

1. NOUN

/ tɔrk / (AmE), / tɔːk / (BrE)
a force that causes an object to rotate around an axis; a twisting force
rotational or twisting force

Examples

The mechanic measured the torque needed to loosen the bolt.
High torque is important for vehicles that tow heavy loads.
If you apply more torque, the lid should come off easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - rotational effect of a force

Etymology

From Latin 'torquere' (to twist).
