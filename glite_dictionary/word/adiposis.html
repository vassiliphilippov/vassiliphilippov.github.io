adiposis

1. NOUN

/ ˌædɪˈpoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌadɪˈpəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by excessive fatness or the accumulation of fat in the body.
excessive accumulation of body fat
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that adiposis could increase the risk of several chronic diseases.
Researchers are studying the genetic factors that contribute to adiposis in patients.
A sedentary lifestyle may lead to adiposis over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obesity - the condition of being very overweight

Etymology

From Latin 'adeps, adipis' (fat) and Greek '-osis' (condition).
