Mutinus caninus

1. NOUN

/ mjuːˈtaɪnəs kəˈnaɪnəs / (AmE), / mjuːˈtiːnəs kəˈnaɪnəs / (BrE)
A type of stinkhorn fungus with a slender, phallic-shaped stalk covered in slimy spore mass near the tip, often attracting insects.
species of stinkhorn fungus

Examples

We found a cluster of Mutinus caninus growing in the damp forest floor yesterday.
Scientists studied the spore dispersal methods of Mutinus caninus to understand how insects contribute to its propagation.
The distinctive shape and foul odor of Mutinus caninus make it easy to identify among other fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinkhorn - a group of fungi known for their foul odor and phallic shape

Etymology

From Latin, 'Mutinus' (Roman god of fertility) and 'caninus' (of a dog), referring to its shape.
