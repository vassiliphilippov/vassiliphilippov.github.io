subversion

1. NOUN

/ səbˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of undermining someone's honesty, loyalty, or moral integrity
undermining honesty or loyalty

Examples

The scandal was seen as a subversion of public trust in government institutions.
He feared the subversion of his core values after moving to the city.
Some critics argue that the film depicts the subversion of innocence through exposure to urban life.

Synset Examples

corruption of a minor
the big city's subversion of rural innocence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct, often involving moral decay

Etymology

From Latin 'subvertere' meaning 'to overthrow'.

2. NOUN

/ səbˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of undermining or overthrowing an established authority, especially a government
undermining or overthrowing authority

Examples

The accused was charged with subversion for attempting to overthrow the government.
Through propaganda and secret meetings, they planned acts of subversion against the regime.
Fears of subversion led to stricter security measures in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurrection - an act of rebellion or uprising against authority
  • Synonym: sedition - conduct or speech inciting rebellion against authority
  • Antonym: loyalty - faithful allegiance to a government or leader

Etymology

From Late Latin subversio (overthrow), from Latin subvertere (to overturn).
