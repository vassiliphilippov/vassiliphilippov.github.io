bennet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪt / (BrE)
A North American plant of the avens genus, with white flowers, typically found in moist habitats.
North American white-flowered avens plant

Examples

We spotted a patch of bennet growing along the edge of the stream.
The delicate white blossoms of the bennet attract a variety of pollinators each spring.
Gardeners often appreciate bennet for its resilience and tolerance of damp soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - Any plant of the genus Geum, of which bennet is a species

Etymology

From Middle English 'benet', originally referring to the European avens, possibly from Medieval Latin 'herba benedicta' meaning 'blessed herb'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnət / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪt / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Geum, specifically an avens of Virginia with pale or greenish yellow flowers.
a plant of the geum (avens) genus

Examples

The shady woodland was filled with clusters of bennet growing along the path.
Gardeners sometimes plant bennet for its subtle greenish-yellow flowers and hardy nature.
While hiking, Ella identified several wildflowers, including bennet and wild violets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - any plant of the genus Geum, especially garden species

Etymology

From Old French benoit, from Latin benedictus (blessed), referring to the plant's reputed medicinal uses.
