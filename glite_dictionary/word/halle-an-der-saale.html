Halle-an-der-Saale

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑː.lə ˌɑn dər ˈzɑː.lə / (AmE), / ˈha.lə an də ˈzaː.lə / (BrE)
a city in the state of Saxony-Anhalt, central Germany, situated on the Saale River, historically a member of the Hanseatic League in the Middle Ages
city in eastern Germany on the Saale River

Examples

The historic city of Halle-an-der-Saale played a significant role in medieval trade routes.
Many renowned musicians have studied at the university in Halle-an-der-Saale.
During their trip to Germany, they visited Halle-an-der-Saale and explored its old town.

Etymology

From German Halle (the city's name) + an der Saale ('on the Saale' river), reflecting its location.
