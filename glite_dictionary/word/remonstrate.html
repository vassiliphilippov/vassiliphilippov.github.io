to remonstrate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmɑnstreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒnstreɪt / (BrE)
to protest or argue forcefully against something; to express strong objection or disapproval
to protest or object forcefully

Examples

Several employees remonstrated with the manager about the new overtime policy.
She remonstrated that the decision was unfair and demanded a reconsideration.
He remonstrated vigorously when accused of cheating during the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - to express objection or disapproval
  • Antonym: to agree - to share the same opinion; to accede

Etymology

From Latin remonstrari (to show, point out), from re- (again) + monstrare (to show).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈmɑːnstreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒnstreɪt / (BrE)
to express forceful criticism, protest, or objection, especially in a strong or angry manner
to protest or criticize strongly

Examples

He did not hesitate to remonstrate with the manager about the poor service he had received.
When she saw her brother break the vase, she felt compelled to remonstrate angrily.
The principal remonstrated with the students for being late to class so often.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to criticize angrily
  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin remonstrari (to show, demonstrate), from re- (again) + monstrare (to show).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈmɑnstreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒnstreɪt / (BrE)
to argue or plead in protest or opposition; to express forceful objection or disapproval
express strong objection or protest

Examples

The employees remonstrated with management over the sudden changes to their work schedules.
When she received the unfair penalty, she remonstrated that she had followed all the rules.
Several members of the audience remonstrated against the speaker's controversial remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - express objection or disapproval
  • Antonym: to agree - have the same opinion; not to oppose or protest

Etymology

From Latin remonstrari (to show, demonstrate), from re- (again) + monstrare (to show).

Synset POS: v
