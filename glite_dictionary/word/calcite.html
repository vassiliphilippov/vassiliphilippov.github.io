calcite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.saɪt / (BrE)
a common white or colorless mineral consisting of crystallized calcium carbonate, found in limestone and marble
crystallized calcium carbonate mineral

Examples

The cave walls were coated in sparkling calcite crystals that reflected her flashlight beam.
Limestone is primarily made up of calcite and can contain fossils of ancient sea creatures.
Geologists often study calcite to learn more about Earth's sedimentary processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aragonite - a mineral form of calcium carbonate, polymorphous with calcite
  • Synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcite

Etymology

From German 'Calcit', Latin 'calx' (lime).
