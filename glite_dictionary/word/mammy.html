mammy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæmi / (AmE), / ˈmæmi / (BrE)
an informal or dialect word for mother, chiefly used in some English-speaking regions
informal term for mother
informal

Examples

As a child, he always ran to his mammy when he was upset.
Her mammy made the best apple pie in the village.
Even now, she calls her mother 'mammy' out of affection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mommy - informal American English word for mother
  • Synonym: mum - British informal word for mother
  • Synonym: mother - a female parent

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæmi / (AmE), / ˈmæmi / (BrE)
An offensive term for a Black woman who worked as a nursemaid or nanny to white children in the southern United States, especially during the era of slavery and segregation.
offensive term for Black nursemaid in U.S. South
offensive american

Examples

The movie perpetuated the stereotype of the mammy, which is now widely criticized.
Historians discussed how the image of the mammy was used to justify racism in the South.
She wrote her thesis on the portrayal of the mammy figure in American literature.
