Quercus coccinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs kɑkˈsiniə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs kɒkˈsiːnɪə / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous oak tree native to eastern North America, known for its deeply lobed leaves that turn bright scarlet in autumn.
species of oak tree with scarlet autumn leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Quercus coccinea turn a brilliant red in autumn, making it a popular ornamental tree.
Foresters often plant Quercus coccinea to restore native woodlands in the eastern United States.
The wood of Quercus coccinea is close-grained and valued for furniture and flooring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet oak - common name for Quercus coccinea, a North American oak species

Etymology

From Latin: 'quercus' meaning 'oak', and 'coccinea' meaning 'scarlet-colored'.
