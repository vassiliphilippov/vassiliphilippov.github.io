chinchilla

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (BrE)
a small South American rodent with soft, dense pearly-grey fur, native to the Andes and often bred in captivity for its fur.
small Andean rodent with soft fur

Examples

Many people choose a chinchilla as a pet because of its soft, luxurious fur.
The chinchilla is native to the Andes Mountains in South America.
Fur coats made from chinchilla pelts are prized for their exceptional softness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea pig - a small domesticated rodent often kept as a pet
  • Degree Lower: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal with long ears, sometimes bred for fur

Etymology

From Spanish 'chinchilla', probably from a native Quechua word.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (BrE)
A thick, twilled fabric made of wool and cotton, often with a soft, napped surface resembling that of chinchilla fur.
a thick, soft wool-cotton fabric

Examples

The coat was made from a luxurious chinchilla that felt soft to the touch.
Designers often use chinchilla for winter garments because of its warmth and texture.
This antique blanket is woven from genuine chinchilla fabric.

Etymology

From the name of the chinchilla rodent, due to the fabric's resemblance to its fur.

3. NOUN

/ tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (BrE)
the soft, expensive, silvery-grey fur obtained from the chinchilla, often used to make coats and other garments
expensive silvery-grey fur of a chinchilla

Examples

She wore a luxurious chinchilla coat to the gala.
The designer's new collection features chinchilla trims on winter jackets.
Vintage chinchilla is highly prized for its extraordinary softness and color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur - the short, fine, soft hair of certain animals used for garments

Etymology

From Spanish 'chinchilla', diminutive of 'chinchin', from Quechua.
