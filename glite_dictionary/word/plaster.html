plaster

1. VERB

/ ˈplæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
to cover a surface thickly or excessively with a substance
cover thickly with a substance

Examples

She plastered her face with sunscreen before going out.
The walls were plastered with posters for the concert.
He plastered his bread with butter, hardly leaving any room for the jam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover with a layer of a substance

Etymology

From Old French *plastier*, from Latin *plastrum* (patch of plaster).

4. NOUN

/ ˈplæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
A mixture of lime or gypsum with sand and water that hardens to form a smooth solid surface, used for covering walls and ceilings.
building material for walls and ceilings

Examples

The workers applied plaster to the damaged wall to make it smooth again.
After the plaster dried, the ceiling was ready to be painted.
Old houses often have beautifully decorated plaster moldings on the walls and ceilings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stucco - a fine plaster used for coating wall surfaces or molding into architectural decorations

Etymology

From Old French *plastre*, from Latin *plastrum* (plaster, bandage), from Greek *emplastron* (salve, plaster).
