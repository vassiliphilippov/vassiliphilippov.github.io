know-nothing

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ ˌnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ ˌnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who lacks knowledge or is ignorant, often used as a term of derision
an ignorant or uninformed person

Examples

Some called him a know-nothing because he refused to learn the basics of his new job.
That politician is just a know-nothing spreading misinformation to the public.
The team quickly realized their new recruit was no know-nothing, but actually quite skilled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignoramus - a person who is ignorant or uneducated
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill

Etymology

From the phrase 'know nothing', originally used to describe members of the secretive 19th-century American political movement who claimed to know nothing about its activities.
