crevasse

1. NOUN

/ krəˈvæs / (AmE), / krəˈvɑːs / (BrE)
a deep, open crack or fissure, especially in a glacier or the earth's surface
deep crack, especially in ice or earth

Examples

The mountaineers carefully avoided the hidden crevasse as they made their way across the glacier.
A deep crevasse opened up after the earthquake, splitting the road in two.
He peered into the narrow crevasse, unsure of how deep it truly was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a long, narrow opening or break
  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or line of breakage

Etymology

From French 'crevasse' (crack, fissure), from Old French 'crever' (to break).
