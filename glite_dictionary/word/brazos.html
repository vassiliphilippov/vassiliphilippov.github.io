Brazos

1. PROPN

/ ˈbræzəs / (AmE), / ˈbræzɒs / (BrE)
a river in the southern United States that flows from New Mexico across Texas into the Gulf of Mexico
major river in Texas

Examples

The Brazos River is an important source of water for central Texas.
Several towns are situated along the Brazos, benefiting from its fertile valley.
The annual festival celebrates the history and wildlife of the Brazos region.

Etymology

From Spanish 'brazos' meaning 'arms', likely referencing branches or tributaries of the river.
