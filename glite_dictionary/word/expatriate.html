expatriate

1. VERB

/ ɛkˈspeɪtriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɛksˈpætrɪeɪt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave their native country; to banish or exile.
expel from one's native country

Examples

The government decided to expatriate the dissident artists for their political activities.
Following the coup, several leaders were expatriated and stripped of their citizenship.
The regime threatened to expatriate anyone who spoke out against its policies.

Synset Examples

The poet was exiled because he signed a letter protesting the government's actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - to force someone to leave and live outside their native country

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'expatriatus', past participle of 'expatriare', from Latin 'ex-' (out of) + 'patria' (native country).

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˈspeɪtri.eɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈspætrɪət / (BrE)
a person who lives outside their native country, often by choice
person living outside their native country

Examples

After moving to Paris, he joined a community of American expatriates.
Many expatriate workers return home during major holidays.
She felt both liberated and homesick as an expatriate in Japan.

Synset Examples

American expatriates

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country
  • Synonym: emigrant - a person who leaves their own country to settle permanently in another

Etymology

From Latin 'ex-' (out of) + 'patria' (native country).

3. VERB

/ ɛkˈspeɪ.tri.eɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈspæ.tri.eɪt / (BrE)
to leave one's native country to live in another country permanently or for an extended period
leave one's country to live abroad

Examples

After the political unrest, she decided to expatriate and start anew in Canada.
Many writers chose to expatriate during the war and live in Paris.
He expatriated from his homeland in search of better opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emigrate - to leave one's own country to settle in another
  • Antonym: repatriate - to return to one's home country

Etymology

From Medieval Latin expatriatus, past participle of expatriari 'to leave one's country', from Latin ex- 'out of' + patria 'native land'.

Synset POS: v
