alpha-naphthol

1. NOUN

/ ˌælfəˈnæfθɑl / (AmE), / ˌælfəˈnafθɒl / (BrE)
an isomer of naphthol, used especially in making dyes and as a chemical reagent
chemical compound used in dyes and reagents
medical

Examples

Alpha-naphthol is commonly used as a reagent to detect the presence of sugars in laboratory tests.
The production of azo dyes often requires alpha-naphthol as a starting material.
In biochemistry, alpha-naphthol plays a crucial role in the Molisch test for carbohydrates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beta-naphthol - the other isomer of naphthol used in dyes and chemistry

Etymology

From 'alpha-' (denoting the first) and 'naphthol', referring to its chemical structure as one of two isomers.
