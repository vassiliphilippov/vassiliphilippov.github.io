sugariness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɡəriˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈsjʊɡəriˌnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sugary; the sweetness like that of sugar
sweetness like that of sugar

Examples

The sugariness of the cake made it popular among children.
He complained about the sugariness of the soft drink, saying it was too sweet.
Some people prefer fruit desserts for their natural sugariness rather than processed sweets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetness - the quality of being sweet, especially in taste

Etymology

From 'sugary' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the quality of being sugary.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɡəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡərɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of tasting sweet, especially resembling the taste of sugar
the quality of tasting sweet

Examples

The sugariness of the dessert overwhelmed the subtle flavors of the fruit.
Some breakfast cereals are criticized for their excessive sugariness.
The drink's sugariness made it especially popular among children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetness - the quality of being sweet in taste
  • Antonym: bitterness - the quality of having a sharp, unpleasant taste

Etymology

From 'sugar' + '-iness', denoting the state or quality of being sugary.

Synset POS: n
