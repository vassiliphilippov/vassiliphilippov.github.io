to enfetter

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛtə / (BrE)
to restrict or bind someone or something, especially by limiting freedom, creativity, or aspirations
restrict or bind, limit freedom

Examples

The oppressive regime sought to enfetter the citizens' freedoms.
She felt the strict policies at work enfettered her creativity.
We must not let outdated traditions enfetter our progress as a society.

Synset Examples

The oppressive regime sought to enfetter the citizens' freedoms.
She felt the rules enfetter her creativity at work.
Don't let fear enfetter your dreams and ambitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetter - to restrain or shackle, to restrict freedom
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set free, to release someone from restriction

Etymology

From en- (cause to) + fetter (a chain or shackle for the feet); ultimately from Old English *feter*.
