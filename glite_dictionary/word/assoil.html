to assoil

1. VERB

/ əˈsɔɪl / (AmE), / əˈsɔɪl / (BrE)
(archaic) To declare someone not guilty of a criminal charge; to acquit.
declare not guilty; acquit (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the medieval court, the jury assoiled the defendant after hearing all the evidence.
It was a relief to finally be assoiled of the false accusations.
Though rarely used today, judges once had the power to assoil those brought before them.

Synset Examples

The suspect was cleared of the murder charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acquit - to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty
  • Antonym: convict - to declare someone to be guilty of a criminal offense

Etymology

From Old French 'asolver' (to absolve), from Latin 'absolvere' (to set free).
