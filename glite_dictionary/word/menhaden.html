menhaden

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈheɪdən / (AmE), / mɛnˈheɪdən / (BrE)
A shad-like North American marine fish, valued for its use in producing fish meal, oil, and fertilizer.
a North American fish used for meal and oil

Examples

The population of menhaden has declined due to overfishing in coastal waters.
Factories process menhaden to produce valuable fish oil and meal.
Many species of birds and larger fish feed on menhaden in the wild.

Etymology

From Algonquian (Narragansett) 'munnohquohteau', meaning 'he fertilizes', referencing its use as fertilizer.
