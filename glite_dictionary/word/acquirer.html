acquirer

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A bank or financial institution that processes credit or debit card payments on behalf of a merchant, receiving the payment less a processing fee.
bank that processes card payments for merchants

Examples

The merchant's acquirer settled the credit card payments every evening.
Many businesses compare rates before choosing an acquirer to handle their card transactions.
If an acquirer detects unusual activity, it may put a hold on the merchant's funds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: issuer - a bank that issues credit cards to cardholders

Etymology

From acquire + -er, meaning 'one who acquires'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkwaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a financial institution that processes credit or debit card payments and dispenses cash through ATMs, often collecting a fee from the card issuer
financial institution in card transactions

Examples

The customer's bank charged a fee because the acquirer was a different institution than the issuer.
Every time you use an ATM not owned by your bank, the acquirer may charge an additional fee.
Credit card networks coordinate transactions between the acquirer and the card issuer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: issuer - the bank or institution that issues a credit or debit card

Etymology

From acquire + -er, meaning 'one who acquires'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
a company that gains financial control over another company or financial institution, typically through payment or exchange of stock
company gaining control over another

Examples

The acquirer completed the purchase after months of negotiations.
Analysts watched closely as the acquirer announced its latest acquisition strategy.
The acquirer offered both cash and stock to secure the deal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquiree - a company or institution being acquired
  • Synonym: to acquire - to buy or obtain an asset or object for oneself

4. NOUN

/ əˈkwaɪərər / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪərə / (BrE)
a person or company that acquires or gains possession of something, especially by purchase or investment
person or entity that acquires something

Examples

The acquirer of the company must adhere to regulatory requirements.
In a merger, the acquirer often dictates the terms of the deal.
The museum became the new acquirer of the rare artwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buyer - a person who purchases something; purchaser
  • Antonym: seller - a person who sells something

Etymology

From 'acquire' + '-er' (agent noun suffix), ultimately from Latin 'acquirere' (to gain, acquire).
