St. Mark

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt mɑrk / (AmE), / seɪnt mɑːk / (BrE)
an apostle and companion of Saint Peter, traditionally regarded as the author of the second Gospel in the New Testament
apostle; traditionally author of the second Gospel

Examples

The Gospel according to St. Mark is the second book in the New Testament.
Many churches around the world are named after St. Mark.
Tradition holds that St. Mark traveled to Alexandria to spread Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mark - the second Gospel in the New Testament, attributed to St. Mark
