Pylodictus olivaris

1. NOUN

/ paɪləˈdɪktəs ɑːˈlɪvərɪs / (AmE), / paɪləˈdɪktəs ɒˈlɪvərɪs / (BrE)
a large catfish native to central United States, noted for its flattened head and protruding lower jaw; also known as the flathead catfish
large North American flathead catfish

Examples

The Pylodictus olivaris is valued by anglers for its large size and fighting ability.
Biologists studied the diet of Pylodictus olivaris in different river habitats.
The Pylodictus olivaris can weigh over 100 pounds in rare instances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flathead catfish - common English name for Pylodictus olivaris

Etymology

From Greek 'pylē' (gate) + 'diktus' (net) for genus; 'olivaris' from Latin, meaning olive-colored.
