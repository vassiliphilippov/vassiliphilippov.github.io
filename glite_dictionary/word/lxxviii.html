LXXVIII

1. NUM

/ ɛl ɛks ɛks viː aɪ aɪ aɪ / (AmE), / ɛl ɛks ɛks vɪ aɪ aɪ aɪ / (BrE)
The Roman numeral for seventy-eight (78).
Roman numeral for 78

Examples

The inscription read LXXVIII, signifying the year of the emperor's rule.
Chapter LXXVIII begins with a detailed description of the city.
The ancient coin was marked with the value LXXVIII on its reverse side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seventy-eight - the cardinal number 78

Etymology

Roman numerals, from Latin numbering system, L = 50, X = 10, V = 5, III = 3.
