Leptotyphlops

1. NOUN

/ lɛp.toʊ.tɪˈfloʊps / (AmE), / lɛp.təʊ.tɪˈflɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender blind snakes found in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
genus of blind snakes

Examples

Leptotyphlops are known for their burrowing habits and elusive nature.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Leptotyphlops genus in South America.
Unlike larger snakes, Leptotyphlops primarily feed on ants and termites.

Etymology

From Greek leptos (slender) + typhlos (blind) + -ops (appearance).
