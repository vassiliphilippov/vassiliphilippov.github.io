to metrify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to convert something from a non-metric system to the metric system.
convert to the metric system

Examples

After joining the European Union, the country decided to metrify all its road signs and measurements.
The school curriculum was updated to metrify science experiments from imperial to metric units.
Engineers worked to metrify the machinery specifications for international compatibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to metricate - to change to the metric system

Etymology

Formed from 'metric' plus the verb suffix '-ify'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00382002-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛtrɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to compose or arrange (words, lines, etc.) in metrical form; to write in poetic meter
write or arrange in poetic meter

Examples

She attempted to metrify her essay, turning it into a charming poem.
The translation carefully metrifies the original lines to preserve the rhythm.
It is not easy to metrify technical language without losing the meaning.

Synset Examples

The bard metrified his poems very precisely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to versify - to turn into verse or compose in verse

Etymology

From Greek metron (measure) + -ify (to make).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
