Marches

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃɪz / (BrE)
a region of central Italy, known for its rolling hills and historic towns, with capital city Ancona.
region of central Italy (capital: Ancona)

Examples

The Marches are famous for their beautiful landscapes and medieval villages.
She spent the summer traveling through the Marches and sampling local cuisine.
Many poets and artists have found inspiration in the serene scenery of the Marches.

Etymology

From Italian 'Marche', the plural of 'marca' meaning 'march' (borderland).
