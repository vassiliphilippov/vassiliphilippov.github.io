Acrididae

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪdɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈkrɪdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The family of short-horned grasshoppers, including several species known as true locusts.
family of short-horned grasshoppers

Examples

The family Acrididae includes some of the most destructive locust species.
Scientists study Acrididae to understand locust swarming behavior.
Many agricultural pests are found within the Acrididae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locust - a large grasshopper that sometimes migrates in large swarms, causing damage to crops

Etymology

From Modern Latin Acrididae, based on Latin acris (grasshopper).

2. NOUN

/ ˌækrɪˈdaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌakrɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
A large family of grasshoppers known as the short-horned grasshoppers or true locusts, including many species of agricultural importance.
family of short-horned grasshoppers

Examples

The Acrididae family includes many species that are important pests of crops worldwide.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of various Acrididae in the grasslands.
Locust swarms, which can devastate agriculture, are members of the Acrididae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - an insect of the suborder Caelifera, related to the Acrididae family
  • Synonym: locust - a grasshopper species that forms swarms; member of the Acrididae family

Etymology

From New Latin Acrididae, from the type genus *Acrida*, itself from Greek *akris* (locust).
