ZDV

1. NOUN

/ zi di ˈvi / (AmE), / ziː diː ˈviː / (BrE)
an antiviral drug, also known as zidovudine or Retrovir, used in the treatment of HIV/AIDS
antiviral drug for HIV/AIDS treatment
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed ZDV as part of his antiretroviral therapy regimen.
Researchers are studying the long-term effects of ZDV on liver function.
Early use of ZDV has been shown to reduce the risk of HIV transmission from mother to child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zidovudine - antiviral drug used to treat HIV/AIDS
  • Synonym: Retrovir - trade name for zidovudine

Etymology

Acronym of zidovudine, derived from 'azidothymidine'.
