crusader

1. NOUN

/ kruˈseɪdər / (AmE), / kruːˈseɪdə / (BrE)
a warrior who participated in the medieval Crusades, fighting in a holy war, especially to reclaim the Holy Land from Muslims
medieval holy war warrior

Examples

The crusader rode across Europe to join the fight for Jerusalem.
Many crusaders took vows and wore crosses on their chests.
During the First Crusade, thousands of crusaders marched east in the name of Christianity.

Synset Examples

the Crusaders tried to recapture the Holy Land from the Muslims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paladin - a leading champion of a cause, especially one of the twelve peers of Charlemagne's court or a heroic knight

Etymology

From 'crusade', from French 'croisade', from Latin 'crux' (cross), referencing the cross worn by participants.

2. NOUN

/ kruˈseɪdər / (AmE), / kruˈseɪdə / (BrE)
a person who campaigns vigorously for political, social, or moral reform
a reform campaigner

Examples

She has been a leading crusader for environmental causes for over a decade.
As a crusader for justice, he never backed down from a challenge.
The magazine was founded by a crusader determined to expose corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reformer - a person who works to change and improve something, especially in society or politics
  • Antonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values

Etymology

From crusade + -er, originally referring to participants in medieval Crusades, later used figuratively.
