sniper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnaɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnaɪpə / (BrE)
a person who shoots at people from a hidden position, especially with a rifle, usually as a military or police marksman
a concealed marksman or shooter

Examples

The sniper waited patiently on the rooftop for the target to appear.
A skilled sniper can have a significant impact on a battlefield.
Police officers trained as snipers can neutralize threats from a distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpshooter - a person who is highly skilled at shooting a gun accurately
  • Synonym: marksman - a person skilled in shooting, especially with a pistol or rifle

Etymology

From 'snipe' (a type of bird considered hard to shoot) + '-er', originally meaning one skilled at shooting snipes.
