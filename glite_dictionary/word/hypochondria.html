hypochondria

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pəˈkɑn.dri.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈkɒn.dri.ə / (BrE)
excessive and persistent anxiety about having a serious illness, often without medical evidence
excessive anxiety about illness

Examples

Her constant worrying about minor aches was a sign of hypochondria.
He visited several doctors, but his hypochondria prevented him from believing any reassurance.
Books on hypochondria often advise seeking psychological treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypochondriasis - medical term for hypochondria, excessive preoccupation with illness
  • Synonym: health anxiety - persistent worry about health, sometimes used synonymously

Etymology

From Greek hypokhondria (region below the ribs), historically linked to supposed seat of melancholy.
