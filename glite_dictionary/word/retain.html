to retain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈteɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪn / (BrE)
to keep something in your memory; to remember or not forget information
to keep in memory

Examples

Despite reading the article twice, I struggled to retain all the facts.
She has an incredible ability to retain complex scientific concepts.
Many students find it challenging to retain vocabulary from a foreign language course.

Synset Examples

I cannot retain so much information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remember - to have in or be able to bring to one's mind an awareness of
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From Latin retinere (to hold back), from re- (back) + tenere (to hold).
