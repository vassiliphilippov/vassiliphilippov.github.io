Penstemon deustus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstəˌmɒn diˈuːstəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstɪmən dɪˈjuːstəs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to western North America, with clump-forming stems and cream-colored flowers, found from Washington to Utah and California.
species of perennial plant with cream flowers

Examples

Penstemon deustus thrives in dry, rocky soils and is favored in native plant gardens.
The cream-colored blooms of Penstemon deustus attract many species of pollinators throughout the summer.
Botanists discovered a new population of Penstemon deustus in northern California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beardtongue - Common name for plants of the genus Penstemon

Etymology

From the genus name Penstemon (American English variant of Latinized Greek pent- ‘five’ + stēmōn ‘thread, stamen’) and the species epithet deustus (Latin ‘burnt’).
