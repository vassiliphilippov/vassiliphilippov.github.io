oecumenical

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːkjəˈmɛnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkjʊˈmɛnɪkəl / (BrE)
concerned with promoting or fostering unity among different churches or religions, especially within the Christian faith
promoting unity among churches or religions

Examples

The oecumenical council was attended by leaders from many different Christian denominations.
She has a strong interest in oecumenical dialogue and interfaith activities.
An oecumenical approach can help bridge differences between religious communities.

Synset Examples

ecumenical thinking
ecumenical activities
the ecumenical movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenical - concerned with promoting unity among churches or religions (variant spelling)

Etymology

From Greek oikoumenikos (of the inhabited world), from oikoumenē (the inhabited earth).

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːkjəˈmɛnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkjʊˈmɛnɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or representing the whole world; universal or of worldwide scope.
universal; worldwide in scope

Examples

The conference aimed to address oecumenical concerns that affect countries across the globe.
Scholars gathered for an oecumenical discussion on global education reforms.
The oecumenical nature of the agreement was praised by leaders from many nations.

Synset Examples

an issue of cosmopolitan import
the shrewdest political and ecumenical comment of our time
universal experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenical - of worldwide scope or applicability; more common spelling
  • Synonym: universal - applying to or common to all members of a group or set

Etymology

From Greek *oikoumenikos* (from *oikoumenē* 'the inhabited world').
