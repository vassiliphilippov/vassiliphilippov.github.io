enervation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a nerve
surgical removal of a nerve
medical

Examples

The surgeon opted for enervation to relieve the patient's chronic pain.
In veterinary medicine, enervation may be performed to improve mobility.
After the enervation, the patient experienced numbness in the affected area.

Etymology

From Latin 'enervatio', from 'enervare' meaning 'to weaken, to remove sinews or nerves'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛnərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of being weakened and having lost energy; extreme fatigue or lack of vitality.
serious weakening and loss of energy

Examples

The patient's enervation was evident after weeks of illness.
Months of relentless stress led to a feeling of enervation that he found hard to shake.
Some critics blamed the team's late-game enervation for their unexpected defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - the state of lacking strength
  • Synonym: exhaustion - extreme physical or mental fatigue
  • Antonym: invigoration - the act of giving strength or energy

Etymology

From Latin enervatio (weakening, enfeebling), from enervare (to weaken).
