Matricaria oreades

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætrɪˈkɛəriə oʊˈriːədiːz / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈkɛːrɪə əʊˈriːədiːz / (BrE)
A mat-forming perennial herb native to Asia Minor, sometimes classified within the genus Matricaria.
mat-forming herb of Asia Minor

Examples

Matricaria oreades is known for its dense, low-growing foliage that makes it suitable for ground cover.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Matricaria oreades belongs in the genus Matricaria or deserves separate classification.
Gardeners appreciate Matricaria oreades for its resilience and ability to thrive in rocky soils.

Etymology

From Latin 'Matricaria' (a genus of aromatic flowering plants) and 'oreades' (from Greek mythology, nymphs of mountains).
