banality

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnæləti / (AmE), / bəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
A remark or idea that is so lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring; the fact or quality of being banal.
a trite or obvious remark

Examples

The conversation was full of banality and small talk, lacking any real substance.
She tired quickly of the banality of his jokes, which were all too predictable.
Critics accused the film of banality, saying it offered nothing new or thought-provoking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
  • Antonym: originality - the ability to think independently and creatively; the quality of being novel

Etymology

From French banalité, from banal (commonplace).
