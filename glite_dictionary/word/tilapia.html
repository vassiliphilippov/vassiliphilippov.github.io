tilapia

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈlɑːpiə / (AmE), / tɪˈleɪpiə / (BrE)
Any of several species of freshwater fish of the family Cichlidae, native to Africa and widely farmed for food.
type of freshwater fish

Examples

We ordered grilled tilapia with a spicy sauce for dinner.
Tilapia are popular in aquaculture due to their rapid growth and adaptability.
The lake is well known for its abundant tilapia population.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Modern Latin Tilapia (genus name), from Tswana word 'thiape', meaning fish.
