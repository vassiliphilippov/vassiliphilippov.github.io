nodding

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɒdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of branches, flowers, or similar parts) bending or drooping downward.
bending or drooping downward

Examples

The garden was filled with beautiful nodding daffodils in the spring breeze.
We admired the nodding branches of the willow as they touched the pond's surface.
Many wildflowers have nodding heads that sway gently in the wind.

Synset Examples

nodding daffodils
the pendulous branches of a weeping willow
lilacs with drooping panicles of fragrant flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drooping - bending or hanging downward loosely
  • Antonym: erect - upright, not bending or drooping

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'nod' + '-ing'; used in botanical and descriptive contexts.
