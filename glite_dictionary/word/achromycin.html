Achromycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.rəˈmaɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌæk.rəʊˈmaɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked antibiotic, derived from Streptomyces bacteria and used to treat a wide range of infections.
trademarked antibiotic medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Achromycin to help clear the bacterial infection.
Some patients may experience side effects while taking Achromycin.
Resistance to Achromycin has become a concern in recent years.
