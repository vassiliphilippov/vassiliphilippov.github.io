to scend

1. VERB

/ sɛnd / (AmE), / sɛnd / (BrE)
(of a boat or object) to rise or lift upward, especially under the influence of a wave or swell.
rise or heave upward (esp. of a boat)

Examples

The lifeboat began to scend with each rolling wave.
As the storm intensified, the vessel would scend sharply before crashing back down.
They watched the barrels scend on the crests of swelling seas.

Synset Examples

the boats surged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward, often used of the sea

Etymology

From obsolete 'send', altered by association with 'ascend' and nautical usage.
