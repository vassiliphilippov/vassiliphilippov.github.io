trioxide

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɑksˌaɪd / (AmE), / trʌɪˈɒksʌɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound consisting of an element or radical combined with three atoms of oxygen
oxide with three oxygen atoms
medical

Examples

Sulfur trioxide is an important intermediate in the production of sulfuric acid.
Exposure to arsenic trioxide can pose significant health risks.
The reaction produced a white powdery trioxide as a byproduct.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dioxide - an oxide containing two atoms of oxygen

Etymology

From tri- (three) + oxide (compound with oxygen), formed by analogy with other chemical terms.
