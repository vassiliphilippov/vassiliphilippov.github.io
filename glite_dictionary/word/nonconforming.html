nonconforming

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.kənˈfɔr.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kənˈfɔː.mɪŋ / (BrE)
not conforming to established customs, rules, or doctrines, especially in religion
not conforming to established customs or doctrines

Examples

The nonconforming church members held separate services outside the established church.
Her nonconforming ideas challenged the traditional beliefs of her community.
Many artists are celebrated for their nonconforming attitudes toward social conventions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conforming - adhering to established customs or doctrines
  • Synonym: unorthodox - not conforming to traditional beliefs or practices

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) and 'conforming'; 'conform' from Latin 'conformare' (to form, fashion).
