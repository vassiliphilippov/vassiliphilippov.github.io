Weltschmerz

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛltʃˌmɛrts / (AmE), / ˈvɛltʃmɛts / (BrE)
a feeling of deep sadness or melancholy caused by the perceived evils and suffering in the world
world-weariness; profound sadness about the world

Examples

Reading the news each morning often fills her with a sense of Weltschmerz.
The novel captures the Weltschmerz experienced by its protagonist as he confronts modern society.
During periods of war, many people are overcome by a feeling of Weltschmerz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or gloom
  • Synonym: existentialism - a philosophical approach emphasizing individual existence in an indifferent world

Etymology

From German Weltschmerz, literally 'world pain' (Welt 'world' + Schmerz 'pain').
