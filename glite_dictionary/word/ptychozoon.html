Ptychozoon

1. NOUN

/ ˌtaɪkoʊˈzoʊ.ɒn / (AmE), / ˌtɪkəʊˈzəʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
a genus of geckos known as flying geckos, characterized by flaps of skin that enable them to glide between trees
genus of flying geckos

Examples

The Ptychozoon can glide from tree to tree using its unique skin flaps.
Researchers are studying the camouflage abilities of the Ptychozoon in its natural habitat.
The Ptychozoon is often called the flying gecko because of its remarkable gliding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gecko - small lizard of the family Gekkonidae

Etymology

From Greek ptycho- (fold) and zoon (animal), referring to the skin flaps of these geckos.

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈkoʊzoʊˌɒn / (AmE), / tɪˈkɒzəʊˌɒn / (BrE)
A genus of arboreal geckos known as flying geckos, native to Southeast Asia, noted for their ability to glide using skin flaps.
genus of flying geckos

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species in the Ptychozoon genus.
The Ptychozoon is renowned for its gliding ability in the forests of Southeast Asia.
Members of Ptychozoon have skin flaps that help them glide between trees.

Etymology

From Greek ptycho- (fold) + zoon (animal).
