Mount Everest

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈɛvɚɪst, ˈɛvrəst / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈɛvərɪst, ˈɛvrɪst / (BrE)
the world's highest mountain, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China, with an elevation of 8,848 metres (29,029 feet)
the world's highest mountain

Examples

Many climbers attempt to reach the summit of Mount Everest every year.
The snowstorms on Mount Everest can be life-threatening for even experienced climbers.
She trained for years before making her successful ascent of Mount Everest.

Synset Examples

The view from Mount Everest is breathtaking.
Climbing Mount Everest is a dream for many adventurers.

Etymology

Named after Sir George Everest, British surveyor-general of India.
