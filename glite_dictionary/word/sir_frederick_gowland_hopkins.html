Sir Frederick Gowland Hopkins

1. PROPN

/ sɚ ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈɡaʊlənd ˈhɑpkɪns / (AmE), / sə ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈɡaʊlənd ˈhɒpkɪnz / (BrE)
(1861–1947) An English biochemist known for pioneering research that led to the discovery of vitamins.
English biochemist, discovered vitamins

Examples

Sir Frederick Gowland Hopkins was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1929.
Many textbooks highlight the impact of Sir Frederick Gowland Hopkins on modern nutrition science.
The discovery of vitamins by Sir Frederick Gowland Hopkins revolutionized how we understand essential nutrients.
