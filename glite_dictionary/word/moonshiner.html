moonshiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌʃaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmuːnʃaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who makes or sells alcoholic liquor illegally, especially in secret and without government permission
illegal liquor maker or seller

Examples

The old cabin was once used by a moonshiner to distill whiskey deep in the woods.
During Prohibition, many moonshiners risked arrest to sell homemade liquor.
The novel tells the story of a young moonshiner trying to escape the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bootlegger - someone who illegally makes, sells, or transports alcoholic drink

Etymology

From 'moonshine' (illicit liquor), referring to the practice of making liquor at night by moonlight to avoid detection, with agent suffix '-er'.
