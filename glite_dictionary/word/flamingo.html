flamingo

1. NOUN

/ fləˈmɪŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / fləˈmɪŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A large, tall wading bird with mainly pink or scarlet plumage, long legs, a long neck, and a down-curved bill, typically found in brackish or saltwater lakes.
large pink wading bird

Examples

A group of flamingos stood gracefully in the shallow lake, their bright feathers reflected in the water.
The zoo recently welcomed a flock of Caribbean flamingos to their aviary exhibit.
Photographers traveled to the wetlands specifically to capture images of flamingos during mating season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - a large bird with long legs and neck, lives near water
  • Synonym: stork - a large wading bird with long legs and bill

Etymology

From Spanish 'flamenco', from Provençal 'flamenc', possibly from 'flama' meaning 'flame', referring to its bright color.
