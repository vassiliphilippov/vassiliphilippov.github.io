prior

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪər / (AmE), / ˈpraɪə / (BrE)
existing or happening before something else; earlier in time.
earlier in time

Examples

She had no prior experience working in a hospital.
Any prior arrangements must be reported to the manager.
Attendance is mandatory unless you have prior approval to be absent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: previous - existing or occurring before in time or order
  • Antonym: subsequent - coming after something in time; following

Etymology

From Latin 'prior' (former, previous, superior).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪə / (BrE)
the male head of a religious house, especially in monastic orders, ranking just below an abbot
male head of a religious house

Examples

The prior led the monks in their daily prayers and activities.
After the death of the abbot, the prior took charge of the monastery.
As prior, he was responsible for maintaining discipline within the order.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: abbot - the male head of an abbey of monks
  • Synonym: prioress - the female head of a convent or priory

Etymology

From Latin prior (superior, former, earlier).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
