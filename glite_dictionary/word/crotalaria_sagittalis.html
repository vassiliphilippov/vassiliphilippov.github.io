Crotalaria sagittalis

1. NOUN

/ kroʊtəˈlɛriə səˌdʒɪˈtælɪs / (AmE), / krəʊtəˈlɑːriə səˌdʒɪˈtælɪs / (BrE)
A tropical American annual herb with an inflated pod that produces a rattling sound when the ripe seeds move inside.
tropical American plant with rattling seed pods

Examples

The fields were dotted with Crotalaria sagittalis, their pods rattling softly in the breeze.
Farmers sometimes cultivate Crotalaria sagittalis as a cover crop to enrich the soil.
The dried pods of Crotalaria sagittalis are often collected by children for their distinctive rattling sound.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Crotalaria', from Greek krotalon (castanet) + species name 'sagittalis', from Latin sagitta (arrow), referring to the shape of the leaves.
