Cotinus obovatus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtaɪnəs oʊˈboʊvətəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈtaɪnəs əʊˈbɒvətəs / (BrE)
A shrubby tree native to the southern United States, known for its large, feathery plumes of flowers that resemble puffs of smoke.
North American smoke tree species

Examples

The garden features a beautiful Cotinus obovatus with striking, smoke-like flowers in late spring.
They planted a Cotinus obovatus to add unique texture and color to the landscape.
In autumn, the leaves of Cotinus obovatus turn brilliant shades of red and orange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke tree - a common name for several plants with plume-like flowers, including Cotinus obovatus

Etymology

From the genus name Cotinus, and Latin obovatus meaning 'inversely egg-shaped', referring to the leaf shape.
