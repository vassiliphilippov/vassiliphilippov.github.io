mountebank

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntɪˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪˌbaŋk / (BrE)
a person who deceives others, especially by pretending to have skills or knowledge, often while attracting attention through tricks or showmanship
a trickster or deceiver, often flamboyant

Examples

The crowd was captivated as the mountebank performed his latest round of tricks and offered miracle cures.
In the novel, the villain is a classic mountebank who swindles innocent villagers out of their money.
Many were fooled by the mountebank's confident manner and extravagant promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlatan - a person falsely claiming to have special knowledge or skill; a fraud
  • Synonym: quack - a person who dishonestly claims to have knowledge in medicine; a fraud
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who is truthful and trustworthy

Etymology

From Italian 'montimbanco', literally 'one who climbs on benches', referring to people who stood on benches to perform tricks and sell quack medicines.
