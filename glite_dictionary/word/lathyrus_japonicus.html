Lathyrus japonicus

1. PROPN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs dʒəˈpoʊnɪkəs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪrəs dʒəˈpɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of wild pea found on seashores in the north temperate zone, with tough roots and purple flowers, useful for stabilizing sand dunes
wild pea plant of northern seashores

Examples

Lathyrus japonicus is commonly found along sandy shores and helps stabilize the dunes.
The purple flowers of Lathyrus japonicus make it an attractive addition to coastal ecosystems.
Botanists study Lathyrus japonicus for its unique adaptation to salty, wind-blown environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Lathyrus and japonicus, the Latin for 'Japanese'.
