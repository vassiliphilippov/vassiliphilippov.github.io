bristly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪsli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsli / (BrE)
easily annoyed or inclined to show irritation; quick to take offense.
easily irritated; quick to take offense

Examples

Her bristly responses made it clear she was in no mood for small talk.
The manager became bristly when questioned about the delays.
After the criticism, his mood turned noticeably bristly.

Synset Examples

bristly exchanges between the White House and the press
he became prickly and spiteful
witty and waspish about his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly - easily irritated or offended
  • Synonym: waspish - readily expressing irritation or anger
  • Antonym: good-natured - having a pleasant or friendly disposition

Etymology

From 'bristle' (stiff hair) + -y, originally referring to something covered in bristles, later used figuratively for temperament.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪsli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsli / (BrE)
having or covered with short, stiff hairs, spines, or similar projections
covered with stiff hairs or spines

Examples

The hedgehog's back felt bristly when she tried to pet it.
After a week without shaving, his beard became thick and bristly.
We walked through the bristly undergrowth, careful not to scratch our legs.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - covered with spines or thorns
  • Degree Lower: hairy - covered with hair
  • Synonym: spiky - having sharp points or spikes

Etymology

From 'bristle' + '-y'; 'bristle' from Old English 'briostel' (stiff hair or spine).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
