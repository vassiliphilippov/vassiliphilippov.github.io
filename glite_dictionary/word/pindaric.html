Pindaric

1. NOUN

/ pɪnˈdærɪk / (AmE), / pɪnˈdarɪk / (BrE)
An ode form characterized by groups of three stanzas, following the style used by the ancient Greek poet Pindar.
ode form in style of Pindar

Examples

John Dryden was renowned for his use of the Pindaric in English poetry.
The Pindaric is complex, with a structure that challenges many translators.
She devoted her thesis to the evolution of the Pindaric in Renaissance literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ode - a lyric poem with elaborate structure

Etymology

From the name of Pindar, a 5th-century BC Greek lyric poet.
