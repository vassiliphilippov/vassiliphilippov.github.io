scaup

1. NOUN

/ skɑp / (AmE), / skɔːp / (BrE)
A diving duck found in North America and Eurasia, known for its bluish-grey bill; especially either of two species, the greater scaup and the lesser scaup.
a type of diving duck

Examples

We spotted a flock of scaup swimming near the shore during our morning walk.
The male scaup can be recognized by its distinctive bluish bill and dark head.
Biologists studied the migration patterns of the scaup across the continent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet

Etymology

Probably from Scots 'scalp', meaning a bed of shellfish, which scaups often feed on.
