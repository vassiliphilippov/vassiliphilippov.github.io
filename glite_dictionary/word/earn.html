to earn

1. VERB

/ ɝːn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
to receive or deserve something as a result of one's own actions, effort, or behavior
receive or deserve through effort

Examples

She worked hard to earn her promotion within the company.
His honest actions earned him the respect of his peers.
If you want trust, you must earn it through your behavior.

Synset Examples

its beauty won Paris the name ‘City of Lights’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deserve - to be worthy of something, especially as a reward or punishment
  • Synonym: win - to get or achieve something as a result of effort or competition
  • Antonym: forfeit - to lose or be deprived of something as a penalty

Etymology

From Old English *earnian* (to deserve, merit, earn).

2. VERB

/ ɝn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
to receive money as payment for work, services, or as profit from an investment or business
receive money for work or business

Examples

She hopes to earn enough this year to take a long vacation.
He began to earn a steady income after getting his new job.
Their bakery started to earn more money after they launched online sales.

Synset Examples

How much do you make a month in your new job?
She earns a lot in her new job
this merger brought in lots of money
He clears $5,000 each month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make - to receive money as payment, especially as wages or profit
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to keep or earn money, especially in business or investments

Etymology

From Old English *earnian* (deserve, earn), related to *ar* (work, labor).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
