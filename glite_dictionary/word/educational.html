educational

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛdʒʊˈkeɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃənl̩ / (BrE)
relating to the process of education or to the provision of knowledge, learning, or instruction.
relating to education or learning

Examples

The museum offers many educational programs for children and families.
Watching documentaries can be both entertaining and educational.
She is involved in educational research to improve teaching methods.

Synset Examples

educational psychology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic - relating to education or scholarship
  • Antonym: recreational - relating to activities for enjoyment rather than education

Etymology

From Latin 'educatio' (a bringing up, rearing) via French 'éducation'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛdʒʊˈkeɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃənl̩ / (BrE)
providing knowledge, instruction, or information; intended or serving to educate
serving to provide knowledge or instruction

Examples

The museum offers many educational programs for children.
Watching educational videos can help you learn a new language.
This book is both entertaining and educational for young readers.

Synset Examples

an educational film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructive - providing useful or educational information
  • Antonym: entertaining - providing amusement or enjoyment

Etymology

From education + -al, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01327517-s
Synset POS: s
