Junin virus

1. NOUN

/ huˈnin vaɪrəs / (AmE), / huːˈniːn ˈvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
an RNA virus that causes Argentine hemorrhagic fever, transmitted to humans by rats and mice
virus causing Argentine hemorrhagic fever
medical

Examples

The Junin virus is responsible for outbreaks of Argentine hemorrhagic fever in rural Argentina.
Scientists are researching vaccines to protect agricultural workers from the Junin virus.
Mice infested grain storage areas can contribute to the spread of the Junin virus among humans.

Etymology

Named after Junín Province, Argentina, where the virus was first identified.
