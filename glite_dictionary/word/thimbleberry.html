thimbleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪmbəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈθɪmbəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
A North American shrub in the raspberry family, with large, soft, thimble-shaped red or orange fruits and showy pink to purple flowers.
North American shrub with thimble-shaped fruit

Examples

The thimbleberry bush in her garden produced bright red fruit each summer.
Hikers stopped to pick thimbleberry along the forest trail.
The jam was made using freshly picked thimbleberry harvested in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a small, soft red or black fruit; the plant producing this fruit

Etymology

Formed from 'thimble' and 'berry', referring to the fruit's shape.

3. NOUN

/ ˈθɪmbəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈθɪmb(ə)lˌbɛri / (BrE)
A North American shrub in the raspberry family, with white flowers and soft, thimble-shaped orange or red berries.
North American shrub with edible orange berries

Examples

We found wild thimbleberries growing along the hiking trail in Oregon.
The tart flavor of the thimbleberry makes it perfect for homemade jams and desserts.
After lunch, we picked a basket of ripe thimbleberries near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a soft red edible fruit of a bush in the rose family

Etymology

Formed from 'thimble' (for its shape) and 'berry'.
