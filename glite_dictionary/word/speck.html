speck

1. NOUN

/ spɛk / (AmE), / spɛk / (BrE)
a very small amount of something, barely noticeable but still present
a tiny amount; a small particle

Examples

There wasn't a speck of dust to be found on the shelf.
Add a speck of cinnamon to give the dessert extra flavor.
She felt a speck of doubt creep into her mind.

Synset Examples

this dish could use a touch of garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small quantity detectable but barely so
  • Synonym: dash - a small quantity especially of an ingredient
  • Synonym: touch - a small but perceptible amount

Etymology

From Middle English spekke, from Old English specca (spot, stain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13796315-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ spɛk / (AmE), / spɛk / (BrE)
to mark with small spots or specks; to cover or sprinkle with tiny dots or particles
mark with small spots

Examples

Tiny drops of paint specked the surface of the old table.
After the rain, mud specked his clean white pants.
Sunlight specks the forest floor as it filters through the leaves.

Synset Examples

speck the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small or round marks
  • Synonym: stain - to mark or discolor with unwanted spots

Etymology

From Middle English specke, from Old English specca (spot, stain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01275970-v
Synset POS: v
