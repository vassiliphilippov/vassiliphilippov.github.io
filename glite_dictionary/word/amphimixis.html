amphimixis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪˈmɪksɪs / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈmɪksɪs / (BrE)
the process of sexual reproduction in which a male and a female gamete fuse to form a new organism
sexual reproduction via gamete fusion
medical

Examples

Amphimixis is the typical mode of reproduction in most animals and flowering plants.
Scientists study amphimixis to better understand genetic diversity among offspring.
Unlike parthenogenesis, amphimixis requires the contribution of both a male and a female gamete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilization - the union of male and female gametes to form a zygote
  • Antonym: parthenogenesis - reproduction without fertilization by a male gamete

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi' (both) + 'mixis' (mixing, mingling).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fəˈmɪk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈmɪk.sɪs / (BrE)
the fusion of male and female gametes (sperm and egg) during sexual reproduction, resulting in the formation of a zygote
fusion of sperm and egg in reproduction
medical

Examples

The process of amphimixis ensures genetic diversity within a species.
In sexual reproduction, amphimixis occurs when the nuclei of sperm and egg merge.
Scientists study amphimixis to understand inheritance and variation in offspring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilization - the process of a sperm cell joining with an egg cell

Etymology

From Greek amphi- (both) + mixis (mixing, mingling).
