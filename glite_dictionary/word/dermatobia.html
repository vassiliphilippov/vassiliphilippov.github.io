Dermatobia

1. NOUN

/ dɜr.məˈtoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / dɜː.məˈtəʊ.bɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of large parasitic flies whose larvae develop under the skin of mammals, including humans
genus of parasitic flies
medical

Examples

The Dermatobia fly is notorious for infesting cattle in tropical regions.
Humans can become accidental hosts for Dermatobia larvae, leading to painful skin lesions.
Veterinarians often encounter Dermatobia infections in domestic animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a common name for flies of the family Oestridae, including Dermatobia

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) + bios (life), referring to the fly's life cycle under the skin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɜːrməˈtoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌdɜːməˈtəʊbiə / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic flies whose larvae live under the skin of domestic mammals and humans, most notably Dermatobia hominis (the human botfly)
genus of parasitic flies

Examples

The larvae of Dermatobia can cause painful swellings in both livestock and humans.
Dermatobia is most notorious for its species Dermatobia hominis, also known as the human botfly.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Dermatobia to find better methods of controlling infestations.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'bios' (life), referring to its larvae living in skin.
