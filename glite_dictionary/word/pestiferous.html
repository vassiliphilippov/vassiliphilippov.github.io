pestiferous

1. ADJ

/ pɛˈstɪfərəs / (AmE), / pɛˈstɪfərəs / (BrE)
Causing irritation, annoyance, or trouble; troublesome or vexatious.
causing irritation or annoyance

Examples

That pestiferous mosquito kept me awake all night.
The pestiferous noise from construction made it impossible to concentrate.
He regarded bureaucracy as a pestiferous burden slowing progress.

2. ADJ

/ pɛˈstɪfərəs / (AmE), / pɛˈstɪfərəs / (BrE)
Likely to spread and cause an epidemic disease; capable of transmitting infection.
causing or spreading disease

Examples

The swamp was filled with pestiferous mosquitoes, putting the villagers at risk of illness.
Doctors warned about the pestiferous air during the outbreak, urging residents to take precautions.
The abandoned house became a pestiferous breeding ground for disease-carrying rodents.

Synset Examples

a pestilential malignancy in the air
plaguey fevers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pestilential - causing or likely to cause disease, especially plague
  • Antonym: wholesome - promoting health or well-being

Etymology

From Latin pestifer (bringing plague), from pestis (plague) + ferre (to bring).

archaic

Examples
