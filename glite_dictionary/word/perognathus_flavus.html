Perognathus flavus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛroʊɡˈneɪθəs ˈfleɪvəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəɡˈneɪθəs ˈfleɪvəs / (BrE)
a small, pale yellowish soft-furred rodent found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, also known as the silky pocket mouse
a species of soft-furred rodent in North America

Examples

The Perognathus flavus is well adapted to arid environments in the southwestern U.S.
Researchers set traps to study Perognathus flavus populations in the desert.
The fur of Perognathus flavus is soft and pale yellow, helping it blend into sandy habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky pocket mouse - common name for Perognathus flavus

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Perognathus from Greek 'perōn' (pouch) + 'gnathos' (jaw), and Latin 'flavus' (yellow).
