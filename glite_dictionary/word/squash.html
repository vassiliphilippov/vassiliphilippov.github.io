squash

1. NOUN

/ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / skwɒʃ / (BrE)
A game played in an enclosed court by two or four players who hit a small rubber ball against the walls using rackets.
racket sport played in an enclosed court

Examples

He plays squash every Tuesday evening at the local sports center.
The squash match was fast-paced and exciting to watch.
She joined a squash club to improve her fitness and meet new people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racquetball - a similar indoor racket game but with different rules and equipment

Etymology

Shortened from 'squash rackets', referring to the ball's tendency to squash on impact.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / skwɒʃ / (BrE)
to press or crush something with force so that it becomes flat, soft, or loses its normal shape
compress or crush forcefully

Examples

He accidentally squashed the sandwich by sitting on it.
Please don't squash the box, as there are fragile items inside.
She used her hand to squash the bug on the wall.

Synset Examples

crush an aluminum can
squeeze a lemon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or becomes deformed
  • Synonym: to flatten - to make something flat or flatter
  • Antonym: to inflate - to fill something with air or gas so that it expands

Etymology

Probably from Middle English squachen, from Old French escoasser (to crush, break), from Vulgar Latin *exquassare.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / skwɒʃ / (BrE)
An edible fruit of plants in the gourd family, eaten as a vegetable and available in many varieties such as zucchini and pumpkin.
edible gourd fruit eaten as a vegetable

Examples

She made a delicious soup with roasted squash and carrots.
Acorn squash is a popular vegetable in the autumn season.
They grew several kinds of squash in their backyard garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pumpkin - large, round, orange squash; another edible gourd
  • Synonym: gourd - any of various fleshy, typically large fruits with a hard skin

Etymology

From Narragansett or Massachusett word *askutasquash* (a green thing eaten raw).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07731306-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / skwɒʃ / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Cucurbita grown for its edible fleshy fruit, which is used as a vegetable in cooking
an edible plant of the gourd family

Examples

We grew squash and tomatoes in our backyard garden this year.
Butternut squash is delicious when roasted with a little olive oil and garlic.
Many types of squash are harvested in the fall for soups and pies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pumpkin - a large, round orange fruit of the same genus as squash
  • Degree Lower: zucchini - a variety of summer squash with a long, dark green fruit

Etymology

From Narragansett Native American ‘askutasquash’ meaning ‘a green thing eaten raw’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12179315-n
Synset POS: n
