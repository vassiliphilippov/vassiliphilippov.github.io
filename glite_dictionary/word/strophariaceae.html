Strophariaceae

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˌfɛəriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / strɒˌfɛəriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of gilled mushrooms that includes the genera Stropharia, Psilocybe, and others, sometimes formerly included in Agaricaceae
a family of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Many hallucinogenic mushrooms belong to the Strophariaceae family.
The taxonomy of Strophariaceae has been revised several times in recent decades.
Some species within Strophariaceae are known for their striking blue coloration.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Stropharia and the suffix -aceae used for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ strəˌfɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / strɒˌfarɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi including several genera of gilled mushrooms, sometimes classified within the Agaricaceae family
family of certain gilled mushrooms

Examples

Several well-known mushrooms, such as the genus Stropharia, belong to the Strophariaceae.
Some taxonomists now place Strophariaceae within the larger Agaricaceae family.
Identifying members of the Strophariaceae requires careful examination of their spores and gills.

Etymology

From the genus name Stropharia + Latin suffix -aceae, used for plant and fungal families.
