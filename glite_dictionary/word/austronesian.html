Austronesian

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːstroʊˈniːʒən / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəˈniːʒən / (BrE)
of or relating to Austronesia, its people, cultures, or languages
relating to Austronesia or its people

Examples

The Austronesian languages are spoken across a vast area from Madagascar to Easter Island.
Many Austronesian societies have rich maritime traditions.
Archaeologists study the migration routes of Austronesian peoples.

Etymology

From Austronesia, combining Latin 'auster' (south wind) and Greek 'nēsos' (island), referring to southern islands.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːstroʊˈniːʒən / (AmE), / ˌɔːstrəˈniːʒən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Austronesia, or a member of the Austronesian-speaking peoples.
native or inhabitant of Austronesia

Examples

Many Austronesians migrated across vast distances using advanced seafaring techniques.
The Philippines and Indonesia are home to numerous Austronesians.
Linguists study how Austronesians spread their languages throughout the Pacific islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polynesian - member of the indigenous peoples of Polynesia
  • Synonym: Malagasy - a native or inhabitant of Madagascar (an Austronesian people)

Etymology

From Austronesia, formed from Latin 'auster' (south wind) + Greek 'nēsos' (island), with suffix -an.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔːstroʊˈniːʒən / (AmE), / ˌɔːstrəˈniːʒən / (BrE)
a member of a large family of languages spoken mainly in Southeast Asia, Madagascar, the Pacific Islands, and parts of Taiwan and Australia
major language family in Southeast Asia and Pacific

Examples

The Austronesian language family includes Malay, Tagalog, and Hawaiian.
Researchers have traced the migration routes of early Austronesian speakers across the Pacific.
Many Austronesian languages share common words for everyday items like rice and boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malayo-Polynesian - the major branch of the Austronesian family

Etymology

From Latin 'Auster' (south wind) and Greek 'nēsos' (island), meaning 'southern islands'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
