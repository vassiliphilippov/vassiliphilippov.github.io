to interlink

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈlɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɪŋk / (BrE)
to connect or be connected with each other, so that things are closely joined or interwoven
to connect closely together

Examples

Modern cities often interlink through complex transportation networks.
These two themes interlink within the novel, giving the story greater depth.
The computers in the lab are interlinked to share resources efficiently.

Synset Examples

The bones are interconnected via the muscle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join or fasten two or more things together
  • Antonym: disconnect - to separate two or more things that are joined

Etymology

From 'inter-' (between, among) + 'link' (to connect).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈlɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɪŋk / (BrE)
to connect or weave things together so that they are closely related or interdependent
connect or weave together

Examples

The two communities interlink through shared cultural events and traditions.
This report will interlink various scientific disciplines for a comprehensive approach.
New technology can interlink our devices, enabling seamless data sharing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join together so as to provide access or communication
  • Antonym: disconnect - to separate or break the connection of

Etymology

From inter- (between) + link (from Old English hlencan, meaning 'to bind together').
