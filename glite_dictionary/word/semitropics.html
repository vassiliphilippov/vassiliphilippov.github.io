semitropics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈtrɑpɪks, ˌsɛmaɪˈtrɑpɪks / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈtrɒpɪks, ˌsɛmaɪˈtrɒpɪks / (BrE)
the regions of the Earth that lie immediately outside the tropics, typically characterized by a climate transitional between tropical and temperate zones
regions just outside the tropics

Examples

Many crops thrive best in the semitropics where temperatures are moderate and rainfall is sufficient.
She moved to the semitropics to enjoy a milder climate than the humid tropics.
Wildlife in the semitropics is uniquely adapted to both tropical and temperate conditions.

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (half, partially) + 'tropics' (from Greek 'tropikos').
