drinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈdrɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who regularly consumes alcoholic beverages, especially in large amounts
person consuming alcoholic drinks

Examples

He is known around town as a heavy drinker who never refuses a pint.
The bar was filled with regular drinkers watching the football match.
After years as a drinker, she finally decided to quit alcohol for good.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcoholic drinks
  • Synonym: alcoholic - a person addicted to alcohol
  • Synonym: tippler - a person who regularly drinks alcohol, often excessively

Etymology

From Middle English drinkere, from drinken (to drink).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈdrɪŋkə / (BrE)
a person who drinks liquids, especially one who habitually drinks alcoholic beverages
person who drinks liquids

Examples

As a regular drinker, he was concerned about the impact on his health.
Not every drinker at the party chose to have alcohol; some preferred water.
The study compared coffee drinkers and non-coffee drinkers to assess health outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcoholic drinks
  • Degree Higher: alcoholic - a person addicted to alcohol
  • Synonym: imbiber - a person who drinks, especially alcohol

Etymology

From Middle English drinkere, from Old English drincere, from drinkan (to drink).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
