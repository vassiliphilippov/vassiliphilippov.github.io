tonguefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A left-eyed marine flatfish with a pointed tail, found chiefly in warm seas, and of little commercial value.
a flatfish with a pointed tail

Examples

The tonguefish is often found in shallow, sandy areas along tropical coastlines.
That bay is home to several species of tonguefish despite its limited commercial importance.
Researchers discovered a new type of tonguefish off the coast last year.
