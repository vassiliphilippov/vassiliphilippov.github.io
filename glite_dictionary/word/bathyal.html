bathyal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæθiəl / (AmE), / ˈbæθɪəl / (BrE)
relating to the deep-sea zone between 200 and 2,000 meters, typically corresponding to the continental slope.
relating to deep ocean zone (200–2,000 m)

Examples

The submarine descended into the bathyal zone, where sunlight barely penetrates.
Scientists study the unique organisms that inhabit the bathyal depths of the ocean.
Many deep-sea fish are adapted to survive in bathyal environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: abyssal - relating to the ocean zone deeper than bathyal (over 2,000 meters)
  • Degree Lower: neritic - relating to the shallow part of the sea near the shore

Etymology

From Greek 'bathys' (deep).
