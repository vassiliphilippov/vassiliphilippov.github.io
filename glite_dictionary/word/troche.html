troche

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊki / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊki / (BrE)
A small medicated lozenge designed to dissolve in the mouth, used to soothe the throat or deliver medication.
medicated lozenge for the throat
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a troche to relieve her sore throat.
He sucked on a troche to ease his persistent cough during the meeting.
After the procedure, the nurse gave her a troche for mouth discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lozenge - a small medicinal tablet to dissolve in the mouth

Etymology

From Greek trokhos (wheel, round object), through Latin trochiscus.
