Saint Augustine

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstin / (AmE), / seɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstiːn / (BrE)
a city in northeastern Florida, regarded as the oldest city in the United States, known for its historic architecture and tourist attractions.
historic city in Florida, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit Saint Augustine to explore its centuries-old Spanish fort and charming old town.
Saint Augustine is celebrated for being the oldest continuously inhabited European-established settlement in the continental United States.
Historic sites in Saint Augustine attract visitors interested in colonial and Civil War-era history.

Etymology

Named after Saint Augustine of Hippo, a Christian theologian.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstɪn / (AmE), / seɪnt ɔːˈɡʌstɪn / (BrE)
(354–430) An influential early Christian theologian and philosopher, bishop of Hippo, whose writings shaped Western Christianity and philosophy.
early Christian theologian and bishop of Hippo

Examples

Saint Augustine wrote extensively on the nature of grace and free will.
Many scholars study the philosophies of Saint Augustine to understand early Christian doctrine.
The Confessions of Saint Augustine remain influential in theology and philosophy today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Augustine of Hippo - another name for Saint Augustine, theologian and bishop

Etymology

From Latin 'Augustinus,' from 'Augustus,' meaning venerable or exalted.
