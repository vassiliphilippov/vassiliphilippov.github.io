Viburnum trilobum

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɜrnəm traɪˈloʊbəm / (AmE), / vɪˈbɜːnəm traɪˈləʊbəm / (BrE)
A deciduous North American shrub or small tree with three-lobed leaves and clusters of bright red berries, commonly known as the American cranberrybush.
North American shrub with red berries

Examples

The bright red berries of the Viburnum trilobum attract many birds in the fall.
Gardeners often choose Viburnum trilobum for its showy foliage and clusters of fruit.
In the wetland restoration project, several Viburnum trilobum shrubs were planted to support local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American cranberrybush - Common name for Viburnum trilobum, a shrub with red berries

Etymology

From Latin 'viburnum' (a type of tree or shrub) and 'trilobum' (three-lobed).
