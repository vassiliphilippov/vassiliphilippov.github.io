Rosicrucianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊzɪˈkruːʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌrəʊzɪˈkruːʃɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A religious and philosophical movement originating in the early 17th century, venerating the rose and the cross as symbols of Christ's resurrection and redemption, and often associated with claims of occult knowledge and mystical beliefs.
religious and mystical movement venerating the rose and cross

Examples

Many secret societies claim a connection to Rosicrucianism and its mystical teachings.
Scholars have debated the historical origins of Rosicrucianism for centuries.
The symbols of the rose and cross are central to Rosicrucianism and its philosophical ideals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occultism - belief in or study of supernatural powers and hidden knowledge
  • Degree Lower: Christianity - the religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Rosicrucian, from Latin rosa (rose) + crux (cross) + -ian, referring to the symbolic rose and cross.
