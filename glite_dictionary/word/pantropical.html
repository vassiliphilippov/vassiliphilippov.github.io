pantropical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpænˈtrɑpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpænˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
Found or distributed throughout all tropical regions of the world.
distributed across all tropics

Examples

The pantropical distribution of this plant species makes it important for ecological studies.
Many marine organisms are classified as pantropical because they inhabit all major tropical oceans.
Climate change may affect both pantropical and regionally restricted species differently.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tropical - relating to or situated in the tropics

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) + 'tropical', referring to the tropics.
