ratline

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈræt.lʌɪn / (BrE)
(nautical) a small horizontal rope fastened between the shrouds of a sailing ship, forming a ladder for climbing the rigging.
horizontal rope ladder on a sailing ship

Examples

The sailor swiftly climbed the ratlines to reach the top of the mast.
Children visiting the old ship were fascinated by the rows of ratlines strung along the rigging.
Maintaining the ratlines was essential for the crew's safety during storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shroud - supporting rope in a sailing ship's rigging

Etymology

From 'rat' (referring to the way rats run on ropes) + 'line'.
