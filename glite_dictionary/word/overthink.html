to overthink

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
to think about something too much or for too long, often resulting in confusion or an incorrect decision
to think too much about something

Examples

Try not to overthink the situation or you'll stress yourself out unnecessarily.
She tends to overthink every decision, which makes it hard for her to choose.
I realized I was overthinking the problem when the solution was actually quite simple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ruminate - to think deeply about something
  • Antonym: underthink - to think too little about something

Etymology

from 'over-' (excessive) + 'think'

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
to think about something too much or for too long, especially so that it becomes difficult to make a decision or take action
think too much about something

Examples

She tends to overthink even the smallest decisions, which makes her anxious.
If you overthink every situation, you'll never feel confident in your choices.
He realized he was starting to overthink the interview and decided to relax.

Synset Examples

She has a habitual tendency to overthink every decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to consider something carefully
  • Synonym: worry - to feel anxious or concerned about something

Etymology

From the prefix 'over-' (excessively) + 'think'; attested since the late 20th century.
