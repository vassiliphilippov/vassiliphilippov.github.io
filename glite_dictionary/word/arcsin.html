arcsin

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˌsɪn / (AmE), / ɑːkˌsɪn / (BrE)
the inverse function of the sine; the angle whose sine is a given number
inverse sine function (math)

Examples

To find the angle, take the arcsin of the given value.
The calculator has a button specifically for arcsin functions.
In trigonometry, arcsin is often used to determine unknown angles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arccos - the inverse function of the cosine
  • Synonym: arctan - the inverse function of the tangent
  • Antonym: sin - the trigonometric function sine

Etymology

Formed from "arc" (as in arc of a circle) and "sin," abbreviation of sine.
