holler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑlər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
(US, regional) A small, narrow valley between mountains, especially in Appalachia; a hollow.
small valley between mountains (US regional)
american

Examples

He grew up in a remote holler deep in the Appalachian foothills.
The family settled in a quiet holler surrounded by dense forest.
After a long hike, they camped in a cool holler by a clear stream.

Synset Examples

he built himself a cabin in a hollow high up in the Appalachians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollow - a small valley or depression in the land

Etymology

Dialectal variant of 'hollow', particularly in Appalachian English.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
to shout or call out loudly, often to attract attention or express strong emotion
shout or call out loudly
informal

Examples

He hollered for help when he saw smoke coming from the kitchen.
The kids hollered with excitement as the parade went by.
Don't holler at me; I'm standing right here.

Synset Examples

He hollered out to surrender our weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - utter a loud call or cry
  • Synonym: yell - shout loudly, typically in pain, anger, or excitement
  • Antonym: whisper - speak very softly using one's breath

Etymology

Probably variant of 'holler' from Middle English 'holeren', related to 'holl', meaning to shout.

to shout or cry out loudly
informal

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to utter a loud call or cry, typically as an expression of emotion
  • Synonym: yell - to give a loud, sharp cry
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, using one's breath rather than one's throat

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
a very loud shout or cry, often resembling the sound made by an animal
a very loud shout or cry

Examples

We heard a holler from the woods and wondered what animal was out there.
He let out a holler when he saw the surprise party waiting for him.
Her holler echoed across the empty field, startling the birds into flight.

Synset Examples

his bellow filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellow - a deep loud roar, typically of a large animal
  • Synonym: shout - a loud call or cry
  • Antonym: whisper - speaking very softly or quietly

Etymology

Alteration of 'holler', a variant of 'hollo', from earlier 'halloo', as a cry to attract attention.

5. VERB

/ ˈhɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
(informal) To complain loudly or protest, often in an emotional or noisy way.
to complain loudly; to protest
informal

Examples

Stop hollering about your chores and just do them.
Whenever the rules change, someone is sure to holler.
She hollered that it wasn't fair when she lost the game.

Synset Examples

What was he hollering about?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • Synonym: grumble - to complain in a quiet or bad-tempered way

Etymology

Possibly variant of 'holler' (to shout), from an American English dialectal pronunciation of 'holler' for 'holler' (from Middle English 'holeren').
