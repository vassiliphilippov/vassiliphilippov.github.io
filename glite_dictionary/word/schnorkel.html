schnorkel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɔr.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɔː.kəl / (BrE)
A retractable device fitted with intake and exhaust pipes that allows a submarine to operate submerged for extended periods by drawing in air from above the water's surface.
submarine air intake device

Examples

The submarine's schnorkel allowed it to remain hidden beneath the waves for days at a time.
Engineers checked the schnorkel regularly to ensure proper ventilation while submerged.
With a damaged schnorkel, the crew had to surface more frequently than planned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snorkel - device for breathing air from above the surface, especially one used by swimmers or submarines

Etymology

From German 'Schnorchel' (snout, air tube); adopted by English in context of submarine technology during World War II.
