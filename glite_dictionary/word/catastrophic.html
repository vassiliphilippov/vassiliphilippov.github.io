catastrophic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæt̬.əˈstrɑː.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌkæt.əˈstrɒf.ɪk / (BrE)
extremely harmful or disastrous; causing sudden and severe damage or suffering.
extremely harmful or disastrous

Examples

The earthquake had a catastrophic impact on the entire region, destroying homes and infrastructure.
A catastrophic failure of the computer system shut down operations for several hours.
If the dam bursts, the results could be truly catastrophic for the nearby villages.

Synset Examples

a catastrophic depression
catastrophic illness
a ruinous course of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrous - causing great damage or suffering
  • Synonym: devastating - causing severe shock, distress, or grief
  • Antonym: beneficial - producing good results or helpful effects

Etymology

From Greek katastrophē (overturning, disaster), via French catastrophique.
