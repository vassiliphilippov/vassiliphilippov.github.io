undertone

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌndətəʊn / (BrE)
a quiet or hushed tone of voice that is not intended to be heard by everyone
quiet or hushed tone of voice

Examples

She spoke in a low undertone so that no one else would hear.
There was a nervous undertone in their conversation as they discussed the secret.
He delivered his warning in an urgent undertone just before the meeting started.

Synset Examples

spoke in undertones

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning below or beneath) and 'tone'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌtəʊn / (BrE)
a subtle or underlying quality, especially an implicit feeling or meaning beneath what is openly stated
implicit or subdued meaning or feeling

Examples

Her calm words had an undertone of irritation that was hard to miss.
There was a political undertone to his speech, even though he never mentioned any parties.
The novel is light-hearted, but with a darker undertone throughout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle difference in or shade of meaning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05931441-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌn.dɚˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌn.də.təʊn / (BrE)
a pale or subdued color that appears beneath the main color, especially one that subtly influences the overall tone
a pale or subdued color beneath the main color

Examples

The blue paint has a green undertone that becomes visible in sunlight.
Her skin has warm undertones, so gold jewelry suits her best.
The artist added a soft pink undertone to give the portrait more depth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overtone - a color, shade, or quality that is more prominent or apparent than an undertone

Etymology

From 'under-' (beneath) + 'tone' (color or quality).
