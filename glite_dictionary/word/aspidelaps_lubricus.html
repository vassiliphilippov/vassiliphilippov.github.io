Aspidelaps lubricus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspɪˈdilæps luːˈbrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌæspɪˈdiːlæps ljuːˈbrɪkəs / (BrE)
a small, widely distributed arboreal snake of southern Africa, banded in black and orange; commonly known as the coral cobra
small southern African snake, coral cobra

Examples

The brightly colored Aspidelaps lubricus is often mistaken for more dangerous snakes due to its vivid bands.
Researchers discovered several Aspidelaps lubricus specimens in the woodlands during their survey.
Because of its coloration, Aspidelaps lubricus can be spotted easily in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Greek *aspis* (shield) and *elaps* (snake), and Latin *lubricus* (slippery).
