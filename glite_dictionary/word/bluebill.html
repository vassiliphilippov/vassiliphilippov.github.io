bluebill

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɪl / (BrE)
A North American diving duck with a bluish-grey bill, commonly referring to species such as the lesser and greater scaup.
North American diving duck with a bluish bill

Examples

Each spring, flocks of bluebills migrate through the region's marshes.
The hunter spotted a pair of bluebill ducks swimming near the reeds.
A bluebill is easily recognized by its distinctive bluish-grey beak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaup - A diving duck species commonly called 'bluebill'
