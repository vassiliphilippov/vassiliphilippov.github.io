uno ab alto

1. X

/ ˈuːnoʊ ɑːb ˈæltoʊ / (AmE), / ˈjuːnəʊ æb ˈæltəʊ / (BrE)
A Latin phrase meaning 'one from on high', often used to refer to authority or a decision made by a higher power.
Latin for 'one from on high'

Examples

The leader invoked uno ab alto to justify the sudden policy change.
Her justification seemed to rely on uno ab alto rather than logical reasoning.
In court, the lawyer referred to uno ab alto in his closing argument.

Synset Examples

In the debate, she quoted 'uno ab alto' to emphasize her point.

Etymology

Latin, from 'uno' (one) + 'ab alto' (from on high).
