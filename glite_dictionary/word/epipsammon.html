epipsammon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsamən / (BrE)
Microscopic organisms, including flora and fauna, that live on the surface of or are attached to sand grains, especially in aquatic environments.
microscopic organisms living on sand grains
medical

Examples

Samples from the lakebed revealed a diverse epipsammon community attached to each sand grain.
Scientists study the epipsammon to better understand aquatic microecosystems.
Changes in water chemistry can impact the epipsammon and, in turn, affect nutrient cycling.

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (on) + 'psammos' (sand) + scientific suffix '-on'.
