goddamn

1. ADV

/ ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒd.dæm / (BrE)
Used to emphasize anger, annoyance, surprise, or other strong feelings, often considered vulgar.
vulgar intensifier for strong emotion
vulgar

Examples

That was so goddamn loud I nearly jumped out of my seat.
You’re goddamn right I finished the report on time!
It’s goddamn impossible to find a parking spot around here.

Synset Examples

you are goddamn right!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - used for emphasis or to express anger, similar meaning but less strong

Etymology

From 'God damn', an expletive meaning 'may God condemn'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00351763-r

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌdæm / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes vulgar) Used for emphasis to express anger, annoyance, or surprise, or to intensify a statement.
used for emphasis or to show anger
informal vulgar

Examples

I can't find my goddamn keys anywhere this morning.
Turn off that goddamn alarm clock!
She forgot the goddamn tickets at home again.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒd.dæm / (BrE)
(Used for emphasis or as an expletive) expressing annoyance, anger, or surprise.
expletive expressing anger or emphasis
vulgar

Examples

Will someone turn off that goddamn alarm?
She slammed the goddamn door on her way out.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - used as an expletive for emphasis or anger

From 'God damn', an oath invoking God's condemnation.
