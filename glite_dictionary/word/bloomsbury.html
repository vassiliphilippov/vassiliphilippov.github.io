Bloomsbury

1. PROPN

/ ˈbluːmzˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbluːmzˌbəri / (BrE)
an area of central London known for its garden squares, literary heritage, and educational institutions
district in central London, England

Examples

She rented a flat in Bloomsbury to be close to the British Museum.
The literary history of Bloomsbury attracts many students and tourists every year.
Many famous writers once gathered in Bloomsbury during the early twentieth century.

Etymology

Named after the manor of Blemondisberi (13th century), later Bloomsbury, possibly from the personal name Blemond.
