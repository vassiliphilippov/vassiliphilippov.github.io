Tectaria cicutaria

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtɛəri.ə sɪˈkjuːtəri.ə / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɛːrɪə sɪˈkjuːtərɪə / (BrE)
A Jamaican fern species known for its round, buttonlike bulbils.
Jamaican fern species

Examples

The Tectaria cicutaria grows primarily in the moist forests of Jamaica.
Botanists are studying Tectaria cicutaria for its unique round bulbils.
You can identify Tectaria cicutaria by its distinctive buttonlike structures on the fronds.

Etymology

From the genus name Tectaria, derived from Latin 'tectus' (covered), and the species name cicutaria, referring to resemblance to hemlock (Cicuta).
