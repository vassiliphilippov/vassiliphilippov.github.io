declared

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈklɛrd / (AmE), / dɪˈkleəd / (BrE)
publicly stated or acknowledged; made known or openly avowed
publicly acknowledged or stated

Examples

He was a declared supporter of the new policy.
They acted in accordance with their declared intentions.
Despite his declared innocence, many still doubted his story.

Synset Examples

their declared and their covert objectives
a declared liberal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed
  • Synonym: avowed - having been openly declared or acknowledged

Etymology

From the verb 'declare,' which comes from Old French 'declarer,' from Latin 'declarare' (to make clear).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈklɛrd / (AmE), / dɪˈklɛːd / (BrE)
stated openly or officially; made known clearly or formally.
stated openly or officially

Examples

The country became a declared nuclear power after its first successful test.
The declared objective of the organization is to reduce poverty worldwide.
In his declared statement, the president addressed the concerns of the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion
  • Antonym: implied - suggested but not directly expressed

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'declare', from Latin 'declarare' (to make clear).
