fantasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌtæzəm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tæz(ə)m / (BrE)
a figment of the imagination; something that exists only in perception, such as a vision or apparition
imaginary vision or apparition
archaic

Examples

The fantasm of a ghost haunted the old manor at night.
He dismissed her vision as merely a fantasm of an overactive mind.
In the dim light, every shadow became a potential fantasm to the frightened child.

Synset Examples

a ghostly apparition at midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phantasm - an illusory perception or apparition
  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality

Etymology

From Old French 'fantasme', from Latin 'phantasma', from Greek 'phantasma' (apparition, phantom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tæz(ə)m / (AmE), / ˈfan.taz(ə)m / (BrE)
A ghostly or supernatural figure; an apparition.
ghostly apparition

Examples

As the fog thickened, a fantasm drifted silently across the deserted graveyard.
She claimed that a fantasm visited her every night, whispering secrets from the past.
The old tale described a fantasm haunting the castle ruins, frightening all who dared enter.

Synset Examples

we were unprepared for the apparition that confronted us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phantasm - an apparition or ghost
  • Synonym: specter - a ghost or spirit of a dead person

Etymology

From Greek phantasma, meaning 'apparition' or 'phantom', via Late Latin fantasma.
