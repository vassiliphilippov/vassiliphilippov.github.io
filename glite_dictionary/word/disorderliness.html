disorderliness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɔrdɚliˌnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being untidy or lacking order, especially in clothing or appearance.
state of being untidy or disordered

Examples

His disorderliness was evident from his wrinkled shirt and messy hair.
The disorderliness of the room made it hard to find anything.
Teachers often complain about the disorderliness of students' desks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untidiness - the state of being untidy or messy
  • Antonym: orderliness - the state of being orderly or neat

Etymology

From 'disorderly' + '-ness', with 'disorderly' from Old French 'desorde' (lack of order).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɔrdɚli.nəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəli.nɪs / (BrE)
unruly or rowdy behavior; a lack of order or discipline
rowdy or unruly behavior

Examples

The disorderliness of the crowd forced the police to intervene.
Teachers were concerned about the disorderliness during school assemblies.
He complained about the disorderliness in the meeting, saying it made progress impossible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderliness - the quality of being orderly or well-organized
  • Synonym: rowdiness - noisy, rough, or disorderly behavior

Etymology

From 'disorderly' (from Middle English, influenced by Old French 'desordre') + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɔrdərlɪnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəlɪnəs / (BrE)
The state of being untidy or not arranged in the expected order.
lack of order; untidiness

Examples

The disorderliness of his desk made it hard to find anything quickly.
After the party, the room was left in complete disorderliness.
She was criticized for the disorderliness of her classroom.

Synset Examples

the files are in complete disorder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderliness - the quality of being orderly or neatly arranged
  • Synonym: disorder - a lack of order or regular arrangement

Etymology

From 'disorderly' + '-ness', ultimately from Middle English 'disorder'.
