Wales

1. PROPN

/ weɪlz / (AmE), / weɪlz / (BrE)
A country in the west of Great Britain that is part of the United Kingdom, known for its distinct culture and language.
country in west Great Britain, part of the UK

Examples

Many tourists visit Wales each year to explore its beautiful national parks and rich history.
The official languages of Wales are Welsh and English.
She moved from London to Wales for a quieter lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: England - country in southern Great Britain, part of the UK
  • Synonym: Scotland - country in northern Great Britain, part of the UK
  • Synonym: Northern Ireland - country in the northeast of the island of Ireland, part of the UK

Etymology

From the Old English 'Wealas', meaning 'foreigners', originally referring to Celtic inhabitants.
