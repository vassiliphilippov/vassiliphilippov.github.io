abrupt

1. ADJ

/ əˈbrʌpt / (AmE), / əˈbrʌpt / (BrE)
happening suddenly and unexpectedly, often without warning or preparation
sudden and unexpected

Examples

The car came to an abrupt halt when the traffic light turned red.
We experienced an abrupt change in temperature when the storm arrived.
Her abrupt departure surprised everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

came to an abrupt stop
an abrupt change in the weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sudden - occurring quickly and unexpectedly
  • Antonym: gradual - happening slowly or in small stages

Etymology

From Latin abruptus (broken off, steep), past participle of abrumpere (to break off).

2. ADJ

/ əˈbrʌpt / (AmE), / əˈbrʌpt / (BrE)
characterized by sudden changes or sharp transitions, especially in style, subject, or movement
sudden or sharp in change or transition

Examples

The novel's abrupt shifts in tone keep the reader off balance.
Her abrupt change of topic caught everyone by surprise.
The director's abrupt editing style gives the film a jarring effect.

Synset Examples

abrupt prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sudden - occurring quickly and without warning
  • Antonym: smooth - without sharp transitions or sudden changes

Etymology

From Latin abruptus, past participle of abrumpere (to break off).

3. ADJ

/ əˈbrʌpt / (AmE), / əˈbrʌpt / (BrE)
rude and sudden in manner; speaking or acting with little or no warning or courtesy
brusque, curt in manner

Examples

His abrupt response made everyone uncomfortable.
She turned away with an abrupt gesture, ending the conversation.
The manager's abrupt tone discouraged further questions.

Synset Examples

an abrupt reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brusque - rudely abrupt or curt in manner
  • Synonym: curt - rudely brief in speech or manner

Etymology

From Latin abruptus (broken off, steep) from ab- (away) + ruptus (broken).

4. ADJ

/ əˈbrʌpt / (AmE), / əˈbrʌpt / (BrE)
(of a slope or drop) very steep or sudden, often so steep as to be potentially dangerous.
very steep or sudden (of slopes)

Examples

The trail led them to an abrupt cliff overlooking the valley below.
An abrupt change in the slope made the hike more challenging.
The river took an abrupt turn and plunged down a series of dramatic falls.

Synset Examples

an abrupt canyon
the precipitous rapids of the upper river
the precipitous hills of Chinese paintings
a sharp drop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - having a sharp incline; rising or falling sharply
  • Antonym: gentle - having a gradual incline or slope

Etymology

From Latin abruptus (broken off, steep), from ab- (off) + ruptus (broken).
