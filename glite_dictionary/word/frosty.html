frosty

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑːsti / (AmE), / ˈfrɒsti / (BrE)
cold and unfriendly in manner or attitude; showing little warmth or cordiality.
emotionally cold or unfriendly

Examples

Her response to the suggestion was noticeably frosty, making everyone else feel uncomfortable.
They received a frosty welcome upon arriving at the meeting.
After their argument, there was a frosty silence between them.

Synset Examples

a frigid greeting
got a frosty reception
a frozen look on their faces
a glacial handshake
icy stare
wintry smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icy - very cold or showing unfriendliness
  • Antonym: cordial - warm and friendly

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑsti / (AmE), / ˈfrɒsti / (BrE)
(of weather or air) pleasantly cold and invigorating.
pleasantly cold and invigorating (weather)

Examples

The frosty morning air made her cheeks tingle as she walked to work.
We enjoyed a frosty evening stroll under a clear, starry sky.
The mountains were especially beautiful in the frosty autumn air.

Synset Examples

crisp clear nights and frosty mornings
a nipping wind
a nippy fall day
snappy weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisp - (of weather or air) notably fresh, cool, and bracing
  • Synonym: nippy - (informal, of weather) rather cold; chilly

Etymology

From Old English *forst* (frost) + -y, meaning 'covered with frost' or 'cold as frost'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑsti / (AmE), / ˈfrɒsti / (BrE)
covered with frost; marked by a thin layer of ice crystals from cold weather
covered with frost

Examples

The grass was frosty and crunched beneath our boots at sunrise.
She wiped her name onto the frosty window with her finger.
A frosty layer had formed on the car windshield overnight.

Synset Examples

a frosty glass
hedgerows were rimed and stiff with frost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icy - covered with or consisting of ice

Etymology

From frost + -y, originating from Old English *forst* (frost) and the adjectival suffix -y.
