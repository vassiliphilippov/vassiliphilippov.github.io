residuary

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuˌɛri / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjʊəri / (BrE)
Entitled to the remainder of an estate after the payment of debts and distribution of specific gifts.
entitled to remainder of an estate
legal

Examples

The trustee distributed the property to the residuary beneficiary after debts were cleared.
Under the will, her niece was named the residuary legatee.
The residuary clause ensures nothing in the estate is left unallocated.

Synset Examples

the residuary part of the estate
the residuary beneficiary

Etymology

From Latin 'residuus' meaning 'remaining'.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuˌɛri / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjuəri / (BrE)
relating to or indicating what remains, especially after the main part has been taken or used, or in legal contexts, what is left of an estate after the payment of debts and specific bequests.
relating to the remainder or residue

Examples

The lawyer explained the distribution of the residuary estate to the heirs.
After all specific debts were paid, the residuary funds were divided among the children.
Her will named her nephew as the residuary beneficiary.

Synset Examples

residual quantity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residual - relating to a remainder, especially a small part left after the main part is gone

Etymology

From Latin *residuus* (remaining) + English suffix *-ary*.
