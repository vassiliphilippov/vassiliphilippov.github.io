every

1. DET

/ ˈɛv.ri / (AmE), / ˈev.ri / (BrE)
Used before singular countable nouns to mean each individual member of a group, considered singly and including all without exception.
each individual member of a group

Examples

Every child needs love and support as they grow up.
He checked every room in the house for his lost keys.
Every Friday, the team meets to discuss their progress.

Synset Examples

every person is mortal
every party is welcome
had every hope of success
every chance of winning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: each - every individual member of a group considered one by one
  • Synonym: all - the whole number or amount of; every one of

Etymology

From Old English æfre ælc (each one, always each), later conflated with ever.

2. DET

/ ˈɛv.ri / (AmE), / ˈɛv.ri / (BrE)
used to refer to all the individual members or items in a group, without exception, considered separately in a series, interval, or group.
each within a group or series

Examples

You should take your medicine every six hours for best results.
There is a bus every fifteen minutes during rush hour.
He checks every detail before submitting his work.

Synset Examples

every third seat
every two hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: each - every one of two or more people or things, considered separately

Etymology

From Old English 'ǽfre ǽlc' (ever each), reinforced by Middle English 'everi'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02277945-s
Synset POS: s
