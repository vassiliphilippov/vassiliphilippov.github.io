composed

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
calm and in control of your emotions, especially in difficult or stressful situations
calm and self-possessed

Examples

Despite the chaos in the office, she remained composed throughout the meeting.
He gave a composed response when asked about the tough decision.
It is important to appear composed during stressful interviews.

Synset Examples

the performer seemed completely composed as she stepped onto the stage
I felt calm and more composed than I had in a long time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • Synonym: collected - not emotionally affected; self-possessed
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

Past participle of 'compose', from Latin 'componere' meaning 'to put together'.
