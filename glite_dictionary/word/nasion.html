nasion

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪziˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈneɪzɪən / (BrE)
the point on the human skull at the bridge of the nose where the frontal and nasal bones meet
anatomical point at the bridge of the nose
medical

Examples

During the facial measurement, the technician marked the nasion to ensure accuracy.
The surgeon made an incision just above the nasion for the reconstructive procedure.
Researchers use the nasion as a reference point in craniofacial studies.

Etymology

From Latin *nasus* (nose).
