emblem

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.bləm / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bləm / (BrE)
A visible object or design that represents a particular idea, group, or quality.
symbol representing an idea or group

Examples

The dove is an emblem of peace in many cultures.
The national flag serves as an emblem of unity and pride.
She wore a badge that bore the emblem of her school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - Something that stands for or suggests something else by reason of relationship, association, or convention

Etymology

From Middle French 'emblème', from Latin 'emblema' (inlaid ornament), from Greek 'emblēma' (that which is put in or on).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.bləm / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bləm / (BrE)
A design or object that symbolizes or represents a particular quality, group, idea, or organization.
symbolic design or object

Examples

The olive branch is an emblem of peace recognized worldwide.
The school's emblem was proudly displayed on every student's blazer.
The lion has long served as an emblem of courage and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - a mark, sign, or object that represents something else
  • Degree Lower: logo - a graphic mark or emblem used to identify a company or product

Etymology

From Old French *embleme*, from Latin *emblema* (inlaid work), from Greek *emblēma* (insertion, ornament).

Synset POS: n
