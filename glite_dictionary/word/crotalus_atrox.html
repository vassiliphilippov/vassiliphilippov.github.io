Crotalus atrox

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈteɪləs ˈætrɑks / (AmE), / krəʊˈtɑːləs ˈætrɒks / (BrE)
a large, highly venomous rattlesnake of the southwestern United States and Mexico, also known as the western diamondback rattlesnake
western diamondback rattlesnake species

Examples

The venom of the Crotalus atrox can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
Researchers captured a Crotalus atrox in the Arizona desert for study.
Crotalus atrox is often encountered by hikers in its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western diamondback rattlesnake - the common name for Crotalus atrox

Etymology

From Latin: 'Crotalus' meaning 'rattle' and 'atrox' meaning 'fierce' or 'terrible'.
