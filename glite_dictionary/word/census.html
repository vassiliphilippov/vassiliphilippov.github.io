census

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
an official count or survey of a population, typically recording various details of individuals
official population count or survey

Examples

The government conducts a census every ten years to gather up-to-date demographic data.
Many policy decisions are based on the most recent census results.
The census showed a significant increase in the city's population over the last decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - an investigation to collect information, often by asking questions
  • Degree Lower: inventory - a complete list of items such as property or goods in stock

Etymology

From Latin 'census' (a registration of the citizens and their property), from 'censere' (to assess).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
to conduct an official count or survey of a population, typically recording various details of individuals.
to conduct an official population count

Examples

The government decided to census the remote villages to update national records.
Biologists censused the bird population in the wetlands last spring.
A team of researchers is censusing plant species in the region to monitor biodiversity.

Synset Examples

They censused the deer in the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - to systematically collect information from a group

Etymology

From Latin 'census' (a register, an assessment).
