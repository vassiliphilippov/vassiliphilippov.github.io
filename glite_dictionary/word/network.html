network

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
A system of lines, routes, or channels that cross or interconnect with each other.
system of intersecting lines or channels

Examples

The city's subway network makes it easy to travel from one district to another.
A vast network of roads connects even the most remote villages.
The irrigation network efficiently distributes water throughout the entire region.

Synset Examples

a railroad network
a network of canals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web - a complicated system of connections or relationships, often resembling a network

Etymology

From Old English 'net' + 'work', originally referring to woven material.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03826490-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
to interact with others to exchange information and develop professional or social contacts, especially for career or business purposes
make professional or social contacts

Examples

It's important to network at industry conferences to build valuable connections.
She networked with professionals from all over the world during her overseas assignment.
Many people find jobs through networking rather than by applying online.

Synset Examples

You have to network if you want to get a good job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to socialize - to interact socially with others

Etymology

From the noun 'network', originally meaning an interconnected system or group, extended to social and professional connections in the late 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01034712-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
an open structure made of threads, wires, or ropes woven together at regular intervals; a mesh or net.
a mesh of threads, wires, or ropes

Examples

A delicate network of silk covered the window.
The fence was reinforced with a network of steel wires for extra security.
Fishermen repaired the torn network they used for catching fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - an open fabric or structure of interlaced fibers or wires
  • Synonym: net - an open fabric of string, rope, or wire for catching or enclosing

Etymology

From late Middle English, from net + work, after Old English netwyrċ (net-work).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03825135-n
Synset POS: n
