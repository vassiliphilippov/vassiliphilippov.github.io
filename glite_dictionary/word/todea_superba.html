Todea superba

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈdiːə suːˈpɜrbə / (AmE), / təʊˈdiːə suːˈpɜːbə / (BrE)
A large fern native to New Zealand, distinguished by its pinnate fronds and densely woolly stalks; occasionally included in the genus Todea.
New Zealand fern with woolly stalks

Examples

The Todea superba is renowned for its striking appearance in New Zealand forests.
Botanists often debate whether Todea superba should be classified in a separate genus.
Gardeners value Todea superba for its dramatic, woolly fronds and resilience in moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king fern - A common name sometimes used for Todea superba and similar ferns

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'todea', a botanical name; 'superba' from Latin meaning 'splendid' or 'superb'.
