Athapascan

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈpæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (BrE)
A member of any of the Indigenous peoples of North America who speak an Athapaskan language, especially those living in subarctic regions of western Canada and central Alaska.
a person of an Athapaskan-speaking Indigenous group

Examples

Many Athapascan peoples have rich oral storytelling traditions passed down through generations.
The Athapascan tribes adapted to the harsh subarctic climate with ingenuity and resilience.
She studied the migration patterns of early Athapascan groups across Alaska and Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athabaskan - variant spelling for the same group of people/languages

Etymology

From Cree athapaskāw (referring to the area around Lake Athabasca in Canada).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈpæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (BrE)
a family of Amerindian languages spoken in North America, especially in western Canada, Alaska, and the southwestern United States
a group of Native American languages

Examples

Many Native communities in Alaska speak a variety of Athapascan languages.
The Athapascan language family includes Navajo and Apache.
Linguists have studied the migration patterns of Athapascan speakers for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athabaskan - alternative spelling for the same language family

Etymology

From Cree *Athapaskan*, adopted by linguist Edward Sapir.
