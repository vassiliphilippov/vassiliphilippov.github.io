constructivism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstrʌktɪvˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
an artistic movement developed in Russia after World War I, characterized by the use of industrial materials to create abstract, nonrepresentational objects.
Russian abstract art movement using industrial materials

Examples

The principles of constructivism revolutionized Russian art in the twentieth century.
Many artists embraced constructivism to explore the relationship between art and industrial materials.
His sculptures reflect the influence of constructivism, with their geometric forms and use of metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstractionism - a style of art that does not attempt to represent an accurate depiction of visual reality
  • Synonym: suprematism - another Russian abstract art movement around the same period

Etymology

From Russian 'konstruktsiya' (structure) and English '-ism', denoting an art movement based on construction.
