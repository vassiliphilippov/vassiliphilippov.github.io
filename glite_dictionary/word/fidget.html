fidget

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
to make small movements, especially of the hands and feet, usually because of nervousness or impatience
move restlessly or nervously

Examples

Stop fidgeting and try to concentrate on your homework.
She fidgeted nervously with her pen during the interview.
After sitting still for an hour, the children began to fidget in their chairs.

Synset Examples

The child is always fidgeting in his seat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirm - to twist or move about uncomfortably, often due to nervousness
  • Antonym: freeze - to become motionless, especially due to fear or nervousness

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from dialectal English 'fidge' (to move restlessly), of expressive origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪdʒət / (AmE), / ˈfɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
a feeling of restlessness or agitation, often resulting in small, nervous movements
restless or agitated feeling with small movements

Examples

The long lecture gave me the fidgets after an hour.
Children often get the fidgets when they're bored and have to sit still.
He tried to hide his fidget, but kept tapping his foot nervously.

Synset Examples

he's got the fidgets
waiting gave him a feeling of restlessness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restlessness - the state of being unable to relax or remain still
  • Antonym: calm - a peaceful or relaxed state, free from agitation

Etymology

Probably from obsolete fidge (to move restlessly), from dialectal English; related to early modern English usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07529034-n
Synset POS: n
