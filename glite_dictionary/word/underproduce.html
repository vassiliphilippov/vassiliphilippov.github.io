to underproduce

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərprəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌʌndəprəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to produce less than the required amount or below full capacity or demand
produce less than needed or possible

Examples

Many farmers were forced to underproduce due to unfavorable weather conditions.
If the company continues to underproduce, it may lose market share to competitors.
The factory underproduced last quarter as a result of supply chain disruptions.

Synset Examples

The East German factories were underproducing for many years

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overproduce - to produce more than is needed or demanded
  • Degree Higher: produce - to make or manufacture goods or materials

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' meaning 'insufficiently' and 'produce'.
