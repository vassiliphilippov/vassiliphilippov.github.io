braid

1. NOUN

/ breɪd / (AmE), / breɪd / (BrE)
A hairstyle in which three or more strands of hair are interwoven together.
interwoven hairstyle

Examples

She wore her hair in a single long braid down her back.
The little girl's mother tied colorful ribbons at the end of each braid.
French braids are a popular choice for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plait - a hairstyle formed by interlacing three or more strands of hair

Etymology

From Old English 'bregdan' (to move quickly, plait) and Old Norse 'bregða' (to braid, twist).

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ breɪd / (AmE), / breɪd / (BrE)
to form something by interlacing three or more strands, often of hair, thread, or other flexible material
form by intertwining strands

Examples

She braided her daughter's hair every morning before school.
The artisan skillfully braided colorful threads to make bracelets for tourists.
If you braid the dough before baking, it creates a beautiful pattern on the bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plait - to form by interlacing, especially hair or fabric

Etymology

From Middle English 'breyden', from Old English 'bregdan' (to move quickly, intertwine).

4. NOUN

/ breɪd / (AmE), / breɪd / (BrE)
A narrow band of intertwined threads, cord, or ribbon used as decorative trimming on clothes or curtains.
decorative trimming for clothes or curtains

Examples

The dress was finished with a gold braid along the hem.
They used thick red braid to decorate the curtains in the dining room.
Military uniforms often have elaborate braid on the sleeves and collars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace - delicate fabric used for trimming or decoration

Etymology

From Old English 'bregdan' (to move quickly, intertwine).
