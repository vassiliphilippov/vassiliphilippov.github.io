Trichomanes reniforme

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkɑːməˌniːz ˌrɛnəˈfɔːrmi / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒməˌniːz ˌrɛnɪˈfɔːmiː / (BrE)
A large fern native to New Zealand, distinguished by its kidney-shaped fronds.
New Zealand fern with kidney-shaped fronds

Examples

Trichomanes reniforme is often found growing in the moist forests of New Zealand's North Island.
Botanists admire the delicate, translucent fronds of Trichomanes reniforme for their unique kidney shape.
While hiking, we spotted a patch of Trichomanes reniforme along the shaded stream banks.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and Latin 'reniforme' (kidney-shaped), referring to the plant's fine texture and kidney-shaped fronds.
