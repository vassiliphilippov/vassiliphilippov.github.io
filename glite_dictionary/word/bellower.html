bellower

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛloʊər / (AmE), / ˈbɛləʊə / (BrE)
a person who shouts or speaks in a very loud voice
person who shouts loudly

Examples

The bellower interrupted the meeting with his booming voice.
At the auction, the bellower could be heard above all others.
She avoided the gruff bellower who always dominated conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shouter - someone who shouts

Etymology

From the verb 'bellow' (to shout loudly), ultimately from Middle English 'belen','
