bewitchery

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈwɪtʃəri / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪtʃəri / (BrE)
The quality of captivating or enchanting charm that attracts and fascinates others.
captivating or enchanting charm

Examples

Her bewitchery captivated everyone at the party, leaving them spellbound by her presence.
The actor's bewitchery on stage drew the audience into the world of the play.
There was a certain bewitchery in his smile that made people instantly trust him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charisma - compelling attractiveness or charm
  • Synonym: allure - attractive or tempting quality
  • Antonym: repulsiveness - quality of being repulsive or unattractive

Etymology

Formed from 'bewitch' (to cast a spell on) + '-ery' (denoting a quality, condition, or action).
