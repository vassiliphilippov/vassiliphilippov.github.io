cuttingly

1. ADV

/ ˈkʌtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that is deliberately hurtful or unkind, often with sharpness or severity.
in a deliberately hurtful manner

Examples

She spoke cuttingly about his latest failed project, leaving him embarrassed in front of everyone.
He cuttingly remarked that her idea was naive and unrealistic.
When questioned, the manager cuttingly dismissed the employee's concerns without a second thought.

Synset Examples

People may even misunderstand our purpose and cuttingly criticise us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - in a critical or harsh way
  • Antonym: kindly - in a friendly and gentle manner

Etymology

From 'cutting' + '-ly', 'cutting' meaning 'hurtful or caustic'.
