cloverleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊvɚˌlif / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvəˌliːf / (BrE)
a road interchange in which the connecting ramps form a pattern resembling a four-leaf clover, allowing traffic to change directions without making left-hand turns across opposing traffic.
type of road interchange

Examples

The highway construction included a new cloverleaf to handle increased traffic flow.
Drivers found the cloverleaf interchange confusing at first, but it greatly reduced congestion.
A cloverleaf makes entering and exiting the motorway safer by eliminating dangerous left turns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchange - a road junction connecting highways with ramps

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the four-part leaf of a clover plant.
