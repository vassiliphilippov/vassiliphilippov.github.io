coagulation

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌæɡ.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌæɡ.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a liquid changes into a semi-solid or thickened mass, often forming clots or lumps
process of a liquid thickening or clotting

Examples

The coagulation of milk is an essential step in cheese making.
Blood coagulation helps prevent excessive bleeding from wounds.
Scientists studied the coagulation process to develop new treatments for clotting disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clotting - the process of blood changing from liquid to a gel, forming a clot

Etymology

From Latin coagulatio (curdling, clumping), from coagulare (to curdle).
