unpalatability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌpæl.ə.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌpæl.ə.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being unpleasant or unacceptable to taste.
the quality of tasting unpleasant

Examples

The unpalatability of the dish made it difficult for anyone to finish their meal.
She tried to mask the unpalatability of the medicine with a spoonful of honey.
Despite its nutrients, the soup’s unpalatability discouraged children from eating it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: palatability - pleasantness to the taste

Etymology

From un- (not) + palatable + -ity, after palatability.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌpælətəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌpælətəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult or unpleasant to accept or agree with, especially in ideas or decisions
quality of being difficult to accept

Examples

The unpalatability of the proposed budget made it difficult to gain public support.
She struggled with the unpalatability of the truth he had told her.
Despite its unpalatability, the decision was necessary for the company's survival.

Synset Examples

the policy's unpalatability caused an uproar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unacceptability - the state of being unacceptable
  • Antonym: palatability - the quality of being pleasant or acceptable, especially to the mind or taste

Etymology

From unpalatable +‎ -ity, after palatable (from Latin palatum ‘palate’).
