televangelist

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˈvændʒəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈvændʒɪlɪst / (BrE)
An evangelist who conducts religious services or spreads religious messages through television broadcasts.
TV-based evangelist

Examples

The televangelist delivered a powerful sermon to millions of viewers last night.
Several famous televangelists have built large followings through their weekly broadcasts.
The televangelist asked his audience for donations to support his ministry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelist - A person who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith, especially by public preaching

Etymology

Formed from 'television' and 'evangelist'; first used in America in the late 20th century.
