Gavialis

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈvaɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / ɡəˈvaɪ.əlɪs / (BrE)
The genus of reptiles that includes the gharial, a large fish-eating crocodilian found in the Indian subcontinent.
genus of gharials (reptiles)

Examples

The Gavialis genus is known for its long, narrow snout.
Scientists study the Gavialis to learn more about crocodilian evolution.
The only living species in the Gavialis genus is Gavialis gangeticus, the gharial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gharial - a large crocodilian of the genus Gavialis, native to India and Nepal

Etymology

From Latin *Gavialis*, from the Hindi word *gharial*, referring to the species.

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈvaɪəlɪs / (AmE), / ɡəˈvɑːɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, long-snouted crocodilian reptiles native to South Asia, including the gharial.
genus of crocodilians (includes gharial)

Examples

The only living species in the genus Gavialis is the gharial.
Fossil records show that Gavialis once had a much wider distribution.
Researchers are working to conserve endangered members of the genus Gavialis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gharial - a large crocodilian with a long, narrow snout, native to South Asia

Etymology

From modern Latin Gavialis, from 'gavial', the French word for gharial (type of crocodilian).
