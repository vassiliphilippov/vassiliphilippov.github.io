Faroes

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛroʊz / (AmE), / ˈfeərəʊz / (BrE)
a group of 21 volcanic islands in the North Atlantic Ocean, located between Iceland and the Shetland Islands, forming a self-governing territory within the Kingdom of Denmark
North Atlantic island group between Iceland and Shetland

Examples

The Faroes are known for their dramatic cliffs and grassy landscapes.
Fishing is a major part of life on the Faroes.
Tourists are increasingly discovering the natural beauty of the Faroes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faroe Islands - another name for the Faroes, a group of islands in the North Atlantic

Etymology

From Danish 'Færøerne,' meaning 'the sheep islands.'

2. PROPN

/ ˈfɛr.oʊz / (AmE), / ˈfeərəʊz / (BrE)
the Faroe Islands; a group of self-governing islands under the sovereignty of Denmark, located in the North Atlantic
the Faroe Islands, a self-governing Danish territory

Examples

The Faroes are known for their dramatic landscapes and birdlife.
Fishing is one of the main industries in the Faroes.
She traveled to the Faroes last summer to experience their unique culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faroe Islands - self-governing Danish territory in the North Atlantic

Etymology

From Norwegian 'Færøyene', meaning 'Sheep Islands'.
