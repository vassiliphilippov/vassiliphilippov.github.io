pelter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛltər / (AmE), / ˈpɛltə / (BrE)
a person who throws objects, especially missiles such as stones or projectiles
a person who throws missiles or objects

Examples

During the protest, the police identified several pelters in the crowd.
The old stadium had a problem with pelters disrupting matches by throwing objects onto the field.
Witnesses claimed that the pelter escaped quickly after hurling a stone at the window.

Synset Examples

the police were too busy to chase the pelters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrower - a person who throws something

Etymology

From 'pelt' (to throw) + -er (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛltər / (AmE), / ˈpɛltə / (BrE)
a very heavy or violent rainstorm; a downpour
a heavy or violent rain
british

Examples

We got caught in a real pelter on our way home from the market.
Rain poured down in a pelter, drenching everyone within minutes.
They waited under the awning for the pelter to let up before heading outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downpour - a heavy fall of rain
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain
