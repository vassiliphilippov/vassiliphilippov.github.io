Wodan

1. PROPN

/ ˈwoʊ.dɑn / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.dæn / (BrE)
The chief god in ancient Germanic mythology; the counterpart of Norse Odin and Teutonic Wotan.
chief god in Germanic mythology

Examples

Many ancient tribes worshipped Wodan as the chief of all gods.
The festival was held in honor of Wodan, seeking his favor before battle.
In mythology books, Wodan is often compared to the Norse god Odin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odin - chief god in Norse mythology
  • Synonym: Wotan - chief god in Teutonic mythology

Etymology

From Old High German 'Wōdan', related to Norse 'Odin' and Old English 'Wōden'.
