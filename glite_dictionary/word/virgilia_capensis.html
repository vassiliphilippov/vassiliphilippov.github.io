Virgilia capensis

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪliə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪliə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a South African tree with odd-pinnate leaves and fragrant pink to purple flower racemes, commonly known as the Cape virgilia
South African tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

The Virgilia capensis brightens South African gardens with its clusters of purple flowers each spring.
Many landscapers favor Virgilia capensis for its rapid growth and attractive foliage.
The sweet scent of Virgilia capensis blooms attracts bees and butterflies in abundance.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Virgilia' and species name from Latin 'capensis' meaning 'from the Cape (of Good Hope)'.
