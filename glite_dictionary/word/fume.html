fume

1. VERB

/ fjuːm / (AmE), / fjuːm / (BrE)
to emit smoke, vapor, or fine particles, often as a visible cloud
emit smoke, vapor, or fine particles

Examples

The factory chimney continued to fume long after sunset.
The volcano began to fume, sending a gray cloud into the sky.
Old buses used to fume thick black smoke on the streets.

Synset Examples

The chimney was fuming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke - emit smoke, as from a fire or engine
  • Synonym: vent - emit or release (gas, liquid, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin 'fumare' (to smoke), from 'fumus' (smoke).

2. VERB

/ fjuːm / (AmE), / fjuːm / (BrE)
to treat or expose something to fumes, especially to disinfect or eliminate pests
treat with fumes (disinfecting/pest control)

Examples

To control the infestation, workers will fume the greenhouse with a special gas.
Some museums fume ancient artifacts to protect them from insect damage.
They fumed the storage area overnight to eradicate all pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fumigate - to disinfect or purify by exposure to smoke or gas

Etymology

From Latin 'fumare' (to smoke), from 'fumus' (smoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02131565-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ fjuːm / (AmE), / fjuːm / (BrE)
a cloud of fine particles or vapor suspended in air, often with a strong or unpleasant smell
cloud of fine particles or vapor in air

Examples

Car exhaust releases dangerous fumes into the atmosphere.
She coughed as the chemical fumes filled the laboratory.
It is important to ventilate the area to prevent a buildup of toxic fume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke - visible suspension of carbon particles in air from burning
  • Synonym: vapor - substance diffused or suspended in the air, especially as a gas or mist

Etymology

From Old French 'fum' and Latin 'fumus' meaning 'smoke'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11528510-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ fjuːm / (AmE), / fjuːm / (BrE)
to feel or show intense anger, especially without expressing it openly
to be visibly angry

Examples

She continued to fume long after the argument had ended.
He was fuming silently while everyone else laughed about the mistake.
Karen fumed over the unfair treatment for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed anger
  • Degree Higher: rage - to feel or express violent uncontrollable anger
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'fumare' (to smoke), via Old French 'fumer'. Originally meaning 'to emit smoke', later extended metaphorically to anger.
