lavish

1. VERB

/ ˈlævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃ / (BrE)
to give or spend something in great quantities or without restraint
give or spend generously

Examples

She lavished affection on her grandchildren every time she visited.
The critics lavished praise on the director's new film.
They lavished money on the wedding, sparing no expense.

Synset Examples

He was showered with praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heap - to give or accumulate in large amounts
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *lavasse* (deluge), from Latin *lavare* (to wash).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃ / (BrE)
rich, elaborate, or luxurious to an extravagant degree; characterized by great abundance or generosity
extravagant; richly abundant

Examples

The wedding reception featured a lavish display of flowers and gourmet food.
They spent a lavish amount of money renovating their home.
Her birthday party was a lavish affair, complete with live music and fireworks.

Synset Examples

a lavish buffet
a lucullan feast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially in a way that involves great expense
  • Synonym: sumptuous - splendid and expensive-looking; involving great expense
  • Antonym: frugal - sparing or economical with regard to money or food

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle French 'lavasse' (torrent of rain), from 'laver' (to wash), influenced by notion of abundance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02032677-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈlævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃ / (BrE)
giving or showing great generosity; marked by abundance or excess in giving or spending.
very generous; abundant

Examples

The guests were impressed by the lavish decorations at the wedding.
He received lavish praise from his teachers for his hard work.
They donated a lavish amount of money to the charity last year.

lavish praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Middle English *lavasse* (torrent of rain), from Old French, from laver (to wash), influenced by sense of abundance or excess.
