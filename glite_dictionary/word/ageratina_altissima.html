Ageratina altissima

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈreɪtɪnə ælˈtɪsɪmə / (AmE), / ˌædʒəˈreɪtɪnə ælˈtɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A North American herbaceous plant with flat-topped clusters of small white flowers, known as a cause of milk sickness.
a North American herbaceous plant, white flowers

Examples

Cows that grazed on Ageratina altissima often developed symptoms of milk sickness.
Botanists identified the mysterious plant as Ageratina altissima after careful examination.
The presence of Ageratina altissima in pastures led to several health concerns among local farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white snakeroot - common name for Ageratina altissima, a poisonous North American herb
  • Synonym: Eupatorium rugosum - former scientific name for Ageratina altissima

Etymology

From Latin; 'Ageratina' from Greek 'ageratos' (un-aging), referencing the lasting color of the flowers, and 'altissima' meaning 'very tall' in Latin.
