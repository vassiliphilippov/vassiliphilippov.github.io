cryesthesia

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈɛs.θi.ə / (AmE), / kraɪˈɛs.θi.ə / (BrE)
Abnormal sensitivity to cold, especially as a symptom of certain medical conditions.
abnormal sensitivity to cold
medical

Examples

The patient reported intense discomfort due to cryesthesia when exposed to even mildly cold air.
After nerve damage, some individuals may develop cryesthesia, making winter months especially difficult.
Doctors observed cryesthesia in several patients following chemotherapy treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermoesthesia - sensitivity to temperature changes

Etymology

From Greek kryos (frost, cold) + aisthēsis (sensation).
