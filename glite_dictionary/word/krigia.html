Krigia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˈkrɪdʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of small herbaceous plants in the daisy family, closely related to chicory, commonly known as dwarf dandelions.
genus of small herbs (botany)

Examples

Several species within the Krigia genus are native to North America.
The botanist identified the small yellow flower as a member of the Krigia genus.
Gardeners sometimes plant Krigia for its delicate, dandelion-like flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - A common flowering plant with yellow blooms
  • Synonym: chicory - A plant related to dandelion, often with blue flowers

Etymology

Named after David Krieg, 18th-century American botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˈkrɪdʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of small, branched, yellow-flowered North American herbs in the family Asteraceae.
genus of yellow-flowered North American herbs

Examples

We spotted a patch of Krigia blooming in the meadow.
The botanist collected several Krigia specimens during his fieldwork.
Krigia is often confused with other yellow-flowered wildflowers.

Etymology

Named after David Krieg, a German botanist.
