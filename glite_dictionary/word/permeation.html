permeation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɝːmiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːmiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of a substance gradually passing through or spreading throughout something
the process of passing through or spreading

Examples

The permeation of water into the concrete caused it to weaken over time.
The scientist tracked the permeation of chemicals through the soil layers.
Cultural permeation across borders can influence language and tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the spreading of something more widely

Etymology

From Latin 'permeatio', from 'permeare' (to pass through).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɜr.miˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.miˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which one substance spreads through or penetrates another, often involving mutual diffusion
mutual diffusion or spreading through

Examples

The permeation of water through the soil helps nourish the plant roots.
Cultural permeation can lead to significant changes within a community over time.
The scientist studied the permeation of gas molecules through porous materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration
  • Synonym: penetration - the act of passing or forcing through something

Etymology

From Latin 'permeatio', from 'permeare' meaning 'to pass through'.
