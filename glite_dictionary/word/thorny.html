thorny

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɔrni / (AmE), / ˈθɔːni / (BrE)
having or covered with thorns, barbs, spines, or other sharp projections
covered with thorns or sharp points

Examples

The thorny branches scratched my arms as I walked through the woods.
They had to clear a thorny bush from the front yard before planting the flowers.
Some animals avoid eating thorny plants because of their sharp spines.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - having many spines or thorns
  • Synonym: prickly - covered with prickles or sharp points

Etymology

From Old English 'þorn' (thorn) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɔrni / (AmE), / ˈθɔːni / (BrE)
(of a problem or situation) full of difficulties or complications; causing problems or debate.
difficult, complicated (problem or issue)

Examples

The committee struggled with the thorny issue of how to allocate funding fairly.
She avoided answering the thorny questions raised during the interview.
Despite his experience, he found himself challenged by the thorny legal complexities of the case.

Synset Examples

the thorny question of states' rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficult - not easy; requiring great effort
  • Antonym: simple - easy to understand or deal with

Etymology

From Old English *þornig*, from *þorn* (thorn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00752287-s
Synset POS: s
