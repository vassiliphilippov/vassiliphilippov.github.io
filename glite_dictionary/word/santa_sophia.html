Santa Sophia

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɑntə soʊˈfiə, ˌsɑntə səˈfiə / (AmE), / ˌsæntə səʊˈfiːə / (BrE)
a historic building in Istanbul, originally built as a Christian church in the 6th century, later converted into a mosque, and now functioning as a museum; celebrated as a masterpiece of Byzantine architecture.
historic landmark in Istanbul; former church and mosque

Examples

Millions of tourists visit Santa Sophia each year to admire its impressive domed structure.
Santa Sophia has served as a church, a mosque, and a museum throughout its long history.
The mosaics inside Santa Sophia are considered outstanding examples of Byzantine art.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Sancta Sophia (Holy Wisdom), referring to the Greek Hagia Sophia.
