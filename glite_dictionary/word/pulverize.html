to pulverize

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌlvəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌlvəraɪz / (BrE)
to destroy completely; to reduce to nothing or ruin utterly
destroy completely

Examples

The tornado managed to pulverize several homes in the village.
The old computer was so broken that a single blow would pulverize it.
The critics pulverized the new movie, leaving it with terrible reviews.

Synset Examples

the wrecking ball demolished the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy utterly; to obliterate
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something safe from harm or destruction

Etymology

From Latin 'pulverizare', from 'pulvis' (dust).

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌlvəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌlvəraɪz / (BrE)
to be reduced to powder or dust; to become completely broken down into small particles
become powder or dust

Examples

In extremely dry conditions, rocks can pulverize naturally over centuries.
The old plaster began to pulverize as soon as we touched it.
If allowed to age long enough, the bread will eventually pulverize into crumbs.

Synset Examples

When it was blown up, the building powderized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press or squeeze something so hard that it is damaged or loses its shape
  • Synonym: grind - to reduce something to small particles or powder by crushing it

Etymology

From Late Latin pulverizare (to reduce to dust), from Latin pulvis (dust).

3. VERB

/ ˈpʌlvəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌlvəraɪz / (BrE)
to crush or grind something into a fine powder or dust
crush into powder or dust

Examples

The machine can easily pulverize rocks into fine dust.
Chefs use a blender to pulverize the vegetables for the soup.
After years underground, the bones had completely pulverized and mixed with the soil.
