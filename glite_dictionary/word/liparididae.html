Liparididae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌpɛrɪˈdaɪdiː / (AmE), / lɪˌparɪˈdiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as snailfishes, characterized by soft, scaleless bodies and commonly found in cold or deep ocean waters.
family of marine fishes (snailfishes)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Liparididae in the deep Pacific Ocean.
Members of the Liparididae are often found at depths greater than 1000 meters.
The unique physiology of Liparididae allows them to thrive in extremely cold marine environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'liparos' meaning 'fat' or 'oily', plus -idae, a zoological suffix for animal families.
