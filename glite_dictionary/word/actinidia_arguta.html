Actinidia arguta

1. NOUN

/ ækˌtɪnɪdiə ɑrˈɡjutə / (AmE), / ækˌtɪnɪdiːə ɑːˈɡjuːtə / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to East Asia, known for its long finely serrated leaves, clusters of white flowers, and edible greenish-yellow fruit; also called hardy kiwi.
species of climbing vine; hardy kiwi

Examples

The Actinidia arguta vine produces small, smooth-skinned fruits that are sweet and edible.
Gardeners often plant Actinidia arguta for its cold-hardy, high-yielding kiwis.
Researchers are studying Actinidia arguta for its potential health benefits and unique taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardy kiwi - common name for Actinidia arguta, a species of vine with edible fruit

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Actinidia + species epithet arguta (Latin for 'sharp' or 'pointed').
