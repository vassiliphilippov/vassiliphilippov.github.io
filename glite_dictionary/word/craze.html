craze

1. NOUN

/ kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
A fine crack on the surface of a glaze, ceramic, or similar material, often forming a network of lines.
fine crack in a glaze or surface

Examples

The antique vase displayed a beautiful craze pattern across its surface.
Exposure to moisture can cause a craze to develop in ceramic glazes over time.
Light caught the network of craze lines on the pottery, highlighting its age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a break or fissure in a surface

2. VERB

/ kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
(of a surface) to develop a fine network of cracks, especially as a result of aging or improper firing; often used in reference to ceramics or glaze.
develop fine cracks (esp. ceramics)

Examples

The antique vase began to craze after years of display in the sunlight.
Improper cooling can cause a ceramic glaze to craze unexpectedly.
Some collectors value pottery that has started to craze because of its unique appearance.

Synset Examples

Crazed ceramics

3. NOUN

/ kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
An enthusiasm for something that becomes extremely popular for a short time.
sudden, intense popular trend

Examples

The hula-hoop was a worldwide craze in the 1950s.
Social media challenges have quickly become the latest craze among teenagers.
The Pokémon Go craze got everyone outside, searching for virtual creatures.

Synset Examples

he always follows the latest fads
it was all the rage that season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fad - an intense and widely shared enthusiasm for something, especially one that is short-lived
  • Synonym: mania - an excessive enthusiasm or desire; an obsession
  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • Antonym: classic - something of lasting worth or timeless quality, especially as opposed to something that is a passing craze

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'crasen' (to shatter, break), of Scandinavian origin.

4. VERB

/ kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
to make (someone) insane or very agitated; to cause to lose one's mind
to make someone insane or frantic

Examples

The endless noise would craze even the calmest person after a while.
Jealousy can craze a person if left unchecked.
The horrifying experience nearly crazed her with fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to madden - to make someone very angry or insane
  • Synonym: to unhinge - to make someone mentally unstable

Etymology

From Middle English crasen (to crush, shatter), probably of Scandinavian origin.
