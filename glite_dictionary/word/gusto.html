gusto

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌstoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌstəʊ / (BrE)
vigorous and enthusiastic enjoyment or appreciation
enthusiastic enjoyment

Examples

She attacked the project with such gusto that everyone was inspired by her energy.
The band played their encore with gusto, leaving the audience cheering for more.
He eats his meals with gusto, savoring every bite as if it were a feast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Italian 'gusto' (taste, pleasure), from Latin 'gustus' (taste).
