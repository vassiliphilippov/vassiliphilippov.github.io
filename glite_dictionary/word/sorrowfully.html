sorrowfully

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑːroʊfəli / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊfəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or expresses sadness or regret.
in a sad or regretful manner

Examples

She sorrowfully accepted the news that her friend was moving away.
He gazed sorrowfully at the old photographs, lost in memories of the past.
They spoke sorrowfully about the accident that had changed their lives.

Synset Examples

his mother looked at him dolefully when he told her he had joined the Army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadly - in a manner that shows unhappiness or sorrow
  • Antonym: joyfully - in a way that shows happiness or joy

Etymology

From 'sorrowful' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɑːroʊfəli / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊfəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or expresses deep sadness or regret.
in a sad or regretful manner

Examples

She looked sorrowfully at the broken vase on the floor.
He spoke sorrowfully about his childhood experiences.
The child waved sorrowfully as the train pulled away from the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadly - in a sad manner
  • Antonym: joyfully - in a joyful manner

Etymology

From 'sorrowful' + '-ly', where 'sorrowful' is from Old English 'sorgfull'.
