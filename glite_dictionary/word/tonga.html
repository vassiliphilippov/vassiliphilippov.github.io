Tonga

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋɡə / (BrE)
the Bantu language spoken by the Tonga people of southern Zambia and northern Zimbabwe in south central Africa.
Bantu language of southern Zambia and northern Zimbabwe

Examples

The villagers spoke Tonga as their first language.
He is learning Tonga to communicate with the local community in Zambia.
Several books have been translated into Tonga to support literacy efforts.

2. PROPN

/ ˈtɑŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋɡə / (BrE)
a country consisting of a group of islands in Polynesia, in the South Pacific Ocean; a monarchy that gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1970.
country in Polynesia, South Pacific

Examples

The King of Tonga visited New Zealand in 2015.
Tourists flock to Tonga for its pristine beaches and vibrant marine life.
After achieving independence, Tonga maintained a unique monarchy in the Pacific.

Etymology

From Tongan (autonym: Tonga), the local name for the islands and people.
