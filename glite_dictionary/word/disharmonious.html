disharmonious

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˌhɑrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / dɪsˌhɑːˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
Not having or showing harmony; lacking agreement or concord.
lacking harmony; discordant

Examples

The disharmonious sounds from the orchestra made the piece difficult to listen to.
His disharmonious relationship with his colleagues affected the team's performance.
The colors chosen for the room were disharmonious and made the space feel chaotic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmonious - having harmony; being in agreement
  • Synonym: discordant - harsh or jarring because of lack of harmony

Etymology

Formed from 'dis-' (expressing negation) and 'harmonious', from Greek 'harmonia' (agreement, harmony).
