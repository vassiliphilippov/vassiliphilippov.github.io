zany

1. NOUN

/ ˈzeɪni / (AmE), / ˈzeɪni / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a ludicrous, comical, or foolish way; a clown or buffoon
foolish or clownish person

Examples

The party was filled with laughter thanks to a zany who dressed up in a chicken suit.
In every royal court, there was a zany to entertain the nobility with jokes and antics.
Although some called him a zany, his foolishness often brought a smile to everyone's face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - comic entertainer or a foolish person
  • Synonym: buffoon - someone who amuses others by ridiculous behavior

Etymology

From Italian *zanni*, a stock comic servant in commedia dell'arte, from Venetian dialect Zani (a variant of Giovanni, equivalent to John).

2. NOUN

/ ˈzeɪni / (AmE), / ˈzeɪni / (BrE)
a comic performer or clown, especially a buffoon in old comedies who imitates others for ludicrous effect
comic performer; buffoon

Examples

The old play featured a zany who kept the audience laughing with his antics.
As the troupe’s zany, he always imitated the other actors for comic effect.
Harlequin and his zany delighted everyone at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - comic entertainer who performs tricks or jokes
  • Synonym: buffoon - a ridiculous but amusing person; a clown

Etymology

From Italian *zani*, a dialectal variant of *Gianni* (Johnny), a stock comic servant in commedia dell’arte.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
