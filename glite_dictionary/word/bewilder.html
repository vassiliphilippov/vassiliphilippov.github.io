to bewilder

1. VERB

/ bɪˈwɪldər / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪldə / (BrE)
to confuse someone greatly, making them unable to understand or think clearly
confuse greatly; puzzle

Examples

The complex instructions bewildered the students, leaving them unsure of what to do next.
Her sudden change in attitude completely bewildered him.
The strange customs of the new country can bewilder visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make (someone) unable to think clearly or understand something
  • Synonym: to perplex - to make (someone) feel completely baffled
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject

Etymology

From Middle English, possibly from be- (intensive prefix) + obsolete wilder 'to lead astray'.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈwɪldər / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪldə / (BrE)
to cause someone to become confused or puzzled; to perplex
to confuse or puzzle

Examples

The complex instructions bewildered the new employees.
Her sudden change of attitude completely bewildered him.
The wide variety of choices can bewilder first-time visitors.

Synset Examples

This beats me!

a vexing problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make unclear or difficult to understand
  • Synonym: to perplex - to make someone feel baffled or puzzled
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to make someone understand clearly

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + wilder (to lead astray), from Old English wildor (wild animal).
