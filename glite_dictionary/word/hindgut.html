hindgut

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪndˌɡʌt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪndˌɡʌt / (BrE)
the posterior part of the digestive tract in vertebrate embryos, developing into the colon and rectum
posterior part of embryonic digestive tract
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the hindgut eventually forms the colon and rectum.
Scientists observed changes in the hindgut cells during the third week of gestation.
Damage to the hindgut in early development can lead to congenital digestive disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foregut - anterior part of the embryonic digestive tract
  • Antonym: midgut - middle portion of the embryonic digestive tract

Etymology

Formed from 'hind' (back) + 'gut' (intestine), indicating the rear part of the gut.
