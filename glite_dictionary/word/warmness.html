warmness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːm.nəs / (BrE)
a feeling of kindness, affection, or heartfelt friendliness toward someone or something
affectionate kindness or friendliness

Examples

Her warmness put the nervous guests instantly at ease.
Despite his fame, there was a genuine warmness in his smile.
I was moved by the warmness of their welcome.

Synset Examples

he had trouble expressing the affection he felt
the child won everyone's heart
the warmness of his welcome made us feel right at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warmth - a quality of being emotionally warm, affectionate, or friendly
  • Antonym: coldness - a lack of affection or friendliness; emotional distance

Etymology

Formed from the adjective "warm" + suffix "-ness."

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːm.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being warm; moderate heat.
the quality of being warm

Examples

The warmness of the sun made the afternoon pleasant for everyone in the park.
She appreciated the warmness of the cozy living room on a cold day.
Despite the winter outside, there was a gentle warmness inside the old cabin.

Synset Examples

an agreeable warmth in the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warmth - the quality or state of being warm

Etymology

Formed from "warm" + "-ness" (noun suffix).
