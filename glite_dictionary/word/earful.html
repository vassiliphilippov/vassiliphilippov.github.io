earful

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈɪə.fəl / (BrE)
a strong or angry verbal reprimand; a severe scolding
a severe verbal scolding
informal

Examples

After the meeting, the boss gave him an earful about his poor performance.
If you come home late again, you’ll get an earful from your mother.
She got an earful from her coach after missing practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telling-off - an act of speaking angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈɪə.fʊl / (BrE)
a great deal of talk, especially gossip or a lengthy complaint
a lot of gossip or complaint
informal

Examples

She gave me an earful about all the office drama while we were having lunch.
Neighbors often get an earful from her whenever something goes wrong on the street.
After the meeting, he got an earful of gossip from his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism
  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often involving details that are not confirmed as true

Etymology

From the noun 'ear' + 'ful', meaning as much as the ear can take in.
