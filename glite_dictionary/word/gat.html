gat

1. NOUN

/ ɡæt / (AmE), / ɡat / (BrE)
(slang) A pistol or revolver, especially as used by criminals or gangsters.
slang for a pistol or gun
slang american

Examples

The gangster pulled his gat from under his coat and pointed it at the door.
He bragged about always carrying a gat for protection on the streets.
In classic crime movies, a detective might warn, 'Watch out, he's got a gat.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gun - a weapon that shoots bullets or shells
  • Synonym: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand

Etymology

American slang, early 20th century, from Gatling (gun), type of early rapid-fire gun.
