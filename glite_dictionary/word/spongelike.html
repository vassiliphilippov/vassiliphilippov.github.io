spongelike

1. ADJ

/ ˈspʌndʒ.laɪk / (AmE), / ˈspʌn.dʒlaɪk / (BrE)
having a soft, porous texture and compressibility similar to that of a sponge; easily squashed or deformed
resembling a sponge in texture or compressibility

Examples

The spongelike bread absorbed the soup quickly.
She noticed a spongelike texture in the old, damp carpet.
The material is spongelike, making it ideal for cushioning delicate items.

Synset Examples

spongy bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spongy - having the characteristics of a sponge; soft and porous
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈspʌndʒˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈspʌndʒˌlaɪk / (BrE)
having qualities similar to a sponge, especially in absorbing liquids and releasing them when compressed
able to absorb and release liquid like a sponge

Examples

The spongelike material soaked up the spilled water quickly.
Her hair had a spongelike texture after the rain, absorbing moisture from the air.
Doctors used a spongelike dressing to draw fluid from the wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porous - having small holes that allow liquid or air to pass through
