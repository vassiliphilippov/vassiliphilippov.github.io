flinders

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪndɚz / (AmE), / ˈflɪndəz / (BrE)
Small pieces, fragments, or splinters, especially as a result of something being smashed or broken.
small fragments; splinters

Examples

The vase fell from the shelf and shattered into flinders all over the floor.
A loud crash echoed as the ancient wood broke to flinders.
He struck the rock with his hammer, sending it flying apart in flinders.

Synset Examples

it would have shattered in flinders long before it did that damage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragments - small broken pieces of something
  • Synonym: splinters - thin, sharp pieces broken off from a larger object

Etymology

Probably from Scots or northern English dialect; origin uncertain.
