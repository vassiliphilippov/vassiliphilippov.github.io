plugged

1. ADJ

/ plʌɡd / (AmE), / plʌɡd / (BrE)
(of a coin) altered by inserting a piece of base metal, typically to replace valuable material and reduce its intrinsic value.
coin with base metal inserted

Examples

The collector discovered that the old silver dollar was actually plugged and less valuable than it appeared.
In the nineteenth century, it was not uncommon to find plugged coins in circulation.
A careful examination showed that the gold piece had been plugged with a small disc of a cheaper metal.

2. ADJ

/ plʌɡd / (AmE), / plʌɡd / (BrE)
completely blocked or obstructed so that nothing can pass through.
blocked or obstructed

Examples

The kitchen sink was plugged and wouldn’t drain properly.
My nose is so plugged that I can hardly breathe.
After the pipe burst, the main sewer became plugged with debris.

Synset Examples

the storm was responsible for many blocked roads and bridges
the drain was plugged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - completely obstructed or blocked
  • Antonym: unplugged - not blocked or not obstructed

Etymology

From the verb 'plug' (to block an opening), originally from Middle English 'plugge', of uncertain origin.
