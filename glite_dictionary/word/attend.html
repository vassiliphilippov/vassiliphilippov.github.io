to attend

1. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to pay attention to or heed; to listen or watch carefully
pay attention to; heed

Examples

Please attend to the instructions I am about to give.
The students attend closely when the teacher explains a new concept.
She failed to attend to the warnings and made a costly mistake.

Synset Examples

The children in the audience attended the recital quietly
She hung on his every word
They attended to everything he said

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heed - to pay careful attention to something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin attendere (to give heed to), from ad- (to) + tendere (stretch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02174871-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to deal with or take charge of something; to give one's attention to something that needs action
to deal with; take care of

Examples

I have to attend to some urgent emails before I can leave.
The doctor will attend to you in just a moment.
She asked her assistant to attend to the arrangements for the conference.

Synset Examples

Could you see about lunch?
I must attend to this matter
She took care of this business

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something; not deal with it
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or deal with something properly

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' (to give heed to, wait upon), from 'ad-' (to) + 'tendere' (to stretch)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02555278-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to be present at an event, meeting, or place, especially regularly or as a duty
to be present at (an event or place)

Examples

All employees are required to attend the weekly staff meeting.
She hopes to attend the conference in Paris next month.
He has promised to attend every class this semester.

Synset Examples

She attends class regularly
I rarely attend services at my church
did you go to the meeting?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miss - to fail to be present at something
  • Synonym: to participate - to take part in an activity or event

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' meaning 'to give heed to' or 'to stretch toward'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02618797-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
(of an event or state) to be accompanied by another event or circumstance, or to happen as a result of something else.
be accompanied by, or follow as a result

Examples

The celebrations were attended by an air of excitement.
His resignation was attended by a series of protests throughout the city.
Severe headaches often attend this kind of illness.

Synset Examples

Menuhin's playing was attended by a 15-minute standing ovation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accompany - to go along with, to be present with something else
  • Synonym: to follow - to come after as a result or consequence

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' meaning 'to give heed to, to stretch toward'.

5. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to act as a servant or helper to someone, especially as part of one's duties
act as a servant or helper

Examples

The butler was hired to attend the elderly gentleman during his stay.
She attended the queen as her personal maid for several years.
A nurse was assigned to attend the patient at all times.

Synset Examples

May I serve you?
She attends the old lady in the wheelchair
Can you wait on our table, please?
Is a salesperson assisting you?
The minister served the King for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to perform duties or services for another person
  • Synonym: to wait on - to act as a servant; to serve food or attend

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' (to give heed to, to wait upon).
