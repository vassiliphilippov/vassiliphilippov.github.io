James Clark Ross

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz klɑrk rɑs / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz klɑːk rɒs / (BrE)
(1800–1862) British explorer known for his expeditions to the Arctic and Antarctic, including locating the north magnetic pole and discovering the Ross Sea.
British Arctic and Antarctic explorer (1800–1862)

Examples

James Clark Ross is celebrated for his achievements in polar exploration, including the discovery of the Ross Sea.
During his expedition, James Clark Ross successfully located the north magnetic pole in 1831.
The legacy of James Clark Ross is commemorated by several geographic features named after him in Antarctica.
