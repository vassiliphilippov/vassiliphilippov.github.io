allomerous

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɑːmərəs / (AmE), / əˈlɒmərəs / (BrE)
Relating to or exhibiting allomerism, especially in reference to chemical compounds with the same elements but different molecular structures.
relating to allomerism in chemistry
medical

Examples

The study focused on allomerous compounds and their varying crystalline structures.
Two substances may be allomerous if they share the same elements yet form different molecules.
Researchers identified an allomerous relationship between several inorganic salts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allomerism - The property of having the same chemical composition but different molecular structure

Etymology

From 'allomerism', combining Greek 'allo-' (other) and 'meros' (part).
