nudnick

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌdnɪk / (AmE), / ˈnʌdnɪk / (BrE)
A person who is a persistent and boring nuisance or pest.
boring, persistent pest

Examples

Stop being such a nudnick—you've asked me the same question three times already!
Everyone in the office thinks Jeremy is a bit of a nudnick because he never stops complaining about minor issues.
At family gatherings, Uncle Saul has a reputation for being a nudnick with his endless stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pest - An annoying or troublesome person

Etymology

From Yiddish 'nudnik', from Russian 'nudnyi' (tedious, boring).
