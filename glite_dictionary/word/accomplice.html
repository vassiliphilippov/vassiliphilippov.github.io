accomplice

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɑːmplɪs / (AmE), / əˈkʌmplɪs / (BrE)
a person who helps another to commit a crime or do something wrong
person who helps commit a crime

Examples

The police arrested the mastermind and his accomplice after the bank robbery.
She claimed she had no idea she was acting as an accomplice to fraud.
He was found guilty as an accomplice in the smuggling operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - person who assists in committing a crime but is not the main perpetrator
  • Antonym: principal - main person who commits a crime

Etymology

From Old French 'complice', from Late Latin 'complicis', variant of 'complex' (accomplice, partner).
