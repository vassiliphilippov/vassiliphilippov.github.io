Leucaena leucocephala

1. NOUN

/ luːˈsiːnə ˌluːkoʊsɪˈfɑːlə / (AmE), / luːˈsiːnə ˌluːkɪˌsiːˈfɑːlə / (BrE)
A species of low, scrubby tree native to tropical and subtropical North America, with white or yellow-tinged flowers resembling those of the mimosa and long, flattened seed pods.
tree native to tropical America with mimosa-like flowers

Examples

The rapid growth of Leucaena leucocephala makes it popular for reforestation projects in tropical countries.
Farmers often plant Leucaena leucocephala as a source of fodder for livestock.
Many gardens use Leucaena leucocephala for its attractive, mimosa-like flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Leucaena, from Greek 'leukos' (white), and the species name leucocephala from Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'kephalē' (head), referring to the flower clusters.
