regularisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of making something regular or more regular, often by making its rules or system consistent or orderly
process of making regular or orderly
british

Examples

The regularisation of building permits was necessary to ensure safety standards were met.
The city's new policy led to the regularisation of hundreds of informal street vendors.
Her research focused on the regularisation of irregular verbs in the English language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regularization - American spelling of 'regularisation'

Etymology

From regular + -isation, modeled on Latin and French forms; equivalent to American 'regularization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjələrəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something regular or uniform; bringing something into conformity with a rule or standard
process of making regular or uniform
british

Examples

The government announced the regularisation of informal settlements in the city.
Recent regularisation of company policies has improved workflow efficiency.
Language development often involves the regularisation of irregular verb forms over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regularization - the act of bringing to uniformity; making regular (American spelling)

Etymology

From regular + -isation, following the British spelling convention; compare American 'regularization'.
