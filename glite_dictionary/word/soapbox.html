soapbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊp.bɑks / (AmE), / ˈsəʊp.bɒks / (BrE)
a sturdy box or crate originally used for shipping bars of soap
wooden crate for shipping soap

Examples

The old factory was filled with empty soapboxes stacked against the walls.
He stood on a wooden soapbox to reach the high shelf in the garage.
At the antique market, we saw a vintage soapbox used for display.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌbɒks / (BrE)
a raised platform, often improvised, on which someone stands to address an audience and attract attention, especially to express opinions publicly
raised platform for public speaking

Examples

The politician stood on a soapbox in the park to address the crowd.
She never misses an opportunity to climb onto her soapbox and advocate for animal rights.
Street speakers would use a soapbox to make sure everyone could see and hear them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: podium - a small platform on which a person stands to be seen by an audience
  • Synonym: lectern - a tall stand with a sloping top to hold notes or books for speakers

Etymology

Originally, improvised speaking platforms were made from wooden boxes used to transport soap.
