polymorphous

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪmɔr.fəs / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to crystallize in two or more distinct forms.
crystallizing in different forms

Examples

The mineral's polymorphous structure results in different physical properties.
Researchers discovered that the compound could undergo polymorphous crystallization under varying conditions.
A polymorphous material may have different melting points depending on its form.

Synset Examples

polymorphous crystallization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymorphic - having various forms, especially with reference to chemical structures

Etymology

From Greek polumorphos, from 'poly-' (many) + 'morphē' (form).

having or existing in various distinct forms; able to assume several different forms or shapes.
having many forms

Examples

The story featured a polymorphous creature that could change its appearance at will.
Modern art often explores polymorphous identities and abstract forms.

Related Terms

3. ADJ

/ ˌpɑː.liˈmɔːr.fəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.lɪˈmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
(of a species or population) existing in or having several different forms or types, not limited by sex differences.
having many forms (biology, science)

Examples

Some butterflies are polymorphous, with individuals showing distinctly different wing patterns.
The polymorphous nature of the fungus allows it to adapt to a wide range of environments.
Researchers studied a polymorphous species of lizard that included multiple color variations within the same population.

Synset Examples

a polymorphic species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymorphic - having or occurring in several different forms (biology)

Etymology

From Greek polumorphos: 'poly-' (many) + 'morphē' (form).
