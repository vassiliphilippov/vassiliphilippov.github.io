biotypic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈtɪp.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əˈtɪp.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a biotype, especially in biological classification or study
relating to a biotype (biology)
medical

Examples

The biotypic classification of mosquitoes helps track patterns in disease transmission.
Researchers identified several biotypic variations in the insect population.
The study focused on the biotypic diversity present in wild plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biotype - a group of organisms sharing the same genotype

Etymology

From 'biotype' (from Greek 'bios' meaning life + 'typos' meaning type) + '-ic'.
