to territorialise

1. VERB

/ təˌrɪtɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / təˌrɪtəriəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize, arrange, or place on a territorial basis, especially with reference to administrative divisions or control
establish on a territorial basis

Examples

The government decided to territorialise the education system to reflect local needs.
Several public services have been territorialised to improve efficiency and accountability.
Efforts to territorialise healthcare provision have met with mixed success across different regions.

Synset Examples

The railways were territorialized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to territorialize - American English spelling of 'territorialise'

Etymology

From territory +‎ -ise, after French territorialiser or derived from territory.

2. VERB

/ ˌtɛrɪˈtɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize or treat an area as a territory, especially by defining its boundaries or establishing administrative control
to organize as a territory
british

Examples

The new government moved quickly to territorialise the region, marking new boundaries and setting up local administrations.
Colonial powers sought to territorialise vast areas of Africa in the late nineteenth century.
Efforts to territorialise cyberspace pose significant challenges due to its borderless nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to territorialize - to organize as a territory (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'territory' + '-ise', patterned after French 'territorialiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02437822-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌtɛrɪˈtɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to extend by acquiring or adding territory to a domain or country
extend by adding territory

Examples

The nation sought to territorialise the neighboring islands in pursuit of economic expansion.
Empires throughout history often attempted to territorialise new lands to increase their influence.
Some colonial powers tried to territorialise indigenous regions, often without regard for existing populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annex - to add territory to one's own territory

Etymology

From territory + -ise, modeled after French 'territorialiser'.
