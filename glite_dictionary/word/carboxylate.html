to carboxylate

1. VERB

/ kɑrˈbɑksəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒksɪleɪt / (BrE)
to treat or react a chemical compound with a carboxyl group or carboxylic acid, typically to introduce a carboxyl group into the molecule
treat with carboxyl or carboxylic acid (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The scientists decided to carboxylate the compound to improve its solubility.
In the laboratory, chemists often carboxylate aromatic rings during synthesis.
The reaction was monitored carefully as the solution began carboxylating the substrate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decarboxylate - to remove a carboxyl group from a molecule

Etymology

Formed from 'carboxyl' + '-ate' (verb-forming suffix). Ultimately from 'carboxylic acid'.
