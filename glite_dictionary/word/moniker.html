moniker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪkə / (BrE)
a name or nickname, especially a familiar or informal one used to refer to a person
a person's name or nickname

Examples

He adopted the moniker 'The Shadow' during his time as a stage performer.
Her witty online moniker quickly became well-known among forum members.
The artist is better known by his moniker than his real name.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - an informal, often affectionate, name for a person

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly from Shelta or related to the word 'monica' (archaic slang for a name).
