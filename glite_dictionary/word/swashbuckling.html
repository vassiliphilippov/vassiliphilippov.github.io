swashbuckling

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑʃˌbʌklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɒʃˌbʌklɪŋ / (BrE)
bold, adventurous, and often recklessly daring behavior, especially in a flamboyant or showy manner.
adventurous, flamboyant daring behavior

Examples

The film is filled with swashbuckling, sword fights, and daring sea escapes.
He admired the swashbuckling of old-time explorers who braved the unknown.
Despite the risks, her swashbuckling in business made headlines across the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravado - a bold manner intended to impress or intimidate
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery

Etymology

From 'swashbuckler' (a swaggering or daring adventurer), itself from 'swash' (swagger, bluster) + 'buckler' (a small shield).

2. ADJ

/ ˈswɑʃˌbʌklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɒʃˌbʌklɪŋ / (BrE)
engaging in daring, adventurous, and flamboyant behavior, often in the style of a heroic swordsman or adventurer
daring and flamboyantly adventurous

Examples

The actor was known for playing swashbuckling heroes in countless adventure films.
Her swashbuckling spirit led her to explore remote corners of the world.
With his swashbuckling charm, he captivated audiences wherever he went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventurous - willing to take risks or try out new methods, ideas, or experiences
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'swashbuckler' (a swaggering swordsman), from 'swash' (to swagger with a drawn sword) + 'buckler' (a small shield).
