Nikolai Lenin

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkoʊlaɪ ˈlɛnɪn / (AmE), / nɪˈkəʊlaɪ ˈlɛnɪn / (BrE)
The Russian revolutionary leader who founded the Bolsheviks, led the Russian Revolution, and served as the first head of the USSR (1870–1924).
Russian revolutionary and first Soviet leader

Examples

Many historians regard Nikolai Lenin as a key architect of the Soviet state.
The policies implemented by Nikolai Lenin reshaped the course of Russian history.
During the early years of the USSR, Nikolai Lenin was at the forefront of political change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lenin - Revolutionary leader, more commonly used form of the name

Etymology

Lenin is the revolutionary pseudonym of Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov, adopted from the River Lena in Siberia.
