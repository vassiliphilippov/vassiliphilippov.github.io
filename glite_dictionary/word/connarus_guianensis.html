Connarus guianensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɑːrəs ɡiˌænˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːrəs ɡiˌænˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A tropical American and East African tree with distinctive, strikingly marked hardwood, commonly used in cabinetwork.
tropical tree valued for fine hardwood

Examples

The wood of Connarus guianensis is highly prized for luxurious furniture and interior design.
Several botanical gardens showcase Connarus guianensis for its striking appearance and valuable timber.
Harvesting Connarus guianensis is regulated to protect its natural habitat and ensure sustainable use.

Etymology

From Latinized genus Connarus, after 'Guiana' (region in South America) and Latin '-ensis' (belonging to).
