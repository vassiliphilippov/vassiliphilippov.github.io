preclusion

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈkluʒən / (AmE), / prɪˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
the action of preventing something by anticipating and dealing with it in advance
act of preventing something in advance

Examples

The preclusion of errors is critical in safety protocols.
Legal preclusion prevents the same issue from being litigated twice.
His meticulous planning led to the preclusion of any major setbacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevention - the act of stopping something from happening
  • Antonym: allowance - the act of permitting or allowing something to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'praecludere' (to shut off, precede) via Late Latin 'praeclusio'.
