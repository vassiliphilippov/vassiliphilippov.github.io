pelt

1. VERB

/ pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall very heavily and forcefully.
rain heavily and forcefully

Examples

The rain began to pelt down just as we left the house.
We got soaked when it started to pelt on our way home.
Sheets of rain pelted against the window during the storm.

Synset Examples

Put on your rain coat — it's pouring outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour - to rain heavily and continuously
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02763991-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
the skin of an animal with the hair, wool, or fur still attached, especially after being removed and prepared for use
animal skin with hair or fur

Examples

The hunter carried a pelt of a wolf draped over his shoulder.
She bought a luxurious rug made from the pelt of a reindeer.
Traders once traveled long distances to exchange animal pelts for goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - the skin of a large animal, especially when tanned or dressed
  • Synonym: fur - the short, soft hair of certain animals, or the dressed skin with the hair left on

Etymology

From Middle English *pelt*, from Old French *pel*, from Latin *pellis* (skin, hide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14788240-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
to throw objects at someone or something repeatedly, usually with force
throw objects repeatedly at

Examples

Protesters began to pelt the police with stones during the demonstration.
Every winter, children pelt each other with snowballs in the park.
Angry fans pelted the football field with bottles and cans after the match.

Synset Examples

They pelted each other with snowballs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack persistently with bombs or other objects
  • Synonym: shower - to fall or throw in large quantities

Etymology

From Middle English pelten (to throw), possibly of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian dialect 'pelta' (to knock, beat).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
the skin of an animal, especially with the fur, wool, or hair still on it
animal skin with fur or wool

Examples

The hunter sold the fox's pelt at the market.
The luxurious pelt of the mink is highly valued in the fashion industry.
After molting, the animal's pelt appears soft and shiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - the skin of an animal, especially when tanned or dressed

Etymology

From Middle English *pelte*, from Old French *pelete*, diminutive of *pele* (skin), from Latin *pellis* (skin, hide).

5. VERB

/ pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
to attack or bombard (someone or something) repeatedly, especially by throwing things or by delivering rapid actions or questions
bombard with thrown objects or rapid actions

Examples

After the announcement, the crowd began to pelt the stage with tomatoes.
Reporters pelted the politician with questions as he left the building.
Children pelted each other with snowballs on their way home.

Synset Examples

pelt the speaker with questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack repeatedly, often with artillery or objects
  • Synonym: barrange - to subject to a rapid outpouring, such as of questions

Etymology

From Middle English 'pelten', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'pelt' meaning 'skin' (as to throw quickly).
