exoskeleton

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.soʊˈskɛl.ɪ.tən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊˈskel.ɪ.tən / (BrE)
an external rigid or hard structure that supports and protects the body of many invertebrate animals, such as insects, crustaceans, and some other groups
external body covering of animals

Examples

The crab's exoskeleton provides essential protection from predators and harsh environments.
As insects grow, they must shed their exoskeleton in a process called molting.
Some robots are designed with an external frame similar to an exoskeleton for added strength.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoskeleton - an internal skeleton or supporting framework in an animal

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + skeleton, from Greek skeletos (dried up, mummy).
