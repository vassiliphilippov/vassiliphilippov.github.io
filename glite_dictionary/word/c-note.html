c-note

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌnəʊt / (BrE)
a one-hundred-dollar bill in the United States (slang)
slang for US $100 bill
slang american

Examples

He pulled out a c-note to pay for dinner.
Winning the bet, she proudly flashed a crisp c-note.
The shoes cost a c-note, but they were worth it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundred-dollar bill - a banknote worth 100 US dollars

Etymology

From 'C', the Roman numeral for 100, plus 'note' (banknote).
