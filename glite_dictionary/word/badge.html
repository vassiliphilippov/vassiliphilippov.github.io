badge

1. NOUN

/ bædʒ / (AmE), / bædʒ / (BrE)
a mark or feature that is regarded as a sign of status, quality, or rank
symbol or sign of status or quality

Examples

Her accent became a badge of her upper-class upbringing.
For him, the scar was a badge of honor earned in battle.
Wearing expensive watches is sometimes used as a badge of success.

Synset Examples

wearing a tie was regarded as a badge of respectability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emblem - a thing serving as a symbolic representation, especially a distinctive badge of a nation, organization, or family
  • Synonym: symbol - a thing that represents or stands for something else, especially a material object representing something abstract
  • Synonym: mark - a visible indication or sign used for identification or to indicate status

Etymology

From Middle English bage, badge, possibly from Anglo-French bage, bague (ring, ornament).

2. NOUN

/ bædʒ / (AmE), / bædʒ / (BrE)
a small piece of metal, cloth, or plastic worn to show your membership, rank, or status in an organization
emblem showing rank or membership

Examples

Each employee must display their badge while on company property.
The police officer wore his badge proudly on his uniform.
She collected every conference badge she had ever received.

Synset Examples

they checked everyone's badge before letting them in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignia - official sign or emblem of rank, office, or membership
  • Synonym: medal - a metal disc or other object awarded as a prize or honor

Etymology

From Middle French *bague* (a sign, ring, ornament), possibly of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06895611-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbædʒ / (AmE), / ˈbadʒ / (BrE)
to put a badge on someone, or to provide someone with a badge as identification
to put a badge on someone

Examples

All employees must be badged before entering the restricted area.
Each visitor was badged at the reception desk upon arrival.
Contractors are required to badge new staff members immediately during onboarding.

Synset Examples

The workers here must be badged

Etymology

From Middle English bage, probably from Old French bage (badge, distinguishing mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01592790-v
Synset POS: v
