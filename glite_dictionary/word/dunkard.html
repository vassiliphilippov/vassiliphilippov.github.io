Dunkard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌŋ.kərd / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋ.kəd / (BrE)
A member of a Christian sect, particularly one of the German Baptist Brethren, known for practicing baptism by immersion.
member of a Christian sect practicing immersion baptism

Examples

The Dunkard community is known for its commitment to peaceful living and simplicity.
She attended a service with a group of Dunkards who welcomed her warmly.
Many Dunkard traditions have been preserved through centuries of careful observance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baptist - member of a Protestant Christian denomination practicing baptism by immersion

Etymology

From Pennsylvania German Dunker, from German tunken (to dip), referring to the practice of baptism by immersion.
