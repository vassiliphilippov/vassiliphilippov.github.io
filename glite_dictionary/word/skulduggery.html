skulduggery

1. NOUN

/ skʌlˈdʌɡəri / (AmE), / skʌlˈdʌɡəri / (BrE)
dishonest or deceitful behaviour; trickery or fraud, especially intended to deceive or gain an unfair advantage
deceitful or dishonest behaviour

Examples

The company's financial skulduggery went unnoticed for years before the scandal broke.
Voters are tired of political skulduggery and demand more transparency in government.
There was evidence of skulduggery in the bidding process for the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - the practice of deception or cheating
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

Alteration of Scots 'sculduddery' (obscenity, adultery) of uncertain origin.
