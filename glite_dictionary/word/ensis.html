Ensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of long, narrow marine bivalve mollusks known as razor clams, found in the family Pharidae.
genus of marine razor clams

Examples

Fishermen collected several Ensis from the sandy shore during low tide.
The shell of an Ensis is shaped much like a straight razor.
Many species in the genus Ensis are popular as seafood in various coastal cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razor clam - long, narrow marine bivalve mollusk

Etymology

From Latin *ensis* (sword), referring to the shape of the shell.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of razor clams in the family Pharidae, characterized by elongated, compressed shells and commonly found in sandy marine habitats.
genus of razor clams

Examples

Many species of Ensis are prized as delicacies in coastal regions around the world.
The long, narrow shells of Ensis make them easily recognizable on the beach.
Marine biologists often study Ensis populations to assess the health of sandy shore ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: razor clam - a common name for mollusks of the genus Ensis and related genera

Etymology

From Latin 'ensis' meaning 'sword', referring to the sword-like shape of the shell.
