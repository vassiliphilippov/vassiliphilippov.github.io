atherogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəroʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæθərəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of fatty deposits (atheromas) on the inner walls of arteries, leading to atherosclerosis
formation of arterial fatty deposits
medical

Examples

The process of atherogenesis begins early in life and can progress silently for decades.
Researchers study the molecular mechanisms involved in atherogenesis to better understand cardiovascular disease.
Lifestyle choices such as diet and exercise can influence the rate of atherogenesis in individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atherosclerosis - hardening and narrowing of the arteries due to atheroma buildup
  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - general thickening and hardening of artery walls

Etymology

From Greek *athero-* (gruel, porridge, referring to fatty deposits) + *genesis* (origin, formation).
