clump

1. NOUN

/ klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)
a compact mass of a substance, often loosely grouped together or stuck together
compact mass or cluster

Examples

A thick clump of grass blocked the narrow path.
She found a clump of earth stuck to her shoe after the walk.
The cat played with a clump of yarn on the floor.

Synset Examples

a ball of mud caught him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluster - a group of similar things or people positioned closely together
  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass of a substance, usually solid and without a regular shape

/ klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)

7. NOUN

/ klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)
a group of things of the same kind growing or placed closely together
group of things close together

Examples

A clump of wildflowers brightened the edge of the path.
He found a clump of hair in the brush.
There was a clump of bushes blocking the view.

Synset Examples

a bunch of trees
a cluster of admirers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluster - a group of similar things or people positioned closely together
  • Synonym: bunch - a number of things, typically of the same kind, growing or fastened together
  • Antonym: scatter - things spread out or separated, not grouped together

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *clompe* (lump, mass), of Germanic origin.
