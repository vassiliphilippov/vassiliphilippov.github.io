Lilliputian

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪlɪˈpjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪlɪˈpjuːʃən / (BrE)
a very small person or being, originally one of the tiny inhabitants of Lilliput in Jonathan Swift's novel 'Gulliver's Travels'; by extension, a person or thing that is unusually small or miniature.
a tiny person or thing; inhabitant of Lilliput

Examples

The museum displayed a collection of Lilliputian chairs, each perfectly crafted to scale.
He marveled at the Lilliputian proportions of the rare bonsai trees.
In Swift's tale, a Lilliputian could easily fit in the palm of a hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miniature - something much smaller than usual size
  • Antonym: giant - a very large person or thing, opposite of tiny

Etymology

From Lilliput, the fictional island in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726), inhabited by tiny people.

2. ADJ

/ ˌlɪlɪˈpjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪlɪˈpjuːʃən / (BrE)
extremely small; tiny; or relating to the imaginary country of Lilliput in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels'.
extremely small; tiny

Examples

After seeing the Lilliputian model train, the visitors marveled at the craftsmanship.
The company's latest gadget is so Lilliputian it can fit in a pocket.
The Lilliputian houses on the display looked like toys beside the real buildings.

Synset Examples

the Lilliputian population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - very small; minute
  • Antonym: gigantic - extremely large; huge

Etymology

From Lilliput, the imaginary country of tiny people in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726).
