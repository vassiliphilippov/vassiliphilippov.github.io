subconscious

1. ADJ

/ səbˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
(of thoughts or feelings) existing or operating just below the level of conscious awareness, but not fully accessible to consciousness.
just below conscious awareness

Examples

She had a subconscious fear of dogs, even though she couldn't remember being bitten.
Sometimes our subconscious desires influence our decisions without us realizing it.
He scratched his head in a subconscious response to the difficult question.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conscious - aware and able to perceive or feel
  • Degree Lower: unconscious - not conscious; lacking awareness; also, the part of the mind inaccessible to the conscious mind

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'conscious', first used in psychological writings of the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ səbˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
the part of the mind that is not fully aware but influences thoughts and actions
part of the mind below conscious awareness

Examples

Dreams are often influenced by the subconscious and can reveal hidden thoughts or feelings.
Meditation helps some people connect with their subconscious mind.
Artists sometimes say their creativity flows from the subconscious rather than logical thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - the part of the mind inaccessible to the conscious mind but affecting behavior and emotions
  • Antonym: conscious - the part of the mind aware of and able to think about itself and the environment

Etymology

From sub- (below) + conscious, first used in late 19th century psychology.
