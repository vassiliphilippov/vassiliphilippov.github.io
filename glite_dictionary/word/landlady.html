landlady

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)leɪdi / (BrE)
a woman who owns and rents out land, buildings, or rooms to tenants
female landlord; woman who rents property

Examples

Our landlady stopped by to collect the rent this morning.
After living there for three years, I became good friends with my landlady.
The landlady quickly responded when we called about the broken heater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landlord - a person who owns and rents out property
  • Antonym: tenant - a person who rents property from a landlord or landlady

Etymology

From 'land' + 'lady', modeled on 'landlord'.
