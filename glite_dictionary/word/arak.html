arak

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɑk / (AmE), / əˈræk / (BrE)
A strong distilled liquor made from the fermented sap of toddy palms or from fermented molasses, common in parts of South and Southeast Asia and the Middle East.
distilled liquor from palm sap or molasses

Examples

He sipped the local arak and enjoyed the warm evening breeze.
The distillery specializes in producing traditional arak from coconut palm sap.
Tourists are often advised to try arak when visiting the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrack - a similar distilled liquor from South and Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Arabic ʿaraq (sweat, juice, spirit), referring to the distillation process.
