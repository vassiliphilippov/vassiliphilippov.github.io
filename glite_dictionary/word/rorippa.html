Rorippa

1. NOUN

/ rəˈrɪpə / (AmE), / rəˈrɪpə / (BrE)
a genus of annual and perennial herbs in the cabbage family, found in damp habitats worldwide except Antarctica
a plant genus of damp habitats

Examples

Rorippa species commonly grow along streams and marshy areas across the northern hemisphere.
The leaves of some Rorippa plants are used in traditional medicine in certain regions.
Botanists have identified new varieties within the Rorippa genus in recently explored wetlands.

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name established in botanical classification.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈrɪpə / (AmE), / rəˈrɪpə / (BrE)
a genus of annual and perennial herbs found in damp habitats, widely distributed around the world except Antarctica
a genus of herbs in damp habitats

Examples

Many species in the Rorippa genus are commonly known as watercress.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Rorippa growing near the riverbank.
The Rorippa plants thrive in wet, marshy environments across several continents.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'rōrippa' (a kind of watercress), used as the genus name in botany.
