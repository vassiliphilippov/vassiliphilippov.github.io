compliancy

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplaɪ.ən.si / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪ.ən.si / (BrE)
a tendency or willingness to yield to the wishes or authority of others
willingness to yield or comply

Examples

Her natural compliancy made her popular among her colleagues, as she rarely challenged authority.
The organization took advantage of his compliancy to push through unpopular decisions.
Excessive compliancy can sometimes be mistaken for weakness or lack of conviction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliance - the act of complying with a command or wish
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance; bold disobedience

Etymology

From comply + -ancy, modeled after 'compliance'.
