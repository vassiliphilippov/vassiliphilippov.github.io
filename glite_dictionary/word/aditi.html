Aditi

1. PROPN

/ əˈdiːti / (AmE), / əˈdiːti / (BrE)
a Hindu goddess regarded as the mother of the Adityas and associated with release from sin or disease
Hindu goddess, mother of the Adityas

Examples

In Hindu mythology, Aditi is revered as the universal mother of the gods known as the Adityas.
The Rigveda contains several hymns dedicated to Aditi and her powers of protection and liberation.
Scholars often interpret Aditi as a symbol of infinity and motherhood in ancient Indian texts.

Etymology

From Sanskrit अदिति (aditi), meaning 'boundless' or 'limitless'.
