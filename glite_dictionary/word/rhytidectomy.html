rhytidectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪ.təˈdɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌrɪ.tɪˈdɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove wrinkles and other signs of aging from the face by tightening the skin; also called a face-lift
plastic surgery to reduce facial wrinkles
medical

Examples

After her rhytidectomy, she looked several years younger and felt more confident.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of a rhytidectomy before scheduling the procedure.
Some patients choose to combine a rhytidectomy with other cosmetic treatments for a more complete rejuvenation.

Synset Examples

some actresses have more than one face lift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face-lift - a cosmetic surgical procedure to reduce wrinkles by tightening the skin of the face

Etymology

From Greek 'rhytis' (wrinkle) + 'ektomē' (excision, removal).
