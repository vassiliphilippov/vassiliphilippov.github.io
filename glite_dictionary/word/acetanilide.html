acetanilide

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈtænɪˌlaɪd / (AmE), / ˌasɪˈtanɪlʌɪd / (BrE)
A white, odorless, solid compound used in the manufacture of dyes, rubber accelerators, and pharmaceuticals; metabolized to paracetamol (acetaminophen) in the body.
chemical used in dyes and rubber manufacture
medical

Examples

Acetanilide was one of the first synthetic fever-reducing drugs, though its use has declined due to side effects.
The laboratory ordered a shipment of acetanilide for use in their research on dye synthesis.
Exposure to acetanilide in large amounts can cause toxic effects, so proper safety measures are essential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetaminophen - pain reliever metabolite of acetanilide

Etymology

From acetic acid + aniline, named in the 19th century.
