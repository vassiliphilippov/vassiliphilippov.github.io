mentalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.t̬ə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
(philosophy) The doctrine that mind or consciousness is the fundamental reality and that objects exist only as aspects of mental awareness.
philosophy: doctrine that mind is fundamental reality

Examples

In certain schools of philosophy, mentalism is seen as the basis for understanding reality.
His arguments favored mentalism over materialism in explaining human experience.
Many critics of mentalism argue that it cannot account for the existence of an external world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - philosophy: the belief that reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual

Etymology

From 'mental' + '-ism', modeled on similar philosophical terms.
