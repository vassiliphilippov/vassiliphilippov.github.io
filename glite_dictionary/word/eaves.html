eaves

1. NOUN

/ ivz / (AmE), / iÀêvz / (BrE)
the part of a roof that projects beyond the side of a building, forming an overhanging edge at the lower boundary
overhanging lower edge of a roof

Examples

Rainwater dripped steadily from the eaves after the heavy storm.
Birds had built a nest under the eaves of the old barn.
Icicles often hang from the eaves during the winter months.

Etymology

From Old English efes (edge of a roof, border).
