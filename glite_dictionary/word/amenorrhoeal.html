amenorrhoeal

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪ.məˌnɔːrˈiː.əl / (AmE), / ˌæm.əˌnɔːˈriː.əl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by the absence or suppression of normal menstrual periods for reasons other than pregnancy
pertaining to absence of menstruation
medical

Examples

The patient was described as amenorrhoeal following intensive training and weight loss.
Chronic stress may result in amenorrhoeal conditions in some women.
The doctor noted her amenorrhoeal symptoms when evaluating her hormonal balance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: menstrual - relating to menstruation

Etymology

From amenorrhea (medical term) + -al (forming adjectives); 'amenorrhea' from Greek 'a-' (without) + 'men' (month) + 'rhein' (to flow).
