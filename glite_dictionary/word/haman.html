Haman

1. PROPN

/ ˈheɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.mən / (BrE)
A minister of the Persian emperor in the Old Testament who plotted to massacre the Jews and was hanged for his conspiracy.
Biblical enemy of the Jews, minister in Persia

Examples

The story of Haman is central to the Jewish festival of Purim.
In the Old Testament, Haman becomes infamous for his plot against the Jews.
According to tradition, the villainous Haman wore elaborate robes in the king's court.

Etymology

From Hebrew Hāmān, the name as used in the Old Testament.
