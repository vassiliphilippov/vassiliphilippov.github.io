to beware

1. VERB

/ bɪˈwɛr / (AmE), / bɪˈweə / (BrE)
to be cautious and alert to dangers or risks; to be on guard
to be cautious or on guard

Examples

Beware of the dog sign on the gate warns visitors to be careful.
Travelers are advised to beware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.
She told him to beware when walking alone at night.

Synset Examples

Beware of telephone salesmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to watch out - to be careful or alert to danger
  • Antonym: to ignore - not to pay attention to; to disregard warnings

Etymology

From Middle English 'be ware', imperative of 'be' and 'ware' (aware or cautious).
