hydrocephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrə(ʊ)ˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A medical condition in which excess cerebrospinal fluid accumulates in the ventricles of the brain, causing swelling and potentially neurological problems.
excess fluid in the brain (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the infant with hydrocephaly after noticing the rapid increase in head size.
Early treatment of hydrocephaly can help prevent serious neurological complications.
Adults with hydrocephaly may experience balance problems and mental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrocephalus - abnormal accumulation of fluid in the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'kephalē' (head).
