vena intercostalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˌɪn.tərˌkɒsˈteɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.nə ˌɪn.təkɒsˈteɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
Any of several veins that drain blood from the intercostal spaces between the ribs.
vein draining intercostal spaces
medical

Examples

A blockage in the vena intercostalis can lead to impaired drainage of the chest wall.
The surgeon identified the vena intercostalis during the procedure to prevent accidental bleeding.
Multiple vena intercostalis are found running parallel to the intercostal arteries.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'intercostalis' (between the ribs).
