archly

1. ADV

/ ˈɑrtʃli / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃli / (BrE)
in a manner that is playfully sly or mischievously teasing
in a playfully sly or mischievous way

Examples

She smiled archly at him, hinting at a secret joke only they understood.
The child responded archly, delighting everyone with her playful wit.
He remarked archly that he wouldn't dream of breaking the rules—unless no one was watching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievously - in a way that shows a playful desire to cause trouble
  • Antonym: innocently - in a way that is not intended to cause harm or show slyness

Etymology

From 'arch' (playful, mischievous), ultimately from Old French 'arché', from Latin 'arcus' (bow, curve) conveying a sense of bending or indirectness.
