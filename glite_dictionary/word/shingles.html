shingles

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡəlz / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡəlz / (BrE)
a painful skin condition with blisters that appear along the path of a nerve, caused by reactivation of the chickenpox virus
painful skin condition with blisters
medical

Examples

My grandmother had shingles last year and suffered from intense pain in her back.
Doctors prescribed antiviral medication to treat his case of shingles.
The symptoms of shingles included a red, blistering rash along her ribcage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes zoster - medical term for shingles, the viral infection causing blisters along nerves

Etymology

Originally referred to 'belt, girdle' in Late Latin *cingulum*, in reference to the band-like pattern of the rash.
