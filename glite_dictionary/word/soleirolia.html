Soleirolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊliˈroʊliə / (AmE), / ˌsəʊliːˈrəʊliə / (BrE)
A genus of mat-forming evergreen herbaceous plant, known for its dense, creeping green foliage, often grown as ground cover or a houseplant.
a mat-forming evergreen herb genus

Examples

Soleirolia is a popular choice for adding lush greenery to terrariums and dish gardens.
The gardener recommended planting Soleirolia in shaded areas for best results.
Despite its delicate leaves, Soleirolia is quite hardy and spreads quickly across moist soil.

Etymology

Named after French army engineer Joseph-François Soleirol (1781–1863), who collected the plant.

2. NOUN

/ soʊˌleɪəˈroʊliə / (AmE), / sɒˌleɪəˈrɔːliə / (BrE)
A genus of small, evergreen, mat-forming creeping herbs containing a single species, often grown as a ground cover or ornamental plant.
genus of creeping evergreen herbs

Examples

Soleirolia grows quickly and forms a dense carpet of greenery in shaded garden corners.
Gardeners often use Soleirolia as a ground cover for its delicate texture and low-maintenance growth.
The tiny leaves of Soleirolia make it ideal for terrariums and indoor plant displays.

Etymology

Named after Joseph-François Soleirol, French engineer and plant collector.
