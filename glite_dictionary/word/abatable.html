abatable

1. ADJ

/ əˈbeɪɾəbəl / (AmE), / əˈbeɪtəbl / (BrE)
(formal) Capable of being reduced, lessened, or ended; able to be abated.
capable of being reduced or ended

Examples

Since the noise was abatable, the city ordered the factory to install soundproofing measures.
The judge ruled that the nuisance was abatable and required the defendant to take action.
Certain environmental hazards are abatable if addressed promptly with the right technology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unabatable - not able to be abated or reduced

Etymology

From abate + -able, meaning able to be abated.
