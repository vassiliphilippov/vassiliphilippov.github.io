hymeneal

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪməˈniːəl / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɪˈniːəl / (BrE)
a song or poem celebrating a wedding or marriage; a wedding hymn
wedding hymn or song
archaic

Examples

The choir sang a beautiful hymeneal at the wedding ceremony.
Poets of the Renaissance often composed hymeneals for noble marriages.
The hymeneal echoed joyfully through the ancient chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epithalamium - a poem or song written for a bride on her way to the marital chamber

Etymology

From Latin hymenaeus (wedding song), from Greek hymenaios, relating to Hymen, the god of marriage.
