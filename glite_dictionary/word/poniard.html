poniard

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːniərd / (AmE), / ˈpɒniəd / (BrE)
a dagger with a slender, often pointed blade, used especially in the past as a weapon
slender-bladed dagger

Examples

The assassin drew a poniard from his belt in a swift motion.
He bought a decorative poniard at the antique market.
The knight's poniard gleamed in the candlelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - a short knife with a pointed and edged blade, used as a weapon

Etymology

From Old French *poignard*, related to *poing* (fist) from Latin *pugnus* (fist).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑːniərd / (AmE), / ˈpɒniəd / (BrE)
to stab or pierce with a poniard (a small, slender dagger)
stab with a poniard (dagger)

Examples

The assassin poniarded his target in the shadows of the alley.
In the play, the hero poniards his enemy during the final act.
The villain was caught as he tried to poniard the duke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stab - to thrust a pointed weapon into someone or something

Etymology

From French *poignard*, from Old French *poigne* (fist).
