instep

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stɛp / (BrE)
the curved upper part of the foot between the ball and the ankle
the arch of the foot

Examples

She injured her instep while running on the uneven trail.
The shoe pressed uncomfortably against his instep.
Stretching can help relieve pain in your instep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - the curved part of the foot forming the instep

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'in' + 'step'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stɛp / (BrE)
the part of a shoe or stocking that covers the arch of the foot
part of shoe or stocking over foot arch

Examples

The shoe was too tight over the instep, making it uncomfortable to wear.
She admired the delicate embroidery on the instep of her new stockings.
Some boots have extra padding in the instep for added comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - the curved part of the foot between the ball and the heel

Etymology

From 'in' + 'step', originally referring to the raised part of the foot.
