subduction

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / səbˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the process in geology where one edge of a crustal plate is forced underneath another plate into the mantle
downward movement of one crustal plate under another

Examples

The process of subduction is responsible for the formation of deep ocean trenches.
Volcanoes often form near areas of active subduction.
Scientists study subduction zones to better understand the causes of earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subduct - to pull under or draw downward, especially a tectonic plate
  • Antonym: uplift - the upward movement of the Earth's surface

Etymology

From Latin subducere (to draw away, withdraw), via French subduction.
