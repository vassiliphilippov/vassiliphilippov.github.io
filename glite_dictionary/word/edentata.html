Edentata

1. NOUN

/ iːˌdɛnˈteɪtə / (AmE), / iːˌdɛnˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
a former order of mammals characterized by having few or no teeth, including anteaters, sloths, and armadillos, now largely replaced by separate orders in scientific classification
historical mammal order with few or no teeth

Examples

The extinct giant ground sloths were members of the Edentata.
Scientists once grouped armadillos with anteaters under the Edentata order.
Recent genetic studies have led to the reclassification of animals formerly known as Edentata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xenarthra - the modern taxonomic order including anteaters, sloths, and armadillos

Etymology

From Latin 'ēdentātus' meaning 'toothless'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌiː.dɛnˈteɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌiː.dɛnˈtɑː.tə / (BrE)
an order of mammals characterized by having few or no teeth, including anteaters, sloths, and armadillos (now largely obsolete in taxonomy)
order of toothless or nearly toothless mammals
medical

Examples

Some biologists once classified armadillos, sloths, and anteaters in the order Edentata.
Fossil discoveries have changed our understanding of the mammals in Edentata.
The classification of Edentata is now largely considered outdated in modern taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xenarthra - modern order including anteaters, sloths, and armadillos

Etymology

From Latin 'edentatus' meaning 'toothless', from 'e-' (without) + 'dens' (tooth).
