maxilliped

1. NOUN

/ mækˈsɪlɪˌpɛd / (AmE), / mækˈsɪlɪˌpɛd / (BrE)
one of a set of paired appendages on the thorax of a decapod crustacean, located just behind the maxillae and used in feeding
feeding appendage of some crustaceans

Examples

The crab uses its maxillipeds to manipulate food into its mouth.
Scientists studied the structure of the lobster's maxillipeds under a microscope.
The shrimp lost one maxilliped during a molting accident.

Synset Examples

An appendage which is modified to assist in feeding is known as a maxilliped or gnathopod.

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' (jaw) + '-ped' (foot).
