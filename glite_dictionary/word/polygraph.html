polygraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːliˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument that simultaneously records multiple physiological processes, such as pulse rate, blood pressure, respiration, and perspiration.
instrument recording multiple body functions
medical

Examples

The laboratory used a polygraph to monitor heart rate and breathing during the experiment.
Doctors relied on the polygraph to collect data on several vital signs at once.
A modern polygraph can record electrical activity in the skin as well as blood pressure.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'graph' (writing/instrument for recording).
