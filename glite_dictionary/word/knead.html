to knead

1. VERB

/ nid / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
to press, rub, or squeeze someone's body, especially with the hands, in order to relax muscles or relieve pain
press or massage someone's body

Examples

The therapist began to knead my shoulders to help ease the tension.
She gently kneaded her grandmother's stiff muscles until they relaxed.
During the session, he used his strong hands to knead the knots out of my back.

Synset Examples

She rubbed down her child with a sponge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massage - to rub and press someone's body, especially to relieve pain or relax muscles

Etymology

From Old English *cnedan*, related to German *kneten* (to knead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01235302-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ nid / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
to work and mix a substance, such as dough or clay, into a smooth, uniform mass by pressing, folding, and stretching it with the hands.
work or mix dough or clay by hand

Examples

Always knead the bread dough for at least ten minutes to develop the gluten.
She kneaded the clay until it was perfectly smooth and ready to shape.
The baker kneads the dough every morning before opening the shop.

Synset Examples

knead dough
work the clay until it is soft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend different substances together

Etymology

From Old English *cnedan*, related to German *kneten* and Dutch *kneden*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01237919-v
Synset POS: v
