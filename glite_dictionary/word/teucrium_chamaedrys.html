Teucrium chamaedrys

1. NOUN

/ ˈtjuːkriəm kəˈmiːdrɪs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːkriəm ˌkæmiˈdrɪs / (BrE)
A European perennial subshrub with red-purple or bright rose flowers spotted with red and white, commonly known as wall germander.
a European subshrub with red-purple flowers

Examples

Teucrium chamaedrys is often grown in gardens for its attractive, aromatic foliage.
Herbalists have used Teucrium chamaedrys for centuries in traditional remedies.
The borders of the park were lined with flowering Teucrium chamaedrys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wall germander - common name for Teucrium chamaedrys

Etymology

From Latin *Teucrium* (a plant genus), *chamaedrys* (from Greek khamaidrys, meaning 'ground oak').
