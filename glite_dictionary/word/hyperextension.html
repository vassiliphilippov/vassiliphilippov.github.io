hyperextension

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpɚɪkˈstɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərɪkˈstɛnʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or condition of extending a limb or joint beyond its normal range of motion, often resulting in injury
extension of a joint beyond normal limits
medical

Examples

She experienced hyperextension of her knee during the game, which required a few weeks of rest.
Physical therapists often warn against hyperextension to prevent strain or ligament injury.
A sudden hyperextension of the elbow can happen if you fall onto an outstretched arm.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, beyond) + Latin 'extensio' (stretching out).
