Ctenocephalides

1. NOUN

/ tnɛˌɑsɪˈfælaɪdiːz / (AmE), / tniˌɒsɪˈfæləɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fleas that includes several species parasitizing mammals, such as the cat flea and the dog flea.
genus of fleas including cat and dog flea

Examples

The most common flea found on domestic cats is classified within the genus Ctenocephalides.
Veterinarians often check for Ctenocephalides infestations during routine exams of pets.
Researchers study Ctenocephalides to understand the transmission of certain diseases.

Etymology

From Greek ktenos (comb) + kephalē (head), referring to the comb-like structures on the head.

2. NOUN

/ tɛˌnoʊˌsɛfəˈlaɪdiːz / (AmE), / tɛˌniːəˌsɛfəˈlaɪdiːz / (BrE)
a genus of fleas that includes several common ectoparasites of mammals, such as the cat flea and dog flea
genus of fleas including the cat and dog flea

Examples

The most common flea affecting domestic cats is the Ctenocephalides felis.
Veterinarians often encounter Ctenocephalides infestations in household pets.
Identification of Ctenocephalides species is important for effective pest control.

Etymology

From Greek 'ktenos' (comb) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to the comb-like structures on the head.
