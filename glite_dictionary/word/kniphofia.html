kniphofia

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈfoʊfiə / (AmE), / nɪˈfəʊfiə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Kniphofia, known for its long, grasslike leaves and tall spikes of red or yellow drooping flowers.
a flowering plant with tall spikes of bright flowers

Examples

The vibrant red blooms of the kniphofia stood out in the summer garden.
Many gardeners plant kniphofias to attract hummingbirds and butterflies.
She admired a row of kniphofia by the pond, their bright flowers swaying in the breeze.

Etymology

Named after Johann Hieronymus Kniphof, an 18th-century German botanist.
