Murrumbidgee

1. PROPN

/ mərəmˈbɪdʒi / (AmE), / mʌrəmˈbɪdʒi / (BrE)
a river in southeastern Australia that flows westward into the Murray River
river in southeastern Australia

Examples

The Murrumbidgee Valley is known for its rich farmland and diverse wildlife.
After weeks of rain, the Murrumbidgee flooded several small towns near its banks.
Farmers rely on water from the Murrumbidgee for irrigation during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Murray River - major river in southeastern Australia into which the Murrumbidgee flows

Etymology

From an Aboriginal (Wiradjuri) word meaning 'big water' or 'plenty water'.
