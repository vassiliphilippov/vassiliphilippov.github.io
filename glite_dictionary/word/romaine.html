romaine

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with long, crisp, dark-green leaves forming a loosely packed, elongated head.
variety of lettuce with long dark-green leaves

Examples

I always use romaine in my Caesar salads for its crisp texture.
The romaine leaves were washed and chopped before being added to the bowl.
She prefers romaine over iceberg lettuce for the extra nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cos lettuce - another name for romaine, especially in British English

Etymology

From French 'romaine', meaning 'Roman', referring to its reputed origins or association with Rome.
