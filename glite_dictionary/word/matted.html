matted

1. ADJ

/ ˈmætɪd / (AmE), / ˈmætɪd / (BrE)
(of a surface) not shiny or glossy; not reflecting light.
not glossy; not reflecting light

Examples

The artist preferred matted paper to give the drawing a subtle, muted appearance.
A matted finish on the photograph helped reduce glare from overhead lights.
She chose matted wall paint rather than a glossy one for a more modern look.

Synset Examples

flat wall paint
a photograph with a matte finish

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glossy - shiny and smooth; reflecting light

Etymology

From Old French mat (dull, dejected), of uncertain origin; related to 'matte' for the dull surface finish.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmætɪd / (AmE), / ˈmætɪd / (BrE)
(of hair, fur, or vegetation) tangled into a dense, compact mass, often becoming twisted or stuck together.
tangled into a dense mass

Examples

The dog's matted fur needed to be carefully brushed out by the groomer.
After the rain, hikers found the trail blocked by matted leaves and branches.
Her hair became matted after swimming in the lake without a cap.

Synset Examples

tried to push through the matted undergrowth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangled - twisted together in a disorderly mass
  • Antonym: smooth - having a flat, even surface, not tangled or rough

Etymology

From Middle English 'mat' (tangled mass), from Old French 'mat' (dead, dull, matted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256784-s
Synset POS: s
