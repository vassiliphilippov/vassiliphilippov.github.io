to frizzle

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɪzəl / (BrE)
to fry (food) until it curls up and becomes crisp
fry until curled and crisp

Examples

She loves to frizzle bacon until it's perfectly crispy for breakfast.
The chef will frizzle thin strips of onion as a garnish for the soup.
If you frizzle the ham too long, it turns tough and curly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fry - to cook in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan
  • Synonym: sizzle - to make a hissing sound, as food does when frying

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɪzəl / (BrE)
to form hair or other material into tight curls or twists, often using heat or chemicals
to curl hair tightly

Examples

She loves to frizzle her hair before a night out.
The humid weather caused my bangs to frizzle uncontrollably.
If you frizzle the ribbons with scissors, they will curl nicely for gift wrapping.

Synset Examples

crimp hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimp - to make wavy or curl by pressing or pinching
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make hair or another material straight, removing any curl

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'frizz', from French 'friser' (to curl).
