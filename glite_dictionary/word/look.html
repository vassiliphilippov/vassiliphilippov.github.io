look

1. NOUN

/ lʊk / (AmE), / lʊk / (BrE)
an act of turning one's eyes toward something or someone to see them
an act of looking at something

Examples

He gave her a curious look as she entered the room.
Let me have a look at those documents before you send them.
With just one look, she understood everything he meant.

Synset Examples

he went out to have a look
his look was fixed on her eyes
he gave it a good looking at
his camera does his looking for him

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glance - a quick or brief look
  • Degree Higher: stare - a long or fixed look

Etymology

From Old English *lōcian* (to look, see).
