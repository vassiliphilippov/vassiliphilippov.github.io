histogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɪstəɡram / (BrE)
A type of bar chart representing the frequency distribution of a set of data, where the heights of the bars indicate observed frequencies in each category or range.
bar chart for frequency distribution

Examples

The histogram clearly shows that most students scored between 70 and 80 percent on the test.
After entering the data, she created a histogram to visualize the distribution of ages.
When comparing the two histograms, it was evident that the second dataset had a wider spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar chart - a diagram using bars of different heights to represent values

Etymology

From Greek 'histos' (anything set upright) + 'gramma' (something written, a drawing), first used in the late 19th century in statistics.
