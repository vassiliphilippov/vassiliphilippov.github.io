title

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtəl / (BrE)
a heading that names a statute or legislative bill, often summarizing its contents
statutory or legislative heading
legal

Examples

Title IX of the Education Amendments prohibits sex discrimination in schools.
Congress passed several reforms under Title II of the Social Security Act.
The senator argued for changes to Title 21, which regulates controlled substances.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old French *titel*, from Latin *titulus* (inscription, label, title).
