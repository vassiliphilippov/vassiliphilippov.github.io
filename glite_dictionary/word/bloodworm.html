bloodworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a small, bright red marine worm often used as bait by anglers
marine worm used as fishing bait

Examples

Fishermen often use bloodworm as bait for catching a variety of fish.
The aquarium store sells both live and frozen bloodworms for feeding tropical fish.
The bright red color of the bloodworm makes it attractive to fish in murky water.
