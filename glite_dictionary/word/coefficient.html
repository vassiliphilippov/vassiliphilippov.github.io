coefficient

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈfɪʃ.ənt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.ɪˈfɪʃ.ənt / (BrE)
a constant number that is used to measure, multiply, or express a property or characteristic, especially in mathematics or science
constant multiplier or measure in maths/science

Examples

The friction coefficient depends on the materials in contact and their surface texture.
In the equation y = 2x + 3, the number 2 is called the coefficient of x.
Researchers calculated the correlation coefficient to assess the relationship between the two variables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - a number or quantity that when multiplied with another produces a given number or expression

Etymology

From French 'coefficient', from Latin 'co-' (together) + 'efficiens' (effecting, producing).
