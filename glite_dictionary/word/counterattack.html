counterattack

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ.əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tər.əˌtæk / (BrE)
an attack made in response to an enemy's assault, especially to regain lost ground or stop the enemy’s advance
military attack in response to an enemy

Examples

The army launched a swift counterattack after their defensive line was breached.
After retreating briefly, the team mounted a fierce counterattack that surprised their opponents.
The general's strategy relied on a timely counterattack to regain control of the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attack - an aggressive military action

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'attack'.

2. VERB

/ ˌkaʊntərəˈtæk / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərəˈtæk / (BrE)
to attack in response to an enemy's attack; to make a retaliatory attack against an opponent
to make a retaliatory attack

Examples

After regaining their strength, the soldiers decided to counterattack at dawn.
When the company was criticized in the media, it chose to counterattack with a strong public statement.
The team quickly counterattacked, surprising their opponents and scoring a goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against
  • Antonym: to defend - to protect against attack

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'attack', first used in military contexts c. 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntər əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntərəˌtæk / (BrE)
(in chess) an attack made in response to an opponent's offensive, often aimed at countering their advantage in another area of the board.
in chess, a responsive attack to counter an opponent's advantage

Examples

After losing his queen's pawn, he launched a counterattack on the kingside.
The grandmaster found an unexpected counterattack that forced his opponent onto the defensive.
A well-timed counterattack can quickly turn the tables in a chess match.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attack - an aggressive move in chess to gain advantage
  • Antonym: defense - moves made to protect one's position in chess

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'attack'; used in chess since the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
