capableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪpəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being capable, especially in physical, intellectual, or legal respects
being capable; ability or competence

Examples

Her capableness in managing complex projects impressed the entire team.
The interviewers questioned his capableness to handle stressful situations effectively.
They evaluated the capableness of each candidate before making a final decision.

Synset Examples

he worked to the limits of his capability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capability - the ability or power to do something; the quality of being capable
  • Antonym: incapacity - lack of ability or power to do something

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being capable; the ability to do something or develop an aptitude
the quality of being capable

Examples

The capableness of the team improved with each new training session.
Her capableness as a leader became evident in times of crisis.
We are seeking applicants whose capableness matches the demands of this challenging role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capability - the power or ability to do something
  • Antonym: incapacity - lack of ability or power

Etymology

From 'capable' + '-ness', formed on the model of 'capability'.
