idealist

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdiəlɪst / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who is guided more by ideals or principles than by practical considerations
person guided by ideals rather than practicality

Examples

Despite criticism, she remained an idealist determined to improve the world.
Some called him an idealist for believing that peace was possible.
As an idealist, he often clashed with his more pragmatic colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realist - a person guided by practical considerations rather than by ideals

Etymology

From French 'idéaliste', from 'idéal' (ideal) and '-iste' (-ist).
