disappointment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
An event, action, or situation that causes someone to feel disappointed.
something that causes disappointment

Examples

The cancellation of the concert was a major disappointment to the fans.
His low test score was a disappointment after all his hard work.
The team's early exit from the tournament was yet another disappointment this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letdown - an instance of being let down or disappointed
  • Antonym: delight - something that gives great pleasure or satisfaction

Etymology

From Old French 'desapointement,' from 'desapointer' (to undo the appointment).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of sadness or dissatisfaction caused by not achieving what one hoped or expected
feeling of unmet expectations or sadness

Examples

She felt deep disappointment when she did not get the job she wanted.
The movie was a major disappointment compared to the exciting trailer.
He tried to hide his disappointment at the team's loss.

Synset Examples

his hopes were so high he was doomed to disappointment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfaction - a feeling of contentment or fulfillment of one's wishes and expectations
  • Synonym: frustration - a feeling of being upset or annoyed, especially because of inability to change or achieve something

Etymology

From Old French 'desapointer' (to undo an appointment), from late Latin 'de(s)–' (undo) + 'appointare' (appoint).
