Malacothamnus fasciculatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˈθæmnəs ˌfæsɪˈkjuːlətəs / (AmE), / ˌmæləkəˈθæmnəs ˌfæsɪˈkjuːlətəs / (BrE)
A shrub native to the coastal ranges of California and Baja California, with hairy branches and spikes of numerous mauve flowers; sometimes placed in the genus Sphaeralcea.
a shrub native to California and Baja California

Examples

Malacothamnus fasciculatus is often found growing in chaparral habitats along the California coast.
Botanists study Malacothamnus fasciculatus to understand its ecological role in wildfire recovery.
The delicate mauve flowers of Malacothamnus fasciculatus bloom in late spring.

Etymology

From Greek malakos (soft) and thamnos (shrub); Latin fasciculatus (clustered).
