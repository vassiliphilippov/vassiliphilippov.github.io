retracted

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtræktɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈtraktɪd / (BrE)
drawn back or pulled in; not extended outward
drawn back or pulled in

Examples

The cat sat calmly with its retracted claws hidden from view.
The plane's landing gear remained retracted during flight.
He apologized for his retracted statement earlier in the meeting.

Synset Examples

a cat with retracted claws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extended - stretched out to full length or beyond the normal position

Etymology

From Latin 'retractus', past participle of 'retrahere' (to draw back).
