crepuscle

1. NOUN

/ krəˈpʌskəl / (AmE), / krɪˈpʌskəl / (BrE)
The period of twilight immediately following sunset; dusk.
the period after sunset; dusk

Examples

The mountain range appeared in silhouette against the glowing crepuscle.
Birds began to return to their nests as crepuscle settled over the fields.
They preferred to take walks in the crepuscle when the air was cool and still.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the time between sunset and darkness
  • Synonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight after sunset
  • Antonym: dawn - the period of twilight before sunrise

Etymology

From Latin 'crepusculum' (twilight).
