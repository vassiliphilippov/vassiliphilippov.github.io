Apios

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pi.ɑs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
A genus of twining perennial plants native to North America, including species known for edible tubers, such as the groundnut.
genus of North American perennial plants

Examples

Apios is an important genus for those studying edible wild plants in North America.
The tubers of Apios americana were once a staple food for Native American peoples.
Botanists have recently discovered a new species within the Apios genus.

Etymology

From Greek *apios* (pear), referring to the pear-shaped tubers of some species.
