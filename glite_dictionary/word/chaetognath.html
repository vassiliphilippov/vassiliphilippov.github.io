chaetognath

1. NOUN

/ kiˈtɑɡ.næθ / (AmE), / kiːˈtɒɡ.næθ / (BrE)
A transparent marine worm of the phylum Chaetognatha, characterized by horizontal lateral and caudal fins and curved spines on each side of the mouth.
transparent marine worm (phylum Chaetognatha)

Examples

The chaetognath is an important predator in many planktonic ecosystems.
Scientists collected several chaetognaths during the marine biology survey.
Unlike many other worms, a chaetognath has distinctive bristle-like jaws for catching prey.

Etymology

From Greek 'khaitē' (hair) + 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to bristle-like jaws.
