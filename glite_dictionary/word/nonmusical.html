nonmusical

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈmjuːzɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈmjuːzɪkəl / (BrE)
not having an interest in or talent for music; unmusical.
lacking interest or talent in music

Examples

Although her parents both played instruments, Anna remained completely nonmusical throughout her life.
He always found music class boring, admitting he was rather nonmusical.
Their son is nonmusical, but he excels in mathematics and science.

Synset Examples

too unmusical to care for concerts
it is unfortunate that her children were all nonmusical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmusical - not musical; lacking aptitude for music

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'musical'.

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈmjuːzɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈmjuːzɪkəl / (BrE)
not related to, producing, or having the qualities of music; lacking a musical nature or quality.
not related to or lacking music

Examples

Her interests are mostly nonmusical and include painting and hiking.
The bird made a harsh, nonmusical sound in the early morning.
He was assigned to write a nonmusical review for the arts section.

Synset Examples

the unmusical cry of the bluejay

Related Terms

  • Antonym: musical - relating to music or having the qualities of music

Etymology

From prefix non- (not) + musical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01508997-a
Synset POS: a
