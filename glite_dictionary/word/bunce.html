bunce

1. NOUN

/ bʌns / (AmE), / bʌns / (BrE)
a sudden or unexpected gain, especially a windfall of profit or money
unexpected windfall or profit
informal british

Examples

He couldn't believe his luck when the old painting turned out to be a bunce worth thousands.
Winning that unexpected bonus was a real bunce for the whole team.
Many small suppliers have seen a bunce from the recent surge in demand.

Synset Examples

the demand for testing has created a boom for those unregulated laboratories where boxes of specimen jars are processed like an assembly line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - an unexpected gain or piece of good fortune

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from British slang in early 20th century.
