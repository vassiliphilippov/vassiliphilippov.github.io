parvenue

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːr.vəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.və.njuː / (BrE)
Of or characteristic of a person who has recently acquired wealth or status and is considered lacking the manner or background of the established upper class.
characteristic of a newly rich or socially climbing person

Examples

Her parvenue tastes were evident in the lavish, yet gaudy, decorations of her new mansion.
The gala was filled with parvenue displays of wealth and ostentation.
Critics dismissed his parvenue style as lacking the refinement of true aristocracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - person who has recently gained wealth or social status

Etymology

From French 'parvenue', feminine of 'parvenu', past participle of 'parvenir' (to arrive, to succeed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːr.vəˌnuː / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.və.njuː / (BrE)
(of a person) having recently gained wealth or social status but lacking the manners or refinement considered appropriate for that position.
having recently acquired wealth or status but lacking refinement

Examples

She was often dismissed as a parvenue hostess by the city's old elite.
Although wealthy, his parvenue behavior set him apart from the established aristocracy.
The author's parvenue characters are eager to impress but fail to fit in with high society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nouveau riche - people who have recently acquired wealth, especially those lacking refinement

Etymology

From French 'parvenu', past participle of 'parvenir' (to arrive, to attain).
