medevac

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəˌvæk / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪˌvæk / (BrE)
the evacuation of people, especially by helicopter or airplane, to a place where they can receive medical treatment
emergency medical evacuation by air

Examples

The injured soldiers were quickly transported by medevac to the nearest field hospital.
During the disaster, several medevac helicopters flew nonstop missions to help the wounded.
A swift medevac is crucial in remote areas with limited medical facilities.

Etymology

Blend of 'medical' and 'evacuation'.
