indictability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌdaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being liable to indictment or legal prosecution
state of being liable to indictment
legal

Examples

The court had to determine the indictability of the suspect before proceeding with charges.
Questions about the indictability of public officials often arise during political scandals.
His legal team argued that his actions did not meet the threshold of indictability under state law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indict - to formally accuse of or charge with a serious crime

Etymology

From 'indict' + '-ability', after the verb 'to indict' (from Latin 'indictare', to proclaim).
