to detrain

1. VERB

/ diˈtreɪn / (AmE), / diˈtreɪn / (BrE)
to leave or disembark from a train
leave or get off a train

Examples

The passengers were instructed to detrain at the next station due to an emergency.
After a long journey, the soldiers began to detrain quickly and line up on the platform.
We watched as the commuters detrained and hurried toward the exits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to embark - to go onto a vehicle for travel
  • Synonym: to disembark - to leave a vehicle, especially a train or ship

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (indicating removal or reversal) + train.
