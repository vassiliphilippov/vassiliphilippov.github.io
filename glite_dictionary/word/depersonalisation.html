depersonalisation

1. NOUN

/ diːˌpɝːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A mental condition in which a person feels detached from their own thoughts, feelings, or sense of identity, often accompanied by feelings of unreality or strangeness.
feeling of detachment from oneself
medical

Examples

People experiencing depersonalisation often report feeling like they are observing themselves from outside their body.
During periods of intense stress, he suffered episodes of depersonalisation that made daily life difficult.
Therapists use specific techniques to help patients cope with severe depersonalisation symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derealisation - a feeling that the external world is unreal or strange

Etymology

From de- (removal, reversal) + personal + -isation (from French, based on Latin 'persona')

2. NOUN

/ diˌpɜrsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a state or feeling in which a person loses their sense of personal identity and feels like a part of an impersonal system
loss of personal identity; feeling like an anonymous part

Examples

After months of monotonous work, he began to experience depersonalisation and a sense of disconnection from his life.
Bureaucratic environments can often cause feelings of depersonalisation among employees.
The philosopher described modern society as producing depersonalisation and a loss of individuality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alienation - a feeling of being isolated or estranged

Etymology

From French dépersonnalisation, based on Latin persona (person) + -isation.

3. NOUN

/ diˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of regarding or treating someone as a thing rather than a person, thus depriving them of individuality or personal qualities.
treatment as a non-person; loss of individuality

Examples

The company's strict rules led to the depersonalisation of its staff, making everyone feel like a mere number.
Some critics argue that technology causes the depersonalisation of our interactions, making relationships feel less genuine.
The depersonalisation of patients in large hospitals can negatively affect their mental well-being.

Synset Examples

according to Marx, treating labor as a commodity exemplified the reification of the individual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dehumanization - The process of depriving a person or group of positive human qualities.
  • Antonym: personalization - The act of designing or producing something to meet someone's individual requirements.

Etymology

From de- (removal) + personal + -isation, patterned after French 'dépersonnalisation' and related to Latin 'persona' (person).
