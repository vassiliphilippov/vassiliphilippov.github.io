lithium

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθiəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪθiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silver-white metal that is the lightest of all metals and is used in batteries, medicines, and alloys
a soft, light metal element

Examples

Rechargeable batteries often contain lithium because of its lightweight properties.
Doctors sometimes prescribe lithium to treat certain mood disorders.
The periodic table lists lithium as atomic number 3.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkali metal - any of the elements in Group 1 of the periodic table

Etymology

From New Latin 'lithium', from Greek 'lithos' (stone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθiəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪθiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silver-white chemical element of the alkali metal group, the lightest metal, occurring in several minerals and used in batteries and pharmaceuticals
soft, light alkali metal element

Examples

Rechargeable batteries often contain lithium due to its lightweight and high energy density.
Doctors sometimes prescribe lithium to treat certain mood disorders.
Pure lithium is rarely found in nature because it reacts quickly with water and air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium - a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive metal of the alkali metal group
  • Synonym: potassium - a soft, silvery-white metal of the alkali metal group, elements with similar properties to lithium

Etymology

From New Latin 'lithium', from Greek 'lithos' (stone), due to its discovery in mineral form.
