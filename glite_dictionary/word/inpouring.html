inpouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌpɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌpɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a flowing in or influx, especially of something abstract such as feeling or ideas
an inflow or influx

Examples

The city saw an inpouring of refugees during the conflict.
There was an inpouring of donations after the disaster struck.
She felt an inpouring of gratitude for her friends' support.

Synset Examples

an inpouring of spiritual comfort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influx - a flowing in, especially of a large number of people or things
  • Antonym: outflow - a flowing out; efflux

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (into) + 'pouring' (present participle of pour).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌpɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌpɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
(of people, things, or substances) pouring or flowing inward; entering in large quantities or continuously.
pouring or flowing inward

Examples

The city saw an inpouring of tourists during the summer festival.
The government struggled to manage the inpouring funds from international donors.
The sudden inpouring of rain flooded the narrow streets.

Synset Examples

inpouring throngs of immigrants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influx - an arrival or entry, especially of large numbers of people or things

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (into) and 'pouring' (from 'pour').

Synset POS: s
