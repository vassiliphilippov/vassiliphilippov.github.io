Prussian

1. ADJ

/ ˈprʌʃən / (AmE), / ˈprʌʃ.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to Prussia or its inhabitants, especially in terms of history, culture, or characteristics associated with the former kingdom of Prussia.
relating to Prussia or its people

Examples

The museum features a collection of Prussian military uniforms from the nineteenth century.
His leadership style was often described as Prussian in its discipline and rigor.
They admired the efficiency of the Prussian education system.

Synset Examples

Prussian officers
Prussian aristocracy

Etymology

From Latin Prussiānus, after the region of Prussia (German Preußen).

WordNet ID: oewn-03115606-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈprʌʃən / (AmE), / ˈprʌʃən / (BrE)
a person from Prussia, a former historical region in northern Europe
person from Prussia

Examples

Her grandfather was a proud Prussian who often reminisced about his homeland.
The discipline and orderliness of the Prussians became legendary in European history.
Many Prussian traditions influenced the culture of modern Germany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German - a native or inhabitant of Germany

Etymology

From Latin Prussia, referring to the Baltic region once inhabited by the Old Prussians.

Synset POS: n
