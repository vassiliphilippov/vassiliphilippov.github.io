Cebus capucinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːbəs kəˈpjuːsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːbʊs kəˈpjuːsɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of capuchin monkey found in Central and South America, notable for its distinctive white head and thick hair resembling a monk's cowl.
species of capuchin monkey

Examples

The Cebus capucinus is often seen using tools to access food in the wild.
Researchers studied the intelligence of Cebus capucinus in various problem-solving experiments.
Tourists are sometimes fascinated by the playful behavior of the Cebus capucinus in Costa Rican forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capuchin monkey - a common name for monkeys of the genus Cebus, closely related species

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Cebus' (from Greek kēbos, meaning 'a long-tailed monkey') and species name 'capucinus' (from Italian 'Capuccino', referring to Capuchin friars, whose cowls the monkey's head resembles).
