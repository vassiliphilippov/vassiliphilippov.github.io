coati-mondi

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑːtiˈmɑndi / (AmE), / kəʊˌɑːtiˈmɒndi / (BrE)
An omnivorous mammal of Central and South America, related to raccoons, with a long flexible snout and tail.
Central/South American mammal, related to raccoon

Examples

The coati-mondi foraged in the underbrush looking for fruit and insects.
We spotted a family of coati-mondi in the rainforest during our tour.
Unlike raccoons, the coati-mondi is active mainly during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coati - another name for the same animal

Etymology

From Tupi (an indigenous language of Brazil) components likely referring to 'coati', meaning 'belt' and 'nose'.
