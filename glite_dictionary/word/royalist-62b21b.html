royalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the principle of monarchy or the authority of a king or queen
supporter of monarchy

Examples

During the English Civil War, many royalists remained loyal to King Charles I.
The royalist cause gradually lost popular support as the republic became established.
As a proud royalist, she attended every major event at the palace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: republican - a person who supports a republic and opposes monarchy
  • Synonym: monarchist - a person who supports monarchy

Etymology

From French 'royaliste', based on 'royal' (royal) + '-ist' (suffix denoting supporter of).
