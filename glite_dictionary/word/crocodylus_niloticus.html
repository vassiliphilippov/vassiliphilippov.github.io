Crocodylus niloticus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈkɑː.dɪ.ləs naɪˈlɒ.tɪ.kəs / (AmE), / krɒˈkɒ.dɪ.ləs naɪˈlɒ.tɪ.kəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Nile crocodile, a large and dangerous species widely distributed in Africa
the Nile crocodile, African species

Examples

Crocodylus niloticus is known for its size and aggressive nature throughout sub-Saharan Africa.
The National Geographic documentary focused on the hunting techniques of Crocodylus niloticus.
Scientists study the nesting habits of Crocodylus niloticus in the Nile River region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nile crocodile - large African crocodile species

Etymology

From Latin: 'crocodylus' (crocodile) and 'niloticus' (of the Nile).
