briss

1. NOUN

/ brɪs / (AmE), / brɪs / (BrE)
A Jewish religious ceremony of circumcision performed on a male child, typically on the eighth day after birth.
Jewish circumcision ceremony

Examples

The family gathered to celebrate the baby's briss in accordance with Jewish tradition.
A rabbi was invited to officiate the briss on the eighth day after birth.
Many friends and relatives attended the briss and the festive meal that followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumcision - The act or process of cutting off the foreskin, especially for medical or religious reasons

Etymology

From Yiddish ברית (bris), from Hebrew בְּרִית (b'rith) meaning 'covenant'.
