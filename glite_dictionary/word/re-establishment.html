re-establishment

1. NOUN

/ ˌri ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˌriː ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of restoring something to its former condition or position
restoration to a previous state

Examples

After years of neglect, the re-establishment of the park brought life back to the community.
Diplomatic re-establishment between the two countries was welcomed by the international community.
The re-establishment of order took time after the civil unrest.

Synset Examples

regular exercise resulted in the re-establishment of his endurance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former state
  • Antonym: abolition - the act of formally putting an end to a system or practice

Etymology

From re- (again) + establishment (the act of establishing)
