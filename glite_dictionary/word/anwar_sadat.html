Anwar Sadat

1. PROPN

/ ænˈwɑr səˈdɑt / (AmE), / ænˈwɑː səˈdæt / (BrE)
(1918–1981) Egyptian president who negotiated a peace treaty with Israel, receiving the Nobel Peace Prize in 1978.
Egyptian president and Nobel laureate (1918–1981)

Examples

Anwar Sadat played a crucial role in establishing peace between Egypt and Israel.
Many Egyptians remember Anwar Sadat for his vision and leadership.
The historic treaty was signed by Anwar Sadat and Menachem Begin in 1979.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Menachem Begin - Israeli prime minister involved in the peace treaty with Sadat
  • Synonym: President of Egypt - Head of state of Egypt; role held by Anwar Sadat
