Rub al-Khali

1. PROPN

/ ˌruːb æl ˈkɑːli / (AmE), / ˌruːb æl ˈkɑːli / (BrE)
a vast desert region in southern Saudi Arabia, known as the Empty Quarter, and one of the largest sand deserts in the world
desert in southern Saudi Arabia

Examples

The Rub al-Khali is known for its towering dunes and harsh climate.
Few explorers have managed to cross the Rub al-Khali due to its extreme conditions.
The Rub al-Khali covers parts of Saudi Arabia, Oman, the UAE, and Yemen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Empty Quarter - another name for the Rub al-Khali desert

Etymology

From Arabic ربّ الخالي (rubʿ al-khālī), meaning 'the empty quarter'.
