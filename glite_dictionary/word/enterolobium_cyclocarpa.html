Enterolobium cyclocarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.tə.roʊˈloʊ.bi.əm ˌsaɪ.kloʊˈkɑːr.pə / (AmE), / ˌɛn.tə.rəˈləʊ.bi.əm ˌsaɪ.kləʊˈkɑː.pə / (BrE)
A tropical South American tree with a wide-spreading crown of bipinnate leaves and distinctive coiled, ear-shaped fruit; cultivated as a shade tree, for ornament, and for its valuable timber.
tropical tree with ear-shaped fruit used for timber and shade

Examples

The Enterolobium cyclocarpa is valued for its broad canopy, which provides excellent shade in city parks.
Farmers often plant Enterolobium cyclocarpa to prevent soil erosion and improve the landscape.
Artisans use timber from the Enterolobium cyclocarpa to create furniture and decorative woodwork.

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) and 'lobos' (lobe), referring to the fruit shape; 'cyclocarpa' from Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'karpos' (fruit), describing the coiled fruit.
