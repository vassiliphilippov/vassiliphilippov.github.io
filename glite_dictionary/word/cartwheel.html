cartwheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtˌwil / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtˌwiːl / (BrE)
a wheel with wooden spokes and a metal rim, typically used on carts
wheel with wooden spokes for carts

Examples

The old wagon had a broken cartwheel that needed replacing.
He admired the intricate design of the wooden cartwheel at the museum.
A blacksmith was busy repairing a rusty cartwheel in his workshop.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02976695-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtˌwiːl / (BrE)
an acrobatic movement in which a person turns sideways with their hands and feet touching the ground alternately, resembling the motion of a wheel
sideways acrobatic revolution

Examples

She performed a perfect cartwheel across the gym mat during practice.
Excited by the news, the children did cartwheels on the lawn.
Learning to do a cartwheel takes balance and confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somersault - an acrobatic movement in which a person turns head over heels in the air or on the ground

Etymology

From 'cart' + 'wheel', referring to the resemblance of the motion to a wheel's spokes.

4. VERB

/ ˈkɑrtˌwil / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtˌwiːl / (BrE)
to perform a sideways somersault in which the body turns over completely with the hands and feet touching the ground alternately
perform a sideways acrobatic roll

Examples

The gymnast cartwheeled across the mat with perfect form.
Children love to cartwheel on the grass during recess.
She cartwheeled away from the group, laughing and full of energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somersault - an acrobatic movement in which a person turns head over heels in the air or on the ground

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'cartwheel', which refers to the movement's resemblance to the turning of a large wheel.
