Saccharomycetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsækəroʊˌmaɪsəˈtæsiː / (AmE), / ˌsakərəʊˌmaɪsɪˈtɛːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi comprising the typical yeasts, characterized by reproduction through budding and fermentation of carbohydrates.
family of yeasts (fungi)
medical

Examples

Researchers are studying the Saccharomycetaceae to learn more about fermentation processes.
Most commercial baking yeasts belong to the Saccharomycetaceae family.
The Saccharomycetaceae are important in biotechnology for their role in producing ethanol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - unicellular fungi that reproduce by budding

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'sakkhar' (sugar) + 'mykes' (fungus) + '-aceae' (family suffix).
