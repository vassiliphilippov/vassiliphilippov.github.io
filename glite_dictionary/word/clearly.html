clearly

1. ADV

/ ˈklɪrli / (AmE), / ˈklɪəli / (BrE)
In a way that is easy to see, hear, or understand; in an intelligible manner.
in an intelligible or obvious manner

Examples

She explained the process clearly so that everyone could understand.
The sign was clearly visible from the road.
He spoke clearly, making it easy for the audience to follow his presentation.

Synset Examples

the foreigner spoke to us quite intelligibly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligibly - in a way that can be easily understood
  • Antonym: unclearly - in a way that is not easy to understand or is ambiguous

Etymology

From clear + -ly; 'clear' from Old French *cler*, from Latin *clarus* (clear, bright).

easily seen, heard, or understood

Examples

Through the window, she could clearly see the mountains in the distance.

3. ADV

/ ˈklɪrli / (AmE), / ˈklɪəli / (BrE)
In a way that is easy to perceive, understand, or recognize; without doubt or confusion.
in an obvious, easy-to-understand way

Examples

The evidence clearly shows that he was not involved in the crime.
She spoke clearly so that everyone could understand her instructions.
It was clearly a mistake to trust him with the project.

Synset Examples

it's distinctly possible
I could clearly see myself in his situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easy to see or understand; plainly
  • Antonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear or definite

Etymology

From 'clear' + -ly, where 'clear' is from Old French 'cler', from Latin 'clarus' (clear, bright).
