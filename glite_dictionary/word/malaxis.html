Malaxis

1. NOUN

/ məˈlæksɪs / (AmE), / məˈlaksɪs / (BrE)
A large genus of mainly terrestrial orchids with one or a few pleated leaves and slender spikes of tiny mostly green flowers, distributed worldwide.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The botanist discovered a new species within the Malaxis genus during her expedition.
Some Malaxis orchids are prized by collectors for their unique foliage and delicate flowers.
Malaxis species thrive in a variety of habitats across several continents.

Etymology

From Greek *malaxis* (softening), referencing the soft or flexible texture of some plant tissues.
