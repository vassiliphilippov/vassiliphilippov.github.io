conforming

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
following accepted customs, standards, or doctrines, especially in religion or social behavior.
adhering to accepted customs or doctrines

Examples

As a conforming member of the community, she attended church every Sunday.
The school's strict rules ensured that most students maintained a conforming attitude.
Many societies reward conforming behavior while discouraging those who challenge tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconforming - not following accepted customs or standards
  • Synonym: orthodox - conforming to traditional or established beliefs, especially in religion

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'conform' (from Latin conformare: to shape, to form) with the present participle suffix '-ing'.
