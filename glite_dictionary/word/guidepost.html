guidepost

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪdˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdˌpəʊst / (BrE)
a post with a sign that gives directions or indicates the way to a place
post with sign giving directions

Examples

A guidepost marked the trailhead at the entrance to the national park.
We stopped at the guidepost to check which direction we should take.
Each intersection along the hiking route had a guidepost with distances to the next landmark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signpost - a post at the side of a road giving information such as the direction and distance to a place

Etymology

From 'guide' (to lead or direct) + 'post' (upright pole or stake).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪdˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdˌpəʊst / (BrE)
a principle or rule that offers guidance, especially in determining appropriate actions or behavior
principle offering guidance

Examples

Honesty and transparency serve as important guideposts for effective leadership.
We established several guideposts to inform the team's decision-making process.
Ethical considerations act as guideposts when facing challenging situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guideline - a general rule or principle intended to advise or direct actions

Etymology

Formed from 'guide' (to show the way) and 'post' (a marker or sign), originally referring to physical signposts.
