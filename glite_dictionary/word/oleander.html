oleander

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊliˌændər / (AmE), / ˈəʊliˌændə / (BrE)
a poisonous evergreen shrub with narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant white, pink, or red flowers, widely grown as an ornamental plant
poisonous ornamental shrub with showy flowers

Examples

We planted a row of oleander along the fence for its vibrant pink blossoms.
Children should never touch oleander leaves or flowers because the plant is highly toxic.
The hotel garden was filled with sweet-smelling oleander and other tropical plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerium - the botanical genus to which the oleander belongs

Etymology

From French 'oléandre', alteration of Late Latin 'oleandra', blend of 'olea' (olive) and 'laurus' (laurel).
