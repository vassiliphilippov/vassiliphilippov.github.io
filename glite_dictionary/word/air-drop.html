to air-drop

1. VERB

/ ˈɛrˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈeəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
to deliver or drop (supplies, equipment, etc.) by parachute from an aircraft
to drop supplies from an aircraft

Examples

The relief agency decided to air-drop food and medicine to the stranded villagers.
They air-dropped supplies behind enemy lines during the mission.
Helicopters will air-drop water to firefighters battling the remote blaze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachute - device for slowing descent from an aircraft, related to method of air-drop

Etymology

From 'air' (in the sense of atmosphere or aircraft) + 'drop'.
