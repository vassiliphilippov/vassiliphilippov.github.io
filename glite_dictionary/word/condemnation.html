condemnation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.dɛmˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɛmˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(in criminal law) a final judgment declaring someone guilty in a criminal case and the punishment imposed.
final guilty judgment and punishment (law)
legal

Examples

After the lengthy trial, the condemnation of the defendant was read out in court.
The judge’s condemnation included a lengthy prison sentence and a heavy fine.
His lawyer plans to appeal the condemnation on the grounds of insufficient evidence.

Synset Examples

the conviction came as no surprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conviction - the act of officially finding someone guilty of a crime in a court of law
  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they have been charged

Etymology

From Latin condemnatio (damnation, sentence), from condemnare (to condemn).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.dəmˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dəmˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(law) The act of officially declaring something, such as property or a building, unfit for use or subject to seizure for public use.
official declaration of unfitness or expropriation
legal

Examples

After the city's condemnation of the old theater, it was demolished to make way for a new park.
The condemnation process requires authorities to prove the building is unsafe for residents.
Government condemnation of land for highway expansion often leads to disputes with property owners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expropriation - the act of taking away property for public use
  • Synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession of something by legal right

Etymology

From Latin 'condemnatio', from 'condemnare' (to sentence, to condemn).

an invocation or appeal to a supernatural power asking for harm or misfortune to befall someone or a group
invocation of evil or curse

Examples

The witch uttered a condemnation against those who had wronged her.
Many feared the condemnation of the gods would fall upon the city.
In ancient texts, a condemnation was believed to bring sickness and misfortune.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blessing - an invocation of divine favour and protection
