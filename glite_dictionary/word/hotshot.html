hotshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌʃɒt / (BrE)
a person who is exceptionally skilled or talented at something, often regarded with admiration or envy
exceptionally skilled or talented person
informal

Examples

The company hired a hotshot from Silicon Valley to lead their new project.
Everyone expected the new hotshot lawyer to win the high-profile case.
Despite being a hotshot in his field, he remained surprisingly humble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with great knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to a field or activity

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang (mid-20th century), from 'hot' (excellent) + 'shot' (person of importance or talent).
