sightless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪtlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtlɪs / (BrE)
unable to see; without sight.
lacking sight; unable to see

Examples

The sightless man navigated the room by touch and sound alone.
In total darkness, even the most alert person becomes effectively sightless.
Poets often use the word sightless in reference to night or blindness.

Synset Examples

blind as an eyeless beggar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind - unable to see
  • Antonym: seeing - having the ability to see

Etymology

From Middle English sightles, from sight + -less.
