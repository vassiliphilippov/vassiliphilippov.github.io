barbeque

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
a rack or grill for cooking meat over hot charcoal, usually outdoors
outdoor rack or grill for cooking meat

Examples

We cooked steaks on the barbeque in the backyard all afternoon.
The old barbeque needed cleaning before the party started.
He built his own brick barbeque next to the patio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a metal frame for cooking food over fire

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', adapted from Haitian Taino 'barabicu' (framework of sticks for cooking).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
a meal or gathering at which meat and other food are cooked out of doors on a rack over an open fire or hot coals, especially a whole animal roasted on a spit
outdoor meal with food cooked over an open fire

Examples

We invited our neighbors to a barbeque in the backyard last Saturday.
The annual barbeque featured a whole pig roasted over an open fire.
At the beach, they set up a barbeque and enjoyed grilled chicken and vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookout - informal outdoor meal where food is cooked over an open flame

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', from Taíno, referring to a framework of sticks for roasting or sleeping.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07592346-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
Meat that has been cooked slowly on a grill or over an open fire, typically seasoned with a highly spiced sauce.
meat cooked with spiced sauce on a grill

Examples

He ordered a plate of barbeque with a side of coleslaw and beans.
The barbeque was tender, smoky, and full of flavor.
Nothing beats homemade barbeque at a summer picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - Alternative spelling for barbeque (meat cooked over fire with sauce)

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', possibly from Taino (Arawakan) language, referring to a framework of sticks for grilling meat.

Synset POS: n
