Malaconotinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˌkoʊˈnoʊtɪni / (AmE), / ˌmaləkəˈnəʊtɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of African bush shrikes, small to medium-sized predatory songbirds found mainly in sub-Saharan Africa.
subfamily of African bush shrikes

Examples

The Malaconotinae are known for their varied and colorful plumage.
Researchers observed several species within the Malaconotinae during their expedition to East Africa.
Birdwatchers often try to spot members of the Malaconotinae in their natural bushveld habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) + 'noton' (back), used in zoological nomenclature.
