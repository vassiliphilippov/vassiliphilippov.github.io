duellist

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a duel, especially one fought according to formal rules
person who fights in a duel

Examples

The notorious duellist challenged several men to fight over the years.
As a skilled duellist, he was feared and respected in society.
The code of honor was strictly followed by every duellist involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duelist - person who fights in a duel (US spelling)

Etymology

From duel + -ist, modeled after French 'duelliste'.
