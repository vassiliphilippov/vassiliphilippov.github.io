apartheid

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːr.taɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɑː.taɪd / (BrE)
a system or policy of segregation or discrimination on grounds of race, especially the former official policy in South Africa.
racial segregation policy (esp. South Africa)

Examples

The end of apartheid marked a new era of equality in South Africa.
Many international communities imposed sanctions to protest apartheid policies.
She studied the effects of apartheid on education and social structure.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'apartheid' (separateness), from 'apart' (separate) + '-heid' (hood, state).
