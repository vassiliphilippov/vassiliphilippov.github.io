Begonia dregei

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡoʊniə ˈdreɪdʒiː / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə ˈdreɪdʒiː / (BrE)
a species of tuberous or semi-tuberous begonia native to South Africa, known for its shallowly lobed ovate leaves and small white flowers
South African tuberous begonia species

Examples

The Begonia dregei is prized by collectors for its distinctive foliage and delicate flowers.
She grew a Begonia dregei in her greenhouse alongside other rare begonia species.
Because of its tuberous roots, Begonia dregei requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Etymology

Named after Johann Franz Drege, a 19th-century German plant collector; 'Begonia' from Michel Bégon, a French patron of botany.
