gingiva

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪnˈdʒaɪvə / (AmE), / dʒɪnˈdʒɪvə / (BrE)
the tissue of the jaws, covered by mucous membrane, that surrounds the bases of the teeth; gums
gum tissue around the teeth
medical

Examples

Inflammation of the gingiva can be an early sign of gum disease.
The dentist examined the patient's gingiva for signs of recession.
Proper oral hygiene helps maintain healthy gingiva and prevents tooth loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - the flesh that surrounds the base of the teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'gingiva' (the gums).
