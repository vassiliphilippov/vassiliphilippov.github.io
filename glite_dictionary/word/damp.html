damp

1. VERB

/ dæmp / (AmE), / damp / (BrE)
to make a sound or noise less strong or loud, especially by wrapping or covering; to deaden or muffle
to deaden or muffle a sound

Examples

The technician used foam panels to damp the noise in the recording studio.
She quickly tried to damp the sound of the door by wrapping it with a towel.
Heavy curtains were installed to damp the echoes in the concert hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muffle - to make a sound quieter or less distinct

2. NOUN

/ dæmp / (AmE), / dæmp / (BrE)
A slight or moderate amount of moisture in the air or on a surface, making something slightly wet.
slight wetness or moisture

Examples

A cold damp settled over the room after the rain.
He wiped the damp from the window with a cloth.
You can feel the damp in old cellars, especially in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moisture - water or liquid diffused in a small quantity
  • Antonym: dryness - the state of not having moisture

Etymology

From Middle English *dampe* (vapor), probably of Germanic origin.

slightly wet

make less strong or intense

Examples

New safety measures are designed to damp the impact of sudden shocks on the machinery.
The administration tried to damp public fears after the announcement.
She placed a cushion to damp the noise of the falling vase.

Synset Examples

soften a shock
break a fall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dampen - to make less strong or intense; common variant form
  • Synonym: to soften - to make less severe, harsh, or strong

5. VERB

/ dæmp / (AmE), / damp / (BrE)
to make something less strong, lively, or exciting; to restrain or discourage
restrain or make less strong or lively

Examples

The CEO's criticism damped the team's enthusiasm.
Rain during the festival damped spirits, but the crowd remained cheerful.
Her worries about the future damp her excitement over the new job.

Synset Examples

the sudden bad news damped the joyous atmosphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: to restrain - hold back or restrict
  • Antonym: to encourage - give support or confidence to

Etymology

From Middle English 'damp', possibly related to Old Norse 'dampi' (vapor). Verb sense extended metaphorically from literal meaning.
