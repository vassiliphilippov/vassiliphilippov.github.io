Jasminum mesnyi

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæzmɪnəm ˈmɛsni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈjasmɪnəm ˈmɛsni.aɪ / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to western China, known for its rambling habit and yellow flowers; commonly called primrose jasmine.
evergreen yellow-flowered shrub native to China

Examples

The Jasminum mesnyi in my garden blooms with a cascade of bright yellow flowers each spring.
Botanists recommend Jasminum mesnyi as an ornamental plant for its evergreen foliage.
She planted a row of Jasminum mesnyi along her fence for year-round greenery and color.

Etymology

Latin scientific binomial: genus 'Jasminum' from Arabic 'yasemin' (jasmine); species 'mesnyi' named after Alexandre Henri Gabriel de Cassini de Mesny, a French botanist.
