Melanogrammus aeglefinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlənoʊˌɡræməs ˌiːˈɡlɛfɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛlənəʊˌɡræməs ˌiːˈɡlɛfɪnəs / (BrE)
a North Atlantic food fish, related to cod but typically smaller, commonly known as haddock
North Atlantic food fish; haddock

Examples

The North Atlantic fisheries rely heavily on Melanogrammus aeglefinus as a key export.
Chefs often prefer Melanogrammus aeglefinus for its delicate, flaky flesh in traditional dishes.
Scientists monitor Melanogrammus aeglefinus populations to assess the health of ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haddock - common name for Melanogrammus aeglefinus, a North Atlantic food fish
  • Synonym: cod - a large, commercially important food fish, closely related but distinct from haddock

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus Melanogrammus (from Greek melas 'black' + gramma 'line') and species name aeglefinus.
