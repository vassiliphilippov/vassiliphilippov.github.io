swingletree

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪŋltri / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋltri / (BrE)
A horizontal bar attached to the traces of a draft horse, connecting them to the vehicle or implement being pulled, allowing for even distribution of force.
crossbar for draft horse harness

Examples

The old wagon still had a sturdy swingletree connecting the horse to the cart.
After years of use, the wooden swingletree needed to be replaced due to wear.
Each draft horse was fitted with a harness and a swingletree for plowing the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiffletree - another word for swingletree, especially in North American English

Etymology

From 'swingle' (a pivoting bar) and 'tree' (bar of wood or metal), both of Middle English origin.
