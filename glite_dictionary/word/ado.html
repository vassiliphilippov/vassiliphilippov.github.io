ado

1. NOUN

/ əˈdu / (AmE), / əˈduː / (BrE)
Busy activity, fuss, or commotion, especially when unnecessary.
commotion; unnecessary fuss

Examples

There was much ado about the new policy at the meeting.
Without further ado, let us begin the ceremony.
All that ado over a minor mistake seemed unnecessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuss - unnecessary or excessive activity or worry
  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance

Etymology

From Middle English 'adoen' (to do), from 'at do', meaning 'to do'.
