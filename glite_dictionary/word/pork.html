pork

1. NOUN

/ pɔrk / (AmE), / pɔːk / (BrE)
the meat from a domestic pig, eaten as food
meat from a pig

Examples

She made a delicious stew with fresh pork and vegetables.
Many people enjoy pork chops grilled or roasted for dinner.
The restaurant offers a variety of pork dishes on its menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beef - meat from cattle
  • Synonym: lamb - meat from young sheep
  • Synonym: chicken - meat from chicken

Etymology

From Old French *porc*, from Latin *porcus* (pig).

2. NOUN

/ pɔrk / (AmE), / pɔːk / (BrE)
Government funds, projects, or spending intended to benefit a particular legislator's constituents, often to gain political support rather than for public good.
government funds for local projects to win support
american slang

Examples

Critics argue that much of the spending in the bill is simply pork meant to appease local voters.
The senator was accused of securing pork for his district rather than addressing national priorities.
Voters are growing tired of politicians who focus on bringing home pork instead of real reform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pork barrel - government appropriation for local projects to win votes

Etymology

American political slang, from the practice of distributing pork as a reward; figurative use dates to 19th-century U.S. politics.
