Donato Bramante

1. PROPN

/ doʊˈnɑːtoʊ brɑːˈmɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / dəˈnɑːtəʊ brəˈmænti / (BrE)
(1444–1514) Italian architect of the High Renaissance, known for designing St. Peter's Basilica and influencing Renaissance architecture.
Italian High Renaissance architect (1444–1514)

Examples

Donato Bramante played a pivotal role in the development of Renaissance architecture in Rome.
The Tempietto in San Pietro in Montorio was designed by Donato Bramante and is considered a masterpiece.
Many historians regard Donato Bramante as the original architect behind St. Peter's Basilica.

Etymology

Italian name; Donato from Latin 'Donatus' (given), Bramante a surname.
