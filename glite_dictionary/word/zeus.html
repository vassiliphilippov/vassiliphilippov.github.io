Zeus

1. NOUN

/ ˈzuːs / (AmE), / ˈzjuːs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Zeidae, consisting of certain marine fish including the John Dory.
genus of marine fish (Zeidae family)

Examples

The John Dory belongs to the genus Zeus in the family Zeidae.
Researchers identified a new species within the Zeus genus off the Australian coast.
Members of the genus Zeus are known for their distinctive body shape.

Etymology

From Greek mythology; applied in taxonomy for the genus.

2. PROPN

/ zuːs / (AmE), / zjuːs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the chief god, ruler of Mount Olympus, and god of the sky and thunder.
chief god in Greek mythology

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed that Zeus controlled the sky and wielded thunderbolts.
Many myths describe how Zeus intervened in the affairs of gods and mortals alike.
The statue of Zeus at Olympia was considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jupiter - chief god in Roman mythology; counterpart of Zeus

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ζεύς (Zeús), chief deity in Greek religion.

3. NOUN

/ ˈzuː.əs / (AmE), / ˈzjuː.əs / (BrE)
A genus of fishes that is the type genus of the family Zeidae, including the well-known John Dory.
genus of fishes (family Zeidae)

Examples

The Zeus genus includes the John Dory, a well-known marine fish.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Zeus genus off the coast of Australia.
Several members of the Zeus genus are found in temperate and subtropical waters.

Etymology

Named after Zeus, the principal god in Greek mythology.
