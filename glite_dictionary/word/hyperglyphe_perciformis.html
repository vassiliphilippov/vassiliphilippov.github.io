Hyperglyphe perciformis

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.pɚˈɡlɪf fiː ˌpɜːr.sɪˈfɔːr.mɪs / (AmE), / haɪ.pəˈɡlɪf fiː ˌpɜː.sɪˈfɔː.mɪs / (BrE)
A species of blackish fish native to the waters of New England, commonly known as the barrelfish.
blackish New England fish species

Examples

The Hyperglyphe perciformis is often found in deep Atlantic waters off the coast of New England.
Fishermen sometimes catch Hyperglyphe perciformis while searching for other deep-sea species.
The distinctive scales of Hyperglyphe perciformis help differentiate it from other Perciformes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrelfish - Common name for Hyperglyphe perciformis, a dark-colored deep-sea fish

Etymology

From Greek 'hyperglyphe' (a genus name), and Latin 'perciformis' meaning 'perch-like'.
