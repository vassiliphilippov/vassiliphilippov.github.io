Ling-pao

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈpaʊ / (AmE), / lɪŋˈpaʊ / (BrE)
the second member of the Taoist Trinity, regarded as a divine being in Taoist cosmology
second deity of the Taoist Trinity

Examples

The temple was dedicated to Ling-pao, the second person of the Taoist Trinity.
In Taoist rituals, offerings are often made to Ling-pao for protection and blessings.
Scholars study the myths surrounding Ling-pao to understand Taoist cosmology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Yuanshi Tianzun - the first member of the Taoist Trinity
  • Degree Higher: Daode Tianzun - the third member of the Taoist Trinity

Etymology

From Mandarin 靈寶 (Língbǎo), meaning 'Divine Treasure' or 'Spiritual Treasure'.
