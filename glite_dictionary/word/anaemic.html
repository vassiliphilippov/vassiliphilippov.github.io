anaemic

1. ADJ

/ əˈniːmɪk / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪk / (BrE)
lacking in energy, strength, or vitality; weak or feeble
lacking energy or strength

Examples

The play received anaemic reviews from the critics, who found it slow and unconvincing.
After a week of illness, her performance at work was noticeably anaemic.
His argument for the proposal was anaemic and failed to persuade the board.

Synset Examples

an anemic attempt to hit the baseball

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vigorous - strong, healthy, and full of energy
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
  • Synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness

Etymology

From Greek 'anaimos' meaning 'without blood', via French 'anémique'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈniːmɪk / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪk / (BrE)
suffering from anemia or relating to anemia, a condition in which there is a deficiency of red cells or hemoglobin in the blood.
lacking healthy red blood cells (medical)
medical british

Examples

After months of feeling tired, the doctor told her she was anaemic and needed more iron.
The patient’s anaemic condition required careful monitoring by the medical team.
Her anaemic appearance worried her friends and family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemic - suffering from or relating to anemia (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek anaimia (lack of blood): 'an-' (without) + 'haima' (blood).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03052845-a
Synset POS: a
