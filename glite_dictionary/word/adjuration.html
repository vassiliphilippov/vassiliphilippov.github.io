adjuration

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a solemn and earnest urging or appeal to someone to do something
solemn, earnest appeal or urging

Examples

Despite her mother's adjuration to be careful, she took the risk anyway.
The knight made an adjuration to his companions, urging them not to lose hope.
His speech ended with an adjuration for unity in the face of adversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entreaty - an earnest or humble request
  • Antonym: command - an authoritative order or directive

Etymology

From Latin adjuratio (a swearing to), from adjurare (to swear to).
