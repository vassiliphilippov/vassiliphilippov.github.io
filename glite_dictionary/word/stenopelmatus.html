Stenopelmatus

1. NOUN

/ stɛnoʊˈpɛlmətəs / (AmE), / stɛnəʊˈpɛlmətəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, wingless insects commonly known as sand crickets, native to the Americas.
a genus of sand crickets

Examples

The Stenopelmatus is usually found under rocks and logs in sandy areas.
Entomologists are studying the behavior of several Stenopelmatus species in their natural habitat.
Unlike other crickets, the Stenopelmatus lacks wings and cannot chirp.

Etymology

From Greek *stenos* (narrow) + *pelma* (sole, foot), referring to the insect's narrow feet.

2. NOUN

/ stɛnəˈpɛlmətəs / (AmE), / stɛnəˈpɛlmətəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, flightless insects known as sand crickets, native to the Americas
genus of sand crickets

Examples

The Stenopelmatus genus includes the well-known Jerusalem cricket of western North America.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Stenopelmatus genus during their expedition.
Unlike other crickets, members of Stenopelmatus lack the ability to chirp.

Etymology

From Greek 'steno-' (narrow) + 'pelma' (sole of the foot), referring to the insect’s body structure.
