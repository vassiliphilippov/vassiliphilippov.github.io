burthen

1. VERB

/ ˈbɜːrðən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθən / (BrE)
(archaic) To weigh down with a load; to burden or oppress.
to weigh down with a load
archaic

Examples

The old horse was burthened with heavy sacks of grain along the narrow road.
She felt burthened by the constant worries of her family.
The ship was burthening under the weight of its precious cargo as it sailed into the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or oppress

Etymology

From Middle English burthen, an older form of 'burden,' from Old English byrþen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːðən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Archaic) An alternative spelling of 'burden'; a load, responsibility, or something carried, figuratively or literally.
archaic spelling of 'burden'
archaic

Examples

The burthen of leadership often weighs heavily on those in power.
He laid down his burthen after a long and difficult journey.
Poets sometimes use the word burthen instead of 'burden' in old verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a load, especially a heavy one; responsibility

Etymology

Obsolete variant spelling of 'burden', from Middle English 'burthen', influenced by Old English 'byrþen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02927407-n
Synset POS: n
