mantelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæntəlɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæntəlɪt / (BrE)
a portable shelter, often bulletproof, used to protect soldiers from enemy fire; especially used in sieges or on battlefields in the past
portable bulletproof military shelter

Examples

During the siege, soldiers moved a mantelet forward to protect themselves from arrows.
The old fortress still displayed a wooden mantelet once used by defenders.
Engineers built a makeshift mantelet to shield workers repairing the breach in the wall.

Etymology

From French 'mantelet', diminutive of 'mantel' (cloak), from Latin 'mantellum'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæntələt / (AmE), / ˈmantəlɛt / (BrE)
A short cape or cloak worn by women, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.
short women's cape or cloak

Examples

She wore a delicate lace mantelet over her gown at the ball.
The museum displayed a velvet mantelet from the Victorian era.
Her grandmother's old photographs showed her in a mantelet and long skirt.

Etymology

From French 'mantelet', diminutive of 'mantel' meaning cloak or mantle.
