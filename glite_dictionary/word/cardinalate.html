cardinalate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdənəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnələt / (BrE)
the collective body of cardinals in the Roman Catholic Church, responsible for electing and advising the pope
body of cardinals in the Catholic Church

Examples

The death of the pope prompted the cardinalate to convene and select his successor.
Throughout history, the cardinalate has played a vital role in shaping church policies.
He was honored to join the cardinalate after years of devoted service to the Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: college of cardinals - the official assembly of all cardinals responsible for electing the pope

Etymology

From Latin cardinalatus (office of a cardinal), from cardinalis (cardinal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdɪn(ə)lˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪn(ə)lət / (BrE)
the office or rank of a cardinal in the Roman Catholic Church
office or rank of a cardinal

Examples

The pope appointed him to the cardinalate after years of service.
Her uncle’s cardinalate was marked by a commitment to social reform.
Following the scandal, he resigned from the cardinalate in disgrace.

Synset Examples

following the scandal, the cardinal resigned his cardinalate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal - a leading dignitary of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cardinalatus, from cardinalis (cardinal).
