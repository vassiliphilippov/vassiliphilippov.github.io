forepart

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌpɑrt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːpɑːt / (BrE)
the front part or section of something, especially of an object, animal, or structure
front section of something

Examples

The lion's forepart is massive and muscular, built for strength and speed.
Damage to the ship's forepart made it difficult to stay afloat during the storm.
The architect focused on the forepart of the building, designing an impressive entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: front - the forward part or side of something
  • Antonym: rear - the back part or side of something

Etymology

From Middle English forepart, from fore- (meaning 'before, front') + part.
