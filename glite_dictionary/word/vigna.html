Vigna

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnjə / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical vines and erect herbs, including cowpeas and mung beans, with trifoliate leaves and yellowish or purplish flowers
genus of tropical vines and herbs (e.g., cowpea, mung bean)

Examples

The Vigna genus includes economically important crops like the mung bean and the cowpea.
Studies on Vigna species help improve agricultural productivity in tropical regions.
Botanists often debate the classification of Vigna in relation to the genus Phaseolus.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Domenico Vigna (died 1647).

2. NOUN

/ ˈviːn.jə / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡ.nə / (BrE)
A genus of climbing or erect herbs with trifoliate leaves and yellowish or purplish flowers, found in warm or tropical regions, including important food plants such as cowpeas and mung beans.
genus of tropical legumes, including cowpea and mung bean

Examples

Several species in the Vigna genus are cultivated for their edible beans.
The cowpea, a staple in many African cuisines, belongs to the Vigna genus.
Botanists debated whether to classify certain beans under Vigna or Phaseolus.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Domenico Vigna (died 1647).
