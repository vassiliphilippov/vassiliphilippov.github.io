Himantopus novae-zelandiae

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəpəs ˌnoʊveɪ zəˈlændi.aɪ / (AmE), / hɪˌmæntəpəs ˌnəʊ.viː zɪˈlændi.aɪ / (BrE)
a rare, mostly black wading bird native to New Zealand, sometimes considered a subspecies or color form of the white-headed stilt
rare black stilt of New Zealand

Examples

The Himantopus novae-zelandiae is one of the world's rarest wading birds, with only a few individuals remaining in the wild.
Efforts to save the Himantopus novae-zelandiae have included captive breeding and habitat restoration.
Unlike its relatives, the Himantopus novae-zelandiae is distinguished by its mostly black plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black stilt - common name of Himantopus novae-zelandiae, a rare black wading bird from New Zealand

Etymology

From Latin: Himantopus (genus name from Greek for 'strap foot'), novae-zelandiae (of New Zealand).
