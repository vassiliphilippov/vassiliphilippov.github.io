Rhamnus frangula

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmnəs frænˈɡjulə / (AmE), / ˈræmnəs fraŋˈɡjuːlə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, commonly known as alder buckthorn.
a Eurasian buckthorn tree species

Examples

The berries of the Rhamnus frangula are known for their purgative properties.
Botanists surveyed a woodland dominated by Rhamnus frangula near the riverbank.
Many birds feed on the fruit of Rhamnus frangula in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder buckthorn - Common name for Rhamnus frangula

Etymology

From Latin: Rhamnus (the genus name for buckthorns) and frangula (possibly from Latin frangere, to break, referring to the brittle wood).
