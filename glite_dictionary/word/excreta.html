excreta

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskritə / (AmE), / ɪkˈskriːtə / (BrE)
Waste matter, especially feces or urine, discharged from the body.
bodily waste matter (esp. feces, urine)
medical

Examples

Hospital workers must take care when handling human excreta to prevent the spread of infection.
Contaminated water sources are often polluted with animal excreta.
Proper disposal of excreta is essential for good sanitation in communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feces - Waste matter discharged from the bowels; excrement
  • Synonym: urine - Liquid waste discharged by the kidneys

Etymology

From Latin 'excretus', past participle of 'excerere', meaning 'to separate or discharge'.
