to appear

1. VERB

/ əˈpɪr / (AmE), / əˈpɪə / (BrE)
to come into sight or become visible
come into sight; become visible

Examples

A rainbow may appear in the sky after a rainstorm.
She was surprised when he appeared at her front door unannounced.
The ship suddenly appeared on the horizon, exciting all the passengers.

Synset Examples

He suddenly appeared at the wedding
A new star appeared on the horizon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear from view
  • Synonym: to emerge - to come out into view

Etymology

From Latin apparēre (to come in sight, become visible).

/ əˈpɪr / (AmE), / əˈpɪə / (BrE)
(of a situation or fact) to give the impression of being true, probable, or apparent, often used with 'to' or 'that'.

Examples
