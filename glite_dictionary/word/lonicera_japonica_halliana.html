Lonicera japonica halliana

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə hæˈliːənə / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə dʒəˈpəʊnɪkə hæˈliːənə / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of Japanese honeysuckle that grows as a vine and is known for its aggressive spread, especially in the southeastern United States.
variety of Japanese honeysuckle vine

Examples

Lonicera japonica halliana has quickly taken over the fence in our backyard with its dense, leafy vines.
Gardeners appreciate Lonicera japonica halliana for its fragrant white and yellow flowers, though it can become invasive.
The southeastern United States has seen the spread of Lonicera japonica halliana in many natural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese honeysuckle - A species of honeysuckle native to East Asia, often invasive in other regions

Etymology

From modern Latin, with genus Lonicera named after German botanist Adam Lonicer, and japonica meaning 'of Japan'.
