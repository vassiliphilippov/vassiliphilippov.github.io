flower

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the period of greatest prosperity, development, or productivity; the best part or prime of something
the prime or best period

Examples

During the flower of her career, she received many awards for her achievements.
The city was in the flower of its prosperity during the Renaissance.
He was still in the flower of youth when he wrote his best works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime - the period of greatest vigor or success
  • Antonym: decline - the period after the height of success or productivity

Etymology

From Old French 'flor', from Latin 'flos' (flower, blossom); figurative use for 'prime' dates to Middle English.

2. VERB

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce or yield flowers; to bloom.
produce or yield flowers (of a plant)

Examples

The tulips in the garden flower every spring.
Many desert plants only flower after a heavy rainfall.
The apple tree flowered beautifully this year, promising a good harvest.

Synset Examples

The cherry tree bloomed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloom - (of a plant) to produce flowers; to be in flower
  • Antonym: to wither - (of a plant) to become dry and shrivelled; lose freshness and vitality

3. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
a plant that is grown for its blooms or blossoms, especially in a garden or for decorative purposes
plant cultivated for its blooms

Examples

She planted a flower in the front yard to add some color to her garden.
Roses are a popular type of flower grown for their beautiful blooms.
Every spring, the flowers in the park attract crowds of visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossom - flower of a plant, especially one bearing fruit

Etymology

From Old French *flor*, from Latin *flos* (flower, blossom).

4. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
The reproductive structure of flowering plants, typically consisting of petals and various reproductive organs, often showy or colorful.
reproductive part of flowering plants

Examples

The flower of the rose bush opened early in the morning.
She picked a flower and placed it in her hair for the party.
Each flower on the tree has delicate white petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossom - a flower or group of flowers, especially of a tree or bush

Etymology

From Old French 'flor', from Latin 'flos', 'floris' (flower, blossom).
