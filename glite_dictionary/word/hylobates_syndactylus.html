Hylobates syndactylus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈloʊbətiːz sɪnˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / hɪˈlɒbətiːz sɪnˈdaktɪləs / (BrE)
a large black gibbon native to Sumatra, distinguished by the partial webbing between its second and third toes
large Sumatran black gibbon species

Examples

The Hylobates syndactylus is notable for its haunting calls echoing through the Sumatran rainforest.
Researchers observed a family of Hylobates syndactylus swinging gracefully among the treetops.
Unlike most gibbons, the Hylobates syndactylus has partially united toes, aiding its arboreal lifestyle.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Hylobates' (the genus name, from Greek 'hylē' forest and 'bates' walker) and Latinized Greek 'syndactylus' meaning 'with joined fingers'.
