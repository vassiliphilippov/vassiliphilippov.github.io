monorchism

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɔrkɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈnɔːkɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the condition of having only one testis, especially due to the failure of one testis to descend into the scrotum
condition of having only one testis
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with monorchism after a routine physical examination.
Monorchism can be congenital or result from injury or disease.
The doctor explained that monorchism rarely affects fertility if the remaining testis is healthy.

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + orkhis (testicle) + -ism.
