restorer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstɔrər / (AmE), / rɪˈstɔːrə / (BrE)
a skilled person who restores buildings, artworks, furniture, or other objects to their original condition
person who restores buildings or objects

Examples

The museum hired a restorer to repair the damaged painting.
A skilled restorer can make old furniture look as good as new.
They consulted a restorer before renovating the historic manor house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservator - a person responsible for the preservation and restoration of artworks or historical items

Etymology

From restore + -er, meaning one who restores.
