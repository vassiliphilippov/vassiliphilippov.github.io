seaquake

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌkweɪk / (BrE)
An earthquake that takes place on the sea floor, especially one that may cause a tsunami.
earthquake at the sea bed

Examples

The devastating seaquake triggered a massive tsunami along the coastline.
Scientists use seismographs to detect seaquakes deep under the ocean.
A powerful seaquake was felt by crews on several fishing boats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthquake - a sudden shaking of the ground caused by the movement of the Earth's crust

Etymology

Formed from 'sea' + 'quake,' modeled on 'earthquake.'
