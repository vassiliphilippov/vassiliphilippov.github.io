Myrciaria cauliflora

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˌsiːɛəriə ˌkɔləˈflɔrə / (AmE), / mɪˌsɪəriə ˌkɔːlɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A small evergreen tropical tree native to Brazil and the West Indies, grown for its edible, tough-skinned, purple, grape-like fruit that grows directly on the branches.
tropical tree grown for grape-like fruit

Examples

The Myrciaria cauliflora is prized in Brazil for the sweet fruit that grows directly on its trunk and branches.
Farmers in Florida have started cultivating Myrciaria cauliflora to supply the growing demand for exotic fruits.
Unlike most trees, the fruit of Myrciaria cauliflora appears all along its woody stems rather than at the tips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaboticaba - the common name for the fruit of Myrciaria cauliflora

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name 'Myrciaria' (from Greek 'myrciaria', relating to myrtle) and the Latin 'cauliflorus' (flowering on the stem).
