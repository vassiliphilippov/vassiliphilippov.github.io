elbow

1. VERB

/ ˈɛlˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛlbəʊ / (BrE)
to push one's way through a crowd by using the elbows
push one's way with the elbows

Examples

She elbowed her way to the front of the crowded bus.
Fans elbowed past each other to get closer to the stage.
He elbowed through the packed hallway, determined not to be late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jostle - push or bump against someone roughly

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛlˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛl.bəʊ / (BrE)
the part of a sleeve that covers the elbow joint
part of sleeve at the elbow

Examples

My shirt has holes at the elbows from years of wear.
She rested her elbow on the table, wrinkling the sleeve at the joint.
The tailor added extra fabric to the elbows to prevent them from wearing out.

Synset Examples

his coat had patches over the elbows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuff - the end part of a sleeve, where the material is turned back or a separate band is sewn on

Etymology

From Old English elnboga (meaning bend of the arm), related to the joint.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛlˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛl.bəʊ / (BrE)
the joint between the upper and lower parts of the arm in humans and corresponding joints in mammals and birds
joint between upper and lower arm

Examples

She accidentally bumped her elbow against the table.
The bird tucked its wing close to its elbow as it landed.
He wore pads to protect his elbows during the game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knee - joint between thigh and lower leg

Etymology

Old English 'elnboga', from 'eln' (forearm) + 'boga' (bow, bend).
