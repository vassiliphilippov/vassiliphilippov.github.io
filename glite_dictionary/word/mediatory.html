mediatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiətəri / (BrE)
relating to or intended to mediate or bring about an agreement between parties in conflict
related to mediation or mediating

Examples

The ambassador took a mediatory approach to ease tensions between the two countries.
Their mediatory efforts led to a successful compromise during the negotiations.
A mediatory role is essential in resolving longstanding disputes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciliatory - intended to pacify or bring about agreement
  • Antonym: adversarial - involving conflict or opposition rather than mediation

Etymology

From Latin 'mediator' (one who mediates) + English '-y'.
