respectably

1. ADV

/ rɪˈspɛktəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛktəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that is considered decent, proper, or morally reputable.
in a decent or reputable manner

Examples

The children dressed respectably for the school ceremony, wearing clean shirts and polished shoes.
He conducted himself respectably in court, earning the judge's admiration for his composure.
Although they struggled financially, they managed to live respectably in a modest home.

Synset Examples

the film ends with the middle-aged romancers respectably married

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decently - in a decent or proper manner
  • Antonym: improperly - in a manner not conforming to accepted standards

Etymology

From 'respectable' + '-ly', where 'respectable' comes from Latin 'respectare' (to look back at, regard).

2. ADV

/ rɪˈspɛktəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛktəbli / (BrE)
to a fairly good or acceptable degree; in a way that is decent, proper, or worthy of respect
to a fairly good or acceptable degree

Examples

Despite his lack of experience, he performed respectably in the competition.
The team finished respectably in the league, taking fifth place overall.
She managed to recover respectably after the initial setback.

Synset Examples

he did respectably well for his age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decently - to a reasonable or satisfactory degree
  • Antonym: poorly - to an unsatisfactory or bad degree

Etymology

From the adjective 'respectable' + -ly, forming the adverb.
