consolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the comfort or relief provided to someone in distress or disappointment
comfort given during distress

Examples

After her loss, she found consolation in the kind words of her friends.
His support during my illness was a great consolation to me.
A small victory was some consolation after a difficult season.

Synset Examples

his presence was a consolation to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint
  • Antonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

From Latin 'consolatio', from 'consolari' (to comfort).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
Comfort received by a person after a loss or disappointment.
comfort after disappointment or loss

Examples

Winning the silver medal was small consolation for the athlete who had hoped for gold.
After losing her job, she took consolation in the support of her friends.
His words of consolation helped ease my disappointment.

Synset Examples

second place was no consolation to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - soothing or relief given during distress or sorrow
  • Antonym: distress - great pain, anxiety, or sorrow; acute physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Latin consolatio, from consolari (to console).
