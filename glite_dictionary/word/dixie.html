Dixie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪksi / (AmE), / ˈdɪksi / (BrE)
the southern states of the United States, especially those that seceded during the Civil War (1861–1865)
the southern US states, especially in the Civil War era

Examples

Many people in Dixie still celebrate traditional Southern customs and festivals.
The Civil War changed life in Dixie forever.
Tourists often visit historic plantations throughout Dixie to learn about its past.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the Mason–Dixon Line or ten-dollar notes from Louisiana marked 'dix'.
