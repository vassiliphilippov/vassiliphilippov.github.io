wheeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlər / (AmE), / ˈwiːlə / (BrE)
the person positioned at the outermost end of a rank during a wheeling maneuver, such as in marching or military formations.
outermost person in wheeling formation

Examples

During the parade, the wheeler guided the turn around the corner.
The success of the maneuver depended on the precision of the wheeler at the end of the row.
Each wheeler received special instructions before the drill began.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlər / (AmE), / ˈwiːlə / (BrE)
a person who rides a bicycle
person who rides a bicycle

Examples

The club welcomed any enthusiastic wheeler to join their weekend rides.
As a keen wheeler, she cycled through the countryside every morning.
Old posters depicted a group of wheelers gathering for a race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclist - a person who rides a bicycle

Etymology

Formed from 'wheel' + '-er', denoting a person associated with wheels.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwiːlə / (BrE)
a person who makes and repairs wooden wheels, especially for carriages and wagons
person who makes wooden wheels

Examples

The village wheeler was renowned for his craftsmanship and attention to detail.
As a skilled wheeler, she restored antique wooden cartwheels for the local museum.
In the 19th century, every rural community relied on its wheeler to keep wagons and carriages running.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheelwright - a person who makes or repairs wooden wheels

Etymology

From 'wheel' + agent suffix '-er'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwiːlə / (BrE)
A draft horse harnessed behind others and nearest the wheels of a vehicle, especially in a team pulling a carriage or wagon.
horse nearest the wheels in a draft team

Examples

The wheeler must be strong enough to help control the heavy carriage on turns.
They harnessed the wheeler and lead horses before setting out on the dirt road.
Each wheeler had a special harness designed to distribute the heavy load.
