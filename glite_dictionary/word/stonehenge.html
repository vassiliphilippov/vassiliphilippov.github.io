Stonehenge

1. PROPN

/ ˈstoʊnˌhɛndʒ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌhɛndʒ / (BrE)
an ancient stone circle monument in southern England, believed to have been used for ritual or ceremonial purposes
prehistoric stone monument in England

Examples

Many tourists visit Stonehenge every year to marvel at its mysterious stone structures.
Archaeologists continue to uncover new theories about the original purpose of Stonehenge.
On the summer solstice, crowds gather at Stonehenge to watch the sunrise over the ancient stones.

Etymology

Name of the site, first recorded in Old English as 'Stanenges' or 'Stanhenge', meaning ‘stone hanging’ or ‘supported stone’.
