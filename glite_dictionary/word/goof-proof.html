to goof-proof

1. VERB

/ ɡuːf pruːf / (AmE), / ɡuːf pruːf / (BrE)
to make something resistant to human error or misuse; to design so that mistakes are unlikely.
make resistant to human error
informal american

Examples

They tried to goof-proof the new voting machines to avoid any mistakes.
The software engineer spent weeks goof-proofing the interface so users couldn't delete files by accident.
Make sure you've goof-proofed your instructions before handing them to new employees.

Synset Examples

foolproof this appliance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolproof - designed to be safe against errors and misuse; incapable of going wrong

Etymology

Blend of 'goof' (slang for mistake) and 'proof' as in 'foolproof'.
