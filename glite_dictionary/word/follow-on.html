follow-on

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːloʊ ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ɒn / (BrE)
(in cricket) an immediate second innings forced on a team that scores a certain number of runs fewer than its opponents in the first innings.
immediate second innings in cricket

Examples

The team was forced to follow-on after a disappointing first innings total.
Enforcing the follow-on can be a risky strategy in a test match.
The threat of a follow-on loomed as the batting side struggled to add runs.

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'follow on', used in cricketing context since the 19th century.
