opisthognathous

1. ADJ

/ əˌpɪsθəɡˈneɪθəs / (AmE), / əˌpɪsθɒɡˈneɪθəs / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially an insect) having jaws that are directed backward or receding.
having backward-pointing jaws
medical

Examples

The beetle was classified as opisthognathous due to its receding mandibles.
Many true bugs have opisthognathous mouthparts that enable them to feed effectively.
An opisthognathous jaw orientation is uncommon among predatory mammals.

Etymology

From Greek 'opistho-' (behind) + 'gnathos' (jaw).
