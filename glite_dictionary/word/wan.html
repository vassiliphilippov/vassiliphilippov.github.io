wan

1. ADJ

/ wɑn / (AmE), / wɒn / (BrE)
(of a person's appearance or expression) pale and giving the impression of illness, exhaustion, or unhappiness.
pale and weak in appearance

Examples

She forced a wan smile, trying to hide her disappointment.
After a week of illness, he looked wan and tired.
The child's wan face worried his mother.

Synset Examples

a wan smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - having a light or whitish color; lacking color or brightness

Etymology

From Old English 'wann' (dark, gloomy, pale).

2. ADJ

/ wɑn / (AmE), / wɒn / (BrE)
(of light or color) pale and weak; lacking intensity or brightness.
pale and faint (of light or color)

Examples

The wan glow of dawn barely illuminated the empty street.
A wan moon hung silently above the misty fields.
Through the dusty curtains, only a wan beam of sunlight entered the room.

Synset Examples

a pale sun

a pallid sky
the pale (or wan) stars
the wan light of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - lacking strong color or brightness
  • Antonym: bright - having a lot of light or strong color

Etymology

From Old English 'wann' (dark, lacking color, livid).

3. ADJ

/ wɑn / (AmE), / wɒn / (BrE)
(of a person's face or complexion) pale and giving the impression of illness or exhaustion.
pale in appearance, suggesting illness

Examples

After the long illness, her wan face showed how weak she still was.
The child gave a wan smile, barely lifting the corners of his mouth.
He appeared wan and tired after working through the night.

Synset Examples

the pallid face of the invalid
her wan face suddenly flushed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallid - having an abnormally pale or wan complexion
  • Antonym: florid - having a red or flushed complexion

Etymology

From Old English 'wann' (dark, lacking color).

4. VERB

/ wæn / (AmE), / wæn / (BrE)
(of a person's face or complexion) to become pale and sickly, as from illness, fear, or exhaustion.
become pale and sickly
archaic

Examples

After hearing the tragic news, her face began to wan visibly.
He watched his opponent wan as the illness took its toll.
In the dim candlelight, the young boy seemed to wan with each passing hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - (of a person's face) light in color; lacking healthy color

Etymology

From Old English 'wann' (dark, pale, wan), originally an adjective, later used as a verb.
