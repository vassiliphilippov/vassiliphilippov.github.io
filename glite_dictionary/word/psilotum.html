Psilotum

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈloʊtəm / (AmE), / saɪˈləʊtəm / (BrE)
A genus of primitive, fern-like plants in the family Psilotaceae, often called whisk ferns, found in tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of primitive fern-like plants

Examples

Psilotum is often called a whisk fern due to its simple, leafless appearance.
Some botanists study Psilotum to understand the evolution of vascular plants.
The genus Psilotum is notable for having no true roots or leaves.

Etymology

From Greek psilós (bare, naked), referring to the plant's lack of true leaves or roots.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈloʊtəm / (AmE), / saɪˈləʊtəm / (BrE)
A genus of primitive vascular plants in the family Psilotaceae, commonly known as whisk ferns.
genus of primitive vascular plants (whisk ferns)

Examples

Some of the oldest known vascular plants belong to the genus Psilotum.
The whisk fern, classified under Psilotum, has very simple, leafless stems.
Botanists study Psilotum to understand the evolution of early land plants.

Etymology

From Greek psilos (bare, naked), referencing the lack of true leaves.
