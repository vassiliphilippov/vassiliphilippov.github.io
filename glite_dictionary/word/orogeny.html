orogeny

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / ɒˈrɛdʒəni / (BrE)
the process of mountain formation, especially by the upward displacement of the Earth's crust
mountain-building process in geology

Examples

The Himalayas were formed by a complex orogeny that lasted millions of years.
Scientists study orogeny to understand how Earth's surface changes over time.
Plate collisions play a crucial role in the process of orogeny.

Etymology

From Greek oros (mountain) + -geny (producing).
