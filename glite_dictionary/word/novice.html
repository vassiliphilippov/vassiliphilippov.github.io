novice

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑvɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɒvɪs / (BrE)
a person who is new to and inexperienced in a particular activity or field
a beginner; new learner

Examples

As a novice in photography, she still has much to learn about using her camera.
It's normal for a novice to make mistakes in the early stages of training.
The club welcomes both novice and experienced climbers to its weekly sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginner - a person just starting to learn a skill or activity
  • Antonym: expert - a person with in-depth knowledge or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From Latin 'novicius' (new, newly entered into a condition or office).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑvɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɒvɪs / (BrE)
a person who has entered a religious order and is preparing to take final vows, but has not yet done so
person new in a religious order

Examples

The novice attended daily prayers with the rest of the community.
As a novice, she learned the traditions and rules of the convent.
Before taking her vows, she spent a year living as a novice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probationer - a person undergoing trial or probation, especially in a religious context

Etymology

From Latin novicius (new, inexperienced), from novus (new).
