fossilist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːsɪlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɒsɪlɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or specializes in fossils, especially as a branch of paleontology
person specializing in fossils

Examples

The fossilist devoted her career to uncovering prehistoric marine life.
As an amateur fossilist, he spent weekends searching for trilobites in the cliffs.
The museum invited a renowned fossilist to give a lecture on dinosaur evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontologist - a scientist who studies fossils to learn about ancient life

Etymology

From 'fossil' + '-ist', denoting a person engaged in a specified activity.
