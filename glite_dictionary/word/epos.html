epos

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌpɑs / (AmE), / ˈiːpɒs / (BrE)
A body of epic poetry, usually representing the traditions and heroic legends of a society.
body of epic poetry

Examples

The Iliad and the Odyssey are classic examples of Greek epos that have shaped Western literature.
Scholars have spent decades analyzing the epos of different cultures to trace their common themes.
The epos celebrates not just the heroes, but also the values and beliefs of the society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - A long narrative poem about heroic deeds

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'epos' (word, song, epic poem).
