Anogramma leptophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡræmə lɛpˈtɒfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈɡræmə lɛpˈtɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A small, short-lived fern species native to Central and South America, known for its delicate, finely divided leaves.
species of small fern

Examples

Anogramma leptophylla is often found growing on moist rock faces in tropical forests.
Botanists observed the rapid life cycle of Anogramma leptophylla in South American habitats.
The delicate fronds of Anogramma leptophylla make it a popular subject for plant illustrations.

Etymology

From Greek 'ano-' (upward) + 'gramma' (line, letter) and 'leptos' (slender, thin) + 'phyllon' (leaf).
