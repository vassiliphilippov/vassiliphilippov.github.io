intestacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛstəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstəsi / (BrE)
the state of dying without having made a legally valid will, so that the estate is distributed according to law
state of dying without a will
legal

Examples

The estate was divided according to intestacy laws because he died without leaving a will.
If you do not write a will, your assets may be distributed by intestacy.
Legal advice is recommended to avoid the complications of intestacy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: testacy - the state of having left a valid will at death

Etymology

From Latin intestatus (without a will) + -cy (forming nouns denoting a state or condition).
