reelection

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of being elected to an office or position again, after already having served a previous term
being elected again to a position

Examples

He announced his intention to seek reelection as mayor next year.
After a successful first term, she easily won reelection.
The governor's policies became more popular in the months leading up to reelection.

Synset Examples

he did not run for reelection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: election - the act of choosing someone by vote for a position or office

Etymology

From re- (again) + election, from Latin 'eligere' (to choose).
