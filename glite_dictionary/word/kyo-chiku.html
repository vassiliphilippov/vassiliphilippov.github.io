kyo-chiku

1. NOUN

/ kjoʊˈtʃiːkuː / (AmE), / kjəʊˈtʃiːkuː / (BrE)
A large tropical Southeast Asian bamboo with tough, hollow stems that resemble tree trunks.
giant Southeast Asian bamboo

Examples

The artisan constructed furniture from sturdy kyo-chiku harvested in the region.
Botanical gardens often display kyo-chiku because of its impressive height and thick culms.
Traditional houses in Southeast Asia sometimes use kyo-chiku as structural supports.

Etymology

From Japanese *kyōchiku* (giant bamboo).
