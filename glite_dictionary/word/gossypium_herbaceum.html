Gossypium herbaceum

1. NOUN

/ ˌgɑːˈsɪpiəm hərˈbeɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˌɡɒˈsɪpiəm hɜːˈbeɪsiəm / (BrE)
A species of cotton plant native to the Old World, with heart-shaped leaves and large seeds, considered an ancestor of modern short-staple cottons.
species of cotton plant, Old World origin

Examples

Gossypium herbaceum has been cultivated for thousands of years in Asia and Africa.
Unlike American cotton species, Gossypium herbaceum produces shorter staple fibers.
Botanists study Gossypium herbaceum to understand the domestication of modern cotton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft, white fibrous substance from cotton plants

Etymology

From Latin: 'gossypium' (cotton) + 'herbaceum' (herbaceous).
