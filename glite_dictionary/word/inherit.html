to inherit

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (BrE)
to receive a quality, characteristic, or trait genetically from one's parents or ancestors
receive genetically from parents

Examples

Many children inherit their eye color from their parents.
She inherited her father's sense of humor and her mother's artistic talent.
Genetically transmitted diseases can be inherited from either parent.

Synset Examples

I inherited my good eyesight from my mother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acquire - to come into possession of something, especially non-genetically

Etymology

From Latin 'inheritare', from 'hereditare' (to inherit), from 'heres' (heir).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320739-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (BrE)
to receive money, property, or a title from someone who has died, usually a relative
receive from someone after death

Examples

After his aunt passed away, he inherited her old farmhouse and land.
She hopes to inherit her grandmother's jewelry collection some day.
Many children inherit fortunes from their parents when they die.

Synset Examples

I inherited a castle from my French grandparents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to bequeath - leave (property) to a person by a will
  • Synonym: to receive - be given something

Etymology

From Latin heres (heir) via Old French enheriter (to make heir).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320505-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɛrət / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (BrE)
to receive (something, such as property, title, problem, or characteristic) from a predecessor or previous generation
receive from a predecessor

Examples

The new CEO inherited a team of talented but unmotivated employees from her predecessor.
We inherited many traditions and values from our grandparents.
After the merger, the company inherited several unresolved legal disputes.

Synset Examples

The new chairman inherited many problems from the previous chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acquire - to gain possession of something
  • Antonym: to bequeath - to leave or give by will after one's death

Etymology

From Latin 'inhereditare' (to appoint as heir), from 'in-' (in) + 'heres' (heir).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320918-v
Synset POS: v
