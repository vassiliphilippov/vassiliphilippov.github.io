cormose

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrˌmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːməʊs / (BrE)
(of a plant) having or producing corms.
having or producing corms (botany)

Examples

The gladiolus is a cormose plant that stores nutrients in its underground stem.
Certain cormose species bloom annually after storing food in their corms over winter.
Botanists identified the plant as cormose due to its thick, underground storage organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corm - a swollen underground plant stem serving as a storage organ

Etymology

From 'corm' (a swollen underground plant stem) + '-ose' (having, full of).
