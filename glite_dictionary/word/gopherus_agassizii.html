Gopherus agassizii

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.fə.rəs æˌɡæs.ɪˈzaɪ.iː / (AmE), / ˈɡɒ.fə.rəs aˌɡæs.ɪˈzaɪ.iː / (BrE)
a species of burrowing tortoise native to the arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico, often called the desert tortoise
burrowing tortoise of the western US and northern Mexico

Examples

The Gopherus agassizii is well adapted to surviving the harsh desert environment.
Many conservation programs have been launched to protect the habitat of Gopherus agassizii.
Researchers are monitoring the population of Gopherus agassizii to ensure its continued survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert tortoise - common name for Gopherus agassizii, a burrowing tortoise of desert regions

Etymology

Named after Swiss-American zoologist Jean Louis Rodolphe Agassiz; genus from Neo-Latin.
