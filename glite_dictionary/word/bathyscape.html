bathyscape

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθɪˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈbæθɪskeɪp / (BrE)
a navigable deep-diving vessel designed for underwater exploration of great depths
deep-diving underwater vessel

Examples

The bathyscape descended into the ocean's deepest trench to collect samples.
Scientists aboard the bathyscape observed rare marine life never seen before.
After several hours underwater, the bathyscape resurfaced with valuable data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - an underwater vessel; general term for underwater craft
  • Synonym: bathysphere - a spherical deep-sea submersible lowered by cable

Etymology

From French 'bathyscaphe', from Greek 'bathys' (deep) + 'skaphos' (ship, vessel).
