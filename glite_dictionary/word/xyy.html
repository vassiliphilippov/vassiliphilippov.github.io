XYY

1. NOUN

/ ɛks waɪ waɪ / (AmE), / ɛks waɪ waɪ / (BrE)
(genetics) A chromosomal condition in a male in which there are two Y chromosomes, resulting in a 47,XYY karyotype instead of the typical 46,XY.
genetic condition—male with two Y chromosomes
medical

Examples

Genetic testing revealed that the patient had an XYY karyotype.
Researchers are studying the physical characteristics of individuals with XYY syndrome.
A small percentage of males are born with the XYY chromosomal pattern.

Etymology

Based on the scientific notation for the presence of one X chromosome and two Y chromosomes.
