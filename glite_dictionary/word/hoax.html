hoax

1. VERB

/ hoʊks / (AmE), / həʊks / (BrE)
to deceive someone as a joke, often for amusement, by making them believe something false
trick someone as a joke

Examples

The students managed to hoax their teacher with a fake test announcement.
An internet group recently hoaxed thousands by spreading a fake celebrity death report.
She enjoys hoaxing her friends every April Fools' Day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Synonym: dupe - to trick or deceive someone
  • Synonym: scam - to cheat or defraud someone

Etymology

Apparently from 'hocus' (to cheat, trick), possibly as a contraction.

2. NOUN

/ hoʊks / (AmE), / həʊks / (BrE)
a trick or intended deception, especially one intended to mislead people into believing something false
deceptive trick or fraud

Examples

The news website later admitted that the viral story was a hoax designed to boost views.
Many people believed the email was real, but it turned out to be an elaborate hoax.
After the student confessed, the school realized the bomb threat had been just a hoax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • Degree Lower: prank - a practical joke or mischievous act
  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme; a fraud

Etymology

Probably from hocus, in the sense of trickery or deception, influenced by hocus-pocus (meaning trick or deception).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00755028-n
Synset POS: n
