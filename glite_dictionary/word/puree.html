puree

1. NOUN

/ pjʊˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊəreɪ / (BrE)
a smooth food made by cooking and then crushing or blending fruit, vegetables, or other ingredients until soft and smooth
smooth, blended food preparation

Examples

She spooned some apple puree into the baby's bowl for breakfast.
The chef prepared a delicious carrot puree to accompany the roasted meat.
You can freeze leftover tomato puree for later use in soups or sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mash - food that has been crushed into a soft mass, especially potatoes

Etymology

From French 'purée', from Old French 'purer' (to purify).

WordNet ID: oewn-07666634-n

2. VERB

/ pjʊˈreɪ / (AmE), / pjʊəˈreɪ / (BrE)
to make food smooth and creamy by crushing or blending it thoroughly, especially by rubbing it through a sieve or using a blender.
blend or crush food to a smooth paste

Examples

She decided to puree the soup to make it smoother for the guests.
After steaming the carrots, he pureed them for his infant daughter.
Some recipes call for you to puree the sauce before serving.

Synset Examples

puree the vegetables for the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix or combine two or more substances so that they become a single smooth substance
  • Synonym: mash - to crush food into a soft, pulpy mass

Etymology

From French *purée* (strained food), from Old French *purer* (to purify).
