basiscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbeɪsɪˈskoʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌbeɪsɪˈskəʊpɪk / (BrE)
(of a structure, especially a leaf or its part) facing or situated on the side toward the base.
facing toward the base (botany)
medical

Examples

The basiscopic side of the fern pinna faces toward the stem.
Certain scales are described as basiscopic when oriented toward the base of the plant.
Botanists note that the basiscopic vein is closest to the main stem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acroscopic - facing toward the apex or tip, especially in plant structures

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base) and Greek 'skopos' (watcher, looking), referring to orientation toward the base.
