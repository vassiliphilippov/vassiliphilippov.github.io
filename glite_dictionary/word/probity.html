probity

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊbəti / (AmE), / ˈprəʊbəti / (BrE)
the quality of having strong moral principles; honesty and integrity
complete moral integrity; honesty

Examples

Her reputation for probity made her the best choice to lead the investigation.
The company values probity above all when selecting new partners.
Despite great temptation, he maintained his probity throughout his career.

Synset Examples

in a world where financial probity may not be widespread
he enjoys an exaggerated reputation for probity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power

Etymology

From Latin 'probitas' (uprightness, goodness), from 'probus' (good, honest).
