exuviae

1. NOUN

/ ɪgˈzuː.vi.i / (AmE), / ɪgˈzjuː.vi.iː / (BrE)
the cast-off skins, shells, or external coverings of animals, especially after molting or ecdysis
cast-off skins or coverings after molting

Examples

The dragonfly's exuviae clung to the reed near the pond's edge.
Researchers collected the exuviae of cicadas to study their life cycles.
After molting, the snake left its exuviae in the grass.

Etymology

From Latin 'exuviae', meaning 'things stripped off' or 'cast-off garments'.
