guano

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡwɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
The excrement of sea birds, bats, or seals, used as a fertilizer due to its high content of nitrogen and phosphates.
excrement of sea birds used as fertilizer

Examples

Nitrate-rich guano was exported from islands in the Pacific for use in agriculture.
The cave floors were covered in thick layers of guano deposited by bats.
Farmers value guano as a natural way to enrich soil nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance used to make soil more fertile

Etymology

From Spanish 'guano,' from Quechua 'wanu' (dung).
