thousandth

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaʊzəndθ / (AmE), / ˈθaʊzəndθ / (BrE)
each of one thousand equal parts into which something can be divided; the ordinal number matching one part in a thousand.
one part in a thousand

Examples

One thousandth of a millimeter can make a difference in precision engineering.
The solution had only a thousandth of a gram of the chemical in it.
She won the race by less than a thousandth of a second.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hundredth - one part in a hundred equal parts
  • Degree Higher: millionth - one part in a million equal parts

Etymology

From 'thousand' + '-th', used to form ordinal numbers and fractions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθaʊzəndθ / (AmE), / ˈθaʊzəndθ / (BrE)
The ordinal number matching the number 1,000 in a sequence; the item in position 1,000.
the 1,000th position in a sequence

Examples

She was the thousandth customer at the store and received a special prize.
The marathon runner finished in thousandth place out of over five thousand participants.
This is the thousandth time I've told him to be careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thousand - the number 1,000
  • Degree Lower: nine hundred ninety-ninth - the 999th item in a sequence
  • Degree Higher: thousand and first - the 1001st item in a sequence

Etymology

Formed from 'thousand' + '-th', an ordinal suffix in English.
