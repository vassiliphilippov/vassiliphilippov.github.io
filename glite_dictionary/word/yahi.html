Yahi

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːhi / (AmE), / ˈjɑːhi / (BrE)
a member of an extinct Native American people who lived in northern California
member of extinct Native American people

Examples

The last known Yahi was Ishi, who emerged in California in 1911.
Historians study the traditions and language of the Yahi to better understand their culture.
Few artifacts remain to tell the story of the Yahi people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yana - a Native American people of northern California closely related to the Yahi

Etymology

Name of the people, likely from their own language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːhi / (AmE), / ˈjɑːhi / (BrE)
the now-extinct Yanan language formerly spoken by the Yahi people of California
language of the Yahi people

Examples

Few records of the Yahi language survived after the last speakers passed away.
Linguists have studied Yahi to understand more about California's indigenous languages.
He presented a paper on the comparative grammar of Yahi and other Yanan languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yana - Native American language group formerly spoken in northern California

Etymology

From the name of the Yahi people, a subgroup of the Yana of northern California.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06936481-n
Synset POS: n
