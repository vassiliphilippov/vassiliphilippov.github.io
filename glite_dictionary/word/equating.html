equating

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of regarding or treating different things as equal.
the act of treating as equal

Examples

The equating of money with happiness can lead to disappointment.
Some critics objected to the equating of patriotism with support for the war.
His argument relies on the equating of correlation with causation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equate - to consider as equal

Etymology

From the verb 'equate' + '-ing', ultimately from Latin 'aequare' (to make equal).
