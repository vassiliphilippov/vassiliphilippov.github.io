directness

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of moving straight toward a goal or target without deviation
quality of being straightforward or on course

Examples

The directness of his approach impressed the interviewers.
A hawk's directness in flight makes it a formidable hunter.
We admired her directness in pursuing her ambitions without hesitation.

Synset Examples

rivaling a hawk in directness of aim

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indirectness - the quality of not being straightforward or deviating from a course
  • Synonym: straightforwardness - the quality of being straightforward, frank, or direct in action or speech

Etymology

From direct + -ness, forming a noun meaning the state or quality of being direct.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being honest, straightforward, and clear in attitude or speech
quality of being straightforward or clear

Examples

Her directness in meetings often led to quick decisions and fewer misunderstandings.
Some people appreciate directness, while others may find it too blunt or harsh.
He was known for his directness with clients, never sugarcoating the facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest
  • Antonym: indirectness - the quality of being not straightforward; evasiveness

Etymology

From 'direct' + '-ness', modeled after similar English formations.
