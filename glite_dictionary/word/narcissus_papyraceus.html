Narcissus papyraceus

1. NOUN

/ nɑrˈsɪsəs ˌpæ.piˈreɪ.si.əs / (AmE), / nɑːˈsɪs.əs ˌpæ.pɪˈreɪ.si.əs / (BrE)
A species of daffodil with star-shaped white blossoms, often grown indoors for winter flowering; commonly known as paperwhite.
species of daffodil with white star-shaped flowers

Examples

Many people force Narcissus papyraceus bulbs indoors during the holidays for their fragrant blooms.
The delicate, star-shaped flowers of Narcissus papyraceus brighten up any winter garden.
Because Narcissus papyraceus thrives indoors, it is a popular choice for winter decoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paperwhite - common name for Narcissus papyraceus, a type of daffodil
  • Degree Lower: daffodil - a bulbous plant of the genus Narcissus, with yellow or white flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Narcissus' (the daffodil genus) and 'papyraceus' (paper-like), referring to the thin texture of the petals.
