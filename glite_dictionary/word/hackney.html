hackney

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæk.ni / (AmE), / ˈhak.ni / (BrE)
a compact breed of harness horse, originally developed in England, known for its high-stepping gait and elegance in carriage driving.
a breed of carriage horse

Examples

The hackney is prized for its elegant, high-stepping gait in carriage competitions.
They imported a hackney from England to improve their horse breeding program.
Her collection of horse figurines included a beautifully detailed hackney on display.

Etymology

Named after Hackney, an area in London, England, where the breed was developed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhækni / (AmE), / ˈhakni / (BrE)
a horse-drawn carriage for hire, especially one used as a taxi in former times
horse-drawn carriage for hire
archaic

Examples

In Victorian London, a hackney could be hailed for a ride across the city.
She arrived at the mansion in a splendid hackney shortly before midnight.
The old painting depicts a bustling street filled with hackney carriages and townsfolk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cab - a vehicle for public hire, usually referring to a taxi
  • Synonym: taxi - a motor vehicle licensed to transport passengers for hire

Etymology

From Hackney, a district in London once famed for its horses and hire carriages.
