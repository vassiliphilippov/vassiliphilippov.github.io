Beatles

1. PROPN

/ ˈbiːtəlz / (AmE), / ˈbiːtlz / (BrE)
an English rock group from Liverpool, active from 1960 to 1970, renowned for their influential music and cultural impact
famous English rock band, 1960s

Examples

The Beatles revolutionized popular music during the 1960s.
Many critics consider the Beatles to be the most influential band of all time.
My father owns every Beatles album ever released.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Rolling Stones - another major English rock band from the same era
  • Synonym: Elvis Presley - iconic American rock and roll singer of the same era

Etymology

The group's name is a pun on 'beetles' and 'beat' (music rhythm).
