spoilable

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɔɪ.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪ.lə.bəl / (BrE)
liable to decay, spoil, or become unfit for use or consumption
liable to spoil or decay

Examples

Most fresh vegetables are highly spoilable and need refrigeration.
Because the product is spoilable, it's important to check the expiration date before purchase.
They used non-spoilable materials to avoid any risk of decay during shipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perishable - likely to decay or go bad quickly
  • Antonym: nonperishable - not subject to rapid decay or spoilage

Etymology

From 'spoil' + '-able', meaning capable of being spoiled.
