Arthur Wellesley

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrθər ˈwɛlzli / (AmE), / ˈɑːθə ˈwɛlzli / (BrE)
(1769–1852) British general and statesman, famed for defeating Napoleon at Waterloo and later serving as Prime Minister.
British general and Prime Minister (1769–1852)

Examples

Arthur Wellesley is best known for his victory at the Battle of Waterloo.
In 1828, Arthur Wellesley became Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.
The military tactics of Arthur Wellesley are still studied today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Duke of Wellington - title held by Arthur Wellesley, British military leader and statesman
  • Antonym: Napoleon Bonaparte - French general defeated by Arthur Wellesley at Waterloo
