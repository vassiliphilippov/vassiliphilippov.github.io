confounded

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfaʊndɪd / (AmE), / kənˈfaʊndɪd / (BrE)
confused and perplexed; filled with bewilderment by complex or conflicting situations
perplexed; bewildered

Examples

He looked confounded by the complexity of the instructions.
The students were confounded by the unexpected results of the experiment.
I was completely confounded after reading the contradictory reports.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewildered - confused and puzzled
  • Synonym: perplexed - completely baffled; very puzzled
  • Antonym: clearheaded - able to think clearly and sensibly

Etymology

Past participle of 'confound', from Old French *confondre*, from Latin *confundere* (to mix together, confuse).
