bagman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡ.mæn / (AmE), / ˈbæɡ.mən / (BrE)
a man employed by a criminal organization to collect or distribute illegal payoff money
collector/distributor of criminal payoff money

Examples

The police arrested the bagman as he was delivering money to the mob boss.
He worked as a bagman for several years before getting caught.
Authorities suspected that the bagman was laundering money for the entire operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courier - a person who carries messages or packages, especially on behalf of a criminal organization

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈbæɡˌman / (BrE)
a traveling salesman who calls on customers, especially carrying samples of goods for sale
traveling salesman

Examples

The bagman arrived at the store with his latest collection of fabric samples.
In the early twentieth century, a bagman would travel from town to town selling goods directly to shopkeepers.
Before the rise of the telephone, the role of the bagman was essential for connecting manufacturers with retailers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salesman - a man whose job is to sell products or services
  • Synonym: traveling salesman - a salesperson who travels to meet customers

Etymology

From 'bag' + 'man', referring to a man carrying a bag of goods.
