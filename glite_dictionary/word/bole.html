bole

1. NOUN

/ boʊl / (AmE), / bəʊl / (BrE)
the main stem or trunk of a tree, especially the part used for timber
main stem or trunk of a tree

Examples

The thick bole of the oak tree provided shade during the hot afternoon.
Lumberjacks measured the bole to estimate how much timber could be harvested.
Moss grew along the damp bole after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trunk - main woody stem of a tree

Etymology

From Middle French 'bole', from Latin 'bōlus' (clod, lump).

2. NOUN

/ boʊl / (AmE), / bəʊl / (BrE)
a soft, oily clay, especially reddish brown in color, used as a pigment
reddish brown clay pigment

Examples

The artist mixed bole with water to create a base layer for gilding.
Restorers carefully matched the original bole pigment on the frame's surface.
Traditional icon painters often use a reddish bole beneath gold leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red ochre - a natural earth pigment containing ferric oxide, used as a pigment
  • Degree Higher: sienna - a reddish-brown earth pigment containing iron oxide and manganese oxide

Etymology

From French 'bole', from Latin 'bolus' (clod, lump of earth), from Greek 'bōlos'.
