tarsitis

1. NOUN

/ tɑrˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / tɑːˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the eyelid, especially affecting the tarsal plate
inflammation of the eyelid
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist diagnosed the patient with tarsitis after observing swelling and redness.
Chronic tarsitis can lead to discomfort and sensitivity to light.
Treatment for tarsitis may include antibiotics or anti-inflammatory medications, depending on the cause.

Etymology

From Greek 'tarsos' (flat surface, edge of the eyelid) + '-itis' (inflammation).
