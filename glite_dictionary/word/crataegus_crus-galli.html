Crataegus crus-galli

1. NOUN

/ ˌkreɪtəˈiɡəs ˌkrʊs ˈɡælaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkreɪtəˈiːɡəs ˌkrʌs ˈɡæli / (BrE)
A species of hawthorn native to eastern North America, characterized by long, straight thorns; also known as cockspur hawthorn.
a North American hawthorn with long thorns

Examples

The Crataegus crus-galli is often planted as an ornamental tree because of its attractive blossoms.
Gardeners must be cautious of the Crataegus crus-galli's long, sharp thorns when pruning.
Crataegus crus-galli produces bright red fruit that birds enjoy in the autumn.

Etymology

From Latin; 'crus-galli' means 'cock's spur', referring to the shape of the thorns.
