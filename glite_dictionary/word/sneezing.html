sneezing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsniːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsniːzɪŋ / (BrE)
An involuntary expulsion of air from the nose and mouth, often as a symptom of irritation in the nasal passages.
involuntary expulsion of air from the nose

Examples

Allergy season always brings on a bout of sneezing for many people.
Loud sneezing echoed through the quiet library, startling everyone nearby.
Frequent sneezing can be a sign of a cold or another respiratory illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coughing - the act of forcefully expelling air from the lungs

Etymology

From the verb 'sneeze', which is of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'sniesen'.
