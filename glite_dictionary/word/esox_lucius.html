Esox lucius

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.sɑks ˈluː.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈiː.sɒks ˈluː.si.əs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the northern pike, a voracious predatory fish found in the freshwaters of the Northern Hemisphere
scientific name for northern pike (fish)

Examples

The Esox lucius is renowned for its speed and aggressive hunting behavior in northern lakes.
Anglers often target Esox lucius because of its size and fighting spirit.
Studying the diet of Esox lucius helps biologists understand freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - a large predatory freshwater fish with a long body and pointed snout

Etymology

From Latin: 'Esox' (a name used by ancient Romans for a fish, probably salmon or pike) and 'lucius' (pike).
