sib

1. NOUN

/ sɪb / (AmE), / sɪb / (BrE)
a brother or sister; a sibling.
brother or sister; sibling

Examples

He is the youngest sib in his family.
Each sib was given an equal share of the inheritance.
Researchers interviewed every sib in the extended family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sibling - a brother or sister

Etymology

From Old English *sib* (kinship, relation), related to German *Sippe* (clan, kin).

2. NOUN

/ sɪb / (AmE), / sɪb / (BrE)
a person related by blood; especially someone sharing an ancestor with another
blood relative, especially with shared ancestor
archaic

Examples

Though distant, she discovered they were still sib through a common ancestor.
The clan gathered every year so that each sib would remember their shared roots.
In old legal texts, the term sib is used to indicate blood relations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kin - one's family or relatives; kinfolk
  • Synonym: relative - a person connected by blood or marriage
  • Antonym: stranger - a person with no familial or known connection

Etymology

Old English *sib(b)* (kinship, relative), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *sibjō* (family, peace).
