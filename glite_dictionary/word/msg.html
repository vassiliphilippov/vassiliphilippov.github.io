MSG

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˌɛs ˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˌɛs ˈdʒi / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (monosodium glutamate) used as a food additive to enhance the flavor of foods
monosodium glutamate food additive

Examples

Some people avoid products that contain MSG due to sensitivity to the additive.
The restaurant proudly states that they do not use MSG in any of their dishes.
Instant noodles often include MSG to enhance their savory flavor.

Synset Examples

food manufacturers sometimes list MSG simply as ‘artificial flavors’ in ingredient lists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monosodium glutamate - white crystalline compound used as a flavor enhancer

Etymology

Abbreviation of monosodium glutamate.
