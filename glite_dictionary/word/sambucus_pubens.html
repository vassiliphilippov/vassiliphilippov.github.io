Sambucus pubens

1. NOUN

/ sæmˈbjuːkəs ˈpjuːbɛnz / (AmE), / samˈbjuːkəs ˈpjuːbɛnz / (BrE)
A species of elderberry native to North America, commonly known as red-berried elder, which grows as a shrub or small tree and bears clusters of bright red berries.
species of North American elderberry shrub or tree

Examples

The bright red berries of the Sambucus pubens are a distinctive feature in northern forests.
Native birds often feed on the fruit of the Sambucus pubens during the late summer months.
Gardeners should note that all parts of Sambucus pubens can be toxic if ingested raw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-berried elder - common name for Sambucus pubens
  • Degree Higher: elderberry - any of several species in the genus Sambucus

Etymology

From Latin; Sambucus is the genus (elderberry), pubens means 'downy' or 'hairy'.
