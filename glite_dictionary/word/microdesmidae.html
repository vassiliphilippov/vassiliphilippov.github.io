Microdesmidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈdɛs.mɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəʊˈdɛz.mɪ.di / (BrE)
A family of small, slender marine fishes, commonly called wormfishes, found in warm coastal waters.
family of slender marine wormfishes

Examples

The family Microdesmidae includes several species of small, elongated wormfishes found in tropical waters.
Scientists have studied the behavior of Microdesmidae to better understand their burrowing habits.
Several members of the Microdesmidae are popular in home aquariums for their slender shapes and peaceful nature.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'desmos' (bond), with the scientific family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈdɛsmɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəʊˈdɛsmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, elongated marine fishes known as wormfishes, typically found in tropical and subtropical waters.
family of wormfishes (fish)

Examples

The Microdesmidae are commonly known as wormfishes due to their slender bodies.
Marine biologists study the habitats of Microdesmidae in tropical coral reefs.
Members of the Microdesmidae family are distinguished by their elongated shapes and burrowing habits.

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + desmos (bond), referring to the members' slender form.
