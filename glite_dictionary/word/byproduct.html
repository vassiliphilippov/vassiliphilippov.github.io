byproduct

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌprɑːdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
a secondary product that is produced during the manufacture or processing of something else
secondary product from manufacturing

Examples

The plant produces chemicals as its main output and salt as a byproduct.
Pollution is often an unwanted byproduct of industrial growth.
Glycerin is obtained as a byproduct in soap manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: co-product - a product produced jointly with another in a process
  • Antonym: main product - the principal product in a manufacturing process

Etymology

Formed from 'by-' (meaning secondary) + 'product'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌprɑːdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
A secondary result, often unintended or unexpected, that happens in addition to the main outcome.
secondary or unintended result

Examples

Stress can be a byproduct of working too many hours without rest.
Innovation is often a byproduct of creative problem-solving rather than the main goal itself.
The new law had some unexpected byproducts that affected small businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: result - something that happens as an effect or outcome
  • Antonym: main effect - the primary result, as opposed to a secondary or incidental one

Etymology

From 'by-', meaning secondary, + 'product', originally referring to an additional product of a process or manufacture.
