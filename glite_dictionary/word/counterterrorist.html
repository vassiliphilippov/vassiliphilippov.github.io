counterterrorist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərˈtɛrəˌrɪst / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈtɛrərɪst / (BrE)
a person who works to prevent or combat terrorism
person working against terrorism

Examples

The government hired experienced counterterrorists to strengthen airport security.
He spent his career as a counterterrorist protecting the country from extremist threats.
An elite team of counterterrorists responded quickly to the foiled attack.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrorist - a person who uses unlawful violence for political aims

Etymology

From counter- (against) + terrorist.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkaʊntərˈtɛrəˌrɪst / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈtɛrəˌrɪst / (BrE)
intended to prevent or combat terrorism.
preventing terrorism

Examples

The government launched a series of counterterrorist operations after the attack.
Special units are trained in counterterrorist tactics.
International cooperation is crucial for effective counterterrorist strategies.

Synset Examples

the government took counterterror measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiterrorist - opposing or preventing terrorism

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'terrorist.'
