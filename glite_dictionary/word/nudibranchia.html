Nudibranchia

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːdəˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / ˌnjuːdɪˈbræŋkɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of soft-bodied, shell-less marine gastropod mollusks, often brightly colored and resembling slugs.
an order of soft-bodied marine mollusks

Examples

The Nudibranchia are known for their vivid colors and intricate patterns.
Marine biologists often study Nudibranchia because of their remarkable adaptations.
Unlike many mollusks, adult Nudibranchia lack a protective shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudibranch - a common name for any animal in the Nudibranchia order

Etymology

From Latin nudus (naked) + Greek brankhia (gills).

2. NOUN

/ nuː.dɪˈbræŋ.ki.ə / (AmE), / njuː.dɪˈbræŋ.kɪə / (BrE)
an order of soft-bodied, marine gastropod mollusks which, as adults, lack a shell and are often vividly colored; commonly known as sea slugs
order of shell-less marine sea slugs

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Nudibranchia off the coast of Indonesia.
Members of Nudibranchia are admired for their bright colors and striking patterns.
The biology student specialized in studying Nudibranchia for her graduate research project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea slug - a common name for various marine gastropods, including nudibranchs

Etymology

From Latin 'nudus' (naked) + Greek 'brankhia' (gills), referring to their exposed gills.
