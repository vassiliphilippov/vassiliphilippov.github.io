altitudinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæl.tɪˈtuː.dɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˌæl.tɪˈtjuː.dɪ.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or arising from altitude, especially with reference to differences in elevation above sea level
relating to altitude or elevation

Examples

The altitudinal range of the species determines where it can survive in the mountains.
A sharp altitudinal gradient affects both climate and vegetation types in the region.
Researchers measured altitudinal differences to analyze temperature variation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altitude - height above sea level or ground level

Etymology

From Latin 'altitudo' (height, depth) + English '-al' (forming adjectives).
