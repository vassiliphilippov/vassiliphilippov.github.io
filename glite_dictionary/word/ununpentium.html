ununpentium

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.ʌnˈpɛn.ti.əm / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ʌnˈpɛn.ti.əm / (BrE)
a radioactive synthetic chemical element with atomic number 115, formerly known as ununpentium, now officially named moscovium
synthetic element, atomic number 115

Examples

Scientists synthesized ununpentium in a laboratory using particle accelerators.
The team studied the properties of ununpentium before it was officially named moscovium.
Due to its instability, ununpentium exists only for a few seconds before decaying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moscovium - the official name of the chemical element with atomic number 115, formerly ununpentium

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'un' (one) + 'un' (one) + 'pent' (five) + '-ium', following IUPAC systematic element naming convention.
