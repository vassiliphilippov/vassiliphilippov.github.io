spindleshanks

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪndəlˌʃæŋks / (AmE), / ˈspɪndlʃæŋks / (BrE)
a person who is unusually thin and has long, thin legs
thin person with long, thin legs

Examples

Everyone teased the new student, calling him spindleshanks because of his thin legs.
Despite being called spindleshanks as a child, he embraced his unique appearance.
In the novel, the villain was described as a sinister spindleshanks lurking in the shadows.

Etymology

From 'spindle' (a thin rod used in spinning) + 'shanks' (legs).
