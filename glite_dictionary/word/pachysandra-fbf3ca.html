pachysandra

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈsændrə / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈsandrə / (BrE)
A low-growing evergreen plant of the genus Pachysandra, often used as ground cover in gardens.
evergreen ground cover plant

Examples

Pachysandra is a popular choice for shady areas where grass won’t grow.
Gardeners often plant pachysandra to create a dense, green carpet under trees.
The landscaper recommended pachysandra because of its hardiness and low maintenance.

Etymology

From modern Latin Pachysandra, from Greek 'pakhus' (thick) and 'andros' (man, male), referring to the thick stamens.
