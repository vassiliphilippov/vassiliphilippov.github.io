totara

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊtərə / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtərə / (BrE)
A tall, valuable timber tree of New Zealand (Podocarpus totara), with hard reddish wood used for furniture, bridges, and wharves.
New Zealand timber tree

Examples

The Maori people traditionally used the wood of the totara to build canoes.
Many old bridges in New Zealand were constructed from sturdy totara logs.
A craftsman selected a piece of totara for making a beautiful table.

Etymology

From Māori *tōtara*, the indigenous name for the species.
