arteria musculophrenica

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə ˌmʌs.kjə.loʊˈfrɛn.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləʊˈfrɛn.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the lower intercostal muscles and parts of the diaphragm and abdominal wall
artery supplying intercostal muscles and diaphragm
medical

Examples

The arteria musculophrenica branches from the internal thoracic artery.
During surgery, the arteria musculophrenica was found supplying the lower intercostal spaces.
An injury to the arteria musculophrenica can reduce blood flow to the diaphragm and abdominal wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal thoracic artery - major artery that gives rise to the musculophrenic artery

Etymology

From Latin: arteria (artery) + musculus (muscle) + phren (diaphragm).
