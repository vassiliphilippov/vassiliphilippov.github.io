Anthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˈænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of small, slender songbirds known as pipits, found in open habitats worldwide
genus of birds; pipits

Examples

The birdwatcher was excited to spot a rare species of Anthus in the grasslands.
Several species within the Anthus genus are difficult to distinguish in the field.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Anthus birds across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipit - a small songbird of the genus Anthus

Etymology

From Latin 'anthus', a kind of small bird, from Ancient Greek 'ánthos'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˈænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender birds known as pipits, found worldwide, especially in open country.
genus of small pipit birds

Examples

The Anthus genus includes several species commonly referred to as pipits.
Ornithologists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Anthus genus in Asia.
Members of Anthus are known for their ground-dwelling habits and subtle plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipit - a small songbird of open country

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek anthos (flower); coined in scientific taxonomy.
