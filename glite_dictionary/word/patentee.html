patentee

1. NOUN

/ ˌpætənˈti / (AmE), / ˌpat(ə)nˈtiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom a patent has been officially granted, especially the original inventor
person holding a granted patent

Examples

The patentee has the exclusive right to manufacture and sell the invention.
Only the patentee can sue others for unauthorized use of the patented technology.
After receiving the patent, she became the official patentee of the new medical device.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent holder - person or entity that legally possesses a patent
  • Synonym: assignee - person or entity to whom a patent is legally transferred

Etymology

From French 'patenté', from 'patenter' (to grant a patent), from Middle French 'patente' (patent).
