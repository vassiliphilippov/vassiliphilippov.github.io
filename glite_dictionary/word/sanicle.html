sanicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsanɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Sanicula, with palmately lobed leaves and clustered small flowers, reputed in folklore to have healing properties
a herbaceous plant of the genus Sanicula

Examples

The old herbalist collected sanicle in the woods for its reputed medicinal effects.
Clusters of sanicle blooms brightened the edges of the forest trail in spring.
Some traditional remedies use extracts from sanicle leaves to promote healing.

Etymology

From Latin sanicula, probably from sanus (healthy), due to its traditional medicinal uses.
