Phyllostomidae

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌlɒstəˈmɪdiː / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒstəˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of New World leaf-nosed bats, native to Central and South America, distinguished by their fleshy nose-leaf.
a family of New World leaf-nosed bats

Examples

The Phyllostomidae are notable for their remarkable diversity in feeding habits.
Researchers observed several species from the Phyllostomidae family in the Amazon rainforest.
Vampire bats, a member of the Phyllostomidae, feed on the blood of other animals.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'stoma' (mouth), referencing their nose-leaf.

2. NOUN

/ fɪləˈstɑːmɪˌdi / (AmE), / fɪlɪˈstɒmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of New World leaf-nosed bats found in Central and South America, including various species with distinctive nose structures.
family of New World leaf-nosed bats

Examples

The Phyllostomidae family is known for its diversity among New World bats.
Researchers studied the unique echolocation abilities of Phyllostomidae species in the Amazon.
Many members of Phyllostomidae have a leaf-shaped nose structure that aids in navigation.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'stoma' (mouth) + '-idae' (family suffix).
