tilefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A yellow-spotted, violet food fish found in warm, deep waters, valued as seafood.
a violet food fish of deep warm waters

Examples

The restaurant served grilled tilefish with a lemon butter sauce.
Fishermen often catch tilefish in deep waters off the coast.
The violet coloring and yellow spots make the tilefish easy to identify.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A marine fish of the family Malacanthidae, found chiefly in the Atlantic and valued as food.
marine fish valued for food

Examples

The restaurant’s special featured grilled tilefish caught fresh that morning.
Commercial fishing regulations often protect tilefish populations to prevent overfishing.
Divers observed a large tilefish burrowing into the sandy ocean floor.
