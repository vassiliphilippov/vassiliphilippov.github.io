to disagree

1. VERB

/ dɪsəˈɡri / (AmE), / dɪsəˈɡriː / (BrE)
to have or express a different opinion; to not agree
to have a different opinion

Examples

It's natural for people to disagree on complex issues like politics or religion.
Her parents often disagree about how to discipline their children.
If you disagree with this proposal, please explain your reasons.

Synset Examples

I beg to differ!
She disagrees with her husband on many questions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion; to consent

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'agreer' (to agree) via Old French 'desagreer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00806464-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡri / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriː / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to be different from each other; not match or correspond.
not be the same; differ

Examples

The two witnesses' stories disagree on several important details.
These results disagree with those presented in the previous study.
The maps disagree in their depiction of the boundary lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differ - to be unlike or distinct in nature, form, or characteristics

Etymology

From Old French desagreer (to displease); from des- (dis-) + agreer (to agree).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02724418-v
Synset POS: v
