hatted

1. ADJ

/ ˈhætɪd / (AmE), / ˈhætɪd / (BrE)
wearing a hat, especially of a particular kind
wearing a hat

Examples

The room was filled with elegantly hatted guests for the afternoon tea.
A hatted figure approached the entrance, drawing curious glances from passersby.
Most of the parade's participants were brightly hatted children waving flags.

Synset Examples

two old ladies, neatly hatted and gloved
a bearskin-hatted sentry

Etymology

Formed from the noun "hat" + the adjective-forming suffix -ed, indicating possession or wearing.
