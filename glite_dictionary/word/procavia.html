Procavia

1. PROPN

/ proʊˈkeɪ.vi.ə / (AmE), / prəˈkɑː.vi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Procaviidae, which includes the rock hyrax.
genus of hyraxes

Examples

Procavia is the only genus in its family that lives in rocky habitats.
The best-known species in the genus Procavia is the rock hyrax.
Researchers observed that Procavia colonies communicate through a variety of vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small, thickset mammal of Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From New Latin, introduced as a scientific genus name.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈkeɪviə / (AmE), / prəˈkeɪvɪə / (BrE)
a genus of mammals in the family Procaviidae, which includes the rock hyrax and its relatives.
genus of mammals (hyraxes) in Procaviidae

Examples

The rock hyrax is the best-known member of the genus Procavia.
Scientists recently discovered a new fossil species within the Procavia genus.
The Procavia genera are native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Procaviidae - the family of mammals containing the genus Procavia and other hyraxes

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'pro-' (before) and Latin 'cavia' (cavy/guinea pig), classically used in scientific taxonomy.
