snack

1. NOUN

/ snæk / (AmE), / snæk / (BrE)
A small amount of food eaten between regular meals, typically informal and lighter than a full meal.
light informal meal between meals

Examples

After school, I usually have a snack like fruit or yogurt.
We stopped for a quick snack during our hike.
Try not to eat too many snacks right before dinner.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: meal - an occasion of eating food, especially one taken at a specific time

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Dutch snacken (to snatch, bite).

2. VERB

/ snæk / (AmE), / snæk / (BrE)
to eat a small amount of food between regular meals
eat a small amount between meals

Examples

I often snack on fruit in the afternoons when I get hungry.
She tries not to snack after dinner to maintain her diet.
He was snacking on chips while watching the movie.

Synset Examples

She never loses weight because she snacks between meals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nibble - to take small bites; to eat in small amounts
  • Antonym: feast - to eat and drink sumptuously

Etymology

From Dutch *snacken* (to bite, eat), related to 'snack' (noun).
