Cypripedium reginae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪprəˈpiːdiəm rɪˈdʒaɪni / (AmE), / ˌsɪprɪˈpiːdiəm rɪˈdʒiːniː / (BrE)
A species of wild orchid native to northeastern North America, known for its pale pink, pouch-like flowers.
a North American wild orchid species

Examples

The Cypripedium reginae blooms each spring in shaded wetlands across the northeastern United States.
Gardeners often admire the striking pouch-like flower of Cypripedium reginae.
Conservationists monitor local populations of Cypripedium reginae because it is threatened by habitat loss.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Cypripedium' (genus of orchids) + 'reginae' (Latin for 'of the queen').
