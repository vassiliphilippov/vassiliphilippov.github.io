caudated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔː.deɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having a tail or taillike appendage
having a tail or taillike appendage

Examples

Many fish are caudated, allowing them to move swiftly through water.
The lizard's caudated appearance distinguishes it from tailless amphibians.
Researchers noted that the embryo exhibited a distinctly caudated shape at this development stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acaudate - lacking a tail or taillike appendage

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' meaning 'tail', with the suffix '-ated'.
