Calymmatobacterium

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪmˌmətəˈbæktɪriəm / (AmE), / kəˌlɪmˌmətəˈbæktɪərɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of rod-shaped bacteria that includes the species responsible for granuloma inguinale, a sexually transmitted disease
genus of bacteria causing granuloma inguinale
medical

Examples

Granuloma inguinale is caused by bacteria of the genus Calymmatobacterium.
The identification of Calymmatobacterium is crucial for diagnosing certain sexually transmitted infections.
Researchers studied the transmission patterns of Calymmatobacterium in affected communities.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalymma' (covering, veil) and Neo-Latin 'bacterium' (bacterium).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪmmətəboʊˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / kəˌlɪm.mə.tə.bəˈtɪə.ri.əm / (BrE)
a genus of rod-shaped bacteria, formerly used to classify the species that causes granuloma inguinale; now usually classified under Klebsiella granulomatis.
bacterial genus causing granuloma inguinale
medical

Examples

Calymmatobacterium was once considered the causative agent of granuloma inguinale.
The reclassification of Calymmatobacterium has clarified its relationship to other bacteria.
Medical textbooks now refer to Calymmatobacterium as a synonym of Klebsiella granulomatis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Klebsiella - genus of bacteria now including former Calymmatobacterium species

Etymology

From Greek 'kálumma' (covering) and 'baktērion' (small rod).
