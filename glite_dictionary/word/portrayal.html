portrayal

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtreɪ.əl / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ.əl / (BrE)
a representation or likeness of a person in any medium, such as painting, sculpture, or photograph
representation or likeness of a person

Examples

The artist's portrayal of the queen captured her regal presence perfectly.
Her photographic portrayal of the homeless brought attention to their struggles.
His portrayal in the sculpture was both realistic and expressive.

Synset Examples

the photographer made excellent portraits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portrait - a painting, photograph, or drawing of a person; close synonym of portrayal, especially for visual art

Etymology

From verb 'portray', from Old French 'portraire' (to portray), from Latin 'protrahere' (to draw forth).

2. NOUN

/ pɔːrˈtreɪ.əl / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act of representing a character in a play, film, or other performance through speech, action, and gesture
representation of a character by an actor

Examples

Her compelling portrayal of the queen earned her critical acclaim.
The movie was praised for its realistic portrayal of wartime struggles.
His portrayal of Hamlet captured the complexity of the character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of performing or expressing a character in a particular way
  • Synonym: impersonation - the act of pretending to be another person for entertainment or fraud

Etymology

From portray + -al, formed from Old French 'portraire' (to depict) and the suffix '-al'.

3. NOUN

/ pɔːrˈtreɪ.əl / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act or result of depicting, describing, or representing a person, character, or situation, especially in a work of art, literature, or performance
depiction or representation of someone

Examples

Her portrayal of the queen in the play was widely praised by critics.
The film offered a moving portrayal of life during the war.
His portrayal of the character captured both vulnerability and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the action or result of representing something in art or literature
  • Synonym: representation - the action of standing for or depicting someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'portray', from Anglo-French 'portrayer' (to depict).

4. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtreɪ.əl / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act or result of representing someone or something, especially in a drawing, painting, or other artistic medium
representation in art or depiction

Examples

The artist's portrayal of the landscape captured the serene beauty of the countryside.
Critics praised the portrayal of emotion in her latest portrait.
His accurate portrayal of the historic event was featured in the museum's new exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the action of depicting something, especially in art
  • Synonym: representation - the action of representing or the state of being represented

Etymology

From 'portray' + '-al', patterning after 'betrayal'.

the act or result of representing someone or something in a picture or portrait
representation in picture or portrait

Examples

Related Terms
