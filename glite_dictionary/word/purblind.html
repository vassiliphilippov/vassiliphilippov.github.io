purblind

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɜrˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈpɜːˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
having greatly reduced vision; almost or partially blind
having greatly reduced vision

Examples

The purblind traveler struggled to read the faded street signs at dusk.
His purblind eyes could barely make out the shapes in the foggy room.
Despite being purblind, she managed to complete the painting with remarkable detail.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blind - unable to see; sightless
  • Antonym: sighted - having normal vision

Etymology

From Middle English, partially blind, from pur- (utterly) + blind.

/ ˈpɝːˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈpɜːˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
Lacking in insight or discernment; slow to understand or recognize things.
lacking insight or discernment

Examples

The company's purblind management failed to see the changes in the market.
Only a purblind observer would dismiss the warning signs around them.
His purblind approach to the problem resulted in missed opportunities.

Synset Examples

Lacking in vision, understanding, or awareness; short-sighted or oblivious to what is obvious.
lacking insight or awareness

Examples
