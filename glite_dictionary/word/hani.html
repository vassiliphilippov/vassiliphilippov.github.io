Hani

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːni / (AmE), / ˈhɑːni / (BrE)
a Loloish language spoken by the Hani people in southern Yunnan Province, China, especially in Jingdong and Jinggu counties and along the Yuanjiang and Lancang rivers.
Loloish language of southern Yunnan, China

Examples

Many Hani children learn both Mandarin and their native Hani language at school.
Linguists are working to document traditional stories told in Hani.
She traveled to Yunnan to study the grammar of Hani.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loloish - a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family including Hani

Etymology

From the name of the Hani people, the language's native speakers.
