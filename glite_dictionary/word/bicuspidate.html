bicuspidate

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈkʌspɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈkʌspɪˌdeɪt / (BrE)
having two cusps or points, especially describing a tooth or a leaf
having two points or cusps

Examples

The botanist described the bicuspidate leaves found on the rare shrub.
Human premolars are sometimes referred to as bicuspidate because of their two pointed cusps.
Some animals possess bicuspidate teeth that help them grasp and tear their food.

Synset Examples

bicuspid teeth
bicuspid leaves

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tricuspidate - having three cusps or points
  • Degree Lower: unicusp - having one cusp or point

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + cuspidatus (pointed, having a cusp).
