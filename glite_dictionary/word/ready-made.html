ready-made

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdiˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdiˌmeɪd / (BrE)
an item, such as a garment or piece of furniture, that is manufactured in advance rather than made to order
pre-manufactured item; not custom-made

Examples

The boutique sells both handmade dresses and ready-mades for customers in a hurry.
She decorated her first apartment with inexpensive ready-mades from a local department store.
Many people prefer to buy ready-mades rather than waiting for custom orders.

Synset Examples

their apartment was furnished with ready-mades

Related Terms

  • Antonym: custom-made - an item made to order according to specific requirements

Etymology

Formed from 'ready' and 'made'; originally from 19th century retail trade.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04066033-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛdiˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌrɛdiˈmeɪd / (BrE)
(especially of goods such as clothes or meals) made in standard sizes or forms and available for immediate use, not made to order.
made in advance; not custom-made

Examples

She bought a ready-made dress for the party instead of having one tailored.
The supermarket offers a wide variety of ready-made meals for busy customers.
Unlike ready-made suits, bespoke suits are crafted to fit individual measurements.

Synset Examples

ready-made clothes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: custom-made - made according to the specifications of an individual buyer

Etymology

Formed from 'ready' + 'made', first recorded in the early 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00678855-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌrɛdiˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌrɛdiˈmeɪd / (BrE)
(of goods) made in advance to be sold and used immediately, rather than made to order.
made in advance for immediate use

Examples

She bought a ready-made dress for the party instead of having one tailored.
These ready-made curtains are a quick and easy way to update your home decor.
The supermarket sells ready-made meals for people who don't have time to cook.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: custom-made - made according to the specifications of an individual buyer
  • Synonym: off-the-shelf - available as a standard product, not custom-made

Etymology

From 'ready' + 'made', first attested in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00675886-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˌrɛdiˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌrɛdiˈmeɪd / (BrE)
(of a phrase, remark, idea, etc.) repeated so often as to lack originality or thought.
(of phrase/idea) unoriginal, often repeated

Examples

Politicians often rely on ready-made phrases when responding to difficult questions.
The review criticized the novel for its use of ready-made characters and predictable storyline.
Instead of offering ready-made solutions, the teacher encouraged creative thinking among her students.

Synset Examples

ready-made phrases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliché - a phrase or opinion that is overused and betrays a lack of original thought
  • Synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) lacking significance through having been overused

Etymology

From 'ready' + 'made', originally used for pre-prepared goods; figurative sense since late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01693895-s
Synset POS: s
