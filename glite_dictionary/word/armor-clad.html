armor-clad

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrmərˌklæd / (AmE), / ˈɑːməˌklæd / (BrE)
Covered with heavy steel or other protective armor, especially for defense.
covered with heavy protective armor

Examples

The old warship sat in the harbor, its armor-clad hull glinting in the sun.
The soldiers advanced behind an armor-clad vehicle for protection.
Medieval knights sometimes rode armor-clad horses into battle.

Synset Examples

armor-plated vehicles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor-plated - covered with a layer of protective metal

Etymology

Formed from 'armor' (protective covering) + 'clad' (clothed, covered).
