wantonly

1. ADV

/ ˈwɑn.tən.li / (AmE), / ˈwɒn.tən.li / (BrE)
in a licentious, sexually unrestrained, or promiscuous manner
in a sexually unrestrained manner

Examples

The character in the novel behaved wantonly, disregarding all social conventions.
She was accused of acting wantonly at the gathering, shocking everyone present.
He regretted living wantonly during his youth, seeking a more settled life now.

Synset Examples

this young girl has to share a room with her mother who lives promiscuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promiscuously - in a casual and unrestrained sexual manner
  • Antonym: chastely - in a morally pure or sexually abstinent manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'wantoun' (undisciplined), ultimately from Old English 'wan-' (wanting) + 'towen' (trained).

2. ADV

/ ˈwɑːntənli / (AmE), / ˈwɒntənli / (BrE)
in a deliberate and unrestrained manner, often with cruelty or without justification
in a cruel or reckless manner

Examples

The soldiers destroyed the village wantonly, leaving nothing behind.
He was accused of acting wantonly when he broke the windows for no reason.
The ancient statues were wantonly defaced during the invasion.

Synset Examples

the animals were killed wantonly for sport

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessly - without regard for consequences
  • Antonym: mercifully - in a compassionate or forgiving manner

Etymology

From wanton + -ly; 'wanton' from Middle English 'wantowen' (undisciplined), from wan- (lacking) + towen (discipline).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
