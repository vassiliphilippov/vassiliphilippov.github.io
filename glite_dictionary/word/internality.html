internality

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɜrnˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːnˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of focusing on or being concerned with one's inner nature, especially ethical or ideological values
preoccupation with one's inner nature or values

Examples

The philosopher's internality was evident in his constant search for moral truth.
Psychological theories often distinguish between internality and externality in explaining motivation.
A focus on internality can lead one to reflect deeply on personal values and beliefs.

Synset Examples

Socrates' inwardness, integrity, and inquisitiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inwardness - concern with one's own inner thoughts or feelings

Etymology

From internal + -ity, modeled after 'externality'.
