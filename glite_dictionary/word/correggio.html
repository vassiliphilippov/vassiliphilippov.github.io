Correggio

1. PROPN

/ kəˈreɪdʒioʊ / (AmE), / kəˈredʒiəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 1489–1534) An Italian painter known for his pioneering use of chiaroscuro and perspective in the High Renaissance.
Italian Renaissance painter (c. 1489–1534)

Examples

The soft, luminous style characteristic of Correggio had a major influence on later Baroque painters.
Her thesis focused on the innovative use of light in Correggio's frescoes.
Many art historians admire Correggio for his masterful depiction of perspective and emotion.

Etymology

Italian, from the artist's birthplace Correggio, in Emilia-Romagna, Italy.
