drawshave

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔˌʃeɪv / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːˌʃeɪv / (BrE)
A tool with a blade and two handles, drawn toward the user to shave wood surfaces smooth, used in woodworking.
woodworking tool for shaving wood

Examples

The carpenter skillfully used a drawshave to smooth the chair leg.
She sharpened her drawshave before trimming the rough timber.
A well-maintained drawshave can make quick work of shaping wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spokeshave - a similar tool for shaping wood with a blade and two handles

Etymology

From 'draw' (to pull) and 'shave' (to pare or smooth), describing how the tool is used.
