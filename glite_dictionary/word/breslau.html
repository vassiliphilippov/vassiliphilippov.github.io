Breslau

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɛs.laʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɛs.laʊ / (BrE)
the former German name for the city of Wrocław, located in southwestern Poland on the Oder River
former name of Wrocław, Poland

Examples

During the nineteenth century, Breslau was a thriving industrial center in Silesia.
Her ancestors emigrated from Breslau before World War II.
The university in Breslau was renowned for its contributions to the sciences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wrocław - modern name of the same city in southwestern Poland

Etymology

From German 'Breslau', formerly the official name of the city now known as Wrocław.
