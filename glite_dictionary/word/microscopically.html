microscopically

1. ADV

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑpɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or resembles the use of a microscope; with extreme precision and attention to minute detail.
with extreme attention to detail

Examples

The jeweler examined the diamond microscopically to detect any flaws.
Every document was reviewed microscopically before publication.
She studied the artifact microscopically, noting every small imperfection.

Synset Examples

every manuscript was edited microscopically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minutely - in great detail; in a very detailed manner

Etymology

From 'microscopic' + '-ally', from Greek 'micros' (small) and 'skopein' (to look).

2. ADV

/ ˌmaɪ.krəˈskɑː.pɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəˈskɒ.pɪ.kli / (BrE)
in a way that can only be seen with a microscope, or by means of a microscope; on a very small scale
in a way visible only with a microscope

Examples

The blood sample was examined microscopically for the presence of bacteria.
The crystals were microscopically small, requiring special equipment to see them.
When viewed microscopically, the tissue showed signs of infection.

Synset Examples

the blood was examined microscopically
the seeds were microscopically small

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microscopic - so small as to be visible only with a microscope

Etymology

From 'microscopic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'micros' (small) and 'skopein' (to look).
