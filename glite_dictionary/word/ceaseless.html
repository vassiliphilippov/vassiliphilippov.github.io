ceaseless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːsləs / (AmE), / ˈsiːsləs / (BrE)
continuing without stopping or pausing; seeming to never end
never-ending; continuous

Examples

The ceaseless sound of traffic kept her awake all night.
Their ceaseless efforts finally paid off when the project succeeded.
Rain fell in a ceaseless drizzle that lasted for days.

Synset Examples

the ceaseless thunder of surf
in constant pain
night and day we live with the incessant noise of the city
the never-ending search for happiness
the perpetual struggle to maintain standards in a democracy
man's unceasing warfare with drought and isolation
unremitting demands of hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endless - having or seeming to have no end or limit
  • Synonym: incessant - continuing without interruption
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

Formed from 'cease' (Middle English cesen, from Old French cesser, from Latin cessare) + '-less'.
