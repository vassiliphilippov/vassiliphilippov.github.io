Guillaume de Grimoard

1. PROPN

/ ɡiːˈjoʊm də ɡriːˈmɑːrd / (AmE), / ɡiːˈjɔːm də ɡriːˈmɑːr / (BrE)
(1310–1370) French pope (Urban V) who served from 1362 to 1370, attempted to restore the papacy to Rome, and was canonized in 1870.
French pope (Urban V), 14th century

Examples

Guillaume de Grimoard became pope in 1362 and was later canonized as Urban V.
Historians note Guillaume de Grimoard's efforts to move the papacy back to Rome.
The papal reign of Guillaume de Grimoard was marked by his attempts to mediate international conflicts.
