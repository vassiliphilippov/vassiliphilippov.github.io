Ranunculales

1. NOUN

/ rəˌnʌŋkjəˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / rəˌnʌŋkjʊˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes the buttercup family and related families of herbs, shrubs, and trees
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Ranunculales order contains many familiar garden plants, including various species of buttercups.
Scientists studied the evolutionary relationships within the Ranunculales using DNA analysis.
Several medicinal plants belong to the Ranunculales, valued for their bioactive compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ranunculaceae - the buttercup family of flowering plants

Etymology

From the Latin genus name *Ranunculus* (buttercup) and taxonomic suffix -ales (denoting plant orders).

2. NOUN

/ rəˌnʌŋkjəˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / rəˌnʌŋkjʊˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
a large order of flowering plants that includes herbs, shrubs, and trees, such as buttercups and barberries.
an order of flowering plants

Examples

The Ranunculales order includes both well-known garden flowers and less familiar woodland plants.
Many plants in the Ranunculales are used in traditional medicines worldwide.
Botanists often study Ranunculales to better understand the evolution of flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ranunculaceae - the buttercup family, a family within Ranunculales
  • Degree Lower: Magnoliales - a related order of flowering plants

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Ranunculus (the type genus) + -ales, a suffix for plant orders.
