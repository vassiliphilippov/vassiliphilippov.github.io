denunciatory

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈnʌnsiˌətɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈnʌnsiətəri / (BrE)
expressing strong condemnation or containing a warning of punishment.
expressing condemnation or warning of punishment

Examples

The prosecutor's denunciatory remarks left no doubt about her view of the defendant's actions.
His speech was filled with denunciatory language, warning of consequences for wrongdoers.
A denunciatory tone ran throughout the editorial, criticizing the government's handling of the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusatory - suggesting someone has done something wrong; accusing
  • Antonym: commendatory - expressing praise or recommendation

Etymology

From Latin 'denuntiare' (to denounce, announce), via French 'dénonciatoire'.
