Boletellus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈlɛtələs / (AmE), / bəʊˈlɛtələs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi belonging to the Boletaceae family, characterized by annual fruit bodies that grow on wood and a centrally placed stem.
genus of fungi with wood-growing fruit bodies

Examples

Many mycologists study species of Boletellus due to their distinctive spore prints.
The fruit body of Boletellus often appears on decaying wood in damp forests.
Some types of Boletellus are easily confused with other boletes because of their similar shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boletus - genus of fungi closely related to Boletellus

Etymology

From Latin 'boletus' (mushroom) and diminutive suffix '-ellus'.

2. NOUN

/ boʊˌlɛˈtɛləs / (AmE), / bəʊˌlɛˈtɛləs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, characterized by annual fruiting bodies that grow on wood and have centrally placed stems.
genus of wood-growing fungi

Examples

Several species within the genus Boletellus are known for their distinctive rough or scaly mushroom caps.
Mycologists discovered a new variety of Boletellus growing on decaying logs in the rainforest.
The fruit bodies of Boletellus typically appear on wood rather than on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boletus - another genus in the family Boletaceae, often with fleshy, pored mushrooms

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive of Boletus, the type genus of the Boletaceae.
