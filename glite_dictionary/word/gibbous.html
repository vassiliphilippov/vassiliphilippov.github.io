gibbous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪb.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪb.əs / (BrE)
(of the moon or a planet) more than half but not fully illuminated by the sun; between a half moon and full moon.
more than half full (of the moon/planet)

Examples

Tonight, the gibbous moon cast a bright glow across the lake.
The astronomer explained that after a crescent phase, the moon becomes gibbous before reaching fullness.
Through the telescope, we could clearly see the features of the gibbous moon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crescent - less than half illuminated phase of the moon
  • Degree Higher: full moon - moon when it is fully illuminated

Etymology

From Latin gibbosus (humpbacked), from gibbus (hump).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪb.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪb.əs / (BrE)
having a hump or being affected by kyphosis; describing a body part that is rounded or protuberant, especially in reference to abnormal spinal curvature.
having a hump; affected by kyphosis
medical

Examples

The elderly man walked with a gibbous back due to years of spinal problems.
A gibbous deformity was evident on the patient's upper spine.
After the injury, her posture became noticeably gibbous over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kyphotic - relating to or affected by kyphosis (abnormal spinal curvature)

Etymology

From Latin 'gibbosus' (humped, hunched), from 'gibbus' (hump).

Synset POS: s
