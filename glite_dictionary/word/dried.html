dried

1. ADJ

/ draɪd / (AmE), / draɪd / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) preserved by removing natural moisture, often to increase shelf life or for culinary purposes.
preserved by moisture removal

Examples

We always keep some dried fruit in the pantry for baking and snacking.
The hikers packed dried beef as a lightweight source of protein for their trip.
She added a handful of dried herbs to the sauce for extra flavor.

Synset Examples

dried beef
dried fruit
dehydrated eggs
shredded and desiccated coconut meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dehydrated - preserved by removing water; made dry
  • Synonym: desiccated - dried to remove moisture, often finely shredded

2. ADJ

/ draɪd / (AmE), / draɪd / (BrE)
no longer wet; having had all moisture removed or evaporated.
no longer wet

Examples

She hung the towels outside until they were completely dried.
The dried paint began to crack and peel off the wall.
He brushed away the dried mud from his shoes before entering the house.

Synset Examples

the ink has dried
a face marked with dried tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet
  • Antonym: wet - covered or saturated with water or another liquid
