streamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstriː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈstriː.mə / (BrE)
a long, narrow flag, often tapering, typically used as a decoration or signal
long, narrow, tapering flag

Examples

Colorful streamers hung from every window at the festival.
The soldiers saluted as the streamer fluttered in the breeze above the fort.
She decorated the hall with red, white, and blue streamers for Independence Day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design, carried in a demonstration or procession

3. NOUN

/ ˈstriːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmə / (BrE)
A long, narrow strip of cloth, paper, or other material, often used for decoration or advertising by being hung or waved.
long decorative strip of material

Examples

Colorful streamers hung from the ceiling for the birthday party.
They decorated the parade float with red and white streamers.
The company used a giant streamer to advertise its anniversary sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design, hung in a public place
  • Synonym: pennant - a tapering flag, often used for decoration or in sports

Etymology

From 'stream', Middle English, referring to a flowing movement; + '-er', meaning something that flows or hangs.

4. NOUN

/ ˈstriːmər / (AmE), / ˈstriːmə / (BrE)
a long, narrow line or trail of light, flame, or similar substance that streams through the air
long, narrow trail of light or flame

Examples

The fireworks left bright streamers of light across the night sky.
We marveled at the streamers of aurora dancing above the mountains.
The building was engulfed in streamers of flames after the explosion.

Synset Examples

streamers of flames

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trail - a mark or line left by something moving
  • Synonym: flare - a bright and brief burst of light or flame

Etymology

From stream + -er, meaning something that streams or flows.
