canalization

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnæl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌnæl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of creating a canal or converting land or waterways into canals
the making or conversion of canals

Examples

The canalization of the river improved irrigation for nearby farms.
Urban canalization projects can help control flooding in low-lying areas.
Concerns were raised over the environmental impact of the canalization of the wetland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channelization - the process of making or modifying a channel, especially of a river

Etymology

From French canalisation, from 'canal' (channel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkænəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌnælɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of directing or managing activities, ideas, or communications through specific channels or established pathways
management via specified channels

Examples

Effective canalization of information ensures clear communication within large organizations.
The canalization of customer feedback helps the company address issues efficiently.
Successful projects often depend on the proper canalization of resources and ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channeling - the act of directing something through a channel

Etymology

From French canalisation, from canal (channel) + -isation (-ization).

Synset POS: n
