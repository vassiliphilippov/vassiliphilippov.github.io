ungulate

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌŋɡjʊlət / (BrE)
any mammal with hooves, such as horses, cows, deer, or pigs, not necessarily closely related
hoofed mammal (e.g., horse, deer, pig)

Examples

The giraffe is an unusual ungulate with a long neck adapted for feeding on tall trees.
Many African savannas are home to numerous ungulates, including zebras, antelopes, and gazelles.
Paleontologists discovered fossilized remains of a prehistoric ungulate in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hooved - having hooves

Etymology

From Latin 'ungulatus' (hoofed), from 'ungula' (hoof, claw).

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌŋɡjələt, ˈʌŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌŋɡjʊlət, ˈʌŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal) having hoofs or resembling animals with hoofs.
having hoofs or resembling hoofed animals

Examples

The giraffe is an ungulate species known for its long neck and legs.
Many ungulate mammals wander the grasslands in search of food.
Paleontologists discovered the fossilized remains of an ungulate ancestor in the ancient sediment.

Synset Examples

horses and other hoofed animals

Etymology

From Latin 'ungulatus' (hoofed), from 'ungula' (hoof).
