coccidium

1. NOUN

/ kɑkˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a microscopic, single-celled parasite that infects the digestive tract of vertebrates and some invertebrates
single-celled digestive tract parasite
medical

Examples

The veterinarian detected a coccidium infection in the puppy's stool sample.
Many birds are susceptible to illness caused by coccidium parasites in their intestines.
The lifecycle of a coccidium typically involves multiple stages within a single host.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic organism, often parasitic

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek kokkos (grain, seed).
