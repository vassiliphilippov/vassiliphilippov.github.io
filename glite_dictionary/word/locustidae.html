Locustidae

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈkʌstɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ləʊˈkʌstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects comprising the short-horned grasshoppers, including the true locusts.
family of short-horned grasshoppers

Examples

Scientists classified the insect within the Locustidae family because of its short antennae.
Many of the most destructive agricultural pests belong to the Locustidae.
Members of the Locustidae are notable for their ability to form large swarms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acrididae - the family now commonly known as the short-horned grasshoppers (sometimes synonymized with Locustidae)
  • Synonym: locust - a large grasshopper that often migrates in swarms and causes damage to crops

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'locusta' (locust) + family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ loʊˈkʌstɪˌdi / (AmE), / ləʊˈkʌstɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as short-horned grasshoppers or true locusts, characterized by their short antennae and ability to swarm.
family of true locusts (short-horned grasshoppers)

Examples

Scientists studied the migration patterns of insects in the Locustidae family to understand swarm behavior.
The red-winged grasshopper belongs to the Locustidae family, known for their powerful jumping legs.
Members of the Locustidae are often destructive to crops during plague years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acrididae - a synonymic family name sometimes used for locusts and short-horned grasshoppers
  • Synonym: locust - the insect species of the Locustidae family that can swarm

Etymology

From New Latin Locusta (locust) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
