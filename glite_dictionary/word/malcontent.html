malcontent

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæl.kənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˌmæl.kənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
Dissatisfied and rebellious, especially toward authority or established order.
discontented, especially with authority

Examples

The malcontent employees organized a protest against the new company policies.
His malcontent attitude made him unpopular with the school administration.
Some malcontent voices were raised during the meeting, questioning the committee's decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discontented - feeling dissatisfaction or unrest
  • Antonym: complacent - showing smug satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements

Etymology

From French *malcontent*, from Italian *malcontento* (discontented), from Latin *male* (badly) + *contentus* (contented).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæl.kənˌtɛnt / (AmE), / ˈmæl.kən.tɛnt / (BrE)
a person who is dissatisfied or rebellious, often against established authority or prevailing conditions
dissatisfied or rebellious person

Examples

The new regulations turned several employees into outspoken malcontents unwilling to accept the changes.
He was dismissed as a malcontent who always complained about management, regardless of the situation.
Throughout history, malcontents have often sparked significant social and political change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - a person who resists authority or control
  • Antonym: contented - feeling or expressing happiness or satisfaction

Etymology

From Middle French 'malcontent', from Italian 'malcontento', from Latin 'malus' (bad) + 'contentus' (content).
