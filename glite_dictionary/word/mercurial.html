mercurial

1. ADJ

/ mərˈkjʊəriəl / (AmE), / məˈkjʊəriəl / (BrE)
relating to or influenced by the planet Mercury, especially in astrology.
relating to the planet Mercury (astrology)

Examples

Some ancient texts describe mercurial characteristics as quick-witted and changeable.
Astrologers believe that a strong mercurial influence leads to great communicative skills.
The city's layout was once thought to follow mercurial channels, tied to planetary movements.

Synset Examples

the Mercurial canals

Etymology

From Latin Mercuriālis, from Mercurius (Mercury, the Roman god and planet).

with qualities of the god Mercury

Examples

His mercurial wit and eloquence won over even his harshest critics.
The diplomat's mercurial shrewdness helped negotiate a difficult treaty.

4. ADJ

/ mɚˈkjʊriəl / (AmE), / mɜːˈkjʊəriəl / (BrE)
(of a person or mood) subject to sudden or unpredictable changes of mood or mind.
liable to sudden, unpredictable change

Examples

Her mercurial moods made it difficult to predict how she would react.
The weather here is notoriously mercurial, changing from sunshine to storms within minutes.
As a manager, his mercurial nature kept the whole team on their toes.

Synset Examples

erratic behavior
fickle weather
mercurial twists of temperament
a quicksilver character, cool and willful at one moment, utterly fragile the next

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially in regards to loyalties or affections
  • Synonym: unstable - prone to change or fluctuation; not stable
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not likely to change

Etymology

From Latin 'Mercurius' (Mercury), originally relating to the swift Roman god Mercury.
