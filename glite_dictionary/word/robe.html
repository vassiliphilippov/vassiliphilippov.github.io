robe

1. NOUN

/ roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
a long, loose outer garment worn on official or ceremonial occasions, such as by judges, academics, or clergy
long formal ceremonial garment

Examples

The judge entered the courtroom wearing a black robe and took his seat.
Graduates proudly put on their caps and robes for the commencement ceremony.
In medieval portraits, kings and queens are often shown in richly decorated robes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gown - a long formal dress or garment, sometimes used for ceremonies

Etymology

From Old French *robe*, meaning 'clothing', originally from a Germanic root related to 'booty' or 'spoils'.

2. NOUN

/ roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
a long, loose piece of clothing, often worn on special occasions or for comfort, that hangs loosely from the shoulders
long, loose outer garment

Examples

She wore a silk robe when she stepped out of the shower.
Judges traditionally wear a black robe in court.
He entered the room dressed in a flowing ceremonial robe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gown - a long dress or robe, especially for formal occasions
  • Synonym: cloak - a loose outer garment, typically sleeveless, that hangs from the shoulders

Etymology

From Old French *robe* (garment, spoils), from Frankish *rouba* (spoils, things stolen), related to English 'rob'.

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
to dress someone in a robe or formal ceremonial garments, especially in a religious context
to dress in formal robes

Examples

The bishop was robed in ornate vestments for the ceremony.
Before the play, the actors robe themselves in elaborate costumes backstage.
She robed her students in academic gowns for the graduation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes on oneself or someone else

Etymology

From Old French *robe* (garment), from Frankish or Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
to cover or dress something as if with clothing, especially in a formal or figurative sense
cover or dress as with clothing

Examples

The mountain was robed in a thick blanket of mist by early morning.
The trees were robed in vibrant autumn colors each October.
The ancient statue is robed in legends and stories from the past.

Synset Examples

the mountain was clothed in tropical trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clothe - to put clothes on; to cover as with clothing
  • Antonym: to uncover - to remove a covering from something; to expose

Etymology

From Old French 'robe' (garment), from Frankish or Germanic origin.
