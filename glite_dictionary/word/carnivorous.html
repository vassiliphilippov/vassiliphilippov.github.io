carnivorous

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈnɪvərəs / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɪvərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) feeding on other animals; relating to or characteristic of meat-eating organisms.
feeding on other animals

Examples

The carnivorous diet of the tiger helps maintain the balance of the ecosystem.
Some plants, like the Venus flytrap, are carnivorous and digest insects to supplement their nutrition.
Bears are not strictly carnivorous because they also eat berries and plants.

Synset Examples

the lion and other carnivorous animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants
  • Antonym: omnivorous - feeding on both animals and plants

Etymology

From Latin carnivorus, from caro (flesh) + vorare (to devour).

2. ADJ

/ kɑrˈnɪv.ɚ.əs / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɪv.ər.əs / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) feeding on other animals.
feeding on animals

Examples

Lions are carnivorous predators that thrive at the top of the food chain.
Some plants, like the venus flytrap, are carnivorous and capture insects for nutrients.
Bears, although mostly omnivorous, can exhibit carnivorous behavior during certain seasons.

Synset Examples

carnivorous plants are capable of trapping and digesting small animals especially insects

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants
  • Antonym: omnivorous - feeding on both animal and plant matter

Etymology

From Latin carnivorus: caro, carn- (flesh) + vorare (to devour).
