daze

1. NOUN

/ deɪz / (AmE), / deɪz / (BrE)
A state of confusion or stunned disbelief, often caused by shock or distress.
state of stunned confusion

Examples

After hearing the news of his job loss, Mark walked around in a daze for hours.
The accident left her in such a daze that she could hardly speak.
He stared at the broken vase, still in a daze from what had just happened.

Synset Examples

his mother's death left him in a daze
he was numb with shock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or surprising event or experience
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in contrast to confusion

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse *deisa* (to stun, frighten).

2. VERB

/ deɪz / (AmE), / deɪz / (BrE)
to make someone unable to see clearly or think properly, especially due to a sudden intense light or shock
make unable to see or think clearly

Examples

The sudden camera flash dazed the driver, making it hard for him to see the road.
She was dazed by the midday sun after stepping outside from the dark theater.
The powerful spotlight dazed the performer for a moment before she recovered.

Synset Examples

She was dazzled by the bright headlights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzle - to blind temporarily with a bright light
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02166865-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ deɪz / (AmE), / deɪz / (BrE)
to stun or overwhelm someone, as with astonishment, shock, or disbelief, leaving them unable to think clearly
to stun or overwhelm with surprise

Examples

The sudden announcement dazed everyone in the room.
She was completely dazed by the unexpected turn of events.
His surprise confession seemed to daze his friends into silence.

Synset Examples

The news stunned her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stun - to shock or overwhelm someone so that they cannot react immediately
  • Synonym: to amaze - to fill someone with great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to confuse or puzzle someone completely

Etymology

Middle English, perhaps from Old Norse dasa (to become weary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02119913-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ deɪz / (AmE), / deɪz / (BrE)
A state of confusion and lack of clear awareness, often caused by shock, fatigue, or surprise.
state of confusion or bewilderment

Examples

After the accident, she wandered around in a daze for several minutes.
He sat staring at the television, lost in a daze of exhaustion.
The shocking news left everyone in a daze during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupefaction - a state of being stupefied or in stunned amazement
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

Probably from Middle English dasen (to become bewildered).

Source: WordNet
