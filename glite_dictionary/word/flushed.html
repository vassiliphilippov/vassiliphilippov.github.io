flushed

1. ADJ

/ flʌʃt / (AmE), / flʌʃt / (BrE)
(of a person's face or skin) red or pink because of emotion, exertion, or heat.
reddened in the face from emotion or effort

Examples

Her cheeks were flushed with excitement after hearing the good news.
He returned from his run, flushed and breathing heavily.
She grew flushed with embarrassment when she realized her mistake.

Synset Examples

crimson with fury
turned red from exertion
with puffy reddened eyes
red-faced and violent
flushed (or crimson) with embarrassment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pale - having less color than usual, especially in the face
  • Synonym: blushing - becoming red in the face from embarrassment or shyness

2. ADJ

/ flʌʃt / (AmE), / flʌʃt / (BrE)
(of a person or their face) having a pink or red color, especially as a sign of good health, emotion, or excitement.
having a pink or red face, esp. from health or emotion

Examples

After her morning run, her cheeks were flushed and glowing with energy.
He looked flushed with pride as he accepted the award.
Her face became flushed after spending the afternoon in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pale - having less color or lacking in color

Etymology

Past participle of 'flush', from Middle English *flusshen* (to rinse, rush out), possibly from Middle French *flus* (flow).
