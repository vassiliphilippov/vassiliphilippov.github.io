chowchow

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaʊˌtʃaʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaʊˌtʃaʊ / (BrE)
a type of pickled relish made from chopped vegetables and pickles, often seasoned with mustard; popular in the southern United States
pickled relish with chopped vegetables and mustard

Examples

She served homemade chowchow alongside the pulled pork sandwiches.
A jar of spicy chowchow added a tangy kick to the meal.
Many Southern families have their own recipes for chowchow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relish - a condiment made of pickled vegetables and spices

Etymology

From Chinese pidgin English, possibly related to the Mandarin term for 'mixed' (杂杂, zá zá), brought to North America by Chinese immigrants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaʊˌtʃaʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaʊˌtʃaʊ / (BrE)
a Chinese preserve made from mixed fruits and ginger, typically sweet and often used as a condiment or relish
Chinese fruit and ginger preserve

Examples

She served homemade chowchow with the meal, adding a sweet and tangy flavor to the dishes.
The recipe calls for a jar of chowchow made from peaches, ginger, and pineapples.
I bought some chowchow at the Asian market to use as a topping on grilled meats.

Etymology

From Chinese Mandarin dialect (preserved mixture), possibly related to Cantonese 'chāu-chāu'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
