patronizingly

1. ADV

/ ˌpeɪ.trəˈnaɪ.zɪŋ.li / (AmE), / ˌpæ.trəˈnaɪ.zɪŋ.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows a superior or condescending attitude toward others.
in a condescending manner

Examples

She spoke patronizingly to the children, assuming they would not understand.
He smiled patronizingly at her suggestion, dismissing it without real consideration.
The manager patronizingly explained basic concepts to his experienced staff.

Synset Examples

he treats his secretary condescendingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condescendingly - in a way that shows you think you are more important than others

Etymology

From 'patronizing' + '-ly'; 'patronizing' from 'patronize', ultimately from Latin 'patronus' (protector).
