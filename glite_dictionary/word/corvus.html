Corvus

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrvəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːvəs / (BrE)
a small constellation in the Southern Hemisphere near Virgo, represented as a crow in traditional astronomy
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The stars of Corvus are easily visible on clear spring nights in the Southern Hemisphere.
Astronomers use Corvus as a reference point for celestial navigation.
According to myth, Corvus represents a crow sent by Apollo.

Etymology

Latin for 'crow', from classical constellation names.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrvəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːvəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Corvidae, including crows and ravens
genus of crows and ravens

Examples

The genus Corvus encompasses both crows and ravens, which are known for their intelligence.
Ornithologists have classified several species of Corvus around the world.
Large flocks of Corvus can often be seen in rural and urban environments alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - a large, typically all-black bird of the genus Corvus
  • Synonym: raven - a large bird of the genus Corvus, especially Corvus corax

Etymology

From Latin 'corvus' meaning 'raven' or 'crow'.
