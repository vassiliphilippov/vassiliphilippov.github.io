eminence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnəns / (BrE)
a raised area or projection on a bone, typically serving as a site for the attachment of a muscle or ligament
bony protrusion for muscle or ligament attachment
medical

Examples

The greater trochanter is a large eminence on the femur for muscle attachment.
An anatomical eminence can be palpated just above the knee joint.
The surgeon identified the bony eminence before making the incision.

Etymology

From Latin eminentia (prominence, projection), from eminēre (to stand out).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnəns / (BrE)
the state of being highly respected or distinguished, especially in a particular field or profession
high status or distinction

Examples

The scientist gained worldwide eminence for her breakthrough discoveries.
He is recognized for his eminence in the field of medicine.
Rising to eminence requires years of dedication and hard work.

Synset Examples

a scholar of great eminence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - excellence that sets someone or something apart from others
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From Latin eminentia (prominence, excellence), from eminēre (to stand out).
