poetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
language or expression that has great beauty or evokes deep feeling, resembling the qualities of traditional poetry.
expression of beauty or emotion

Examples

There is a certain poetry in the way she describes the night sky.
The movement of the dancer had the poetry of a gentle breeze.
His speech contained a poetry that moved the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prose - ordinary written or spoken language without metrical structure; the opposite of poetry

Etymology

From Greek 'poietēs' (maker, poet) via Latin 'poeta' and Old French 'poetrie'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
Literature in metrical form, often expressing ideas and emotions through distinctive style, rhythm, and imagery.
literature in metrical form

Examples

She has been passionate about poetry since she was a child.
Many people find comfort in reading poetry during difficult times.
His latest book explores the boundaries between poetry and visual art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verse - writing arranged with metrical rhythm, typically having rhyme
  • Antonym: prose - written or spoken language in ordinary form, without metrical structure

Etymology

From Old French *poetrie*, from Latin *poetria*, from Greek *poietēs* (maker, poet).
