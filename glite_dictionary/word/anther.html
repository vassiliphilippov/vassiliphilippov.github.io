anther

1. NOUN

/ ˈænθər / (AmE), / ˈænθə / (BrE)
the part of a flower's stamen that produces and contains pollen, usually borne on a stalk
pollen-producing part of a flower's stamen

Examples

The bright yellow anther at the tip of each stamen is packed with pollen.
During pollination, bees collect pollen from the anther of each flower they visit.
Botanists observed that the anther opens at sunrise to release pollen.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stamen - the pollen-producing reproductive organ of a flower, typically consisting of an anther and a filament
  • Degree Lower: filament - the stalk that supports the anther in a flower's stamen

Etymology

From Greek 'anthēra', meaning 'flowery', from 'anthos' (flower).
