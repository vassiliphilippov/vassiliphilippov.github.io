to disparage

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɛrɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈspærɪdʒ / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something in a way that shows you do not respect or value them; to speak about in a derogatory manner
to belittle or speak negatively about

Examples

It is unfair to disparage someone based on rumors rather than facts.
He tends to disparage his competitors whenever he is interviewed.
Her achievements were often disparaged by those jealous of her success.

Synset Examples

She disparaged her student's efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'desparagier' (to degrade), from 'des-' (apart) + 'parage' (rank, lineage).
