balefire

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪlˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A large outdoor fire lit as a signal or for celebration, especially in historical or traditional contexts.
large outdoor signal or celebration fire

Examples

Villagers gathered around the balefire to celebrate the midsummer festival.
A single balefire burned on the hilltop as a warning to the valley below.
The ancient ritual called for lighting a balefire to drive away evil spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonfire - a large open-air fire used for celebration or disposal of waste

Etymology

From Old English bǣl (fire, funeral pyre) + fire.
