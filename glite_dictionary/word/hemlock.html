hemlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.lɒk / (BrE)
An evergreen tree of the genus Tsuga, found chiefly in North America and eastern Asia, with flat needle-like leaves and small cones.
evergreen tree of the genus Tsuga

Examples

The forest was dominated by towering hemlock trees that blocked out much of the sunlight.
After the storm, several ancient hemlocks had fallen across the trail.
He built a rustic bench out of hemlock wood near the cabin.

Etymology

From Middle English 'hemlok', originally applied to the poisonous plant, later also to the tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.lɒk / (BrE)
A tall, poisonous plant with fern-like leaves and clusters of small white flowers, native to Europe and North Africa and naturalized elsewhere.
poisonous tall plant with white flowers

Examples

The ancient Greeks used hemlock as a method of execution due to its deadly poison.
She mistook the plant for parsley but it was actually hemlock, which is extremely toxic.
Large clusters of white hemlock flowers were growing near the edge of the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conium - Genus of poisonous plants including the hemlock
  • Synonym: cicuta - Water hemlock, another poisonous plant sometimes confused with hemlock

Etymology

Old English *hemlic*; probably from Proto-Germanic *hamaliko*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.lɒk / (BrE)
soft, coarse, splintery wood from a hemlock tree, especially the western hemlock, used in construction and carpentry
wood from a hemlock tree, especially western hemlock

Examples

The beams were made of hemlock, chosen for its strength and availability.
Carpenters often use hemlock for framing because it is easy to work with.
Unlike oak or maple, hemlock tends to split more easily when nailed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock (tree) - a tree of the genus Tsuga, native to North America and Asia

Etymology

From Middle English 'hemlok', of Germanic origin; the wood is named after the tree.

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.lɒk / (BrE)
a poisonous drug obtained from the Eurasian plant Conium maculatum, historically used as a method of execution
poisonous drug from the Conium plant

Examples

The philosopher Socrates was sentenced to death by hemlock.
Even a small dose of hemlock can be fatal if ingested.
The study described the effects of hemlock poisoning on the nervous system.

Synset Examples

Socrates refused to flee and died by drinking hemlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Conium - a genus of poisonous plants including the source of hemlock

Etymology

Old English *hemlic*, related to Old High German *hemlīh* (plant name).
