ocellated

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈsɛl.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əʊˈsɛl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having eye-like spots or markings, called ocelli, often used in descriptions of animals or feathers
having eye-like spots or markings

Examples

The ocellated turkey is known for the striking eye-like spots on its tail feathers.
Several species of butterflies display ocellated patterns on their wings to deter predators.
Researchers observed an ocellated lizard basking on a rocky outcrop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - having spots; marked with dots
  • Antonym: plain - having no markings or decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'ocellatus', from 'ocellus' (little eye, eyelet), diminutive of 'oculus' (eye).
