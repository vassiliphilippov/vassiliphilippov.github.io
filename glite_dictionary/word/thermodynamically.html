thermodynamically

1. ADV

/ ˌθɜːrmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪkli / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to the principles or laws of thermodynamics.
in relation to thermodynamics

Examples

The reaction is thermodynamically unfavorable under standard conditions.
Changing the pressure alters whether the process is thermodynamically possible.
Water can be thermodynamically stable in all three phases depending on temperature and pressure.

Synset Examples

this phenomenon is thermodynamically impossible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermodynamic - relating to the study of energy and its transformations

Etymology

From 'thermodynamic' (relating to thermodynamics) + '-ally' (adverb suffix).
