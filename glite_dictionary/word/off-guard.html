off-guard

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔfˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
not prepared for something; not alert or vigilant
not prepared; not alert

Examples

The sudden question caught her completely off-guard during the interview.
He was taken off-guard by the unexpected announcement.
The team played so quickly that their opponents were caught off-guard.

Synset Examples

the blow caught him napping
caught in an off-guard moment
found him off his guard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprepared - not ready or equipped for something
  • Antonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
