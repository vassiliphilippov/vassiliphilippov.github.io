electrification

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or activity of causing strong excitement or thrill in a person or group
causing strong excitement or thrill

Examples

The electrification of the crowd was evident as the band played their biggest hit.
Her speech led to the electrification of the entire audience, leaving everyone inspired.
There was a sense of electrification in the air on the night of the championship match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of feeling bored or uninterested

Etymology

From electrify + -ation, modeled after similar formations.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktrəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of providing a region, building, or system with electric power
the process of supplying electricity

Examples

The electrification of rural areas transformed the quality of life for many communities.
Major investments in electrification are needed to modernize the country's railways.
The government's plan aims to speed up the electrification of public transportation systems.

Synset Examples

the electrification of rural Tennessee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to electrify - to supply with electricity

Etymology

From electrify + -ation; electrify from French électrifier, from électricité (electricity).
