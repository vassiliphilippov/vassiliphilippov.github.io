typicality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪpɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌtɪpɪˈkæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being typical; the degree to which something represents a standard case or example
state of being typical

Examples

The typicality of his behavior made him an excellent representative of the group.
Researchers analyzed the typicality of the chosen samples before drawing conclusions.
Its design has a certain typicality found in most modern architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typical - having the usual qualities of a particular group or thing
  • Antonym: atypicality - the state of not being typical or usual

Etymology

From typical + -ity, modeled after the pattern of Latin-derived abstract nouns.
