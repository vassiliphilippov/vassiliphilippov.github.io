drivel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvəl / (BrE)
silly or senseless talk or ideas; nonsense.
silly or senseless talk; nonsense

Examples

Most of what he said during the meeting was just drivel.
The article was dismissed as pure drivel by the critics.
She grew tired of listening to his endless drivel about nothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Antonym: sense - something that has meaning or is reasonable

Etymology

From Middle English *drivelen* (to slobber), related to drool and nonsense speech.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvəl / (BrE)
Saliva that flows or spills from the mouth, especially unintentionally.
saliva coming from the mouth

Examples

The old man wiped the drivel from his chin with a handkerchief.
She was so exhausted that a little drivel escaped while she dozed in her chair.
The infant's face was covered in drivel after his nap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - to let saliva run from the mouth

Etymology

From Old English *drifan* (to let fall in drops).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05424022-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈdrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvəl / (BrE)
(of saliva) to flow from the mouth; to let saliva run from the mouth, often unintentionally.
let saliva flow from the mouth

Examples

The elderly man's lips parted as he began to drivel in his sleep.
Infants often drivel when they are teething.
She wiped the puppy's mouth, which continued to drivel onto her shirt.

Synset Examples

The baby drooled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - let saliva flow from the mouth

Etymology

From Old English *dreflian* (to slobber, dribble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00102728-v
Synset POS: v
