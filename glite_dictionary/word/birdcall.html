birdcall

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdˌkɑl / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdˌkɔːl / (BrE)
A device designed to imitate the sound of a bird, often used by hunters or bird watchers.
device for imitating bird sounds

Examples

The hunter used a birdcall to attract wild turkeys in the forest.
She collects different types of birdcalls for her birdwatching hobby.
With a simple squeeze, the birdcall mimicked a robin's song perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birdsong - the musical calls or songs made by birds

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝdˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdˌkɔːl / (BrE)
the characteristic sound or song made by a bird, typically used to communicate with others of its species
the sound or song made by a bird

Examples

We woke up to the beautiful birdcall outside our window.
Each species has its own distinct birdcall for attracting mates or warning of danger.
The forest was alive with the birdcall of robins and sparrows in the early morning.

Synset Examples

a bird will not learn its song unless it hears it at an early age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birdsongs - the musical sounds produced by birds, often more structured than calls
  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds
