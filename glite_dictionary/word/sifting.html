sifting

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of separating and removing unwanted material, especially the separation of grain from chaff by passing it through a sieve
separating grain from chaff or impurities

Examples

Traditional farming methods included sifting by hand to remove impurities from the grain.
After harvesting, the women performed the sifting to separate the wheat kernels from the chaff.
The sifting of flour ensures a smooth texture for baking.

Synset Examples

the winnowing was done by women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winnowing - the process of separating grain from chaff by air movement

Etymology

From Middle English siften, from Old English siftan, related to 'sieve'.
