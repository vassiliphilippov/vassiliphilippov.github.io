actus reus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑktəs ˈreɪəs / (AmE), / ˈæktəs ˈreɪəs / (BrE)
The physical act or unlawful conduct that constitutes a criminal offense, as opposed to the mental state or intent.
physical act constituting a crime
legal

Examples

To establish guilt, both actus reus and mens rea must generally be proven.
The prosecution could not demonstrate the actus reus necessary for a conviction.
Without evidence of actus reus, the charges had to be dropped.

Synset Examples

he denied any wrongdoing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mens rea - the mental state or intent required to commit a crime

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'guilty act.'
