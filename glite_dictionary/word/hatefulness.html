hatefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈheɪtfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being hateful or full of hate
the quality of being hateful

Examples

The hatefulness in his words shocked everyone at the meeting.
She could not understand the hatefulness that had grown between the two families.
Stories like this only serve to highlight the hatefulness that exists in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatred - intense dislike or hostility
  • Antonym: love - an intense feeling of deep affection; opposite of hate

Etymology

From 'hateful' + '-ness', where 'hateful' is from Middle English, from 'hate' + '-ful'.
