Istiophorus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪstioʊˈfɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌɪstɪəˈfɒrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fish in the family Istiophoridae, which includes the sailfish.
genus of marine fish (sailfish)

Examples

The Istiophorus genus includes some of the fastest fish in the ocean.
Scientists recently studied the migratory patterns of Istiophorus in the Atlantic Ocean.
The distinctive dorsal fin is a key feature of species belonging to Istiophorus.

Etymology

From Greek istios (sail) and pherein (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪstiˈɑːfərəs / (AmE), / ˌɪstiˈɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fish in the family Istiophoridae, commonly known as sailfish, characterized by their long bills and prominent dorsal fins.
genus of sailfish

Examples

The genus Istiophorus includes the well-known sailfish, prized by sport fishermen worldwide.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Istiophorus in the Atlantic Ocean.
Members of Istiophorus are easily identified by their distinctive, elongated dorsal fins.

Etymology

From Greek 'istios' (sail) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the sail-like dorsal fin.
