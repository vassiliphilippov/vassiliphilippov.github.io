enflurane

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈflʊr.eɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈflʊə.reɪn / (BrE)
a nonflammable liquid used as an inhalation general anesthetic, especially under the trade name Ethrane
inhalation general anesthetic (liquid)
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered enflurane to ensure the patient remained unconscious during surgery.
Research has shown that enflurane has a rapid onset and recovery profile compared to older anesthetics.
Although enflurane is less commonly used today, it played a vital role in the development of modern anesthesia.

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature, related to its structure (containing fluorine atoms); trade name Ethrane.
