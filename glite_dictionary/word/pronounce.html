to pronounce

1. VERB

/ prəˈnaʊns / (AmE), / prəˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to declare or state a formal judgment or opinion about someone or something, especially officially or authoritatively
formally declare a judgment

Examples

The committee will pronounce judgment on the allegations next week.
He was pronounced guilty by the court after a lengthy trial.
The doctor refused to pronounce on the patient's chances of recovery.

Synset Examples

They labeled him unfit to work here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to make known formally or officially
  • Synonym: judge - to form an opinion or conclusion about

Etymology

From Latin pronuntiare (to proclaim, announce).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00973682-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈnaʊns / (AmE), / prəˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to say a word or sound in a particular way; to articulate the sounds of a word
say the sounds of a word

Examples

She always tries to pronounce foreign names correctly.
How do you pronounce this word?
The teacher helped the students pronounce the difficult sounds clearly.

Synset Examples

She pronounces French words in a funny way
I cannot say ‘zip wire’
Can the child sound out this complicated word?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express or pronounce clearly
  • Antonym: to mispronounce - to pronounce incorrectly

Etymology

From Old French *pronuncier*, from Latin *pronuntiare* (to proclaim, announce).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00980581-v
Synset POS: v
