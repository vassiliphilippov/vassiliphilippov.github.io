tangibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæn.dʒəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌtan.dʒɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be touched or physically felt; perceptibility by touch.
quality of being touchable

Examples

The tangibility of the sculpture allowed visitors to appreciate its intricate details firsthand.
Some people prefer physical books for their tangibility over digital versions.
The architect emphasized the importance of tangibility in modern building materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intangibility - the quality of not being able to be touched

Etymology

From Latin 'tangibilis' (that may be touched) + English suffix '-ity'.
