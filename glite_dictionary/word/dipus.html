Dipus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪpəs / (BrE)
The type genus of Dipodidae, comprising typical jerboas that have three toes on each hind foot.
genus of three-toed jerboas

Examples

The genus Dipus is known for its distinctive three-toed hind feet among rodents.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Dipus genus in Central Asia.
Unlike other jerboas, Dipus species typically live in burrows in sandy deserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerboa - a small, jumping desert rodent with long hind legs

Etymology

From Greek 'dí-,' meaning 'two,' and 'poús,' meaning 'foot,' referencing the rodent's distinctive feet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪp.əs / (BrE)
A genus of jerboas in the family Dipodidae, characterized by three toes on each hind foot; the type genus of Dipodidae.
genus of jerboas (type genus Dipodidae)

Examples

The Dipus genus is notable for its characteristic three-toed hind feet.
Scientists have studied various species within the Dipus genus to understand rodent locomotion.
A common example of Dipus is the northern three-toed jerboa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerboa - small jumping rodent of arid regions of Asia and northern Africa

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek dipous (two-footed, with 'di-' meaning 'two' and 'pous' meaning 'foot').
