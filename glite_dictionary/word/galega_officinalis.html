Galega officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈliːɡə ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ɡəˈliːɡə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A tall, bushy perennial plant native to Europe, known for its pinnate leaves and spikes of blue flowers, sometimes used in medicine.
European perennial plant with blue flowers

Examples

The gardens featured several Galega officinalis plants in full bloom beside the pond.
Herbalists have long used Galega officinalis in traditional remedies for various ailments.
Bees are especially attracted to the blue flowers of Galega officinalis during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Galega' (genus name); 'officinalis' indicating traditional medicinal use.
