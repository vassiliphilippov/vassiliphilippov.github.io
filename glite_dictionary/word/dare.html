dare

1. NOUN

/ dɛr / (AmE), / dɛə / (BrE)
a challenge to do something that may be dangerous, difficult, or foolish
challenge to do something risky

Examples

Tom couldn't say no to a dare and ended up jumping into the lake.
She accepted the dare to dance in front of the whole class.
The game got out of hand when the dare became too dangerous.

Synset Examples

he could never refuse a dare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - an invitation to prove or justify something, especially by action

Etymology

From Old English durran (to dare, risk), related to Proto-Germanic *durraną.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07246192-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ dɛr / (AmE), / dɛə / (BrE)
to challenge someone to do something difficult, dangerous or embarrassing, often to provoke a response
challenge someone to do something

Examples

I dare you to tell her the truth about what happened.
He didn't dare his friend to jump off the wall, but she did it anyway.
Sometimes children dare each other to do silly or risky things.

Synset Examples

I dare you!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - invite someone to take part in a contest or prove something

Etymology

Old English durran, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch durven and German dürfen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00869761-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɛr / (AmE), / dɛə / (BrE)
to have enough courage to do something or to try doing something, especially something difficult or risky
to have courage to do something

Examples

He didn't dare speak up in the meeting, even though he disagreed.
Would you dare to jump off the high diving board?
She dares to question traditional ways in her art.

Synset Examples

I don't dare call him
she dares to dress differently from the others

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to take part in a contest; a closely related verb
  • Antonym: fear - to be afraid or worried

Etymology

Old English *dearr* (present), *dorste* (past), related to Old High German *tar* (he dares).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02550742-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dɛr / (AmE), / dɛə / (BrE)
to act boldly or presumptuously, especially without permission; to have the courage to do something that is considered rude or improper
act boldly or presumptuously

Examples

How dare you question my honesty in front of everyone?
She didn't dare defy the manager's instructions, even though she disagreed.
Do you dare interrupt the meeting again without permission?

Synset Examples

How dare you call my lawyer?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to presume - to act without permission or take liberties
  • Synonym: challenge - to dare or provoke someone to do something

Etymology

From Old English *dearr* (to dare, venture).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02380015-v
Synset POS: v
