Sanicula bipinnatifida

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɪkjʊlə ˌbaɪˌpɪnəˈtɪfɪdə / (AmE), / səˈnɪkjʊlə ˌbaɪˌpɪnəˈtɪfɪdə / (BrE)
A species of sanicle native to the northwestern United States and British Columbia, known for its yellow, red, or purple flowers.
species of sanicle with colored flowers

Examples

Sanicula bipinnatifida blooms in early spring, brightening the woodland floor with its colorful flowers.
Botanists often study Sanicula bipinnatifida because of its unusual leaf structure and vibrant hues.
The conservation area protects rare plants, including Sanicula bipinnatifida and other native species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanicle - a plant of the genus Sanicula in the parsley family

Etymology

From Latin; 'Sanicula' is a genus name, 'bipinnatifida' indicates bipinnately divided leaves.
