vim

1. NOUN

/ vɪm / (AmE), / vɪm / (BrE)
energy, enthusiasm, or lively spirit; a healthy capacity for vigorous activity.
energy; enthusiasm; lively spirit

Examples

Despite his age, the coach approached every practice with incredible vim.
The children ran around the playground, bursting with vim.
She attacked the new project with her usual vim and creativity.

Synset Examples

jogging works off my excess energy
he seemed full of vim and vigor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - active physical or mental strength or energy
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Latin 'vim', accusative of 'vis' (strength, energy).

2. NOUN

/ vɪm / (AmE), / vɪm / (BrE)
enthusiastic energy and liveliness; exuberance, especially in style or manner
energy and liveliness

Examples

Despite his age, the speaker delivered his address with surprising vim.
Her latest novel is written with the same vim that made her famous.
The festival was filled with music, dancing, and general vim.

Synset Examples

his writing conveys great energy
a remarkable muscularity of style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - active bodily or mental strength or energy
  • Synonym: energy - the strength and vitality required for sustained activity

Etymology

Probably of U.S. origin, perhaps a shortened form of 'vim and vigor' or influenced by Latin 'vis, vim' (force, energy).
