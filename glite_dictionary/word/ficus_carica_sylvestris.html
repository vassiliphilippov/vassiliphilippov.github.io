Ficus carica sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.kəs ˈkær.ɪ.kə sɪlˈvɛs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.kəs ˈkær.ɪ.kə sɪlˈvɛs.trɪs / (BrE)
A wild variety of the common fig, used to facilitate pollination of certain figs.
wild variety of the common fig

Examples

The caprifig, or Ficus carica sylvestris, is often planted near orchards to aid pollination.
Wild populations of Ficus carica sylvestris play an important role in the ecosystem of the Mediterranean region.
Botanists studied Ficus carica sylvestris to better understand the genetic diversity within the fig species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig - fruit-bearing tree of the species Ficus carica

Etymology

From Latin: Ficus (fig), carica (Carian, type locality), sylvestris (of the woods, wild).
