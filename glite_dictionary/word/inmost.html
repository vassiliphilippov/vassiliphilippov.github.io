inmost

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪn.məʊst / (BrE)
(of thoughts, feelings, etc.) most deeply felt or most private; deepest within.
deepest or most private part

Examples

She rarely shared her inmost thoughts with anyone outside her family.
Meditation can help you connect with your inmost feelings.
He kept his inmost fears hidden from the world.

Synset Examples

one's innermost feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innermost - furthest inward or most private

Etymology

From Middle English innemost, from Old English innemāst (meaning 'most inner').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00954899-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪnməʊst / (BrE)
Situated or occurring deepest within or farthest from the outside.
deepest or farthest within

Examples

She kept her inmost thoughts hidden from her friends.
The explorer ventured into the inmost parts of the cave.
His inmost feelings were difficult to express in words.

Synset Examples

the innermost chamber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innermost - situated farthest within; deepest

Etymology

From Middle English innemost, from in + most.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00953673-s
Synset POS: s
