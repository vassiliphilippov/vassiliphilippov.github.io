accoucheuse

1. NOUN

/ ə.kuˈʃuz / (AmE), / a.kuˈʃɜːz / (BrE)
a woman, especially one professionally trained, who assists at childbirth; a midwife
female midwife; woman who assists at childbirth

Examples

The noblewoman insisted that only a trusted accoucheuse attend her during childbirth.
In the 18th century, an accoucheuse played a central role in rural communities.
The accoucheuse was summoned urgently when labor began unexpectedly late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwife - a person trained to assist women in childbirth

Etymology

From French 'accoucheuse', feminine of 'accoucheur' (obstetrician), from 'accoucher' (to give birth).
