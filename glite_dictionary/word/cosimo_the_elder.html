Cosimo the Elder

1. PROPN

/ ˈkəʊzɪmoʊ ði ˈɛldər / (AmE), / ˈkɒzɪməʊ ði ˈɛldə / (BrE)
(1389–1464) An Italian financier and statesman who played a key role in the rise of the Medici family and was influential in Florence's political and artistic life.
Italian financier and statesman (1389–1464)

Examples

Cosimo the Elder laid the foundation for Medici power in Florence through his political savvy and patronage.
The art and architecture of Renaissance Florence flourished under Cosimo the Elder's guidance and support.
Historians consider Cosimo the Elder one of the most influential figures in Italian Renaissance history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lorenzo de' Medici - Italian statesman and prominent figure of the Medici family
  • Synonym: Medici - Powerful Florentine family during the Renaissance

Etymology

Cosimo from Italian, meaning 'order' or 'decency'; 'the Elder' to distinguish him from younger family members.
