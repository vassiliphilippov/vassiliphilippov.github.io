downtick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.tɪk / (BrE)
a stock market transaction made at a price lower than that of the previous transaction
stock trade at a lower price than previous

Examples

After a series of downticks, investors grew nervous about the company's performance.
The analyst noted the first downtick of the day in the technology sector.
A sudden downtick in trading can signal shifting market sentiment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uptick - a stock market transaction made at a price higher than the previous transaction

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'tick', referring to a downward move in stock price notation.
