allegretto

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈɡrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌalɪˈɡrɛtəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) a tempo that is moderately quick, faster than andante but slower than allegro.
moderately quick musical tempo

Examples

The composer marked the third movement as allegretto, giving it a sense of lightness and energy.
After the slow introduction, the piece switched to an allegretto tempo.
The orchestra played the melody at an allegretto pace, creating a cheerful atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: andante - a moderately slow musical tempo
  • Degree Higher: allegro - a fast and lively musical tempo

Etymology

From Italian, diminutive of 'allegro' (cheerful, lively).

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: allegro - a fast, lively tempo in music
  • Degree Lower: andante - a moderately slow tempo in music
