fighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who fights, especially for a cause or on behalf of something
person who fights for a cause

Examples

She has always been a fighter for social justice and equal rights.
Despite all the setbacks, he proved himself to be a true fighter.
Many consider her a fighter because of her tireless work on behalf of the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrior - someone who fights in battles, especially in the past
  • Antonym: pacifist - a person who is opposed to war or violence

Etymology

From fight + -er, Old English 'feohtan' (to fight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
A high-speed military aircraft designed to destroy enemy aircraft in the air.
military airplane designed to destroy enemy aircraft

Examples

The air force deployed a fighter to intercept the unidentified aircraft.
A new generation of stealth fighters was unveiled at the air show.
Each fighter is equipped with advanced radar and missile systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bomber - a military aircraft designed to drop bombs
  • Synonym: interceptor - a fighter aircraft designed to intercept and destroy enemy aircraft, especially bombers

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who fights, especially as a profession or in a conflict
a person who fights

Examples

The boxer was a true fighter who never gave up in the ring.
She proved herself to be a fighter during the long and difficult recovery.
As a political activist, he has always been a passionate fighter for justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrior - a person experienced in warfare; a brave or experienced fighter
  • Antonym: pacifist - a person who opposes war or violence

Etymology

From Old English *feohtan* (to fight) plus agent suffix *-er*.
