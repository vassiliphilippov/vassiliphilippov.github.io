longness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being long in spatial extent
quality of being long

Examples

The longness of the rope made it suitable for climbing high trees.
He commented on the longness of her letter, which detailed every event of her trip.
In genetics, longness of limbs can be inherited from one or both parents.

Synset Examples

one gene causes shortness and the other causes longness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: length - the measurement or extent of something from end to end
  • Antonym: shortness - the property of being of little spatial extent

Etymology

From 'long' + '-ness' forming a noun meaning the state of being long.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being long in duration or extent
quality of being long (duration or extent)

Examples

The longness of the movie made it difficult for some viewers to stay focused.
She couldn't endure the longness of the winter nights.
We were surprised by the longness of the journey, which seemed endless at times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: length - distance from end to end or long extent
  • Antonym: shortness - the quality or condition of being short in duration or extent

Etymology

Formed from 'long' + '-ness', a common English suffix forming nouns denoting quality or state.
