temporizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who delays taking action or making a decision in order to gain time or wait for a more advantageous moment
someone who delays to gain time

Examples

The committee accused him of being a temporizer who always avoided making tough decisions.
As a skilled temporizer, she managed to delay the negotiations for weeks.
History remembers him not as a bold leader, but as a temporizer reluctant to take decisive action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastinator - a person who delays or postpones tasks

Etymology

From the verb 'temporize,' based on Latin 'temporizare' (to pass the time), from 'tempus' (time).
