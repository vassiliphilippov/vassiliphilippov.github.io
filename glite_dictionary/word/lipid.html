lipid

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪp.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.ɪd / (BrE)
any of a group of oily organic compounds that are insoluble in water but soluble in organic solvents, and are essential structural components of living cells
fat-like organic substance in cells
medical

Examples

Dietary guidelines recommend limiting saturated lipid intake for heart health.
Cell membranes are primarily composed of lipids and proteins.
The nutritionist explained how lipids serve as an energy reserve in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fat - organic compounds storing energy in animals and plants
  • Degree Lower: protein - large, complex molecules essential for cell structure and function
  • Degree Lower: carbohydrate - organic compound consisting of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, important for energy

Etymology

From Greek lipos (fat).
