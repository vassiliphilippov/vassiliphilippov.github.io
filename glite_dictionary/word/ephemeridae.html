Ephemeridae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌfɛmɚˈɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪˌfiːməˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mayflies comprising short-lived aquatic insects with delicate bodies and long, slender tails.
a family of mayflies (insects)
medical

Examples

The Ephemeridae are known for their extremely short adult lifespan, sometimes lasting only a day.
Scientists collected specimens from the Ephemeridae family to study their life cycle.
Unlike other insects, most species within the Ephemeridae emerge synchronously each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - a flying insect of the family Ephemeridae with a very short adult life

Etymology

From Modern Latin Ephemeridae, from Greek ephemeros (lasting a day) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌfɛməˈraɪdi / (AmE), / ɪˌfiːməˈriːdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic insects within the order Ephemeroptera, commonly known as mayflies, noted for their short adult lifespan.
family of aquatic insects (mayflies)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Ephemeridae family during their survey of the river.
The delicate wings of most Ephemeridae are well-adapted for their brief adult life.
Larval development in Ephemeridae can last much longer than the fleeting adult stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - an aquatic insect of the order Ephemeroptera

Etymology

From New Latin Ephemeridae, based on Greek ephēmeros (lasting a day) + -idae (family suffix).
