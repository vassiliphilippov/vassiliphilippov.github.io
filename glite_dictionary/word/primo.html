primo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriːmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpriːməʊ / (BrE)
the principal part in a duet, especially in a piano duet, typically played by the performer on the right side of the keyboard.
principal part in a piano duet

Examples

During the concert, she played the primo in the piano duet with great energy.
The composer wrote passages of virtuosic runs for the primo.
Assigning the primo to the more experienced pianist helped balance the performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondo - the second or lower part in a musical duet, especially a piano duet

Etymology

From Italian 'primo' meaning 'first'.
