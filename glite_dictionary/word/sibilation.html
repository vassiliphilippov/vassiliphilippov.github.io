sibilation

1. NOUN

/ sɪbəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪbɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of pronouncing with a sibilant (hissing or whistling) sound
pronunciation with a hissing sound

Examples

The excessive sibilation in her speech made her words sound almost musical.
Speech therapists often work with children to reduce unwanted sibilation.
English learners sometimes struggle with the sibilation of certain consonants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sibilant - a speech sound characterized by a hissing quality, such as 's' or 'sh'

Etymology

From Latin sībilātiō (hissing), from sībilāre (to hiss).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪbəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪbɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the production of a hissing or sibilant sound, especially as a fricative in speech or as an expression of disapproval
production of a hissing or sibilant sound

Examples

The sibilation of the crowd grew louder as the speaker continued his unpopular remarks.
She noticed a strong sibilation in his speech, especially when he pronounced the letter 's'.
The audience's sibilation drowned out the music, expressing their clear disappointment.

Synset Examples

the performers could not be heard over the hissing of the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hissing - the act of making a sharp sibilant sound

Etymology

From Latin 'sibilatio' (hissing), from 'sibilare' (to hiss).
