unfeasible

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfiːzəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfiːzɪbəl / (BrE)
not possible to do or achieve; impracticable
not possible or impracticable

Examples

Building a bridge across the canyon was deemed unfeasible due to the terrain.
The proposed policy changes were considered unfeasible without additional funding.
It quickly became clear that their plan was unfeasible with the available resources.

Synset Examples

refloating the sunken ship proved impracticable because of its fragility
a suggested reform that was unfeasible in the prevailing circumstances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infeasible - not possible to do; impossible
  • Antonym: feasible - possible to do or achieve
  • Synonym: impracticable - not able to be put into practice or carried out

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + feasible (able to be done), from Old French faisable and Latin facere (to do, make).
