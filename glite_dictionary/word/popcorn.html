popcorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈpɒpˌkɔːn / (BrE)
a type of corn with small kernels that puff up and burst open when heated, often eaten as a snack
a corn variety that bursts when heated

Examples

The smell of freshly made popcorn filled the movie theater.
She bought a bag of popcorn to snack on during the show.
Farmers grow special varieties of popcorn that are different from regular sweet corn.

Etymology

From 'pop' (imitating the sound) + 'corn' (maize).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈpɒpˌkɔːn / (BrE)
small, hard kernels of corn that burst open and become light and fluffy when heated, often eaten as a snack
exploded corn snack

Examples

We made a big bowl of popcorn to eat during the movie.
The smell of fresh popcorn filled the entire theater lobby.
She prefers her popcorn with just a little salt and butter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn - the plant from which popcorn kernels are obtained
