a.m.

1. ADV

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
Used with clock times to indicate the period between midnight and noon.
before noon; time indicator

Examples

The train is scheduled to arrive at 9:30 a.m..
She usually exercises early in the a.m..
Our appointment is set for 7 a.m. tomorrow.

Synset Examples

let's meet at 11 A.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: p.m. - Used with times to indicate the period from noon to midnight

Etymology

From Latin 'ante meridiem' meaning 'before midday'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
the period of time from midnight to noon, used to specify times in the morning
the time from midnight to noon

Examples

The meeting is scheduled for 10:00 a.m. tomorrow.
She usually gets up at 6 a.m. to go for a run.
Breakfast is served every day from 7 a.m. to 9 a.m. in the hotel dining room.

Synset Examples

Made in the A.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: p.m. - the period of time from noon to midnight, used to specify times in the afternoon and evening

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'ante meridiem', meaning 'before midday'.
