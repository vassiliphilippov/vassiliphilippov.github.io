Amaranthaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˌrænˈθeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌaməˌranˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of herbs and shrubs, including amaranth, found worldwide in various habitats
plant family including amaranth

Examples

The Amaranthaceae family includes both cultivated crops and weedy species found around the world.
Researchers studied drought resistance in several members of the Amaranthaceae.
Many species in Amaranthaceae have colorful flowers and leaves, making them popular in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amaranthus - genus of flowering plants commonly called amaranth

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Amaranthus + -aceae (Latin plural of -acea, indicating plant families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæm.ə.rænˈθeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌam.ə.ranˈθiː.siː / (BrE)
a large, cosmopolitan family of flowering herbs and shrubs, including the amaranths and related plants
family of flowering herbs and shrubs

Examples

Many edible plants, such as spinach and quinoa, belong to the Amaranthaceae family.
The Amaranthaceae are well known for their ability to thrive in arid environments.
Researchers are studying drought resistance in various Amaranthaceae species.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Amaranthus combined with the standard plant family suffix '-aceae'.
