Monongahela

1. PROPN

/ məˌnɑːŋgəˈhiːlə / (AmE), / məˌnɒŋgəˈhiːlə / (BrE)
a river rising in northern West Virginia and flowing north into Pennsylvania, where it joins the Allegheny River at Pittsburgh to form the Ohio River
river in West Virginia and Pennsylvania, USA

Examples

The Monongahela Valley played a key role in the region's industrial history.
Pittsburgh lies at the confluence of the Allegheny and Monongahela Rivers.
The fog rising above the Monongahela made the early morning commute picturesque.

Etymology

From a Native American (Lenape) word meaning 'falling banks.'
