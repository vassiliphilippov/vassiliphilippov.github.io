Hasid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːsɪd, həˈsid / (AmE), / ˈhɑːsɪd, həˈsiːd / (BrE)
a member of a Jewish sect known for its strict observance of Orthodox Judaism and adherence to spiritual and mystical traditions
member of a Jewish Orthodox sect

Examples

The Hasid wore traditional black garb and a wide-brimmed hat.
As a Hasid, he follows the teachings of his Rebbe closely.
Many Hasidim live in Brooklyn, forming vibrant communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidic - relating to Hasidim or their practices

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasid (pious, devout).
