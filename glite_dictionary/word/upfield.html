upfield

1. ADV

/ ˈʌpˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌfiːld / (BrE)
in or toward a position away from the defending team's end of the playing field; nearer to the opponent's goal or end zone.
away from the defending team's end (sports)

Examples

The quarterback threw the ball upfield to the wide receiver.
He sprinted upfield as his team launched a rapid counterattack.
The defender cleared the ball upfield to relieve the pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downfield - toward the defending team's end of the field
