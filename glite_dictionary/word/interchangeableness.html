interchangeableness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈtʃeɪndʒəblnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtʃeɪndʒəb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be exchanged or substituted with something else
ability to be exchanged or substituted

Examples

The interchangeableness of these parts makes maintenance much easier.
Their roles have a certain interchangeableness that allows for flexible scheduling.
The interchangeableness of currencies simplifies international transactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeability - the quality of being interchangeable

Etymology

From 'interchangeable' + '-ness'; 'interchangeable' from Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'cambiare' (to exchange).
