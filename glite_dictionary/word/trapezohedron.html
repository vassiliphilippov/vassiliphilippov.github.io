trapezohedron

1. NOUN

/ trəˌpiːzoʊˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / trəˌpiːzɪəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A polyhedron whose faces are all trapeziums (quadrilaterals with at least one pair of parallel sides).
polyhedron with trapezium faces

Examples

The crystal formed a perfect trapezohedron with eight identical faces.
In geometry class, we studied the unique properties of a trapezohedron.
The museum displayed several minerals shaped like a trapezohedron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyhedron - a solid figure with many plane faces, typically more than six

Etymology

From Greek 'trapezion' (trapezium) and 'hedron' (face, seat).
