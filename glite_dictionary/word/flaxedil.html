Flaxedil

1. NOUN

/ flækˈsɛdɪl / (AmE), / flækˈsɛdɪl / (BrE)
a trade name for gallamine, a neuromuscular blocking agent used as a muscle relaxant during anesthesia
trade name for a muscle relaxant drug
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered Flaxedil to ensure the patient's muscles stayed relaxed during surgery.
Because of its side effects, Flaxedil has largely been replaced by newer muscle relaxants.
Some older textbooks still list Flaxedil as an example of a neuromuscular blocking agent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallamine - a synthetic muscle relaxant used in anesthesia
