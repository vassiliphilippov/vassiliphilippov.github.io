to foist

1. VERB

/ fɔɪst / (AmE), / fɔɪst / (BrE)
to force someone to accept or deal with something unwanted or unpleasant
impose something unwanted

Examples

They tried to foist their outdated technology on unsuspecting customers.
The committee resented having unnecessary tasks foisted upon them by the management.
He attempted to foist his personal beliefs onto the rest of the team.

Synset Examples

He foisted his work on me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impose - to force something unwelcome to be accepted or put in place
  • Synonym: thrust - to push or force something suddenly or violently

Etymology

Possibly from Dutch 'vuisten' (to take in the hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00751098-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fɔɪst / (AmE), / fɔɪst / (BrE)
to introduce or impose something or someone on others stealthily or without justification, especially by deceit or trickery
to impose or introduce surreptitiously

Examples

The company tried to foist inferior goods on unsuspecting customers.
She resented their attempts to foist new rules without proper discussion.
He managed to foist his opinions into every conversation, even when they weren't relevant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impose - to force something unwelcome or unfamiliar to be accepted
  • Synonym: thrust - to push suddenly or forcibly

Etymology

From Dutch 'vuisten' (to take in the hand), related to 'vuist' (fist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00190155-v
Synset POS: v
