heedless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhidləs / (AmE), / ˈhiːdləs / (BrE)
not paying careful attention to something; showing a lack of care or regard.
not paying attention; careless

Examples

He drove through the rain heedless of the slippery roads.
Her heedless remarks hurt his feelings more than she realized.
Many accidents happen because people are heedless of safety warnings.

Synset Examples

We have always known that heedless self-interest was bad morals; we know now that it is bad economics
heedless of danger
heedless of the child's crying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention to something

Etymology

Formed from 'heed' + '-less', meaning 'without attention or care'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhidləs / (AmE), / ˈhiːdləs / (BrE)
showing a lack of care or attention; careless or unconcerned about possible dangers or problems.
careless; unconcerned

Examples

Her heedless comments sometimes hurt people's feelings without her realizing it.
The hikers acted in a heedless manner, wandering off the trail despite the warnings.
Ignoring all advice, he spent his money with heedless abandon.

Synset Examples

the heedless generosity and the spasmodic extravagance of persons used to large fortunes
reckless squandering of public funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought; negligent
  • Synonym: reckless - without regard for danger or consequences
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention; considerate

Etymology

From Old English hēd (heed) + -less (without).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00313654-s
Synset POS: s
