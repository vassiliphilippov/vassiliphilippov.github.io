muff

1. NOUN

/ mʌf / (AmE), / mʌf / (BrE)
a warm, tubular covering for the hands, usually made of fur or other soft material, into which both hands are placed for warmth
a handwarmer tube

Examples

She wore a velvet muff to keep her hands warm on the cold winter day.
In old paintings, you can often see ladies holding a fur muff.
My grandmother gave me her antique muff as a keepsake.

Etymology

From Middle French moufle (mitten), from Old French, of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ mʌf / (AmE), / mʌf / (BrE)
to fail to catch, handle, or hit (a ball) properly, especially in sports
fail to catch or handle (ball)

Examples

The outfielder muffed an easy fly ball in the ninth inning.
She almost caught the basketball but muffed it at the last moment.
He admitted he had muffed the crucial catch that cost his team the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fumble - to mishandle or drop (a ball), especially in sports
  • Antonym: catch - to seize and hold (something, especially a ball) that is moving through the air

3. NOUN

/ mʌf / (AmE), / mʌf / (BrE)
(sports) A dropped ball, especially one that should have been caught, in sports such as baseball or football.
dropped ball in sports
american

Examples

After the muff, the opposing team quickly regained possession of the ball.
Fans groaned when the wide receiver made a crucial muff during the kickoff.

4. VERB

/ mʌf / (AmE), / mʌf / (BrE)
to handle or perform something clumsily; to make a mess of or fail to do something successfully
fail or handle badly
informal

Examples

He completely muffed his lines during the school play.
The goalie muffed the catch, costing his team the match.
You really muffed it this time by sending the email to the wrong person.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: bungle - to do something clumsily or incompetently
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'muffen' (to work roughly), related to German 'muffeln'.
