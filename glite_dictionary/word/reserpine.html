reserpine

1. NOUN

/ rəˈsɝːˌpin / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːpiːn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat high blood pressure, extracted from the roots of the plant Rauwolfia serpentina
antihypertensive drug; plant-derived alkaloid
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed reserpine to help control his high blood pressure.
Researchers are studying the side effects of reserpine in different patient populations.
Reserpine is derived from the root of the Rauwolfia serpentina plant.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Rauwolfia + chemical suffix -ine.
