metaphysical

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
(of an idea, argument, or reasoning) highly abstract or overly theoretical, often lacking practical grounding.
highly abstract or overly theoretical

Examples

Her arguments became increasingly metaphysical and detached from real-world concerns.
The professor's lecture was criticized for being too metaphysical to be useful for new students.
The discussion quickly turned metaphysical, with little connection to practical outcomes.

Synset Examples

metaphysical reasoning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretical - concerned with or involving the theory of a subject rather than its practical application
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory or ideas

Etymology

From Medieval Latin metaphysica (the science of things transcending what is physical).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy that deals with the fundamental nature of reality and existence; of or pertaining to metaphysics.
relating to metaphysics or philosophical theory

Examples

She has a keen interest in metaphysical questions about time and existence.
The novel explores metaphysical themes through its dreamlike narrative.
Some critics argue that poetry can address metaphysical ideas more effectively than prose.

Synset Examples

metaphysical philosophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosophical - relating to the study or knowledge of fundamental nature of reality

Etymology

From medieval Latin metaphysicus, from Greek ta meta ta physika (the works after the Physics, i.e., Aristotle's works after his Physics).

3. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting realities that are beyond what is perceptible to the senses or beyond the laws of nature.
beyond physical or natural laws

Examples

Some philosophers debate the existence of metaphysical forces that lie beyond scientific explanation.
The story explores metaphysical realms where the boundaries of reality and imagination blur.
She was fascinated by metaphysical questions about the nature of existence and consciousness.

Synset Examples

metaphysical forces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supernatural - existing or occurring outside the laws of nature
  • Antonym: physical - relating to material nature or the body

Etymology

From Greek meta- (beyond) + physika (physics, physical things).
