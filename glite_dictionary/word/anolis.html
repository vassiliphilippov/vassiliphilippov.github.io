Anolis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊlɪs / (AmE), / əˈnəʊlɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small, primarily arboreal lizards native to the Americas, commonly called anoles, known for their ability to change color and for their dewlap displays
genus of small American lizards (anoles)

Examples

Many biologists study Anolis lizards to understand adaptation and natural selection.
The Anolis genus includes hundreds of species across the Caribbean and the Americas.
Researchers observed that Anolis lizards can rapidly change color in response to their environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anole - a small, chiefly arboreal lizard of the genus Anolis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on French 'anolis', from Cariban languages of the Caribbean.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊlɪs / (AmE), / əˈnəʊlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized lizards native to the Americas, commonly known as anoles and sometimes referred to as New World chameleons.
a genus of American lizards (anoles)

Examples

The Anolis lizards are noted for their ability to change color and their impressive dewlap displays.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Anolis in different Caribbean islands.
Several new species in the genus Anolis have been discovered in Central and South America.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Anolis, the scientific name given to the genus.
