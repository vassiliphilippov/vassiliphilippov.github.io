squint-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɪnt.aɪd / (AmE), / ˈskwɪnt.aɪd / (BrE)
Having eyes affected by strabismus; having eyes that do not look in the same direction at the same time.
affected by strabismus (misaligned eyes)

Examples

The artist depicted the character as squint-eyed to emphasize his unique appearance.
She felt self-conscious about her squint-eyed look, especially in photographs.
Some people are born squint-eyed and may choose corrective surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-eyed - having eyes that turn inwards toward the nose
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02810620-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ skwɪnt-aɪd / (AmE), / skwɪnt-aɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or look) having the eyes turned partly to one side, often suggesting suspicion, doubt, or envy.
having eyes or a glance turned to the side

Examples

She gave him a squint-eyed glance, clearly doubting his explanation.
The detective watched the suspect with a squint-eyed suspicion.
He shot a squint-eyed look at his rival, not hiding his envy.

Synset Examples

her eyes with their misted askance look
sidelong glances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askance - with a look of suspicion or disapproval
  • Synonym: sidelong - directed to one side, often with suspicion or interest

Etymology

From 'squint' (to look with partly closed eyes) + 'eyed'.
