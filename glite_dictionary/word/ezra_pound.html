Ezra Pound

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛzɹə paʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɛzrə paʊnd / (BrE)
(1885–1972) An American poet and critic who lived in Europe and was a major influence on the development of modernist literature.
American modernist poet and critic

Examples

Ezra Pound played a vital role in establishing the modernist movement in poetry.
Many writers of the twentieth century admired the literary innovations of Ezra Pound.
The influence of Ezra Pound can still be seen in contemporary poetry.
