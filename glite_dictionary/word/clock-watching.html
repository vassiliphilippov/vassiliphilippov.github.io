clock-watching

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑk ˌwɑtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɒk ˌwɒtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of frequently checking the time, especially in anticipation of stopping work or an event ending.
frequently checking the time at work

Examples

Her constant clock-watching made it clear she couldn't wait to go home.
There's a lot of clock-watching on Friday afternoons in this office.
If you find yourself clock-watching, try taking a short break to refocus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timekeeping - the activity of recording or monitoring time
