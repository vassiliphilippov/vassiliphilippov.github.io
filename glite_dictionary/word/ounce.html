ounce

1. NOUN

/ aʊns / (AmE), / aʊns / (BrE)
a unit of weight equal to one sixteenth of a pound, or approximately 28.35 grams
unit of weight (1⁄16 pound)

Examples

The recipe calls for just one ounce of chocolate.
He bought a gold bar weighing ten ounces.
Doctors recommend drinking at least eight ounces of water with each meal.

Etymology

From Old French 'unce', from Latin 'uncia' (a twelfth part, an ounce or inch).

2. NOUN

/ aʊns / (AmE), / aʊns / (BrE)
an old or literary term for the snow leopard, a large wild cat with long, thick, pale fur native to upland regions of central Asia
archaic term for snow leopard
archaic

Examples

The elusive ounce prowled silently along the mountain ledge.
In ancient texts, the ounce is celebrated for its beauty and strength.
The thick fur of the ounce helps it survive the cold Himalayan climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow leopard - a large, pale-furred wild cat native to central Asia

Etymology

From Old French *once*, from Latin *lynx*, influenced by confusion with 'an ounce' (weight).

3. NOUN

/ aʊns / (AmE), / aʊns / (BrE)
A unit of apothecary weight equal to 480 grains or one twelfth of a troy pound, used chiefly in pharmacy and precious metals.
apothecary weight unit (480 grains)

Examples

A goldsmith measured the bar to be exactly one ounce in apothecary weight.
The apothecary dispensed the medicine in doses of half an ounce.
An ounce in this old system is different from the more common ounce used to weigh groceries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ounce - a unit of weight equal to one sixteenth of a pound avoirdupois (approx. 28.35 grams)

Etymology

From Latin 'uncia' (a twelfth part).
