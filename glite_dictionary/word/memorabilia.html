memorabilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛməɹəˈbɪliə / (AmE), / ˌmɛm(ə)rəˈbɪlɪə / (BrE)
objects or items collected because they are associated with memorable people, events, or occasions and are kept as reminders.
items kept as reminders of notable people or events

Examples

He displayed his sports memorabilia in a glass case in the living room.
The museum's collection includes memorabilia from famous historical figures.
Fans eagerly collected movie memorabilia after the premiere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a place or event

Etymology

From Latin *memorabilis* meaning 'memorable'.
