eminently

1. ADV

/ ˈɛm.ɪ.nənt.li / (AmE), / ˈɛm.ɪ.nənt.li / (BrE)
To a notable or outstanding degree; very.
to a notable or outstanding degree

Examples

She is eminently qualified for the position due to her extensive experience.
The results of the project were eminently satisfactory to everyone involved.
His suggestions were eminently sensible and well-received by the committee.

Synset Examples

two subjects on which he was eminently qualified to make an original contribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highly - to a high degree; very
  • Antonym: barely - only just; by a small margin

Etymology

From 'eminent' (from Latin 'eminens', meaning 'standing out') + '-ly'.
