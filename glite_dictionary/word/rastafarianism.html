Rastafarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑːstəˈfɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌrastəˈfɛːrɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A religious movement originating in Jamaica, based on the belief that Haile Selassie I is the Messiah and that Africa, especially Ethiopia, is the promised land for people of African descent.
religious movement venerating Haile Selassie as Messiah

Examples

Many people traveled to Jamaica to learn more about Rastafarianism and its spiritual practices.
The influence of Rastafarianism is evident in reggae music and the writings of Bob Marley.
He became interested in Rastafarianism after reading about Haile Selassie's visit to Jamaica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rastafarian - a follower of Rastafarianism

Etymology

From 'Rastafarian', itself from Ras Tafari, the pre-coronation name of Haile Selassie I of Ethiopia, + -ism.
