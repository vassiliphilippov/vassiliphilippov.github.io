Maleseet

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛˌsiːt / (AmE), / məˈliːsiːt / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Malecite and Passamaquoddy peoples of northeastern North America
Algonquian language of the Malecite and Passamaquoddy

Examples

Few people today speak Maleseet as their first language, making preservation efforts critical.
She is studying Maleseet linguistics at the university.
A new Maleseet dictionary was recently published to support language revitalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malecite - alternative form of Maliseet, also refers to the people
  • Synonym: Passamaquoddy - related Algonquian language and people

Etymology

From Malecite-Passamaquoddy *Wolastoqiyik*, meaning 'people of the beautiful river'.
