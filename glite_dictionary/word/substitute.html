substitute

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
an athlete who plays only when taking the place of another player on the team
athlete who replaces another player

Examples

The coach sent on a substitute after the starting midfielder was injured.
In the second half, she came off the bench as a substitute.
Their best substitute scored the winning goal in extra time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - a person or thing that takes the place of another

Etymology

From Latin substitutus, past participle of substituere (put in place of).
