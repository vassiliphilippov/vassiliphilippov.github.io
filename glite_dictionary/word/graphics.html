graphics

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræfɪks / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪks / (BrE)
photographs, drawings, or other visual representations, especially in a printed or digital publication
visual representations in media

Examples

The graphics in this textbook make the scientific concepts much easier to understand.
She designed the graphics for the marketing brochure.
Modern video games use highly advanced graphics to create realistic worlds.

Synset Examples

the publisher was responsible for all the artwork in the book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - a visual representation used to clarify or decorate text
  • Synonym: artwork - the art or illustrations in a publication or product
  • Antonym: text - written words in a publication, as opposed to images

Etymology

From Greek 'graphikos' (of or for drawing, written), from 'graphein' (to write, draw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræf.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈɡraf.ɪks / (BrE)
the drawings, photographs, diagrams, and other visual elements included in the design or layout of a book, magazine, or similar media
visual elements in a book or layout

Examples

The designer spent hours choosing the right graphics for the children's book.
Magazines often use colorful graphics to attract readers' attention.
The layout combines both text and graphics to make the information clearer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - a picture or diagram that helps explain or decorate a text
  • Antonym: text - the written words in a document or publication

Etymology

From Greek 'graphikos' (of or for drawing or writing), from 'graphein' (to write, draw).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03458929-n
Synset POS: n
