Gempylus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛm.pɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛm.pɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Gempylidae, known for elongated bodies and predatory habits.
genus of marine fish (Gempylidae)

Examples

The deep-sea predator Gempylus is studied for its unique hunting strategies.
Members of the genus Gempylus inhabit tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide.
Researchers identified a new species within the Gempylus genus during the expedition.

Etymology

From Greek *gemphys* (a kind of fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛm.pɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈɡɛm.pɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Gempylidae, serving as the type genus for the family.
a genus of fishes (Gempylidae)

Examples

The Gempylus genus includes species commonly found in deep tropical oceans.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the Gempylus genus.
Members of the Gempylus are characterized by their elongated bodies and predatory habits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gempylidae - the family of fishes to which Gempylus belongs

Etymology

From Greek *gempylos*, meaning 'fat' or 'swollen', used as a fish name.
