James I

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1566–1625) King of England and Ireland from 1603 to 1625, and King of Scotland from 1567 to 1625; the first Stuart king of England, known for his belief in the divine right of kings and for commissioning the King James Bible.
first Stuart king of England and Scotland

Examples

James I succeeded Elizabeth I as king of England in 1603.
The reign of James I marked the beginning of the Stuart dynasty in England.
James I is perhaps best known for commissioning the King James Version of the Bible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: James VI - King of Scotland, later known as James I of England
  • Antonym: Elizabeth I - Predecessor of James I as queen of England
