ponderousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːndərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very heavy or massive; great weight or bulk
great weight or massiveness

Examples

The ponderousness of the ancient stone door made it nearly impossible to open.
She was struck by the ponderousness of the sculpture, which dominated the small room.
Engineers had to account for the ponderousness of the machinery when designing the building's structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of being light in weight

Etymology

From Latin 'ponderosus' (heavy, weighty), from 'pondus' (weight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːndɚəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being dull, heavy, awkward, or lacking grace and lightness, especially in style or manner.
lack of grace or fluency; heaviness

Examples

The ponderousness of the report made it difficult for most readers to remain engaged.
Despite the film's brilliant visuals, its ponderousness made it a challenge to watch.
Her lectures were marked by a ponderousness that often left the audience weary.

Synset Examples

a book so serious that it sometimes subsided into ponderousness
his lectures tend to heaviness and repetition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy or weighty, especially in figurative sense, e.g. style or manner
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of being light, graceful, or easy, often referring to style, tone, or manner

Etymology

From 'ponderous' (from Latin ponderosus, from pondus 'weight') + '-ness'.
