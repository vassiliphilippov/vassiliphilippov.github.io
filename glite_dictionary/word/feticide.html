feticide

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfiːtɪsaɪd / (BrE)
The act of causing the death of a fetus.
the act of killing a fetus
medical legal

Examples

The doctor was charged with feticide after the investigation into the clinic's practices.
Some laws specifically prohibit feticide as a crime distinct from homicide or abortion.
Activists debated whether the new legislation on feticide would impact women's rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infanticide - the act of killing an infant

Etymology

From Latin 'fetus' (offspring) and '-cide' (from Latin 'caedere', to kill).
