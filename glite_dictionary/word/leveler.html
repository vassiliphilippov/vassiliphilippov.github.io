leveler

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvələr / (AmE), / ˈlɛvələ / (BrE)
a person who supports the abolition of social distinctions and advocates for equality among all classes
advocate of abolishing social distinctions

Examples

The leveler believed society should eliminate all class differences.
Many 17th-century English levelers fought for political and social reforms.
She was called a leveler after voicing her radical views on equality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egalitarian - a person who believes in the equality of all people

Etymology

From English 'level' (to make even or equal) + '-er' (agent noun); associated with the 17th-century English political movement 'the Levellers'.
