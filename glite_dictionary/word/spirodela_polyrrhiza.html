Spirodela polyrrhiza

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˌroʊˈdiːlə ˌpɒlɪˈraɪzə / (AmE), / spaɪˌrəʊˈdiːlə ˌpɒlɪˈraɪzə / (BrE)
a widespread species of duckweed, an aquatic floating plant, found in most parts of the world except South America, New Zealand, and some oceanic islands
aquatic floating plant species (duckweed)

Examples

Spirodela polyrrhiza is one of the world's most widely distributed duckweed species.
Researchers studied the rapid growth of Spirodela polyrrhiza in controlled aquatic environments.
After the flood, Spirodela polyrrhiza quickly covered the surface of the local pond.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Spirodela' (the genus) and Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'rhiza' (root).
