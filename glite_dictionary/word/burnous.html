burnous

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈnuːs / (AmE), / bɜːˈnuːs / (BrE)
A long, hooded cloak made of wool, traditionally worn by Arabs and Moors.
long, hooded wool cloak

Examples

He wore a white burnous to protect himself from the harsh desert sun.
The traveler wrapped his burnous tightly against the cold night air.
In the market, many locals were dressed in traditional robes and burnouses.

Etymology

From French 'burnous', from Arabic 'burnus'.
