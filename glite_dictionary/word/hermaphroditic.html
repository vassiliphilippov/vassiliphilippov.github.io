hermaphroditic

1. ADJ

/ hɝːˌmæf.rəˈdɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / hɜːˌmæf.rəˈdɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
Having both male and female reproductive organs in the same individual; specifically, of or relating to monoclinous (bisexual) plants.
having both male and female reproductive organs

Examples

Many flowering plants are hermaphroditic, meaning each flower contains both stamens and pistils.
The hermaphroditic nature of certain algae allows them to self-fertilize.
Some species exhibit a hermaphroditic reproductive system as an adaptation to isolated habitats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dioecious - having male and female reproductive organs in separate individuals (especially in plants)
  • Synonym: monoecious - having both male and female reproductive organs in the same plant or individual

Etymology

From Greek hermaphroditos, referring to Hermaphroditus, a figure in Greek mythology who combined both male and female characteristics.

2. ADJ

/ hɚˌmæf.rəˈdɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / hɜːˌmæf.rəˈdɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) having both male and female reproductive organs.
having both male and female reproductive organs

Examples

Many earthworms are hermaphroditic, meaning each individual can produce both eggs and sperm.
Some fish species exhibit hermaphroditic traits and can change sex during their lives.
The hermaphroditic flowers of the plant produce both pollen and ovules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dioecious - having male and female reproductive organs in separate individuals

Etymology

From Greek Hermaphroditos, a mythical figure combining male and female characteristics.
