Sarcocephalus diderrichii

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːrkoʊˈsɛfələs dɪˌdɛrɪˈkʰiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəʊˈsɛfələs dɪˌdɛrɪˈkʰiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
a large African forest tree that produces strong, hard yellow to golden brown wood, sometimes classified in the genus Sarcocephalus
African forest tree with hard yellow-brown wood

Examples

The timber of the Sarcocephalus diderrichii is highly valued for construction and furniture making.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Sarcocephalus diderrichii from overexploitation in West Africa.
The leaves of Sarcocephalus diderrichii are broad and glossy, making the tree easily recognizable in the wild.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'sarx' (flesh) + 'kephalē' (head), referring to the rounded flower heads; 'diderrichii' honors Diderrich, a botanist.
