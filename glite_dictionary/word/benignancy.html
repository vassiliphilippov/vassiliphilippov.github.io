benignancy

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈnɪɡ.nən.si / (AmE), / bɪˈnɪɡ.nən.si / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, gentle, and benevolent
kind and gentle quality

Examples

Her benignancy inspired everyone in the community to be more caring.
We were struck by the benignancy in his eyes as he greeted us.
The benignancy of the nurse comforted many of her patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benignity - the quality of being kindly and benevolent
  • Antonym: malevolence - the quality of having or showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin benignitas (kindness, benignity), from benignus (kind, friendly).
