spinet

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.ɪt / (BrE)
a small and compact upright piano, typically used in homes and practice spaces
small upright piano

Examples

The music room was dominated by a polished spinet in the corner.
She practiced her scales every morning on the family spinet.
They bought a used spinet for their daughter's piano lessons.

Etymology

From Italian 'spinetta', originally referring to a small harpsichord.

2. NOUN

/ spɪˈnɛt / (AmE), / spɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
a small early type of harpsichord with a single string for each note, typically having a compact, oblong shape
small early harpsichord with one string per note

Examples

The antique spinet in the corner belonged to her great-grandmother.
He learned to play Baroque music on a replica spinet.
Restorers carefully tuned each string on the fragile spinet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpsichord - a keyboard instrument in which the strings are plucked, not struck

Etymology

From Italian 'spinetta', possibly referring to quills ('spina' meaning 'thorn, spine') used to pluck the strings.
