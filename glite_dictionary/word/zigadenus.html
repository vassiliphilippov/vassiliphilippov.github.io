Zigadenus

1. NOUN

/ zɪgəˈdiːnəs / (AmE), / zɪgəˈdiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly North American poisonous plants, commonly called death camas, sometimes classified under the family Melanthiaceae.
genus of North American poisonous plants

Examples

The botanist identified the plant as a member of the Zigadenus genus.
Livestock can become ill if they ingest Zigadenus species, also known as death camas.
Native tribes historically recognized the toxicity of Zigadenus and avoided its bulbs.

Etymology

From Greek 'zigon' (yoke) and 'aden' (gland), referring to the plant's floral structure.

a genus of North American poisonous plants

Examples

Etymology

From Greek 'zigadēn', a kind of plant, plus Latin '-us', a suffix for genus names.
