Siluriformes

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌlʊrəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / sɪˌljʊərɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of freshwater and marine fishes including the catfishes, characterized by their whisker-like barbels and often scaleless bodies
order of fishes including catfishes

Examples

The family of Siluriformes is distinguished by their prominent barbels and bottom-dwelling habits.
Many species within Siluriformes are important in both commercial and recreational fishing.
Biologists study Siluriformes to understand the evolution of freshwater fish diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of fish with barbels around the mouth, belonging to the order Siluriformes

Etymology

From Latin 'Silurus' (a kind of catfish) + Greek 'iformes' (having the form of).
