muscadel

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.əˈdɛl / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kəˈdɛl / (BrE)
A sweet wine made from muscat grapes, especially associated with South Africa.
sweet wine from muscat grapes

Examples

The dessert was paired with a glass of muscadel to enhance the flavors.
She purchased a bottle of muscadel while visiting a South African vineyard.
A sip of chilled muscadel complemented the rich chocolate cake perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscatel - A wine made from muscat grapes, often sweet

Etymology

From Dutch 'muscadel', ultimately from Italian 'moscatello', a diminutive of 'moscato' (muscat grape).
