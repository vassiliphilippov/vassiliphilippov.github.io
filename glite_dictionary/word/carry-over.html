carry-over

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæri ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the accumulated and undivided profits of a corporation remaining after dividends and reserves have been accounted for
undivided corporate profits carried forward

Examples

The company's large carry-over provided a buffer for future investments.
Auditors reviewed the carry-over to ensure all profits were properly accounted for.
A significant carry-over can indicate financial stability within a corporation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæriˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæriˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the transfer or application of a skill or knowledge learned in one situation to another similar situation
transfer of a learned skill to another situation

Examples

The carry-over of reading skills from one language to another can help bilingual children excel.
Athletes often benefit from the carry-over of strength training into improved sports performance.
There's significant carry-over from classroom learning to practical work tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - the application of previously acquired skills or knowledge to new situations
