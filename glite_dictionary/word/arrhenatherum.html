Arrhenatherum

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɛnəˈθiːrəm / (AmE), / əˌrɛnəˈθɪərəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses commonly known as oat grass, native to Eurasia and North Africa, often found in meadows and pastures.
a genus of grasses (oat grass)

Examples

Arrhenatherum is frequently used in the restoration of natural meadows in Europe.
Botanists have identified several new species within the genus Arrhenatherum.
The tall stems of Arrhenatherum make it useful for hay production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oat grass - a common name for various grasses resembling oats, including Arrhenatherum

Etymology

From Greek 'arrhēn' (male) and 'ather' (awn, bristle), referring to the plant's features.
