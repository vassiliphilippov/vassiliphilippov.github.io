Prunus cerasus caproniana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs sərˈeɪsəs ˌkæprəˈnaɪənə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs səˈrɑːsəs ˌkæprəˈnaɪənə / (BrE)
A variety of cultivated sour cherry tree that produces pale red fruit with colorless juice.
variety of sour cherry tree with pale red fruit

Examples

The orchard was planted with Prunus cerasus caproniana to produce cherries for cooking.
She prefers using Prunus cerasus caproniana in her preserves because of its delicate flavor.
The pale red fruit of Prunus cerasus caproniana sets it apart from other cherry varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour cherry - a species of cherry tree known for tart fruit (Prunus cerasus)

Etymology

From Latin: Prunus (plum/genus name), cerasus (cherry), caproniana (varietal or place origin).
