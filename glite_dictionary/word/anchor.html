anchor

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
A heavy object attached to a rope or chain and used to moor a vessel to the sea bottom, preventing it from drifting.
device for mooring vessels

Examples

The ship dropped its anchor as it approached the harbor.
After the storm, they pulled up the heavy anchor to set sail again.
Divers inspected the anchor to ensure it was securely lodged on the seabed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooring - the act of securing a boat by cables or anchors

Etymology

From Latin 'anchora', from Greek 'ankura' meaning 'anchor'.

2. VERB

/ ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to fix or secure something firmly in position, often so that it cannot move
fix securely in place

Examples

They anchored the fence posts in concrete to withstand strong winds.
A heavy statue was anchored to the ground to prevent theft.
She used large stones to anchor the tent in place during the storm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to fasten or make stable
  • Antonym: to detach - to unfasten or disconnect

Etymology

From Old English 'ancor', from Latin 'anchora', from Greek 'ankura' (anchor).

4. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
a person, especially a television reporter, who presents and coordinates a broadcast involving contributions from several correspondents
main presenter of a news broadcast

Examples

She worked for ten years as the anchor of the evening news program.
The anchor introduced live updates from reporters across the country.
After the retirement of the long-time anchor, the station saw a drop in ratings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newsreader - a person who reads the news on TV or radio
  • Degree Lower: correspondent - a journalist who reports from a particular place or on a particular subject

Etymology

Derived from the sense of 'anchor' as something that provides stability, transferred to television journalism in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈaŋkə / (BrE)
a person or thing that provides stability, support, or confidence, especially in difficult situations
person or thing providing support or stability

Examples

She relied on her husband as an anchor during times of crisis.
Faith has been the anchor that kept him steady through all difficulties.
For the team, their captain was the anchor they could always count on.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main source of stability
  • Synonym: linchpin - a vital person or thing that holds something together
  • Synonym: keystone - the central, most important element

Etymology

From Latin ancŏra, from Greek ánkyra (anchor); figurative use developed in English by extension.
