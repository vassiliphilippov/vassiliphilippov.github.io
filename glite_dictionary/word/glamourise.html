to glamourise

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlæmɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmərʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something appear glamorous and attractive, often more so than it actually is
make something appear glamorous
british

Examples

The film tends to glamourise the lives of criminals.
They tried to glamourise their holiday photos with special filters and effects.
Some ads glamourise smoking, ignoring its health risks.

Synset Examples

This new wallpaper really glamorizes the living room!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to glamorize - to make something appear glamorous and attractive (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'glamour' (from Scots, meaning magic or enchantment) + '-ise', British spelling of '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡlæmɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlæməraɪz / (BrE)
To make something seem more attractive, exciting, or appealing than it really is, often by interpreting it in a romantic or idealized way.
make something seem more attractive than it is

Examples

The film tends to glamourise the lives of gangsters, ignoring their violent crimes.
Some advertisements glamourise unhealthy food to attract young customers.
We shouldn't glamourise the idea of working long hours at the expense of our health.

Synset Examples

Don't romanticize this uninteresting and hard work!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something appear more romantic or ideal than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or significant than it is

Etymology

From 'glamour' + '-ise', with alternate American spelling 'glamorize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00694938-v
Synset POS: v
