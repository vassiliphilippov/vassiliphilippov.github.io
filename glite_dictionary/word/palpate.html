to palpate

1. VERB

/ ˈpælpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpælpeɪt / (BrE)
to examine a part of the body by touch, especially to diagnose disease or assess its condition
examine by touch (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor carefully palpated the patient's abdomen for any signs of tenderness.
To check for swollen lymph nodes, the nurse will palpate your neck and jawline.
After the injury, the veterinarian palpated the animal's leg to assess the damage.

Synset Examples

The nurse palpated the patient's stomach
The runner felt her pulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpation - the act of examining by touch, especially in a medical context

Etymology

From Latin palpatus, past participle of palpare (to touch softly).
