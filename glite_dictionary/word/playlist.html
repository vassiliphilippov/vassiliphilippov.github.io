playlist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.lɪst / (BrE)
a list of recorded songs or pieces of music chosen to be played in a particular order, especially by a radio station or on a digital device
a list of songs or music tracks

Examples

I created a new playlist for my morning workout.
The radio DJ announced that the next song on the playlist would be a classic hit.
She shared her favorite playlist with her friends online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setlist - a list of songs performed at a concert

Etymology

From 'play' + 'list', first popularised with radio broadcasting in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.lɪst / (BrE)
a list of musical recordings that are compiled and arranged for playback, broadcast, or performance, often in a specific order
a list of songs or musical recordings

Examples

She created a playlist for her morning run, full of energetic songs.
The radio DJ selected tracks from a carefully curated playlist.
You can share your favorite playlist with friends using the app.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setlist - a list of songs to be performed during a concert

Etymology

Formed from 'play' + 'list' in English; in widespread use since the late 20th century, especially with the rise of digital media.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
