oligodendroglia

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊlɪgoʊdɛnˈdrɑːgliə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪgəʊdɛnˈdrɒglɪə / (BrE)
tissue in the central nervous system composed of glial cells with sheetlike processes that form the myelin sheath around nerve fibers
type of neural tissue forming myelin sheath
medical

Examples

Demyelinating diseases can damage oligodendroglia and disrupt nerve function.
Researchers studied the regeneration of oligodendroglia in spinal cord injuries.
Loss of oligodendroglia leads to significant neurological deficits in multiple sclerosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oligodendrocyte - a type of cell that forms the myelin sheath in the central nervous system

Etymology

From Greek *oligos* (few), *dendron* (tree), and *glia* (glue).
