to reline

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to put new lines on a surface, such as paper or a form, usually for writing or organization
put new lines on a surface

Examples

After many erasures, the teacher decided to reline the paper for clarity.
You may need to reline the form before filling it out again.
The artist prefers to reline her sketchbook pages to keep her writing neat.

Synset Examples

reline the paper

Etymology

From prefix re- (again) + line (to mark with lines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00510430-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈlaɪn / (AmE), / riˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to provide (something) with a new lining
provide with a new lining

Examples

The tailor will reline your jacket to make it feel like new again.
After years of use, we decided to reline the old suitcase to repair the interior.
Many customers ask us to reline their handbags when the original fabric wears out.

Synset Examples

the old fur coat must be relined

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) + 'line' (to cover the inside).
