phenytoin

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪtɔɪn / (AmE), / fəˈnɪtɔɪn / (BrE)
an anticonvulsant drug used to treat and prevent seizures in epilepsy, not classified as a sedative
anticonvulsant drug for epilepsy
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed phenytoin to help control her seizures.
Patients taking phenytoin should have their blood levels monitored regularly.
He experienced side effects after starting phenytoin therapy for epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dilantin - trade name for phenytoin
  • Synonym: anticonvulsant - drug preventing or reducing convulsions

Etymology

From phenyl and hydantoin, reflecting its chemical structure.
