perm

1. VERB

/ pɝːm / (AmE), / pɜːm / (BrE)
to treat (hair) with chemicals so that it remains curly or wavy for a period of time
to chemically curl or wave hair

Examples

She decided to perm her hair for a fresh new look.
The salon is offering discounts for anyone wanting to perm their hair this month.
He regrets letting his sister perm his hair as a prank.

Synset Examples

She perms her hair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to straighten - make hair straight by removing curls or waves

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'permanent wave', a hairstyle popularized in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00039537-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ pɜrm / (AmE), / pɜːm / (BrE)
a hairstyle created by chemically treating and setting the hair to produce permanent waves or curls
hair style with permanent waves or curls

Examples

She decided to get a perm before her high school reunion.
His mother wore her hair in a tight perm throughout the 1980s.
After having a perm, her straight hair became full of bouncy curls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanent wave - hairstyle produced by a chemical process to set curls or waves

Etymology

Shortened from 'permanent wave', referring to the lasting effect of the treatment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05268016-n
Synset POS: n
