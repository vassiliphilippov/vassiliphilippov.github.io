ambulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌambjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of walking or moving about, especially as a medical or therapeutic activity.
the act of walking about
medical

Examples

After surgery, the doctor emphasized the importance of early ambulation to speed up recovery.
Physical therapists often assist patients with ambulation after they have been bedridden.
The nurse recorded the patient's level of ambulation during each hospital shift.

Synset Examples

the hospital encouraged early ambulation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking - the activity of moving on foot at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

From Latin ambulationem, from ambulare (to walk).
