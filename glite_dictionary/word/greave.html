greave

1. NOUN

/ ɡriv / (AmE), / ɡriːv / (BrE)
A piece of armor that protects the leg below the knee.
leg armor below the knee

Examples

The knight fastened his greaves before mounting his horse.
Ancient warriors often wore greaves for protection in battle.
The museum display included a pair of bronze greaves from the Greek era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuirass - body armor covering the torso
  • Synonym: gauntlet - armor covering the hand

Etymology

From Old French *greve* (shin, shin armor), probably of Germanic origin.
