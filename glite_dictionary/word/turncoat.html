turncoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌkəʊt / (BrE)
a person who deserts or betrays their cause, party, or friends to join an opposing side
a person who betrays their side

Examples

During the revolution, he was branded a turncoat by his former allies.
The once-loyal supporter became a turncoat and joined the rival party.
Many viewed her as a turncoat for abandoning her principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays a country or cause by aiding the enemy
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or party

Etymology

Originally refers to a person who reverses their coat to show opposite allegiance in the 16th century.
