Parmesan

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːr.məˈzæn / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.mɪ.zæn / (BrE)
A hard, dry, sharp-flavored Italian cheese, often grated and used in cooking.
type of Italian cheese

Examples

She sprinkled freshly grated Parmesan over her pasta.
The recipe calls for a cup of shredded Parmesan cheese.
I prefer the flavor of Parmesan to other cheeses on pizza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grana padano - a type of hard, Italian cheese similar to Parmesan

Etymology

From Italian Parmigiano, after Parma, a city in Italy where the cheese originated.
