to turbocharge

1. VERB

/ ˈtɝːboʊˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to enhance or accelerate something significantly, especially by adding energy or efficiency
make something much faster or more effective

Examples

The company plans to turbocharge its digital marketing strategy next year.
This new initiative will turbocharge our sales process, making it more efficient.
Adding a dedicated project manager really turbocharged our workflow.

Synset Examples

They plan to turbocharge their marketing strategy next quarter.
The new software update will turbocharge your computer's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supercharge - to increase the power, effectiveness, or intensity of something

Etymology

From 'turbo-' (from Latin 'turbo', meaning 'spinning top' or 'whirlwind') and 'charge', originally referring to fitting a turbocharger to an engine.
