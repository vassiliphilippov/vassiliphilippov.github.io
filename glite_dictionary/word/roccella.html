Roccella

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈsɛlə / (AmE), / rɒˈsɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly fruticose lichens that inhabit rocks, particularly in maritime environments
a genus of maritime rock-dwelling lichens

Examples

The Roccella species are well known for their use in producing natural dyes.
Scientists discovered a thriving colony of Roccella on the coastal cliffs.
Some Roccella lichens are important indicators of clean air in maritime habitats.

Etymology

From Italian 'roccella', used in botanical Latin.
