roccella

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈtʃɛlə / (AmE), / rɒˈtʃɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of lichen used as a source of the dyes archil and litmus
genus of dye-producing lichen

Examples

Roccella has historically been harvested along Mediterranean coasts to produce purple and blue dyes.
The chemical properties of roccella make it valuable for traditional dyeing processes.
Litmus paper owes its color-changing ability to substances derived from roccella.

Etymology

From Italian 'roccella', used for the lichen; ultimately from Latin 'roccella' (a kind of lichen).
