absorbable

1. ADJ

/ əbˈzɔrbəbəl / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being absorbed, especially through the pores or surface of something.
able to be absorbed

Examples

The doctor recommended using absorbable stitches that would dissolve over time.
This sunscreen contains absorbable ingredients that penetrate the skin quickly.
Allergy medications are available in both absorbable tablets and non-absorbable forms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonabsorbable - not capable of being absorbed
  • Synonym: absorbent - having the ability to absorb

Etymology

From absorb + -able, from Latin absorbere (to suck in) and suffix -able (able to be).
