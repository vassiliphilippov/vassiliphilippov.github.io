semblance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmbləns / (AmE), / ˈsɛmbləns / (BrE)
the outward appearance or apparent form of something, especially when the reality is different; a misleading or deceptive appearance.
outward or misleading appearance

Examples

Even after the accident, he tried to maintain a semblance of normalcy.
The negotiations retained only a semblance of order amid the chaos.
Her cheerful smile gave the semblance of happiness, though she was struggling inside.

Synset Examples

Artists use perspective in art to create an illusion of depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief; deceptive appearance
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Old French 'semblance' (appearance), from sembler (to seem), from Latin 'simulare' (to imitate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛm.bləns / (AmE), / ˈsɛm.bləns / (BrE)
the outward appearance or form of something, especially when the reality is different or the appearance is misleading
misleading outward appearance

Examples

The manager's apology had only a semblance of sincerity, leaving many employees unconvinced.
Despite the chaos, she managed to maintain a semblance of calm for her children.
His office gave the semblance of order, but papers were hidden in every drawer.

Synset Examples

he hoped his claims would have a semblance of authenticity
he tried to give his falsehood the gloss of moral sanction
the situation soon took on a different color

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance; a front
  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Old French 'semblance' (appearance, likeness), from Latin 'similantia', from 'similare' (to make like).
