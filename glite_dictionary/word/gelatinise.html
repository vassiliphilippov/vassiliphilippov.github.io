to gelatinise

1. VERB

/ dʒəˈlætəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈlætɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to become gelatinous or to change into a jelly-like substance, especially when heated
become gelatinous or jelly-like
british

Examples

When heated in water, cornflour will gelatinise and thicken the mixture.
During cooking, the rice began to gelatinise, making the grains soft and sticky.
Once the sauce reached a high enough temperature, it started to gelatinise rapidly.

Synset Examples

the starch gelatinized when it was heated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gelatinize - to become gelatinous or to change into a jelly-like substance; American spelling

Etymology

From 'gelatin' + '-ise', formed after French 'gélatiniser' or similar formations.

2. VERB

/ dʒəˈlætənaɪz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈlætɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to convert or be converted into a gelatinous or jelly-like form
make or become gelatinous
british

Examples

When you heat the mixture, the starch will gelatinise and thicken the sauce.
The recipe calls for flour that can gelatinise at a lower temperature for best results.
If left too long on the stove, the pudding may gelatinise and become overly firm.

Synset Examples

hot water will gelatinize starch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gelatinize - to convert or be converted into a gelatinous or jelly-like form (US spelling)

Etymology

From gelatin + -ise, patterned after Latin root 'gelatina' (gelatin).
