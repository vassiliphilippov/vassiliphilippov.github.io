bioterrorism

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈtɛr.əˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈtɛr.ər.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the use of biological agents such as viruses, bacteria, or toxins to intimidate or harm populations, typically for political or ideological purposes
use of biological agents in terrorism
medical

Examples

The government has established special protocols to respond to potential bioterrorism threats.
Experts at the conference discussed the challenges of detecting bioterrorism attacks before they spread widely.
Advances in biotechnology have increased concerns about the risk of bioterrorism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological warfare - the use of toxins or infectious agents as weapons in war

Etymology

Formed from 'bio-' (relating to life or biology) and 'terrorism.'
