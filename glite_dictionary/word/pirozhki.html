pirozhki

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌroʊʒˈki / (AmE), / pɪˌrɒʒˈki / (BrE)
A small Russian pastry filled with meat, cheese, or fruit, and typically baked or fried.
Russian filled pastry (baked or fried)

Examples

She made a batch of pirozhki filled with mushrooms and potatoes for the party.
At the market, you can find freshly baked pirozhki alongside other Russian delicacies.
My grandmother taught me how to fold the dough for pirozhki to keep the filling inside.

Etymology

From Russian пирожки (pirozhki), plural of пирожок (pirozhok, 'little pie').
