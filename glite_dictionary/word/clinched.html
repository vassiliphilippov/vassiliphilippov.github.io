clinched

1. ADJ

/ klɪntʃt / (AmE), / klɪntʃt / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) closed or squeezed tightly together, especially out of tension or determination.
closed or squeezed tightly together

Examples

She spoke through clinched teeth, barely able to hide her anger.
He walked onto the stage with clinched fists, ready for the challenge.
The tension in the room was evident from his clinched jaw.

Synset Examples

a clenched fist
his clenched (or clinched) teeth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clenched - closed or squeezed together tightly (esp. of hands, teeth, etc.)
