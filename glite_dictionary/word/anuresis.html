anuresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.jʊˈriː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌan.juːˈriː.sɪs / (BrE)
inability to pass urine; failure of the body to produce or excrete urine
inability to urinate (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with anuresis after he was unable to urinate for several hours.
Acute anuresis can be a sign of a serious underlying condition.
Emergency treatment was required due to the sudden onset of anuresis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urinary retention - inability to empty the bladder completely
  • Antonym: enuresis - involuntary urination, especially in children at night

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'ourēsis' (urination).
