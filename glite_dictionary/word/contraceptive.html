contraceptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈsɛp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈsɛp.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a device, method, or substance) capable of preventing conception or impregnation.
preventing conception or impregnation
medical

Examples

Doctors often discuss contraceptive options with their patients to ensure safe family planning.
She started using a new contraceptive method after consulting her doctor.
The effectiveness of each contraceptive device may vary depending on correct usage.

Synset Examples

contraceptive devices and medications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraception - the deliberate use of artificial methods to prevent pregnancy
  • Antonym: fertile - able to conceive or impregnate

Etymology

From Latin 'contra-' (against) + 'conceptio' (conception).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.trəˈsɛp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈsɛp.tɪv / (BrE)
A drug, device, or method used to prevent pregnancy.
method or device to prevent pregnancy

Examples

She decided to use a contraceptive to avoid an unplanned pregnancy.
Many young adults are now more aware of the variety of contraceptives available.
The doctor discussed different contraceptive methods with the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth control - the practice of preventing unwanted pregnancies
  • Antonym: fertility treatment - medical treatment to increase the chance of pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin 'contra' (against) + 'concipere' (to conceive).
