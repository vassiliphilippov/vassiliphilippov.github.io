to ransack

1. VERB

/ ˈrænˌsæk / (AmE), / ˈræn.sæk / (BrE)
to search a place or container thoroughly and often violently, typically in order to steal valuable things or cause damage
search violently, often to steal

Examples

Burglars managed to ransack the entire house while the owners were away on holiday.
The rebels ransacked the village, leaving nothing untouched in their search for valuables.
Police believe the suspects ransacked several offices looking for confidential documents.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - to steal goods from a place, typically during a war or riot
  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods using force, especially during war or civil disorder
  • Synonym: to burgle - to enter a building illegally with intent to commit theft

Etymology

From Old Norse *rannsaka* (to search a house, examine), from *rann* (house) + *saka* (to search).

2. VERB

/ ˈræn.sæk / (AmE), / ˈræn.sæk / (BrE)
to search a place thoroughly and often roughly, especially to find something or cause disorder
search thoroughly, often with disorder

Examples

The thieves ransacked the apartment, leaving everything in disarray.
She ransacked her closet searching for the missing necklace before the party.
Police ransacked the warehouse looking for evidence of illegal activity.

Synset Examples

They combed the area for the missing child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to try to find something by looking or otherwise seeking
  • Synonym: comb - to search carefully and systematically

Etymology

From Old Norse 'rannsaka' (to search a house), from 'rann' (house) + 'saka' (to seek).
