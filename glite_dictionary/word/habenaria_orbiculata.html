Habenaria orbiculata

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæbəˈnɛriə ˌɔrbɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / ˌhæbəˈnɪə riːə ˌɔːbɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to North America, characterized by large greenish-white flowers and two rounded basal leaves.
a North American orchid species

Examples

We spotted a rare Habenaria orbiculata on our hike through the Vermont woods.
The rounded leaves of Habenaria orbiculata make it easy to distinguish from other wild orchids.
Botanists often cite Habenaria orbiculata as an indicator of healthy forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus named 'Habenaria' from Latin 'habena' (rein/strap), referring to the shape of the flower parts; 'orbiculata' from Latin 'orbiculatus' (circular), alluding to the leaf shape.
