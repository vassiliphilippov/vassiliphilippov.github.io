Zyrian

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪriən / (AmE), / ˈzɪəriən / (BrE)
The Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Komi people of north-eastern European Russia.
Finno-Ugric language of the Komi people

Examples

The Zyrian language has a rich tradition of oral folklore.
Researchers are documenting endangered dialects of Zyrian in remote villages.
He learned basic Zyrian phrases before traveling to the Komi Republic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Komi - the Finno-Ugric language of the Komi; alternative name for Zyrian

Etymology

From Russian Зырянский (Zyryanskiy), relating to the Zyryans (Komi people).
