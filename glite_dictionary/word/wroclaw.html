Wroclaw

1. PROPN

/ ˈvrɔts.wɑf / (AmE), / ˈvrɒts.waf / (BrE)
a city in southwestern Poland, situated on the Oder River
city in southwestern Poland

Examples

My grandparents were born in Wroclaw before moving to the United States.
The University of Wroclaw is one of the oldest in Central Europe.
Many tourists visit Wroclaw for its beautiful market square and historic architecture.

Etymology

From Polish Wrocław, originally from the name of the Slavic duke Vratislav.
