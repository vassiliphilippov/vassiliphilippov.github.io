Cirsium flodmanii

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrziəm floʊdˈmæni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːziəm flɒdˈmɑːni.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of thistle native to western North America, distinguished by its white woolly leaves.
a thistle species with white woolly leaves

Examples

Cirsium flodmanii is often found in prairies and meadows across western North America.
The white woolly leaves help Cirsium flodmanii conserve moisture in dry environments.
Many pollinators are attracted to the purple flowers of Cirsium flodmanii during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Cirsium' (thistle) and the specific epithet honoring F.F. Flodman, a Swedish-American botanist.
