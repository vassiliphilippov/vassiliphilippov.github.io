seasoned

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːzənd / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənd / (BrE)
having a lot of experience of doing something and therefore knowing how to do it well; rendered competent through trial and experience
experienced and competent

Examples

She is a seasoned journalist who has covered stories around the world.
The company hired a seasoned manager to turn things around.
After many years in the army, he became a seasoned officer.

Synset Examples

a seasoned traveler
veteran steadiness
a veteran officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experienced - having knowledge or skill in a particular field, especially through long involvement
  • Antonym: inexperienced - lacking experience or skill

Etymology

From 'season', meaning to accustom or temper, later referring to people rendered experienced.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːzənd / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənd / (BrE)
(of food, materials, etc.) having been aged or processed, often to improve suitability or quality, especially by being exposed to the elements for a period.
aged or processed for use

Examples

The carpenter prefers to work with seasoned wood because it is less likely to warp.
Only seasoned timber should be used for building furniture to ensure durability.
For the best flavor, the recipe calls for seasoned firewood that has been dried for at least a year.

Synset Examples

seasoned wood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unseasoned - not having been aged or processed; raw or fresh

Etymology

From the verb 'season' (to mature or ripen), ultimately from Old French 'saisir' (to ripen), based on Latin 'satio' (sowing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01498107-a
Synset POS: a
