caterpillar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬ɚˌpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌpɪlə / (BrE)
a large vehicle with continuous tracks instead of wheels, used mainly in construction or farming for moving earth
tracked earth-moving vehicle

Examples

The construction crew used a caterpillar to clear the land before building began.
Farmers often rely on a caterpillar when working on large fields.
A massive caterpillar rumbled past us, flattening the old road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldozer - a powerful tracked vehicle with a broad blade for moving earth

Etymology

Originally a trademark of the Caterpillar Tractor Company; named after the motion resembling a caterpillar's movement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬ɚˌpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkætəˌpɪlə / (BrE)
the wormlike larval stage of a butterfly or moth, often brightly colored and sometimes covered in hairs or spines
larval stage of butterfly or moth

Examples

The caterpillar inched its way along the leaf, eating as it went.
Monarch butterflies begin life as a caterpillar before transforming into their adult form.
We found a brightly colored caterpillar crawling on the garden fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larva - immature form of an animal that undergoes transformation

Etymology

From Middle English 'catyrpel', probably from Old North French 'catepelose' (hairy cat), from 'cat' + 'pelose' (hairy).
