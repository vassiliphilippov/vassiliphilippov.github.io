bolero

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈlɛɹoʊ / (AmE), / bəˈlɛərəʊ / (BrE)
A short, close-fitting jacket, usually worn open at the front and typically by women.
short, close-fitting woman's jacket

Examples

She wore a beautiful lace bolero over her evening dress at the party.
The designer's new collection featured a leather bolero paired with high-waisted trousers.
Adding a knitted bolero can give your outfit a touch of elegance and warmth.

Etymology

From Spanish 'bolero', originally referring to a Spanish dance and the associated costume.

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈlɛroʊ / (AmE), / bəˈlɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a lively Spanish dance in triple time, traditionally performed to guitar and castanets accompaniment
Spanish dance in triple time

Examples

The dancers performed a traditional bolero in colorful costumes during the festival.
She learned the bolero while studying Spanish folk dances.
The graceful movements of the bolero captivated the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamenco - a Spanish art form involving singing, guitar playing, dance, and handclaps

Etymology

From Spanish 'bolero', the name of the dance.

3. NOUN

/ boʊˈlɛɹoʊ / (AmE), / bəˈlɛə.rəʊ / (BrE)
A slow-tempo Latin dance or musical composition characterized by a distinctive rhythm, often played on guitar or with orchestral accompaniment.
slow Latin dance/music

Examples

The orchestra performed a passionate bolero that captivated the entire audience.
He composed a bolero which became a classic in Latin music.
Many dancers practiced the bolero before the festival began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhumba - a ballroom dance or music with a lively rhythm of Afro-Cuban origin

Etymology

From Spanish 'bolero', originally denoting the dance form.
