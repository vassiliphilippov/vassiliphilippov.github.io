agreeableness

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡriː.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / əˈɡriː.ə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant or enjoyable, often due to agreeable conditions or surroundings
pleasant or enjoyable quality

Examples

The agreeableness of the weather made their picnic especially enjoyable.
Her agreeableness contributed greatly to the friendly atmosphere in the office.
Many guests commented on the agreeableness of the hotel's atmosphere and staff.

Synset Examples

a well trained staff saw to the agreeableness of our accommodations
he discovered the amenities of reading at an early age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasantness - the state of being pleasant
  • Antonym: disagreeableness - the quality of being unpleasant, opposite of agreeableness

Etymology

From Middle French *agréable* (pleasant) + English suffix *-ness* (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡriəbəlnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡriːəblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant, friendly, and easy to get along with; a personality trait characterized by kindness, cooperativeness, and warmth
quality of being pleasant and friendly

Examples

Her agreeableness made her a popular member of the team.
Employers often value agreeableness when hiring for customer service positions.
While his agreeableness helped him make friends, he sometimes found it hard to say no.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly and kind
  • Antonym: disagreeableness - the quality of being unpleasant or difficult to get along with

Etymology

From 'agreeable' (pleasant), with the suffix '-ness' denoting a state or quality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04648284-n
Synset POS: n
