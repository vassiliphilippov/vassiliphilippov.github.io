demobilization

1. NOUN

/ diˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transitioning from a state of war to peace, including the disbanding of armed forces and conversion of resources to civilian purposes
transition from war to peace, disbanding troops

Examples

The government's demobilization plan aimed to reintegrate soldiers into civilian life quickly.
After the war ended, demobilization of military equipment began across several countries.
The rapid demobilization of factories led to a sharp rise in unemployment.

Synset Examples

demobilization of factories
immediate demobilization of the reserves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mobilization - the act of preparing for war or other emergencies by organizing troops and resources

Etymology

From French démobilisation, from dé- (reversal) + mobilisation (mobilization).
