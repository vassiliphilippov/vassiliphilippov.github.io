Tunica-Biloxi

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːnɪkə bəˈlɑːksi / (AmE), / ˌtjuːnɪkə bɪˈlɒksi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from southeastern Mississippi and Louisiana, or relating to their culture or language
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Tunica-Biloxi have worked to preserve their language and cultural traditions.
A Tunica-Biloxi delegation attended the powwow to represent their tribe.
She is proud to be a descendant of the Tunica-Biloxi people.

Etymology

From the names of the Tunica and Biloxi tribes, now united as a federally recognized nation.
