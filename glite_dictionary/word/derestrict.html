to derestrict

1. VERB

/ ˌdiː.rɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˌdiː.rɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to remove limits or restrictions from something, making it free from previous constraints
remove restrictions or limits

Examples

The government decided to derestrict the use of certain medications for public health reasons.
Some models allow you to derestrict the engine and increase its power output.
After years of regulation, authorities voted to derestrict the fishing zones in the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to restrict - to limit or put controls on something
  • Synonym: unrestrict - to make something unrestricted or without limits; less common variant

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'de-' (indicating removal) and 'restrict'; modeled after similar verbs such as 'decontrol'.
