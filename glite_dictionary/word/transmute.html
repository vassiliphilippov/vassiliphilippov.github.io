to transmute

1. VERB

/ trænˈsmjut / (AmE), / trænzˈmjuːt / (BrE)
to change the form, nature, or substance of something, especially into a different or more refined state
change into a different form or nature

Examples

Ancient alchemists tried to transmute lead into gold through secret formulas.
Exposure to intense heat can transmute some minerals into entirely new substances.
Over time, the artist's experiences transmuted sorrow into creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form, appearance, or structure
  • Synonym: convert - to change into a different form or purpose

Etymology

From Latin transmutare (to change across), from trans- (across) + mutare (to change).

2. VERB

/ trænzˈmjut / (AmE), / trænzˈmjuːt / (BrE)
to change, or cause to change, in form, nature, or appearance
change form or nature

Examples

Over time, intense heat and pressure can transmute coal into diamonds.
The novel explores how suffering can transmute anger into compassion.
Legends tell of wizards who could transmute common metals into gold.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form, appearance, or structure
  • Synonym: to metamorphose - to change completely in form or nature

Etymology

From Latin transmutare (‘to change’), from trans- (‘across’) + mutare (‘to change’).

Examples

Over time, hardship may transmute into wisdom.

  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or purpose
