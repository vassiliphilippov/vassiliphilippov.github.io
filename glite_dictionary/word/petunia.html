petunia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtuniə / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuːniə / (BrE)
a plant of the nightshade family, native to South America, widely cultivated for its colorful funnel-shaped flowers
a flowering plant with colorful blooms

Examples

The petunia is a popular choice for gardeners because of its long-lasting blooms.
She planted pink and purple petunias along the front walkway.
A single petunia added a splash of color to the small balcony garden.

Etymology

From French pétunia, from Tupi petynua (tobacco plant), as petunias are related to tobacco.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtuniə / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuːniə / (BrE)
a plant of a South American genus with funnel-shaped brightly colored flowers commonly cultivated as garden ornamentals
a garden plant with funnel-shaped flowers

Examples

The petunia blooms add a splash of color to the front yard.
She planted pink and purple petunias along the garden path.
After watering, the petunia seemed to perk up quickly in the sunlight.

Etymology

From French *pétunia*, from obsolete Portuguese *petum*, from Tupi *petɨma* (tobacco, as petunias are related to tobacco).
