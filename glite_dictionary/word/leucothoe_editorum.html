Leucothoe editorum

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkɑːθəʊ ˌɛdɪˈtɔːrəm / (AmE), / ljuːˈkɒθiː ˌɛdɪˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A fast-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by arching interlaced branches and clusters of white flowers.
evergreen shrub of southeastern US

Examples

The Leucothoe editorum thrives in shaded woodland gardens, adding year-round greenery and delicate white blossoms in spring.
Gardeners often plant Leucothoe editorum as an ornamental ground cover due to its attractive foliage and cascading branches.
Botanists study Leucothoe editorum to understand more about native southeastern U.S. flora and their ecological roles.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek mythology, Leucothoe, combined with Latin 'editorum', a species epithet.
