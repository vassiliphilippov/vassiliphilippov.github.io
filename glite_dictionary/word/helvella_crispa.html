Helvella crispa

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈvɛlə ˈkrɪspə / (AmE), / hɛlˈvɛlə ˈkrɪspə / (BrE)
a species of fungus with a saddle-shaped, creamy-colored cap and a fluted, pitted stalk, commonly found in woodland areas
a species of saddle-shaped fungus

Examples

The distinctive cap of the Helvella crispa makes it easy to identify in the forest.
After the rain, several Helvella crispa appeared along the wooded path.
Many foragers admire the unusual appearance of the Helvella crispa mushroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle fungus - a fungus with a saddle-shaped cap (Helvella species)

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Helvella' and species epithet 'crispa' meaning 'curled' in Latin.
