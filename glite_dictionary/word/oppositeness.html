oppositeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːpəsɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈɒpəzɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being opposite or in opposition; the relation between two things that are opposed to each other
the quality of being opposite or opposed

Examples

The oppositeness of their opinions led to a heated debate.
Children are often fascinated by the oppositeness of day and night.
In philosophy, oppositeness is essential to understanding concepts like good and evil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposition - the state of being in conflict or disagreement
  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike

Etymology

From 'opposite' + '-ness', forming a noun from the adjective.
