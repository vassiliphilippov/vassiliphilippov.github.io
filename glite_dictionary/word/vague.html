vague

1. ADJ

/ veɪɡ / (AmE), / veɪɡ / (BrE)
not clearly defined, described, or marked; lacking precision or distinctness
unclearly defined or indistinct

Examples

Her explanation for missing the meeting was rather vague and left us confused.
He gave only a vague description of the suspect to the police.
There was a vague sense of familiarity about the old house.

Synset Examples

an undefined term
undefined authority
some undefined sense of excitement
vague feelings of sadness
a vague uneasiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unclear - not easily understood or definite
  • Antonym: definite - clearly defined, precise, certain

Etymology

From Latin 'vagus' (wandering, indefinite).

2. ADJ

/ veɪɡ / (AmE), / veɪɡ / (BrE)
not clearly or fully expressed, described, or understood; lacking definite shape or meaning.
not clearly expressed or understood

Examples

Her explanation was so vague that nobody understood what she meant.
He gave a vague answer when asked about his plans for the weekend.
Memories of that day are now just a vague blur in my mind.

Synset Examples
