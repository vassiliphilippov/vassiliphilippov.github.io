uptown

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌp.taʊn / (BrE)
The residential part of a city or town, typically located away from the central business district.
residential area of a city away from downtown

Examples

They moved to uptown to enjoy a quieter environment.
Property values in uptown have risen significantly over the past decade.
Many people prefer living uptown rather than in the bustling city center.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downtown - the main commercial area of a city or town

Etymology

From the phrase 'up town,' originally referring to the higher or northern sections of a city.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌtaʊn / (BrE)
Located in or relating to the upper part of a town or city, often considered more fashionable or residential.
in or of the upper part of a town

Examples

They live in an uptown neighborhood known for its tree-lined streets and historic homes.
The uptown shopping district attracts people from all over the city.
Many artists moved into the uptown area as rents in the city center increased.
