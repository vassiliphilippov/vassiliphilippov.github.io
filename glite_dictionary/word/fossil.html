fossil

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒsəl / (BrE)
(informal) A person whose habits or style are considered old-fashioned or outdated.
person with outdated style
informal

Examples

He's a bit of a fossil when it comes to technology, still using a flip phone.
The company was run by a group of fossils unwilling to embrace new trends.
My niece called me a fossil for wearing clothes from the 1990s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - a person who is old-fashioned or outdated
  • Antonym: modern - person with up-to-date style or habits

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒsəl / (BrE)
the remains or impression of a prehistoric plant or animal that have been preserved in rock and excavated from the earth
preserved remains of ancient life

Examples

The museum displays a fossil of a dinosaur found in the desert.
She found a fossil embedded in the limestone while hiking.
Paleontologists study fossils to learn about ancient life on Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrifaction - the process of turning organic material into stone
  • Synonym: to fossilize - to become or make into a fossil

Etymology

From Latin 'fossilis' (dug up), from 'fodere' (to dig).
