geezerhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡiːzɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡiːzəhʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being an old man, especially in a humorous or informal way
the state of being an old man
informal

Examples

He joked that he was sliding gracefully into geezerhood after his sixtieth birthday.
Retirement brought new adventures, but geezerhood also meant slowing down a little.
With white hair and playful wisdom, he embraced his geezerhood with a smile.

Synset Examples

old age is not for sissies

on the brink of geezerhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old age - the later period of life when a person is elderly

Etymology

From 'geezer' (informal term for an old man) + '-hood' (denoting state or condition).
