piratical

1. ADJ

/ paɪˈrætɪkəl / (AmE), / paɪˈrætɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, characteristic of, or resembling pirates or their actions
characteristic of pirates

Examples

The navy set out to stop the piratical raids on coastal villages.
His piratical grin and eye patch made him look like a villain in an old movie.
The novel is filled with tales of piratical adventures on the high seas.

Synset Examples

piratical attackers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea

Etymology

From Medieval Latin piraticus, from Latin pirata (pirate).

2. ADJ

/ paɪˈrætɪkəl / (AmE), / paɪˈrætɪkəl / (BrE)
typical of or relating to pirates or piracy, especially in being unlawful or aggressive
characteristic of or relating to piracy

Examples

The publisher took legal action against the piratical reproduction of their novel.
His piratical exploits made him the most feared captain along the coast.
She viewed the unauthorized use of her design as a piratical act.

Synset Examples

piratical editions of my book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - a person who commits piracy
  • Antonym: lawful - conforming to the law; not piratical

Etymology

From Latin piraticus, from pirata (pirate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03111641-a
Synset POS: a
