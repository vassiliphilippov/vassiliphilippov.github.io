Symphalangus

1. NOUN

/ sɪm.fəˈlæŋ.ɡəs / (AmE), / sɪm.fəˈlæŋ.ɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of primates in the family Hylobatidae, comprising the siamangs.
genus of primates (siamangs)

Examples

The Symphalangus genus is recognized for its distinctive throat sac, used in vocal communication.
Many biologists study Symphalangus to understand the evolution of gibbons.
A Symphalangus can be identified by its larger size compared to other gibbons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siamang - a large gibbon native to Southeast Asia, species in the genus Symphalangus
  • Synonym: Hylobatidae - the family of apes that includes gibbons and siamangs

Etymology

From Greek 'symphalangos', meaning 'with joined phalanges', referencing the fused fingers of the siamang.

2. NOUN

/ sɪm.fəˈlæŋ.ɡəs / (AmE), / sɪm.fəˈlæŋ.ɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of gibbons that includes the siamang, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of gibbons including the siamang

Examples

The siamang is classified in the genus Symphalangus.
Zoologists study Symphalangus to understand gibbon evolution.
There is only one species in the genus Symphalangus, the siamang.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siamang - a large gibbon native to Malaysia and Indonesia
  • Degree Higher: gibbon - a type of small ape of the family Hylobatidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'syn-' (together) and 'phalangos' (of the phalanges/fingers), referring to the fused fingers characteristic of siamangs.
