to monopolize

1. VERB

/ məˈnɑː.pə.laɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒp.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to acquire or maintain exclusive control over a commodity, market, or resource
to have exclusive control over something

Examples

OPEC has often tried to monopolize the oil market.
Large companies sometimes monopolize the technology sector, leaving little room for competition.
He tends to monopolize the conversation, rarely letting others speak.

Synset Examples

OPEC wants to monopolize oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to have power over something
  • Antonym: share - to have or use something jointly with others

Etymology

From French *monopoliser*, from *monopole* (monopoly), itself from Late Latin *monopolium*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02209024-v

2. VERB

/ məˈnɑː.pə.laɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒp.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to have or take complete control or possession of something, so that others cannot share it or use it
have exclusive control over something

Examples

The large corporation tried to monopolize the entire telecommunications market.
During meetings, he tends to monopolize the conversation and not let others speak.
After acquiring two competitors, the company monopolized the local grocery business.

Synset Examples

He monopolizes the laser printer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have a commanding influence or control over; synonym of monopolize
  • Antonym: share - to have a portion of something with others; antonym of monopolize

Etymology

From French 'monopoliser', based on 'monopole' (monopoly), ultimately from Greek 'monos' (alone) + 'polein' (to sell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02447411-v
Synset POS: v
