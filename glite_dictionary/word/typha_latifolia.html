Typha latifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪfə læˈtɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈtɪfə læˈtɪfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tall marsh plant, also known as the common cattail, with cylindrical seed heads that release fluffy down when mature and long flat leaves used for weaving mats and chair seats, found in North America, Europe, Asia, and North Africa.
common cattail marsh plant species

Examples

The wetlands were filled with Typha latifolia, their brown seed heads swaying in the breeze.
Her grandmother wove baskets from the leaves of Typha latifolia gathered by the river.
In late summer, the Typha latifolia released its down, fluffing the edges of the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - marsh plant of the Typha genus with characteristic brown seed spikes

Etymology

From genus Typha (Latin for 'cattail') and Latin latifolia (broad-leaved).
