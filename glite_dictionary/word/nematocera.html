Nematocera

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛməˈtɑsərə / (AmE), / ˌnɛməˈtɒsərə / (BrE)
A suborder of slender, long-antennaed flies including mosquitoes, gnats, crane flies, and related insects.
suborder of slender, long-antennaed flies

Examples

The Nematocera suborder includes well-known insects like mosquitoes and gnats.
Researchers study the life cycles of Nematocera to better understand disease transmission.
Adult Nematocera are often characterized by their long, delicate legs and antennae.

Etymology

From Greek nēma (thread) + keras (horn), referring to the long, thread-like antennae.
