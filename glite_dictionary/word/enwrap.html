to enwrap

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈræp / (AmE), / ɪnˈrap / (BrE)
to cover, enfold, or surround (something) completely
cover or surround completely

Examples

A thick blanket of snow enwrapped the quiet village.
She allowed the warm shawl to enwrap her shoulders on the cold evening.
Mystery seemed to enwrap the ancient castle at night.

Synset Examples

Fog enveloped the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to envelop - to surround completely
  • Antonym: to unwrap - to remove a covering from

Etymology

From en- (a prefix meaning 'put in' or 'on') + wrap (from Old English 'wrappian').
