desperately

1. ADV

/ ˈdɛspɚətli / (AmE), / ˈdɛspərətli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or involves great urgency or a strong need.
with great urgency

Examples

The city desperately needs more affordable housing for its residents.
She desperately searched for her lost keys before leaving for work.
During the drought, farmers desperately awaited the arrival of rain.

Synset Examples

health care reform is needed urgently
the soil desperately needed potash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgently - in a way that requires immediate action or attention

Etymology

From Latin desperatus, past participle of desperare (to despair), via Old French desesperé, Middle English desperat.

2. ADV

/ ˈdɛspərətli / (AmE), / ˈdɛspərətli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or involves intense despair, urgent need, or extreme effort.
with intense despair or urgent need

Examples

She desperately tried to find her lost keys before the rain started.
The refugees desperately needed food and shelter after the storm.
He desperately wished for another chance to make things right.

Synset Examples

the child clung desperately to her mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgently - with urgent need or insistence

Etymology

From desperate + -ly.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
