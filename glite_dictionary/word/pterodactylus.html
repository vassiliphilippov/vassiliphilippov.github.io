Pterodactylus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / ˌtɛərəˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct flying reptiles of the Late Jurassic period, known for their long, slender jaws and wings, and one of the first pterosaurs discovered.
genus of extinct flying reptiles

Examples

The first complete Pterodactylus fossil was discovered in Bavaria in the late eighteenth century.
Scientists believe Pterodactylus lived near lagoons and fed mainly on fish.
The wingspan of Pterodactylus was relatively small compared to some later pterosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pterosaur - any flying reptile of the order Pterosauria

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek pteron (wing) + daktylos (finger).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈdæktələs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct flying reptiles (pterosaurs) from the Jurassic period, characterized by a long beak and membranous wings.
genus of Jurassic flying reptiles

Examples

Fossils of Pterodactylus have been discovered in limestone deposits in Germany.
The distinctive wings of Pterodactylus were supported by an elongated fourth finger.
In her report, Emily explained how Pterodactylus lived during the late Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pterosaur - any extinct flying reptile of the order Pterosauria

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + daktylos (finger), referring to the wing supported by one long finger.
