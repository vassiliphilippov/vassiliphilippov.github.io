co-option

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑːpʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
the selection of a new member to a committee, board, or group by decision of the existing members, often through a vote.
selection of a new member by existing group

Examples

The club used co-option to fill the vacant seat on its board.
Several committee vacancies were filled through co-option rather than by open election.
Some organizations allow for co-option when an urgent replacement is required.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: election - the formal process of choosing someone by vote

Etymology

From Latin 'cooptatio,' from 'cooptare' (to choose together).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
the act of appointing someone to a committee, board, or group, often without a full election or sometimes without the appointee's explicit consent.
the act of appointing summarily

Examples

The committee secured her membership through co-option rather than an open election.
Critics argued that co-option undermined the democratic process by bypassing a vote.
He refused the co-option onto the board, preferring to be elected by his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'cooptatio', from 'cooptare' (to choose together, elect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00165820-n
Synset POS: n
