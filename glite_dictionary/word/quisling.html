quisling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪz.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪz.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who collaborates with an enemy occupying force; a traitor to one's country.
collaborator with an occupying enemy

Examples

He was branded a quisling for cooperating with the occupying regime.
During the war, several quislings provided information to the enemy.
History remembers him as the ultimate quisling, betraying his nation for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays their country by aiding an enemy
  • Antonym: patriot - a person who loyally supports and defends their country

Etymology

From the surname of Vidkun Quisling, a Norwegian politician who collaborated with Nazi Germany during World War II.
