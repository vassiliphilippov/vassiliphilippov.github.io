Palladianism

1. NOUN

/ pəˈleɪdiənɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈleɪdɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an architectural style based on the principles and designs of Andrea Palladio, characterized by symmetry, classical forms, and proportion.
architectural style inspired by Palladio

Examples

The grand estate was designed in the style of Palladianism, with its harmonious lines and classical proportions.
Many eighteenth-century British country houses reflect the influence of Palladianism.
His research focuses on the spread of Palladianism across Europe and its adaptation in various local contexts.

Synset Examples

Richard Boyle, 3rd Earl of Burlington was an architect and enthusiastic promoter of Palladianism and was influential in establishing it as a new national style.

Etymology

From the name of Italian architect Andrea Palladio (1508–1580) plus the suffix -ism.
