to out-Herod

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈhɛr.əd / (AmE), / aʊtˈhɛr.əd / (BrE)
to surpass someone in cruelty or wickedness; to go beyond in acting violently or ruthlessly
surpass in cruelty or evil
archaic

Examples

In his rage, the tyrant sought to out-Herod every story he had heard of cruelty.
Some modern films tend to out-Herod Herod in their depiction of violence.
Determined to out-Herod his rivals, the warlord unleashed unprecedented brutality upon the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Synonym: to surpass - to exceed or go beyond
  • Synonym: to outshine - to surpass in excellence or achievement

Etymology

From the character Herod in medieval mystery plays, famous for his excessive cruelty; popularized by Shakespeare's 'to out-Herod Herod' in Hamlet.
