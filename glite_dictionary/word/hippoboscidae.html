Hippoboscidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈbɒsəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈbɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of bloodsucking flies, some of which are wingless, known commonly as louse flies and found on birds and mammals
a family of bloodsucking louse flies

Examples

Hippoboscidae are commonly found on sheep, birds, or other mammals, where they survive by feeding on blood.
Some members of the Hippoboscidae family have lost their wings through adaptation to a parasitic lifestyle.
Veterinary scientists often study Hippoboscidae to understand the diseases they can transmit to livestock.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'boskō' (to feed), referring to their tendency to feed on large mammals.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈbɒskɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈbɒskɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of dipteran insects commonly known as louse flies, many of which are parasitic on birds and mammals and can be winged or wingless.
a family of parasitic louse flies

Examples

Many species within the Hippoboscidae family are known to infest birds and cause discomfort.
The Hippoboscidae can be found worldwide, often living as parasites on livestock.
Researchers are studying the Hippoboscidae to better understand host-parasite relationships in nature.

Etymology

From the genus name Hippobosca, from Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'boskein' (to feed), relating to their parasitic nature on horses and other animals.
