quartet

1. NOUN

/ kwɔrˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɔːˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of four people regarded as a single unit, especially for performing music or working together.
group of four people

Examples

She sang soprano in the school's award-winning quartet.
The string quartet performed at the wedding with exquisite harmony.
Each member of the quartet contributed their unique style to the group's dynamic.

Synset Examples

he joined a barbershop quartet
the foursome teed off before 9 a.m.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trio - group of three people considered as a unit
  • Degree Higher: quintet - group of five people considered as a unit

Etymology

From Italian 'quartetto', from 'quarto' (fourth).

2. NOUN

/ kwɔrˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɔːˈtɛt / (BrE)
a musical composition written for four performers or instruments
musical piece for four performers

Examples

Beethoven's late string quartets are considered masterpieces of chamber music.
The ensemble performed a modern quartet that challenged traditional harmonies.
She composed a quartet for piano, violin, viola, and cello.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duet - a musical composition for two performers
  • Degree Lower: trio - a musical composition for three performers
  • Degree Higher: quintet - a musical composition for five performers

Etymology

From Italian 'quartetto,' diminutive of 'quarto' (fourth), from Latin 'quartus' (fourth).
