maiden

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdn / (BrE)
(in cricket) an over in which no runs are scored by the batting team.
cricket: over with no runs scored
british

Examples

The bowler managed to deliver a maiden in his first over, increasing pressure on the batsmen.
Spin bowlers often aim to bowl a maiden to frustrate the opposition.
He celebrated after bowling his first maiden in international cricket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maiden over - an over in cricket in which no runs are scored

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'maiden' meaning 'untouched; unused,' reflecting that no runs have been scored.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdn / (BrE)
an unmarried young woman, especially one who has never had sexual intercourse; often used in literary or old-fashioned contexts
unmarried young woman; virgin
archaic

Examples

The knight vowed to protect the helpless maiden from harm.
In ancient tales, a maiden often symbolizes innocence and purity.
She was regarded as the fairest maiden in the entire village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virgin - a person who has never had sexual intercourse
  • Degree Higher: woman - an adult female human
  • Degree Higher: bride - a woman on her wedding day or just before and after the event

Etymology

From Old English mæġden, diminutive of mæġd (maiden, virgin, girl).
