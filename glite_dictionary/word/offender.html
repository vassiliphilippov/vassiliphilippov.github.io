offender

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛndər / (AmE), / əˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a person who has committed a crime or broken a law
person who commits an offense

Examples

The police are searching for the offender responsible for the recent burglaries.
First-time offenders may receive a lighter sentence than repeat criminals.
The new law aims to rehabilitate young offenders rather than punish them severely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed by the actions of others

Etymology

From Latin 'offendere' (to strike against, to commit an offense).
