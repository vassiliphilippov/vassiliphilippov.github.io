blizzard

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪz.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈblɪz.əd / (BrE)
A severe snowstorm with strong winds and low visibility.
severe snowstorm with strong winds

Examples

The city shut down as a blizzard dumped two feet of snow overnight.
We were stranded on the highway during a fierce blizzard in January.
Schools were closed because a blizzard was expected to hit the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowstorm - a storm with heavy snowfall

Etymology

Probably of American origin, first recorded in the 19th century in the United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈblɪzəd / (BrE)
A sudden large amount or overwhelming outburst of things, especially unpleasant or difficult events, happening all at once.
sudden overwhelming outburst

Examples

The company faced a blizzard of criticism after announcing the policy change.
We've received a blizzard of emails about the upcoming event.
The manager is trying to cope with a blizzard of last-minute requests.

Synset Examples

a rash of bank robberies
a blizzard of lawsuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deluge - an overwhelming flood of things or events
  • Synonym: flood - a large amount of things that come suddenly
