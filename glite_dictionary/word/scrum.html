scrum

1. NOUN

/ skrʌm / (AmE), / skrʌm / (BrE)
In rugby, a method of restarting play in which the forwards of each team bind together and push against each other while the ball is thrown in between them to contest possession.
rugby method of restarting play with forwards pushing

Examples

The referee called for a scrum after the ball was knocked on.
A powerful scrum can give a rugby team a big advantage in regaining possession.
England's forwards dominated every scrum during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrummage - alternative term for scrum in rugby

Etymology

Originally from English dialect 'scroom' or 'scrum', meaning a confused throng, probably a shortening of 'scrummage', an alteration of 'scrimmage'.
