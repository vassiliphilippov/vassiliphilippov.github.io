Mustelidae

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛləˌdi / (AmE), / mʌˈstɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
the family of carnivorous mammals that includes weasels, otters, badgers, skunks, wolverines, and similar animals
family of carnivorous mammals (weasels, otters, etc.)

Examples

Members of the Mustelidae family include otters, ferrets, and wolverines.
Scientists have studied the behavior of Mustelidae across different continents.
The Mustelidae are known for their elongated bodies and short legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weasel - small carnivorous mammal of the Mustelidae family
  • Synonym: badger - burrowing carnivorous mammal of the Mustelidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'mustela' meaning 'weasel'.

2. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛləˌdi / (AmE), / mʌˈstɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of carnivorous mammals including weasels, otters, badgers, wolverines, and related animals
family of carnivorous mammals (weasel family)

Examples

The Mustelidae family includes animals such as weasels and otters.
Badgers and wolverines are both part of the Mustelidae family.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Mustelidae in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustelid - a mammal belonging to the Mustelidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Mustelidae, from Latin mustela (weasel).
