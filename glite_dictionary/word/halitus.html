halitus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhælɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈhælɪtəs / (BrE)
A breath or vapor, especially when exhaled; exhaled breath.
exhaled breath or vapor

Examples

The scientist collected samples of the patient's halitus for further analysis.
A faint halitus of lavender lingered in the room.
Through the cold air, his halitus was visibly rising in small clouds.

Etymology

From Latin *halitus* (breath, vapor), from *halare* (to breathe).
