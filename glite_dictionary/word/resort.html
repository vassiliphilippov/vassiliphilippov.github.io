resort

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
a hotel or complex offering recreation and entertainment in a vacation area
a vacation hotel or complex

Examples

We decided to book a week at a seaside resort for our family vacation.
The resort offers a variety of activities, including golf, tennis, and spa treatments.
Many tourists come to this mountain resort for its stunning views and luxury amenities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotel - a building where people pay to stay and eat meals

Etymology

From Old French *resortir* (to return, resort), from Latin *resurgere* (to rise again), though the modern sense developed in English in reference to places people visit for relaxation.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
an action of turning to someone or something for help, especially as a final option
action of seeking help as a last option

Examples

When negotiations failed, their only resort was legal action.
She viewed asking for financial help as a last resort.
Violence must never be a first resort in resolving disputes.

Synset Examples

have recourse to the courts
an appeal to his uncle was his last resort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recourse - the use of someone or something as a source of help
  • Synonym: appeal - an earnest request or plea for help or support

Etymology

From Old French *resortir* 'to return, come back', later developed to mean 'turning to for help' in English.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
a place that is popular and frequently visited, especially for holidays or recreation
popular vacation or recreation place

Examples

The seaside resort attracts thousands of tourists every summer.
Many families choose a mountain resort for their winter holidays.
The town developed rapidly after the opening of the luxury resort nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - a quiet place people go for rest or privacy

Etymology

From Old French *resort* (a place one goes back to), from *resortir* (to return), from Latin *re-* (again) + *sortiri* (to obtain by lot).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
to turn to and adopt a course of action, especially one that may be undesirable, as a means of achieving something or solving a problem
turn to as a solution, often as a last option

Examples

When negotiations failed, the team had to resort to legal action.
She promised not to resort to lying, no matter the circumstances.
Due to the storm, we may have to resort to using candles for light.

Synset Examples

The government resorted to rationing meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn to - to seek help, advice, or a solution from someone or something
  • Synonym: fall back on - to rely on or use something as a backup when needed

Etymology

From Old French 'resortir' (to go out again, to return), from re- (back) + sortir (to go out).

5. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
a person or thing that is turned to for help, protection, or a solution, especially as a final option
thing or person relied on for help or security

Examples

Charities often become a resort for people in desperate circumstances.
When negotiations failed, military action became the only resort.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - something providing shelter or protection
  • Synonym: resource - something that can be used for support or help

Etymology

From Old French *resortir* (to go out again), from re- (again) + sortir (to go out).
