brusk

1. ADJ

/ brʌsk / (AmE), / brʌsk / (BrE)
abrupt or curt in manner or speech, often to the point of appearing rude
abrupt or curt; rude in manner

Examples

He gave a brusk reply and walked away without another word.
Her brusk tone made it clear she was in no mood to chat.
The manager's brusk dismissal of the question surprised everyone.

Synset Examples

try to cultivate a less brusque manner
a curt reply
the salesgirl was very short with him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brusque - abrupt or curt in manner; alternate spelling
  • Synonym: curt - rudely brief in speech or manner

Etymology

From French 'brusque', likely from Italian 'brusco' (sharp, tart).
