apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəl / (AmE), / ˈæp.əl / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to Eurasia, widely cultivated for its firm, round, edible fruit.
tree cultivated for its fruit

Examples

The apple is one of the most widely grown fruit trees in the world.
Farmers pruned each apple in the orchard to ensure a healthy harvest.
An apple can live for decades and produce fruit for many years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: apple - the edible fruit of the apple tree

Etymology

Old English æppel, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch appel and German Apfel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpəl / (AmE), / ˈapəl / (BrE)
A round fruit with smooth red, yellow, or green skin, firm white flesh, and a crisp, sweet to tart taste, produced by the apple tree.
round fruit with crisp, sweet or tart flesh

Examples

She brought a shiny red apple to school for her teacher.
I like to snack on a crisp apple in the afternoon.
We picked green apples from the tree in Grandma's yard.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pear - a sweet, yellowish or brownish fruit with a rounded shape, narrowing towards the stalk
  • Degree Lower: crabapple - a small, sour fruit closely related to the cultivated apple
  • Degree Higher: orange - a round citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow rind

Etymology

Old English æppel; related to Dutch appel and German Apfel.
