understeer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌstɪr / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌstɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which a vehicle turns less sharply than intended by the driver, typically because the front wheels lose grip before the rear wheels
car steering less than intended

Examples

The car began to understeer in the wet curve, forcing the driver to slow down.
Racing drivers must know how to handle understeer to maintain control at high speeds.
Front-wheel-drive cars are often more prone to understeer than rear-wheel-drive cars.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oversteer - when a car steers more than intended by the driver

Etymology

From 'under-' (insufficiently) + 'steer' (to guide a vehicle).
