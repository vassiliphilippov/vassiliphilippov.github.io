osteoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑstiˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A benign, slow-growing tumor made of bone tissue, typically found on the skull or jaw.
benign bone tumor, often on skull or jaw
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed an osteoma from the patient’s skull after a routine scan revealed its presence.
Most osteomas are painless and discovered incidentally during imaging for other conditions.
A dentist noticed a small osteoma on the patient’s jaw during a dental X-ray.

Etymology

From Greek 'osteon' (bone) + '-oma' (tumor, swelling).
