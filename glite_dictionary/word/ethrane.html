Ethrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθˌreɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛθˌreɪn / (BrE)
A nonflammable liquid used as an inhalation general anesthetic, also known by its trade name.
trade name for a general anesthetic
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered Ethrane before the surgical procedure began.
Due to its properties as a general anesthetic, Ethrane is commonly used in operating rooms.
She had an allergic reaction to Ethrane during her last surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enflurane - a volatile liquid used as an inhalational anesthetic (generic name for Ethrane)
