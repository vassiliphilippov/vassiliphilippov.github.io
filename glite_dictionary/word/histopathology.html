histopathology

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstoʊpæˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌhɪstəʊpəˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study and diagnosis of diseases by examining tissues under a microscope
microscopic study of diseased tissue
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a histopathology report to confirm the diagnosis.
After the surgery, the tissue was sent for histopathology to detect any malignancy.
Advances in histopathology have improved early detection of many diseases.

Synset Examples

The histopath revealed unexpected findings in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathology - study of diseases and their effects

Etymology

From Greek 'histos' (tissue) and 'pathos' (disease, suffering), with '-logy' (study of).
