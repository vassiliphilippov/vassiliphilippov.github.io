boost

1. VERB

/ bust / (AmE), / buːst / (BrE)
to increase or improve something, especially by making it greater or more effective
increase or improve something

Examples

The new advertising campaign helped boost sales last quarter.
Regular exercise can boost your energy levels naturally.
The government promised to boost funding for education next year.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - to make or become greater in amount, number, or degree
  • Synonym: raise - to lift or move to a higher position or level
  • Antonym: decrease - to become or make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Etymology

Probably from earlier Scots 'boost' (a push, help).
