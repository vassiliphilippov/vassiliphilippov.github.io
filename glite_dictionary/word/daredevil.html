daredevil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrˌdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛəˌdɛvəl / (BrE)
a person who enjoys taking dangerous risks and acts recklessly or impulsively
reckless risk-taker

Examples

The daredevil performed a backflip on his motorcycle over five cars.
Despite warnings, she showed her daredevil side by climbing the cliff without ropes.
His parents worried about his daredevil stunts on his skateboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventurer - a person who enjoys or seeks adventure
  • Antonym: coward - a person who lacks courage or is excessively afraid of danger

Etymology

Formed from 'dare' + 'devil' in early 19th-century English, originally as a noun for one who challenges fate like the devil.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛrˌdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈdeəˌdevl / (BrE)
recklessly bold and adventurous in a way that takes dangerous risks
recklessly daring or risk-taking

Examples

The stuntman’s daredevil performance left the audience breathless.
She became famous for her daredevil driving in high-speed races.
Only a daredevil climber would attempt to scale that dangerous peak without safety equipment.

Synset Examples

a daredevil test pilot having the right stuff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - without heed of danger or consequences
  • Antonym: cautious - showing care to avoid danger or risk

Etymology

Formed from 'dare' (to have the courage to do something) and 'devil,' implying reckless courage.
