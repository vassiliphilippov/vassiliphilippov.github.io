mare

1. NOUN

/ mɛr / (AmE), / meə / (BrE)
an adult female horse
adult female horse

Examples

The mare gave birth to a healthy foal last spring.
On the farm, each mare is cared for by an experienced handler.
Her favorite horse was a gentle gray mare named Daisy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stallion - an adult male horse

Etymology

From Old English *mere* (female horse), related to Old High German *mara*.

adult female horse

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stallion - an adult male horse

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛəɹi / (AmE), / ˈmɑːreɪ / (BrE)
a large, dark, basaltic plain on the surface of the moon, formed by ancient volcanic eruptions
dark region on the moon's surface

Examples

The astronauts landed near a mare visible from Earth with the naked eye.
Scientists studied the composition of the lunar mare to learn about the Moon's volcanic history.
A telescope reveals the detailed boundaries of the mare across the Moon's surface.

Etymology

From Latin 'mare' meaning 'sea', as early astronomers thought these areas resembled seas.
