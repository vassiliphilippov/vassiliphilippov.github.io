wintriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tri.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being characteristic of or resembling winter, typically in being cold or bleak.
the quality of being wintry or like winter

Examples

The wintriness of the wind made it hard to stay outside for long.
She admired the wintriness in the landscape, with its bare trees and icy silence.
There was a certain wintriness in his mood that matched the grey skies.

Synset Examples

The wintriness of the day got us into the mood for a snow fight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintry - characteristic of winter; cold, bleak

Etymology

From 'wintry' + '-ness', formed from 'winter' (Old English 'winter').
