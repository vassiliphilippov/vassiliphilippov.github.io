philosopher

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfər / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfə / (BrE)
a person who is wise, calm, and rational, and lives a life guided by reason and equanimity
wise, calm, rational person

Examples

Even in difficult times, she remained a true philosopher, never losing her composure.
He approached every problem like a philosopher, weighing the options calmly and logically.
Many admired her for being a philosopher in both action and attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - a profoundly wise person, often sought for advice or judgment
  • Antonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently

Etymology

From Greek philosophos (lover of wisdom).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfər / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒsəfə / (BrE)
a person who studies or is an expert in philosophy, especially one who seeks wisdom and knowledge about fundamental questions of existence, reason, and values
expert in philosophy

Examples

Plato is regarded as one of the greatest philosophers in Western history.
She aspired to become a philosopher and spent years studying ancient texts.
The discussion was led by a well-known philosopher from the local university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinker - someone who thinks deeply about important subjects

Etymology

From Greek philosophos (lover of wisdom), via Latin philosophus.
