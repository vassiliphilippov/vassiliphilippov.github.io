Syringa persica

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə pərˈsiːkə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə pəˈsiːkə / (BrE)
A small, densely branching shrub native to Asia, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant lilac flowers; commonly known as the Persian lilac.
Asiatic shrub with lilac flowers

Examples

The Syringa persica in our garden blooms beautifully in late spring.
Horticulturists recommend planting Syringa persica for its fragrant, showy flowers.
Botanists often study Syringa persica as an example of Asiatic ornamental shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilac - a flowering plant of the genus Syringa, noted for its fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Syringa' (the genus name) and 'persica' (meaning 'Persian', referring to its origin).
