puffery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌfəri / (AmE), / ˈpʌfəri / (BrE)
Exaggerated or flattering praise, especially when used for promotional or advertising purposes and not meant to be taken literally.
exaggerated promotional praise

Examples

The company's advertising was filled with puffery about its revolutionary new product.
Most consumers recognize puffery and do not take every claim at face value.
His speech was dismissed as mere puffery by his critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperbole - exaggerated statements not meant to be taken literally
  • Antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being true or accurate

Etymology

From 'puff' in the sense of a promotional statement, + '-ery', denoting a practice or characteristic.
