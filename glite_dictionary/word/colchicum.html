Colchicum

1. NOUN

/ kɒlˈtʃɪkəm / (AmE), / kɒlˈkɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants, commonly known as autumn crocuses, with cormous roots and typically blooming in the fall.
a genus of autumn-blooming plants

Examples

The Colchicum in our garden blooms late in autumn, adding color after most flowers have faded.
Botanists study various species of Colchicum for their medicinal properties and unique blooming cycle.
She mistook a Colchicum for a crocus, not realizing it can be toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autumn crocus - a common name for Colchicum species

Etymology

From Latin 'colchicum', from Ancient Greek 'κολχικόν' (kolkhikón), named after Colchis, a region on the Black Sea.
