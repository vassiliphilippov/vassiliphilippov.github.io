drought

1. NOUN

/ draʊt / (AmE), / draʊt / (BrE)
A long period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water.
a prolonged lack of rainfall

Examples

The region is suffering from a severe drought that has lasted for months.
Many crops failed because the drought reduced the water supply to dangerously low levels.
Authorities implemented water restrictions due to the ongoing drought.

Synset Examples

farmers most affected by the drought hope that there may yet be sufficient rain early in the growing season

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flood - an overflow of water that submerges land

Etymology

Old English *drūgath* (dryness), from *drūgian* (to dry).

2. NOUN

/ draʊt / (AmE), / draʊt / (BrE)
A prolonged period without something that is wanted or needed, such as success, supply, or activity.
prolonged shortage or absence

Examples

After three years without a hit, the singer finally ended her drought with a chart-topping single.
The team is determined to break its championship drought this season.
The city experienced a ten-year drought of major cultural events before the new festival was launched.

Synset Examples

when England defeated Pakistan it ended a ten-year drought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famine - a severe shortage of food
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something; plenty

Etymology

From Old English *drūgath* (dryness), related to *dry*.
