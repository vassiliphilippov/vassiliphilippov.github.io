update

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something more modern or accurate by adding new information or changing it to reflect recent developments
bring something up to date

Examples

I need to update my resume before applying for the new job.
The software automatically updates itself whenever a new version is available.
She updated the committee on the latest changes in the regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refresh - to update or make current, especially digital content or data
  • Synonym: to modernize - to make something more modern

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'up-' + 'date', originally meaning 'to bring a date up to the present'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00835207-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌpˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to bring something up to date by adding new information, making changes, or adopting the latest technology
make more current; add latest information

Examples

You should update your software regularly to protect against security threats.
The website was updated with new photos and information last week.
She is updating her résumé to include her latest job experience.

Synset Examples

tonight, I will update my operating system
we updated the database with the most recent figures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upgrade - to raise something to a higher standard, often technologically
  • Synonym: to refresh - to update or renew, often used for data or computer screens

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'datare' (to give), via the noun 'date' in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00170701-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌpˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something more modern or bring it up to date by adding new information or improvements
make more modern; bring up to date

Examples

We're planning to update the company website next month.
She hired a contractor to update her bathroom with modern fixtures.
The software will automatically update your files to the latest version.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modernize - to make something more modern
  • Synonym: to renew - to make new or fresh again

Etymology

From the noun 'date', with 'up-' meaning 'to make current'; coined in American English in the mid-20th century.
