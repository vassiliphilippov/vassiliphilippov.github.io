viricidal

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɪrɪˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌvɪrɪˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
(of a substance or process) tending to destroy or inactivate viruses.
destroying or inactivating viruses
medical

Examples

This disinfectant has strong viricidal properties and is commonly used in hospitals.
Experts recommend using a viricidal solution to clean surfaces during a viral outbreak.
The viricidal effect of ultraviolet light was demonstrated in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virucidal - tending to destroy viruses (alternative form)

Etymology

From Latin 'virus' (poison) + '-cidal' from Latin 'caedere' (to kill).
