jackdaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk.dɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.dɔː / (BrE)
a small black-and-grey Eurasian crow (Corvus monedula), noted for its intelligence and sometimes for thievery
a small Eurasian crow

Examples

A jackdaw can often be seen picking up shiny objects in the garden.
The jackdaw is known for its harsh call and agile flight.
We watched a jackdaw perch on the old stone wall, scanning the field for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - large black bird of the genus Corvus

Etymology

From Middle English jacke (the name Jack used for small animals) + daw (old word for the bird).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌdɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.dɔː / (BrE)
a small black-and-grey Eurasian bird of the crow family, noted for its intelligence and sometimes for taking shiny objects
small black-and-grey Eurasian bird

Examples

A flock of jackdaws gathered noisily on the church roof.
The jackdaw is known for its habit of stealing shiny objects.
We spotted a jackdaw perched on the fence by the old barn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - large black bird of the Corvidae family

Etymology

From Middle English jakdaw, from Jak (a nickname for Jack) + daw (an old word for the bird).
