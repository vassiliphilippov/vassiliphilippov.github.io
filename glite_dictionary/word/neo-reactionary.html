neo-reactionary

1. ADJ

/ ˌnioʊ riˈækʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ riˈækʃənəri / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting a political movement that opposes modern liberalism and advocates a return to traditional social or political orders.
person/movement opposing modern liberalism

Examples

Some writers on the internet have developed a distinctly neo-reactionary ideology.
The neo-reactionary movement is sometimes called the 'dark enlightenment.'
Critics argue that neo-reactionary views threaten the foundations of liberal democracy.

Synset Examples

He often engages in neo-reactionary debates online.
The neo-reactionary group promotes traditional hierarchies.
The neo-reactionary philosophy challenges contemporary democratic values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - strongly opposed to social or political change
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new, liberal ideas

Etymology

From neo- (new) + reactionary (opposing political or social progress), forming in the 21st century online discourse.
