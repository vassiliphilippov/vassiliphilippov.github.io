disordered

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
not arranged in a regular or neat order; lacking organization or structure
not arranged in order

Examples

The papers on his desk were completely disordered after the hectic meeting.
The city was left disordered by the sudden storm, with debris everywhere.
Her thoughts became disordered as she tried to make sense of the confusing news.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - arranged in a regular or neat way
  • Synonym: chaotic - in a state of complete disorder or confusion

Etymology

Formed from 'dis-' (not) + 'ordered'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
in a state of confusion or untidiness; not arranged in an orderly way
in a confused or untidy state

Examples

After the party, the living room was left completely disordered.
The files were so disordered that it took hours to find the right one.
His thoughts were disordered by the sudden bad news.

Synset Examples

troops fleeing in broken ranks
a confused mass of papers on the desk
the small disordered room
with everything so upset

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a neat and regular way
  • Synonym: messy - untidy or dirty

Etymology

From the prefix 'dis-' (expressing negation or reversal) + 'ordered'.

3. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔrdərd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəd / (BrE)
lacking organization, order, or logical continuity; confused or chaotic.
lacking orderly arrangement; chaotic

Examples

The papers on her desk were so disordered that she couldn't find the report she needed.
His disordered thoughts made it difficult for him to focus during the meeting.
We became lost in the disordered streets of the unfamiliar city.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a methodical way
  • Synonym: chaotic - completely disorganized or in a state of chaos

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + ordered; modeled after order and disorder.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00467212-s
Synset POS: s
