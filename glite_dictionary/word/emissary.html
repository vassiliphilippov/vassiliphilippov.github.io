emissary

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪsəri / (BrE)
A person sent on a special mission, usually as a diplomatic representative for someone else.
person sent as a representative on a mission

Examples

The government sent an emissary to negotiate peace with the rebels.
As an emissary of the king, she carried an important message to the foreign court.
The UN appointed a special emissary to mediate in the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envoy - a representative or messenger (especially in diplomacy)
  • Synonym: ambassador - an accredited diplomat sent by a country as its official representative

Etymology

From Latin 'emissarius', from 'emittere' (to send out).
