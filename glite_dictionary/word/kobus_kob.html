Kobus kob

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊbəs ˈkɑb / (AmE), / ˌkəʊbəs ˈkɒb / (BrE)
an orange-brown antelope native to southeast Africa, known for its lyre-shaped horns and herd behavior
African orange-brown antelope species

Examples

The Kobus kob is often seen grazing in the floodplains of southeast Africa.
Wildlife photographers are attracted by the elegant shape of the Kobus kob's horns.
A large group of Kobus kobs moved across the savanna at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running herbivorous animal with hollow horns

Etymology

From modern Latin, genus name 'Kobus' and species name from local vernacular names for the animal.
