parotitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæɹ.əˈtaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈtaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of one or both parotid glands, often causing swelling near the jaw
inflammation of the parotid gland(s)
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with parotitis after presenting with swelling below the ear.
Viral infections are a common cause of parotitis in young adults.
Treatment of parotitis often includes rest, hydration, and sometimes antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mumps - a viral infection causing swelling of the parotid glands, a common cause of parotitis

Etymology

From Greek 'parotis' (parotid gland) + '-itis' (inflammation).
