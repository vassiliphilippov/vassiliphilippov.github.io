mossy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑsi / (AmE), / ˈmɒsi / (BrE)
(of ideas, things, etc.) old-fashioned or out of date; not in keeping with modern styles or thinking, often in a pejorative sense.
outdated or old-fashioned (pejorative)

Examples

His mossy views on marriage no longer resonate with today’s generation.
The committee rejected the mossy procedures in favor of a fresh approach.
I wish my supervisor would stop relying on mossy business practices that haven’t changed in decades.

Synset Examples

moss-grown ideas about family life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquated - outdated or old-fashioned
  • Antonym: modern - relating to present or recent times

Etymology

From 'moss', with the sense of something covered in moss, figuratively implying age and neglect.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɒsi / (AmE), / ˈmɒsi / (BrE)
covered with or overgrown by moss.
covered with moss

Examples

The stone wall was mossy and damp after years of neglect.
She slipped on the mossy rocks by the edge of the stream.
A mossy log lay across the forest path, covered in vibrant green.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luscious - (as a synonym in some extended senses) richly verdant, often of foliage
  • Antonym: bare - without any covering, lacking moss or anything on the surface

Etymology

From 'moss' + '-y', denoting 'full of' or 'characterized by'.
