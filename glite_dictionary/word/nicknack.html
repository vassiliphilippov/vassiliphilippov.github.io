nicknack

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪk.næk / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.næk / (BrE)
a small decorative object, especially one kept for its ornamentation rather than practical use
small decorative object

Examples

She filled the shelves with colorful nicknacks from her travels around the world.
A collection of porcelain nicknacks decorated the mantelpiece.
The shop sold all sorts of nicknacks, from tiny figurines to painted eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knickknack - small decorative object

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'knickknack', probably a reduplication of 'knack' (trick or object of small value), first recorded in the 17th century.
