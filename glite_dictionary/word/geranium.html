Geranium

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪnɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants known as cranesbills, comprising mostly herbaceous species found worldwide, especially in temperate regions.
genus of flowering plants (cranesbills)

Examples

The Geranium is prized for its colorful flowers and deeply lobed leaves.
Several species within the Geranium genus are native to North America.
Gardeners often plant Geraniums to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranesbill - another name for any plant of the genus Geranium

Etymology

From Greek *geranion* (crane), referring to the beak-like shape of the fruit.
