lifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfə / (BrE)
a person who is serving a life sentence in prison
person serving a life sentence

Examples

The notorious lifer was transferred to a maximum security facility.
Many lifers spend decades behind bars without hope of parole.
After the verdict, he became a lifer in the state penitentiary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - a person legally held in prison as a punishment

Etymology

From "life" + "-er", denoting a person involved with something for life.
