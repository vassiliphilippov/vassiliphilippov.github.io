Veronica chamaedrys

1. NOUN

/ vəˌrɑnɪkə kəˈmidrɪs / (AmE), / vəˌrɒnɪkə kəˈmiːdrɪs / (BrE)
An Old World plant of the speedwell family, with axillary racemes of small blue-and-white flowers.
Old World plant with blue-and-white flowers

Examples

Veronica chamaedrys is often found growing wild in European meadows.
Gardeners appreciate Veronica chamaedrys for its attractive blue flowers.
If left to spread, Veronica chamaedrys can form a pretty ground cover.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Veronica' is the genus and 'chamaedrys' derives from Greek 'chamai' (on the ground) and 'drus' (oak).
