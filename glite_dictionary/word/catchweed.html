catchweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkatʃwiːd / (BrE)
an annual plant with stems covered in curved prickles, found in North America, Europe, and Asia; also known as cleavers or goosegrass
a prickly annual plant (Galium aparine)

Examples

The catchweed clung to her clothes as she walked through the meadow.
Gardeners often remove catchweed to prevent it from overtaking their flower beds.
Catchweed thrives in hedgerows and along riverbanks throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleavers - another common name for Galium aparine, the same plant as catchweed
