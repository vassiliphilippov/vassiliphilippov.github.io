absorbency

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrbənsi / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbəntsi / (BrE)
the ability of a material to soak up liquid or moisture
capacity to absorb liquid

Examples

Paper towels are valued for their high absorbency in cleaning up spills.
The absorbency of this fabric makes it suitable for sportswear.
Manufacturers often test the absorbency of diapers to ensure quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquid easily

Etymology

From 'absorbent' + '-cy', following the pattern of forming nouns denoting quality or state.
