bowling

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
A game in which balls are rolled toward objects, typically pins, with the aim of knocking them over or moving them.
game of rolling balls to knock over pins

Examples

We went bowling with friends last weekend and had a great time.
The local bowling alley offers league games every Tuesday night.
She scored a perfect 300 in her last bowling game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowls - lawn game using biased balls to approach a target ball

Etymology

From 'bowl' (to roll a ball) + -ing, referring to the action of rolling balls in the game.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
(in cricket) the act or action of delivering the ball to the batsman by the bowler.
delivering the ball to the batsman (cricket)

Examples

His fast bowling intimidated even the best batsmen during the match.
Spin bowling requires great skill and accuracy.
She has been practicing her bowling technique every weekend at the cricket club.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: batting - the act of hitting the ball with a bat (cricket)

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
A game in which players roll a heavy ball towards a group of pins, aiming to knock down as many as possible.
game of rolling a ball to knock down pins

Examples

We went bowling last night and had a great time at the alley.
The local league holds bowling tournaments every Saturday afternoon.
She took up bowling as a fun way to stay active and social.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenpin bowling - bowling played with ten pins at the end of the lane
  • Synonym: duckpin bowling - variation of bowling with smaller pins and balls
  • Antonym: bowls - a lawn game involving rolling balls towards a smaller ball

Etymology

From bowl (the act of rolling a ball), with -ing denoting the action or activity.
