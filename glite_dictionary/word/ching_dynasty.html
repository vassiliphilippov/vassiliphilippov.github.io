Ch'ing dynasty

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪŋ ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / tʃɪŋ ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
the last imperial dynasty of China, ruling from 1644 to 1912, established by the Manchu and overthrown following a revolution.
last imperial dynasty of China (1644–1912)

Examples

The Ch'ing dynasty was marked by both great prosperity and significant internal turmoil.
Many priceless artifacts in the museum date back to the Ch'ing dynasty.
The fall of the Ch'ing dynasty led to the establishment of the Republic of China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Qing dynasty - alternate spelling for the last imperial dynasty of China

Etymology

Wade-Giles spelling of 'Qing', from Mandarin 清 (Qīng), meaning 'clear, pure'.
