fallible

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfæl.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
capable of making mistakes or being wrong; not perfect.
capable of making mistakes

Examples

All humans are fallible, so we must learn from our mistakes.
The judge reminded the jury that eyewitness testimony is often fallible.
She admits she is fallible, but strives to do her best.

Synset Examples

everyone is fallible to some degree

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infallible - incapable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Medieval Latin fallibilis, from Latin fallere (to deceive).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæ.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfal.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
capable of making mistakes or being wrong; imperfect, especially in the way humans differ from divine or perfect beings.
capable of making mistakes; imperfect

Examples

Even the most experienced leaders are fallible and sometimes make serious errors.
She reminded her team that everyone is fallible and allowed space for honest mistakes.
He admitted being fallible, emphasizing that perfection is not expected from anyone.

Synset Examples

I'm only a fallible human
frail humanity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infallible - incapable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Medieval Latin fallibilis, from Latin fallere (to deceive, err).
