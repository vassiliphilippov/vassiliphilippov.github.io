gestural

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛs.tʃə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛs.tjʊ.rəl / (BrE)
relating to gestures or movements of the body, especially used as a means of communication, such as in the language of the deaf.
relating to gestures or body language

Examples

American Sign Language is a gestural system used by the deaf community to communicate.
Her gestural expressions made her message clear, even to those who did not speak her language.
The teacher’s use of gestural communication helped the hearing-impaired students understand the lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual - performed or operated by the hands; often used of communication with hand signs

Etymology

From gesture + -al, the suffix forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛstʃərəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstjʊərəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving communication or expression through gestures rather than words
related to gestures or nonverbal communication

Examples

Her presentation included detailed gestural cues to emphasize her points.
Researchers analyzed the gestural signals used in early childhood communication.
The dancers used gestural movements to tell a story without words.

Synset Examples

the study of gestural communication
art like gesture is a form of nonverbal expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonverbal - not involving or using words; not spoken

Etymology

From gesture + -al, formed on the model of adjectives meaning 'relating to' or 'characterized by'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500009-s
Synset POS: s
