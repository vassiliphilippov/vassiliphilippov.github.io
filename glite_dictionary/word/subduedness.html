subduedness

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdudnəs / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being quiet, restrained, or showing patient endurance, especially in response to difficulty or adversity
the state of being subdued or restrained

Examples

Her subduedness in the face of criticism was truly admirable.
Despite the chaos around him, his subduedness helped to calm the team.
The subduedness of her response showed remarkable self-control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, problems, or suffering without becoming annoyed

Etymology

From 'subdued' (past participle of 'subdue'), + '-ness' forming a noun.

2. NOUN

/ səbˈduːdnəs / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being less intense or less bright, especially in lights or sounds
lack of intensity or brilliance (lights/sounds)

Examples

The subduedness of the lighting in the restaurant made for a cozy atmosphere.
There was a gentle subduedness to the music that helped guests relax.
Painters often use subduedness in their color choices to create a sense of calm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutedness - quality of being muted or softened in tone or intensity
  • Antonym: vividness - quality of being intense or full of life in color or sound

Etymology

From 'subdue' (to make less intense or forceful) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting states or qualities).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
