to junketeer

1. VERB

/ ˌdʒʌŋ.kəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌŋ.kəˈtɪə / (BrE)
to travel or attend events, especially at another's expense, for the sake of pleasure or personal gain rather than for work or duty
travel for pleasure, often at another's expense

Examples

The politician was criticized for choosing to junketeer across Europe instead of attending to his duties.
After winning the lottery, she decided to junketeer through several exotic countries.
Many journalists junketeer to conferences, enjoying luxury at little personal cost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junket - a pleasure trip, especially one paid for by someone else

Etymology

From 'junket' (a pleasure trip or excursion), + '-eer' (agent or verb-forming suffix).
