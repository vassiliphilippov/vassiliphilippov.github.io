tenantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnəntri / (AmE), / ˈtɛnəntri / (BrE)
the tenants of an estate or property considered collectively as a group
group of tenants on an estate

Examples

The tenantry gathered annually to celebrate the harvest festival on the manor grounds.
Improvements to the cottages greatly benefited the tenantry of the estate.
Changes in law affected the rights of the tenantry during the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenant - a person who rents land or property from a landlord

Etymology

From tenant + -ry, with -ry forming collective nouns.
