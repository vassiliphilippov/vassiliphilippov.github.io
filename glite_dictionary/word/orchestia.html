Orchestia

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈkɛstiə / (AmE), / ɔːˈkɛstɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small amphipod crustaceans, type genus of the family Orchestiidae, commonly found in marine and terrestrial habitats.
genus of small amphipod crustaceans

Examples

Several species within the genus Orchestia are adapted to life on sandy beaches.
Biologists discovered a new Orchestia species near the estuary last year.
The Orchestia crustaceans play a crucial role in the shoreline ecosystem.

Etymology

From Greek orchēstēs (dancer), likely referring to the jumping movement of these crustaceans.

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈkɛstiə / (AmE), / ɔːˈkɛstɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small, typically shore-dwelling amphipod crustaceans that is the type genus of the family Orchestiidae.
genus of small amphipod crustaceans

Examples

The Orchestia can often be found hiding under damp seaweed at the high-tide line.
Biologists collected samples of Orchestia from several coastal locations for their study.
A species in the genus Orchestia displays remarkable jumping ability for an amphipod.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphipod - a type of small crustacean with a laterally compressed body

Etymology

From Greek 'orkhēstēs' (dancer), possibly referring to the jumping movement of the animals.
