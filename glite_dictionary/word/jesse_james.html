Jesse James

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛsi ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsi ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(1847–1882) A famous American outlaw who fought as a Confederate soldier and later led a gang that robbed banks and trains in the Western United States.
notorious American outlaw, 19th century

Examples

The legend of Jesse James continues to capture the imagination of many Americans today.
Some historians have tried to separate the myths from the facts about Jesse James's life.
Tourists still visit the Missouri town where Jesse James was born.
