Hindooism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.duˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.dʊˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
an archaic spelling of 'Hinduism', the major religion originating on the Indian subcontinent, practiced especially in India, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and Nepal
archaic spelling of Hinduism, a major Asian religion
archaic

Examples

Many 19th-century writers referred to the religion as Hindooism rather than Hinduism.
References to Hindooism are now considered outdated in academic writing.
The spread of Hindooism was noted by British colonial officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hinduism - major religion originating in the Indian subcontinent

Etymology

Variant of 'Hinduism', from 'Hindoo', an old-fashioned form of 'Hindu'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.duˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.dʊˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
(archaic) An alternative spelling of Hinduism; the religious and philosophical system native to India characterized by a belief in reincarnation, a caste system, and a supreme being of many forms.
archaic alternative spelling of Hinduism
archaic

Examples

The British authors of the 19th century often referred to Hindu beliefs as Hindooism.
Scholars nowadays use 'Hinduism' rather than Hindooism in academic works.
Hindooism, though archaic, can be found in many older books on Indian religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hinduism - The major religion and philosophy native to India

Etymology

Archaic spelling based on 'Hindoo', variant of 'Hindu' (from Persian 'Hindū', from Sanskrit 'Sindhu').
