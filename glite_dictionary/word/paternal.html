paternal

1. ADJ

/ pəˈtɝː.nəl / (AmE), / pəˈtɜː.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a father; fatherly.
characteristic of a father

Examples

He always took a paternal interest in the welfare of his students.
Everyone admired his paternal patience and understanding with the children.
She often sought his paternal advice when faced with difficult decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maternal - characteristic of a mother
  • Synonym: fatherly - having qualities of a father

Etymology

From Latin 'paternus' (of a father), from 'pater' (father).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
Belonging to or inherited from one's father or the father's side of the family.
of or from one's father

Examples

She grew up on her family's paternal estate in the countryside.
The paternal surname has been passed down for generations.
Certain diseases can be traced through the paternal line of ancestry.

Synset Examples

spent his childhood on the paternal farm
paternal traits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maternal - belonging to or inherited from one's mother

Etymology

From Latin 'paternus' (of a father), from 'pater' (father).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ pəˈtɝːnəl / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
related to or characteristic of the father's side of the family
of or relating to the father's side

Examples

Her paternal grandfather immigrated from Italy in the 1920s.
All the paternal relatives came together for the family reunion.
She inherited the ring from her paternal aunt.

Synset Examples

a paternal aunt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maternal - related to the mother's side of the family

Etymology

From Latin 'paternus' (of a father), from 'pater' (father).
