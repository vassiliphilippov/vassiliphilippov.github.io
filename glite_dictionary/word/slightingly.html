slightingly

1. ADV

/ ˈslaɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that shows disrespect or disparagement; scornfully or with a lack of regard.
in a disparaging or disrespectful way

Examples

She spoke slightingly of his achievements, ignoring his hard work.
The critic referred slightingly to the artist's early efforts as amateurish.
He always mentions their ideas slightingly, never giving them proper credit.

Synset Examples

these mythological figures are described disparagingly as belonging ‘only to a story’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparagingly - in a manner that belittles or devalues

Etymology

Formed from 'slight' (verb or adj.) + adverbial suffix '-ly.'
