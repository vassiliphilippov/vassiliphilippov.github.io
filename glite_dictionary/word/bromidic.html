bromidic

1. ADJ

/ broʊˈmɪdɪk / (AmE), / brəʊˈmɪdɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or remark) lacking originality or freshness; trite or unoriginal, especially due to overuse of clichés or platitudes.
trite or unoriginal; given to clichés

Examples

The professor's bromidic speech failed to inspire the audience.
She grew tired of his bromidic compliments and wished for something genuine.
Critics found the film's dialogue bromidic and predictable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platitudinous - (of speech or writing) full of flat, dull remarks; trite
  • Antonym: original - new, novel, or creative

Etymology

From 'bromide', used figuratively for a trite saying or person, influenced by the chemical's sedative effects.

2. ADJ

/ broʊˈmɪdɪk / (AmE), / brəʊˈmɪdɪk / (BrE)
(of a remark, idea, or style) dull and unoriginal, especially while pretending to be important or significant.
trite and pretentious

Examples

The professor's lecture was filled with bromidic observations that failed to inspire the audience.
Critics dismissed the novel as bromidic, claiming it offered nothing readers hadn't heard before.
She tried to impress her peers, but her comments were painfully bromidic and predictable.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
  • Antonym: original - novel, fresh, or innovative

Etymology

From 'bromide', meaning a trite saying, ultimately from the chemical name for the sedative potassium bromide, which suggested dullness.
