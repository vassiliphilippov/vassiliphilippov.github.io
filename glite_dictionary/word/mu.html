mu

1. NOUN

/ mjuː / (AmE), / mjuː / (BrE)
the twelfth letter of the Greek alphabet (Μ, μ), used especially in scientific and mathematical contexts as a symbol for micro- or other quantities.
Greek letter; symbol in science

Examples

In physics, the symbol mu often represents the coefficient of friction.
The Greek letter mu is used to denote the mean in statistics.
Each row was labeled with a different Greek letter, beginning with alpha and ending with mu.

Etymology

From Greek μῦ (mū), the twelfth letter of the Greek alphabet.
