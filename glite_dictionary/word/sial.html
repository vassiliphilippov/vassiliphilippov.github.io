sial

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪˌæl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.al / (BrE)
the granitelike rocks forming the outermost layer of the Earth's crust, rich in silicon and aluminum
granitelike layer of Earth's crust

Examples

Geologists study the composition of the sial to understand continental formation.
The sial is typically less dense than the underlying sima.
Continental crust is primarily composed of sial, enriched with silicon and aluminum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sima - the layer beneath the sial, rich in silica and magnesium

Etymology

Blend of silicon and aluminium, indicating its main components.
