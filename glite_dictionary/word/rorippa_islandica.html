Rorippa islandica

1. NOUN

/ rəˈrɪpə aɪˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / rəˈrɪpə ɪˈlændɪkə / (BrE)
an annual or biennial cress plant found in damp areas, sometimes used in salads or as a cooked vegetable, and considered a weed in some places
a cress plant found in damp places

Examples

Rorippa islandica often thrives along riverbanks and wet meadows during the summer months.
Some gardeners remove Rorippa islandica because it can become invasive in moist soil.
Early settlers used Rorippa islandica as a leafy vegetable in soups and salads.

Etymology

From the genus name Rorippa and Latin form of 'islandica', meaning 'of Iceland'.
