lightless

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtlɪs / (BrE)
without light; lacking illumination or brightness
without light or illumination

Examples

She walked into the lightless corridor, searching for a switch.
The cave remained lightless even at midday.
A lightless lantern sat abandoned beside the path.

Synset Examples

came up the lightless stairs
the unilluminated side of Mars
through dark unlighted (or unlit) streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark - with little or no light; not light
  • Antonym: illuminated - lit by light; having illumination

Etymology

From 'light' + suffix '-less', indicating absence.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtləs / (BrE)
Not producing or emitting any light; completely dark.
without light; completely dark

Examples

The basement was completely lightless after the power went out.
She fumbled through the lightless corridor, searching for a flashlight.
Space between the stars is vast and lightless.

Synset Examples

lightless stars ‘visible’ only to radio antennae

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark - having little or no light
  • Antonym: luminous - emitting light

Etymology

Formed from 'light' + suffix '-less' meaning 'without'.

Source: WordNet
