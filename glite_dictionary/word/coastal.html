coastal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstl̩ / (BrE)
Located on, near, or relating to a coast.
located on or near a coast

Examples

The coastal town attracts many tourists during the summer months.
They studied the effects of coastal erosion on local wildlife.
A series of coastal storms caused extensive flooding along the shoreline.

Synset Examples

coastal shipping
coastwise winds contributed to the storm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inland - situated away from the coast

Etymology

From coast + -al, meaning 'of the coast'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstl̩ / (BrE)
of or relating to the coast or the land near the sea.
of or relating to the coast

Examples

The coastal villages depend on fishing for their livelihood.
Severe coastal storms have caused flooding this year.
Many tourists are attracted to coastal resorts in the summer.

Synset Examples

coastal erosion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inland - situated away from the coast

Etymology

From 'coast' + '-al', forming an adjective.

3. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstl̩ / (BrE)
located on, near, or bordering a coast.
relating to or near the coast

Examples

The coastal towns rely heavily on fishing and tourism for their economy.
Strong winds battered the coastal areas during the storm last night.
They bought a house with a stunning coastal view overlooking the ocean.

Synset Examples

coastal marshes
coastal waters
the Atlantic coastal plain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inland - located away from the coast, towards the interior of a country

Etymology

From 'coast' + '-al', where 'coast' comes from Middle English coste, from Old French coste, from Latin costa (rib, side).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00464942-a
Synset POS: a
