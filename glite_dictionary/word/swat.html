swat

1. VERB

/ swɑt / (AmE), / swɒt / (BrE)
to hit or slap something swiftly and forcefully, especially to kill an insect
hit or slap swiftly and forcefully

Examples

He tried to swat the mosquito on his arm before it bit him.
The cat jumped up to swat at the toy dangling from the string.
She missed when she tried to swat the fly buzzing around the kitchen.

Synset Examples

Swat flies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • Degree Lower: tap - to touch lightly or gently

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative or related to earlier 'swat' meaning 'to strike'.

2. NOUN

/ swɑt / (AmE), / swɒt / (BrE)
a sharp, often quick blow, especially with the hand or a flat object
a sharp blow with hand or object

Examples

With a quick swat, she killed the mosquito on her arm.
The coach gave the ball a hard swat with his hand.
He responded to the annoying fly with a well-aimed swat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - a sharp blow with the open hand
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light touch or gentle stroke

3. VERB

/ swɑt / (AmE), / swɒt / (BrE)
to make a false or misleading report to the police in order to have a SWAT team sent to a specific location, typically as a prank or to harass the occupants.
make a false police report to dispatch a SWAT team
informal american

Examples

Police warned that if you swat someone, you could face serious criminal charges.
After losing the game, he threatened to swat the streamer out of spite.
Several celebrities have been swatted in recent years, leading to increased security measures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank call - a telephone call intended as a prank or practical joke
  • Degree Lower: report - to give information to authorities about something, often officially

Etymology

From the acronym SWAT (Special Weapons And Tactics), referring to specialized police units; sense arises from the act of sending such teams via a hoax call.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-91000111-v
Synset POS: v
