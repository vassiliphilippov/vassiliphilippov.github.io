Phlebodium

1. NOUN

/ flɪˈboʊdiəm / (AmE), / flɪˈbəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly epiphytic tropical ferns with free veins and sori, commonly found in the Americas.
genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Phlebodium fern thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches in the rainforest.
One popular houseplant species from the Phlebodium genus is Phlebodium aureum, also known as the blue star fern.
Botanists are studying several Phlebodium species to understand their resilience in changing climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'phlebos' (vein), referring to the vein pattern in the fern's fronds.

Examples

Phlebodium is commonly found growing on tree trunks in tropical rainforests.

Scientists study Phlebodium to better understand the diversity of epiphytic ferns.
