Forficula auricularia

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈfɪkjələ ɔˌrɪkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / fɔːˈfɪkjʊlə ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
a species of earwig, commonly known as the European earwig, sometimes considered a garden pest for damaging cultivated bulbs and plants
species of earwig (European earwig)

Examples

Gardeners often find Forficula auricularia hiding under flower pots and stones.
Damage to the tulip bulbs was traced back to Forficula auricularia activity.
Forficula auricularia can be both a helpful predator and a nuisance in vegetable gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwig - a small insect with pincers on its abdomen

Etymology

From Latin: 'forficula' (little scissors) and 'auricularia' (of the ear), referring to its pincers and mistaken association with entering ears.
