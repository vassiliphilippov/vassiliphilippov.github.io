fly-by-night

1. NOUN

/ ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt / (BrE)
a person or business that disappears suddenly, especially to avoid paying debts or fulfilling obligations
person or business avoiding debts

Examples

The company turned out to be a fly-by-night operation and disappeared after collecting deposits.
He warned us not to trust fly-by-night contractors who ask for full payment upfront.
After owing thousands, the fly-by-night vanished without a trace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindler - a person who cheats others out of money or assets
  • Synonym: defaulter - a person who fails to pay a debt or fulfil an obligation
  • Antonym: honest - a person who is truthful and does not cheat or deceive

2. ADJ

/ ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt / (BrE)
(of a business, person, or organization) not reliable or trustworthy, often disappearing quickly after receiving money or taking advantage of clients.
unreliable or untrustworthy, often disappearing quickly
informal

Examples

The city cracked down on fly-by-night contractors who took deposits and vanished.
Be wary of fly-by-night companies offering deals that seem too good to be true.
Many people were scammed by a fly-by-night operation that promised instant profits.

Synset Examples

a fly-by-night operation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trustworthy - reliable and deserving of trust

Etymology

Originally from the idea of someone leaving at night to escape obligations.

3. ADJ

/ ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌflaɪ baɪ ˈnaɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or entity) not reliable or lasting; likely to disappear or fail quickly, especially for dishonest reasons.
unreliable or transient; not lasting

Examples

The company turned out to be a fly-by-night operation that folded within months.
We need to avoid fly-by-night contractors who may not finish the work.
Her promise seemed fly-by-night, with no guarantee of lasting commitment.

Synset Examples

the symphony is no fly-by-night venture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

Originally referred to a fraudulent operator who absconded overnight; first recorded in the 19th century.
