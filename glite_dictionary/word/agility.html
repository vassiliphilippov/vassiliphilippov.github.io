agility

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒɪlɪti / (AmE), / əˈdʒɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability to move quickly and easily with grace and coordination
graceful quickness and nimbleness

Examples

The dancer impressed everyone with her incredible agility on stage.
Cheetahs are admired for their speed and agility in the wild.
Improving your agility can help prevent injuries during athletic activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nimbleness - the quality of being quick and light in movement
  • Antonym: clumsiness - the quality of being awkward or lacking grace in movement

Etymology

From Old French *agilité*, from Latin *agilitas* (quickness, nimbleness), from *agilis* (quick, nimble).
