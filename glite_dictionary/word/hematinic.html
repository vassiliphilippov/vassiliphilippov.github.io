hematinic

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛməˈtɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtɪnɪk / (BrE)
a medicine that increases the hemoglobin content of the blood, used especially to treat iron-deficiency anemia
medicine that boosts blood hemoglobin
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a hematinic to help correct her iron-deficiency anemia.
Many iron supplements act as effective hematinics for patients with low hemoglobin levels.
Long-term use of a hematinic can significantly improve red blood cell counts in anemic individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antianemic - a medicine used to prevent or cure anemia

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) with suffix -ic; refers to blood-enhancing properties.
