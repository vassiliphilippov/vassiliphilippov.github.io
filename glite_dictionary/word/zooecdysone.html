zooecdysone

1. NOUN

/ ˌzoʊ.iˈkdɪsoʊn / (AmE), / ˌzəʊ.iˈkdɪsəʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by insects that regulates molting and metamorphosis
an insect molting hormone
medical

Examples

The action of zooecdysone triggers the larva to shed its old cuticle and begin the next stage of development.
Researchers isolated zooecdysone to better understand how insect growth is regulated by hormones.
Some pesticides work by disrupting the natural effects of zooecdysone in insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecdysone - an insect hormone that promotes molting

Etymology

From German Zooecdysone, combining zoo- (animal) and ecdysone (molting hormone), from Greek ekdusis (shedding).
