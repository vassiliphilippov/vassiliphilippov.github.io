shred

1. NOUN

/ ʃrɛd / (AmE), / ʃrɛd / (BrE)
a very small piece or amount of something, especially something barely noticeable
tiny amount; small piece

Examples

He didn't show a shred of remorse for his actions.
There wasn't a shred of evidence to support the rumor.
Even a shred of hope kept her going during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small amount or piece
  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken or separated off something

Etymology

From Old English 'scrēad' (piece cut off, strip), related to Middle Low German 'schrōt' (cut piece).

2. NOUN

/ ʃrɛd / (AmE), / ʃrɛd / (BrE)
a very small, thin piece that has been torn or cut from something, especially cloth, paper, or food
small torn or cut piece

Examples

She kept a shred of cloth from her childhood blanket as a keepsake.
Not a shred of evidence was left behind at the scene.
After tearing the letter, he threw every shred away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken or separated off something
  • Synonym: scrap - a small piece or amount of something, especially one that is left over after the greater part has been used

Etymology

From Old English screade (scrap, strip of cloth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04053034-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ʃrɛd / (AmE), / ʃrɛd / (BrE)
to tear or cut something into small, thin strips; to destroy or damage by tearing into shreds
tear or cut into shreds

Examples

She used a fork to shred the chicken for the tacos.
The old documents were shredded before disposal to protect sensitive information.
The cat loves to shred the newspaper into tiny pieces when left alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull or rip something apart or to pieces by force
  • Synonym: rip - to tear or split apart or open

Etymology

From Old English 'scrēadian' (to trim, cut); related to Old Norse 'skreyta'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01576722-v
Synset POS: v
