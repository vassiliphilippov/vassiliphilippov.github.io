to adopt

1. VERB

/ əˈdɑpt / (AmE), / əˈdɒpt / (BrE)
to begin to use or follow an idea, method, or practice as one's own
to take up and use (idea, method, practice)

Examples

After much debate, the committee decided to adopt the new safety regulations.
Many people quickly adopt new technologies to make their lives easier.
She was inspired by the philosophy and chose to adopt it as her own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - to readily accept or support an idea or belief
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, use, or believe something

Etymology

From Latin adoptare, from ad- (to) + optare (choose).
