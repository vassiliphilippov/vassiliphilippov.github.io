afloat

1. ADJ

/ əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
covered or flooded with water; awash
covered with water; flooded

Examples

After the storm, the basement was completely afloat with several inches of water.
The heavy rains left the entire street afloat and impassable for cars.
By morning, the tent was afloat due to the leaky roof and relentless downpour.

Synset Examples

the main deck was afloat (or awash)

a flooded bathroom
inundated farmlands
an overflowing tub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awash - covered or flooded with water
  • Synonym: flooded - filled or covered with water

2. ADJ

/ əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
floating on or supported by water; not sinking.
floating on water

Examples

The small wooden boat remained afloat despite the rough waves.
After the flood, several tree branches were seen afloat on the river.
Their goal was to keep the company afloat during the economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrift - floating without being steered or controlled; drifting on water
  • Antonym: submerged - completely under water; below the surface

Etymology

From Old English 'on flote' (on water), related to 'float'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00077529-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
Drifting or floating aimlessly, either literally on water or figuratively without clear direction or purpose.
drifting aimlessly on water or in life

Examples

After the storm, the boat was left afloat in the open sea, with no one to guide it.
Since graduating, he has felt afloat, unsure of what to do next in life.
With no clear plan, the organization is just afloat and lacks real direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrift - floating without being steered; also without direction or purpose

Etymology

From Middle English aflot, from a- (on) + float.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01917033-s
Synset POS: s
