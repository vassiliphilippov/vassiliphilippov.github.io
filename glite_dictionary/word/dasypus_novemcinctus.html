Dasypus novemcinctus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsɪpəs noʊˌvɛmˈsɪŋktəs / (AmE), / ˌdasɪpəs nəʊˌvɛmˈkɪŋktəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the nine-banded armadillo, a mammal with nine hinged bands of bony plates, found from Texas to Paraguay
nine-banded armadillo (scientific name)

Examples

Scientists often study the Dasypus novemcinctus to understand the spread of leprosy in wildlife.
The range of Dasypus novemcinctus extends from the southern United States to South America.
Unlike other armadillos, the Dasypus novemcinctus is noted for its nine distinct armor bands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nine-banded armadillo - a mammal with nine movable bands of armor

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dasypus' (genus of armadillos) and 'novemcinctus' meaning 'nine-banded'.
