persicot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsɪˌkɒt / (BrE)
a sweet liqueur made from the kernels of apricots, nectarines, or similar fruits, blended with distilled spirits
apricot-kernel liqueur

Examples

After dinner, she offered her guests a glass of persicot as a sweet digestif.
The bartender recommended persicot for those looking to try something fruity and unique.
Traditional recipes for persicot call for the careful distillation of apricot or nectarine kernels.

Etymology

From French 'persicot', from 'pêche' (peach) or 'persique' (of Persia), with the suffix '-ot'.
