Thallophyta

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɑːləˌfaɪtə / (AmE), / θəˈlæləˌfaɪtə / (BrE)
(in former classifications) A major group of flowerless and seedless organisms, such as algae, fungi, and lichens, now considered a heterogeneous assemblage.
former classification of seedless, flowerless organisms
archaic medical

Examples

In older textbooks, Thallophyta included organisms like algae, fungi, and lichens.
Scientists no longer use Thallophyta as a formal classification, but the term appears in historical biology literature.
The kingdom Thallophyta once grouped together several diverse, non-flowering organisms.

Etymology

From Greek 'thallos' (a young shoot) + 'phyton' (plant).
