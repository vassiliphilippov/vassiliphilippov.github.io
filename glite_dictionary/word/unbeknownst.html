unbeknownst

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnbɪˈnoʊnst / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈnəʊnst / (BrE)
without someone knowing; unknown to (someone)
without someone's knowledge
archaic

Examples

Unbeknownst to her parents, she had already bought the ticket.
The keys had, unbeknownst to me, fallen behind the sofa.
Unbeknownst to the public, the company was planning a major merger.

Synset Examples

unbeknownst to me, she made all the arrangements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbeknown - without the knowledge of; unknown to

Etymology

Formed from 'unbeknown' + 'st', influenced by similar constructions in English.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnbɪˈnoʊnst / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈnəʊnst / (BrE)
(usually with 'to') happening or existing without someone's knowledge.
without someone's knowledge

Examples

Unbeknownst to the manager, the team had already finished the project ahead of schedule.
She left a gift on his desk, unbeknownst to anyone else in the office.
Unbeknownst to her parents, she had applied for colleges abroad.

Synset Examples

a crisis unbeknown to me
she had been ill for months, unbeknownst to the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbeknown - without someone's knowledge; unknown
  • Antonym: known - understood or recognized by someone

Etymology

19th century variant of 'unbeknown,' patterned after 'beknown,' from Middle English, from 'be-' + 'known.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
