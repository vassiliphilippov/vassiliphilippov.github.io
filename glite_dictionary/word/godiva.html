Godiva

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈdaɪvə / (AmE), / ɡəˈdiːvə / (BrE)
a legendary English noblewoman who, according to legend, rode naked through Coventry to persuade her husband to reduce taxes on the townspeople
legendary English noblewoman

Examples

Every year, the city celebrates Godiva with a festival reenacting her famous ride.
The legend of Godiva has inspired countless artists and writers over the centuries.
People still debate whether Godiva's ride through Coventry actually happened or is just a myth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Peeping Tom - a curious or prying person, especially one who spies on people in private situations

Etymology

From Latinized form of Old English Godgifu, meaning 'God's gift', borne by an 11th-century English noblewoman.
