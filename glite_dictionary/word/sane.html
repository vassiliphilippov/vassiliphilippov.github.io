sane

1. ADJ

/ seɪn / (AmE), / seɪn / (BrE)
having or showing good judgment; sensible and reasonable
having sound judgment

Examples

Her decision to leave the company was considered quite sane by her colleagues.
It's important to have a sane approach to managing stress at work.
After a heated argument, we needed a sane voice to calm things down.

Synset Examples

sane nuclear policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic
  • Synonym: sensible - chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence
  • Antonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'sanus' (healthy, sound).

2. ADJ

/ seɪn / (AmE), / seɪn / (BrE)
having a healthy mind and able to think normally; free from serious mental disorder
mentally healthy; rational

Examples

Despite the chaos, she remained remarkably sane throughout the ordeal.
After weeks of isolation, he questioned whether he was still sane.
Her explanation proved she was entirely sane, despite others' doubts.

Synset Examples

appears to be completely sane

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insane - mentally ill; not sane
  • Synonym: rational - able to think clearly and sensibly

Etymology

From Latin sanus (healthy, sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02080847-a
Synset POS: a
