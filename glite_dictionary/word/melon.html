melon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
A large, round or oval fruit with a hard rind and sweet, juicy flesh, typically eaten fresh; includes varieties such as cantaloupe, watermelon, and muskmelon.
a large, sweet fleshy fruit

Examples

She sliced the melon and served it as a refreshing dessert.
A ripe melon has a sweet aroma and yields slightly to pressure.
Farmers brought crates of melons to the local market in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watermelon - a large, round or oval fruit with green rind and sweet, juicy red pulp
  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a small, round melon with orange flesh and rough skin

Etymology

From French 'melon', from Latin 'melopepo', from Greek 'mēlopepōn' (apple-shaped melon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
a large, round fruit of the gourd family with a hard rind and sweet, juicy flesh, such as cantaloupe or watermelon
large sweet fruit with hard rind

Examples

She bought a ripe melon at the farmer's market for dessert.
A slice of chilled melon is refreshing on a hot summer day.
This variety of melon has a particularly sweet and fragrant flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watermelon - a large, round melon with green rind and red pulp
  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a variety of melon with orange flesh

Etymology

From Old French 'melon', from Latin 'melo', from Greek 'mēlon' (apple, fruit).
