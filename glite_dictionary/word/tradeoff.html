tradeoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdɒf / (BrE)
A balance achieved between two desirable but incompatible features; a compromise where one thing is given up in return for another.
a compromise between conflicting options

Examples

There is always a tradeoff between cost and quality in manufacturing.
She had to accept a tradeoff between work and family time.
Making the system faster involved a tradeoff in battery life.

Synset Examples

I faced a tradeoff between eating and buying my medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - an agreement in which each side concedes something

Etymology

Formed from the verb phrase 'trade off', meaning to exchange one thing for another; noun use since mid-20th century.
