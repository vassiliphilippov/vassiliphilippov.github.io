geographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒioʊˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the science of geography or to the features of the Earth's surface
relating to geography

Examples

The geographic distribution of this species is limited to South America.
Climate change will have different geographic impacts around the world.
She is conducting a geographic study of mountain ranges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geographical - relating to geography, alternative form

Etymology

From French *géographique*, from Greek *geōgraphikos* (of or pertaining to geography).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the natural features, population, industries, etc., of a region or regions; determined by geography.
relating to geography

Examples

The Arctic and Antarctic are important geographic regions for climate research.
This map highlights the geographic boundaries between different countries.
There are significant geographic differences in the distribution of various animal species.

Synset Examples

the north and south geographic poles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geographical - relating to geography (alternative form)

Etymology

From Greek geōgraphikos (geographical), from geōgraphiā (description of the earth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01473411-a
Synset POS: a
