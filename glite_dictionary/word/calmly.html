calmly

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑmli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmli / (BrE)
in a calm manner, showing self-control and not agitated, especially in difficult situations
in a calm manner

Examples

Despite the emergency, she answered the questions calmly and clearly.
He spoke calmly to the rioting students, hoping to restore order.
She calmly explained the situation, even though her heart was racing.

Synset Examples

he spoke calmly to the rioting students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacefully - in a peaceful and quiet manner
  • Antonym: nervously - in an anxious or agitated manner

Etymology

From 'calm' + '-ly', with 'calm' from Old French 'calme', from Italian 'calma', of Latin or Greek origin.

2. ADV

/ ˈkɑmli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmli / (BrE)
In a peaceful and untroubled manner; without showing anger, nervousness, or agitation.
in a peaceful, untroubled manner

Examples

She spoke calmly despite the chaos around her.
He handled the stressful situation calmly and professionally.
The teacher asked the children to wait calmly in line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacefully - in a peaceful manner
  • Antonym: angrily - in an angry manner

Etymology

From 'calm' + '-ly', with 'calm' from Old French 'calme', from Italian 'calma', from Late Latin 'calma' (calm, tranquility).
