one-handedness

1. NOUN

/ wʌn ˈhændɪdnɪs / (AmE), / wʌn ˈhændɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the condition or quality of using one hand predominantly or skillfully
use of one hand predominantly

Examples

Her one-handedness became an asset when she joined the wheelchair basketball team.
Despite his one-handedness, he mastered complex guitar pieces with ease.
Coaches often design drills to encourage or challenge one-handedness in players.

Synset Examples

One-handedness can be an advantage in certain sports.
He showed remarkable one-handedness in juggling.
Her one-handedness didn't stop her from playing the piano.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambidexterity - the ability to use both hands equally well
