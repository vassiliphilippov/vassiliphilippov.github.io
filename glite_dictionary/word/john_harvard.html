John Harvard

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈhɑrvərd / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈhɑːvəd / (BrE)
(1607–1638) An English-American clergyman and philanthropist who bequeathed his library and half of his estate to the college in Massachusetts that was later named Harvard University in his honor.
English-American clergyman, Harvard University namesake

Examples

John Harvard is commemorated by a statue in Harvard Yard, a popular attraction for visitors.
Many people do not realize that John Harvard was only thirty-one when he died.
The college was renamed Harvard University in honor of John Harvard's generous donation.
