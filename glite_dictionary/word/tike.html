tike

1. NOUN

/ taɪk / (AmE), / taɪk / (BrE)
a young child, especially a small boy; sometimes used as a term of mild reproach or affection
young child; small boy
archaic

Examples

The little tike ran across the yard with a toy truck.
She scolded the mischievous tike for tracking mud into the house.
Even as a tike, he loved to tell tall tales to anyone who would listen.

Synset Examples

she writes books for children
they're just kids
‘tiddler’ is a British term for youngster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty
  • Synonym: tyke - a small child, often used affectionately or humorously

Etymology

Related to 'tyke', of uncertain origin; possibly Scandinavian.

2. NOUN

/ taɪk / (AmE), / taɪk / (BrE)
an uncouth, ill-bred person, especially one lacking in manners or refinement
uncouth or ill-bred person

Examples

The old man called the unruly boy a tike for his bad behavior.
She refused to associate with someone she considered a tike.
In the pub, the drunk tike shouted insults at everyone around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lout - an uncouth or aggressive person
  • Synonym: boor - a rude or unmannerly person

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *tike* (cur, dog), of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian *tik* (bitch).
