tympanum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpənəm / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpənəm / (BrE)
the main cavity of the middle ear, situated between the eardrum and the inner ear
middle ear cavity
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's tympanum for signs of infection.
Damage to the tympanum can affect a person's ability to hear.
In mammals, the ossicles transmit vibrations from the eardrum to the tympanum and eventually to the inner ear.

Etymology

From Latin 'tympanum' (drum), from Greek 'τύμπανον' (tympanon, drum).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpənəm / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpənəm / (BrE)
the thin membrane in the ear that vibrates in response to sound waves; the eardrum
membrane in the ear; eardrum
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the tympanum carefully for signs of infection.
A sudden loud noise can damage the tympanum, leading to hearing problems.
Vibrations from the air are transmitted through the tympanum to the inner ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eardrum - the membrane in the ear that transmits sound vibrations

Etymology

From Latin 'tympanum', from Greek 'týmpanon' (drum, kettledrum).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpənəm / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpənəm / (BrE)
A large hemispherical brass or copper percussion instrument with a drumhead that can be tuned by adjusting its tension; also called a kettledrum or timpani.
large tunable percussion instrument; kettledrum

Examples

The conductor emphasized the deep resonance of the tympanum in the orchestra's opening piece.
A skilled percussionist can produce a wide range of tones on a single tympanum.
During the ceremonial procession, the tympanum provided a dramatic, thunderous beat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timpani - plural form and synonym for kettledrum; set of pitched percussion drums
  • Synonym: kettledrum - another term for tympanum, the percussion instrument

Etymology

From Latin tympanum (drum, tambourine), from Ancient Greek τύμπανον (týmpanon, drum, kettledrum).
