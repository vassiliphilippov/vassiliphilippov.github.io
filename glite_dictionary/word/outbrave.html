to outbrave

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈbreɪv / (AmE), / aʊtˈbreɪv / (BrE)
to show more bravery than (someone else); to face or resist with superior courage
to surpass in bravery or courage

Examples

Despite being outnumbered, she outbraved her opponents during the debate.
The small dog outbraved the larger animals to defend its owner.
He outbraved his fears and stepped onto the stage to perform.

Synset Examples

He outbraved the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • Antonym: to succumb - to give in or yield to something

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (meaning 'surpassing') + 'brave'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01119637-v

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈbreɪv / (AmE), / aʊtˈbreɪv / (BrE)
to be braver than someone; to show more courage than another person
be braver than someone

Examples

She managed to outbrave all her competitors during the difficult climb.
The soldiers tried to outbrave each other on the battlefield.
No one could outbrave the old captain in the face of danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Synonym: to outshine - to surpass in excellence or achievement

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning surpass) + 'brave'.

Synset POS: v
