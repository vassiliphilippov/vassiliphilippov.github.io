Methodist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.ə.dɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əd.ɪst / (BrE)
a member of a Christian denomination that follows the teachings of John Wesley and the practices of the Methodist Church
member of the Methodist Church

Examples

Her grandmother was a devoted Methodist who attended services every Sunday.
Many early American settlers were Methodists and contributed to the spread of the church.
The Methodist tradition emphasizes social justice and outreach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wesleyan - a follower of John Wesley's religious teachings
  • Antonym: Baptist - member of a Protestant Christian denomination emphasizing baptism

Etymology

From method + -ist, in reference to the 'methods' of religious practice stressed by early followers; later associated with John Wesley.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.ə.dɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.ə.dɪst / (BrE)
A member of a Protestant Christian denomination founded on the teachings of John Wesley and Charles Wesley, emphasizing practical faith and social justice.
member of a Protestant Christian denomination

Examples

As a lifelong Methodist, she volunteers regularly at her local church.
The Methodists held a community dinner to raise money for charity.
John Wesley is recognized as the founder of the Methodist movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Presbyterian - member of a Protestant Christian denomination governed by elders
  • Antonym: Catholic - member of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From 'method' and the suffix '-ist', initially used to describe followers of John and Charles Wesley's methodical approach to faith.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɛθədɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɛθədɪst / (BrE)
of or relating to the branch of Protestant Christianity that follows the teachings of John and Charles Wesley.
relating to the Methodist church or its beliefs

Examples

The church has a strong Methodist tradition that shapes its community services.
She attended a renowned Methodist seminary to complete her theological studies.
The charitable spirit is a key aspect of Methodist belief.

Synset Examples

Methodist theology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodist - a member of the Methodist church

Etymology

From Methodist, the name given to followers of the Wesley brothers, originally referring to their methodical practice of religion.
