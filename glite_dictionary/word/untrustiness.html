untrustiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtrʌstɪnɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtrʌstɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being untrustworthy; the trait of not deserving trust or confidence.
lack of trustworthiness

Examples

Her untrustiness became apparent when she lied about her whereabouts.
The deal fell through because of the untrustiness of the company involved.
He was criticized for his untrustiness in both business and personal matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untrustworthiness - the quality of being unworthy of trust
  • Antonym: trustworthiness - the quality of being trustworthy; deserving trust

Etymology

From un- (not) + trust + -iness (noun-forming suffix).
