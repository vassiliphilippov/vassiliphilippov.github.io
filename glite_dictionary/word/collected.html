collected

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of things) brought together into one place or group; assembled.
gathered in one place or group

Examples

A library may house the collected works of important authors.
The museum displayed a collected array of ancient artifacts.
Her collected poems were published in a single volume.

Synset Examples

the collected works of Milton
the gathered folds of the skirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gathered - brought together or assembled; collected
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) calm and in control of oneself, especially in a difficult situation.
calm; self-possessed

Examples

Despite the chaos around her, she remained collected and handled the emergency with ease.
He gave a collected response to the unexpected question, showing no signs of nervousness.
Her collected demeanor reassured the team during the stressful meeting.

Synset Examples

the witness remained collected throughout the cross-examination
perfectly poised and sure of himself
more self-contained and more dependable than many of the early frontiersmen
strong and self-possessed in the face of trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composed - calm and in control of one's emotions
  • Synonym: unflappable - not easily upset or disconcerted
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • Antonym: distraught - deeply upset and agitated

Etymology

Past participle of 'collect', from Latin 'collectus', meaning 'gathered together'; figurative sense of 'gathering one's faculties' from 17th century.
