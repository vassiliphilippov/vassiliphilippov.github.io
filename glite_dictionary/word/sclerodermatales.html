Sclerodermatales

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˌdɜrməˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / sklɪərəˌdɜːməˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi characterized by a peridium surrounding a gleba, sometimes classified in subclass Homobasidiomycetes
order of fungi with peridium and gleba
medical

Examples

The Sclerodermatales are distinguished by their thick-skinned fruiting bodies.
Researchers discovered several new species within the order Sclerodermatales during the forest survey.
The classification of Sclerodermatales has been revised based on recent genetic studies.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'sclero-' (hard), 'derma' (skin), and '-tales' (order).
