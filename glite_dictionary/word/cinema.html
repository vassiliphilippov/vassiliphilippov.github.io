cinema

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmə / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmə / (BrE)
A building where films are shown to the public; a movie theater.
movie theater

Examples

We decided to meet at the cinema to watch the latest blockbuster.
The old cinema downtown was renovated into a luxury theater.
She works at a cinema selling tickets and popcorn on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movie theater - a building where movies are shown to the public, chiefly American term

Etymology

From French *cinéma*, short for *cinématographe*, from Greek *kinēma* (movement) + *-graphos* (writing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmə / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmə / (BrE)
a medium that uses moving pictures to tell stories or present information; motion pictures as an art or entertainment form
motion pictures as a medium or art form

Examples

Many people consider French cinema to be among the finest in the world.
Advancements in technology have transformed the way we experience cinema.
Documentaries have become an important genre within the art of cinema.

Synset Examples

theater pieces transferred to celluloid
this story would be good cinema
film coverage of sporting events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a motion picture; the art or industry of making movies
  • Antonym: television - a system for transmitting visual images and sound to screens

Etymology

From French 'cinéma', shortened from 'cinématographe', from Greek 'kínēma' (movement).
