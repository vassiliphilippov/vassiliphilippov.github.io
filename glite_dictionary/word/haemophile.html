haemophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːməˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhiːməʊˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a person who has hemophilia and is prone to uncontrollable bleeding
person with hemophilia
medical british

Examples

Since birth, her brother has been a haemophile and receives regular medical treatment.
The summer camp provided special activities to ensure every haemophile could participate safely.
Doctors explained that being a haemophile requires careful attention to any injuries, however minor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemophiliac - a person with hemophilia

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'philos' (loving), by analogy with 'hemophilia.'
