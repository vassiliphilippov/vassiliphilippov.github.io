clevis

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛvɪs / (AmE), / ˈklɛvɪs / (BrE)
A U-shaped metal fastener with holes at the ends, through which a bolt or pin is inserted to complete the coupling, commonly used to connect a drawbar to machinery such as a plow, wagon, or trailer.
U-shaped metal fastener for coupling

Examples

The trailer was attached to the tractor using a sturdy clevis.
Make sure the pin is secure when assembling the clevis to the drawbar.
A broken clevis can cause the machinery to become detached during operation.
