pale

1. ADJ

/ peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
(of a person's face or complexion) abnormally lacking in color, typically because of illness, shock, or fear.
lacking normal color, often from illness or distress

Examples

She looked pale after hearing the shocking news.
His pale cheeks made everyone worry about his health.
Standing under the harsh lights, her face appeared unusually pale and drawn.

Synset Examples

the pallid face of the invalid
her wan face suddenly flushed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallid - having an unhealthy pale appearance
  • Synonym: wan - pale and appearing tired or ill

Etymology

From Latin 'pallidus' (pale).
