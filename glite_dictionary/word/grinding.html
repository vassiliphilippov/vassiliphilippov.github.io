grinding

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which rock particles are worn down by friction caused by water, wind, or ice
wearing down of rocks by friction

Examples

The slow grinding of rocks by glaciers shapes many mountain valleys.
Erosion by wind involves the grinding of sand particles against rock surfaces.
Over time, the constant grinding of water smooths out sharp corners of stones in the riverbed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
a harsh, scraping, or grating sound, often produced by friction between hard surfaces
a harsh or grating sound

Examples

The grinding of the gears made it clear that something was wrong with the car.
All night, she was kept awake by the grinding coming from the construction site.
He winced at the loud grinding of metal against metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grate - to make a harsh grinding noise
  • Synonym: screech - a loud, harsh, piercing sound

Etymology

From the verb 'grind', from Old English *grindan* (to crush, reduce to small particles).

/ ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the small pieces or powder produced by the process of grinding something
material resulting from grinding
