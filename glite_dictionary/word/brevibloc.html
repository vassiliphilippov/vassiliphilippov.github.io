Brevibloc

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛvɪˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈbrɛvɪˌblɒk / (BrE)
A trademarked intravenous beta blocker used primarily to treat cardiac arrhythmias, known for its short duration of action.
short-acting intravenous beta blocker (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor administered Brevibloc intravenously to control the patient's rapid heart rate.
Because Brevibloc acts quickly and wears off fast, it's ideal for emergency cardiac situations.
One possible side effect of Brevibloc is a significant drop in blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esmolol - short-acting beta blocker used in cardiac arrhythmias (generic name for Brevibloc)
