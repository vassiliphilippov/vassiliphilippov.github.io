enraged

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈreɪdʒd / (AmE), / ɪnˈreɪdʒd / (BrE)
extremely angry; filled with intense rage.
extremely angry

Examples

The enraged customer demanded to speak to the manager immediately.
She was so enraged by the unfair decision that she nearly quit her job.
An enraged mob gathered outside the building, shouting demands for justice.

Synset Examples

the enraged bull attached
furious about the accident
a furious scowl
infuriated onlookers charged the police who were beating the boy
could not control the maddened crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furious - feeling or showing strong anger
  • Synonym: infuriated - extremely angry; filled with fury
  • Synonym: maddened - made very angry or insane with anger
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or strong emotion

Etymology

Past participle of 'enrage', from Old French 'enragier', from Latin 'in-' (in) + 'rabia' (rage).
