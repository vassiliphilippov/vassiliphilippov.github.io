radiolysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˈɑləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪˈɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the decomposition of molecules caused by exposure to radiation
molecular decomposition by radiation
medical

Examples

The radiolysis of water can produce reactive oxygen species in biological systems.
Scientists studied the effects of radiolysis on organic compounds in the laboratory.
The process of radiolysis plays an important role in nuclear reactor chemistry.

Etymology

From radio- (relating to radiation) + Greek lusis (loosening, dissolution).
