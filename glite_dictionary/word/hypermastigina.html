Hypermastigina

1. NOUN

/ haɪpərˌmæstɪˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˌmastɪˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of cellulose-producing flagellate protozoa, often found in the guts of wood-eating insects.
group of cellulose-producing flagellate protozoa

Examples

Some termites rely on Hypermastigina in their digestive tracts to break down cellulose from wood.
Researchers observed that Hypermastigina can efficiently convert plant fibers into usable nutrients for their hosts.
The symbiotic relationship between Hypermastigina and wood-eating insects is essential for the insects’ survival.

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over, excessive) + mastix (whip), referring to their numerous flagella.
