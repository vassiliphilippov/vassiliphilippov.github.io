woodscrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.skru / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.skruː / (BrE)
a metal screw with a pointed end and coarse threads, designed to be driven into wood with a screwdriver
pointed screw for wood

Examples

He used a woodscrew to fasten the hinge securely onto the door frame.
Make sure you choose a woodscrew that's long enough for the thickness of the board.
The shelf collapsed because the wrong type of woodscrew was used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screw - a metal fastener with a helical ridge
