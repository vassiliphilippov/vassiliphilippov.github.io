asclepiadaceous

1. ADJ

/ əˌskliːpiəˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / əˌskliːpɪəˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of plants of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae)
relating to milkweed family of plants

Examples

Several asclepiadaceous species are cultivated for their unique flowers and medicinal properties.
Many asclepiadaceous plants produce a milky sap that can be toxic to livestock.
The asclepiadaceous group includes familiar genera such as Asclepias and Ceropegia.

Etymology

From modern Latin Asclepiadaceae (the family name), after Asclepias (milkweed genus), from Greek Asklēpias (name of a plant).
