organization

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging and coordinating the elements of a business or related activity effectively; the action of organizing
the act of organizing a business or activity

Examples

He oversaw the organization of the company's annual conference.
The organization of the project took several months to complete.
Effective organization of resources is crucial for any new business venture.

Synset Examples

he was brought in to supervise the organization of a new department

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the act of arranging or being arranged
  • Antonym: disorganization - the state of being disorganized, lack of order

Etymology

From French 'organisation', from Medieval Latin 'organizatio', from Latin 'organum' (instrument, tool).
