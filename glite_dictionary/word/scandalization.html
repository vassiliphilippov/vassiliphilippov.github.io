scandalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌskændəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌskændəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of causing public shock or outrage, especially by violating standards of morality or propriety
the act of causing scandal or outrage

Examples

The scandalization of the celebrity shocked her devoted fans across the country.
He was deeply troubled by the public scandalization that followed the allegations.
Media coverage contributed to the scandalization of political leaders during the controversy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandal - an action or event causing public outrage or censure
  • Synonym: outrage - an act causing strong moral indignation

Etymology

From scandalize + -ation; ultimately from Greek *skandalon* (snare, offense).

2. NOUN

/ ˌskæn.də.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌskæn.də.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the state of being shocked or offended, especially by behavior regarded as improper or immoral
state of being shocked by impropriety

Examples

Her scandalization was apparent when she discovered the fraud within the organization.
The press reported on the public's scandalization at the politician's actions.
Such behavior led to widespread scandalization among the city's residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or surprising event or experience
  • Synonym: outrage - a strong feeling of anger or shock about something perceived as unjust or immoral
  • Antonym: acceptance - the process of accepting or tolerating behavior

Etymology

From scandalize + -ation, ultimately from Latin scandālum (stumbling block, offense).
