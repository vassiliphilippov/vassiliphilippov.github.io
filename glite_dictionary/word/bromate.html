bromate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the bromate ion, BrO₃⁻
chemical compound with bromate ion

Examples

Potassium bromate is often used as a flour improver in baking.
Scientists detected traces of bromate in the treated water sample.
Exposure to high levels of bromate can be harmful to human health.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bromide - a compound containing the bromide ion

Etymology

From 'bromine', element name, plus the suffix '-ate' denoting a salt or ester of an acid.

2. VERB

/ ˈbroʊ.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.meɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to react with bromine, especially by incorporating bromine atoms into a compound.
react with bromine (chemistry)

Examples

Many industrial processes bromate organic compounds to produce flame retardants.
Scientists observed how easily the solution could bromate under controlled laboratory conditions.
To enhance effectiveness, the water was deliberately bromated before further analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chlorinate - to react with chlorine, especially by introducing chlorine atoms into a compound

Etymology

From bromine, with the verb-forming suffix -ate, modeled after similar chemical nomenclature.

3. VERB

/ ˈbroʊˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊˌmeɪt / (BrE)
to treat or combine with bromine, especially by adding bromine to another substance
treat or combine with bromine

Examples

Some water treatment facilities bromate the water to disinfect it.
Industrial processes often bromate organic compounds to produce specialized chemicals.
The lab technician will bromate the sample as part of the experiment.

Etymology

Formed from 'bromine' (from Greek 'brōmos', meaning stench) plus the verb-forming suffix '-ate'.
