closed-in

1. ADJ

/ kloʊzd ˈɪn / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈɪn / (BrE)
(of a space) surrounded or enclosed on all sides so as to be blocked against entry or exposed only to the interior.
surrounded; blocked against entry

Examples

The sunroom feels cozy because it is closed-in with glass walls.
She likes having a closed-in garden to keep out the wind.
They converted their porch into a closed-in space for year-round use.

Synset Examples

a closed porch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - not enclosed or blocked; allowing entry
