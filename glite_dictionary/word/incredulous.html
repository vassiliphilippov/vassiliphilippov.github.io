incredulous

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkrɛdʒələs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɛdjʊləs / (BrE)
unwilling or unable to believe something; showing disbelief
unwilling to believe; skeptical

Examples

She gave him an incredulous look when he told her the unbelievable story.
His incredulous reaction made it clear he doubted every word.
Many people were incredulous at the announcement, struggling to accept it as true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - not easily convinced; having doubts or reservations
  • Antonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; credulous
  • Antonym: credulous - having or showing too great a readiness to believe things

Etymology

From Latin incredulus (unbelieving), from in- (not) + credulus (believing).
