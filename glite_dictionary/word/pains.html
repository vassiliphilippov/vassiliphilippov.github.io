pains

1. NOUN

/ peɪnz / (AmE), / peɪnz / (BrE)
Great care or effort taken to achieve something.
careful effort or diligence

Examples

She took great pains to ensure the report was accurate and thorough.
He goes to extraordinary pains to make his guests feel comfortable.
The decorator took pains with every detail of the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or care

Etymology

Plural of 'pain,' from Old French 'peine,' from Latin 'poena' (penalty, punishment).
