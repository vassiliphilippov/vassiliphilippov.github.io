cut-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌt ɪn / (BrE)
A still image inserted into a film sequence that interrupts the flow of the main action.
film: still image interrupting action

Examples

The director used a cut-in to show the character’s reaction more clearly.
A sudden cut-in of a childhood photograph brought emotional impact to the film.
Editors often add a cut-in to provide necessary information without dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutaway - a film shot that cuts from the main action to a secondary action and then returns

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌt ɪn / (BrE)
A local announcement or piece of content inserted into a network program, typically interrupting the regular broadcast.
local broadcast interruption; network programming insertion

Examples

The station aired a cut-in with breaking local news during the national broadcast.
Viewers were surprised when a cut-in interrupted the football game for a weather alert.
Each cut-in lasted only a few minutes before returning to the regular program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - an act of stopping something for a short period
