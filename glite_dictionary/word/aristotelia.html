Aristotelia

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəstəˈtiːliə / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈtiːlɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of shrubs or small trees native to Australia, New Zealand, and western South America
genus of shrubs and small trees

Examples

The botanical garden cultivated several species of Aristotelia for educational purposes.
Researchers discovered a new variety within the genus Aristotelia in southern Chile.
Birds are attracted to the berries produced by some Aristotelia species.

Etymology

Named after Aristotle (384–322 BC), the Greek philosopher, reflecting a tradition of naming genera after prominent figures.
