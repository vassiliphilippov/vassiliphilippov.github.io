laundry

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔndɹi / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndɹi / (BrE)
clothes, linens, or other washable items that are to be washed, are being washed, or have just been washed
clothes or linens to be washed

Examples

I need to do the laundry before my trip tomorrow.
She left a pile of dirty laundry on the bathroom floor.
The hotel offers a same-day laundry service for its guests.

Etymology

From Old French laverie (place for washing), from laver (to wash), from Latin lavare.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔndɹi / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndri / (BrE)
a place or business where clothes, linens, and other textiles are washed and ironed
place where clothes are washed and ironed

Examples

The hotel offers a laundry service for guests who need their clothes cleaned.
She took her shirts to the local laundry to have them washed and pressed.
Many hospitals have an on-site laundry to handle their large amount of linens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laundromat - self-service place with machines for washing and drying clothes

Etymology

From Old French *lavanderie*, from *lavander* (to wash), from Latin *lavare* (to wash).
