bureaucratically

1. ADV

/ bjʊˌroʊkrəˈtɪkli / (AmE), / bjʊəˌrəʊkrəˈtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner related to bureaucracy; following bureaucratic methods or procedures.
in a bureaucratic manner

Examples

The application process is bureaucratically complex and can take several months to complete.
Some organizations are bureaucratically structured, making quick decisions almost impossible.
The new regulations were implemented bureaucratically, with endless paperwork and strict adherence to protocol.

Synset Examples

it's bureaucratically complicated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucratic - relating to bureaucracy or official procedures

Etymology

From 'bureaucratic' + '-ally'.

2. ADV

/ bjʊˌroʊkrəˈtɪkli / (AmE), / bjʊəˌrɒkrəˈtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is characteristic of bureaucracy, especially with excessive red tape or rigid procedures.
in a bureaucratic manner

Examples

The process was handled bureaucratically, causing months of unnecessary delay.
Decisions were made bureaucratically, following strict rules rather than considering individual cases.
The new policy was bureaucratically enforced, frustrating many employees.

Synset Examples

his bureaucratically petty behavior annoyed her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucratic - relating to bureaucracy; overly concerned with procedures

Etymology

From 'bureaucratic' + '-ally', ultimately from French 'bureaucratie'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00282529-r
Synset POS: r
