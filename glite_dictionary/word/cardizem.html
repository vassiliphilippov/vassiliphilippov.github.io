Cardizem

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdɪˌzɛm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪˌzɛm / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used to treat hypertension, angina, or heart failure; the brand name for diltiazem.
trademarked drug for hypertension and angina
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Cardizem to help control her high blood pressure.
Patients with chronic angina often benefit from taking Cardizem.
My cardiologist adjusted my dose of Cardizem after my last checkup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diltiazem - a calcium channel blocker used to treat hypertension and angina

Etymology

Brand name; trademark for diltiazem, derived from the chemical name.
