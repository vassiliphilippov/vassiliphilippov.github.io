Parula americana

1. NOUN

/ pəˌruːlə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / pəˌruːlə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
a small North American warbler with bluish-grey plumage and a yellow throat and breast, commonly known as the Northern Parula.
North American warbler (Northern Parula)

Examples

Birdwatchers often seek the Parula americana during spring migration.
The bright throat patch helps identify Parula americana in dense forest canopies.
In eastern forests, the Parula americana prefers to nest in hanging Spanish moss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Northern Parula - North American warbler with bluish-grey plumage and yellow throat

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Parula and the species epithet americana, meaning 'American'.
