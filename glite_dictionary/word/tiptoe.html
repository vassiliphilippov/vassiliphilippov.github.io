tiptoe

1. ADV

/ ˈtɪpˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɪptəʊ / (BrE)
on the toes or as if on the toes, often in order to move quietly or reach something high
on one's toes; quietly or carefully

Examples

She waited tiptoe outside the room, hoping not to wake the baby.
The cat crept tiptoe across the kitchen floor.
He stood tiptoe to reach the top shelf.

Synset Examples

standing tiptoe

Etymology

From Middle English tipto (tip of the toe).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪpˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɪptəʊ / (BrE)
to walk quietly and carefully on the toes, usually to avoid making noise or to move stealthily
walk on tiptoes

Examples

She tried to tiptoe past the sleeping baby without making a sound.
He tiptoed into the kitchen for a midnight snack.
The children were tiptoeing around the classroom during the surprise party preparations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and quietly, especially in order to avoid being noticed
  • Synonym: sneak - to move in a secretive or stealthy manner

Etymology

From the noun 'tiptoe', formed from 'tip' (the end) and 'toe'. Verb use dates from the late 16th century.
