parabolograph

1. NOUN

/ pəˈræbəloʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / pəˈrabələˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
An old instrument designed for drawing accurate parabolic curves.
historical instrument for drawing parabolas
archaic

Examples

The mathematician used a parabolograph to sketch precise curves for his experiments.
Historical museums sometimes display a parabolograph alongside other antique drafting tools.
Before computer software, a parabolograph was essential for drafting engineers working with parabolic shapes.

Etymology

From Greek 'parabolē' (parabola) + '-graph' (writing instrument).
