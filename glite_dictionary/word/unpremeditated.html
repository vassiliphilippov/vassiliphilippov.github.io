unpremeditated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌpriməˈdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌpriːməˈdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an action or remark) done without being planned or thought about beforehand.
not preplanned; spontaneous

Examples

Her apology was completely unpremeditated and came straight from the heart.
The actor delivered an unpremeditated speech that surprised the audience.
An unpremeditated joke eased the tension in the meeting.

Synset Examples

asked an unpremeditated question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spontaneous - done without being planned or forced
  • Antonym: premeditated - planned or thought out beforehand

Etymology

From un- (not) + premeditated (from Latin prae- 'before' + meditari 'to consider').

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.priːˈmɛd.ɪ.teɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.priːˈmɛd.ɪ.teɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of an action) not planned in advance; spontaneous.
not planned in advance

Examples

His apology seemed entirely unpremeditated and sincere.
The artist's unpremeditated brushstrokes gave the painting a sense of life and spontaneity.
The judge ruled that it was an unpremeditated act rather than a planned crime.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: premeditated - planned in advance
  • Synonym: spontaneous - performed without planning

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'premeditated' (from Latin prae- 'before' + meditari 'to contemplate').
