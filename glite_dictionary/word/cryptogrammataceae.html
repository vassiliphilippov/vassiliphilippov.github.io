Cryptogrammataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˌɡræməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˌɡræməˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns, formerly regarded as a separate family within Polypodiaceae in some classification systems.
family of ferns (taxonomy)
medical

Examples

The Cryptogrammataceae are mostly found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Some taxonomists separate Cryptogrammataceae from the larger family Polypodiaceae.
Recent studies suggest that Cryptogrammataceae may be more closely related to other fern families than previously thought.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'cryptogramma' (hidden letter, a genus of ferns) + '-aceae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˌɡræməˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˌɡræməˈteɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns that is sometimes distinguished as separate from Polypodiaceae in certain botanical classification systems.
a family of ferns in some classifications

Examples

The Cryptogrammataceae are commonly known as rockbrake ferns and are found in various parts of the world.
Recent studies have debated whether Cryptogrammataceae should remain a distinct family or be merged with Polypodiaceae.
Botanists collected several species that belong to the Cryptogrammataceae family during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - a large family of ferns, sometimes split into smaller families

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus name Cryptogramma and the standard suffix -aceae for plant families.
