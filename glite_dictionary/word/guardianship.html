guardianship

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑr.di.ənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.di.ən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or responsibility of protecting or managing someone or something, especially to ensure safety or well-being
the responsibility of protecting or managing

Examples

Under her guardianship, the children always felt safe and cared for.
The museum's collection has flourished thanks to the guardianship of dedicated curators.
Legal guardianship was granted to his aunt after his parents' accident.

Synset Examples

he is in the care of a bodyguard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe
  • Synonym: care - the process of looking after someone or something

Etymology

From 'guardian' (Old French gardein, from garder 'to guard') + '-ship' (denoting state or condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdiənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdɪənʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or responsibility of being a guardian; the care and protection of someone, especially a minor, by a legally appointed person
duty or office of being a guardian

Examples

After her parents died, her aunt applied for guardianship of the children.
The court transferred guardianship to a responsible adult.
He took his guardianship duties very seriously and ensured the child's well-being.

Synset Examples

he left his car in my keeping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custody - the protective care or guardianship of someone or something
  • Antonym: ward - a person, especially a minor, under the care of a guardian

Etymology

From guardian + -ship, denoting state or office.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
