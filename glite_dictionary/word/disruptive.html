disruptive

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
Causing or tending to cause unrest, disorder, or disturbance; characterized by insubordination or interruption to normal activity.
causing disorder or disturbance

Examples

The protest turned disruptive, forcing the police to intervene and restore order.
His disruptive behavior in class made it hard for others to concentrate.
Security was called after a disruptive incident broke out in the audience.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - well-arranged, peaceful, or behaving in a controlled way
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control; disorderly or disruptive
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion

Etymology

From Latin disruptus, past participle of disrumpere (‘to break apart’).
