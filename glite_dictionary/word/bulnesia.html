Bulnesia

1. NOUN

/ bʊlˈniːziə / (AmE), / bʊlˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees and shrubs native to South America, some species of which are known as palo santo and valued for their fragrant wood.
genus of South American trees (palo santo)

Examples

The aromatic wood of Bulnesia is highly valued for incense and traditional medicine.
Scientists are studying the ecological impact of Bulnesia plantations in arid regions.
Several species within the Bulnesia genus are threatened by overharvesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palo santo - aromatic wood from several South American trees, especially in the genus Bulnesia

Etymology

From Latinized form of 'Bulnes', in honor of Manuel Bulnes, a Chilean statesman and president.

2. NOUN

/ bʊlˈniːziə / (AmE), / bʊlˈniːziə / (BrE)
A small genus of trees and shrubs native to South America, some species of which are commonly known as palo santo and valued for their fragrant wood.
a genus of South American trees or shrubs

Examples

The fragrant wood of Bulnesia is often used in perfumes and incense.
Several species within the Bulnesia genus are known for their medicinal properties.
Deforestation threatens wild populations of Bulnesia in South America.

Related Terms
