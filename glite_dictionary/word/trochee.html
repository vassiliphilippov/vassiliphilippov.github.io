trochee

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊki / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊki / (BrE)
A metrical foot consisting of a stressed syllable followed by an unstressed syllable.
metrical foot: stressed then unstressed

Examples

The word 'poet' is a classic example of a trochee.
In scansion, a trochee is marked with a falling pattern of stress.
Shakespeare often used trochees for dramatic emphasis in his plays.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iamb - metrical foot: unstressed then stressed

Etymology

From Greek 'trokhaios' (running, trochee), relating to 'trekhein' (to run).
