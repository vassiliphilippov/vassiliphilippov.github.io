forensics

1. NOUN

/ fəˈrɛnsɪks / (AmE), / fəˈrɛnsɪks / (BrE)
the use of scientific methods and techniques in the investigation of crimes
science in crime investigations

Examples

Advances in forensics have made it easier to solve decades-old murder cases.
She studied forensics in college to become a crime scene analyst.
DNA evidence is one of the most important tools in modern forensics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminalistics - scientific study and analysis of crime evidence
  • Synonym: criminology - the scientific study of crime and criminals (broader field)

Etymology

From Latin 'forensis' (of the forum, public), originally relating to legal or court matters.
