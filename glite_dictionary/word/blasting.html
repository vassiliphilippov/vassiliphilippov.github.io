blasting

1. ADJ

/ ˈblæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
causing injury, destruction, or blight, especially with sudden violence or intensity.
causing injury or destruction violently

Examples

The blasting winds destroyed several trees during the storm.
Farmers were worried about the blasting effects of the unexpected frost on their crops.
The blasting criticism from the media left the company struggling to recover its reputation.

Synset Examples

the blasting effects of the intense cold on the budding fruit
the blasting force of the wind blowing sharp needles of sleet in our faces
a ruinous war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruinous - causing great damage or destruction
  • Antonym: benign - gentle; not harmful

Etymology

Present participle of "blast" (from Old English blǣstan, to blow or smite).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) extremely loud, harsh, and penetrating, often causing discomfort.
unpleasantly loud and penetrating

Examples

She turned up the stereo, and the blasting music woke everyone in the house.
I had to cover my ears to escape the blasting horns during rush hour.
The festival was filled with blasting speakers that could be heard blocks away.

Synset Examples

the blaring noise of trumpets
shut our ears against the blasting music from his car radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blaring - very loud and harsh in sound
  • Synonym: deafening - so loud as to make hearing difficult

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'blast', meaning to make a loud noise or explosion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01455966-s
Synset POS: s
