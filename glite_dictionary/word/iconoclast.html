iconoclast

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈkɑnəˌklæst / (AmE), / aɪˈkɒnəklæst / (BrE)
a person who attacks or criticizes cherished beliefs or established institutions
someone who challenges traditional beliefs or institutions

Examples

As an outspoken iconoclast, she often challenges the status quo in her articles.
The artist gained fame for being an iconoclast in a field dominated by tradition.
His iconoclast views made him unpopular among conservative politicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to orthodox doctrine
  • Antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports established customs or beliefs

Etymology

From Greek eikonoklastēs (image-breaker): eikōn (image) + klan (to break).

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈkɑnəˌklæst / (AmE), / aɪˈkɒnəˌklæst / (BrE)
A person who destroys images, especially those used in religious worship.
person who destroys religious images

Examples

During the Byzantine Empire, many iconoclasts destroyed religious artworks in churches.
The actions of the iconoclast were considered controversial by many worshippers.
Some historians believe the rise of iconoclasts led to lasting changes in church decor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: image-breaker - a person who destroys religious images
  • Antonym: iconophile - a person who supports the veneration of icons

Etymology

From Greek eikonoklastēs, from eikōn (image) + klastēs (breaker).
