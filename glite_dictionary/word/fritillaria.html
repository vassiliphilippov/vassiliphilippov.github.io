Fritillaria

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪlɪˈɛriə / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪlɪˈɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the lily family, often with bell-shaped, spotted flowers, commonly known as fritillaries.
a genus of flowering plants (fritillary)

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the unique beauty of the Fritillaria in their spring displays.
The checkered petals of the Fritillaria make it easily recognizable among other bulbous plants.
Some species of Fritillaria are valued for their unusual shapes and vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fritillary - any plant of the genus Fritillaria

Etymology

From Latin 'fritillus' (dice-box), referring to the spotted pattern of the flowers.
