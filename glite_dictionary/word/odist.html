odist

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.dɪst / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.dɪst / (BrE)
a poet who composes or writes odes
poet who writes odes

Examples

The renowned odist crafted verses that celebrated the beauty of nature.
Keats is often admired as a master odist for his celebrated works.
As an odist, she revived the classical form of the ode in modern poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poet - a person who writes poems

Etymology

From French 'odiste', from 'ode' (a lyric poem), ultimately from Greek 'ōidē' (song).
