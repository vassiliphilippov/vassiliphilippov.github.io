frothy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑːθi / (AmE), / ˈfrɒθi / (BrE)
(of a liquid) containing many small bubbles; emitting or covered with foam or froth as from carbonation or fermentation.
full of bubbles or foam

Examples

The waiter poured a glass of frothy beer for each guest at the table.
After shaking the bottle, the soda became extremely frothy when opened.
She enjoyed a frothy cappuccino while reading her morning paper.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foamy - full of or covered with foam
  • Synonym: bubbly - full of bubbles; effervescent

Etymology

From froth + -y, formed after the Middle English word 'frothe' (foam or bubbles), from Old Norse 'froða'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑːθi / (AmE), / ˈfrɒθi / (BrE)
full of high spirits, excitement, or lightheartedness; lacking depth or seriousness.
full of high spirits or excitement

Examples

The party had a distinctly frothy atmosphere, with laughter and music filling the air.
Her frothy comments lightened the mood during the tense meeting.
Critics dismissed the novel as frothy, but fans enjoyed its cheerful tone.

Synset Examples

his fertile effervescent mind
scintillating personality
a row of sparkly cheerleaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effervescent - full of life and excitement
  • Synonym: scintillating - sparkling or brilliantly lively
  • Antonym: serious - having deep thought or substance

Etymology

From froth + -y. Froth from Old Norse 'froða' meaning foam.
