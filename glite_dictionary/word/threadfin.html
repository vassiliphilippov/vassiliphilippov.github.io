threadfin

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛdˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈθrɛd.fɪn / (BrE)
A tropical marine fish resembling a mullet, characterized by pectoral fins with long, threadlike rays.
tropical fish with threadlike pectoral fins

Examples

The fisherman caught a large threadfin near the shore early in the morning.
Researchers are studying the unique pectoral fins of the threadfin to understand its swimming behavior.
A school of threadfins darted past the coral reef, their slender rays flickering in the sunlight.

Etymology

From 'thread' + 'fin', describing the long, thin rays on the fins.
