incienso

1. NOUN

/ inˈsjen.soʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsjɛn.səʊ / (BrE)
A fragrant shrub (Bursera arida) native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with brittle stems, small blue-green leaves, and yellow flowers, whose resin is used in incense, varnish, and folk medicine.
a fragrant shrub native to SW US and Mexico

Examples

The resin harvested from the incienso shrub is valued in traditional medicine.
During the dry season, the incienso covers the hillsides with its yellow blossoms.
Locals use incienso to make incense and varnish due to its aromatic properties.

Etymology

From Spanish 'incienso' (incense), referring both to the plant and the aromatic resin it produces.
