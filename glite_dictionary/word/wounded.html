wounded

1. ADJ

/ ˈwundɪd / (AmE), / ˈwuːndɪd / (BrE)
Suffering from a physical injury, especially one received in battle or as the result of violence or an accident.
having a physical injury, often from battle or violence

Examples

The soldier was wounded in the leg but managed to stay calm.
Several wounded passengers were taken to the hospital after the accident.
She tended to the wounded animal until it was safe to move.

Synset Examples

nursing his wounded arm
ambulances … for the hurt men and women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injured - hurt or physically damaged
  • Antonym: unharmed - not physically injured or hurt

Etymology

From the verb 'wound', from Old English wundian (to injure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwundɪd / (AmE), / ˈwuːndɪd / (BrE)
people who are injured, especially in war or an accident
people injured, especially in war

Examples

Several of the wounded were airlifted to a nearby hospital for treatment.
The army medics prioritized help for the wounded after the battle.
Rescue workers searched through the rubble for survivors and the wounded.

Synset Examples

they had to leave the wounded where they fell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injured - people hurt or impaired physically
  • Antonym: dead - people who have died, especially in war or disaster contexts
