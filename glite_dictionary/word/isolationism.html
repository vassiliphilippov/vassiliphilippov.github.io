isolationism

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
A national policy of avoiding political or economic involvement with other countries.
policy of national non-involvement

Examples

The country's leaders adopted isolationism to avoid entanglement in foreign wars.
Historical debates about isolationism shaped the nation's foreign policy before World War II.
Critics argue that economic isolationism can harm both domestic and global prosperity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internationalism - the principle of cooperation among nations for the promotion of their common good
  • Synonym: noninterventionism - policy or practice of not interfering in the affairs of others, especially other countries

Etymology

From 'isolation' + '-ism', first used in the late 19th century.
