Victrola

1. NOUN

/ vɪkˈtroʊlə / (AmE), / vɪkˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
a brand of gramophone or early phonograph, especially one manufactured by the Victor Talking Machine Company; often used to refer generally to early phonographs
brand of early gramophone

Examples

He wound up the Victrola and put on his favorite record from the 1920s.
The antique Victrola in the corner still works perfectly after a hundred years.
Collectors paid a premium for a well-preserved Victrola at the auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gramophone - an old-fashioned record player for playing music
  • Synonym: phonograph - an early device for reproducing recorded sound

Etymology

Coined from 'Victor', the company name, and the suffix '-ola' used in early 20th-century product naming.
