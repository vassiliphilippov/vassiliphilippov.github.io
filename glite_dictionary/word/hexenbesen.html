hexenbesen

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛksənˌbeɪzən / (AmE), / ˈhɛksənˌbeːzn̩ / (BrE)
An abnormal, dense cluster of small branches growing from a single point on a tree or shrub, typically caused by fungi, insects, or other physiological disturbances.
dense abnormal growth on trees or shrubs

Examples

The oak tree in the garden developed a distinctive hexenbesen after a severe fungal infection.
Botanists studied the hexenbesen to determine the cause of its rapid growth.
A large hexenbesen can weaken the branches of susceptible species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch's broom - Abnormal, broom-like growth in woody plants caused by parasites or disease

Etymology

From German 'Hexenbesen', literally 'witch's broom' ('Hexe' meaning 'witch' and 'Besen' meaning 'broom').
