horripilation

1. NOUN

/ hɔːˌrɪpɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / hɒˌrɪpɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a reflex reaction in which hairs on the skin stand up, typically in response to cold, fear, or irritation; also known as goosebumps
reflex raising of body hair (goosebumps)
medical

Examples

The sudden chill sent a wave of horripilation across her arms and neck.
Watching the horror movie induced horripilation as the terrifying scene unfolded.
Some people experience horripilation as a reaction to certain types of music or strong emotions.

Etymology

From Latin horripilatio (bristling of the hair), from horrere (to stand on end, shudder) + pilus (hair).
