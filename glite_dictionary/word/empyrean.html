empyrean

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛm.pɪˈriː.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.pɪˈriː.ən / (BrE)
relating to the highest part of the heavens or the sky; celestial or heavenly.
celestial; relating to the heavens

Examples

The painter captured an empyrean light that seemed to glow from beyond the clouds.
They gazed at the empyrean expanse above, lost in wonder at the night sky.
Poets often invoke empyrean images to convey a sense of awe and transcendence.

Synset Examples

the empyrean sphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens
  • Antonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth as opposed to the sky or heavens

Etymology

From Greek empyrios (in or on the fire), from em- (in) + pyr (fire).

2. ADJ

/ ɛmˌpɪˈriː.ən / (AmE), / ɛmˌpɪəˈriː.ən / (BrE)
inspiring awe or suggesting the heavens; sublime and celestial
celestial; awe-inspiring; sublime

Examples

The sunset bathed the mountains in an empyrean glow that left us speechless.
She regarded the conductor with an empyrean respect, as if he commanded the stars themselves.
The choir's voices rose in an empyrean harmony, filling the cathedral with a sense of awe.

Synset Examples

well-meaning ineptitude that rises to empyreal absurdity
empyrean aplomb
the sublime beauty of the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sublime - of such excellence, grandeur, or beauty as to inspire awe
  • Synonym: heavenly - of or relating to the heavens; celestial
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull, of the earthly world

Etymology

From Latin empyreus (fiery, from the highest heaven), from Greek empyrios (in or on the fire).

WordNet ID: oewn-01125519-s

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.pɪˈri.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.pɪˈriː.ən / (BrE)
the highest part of heaven or the sky, especially conceived as a sphere or vault in which celestial bodies move or are fixed
the highest heaven; celestial sphere

Examples

The poet gazed upward, imagining angels dwelling in the empyrean above.
Legends often speak of gods residing in the empyrean far beyond mortal reach.
Stargazers contemplated the vastness of the empyrean on a clear night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmament - the heavens or the sky, especially as a tangible thing

Etymology

From Late Latin empyreus (fiery), from Ancient Greek empyros (in or on the fire), from en- (in) + pyr (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08538450-n
Synset POS: n
