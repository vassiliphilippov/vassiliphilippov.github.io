linguistics

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of language and its structure, including the study of grammar, syntax, phonetics, and semantics
the scientific study of language

Examples

She is majoring in linguistics at the university and hopes to become a language researcher.
The field of linguistics covers topics such as syntax, phonology, and semantics.
Recent advances in linguistics have improved our understanding of how children acquire language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philology - the study of language in written historical sources; a synonym in older or literary contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'lingua' (language, tongue) + '-istics', via French 'linguistique'.

2. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of language and its structure, including the study of grammar, syntax, and phonetics
the scientific study of language

Examples

She decided to major in linguistics because of her fascination with how languages evolve.
The conference brought together leading experts in linguistics from around the world.
His work in linguistics contributed to new theories about language acquisition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philology - the study of language in historical and literary texts

Etymology

From Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language) + -istics, modeled after 'statistics'.

Synset POS: n
