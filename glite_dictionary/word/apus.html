Apus

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds comprising the swifts, known for their fast flight and aerial lifestyle.
genus of birds (swifts)

Examples

The genus Apus contains several species of swift found across Europe and Asia.
Research on Apus has revealed fascinating insights into their migratory patterns.
Scientists have discovered a new member of the Apus genus in Central Africa.

Etymology

From Latin 'apus', from Ancient Greek 'ἀπούς' (apous, meaning 'without feet', referring to the small, weak legs of swifts).

2. PROPN

/ ˈeɪ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pəs / (BrE)
a small constellation in the southern polar region of the sky, near Octans
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The constellation Apus is visible only from the Southern Hemisphere.
Astronomers often observe Apus when mapping southern stars.
Apus lies close to the celestial south pole, near Octans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Octans - a nearby southern constellation

Etymology

From Latin 'apus', meaning 'without feet', referring to the bird-of-paradise which was once believed to have no feet.
