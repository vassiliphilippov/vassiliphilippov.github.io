to economize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to use resources carefully so as to avoid waste or save money
use resources carefully; avoid waste

Examples

After losing his job, he had to economize on groceries to make ends meet.
Families often economize by cutting out unnecessary expenses during tough times.
To travel more, she tried to economize wherever possible, choosing budget accommodations and cheap flights.

Synset Examples

This move will save money
The less fortunate will have to economize now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to avoid spending money or resources
  • Antonym: waste - to use resources carelessly or extravagantly

Etymology

From French 'économiser', from Greek 'oikonomia' (household management).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈkɑːnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to use money, resources, or time carefully so that you do not waste any; to be frugal or sparing
use resources carefully; be frugal

Examples

We've had to economize since Jack lost his job.
She tries to economize on electricity to lower the monthly bill.
When traveling, it's wise to economize and avoid unnecessary expenses.

Synset Examples

I try to economize my spare time
conserve your energy for the ascent to the summit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to avoid using something unnecessarily; to keep for future use
  • Synonym: conserve - to use something as little as possible so that it lasts longer
  • Antonym: waste - to use something carelessly and without purpose

Etymology

From Greek oikonomos (manager of a household) via Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02273988-v
Synset POS: v
