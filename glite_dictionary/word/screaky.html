screaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈskriːki / (AmE), / ˈskriːki / (BrE)
making or having a high-pitched, squeaking sound, like a mouse or a rusty hinge
making a high-pitched squeaking sound

Examples

The door was so old and screaky that it woke everyone when opened.
I could hear the screaky floorboards with every step she took.
The violin made a screaky noise when it was played poorly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeaky - making a short, high-pitched sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01217277-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈskriːki / (AmE), / ˈskriːki / (BrE)
making a rasping, creaking, or grating sound, especially when moved or touched.
making a rasping or creaking sound

Examples

The old wooden floorboards were screaky every time someone walked through the hallway.
She tried to open the screaky door as quietly as possible, but it still made a noise.
His shoes were so screaky that everyone could hear him coming down the corridor.

Synset Examples

creaky stairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creaky - making a creaking or squeaking sound
