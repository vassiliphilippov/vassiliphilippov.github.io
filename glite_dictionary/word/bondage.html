bondage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑn.dɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.dɪdʒ / (BrE)
the state of being under the control of another person, especially in conditions of slavery or servitude
state of being controlled by another; servitude

Examples

After years of bondage, the workers finally gained their freedom.
She escaped the bondage of her oppressive marriage and started a new life.
History is full of stories of people who rose up against bondage and fought for liberty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slavery - the condition in which a person is owned by another
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free from control or restraint

Etymology

From Middle English bondage, from bond (‘serf, tenant, bonded person’) + -age.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑn.dɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.dɪdʒ / (BrE)
the sexual practice of physically restraining a partner during erotic activity, typically using cords, handcuffs, or similar devices
sexual practice involving physical restraint

Examples

He was curious about trying bondage as part of their intimate life.
Many people interested in bondage value trust and communication with their partners.
They bought new restraints to experiment with bondage safely and consensually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: BDSM - sexual practices involving bondage, discipline, dominance, submission, sadism, and masochism

Etymology

From Old French 'bondage' (condition of serfdom), from 'bond' (a serf or slave). Modern usage extended in the 20th century to refer to sexual restraint practices.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːn.dɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.dɪdʒ / (BrE)
the state of being under the control of a force or influence, such as fear or addiction, especially when difficult to escape
state of being controlled by a force or influence

Examples

He struggled for years to break free from the bondage of addiction.
Her beliefs kept her in mental bondage, unable to accept new ideas.
The story is about a journey from the bondage of fear to personal empowerment.

Synset Examples

he was in bondage to fear
he sought release from his bondage to Satan
a self freed from the bondage of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servitude - the state of being a slave or completely subject to someone more powerful
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants

Etymology

From Old French *bondage* (condition of a serf), from *bond* (serf, slave).
