Sabal

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəl / (BrE)
A genus of palm trees native to the Americas, known for their fan-shaped leaves, including species commonly referred to as American dwarf fan palms.
genus of American dwarf fan palms

Examples

The Sabal grows in coastal regions and can tolerate salt spray.
Gardeners often choose the Sabal for its drought resistance and attractive foliage.
Many species in the Sabal genus provide important habitat for wildlife in their native areas.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after a genus of palms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəl / (BrE)
a genus of American fan palms, including several species known as palmettos, found mainly in the southeastern United States, the Caribbean, and Central America.
a genus of American fan palms

Examples

The Sabal is commonly found in coastal habitats across Florida and the Caribbean.
Several species within the Sabal genus are known for their distinctive fan-shaped leaves.
Botanists study Sabal to better understand the diversity of American palm trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmetto - a type of palm tree in the genus Sabal

Etymology

From the French 'sabal', from Spanish 'sabal', originally of Arawak origin.
