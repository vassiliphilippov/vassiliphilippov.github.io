manufacturer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃər.ər / (BrE)
a company or person that makes goods in large quantities using machines
company that makes products

Examples

The car manufacturer introduced several new models this year.
As a leading manufacturer of electronics, the company exports goods worldwide.
The manufacturer guarantees all its products for at least two years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: producer - a person or organization that makes or grows goods for sale

Etymology

From Latin 'manufactura' meaning 'a making by hand', from 'manus' (hand) and 'facere' (to make).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃər / (BrE)
A person or company that makes goods for sale, especially on a large scale using machinery.
person or company that produces goods

Examples

The manufacturer guarantees the quality of all its products for two years.
After the recall, the manufacturer quickly addressed the safety concerns.
Many car parts are shipped directly from the original manufacturer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: producer - a person or company that makes or grows goods for sale
  • Antonym: consumer - a person who purchases goods and services for personal use

Etymology

From Latin 'manu' (hand) + 'facere' (to make), via French 'manufacturier'.
