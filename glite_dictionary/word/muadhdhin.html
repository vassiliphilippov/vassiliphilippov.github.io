muadhdhin

1. NOUN

/ muˈɑːð.ɪn / (AmE), / muˈæd.ɪn / (BrE)
the person at a mosque who calls Muslims to prayer from a minaret, typically five times a day
person who calls Muslims to prayer

Examples

The muadhdhin climbed to the minaret to call the faithful to prayer at dawn.
Each day, the muadhdhin's call echoes through the neighborhood.
During Ramadan, the muadhdhin plays an especially important role in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muezzin - the person who calls Muslims to prayer from the minaret of a mosque

Etymology

From Arabic *muʾaḏḏin* (person who makes the call to prayer).
