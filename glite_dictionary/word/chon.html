chon

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌn / (AmE), / tʃɒn / (BrE)
A monetary unit of North Korea, equal to one hundredth of a won.
North Korean monetary subunit

Examples

Tourists are rarely allowed to use the North Korean chon during their visits.
The souvenir cost 50 chons, a fraction of a North Korean won.
Historically, the chon was used for small transactions in North Korea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: won - the basic monetary unit of North Korea and South Korea

2. NOUN

/ tʃʌn / (AmE), / tʃɒn / (BrE)
A former monetary subunit of the South Korean won, equal to one hundredth of a won.
former monetary subunit of the South Korean won

Examples

Coins denominated in chon became rare after South Korea stopped minting them.
The vendor priced goods in both won and chon during the 20th century.
He collected old South Korean chon coins as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: won - the basic monetary unit of South Korea

Etymology

From Korean 전 (jeon), originating from the Chinese character 錢 (coin, money).
