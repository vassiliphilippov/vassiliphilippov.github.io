peruke

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːk / (AmE), / pəˈruːk / (BrE)
a man's wig, especially one that was fashionable in the 17th and 18th centuries
historical men's wig

Examples

In portraits from the 18th century, many gentlemen are depicted wearing a peruke.
After the Restoration, it became fashionable for English men to don a powdered peruke.
The judge adjusted his peruke before entering the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wig - an artificial covering of hair for the head

Etymology

From French 'perruque', from Italian 'perrucca' (wig).
