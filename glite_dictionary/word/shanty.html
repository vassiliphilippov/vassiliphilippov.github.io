shanty

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃænti / (AmE), / ˈʃænti / (BrE)
A small, crudely built shelter that is used as a dwelling, typically made from discarded materials.
small, crude shelter for living

Examples

Many families were forced to live in a shanty on the outskirts of the city.
The shanty was built from old wooden planks and sheets of metal.
After the storm, only a single shanty remained standing in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small, simple dwelling or shelter
  • Antonym: mansion - a large and impressive house

Etymology

Perhaps from French *chantier* (yard, building site), referring to makeshift constructions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃænti / (AmE), / ˈʃænti / (BrE)
a rhythmic work song sung by sailors, usually to accompany manual labor on board ship
sailors' rhythmic work song

Examples

The sailors raised their voices in a shanty as they hauled in the heavy rope.
A famous shanty like 'Drunken Sailor' can still be heard at maritime festivals today.
He learned an old shanty from his grandfather, who was once a merchant seaman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea shanty - a work song sung aboard ships to coordinate labor

Etymology

Probably from French 'chanter' (to sing) or from English 'chant'.
