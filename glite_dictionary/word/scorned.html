scorned

1. ADJ

/ skɔːrnd / (AmE), / skɔːnd / (BrE)
Treated with disdain or contempt; rejected or despised.
treated with contempt

Examples

Having been scorned by her former friends, she felt isolated and alone.
The scorned proposal was quickly discarded by the committee.
He wore the label of a scorned artist with a mix of pride and sadness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respected - treated with admiration or esteem
  • Synonym: despised - regarded with contempt or distaste

Etymology

From Middle English *scornen*, from Old French *escarnir* (to mock).
