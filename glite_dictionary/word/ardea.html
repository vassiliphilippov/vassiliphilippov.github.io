Ardea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑr.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈɑː.di.ə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Ardeidae, including large herons found in both the New and Old Worlds.
genus of large herons

Examples

The great blue heron belongs to the genus Ardea.
Several species within Ardea are widespread in wetlands around the world.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of birds classified under Ardea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - large wading bird with a long neck and legs

Etymology

From Latin *ardea*, meaning 'heron'.
