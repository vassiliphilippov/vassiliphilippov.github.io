estimable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛstəməbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛstɪməbəl / (BrE)
deserving of respect or admiration; worthy of esteem.
deserving respect or admiration

Examples

She is an estimable leader who has earned the trust of her colleagues.
His estimable achievements in science have inspired many young students.
Despite his humble background, he is held in estimable regard by his entire community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirable - deserving admiration or approval
  • Antonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable

Etymology

From Latin 'aestimabilis' (that can be estimated, worthy), from 'aestimare' (to estimate).

worthy of respect or admiration

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛstɪməbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛstɪməb(ə)l / (BrE)
able to be estimated or calculated; possible to compute or assess the value, amount, or extent of something
able to be estimated or computed

Examples

The company's total assets are estimable based on its most recent financial statements.
Although the exact impact is uncertain, the potential costs are estimable within a reasonable range.
The risks involved in the project are not precisely known but are generally estimable.

Synset Examples

a calculable risk
computable odds
estimable assets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculable - able to be calculated or estimated
  • Antonym: incalculable - impossible to calculate or estimate

Etymology

From Latin 'aestimabilis' (capable of being estimated), from 'aestimare' (to estimate).
