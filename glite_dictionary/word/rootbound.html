rootbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈrutˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈruːtˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a potted plant) having outgrown its container so that the roots have become densely matted or tangled.
plant with roots tangled from outgrowing its pot

Examples

The fern became rootbound and had to be moved to a larger pot.
If your plant looks unhealthy and is rootbound, consider repotting it soon.
Gardeners often check if a plant is rootbound before transplanting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot-bound - (of a plant) having roots confined by the pot, similar to rootbound

Etymology

From 'root' + 'bound', meaning roots confined by the container.

2. ADJ

/ ˈruːtˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈruːtˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having roots that are densely tangled or matted, typically because they have grown too large for their container.
(of a plant) with tangled, crowded roots

Examples

The plant became rootbound after staying in the same pot for several years.
Rootbound flowers often stop growing properly until they are transplanted to a larger container.
A rootbound tree needs to have its roots loosened before it can thrive in a new location.

Synset Examples

shaggy untended lawns of old trees and rootbound scented flowers and shrubs

Etymology

Formed from 'root' + 'bound', reflecting restriction of roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256908-s
Synset POS: s
