nobility

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɪləti / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having high moral principles, elevated mind, or exalted ideals and conduct.
elevation of character or ideals

Examples

Her nobility was evident in the way she treated everyone with kindness and respect.
Acts of nobility are often remembered long after personal achievements are forgotten.
He was admired for the nobility of his vision and the integrity of his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: baseness - lack of moral principles; bad character

Etymology

From Latin nobilitas (nobleness, fame), via Old French nobilité.

3. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The class of people in society who hold hereditary titles and privileges, often of high social rank.
hereditary privileged class

Examples

The nobility often lived in grand castles and controlled large estates in medieval Europe.
Members of the British nobility are frequently invited to royal events.
After the revolution, many countries abolished the privileges of the nobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, especially those holding hereditary titles or offices
  • Antonym: commoners - people without noble rank or hereditary titles

Etymology

From Latin 'nobilitas' (nobleness, high rank).
