twiddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪd.əl / (BrE)
a series of small, usually idle, twists or turns, often made absentmindedly with the fingers
series of idle twists or turns

Examples

She gave the key a quick twiddle before unlocking the door.
His nervous twiddle of the pen made it clear he was anxious.
With a twiddle of her hair, she seemed lost in thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidget - small restless movements, usually with the hands or feet

Etymology

Imitative; likely related to 'twist' and the frequentative suffix '-le'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00341815-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtwɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪd.əl / (BrE)
to turn or move something repeatedly with your fingers, typically in a nervous or unconscious way
to move or fiddle nervously with fingers

Examples

She began to twiddle her hair while she waited for a response.
He would often twiddle his pen during long meetings.
Nervously, Anna twiddled with her ring as she listened.

Synset Examples

He twiddled his thumbs while waiting for the interview

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidget - to move about restlessly or nervously
  • Synonym: toy - to handle or play with something absentmindedly

Etymology

Perhaps of imitative origin, influenced by 'twiddle' meaning 'to twirl'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01226775-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtwɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪd.əl / (BrE)
to turn or spin something lightly and repeatedly with a twisting motion, usually with the fingers
turn or spin something lightly

Examples

He absentmindedly twiddled his pen during the meeting.
She likes to twiddle her hair when she's deep in thought.
The boy twiddled the dial until the radio found a clear signal.

Synset Examples

The leaves swirled in the autumn wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidget - to make small movements, especially with the hands, through nervousness or impatience

Etymology

Perhaps from a blend of 'twist' and 'fiddle'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
