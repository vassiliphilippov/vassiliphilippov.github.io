illegitimately

1. ADV

/ ˌɪləˈdʒɪtəmətli / (AmE), / ˌɪləˈdʒɪtɪmətli / (BrE)
in a manner that is not in accordance with law, rules, or accepted standards; unlawfully or improperly
in an unlawful or improper manner

Examples

He was accused of acquiring property illegitimately during his tenure in office.
The candidate was removed from the race after being found to have illegitimately altered official documents.
Profits gained illegitimately are subject to legal penalties.

Synset Examples

He acted illegitimately when he increased the rent fourfold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegally - in a manner not permitted by law
  • Antonym: legitimately - in a manner allowed by law, custom, or rules

Etymology

Formed from 'illegitimate' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'illegitimus' (not according to law or rule).

2. ADV

/ ˌɪləˈdʒɪtəmətli / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈdʒɪtɪmətli / (BrE)
In a way that occurs to a child whose parents are not legally married to each other.
in a manner born to unmarried parents

Examples

He was born illegitimately at a time when society strongly disapproved of such circumstances.
The novel's protagonist struggles with the stigma of being illegitimately born.
Decades ago, children born illegitimately often faced discrimination in their communities.

Synset Examples

this child was born illegitimately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimately - in a way that contravenes law or accepted rules
  • Antonym: legitimately - in a way authorized by law; lawfully

Etymology

From Latin 'illegitimus' (not lawful, not legitimate), from 'in-' (not) + 'legitimus' (lawful).

Source: WordNet
