Cretaceous

1. ADJ

/ krɪˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / krɪˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to the third and last period of the Mesozoic era, characterized by the dominance of dinosaurs and the appearance of flowering plants
relating to the last period of the Mesozoic era

Examples

Some of the most famous dinosaurs lived during the Cretaceous period.
The Cretaceous fossils found in this region are remarkably well preserved.
Flowering plants began to flourish in the Cretaceous era.

Etymology

From Latin 'creta' (chalk), referring to chalk deposits from this period.

2. NOUN

/ krəˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / krɪˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
the geological period lasting from about 145 to 66 million years ago, marking the end of the age of dinosaurs and the emergence of flowering plants and modern insects
geological period from 145 to 66 million years ago

Examples

Many of the best-known dinosaur fossils date from the Cretaceous period.
Flowering plants first appeared during the Cretaceous, changing Earth's ecosystems dramatically.
The mass extinction at the end of the Cretaceous wiped out most dinosaurs.

Etymology

From Latin 'cretaceus' (of chalk), referring to the extensive chalk deposits of the period.
