ninny

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪni / (AmE), / ˈnɪni / (BrE)
a person who is silly or lacking in intelligence; a fool
a silly or foolish person

Examples

I felt like a ninny when I forgot my lines during the presentation.
Don’t be such a ninny; it’s just a harmless prank.
Everyone called him a ninny for believing the outrageous rumor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: simpleton - a person lacking in common sense
  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person

Etymology

Probably from Italian *nino* (diminutive of *Giovanni*), possibly influenced by obsolete 'ninnyhammer.'
