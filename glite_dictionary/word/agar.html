agar

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.ɡɑr / (AmE), / ˈɑː.ɡɑː / (BrE)
a gelatinous substance obtained from certain red algae, used in culture media for growing microorganisms and as a thickening agent in foods.
gelatinous substance from algae used in labs and food

Examples

The scientist poured the bacteria onto the agar plate to observe their growth.
Vegetarian desserts sometimes use agar instead of gelatin as a thickener.
He prepared several agar dishes to culture plant tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelatin - a colorless, flavorless food ingredient from collagen, also used as a gelling agent

Etymology

From Malay 'agar-agar', referring to the red algae from which it is derived.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæɡˌɑr / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡɑː / (BrE)
A gelatinous substance derived from seaweed, used as a solid culture medium for growing bacteria and other microorganisms.
gelatinous culture medium (usually for bacteria)

Examples

The scientist prepared a petri dish containing agar to culture the bacteria.
Different types of agar can support the growth of various microorganisms in the laboratory.
Colonies of mold became visible after several days on the nutrient agar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agar-agar - A synonym for agar, especially in some countries

Etymology

From Malay and Indonesian 'agar-agar', meaning jelly.
