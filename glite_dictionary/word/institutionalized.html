institutionalized

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.stəˈtuː.ʃə.nəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nə.laɪzd / (BrE)
Given the character of an institution or incorporated into an established and usually structured system.
incorporated into a structured system

Examples

Corruption in the organization became so widespread that it was practically institutionalized.
She criticized the institutionalized discrimination present in hiring practices.
Their country has an institutionalized system of social welfare.

Synset Examples

institutionalized graft
institutionalized suicide as practiced in Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic - done according to a fixed plan or system

Etymology

From institution + -alize + -ed.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˌstɪˈtuːʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˌstɪˈtjuːʃənəˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
officially placed in or committed to a specialized institution, especially one for care or confinement
placed in a specialized institution

Examples

The report focused on the challenges faced by institutionalized children in long-term care facilities.
After years of living alone, she feared becoming institutionalized in a nursing home.
The government introduced reforms to reduce the number of institutionalized adults with disabilities.

Synset Examples

had hopes of rehabilitating the institutionalized juvenile delinquents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hospitalized - admitted to or treated in a hospital

Etymology

From 'institution' + '-alized', originally meaning to make into an institution; later meaning committed to an institution.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01233723-a
Synset POS: a
