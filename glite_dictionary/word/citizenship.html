citizenship

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt̬.ə.zən.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.ɪ.zən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the status of being a citizen of a particular country, involving legal rights and responsibilities
status of being a citizen

Examples

He applied for citizenship after living in the country for five years.
Dual citizenship allows her to hold passports from two different countries.
Studying the constitution is an important part of the citizenship test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationality - the status of belonging to a particular nation by origin or naturalization
  • Antonym: alienage - the state of being a foreigner, especially with respect to rights and duties

Etymology

Formed from 'citizen' + '-ship', denoting state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt̬.ə.zən.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.ɪ.zən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the qualities or conduct expected of a responsible member of a community; good behaviour as a citizen
conduct or qualities of a citizen

Examples

The school recognized Maria's exemplary citizenship with a special award.
Programs that promote citizenship teach students to be active and responsible community members.
Your consistent volunteering shows true citizenship and commitment to improving our neighborhood.

Synset Examples

award for good citizenship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civility - politeness and courtesy in behavior or speech
  • Antonym: incivility - rude or unsociable speech or behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'citizen' + '-ship', mirroring the sense of status or condition.
