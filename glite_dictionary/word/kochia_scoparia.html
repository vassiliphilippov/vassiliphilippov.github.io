Kochia scoparia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkiə skoʊˈpɛriə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkiə skəʊˈpɑːriə / (BrE)
A densely branched Eurasian plant whose foliage turns purple-red in autumn, often grown as an ornamental or used in soil erosion control.
a Eurasian plant with red autumn foliage

Examples

In the fall, the Kochia scoparia in our garden turns a brilliant red-purple color.
Farmers sometimes plant Kochia scoparia to control soil erosion on slopes.
The dense, bushy form of Kochia scoparia makes it popular as an ornamental border plant.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Kochia' after Wilhelm D. J. Koch (a German botanist) and 'scoparia' (Latin for 'broom-like').
