transience

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænziəns / (AmE), / ˈtrænziəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of lasting only for a short time; briefness; impermanence.
state of being brief or short-lived

Examples

The transience of youth makes many people long for their childhood days.
Modern technology only adds to the transience of trends and fashions.
Buddhist philosophy often emphasizes the transience of all things in life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanence - the state of lasting for a long time or forever
  • Synonym: ephemerality - the quality of lasting for a very short time

Etymology

From Latin 'transiens', present participle of 'transīre' (to go across, pass by).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrænziəns / (AmE), / ˈtrænziəns / (BrE)
The state or quality of lasting only for a short time; impermanence, especially with the suggestion of something coming to an end or dying.
the state of being brief or impermanent

Examples

The transience of youth is a common theme in poetry and literature.
He was struck by the transience of happiness, realizing how quickly moments can pass.
Spring flowers are a beautiful symbol of nature's transience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impermanence - the quality of not lasting forever; instability
  • Antonym: permanence - the state of lasting forever or for a very long time

Etymology

From Latin transiens (passing over), from transire (to go across).
