Chaenomeles

1. NOUN

/ kiˈnɒməliːz / (AmE), / kiːˈnɒmɪliːz / (BrE)
a small genus of deciduous shrubs commonly known as flowering quinces, native to East Asia, bearing bright flowers and hard fruit
genus of flowering quinces

Examples

The Chaenomeles in our garden bloomed earlier than usual this spring.
Many gardeners cultivate Chaenomeles for their brightly colored flowers and ornamental value.
Fruit from Chaenomeles can be used to make jellies and preserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quince - the fruit of a closely related genus, Cydonia, often called simply quince

Etymology

From Greek khaínō (to gape, open) + mēlon (apple), referring to the gaping fruit core.
