Francisco Pizarro

1. PROPN

/ fɾænˌsiskoʊ pɪˈzɑɾoʊ / (AmE), / frænˌsiːskəʊ pɪˈzɑːrəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 1475–1541) Spanish conquistador who conquered the Inca Empire in present-day Peru and founded Lima.
Spanish conquistador who conquered the Incas

Examples

Francisco Pizarro led the expedition that ultimately overthrew the Inca Empire in the 16th century.
The city of Lima was founded by Francisco Pizarro in 1535.
Historians often debate the legacy of Francisco Pizarro in South American history.
