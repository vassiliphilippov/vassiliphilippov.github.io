linden

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪn.dən / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.dən / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of the genus Tilia, having heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant yellowish flowers; some species provide valuable timber.
deciduous tree of the genus Tilia

Examples

A row of mature linden trees lined the avenue, filling the air with their sweet fragrance in spring.
The furniture was crafted from linden wood, known for its fine grain and light color.
In many European cities, you can find ancient linden trees planted in public squares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basswood - another common name for trees of the genus Tilia

Etymology

From Old English *lind*, related to German *Linde*, referring to trees in the Tilia genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪndən / (AmE), / ˈlɪndən / (BrE)
The soft, light-colored wood obtained from any of various linden trees, used in making crates, boxes, carving, and millwork.
soft wood from linden trees

Examples

The artist preferred to carve figures from linden because of its fine, even grain.
They received crates made of linden, which were lightweight but sturdy.
Some of the best traditional millwork uses linden for its smooth finish and ease of shaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basswood - wood from the American linden tree, often used interchangeably

Etymology

From Old English *lind* (linden tree), related to German *Linde*.
