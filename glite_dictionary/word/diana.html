Diana

1. PROPN

/ daɪˈænə / (AmE), / daɪˈænə / (BrE)
(1961–1997) Princess of Wales; the first wife of Prince Charles, remembered for her charitable work and tragic death in a car accident in Paris.
Princess of Wales (1961–1997); first wife of Prince Charles
american british

Examples

Diana was beloved for her compassion and charitable work around the world.
The news of Diana's death shocked millions and led to national mourning in Britain.
Many documentaries have explored the life and legacy of Diana, Princess of Wales.

2. PROPN

/ daɪˈænə / (AmE), / daɪˈanə / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the virgin goddess of the hunt and the Moon, equivalent to the Greek Artemis.
Roman goddess of the hunt and Moon

Examples

The temple of Diana at Ephesus was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
In Roman mythology, Diana is often depicted with a bow and arrows.
Many ancient festivals were held in honor of Diana, celebrating the cycles of the moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Artemis - Greek goddess of the hunt, counterpart of Diana in mythology

Etymology

From Latin *Diana*, meaning 'divine' or 'heavenly.'
