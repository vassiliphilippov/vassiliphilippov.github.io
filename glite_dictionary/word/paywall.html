paywall

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌwɔːl / (BrE)
A system that prevents users from accessing certain online content unless they have paid for it.
system restricting online content to paying users

Examples

Many newspapers have introduced a paywall to generate more revenue from their online editions.
After reading three free articles, the website prompted me to subscribe to bypass the paywall.
The magazine decided to implement a paywall to support its investigative journalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subscription - paid arrangement for access to a service or publication
  • Antonym: free access - unrestricted availability of online content without payment

Etymology

From 'pay' + 'wall,' modeled after 'firewall,' first used in digital publishing in the 2000s.
