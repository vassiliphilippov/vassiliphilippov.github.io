dropping

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
falling or moving downward freely under the influence of gravity.
falling downward; descending freely

Examples

The sky was filled with dropping leaves during the autumn storm.
She was startled by the sound of dropping stones from the old wall.
Firefighters watched the dropping water from the helicopter above.

Synset Examples

the eerie whistle of dropping bombs
falling rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falling - coming or going down freely under gravity

Etymology

Present participle of 'drop', from Old English 'dropian'.
