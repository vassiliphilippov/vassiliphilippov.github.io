particolored

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːrtɪˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having sections, patches, or areas of different, usually bright, colors.
having sections of different colors

Examples

The parade featured a particolored float with vibrant ribbons and streamers.
She wore a striking particolored scarf that stood out in the crowd.
The artist's mural was particolored, blending bold reds, blues, and yellows in swirling patterns.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many colors
  • Synonym: piebald - having irregular patches of two colors, especially black and white
  • Antonym: monochromatic - having or consisting of one color or hue

Etymology

From Italian 'particolore', from 'parti-' (part) and 'colore' (color).
