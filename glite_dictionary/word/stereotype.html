stereotype

1. VERB

/ ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəˌtaɪp / (BrE)
to have a fixed idea of what a particular type of person or thing is like, often unfairly, and to treat or categorize them accordingly
to judge or classify by stereotypes

Examples

It's wrong to stereotype people based on their appearance alone.
The media often stereotypes teenagers as rebellious and uninterested in school.
She felt frustrated that she had been stereotyped because of her accent.

Synset Examples

I was stereotyped as a lazy Southern European

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prejudge - form a judgment on an issue or person before having adequate information
  • Antonym: to individualize - consider or treat as an individual, not as part of a group or category

Etymology

From French 'stéréotype', originally referring to a printing plate; later figuratively extended to fixed ideas.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəˌtaɪp / (BrE)
a widely held but fixed and oversimplified idea or image of a particular type of person or thing
fixed, oversimplified idea or image

Examples

It's important to challenge the stereotype that all artists are eccentric.
The movie aimed to break the stereotype of women as passive characters.
She refused to let anyone box her into a stereotype based on her background.

Synset Examples

regional stereotypes have been part of America since its founding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliché - an overused phrase or opinion lacking original thought
  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Antonym: individuality - the quality that makes one person or thing different from others

Etymology

From French 'stéréotype', originally referring to a printing plate, from Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'typos' (impression).

Synset POS: n
