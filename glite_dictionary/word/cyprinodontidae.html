Cyprinodontidae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌprɪnəˈdɒntɪdi / (AmE), / sɪˌprɪnɒnˈtɪːdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small soft-finned freshwater fishes, including killifishes, flagfishes, swordtails, and guppies.
a family of small freshwater fishes (killifishes, guppies, etc.)

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts breed Cyprinodontidae for their vibrant colors and lively behavior.
The Cyprinodontidae family includes popular species like guppies and swordtails.
Researchers study Cyprinodontidae to learn more about evolutionary adaptation in freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killifish - a small fish belonging to the family Cyprinodontidae
  • Synonym: guppy - a small tropical fish, member of Cyprinodontidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cyprinodontidae, based on genus Cyprinodon, from Greek kyprinos (carp) + odous (tooth).
