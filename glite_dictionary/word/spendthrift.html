spendthrift

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛndˌθrɪft / (AmE), / ˈspɛndˌθrɪft / (BrE)
spending money in a reckless or wasteful manner; given to extravagant expenditures
recklessly wasteful with money

Examples

Her spendthrift habits quickly drained her savings account.
The spendthrift lifestyle of the celebrity attracted both admiration and criticism.
He regretted his spendthrift ways when he found himself deep in debt.

Synset Examples

prodigal in their expenditures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prodigal - wastefully extravagant
  • Antonym: frugal - being careful about spending money

Etymology

From 'spend' + 'thrift', originally meaning one who has spent his or her wealth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛndˌθrɪft / (AmE), / ˈspɛndˌθrɪft / (BrE)
a person who spends money in a careless or extravagant way
person who spends money extravagantly

Examples

Sarah is a notorious spendthrift who buys expensive clothes she doesn't need.
The company suffered under the leadership of a spendthrift CEO.
His spendthrift ways left him with little savings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible
  • Synonym: prodigal - a person who spends money wastefully

Etymology

From 'spend' + archaic 'thrift' (profit, wealth), originally meaning one who spends their wealth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10654858-n
Synset POS: n
