conversion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) a defense mechanism in which emotional conflicts or stress are unconsciously transformed into physical symptoms without a medical cause.
psychiatric defense mechanism causing physical symptoms
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist explained that her paralysis was a form of conversion related to unresolved emotional trauma.
Patients with conversion disorder may experience blindness, paralysis, or other symptoms that cannot be explained medically.
The onset of physical symptoms after stress suggested a classic case of conversion.

Etymology

From Latin conversio (a turning around), from convertere (to turn around).

6. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝʒən / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(Logic) The operation of interchanging the subject and predicate of a given proposition.
interchange of subject and predicate (logic)

Examples

In logic class, we learned that conversion involves swapping the subject and predicate of a proposition.
The conversion of the statement 'All cats are mammals' results in 'Some mammals are cats.'
He explained that conversion only preserves truth in certain types of propositions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: conversion by limitation - a kind of conversion where the quantity of the proposition is limited
  • Degree Lower: obversion - logical process changing the quality of a proposition while keeping terms unchanged

Etymology

From Latin 'convertio', from 'convertere' meaning 'to turn around'.
