Paranthias

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrænθiəs / (AmE), / pəˈranθɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Serranidae, including species such as Paranthias colonus and Paranthias furcifer.
a genus of sea basses (Serranidae)

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Paranthias.
The diet of Paranthias species mainly consists of smaller fish and plankton.
Marine biologists study Paranthias to better understand coral reef ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly from Greek 'para-' (beside, near) + 'anthias', a kind of fish.
