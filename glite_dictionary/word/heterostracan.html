heterostracan

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtərəˈstrækən / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈstrækən / (BrE)
an extinct jawless fish of the group Heterostraci, with the anterior part of the body covered by bony plates, found in the Silurian and Devonian periods
extinct jawless fish with bony plates

Examples

Fossils of the heterostracan reveal a heavily armored head and a tail adapted for swimming.
The heterostracan lineage offers insight into the early evolution of vertebrate skeletons.
Many museum collections feature specimens of heterostracan fishes from the Devonian period.

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + ostrakon (shell), referring to the distinct armored plates.
