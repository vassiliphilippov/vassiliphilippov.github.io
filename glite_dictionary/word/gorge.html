gorge

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
the passage between the pharynx and the stomach, especially in some animals or in historical/anatomical contexts
the passage between the throat and the stomach

Examples

Food passes through the gorge before reaching the stomach in some animals.
Ancient texts sometimes refer to the esophagus as the gorge.
In her anatomy class, she learned that the gorge is crucial for swallowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esophagus - the muscular tube connecting the throat (pharynx) with the stomach

Etymology

From Old French 'gorge' (throat), from Late Latin 'gurga' (throat, gullet).

2. NOUN

/ ɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
a deep, narrow valley with steep rocky sides, often with a river flowing through it
deep, narrow valley with steep sides

Examples

The river carved a stunning gorge through the mountains over thousands of years.
Hikers followed the narrow trail along the edge of the gorge.
A small bridge spanned the deep gorge, offering breathtaking views below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravine - a deep, narrow gorge with steep sides
  • Synonym: canyon - a deep gorge, typically with a river flowing through it
  • Degree Lower: valley - a low area of land between hills, often with a river

Etymology

From Old French 'gorge' meaning 'throat', from Late Latin 'gurga', related to Latin 'gurges' (whirlpool, abyss).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
a deep, narrow valley with steep rocky sides, especially between mountains
deep, narrow mountain valley

Examples

The river runs through a dramatic gorge carved into the mountainside.
Tourists hiked along the edge of the gorge to admire the breathtaking views.
Bridges span the gorge, connecting one side of the canyon to the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravine - a deep, narrow gorge with steep sides
  • Synonym: canyon - a deep valley with steep sides, often with a river
  • Degree Higher: valley - an elongated depression between hills or mountains

Etymology

From Old French *gorge* (throat, narrow passage).

4. VERB

/ ɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to eat a large amount of food greedily; to overeat or consume immoderately
to eat immoderately or greedily

Examples

On Thanksgiving, I always gorge on turkey and pie until I can't eat another bite.
After the long hike, the group gorged themselves on sandwiches and fruit.
You shouldn't gorge on sweets before dinner, or you'll spoil your appetite.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuff oneself - to eat until completely full, often excessively
  • Synonym: binge - to eat or drink too much in a short time
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer or die from lack of food

Etymology

From Old French *gorger* (to engulf), related to *gorge* (throat).
