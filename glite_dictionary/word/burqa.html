burqa

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːkə / (AmE), / ˈbɜːkə / (BrE)
A long, loose outer garment covering the whole body from head to feet, worn by some Muslim women, typically with a veiled opening or grille for the eyes.
long loose garment worn by some Muslim women

Examples

She wore a burqa that covered her entire body when she went outside.
Debates about the burqa continue in many countries regarding cultural and religious expression.
The law prohibited women from wearing the burqa in public places.

Synset Examples

the Taliban forced all women to wear the burqa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: niqab - a garment covering the face worn by some Muslim women
  • Synonym: hijab - a headscarf worn by some Muslim women covering the hair and neck

Etymology

From Arabic 'burquʻ' meaning 'veil'.
