marvel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvəl / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes great surprise, admiration, or wonder
something causing wonder or admiration

Examples

The new bridge is a true marvel of engineering.
Many people consider ancient pyramids to be a marvel.
The little boy stared in marvel at the magician's tricks.

Synset Examples

the wonders of modern science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - a feeling of amazement and admiration, caused by something beautiful, remarkable, or unfamiliar

Etymology

From Old French *merveille*, from Latin *mirabilia* (wonderful things), from *mirari* (to wonder).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑrvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvəl / (BrE)
to express astonishment or great surprise about something
express astonishment or surprise

Examples

We couldn't help but marvel at the beauty of the sunset.
Many people marveled at her ability to solve complex puzzles so quickly.
As children, we marvel when we see a magician perform amazing tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - to express curiosity or amazement
  • Synonym: to admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Old French 'merveiller' (to wonder at), from Latin 'mirari' (to wonder).

WordNet ID: oewn-00927706-v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɑrvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvəl / (BrE)
to be filled with surprise, admiration, or wonder about something
be amazed or filled with wonder

Examples

Tourists marvel at the ancient architecture every year.
She couldn't help but marvel at the artist's attention to detail.
As we reached the summit, we all marveled at the breathtaking view.

Synset Examples

We marvelled at the child's linguistic abilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - to feel admiration and amazement
  • Synonym: to admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Old French 'merveiller' (to wonder at), from Latin 'mirabilis' (wonderful).
