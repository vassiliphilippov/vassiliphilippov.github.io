flashpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃpɔɪnt / (BrE)
The lowest temperature at which the vapor of a combustible liquid can ignite in air.
lowest ignition temperature of vapor

Examples

The flashpoint of gasoline is much lower than that of diesel fuel.
Proper storage is crucial because the chemical's flashpoint is easily reached in warm conditions.
Always check the flashpoint listed on the container before using flammable liquids.

Etymology

From 'flash' + 'point', referring to the moment a vapor flashes (ignites).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃpɔɪnt / (BrE)
The moment or situation at which something is likely to erupt or suddenly become dangerous or intense.
critical or explosive stage

Examples

The city became a flashpoint for protests after the controversial law was passed.
Tensions in the region have reached a flashpoint that could lead to conflict at any moment.
The manager's harsh words acted as a flashpoint for employee dissatisfaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Synonym: tipping point - the critical point at which a significant change occurs

site of likely unrest or violence

Examples

Related Terms
