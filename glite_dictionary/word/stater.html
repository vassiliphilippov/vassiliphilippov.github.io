stater

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
an ancient Greek coin made of silver or gold, used as a standard unit of currency in various Greek city-states
ancient Greek gold or silver coin

Examples

A gold stater from Macedon was displayed in the museum's ancient coins section.
The value of a single stater could support a family for weeks in ancient Greece.
He collects Greek staters and other ancient coins as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drachma - another ancient Greek coin, smaller denomination than a stater
  • Degree Higher: talent - large unit of currency or weight in ancient Greece, higher value than a stater

Etymology

From Ancient Greek στατήρ (statḗr), meaning 'weight' or 'standard coin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
a resident of a particular US state, often used in combination with the name or nickname of the state
resident of a specific US state

Examples

As a proud stater, she always supports her state’s sports teams.
Each stater brought different traditions to the national conference.
There was a lively debate between the two staters about which state had better food.

Synset Examples

Keystone stater
farm staters

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: state - a nation or territory considered as an organized political community

Etymology

From 'state' + '-er', a suffix forming agent nouns.
