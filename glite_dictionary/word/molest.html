to molest

1. VERB

/ məˈlɛst / (AmE), / məˈlɛst / (BrE)
to harass or assault someone sexually, especially by making unwanted or indecent advances
sexually harass or assault

Examples

The suspect was charged after he tried to molest a passenger on the train.
Parents want schools to do more to protect children from those who might molest them.
Many victims of abuse find it difficult to speak out about being molested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to trouble or irritate repeatedly

Etymology

From Old French *molester*, from Latin *molestare* (to annoy), from *molestus* (troublesome).

2. VERB

/ moʊˈlɛst / (AmE), / məˈlɛst / (BrE)
to trouble, disturb, or annoy someone persistently; to harass repeatedly
to annoy or harass persistently

Examples

The children complained that the noisy construction work continued to molest their sleep every morning.
He felt the need to speak out when his colleagues were unjustly molested for trivial mistakes by management.
If you molest the dog too often, it may become aggressive from constant disturbance.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to bother or trouble someone repeatedly
  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry or irritated
  • Antonym: comfort - to make someone feel better, to soothe

Etymology

From Latin molestare (to trouble), from molestus (troublesome).
