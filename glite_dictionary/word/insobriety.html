insobriety

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌsoʊˈbraɪəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌsəʊˈbraɪəti / (BrE)
A temporary condition of lacking sobriety, especially as a result of excessive consumption of alcohol.
state of being intoxicated

Examples

After several drinks, his insobriety became obvious to everyone at the party.
The officer noticed signs of insobriety while speaking with the driver.
Periods of insobriety caused frequent problems in his work life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being affected by alcohol
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of not being affected by alcohol

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + sobrietas (sobriety).
