specimen

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛsəmən / (AmE), / ˈspɛsɪmɪn / (BrE)
an individual or part that is taken as an example of its class or group, especially for scientific study or display
a typical example of a class or group

Examples

Each museum display included a specimen that illustrated the key features of the species.
The scientist collected a specimen of the mineral for further analysis in the lab.
This is an excellent specimen of Victorian architecture, well preserved and historically significant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: example - something representative of a group or category

Etymology

From Latin 'specimen' (mark, example, indication), from 'specere' (to look at).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛsəmɪn / (AmE), / ˈspɛsɪmɪn / (BrE)
a small sample of tissue, blood, urine, or other material taken from a person or animal for medical examination or testing
sample of tissue or fluid for testing
medical

Examples

The nurse labeled the blood specimen before sending it to the laboratory for analysis.
Please bring a urine specimen to your appointment for testing.
The doctor asked for a tissue specimen to help diagnose the illness.

Synset Examples

they collected a urine specimen for urinalysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like

Etymology

From Latin 'specimen' meaning 'example, pattern', from 'specere' (to look at).

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɛs.ə.mən / (AmE), / ˈspes.ɪ.mən / (BrE)
an individual example of something, especially an animal, plant, or mineral, collected and studied as representative of its species or type, particularly when rare or unusual
an example, especially of a rare species

Examples

The biologist found a specimen of the endangered orchid during her fieldwork.
In the museum collection, the rarest specimen is a blue diamond butterfly.
They traveled deep into the forest to photograph a living specimen of the elusive snow leopard.

Synset Examples

He collected a specimen of the rare butterfly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a part or quantity taken to show what the whole is like

Etymology

From Latin 'specimen' (mark, token, example), from 'specere' (to look).
