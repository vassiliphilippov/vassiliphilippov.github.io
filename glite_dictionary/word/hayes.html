Hayes

1. PROPN

/ heɪz / (AmE), / heɪz / (BrE)
(Rutherford B.) Hayes (1822–1893), the 19th President of the United States, whose administration ended the Reconstruction era by withdrawing federal troops from the South.
19th US President (1822–1893)

Examples

Hayes was elected president after one of the most disputed elections in American history.
Hayes is often noted for ending the Reconstruction period in the United States.
Many historians debate the legacy of Hayes and his policies toward the South.
