undulate

1. VERB

/ ˈʌn.dʒə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌn.djʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to move with a smooth, wavelike motion; to rise and fall or move up and down in a regular manner
move in a wavy, rising and falling motion

Examples

The field of wheat seemed to undulate as the wind swept across it.
Her voice began to undulate with emotion as she spoke to the audience.
The flag undulated in the breeze at the top of the mountain.

Synset Examples

The curtains undulated
the waves rolled towards the beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - to move back and forth in curves or up and down repeatedly

Etymology

From Latin undulatus, from unda (wave).

2. VERB

/ ˈʌn.dʒə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌn.djʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to move or cause something, especially water, to move with a smooth, wave-like motion or to form ripples
move with a wave-like motion

Examples

The breeze caused the surface of the lake to undulate gently.
Children tossed pebbles into the pond, making the water undulate with ripples.
Strong winds can undulate the ocean, creating beautiful patterns on its surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripple - to form small waves on the surface of water

Etymology

From Latin 'undulatus', from 'unda' (wave).
