tern

1. NOUN

/ tɝn / (AmE), / tɜːn / (BrE)
a small, slender seabird from the gull family, with narrow wings and a deeply forked tail, typically found near the sea or inland waters
a slender seabird with a forked tail

Examples

We watched a tern diving elegantly for fish beside the dock.
A flock of terns circled above the coastal marshes at sunset.
This nature reserve is an important breeding site for the Arctic tern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a large seabird with long wings and usually a white or grey plumage

Etymology

From Old French 'terne', from Latin 'terna' (three each), probably referring to groups of three eggs laid; applied to the bird by association.
