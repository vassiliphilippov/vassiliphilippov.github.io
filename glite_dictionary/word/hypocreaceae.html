Hypocreaceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpoʊkriˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / hʌɪˌpəʊkriˈiːsi.iː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that have brightly colored, fleshy or membranous fruiting bodies; sometimes classified in their own order, Hypocreales
family of brightly colored fungi

Examples

Species within the Hypocreaceae family are known for their vividly colored fruiting bodies.
Researchers discovered a new type of fungus belonging to the Hypocreaceae in the tropical forest.
Many members of the Hypocreaceae are important in the decomposition of plant material.

Etymology

From genus Hypocrea + Latin -aceae, a standard ending for plant and fungal family names.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌpoʊkriˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / hɪˌpəʊkriːˈiːsiːaɪ / (BrE)
a family of fungi distinguished by their brightly colored fleshy or membranous fruiting bodies, sometimes classified in the order Hypocreales
family of brightly colored fungi

Examples

The Hypocreaceae family includes several fungi with vivid orange and yellow fruiting bodies.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Hypocreaceae during the expedition.
Some Hypocreaceae are known for their roles in plant diseases and soil ecology.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Hypocreaceae, from the type genus Hypocrea + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant and fungal family names).
