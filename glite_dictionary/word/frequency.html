frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
the number of times that a particular value or event occurs in a given category, especially in statistics
number of times an event occurs

Examples

The frequency of car accidents tends to increase in bad weather.
In the survey, the most common response had a frequency of 45.
The table shows the frequency of each answer given by participants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: relative frequency - the proportion of times an event occurs relative to the total number
  • Degree Lower: absolute frequency - the raw count of occurrences in statistics

Etymology

From Latin frequentia (a crowd, multitude, repetition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
the number of times an event occurs within a specified period of time
number of occurrences in a time period

Examples

The frequency of safety drills increased after the accident.
Doctors monitor the frequency of symptoms to track the progression of the disease.
Radio stations each broadcast on a different frequency.

Synset Examples

the frequency of modulation was 40 cycles per second
the frequency of his seizures increased as he grew older

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidence - the occurrence, rate, or frequency of something
  • Synonym: prevalence - the fact or condition of being prevalent; widespread occurrence

Etymology

From Latin *frequentia* (a crowd, multitude, frequency), from *frequens* (crowded, repeated).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
the ratio of the number of times a particular value or event occurs in a category to the total number of observations, especially in statistical analysis
statistical proportion of observations

Examples

The frequency of red cars in the survey was higher than that of any other color.
Statisticians often use frequency to compare different categories in a dataset.
The frequency with which an outcome appears can reveal important patterns in data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportion - a part or share of a whole, expressed as a fraction or percentage
  • Synonym: rate - a measure, quantity, or frequency, typically one measured against another quantity or measure

Etymology

From Latin 'frequentia' meaning a crowd or multitude, later referring to repeated occurrences.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
