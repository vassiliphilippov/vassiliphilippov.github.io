to kee siao

1. VERB

/ kiː siˈaʊ / (AmE), / kiː siˈaʊ / (BrE)
(in Singapore English) to behave in a crazy or wild manner; to act in an overly eccentric or foolish way.
behave in a crazy or wild manner (Singapore English)
slang

Examples

Whenever he wins a game, he tends to kee siao and shout all around the house.
Don’t kee siao at the office party—remember your boss is watching!
When she heard the good news, she started to kee siao in the middle of the street.

Synset Examples

He always acts kee siao during family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - behaving in a very strange or wild way
  • Synonym: insane - acting or appearing mentally deranged

Etymology

From Hokkien and Cantonese, literally meaning 'go crazy'.
