to reconvene

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈviːn / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈviːn / (BrE)
to gather or assemble again, especially after a pause or interruption
to meet again after a break

Examples

The committee will reconvene after lunch to discuss the final report.
Parliament is set to reconvene in January following the winter recess.
Our study group decided to reconvene next week to review the material.

Synset Examples

The bill will be considered when the Legislature reconvenes next Fall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to convene - to come together or assemble, usually for a public purpose

Etymology

From re- (again) + convene, from Latin convenīre (to come together).
