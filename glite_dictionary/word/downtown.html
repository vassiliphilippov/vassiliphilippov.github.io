downtown

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊntaʊn / (BrE)
in or toward the center or main business district of a city or town.
in/toward a city's central business area

Examples

We decided to go downtown for dinner and a movie.
Traffic is usually heavy when you're driving downtown in the morning.
She works downtown, just a few blocks from the main square.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uptown - in or toward the residential or northern part of a city

Etymology

Originally from American English, combining 'down' and 'town'; first used in New York City in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊntaʊn / (BrE)
situated in or relating to the central business district or main part of a city or town, especially as distinguished from outlying areas
located in or relating to a city's business center
american

Examples

The new restaurant is in the downtown area, close to many offices and shops.
Many downtown apartments offer stunning views of the city skyline.
There has been a recent surge in downtown development and revitalization.

Synset Examples

downtown Manhattan
delinquents roaming the downtown streets

Etymology

Originally American English, from 'down' + 'town', meaning the lower part or business district of a city.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.taʊn / (BrE)
the central business district or main area of a city, often where most offices, shops, and entertainment are located
the city center or business district
american

Examples

The city's downtown is bustling with people during the workweek.
We stayed at a hotel close to downtown so we could walk to all the attractions.
Many new restaurants have opened in downtown over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uptown - a residential or less commercial part of a city, often away from the business district

Etymology

From 'down' + 'town', originally American English in the early 19th century, describing the lower (southern) part of Manhattan in New York City.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08561059-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.taʊn / (BrE)
The central area or main business district of a city or town.
central commercial area of a city

Examples

We went to downtown for dinner and a movie after work.
The company recently moved its headquarters to downtown to be closer to clients.
During the festival, thousands of people gathered in downtown for the parade.

Synset Examples

the heart of Birmingham's downtown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city center - the central part of a city, especially in British English

Etymology

From North American English; literally 'down' + 'town', referring to the historically lower area of cities where businesses concentrated.
