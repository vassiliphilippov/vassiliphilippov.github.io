family Tingidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɪŋˈɡiːdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli tɪŋˈɡiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of small insects known as lace bugs, characterized by lace-like patterns on their wings and body surfaces
family of insects called lace bugs

Examples

Some gardeners struggle to control family Tingidae species, which can damage ornamental plants.
The distinctive, net-like pattern of family Tingidae helps identify them among other insect families.
Research on family Tingidae reveals fascinating adaptations for camouflage and survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace bug - small bug of the family Tingidae with delicate, lace-like wings

Etymology

From New Latin 'Tingidae', based on 'Tingis', the type genus, plus the taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
