crinoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈkraɪ.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
of, relating to, or belonging to the class Crinoidea, a group of marine animals characterized by feathery arms.
relating to the class Crinoidea (marine animals)

Examples

The scientist presented a detailed analysis of crinoid fossils found in the limestone.
Some crinoid features can be observed in these ancient sea creatures.
Researchers have discovered new species exhibiting unique crinoid structures in deep-sea environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crinoid - a marine animal of the class Crinoidea

Etymology

From Greek krinon (lily) + -oid ('like, resembling'), referring to their lily-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪn.ɔɪd / (BrE)
A marine animal of the class Crinoidea, having a mouth on the top surface surrounded by five or more feathery arms radiating from a central disk; also called a sea lily or feather star.
marine animal with feathery arms

Examples

The fossilized remains of a crinoid were discovered in the limestone bed.
Many crinoids wave their feathery arms to catch plankton drifting in the water.
A crinoid can either attach itself to the sea floor or move freely, depending on the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lily - marine animal of the class Crinoidea attached to the sea floor
  • Synonym: feather star - a free-swimming crinoid

Etymology

From Greek krinon (lily) + -oid (like), referring to their lily-like appearance.
