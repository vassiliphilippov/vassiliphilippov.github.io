honcho

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑntʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒntʃəʊ / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is in charge or the leader of a group, organization, or activity
person in charge; leader (informal)
informal

Examples

The new honcho quickly made changes to boost team morale.
Everyone listened carefully when the honcho entered the room to give instructions.
She's the honcho of the project, so any questions should go to her.

Synset Examples

if you want to leave early you have to ask the foreman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boss - person in charge of workers
  • Synonym: leader - person who leads or commands a group
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of another

Etymology

From Japanese hanchō (group leader), han (squad) + chō (head, chief).
