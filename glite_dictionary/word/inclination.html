inclination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪŋklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Geometry) The angle formed between the x-axis and a given line, measured counterclockwise from the positive direction of the x-axis.
angle between x-axis and a line (geometry)

Examples

The inclination of the line was determined to be 30 degrees above the x-axis.
To find the inclination, measure the angle from the positive x-axis to the given line.
In the graph, the steepest inclination indicates the fastest rate of change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angle - the space, measured in degrees, between two intersecting lines or surfaces

Etymology

From Latin inclinatio (a leaning toward), from inclinare (to lean).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The angle in degrees between the plane of an object's orbit and the plane of the ecliptic.
angle between orbital and ecliptic plane

Examples

The planet's inclination determines how its orbit is tilted relative to Earth's.
Saturn has an orbital inclination of about 2.5 degrees.
Astronomers calculated the asteroid's inclination to predict its future path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliquity - the angle between the equatorial plane and the orbital plane

Etymology

From Latin inclinatio (a leaning toward), from inclinare (to lean).
