evacuation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing the contents of something, especially from a container or the body
removal of contents

Examples

The evacuation of the tank took several hours due to its size.
Medical staff ensured the complete evacuation of the patient's stomach contents.
A blocked sewer can prevent the proper evacuation of waste.

Etymology

From Latin evacuatio (a clearing out), from evacuare (to empty).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvækjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of discharging waste material from the body, especially from the bowels
discharging waste from the body
medical

Examples

The doctor asked if he had regular evacuation of his bowels.
Difficulty with evacuation can be a symptom of digestive problems.
After the surgery, she experienced normal evacuation again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defecation - the act of discharging feces from the body
  • Antonym: constipation - condition in which bowel evacuations are infrequent or difficult

Etymology

From Latin evacuatio (a draining, emptying), from evacuare (to empty).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of moving people from a dangerous place to a safer place, especially in an organized way for safety.
orderly removal of people for safety

Examples

The city ordered the evacuation of residents ahead of the approaching hurricane.
During the fire alarm, an orderly evacuation of the building took place.
Authorities coordinated a mass evacuation to ensure everyone's safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evacuate - to remove people from a dangerous place for safety

Etymology

From Latin evacuatio (a clearing out), from evacuare (to empty out).
