omphaloskepsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑmfəloʊˈskɛpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒmfələʊˈskɛpsɪs / (BrE)
The act of contemplating one's navel, especially as an aid to meditation; also used figuratively to mean excessive self-absorption or self-reflection.
contemplation of one's navel; self-absorption

Examples

The philosopher's tendency toward omphaloskepsis sometimes hampered progress on practical matters.
Some critics accused the novel of literary omphaloskepsis that failed to address real-world concerns.
During meditation, he practiced omphaloskepsis to achieve a state of inner calm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navel-gazing - self-indulgent or excessive contemplation of oneself
  • Synonym: self-absorption - preoccupation with oneself or one's own affairs

Etymology

From Greek 'omphalos' (navel) and 'skepsis' (examination, contemplation).
