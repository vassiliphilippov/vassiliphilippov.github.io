obese

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈbis / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːs / (BrE)
having excessive body fat, often to the point that it may have a negative effect on health.
excessively overweight; very fat

Examples

The doctor advised the obese patient to develop a healthier lifestyle.
Researchers found that children who are obese face higher health risks.
He became obese after years of neglecting his diet and exercise.

Synset Examples

a weighty man

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: overweight - having more body weight than is considered normal or healthy
  • Degree Higher: morbidly obese - having extreme or severe obesity; much more than obese
  • Antonym: thin - having little body fat; the opposite of 'obese'

Etymology

From Latin 'obēsus' (fat, plump).
