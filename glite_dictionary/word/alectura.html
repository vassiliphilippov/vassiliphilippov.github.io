Alectura

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɛktjʊrə / (AmE), / əˈlɛktjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of large ground-dwelling birds native to Australia and nearby regions, known as brush turkeys.
genus of brush turkeys (birds)

Examples

The Alectura is commonly observed foraging in Australian forests for insects and seeds.
Scientists studied the nesting behavior of Alectura in the wild.
The Alectura builds large mound nests to incubate its eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush turkey - large ground-dwelling bird of Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Greek alektor (rooster, cock).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktjʊrə / (AmE), / əˈlɛktjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of large, ground-dwelling birds of the family Megapodiidae, known as brush turkeys, native to Australia and nearby regions.
genus of brush turkeys

Examples

The Alectura genus includes the well-known Australian brush turkey.
Scientists studied the nesting habits of birds in the Alectura genus.
Unlike other megapodes, members of Alectura construct large mounds for incubating their eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush turkey - a large ground bird of the genus Alectura, native to Australia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Alectura, based on Greek alektōr (rooster).
