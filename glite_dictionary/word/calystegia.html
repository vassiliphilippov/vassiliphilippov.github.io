Calystegia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪstəˌdʒiə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪstɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
a genus of climbing or scrambling herbs commonly known as bindweed, often with trumpet-shaped flowers.
a genus of bindweed plants

Examples

The invasive Calystegia quickly covered the fence with its dense, green foliage.
Many gardeners struggle to control the fast-spreading Calystegia in their flower beds.
The trumpet-shaped flowers of Calystegia are often white or pale pink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bindweed - commonly used name for plants in the genus Calystegia and related genera, known for twining stems and trumpet-like flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'kalyx' (calyx) and 'stegē' (covering), referring to the plant's floral structure.
