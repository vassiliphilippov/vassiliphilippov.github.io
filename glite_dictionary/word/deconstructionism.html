deconstructionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
a philosophical and literary theory that seeks to reveal hidden contradictions in texts by analyzing their underlying assumptions and meanings
theory analyzing contradictions in texts

Examples

Many scholars use deconstructionism to challenge traditional literary interpretations.
The professor's lectures on deconstructionism changed how students approached reading.
Some critics argue that deconstructionism leads to endless analysis without definite conclusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deconstruction - a method of critical analysis aiming to uncover assumptions and contradictions in texts

Etymology

From 'deconstruction' + '-ism'; 'deconstruction' from French 'déconstruction', popularized by philosopher Jacques Derrida.
