to underplay

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpleɪ / (BrE)
(in card games) to play a card lower than another card held, especially when a higher card could have been played.
play a lower card instead of a higher held card

Examples

If you underplay at this stage, you might lose your advantage later in the hand.
She chose to underplay the queen rather than risk the trick with her king.
It can be wise to underplay a high card, saving it for a more crucial moment.

Etymology

From 'under-' (lower, beneath) + 'play'.

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpleɪ / (BrE)
(of an actor) to perform a role with subtlety or restraint, avoiding exaggeration or overemphasis.
act a role with restraint

Examples

The actor chose to underplay the character's emotions, making the scene more powerful.
In this film, she skillfully underplays her role, letting the story speak for itself.
Critics praised his decision to underplay rather than overact during the dramatic moments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overact - to act with exaggerated expression or emotion
