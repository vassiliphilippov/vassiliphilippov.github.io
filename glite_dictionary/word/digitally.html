digitally

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəli / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəli / (BrE)
by means of the fingers, especially in medical examination
by use of the fingers
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's rectum digitally to check for abnormalities.
Some medical procedures are performed digitally rather than using instruments.
A mass could be felt digitally during the examination.

Synset Examples

the exam was carried out digitally

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger) + English adverbial '-ally'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəli / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəli / (BrE)
in a way that uses or relates to digital technology or discrete numerical values, especially integers or binary data.
using digital technology or integers

Examples

The music was recorded digitally for higher quality and easier editing.
Modern clocks often display the time digitally rather than with hands.
The photograph was enhanced digitally before it was printed.

Synset Examples

the time was digitally displayed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analogue - in a way that uses continuous signals or varying values, not digital

Etymology

From 'digital' (from Latin 'digitus', meaning finger or toe, later relating to numerals), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
