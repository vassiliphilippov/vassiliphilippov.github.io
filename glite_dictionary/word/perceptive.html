perceptive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
Relating to perception or the ability to perceive, especially in terms of mental or sensory faculties.
relating to perception or sensing

Examples

The artist praised the child's perceptive abilities for noticing subtle details in the painting.
He has highly developed perceptive faculties that help him excel in music.
Animals often rely on their perceptive senses to find food and avoid danger.

Synset Examples

perceptive faculties

Etymology

From Latin 'perceptivus' (able to perceive), from 'percipere' (to perceive).

WordNet ID: oewn-02879608-a

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
having or showing an ability to notice or understand things quickly and accurately; keen in discernment
able to notice or understand things easily

Examples

Her perceptive comments during the meeting impressed everyone.
The detective's perceptive nature helped him solve cases quickly.
He gave a perceptive analysis that revealed the true problem.

Synset Examples

a perceptive eye
a perceptive observation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observant - quick to notice things
  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing deep understanding
  • Antonym: unobservant - not quick to notice things
  • Antonym: insensitive - not noticing or caring about other's feelings or circumstances

Etymology

From Latin 'percept-', meaning 'seized, understood', from 'percipere'
