baryta

1. NOUN

/ bəˈraɪtə / (AmE), / bəˈraɪtə / (BrE)
any of several chemical compounds of barium, especially barium oxide or barium hydroxide, used in various industrial and laboratory applications.
barium compound used in chemistry and industry

Examples

The chemist used baryta to neutralize the acidic solution.
Baryta is commonly found in laboratories for various chemical reactions.
Photographic paper is often coated with baryta to improve its appearance and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barium oxide - a specific compound of barium and oxygen
  • Synonym: barium hydroxide - a specific compound of barium and hydroxide ions

Etymology

From New Latin baryta, from Greek barys (heavy), referring to the high atomic weight of barium compounds.
