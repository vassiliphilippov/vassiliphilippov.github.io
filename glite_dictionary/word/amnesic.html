amnesic

1. NOUN

/ æmˈniːzɪk / (AmE), / æmˈniːzɪk / (BrE)
A person who suffers from amnesia, involving partial or total loss of memory.
person with amnesia
medical

Examples

The amnesic could not remember anything about his life before the accident.
Doctors studied the amnesic to better understand the effects of memory loss.
After the trauma, the amnesic struggled to recognize familiar faces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesiac - a person experiencing amnesia

Etymology

From Greek amnēsia (forgetfulness), from amnēstos (‘forgotten’).

3. ADJ

/ æmˈniːzɪk / (AmE), / æmˈniːzɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, affected by, or caused by amnesia.
relating to amnesia
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited amnesic symptoms following the accident.
Researchers studied amnesic disorders to better understand memory loss.
Trauma can sometimes result in amnesic conditions affecting short-term memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesia - loss of memory

Etymology

From Greek 'amnēsikos' (causing forgetfulness), from 'amnēsia' (forgetfulness).
