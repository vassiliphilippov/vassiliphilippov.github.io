lymphocytopenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪm.foʊ.saɪ.təˈpiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌlɪm.fəʊ.saɪ.təˈpiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an abnormally low number of lymphocytes in the blood.
abnormally low lymphocyte count
medical

Examples

The patient's lymphocytopenia made her more susceptible to infections.
Doctors diagnosed lymphocytopenia following a routine blood test after chemotherapy.
Certain viral infections can cause temporary lymphocytopenia in otherwise healthy adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukopenia - a reduction in the number of white blood cells in the blood

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'lymphocyte' and Greek 'penia' (poverty, lack).
