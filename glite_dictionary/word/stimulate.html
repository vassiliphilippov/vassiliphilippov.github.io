to stimulate

1. VERB

/ ˈstɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cause (an event or process) to occur or develop more quickly or intensely than normal
to provoke rapid occurrence

Examples

The loud noise stimulated a sudden rush of adrenaline in the group.
Certain chemicals can stimulate rapid plant growth.
Her arrival stimulated a flurry of activity in the office.

Synset Examples

the infection precipitated a high fever and allergic reactions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a reaction or event, especially one that is strong or unwelcome
  • Synonym: trigger - to cause an event or situation to happen or exist

Etymology

From Latin stimulatus, past participle of stimulare (to goad, incite), from stimulus (goad, spur).

2. VERB

/ ˈstɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to take action or behave in a particular way, often by encouraging or provoking a response
cause to act or take action

Examples

The government hopes that lower interest rates will stimulate investment and spending.
Her curiosity was stimulated by the new scientific discovery.
The coach used inspiring stories to stimulate his team to perform better.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - give support or confidence to do something
  • Synonym: to motivate - provide someone with a reason for doing something
  • Antonym: to discourage - persuade someone not to take action

Etymology

From Latin stimulare (to goad, urge), from stimulus (goad, spur).
