tellingly

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛlɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that reveals something important, often by showing a significant effect or implication.
in a revealing or significant manner

Examples

His silence was tellingly broken only when the subject of money came up.
The critics, tellingly, focused on the scenes that exposed systemic corruption.
She glanced tellingly at the empty chair beside her, making her feelings clear.

Synset Examples

the plain manner of its style all the more tellingly points up the horror of the case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significantly - in a way that has a particular meaning or is important
  • Degree Lower: subtly - in a manner that is not obvious or direct

Etymology

From 'telling' (having a striking effect) with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
