Dryopteridaceae

1. NOUN

/ draɪˌɑptəˌrɪdəˈsiː / (AmE), / drʌɪˌɒptəˌrɪdəˈsiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns, commonly known as wood ferns, which is sometimes separated from the family Polypodiaceae in certain classification systems.
family of ferns (wood ferns)

Examples

Recent studies have reassigned several genera to the Dryopteridaceae family.
Members of the Dryopteridaceae are commonly found in temperate forests worldwide.
Botanists often debate the classification of Dryopteridaceae within the broader group of ferns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - a broader family of ferns including polypodies

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Dryopteris + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ draɪˌɑp.tə.rɪˈdeɪ.si.aɪ / (AmE), / drʌɪˌɒp.tə.rɪˈdiː.siːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns distinguished by their spores and leaf structures, commonly known as the wood fern family
a family of ferns (wood ferns)

Examples

The Dryopteridaceae are widely distributed, especially in temperate and tropical regions.
Many species in the Dryopteridaceae family are commonly grown as ornamental plants.
Botanists recently discovered a new genus within the Dryopteridaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Dryopteris' and the standard botanical suffix '-aceae' for families.
