Gnetum gnemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːtəm ˈniːˌmɒn / (AmE), / ˈgnɛtəm ˈgnɛmɒn / (BrE)
A small tropical tree with tiered branches and glossy dark green leaves, cultivated for its edible young leaves and seeds used to make flour.
tropical tree with edible leaves and seeds

Examples

The leaves of Gnetum gnemon are widely used in Southeast Asian cuisine.
Farmers harvest the seeds of Gnetum gnemon to process into flour for traditional cakes.
Because of its nutritional value, Gnetum gnemon is increasingly grown in home gardens.

Etymology

From the genus name Gnetum and species name gnemon, established in botanical Latin.
