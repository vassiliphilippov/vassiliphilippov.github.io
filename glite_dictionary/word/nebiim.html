Nebiim

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈbi.im / (AmE), / nɛˈbiː.im / (BrE)
the second of the three major divisions of the Hebrew Scriptures, comprising the books of the prophets
division of Hebrew Scriptures (prophets)

Examples

The Nebiim include books such as Joshua, Judges, and Kings in the Hebrew Bible.
Scholars often study the Nebiim to better understand the prophetic tradition in Judaism.
The Nebiim is situated between the Torah and the Ketuvim in the traditional Hebrew canon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ketuvim - the third division of the Hebrew Scriptures (the Writings)
  • Degree Lower: Torah - the first division of the Hebrew Scriptures (the Law)

Etymology

From Hebrew נְבִיאִים (Nəḇīʾīm), meaning 'prophets'.
