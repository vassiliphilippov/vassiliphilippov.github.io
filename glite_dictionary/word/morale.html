morale

1. NOUN

/ məˈræl / (AmE), / məˈrɑːl / (BrE)
the level of confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time
group's spirit or confidence

Examples

The team's morale improved after their recent victory.
Low morale among employees can lead to decreased productivity.
Leaders play a crucial role in maintaining high morale during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit - the prevailing mood or attitude of a group
  • Synonym: confidence - the feeling of self-assurance arising from appreciation of one's abilities

Etymology

From French *moral* (moral), literally 'moral condition' or 'spirit.'

2. NOUN

/ məˈræl / (AmE), / məˈrɑːl / (BrE)
the level of confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time
level of confidence and enthusiasm

Examples

The team's morale was boosted after their first win of the season.
Good leaders help maintain high morale even during challenging times.
Company morale dropped when the layoffs were announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit - feeling of enthusiasm and energy in a group or individual
  • Synonym: confidence - belief in oneself and one's abilities
  • Antonym: despair - complete loss or absence of hope
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From French *moral* (mood), derived from Latin *moralis* (moral).
