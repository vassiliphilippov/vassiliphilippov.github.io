filbert

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl.bɚt / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.bət / (BrE)
The edible nut of a hazel tree, especially any of several species of the genus Corylus.
edible nut of a hazel tree

Examples

She added chopped filberts to the cake batter for extra crunch.
The orchard grows both filberts and walnuts for local markets.
A squirrel gathered a filbert from the ground and scurried away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - The nut of the hazel tree, often synonymous with filbert

Etymology

From Old French 'felibre', from Medieval Latin 'philbertus', named after St. Philibert, whose feast coincided with hazelnut harvest.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlbɚt / (AmE), / ˈfɪlbət / (BrE)
A tree of the hazel family, especially Corylus avellana, that produces edible nuts; also, the nut of this tree.
nut-bearing tree or its nut

Examples

The orchard was full of filbert trees heavy with ripening nuts.
She likes to snack on roasted filbert nuts in the afternoon.
A filbert hedge can provide both privacy and delicious nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the nut of the hazel tree, similar to a filbert

Etymology

From Old French *filbert*, after St. Philibert, whose feast day falls in August when the nuts ripen.
