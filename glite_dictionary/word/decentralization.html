decentralization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌsɛntɚəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which population and industry move from urban centers to outlying areas, leading to less concentration in core cities
movement from cities to outskirts

Examples

The decentralization of the population led to rapid growth in suburban communities.
Government policies encouraged decentralization to reduce pressure on large metropolitan areas.
Economic decentralization shifted many industries to smaller towns outside the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urbanization - the process by which cities grow as more people move to urban areas

Etymology

From French 'décentralisation', from 'dé-' (away from) + 'centralisation' (centralization).

2. NOUN

/ diˌsɛntrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring authority or control from a central organization or government to local branches or authorities
transfer of power away from a central authority

Examples

Many governments pursue decentralization to give more decision-making power to local communities.
The decentralization of the company improved efficiency and employee satisfaction.
Following decentralization, the regional offices started developing their own policies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centralization - the concentration of control under a single authority

Etymology

From French 'décentralisation', from 'décentraliser' (to decentralize), based on 'central'.
