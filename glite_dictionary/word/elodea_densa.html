Elodea densa

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊdiə ˈdɛnsə / (AmE), / ɪˈləʊdiə ˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a South American aquatic plant with deep green foliage, often used in aquariums to oxygenate water; sometimes classified in the genus Egeria
aquatic plant used in aquariums

Examples

Many aquarium owners use Elodea densa to provide oxygen and shelter for their fish.
Scientists observed rapid growth of Elodea densa when water temperatures increased.
After planting Elodea densa in the pond, the water clarity improved significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egeria densa - alternative scientific name for the same aquatic plant species

Etymology

From New Latin genus name Elodea, and Latin densa meaning 'dense'.
