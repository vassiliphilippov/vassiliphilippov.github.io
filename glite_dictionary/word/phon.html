phon

1. NOUN

/ fɑn / (AmE), / fɒn / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the subjective loudness of sounds, based on perceived intensity by the human ear
unit of subjective loudness

Examples

The loudness of the concert was measured at 100 phons by the acoustics team.
A whisper typically registers around 20 phons on the loudness scale.
To compare subjective loudness, engineers often use the phon as a unit.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōnē' (voice, sound), used in the International System of Units for acoustics.
