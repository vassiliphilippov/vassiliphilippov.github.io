Abkhas

1. NOUN

/ æbˈkɑːz / (AmE), / æbˈkɑːz / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group native to the region east of the Black Sea, especially in Abkhazia
member of an ethnic group in Abkhazia

Examples

The Abkhas have a rich tradition of music and dance.
Many Abkhas speak both their native language and Russian.
During the conflict, thousands of Abkhas were displaced from their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhazian - a person from Abkhazia or of Abkhazian ethnicity

Etymology

From Russian Абхаз (Abkhaz), via Turkish Abhaz, referring to the Abkhaz people.

2. NOUN

/ æbˈkɑːz / (AmE), / æbˈkɑːz / (BrE)
a member of a Circassian people living east of the Black Sea, mainly in Abkhazia
member of a people from Abkhazia, eastern Black Sea

Examples

The Abkhas have a rich tradition of music and dance.
Many Abkhas continue to live in their ancestral homeland of Abkhazia.
The language spoken by the Abkhas is unrelated to most other Caucasian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhazian - a member of the same ethnic group; an adjective referring to Abkhazia or its people

Etymology

From Russian 'Abkhaz', from Abkhaz 'Apswa' (self-designation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08504963-n
Synset POS: n
