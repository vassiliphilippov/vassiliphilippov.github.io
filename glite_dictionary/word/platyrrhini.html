Platyrrhini

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈraɪnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈrɪnɪ / (BrE)
A group of primates comprising the New World monkeys, native to Central and South America, characterized by wide, outward-facing nostrils.
group of New World monkeys

Examples

The Platyrrhini are distinguished from Old World monkeys by their widely spaced nostrils.
All members of the Platyrrhini are native to Central and South America.
Scientists study the evolutionary adaptations of Platyrrhini to their rainforest habitats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catarrhini - group of primates including Old World monkeys and apes

Etymology

From modern Latin, plural of Platyrrhinus, from Greek platus (broad) + rhis (nose).

2. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈrɪnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈrɪniː / (BrE)
A superfamily of primates comprising the New World monkeys native to Central and South America, including capuchins, howler monkeys, sakis, spider monkeys, squirrel monkeys, titis, uakaris, woolly monkeys, marmosets, and tamarins.
superfamily of New World monkeys

Examples

The Platyrrhini are distinguished by their broad, laterally spaced nostrils.
Scientists study Platyrrhini to understand primate evolution in the Americas.
Marmosets and tamarins are both members of the Platyrrhini.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catarrhini - superfamily of Old World monkeys and apes

Etymology

From Greek platys (broad) + rhis, rhinos (nose), referring to their broad, flat noses.
