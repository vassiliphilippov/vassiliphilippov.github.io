functionary

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋkʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəri / (BrE)
a person who has a specific official position or role, especially in a government or large organization
official or officeholder

Examples

The event was attended by every senior functionary in the ministry.
As a government functionary, she had limited authority to make changes.
Many functionaries simply follow the rules without questioning their purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: official - a person holding public office or having official duties

Etymology

From French 'fonctionnaire', from 'fonction' (function), from Latin 'functio' (performance).
