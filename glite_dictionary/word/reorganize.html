to reorganize

1. VERB

/ riˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / riˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize something in a new or different way, often to improve efficiency or structure
arrange again in a new way

Examples

After the merger, the new management decided to reorganize the entire department.
The school plans to reorganize its curriculum to better serve students' needs.
They had to reorganize their finances when unexpected expenses arose.

Synset Examples

We must reorganize the company if we don't want to go under

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restructure - to change the structure of something, especially a company
  • Antonym: to disorganize - to disrupt the organization of; to make disorderly

Etymology

From re- (again) + organize; organize from French organiser, from Medieval Latin organizare.

2. VERB

/ riˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / riˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange something again or differently, especially in order to improve it after problems or changes
to arrange again differently

Examples

After the merger, the company decided to reorganize its entire management structure.
Following the fire, the school had to reorganize its classrooms and schedule.
She took some time to reorganize her priorities after the setback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restructure - to organize differently, usually for efficiency after changes
  • Antonym: to disorganize - to destroy the organization or arrangement of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + organize, modeled after French ‘réorganiser’.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
