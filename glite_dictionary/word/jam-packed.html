jam-packed

1. ADJ

/ dʒæm ˈpækt / (AmE), / dʒæm ˈpækt / (BrE)
Extremely full or crowded; filled to capacity.
extremely crowded or full

Examples

The train was so jam-packed that there was barely room to stand.
The stadium was jam-packed with cheering supporters.
Her suitcase was jam-packed with clothes and souvenirs from her trip.

Synset Examples

a suitcase jammed with dirty clothes
stands jam-packed with fans
a packed theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packed - filled to capacity
  • Synonym: crammed - completely filled or crowded
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing

Etymology

From 'jam' (to squeeze tightly) and 'packed' (filled), first attested in early 20th century English.
