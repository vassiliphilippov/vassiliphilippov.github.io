Dhu al-Qadah

1. NOUN

/ duː ælˈkɑːdə / (AmE), / duː ælˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
the eleventh month of the Islamic calendar, regarded as a sacred month during which warfare is traditionally forbidden
eleventh month of the Islamic calendar

Examples

The Hajj pilgrimage often begins in the month of Dhu al-Qadah in the Islamic calendar.
Muslims around the world recognize Dhu al-Qadah as one of the four sacred months.
Many historical treaties were signed during Dhu al-Qadah to honor its peaceful significance.

Etymology

From Arabic ذو القعدة (dhū al-qaʿdah), meaning 'the one of sitting/rest', referring to cessation of hostilities.
