wool

1. NOUN

/ wʊl / (AmE), / wʊl / (BrE)
the soft, thick hair that grows on the bodies of sheep, yaks, and some other animals, used in making yarn, fabric, and textiles
soft hair of sheep and similar animals

Examples

The farmer collected wool from the sheep during the annual shearing.
Merino sheep are prized for their fine wool, which is used in luxury clothing.
Warm sweaters are often made from natural wool to help keep you comfortable in winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleece - woolly coat of a sheep or similar animal, especially as shorn

Etymology

Old English wull (wool), from Proto-Germanic *wullō.

2. NOUN

/ wʊl / (AmE), / wʊl / (BrE)
the soft, thick hair taken from sheep and some other animals, used to make yarn, fabric, or clothes
hair of sheep used for yarn or fabric

Examples

This sweater is made entirely of wool, so it's both warm and soft.
Farmers shear sheep in the spring to collect their wool.
She spun the raw wool into fine yarn for knitting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cotton - soft white fiber from cotton plant, used as textile
  • Synonym: yarn - thread made by twisting fibers, used for knitting or weaving

Etymology

Old English 'wull', of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'wol' and German 'Wolle'.

3. NOUN

/ wʊl / (AmE), / wʊl / (BrE)
A textile fiber or fabric made from the fleece of sheep or similar animals, valued for its warmth and texture.
fabric from sheep's hair

Examples

This sweater is made of pure wool and feels very soft against the skin.
The wool industry is important in countries like Australia and New Zealand.
She knitted a scarf using thick, colorful wool for her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft white fibrous substance from cotton plants, used for textile fabric
  • Synonym: silk - fine, soft, lustrous fiber produced by silkworms, used in textiles
  • Antonym: synthetic - man-made fiber used in textiles

Etymology

Old English wull, from Proto-Germanic *wullō (compare German Wolle, Dutch wol).
