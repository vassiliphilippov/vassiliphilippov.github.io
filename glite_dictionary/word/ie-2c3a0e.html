i.e.

1. ADV

/ aɪ ˈi / (AmE), / aɪ ˈiː / (BrE)
used to introduce a rephrasing, clarification, or more precise explanation of a statement; that is to say.
that is to say; in other words

Examples

Each student must submit one project, i.e., a completed research paper, by next Friday.
She prefers citrus fruits, i.e., oranges and lemons, in her smoothies.
The meeting will be held in the main hall, i.e., Room 101 on the first floor.

Etymology

From Latin 'id est' meaning 'that is'.

2. ADV

/ aɪ ˈi / (AmE), / aɪ ˈiː / (BrE)
used to introduce a clearer, more exact explanation of something just mentioned; that is to say.
that is to say; clarifying explanation

Examples

The report must be finished by EOD, i.e., by the end of the day.
You should list all major expenses, i.e., rent, utilities, and groceries.
We need to address the main issue, i.e., the lack of communication within the team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: e.g. - for example; used to introduce examples

Etymology

From Latin 'id est' meaning 'that is'.
