to lollygag

1. VERB

/ ˈlɑliˌɡæɡ / (AmE), / ˈlɒliˌɡæɡ / (BrE)
to spend time aimlessly without any apparent purpose; to dawdle or loiter
spend time aimlessly; dawdle
informal american

Examples

The children lollygagged in the park instead of going straight home.
If you lollygag any longer, you'll be late for your meeting.
Stop lollygagging and help me clean the kitchen.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to dawdle - to waste time or move slowly

Etymology

Probably from dialectal lolly (to loll) + gag (to fool or deceive), origin late 19th century US slang.
