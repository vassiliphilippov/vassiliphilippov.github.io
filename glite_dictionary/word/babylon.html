Babylon

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæb.ɪ.lɑn / (AmE), / ˈbab.ɪ.lən / (BrE)
the principal city of ancient Mesopotamia, once the capital of the Babylonian Empire, known for its wealth, culture, and the Hanging Gardens
ancient city and capital of Babylonia

Examples

The ruins of Babylon attract archaeologists from around the world.
Legends say that Babylon was home to the Hanging Gardens, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
In the Bible, Babylon is often portrayed as a symbol of decadence and pride.

Etymology

From Greek Babylōn, from Akkadian Bāb-ilu, meaning 'Gate of God'.
