overdone

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈdʌn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdʌn / (BrE)
(of food) cooked for too long so that it is less pleasant to eat but still edible.
cooked for too long (food)

Examples

He complained that his steak was overdone and tough to chew.
The chicken came out a bit overdone, but everyone still ate it.
If vegetables are overdone, they lose their color and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underdone - (of food) not cooked enough
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00620483-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈdʌn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdʌn / (BrE)
made to seem greater, more extreme, or more important than is true or reasonable; exaggerated.
excessively exaggerated or overstated

Examples

His response to the minor problem was completely overdone.
The praise for the film felt a bit overdone given its flaws.
Some of her stories about childhood hardships are clearly overdone for dramatic effect.

Synset Examples

an exaggerated opinion of oneself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggerated - described as greater than is true; overstated
  • Antonym: understated - presented as less important or extreme than is true

Synset POS: s
