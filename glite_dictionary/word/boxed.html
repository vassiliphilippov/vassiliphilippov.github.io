boxed

1. ADJ

/ bɑkst / (AmE), / bɒkst / (BrE)
enclosed in or set off by a border or box, often for emphasis or clarity, especially in printed or graphical material
enclosed in a border or box (layout/design)

Examples

The important updates were presented in a boxed section for easy reference.
Each advertisement appeared as a boxed announcement on the front page.
Instructions are often boxed to distinguish them from the main text.

Synset Examples

boxed sections of the report
boxed announcements in the newspaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framed - enclosed or surrounded by a border or edge
  • Synonym: highlighted - emphasized or made to stand out visually

2. ADJ

/ bɑkst / (AmE), / bɒkst / (BrE)
enclosed in or as if in a box; contained within a box-shaped space or container
enclosed in a box or box-like space

Examples

The cigars were carefully boxed to preserve their freshness.
After moving into the small apartment, she often felt boxed in by the lack of space.
They delivered the boxed set directly to my doorstep.

Synset Examples

boxed cigars
a confining boxed-in space
felt boxed in by the traffic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unboxed - not enclosed in a box or container
