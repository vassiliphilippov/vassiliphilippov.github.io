Blenheim

1. PROPN

/ ˈblɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈblɛnɪm / (BrE)
the Battle of Blenheim; a decisive battle fought in 1704 during the War of the Spanish Succession, where the First Duke of Marlborough and Prince Eugene of Savoy defeated the French forces
historic 1704 battle in the War of the Spanish Succession

Examples

The outcome of Blenheim shifted the balance of power in Europe.
Many historians study Blenheim as a turning point in the War of the Spanish Succession.
Commemorations of the victory at Blenheim are still held in Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Waterloo - decisive 1815 battle marking the final defeat of Napoleon

Etymology

Named after the village of Blindheim (Blenheim), Bavaria, near where the battle was fought.
