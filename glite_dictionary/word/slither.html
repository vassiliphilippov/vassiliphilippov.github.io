to slither

1. VERB

/ ˈslɪðɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɪðə / (BrE)
to move smoothly, quietly, and often secretly, typically by sliding along a surface
move smoothly or sneakily

Examples

The snake managed to slither silently across the crowded path without being noticed.
He tried to slither out of the room before anyone could ask him a question.
The spy slithered into the shadows, blending in with the darkness.

Synset Examples

They slid through the wicket in the big gate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it
  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully in order to avoid being heard or noticed

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to Middle English slitheren (to slide or glide).
