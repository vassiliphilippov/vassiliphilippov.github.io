Darwinian

1. NOUN

/ dɑːrˈwɪniən / (AmE), / dɑːˈwɪniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates the theory of evolution by natural selection as proposed by Charles Darwin
advocate of Darwinism

Examples

As a proud Darwinian, she often defended the theory of natural selection in heated debates.
Many Darwinians contributed to modern evolutionary biology in the twentieth century.
Despite criticism, the Darwinian maintained unwavering support for evolutionary ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolutionist - person who believes in the theory of evolution

Etymology

From Darwin (Charles Darwin, 1809–1882) + -ian, denoting association or advocacy.

2. ADJ

/ dɑrˈwɪniən / (AmE), / dɑːˈwɪniən / (BrE)
relating to Charles Darwin or his theory of evolution by natural selection.
relating to Darwin's theory of evolution

Examples

The Darwinian theory of natural selection changed the way scientists think about evolution.
Many biologists support a Darwinian explanation for the development of complex traits.
Her research takes a Darwinian approach to studying animal behavior.

Synset Examples

Darwinian theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolutionary - relating to the process of evolution

Etymology

Formed from Charles Darwin's surname, with the adjectival suffix -ian.
