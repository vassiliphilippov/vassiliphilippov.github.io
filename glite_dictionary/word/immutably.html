immutably

1. ADV

/ ɪˈmjutəbli / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːtəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be changed or altered; unchangeably.
in an unchangeable manner

Examples

The laws of physics are believed by many to operate immutably throughout the universe.
Her decision was set immutably in her mind, admitting no possibility of reconsideration.
Traditions were immutably passed down through generations in that isolated village.

Synset Examples

his views were unchangeably fixed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchangeably - in a way that cannot be changed

Etymology

From Latin 'immutabilis' (unchangeable), from 'in-' (not) and 'mutabilis' (able to change).
