windtalker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌtɔkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌtɔːkə / (BrE)
A member of the Navajo people who used their native language to create and transmit secret codes for the U.S. military during World War II.
Navajo code maker in World War II

Examples

The film tells the story of a windtalker serving in the Pacific during World War II.
As a windtalker, he played a crucial role in keeping military communications secure.
The contributions of every windtalker were vital to the Allied victory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code talker - a person, especially a Native American, who transmitted messages in a coded language during wartime

Etymology

Formed from 'wind' and 'talker', referring to transmitting speech (messages) by radio waves ('through the air').
