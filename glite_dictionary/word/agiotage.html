agiotage

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒiətɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈadʒɪətɑːʒ / (BrE)
Speculative dealing in stock exchange securities or foreign exchange, especially to profit from fluctuations in values.
speculative dealing in securities or currency

Examples

The company was accused of agiotage during the volatile market conditions.
Regulatory bodies are often concerned about excessive agiotage influencing stock prices.
Historic accounts detail the impact of agiotage on foreign exchange markets in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - investment in stocks, property, or other ventures with the hope of gain but with the risk of loss

Etymology

From French 'agiotage', from 'agioter' (to speculate), related to 'agio' (exchange rate difference).

2. NOUN

/ ˌædʒiˈoʊtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈadʒɪətɪdʒ / (BrE)
the practice of charging a fee for exchanging currencies or engaging in speculative currency transactions
fee or speculation in currency exchange

Examples

The bank charged a small agiotage for converting dollars into euros.
In the eighteenth century, agiotage was a common practice among currency traders.
Some customers found the agiotage rates at local exchanges to be excessive.

Etymology

From French 'agiotage', from 'agioter' (to speculate), possibly from Italian 'aggiottare'.
