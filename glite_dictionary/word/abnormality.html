abnormality

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.nɔːrˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌæb.nɔːˈmæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
A medical condition in which part of the body does not develop normally, often due to genetic defects or developmental problems.
medical condition of abnormal development
medical

Examples

Genetic testing can help identify an abnormality before symptoms develop.
She was born with a heart abnormality that required surgery.
Doctors detected an abnormality in the baby's chromosome structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defect - a fault or imperfection, especially in a physical structure
  • Synonym: malformation - a deformity or abnormal structural development

Etymology

From Latin 'abnormalis' (irregular) + '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæb.nɔːrˈmæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌæb.nɔːˈmæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A condition in which intelligence is significantly below the normal range, originally referring to intellectual retardation or impairment.
below-normal intelligence condition
medical

Examples

The doctor noted an abnormality in the child's cognitive development.
Certain abnormalities of intelligence may require specialized educational support.
Early intervention can help children with mental abnormality reach their potential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental retardation - significant cognitive impairment; intellectual disability
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical

Etymology

From 'abnormal' + '-ity', from Latin 'abnormis' (irregular) and '-itas' (noun-forming suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæb.nɔːrˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌæb.nɔːˈmæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unusual, strange, or not normal, often in a noticeable or significant way
state of being unusual or not normal

Examples

The doctor found an abnormality in the patient's heartbeat during the examination.
Unusual shapes in the X-ray revealed a possible abnormality in her bone structure.
Scientists studied the genetic abnormality to understand its effects on development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical
  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard or expected

Etymology

From Latin abnormis (abnormal) + -ity suffix.

4. NOUN

/ ˌæbˌnɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌæb.nɔːˈmæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
Behavior or quality that departs from what is considered normal, especially by breaching rules, etiquette, customs, or morality.
departure from normal behavior or rules

Examples

Such abnormality in her behavior surprised everyone at the formal dinner.
The court ruled that repeated abnormality in conduct was a violation of the school's code.
His disregard for social norms was seen as an abnormality within his conservative community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard or expected
  • Antonym: normalcy - state of being normal

Etymology

From Latin abnormalitas, formed from ab- (away from) + norma (rule, standard), via abnormal + -ity.
