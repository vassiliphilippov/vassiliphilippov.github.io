grouse

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊs / (AmE), / ɡraʊs / (BrE)
a plump-bodied game bird with feathered legs and feet, found in the Northern Hemisphere and hunted for sport and food.
a plump-bodied game bird

Examples

A grouse flushed from the underbrush as we walked through the forest.
During hunting season, several grouse were spotted near the moor.
The chef prepared a delicious dish of roasted grouse for the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a similar type of game bird

Etymology

From late 16th-century Scottish dialect, of uncertain origin, possibly from Scottish Gaelic 'grùscar' (heath-dweller).

3. NOUN

/ ɡraʊs / (AmE), / ɡraʊs / (BrE)
the flesh of any of several game birds of the family Tetraonidae, typically roasted and considered too dry to broil
flesh of a game bird, usually roasted

Examples

Roast grouse was the highlight of the evening meal at the country lodge.
The chef recommended red wine to pair with the flavor of grouse.
He had never tasted grouse before and was surprised by its rich, distinctive taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - flesh of a partridge, another game bird eaten as food

Etymology

From Old French 'gruesche' (a kind of bird), further origin uncertain.
