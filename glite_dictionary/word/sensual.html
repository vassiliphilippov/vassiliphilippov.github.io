sensual

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sju.əl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from physical pleasure, especially sexual or involving the senses rather than the intellect
relating to physical or sexual pleasure

Examples

The chef prepared a meal that was a truly sensual experience for all the senses.
She wore a sensual perfume that lingered in the air long after she left the room.
His paintings often evoke a sensual appreciation for color, texture, and form.

Synset Examples

animal instincts
carnal knowledge
fleshly desire
a sensual delight in eating
music is the only sensual pleasure without vice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexual - relating to sex or sexuality
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the mind or soul rather than the body

Etymology

From Latin sensualis, from sensus (sense).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sjʊ.əl / (BrE)
Sexually exciting or gratifying; arousing or intended to arouse sexual desire or pleasure.
sexually exciting or gratifying

Examples

The movie was filled with sensual scenes that pushed the boundaries of traditional romance.
She wore a sensual perfume that seemed to linger in the air even after she had left.
The artist's paintings are known for their sensual depictions of the human form.

Synset Examples

sensual excesses
a sultry look
a sultry dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erotic - relating to sexual desire or excitement
  • Antonym: chaste - abstaining from sexual activity, not sensual

Etymology

From Latin 'sensualis' (endowed with feeling), from 'sensus' (feeling).
