Canis lupus arctos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnɪs ˈluːpəs ˈɑːrktoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnɪs ˈluːpəs ˈɑːktɒs / (BrE)
a subspecies of the gray wolf found in Arctic North America, typically characterized by white fur and a black-tipped tail
subspecies of Arctic wolf

Examples

The Canis lupus arctos is well adapted to survive the freezing temperatures of the Arctic tundra.
Researchers observed a pack of Canis lupus arctos hunting caribou last winter.
Because of its white coat, Canis lupus arctos is often difficult to spot against the snow.

Etymology

From Latin: Canis (dog), lupus (wolf), arctos (bear; here meaning 'of the north' from Greek arktos, 'north' or 'bear').
