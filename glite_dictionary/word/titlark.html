titlark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪtˌlɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtɪtˌlɑːk / (BrE)
A small ground-dwelling songbird, typically found in open country, with streaky brown plumage; a pipit.
a small ground-dwelling songbird (pipit)

Examples

We spotted a titlark hopping through the tall grass near the lake.
The fields in early spring are alive with the songs of the titlark.
A titlark blends in well with its surroundings due to its streaky brown plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipit - a small, ground-dwelling songbird (family Motacillidae)

Etymology

From 'tit' (small bird) + 'lark'; related to the resemblance to larks.
