incisively

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
in a clear, direct, and sharp manner that shows deep understanding or cutting insight
in a clear, penetrating way

Examples

She spoke incisively about the problems facing the community.
The critic analyzed the film incisively, pointing out its strengths and weaknesses with precision.
During the debate, he responded incisively to each question, demonstrating his expertise.

Synset Examples

he was incisively critical

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bluntly - in a direct and sometimes rude manner without sharpness or insight

Etymology

From 'incisive' + '-ly'; 'incisive' from Latin 'incidere' (to cut into).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
in a clear, direct, and analytical manner; expressing ideas sharply and effectively.
in a clear, direct manner

Examples

The lawyer spoke incisively, quickly exposing the flaws in the opposing argument.
She analyzes problems incisively, helping her team reach effective solutions.
His critique was delivered incisively, leaving no room for doubt about his viewpoint.

Synset Examples

she always expressed herself precisely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - in a clear and distinct manner
  • Antonym: vaguely - in an unclear or indistinct manner

Etymology

From 'incisive' (from Latin 'incisivus', from 'incidere' meaning 'to cut into') + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00370083-r
Synset POS: r
