Hygrophorus russula

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌgrɑːfərəs ˈrʌsələ / (AmE), / hɪˌɡrɒfərəs ˈrʌsələ / (BrE)
an edible mushroom with a reddish cap, pale close gills, and a dry stalk, typically found under hardwood trees
a species of edible mushroom with a reddish cap

Examples

We discovered several Hygrophorus russula while foraging in the woods last autumn.
The chef prepared a delicious risotto using fresh Hygrophorus russula mushrooms.
Field guides recommend checking the gill color to distinguish Hygrophorus russula from similar species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Russula - a related genus of similar brittle-gilled mushrooms

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Hygrophorus' (from Greek 'hygros' meaning moist, and 'phoros' meaning bearing) and Latin 'russula' (reddish).
