excoriation

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˌskɔriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌskɔːrɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
severe or harsh criticism; strong censure
severe criticism

Examples

The critic's excoriation of the author's latest novel was both detailed and relentless.
After his speech, the politician faced excoriation from the media for his controversial remarks.
Her decision was met with public excoriation, leading to widespread debate about her actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censure - express severe disapproval of someone or something
  • Antonym: praise - express admiration or approval

Etymology

From Latin ex (out) + corium (skin), originally meaning 'the act of stripping off skin', later extended to strong verbal attack.

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˌskɔriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌskɔːriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a place where the skin has been scraped or worn away, often as a result of injury or irritation
an area of scraped or worn skin
medical

Examples

He went to the doctor to treat the excoriation on his knee after the fall.
Repeated scratching can lead to excoriation of the affected areas.
The nurse cleaned the excoriation carefully to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasion - an area where skin or other tissue is scraped away, typically by friction

Etymology

From Latin excoriatio, from excoriare (to strip the skin), from ex- (out) + corium (skin).
