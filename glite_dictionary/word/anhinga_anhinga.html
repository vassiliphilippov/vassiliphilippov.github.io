Anhinga anhinga

1. NOUN

/ ænˈhɪŋɡə ænˈhɪŋɡə / (AmE), / ænˈhɪŋɡə ænˈhɪŋɡə / (BrE)
a large blackish water bird of the Americas, also known as the snakebird, found in swampy regions and noted for its long neck and swimming habits
blackish water bird of the Americas

Examples

The Anhinga anhinga is often seen drying its wings after swimming in southern wetlands.
Researchers observed a nest of Anhinga anhinga chicks beside the river.
Unlike cormorants, the Anhinga anhinga swims with only its head and neck above water, looking like a snake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anhinga - a long-necked water bird found in warm parts of the Americas, also called snakebird

Etymology

From the Tupi–Guarani (Brazilian indigenous) language, 'anhingá', meaning 'devil bird'. Scientific name standardized in zoological nomenclature.
