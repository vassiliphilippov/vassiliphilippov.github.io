Athyriaceae

1. NOUN

/ æˌθɪriˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / æˌθɪərɪˈeɪsi.i / (BrE)
a family of ferns in some classification systems, sometimes considered a subgroup of Polypodiaceae
a family of ferns (plants)

Examples

The Athyriaceae family includes several species commonly known as lady ferns.
Botanists debate whether Athyriaceae should be recognized as a separate family or included within Polypodiaceae.
The distribution of Athyriaceae spans temperate and tropical regions worldwide.

Etymology

From the genus name Athyrium, combined with the standard scientific family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθɪriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌæθɪrɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns in some botanical classification systems, comprising various genera formerly included in Polypodiaceae.
a family of ferns (botany)

Examples

Many woodland ferns in northern regions belong to the Athyriaceae.
Recent studies have led some botanists to recognize Athyriaceae as a distinct family.
The genus Athyrium gives its name to the Athyriaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - A family of ferns formerly including Athyriaceae; broader family classification

Etymology

From genus name Athyrium, ultimately from Greek athyros (doorless, possibly referring to spore structures).
