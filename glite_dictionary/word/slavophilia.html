Slavophilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌslævəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌslɑːvəˈfɪliə / (BrE)
A 19th-century intellectual movement in Russia that advocated developing the Russian Empire based on traditional Slavic values and institutions.
Russian movement favoring traditional Slavic values

Examples

Many Russian intellectuals in the nineteenth century were attracted to Slavophilia as a way to preserve national identity.
The debate between Slavophilia and Westernization shaped much of Russian society in the 1800s.
His interest in Slavophilia led him to study Slavic folklore and Orthodox traditions.

Synset Examples

The growth of medieval scholarship coincided with the emergence in the early 19th century of Pan-Slavism in the South Slavic and Western Slavic lands and what is called Slavophilia in Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pan-Slavism - The movement advocating political and cultural unity of all Slavic peoples
  • Antonym: Westernization - Adoption of Western ideas and social systems

Etymology

From Russian 'словянофильство' (slovyanofil'stvo), from 'Slav' + Greek 'philia' (love, affinity).
