incitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.saɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.saɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of urging, provoking, or stimulating someone to take action, especially towards something controversial or aggressive
the act of urging or provoking action

Examples

The politician's speech was seen as an incitation to protest among the crowd.
Evidence showed that the group was guilty of incitation to violence during the demonstration.
She was reprimanded for her incitation of unrest at the university.

Synset Examples

the incitement of mutiny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of provoking unlawful behavior or urging someone to behave unlawfully
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of deterring someone from taking action

Etymology

From Latin 'incitatio', from 'incitare' (to urge, encourage).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsaɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌsaɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
something that encourages, provokes, or stirs someone to action or feeling
act or means of provoking or encouraging

Examples

His speech served as an incitation to rebellion among the crowd.
Without any clear incitation, she decided to pursue her dream of starting a business.
The sudden drop in temperature was an incitation for the birds to begin their migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of provoking unlawful behavior or urging someone to behave unlawfully
  • Synonym: provocation - action or speech that makes someone annoyed or angry, especially deliberately

Etymology

From Latin incitatio (an urging on), from incitare (to urge on).
