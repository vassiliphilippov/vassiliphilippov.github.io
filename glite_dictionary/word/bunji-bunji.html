bunji-bunji

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌn.dʒiˈbʌn.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌbʌn.dʒiˈbʌn.dʒi / (BrE)
An Australian timber tree, especially Alphitonia excelsa, whose bark is used to produce a poison.
Australian tree yielding poisonous bark

Examples

The bark of the bunji-bunji was traditionally used by Indigenous Australians to prepare fish poison.
Many farmers value the bunji-bunji for its hard timber and shade in pastures.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of the bunji-bunji to better understand its toxic properties.

Etymology

From an Australian Aboriginal language (specific origin uncertain), referring to the tree Alphitonia excelsa.
