Ulmus campestris sarniensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs kæmˈpɛstrɪs sɑːˈnɪənsɪs / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs kəmˈpɛstrɪs sɑːˈnɪənsɪs / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of the English elm with upright branches and broader leaves, often grown ornamentally
variety of English elm tree

Examples

The striking upright form of the Ulmus campestris sarniensis makes it popular in formal landscaping.
Botanists have studied the unique leaf shape of the Ulmus campestris sarniensis for clues about elm evolution.
A row of Ulmus campestris sarniensis lines the garden, providing shade with their broad leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English elm - a large species of elm native to Europe

Etymology

From Latin: Ulmus (elm) campestris (of the fields) sarniensis (of Guernsey).
