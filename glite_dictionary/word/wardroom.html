wardroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrdˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdˌruːm / (BrE)
The dining and recreation quarters for officers (except the captain) on a warship.
officers' dining room on a warship

Examples

Only officers were permitted to enter the wardroom during meal times.
After the meeting, the officers gathered in the wardroom for coffee and conversation.
The ship's wardroom was elegantly furnished for both dining and relaxation.

Etymology

From 'ward' (a room or division) + 'room', originally referring to a guarded or exclusive room.
