viol

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.əl / (BrE)
a member of a family of bowed stringed instruments, popular during the Renaissance and Baroque periods, that preceded the violin family
historical bowed string instrument

Examples

The musician played a beautiful melody on the viol during the concert.
Many Baroque compositions were originally written for the viol, not the violin.
She was fascinated by antique instruments and added a viol to her collection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: violin - more modern bowed string instrument that succeeded the viol

Etymology

From French 'viole', from Old Provençal 'viola', likely from Latin 'vitula' (stringed instrument).
