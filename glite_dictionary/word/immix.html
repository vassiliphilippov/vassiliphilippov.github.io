to immix

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɪks / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪks / (BrE)
to mix or blend together different elements or substances
mix or blend together
archaic

Examples

You should not immix these two chemicals without proper equipment.
Some artists prefer to immix their own paints to achieve unique colors.
He warned not to immix personal feelings with professional decisions.

Synset Examples

The colors blend well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix substances so that they combine together
  • Synonym: combine - to join or mix two or more things together

Etymology

From Latin 'immiscere', from 'in-' (in) + 'miscere' (to mix).
