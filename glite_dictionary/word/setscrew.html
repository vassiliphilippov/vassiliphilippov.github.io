setscrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌskru / (AmE), / ˈsɛtskruː / (BrE)
a screw, often without a head, used to secure an object within or against another object, typically to prevent relative movement on a shaft
screw for securing parts together

Examples

The mechanic used a setscrew to fasten the pulley securely onto the motor shaft.
Make sure the setscrew is tightened properly to prevent the gear from slipping.
He lost the tiny setscrew that held the knob in place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grub screw - screw without a head, used to secure an object in place on a shaft

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt.skuː / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.skruː / (BrE)
A screw used to adjust the position or tension of a part, especially to secure one object within or against another.
screw for securing or adjusting parts

Examples

The mechanic tightened the setscrew to secure the gear on the shaft.
You'll need a small hex key to adjust the setscrew on this spring mechanism.
If the setscrew is too loose, the tension will not hold properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grub screw - a type of setscrew with a head that sits flush or below the surface

Etymology

From 'set' (to place in a specified position) + 'screw'.
