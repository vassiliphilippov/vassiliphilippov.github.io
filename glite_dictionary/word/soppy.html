soppy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑpi / (AmE), / ˈsɒpi / (BrE)
excessively sentimental or emotional, often to the point of being insincere or foolish
overly sentimental or emotional
informal british

Examples

She burst into tears during that soppy movie about lost puppies.
He always writes such soppy love letters that make me cringe a little.
I can’t stand soppy songs—they’re just too sentimental for my taste.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or causing excessive emotion
  • Synonym: maudlin - self-pityingly tearfully sentimental
  • Antonym: hard-hearted - lacking compassion or sympathy

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑpi / (AmE), / ˈsɒpi / (BrE)
Extremely wet; soaked through with liquid.
thoroughly wet or soaked

Examples

He arrived at the office in soppy clothes after walking in the rain.
The towel was soppy from being left in the bathtub overnight.
My shoes are soppy after stepping into the puddle.

Synset Examples

stood at the door drenched (or soaked) by the rain
the speaker's sodden collar
soppy clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodden - soaked with liquid; saturated
  • Synonym: drenched - very wet; soaked through
