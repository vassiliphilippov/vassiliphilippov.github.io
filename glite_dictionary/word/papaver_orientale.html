Papaver orientale

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər ɔːr.iˈen.teɪl / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə ɔː.rɪˈen.teɪl / (BrE)
A species of perennial poppy native to Asia, cultivated for its large, showy scarlet, pink, or orange flowers.
ornamental Asiatic perennial poppy

Examples

Many gardeners plant Papaver orientale for its dramatic spring blooms.
The bright petals of Papaver orientale stand out in the perennial border.
She ordered seeds of Papaver orientale to add color to her garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oriental poppy - common name for Papaver orientale

Etymology

Latin: 'Papaver' meaning 'poppy'; 'orientale' from Latin meaning 'eastern', referring to its Asian origin.
