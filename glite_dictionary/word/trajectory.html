trajectory

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdʒɛktəˌri / (AmE), / trəˈdʒɛktəri / (BrE)
the curved path that a moving object follows through air or space, especially the path of a projectile
the path of a moving object in space

Examples

The rocket's trajectory was carefully calculated to ensure a safe landing on Mars.
A baseball's trajectory can be affected by the wind.
She tracked the trajectory of the comet across the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: path - the route or course along which something travels or moves

Etymology

From Latin trajectoria via, from traicere 'to throw across'.
