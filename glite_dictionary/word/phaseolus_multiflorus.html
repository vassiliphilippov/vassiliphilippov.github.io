Phaseolus multiflorus

1. NOUN

/ fəˌsiːoʊləs ˌmʌltɪˈflɔrəs / (AmE), / fəˌsiːəʊləs ˌmʌltɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A tropical American perennial bean plant with red flowers and mottled black seeds, widely cultivated as the runner bean, especially in Britain.
species of runner bean plant

Examples

Many gardeners in England grow Phaseolus multiflorus for its attractive flowers and edible pods.
The seeds of Phaseolus multiflorus are often used in traditional British dishes.
Unlike Phaseolus vulgaris, Phaseolus multiflorus is a perennial plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runner bean - common name for Phaseolus multiflorus, cultivated for its edible pods and seeds
  • Synonym: Phaseolus vulgaris - common bean, a related species of bean plant

Etymology

From Latin: Phaseolus (bean genus) + multiflorus (many-flowered).
