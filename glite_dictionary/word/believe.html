to believe

1. VERB

/ bəˈliv / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to accept that something is true, especially without proof; to trust in the truth or reliability of someone or something
accept as true; trust in truth

Examples

Many people believe that honesty is the best policy.
She couldn't believe her eyes when she saw the surprise.
Do you believe everything you read online?

Synset Examples

You cannot believe this man
Should we believe a publication like the National Enquirer?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about; to disbelieve
  • Synonym: trust - to place confidence in or rely on someone or something
  • Antonym: suspect - to have an idea or impression but without certain proof

Etymology

From Old English 'belȳfan', related to Old High German 'gilouben', meaning 'to believe'.

accept as true; consider to be real

  • Synonym: trust - to have confidence in the reliability or truth of something or someone

5. VERB

/ bəˈliv / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to have faith in a religion or creed; to be a believer, especially in a religious sense
have faith; follow a religious doctrine

Examples

Many people believe deeply in their chosen religion.
After years of searching, she began to believe in a higher power.
He was raised to believe, but later found his own path.

Synset Examples

When you hear his sermons, you will be able to believe, too

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - to have confidence in the reliability or truth of something or someone
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about; lack belief or conviction

Etymology

From Old English 'belȳfan', related to 'lief' (dear, willing) and across Germanic roots.
