hob

1. NOUN

/ hɑb / (AmE), / hɒb / (BrE)
A shelf or projection at the side of a fireplace, used for keeping food or utensils warm.
shelf beside fireplace for warming

Examples

She placed the teapot on the hob to keep it warm throughout the evening.
Old cottages often have a stone hob beside the fireplace for resting pots and pans.
He set the stew to simmer on the hob while the bread finished baking.

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from Middle English hobbe (protuberance, knob).

/ hɑb / (AmE), / hɒb / (BrE)
a hard steel tool with cutting edges used to cut gear teeth in manufacturing processes
gear-cutting steel tool

Examples

Precision is crucial when selecting the right hob for gear manufacturing.
After several hours, the hob needed to be sharpened to maintain accuracy.

3. NOUN

/ hɑb / (AmE), / hɒb / (BrE)
In folklore, a small, grotesque supernatural creature said to cause trouble for humans.
troublesome folklore creature

Examples

According to the old tales, a hob would hide in the kitchen and play tricks on the family.
The villagers blamed the missing milk on a mischievous hob living in the barn.
Folklore describes a hob as both a nuisance and sometimes a reluctant helper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly creature in folklore
  • Synonym: brownie - a helpful household spirit in Scottish folklore

Etymology

From Middle English hobbe, a diminutive form of the name Robin, often used for household spirits in English folklore.

/ hɑb / (AmE), / hɒb / (BrE)

5. NOUN

/ hɑb / (AmE), / hɒb / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, mischievous fairy or goblin often considered helpful but sometimes troublesome.
mischievous fairy or goblin (folklore)

Examples

In old tales, a hob would tidy the cottage if offered a bowl of cream.
Some believed a hob lived by the hearth, playing tricks on lazy servants.
The villagers left gifts for the hob, hoping to win its favor and avoid mischief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brownie - a helpful household spirit in Scottish folklore
  • Synonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly fairy in folklore
  • Synonym: elf - a supernatural creature of folklore, usually depicted as small, elusive beings

Etymology

Probably from Middle English hobbe, a diminutive form of the name Robin, commonly used for a rustic sprite.
