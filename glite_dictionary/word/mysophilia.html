mysophilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪsəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəˈfɪlɪə / (BrE)
A sexual or abnormal attraction to filth or dirt.
attraction to filth or dirt
medical

Examples

The psychologist diagnosed the patient with mysophilia after extensive interviews.
Some rare case studies in psychiatry discuss mysophilia as a paraphilia.
His mysophilia led him to seek out unsanitary environments for arousal.

Etymology

From Greek 'mysos' (filth) + 'philia' (love, attraction).
