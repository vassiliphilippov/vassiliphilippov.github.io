atomistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæt̬.əˈmɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæt.əˈmɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
consisting of or divided into separate, often disparate, elements; viewing something as made up of independent parts rather than as a whole
divided into separate parts

Examples

The company's atomistic structure led to poor communication between departments.
Her atomistic approach to society ignores the ways people are influenced by their communities.
Some critics argue that modern economics takes too atomistic a view of individual behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holistic - characterized by the belief that the parts of something are interconnected and can be explained only by reference to the whole

Etymology

From 'atom' (from Greek 'atomos', indivisible) + '-istic'.
