filibuster

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a legislator who gives long speeches to delay or obstruct legislative action, especially in a parliamentary context
legislator who delays by long speeches

Examples

The senator became a famous filibuster, known for his lengthy speeches against proposed laws.
As a dedicated filibuster, she often delayed crucial votes in the legislature.
Some critics argue that the role of a filibuster undermines the efficiency of parliamentary proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructionist - a person who deliberately obstructs progress, especially in a legislative context

Etymology

From Spanish 'filibustero', meaning pirate or freebooter, ultimately from Dutch 'vrijbuiter' meaning freebooter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstə / (BrE)
A lengthy speech or series of speeches made to delay or prevent a legislative action, especially in a parliamentary or legislative context.
obstructive speech in legislature
legal

Examples

The senator launched a filibuster to stop the bill from coming to a vote.
The opposition threatened a filibuster unless their amendments were considered.
Ending a filibuster in the Senate requires a special majority vote known as cloture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockade - an act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving; similar metaphorically as an obstruction
  • Antonym: to expedite - to speed up the progress; opposite meaning to filibuster

Etymology

From Spanish *filibustero* (pirate, freebooter), originally from French *flibustier*, referring to obstructive conduct; later applied to legislative tactics.

3. VERB

/ ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstə / (BrE)
to deliberately obstruct or delay the progress of legislation, especially by making long speeches or using other delaying tactics in a parliamentary procedure
to delay legislation using obstruction

Examples

The senator decided to filibuster the bill in order to prevent it from coming to a vote.
They filibustered for hours, delivering long speeches to delay the passing of the legislation.
She threatened to filibuster if her concerns were not addressed in the new law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement or progress
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easier

Etymology

From Spanish *filibustero*, originally meaning 'pirate'.
