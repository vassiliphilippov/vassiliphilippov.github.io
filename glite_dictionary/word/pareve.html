pareve

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑrʌvə / (AmE), / ˈpɑːrəvə / (BrE)
(of food) containing neither meat nor dairy, and therefore permitted to be eaten with either under Jewish dietary laws.
containing neither meat nor dairy (Judaism)

Examples

Many kosher bakeries ensure their bread is pareve so it can be eaten with any meal.
This chocolate cake is pareve, making it suitable to serve after a meat dinner.
Always check the label to confirm if the margarine is pareve before using it in recipes.

Synset Examples

pareve margarine
parvebread

Etymology

From Yiddish פארעוו (parev), from Hebrew פָּרוֶוה (parvé), meaning 'neutral.'
