Cordia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.di.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical deciduous or evergreen trees and shrubs in the borage family (Boraginaceae), some of which are valued for their timber or ornamental use.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The wood of Cordia trees is often used to make high-quality furniture.
Many gardens in tropical regions feature Cordia as an attractive ornamental plant.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Cordia genus in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after German botanist Euricius Cordus (1486–1535).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrdiə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical deciduous or evergreen trees and shrubs in the family Boraginaceae, some species valued for timber or fruit.
a genus of tropical trees or shrubs

Examples

The Cordia genus includes several species that are prized for their hardwood.
Many Cordia plants are cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens.
Some fruits produced by Cordia trees are edible and used locally in traditional dishes.

Etymology

Named after Euricius Cordus, a 16th-century German botanist.
