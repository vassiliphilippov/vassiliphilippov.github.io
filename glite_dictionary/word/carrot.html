carrot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
an incentive or reward promised to encourage someone to act in a desired way, often contrasted with punishment ('stick')
incentive or reward offered to encourage certain behavior

Examples

The government offered the carrot of tax cuts to businesses to encourage investment.
Sometimes a leader must use both a carrot and a stick to motivate their team.
He dangled the carrot of a promotion to persuade her to take on extra work.

Synset Examples

used the carrot of subsidized housing for the workers to get their vote

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stick - punishment or threat used as a means of persuasion (as contrasted with 'carrot')

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
a long, tapering orange root vegetable eaten raw or cooked, an important source of carotene
orange root vegetable

Examples

She cut a fresh carrot into thin slices for the salad.
Eating a raw carrot can be a healthy snack between meals.
The stew included potatoes, onions, and chopped carrot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - a white root vegetable similar to carrot

Etymology

From Old French 'carote', from Latin 'carota', from Greek 'karōton'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæɹət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
a plant with a long, tapering orange root that is eaten as a vegetable, widely cultivated in temperate and tropical regions
orange root vegetable

Examples

She sliced a carrot and added it to the salad.
The farmer grows carrots along with potatoes and beans.
Eating a raw carrot can be a healthy snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - a root vegetable similar in shape to a carrot, but pale and sweeter

Etymology

From French 'carotte', from Latin 'carōta', from Greek 'karōton'.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæɹət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
a long, tapered, edible root with a deep orange color, produced by the cultivated carrot plant, commonly eaten as a vegetable
orange edible root vegetable

Examples

She sliced a fresh carrot to add to the salad.
Rabbits are often depicted eating a carrot in cartoons.
The stew needs one more carrot for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - pale, edible root vegetable, similar in shape to a carrot

Etymology

From Late Latin *carōta*, from Greek *karōton* (carrot).
