prompt

1. ADJ

/ prɑmpt / (AmE), / prɒmpt / (BrE)
ready and quick to act; done without delay or hesitation.
ready and quick to act

Examples

The manager was prompt to respond to customer complaints.
She is always prompt to help her friends when they are in need.
He gave a prompt reply to the invitation.

Synset Examples

she is always prompt to help her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediate - done or happening at once; instant
  • Antonym: delayed - happening or being done later than planned

Etymology

From Latin promptus (brought forth, ready), past participle of promere (to bring forth).
