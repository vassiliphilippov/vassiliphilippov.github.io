toxicity

1. NOUN

/ tɑkˈsɪsəti / (AmE), / tɒkˈsɪsəti / (BrE)
The quality of being toxic; the degree to which a substance can harm or kill living organisms.
degree of being poisonous or harmful

Examples

The toxicity of the chemical makes it dangerous to handle without protection.
Scientists tested the toxicity levels of the polluted water before declaring it unsafe.
Low toxicity is a key requirement for materials used in children's toys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisonousness - the quality of being poisonous

Etymology

From Medieval Latin toxicitas, from Latin toxicum (poison).

2. NOUN

/ tɑkˈsɪsəti / (AmE), / tɒkˈsɪsəti / (BrE)
The degree to which a substance is poisonous or harmful to living organisms.
degree of being poisonous

Examples

The toxicity of the chemical makes it dangerous to handle without protective gear.
Scientists measured the toxicity levels in the polluted river water.
The drug was withdrawn from the market due to concerns about its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisonousness - the quality of being poisonous
  • Antonym: innocuousness - the quality of being harmless

Etymology

From Medieval Latin toxicitas, from Latin toxicum (poison).
