elegant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛlɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡənt / (BrE)
pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner; showing refined taste
graceful, stylish, and refined

Examples

She wore an elegant black dress to the dinner party.
The elegant design of the building impressed every visitor.
His handwriting was neat and elegant, admired by his teachers.

Synset Examples

elegant handwriting
an elegant dark suit
she was elegant to her fingertips
small churches with elegant white spires
an elegant mathematical solution — simple and precise and lucid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - characterized by elegance or beauty of form, manner, movement, or speech
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease in movement or manner

Etymology

From Latin elegantem (choice, tasteful), from eligere (to select).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛlɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡənt / (BrE)
graceful and stylish in appearance or manner, suggesting good taste, ease, and sometimes wealth
graceful; stylish; tasteful and refined

Examples

She wore an elegant black dress to the gala.
The restaurant features elegant décor and impeccable service.
His elegant handwriting reflected his refined personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - having style or elegance
  • Synonym: graceful - showing grace or elegance
  • Antonym: clumsy - lacking elegance or grace

Etymology

From Latin 'elegans', 'elegantis' (tasteful, choice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01145585-s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛlɪgənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪgənt / (BrE)
displaying effortless beauty, simplicity, and grace in movement or execution
graceful and simple in movement or design

Examples

The elegant dancer captivated everyone with her smooth and flowing movements.
He proposed an elegant solution to the complex mathematical problem, impressing the panel of judges.
The lines of the building's design are elegant, achieving both simplicity and beauty.

Synset Examples

an elegant dancer
an elegant mathematical solution — simple and precise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - having smooth and attractive movement, style, or form
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill in movement or manner

Etymology

From Latin elegans, elegantis (tasteful, refined).

Source: WordNet
