scissors

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪzərz / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəz / (BrE)
A hand-operated cutting instrument with two crossed blades pivoted in the middle, used for cutting paper, cloth, and other thin materials.
tool for cutting, with two blades

Examples

He picked up the scissors and began to cut the fabric.
Please use the scissors to open the package carefully.
The teacher asked if anyone had a spare pair of scissors for the craft project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shears - large scissors for cutting thicker material

Etymology

From Old French *cisoires*, from Late Latin *cisoria* (cutting instrument), from Latin *caedere* (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪzərz / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəz / (BrE)
A wrestling hold in which the legs are wrapped around an opponent's body or head and squeezed together.
wrestling hold using the legs

Examples

The wrestler applied a tight scissors around his opponent's waist.
She won the match by locking in a powerful scissors hold.
He escaped the scissors and countered with his own maneuver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body scissors - A type of scissors hold targeting the body
  • Synonym: head scissors - A type of scissors hold targeting the head

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪzərz / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəz / (BrE)
A gymnastic exercise performed on the pommel horse in which the gymnast swings their legs back and forth in a motion resembling the blades of scissors opening and closing.
a gymnastic movement on the pommel horse

Examples

The gymnast executed perfect scissors on the pommel horse, impressing the judges.
His routine incorporated several difficult scissors that showed off his strength and flexibility.
During practice, she repeatedly worked on her scissors to improve her form and control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pommel horse - a gymnastic apparatus used for swinging exercises
