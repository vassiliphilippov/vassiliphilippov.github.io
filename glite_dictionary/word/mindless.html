mindless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndləs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndləs / (BrE)
Not mindful or attentive; lacking awareness or consideration.
lacking attention or awareness

Examples

She gave a mindless answer without considering the consequences.
He wandered through the museum in a mindless daze, barely noticing the exhibits.
Doing mindless tasks can sometimes help relax your mind after a stressful day.

Synset Examples

while thus unmindful of his steps he stumbled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtless - lacking consideration or attention
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention; mindful
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01985067-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndləs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndləs / (BrE)
Acting or done without thought, reason, or intelligence; lacking purpose or sense.
without thought or reason; senseless

Examples

The protesters condemned the mindless violence that erupted in the streets.
Watching hours of mindless television shows left him feeling bored and empty.
She was disturbed by the mindless destruction caused by the vandals.

Synset Examples

mindless violence
reasonless hostility
a senseless act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senseless - lacking meaning, sense, or purpose
  • Antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration or attention

Etymology

From mind + -less (without), in use since late 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01951756-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndləs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndləs / (BrE)
Lacking intelligence, thought, or sense; not using one's mind.
lacking thought or intelligence

Examples

The movie was entertaining, but it was basically just mindless action from start to finish.
She stared with a mindless look, lost in daydreams.
He found the repetitive task to be completely mindless and boring.

Synset Examples

a vacuous mind
a vacant expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtless - lacking consideration; not thinking about others
  • Synonym: vacant - showing a lack of thought, expressionless
  • Antonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence

Etymology

From 'mind' + '-less', meaning without mind or intelligence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02581841-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndləs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndləs / (BrE)
(of a task or activity) requiring very little mental effort or thought; not engaging the intellect.
requiring little mental effort

Examples

Filing paperwork can be a mindless activity if you do it every day.
He switched to watching a mindless TV show to relax after work.
Many people enjoy doing mindless chores to clear their heads.

Synset Examples

mindless tasks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanical - (of an activity) done automatically with little thought or effort
  • Synonym: brain-dead - requiring no mental effort, very dull (informal)

Etymology

Formed from 'mind' + '-less'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01337229-s
Synset POS: s

5. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndləs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndləs / (BrE)
Lacking the ability to think or the capacity for conscious thought.
without the capacity for thought

Examples

A mindless wind swept through the empty streets, howling all night.
The mindless stare of the doll unnerved her every time she entered the room.
Some horror movies feature mindless creatures driven only by instinct.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtless - lacking consideration or attention
  • Synonym: brainless - not showing intelligence or sense; stupid
  • Antonym: thoughtful - showing consideration; engaged in deep thought

Etymology

mind + -less (without).
