roamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
a person who moves from place to place without settling in one location; a wanderer.
person who wanders without settling

Examples

As a lifelong roamer, he never felt at home in one city for long.
The roamer wandered across the continent, always searching for something new.
Her grandmother was a roamer in her youth, never staying in one place for more than a year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wanderer - a person who travels aimlessly from place to place
  • Synonym: drifter - a person who moves from place to place without a fixed home
  • Antonym: settler - a person who settles in a new place

Etymology

From the verb 'roam', which originates from Middle English 'romen', possibly of Germanic origin.
