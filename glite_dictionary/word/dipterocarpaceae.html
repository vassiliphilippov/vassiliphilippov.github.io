Dipterocarpaceae

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˌtɛrəˌkɑːrˈpeɪsiː / (AmE), / dɪpˌtɛrəˌkɑːˈpeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical Asian trees with two-winged fruits, valued for their timber, aromatic oils, and resins.
family of tropical Asian trees with two-winged fruits

Examples

The forests of Borneo are dominated by trees of the Dipterocarpaceae family.
Many commercial resins and timbers are sourced from various Dipterocarpaceae.
Ecologists study the role of Dipterocarpaceae in maintaining tropical biodiversity.

Etymology

From Greek dipteros (two-winged) and karpos (fruit), with botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪptəroʊˌkɑːrˈpeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌdɪptərəˌkɑːˈpeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical Asian trees distinguished by two-winged fruits and known for yielding valuable timber, aromatic oils, and resins
family of tropical Asian trees yielding timber and oils

Examples

Many rainforests in Southeast Asia are dominated by trees from the Dipterocarpaceae family.
The timber from Dipterocarpaceae species is highly valued for construction and furniture making.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of Dipterocarpaceae in tropical forest regeneration.

Etymology

From Greek *dipteros* ('two-winged') and *karpos* ('fruit'), referencing the fruit type, plus botanical Latin ending -aceae for plant family.
