capelan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpələn / (AmE), / ˈkapələn / (BrE)
A very small northern fish (Mallotus villosus) found in cold ocean waters, important as food for seabirds, marine mammals, and other fish.
small northern forage fish

Examples

Thousands of capelan gathered near shore during the spring spawning season.
Seabirds rely heavily on capelan as a key source of food.
Commercial fisheries in Newfoundland catch large quantities of capelan each year.

Etymology

From French 'capelan', originally from Old Provençal 'capelan'; also the name for 'chaplain', possibly from shape resemblance.
