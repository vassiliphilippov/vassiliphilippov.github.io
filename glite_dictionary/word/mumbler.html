mumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌmblə / (BrE)
a person who speaks in a low or indistinct voice, making what they say difficult to understand
person who speaks indistinctly or quietly

Examples

He's such a mumbler that I can barely understand a word he says.
During the meeting, the mumbler had to repeat himself several times.
Teachers often struggle to get mumblers to speak more clearly in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mumble - to speak in a low or indistinct voice

Etymology

From English 'mumble' plus agentive '-er'.
