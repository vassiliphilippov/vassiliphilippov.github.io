shrieking

1. NOUN

/ ʃriːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃriːkɪŋ / (BrE)
A loud, high-pitched, and piercing cry or sound.
a sharp, piercing cry or sound

Examples

The shrieking from the playground could be heard down the street.
His sudden shrieking startled everyone in the room.
The eerie shrieking of the wind kept me awake all night.

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - a loud, piercing cry
  • Synonym: wail - a long, loud high-pitched cry
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft, quiet way of speaking

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃri.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃriː.kɪŋ / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched sound or cry, especially one resembling a human scream
a high, piercing noise or cry

Examples

The shrieking of the alarm startled everyone in the building.
Sudden shrieking from the playground made the teacher rush outside.
He covered his ears to block out the constant shrieking of the sirens.

Synset Examples

he ducked at the screechings of shells
he heard the scream of the brakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screeching - a loud, harsh, piercing sound
  • Synonym: screaming - a long, loud, sharp cry

Etymology

From the verb "shriek," which is of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse *skrækja* (to shriek).
