Mustelus norrisi

1. NOUN

/ məsˈtɛləs nɔˈrisi / (AmE), / mʌsˈtiːləs nɒˈriːsi / (BrE)
a species of smoothhound shark found from the northern Gulf of Mexico to Brazil
species of smoothhound shark

Examples

Researchers recently discovered a population of Mustelus norrisi along the Brazilian coast.
The Mustelus norrisi is known for its smooth skin and timid behavior.
Fishermen sometimes catch Mustelus norrisi by accident when trawling in the Gulf of Mexico.

Etymology

From Latin; the genus name 'Mustelus' meaning 'weasel', used for smoothhound sharks, and the species name honoring a person (often a scientist), as is common in taxonomy.
