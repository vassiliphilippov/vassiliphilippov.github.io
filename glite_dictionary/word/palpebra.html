palpebra

1. NOUN

/ pælˈpiːbrə / (AmE), / palˈpiːbrə / (BrE)
either of the movable folds of skin that cover and protect the eye; an eyelid
anatomical term for eyelid
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully lifted the palpebra to examine the underlying tissue.
Inflammation of the palpebra can cause significant discomfort and vision problems.
A spasm of the palpebra is sometimes referred to as an eyelid twitch.

Synset Examples

his lids would stay open no longer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelid - the movable fold of skin covering the eye

Etymology

From Latin palpebra (eyelid).
