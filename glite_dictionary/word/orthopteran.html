orthopteran

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɑptɚən / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒptərən / (BrE)
an insect of the order Orthoptera, characterized by leathery forewings, membranous hind wings, and chewing mouthparts, including grasshoppers, crickets, and locusts.
insect of order Orthoptera (e.g. grasshopper, cricket)
medical

Examples

The museum displayed several specimens of orthopteran insects, including grasshoppers and katydids.
Researchers studied the mating behavior of a rare orthopteran found in tropical forests.
Many orthopteran species produce sound by rubbing their wings together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - an insect of the family Caelifera, known for jumping and chirping
  • Synonym: cricket - an insect known for their chirping sound at night, belonging to the order Orthoptera

Etymology

From modern Latin Orthoptera, from Greek 'orthos' (straight) and 'pteron' (wing).
