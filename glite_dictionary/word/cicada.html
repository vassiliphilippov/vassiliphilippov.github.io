Cicada

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈkeɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Cicadidae, comprising the typical cicadas known for their loud mating calls produced by males.
genus of typical cicadas

Examples

The Cicada genus includes species famous for their distinctive mating calls during summer.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Cicada genus in Southeast Asia.
Some members of the Cicada genus can live underground as nymphs for up to seventeen years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - an insect of the family Cicadidae known for its loud sounds

Etymology

From Latin *cicada* (tree cricket, cicada).
