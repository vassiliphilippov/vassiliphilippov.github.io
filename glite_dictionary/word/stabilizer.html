stabilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a device or substance that makes or keeps something steady or stable
device or substance for steadiness

Examples

The aircraft's stabilizer was damaged during the flight, resulting in instability.
Many photographers use a tripod as a stabilizer to prevent their cameras from shaking.
Adding a chemical stabilizer helps keep the mixture uniform over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that bears the weight of another thing

Etymology

From stabilize + -er, modeled after French 'stabilisateur'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a chemical substance added to a solution, mixture, or suspension to maintain stability or prevent unwanted change
chemical that maintains stability in mixtures

Examples

The stabilizer in the paint prevents it from separating over time.
A stabilizer was added to the solution to keep it from spoiling.
Many processed foods contain a chemical stabilizer to maintain freshness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservative - a substance used to preserve food or materials from decay
  • Antonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction

Etymology

From stabilize + -er, meaning one that stabilizes.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a fixed or adjustable airfoil on an aircraft, such as a horizontal or vertical surface, designed to keep the aircraft stable in flight
aircraft surface for stability

Examples

The aircraft's stabilizer ensures level flight even in turbulent conditions.
A damaged stabilizer can cause severe control issues during takeoff.
The mechanic inspected the horizontal stabilizer for any signs of wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin - a fixed vertical surface that provides stability to an aircraft or ship
  • Synonym: rudder - a device used to steer or stabilize an aircraft or ship

Etymology

From stabilize + -er, meaning something that stabilizes.
