to mythicise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪθɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to interpret or treat as a myth, or to represent in terms of mythology
interpret as a myth
british

Examples

Some historians attempt to mythicise certain events to emphasize their cultural significance.
Writers often mythicise the deeds of national heroes in their stories.
The director chose to mythicise the simple tale, giving it an epic, timeless quality.

Synset Examples

mythicize the ancient stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mythicize - to interpret as a myth or in mythical terms

Etymology

From 'mythic' + '-ise', after American 'mythicize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00625328-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪθɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) into a myth or give it mythical status
make into a myth

Examples

Writers often mythicise historical figures to highlight their importance.
In popular culture, Route 66 has been mythicised as the ultimate American road trip.
Scholars warn against attempts to mythicise the past, urging a more critical perspective.

Synset Examples

The Europeans have mythicized Rte. 66

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mythologize - to make mythical or treat as a myth

Etymology

From 'mythic' + '-ise', after the pattern of 'mythicize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00134821-v
Synset POS: v
