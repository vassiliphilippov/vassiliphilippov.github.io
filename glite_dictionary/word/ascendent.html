ascendent

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
rising or moving upward; tending to go upwards
rising; moving upward

Examples

The climber followed the ascendent path up the steep mountain trail.
Her career took an ascendent trajectory after her promotion to manager.
The ascendent branches reached for the sky as the tree grew taller.

Synset Examples

rooted and ascendant strength like that of foliage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upward - moving towards a higher place or position
  • Antonym: descending - moving downward; going down

Etymology

From Latin 'ascendentem', present participle of 'ascendere' (to climb up).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
A position of dominance or control; a state of being in the ascendant.
dominant or controlling position

Examples

After the election, the new party was clearly in the ascendent.
During that era, scientific thinking was in the ascendent throughout Europe.
The artist's influence has been in the ascendent in recent years.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascendancy - a state of being in control or having influence; dominance
  • Antonym: subordinate - a lower or less powerful position

Etymology

From Latin 'ascendentem', present participle of 'ascendere' (to climb).

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
a person from whom one is descended, especially one more remote than a grandparent; ancestor
distant ancestor

Examples

Many people research their family tree to discover unknown ascendents.
The estate was passed down from his ascendents over several generations.
Some traditions honor the memory of ascendents with special ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from a particular ancestor
  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended, typically more remote than a grandparent

Etymology

From Latin ascendere (to climb up), via Old French ascendant (rising) and Latin ascendens (climbing).
