weave

1. NOUN

/ wiːv / (AmE), / wiːv / (BrE)
the pattern or structure formed by interlacing threads in the production of a fabric
pattern or structure of a fabric

Examples

The scarf had a tight weave that made it very warm.
This fabric’s loose weave makes it lightweight and breathable.
She examined the intricate weave of the traditional rug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: texture - the feel or appearance of a fabric's surface

Etymology

From Old English wefan (to weave, form by interlacing).

2. VERB

/ wiːv / (AmE), / wiːv / (BrE)
to make cloth by interlacing threads, typically on a loom
make cloth by interlacing threads

Examples

She learned how to weave cloth using a traditional wooden loom.
The villagers wove beautiful blankets out of wool.
He spent hours weaving threads to make a colorful tapestry.

Synset Examples

tissue textiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knit - to make fabric by interlocking loops of yarn
  • Synonym: braid - to interweave strands, as in hair or thread

Etymology

Old English wefan (to weave), from Proto-Germanic *webaną, from Proto-Indo-European *webʰ- (to weave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01677786-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ wiːv / (AmE), / wiːv / (BrE)
to move or sway from side to side, especially to avoid obstacles or in a winding manner
move or sway from side to side

Examples

The cyclist had to weave through heavy traffic during rush hour.
As she hurried down the crowded hallway, she weaved from side to side.
The dog began to weave across the path, sniffing at different spots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to turn aside abruptly from a straight course
  • Synonym: zigzag - to move forward by making sharp turns first to one side and then to the other

Etymology

Old English wefan (to weave, form by interlacing). The sense of swaying developed metaphorically from 'interlacing' movement.

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ wiːv / (AmE), / wiːv / (BrE)
to interlace threads or other elements together to form fabric or a complex pattern, or to combine different things in a way that forms a connected whole
interlace threads or elements

Examples

She used fine threads to weave a beautiful silk scarf.
The author managed to weave several storylines into a single narrative.
Spiders weave intricate webs to catch their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braid - to interlace three or more strands together
  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist or weave together
  • Antonym: to untangle - to separate things that have been woven or tangled together

Etymology

Old English *wefan*, from Proto-Germanic *webaną*, meaning 'to weave'.
