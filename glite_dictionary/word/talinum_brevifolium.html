Talinum brevifolium

1. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪnəm ˌbrɛvɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / təˈlaɪnəm ˌbrɛvɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
A low plant native to the southwestern United States, with clustered narrow succulent leaves and fairly large deep pink flowers that grow close to the ground.
a Southwestern US succulent with deep pink ground-level flowers

Examples

The Talinum brevifolium thrives in rocky soils and warm climates in the Southwest.
Gardeners prize Talinum brevifolium for its drought tolerance and vibrant pink blossoms.
We spotted a patch of Talinum brevifolium growing along the desert trail.

Etymology

From genus Talinum, derived from Malagasy 'talimo', and Latin 'brevifolium' meaning 'short-leaved'.
