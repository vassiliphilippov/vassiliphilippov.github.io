to place-kick

1. VERB

/ ˈpleɪsˌkɪk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪs.kɪk / (BrE)
To kick a ball from a stationary position on the ground, especially in football or rugby, often as part of a set play.
kick a stationary ball in football or rugby

Examples

The player carefully place-kicked the ball between the posts to score three points.
She practiced how to place-kick with precision during training sessions.
In the final minute, he place-kicked a perfect goal to win the match.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to drop-kick - kick made by dropping the ball and kicking it as it touches the ground

Etymology

From 'place' (to set in position) + 'kick'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpleɪsˌkɪk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪs.kɪk / (BrE)
to score a goal or point in a game such as football or rugby by kicking the ball from a stationary position on the ground
score by kicking a stationary ball (sports)

Examples

He managed to place-kick the winning goal in the final moments of the match.
The team often relies on their kicker to place-kick crucial points after touchdowns.
She practiced every day to improve her ability to place-kick under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop-kick - to score by dropping the ball and kicking it as it bounces

Etymology

From 'place' + 'kick', referring to kicking a ball after it has been placed on the ground.

Synset POS: v
