metacarpal

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈkɑrpəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈkɑːpəl / (BrE)
any of the five long bones in the hand located between the wrist (carpal bones) and the fingers (phalanges)
long bone in the hand between wrist and fingers
medical

Examples

She suffered a fracture to her third metacarpal after falling off her bike.
The surgeon showed us an X-ray of the patient's metacarpal bones.
Pain in the metacarpal area can indicate a stress injury from overuse.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: phalanx - bone of the finger or toe
  • Degree Lower: carpal - bone of the wrist

Etymology

From Greek meta- (beyond) + karpos (wrist).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈkɑrpəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈkɑːpəl / (BrE)
Relating to the metacarpus, the group of five bones of the hand between the wrist and the fingers.
relating to the bones of the hand
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the metacarpal region for any signs of fracture.
A break in a metacarpal bone can seriously affect hand function.
Protective gear can help prevent metacarpal injuries in athletes.

Synset Examples

metacarpal bones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metatarsal - relating to the bones of the foot between the ankle and toes

Etymology

From Greek meta- (after, beyond) and karpós (wrist).
