Jean-Martin Charcot

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn mɑrˈtæn ʃɑrˈkoʊ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ maʁ.tɛ̃ ʃaʁ.ko / (BrE)
(1825–1893) A French neurologist known for his pioneering work in neurology and his use of hypnotism to treat hysteria.
French neurologist, pioneer in hypnosis

Examples

Jean-Martin Charcot is often referred to as the founder of modern neurology.
Sigmund Freud studied under Jean-Martin Charcot in Paris before developing his own theories.
The teaching clinics of Jean-Martin Charcot attracted students from all over the world.
