seagrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of submerged marine plant with long, narrow leaves, found in shallow coastal waters and forming dense underwater meadows.
underwater marine plant in shallow seas

Examples

The coastal area was teeming with life thanks to the dense beds of seagrass.
Many marine animals depend on seagrass for food and shelter.
Researchers are working to restore damaged seagrass meadows to protect local biodiversity.

Synset Examples

seagrass provides a protective environment in which young fish can develop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - a general term for marine algae or plants

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'grass', named for its grass-like appearance and marine habitat.
