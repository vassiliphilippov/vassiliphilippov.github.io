executive

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
Having the function of carrying out plans, orders, or laws; relating to execution or management.
of or relating to carrying out plans or laws

Examples

The executive committee is responsible for implementing the new policy.
She works in an executive capacity within the organization, overseeing daily operations.
The laws are enforced by the executive branch of government.

Synset Examples

the executive branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrative - relating to organizing and running something; management
  • Antonym: legislative - relating to making laws (contrasts with executive)
  • Antonym: judicial - relating to judging and interpreting laws (contrasts with executive)

Etymology

From Latin 'executivus', from 'executus', past participle of 'exsequi' (to carry out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03029435-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
a person in a senior position who manages or administers a government agency or department
senior government manager or administrator

Examples

The new executive took charge of the health department last month.
As a senior executive, she oversees multiple agencies within the ministry.
A government executive must ensure that all policies are implemented effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrator - person responsible for running an organization or department

Etymology

From Latin 'executivus', from 'exsequi' meaning to carry out.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09789895-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
the branch of government or the persons responsible for implementing laws and administering public affairs
branch of government that enforces laws

Examples

The executive is responsible for enforcing the nation's laws and policies.
Many people debate how much power the executive should have within the government.
Checks and balances exist to prevent the executive from overreaching its authority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legislature - the branch of government that makes laws
  • Antonym: judiciary - the branch of government that interprets laws

Etymology

From Latin 'exsecutivus', from 'exsequi' meaning ‘to follow out, accomplish’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08182354-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
a person with senior managerial responsibility in a business or organization
senior manager or business administrator

Examples

The company's chief executive announced the new business strategy at the annual meeting.
Many executives enjoy high salaries and significant decision-making power within their organizations.
She was promoted to a senior executive position after years of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority or control of an executive or manager

Etymology

From Latin executivus, from exsequi (to follow out, accomplish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10089452-n
Synset POS: n
