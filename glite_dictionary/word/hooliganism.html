hooliganism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːlɪˌɡænɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhuːlɪɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
violent, rowdy, or destructive behavior, especially by young people in groups.
violent or destructive behavior in groups

Examples

Acts of hooliganism disrupted the football match and led to several arrests.
The city council is taking measures to prevent hooliganism during public events.
After the wave of hooliganism, stricter laws were introduced to protect public property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vandalism - deliberate destruction or damage to property

Etymology

From 'hooligan' + '-ism'; 'hooligan' possibly from the Irish surname Houlihan.
