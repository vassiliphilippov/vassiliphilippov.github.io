buck

1. VERB

/ bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
(of a horse or other animal) to jump vertically with legs stiff and back arched, often suddenly and with force.
(of animals) jump with arched back

Examples

The startled pony began to buck wildly in the corral.
She managed to stay on as the horse tried to buck her off.
When frightened, a mule will sometimes buck instead of running away.

Synset Examples

the young filly bucked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rear - (of a horse) to rise up on its hind legs
