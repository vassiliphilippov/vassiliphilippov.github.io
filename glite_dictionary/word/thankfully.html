thankfully

1. ADV

/ ˈθæŋkfəli / (AmE), / ˈθæŋkfəli / (BrE)
used to express relief or gratitude that something has happened or been avoided
expressing relief or gratitude

Examples

Thankfully, the storm passed before it reached our town.
Thankfully, no one was hurt in the accident.
The report arrived on time, thankfully.

Synset Examples

thankfully he didn't come to the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunately - used to express that something happened by good luck
  • Antonym: unfortunately - used to express that something happened by bad luck

Etymology

From 'thankful' + '-ly', meaning 'in a thankful manner'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00201311-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈθæŋk.fəli / (AmE), / ˈθæŋk.fəli / (BrE)
In a grateful or appreciative manner; with thanks.
in a grateful manner; with thanks

Examples

He nodded thankfully as she handed him a glass of water.
She thankfully accepted their offer of help during the crisis.
The lost hikers were thankfully found before nightfall.

Synset Examples

he accepted thankfully my apologies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gratefully - in a grateful way; with gratitude

Etymology

Formed from 'thankful' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00201415-r
Synset POS: r
