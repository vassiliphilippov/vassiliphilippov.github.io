myelomeningocele

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəloʊməˈnɪŋɡəsil / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪələʊməˈnɪŋɡəsiːl / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which a sac containing part of the spinal cord and its membranes protrudes through an opening in the spine
congenital spinal cord defect
medical

Examples

The infant was diagnosed with a myelomeningocele shortly after birth.
Surgery is often required to close the defect caused by a myelomeningocele.
Prenatal imaging can help detect a myelomeningocele during pregnancy.

Etymology

From Greek myelos (marrow) + meninx (membrane) + -cele (hernia).
