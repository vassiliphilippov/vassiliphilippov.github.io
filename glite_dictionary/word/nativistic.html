nativistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌneɪ.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌneɪ.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or advocating nativism, especially the belief in or theory of innate ideas or the protection of native interests against outsiders.
relating to nativism or native beliefs

Examples

Some psychologists support nativistic explanations of language acquisition.
There is a long-standing debate between nativistic and environmental approaches in philosophy.
His views on immigration were shaped by a nativistic perspective.

Synset Examples

nativist theories
the traditional controversy between the nativistic and empiristic theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nativist - a person who supports nativism or native interests
  • Antonym: empiricist - a person who emphasizes empirical evidence over innate ideas

Etymology

From 'nativist' + -ic, relating to nativism.

2. ADJ

/ ˌneɪ.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌneɪ.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
advocating or characterized by the preservation or revival of indigenous or traditional cultural practices, values, or societies, especially in opposition to outside influences or innovations
favoring native cultural preservation

Examples

Many leaders in the movement held nativistic beliefs, promoting traditional customs over foreign ones.
The nativistic approach seeks to revive cultural practices that are at risk of disappearing.
Critics argue that nativistic sentiments can lead to resistance against beneficial innovation.

Synset Examples

the old nativist prejudice against the foreign businessman
the nativistic faith preaches the old values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nativist - a person who advocates for protecting native interests or customs

Etymology

From native + -istic, from Latin *nativus* (innate, natural) and the suffix -istic.

WordNet ID: oewn-03000261-a
Synset POS: a
