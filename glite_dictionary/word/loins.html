loins

1. NOUN

/ lɔɪnz / (AmE), / lɔɪnz / (BrE)
the lower part of the abdomen just above the external genital organs
lower abdominal region above genitals

Examples

He put his hands on his loins and stretched after a long run.
Ancient warriors would often gird their loins before battle.
She felt a sudden pain in her loins after lifting the heavy box.

Etymology

From Old French 'loigne', from Latin 'lumbi' (plural of 'lumbus', loin).

2. NOUN

/ lɔɪnz / (AmE), / lɔɪnz / (BrE)
the part of the body including the hips, groin, and lower abdomen, often considered as the area below the ribs and above the legs
hips and lower abdomen region

Examples

The champion had a belt wrapped around his loins after the match.
During the ritual, the participants wore garments girded about their loins.
She placed a warm towel on her loins to ease the muscle ache.

Etymology

From Old French *loigne*, from Latin *lumbus* meaning 'loin' or 'lumbar region'.
