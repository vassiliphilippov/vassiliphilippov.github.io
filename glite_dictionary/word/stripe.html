stripe

1. NOUN

/ straɪp / (AmE), / straɪp / (BrE)
a V-shaped sleeve badge worn on a military uniform to indicate rank or length of service
military badge indicating rank or service

Examples

The sergeant proudly sewed another stripe onto his uniform after his promotion.
He worked hard to earn his third stripe in the army.
The recruits recognized his experience by the stripes on his sleeve.

Synset Examples

they earned their stripes in Kuwait

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chevron - a V-shaped badge or insignia used in military or police uniforms

2. NOUN

/ straɪp / (AmE), / straɪp / (BrE)
a strip of braid or material, typically worn on the sleeve of a uniform, indicating military rank or length of service
insignia indicating military rank or service

Examples

He was promoted and earned another stripe on his uniform.
The sergeant proudly displayed three stripes on his sleeve.
Each stripe represented several years of dedicated service.

Etymology

From Middle English stripe (long, narrow band), possibly from Middle Dutch strippe.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07284463-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ straɪp / (AmE), / straɪp / (BrE)
a narrow band or line that differs in color or texture from its background, typically one of several that are parallel or close together
narrow colored or textured band

Examples

The tiger's stripes help it blend into the tall grass.
She wore a shirt with red and white stripes for the festival.
The artist painted a blue stripe down the center of the canvas.

Synset Examples

a green toad with small black stripes or bars
may the Stars and Stripes forever wave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band - a long, narrow strip or line, especially one of color or texture
  • Antonym: spot - a small round or irregular mark differing from its background

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'stripen' (to whip, to stripe); related to 'strip.'

5. NOUN

/ straɪp / (AmE), / straɪp / (BrE)
a long, narrow band or strip of a different color or material, often used as an adornment
a line or band of contrasting color or material

Examples

The dress was decorated with a bright red stripe down the side.
He earned a stripe on his uniform for good conduct.
A green stripe ran across the length of the flag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band - a long, thin, flat strip or loop of material
  • Antonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark

Etymology

From Middle English 'stripe' (a stroke or band), probably from Middle Dutch 'strîpe' (a stripe, line).

/ straɪp / (AmE), / straɪp / (BrE)
a type or category of person or thing, especially as distinguished by opinion, background, or characteristic
type or category

Examples

Artists and writers of all stripes gathered at the conference.
No matter your stripe, everyone is welcome at this community event.

Synset Examples
