literatim

1. ADV

/ ˌlɪtəˈreɪtɪm / (AmE), / ˌlɪtəˈreɪtɪm / (BrE)
word for word or letter for letter; exactly as written or spoken, without alteration
exactly as written or spoken

Examples

The secretary copied the speech literatim from the original manuscript.
The court record was reproduced literatim for accuracy during the appeal.
She translated the passage literatim rather than paraphrasing it.

Synset Examples

the message was transcribed literatim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbatim - in exactly the same words as originally used

Etymology

From Latin 'litteratim', meaning 'by letters'.
