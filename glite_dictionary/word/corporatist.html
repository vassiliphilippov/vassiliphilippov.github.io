corporatist

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔːrpərətɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpərətɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or advocating corporatism, a political or economic system in which interest groups such as businesses, labor, or other collective organizations have a formal role in governance.
relating to corporatism

Examples

The new government implemented a strictly corporatist model, giving powerful business associations formal input on policy.
Some critics argue that corporatist structures can lead to undue influence by large organizations over public interests.
Her thesis examined the rise of corporatist policies in 20th-century European politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corporatism - the theory or practice of organizing society into corporate groups

Etymology

Formed from 'corporatism' + '-ist', ultimately from Latin 'corpus' (body).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrpərətɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɔːp(ə)rətɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates corporatism, an economic or political system in which interest groups such as businesses, labor, and government work together in collective associations.
supporter of corporatism

Examples

The new policy delighted the corporatist factions within the party.
As a lifelong corporatist, she believed in strong partnerships between government and industry.
Critics accused the administration of favoring corporatist interests over the needs of ordinary citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corporatism - the control of a state or organization by large interest groups

Etymology

From 'corporate' + '-ist'; 'corporatism' from Latin 'corporatus' (formed into a body).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09986000-n
Synset POS: n
