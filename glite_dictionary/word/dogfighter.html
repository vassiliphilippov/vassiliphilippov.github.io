dogfighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌfaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌfaɪtə / (BrE)
a fighter pilot who engages in close-range aerial combat with enemy aircraft
fighter pilot in aerial combat

Examples

The experienced dogfighter relied on quick reflexes to outmaneuver his opponent.
Many WWII aces became legendary dogfighters through their daring tactics in the skies.
Modern jet pilots are still trained as dogfighters despite advances in missile technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogfight - close-range aerial battle between fighter aircraft

Etymology

Formed from 'dogfight' (aerial combat) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
