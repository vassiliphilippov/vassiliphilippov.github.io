cudgel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌdʒəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌdʒəl / (BrE)
a short, thick stick used as a weapon
short, thick weapon stick

Examples

The guard carried a heavy cudgel to defend himself at night.
During medieval times, a cudgel was often used in close combat.
He picked up a cudgel from the ground and prepared to fight off his attackers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club - a heavy stick used as a weapon

Etymology

From Middle English 'cuggel,' possibly of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03150050-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌdʒəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌdʒ(ə)l / (BrE)
to hit or beat someone or something with a short, thick stick or club
hit with a short, heavy stick

Examples

During the brawl, several men began to cudgel their opponents with wooden sticks.
The thief was cudgeled by the townspeople before the constable arrived.
Historians wrote that prisoners were often cudgeled for attempting to escape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bludgeon - to strike with a heavy club

Etymology

From Middle English 'cuggel', probably related to Old Norse 'kǫggull' (club).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01426760-v
Synset POS: v
