Czechoslovakia

1. PROPN

/ ˌtʃɛkoʊsləˈveɪkiə / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛkəsləˈveɪkiə / (BrE)
a former country in central Europe that existed from 1918 to 1992, after which it split into the Czech Republic and Slovakia
former central European country, now Czech Republic and Slovakia

Examples

The country of Czechoslovakia ceased to exist after the peaceful split in 1993.
My grandparents emigrated from Czechoslovakia before World War II.
The history textbook includes a chapter about the formation and dissolution of Czechoslovakia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Czech Republic - country formed from the western part of Czechoslovakia in 1993
  • Degree Lower: Slovakia - country formed from the eastern part of Czechoslovakia in 1993

Etymology

Formed from Czech and Slovak, reflecting the union of these two ethnic groups.
