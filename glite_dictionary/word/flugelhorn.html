flugelhorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluɡəlˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfluːɡl̩ˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a brass musical instrument similar to the cornet but with a wider bore, producing a mellower tone
brass musical instrument with a mellow tone

Examples

The jazz musician played a beautiful solo on his flugelhorn during the concert.
She learned to play the flugelhorn as well as the trumpet in her school band.
The mellow sound of the flugelhorn set the mood for the evening's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornet - a brass instrument similar to the trumpet but with a mellower tone
  • Degree Higher: trumpet - a brass instrument with a bright, penetrating tone

Etymology

From German Flügelhorn, from Flügel (wing, flank) + Horn (horn), originally a signal horn for flankers in the army.
