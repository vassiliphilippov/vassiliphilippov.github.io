to objurgate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑb.dʒɚˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.dʒə.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to rebuke or scold someone severely; to express strong disapproval
rebuke or scold severely

Examples

The professor did not hesitate to objurgate students who failed to complete their assignments on time.
After the mistake was discovered, the manager objurgated the team during the meeting.
She objurgated her friend for spreading false rumors.

Synset Examples

She chastised him for his insensitive remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: reprimand - to rebuke formally or officially
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin objurgare (to scold, rebuke), from ob- (against) + jurgare (to dispute).

2. VERB

/ ˈɑbdʒɚˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒbdʒəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval of; to scold or rebuke sharply
to scold or express strong disapproval

Examples

The teacher did not hesitate to objurgate the students for their thoughtless behavior.
He was strongly objurgated by his colleagues for his unethical comments.
Instead of calmly discussing the issue, she chose to objurgate him in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

We condemn the racism in South Africa
These ideas were reprobated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: to condemn - to express strong disapproval of
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of

Etymology

From Latin objurgare (to chide, scold), from ob- (against) + jurgare (to quarrel, dispute).
