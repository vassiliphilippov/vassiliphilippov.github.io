twinned

1. ADJ

/ twɪnd / (AmE), / twɪnd / (BrE)
consisting of two similar or identical parts, or matched or paired in a close relationship.
paired or consisting of two identical parts

Examples

The experiment was conducted using twinned crystals to ensure accuracy.
Many towns in Europe are twinned with towns in other countries to encourage cultural exchange.
The museum displayed a pair of twinned statues crafted by the same artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twin - one of two children or animals born at the same birth

Etymology

From the verb 'twin', meaning to pair or double, ultimately from Old English 'twin' (double, twofold).
