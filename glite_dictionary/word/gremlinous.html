gremlinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɛm.lɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛm.lɪ.nəs / (BrE)
Having qualities characteristic of a gremlin; mischievous, troublesome, or prone to causing unexpected problems or chaos.
mischievous or troublesome (like a gremlin)

Examples

The new employee was known for his gremlinous sense of humor and unexpected pranks.
A gremlinous character in the novel continually sabotages the protagonist's plans.
Their system was disrupted by a series of gremlinous technical glitches.

Synset Examples

Her stories often feature gremlinous characters wreaking havoc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievous - causing or showing fondness for causing trouble in a playful way
  • Synonym: troublesome - causing difficulty or annoyance

Etymology

Formed from 'gremlin' (a mischievous creature in folklore and popular culture) plus the adjective-forming suffix '-ous'.
