Asurbanipal

1. PROPN

/ ɑːˌsɚˈbɑːnɪˌpɑːl / (AmE), / ˌasʊˈbɑːnɪˌpɑːl / (BrE)
(c. 668–627 BC) King of Assyria, renowned for building a grand palace and founding a famous library at Nineveh.
King of Assyria, founder of the library at Nineveh

Examples

During his reign, Asurbanipal amassed one of the largest libraries of the ancient world.
Many clay tablets discovered at Nineveh are attributed to the collection of Asurbanipal.
Scholars praise Asurbanipal for his efforts to preserve Mesopotamian literature.

Etymology

From Akkadian Ashurbanipal, meaning 'Ashur is creator of an heir'.
