sinister

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnɪstər / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
Suggestive of evil, harm, or wrongdoing; giving the impression that something harmful or evil is happening or will happen.
suggestive of evil or harm

Examples

There was a sinister figure lurking in the shadows, making everyone uneasy.
The old house had a sinister reputation in the neighborhood.
She sensed something sinister behind his forced smile.

Synset Examples

a black lie

dark undercurrents of ethnic hostility
the scheme of some sinister intelligence bent on punishing him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - profoundly immoral and wicked
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' (left, unlucky, unfavorable).

/ ˈsɪnɪstər / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
suggesting that something harmful, evil, or dangerous is likely to happen; threatening or portending ill fortune or harm
threatening evil or harm; ominous

Examples

The abandoned house had a sinister atmosphere that made everyone uneasy.
Dark clouds gathered overhead, casting a sinister shadow on the town.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnəstər / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
(Heraldry) on or pertaining to the left-hand side of a shield from the wearer's perspective; the side opposite 'dexter'.
heraldic term: on the wearer's left

Examples

In heraldry, a bar sinister indicates illegitimacy.
The lion rampant faces the sinister side of the shield in the family crest.
A bend sinister runs from the top right to the bottom left from the observer's view.

Synset Examples

bar sinister

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dexter - heraldic right side (wearer's perspective)

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' (left, on the left side).
