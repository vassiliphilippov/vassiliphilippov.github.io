Ochna

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑk.nə / (AmE), / ˈɒk.nə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen trees and shrubs of the Old World tropics, noted for their glossy leaves and yellow flowers; the type genus of the family Ochnaceae
a genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The Ochna species in the garden are admired for their bright yellow blossoms.
Many horticulturists study Ochna for its unique floral structure.
Botanical texts often reference Ochna as the type genus of Ochnaceae.

Etymology

From Greek ókhne (a type of wild pear), used as the genus name in botany.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑk.nə / (AmE), / ˈɒk.nə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs of the Old World tropics, belonging to the family Ochnaceae.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

Several species in the Ochna genus are cultivated for their attractive yellow flowers.
The Ochna tree is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.
Botanists often study Ochna to understand the diversity within the Ochnaceae family.

Etymology

From Greek okhna (wild pear), adopted as a botanical genus name.
