caliber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
The internal diameter of a tube, especially the barrel of a firearm, or of a bullet or shell.
diameter of a tube or gun barrel

Examples

The rifle was designed to use ammunition of a specific caliber.
Police recovered a handgun with a .45 caliber barrel at the scene.
Because of its small caliber, the gun is lightweight and easy to handle.

Etymology

From French calibre (diameter of a tube) and Italian calibro.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæləbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
the level of quality or ability, especially of a person's character or achievements
level of quality or ability

Examples

She is an athlete of the highest caliber, consistently excelling at every competition.
The company is seeking employees of exceptional caliber for its new project.
Many were impressed by the caliber of the presentations at the scientific conference.

Synset Examples
