anisogamete

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ɪ.soʊˈɡæm.iːt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪ.səʊˈɡæm.iːt / (BrE)
either of a pair of reproductive cells (gametes) that differ in size or form, especially with one larger (female) and one smaller (male)
a gamete differing in size or form from its mate
medical

Examples

In many algae, reproduction occurs by fusion of a large anisogamete and a smaller one.
The presence of an anisogamete indicates a difference between male and female gametes.
Scientists compared the DNA content of each anisogamete to understand fertility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isogamete - a gamete that is similar in size and form to its pair
  • Synonym: heterogamete - another term for anisogamete

Etymology

From Greek anisos (unequal) + gamete (wife/spouse, or reproductive cell).
