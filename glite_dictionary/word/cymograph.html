cymograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.moʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsɪ.məˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a scientific instrument consisting of a rotating drum with paper on which a stylus records continuous traces such as breathing or blood pressure
scientific instrument for recording physiological data

Examples

The researcher used a cymograph to monitor the subject's pulse during the experiment.
Early studies of respiration often employed a cymograph for continuous recording of breathing patterns.
A cymograph produces a visual trace, making it easier to analyze physiological changes over time.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyma' (wave) + '-graph' (instrument for recording).
