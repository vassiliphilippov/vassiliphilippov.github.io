haphazardly

1. ADV

/ hæpˈhæzɚdli / (AmE), / hæpˈhæzədli / (BrE)
In a way that is not planned or lacks order; done randomly or without care.
in a random or careless way

Examples

The books were stacked haphazardly on the shelves, with no regard for order.
She threw her clothes haphazardly into the suitcase as she rushed to leave.
Paint was splattered haphazardly across the canvas, giving the artwork a wild appearance.

Synset Examples

the Prime Minister was wearing a grey suit and a white shirt with a soft collar, but his neck had become thinner and the collar stood away from it as if it had been bought haphazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - without definite aim, reason, or pattern
  • Antonym: carefully - in a way that shows attention and caution

Etymology

From 'haphazard' (meaning: random chance or lack of order), with -ly forming an adverb.

2. ADV

/ hæpˈhæzɚdli / (AmE), / hæpˈhæzədli / (BrE)
in a way that is not planned, organized, or orderly; at random
in a random or disorganized way

Examples

Books were stacked haphazardly on the shelves, making it hard to find anything.
The artist splashed paint haphazardly across the canvas to create an abstract effect.
Traffic cones were placed haphazardly around the construction site, confusing the drivers.

Synset Examples

the houses were randomly scattered
bullets were fired into the crowd at random

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - in a way that happens without method or conscious decision
  • Antonym: methodically - in an orderly or systematic manner

Etymology

From 'haphazard' (chance, risk), originally from 'hap' (chance) and 'hazard' (danger, risk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00071165-r
Synset POS: r
