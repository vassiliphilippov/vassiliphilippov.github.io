impersonation

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɜrsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːsəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of imitating the behavior, speech, or actions of another person, often for entertainment or deception
the act of imitating someone

Examples

Her impersonation of the celebrity was incredibly convincing and entertained the whole audience.
The comedian is famous for his hilarious impersonation of political leaders.
They performed a clever impersonation at the talent show, capturing every detail of the principal's gestures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something
  • Synonym: mimicry - the action of mimicking someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'personare' (to sound through), via 'impersonate'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɜrsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːsəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of pretending to be another person in order to deceive others or for entertainment
pretending to be another person

Examples

The actor's impersonation of the president was surprisingly accurate and entertaining.
She was arrested for her impersonation of a police officer.
His career is built on the impersonation of celebrities in comedy sketches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something
  • Synonym: identity theft - the crime of using someone else's personal information to deceive

Etymology

From 'impersonate' + '-ion', ultimately from Latin 'persona' (character, mask).

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɜrsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːsəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of imitating a person's voice, mannerisms, or appearance, often in an exaggerated way for humorous effect
comic imitation of a person

Examples

His impersonation of the president had everyone in the audience laughing.
She won the talent contest with her hilarious impersonation of a famous singer.
The comedian's impersonation was so accurate that it startled even the real celebrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying or mimicking someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (in) + 'persona' (person, character).
