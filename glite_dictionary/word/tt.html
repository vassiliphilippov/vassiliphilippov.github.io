TT

1. NOUN

/ ti ti / (AmE), / tiː tiː / (BrE)
(astronomy) a precise astronomical time standard based on Earth's orbital motion, used as a reference in calculations of planetary positions.
astronomical time standard (Terrestrial Time)

Examples

Astronomers use TT as a reference when calculating the positions of planets and stars.
Precise measurements of eclipses are recorded using TT rather than ordinary civil time.
Conversion between TT and Universal Time is necessary for some scientific observations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: UT - Universal Time, astronomical time standard based on Earth's rotation

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Terrestrial Time', used in astronomical and scientific contexts.
