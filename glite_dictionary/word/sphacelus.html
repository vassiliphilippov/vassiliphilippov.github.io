sphacelus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfæsələs / (AmE), / ˈsfæsɪləs / (BrE)
Localized death of tissue cells, typically due to infection or interruption of blood supply; gangrene.
localized tissue death (gangrene)
medical

Examples

The surgeon diagnosed the blackened skin as sphacelus resulting from a blocked artery.
If sphacelus is left untreated, the infection can spread rapidly to surrounding tissues.
The patient's toes showed signs of sphacelus, likely caused by severe frostbite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangrene - Death of tissue due to loss of blood supply or infection
  • Synonym: necrosis - Death of body tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'sphacelus', from Greek 'sphakelos' (gangrene, a slough).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsfæsələs / (AmE), / ˈsfæsɪləs / (BrE)
a mass or portion of dead tissue; mortified or gangrenous part, especially as a result of disease or injury
dead or gangrenous tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed the sphacelus to prevent the infection from spreading.
Examination revealed a sphacelus at the site of the wound, requiring immediate medical attention.
A large sphacelus was present, indicating advanced tissue necrosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangrene - death of body tissue due to loss of blood supply

Etymology

From Latin 'sphacelus' (gangrene, mortification), from Greek 'sphakelos' (gangrene, mortification).
