gentian

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Gentiana or related genera in the family Gentianaceae, typically with showy blue or purple tubular flowers
a flowering plant of the genus Gentiana

Examples

The alpine gentian is known for its vibrant blue petals.
She planted gentians along the border of her garden for a splash of color.
Many herbal remedies use extract from the root of the gentian plant.

Etymology

From Latin 'gentiana', likely named after Gentius, an Illyrian king supposedly discovering its medicinal properties.
