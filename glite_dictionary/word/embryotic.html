embryotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒt.ɪk / (BrE)
In an early stage of development; beginning to form or grow.
in an early stage of development

Examples

The company's embryotic technology showed great promise despite its limitations.
These are only embryotic plans, and much work remains to be done.
Her ideas, though embryotic, inspired the team to pursue new directions.

Synset Examples

the embryonic government staffed by survivors of the massacre
an embryonic nation, not yet self-governing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embryonic - in an early stage of development
  • Antonym: developed - fully formed or advanced

Etymology

From embryon (embryo) + -ic, by analogy with 'embryonic.'
