millenarianism

1. NOUN

/ mɪˌlɛˈnɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / mɪˌlɛnəˈrɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in a coming transformation of society, especially the Christian doctrine of a future thousand-year reign of Christ on earth as foretold in the Book of Revelation.
belief in a thousand-year divine reign

Examples

Many religious groups that believe in millenarianism await the prophesied thousand-year reign with great anticipation.
Millenarianism often inspires social movements that anticipate radical change or a new era.
Some historians have linked social unrest in Europe to outbreaks of millenarianism among the population.

Etymology

From Latin 'millenarius' (containing a thousand), from 'mille' (thousand).
