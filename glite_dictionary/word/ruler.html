ruler

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːlər / (AmE), / ˈruːlə / (BrE)
a straight-edged strip, typically made of wood, metal, or plastic, used for drawing straight lines and measuring length
measuring instrument for straight lines and lengths

Examples

She used a ruler to draw a perfect straight line across the page.
The teacher asked everyone to bring a ruler to math class.
My ruler is made of transparent plastic, so I can see the marks underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape measure - flexible measuring instrument marked in units of length

Etymology

From Old French *reule* (rule, straight stick), from Latin *regula* (straight stick, rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈruːlə / (BrE)
a person who governs or exercises authority over a country, region, or group of people
person who governs or leads

Examples

The ancient pharaoh was the absolute ruler of Egypt.
The country has been without a clear ruler since the king's death.
Many people lived in fear of the harsh ruler who controlled the land.

Synset Examples

swayer of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
  • Antonym: subject - a person under the authority of a ruler

Etymology

From Old French 'reuleor', from Medieval Latin 'regulator', from Latin 'regula' (rule).
