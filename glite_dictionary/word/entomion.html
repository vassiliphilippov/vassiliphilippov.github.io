entomion

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtoʊmiən / (AmE), / ɛnˈtəʊmɪən / (BrE)
the craniometric point at the tip of the angular part of the parietal bone where it meets the temporal bone
skull measurement point (parietal-temporal junction)
medical

Examples

The position of the entomion is crucial for accurate cranial measurements in anthropology.
During the anatomy lecture, the professor pointed to the entomion on the human skull model.
Researchers measured the distance between the entomion and other cranial landmarks for the study.

Etymology

From Greek entomon (notch) + -ion (diminutive suffix).
