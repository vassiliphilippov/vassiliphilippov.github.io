Ictonyx

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtoʊnɪks / (AmE), / ɪkˈtəʊnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the weasel family (Mustelidae), including the Saharan striped polecat and the striped polecat.
genus of mammals in the weasel family

Examples

The genus Ictonyx is known for its striking black and white fur patterns.
Researchers have observed Ictonyx species in various regions across Africa.
Both the Saharan striped polecat and the striped polecat belong to the genus Ictonyx.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polecat - a small carnivorous mammal in the weasel family

Etymology

From Greek iktos (yellow ichneumon) + onyx (claw).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtoʊ.nɪks / (AmE), / ɪkˈtəʊ.nɪks / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the weasel family Mustelidae, which includes the Saharan striped polecat and the striped polecat.
genus of striped polecats

Examples

The genus Ictonyx includes two species of striped polecats found in Africa.
Scientists have recently studied the evolutionary history of Ictonyx to understand its relationship with other mustelids.
Both the Saharan striped polecat and the zorilla are classified under the genus Ictonyx.

Etymology

From Greek iktos ('wailing') and onyx ('claw'), used in scientific classification.
