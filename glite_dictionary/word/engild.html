to engild

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡɪld / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡɪld / (BrE)
to decorate or cover with gold or a gold-like substance
decorate or cover with gold

Examples

The artist chose to engild the frames to give them an elegant finish.
Morning sunlight seemed to engild the tops of the mountains.
They engilded the altar with delicate gold leaf for the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild - to cover thinly with gold leaf or gold paint

Etymology

From Middle English 'engilden', from en- (prefix) + gild (to cover with gold).
