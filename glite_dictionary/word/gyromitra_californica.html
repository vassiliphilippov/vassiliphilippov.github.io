Gyromitra californica

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə ˌkæləˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of false morel fungus, characterized by a brown, puffed-up fertile surface and a thick, fluted stalk, typically found under conifers in California.
a California false morel fungus

Examples

The distinctive appearance of Gyromitra californica makes it stand out among other mushrooms in the forest.
Foragers should exercise caution, as Gyromitra californica may be toxic if consumed raw.
Researchers discovered several specimens of Gyromitra californica growing under pine trees in Northern California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - common name for several species of Gyromitra fungi

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'gyros' (circle, round) + 'mitra' (headband); 'californica' refers to California, where it is found.
