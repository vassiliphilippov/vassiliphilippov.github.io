orthoptics

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɑptɪks / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒptɪks / (BrE)
the treatment of defects in binocular vision, such as strabismus or amblyopia, using nonsurgical techniques, especially eye muscle exercises
nonsurgical treatment of binocular vision defects
medical

Examples

The specialist recommended orthoptics to help correct the child's lazy eye.
Many cases of mild strabismus can be managed effectively with orthoptics rather than surgery.
He attends regular orthoptics sessions as part of his vision therapy.

Etymology

From Greek orthos (straight) + optikos (of or for sight).
