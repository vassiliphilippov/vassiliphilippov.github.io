disrespectful

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛktfəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛktfʊl / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect; rude or impolite.
showing a lack of respect

Examples

It is considered disrespectful to interrupt someone while they are speaking.
The students were punished for their disrespectful behavior during the assembly.
She found his tone extremely disrespectful and decided not to engage further.

Synset Examples

remarks disrespectful of the law
disrespectful in the presence of his parents
disrespectful toward his teacher

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect
  • Synonym: rude - impolite; not showing respect or consideration

Etymology

From 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'respectful', modeled after 'respect'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛktfəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛktfʊl / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect; rude or impolite
showing lack of respect

Examples

It was disrespectful of him to interrupt the speaker during her presentation.
Some students were disrespectful towards their teachers, which caused problems in the classroom.
Her disrespectful tone made it clear she did not value their opinions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect
  • Synonym: rude - impolite or offensively blunt

Etymology

From 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'respectful', based on Latin 'respectus'.
