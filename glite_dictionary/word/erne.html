erne

1. NOUN

/ ɜrn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
a large greyish-brown eagle with a short wedge-shaped white tail, found in Europe and Greenland; also known as the white-tailed eagle
a large European sea eagle

Examples

The erne soared majestically above the cliffs, scanning for prey.
Spotting an erne in the wild can be a rare and rewarding experience for birdwatchers.
The powerful talons of the erne allow it to catch fish and waterfowl with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed eagle - large eagle with a white tail, found in Eurasia

Etymology

From Old English earn, related to Old Norse örn (eagle).
