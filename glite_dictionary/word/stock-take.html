to stock-take

1. VERB

/ ˈstɑkˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪk / (BrE)
to make an itemized record of the goods or materials held in stock; to inventory
make an inventory; count items in stock
british

Examples

Every December, the staff are required to stock-take the entire warehouse.
We need to stock-take all our equipment before the audit next week.
She spent the afternoon helping the team stock-take the shop shelves.

Synset Examples

Inventory all books before the end of the year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - to make a complete list of items in stock

Etymology

From 'stock' (supply of goods) + 'take' (to record by counting); common in British business English.
