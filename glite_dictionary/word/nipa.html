Nipa

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːpə / (AmE), / ˈniːpə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical palms native to Australasia, especially known for the nipa palm (Nipa fruticans), which grows in coastal and tidal habitats.
genus of tropical palms of Australasia

Examples

The Nipa genus is unique among palms for its preference for tidal mudflats.
Over vast stretches of coastline, Nipa palms form dense mangrove thickets.
Researchers are studying how Nipa palms contribute to coastal ecosystem stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nipa fruticans - species of palm in the Nipa genus

Etymology

From Malay *nipa*, referring to the nipa palm.

A genus of tropical palms native to Australasia, comprising the nipa palm.
genus of tropical palms

Examples

The Nipa genus is best known for the nipa palm found in tidal wetlands.
Botanists studied the reproductive traits of plants in the Nipa genus.
