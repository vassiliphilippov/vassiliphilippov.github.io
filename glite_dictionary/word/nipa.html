nipa

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːpə / (AmE), / ˈniːpə / (BrE)
A material or product made from the sap of the Australasian nipa palm, often used for thatching, sugar, or alcoholic beverages.
material from the sap of the nipa palm

Examples

The locals harvested nipa to make vinegar and traditional palm wine.
Roofs thatched with nipa keep houses cool even in the hottest weather.
Nipa sap is an important ingredient in many Southeast Asian recipes.

Etymology

From Tagalog and Malay 'nipa', the name of the palm tree.
