homesteader

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌstɛdər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstedə / (BrE)
a person who settles on government land with the intention of gaining ownership, especially under official homesteading laws
person settling to acquire land ownership

Examples

Many homesteaders braved harsh conditions to claim their land on the frontier.
The museum tells the story of early homesteaders in the American West.
Her ancestors were homesteaders who built a life in Nebraska.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area and settles there

Etymology

Formed from 'homestead' (dwelling and its land) + '-er' (one who does), referring to those benefiting from the Homestead Act in the U.S.
