crooked

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrʊkɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪd / (BrE)
having bends, curves, or angles; not straight or aligned
bent or not straight

Examples

The hiker followed the crooked path through the dense forest.
Her crooked smile made him laugh despite his bad mood.
The old house had a crooked chimney and uneven floorboards.

Synset Examples

crooked country roads
crooked teeth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - having no bends or curves; not crooked

Etymology

From Middle English 'crokked', past participle of 'croken' meaning to bend, from Old Norse 'krōkr' (hook).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrʊkɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪd / (BrE)
Having an irregular or bent shape or outline; not straight or even.
irregular or bent in shape

Examples

She noticed that the painting was hanging crooked on the wall and adjusted it.
His tie was crooked, making him look slightly disheveled at the important meeting.
The river took a crooked path through the valley, curving in many directions.

Synset Examples

asymmetrical features
a dress with a crooked hemline

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - having no bends or curves; regular in shape or outline

Etymology

From Middle English 'croket', a diminutive of 'crok', from Old Norse 'krókr' (hook).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01967858-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈkrʊk.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrʊk.ɪd / (BrE)
(of a person's back or posture) having the back and shoulders rounded; not straight or erect.
having a rounded, not straight, back

Examples

After years of hard labor, the farmer walked with a crooked back.
The old man's crooked figure was silhouetted against the setting sun.
She noticed a crooked posture in the portrait of her great-grandmother.

Synset Examples

a little oldish misshapen stooping woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stooped - having a bent or rounded back
  • Antonym: erect - standing upright, not bent

Etymology

From Old English 'crōc' (hook), related to 'crook' (bend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01242072-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈkrʊkɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪd / (BrE)
bent or twisted out of shape; not straight; also, behaving dishonestly or fraudulently.
bent; dishonest

Examples

The old man walked with a crooked cane that barely supported him.
The politician was accused of making crooked deals for personal gain.
A crooked smile appeared on her face as she planned her next move.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - not bent or curved; honest; opposite of 'crooked' in physical or moral sense
  • Synonym: bent - shaped or forced into a curve or angle, especially not straight
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere, opposite of 'crooked' (dishonest sense)

Etymology

From Old Norse 'krókr' (hook, bend).

Source: WordNet
