canonized

1. ADJ

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkænənaɪzd / (BrE)
Regarded as sacred, inviolable, or having official, authoritative status.
regarded as sacred or authoritative

Examples

The ancient text is considered canonized by the religious community.
Her views became canonized in the field of philosophy over time.
Only a few traditions have been truly canonized in the institution's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctified - Declared or made holy, especially officially
  • Synonym: sacred - Regarded with reverence or religious respect
  • Antonym: secular - Not connected with religious or sacred matters

Etymology

From Medieval Latin canonizare (to canonize), from canon (rule or standard).
