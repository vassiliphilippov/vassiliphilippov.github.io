zipper

1. VERB

/ ˈzɪpər / (AmE), / ˈzɪpə / (BrE)
to close or fasten something using a zipper
close or fasten with a zipper

Examples

She quickly zippered her suitcase before leaving for the airport.
It's cold outside, so please zipper your coat all the way up.
He zippered the bag to keep everything secure.

Synset Examples

Zip up your jacket — it's cold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unzip - to open something by releasing the zipper

Etymology

From the noun 'zipper', originally trademarked by B.F. Goodrich in the 1920s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɪpər / (AmE), / ˈzɪpə / (BrE)
a fastening device made of two toothed strips and a sliding piece that locks them together, used especially in clothing and bags
a fastener with interlocking teeth and a slider

Examples

The zipper on my jacket broke during the hike.
She struggled with the zipper on her suitcase at the airport.
Make sure the zipper is closed before you wash the hoodie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - a device used to close or join things, especially clothing
  • Antonym: button - a small disc or knob sewn onto clothing for fastening

Etymology

Coined in the early 20th century, probably from the sound 'zip' plus the agent suffix '-er'. Trademarked by B. F. Goodrich.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04245427-n
Synset POS: n
