to bourgeon

1. VERB

/ ˈbɜːrdʒən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdʒən / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce buds or begin to grow; to develop or flourish rapidly.
produce buds or begin to grow

Examples

In the early spring, many trees in the orchard bourgeon before any leaves appear.
The roses quickly bourgeoned after receiving plenty of sunlight and water.
New ideas began to bourgeon within the creative minds of the artists.

Synset Examples

the potatoes sprouted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bud - to produce or develop buds

Etymology

From Old French *bourjoner*, from *bourgeon* (bud).
