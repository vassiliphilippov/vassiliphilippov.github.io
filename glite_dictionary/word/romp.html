romp

1. NOUN

/ rɑmp / (AmE), / rɒmp / (BrE)
an easy or decisive victory, especially in a competition or contest
an easy victory

Examples

The team's latest game was a romp over its longtime rivals.
What was expected to be a close match turned out to be a romp.
She won the election in a romp, securing over 70% of the votes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkover - an easy victory or unopposed win in a contest
  • Antonym: struggle - a difficult or hard-fought contest

2. NOUN

/ rɑmp / (AmE), / rɒmp / (BrE)
a lively and carefree play or activity, especially involving physical movement and fun
lively, carefree play or activity

Examples

The children had a romp in the park, laughing and chasing each other around the playground.
After finishing their homework, the siblings enjoyed a quick romp in the backyard before dinner.
The puppy's joyful romp through the field delighted everyone watching.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - a playful and lively activity
  • Synonym: play - activity done for enjoyment and recreation
  • Synonym: revel - to take great pleasure in a lively activity or party

3. VERB

/ rɑmp / (AmE), / rɒmp / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to run or move about in a lively, playful, or spirited way, often easily and fairly fast.
run or move playfully and energetically

Examples

The children love to romp in the park after school.
A litter of puppies romped around the backyard, full of energy.
They spent the afternoon romping through the fields, laughing and chasing each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically

4. VERB

/ rɑmp / (AmE), / rɒmp / (BrE)
(of a person or team) to win a competition very easily with little effort or opposition.
win easily, especially in a competition

Examples

The team romped to victory with a score of 5–0.
She romped through her exams, barely breaking a sweat.
Our candidate romped home in the election, winning by a landslide.
