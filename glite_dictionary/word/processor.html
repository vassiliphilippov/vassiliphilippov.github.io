processor

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.ses.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ses.ə / (BrE)
a business that processes agricultural products and prepares them for market
business that processes agricultural products

Examples

The apple processor buys fruit from local farmers, sorts it, and packages it for stores.
Small dairy processors face increasing competition from large companies.
The wheat processor invested in new equipment to improve production efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manufacturer - a person or business that makes goods for sale

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.ses.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ɛs.ə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to process things such as foods, photographs, or applications
a person who processes things

Examples

The job of a processor at the factory is to check and package food products.
As a loan processor, she reviews all the submitted applications for approval.
The photograph processor developed my film in less than an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handler - a person who handles something
  • Antonym: applicant - a person who applies for something, often processed by a processor
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10497540-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.sɛs.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ɛs.ə / (BrE)
the central part of a computer, typically a microprocessor chip, that carries out instructions and processes data
central computer component that processes data

Examples

Most modern laptops have a powerful processor to handle demanding software applications.
Upgrading the processor can significantly improve your computer’s speed and performance.
The smartphone’s processor allows it to run multiple apps smoothly at the same time.

Synset Examples

the CPU and the memory form the central part of a computer to which the peripherals are attached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CPU - central processing unit, main chip that executes instructions
  • Synonym: microprocessor - integrated circuit that contains a computer's processing core

Etymology

From 'process' + '-or', indicating something that processes.
