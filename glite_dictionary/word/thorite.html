thorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔːraɪt / (AmE), / ˈθɔːraɪt / (BrE)
a rare radioactive mineral consisting of thorium silicate, found in coarse granite and used as a source of thorium.
a radioactive mineral; thorium source

Examples

Scientists collected thorite samples from the remote granite outcrop for further analysis.
The mineral thorite is valued for its thorium content, making it important in nuclear research.
Granite formations in Norway are known to contain deposits of thorite.

Etymology

From thorium (the element) + -ite (mineral suffix).
