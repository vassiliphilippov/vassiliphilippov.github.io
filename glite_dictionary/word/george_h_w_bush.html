George H. W. Bush

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ˈeɪtʃ ˈdʌbəl.juː bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ˈeɪtʃ ˈdʌb.l.juː bʊʃ / (BrE)
(1924–2018) The 41st President of the United States (1989–1993) and vice president under Ronald Reagan (1981–1989).
41st U.S. President; vice president under Reagan

Examples

George H. W. Bush served as President of the United States from 1989 to 1993.
Many credit George H. W. Bush with overseeing the end of the Cold War.
As vice president, George H. W. Bush played a key role in shaping foreign policy.
