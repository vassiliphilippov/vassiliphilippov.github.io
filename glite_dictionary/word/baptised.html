baptised

1. ADJ

/ bæpˈtaɪzd / (AmE), / bæpˈtaɪzd / (BrE)
Having undergone the Christian ritual of baptism.
having received Christian baptism

Examples

She was baptised as an infant in her local church.
Only baptised members are allowed to take part in the ceremony.
The child became a baptised member of the congregation last Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbaptised - not having undergone the Christian ritual of baptism

Etymology

From the verb 'baptise,' which comes from Greek 'baptizein' (to immerse, to dip).
