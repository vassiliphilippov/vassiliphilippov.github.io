Syngonium

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈɡoʊniəm / (AmE), / sɪŋˈɡəʊniəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical climbing plants from Central and South America, cultivated as houseplants for their attractive, velvety leaves
genus of tropical climbing houseplants

Examples

I keep a Syngonium on my desk because its leaves are so decorative.
Many plant enthusiasts prefer Syngonium for its easy care and beautiful variegation.
The Syngonium climbed up the moss pole, quickly covering it with lush foliage.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek 'syn' (together) + 'gon' (joint/angle), referring to jointed ovaries in the plant.

2. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈɡoʊniəm / (AmE), / sɪŋˈɡəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical climbing plants native to Central and South America, often grown as ornamental houseplants for their attractive foliage.
a genus of ornamental tropical climbing plants

Examples

The Syngonium thrives indoors, making it a popular choice for houseplant enthusiasts.
She arranged several pots of Syngonium by the window to brighten up the room.
With its arrow-shaped leaves, the Syngonium adds an exotic feel to the office space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aroid - a member of the arum family, to which Syngonium belongs

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek syn (together) + gonē (generation, offspring), referring to the united ovaries.
