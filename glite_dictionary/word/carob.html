carob

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəb / (AmE), / ˈkærəb / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean tree (Ceratonia siliqua) whose long, edible pods are often used as a chocolate substitute
Mediterranean tree with edible pods

Examples

The carob tree produces large, sweet pods that are often ground into powder.
Health-conscious bakers sometimes use carob instead of cocoa in their recipes.
Seeds from the carob are sometimes used as a natural thickener in food products.

Etymology

From Arabic ḵarrūb, via Medieval Latin carrubia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærəb / (AmE), / ˈkærəb / (BrE)
a powder made from the ground seeds and pods of the carob tree, often used as a substitute for chocolate
powder substitute for chocolate

Examples

Many health food recipes use carob instead of cocoa powder.
Some people prefer carob because it is naturally sweeter than chocolate.
The bakery sells carob cookies for those avoiding caffeine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chocolate - food made from roasted cacao seeds, usually sweetened and eaten as candy or used as a flavoring

Etymology

From Arabic ḵarrūb (carob tree or pod), ultimately from Greek kerátion (small horn, carob pod).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæɹəb / (AmE), / ˈkærəb / (BrE)
A long, brown pod from the Mediterranean tree Ceratonia siliqua containing edible sweet pulp and seeds; often used as animal feed or as a chocolate substitute.
sweet brown pod, chocolate substitute

Examples

The health food store sells carob chips as a caffeine-free alternative to chocolate.
Some dog treats use carob instead of chocolate, since it's safe for pets.
She added carob powder to her smoothie for a subtly sweet, earthy flavor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chocolate - a sweet, brown food made from roasted cacao beans
  • Synonym: cocoa - the seed or powder from which chocolate is made

Etymology

From Old French carobe, from Medieval Latin carrubia, from Arabic kharrūb.
