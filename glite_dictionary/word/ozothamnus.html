Ozothamnus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊzoʊˈθæmnəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊzəʊˈθamnəs / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs and perennial herbs, sometimes included in the genus Helichrysum, known for their small, aromatic leaves and clusters of tiny flowers.
genus of Australian shrubs and herbs

Examples

Several species of Ozothamnus are cultivated for their ornamental value in Australian gardens.
Botanists debated whether Ozothamnus should be merged with the genus Helichrysum.
The aromatic leaves of Ozothamnus make it a popular choice in native landscaping projects.

Etymology

From Greek 'ozos' (branch) and 'thamnus' (shrub or bush).

2. NOUN

/ oʊzəˈθæmnəs / (AmE), / əʊzəˈθamnəs / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs and perennial herbs in the family Asteraceae, sometimes formerly included in the genus Helichrysum.
genus of Australian shrubs and herbs

Examples

Several species of Ozothamnus are popular in Australian native gardens.
Botanists debated whether to classify these plants under Ozothamnus or Helichrysum.
The silvery foliage of Ozothamnus makes it a distinctive choice in landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helichrysum - a related genus in Asteraceae, sometimes including Ozothamnus
