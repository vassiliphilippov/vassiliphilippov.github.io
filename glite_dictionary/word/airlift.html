airlift

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈeə.lɪft / (BrE)
The transportation of people or goods by aircraft, especially when other means of access are unavailable.
emergency air transportation

Examples

After the flood cut off road access, an airlift was organized to deliver food and medicine.
The city received crucial supplies through an emergency airlift during the crisis.
The government coordinated an airlift to evacuate stranded tourists from the island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airbridge - supply route by air, especially in emergencies
  • Antonym: blockade - isolating area to prevent entry or exit, often the cause for airlifts

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'lift' in the early 20th century, referring to the act of lifting by aircraft.

2. VERB

/ ˈɛrˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈeəˌlɪft / (BrE)
to transport people or goods to or from a place by aircraft, especially when other means are not possible
transport by aircraft when inaccessible by other means

Examples

The government decided to airlift supplies to the flood victims in the remote village.
During the crisis, thousands of refugees were airlifted to safety.
Humanitarian aid is often airlifted into conflict zones where ground access is impossible.

Synset Examples

Food is airlifted into Bosnia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evacuate - to remove people from a place of danger to a safer place
  • Synonym: ship - to transport goods by ship, truck, or other conveyance

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'lift', originally referring to organized air transport operations, especially in emergencies or blockades.
