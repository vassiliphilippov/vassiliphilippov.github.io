Prunus triloba

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs traɪˈloʊbə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs traɪˈləʊbə / (BrE)
A deciduous Chinese shrub or small tree of the rose family, often with three-lobed leaves and grown for its pink-white flowers.
Chinese ornamental shrub or small tree

Examples

The Prunus triloba is prized for its striking spring blossoms in many ornamental gardens.
Botanists often recommend Prunus triloba for its tolerance of cooler climates.
My neighbor planted a Prunus triloba along the fence for its beautiful pink-white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering almond - common name for Prunus triloba, ornamental shrub known for its pink blossoms

Etymology

From Latin: Prunus (genus name for plum, cherry, and related trees) and triloba (three-lobed, referring to the leaf shape).
