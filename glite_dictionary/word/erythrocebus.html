Erythrocebus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθroʊˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəʊˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys known as patas monkeys, native to central Africa.
genus of patas monkeys
medical

Examples

The Erythrocebus genus includes the well-known patas monkey of Africa.
Researchers observed a troop of Erythrocebus in the savannahs of Kenya.
The Erythrocebus species are recognized for their remarkable speed on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patas monkey - a long-legged ground-dwelling monkey native to Africa

Etymology

From Greek erythros (red) + kēbos (monkey).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ɪ.θroʊˈsiː.bəs / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪ.θrəʊˈsiː.bəs / (BrE)
a genus of Old World monkeys including the patas monkey, native to Central and West Africa
genus of Old World monkeys (patas monkey)

Examples

Scientists observed a group of Erythrocebus in their natural savanna habitat.
The Erythrocebus genus is known for its remarkable speed on the ground.
Unlike many primates, Erythrocebus spend most of their time on open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patas monkey - a species of monkey in the genus Erythrocebus

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'kēbos' (long-tailed monkey).
