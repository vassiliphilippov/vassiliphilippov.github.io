Tanacetum camphoratum

1. NOUN

/ tænəˌsiːtəm kæmfəˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / tanəˌsiːtəm kamfəˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
A densely hairy perennial plant of the aster family, native to the San Francisco Bay area, notable for its rayless flower heads.
species of plant in the San Francisco Bay area

Examples

Tanacetum camphoratum is found primarily in sandy soils near the San Francisco Bay shoreline.
The flowers of Tanacetum camphoratum are unusual because they lack the typical ray petals seen in other daisies.
Botanists study Tanacetum camphoratum to better understand the unique plant communities of California.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Tanacetum (tansy) + species epithet camphoratum (camphor-scented), referring to its aroma.
