Agrostemma githago

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡrɑːˈstɛm.ə ɡɪˈθɑː.ɡoʊ / (AmE), / əˌɡrɒˈstɛm.ə ɡɪˈθɑː.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
A European annual plant with large, trumpet-shaped reddish-purple flowers and poisonous seeds, commonly found as a weed in grainfields and roadsides; also known as corncockle.
a weed with reddish-purple flowers, also called corncockle

Examples

Farmers often remove Agrostemma githago from their fields because its seeds are poisonous to livestock.
While hiking in Europe, she found Agrostemma githago growing beside the old country road.
The striking purple flowers of Agrostemma githago make it easy to spot among the wheat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corncockle - common name for Agrostemma githago

Etymology

From Greek 'agros' (field) and 'stemma' (garland), species name 'githago' from Latin for corncockle.
