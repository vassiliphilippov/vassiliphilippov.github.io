leal

1. ADJ

/ liːl / (AmE), / liːl / (BrE)
Archaic: loyal and true; faithful in allegiance.
loyal; faithful (archaic)
archaic

Examples

He was a leal companion, devoted and trustworthy until the end.
The old ballad praised the leal soldiers who never betrayed their king.
Even in the face of temptation, she remained leal to her beliefs.

Synset Examples

leal to the core of her intrepid Scottish heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyal - faithful in allegiance
  • Synonym: faithful - steady in allegiance or affection
  • Antonym: disloyal - not loyal; unfaithful

Etymology

From Old French *leel*, from Latin *legalis* (legal, loyal).
