cartographical

1. ADJ

/ kɑr.təˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / kɑː.təˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the making or study of maps and charts.
relating to mapping or cartography

Examples

The museum displayed several cartographical artifacts from the early 19th century.
Advances in cartographical techniques have revolutionized how we understand geography.
Her research focuses on the cartographical representation of climate data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartographic - relating to the making of maps
  • Antonym: noncartographical - not relating to map-making

Etymology

From French 'cartographique', from 'cartographie' (cartography), ultimately from Greek 'khartēs' (map) + 'graphē' (writing).
