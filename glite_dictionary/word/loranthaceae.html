Loranthaceae

1. NOUN

/ lɔːˌrænˈθeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / lɒːˌranˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of parasitic or hemiparasitic shrubs, shrublets, or small trees found mainly in tropical and temperate regions, often attaching to host plants by specialized structures called haustoria
parasitic plant family (Loranthaceae)
medical

Examples

Many species in the Loranthaceae family are known for their parasitic growth on tree hosts.
Scientists have debated whether to include Viscaceae within Loranthaceae in botanical classifications.
The bright flowers of some Loranthaceae species attract birds for pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Viscaceae - a closely related plant family sometimes included within Loranthaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Loranthus and the standard botanical suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ lɔːˌrænˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / lɒˌranˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical and temperate parasitic or hemiparasitic shrubs or small trees, whose members often attach to host plants via haustoria; sometimes includes Viscaceae in some classifications
a family of parasitic plants in botany

Examples

Many members of the Loranthaceae are known as mistletoes and are parasitic on tree branches.
Some botanists include Viscaceae within the family Loranthaceae in their classifications.
The Loranthaceae exhibit a variety of adaptations for attaching themselves to host plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Viscaceae - a closely related plant family, sometimes included in Loranthaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Loranthus + standard botanical suffix -aceae.
