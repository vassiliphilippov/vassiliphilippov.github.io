Perisoreus canadensis capitalis

1. NOUN

/ pɛrɪˈsɔːriˌəs kəˈnædənˌsɪs kəˈpɪtəlɪs / (AmE), / pɛrɪˈsɔːriˌəs kəˈnædənˌsɪs kəˈpɪtəlɪs / (BrE)
a subspecies of Canada jay with a white head, widely found from Montana to Arizona
a subspecies of Canada jay (bird)

Examples

The Perisoreus canadensis capitalis is commonly seen in the forests of the Rocky Mountains.
With its distinctive white head, the Perisoreus canadensis capitalis stands out among other jays.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Perisoreus canadensis capitalis to study their habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada jay - a jay species native to North America, also known as gray jay

Etymology

From Latin: genus Perisoreus, species canadensis (meaning 'of Canada'), subspecies capitalis ('of the head', referring to the white head).
