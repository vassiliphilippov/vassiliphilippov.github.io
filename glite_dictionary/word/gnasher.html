gnasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæʃər / (AmE), / ˈnæʃə / (BrE)
An informal term for a dangerous or fearsome creature, often used in fictional or humorous contexts.
informal: dangerous creature
informal

Examples

Local legends say a gnasher roams these hills after dark, scaring away even the bravest hikers.
As the gnasher emerged from the shadows, the villagers scattered in fear.
The fantasy novel describes the gnasher as a beast with razor-sharp teeth and glowing eyes.

Synset Examples

He feared the gnasher would attack if he got too close.

Etymology

From "gnash" (to grind the teeth), suggesting a creature that bites or is fierce.
