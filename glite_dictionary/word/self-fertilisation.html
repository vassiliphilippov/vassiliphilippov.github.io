self-fertilisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˌfɜrtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˌfəːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an organism's own male and female gametes unite to produce offspring
fertilization within the same individual

Examples

Some flowering plants reproduce by self-fertilisation, ensuring genetic continuity.
Researchers observed self-fertilisation in several species of hermaphroditic worms.
Although self-fertilisation is efficient, it limits genetic diversity in the population.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-fertilisation - fertilization involving gametes from different individuals

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + 'fertilisation', from Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful).
