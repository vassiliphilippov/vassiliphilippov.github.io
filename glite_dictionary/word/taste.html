taste

1. NOUN

/ teɪst / (AmE), / teɪst / (BrE)
a brief or slight experience of something, giving a sense of what it is like
brief experience; sample

Examples

Her summer internship gave her a taste of the business world.
He got his first taste of freedom when he traveled alone.
The concert offered a taste of the band's new musical direction.

Synset Examples

he got a taste of life on the wild side
she enjoyed her brief taste of independence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like

Etymology

From Old French *taster*, from Latin *tastare* (to touch, taste).
