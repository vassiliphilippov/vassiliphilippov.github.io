processional

1. ADJ

/ prəˈsɛʃənl / (AmE), / prəˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a procession, especially in a formal or ceremonial context.
relating to a procession

Examples

The choir entered the cathedral in their processional robes.
A slow processional march accompanied the graduates as they walked in.
He admired the processional order of the ceremony.

Synset Examples

in good processional order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - a group of people or vehicles moving forward in an orderly, ceremonial way

Etymology

From Medieval Latin processionalis, from processio (procession).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
designed or intended for use in a procession; relating to a procession.
for or relating to a procession

Examples

The choir entered the cathedral to the sound of processional music.
A large processional cross was carried at the head of the parade.
The wedding ceremony began with a processional piece played by the organist.

Synset Examples

processional music
processional cross

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group of people or vehicles moving together as part of a ceremony

Etymology

From Medieval Latin processionalis, from Latin processio (procession).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03114977-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ prəˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a piece of music played or sung during a religious procession
music for a religious procession

Examples

The organist played a beautiful processional as the bride entered the church.
The choir rehearsed the processional for the upcoming Easter service.
A solemn processional marked the beginning of the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recessional - music played at the end of a religious service or ceremony

Etymology

From Medieval Latin processionalis, from Latin processio (procession).
