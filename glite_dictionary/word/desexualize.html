to desexualize

1. VERB

/ diˈsɛkʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛksjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make infertile or incapable of reproduction, often by removing or altering reproductive organs or functions
make infertile; remove reproductive ability

Examples

The animals were desexualized to prevent them from breeding in the sanctuary.
Certain medical treatments can desexualize a person as a side effect.
Some controversial policies in history sought to desexualize specific populations.

Synset Examples

in some countries, people with genetically transmissible disabilities are sterilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sterilize - to make unable to produce offspring, especially by surgical means

Etymology

From de- (removal) + sexualize, based on sexual.

2. VERB

/ diˈsɛkʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈsɛksjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
To redirect or suppress sexual desires or characteristics, removing or neutralizing sexual content or urges.
suppress or redirect sexual urges or traits

Examples

Some therapies seek to desexualize problematic behaviors by redirecting urges into nonsexual activities.
The media often desexualizes certain body types by omitting any sexual representation.
He tried to desexualize his thoughts through meditation and mindfulness exercises.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sexualize - to make sexual or give a sexual character to something

Etymology

From de- (removal) + sexual + -ize (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01957534-v
Synset POS: v
