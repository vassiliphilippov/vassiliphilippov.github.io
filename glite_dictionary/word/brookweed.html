brookweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʊkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbrʊkˌwiːd / (BrE)
a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant of wet places in Europe and North America, also known as water pimpernel (Samolus valerandi)
small wetland plant; water pimpernel

Examples

The stream was lined with delicate brookweed and other flowering plants.
Researchers collected samples of brookweed from several wetland habitats.
Brookweed thrives in areas with moist, shady soil near fresh water sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water pimpernel - another common name for brookweed, Samolus valerandi

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrʊkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈbrʊkwiːd / (BrE)
A small plant of wet habitats in Europe and Asia, also known as water pimpernel, with small white flowers.
a wetland plant with small white flowers

Examples

Brookweed thrives in damp meadows and along the edges of streams.
You can often spot brookweed in shaded, marshy areas throughout the countryside.
The delicate white flowers of brookweed make it easy to identify among other wetland plants.

Related Terms
