Georgia

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒə / (BrE)
A state in the southeastern United States, known for its history, diverse landscapes, and as one of the Confederate states during the American Civil War.
a state in the southeastern US

Examples

Georgia is famous for its peaches and southern hospitality.
The capital city of Georgia is Atlanta.
During the Civil War, Georgia played a key role as a Confederate state.

Etymology

Named after King George II of Great Britain.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔr.dʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔː.dʒə / (BrE)
A country in the Caucasus region of Eurasia, situated on the Black Sea and bordered by Russia, Turkey, Armenia, and Azerbaijan; formerly part of the Soviet Union, independent since 1991.
country in the Caucasus, Eurasia

Examples

She traveled to Georgia to explore its ancient monasteries and stunning mountain landscapes.
After gaining independence in 1991, Georgia developed a unique wine culture that attracts visitors worldwide.
The cuisine of Georgia is famous for its spicy stews and traditional breads.

Etymology

From Persian 'Gurjistan', ultimately of uncertain origin; adapted into European languages in medieval times.

3. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒə / (BrE)
A former British colony in North America, established in 1732 with its capital at Savannah, which later became one of the original thirteen states of the United States.
former British colony, now U.S. state

Examples

Many settlers traveled to Georgia in search of new opportunities during colonial times.
Savannah served as the capital of Georgia during its years as a British colony.
The colony of Georgia played a key role in the southern campaign of the American Revolution.

Etymology

Named after King George II of Great Britain.
