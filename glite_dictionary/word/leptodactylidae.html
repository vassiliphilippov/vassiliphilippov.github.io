Leptodactylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛp.toʊ.dækˈtɪl.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təʊ.dækˈtɪl.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of frogs, found mainly in Central and South America, known as the southern frogs or tropical frogs.
a family of New World frogs

Examples

The family Leptodactylidae includes hundreds of frog species native to the Americas.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Leptodactylidae during their expedition in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike some other families, Leptodactylidae frogs often nest on land near water sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bufonidae - a related family of frogs, often known as the true toads

Etymology

From Greek leptos (slender) + dactylos (finger) + Latin -idae (family suffix in zoology).
