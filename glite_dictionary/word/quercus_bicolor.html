Quercus bicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs baɪˈkɔːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs baɪˈkɔːlə / (BrE)
a large deciduous oak tree native to eastern North America, with flaky bark and fewer-lobed leaves than other white oaks, typically found in wet soils and valued for its strong wood
species of oak tree native to eastern North America

Examples

The Quercus bicolor is commonly known as the swamp white oak and thrives in wet lowland areas.
Many park planners choose Quercus bicolor for locations prone to flooding because of its adaptability.
Woodworkers prize Quercus bicolor for its dense and durable timber, often used in construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp white oak - common name for Quercus bicolor
  • Degree Higher: Quercus alba - white oak species of eastern and central North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'bicolor' meaning 'two-colored', referring to the different colors of the leaf surfaces.
