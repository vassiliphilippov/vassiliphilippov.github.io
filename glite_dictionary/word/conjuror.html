conjuror

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dʒɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒə.rə / (BrE)
a person who entertains an audience by performing magic tricks or illusions
magician or illusionist

Examples

The conjuror amazed the children by pulling a rabbit out of a hat.
She hired a conjuror for her daughter's birthday party.
The conjuror performed a series of astonishing illusions on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magician - a person who performs magic tricks
  • Synonym: illusionist - a person who performs illusions for entertainment

Etymology

From Old French 'conjureor', from Latin 'conjurator' (swearer or conspirator), later applied to those who 'conjure' supernatural effects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑndʒərər / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒərə / (BrE)
a person who claims to perform magic, especially one thought to have supernatural powers, such as a witch doctor or sorcerer
person claiming magic or supernatural powers

Examples

The village conjuror was called to cure the mysterious illness.
Stories about the conjuror spread fear and fascination among the townsfolk.
Some believed the conjuror could communicate with spirits and cast powerful spells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magician - person skilled in performing magic tricks
  • Synonym: sorcerer - person who claims to use supernatural powers
  • Synonym: witch doctor - a traditional healer who uses magical or supernatural methods

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French conjureor, from Latin conjūrātor (one who swears together).
