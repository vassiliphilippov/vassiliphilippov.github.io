risklessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪsk.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪsk.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being without risk; complete safety or absence of danger.
state of having no risk

Examples

The new drug underwent years of testing to ensure its risklessness before being approved.
Investors are often skeptical of any plan that claims complete risklessness.
Absolute risklessness is rarely possible in business decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety - the state of being safe; freedom from danger or risk
  • Antonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury

Etymology

From 'riskless', based on 'risk' (danger, possibility of loss) and suffix '-less' (without), with '-ness' forming a noun.
