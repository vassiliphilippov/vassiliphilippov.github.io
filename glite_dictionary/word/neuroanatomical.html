neuroanatomical

1. ADJ

/ ˌnʊroʊˌænəˈtɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˌænəˈtɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the anatomical structure of the nervous system or neural tissue.
relating to nervous system anatomy
medical

Examples

Recent advances in imaging allow for detailed neuroanatomical mapping of the human brain.
Damage to specific neuroanatomical regions can lead to distinct behavioral symptoms.
The textbook provides a comprehensive overview of neuroanatomical structures and their functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomical - relating to the structure of organisms

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves) + anatomical, from Greek neuron (nerve) and ana- (up) + tomē (cutting).
