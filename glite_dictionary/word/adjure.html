to adjure

1. VERB

/ əˈdʒʊr / (AmE), / əˈdʒʊə / (BrE)
to urge or request someone solemnly or earnestly to do something
to urge or request solemnly

Examples

The lawyer adjured the witness to tell the whole truth.
He adjured them to remain calm in the face of danger.
I adjure you, do not let anger cloud your judgment.

Synset Examples

The prophet bid all people to become good persons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beseech - to ask someone for something earnestly or anxiously
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Antonym: command - to order someone authoritatively to do something

Etymology

From Latin adjurare, from ad- (to) + jurare (to swear).

2. VERB

/ əˈdʒʊr / (AmE), / əˈdʒʊə / (BrE)
to command someone solemnly, especially under oath or penalty, to do something
command solemnly or under oath

Examples

I adjure you, by all you hold dear, to tell the truth in this matter.
The witness was adjured to speak only what he knew to be true.
They adjured their leader to act in the name of justice and mercy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beseech - to beg earnestly or anxiously
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately

Etymology

From Latin 'adiurare' (to swear to), from 'ad-' (to) + 'iurare' (to swear).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
