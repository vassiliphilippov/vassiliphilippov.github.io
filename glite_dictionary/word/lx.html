LX

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ɛks / (AmE), / ɛl ɛks / (BrE)
the Roman numeral representing the number sixty (60)
Roman numeral for sixty (60)

Examples

The ancient manuscript was dated to the year LX of the emperor's reign.
In Roman numerals, LX is written for sixty.
You might see LX engraved on old clocks to indicate the number 60.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: L - Roman numeral for fifty (50)
  • Degree Higher: LXX - Roman numeral for seventy (70)

Etymology

From Latin, where 'L' represents 50 and 'X' represents 10; combined they denote 60.
