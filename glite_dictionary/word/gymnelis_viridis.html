Gymnelis viridis

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɛlɪs vɪˈriːdɪs / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈniːlɪs vɪˈriːdɪs / (BrE)
A brightly colored, scaleless species of Arctic eelpout found in northern ocean waters.
Arctic eelpout species

Examples

The Gymnelis viridis inhabits cold Arctic waters near the ocean floor.
Researchers recently observed several Gymnelis viridis specimens while conducting marine surveys.
Due to its vivid coloration, Gymnelis viridis is easily distinguished from related eelpout species.

Etymology

From Latin: 'gymnelis' (a type of fish), 'viridis' (green).
