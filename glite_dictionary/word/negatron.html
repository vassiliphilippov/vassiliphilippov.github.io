negatron

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡəˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡəˌtrɒn / (BrE)
an electron; an elementary particle carrying a negative electric charge
electron carrying negative charge

Examples

The negatron is identical to the particle commonly known as the electron.
In particle physics, the negatron and the positron are considered antimatter counterparts.
Some older scientific texts use the term negatron instead of electron for clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron - an elementary particle with negative charge
  • Antonym: positron - an elementary particle with positive charge

Etymology

Formed from 'negative' and '-tron' (as in electron), used to distinguish the negatively charged electron from the positron.
