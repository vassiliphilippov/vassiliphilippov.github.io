Salix alba

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˈsalɪks ˈalbə / (BrE)
A large species of willow tree native to Eurasia and North Africa, with greyish, downy leaves and grey bark.
species of large willow tree

Examples

The bark of Salix alba has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
A row of Salix alba lined the banks of the quiet river.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Salix alba to understand its adaptability to different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white willow - alternative name for Salix alba

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' meaning 'willow' and 'alba' meaning 'white'.
