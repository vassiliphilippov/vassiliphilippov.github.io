argentous

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈdʒɛntəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒɛntəs / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting compounds in which silver has a valence of one (univalent).
of silver with valence one
medical

Examples

The chemist analyzed an argentous compound to determine its properties.
Unlike argentic compounds, argentous compounds contain silver in the +1 oxidation state.
Certain photographic processes rely on argentous solutions to develop images.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: argentic - relating to compounds in which silver is bivalent

Etymology

From Latin 'argentum' (silver) + '-ous' (forming adjectives indicating a lower valence).
