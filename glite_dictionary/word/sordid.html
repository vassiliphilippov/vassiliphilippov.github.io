sordid

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔr.dɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.dɪd / (BrE)
dirty, unpleasant, and run-down; morally or physically repulsive.
dirty and repulsive; run-down

Examples

The family lived in a sordid apartment block that had not been cleaned in years.
The novel describes the sordid reality of life in the city's poorest neighborhoods.
She recoiled at the sordid state of the abandoned house.

Synset Examples

a flyblown bar on the edge of town
a squalid overcrowded apartment in the poorest part of town
squalid living conditions
sordid shantytowns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squalid - extremely dirty and unpleasant, especially as a result of poverty or neglect
  • Degree Lower: filthy - very dirty

Etymology

From Latin 'sordidus' (dirty, filthy), from 'sordere' (to be dirty).
