Sphacele calycina

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfeɪsəli kælɪˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsfeɪsɪli kælɪˈsɪnə / (BrE)
A plant native to California, characterized by woolly stems and leaves and large white flowers.
California plant with woolly stems and white flowers

Examples

Sphacele calycina thrives in the dry, sandy soils of coastal California.
The fuzzy stems and white blooms make Sphacele calycina easy to identify in the wild.
Botanists collected several specimens of Sphacele calycina during the spring flowering season.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Sphacele' is a genus name, 'calycina' refers to the calyx (flower cup or outer part).
