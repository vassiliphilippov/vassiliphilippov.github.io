generation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of coming into being or being produced
the act of coming into being

Examples

The generation of electricity from solar power has increased rapidly in recent years.
The artist was fascinated by the generation of new ideas through collaboration.
Cell division is a process involving the generation of new cells.

Etymology

From Latin 'generatio', from 'generare' (to beget).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
all the people born and living at about the same time, regarded collectively.
people of the same age group

Examples

The younger generation is much more comfortable with technology than their parents.
Each generation faces its own unique set of challenges.
There is often a gap in values between one generation and the next.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'generatio' (a begetting), from 'gignere' (to beget).
