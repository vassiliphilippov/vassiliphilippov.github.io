laid-off

1. ADJ

/ leɪd ˈɔf / (AmE), / leɪd ˈɒf / (BrE)
having lost one's job, especially because of business reasons rather than personal performance
having lost your job

Examples

After the company downsized, many workers were laid-off and had to seek new employment.
She has been laid-off twice in the past five years due to economic downturns.
Hundreds of employees were laid-off when the factory closed last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unemployed - without a paid job but available to work
  • Antonym: employed - having paid work
