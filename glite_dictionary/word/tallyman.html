tallyman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæliˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtæliˌman / (BrE)
a man whose job is to count, record, or keep tallies, especially in commerce, voting, or collecting payments
person who keeps a tally or count

Examples

The tallyman checked each box as it was unloaded from the truck.
During the election, a tallyman keeps track of the votes as they're counted.
Local markets used to employ a tallyman to record all transactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tally clerk - person who counts or records items, especially in a workplace

Etymology

From 'tally' (a recorded count or score) + 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtælimæn / (AmE), / ˈtælɪmæn / (BrE)
a person who sells goods to customers on an installment payment system, chiefly in historical or regional usage
seller on installment plan

Examples

The tallyman would visit each week to collect payments for the furniture.
Many families in the early 20th century relied on the tallyman for household goods.
My grandmother remembers the tallyman coming door to door in her village.

Etymology

From tally (a recorded count or score) + man, originally referring to someone who kept accounts or tallies.
