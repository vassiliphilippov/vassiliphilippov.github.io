Calorie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌri / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əri / (BrE)
A unit of measurement for the energy value of food, equal to the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius; commonly used in nutrition.
unit of food energy (kilocalorie)

Examples

A medium-sized apple contains about 95 Calories.
If you want to lose weight, try to reduce your daily Calorie intake.
The label shows both fat and Calorie content per serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilocalorie - unit equal to 1,000 calories, used in food energy
  • Synonym: joule - SI unit of energy

Etymology

From French 'calorie', from Latin 'calor' (heat).
