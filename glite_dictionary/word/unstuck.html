unstuck

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstʌk / (AmE), / ʌnˈstʌk / (BrE)
no longer stuck; freed from a position or condition in which movement was prevented
freed from being stuck

Examples

After a few tries, the truck finally got unstuck from the mud.
It took some effort, but the window is now unstuck and opens easily.
The zipper was jammed, but with patience I got it unstuck.

Synset Examples

a man with a mule got my car unstuck
the gears locked in second and would not come unstuck

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stuck - unable to move or be moved from a position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00162541-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈstʌk / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈstʌk / (BrE)
thrown into confusion, disorganization, or no longer functioning correctly; disrupted or incoherent.
thrown into confusion, not working

Examples

As soon as their main supplier closed, the entire production schedule came unstuck.
The coach's carefully crafted game plan went unstuck after the first ten minutes.
His argument quickly became unstuck when faced with strong opposition.

Synset Examples

price programs became unstuck because little grain was available

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking organization; in disorder
  • Synonym: chaotic - in a state of complete confusion and disorder
  • Antonym: orderly - neatly and methodically arranged
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01674290-s
Synset POS: s
