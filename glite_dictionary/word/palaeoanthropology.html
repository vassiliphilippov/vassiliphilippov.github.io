palaeoanthropology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˌænθrəˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊˌanθrəˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of anthropology concerned with the study of human fossils and prehistoric human remains.
the study of human fossils

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in palaeoanthropology after discovering her passion for ancient human remains.
Recent advances in palaeoanthropology have shed new light on early human evolution.
Many groundbreaking discoveries about our ancestors have come from the field of palaeoanthropology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anthropology - the study of human societies, cultures, and their development
  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts and other physical remains

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'anthrōpos' (human) + 'logia' (study).
