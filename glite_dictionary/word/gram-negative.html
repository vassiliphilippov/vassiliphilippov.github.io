Gram-negative

1. ADJ

/ ɡræm ˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ɡram ˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
(of bacteria) not retaining the violet stain used in Gram's method of classification; typically having a cell wall with a thin peptidoglycan layer and an outer membrane.
bacteria not retaining violet Gram stain
medical

Examples

Some Gram-negative bacteria can cause serious infections in humans.
The doctor explained that Gram-negative organisms are often more resistant to antibiotics.
Researchers studied the cell walls of several Gram-negative species in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Gram-positive - (of bacteria) retaining the violet stain used in Gram's method

Etymology

Derived from the Gram stain method, named after Danish bacteriologist Hans Christian Gram (1853–1938), combined with 'negative' referring to the result of the test.
