Granicus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrænɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈɡranɪkəs / (BrE)
the battle fought in 334 BC in which Alexander the Great won his first major victory against the Persians.
battle between Alexander and Persians (334 BC)

Examples

The Battle of Granicus marked the beginning of Alexander's conquest of the Persian Empire.
Historians continue to study the tactics used at Granicus for their strategic importance.
Many consider Granicus to be a turning point in ancient military history.

Etymology

From the Ancient Greek 'Granikos,' the river near which the battle took place.
