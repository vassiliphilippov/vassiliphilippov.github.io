waviness

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ.vi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.vɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of having gentle curves or waves, especially in hair.
the quality of being wavy (especially hair)

Examples

Her hair has a natural waviness that makes it look full and lively.
The stylist enhanced the waviness of my hair with a special curling product.
Despite its waviness, her hair always looked neat and well-groomed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightness - the quality of being straight (especially hair)
  • Degree Higher: curliness - the quality of being curly (especially hair)

Etymology

From 'wavy' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the quality of being wavy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ.vi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.vi.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being uneven with gentle curves or undulations, like waves or wrinkles
quality of being wavy or uneven

Examples

The waviness of the curtains added a soft, elegant touch to the room.
Engineers must minimize the waviness of the metal surface to ensure its proper function.
Her hair had a natural waviness that she liked to emphasize with a curling iron.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even and without waves or roughness
  • Synonym: waviness - having a wavy or undulating form

Etymology

From 'wavy' (having waves) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a quality or state).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04780194-n
Synset POS: n
