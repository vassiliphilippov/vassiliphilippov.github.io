speciality

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
an area of work or study that someone has devoted themselves to or is an expert in, especially as a profession
main area of professional expertise

Examples

After years of training, neurology became his speciality.
Her speciality is pediatric medicine, and she loves working with children.
The chef's speciality was creating innovative vegan desserts.

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialty - main area of professional expertise (US spelling)
  • Antonym: generalist - a person competent in several fields rather than specializing in one

Etymology

From Old French 'specialite', from Latin 'specialitas' (particularity, specialty).

2. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
a distinguishing trait or feature that makes someone or something unique or notable
distinguishing trait or feature

Examples

His ability to solve complex puzzles quickly is his true speciality.
Attention to detail is a speciality that sets her apart from her colleagues.
Every artisan has a speciality that defines their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristic - a feature that helps to identify or distinguish something
  • Synonym: feature - a distinctive attribute or aspect
  • Antonym: ordinary - having no special or distinctive features

Etymology

From Latin 'specialitas' through French 'spécialité', meaning 'special quality.'

3. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
A particular skill, area of expertise, or feature for which a person or thing is especially known.
area of special skill or expertise

Examples

French cuisine is his speciality, and he prepares it with great passion.
Her speciality lies in designing sustainable architecture.
The shop's speciality is homemade chocolate truffles.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialty - area in which someone has special skill; alternative spelling
  • Synonym: forte - thing at which someone excels
  • Antonym: weakness - area in which someone is least skilled

Etymology

From Latin specialitas (quality of being special), via Old French spécialité.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
