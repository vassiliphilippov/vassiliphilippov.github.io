thematic

1. ADJ

/ θəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / θɪˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or based on a musical theme or melodic subject.
relating to a musical theme

Examples

The composer developed the piece using a strong thematic structure.
The orchestra returned to the thematic material introduced in the first movement.
Her analysis highlighted the thematic motifs that recurred throughout the symphony.

Etymology

From Greek 'thema' (subject, theme) via Latin 'thematicus' (of a theme).

2. ADJ

/ θəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / θɪˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or connected with the subject or theme of something, especially in speech, writing, or art.
relating to a topic or theme

Examples

The exhibition was organized in a thematic way, showcasing different aspects of the artist's work.
Her dissertation explores the thematic development in modern poetry.
There are strong thematic links between the two novels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topical - pertaining to a particular subject; relevant to current topics

Etymology

From Greek *thematikos*, from *thema* (subject, theme).
