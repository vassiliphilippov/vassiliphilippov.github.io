Robert Bunsen

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊbɚt ˈbʌnsən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbət ˈbʌnsən / (BrE)
(1811–1899) A German chemist known for pioneering spectrum analysis and inventing the Bunsen burner.
German chemist, inventor of the Bunsen burner

Examples

Robert Bunsen was instrumental in the development of the modern laboratory burner.
Many scientific instruments were improved through the efforts of Robert Bunsen.
The chemistry teacher explained how Robert Bunsen's invention changed laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bunsen burner - A type of laboratory gas burner invented by Robert Bunsen
