to sermonize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɜːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to speak in a manner typical of delivering a sermon, especially by expressing moral judgments or giving moral advice in a preachy way
speak as if giving a sermon; moralize

Examples

He tends to sermonize at family gatherings, which annoys his siblings.
Please don't sermonize about responsibility every time we talk.
She sermonized at length about the importance of honesty in business.

Synset Examples

This man always sermonizes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preach - to deliver a sermon or moral lesson
  • Synonym: lecture - to give a moralizing talk or warning

Etymology

From French 'sermoniser', from Latin 'sermonizare', from 'sermo' (speech, discourse).
