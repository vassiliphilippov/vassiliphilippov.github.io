butyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːtɪl / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪl / (BrE)
a hydrocarbon group or radical with the formula C4H9, derived from butane and found in various organic compounds
a hydrocarbon radical (C4H9)

Examples

The plastic was made using butyl rubber, which offers excellent resistance to chemicals.
Chemists often use butyl groups to modify the properties of organic molecules.
Some fuels contain additives with butyl radicals to enhance combustion efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butane - a gaseous hydrocarbon (C4H10) of the alkane series

Etymology

From butane, with the suffix -yl denoting a radical in chemistry.
