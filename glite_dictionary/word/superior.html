superior

1. ADJ

/ suˈpɪriər / (AmE), / suˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
of or characteristic of a higher rank, status, or importance than others
of higher rank or importance

Examples

The superior officer gave the final order to advance.
Her superior status within the organization allowed her to make important decisions.
Only candidates with superior qualifications will be considered for the management position.

Synset Examples

a superior ruler

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferior - of lower rank or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'superior', comparative of 'superus' (that is above).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02346685-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ suˈpɪriɚ / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
(of a planet) having an orbit farther from the Sun than that of the Earth.
(astronomy) orbit beyond Earth's

Examples

Mars is considered a superior planet because it orbits the Sun beyond Earth.
In astronomy, Jupiter is categorized as a superior planet.
The distinction between superior and inferior planets depends on their orbits relative to Earth's.

Synset Examples

Mars and Jupiter are the closest in of the superior planets

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferior - (of a planet) having an orbit closer to the Sun than Earth's orbit

Etymology

From Latin superior (higher, upper), from superus (above).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02357021-a
Synset POS: a
