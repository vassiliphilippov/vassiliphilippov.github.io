Yeatsian

1. ADJ

/ ˈjeɪtsiən / (AmE), / ˈjeɪtsiən / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or in the manner of the poet William Butler Yeats, especially his style, themes, or approach.
in the style of W. B. Yeats

Examples

The poet's latest collection is filled with Yeatsian symbolism and mysticism.
Critics describe her vision of Ireland as distinctly Yeatsian in its romanticism and complexity.
His performance brought a Yeatsian grandeur to the otherwise modern production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shakespearean - relating to or characteristic of Shakespeare
  • Synonym: Dickensian - relating to or characteristic of Charles Dickens

Etymology

From the name of William Butler Yeats (1865–1939), Irish poet and playwright, with the adjective-forming suffix -ian.
