picul

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkjʊl / (BrE)
a unit of weight used in some parts of Asia, approximately equal to 133 pounds (60.5 kilograms)
Asian unit of weight (about 133 lbs)

Examples

The trader purchased 20 piculs of rice for export to Europe.
In the 19th century, tea was often shipped in large picul quantities from China.
A typical picul of spices was a mainstay cargo on colonial merchant ships.

Etymology

From Malay *pikul* (to carry a load), adopted into English during the colonial trade era.
