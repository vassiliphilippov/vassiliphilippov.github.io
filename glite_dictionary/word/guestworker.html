guestworker

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛstˌwɜrkər / (AmE), / ˈɡɛstˌwɜːkə / (BrE)
a person who is given temporary permission to work in a country where they are not a citizen or permanent resident
person allowed to work temporarily in a foreign country

Examples

Many industries rely on guestworkers to fill temporary labor shortages during busy seasons.
The government introduced a new policy to protect the rights of guestworkers.
After her visa expired, the guestworker had to return to her home country.

Synset Examples

a Moroccan guestworker in Canada was accused of aiding terrorists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migrant worker - a person who moves from place to place to do seasonal or temporary work
  • Degree Higher: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country
  • Synonym: expatriate - a person living in a country other than their native country

Etymology

Modelled on German Gastarbeiter (‘guest worker’), from Gast (‘guest’) + Arbeiter (‘worker’).
