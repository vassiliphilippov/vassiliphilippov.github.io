ticktock

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪk.tɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɪk.tɒk / (BrE)
to make a repeated sound like that of a clock ticking
make a ticking sound like a clock

Examples

The old wall clock ticktocks steadily in the hallway, marking every second.
As midnight approached, the silence was broken only by a watch that ticktocked on the bedside table.
In the quiet classroom, the timer ticktocking on the teacher’s desk made everyone nervous.

Synset Examples

the clocks were ticking
the grandfather clock beat midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - to make a regular short, sharp sound, like that of a clock

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a mechanical clock.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02180022-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkˌtɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɪkˌtɒk / (BrE)
a steady, recurring ticking sound, such as the one made by a clock
sound of a clock ticking

Examples

The ticktock of the grandfather clock was the only sound in the quiet room.
I found the constant ticktock strangely calming as I drifted off to sleep.
Suddenly, the ticktock stopped, and she realized the battery had run out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick-tock - alternative spelling for the sound of a clock

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by a mechanical clock.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07412555-n
Synset POS: n
