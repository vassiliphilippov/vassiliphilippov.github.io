to solemnise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɑːləmˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪmnaɪz / (BrE)
to perform (a marriage or another ceremony) with the prescribed formalities and rites
perform a ceremony formally, esp. a wedding

Examples

The priest will solemnise the marriage in the cathedral tomorrow.
Only officially recognised clergy are permitted to solemnise weddings in this country.
He was honoured to solemnise the union of his two close friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to officiate - to act as an official in a ceremony, especially a wedding
  • Synonym: to celebrate - to perform a religious or formal ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'sollemnis' (formal, ceremonial) via Old French 'solemniser'.

2. VERB

/ sɑːləmlaɪz / (AmE), / sɒlɪmnaɪz / (BrE)
to mark or observe (an occasion, event, or ceremony) with formal dignity or seriousness
mark with formality or seriousness

Examples

The couple chose to solemnise their marriage with a traditional church ceremony.
The annual memorial service solemnises the sacrifices made by veterans.
The event was solemnised by a moment of silence.

Synset Examples

This ceremony solemnized our hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to mark (an event) with festivities or ceremony
  • Antonym: to trivialize - to make something seem less important or serious

Etymology

From Latin 'solemnizare', from 'solemnis' (customary, solemn).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ səˈlɛm.nɑɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.əm.naɪz / (BrE)
to observe or perform (a ceremony, event, etc.) with formal dignity or seriousness
perform with formal dignity; make solemn

Examples

The mayor solemnised the newly built bridge's opening with a formal ceremony.
They solemnised their union in a traditional church wedding.
Every year, the community solemnises the historic event with prayers and music.

Synset Examples

The King solemnized this day of mourning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - mark or celebrate an event with respect or ceremony
  • Antonym: to trivialise - make (something) seem less important or serious

Etymology

From Latin 'solemnizare' via Old French 'solemniser', meaning to celebrate solemnly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02585332-v
Synset POS: v
