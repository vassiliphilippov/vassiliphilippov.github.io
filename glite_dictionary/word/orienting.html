orienting

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔriˌɛntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪəntɪŋ / (BrE)
serving to guide or determine direction, position, or awareness, especially in relation to a reference system; providing orientation
providing direction or orientation

Examples

The company organized an orienting seminar to help new staff adjust to the workplace culture.
Her orienting instincts helped her quickly find her way in the unfamiliar city.
The animal made an orienting movement before deciding which direction to travel.

Synset Examples

noticed the bee's momentary orienting pause before heading back to the hive
an orienting program for new employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orient - to align or position in relation to a point of reference
  • Antonym: disorienting - causing confusion about direction or position

Etymology

Present participle of 'orient' (to align or position); from Latin 'orient-' (rising, eastern).
