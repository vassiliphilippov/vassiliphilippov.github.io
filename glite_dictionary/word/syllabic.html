syllabic

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of verse) having lines measured by the number of syllables, rather than by the arrangement of stresses or quantities.
based on number of syllables

Examples

Japanese haiku is a form of syllabic poetry.
The poem's syllabic structure gives it a unique rhythmic pattern.
Many French poets experimented with syllabic verse during the 19th century.

Etymology

From Greek 'syllabikos', from 'syllabē' (syllable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02297997-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) forming the nucleus of a syllable, usually as a vowel or a consonant acting as a vowel.
forming the nucleus of a syllable (linguistics)

Examples

The syllabic 'n' in 'button' acts as the vowel of the final syllable.
Some English dialects use a syllabic 'm' sound at the end of certain words.
In 'table', the 'l' is not syllabic, unlike in 'bottle'.

Synset Examples

the syllabic ‘l’ in ‘riddle’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonsyllabic - (of speech sounds) not forming the nucleus of a syllable

Etymology

From Greek syllabikos (relating to a syllable), from syllabē (syllable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02296082-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ sɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
consisting of or relating to syllables; composed of or pronounced with distinct syllables.
relating to or consisting of syllables

Examples

Each syllabic character in the script represents a distinct syllable.
The choir performed the chant in a syllabic style, singing one note per syllable.
Some languages use syllabic writing systems rather than alphabetic ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to syllabify - to divide into syllables
  • Synonym: syllabic consonant - a consonant that forms a syllable on its own

Etymology

From Late Latin syllabicus, from Ancient Greek συλλαβικός (sullabikos), from συλλαβή (syllabē, 'syllable').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02296400-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ sɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
relating to or based on syllables, especially in language or writing systems.
relating to syllables

Examples

The Japanese writing system includes a syllabic script called kana.
Stress in the word 'banana' follows a syllabic pattern.
Each syllabic character stands for one spoken syllable.

Synset Examples

syllabic accent
syllabic characters each represent a syllable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syllabary - a set of written characters representing syllables
  • Synonym: syllable - a unit of pronunciation having one vowel sound

Etymology

From Latin syllabicus, from Greek syllabikos (‘of a syllable’).

5. ADJ

/ sɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
consisting of or relating to a writing system in which each symbol represents a syllable rather than an individual sound; using a syllabary.
consisting of or using a syllabary

Examples

The Cherokee language employs a syllabic writing system developed in the early 19th century.
Canadian Aboriginal scripts include syllabic characters that represent complete syllables.
In linguistics, a syllabic script differs from an alphabetic script, as each sign corresponds to a syllable.

Synset Examples

eskimos of the eastern Arctic have a system of syllabic writing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alphabetic - using or relating to an alphabet, where each symbol represents an individual sound

Etymology

From Greek syllabikos, from syllabē (syllable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02817388-a
