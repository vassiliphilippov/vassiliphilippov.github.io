Pyrrhuloxia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪr.əˈlɒk.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɪr.ʊˈlɒk.si.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large, showy North American finches closely related to cardinals, known for their distinctive crests and red coloring.
genus of large, showy finches

Examples

The Pyrrhuloxia is often mistaken for the northern cardinal due to its striking appearance.
Desert scrublands are the natural habitat of the Pyrrhuloxia in the southwestern United States.
Birdwatchers frequently travel to Texas to spot a Pyrrhuloxia in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal - a related genus of songbirds with bright red plumage

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Latin 'pyrrhula' (bullfinch) and 'Loxia' (a genus of crossbills).
