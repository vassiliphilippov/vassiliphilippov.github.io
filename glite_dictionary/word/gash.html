gash

1. VERB

/ ɡæʃ / (AmE), / ɡæʃ / (BrE)
to make a long, deep cut in something, especially accidentally
to make a deep cut

Examples

He accidentally gashed his hand while chopping vegetables.
The sharp rock gashed the side of the boat, causing it to take on water.
She gashed her knee when she fell off her bicycle.

Synset Examples

she slashed her wrists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slash - to cut with a wide, sweeping movement, often violently
  • Synonym: wound - to injure the body, especially by cutting or breaking the skin

2. NOUN

/ ɡæʃ / (AmE), / ɡæʃ / (BrE)
a deep, narrow cut or trench in a surface, especially one made by natural forces or excavation
deep cut or trench in a surface

Examples

A deep gash in the hillside revealed layers of ancient rock.
The earthquake left a massive gash running through the city park.
Years of mining created a gash in the once-untouched landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - an opening made by something sharp

3. NOUN

/ ɡæʃ / (AmE), / ɡæʃ / (BrE)
A long, deep cut or wound in the skin or flesh.
deep cut or wound

Examples

He suffered a deep gash on his arm when he fell off his bike.
The nurse cleaned the gash and wrapped it with a sterile bandage.
Blood trickled from the gash on his forehead after the accident.

Synset Examples

he put a bandage over the cut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - an opening or wound made by a sharp edge
  • Degree Lower: scratch - a slight or superficial wound or mark caused by scratching
  • Synonym: laceration - a deep cut or torn wound in flesh

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps related to the Old French "garser" (to cut or slash).

4. NOUN

/ ɡæʃ / (AmE), / ɡæʃ / (BrE)
A deep, long cut or wound made by a sharp instrument, typically with a sweeping motion.
a deep, long cut or wound

Examples

The jagged gash on his arm required several stitches at the hospital.
She accidentally made a deep gash in the wooden table with the knife.
A long gash ran across the side of the old boat, letting water seep in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - an opening or wound made by something sharp
  • Degree Lower: scratch - a slight or shallow cut on the skin
  • Synonym: slash - a strong, sweeping cutting movement or wound

Etymology

Possibly from Middle French 'gache' (a notch or cut), or related to 'gacher' (to spoil).
