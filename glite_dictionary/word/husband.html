husband

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌzbənd / (AmE), / ˈhʌzbənd / (BrE)
a married man, especially in relation to his wife
a married man; spouse

Examples

After ten years of marriage, Maria and her husband still enjoy traveling together.
She introduced her husband at the company dinner last night.
Her husband is supportive of her career ambitions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wife - a married woman, especially in relation to her spouse
  • Synonym: spouse - a person's husband or wife

Etymology

From Old English 'hūsbonda', from Old Norse 'húsbóndi' (master of a house).

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌzbənd / (AmE), / ˈhʌzbənd / (BrE)
to use resources, such as money or energy, carefully and sparingly; to conserve or manage frugally
to use resources sparingly

Examples

If we husband our resources now, we'll have enough for the winter.
She carefully husbanded her energy throughout the long competition.
It is important to husband water supplies during a drought.

Synset Examples

I try to economize my spare time
conserve your energy for the ascent to the summit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conserve - to protect from loss or waste; to use sparingly
  • Antonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly or to no purpose
  • Synonym: to economize - to use less of something; to save resources

Etymology

From Old Norse 'húsbóndi' (master of a house), with later use as a verb meaning 'to manage carefully.'
