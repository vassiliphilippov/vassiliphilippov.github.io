leavened

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛvənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛvənd / (BrE)
(of bread or dough) made light and porous by the addition of a leavening agent, such as yeast or baking powder.
made light by yeast or baking powder

Examples

The bakery specializes in leavened breads with a soft, airy texture.
Unlike unleavened flatbreads, leavened ones rise and become light during baking.
Traditional sourdough is a type of naturally leavened bread.

Synset Examples

leavened bread
well-leavened
yeast-leavened breads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unleavened - (of bread or dough) made without yeast or other leavening agent

Etymology

From 'leaven' (Old French 'levain', from Latin 'levamen', meaning 'lighten, raise').
