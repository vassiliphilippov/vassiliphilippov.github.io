blue-blindness

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / bluː ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
A rare form of color vision deficiency in which a person has difficulty distinguishing blue and yellow hues due to reduced sensitivity to blue light.
inability to distinguish blue and yellow
medical

Examples

Individuals with blue-blindness may confuse blue and yellow objects, affecting daily activities.
The diagnosis of blue-blindness is confirmed through specialized color vision tests.
He learned about blue-blindness when studying different types of color vision deficiencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - an inability to distinguish certain colors, typically red-green or blue-yellow

Etymology

Formed from 'blue' + 'blindness', indicating blindness to the color blue.
