cyprinid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪprɪnɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪprɪnɪd / (BrE)
A fish belonging to the family Cyprinidae, which typically has soft fins, toothless jaws, and cycloid scales; includes carps, minnows, and related species.
a fish of the carp family (Cyprinidae)

Examples

The lake is home to several species of cyprinid including carp and minnows.
Researchers studied the diet of a rare cyprinid found only in this river system.
Many popular aquarium fish are classified as cyprinids due to their hardy nature.

Etymology

From New Latin Cyprinidae, from Latin cyprinus (a carp).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪprɪnɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪprɪnɪd / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the family Cyprinidae, which includes many freshwater fish such as carps and minnows
relating to the fish family Cyprinidae

Examples

The cyprinid fishes are widespread in Asian rivers and lakes.
Scientists studied a rare cyprinid species found only in this region.
Many cyprinid species are popular in freshwater aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish belonging to the family Cyprinidae
  • Synonym: minnow - a small freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae

Etymology

From Latin *cyprinus* (carp), from Greek *kyprinos*, referring to a type of carp.
