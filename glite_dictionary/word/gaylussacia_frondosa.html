Gaylussacia frondosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˌlʌˈseɪʃə frɒnˈdoʊsə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˌlʌˈseɪsɪə frɒnˈdəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of huckleberry native to the eastern United States, noted for its pink flowers and sweet blue fruit.
species of eastern U.S. huckleberry

Examples

The woods behind our house are filled with Gaylussacia frondosa, making blueberry picking a summer tradition.
Botanists identified the shrub as Gaylussacia frondosa based on its pink blossoms and sweet blue berries.
Birds love to feed on the blue fruit of Gaylussacia frondosa during the late summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - an edible berry native to North America

Etymology

Named after the French chemist Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac; 'frondosa' is from Latin meaning 'leafy'.
