scoopful

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːpˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈskuːpˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to what a scoop can hold.
quantity held by a scoop

Examples

She added a scoopful of flour to the mixing bowl.
The recipe calls for two scoopfuls of ice cream.
With one scoopful of soil, the gardener filled the flowerpot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonful - the quantity a spoon can hold
  • Synonym: handful - the quantity held in a hand

Etymology

Formed from 'scoop' + 'ful', similar to 'spoonful' and 'handful'.
