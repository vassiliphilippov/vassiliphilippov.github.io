goethite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəːtʌɪt / (BrE)
a red, yellow, or brown mineral consisting of iron oxide, commonly found as a constituent of rust
iron oxide mineral; component of rust

Examples

The reddish layer on the rock was identified as goethite under the microscope.
Scientists collected samples of goethite from the old mine for analysis.
Rusty objects often contain goethite as part of their chemical composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limonite - a brownish iron ore consisting mainly of hydrated iron oxide; often contains goethite
  • Synonym: hematite - an iron ore; another common iron oxide mineral

Etymology

Named after the German writer Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749–1832).
