modesty

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdəsti / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəsti / (BrE)
the quality of not being too proud or boastful about oneself or one's abilities; freedom from vanity or conceit
lack of pride or boastfulness

Examples

Her modesty prevented her from taking credit for the team's success.
Despite his achievements, he always spoke with modesty.
People admired his modesty as much as his talent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogance - the quality of being overly proud or having an exaggerated sense of self-importance

Etymology

From Latin 'modestia' (moderation, modesty), from 'modestus' (moderate, keeping measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdəsti / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəsti / (BrE)
the quality of behaving, dressing, or speaking in a way that is considered proper, decent, and not intended to attract attention or show off.
the quality of being proper and unassuming

Examples

Her modesty prevented her from taking credit for the team's success.
A sense of modesty is valued in many traditional societies.
He accepted the award with great modesty and thanked everyone who helped him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogance - the quality of being proud and overbearing

Etymology

From Latin 'modestia' (moderation, sense of propriety), from 'modestus' (keeping measure).

Synset POS: n
