Quercus nuttalli

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜːrkəs nʌˈtælaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs nʌˈtalɪ / (BrE)
a species of oak tree, similar to the pin oak, native to damp sites in the Mississippi River basin
species of oak tree

Examples

The Quercus nuttalli thrives in wet, bottomland forests along the Mississippi River.
Botanists often compare the Quercus nuttalli to the closely related pin oak.
Many wildlife species depend on the acorns of Quercus nuttalli for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin oak - a species of oak tree similar in appearance to Quercus nuttalli

Etymology

From Latin Quercus (oak) + the species name nuttalli, honoring botanist Thomas Nuttall.
