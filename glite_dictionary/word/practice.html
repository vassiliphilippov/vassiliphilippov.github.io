practice

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
the business or work of a professional person such as a doctor or lawyer; the act of carrying out a profession
professional work or business (e.g. doctor or lawyer)

Examples

After years abroad, she returned home to open her own practice as a family doctor.
He decided to join an established law practice rather than start from scratch.
When Dr. Smith retired, his entire practice was passed on to his partner.

Synset Examples

the practice of the law
I took over his practice when he retired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one with specialized training

Etymology

From Old French *practiser*, from Medieval Latin *practicare*, from Greek *praktikos* (practical).
