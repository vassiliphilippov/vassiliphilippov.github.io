illustration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
an example, story, or demonstration that helps explain or clarify a point
explanatory example or demonstration

Examples

The teacher gave an illustration of the concept using a real-life situation.
His speech included an illustration of how teamwork leads to success.
Let me offer a simple illustration to make this point clearer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: example - a representative or typical instance used to illustrate a point
  • Synonym: demonstration - an act of showing or making something clear

Etymology

From Latin 'illustratio' (enlightenment, explanation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a picture, diagram, or other image used to explain or decorate something, especially in a book or article
visual explanation or decoration

Examples

The textbook includes an illustration of the human heart.
Each chapter begins with a colorful illustration that summarizes the main idea.
Her job is to create illustrations for children's books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something
  • Synonym: photograph - an image made using a camera

Etymology

From Latin illustratio (a making light or clear), from illustrare (to make bright) + -ion.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪl.əˈstreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪl.əˈstreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a drawing, picture, or diagram used to explain or decorate something, especially in a book or article
picture or diagram that explains or decorates

Examples

The textbook contains an illustration of the heart to help students understand its structure.
Each chapter begins with a colorful illustration to capture the reader's attention.
She created a detailed illustration to accompany her scientific article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance or structure of something
  • Degree Lower: photograph - a picture made using a camera
  • Degree Higher: painting - a picture made with paint, often artistic rather than explanatory

Etymology

From Latin illustratio (an enlightening), from illustrare (to make light or bright).
