vena centralis retinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪ.nə sɛnˈtreɪ.lɪs rɪˈtiːni / (AmE), / ˌveɪ.nə sɛnˈtrɑː.lɪs rɪˈtiːni / (BrE)
the central vein of the retina, formed by the union of the retinal veins and running alongside the central artery within the optic nerve
central vein of the retina
medical

Examples

A blockage in the vena centralis retinae can cause sudden vision loss in one eye.
The ophthalmologist examined the flow of blood through the vena centralis retinae during the procedure.
Damage to the vena centralis retinae is associated with certain types of retinal vein occlusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central retinal vein - the main vein draining blood from the retina
  • Antonym: central retinal artery - the main artery supplying blood to the retina

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'centralis' (central), 'retinae' (of the retina).
