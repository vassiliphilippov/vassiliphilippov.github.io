to protrude

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtruːd / (AmE), / prəˈtruːd / (BrE)
to stick out from a surface or beyond the usual limit
stick out; extend beyond

Examples

The corners of the metal sheet protrude dangerously from the wall.
His lower jaw protrudes slightly, giving him a distinctive look.
You could see a bone protruding through the cut in his leg.

Synset Examples

His stomach bulged after the huge meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: project - to extend outward beyond a surface
  • Antonym: to recede - to move back from a previous position; to retreat

Etymology

From Latin protrudere, from pro- (forward) + trudere (to thrust).

2. VERB

/ proʊˈtruːd / (AmE), / prəˈtruːd / (BrE)
to extend beyond or above a surface; to stick out
extend or stick out from a surface

Examples

A broken branch began to protrude from the thick hedge.
The mountain appeared to protrude sharply above the valley mist.
His bottom lip would protrude whenever he was upset.

Synset Examples

His sharp nose jutted out
A single rock sticks out from the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jut - to stick out, especially above or beyond the surface
  • Antonym: to recede - to move back or further away from a previous position

Etymology

From Latin protrudere, from pro- (forward) + trudere (to thrust).

3. VERB

/ proʊˈtruːd / (AmE), / prəˈtruːd / (BrE)
to stick out or extend beyond the surrounding surface
to stick out beyond a surface

Examples

Sharp rocks protrude from the surface of the cliff.
His lower jaw seemed to protrude slightly when he spoke.
A nail was protruding dangerously from the wooden board.

Synset Examples

His eyes popped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stick out - to be prominent or extend outward
  • Antonym: to recede - to move back or withdraw

Etymology

From Latin protrudere, from pro- (forward) + trudere (to thrust).
