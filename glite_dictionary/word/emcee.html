emcee

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈsi / (AmE), / ɛmˈsi / (BrE)
to act as the master of ceremonies at an event or performance
serve as master of ceremonies

Examples

She was invited to emcee the awards ceremony last night.
He has often emceed charity events in the city.
They asked me if I would emcee the conference this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - to act as the person organizing or presenting an event

Etymology

Representation of the initialism 'MC', from 'master of ceremonies'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02598553-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈsiː / (AmE), / ɛmˈsiː / (BrE)
a person who acts as a host at a formal event, introducing speakers and performers
host of a formal event

Examples

The emcee welcomed the guests and set the tone for the evening.
She served as the emcee at the charity gala, introducing each performer with enthusiasm.
Our company dinner was well-organized by a professional emcee who kept the schedule moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master of ceremonies - a person who hosts an event and introduces speakers

Etymology

Altering the pronunciation of the abbreviation 'MC', from 'Master of Ceremonies'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10318917-n
Synset POS: n
