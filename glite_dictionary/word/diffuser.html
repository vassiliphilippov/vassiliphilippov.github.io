diffuser

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuzər / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzə / (BrE)
a device or structure that spreads sound waves evenly in a space, reducing echoes and improving acoustic quality
device that distributes sound waves evenly

Examples

The recording studio installed a diffuser on the back wall to improve the sound quality.
A well-placed diffuser can help eliminate echoes in an auditorium.
Acoustic engineers use a diffuser to distribute sound evenly throughout the space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baffle - a device used to control or direct the flow of sound or light
  • Antonym: absorber - a device that absorbs sound waves, reducing reverberation

Etymology

From Latin diffundere (to spread out), via French diffuseur.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuːzər / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzə / (BrE)
An optical device that disperses the light from a lamp or source so it is spread evenly.
device for spreading light evenly

Examples

The photographer positioned a diffuser in front of the lamp to soften the shadows.
Modern office lights often include a diffuser to prevent harsh glare.
We installed a new ceiling light with a frosted diffuser for more even illumination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatterer - device or material that scatters light or other energy

Etymology

From Latin 'diffundere' meaning 'to pour out', via French 'diffuser'.
