lazy

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪzi / (AmE), / ˈleɪzi / (BrE)
Unwilling to work or use energy; disinclined to activity or effort.
unwilling to work or make effort

Examples

He was too lazy to get out of bed on Saturday morning.
Some students become lazy about completing their assignments.
Don't be lazy; help us clean up after dinner.

Synset Examples

faineant kings under whose rule the country languished
an indolent hanger-on
too lazy to wash the dishes
slothful employees
the unemployed are not necessarily work-shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolent - habitually lazy or avoiding activity; synonym of 'lazy'
  • Antonym: diligent - showing care and effort in work; antonym of 'lazy'
  • Synonym: slothful - lazy, disinclined to effort; synonym of 'lazy'

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *lasich* (slack, feeble); related to English 'lax'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪzi / (AmE), / ˈleɪzi / (BrE)
moving slowly and gently; lacking briskness or energy in movement or progression
moving slowly and gently

Examples

We spent the afternoon drifting down the lazy river under the sun.
Fluffy, lazy clouds floated slowly across the summer sky.
The hikers proceeded at a lazy pace, savoring the scenery.

Synset Examples

up a lazy river
lazy white clouds
at a lazy pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargic - sluggish or lacking energy
  • Antonym: energetic - having or showing a lot of energy and movement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00984495-s
Synset POS: s
