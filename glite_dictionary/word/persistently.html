persistently

1. ADV

/ pərˈsɪstəntli / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstəntli / (BrE)
In a way that continues firmly or obstinately, especially despite difficulty or opposition.
in a persistent manner

Examples

Despite repeated setbacks, she worked persistently toward her goal.
The rain fell persistently throughout the night, soaking the fields.
He persistently asked questions until he found the solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistently - in a persistent manner
  • Antonym: occasionally - not often; at irregular or infrequent intervals

Etymology

From persistent + -ly, where persistent is from Latin 'persistens' (continuing steadfastly).

2. ADV

/ pərˈsɪstəntli / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstəntli / (BrE)
In a way that continues firmly and steadily, especially despite difficulty or opposition.
in a persistent way

Examples

She persistently called his office until she finally reached him.
The rain fell persistently throughout the entire week, causing widespread flooding.
He persistently pursued his dream of becoming an artist despite many setbacks.

Synset Examples

he was asking questions, unavoidable questions, persistently …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadily - in a regular, continuous way without interruption
  • Synonym: doggedly - in a manner that shows grim persistence

Etymology

From 'persistent' + '-ly'. 'Persistent' from Latin 'persistens', present participle of 'persistere' (to persevere).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
