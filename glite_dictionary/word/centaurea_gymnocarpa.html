Centaurea gymnocarpa

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə dʒɪmnəˈkɑrpə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə dʒɪmnəˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A perennial plant species with leaves and stems covered in fine down, giving a dusty appearance, native to certain Mediterranean islands.
a plant with dusty-looking downy leaves and stems

Examples

The botanical garden is home to a rare Centaurea gymnocarpa specimen, recognizable by its grayish, downy leaves.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered Centaurea gymnocarpa on its native island habitats.
She photographed the silvery foliage of Centaurea gymnocarpa during her trek along the Mediterranean cliffs.

Etymology

From the genus name Centaurea (from Latin, named after the centaur Chiron) and gymnocarpa (from Greek 'gymno-' meaning naked and '-carpa' meaning fruit).
