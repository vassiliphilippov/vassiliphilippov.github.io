Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzəˌvɛlt / (BrE)
Eleanor Roosevelt (1884–1962), wife of U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt and a noted advocate for human rights.
Eleanor Roosevelt, U.S. human rights advocate

Examples

Roosevelt was known for her tireless efforts to advance civil rights and women’s rights.
Many historians regard Roosevelt as one of the most influential First Ladies in American history.
The United Nations recognized Roosevelt's contributions to the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Franklin D. Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States, husband of Eleanor Roosevelt

Etymology

Surname of Dutch origin; Eleanor Roosevelt's family name.

2. PROPN

/ ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt, ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzəˌvɛlt, ˈrəʊzəˌvɛlt / (BrE)
Theodore Roosevelt (1858–1919), the 26th President of the United States, known for his progressive reforms, leadership during the Spanish-American War, and the construction of the Panama Canal.
26th President of the United States (Theodore Roosevelt)

Examples

Roosevelt was known for his vigorous approach to politics and reform.
The national park system expanded greatly under Roosevelt's leadership.
Many historians consider Roosevelt one of the most influential U.S. presidents.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Franklin D. Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States, Theodore Roosevelt's distant cousin

3. PROPN

/ ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈruːzəvɛlt / (BrE)
(Franklin D.) the 32nd President of the United States (1933–1945), known for the New Deal policies and leadership during the Great Depression and World War II.
32nd U.S. President; New Deal leader

Examples

Roosevelt led the United States through the Great Depression and most of World War II.
The New Deal was an ambitious set of programs introduced by Roosevelt to help America recover economically.
Many historians regard Roosevelt as one of the most influential presidents in U.S. history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: FDR - Abbreviation for Franklin Delano Roosevelt, 32nd U.S. President
  • Degree Higher: Truman - 33rd President of the United States, succeeded Roosevelt
  • Degree Lower: Hoover - 31st President of the United States, preceded Roosevelt
