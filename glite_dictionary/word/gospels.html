Gospels

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑspəlz / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəlz / (BrE)
the four books in the New Testament (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) that recount the life and teachings of Jesus Christ
the four books about Jesus in the New Testament

Examples

Many people find inspiration in the stories told in the Gospels.
Biblical scholars often compare the accounts in the four Gospels.
She studied passages from the Gospels during her theology course.

Etymology

From Old English *gōdspel* (‘good news’), later used for the biblical books.
