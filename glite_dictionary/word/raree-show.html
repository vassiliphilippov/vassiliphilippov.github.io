raree-show

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛri ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈreəri ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
A kind of entertainment in which pictures or small objects are exhibited for viewing through a small hole or magnifying glass, often in a portable box.
historical peephole exhibition
archaic

Examples

The traveling entertainer set up his raree-show at the busy marketplace, attracting curious children and adults alike.
For a small fee, visitors could peer into the raree-show and marvel at the colorful images inside.
In Victorian times, a raree-show was a popular way for people to catch glimpses of distant lands and wonders.

Etymology

From 'rare' (marvel, curiosity) + 'show', dating from 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛri ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛəri ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
a street entertainment in which pictures or scenes are exhibited, often through a peephole or small viewing area; a peep show, especially as given by itinerant showmen in past centuries
street peep show or exhibition
archaic

Examples

Children gathered around the colorful raree-show as the showman turned the crank to reveal new pictures.
In eighteenth-century London, a raree-show was a popular form of street entertainment for people of all ages.
Historical novels sometimes describe a raree-show traveling from village to village, delighting crowds with curious sights.

Etymology

Obsolete form of 'rarity show', from 'rare' (unusual) + 'show', originally referring to exhibitions of rare or curious things.
