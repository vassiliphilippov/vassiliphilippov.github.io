galvaniser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɡalvənʌɪzə / (BrE)
a skilled worker who coats iron or steel with a layer of zinc to prevent rusting
worker who zinc-coats metal

Examples

The galvaniser applied a protective layer of zinc to the steel beams in the factory.
As a certified galvaniser, she ensures that the metal components resist corrosion for many years.
Modern manufacturing relies on the skills of a galvaniser to produce durable building materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanizer - variant American spelling for the same occupation

Etymology

From galvanize + -er, with galvanize from Italian Galvani (surname), after Luigi Galvani.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæv.lə.naɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who stimulates and excites others into taking action or becoming more active
one who motivates others to act

Examples

As a true galvaniser, she transformed her team’s attitude and turned the project around.
His speech was so powerful that he earned the reputation of being a natural galvaniser.
Every community needs a galvaniser who can inspire positive change among its members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivator - a person who provides others with a reason or incentive to act
  • Synonym: inspirer - a person who inspires others
  • Antonym: demotivator - a person who discourages others from action

Etymology

From French *galvaniser* (to stimulate, excite by electricity), from Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist.
