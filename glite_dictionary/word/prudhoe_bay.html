Prudhoe Bay

1. PROPN

/ ˈpruːd.hoʊ beɪ / (AmE), / ˈprʊd.həʊ beɪ / (BrE)
a bay on the northern coast of Alaska, notable for its large oil field discovered in 1968
bay in Alaska known for oil discovery

Examples

The discovery of oil at Prudhoe Bay transformed Alaska's economy in the late twentieth century.
Engineers worked through harsh Arctic conditions to build the pipeline from Prudhoe Bay to Valdez.
Tourists rarely visit Prudhoe Bay due to its remote location and industrial landscape.
