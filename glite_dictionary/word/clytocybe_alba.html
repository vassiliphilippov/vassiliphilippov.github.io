Clytocybe alba

1. NOUN

/ klaɪˈtɑsəbi ˈælbə / (AmE), / klaɪˈtɒsɪbi ˈalbə / (BrE)
A large, white species of mushroom in the genus Clytocybe; edible but not considered palatable.
a large white mushroom species

Examples

The forager picked a Clytocybe alba but decided not to eat it due to its bland taste.
Although Clytocybe alba is technically edible, experts warn that it lacks any distinct flavor.
In the damp forest, clusters of Clytocybe alba could be seen among the fallen leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'klytos' (spoken of, famous) and '-cybe' (head), and Latin 'alba' (white).
