Asplenium montanum

1. NOUN

/ æsˈpliːniəm mɒnˈteɪnəm / (AmE), / asˈpliːniəm mɒnˈteɪnəm / (BrE)
a species of spleenwort fern native to eastern North America, known for its small, evergreen fronds
a spleenwort fern of eastern North America

Examples

The shady slopes of the Appalachian Mountains are home to many ferns, including Asplenium montanum.
Botanists identified the rare Asplenium montanum growing among the rocks in the forest preserve.
Asplenium montanum can be distinguished from other ferns by its unique, narrowly triangular fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spleenwort - any fern of the genus Asplenium

Etymology

From Latin: 'Asplenium' (a genus of ferns), 'montanum' meaning 'of the mountains'.
