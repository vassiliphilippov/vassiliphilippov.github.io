vegetal

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛdʒ.ɪ.təl / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒ.ɪ.təl / (BrE)
composed of or relating to vegetation or plants; characteristic of plants.
relating to vegetation or plants

Examples

The rainforest is known for its dense vegetal cover and biodiversity.
Scientists study the vegetal layers of soil to understand ecosystem changes.
The salad had a fresh, almost vegetal flavor, reminiscent of raw greens.

Synset Examples

regions rich in vegetal products
vegetational cover
the decaying vegetative layer covering a forest floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetative - relating to or characteristic of vegetation or plants

Etymology

From Latin 'vegetalis' (animating, enlivening).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛdʒɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒɪtəl / (BrE)
(of reproduction) characterized by asexual processes, especially as in some plants and lower animals.
asexual reproduction (botany, biology)

Examples

Some algae undergo vegetal reproduction, bypassing the need for fertilization.
Vegetal propagation allows gardeners to clone plants from cuttings rather than seeds.
Yeast cells can divide through vegetal reproduction, creating genetically identical offspring.

Etymology

From Latin 'vegetalis', from 'vegetare' (to enliven, quicken), related to 'vegeo' (to be lively).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02144077-s
Synset POS: s
