supervisor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpərˌvaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəvaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who oversees the work or tasks of others, ensuring that standards and objectives are met
person who oversees others' work

Examples

The supervisor walked around the factory floor to check on the workers' progress.
If you have any problems with your assignment, speak to your supervisor for guidance.
Each team has a supervisor who is responsible for maintaining safety protocols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person who directs and controls a business or department
  • Antonym: subordinate - person under the authority of another

Etymology

From supervise + -or, influenced by Medieval Latin 'supervisor' (overseer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌvaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌvaɪ.zə / (BrE)
a program in a computer system that controls the execution of other programs, typically managing resources and enforcing rules
program controlling other programs (computing)

Examples

The supervisor ensures that each user's process does not interfere with others on the system.
When the system boots, the supervisor is loaded first to coordinate program execution.
A fault in the supervisor can lead to failures in multiple applications running on the computer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operating system - main software controlling a computer and its resources
