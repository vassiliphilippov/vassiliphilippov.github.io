to inseminate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɛmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to introduce semen into the reproductive system of a female, typically to achieve fertilization
to introduce semen for fertilization
medical

Examples

The veterinarian will inseminate the cows to improve their genetic stock.
Artificial methods can be used to inseminate women experiencing infertility.
Farmers often decide when to inseminate their livestock to optimize birth timing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to cause an egg to develop by joining with a male cell

Etymology

From Latin inseminare, from in- (into) + semen (seed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00052265-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɛm.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛm.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to place seeds in or on (the ground) for the purpose of growth; to sow
place seeds in/on the ground

Examples

The farmer carefully inseminated the field with new wheat seeds for the coming season.
Some gardeners prefer to inseminate their flower beds by hand to control plant spacing.
In April, the volunteers inseminated the barren hillside with native wildflower seeds.

Synset Examples

sow the ground with sunflower seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow - to plant seeds in the ground

Etymology

From Latin 'inseminare', meaning 'to sow seed', from 'in-' (in) + 'semen, seminis' (seed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503986-v
Synset POS: v
