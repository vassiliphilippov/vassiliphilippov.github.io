elastic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
A fabric made of threads containing elastic material, used especially for making items that need to stretch.
stretchable fabric containing elastic

Examples

He sewed a piece of elastic into the waistband of his trousers.
The mask stays on your face with a strip of elastic behind your head.
You can buy elastic at any fabric or craft store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elastic band - a loop of elastic fabric or rubber

Etymology

From Greek elastos (ductile, flexible), via French élastique.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
a narrow band of elastic rubber, often used to hold things such as papers together or keep items in place
rubber band for holding things together

Examples

She used an elastic to bundle all the loose pencils together.
The stack of letters was held by a red elastic.
Do you have an elastic I can borrow for these documents?

Etymology

From Greek elaastos (driven, beaten out), via French élastique.

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
able to adapt easily to different situations or conditions; flexible and responsive to change
adaptable; able to adjust easily

Examples

The company has an elastic policy that allows employees to work from home or in the office.
Her elastic approach to problem-solving made her a valuable team member.
The contract includes an elastic clause that permits modifications if circumstances change.

Synset Examples

a flexible personality
an elastic clause in a contract
a pliable person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - able to change or adapt easily to different situations
  • Antonym: rigid - unable or unwilling to change; inflexible

Etymology

From Greek 'elastikos' (ductile), via Latin and French.

4. ADJ

/ ɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
(of a material or object) able to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed; springy.
able to resume original shape after stretching

Examples

This fabric is highly elastic and perfect for sportswear.
The gymnast's elastic movements impressed the judges.
Rubber bands are known for being elastic, making them useful for many tasks.

Synset Examples

an elastic band
a youthful and elastic walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resilient - (of a substance or object) able to spring back into shape; similar meaning
  • Antonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; opposite meaning

Etymology

From Greek 'elastos' (ductile), from 'elaunein' (to drive).
