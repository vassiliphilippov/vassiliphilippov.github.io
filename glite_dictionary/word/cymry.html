Cymry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmri / (AmE), / ˈkəmri / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Wales; the Welsh people collectively
a native or inhabitant of Wales

Examples

The Cymry have a rich cultural heritage dating back thousands of years.
Many Cymry still speak Welsh as their first language.
The traditions of the Cymry are celebrated in music, poetry, and festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welshman - a man from Wales
  • Synonym: Welshwoman - a woman from Wales

Etymology

From Welsh 'Cymry' (the Welsh people), plural of 'Cymro' (a Welshman), related to 'Cymru' (Wales).
