ribbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪbɪŋ / (BrE)
a framework or arrangement of ribs, especially as part of a structure such as a building or ship
framework of ribs in a structure

Examples

The vaulted ceiling was supported by a complex ribbing system.
The architect designed the bridge with reinforced steel ribbing for greater stability.
During restoration, workers discovered the original wooden ribbing beneath the plaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rib - a long curved bone or structural member

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪbɪŋ / (BrE)
good-natured teasing or mockery, often intended to amuse or provoke without causing offense
good-natured teasing or mockery

Examples

The group engaged in some friendly ribbing before the meeting started.
He took the constant ribbing from his friends in good humor.
Despite all the ribbing about his new haircut, he didn't seem bothered.

Synset Examples

he ignored their teases
his ribbing was gentle but persistent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasing - the act of making fun of someone in a playful way
  • Synonym: mockery - the act of ridiculing or making fun of someone
  • Antonym: bullying - the act of intimidating or mistreating someone repeatedly

Etymology

From the verb 'rib' (to tease), possibly from the idea of poking someone in the ribs to provoke laughter.
