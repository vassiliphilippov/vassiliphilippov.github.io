Dacrycarpus dacrydioides

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækriˈkɑːrpəs ˌdækriˌdiˈoʊɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˈkɑːpəs ˌdakrɪdɪˈəʊɪdiːz / (BrE)
A large evergreen coniferous tree native to New Zealand, valued for its light and soft wood, commonly known as kahikatea.
New Zealand evergreen conifer (kahikatea)

Examples

The towering Dacrycarpus dacrydioides forests once dominated New Zealand's lowland swamps.
Furniture makers prize Dacrycarpus dacrydioides for its lightweight and workable timber.
We saw a magnificent Dacrycarpus dacrydioides standing by the river during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kahikatea - common name for Dacrycarpus dacrydioides

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name from Greek 'dakryon' (tear) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the juicy aril; species from Latinized form of the related genus Dacrydium.
