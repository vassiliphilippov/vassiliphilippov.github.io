to legislate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪt / (BrE)
to make or enact laws, or to bring something into effect by making laws
make or enact laws

Examples

The government plans to legislate new safety standards for all workplaces.
It can be difficult to legislate changes in social behavior.
Congress may legislate stricter regulations on environmental pollution.

Synset Examples

They passed the amendment
We cannot legislate how people spend their free time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enact - to make (a bill or proposal) into law

Etymology

From Latin 'legis lat-' ('law' + 'carried, brought'), from 'lex' (law) and 'ferre' (to bring).
