Tamarix

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæm.əˌrɪks / (AmE), / ˈtam.ər.ɪks / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees native to eastern Mediterranean regions and tropical Asia, known for their slender branches and small scale-like leaves.
genus of deciduous shrubs or trees

Examples

The Tamarix genus includes several species well adapted to arid environments.
Conservationists are studying the impact of Tamarix on local river ecosystems.
Botanists planted Tamarix to stabilize the soil in coastal regions.

Etymology

From Latin Tamarix, the classical name for the shrub.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæmərɪks / (AmE), / ˈtæmərɪks / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees native to the eastern Mediterranean and tropical Asia, often grown for ornamental purposes or for soil stabilization
genus of shrubs or small trees (Tamarix)

Examples

Several species in the Tamarix genus are known for their ability to tolerate saline soils.
The Tamarix was introduced in some regions to help control soil erosion along riverbanks.
Botanists studied the flowering patterns of Tamarix throughout the spring and summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarisk - common name for shrubs or trees of the genus Tamarix

Etymology

From Latin Tamarix, the genus name, possibly from an ancient Iberian place name.
