lough

1. NOUN

/ lɑk / (AmE), / lɒx / (BrE)
An Irish or Scottish term for a lake, especially as used in place names.
an Irish/Scottish term for a lake

Examples

We enjoyed a picnic by the tranquil lough during our holiday in Ireland.
The famous lough attracts thousands of visitors every year for boating and fishing.
Mist rose over the surface of the lough as dawn broke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake - a large body of water surrounded by land

Etymology

From Irish Gaelic 'loch' (lake), with influence from Old Irish.

2. NOUN

/ lɒx / (AmE), / lɒx / (BrE)
A long, narrow, almost landlocked body of water, particularly one situated in Ireland, such as a cove or inlet.
Irish cove or inlet; narrow lake

Examples

We took a boat trip across the tranquil lough during our holiday in Ireland.
Many birds nest along the shores of the lough every spring.
The small fishing village is located at the edge of a scenic lough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loch - A lake or sea inlet, especially in Scotland

Etymology

From Irish *loch* or Scottish Gaelic *loch*, meaning lake or sea inlet.
