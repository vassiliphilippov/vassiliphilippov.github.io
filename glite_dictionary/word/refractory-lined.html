refractory-lined

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfræktɛri laɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktəri laɪnd / (BrE)
(of furnaces or containers) having an inner lining made of a material with a very high melting point, designed to withstand intense heat.
having a high-melting-point lining

Examples

The steel plant uses a refractory-lined furnace to process molten metal safely.
Old kilns that are not refractory-lined often suffer severe damage from high temperatures.
A typical gasifier is refractory-lined to prevent heat loss and protect the steel walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refractory - a material resistant to heat, used in furnace linings

Etymology

From 'refractory' (Latin 'refractarius', stubborn) + 'lined', referring to being fitted with an internal layer.
