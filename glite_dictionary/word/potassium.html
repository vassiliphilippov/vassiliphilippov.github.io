potassium

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtæsiəm / (AmE), / pəˈtasiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic element of the alkali metal group that reacts vigorously with water and is found widely in nature, especially in minerals and sea water
a metallic chemical element (K), alkali metal

Examples

Bananas are a good source of potassium, which is essential for muscle function.
The laboratory stores potassium under oil because it reacts explosively with water.
Doctors may recommend foods high in potassium to help regulate blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium - a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive metal in the alkali metal group

Etymology

From New Latin 'potassium', from 'potash', influenced by Latin 'kalium' (K).
