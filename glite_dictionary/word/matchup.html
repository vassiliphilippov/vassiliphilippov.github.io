matchup

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈmætʃʌp / (BrE)
an instance or act of pairing people or things, especially for comparison or competition
pairing for comparison or competition
american

Examples

The championship matchup drew a large audience from all over the city.
Analysts are already discussing next week’s exciting matchup between the two rival teams.
There’s a good matchup between her experience and the requirements of the job.

Synset Examples

it was a good match-up but the home team won
we need a matchup of the best teachers with the neediest schools

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - contest or rivalry between individuals or groups
  • Synonym: contest - an event in which people compete for supremacy

Etymology

Formed from 'match' + 'up' in American English, mid-20th century.
