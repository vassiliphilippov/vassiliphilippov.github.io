Centropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.troʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.trə.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the cuckoo family, known as coucals, found in the Old World tropics and subtropics
genus of birds (coucals) in the cuckoo family

Examples

The Centropus genus includes several species of large, ground-dwelling birds.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of a rare Centropus in Southeast Asia.
Farmers often spot a Centropus moving through tall grass in search of insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a bird of the Cuculidae family, known for laying eggs in other birds' nests

Etymology

From Greek kentron (spur) + pous (foot), used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛnˈtroʊpəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛnˈtrəʊpəs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the cuckoo family (Cuculidae), commonly known as coucals, found in Africa, Asia, and Australia
genus of birds called coucals

Examples

The black coucal, a member of the genus Centropus, is native to sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers observed several Centropus species in the wetlands during their field study.
The Centropus genus is distinguished by its robust legs and skulking habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - any of various birds of the family Cuculidae

Etymology

From Greek 'kentron' (spur) and 'pous' (foot), referring to the bird's strong toes.
