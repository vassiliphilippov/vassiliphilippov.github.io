pell-mell

1. ADV

/ pɛlˈmɛl / (AmE), / pɛlˈmɛl / (BrE)
In a confused, hurried, or reckless way; without order or control.
in a wild or reckless manner

Examples

As the fire alarm rang, students rushed pell-mell out of the building.
The dogs ran pell-mell across the field, barking excitedly.
Papers flew pell-mell as the wind swept through the open window.

Synset Examples

dashing harum-scarum all over the place
running pell-mell up the stairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helter-skelter - in a disorderly or chaotic manner

Etymology

From French 'pêle-mêle', meaning mixed haphazardly.

2. ADV

/ ˌpɛlˈmɛl / (AmE), / ˌpelˈmel / (BrE)
in a disorderly, hurried, and confused manner
in a rushed, confused way

Examples

The crowd fled pell-mell when the alarm was raised.
They left the building pell-mell after the fire alarm sounded.
Clothes and books were scattered pell-mell across the floor.

Synset Examples

a helter-skelter kind of existence with never a pause
a pell-mell dash for the train

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helter-skelter - in a confused, disorderly, or hurried manner
  • Antonym: orderly - in a neat, systematic, or controlled way

Etymology

From French 'pêle-mêle', perhaps from Old French 'mesler' (to mix) and 'pêle', probably an echoic word indicating disorder.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01274520-s
Synset POS: s
