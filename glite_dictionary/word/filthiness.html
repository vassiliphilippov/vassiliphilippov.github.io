filthiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlθinəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪlθinɪs / (BrE)
the state of being extremely dirty or foul, often due to the presence of dirt, refuse, or other unsanitary conditions
extreme dirtiness or foulness

Examples

The filthiness of the abandoned house made it unfit for living.
Despite the filthiness of the river, some children still played near its banks.
She was shocked by the filthiness of the public restroom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanliness - the quality of being clean and free from dirt

Etymology

From Middle English *filthinesse*, from 'filthy' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlθinəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪlθinɪs / (BrE)
the state of being morally corrupt or polluted; moral impurity
moral corruption or impurity

Examples

The preacher warned of the filthiness that can result from a life of dishonesty.
She was troubled by the moral filthiness depicted in the novel.
Many ancient texts caution against the filthiness of the soul.

Synset Examples

this deformity and filthiness of sin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purity - the state of being morally clean or free from sin

Etymology

From Old English 'fylþ' (filth) + '-ness' (noun suffix).
