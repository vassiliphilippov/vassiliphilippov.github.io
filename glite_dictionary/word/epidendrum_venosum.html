Epidendrum venosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm viˈnoʊsəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm vɪˈnəʊsəm / (BrE)
A Mexican epiphytic orchid with pale green or yellow-green flowers and a white, purple-veined lip.
Mexican epiphytic orchid species

Examples

The greenhouse proudly displays its rare Epidendrum venosum in full bloom each spring.
Botanists classify the Epidendrum venosum as native to Mexico’s higher elevations.
The pale green blossoms of Epidendrum venosum make it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Latin 'Epidendrum' (a genus of orchids) and Latin 'venosum' (veined).
