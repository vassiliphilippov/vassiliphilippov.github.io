guaiacum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡwaɪ.ə.kəm / (AmE), / ˈɡwaɪ.ə.kəm / (BrE)
A medicinal resin obtained from the wood of the lignum vitae tree, formerly used in medicine, or the tree itself.
medicinal resin or tree (lignum vitae)
medical

Examples

The bark and resin of guaiacum were once used as remedies for a variety of ailments.
Early physicians believed that guaiacum could cure syphilis and other diseases.
Harvesting guaiacum contributed to the tree becoming endangered in some regions.

Etymology

From Spanish 'guayaco', from Taíno 'guayacán' (the tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡwaɪ.ə.kəm / (AmE), / ˈɡwaɪ.ək.əm / (BrE)
the hard, greenish-brown wood obtained from the lignum vitae tree and other trees of the genus Guaiacum, valued for its density and strength
hard wood from the lignum vitae tree

Examples

The craftsman used guaiacum to make durable tool handles and mallets.
Because of its density, guaiacum is ideal for ship propeller bearings.
Furniture made from guaiacum is known to last for generations due to the wood's strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lignum vitae - very hard wood from trees of the genus Guaiacum

Etymology

From Latin 'guaiacum', via Spanish 'guayaco', originally from Taíno, the indigenous language of the Caribbean.
