sulfamethazine

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌlfəˈmɛθəˌziːn / (AmE), / ˌsʌlfəˈmiːθəˌziːn / (BrE)
a sulfa drug used to treat bacterial infections in humans and animals, particularly in veterinary medicine
a sulfa drug for bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The veterinarian prescribed sulfamethazine to treat the cattle's respiratory infection.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to sulfamethazine, making treatment more challenging.
Regulations restrict the use of sulfamethazine in animals intended for human consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfadiazine - a related sulfa drug used to treat bacterial infections

Etymology

Formed from sulfa- (from sulfanilamide) and methazine (derived from its chemical structure).
