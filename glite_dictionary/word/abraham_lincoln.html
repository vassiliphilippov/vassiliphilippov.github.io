Abraham Lincoln

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪbrəˌhæm ˈlɪŋkən / (AmE), / ˌeɪbrəˌhæm ˈlɪŋkən / (BrE)
(1809–1865) The 16th President of the United States, noted for leading the country during the Civil War and abolishing slavery.
16th U.S. President (1809–1865)

Examples

Abraham Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address in 1863, one of the most famous speeches in American history.
Many historians agree that Abraham Lincoln played a crucial role in ending slavery in the United States.
The assassination of Abraham Lincoln marked a tragic moment in American history.
