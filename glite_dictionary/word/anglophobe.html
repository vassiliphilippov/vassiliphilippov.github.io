anglophobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋ.ɡləˌfoʊb / (AmE), / ˈaŋ.ɡlə.fəʊb / (BrE)
A person who dislikes or has a strong prejudice against England or English people and things.
someone who dislikes England or the English

Examples

His grandfather was known to be an outspoken anglophobe throughout his life.
It's unfair to label someone an anglophobe just because they dislike British cuisine.
She wrote a book documenting the rise of anglophobes during the colonial era.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anglophile - a person who admires England or English things

Etymology

From French 'anglophobe', from 'anglo-' (England) + '-phobe' (one who fears or dislikes).
