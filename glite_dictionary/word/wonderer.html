wonderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌndɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʌndərə / (BrE)
a person who feels or expresses wonder, admiration, or awe, especially in response to something remarkable
a person filled with wonder or admiration

Examples

She looked at the night sky like a true wonderer, amazed by the stars.
Every wonderer finds magic in the ordinary moments of life.
The museum was filled with wonderers captivated by the ancient artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - someone who regards something with respect or warm approval

Etymology

Formed from 'wonder' (Old English wundor) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʌndərər / (AmE), / ˈwʌndərə / (BrE)
a person who is curious or who marvels at things they do not understand
a person who wonders or is curious

Examples

The story was told by a wonderer who never stopped questioning the mysteries of the world.
As a child, she was a constant wonderer, always fascinated by how things worked.
Every great scientist begins as a wonderer, full of curiosity and awe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquirer - a person who asks questions or seeks information
  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to question or doubt

Etymology

From 'wonder' + agent suffix '-er', in analogy with similar formations like 'thinker'.
