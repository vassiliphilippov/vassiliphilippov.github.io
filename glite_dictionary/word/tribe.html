tribe

1. NOUN

/ traɪb / (AmE), / traɪb / (BrE)
a social group consisting of people of the same race, language, and customs, especially one that is larger than a family but smaller than a nation, and often led by a chief
social group sharing ancestry and culture

Examples

Many indigenous peoples were organized into tribes with distinct languages and customs.
The Cherokee are one of the best-known Native American tribes in the United States.
Anthropologists studied the social structure of the desert tribe for many years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of close-knit and interrelated families

Etymology

From Latin 'tribus' (a division of the Roman people).

2. NOUN

/ traɪb / (AmE), / traɪb / (BrE)
A social group, especially among Indigenous peoples of North America, consisting of families or communities linked by social, economic, religious, or blood ties and usually sharing a common culture and language.
social group or federation, especially of Indigenous peoples

Examples

The Cherokee tribe is one of the most well-known Native American groups.
Each tribe had its own unique traditions and government.
Several families came together to form a new tribe in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of close-knit and interrelated families
  • Degree Higher: nation - a large group of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language

Etymology

From Latin tribus (a division of the Roman people).

3. NOUN

/ traɪb / (AmE), / traɪb / (BrE)
a social group consisting of people who are related by blood or marriage, typically sharing customs, language, and ancestry
a social group related by blood or marriage

Examples

The indigenous tribe preserves its traditions through music and storytelling.
Several families in the valley consider themselves members of the same tribe.
Marriage outside of the tribe was once forbidden by their customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of families with a common ancestor
  • Degree Lower: family - a group of people related by blood or marriage
  • Degree Higher: nation - a large group of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language

Etymology

From Latin tribus (one of the three divisions of the Roman people).

4. NOUN

/ traɪb / (AmE), / traɪb / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic rank between a genus and a subfamily, used in the classification of organisms.
biological taxonomic rank between genus and subfamily
medical

Examples

The rose family includes a tribe called the Rosaceae, which contains over 100 species.
Some taxonomists debate whether certain genera should be grouped within the same tribe.
In beetles, the tribe Cychrini is distinguished by their unique mandibles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: genus - a taxonomic category ranking below family and above species
  • Degree Higher: subfamily - a taxonomic category ranking above tribe and below family

/ traɪb / (AmE), / traɪb / (BrE)
a group of people who share similar interests, values, or ways of life, especially used to describe a close-knit community or group
group sharing interests or values

Examples

The online gaming world introduced her to a tribe of like-minded players.
After college, I struggled to find my tribe in a new city.
Artists often seek out a tribe where they feel understood and inspired.

Related Terms
