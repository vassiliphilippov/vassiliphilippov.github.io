asafetida

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈfɛtɪdə / (AmE), / ˌasəˈfiːtɪdə / (BrE)
A brownish, strong-smelling gum resin obtained from certain plants, used as a spice in cooking and formerly in medicine.
strong-smelling gum resin used as a spice

Examples

A pinch of asafetida adds a unique flavor to Indian curries.
Traditional remedies sometimes include asafetida for digestive issues.
The chef advised using asafetida sparingly due to its pungent aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hing - Indian English word for asafetida, used as spice

Etymology

From Persian 'azā fētīdah', meaning 'fetid resin'.
