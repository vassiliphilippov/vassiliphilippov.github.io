unsynchronized

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɪŋkrənaɪzd / (BrE)
not happening, moving, or operating at the same time as something else; not synchronized.
not happening at the same time

Examples

The unsynchronized clocks in the office always confused new employees.
Their movements were unsynchronized, making the dance performance look messy.
Because the two servers were unsynchronized, the data was often inconsistent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronized - occurring at the same time; made to happen or operate together

Etymology

From un- (not) + synchronized, from Greek syn- (together) + chronos (time) via French/Latin.
