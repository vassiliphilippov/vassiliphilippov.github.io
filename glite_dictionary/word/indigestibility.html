indigestibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˌdʒɛs.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˌdʒɛs.tɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being difficult to digest.
state of being hard to digest

Examples

The indigestibility of certain foods can cause discomfort after meals.
Researchers studied the indigestibility of high-fiber diets in elderly patients.
Excessive fat increases the indigestibility of some processed snacks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: digestibility - the quality of being easily digested

Etymology

From in- (not) + digest + -ibility (from Latin -ibilitas, forming nouns of quality).
