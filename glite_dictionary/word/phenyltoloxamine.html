phenyltoloxamine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛnɪltoʊˈlɒksəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɪltɒˈlɒksəmiːn / (BrE)
An antihistamine drug used in combination with other medications to treat symptoms such as coughing and nasal congestion.
antihistamine drug for coughs and congestion
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed phenyltoloxamine to help relieve her allergy symptoms.
Some cough syrups contain phenyltoloxamine as an active ingredient for nasal decongestion.
Patients should inform their physician about any allergies before using medications with phenyltoloxamine.

Etymology

Formed from 'phenyl' (a chemical group) and 'toloxamine' (an antihistamine compound).
