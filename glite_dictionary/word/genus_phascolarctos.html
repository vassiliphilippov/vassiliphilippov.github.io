genus Phascolarctos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs fæskoʊˈlɑːrktəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs fæskəʊˈlɑːktɒs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials that includes the koalas, native to Australia.
koala's genus (marsupial)

Examples

The genus Phascolarctos consists of the well-known marsupial, the koala.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of the genus Phascolarctos using fossil records.
The genus Phascolarctos is unique to the forests of eastern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: koala - a tree-dwelling Australian marsupial

Etymology

From Greek 'phaskolos' (pouch) + 'arktos' (bear), referring to the koala's appearance.
