mudguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌdˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
A curved or flat piece fitted above the wheel of a bicycle or motorcycle to prevent water or mud from being splashed onto the rider.
wheel cover to prevent splashing

Examples

The mudguard on his bicycle prevented his pants from getting dirty after the rain.
She noticed that the mudguard was bent after the accident.
Some motorbikes have stylish metal mudguards above each wheel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fender - a similar protective cover, especially on American vehicles

Etymology

Formed from 'mud' + 'guard', indicating its function of guarding against mud.
