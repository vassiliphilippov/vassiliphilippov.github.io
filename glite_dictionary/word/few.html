few

1. ADJ

/ fju / (AmE), / fjuː / (BrE)
A small number of people or things; not many.
a small number; not many

Examples

Only a few people attended the early morning meeting.
There are few opportunities like this in a lifetime.
Despite the heavy rain, few flowers were damaged.

Synset Examples

a few weeks ago
a few more wagons than usual
an invalid's pleasures are few and far between
few roses were still blooming
few women have led troops in battle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: many - a large number; a greater quantity
  • Degree Higher: several - more than two but not many; an indefinite small number
  • Degree Lower: fewer - a smaller number (comparative of few)

Etymology

From Old English fēawe (few), related to Old High German fao.
