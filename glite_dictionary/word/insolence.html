insolence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.səl.əns / (BrE)
rude and disrespectful behavior; the quality of being boldly disrespectful to someone in authority.
rude or disrespectful behavior

Examples

The student's insolence toward the teacher shocked everyone in the room.
She could not believe the insolence of the customer who demanded a refund without cause.
His insolence finally led to a formal warning from his supervisor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudence - impertinent or bold disrespect
  • Antonym: respect - polite regard, opposite of insolence

Etymology

From Latin insolentia (unusualness, arrogance), from insolens (immoderate, arrogant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn·sə·ləns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.səl.əns / (BrE)
rude and disrespectful behavior or speech; impudence.
rude or disrespectful behavior

Examples

Her insolence during the meeting shocked everyone present.
The teacher would not tolerate students’ insolence in the classroom.
He was fired for his repeated acts of insolence toward his supervisor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudence - rude or bold lack of respect
  • Antonym: politeness - showing good manners and respect

Etymology

From Latin 'insolentia' (unusualness, arrogance), from 'insolens' (unusual, immoderate, arrogant).
