Padda

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːdə / (AmE), / ˈpadə / (BrE)
A genus of small finches in the family Estrildidae, native to South and Southeast Asia, including the Java sparrow.
a genus of Asian finches

Examples

The Java sparrow, classified in the genus Padda, is a popular cage bird worldwide.
Species of the genus Padda are native to the grasslands of Southeast Asia.
Ornithologists have debated the taxonomy of Padda in recent years.

Etymology

From modern Latin Padda, genus name given by naturalists.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpædə / (AmE), / ˈpadə / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the family Estrildidae, known as mannikins or munias, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of small Southeast Asian birds

Examples

The Java sparrow belongs to the genus Padda.
Researchers observed several species within the Padda genus in the rice fields.
The conservation status of birds in the Padda genus has raised concerns among scientists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Estrildidae - family of finch-like passerine birds

Etymology

From Sinhalese *padda* (a kind of finch), adopted in scientific Latin.
