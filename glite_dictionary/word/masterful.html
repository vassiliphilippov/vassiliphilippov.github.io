masterful

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæs.tɚ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stə.fəl / (BrE)
demonstrating or showing great skill or mastery; performed with authority and competence.
showing great skill or authority

Examples

The pianist gave a masterful performance that left the audience in awe.
Her masterful handling of the negotiations led to a successful agreement.
The director's masterful storytelling kept viewers engaged from start to finish.

Synset Examples

a consummate artist
consummate skill
a masterful speaker
masterful technique
a masterly performance of the sonata
a virtuoso performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterly - showing skill or expertise, especially in art or performance
  • Antonym: inept - lacking skill or ability

Etymology

From Middle English 'maisterful', from 'maister' (master) + '-ful'.
