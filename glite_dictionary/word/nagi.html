nagi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːɡi / (AmE), / ˈnɑːɡi / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree (Nageia nagi) with glossy lance-shaped leaves, native to southern China, Taiwan, and southern Japan.
East Asian tree (Nageia nagi)

Examples

The sacred grove contained an ancient nagi that was venerated by the local community.
In Japan, the nagi is often planted near temples for its symbolic significance.
Botanists studied the unique structure of the nagi leaves during their expedition.

Etymology

From Japanese ナギ (nagi), referring to the tree Nageia nagi.
