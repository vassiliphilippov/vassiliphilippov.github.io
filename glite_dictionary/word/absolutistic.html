absolutistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæb.sə.luˈtɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌab.sə.luːˈtɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by absolutism, especially supporting totalitarian principles or absolute authority
relating to absolutism or absolute authority

Examples

The new leader enacted absolutistic policies that limited all forms of opposition.
Such absolutistic thinking leaves little room for compromise or dissent.
Critics argue that his rule was marked by an absolutistic approach to power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutist - a person who supports absolute principles in political, moral, or theological matters
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles (opposed to absolutism)

Etymology

From 'absolute' + '-istic', modeled on 'absolutism'.
