to pressurize

1. VERB

/ ˈprɛʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəraɪz / (BrE)
to maintain or increase the air pressure within a container or space, often to a specific level above normal atmospheric pressure
maintain or increase air pressure

Examples

Modern airplanes pressurize their cabins to ensure passenger comfort at high altitude.
Engineers had to pressurize the pipes for the new water system test.
The spacecraft was carefully pressurized before the astronauts boarded.

Synset Examples

the airplane cabin is pressurized
pressurize a space suit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to depressurize - to reduce or release the pressure in a space

Etymology

From 'pressure' + '-ize', modeled after analogous verb forms.

2. VERB

/ ˈprɛʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəraɪz / (BrE)
to increase the pressure in a container or space, especially by pumping in air or gas
increase internal pressure in a space

Examples

The engineers need to pressurize the tank before starting the process.
Modern airplanes pressurize the cabin to make flights more comfortable for passengers.
You should never attempt to pressurize a damaged vessel as it could be dangerous.

Synset Examples

The captain will pressurize the cabin for the passengers' comfort

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to depressurize - to reduce or remove the pressure in something

Etymology

From 'pressure' + '-ize', first attested in 20th century aviation contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00404056-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈprɛʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəraɪz / (BrE)
to increase the pressure of a gas or liquid, especially in a container or environment
increase pressure of a gas or liquid

Examples

The engineers needed to pressurize the tank before conducting the test.
It is essential to pressurize airplane cabins for passenger safety at high altitudes.
We pressurized the vessel to prevent the gas from leaking out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to depressurize - to reduce the pressure, especially in a sealed environment

Etymology

From 'pressure' + '-ize', first attested in early 20th century.
