Merthiolate

1. NOUN

/ mərˈθaɪ.əˌleɪt / (AmE), / mɜːˈθaɪ.əˌleɪt / (BrE)
A trademarked light-colored crystalline compound, used as an antiseptic, especially in surgical settings; trade name for thimerosal.
antiseptic compound (trade name)

Examples

The nurse applied Merthiolate to the cut before bandaging it.
Parents once kept a bottle of Merthiolate in the medicine cabinet for minor wounds.
Because of its distinctive color, you could always tell when Merthiolate had been used on a scrape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thimerosal - sodium ethylmercurithiosalicylate, the chemical compound in Merthiolate

Etymology

Trade name, combining 'mercury' and 'thiolate' (a chemical term for a sulfur compound).
