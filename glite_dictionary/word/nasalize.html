to nasalize

1. VERB

/ ˈneɪzəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəlaɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce a sound, especially a vowel, with air escaping through the nose by lowering the velum
pronounce a sound through the nose

Examples

Some regional accents tend to nasalize certain vowels more than others.
The speech coach taught her not to nasalize her consonants when speaking.
In French, the letter 'n' after a vowel will often cause it to be nasalized.

Synset Examples

She nasalizes all her vowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denasalize - to pronounce a sound without nasal resonance
  • Synonym: nasal - a sound produced with air flowing through the nose

Etymology

From French nasaliser, from Latin nasalis (of the nose).

2. VERB

/ ˈneɪzəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəlaɪz / (BrE)
to speak with a nasal quality or through the nose
speak with a nasal sound

Examples

Some people unintentionally nasalize when they're suffering from allergies.
She tried not to nasalize her words during the performance, but her cold made it difficult.
Regional accents sometimes cause people to nasalize certain vowels in conversation.

Synset Examples

In this part of the country, people tend to nasalize

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denasalize - to make speech less nasal in quality

Etymology

From 'nasal' (relating to the nose) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00953431-v
Synset POS: v
