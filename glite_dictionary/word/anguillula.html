Anguillula

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡwɪˈluːlə / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡwɪˈljuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of nematode worms in the family Cephalobidae, commonly known as vinegar eels.
genus of nematode worms

Examples

Researchers observed the movement of Anguillula under the microscope.
The presence of Anguillula in vinegar is harmless to humans.
Anguillula species are studied in laboratories for their role in decomposition.

Etymology

From Latin 'anguilla' (eel) with a diminutive suffix, referring to the worm's eel-like shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡwɪˈluːlə / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡwɪˈluːlə / (BrE)
a genus of minute nematode worms in the family Cephalobidae, some species of which are found in soil or decaying matter
genus of tiny nematode worms

Examples

The species Anguillula aceti is commonly known as the vinegar eel.
Researchers studied the life cycle of several Anguillula species in laboratory conditions.
Soil samples revealed the presence of Anguillula nematodes in large numbers.

Etymology

From Latin 'anguilla' meaning 'eel', referring to the eel-like shape; with modern Latin genus suffix '-ula'.
