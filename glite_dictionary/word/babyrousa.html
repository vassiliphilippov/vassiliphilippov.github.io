Babyrousa

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪbiˈruːsə / (AmE), / ˌbeɪbɪˈruːsə / (BrE)
A genus of wild pigs native to Indonesia, known for the males' distinctive upward-curving tusks.
genus of wild pigs from Indonesia

Examples

The Babyrousa is recognized for the males' remarkable, curling upper tusks.
Researchers observed a group of Babyrousa in the rainforests of Sulawesi.
Unlike most pigs, the Babyrousa can stand on its hind legs to reach higher branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babirusa - a member of the genus Babyrousa, noted for its unusual tusks
  • Degree Higher: pig - general term for domesticated and wild suids

Etymology

From Malay 'babi rusa', meaning 'deer pig'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪ.biˈruː.sə / (AmE), / ˌbeɪ.bɪˈruː.sə / (BrE)
A genus of wild pigs found in Indonesia, known as babirusas, characterized by their distinctive curved tusks.
genus of Indonesian wild pigs

Examples

The Babyrousa is native to the Indonesian islands of Sulawesi, Togian, Sula, and Buru.
Researchers have studied the unusual tusks of the Babyrousa for many years.
Members of the genus Babyrousa are sometimes called 'deer pigs' due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babirusa - a wild pig from Indonesia

Etymology

From Latinized form of Malay 'babi rusa' (literally 'deer pig'), named for the animal's appearance.
