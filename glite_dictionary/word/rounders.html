rounders

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndərz / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəz / (BrE)
an English team game similar to baseball, in which a small ball is hit with a bat and players run around bases to score
English ball game similar to baseball
british

Examples

We loved playing rounders during physical education lessons at school.
Each summer, the village organizes a charity rounders match on the green.
The rules of rounders are simple, making it easy for everyone to join in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseball - an American bat-and-ball game played between two teams

Etymology

From the verb 'round' (to go round bases); name attested since the 19th century.
