Cercosporella

1. NOUN

/ sɜr.koʊ.spəˈrɛl.ə / (AmE), / sɜː.kə.spəˈrel.ə / (BrE)
A form genus of imperfect fungi characterized by colorless spores and conidiophores, some species of which are plant pathogens.
form genus of colorless imperfect fungi

Examples

Several species of Cercosporella can infect crops and cause significant yield loss.
The presence of Cercosporella on the leaves was confirmed through microscopic examination.
Researchers are studying ways to control Cercosporella infections in wheat fields.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Cercospora (another genus of fungi) and the diminutive suffix -ella.

2. NOUN

/ sɜrkˌspɔːˈrɛlə / (AmE), / sɜːkˌspɔːˈrɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of imperfect fungi that lack pigment in their spores and conidiophores
genus of pigmentless fungi
medical

Examples

The fungus Cercosporella is known to cause leaf spot diseases in various plants.
Researchers studied how Cercosporella spreads in agricultural environments.
Symptoms of infection by Cercosporella include the appearance of pale, chlorotic spots on the leaves.

Etymology

From Greek *kerkos* (tail), *spora* (seed, spore), and the Latin diminutive suffix *-ella*.
