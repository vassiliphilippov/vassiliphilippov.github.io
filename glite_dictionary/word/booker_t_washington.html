Booker T. Washington

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊkər ti ˌwɑːʃɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈbʊkə ti ˌwɒʃɪŋtən / (BrE)
(1856–1915) An American educator, author, and advisor to several U.S. presidents who was born into slavery but became a leading advocate for African American education and founded the Tuskegee Institute in Alabama.
African American educator and founder of Tuskegee Institute

Examples

Booker T. Washington played a crucial role in advancing education for African Americans in the late 19th century.
The legacy of Booker T. Washington continues to influence educational policy and civil rights discussion today.
As the founder of the Tuskegee Institute, Booker T. Washington stressed the importance of industrial education and self-reliance.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbʊkər ti ˈwɑʃɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈbʊkə ti ˈwɒʃɪŋtən / (BrE)
(1856–1915) An American educator, author, and leader who was born into slavery but later founded the Tuskegee Institute in Alabama and became a prominent advocate for African-American advancement.
U.S. educator and civil rights leader

Examples

Booker T. Washington founded the Tuskegee Institute to promote education for African Americans.
Many historians consider Booker T. Washington a pivotal figure in the progress of civil rights in the United States.
The life story of Booker T. Washington is an inspiring example of overcoming adversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tuskegee Institute - Historically black university founded by Booker T. Washington
  • Antonym: W. E. B. Du Bois - African-American intellectual and contemporary of Washington, often contrasted with him in civil rights philosophy
