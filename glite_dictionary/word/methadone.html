methadone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəˌdoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɛθədəʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic narcotic drug used to treat pain and as part of drug addiction detoxification and maintenance programs, especially for heroin addiction.
synthetic narcotic for drug treatment
medical

Examples

The clinic offers methadone treatment to those recovering from opioid addiction.
After switching to methadone, he experienced fewer withdrawal symptoms than with morphine.
Doctors must monitor patients closely when prescribing methadone for pain management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphine - powerful opioid analgesic drug derived from opium

Etymology

Coined in German as 'Methadon' from meth(yl) + (ami)don (amide), referring to its chemical structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəˌdoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɛθədəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic narcotic drug, less addictive than morphine, used in the treatment of pain and for the detoxification and maintenance of heroin addiction
synthetic opioid drug for pain and addiction treatment
medical

Examples

The clinic provides methadone as part of its program to help people recover from opioid addiction.
After her surgery, she was prescribed methadone to manage her chronic pain.
Some patients take methadone daily to help prevent withdrawal symptoms from heroin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphine - an opioid narcotic used to treat severe pain

Etymology

From German Methadon, based on meth(yl) + (a)don (patterned after aminopyridine derivatives).
