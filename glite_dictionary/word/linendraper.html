linendraper

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnənˌdreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪnˌdreɪpə / (BrE)
a person or shop that sells linen and other fabrics by the yard; a dealer in household textiles and fabrics
dealer in linen and fabrics
archaic british

Examples

In Victorian London, the linendraper was a common presence on bustling shopping streets.
Her father worked as a linendraper, supplying fine fabrics to local households.
The young apprentice learned the trade under the careful guidance of a respected linendraper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draper - a dealer in cloth or fabrics

Etymology

From linen + draper, where draper comes from Old French drapier, meaning cloth-seller.
