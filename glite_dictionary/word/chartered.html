chartered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑrtərd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtəd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or service) hired for the exclusive temporary use of a particular person or group, especially for transportation.
hired for exclusive temporary use

Examples

We traveled on a chartered bus for the entire trip.
Their company arranged a chartered plane for the conference attendees.
The school hired a chartered coach to take students to the museum.

Synset Examples

a chartered plane
the chartered buses arrived on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private - intended for or restricted to the use of a particular person or group
  • Antonym: public - open to or shared by all people; not restricted

Etymology

From the verb 'charter', ultimately from Old French 'chartre' (charter, document), from Latin 'charta' (paper, card).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑrtərd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtəd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle such as a plane, bus, or boat) hired for the exclusive temporary use of a group of people, especially travelers.
hired for exclusive use by a group

Examples

The tour group arrived at the airport in a chartered bus that picked them up from their hotel.
They took a chartered flight to the remote island, organized especially for the event.
After the ceremony, guests traveled together in a chartered coach back to the city.

Synset Examples

a chartered plane
the chartered buses arrived on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leased - hired or rented for a period of time

Etymology

From Middle English 'chartre', from Old French, from Latin 'chartula' (a little paper, document of privilege), related to 'charter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00361121-a
Synset POS: a
