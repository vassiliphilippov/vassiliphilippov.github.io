haematocytopenia

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmæt̬oʊˌsaɪtəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / hiːˌmətəʊˌsaɪtəʊˈpiːniə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an abnormally low number of red blood cells in the blood.
abnormally low red blood cell count
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with haematocytopenia after noting his unusually low red cell count.
Severe haematocytopenia may lead to symptoms like fatigue and shortness of breath.
Treatment options for haematocytopenia depend on its underlying cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - a condition with decreased red blood cells or hemoglobin

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + kytos (cell) + penia (poverty, lack).
