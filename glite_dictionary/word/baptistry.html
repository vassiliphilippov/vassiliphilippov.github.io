baptistry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæp.tɪ.stri / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪ.stri / (BrE)
A bowl or vessel used to hold water for baptism in a church.
bowl for baptismal water

Examples

The ornate baptistry was placed at the front of the church for the christening ceremony.
After the restoration, the ancient baptistry gleamed with new gold inlay.
The priest filled the baptistry with holy water before the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - a basin for holding baptismal water in a church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *baptisterium* (place of baptism), from Greek *baptistērion*.
