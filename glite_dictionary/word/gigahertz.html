gigahertz

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪɡəˌhɝts / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡəhɜːts / (BrE)
A unit of frequency equal to one billion cycles per second (1,000,000,000 Hz).
unit of frequency: one billion hertz

Examples

The new processor runs at 3 gigahertz, making it much faster than previous models.
Wireless routers often operate on the 2.4 gigahertz or 5 gigahertz frequency bands.
Scientists measured signals in the range of several gigahertz during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: megahertz - unit of frequency: one million hertz
  • Degree Higher: terahertz - unit of frequency: one trillion hertz

Etymology

From giga- (meaning 'billion') + hertz (the SI unit of frequency, named after Heinrich Hertz).
