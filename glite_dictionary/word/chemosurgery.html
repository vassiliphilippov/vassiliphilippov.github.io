chemosurgery

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːmoʊˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˌkɛməʊˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which chemicals are used to destroy diseased or malignant tissue, especially for skin cancer
chemical destruction of diseased tissue
medical

Examples

The dermatologist recommended chemosurgery to treat the patient's skin carcinoma.
Advances in chemosurgery have significantly improved cure rates for certain skin cancers.
After undergoing chemosurgery, the patient was monitored closely for any signs of recurrence.

Etymology

From chemo- (relating to chemicals) + surgery.
