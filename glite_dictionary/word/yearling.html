yearling

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɪr.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɪə.lɪŋ / (BrE)
An animal, especially a young horse or sheep, that is between one and two years old.
animal in its second year

Examples

The farmer separated the yearling calves from the older cows.
Each yearling was examined before being sold at auction.
A healthy yearling typically grows rapidly during its second spring.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: foal - a young horse, especially less than one year old
  • Degree Higher: adult - a fully grown animal

Etymology

From 'year' + '-ling', a diminutive suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɪr.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɪə.lɪŋ / (BrE)
A racehorse that is considered one year old, specifically until the second January 1 after its birth.
one-year-old racehorse

Examples

The trainer carefully selected the yearling for the upcoming racing season.
Several yearlings were auctioned off at the horse sale last weekend.
After his first birthday, the foal officially became a yearling in racing terms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: weanling - a young animal that has just been weaned, especially a horse between weaning and one year old
  • Degree Higher: juvenile - a young animal, especially one older than a yearling but not fully mature

Etymology

From 'year' + '-ling', an Old English diminutive suffix.
