Solenogastres

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊlɪnoʊˈɡæstriːz / (AmE), / ˌsəʊlɪnəʊˈɡæstriːz / (BrE)
an order of small, worm-like marine mollusks lacking shells, belonging to the class Aplacophora.
order of small, shell-less marine mollusks

Examples

Members of the Solenogastres are notable for their lack of a true shell.
New marine species within the Solenogastres have been described in recent years.
Research on Solenogastres helps scientists understand the evolution of mollusks.

Etymology

From Greek 'solen' (pipe) + 'gaster' (stomach/belly), referring to their body structure.
