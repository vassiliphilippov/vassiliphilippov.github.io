anapestic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈpɛs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈpɛs.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a metrical foot) consisting of two short or unstressed syllables followed by one long or stressed syllable; relating to, or characteristic of, anapest.
relating to anapest in poetry

Examples

The poem is written in anapestic meter, giving it a rhythmic, galloping feel.
Children's nursery rhymes often feature anapestic lines for a playful sound.
He identified the anapestic foot by the pattern of unstressed and stressed syllables.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dactylic - of a metrical foot with one stressed followed by two unstressed syllables

Etymology

From Greek anapaistikos, from anapaistos (reversed, struck back).
