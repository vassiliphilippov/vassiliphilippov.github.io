agon

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈæɡɒn / (BrE)
A public contest, festival, or competition in ancient Greece, especially one at which competitors struggled for prizes.
festival or contest in ancient Greece

Examples

During the ancient Olympic agon, athletes from all over Greece gathered to compete.
The poet was awarded a laurel wreath at the dramatic agon held in Athens.
Scholars study the structure of the Greek agon to understand classical competition.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ἀγών (agōn, meaning 'contest, competition, struggle').
