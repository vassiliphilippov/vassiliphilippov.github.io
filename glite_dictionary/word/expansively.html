expansively

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɑːnsɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that is open, enthusiastic, or showing high spirits
in an open or enthusiastic manner

Examples

She greeted her old friends expansively, with arms wide open and a beaming smile.
The CEO expansively outlined his vision for the company's future at the annual meeting.
He laughed expansively, filling the room with warmth and good humor.

Synset Examples

Khrushchev ebulliently promised to supply rockets for the protection of Cuba against American aggression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebulliently - in a cheerful or exuberant manner

Etymology

From 'expansive' + '-ly'; 'expansive' from Latin 'expansivus'.

2. ADV

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɑːnsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is open, generous, or grand in manner or scope
in a grand or generous way

Examples

The CEO spoke expansively about the company's future plans during the meeting.
She gestured expansively while telling her exciting story.
They hosted their friends expansively, ensuring everyone felt warmly welcomed.

Synset Examples

she managed to live rather expansively on her modest income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generously - in a generous manner
  • Antonym: narrowly - in a limited or restricted manner

Etymology

From expansive + -ly, after Latin expandere (to spread out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00507174-r
Synset POS: r
