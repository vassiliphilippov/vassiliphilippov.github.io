anapsid

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæpsɪd / (AmE), / əˈnapsɪd / (BrE)
a primitive reptile distinguished by having no openings in the temporal region of the skull; includes extinct groups and turtles
primitive reptile with no skull openings
medical

Examples

The fossil revealed that the creature was an anapsid, indicating it was closely related to turtles.
Unlike diapsids, anapsids lack temporal fenestrae in their skulls.
Paleontologists debated whether turtles are truly anapsids or if they lost their skull openings secondarily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diapsid - reptile with two temporal openings in the skull
  • Antonym: synapsid - reptile/mammal ancestor with one temporal opening in the skull

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'hapsis' (arch), referring to the absence of temporal openings.
