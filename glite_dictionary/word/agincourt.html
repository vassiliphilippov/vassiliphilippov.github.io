Agincourt

1. PROPN

/ ˈædʒ.ɪn.kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ɪn.kɔːt / (BrE)
the site of a major battle in northern France in 1415, where English forces under Henry V defeated a much larger French army
historic battle site in northern France (1415)

Examples

The Battle of Agincourt is remembered as one of England's greatest military victories.
Shakespeare's play 'Henry V' dramatizes the events leading up to Agincourt.
Many historians consider Agincourt a turning point in the Hundred Years' War.

Etymology

From French Agincourt, the name of the village near where the battle was fought.
