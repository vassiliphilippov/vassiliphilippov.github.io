Cleome serrulata

1. NOUN

/ kliˈoʊmi sɛrəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / kliːˈəʊmi sɛrəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a plant native to western North America, with trifoliate leaves and white or pink, spider-shaped flowers, often grown as an ornamental
North American plant with spider-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden's wildflower section included a cluster of Cleome serrulata in bloom.
Researchers documented pollinator visits to Cleome serrulata throughout the summer.
The pink flowers of Cleome serrulata attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider flower - another common name for species in the Cleome genus

Etymology

From the genus name Cleome, from Greek kleō (to shut, close), and the species epithet serrulata, Latin for 'finely saw-toothed'.
