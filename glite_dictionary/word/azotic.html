azotic

1. ADJ

/ əˈzoʊtɪk / (AmE), / əˈzɒtɪk / (BrE)
(archaic) Of or containing nitrogen.
archaic; relating to nitrogen
archaic

Examples

Early chemists studied azotic compounds to learn more about the properties of nitrogen.
The laboratory produced several azotic acids during the experiment.
In historical texts, one often encounters the term azotic in place of nitrogenous.

Synset Examples

nitric acid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrogenous - containing or relating to nitrogen

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen), coined by Lavoisier from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'zōē' (life), as nitrogen does not support life.
