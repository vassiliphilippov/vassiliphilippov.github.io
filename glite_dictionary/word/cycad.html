cycad

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkæd / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪkad / (BrE)
A tropical gymnosperm plant of the order Cycadales, typically with an unbranched stem topped by a crown of large, fernlike leaves.
a tropical plant with fernlike leaves

Examples

The gardens featured a rare cycad that delighted botanists from around the world.
A cycad can live for hundreds of years if left undisturbed in its native habitat.
Many cycads are endangered due to habitat loss and illegal collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - tree with a crown of large compound leaves, often tropical

Etymology

From Greek 'kykas', possibly an error for 'koikas', meaning a kind of palm.
