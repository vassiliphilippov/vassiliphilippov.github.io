Boletellus russellii

1. PROPN

/ boʊləˈtɛləs rʌˈsɛli.aɪ / (AmE), / bəʊlɪˈtɛləs rʌˈsɛli.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of bolete fungus with a long, coarsely shaggy, netted stalk and a cracked, patterned cap surface, found in North America.
a North American bolete fungus species

Examples

Boletellus russellii can be identified by its unique netted stalk and cracked cap.
Mushroom enthusiasts in North America often search for Boletellus russellii during the summer months.
The field guide included a detailed illustration of Boletellus russellii among other bolete species.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Boletus' (mushroom); species named after American mycologist Henry Russell.
