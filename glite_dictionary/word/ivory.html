ivory

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri / (BrE)
a hard, smooth, creamy-white substance forming the tusks of elephants and walruses, valued for carving and ornamentation
hard white substance from tusks

Examples

The statue was carved from a single piece of ivory.
Trade in ivory is banned in many countries to protect endangered elephants.
Antique pianos often have keys made from real ivory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bone - the hard material that forms the skeleton of vertebrates
  • Degree Higher: enamel - the hard, glossy outer layer that covers teeth

Etymology

From Old French ivoire, from Latin ebur (ivory), from Egyptian ȝbw (elephant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.vɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.vəri / (BrE)
a pale, creamy-white color resembling that of bleached bones or elephant tusks
a pale creamy-white color

Examples

She chose curtains in an elegant ivory to match the decor.
The wedding dress was a soft shade of ivory, not pure white.
He painted the walls ivory to give the room a warm, inviting look.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: white - the color of snow or milk; the lightest color
  • Degree Lower: cream - a pale yellowish-white color

Etymology

From Old French 'ivurie', from Latin 'ebur', 'eboris' (ivory).
