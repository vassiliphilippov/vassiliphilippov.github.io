overhasty

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈheɪsti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈheɪsti / (BrE)
done too quickly, without enough thought or consideration; excessively hasty
too hasty; excessively rushed

Examples

Making an overhasty decision can lead to regret later on.
His overhasty response surprised everyone at the meeting.
They warned against an overhasty conclusion before all the evidence was reviewed.

Synset Examples

hasty marriage seldom proveth well
hasty makeshifts take the place of planning
rejected what was regarded as an overhasty plan for reconversion
wondered whether they had been rather precipitate in deposing the king

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hasty - done or acting with excessive speed or urgency
  • Synonym: rash - displaying or proceeding from a lack of careful consideration

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + hasty.
