feminist

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛmɪnɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɛmɪnɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the belief that women should have the same rights and opportunities as men
supporting equality for women

Examples

Her feminist perspective influenced the policies she introduced at the workplace.
The novel received praise for its feminist themes and strong female characters.
They organized a feminist lecture series on campus to promote gender equality.

Synset Examples

feminist critique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminism - belief in and advocacy for women's rights and equality

Etymology

From French *féministe*, from Latin *fēmina* (woman).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02850242-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɛmɪnɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports feminism and advocates for equal rights for women.
supporter of feminism

Examples

As a proud feminist, she has long advocated for equal pay in the workplace.
The author’s latest book explores what it means to be a feminist today.
Many young people identify as feminists and support gender equality initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misogynist - a person who dislikes, despises, or is strongly prejudiced against women
  • Synonym: feminism - the advocacy of women's rights on the ground of the equality of the sexes

Etymology

From French *féministe*, based on Latin *femina* (woman).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10104404-n
Synset POS: n
