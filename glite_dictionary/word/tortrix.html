Tortrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.trɪks / (BrE)
A genus of moths that is the type genus of the family Tortricidae, containing many species known as leafroller moths.
genus of leafroller moths

Examples

The larvae of some Tortrix species are agricultural pests that damage fruit crops.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Tortrix genus in South America.
Many fruit farmers monitor Tortrix populations to protect their orchards from infestation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tortricidae - family of moths that includes Tortrix

Etymology

From Latin 'tortrix' (a female who twists), from 'torquēre' (to twist), referring to the leaf-rolling habit of the larvae.
