tortrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.trɪks / (BrE)
A small moth of various species, especially those that infest crops such as tea and coffee plants.
small moth infesting crops

Examples

The farmer discovered a tortrix infestation in his tea plantation.
During the summer, several tortrix moths were found damaging the coffee plants.
Entomologists studied the life cycle of the tortrix to develop better pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafroller - A caterpillar that rolls leaves of plants, usually the larva of a tortrix moth

Etymology

From New Latin *Tortrix*, genus name, from Latin *torquere* (to twist), referring to the larva's habit of twisting leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrtrɪks / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtrɪks / (BrE)
A type of moth whose larvae feed in fruits, especially oranges, in regions such as California.
fruit-feeding moth

Examples

The tortrix is a pest that can cause considerable damage to orange crops.
Farmers monitored the orchards closely for signs of tortrix infestation.
In California, special traps are used to detect adult tortrix moths early in the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafroller - a moth whose larvae roll leaves to create shelters

Etymology

From modern Latin Tortrix, genus name, from Latin torquere (to twist), referring to twisting leaf behavior.
