Centropomidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.trəˈpɑ.mɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.trəʊˈpɒ.mɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly marine fishes in the order Perciformes, including robalos and snooks, found mostly in warm coastal waters.
family of fishes including robalos/snooks
medical

Examples

Members of the Centropomidae family are popular among recreational fishermen for their fighting ability.
The Centropomidae family includes fishes commonly known as snooks and robalos.
Scientists are conducting research on the population dynamics of Centropomidae species along the Atlantic coast.

Etymology

From New Latin Centropomidae, based on the type genus Centropomus.

2. NOUN

/ sɛn.trəˈpɑː.məˌdi / (AmE), / sɛn.trəˈpɒ.mɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of fish in the order Perciformes that includes robalos and related species, found primarily in warm coastal waters.
a family of fish including robalos

Examples

The Centropomidae family is best known for including popular sport fish such as the snook.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Centropomidae along the South American coast.
Many anglers seek fish from the Centropomidae for their challenging fight and tasty flesh.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Centropomidae', based on the type genus Centropomus, from Greek 'kentron' (spur) + 'poma' (cover, lid).
