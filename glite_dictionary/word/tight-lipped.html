tight-lipped

1. ADJ

/ taɪtˈlɪpt / (AmE), / taɪtˈlɪpt / (BrE)
unwilling to speak or reveal information; silent or secretive
unwilling to speak; secretive

Examples

The spokesperson remained tight-lipped when questioned by reporters after the meeting.
Despite several attempts to get information, he stayed tight-lipped about the project.
She gave a tight-lipped smile but refused to answer any questions.

Synset Examples

The officials remained tight-lipped about the investigation.
She was tight-lipped about her promotion.
He was tight-lipped regarding his plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticent - reluctant to speak or reveal thoughts or information
  • Synonym: secretive - tending to conceal information or thoughts; not open
  • Antonym: forthcoming - willing to give information; communicative

Etymology

Formed from 'tight' + 'lipped', referring metaphorically to lips held tightly together as a sign of silence.
