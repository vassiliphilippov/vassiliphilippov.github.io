disembarkation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəmˌbɑːrˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɛm.bɑːˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle at the end of a journey
the act of leaving a ship or aircraft

Examples

The disembarkation process took longer than expected due to customs checks.
Passengers were asked to wait until the crew announced that disembarkation could begin.
After hours at sea, the cruise ship's disembarkation commenced smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: embarkation - the act of boarding a ship or aircraft
  • Synonym: to disembark - to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle

Etymology

From French 'débarquement', from 'débarquer' (to disembark), and Latin 'ex-' (out of) + 'barca' (boat).
