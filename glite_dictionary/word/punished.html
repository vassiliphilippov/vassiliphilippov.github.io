punished

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃt / (BrE)
Having been subjected to a penalty or punishment for an offense or wrongdoing.
having received punishment

Examples

She felt deeply punished after the harsh criticism from her manager.
The punished students were required to stay after class and write an essay.
He looked punished, his shoulders slumped in defeat after the verdict was announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - inflicting or intended as punishment
  • Antonym: rewarded - having received a reward or benefit for an action

Etymology

Past participle of 'punish', from Old French 'puniss-', stem of 'punir', from Latin 'punire' (to punish).
