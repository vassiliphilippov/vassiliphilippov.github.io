lapsing

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlæpsɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or instance of failing to maintain a previous higher state or standard.
failure to maintain a higher state

Examples

His lapsing into bad habits worried his family greatly.
The committee discussed the lapsing of safety standards over time.
Her lapsing from sobriety set back her recovery process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapse - a temporary failure of concentration, memory, or judgement
  • Antonym: improvement - becoming better or returning to a higher state

Etymology

From 'lapse', from Latin 'lapsus' (a slip or fall), with the suffix '-ing' forming a noun.
