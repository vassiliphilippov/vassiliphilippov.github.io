Armagnac

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmənˌjæk / (AmE), / ˈɑːmənjak / (BrE)
A type of dry brandy distilled in the Armagnac district of southwestern France, known for its distinctive flavor and traditional production methods.
French brandy from the Armagnac region

Examples

He poured himself a glass of Armagnac after dinner to relax.
The distillery offers tastings of its award-winning Armagnac to visitors.
She prefers Armagnac to other brandies because of its rich, complex flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cognac - A type of brandy from the Cognac region of France

Etymology

From French 'Armagnac', the name of the region in Gascony, France, where the brandy is produced.
