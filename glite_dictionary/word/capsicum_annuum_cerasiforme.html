Capsicum annuum cerasiforme

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæp.sɪ.kəm əˈnuː.əm ˌsɛrəˈsɪfɔr.mi / (AmE), / ˌkap.sɪ.kəm əˈnjuː.əm ˌsɛərəˈsɪfɔː.mi / (BrE)
A variety of pepper plant bearing small, rounded, usually pungent fruits, commonly known as cherry peppers.
a type of cherry pepper plant
medical

Examples

The Capsicum annuum cerasiforme is widely cultivated for its small, spicy fruits.
Many gardeners grow Capsicum annuum cerasiforme for use in salsa and pickling recipes.
The bright red Capsicum annuum cerasiforme added both color and heat to the dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry pepper - common name for Capsicum annuum cerasiforme, small round pepper
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - a mild, sweet variety of Capsicum annuum with large blocky fruits

Etymology

From Latin: 'capsicum' (pepper), 'annuum' (annual), 'cerasiforme' (cherry-shaped).
