iva

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvə / (AmE), / ˈaɪvə / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, shrubby plants with small greenish flowers, commonly found in moist areas such as salt marshes in eastern and central North America.
genus of shrubby North American plants

Examples

Iva species are often found growing along the edges of salt marshes.
Many gardeners avoid planting iva because of its coarse appearance and rapid spread.
Researchers studied how iva helps support local insect populations in coastal wetlands.

Etymology

From New Latin Iva, the genus name in botanical classification.
