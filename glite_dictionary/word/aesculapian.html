aesculapian

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛskjəˈleɪpiən / (AmE), / ˌiːskjʊˈleɪpɪən / (BrE)
Relating to Aesculapius, the Roman god of medicine, or to the art and practice of healing or medicine.
relating to Aesculapius or medicine

Examples

She admired his aesculapian skills and dedication to his patients.
The hospital's logo depicts an aesculapian staff, a symbol of medical care.
He delivered an aesculapian lecture on the progress of modern medicine.

Etymology

From Latin Aesculapius, the Roman god of healing.
