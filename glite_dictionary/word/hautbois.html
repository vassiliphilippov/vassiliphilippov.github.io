hautbois

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈbwaɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈbwa / (BrE)
an early form of the oboe; a woodwind instrument with a conical bore and a double-reed mouthpiece, used especially in Baroque music
early oboe; baroque woodwind instrument

Examples

The baroque ensemble included a hautbois and a harpsichord.
In 17th-century orchestras, the hautbois replaced the shawm as a principal woodwind instrument.
He specializes in historical performance and plays a replica hautbois.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oboe - a modern woodwind instrument with a double-reed mouthpiece

Etymology

French, literally 'high wood', from 'haut' (high) + 'bois' (wood).
