holding

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of retaining or keeping something in one's possession or control
act of retaining something

Examples

His holding of the documents was questioned during the investigation.
Prolonged holding of your breath can be dangerous.
The holding of power by a single individual often leads to conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keeping - the act of retaining possession or custody
  • Antonym: letting go - the act of releasing something held or retained

Etymology

From the verb "hold" + -ing.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
something, such as property, shares, or other assets, that is owned by an individual, company, or organization
an owned asset or property

Examples

She sold several of her holdings in the company to raise money for her startup.
The museum's art holdings include several rare paintings from the 18th century.
Investors are advised to diversify their holdings to reduce risk.

Synset Examples

that hat is my property
he is a man of property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asset - a useful or valuable thing or person
  • Synonym: property - something owned by someone; a possession

Etymology

From Old English 'healdan' (to hold) + -ing (noun suffix).
