Budorcas taxicolor

1. NOUN

/ buˈdɔrkəs ˌtæksɪˈkɔlər / (AmE), / bjuːˈdɔːkəs ˌtæksɪˈkɔːlə / (BrE)
a large, heavily built goat-antelope found in the eastern Himalayan region, known as the takin
large Himalayan goat-antelope (takin)

Examples

The Budorcas taxicolor is well-adapted to mountainous regions with steep terrain and thick forests.
Many nature documentaries feature the unique appearance of the Budorcas taxicolor, commonly known as the takin.
Researchers are studying how the Budorcas taxicolor survives the cold climates of the eastern Himalayas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takin - a large, muscular goat-antelope of the Himalayas

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus 'Budorcas' and species 'taxicolor', used in zoological classification.
