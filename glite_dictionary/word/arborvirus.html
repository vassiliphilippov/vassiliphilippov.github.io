arborvirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbɚˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːbəˌvaɪrəs / (BrE)
A group of RNA viruses transmitted by arthropods such as mosquitoes and ticks, often causing fever or encephalitis in humans and animals.
virus transmitted by arthropods
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of arborvirus infections are often associated with mosquito breeding during rainy seasons.
Scientists are studying new ways to control arborvirus transmission in tropical regions.
Some arborvirus infections can lead to serious neurological complications in humans.

Etymology

Blend of 'arthropod' and 'virus', indicating viruses transmitted by arthropods.
