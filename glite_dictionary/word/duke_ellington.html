Duke Ellington

1. PROPN

/ ˈduːk ˈɛlɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈdjuːk ˈɛlɪŋtən / (BrE)
(1899–1974) United States jazz composer, pianist, and bandleader, widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in jazz history.
jazz composer, pianist, and bandleader

Examples

Duke Ellington revolutionized jazz with his innovative compositions and arrangements.
Many musicians consider Duke Ellington one of the greatest bandleaders in American music history.
The orchestra performed a tribute to Duke Ellington on the anniversary of his birth.
