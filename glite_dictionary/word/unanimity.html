unanimity

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnəˈnɪməti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnəˈnɪmɪti / (BrE)
complete agreement by everyone involved; the state of being unanimous
complete agreement by everyone

Examples

The board reached unanimity on the decision to move forward with the plan.
There was unanimity among the judges on the winner of the competition.
Despite hours of discussion, complete unanimity proved difficult to achieve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consensus - general agreement
  • Antonym: disagreement - lack of consensus or agreement

Etymology

From Latin unanimitas, from unanimus (of one mind, unanimous)
