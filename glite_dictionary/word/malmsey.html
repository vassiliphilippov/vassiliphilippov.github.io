malmsey

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːmzɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːmzi / (BrE)
A sweet variety of Madeira wine, originally made from Malvasia grapes.
sweet variety of Madeira wine

Examples

He poured himself a glass of malmsey after dinner to enjoy with dessert.
The cellar contained several rare bottles of vintage malmsey.
Traditionally, malmsey is paired with rich cakes and fruit tarts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Madeira - a fortified wine from the island of Madeira

Etymology

From Middle English Malmesy, from Old French malvoisie, from Italian malvasia, after the Greek port of Monemvasia, famous for its wine.
