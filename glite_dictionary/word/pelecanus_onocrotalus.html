Pelecanus onocrotalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs ˌɒnəˈkrəʊtələs / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˈkeɪnəs ˌɒnəˈkrɒtʌləs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the great white pelican, a large waterbird native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa
scientific name of great white pelican

Examples

The Pelecanus onocrotalus can often be seen gliding over lakes in southern Africa.
Scientists observed a colony of Pelecanus onocrotalus nesting together on the wetland island.
Unlike its American counterpart, the Pelecanus onocrotalus tends to migrate between Europe and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great white pelican - large white waterbird, common name for Pelecanus onocrotalus

Etymology

From Latin: 'pelecanus' (pelican) and 'onocrotalus' (great white pelican).
