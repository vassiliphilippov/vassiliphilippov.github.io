downwind

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwɪnd / (BrE)
In the direction that the wind is blowing; away from the source of the wind.
in the direction the wind is blowing

Examples

The smoke drifted downwind and covered the nearby fields.
Make sure you stand downwind of the campfire to avoid the smoke.
The boat moved quickly downwind after the sails were adjusted.

Synset Examples

they were sailing windward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upwind - in the direction from which the wind is coming

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'wind,' in reference to the direction taken by the wind.

Source: WordNet

2. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwɪnd / (BrE)
in the direction that the wind is blowing; with the wind rather than against it
in the direction the wind is blowing

Examples

The hikers set up camp downwind to avoid the smoke from their fire.
It's best to position yourself downwind if you want to catch the scent of the flowers.
The pollution drifted downwind from the factory to neighboring towns.

Synset Examples

they flew downwind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upwind - in the opposite direction to that which the wind is blowing; against the wind
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00095315-r

3. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌwɪnd / (BrE)
in the direction that the wind is blowing; towards the side away from the source of the wind
in the direction the wind is blowing

Examples

The sailboat drifted downwind toward the open sea.
Be sure to stand downwind of the campfire to avoid getting smoke in your eyes.
The smell of baking bread carried downwind across the fields.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upwind - in the direction from which the wind is blowing

Etymology

Formed from 'down-' (meaning 'towards a lower place or along') + 'wind'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01403241-s
Synset POS: s
