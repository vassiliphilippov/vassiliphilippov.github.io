neighborhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəhʊd / (BrE)
The area or region surrounding a particular place; a nearby locality.
surrounding area or locality

Examples

There are several good restaurants in the neighborhood of the university.
We heard sirens coming from the neighborhood near the river last night.
Let me know if you are ever in my neighborhood and want to stop by.

Synset Examples

the plane crashed in the vicinity of Asheville
it is a rugged locality
he always blames someone else in the immediate neighborhood
I will drop in on you the next time I am in this neck of the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicinity - the area near or surrounding a particular place
  • Synonym: locality - an area or neighborhood, especially as regards its physical features

Etymology

From Middle English 'neighbourhede', from 'neighbour' + '-hood'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəhʊd / (BrE)
an approximate amount or value; a figure close to a stated number or amount, especially used in the phrase 'in the neighborhood of'.
approximate amount or value

Examples

The car is priced in the neighborhood of $30,000, give or take a few thousand.
Their ages are all in the neighborhood of forty.
The total number of employees is somewhere in the neighborhood of 500.

Synset Examples

it was going to take in the region of two or three months to finish the job
the price is in the neighborhood of $100

3. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəhʊd / (BrE)
A district or community within a town or city, especially one forming a close-knit community of people living near one another.
district/community within a city or town

Examples

The entire neighborhood came together for the annual block party.
She grew up in a quiet neighborhood on the edge of the city.
After moving to a new neighborhood, he quickly made friends with his neighbors.

Synset Examples

it is a friendly neighborhood
my neighborhood voted for Bush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district - an area of a country or city, often for administrative purposes
  • Synonym: community - a group of people living together in one place

Etymology

From Middle English 'neigheborhede', combining 'neighbor' with '-hood' (state or condition).

4. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəhʊd / (BrE)
an area within a city or town that has distinctive characteristics, often forming a community
distinct area within a city or town

Examples

Everyone in our neighborhood knows each other and often gathers for events.
The new restaurant quickly became popular throughout the entire neighborhood.
She moved to a different neighborhood to be closer to her office.

Synset Examples

an ethnic neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district - an area of a town or city with particular features or characteristics
  • Synonym: community - a group of people living in the same area or having shared interests

Etymology

Formed from 'neighbor' + 'hood', originally in Middle English as 'neighbourhod'.
