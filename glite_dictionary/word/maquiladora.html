maquiladora

1. NOUN

/ məˌkiː.ləˈdɔː.rə / (AmE), / məˌkiː.ləˈdɔː.rə / (BrE)
a factory in Mexico, typically near the US border, where parts are imported to be assembled and the finished products are exported, mainly to the United States
Mexican border assembly plant

Examples

The company set up a maquiladora in Tijuana to assemble electronics for the U.S. market.
Many workers commute daily to the maquiladora zone on the Mexican side of the border.
Labor conditions in some maquiladoras have attracted international attention and criticism.

Etymology

From Spanish 'maquiladora', from 'maquilar' (to process, to assemble)
