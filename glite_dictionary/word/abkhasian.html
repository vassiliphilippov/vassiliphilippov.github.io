Abkhasian

1. NOUN

/ æbˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / abˈkeɪziən / (BrE)
the language of the Abkhaz people, belonging to the Northwest Caucasian language family
language spoken by the Abkhaz people

Examples

The Abkhasian language uses an alphabet that is adapted from Cyrillic.
She is fluent in both Russian and Abkhasian.
Many works of folklore have been preserved in Abkhasian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhaz - a person from Abkhazia or the language they speak

Etymology

From Abkhaz, the self-designation of the people, with English -ian suffix.

2. NOUN

/ æbˈkeɪziən / (AmE), / æbˈkeɪziən / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group native to the region of Abkhazia, located to the east of the Black Sea
member of the Abkhaz ethnic group

Examples

Many Abkhasians speak both Abkhaz and Russian in their daily lives.
Traditional dances remain an important part of Abkhasian culture.
The Abkhasians have a rich history closely tied to the Black Sea region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhaz - a member of the Abkhaz ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian *Abkhaz* (Абхаз), from the native endonym *Apswa*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
