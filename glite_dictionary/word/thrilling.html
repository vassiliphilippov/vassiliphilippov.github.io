thrilling

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a sudden feeling of shivering or quivering, as from cold, fear, or electric shock
causing shivering or quivering

Examples

A thrilling wind swept through the abandoned building, making everyone shiver.
The touch of the icy water was unexpectedly thrilling on my skin.
At night, a thrilling draft entered the room and made the curtains flutter.

Synset Examples

a thrilling wind blew off the frozen lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilling - causing a feeling of coldness or fear

Etymology

From the verb 'thrill,' ultimately from Middle English *thrillen* (to pierce), from Old English *þyrlian*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθrɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
causing great excitement or emotion; very exciting or gripping.
causing excitement or strong emotion

Examples

The roller coaster ride was absolutely thrilling from start to finish.
She found the detective novel thrilling and couldn't put it down.
It was a thrilling experience to watch the fireworks display over the city.

Synset Examples

she gave an electrifying performance
a thrilling performer to watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; dull

Etymology

From the verb 'thrill', which comes from Middle English 'thrillen', meaning to pierce.

Source: WordNet
