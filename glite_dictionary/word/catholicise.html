to catholicise

1. VERB

/ ˈkæθəlɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈθɒlɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to adopt the beliefs, practices, or characteristics of Catholicism
cause to adopt Catholicism

Examples

The missionaries sought to catholicise the indigenous population through education and conversion.
It was controversial when the state attempted to catholicise public institutions in the nineteenth century.
Some historians analyze efforts to catholicise local traditions during the colonial period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change or cause to change beliefs, especially religious

Etymology

From Catholic + -ise, modeled after similar religious verbs.
