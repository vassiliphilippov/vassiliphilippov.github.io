cotyledon

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːtəˈlidn̩ / (AmE), / ˌkɒtɪˈliːdən / (BrE)
an embryonic leaf in seed-bearing plants, one of the first leaves to appear from a germinating seed
embryonic leaf in plants
medical

Examples

The cotyledon supplies nutrients to the young seedling until true leaves develop.
In beans, the cotyledons remain visible above the soil after germination.
Scientists studied the role of the cotyledon in plant development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed leaf - an alternative term for an embryonic leaf in plants

Etymology

From Greek kotylēdōn (cup-shaped hollow), from kotylē (cup).
