redbrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
a common North American shrub with reddish-purple twigs and white flowers, often found in wetlands or thickets
North American shrub with reddish twigs

Examples

The fields were dotted with redbrush, their twigs casting a reddish hue in the sunlight.
After the snow melted, clusters of redbrush stood out against the greening marshland.
Wildlife often shelters among the dense branches of redbrush along the river bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red osier - common name for the same or closely related shrub species with red stems

Etymology

From 'red' + 'brush', referring to the shrub's reddish twigs and brush-like growth pattern.
