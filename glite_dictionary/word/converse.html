converse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnˌvɝːs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɜːs / (BrE)
(Logic, mathematics) A statement or proposition derived from another by reversing its terms, especially in conditional statements.
proposition obtained by reversing terms

Examples

In logic, the converse of 'If it rains, then the ground is wet' is 'If the ground is wet, then it rains.'
Students often confuse the converse of a statement with its inverse.
The converse is not always true, even if the original proposition is valid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: inverse - Opposite in relation or position; in mathematics, the operation that reverses another

Etymology

From Latin 'conversus,' past participle of 'convertere' (to turn about, reverse).

2. VERB

/ kənˈvɝːs / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːs / (BrE)
to engage in conversation; to talk informally with someone
talk in conversation

Examples

The two scientists conversed for hours about their research.
She enjoys conversing with people from different cultures.
We conversed quietly while waiting for the train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly, informal way
  • Synonym: to discuss - to talk about something with someone

Etymology

From Latin 'conversari' (to associate with), from 'con-' (with) + 'versari' (to turn, to move about, to dwell).

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnˌvɜrs / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvɜːs / (BrE)
(of words, terms, or relations) having a reversed relationship so that one denotes the relation of the other in reverse (e.g. ‘parental’ is converse to ‘filial’).
having a reversed relational sense

Examples

‘Parent’ and ‘child’ are converse terms in familial relationships.
In logic, the statement and its converse may not have the same truth value.
‘Give’ and ‘receive’ have a converse relationship in semantics.

Synset Examples

‘parental’ and ‘filial’ are converse terms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverse - opposite in order, direction, or relation
  • Synonym: reciprocal - mutually corresponding or reversed in relation

Etymology

From Latin 'conversus', past participle of 'convertere' (to turn around).
