cobnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑbˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkɒbˌnʌt / (BrE)
the edible nut of certain hazel trees, especially the cultivated varieties of Corylus avellana
edible hazel nut
british

Examples

We picked fresh cobnuts from the trees in the garden.
The recipe calls for crushed cobnuts to add a unique flavor.
During autumn, baskets of cobnuts are sold at the local market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the round nut of the hazel tree

Etymology

From 'cob' (a round object) + 'nut', used since the 15th century in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑbˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkɒb.nʌt / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of hazelnut, especially the nut or the tree producing it, grown mainly in Britain and parts of Europe
cultivated variety of hazelnut tree/nut

Examples

Every autumn, we gather fresh cobnuts from the orchard.
The cobnut tree is prized for its clusters of edible nuts.
She baked a delicious loaf using flour and chopped cobnuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the nut of the hazel tree, closely related to the cobnut

Etymology

From English dialect 'cob' (a round mass) + 'nut'.
