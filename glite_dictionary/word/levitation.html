levitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛvəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɛvɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of rising or causing something to rise and float in the air, often without visible means of support
rising and floating in the air

Examples

The magician amazed the audience with his levitation act.
Scientists have demonstrated levitation using powerful magnets to lift objects.
Stories of levitation often appear in historical accounts of mystics and spiritualists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floatation - the process of floating or making something float

Etymology

From Latin 'levitas' (lightness) and French 'lévitation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌlevɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɛvɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the phenomenon of rising or floating in the air, especially by apparently supernatural or magical means
rising into the air supernaturally or magically

Examples

The magician amazed the audience with his dramatic levitation trick.
Legends often tell of monks achieving levitation during deep meditation.
The film depicted levitation as a sign of supernatural power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floatation - the act or process of floating or being kept afloat
  • Antonym: gravity - the natural force that causes things to fall toward the earth

Etymology

From Latin 'levitas' (lightness) and French 'lévitation'.
