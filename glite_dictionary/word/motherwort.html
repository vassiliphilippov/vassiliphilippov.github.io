motherwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a bitter, aromatic Old World herb with toothed leaves and white or pale pink flowers, commonly found in hedgerows and woodland margins; sometimes used in herbal medicine
a bitter aromatic herb of the mint family

Examples

Many herbalists recommend using motherwort to support women's reproductive health.
The motherwort in her garden grew tall and blossomed with delicate pink flowers.
He identified the plant as motherwort by its jagged leaves and square stem.

Etymology

From Middle English 'moderworte', from 'moder' (mother) and 'wort' (herb, plant), referring to its use in traditional medicine for women.
