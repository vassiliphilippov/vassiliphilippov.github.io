capitalist roadist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪst ˈroʊdɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst ˈrəʊdɪst / (BrE)
A person who advocates capitalism, particularly as an ideological perspective or as a supporter of policies favoring privatization and market-based approaches; originally used in a derogatory sense within Chinese political discourse to refer to those seen as straying from socialist principles.
advocate of capitalism (esp. in political context)

Examples

Some critics accuse the government of being influenced by capitalist roadists in economic reforms.
During the debate, she was labeled a capitalist roadist for promoting free-market policies.
The concept of the capitalist roadist emerged during ideological struggles in China.

Synset Examples

The capitalist roadist agenda focuses on privatization and deregulation.
The capitalist roadist perspective is gaining traction among young entrepreneurs.
She identifies as a capitalist roadist, advocating for market-based solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalist - a person who supports or believes in capitalism
  • Antonym: socialist - a person who supports or believes in socialism

Etymology

Formed from 'capitalist' + 'road' + '-ist', modelled on Chinese political terminology (走资本主义道路的人, literally 'person taking the capitalist road').
