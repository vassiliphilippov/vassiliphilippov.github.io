conditional

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nəl / (BrE)
subject to one or more conditions or qualifications; not absolute or definite
subject to conditions or reservations

Examples

Her acceptance of the job offer was conditional on receiving a higher salary.
You may go to the party, but my permission is conditional upon your finishing your homework first.
The ceasefire is only conditional; it will remain in place as long as both sides follow the agreement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditional - not subject to any conditions
  • Synonym: qualified - limited or restricted in some way

Etymology

From Late Latin conditionalis, from Latin conditio (agreement, condition).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
dependent on or subject to one or more conditions being met.
dependent on a condition

Examples

His offer of help was conditional on receiving an apology.
The contract is only conditional upon successful completion of the trial period.
Their support for the proposal was strictly conditional.

Synset Examples

conditional acceptance of the terms
lent conditional support
the conditional sale will not be complete until the full purchase price is paid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditional - not subject to any conditions; absolute

Etymology

From Latin 'condicionālis', from 'condicio' (agreement, condition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00557545-a
Synset POS: a
