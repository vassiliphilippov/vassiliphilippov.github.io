Citrus nobilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs noʊˈbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs nəʊˈbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A citrus tree native to Asia, known for its large, sweet, easy-to-peel orange fruit; commonly cultivated in warm climates.
species of citrus tree with sweet orange fruit

Examples

Citrus nobilis is prized for its sweet, easily peeled fruit in many Asian markets.
The grove specializes in growing Citrus nobilis for export to international supermarkets.
Researchers are studying the disease resistance of Citrus nobilis compared to other citrus varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin orange - small sweet citrus fruit of the same species
  • Synonym: Citrus reticulata - scientific name for the mandarin orange, closely related species

Etymology

Latin: 'citrus' (citrus tree) + 'nobilis' (noble, distinguished).
