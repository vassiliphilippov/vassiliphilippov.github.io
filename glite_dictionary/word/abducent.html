abducent

1. ADJ

/ æbˈdusənt / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːsənt / (BrE)
(of a muscle) causing movement away from the midline of the body or from an adjacent part.
drawing away from the midline (esp. muscles)
medical

Examples

The abducent muscles are responsible for moving the limbs away from the central axis of the body.
During physical therapy, exercises targeting abducent muscles can help improve mobility.
Damage to abducent muscles may result in limited ability to move the limb outward.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adducent - (of a muscle) drawing toward the midline of the body or adjacent part

Etymology

From Latin 'abducent-', present participle of 'abducere' (to lead away).

2. NOUN

/ æbˈduːsənt / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːsənt / (BrE)
a cranial nerve (the sixth nerve) that controls the lateral rectus muscle of the eye, responsible for moving the eye outward
sixth cranial nerve of the eye
medical

Examples

The abducent is frequently examined in patients with double vision.
Damage to the abducent may cause an inability to move the eye laterally.
The medical student learned that the abducent controls the lateral rectus muscle of the eye.

Etymology

From Latin abducent-, present participle of 'abducere' meaning 'to lead away.'
