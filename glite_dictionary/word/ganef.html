ganef

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːnəf / (AmE), / ˈɡænəf / (BrE)
(informal) A thief or unscrupulous, dishonest person; a scoundrel.
a thief or dishonest person
informal

Examples

The art dealer turned out to be a real ganef, cheating clients out of thousands.
You can't trust that ganef with your money; he's always up to something shady.
She called him a ganef when she discovered her wallet was missing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crook - a person who is dishonest or a criminal
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals

Etymology

From Yiddish 'ganef', from Hebrew 'gannābh' (thief).
