cordon bleu

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˌdɑn ˈblu / (AmE), / kɔːˌdɒ̃ ˈblɜː / (BrE)
an honor or distinction awarded for outstanding excellence, originally referring to the highest order of knighthood in France.
a mark of highest distinction or honor

Examples

Winning the Nobel Prize is often considered a cordon bleu of academic achievement.
The chef wore his cordon bleu with pride, signifying his distinction in the culinary world.
Achieving such a cordon bleu was a lifetime goal for many ambitious artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - a special honor or recognition
  • Synonym: award - a prize or honor given to recognize achievement
  • Antonym: disgrace - loss of honor or respect; opposite of distinction

Etymology

From French, literally 'blue ribbon'; originally the highest order of knighthood in France, L'Ordre du Saint-Esprit.
