shoddiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑd.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɒd.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being badly made or done, especially as a result of carelessness or poor materials.
poor quality or workmanship

Examples

The shoddiness of the repair work was obvious when the handle fell off again.
Critics pointed out the shoddiness in the film's special effects and set design.
She was frustrated by the shoddiness of service she received from the company.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Synonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in quality or value

Etymology

From 'shoddy,' meaning inferior material or workmanship, likely originating in 19th-century English textile industry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑd.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɒd.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being poorly made, inferior, or cheaply imitative of something better
poor quality or workmanship

Examples

The shoddiness of the construction became clear after the first heavy rainfall.
Customers returned the product, complaining about its shoddiness.
He was frustrated by the shoddiness he encountered in mass-produced goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in quality or value
  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good

Etymology

From 'shoddy' (poor quality material or workmanship) + -ness (forming nouns of quality or state).
