underwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌwʊd / (BrE)
the low-growing trees, bushes, and plants that grow beneath taller trees in a wood or forest
low vegetation beneath taller trees

Examples

The forest floor was thick with underwood, making it hard to walk through.
Deer often hide in the underwood to avoid predators.
They cleared away the dense underwood to plant new saplings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: understory - the layer of vegetation beneath the forest canopy

Etymology

From Old English underwudu, meaning 'wood beneath' or 'lower forest growth'.
