Huainaputina

1. PROPN

/ waɪˌnɑːpuːˈtiːnə / (AmE), / waɪˌnɑːpʊˈtiːnə / (BrE)
an inactive volcano in the Andes of southern Peru, noted for its catastrophic eruption in 1600
inactive volcano in southern Peru

Examples

The eruption of Huainaputina in 1600 was one of the most powerful volcanic events in South American history.
Many geologists study Huainaputina to understand the effects of large volcanic eruptions on climate.
The summit of Huainaputina offers a panoramic view of the surrounding Andean landscape.

Etymology

From Quechua, likely meaning 'young volcano'.
