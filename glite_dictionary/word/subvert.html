to subvert

1. VERB

/ səbˈvɝt / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to undermine or destroy the power, authority, or effectiveness of a system, institution, or belief, often in a secret or gradual way
undermine or overthrow authority or systems

Examples

The dictator attempted to subvert the election process through propaganda and intimidation.
Foreign powers were accused of trying to subvert the government from within.
He feared that radical ideas could eventually subvert traditional values.

Synset Examples

we must not let our civil liberties be subverted by the current crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undermine - to weaken or lessen the effectiveness or power of something

Etymology

From Latin 'subvertere', from 'sub-' (under) + 'vertere' (to turn).

4. VERB

/ səbˈvɝt / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to undermine, weaken, or destroy the power, authority, or effectiveness of something, especially a system or established institution
undermine or overthrow a system or authority

Examples

The organization tried to subvert the government's authority through propaganda and secret meetings.
Hackers can subvert a website by exploiting security flaws.
He accused his rivals of attempting to subvert the company’s operations from within.

Synset Examples

The Resistance sabotaged railroad operations during the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undermine - to weaken or gradually destroy the foundation or effectiveness of something
  • Synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy or obstruct something, especially for political or military advantage
  • Antonym: support - to give assistance or aid; to help keep something up

Etymology

From Latin subvertere (to overturn, overthrow), from sub- (under) + vertere (to turn).
