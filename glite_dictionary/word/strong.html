strong

1. ADJ

/ strɔŋ / (AmE), / strɒŋ / (BrE)
having strength, power, or intensity greater than average or expected
having greater than average strength or power

Examples

He has a strong voice that can be heard across the room.
The bridge must be strong enough to support heavy traffic.
This medicine is too strong for children and should be given with caution.

Synset Examples

a strong radio signal
strong medicine
a strong man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great power or strength
  • Antonym: weak - lacking strength or power

Etymology

From Old English 'strang' (strong, severe, bold), related to Dutch 'streng' and German 'streng'.

6. ADJ

/ strɔŋ / (AmE), / strɒŋ / (BrE)
(of a verb) having inflection that does not follow the regular pattern, often using vowel changes rather than adding -ed for the past tense and past participle; irregular.
not having regular verb inflection

Examples

In German, strong verbs change their vowel in the past tense.
The verb 'swim' is a strong verb because its past form is 'swam'.
Linguists classify verbs like 'sing' as strong due to their irregular conjugation patterns.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weak - (of verbs) having regular inflection, usually by adding -ed in the past tense
