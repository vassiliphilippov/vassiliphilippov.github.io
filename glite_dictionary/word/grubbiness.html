grubbiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌbinəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbɪnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being dirty or grimy
the state of being dirty or grimy

Examples

The grubbiness of the city streets after the festival was hard to ignore.
His shirt showed signs of grubbiness from working outside all day.
She disliked the grubbiness that seemed to cling to the abandoned house.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean
  • Synonym: grimy - covered with or characterized by grime

Etymology

From 'grubby' + '-ness', where 'grubby' comes from 'grub' (an insect larvae) + '-y' (forming adjectives).
