revitalised

1. ADJ

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / riˈvaɪtəlaɪzd / (BrE)
restored to new life, energy, or strength; made active or effective again
restored to new life or vigor
british

Examples

The city center was revitalised after years of neglect.
The company has been revitalised by new leadership and investment.
Many artists moved into the revitalised district, making it a cultural hotspot.

Synset Examples

a revitalized economy
a revitalized inner-city neighborhood
Berlin has been reborn after probably the most intense period of construction since the post-war period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revitalized - restored to new life or vigor (American spelling)
  • Synonym: restored - brought back to a former or original state
  • Antonym: depleted - reduced in quality, quantity, or vitality

Etymology

From 'revitalise' (British spelling), from re- (again) + vital (life-giving) + -ise.
