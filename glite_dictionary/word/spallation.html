spallation

1. NOUN

/ spəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / spəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a nuclear reaction in which a nucleus hit by a high-energy particle breaks up into several smaller pieces or particles
nuclear reaction where a nucleus splits into many parts

Examples

The scientists studied spallation to understand the creation of certain elements in space.
Cosmic rays can cause spallation in the Earth's atmosphere, producing a variety of particles.
Recent experiments have measured the effects of spallation at particle accelerators.

Synset Examples

some astronomers believe that the solar system was formed by spallation when the sun was a very young star

Etymology

From 'spall' (to break off fragments) + '-ation', modeled after similar scientific formation.
