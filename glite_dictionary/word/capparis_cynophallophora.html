Capparis cynophallophora

1. NOUN

/ kæˈpɛrɪs saɪˌnoʊfəˈlɒfərə / (AmE), / kəˈpærɪs sɪˌnɒfəˈlɒfərə / (BrE)
A shrub native to southern Florida and the West Indies, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, with fragrant flowers and edible fruit.
shrub native to southern Florida and the West Indies

Examples

The Capparis cynophallophora is often planted in tropical gardens for its attractive blossoms.
Ecologists are studying the role of Capparis cynophallophora in supporting native pollinators.
Fruit from the Capparis cynophallophora is sometimes eaten by birds in its native habitat.

Etymology

From Latin; Capparis (the caper plant genus) and cynophallophora (from Greek 'kynos' meaning 'dog' and 'phallos' meaning 'phallus', referring to the shape of the fruit).
