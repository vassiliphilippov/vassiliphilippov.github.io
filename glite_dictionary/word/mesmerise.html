to mesmerise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛzməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛzməraɪz / (BrE)
to attract and hold the attention of someone as if by magic or hypnotic power
hold attention as if by magic

Examples

The magician managed to mesmerise the entire crowd with her performance.
Children were mesmerised by the bright colors and fast movements of the parade.
His deep voice seemed to mesmerise everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

She magnetized the audience with her tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest and attention of; charm
  • Synonym: to fascinate - to attract the strong attention and interest of; charm
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or dull; the opposite of captivate

Etymology

From the name of Franz Mesmer (1734–1815), an Austrian physician who theorized about animal magnetism.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛzməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛzməraɪz / (BrE)
to induce a trance-like state of hypnosis in someone; to hypnotize
to hypnotize or induce hypnosis

Examples

The skilled hypnotist was able to mesmerise several volunteers in the audience with ease.
She tried to mesmerise her client using gentle, rhythmic speech and a swinging pendulum.
During the demonstration, the doctor mesmerised the patient until he fell into a deep trance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hypnotize - to induce a state of hypnosis in someone

Etymology

From the name of Franz Mesmer, an 18th-century German physician associated with hypnotism.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00020664-v
Synset POS: v
