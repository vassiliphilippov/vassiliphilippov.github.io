rettery

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtəri / (AmE), / ˈrɛtəri / (BrE)
A place or establishment where flax is retted.
place where flax is retted

Examples

The old rettery by the river was once vital for the local textile industry.
After harvesting, the flax was transported to the rettery for processing.
Environmental regulations now control the waste from every rettery in the region.

Etymology

From 'ret' (to soak flax or hemp to loosen fibers) + '-ery' (denoting a place of activity).
