harpsichord

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrpsɪˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpsɪˌkɔːd / (BrE)
A keyboard musical instrument with strings that are plucked by plectra when the keys are pressed, producing a distinctive, bright sound.
keyboard instrument with plucked strings

Examples

The musician played a beautiful melody on the harpsichord during the Baroque concert.
Unlike the piano, the harpsichord produces sound by plucking its strings.
Several pieces by Bach were originally composed for the harpsichord.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: piano - Modern keyboard instrument in which strings are struck by hammers

Etymology

From French *clavecin*, from Italian *clavicembalo*, ultimately from Latin *clavis* ('key') and *chorda* ('string').
