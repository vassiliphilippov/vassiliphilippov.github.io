to decimate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to kill or destroy a large proportion of a group or population
kill or destroy in large numbers

Examples

The wildfire decimated large areas of forest in just a few hours.
War and famine decimated the country's population over the past decade.
An invasive disease can decimate crops if not controlled quickly.

Synset Examples

the plague wiped out an entire population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to damage something so badly it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Synonym: to devastate - to cause great destruction or damage to something

Etymology

From Latin 'decimare', meaning 'to take a tenth', originally referring to killing one in every ten as punishment in a Roman military unit.

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪmeɪt / (BrE)
(historical) To kill one in every ten (of a group of people), especially as a punishment used in the Roman army.
kill one in every ten (historical)

Examples

The general ordered his men to decimate the mutinous legion as a warning to others.
In ancient Rome, commanders would sometimes decimate a unit to punish rebellion.
Historical records confirm that the Romans would decimate their own troops for grave offenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Etymology

From Latin decimare (to take a tenth), from decimus (tenth).
