philosophicalness

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlɑːsəˈfɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒsəˈfɪkəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being philosophical, especially showing calmness or composure in the face of difficulty or loss.
quality of being philosophical, calm in trouble

Examples

She maintained her philosophicalness even after receiving the disappointing news.
His philosophicalness helped the team stay calm during the crisis.
The philosophicalness of her reply impressed everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosophy - the study of the fundamental nature of knowledge, reality, and existence
  • Synonym: philosophical - relating to philosophy or having a calm, reasoned attitude

Etymology

From 'philosophical' + '-ness', forming an abstract noun.
