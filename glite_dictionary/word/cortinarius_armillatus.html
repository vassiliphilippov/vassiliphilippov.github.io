Cortinarius armillatus

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˌtɪniˈɛriəs ɑrˌmɪˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / kɔːˌtɪniˈeəriəs ɑːˌmɪlɪˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of mushroom with large tawny caps, pale cinnamon gills, and a distinctive red band of veil around the stalk, typically found near birch trees.
a species of mushroom often found near birch trees

Examples

The mycologist discovered a cluster of Cortinarius armillatus near the edge of the birch grove.
Many foragers recognize Cortinarius armillatus by its red band and large tawny cap.
Photographs of Cortinarius armillatus often highlight the pale cinnamon color of its gills.

Etymology

From Latin: 'cortinarius' meaning 'curtained' (referring to the web-like veil), and species name 'armillatus' meaning 'braceleted'.
