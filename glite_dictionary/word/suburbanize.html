to suburbanize

1. VERB

/ səˈbɝː.bə.naɪz / (AmE), / səˈbɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
to give a place or area the characteristics of a suburb, or to make it more like a suburb, typically through increased residential development and outward expansion from a city center
make or become more like a suburb

Examples

The town began to suburbanize rapidly in the 1980s as families moved out of the city.
Over the past decade, the area has suburbanized with new housing developments and shopping centers.
If unchecked, increased development will continue to suburbanize the once-rural landscape.

Synset Examples

the city suburbanized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to urbanize - to make or become more urban

Etymology

From 'suburb' + '-anize', formed in analogy to 'urbanize'.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ səˈbɝː.bəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / səˈbɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
To make an area suburban in character, often by developing housing and infrastructure typical of suburbs.
make suburban in character

Examples

Many towns have begun to suburbanize as more people move out of the city centers.
Over the last decade, developers suburbanized large areas of farmland near the highway.
The local government is considering policies to prevent the region from becoming overly suburbanized.

Synset Examples

highly suburbanized cities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to urbanize - to make or become urban in character

Etymology

Formed from 'suburban' + '-ize', meaning to make or become suburban; first recorded in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
