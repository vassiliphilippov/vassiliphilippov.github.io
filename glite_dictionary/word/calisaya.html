calisaya

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈsaɪə / (AmE), / ˌkalɪˈsaɪə / (BrE)
A Peruvian shrub or small tree (Cinchona calisaya) with large glossy leaves and fragrant yellow, green, or red flowers, cultivated for its medicinal bark.
Peruvian medicinal shrub/tree

Examples

The bark of the calisaya tree has long been valued for its medicinal properties.
Botanists identified the rare calisaya growing high in the Peruvian Andes.
Traditional remedies often used powdered calisaya bark to treat fevers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinchona - any of several trees of the genus Cinchona, the bark of which yields quinine

Etymology

From Spanish 'calisaya', probably of indigenous South American origin, referring to the tree's bark.
