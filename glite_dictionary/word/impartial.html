impartial

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑːrʃəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːʃəl / (BrE)
Not supporting one person or group more than another; treating all rivals or disputants equally.
not biased; fair and neutral

Examples

A good mediator must remain impartial throughout the negotiation process.
Journalists are expected to report the news in an impartial manner.
It’s important that judges be impartial and objective when making decisions.

Synset Examples

the cold neutrality of an impartial judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbiased - not showing bias; impartial
  • Antonym: biased - showing an unfair preference; not impartial

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'partialis' (partial).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːʃəl / (BrE)
Treating all sides or people equally; not influenced by personal feelings or bias.
not influenced by bias; fair

Examples

The judge was known for his impartial decisions in the courtroom.
To ensure an impartial review, the committee included people from different backgrounds.
It is important that news reporters remain impartial when covering political events.

Synset Examples

an unprejudiced appraisal of the pros and cons
the impartial eye of a scientist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • Synonym: neutral - not supporting or helping either side in a conflict

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'impartiallis', from Latin 'im-' (not) + 'partialis' (partial).
