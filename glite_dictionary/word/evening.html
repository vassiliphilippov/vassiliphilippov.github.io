evening

1. NOUN

/ ˈivnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːvnɪŋ / (BrE)
the final stage or concluding period of a process, era, or entity, often used metaphorically
final or concluding period

Examples

Many historians regard the Renaissance as the evening of the Middle Ages.
His latest works were written in the evening of his career.
The reforms came during the evening of the empire's power.

Synset Examples

it was the evening of the Roman Empire

Etymology

From Old English *ǣfnung* (the coming of evening), based on *ǣfen* (evening).

2. NOUN

/ ˈivnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːvnɪŋ / (BrE)
The part of the day from late afternoon until nightfall, when daylight gradually decreases.
the time from late afternoon to nightfall

Examples

We went for a walk in the evening to enjoy the cool air.
She likes to read every evening before bed.
The sky turned pink and orange as the evening approached.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed the evening light across the lake

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morning - the early part of the day, from sunrise until noon
  • Degree Lower: afternoon - the time from noon until early evening
  • Degree Higher: night - the period of darkness between sunset and sunrise

Etymology

From Old English 'æfnung', related to 'æfen' (even, eve, the time between sunset and darkness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈiv.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːv.nɪŋ / (BrE)
used as a polite expression for greeting or taking leave in the evening, often as 'Good evening'.
polite greeting or farewell at night

Examples

She smiled and said 'evening' as she entered the room.
I nodded a quick 'evening' to my neighbors on the porch.
He waved goodbye with a cheerful 'evening' before heading home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good evening - a polite greeting said in the evening

4. NOUN

/ ˈiːvnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːv.nɪŋ / (BrE)
An occasion or event that takes place in the early part of the night, from dinner until bedtime, often spent in a special way.
event or occasion in the early night

Examples

We had a wonderful evening at the theater last night.
She spent the evening reading her favorite book by the fireplace.
Their anniversary evening was full of laughter and music.

Synset Examples

an evening at the opera

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: night - the period of darkness in each twenty-four hours; the time from sunset to sunrise
  • Degree Lower: afternoon - the part of the day from noon to evening

Etymology

From Old English 'æfnung', meaning the coming of evening, related to 'æfnian' (to become evening).
