programming

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊɡræmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡræmɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of arranging or scheduling planned events, especially by setting their order and times.
arranging planned events in order and time

Examples

The festival's programming includes concerts, lectures, and art exhibitions throughout the week.
Effective programming of television shows can increase a channel's audience.
She is responsible for the programming of activities at the community center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheduling - the act of planning or arranging events at specific times

Etymology

From 'program' + '-ing', based on Greek 'programma' (a written public notice).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊɡræmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡramɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of writing instructions for a computer to perform specific tasks or solve problems
writing computer instructions

Examples

She spent the entire weekend programming a new mobile app for her company.
Learning programming can open many career opportunities in the tech industry.
Modern programming often involves collaborating with others on complex projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coding - the activity of writing code for computers
  • Synonym: debugging - the process of finding and fixing errors in computer programs

Etymology

From 'program' + '-ing', where 'program' comes from Greek 'programma' meaning 'a written public notice'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00930762-n
Synset POS: n
