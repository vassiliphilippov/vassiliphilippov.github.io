Matteuccia struthiopteris

1. NOUN

/ məˌtuː.tʃi.ə ˌstrʌθ.iˈɒp.tɪ.rɪs / (AmE), / mætˌtjuː.tʃi.ə ˌstrʌθ.iˈɒp.tɪ.rɪs / (BrE)
A tall fern native to northern temperate regions, known for its graceful, arching fronds and ostrich plume-like fertile leaves.
tall northern fern, ostrich fern

Examples

The Matteuccia struthiopteris thrives in moist woodlands and is often used in shade gardens.
Gardeners prize the Matteuccia struthiopteris for its lush, feathery foliage that resembles ostrich plumes.
In spring, the young fiddleheads of Matteuccia struthiopteris are sometimes harvested for culinary use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich fern - Common name for Matteuccia struthiopteris

Etymology

From Italian botanist Carlo Matteucci (Matteuccia) and Greek 'strouthion' (ostrich) + 'pteris' (fern).
