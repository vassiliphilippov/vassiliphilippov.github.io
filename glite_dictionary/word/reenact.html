to reenact

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.ɪˈnækt / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪˈnækt / (BrE)
to act out or perform (an event, especially a past event) again; to enact once more
to act out or perform again

Examples

Every summer, volunteers reenact scenes from the town's colonial past.
The students reenacted the famous courtroom trial for their history project.
During the festival, actors reenact the legendary battle for the audience.

Synset Examples

They reenacted the battle of Princeton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enact - to act out or perform; to make into law

Etymology

From re- (again) + enact; based on 'enact' meaning 'to act out' or 'to make into law'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01726692-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪˈnækt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈnækt / (BrE)
to make (a law or regulation) valid again after it has lapsed, or to perform or repeat the actions of a past event
enact again; repeat or perform again

Examples

Congress decided to reenact the legislation that had expired last year.
The students reenacted the famous battle for their history project.
Every year, the town reenacts scenes from its founding for the local festival.

Synset Examples

Congress reenacted the law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enact - to make into law

Etymology

From re- (again) + enact.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02433049-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriː.ɪˈnækt / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪˈnækt / (BrE)
to act out or perform again, especially as if in a play or as a recreation of an earlier event
to act out or perform again

Examples

The students reenacted the signing of the Declaration of Independence for their history project.
Police sometimes reenact crimes to better understand how they happened.
During the festival, actors reenact the famous battle to educate and entertain visitors.

Synset Examples

She reenacted what had happened earlier that day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reenactment - the act of performing an event again, especially as a recreation

Etymology

From re- (again) + enact (to act out, to make into law).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01726324-v
Synset POS: v
