offshoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈɒfʃuːt / (BrE)
something that develops from something else, especially as a result or by-product of a process or activity
something that develops from something else

Examples

The new committee was an offshoot of the original environmental group.
Modern jazz is often seen as an offshoot of earlier musical traditions.
The software started as an offshoot from a university research project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by-product - an incidental or secondary product
  • Synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence
  • Antonym: mainstream - the principal or dominant direction

Etymology

Formed from 'off' + 'shoot', originally used for plant shoots.
