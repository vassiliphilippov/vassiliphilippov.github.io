boding

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
a sense or feeling that something bad or unpleasant will happen
sense of impending evil or misfortune

Examples

A sense of boding hung over the town as the dark clouds gathered.
He entered the room with a boding that something was terribly wrong.
Her words left a boding in my mind that I could not shake.

Synset Examples

a steadily escalating sense of foreboding
the lawyer had a presentiment that the judge would dismiss the case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreboding - a strong feeling that something bad will happen
  • Synonym: presentiment - an intuitive feeling about the future, often something bad

Etymology

From the verb 'bode', from Old English 'bodian' (to announce, foretell).
