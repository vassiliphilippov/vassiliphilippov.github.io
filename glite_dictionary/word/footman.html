footman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtmən / (AmE), / ˈfʊtmən / (BrE)
a male servant, especially in a large house, responsible for running errands and performing various household duties
a male household servant

Examples

The manor employed a footman to greet visitors at the door and assist the butler.
In period dramas, a footman is often shown dressed in livery and serving at the dining table.
As a young footman, he was proud of his neatly polished shoes and formal uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person employed to perform duties for others, especially in a household

Etymology

From foot + man, originally referring to a servant who ran beside or attended his master's carriage on foot.
