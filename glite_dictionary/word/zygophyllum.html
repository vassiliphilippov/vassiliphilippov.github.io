Zygophyllum

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈɡɒfɪləm / (AmE), / zaɪˈɡɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical herbs or shrubs, often with ill-smelling foliage, some of whose flower buds are used as capers (bean capers).
genus of tropical herbs and shrubs

Examples

Zygophyllum species are often found in arid and semi-arid regions across the world.
The flower buds of certain Zygophyllum plants are collected and preserved as bean capers in some cuisines.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety of Zygophyllum growing in the coastal desert.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'zygon' (yoke) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the paired leaves

2. NOUN

/ zaɪˈɡɑːfɪləm / (AmE), / zaɪˈɡɒfɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical or subtropical herbs or shrubs with ill-smelling foliage, some species of which have flower buds used as capers
genus of tropical/subtropical herbs or shrubs

Examples

Several species of Zygophyllum grow in arid regions and are valued for their drought resistance.
The ill-smelling leaves are a characteristic feature of the genus Zygophyllum.
Flower buds from Zygophyllum are sometimes pickled and used as a condiment in local cuisines.

Etymology

From Greek zygon (yoke) and phyllon (leaf), referring to the paired leaflets.
