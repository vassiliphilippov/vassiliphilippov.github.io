schottische

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑˈtiʃ / (AmE), / ʃɒˈtɪʃ / (BrE)
a round dance of German origin similar to a slow polka, typically danced in quick steps and hops
a German folk dance resembling a slow polka

Examples

The band played a lively schottische at the village festival.
They learned the steps to the schottische during their folk dance class.
Many traditional dances, including the schottische, were performed at the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polka - a lively dance of Bohemian origin with quick steps

Etymology

From German 'Schottisch', meaning 'Scottish', reflecting the supposed Scottish origin of the dance.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɑˈtiʃ / (AmE), / ʃɒˈtiʃ / (BrE)
Music composed or performed for dancing the schottische, a type of traditional partnered dance.
music for the schottische dance

Examples

The band played a lively schottische at the folk festival.
She requested a traditional schottische for the next dance.
The collection includes several classic schottisches popular in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polka - lively dance music of Bohemian origin

Etymology

From German 'Schottisch', meaning 'Scottish', referring to the origin of the dance.
