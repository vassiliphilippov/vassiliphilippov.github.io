guilloche

1. NOUN

/ ɡiˈloʊʃ / (AmE), / ɡiˈlɒʃ / (BrE)
An architectural ornament or decorative pattern consisting of two or more interlacing wavy bands.
ornamental pattern of interlaced wavy bands

Examples

The ancient Greek vase was adorned with a fine guilloche pattern around its rim.
Intricate guilloche designs are often found on banknotes and official documents as a security feature.
The architect incorporated a classical guilloche frieze into the building's façade.

Etymology

From French 'guilloché', past participle of 'guillocher' meaning 'to ornament by engine-turning'.
