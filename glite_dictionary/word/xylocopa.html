Xylocopa

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɑkəpə / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɒkəpə / (BrE)
A genus of large bees commonly known as carpenter bees, noted for their habit of boring into wood to make nests.
genus of carpenter bees

Examples

The genus Xylocopa includes many species known for nesting in wood.
Researchers observed Xylocopa constructing tunnels in dead tree branches.
Unlike honey bees, Xylocopa are solitary and do not form large colonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpenter bee - a common name for bees of the genus Xylocopa

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood) + kopos (labor), referring to their wood-boring behavior.
