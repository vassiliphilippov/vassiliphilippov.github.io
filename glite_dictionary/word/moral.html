moral

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
concerned with the principles of right and wrong behavior or conforming to standards of what is considered right by society or an individual
relating to right and wrong behavior

Examples

She faced a moral dilemma when asked to lie for her friend.
The committee will consider the moral implications of the new policy.
Many people admire her strong moral convictions.

Synset Examples

moral sense
a moral scrutiny
a moral lesson
a moral quandary
moral convictions
a moral life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethical - relating to moral principles
  • Antonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality
  • Antonym: amoral - lacking a moral sense; unconcerned with rightness or wrongness

Etymology

From Latin moralis (relating to manners), from mos, moris (custom, manner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
the lesson or principle that can be learned from a story, event, or experience
lesson from a story or event

Examples

The moral of the story is that honesty is always the best policy.
Each fable ends with a clear moral for readers to consider.
She missed the moral of the tale because she wasn't paying attention.

Synset Examples

the moral of the story is to love thy neighbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesson - something to be learned or taught, especially from an experience or story

Etymology

From Latin moralis (relating to manners, morals).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06618397-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
(of a victory, support, etc.) achieved or given in a way that affects the mind or feelings rather than having a direct physical or tangible effect.
psychological rather than physical in effect

Examples

Although his team lost the final, they still claimed a moral victory.
She appreciated the moral support from her friends during the difficult time.
The protestors offered only moral encouragement, avoiding any physical involvement.

Synset Examples

a moral victory
moral support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological - relating to the mind or emotional state
  • Antonym: physical - relating to the body or tangible reality rather than the mind
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01785141-s
Synset POS: s
