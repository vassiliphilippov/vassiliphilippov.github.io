pachyderma

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæk.ɪˈdɝː.mə / (AmE), / ˌpæk.ɪˈdɜː.mə / (BrE)
abnormal thickening of the skin, especially due to congenital enlargement or obstruction of lymph vessels
abnormal thickening of the skin
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pachyderma after presenting with severe skin thickening on her leg.
Pachyderma is often associated with chronic lymphatic obstruction and may require specialized treatment.
Doctors noted that the pachyderma affected only one extremity, which helped determine the underlying cause.

Etymology

From Greek 'pakhys' (thick) + 'derma' (skin).
