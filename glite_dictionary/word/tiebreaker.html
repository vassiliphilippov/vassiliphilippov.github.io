tiebreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
an extra period or method used to decide the winner when scores are tied, especially in sports or competitions
extra play to decide a tie in a game

Examples

The tennis match was so close that it went to a tiebreaker in the final set.
After both teams finished with equal points, a tiebreaker was used to decide the champion.
He scored the winning goal during the tiebreaker, leading his team to victory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtime - additional play time to decide a game's outcome
  • Antonym: draw - a final result in a game where scores are equal

Etymology

Formed from 'tie' + 'breaker', indicating something that breaks a tie in a score.
