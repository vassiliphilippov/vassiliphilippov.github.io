mujahideen

1. NOUN

/ muˌdʒɑː(h)iˈdin / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑː(h)iːˈdiːn / (BrE)
Muslim guerrilla fighters, especially those engaged in a jihad or holy war, typically against non-Muslim forces or secular governments.
Muslim guerrilla fighters engaged in jihad

Examples

The Afghan mujahideen fought Soviet forces during the 1980s.
Many Western journalists interviewed mujahideen leaders in the mountains near Kabul.
The training camps produced thousands of future mujahideen from across the Muslim world.

Synset Examples

some call the mujahidin international warriors but others just call them terrorists

Etymology

From Arabic 'mujāhidīn', plural of 'mujāhid' (one who struggles in the path of God).

2. NOUN

/ muːˌʤɑːˈdiːn / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑːˈdiːn / (BrE)
Muslim guerrilla fighters, especially those engaged in a jihad or religious war.
Muslim guerrilla fighters

Examples

Many mujahideen fought against Soviet forces in Afghanistan during the 1980s.
International media often refer to Islamic guerrilla fighters as mujahideen.
The government tried to negotiate with the local mujahideen for peace.

Synset Examples

some call the mujahidin international warriors but others just call them terrorists

Etymology

From Arabic مُجَاهِدِين‎ (mujāhidīn), plural of مُجَاهِد‎ (mujāhid, 'one who struggles').
