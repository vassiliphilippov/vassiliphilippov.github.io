slackening

1. NOUN

/ ˈslækənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslækənɪŋ / (BrE)
a process or occurrence in which something becomes less tight, less strong, or less intense
an act of becoming less tight or strong

Examples

There was a sudden slackening of the rope as the weight hit the ground.
The slackening of the company's growth worried investors.
A slackening of the wind allowed the sails to hang loose.

Synset Examples

the relaxation of requirements
the loosening of his grip
the slackening of the wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosening - the process of making something less tight or firm
  • Antonym: tightening - the process of making something tighter or stronger

Etymology

From slacken (to become less tight or intense), from Old English slæc (slack, loose, negligent), with -ing suffix forming a noun.
