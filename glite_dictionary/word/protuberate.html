to protuberate

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtuːbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / prəˈtjuːbəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to cause to bulge out or project
to bulge or stick out

Examples

Heavy rainfall can protuberate the river banks, causing dangerous flooding in some areas.
The old oak tree's roots protuberated from the ground, making the path uneven.
A swelling beneath the skin began to protuberate noticeably, alarming the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - to swell or stick out
  • Synonym: to protrude - to extend beyond or above a surface

Etymology

From Latin protuberare (to swell, bulge out).

2. VERB

/ proʊˈtuːbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / prəˈtjuːbəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to form a rounded bulge or projection; to swell out or protrude
form a rounded bulge or protrusion

Examples

The roots began to protuberate beneath the surface of the soil.
After the injury, his ankle started to protuberate noticeably.
The ancient wall appeared to protuberate where the stones had shifted over time.

Synset Examples

The starved child's belly protuberated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to protrude - extend beyond or above a surface
  • Synonym: bulge - to swell or protrude outward

Etymology

From Latin protuberare (to swell out), from pro- (forward) + tuber (swelling, lump).
