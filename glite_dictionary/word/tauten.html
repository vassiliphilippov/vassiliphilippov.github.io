to tauten

1. VERB

/ ˈtɔtən / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtən / (BrE)
to make or become taut; to tighten or grow more tense
become or make taut; tighten

Examples

The sailor began to tauten the ropes before the storm hit.
When she heard the surprising news, her expression seemed to tauten with anxiety.
The material will tauten as it dries in the sun.

Synset Examples

Your muscles will firm when you exercise regularly
the rope tautened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tighten - to make or become tight or tighter
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make or become less tight

Etymology

From taut + -en, the verb-forming suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00421502-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈtɔtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtn̩ / (BrE)
to make or become taut or tighter
make or become tighter

Examples

You should tauten the tent ropes before the storm hits.
The muscles in her arms tautened as she lifted the heavy box.
Gradually, he tautened the line until it was perfectly straight.

Synset Examples

tauten a rope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to loosen - make less tight or more slack

Etymology

From taut (tight, stretched) + -en (verb-forming suffix).
