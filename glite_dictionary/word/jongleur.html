jongleur

1. NOUN

/ ʒɒŋˈɡlɜr / (AmE), / ʒɒŋˈɡlɜː / (BrE)
a medieval entertainer who sang folk songs, recited poetry, or performed tricks, typically traveling from place to place
medieval entertainer or folk singer
archaic

Examples

The jongleur amazed the villagers with his lively ballads and clever tricks.
In medieval courts, a jongleur often entertained nobles with stories and music.
Legends about the wandering jongleur have inspired many poets and playwrights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minstrel - a medieval musician or singer, especially one who sang or recited poetry

Etymology

From Old French 'jongleur' (entertainer), from Latin 'ioculator' (jester), from 'ioculari' (to jest).
