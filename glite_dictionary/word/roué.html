roué

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈeɪ / (AmE), / ruːˈeɪ / (BrE)
a man who is debauched or morally unrestrained, especially one who is part of fashionable society
dissolute man in society

Examples

The infamous roué charmed his way through London's elite circles, leaving a trail of scandal behind.
Critics described the film's protagonist as a classic roué with no regard for social norms.
He was once a notorious roué, but age and wisdom led him to a quieter life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rake - a man who is habituated to immoral conduct, particularly women and gambling
  • Synonym: libertine - a person, especially a man, who behaves without moral principles or a sense of responsibility

Etymology

From French roué (debauchee, profligate), literally 'broken on the wheel', referring to a supposed punishment deserved by those who live debauched lives.
