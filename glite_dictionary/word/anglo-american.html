Anglo-American

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡləʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
an American who was born in Britain or whose ancestors were British
an American of British birth or ancestry

Examples

Many Anglo-Americans settled in the region during the nineteenth century.
She is proud of her Anglo-American heritage, celebrating both American and British traditions.
The conference brought together Anglo-Americans and other immigrant communities to share their cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglophile - a person who admires England or English customs

Etymology

From Latin 'Anglus' (English) and 'Americanus' (American); compounded in English.
