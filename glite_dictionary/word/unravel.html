to unravel

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈrævəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrævəl / (BrE)
To separate or disentangle the threads of something; to solve or clarify something that is confusing or mysterious.
disentangle or resolve something complicated

Examples

It took hours to unravel all the knots in the rope.
Detectives worked tirelessly to unravel the secret behind the crime.
Sometimes, childhood memories unravel when you least expect them.

Synset Examples

can you unravel the mystery?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to untangle - to make something free from knots or twists
  • Synonym: to solve - to find an answer to a problem or mystery

Etymology

From un- (reverse) + ravel (to tangle), first used in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01524000-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈrævəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈravəl / (BrE)
to separate or cause to separate the threads or fibers of something; to come apart as threads
separate into threads or fibers

Examples

She began to unravel the old sweater to reuse the yarn.
After washing, the edges of the fabric started to unravel.
He tried not to let the rope unravel as he tied the knot.

Synset Examples

unravel the thread

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to ravel - to tangle or entangle threads or fibers

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (reversal) + 'ravel' (to entangle or fray), originally meaning to untangle.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01523477-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈrævəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈravəl / (BrE)
(of cloth, rope, or similar material) to become separated into threads; come apart or undone.
become undone or come apart (material)

Examples

The sleeve began to unravel after it caught on the nail.
If you pull that loose thread, the whole scarf will unravel.
Over time, the rug started to unravel at the edges.

Synset Examples

the sweater unraveled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ravel - to untangle or become untangled

Etymology

From un- (reversal) + ravel (to entangle); original sense 'undo an entanglement'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00333482-v
Synset POS: v
