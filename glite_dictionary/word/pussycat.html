pussycat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊsiˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈpʊsiˌkat / (BrE)
A person who is gentle, easygoing, and agreeable, often appearing kind or harmless.
gentle, easygoing person

Examples

Everyone thinks the new manager is strict, but he's actually a real pussycat at heart.
She may seem intimidating, but get to know her and you'll find she's a pussycat.
Despite his size, Jim turned out to be a pussycat when we talked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softie - a person who is gentle and not harsh or tough

Etymology

Formed from 'pussy' (cat) as a term of endearment or metaphor for a gentle disposition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʊsiˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈpʊsiˌkæt / (BrE)
An informal term for a domestic cat; also used affectionately to refer to a gentle or endearing person.
informal word for domestic cat
informal

Examples

The little pussycat curled up on my lap and started to purr.
Although he acts tough, he's really a pussycat at heart.
A stray pussycat wandered into our garden yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - a small domesticated carnivorous mammal

Etymology

From 'pussy' (cat) + 'cat'; 'pussy' as an affectionate term for cat has uncertain origin, possibly imitative.
