unstilted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstɪltɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɪltɪd / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or behavior) natural, relaxed, and not stiff, formal, or artificial.
natural, relaxed (not stilted)

Examples

Her unstilted laughter put everyone at ease during the formal meeting.
The interview was refreshingly unstilted, making the guest feel comfortable to share personal stories.
He appreciated the unstilted tone of the conversation, which helped build trust.

Synset Examples

unstilted conversation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stilted - unnaturally formal, stiff or artificial, especially in speech or writing

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'stilted'.
