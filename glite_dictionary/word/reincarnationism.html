reincarnationism

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˌkɑrnəˈneɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌriːɪŋˌkɑːnəˈneɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that, after death, the soul migrates to or is reborn in a new body
belief in rebirth of the soul

Examples

Many Eastern religions are based on the principles of reincarnationism.
She studied various philosophies, including reincarnationism, while traveling in India.
The idea of reincarnationism offers a different perspective on life and death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reincarnation - the rebirth of a soul in a new body

Etymology

From 'reincarnation' + '-ism', originating from Latin 're-' (again) + 'incarnare' (to make flesh).
