to enamor

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnæmər / (AmE), / ɪˈnæmə / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel love, admiration, or fascination for someone or something
to inspire love or admiration

Examples

The young musician's talent quickly enamored everyone who heard her play.
He was so enamored with the city that he decided to move there permanently.
The film's unique style enamored critics and audiences alike.

Synset Examples

She captured all the men's hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest of
  • Synonym: charm - to attract and delight someone greatly
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or force away; to cause dislike

Etymology

From Old French 'enamourer', from en- (in) + amour (love), from Latin 'amor'.
