Rodentia

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈdɛnʃə / (AmE), / rəʊˈdɛnʃɪə / (BrE)
the order of small, gnawing mammals that includes rats, mice, squirrels, beavers, and similar animals
order of rodents (gnawing mammals)

Examples

The brown rat is a member of the order Rodentia.
Scientists study Rodentia to better understand mammalian evolution.
Many common household pests belong to Rodentia, including mice and squirrels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a small mammal of the order Rodentia, such as a rat or squirrel

Etymology

From Latin rodentia, from rodere meaning 'to gnaw'.
