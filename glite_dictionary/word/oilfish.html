oilfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a very large deep-water fish of the snake mackerel family, found in tropical and subtropical oceans
large deep-water snake mackerel

Examples

The oilfish is prized by some fishermen for its size and firm flesh.
Consuming too much oilfish can cause digestive discomfort due to its high oil content.
We spotted an enormous oilfish during our deep-sea expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake mackerel - a type of long-bodied deep-sea predatory fish
