Carolinian

1. NOUN

/ ˌker.əˈlɪn.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌkar.əˈlɪn.i.ən / (BrE)
a native or resident of North or South Carolina in the United States
person from the Carolinas

Examples

As a proud Carolinian, she cherished the traditions of her home state.
Many Carolinians travel to the coast for summer vacations every year.
The festival attracted Carolinian artists from both North and South Carolina.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: North Carolinian - a native or resident of North Carolina, USA
  • Degree Lower: South Carolinian - a native or resident of South Carolina, USA

Etymology

Formed from 'Carolina' (name of two US states) + '-ian' (denoting belonging).
