glumness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlʌmnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌmnəs / (BrE)
a feeling or atmosphere of sadness, depression, or low spirits
state of being sad or gloomy

Examples

The glumness in the room was obvious after the team lost the championship.
She tried to shake off her glumness before meeting her friends for dinner.
A sense of glumness hung over the office as news of the layoffs spread.

Synset Examples

gloom pervaded the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloominess - a state or feeling of being gloomy or sad
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'glum' (sullen, gloomy) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlʌmnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌmnɪs / (BrE)
a state of being gloomy or silently unhappy; a moody or downcast feeling
gloomy or downcast feeling

Examples

Her glumness was apparent to everyone after she heard the disappointing news.
He sat in silence, his glumness making the room feel heavy.
Despite the festive occasion, a persistent glumness lingered over the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloom - a feeling or atmosphere of depression or despondency
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'glum' (of obscure origin) + '-ness', forming a noun indicating state or quality.
