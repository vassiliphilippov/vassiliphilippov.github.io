Zithromax

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪθrəˌmɑks / (AmE), / ˈzɪθrəˌmæks / (BrE)
A trademarked antibiotic medication used to treat a wide variety of bacterial infections.
trademarked antibiotic medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Zithromax to help clear up her sinus infection.
He was allergic to penicillin, so he was given Zithromax as an alternative.
Many travelers carry Zithromax in case they develop a bacterial infection abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azithromycin - the generic name for Zithromax, an antibiotic
