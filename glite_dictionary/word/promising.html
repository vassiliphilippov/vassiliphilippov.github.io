promising

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːmɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒmɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
Showing signs of future success or good results.
showing signs of future success

Examples

The young scientist's work is very promising and could lead to a breakthrough.
She received several offers after her promising performance at the competition.
Despite a promising start, the project eventually ran into difficulties.

Synset Examples

had a bright future in publishing
the scandal threatened an abrupt end to a promising political career
a hopeful new singer on Broadway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopeful - showing hope or likely to succeed
  • Antonym: unpromising - not showing signs of future success

Etymology

From promise + -ing, formed from Latin promissum (promise) and English suffix -ing.

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.mɪ.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.sɪŋ / (BrE)
showing signs that success or achievement is likely; likely to develop in a good or successful way
showing signs of future success

Examples

She is a promising artist, already recognized for her unique style.
The company invested in several promising start-ups this year.
He showed promising talent as a violinist from an early age.

Synset Examples

a promising young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopeful - having qualities that inspire hope; likely to succeed

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'promise' (from Latin promissum, a past participle of 'promittere' meaning 'put forth, forward, or promise') plus the '-ing' suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01415143-s
Synset POS: s
