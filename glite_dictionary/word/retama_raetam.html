Retama raetam

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɑːmə ˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / rɛˈtɑːmə ˈrætəm / (BrE)
A desert shrub of Syria and Arabia with small white flowers, sometimes considered the juniper of the Old Testament; occasionally placed in the genus Genista.
desert shrub of Syria and Arabia

Examples

The Retama raetam thrives in the arid regions of the Middle East.
Biblical scholars often identify the juniper mentioned in scripture as Retama raetam.
Botanists sometimes classify Retama raetam within the genus Genista.

Etymology

From Latin 'retama', possibly of Semitic origin; 'raetam' from Hebrew 'retam', a biblical plant.
