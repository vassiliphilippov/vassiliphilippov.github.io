valour

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælər / (AmE), / ˈvælə / (BrE)
great courage in the face of danger, especially in battle or other difficult situations
courage, especially in battle
british

Examples

The knight was renowned for his valour on the battlefield.
She was awarded a cross for her valour during the rescue mission.
Stories of their valour have inspired generations.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valor - great courage in the face of danger (American spelling)
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage or bravery

Etymology

From Old French 'valeur' (worth, bravery), from Latin 'valorem' (strength, value).
