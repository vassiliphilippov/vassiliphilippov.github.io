polish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.lɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪʃ / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth and glossy in appearance or texture
smoothness and glossiness

Examples

The marble floors had a beautiful polish that reflected the light.
Years of handling gave the wood a natural polish and warmth.
A good wax will restore the lost polish to your old car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft, smooth shine on a surface
  • Synonym: lustre - a gentle shine or soft glow, especially of a surface
  • Antonym: dullness - the lack of shine or gloss
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
a high degree of refinement or elegance in manner, performance, or style, resulting from practice or cultivation
refinement or elegance of manner or style

Examples

Her speech was delivered with remarkable polish and confidence.
The polish of the final product set it apart from the competition.
He lacks the polish needed for a professional performance.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refinement - elegance and sophistication in manner or taste
  • Antonym: crudeness - the state of being rough or unrefined

Etymology

From Middle French *polir* (to make smooth), from Latin *polire* (to polish, to make smooth).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
a substance used to make a surface smooth and shiny by rubbing, often applied to shoes, furniture, or cars
a substance for making surfaces shiny

Examples

She applied a layer of polish to her shoes to make them shine.
Use a furniture polish to restore the table's glossy finish.
The car polish removed all the small scratches and made the paint look new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax - a solid substance used to polish or protect surfaces
  • Synonym: cleaner - a substance used for cleaning surfaces
