Polish

1. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊ.lɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Poland, its people, or its culture
relating to Poland or its people

Examples

She cooked a traditional Polish dinner for her friends.
Many Polish artists have gained international recognition.
The Polish language uses a unique alphabet.

Synset Examples

Polish sausage

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Poland - a country in Central Europe

Etymology

From Poland + -ish, denoting origin or affiliation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.lɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪʃ / (BrE)
the West Slavic language spoken primarily in Poland and by Polish communities worldwide
the language of Poland

Examples

She is fluent in both Polish and English.
I am taking a Polish language course at the university.
The instructions were written only in Polish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - relating to the group of languages including Polish, Russian, Czech, etc.

Etymology

From Polish 'polski', relating to Poland.
