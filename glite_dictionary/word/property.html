property

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a quality or characteristic that is inherent in and shared by all members of a particular group or class
essential quality shared by a class

Examples

Scientists are researching the magnetic properties of new materials.
Solubility is a key property of many chemicals used in industry.
One important property of water is its ability to dissolve many substances.

Synset Examples

a study of the physical properties of atomic particles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristic - a typical or noticeable quality of someone or something
  • Synonym: attribute - a quality or feature regarded as a characteristic or inherent part

Etymology

From Old French *propriete*, from Latin *proprietas* (ownership, peculiar quality), from *proprius* (one's own).

Synset Examples
