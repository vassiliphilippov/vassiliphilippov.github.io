judge

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
a public official who has the authority to hear and decide cases in a court of law
public official who decides court cases

Examples

The judge listened carefully to all the evidence before reaching a verdict.
After many years as a lawyer, she was appointed as a judge.
The judge explained the sentence clearly to everyone in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justice - a judge, especially of a higher court
  • Antonym: defendant - person against whom a legal case is brought

Etymology

From Old French juge, from Latin judex (one who declares the law).
