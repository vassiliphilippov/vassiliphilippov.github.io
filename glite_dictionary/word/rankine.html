Rankine

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋkɪn / (AmE), / ˈraŋkɪn / (BrE)
a unit of thermodynamic temperature on the Rankine scale, equal to one degree Fahrenheit above absolute zero
unit of temperature (Rankine scale)

Examples

The temperature was measured as 491.67 Rankine in the experiment.
Engineers sometimes prefer to use Rankine instead of Kelvin for thermodynamic calculations.
Zero Rankine is equivalent to absolute zero on the Fahrenheit temperature scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelvin - SI unit of thermodynamic temperature
  • Synonym: celsius - unit of temperature; centigrade scale
  • Synonym: fahrenheit - temperature scale based on Fahrenheit degrees

Etymology

Named after Scottish engineer and physicist William John Macquorn Rankine (1820–1872).
