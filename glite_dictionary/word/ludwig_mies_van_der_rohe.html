Ludwig Mies van der Rohe

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːdvɪɡ ˈmiːs væn dər ˈroʊə / (AmE), / ˈluːdvɪɡ ˈmiːs van də ˈrəːə / (BrE)
(1886–1969) German-born American architect known for designing unornamented steel frame and glass skyscrapers, influential in modernist architecture.
German-American modernist architect (1886–1969)

Examples

The Barcelona Pavilion was designed by Ludwig Mies van der Rohe for the 1929 International Exposition.
Many contemporary office buildings are inspired by the minimalist principles of Ludwig Mies van der Rohe.
Students of architecture often study the skyscrapers built by Ludwig Mies van der Rohe in Chicago.
