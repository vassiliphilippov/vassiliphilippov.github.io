buxom

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌksəm / (AmE), / ˈbʌksəm / (BrE)
(of a woman) having a full, rounded figure, especially with a large bosom; attractively plump or curvaceous.
full-figured and curvaceous woman

Examples

The actress was praised for her buxom figure in the classic film.
He described the legendary character as a buxom barmaid with a hearty laugh.
Vintage artwork often depicts buxom women as symbols of health and fertility.

Synset Examples

Hollywood seems full of curvaceous blondes
a curvy young woman in a tight dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvaceous - (of a woman) having an attractively curved shape
  • Antonym: slender - thin or gracefully slim in figure

Etymology

From Middle English 'buxom' (obedient, pliant), later associated with health and plumpness.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌksəm / (AmE), / ˈbʌksəm / (BrE)
(of a woman) healthily plump, with a full, shapely figure, and often vigorous or lively.
full-figured and healthy (of a woman)

Examples

The actress was renowned for her buxom figure and cheerful personality.
In classic paintings, many women are depicted as buxom and robust.
A buxom waitress greeted us warmly at the countryside inn.

Synset Examples

a generation ago … buxom actresses were popular

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slender - gracefully thin or slim (of a person)

Etymology

From Middle English 'buxom' (obedient, pliant), later shifting to mean 'healthy and plump.'
