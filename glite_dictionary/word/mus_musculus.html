Mus musculus

1. NOUN

/ mʌs ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs / (AmE), / mʊs ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
a small brownish-grey Old World mouse, now widespread as a common household pest worldwide and frequently used as a laboratory animal
the common house mouse (species)
medical

Examples

Mus musculus is often used in scientific research due to its genetic similarity to humans.
The population of Mus musculus in urban areas increases during the winter.
Researchers observed the behavior of Mus musculus in a controlled environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house mouse - the common mouse found in houses worldwide

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'mouse' (mus) and 'little mouse' (musculus).
