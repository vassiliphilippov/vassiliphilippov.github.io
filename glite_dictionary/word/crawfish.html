crawfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large edible marine crustacean with a spiny shell, related to the lobster but lacking large pincers.
spiny-shelled edible marine crustacean

Examples

Many people in Louisiana enjoy eating crawfish at traditional boils during spring festivals.
The waters near the reef are home to different species of crawfish prized by local fishermen.
The chef prepared a delicious pasta with freshly caught crawfish and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster

Etymology

From craw (cray, alteration of 'crayfish') + fish; influenced by folk etymology connecting it with 'fish.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔːˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster, found in rivers and streams, especially in North America.
freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster

Examples

We caught several crawfish in the creek this afternoon.
The restaurant is famous for its spicy boiled crawfish.
A crawfish darted under a rock as we approached the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster, alternative name

Etymology

Variant of 'crayfish', influenced by folk etymology relating to 'crawl'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster, commonly found in streams and rivers, and often used as food.
small freshwater crustacean
american

Examples

Every spring, people gather along the riverbanks to catch crawfish for a traditional boil.
The restaurant's signature dish features spicy crawfish served with corn and potatoes.
She learned how to peel a crawfish at her first Southern cookout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - another term for small freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster

Etymology

From Middle English 'crevis', from Old French 'crevice' (modern French 'écrevisse'), altered by folk etymology to resemble 'fish'.
