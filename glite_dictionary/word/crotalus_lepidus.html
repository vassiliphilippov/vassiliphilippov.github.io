Crotalus lepidus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈteɪləs ˈlɛpɪdəs / (AmE), / krəʊˈtɑːləs ˈlɛpɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of small rattlesnake, commonly known as the rock rattlesnake, native to Mexico and the southern United States.
species of rock rattlesnake

Examples

The Crotalus lepidus is well adapted to rocky mountainous habitats.
Researchers observed a Crotalus lepidus basking on a sunlit ledge.
Venom from the Crotalus lepidus is less potent than that of some larger rattlesnakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock rattlesnake - Common name for Crotalus lepidus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Crotalus' from Greek 'krotalon' (rattle), 'lepidus' Latin for 'scaly' or 'pleasant'.
