Psyllidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small plant-feeding insects known as jumping plant lice, important in agriculture as some species are pests.
family of jumping plant lice

Examples

Several species in the Psyllidae family are significant pests of fruit trees.
Entomologists have studied the Psyllidae for their impact on agricultural crops.
The Psyllidae are recognized for their powerful jumping legs and small size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psyllid - A member of the Psyllidae family, a jumping plant louse

Etymology

From New Latin Psyllidae, based on Greek psylla (flea), referencing their jumping ability.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small plant-feeding insects known as jumping plant lice, which infest various plants and can be agricultural pests
family of jumping plant lice insects

Examples

The citrus crops were damaged by an infestation of Psyllidae.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Psyllidae to develop better pest control methods.
Several species in the Psyllidae family are notorious for spreading plant diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumping plant louse - insect belonging to the family Psyllidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Psyllidae*, derived from Greek *psylla* (flea), referencing their jumping ability.
