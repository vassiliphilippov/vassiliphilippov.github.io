aberrance

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɛrəns / (AmE), / əˈbɛr(ə)ns / (BrE)
A state or condition that is markedly different from what is normal or expected.
deviation from the norm

Examples

The scientist studied the genetic aberrance found in certain populations.
Such aberrance in behavior was unexpected among the group members.
Any significant aberrance from standard protocol must be reported immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abnormality - deviation from what is normal or usual
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal

Etymology

From Latin aberrāns, present participle of aberrāre (to wander away or deviate).
