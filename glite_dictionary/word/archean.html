Archean

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈkiː.ən / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiː.ən / (BrE)
The geologic era spanning from about 3,800 million to 2,500 million years ago, during which Earth's crust formed and the earliest unicellular life appeared.
geologic era; early Earth; first life forms

Examples

The oldest rocks on Earth are believed to have formed during the Archean.
Scientists study Archean fossils to learn about the planet's earliest life forms.
During the Archean, Earth's atmosphere was very different from what it is today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Proterozoic - the geologic era following the Archean
  • Degree Lower: Hadean - the geologic eon before the Archean

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' meaning 'ancient'.

2. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈkiː.ən / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiː.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to the earliest geological eon (the Archean), or to the oldest known rocks formed during this period, roughly 4,000 to 2,500 million years ago.
pertaining to the earliest geological eon or its rocks

Examples

The Archean rocks contain some of Earth's oldest fossils.
Geologists study Archean formations to understand early continental crust.
Many important mineral deposits are found in Archean greenstone belts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Proterozoic - pertaining to the geological eon following the Archean

Etymology

From Greek arkhaios (ancient), via scientific Latin Archeanus; refers to the ancient era of Earth's geology.
