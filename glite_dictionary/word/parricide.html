parricide

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈpærɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
the act of killing one's own father or mother; a person who commits such a killing
murder of one's parent

Examples

The court found the defendant guilty of parricide after a lengthy trial.
Stories of parricide have appeared in literature since ancient times.
He was horrified by the accusation of parricide and denied any involvement in his parents' deaths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patricide - the killing of one's father
  • Synonym: matricide - the killing of one's mother
  • Synonym: filicide - the killing of one's son or daughter

Etymology

From Latin 'parricida' (parricide, murderer of a near relative), from 'parens' (parent) + 'cida' (killer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpær.ɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈpær.ɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
a person who kills his or her own parent or a close relative
person who kills a parent or close relative

Examples

The police arrested the parricide after a lengthy investigation.
Historically, parricide was considered one of the most heinous crimes.
The novel's main character is a parricide struggling with guilt and remorse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patricide - a person who kills his or her father
  • Synonym: matricide - a person who kills his or her mother

Etymology

From Latin parricida (murderer of a close relative), from parens (parent) + -cida (killer).
