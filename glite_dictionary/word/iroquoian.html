Iroquoian

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌroʊˈkwɔɪ.ən / (AmE), / ɪˌrəʊˈkwɔɪ.ən / (BrE)
a family of North American Indigenous languages spoken by the Iroquois and related peoples
family of North American Indigenous languages

Examples

The Iroquoian languages include Mohawk, Oneida, and Seneca.
Anthropologists have extensively studied the structure of Iroquoian grammar.
Many Iroquoian words have been preserved in place names across North America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Algonquian - another major language family of North American Indigenous peoples

Etymology

From Iroquois, the name of the Indigenous people, plus -an, forming the family name.
