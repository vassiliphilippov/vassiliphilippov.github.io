Ibero-mesornis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪbɛroʊ mɛˈsɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪbərəʊ mɛˈsɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct fossil birds within the subclass Archaeornithes, known from the Early Cretaceous period and notable for primitive avian features.
fossil bird genus of Archaeornithes

Examples

The discovery of Ibero-mesornis provided valuable insight into early bird evolution.
Fossils of Ibero-mesornis have been found in Early Cretaceous deposits in Spain.
Researchers debated the flight capability of Ibero-mesornis based on its fossilized wing structure.

Etymology

From Latinized combination of 'Iberia' (the Iberian Peninsula) and 'mesornis' (Greek 'mesos' middle + 'ornis' bird).
