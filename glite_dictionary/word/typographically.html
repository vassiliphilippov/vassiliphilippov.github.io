typographically

1. ADV

/ ˌtaɪpəˈɡræfɪkli / (AmE), / ˌtɪpəˈɡræfɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to typography or the style, appearance, or arrangement of printed material.
in a manner concerning typography

Examples

The document was typographically well designed, making it easy to read and visually appealing.
That book is typographically inconsistent, with different fonts and styles on every page.
The essay was typographically flawless, with perfect alignment and spacing throughout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typographic - relating to typography or printed type

Etymology

From 'typographical' (from Greek 'typos' meaning 'impression' or 'mark' and 'graphia' meaning 'writing') + '-ly'.
