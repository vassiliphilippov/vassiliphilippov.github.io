cassocked

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæs.ɑːkt / (AmE), / ˈkæsɒkt / (BrE)
wearing a cassock; dressed in a long, close-fitting garment worn by clergy or church officials.
wearing a cassock

Examples

The cassocked priest led the procession through the cathedral.
A group of cassocked altar boys waited quietly near the altar.
The cassocked bishop greeted parishioners after the service.

Synset Examples

cassocked monks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassock - a long, close-fitting garment worn by clergy or church officials

Etymology

From 'cassock' + '-ed', forming the adjectival past participle.
