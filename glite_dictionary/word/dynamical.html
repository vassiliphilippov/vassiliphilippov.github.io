dynamical

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈnæmɪkəl / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪkəl / (BrE)
characterized by vigorous activity, energy, or forcefulness, especially in personality or action; also used in scientific contexts to mean relating to dynamics.
marked by vigor or energetic action

Examples

The company's dynamical leadership helped guide it through difficult times.
She possessed a dynamical personality that inspired everyone around her.
Physicists study the dynamical properties of moving objects.

Synset Examples

a dynamic market
a dynamic speaker
the dynamic president of the firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamic - characterized by constant change, activity, or progress; forceful

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamikos' (powerful) via Late Latin 'dynamica' (force), with the suffix '-al' forming the adjective.
