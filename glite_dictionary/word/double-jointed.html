double-jointed

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌb.əl ˌdʒɔɪn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˌdʒɔɪn.tɪd / (BrE)
Having unusually flexible joints, especially in the limbs or fingers, allowing for a greater than normal range of movement.
having unusually flexible joints

Examples

Sarah is double-jointed and can bend her fingers in strange ways.
Some athletes are double-jointed, which can give them an advantage in gymnastics.
He amazed the crowd by showing how double-jointed his elbows were.

Etymology

From 'double' + 'jointed', referring to the appearance of greater joint flexibility.
