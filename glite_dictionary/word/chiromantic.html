chiromantic

1. ADJ

/ kaɪ.rəˈmæn.tɪk / (AmE), / kaɪ.rəˈmæn.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of palmistry, the practice of interpreting lines on the palm to predict a person's future or characteristics
relating to palmistry

Examples

She wore a chiromantic pendant, believing it would bring insight into her future.
His chiromantic skills impressed those seeking fortunes at the fair.
The museum displayed chiromantic charts used by palm readers in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chiromancy - the practice of palm reading to tell fortunes

Etymology

From Greek kheir (hand) and manteia (divination), via chiromancy.
