aeriferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛrˈɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌɛːˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of a structure or passage) conveying or carrying air, especially within an organism.
conveying or carrying air

Examples

The bronchial tubes are aeriferous passages that conduct air to the lungs.
Some aquatic plants have aeriferous tissues to transport oxygen internally.
The structure of certain insects includes aeriferous tracheae delivering air directly to their tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airway - a passage for air, especially in the respiratory system

Etymology

From Latin 'aer' (air) + 'ferre' (to carry) + '-ous' (forming adjectives).
