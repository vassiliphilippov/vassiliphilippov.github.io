babysitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪbiˌsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbiːˌsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking care of children temporarily while their parents are away, typically for pay
caring for children when parents are away

Examples

She earned extra money by babysitting for her neighbors every weekend.
Finding reliable babysitting can be challenging for working parents.
He offered to do babysitting during the summer holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childcare - the care of children, especially as a service while parents are working

Etymology

From 'babysit' + '-ing', with 'babysit' modeled after 'baby' + 'sit'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.biˌsɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of taking care of children temporarily while their parents are away, usually as a paid job.
caring for children while parents are away

Examples

She earns extra money doing babysitting on weekends for her neighbors.
Finding reliable babysitting can be a challenge for working parents.
He offered to do some babysitting while his sister attended evening classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childcare - the care of children, especially as a professional service while parents are at work
  • Degree Higher: nannying - the work of a nanny in caring for children full-time

Etymology

Formed from 'baby' + 'sitting', by analogy with 'house-sitting'.
