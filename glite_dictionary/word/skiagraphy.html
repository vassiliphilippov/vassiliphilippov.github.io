skiagraphy

1. NOUN

/ skaɪˈæɡrəfi / (AmE), / skaɪˈaɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of producing an image on a photosensitive surface by using radiation other than visible light, such as in medical radiography
production of images using non-visible radiation
medical

Examples

Early medical practitioners used skiagraphy to obtain diagnostic images of the human body.
The development of skiagraphy was a major breakthrough in the history of radiology.
She specialized in skiagraphy, using X-rays to visualize internal structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiography - the process or technique of producing images using X-rays or similar radiation

Etymology

From Greek 'skia' (shadow) and 'graphia' (writing).
