vacuolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæk.ju.ə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈvak.ju.ə.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell) formed into or containing one or more vacuoles or small membrane-bound cavities.
having vacuoles in a cell
medical

Examples

The scientist observed vacuolate cells under the microscope during the experiment.
Many plant cells are naturally vacuolate due to their storage requirements.
The presence of vacuolate regions was indicative of cellular stress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonvacuolate - (of a cell) not containing vacuoles
  • Synonym: vacuole - a small cavity or vesicle in the cytoplasm of a cell

Etymology

From 'vacuole' (small cavity) and the suffix '-ate' forming adjectives.
