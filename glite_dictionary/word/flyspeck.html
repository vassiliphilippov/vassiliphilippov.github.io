flyspeck

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌspɛk / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌspɛk / (BrE)
a tiny dark spot or stain, especially one made by the excrement of a fly
tiny spot, esp. from fly excrement

Examples

The old painting was covered in flyspecks after years of neglect.
She wiped the flyspeck off the window with a cloth.
A flyspeck on the white tablecloth caught his attention during dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a small spot or mark; a tiny stain or particle

2. ADJ

/ ˈflaɪˌspɛk / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌspɛk / (BrE)
extremely small; tiny or insignificant in size or amount
extremely small, insignificant

Examples

The novelist described the village as a flyspeck settlement lost in the desert.
Compared to its neighbors, the country was a mere flyspeck on the map.
His contribution was a flyspeck amount, but it still helped the cause.

Etymology

From 'fly' and 'speck', referring originally to the small spots made by flies.
