shroud

1. NOUN

/ ʃraʊd / (AmE), / ʃraʊd / (BrE)
A line that suspends the harness from the canopy of a parachute.
parachute suspension line

Examples

One of the shrouds became tangled, causing the parachutist to drift off course.
Before packing the parachute, she carefully inspected each shroud for signs of wear.
If a shroud breaks during descent, the parachute may malfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - cord or rope used in parachutes

2. NOUN

/ ʃraʊd / (AmE), / ʃraʊd / (BrE)
a length of cloth or an enveloping garment in which a dead person is wrapped for burial
burial cloth or garment

Examples

The deceased was wrapped in a simple shroud before being placed in the coffin.
According to tradition, the shroud was made of white linen.
The ancient tomb contained a shroud remarkably well preserved after centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winding sheet - a sheet in which a corpse is wrapped for burial

Etymology

From Old English scrūd (garment), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ʃraʊd / (AmE), / ʃraʊd / (BrE)
(Nautical) A rope or chain that supports a ship's mast and helps regulate the angle of the sails.
rope or chain supporting a mast (nautical)

Examples

The sailors checked the shroud to ensure the mast was stable during the storm.
A loose shroud can affect the way the sails catch the wind.
He climbed the shroud to repair a torn sail high above the deck.

Etymology

From Old English *scrūd* (covering, garment); nautical sense by extension to the rigging that 'covers' or supports the mast.
