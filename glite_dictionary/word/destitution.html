destitution

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
The state of having no money, possessions, or means of support; extreme poverty.
state of extreme poverty

Examples

After losing his job and home, he fell into complete destitution.
Their charity aims to help families facing destitution during hard economic times.
The novel depicted the destitution experienced by many during the Great Depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being poor
  • Antonym: affluence - the state of having a great deal of money

Etymology

From Latin 'destitutio' (desertion, abandonment), from 'destituere' (to abandon).
