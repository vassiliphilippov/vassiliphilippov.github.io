albatross

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bəˌtrɔs / (AmE), / ˈæl.bə.trɒs / (BrE)
a large seabird of the southern oceans, with long narrow wings and known for its powerful gliding flight
large seabird with long wings

Examples

The albatross soared effortlessly above the rolling waves for hours.
An albatross can glide great distances without flapping its wings.
Several albatrosses were spotted by the research vessel near Antarctica.

Etymology

From Portuguese or Spanish 'alcatraz', meaning 'pelican', likely influenced by Arabic 'al-qatras'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bəˌtrɑs / (AmE), / ˈæl.bə.trɒs / (BrE)
(figurative) a constant burden or obstacle that causes ongoing trouble or anxiety.
a troublesome burden or handicap

Examples

The failed project became an albatross for the company, draining resources and lowering morale.
His debt proved to be an albatross around his neck for many years.
She feared the scandal would be an albatross that would haunt her career.

Synset Examples

she was an albatross around his neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a load or responsibility that is difficult to carry
  • Synonym: millstone - a heavy or inescapable responsibility
  • Antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or relief

Etymology

From the poem 'The Rime of the Ancient Mariner' by Samuel Taylor Coleridge, where the albatross becomes a symbol of guilt and burden.
