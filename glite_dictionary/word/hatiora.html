Hatiora

1. NOUN

/ həˈtiːərə / (AmE), / həˈtiːərə / (BrE)
A small genus of South American epiphytic or lithophytic cacti, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of South American cacti

Examples

The bright flowers of the Hatiora are especially attractive in springtime.
Many plant collectors prize Hatiora for its adaptability to indoor conditions.
Some species of Hatiora grow naturally on rocks in Brazilian forests.

Etymology

Named after the Brazilian botanist Harry Norton Ridley (Ha-ti-or-a is an anagram of Hariota, an older genus name).

2. NOUN

/ hæˈtiɔrə / (AmE), / hæˈtiːərə / (BrE)
A small genus of South American epiphytic or lithophytic cacti, some species of which are cultivated as houseplants.
genus of South American cacti

Examples

Several species of Hatiora are popular among collectors of unusual houseplants.
The genus Hatiora is native to the forests of Brazil where it grows on trees and rocks.
Botanists recently reclassified some Hatiora species based on genetic studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - any plant of the family Cactaceae, typically bearing spines and adapted to arid conditions

Etymology

Named after Thomas Hariot (Hatiora is an anagram of Hariot), English mathematician and astronomer.
