Nigroporus vinosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪɡrəˈpɔːrəs vaɪˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌnɪɡrəˈpɔːrəs vɪˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A species of woody pore fungus with a dark brown to reddish-brown cap, spore surface, and small pores.
a species of woody pore fungus

Examples

Nigroporus vinosus is often found on decaying wood in tropical and subtropical forests.
The dark reddish hues of Nigroporus vinosus make it easy to identify among other polypores.
Recent studies have examined the unique enzymes present in Nigroporus vinosus for possible biotechnological uses.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Nigroporus' meaning 'black pore' (niger + porus), 'vinosus' meaning 'wine-colored'.
