thalloid

1. ADJ

/ ˈθælˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈθal.ɔɪd / (BrE)
of, relating to, or resembling a thallus (a plant body undifferentiated into stem, root, or leaf)
resembling or relating to a thallus
medical

Examples

The growth habit of liverworts can be classified as either leafy or thalloid.
Mosses rarely display a thalloid structure, unlike some other primitive plants.
Some algae possess a distinctly thalloid body, lacking true leaves or roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thallus - an undifferentiated plant body without true stem, root, or leaf

Etymology

From Greek thallos (a young shoot or branch) + -oid (resembling).
