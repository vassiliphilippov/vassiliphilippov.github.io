lifelike

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪflaɪk / (BrE)
very similar to or representative of real life; free from artificiality or resembling a living person or thing.
realistic; resembling real life

Examples

The artist's sculpture was so lifelike that many people mistook it for a real person.
The movie used advanced technology to create lifelike animations of animals.
Her lifelike painting of the forest was praised for its vivid detail.

Synset Examples

a lifelike pose
a natural reaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realistic - closely resembling real life
  • Antonym: artificial - not natural or not resembling real life

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'like', meaning 'like life'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.laɪk / (BrE)
very similar to the person or thing represented, especially so as to seem real; vividly realistic.
realistic; resembling real life

Examples

The artist's lifelike sculpture captured every detail of the model's face.
The movie used special effects to create lifelike dinosaurs that amazed the audience.
His lifelike account of the event made listeners feel as if they were there.

Synset Examples

pictorial poetry and prose
graphic accounts of battle
a lifelike portrait
a vivid description

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realistic - closely resembling real life
  • Synonym: vivid - producing strong, clear images in the mind
  • Antonym: artificial - not natural or real, made to look like something else
Source: WordNet
