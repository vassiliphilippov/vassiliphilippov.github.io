errant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛrənt / (BrE)
moving in an uncontrolled, irregular, or unpredictable way; straying from the intended path or pattern.
uncontrolled or unpredictable in motion

Examples

An errant breeze blew through the open window, scattering the papers on the desk.
The errant ball bounced unpredictably across the field.
She tried to brush an errant strand of hair from her face.

Synset Examples

an errant breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wayward - difficult to control or predict because of unusual behavior
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to discipline or control
  • Antonym: predictable - able to be predicted or controlled

Etymology

From Old French *errant* (wandering), present participle of *errer* (to wander), from Latin *errare* (to stray).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛr.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɛr.ənt / (BrE)
behaving wrongly by straying from the proper course or accepted standards
straying from accepted standards

Examples

The principal was frustrated by the errant behavior of some students.
Despite their best efforts, the parents could not control their errant teenager.
He returned home with an errant look in his eyes after skipping school.

Synset Examples

errant youngsters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wayward - difficult to control or predict because of willful or perverse behavior
  • Antonym: correct - in accordance with fact, truth, or accepted standards

Etymology

From Old French 'errant', from Latin 'errans' (wandering), present participle of 'errare' (to stray).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00967492-s
Synset POS: s
