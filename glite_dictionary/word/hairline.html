hairline

1. NOUN

/ ˈherˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈheəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a very thin or fine line, especially one that is barely visible
very thin or barely visible line

Examples

A hairline crack appeared in the glass after the accident.
Draw a hairline border around the edge of the paper for a subtle effect.
The pottery had barely noticeable hairline fractures running through it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a narrow opening or line, often in a surface

Etymology

From 'hair' + 'line', referring to something as thin as a strand of hair.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈheə.laɪn / (BrE)
the edge or boundary where the hair grows on a person's head.
edge where hair grows on head

Examples

Genetic factors often influence a person's hairline and its shape.
He noticed his hairline was receding as he got older.
The barber suggested a new style to suit his widow's peak hairline.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: scalp - the skin covering the top of the human head, where hair grows
