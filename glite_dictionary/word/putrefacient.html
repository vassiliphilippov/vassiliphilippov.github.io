putrefacient

1. ADJ

/ ˌpjuːtrəˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
causing or promoting the decomposition of organic matter, especially by bacterial action; inducing putrefaction.
causing putrefaction or decay
medical

Examples

The stagnant water had a putrefacient effect on the nearby organic waste.
In medical history, certain wounds were treated with substances believed to be putrefacient.
Scientists study bacteria that release putrefacient enzymes during decomposition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antiseptic - preventing the growth of microorganisms

Etymology

From Latin 'putrefacere' (to cause to rot).
