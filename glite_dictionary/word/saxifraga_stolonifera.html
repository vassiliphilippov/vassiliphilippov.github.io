Saxifraga stolonifera

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə stoʊˌlɒnɪˈfɛrə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfrɑːɡə stɒˌlɒnɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of eastern Asia, known for its small red-and-white flowers and spreading by creeping stolons, often grown as a houseplant or in gardens.
species of ornamental perennial plant

Examples

The Saxifraga stolonifera grew quickly in the shaded corner of her garden.
Many gardeners prize Saxifraga stolonifera for its delicate, star-shaped flowers and attractive foliage.
We noticed spreading runners as our Saxifraga stolonifera established itself along the stone path.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Saxifraga', the genus name meaning 'stone-breaker'; 'stolonifera' meaning 'bearing runners'.
