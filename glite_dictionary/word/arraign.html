to arraign

1. VERB

/ əˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈreɪn / (BrE)
to accuse or criticize someone, especially to call them publicly to account for a fault or failing
accuse or criticize openly

Examples

Several critics arraigned the government for its handling of the crisis.
He was arraigned in the media for his lack of leadership.
The manager was swiftly arraigned for the team's poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accuse - to charge someone with a fault or offense
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *araisnier* (to address in court), from Latin *ad* (to) + *rationem* (account).

2. VERB

/ əˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈreɪn / (BrE)
to officially call someone before a court to answer a criminal charge or indictment
call to court to answer a charge
legal

Examples

The defendant will be arraigned in court on Monday morning.
She was arraigned on charges of fraud and theft.
After being indicted, the suspect was formally arraigned before the judge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indict - to formally accuse of or charge with a crime
  • Antonym: to acquit - to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty

Etymology

From Old French *arraigner* (to call to account), from Vulgar Latin *adrationare*.
