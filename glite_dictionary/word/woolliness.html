woolliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being like wool in texture, appearance, or shape.
wool-like texture or quality

Examples

The woolliness of the sheep's coat made it especially prized by farmers.
He preferred sweaters with a bit of woolliness because they felt warmer and softer.
Some plants have adapted a kind of woolliness on their leaves to retain moisture.

Synset Examples

The hair is either straight or curly, but never approaching to the woolliness of the negro.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluffiness - the quality of being light and soft like fluff
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even and without roughness

Etymology

From 'woolly' + '-ness'; 'woolly' from Old English 'wull' (wool).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being made of or covered with wool, or resembling wool.
the state of being woolly

Examples

The woolliness of the sheep's coat keeps it warm during the winter months.
She admired the soft woolliness of her new sweater.
The plant's stems have a distinctive woolliness that protects them from pests.

Synset Examples

Some oats are given to the rams during the season of copulation; and the French think that a great deal of the size and woolliness of the offspring depends on the vigour of the ram, rather than the ewe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furriness - The state of being covered with fur
  • Antonym: smoothness - The state of being smooth, lacking roughness or fuzziness

Etymology

From 'woolly' + '-ness', forming a noun denoting the state or quality of being woolly.
