bounteousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊntiəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈbaʊntiəsnəs / (BrE)
a literary term for generosity, shown by a willingness to give freely or abundantly
generous willingness to give freely

Examples

The bounteousness of her gifts surprised everyone at the gathering.
We were humbled by the bounteousness of the harvest this year.
He was admired for the bounteousness he showed to strangers and friends alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and ready to give to others
  • Antonym: stinginess - unwillingness to give or spend; meanness

Etymology

Formed from 'bounteous' (generous), ultimately from Middle English via Old French, from Latin 'bonitas' (goodness).
