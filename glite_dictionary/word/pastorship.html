pastorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, duties, or term of office of a pastor in a church
the position of a pastor

Examples

His twenty years of pastorship greatly influenced the congregation.
During her pastorship, the church grew in both size and spirit.
He prepared thoroughly for the responsibilities that come with pastorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastor - a minister in charge of a Christian church or congregation

Etymology

From 'pastor' (from Latin pastor ‘shepherd’) + '-ship' (denoting status or office).
