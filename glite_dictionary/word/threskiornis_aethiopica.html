Threskiornis aethiopica

1. NOUN

/ θrɛskiˈɔrnɪs iˌθiəˈpɪkə / (AmE), / θrɛskiˈɔːnɪs iˌθiːəˈpɪkə / (BrE)
a species of white African ibis with black head and neck, historically venerated by ancient Egyptians
African sacred ibis species

Examples

The ancient Egyptians often mummified the Threskiornis aethiopica as part of their religious rituals.
You can spot the Threskiornis aethiopica wading in shallow waters along the Nile.
The striking black-and-white feathers of the Threskiornis aethiopica make it easy to recognize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibis - a long-legged wading bird with a long down-curved bill

Etymology

From Greek 'Threskiornis', meaning 'religious bird', and Latin 'aethiopica', meaning 'of Ethiopia'.
