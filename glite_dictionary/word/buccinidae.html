Buccinidae

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bʊˈsɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine gastropod mollusks comprising the true whelks and their relatives
family of marine whelks

Examples

The Buccinidae are found in cold waters throughout the world.
Marine biologists collected several specimens of Buccinidae for their research.
Members of the Buccinidae family are commonly referred to as true whelks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelk - common name for various marine snails, including Buccinidae

Etymology

From Latin 'buccinum' (a kind of sea-snail or whelk) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
