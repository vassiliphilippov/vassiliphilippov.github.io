wigwam

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡˌwɑm / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡwæm / (BrE)
A domed or oval-shaped hut traditionally made and used by certain Native American peoples, typically constructed with a framework of poles and covered with bark, mats, or hides.
Native American domed hut

Examples

The tribe built a new wigwam near the river each spring.
Historical reenactors constructed a replica wigwam using traditional materials and techniques.
A wigwam differs from a tipi in both shape and construction.

Etymology

From Algonquian (Ojibwa, Potawatomi) 'wigwaum', meaning 'their house'.
