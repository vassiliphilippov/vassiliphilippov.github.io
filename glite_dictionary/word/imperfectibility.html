imperfectibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɝːfɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːfɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being incapable of perfection or prone to imperfection
state of being imperfect or not perfectible

Examples

The imperfectibility of human nature is a common theme in philosophy.
Many critics argue that the imperfectibility of justice systems should be acknowledged rather than ignored.
He spoke at length about the imperfectibility inherent in all artistic endeavors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfectibility - the capability of becoming perfect

Etymology

From imperfect +‎ -ibility, modeled after perfectibility.
