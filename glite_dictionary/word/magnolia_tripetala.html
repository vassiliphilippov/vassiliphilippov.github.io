Magnolia tripetala

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliə trɪˈpɛtələ / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊliə trɪˈpɛtələ / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree of eastern North America, having large leaves shaped like umbrellas and creamy white flowers.
a deciduous North American tree with umbrella-like leaves

Examples

The Magnolia tripetala in our garden blooms beautifully every spring.
With its broad, umbrella-shaped leaves, the Magnolia tripetala stands out among native trees.
Botanists noted the distinct creamy white flowers of the Magnolia tripetala during their survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella magnolia - another common name for Magnolia tripetala

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Magnolia' named after French botanist Pierre Magnol, and 'tripetala' meaning 'three petals'.
