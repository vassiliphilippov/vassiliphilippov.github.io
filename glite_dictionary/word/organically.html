organically

1. ADV

/ ɔrˈɡænɪkli / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner such that something serves as an inherent or integral part of the whole.
as an integral or inherent part

Examples

The arches blend organically into the overall design of the cathedral.
In her compositions, every theme is developed organically rather than being artificially added.
The supporting characters are connected organically to the main plot, enhancing the story's unity.

Synset Examples

the drapery served organically to cover the Madonna

Etymology

From 'organic' (from Greek organikos, relating to an organ or instrument) + -ally.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00114489-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ɔrˈɡænɪkli / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses or relates to organic methods, especially in farming or food production; without synthetic chemicals or artificial additives.
in an organic manner, especially in farming

Examples

All of the vegetables in this market are grown organically without synthetic pesticides.
She prefers to farm organically, using only natural fertilizers and crop rotation.
The company was certified for producing wine organically, in line with strict environmental standards.

Synset Examples

this food is grown organically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inorganically - in an inorganic manner; not relating to organic methods

Etymology

Formed from 'organic' + '-ally', patterned on adverb formation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00114629-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ɔːrˈɡænɪkli / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves carbon compounds or is related to the chemistry of living things.
relating to carbon compounds

Examples

Some trace elements must be present organically bound within living tissues to be absorbed.
The compound is organically linked to the main structure by a carbon atom.
Scientists studied how iodine is organically incorporated into soil bacteria.

Synset Examples

organically bound iodine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inorganically - in a way not involving carbon compounds or living matter

Etymology

From organic + -ally, relating to organic compounds in chemistry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00114218-r
Synset POS: r
