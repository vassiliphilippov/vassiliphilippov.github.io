capercaillie

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpərˈkeɪli / (AmE), / ˌkapəˈkeɪli / (BrE)
A large, dark-plumaged grouse native to northern Europe and Asia, known for its elaborate courtship display.
large Old World grouse

Examples

The capercaillie is renowned for its spectacular mating dance in the forests of Scotland.
We spotted a male capercaillie displaying its tail feathers at dawn.
Habitat loss has made the capercaillie increasingly rare in certain parts of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouse - Any of various plump-bodied game birds

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'capall-coille' meaning 'horse of the woods'.
