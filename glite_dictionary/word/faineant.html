faineant

1. ADJ

/ ˌfeɪniˈænt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪniːənt / (BrE)
(rare) Disinclined to work or exertion; idle or lazy.
idle; lazy; disinclined to work

Examples

The country stagnated under a faineant government unwilling to address pressing issues.
Despite his reputation as a faineant, he occasionally surprised everyone with bursts of energy.
The old manor was run by a faineant caretaker who preferred reading to working.

Synset Examples

faineant kings under whose rule the country languished
an indolent hanger-on
too lazy to wash the dishes
slothful employees
the unemployed are not necessarily work-shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolent - disliking or avoiding work; lazy
  • Antonym: industrious - diligent and hard-working

Etymology

From French fainéant, from fait (does) + néant (nothing); literally 'does nothing'.
