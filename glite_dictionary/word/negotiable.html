negotiable

1. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡoʊʃiəbl̩ / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊʃiəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be discussed and changed through bargaining or compromise; not fixed or final.
able to be discussed or changed

Examples

The terms of the contract are still negotiable at this stage.
Our prices are negotiable depending on the quantity you order.
She made it clear that her schedule was not negotiable.

Synset Examples

negotiable demands
the proposal is still on the table

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed - not able to be changed; firmly established

Etymology

From Latin negotiabilis (that can be transacted) via French négociable.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01028015-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡoʊdʒəbəl / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊdʒəbəl / (BrE)
(of a route or obstacle) able to be traversed, crossed, or passed through.
able to be traversed or passed through

Examples

The mountain pass was barely negotiable after the heavy snowfall.
The dirt track remained negotiable despite the recent rains.
Some sections of the river are only negotiable by experienced kayakers.

Synset Examples

a negotiable road

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impassable - not able to be crossed or traveled through

Etymology

From negotiate + -able, originally meaning 'able to be managed or traveled'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01729130-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡoʊʃiəbl̩ / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊʃəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a document or instrument) able to be legally transferred by endorsement or delivery to another party, so that the rights of ownership pass to the transferee.
able to be legally transferred to another
legal

Examples

The company invested in negotiable bonds to maintain liquidity and flexibility.
A check is considered a negotiable instrument and can be endorsed to another party.
These shares are negotiable and may be sold or transferred to another holder.

Synset Examples

negotiable bonds

Etymology

From Latin 'negotiari' (to trade, do business) via French 'négociable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00094409-s
Synset POS: s
