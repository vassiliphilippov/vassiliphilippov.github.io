lamentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlæmənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the passionate expression of grief or sorrow
passionate expression of grief

Examples

Her lamentation could be heard throughout the village after the tragic news.
The old poet wrote a moving lamentation for his lost homeland.
The ceremony included a ritual of lamentation performed by mourners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourning - the feeling or act of showing sorrow, especially after someone dies
  • Antonym: jubilation - a feeling or expression of great joy

Etymology

From Latin 'lamentatio', from 'lamentari' (to wail, lament).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlæmənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an act or expression of deep sorrow or mourning, especially a loud cry or weeping
expression of deep sorrow or mourning

Examples

The sound of lamentation echoed through the empty church after the tragic news.
Historical texts often describe public lamentation following the death of a king.
The movie was filled with scenes of lamentation at the loss of loved ones.

Synset Examples

their pitiful laments could be heard throughout the ward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret
  • Antonym: rejoicing - expression of great happiness or joy

Etymology

From Latin lamentatio (weeping, wailing), from lamentari (to lament).
