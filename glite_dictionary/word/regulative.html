regulative

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
serving to restrict or control by rules, principles, or laws
serving to restrict or control by rules

Examples

The board implemented regulative policies to ensure ethical business practices.
Many philosophers distinguish between constitutive and regulative principles in explaining human behavior.
Environmental agencies may create regulative frameworks to manage natural resources effectively.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'regulativus', from 'regula' (rule).
