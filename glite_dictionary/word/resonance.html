resonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzənəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzənəns / (BrE)
A state in which a stable particle temporarily enters an excited state, increasing its probability of absorbing electromagnetic radiation at a specific frequency.
excited state increasing absorption probability

Examples

Physicists observed a resonance when the particle absorbed energy at a specific frequency.
The gamma-ray resonance led to a peak in the absorption spectrum.
A sharp resonance can indicate the presence of an excited state in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitation - the act of energizing to a higher energy state

Etymology

From Latin resonantia (echo, resounding), from resonare (to resound).

5. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌnæns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzənəns / (BrE)
A phenomenon in which a system vibrates with increasing amplitude at certain frequencies when exposed to a periodic force, especially when the frequency of the force matches the system's natural frequency.
vibration amplifying at natural frequency

Examples

When a singer hits the exact note, the glass can shatter due to resonance.
Engineers design bridges to withstand resonance caused by strong winds or vibrations.
The phenomenon of resonance is crucial in tuning musical instruments to achieve the right sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: damping - the reduction in amplitude of a resonance

Etymology

From Latin *resonantia* (echo, resounding), from *resonare* (to resound).
