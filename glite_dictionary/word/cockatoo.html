cockatoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.kəˌtuː / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.əˌtuː / (BrE)
a large, white or light-colored parrot with a prominent crest, native to Australia and nearby regions, often kept as a pet
large crested parrot of Australia

Examples

The cockatoo perched on the branch, preening its brilliant white feathers.
My neighbors have a pet cockatoo that can mimic human speech.
During our trip to Australia, we spotted a wild cockatoo with a striking yellow crest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a bird of the family Psittacidae, often brightly colored

Etymology

From Malay 'kakatua', meaning 'parrot'.
