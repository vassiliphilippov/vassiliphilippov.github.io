Platanthera

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtænθərə / (AmE), / pləˈtanθərə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous terrestrial orchids found in temperate regions of both the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.
a genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

Several species within the genus Platanthera are commonly known as fringed orchids.
The white-flowered Platanthera blooms in damp meadows during the summer months.
Botanists discovered a new Platanthera species in the mountainous region last year.

Etymology

From Greek 'platy-' (broad) and 'anthera' (anther), referring to the broad anther lobes in the flower.

A genus of herbaceous terrestrial orchids found in temperate regions of the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.
genus of temperate orchids

Examples

The Platanthera genus includes many striking and fragrant wild orchids.
Many conservationists study Platanthera species due to their sensitivity to habitat changes.
Some Platanthera orchids are commonly known as butterfly orchids because of their flower shape.

Etymology

From Greek 'platanos' (broad) and 'anthera' (anther), referring to the flower's shape.
