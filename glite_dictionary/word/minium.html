minium

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪniəm / (BrE)
a bright red or orange-red oxide of lead, used as a pigment in paints and in manufacturing glass and ceramics
red lead pigment; lead oxide

Examples

The artist mixed minium with linseed oil to achieve a vivid orange hue.
Traditionally, minium was used to decorate manuscripts during the Middle Ages.
Due to its toxicity, alternatives have replaced minium in most modern paints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red lead - an alternative name for lead(II,IV) oxide, used as a pigment

Etymology

From Latin 'minium', referring to cinnabar or red lead.
