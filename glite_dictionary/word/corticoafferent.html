corticoafferent

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈæfərənt / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈæfərənt / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber or impulse) originating outside and passing toward the cerebral cortex.
nerve input to cerebral cortex
medical

Examples

Damage to corticoafferent pathways can impair sensory information processing in the cerebral cortex.
Researchers studied the function of corticoafferent fibers in relation to auditory perception.
The abnormal firing of corticoafferent impulses was observed in patients with epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corticoefferent - nerve fiber carrying impulses away from the cerebral cortex

Etymology

From Latin 'cortex' (bark, outer layer, here: cortex) + 'afferent' (carrying toward).
