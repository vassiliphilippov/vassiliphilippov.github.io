Lagarostrobus franklinii

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæɡəˌrɑːˈstroʊbəs ˌfræŋˈklɪniː / (AmE), / ˌlæɡəˌrɒˈstrəʊbəs ˌfræŋˈklɪniː / (BrE)
A Tasmanian coniferous tree known for its yellow, aromatic wood, used in carving and shipbuilding; also called Huon pine.
Tasmanian timber tree (Huon pine)

Examples

The Lagarostrobus franklinii is prized for its durable and fragrant wood.
Many antique ship fittings in Tasmania were crafted from Lagarostrobus franklinii.
Botanists study Lagarostrobus franklinii due to its unique evolutionary history in Tasmania.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Huon pine - Common name for Lagarostrobus franklinii, valued Tasmanian timber tree

Etymology

From Greek *lagaros* (‘slack, pliant’), *strobos* (‘pine cone’), and Latinized after Sir John Franklin, explorer.
