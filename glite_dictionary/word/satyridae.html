Satyridae

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɪrɪˌdi / (AmE), / səˈtɪərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of butterflies commonly found near woodland edges, known for their brown or earthy colors and eye spots on wings.
a family of woodland butterflies

Examples

Many species within the Satyridae family display striking eye spots that help deter predators.
Woodland paths in summer are often inhabited by butterflies belonging to the Satyridae.
Researchers have documented more than a hundred varieties of Satyridae in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Satyridae, based on Satyrus, a genus of butterflies; named after satyrs from Greek mythology.
