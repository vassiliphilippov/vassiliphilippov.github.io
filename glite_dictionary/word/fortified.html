fortified

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrtɪfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or building) strengthened and secured with defensive military works such as walls or bastions.
secured with defensive military works

Examples

The fortified castle stood atop the hill, making it difficult for enemies to attack.
Many medieval towns were fortified with thick stone walls and watchtowers.
The villagers took shelter in a fortified church during the invasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defended - protected from attack; shielded
  • Antonym: unprotected - not defended or guarded

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.tə.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
(of food or drink) having added substances to increase strength or nutritional value.
having added substances for greater strength

Examples

Many breakfast cereals are fortified with extra vitamins and minerals to improve nutrition.
This cheese is made from fortified milk to increase its protein content.
Port is a type of fortified wine containing added brandy.

Synset Examples

fortified wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enriched - (of food) having nutrients added to replace those lost during processing

Etymology

From Latin fortificare (to make strong), via Old French fortifier.
