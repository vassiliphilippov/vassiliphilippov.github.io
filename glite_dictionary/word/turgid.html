turgid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɝːdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdʒɪd / (BrE)
(of language or style) pompous, inflated, or overblown; attempting to sound important or impressive but often perceived as tedious or pretentious.
pompous or inflated in style

Examples

The professor's turgid lecture left the students confused and bored.
Critics panned the novel for its turgid prose and lack of clarity.
Her turgid speech was filled with long, complicated words meant to impress the audience.

Synset Examples

a man given to large talk
tumid political prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombastic - inflated or pretentious in speech or writing
  • Synonym: grandiloquent - using lofty or extravagant language
  • Antonym: simple - plain, clear, and straightforward in style

Etymology

From Latin turgidus (swollen, inflated), from turgēre (to swell).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɝːdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdʒɪd / (BrE)
swollen and distended, especially due to the accumulation of fluids or gas
swollen or distended

Examples

The patient's abdomen was turgid, indicating a possible buildup of fluid.
After the allergic reaction, her lips became noticeably turgid and painful.
A turgid blister had formed on his finger after the burn.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - unusually enlarged or puffed up, especially due to accumulation of fluid
  • Synonym: distended - enlarged or expanded from internal pressure
  • Antonym: collapsed - fallen down or caved in, the opposite of being swollen or distended

Etymology

From Latin turgidus (swollen), from turgēre (to swell).
