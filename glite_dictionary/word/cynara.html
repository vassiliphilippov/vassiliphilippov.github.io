Cynara

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɛrə / (AmE), / sɪˈnɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of thistle-like perennial plants of the family Asteraceae, including the artichoke and cardoon.
genus of plants including the artichoke

Examples

Cynara is best known as the genus that includes the globe artichoke.
Botanists studied various species within the Cynara genus for their medicinal properties.
The Cynara plants in the garden are flowering beautifully this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artichoke - a plant (Cynara scolymus) with edible flower buds
  • Synonym: cardoon - a plant (Cynara cardunculus) with edible leaf stalks

Etymology

From Latin Cynara, from Greek kynara (artichoke).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnərə / (AmE), / ˈsɪnərə / (BrE)
a genus of thistle-like flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, including the artichoke and cardoon
genus of plants including artichoke

Examples

The Cynara genus includes both the globe artichoke and the cardoon.
Botanists study Cynara to understand the differences between artichokes and related plants.
Some species of Cynara are grown for ornamental purposes as well as for eating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artichoke - a plant of the species Cynara cardunculus, cultivated for its edible flower buds
  • Synonym: cardoon - a thistle-like plant related to the artichoke

Etymology

From Latin *Cynara*, from Ancient Greek *kynára* (artichoke).
