summum bonum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌməm ˈboʊnəm / (AmE), / ˌsʊmʊm ˈbəʊnəm / (BrE)
the supreme good, regarded as the ultimate goal or highest value in moral philosophy, from which all moral values derive
the supreme good; ultimate moral value

Examples

Many philosophers have debated what the summum bonum of human life truly is.
She devoted her life to the pursuit of the summum bonum, always seeking moral excellence.
In ancient philosophy, happiness or eudaimonia was often considered the summum bonum of existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimate good - the highest good or final objective in ethics

Etymology

From Latin, literally ‘the highest good’ (summum: highest, bonum: good).
