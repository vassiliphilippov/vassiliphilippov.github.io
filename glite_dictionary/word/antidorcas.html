Antidorcas

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈdɔr.kəs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdɔː.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes comprising the springbok species.
genus of African antelopes (springboks)

Examples

The genus Antidorcas includes only one living species, the springbok.
Zoologists classified the springbok as Antidorcas marsupialis based on its unique characteristics.
Fossil evidence suggests that Antidorcas once had other extinct species besides the modern springbok.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springbok - a medium-sized antelope of southern Africa, Antidorcas marsupialis

Etymology

From Greek anti- (opposite, instead of) + dorkas (gazelle) — referring to its similarity and difference from true gazelles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈdɔr.kəs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdɔː.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of antelopes in southern Africa, best known for the springbok.
genus of antelopes including the springbok

Examples

The Antidorcas genus is well known for its distinctive leaping behavior.
Among the members of Antidorcas, the springbok is the most famous species.
Researchers study Antidorcas to understand antelope evolution in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springbok - a medium-sized antelope found in southern Africa

Etymology

From Greek anti- (opposite) + dorkas (gazelle).
