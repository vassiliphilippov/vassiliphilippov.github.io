bonnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑː.nət / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.ɪt / (BrE)
the hinged metal cover over the engine of a car, especially in British English
metal cover over a car's engine (British)
british

Examples

The mechanic lifted the bonnet to examine the engine.
In Britain, drivers check the oil level by opening the bonnet.
The car's bonnet was dented in the minor accident.

Synset Examples

there are powerful engines under the hoods of new cars
the mechanic removed the cowling in order to repair the plane's engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hood - the hinged metal cover over a car's engine (American)

Etymology

From Old French 'bonet' (material, headdress), later applied in English to various types of coverings, including automobile engines.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɒn.ɪt / (BrE)
a woman's or child's hat that is tied under the chin and often covers the ears and the back of the head
hat tied under the chin

Examples

She wore a flowery bonnet to protect her head from the sun.
In period dramas, young girls are often seen in lace-trimmed bonnets.
The baby's bonnet was tied securely under her chin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cap - close-fitting head covering

Etymology

From Middle French 'bonet' (material for headdress), from Old French, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
