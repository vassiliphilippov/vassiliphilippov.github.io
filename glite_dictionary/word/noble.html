noble

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
of or belonging to the hereditary aristocracy, especially as derived from feudal times; relating to people of high social rank by birth or title
relating to hereditary aristocracy

Examples

He was of noble descent, tracing his family back to medieval lords.
The palace was once home to many noble families.
Despite her noble birth, she supported reforms to aid the poor.

Synset Examples

of noble birth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocratic - relating to the aristocracy; having qualities associated with nobility
  • Antonym: common - belonging to or characteristic of the lower social classes; not noble

Etymology

From Latin nobilis (well-known, famous, high-born).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01594522-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
a person who belongs to the aristocracy, especially one with a hereditary or official title in a country; a titled peer of the realm
a titled member of the aristocracy

Examples

The medieval castle was the residence of a powerful noble and his family.
Many reforms were resisted by the nobles who wished to keep their privileges.
He was made a noble by the king in recognition of his service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocrat - a member of the aristocracy
  • Antonym: commoner - a person without noble rank

Etymology

From Latin *nobilis* (well-known, high-born).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10291374-n
Synset POS: n

5. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
(of a gas or metal) chemically inert, especially towards oxygen or other substances; not easily combining with other elements.
chemically inert (of gas or metal)

Examples

Gold is considered a noble metal because it resists corrosion and oxidation.
Helium, a noble gas, does not form compounds easily under normal conditions.
Platinum is valued for being a noble metal, remaining unaffected by most acids.

Synset Examples

a noble gas such as helium or neon
noble metals include gold and silver and platinum

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reactive - tending to react chemically with other substances

Etymology

From Latin nobilis (notable, excellent), extended in technical use to describe elements of 'high' resistance to chemical reaction.
