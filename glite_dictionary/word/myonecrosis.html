myonecrosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪoʊnɛˈkroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəʊnɛˈkrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
Localized death of muscle tissue fibers, often due to infection or injury.
death of muscle fibers
medical

Examples

Clostridial infection can rapidly lead to myonecrosis if not treated promptly.
The biopsy confirmed the presence of acute myonecrosis in the affected muscle tissue.
Early recognition of myonecrosis is crucial to prevent severe complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necrosis - death of body tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'nekrosis' (process of becoming dead).
