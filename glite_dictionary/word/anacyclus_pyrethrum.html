Anacyclus pyrethrum

1. NOUN

/ əˈneɪsɪkləs paɪˈrɛθrəm / (AmE), / əˈnæsɪkləs paɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
A small Mediterranean plant whose roots contain a volatile oil formerly used to relieve toothache.
Mediterranean plant used for toothache relief

Examples

The roots of Anacyclus pyrethrum were traditionally chewed to ease toothaches.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of Anacyclus pyrethrum in recent years.
In herbal medicine, Anacyclus pyrethrum is valued for its analgesic effects.

Etymology

From Greek anakyklos (a plant name) and pyrethron (feverfew, related to fire/heat).
