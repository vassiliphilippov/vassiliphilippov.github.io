boreal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔriəl, ˈboʊriəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the north; situated in or moving toward the northern regions
relating to the north

Examples

The boreal forests of Canada are home to many unique species.
Astronomers study the boreal lights, also known as the aurora borealis.
The climate in boreal regions can be harsh and cold.

Synset Examples

the boreal signs of the Zodiac

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern - relating to or situated in the north
  • Antonym: southern - relating to or situated in the south

Etymology

From Latin borealis, from Boreas, the Greek god of the north wind.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔːriəl, ˈboʊriəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːriəl, ˈbɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the northern regions or the north wind, especially in reference to cool, northern climates.
of the northern regions; northern

Examples

The boreal forests cover vast stretches of Canada and Russia.
During winter, strong boreal winds sweep down from the Arctic.
Many migratory birds breed in the boreal regions during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southern - relating to or characteristic of the south or southern regions

Etymology

From Latin borealis, from Boreas (the north wind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03145198-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɔːriəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the far northern regions, especially of the northern forests or climatic zones.
of the northern regions or climates

Examples

The boreal forests of Canada are home to many unique animal species.
Scientists are studying the effects of climate change on boreal ecosystems.
Migratory birds breed in the boreal regions during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: arctic - relating to the regions around the North Pole

Etymology

From Latin 'borealis' (northern), from 'Boreas', the Greek god of the north wind.
