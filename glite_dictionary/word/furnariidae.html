Furnariidae

1. NOUN

/ fərˌnɛəriˈɪdiː / (AmE), / fɜːˌnɛərɪˈɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly South American birds, including ovenbirds and related species, known for their elaborate nests
a family of ovenbirds and related birds

Examples

The Furnariidae are remarkable for the diversity of their nesting habits.
Many species in the Furnariidae family build intricate oven-shaped nests.
Ornithologists often study Furnariidae to understand avian adaptation in South America.

Etymology

From New Latin Furnariidae, from genus name Furnarius (type genus), itself from Latin furnus (oven) due to nest shape.

2. NOUN

/ fɚˌnɛriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / fɜːˌnɑːrɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized passerine birds native to Central and South America, known as ovenbirds, which are noted for their elaborate clay nests.
family of ovenbirds native to the Americas

Examples

The Furnariidae family includes some of the most adaptable bird species in South America.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Furnariidae.
Members of the Furnariidae are easily recognized by their intricate clay nests.

Etymology

From Latin *Furnarius* (a baker, referring to the nest shape) + scientific suffix *-idae* (denoting a family).
