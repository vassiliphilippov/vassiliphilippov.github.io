to dumbfound

1. VERB

/ ˌdʌmˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌdʌmˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
to greatly astonish or bewilder someone; to make someone so surprised or confused that they are unable to respond
to astonish or bewilder

Examples

The magician's final trick dumbfounded the entire audience.
I was completely dumbfounded when I heard the unexpected news.
Her response to the difficult question dumbfounded the interviewer.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to cause someone to become perplexed or confused
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject

Etymology

From 'dumb' (unable to speak) and 'found' (variant of 'confound') in 17th-century English.
