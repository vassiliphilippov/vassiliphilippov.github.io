stickball

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈstɪkˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A street game similar to baseball, played with a broomstick as a bat and a rubber ball, typically in urban areas.
street baseball-like game with broomstick and rubber ball
informal american

Examples

Every summer, we played stickball in the alley until it got dark.
The rules of stickball varied from neighborhood to neighborhood.
His father told stories of the fierce stickball games played on city streets decades ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseball - a bat-and-ball game played between two teams

Etymology

Formed from 'stick' + 'ball', referring to the equipment used in the game.
