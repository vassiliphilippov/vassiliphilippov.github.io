accented

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.sɛn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.sɛntɪd / (BrE)
(of a syllable or word) pronounced with greater emphasis or stress than the surrounding syllables or words, often marked by a change in pitch.
bearing the main stress in pronunciation

Examples

The second syllable in 'guitar' is accented when spoken aloud.
In English, the first syllable is often accented in two-syllable nouns.
She marked the accented syllables with a bold underline for clarity.

Synset Examples

a tonic syllable carries the main stress in a word

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaccented - (of a syllable) not bearing principle stress

Etymology

From 'accent', from Latin accentus (song added to speech).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæk.sɛn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.sɛn.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a syllable or word) pronounced with greater stress or emphasis than others.
having stress or emphasis (in speech)

Examples

The first syllable in 'happy' is accented while the second is unstressed.
In poetry, an accented beat is often marked with a special symbol.
Some English learners struggle to identify which syllable should be accented in a word.

Synset Examples

an iambic foot consists of an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable as in ‘delay’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stressed - (of a syllable) pronounced with greater emphasis
  • Antonym: unstressed - (of a syllable) not pronounced with stress

Etymology

From Latin accentus (accent), via French accenter (to accentuate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02327216-a
Synset POS: a
