to teleport

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛləˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌpɔːt / (BrE)
(in science fiction) to transport someone or something instantly from one place to another, typically by dematerializing at one point and reassembling at another.
instantaneously transport by dematerializing and rematerializing

Examples

In the movie, the aliens could teleport entire cities across the galaxy.
She wished she could teleport home instead of sitting in traffic for hours.
The villain tried to teleport away, but the superhero stopped him just in time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transport - to carry something from one place to another
  • Antonym: to materialize - to appear, especially suddenly or magically

Etymology

From 'tele-' (distant) and 'port' (carry), coined in science fiction.
