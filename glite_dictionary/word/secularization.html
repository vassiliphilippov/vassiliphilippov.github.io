secularization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkjələraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛkjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something, such as art, education, or society, becomes less influenced or controlled by religion
process of making less religious

Examples

The rapid secularization of society in the twentieth century changed traditional values.
Many historians study the secularization of education in the modern era.
Debates over secularization often center on the decline of religious authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secularism - the belief that religion should not play a role in government or public affairs

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'saecularisatio', from 'saecularis' (worldly, secular).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkjəlɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛkjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The transfer of property or institutions from religious (especially church) control to civil or government ownership.
transfer from church to civil ownership

Examples

The secularization of monasteries drastically altered the distribution of land in the country.
During the Reformation, secularization led to many church properties becoming state assets.
Historians debate the long-term effects of secularization on European society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disestablishment - the action of depriving a church of its official status

Etymology

From Medieval Latin secularisatio (conversion from religious to secular use), from Latin saecularis (worldly).
