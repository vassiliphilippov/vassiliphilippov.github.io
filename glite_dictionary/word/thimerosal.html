thimerosal

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈmɛrəsɔl / (AmE), / θɪˈmɪərəsɒl / (BrE)
A light-colored crystalline compound used as an antiseptic and preservative, especially in vaccines and other biological products.
antiseptic and vaccine preservative
medical

Examples

Certain vaccines used to contain thimerosal as a preservative.
Some people have expressed concerns about thimerosal in medical products.
The laboratory handled the thimerosal powder with protective gloves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merthiolate - trade name for thimerosal, an antiseptic and preservative

Etymology

Blend of 'thi(o)-' (relating to sulfur), 'mer(cury)', and 'sal' (salt).
