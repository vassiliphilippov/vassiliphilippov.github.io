abracadabra

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbrəkəˈdæbrə / (AmE), / ˌæbrəkəˈdæbrə / (BrE)
speech or writing that is meaningless or unintelligible; nonsense.
meaningless or nonsensical language

Examples

His explanation was just abracadabra to most listeners.
All that technical abracadabra only confused the audience.
She waved her hands and shouted some abracadabra, hoping to impress the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - language that has no meaning or makes no sense

Etymology

From Late Latin 'abracadabra', used as a magical word.
