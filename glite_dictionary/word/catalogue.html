catalogue

1. VERB

/ ˈkæt̬.ə.lɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæt.əl.ɒɡ / (BrE)
to make a systematic list of items, often with details about each; to classify or arrange methodically
systematically list or classify

Examples

The university has begun to catalogue all rare manuscripts in its collection.
She spent hours cataloguing her father’s stamp collection.
Every photograph was carefully catalogued and stored in albums.

Synset Examples

He is cataloguing his photographic negatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - to make a complete list of items in a place
  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange into categories

Etymology

From French 'catalogue', via Late Latin 'catalogus', from Greek 'katalogos' (a list, register).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00654293-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætəlɔg / (AmE), / ˈkætəlɒg / (BrE)
a complete list of items, often systematically arranged and sometimes with descriptive details
a list of items, systematically arranged

Examples

The museum published a new catalogue featuring all its latest acquisitions.
Before ordering, please check the catalogue for product details and prices.
Their music catalogue includes albums from the past two decades.

Synset Examples

it does not pretend to be a catalog of his achievements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a detailed list of items or goods in stock
  • Synonym: register - an official or formal list of names or items
  • Degree Lower: collection - a group of things or people assembled for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'catalogue' and Latin 'catalogus', from Greek 'katalogos' (a list).

book listing items for sale or display

Examples

She requested a catalogue from the furniture store to compare prices.
