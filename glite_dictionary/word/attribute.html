attribute

1. NOUN

/ ˈætrɪˌbjut / (AmE), / ˈætrɪˌbjuːt / (BrE)
a quality, feature, or characteristic that belongs to or identifies someone or something
quality or characteristic of someone or something

Examples

Honesty is an important attribute for any leader.
The painting's most striking attribute is its vibrant use of color.
Patience is a valuable attribute when working with children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: property - a quality or feature that belongs to something, especially a physical object
  • Synonym: characteristic - a typical feature or quality that makes something different
  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing quality, typically of a person

Etymology

From Latin attributum (that which is assigned), from attribuere (to assign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024444-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ əˈtrɪbjut / (AmE), / əˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to consider that something is caused by or belongs to a particular person or thing
to assign as a cause or source

Examples

Scholars attribute this ancient text to an anonymous monk.
Many people attribute their success to hard work and persistence.
The museum attributed the painting to a famous Renaissance artist.

Synset Examples

We attributed this quotation to Shakespeare
People impute great cleverness to cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impute - to attribute (especially something undesirable) to someone
  • Synonym: to ascribe - to refer something to a supposed cause, source, or author

Etymology

From Latin attribuere (to assign), from ad- (to) + tribuere (to allot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00728262-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ əˈtrɪbjut / (AmE), / əˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
a quality or characteristic that distinguishes an object or person from others
distinguishing quality or characteristic

Examples

Patience is an important attribute for anyone working in customer service.
Honesty is a valued attribute in a friend.
One attribute that sets her apart is her remarkable creativity.

Synset Examples

self-confidence is not an endearing property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: property - a quality or trait belonging to someone or something
  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing feature, as of a person's character
  • Synonym: feature - a distinctive attribute or aspect of something

Etymology

From Latin attributum (a thing assigned), from attribuere (to assign to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05857567-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ əˈtrɪbjut / (AmE), / əˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to regard something as belonging to or resulting from a particular cause, source, or category; to assign or classify.
regard or classify as belonging to something

Examples

Taxonomists often attribute newly discovered plants to existing species based on their characteristics.
The committee will attribute patients to different categories according to their symptoms.
Some researchers attribute these findings to errors in the initial experiment design.

Synset Examples

The biologist assigned the mushroom to the proper class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to place something in a particular group or category

Etymology

From Latin attribuere (to assign).

Synset POS: v
