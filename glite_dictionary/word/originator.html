originator

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdʒ.əˌneɪ.tər / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.neɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who creates, initiates, or invents something new, especially an idea, plan, or process
person who initiates or creates something

Examples

As the originator of the project, she oversaw every stage from conception to completion.
The patent listed him as the originator of the new technology.
Many consider Leonardo da Vinci an originator of modern scientific thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creator - a person who brings something into existence
  • Synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new methods or ideas
  • Antonym: follower - a person who follows or imitates another

Etymology

From Latin originare (to originate) + -or (agent noun suffix).
