pang

1. NOUN

/ pæŋ / (AmE), / pæŋ / (BrE)
a sudden sharp pain or painful emotion
a sharp, sudden pain

Examples

She felt a sudden pang of guilt as she remembered her promise.
A pang shot through his chest, making him stop in his tracks.
Seeing the goodbye letter, he experienced a brief but intense pang of regret.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ache - a continuous or dull pain
  • Synonym: twinge - a sudden, brief pang of pain

Etymology

Probably from Latin *punctum* (prick, point), influenced by Old French *panque*.

2. NOUN

/ pæŋ / (AmE), / pæŋ / (BrE)
A sudden sharp feeling of emotional or mental pain or distress.
sudden sharp feeling of pain or distress

Examples

He felt a pang of guilt after telling the lie.
The sight of the old house gave her a pang of nostalgia.
I suddenly experienced a pang of jealousy when I saw them together.

Synset Examples

a pang of conscience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinge - a sudden, sharp localized pain, either physical or emotional

Etymology

From Latin *pangere* (to fix, fasten), related to pain and punishment.

3. NOUN

/ pæŋ / (AmE), / pæŋ / (BrE)
a sudden sharp feeling, especially of pain or emotion
sudden sharp feeling

Examples

She felt a sudden pang of guilt when she saw the old photograph.
Hunger pangs kept him awake late into the night.
He tried to ignore the pang of envy as he watched his friend's success.

Synset Examples

pangs of regret
she felt a stab of excitement
twinges of conscience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinge - a sudden, sharp localized pain
  • Synonym: stab - a sudden sharp sensation or feeling

Etymology

From Latin *pangere* (to fasten, fix), related to pain.
