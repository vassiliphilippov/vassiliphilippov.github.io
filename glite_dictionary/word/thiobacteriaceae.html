Thiobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪ.oʊ.bækˌtɪəriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌθʌɪ.əʊ.bækˌtɪəriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of free-living, coccoid to rod-shaped bacteria that obtain energy by oxidizing sulfur or sulfur compounds.
family of sulfur-oxidizing bacteria
medical

Examples

Scientists isolated a new species within the Thiobacteriaceae from a hot spring environment.
Members of the Thiobacteriaceae are known for their role in the sulfur cycle.
The metabolism of Thiobacteriaceae involves the oxidation of sulfur compounds for energy.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'thio-' (sulfur) and 'bacterium' with the family suffix '-aceae.'

2. NOUN

/ θaɪ.oʊ.bækˌtɪəriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / θaɪ.əʊ.bækˌtɪəriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of free-living, coccoid to rod-shaped bacteria that derive energy from oxidizing sulfur or sulfur compounds
family of sulfur-oxidizing bacteria
medical

Examples

Members of the Thiobacteriaceae family are commonly found in sulfur-rich environments.
Scientists have isolated new species of Thiobacteriaceae from deep-sea vent ecosystems.
The Thiobacteriaceae exhibit metabolic pathways that enable them to oxidize sulfur compounds.

Etymology

From Greek 'theion' (sulfur) + Neo-Latin 'bacteria' + '-aceae' (family suffix).
