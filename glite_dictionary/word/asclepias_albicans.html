Asclepias albicans

1. NOUN

/ æsˈkliːpiəs ælˈbaɪkənz / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpiəs ælˈbaɪkənz / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to the southwestern United States, characterized by mostly leafless, white, waxy stems and whitish, star-shaped flowers.
a southwestern US milkweed plant species

Examples

Asclepias albicans can be found in arid regions of Arizona and California.
Botanists studied the unique waxy stems of Asclepias albicans to understand its drought adaptation.
The whitish, star-shaped flowers of Asclepias albicans attract a variety of pollinators in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - any of various plants of the genus Asclepias

Etymology

Asclepias from Greek Asklēpiós, the god of medicine; albicans from Latin 'albicare' (to be white).
