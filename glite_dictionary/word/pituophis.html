Pituophis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtuˈoʊfɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɪtjʊˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, non-venomous snakes found in North America, including gopher snakes and bull snakes.
a genus of large North American snakes

Examples

The Pituophis are commonly found in sandy plains and prairies across North America.
Herpetologists often study Pituophis due to their varied patterns and widespread habitat.
Some farmers appreciate Pituophis snakes for their role in controlling rodent populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull snake - a large, non-venomous North American snake, Pituophis catenifer sayi
  • Synonym: gopher snake - another common name for Pituophis snakes

Etymology

From Greek 'pitys' (pine) and 'ophis' (snake), naming for habitat and form.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtuˈoʊfɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɪtjuːˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, nonvenomous colubrid snakes native to North America, commonly known as bull snakes.
genus of North American bull snakes

Examples

The Pituophis genus includes several species found throughout North America.
Some farmers encourage Pituophis in their fields because the snakes help control rodents.
The distinctive hissing sound is a notable behavior of many Pituophis species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull snake - a large nonvenomous snake of North America, member of Pituophis genus

Etymology

From Greek 'pitys' meaning pine and 'ophis' meaning snake, referring to their preferred habitats and reptilian nature.
