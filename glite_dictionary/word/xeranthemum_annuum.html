Xeranthemum annuum

1. NOUN

/ zɛrˈænθəməm æˈnuəm / (AmE), / zɪəˈrænθɛməm æˈnjuːəm / (BrE)
A species of everlasting flower usually bearing purple blooms, native to southern Europe to Iran and widely cultivated.
a species of everlasting flower

Examples

The Xeranthemum annuum is prized by gardeners for its vivid purple flowers and long-lasting blooms.
Dried Xeranthemum annuum are often used in floral arrangements because they retain their color.
She planted Xeranthemum annuum in her garden to attract butterflies and create beautiful dried bouquets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everlasting flower - common name for species with durable, papery blooms

Etymology

From Greek 'xeros' (dry) + 'anthemon' (flower) and Latin 'annuum' (annual).
