Beethoven, Ludwig van

1. PROPN

/ bəˈtoʊvəˌn / (AmE), / bəˈtəʊvən / (BrE)
(1770–1827) A German composer and pianist, widely recognized as one of the greatest composers in the history of Western music. He is known for his symphonies, sonatas, and chamber works, and continued composing despite losing his hearing later in life.
German composer and pianist, 1770–1827

Examples

Many consider Beethoven to be the greatest composer of all time.
Despite becoming deaf, Beethoven composed some of his most famous works later in life.
The orchestra performed Beethoven's Ninth Symphony to a standing ovation.

Etymology

From Ludwig van Beethoven, his full name, of Dutch and German origin.
