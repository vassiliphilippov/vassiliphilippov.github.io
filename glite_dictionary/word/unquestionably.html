unquestionably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be doubted; without question or doubt.
without doubt; certainly

Examples

He is unquestionably the best player on the team this year.
The restaurant is unquestionably popular among locals and tourists alike.
Her dedication to her work is unquestionably impressive.

Synset Examples

Fred Winter is unquestionably the jockey to follow
they hired unimpeachably first-rate faculty members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without any doubt; surely
  • Antonym: doubtfully - in a manner marked by doubt or uncertainty

Etymology

From 'unquestionable' + '-ly', based on 'question' from Latin 'quaestio'.

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is without doubt; certainly or definitely.
without doubt; certainly

Examples

Her talent is unquestionably recognized by critics and audiences alike.
That artwork is unquestionably authentic, according to the expert's report.
He is unquestionably the best candidate for the position.

Synset Examples

it was decidedly too expensive
she told him off in spades
by all odds they should win

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without any doubt
  • Antonym: doubtfully - in a doubtful or uncertain manner

Etymology

From 'unquestionable' (from Latin 'quaestiō' meaning 'question') + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00037329-r
Synset POS: r
