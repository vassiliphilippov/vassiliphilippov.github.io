viscose

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪs.koʊs / (AmE), / ˈvɪs.kəʊs / (BrE)
A type of rayon fabric made from cellulose fibers using the viscose process, known for its silk-like feel and drape.
rayon fabric from cellulose fibers

Examples

The dress was made of a soft viscose that draped beautifully.
Manufacturers often use viscose for lightweight summer clothing.
This shirt feels like silk, but it's actually viscose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rayon - a synthetic fiber made from regenerated cellulose

Etymology

From Latin 'viscosus' (sticky) via French 'viscose', due to its sticky solution in production.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈvɪs.kəʊs / (BrE)
a cellulose-based material made by treating cellulose with caustic soda and carbon disulfide, used in the production of rayon and films
cellulose-based material for making rayon

Examples

The shirt was made from soft viscose, which draped beautifully.
Manufacturers often blend viscose with other fibers to improve texture and durability.
She prefers dresses made of viscose for their lightweight feel and breathability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rayon - a type of textile fiber made from regenerated cellulose

Etymology

From Latin 'viscosus' (sticky, viscous); adopted in English in the 20th century for the textile process.
