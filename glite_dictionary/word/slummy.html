slummy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslʌmi / (AmE), / ˈslʌmi / (BrE)
(of an area or housing) of poor quality, dirty, and resembling a slum; indicative of poverty or neglect.
resembling a slum; poor and neglected

Examples

The old buildings in that neighborhood look quite slummy after years of neglect.
She grew up in a slummy district where poverty was everywhere.
The city is working to renovate its most slummy areas to improve living conditions.

Synset Examples

a slummy part of town
industrial growth pockets have very slummy conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a squalid and overcrowded urban street or district inhabited by very poor people

Etymology

From 'slum' (a poor, overcrowded urban area) + '-y' (adjectival suffix).
