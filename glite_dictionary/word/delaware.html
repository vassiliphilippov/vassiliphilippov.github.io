Delaware

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛləˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈdɛləˌwɛə / (BrE)
a river in the eastern United States that rises in the Catskill Mountains, flowing south along the Pennsylvania-New York and New Jersey borders before emptying into Delaware Bay.
river in eastern United States

Examples

The Delaware is well known for its scenic beauty and historic significance.
Many people enjoy kayaking on the Delaware during the summer months.
The Delaware forms part of the boundary between Pennsylvania and New Jersey.

Etymology

Named after Thomas West, 3rd Baron De La Warr, an early English colonial governor of Virginia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛləˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈdɛləwɛə / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian-speaking Native American people originally inhabiting New Jersey, New York, Delaware, and Pennsylvania
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Delaware were known for their skill in fishing and farming.
A descendant of the Delaware tribe spoke at the ceremony.
Many Delaware were forced to move west during the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lenape - another name for the Delaware people

Etymology

Named after the Delaware River, which in turn was named after Lord De La Warr, an early English colonial governor.

Named after the Delaware people, who in turn were named after Lord De La Warr, an early English colonial governor.
