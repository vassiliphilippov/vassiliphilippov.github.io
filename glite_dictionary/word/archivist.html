archivist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect, organize, maintain, and provide access to archives or historical records
person responsible for archives

Examples

The archivist spent years preserving important historical documents for future generations.
She consulted the archivist to locate records from the early 1900s.
A skilled archivist must be highly organized and detail-oriented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: librarian - a person who works in or manages a library

Etymology

From French 'archiviste', from 'archive', ultimately from Latin 'archīvum' (government records).
