arboretum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrbəˈritəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːbəˈriːtəm / (BrE)
A place where many different types of trees and shrubs are grown for study, exhibition, and conservation.
garden or facility for trees and shrubs

Examples

The university campus includes an impressive arboretum open to the public year-round.
Many rare species of trees are preserved in the national arboretum for research purposes.
On our field trip, we toured an arboretum and learned about different kinds of conifers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botanical garden - garden dedicated to the collection and study of a wide variety of plants

Etymology

From Latin 'arbor' (tree) + '-etum' (a place for).
