independently

1. ADV

/ ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəntli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is done individually, without being connected with others; separately.
individually or separately

Examples

Each student worked independently on the assignment to avoid influencing each other's results.
The twins prefer to dress independently, choosing their own unique styles.
Each witness was interviewed independently to ensure unbiased testimony.

Synset Examples

the clothes were hung severally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separately - as a distinct or individual entity

Etymology

From independent + -ly; 'independent' from Latin 'independens', meaning 'not hanging from' or 'not contingent'.

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəntli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəntli / (BrE)
In a way that does not rely on or involve others; on one's own.
without help from others

Examples

The company operates independently from its parent organization.
She was able to solve the problem independently without any supervision.
After moving away to college, he learned to live independently.

Synset Examples

the children worked on the project independently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomously - in a self-governing or self-directed way
  • Antonym: dependently - in a way that relies on others

Etymology

From 'independent' + '-ly'; 'independent' from Latin 'independens' (not hanging from something).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00182097-r
Synset POS: r
