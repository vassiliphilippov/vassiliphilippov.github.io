pintle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪntəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪntəl / (BrE)
A pin or bolt on which something pivots, especially forming the pivot of a hinge.
pivot pin or bolt of a hinge

Examples

The door swung smoothly on its pintle without any creaking.
The gate's broken pintle made it impossible to open properly.
They replaced the rusted pintle to restore the hinge's function.

Etymology

From Middle English 'pyntel', probably from Old French diminutive of 'pointe' (point).
