anorectic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈrɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈrɛktɪk / (BrE)
suffering from anorexia nervosa; abnormally thin as a result of this condition.
relating to anorexia nervosa; pathologically thin
medical

Examples

The patient appeared anorectic after months of extreme dieting.
Some fashion models have been criticized for looking anorectic on the runway.
Doctors monitored her closely because she was dangerously anorectic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorexic - suffering from or relating to anorexia nervosa

Etymology

From Greek anorektikos, from an- (not) + orektikos (appetite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00992003-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈrɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈrɛktɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from anorexia nervosa, an eating disorder characterized by an abnormally low body weight and fear of gaining weight.
person with anorexia nervosa
medical

Examples

The clinic specializes in treating anorectic patients with a multidisciplinary approach.
After months of counseling, the young anorectic began to regain a healthier outlook on food.
Family support is crucial for the recovery of an anorectic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorexic - person suffering from anorexia nervosa

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + orexis (appetite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09815192-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈrɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈrɛktɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) causing a loss of appetite.
causing loss of appetite (drug)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anorectic medication to help control the patient's excessive appetite.
Researchers tested several anorectic agents for their potential use in treating obesity.
Although effective as an anorectic drug, it must be used under strict medical supervision.

Synset Examples

an anorectic (or anorexigenic) drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorexigenic - causing loss of appetite

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + orektikos (appetite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00323945-s
Synset POS: s
