Manawydan

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæ.nəˈwɪ.dæn / (AmE), / ˌman.əˈwɪ.dan / (BrE)
In Welsh mythology, a Celtic sea god and son of Llyr, featured in the Mabinogion.
Celtic sea god in Welsh mythology

Examples

The legend describes how Manawydan guided his people across the sea.
In Welsh tales, Manawydan is praised for his wisdom and resilience.
Scholars debate the ancient stories concerning Manawydan and his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manannán - Irish sea god, analogous figure in Celtic mythology

Etymology

From Welsh mythology; Manawydan fab Llŷr, related to the Irish sea god Manannán mac Lir.
