ontological

1. ADJ

/ ɑnˈtɑlədʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒlədʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy that deals with the nature of existence, being, or reality (ontology)
relating to existence or being (philosophy)

Examples

The philosopher presented an ontological argument for the existence of God.
Debates about artificial intelligence often raise ontological questions about what it means to be conscious.
Her research focuses on the ontological status of mathematical objects.

Synset Examples

ontological speculations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphysical - relating to metaphysics, a branch of philosophy concerned with the fundamental nature of reality
  • Degree Lower: epistemological - relating to epistemology, the branch of philosophy dealing with knowledge and belief

Etymology

From French 'ontologique', from Greek 'on' (being) + '-logy' (study of).
