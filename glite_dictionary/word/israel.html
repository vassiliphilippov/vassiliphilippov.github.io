Israel

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪz.reɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈɪz.reɪ.əl / (BrE)
A country in southwestern Asia, located at the eastern end of the Mediterranean Sea; established as a Jewish republic in 1948.
country in southwestern Asia

Examples

After years of planning, they finally visited Israel on a cultural tour.
The technology sector in Israel has grown rapidly over the past decade.
Many international headlines focus on the ongoing conflict affecting Israel and its neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palestine - historic region in Western Asia

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Yisra'el', the biblical patriarch and name of the ancient kingdom.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɪz.ri.əl / (AmE), / ˈɪz.ri.əl / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom of the Hebrew tribes in the southeastern Mediterranean, established around 1025 BC and conquered by the Assyrians in 721 BC.
ancient Hebrew kingdom in the southeastern Mediterranean

Examples

The kingdom of Israel was founded by Saul around the 11th century BC.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from ancient Israel near the Mediterranean coast.
The fall of Israel to the Assyrians marked a turning point in Hebrew history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Judah - ancient southern kingdom of the Hebrews after the division from Israel
  • Degree Higher: Assyria - ancient empire that conquered Israel in 721 BC

Etymology

From Hebrew Yisra'el, meaning 'God contends' or 'he struggles with God'.
