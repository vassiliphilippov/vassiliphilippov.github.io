chinaberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnəˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnəbɛri / (BrE)
A tree native to northern India and China, with purple blossoms and small inedible yellow fruits, often grown as a shade tree in the southern United States.
tree with purple flowers and yellow berries

Examples

The chinaberry tree in her backyard provided much-needed shade during the summer.
After the storm, yellow berries from the chinaberry lay scattered across the lawn.
Some gardeners plant chinaberry for its decorative leaves and flowers.

Etymology

From 'China' (the country) and 'berry', referring to the origin and fruit of the tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnəˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnəbɛri / (BrE)
An evergreen tree of tropical America, having pulpy fruit that contains saponin, which was used as soap by Native Americans.
a tree with soap-like fruit, tropical America

Examples

The chinaberry tree's fruit was once used by Native Americans as a natural soap.
A large chinaberry grows near the river, its leaves always green even in winter.
Children were warned not to eat the chinaberry tree's bright but bitter berries.

Etymology

Likely from resemblance to the Asian chinaberry tree (Melia azedarach), though the species differs.
