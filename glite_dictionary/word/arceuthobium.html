Arceuthobium

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌsuːθoʊˈbiːəm / (AmE), / ɑːˌsjuːθəˈbiːəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly American parasitic plants, known as dwarf mistletoes, that grow on conifers.
genus of parasitic plants on conifers

Examples

Arceuthobium species are important parasites that threaten commercial forests.
Foresters monitor Arceuthobium infestations to protect pine crops.
The growth of Arceuthobium on conifer branches can severely damage the host tree.

Etymology

From Greek 'arkeuthos' (juniper) and 'bios' (life), used as a botanical genus name.

genus of dwarf mistletoes parasitic on conifers

Arceuthobium species are notorious for causing significant damage to forest conifers.

Etymology

From Greek arkeuthos (juniper) + bios (life), referring to the plant's life on conifers.
