bindweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪndˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndwiːd / (BrE)
any of several twining or creeping plants of the genera Convolvulus and Calystegia, often regarded as weeds due to their fast-growing and invasive nature
twining or creeping weed; plant genus Convolvulus or Calystegia

Examples

Bindweed can quickly take over flowerbeds if not kept in check.
Gardeners often struggle to remove bindweed from their vegetable patches.
The white, funnel-shaped flowers of bindweed look attractive but can smother other plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - climbing or twining plants of the morning glory family, often with showy flowers

Etymology

From 'bind' + 'weed', referring to its twining habit of binding around other plants.
