arid

1. ADJ

/ ˈær.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈær.ɪd / (BrE)
(of land or climate) having very little or no rain; too dry or barren to support vegetation.
too dry; lacking rainfall

Examples

The region's arid landscape makes agriculture nearly impossible without irrigation.
Prolonged droughts have made the soil increasingly arid and infertile.
Despite the arid conditions, some hardy plants manage to survive in the desert.

Synset Examples

an arid climate
a waterless well
miles of waterless country to cross

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry - lacking moisture or water
  • Antonym: humid - having a high amount of moisture in the air

Etymology

From Latin aridus (dry, parched).

2. ADJ

/ ˈærɪd / (AmE), / ˈærɪd / (BrE)
Lacking interest, excitement, or vitality; lifeless and dull.
lacking vitality; dull, lifeless

Examples

His lectures were so arid that students often struggled to stay awake.
The novel has an arid style that makes it difficult to engage with emotionally.
Despite its technical brilliance, the film is ultimately arid and uninspiring.

Synset Examples

a technically perfect but arid performance of the sonata
a desiccate romance
a prissy and emotionless creature … settles into a mold of desiccated snobbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeless - having no vitality; dull or dead
  • Antonym: vital - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin aridus (dry, parched).

Synset POS: s
