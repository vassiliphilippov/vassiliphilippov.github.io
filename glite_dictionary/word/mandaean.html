Mandaean

1. NOUN

/ mænˈdiː.ən / (AmE), / mænˈdeɪ.ən / (BrE)
a member of a small Gnostic religious sect originating in Jordan, now mainly in Iraq and Iran, that regards John the Baptist as its chief prophet
member of a Gnostic sect recognizing John the Baptist

Examples

The Mandaean community has preserved many ancient traditions for centuries.
As a Mandaean, he participated in rituals unique to his faith.
Many Mandaeans now live as refugees due to unrest in their homeland.

Etymology

From Mandaic *mandayya* (gnostics, knowers), from *manda* (knowledge).

2. ADJ

/ mænˈdiːən / (AmE), / manˈdiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Mandaean people, their language, or their culture
relating to the Mandaean people or culture

Examples

She studied the ancient Mandaean texts for her research in religious studies.
The Mandaean community has preserved many unique traditions over centuries.
They spoke in the Mandaean language during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mandaean - a member of the Mandaean people

Etymology

From Neo-Mandaic Mandāʾī, from Mandaic *manda* (knowledge).

3. NOUN

/ mænˈdiːən / (AmE), / mænˈdiːən / (BrE)
the form of Aramaic spoken and written by the Mandaeans, an ethno-religious group from the Middle East
form of Aramaic used by the Mandaeans

Examples

Scholars study Mandaean texts to learn about Gnostic traditions and language.
The Mandaean dialect preserves many features lost in other forms of Aramaic.
A rare Mandaean manuscript was discovered in southern Iraq.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aramaic - a Semitic language once widespread in the Near East

Etymology

From Mandaean, relating to the Mandaeans, an ancient Gnostic people; from Mandaic (language name) and 'Mandaean' (ethnonym from Mandaean word for 'knowledge').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07001468-n
Synset POS: n
