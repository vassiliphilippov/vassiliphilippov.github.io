grooved

1. ADJ

/ ɡruvd / (AmE), / ɡruːvd / (BrE)
(of a pattern, habit, or activity) established as if settled into a groove or rut; regular and unchanging.
settled as if in a rut or routine

Examples

After years in the same job, his days became grooved and predictable.
Their conversation followed a grooved pattern, rarely venturing into new topics.
She worried that her life had become too grooved to allow for spontaneity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - done repeatedly or regularly, as a habit
  • Synonym: routine - regular way of doing things
  • Antonym: changeable - likely to change; not fixed or settled
