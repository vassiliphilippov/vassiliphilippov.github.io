stoicism

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊ ɪˌsɪz əm / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ ɪˌsɪz əm / (BrE)
the quality of enduring pain or hardship without displaying feelings or complaint; indifference to pleasure or pain
enduring pain without complaint

Examples

She faced the loss of her job with remarkable stoicism, refusing to let it upset her.
The survivors endured the harsh winter with quiet stoicism and determination.
Despite the pain, he maintained his stoicism and never complained to anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resignation - the acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable
  • Synonym: resilience - capacity to recover quickly from difficulties
  • Antonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive to feelings or pain

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Stoikē (Στοά), referring to the Stoa Poikile where Zeno taught, and 'Stoic' (philosopher), plus '-ism'.
