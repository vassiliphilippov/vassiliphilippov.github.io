exciting

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing feelings of eagerness and enthusiasm; making someone feel very interested or eager.
causing excitement or enthusiasm

Examples

The roller coaster was the most exciting ride at the amusement park.
She got an exciting new job in a different city.
It was an exciting moment when the team scored the winning goal.

Synset Examples

an exciting account of her trip

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; causing weariness or lack of excitement
  • Synonym: thrilling - extremely exciting or stimulating
  • Degree Lower: interesting - holding the attention; arousing curiosity or interest

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'excite,' from Latin 'excitare' (to call out, rouse).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing great interest or enthusiasm; stimulating and engaging.
stimulating interest, enthusiasm

Examples

The new roller coaster was the most exciting ride I have ever experienced.
She found the novel so exciting that she finished it in one sitting.
Their discussion about the future of technology was truly exciting.

Synset Examples

an exciting novel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boring - uninteresting; dull; not stimulating

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'excite', from Latin 'excitare' (to call out, rouse).
