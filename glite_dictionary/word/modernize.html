to modernize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become more modern, especially by adopting the latest technology or ideas
make or become more modern

Examples

Several public schools have begun to modernize their science labs with new equipment.
The city plans to modernize its transportation system over the next five years.
As industries modernize, job requirements and workplaces continue to change.

Synset Examples

Many countries in Asia are now developing at a very fast pace
Viet Nam is modernizing rapidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - to make something more modern or up to date
  • Antonym: to antiquate - to make obsolete or old-fashioned

Etymology

From French 'moderniser', based on Latin 'modernus' (modern).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑdərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪz / (BrE)
to make changes or improvements to something so that it is up to date or more suitable for current needs
make something more modern or up to date

Examples

The city plans to modernize its public transportation system over the next decade.
They decided to modernize the kitchen by installing new appliances and cabinets.
Many companies need to modernize their websites to stay competitive in today's market.

Synset Examples

You should overhaul your car engine
overhaul the health care system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - make something more current or modern
  • Synonym: to renovate - restore to a better or more modern condition

Etymology

From French moderniser and Latin modernus (modern).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00262962-v
Synset POS: v
