dyspnoea

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈnoʊ.i.ə / (AmE), / dɪsˈnɪə / (BrE)
difficulty or labored breathing; shortness of breath, especially as a medical symptom.
difficulty or labored breathing (medical)
medical british

Examples

The patient complained of severe dyspnoea following exertion.
Chronic heart failure can often present with symptoms like dyspnoea and fatigue.
The doctor noted the onset of dyspnoea during the physical examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyspnea - the American English spelling of 'dyspnoea'
  • Degree Lower: apnea - temporary cessation of breathing
  • Degree Higher: hyperpnea - increased depth and rate of breathing

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'pnoia' (breathing).
