to etherialise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈθɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something ethereal; to render light, airy, or otherworldly.
make ethereal or airy
archaic

Examples

The artist sought to etherialise her feelings through delicate watercolor paintings.
His poetic words seemed to etherialise the mundane, turning daily life into a dreamscape.
The morning mist would etherialise the landscape, making it seem unearthly and serene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to etherealize - make ethereal (alternate spelling, especially US)

Etymology

From 'ethereal' + '-ise'; influenced by the adjective 'ethereal', from Latin 'aetherius'.
