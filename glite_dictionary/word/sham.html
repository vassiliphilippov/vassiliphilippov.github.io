sham

1. NOUN

/ ʃæm / (AmE), / ʃæm / (BrE)
a thing that is not what it is purported to be; a fake or a fraud
something fake or false

Examples

Many believed the charity was a sham set up to steal donations.
The entire contest turned out to be a sham and no prize was ever awarded.
He exposed the document as a complete sham after a careful review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - an act of deceiving or misrepresenting; something intended to deceive
  • Antonym: genuine - something real or authentic

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect sham (to shame, to trick), possibly related to 'shame'.

2. ADJ

/ ʃæm / (AmE), / ʃæm / (BrE)
made or done to deceive; pretended or false
false or pretended (adjective)

Examples

Her sham confidence quickly vanished under pressure.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; imitation or counterfeit
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic

Etymology

Perhaps from early 17th-century English slang, of unknown origin.

3. VERB

/ ʃæm / (AmE), / ʃam / (BrE)
to pretend to be or to have something in order to deceive others; to feign
pretend; feign

Examples

He tried to sham interest in the conversation, but it was obvious he was distracted.
She decided to sham illness to avoid attending the meeting.
They could tell he was shamming sleep when the lights turned on.

Synset Examples

She assumed indifference, even though she was seething with anger
he feigned sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feign - to pretend to feel or be affected by something
  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: to assume - to put on a false appearance

Etymology

Originates from English 'sham' (noun), possibly from earlier slang or Middle English, imitative.

4. NOUN

/ ʃæm / (AmE), / ʃæm / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be something they are not, or who deceives others by false pretenses
person who pretends or deceives

Examples

Many people were taken in by the sham posing as a charity worker.
The crowd soon realized the speaker was a sham with no real qualifications.
He was exposed as a sham after his lies came to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposter - a person who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive
  • Synonym: fraud - a person intending to deceive for personal gain
  • Antonym: genuine - real; truly what it is said to be

Etymology

Originated in the 17th century, possibly from the English dialect sham (to trick, to mock).

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ ʃæm / (AmE), / ʃæm / (BrE)
to pretend to be or to have something in order to deceive others
pretend with intent to deceive

Examples

He tried to sham illness to avoid going to work.
She managed to sham surprise when she opened the predictable gift.
He shammed a limp to get extra sympathy from the audience.

Synset Examples

He feigned that he was ill
He shammed a headache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feign - to pretend to feel or be affected by something
  • Synonym: fake - to pretend something is real or true with intent to deceive
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true

Etymology

Probably from earlier noun 'sham' meaning 'a trick', of unknown origin.
