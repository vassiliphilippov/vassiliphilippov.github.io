longitudinally

1. ADV

/ lɑːn.dʒəˈtuː.dɪn.əl.i / (AmE), / lɒn.dʒɪˈtjuː.dɪ.nəl.i / (BrE)
In a way that involves repeated observations or measurements over a period of time, especially to study changes and developments.
over time; through repeated measurements

Examples

The researchers tracked changes in the participants' health longitudinally over ten years.
She examined the effects of the intervention by collecting data longitudinally.
Educational progress was measured longitudinally from primary school through high school.

Synset Examples

We studied the development of the children longitudinally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-sectionally - in a way that gives a snapshot at one point in time

Etymology

From 'longitudinal' + '-ly'; 'longitudinal' from Latin 'longitudo' (length).

2. ADV

/ ˌlɑːndʒəˈtjuːdənəli / (AmE), / ˌlɒndʒɪˈtjuːdɪnəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is measured by longitude, or along the long axis of something.
in the direction of longitude or lengthwise

Examples

The researchers measured the specimens longitudinally to track growth over time.
The bridge was reinforced longitudinally to improve its structural integrity.
The fibers run longitudinally along the length of the leaf, providing strength.

Synset Examples

longitudinally measured

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laterally - in a way that relates to the side or sides

Etymology

From 'longitudinal' + '-ly'; 'longitudinal' from Medieval Latin 'longitudinalis', from Latin 'longitudo' (length).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00130452-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˌlɑːndʒəˈtuːdɪnəli / (AmE), / ˌlɒndʒɪˈtjuːdɪnəli / (BrE)
In the direction of the length of something; along the longest dimension.
in the direction of length

Examples

The beam was split longitudinally for analysis.
The scientist measured the growth of the plant longitudinally over several months.
When cutting the cucumber, slice it longitudinally to get long strips.

Synset Examples

He cut the paper lengthwise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length

Etymology

From 'longitudinal' + '-ly'; 'longitudinal' from Latin 'longitudo' (length).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
