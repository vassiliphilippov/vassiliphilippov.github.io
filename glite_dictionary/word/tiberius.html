Tiberius

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈbɪəriəs / (AmE), / tɪˈbɪəriəs / (BrE)
(42 BC–AD 37) The second Roman emperor, successor and stepson of Augustus, known for his capable military leadership and later for his autocratic rule.
second Roman emperor (42 BC–AD 37)

Examples

The reign of Tiberius was marked by political intrigue and suspicion.
After Augustus died, Tiberius ascended the throne as Emperor of Rome.
Historians debate whether Tiberius was an effective ruler or a paranoid despot.

Etymology

From Latin 'Tiberius', a Roman family name.

2. PROPN

/ taɪˈbɪəriəs / (AmE), / taɪˈbɪəriəs / (BrE)
(42 BC–AD 37) The second Emperor of Rome, succeeding Augustus, known for his military successes and later for his suspicious and autocratic rule.
Roman Emperor (42 BC–AD 37), successor of Augustus

Examples

Tiberius ruled the Roman Empire after the death of Augustus.
The reign of Tiberius was marked by both military strength and political suspicion.
Historians debate whether Tiberius was a wise ruler or a paranoid tyrant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Augustus - First Roman Emperor and predecessor of Tiberius
  • Degree Higher: Caligula - Third Roman Emperor, successor to Tiberius

Etymology

From Latin Tiberius, a Roman family name, possibly derived from the river Tiber (Tiberis).
