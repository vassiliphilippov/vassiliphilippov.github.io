unilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈlætərəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
involving, occurring on, or affecting only one side, part, or party
involving one side or party

Examples

The company made a unilateral decision without consulting the other stakeholders.
The patient suffered from unilateral hearing loss in his right ear.
A unilateral ceasefire was declared by one side of the conflict.

Synset Examples

unilateral paralysis
a unilateral decision

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bilateral - involving two sides or parties

Etymology

From Latin 'unus' (one) + 'latus, later-' (side).

2. ADJ

/ ˌjuː.nəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(in anthropology or biology) Tracing descent from only one parent or ancestral line, either the paternal or maternal side.
tracing descent from one parental line

Examples

In certain cultures, family inheritance is strictly unilateral, passing down only through the mother's side.
The tribe has a unilateral system of descent, so children belong officially to the father's lineage.
Their study examined the effects of unilateral kinship patterns on social organization.

Etymology

From Latin 'unus' (one) + 'latus' (side).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01419956-s
Synset POS: s
