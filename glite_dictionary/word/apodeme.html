apodeme

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌdiːm / (AmE), / ˈapəʊˌdiːm / (BrE)
A ridge-like internal projection of the exoskeleton of an arthropod that supports internal organs and serves as an attachment point for muscles.
internal support structure in arthropods
medical

Examples

The apodeme in certain beetles is exceptionally large, providing greater leverage for the jaw muscles.
Researchers examined the strength of the apodeme to understand how it supports the insect’s internal organs.
Muscle fibers attach to the apodeme, enabling the arthropod to move its limbs efficiently.

Etymology

From Greek apodēma (support), from apo- (from) + dēma (a body, structure).
