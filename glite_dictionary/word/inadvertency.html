inadvertency

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnədˈvɝːtəntsi / (AmE), / ˌɪnədˈvɜːtəntsi / (BrE)
the quality of being inattentive or careless, especially by failing to fulfill duties or responsibilities
inattentiveness; neglect of responsibility

Examples

His repeated inadvertency at work eventually led to serious mistakes.
The accident resulted from inadvertency rather than deliberate misconduct.
She apologized for her inadvertency in missing the important meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'inadvertentia' (inattention), from 'in-' (not) + 'advertere' (to turn toward).
