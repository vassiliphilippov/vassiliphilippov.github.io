psychotherapist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈθɛrəpɪst / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈθɛrəpɪst / (BrE)
A person trained to treat mental and emotional disorders by psychological methods rather than medical or physical ones.
mental health professional; therapist for emotional disorders
medical

Examples

She started seeing a psychotherapist to cope with her anxiety and depression.
The psychotherapist helped his clients develop healthier coping mechanisms.
Becoming a licensed psychotherapist requires years of study and supervised training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapist - A person skilled in a particular kind of therapy
  • Degree Higher: psychiatrist - A medical doctor specializing in mental illness

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + therapist, from Greek therapeia (healing).
