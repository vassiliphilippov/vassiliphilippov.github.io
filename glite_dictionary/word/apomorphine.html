apomorphine

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈmɔːrfin / (AmE), / ˌapəˈmɔːfiːn / (BrE)
a drug derived from morphine, used as an emetic and in small doses as a sedative, but less potent than morphine
a morphine derivative drug
medical

Examples

The doctor administered apomorphine to induce vomiting in the case of poisoning.
Researchers studied the effects of apomorphine on patients with Parkinson's disease.
Because apomorphine is less potent than morphine, its use is limited to specific medical situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphine - a powerful opiate painkiller

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (away from) + 'morphine', indicating its derivation from morphine.
