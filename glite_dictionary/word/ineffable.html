ineffable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛfəbl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛfəbl / (BrE)
too great or extreme to be expressed or described in words; beyond expression.
too great to be described in words

Examples

The ineffable beauty of the sunset left everyone speechless.
She was overwhelmed by an ineffable feeling of joy.
Some experiences are so profound, they are simply ineffable.

Synset Examples

indefinable yearnings
indescribable beauty
ineffable ecstasy
unspeakable happiness
unutterable contempt
a thing of untellable splendor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe; beyond words
  • Synonym: unspeakable - too bad or extreme to be expressed in words
  • Antonym: expressible - able to be expressed or described in words

Etymology

From Latin ineffabilis, from in- (not) + effabilis (able to be expressed).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛfəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛfəbl̩ / (BrE)
too sacred or holy to be spoken or named
too sacred to be uttered

Examples

To some believers, the name of God is so ineffable that it must never be spoken aloud.
Ancient texts refer to an ineffable word that only high priests could know.
In certain traditions, the divine is considered ineffable and thus unpronounceable by mortals.

Synset Examples

the ineffable name of the Deity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unspeakable - too shocking or dreadful to be described or spoken about

Etymology

From Latin ineffabilis, from in- (not) + effari (to utter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02063021-s
Synset POS: s
