Buphthalmum

1. NOUN

/ bʌpˈθælməm / (AmE), / bʌpˈθælməm / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the aster family, native to Europe and characterized by yellow daisy-like flowers.
genus of flowering plants in the aster family

Examples

Buphthalmum is commonly found in meadows across central and southern Europe.
The vibrant yellow flowers of Buphthalmum make it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Buphthalmum genus in the Mediterranean region.

Etymology

From Greek *bous* (ox) + *ophthalmos* (eye), referring to the eye-like shape of the flower heads.

A genus of flowering plants in the aster family (Asteraceae), native to Europe and containing two or three species.
a plant genus in the aster family

Examples

The genus Buphthalmum is known for its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers.
Many gardeners appreciate Buphthalmum for its long blooming season in the summer.
