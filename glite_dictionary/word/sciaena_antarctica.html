Sciaena antarctica

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈiː.nə ænˌtɑːrkˈtɪkə / (AmE), / saɪˈiː.nə ænˌtɑːkˈtɪkə / (BrE)
A large food fish found in Australian waters, closely resembling the maigre.
large Australian food fish species

Examples

The commercial catch of Sciaena antarctica has declined significantly in recent years.
Many chefs in Australia prize Sciaena antarctica for its delicate flavor and texture.
The Sciaena antarctica is often confused with the maigre due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maigre - a large food fish of warm seas (Argyrosomus regius), closely related species

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus name 'Sciaena', species name from 'antarctica', referring to southern distribution.
