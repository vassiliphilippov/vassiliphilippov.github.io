Lampris

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmprɪs / (AmE), / ˈlamprɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fish known as opahs, belonging to the family Lampridae.
genus of marine fish (opahs)

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Lampris.
The Lampris are noted for their deep, laterally compressed bodies and vivid coloration.
Researchers study the migratory patterns of Lampris to understand more about ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opah - a large, colorful marine fish of the genus Lampris

Etymology

From Greek lampros (bright, shining), possibly referring to the fish's coloration.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.prɪs / (AmE), / ˈlam.prɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, colorful oceanic fishes in the family Lampridae, commonly known as opahs.
a genus of oceanic fish (opahs)

Examples

The genus Lampris includes large fish known as opahs or moonfish.
Scientists have observed that Lampris species are capable of whole-body endothermy.
Members of the genus Lampris are found in temperate and tropical oceans worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lampridae - the family of brightly colored oceanic fishes including the opah
