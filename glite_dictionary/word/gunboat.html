gunboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a small, shallow-draft vessel equipped with mounted guns, used for coastal patrols or operations in rivers and shallow waters
small armed patrol vessel

Examples

The navy deployed a gunboat to patrol the river and deter smugglers.
During the conflict, several gunboats escorted the larger warships near the coast.
A single gunboat can navigate shallow waters where larger vessels cannot enter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: warship - a ship equipped for warfare
  • Synonym: patrol boat - a small naval vessel used for patrolling coastal areas

Etymology

Formed from 'gun' + 'boat', denoting a boat fitted with guns.
