empathy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpəθi / (AmE), / ˈɛmpəθi / (BrE)
the ability to understand and share another person's feelings and experiences
understanding and sharing others' feelings

Examples

Her empathy towards the victims helped them feel understood and supported.
Teaching children empathy can improve their social relationships and emotional well-being.
He showed great empathy when he listened patiently to his friend’s problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathy - feeling of pity and sorrow for someone else's misfortune
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of feeling or interest

Etymology

From Greek 'empatheia' (passion, state of emotion), from 'en' (in) + 'pathos' (feeling).
