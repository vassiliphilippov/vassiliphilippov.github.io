Homo sapiens

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊ ˈseɪpiənz / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ ˈseɪpiənz / (BrE)
the species of modern humans, distinguished by advanced cognitive abilities, language, and complex tool use
the species of modern humans

Examples

Fossil evidence shows that Homo sapiens migrated out of Africa roughly 70,000 years ago.
Unlike other primates, Homo sapiens developed complex language and symbolic thought.
The DNA of Homo sapiens is remarkably similar to that of other great apes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human - a member of the species Homo sapiens; a person

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'wise man.'

3. NOUN

/ hoʊmoʊ ˈseɪpiənz / (AmE), / həʊməʊ ˈsapiːənz / (BrE)
the species of modern humans distinguished by advanced cognitive abilities and upright posture
the scientific name for modern humans

Examples

Many scientists agree that Homo sapiens first appeared in Africa about 300,000 years ago.
Fossil evidence indicates that Homo sapiens coexisted with other hominid species for thousands of years.
As a species, Homo sapiens have developed complex languages and cultures.

Synset Examples

Hetero sapiens often overlook the diversity of human relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human - a member of the species Homo sapiens

Etymology

From Latin 'homo' (man) and 'sapiens' (wise).
