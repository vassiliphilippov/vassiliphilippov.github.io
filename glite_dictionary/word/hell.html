hell

1. NOUN

/ hɛl / (AmE), / hɛl / (BrE)
(in various religions) a place where the souls of the dead are believed to go, often as a realm of punishment or the underworld.
the world of the dead (religious concept)

Examples

Many ancient cultures imagined hell as a place beneath the earth where souls are judged.
The story described a hero who traveled to hell to rescue a lost friend.
In some beliefs, hell is divided into different regions for different kinds of souls.

Synset Examples

No one goes to Hades with all his immense wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - the underworld in Greek mythology, dwelling place of the dead

Etymology

From Old English *hell*, from Proto-Germanic *haljō* (underworld).

2. NOUN

/ hɛl / (AmE), / hɛl / (BrE)
A situation or experience that causes great difficulty, pain, or suffering.
a cause of great suffering or difficulty

Examples

After losing his job and home, his life became a living hell.
Some people say that war is hell because of the suffering it brings.
Waiting for the medical results was sheer hell for her.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heaven - a place or state of perfect happiness, often contrasted with hell

Etymology

/ hɛl / (AmE), / hɛl / (BrE)

a place or state of intense pain or turmoil
