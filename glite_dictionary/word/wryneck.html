wryneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈraɪnɛk / (BrE)
A condition in which the head is twisted to one side due to muscle contraction in the neck.
neck condition causing twisted head
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with wryneck after observing the abnormal tilt of his head.
Long-term wryneck can lead to discomfort and restricted movement in the neck.
She wore a special collar to help relieve her wryneck condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.nɛk / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.nɛk / (BrE)
an Old World woodpecker (genus Jynx) that is noted for its ability to twist its neck in a peculiar manner, often in a snake-like fashion when threatened
Old World woodpecker with twisting neck habit

Examples

The wryneck startled the researchers with its unusual twisting motion.
During migration, a wryneck was spotted in the park by local birdwatchers.
Unlike most woodpeckers, the wryneck rarely drums on trees.

Etymology

From the bird's habit of twisting its neck, using 'wry' (twisted) and 'neck'.
