perfunctory

1. ADJ

/ pərˈfəŋktəri / (AmE), / pəˈfʌŋktəri / (BrE)
(of an action or gesture) carried out with minimum effort or reflection, as a mere formality.
done with little effort or as a formality

Examples

She gave a perfunctory nod and walked away without saying a word.
His apology sounded perfunctory, lacking any genuine regret.
The inspector made only a perfunctory check of the building before leaving.

Synset Examples

a one-candidate pro forma election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough or detailed; on the surface only
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From Latin perfunctorius (careless, perfunctory), from perfungi (to discharge, to get through with).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈfʌŋktəri / (AmE), / pəˈfʌŋktəri / (BrE)
(of an action or gesture) carried out with minimal effort, reflection, or interest; done as a formality without real attention or enthusiasm.
done with minimal effort or care

Examples

She gave a perfunctory nod before turning away to continue her work.
His apology sounded perfunctory, as if he didn’t really mean it.
During the meeting, the manager made a perfunctory review of the report without discussing any details.

Synset Examples

a casual (or cursory) inspection failed to reveal the house's structural flaws
a passing glance
perfunctory courtesy
In his paper, he showed a very superficial understanding of psychoanalytic theory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough or deep; only concerned with surface aspects
  • Antonym: thorough - done with great care and attention to detail

Etymology

From Latin 'perfunctorius' (careless, perfunctory), from 'perfungi' (to discharge, get through with).
