tickseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkˌsid / (AmE), / ˈtɪksiːd / (BrE)
Any of various plants of the genus Coreopsis, typically having bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and grown as ornamentals.
plant; Coreopsis genus with daisy-like yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with bright yellow tickseed that bloomed all summer.
She planted tickseed along the walkway to add a splash of color.
Many wildflower meadows include tickseed because it attracts butterflies and bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coreopsis - the genus of flowering plants known as tickseed

Etymology

Refers to the small, seed-like fruit resembling ticks.
