dominance

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːmənəns / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəns / (BrE)
the state in which one side of the body is more developed or more frequently used than the other, often seen in handedness or footedness
superior development of one body side
medical

Examples

The dominance of his right hand made tasks with his left feel awkward.
Physical therapy helped balance out the dominance of her stronger leg.
Language skills are sometimes linked to cerebral dominance in the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laterality - preference for or greater development of one side of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'dominari' (to rule, dominate) via French 'dominance'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəns / (BrE)
the state of having power and control over someone or something
state of having power or control over others

Examples

The company established its dominance in the market through aggressive expansion.
Her dominance over the group was clear from the way everyone deferred to her decisions.
The pack leader asserted his dominance by standing tall and making eye contact.

Synset Examples

her apparent dominance of her husband was really her attempt to make him pay attention to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supremacy - the state of being superior to all others in authority, power, or status
  • Antonym: subordination - the act or state of being lower in rank or position

Etymology

From Latin 'dominari' (to rule), via French 'dominance'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəns / (BrE)
(Genetics) The phenomenon in which one allele of a gene pair is expressed in the phenotype while the other is not.
genetic expression of one allele over another
medical

Examples

In Mendelian genetics, dominance explains why some traits mask the presence of others.
The concept of dominance is crucial in understanding patterns of inheritance.
Incomplete dominance results in a blending of parental traits rather than one dominating.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recessive - an allele or trait only expressed when two copies are present
  • Degree Lower: codominance - a situation where both alleles are fully expressed in the phenotype

Etymology

From Latin dominantia (rule, ascendancy), from dominari (to rule).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.mə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəns / (BrE)
the power or right to control others, give orders, or make decisions
power to control or command

Examples

The company's absolute dominance in the market left little room for competitors.
His dominance over the group was clear from the way everyone followed his instructions.
She quickly established her dominance in the debate with confident and assertive arguments.

Synset Examples

he has the authority to issue warrants
deputies are given authorization to make arrests
a place of potency in the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders or make decisions
  • Antonym: subordination - the state of being lower in rank or position

Etymology

From Latin 'dominari' (to rule, govern), from 'dominus' (lord, master).
