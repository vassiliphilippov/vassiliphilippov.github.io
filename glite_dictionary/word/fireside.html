fireside

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪər.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə.saɪd / (BrE)
the home, especially as a place of warmth, comfort, and family life, often symbolized by the side of a fireplace
home and family life (figurative)

Examples

The family gathered by the fireside to share stories on winter evenings.
Defending our firesides became a rallying cry during the invasion.
She longed for the peace and security of her childhood fireside.

Synset Examples

driven from hearth and home
fighting in defense of their firesides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearth - home as symbolized by the area in front of a fireplace

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'side', originally referring to the side of a fireplace, later used figuratively for home life.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌsaɪd / (BrE)
the area near or in front of a fireplace, often paved and extending into a room
area near a fireplace

Examples

On cold winter evenings, they like to gather by the fireside with a good book.
She placed a rug near the fireside to make the room feel warmer and cozier.
The family spent hours talking and laughing around the old stone fireside.

Synset Examples

they sat on the hearth and warmed themselves before the fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearth - the floor of a fireplace, often extending into the room

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'fire' and 'side', referring to the side of the fire.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08599227-n
Synset POS: n
