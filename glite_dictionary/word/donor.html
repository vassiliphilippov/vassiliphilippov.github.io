donor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.nər / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.nə / (BrE)
a person who gives blood, tissue, or an organ for medical use in another person
person giving blood, tissue, or organ for transplant
medical

Examples

The patient was lucky to find a matching donor in time for the transplant.
Blood banks rely on regular donors to maintain necessary supplies.
Becoming an organ donor can help save several lives after your death.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recipient - a person who receives blood, tissue, or an organ in a medical procedure

Etymology

From Latin *donare* (to give); related to Latin *donum* (gift).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.nə / (BrE)
a person who gives something, especially money or property, to help a person or organization
person who gives something, especially for charity

Examples

The museum was able to build a new wing thanks to a generous donor.
Each donor received a letter of thanks for their contribution.
She became a regular donor to several environmental charities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recipient - a person who receives something
  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or help to a person or cause

Etymology

From Latin 'donare' (to give), from 'donum' (gift).
