steps

1. NOUN

/ stɛps / (AmE), / stɛps / (BrE)
the route, course, or path taken by someone as they move or have moved along, often used figuratively for following someone's actions or decisions
route or path taken by someone

Examples

She retraced her steps to find her lost keys.
He tried to follow in his father's steps and become a doctor.
After wandering for hours, their steps finally led them back to the campsite.

Synset Examples

I followed in his steps
he retraced his steps
his steps turned toward home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footsteps - the marks or impressions left by feet, or the path taken by walking

2. NOUN

/ stɛps / (AmE), / stɛps / (BrE)
A set or flight of stairs, especially one located outside a building.
a set of stairs or stairway

Examples

We sat on the steps outside the house, watching the sunset.
The front steps were slippery after the rain, so be careful.
Tourists gathered on the stone steps of the cathedral to take photos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staircase - a set of stairs and its framework
  • Antonym: ladder - a structure for climbing consisting of two side pieces and rungs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04305309-n
Synset POS: n
