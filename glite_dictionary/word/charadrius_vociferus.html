Charadrius vociferus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪdriəs voʊˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / kəˈreɪdriəs vəˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
A North American plover, commonly known as the killdeer, found near inland waters and fields, distinguished by its loud, high-pitched call.
species of North American plover, the killdeer

Examples

The Charadrius vociferus is easily recognized by its piercing, two-note call near riverbanks and open fields.
Birdwatchers spotted a Charadrius vociferus sheltering its eggs on the gravelly shore.
Researchers documented the habits of Charadrius vociferus throughout the breeding season in agricultural landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killdeer - common name for Charadrius vociferus, a North American plover

Etymology

From Latin: 'Charadrius' (a bird mentioned by Pliny) and 'vociferus' (loud-voiced).
