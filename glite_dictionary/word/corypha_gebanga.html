Corypha gebanga

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈriːfə dʒəˈbæŋgə / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪfə dʒəˈbaŋgə / (BrE)
a large-leaved palm native to Malaysia, the Philippines, and northern Australia, with leaves used for thatching or plaiting containers
large tropical palm with useful leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Corypha gebanga are traditionally used for thatching roofs in rural villages.
Farmers often weave baskets from the sturdy fronds of the Corypha gebanga.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Corypha gebanga across northern Australia.
