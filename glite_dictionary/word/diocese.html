diocese

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.siːs / (BrE)
A district under the pastoral care and jurisdiction of a bishop in the Christian Church.
district under a bishop's care

Examples

The diocese will hold a charity event to support local families next month.
He was appointed bishop of a large diocese in the south of the country.
New parishes have been established within the diocese to serve the growing population.

Etymology

From Latin *diœcesis*, from Greek *dioikēsis* (administration, government).
