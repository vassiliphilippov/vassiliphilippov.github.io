durian

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊriən / (AmE), / ˈdjʊəriən / (BrE)
A tree of southeastern Asia that bears large, edible, oval fruit with a hard, spiny rind.
tree bearing spiny edible fruit

Examples

The durian is famous for both its strong smell and unique flavor.
Farmers cultivate the durian tree in many parts of Southeast Asia.
Some people travel long distances to sample fresh durian during its harvesting season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durian - the fruit of the durian tree

Etymology

From Malay 'duri' meaning 'thorn', referring to the fruit's spiny covering.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʊriən / (AmE), / ˈdjʊəriən / (BrE)
A large, spiny fruit native to Southeast Asia, noted for its strong odor and creamy, flavorful flesh; its seeds can be roasted and eaten.
large spiny Southeast Asian fruit with strong smell

Examples

The durian is famous for its pungent smell, which some people find overwhelming.
We tasted fresh durian at the market during our trip to Malaysia.
After removing the spiky shell, the creamy flesh of the durian was surprisingly sweet.

Etymology

From Malay 'duri' meaning 'thorn', referring to the fruit's spiky husk.
