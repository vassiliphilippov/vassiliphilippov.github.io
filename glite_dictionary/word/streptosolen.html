Streptosolen

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptoʊˈsoʊlən / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəʊˈsəʊlən / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plant in the nightshade family, represented by the marmalade bush, known for its bright orange flowers.
genus of plant; marmalade bush

Examples

The Streptosolen is often grown in gardens for its vibrant orange blossoms.
Botanists have classified the marmalade bush under the genus Streptosolen.
Many hummingbirds are attracted to the bright flowers of the Streptosolen.

Etymology

From Greek 'streptos' (twisted) + 'solen' (tube), referring to the shape of the flower's corolla.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptəˈsoʊlən / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈsəʊlən / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, containing a single species, the marmalade bush.
genus of flowering plants (marmalade bush)

Examples

The Streptosolen is known for its bright orange flowers resembling marmalade.
Gardeners often cultivate Streptosolen as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
Streptosolen thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmalade bush - a species of flowering plant in the genus Streptosolen

Etymology

From Greek 'streptos' (twisted) and 'solen' (tube), referring to the plant's twisted floral tube.
