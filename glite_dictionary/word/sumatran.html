Sumatran

1. ADJ

/ suːˈmɑːtrən / (AmE), / suːˈmɑːtrən / (BrE)
of or relating to the island of Sumatra or its people, culture, or products
of or relating to Sumatra or its people

Examples

He ordered a box of Sumatran cigars from the shop.
The zoo recently welcomed a rare Sumatran tiger cub.
She admired the intricate patterns of the Sumatran textiles displayed in the market.

Synset Examples

Sumatran cigars

Etymology

From Sumatra, the name of the island + -an (adjective and demonym suffix).

2. NOUN

/ suːˈmɑːtrən / (AmE), / suːˈmɑːtrən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Sumatra, an island in Indonesia
native or inhabitant of Sumatra

Examples

Many Sumatrans rely on agriculture and fishing for their livelihood.
The Sumatran community in the city celebrates their cultural festivals every year.
She interviewed a Sumatran about life on the island after the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indonesian - a native or inhabitant of Indonesia

Etymology

From Sumatra (the island) + -an (demonymic suffix).
