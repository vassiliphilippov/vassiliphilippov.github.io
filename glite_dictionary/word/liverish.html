liverish

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvərɪʃ / (BrE)
Feeling slightly ill or irritable, especially as if suffering from indigestion.
irritable or slightly ill, as if from indigestion

Examples

After eating too much rich food, he felt liverish and grumpy all morning.
She grew liverish whenever she missed her usual morning walk.
If you wake up liverish, a brisk walk might help your mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Synonym: bilious - affected by or associated with nausea or vomiting; bad-tempered

Etymology

From 'liver', based on the old belief that the liver influences mood and health, plus '-ish'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvərɪʃ / (BrE)
Suffering from or resembling the symptoms of a liver disorder, often appearing irritable, sickly, or experiencing gastric distress.
showing signs of liver trouble or gastric upset

Examples

After eating too much rich food, I often feel a bit liverish the next morning.
He appeared liverish and out of sorts after days of overindulgence.
She complained of a liverish sensation and refused breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilious - affected by or associated with nausea or vomiting; irritable

Etymology

From 'liver' + suffix '-ish', meaning 'like or pertaining to the liver'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
