to deplumate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpluː.meɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpluː.meɪt / (BrE)
to remove the feathers from (a bird); to strip of feathers
remove feathers from a bird

Examples

The farmhand began to deplumate the chickens in preparation for cooking.
Scientists carefully deplumated several birds to study their skin condition.
After catching the goose, he set about deplumating it for the feast.

Synset Examples

pull a chicken
pluck the capon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - remove feathers from a bird

Etymology

From Latin 'deplumare', from 'de-' (off) + 'pluma' (feather).
