chichi

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃiːˌʃiː / (AmE), / ˈʃiːʃiː / (BrE)
affectedly trendy and fashionable, especially in a way perceived as pretentious or showy
pretentiously fashionable

Examples

The restaurant has a chichi décor that some guests find a bit pretentious.
She moved to a chichi neighborhood known for its upscale shops and trendy cafes.
He wore a chichi suit that stood out at the casual party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - fashionable and elegant
  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not following current fashion, out of style

Etymology

From French chichi (affectation), originally an exclamation of surprise or admiration.

2. NOUN

/ ʃiːˈʃiː / (AmE), / ʃiːˈʃiː / (BrE)
a person who dresses in a way that is very fashionable or trend-conscious, sometimes considered pretentious or affected
a trendily dressed or pretentious person

Examples

At every party, there’s always at least one chichi making a dramatic entrance.
He liked to play the role of the chichi in his stylish, expensive suits.
Some people found her a bit of a chichi, but others admired her style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fashionista - a person who is passionate about fashion, especially one who works in fashion

Etymology

From French chichi (airs and graces), probably imitative of affected speech.

3. NOUN

/ ʃiːˈʃiː / (AmE), / ʃiːˈʃiː / (BrE)
affected or elaborate elegance, especially in fashion or style; pretentious chic.
affected or pretentious elegance

Examples

The restaurant is known for its chichi décor and trendy clientele.
Her style leaned towards chichi, with elaborate dresses and bold accessories.
Despite its chichi, the party lacked warmth and genuine hospitality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chic - stylish and fashionable elegance
  • Synonym: pretentiousness - showiness or an attempt to impress that is not genuine
  • Antonym: understated - elegant in a subtle, not showy, way

Etymology

From French *chichi* (affectation, fuss).
