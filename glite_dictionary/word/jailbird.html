jailbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a person who has been in prison repeatedly; a habitual criminal
person repeatedly imprisoned; habitual criminal

Examples

He was a well-known jailbird with a long record of offenses.
After his fourth arrest, the police labeled him a habitual jailbird.
Despite his reputation as a jailbird, he claimed he wanted to change his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convict - a person found guilty of a crime and sentenced to imprisonment
  • Synonym: prisoner - someone incarcerated in prison

Etymology

Formed from 'jail' + 'bird', likening a prisoner to a bird in a cage; first known use in the 17th century.
