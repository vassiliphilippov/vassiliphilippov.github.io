courtship

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtʃɪp / (BrE)
the act of seeking the romantic affection of someone, especially with the intention of marriage
period of seeking romantic affection

Examples

Their courtship lasted two years before they decided to get married.
During the courtship, he sent her flowers and wrote her love letters regularly.
The film tells the story of an unusual courtship in a small rural town.

Synset Examples

it was a brief and intense courtship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooing - the act of trying to win the love of someone, especially with marriage in mind
  • Degree Lower: flirtation - playful behavior intended to arouse romantic interest
  • Degree Higher: marriage - the formal union of two people as partners in a personal relationship

Etymology

From court +‎ -ship; 'court' from Old French 'courre' (to be in attendance at a royal court), meaning to seek favor.
