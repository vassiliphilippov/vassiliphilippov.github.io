scholarship

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːlərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈskɒləʃɪp / (BrE)
an amount of money given to a student to help pay for their education, usually awarded on the basis of academic achievement or merit
financial aid for students based on merit

Examples

She received a full scholarship to study engineering at the university.
His excellent grades helped him win a scholarship for the upcoming academic year.
Applying for a scholarship can help reduce the financial burden of higher education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - an amount of money given for a purpose, especially study or research
  • Synonym: bursary - a scholarship to attend a college or university, usually based on financial need

Etymology

From Middle English 'scolarschip' (state of being a scholar).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɑː.lɚ.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈskɒl.ə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
profound academic or scholarly knowledge, especially demonstrated by extensive study or learning
profound academic or scholarly knowledge

Examples

Her scholarship on medieval history is widely respected in academic circles.
The scholarship demonstrated in this book sets a new standard for the field.
He was admired for his scholarship and dedication to advancing literary criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning - knowledge acquired by study
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or education

Etymology

From Middle English *scoler* (scholar) + *-ship* (state or condition).
