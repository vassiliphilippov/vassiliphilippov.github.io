skank

1. NOUN

/ skæŋk / (AmE), / skæŋk / (BrE)
a rhythmic dance performed to reggae or ska music, characterized by bending forward and extending the hands while bending the knees
a reggae or ska dance style

Examples

The crowd started to skank enthusiastically to the ska band's lively beat.
He learned how to skank at his first reggae concert.
Fans filled the dance floor, each showing off their unique skank moves.

slang

3. VERB

/ skæŋk / (AmE), / skæŋk / (BrE)
to dance in a style with bent knees and swinging arms, especially to ska or reggae music
dance in a ska/reggae style

Examples

The crowd started to skank as soon as the ska band began playing.
He loves to skank at reggae concerts all night long.
Teenagers were skanking energetically to the upbeat rhythm.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Jamaican English, origin uncertain; associated with ska and reggae culture.
