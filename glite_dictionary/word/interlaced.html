interlaced

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈleɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈleɪst / (BrE)
having a pattern in which lines, strips, or parts cross over and under each other, resembling fretwork or latticework.
with crossing or woven pattern

Examples

The windows featured beautiful interlaced ironwork in a decorative pattern.
She admired the interlaced branches forming a natural canopy above the garden path.
The artist created an interlaced border that resembled Celtic designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woven - made by interlacing threads or strips
  • Antonym: solid - without openwork or interlacing; continuous or unbroken

Etymology

From interlace, from Old French entrelacer, from entre- (between) + lacer (to lace).
