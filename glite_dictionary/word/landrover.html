landrover

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌroʊvər / (AmE), / ˈlændˌrəʊvə / (BrE)
a vehicle designed for travel over rough or uneven ground; originally a trademark for a type of four-wheel-drive vehicle.
vehicle for rough terrain

Examples

The old landrover took us safely across the rocky paths of the mountain.
Farmers in the region rely on their landrover to reach remote fields.
With a sturdy landrover, the team could transport supplies through muddy trails with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeep - a small, rugged four-wheel drive vehicle originally used by the military

Etymology

From 'Land Rover', a trademarked name for a British automotive brand; by extension, used generically for similar vehicles.
