pooler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpuːlər / (AmE), / ˈpuːlə / (BrE)
a person who shares in and contributes to a general fund or resource for collective use
person contributing to a shared fund or resource

Examples

Each pooler must deposit their share into the community savings account.
The cost of the group gift was easily managed by having every pooler chip in a small amount.
As a regular pooler on shared rides, she rarely drove alone to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contributor - a person who contributes to something, especially a fund

Etymology

Formed from 'pool' (to combine resources) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
