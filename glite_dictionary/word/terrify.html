to terrify

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛr.ɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make someone feel extremely frightened
frighten greatly

Examples

The sudden noise terrified the children during the thunderstorm.
The idea of speaking in public terrifies me.
The horror movie was so realistic that it terrified the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten - to make someone afraid or anxious
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone less upset or frightened

Etymology

From Latin terrere (to frighten) + -fy (a verb-forming suffix from Latin facere, to make).
