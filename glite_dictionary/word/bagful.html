bagful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbæɡ.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a bag; as much as a bag can hold.
the amount a bag holds

Examples

He brought a bagful of apples from the orchard.
She always has a bagful of tricks when entertaining children.
After the fair, they came home with a bagful of candies.

Synset Examples

he ate a large bag of popcorn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basketful - the quantity that a basket will hold
  • Synonym: handful - the amount that can be held in one hand

Etymology

Formed from 'bag' + 'ful' (Old English 'full', denoting 'as much as will fill').
