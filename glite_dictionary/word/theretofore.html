theretofore

1. ADV

/ ˌðɛr.tuˈfɔr / (AmE), / ˌðeə.tuːˈfɔː / (BrE)
Up to that time; before then.
up to that time; before then
archaic legal

Examples

The company had been unprofitable theretofore, but new management turned things around.
No evidence had emerged theretofore to prove his innocence.
He lived in Paris, having theretofore resided in London for many years.

Synset Examples

they had not done any work theretofore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: before - at an earlier time
  • Antonym: afterwards - at a later or subsequent time

Etymology

Formed from 'there' + archaic 'tofore' (before).
