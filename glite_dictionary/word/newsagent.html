newsagent

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person or shop that sells newspapers, magazines, and often other items such as sweets and tobacco
a person or shop selling newspapers and magazines
british

Examples

She stopped by the newsagent on her way to work to buy a newspaper.
Our local newsagent always has the latest magazines and a variety of snacks.
I used to work as a newsagent during the summer holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newsstand - a stall or shop where newspapers and magazines are sold

Etymology

Formed from 'news' + 'agent', indicating an agent dealing in news publications.
