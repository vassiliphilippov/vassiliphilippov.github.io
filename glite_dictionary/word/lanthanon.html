lanthanon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænθəˌnɒn / (AmE), / ˈlanθənɒn / (BrE)
any of the chemical elements in the lanthanide series, with atomic numbers 57 to 71.
element in the lanthanide series
medical

Examples

The rare earth element europium is a lanthanon used in television screens.
Chemists often study lanthanons because of their unique magnetic properties.
Cerium is one of the most common lanthanons found in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanthanide - any of the 15 elements with atomic numbers 57-71

Etymology

From Greek 'lanthanein' (to lie hidden), after the element lanthanum, the first in the series.
