thermostatics

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrmoʊˈstætɪks / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈstætɪks / (BrE)
The branch of thermodynamics concerned with systems in thermal equilibrium, typically studying the equilibrium properties of matter without considering time-dependent processes.
study of thermal equilibrium in thermodynamics

Examples

The principles of thermostatics apply only when the system is in equilibrium.
He studied thermostatics before progressing to thermodynamic processes involving change.
In thermostatics, temperature gradients are assumed to be absent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thermodynamics - branch of physics dealing with heat and energy
  • Degree Higher: thermokinetics - study of thermodynamic processes involving movement or change

Etymology

From 'thermo-' (relating to heat) + 'statics' (study of forces in equilibrium).
