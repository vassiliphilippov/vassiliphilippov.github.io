Polygala vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɪɡələ vəlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪɡələ vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a small perennial plant native to Europe, with branching stems and clusters of blue, pink, or white flowers; formerly believed to stimulate lactation
a European flowering plant species

Examples

The field was dotted with the delicate blue flowers of Polygala vulgaris.
Some herbalists once used Polygala vulgaris in remedies to promote lactation.
We found a thriving patch of Polygala vulgaris growing alongside the country path.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Polygala' meaning 'much milk', and 'vulgaris' meaning 'common'.
