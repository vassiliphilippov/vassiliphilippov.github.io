lonesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊnsəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊnsəm / (BrE)
feeling sad or lonely because of being alone; causing a sense of loneliness
sad or lonely from being alone

Examples

He sometimes feels lonesome when working late at night.
After her friends moved away, the house seemed especially lonesome.
It was a lonesome walk down the empty road.

Synset Examples

felt sad and lonely
the loneliest night of the week
lonesome when her husband is away
spent a lonesome hour in the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lonely - feeling sad because one has no company

Etymology

From lone + -some, dating back to the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊnsəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊns(ə)m / (BrE)
Being the only one; single and isolated from others.
single and isolated; the only one

Examples

A lonesome ship drifted far from the busy ports.
He spent the night beside a lonesome road, miles from any town.
In the garden stood a single, lonesome rose.

Synset Examples

the lone doctor in the entire county
a lonesome pine
an only child
the sole heir
the sole example
a solitary instance of cowardice
a solitary speck in the sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary - existing alone; characterized by being alone
  • Synonym: lone - standing alone or isolated; single
  • Synonym: sole - being the only one

Etymology

From lone + -some, first used in early 19th century American English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02222405-s
Synset POS: s
