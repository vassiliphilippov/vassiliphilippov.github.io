localised

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪzd / (BrE)
confined or restricted to a particular area or location; not spread over a wide area
restricted to a particular area
british

Examples

The doctor determined that the localised pain was due to a minor muscle injury.
A localised power outage affected only three blocks in the neighborhood.
The environmental impact was fortunately localised and did not harm the surrounding region.

Synset Examples

the localized infection formed a definite abscess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: localized - confined or restricted to a particular location (American spelling)

Etymology

From English 'local' + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix) + '-ed' (adjectival past participle suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Limited or restricted to a particular area or place; adapted or oriented for a specific locality.
restricted to a specific place
british

Examples

Efforts to contain the outbreak were successful, and the disease became localised to just a few villages.
The television station provided localised weather reports for each city.
These reforms resulted in a more localised approach to government.

Synset Examples

a decentralized and localized political authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: localized - restricted to a particular place or area (American spelling)
  • Synonym: regional - of or relating to a particular region
  • Antonym: global - worldwide, not limited to a specific place

Etymology

From 'local' + '-ise' (British spelling of '-ize'), meaning to make local.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01111969-s
Synset POS: s
