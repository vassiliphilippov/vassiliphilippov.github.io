Scottishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑː.tɪʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskɒt.ɪʃ.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being Scottish; the characteristics, culture, or identity associated with Scotland or its people.
state or quality of being Scottish

Examples

His deep sense of Scottishness was evident in all his speeches and artwork.
The festival aimed to celebrate Scottishness through traditional music and food.
Many people living abroad try to maintain a strong feeling of Scottishness in their communities.

Synset Examples

Indeed the formation of these regiments helped to unite the Highlanders and Lowlanders, and give them a shared sense of ‘Scottishness’, by changing the image of Highlanders from being backward and savage, to being ‘the very embodiment of Scotland’ (which became clearly evident during the Romanticist period in Scotland).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Englishness - the state or quality of being English
  • Antonym: Irishness - the state or quality of being Irish
  • Antonym: Welshness - the state or quality of being Welsh

Etymology

Formed from 'Scottish' + suffix '-ness' (denoting a state or quality).
