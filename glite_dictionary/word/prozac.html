Prozac

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌzæk / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌzæk / (BrE)
A trademarked name for a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) used as an antidepressant.
antidepressant medication (trademark)
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with depression, her doctor prescribed Prozac to help manage her symptoms.
Many patients have reported feeling better after starting Prozac, but side effects can occur.
Prozac is often discussed in literature about mental health and medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluoxetine - the generic name for the drug sold under the brand Prozac
  • Synonym: antidepressant - drug used to alleviate depression

Etymology

Trademarked brand name for fluoxetine, introduced by Eli Lilly in 1987.
