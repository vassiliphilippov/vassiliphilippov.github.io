affliction

1. NOUN

/ əˈflɪkʃən / (AmE), / əˈflɪkʃən / (BrE)
A cause of great pain, suffering, or distress.
something causing pain or suffering

Examples

After years of searching for a cure, she finally learned to live with her affliction.
Poverty is a terrible affliction that affects millions of people worldwide.
The disease was a serious affliction for the entire community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misery - great suffering or unhappiness
  • Antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or good fortune

Etymology

From Latin afflictio (distress, suffering), from affligere (to strike down).

2. NOUN

/ əˈflɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈflɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
a condition of great suffering, distress, or pain, typically caused by adversity or illness
great suffering or distress

Examples

She endured the terrible affliction with remarkable courage and dignity.
Poverty can be a severe affliction affecting millions worldwide.
After years of suffering, he was finally free of the affliction that had plagued him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misery - a state or feeling of great distress or discomfort
  • Antonym: relief - the alleviation or removal of pain or distress

Etymology

From Latin afflictio, from affligere (to strike down, distress).

3. NOUN

/ əˈflɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈflɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
a state of pain, suffering, or distress, especially one caused by illness or misfortune
pain, suffering, or distress (esp. from illness)

Examples

Despite her chronic affliction, she maintained an optimistic outlook on life.
The drought was a severe affliction for people living in the rural areas.
He devoted his life to helping those suffering from physical or mental afflictions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misery - a state of great suffering or discomfort
  • Antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or benefit

Etymology

From Latin afflictio, afflictionis (a striking down), from affligere (to strike down).
