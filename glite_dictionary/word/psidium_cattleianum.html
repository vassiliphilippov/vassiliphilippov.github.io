Psidium cattleianum

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdiː.əm kætˌliːˈæn.əm / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪ.i.əm kætˌliːˈæn.əm / (BrE)
A small tropical shrub or tree of the myrtle family, native to South America, bearing deep red oval fruit known as strawberry guava.
tropical shrub/tree; strawberry guava

Examples

The Psidium cattleianum thrives in subtropical climates and can be invasive in some regions.
The fruit of Psidium cattleianum is often called strawberry guava due to its sweet flavor.
Gardeners appreciate Psidium cattleianum for its attractive foliage and edible red fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawberry guava - common name for Psidium cattleianum
  • Degree Higher: guava - any tropical tree or shrub of the genus Psidium

Etymology

From Latin; 'Psidium' (genus name) from Greek 'psidion' (pomegranate); 'cattleianum' after British horticulturist William Cattley.
