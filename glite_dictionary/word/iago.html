Iago

1. PROPN

/ iˈɑɡoʊ / (AmE), / iˈɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
the scheming and manipulative villain in William Shakespeare's tragedy 'Othello,' who deceives Othello into killing his wife, Desdemona
villain in Shakespeare's 'Othello'

Examples

Many critics regard Iago as one of the most compelling villains in Shakespearean drama.
The character of Iago is known for his cunning manipulation and deceitful nature.
In 'Othello,' Iago's actions ultimately lead to tragedy for all involved.

Etymology

From Spanish Iago, a form of Jacob; notably used by Shakespeare for the character's name.
