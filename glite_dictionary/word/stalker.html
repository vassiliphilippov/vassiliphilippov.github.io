stalker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔkər / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who moves stealthily or follows and observes someone, typically with unlawful or obsessive intent
person who follows or harasses others

Examples

The police arrested the stalker outside the celebrity's home after a week of surveillance.
She lived in fear after learning that a stalker had been following her for months.
Security cameras caught the stalker prowling around the neighborhood late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursuer - someone who follows another
  • Antonym: victim - person who is harmed or harassed by a stalker

Etymology

From 'stalk' (to pursue stealthily) + '-er' agent noun suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔkər / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who hunts animals by following them stealthily; someone who stalks game
person who hunts game by stealth

Examples

The experienced stalker moved silently through the forest, searching for deer.
As a skilled stalker, he knew how to blend in with his surroundings to avoid being noticed by animals.
The royal estate hired a stalker to manage the deer population on the grounds.

Etymology

From the verb 'stalk' (to pursue stealthily), plus the agent suffix '-er.'
