gyromitra

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪərəˈmʌɪtrə / (BrE)
A fungus of the genus Gyromitra, some species of which are known as false morels and can be poisonous.
a genus of poisonous fungi

Examples

Some species of gyromitra are highly toxic and can be mistaken for edible mushrooms.
The forest floor was dotted with gyromitra after the spring rains.
Mycology students collected several gyromitra to study their unique structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - a common name for fungi in the genus Gyromitra that resemble true morels

Etymology

From New Latin Gyromitra, from Greek gyros (circle) + mitra (headband, turban).
