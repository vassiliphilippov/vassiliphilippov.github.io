Andricus

1. PROPN

/ ˈændrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈandrɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of cynipid gall wasps, chiefly affecting oak trees.
genus of gall wasps affecting oaks

Examples

Researchers discovered several new Andricus species during the study of oak forests.
The galls produced by Andricus wasps are often visible on the branches of oak trees.
Entomologists are interested in how Andricus affects the growth of different oak species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.drɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈæn.drɪ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of gall wasps in the family Cynipidae, chiefly affecting oak trees.
genus of gall wasps affecting oaks

Examples

The Andricus wasps are known for the elaborate galls they induce on oak trees.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Andricus genus in North America.
Many Andricus larvae develop safely inside the plant tissue of their host oaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - small wasps that induce growths (galls) on plants

Etymology

From Greek *andrikos* (manly), referring to a masculine aspect, used in taxonomy.
