Excalibur

1. PROPN

/ ɛkˈskæləbɚ / (AmE), / ɛkˈskælɪbə / (BrE)
the legendary sword of King Arthur, said to possess magical powers
legendary sword of King Arthur

Examples

According to legend, only the true king could pull Excalibur from the stone.
Many stories describe how King Arthur received Excalibur from the Lady of the Lake.
The shining blade of Excalibur became a symbol of Arthur's rightful rule.

Etymology

From Old French Escalibor, from Medieval Latin Caliburnus, of uncertain origin, associated with 'steel' or a Latinized Celtic term.
