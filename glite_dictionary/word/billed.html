billed

1. ADJ

/ bɪld / (AmE), / bɪld / (BrE)
having a beak or bill of a specified kind, usually in compound adjectives.
having a particular type of beak or bill

Examples

The pelican is a billed bird known for its large pouch.
Several species of billed birds inhabit the wetlands.
The researcher observed a sharp-billed finch in the forest.

Synset Examples

a thick-billed bird
a long-billed cap

Etymology

From 'bill' (beak) + -ed, used to form adjectives.
