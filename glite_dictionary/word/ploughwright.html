ploughwright

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈplaʊˌraɪt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make and repair ploughs
person who makes and repairs ploughs
archaic british

Examples

The village once relied on its skilled ploughwright for maintaining farming equipment.
As a young man, he apprenticed under a renowned ploughwright to learn the craft.
With fewer farms using traditional ploughs, the profession of ploughwright has all but disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheelwright - person who makes or repairs wheels
  • Synonym: blacksmith - person who makes or repairs things made of iron

Etymology

Formed from 'plough' + 'wright' (maker or builder), with 'wright' from Old English 'wyrhta' (worker).
