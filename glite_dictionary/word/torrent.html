torrent

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɹ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒr.ənt / (BrE)
A sudden and heavy outpouring of rain.
a heavy outpouring of rain

Examples

The hikers were caught in a torrent that flooded the trail within minutes.
A sudden torrent drenched the city, causing traffic to come to a halt.
Despite the torrent outside, the event continued as planned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downpour - a heavy fall of rain

Etymology

From Latin *torrentem* (rushing, roaring stream).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒrənt / (BrE)
a strong and fast-moving stream of water or other liquid, especially one flowing suddenly or violently
violent, fast-flowing stream of liquid

Examples

During the storm, a torrent of water rushed down the hillside and flooded the village.
After the dam broke, a torrent swept away the bridge in seconds.
He struggled to cross the torrent without being carried off by the powerful current.

Synset Examples

the houses were swept away in the torrent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflowing of water beyond its normal confines
  • Degree Lower: trickle - a small, slow flow of liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'torrentem', meaning 'rushing, roaring', from 'torrēre' (to burn, parch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07422193-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒrənt / (BrE)
A large amount of something that comes suddenly and forcefully; an overwhelming outpouring, usually of words, emotions, or things.
overwhelming outpouring or flood

Examples

The politician faced a torrent of criticism after his remarks.
She burst into a torrent of tears when she heard the news.
Social media was flooded with a torrent of comments during the live broadcast.

Synset Examples

a flood of requests
a torrent of abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - a large amount of something that comes suddenly

Etymology

From Latin 'torrentem' (rushing, roaring), present participle of 'torrēre' (to burn, parch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13797906-n
Synset POS: n
