vaticinator

1. NOUN

/ ˌvætɪˈsɪneɪtər / (AmE), / ˌvatɪˈsɪnətə / (BrE)
A person who foretells or predicts future events, especially with authority or as a profession.
one who foretells the future

Examples

Many regarded her as a vaticinator after her predictions came true.
The medieval court often sought counsel from its trusted vaticinator.
Skeptics doubted the claims of the self-proclaimed vaticinator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophet - a person who predicts the future, often as a divinely inspired leader
  • Synonym: seer - someone who claims to see or predict future events

Etymology

From Latin 'vaticinator', from 'vaticinari' (to prophesy).
