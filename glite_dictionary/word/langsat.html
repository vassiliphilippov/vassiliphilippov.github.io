langsat

1. NOUN

/ læŋˈsɑt / (AmE), / læŋˈsæt / (BrE)
A small, edible tropical fruit with yellowish skin and translucent, fleshy pulp, tasting like a combination of grape and grapefruit.
small tropical fruit with translucent flesh

Examples

The market stall offered fresh langsat alongside mangosteens and durians.
I tasted langsat for the first time during my vacation in Malaysia.
The sweet and slightly tart flavor of langsat makes it a favorite among tropical fruits.

Etymology

From Malay 'langsat', the native name for the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ lɑŋˈsɑt / (AmE), / lɑːŋˈsæt / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Lansium domesticum) of the mahogany family, bearing edible yellowish fruit with sweet-sour pulp.
tropical fruit tree and its edible fruit

Examples

The langsat is prized in Southeast Asia for its delicious, tangy fruit.
We found fresh langsats at the market during our trip to Malaysia.
The langsat tree grows best in warm, humid tropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longan - a related tropical Asian fruit tree

Etymology

From Malay 'langsat', the name of the fruit.
