to announce

1. VERB

/ əˈnaʊns / (AmE), / əˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to formally give the names or details of something, especially winners or participants, usually in public
formally give names or details

Examples

At the end of the ceremony, the host announced the names of the scholarship recipients.
The judge will announce the winners of the competition after the final round.
Once all the votes are counted, they will announce the results on stage.

Synset Examples

He announced the winners of the spelling bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to state something formally or officially

Etymology

From Old French *anoncer*, from Latin *annuntiare* (to announce, report).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00977459-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈnaʊns / (AmE), / əˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to make a public or official statement about something; to make known formally or openly
to make known publicly or officially

Examples

The company will announce its new product line next week.
They announced the results of the vote at noon.
She is planning to announce her retirement this Friday.

Synset Examples

The President declared war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to state something formally or officially
  • Synonym: to proclaim - to announce something officially or publicly
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret; not to announce

Etymology

From Latin annuntiare, from ad- (to) + nuntiare (to report, announce), from nuntius (messenger).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00967903-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈnaʊns / (AmE), / əˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to make something known publicly or officially, especially by speaking or writing
make something known publicly

Examples

The company will announce its new product line next week.
She announced her resignation in a brief statement to the press.
They are announcing the winners of the competition this evening.

Synset Examples

She denoted her feelings clearly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to state officially or formally
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep secret

Etymology

From Latin 'annuntiare' (to announce), from 'ad-' (to) + 'nuntiare' (to report).

4. VERB

/ əˈnaʊns / (AmE), / əˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to give a warning or indication of something that will happen; to foreshadow or presage
to foreshadow or presage

Examples

The first drops of rain announced the impending storm.
Distant thunder announced the arrival of the cold front.
The scent of fresh bread announced that breakfast was nearly ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herald - to be a sign that something is about to happen
  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen before it happens
  • Antonym: to conceal - to prevent something from being seen or known; opposite of foreshadow

Etymology

From Old French 'anoncer', from Latin 'annuntiare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'nuntiare' (to report).

Source: WordNet
