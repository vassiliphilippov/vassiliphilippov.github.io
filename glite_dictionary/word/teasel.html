teasel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈtiːzəl / (BrE)
a tall prickly plant of the genus Dipsacus, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, with flower heads surrounded by spiny bracts; formerly used in textile processing.
prickly plant of the genus Dipsacus

Examples

The field was dotted with tall teasel plants swaying gently in the wind.
In the past, weavers used dried teasel heads to raise the nap on woolen cloth.
The spiny bracts of the teasel make it easy to recognize among other wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dipsacus - the plant genus to which teasels belong

Etymology

From Old English *tesle*, related to 'tease', because the dried heads were used to tease cloth.
