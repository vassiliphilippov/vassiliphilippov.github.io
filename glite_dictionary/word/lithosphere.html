lithosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəsfɪə / (BrE)
The rigid outer part of the Earth, consisting of the crust and upper mantle.
the Earth's rigid outer layer (crust and upper mantle)

Examples

The movement of tectonic plates occurs within the lithosphere of the Earth.
Scientists study the lithosphere to better understand earthquakes and volcanic activity.
The thickness of the lithosphere varies across different regions of the planet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asthenosphere - the semi-fluid layer beneath the lithosphere

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + sphaira (sphere).
