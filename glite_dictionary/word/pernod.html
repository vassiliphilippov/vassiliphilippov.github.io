Pernod

1. PROPN

/ pɛrˈnoʊ / (AmE), / pɛəˈnəʊ / (BrE)
A French anise-flavored liqueur, originally produced as a substitute for absinthe.
anise-flavored French liqueur

Examples

He ordered a glass of Pernod at the café, savoring its distinctive anise flavor.
The recipe calls for a splash of Pernod to enhance the seafood stew.
Many people enjoy their Pernod diluted with cold water over ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastis - an anise-flavored spirit from France, similar to Pernod

Etymology

Named after Henri-Louis Pernod, founder of the company that first produced it in France.
