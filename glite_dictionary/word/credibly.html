credibly

1. ADV

/ ˈkrɛd.ə.bli / (AmE), / ˈkrɛd.ɪ.bli / (BrE)
In a way that can be believed or accepted as true; plausibly or convincingly based on evidence.
in a believable or convincing way

Examples

The witness was able to answer every question credibly during the trial.
She credibly explained the reasons for her late arrival, so nobody doubted her story.
The company credibly claimed that its product was environmentally friendly.

Synset Examples

he talked plausibly before the committee
he will probably win the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plausibly - in a way that seems reasonable or probable
  • Antonym: incredibly - in a way that cannot be believed; extraordinarily

Etymology

From Latin 'credibilis' (worthy of belief) via French 'crédible'.
