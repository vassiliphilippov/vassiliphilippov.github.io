warm-blooded

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔrm ˌblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːm ˌblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of animals) having a body temperature that is internally regulated and relatively constant, regardless of the environment.
having internally regulated body temperature (animals)

Examples

Birds are warm-blooded, meaning their body temperature does not change much with the environment.
Unlike reptiles, mammals are warm-blooded and can thrive in colder climates.
The warm-blooded nature of humans allows us to survive in a wide range of conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold-blooded - (of animals) having a body temperature that changes with the environment

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɔrmˌblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːmˌblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of animals) having a constant body temperature maintained internally, regardless of the external environment.
having an internally regulated body temperature

Examples

Unlike reptiles, birds are warm-blooded and can stay active in cold weather.
Whales are warm-blooded mammals that live in the ocean.
Many warm-blooded animals can survive in both hot and cold climates.

Synset Examples

Cats are warm-blooded creatures that need to stay cozy.
Being warm-blooded allows humans to survive in various climates.
Warm-blooded animals maintain their body heat effectively.
