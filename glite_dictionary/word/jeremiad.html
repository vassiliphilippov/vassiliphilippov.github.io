jeremiad

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛr.əˈmaɪ.æd / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛr.ɪˈmaɪ.ad / (BrE)
A long and mournful complaint or lamentation; a list of woes.
a lengthy mournful complaint

Examples

The editorial turned into a jeremiad about the failings of modern society.
Her speech was little more than a prolonged jeremiad against economic inequality.
Instead of proposing solutions, he delivered a jeremiad filled with dire predictions and sorrowful tales.

Synset Examples

a jeremiad against any form of government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamentation - an expression of sorrow or grief
  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or grievance

Etymology

From French 'jérémiade', from Jérémie (Jeremiah), in reference to the prophet's lamentations.
