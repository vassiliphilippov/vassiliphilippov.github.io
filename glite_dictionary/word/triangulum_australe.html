Triangulum Australe

1. PROPN

/ traɪˌæŋɡjələm ɔˈstreɪli / (AmE), / traɪˌaŋɡjʊləm ɔːˈstreɪliː / (BrE)
a small, bright constellation in the southern sky, located near Circinus and Apus
constellation in southern sky

Examples

The stars of Triangulum Australe are easily visible from the Southern Hemisphere on a clear night.
We located Triangulum Australe with the aid of a star chart.
Triangulum Australe is known for forming a noticeable triangle shape in the sky.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'southern triangle' (triangulum = triangle, australe = southern).
