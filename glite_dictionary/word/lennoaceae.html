Lennoaceae

1. NOUN

/ lɛˌnon.iˈeɪ.siː / (AmE), / lɛnˌnəʊ.iˈæsiː / (BrE)
a family of fleshy, parasitic herbs lacking green foliage and bearing heads of small flowers, native to California and Mexico
family of parasitic herbs (botany)

Examples

The Lennoaceae are notable for their lack of chlorophyll and dependence on host plants.
Research in California has identified several rare plants belonging to the Lennoaceae.
Unlike many other families, members of the Lennoaceae do not have conventional green leaves.

Etymology

Named after the genus Lennona, which was named in honor of the plant collector Juan Lennox; standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ lɛn.i.oʊˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / lɛn.i.əʊˈiː.si.iː / (BrE)
A family of fleshy, parasitic herbaceous plants lacking green foliage and bearing small-headed flowers, native to California and Mexico.
family of parasitic plants (California, Mexico)

Examples

The Lennoaceae are unusual in that they lack green foliage and rely entirely on other plants for nutrition.
Most members of the Lennoaceae can be found in arid regions of California and Mexico.
Botanists study the Lennoaceae to understand the evolution of parasitic plants.

Etymology

From the genus name Lennoa, after Spanish botanist Joaquín Gómez de la Lenna, with the botanical suffix -aceae denoting a plant family.
