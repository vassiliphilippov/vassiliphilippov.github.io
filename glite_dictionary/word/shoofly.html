shoofly

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːflaɪ / (BrE)
An undercover police officer assigned to investigate other police officers, especially suspected of corruption or misconduct.
undercover police officer who investigates other officers

Examples

The department brought in a shoofly to look into the corruption scandal.
No one knew the new detective was really a shoofly sent to observe the squad.
The presence of a shoofly made many officers uneasy during the investigation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːflʌɪ / (BrE)
a child's rocking chair with a seat built between two flat sides shaped like an animal, often a swan or duck
child's animal-shaped rocking chair

Examples

The antique store displayed a beautiful shoofly that resembled a graceful swan.
Her grandmother used to rock her to sleep in a wooden shoofly shaped like a duck.
Collectors prize the intricate animal carvings on old shooflies from the 19th century.
