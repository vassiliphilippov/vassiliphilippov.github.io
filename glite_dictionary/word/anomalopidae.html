Anomalopidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌnoʊməˈlɒpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˌnɒmələʊˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as flashlight fishes, distinguished by light-producing organs beneath their eyes.
family of flashlight fishes

Examples

The Anomalopidae are famed for their ability to produce light beneath their eyes.
Scientists have discovered several new species within the Anomalopidae family in the Pacific Ocean.
Members of Anomalopidae use their glowing organs to attract prey and communicate in the dark waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashlight fish - a common name for fish in the family Anomalopidae, known for their bioluminescent organs

Etymology

From Greek 'anomalos' (irregular) and 'ops' (eye), referring to the fishes' unusual light organs near the eyes.

2. NOUN

/ əˌnoʊməˈlɑːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˌnɒməˈlæpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as flashlight fishes, characterized by light-producing organs beneath their eyes.
family of flashlight fishes

Examples

Some species in the Anomalopidae family emit light from organs under their eyes.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Anomalopidae near coral reefs in the Pacific.
The Anomalopidae are often called flashlight fishes because of their bioluminescent features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashlight fish - A fish with light-producing organs under its eyes, member of Anomalopidae

Etymology

From Greek anōmalos (irregular) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
