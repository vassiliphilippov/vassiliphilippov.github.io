Isolde

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈsoʊldə / (AmE), / ɪˈzəʊldə / (BrE)
A legendary princess in Arthurian romance, the bride of the king of Cornwall, who fell in love with Tristan after they drank a love potion.
legendary princess in Arthurian romance

Examples

The tragic love story of Isolde and Tristan has inspired countless adaptations in art and music.
In Wagner's famous opera, Isolde drinks the potion that seals her fate with Tristan.
Many legends describe Isolde as beautiful and courageous, doomed by the love potion she shared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tristan - A legendary knight and lover of Isolde in Arthurian romance

Etymology

From Old French Iseut/Isault, ultimately from Brythonic names, popularized by medieval romance literature.
