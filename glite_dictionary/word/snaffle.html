snaffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnæfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnafəl / (BrE)
a simple, jointed bit for a horse, usually without a curb chain, used to control the animal's movements
simple jointed horse bit without a curb

Examples

She fitted a new snaffle to the bridle before taking the horse out for a ride.
Many riders prefer using a snaffle for young or inexperienced horses.
The trainer explained the difference between a curb bit and a snaffle to the class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a piece of metal put in a horse’s mouth to control it

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly related to Low German *Snavel* (beak, snout).

/ ˈsnæfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnæfəl / (BrE)

/ ˈsnæf.əl / (AmE), / ˈsnæf.əl / (BrE)

british informal

Examples

If you get there early, you can snaffle all the best bargains.
