Cryptocercidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈsɜːrsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈsɜːsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of wood cockroaches in the order Blattodea, characterized by their association with decaying wood.
family of wood cockroaches

Examples

The Cryptocercidae are known for their symbiotic relationship with wood-digesting protozoa.
Many researchers study Cryptocercidae to understand the evolution of social behavior in cockroaches.
Cryptocercidae primarily inhabit decaying logs in temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Blattodea - an order of insects including cockroaches and termites

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'crypto-' (hidden) and the genus 'Cercus'.

2. NOUN

/ krɪp.toʊ.sɜrˈsɪd.i.aɪ / (AmE), / krɪp.təʊ.sɜːˈsɪd.i.aɪ / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Blattodea, commonly known as wood roaches, found mostly in temperate regions and closely related to termites.
family of wood roaches (insects)

Examples

The Cryptocercidae are often called wood roaches because they live inside decaying logs.
Scientists study Cryptocercidae to better understand the evolutionary relationship between cockroaches and termites.
Unlike most roaches, members of the Cryptocercidae share social behavior traits with termites.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Blattodea - order of insects including cockroaches and termites

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) and 'kerkos' (tail), referring to features of the genus Cryptocercus.
