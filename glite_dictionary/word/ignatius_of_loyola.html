Ignatius of Loyola

1. PROPN

/ ɪɡˈneɪʃəs əv lɔɪˈoʊlə / (AmE), / ɪɡˈneɪʃɪəs əv lɔɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
(1491–1556) A Spanish priest and theologian, founder of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits), and a prominent leader in the Counter-Reformation.
Spanish founder of the Jesuits (1491–1556)

Examples

Many universities around the world are named after Ignatius of Loyola.
Ignatius of Loyola played a crucial role in the Catholic Counter-Reformation.
As the founder of the Jesuits, Ignatius of Loyola emphasized education and missionary work.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ignatius' and the place name 'Loyola', in present-day Spain.
