Muntingia calabura

1. NOUN

/ mʌnˈtɪndʒiə kəˈlæbjʊrə / (AmE), / mʌnˈtɪndʒiə kəˈlæbjʊərə / (BrE)
A fast-growing tropical American evergreen tree with white flowers and edible, fleshy red fruit, grown for its timber, fiber, and fruit.
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Muntingia calabura is sweet and popular with children in tropical regions.
Many gardeners plant Muntingia calabura as a fast-growing shade tree in warm climates.
The bark of Muntingia calabura yields a strong fiber used locally for making ropes.

Etymology

Named after Dutch botanist Abraham Munting; 'calabura' is derived from Spanish, possibly referencing resemblance to the calabash.
