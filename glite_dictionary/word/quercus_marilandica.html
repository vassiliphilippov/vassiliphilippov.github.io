Quercus marilandica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌmærəˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌmærɪˈlændɪkə / (BrE)
a species of small, scrubby oak tree native to central and southeastern United States, known for its dark bark and broad, three-lobed leaves
a type of scrubby oak tree

Examples

The Quercus marilandica is often found in dry, sandy soils of the southeastern United States.
Many wildlife species rely on the acorns produced by the Quercus marilandica for food.
Botanists conducted a survey of Quercus marilandica populations across Texas and Oklahoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackjack oak - common name for Quercus marilandica

Etymology

From Latin: Quercus (oak) + marilandica (of Maryland), indicating origin.
