Amelanchier bartramiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈlænkiɚ bɑːrtrəˈmaɪənə / (AmE), / ˌæmɛˈlæŋkɪə bɑːtrəˈmɪənə / (BrE)
An open-growing shrub native to eastern North America, with pure white flowers and small, waxy, almost black fruits.
shrub with white flowers and dark fruits

Examples

The Amelanchier bartramiana adds striking white blooms to the spring garden.
Botanists often study the Amelanchier bartramiana because of its unique dark fruit.
Berries produced by Amelanchier bartramiana are favored by many birds in eastern forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juneberry - another common name for Amelanchier species

Etymology

From genus name Amelanchier (possibly from Provençal *amelanquier*, meaning service tree) and specific epithet honoring botanist William Bartram.
