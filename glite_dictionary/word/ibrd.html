IBRD

1. PROPN

/ aɪ bi ɑr di / (AmE), / aɪ bi ɑː diː / (BrE)
Initialism for 'International Bank for Reconstruction and Development', a United Nations agency that provides loans to developing countries, guaranteed by its member governments.
United Nations agency providing loans to developing nations

Examples

The IBRD finances projects aimed at reducing poverty in developing countries.
India secured a large infrastructure loan from the IBRD to upgrade its transportation network.
The IBRD is one of the five institutions that form the World Bank Group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Bank - an international financial institution that provides financial and technical assistance to developing countries

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'International Bank for Reconstruction and Development', established in 1944 as part of the World Bank Group.
