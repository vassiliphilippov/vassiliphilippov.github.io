text

1. NOUN

/ tɛkst / (AmE), / tɛkst / (BrE)
a passage from the Bible chosen as the subject of a sermon or discourse, especially in Christian practice
Biblical passage for a sermon

Examples

The preacher selected a text from the Gospel of Matthew to begin his sermon.
Each Sunday, a different text was chosen to guide the congregation's reflections.
She often reflected on the text discussed in church to find personal inspiration.

Synset Examples

the preacher chose a text from Psalms to introduce his sermon

Etymology

From Latin 'textus' (that which is woven, a written work).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07187208-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ tɛkst / (AmE), / tɛkst / (BrE)
a book, especially one used as a standard work for the study of a particular subject in schools or colleges
school or college book for study

Examples

This course uses a new text on modern biology.
Her text for the chemistry class was very informative and up-to-date.
All students are required to purchase the assigned text before the semester begins.

Synset Examples

his economics textbook is in its tenth edition
the professor wrote the text that he assigned students to buy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textbook - a book used as a standard work for the study of a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin 'textus' (woven), referring originally to written material.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06426015-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ tɛkst / (AmE), / tɛkst / (BrE)
to send (someone) a message using SMS or a similar text-based messaging service on a mobile device
send a message by SMS or similar service

Examples

Please text me when you get home so I know you're safe.
She texted her friend the details about the meeting.
I'm texting my boss to let him know I'll be late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: message - to send a written or electronic communication

Etymology

From the noun 'text', repurposed as a verb in the digital age to mean sending written messages electronically.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90001251-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ tɛkst / (AmE), / tɛkst / (BrE)
the main body of written material in a book or document, excluding illustrations, footnotes, or other supplementary elements
main body of written material in a work

Examples

The diagrams help clarify the text for visual learners.
Before reading the text, check the glossary at the end of the chapter.
The text was accompanied by detailed maps and charts.

Synset Examples

pictures made the text easier to understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body - the main part of a book or document

Etymology

From Latin textus (structure, written composition), from texere (to weave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06400222-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ tɛkst / (AmE), / tɛkst / (BrE)
the written words of a document, book, speech, or other piece of writing, as distinct from notes or commentary
the words of a written work

Examples

The text of the speech was distributed to all attendees before the event.
Please read the text carefully and underline important points as you go.
Scholars compared the ancient manuscript's text with modern translations to identify changes.

Synset Examples

there were more than a thousand words of text
they handed out the printed text of the mayor's speech
he wants to reconstruct the original text

Related Terms

  • Antonym: commentary - explanatory notes or interpretation accompanying a text
  • Synonym: manuscript - a handwritten or typed document, especially the author's original text

Etymology

From Latin 'textus' (tissue, literary style), from 'texere' (to weave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06399623-n
Synset POS: n
