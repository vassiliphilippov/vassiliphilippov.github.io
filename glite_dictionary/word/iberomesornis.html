Iberomesornis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪbəroʊmɛˈsɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪbərəʊmɛˈsɔːnɪs / (BrE)
An extinct genus of small, sparrow-sized bird from the Early Cretaceous period, with a vestigial tail, known from fossils found in Spain and considered one of the most primitive birds.
extinct Cretaceous bird genus from Spain

Examples

Fossil remains of Iberomesornis have provided insights into the early evolution of birds.
The discovery of Iberomesornis in Spain highlights the diversity of Cretaceous avifauna.
Researchers believe that Iberomesornis had both primitive and advanced avian features.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek elements: 'Iberia' (the Iberian Peninsula) + 'mesornis' (middle bird).
