to deduce

1. VERB

/ dɪˈdus / (AmE), / dɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to reach a conclusion or decision by reasoning from the available facts or evidence
reach a conclusion by reasoning

Examples

From the clues, the detective was able to deduce who committed the crime.
We can deduce from her accent that she grew up in Scotland.
By examining the evidence, scientists deduced the cause of the phenomenon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infer - to reach a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning
  • Degree Lower: guess - to estimate or suppose without strong evidence

Etymology

From Latin deducere (to lead away, derive).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈdus / (AmE), / dɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
To reach a conclusion by reasoning from available information.
arrive at a conclusion by reasoning

Examples

Based on the evidence, the detective managed to deduce who the culprit was.
Scientists can deduce the age of a fossil through carbon dating.
She tried to deduce the meaning of the word from the context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infer - To deduce or conclude information from evidence and reasoning rather than from explicit statements
  • Antonym: to induce - To derive a general principle from specific instances

Etymology

From Latin deducere (to lead down, derive).
