Threskiornis

1. NOUN

/ θrɛsˈkiːɔrnɪs / (AmE), / θrɛsˈkiːɔːnɪs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Threskiornithidae, commonly known as ibises, characterized by long, down-curved bills and wading habits
genus of ibises (birds)

Examples

The sacred ibis, once widespread in Egypt, belongs to the genus Threskiornis.
Researchers conducted a field study on several species classified within Threskiornis.
Unlike spoonbills, Threskiornis species have straight, slender bills with a distinctive curve.

Etymology

From Greek *thréskios* (religious) + *ornis* (bird).
