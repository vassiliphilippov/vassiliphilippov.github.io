temple

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl / (BrE)
A building or place where a Jewish congregation gathers for worship and religious activities.
Jewish place of worship

Examples

The members of the congregation gathered at the temple for Yom Kippur services.
She attends Hebrew school every Sunday morning at the local temple.
The city built a new temple to serve the growing Jewish community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synagogue - A Jewish house of worship

Etymology

From Latin templum (a consecrated space), passed into English via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl / (BrE)
the flat area on each side of the forehead between the eye and the hairline
side of the forehead

Examples

He rubbed his temple as a headache began to develop.
A blue bruise appeared on her left temple after the fall.
You could see the pulse beating at his temple when he was nervous.

Synset Examples

the veins in his temple throbbed

Etymology

From Old French 'temple', from Latin 'tempulum', possibly by confusion with 'tempor-' (time, relating to the temporal bone).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl / (BrE)
a building devoted to the worship of a god or gods, especially in religions other than Christianity
place of worship building

Examples

The ancient Greeks built impressive temples to honor their gods.
Visitors are required to remove their shoes before entering the Hindu temple.
The city is known for its beautiful temple overlooking the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - Christian place of worship
  • Synonym: mosque - Muslim place of worship
  • Synonym: shrine - place regarded as holy, often housing something sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'templum' (a sacred place).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɛm.pəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.pəl / (BrE)
A building devoted to the worship of a god or gods, or to other special or exalted purposes.
building for worship or special purposes

Examples

They visited the ancient temple perched on the hilltop.
The city constructed a new temple dedicated to science and knowledge.
Many tourists admired the intricate carvings on the temple's walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - a building used for Christian worship
  • Synonym: shrine - a place regarded as holy or sacred

Etymology

From Latin templum (a piece of ground consecrated for worship).
