Dnieper

1. PROPN

/ ˈdniːpər / (AmE), / ˈdniːpə / (BrE)
a major river in Eastern Europe, flowing from Russia through Belarus and Ukraine to the Black Sea
a river in Eastern Europe

Examples

The Dnieper is one of the longest rivers in Europe, stretching for over 2,200 kilometers.
Kyiv, the capital of Ukraine, is located on the banks of the Dnieper.
Many historical events have taken place along the Dnieper River's course.

Etymology

From Russian Днепр (Dnepr), Ukrainian Дніпро (Dnipro), ultimately from an ancient Scythian or Iranian river-name.
