fieldworker

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːldˌwɝːkər / (AmE), / ˈfiːldˌwɜːkə / (BrE)
a researcher who conducts studies or gathers data at the location where the subject of study occurs, rather than in a laboratory or office
person conducting research in the field

Examples

The fieldworker interviewed dozens of villagers to understand local agricultural practices.
Every fieldworker must be prepared to adapt to changing conditions during their research trips.
As a fieldworker, she spent months observing elephants in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: researcher - a person who conducts academic or scientific investigation
  • Antonym: lab worker - someone who conducts research in a laboratory setting

Etymology

Formed from the words 'field' and 'worker', indicating one who works or conducts activities outside, in a specified area of study.
