rehearsal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈhɜrsəl / (AmE), / rɪˈhɜːsəl / (BrE)
a session in which a play, piece of music, or other performance is practiced before being performed in front of an audience
practice session before a performance

Examples

We have a final rehearsal tonight before the opening show.
The choir met every week for rehearsal at the community center.
Her anxiety eased after several rehearsals with the cast.

Synset Examples

he missed too many rehearsals
a rehearsal will be held the day before the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - repeated exercise in or performance of an activity for improvement or preparation

Etymology

From Middle French 'rehercer' (to repeat), from Old French 're-' (again) + 'hercer' (to harrow).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈhɜrsəl / (AmE), / rɪˈhɜːsəl / (BrE)
(psychology) The process of repeating information, either silently or aloud, to keep it in short-term memory.
repetition in memory retention (psychology)
medical

Examples

Students often rely on rehearsal when trying to remember a new phone number.
Mental rehearsal can help you retain information for short quizzes and tests.
The psychologist explained that rehearsal is essential for transferring details into long-term memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorization - the process of committing something to memory
  • Antonym: forgetting - the loss of information from memory

Etymology

From Old French *rehercier* (to go over again, repeat), from re- (again) + hercier (to harrow).
