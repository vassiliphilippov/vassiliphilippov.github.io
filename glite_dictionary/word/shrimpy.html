shrimpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪmpi / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪmpi / (BrE)
(informal) Very small or of inferior size, especially referring to a person.
very small; undersized (person, informal)
informal

Examples

Everyone teased him in school for being so shrimpy, but he was quick and clever.
She felt shrimpy standing next to the tall basketball players at the awards ceremony.
Although he was a bit shrimpy, he had the biggest personality in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminutive - very small or tiny
  • Synonym: puny - small and weak
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built (opposite physique)

Etymology

From "shrimp" (a small crustacean), used figuratively for a small person, plus the diminutive suffix "-y".
