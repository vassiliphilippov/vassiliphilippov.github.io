barred

1. ADJ

/ bɑrd / (AmE), / bɑːd / (BrE)
prevented from entering or leaving, or from proceeding with an action, often by means of a physical barrier or restriction
prevented from entering, leaving, or acting

Examples

The entrance to the cave was barred with heavy wooden beams.
After the scandal, he found himself barred from every major event in the city.
All windows were barred to prevent unauthorized access.

Synset Examples

a barricaded street
barred doors
the blockaded harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - prevented from moving or proceeding
  • Antonym: unimpeded - not obstructed or hindered

Etymology

From past participle of 'bar,' originally from Old French 'barre' (bar, barrier).

2. ADJ

/ bɑrd / (AmE), / bɑːd / (BrE)
marked with stripes or bands of colour, often across the surface
marked with stripes or bands

Examples

The owl's wings were clearly barred with dark lines.
Several species of fish display a barred pattern for camouflage.
The caterpillar was green and barred with yellow bands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped - having stripes or lines of colour
  • Antonym: spotted - marked with spots
