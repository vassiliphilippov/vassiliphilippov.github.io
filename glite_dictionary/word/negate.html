to negate

1. VERB

/ nɪˈɡeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪt / (BrE)
to make something ineffective or invalid; to be in contradiction with or to deny the existence or truth of something
make ineffective or contradict

Examples

His actions serve to negate his earlier promises of reform.
If one fact negates another, they cannot both be true at the same time.
The results of the experiment negate the theory proposed by the researchers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to affirm - to state something positively and with certainty

Etymology

From Latin negare (to deny).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02669219-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ nɪˈɡeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪt / (BrE)
to deny the existence, truth, or validity of something
deny the truth of

Examples

He attempted to negate the allegations made against him during the interview.
Her recent actions did nothing to negate the perception that she was dishonest.
You cannot simply negate established facts because they are inconvenient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to affirm - to state something is true or valid

Etymology

From Latin 'negatus', past participle of 'negare' (to deny).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00825097-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ nɪˈɡeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪt / (BrE)
to prove something to be false or invalid; to show that something is not true
prove false or invalidate

Examples

The scientist worked to negate the previous theory with new experimental evidence.
One strong counterexample was enough to negate his argument.
If the new data is accurate, it could negate the need for further testing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to affirm - to state something is true or to validate
  • Synonym: to disprove - to show that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin negatus, past participle of negare (to deny).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00668804-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ nɪˈɡeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪt / (BrE)
to make something ineffective or invalid by counteracting its effect
make ineffective or nullify

Examples

Carelessness can negate the benefits of hours of hard work.
The new evidence may negate the need for a retrial.
Heavy rain did not negate their enthusiasm for the event.

Synset Examples

Her optimism neutralizes his gloom
This action will negate the effect of my efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nullify - make legally null and void; invalidate
  • Synonym: to invalidate - make an argument, statement, or theory unsound or erroneous
  • Antonym: to affirm - to state as a fact, or to validate

Etymology

From Latin negare (to deny).

Source: WordNet
