Tulostomataceae

1. NOUN

/ tjuːˌloʊstoʊməˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / tjuːˌlɒstəməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi known as stalked puffballs, characterized by their ovate fruiting bodies on long stalks
a family of stalked puffball fungi

Examples

The Tulostomataceae are commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions around the world.
Mycologists were excited to discover several specimens belonging to the Tulostomataceae family in the desert.
Unlike typical puffballs, members of Tulostomataceae grow on distinctive stalks above the ground.

Etymology

Based on genus Tulostoma + standard taxonomic suffix -aceae, from Modern Latin.
