simazine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪməˌziːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪməziːn / (BrE)
a synthetic herbicide used to control weeds, especially among crops such as corn and fruit.
a chemical weed-control herbicide

Examples

Farmers often apply simazine to prevent the growth of weeds in cornfields.
The use of simazine has helped increase crop yields by minimizing competition from unwanted plants.
Environmental concerns have led to stricter regulations on simazine usage in some countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbicide - a substance used to destroy unwanted plants

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: combining 'sim-' (variant of symmetrical) and 'azine' (a class of chemical compounds).
