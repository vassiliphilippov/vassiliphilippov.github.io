unostentatious

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌɑːstɛnˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌɒstɛnˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
Not showy, pretentious, or designed to attract notice; modest or restrained in appearance or manner.
not showy; modest or restrained

Examples

The restaurant's unostentatious decor made it feel warm and welcoming.
Her unostentatious generosity won her the admiration of many coworkers.
They preferred an unostentatious lifestyle despite their immense wealth.

Synset Examples

his unostentatious office
unostentatious elegance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modest - not overly showy or boastful
  • Antonym: ostentatious - showy and intended to attract notice

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'ostentatious', which comes from Latin 'ostentare' (to show).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ˌɑː.stɛnˈteɪ.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ˌɒs.tɛnˈteɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
not showy or pretentious; exhibiting simplicity and restrained good taste
not showy; modest in appearance

Examples

The mansion, though large, was decorated in an unostentatious style that reflected the owner's modesty.
She wore an unostentatious dress to the party, avoiding flashy accessories.
The restaurant’s unostentatious atmosphere made it popular with locals who preferred simplicity.

Synset Examples

the room is pleasant and understated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way; not obvious or showy
  • Antonym: ostentatious - characterized by pretentious or showy display; designed to impress

Etymology

From un- (not) + ostentatious, from Latin ostentare (to show).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
