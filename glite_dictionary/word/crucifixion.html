crucifixion

1. NOUN

/ ˌkruːsəˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
The act of executing someone by fastening them to a cross, typically by nailing or binding the hands and feet, a practice especially widespread in the ancient world.
execution by fastening to a cross

Examples

The crucifixion of criminals was a common punishment in the Roman Empire.
Many artists have depicted the crucifixion of Jesus in their works.
The brutality of crucifixion made it a feared method of execution throughout ancient history.

Etymology

From Late Latin crucifixio(n-), from Latin crucifigere (to fasten to a cross).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
extremely painful punishment or suffering, often experienced as though one were being tortured or persecuted
extremely painful punishment or suffering

Examples

After his public disgrace, he described the ordeal as a personal crucifixion.
The artist depicted the emotional crucifixion she went through after the divorce in her latest painting.
Political opponents often use the media for a public crucifixion of unpopular figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: suffering - the state of undergoing pain, distress, or hardship

Etymology

From Latin 'crucifixio', from 'crucifixus' (fixed to a cross).
