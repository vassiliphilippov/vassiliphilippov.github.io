Opheodrys aestivus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːfiˈɒdrɪs ɛˈstɪvəs / (AmE), / ˌɒfiˈɒdrɪs iːˈstɪvəs / (BrE)
a slender, bright green nonvenomous snake of the southern and eastern United States, commonly found in shrubs and trees; also known as the rough green snake.
rough green snake species of the US

Examples

The Opheodrys aestivus is often seen resting on branches near streams or ponds.
Unlike many snakes, Opheodrys aestivus spends much of its time in bushes and shrubs.
Researchers observed that Opheodrys aestivus is primarily active during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough green snake - common name for Opheodrys aestivus

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake), 'dryos' (tree) for genus, and Latin 'aestivus' (of summer) for species.
