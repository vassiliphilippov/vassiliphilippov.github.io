lobster

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑbstɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
The flesh of a lobster, used as food.
flesh of a lobster as food

Examples

I ordered grilled lobster at the restaurant for a special treat.
The chef prepared a creamy pasta with chunks of fresh lobster.
This shop sells the finest lobster you can find in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab - flesh of a crab, used as food
  • Synonym: shrimp - flesh of a shrimp, used as food

Etymology

From Old English *loppestre*, related to Old Norse *humar* and German *Hummer* (lobster).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑbstər / (AmE), / ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
a large marine crustacean with a cylindrical body, stalked eyes, and large pincers, prized as seafood
large edible marine crustacean

Examples

The chef prepared the freshest lobster for the evening special at the restaurant.
She caught a lobster while snorkeling near the rocky coastline.
The price of lobster tends to rise during the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab - a marine crustacean with a broad carapace and pincers
  • Synonym: shrimp - a small, slender marine crustacean

Etymology

From Old English 'loppestre', related to Latin 'locusta' (locust, lobster).
