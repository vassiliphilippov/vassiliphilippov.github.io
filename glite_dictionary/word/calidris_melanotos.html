Calidris melanotos

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪdrɪs ˌmɛləˈnəʊtəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪdrɪs ˌmɛləˈnətɒs / (BrE)
a species of American sandpiper known for inflating its chest during courtship displays; commonly called the pectoral sandpiper
species of American sandpiper

Examples

The Calidris melanotos migrates annually between North and South America, covering remarkable distances each year.
During the breeding season, the male Calidris melanotos inflates its chest to attract a mate.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Calidris melanotos near the wetland reserve this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectoral sandpiper - common name for Calidris melanotos, an American sandpiper

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Calidris', species 'melanotos' from Greek 'melanōtos' (black-backed).
