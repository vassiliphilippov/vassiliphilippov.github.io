to consist

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɪst / (AmE), / kənˈsɪst / (BrE)
(of something) to be made up or composed of particular components or elements.
to be made up of

Examples

The committee will consist of five members from different departments.
Her diet mainly consists of fruits and vegetables.
The event consisted entirely of live performances and interactive workshops.

Synset Examples

The land he conquered comprised several provinces
What does this dish consist of?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to comprise - to consist of; to include all the parts
  • Synonym: to include - to contain as part of a whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out; not to include

Etymology

From Latin 'consistere' (to stand together, be composed of).
