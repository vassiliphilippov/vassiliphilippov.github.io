pyracanth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəˌkænθ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.rəˌkænθ / (BrE)
A thorny shrub of the genus Pyracantha, bearing small white flowers and bright red or orange-red berries.
thorny shrub with red or orange-red berries

Examples

The garden was lined with pyracanth to create a natural barrier.
In autumn, the pyracanth produces a vivid display of berries.
Birds are attracted to the bright fruit of the pyracanth shrub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firethorn - common name for shrubs of the genus Pyracantha

Etymology

From genus name Pyracantha, from Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'akantha' (thorn).
