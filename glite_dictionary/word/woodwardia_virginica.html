Woodwardia virginica

1. NOUN

/ wʊdˈwɑɹdiə vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / wʊdˈwɔːdɪə vəˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of large, deciduous fern native to North America, commonly known as Virginia chain fern.
North American deciduous fern species

Examples

The wetland restoration project included planting Woodwardia virginica along the stream banks.
Many gardeners appreciate Woodwardia virginica for its graceful, arching fronds and tolerance of damp soil.
You can easily identify Woodwardia virginica by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive chain-like sori.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia chain fern - Common English name for Woodwardia virginica

Etymology

Genus name honors British botanist Thomas Jenkinson Woodward; species name from Latin virginica (of Virginia).
