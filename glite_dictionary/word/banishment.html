banishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈbænɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of sending someone away from a place as an official punishment; the act of forcing someone to leave a country, community, or group, often by law or authority
the act of sending someone away as punishment

Examples

The king ordered the banishment of the traitor from the kingdom.
After years of banishment, she was finally allowed to return home.
Political banishment was a common punishment in ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • Synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave a place

Etymology

From Old French *banissement*, from *banir* 'to banish', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈbænɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act or state of being forced to leave a place or community, often as a punishment or due to exclusion by general consent
state of being banished or excluded

Examples

His banishment from the country lasted several years.
Social banishment can be just as painful as physical exile.
The law once allowed for the banishment of criminals to distant colonies.

Synset Examples

the association should get rid of its elderly members — not by euthanasia, of course, but by Coventry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • Synonym: ostracism - exclusion from a society or group
  • Antonym: inclusion - the action or state of including or being included

Etymology

From Middle English banishement, from Old French banissement, from banir (to banish).
