adjudication

1. NOUN

/ əˌdʒuːdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌdʒuːdɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of making a formal judgment or decision about a dispute, especially in a court of law
formal legal decision or judgment
legal

Examples

The adjudication of the case took several months, as both sides presented extensive evidence.
After a lengthy trial, the court’s adjudication finally settled the issue.
Final adjudication on pension claims will be made by the benefits tribunal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - a decision by a court or judge
  • Synonym: arbitration - the formal judgment or resolution of a dispute by a third party

Etymology

From Latin adjudicatio (act of awarding or adjudging), from adjudicare (to judge).
