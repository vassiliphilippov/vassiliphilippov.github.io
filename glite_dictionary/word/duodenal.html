duodenal

1. ADJ

/ ˌduːəˈdinəl, ˌdjuː- / (AmE), / ˌdjuːəˈdiːnəl / (BrE)
relating to or located in the duodenum, the first part of the small intestine
relating to the duodenum
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a duodenal ulcer after experiencing persistent abdominal pain.
Doctors performed a biopsy of the duodenal tissue to investigate the cause of the symptoms.
Treatment for duodenal bleeding often requires both medication and endoscopic procedures.

Synset Examples

duodenal ulcer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastric - relating to the stomach
  • Degree Higher: jejunal - relating to the jejunum, the part of the small intestine after the duodenum

Etymology

From Latin 'duodenum' (duodenum) + '-al' (adjective suffix).
