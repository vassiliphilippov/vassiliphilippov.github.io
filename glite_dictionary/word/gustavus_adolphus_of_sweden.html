Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden

1. PROPN

/ ɡʌsˈteɪvəs əˈdɒlfəs əv ˈswidn̩ / (AmE), / ɡʌsˈtɑːvəs əˈdɒlfəs əv ˈswiːdn̩ / (BrE)
(1594–1632) King of Sweden who expanded Swedish power through military successes in the Thirty Years' War and implemented significant domestic reforms.
Swedish king and military leader, 1594–1632

Examples

Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden is often credited with transforming Sweden into a major European power.
Historians often praise Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden for his innovative military tactics.
During the Thirty Years' War, Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden became a key leader of the Protestant forces.

Etymology

Latinized form of Swedish name Gustav II Adolf; 'Gustavus Adolphus' used in historical English sources.
