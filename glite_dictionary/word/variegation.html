variegation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛri.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɑː.ri.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the presence of different colors, especially as irregular patches or streaks, often used to describe plants or patterns
appearance of different colors or patches

Examples

The variegation in the leaves made the plant especially attractive in the garden.
Gardeners prize this species for its natural variegation of whites and greens.
The bird’s feathers showed striking variegation across its chest and wings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color or shades of one color
  • Synonym: mottling - marking with spots or smears of color

Etymology

From Latin 'variegatus' (made of various sorts or colors), from 'variare' (to vary).
