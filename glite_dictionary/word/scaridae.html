Scaridae

1. NOUN

/ skɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / skɑːrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored marine fishes known as parrotfishes, characterized by their beak-like teeth fused into a parrot-like structure; found primarily in tropical reefs.
family of parrotfishes

Examples

Scientists have identified more than eighty species in the family Scaridae.
Coral reefs depend on Scaridae to keep algae levels in check.
The diet of many Scaridae consists mainly of algae scraped from rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrotfish - a brightly colored tropical fish of the family Scaridae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Scaridae', based on Greek 'skaros' meaning parrotfish; used in zoological classification.
