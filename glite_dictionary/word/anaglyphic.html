anaglyphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈɡlɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡlɪfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving anaglyphs, especially images or designs in which two visuals are superimposed in contrasting colors to create a 3D effect when viewed with special glasses.
relating to anaglyph images or 3D effects

Examples

The anaglyphic process allows viewers to experience 3D illusions with simple red-cyan glasses.
Many early 3D movies used anaglyphic technology to create a sense of depth.
He was fascinated by the anaglyphic images in the old comic books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaglyph - a stereoscopic image composed of two superimposed contrasting colors

Etymology

From Greek ana- (up) + glyphē (carving), via 'anaglyph' (embossed or 3D image).
