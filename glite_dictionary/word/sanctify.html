to sanctify

1. VERB

/ ˈsæŋktəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something pure or free from sin or guilt, often in a religious sense
make pure or free from sin or guilt

Examples

The priest hoped to sanctify the ceremony by blessing the participants.
Through sincere repentance, she believed she could sanctify her soul.
Some rituals are performed to sanctify the place before important events.

Synset Examples

he left the monastery purified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - make clean or free from impurities or guilt
  • Antonym: defile - make dirty or impure, especially morally or spiritually

Etymology

From Latin sanctificare, from sanctus (holy) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈsæŋk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to give religious approval or blessing to, especially by means of a ceremony or ritual; to make something holy
to make holy or bless ceremonially

Examples

The priest was called to sanctify the church before the opening ceremony.
Many cultures sanctify marriages through special rituals or vows.
They hope to sanctify their new home with a blessing from their spiritual leader.

Synset Examples

sanctify the marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bless - to invoke or bestow favor or holiness upon
  • Antonym: to desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect

Etymology

From Latin sanctificare, from sanctus (holy) + facere (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈsæŋk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make holy or set apart as sacred, especially by means of religious rites
make holy by religious ceremony

Examples

The priest will sanctify the church with a special ceremony.
These waters are believed to sanctify those who bathe in them.
They sanctified the new altar before the festival began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bless - to make or pronounce holy
  • Antonym: to desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect

Etymology

From Latin sanctificare, from sanctus (holy) + facere (to make).
