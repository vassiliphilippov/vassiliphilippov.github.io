flashboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈflɑːʃ.bɔːd / (BrE)
a removable board or plank placed along the top of a dam to increase its height and temporarily raise the water level
removable board atop a dam

Examples

The workers installed a new flashboard to raise the reservoir's water level before the dry season.
After heavy rains, the flashboards were removed to prevent flooding downstream.
Maintenance of the dam includes regular inspection of each flashboard for damage or leaks.

Etymology

From 'flash' (referring to a sudden flow of water) + 'board'.
