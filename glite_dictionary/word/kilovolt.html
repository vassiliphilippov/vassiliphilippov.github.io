kilovolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪləˌvoʊlt / (AmE), / ˈkɪləˌvəʊlt / (BrE)
A unit of electric potential equal to one thousand volts.
unit of electric potential (1,000 volts)

Examples

The transformer increases the voltage to several kilovolts for transmission over long distances.
A power line typically operates at tens or hundreds of kilovolts.
The equipment was rated for a maximum of one kilovolt to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: volt - unit of electric potential
  • Degree Higher: megavolt - unit of electric potential equal to a million volts

Etymology

From kilo- (thousand) + volt, named after Alessandro Volta.
