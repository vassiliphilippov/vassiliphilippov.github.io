to seem

1. VERB

/ siːm / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
to give the impression of being or doing something, especially without certainty or proof; to appear to be
appear to be; give an impression

Examples

It seems that Margaret will arrive ahead of schedule despite the traffic.
From their conversation, it seemed like they already knew the outcome.
Sometimes the truth seems stranger than fiction.

Synset Examples

It seems that he is very gifted
It appears that the weather in California is very bad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appear - to become visible or to give the impression of being something

Etymology

From Old Norse *sæma* (to conform to), related to Old English *sēman* (to fit, be fitting).

2. VERB

/ siːm / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
to give the impression of being or doing something, often without definite proof
give the impression of being something

Examples

She doesn't seem interested in joining the team, though she hasn't said no directly.
It may seem strange at first, but you'll get used to it.
After the rain, the air seemed fresher and cooler.

Synset Examples

She seems to be sleeping
This appears to be a very difficult problem
This project looks fishy
They appeared like people who had not eaten or slept for a long time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appear - to give the impression of being or doing something
  • Synonym: look - to have the appearance of being something

Etymology

From Middle English semen, from Old Norse sœma (to seem, beseem).
