to abye

1. VERB

/ əˈbaɪ / (AmE), / əˈbʌɪ / (BrE)
(archaic) To make amends for; to atone for.
make amends for (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In days past, a man might abye his misdeeds with years of service.
She sought to abye her errors through charity and prayer.
They believed one must abye for any wrong committed against the village.

Synset Examples

expiate one's sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expiate - to make amends or atone for (guilt or sin)

Etymology

From Old English *abīcgan* (to pay for, atone for).
