iridocyclitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrɪdoʊsaɪˈklaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪdəʊsaɪˈklaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the iris and ciliary body in the eye, often causing pain and vision problems
inflammation of the iris and ciliary body in the eye
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with iridocyclitis after complaining of redness and discomfort in her eye.
Untreated iridocyclitis can sometimes lead to complications such as glaucoma or cataracts.
The ophthalmologist prescribed medication to reduce the inflammation caused by iridocyclitis.

Etymology

From Greek iris (rainbow, iris of the eye) + kyklos (circle) + -itis (inflammation).
