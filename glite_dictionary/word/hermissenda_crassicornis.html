Hermissenda crassicornis

1. NOUN

/ hərˌmɪsənˈdiː kræsɪˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / hɜːˌmɪsənˈdiː kræsɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A species of brightly colored sea slug found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for its role in neuroscience research.
a species of sea slug

Examples

The laboratory used Hermissenda crassicornis to study learning and memory in marine mollusks.
During a tidepool exploration, we found a colorful Hermissenda crassicornis among the seaweed.
Researchers often choose Hermissenda crassicornis because its nervous system is easy to observe.

Etymology

From the Greek 'Hermissenda' (a genus name) and Latin 'crassicornis' meaning 'thick-horned'.
