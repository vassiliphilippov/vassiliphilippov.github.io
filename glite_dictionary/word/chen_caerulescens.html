Chen caerulescens

1. NOUN

/ kɛn sɛəˌruːlˈɛskɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkɛn siːəˌruːlˈɛskɛnz / (BrE)
a species of North American wild goose, also known as the snow goose, with dark plumage in summer and white plumage in winter
a North American wild goose species (snow goose)

Examples

Large flocks of Chen caerulescens migrate each spring and autumn across the North American continent.
The plumage of Chen caerulescens changes from darker shades in summer to white in winter.
Conservationists monitor Chen caerulescens populations to ensure the species remains abundant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow goose - common name for Chen caerulescens, a North American goose

Etymology

From New Latin: Chen (goose) + Latin caerulescens (bluish), referring to the bluish morph of the species.
