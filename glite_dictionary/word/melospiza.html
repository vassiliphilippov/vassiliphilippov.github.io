Melospiza

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈspaɪzə / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈspaɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of North American songbirds in the family Emberizidae, which includes the song sparrow and swamp sparrow.
genus of North American sparrows

Examples

The Melospiza genus includes both the song sparrow and the swamp sparrow.
Scientists studied the plumage variations among North American Melospiza species.
Birdwatchers often seek out members of the Melospiza genus in marshes and gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song sparrow - a species of sparrow found in North America, known for its melodious song
  • Synonym: swamp sparrow - a species of sparrow common in marshes and wet habitats of North America

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek melos (song) and spiza (finch).
