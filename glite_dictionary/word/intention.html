intention

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a plan or aim that you intend to carry out; the determination to act in a certain way
plan or aim to act

Examples

It's always been my intention to travel the world after graduation.
She made her intention clear with a simple, direct statement.
Despite his good intentions, the plan did not succeed.

Synset Examples

my intention changed once I saw her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something
  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created
  • Antonym: accident - an event that happens by chance, not intended

Etymology

From Latin intentio (a stretching toward, purpose), from intendere (to intend).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a thing that you plan to do or achieve; a purpose or aim that guides your actions
something you plan or aim to do

Examples

Her intention was to finish the project before the deadline.
He announced his intention to run for president next year.
Despite their good intentions, things did not go as planned.

Synset Examples

his intent was to provide a new translation
good intentions are not enough
it was created with the conscious aim of answering immediate needs
he made no secret of his designs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intent - something that is intended; purpose or aim
  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created
  • Synonym: aim - a result that someone tries to achieve
  • Synonym: goal - the object of a person's ambition or effort; an aim or desired result

Etymology

From Latin 'intentio', from 'intendere' (to stretch toward, aim at).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
(usually intentions) a person's aim or purpose, especially in regard to proposing marriage or forming a serious romantic relationship.
aim or purpose, esp. regarding marriage proposal

Examples

He told her that his intentions were serious and honorable.
Rumors spread quickly about his intentions toward the mayor's daughter.
She doubted his intentions until he finally proposed.

Synset Examples

his intentions are entirely honorable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created
  • Degree Lower: proposal - an act of asking someone to marry you
  • Degree Higher: commitment - the state or quality of being dedicated to a cause, often used of marriage

Etymology

From Latin intentio (stretching, purpose), from intendere (to stretch out, intend).
