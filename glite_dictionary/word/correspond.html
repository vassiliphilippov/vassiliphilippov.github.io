to correspond

1. VERB

/ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈspɒnd / (BrE)
to be similar or equivalent in function, character, amount, or position to something else; to match or be in parallel
be equivalent or parallel to something else

Examples

In some languages, the word for 'sun' does not correspond directly to the English usage.
The red pins on this map correspond to the locations of major cities.
Each symbol on the chart corresponds to a different type of mineral.

Synset Examples

Because of the sound changes in the course of history, an ‘h’ in Greek stands for an ‘s’ in Latin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - to be equal to or in agreement with something
  • Antonym: to differ - to be unlike or not equivalent to something

Etymology

From Latin 'correspondere', from 'cor-' (together) + 'respondere' (to answer).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌkɔrəˈspɑnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈspɒnd / (BrE)
to be very similar or match in form, character, or function; to be consistent or in agreement
be similar, match, or be consistent

Examples

The results of the experiment correspond closely with our predictions.
Her actions did not always correspond to her words.
The two maps correspond in every significant detail.

Synset Examples

The two stories don't agree in many details
The handwriting checks with the signature on the form
The suspect's fingerprints don't match those on the gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - to be the same or very similar; to correspond
  • Antonym: to disagree - to not be consistent or in agreement; not to correspond

Etymology

From Latin correspondere, from com- (together) + respondere (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02663315-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈspɒnd / (BrE)
(of mathematical objects or quantities) to be equivalent or parallel in position, function, or value.
be equivalent or parallel in mathematics

Examples

Each side of the square will correspond to a specific value in the table.
Angles A and B correspond because they are both adjacent to the same side.
In this diagram, points on the x-axis correspond to their images on the y-axis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - to be equal or closely similar to something
  • Antonym: to differ - to be unlike or dissimilar

Etymology

From Latin 'correspondere', from 'cor-' (together) + 'respondere' (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02639615-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˌkɔːr.əˈspɑːnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.ɪˈspɒnd / (BrE)
to communicate with someone by exchanging letters or messages, especially regularly over a period of time
exchange written messages with someone

Examples

She likes to correspond with friends she met while traveling.
During the war, soldiers would often correspond with their families back home.
They continued to correspond for years after graduating from university.

Synset Examples

My Russian pen pal and I have been corresponding for several years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communicate - share or exchange information, news, or ideas

From Latin 'correspondere', from 'cor-' (together) + 'respondere' (to answer).
