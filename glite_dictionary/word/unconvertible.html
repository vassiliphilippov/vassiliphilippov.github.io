unconvertible

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈvɝːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈvɜːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) not able to be exchanged for or replaced by another currency or asset of equal value.
not able to be exchanged for another currency

Examples

The country's unconvertible currency made foreign trade nearly impossible.
Because the bonds were unconvertible, investors sought other options.
Travelers found it difficult to exchange unconvertible banknotes after leaving the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconvertible - not able to be converted; especially of currency
  • Antonym: convertible - able to be converted or exchanged, especially of currency or assets

Etymology

From un- (not) + convertible, ultimately from Latin convertere (to turn around, change).
