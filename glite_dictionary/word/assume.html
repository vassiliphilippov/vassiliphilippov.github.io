to assume

1. VERB

/ əˈsum / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
(in religion) to take up or receive a person, especially their soul, into heaven.
take up (a soul) into heaven

Examples

According to Christian doctrine, the Virgin Mary was assumed into heaven at the end of her earthly life.
Many believers gather to celebrate the day when saints are assumed into paradise.
The priest spoke of the faithful being assumed by God into eternal bliss.

Synset Examples

This is the day when Mary was assumed into heaven

Etymology

From Latin assumere, meaning 'to take up' or 'to take to oneself'.
