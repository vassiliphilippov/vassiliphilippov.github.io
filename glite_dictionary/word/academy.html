academy

1. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / əˈkædəmi / (BrE)
(especially in the US) a secondary school, typically a private one.
private secondary school

Examples

She attended a prestigious academy in New England.
Many parents choose a private academy for their children's education.
The academy offers small class sizes and a challenging curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school - an institution for educating children

Etymology

From French 'académie', from Latin 'academia', from Ancient Greek 'Akadēmeia', the name of Plato's school.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / əˈkædəmi / (BrE)
a school or institution for special training in a particular field, such as art, music, or military subjects
school for special training

Examples

She dreams of attending a ballet academy in New York.
The police academy trains recruits in law enforcement skills.
After graduating from art school, he joined a prestigious academy to further his studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: institute - an organization for education or research
  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher learning

Etymology

From French *académie*, from Latin *academia*, from Ancient Greek *Akadēmeia* (the name of Plato's school).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08294726-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / əˈkadəmi / (BrE)
an institution or society dedicated to the advancement of knowledge, learning, or scholarship
learned institution or society

Examples

The National Academy of Sciences promotes research and innovation in many fields.
He was elected a fellow of the French Academy for his contributions to mathematics.
The academy held its annual conference on the latest developments in technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: institute - an organization for educational, professional, or scientific purposes
  • Synonym: school - an institution for educating people

Etymology

From Greek akadēmia, the name of the school where Plato taught, named after Akadēmos, a local hero.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ əˈkædəmi / (AmE), / əˈkædəmi / (BrE)
an organization or institution established to promote the advancement of art, science, or literature
institution promoting art, science, or literature

Examples

The French Academy sets official standards for the language.
She was inducted into the National Academy of Sciences for her groundbreaking work.
Many artists dream of exhibiting at the Royal Academy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: institute - an organization for a specific purpose, especially research or education

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *akadēmeia*, the name of Plato's school near Athens.
