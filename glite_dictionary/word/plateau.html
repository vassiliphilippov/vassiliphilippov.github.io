plateau

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtoʊ / (AmE), / plæˈtəʊ / (BrE)
a large area of flat land that is higher than the land around it
elevated flat area of land

Examples

The Colorado plateau is famous for its striking red rock formations and canyons.
The city is located on a plateau overlooking the surrounding lowlands.
Scientists studied the plants growing on the high plateau where the climate was much cooler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - a large area of flat land with few trees, typically not elevated

Etymology

From French 'plateau' (flat surface), diminutive of 'plat' (flat), from Old French, from Latin 'plattus' (flat).
