Cepphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛfəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛfəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds known as guillemots, found in northern coastal regions.
genus of seabirds (guillemots)

Examples

The black guillemot belongs to the genus Cepphus.
Researchers studied populations of Cepphus along the rocky coasts of Alaska.
Several species within Cepphus have distinctive white wing patches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guillemot - a seabird belonging to the genus Cepphus or related genera

Etymology

From Ancient Greek κεπφός (kepphos), a pale water bird mentioned by Aristotle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛfəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛfəs / (BrE)
a genus of marine birds in the auk family, which includes the guillemots
genus of seabirds including guillemots

Examples

The Cepphus genus is characterized by its black plumage and white wing patches.
Several species within the Cepphus genus are found along northern ocean coasts.
Ornithologists observed a Cepphus nesting colony on the remote island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guillemot - common name of birds within the genus Cepphus

Etymology

From New Latin Cepphus, probably from Greek kepphos, a water bird mentioned by Aristotle.
