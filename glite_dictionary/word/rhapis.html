Rhapis

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪs / (AmE), / ˈræpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, clump-forming fan palms native to China and Japan, often cultivated as ornamental plants.
genus of small Asian fan palms

Examples

The Rhapis palm is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its elegant leaves.
Several varieties of Rhapis thrive in shady indoor environments.
Gardeners value Rhapis for its ability to tolerate low light and minimal care.

Etymology

From Greek rhaphis (needle), referring to the plant's slender segments.
