Kafir

1. NOUN

/ kəˈfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkæfɪə / (BrE)
a member of a people formerly inhabiting northeastern Afghanistan, known for their distinct culture and language; now largely integrated or dispersed
member of an ethnic group from northeastern Afghanistan

Examples

The Kafir tribes maintained their traditional customs despite outside influences for many centuries.
A group of Kafir elders gathered to discuss the upcoming festival.
Historical accounts describe the mountainous homeland of the Kafir people in Afghanistan.

Etymology

From Arabic *kāfir* (unbeliever), applied to the people by neighboring Muslims; not originally their own name.
