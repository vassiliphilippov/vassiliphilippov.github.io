pope

1. NOUN

/ poʊp / (AmE), / pəʊp / (BrE)
the bishop of Rome and the head of the Roman Catholic Church
head of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The pope addressed thousands of people during the Easter Sunday mass in St. Peter's Square.
Many pilgrims travel to the Vatican to catch a glimpse of the pope.
Throughout history, each pope has played a significant role in the development of the Catholic Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pontiff - the Pope; the bishop of Rome
  • Degree Lower: bishop - senior member of the Christian clergy; in charge of a diocese

Etymology

From Latin 'papa', meaning 'father', from Greek 'pappas'.
