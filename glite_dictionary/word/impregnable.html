impregnable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛɡnəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛɡnəbl / (BrE)
unable to be captured, defeated, or overcome; impossible to attack successfully or to be tampered with.
unable to be overcome or attacked

Examples

The castle was considered impregnable due to its thick walls and strategic location.
Despite numerous attempts, the hackers found the new security system impregnable.
Her confidence in her decision seemed impregnable, no matter what others said.

Synset Examples

an impregnable fortress
fortifications that made the frontier inviolable
a secure telephone connection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invincible - too powerful to be defeated or overcome
  • Antonym: vulnerable - susceptible to being attacked or harmed

Etymology

From Old French *imprenable*, from Latin *im-* (not) + *prendere* (to take).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛɡnəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛɡnəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to defeat, overcome, or challenge; not able to be refuted or harmed.
impossible to defeat or overcome

Examples

His impregnable self-confidence inspired everyone around him to take on new challenges.
The lawyer presented an impregnable argument that could not be easily disputed.
The fortress of her beliefs was impregnable, regardless of criticism or doubt.

Synset Examples

an impregnable argument
impregnable self-confidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invincible - too powerful to be defeated or overcome
  • Antonym: vulnerable - able to be easily hurt, influenced, or attacked

Etymology

From late Middle English, from Old French *impregnable*, from in- ‘not’ + *prenable* ‘able to be taken’ (from prendre ‘to take’).

Source: WordNet
