guardroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdˌruːm / (BrE)
a room where soldiers or guards stay while on duty, especially when not actively patrolling
room for soldiers or guards on duty

Examples

The officer inspected the guardroom to ensure everything was in order.
The soldiers gathered in the guardroom during their shift change.
When off duty, they relaxed and played cards in the guardroom.

Etymology

From 'guard' (to watch over) + 'room'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdˌrum / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdˌruːm / (BrE)
A room or cell in a military building where soldiers who are prisoners are held in confinement.
military room for confining soldiers

Examples

After breaking the rules, the officer was locked in the guardroom.
The guardroom was cold and sparsely furnished, with only a bench and a small window.
Punishments for minor offenses often included a night in the guardroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small room in a prison or jail for confining a prisoner

Etymology

From 'guard' + 'room'.
