recent

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːsənt / (AmE), / ˈriːsənt / (BrE)
having happened, begun, or come into existence not long ago; new or fresh.
new; not long ago

Examples

The company is proud of its recent achievements in renewable energy.
She displayed the recent paintings she had completed over the summer.
A recent storm caused significant damage in several neighborhoods.

Synset Examples

recent graduates
a recent addition to the house
recent buds on the apple trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: new - not existing before; made, introduced, or discovered recently
  • Antonym: old - having lived or existed for a long time; not new

Etymology

From Latin 'recent-', from 'recens' meaning 'fresh, new'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈriːsənt / (AmE), / ˈriːsənt / (BrE)
having happened or started a short time ago, not long before the present moment
of the immediate past; not long ago

Examples

The earthquake was a recent event that affected the whole community.
I've read about this breakthrough in a recent scientific journal.
Her promotion to manager is a fairly recent development in the company.

Synset Examples

a late development
their late quarrel
his recent trip to Africa
in recent months
a recent issue of the journal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: new - recently created or introduced; not existing before
  • Antonym: old - having existed for a long time; not recent

Etymology

From Latin recens (fresh, new).
