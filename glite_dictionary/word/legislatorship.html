legislatorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or status of being a legislator
the office or status of a legislator

Examples

His legislatorship was marked by a commitment to educational reform.
During her legislatorship, she introduced several key bills on healthcare.
He reflected on the responsibilities that came with his new legislatorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes or enacts laws

Etymology

From legislator + -ship (forming nouns indicating state or condition).
