appositeness

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːzɪtnəs / (AmE), / əˈpɒzɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being appropriate or well-suited to a particular situation or purpose
quality of being appropriate or suitable

Examples

The appositeness of his comments impressed everyone at the meeting.
She questioned the appositeness of that remark in such a formal setting.
Critics praised the appositeness of the film's soundtrack to its mood.

Synset Examples

the phrase had considerable aptness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aptness - the quality of being appropriate or suitable
  • Antonym: inappropriateness - the quality of not being suitable or appropriate

Etymology

From Latin 'appositus' (suitable, appropriate), past participle of 'adponere' (to place near).
