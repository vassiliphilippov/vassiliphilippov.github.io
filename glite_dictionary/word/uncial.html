uncial

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˈʌn.sɪ.əl / (BrE)
written in or relating to a style of large, rounded majuscule lettering, especially used in Latin and Greek manuscripts from the 3rd to 8th centuries.
relating to a style of majuscule writing

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written in uncial script, making it easier to read.
Scholars identified the text based on its distinctive uncial letters.
Uncial writing was commonly used in early medieval religious texts.

Synset Examples

uncial letters

Etymology

From Latin 'uncialis' (of an ounce, inch-high), from 'uncia' (twelfth part, inch, ounce).

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌnʃiəl / (AmE), / ˈʌnsɪəl / (BrE)
a script characterized by rounded, separated capital letters, used especially in Greek and Latin manuscripts from the 4th to 8th centuries
ancient script with rounded capitals

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written entirely in uncial, making it easier to read than other scripts of that period.
Scholars study uncial to better understand the transmission of early Christian texts.
Some modern fonts are inspired by the rounded forms typical of uncial lettering.

Etymology

From Latin 'uncialis' (of an inch, of an ounce), referring to the large letters.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06364632-n
Synset POS: n
