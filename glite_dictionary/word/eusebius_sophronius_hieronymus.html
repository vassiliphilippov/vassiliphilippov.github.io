Eusebius Sophronius Hieronymus

1. PROPN

/ juˈsiːbi.əs sɑːˈfroʊ.ni.əs hʌɪəˈrɒnɪməs / (AmE), / juːˈsiːbɪ.əs sɒˈfrəʊ.nɪ.əs hʌɪəˈrɒnɪməs / (BrE)
Saint Jerome (c. 347–420), one of the great Fathers of the early Christian Church, renowned for translating the Scriptures into Latin (the Vulgate) and recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
early Christian scholar and translator; Saint Jerome

Examples

Eusebius Sophronius Hieronymus is best known for his Latin translation of the Bible, known as the Vulgate.
Many scholars refer to Eusebius Sophronius Hieronymus as one of the most influential early Church Fathers.
The feast day of Eusebius Sophronius Hieronymus, also called Saint Jerome, is celebrated on September 30th.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerome - English name for Eusebius Sophronius Hieronymus, early Christian scholar and saint

Etymology

Latinized form of his Greek name, Hieronymos; known as Jerome in English.
