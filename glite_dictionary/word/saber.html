saber

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbər / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə / (BrE)
A heavy sword with a curved, single-edged blade and a thick back, traditionally used by cavalry.
a curved cavalry sword

Examples

The officer drew his saber and led the charge against the enemy.
An antique saber hung proudly above the fireplace.
He collected military artifacts, including a 19th-century cavalry saber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabre - Alternative spelling for the curved sword, chiefly British

Etymology

From French "sabre", itself from Hungarian "szablya" (saber, cutlass).

2. VERB

/ ˈseɪbər / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə / (BrE)
to kill or injure someone with a saber (a type of sword)
kill or injure with a saber

Examples

The solider threatened to saber his opponent during the fierce battle.
In the old tales, knights would saber their enemies on the field.
He nearly sabered the bandit who tried to rob him.

Etymology

From French 'sabre' (sword), itself from Hungarian 'szablya'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbər / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə / (BrE)
a type of fencing sword with a v-shaped blade and a slightly curved handle, used especially in competitive fencing
type of fencing sword

Examples

He won the match by striking his opponent with his saber during a tense bout.
The fencer polished his saber before the championship to ensure it was in perfect condition.
Unlike the foil, the saber allows for both cutting and thrusting attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epee - a fencing sword with a stiff, triangular blade and a large guard
  • Synonym: foil - a light fencing sword with a flexible rectangular blade and a small guard

Etymology

From French 'sabre', from German 'Säbel', likely of Hungarian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04128605-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈseɪbər / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə / (BrE)
to cut or injure with a saber (a type of sword used in fencing or combat)
cut or injure with a saber

Examples

The officer threatened to saber anyone who attempted to escape.
During the cavalry charge, several soldiers were sabered by the enemy.
He watched in horror as the villain attempted to saber his opponent during the duel.

Etymology

From French 'sabre', from German 'Säbel', referring to the type of sword.
