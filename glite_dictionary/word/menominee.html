Menominee

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːməˌni / (AmE), / mɪˈnɒmɪni / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of Algonquian stock, living primarily on a reservation in central Wisconsin
a Native American tribe of Wisconsin

Examples

The Menominee have maintained their traditions despite centuries of change.
A Menominee elder shared stories about the tribe's history at the community center.
Many members of the Menominee tribe live on a reservation in central Wisconsin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a member of a group of indigenous North American peoples who speak Algonquian languages

Etymology

From Ojibwe ‘manoominii’ meaning ‘wild rice people’.

2. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːməˌni / (AmE), / məˈnɒmɪni / (BrE)
the Algonquian language historically spoken by the Menominee people of Wisconsin and Michigan
Algonquian language of the Menominee people

Examples

She is studying the Menominee language as part of her research on indigenous cultures.
There are very few fluent speakers of Menominee remaining today.
The university offers courses in Menominee to help preserve the language.

Etymology

From Ojibwe manoominii, meaning 'wild rice people,' the name given to the tribe and their language.
