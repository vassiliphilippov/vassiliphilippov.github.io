to grunt-hoot

1. VERB

/ ɡrʌnt huːt / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt huːt / (BrE)
(of primates) to communicate by making grunting and hooting vocal sounds.
to communicate by grunting and hooting

Examples

The chimpanzees grunt-hooted to alert the others of danger nearby.
During the early morning, the gibbons grunt-hoot in a rhythmic chorus.
Scientists observed the gorillas grunt-hooting as part of their group interaction.
