alienating

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪliəˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪliəneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone feel isolated, excluded, or unfriendly towards others.
causing someone to feel isolated

Examples

His dismissive attitude had an alienating effect on the team.
The company's new policies were seen as alienating to long-term employees.
Her alienating behavior during meetings made collaboration difficult.

Synset Examples

her sudden alienating aloofness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: welcoming - making someone feel accepted or included
  • Degree Higher: hostile - showing or feeling opposition or dislike; unfriendly
  • Degree Lower: unfriendly - not friendly; cold or distant

Etymology

From 'alienate' + '-ing'. 'Alienate' from Latin 'alienatus', past participle of 'alienare' (to estrange).
