replication

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a copy or reproduction of something, especially one that is not the original
copy or reproduction

Examples

The replication of the ancient vase was displayed in the museum alongside the shattered original.
Scientists made a replication of the experiment to verify the results.
Each replication was carefully examined for accuracy and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - an exact copy of something
  • Antonym: original - the first or initial version of something, not a copy

Etymology

From Latin replicatio (reply, repetition), from replicare (to fold back).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) The process by which DNA makes a copy of itself before cell division.
copying of DNA before cell division
medical

Examples

During replication, the DNA unwinds so each strand can serve as a template.
Mutations can occur when mistakes happen during replication of genetic material.
Accurate replication is essential for the growth and development of living organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplication - the act of making a copy or duplicate of something

From Latin replicatio (a folding back, repetition), from replicare (to fold back, repeat).
