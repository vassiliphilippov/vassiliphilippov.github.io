gyrocompass

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪroʊˌkʌmpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəʊˌkʌmpəs / (BrE)
A navigational compass that determines true north by means of a gyroscope rather than by magnetism.
navigation compass using a gyroscope

Examples

The ship's gyrocompass was essential for accurate navigation during the storm.
Unlike a magnetic compass, a gyrocompass always points to true north.
Modern submarines rely on the gyrocompass for precise orientation underwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compass - an instrument for determining direction, usually by means of a magnetic needle

Etymology

From 'gyro-' (relating to rotation) and 'compass'; popularized in the early 20th century with the introduction of gyroscopic navigation devices.
