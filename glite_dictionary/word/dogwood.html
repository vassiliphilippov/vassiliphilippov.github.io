dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
the hard, tough wood from trees of the genus Cornus, resembling boxwood and often used in fine woodworking
hard wood from dogwood trees

Examples

Furniture makers appreciate the strength and smooth finish of dogwood for intricate details.
The craftsman chose dogwood because it carves beautifully and holds fine lines.
This sculpture is made from dogwood, giving it both durability and unique color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxwood - hard, light-colored wood from box trees used in carving and turning

Etymology

Named for the dogwood tree (genus Cornus); the origin of 'dogwood' for the tree dates to the 17th century, possibly from 'dagwood' (wood for making daggers or skewers).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tree or shrub of the genus Cornus, typically with showy bracts that resemble petals, often grown for their ornamental flowers or colorful stems
ornamental tree or shrub of the genus Cornus

Examples

The dogwood bursts into bloom every spring, covering the yard with delicate white flowers.
We planted a pink dogwood near the front entrance to add some color in the spring.
Several native dogwoods provide shade along the woodland path.

Etymology

From 'dog' + 'wood', possibly referring to an old belief that its bark could cure dogs of mange.
