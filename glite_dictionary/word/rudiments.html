rudiments

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːdəˌmɛnts / (AmE), / ˈruːdɪmənts / (BrE)
the basic principles or elements of a subject, especially those that are first learned
basic principles of a subject

Examples

Even after a year of lessons, she only knew the rudiments of the piano.
Children begin by learning the rudiments of reading and writing in school.
He tried to explain the rudiments of chess to his nephew.

Synset Examples

he mastered only the rudiments of geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentals - essential or primary principles of a subject
  • Synonym: basics - the simplest and most essential facts or principles

Etymology

From Latin 'rudimentum' (beginning, first attempt).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːdəˌmɛnts / (AmE), / ˈruːdɪmənts / (BrE)
the basic facts, principles, or elements of a subject or skill; the foundations upon which more complex ideas are built
basic principles or elements

Examples

She taught her students the rudiments of music theory on their first day.
Before advanced mathematics, you must first learn the rudiments of algebra and geometry.
The guide outlined the rudiments of survival in the wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basics - simple, fundamental aspects or principles
  • Synonym: fundamentals - primary or essential principles

Etymology

From Latin 'rudimentum' (early training, beginning), from 'rudis' (unwrought, uncultivated, rough).

Synset POS: n
