II Chronicles

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˌkraɪˈɑnɪkəlz / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˌkrɒɪˈnɪklz / (BrE)
the second of two Old Testament books, describing the history of the kingdoms of Judah and Israel up to the return from the Babylonian Captivity
biblical book; Old Testament history

Examples

The priest read a passage from II Chronicles during the evening service.
Scholars often debate the historical accuracy of the events described in II Chronicles.
My grandmother's favorite story from the Bible is found in II Chronicles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: I Chronicles - first Old Testament book recounting history of Judah and Israel

Etymology

From Latin 'Chronica', from Greek 'chronika' (annals, chronicles).
