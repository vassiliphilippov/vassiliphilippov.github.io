panderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpændərər / (AmE), / ˈpændərə / (BrE)
a person who caters to the immoral or base desires of others, often for personal gain
one who caters to the base desires of others

Examples

The corrupt official acted as a panderer for the wealthy elite in the city.
She accused the media mogul of being a panderer to public fear for higher ratings.
Throughout his career, the lobbyist was criticized as a panderer to powerful corporate interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pander - to cater to the immoral desires of others

Etymology

From Middle English 'pandare', referring to Pandarus, a character who facilitated a love affair in medieval literature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpændɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈpændərə / (BrE)
a person who procures clients for prostitutes; a pimp
one who procures clients for prostitutes

Examples

The police arrested the panderer on suspicion of running an illegal operation.
He was notorious as a panderer who took advantage of vulnerable people.
The film depicted the downfall of a panderer in the city's underworld.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pimp - a person, usually a man, who controls prostitutes and arranges clients for them

Etymology

From Middle English *Pandare*, the name of a character in Chaucer's 'Troilus and Criseyde', who acted as a go-between for lovers.
