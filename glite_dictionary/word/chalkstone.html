chalkstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɔkˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔːkˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a hard, whitish deposit of uric acid salts, typically found around joints or in the external ear, occurring in advanced or chronic gout
deposit of uric acid salts in gout
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a chalkstone near the patient's big toe, confirming a case of long-term gout.
Years of untreated gout led to painful chalkstones forming around his finger joints.
A large chalkstone had developed on the helix of her ear, causing discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tophus - a deposit of uric acid crystals in the tissues, characteristic of gout

Etymology

From 'chalk' (referring to the deposit's color and texture) + 'stone.'
