lambent

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæmbənt / (AmE), / ˈlæmbənt / (BrE)
softly glowing or gently radiant, often with a flickering or wavering light
softly glowing; gently radiant

Examples

The lambent glow of the candles filled the room with a warm, gentle light.
A lambent shimmer played across the surface of the tranquil lake at dusk.
Her eyes sparkled with a lambent light, bright but soft and inviting.

Synset Examples

a house aglow with lights
glowing embers
lambent tongues of flame
the lucent moon
a sky luminous with stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glowing - emitting a steady light; radiant
  • Synonym: radiant - sending out light; bright or shining

Etymology

From Latin lambent-, lambens, present participle of lambere (to lick), with reference to the flickering motion of flames.
