antecedence

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dəns / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dəns / (BrE)
The fact or state of preceding in time; precedence or priority.
the state of preceding in time

Examples

The antecedence of his research laid the foundation for later discoveries.
Understanding the antecedence of certain events is crucial in historical studies.
Legal arguments often hinge on the antecedence of contractual agreements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precedence - the condition of coming before in order or importance

Etymology

From Latin antecedentia (preceding), from antecedere (to go before).
