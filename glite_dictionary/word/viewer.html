viewer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvjuːər / (AmE), / ˈvjuːə / (BrE)
a person who watches television, films, or online video broadcasts
person who watches TV or videos

Examples

The documentary attracted millions of viewers on its first night.
Some viewers complained about the quality of the subtitles during the broadcast.
Television viewers were shocked by the breaking news segment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audience - a group of people watching or listening to something

Etymology

From 'view' + '-er', meaning 'one who views'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvjuːɚ / (AmE), / ˈvjuːə / (BrE)
a device with a lens or other optical element used to look at photographic transparencies, such as slides or film strips
optical device for viewing transparencies

Examples

He inserted the slide into the viewer to examine the photograph in detail.
The old viewer allows you to look at each frame of the film strip.
She found an antique viewer at the photography shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnifier - a device for enlarging the appearance of objects

Etymology

From the verb 'view' + -er.

3. NOUN

/ ˈvjuː.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈvjuː.ə / (BrE)
a person who looks at or observes something, such as a show, exhibition, or televised event
a person who observes or watches something

Examples

The museum welcomed over a thousand viewers to the new art exhibition on opening night.
Television ratings are calculated based on how many viewers watch a particular program.
As a viewer of the game, she cheered with the rest of the crowd.

Synset Examples

the spectators applauded the performance
television viewers
sky watchers discovered a new star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectator - a person who watches an event, show, or activity
  • Synonym: audience - a group who watch or listen to something
  • Antonym: participant - someone who takes part in something (opposite of observer)

Etymology

From view + -er (agent noun suffix).
