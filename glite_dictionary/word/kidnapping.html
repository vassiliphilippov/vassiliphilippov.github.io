kidnapping

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdˌnæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɪdˌnapɪŋ / (BrE)
The crime of taking someone away by force or fraud, often to obtain a ransom.
abducting someone unlawfully
legal

Examples

The police launched an investigation into the kidnapping of the business tycoon.
After the kidnapping, the family received several ransom notes.
Laws against kidnapping are strictly enforced in most countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abduction - the action of forcibly taking someone away

Etymology

From 'kidnap' (from 'kid' + 'nap', originally meaning 'seize a child'), + '-ing'.
