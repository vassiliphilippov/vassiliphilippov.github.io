incurved

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɝvd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːvd / (BrE)
bent inward or having an inward curve.
bent inward or curved inward

Examples

The flower's petals were incurved at the tips, giving it a graceful appearance.
The artist drew a vase with an incurved rim to add elegance to the design.
The bird's beak was slightly incurved, making it ideal for extracting seeds from narrow spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a continuously bending line without angles
  • Antonym: outcurved - bent or curved outward

Etymology

From Latin 'incurvus', from 'in-' (inward) + 'curvus' (curved).
