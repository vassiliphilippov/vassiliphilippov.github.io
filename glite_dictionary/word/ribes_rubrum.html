Ribes rubrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪbiːz ˈruːbrəm / (AmE), / ˈriːbiːz ˈruːbrʊm / (BrE)
A species of currant native to Europe, cultivated for its clusters of small, edible red berries.
European shrub bearing red edible berries

Examples

The garden was lined with rows of Ribes rubrum, their bright red berries glistening in the morning sun.
Jam made from Ribes rubrum has a tangy flavor prized in traditional European recipes.
Botanists studied Ribes rubrum to understand its resistance to common plant diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red currant - common name for Ribes rubrum, a European shrub with red berries

Etymology

Modern Latin, from genus name Ribes and Latin rubrum meaning 'red'.
