exasperating

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzæspəˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːspəˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely annoying; causing great irritation or frustration.
extremely annoying or irritating

Examples

Her exasperating habit of interrupting everyone made the meeting last much longer than necessary.
It's exasperating when people leave their dirty dishes in the sink all day.
The exasperating noise from the construction work kept me awake all night.

Synset Examples

his cavalier curtness of manner was exasperating
I've had an exasperating day
her infuriating indifference
the ceaseless tumult of the jukebox was maddening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infuriating - making one extremely angry or annoyed
  • Degree Lower: annoying - causing irritation or slight anger
  • Synonym: maddening - causing anger or extreme irritation; similar intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'exasperare' (to roughen, irritate), via late Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzæspəˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːspəˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Making someone feel very annoyed, irritated, or frustrated; extremely irritating.
causing irritation or frustration

Examples

Waiting in that endless line was absolutely exasperating.
His exasperating tendency to interrupt everyone made the meeting drag on.
The exasperating sound of the alarm clock jolted her out of bed every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoying - causing irritation or bother
  • Synonym: infuriating - causing intense anger or outrage
  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming effect

Etymology

From Latin 'exasperare' (to make rough, irritate), via the verb 'exasperate' and the adjective suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01343475-s
Synset POS: s
