landlubberly

1. ADJ

/ ˈlændˌlʌbɚli / (AmE), / ˈlændˌlʌbəli / (BrE)
Lacking experience or skill in seamanship; characteristic of a landlubber.
inexperienced in seamanship

Examples

His landlubberly attempts to tie a knot amused the seasoned sailors.
She felt landlubberly on her first voyage, struggling to keep her balance on deck.
The captain spotted his landlubberly stance and offered advice on how to stand at sea.

Synset Examples

of all landlubbers the most lubberly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or seamanship
  • Antonym: seamanlike - skilled or expert in seamanship

Etymology

From 'landlubber', meaning a person inexperienced with the sea, + '-ly'.
