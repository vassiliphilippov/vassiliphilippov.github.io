vignette

1. NOUN

/ vɪnˈjɛt / (AmE), / vɪnˈjɛt / (BrE)
a small illustration or decorative design, especially at the beginning or end of a book chapter
small decorative illustration in a book

Examples

The first page of the novel features a beautiful vignette of a tree.
Each chapter in the book begins with a small vignette drawn by the author.
A delicate vignette of a ship adorned the bottom of the title page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - a picture or diagram that helps make something clear

Etymology

From French 'vignette', diminutive of 'vigne' (vine), referring originally to vine-like decorations.

2. NOUN

/ vɪnˈjɛt / (AmE), / vɪnˈjɛt / (BrE)
a short, descriptive piece of writing that clearly presents a scene, character, or mood; a brief literary sketch
brief descriptive literary sketch

Examples

The novel opens with a vignette describing a quiet morning in the village.
Each vignette in the collection captures a fleeting moment from the author's travels.
She included a short vignette about her childhood in her memoir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a short descriptive piece of writing or performance
  • Degree Higher: scene - a representation of a location or situation in writing or performance

Etymology

From French 'vignette', diminutive of 'vigne' (vine), originally referring to ornamental designs of vine leaves.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07217676-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ vɪˈnɛt / (AmE), / vɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
a photograph whose edges fade out gradually to the background instead of forming a clear border
photo with fading edges

Examples

The old wedding vignette on the mantelpiece has soft, faded edges that add to its charm.
I applied a black and white vignette to my portrait, making the center stand out.
Professional photographers sometimes use a vignette to give images a nostalgic or classic look.

Etymology

From French *vignette* (little vine), originally a decorative design, later applied to faded photographic borders.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04542448-n
Synset POS: n
