stager

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person who supervises the physical aspects of stage production and is in charge of the stage during a performance
person managing a stage during a show

Examples

The stager coordinated the set changes swiftly between scenes.
Every theater production relies on a skilled stager to ensure everything runs smoothly on stage.
As a stager, she resolved last-minute issues backstage with impressive calm.

Etymology

From stage + -er, indicating a person connected with the stage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person with considerable experience, especially someone who has served for a long time or been through many challenges
an experienced person; veteran

Examples

After three decades at the company, he was known as an old stager among his colleagues.
The general relied on the advice of a seasoned stager during the campaign.
In politics, a stager often recognizes patterns that less experienced members miss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From 'stage' meaning a period in a process; by analogy, one who has passed through many stages.
