Euphorbia caput-medusae

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔːrbiə ˌkæpət mɛˈdjuːzi / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˌkæpʊt mɛˈdjuːziː / (BrE)
A species of African dwarf succulent perennial shrub with numerous slender, drooping branches, belonging to the spurge family.
African succulent shrub species

Examples

The Euphorbia caput-medusae thrives in sandy soils along the South African coastline.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique appearance of Euphorbia caput-medusae and its Medusa-like branches.
Many gardeners grow Euphorbia caput-medusae as a striking ornamental succulent in their collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Medusa's head spurge', named for its resemblance to Medusa's hair of snakes in Greek mythology.
