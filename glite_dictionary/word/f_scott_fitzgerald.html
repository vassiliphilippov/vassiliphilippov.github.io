F. Scott Fitzgerald

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛf ˈskɑt ˈfɪdʒ.əɹ.əld / (AmE), / ˌef ˌskɒt ˈfɪdʒ.ər.əld / (BrE)
(1896–1940) American novelist whose works, such as 'The Great Gatsby', defined the Jazz Age in the United States.
American novelist of the Jazz Age

Examples

F. Scott Fitzgerald is best known for authoring 'The Great Gatsby', a classic American novel.
Many literary critics consider F. Scott Fitzgerald an essential voice of the Jazz Age.
The life of F. Scott Fitzgerald was marked by both critical acclaim and personal struggle.
