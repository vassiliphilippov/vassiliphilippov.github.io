venae conjunctivales

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːni kənˌdʒʌŋktɪˈveɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈviːni kənˌdʒʌŋktɪˈveɪliːz / (BrE)
Veins that drain blood from the conjunctiva of the eye.
veins draining the conjunctiva
medical

Examples

The venae conjunctivales play a crucial role in the eye's vascular system.
Inflammation can sometimes affect the venae conjunctivales, leading to redness in the eyes.
Ophthalmologists observed dilation of the venae conjunctivales during the examination.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'conjunctivus' (conjunctival; relating to the conjunctiva).
