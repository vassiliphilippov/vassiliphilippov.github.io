Catostomus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtɑstəˌməs / (AmE), / kəˈtɒstəˌməs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fishes (suckers) in the family Catostomidae, found mainly in North America.
genus of freshwater fishes (suckers)

Examples

Species in the genus Catostomus are commonly known as suckers.
The longnose sucker, or Catostomus catostomus, is widespread in North American rivers.
Researchers documented several new Catostomus populations in the Great Basin area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Catostomidae - the family of freshwater fishes that includes Catostomus

Etymology

From Greek *katostomos* (‘with a large mouth’), referring to the prominent mouth of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtɑstəməs / (AmE), / kəˈtɒstəməs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Catostomidae, commonly known as suckers, native to North America and parts of Asia.
genus of freshwater fish (suckers)

Examples

The fish in the genus Catostomus are often found in slow-moving streams and rivers.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Catostomus genus in the mountain lake.
Many North American anglers are familiar with Catostomus because of the common white sucker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucker - a common name for fish of the family Catostomidae

Etymology

From Greek 'katostomus' meaning 'downward-mouthed,' referring to the fish's mouth structure.
