gazetteer

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˌzɛˈtɪr / (AmE), / ɡəˌzɛˈtɪə / (BrE)
(historical) A journalist or writer for a gazette, especially a newspaper or periodical.
journalist who writes for a gazette (historical)
archaic

Examples

In the eighteenth century, the gazetteer played a crucial role in shaping public opinion.
Samuel Johnson was once rumored to have been a gazetteer for a London newspaper.
The duties of a gazetteer often included gathering reports from foreign correspondents.

Etymology

From 'gazette', used with the agent suffix '-eer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡæzəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌɡæzɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
A book or section of a book containing a list of geographical names with information about them, often found at the back of an atlas.
geographical dictionary or index

Examples

The gazetteer at the end of the atlas provides details about each country's capital city.
Before traveling, she consulted a gazetteer to learn more about the towns along her route.
This historical gazetteer lists hundreds of villages that have since disappeared.

Etymology

From French 'gazetier' (journalist, publisher), adopted for reference works on place names in English in the 18th century.
