specialism

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of concentrating on and developing expertise in a particular area of study, occupation, or activity.
focus on a particular field or activity

Examples

Medicine has seen increasing specialism over the last century, with doctors focusing on narrower fields.
Her specialism in medieval literature made her a sought-after expert at academic conferences.
We value breadth of knowledge, but specialism is equally important in today's workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - the process of becoming an expert in a particular subject or skill

Etymology

From 'special' + '-ism', patterned after 'specialization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a particular area of study or work to which someone has devoted themselves, especially as a profession
a specific area of expertise or study

Examples

After medical school, she chose cardiology as her specialism.
His specialism in marine biology led him to groundbreaking research on coral reefs.
Companies often look for candidates with a specialism relevant to the industry.

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - the process of concentrating on and becoming expert in a particular subject or skill

Etymology

From 'special', ultimately from Latin 'specialis' (particular, individual) + '-ism' (denoting practice or system).
