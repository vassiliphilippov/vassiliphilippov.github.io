Dendroica striata

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnˌdroʊˈaɪkə straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrɔɪˈkə straɪˈeɪtə / (BrE)
A North American warbler with a black-and-white head, commonly known as the blackpoll warbler.
a North American warbler species

Examples

The Dendroica striata migrates thousands of miles each year across North America.
Researchers studied the plumage of the Dendroica striata during the breeding season.
We spotted a Dendroica striata in the spruce forest during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackpoll warbler - Common name for Dendroica striata, a North American warbler

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, Dendroica from Greek dendron (tree) and oikos (house), striata from Latin striatus (striped).
