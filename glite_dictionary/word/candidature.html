candidature

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændɪˌdeɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdətʃə / (BrE)
the fact or state of being a candidate for an office or position, especially in an election; the campaign or period during which someone is a candidate.
state of being a candidate (esp. in elections)
british

Examples

His candidature for the leadership position was supported by many party members.
After announcing her candidature, she toured the country to gain support.
The party debated internally before officially endorsing his candidature for mayor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candidacy - state of being a candidate (esp. American English)

Etymology

From French 'candidature', from Latin 'candidatus' (clothed in white, candidate).
