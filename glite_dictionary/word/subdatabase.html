subdatabase

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌdeɪtəˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌdeɪtəˌbeɪs / (BrE)
a subset of data within a database, typically used for a specific application or purpose
subset within a database

Examples

Each department maintains its own subdatabase within the company’s main information system.
The application only requires access to a small subdatabase rather than the entire dataset.
You can improve performance by restricting queries to a relevant subdatabase.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: database - an organized collection of structured information or data

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'sub-' (meaning 'under' or 'part of') to 'database'.
