Daubentoniidae

1. NOUN

/ dɔːbɛnˈtoʊniˌaɪdiː / (AmE), / dɔːbɛnˈtəʊnɪˌiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of lemur-like primates comprising only the aye-aye (Daubentonia madagascariensis), native to Madagascar.
family of primates containing only the aye-aye

Examples

The Daubentoniidae family is unique in comprising just a single species, the aye-aye.
Researchers study Daubentoniidae to better understand primate evolution in Madagascar.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the endangered Daubentoniidae from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Daubentonia - Genus containing the aye-aye
  • Synonym: aye-aye - A lemur native to Madagascar, only member of Daubentoniidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Daubentonia, named after French naturalist Louis-Jean-Marie Daubenton, and the family suffix -idae.
