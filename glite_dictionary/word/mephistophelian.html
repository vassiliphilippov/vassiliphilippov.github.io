mephistophelian

1. ADJ

/ məˌfɪstəˈfiliən / (AmE), / mɛˌfɪstəˈfiːlɪən / (BrE)
Clever, cunning, or wicked in a way that is characteristic of a devil, especially Mephistopheles.
cunning or devilish (like Mephistopheles)

Examples

The villain's mephistophelian grin revealed his malicious intentions to everyone in the room.
She devised a mephistophelian plot to outwit her rivals and seize the company.
His mephistophelian charm masked a deeply manipulative nature.

Synset Examples

devilish schemes
the cold calculation and diabolic art of some statesmen
the diabolical expression on his face
a mephistophelian glint in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - extremely evil or wicked, characteristic of the devil
  • Synonym: satanic - related to or characteristic of Satan or great wickedness
  • Antonym: angelic - like or appropriate to an angel, especially in virtue, innocence, or beauty

Etymology

From the name Mephistopheles, a demon in German folklore and literature, especially Goethe's 'Faust', with the English adjectival suffix -ian.
