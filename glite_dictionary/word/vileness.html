vileness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant, morally offensive, or disgusting
the state of being vile or disgusting

Examples

The vileness of the crime shocked even the most hardened detectives.
She recoiled at the vileness of the smell coming from the abandoned building.
Many condemned the vileness of his actions during the trial.

Synset Examples

the vileness of his language surprised us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vileness - the state or quality of being vile; same meaning, alternative spelling
  • Antonym: virtue - moral excellence or goodness; opposite of vileness

Etymology

From Middle English vyle, from Old French vil (base of vilain), from Latin vilis (cheap, base) with the suffix -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely wicked, unpleasant, or morally bad
wickedness; moral depravity

Examples

The vileness of his actions shocked everyone in the courtroom.
Some stories reveal the vileness that can exist within the human heart.
Many were outraged by the vileness of the crime and demanded justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From 'vile' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin 'vilis' (cheap, base).
