plagiarizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪdʒəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒərʌɪzə / (BrE)
a person who copies someone else's words or ideas and presents them as their own
person who commits plagiarism

Examples

The university expelled the plagiarizer after discovering he had copied his final essay.
As a writer, she found it frustrating when a plagiarizer stole her work without credit.
In academia, being labeled a plagiarizer can ruin your reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarist - someone who commits plagiarism
  • Antonym: original thinker - a person who creates ideas independently

Etymology

From plagiarize + -er, modeled after 'plagiarist'.
