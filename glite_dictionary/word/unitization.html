unitization

1. NOUN

/ ˌjunɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting an investment trust into a unit investment trust, where individual units can be bought and sold by investors
conversion of an investment trust into a unit investment trust

Examples

The unitization of the fund allowed investors to trade units on the open market.
Regulatory changes led to the unitization of several traditional investment trusts.
Following unitization, shareholders could more easily manage their portfolios.

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ization', following the pattern of forming nouns indicating the process of making into units.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of packaging cargo or materials into unit loads to facilitate handling, storage, or transport
the packaging of cargo into unit loads

Examples

The unitization of shipments allows faster loading and unloading at the distribution center.
Modern warehouses rely heavily on unitization to improve efficiency and reduce manual handling.
Implementing unitization procedures has significantly decreased shipping costs for the company.

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ization', patterned after related industrial nouns like 'containerization'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in psychology) the process of combining smaller pieces of information into larger, coordinated units to aid memory or understanding.
combining information into larger units

Examples

The teacher encouraged unitization to help students remember the list of numbers more effectively.
Memory researchers study how unitization improves recall during learning tasks.
By practicing unitization, she could remember long sequences with greater ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chunking - process of grouping information for easier recall

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ization'; pattern of forming nouns meaning 'the process of making into a unit.'

4. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of combining interests or operations, especially for the joint development of an oil or gas reservoir that extends under land controlled by different parties
joint development of oil or gas resource
legal

Examples

The government required unitization to ensure efficient extraction of the oil field.
Unitization agreements can be complex, given the number of stakeholders involved.
Without unitization, companies might compete wastefully over shared petroleum reservoirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pooling - the combining of resources, especially in oil and gas development

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ization', patterned after similar terms such as 'organization'.
