Sir Karl Raimund Popper

1. PROPN

/ sɝ kɑrl ˈraɪmənd ˈpɒpər / (AmE), / sə kɑːl ˈraɪmənd ˈpɒpə / (BrE)
(1902–1994) Austrian-born British philosopher known for his influential work on the philosophy of science, particularly the idea that scientific theories cannot be conclusively proven, only falsified.
Austrian-born British philosopher (1902–1994)

Examples

Sir Karl Raimund Popper was a leading figure in twentieth-century philosophy of science.
The notion of falsifiability is central to the work of Sir Karl Raimund Popper.
Many scientists credit Sir Karl Raimund Popper with challenging the way we think about scientific progress.
