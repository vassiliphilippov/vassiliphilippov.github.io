Charybdis

1. PROPN

/ kəˈrɪb.dɪs / (AmE), / kəˈrɪb.dɪs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a monstrous whirlpool that threatens sailors, located opposite Scylla in a narrow strait.
mythological whirlpool monster

Examples

The ship barely escaped being swallowed by the fearsome Charybdis during its treacherous voyage.
In the myth, Odysseus must steer a course between Scylla and Charybdis to survive.
Writers often reference Charybdis as a metaphor for an unavoidable danger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Scylla - a sea monster from Greek mythology, positioned opposite Charybdis

Etymology

From Greek Χάρυβδις (Khárybdis), the name of the mythical whirlpool.
