legislator

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who has the authority to make or enact laws, especially as a member of a legislature
person who makes or enacts laws

Examples

The legislator introduced a new bill to address climate change.
Each legislator must vote according to their conscience and the interests of their constituents.
Experienced legislators often serve on important committees within the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawmaker - a person who makes or enacts laws
  • Antonym: judge - a public official who decides cases in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin 'legislator' (law proposer), from 'lex' (law) + 'lator' (proposer/carrier).
