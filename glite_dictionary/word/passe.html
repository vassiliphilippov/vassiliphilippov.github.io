passe

1. ADJ

/ pæˈseɪ / (AmE), / paˈseɪ / (BrE)
No longer fashionable or current; out of date.
out of fashion; outdated

Examples

The old coffee shop's decor is charmingly passe by modern standards.
Those bell-bottom jeans are considered passe in today’s fashion industry.
He fears his ideas may seem passe to the younger generation.

Synset Examples

a suit of rather antique appearance
demode (or outmoded) attire
outmoded ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outmoded - no longer fashionable or current
  • Synonym: old-fashioned - characteristic of past styles, not current
  • Antonym: trendy - very fashionable or up to date

Etymology

From French passé (past, out of date), past participle of passer (to pass).
