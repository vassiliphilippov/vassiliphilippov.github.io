counsellor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl.ə / (BrE)
A lawyer who gives legal advice or represents clients in a court of law.
lawyer; legal advisor
legal british

Examples

The defense counsellor presented a compelling argument to the jury.
She consulted her counsellor before making any legal decisions.
The judge addressed the counsellor directly during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters; lawyer
  • Synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to practice as an advocate, particularly in higher courts
  • Synonym: solicitor - a lawyer who gives legal advice and prepares legal documents, especially in Britain

Etymology

From Old French 'conseillour', from Latin 'consiliarius' (one who gives advice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.sə.lə / (BrE)
a person who supervises and cares for children at a summer camp
supervisor at a summer camp

Examples

Each counsellor is assigned a group of campers to look after during the summer.
My older sister worked as a counsellor at a lakeside camp last year.
The counsellor organized various outdoor activities for the children every afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counselor - American spelling of 'counsellor'

Etymology

From Latin consiliarius (advisor), via French conseiller. British spelling of 'counselor'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.sə.lə / (BrE)
a person trained to give guidance on personal, social, or psychological problems.
person who gives advice or guidance

Examples

After the accident, she met regularly with a counsellor to talk about her feelings.
Many schools provide a counsellor to help students cope with personal issues.
He decided to become a counsellor because he enjoys helping people overcome their difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisor - someone who gives advice in a professional context
  • Synonym: therapist - a person trained to treat people with emotional problems
  • Antonym: client - person receiving guidance from a counsellor

Etymology

From Latin 'consiliarius', from 'consilium' (counsel, advice).
