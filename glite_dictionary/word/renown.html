renown

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈnaʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊn / (BrE)
the state of being widely known, respected, and admired for achievements or qualities
widespread fame and honor

Examples

His scientific discoveries brought him international renown.
The artist achieved great renown during his lifetime.
The city's renown for beautiful architecture attracts many tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fame - the state of being known by many people
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown, inconspicuous, or unimportant

Etymology

From Old French renon, from Latin renomen (fame, report).
