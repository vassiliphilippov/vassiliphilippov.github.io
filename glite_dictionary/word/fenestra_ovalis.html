fenestra ovalis

1. NOUN

/ fɛˈnɛstrə oʊˈvælɪs / (AmE), / fɛˈnɛstrə əʊˈveɪlɪs / (BrE)
An oval opening in the middle ear, with the base of the stapes attached to it, that transmits sound vibrations to the inner ear.
oval opening in the middle ear
medical

Examples

The vibration of the stapes at the fenestra ovalis is crucial for hearing.
Damage to the fenestra ovalis can result in hearing impairment.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully examined the fenestra ovalis for any abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval window - Anatomical term for the fenestra ovalis in the middle ear

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'oval window'.
