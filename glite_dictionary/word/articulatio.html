articulatio

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌtɪk.juˈleɪ.ʃioʊ / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪk.juˈleɪ.ʃiəʊ / (BrE)
(Medical) A joint; the point where two bones or elements of a skeleton meet, especially if motion is possible.
anatomical joint; skeletal connection
medical

Examples

Damage to the articulatio can lead to restricted movement in the limb.
The knee is an example of a complex articulatio that allows for flexion and extension.
Inflammation at the articulatio may be a symptom of arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - the point where two bones meet in the body

Etymology

From Latin articulatio (a joint, joining together).
