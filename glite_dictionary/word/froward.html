froward

1. ADJ

/ ˈfroʊ.ərd / (AmE), / ˈfrəʊ.əd / (BrE)
(archaic) habitually disobedient, difficult to deal with, or contrary.
disobedient or contrary (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The froward child refused to follow any of the teacher’s instructions, no matter how simple.
In old stories, a froward servant often brought mischief to the household.
Her froward attitude made her difficult to manage in a team environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - opposing or stubborn in attitude
  • Antonym: tractable - easy to control or influence

Etymology

From Old English 'fromweard' (turned away), from 'from' (away) + 'weard' (toward).
