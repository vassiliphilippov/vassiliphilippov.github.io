disquieted

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkwaɪətɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwaɪətɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing anxious uneasiness, worry, or discontent
feeling anxious or uneasy

Examples

He appeared visibly disquieted by the sudden change in the meeting's tone.
She felt disquieted after reading the disturbing news article.
Their disquieted expressions revealed their deep concern about the issue.

Synset Examples

too upset to say anything
spent many disquieted moments
distressed about her son's leaving home
lapsed into disturbed sleep
worried parents
a worried frown
one last worried check of the sleeping children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbed - feeling upset or troubled
  • Synonym: worried - experiencing or showing concern or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From the verb 'disquiet' (to make uneasy), based on Latin 'dis-' (lack of) + Old French 'quiet' (quiet, still).
