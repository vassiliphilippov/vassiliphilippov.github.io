Amaranthus graecizans

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈrænθəs ɡriːˈsaɪzænz / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθəs ɡriːˈkɪzanz / (BrE)
a bushy annual plant native to the western United States, belonging to the amaranth family
bushy plant of the amaranth family

Examples

The leaves of Amaranthus graecizans are often consumed in salads and soups.
Amaranthus graecizans can thrive in arid soils, making it useful for erosion control.
Botanists recently discovered several new populations of Amaranthus graecizans in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - a plant of the amaranth family, often with brightly colored flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'amaranthos' (unfading flower) and Latinized species name 'graecizans' (resembling Greek).
